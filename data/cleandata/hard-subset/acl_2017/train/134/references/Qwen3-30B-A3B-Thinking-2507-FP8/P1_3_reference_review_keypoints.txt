- Proposes the first neural end-to-end solutions for full computational argumentation mining, offering four framings (token-level dependency parsing, sequence tagging with distance encoding, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER evaluation).  
- Shows neural end-to-end models outperform feature-based ILP baselines without hand-crafted features.  
- Finds token-level dependency parsing ineffective for AM while sequence tagging with distance encoding is robust.  
- Demonstrates multi-task learning with component detection and relation identification as auxiliary tasks significantly improves performance.

- Significant methodological advance by removing need for manual feature engineering and ILP constraints.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparison across models and text granularity (paragraph vs essay), with STagBLCC performing best.  
- Provides theoretical insight that token-level dependency parsing fails despite near tree-like argument structures, guiding future work.  
- Practical implication: MTL improvements suggest broader applicability to structured prediction and relation extraction tasks.

- Fails to analyze why token-level dependency parsing performs poorly despite near tree-like structures.  
- Omits comparison to Potash et al. (2017), weakening the claim of being the first neural end-to-end AM solution.  
- Sequence tagging label space encodes distances (potentially infinite) without adequate discussion of handling out-of-range distances.  
- Uses C-F1 and R-F1 with 100%/50% matching levels without sufficient justification or comparison to other metrics.  
- Key hyperparameters are relegated to supplementary material, reducing reproducibility and assessment of model complexity.

- Why does token-level dependency parsing underperform compared to sequence tagging—noise or dataset structure?  
- How will the model handle distances between components that exceed the training range or are effectively unbounded?  
- Why was Potash et al. (2017) not included in evaluations, and how would inclusion affect results?  
- Can the authors quantify how possible relations scale with text length and suggest mitigation strategies for LSTM-ER degradation on essays?

- Overall: significant contribution with compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing Potash (2017), deeper analysis of token-level parsing failure, label-space handling, and metric/hyperparameter transparency.
