- Proposes neural end-to-end models for full AM (component segmentation/classification and relation identification/classification) without hand-crafted features or ILP constraints.
- Investigates four neural framings: dependency parsing, sequence tagging with distance encoding, multi-task learning, and the LSTM-ER model.
- Evaluated on the Persuasive Essays dataset (Stab & Gurevych 2016); neural end-to-end approaches outperform the ILP baseline, with sequence tagging particularly effective.
- Finds dependency parsing ineffective for end-to-end AM, with performance degrading from paragraph to essay level.
- Shows multi-task learning (component and relation detection as auxiliary tasks) significantly improves performance.
- First demonstration of neural end-to-end solutions for AM, removing reliance on feature engineering and ILP.
- Comparative analysis explains why dependency parsing fails and why sequence tagging remains robust across evaluation levels.
- Effective multi-task learning framework that materially boosts main-task performance.
- Strong empirical evaluation with careful model variant comparisons and detailed error analyses (relation distance distributions, relation identification probabilities).
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (PE), reducing claims about generalizability to other AM corpora.
- Lacks a systematic analysis of error types, particularly relation prediction errors when many components are present.
- Comparison with the ILP model may be unfair because the ILP model uses paragraph-level information advantage and evaluation levels are mismatched.
- Does not thoroughly address handling of long-range dependencies beyond observed distances (e.g., relations >10 components apart).
- Question: How would sequence tagging with distance encoding handle relations beyond the dataset’s maximum observed distance (>10 components)?
- Question: How can the multi-task learning setup be generalized to other structured prediction tasks in NLP?
- Question: Have the authors evaluated models on other AM datasets to assess generalizability beyond PE?
- Question: Could document-level context or hierarchical modeling mitigate the essay-level performance drop for dependency parsers?
- Overall: A well-written, significant contribution to neural end-to-end AM that should address dataset scope, error analysis, comparison fairness, and long-range dependency handling in future work.
