- Paper proposes four neural end-to-end AM frameworks (dependency parsing, sequence tagging with distance encoding, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER) and evaluates them on the PE dataset.  
- Neural methods outperform the ILP baseline, with sequence tagging particularly effective.  

- Contribution: first comprehensive evaluation of neural end-to-end approaches for AM showing clear gains over ILP.  
- Contribution: empirical finding that token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for AM at larger scopes, while sequence tagging with distance encoding is robust.  
- Contribution: multi-task learning (especially using component detection as an auxiliary task) improves performance.

- Strength: Significant absolute performance gains over ILP baselines (notably STagBLCC improvements).  
- Strength: Strong analysis of modeling choices explaining why parsing fails at essay level and why sequence tagging works for long sequences.  
- Strength: Practical insight that component detection as an auxiliary task yields larger benefits than relation detection.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a single dataset (PE), raising questions about generalizability across different AM corpora.  
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to isolate the impact of key design choices (e.g., distance encoding).  
- Weakness: Did not evaluate alternative neural architectures (e.g., encoder-decoder or pointer networks) that prior work has used.  
- Weakness: Attribution of LSTM-ER’s relation weaknesses to architectural causes lacks rigorous statistical evidence.

- Question: Will the authors evaluate on additional datasets (e.g., Peldszus & Stede microtext) to show broader applicability?  
- Question: Can the authors provide ablation results to quantify the contribution of distance encoding?  
- Question: Why conclude token-level dependency parsing is ineffective when some neural parsers (LSTM-Parser) perform well?  
- Question: How exactly does LSTM-ER’s “de-coupling” of component and relation tasks lead to its strong component performance given similar entity detection schemes?

- Conclusion: The paper makes a valuable, strong case that neural end-to-end AM outperforms ILP, with sequence tagging and multi-task component detection being key strengths, but limited dataset scope and missing ablations prevent definitive claims.
