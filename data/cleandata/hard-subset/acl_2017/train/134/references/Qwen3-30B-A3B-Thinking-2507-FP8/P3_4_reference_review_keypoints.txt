- Presents first neural end-to-end approaches for computational argumentation mining, proposing four frameworks and evaluating on the Stab & Gurevych (2016) persuasive essays dataset.  
- Contribution: first comprehensive neural end-to-end AM solution eliminating manual feature engineering.  
- Contribution: neural methods outperform ILP baseline significantly (up to 13% component F1).  
- Contribution: shows token-level dependency parsing is ineffective compared to component-level approaches.  
- Strength: significant methodological advance replacing pipeline ILP and hand-crafted features with neural end-to-end models.  
- Strength: rigorous experimental comparison across multiple neural models, metrics (exact/approx), and input levels (paragraph/essay).  
- Strength: theoretical insight that token-level dependency parsing struggles due to complexity and large search space on long sequences.  
- Strength: multi-task learning with auxiliary subtasks (component detection, relation ID) provides practical performance gains.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single student-essay dataset, raising generalization concerns to other domains (legal, political, scientific).  
- Weakness: insufficient error analysis to identify causes of model failures (component segmentation vs relation ID, etc.).  
- Weakness: overgeneralized claim that token-based dependency parsing is ineffective; specific implementations may not rule out AM-specific parsers.  
- Weakness: lacks ablation studies to quantify contributions of model components (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF vs character CNN).  
- Question: clarify how ILP uses paragraph-level information and why it underperforms LSTM-ER at paragraph level.  
- Question: reconcile claim that LSTM-ER need not produce trees with the observation that essay argument structures are almost trees, and explain how LSTM-ER handles tree-like structure without constraints.  
- Question: explain impact of 92% cross-sentence relationships on neural design and why sequence tagging remains viable despite token-level relation challenges.  
- Overall: strong paper with convincing empirical gains and insights; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address dataset scope, error analysis, and claim tempering.
