- Paper compares four neural end-to-end argumentation mining framings on PE corpus and reports new SOTA in multiple settings.
- Proposes a sequence-tagging label that encodes BIO, component type, link distance, and stance (STagT) and shows MTL improvements.
- Shows token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for end-to-end AM on long documents (negative result).

- Comprehensive empirical study evaluating four framings at paragraph and essay levels and comparing to a strong ILP pipeline.
- STagT (distance- and stance-encoded tagging) is simple, feature-free, and competitive or superior to baselines.
- Multi-task learning with auxiliary component/relation taggers consistently improves end-to-end tagging.
- Strong baselines and statistical testing (Wilcoxon) boost credibility.
- Provides practical guidance: pairwise scorers help with few entities but scale poorly on long documents; constrained taggers scale better.

- Novelty is mainly empirical and incremental; core architectures are established aside from the tag design.
- Re-linking claim–major-claim many-to-one into a tree may lose information and the “loss-less” claim is unclear.
- Large, sparse tag space (b,t,d,s) and decoding/post-processing details (clipping distances, unseen d, enforcing single outgoing links) are under-specified.
- “Global F1” and other metric definitions are ambiguous; key experimental details (embeddings, dev split, hyperparams) are deferred to supplement.
- Dependency-parsing framing may be unfairly handicapped by token-level design, paragraph-only training, and atypical head assignments.

- Clarify how claim–major-claim many-to-all links are converted into a tree and how stance is preserved.
- Specify maximum distance |d| used, clipping/backoff strategy for distances, and decoding/post-processing to reconstruct spans and enforce one outgoing link.
- Define exactly how “global F1” is computed (harmonic mean vs macro/micro) and whether significance tests use document-level paired scores.
- Provide development split, pretrained embeddings and fine-tuning details, and ablations for CRF and char-CNN in STagBLCC.
- For LSTM-ER, did you try document-level encoders, distance-aware biaffine scorers, or pruning candidate pairs by distance?

- Recommend reframing the parsing negative result as a component-level parsing baseline to avoid segmentation confounds.
- Suggest adding a span-based joint model (predict spans then relations with distance-aware scoring) as a natural comparator to STagT.
- Fix reference formatting and typos (e.g., “seperately”).
