- Paper evaluates end-to-end neural approaches for argumentation mining on the Stab & Gurevych persuasive essays corpus, comparing sequence tagging, token-level dependency parsing, multi-task tagging, and LSTM-ER.  
- Introduces a distance-aware composite sequence-tagging label encoding BIO, component type, link distance, and stance, implemented with a BiLSTM-CRF-CNN tagger (STagT).  
- Shows STagT achieves new SOTA on the PE dataset without hand-crafted features and is robust across paragraph and essay settings.  
- Demonstrates token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for end-to-end AM on this dataset.  
- Finds a length-dependent trade-off: LSTM-ER excels on shorter paragraphs but degrades on long essays, while the constrained distance-aware tagger is more robust on essays.  
- Reports that multi-task training with component- and relation-only auxiliary tasks gives consistent, modest gains over single-task tagging.  
- Strong empirical results with clear takeaways and significance testing; paragraph vs essay analysis is particularly informative.  
- Elegant, minimally engineered approach: distance-aware tagging biases toward locality without complex feature/constraint engineering.  
- Comprehensive and fair comparisons against a joint ILP system, multiple dependency parsers, and LSTM-ER, using exact and approximate span matching.  
- The negative result for token-level dependency parsing is carefully analyzed and deemed valuable for the community.  
- Multi-task findings are practically relevant, showing gains even with a weaker baseline tagger.  
- Experiments are limited to a single corpus with idiosyncratic properties, raising generalization concerns to cross-paragraph links or denser graphs.  
- The “end-to-end” claim is weakened by reliance on paragraph-level processing and assumed paragraph boundaries at test time.  
- The dependency parsing baseline may be a strawman since it uses token-level nodes rather than the more natural component-level granularity.  
- The distance-augmented label space can grow with |d|; the paper lacks details on caps, bucketing, or handling unseen distances.  
- Ablations are insufficient: several factors (CRF, char-CNN, distance encoding, scheduled sampling, tree constraints) are confounded across systems.  
- Questions about distance encoding: max |d|, bucketing, signed vs absolute distances, and handling unseen values.  
- Questions about paragraph vs essay processing: whether gold paragraph boundaries are used, handling absent boundaries, and cross-paragraph relations in PE.  
- Questions about dependency parsing: whether a component-level parsing variant was tried or justified why token-level parsing is an appropriate baseline.  
- Questions about CRF constraints and within-span consistency for (t, d, s) tags.  
- Questions about LSTM-ER training details: number of negative pairs, constraints on outgoing links, sensitivity to scheduled sampling and pretraining.  
- Requests for reproducibility details: hyperparameters, pretrained embeddings, early stopping, and number of runs/seeds averaged for reported scores.
