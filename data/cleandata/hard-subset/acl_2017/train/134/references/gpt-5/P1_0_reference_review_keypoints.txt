- Empirical study showing neural end-to-end AM is competitive or better than a prior ILP joint model, compared across paragraph vs. essay and multiple modeling paradigms.  
- Practical sequence-tagging formulation encoding relation distance and stance, yielding robust gains and favoring short-range links.  
- Multi-task sequence tagging (component-only and relation-only auxiliary tasks) improves performance over monolithic tagging.  
- Token-level dependency parsing performs poorly end-to-end for AM due to sensitivity to long sequences and large search space.  

- Strong, comprehensive empirical evaluation across several neural paradigms and a competitive ILP baseline, reporting exact/approx span matching and relation F1 with significance testing.  
- Distance-aware labels provide a simple, effective locality bias without hand-crafted features or global constraints.  
- Clear analysis of length effects and trade-offs, explaining when LSTM-ER vs. constrained tagging is preferable.  
- Multi-task learning yields measurable gains, supporting decoupled supervision with shared representations.  
- Useful negative result and analysis discouraging token-level dependency parsing for this task.  

- Limited methodological novelty: architectures are largely off-the-shelf and main novelty is in label design and comparative study.  
- Results rely on a single-domain PE dataset; generalization to other AM schemas/graph structures is uncertain.  
- Potential label sparsity and under-specification in the distance-coded tag space (unclear capping/bucketing and handling of unseen distances).  
- Baselines may be incomplete (missing a strong CRF/non-CNN tagger and a modern neural pipeline); LSTM-ER may be handicapped by sentence-level TreeLSTMs.  
- Reproducibility concerns: many training details in supplement and lack of variance/significance reporting across random seeds.  

- Was a maximum |d| enforced or distance bucketing used, and how were rare/long distances handled at train/test?  
- How are claim–major-claim relations represented in STagT and counted in R-F1?  
- Do models know paragraph boundaries during essay-level training/inference; does resetting states by paragraph help?  
- For LSTM-ER, were dependency trees gold or automatic, and were alternatives (remove TreeLSTM or use document-level encoders) tried?  
- What are mean±std over seeds for STagBLCC, LSTM-ER, and best MTL runs, and are Table 2 gains significant?  

- Recommend an ablation isolating distance coding (tags with vs. without d).  
- Suggest error analysis per class (Premise/Claim/MC; Support/Attack/For/Against).  
- Public release of code and preprocessing would improve reproducibility.  

- Overall: solid empirical contribution with actionable takeaways for end-to-end AM but limited novelty and some missing experimental details.
