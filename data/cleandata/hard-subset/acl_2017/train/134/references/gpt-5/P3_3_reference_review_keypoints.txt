- Thorough empirical comparison of neural end-to-end formulations for argumentation mining, showing sequence tagging and LSTM-ER often outperform an ILP baseline, especially at paragraph level.  
- Proposal of a distance-augmented sequence tagging label that encodes relation distance, improving long-range dependency handling.  
- Evidence that multi-task learning with natural AM subtasks yields modest gains and analysis of paragraph vs essay behavior.  

- Comprehensive, well-controlled evaluation (multiple paradigms, exact/approx span metrics, document-level significance tests) and useful essay vs paragraph analysis.  
- Methodological insight that document-length sequences favor constrained local models (distance-aware tagging) while short documents can benefit from general relation models (LSTM-ER).  
- Practical end-to-end approaches reduce feature engineering and can match/surpass ILP on paragraphs; distance-aware tags use empirical priors to regularize relations.  
- Negative result for token-level dependency parsing with plausible explanations (global tree search over long sequences, token-tree mismatch, memory issues).  
- Informative error analysis (distance distribution, conditional accuracy vs |d|) that supports modeling choices.  

- Limited novelty in architectures and only modest gains on essays; improvements over ILP mainly at paragraph level.  
- Label-space inflation/sparsity from encoding distance not fully quantified (number of labels, capping/bucketing, unseen distances handling).  
- LSTM-ER comparison may be unfair because it relies on sentence-level dependency trees while most relations are cross-sentence.  
- Structural relabeling to fit tree constraints (relinking claims, adding root) may change gold structure and affect stance/distance distributions; "lossless" claim needs justification.  
- Analysis lacks per-class (MC/claim/premise), stance-wise, and distance-wise error breakdowns; only one dataset used so generality is untested.  
- MTL evaluated with a weaker backbone (BL) rather than the stronger BLCC, so reported gains may be confounded.  

- Question: How many distinct distance-tag labels were used, were distances capped/bucketed, and how are unseen distances at test handled?  
- Question: Please justify the "lossless" relinking of claims to a single major claim and adding a root; how does this affect stance, distance distribution, and metrics; can you report results without relinking?  
- Question: Did you try LSTM-ER without reliance on sentence-level dependency trees (e.g., document-level encoders), and is the essay-level R-F1 comparison fair?  
- Question: Why was MTL evaluated only with the BL backbone rather than BLCC; do gains persist with CRF and char-CNNs?  
- Question: Will you release code/configs; what dev split and early stopping criteria were used and were hyperparameters tuned on a separate dev set?  
- Question: Can you report per-class and per-stance results and performance as a function of |d| to clarify where each approach succeeds or fails?
