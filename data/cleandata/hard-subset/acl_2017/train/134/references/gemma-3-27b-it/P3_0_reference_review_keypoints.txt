- Comprehensive empirical evaluation: compares multiple neural approaches (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, MTL, LSTM-ER) for end-to-end argumentation mining and shows neural methods beat an ILP baseline on a persuasive essay dataset.
- Demonstrates feasibility: shows end-to-end neural models can perform competitively, reducing need for manual feature engineering and ILP constraints.
- Framing analysis: discusses trade-offs between dependency parsing and sequence tagging and the importance of task/data characteristics for model choice.

- Thorough experimental setup: uses a well-established dataset and evaluates at paragraph and essay levels against a strong baseline.
- Clear motivation and problem formulation: convincingly argues limitations of traditional AM and motivates neural end-to-end approaches.
- Detailed results analysis: investigates why certain methods work better, highlighting limitations of parsing and benefits of sequence tagging with distance encoding.
- Practical recommendations: offers actionable guidance on model selection based on document length and data traits.
- Multi-task learning insight: finds auxiliary component and relation detection tasks improve performance.

- Limited novelty in individual models: most architectures (BiLSTM-CRF, TreeLSTMs) are not novel; contribution is mainly in their application and evaluation for AM.
- Reliance on distance encoding: sequence tagging success depends heavily on distance encoding, with limited analysis of why or alternatives.
- Lack of ablation studies: missing systematic ablations within frameworks to show component-wise contributions (e.g., BiLSTM vs CRF).
- Limited error analysis: needs deeper qualitative examination of common error patterns across models.

- Did you try attention or hierarchical methods to mitigate parsing deterioration on longer (essay) sequences?
- Were alternative distance encodings (e.g., embeddings) explored and how did they compare?
- What was the hyperparameter tuning process, which hyperparameters were most critical, and how were optimal values chosen?
- In MTL, did you experiment with weighting task losses or dynamic weighting schemes to further improve performance?

- Expand related work to include recent neural relation extraction advances.
- Add a table summarizing model characteristics (local vs global, feature engineering, complexity).
- Some sections need more elaboration and contextualization for clarity.

- Overall: solid, well-executed paper with valuable empirical insights and practical recommendations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
