- Summary: Evaluates multiple neural end-to-end architectures for argument mining (dependency parsing, sequence tagging with distance encoding, MTL, LSTM-ER) on the PE dataset and outperforms an ILP baseline.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates neural models can perform competitive end-to-end AM without hand-engineered features.

- Contribution 2: Provides a comparative analysis of several neural framings, highlighting relative strengths and weaknesses.

- Contribution 3: Shows dependency parsing framing is ineffective for AM, presenting a valuable negative result.

- Strength 1: Strong baseline comparison to the ILP model from Stab & Gurevych (2016).

- Strength 2: Thorough exploration of diverse neural architectures.

- Strength 3: Clear problem framing and motivation for end-to-end neural approaches.

- Strength 4: Thoughtful error analysis, especially on paragraph vs. essay-level performance and long-range dependencies.

- Strength 5: Practical considerations included (hyperparameter details, software availability) aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation limited to a single dataset (PE essays), raising generalizability concerns.

- Weakness 2: Sequence tagging scheme with distance encoding is complex and may suffer from data sparsity.

- Weakness 3: Multi-task learning lacks detailed ablation studies to quantify auxiliary task contributions.

- Weakness 4: Missing comparative analysis of computational cost (training and inference time).

- Weakness 5: Limited exploration of more recent architectures (Transformers, GNNs) that might better capture complex relations.

- Question 1: Request for detailed label distribution analysis in the STagT scheme (distance frequencies, relation types, section variation).

- Question 2: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters and what hyperparameter search was performed?

- Question 3: Could more advanced architectures (Transformers, GNNs) help, and what challenges would they pose for AM?

- Question 4: What are the computational costs of the proposed approaches compared to the ILP baseline?

- Question 5: Beyond PE essays, which argumentative text domains are these models applicable to and what adaptations are needed?

- Additional comment 1: Some sections (e.g., dependency parsing description) need more intuitive explanations for non-experts.

- Additional comment 2: Suggest adding a table summarizing each approach’s key characteristics (modularity, constraints, complexity).

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with compelling results and insights; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
