- Paper explores neural end-to-end approaches (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, MTL, LSTM-ER) for computational argumentation mining (AM).  
- Several neural models outperform a strong ILP baseline on the Stab & Gurevych (2016) dataset.  
- Token-based dependency parsing framing for AM is ineffective.  
- Sequence tagging with distance encoding is robust and effective for relation identification.  
- Multi-task learning with auxiliary tasks yields modest improvements.  
- Contribution: first empirical demonstration of neural end-to-end solutions for AM.  
- Contribution: clear negative result guiding researchers away from token-level dependency parsing for AM.  
- Contribution: practical demonstration of distance-encoded sequence tagging for AM relations.  
- Strength: comprehensive exploration of diverse neural architectures enabling comparative analysis.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on a standard dataset demonstrating practical potential.  
- Strength: clear, well-reasoned analysis explaining why tagging succeeds and parsing fails.  
- Strength: practical focus on a simple, implementable sequence-tagging approach.  
- Weakness: individual models (BiLSTM-CRF, LSTM-ER, etc.) lack novelty—novelty is primarily in application and comparison.  
- Weakness: evaluation restricted to a single dataset (Stab & Gurevych 2016), limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Weakness: insufficient granular error analysis to explain model failure modes.  
- Weakness: MTL results are modest and sensitive to hyperparameters; configuration details and justification are unclear.  
- Question: Have models been tested on other AM datasets or domains to assess generalizability?  
- Question: Can the authors provide detailed error analysis for STagBLCC and LSTM-ER (most frequent misclassifications)?  
- Question: What hyperparameter tuning and auxiliary-task choices guided the MTL experiments?  
- Question: Why was the specific distance encoding chosen for sequence tagging; were alternatives tried?  
- Question: What are the computational costs (training/inference time) of each model compared to the ILP baseline?  
- Additional suggestion: expand descriptions (e.g., LSTM-ER), add a table summarizing model parameters and training times.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, addressing dataset scope, error analysis, and MTL clarity.
