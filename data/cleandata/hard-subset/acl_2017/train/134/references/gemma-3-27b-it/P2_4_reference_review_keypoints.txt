- Paper evaluates several neural end-to-end approaches for computational argumentation mining on the Stab & Gurevych (2016) dataset.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive empirical comparison establishing neural baselines for end-to-end AM.  
- Main contribution: shows sequence tagging with distance encoding is an effective, robust method outperforming ILP and other approaches.  
- Main contribution: provides analysis of framing choices (parsing vs. tagging, modularity, constraints) and their trade-offs.  
- Strength: strong experimental setup using a reputable dataset and clear paragraph vs. essay-level evaluation.  
- Strength: thorough exploration of multiple neural architectures across the task.  
- Strength: clear, insightful analysis explaining why certain methods work better, especially on local vs. global modeling and long-range dependencies.  
- Strength: practical recommendations for future work, including cautious modeling for longer documents.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in individual models—the contribution is mainly application and comparison rather than new architectures.  
- Weakness: LSTM-ER shows a large performance drop on essay-level relation identification, raising scalability concerns.  
- Weakness: lack of detailed ablation studies to quantify each auxiliary task’s contribution in MTL experiments.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis; more in-depth error characterization is needed to guide improvements.  
- Question: Could authors propose or test strategies to mitigate LSTM-ER’s essay-level relation identification drop (e.g., limiting candidate relations or alternate architectures)?  
- Question: Please detail the hyperparameter optimization process and whether tuning was model-specific.  
- Question: What criteria guided selection of auxiliary tasks in the MTL experiments—intuition or preliminary experiments?  
- Additional: paper is well-written and organized.  
- Additional: references are comprehensive and up-to-date.  
- Additional: include a more detailed dataset description (distribution of argument and relation types).  
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommending acceptance with minor revisions to address LSTM-ER scaling, add ablation studies, and deepen error analysis.
