- Paper investigates neural end-to-end approaches for argumentation mining (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task, LSTM-ER) and evaluates on the PE dataset vs a strong ILP baseline.  
- Key claimed contributions: first neural end-to-end solutions for AM; neural models outperform ILP; token-based dependency parsing is ineffective; sequence tagging with distance info and multi-task learning work well.  
- Primary assessment: convincingly demonstrates neural models can match or beat feature-engineered ILP, reducing reliance on manual features.  
- Secondary assessment: provides a comprehensive empirical comparison of neural framings, identifying promising and weak approaches.  
- Tertiary assessment: highlights the importance of relation modeling and task interaction (multi-task learning and LSTM-ER insights).  
- Strength: solid experimental setup using a well-annotated dataset and a strong ILP baseline for comparison.  
- Strength: clear problem framing and motivation for moving beyond feature engineering and pipelines.  
- Strength: insightful analyses and ablations (dependency parsing failure, distance encoding impact, multi-task benefits).  
- Strength: reproducibility improved by providing links to software.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis across models; lacks concrete examples of failure modes for top systems.  
- Weakness: sequence tagging scheme is complex (BIO, component type, distance, relation type), potentially harming interpretability and training.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to other state-of-the-art neural relation extraction models.  
- Weakness: sparse detail on hyperparameter optimization strategy, reducing reproducibility.  
- Question: please provide detailed error analysis with examples for STagBLCC and LSTM-ER — what argument types/relations are misidentified?  
- Question: rationale for including all four STagT components and results of experiments with simpler tag sets (e.g., removing distance)?  
- Question: elaborate on hyperparameter optimization process, search strategies, and key tuned hyperparameters per model.  
- Question: did you try techniques (attention, hierarchical models) to mitigate LSTM-ER’s long-range dependency issues?  
- Additional comment: paper is well-written, organized, and cites relevant literature.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor revisions recommended for acceptance.
