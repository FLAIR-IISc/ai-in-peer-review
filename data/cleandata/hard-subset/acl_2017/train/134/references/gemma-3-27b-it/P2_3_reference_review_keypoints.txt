- Paper investigates neural end-to-end approaches for Argumentation Mining (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, MTL, LSTM-ER) and shows several outperform an ILP baseline.  
- Empirical demonstration that neural models can effectively solve end-to-end AM, reducing need for hand-crafted features.  
- Systematic exploration of multiple neural framings (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning) with comparative insights.  
- Analysis shows token-level dependency parsing is ill-suited to AM and highlights the importance of capturing long-range dependencies.  
- Strong empirical results: sequence tagging approaches consistently outperform a strong ILP baseline (clear F1 improvements).  
- Thorough experimental setup: standard dataset (Stab & Gurevych 2016), paragraph vs essay evaluations, and comparisons across architectures.  
- Clear motivation for end-to-end learning in AM, emphasizing issues with pipelines and manual features (domain adaptation, error propagation).  
- Insightful analysis explaining why some approaches work better (limitations of dependency parsing, benefits of sequence tagging).  
- Effective use of multi-task learning; component classification as an auxiliary task provides notable gains over relation classification.  
- Limited novelty: individual architectures (BiLSTM-CRF, LSTM-ER) are established methods applied to AM rather than new models.  
- LSTM-ER shows a significant performance drop on essay-level relation identification, needing deeper error analysis and mitigation.  
- No ablation study to quantify contributions of model components (e.g., CNN layer, distance encoding) in sequence tagging models.  
- Limited error analysis; paper lacks concrete examples and breakdowns of challenging relation types or recurring error patterns.  
- Request for detailed error analysis of LSTM-ER’s essay-level failures, especially for long-distance relations.  
- Request for a table of hyperparameters for reproducibility (STagBLCC, LSTM-ER, etc.).  
- Question why feeding the component (C) task from the same layer improved MTL performance and what this implies about task dependency.  
- Request for discussion of practical considerations and computational cost for each approach, particularly dependency parsing.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- References are comprehensive and relevant.  
- Related work discussion is thorough.  
- Overall assessment: solid and valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address LSTM-ER discrepancy and add ablation/error analyses.
