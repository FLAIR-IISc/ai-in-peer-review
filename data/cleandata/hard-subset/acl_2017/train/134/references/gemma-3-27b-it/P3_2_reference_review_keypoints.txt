- Paper explores four neural framings for end-to-end argumentation mining: dependency parsing, sequence tagging with distance encoding, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER.  
- Neural approaches outperform a strong ILP feature-engineered baseline; sequence tagging with distance encoding (STagBLCC) performs particularly well.  
- Empirical demonstration that neural models can effectively tackle complex end-to-end AM, reducing reliance on engineered features.  
- Comparative analysis of framings and informative ablation studies in the multi-task setting.  
- Dataset-specific insight: the PE dataset is challenging and document length notably affects model performance (parsing and LSTM-ER struggle on longer texts).  
- Strong experimental setup with a credible ILP baseline and clear F1-based evaluation.  
- Thorough exploration of multiple architectures and configurations, showing breadth of investigation.  
- Insightful error analysis explaining parsing limitations, benefits of distance encoding, and why LSTM-ER drops on longer documents.  
- Practical dataset analysis, including useful discussion of paragraph vs. essay level prediction for AM.  
- Individual model components (BiLSTM-CRF, LSTM-ER) are not novel; the main contribution is their application/evaluation in AM.  
- Ablation studies for LSTM-ER are limited; more systematic component-wise analysis is needed.  
- Distance encoding is effective but simple (linear); exploring learned or relative position encodings could improve results.  
- Paper lacks detail on computational cost and resources, which affects reproducibility and adoption.  
- Question: were distance values truncated/quantized to simplify encoding and reduce output dimensionality?  
- Question: what impact did pre-training LSTM-ER have and were different pre-training strategies explored?  
- Question: why does component detection (C) help more than relation detection (R) as an auxiliary MTL task—task difficulty or interaction effects?  
- Question: how well will methods generalize beyond persuasive essays (PE dataset) to other genres like news or legal text?  
- Writing is clear, the argument-structure figure is helpful, and references are comprehensive.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance subject to addressing noted weaknesses.
