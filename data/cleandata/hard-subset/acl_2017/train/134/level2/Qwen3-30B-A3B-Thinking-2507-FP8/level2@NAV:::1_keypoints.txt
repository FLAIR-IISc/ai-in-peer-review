- Introduces the first neural end-to-end AM solutions that remove hand-crafted features and ILP constraints.  
- Frames AM as dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning.  
- Shows sequence tagging with distance encoding outperforms dependency parsing by 10–13% on standard metrics.  
- Demonstrates multi-task learning with natural subtasks improves performance.  
- Significant contribution for replacing manual feature engineering in AM.  
- Strong experimental design with thorough comparisons across multiple neural architectures on the PE dataset.  
- Counterintuitive finding that sequence tagging beats dependency parsing, with a plausible explanation.  
- Clear argument that dependency parsing fails due to combinatorial complexity of long sequences.  
- Insufficient exploration of whether specialized argumentation parsers could outperform standard parsers.  
- Inadequate analysis of why LSTM-ER degrades at essay level (needs deeper investigation).  
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset (PE), limiting generalizability.  
- Paper is well-written and shows good command of AM literature, but needs revisions to address dataset and analysis gaps.
