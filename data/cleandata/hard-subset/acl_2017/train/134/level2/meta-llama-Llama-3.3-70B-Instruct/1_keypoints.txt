- Summary: Studies neural end-to-end computational argumentation mining, comparing dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning against a feature-based ILP model on persuasive essays.
- Contribution: Presents the first neural end-to-end solutions to computational argumentation mining.
- Contribution: Shows several neural models outperform the state-of-the-art joint ILP model.
- Contribution: Demonstrates that a standard neural sequence tagging model is robust across environments.
- Strength: Provides a comprehensive evaluation of multiple neural architectures (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning).
- Strength: Demonstrates effectiveness of neural models in removing manual feature engineering and ILP constraint design.
- Strength: Reports new state-of-the-art end-to-end AM results on the PE dataset (Stab & Gurevych 2016).
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed error analysis to identify model failure modes and areas for improvement.
- Weakness: Does not compare results to other neural AM approaches such as encoder-decoder models.
- Weakness: Offers limited discussion of the broader implications for AM and NLP.
- Question: Please provide detailed error analysis including error types and their frequencies.
- Question: How could these results be applied or transferred to other NLP tasks (e.g., text classification, sentiment analysis)?
- Question: Are there plans to extend the approach to other datasets or domains (e.g., legal or scientific texts)?
