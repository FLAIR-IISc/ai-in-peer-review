- Introduces neural end-to-end AM framings: dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning (MTL), and hybrid LSTM-ER.  
- Claims first neural end-to-end solutions for AM and reports new state-of-the-art results on the PE dataset.  
- Shows token-based dependency parsing is ineffective for AM.  
- Demonstrates sequence tagging with distance encoding is robust, especially for long documents.  
- Finds multi-task learning with auxiliary subtasks (component detection, relation classification) improves performance.  
- Comprehensive experimental comparison of multiple neural framings, providing insights on their strengths and weaknesses.  
- Achieves significant improvements over ILP baselines, notably for sequence tagging and MTL setups.  
- Proposes a novel MTL setup that yields measurable gains and guides future AM work.  
- Provides a critical analysis explaining why dependency parsing underperforms for this task.  
- Offers practical recommendations: favor simpler sequence tagging for long documents and decouple component and relation detection.  
- Evaluation limited to the PE dataset, raising concerns about generalizability to other domains and argumentation structures.  
- Lacks deeper qualitative error analysis (e.g., common failure modes in relation detection).  
- Omits encoder-decoder models from experiments, leaving an unexplored architecture space.  
- Uses a highly complex sequence-tagging label encoding (BIO + type + distance + stance), which may hinder interpretability and scalability.  
- Questions remain on cross-dataset generalization, computational efficiency for long documents, and hyperparameter sensitivity in MTL.  
- Overall: strong, methodologically sound paper with SOTA results; recommend accept with minor revisions to address dataset scope and missing comparisons.
