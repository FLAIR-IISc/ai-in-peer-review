- Compares four end-to-end neural framings for argumentation mining on the PE dataset: token-level dependency parsing, composite-label token sequence tagging, multi-task sequence tagging, and LSTM-ER.
- Main empirical findings: BiLSTM-CRF-CNN taggers outperform ILP pipeline and parsers; MTL yields consistent gains; LSTM-ER excels at component ID (short texts) but worsens on relations for long texts; token-level dependency parsing is ineffective.
- Contribution: thorough comparison showing local sequence tagging with distance-coded labels is a strong, robust baseline and MTL helps.
- Contribution: analysis of when joint entity–relation models help (short texts) versus when local models are preferable (long texts).
- Contribution: negative result for token-level dependency parsing with plausible explanations tied to search space and sequence length.
- Strength: clear end-to-end framing and strong baselines avoiding hand-engineered features and ILP constraints.
- Strength: simple, effective sequence tagging that encodes relation distance and stance into tags, improving long-range dependency handling.
- Strength: careful analysis of document length differences (paragraph vs. essay) showing LSTM-ER degradation on long texts and tagger robustness.
- Strength: multi-task learning with natural subtasks consistently improves robustness.
- Strength: reproducibility considerations—released code/settings, multiple variants, span-matching metrics, and significance testing.
- Weakness: conclusions drawn from a single dataset (PE), limiting claims about generality across domains/theories.
- Weakness: MTL experiments use a weaker backbone (no char-CNN/CRF), so MTL gains may confound with architectural differences.
- Weakness: token-level head encoding for parsing may be suboptimal; span-level or biaffine graph parsers might be more appropriate.
- Weakness: unclear presentation and fairness of baselines (ILP reporting, absence of modern strong neural parsers like deep biaffine).
- Weakness: no ablation on the distance-coded composite label to isolate the contribution of explicit distance supervision.
- Question: please report ILP results separately for paragraph vs. essay levels for fair comparisons.
- Question: how sensitive are STag results to the maximum distance d and how are out-of-range distances handled at test time?
- Question: can you provide an ablation using the same BLCC backbone with and without MTL auxiliaries to isolate MTL effects?
- Question: did you try span-level representations or graph parsers for dependency parsing, and would those mitigate token-level issues?
- Question: for LSTM-ER on essays, did you try pruning relations by distance or paragraph locality to reduce O(m^2) candidates?
- Suggestion: evaluate on at least one additional AM dataset (e.g., microtexts or legal/web discourse) to test robustness.
- Suggestion: provide qualitative error analysis of common failure modes and how MTL affects them.
- Suggestion: explore constrained decoding for tagging to enforce valid BIO transitions and structural consistency.
- Suggestion: report training/inference time and memory to inform practical choices between framings.
- Suggestion: clarify table notations (e.g., “Acc.”) and harmonize stance label notation across sections.
