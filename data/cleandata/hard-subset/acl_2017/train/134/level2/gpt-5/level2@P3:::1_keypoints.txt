- Thorough empirical study comparing end-to-end neural AM framings (token-level parsing, distance-augmented sequence tagging, MTL tagging, joint entity–relation LSTM-ER) with paragraph- and essay-level evaluation.

- Sequence tagging that encodes inter-component distances achieves strong, often state-of-the-art performance without hand-crafted features or ILP.

- Multi-task learning with natural subtasks (component and relation prediction) improves end-to-end tagging; token-level dependency parsing underperforms here.

- Strong empirical comparison with careful baselines, exact/approximate matching, and competitive results versus prior ILP systems.

- Data-driven analysis links model behavior to data properties (relation distance distributions, entity-pair combinatorics, long-sequence effects).

- New neural SOTA results on paragraph-level component and relation F1, showing neural end-to-end AM can match or exceed feature-intensive pipelines.

- Findings that decoupling components and relations (via MTL or LSTM-ER) benefits component detection; single-label coupling is suboptimal.

- Relevant and timely work addressing practical concerns and long-range dependency generalization without dataset-specific engineering.

- Limited dataset scope (single English PE corpus) raises questions about generalization to other AM domains and graph structures.

- Dependency-parsing conclusion is tied to token-level design and resource limits; span-based or component-level parsers might perform differently.

- Distance-augmented label-space causes scalability and data-sparsity concerns for long-distance links; tagset size and bucketing/pruning not fully analyzed.

- Structural validity and constrained decoding are under-explored; simple constraints may improve LSTM-ER essay performance but were not evaluated.

- Missing multi-run statistics and variance reporting; training details (class weighting, early stopping, distance cutoffs) need clarification.

- Clarification requested on STag label-set size and bucketing, handling of rare distance labels, class-imbalance treatment, constrained decoding experiments for LSTM-ER, and number of random seeds/variance.

- Essential suggestions: add multi-run statistics, include constrained-decoding ablation for LSTM-ER/STag, and temper or add a component/span-based parsing baseline.

- Optional suggestions: evaluate on another AM corpus or cross-domain analysis, ablate distance encoding/bucketing and report tagset frequencies, and provide per-class F1 plus structural-validity metrics.

- Overall assessment: solid, insightful empirical contribution that advances neural end-to-end AM and would be stronger with the essential clarifications and modest additional experiments.
