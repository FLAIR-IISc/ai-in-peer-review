- Summary: Empirical comparison of four end-to-end AM framings on PE essays finds distance-augmented BiLSTM-CRF(-CNN) tagging best overall; parsing lags; MTL helps modestly; LSTM-ER strong on paragraphs but degrades on essays (relations).

- Contribution 1: Shows distance-augmented BiLSTM-CRF(-CNN) delivers SOTA end-to-end AM on PE, outperforming an ILP baseline without hand-engineered features.

- Contribution 2: Provides a clear negative result that token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for end-to-end AM, especially on long documents.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates auxiliary multitask supervision (component/relation subtasks) yields consistent, modest improvements.

- Strength: Thorough comparative evaluation across modeling paradigms with significance testing, showing tagging improves component and relation F1 over baselines and parsers.

- Strength: Provides practical insight that distance-aware tagging constrains relation search and copes better with long essays than pairwise relation models.

- Strength: Reduces reliance on hand-crafted features/constraints while matching or exceeding prior SOTA.

- Strength: Sensible analysis explaining LSTM-ER’s essay-level drop (O(m^2) relation candidates, many cross-sentence links) and why distance biases help.

- Weakness: Limited external validity — experiments run on a single dataset with specific structure; generalization to other AM corpora untested.

- Weakness: Composite tag space grows with distance values, risking sparsity; paper lacks quantification of tag cardinality, capping, or ablation on distance encoding.

- Weakness: LSTM-ER may be disadvantaged by sentence-level TreeLSTM despite predominantly cross-sentence relations; alternative document-level relation architectures not evaluated.

- Weakness: Dependency parsing mainly evaluated at paragraph level due to memory; cross-level comparability is uneven and stronger global decoding constraints for taggers are not explored.

- Question: What is the realized size of the composite tag set (unique (b,t,d,s)) and how are long-distance values handled (capping/bucketing)? Sensitivity?

- Question: Do results hold on other AM datasets with different structures or in domain transfer settings?

- Question: Can a document-level relation classifier (instead of sentence-level trees) within LSTM-ER mitigate the essay-level R-F1 drop?
