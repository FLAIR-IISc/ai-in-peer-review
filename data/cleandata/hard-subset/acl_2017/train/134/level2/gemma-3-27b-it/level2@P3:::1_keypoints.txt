- Paper explores neural end-to-end argumentation mining, comparing dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning.
- Main finding: token-based dependency parsing is ineffective for AM.
- Sequence tagging with distance encoding (STagBLCC) performs robustly and sets a new state-of-the-art on the PE dataset.
- Incorporating distance information into sequence tagging effectively models long-range relationships between argument components.
- Multi-task learning (e.g., joint component detection) improves performance by leveraging subtask relationships.
- The work is a valuable departure from prior feature-based, pipeline approaches and provides a useful comparative analysis of neural framings.
- Strong empirical results despite limited annotated data, outperforming a feature-based ILP baseline.
- Negative result on dependency parsing is insightful and challenges the assumption that argument structures are tree-like.
- Experiments are limited to the PE dataset, raising concerns about generalizability to other domains and structures.
- The paper lacks a detailed error analysis identifying common error types (component/relation misclassifications, missed components).
- The sequence tagging label set is large and complex (BIO, types, distances, relation types), which may hinder training and generalization.
- Limited exploration and reporting of LSTM-ER hyperparameter tuning; performance appears sensitive to settings.
- Insufficient justification or ablation for choosing specific BiLSTM layers for MTL task outputs.
- Question about computational cost and practical feasibility of training dependency parsers and LSTM-ER.
- Question about the most common error types for STagBLCC and whether errors are systematic by relation or component type.
- Question about sensitivity to the distance encoding scheme and whether alternatives were explored.
- Question whether LSTM-ER’s degradation on longer sequences is due to memory limits or inability to capture long-range dependencies.
- Suggestion to include a visual example illustrating the complex tagging scheme for clarity.
- Minor issues: inconsistent citation formatting in places.
- Recommend releasing code and data links to improve reproducibility.
