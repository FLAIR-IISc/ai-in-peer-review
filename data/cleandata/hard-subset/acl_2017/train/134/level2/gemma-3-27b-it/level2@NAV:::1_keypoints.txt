- Investigates neural end-to-end argumentation mining, framing it as token-based dependency parsing and sequence tagging with multi-task learning.
- Key finding: BiLSTM sequence tagging with distance encoding outperforms dependency parsing for this task.
- Primary contribution: thorough empirical evaluation showing simpler sequence tagging beats more complex parsing models.
- Shows multi-task learning provides additional performance gains and a promising research direction.
- Provides comprehensive experimental results on the PE dataset, offering a strong baseline for future work.
- Strength: extensive model comparison including established parsers, proposed tagger, and prior LSTM-ER system.
- Strength: detailed analysis with ablation studies and error analysis (supplementary), and clear articulation of approach limitations.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why dependency parsing fails to capture argumentation structure beyond noting long sequences.
- Weakness: limited discussion of PE dataset limitations and how its characteristics may bias generalizability.
- Weakness: unclear connection between the importance of distance encoding and LSTM-ER’s drop in performance on longer texts.
- Questions for authors: request detailed error analysis of parsing models and specifics on which argumentation aspects are missed.
- Questions for authors: ask how PE essay characteristics (style, domain) influence model performance.
- Questions for authors: request intuition on why distance encoding helps sequence tagging and its relation to LSTM-ER results.
- Additional comment: formatting/readability good; request authors to provide links to code and datasets.
