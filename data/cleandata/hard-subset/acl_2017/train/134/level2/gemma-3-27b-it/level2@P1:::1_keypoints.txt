- Main finding: Dependency-parsing framing performs poorly, while a simple BiLSTM sequence tagging model with distance encoding is robust and effective.  
- Main finding: Multi-task learning with auxiliary tasks (component detection and relation identification) improves performance.  

Contributions:
- Empirical investigation of neural end-to-end AM, comparing dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning against a strong ILP baseline.  
- Demonstration that a BiLSTM sequence tagging model outperforms more complex dependency-parsing approaches for AM.  
- Evidence that multi-task learning boosts overall AM performance by incorporating auxiliary tasks.  

Strengths:
- Strong experimental setup using the Stab & Gurevych (2016) dataset and comparison to a solid ILP baseline with clear C-F1 and R-F1 reporting.  
- Clear and concise writing with well-articulated motivation and experimental details.  
- Thorough ablation studies exploring tagging variants (BLCC, BL) and multi-task configurations.  
- Insightful error analysis highlighting LSTM-ER limitations on long sequences and the benefits of distance encoding.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited exploration of dependency parsing architectures and hyperparameters, leaving uncertainty whether negative results are inherent or implementation-dependent.  
- Lack of detailed error analysis for the tagging model (e.g., confusion matrices for component/relation types).  
- Over-reliance on a single dataset (PE), raising concerns about generalization to other domains or argumentation styles.  
- Limited discussion of generalization to unseen argument structures or dataset-specific effects.  

Questions for authors:
- What was the hyperparameter tuning process for dependency parsing models and were other architectures/configurations tried?  
- Can you provide a more granular error analysis for the sequence tagging models (e.g., specific component/relation misclassifications)?  
- Are there any results or plans to evaluate the models on additional argumentation datasets or to adapt them to new domains?  
- Could attention mechanisms or hierarchical models mitigate LSTM-ER’s performance drop on longer sequences?  

Additional comments:
- Reference list is comprehensive.  
- Tagging scheme notation is clear and useful.  
- Supplementary material (hyperparameters, software links, error analysis) would be valuable to review.
