- Paper compares multiple end-to-end neural framings for argumentation mining (parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task, joint extraction) on the Persuasive Essays dataset.  
- Token-level dependency parsing performs poorly, especially for longer documents; sequence tagging (BiLSTM-CRF-CNN) with a complex tag outperforms baselines.  
- Neural sequence tagger and LSTM-ER (on shorter texts) set a new state-of-the-art, beating a strong ILP feature-based system.  

- Contribution: systematic empirical comparison of neural architectures and problem framings, analyzing trade-offs (document length sensitivity).  
- Contribution: novel sequence-tagging formulation (4-tuple tag (b,t,d,s)) that encodes component boundaries, types, and relations into a single label.  
- Contribution: demonstration that feature-free neural models can surpass heavily engineered ILP pipeline systems.  

- Strength: very comprehensive experimental setup evaluating multiple neural paradigms and dependency parsers.  
- Strength: insightful analysis explaining why parsing and LSTM-ER fail on long texts (combinatorial complexity, quadratic candidate growth) with paragraph-vs-essay experiments.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains establishing new state-of-the-art results on the benchmark.  
- Strength: creative tagging formulation that encodes relative relation distance into tags, aiding long-document performance.  

- Weakness: extremely large and potentially sparse label set from the Cartesian-product tag (especially unbounded distance d) with limited discussion of vocabulary size or scalability.  
- Weakness: calling graph-to-tree re-linking "loss-less" is misleading—re-linking claims to a single major claim can alter annotated semantics and may affect evaluation.  
- Weakness: MTL experiments use a weaker base model (STagBL) rather than the stronger STagBLCC, so reported MTL benefits may not generalize to the best architecture.  

- Question: report total label vocabulary size for STagT on PE and how distance d is encoded (unique distances, binning, thresholds?).  
- Question: clarify how re-linked relations (e.g., C->MC1 removed when re-linking to MC2) are handled during training/evaluation and whether gold relations are ignored.  
- Question: did you try constraining LSTM-ER (e.g., distance window) to reduce candidate pairs and better compare to STagT’s implicit distance-awareness?
