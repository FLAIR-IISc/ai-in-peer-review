- Summary: Studies neural end-to-end argumentation mining, comparing token-based dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning on the PE dataset versus a feature-based ILP model; finds neural methods can reach state-of-the-art without manual features or ILP constraints.

- Contribution: Presents the first neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining.

- Contribution: Compares different neural framings for AM (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning).

- Contribution: Evaluates these models on the PE dataset and compares performance with a feature-based ILP model.

- Strength: Comprehensive study exploring multiple neural framings on a benchmark dataset.

- Strength: Detailed analysis of results highlighting model strengths, weaknesses, and implications.

- Strength: Demonstrates neural AM can achieve SOTA performance without manual feature engineering or costly ILP design.

- Weakness: Evaluation is mainly on the PE dataset, limiting claims about generalizability to other AM tasks.

- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of computational resources required by each model for practical deployment.

- Weakness: Some models (e.g., dependency parsing formulation) may not generalize to other AM tasks due to design choices.

- Question: How will you extend this work to other AM tasks and datasets?

- Question: Can you provide details on computational resources for each model and comparisons to the ILP baseline?

- Question: How would results change under different evaluation metrics (e.g., MAP or AUC)?
