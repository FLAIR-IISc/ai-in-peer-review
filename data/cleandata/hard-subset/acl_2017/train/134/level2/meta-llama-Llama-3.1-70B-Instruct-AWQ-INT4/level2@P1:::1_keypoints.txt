- Presents neural end-to-end approaches to argumentation mining: token-based dependency parsing, token-based sequence tagging, and multi-task learning.
- Evaluates models on the Persuasive Essays (PE) dataset and compares against a feature-based ILP model.
- Shows neural models match or outperform the ILP baseline while removing manual feature and constraint engineering.
- Contribution: first neural end-to-end solutions for computational AM that avoid handcrafted features/constraints.
- Contribution: empirical comparison demonstrating neural methods can equal or surpass feature-based ILP.
- Contribution: investigates multi-task learning and finds joint learning of subtasks can improve performance.
- Contribution: shows standard neural sequence tagging is robust, particularly for component detection.
- Strength: novel framing of AM as token-based parsing and sequence tagging, distinct from traditional feature-based work.
- Strength: reports state-of-the-art end-to-end AM results on the PE dataset.
- Strength: thorough evaluation including paragraph vs. essay level analyses and ILP comparison.
- Strength: provides insights on long-range dependencies and multi-task learning effectiveness in AM.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single dataset, raising generalization concerns.
- Weakness: some proposed models (e.g., multi-task setup) are complex and may be hard to implement.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of overfitting risks across models.
- Weakness: absence of a detailed error analysis to characterize model failures.
- Question: how will the approach generalize to other text types (news, social media)?
- Question: request for implementation details of the multi-task setup, hyperparameters, and architecture.
- Question: how will the authors mitigate overfitting, especially in multi-task models?
- Question: can the authors provide a detailed error analysis by model and text characteristics?
