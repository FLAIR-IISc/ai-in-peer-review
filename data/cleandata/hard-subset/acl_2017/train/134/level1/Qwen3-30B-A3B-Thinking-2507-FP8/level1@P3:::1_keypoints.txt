- First comprehensive comparison of neural end-to-end approaches for argumentation mining, showing neural methods remove need for hand-crafted features and ILP constraints.  
- Demonstration that sequence tagging with distance encoding (STagT) significantly outperforms dependency parsing for AM.  
- Finding that multi-task learning with natural subtasks (component detection and relation identification) improves performance.  
- Novel framing of AM as sequence tagging with distance encoding and an effective label encoding (b, t, d, s) that captures long-range dependencies.  
- Thorough empirical comparison of multiple neural architectures (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER) across paragraph and essay levels with insightful model analyses.  
- Meaningful multi-task learning results showing component detection as a particularly beneficial auxiliary task with broader implications.  
- Clear explanation of limitations, e.g., why dependency parsing and LSTM-ER struggle on longer/complex structures.  
- Overstated novelty claim: prior work (Miwa & Bansal 2016) provides a relevant neural end-to-end model not adequately compared.  
- Insufficient analysis of STagT limitations, especially how distance encoding handles components farther apart than seen in training.  
- Inadequate justification/discussion of evaluation metrics (level α matching thresholds and their suitability for AM applications).  
- Lack of comparison to recent sequence labeling advances (e.g., BERT-based models) and more modern architectures.  
- Question: Why wasn’t Miwa & Bansal (2016) included in comparisons and how would results change if it were?  
- Question: How would STagT handle argument components separated by more than the maximum observed distance (e.g., >10)?  
- Question: How will you address the evaluation discrepancy where LSTM-ER uses essay-level info but other neural models train on paragraph-level only?  
- Question: How can your approach be extended to handle extremely long documents without performance degradation?
