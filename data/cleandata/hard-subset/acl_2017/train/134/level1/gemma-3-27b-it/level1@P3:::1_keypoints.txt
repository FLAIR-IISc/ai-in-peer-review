- Contribution: Empirical evaluation shows BiLSTM-based sequence tagging is an effective end-to-end approach for argumentation mining, outperforming a strong ILP baseline.
- Contribution: Token-level dependency parsing for AM performs poorly, providing a negative result that discourages this framing.
- Contribution: Multi-task learning with auxiliary tasks (component detection and relation identification) improves overall performance.
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental comparison across dependency parsing, sequence tagging, LSTM-ER, and MTL setups against an ILP baseline.
- Strength: Clear problem motivation and well-explained modeling choices for end-to-end neural AM.
- Strength: Detailed analysis explaining why dependency parsing fails and why sequence tagging succeeds, including discussion of long-range dependencies and LSTM-ER limits.
- Strength: Use of the well-established Stab & Gurevych (2016) dataset enhances validity and reproducibility.
- Strength: Thorough related work coverage in AM, discourse parsing, and neural sequence modeling.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of alternative or specialized neural dependency parsing architectures that might suit argumentation structures.
- Weakness: Insufficient investigation of the distance encoding scheme’s sensitivity to the chosen maximum distance and its implications.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter optimization is mentioned but lacks specific details (search space, algorithm), hurting reproducibility.
- Weakness: Missing in-depth error analysis categorizing common error types and providing illustrative examples.
- Weakness: Comparisons are mainly to the ILP baseline; more comparisons to other recent neural AM models are needed.
- Question: Please elaborate on the hyperparameter optimization process (search space and optimization algorithm) for each model.
- Question: How sensitive is sequence tagging performance to the maximum distance in the distance encoding scheme, and were different values tested?
- Question: Can you provide concrete examples and frequency counts of common errors for the dependency parser and sequence tagger?
- Question: Discuss the trade-off between modularity and constraints—how might dependency parsing constraints specifically hinder performance?
- Additional: Figure 2 is crucial to understand the distance encoding motivation.
- Additional: The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Additional: The ablation study in Table 2 provides particularly insightful analysis.
