- Compares token-based dependency parsing vs. sequence tagging for end-to-end AM; finds dependency parsing performs poorly while a BiLSTM sequence-tagging model with distance encoding is robust.
- Multi-task learning with natural subtasks further improves performance.
- Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation, including paragraph and essay-level tests and comparison to a strong feature-based ILP baseline.
- Important negative result: token-based dependency parsing is ineffective for end-to-end AM, steering future work away from that framing.
- Establishes a strong baseline with the sequence-tagging model and distance encoding.
- Releases results on the standard Stab & Gurevych (2016) dataset, offering useful benchmarks for the community.
- Individual model components (BiLSTM-CRF, multi-task learning) are not novel; the contribution is mainly empirical/application-focused.
- The distance encoding (coding distances into tag labels) is simplistic and may be arbitrary or limiting.
- The paper lacks an in-depth error analysis, especially regarding long-range dependency failures.
- Question: Why were the specific auxiliary tasks chosen for multi-task learning and were other tasks considered?
- Question: Were alternative relational encodings (e.g., learned relation embeddings) experimented with, and what were the results?
- Question: Can the authors provide more detail on types and patterns of model errors, particularly for long-range dependencies?
