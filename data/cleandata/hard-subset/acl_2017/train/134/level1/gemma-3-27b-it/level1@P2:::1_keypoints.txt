- Paper investigates end-to-end computational argumentation mining framed as token-based dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning.  
- Dependency parsing performs poorly for end-to-end AM.  
- Sequence tagging (BiLSTM-CRF) is robust and achieves strong performance.  
- Multi-task learning (MTL) improves overall performance by leveraging auxiliary tasks.  
- Primary contribution: empirical exploration of neural framings and demonstration that simple sequence tagging is highly effective.  
- Secondary contribution: insightful negative result showing dependency parsing is an ineffective framing for this task.  
- Strength: Empirical results consistently outperform a feature-based ILP baseline (Stab & Gurevych 2016), clearly presented (Table 1).  
- Strength: The negative finding on dependency parsing directs future work away from a seemingly intuitive but unproductive approach.  
- Strength: Demonstrates that a relatively simple BiLSTM-CRF can handle long sequences and avoid overfitting better than complex models.  
- Strength: Shows MTL benefits by exploiting relationships between component detection and relation identification.  
- Weakness: Experiments use only the Stab & Gurevych (2016) dataset, raising concerns about generalizability beyond student essays.  
- Weakness: Distance encoding via tag labels (STagT) discretizes and may fail for very large distances or complex relations; alternatives like attention could help.  
- Weakness: LSTM-ER’s strong paragraph-level results degrade significantly at essay level; causes need deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: Absence of systematic ablation studies leaves the individual contributions of model components unclear.  
- Question: How were potentially unbounded distance values in STagT handled in practice (truncation or regularization)?  
- Question: Were alternative dependency parsing architectures or pretraining strategies tried to salvage parsing performance?  
- Question: Can the authors analyze LSTM-ER’s essay-level errors—are relations misclassified or simply missed compared to STagBLCC?  
- Question: How sensitive are MTL gains to the choice of auxiliary tasks, and were other auxiliary tasks explored?
