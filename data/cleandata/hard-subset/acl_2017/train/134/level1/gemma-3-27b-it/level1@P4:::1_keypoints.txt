- Empirical demonstration that end-to-end neural AM is effective, with sequence tagging (BiLSTM-CRF) as a strong baseline.
- Negative result: framing AM as token-based dependency parsing performs poorly.
- Multi-task learning (auxiliary tasks for components and relations) improves AM performance.
- Use of the Stab and Gurevych (2016) dataset is a useful resource for the community.
- Strength: thorough experiments show a simple BiLSTM-CRF tagging model is robust across settings.
- Strength: the dependency-parsing negative result challenges assumptions and guides future work away from tree-based formulations.
- Strength: multi-task learning demonstrates clear gains, suggesting productive joint modeling of subtasks.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across models, paragraph vs. essay level, and metrics strengthens findings.
- Weakness: individual methods (BiLSTM-CRF, MTL) are not novel—novelty is mainly empirical application and evaluation.
- Weakness: insufficient detailed error analysis across models, especially for relation classification errors.
- Weakness: unclear whether LSTM-ER was adequately tuned for essay-level data; its degradation may be due to suboptimal tuning or sequence-length limitations.
- Weakness: paper omits discussion of computational cost (training and inference), limiting assessment of practicality.
- Question: please provide a detailed error analysis for sequence tagging models, focusing on relation classification error types.
- Question: were different parameter settings or optimization strategies tried for LSTM-ER on essay-level data, and what were the results?
- Question: what are the training and inference times (computational cost) of the best models (STagBLCC and LSTM-ER) on essay-level data?
- Question: how sensitive are MTL gains to the choice of auxiliary tasks, and were other auxiliaries tested?
- Additional: clarify dense notation in Equation (1) for the tag set Y.
- Additional: justify why the chosen auxiliary tasks are considered "natural" with more concrete reasoning.
- Additional: improve figure captions/explanations to make each component clearer.
