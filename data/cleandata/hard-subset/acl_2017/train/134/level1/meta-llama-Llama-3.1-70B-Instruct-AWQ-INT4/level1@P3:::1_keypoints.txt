- Study proposes neural end-to-end approaches to computational argumentation mining (token-based dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning) evaluated on the PE dataset.
- Finds a standard neural sequence tagging model that encodes distance between components performs robustly.
- Shows multi-task learning with natural AM subtasks as auxiliary tasks improves performance.
- Claims novelty as the first neural end-to-end solutions for computational AM.
- Eliminates manual feature engineering and ILP constraint design via the neural end-to-end approach.
- Sequence tagging model demonstrates robustness and generalizability across environments.
- Multi-task learning yields measurable performance gains.
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (PE), raising concerns about generality across AM tasks and domains.
- Potential overfitting risk; paper lacks regularization strategies or early stopping details.
- Missing comparisons to other neural baselines (e.g., attention-based models, graph convolutional networks).
- Requests clarification on how overfitting will be addressed in future work.
- Requests empirical comparisons to other neural architectures (attention, GCNs).
- Suggests evaluating on additional AM datasets with different structures or domains.
- Recommends more robust evaluation practices (cross-validation, multiple datasets) and fuller implementation details (hyperparameters, model architectures).
