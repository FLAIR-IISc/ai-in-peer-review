- First neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining (AM).
- Comparative evaluation of neural approaches: token-based dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning.
- Evaluation on the persuasive essays dataset with comparison to a feature-based ILP model.
- Thorough evaluation highlighting strengths and weaknesses of different neural approaches.
- Novel framing of AM as a multi-task learning problem showing promising results.
- Detailed comparison demonstrating advantages of neural models over a feature-based ILP baseline.
- Use of a robust evaluation metric that considers both component and relation identification.
- Paper is well-organized with clear and concise writing.
- Heavy reliance on existing architectures (BiLSTM-CRF, LSTM-ER) without major methodological innovations.
- Missing detailed error analysis to reveal error types and frequencies.
- No discussion of neural model interpretability, an important aspect for AM.
- Unclear why LSTM-ER performs poorly on relation identification at the essay level.
- Insufficient discussion of the implications and potential applications of the results.
- Request for a detailed error analysis covering error types and frequencies for each approach.
- Ask how the authors will improve interpretability and which techniques they might use.
- Request clarification on causes of LSTM-ER's poor essay-level relation identification and possible fixes.
- Ask how results could be applied in real-world scenarios, e.g., educational settings.
- Query about plans to extend the work to other datasets or domains and how methods could be adapted.
