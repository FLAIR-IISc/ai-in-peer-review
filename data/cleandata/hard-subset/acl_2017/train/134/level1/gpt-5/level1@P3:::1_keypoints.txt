- Contribution: Simple neural sequence taggers with a distance-informed label space set new SOTA on end-to-end AM on PE, outperforming a prior ILP joint model, especially for essay-level relations.
- Contribution: Token-level dependency parsing (even neural) performs substantially worse for end-to-end AM, particularly on longer sequences — a useful negative result.
- Contribution: Multi-task learning with component-only and relation-only auxiliary tasks improves weaker taggers, and decoupling component and relation modeling (e.g., LSTM-ER or MTL) is beneficial.

- Strength: Clear, well-justified comparisons across framings that tie performance patterns to data properties and inductive biases.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains and broad evaluation (paragraph vs essay) using accepted metrics and composite F1 reporting.
- Strength: The negative parsing result is diagnostically valuable and highlights limits of global token-level tree constraints on long documents.
- Strength: Multi-task learning insights are concrete and transferable (auxiliary tasks help; layer assignment is non-trivial).
- Strength: Thoughtful label design encoding inter-component distance and analysis of distance effects on relation accuracy.

- Weakness: Comparability and fairness issues — dependency parsers evaluated only at paragraph level, unclear ILP settings, and potential information advantages not fully clarified.
- Weakness: Label space explosion and dataset-specific assumptions (distance priors, near-tree structure) may limit generalizability beyond PE.
- Weakness: Token-level dependency parsing encoding is misaligned with argument structure and may bias the negative result.
- Weakness: Limited structural validity analysis — no quantification of constraint violations (multiple heads, cycles, invalid type combinations) per model.
- Weakness: LSTM-ER setup lacks standard pruning or candidate-selection adaptations to address O(m^2) relation space at essay level.
- Weakness: No experiments on corpora beyond PE to assess robustness of the proposed label and modeling choices.
