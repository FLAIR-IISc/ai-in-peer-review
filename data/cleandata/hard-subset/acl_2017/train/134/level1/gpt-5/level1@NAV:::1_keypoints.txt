- Summary: Compares four neural end-to-end framings for argumentation mining on PE corpus and finds local sequence tagging (BiLSTM-CRF-CNN with distance-coded labels) outperforms token-level dependency parsing; MTL helps; LSTM-ER is strong on paragraphs but worse on essays; several neural setups match/exceed an ILP pipeline.

- Contribution: Empirically shows distance-coded local sequence tagging yields strong, robust end-to-end AM performance, beating parsers and matching/exceeding ILP baselines.

- Contribution: Provides a careful analysis of model choices across document granularity (paragraph vs essay), identifying when constrained local models or entity–relation architectures are preferable.

- Contribution: Demonstrates multi-task learning with component/relation subtasks consistently improves performance.

- Strength: Thorough, well-motivated comparison with consistent evaluation and significance testing; STagBLCC substantially improves C-F1 and R-F1 at paragraph level.

- Strength: Convincing diagnosis of long-range dependency issues via distance encoding and analysis of distance distributions and success probabilities.

- Strength: Practical, reproducible finding that removing handcrafted features/ILP can retain or improve AM performance across divergent annotation schemes.

- Strength: Useful granularity study that teases apart sequence-length effects and relation combinatorics, clarifying LSTM-ER’s strengths and limits.

- Strength: Informative MTL ablations showing auxiliary component labeling helps and providing useful architectural placement insights.

- Weakness: Methodological novelty is moderate—mostly adaptations of existing architectures; main novelty is in task framing and labeling.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a single domain (PE essays), reducing external validity and lacking cross-dataset transfer tests.

- Weakness: Baseline fairness/scope issues: ILP uses essay/paragraph constraints while some neural models are paragraph-only; newer seq2seq/pointer baselines cited but not compared.

- Weakness: Tag-set explosion and sparsity from distance×stance labeling are under-analyzed (OOV distances, rare labels); no discussion of decoding guarantees for global consistency.

- Weakness: Limited reporting on stability—no quantified variance across random seeds or hyperparameter sensitivity despite apparent parametrization variance.

- Question: What is the effective tag inventory (distance cutoffs) and how are unseen distances handled at test time?

- Question: What is the performance variance across random seeds for STagBLCC and MTL setups?

- Question: Can the distance-coded tagger generalize to corpora with non-tree, many-to-many links?

- Question: Would lightweight global consistency enforcement (constrained decoding or post-hoc ILP) improve R-F1 without heavy engineering?
