- Summary: Compares four end-to-end neural framings for argumentation mining on the PE dataset and finds sequence tagging (BiLSTM-CRF-CNN with distance-augmented labels) and LSTM-ER best depending on input granularity, outperforming an ILP baseline.

- Contribution 1: Empirical study showing distance-augmented neural sequence tagging is a strong end-to-end AM solution and that token-level dependency parsing underperforms on this dataset.

- Contribution 2: Analysis of input granularity trade-offs: LSTM-ER excels on paragraphs but degrades on essays due to relation combinatorics; MTL yields consistent gains.

- Contribution 3: Practical labeling scheme encoding long-range dependencies via component-distance within token tags, enabling local models to capture relations without explicit global constraints.

- Strength: Thorough, controlled comparison across multiple parsers and models with consistent metrics and significance testing.

- Strength: Clear, technically motivated task formulations and analyses explaining parser and model behaviors with supporting plots.

- Strength: Robust results that match/exceed prior ILP-based systems while reducing hand-crafted features and constraints.

- Strength: Insightful multi-task learning findings showing auxiliary component/relation tasks improve tagging, with practical guidance on MTL design.

- Strength: Evaluation explicitly contrasts paragraph vs. essay settings, quantifying granularity impacts.

- Weakness: Single-dataset focus (PE) limits generalization to other AM corpora with different discourse structures or cross-paragraph relations.

- Weakness: Distance-augmented tag space may cause sparsity; paper lacks details on effective label cardinality, capping/bucketing, and ablations.

- Weakness: Dependency parsing framing may be suboptimally instantiated (token-level only); component-level parsing alternatives were not evaluated.

- Weakness: Key implementation details (embeddings, hyperparameters, optimization, scheduled sampling, MTL selection) are deferred to supplementary material, hurting reproducibility.

- Weakness: Does not explore light-weight constrained decoding or ablations measuring the benefit of explicit structural constraints versus implicit learning.

- Question: What was the maximum observed distance |d| and how many distinct labels were used; were distances capped or bucketed and how does performance vary with coarser bins?

- Question: How are stance labels mapped across component types and is there confusion between Supp/Att and For/Ag in different component pairs?

- Question: How were task-to-layer assignments for MTL selected and was a CRF/top-layer dependency tried for the MTL tagger?

- Question: Did you try a two-stage approach (component detection then component-level neural parser/graph model) and how does it compare to STag and LSTM-ER?

- Question: What are training/inference times and memory footprints across models, and how do they scale with longer documents or more entities?

- Question: Please detail the exact pairing and multiple-comparison handling for the Wilcoxon statistical tests.

- Overall: Technically sound, empirically strong and practically useful within scope, but would benefit from broader datasets, more methodological detail, and evaluations of component-level relation modeling.
