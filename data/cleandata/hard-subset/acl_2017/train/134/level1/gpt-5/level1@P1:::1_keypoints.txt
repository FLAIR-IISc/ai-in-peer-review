- Summary: Empirical comparison of four end-to-end neural framings for argumentation mining on the Persuasive Essays dataset, finding sequence tagging and MTL strong, parsing weak, and LSTM-ER best on paragraphs but worse on long essays.

- Contribution 1: Simple BiLSTM sequence tagging with distance-augmented labels outperforms token-level dependency parsing and a strong ILP pipeline across paragraph/essay settings.

- Contribution 2: Multi-task learning with component-only and relation-only auxiliary tasks improves end-to-end tagging; decoupling entities and relations (LSTM-ER) helps when entities are few.

- Contribution 3: Provides a careful negative result showing token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for end-to-end AM and explains when global tree structure is counterproductive.

- Strength: Thorough comparative study across parsers, taggers, MTL, LSTM-ER, and ILP baseline with clear quantitative takeaways (e.g., STagBLCC and LSTM-ER lead on paragraphs).

- Strength: Clear analysis of document-length effects and search-space growth explaining why constrained tagging outperforms relation-heavy models on essays.

- Strength: Demonstrates practical end-to-end neural modeling that matches/exceeds ILP-based feature-rich systems without hand-crafted features or constraints.

- Strength: Sensible structural exploitation via distance-encoded labels and auxiliary tasks; shows component-only auxiliary task is particularly helpful.

- Strength: Provides diagnostic insights and negative results for dependency parsing, attributing failures to search complexity and span-boundary issues.

- Weakness: Results depend on a single domain-specific dataset with label choices (stance types, distance caps, single-head constraint) that may not generalize to other AM corpora.

- Weakness: Comparisons are uneven/incomplete (some parsers only on paragraphs, MTL base weaker than BLCC, ILP results not clearly separated), limiting balance of claims.

- Weakness: Decoding/post-processing details for converting token tags into coherent spans and single-head relations are under-specified.

- Weakness: Composite tagset grows with distances and stances, risking data sparsity; no ablation on distance capping or alternative relation encodings is provided.

- Weakness: Limited error analysis on boundary/type errors and cross-sentence relation failures; finer-grained diagnostics are missing.

- Question: How are sequence tags decoded into coherent components and relations, and how are conflicts/consistency enforced?

- Question: Was the set of distance values in labels capped during training/inference, and how sensitive are results to that cap?

- Question: Can essay-level results be reported for all dependency parsers and paragraph/essay results for the ILP baseline for fair comparison?

- Question: Have you tried MTL with the stronger BLCC backbone (CRF + char-CNN) rather than BL, and would gains persist?

- Question: How sensitive are models to PE corpus assumptions (cross-paragraph links, multiple heads), and can the tagging formulation handle many-to-many relations?

- Question: For LSTM-ER, did scheduled sampling and entity pretraining follow Miwa & Bansal exactly, and could removing sentence-level TreeLSTM improve essay-level relations?
