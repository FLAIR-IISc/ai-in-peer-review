- Paper presents a joint neural approach to end-to-end argumentation mining.  
- Explores multiple approaches: dependency parsing (several parsers), sequence labeling, multi-task learning (on seq. labeling), LSTM-ER (entity/relation neural model), and ILP-based baselines.  
- Evaluation uses F1 for concepts and relations.  
- Dependency-based solutions perform poorly.  
- Sequence labeling solutions are effective.  
- The out-of-the-box LSTM-ER model performs very well, especially at paragraph level.  
- Sequence labeling and LSTM-ER both outperform the ILP state-of-the-art.  
- Multi-task learning substantially improves sequence labeling, with the claim task contributing more than the relation task.  
- The supplement is comprehensive, detailing training and hyper-parameter optimization.  
- Concern: possible data leakage if train and test essays share topics/arguments, potentially inflating performance.  
- Concern: one of the best models (LSTM-ER) is essentially an out-of-the-box application of prior work.  
- Clarify how a tree output can be used to reconstruct the full graph (lines 222–226).  
- Suggest reordering sections for clarity (swap seq. tagging and dependency-based descriptions; lines 443–444).  
- Clarify the meaning of “de-couples them but jointly models them” and the claimed decoupling of relation vs entity information (lines 455, 477–479).  
- Question: Are F1 scores across paragraph and essay settings comparable, and do paragraph models miss cross-paragraph relations?
