- Proposes neural end-to-end argumentation mining, comparing sequence labeling and dependency parsing, including a multitask sequence-labeling variant.  
- Paper is well-written and methods/experimental results are clearly presented.  
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 training essays, 80 test), development set size unspecified, raising high risk of overfitting for large BiLSTM models.  
- Paper lacks analysis of overfitting (e.g., training vs. development loss curves).  
- Results reported from a single run; model stability is questionable—need mean and standard deviation of F1 over multiple runs.  
- Statistical significance tests are missing to confirm reported improvements.  
- Regularization is not discussed in detail; only a brief supplement mention—need explicit regularization methods and their impact.  
- Actionable: report development set size and move pre-trained embedding details and error analysis into the main paper.  
- Actionable: include inter-annotator agreement from the original corpus to contextualize performance and provide a soft upper bound.  
- Actionable: consider more advanced hyper-parameter optimization (e.g., Bayesian optimization) in future work.  
- Presentation fix: revise Figure 1 for legibility in black-and-white prints.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer raised score to 4 but insists the camera-ready include F1 ranges/mean+variance for all settings to address stability concerns, noting large variances in both baseline and authors' model.
