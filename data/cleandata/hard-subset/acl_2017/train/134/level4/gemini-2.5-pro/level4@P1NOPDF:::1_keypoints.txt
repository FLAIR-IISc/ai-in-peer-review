- Strength: Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and presents interesting, valuable methods and results.  
- Proposal: Introduces a neural end-to-end argumentation mining system using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus multitask learning.  
- Contribution: Avoids ILP, heavy feature engineering, and pipeline error propagation by joint learning of subtasks.  
- Clarity: Methods are generally well-explained, with a few minor missing details.  
- Main weakness: Experimental evaluation is weak due to a very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test) and undeclared development set size.  
- Data concern: Training likely only a few thousand annotated tags vs. typical large-scale sequence-labeling corpora, raising generalization worries.  
- Overfitting analysis missing: No training/dev loss curves or analysis to detect overfitting.  
- Stability concern: No reporting of mean/std over multiple runs or significance testing, making improvements hard to trust.  
- Regularization: Insufficient discussion of explicit regularization methods (only brief mention in supplementary).  
- Suggested improvements: Use better hyperparameter search (e.g., Bayesian optimization); move pretrained-embedding details and error analysis into main paper; include inter-annotator agreement; make Figure 1 black-and-white friendly.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer raised score to 4 but requests inclusion of F1-score ranges, means, and variances due to concerns about high variance (e.g., Kiperwasser et al. setting).
