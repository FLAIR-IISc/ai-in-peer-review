- Paper is clearly written, methods are novel, and reported results are compelling.
- Evaluation methodology and reliability of results raise concerns.
- Proposes neural end-to-end argumentation mining via sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus multitask learning for sequence labeling.
- Motivation clear: avoids ILP/handcrafted features and reduces pipeline error propagation through joint learning.
- Experiments are substantial and comparisons are appropriate.
- Major concern: small dataset (≈320 train, 80 test essays; dev size unspecified) risks overfitting given high-capacity BiLSTM models.
- Need analysis of overfitting (e.g., training and development loss curves over epochs).
- Model stability is questionable; results may be sensitive to random initialization—report mean and std over multiple runs.
- Require statistical significance testing to validate reported improvements.
- Regularization is insufficiently discussed and only briefly noted in the supplement; needs fuller treatment.
- Suggest using systematic hyper-parameter optimization (e.g., Bayesian optimization).
- Move information on pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplement into the main paper.
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores to provide performance ceiling context.
- Improve Figure 1 for black-and-white printing (patterns or grayscale).
- Reviewer raised recommendation to 4 and requests final version to report F1-score ranges and mean/variance for all experimental settings due to ongoing concerns about stability.
