- Paper proposes end-to-end neural argumentation mining using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, with single-task and multitask sequence models.  
- Motivation: avoid manual feature engineering, complex ILP constraints, and pipeline error propagation.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and presents interesting models with substantial experiments.  
- Major concern: very small training dataset (~320 essays) vs high-capacity BiLSTM models risks severe overfitting.  
- Development set size is not specified, a crucial missing detail for reproducibility and evaluation.  
- Authors should analyze overfitting (e.g., plot training vs development loss curves).  
- Result stability is questionable; single-run scores may be unrepresentative on small data.  
- Recommend reporting mean and standard deviation of F1 over multiple runs with different random seeds.  
- Statistical significance testing is required to validate improvements over baselines.  
- Paper lacks comprehensive discussion of regularization for the proposed high-capacity models.  
- Suggest using more advanced hyper-parameter optimization (e.g., Bayesian optimization).  
- Move key supplementary details (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) into the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement (IAA) from the dataset paper to contextualize results.  
- Improve figure accessibility for black-and-white printing (use patterns/markers, not just color).  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer raised score but still requires final version to report mean/variance for all settings and analyze high-variance cases (e.g., Kiperwasser baseline and proposed model F1 range [0.56, 0.61]).
