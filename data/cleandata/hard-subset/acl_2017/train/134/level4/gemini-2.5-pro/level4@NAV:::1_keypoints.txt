- Paper strengths: clear presentation and compelling methods/results.
- Presents end-to-end neural framework for argumentation mining using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus multitask learning.
- Method motivated as avoiding ILP/manual feature engineering and reducing pipeline error propagation.
- Experiments are comprehensive and comparisons appropriate.
- Primary concern: very small dataset (~320 train, 80 test) relative to high-capacity BiLSTM models.
- Development set size is not specified, hindering assessment of training validity.
- Lack of overfitting analysis; training and development loss curves should be provided.
- Model stability is questionable; report mean and standard deviation over multiple runs with different initializations.
- Statistical significance tests are missing and needed to assess result reliability.
- Regularization details are insufficient in the main paper and require proper treatment.
- Recommend using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning instead of current approach.
- Move details on pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize task difficulty and headroom.
- Improve Figure 1 readability for black-and-white prints (use distinct patterns/colors).
- After revision, include F1-score ranges and mean/variance for settings; address concerns about large variance (e.g., Kiperwasser).
