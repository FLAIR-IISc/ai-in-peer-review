- Proposal: end-to-end neural system for argumentation mining framed as sequence labeling or dependency parsing, including a multitask sequence-labeling variant.  
- Contribution: moves away from heavy feature engineering and ILP, jointly learns subtasks to avoid pipeline error propagation.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, motivated, models are interesting, experiments are substantial, comparisons appropriate, and results promising.  
- Main concern: dataset is very small (~320 training essays, 80 test); development set size not reported, raising data-scarcity issues.  
- Capacity mismatch: high-capacity BiLSTM-based models (BLC/BLCC) may be inappropriate given the limited data.  
- Overfitting worry: no analysis (e.g., training/dev loss curves) or comprehensive discussion of regularization to show models aren’t memorizing.  
- Stability/reliability: results from single runs are insufficient—need mean and variance (or ranges) of F1 over multiple random initializations and statistical significance tests.  
- Post-rebuttal action: reviewer raised recommendation but asks authors to include model stability reporting (F1 ranges/mean±std) in the final paper.  
- IAA suggestion: report inter-annotator agreement from the original dataset to contextualize system performance.  
- Manuscript organization: move key details (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) from supplementary material into the main paper.  
- Hyperparameter tuning: consider more advanced methods (e.g., Bayesian optimization) to improve model performance.  
- Figure clarity: revise Figure 1 to be interpretable in black-and-white (use patterns/line styles in addition to colors).
