- Manuscript is well-structured and clearly presented.
- Methodologies and results are intriguing and demonstrate a thorough approach.
- Proposes end-to-end argumentation mining via sequence labeling and dependency parsing and explores multitask learning.
- Model removes need for ILP and manual feature/constraint engineering and jointly learns subtasks to avoid pipeline error propagation.
- Some methodological details are missing but overall explanations are adequate.
- Experimental design and comparisons are substantial and results are noteworthy.
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 train, ~80 test) and unspecified development set size.
- Large-capacity (Bi)LSTM models may be undertrained given the limited number of tags.
- Manuscript lacks analysis of overfitting; should include training/development loss curves.
- Model stability is questionable; should report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and perform statistical significance tests.
- Regularization is insufficiently discussed; more attention to regularization methods is needed.
- Suggest using Bayesian optimization for hyper-parameter tuning instead of current method.
- Move pre-trained word-embedding details and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance and room for improvement.
- Improve figure 1 for black-and-white printing (use distinguishable colors/patterns).
- After author response, reviewer raised score to 4 and recommends reporting F1-score ranges and mean/variance; remains concerned about model stability and high variance in the Kiperwasser setting.
