- Paper explores multiple neural formulations for argumentation mining: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task sequence labeling, LSTM-ER, and ILP-based models.
- Evaluation uses F1 scores on concepts and relations to compare approaches.
- Dependency-parsing-based solutions perform poorly compared to other methods.
- Sequence-labeling models achieve better performance than dependency-based approaches.
- The off-the-shelf LSTM-ER model shows exceptional performance, especially at the paragraph level.
- Both sequence-labeling and LSTM-ER models outperform the ILP-based state-of-the-art approach.
- Multi-task learning substantially improves sequence-labeling models, with the claim task contributing more than the relation task.
- The paper provides a detailed supplement on training procedures and hyper-parameter tuning.
- Major concern: possible dataset leakage because training and test essays may cover the same topics, potentially inflating performance estimates.
- Major concern: one top-performing model (LSTM-ER) is mainly an out-of-the-box application of prior work, raising questions about novelty.
- Minor issue: explanation of reconstructing the full graph from a tree output (lines 222–226) is unclear and needs clarification.
- Minor suggestion: the paper would be easier to follow if the sequence-tagging and dependency-based sections were reordered.
- Minor confusion: statements claiming the model “de-couples” relation from entity information while jointly modeling them are contradictory and should be clarified (lines 455, 477–479).
- Minor question: unclear whether paragraph- and essay-level F1 scores are directly comparable, since paragraph models may miss cross-paragraph relations.
