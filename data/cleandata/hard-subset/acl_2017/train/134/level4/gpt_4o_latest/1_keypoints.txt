- Strength: Manuscript well-structured, clearly written, with engaging and thought-provoking methods and results.
- Contribution: Presents end-to-end argumentation mining using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus a multitask learning variant.
- Novelty: Removes manual ILP/feature engineering and jointly learns subtasks to reduce pipeline error propagation.
- Method clarity: Methods generally well-explained, though a few details are missing.
- Main concern: Extremely small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test; dev size unspecified) relative to high-capacity (Bi)LSTM models.
- Data vs. model capacity: Training tags likely only a few thousand, raising doubt whether model parameters are adequately learned.
- Overfitting: Paper does not analyze or report training/dev loss curves or explicitly address overfitting risk.
- Stability: Results lack mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and no statistical significance testing to confirm robustness.
- Regularization: Insufficient discussion of regularization strategies beyond a brief mention for LSTM-ER; needs more thorough treatment.
- Hyperparameter tuning: Recommend using Bayesian optimization instead of the current tuning approach.
- Presentation: Move details about pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplementary materials into the main paper.
- Annotation quality: Include inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset paper to contextualize performance and improvement potential.
- Visualization: Improve Figure 1 with distinct colors for clarity, especially in black-and-white prints.
- Reproducibility: Include F1-score ranges, means, and variances for different settings (reported recommendation update asks for these), citing instability in settings like Kiperwasser (wide F1 ranges).
