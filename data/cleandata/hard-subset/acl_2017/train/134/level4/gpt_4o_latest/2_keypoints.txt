- Paper proposes a joint neural framework for end-to-end argumentation mining and evaluates multiple modeling approaches.  
- Evaluated approaches: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multitask learning (on sequence labeling), LSTM-ER entity/relation model, and ILP-based state-of-the-art.  
- Evaluation uses F1 scores for concepts and relations across paragraph- and essay-level settings.  
- Dependency-based methods perform poorly/offer limited effectiveness.  
- Sequence labeling approaches perform well and are competitive.  
- LSTM-ER achieves strong results, especially at the paragraph level.  
- Sequence labeling and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP-based model.  
- Multitask learning significantly improves sequence labeling, with the claim task contributing more than the relation task.  
- Paper includes a detailed supplementary section covering training, hyperparameter tuning, and technical details.  
- Major concern: potential dataset leakage if training/test essays share topics or reused arguments, possibly inflating performance estimates.  
- Major concern: top-performing LSTM-ER is largely an out-of-the-box application of a pre-existing model.  
- Minor issue: unclear claim about reconstructing full graph from a tree output (Lines 222–226) — needs clarification.  
- Minor issue: confusing presentation order in Lines 443–444; recommend reordering for readability.  
- Minor issue: unclear phrasing about “de-couples” vs “jointly modeling” tasks (Line 455) and lack of explanation.  
- Minor issue: confusing statement about decoupling relation and entity information (Lines 477–479) — needs clearer explanation.  
- Minor question: are paragraph- and essay-level F1 scores directly comparable for relations, given paragraph models may miss cross-paragraph relations?
