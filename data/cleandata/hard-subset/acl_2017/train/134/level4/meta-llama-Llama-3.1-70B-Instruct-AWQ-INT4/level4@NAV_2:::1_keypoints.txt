- Summary: Proposes neural end-to-end approaches to argumentation mining using token-based dependency parsing, token-based sequence tagging, and multi-task learning; evaluated on persuasive essays against a feature-based ILP model.

- Contribution 1: Introduces the first neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining.

- Contribution 2: Shows several neural models outperform the state-of-the-art joint ILP model.

- Contribution 3: Finds that framing AM as token-based dependency parsing is ineffective.

- Contribution 4: Demonstrates that a standard neural sequence tagging model is robust across settings.

- Contribution 5: Shows multi-task learning improves performance.

- Strength 1: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Strength 2: Methods and results are interesting and substantial.

- Strength 3: Motivation for the proposed model is clearly explained.

- Strength 4: Experiments are well-designed and comparisons are appropriate.

- Weakness 1: Results may be unreliable due to small dataset and high-capacity RNNs.

- Weakness 2: No analysis of overfitting or presentation of training/development loss curves.

- Weakness 3: Missing mean and standard deviation from multiple runs and no statistical significance testing.

- Weakness 4: No discussion of regularization methods despite small dataset.

- Weakness 5: Hyper-parameter optimization method is inadequate; Bayesian optimization recommended.

- Question 1: Provide details on the development set size and how it was used.

- Question 2: Analyze overfitting and supply training and development loss curves.

- Question 3: Report mean and standard deviation across runs and include significance tests.

- Question 4: Describe the regularization methods used and how they mitigate overfitting.

- Question 5: Consider replacing the current hyper-parameter optimization with Bayesian optimization.
