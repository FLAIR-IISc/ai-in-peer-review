- Paper proposes joint neural approaches to argumentation mining: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task sequence labeling, LSTM-ER (out-of-the-box), and ILP baselines, evaluated with F1 on concepts and relations.
- Experimental finding: dependency-based methods perform poorly while sequence labeling methods are effective.
- The LSTM-ER model achieves excellent results, especially at the paragraph level.
- Both sequence labeling and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP-based state-of-the-art approach.
- Authors supply a comprehensive supplement describing training procedures and hyperparameter optimization.
- Multi-task learning significantly improves sequence labeling models, with the claim task helping more than the relation task.
- Overall assessment: thorough investigation of neural end-to-end argumentation mining; work merits publication despite using an out-of-the-box LSTM-ER.
- Major concern: possible dataset leak because training and test essays may share topics, risking overly optimistic performance estimates and requiring discussion.
- Major concern: one top-performing model (LSTM-ER) is an out-of-the-box application from prior work, which should be acknowledged.
