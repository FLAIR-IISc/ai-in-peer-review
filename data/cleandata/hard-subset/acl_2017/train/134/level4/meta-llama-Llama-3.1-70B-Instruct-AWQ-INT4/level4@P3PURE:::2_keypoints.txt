- Paper proposes joint neural approaches to argumentation mining: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task sequence labeling, off-the-shelf LSTM-ER, and ILP-based comparison.  
- Evaluation is based on F1 scores over concepts and relations.  
- Dependency-based solutions perform poorly.  
- Sequence labeling approaches are effective.  
- Out-of-the-box LSTM-ER achieves excellent performance, especially at the paragraph level.  
- Sequence labeling and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP baseline.  
- Supplement provides detailed training and hyperparameter optimization information.  
- Multi-task learning substantially improves sequence labeling models, with the claim task helping more than the relation task.  
- Major concern: possible train/test topic overlap in essays could cause information leakage and overly optimistic results; this needs discussion.  
- Major concern: LSTM-ER is essentially an off-the-shelf model from prior work, raising novelty questions despite useful experimental insights.  
- Minor: explanation about reconstructing the full graph from a tree (lines 222–226) is unclear.  
- Minor: section ordering (lines 443–444) would be clearer if sequence tagging preceded dependency-based methods.  
- Minor: phrase “it de-couples them but jointly models them” (line 455) is unclear and not found in referenced work.  
- Minor: explanation of decoupling relation and entity information (lines 477–479) is confusing; reviewer suggests framing as edges vs. labels on edges.  
- Minor: unclear whether paragraph- and essay-level F1 scores are comparable, since paragraph models may miss cross-paragraph relations.
