- Proposes neural end-to-end approaches to computational argumentation mining framed as token-based dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning.  
- Claims to be the first neural end-to-end solutions for computational AM and compares different neural formulations.  
- Evaluates approaches on a persuasive essays dataset and compares against a feature-based ILP model.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation; methods and results are interesting and comparisons substantial.  
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 training, ~80 test essays) versus high-capacity (Bi)LSTM models risking overfitting.  
- Training set likely contains only a few thousand tags, possibly insufficient to reliably train model parameters.  
- Paper does not analyze overfitting nor report training/development loss curves.  
- No mention of regularization methods, which is problematic given dataset size.  
- Asks authors how they addressed overfitting and what regularization methods were used.  
- Requests training and development loss values for transparency.  
- Requests inter-annotator agreement scores for the dataset.  
- Suggests clarifying hyperparameter selection and considering Bayesian optimization for tuning.
