- Strength: Paper is well-written, clear, and presents interesting methods and results.
- Strength: Clear motivation for an end-to-end neural argumentation mining model that avoids manual feature engineering and ILP constraints.
- Strength: Methods (sequence labeling, dependency parsing, multitask learning) are clearly explained except for a few minor missing details.
- Strength: Experiments are substantial and comparisons are performed properly.
- Weakness: Training dataset is very small (~320 training essays, ~80 test essays) and development set size is not reported.
- Concern: Large-capacity (Bi)LSTM models may be overfitting given the small number of tags versus typical sequence-labeling datasets.
- Suggestion: Report training and development loss curves (per update/epoch) to analyze overfitting.
- Suggestion: Provide mean and standard deviation over multiple runs with different random initializations to assess model stability.
- Suggestion: Perform statistical significance testing to verify that improvements are not due to chance.
- Weakness: Regularization methods are insufficiently discussed and appear inadequate given dataset size; more regularization details are needed.
- Suggestion: Improve hyperparameter search (e.g., Bayesian optimization) instead of current method.
- Suggestion: Move key information (pre-trained word embeddings, error analysis) from supplementary material into the main paper.
- Suggestion: Report inter-annotator agreement scores from dataset paper to contextualize system performance and headroom.
- Minor: Improve Figure 1 coloring to be readable in black-and-white prints.
- Edit request followed up: Include F1-score ranges and report mean and variance for different settings (e.g., Kiperwasser) to show stability and facilitate replication.
