- Paper is well-written, easy to understand, and presents interesting methods and results.  
- Proposes an end-to-end argumentation mining system using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus multitask learning.  
- Model avoids manual ILP constraints and manual feature engineering by joint learning of subtasks.  
- Methods are clearly explained overall, but a few implementation details are missing.  
- Experiments are substantial and comparisons are appropriate.  
- Dataset is very small (~320 training, ~80 testing essays) with unspecified development size, raising training concerns.  
- High-capacity (Bi)LSTM models may overfit given the small dataset; paper lacks overfitting analysis.  
- Authors should provide training and development loss curves and analyze overfitting.  
- Model stability is questionable; report mean and standard deviation over multiple runs and include statistical significance tests.  
- Regularization is not adequately discussed; supplementary material only briefly mentions it for one model.  
- Recommend using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning instead of current method.  
- Move information on pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance and headroom.  
- Improve Figure 1 for black-and-white prints (e.g., use different colors/styles).  
- Report F1-score ranges, means, variances, and analyze high variance in Kiperwasser setting; reviewer raised score to 4 after responses.
