- Proposes joint neural approaches to argumentation mining: dependency parsing (various parsers), sequence labeling, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER, and ILP baselines; evaluated with F1 on concepts and relations.
- Dependency-based solutions perform poorly while sequence labeling approaches are effective.
- Out-of-the-box LSTM-ER achieves high performance, particularly at the paragraph level.
- Both sequence labeling and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP-based state-of-the-art.
- Comprehensive supplement detailing training procedures and hyper-parameter optimization is provided.
- Multi-task learning significantly improves sequence labeling, with the claim task contributing more than the relation task.
- Major concern: potential dataset leakage — training and test essays may share topics, possibly inflating performance.
- Major concern: top-performing LSTM-ER is largely an out-of-the-box application, raising novelty questions despite valuable experimental insights.
