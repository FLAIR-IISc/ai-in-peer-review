- Paper is well-written, easy to understand, and presents interesting methods and results.  
- Proposes end-to-end argumentation mining using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus multitask learning.  
- Method avoids manual feature engineering and ILP constraint design, and joint learning reduces pipeline error propagation.  
- Methods are clearly explained except for a few missing details.  
- Experiments are substantial and comparisons are appropriate; results are interesting.  
- Main concern: very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test; dev set not reported) relative to model capacity.  
- Large-capacity (Bi)LSTM models likely prone to overfitting given few thousand tags versus typical large sequence-labeling datasets.  
- Paper lacks overfitting analysis; request to show training and development loss curves.  
- Model stability is uncertain; need mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and statistical significance tests.  
- Regularization is not discussed sufficiently; more attention and details required (supplement only briefly mentions LSTM-ER).  
- Suggest using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter search instead of current method.  
- Move details on pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset paper to indicate performance bounds and room for improvement.  
- Improve Figure 1 for black-and-white printing (use colors or distinct styles).  
- After author response, reviewer increased score but requests inclusion of F1-score ranges and reporting of mean/variance for settings, and analysis of high-variance settings (e.g., Kiperwasser 0.56–0.61).
