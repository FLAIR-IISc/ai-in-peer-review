- Summary: Paper studies neural end-to-end approaches to argumentation mining, comparing dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER, evaluated by concept and relation F1 scores.

- Contribution: First neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining.

- Contribution: Comparative evaluation of framings including dependency parsing, sequence labeling, and multi-task learning.

- Contribution: Finds sequence labeling effective while dependency-based solutions perform poorly.

- Contribution: Shows LSTM-ER performs very well, particularly at paragraph level.

- Contribution: Demonstrates sequence labeling improvements via a multi-task approach.

- Strength: Thorough investigation of neural end-to-end AM approaches.

- Strength: Comprehensive supplementary material with training and hyperparameter details.

- Strength: Empirical evidence that multi-task learning improves sequence labeling models.

- Weakness: Potential information leakage in dataset due to overlapping essay topics between train and test sets.

- Weakness: LSTM-ER may be merely an out-of-the-box application from prior work, limiting novelty.

- Question: Please discuss the dataset information leakage issue and its implications for results.

- Question: How do you respond to the concern that LSTM-ER is not a significant novel contribution?

- Question: Clarify what is meant by "de-coupling" when jointly modeling entities and relations.

- Question: Are F1 scores from paragraph and essay settings comparable, especially for relation tasks?
