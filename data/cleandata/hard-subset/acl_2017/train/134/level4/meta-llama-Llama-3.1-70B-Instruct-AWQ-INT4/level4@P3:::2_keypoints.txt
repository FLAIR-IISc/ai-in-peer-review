- Paper frames end-to-end argumentation mining as token-based dependency parsing, token-based sequence tagging, and a multi-task learning setup, and evaluates those plus LSTM-ER against a feature-based ILP model.
- Contribution: first neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining.
- Contribution: several neural approaches outperform the state-of-the-art joint ILP model.
- Contribution: a neural sequence tagging model with encoded distance information is robust across settings.
- Contribution: multi-task learning with natural auxiliary subtasks improves performance.
- Strength: comprehensive investigation of neural approaches to end-to-end AM.
- Strength: multiple approaches evaluated using a robust evaluation metric.
- Strength: provides insights into strengths and weaknesses of different AM approaches.
- Strength: demonstrates effectiveness of multi-task learning for this task.
- Weakness: potential dataset issue—train and test essays may share topics, causing information leakage and optimistic results.
- Weakness: one top-performing model (LSTM-ER) is largely an out-of-the-box application from prior work, limiting novelty.
- Minor remark: need clearer explanation of reconstructing the full graph from a tree output.
- Minor remark: section ordering could be improved for readability.
- Minor remark: clarify what is meant by decoupling entity and relation information while jointly modeling them.
- Minor remark: paragraph and essay F1 scores (especially for relations) may not be directly comparable due to missed cross-paragraph relations.
- Question: discuss dataset issues and their impact on performance estimates.
- Question: detail how LSTM-ER was applied and what adaptations were made for AM.
- Question: clarify differences between models and how they decouple entity vs. relation information.
- Question: provide more insight into model performance on cross-paragraph relations.
