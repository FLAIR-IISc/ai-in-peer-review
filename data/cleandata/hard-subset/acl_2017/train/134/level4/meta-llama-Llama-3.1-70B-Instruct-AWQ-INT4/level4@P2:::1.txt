**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a system for end-to-end argumentation mining using neural networks. The authors model the problem using two approaches: sequence labeling and dependency parsing. The paper also includes the results of experimenting with a multitask learning setting for the sequence labeling approach.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper presents the first neural end-to-end solutions to computational argumentation mining.
2. The authors show that several of their proposed methods perform better than the state-of-the-art joint ILP model.
3. The paper demonstrates that a framing of argumentation mining as a token-based dependency parsing problem is ineffective, while a standard neural sequence tagging model performs robustly in different environments.

**Strengths**

1. The paper is well-written and easy to understand.
2. The methods and results are interesting and substantial.
3. The authors clearly explain the motivation behind the proposed model and provide a thorough comparison with existing methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. The evaluation and the obtained results might be problematic due to the small size of the dataset and the large capacity of the used (Bi)LSTM-based recurrent neural networks.
2. The paper does not analyze the overfitting problem and does not provide training and development "loss" values during training.
3. The authors do not include mean and standard deviation of multiple runs, which makes it hard to evaluate the stability of the models and the reliability of results.
4. The paper does not mention regularization methods, which is a crucial aspect given the small size of the dataset.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Please provide more information about the development set size and the number of tags in the training data.
2. Can you include the training and development "loss" values during training to analyze the overfitting problem?
3. How do you plan to address the regularization methods given the small size of the dataset?
4. Can you include mean and standard deviation of multiple runs to evaluate the stability of the models and the reliability of results?
5. Please consider using Bayesian optimization methods instead of the current hyper-parameter optimization method.
6. Can you move the information about pre-trained word embeddings and the error analysis from the supplementary material to the paper?
7. Please include some inter-annotator agreement scores to provide insight about the performance of the systems and the available room for improvement.
8. Can you illustrate figure 1 with different colors to make the quality better for black and white prints?
