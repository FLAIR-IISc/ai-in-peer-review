- Summary: Proposes neural end-to-end argumentation mining using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, with multitask learning experiments for the sequence labeling approach.
- Contribution 1: First neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining.
- Contribution 2: Several proposed methods outperform the prior joint ILP state-of-the-art.
- Contribution 3: Token-based dependency parsing framing is ineffective; standard neural sequence tagging is robust across settings.
- Strength 1: Well-written and easy to understand.
- Strength 2: Methods and results are interesting and substantial.
- Strength 3: Clear motivation and thorough comparison with existing methods.
- Weakness 1: Small dataset vs. large (Bi)LSTM model capacity raises concerns about evaluation validity.
- Weakness 2: No analysis of overfitting or reported training/development loss curves.
- Weakness 3: Missing mean and standard deviation from multiple runs, hindering stability assessment.
- Weakness 4: No mention of regularization methods despite small dataset size.
- Question 1: Provide development set size and number of tags in the training data.
- Question 2: Include training and development loss values to analyze overfitting.
- Question 3: How will you address regularization given the small dataset?
- Question 4: Include mean and standard deviation over multiple runs to assess stability.
- Question 5: Consider using Bayesian optimization for hyper-parameter tuning.
- Question 6: Move details on pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the paper.
- Question 7: Provide inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance and headroom.
- Question 8: Make Figure 1 clearer for black-and-white prints (e.g., use different colors or styles).
