- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents interesting methods and results.  
- Proposes end-to-end argumentation mining using neural sequence labeling and dependency parsing, with a multitask learning variant.  
- Model removes need for manual feature engineering and ILP constraints by jointly learning subtasks.  
- Experiments are substantial with proper comparisons and promising results.  
- Major concern: dataset is very small (~320 training essays, ~80 test), development set size not reported.  
- Training data likely yields only a few thousand tags versus typical sequence-labeling tasks with orders of magnitude more, raising training adequacy concerns.  
- No analysis of overfitting; authors should report training and development loss curves during training.  
- Model stability is uncertain; need results over multiple runs (different initializations) with means and standard deviations.  
- Statistical significance testing is missing and necessary to judge whether improvements are real or due to chance.  
- Regularization is underreported; stronger/more explicit regularization discussion and methods are needed given small data.  
- Suggest replacing current hyperparameter search with Bayesian optimization.  
- Move details on pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary to main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores from dataset paper to contextualize achievable performance.  
- Improve Figure 1 for black-and-white printing (e.g., use different colors/patterns).  
- After author response reviewer raised score to 4 but still requests F1-score ranges and mean/variance reporting due to concerns about high variance (e.g., Kiperwasser setting).
