- Paper is well-structured, clear, and presents methodology and results thoughtfully.  
- Methodology is interesting: end-to-end argumentation mining via sequence labeling and dependency parsing, with multitask learning for sequence labeling.  
- Model advantage: avoids manual feature engineering and ILP constraint design by jointly learning subtasks, mitigating pipeline error propagation.  
- Experiments are substantial, comparisons are appropriate, and results are interesting.  
- Key missing detail: development set size is not reported.  
- Dataset is small (≈320 training essays, 80 test essays), raising concerns about model capacity (BiLSTM-based models) and proper parameter training.  
- Overfitting analysis is lacking; training/dev loss curves and evidence of overfitting should be reported.  
- Model stability is uncertain; report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and perform statistical significance tests.  
- Regularization treatment is insufficiently described; stronger/more explicit regularization is needed given the small dataset.  
- Hyper-parameter search could be improved (suggestion: use Bayesian optimization instead of current method).  
- Move key supplementary information (pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis) into the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance and room for improvement.  
- Improve Figure 1 for black-and-white prints (use differing patterns/colors or clearer distinctions).  
- Report F1-score ranges and mean/variance for different settings; analyze high variance cases (notably the Kiperwasser setting).
