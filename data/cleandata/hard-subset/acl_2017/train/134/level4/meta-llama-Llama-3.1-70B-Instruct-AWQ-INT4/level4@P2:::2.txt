**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a comprehensive study on neural end-to-end approaches to computational argumentation mining (AM). The authors explore various framings, including dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, and a neural model for labeling entities and relations (LSTM-ER). They evaluate these approaches using F1 scores defined on concepts and relations.

**Contributions of the Work**

The paper's main contributions are:

1. The first neural end-to-end solutions to computational AM.
2. A comparison of different framings, including dependency parsing, sequence labeling, and multi-task learning.
3. The finding that sequence labeling solutions are effective, while dependency-based solutions do not work well.
4. The demonstration that the LSTM-ER model performs very well, especially on paragraph level.
5. The improvement of sequence labeling models using a multi-task approach.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a thorough investigation of neural-based approaches to end-to-end argumentation mining.
2. The authors provide a comprehensive supplement with technical details on training models and optimizing hyperparameters.
3. The paper shows that sequence labeling models can be improved using a multi-task approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. The dataset used in the study may have information leakage from the train to the test set, as essays in the train and test sets might be on the same topics.
2. One of the best models, LSTM-ER, is an out-of-the-box application of a model from related work, which might not be a significant contribution.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you discuss the potential information leakage issue in the dataset and its implications for the results?
2. How do you respond to the concern that the LSTM-ER model is not a significant contribution, as it is an out-of-the-box application of a model from related work?
3. Can you clarify the meaning of "de-coupling" in the context of jointly modeling entities and relations?
4. Are the F1 scores in the paragraph and essay settings comparable, particularly for the relation tasks?
