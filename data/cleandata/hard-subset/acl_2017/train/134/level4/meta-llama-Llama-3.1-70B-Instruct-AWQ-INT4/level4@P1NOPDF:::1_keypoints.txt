- Paper is well-written, clear, and makes an interesting contribution.  
- Proposes end-to-end argumentation mining with sequence labeling, dependency parsing, and multitask learning.  
- Model avoids manual ILP, feature engineering, and pipeline error propagation by joint learning.  
- Methods are clearly explained overall, with a few missing details.  
- Experiments are substantial and comparisons are appropriate; results look interesting.  
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test) and unspecified dev set size.  
- Model capacity (BiLSTM-based) is large relative to data size, raising overfitting concerns.  
- Training data has only a few thousand tags versus hundreds of thousands in typical tasks.  
- Paper lacks analysis of overfitting (no training/dev loss curves reported).  
- Model stability is unclear; need mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and statistical significance tests.  
- Regularization is not adequately discussed; more regularization details and experiments are needed.  
- Recommend using Bayesian hyperparameter optimization and moving pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplement to main paper.  
- Ask to report inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset to contextualize system performance.  
- Suggest improving Figure 1 colors for black-and-white printing.  
- After author response, reviewer raised score to 4 but requests F1-score ranges and mean/variance for different settings and analysis of large variances (e.g., Kiperwasser).
