**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural end-to-end approach for computational argumentation mining (AM). The authors frame the problem as a token-based dependency parsing and a token-based sequence tagging model, including a multi-task learning setup. They investigate the performance of these models on a dataset of persuasive essays and compare the results with a feature-based ILP model.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. The authors present the first neural end-to-end solutions to computational AM.
2. They show that several of their models perform better than the state-of-the-art joint ILP model.
3. They demonstrate that a framing of AM as a token-based dependency parsing problem is ineffective.
4. They show that a standard neural sequence tagging model performs robustly in different environments.
5. They demonstrate that a multi-task learning setup improves performance.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. The paper is well-written and easy to understand.
2. The methods and results are interesting and substantial.
3. The authors clearly explain the motivation behind the proposed model.
4. The experiments are well-designed, and the comparisons are performed properly.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. The evaluation and the obtained results might be problematic due to the small size of the dataset and the large capacity of the used recurrent neural networks.
2. The paper does not analyze the overfitting problem and does not provide training and development "loss" values during training.
3. The authors do not provide mean and standard deviation of multiple runs, and statistical significance tests are not included.
4. The paper does not mention regularization methods, which is a crucial problem given the small size of the dataset.
5. The hyper-parameter optimization method is not well-suited for the problem, and Bayesian optimization methods should be considered instead.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Please provide more information about the development set, including its size and how it was used in the experiments.
2. Please analyze the overfitting problem and provide training and development "loss" values during training.
3. Please include mean and standard deviation of multiple runs and statistical significance tests to ensure the stability of the models and the reliability of the results.
4. Please discuss the regularization methods used in the paper and how they address the problem of overfitting.
5. Please consider using Bayesian optimization methods instead of the current hyper-parameter optimization method.
