- Paper proposes a joint neural approach to argumentation mining and evaluates multiple methods (dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER, ILP).  
- Evaluation uses F1 scores for concepts and relations across paragraph and essay settings.  
- Dependency-parsing-based solutions perform poorly.  
- Sequence-labeling solutions are effective and competitive.  
- The out-of-the-box LSTM-ER model performs exceptionally well, especially at paragraph level.  
- Sequence-labeling and LSTM-ER models both outperform the ILP-based state-of-the-art.  
- Authors provide a comprehensive supplement detailing training procedures and hyperparameter optimization.  
- Multi-task learning improves sequence-labeling models, with the claim task contributing more benefit than the relation task.  
- Major concern: possible dataset leakage because training and test essays may share topics, potentially inflating performance.  
- Major concern: strongest model (LSTM-ER) is an out-of-the-box application from prior work, raising novelty questions despite useful experimental insights.  
- Minor concern: unclear explanation of reconstructing the full graph from a tree (Lines 222–226).  
- Minor clarity issue: suggested reordering of sections for readability (Lines 443–444).  
- Confusion over the claim that the system “de-couples relation information from entity information” while jointly modeling them; needs clarification and reference (Lines 455, 477–479).  
- Question about whether F1 scores are comparable between paragraph and essay settings, and whether paragraph models miss cross-paragraph relations.
