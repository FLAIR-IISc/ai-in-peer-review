- Presents multiple joint neural approaches to end-to-end argumentation mining, including dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER, and ILP baselines.
- Evaluates all methods using concept- and relation-level F1 scores.
- Finds dependency-parsing formulations perform poorly for this task.
- Shows sequence labeling models are effective and robust across settings.
- Demonstrates an out-of-the-box LSTM-ER model performs very well, especially at paragraph level.
- Both sequence-labeling and LSTM-ER models outperform the prior ILP-based state-of-the-art.
- Provides a comprehensive supplement with training and hyperparameter optimization details.
- Multi-task learning substantially improves sequence labeling models, with the claim subtask helping more than the relation subtask.
- Claims to be the first neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining.
- Argues token-based dependency parsing framing is ineffective for argumentation mining.
- Shows a standard neural sequence tagging model with distance encoding performs robustly in varied environments.
- Demonstrates that adding natural subtasks as auxiliary tasks in MTL improves overall performance.
- Strength: comprehensive comparison of neural approaches (dependency, sequence labeling, MTL).
- Strength: uses a robust evaluation metric and provides detailed result analysis.
- Strength: thorough technical supplement on training and hyperparameters.
- Strength: effective on a challenging dataset, with clear insights on method strengths/weaknesses.
- Weakness: potential dataset topic overlap between train and test may cause information leakage.
- Weakness: LSTM-ER is an out-of-the-box model from prior work, which may limit novelty for that component.
- Question: How is the full graph reconstructed from a tree output by the dependency parsing approach?
- Question: Why present sequence tagging and dependency approaches in the chosen order; would reversing improve clarity?
- Question: Please clarify what “decoupling” entity and relation information in LSTM-ER means and how it differs from joint modeling.
- Question: Are paragraph- and essay-level F1 scores comparable, and do paragraph models miss cross-paragraph relations by default?
