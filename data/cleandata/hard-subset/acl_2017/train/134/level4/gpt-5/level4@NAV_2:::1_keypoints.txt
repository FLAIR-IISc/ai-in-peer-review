- Manuscript is clearly written and accessible; proposed approaches and empirical findings are engaging.  
- Evaluation setup and reported gains raise concerns.  
- Introduces end-to-end neural argumentation mining with sequence-tagging, dependency-parsing, and a multitask tagging extension.  
- Well motivated versus prior ILP/manual-feature systems; aims to jointly learn AM subtasks to reduce pipeline error propagation.  
- Technical presentation is clear, though a few methodological/details are missing.  
- Experimental study is substantial with appropriate comparisons and interesting outcomes.  
- Primary concern: dataset is small (≈320 training essays, ≈80 test; dev size unspecified), likely insufficient for BiLSTM capacity and risking unreliable parameter estimates.  
- Paper does not address overfitting; request training and development loss curves.  
- Model stability uncertain; report means and standard deviations over multiple runs with different random seeds and include statistical significance tests.  
- Regularization strategy is inadequately described and needs comprehensive treatment.  
- Hyperparameter selection strategy should consider Bayesian optimization.  
- Move key supplement material (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) into the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement statistics from the dataset paper to contextualize system performance.  
- Adjust Figure 1 styling to remain clear in grayscale (use patterns or distinct line styles).  
- After clarifications reviewer raised recommendation to 4; ask authors to report F1-score ranges and means/variances and analyze notable run-to-run variability (e.g., Kiperwasser configuration).
