- Strength: Paper is clearly written, accessible, and proposes interesting end-to-end argumentation mining methods.

- Strength: Avoids manual ILP constraints and feature engineering by jointly learning subtasks, reducing pipeline error propagation.

- Weakness: Dataset is very small (≈320 train, 80 test essays); model capacity (BiLSTM) may be too large for available data.

- Weakness: Development set size is not reported, raising concerns about how little data remains for training.

- Weakness: No analysis of overfitting (no training/dev loss curves); authors’ own claim suggests possible overfitting.

- Weakness: Model stability is untested — need mean and standard deviation across multiple runs with different random seeds.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing to confirm reported improvements are reliable.

- Weakness: Regularization strategy is insufficiently discussed in the main paper (only brief mention in supplement); more thorough treatment needed.

- Suggestion: Report training and development loss over time (per update/epoch) to assess overfitting and convergence.

- Suggestion: Use Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter search instead of current procedure.

- Suggestion: Move details on pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.

- Suggestion: Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance and headroom.

- Suggestion: Improve Figure 1 legibility (distinct colors/styles) for black-and-white prints.

- Edit request: Report F1-score ranges and the mean/variance across different runs/settings; analyze large variance cases (e.g., Kiperwasser) to assess stability and replication feasibility.
