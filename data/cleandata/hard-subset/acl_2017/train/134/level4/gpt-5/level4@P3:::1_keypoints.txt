- Systematic comparison of four neural end-to-end framings for argumentation mining on the PE corpus, finding tagging and adapted LSTM-ER generally outperform dependency parsing and match/outperform an ILP baseline.

- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that sequence tagging with distance-aware labels and adapted LSTM-ER beat a feature-engineered ILP baseline in several settings.

- Contribution: Evidence that simple local BiLSTM tagging outperforms token-level dependency parsing, and that multi-task learning over natural subtasks yields additional gains.

- Contribution: Analysis of paragraph vs. essay granularity and relation distance showing when LSTM-ER’s general relation modeling helps versus when distance-aware tagging is preferable.

- Strength: Clear problem framing and motivation for end-to-end neural approaches that avoid manual features and ILP constraints and for token-level modeling to avoid assuming component boundaries.

- Strength: Thorough, relevant experimental comparisons across dependency parsers, sequence taggers (with/without CNN/CRF), MTL, LSTM-ER, and a strong ILP baseline with paragraph/essay evaluations and span-level F1s.

- Strength: Insightful empirical findings on parser underperformance on long sequences, robustness of BiLSTM taggers via distance-encoded labels, MTL benefits, and LSTM-ER’s trade-offs with relation candidate explosion.

- Strength: Practical significance—neural models reduce manual engineering and provide actionable guidance on model choice for different document lengths and task couplings.

- Weakness: Small dataset (~320 essays) vs. model capacity and lack of reported stability measures (mean/std across seeds, training curves) raising overfitting and robustness concerns.

- Weakness: Limited reporting on regularization and optimization details (dropout, L2, gradient clipping, early stopping, epochs, parameter counts) and a limited hyperparameter search description.

- Weakness: Important practical details (pretrained embeddings, tokenization, OOV handling, error taxonomy) are in the supplement rather than main text; IAA should be summarized to contextualize performance.

- Weakness: Presentation issues—Figure 1 needs higher-contrast line styles and clearer labeling of paragraph vs. essay settings in tables/figures.

- Question: Provide development set size, selection protocol, and mean±std (or ranges) across multiple seeds per main model and setting; how many runs per configuration and was there hyperparameter sensitivity?

- Question: Report dropout rates (embedding/LSTM/CRF/char-CNN), L2 penalties, gradient clipping, early stopping, epochs, and parameter counts; include training vs. dev loss/F1 curves.

- Question: Clarify significance testing beyond Table 1 for MTL and LSTM-ER comparisons, including test type, pairing protocol, and multiple-comparison corrections.

- Question: Describe hyperparameter search ranges and budgets; would broader/better search (e.g., Bayesian optimization) change conclusions?

- Question: Detail LSTM-ER specifics—scheduled sampling, entity pretraining, use of sentence-level dependency info given many cross-sentence relations, and ablations (e.g., TreeLSTM contribution).

- Question: Will code, preprocessing scripts, and exact configurations/checkpoints be released for reproducibility?
