- Empirical study showing neural end-to-end models (especially BiLSTM sequence tagging and LSTM-ER) match or outperform a strong ILP baseline on PE for end-to-end argumentation mining.  
- Evidence that token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for AM, while BiLSTM sequence taggers with distance-coded labels handle long-range dependencies better.  
- Multi-task learning with natural subtasks (notably component detection) improves sequence tagging performance and offers practical model design guidance.  
- Strong empirical coverage: multiple neural model families evaluated against a competitive ILP baseline at paragraph and essay levels with careful analysis of paragraph→essay degradation.  
- Practical modeling insight: general relation models help on short texts/few entities, constrained approaches and distance-encoded labels are crucial for longer texts and many entities.  
- Decoupling entity and relation prediction (LSTM-ER) and adding MTL auxiliaries substantially boost component identification; component detection is the most helpful auxiliary.  
- Thorough methodology and transparency: extensive supplementary material, hyperparameter details, software, error analysis, and significance testing aiding reproducibility.  
- Potential data leakage via topic overlap between train/test essays could inflate performance and raise generalization concerns.  
- Limited methodological novelty for the best-performing model (LSTM-ER) since it’s largely an off-the-shelf adaptation; main contribution is empirical.  
- Clarity/exposition issues: unclear tree-to-graph reconstruction (Lines 222–226) and contradictory phrasing about decoupling vs joint modeling (Line 455).  
- Additional clarity needed on information flow/interface between component and relation modules (Lines 477–479) and presentation ordering (Lines 443–444).  
- Unclear comparability of paragraph- vs essay-level F1, especially for relations and cross-paragraph links; a quantitative breakdown is needed.  
- Request to report whether train/test essays share topics and provide seen-vs-unseen or topic-held-out robustness analyses.  
- Request a detailed description of reconstructing full argument graphs from token-level dependency outputs, including multiple links and root handling.  
- Ask for clarification of LSTM-ER architecture/training: what is shared vs pipelined and which losses are optimized jointly.  
- Request ablations quantifying contributions of distance encoding and stance in tagset, and MTL gains per auxiliary task and layer choice.  
- Overall: thorough, impactful empirical study with publishable results; addressing clarity and topic-overlap concerns would strengthen the paper.
