- Strength: paper is clearly written and methods/results are engaging.
- Contribution: end-to-end argumentation mining framed as sequence labeling and dependency parsing, with multitask learning for sequence labeling.
- Motivation: reduces manual feature engineering and pipeline error propagation compared to prior ILP-based work.
- Experiments: substantial and comparisons appropriate; results interesting.
- Main concern: dataset is very small for Bi(LSTM) models (≈320 train, ≈80 test) and development set size is unspecified.
- Overfitting risk: paper lacks analysis (report training and development losses per epoch/ update).
- Model stability: need means and standard deviations over multiple runs with different random seeds and statistical significance tests.
- Regularization: insufficient detail—report techniques used (dropout, weight decay, early stopping) and chosen hyperparameters.
- Hyperparameter search: consider Bayesian optimization instead of (or in addition to) current approach.
- Paper organization: move key details from supplement (pretrained word embeddings, error analysis) into the main paper.
- Evaluation context: report inter-annotator agreement from the dataset paper to show remaining headroom.
- Figure usability: adjust Figure 1 colors to be readable in grayscale for print.
- Revision note: reviewer raised recommendation to 4 and requests reporting F1-score ranges, means, and variances across runs (noting substantial variance in some settings).
