- Proposes end-to-end neural approaches for argument mining: token-level sequence labeling, token-level dependency parsing, and an MTL variant, compared to an ILP pipeline baseline.
- Key finding: token-level dependency parsing underperforms, while BiLSTM sequence tagging is robust and often superior to the ILP baseline.
- Key finding: multi-task learning with “natural” auxiliary tasks improves performance.
- Contribution: empirical study showing parsing framing is ineffective and BiLSTM tagging is strong.
- Contribution: practical neural end-to-end alternative that avoids manual features and ILP constraints, with MTL evidence.
- Contribution: thorough comparison of paragraph- vs. essay-level settings showing effects of sequence length and search space.
- Strength: clear motivation, writing, and well-explained modeling choices.
- Strength: substantial and fair experimental comparisons with informative analyses (distance distributions, constraints, search space).
- Strength: useful negative result for token-based parsing and practical takeaways on BiLSTM and MTL.
- Weakness: small dataset (~320 training essays) raises overfitting concerns; no training/dev loss curves or overfitting analysis.
- Weakness: lacks reporting of mean/std and statistical significance across multiple runs; stability and reliability are unclear.
- Weakness: insufficient regularization details in main paper (dropout, weight decay, early stopping, etc.); supplement only briefly mentions LSTM-ER.
- Weakness: hyperparameter optimization approach may be inadequate for this small-data regime; suggest Bayesian optimization.
- Weakness: important details (pretrained embeddings, error analysis) are only in the supplement and should be moved to the main paper.
- Weakness: missing inter-annotator agreement (IAA) figures to contextualize achievable performance.
- Weakness: Figure 1’s color scheme may not be clear in black-and-white print.
- Question: what is the development set size and selection protocol; how are early stopping and model selection performed?
- Question: please provide training and development loss curves and performance-over-epoch plots to assess overfitting.
- Question: what regularization techniques were used per model (dropout placements/rates, L2, gradient clipping, label smoothing); were alternatives tried and discarded?
- Question: report mean, standard deviation, and F1-score ranges across multiple seeds and include statistical significance tests; how sensitive are conclusions to initialization?
- Question: explain the observed variance in the Kiperwasser setting and analyze reasons for substantial fluctuation.
- Question: how many training instances per class (components, relations, tokens per label) exist and how is class imbalance addressed?
- Additional comment: move key supplementary details into the main paper to improve reproducibility and interpretation.
- Edit note: reviewer raised recommendation to 4 after authors’ response but still requests F1 ranges, mean/variance reporting, and further analysis of model stability (e.g., Kiperwasser variance).
