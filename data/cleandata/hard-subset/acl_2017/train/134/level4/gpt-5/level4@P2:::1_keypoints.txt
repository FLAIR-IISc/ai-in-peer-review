- Paper compares end-to-end neural framings for token-level argument mining: sequence tagging, token-level dependency parsing, MTL variants, and LSTM-ER adaptation.
- Main findings: token-level dependency parsing underperforms sequence tagging.
- Main findings: BiLSTM-based taggers achieve strong results and can surpass a feature-rich ILP baseline without hand-crafted features.
- Main findings: Multi-task learning of component and relation labeling yields additional gains.
- Strength: Clear problem framing and motivation for end-to-end neural approaches and the token-level tagging label design.
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical comparison across models, document levels, and exact/approximate matching metrics.
- Strength: Useful methodological insights about model suitability, distance encoding, MTL benefits, and document-length dependence of model choice.
- Strength: Competitive performance achieved without manual feature engineering or ILP constraints.
- Weakness: Small training set (~320 essays) with no reported development split, raising concerns about tuning and early stopping.
- Weakness: No analysis of training vs. dev loss/metric curves to assess overfitting or convergence.
- Weakness: Results reported as single runs; missing mean, standard deviation, ranges, and consistent significance testing across seeds.
- Weakness: Regularization details (dropout, L2, gradient clipping, learning schedules, etc.) are underreported and need full disclosure and ablation.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter search methodology appears limited; recommend systematic or Bayesian search and reporting of parameter counts.
- Weakness: Some essential implementation details and analyses are relegated to the supplement; main paper should include key items (pretrained embeddings, core error analysis, inter-annotator agreement).
- Weakness: Figure 1 needs color/line-style choices that remain clear in grayscale.
- Question: What exact development set size and selection protocol were used, and was test data ever used for tuning?
- Question: What regularization settings were used per model and how sensitive are results to them?
- Question: How many parameters does each model have and how do capacity-controlled (smaller) variants perform?
- Question: Ablations requested for CRF layer, character CNNs, distance feature d, stance encoding, and MTL layer choices.
- Question: How often do neural models violate structural constraints (e.g., one parent per premise), and do they learn these constraints from data?
- Question: Please provide F1 means, standard deviations, and ranges across 5–10 seeds for key systems at paragraph and essay levels.
- Post-response note: Reviewer raised score to 4 but requests inclusion of F1 ranges and mean/variance across seeds in the paper.
- Post-response note: Reviewer remains concerned about model stability (notably Kiperwasser) and asks for a brief analysis of observed variability.
