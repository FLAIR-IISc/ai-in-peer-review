- Strength: Paper is well written, easy to follow, with interesting methods and results.
- Strength: Proposes end-to-end neural argumentation mining avoiding manual ILP constraints and feature engineering.
- Strength: Jointly learns subtasks to mitigate pipeline error propagation.
- Weakness: Training data is very small (~320 train essays, ~80 test); dev set size not reported, raising concerns about parameter estimation.
- Weakness: Model capacity (BiLSTM-based) likely too large for dataset, increasing risk of overfitting.
- Missing analysis: No training/development loss curves to assess overfitting during training.
- Missing analysis: No reporting of mean and standard deviation over multiple runs or statistical significance tests to assess model stability.
- Regularization: Paper lacks discussion of regularization methods and their impact; supplementary material only briefly mentions it for one model.
- Hyperparameter tuning: Current method is unclear; reviewer suggests using Bayesian optimization.
- Reporting details: Move pre-trained embedding details and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.
- Dataset context: Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance and remaining headroom.
- Figure issue: Figure 1 needs clearer, distinguishable colors/styles for legibility in black-and-white prints.
- Revision requested: Report F1-score ranges and mean/variance across runs/settings to demonstrate stability and reproducibility.
- Specific stability concern: Analyze large variance in the Kiperwasser setting (e.g., F1 ranges like [0.56, 0.61]) and discuss causes.
