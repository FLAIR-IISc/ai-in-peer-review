- Proposes end-to-end neural argumentation mining with two formulations (sequence labeling and dependency parsing) and a multitask sequence-labeling variant.  
- Motivation: aims to replace ILP-based, hand-engineered pipelines with joint, end-to-end learning to reduce manual effort and error propagation.  
- Strength: clear motivation and well-written presentation.  
- Strength: interesting modeling choices (seq-labeling vs dependency parsing) and inclusion of multitask learning.  
- Strength: thorough baselines and careful empirical comparisons.  
- Strength: potentially impactful shift away from ILP/feature engineering to end-to-end methods.  
- Major concern: high risk of overfitting given small dataset (~320 train, ~80 test essays) versus high-capacity BiLSTM models.  
- Missing analysis: training and development losses/F1 across epochs and explicit early stopping criteria are not reported.  
- Model stability: needs reporting of mean and standard deviation (or 95% CI) over multiple random seeds for main results.  
- Statistical validity: requires significance testing (e.g., paired bootstrap or approximate randomization) for key comparisons.  
- Development set and protocol: dev set size/construction not specified; all tuning/early stopping should use only dev data.  
- Suggestion: consider k-fold cross-validation or repeated hold-out to improve reliability with small data.  
- Regularization details lacking: specify dropout (types/locations/rates), L2, gradient clipping, label smoothing, embedding regularization, and early stopping.  
- Recommend ablation studies to show impact of regularizers on performance and stability.  
- Hyperparameter search: current procedure seems limited; suggest Bayesian optimization (GP or TPE) for learning rates, hidden sizes, dropout, layers.  
- Reproducibility: move critical supplement details (pretrained embeddings, OOV handling, tokenization/casing, error analysis) into main paper.  
- Report IAA (e.g., Cohen’s κ or Krippendorff’s α) from dataset paper to contextualize attainable performance and error sources.  
- Report label distribution/class imbalance and how it was handled (loss weighting, sampling).  
- Presentation fixes: improve Figure 1 readability in grayscale and define all acronyms on first use.  
- After author response: reviewer increased score to 4 but demands reported F1 ranges and mean/variance across seeds and deeper analysis of large variance sources (data scarcity, initialization, optimizer, regularization).
