- Paper explores multiple neural formulations for end-to-end argumentation mining: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multitask learning, LSTM-ER, and ILP baselines.  
- Evaluation uses F1 for concepts and relations; dependency-based methods perform poorly.  
- Sequence labeling models are effective and benefit substantially from multitask learning.  
- LSTM-ER (out-of-the-box entity/relation model) performs very well, especially at paragraph level.  
- Both sequence labeling and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP baselines.  
- Supplementary material is comprehensive (training details, hyperparameter optimization, technicals).  
- Major concern: dataset split may allow topic overlap between train and test, risking information leakage and optimistic scores.  
- Major concern: LSTM-ER is essentially an off-the-shelf model from prior work, reducing novelty of that strong result.  
- Clarify how the full graph is reconstructed from a produced tree and under what assumptions (Lines 222–226).  
- Reorder presentation of evaluation results for clarity (swap sequence tagging and dependency-based order; Lines 443–444).  
- Clarify the meaning of “decouple” components while “jointly modeling” them and provide precise citation (Line 455).  
- Clarify the claim that the system “decouples relation information from entity information” (Lines 477–479) — likely referring to separating edge prediction from edge labeling.  
- Discuss comparability of paragraph-level vs essay-level F1 scores, especially for relations, since paragraph models cannot capture cross-paragraph relations.
