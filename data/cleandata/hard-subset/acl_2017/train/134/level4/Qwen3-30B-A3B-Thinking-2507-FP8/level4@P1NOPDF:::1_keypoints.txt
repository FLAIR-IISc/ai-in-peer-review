1. Strength: Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and methods/results are interesting and well-motivated.  
2. Strength: Removes need for ILP and manual feature engineering by jointly learning subtasks end-to-end.  
3. Weakness: Evaluation methodology and results raise concerns about robustness of findings.  
4. Weakness: Dataset is critically small (~320 training essays, ~80 test essays) and development set size is unreported.  
5. Weakness: Total number of training tags is very small compared to typical sequence labeling tasks, risking undertraining.  
6. Weakness: No analysis of overfitting—training and development loss curves are missing.  
7. Weakness: Claim that taggers need less data and are less prone to overfitting is unsupported by empirical evidence.  
8. Weakness: Model stability is questionable; large performance variation reported without mean/std across runs or significance testing.  
9. Weakness: Reliance on inherent regularization of BiLSTM models is insufficient given tiny dataset; regularization methods are not detailed.  
10. Recommendation: Explicitly detail regularization methods (e.g., dropout, weight decay) in the main text.  
11. Recommendation: Use Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning rather than ad-hoc search.  
12. Recommendation: Move critical details from supplementary material (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) into the main paper.  
13. Additional requests: Report inter-annotator agreement for the dataset and improve Figure 1 readability for B&W prints.  
14. Edit/Follow-up: After revisions, reviewer requests explicit reporting of F1-score ranges and mean/variance across experimental settings to assess statistical significance and robustness.
