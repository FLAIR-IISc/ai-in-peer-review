- Proposes an end-to-end neural argumentation mining system using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, with multitask learning.  
- Clear writing, well-motivated shift from manual feature engineering to neural end-to-end learning.  
- Experiments show improvements over baselines and the multitask component adds analytical value.  
- Critical concern: very small dataset (320 essays, ~3k tokens) raises strong overfitting risk given model capacity.  
- Paper omits training/validation loss curves needed to diagnose overfitting.  
- No reporting of mean±SD over multiple runs or model stability across random seeds.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for performance differences.  
- Regularization details are insufficient and largely relegated to supplementary material.  
- Reproducibility issues: unclear hyperparameter tuning methodology and lack of recommended Bayesian optimization.  
- Missing dataset context: development set size and inter-annotator agreement (IAA) are not reported.  
- Technical details (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) are in supplement and should be moved to the main text; Figure 1 needs better color/print readability.  
- Recommendations: report mean±SD over ≥5 runs, include statistical tests, provide loss curves, detail regularization and hyperparameter search (preferably Bayesian), and add dev size and IAA.  
- Minor issues: unjustified claim that taggers are simpler without overfitting evidence; expand regularization discussion from supplement.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires major revision to address model robustness, dataset context, and methodological rigor before acceptance.
