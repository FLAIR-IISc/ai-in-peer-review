- Introduces neural end-to-end approaches for computational argumentation mining to replace manual feature/ILP pipeline methods.  
- Claims neural methods outperform the state-of-the-art ILP model.  
- Finds token-level dependency parsing ineffective for argumentation mining compared to component-level approaches.  
- Shows robust performance of neural sequence tagging with distance encoding across scenarios.  
- Improves performance further via multi-task learning with natural subtasks.  

- Strong motivation for neural end-to-end methods and clear argument about avoiding pipeline error propagation.  
- Thorough, well-structured experimental evaluation comparing multiple neural approaches to ILP, with STagBLCC performing best.  
- Valuable finding that token-level dependency parsing is ineffective, guiding future research.  
- Clear, accessible explanation of model architectures, especially sequence tagging with distance-encoded labels.  

- Very small dataset (320 train / 80 test) raises serious concerns about overfitting and reliability; development set size not reported.  
- Insufficient analysis of model stability and generalizability: no mean/variance over runs, no statistical significance tests, no loss curves.  
- Inadequate discussion and empirical evidence of regularization despite small data; regularization details largely relegated to supplement.  
- Omits inter-annotator agreement scores for the dataset, limiting contextual interpretation of results.  
- Hyperparameter optimization method is under-justified given small data; more sophisticated methods (e.g., Bayesian optimization) should be considered.  

- Request for the size of the development set used for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Request mean and standard deviation of F1 scores across multiple random-seed runs to assess stability.  
- Request training and development loss curves to evaluate overfitting.  
- Request inter-annotator agreement scores for the dataset.  
- Ask for empirical evidence supporting the claim that taggers need less training data and are less prone to overfitting.  

- Suggest moving key details about pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplementary materials to the main paper.  
- Recommend improving Figure 1 readability by using color differentiation for black-and-white prints.
