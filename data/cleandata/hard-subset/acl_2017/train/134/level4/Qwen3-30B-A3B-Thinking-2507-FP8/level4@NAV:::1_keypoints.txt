- Strength: Paper is well-written, accessible, and presents compelling methods and results.  
- Strength: Introduces an end-to-end argumentation mining system using sequence labeling, dependency parsing, and multitask learning.  
- Strength: Clear motivation to remove ILP/manual feature engineering and joint learning to avoid pipeline error propagation.  
- Strength: Methodology is well-explained aside from minor missing details.  
- Strength: Experiments are comprehensive and comparisons are appropriate.  

- Weakness: Very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test essays) raises concerns about training adequacy for high-capacity BiLSTM models.  
- Weakness: Development set size is omitted from paper and supplementary materials.  
- Weakness: Total number of training tags is likely orders of magnitude smaller than typical sequence-labeling tasks.  
- Weakness: No overfitting analysis provided (e.g., training/dev loss curves across epochs).  
- Weakness: Model stability not assessed — missing mean/std across multiple runs and statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: Regularization details are insufficiently reported (only brief mention in supplementary).  

- Recommendation: Replace current hyperparameter search with Bayesian optimization.  
- Recommendation: Move pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary to main paper (add one page).  
- Recommendation: Report inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance.  
- Recommendation: Improve Figure 1 with color differentiation for clarity in black-and-white print.  
- Edit request: Report F1-score ranges and means/variances across runs/settings to aid reproducibility and assess stability (noting high variance in baseline results).
