- Strength: Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and methods/results are interesting and well-motivated.  
- Strength: Proposes an end-to-end neural system for argumentation mining using sequence labeling and dependency parsing.  
- Strength: Explores multitask learning to jointly model subtasks and avoid pipeline error propagation.  
- Strength: Eliminates manual ILP constraints and feature engineering used in prior work.  
- Strength: Experiments are substantial and comparisons are appropriate.  

- Weakness: Primary concern about small dataset size (320 train, 80 test) relative to high-capacity (Bi)LSTM models.  
- Weakness: Development set size is not reported, raising concerns about evaluation validity.  
- Weakness: Total number of training tags likely very small, risking undertrained parameters and overfitting.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks overfitting analysis (no training/dev loss curves reported).  
- Weakness: Model stability not assessed—no mean/std over multiple runs or statistical significance tests.  
- Weakness: Specific regularization methods are omitted or insufficiently described.  

- Recommendation: Use Bayesian optimization for hyper-parameter tuning instead of current method.  
- Recommendation: Move pre-trained embedding details and error analysis from supplementary to the main paper.  
- Recommendation: Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize performance margins.  
- Recommendation: Improve Figure 1 with distinct line styles/markers for black-and-white reproduction.  
- Post-review request: Report F1-score ranges and mean/variance across runs; address large variance in baseline results (e.g., Kiperwasser).
