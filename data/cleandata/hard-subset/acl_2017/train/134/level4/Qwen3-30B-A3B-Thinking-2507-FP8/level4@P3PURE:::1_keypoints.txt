- Strength: Paper is well-written, accessible, with clear method explanations and compelling results.  
- Weakness: Evaluation and results need careful scrutiny.  
- Proposal: End-to-end neural approach for argumentation mining using sequence labeling, dependency parsing, and multitask learning.  
- Motivation: Eliminates ILP/manual features and jointly learns subtasks to avoid pipeline error propagation.  
- Clarity: Methods are clearly explained overall, with a few missing details.  
- Experiments: Substantial experimental setup with appropriate comparisons and interesting findings.  
- Critical concern 1 (data/overfitting): Training set is very small (~320 essays, ~80 test essays), likely only thousands of tags, risking severe overfitting and limited generalization.  
- Critical concern 1 (evidence): No training/development loss curves provided to support claims about overfitting or tagger behavior.  
- Critical concern 2 (stability/statistics): Results lack stability metrics—no means, standard deviations, ranges across runs, or significance tests.  
- Critical concern 2 (example): Reported F1 variance (e.g., [0.56, 0.61] in Kiperwasser setting) suggests instability that must be analyzed.  
- Regularization: Paper omits discussion of regularization details despite small dataset; supplementary notes are insufficient.  
- Hyperparameters: Hyper-parameter search should adopt Bayesian optimization rather than current approach.  
- Presentation: Move pretrained embeddings and error analysis from supplementary to main paper (one page).  
- Dataset context: Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance and improvement margins.  
- Figure quality: Figure 1 should use distinct colors for readability in black-and-white prints.  
- Post-revision note: Reviewer raised recommendation after revisions but insists explicit variance/stability analysis (means, ranges, significance) is still required before final acceptance.
