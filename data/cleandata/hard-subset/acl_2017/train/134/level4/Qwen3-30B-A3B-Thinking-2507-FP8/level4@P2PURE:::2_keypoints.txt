- Evaluates five neural approaches: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning (on sequence labeling), LSTM-ER, and ILP-based models.  
- Uses F1 scores over concepts and relations for evaluation.  
- Dependency-based methods underperform.  
- Sequence labeling approaches are effective.  
- LSTM-ER achieves exceptional results, especially at paragraph level.  
- Sequence labeling and LSTM-ER significantly outperform the ILP baseline.  
- Multi-task learning substantially benefits sequence labeling, with the claim task yielding more improvement than the relation task.  
- Supplementary material thoroughly documents training procedures, hyperparameter tuning, and implementation details.  
- Major concern: possible dataset leakage—train/test essays may share topics and reused arguments, potentially inflating performance; authors should discuss.  
- Major concern: top-performing LSTM-ER is an off-the-shelf model, limiting novelty despite strong empirical findings.
