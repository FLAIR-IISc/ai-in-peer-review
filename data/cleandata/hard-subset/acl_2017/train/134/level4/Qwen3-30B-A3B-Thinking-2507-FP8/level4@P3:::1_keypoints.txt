- Paper frames end-to-end argumentation mining as token-based dependency parsing and sequence tagging with multi-task learning and claims five contributions (first neural end-to-end AM, outperforming ILP, dependency parsing ineffective, distance-encoded tagging robust, MTL helps).

- Reviewer extracts the core contributions as: sequence tagging with distance encoding is robust; token-level dependency parsing is ineffective; multi-task learning with natural subtasks improves performance.

- Strength: Introduces neural end-to-end AM approaches, removing manual feature engineering and ILP constraint design, and compares several neural architectures.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation is substantial; sequence tagging models (notably STagBLCC) outperform dependency parsing and ILP baselines with up to ~13% component F1 and ~11% relation F1 gains at paragraph level.

- Strength: Provides valuable insight that token-level dependency parsing performs poorly for AM despite argument structures often forming trees.

- Weakness: Dataset is very small (320 train, 80 test essays), raising serious overfitting and reliability concerns; authors provide no evidence against overfitting.

- Weakness: Lacks model stability reporting — no means, standard deviations across runs, or statistical significance tests to assess whether differences are robust.

- Weakness: Inadequate discussion of regularization choices for the small dataset; details like dropout, weight decay, and early stopping are missing or relegated to supplements.

- Weakness: Omits inter-annotator agreement scores for the dataset, making it hard to contextualize model performance relative to human agreement.

- Question: Please provide training and validation loss curves for main models to demonstrate that overfitting is not occurring.

- Question: Report mean and standard deviation across multiple random seeds and perform statistical significance testing.

- Question: Specify which regularization techniques were used and justify their selection for the small dataset.

- Question: Provide inter-annotator agreement scores for the dataset to contextualize model performance.

- Additional comment: Move details about pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material to the main paper.

- Additional comment: Include F1-score ranges in the paper to aid reproducibility.

- Additional comment: Improve Figure 1 color contrast for black-and-white printing.

- Additional comment: Discuss model capacity versus dataset size and explain why models are expected to generalize despite limited data.
