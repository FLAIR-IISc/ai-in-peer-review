- Strength: Clear, well-structured proposal for end-to-end argumentation mining using neural networks.  
- Strength: Avoids manual feature engineering and ILP constraints common in prior work.  
- Strength: Joint learning framework reduces error propagation from pipeline methods.  
- Strength: Multitask learning experiments add meaningful depth.  
- Strength: Methods are logically explained with substantial experimental validation and appropriate comparisons.

- Weakness: Extremely small dataset (≈320 training essays, ≈80 test essays) and development set size omitted, raising data adequacy concerns.  
- Weakness: Total tag counts are low compared to typical sequence-labeling tasks, increasing risk of underfitting and poor parameter learning.  
- Weakness: Claim that "taggers are simpler local models" lacks empirical support and conflates model complexity with data needs.

- Weakness: No training/development loss curves or thorough discussion of regularization; supplementary regularization details insufficient.  
- Weakness: Model stability unverified—no mean/std across multiple runs and no statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: Reported performance variance (e.g., Kiperwasser F1 0.56–0.61) is substantial and unexplained.

- Concern: Hyperparameter tuning via grid search is suboptimal for the small-data regime; Bayesian optimization recommended.  
- Concern: Inter-annotator agreement scores are omitted and should be included to contextualize task difficulty.  
- Concern: Figure 1 needs distinct colors for black-and-white readability.  
- Concern: Pre-trained embedding details and error analysis should be moved from supplementary material into the main paper.

- General: Evaluation framework and limited data undermine confidence in the results; conclusions about model superiority are tentative without stability and overfitting analyses.  
- Recommendation: Marginal reject—authors must address dataset size concerns, provide stability metrics (mean/std), loss curves, clarify regularization, include IAA, and resolve unexplained variance before acceptance.  
- Note: Authors partially addressed F1-range request, but development set size omission and stability issues remain unresolved.
