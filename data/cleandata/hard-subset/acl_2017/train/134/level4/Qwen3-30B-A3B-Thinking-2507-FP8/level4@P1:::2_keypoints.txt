- Contributions: first neural end-to-end solutions for argumentation mining; sequence tagging with distance encoding outperforms dependency parsing; multi-task learning with component detection improves performance; sequence tagging models are robust across environments.

- Strength: comprehensive, well-structured comparison of multiple neural architectures (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, MTL, LSTM-ER) with thorough evaluation.

- Strength: sequence tagging with distance encoding (STagT) consistently outperforms dependency parsing and ILP baselines, effectively capturing long-range dependencies.

- Strength: multi-task learning experiments show component detection as an auxiliary task yields meaningful gains, while relation detection helps less.

- Weakness: potential data leakage from topic overlap between train and test sets (student essays on controversial topics) is not adequately addressed.

- Weakness: LSTM-ER is largely an out-of-the-box application with insufficient explanation of adaptations and why it performs best at paragraph level.

- Weakness: explanation of how LSTM-ER "de-couples" entities and relations while "jointly modeling" them is confusing and needs clarification.

- Question: provide topic distribution statistics and evidence that train/test topic overlap is low enough to avoid data leakage.

- Question: clarify how cross-paragraph relations are handled in paragraph-level evaluation and whether paragraph boundaries affect relation extraction metrics.

- Question: explicitly explain how LSTM-ER models the relationship between entity detection and relation identification (resolve the "de-couples" vs. "jointly modeling" contradiction).

- Overall: strong empirical contribution demonstrating effectiveness of neural sequence tagging for argumentation mining; minor issues can be fixed in revision; supplementary materials are detailed and helpful.
