- Summary: Proposes first neural end-to-end approaches to argumentation mining (token tagging, dependency parsing, multi-task learning), finds BiLSTM sequence tagging > dependency parsing, and multi-task learning helps.

- Strength 1: First neural end-to-end solution removing manual feature engineering and constraint design from prior pipeline methods.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical gains: STagBLCC outperforms ILP baseline by ~5–10% on most metrics.

- Strength 3: Provides insight that token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for argumentation, explaining prior component-level successes.

- Weakness 1: Very small dataset (320 training essays) creates overfitting and statistical-reliability concerns; dev set size and stability not reported.

- Weakness 2: No proper overfitting analysis—no training/validation loss curves or evidence for the claim that taggers need less data.

- Weakness 3: Missing details on regularization in the main paper (only briefly mentioned for LSTM-ER in supplement), weakening confidence given limited data.

- Weakness 4: Omits inter-annotator agreement scores that would contextualize model performance and upper bounds.

- Question 1: What is the development set size and why is it not reported?

- Question 2: Can you provide training and validation loss curves to assess overfitting?

- Question 3: Please report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs to demonstrate model stability (especially for Kiperwasser).

- Question 4: Please detail the regularization techniques used for the neural models given the small dataset.
