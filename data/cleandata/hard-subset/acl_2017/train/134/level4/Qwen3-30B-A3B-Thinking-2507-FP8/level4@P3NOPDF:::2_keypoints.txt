- Paper conducts a thorough empirical comparison of neural architectures for end-to-end argumentation mining, including dependency parsing, sequence labeling, MTL, LSTM-ER, and ILP-based methods.  
- Evaluations use F1 scores for both concept and relation extraction at paragraph and essay levels.  
- Main result: dependency-based methods perform poorly while sequence labeling models achieve strong results.  
- Off-the-shelf LSTM-ER attains state-of-the-art performance, especially at the paragraph level.  
- Sequence labeling and LSTM-ER substantially outperform the ILP baseline.  
- Multi-task learning (MTL) notably improves sequence labeling, with the claim task providing more benefit than the relation task.  
- Supplementary materials are comprehensive and enhance reproducibility (training, hyperparameters, setups).  
- Strength: rigorous, multi-faceted comparison isolates impact of modeling choices and validates leveraging existing entity-relation frameworks.  
- Major concern: potential data leakage from topic overlap between train and test essays could inflate reported performance and should be analyzed and acknowledged.  
- Major concern: LSTM-ER is an adaptation of prior work (Miwa & Bansal 2016) and should be clearly positioned as validation/adaptation rather than a novel architecture, with details on differences.  
- Minor concern: unclear claim that a full argument graph can be reconstructed from a tree—needs explanation of reconstruction method or scope.  
- Minor concern: confusing model comparison order and undefined acronym "STag_T" on first use; reorder for clarity.  
- Minor concern: contradictory phrasing about "decoupling" vs "jointly modeling" tasks—clarify whether tasks share backbone but have separate outputs.  
- Minor concern: ambiguous statement that the system "decouples relation info from entity info"—specify whether this means separate subnetworks or edge-attribute modeling.  
- Minor concern: paragraph- and essay-level F1 scores may not be directly comparable due to missing cross-paragraph relations in paragraph setting and should be discussed.  
- Recommendation: strong study suitable for publication pending acknowledgment/analysis of data leakage and clearer positioning of LSTM-ER adaptation, plus addressing minor clarifications.
