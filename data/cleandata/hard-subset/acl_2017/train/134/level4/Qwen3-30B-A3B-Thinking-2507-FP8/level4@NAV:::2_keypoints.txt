- Evaluates five strategies for end-to-end argumentation mining: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, sequence-labeling‑based multi-task learning, off‑the‑shelf LSTM‑ER, and ILP baselines.  
- All methods are assessed using F1 scores computed over concepts and relations.  
- Dependency‑based approaches perform poorly.  
- Sequence labeling solutions are effective.  
- The off‑the‑shelf LSTM‑ER model achieves strong results, particularly at paragraph level.  
- Both sequence labeling and LSTM‑ER models outperform the ILP approach.  
- The supplementary material thoroughly documents training procedures, hyperparameter tuning, and implementation details.  
- Multi‑task learning significantly improves sequence labeling, with the claim task yielding more benefit than the relation task.  
- Major concern: dataset construction may leak topics between train and test (identical/similar arguments), risking overly optimistic performance estimates and requiring discussion.  
- Secondary concern: the top‑performing model is an off‑the‑shelf LSTM‑ER; nevertheless, extensive experiments and insights justify publication.
