- Paper presents a comprehensive comparison of neural methods (dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER) for end-to-end argumentation mining against ILP baselines, evaluated by concept and relation F1.  
- Sequence labeling approaches, especially LSTM-ER at the paragraph level, perform strongly and outperform ILP baselines.  
- The paper includes detailed supplementary material on training, hyperparameters, and experiments.  
- Major concern: potential topic overlap between training and test essays could cause information leakage and inflate performance estimates.  
- The dataset overlap issue likely affects ILP baselines too but needs explicit acknowledgment and analysis of overlap extent.  
- Secondary concern: the top-performing LSTM-ER is largely an out-of-the-box application of existing work, reducing novelty.  
- Despite that, the extensive experiments and insights justify publication.  
- Minor point: clarify how the full argument graph is reconstructed from a tree structure (Lines 222–226).  
- Minor point: reorder presentation (dependency-based methods before sequence tagging) for better readability (Lines 443–444).  
- Minor point: rephrase or clarify the contradictory wording “de-couples them but jointly models them” and verify cited references (Line 455).  
- Minor point: clarify the description of decoupling relation information from entity information—are representations or learning processes being decoupled? (Lines 477–479).  
- Question: are paragraph and essay F1 scores directly comparable for relation tasks, given paragraph models may miss cross-paragraph relations?
