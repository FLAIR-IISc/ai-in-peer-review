- Presents a novel end-to-end argumentation mining system using neural sequence labeling and dependency parsing.  
- Strong motivation to reduce manual feature engineering and ILP constraint reliance.  
- Key strength: joint learning of argumentation subtasks to reduce pipeline error propagation.  
- Paper is well-written with clear method descriptions and substantial experimental comparisons.  
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 train, ~80 test) relative to high-capacity BiLSTM models.  
- Development set size is omitted, raising doubts about training adequacy and evaluation validity.  
- Potential overfitting given likely small number of training tags and model complexity.  
- Authors should include training and development loss curves to illuminate training behavior.  
- Report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs with different initializations to assess stability.  
- Conduct statistical significance testing to determine whether improvements are meaningful.  
- Main paper lacks detailed discussion of regularization; supplementary notes are insufficient.  
- Recommend exploring Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning instead of current approach.  
- Move key supplementary details (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) into the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize dataset quality and system performance.  
- Improve Figure 1 for black-and-white printing (use distinct colors/patterns).  
- Reviewer raised recommendation to 4 but remains concerned about model stability and F1 variance (e.g., 0.56–0.61); request reporting means and variances across settings.
