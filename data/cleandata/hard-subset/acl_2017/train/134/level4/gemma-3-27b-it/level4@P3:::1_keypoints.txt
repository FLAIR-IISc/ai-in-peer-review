- Proposes end-to-end argumentation mining using neural networks (sequence labeling and dependency parsing) and a multi-task learning variant.  
- Motivation: addresses limitations of prior ILP/manual-feature approaches and aims to reduce pipeline error propagation via joint learning.  
- Contribution: applies neural architectures (sequence tagging, dependency parsing) to end-to-end argumentation mining.  
- Contribution: compares modeling approaches and finds a simple sequence tagging model (STagBLCC) performs best.  
- Contribution: shows multi-task learning with subtasks (component detection, relation ID) improves performance.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with generally well-explained methods.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments including a strong ILP baseline and exact vs. approximate matching results.  
- Strength: notable finding that sequence tagging outperforms dependency parsing for this task.  
- Strength: error analysis improved and integrated into the main paper.  
- Weakness: small dataset (~320 essays) raises overfitting concerns; needs more rigorous analysis (e.g., train/dev loss curves).  
- Weakness: lacks stability analysis—needs mean and standard deviation across multiple runs due to observed variance.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on regularization strategies and hyperparameter tuning; consider Bayesian optimization.  
- Weakness: missing inter-annotator agreement statistics for the dataset.  
- Request: provide training and development loss curves for best models to demonstrate learning/overfitting behavior.  
- Request: report mean and standard deviation of C-F1, R-F1, and overall F1 across multiple random initializations.  
- Request: detail regularization techniques and specific hyperparameters (dropout rates, L2 strengths).  
- Request: elaborate on specific error cases and any systematic error patterns for future improvement.  
- Additional: improve Figure 1 for black-and-white printing.  
- Additional: include F1-score ranges to aid reproducibility.  
- Additional: discuss limitations of single-dataset evaluation and potential domain-adaptation challenges.
