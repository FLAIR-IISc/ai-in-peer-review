- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents interesting methods and results.  
- Proposes end-to-end argumentation mining with neural nets using sequence labeling, dependency parsing, and multitask learning.  
- Motivation is clear: avoid ILP/manual feature engineering and reduce pipeline error propagation via joint learning.  
- Methods are clearly explained with only minor missing details.  
- Experiments are substantial and comparisons rigorous; results are intriguing.  
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test) relative to high-capacity (Bi)LSTM models.  
- Development set size is not specified, which is problematic given limited training data.  
- Training likely yields only a few thousand tags (much smaller than typical sequence labeling tasks), raising doubts about adequate parameter training.  
- Potential overfitting: authors hint at this; recommend reporting training and development loss curves.  
- Model stability is uncertain; require mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and statistical significance tests.  
- Regularization discussion is insufficient and needs more thorough treatment.  
- Suggest using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning instead of current approach.  
- Move information on pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores and revise Figure 1 for black-and-white printing.  
- After author response, reviewer raised score to 4 but still requests reporting F1-score ranges and variance due to observed large variance in some settings.
