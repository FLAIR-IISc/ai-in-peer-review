- Paper proposes end-to-end argumentation mining using neural networks with two formulations: sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus multitask learning for sequence labeling.
- Clear motivation contrasting neural approaches with prior ILP/manual-feature methods and emphasizing joint learning to avoid pipeline error propagation.
- Methods are largely well-explained, with only minor missing details.
- Experiments are substantial and comparisons are appropriate; results are interesting.
- Primary concern: very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test) relative to high-capacity (Bi)LSTM models, risking overfitting.
- Development set size is not stated, which is problematic given limited training data.
- Total tag count appears to be only a few thousand, far smaller than typical sequence-labeling corpora, raising training feasibility questions.
- Paper does not adequately address overfitting risk; inclusion of training and development loss curves is recommended.
- Model stability needs more scrutiny: report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs with different initializations.
- Statistical significance tests are necessary to confirm improvements are not due to chance.
- Regularization strategies are under-discussed; stronger focus and reporting on regularization (beyond limited LSTM-ER details) is needed.
- Recommend considering Bayesian optimization or other rigorous hyperparameter tuning methods.
- Move key supplementary material (pre-trained word embedding details and error analysis) into the main text, adding roughly one page.
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset source to contextualize system performance and headroom for improvement.
- Improve Figure 1 clarity by using distinct colors for better reproduction in black-and-white print.
- Main contributions: empirical comparison of neural architectures for end-to-end argumentation mining versus ILP-based methods.
- Main contributions: identification of sequence tagging, especially BiLSTM-CRF, as a strong baseline for the task.
- Main contributions: analysis showing limitations of dependency parsing for argumentation mining in this context.
