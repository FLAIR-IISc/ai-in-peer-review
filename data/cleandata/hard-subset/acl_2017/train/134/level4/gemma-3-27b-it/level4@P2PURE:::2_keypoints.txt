- Paper presents a comprehensive neural study of argumentation mining using dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER, and ILP baselines.
- Evaluation uses F1 scores for concepts and relations across paragraph and essay settings.
- Dependency parsing–based methods perform poorly compared to other approaches.
- Sequence labeling approaches show strong performance overall.
- LSTM-ER achieves the best results, particularly at the paragraph level.
- Both sequence labeling and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP baselines.
- Sequence labeling benefits substantially from multi-task learning; the claim task contributes more than the relation task.
- Detailed supplementary material on model training and hyperparameter tuning is provided.
- Major concern: potential dataset leakage due to overlapping essay topics between training and test sets, possibly inflating performance.
- Major concern: strongest model (LSTM-ER) is largely an application of an existing model, raising novelty questions despite strong empirical results.
- Minor: lines 222–226 — claim that a full graph can be reconstructed from a tree output needs clarification.
- Minor: lines 443–444 — section order (sequence tagging, dependency-based, multi-task) is disruptive and could be improved.
- Minor: line 455 — wording claiming the model both “de-couples” and “jointly models” information is paradoxical and needs clarification.
- Minor: lines 477–479 — description of decoupling relation vs. entity information is confusing and should be rephrased.
- Minor/question: Are paragraph- and essay-level F1 scores directly comparable for relations, given paragraph models may miss cross-paragraph relations?
