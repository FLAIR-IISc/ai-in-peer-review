- Proposes a novel end-to-end neural system for argumentation mining that avoids ILP, manual features, and hand-crafted constraints.  
- Evaluates two approaches: sequence labeling and dependency parsing, and investigates multitask learning within sequence labeling.  
- Clear motivation: joint learning reduces error propagation compared to pipeline approaches.  
- Methods are generally well-explained, with only minor missing details.  
- Experiments are substantial, comparisons thorough, and results initially promising.  
- Major concern: very small dataset (≈320 training essays, 80 test), with development set size unspecified.  
- Limited number of tagged instances (likely low thousands) raises doubts about parameter estimation and generalizability.  
- Paper lacks overfitting analysis; should include training and development loss curves.  
- Model stability is questionable; must report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and conduct statistical significance tests.  
- Discussion of regularization is insufficient; supplementary material only briefly addresses it for LSTM-ER.  
- Recommend using more sophisticated hyperparameter optimization (e.g., Bayesian optimization).  
- Important details (pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis) should be moved from supplementary to the main paper.  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset paper to contextualize task difficulty.  
- Suggest figure improvements (use different colors for better black-and-white printing).  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer raised recommendation to 4 but reiterates need to report F1-score ranges, mean/variance, and address instability (e.g., Kiperwasser F1 range [0.56, 0.61]).
