- Paper proposes end-to-end neural argumentation mining using sequence labeling and dependency parsing, with a multi-task learning variant for sequence labeling.  
- Clear motivation: avoid ILP, manual feature engineering, and hand-crafted constraints by joint neural learning.  
- Methods are clearly explained with only a few minor missing details.  
- Experiments are substantial and comparisons are appropriate; results are interesting.  
- Main concern: very small dataset (≈320 train, 80 test) raises risk of undertraining given high-capacity (Bi)LSTM models.  
- Development-set size is not reported, which may further reduce available training data.  
- Total number of training tags likely only a few thousand versus typical hundreds of thousands, calling parameter estimation into question.  
- No analysis of overfitting; authors should report training and development loss curves over training.  
- Model stability is unclear; report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs with different initializations.  
- Statistical significance tests are missing and needed to assess reliability of reported improvements.  
- Regularization is not discussed adequately; supplementary material only briefly mentions it for LSTM-ER.  
- Recommend using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning instead of the current approach.  
- Move information about pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper (one extra page suggested).  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset paper to contextualize system performance and improvement potential.  
- Improve Figure 1 readability by using different colors for clarity in black-and-white prints.  
- Primary contribution: shows feasibility of neural end-to-end argumentation mining competitive with feature-engineered ILP baselines.  
- Secondary contribution: dependency-parsing framing performs poorly while sequence tagging with multi-task learning is more effective.  
- Third contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation across parsing, tagging, MTL, and LSTM-ER variants.  
- Strength: eliminates manual feature engineering while achieving competitive performance.  
- Strength: joint/multi-task learning leverages subtask relationships and improves performance.  
- Strength: thorough empirical comparison against a strong baseline.  
- Weakness: small dataset size threatens generalizability and may cause overfitting.  
- Weakness: lack of stability analysis (no mean/std, no significance tests) limits confidence in results.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and detail on regularization techniques used.  
- Questions to authors: report development set size; provide training/dev loss curves; give mean/std of F1 across runs; detail regularization techniques and settings.  
- Additional comment: clarify the observed F1 score range across runs, especially high variance for Kiperwasser, to aid reproducibility.
