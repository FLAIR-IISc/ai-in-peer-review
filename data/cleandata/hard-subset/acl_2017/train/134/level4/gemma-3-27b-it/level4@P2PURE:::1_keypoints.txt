- Presents end-to-end argumentation mining with neural nets using sequence labeling and dependency parsing; multitask learning in sequence labeling is explored.  
- Strong motivation: avoids ILP/manual features and pipeline error propagation; methods generally well-explained with minor missing details.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and comparisons appropriate; initial results are promising.  
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 train, ~80 test) and unspecified dev set size, raising doubts about adequate training data.  
- High-capacity BiLSTM models (BLC/BLCC) risk overfitting given the limited number of training tags compared to typical sequence tasks.  
- Paper lacks overfitting analysis; training and dev loss curves should be provided.  
- Authors’ claim that taggers need less data could be evidence of overfitting rather than robustness.  
- Model stability is unclear; report mean and standard deviation over multiple runs with different initializations and run statistical significance tests.  
- Provide F1-score ranges and mean/variance for different settings—current Kiperwasser F1 range [0.56, 0.61] shows large variance.  
- Regularization strategies are insufficiently detailed; discuss them explicitly beyond brief supplementary mentions (e.g., LSTM-ER).  
- Suggest Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning to improve reproducibility and performance.  
- Move key details (pre-trained word embeddings, error analysis) from supplementary material into the main paper (one extra page suggested).  
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores (cite original dataset) to contextualize system performance.  
- Improve Figure 1 visibility for black-and-white printing by using distinct colors/markers.
