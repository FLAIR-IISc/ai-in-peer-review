- Paper is clearly written and accessible.
- Methods and results are of interest and avoid manual ILP/feature engineering; joint learning reduces pipeline error propagation.
- Evaluation and resulting conclusions require careful scrutiny.
- Dataset is small (~320 training essays, 80 test); development set size is not reported.
- High-capacity (Bi)LSTM models may be poorly supported by the limited data, risking overfitting.
- No analysis of overfitting provided; training/dev loss curves are missing.
- Model robustness is uncertain; results should report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs.
- Statistical significance testing is absent and needed to validate reported improvements.
- Regularization discussion is inadequate and largely relegated to supplementary material.
- Recommend using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning.
- Move details from supplementary (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) into the main paper.
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores from the original dataset to contextualize system performance.
- Revise Figure 1 to use different colors for better grayscale reproduction.
- Report F1-score ranges and mean/variance across runs (reviewer notes substantial variance, e.g., [0.56, 0.61] in one setting).
