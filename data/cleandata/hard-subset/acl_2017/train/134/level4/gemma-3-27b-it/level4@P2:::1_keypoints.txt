- Paper frames end-to-end computational argumentation mining (AM) as token-based dependency parsing and sequence tagging, with multi-task learning experiments.  
- Strong motivation: current AM relies on hand-crafted features and ILP, which are brittle and data-dependent.  
- Primary contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation of neural architectures for end-to-end AM.  
- Key finding: dependency parsing formulation is ineffective for AM.  
- Key finding: sequence tagging, especially BiLSTMs, yields robust performance.  
- Key finding: auxiliary tasks via multi-task learning improve performance.  
- Secondary contribution: neural methods achieve competitive performance with a state-of-the-art ILP baseline, removing need for manual feature engineering.  
- Strength: elimination of feature engineering simplifies adaptation to new datasets and domains.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of results, including limitations of parsing and benefits of multi-task learning and distance encoding.  
- Weakness: small dataset (~320 training essays) raises overfitting concerns without sufficient analysis.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported, weakening confidence in result differences.  
- Weakness: limited details on regularization techniques, hindering reproducibility and assessment of overfitting mitigation.  
- Weakness: model stability concerns—high variance across runs (e.g., dependency parser F1 range [0.56, 0.61]).  
- Weakness: missing inter-annotator agreement scores for the dataset, limiting interpretation of task difficulty.  
- Question: provide training and development loss curves for sequence tagging and LSTM-ER to assess learning and overfitting.  
- Question: perform statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-test or Wilcoxon) to confirm observed differences.  
- Question: elaborate on regularization used for each model and justify choices.  
- Question: report mean and variance of metrics across multiple runs with different random seeds, especially for high-variance models.  
- Question: state the size of the development set used for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Additional comment: include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize dataset quality.  
- Additional comment: move error analysis and pre-trained embedding details from supplementary material into the main paper.  
- Additional comment: consider Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Additional comment: clarify figure 1 with more distinct colors for better readability.
