- Investigates neural approaches to end-to-end argumentation mining, comparing dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER.
- Uses rigorous evaluation with standard F1 metrics and provides comprehensive supplementary material and hyperparameter details.
- Key finding: dependency-based solutions struggle, while sequence labeling and especially LSTM-ER achieve strong performance, outperforming an ILP baseline.
- Multi-task learning yields improvements, with component detection a more effective auxiliary task than relation detection.
- Contribution: shows neural models (sequence labeling, LSTM-ER) can achieve state-of-the-art results on the PE dataset, reducing reliance on hand-crafted features and ILP.
- Contribution: offers a comparative analysis of different neural framings for AM, highlighting strengths and weaknesses of each.
- Contribution: provides empirical guidance on multi-task learning for AM, recommending component detection as a beneficial auxiliary task.
- Strength: comprehensive, well-documented experimental evaluation with detailed results for multiple models and configurations.
- Strength: clear and insightful analysis that explains observed trends and limitations.
- Strength: strong baseline comparison demonstrating neural approaches outperform feature-based ILP.
- Strength: practical insights for future research regarding framing choices and multi-task setups.
- Strength: paper is generally well-written and organized.
- Weakness: potential topic overlap between training and test sets could cause data contamination and overly optimistic results.
- Weakness: limited novelty because LSTM-ER is adapted from prior work, though experimental contributions remain valuable.
- Weakness: some terminology and explanations are unclear (e.g., “decoupling” relation/entity info; reconstructing a graph from a tree).
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of paragraph- vs. essay-level performance differences and risks of missing cross-paragraph relations.
- Question: please clarify the extent of topic overlap between train and test and whether its impact was assessed.
- Question: explain which aspects of LSTM-ER’s architecture or training lead to its strong performance in AM.
- Question: clarify the meaning of “decoupling” relation information from entity information with a concrete example.
- Question: discuss limitations of paragraph-level models for capturing cross-paragraph relations and plans to address this.
- Additional comment: clarify lines 222–226 on reconstructing the full graph from a tree.
- Additional comment: consider reordering sequence tagging and dependency parsing for better flow (lines 443–444).
- Additional comment: define “coupling” given prior assertions of decoupling (line 455).
- Additional comment: rephrase the decoupling explanation for clarity (lines 477–479).
- Additional comment: provide direct comparison of relation F1 scores at paragraph and essay levels to assess document-length impact.
