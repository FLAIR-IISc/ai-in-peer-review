- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with interesting and promising methods and results.
- Presents end-to-end argumentation mining using neural nets, comparing sequence labeling, dependency parsing, and multitask sequence labeling.
- Clear motivation: avoids ILP/manual feature engineering and error propagation of pipeline methods by joint learning.
- Methods are clearly explained with only a few minor missing details.
- Experiments are comprehensive and comparisons are well-conducted; results are intriguing.
- Major concern: very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test) and unspecified development set size.
- Small data vs. high-capacity (Bi)LSTM models raises doubts about adequate parameter training and risk of overfitting.
- Paper lacks overfitting analysis (e.g., training/dev loss curves) and regularization details.
- Model stability is unclear; authors should report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and perform statistical significance tests.
- Suggest using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning instead of current approach.
- Move pre-trained word embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material into the main paper.
- Include inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset paper to contextualize system performance.
- Improve Figure 1 colors for black-and-white printing clarity.
- After author response, reviewer raised score to 4 and requests reporting F1-score ranges and mean/variance across settings to address observed large variances (e.g., Kiperwasser).
