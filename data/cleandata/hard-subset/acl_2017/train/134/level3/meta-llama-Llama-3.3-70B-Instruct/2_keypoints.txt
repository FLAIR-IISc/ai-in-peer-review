- Introduces the first neural end-to-end solutions for computational argumentation mining.  
- Demonstrates several neural approaches outperform the previous joint ILP state-of-the-art.  
- Shows token-based dependency parsing framing is ineffective for argumentation mining.  
- Finds standard neural sequence tagging models are robust across environments.  
- Uses multi-task learning to improve performance.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the PE dataset.  
- Sequence labeling and out-of-the-box LSTM-ER models perform particularly well, especially at paragraph level.  
- Supplement provides detailed training and optimization technicalities.  
- Potential risk of information leakage between train and test sets due to similar topics/arguments.  
- Out-of-the-box LSTM-ER approach may lack novelty.  
- Some sections need clearer explanations (model ordering; decoupling relation vs. entity information).  
- Requests for clarification: F1 comparisons between paragraph and essay settings, impact of multi-task learning on sequence labeling, relation to prior work and future research implications.  
- Overall: significant and well-written contribution with minor issues.
