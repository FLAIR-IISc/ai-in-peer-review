- Comprehensive study of neural end-to-end approaches for computational argumentation mining (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning); BLCC excels at component detection, LSTM-ER at component identification but struggles with relation ID on long documents.
- Proposes neural end-to-end solutions for AM, removing need for manual feature engineering and ILP constraints.
- Demonstrates BiLSTM taggers perform well for component detection.
- Finds naive coupling of component and relation detection is suboptimal; tasks should be treated separately but modeled jointly.
- Thorough evaluation of different framings, clarifying strengths and weaknesses of each approach.
- Uses a robust evaluation metric that accounts for both component and relation identification.
- Compares to a feature-based ILP baseline, showing effectiveness of neural end-to-end approaches.
- Small dataset size may limit generalizability to the full AM domain.
- Lacks analysis of overfitting despite large model capacity.
- Missing discussion of regularization methods to mitigate overfitting.
- Suggests moving key info (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) from supplement to main paper for clarity.
- Recommends including inter-annotator agreement scores to assess annotation quality.
- Advises improving figure print quality (use different colors).
- Requests more detailed performance analysis (means, variances, F1-score ranges) to address model stability concerns.
- Overall: significant contribution to AM that could be strong with revisions.
- Question: How will you address overfitting given the large neural networks?
- Question: Provide more details on the hyperparameter optimization process.
- Question: How would results change with a larger dataset and how would you collect and annotate it?
