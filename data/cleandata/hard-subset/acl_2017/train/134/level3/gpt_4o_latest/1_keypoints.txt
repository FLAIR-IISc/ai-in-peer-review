- Proposes neural end-to-end argumentation mining frameworks that remove manual feature engineering and ILP constraints.  
- Introduces sequence tagging with distance encoding (STagT) to jointly encode component and relation information.  
- Finds that naive coupling of component and relation detection is suboptimal; separate but jointly modeled tasks work better.  
- Provides extensive empirical evaluation comparing neural models to ILP-based baselines on paragraph- and essay-level data.  
- Clear, well-written presentation that makes methods and results accessible.  
- Tackles a relevant and timely problem by addressing limitations of pipeline approaches like error propagation.  
- Comprehensive experiments with sensible baselines and insights on trade-offs across model framings and document lengths.  
- Offers practical recommendations: prefer simpler sequence tagging for long texts and use multi-task learning for subtasks.  
- Uses a small dataset (320 training essays), raising serious overfitting concerns absent loss-curve analysis.  
- Lacks analysis of model stability: no multiple runs, standard deviations, or statistical significance testing reported.  
- Insufficient discussion of regularization techniques (e.g., dropout, weight decay) given dataset size.  
- Hyperparameter optimization methods are not detailed; alternatives like Bayesian optimization or grid search were not explored.  
- Key details (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) relegated to supplementary material instead of the main paper.  
- Requests clarification on how overfitting was handled and asks for training/validation loss curves.  
- Asks which regularization techniques were applied to improve generalization.  
- Seeks elaboration on hyperparameter optimization choices and their impact.  
- Requests inclusion of statistical significance tests in the final version.  
- Suggests adding inter-annotator agreement scores to strengthen dataset evaluation.  
- Recommends revising Figure 1 for better black-and-white readability.  
- Advises reporting F1-score means, ranges, and variances to improve reproducibility and robustness.  
- Concludes the paper makes significant contributions but needs to address dataset size, overfitting, and model stability to increase impact and reliability.
