- Neural approaches for end-to-end argumentation mining (AM) are evaluated across five methods: dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multitask learning (MTL), LSTM-ER, and an ILP-based baseline.  
- Key contribution: sequence labeling and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP-based state-of-the-art, eliminating reliance on hand-crafted features and constraints.  
- Key contribution: token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for AM, contrasting with its success in coarser-grained systems.  
- Key contribution: multitask learning notably improves sequence labeling, especially with auxiliary tasks like claim detection.  
- Key contribution: provides a comprehensive evaluation with detailed experimental setups and hyperparameter optimization for reproducibility.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation using F1 for components and relations, with both paragraph- and essay-level analyses.  
- Strength: novel insight that sequence labeling and LSTM-ER are more effective and that LSTM-ER is robust at paragraph level.  
- Strength: extensive supplementary material detailing training and hyperparameter tuning, supporting reproducibility.  
- Strength: offers practical recommendations for task framing, including decoupling component and relation detection and guidance on short vs. long documents.  
- Weakness: potential data leakage from topic overlap between training and test sets threatens the validity of results.  
- Weakness: some methodological explanations (tree-to-graph reconstruction, decoupling relations/entities) lack clarity.  
- Weakness: presentation order of approaches could be improved for readability and logical flow.  
- Weakness: LSTM-ER is an off-the-shelf model, raising questions about the novelty of the contribution.  
- Weakness: paper does not address whether paragraph-based models miss cross-paragraph relations.  
- Question: please clarify how potential data leakage between training and test sets was addressed.  
- Question: how could the proposed models be adapted to capture cross-paragraph relations for essay-level tasks?  
- Question: what specific adaptations or innovations in your LSTM-ER implementation explain its success in this context?  
- Recommendation: recommend publication pending resolution of clarity issues and mitigation/clarification of data leakage concerns.
