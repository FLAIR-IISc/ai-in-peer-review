- Main contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation of neural architectures for end-to-end argumentation mining, showing sequence tagging is effective.
- Negative result: framing AM as dependency parsing is ineffective for this task.
- Multi-task learning insight: adding component detection as an auxiliary task improves performance.
- Strong empirical finding: neural sequence-tagging models and adapted LSTM-ER outperform a strong feature-engineered ILP baseline, especially at paragraph level.
- Thorough experimental setup: consistent dataset/metrics, hyperparameter details, and code links enhance reproducibility.
- Strength: systematic comparison of dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER provides valuable guidance for AM modeling.
- Strength: eliminates need for heavy feature engineering by showing neural methods surpass ILP baseline.
- Strength: negative result about dependency parsing challenges intuitive modeling choices and informs future work.
- Strength: empirical evidence that component detection is a more useful auxiliary task than relation detection.
- Weakness: limited novelty in LSTM-ER adaptation — mainly leveraging an existing model rather than proposing a novel architecture.
- Weakness: potential data leakage due to topic overlap in the student essay dataset could inflate performance estimates.
- Weakness: F1 metric may not capture structural/typing errors (e.g., mislabeling claim vs. premise) important for downstream tasks.
- Weakness: LSTM-ER complexity hinders interpretability; lacks detailed error analysis to explain why it succeeds or fails.
- Question: please clarify steps taken to mitigate topic overlap between training and test essays (data leakage).
- Question: clarify comparability of paragraph vs. essay F1 scores and whether paragraph models miss cross-paragraph relations.
- Question: explain what is meant by “de-couples them but jointly models them” regarding relation vs. entity information and how LSTM-ER implements this.
- Question: clarify the loss-less re-linking procedure that reconstructs claim-to-major-claim trees with an illustrative example.
- Additional comment: consider reordering sections to present sequence tagging before dependency parsing for better flow.
- Additional comment: improve transitional phrasing in dependency parsing section where design decisions are introduced.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
