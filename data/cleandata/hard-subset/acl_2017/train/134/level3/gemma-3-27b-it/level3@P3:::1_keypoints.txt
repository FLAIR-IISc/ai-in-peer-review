- Primary contribution: explores end-to-end neural models for argumentation mining, showing sequence tagging (with multitask learning) outperforms token-based dependency parsing.  
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates token-based dependency parsing is ineffective for end-to-end AM, contrary to prior suggestions.  
- Empirical contribution: provides a comprehensive evaluation of multiple models (dependency parsers, BLCC sequence taggers, LSTM-ER, ILP baseline) on a challenging dataset.  
- Strength: paper is very clear and well-written, with a well-motivated problem statement and contextualization.  
- Strength: experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing a wide range of models and baselines.  
- Strength: findings are insightful—sequence tagging and multitask learning improve AM performance.  
- Strength: motivation to avoid pipeline errors and manual feature engineering is strong and convincing.  
- Strength: multitask learning (component detection + relation identification) is a valuable explored strategy.  
- Weakness: dataset is very small (~320 train, ~80 test), limiting generalizability and raising overfitting concerns.  
- Weakness: high-capacity Bi(LSTM) models may be too complex for the limited data, risking memorization.  
- Weakness: no overfitting analysis provided (missing training/dev loss curves).  
- Weakness: results reported as single-point estimates without variance (no mean/std over runs).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of regularization techniques and hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning procedure appears suboptimal; Bayesian optimization recommended.  
- Request: provide training and development loss curves for all models to assess overfitting.  
- Request: report mean and standard deviation of F1 across multiple random initializations and conduct statistical significance tests.  
- Request: provide detailed description of all regularization methods and their hyperparameters.  
- Request: report full F1 score ranges across runs (notably Kiperwasser’s large range [0.56,0.61]).  
- Request: state the development set size used for tuning and model selection.  
- Additional: move pre-trained embedding details from supplement to main paper.  
- Additional: include inter-annotator agreement from the original dataset paper for context.  
- Additional: improve Figure 1 contrast for black-and-white printing.  
- Additional: include a more detailed error analysis with specific misclassification examples.
