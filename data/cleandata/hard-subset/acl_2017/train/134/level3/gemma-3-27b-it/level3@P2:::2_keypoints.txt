- Paper evaluates end-to-end neural AM framed as dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER.  
- Main finding: dependency parsing is ineffective; sequence tagging and LSTM-ER perform strongly and beat an ILP baseline; multi-task learning boosts sequence tagging.  
- Primary contribution: a systematic empirical comparison of multiple neural framings for argumentation mining.  
- Secondary contribution: simple BiLSTM-CRF variants (especially with MTL) achieve competitive performance, suggesting complex architectures may be unnecessary.  
- Strong empirical results across paragraph and essay levels demonstrate viability of end-to-end neural AM.  
- Comprehensive evaluation includes a broad set of architectures, LSTM-ER as a comparator, and comparison to dependency parsing methods.  
- Insightful analysis explains dependency parsing failures, benefits of MTL, trade-offs between modularity and constraints, and difficulties of relation ID with longer sequences.  
- Well-documented experiments and supplementary material improve reproducibility and credibility.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in applying off-the-shelf LSTM-ER without major architectural innovation.  
- Weakness: potential data leakage from persuasive-essays dataset not discussed or mitigated.  
- Weakness: large performance drop from paragraph- to essay-level (especially for dependency parsing and LSTM-ER) needs deeper error analysis.  
- Weakness: ambiguous phrasing (e.g., “de-couples them but jointly models them”) requires clearer explanation.  
- Question: were steps taken to detect or prevent data leakage across essays in train/test splits?  
- Question: which LSTM-ER architectural choices or hyperparameters were most critical for performance?  
- Question: how is the global F1 score computed—simple average or weighted combination, and why?  
- Question: are paragraph- and essay-level F1 scores directly comparable, and do paragraph models capture cross-paragraph relations?  
- Suggestion: reorder sections (present sequence tagging before dependency parsing) to improve flow.  
- Suggestion: clarify how the dependency parsing tree output reconstructs the full graph structure.  
- Suggestion: expand the discussion (lines 222–226) with a small example illustrating lossless relinking of claims to major claims.
