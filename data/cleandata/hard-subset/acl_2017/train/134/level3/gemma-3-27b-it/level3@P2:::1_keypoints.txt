- Explores neural end-to-end argumentation mining, moving beyond feature-engineered and pipeline approaches.  
- Shows sequence tagging models outperform dependency parsing for end-to-end AM.  
- Finds multi-task learning with auxiliary component-detection tasks improves performance.  

- Clearly formulates the problem and motivates end-to-end neural approaches over pipelines.  
- Provides a comprehensive experimental comparison of dependency parsing, sequence tagging, multi-task learning, and a strong ILP baseline.  
- Reports the surprising result that dependency parsing performs poorly while simpler tagging models succeed.  
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible, with clear model explanations.  

- Dataset is small (~320 train, ~80 test) and lacks a dedicated development set, limiting generalization and risking overfitting.  
- No analysis of overfitting (e.g., training/dev loss curves) is provided.  
- Results lack sufficient statistical reporting (means, stddevs across runs) and significance testing.  
- Discussion of regularization is limited and moved to supplementary material despite its importance given small data.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details are insufficient; more information on search space and process is needed.  

- Provide training and development loss curves to demonstrate (or rule out) overfitting.  
- Report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and conduct statistical significance tests.  
- Specify the regularization techniques used and how regularization hyperparameters were tuned.  
- Report dataset inter-annotator agreement to contextualize performance levels.  
- Provide a more detailed error analysis of model-specific failure modes.  

- Improve color/contrast of Figure 1 for black-and-white printing.  
- Move details on pre-trained embeddings and error analysis from supplementary material to the main paper.  
- Report F1-score means and variance ranges for results with high observed variance.  

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the above concerns.
