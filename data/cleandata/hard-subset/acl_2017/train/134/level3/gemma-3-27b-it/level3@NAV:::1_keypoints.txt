- Frames AM as token-based dependency parsing and sequence tagging with a multi-task extension.  
- Clear, compelling motivation to avoid pipeline methods and manual feature engineering.  
- Methods are generally well-explained but some implementation/details are missing.  
- Experimental setup is substantial and includes a strong ILP baseline comparison.  
- Key empirical finding: dependency parsing performs surprisingly poorly compared to sequence tagging.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive empirical comparison of neural framings and useful insight about parsing’s ineffectiveness; multi-task learning shown beneficial.  
- Critical weakness: very small dataset (~320 training, ~80 test) threatens generalizability and risks overfitting given large BiLSTM models.  
- Missing overfitting analysis: no training/development loss curves provided.  
- Results reported as single runs with no mean/standard deviation or statistical significance testing.  
- Regularization details are omitted (only briefly mentioned in supplement); hyperparameter tuning procedure is not described.  
- Suggested fixes: add overfitting analysis, report stability stats across runs, justify/describe regularization, consider Bayesian optimization for hyperparameters, move embeddings and error analysis into main text, and include inter-annotator agreement.  
- Minor suggestion: improve contrast in Figure 1 for black-and-white readability.  
- Questions for authors: what steps mitigated overfitting (provide loss curves); report mean/std and significance across runs; detail hyperparameter tuning and rationale (why not Bayesian optimization); clarify regularization choices.
