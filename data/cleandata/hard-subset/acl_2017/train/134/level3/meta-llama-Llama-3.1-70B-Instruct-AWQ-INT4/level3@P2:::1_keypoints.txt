- Paper proposes end-to-end argumentation mining with neural networks using two approaches (sequence labeling and dependency parsing) plus a multitask variant.  
- Clear motivation for joint learning presented (avoids ILP/manual features and pipeline error propagation).  
- Methods are well-explained and the results are interesting.  

- Main contributions: (1) end-to-end neural argumentation mining, (2) two modeling approaches + multitask variant, (3) substantial experiments and comparisons.  

- Strength: well-written and easy to understand.  
- Strength: methods and results are novel and interesting.  
- Strength: clear rationale for joint learning and novelty of neural end-to-end approach.  

- Weakness: very small dataset (~320 training essays, ~80 test essays) and development set size not reported.  
- Weakness: large-capacity (Bi)LSTM models may be too big for the limited data, risking overfitting.  
- Weakness: training tags likely only a few thousand, causing data sparsity compared to typical sequence tasks.  
- Weakness: no analysis of overfitting and no training/dev loss curves provided.  
- Weakness: model stability not assessed—no mean/std across runs or statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: regularization methods not discussed.  
- Weakness: LSTM-ER only briefly mentioned in supplementary material without proper treatment.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning method in supplement is suboptimal; Bayesian optimization recommended.  
- Weakness: pre-trained embeddings details and error analysis relegated to supplement.  
- Weakness: Figure 1 not optimized for black-and-white printing.  

- Suggested improvement: report experimental details including development set size and number of training tags.  
- Suggested improvement: analyze overfitting and include training/development loss curves.  
- Suggested improvement: assess model stability via mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and run statistical significance tests.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss and apply regularization methods and provide full treatment of LSTM-ER.  
- Suggested improvement: use Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Suggested improvement: move pre-trained embeddings details and error analysis into the main paper.  
- Suggested improvement: include inter-annotator agreement scores from the dataset paper to contextualize performance.  
- Suggested improvement: improve Figure 1 for black-and-white readability.  

- Questions to authors: provide development set size and number of training tags.  
- Questions to authors: how will you address potential overfitting given large models and limited data?  
- Questions to authors: can you provide training/development loss curves?  
- Questions to authors: how do you assess model stability—can you report mean/std across runs and significance tests?  
- Questions to authors: discuss regularization methods and provide proper treatment of LSTM-ER.  
- Questions to authors: why choose the current hyperparameter tuning method and will you consider Bayesian optimization?  
- Questions to authors: will you move pre-trained embeddings details and error analysis from supplement to main paper?  
- Questions to authors: can you include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance?  
- Questions to authors: can you improve Figure 1 for black-and-white printing?  

- Post-response requests: include F1-score ranges and report mean and variance for different settings.  
- Post-response requests: explain how you will address high variance (e.g., Kiperwasser setting F1 in [0.56, 0.61]) to aid replication and assess stability.
