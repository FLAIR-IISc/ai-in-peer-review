- Proposes neural end-to-end solutions for argumentation mining, framing the task as dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning.  
- Evaluates several neural architectures (BiLSTM-CRF, BiLSTM-CRF-CNN, LSTM-ER) and compares them to a feature-based ILP model.  
- Finds the sequence tagging approach with BiLSTM-CRF-CNN achieves the best performance.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains methods and results.  
- Novel framing of AM problems (parsing, tagging, multi-task) is a contribution.  
- Provides a thorough empirical comparison of multiple neural architectures against an ILP baseline.  
- Results offer insights into strengths and weaknesses of different AM approaches.  
- Evaluation dataset is small (320 training essays, 80 test essays), raising representativeness concerns.  
- No detailed analysis of overfitting; training/development loss curves are absent.  
- Regularization methods are not thoroughly discussed; LSTM-ER is only briefly mentioned in supplementary material.  
- Model stability is not analyzed; means and standard deviations across multiple runs are missing.  
- Requests more details on the evaluation dataset (topic distribution, annotation process).  
- Asks how overfitting was addressed and which regularization methods were used.  
- Requests full details on the LSTM-ER model architecture and training procedure.  
- Asks for model stability evaluation and results.  
- Suggests adding detailed error analysis and deeper discussion of approach-specific strengths/weaknesses.  
- Recommends using stronger regularization techniques (e.g., dropout, weight decay).  
- Advises a fuller discussion of the implications of the results for AM and related applications.
