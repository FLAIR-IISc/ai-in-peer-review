- Presents neural end-to-end approaches to argumentation mining (dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, LSTM-ER) evaluated on a persuasive-essays dataset.  
- Uses concept and relation F1 as metrics; sequence labeling works well while dependency-based solutions perform poorly.  
- LSTM-ER yields strong performance, particularly at the paragraph level, and outperforms the ILP state-of-the-art.  
- Multi-task learning improves sequence labeling performance.  
- Contribution: first neural end-to-end solutions for computational AM, removing manual feature engineering and ILP constraint design.  
- Contribution: shows sequence labeling is an effective paradigm for AM.  
- Contribution: demonstrates LSTM-ER’s strong results, especially for paragraph-level evaluation.  
- Contribution: demonstrates multi-task learning benefits for sequence labeling.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across multiple approaches giving insight into method trade-offs.  
- Strength: detailed analysis including paragraph-level vs. essay-level performance distinctions.  
- Strength: establishes the effectiveness of sequence labeling for AM as a significant finding.  
- Strength: highlights multi-task learning as a useful direction for future work.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on existing models/techniques, with LSTM-ER largely an out-of-the-box application.  
- Weakness: unclear explanation of how a tree output can be used to reconstruct the full argumentation graph.  
- Weakness: potential data leakage not addressed (train/test essays may share topics/arguments).  
- Weakness: lacks a clear side-by-side comparison of F1 scores across paragraph and essay settings.  
- Question: clarify how tree outputs are converted to full graphs (lines 222–226).  
- Question: how is potential data leakage between train and test essays handled?  
- Question: provide a clear comparison and discussion of F1 scores for paragraph vs. essay evaluations.  
- Question: how will the approach be extended to handle more complex, real-world argumentation structures?
