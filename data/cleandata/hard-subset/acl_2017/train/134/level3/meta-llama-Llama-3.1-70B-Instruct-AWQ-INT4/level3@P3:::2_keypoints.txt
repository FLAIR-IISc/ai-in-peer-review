- Paper studies neural end-to-end approaches for computational argumentation mining (AM), exploring dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER.  
- Main contribution: investigation of neural techniques for end-to-end AM.  
- Several neural approaches outperform the prior state-of-the-art joint ILP model.  
- Framing AM as token-based dependency parsing is ineffective.  
- Standard neural sequence tagging model is robust across settings.  
- Multi-task learning setup yields improved performance.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains methods and results.  
- Comprehensive evaluation and detailed analysis of strengths, weaknesses, and hyperparameter effects.  
- Uses F1 score as a robust evaluation metric for fair comparison.  
- Unclear how LSTM-ER is adapted for the PE dataset’s argumentative entity and relation types.  
- Concern about possible data leakage if train and test essays share topics or arguments, potentially inflating results.  
- Question: provide details on LSTM-ER adaptation for PE (premise/claim/major claim and relation types).  
- Question: how are train/test splits ensured to be disjoint to prevent data leakage?  
- Question: clarify the phrase “de-couples them but jointly models them” regarding relation vs entity information.  
- Question: are F1 scores across paragraph and essay settings comparable, and do paragraph models miss cross-paragraph relations?
