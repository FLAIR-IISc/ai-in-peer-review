- Paper proposes novel end-to-end neural approaches to argumentation mining: sequence labeling, dependency parsing, and a multi-task variant.
- Clear motivation: use neural nets to avoid manual feature engineering and pipeline error propagation via joint learning.
- Methods are generally well explained, though a few implementation details are missing.
- Experiments are substantial and include proper baselines and comparisons; results are interesting.
- Dataset is very small (~320 training essays, ~80 test essays); development set size is not reported.
- Small data size raises concerns about model–data mismatch and overfitting.
- Models use large-capacity (Bi)LSTMs (BLC/BLCC) which may be too large for the limited data.
- Training tags appear to be only a few thousand, far less than typical sequence-task scales.
- No analysis of overfitting presented (e.g., training/dev loss curves are missing).
- Paper does not report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs or run statistical significance tests.
- Omission of discussion on regularization methods and limited treatment of LSTM-ER in the supplement.
- Hyperparameter tuning method in supplement could be improved (suggestion: Bayesian optimization).
- Supplementary material contains important details (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis) that should be moved to the main paper.
- Recommend reporting inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance.
- Figure 1 should be improved for better black-and-white printing readability.
- Post-response should include F1-score ranges and report mean/variance across settings to address high variance and aid replication.
- Main claimed contributions: end-to-end neural AM methods, justification for joint learning over feature engineering/ILP, and substantial experiments with interesting results.
- Main weaknesses summarized: small dataset and model–data mismatch, no overfitting analysis, lack of regularization/tuning discussion, and missing run variability/statistical testing.
- Questions for authors: report development set size and usage, describe steps taken to mitigate overfitting, detail regularization methods, explain hyperparameter selection/tuning, and provide inter-annotator agreement scores.
