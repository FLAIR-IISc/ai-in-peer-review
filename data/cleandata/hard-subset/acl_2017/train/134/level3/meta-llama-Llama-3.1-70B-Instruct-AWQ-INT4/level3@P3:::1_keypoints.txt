- Paper presents a comprehensive study of neural end-to-end learning for computational argumentation mining (AM), proposing sequence tagging, dependency parsing, and multi-task framings.  
- The manuscript is well-written and accessible to a broad audience.  
- Main contribution: end-to-end neural AM that removes manual feature engineering and ILP constraint design.  
- Neural models perform better than or comparable to a competing feature-based ILP formulation.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, thorough evaluation of multiple model variants, and detailed result discussion.  
- Dataset is small (~320 training essays, ~80 test essays), limiting generalizability.  
- Development set size is not reported, hindering assessment of model selection and validation.  
- No training/development loss curves provided, preventing analysis of overfitting.  
- Large-capacity (Bi)LSTM models (BLC/BLCC) may be too large for the limited data, risking poor training.  
- Paper lacks discussion of regularization methods and their impact on preventing overfitting.  
- LSTM-ER is mentioned only in the supplementary material and not sufficiently addressed in the main paper.  
- Evaluation uses F1 but omits F1-score ranges and mean/variance, reducing replicability and stability assessment.  
- Results: sequence tagging model (STagBLCC) outperforms dependency parsing (LSTM-Parser) at paragraph and essay levels.  
- Multi-task model (STagBL) benefits from auxiliary "C" and "R" tasks and performs well.  
- LSTM-ER performs well on component identification but poorly on relation identification at the essay level.  
- Recommendation: report dataset details including development set size and preprocessing steps.  
- Recommendation: include training and development loss curves to analyze overfitting.  
- Recommendation: discuss and report regularization strategies and their effects.  
- Recommendation: provide F1-score ranges and report mean/variance across runs for stability.  
- Recommendation: consider Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning rather than the current supplement-described method.  
- Recommendation: move pretrained embedding details and error analysis from supplementary material to the main paper.  
- Recommendation: include inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize system performance.  
- Recommendation: improve Figure 1’s color/contrast for black-and-white printing.  
- Contributions summarized: proposal of end-to-end neural AM, empirical demonstration vs ILP, and introduction of a multi-task AM framework.  
- Acceptance arguments: fills a gap with a comprehensive neural AM study, shows competitive/better performance vs ILP, and provides thorough comparative evaluation.  
- Main remaining weaknesses reiterated: limited dataset size, insufficient regularization discussion, and limited overfitting analysis.  
- Questions for authors: how will the limited dataset issue be addressed?  
- Questions for authors: what regularization methods were used and can you provide details?  
- Questions for authors: how will overfitting be analyzed and prevented, and what measures will be taken?
