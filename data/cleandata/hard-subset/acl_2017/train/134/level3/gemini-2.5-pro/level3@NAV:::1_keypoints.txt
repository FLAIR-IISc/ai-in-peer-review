- Investigates neural end-to-end models for computational argumentation mining using token-based sequence labeling and dependency parsing, plus an MTL variant.  
- Key finding: simple BiLSTM-based sequence tagging (distance-based tags) is more robust and outperforms dependency parsing; MTL gives additional gains.  
- Neural models outperform a feature-based ILP state-of-the-art baseline.  
- Contribution: systematic comparison of neural end-to-end frameworks on a standard dataset, clarifying which architectures work best.  
- Contribution: shows a relatively simple sequence tagging model can beat more structurally complex models, especially on small data.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly motivates end-to-end joint learning over pipeline/manual feature approaches.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple architectures and a strong baseline.  
- Strength: results are insightful—dependency parsing fails despite intuitive appeal, tagging succeeds—informing model design choices.  
- Weakness: very small dataset (~320 training essays) raises overfitting and generalization concerns, undermining reliability.  
- Weakness: lacks statistical rigor and stability analysis—no mean/std over multiple runs or significance testing and no training/dev curves.  
- Weakness: important experimental details missing or in supplementary material (regularization, pretrained embeddings, inter-annotator agreement).  
- Question: please provide training and development loss/F1 curves to analyze overfitting.  
- Question: please report mean and variance of F1 over multiple runs (e.g., 5 random seeds) for main models to assess stability.
