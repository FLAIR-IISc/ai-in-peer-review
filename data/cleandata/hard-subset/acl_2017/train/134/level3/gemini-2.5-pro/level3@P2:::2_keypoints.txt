- Paper presents a comprehensive empirical study framing full argumentation mining (component segmentation, typing, relation ID) as a joint end-to-end neural learning problem.  
- Authors compare multiple neural paradigms: token-level dependency parsing, sequence tagging with a rich label set (STag), multi-task-enhanced tagging, and an adapted LSTM-ER joint entity-relation model.  
- Experiments on the persuasive essays dataset show token-level dependency parsing performs poorly while sequence tagging (STag) and LSTM-ER outperform the prior ILP-based state-of-the-art.  
- Contribution: first systematic comparison of diverse neural architectures for end-to-end argumentation mining, establishing strong empirical baselines.  
- Contribution: novel sequence tagging formulation (STag) encoding boundaries, types, relation targets (relative distance), and relation types in a unified label.  
- Contribution: evidence that neural end-to-end models surpass feature-engineered ILP systems, advocating representation learning over manual constraints.  
- Strength: very thorough evaluation covering multiple parsers, BiLSTM-CRF-CNN tagger, multi-task variant, and joint entity-relation model.  
- Strength: strong empirical results with insightful analysis (e.g., why LSTM-ER degrades at essay level due to quadratic candidate pairs).  
- Strength: multi-task learning demonstrably improves performance by using natural subtasks as auxiliary objectives.  
- Strength: high reproducibility—detailed experimental setup and hyperparameter info provided.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty since a top-performing model (LSTM-ER) is a direct application of prior work, making the paper more analysis-focused.  
- Weakness: potential data leakage—no discussion of essay topic distribution across train/test splits, risking topic-specific overfitting.  
- Weakness: some presentation/clarity issues (confusing phrasing about LSTM-ER and suboptimal section ordering).  
- Question: please clarify whether essay topics overlap between train and test splits and how that might affect reported performance.  
- Question: how is the original multi-head argumentation graph reconstructed from the parser’s single-head-per-token tree output for evaluation?  
- Question: please clarify what is meant by LSTM-ER “de-couples entity and relation detection but jointly models them” (architecture and joint loss details).  
- Question: do relations span paragraphs in the dataset, and are paragraph- and essay-level evaluations directly comparable given potential cross-paragraph relations?
