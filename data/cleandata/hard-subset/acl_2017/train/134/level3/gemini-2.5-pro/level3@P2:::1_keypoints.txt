- Empirical study comparing end-to-end neural framings (parsing, tagging, MTL) for argumentation mining.
- Key finding: token-level dependency parsing is ill-suited; a BiLSTM sequence tagger encoding relational distance achieves strong, state-of-the-art results.
- Multi-task learning improves performance by jointly training full AM task with component and relation identification as auxiliary tasks.
- Clear, well-written presentation with intuitive explanations, helpful examples, and informative figures.
- Rigorous, comprehensive experiments comparing multiple parsers, taggers, and a strong feature-based ILP baseline across paragraph and essay levels.
- Novel and impactful insights: negative result for parsing, success of the STagBLCC labeling scheme, and useful analysis contrasting LSTM-ER and STagBLCC.
- High risk of overfitting given large-capacity models trained on a small PE dataset; no learning curves provided.
- Lack of robustness analysis: results appear from single runs and need mean/std over multiple random seeds due to potential instability.
- Insufficient detail on regularization methods and hyperparameter values in the main text (dropout, L2, early stopping).
- Missing evaluation context: inter-annotator agreement (IAA) from the dataset is not reported to contextualize performance.
- Question: clarify the size of the development set used for hyperparameter tuning.
- Question: provide learning curves or other evidence that models are not overfitting (especially STagBLCC and LSTM-ER).
- Question: report mean and standard deviation of F1 scores for main models over at least 3–5 runs with different random seeds.
- Question: elaborate on specific regularization techniques and their hyperparameters used for STagBLCC and MTL.
