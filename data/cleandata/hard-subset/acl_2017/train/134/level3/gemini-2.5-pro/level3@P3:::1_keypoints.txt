- Paper studies neural end-to-end models for argumentation mining, framing the task as token-level sequence tagging and dependency parsing, proposes a novel tag encoding components + relation distance, and evaluates BiLSTM-CRF-CNN and MTL setups on the PE dataset, outperforming prior baselines.

- Contribution: first comprehensive investigation of end-to-end neural architectures for this task, moving away from feature-heavy pipeline systems.

- Contribution: introduces creative task framings (token-level parsing and complex sequence tagging) and shows a local sequence-tagging model (STagBLCC) is more robust than global parsers on long documents.

- Contribution: establishes new state-of-the-art on the PE dataset and provides analyses explaining model behaviors and failure modes.

- Strength: paper is clearly written with well-articulated motivation and illustrative examples.

- Strength: experiments are thorough, comparing many strong baselines and analyzing paragraph- vs essay-level performance to diagnose architecture strengths/weaknesses.

- Strength: the sequence-tagging formulation integrating boundaries, types, and relation distances is elegant and effective; MTL shows gains for joint subtasks.

- Weakness: dataset is very small (~320 train, ~80 test essays), raising strong overfitting concerns and no training/dev loss or overfitting analysis is provided.

- Weakness: results lack stability/statistical rigor—only single F1 scores reported; no mean/std over multiple random runs to assess variance.

- Weakness: insufficient reporting of regularization and hyperparameter tuning details in the main text (e.g., dropout, L2), with only brief mentions in the supplement.

- Weakness: crucial reproducibility details (pretrained embeddings, error analysis) are relegated to the supplementary material instead of being summarized in the paper.

- Question: provide training and development loss/F1 curves to demonstrate models are not overfitting.

- Question: report mean and standard deviation of F1 for main models (e.g., STagBLCC, LSTM-ER) over 5–10 runs with different random seeds.

- Question: state the development set size used for tuning and detail regularization techniques (dropout rates, weight decay) applied to STag models.

- Question: include inter-annotator agreement (IAA) scores from the dataset paper and discuss results relative to that practical upper bound.
