- Empirical study comparing neural framings for end-to-end argumentation mining on the PE dataset (dependency parsing, sequence tagging, MTL, and LSTM-ER).  
- Token-level dependency parsing performs poorly; BiLSTM-CRF sequence tagging and LSTM-ER perform best (LSTM-ER on paragraphs, STagBLCC on essays).  
- Both neural approaches substantially outperform the prior feature-based ILP state-of-the-art.  
- Multi-task learning that decomposes the task into subtasks improves performance on the full task.  
- Contribution: first systematic evaluation of multiple neural end-to-end AM architectures, establishing strong neural baselines.  
- Contribution: insightful comparison showing global structured parsing is less effective than local sequence tagging for this task, with plausible explanations.  
- Contribution: shows complex multi-part labels can be handled by sequence taggers and that MTL is beneficial for structured outputs.  
- Strength: broad, rigorous experimental comparison across diverse models and parsers increases confidence in conclusions.  
- Strength: achieves new state-of-the-art results on PE with clear empirical gains over ILP baselines.  
- Strength: provides useful negative results and analysis explaining model performance differences (e.g., sequence length and relation combinatorics).  
- Strength: good reproducibility efforts with detailed descriptions and supplementary hyperparameters/software.  
- Weakness: limited novelty in the best-performing LSTM-ER model, which is largely an application of prior work.  
- Weakness: possible data leakage—unclear whether essay topics are disjoint between train and test, risking topic-specific cue learning.  
- Weakness: some model descriptions and conversion/constraint details (e.g., graph→tree→graph reconstruction, LSTM-ER vs MTL distinction) lack clarity.  
- Questions: clarify whether train/test essay topics are disjoint to rule out topic-based leakage.  
- Questions: confirm comparability of paragraph- vs essay-level F1 scores and quantify how many inter-paragraph relations paragraph-level evaluation misses.  
- Questions: explain the reverse process of reconstructing the full argument graph from the single-head tree predicted by dependency parsers.  
- Questions: clarify what is meant by LSTM-ER “de-couples” entity and relation detection while “jointly” modeling them, and how this differs from the MTL setup.
