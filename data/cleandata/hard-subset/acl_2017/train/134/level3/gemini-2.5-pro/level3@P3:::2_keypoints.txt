- Empirical study of neural end-to-end models for argumentation mining on the PE corpus, framed as dependency parsing, sequence tagging, MTL tagging, and joint entity-relation (LSTM-ER), compared to an ILP baseline.  
- Token-level dependency parsing performs poorly; sequence tagging models are highly effective.  
- Off-the-shelf LSTM-ER achieves best paragraph-level performance; authors' sequence tagging model is superior at full-essay level.  
- Both neural approaches outperform the prior ILP state-of-the-art, supporting end-to-end learning for this task.  
- Multi-task learning (MTL) substantially improves sequence tagging performance, especially via component identification.  
- Contribution: a comprehensive empirical comparison of diverse neural paradigms for end-to-end argumentation mining.  
- Contribution: demonstration that simpler neural models can outperform a complex, feature-engineered SOTA ILP system.  
- Contribution: a valuable negative result showing token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for this task, with analysis attributing this to long sequences and global parsing constraints.  
- Strength: thorough, systematic evaluation across four neural frameworks, multiple parsers, and both paragraph and essay levels.  
- Strength: strong results with insightful analyses (performance drop paragraph→essay, relation accuracy vs. distance) and MTL benefits.  
- Strength: clear writing and reproducible experimental details, with comprehensive supplementary material.  
- Weakness: possible data leakage if essays on the same topics appear in both train and test splits, risking overestimation of generalization.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty since a top-performing model (LSTM-ER) is a direct application of prior work.  
- Weakness: minor clarity/organization issues (need clearer explanation of LSTM-ER “de-coupling” and suggested reordering of sections).  
- Question: clarify distribution of essay topics across train/test and consider leave-one-topic-out evaluation to test generalization.  
- Question: confirm whether paragraph-level evaluation ignores inter-paragraph links and whether paragraph vs. essay F1 scores are directly comparable.  
- Question: detail the exact procedure for reconstructing the argument graph from dependency trees, handling claims linked to multiple major claims, and whether reconstruction was included in parsing evaluation.  
- Question: explain what is meant by “de-couples entity and relation detection but jointly models them” for LSTM-ER—specifically which architectural/training aspects decouple and which enable joint modeling.
