- Contributions: first neural end-to-end AM approach using sequence tagging with distance encoding, dependency parsing comparison, and multi-task learning.

- Contribution: introduces neural end-to-end AM without ILP or manual feature engineering.

- Contribution: shows BiLSTM-based sequence tagging outperforms dependency parsing for AM.

- Contribution: demonstrates multi-task learning with natural subtasks improves performance.

- Strength: paper is very well-written with clear motivation against pipeline approaches and ILP constraints.

- Strength: sequence tagging with distance encoding is a novel, well-justified insight for capturing long-range dependencies.

- Strength: substantial experiments with thorough comparisons across multiple neural architectures and better results than ILP baseline.

- Weakness: dataset is extremely small (~320 train, ~80 test), raising concerns about generalization and representativeness.

- Weakness: large-capacity BiLSTM models risk severe overfitting given the small number of training tags.

- Weakness: lacks overfitting analysis (no training/development loss curves), model stability assessment (no mean/std across runs), and statistical significance testing.

- Weakness: regularization details are only in supplementary material and hyperparameter tuning did not use Bayesian optimization.

- Weakness: omits inter-annotator agreement, moves error analysis and pre-trained embeddings details to supplement instead of main text.

- Question: provide F1-score ranges across multiple runs for different settings and address observed high variance (e.g., Kiperwasser F1 in [0.56, 0.61]).
