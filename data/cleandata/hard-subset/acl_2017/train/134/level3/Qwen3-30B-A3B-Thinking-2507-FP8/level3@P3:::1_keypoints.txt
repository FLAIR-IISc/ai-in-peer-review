- Paper proposes neural end-to-end argumentation mining framed as token-based dependency parsing, sequence tagging (with distance encoding), and multi-task learning, claiming first neural end-to-end solutions.  
- Key contributions: sequence tagging with distance encoding outperforms dependency parsing; multi-task learning improves performance; neural models match or beat ILP pipelines without manual features.  
- Strength: paper is very well-written with clear motivation and an elegant sequence-tagging + distance-encoding approach.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation across architectures and metrics, including comparison to state-of-the-art ILP.  
- Strength: novel framing that effectively addresses long-range dependencies via distance encoding.  
- Weakness: dataset is very small (~320 training essays, ~80 test essays), creating high overfitting risk for large BiLSTM models.  
- Weakness: missing overfitting analyses (e.g., training/dev loss curves) to assess model generalization.  
- Weakness: no stability analysis—results reported as single runs without means, standard deviations, or statistical significance tests despite noted variance.  
- Weakness: insufficient methodological detail about regularization, hyperparameter tuning, pre-trained embeddings, and moving important supplement content to the main paper.  
- Question: how was overfitting prevented on the small dataset—please provide training/dev loss curves and generalization evidence.  
- Question: provide mean and standard deviation for all results and statistical significance tests given observed run-to-run variance.  
- Question: why use a non-optimized hyperparameter tuning method instead of standard Bayesian optimization?  
- Question: provide full description of the "LSTM-ER" regularization technique in the main paper.  
- Question: include dataset annotation quality (inter-annotator agreement) to contextualize system performance.  
- Recommendation: interesting and valuable contributions, but current claims are undermined by data limitations and lack of stability/overfitting analyses; major revisions (statistical analysis, moving supplement details to main text, addressing data/regularization concerns) are required before acceptance.
