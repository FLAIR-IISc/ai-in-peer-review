- Paper proposes neural end-to-end approaches for argumentation mining framed as token-based dependency parsing, sequence tagging, and multi-task learning, finding sequence tagging with distance encoding most effective.  
- Main contributions: first neural end-to-end solutions for computational AM; neural models outperform joint ILP baseline; sequence tagging with distance encoding (and MTL) yields best results.  

- Strength: Strong motivation and clear problem statement, with insightful explanation why dependency parsing is ineffective at token-level.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation comparing multiple neural approaches against a strong ILP baseline with analysis of why methods differ.  
- Strength: Well-written and accessible presentation with helpful figures.  
- Strength: Practical contribution showing multi-task learning of component detection and relation identification improves performance.  

- Weakness: Severe data limitations (320 train / 80 test) raise overfitting and generalization concerns and lack analysis of model stability.  
- Weakness: Insufficient model evaluation — missing training/dev loss curves and no discussion of overfitting despite observed high variance.  
- Weakness: Inadequate details on regularization and hyperparameter tuning, with ad-hoc tuning approach not justified for small data.  
- Weakness: Missing contextualization of results via inter-annotator agreement from the dataset, making it hard to gauge proximity to human performance.  
- Weakness: Critical information (pre-trained embeddings, error analysis, hyperparameter tuning) relegated to supplement rather than main paper, hurting reproducibility and accessibility.  

- Question: Provide mean and standard deviation of F1 across multiple random initializations to demonstrate model stability.  
- Question: Elaborate on specific regularization techniques used and how their settings were determined given limited data.  
- Question: Include inter-annotator agreement scores from Stab and Gurevych (2016) to contextualize results.  
- Question: Share training and development loss curves to clarify model convergence and potential overfitting.  

- Overall: Interesting and well-written work with meaningful contributions, but severe data and evaluation shortcomings significantly undermine confidence in the reported results.
