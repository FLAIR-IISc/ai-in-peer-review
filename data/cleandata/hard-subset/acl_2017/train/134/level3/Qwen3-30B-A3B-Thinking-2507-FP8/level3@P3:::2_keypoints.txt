- Summary: Evaluates neural end-to-end argumentation mining as dependency parsing, sequence labeling, multi-task learning, and LSTM-ER; sequence labeling (with MTL) and LSTM-ER perform best while dependency parsing performs poorly, evaluated at paragraph vs essay granularity.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates sequence labeling with distance encoding is superior to dependency parsing and outperforms prior state-of-the-art.

- Contribution 2: Shows multi-task learning with component detection as an auxiliary task substantially improves performance, more so than relation detection.

- Contribution 3: Provides the first comprehensive empirical comparison of neural approaches for end-to-end AM and establishes new state-of-the-art results.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical evidence that sequence labeling beats dependency parsing by ~11–13% and outperforms ILP SOTA without hand-crafted features.

- Strength 2: Compelling multi-task learning gains, with component detection improving C-F1 by up to ~5.5%.

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation across models, levels (paragraph vs essay), and matching metrics gives clear insights into method strengths and limits.

- Weakness 1: The top-performing LSTM-ER is essentially an out-of-the-box application of prior work, weakening the paper’s novelty claim.

- Weakness 2: Potential data leakage: unclear whether train and test essays share topics, which could inflate results via topic-specific patterns.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis of paragraph vs essay comparison—paragraph models may miss cross-paragraph relations, limiting real-world applicability.

- Question 1: How does the LSTM-ER out-of-the-box application constitute a meaningful novel contribution to argumentation mining?

- Question 2: How can the dependency parsing tree output reconstruct full argumentation graphs when argument structures are not strict trees and may include multiple major claims?

- Question 3: Are F1 scores across paragraph and essay settings comparable given paragraph models miss cross-paragraph relations—does paragraph-level performance indicate true improvement or a restricted task?

- Overall: Valuable empirical contribution demonstrating sequence labeling and MTL effectiveness, but novelty of LSTM-ER and potential data/topic and paragraph-vs-essay concerns need addressing.
