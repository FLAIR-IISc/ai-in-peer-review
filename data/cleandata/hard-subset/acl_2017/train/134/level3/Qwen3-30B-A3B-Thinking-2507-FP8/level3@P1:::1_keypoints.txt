- Contribution: Proposes neural end-to-end computational argumentation mining (AM) removing hand-crafted features and pipeline architectures.  
- Contribution: Shows sequence tagging (BiLSTM-CRF variants) outperforms dependency parsing for AM.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates multi-task learning with component detection and relation identification improves performance.  

- Strength: Makes a compelling case that joint neural learning avoids pipeline error propagation and costly feature engineering.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental comparison across architectures vs prior ILP-based state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: Provides insightful analysis explaining why dependency parsing fails (long-range dependencies, tree mismatch) and why sequence tagging succeeds (encodes component distances).  
- Strength: Well-structured and clearly written, making technical contributions accessible.  

- Key empirical claim: Sequence tagging (BiLSTMs) outperforms ILP baseline by 10–15% F1 on the PE dataset; multi-task learning adds up to ~5% F1.  

- Weakness: Extremely small dataset (∼320 training essays, ∼80 test essays) raises serious overfitting concerns for high-capacity models.  
- Weakness: No analysis of model stability or overfitting—missing training/validation loss curves, multiple-run averages, standard deviations, and significance tests.  
- Weakness: Reported results appear to be single runs, making it hard to judge whether improvements are meaningful or due to randomness.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of regularization and limited hyperparameter-tuning details (mostly in supplementary material).  
- Weakness: Fails to address broader model–data mismatch given typical sequence-labeling needs for much larger datasets.  
- Weakness: Lacks inter-annotator agreement scores to contextualize achievable performance and judge reported gains.  

- Question: How was overfitting prevented given only ~320 training essays—please provide training/validation loss curves?  
- Question: Can you report mean and standard deviation across multiple runs and statistical significance tests?  
- Question: Move Bayesian optimization hyperparameter-tuning details from the supplementary material to the main paper and explain how tuning avoided overfitting.
