- Paper benchmarks neural end-to-end AM on the PE dataset, comparing token-level dependency parsing, composite sequence tagging, multi-task tagging, and LSTM-ER, with paragraph and essay evaluations against an ILP baseline.
- Contributions: thorough empirical benchmark with new SOTA, an effective composite sequence-tagging formulation and multi-task setup, and a negative result showing token-level dependency parsing is ineffective for long sequences.
- Strong empirical results: STagBLCC and LSTM-ER outperform the ILP baseline on paragraphs and remain competitive on essays, with statistical validation.
- Analysis insight: local BiLSTM taggers are robust to input length, dependency parsers degrade on longer texts due to combinatorial/global constraints, and LSTM-ER struggles with many relation candidates.
- Multi-task learning consistently improves performance, with component supervision providing the largest gains.
- Experimental design is careful and transparent: evaluations at two granularities, multiple metrics, and a comprehensive supplement.
- Practical recommendations: joint-but-decoupled entity/relation modeling, prefer local tagging for long documents, and use general relation models when entity counts are small.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—primarily architecture application, tag design, comparison, and a negative result.
- Weakness: token-level dependency parsing framing is misaligned with AM’s component-level nature, so the negative result may partly reflect this mismatch.
- Weakness: unclear comparability between paragraph and essay results due to differing entity counts and potential loss of cross-paragraph relations.
- Weakness: potential data leakage or topic overlap across train/dev/test could inflate results; split protocol needs documentation.
- Weakness: clarity issues around what is trained jointly vs. conditioned and how parameter sharing is implemented.
- Weakness: unclear tree construction/reconstruction process—how original multi-link graphs are recovered for evaluation.
- Question: how are essays split across topics—do topics overlap and was lexical overlap inspected to rule out leakage?
- Question: should paragraph-level F1 be compared to essay-level F1, and what cross-paragraph relations are lost in paragraph experiments?
- Question: detail the tree-to-graph conversion and whether reconstruction is invertible or biased.
- Question: did LSTM-ER use scheduled sampling/entity pretraining, and what is the contribution of TreeLSTM—can you ablate it?
- Question: how was distance d handled in tags—was it capped/bucketed and how were unseen/rare distances treated at test time?
- Question: why do component and full tasks work best when fed from the same layer—can you provide rationale or probing analysis?
- Additional comments: consider presenting sequence tagging before parsing, clarify “decoupling but joint modeling,” define table labels (e.g., “Acc.”), and fix minor typos.
