- Paper proposes end-to-end neural approaches to argumentation mining (AM) from raw text, comparing token-level dependency parsing, token-level sequence tagging (with augmented labels), MTL tagging, and LSTM-ER joint entity–relation modeling on the PE dataset.  
- Main empirical claim: BiLSTM sequence tagging with augmented label space outperforms token-level parsing and an ILP baseline; MTL with natural auxiliary tasks further improves performance.  
- Comparative finding: LSTM-ER is strong for component detection and short texts but degrades relation F1 on essays; token-level dependency parsing performs poorly for end-to-end AM relative to tagging.  
- Contribution: an end-to-end neural framing for AM that avoids hand-crafted features/constraints and achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on the PE dataset.  

- Strength: Clear problem framing and convincing motivation for neural end-to-end AM as an alternative to feature-heavy ILP pipelines.  
- Strength: Broad, relevant experimental comparisons across modeling paradigms and paragraph vs. essay settings.  
- Strength: Useful empirical insights (tagging > token parsing; joint modeling without hard constraints can capture structure; MTL helps) with thoughtful qualitative interpretation.  
- Strength: Generally strong clarity and presentation; methods mostly well specified and results interpretable.  
- Strength: Proper baselines and metrics (ILP baseline, component/relation F1 at 100%/50%) enabling comparability.  

- Weakness: Small dataset (~322 train essays) vs. large tag space creates acute data sparsity and potential overfitting risk for high-capacity models.  
- Weakness: No reporting of development split size, learning curves, or multi-seed variability — robustness and stability are unclear.  
- Weakness: Regularization and tuning details are incomplete in main text (dropout, weight decay, clipping, early stopping, hyperparameter search), especially for LSTM-ER.  
- Weakness: Token-level dependency parsing may be a weak comparison; lack of component-level parsing experiments makes conclusions about parsing’s weakness premature.  
- Weakness: Limited external validation — results confined to one dataset/domain, so generalization is uncertain.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps — missing key experimental details (dev split, embeddings, epochs/early stopping, compute budget) and omission of dataset inter-annotator agreement context.  

- Question: What was the development set size and selection protocol; was any cross-validation used?  
- Question: How many epochs were trained and what early stopping criterion was used (dev C-F1, R-F1, or global F1)?  
- Question: Please report mean±std and ranges for component and relation F1 over at least 5 random seeds for each main model.  
- Question: Are the significance tests in Table 1 computed over documents for a single seed or averaged across seeds?  
- Question: Provide full regularization and hyperparameter details: dropout placements/rates, L2 weight decay, gradient clipping, learning rate schedule, batch sizes, weight initialization.  
- Question: For LSTM-ER, supply equivalent hyperparameter/regularization details and tuning strategy.  
- Question: How large is the effective tag set Y (unique (b,t,d,s)), do you clip distance d (±K or other bucket), and is any class-imbalance handling used?  
- Question: How is the “each premise links to exactly one target” constraint enforced at inference in the tagging model; does decoding produce invalid structures and how are they fixed?  
- Question: Did you try component-level parsing/tagging pipelines (detect components then parse relations) to disentangle token-level errors from parsing ability?  
- Question: For MTL, how sensitive are gains to which layers auxiliary tasks feed from; please provide ablation of YC vs YR and their combinations across layers.  
- Question: Have you tried the best model on other AM datasets (e.g., microtexts) to assess portability?  

- Suggestion: Move essential experimental details (embeddings, dev split, regularization, main error types) from supplement into the main paper.  
- Suggestion: Add inter-annotator agreement from Stab & Gurevych (2016) to contextualize achievable F1 and headroom.  
- Suggestion: Provide learning curves (train vs. dev) for main models to assess overfitting.  
- Suggestion: Perform more systematic hyperparameter search (e.g., Bayesian optimization) and report search budget and candidate ranges.  
- Suggestion: Include ablations on tagging label space: distance bucketing schemes, removing stance from tags, and analyze effects on C-F1/R-F1.  
- Suggestion: For fairness, report compute budgets and memory constraints motivating paragraph-level parser training, and consider a component-level parsing baseline.  
- Suggestion: Improve Figure 1 for grayscale/print (contrast/line styles) for clarity.  
- Suggestion (post-response): Include mean, std, and ranges of F1 across seeds and settings, and release code, configs, and random seeds for reproducibility.
