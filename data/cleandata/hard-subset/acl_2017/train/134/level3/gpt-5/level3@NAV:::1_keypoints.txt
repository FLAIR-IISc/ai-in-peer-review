- Motivation: evaluate end-to-end neural AM to avoid hand-engineered features/ILP and reduce pipeline error propagation.  
- Main finding: token-level dependency parsing underperforms for AM.  
- Main finding: simple BiLSTM sequence tagging with distance-encoded labels is a strong end-to-end baseline.  
- Main finding: multi-task learning separating component vs. relation yields measurable gains over single-task tagging.  
- Comparison: informative analysis contrasting local tagging and joint models (LSTM-ER), showing different strengths at paragraph vs. essay level.  
- Presentation strength: clear writing and well-articulated motivation for neural joint learning without ILP.  
- Experimental strength: substantial comparisons across modeling families with useful analyses (distance distributions, paragraph vs. essay effects).  
- Modeling insight: proposes replicable ideas (distance-encoded labels) and explores modularity vs. constraints trade-offs.  
- Baselines/metrics: includes proper ILP baselines and task-relevant evaluation metrics.  
- Data scale concern: training data is very small (~320 train essays), raising robustness and generalization worries.  
- Overfitting risk: large-capacity Bi(LSTM) models on limited tags may overfit; no train/dev curves or regularization details reported.  
- Stability/significance: results reported for a single seed only; need mean±std over multiple runs and statistical tests due to apparent high variance.  
- Missing details: key experimental details (pre-trained embeddings, hyperparameter tuning, error analysis) are in the supplement and should be moved to main paper.  
- Comparability issue: ILP’s paragraph/essay awareness may inflate scores versus essay-level evaluation; report inter-annotator agreement for context.  
- Requested reporting: provide dev set construction, multiple-seed results (means, stds, ranges), train/dev curves, F1 ranges per setting, and statistical significance tests.  
- Requested details: specify pre-trained embedding source and OOV handling, expand error analysis, and improve Figure 1 color/contrast for B/W printing.
