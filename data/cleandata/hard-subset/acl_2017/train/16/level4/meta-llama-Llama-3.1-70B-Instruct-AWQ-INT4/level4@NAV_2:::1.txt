**Summary of the Paper**
This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that existing joint methods, which tackle ED and argument extraction simultaneously, often ignore or indirectly use argument information, leading to insignificant improvements in ED. In contrast, this paper focuses on explicitly using annotated argument information to enhance ED performance.

**Main Contributions**
1. The authors analyze the problem of joint models on the task of ED and propose to use annotated argument information explicitly for this task.
2. They introduce a supervised attention-based ED model and investigate different attention strategies for the proposed model.
3. They improve the performance of ED and achieve the best performance on the widely used benchmark dataset ACE 2005.

**Strengths**
This paper proposes a clear and simple framework to improve ED performance by leveraging argument information. The use of supervised attention mechanisms, a method commonly employed in tasks like machine translation, significantly outperforms the baseline. The experimental results are solid, and the approach can be considered effective.

**Weaknesses**
1. The attention vector calculation is straightforward and might be improved by more sophisticated approaches.
2. The supervised attention mechanism relies on two simple strategies, which could be enhanced by exploring more complex methods.
3. The authors could further discuss the potential limitations and challenges of their approach, particularly in handling cases where argument information is scarce or noisy.

**Questions to Authors**
1. How do the authors plan to address the issue of data sparsity, where annotated argument information might be limited or unavailable?
2. Can the authors provide more insights into the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the value of λ, and how it affects the performance of the model?
3. How do the authors envision their approach being applied to other tasks or domains, such as event extraction in social media or biomedical texts?
