**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that existing methods either ignore or indirectly utilize argument information, leading to suboptimal performance. The main contributions of this work are: (1) analyzing the limitations of joint models for ED and proposing to use annotated argument information explicitly, (2) introducing a supervised attention-based ED model, and (3) investigating different attention strategies for the proposed model.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of argument information**: The paper demonstrates the importance of argument information in ED and proposes a novel approach to utilize it, leading to significant performance improvements.
2. **Clear and simple framework**: The proposed framework is easy to understand and implement, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
3. **Solid experimentation**: The authors conduct thorough experiments on the ACE 2005 dataset, providing a comprehensive evaluation of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Simplistic attention vector calculation**: The attention vector is calculated as the summation of two attention vectors, which might not be the most effective approach. Alternative methods, such as weighted summation or more complex attention mechanisms, could be explored.
2. **Limited attention strategies**: The two proposed attention strategies are straightforward and might not be optimal. More sophisticated strategies, such as those incorporating additional linguistic features or contextual information, could be investigated.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Have you considered using more advanced attention mechanisms, such as hierarchical or multi-head attention, to further improve the performance of your approach?
2. How do you plan to adapt your approach to handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words or entities, which are common in real-world datasets?
3. Can you provide more insights into the impact of the hyper-parameter λ on the performance of your approach, and how it was tuned during experimentation?
