**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that arguments provide significant clues for identifying and categorizing events, especially for ambiguous trigger words. They introduce a neural network-based approach that uses a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words. The proposed model is evaluated on the ACE 2005 dataset and achieves state-of-the-art performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel approach to exploit argument information explicitly for ED via supervised attention mechanisms.
2. They introduce a neural network-based approach that uses a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words.
3. The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ACE 2005 dataset.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a clear and simple framework to improve ED by exploiting argument information.
2. The use of supervised attention mechanisms is an effective method to focus on argument words.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the ACE 2005 dataset.

**Weaknesses**

1. The attention vector is calculated as a simple summation of two attention vectors, which may not be the most effective approach.
2. The supervised attention mechanism uses two straightforward strategies, which may not be sufficient to capture the complexity of the task.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the proposed approach and its limitations.

**General Discussion**

Overall, the paper proposes a effective framework to improve ED by exploiting argument information. While there are some areas for improvement, the experimental results demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach. With some refinements, this work could make a significant contribution to the field of event detection.
