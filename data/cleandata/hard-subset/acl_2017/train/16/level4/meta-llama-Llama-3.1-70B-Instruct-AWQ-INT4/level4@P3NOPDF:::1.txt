This paper proposes an innovative approach to event detection by leveraging information from arguments, which is often overlooked but crucial for improvement. The framework is well-structured and straightforward, making it easy to follow. The incorporation of a supervised attention mechanism, a technique widely used in tasks such as machine translation, significantly enhances the system's performance compared to the baseline.

However, there are a few areas that can be improved upon. The calculation of the attention vector, which is simply the summation of two attention vectors from each part, may not be the most effective approach. A more sophisticated method for calculating the attention vector could potentially yield better results. Additionally, the two strategies proposed for the supervised attention mechanism are somewhat straightforward and may not fully exploit the capabilities of this technique. Exploring more complex strategies could lead to further improvements.

Despite these potential areas for improvement, this paper presents a highly effective framework, and the experimental results are promising. The experiment is solid and well-executed, providing a convincing demonstration of the proposed approach. Overall, the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of event detection, and its strengths outweigh its weaknesses. Therefore, it is recommended for acceptance.
