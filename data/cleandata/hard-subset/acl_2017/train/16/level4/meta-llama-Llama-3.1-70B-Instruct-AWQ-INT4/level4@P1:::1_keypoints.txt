- Proposes a neural ED method that exploits annotated argument information via supervised attention to better detect and classify events, especially for ambiguous triggers.  
- Claims supervised attention forces the model to focus on argument words more than others, improving signal for ED.  
- Experimental results show the approach outperforms state-of-the-art on the ACE 2005 dataset.  
- Contribution: explicitly uses annotated argument information rather than relying on joint models for ED.  
- Contribution: introduces a supervised attention-based ED model and evaluates different attention strategies.  
- Contribution: achieves best reported performance on ACE 2005.  
- Strength: Exploiting argument information is a valuable and often-ignored signal for ED.  
- Strength: Framework is clear, simple, and supervised attention is an effective improvement; experiments are solid.  
- Weakness: Attention vector is computed by simply summing two attention vectors, which may be overly simplistic.  
- Weakness: The two proposed supervised attention strategies are straightforward; more sophisticated strategies could further improve performance.  
- Overall: Effective framework with solid results; paper is a valuable contribution and suitable for acceptance.
