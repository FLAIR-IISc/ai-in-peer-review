**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that arguments provide significant clues for identifying and categorizing events, especially for ambiguous trigger words. They introduce a neural network-based approach that adopts a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words, which are expected to acquire more attention than other words. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed approach outperforms state-of-the-art methods on the ACE 2005 dataset.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors analyze the problem of joint models on the task of ED and propose to use annotated argument information explicitly for this task.
2. They introduce a supervised attention-based ED model that investigates different attention strategies for the proposed model.
3. The authors improve the performance of ED and achieve the best performance on the ACE 2005 dataset.

**Strengths**

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of event detection by exploiting argument information, which is usually ignored yet actually quite important. The framework is clear and simple, and the use of supervised attention mechanisms is an effective way to improve the performance of the system. The experimental results are solid, and the paper outperforms the baseline significantly.

**Weaknesses**

One potential weakness of the paper is the calculation of the attention vector, which is simply the summation of two attention vectors of each part. A more sophisticated approach to calculating the attention vector could be explored. Additionally, the two strategies proposed for the supervised attention mechanism are quite straightforward, and more complicated strategies could be tried to further improve the performance of the system.

**General Discussion**

Although there are some areas that can be improved, this paper proposes a quite effective framework, and the performance is good. The experiment is solid, and the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of event detection. Overall, it can be considered for acceptance.
