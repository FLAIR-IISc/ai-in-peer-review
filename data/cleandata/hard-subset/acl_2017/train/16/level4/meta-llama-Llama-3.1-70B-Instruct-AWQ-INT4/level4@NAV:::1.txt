**Strengths:**

This paper explores the utilization of argument information, often overlooked but significant, to enhance event detection performance. The proposed framework is characterized by its clarity and simplicity. Notably, the incorporation of a supervised attention mechanism, a widely employed method in tasks such as machine translation, yields a substantial improvement over the baseline.

**Weaknesses:**

A potential area for improvement lies in the calculation of the attention vector, which is currently derived from the simple summation of two attention vectors. A more sophisticated approach to calculating the attention vector may be warranted. Additionally, while the two proposed strategies for the supervised attention mechanism are straightforward, exploring more complex strategies could potentially yield better results.

**General Discussion:**

Despite areas for improvement, this paper presents an effective framework with promising performance. The experimental design is robust, and the findings are compelling. Overall, the paper demonstrates significant merit and can be considered for acceptance.
