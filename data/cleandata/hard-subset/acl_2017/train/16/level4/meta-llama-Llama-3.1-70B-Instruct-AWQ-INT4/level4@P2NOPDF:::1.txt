**Strengths:**

This paper explores the often-overlooked yet crucial information from arguments to enhance event detection performance. The proposed framework is clear and straightforward, leveraging a supervised attention mechanism that has proven effective in various tasks, including machine translation. Notably, the system's performance surpasses the baseline significantly, demonstrating the potential of this approach.

**Weaknesses:**

One potential limitation lies in the calculation of the attention vector, which is simply the sum of two attention vectors from each part. A more sophisticated approach to computing the attention vector might yield better results. Furthermore, the two strategies proposed for the supervised attention mechanism, although straightforward, may not be the most effective. Exploring more complex strategies could lead to improved performance.

**General Discussion:**

Despite areas for improvement, this paper presents a remarkably effective framework, achieving impressive performance. The experimental design is solid, providing a convincing demonstration of the approach's value. Overall, this work makes a compelling case for acceptance, with its contributions and results offering a valuable addition to the field.
