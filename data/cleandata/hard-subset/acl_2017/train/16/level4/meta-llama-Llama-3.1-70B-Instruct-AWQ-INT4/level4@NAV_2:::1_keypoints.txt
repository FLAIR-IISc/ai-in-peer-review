- Proposes using supervised attention with annotated argument information to explicitly improve event detection, arguing joint models underuse argument signals.
- Analyzes limitations of joint ED/argument-extraction models and advocates explicit use of annotated arguments.
- Introduces a supervised attention-based ED model and investigates different attention strategies.
- Reports improved ED performance, achieving best results on the ACE 2005 benchmark.
- Strength: clear, simple framework that effectively leverages argument information; supervised attention yields significant gains and solid experiments.
- Weakness: attention vector computation is straightforward and could benefit from more sophisticated techniques.
- Weakness: supervised attention relies on two simple strategies; more complex approaches are not explored.
- Weakness: limited discussion of challenges when argument annotations are scarce or noisy.
- Question: How will the method handle data sparsity or lack of annotated argument information?
- Question: Request for more insight into hyperparameter choices (especially λ) and their impact on performance.
- Question: How can the approach be applied to other domains/tasks (e.g., social media or biomedical event extraction)?
