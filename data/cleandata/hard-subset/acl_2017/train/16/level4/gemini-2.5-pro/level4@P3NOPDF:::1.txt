### **Summary**

This paper introduces a novel and effective framework for event detection that leverages argument information, a source of semantic cues often overlooked in previous work. The core contribution is the application of a supervised attention mechanism to guide the model's focus, resulting in significant performance improvements over a strong baseline. The proposed model is simple, clearly articulated, and supported by solid experimental results. While there are opportunities to explore more complex architectural choices, the paper stands as a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend this paper for acceptance.

### **Strengths**

1.  **Novelty and Impact:** The primary strength of this work lies in its core idea: incorporating argument information to improve event detection. The authors correctly identify that event arguments provide crucial context that can help disambiguate and identify event triggers. This is an intuitive yet underexplored approach that convincingly demonstrates its value through the experimental results.

2.  **Effective Use of Attention:** The paper successfully adapts the supervised attention mechanism, a technique proven effective in other domains like machine translation, to the task of event detection. This not only serves as a strong modeling choice but also helps in making the model's decisions more interpretable, by highlighting which parts of the context the model deems important.

3.  **Clarity and Simplicity:** The proposed framework is elegant in its simplicity and is described with excellent clarity. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, allowing the reader to grasp the main contributions without difficulty.

4.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate that the proposed model significantly outperforms the baseline system. This provides compelling evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach and validates the authors' central hypothesis regarding the utility of argument information.

### **Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

While the overall framework is sound, there are several areas where the modeling could be explored further to potentially yield even better results. These points should be viewed as suggestions for future work rather than fundamental flaws.

1.  **Attention Vector Combination:** The current model combines attention vectors through simple summation. While this is a reasonable starting point, it may not be the optimal method for integrating information from different sources. The authors could explore more sophisticated fusion techniques, such as a gated mechanism or concatenation followed by a non-linear transformation. Such methods might be better at capturing complex interactions between the different attention signals.

2.  **Supervision Strategy:** The two proposed strategies for supervising the attention mechanism are effective but relatively straightforward. It would be interesting to investigate more nuanced supervision strategies. For instance, could the supervision signal be weighted based on the type or centrality of an argument? Exploring and comparing these simpler strategies against more complex alternatives in an ablation study would further strengthen the paper's claims and provide deeper insights for future research in this area.
