- Problem & approach: Addresses event detection (ED) by explicitly incorporating annotated event arguments via a supervised attention mechanism to improve trigger identification/classification.

- Contribution: Proposes a novel supervised-attention framework that uses argument annotations to guide ED.

- Contribution: Provides strong empirical validation on ACE 2005 (state-of-the-art F1 71.7, 71.9 with FrameNet) and ablation studies confirming gains come from argument information.

- Strength: Well-motivated, intuitive, and elegant solution that directly targets a known shortcoming of prior joint models.

- Strength: Significant performance improvement and high precision that suggests arguments help disambiguate triggers.

- Strength: Thorough experimental analysis, including ablations and additional FrameNet data showing robustness and the role of data sparsity.

- Weakness: Simple summation fusion of word and entity attention (α = αw + αe) is heuristic and may not learn relative importance across contexts.

- Weakness: Gold attention construction strategies (S1 uniform, S2 Gaussian) are heuristic and ignore that some argument roles/types are more indicative of event types.

- Question: Have you tried more sophisticated fusion methods (e.g., learned gating, concatenation + linear) to combine αw and αe?

- Question: Have you considered role- or type-dependent gold attention weights so more informative arguments receive higher supervisory weight?
