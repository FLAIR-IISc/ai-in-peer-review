### Review

**Strengths:**

The primary strength of this work lies in its novel approach to event detection, which effectively leverages information from event arguments. The authors correctly identify that this information, while crucial for understanding an event, is often underutilized in existing models. The proposed framework is clear, simple, and well-articulated. By incorporating a supervised attention mechanism—a technique that has proven successful in other NLP tasks such as machine translation—the model is able to focus on the most relevant parts of the input, leading to performance that significantly outperforms the baseline.

**Weaknesses:**

While the overall framework is effective, there are areas where the methodology could be further explored. First, the method for combining the attention vectors is a simple summation. It would be interesting to investigate whether more sophisticated combination functions, such as a gated or parameterized approach, could yield further improvements. Second, the two strategies proposed for supervising the attention mechanism are relatively straightforward. The authors could consider exploring more complex or adaptive supervision techniques that might capture finer-grained semantic relationships and provide additional performance gains.

**General Discussion:**

In summary, despite the opportunities for methodological refinement mentioned above, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of event detection. The authors propose a simple yet highly effective framework that demonstrates the importance of incorporating argument information. The experimental evaluation is sound, and the results provide convincing evidence for the model's efficacy. Given its clear contribution, novel approach, and strong performance, I recommend this paper for acceptance.
