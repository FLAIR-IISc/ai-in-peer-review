- Strengths:
The primary strength of this work lies in its novel use of argument information, an often-overlooked yet valuable signal, to enhance event detection performance. The proposed framework is both clear and elegantly simple. By successfully employing a supervised attention mechanism, a powerful technique proven effective in other domains like machine translation, the authors demonstrate that their system achieves significant performance gains over the established baseline.

- Weaknesses:
The current implementation computes the final attention vector via a simple summation of its two constituent parts; exploring more sophisticated aggregation methods could be beneficial. Furthermore, the two strategies proposed for the supervised attention mechanism are relatively direct. Investigating more complex or nuanced strategies might yield further performance improvements and is a worthwhile direction for future work.

- General Discussion:
In summary, while there are opportunities for further refinement, the paper introduces a demonstrably effective framework that achieves strong performance. The experimental evaluation is robust and the results are convincing. I believe this paper is a solid contribution and recommend it for acceptance.
