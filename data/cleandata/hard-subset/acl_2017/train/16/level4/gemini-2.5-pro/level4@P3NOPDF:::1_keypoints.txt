- Introduces a novel framework for event detection that leverages argument information to improve trigger identification.  
- Applies a supervised attention mechanism to guide model focus, yielding substantial performance gains over a strong baseline.  
- Model is simple, clearly explained, and experimentally well-supported.  
- Paper is a valuable contribution and recommended for acceptance.  
- Strength: Incorporating event arguments provides crucial semantic context and is an underexplored yet effective idea.  
- Strength: Supervised attention adapts a proven technique, improving interpretability by highlighting important context.  
- Strength: The framework is elegant, easy to follow, and well-structured.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results convincingly validate the approach.  
- Weakness: Current method combines attention vectors by simple summation; more sophisticated fusion (gating, concatenation + nonlinearity) might capture interactions better.  
- Weakness: Supervision strategies for attention are straightforward; exploring weighted or argument-type/centrality-aware supervision and ablation studies could deepen insights.
