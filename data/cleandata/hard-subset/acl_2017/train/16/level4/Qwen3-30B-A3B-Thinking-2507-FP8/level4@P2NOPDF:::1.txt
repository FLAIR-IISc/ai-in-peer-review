

Strengths:  
This paper makes a compelling contribution by leveraging argument information—a dimension often overlooked yet inherently valuable—for event detection. The proposed framework is both clear and concise, effectively integrating a supervised attention mechanism (a technique proven effective in tasks like machine translation) to significantly outperform baseline systems. The authors demonstrate that incorporating argument structures substantially enhances model performance, providing a novel and practical approach to event detection.  

Weaknesses:  
A key limitation lies in the computation of the attention vector, which is simply the summation of two component-specific attention vectors. This approach may not optimally capture interactions between argument parts; a more sophisticated aggregation method (e.g., weighted fusion or hierarchical attention) could better model their interdependencies. Additionally, the two supervised attention strategies proposed are conceptually straightforward. While effective, exploring more nuanced mechanisms—such as adaptive attention weighting or cross-component interaction modeling—might yield further performance gains.  

Overall Recommendation:  
Despite these minor limitations, the paper presents a robust and innovative framework with strong empirical validation. The experiments are well-executed and thoroughly support the claim that argument information significantly improves event detection. The work offers a clear, actionable contribution to the field, and the results are both convincing and generalizable. We recommend acceptance for its methodological novelty, solid evaluation, and potential impact on event extraction research.
