

Strengths:  
This paper addresses an important yet often overlooked aspect of event detection—leveraging argument information typically disregarded in existing approaches. The proposed framework is clear and well-motivated, effectively integrating argument features to enhance performance. The use of a supervised attention mechanism, a technique widely adopted in tasks like machine translation, is particularly well-suited here. As a result, the system significantly outperforms the baseline, demonstrating the value of this design choice.

Weaknesses:  
The attention vector is computed merely as the sum of two component attention vectors, which seems overly simplistic. A more sophisticated approach to combining these vectors could better capture nuanced relationships between argument elements. Additionally, while the paper proposes two strategies for implementing the supervised attention mechanism, both are relatively straightforward. More complex alternatives—such as adaptive weighting or hierarchical fusion—might yield further improvements and are worth exploring to maximize performance.

General Discussion:  
Despite these minor limitations, the paper presents a highly effective framework with strong empirical results. The experimental evaluation is thorough and well-structured, providing convincing evidence of the approach’s efficacy. The core contribution—leveraging argument information for event detection—is both novel and practically valuable. Given the solid methodology and clear impact, this work merits acceptance.
