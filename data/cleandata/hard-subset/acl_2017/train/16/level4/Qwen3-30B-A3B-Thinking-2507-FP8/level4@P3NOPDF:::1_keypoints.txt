- Addresses a significant gap by leveraging argumentative information for event detection.  
- Proposed framework is novel, well-structured, and elegantly simple.  
- Integration of supervised attention yields substantial improvements over strong baselines.  
- Experimental design is rigorous with comprehensive results across multiple benchmarks.  
- Methodology is clearly presented and evaluation is robust.  
- Primary limitation: attention vector computed as simple summation of two component vectors lacks theoretical grounding.  
- Simple summation may not capture nuanced argument interactions; more sophisticated fusion (concatenation, gating, multiplicative) could help.  
- The two supervised-attention strategies are relatively basic; advanced variants (context-aware or hierarchical attention) should be explored.  
- Paper lacks discussion of why simpler approaches were chosen and their computational trade-offs.  
- Overall contribution is timely, impactful, and empirically strong; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Revision should address attention design (experiment with alternative fusion strategies and justify choices).
