- Addresses an important, overlooked aspect of event detection by leveraging argument information ignored by prior work.  
- Proposed framework is clear, well-motivated, and effectively integrates argument features.  
- Use of a supervised attention mechanism is appropriate and well-suited to the task.  
- System significantly outperforms the baseline, showing the value of the design choice.  
- Attention vector computed as a simple sum of two component vectors is overly simplistic.  
- Combining attention vectors with a more sophisticated method could better capture nuanced argument relationships.  
- Both proposed supervised attention strategies are straightforward; more complex alternatives (e.g., adaptive weighting, hierarchical fusion) should be explored.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating strong empirical results.  
- Core contribution—leveraging argument information for event detection—is novel and practically valuable.  
- Overall methodology and impact are solid, and the work merits acceptance.
