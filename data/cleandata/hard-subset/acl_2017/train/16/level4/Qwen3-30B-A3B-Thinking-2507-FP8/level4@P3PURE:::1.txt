

Strengths:  
This paper addresses a notable gap by leveraging argument information—typically overlooked yet critically important—for enhancing event detection performance. The framework is clearly articulated and conceptually simple. By integrating a supervised attention mechanism (a well-established technique in tasks like machine translation), the authors achieve significant performance gains over the baseline.

Weaknesses:  
The attention vector is computed as a simple sum of two component attention vectors, which may not be the most effective approach. More sophisticated methods for calculating the attention vector could yield better results. Additionally, while the paper proposes two strategies for the supervised attention mechanism, both are relatively straightforward. Exploring more complex strategies that might deliver improved performance would be worthwhile.

General Discussion:  
Despite these points for improvement, the paper presents a highly effective framework with strong empirical results. The experimental design is robust and well-executed. The contributions are meaningful, and the work merits acceptance.
