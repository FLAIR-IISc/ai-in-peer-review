- Leverages argument information for event detection, addressing an often-overlooked but valuable dimension.  
- Presents a clear, concise framework that integrates supervised attention effectively.  
- Supervised attention substantially outperforms baseline systems.  
- Demonstrates that incorporating argument structures significantly improves model performance and offers a novel, practical approach.  
- Attention vector computation is simplistic—summing two component-specific attention vectors—which may fail to capture inter-component interactions.  
- Recommends more sophisticated aggregation (e.g., weighted fusion or hierarchical attention) to better model dependencies.  
- The two supervised attention strategies are conceptually straightforward; exploring adaptive attention weighting or cross-component interaction modeling could yield further gains.  
- Experiments are well-executed and empirically validate the paper’s claims; results are convincing and generalizable.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for methodological novelty, solid evaluation, and potential impact despite minor limitations.
