- Proposes using argument information via supervised attention to improve event detection, showing significant performance gains.
- Main contribution: explicitly incorporates annotated argument information into an event detection model through supervised attention.
- Secondary contribution: systematically investigates and compares different attention strategies within the framework.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on the ACE 2005 dataset.
- Novelty: directly leverages arguments for event detection rather than relying on joint methods that mainly help argument extraction.
- Shows supervised attention effectively focuses the model on relevant arguments, improving detection accuracy.
- Strong empirical validation with comparisons to multiple baselines and ablation studies.
- Paper is clearly written and well structured.
- Weakness: combines word and entity attention vectors by simple summation, which may be overly simplistic.
- Weakness: limited exploration of attention strategies; more complex/adaptive mechanisms and external data (e.g., FrameNet) could be evaluated.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed error analysis to identify remaining weaknesses and failure cases.
- Question: why choose simple summation for combining attention vectors and were other combination methods tried?
- Question: what is the computational overhead of the attention mechanism compared to baselines, and how can it be mitigated?
- Question: can the authors provide examples where attention on arguments enabled correct event detection?
- Additional comment: expand related work to cover more recent advances in attention mechanisms and event detection.
- Additional comment: discuss and justify hyperparameter settings more thoroughly.
