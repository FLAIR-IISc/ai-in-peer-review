- Proposes leveraging argumentative structure for event detection—an intuitive and potentially impactful idea.
- Framework is clearly articulated and relatively simple to understand—a major strength.
- Integrates supervised attention (inspired by machine translation) which improves performance over strong baselines.
- Reported results are encouraging and suggest a worthwhile contribution.
- Current method for combining attention vectors (simple summation) is rudimentary.
- Recommends exploring more sophisticated combination strategies (weighted averaging, concatenation + linear transform, learnable weights).
- Two supervised attention strategies are functional but relatively straightforward; discussion of more complex mechanisms is needed.
- Suggests attention informed by rhetorical roles (e.g., prioritizing premises) and exploring multi-head attention.
- Experimental evaluation is solid, baselines appropriate, and improvements are statistically significant.
- Recommends an ablation study to quantify contributions of argument information, supervised attention, and specific attention strategies.
- Overall a promising, well-motivated, and empirically validated paper that merits acceptance despite areas for refinement.
