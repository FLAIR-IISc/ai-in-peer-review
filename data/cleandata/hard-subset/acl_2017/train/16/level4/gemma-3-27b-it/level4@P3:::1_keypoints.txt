- Tackles event detection by explicitly leveraging argument information via supervised attention; achieves state-of-the-art on ACE 2005.  
- Contribution: Shows that modeling arguments with supervised attention improves ED, systematically studies gold-attention construction strategies, and provides a strong ACE2005 baseline.  
- Strength: Novel motivation—arguments are underutilized and help disambiguate triggers.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance with notable precision gains over prior methods.  
- Strength: Clear, well-defined methodology and two interpretable gold-attention strategies.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation with strong baselines, ablations, and FrameNet-augmented experiments.  
- Weakness: Simple summation for combining word and entity attention may be suboptimal; more sophisticated fusion could help.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of attention strategies; use of a Gaussian in S2 appears arbitrary and needs justification.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on ACE 2005 limits claims about generalization to other datasets.  
- Weakness: Missing detailed error analysis to pinpoint failure modes.  
- Question: Why choose a Gaussian for S2 and were alternatives tested?  
- Question: Were more sophisticated attention-combination methods (gating, concatenation + learned transform) tried and with what results?  
- Question: Are there event/trigger types where the approach excels or fails?  
- Additional: Paper is well-written; add a comparison table to related joint models and fix inconsistent ACL reference formatting.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
