- Paper investigates using argument information for event detection and proposes a supervised attention framework.  
- The supervised attention mechanism effectively focuses the model on relevant argument words during event classification.  
- The framework is clear, simple, and reproducible.  
- Experimental results show substantial performance gains on the ACE 2005 dataset, outperforming several state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: attention vectors are combined by simple summation, which may not capture complex word–entity interactions.  
- Weakness: gold attention construction strategies (S1 and S2) are straightforward and may miss more nuanced semantic signals.  
- Weakness: FrameNet augmentation yields only modest gains, suggesting limitations in external data integration.  
- Question: Why choose summation to combine word and entity attention vectors, and were alternatives (e.g., concatenation, gating) tried?  
- Question: Were other methods explored for generating gold attention vectors, especially those incorporating semantic relationships?  
- Question: What filtering/preprocessing was applied to FrameNet data, and were alternative integration strategies considered?  
- Overall: Despite minor issues, the work is a valuable, well-executed contribution and is suitable for acceptance.
