This paper presents a valuable approach to event detection by incorporating information from argument structure, a feature often overlooked in prior work. The core idea is sound and the proposed framework is both clear and relatively simple to understand. The authors effectively leverage a supervised attention mechanism – a technique proven successful in related fields like machine translation – to focus on relevant argumentative components, resulting in significant performance gains over baseline methods.

However, the attention mechanism itself could benefit from further exploration. The current implementation simply sums two attention vectors, which feels somewhat basic. More sophisticated approaches to attention vector calculation might yield further improvements. Similarly, while the two proposed strategies for supervised attention are reasonable starting points, they are relatively straightforward. The authors could strengthen their work by investigating more complex attention strategies. 

Overall, this is a solid contribution. Despite areas for potential refinement, the paper introduces an effective framework and demonstrates strong experimental results. The experiments appear well-designed and provide convincing evidence of the method’s efficacy. I recommend acceptance.
