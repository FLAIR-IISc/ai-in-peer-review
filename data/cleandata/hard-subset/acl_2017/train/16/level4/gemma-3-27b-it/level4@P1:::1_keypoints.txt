- Explicit incorporation of argument information into event detection yields clear performance gains using supervised attention.  
- Supervised attention is a well-motivated technique and experimental results convincingly show its effectiveness.  
- Ablation study demonstrates the benefit of including argument information.  
- Use of FrameNet data helps address data sparsity in event detection.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing to multiple state-of-the-art methods and analyzing attention strategies.  
- Combining word and entity attention vectors by simple summation is rudimentary and may limit performance.  
- Proposed gold-attention strategies (S1, S2) are straightforward; more complex strategies (e.g., exploiting syntactic dependencies) could help.  
- Paper lacks a detailed error analysis to identify common failure modes and guide future work.  
- Question: What is the computational/inference cost of incorporating argument information via attention compared to baselines?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the hyperparameter λ in Eq. 9, and was a comprehensive search performed?  
- Overall assessment: compelling, well-motivated framework with solid results; recommended for acceptance.  
- Minor editorial issues: "exiting" should be "existing" and "state-of-the-arts" should be "state of the art."  
- Formatting issues: Table 1 alignment and missing reference formatting (e.g., journal titles).
