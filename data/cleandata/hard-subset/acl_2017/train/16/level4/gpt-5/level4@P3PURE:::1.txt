- Strengths:
This paper leverages information from arguments—often overlooked yet important—to improve event detection performance. The framework is clear and simple. By incorporating a supervised attention mechanism, a method widely used in tasks such as machine translation, the system significantly outperforms the baseline.

- Weaknesses:
The final attention vector is obtained by simply summing the two attention vectors from each component. A more principled approach to combining these vectors could be more effective. For the supervised attention mechanism, two strategies are proposed, both of which are quite straightforward. Exploring more sophisticated strategies may yield better performance and is worth trying.

- General Discussion:
Although there are areas for improvement, this paper proposes an effective framework with strong performance. The experiments are solid. It can be considered for acceptance.
