- Strengths:
This work explicitly exploits argument information—often overlooked yet highly informative—to enhance event detection. The overall framework is clear and straightforward. By introducing a supervised attention mechanism (a technique widely used in tasks like neural machine translation), the approach yields substantial improvements over the baseline.

- Weaknesses:
The final attention is obtained by simply summing two attention vectors; a more principled fusion or interaction mechanism could be more appropriate. The two proposed supervision strategies for attention are fairly basic; exploring richer or more adaptive strategies might deliver further gains.

- General Discussion:
While there is room for refinement, the paper presents an effective framework with strong empirical performance. The experiments are solid. It is suitable for acceptance.
