- Strengths:
  - The paper explicitly leverages argument information—which is often ignored yet highly informative—to improve event detection (ED).
  - The framework is clear and simple.
  - The use of supervised attention to bias the model toward arguments is sensible and aligns with successful applications in other tasks such as machine translation.
  - The approach yields significant improvements over the baseline.

- Weaknesses:
  - The final attention vector is obtained by simply summing two attention vectors (from contextual words and entities). A more principled combination (e.g., learned weighting or gating) might be more appropriate and potentially more effective.
  - The two proposed supervision strategies for attention are quite straightforward. More sophisticated strategies may work better and are worth exploring.

- General Discussion:
  - Although there are aspects that can be improved, the paper presents an effective framework with strong empirical performance.
  - The experimental evaluation is solid.
  - Overall, this paper can be considered for acceptance.
