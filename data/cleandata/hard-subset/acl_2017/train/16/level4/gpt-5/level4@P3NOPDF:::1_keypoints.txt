- Summary: Leverages argument information via supervised attention to improve event detection, yielding significant gains over a strong baseline.
- Strength: Clear, simple, and reproducible framework that is well motivated.
- Strength: Effective use of supervised attention to focus on informative tokens and arguments.
- Strength: Empirical gains are substantial and experiments are carefully conducted.
- Weakness: Attention composition sums two attention vectors, possibly missing interactions and diluting signals.
- Suggestion: Explore alternatives to summation—learned weighted/gating, concatenation+projection, bilinear/tensor fusion, or multi-head attention—and include ablations.
- Weakness: Supervised attention strategies are straightforward and may underutilize supervision.
- Suggestion: Consider span-level/role-aware supervision, margin/posterior-regularization objectives, curriculum/confidence-weighted supervision, or multi-task learning with argument role labeling.
- Weakness: Need clarification on how supervision signals are derived, their noise characteristics, and sensitivity to supervision strength/quantity.
- Weakness: Additional qualitative analysis (e.g., attention visualizations for successes and failures) would clarify model behavior.
- Overall: Sound and effective approach with solid experiments; reviewer leans toward acceptance but recommends refinements.
- Minor: Provide implementation/training details, report variance across runs, and include a brief error analysis.
