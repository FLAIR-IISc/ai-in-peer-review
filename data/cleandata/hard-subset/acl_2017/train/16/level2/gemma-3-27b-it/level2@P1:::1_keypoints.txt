- Highlights that joint ED and argument extraction models under-utilize argument information, yielding minimal ED benefit despite joint training.  
- Proposes a supervised attention mechanism that focuses on argument words for trigger classification, exploring two strategies for gold attention vectors (S1, S2).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005 and further improvements with FrameNet augmentation.  
- Clear motivation and problem definition, illustrated with effective examples (e.g., "Mohamad fired Anwar...").  
- Sound methodology: appropriate use of attention and reasonable supervised guidance via gold attention vectors.  
- Thorough experimentation including strong baselines and ablation studies (ANN, ANN-S1, ANN-S2).  
- Strong empirical performance validating the proposed approach.  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized.  
- Limited novelty: attention mechanism is standard; main novelty is in how attention is supervised.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: optimal λ differs between S1 and S2 (1.0 vs 5.0), with unclear selection procedure.  
- Reliance on ACE 2005, a small/dated dataset; generalizability to larger/recent datasets is untested.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis to characterize remaining failure modes.  
- Question: How was λ chosen for S1 and S2 (grid search or intuition)?  
- Question: What are specific failure examples and their causes?  
- Question: Were other techniques explored to address the argument vs. trigger imbalance during training?  
- Question: What is the computational complexity compared to other state-of-the-art methods?  
- Additional: Reference formatting is inconsistent and should be fixed.  
- Additional: Remove informal phrasing like "on earth."  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with valuable contribution; recommend acceptance.
