- Existing joint ED+argument models underutilize argument information, yielding minimal ED gains.
- Proposes supervised attention guided by annotated arguments to focus on relevant context for trigger classification.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the ACE 2005 dataset.

- Clear motivation with illustrative examples showing how arguments disambiguate triggers.
- Well-defined supervised attention method with concrete gold-attention strategies and joint loss.
- Thorough experimental evaluation, including comparisons to baselines and FrameNet augmentation.
- Ablation study and results convincingly show argument information improves ED.

- Supervised attention itself is not novel; novelty is primarily its application to ED and supervision by arguments.
- Heavy reliance on ACE 2005 raises concerns about generalizability to other datasets or scenarios.
- Possible sensitivity to hyperparameters (differences between S1/S2 and lambda choices).
- Lacks detailed error analysis identifying event types or common failure cases.

- What is the computational cost of the supervised attention compared to standard ED models?
- How does performance vary across different ACE 2005 event types; which types benefit most from argument info?
- What filtering/preprocessing was applied to FrameNet data and how does it affect results?
- How much performance drop do you expect on datasets with sparser or lower-quality argument annotations?

- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Should add discussion of limitations, generalizability, and richer argument representations as future work.
- Citation style is slightly inconsistent and should be standardized.
