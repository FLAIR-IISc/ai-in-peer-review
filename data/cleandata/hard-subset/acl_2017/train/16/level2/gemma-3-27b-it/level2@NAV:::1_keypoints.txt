- Primary contribution: Proposes supervised attention to incorporate event-argument information into event detection, yielding measurable gains.  
- Secondary contribution: Systematically investigates different attention strategies within the proposed model.  
- Empirical result: Achieves new state-of-the-art F1 on the ACE 2005 dataset.  
- Clear motivation: Argues and exemplifies that argument information helps resolve event detection ambiguity.  
- Strong empirical validation: Thorough comparisons to pipeline and joint methods showing benefits.  
- Systematic analysis: Explores and reports the impact of various attention strategies.  
- Data imbalance awareness: Acknowledges and discusses trigger/argument imbalance and its effect on joint models.  
- FrameNet augmentation: Shows robustness of the approach by augmenting training with FrameNet data.  
- Limited attention analysis: Lacks detailed inspection/visualization of learned attention weights to explain model focus.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Missing thorough exploration of crucial hyperparameters, especially the λ in the joint loss.  
- Generalizability concerns: Experiments confined to ACE 2005; unclear performance on other datasets or domains.  
- Presentation issues: Some repetition and verbosity in motivation and related work sections.  
- Requested clarification: Provide attention-weight visualizations for representative examples.  
- Requested clarification: Report the range tested for λ and how performance varied.  
- Requested clarification: Discuss expected behavior on datasets with different event types, sizes, or annotation styles.
