- Proposes supervised attention for event detection (ED) that explicitly exploits annotated argument information.  
- Argues existing methods underuse argument cues, leading to suboptimal ED performance.  
- Uses a neural model with supervised attention to focus on argument words and is trained on ACE 2005.  
- Analyzes joint ED-AE models and shows they tend to favor argument extraction over ED due to data imbalance.  
- Introduces a supervised attention-based ED model to directly leverage argument signals.  
- Examines two strategies for constructing gold attentions from annotated arguments and shows they improve ED.  
- Novel approach that departs from prior methods by explicitly using argument information.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the ACE 2005 dataset.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of joint model limitations, contributing useful insights.  
- Evaluation limited to ACE 2005, raising concerns about generalizability to other datasets/domains.  
- Heavy reliance on annotated arguments, which may be unavailable or noisy in practice.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Asks how the method would extend to domains without reliable argument annotations.  
- Requests a more detailed computational complexity analysis and scalability implications.  
- Raises concern about potential overfitting of the supervised attention mechanism to training data.
