**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that existing joint methods for ED and argument extraction (AE) do not effectively utilize argument information for ED, leading to insignificant improvements in ED performance. The proposed approach uses a neural network-based model with a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words, which are expected to provide significant clues for ED. The authors evaluate their approach on the ACE 2005 dataset and demonstrate its effectiveness in improving ED performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel approach to exploit argument information explicitly for ED via supervised attention mechanisms.
2. They introduce a neural network-based model with a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words.
3. They evaluate their approach on the ACE 2005 dataset and demonstrate its effectiveness in improving ED performance.

**Strengths**

1. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated argument for the importance of exploiting argument information for ED.
2. The proposed approach is novel and well-designed, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of its effectiveness.
