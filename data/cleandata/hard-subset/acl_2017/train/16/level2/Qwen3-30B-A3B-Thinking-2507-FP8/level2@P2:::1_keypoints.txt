- Contribution: Shows joint models mainly help argument extraction (AE) but not event detection (ED), since arguments are used indirectly.
- Contribution: Proposes a supervised attention mechanism for ED that explicitly leverages argument information via two strategies (S1: argument words; S2: arguments + surrounding words).
- Contribution: Reports state-of-the-art results on the ACE 2005 dataset.

- Strength: Convincing analysis that joint methods underuse argument information for ED, with well-supported reasons (data imbalance, pre-prediction of triggers).
- Strength: Thorough experiments and ablation studies demonstrating benefits of argument information and the precision/recall trade-off between S1 and S2.
- Strength: Effective use of FrameNet to augment training data, showing robustness to data sparsity.
- Strength: Clear explanation of the attention mechanism and its supervised training procedure.

- Weakness: Claiming state-of-the-art is overstated due to limited baseline comparisons and omission of some relevant recent methods.
- Weakness: The distinction between their "supervised" attention and other attention mechanisms is insufficiently justified, and its advantage for data sparsity is not proven.
- Weakness: Assumes argument annotations are available during training and does not address real-world settings where arguments may be unavailable at inference.
- Weakness: The ANN baseline is weak (F1=64.6) compared to stronger baselines like Liu's PSL, making improvements over it potentially misleading.
- Weakness: Lacks an ablation that isolates the specific contribution of the supervised attention mechanism versus other model components.

- Question: Why were only the specific baselines in Table 3 chosen instead of more recent state-of-the-art methods?
- Question: How would the method perform when argument information is not available during training or inference?
- Question: Can you provide stronger evidence that supervised attention is fundamentally different from and superior to other attention-based approaches for addressing data sparsity?
