- Proposes a supervised attention mechanism that leverages annotated arguments via gold attention vectors to improve Event Detection, achieving SOTA on ACE 2005.  
- Contributions: analysis of joint model limitations, a supervised attention ED model with two gold-vector strategies (S1, S2), and experimental validation including FrameNet data.  
- Strength: Clear analysis of joint-model shortcomings and the argument-trigger annotation imbalance; well-motivated novel solution.  
- Strength: Innovative supervised-attention mechanism that explicitly incorporates argument information.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments with significant F1 improvements and robustness gains using additional FrameNet data.  
- Strength: Well-written paper with detailed methodology and reproducible setup.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to ACE 2005, raising generalization concerns to other domains/datasets.  
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on annotated arguments; unclear behavior in low-resource or noisy-annotation scenarios.  
- Weakness: No comparison with recent unsupervised or semi-supervised attention methods.  
- Weakness: Computational cost and scalability of constructing gold attention vectors not analyzed.  
- Question: How does the method perform on datasets other than ACE 2005?  
- Question: Can the model be adapted to settings without annotated arguments or with noisy annotations, and what is the performance impact?  
- Question: What is the computational overhead and how does the approach scale to larger datasets?  
- Overall: Strong, well-supported contribution but limited by reliance on annotated arguments and narrow evaluation; recommend accept with minor revisions.
