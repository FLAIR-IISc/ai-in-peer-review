- Proposes supervised attention for Event Detection that explicitly uses annotated event arguments to guide trigger classification.
- Contribution: novel method that leverages argument annotations via supervised attention to address weaknesses in prior joint models.
- Contribution: strong empirical validation on ACE 2005, achieving new SOTA F1 (71.7) with thorough ablation and oracle experiments.
- Strength: excellent motivation and novelty—clearly argues and remedies why prior joint models failed to pass argument information back to triggers.
- Strength: strong and convincing results, notably high precision (78.0) and substantial gains over recent baselines.
- Strength: thorough experimental design isolating the effect of supervised attention and validating the core hypothesis with ANN-Gold.
- Strength: paper is clear and well-written; model, objectives, and experiments are explained concisely.
- Weakness: requires gold-standard argument annotations for training, limiting applicability to datasets with argument labels.
- Weakness: unclear robustness to noisy automatic argument labels from FrameNet augmentation; lacks analysis of label quality and noise impact.
- Weakness: base event detector is a simple 3-layer feed-forward network, so interaction with stronger encoders (e.g., BiLSTM) is not explored.
