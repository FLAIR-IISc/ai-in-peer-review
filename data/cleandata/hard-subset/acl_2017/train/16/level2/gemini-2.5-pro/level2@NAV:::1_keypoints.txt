- Summary/Contribution: Proposes supervising an attention mechanism with annotated argument locations to improve event detection, showing state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005.

- Strength — Well-motivated/Novel: Supervising attention with argument locations is an elegant, direct way to inject argument information for disambiguating triggers.

- Strength — Strong empirical performance: Achieves new SOTA F1 (71.7 vs. prior 71.3) on ACE 2005, outperforming strong baselines.

- Strength — Thorough analysis: Experiments show gains come specifically from argument information (not just entities), validating the core hypothesis.

- Weakness — Requires annotated arguments: Method depends on training data with argument annotations and lacks discussion of partial/no-argument settings.

- Weakness — Simple base architecture: Uses a standard ANN; unclear if improvements persist with stronger sequence models (RNNs/Transformers).

- Weakness — Limited ablation on attention strategies: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameters (Gaussian, λ) and limited visualization/intuition for why S2 outperforms S1.

- Question — Robustness to noisy supervision: How well does the model perform if attention is supervised with imperfect, predicted argument labels?

- Question — Request for qualitative evidence: Can the authors provide attention visualizations or examples for ambiguous triggers to show how attention focuses on key arguments?
