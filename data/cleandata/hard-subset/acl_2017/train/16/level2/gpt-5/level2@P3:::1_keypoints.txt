- Proposes supervising attention for event detection using argument annotations with two strategies: S1 (argument tokens) and S2 (arguments + Gaussian neighborhood).  
- Integrates word- and entity-type attention and trains ED classifier to align attention to constructed gold targets.  
- Evaluates on ACE 2005 and augments data using FrameNet-derived events; reports improvements over baselines and prior SOTA.  
- Main contribution 1: supervised attention formulation using argument annotations with concrete S1/S2 strategies.  
- Main contribution 2: empirical evidence that argument-informed attention improves ED F1 on ACE 2005 vs. unsupervised attention and prior systems.  
- Main contribution 3: diagnostics showing gains stem from argument supervision rather than entity-type cues alone.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated problem framing with quantitative context on joint EE vs ED benefits.  
- Strength: method is simple, plausible, and requires no argument information at test time.  
- Strength: substantial and statistically significant empirical gains (e.g., ANN 64.6 vs ANN-S2 71.7 F1).  
- Strength: diagnostic comparisons (ANN-ENT vs ANN-Gold variants) effectively isolate argument supervision effects.  
- Strength: sensible interpretation that S1 favors precision and S2 improves recall, aligning with supervision designs.  
- Weakness: internal baseline (ANN unsupervised) is weak and lacks stronger sequence encoders, muddying attribution of gains.  
- Weakness: relies on gold argument/entity annotations for supervision, limiting portability to domains without such labels.  
- Weakness: unclear attention combination/normalization—summing αw and αe then comparing to normalized α* may create scale mismatch.  
- Weakness: incomplete ablations (e.g., only αw or αe supervised, σ/window sensitivity, using predicted entity types).  
- Weakness: reproducibility issues—missing specifics on CRL context window, tokenization, test-time use of gold entities, and λ selection.  
- Question: Is α renormalized after summing αw and αe, and how does normalization affect the supervised loss?  
- Question: What exact context window size n was used and how sensitive are results to n?  
- Question: Are gold entity types used at test time on ACE, and can results with predicted entities be provided?  
- Question: Can Skip-CNN F1 on the same split be reported or approximated for fairer comparison?  
- Question: For S2, how sensitive are results to window size and σ, and was µ fixed at 0?  
- Suggestion: add a stronger context encoder (e.g., BiLSTM/CNN) to isolate attention’s contribution.  
- Suggestion: include ablations on attention normalization, word vs. entity attention, and attention quality metrics (top-k alignment).  
- Suggestion: evaluate with silver/noisy argument supervision (predicted arguments) to show applicability without gold labels.  
- Suggestion: expand comparisons (include Skip-CNN F1), report runtime/params, and improve clarity/hyperparameter reporting and error analysis.  
- Overall: promising, easy-to-adopt idea that yields consistent gains but needs stronger baselines, clearer normalization/ablations, and fuller comparisons to be fully compelling.
