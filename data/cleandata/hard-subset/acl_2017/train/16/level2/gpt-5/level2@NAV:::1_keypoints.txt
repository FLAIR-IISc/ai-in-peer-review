- Proposes supervised attention that leverages argument information to disambiguate event triggers for event detection (ED).  
- Introduces two attention supervision strategies: S1 (argument-only) and S2 (argument-plus-neighborhood via Gaussian smoothing) with a joint loss aligning learned and gold attention.  
- Reports state-of-the-art F1 on ACE 2005 with consistent gains over baselines and further recall improvement using FrameNet augmentation.  
- Contribution: Shows argument-supervised attention substantially improves ED over unsupervised attention and prior systems on ACE 2005.  
- Contribution: Offers a simple, general attention supervision scheme (S1/S2) that can be applied at training time without requiring arguments at test time.  
- Contribution: Provides empirical analysis explaining limited gains of prior joint models and proposes a targeted ED-focused remedy.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains (ANN-S2 achieves best reported F1 on ACE 2005) with statistical significance and higher precision than prior SOTA.  
- Strength: Well-designed ablations (ANN, ANN-ENT, ANN-Gold1/2) that isolate the value of argument supervision beyond entity information.  
- Strength: Practical approach using gold arguments only for training supervision, so no arguments are needed at inference.  
- Strength: Transparent experimental setup with clear hyperparameters, training details, and documented FrameNet augmentation pipeline.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether entity types used at test time on ACE 2005 are gold or predicted; using gold entities could inflate results.  
- Weakness: Core ANN baseline (fixed window, no sequence model) may be weaker than contemporary BiLSTM/CNN baselines, weakening comparative claims.  
- Weakness: Sparse details on FrameNet-to-ACE mapping and automatic entity label quality; possible noise affecting precision/recall is underanalyzed.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis—no per-event-type breakdown, error analysis, or attention visualizations to show where the method helps or fails.  
- Question: Are entity types at ACE test time gold or predicted; if predicted, which model and its performance, and ED results with predicted entities?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to window size, λ, and S2’s Gaussian parameters; is there a robustness study?  
- Question: Can the authors provide per-event-type results and qualitative attention examples showing disambiguation of ambiguous triggers?  
- Question: How was the FrameNet-to-ACE argument mapping validated and what proportion of FN-augmented samples are noisy?
