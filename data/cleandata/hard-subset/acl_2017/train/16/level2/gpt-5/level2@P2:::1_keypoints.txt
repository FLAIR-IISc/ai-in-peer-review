- Proposes supervising attention for event detection using annotated argument positions/types (ANN-S1/S2) with Gaussian smoothing as an alternative.  
- Shows supervised attention improves ED over unsupervised attention and reports competitive SOTA F1 on ACE 2005; also experiments with FrameNet augmentation.  
- Claims joint ED+AE methods help AE more than ED and motivates directly using arguments for ED.

Strengths:
- Clear, focused idea with simple implementation that plausibly injects useful inductive bias.  
- Direct contrast between unsupervised and supervised attention isolates the benefit of argument supervision.  
- Systematic comparison of hard (S1) vs. smoothed (S2) attention provides useful insight into precision/recall trade-offs.  
- Upper-bound ablations using gold attentions illustrate the potential value of argument information.  
- Lightweight model achieves competitive results and FrameNet augmentation shows gains from extra data.  
- Addresses a practical limitation of joint models and proposes a direct remedy.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: essentially adds argument supervision to a standard context-weighting mechanism.  
- Small improvement over prior SOTA with unclear statistical support (missing variance, seeds, and multiple runs).  
- Heavy reliance on entity annotations with unclear test-time assumptions (gold vs. predicted entities not specified).  
- Incomplete model specification and missing ablations (e.g., exact we computation, window size, positional embeddings).  
- No ablation disentangling word vs. entity attention (αw vs. αe) or a stronger entity-attention control.  
- Analysis of joint models’ shortcomings is speculative and lacks empirical validation.  
- Baseline comparisons incomplete — lacks predicted-argument feature baselines and some literature under same conditions.

Questions for authors:
- Are entity mentions/types assumed gold at test time, or are they predicted? Report robustness with predicted entities.  
- Precisely how is we computed (transformations, shapes, activations, shared parameters)?  
- What is the context window size n, and were larger windows/positional embeddings tried?  
- How many runs/seeds were used; report mean and std and rerun competitors where feasible for fair stats.  
- Provide ablations for αw-only, αe-only, αw+αe and a variant without entity inputs.  
- How sensitive is S2 to μ, σ, and window size; were these tuned on dev and how robust are results?

Additional comments:
- Clarify FrameNet training/test pipeline and whether entity annotations there are gold or predicted; discuss domain mismatch.  
- Fix typos and reference formatting; ensure tables consistently report P/R/F1 and resource conditions.  
- Consider releasing code and FrameNet processing scripts for reproducibility.

Overall:
- Clear and useful idea with competitive results, but moderate novelty and several methodological/evaluation details need clarification and stronger ablations, robustness tests, and statistically grounded comparisons.
