- Proposes supervising attention with annotated event arguments (and optional neighbor words) to improve trigger classification, combining word and entity-type contexts with joint classification and attention losses.
- Contributions: explicit argument-supervised attention for ED with consistent ACE 2005 gains; careful ablations comparing supervision strategies and oracles; FrameNet-derived augmentation to address data sparsity.

- Clear, focused motivation: addresses why prior joint models may fail by directly supervising attention on arguments.
- Strong empirical gains and solid ablations: achieves new SOTA on ACE 2005 (ANN-S2 F1 71.7) with statistically significant improvements and oracle controls.
- Demonstrates arguments add signal beyond generic entity presence: ANN-ENT underperforms supervised-attention variants.
- Simple, interpretable attention design: dot-product attention with supervised targets and a clear trade-off between S1 (precision) and S2 (recall).
- FrameNet augmentation further improves F1 and recall, supporting the data-sparsity hypothesis.

- Relies on gold entity types/argument positions for supervision; unclear whether test-time entities are gold or predicted, affecting realism and comparability.
- Moderate novelty in mechanism and uses a simple fixed-window encoder (no BiLSTM/CNN); needs comparison showing supervision, not encoder choice, drives gains.
- Missing experimental details and sensitivity analyses (context length n, λ, Gaussian window/σ, early stopping, runtime, seed variance).
- Comparisons and tables lack clarity and completeness (formatting issues, inconsistent FrameNet augmentation comparisons vs prior work).
- Limited analysis: lacks error breakdowns by event type/ambiguity/distance and attention visualizations; claims about joint models remain speculative without targeted experiments.

- Clarify whether ACE test uses gold or predicted entities; provide results with predicted entities (e.g., RPI) if possible.
- Report the fixed context length n and sensitivity to n.
- For NA candidates, clarify whether attention supervision uses averaged entities only or includes other event arguments and how that affects precision/recall.
- Quantify FrameNet augmentation: number of added instances, class distribution shifts, and mapping quality from FN frames to ACE types.
- Provide robustness analyses for λ, Gaussian window size, and σ.

- Clarify the t-test protocol and report mean ± std over multiple random seeds.
- Fix citation/table formatting and proofreading issues.
