- Proposes supervised attention using argument information to improve event detection and address imbalance in joint ED-AE models.
- Empirically demonstrates improved ED performance on ACE 2005, with further gains from FrameNet augmentation.
- Contribution: provides empirical justification that argument information benefits ED, especially for ambiguous triggers.
- Contribution: identifies a limitation of joint ED-AE models due to trigger-argument data imbalance.
- Contribution: introduces a simple, practical supervised attention mechanism using gold attentions.
- Strength: strong, statistically significant empirical results over baselines and prior SOTA.
- Strength: clear motivation and illustrative example (e.g., "fired") showing argument utility.
- Strength: thorough evaluation with ablation studies isolating component impacts (ANN, ANN-ENT, ANN-S1, ANN-S2).
- Strength: paper is well-written, logically structured, and method description is clear.
- Weakness: supervised attention itself is not novel; novelty is primarily in its application to argument-aware ED.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on gold argument annotations; potential poor generalization to noisy or limited annotations.
- Weakness: limited analysis and visualization of learned attention weights; lacks qualitative insights into model behavior.
- Weakness: comparisons to SOTA are somewhat dated; missing evaluation against transformer/BERT-based ED models.
- Question: how sensitive is model performance to errors or inconsistencies in argument annotations?
- Question: can authors provide examples/analysis of attention weights and any surprising patterns?
- Question: how does the model compare to transformer-based ED models and what are challenges/benefits of integration?
- Question: how does computational cost and performance scale with larger context window sizes?
- Additional: notation should be clarified/consistent; the "Only 10% of triggers in ACE 2005 are entities" statement needs context.
- Overall: solid, incremental contribution with strong empirical support; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
