- Paper tackles event detection (ED) by explicitly using argument information via supervised attention to resolve ambiguous triggers.  
- Identifies that joint ED and argument extraction (AE) models often prioritize AE due to data imbalance and fail to leverage arguments for ED.  
- Proposes a supervised attention mechanism trained on gold-standard arguments to incorporate argument information directly into ED.  
- Shows improved performance on ACE 2005 (with and without FrameNet augmentation), achieving state-of-the-art results.  

- Empirical analysis highlights limitations of existing joint ED-AE models and their tendency to underuse argument cues.  
- The supervised attention approach provides a practical way to inject argument information into ED.  
- Strong empirical results on ACE 2005 support the method’s effectiveness.  

- Clear motivation and problem definition, illustrated with a concrete example (e.g., "fired").  
- Well-described methodology and architecture with two strategies (S1, S2) for constructing gold attention vectors.  
- Comprehensive experiments including baselines, ablations, FrameNet augmentation, and t-test significance.  
- Significant gains in precision, recall, and F1, reaching state-of-the-art on ACE 2005.  
- Experimental details given to aid reproducibility (hyperparameters, dataset prep).  

- Supervised attention mechanism is not fundamentally novel; novelty is mainly in its supervision and application to ED.  
- Method depends heavily on quality of argument annotations; sensitivity to noisy/incomplete annotations is unexplored.  
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis to show where the model still fails.  
- Limited discussion of generalizability beyond ACE 2005 to other datasets or domains.  

- How sensitive is model performance to argument annotation quality, and can noisy annotations be mitigated?  
- Request for a detailed error analysis identifying common model failures and causes.  
- How will the model perform on other ED datasets or domains, and can generalizability be improved?  
- What is the computational overhead of the supervised attention versus standard ED models for large-scale use?  
- Provide detailed ablation results for attention strategies S1 and S2, including λ values and impacts on precision/recall/F1.  

- References are mostly consistent but a few lack complete details (e.g., page numbers).  
- Suggest adding a limitations section and future research directions.  
- Related work discussion should be expanded, especially on recent attention mechanisms and ED advances.
