- Proposes supervised attention using annotated arguments to improve event detection (ED), addressing weaknesses of joint ED/AE models.
- Empirically shows explicit argument incorporation helps disambiguate triggers and benefits ED more than traditional joint models.
- Introduces a supervised attention framework with two strategies for constructing "gold attentions" to integrate argument information.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005 and further improvements with FrameNet data augmentation.
- Clear motivation and illustrative example (e.g., “fired”) demonstrating need to leverage arguments for disambiguation.
- Methodology is sound: gold-attention supervision effectively guides model focus toward relevant arguments.
- Strong empirical validation with insightful ablation studies demonstrating impact of attention strategies.
- Thorough experimental setup with detailed dataset, hyperparameters, and evaluation metrics to facilitate reproducibility.
- Well-written and organized paper; figures effectively illustrate model architecture and attention mechanism.
- Core components (neural architecture, attention) are not novel; main contribution is their specific combination/application to argument-aware ED.
- Model performance heavily depends on the quality of argument annotations; paper lacks discussion of noisy/inconsistent annotations’ impact.
- Limited analysis of attention weights—more visualization needed to show what the model actually attends to.
- Evaluation confined to ACE 2005; unclear generalizability to other datasets, event schemas, or domains.
- Request: provide attention weight visualizations for representative sentences to illustrate model behavior.
- Question: how sensitive is performance to annotation quality and are strategies considered for noisy/incomplete annotations?
- Question: how does the ED-focused model affect downstream argument extraction compared to joint ED/AE models?
- Question: did authors try imbalance-handling techniques (class weighting, oversampling) for the joint baselines?
- Suggestion: include F1 scores for the NA class in Table 3 for a more complete comparison.
- Suggestion: add discussion of related work on attention mechanisms in other NLP tasks to better contextualize the approach.
- Overall assessment: strong empirical contribution with solid results; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing weaknesses.
