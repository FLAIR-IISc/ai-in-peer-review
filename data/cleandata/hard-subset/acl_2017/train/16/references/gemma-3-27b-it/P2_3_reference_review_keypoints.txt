- Paper proposes using argument information with a supervised attention mechanism to improve event detection (ED).
- Demonstrates improved ED performance on ACE 2005, both with and without FrameNet augmentation.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that explicit argument information benefits ED beyond prior joint ED/AE work.
- Contribution: introduces a supervised attention framework that focuses on argument words for trigger identification/categorization.
- Contribution: conducts comprehensive experiments, including ablations and FrameNet data augmentation.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem formulation, with an effective illustrative example (e.g., “fired”).
- Strength: strong empirical results with consistent gains over strong pipeline and joint baselines and significance testing.
- Strength: well-designed supervised attention mechanism and exploration of gold attention strategies (S1, S2).
- Strength: thorough experimentation including data augmentation and systematic comparisons.
- Weakness: limited novelty in core components—attention and neural ED models are not fundamentally new; novelty is in their combination/application.
- Weakness: lacks attention visualizations or detailed analysis to show what the model actually attends to.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameter λ (attention loss weight) is underexplored and seems arbitrary.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—paper should identify event types or triggers that remain challenging.
- Question: please provide examples of learned attention weights to validate model focus on argument words.
- Question: elaborate on selection and sensitivity analysis for λ and report its effect on performance.
- Question: provide a more detailed error analysis identifying problematic event/trigger types.
- Question: discuss generalizability of results to other datasets with different event types/argument schemas.
- Additional: paper is well-written and organized; references comprehensive.
- Additional: FrameNet data link is broken; footnote on percentage of triggers that are entities is helpful.
- Overall assessment: recommend acceptance with minor revisions, especially adding attention visualizations and deeper error analysis.
