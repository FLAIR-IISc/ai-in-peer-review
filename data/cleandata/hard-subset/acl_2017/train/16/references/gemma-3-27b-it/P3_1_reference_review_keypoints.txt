- Paper proposes supervised attention on argument words to improve event detection (ED) and shows gains on ACE 2005, with further improvement using FrameNet augmentation.
- Argues existing joint ED+AE models fail to effectively leverage argument information for ED.

- Contribution: Empirical observation that joint models do not significantly improve ED, motivating alternative approaches.
- Contribution: Introduces a supervised attention mechanism focused on argument words, using gold attention supervision (two strategies S1 and S2).
- Contribution: Demonstrates consistent, significant performance gains over prior state-of-the-art and with FrameNet augmentation.

- Strength: Clear problem statement and motivating examples illustrating argument-driven disambiguation (e.g., "Mohamad fired Anwar...").
- Strength: Well-designed supervised attention approach with two gold-attention construction strategies.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation including ablations (ANN, ANN-S1, ANN-S2) and statistical significance testing.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance improvements on ACE 2005.
- Strength: Well-written and well-organized manuscript.

- Weakness: Supervised attention mechanism is not fundamentally novel; novelty is primarily in application and supervision design.
- Weakness: Method relies on gold argument annotations during training; robustness to noisy/incomplete annotations is not addressed.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, particularly the λ weight in joint loss.
- Weakness: Limited error analysis—missing insights on remaining error types and failure cases.
- Weakness: Sparse description of FrameNet processing and use of the RPI Joint Information Extraction System to label entities.

- Q: Can you quantify how "insignificant" improvements from existing joint models are across different systems?
- Q: How sensitive is model performance to λ; can you provide F1 vs. λ results?
- Q: How robust is the model to noisy or incomplete argument annotations or predicted-argument supervision?
- Q: Can you provide a more detailed error analysis identifying frequently misclassified triggers and common error patterns?
- Q: How exactly was the RPI system used to label ACE entity mentions in FrameNet, and what are its limitations?

- Additional: Related work section should be expanded to cover more recent attention and event extraction literature.
- Additional: Discuss computational cost of the proposed approach compared to existing methods.

- Overall: Strong, well-executed paper with effective idea and compelling results; recommend acceptance after addressing noted weaknesses.
