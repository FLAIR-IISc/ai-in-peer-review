- Paper improves event detection by supervising attention with annotated argument information to better disambiguate trigger words.  
- Identifies that joint ED+AE models often underperform on ED due to training-data imbalance favoring arguments over triggers.  
- Proposes a supervised attention mechanism trained with gold attention vectors derived from argument annotations (two strategies: S1 and S2).  
- Provides empirical analysis explaining why existing joint models fail to boost ED, attributing it to data imbalance and indirect use of arguments.  
- Shows consistent performance improvements on ACE 2005, with and without FrameNet augmentation, outperforming several baselines.  
- The attention mechanism itself is standard; the main novelty is how the attention is supervised rather than the mechanism.  
- Method depends heavily on high-quality, complete argument annotations; robustness to noisy/incomplete labels is a concern.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study on attention construction parameters (e.g., Gaussian window size in S2).  
- Evaluation is limited to ACE 2005, so generalizability to other datasets and genres is untested.  
- Strength: clear problem statement and motivating examples (e.g., “fired”) that illustrate argument utility.  
- Strength: well-designed experimental setup with multiple baselines and careful hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: intuitive and effective use of gold attention vectors, with exploration of two construction strategies.  
- Strength: reported gains are consistent and statistically significant, supporting the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: thorough related work discussion that accurately positions the contribution.  
- Question: How sensitive is model performance to the quality of argument annotations, and can the method handle noisy/incomplete annotations?  
- Question: Can the authors provide more ablations (e.g., varying S2 window size) to clarify parameter impacts?  
- Question: Why does S2 consistently outperform S1—more information vs. specific attention-weight distribution effects?  
- Question: Have the authors considered joint ED and AE training to see if supervised attention could also benefit argument extraction?  
- Additional: Suggest simplifying some mathematical notation (Section 3.1) and adding attention visualizations for examples.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper with meaningful empirical contribution; accept with minor revisions.
