- Paper proposes supervised attention to focus on argument words to improve event detection, showing gains on ACE2005 and with FrameNet augmentation.  
- Empirically demonstrates that incorporating argument information improves event detection, challenging the independence of ED and AE.  
- Introduces a supervised attention framework to integrate argument cues into a neural ED system, simple but effective.  
- Provides thorough experiments, including baselines, ablations, and FrameNet augmentation, strengthening result validity.  
- Strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements over prior state-of-the-art on ACE2005.  
- Clear motivation and problem definition, with convincing examples (e.g., ambiguous triggers like “fired”).  
- Well-structured, readable presentation with helpful diagrams.  
- Comprehensive baseline comparisons covering joint, pipeline, feature-based, and embedding-based methods.  
- Effective use of FrameNet data to mitigate data sparsity and boost performance.  
- Core technique (supervised attention) is not novel; contribution is incremental.  
- Argument representation is simplified (binary present/absent); richer role/semantic modeling not explored.  
- Reliance on ACE2005 limits generalizability; dataset is small and single-domain.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis to show where the method still fails.  
- Asks why simple additive attention was chosen over more advanced variants (e.g., self-attention/transformers).  
- Queries whether masking or down-weighting entity-based trigger candidates was investigated given few entity triggers.  
- Requests concrete ACE2005 examples where argument information was crucial for disambiguation.  
- Asks about computational overhead of supervised attention versus baseline in training and inference.  
- Suggests improving equation formatting and discussing other external knowledge sources beyond FrameNet.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with incremental technical novelty but valuable empirical contribution; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
