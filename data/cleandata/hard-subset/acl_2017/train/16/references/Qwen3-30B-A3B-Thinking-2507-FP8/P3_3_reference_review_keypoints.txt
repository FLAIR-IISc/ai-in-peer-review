- Identifies a key limitation: joint event extraction models incorporate arguments but do not directly improve event detection (ED).
- Proposes a supervised attention mechanism that uses argument information for ED, with two strategies (S1 and S2) to construct gold attention vectors.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005 (ANN-S2 achieving 71.7% F1).

- Clearly identifies and supports the gap in literature showing why joint models fail to improve ED.
- Provides compelling experimental validation and ablation studies showing gold-attention models outperform baselines.
- Addresses data sparsity by augmenting with FrameNet, improving results further (ANN-S2 + FrameNet = 71.9% F1).
- Presents a clear, well-explained methodology and implementation for supervised attention and the two strategies.

- Supervised attention mechanism lacks novelty given prior work on attention supervision.
- Potential data leakage: using gold argument annotations to train attention may give the model information unavailable at test time.
- Insufficient comparisons with more recent event extraction models that might better leverage argument information.
- No qualitative analysis or visualizations of learned attention weights to verify that attention focuses on argument words.
- Overstates critique of joint models and does not explore integration of the supervised attention mechanism into joint frameworks.

- How would the supervised attention mechanism operate in a joint framework where arguments are predicted rather than given?
- Can you provide visualizations/examples showing the model’s attention focusing on argument words?
- How do you mitigate the data leakage concern from using argument annotations as supervision?
- Have you compared your method to more recent models (e.g., graph neural networks or transformer-based approaches) that incorporate argument information?
