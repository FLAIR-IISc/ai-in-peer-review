- Proposes supervised attention to explicitly leverage argument information for event detection, addressing limitations of joint ED+AE models.  
- Main contributions: (1) identifies joint-model limitation in using argument information, (2) introduces supervised attention using gold argument vectors, (3) achieves SOTA on ACE 2005 (ANN-S2 71.7% F1 vs prior 70.7%).  
- Strong motivation and problem identification, illustrated with the "fired" ambiguity and analysis of data imbalance and lack of argument info in trigger pre-prediction.  
- Clear, well-designed experiments with systematic comparisons, ablation studies, and FrameNet augmentation validating robustness.  
- Novel technical approach: supervised attention with two strategies (S1, S2) trained from annotated arguments rather than unsupervised attention.  
- Highly relevant to event extraction; improvements on ACE 2005 make results directly comparable to prior work.  
- Weakness: Core attention mechanism builds on prior work and the paper under-articulates what differentiates its supervised attention from existing attention methods.  
- Weakness: Lacks qualitative examples showing attention behavior and concrete cases where argument information corrected ambiguous triggers.  
- Weakness: Does not analyze failure cases or identify which event types or sentence structures remain challenging.  
- Weakness: Unclear how supervised attention specifically addresses data sparsity and how it interacts with FrameNet augmentation.  
- Question: Precisely how does your supervised attention differ from attention used in other NLP tasks and prior ED work?  
- Question: Can you provide qualitative examples demonstrating attention helping trigger identification in ambiguous cases (e.g., "fired")?  
- Question: Please clarify the relationship between supervised attention and data sparseness, and how FrameNet augmentation affects this.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — strong contribution, well-validated improvements, and weaknesses are addressable.
