- Summary: Proposes supervised attention for event detection that uses gold argument information (two strategies S1/S2); ANN-S2 achieves 71.7% F1 on ACE 2005.

- Contribution 1: Identifies that argument information is underutilized in ED and provides crucial disambiguating clues for triggers.

- Contribution 2: Introduces a supervised attention mechanism trained with argument supervision to focus on argument words for trigger detection.

- Contribution 3: Systematically evaluates different gold-attention construction strategies and their effects on precision/recall.

- Strength 1: Clear problem statement and motivation with an illustrative example (e.g., "fired") showing why arguments matter.

- Strength 2: Rigorous empirical evaluation with ablation studies and statistically significant improvement over prior state-of-the-art.

- Strength 3: Simple, practical approach with clear implementation and hyper-parameter details enabling reproducibility.

- Strength 4: Ablation results convincingly show that argument information — not just attention — drives the performance gains.

- Weakness 1: Supervised attention itself lacks novelty; similar techniques exist in other NLP tasks.

- Weakness 2: Inadequate comparison to recent attention-based ED methods (e.g., Skip-CNN) and limited discussion of differences.

- Weakness 3: Missing qualitative analysis/examples demonstrating how supervised attention changes model behavior on ambiguous triggers.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient discussion of limitations and robustness when argument annotations are scarce, noisy, or uninformative.

- Question 1: How does the supervised attention compare empirically and architecturally to other attention mechanisms in recent ED work (e.g., Skip-CNN)?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide concrete qualitative examples showing the mechanism helping ambiguous triggers?

- Question 3: How robust is the method in domains without argument annotations or with noisy/limited argument information?

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-evaluated contribution that leverages argument supervision to improve ED; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty discussion, comparisons, qualitative analysis, and limitations.
