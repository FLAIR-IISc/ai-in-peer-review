- Proposes supervised attention leveraging argument information for event detection with two gold-attention strategies (S1, S2) evaluated on ACE 2005.  
- Argues existing joint models improve argument extraction but not event detection, so arguments are underused for ED.  
- Contribution: novel supervised attention mechanism explicitly using argument info for ED.  
- Contribution: two strategies (S1, S2) to construct gold attention vectors from annotations.  
- Contribution: strong empirical results showing significant improvement over prior methods.  
- Strength: convincingly identifies a critical gap in joint event extraction (Table 1 evidence).  
- Strength: clear, simple, and effective methodology with ablation studies showing argument info is crucial.  
- Strength: strong experimental performance (ANN-S2 71.7% F1) and benefits from FrameNet augmentation.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—insufficient differentiation from existing attention-based methods (e.g., Mi et al. 2016).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why the approach works; lacks qualitative inspection of attention weights.  
- Weakness: assumes perfect argument annotations; no evaluation under noisy or incomplete annotations.  
- Weakness: overstates claims about joint models without detailed comparison of argument usage.  
- Question: How does the supervised attention handle missing/incomplete argument annotations—does it revert to unsupervised attention?  
- Question: Can the authors provide detailed analysis of learned attention weights and differences between S1 and S2?  
- Question: Why does FrameNet augmentation give only modest gains despite being an additional resource?  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address novelty clarification, deeper analysis, and limitations.
