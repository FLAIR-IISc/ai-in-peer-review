- Paper proposes supervising attention for event detection (ED) using gold argument spans to guide attention over context and entity types.
- Model treats each token as a trigger candidate and computes similarity-based attention over nearby words and entity-type embeddings, with two supervision variants: S1 (arguments only) and S2 (arguments + Gaussian neighborhood).
- Attention supervision is added via a squared-loss auxiliary loss combined with trigger classification loss.
- Experiments on ACE 2005 show substantial gains over an unsupervised-attention baseline and improvements over reported prior systems; FrameNet-augmented training yields further gains.

Main contributions:
- Introduces supervised attention for ED using gold argument spans, showing improved trigger classification.
- Compares two supervision strategies (argument-only vs. argument+neighborhood) with ablations demonstrating arguments (not just entities) drive gains.
- Attempts to leverage FrameNet-derived events (with added arguments/entities) to mitigate data sparsity.

Strengths:
- Clear, well-motivated idea that directly supervises attention with argument information.
- Strong empirical improvements and reasonable ablation controls (ANN, ANN-ENT, ANN-Gold) with statistical testing.
- Simple, practical training objective (squared-loss attention supervision) likely robust with modest data.
- Lightweight, reproducible architecture and reported hyperparameters.
- Sensible analysis of S1 vs. S2 showing precision/recall trade-offs.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty beyond applying supervised attention to ED; architecture and mechanisms are straightforward.
- Primary baseline (unsupervised attention) is weak; lacks reimplementation of stronger neural baselines on the same split for fair comparison.
- Appears to rely on gold entity mentions/types (possibly at test time), raising realism concerns and unquantified sensitivity to entity quality.
- Insufficient detail and noise analysis for FrameNet augmentation and mapping/alignment to ACE roles.
- Lacks qualitative diagnostics: attention visualizations, per-type breakdowns, error analysis, and ablations for fusion of αw/αe, window size, or entity-only/word-only attention.

Questions for authors (key clarifications requested):
- Are gold entity mentions/types assumed at test time; if not, what NER/typing system and performance were used, and how sensitive are results to entity quality?
- How is gold attention constructed for NA trigger candidates and overlapping events; does supervision misguide NA candidates near other-event arguments?
- What is the context window size n and sensitivity to it; were sequence encoders (CNN/biLSTM) tried with the same attention supervision?
- How are αw and αe combined (fixed sum vs. learned gating); please ablate word-only, entity-only, and learned fusion.
- For FrameNet augmentation: provide mapping of frames→ACE types, alignment of frame elements→ACE roles, entity recognition/typing accuracy on FN data, and estimated noise/false positives.
- Please provide per-event-type P/R/F1 and attention heatmaps to demonstrate that the model focuses on arguments as intended.
