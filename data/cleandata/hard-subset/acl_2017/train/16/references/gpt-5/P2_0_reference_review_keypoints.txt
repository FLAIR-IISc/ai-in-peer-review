- Paper proposes supervising attention during training for event detection using gold argument spans, removing need for arguments at test time.
- Model computes attention over contextual words and entity types per trigger; gold attention derived via two strategies (S1: arguments only; S2: arguments + nearby words with Gaussian).
- Claims: (1) argument-based supervised attention improves ED without joint inference at test time.
- Claims: (2) two supervision strategies show a precision/recall trade-off.
- Claims: (3) competitive-to-SOTA results on ACE2005 and further gains from FrameNet augmentation.
- Strength: Simple, intuitive method that decouples training-time argument supervision from test-time requirements.
- Strength: Substantial empirical gains over the unsupervised-attention baseline (64.6 → 71.7 F1) and informative S1 vs S2 comparison.
- Strength: Data augmentation with FrameNet increases recall and F1, addressing data sparsity.
- Strength: Approach is conceptually generalizable to other span-annotated IE tasks.
- Weakness: Primary baseline (unsupervised-attention MLP) is weak; lacks strong CNN/RNN or entity-aware baselines for fair comparison.
- Weakness: Possible reliance on gold entity types at test time; no evaluation with predicted entities or ablation without entity info.
- Weakness: Several implementation details are underspecified (attention normalization, transformation producing entity-aware vector, handling of candidates without arguments).
- Weakness: Statistical significance claim lacks details (number of runs, seeds, variances); no std/CI reported.
- Weakness: Limited analysis—no per-type breakdown, error analysis, or inspection of learned attention alignments.
- Weakness: Related work could better contrast other ways of exploiting argument signals and joint ED-AE models.
- Question: Is α = αw + αe renormalized before computing cw/ce and the supervised loss, and if not, why use squared error?
- Question: What are the dimensions and parameters of the transformation producing we? Is it separate from Ww?
- Question: Are gold ACE entity types used at test time, and can results with predicted entities be reported?
- Question: Can authors provide stronger in-house baselines: ANN without attention, CNN/BiLSTM baseline, and entity-aware baseline without attention?
- Question: How are gold attention targets constructed for negative (NA) candidates, especially when negatives co-occur with other events?
- Question: How many random seeds/runs were used, and what are the mean/std across runs to substantiate significance?
- Question: Can an ablation removing entity attention (αe) be included to quantify its contribution?
- Overall: Sound but missing technical and evaluation details; originality moderate as supervised attention is a natural extension; significance moderate pending stronger baselines and realistic settings; clarity generally ok but needs more specifics.
