- Paper proposes supervising attention for event detection on ACE 2005 using annotated event arguments (gold attention from argument spans).
- Model computes attention over context words and entity types w.r.t. a candidate trigger and uses joint loss combining trigger classification and squared-error attention supervision.
- Two supervision strategies: S1 (only argument tokens) and S2 (Gaussian-smoothed attention around arguments).
- Authors augment training with FrameNet-derived events (core FEs as pseudo-arguments) for additional data.
- Main claimed contribution: explicit supervised-attention using argument spans to improve ED without requiring arguments at test time.
- Contribution: simple training protocol with S1/S2 and analysis showing S2 increases recall at some precision cost.
- Contribution: empirical results on ACE 2005 that match or surpass several prior reported systems.
- Strength: conceptually simple, practical method that leverages existing ACE argument annotations.
- Strength: strong empirical gains (example: baseline F1 64.6 → supervised S2 71.7), outperforming several cited systems.
- Strength: sensible analysis of precision/recall trade-off between S1 and S2, increasing interpretability.
- Strength: FrameNet augmentation study highlights data sparsity effects and makes augmentation steps reproducible in principle.
- Strength: attention supervision offers improved interpretability of model focus.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—supervised attention is incremental relative to prior work.
- Weakness: unclear comparability—train/dev/test splits and test-time access to gold entities are not explicitly stated; some baseline metrics incomplete.
- Weakness: missing ablations and underspecified technical details (combination/normalization of αw and αe, transform to entity-type space, embedding tuning).
- Weakness: limited generality—experiments only on ACE 2005, no per-type error analysis, attention visualizations, or hyperparameter robustness checks.
- Weakness: practical deployment concerns if model relies on gold entity types at test time; FrameNet augmentation depends on external entity tagger.
- Question: What entity information is used at test time (gold or predicted), and do baselines use the same setting?
- Question: Do you renormalize attention after summing αw and αe, and how is squared-error loss reconciled with attention normalization?
- Question: How is the transform from word space to entity-type space implemented (parameters, dimensions)?
- Question: Are splits identical to compared systems, or were numbers copied from papers; can strong baselines be rerun under your split?
- Question: Can you provide ablations: without entity-type features, only word vs only entity attention supervision, and replacing MLP with BiLSTM/CNN?
- Question: How sensitive are results to λ and S2’s Gaussian window/σ, and are results stable across random seeds?
- Question: Do you have qualitative attention visualizations showing test-time focus on argument-like context around ambiguous triggers?
