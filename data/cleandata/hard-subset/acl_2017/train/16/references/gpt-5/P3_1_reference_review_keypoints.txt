- Core idea: supervise attention with gold argument spans so context positions with arguments get higher weight for trigger classification.
- Two supervision strategies: S1 (attend only to argument tokens) and S2 (attend to arguments + neighborhood via Gaussian decay).
- Model combines attention over words and entity-type contexts (α = αw + αe) and feeds weighted context + trigger into a feed-forward classifier.
- Contribution 1: simple supervised-attention that uses gold arguments during training without joint inference at test time.
- Contribution 2: empirical gains on ACE 2005 and further improvements with FrameNet augmentation versus unsupervised attention and some prior systems.
- Contribution 3: analysis comparing S1 vs. S2 and the roles of argument vs. entity information.
- Strength: clear, plausible motivation showing arguments help disambiguate ambiguous triggers.
- Strength: methodological simplicity and practicality; avoids complex joint inference and is easy to implement.
- Strength: solid empirical improvements, especially for supervised attention (S2) over unsupervised attention baseline.
- Strength: sensible exploration showing S1 yields higher precision while S2 yields higher recall.
- Strength: external data (FrameNet) augmentation provides additional improvements, supporting data-sparsity claims.
- Weakness: limited novelty—supervised attention and argument indicators are well-known; more comparisons to simpler baselines are needed.
- Weakness: unclear whether gold entity mentions/types are used at test time, raising fairness concerns with baselines.
- Weakness: unclear modeling details—whether α is renormalized after summation, how entity-type projections are computed, and impact of fixed-length windowing.
- Weakness: missing ablations—no experiments for αw-only vs αe-only, varying λ/σ, removing entity features, or replacing attention supervision with simple indicators.
- Weakness: lack of diagnostics—no attention visualizations, alignment scores at test time, or per-event-type error analysis.
- Weakness: comparison/reporting issues—unclear table entries (e.g., “N/A 71.3”), missing recent baselines, and insufficient statistical testing details.
- Question: do you use gold entity mentions/types at test time on ACE 2005 and do compared baselines do the same?
- Question: is α renormalized after summing αw and αe, and what are the exact equations for we and entity projections?
- Question: how sensitive are results to λ, σ, Gaussian window size, and can you provide ablations requested?
- Question: how are FrameNet frames mapped to ACE event types, what is the noise rate, and does filtering affect performance?
- Question: what is the exact evaluation protocol, preprocessing, number of runs/seeds, and can you report mean±std with appropriate significance tests?
- Additional comments: fix notation/typos, justify the fixed-length context window and report truncation rate.
- Additional comments: include qualitative attention maps and error examples (e.g., ambiguous triggers like “fired”).
- Additional comments: release code and data-processing scripts (including FrameNet mapping) to improve reproducibility.
