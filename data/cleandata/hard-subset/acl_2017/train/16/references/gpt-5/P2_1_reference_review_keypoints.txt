- Proposes supervised attention for event detection by training attention to match gold argument-derived distributions (S1: argument tokens; S2: arguments plus nearby words via Gaussian).  
- Treats every token as a trigger candidate, attends over context words and entity types conditioned on the candidate, and combines attention supervision with ED cross-entropy loss.  
- Shows consistent F1 gains on ACE05 and small additional improvement using FrameNet-derived augmentation.  
- Contribution: simple, explicit injection of argument supervision into ED via supervised attention that integrates easily with standard classifiers.  
- Contribution: empirical study showing argument-based supervision helps ED beyond entity-only signals, supported by ablations (gold, supervised, unsupervised attention).  
- Contribution: practical exploration of using FrameNet as extra training data for ED, though impact is modest.  
- Strength: conceptually clean, direct supervision that avoids changing ED label space and is easy to integrate and reproduce.  
- Strength: solid empirical gains on a standard benchmark, with informative precision/recall differences between S1 and S2.  
- Strength: ablations convincingly isolate that argument-focused signals drive the improvement (unsupervised and entity-only underperform).  
- Strength: implementation uses standard components, making the approach portable and transparent.  
- Weakness: core ANN baseline is weak relative to modern encoders, so comparisons may not be apples-to-apples.  
- Weakness: method relies on gold entity mentions/types at test time and gold argument annotations during training, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: ambiguity in attention formulation—αw and αe are summed but paper does not clarify renormalization before use or loss computation.  
- Weakness: limited context modeling (fixed window weighted sum) may miss long-range/structural cues; coverage and sensitivity to window size are unreported.  
- Weakness: statistical testing lacks detail on unit of analysis, variance estimation, and reproducibility (code/splits not provided).  
- Question: Is there a renormalization after α = αw + αe before computing context vectors and attention loss?  
- Question: What is the window size and what fraction of gold arguments fall within it; how sensitive are results to window size?  
- Question: Are gold entity mentions/types used at test time and are comparisons aligned with baselines; what happens with automatic entities?  
- Question: Can supervised attention be added to stronger encoders (BiLSTM/CNN) and still yield gains?  
- Question: What is the exact significance test setup (test unit, number of runs/seeds); are improvements over baselines significant?  
- Question: For FrameNet augmentation, how many examples were added, what is their noise rate, and how much do they contribute to the attention supervision?  
- Overall: A novel, practical idea with competitive results and convincing ablations, but needs clarifications and stronger, controlled baselines to fully validate impact.
