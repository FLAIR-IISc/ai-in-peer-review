- Proposal: supervise token-level attention with gold argument spans to inject trigger–argument supervision into trigger classification.
- Empirical claim: argument-supervised attention improves ACE2005 performance over unsupervised attention and ANN-S2 achieves the best reported F1 among compared systems with higher precision.
- Data augmentation: use FrameNet-derived events to mitigate data sparsity and compare two attention supervision strategies (S1 argument-only, S2 argument+neighbors).

- Strength: simple, plausible way to pass argument information to ED without full joint inference.
- Strength: substantial F1 gains (64.6 → 70.8/71.7) and notably higher precision for ANN-S2.
- Strength: ablation shows argument supervision outperforms entity-only signals, supporting the main claim.
- Strength: S1 vs S2 produce predictable precision/recall trade-offs, offering a tunable bias.
- Strength: FrameNet augmentation helps the weaker baseline, supporting a data-sparsity diagnosis.

- Weakness: novelty is incremental—supervised attention and leveraging arguments have prior precedent.
- Weakness: unclear methodological details and missing ablations (normalization of α = αw+αe, no αw/αe-only or learned gating).
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity not reported (context window n, λ, Gaussian σ).
- Weakness: incomplete specification of we/transformations in Eq.(3), harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: baseline/comparability concerns—no no-attention baseline, unclear split comparability to prior work, and ambiguous Skip-CNN numbers.
- Weakness: apparent reliance on gold entity types/argument annotations (train/test assumptions not clarified), reducing practical applicability.
- Weakness: limited analysis—no attention visualizations, error breakdowns by event type, or robustness tests with predicted entities/cross-domain.

- Q1: Do you re-normalize α after summing αw and αe, and how sensitive are results to learned/tuned weighting between them?
- Q2: What is the context window size n, and how sensitive are results to n, λ, and Gaussian σ (please provide ablations)?
- Q3: What exactly is we in Eq.(3) and are entity type embeddings transformed similarly before dot products?
- Q4: What is the performance of a no-attention baseline using the same classifier and inputs (mean/max pooling or concatenation)?
- Q5: Are gold or predicted entity annotations used at test time, and do compared baselines also use gold entities?
- Q6: Are your ACE train/dev/test splits identical to prior work; if not, can you release splits/code or re-run prior methods on your split?
- Q7: In Table 3, is 71.3 the F1 for Skip-CNN; please clarify the “best F1” claim.
- Q8: Can you share attention heatmaps or statistics showing attention peaks on argument tokens at test time and examples where attention corrects ambiguous triggers?

- Additional: estimate noise introduced by FrameNet predicted entities and report effects of filtering.
- Additional: paper needs minor proofreading and more precise reference formatting.
