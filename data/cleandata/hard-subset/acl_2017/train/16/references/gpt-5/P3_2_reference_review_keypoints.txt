- Proposes an ED model that supervises token-level attention using gold argument annotations (S1: argument-only; S2: Gaussian-smoothed neighborhood) to improve trigger classification.
- Combines attention-weighted word and entity-type embeddings fed to an MLP; shows gains on ACE 2005 and further improvements using FrameNet-derived event augmentation.
- Main claimed contributions: simple supervised-attention scheme with S1/S2, empirical evidence that argument info (not just entities) matters, and a practical FrameNet-based data augmentation path.

- Strength: Clear, minimal methodological novelty that directly injects argument information into trigger classification without complex global inference.
- Strength: Two supervision strategies capture complementary precision/recall biases with results reflecting those differences.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains with careful ablations showing unsupervised attention underperforms and entity-only attention is insufficient.
- Strength: Practical and lightweight approach (adds a supervised attention loss) that’s easy to integrate into existing systems.
- Strength: Effective use of FrameNet frame elements as additional (noisy) supervision, yielding statistically meaningful improvements.
- Strength: Sound motivation explaining why prior joint models help AE more than ED and how this method addresses that gap.

- Weakness: Relies on gold argument annotations in training, limiting applicability to corpora without argument labels; no evaluation with noisy/predicted arguments.
- Weakness: Core encoder is relatively weak (shallow attention over fixed window + MLP), so gains may not hold against stronger encoders (BiLSTM/CNN/contextualized models).
- Weakness: Unclear whether gold entity mentions/types are used at test time; comparisons may be unfair if other methods use predicted entities.
- Weakness: Limited analysis — missing per-event-type results, coverage of arguments within window, attention visualizations, and sensitivity studies for λ/window/σ.
- Weakness: FrameNet augmentation pipeline uses an external IE system without noise/quality analysis or discussion of potential domain/label leakage; statistical testing not described.

- Question: Are gold entity mentions/types used at test time on ACE 2005? If not, which NER/typing system was used and what’s the delta vs gold?
- Question: What fraction of training trigger candidates have at least one gold argument within the fixed context window, and how does performance vary with/without in-window arguments?
- Question: How sensitive are results to λ, window size n, and Gaussian parameters (σ, window width) in S2 — provide development curves.
- Question: Can supervised attention be trained with automatically predicted arguments from a separate AE model and retain most gains?
- Question: Please provide per-event-type P/R/F1 and attention visualizations to show the mechanism focuses on arguments and disambiguates triggers.
- Question: Which statistical test was used for significance (paired bootstrap/approximate randomization) and against which baselines?
