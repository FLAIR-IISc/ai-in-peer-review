- Paper proposes supervising attention with gold argument positions to improve ACE-style event detection, using two supervision strategies (S1: annotated arguments only; S2: arguments + nearby tokens via Gaussian).

- Core classifier is a simple ANN consuming attended word and entity-type context; FrameNet-derived events used for data augmentation.

- Main contributions: empirical gains from supervised attention, a simple word+entity attention scheme with S1/S2 trade-off, and complementary improvements from FrameNet augmentation.

- Strength: Clear motivation and operationalization of argument supervision showing large F1 improvements over unsupervised attention and prior systems.

- Strength: Lightweight, effective attention mechanism with interpretable precision/recall behavior between S1 and S2.

- Strength: Solid experimental comparisons on ACE 2005 with ablations, and evidence that entities alone are insufficient.

- Strength: Data augmentation with FrameNet yields additional recall gains, indicating robustness to noisy supervision.

- Strength: Sufficient implementation detail (hyperparameters, embeddings, S2 windowing) aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—supervised attention is a direct adaptation and gains may partly reflect improving a relatively weak ANN baseline rather than a fundamentally new model.

- Weakness: Methodological ambiguities around combining αw and αe (whether renormalized) and potential scaling mismatch with normalized gold attention α*.

- Weakness: Gold attention for negative candidates is defined as uniform over entities, which may encourage entity attention for negatives; alternatives were not explored.

- Weakness: Evaluation clarity issues—ACE split choice, Table 3 formatting, unspecified statistical testing, and no results with automatic entities, limiting realism.

- Weakness: Missing ablations and analyses for αw vs αe vs combined, sensitivity to λ and context length n, per-event-type performance, and attention visualizations.

- Weakness: Unsubstantiated explanations for limited gains in joint models without controlled experiments.

- Questions for authors: Do you renormalize α=αw+αe before pooling and loss computation?

- Questions for authors: How is gold attention constructed for negative candidates; were alternatives (zero or uniform over tokens) tried and with what effect?

- Questions for authors: What context length n was used and how sensitive are results to n?

- Questions for authors: Please report results using automatic entity mentions to assess robustness without gold entities.

- Questions for authors: Clarify Table 3’s Skip-CNN number and the feature/settings, number of runs averaged, and exact statistical test used.

- Questions for authors: Please ablate αw vs αe vs αw+αe, report the effect of varying λ, and provide attention visualizations.

- Overall recommendation: Promising, well-motivated, and effective application of supervised attention to ED with modest novelty; lean toward acceptance if methodological ambiguities, additional ablations, and evaluation clarifications are addressed.
