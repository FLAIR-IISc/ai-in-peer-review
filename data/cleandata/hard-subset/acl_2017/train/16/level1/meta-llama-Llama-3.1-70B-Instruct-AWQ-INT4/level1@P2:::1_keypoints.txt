- Proposes supervised attention for event detection that explicitly uses annotated argument information.  
- Constructs gold attention vectors from annotated arguments and uses them to supervise attention training.  
- Reports state-of-the-art F1 on ACE 2005, outperforming prior methods.  
- Addresses limitation of joint ED+AE methods that bias toward argument extraction due to data imbalance.  
- Novel approach that directly leverages argument information for ED rather than indirect joint modeling.  
- Thorough experimental analysis including comparisons to prior work and ablations of attention strategies.  
- Paper is clearly written with well-explained methods and experimental setup.  
- Heavy reliance on the ACE 2005 dataset, raising concerns about generalizability to other domains.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Does not explore alternative attention mechanisms or compare different attention strategies beyond the proposed ones.  
- Unclear how the method would transfer to other NLP tasks or domains.  
- Asks how the approach can be extended to other datasets/domains and what challenges are expected.  
- Requests a more detailed computational complexity analysis and implications for large-scale use.  
- Asks how other attention mechanisms might be investigated and what benefits/limitations they could bring.  
- Seeks insight into applying the approach to other NLP tasks and the potential benefits or challenges.
