- Summary: Proposes a neural ED model that explicitly uses annotated argument information via supervised attention to focus on argument words, achieving state-of-the-art results on ACE 2005.

- Contribution 1: Explicitly exploits argument information for ED, unlike prior work that ignores or indirectly uses it.

- Contribution 2: Introduces a supervised attention mechanism to prioritize argument words during event detection.

- Contribution 3: Provides a systematic evaluation on the ACE 2005 dataset and compares with state-of-the-art methods.

- Strength 1: Novel approach that explicitly leverages argument information.

- Strength 2: Supervised attention is shown to effectively improve ED performance.

- Strength 3: Thorough empirical evaluation and comparisons on ACE 2005.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (ACE 2005), limiting generalizability.

- Weakness 2: Approach depends on availability of annotated arguments, which may be scarce in real-world settings.

- Weakness 3: Potential computational expense due to supervised attention mechanisms.

- Question 1: How will the method generalize to other ED tasks or datasets?

- Question 2: How are cases without annotated arguments handled?

- Question 3: What strategies can reduce the computational complexity of the approach?
