Summary — Proposes a supervised-attention neural approach that exploits annotated arguments to improve event detection, arguing existing joint ED+AE models underuse argument information; evaluated on ACE 2005.

Contribution 1 — Analyzes limitations of existing joint ED and AE models and explains why they fail to leverage argument information effectively.

Contribution 2 — Proposes a novel supervised attention-based ED model that focuses on argument words and shows improvements on ACE 2005.

Contribution 3 — Investigates two strategies to construct gold attention signals from annotated arguments and evaluates their effectiveness.

Strength 1 — Introduces a novel method to incorporate argument information into ED via supervised attention.

Strength 2 — Provides a thorough evaluation on ACE 2005 demonstrating effectiveness compared to state-of-the-art methods.

Strength 3 — Offers a detailed analysis of why joint models fall short, which aids understanding and motivates the approach.

Weakness 1 — Evaluation is limited to the ACE 2005 dataset, raising concerns about generalizability to other datasets/domains.

Weakness 2 — Lacks comparisons to other attention mechanisms beyond an unsupervised baseline.

Weakness 3 — Provides limited discussion of hyper-parameter tuning, hindering reproducibility and understanding of sensitivity.

Question 1 — How will the approach be extended and validated on other datasets or domains?

Question 2 — Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison of their supervised attention against other attention mechanisms?

Question 3 — How were hyper-parameters tuned and which hyper-parameters are most critical for performance?
