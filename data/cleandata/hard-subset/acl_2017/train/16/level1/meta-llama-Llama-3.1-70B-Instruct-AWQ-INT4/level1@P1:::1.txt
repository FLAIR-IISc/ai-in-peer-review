**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that existing joint models, which tackle ED and argument extraction simultaneously, fail to effectively utilize argument information for ED. They introduce a neural network-based approach that uses a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words, which are expected to acquire more attention than other words. The proposed approach is evaluated on the ACE 2005 dataset and achieves state-of-the-art performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to exploit argument information explicitly for ED via supervised attention mechanisms.
2. The authors introduce a neural network-based approach that uses a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words.
3. The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ACE 2005 dataset.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective utilization of argument information**: The paper proposes a novel approach to exploit argument information explicitly for ED, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **State-of-the-art performance**: The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ACE 2005 dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness.
3. **Well-designed experiments**: The authors conduct a series of well-designed experiments to evaluate the proposed approach, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and ablation studies.
4. **Clear and concise writing**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach and experimental results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited analysis of the proposed approach**: While the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, they could have provided more in-depth analysis of the proposed supervised attention mechanism and its effects on ED.
2. **Lack of comparison with other attention mechanisms**: The authors only compare their proposed approach with unsupervised attention mechanisms and do not provide a comparison with other attention mechanisms, such as self-attention or hierarchical attention.
3. **Limited discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach**: The authors could have provided more discussion on the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future research.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more in-depth analysis of the proposed supervised attention mechanism and its effects on ED?
2. How do you plan to extend your approach to other NLP tasks, such as argument extraction or event coreference resolution?
3. Can you provide more discussion on the limitations of your approach and potential avenues for future research?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach and experimental results. However, the authors could have provided more in-depth analysis of the proposed supervised attention mechanism and its effects on ED. Additionally, a comparison with other attention mechanisms would have been beneficial. Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of ED and is a valuable addition to the literature.
