**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that existing joint models, which simultaneously solve ED and argument extraction (AE), do not effectively utilize argument information for ED. They introduce a neural network-based approach that uses a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words, and demonstrate its effectiveness on the ACE 2005 dataset.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Analysis of joint models**: The authors analyze the limitations of existing joint models for ED and AE, and identify the reasons why they do not effectively utilize argument information for ED.
2. **Supervised attention-based ED model**: The authors propose a novel ED model that uses a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words, and demonstrate its effectiveness on the ACE 2005 dataset.
3. **Investigation of attention strategies**: The authors investigate two strategies to construct gold attentions using annotated arguments, and evaluate their effectiveness on the ACE 2005 dataset.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to improve ED by exploiting argument information via supervised attention mechanisms, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Effective evaluation**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their approach on the ACE 2005 dataset, and demonstrate its effectiveness compared to state-of-the-art methods.
3. **Analysis of joint models**: The authors provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of existing joint models for ED and AE, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited dataset**: The authors only evaluate their approach on the ACE 2005 dataset, which may not be representative of other datasets or domains.
2. **Lack of comparison to other attention mechanisms**: The authors only compare their supervised attention mechanism to an unsupervised attention mechanism, and do not evaluate its effectiveness compared to other attention mechanisms.
3. **Limited discussion of hyper-parameter tuning**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of hyper-parameter tuning, which may be important for reproducing their results.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend your approach to other datasets or domains?
2. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the effectiveness of your supervised attention mechanism compared to other attention mechanisms?
3. How did you tune the hyper-parameters of your approach, and what are the most important hyper-parameters for achieving good performance?
