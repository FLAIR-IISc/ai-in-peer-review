- Summary: Proposes supervised attention to explicitly exploit argument words for improved event detection, evaluated on ACE 2005 with strong results.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel supervised-attention approach to incorporate argument information into ED.

- Contribution 2: Presents a neural network model that forces higher attention on argument words.

- Contribution 3: Reports state-of-the-art performance on the ACE 2005 dataset.

- Strength 1: Effectively utilizes argument information, addressing a shortcoming of joint ED/argument models.

- Strength 2: Achieves strong empirical performance, outperforming prior work on ACE 2005.

- Strength 3: Experiments are well-designed, including comparisons and ablation studies.

- Strength 4: Writing is clear and explanations are concise.

- Weakness 1: Limited in-depth analysis of how the supervised attention mechanism affects ED behavior.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison with other attention types (e.g., self-attention, hierarchical attention).

- Weakness 3: Insufficient discussion of limitations and directions for future work.

- Question 1: Can you provide deeper analysis of the supervised attention mechanism and its impact on ED?

- Question 2: How would the approach generalize to related NLP tasks like argument extraction or event coreference?

- Additional comment: Overall a valuable contribution but would benefit from more analysis and broader attention comparisons.
