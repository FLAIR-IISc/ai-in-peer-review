- Proposes supervised attention for event detection by using gold argument annotations (S1: argument words, S2: arguments + Gaussian context) to guide trigger classification.
- Combines word and entity-type attentions with a simple feed-forward trigger classifier over attention-weighted context.
- Shows substantial gains on ACE 2005 versus unsupervised-attention baselines and competitive or better F1 versus strong published systems.
- Augments training with FrameNet-derived events to reduce sparsity, yielding further F1 improvements.
- Contribution: a simple, effective mechanism to inject argument supervision into ED via supervised attention.
- Contribution: a systematic ablation comparing unsupervised attention, entity-only attention, and gold-attention, and contrasting S1 vs S2 supervision.
- Contribution: competitive SOTA F1 on ACE 2005 and evidence that weak external supervision (FrameNet) helps.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated insight with convincing ablation showing oracle attention dramatically outperforms unsupervised attention.
- Strength: strong empirical gains achieved with a straightforward, reproducible architecture (no complex structured inference).
- Strength: thoughtful supervised-attention design—S1 favors precision, S2 boosts recall—and results align with expectations.
- Strength: sensible external data use (FrameNet) that improves both the proposed model and the baseline, supporting the sparsity diagnosis.
- Strength: ablation ANN-ENT shows entities alone are insufficient, strengthening the claim that argument supervision drives improvements.
- Weakness: potential supervision mismatch—proposed method uses gold arguments in training while many baselines do not, raising fairness concerns.
- Weakness: unclear evaluation protocol and split details (no doc IDs), and statistical-test reporting lacks specifics, complicating comparability.
- Weakness: missing implementation details (context window size n, handling longer sentences, parameterization of we, training/early-stopping/hyperparams) hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited analysis—no per-type results, error analysis, qualitative attention comparisons, or sensitivity studies for λ/σ.
- Weakness: practicality concerns about dependence on gold arguments/entities in training and possible use of gold entities at test time—clarity needed.
- Questions for authors: Which baselines use gold argument annotations and can you add a baseline that uses gold argument indicators without attention?
- Questions for authors: Are entities at test time gold or predicted (which system/accuracy) and how does this affect comparisons?
- Questions for authors: Provide exact train/dev/test document lists and clarify the t-test unit and which comparisons are significant at p ≤ 0.05.
- Questions for authors: Report context window n, sensitivity to n/λ/σ, exact we parameterization, and other missing hyperparameters/training details.
- Questions for authors: For FrameNet augmentation, confirm no leakage into ACE test, quantify noise from RPI entity labeling, and report effects of using predicted vs gold entities.
