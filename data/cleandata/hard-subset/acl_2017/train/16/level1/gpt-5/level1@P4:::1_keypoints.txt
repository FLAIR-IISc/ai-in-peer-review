- Tackles ACE 2005 event detection by supervising an attention mechanism with gold argument annotations to guide context selection.  
- Proposes two attention supervision strategies: S1 (argument-only) and S2 (argument + neighboring words via Gaussian).  
- Reports improved F1 over prior systems on ACE 2005 and small additional gains using FrameNet augmentation.

- Introduces supervised attention using gold argument locations to directly guide ED, avoiding indirect benefits from joint ED+AE models.  
- Empirically compares attention supervision strategies and shows argument information helps ED beyond entity presence.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art ED performance on ACE 2005 under the reported setup.

- Clear, targeted use of argument supervision with a simple joint loss yielding substantial gains over an unsupervised-attention baseline.  
- Oracle and ablation experiments indicate argument cues, not just entities, drive performance improvements.  
- S1/S2 behaviors align with design: S1 increases precision, S2 increases recall, providing a tunable trade-off.  
- Training uses argument annotations only during training; inference requires no arguments, preserving practicality.  
- FrameNet augmentation consistently improves performance, suggesting data sparsity limits attention learning.

- Baseline architecture is simple (feed-forward over fixed windows) and may not control for architectural differences versus stronger CNN/RNN baselines.  
- Unclear evaluation details: whether gold or predicted entities are used at test, incomplete reporting of prior baselines, and insufficient significance-test details.  
- Missing ablations and sensitivity analyses (e.g., remove entity attention, learn fusion weights, vary λ/window/Gaussian) leave robustness uncertain.  
- Context trimming may exclude distant arguments; no analysis of how often arguments fall outside windows or the impact of FrameNet labeling noise/quantity.  
- Presentation issues: typos, formatting errors, incomplete citations, and inconsistent tables/figures hinder clarity and reproducibility.

- Do you use gold or predicted entity mentions/types at test time; please report both if predicted.  
- What fraction of gold arguments fall within the fixed context window after trimming, and how does coverage affect performance?  
- Provide ablations: (a) remove entity attention; (b) learn a weight for word vs. entity attention; (c) test supervised attention on CNN/RNN classifiers.  
- Report sensitivity to λ, window size, and Gaussian parameters, plus variance across multiple random seeds.  
- Clarify the statistical significance testing protocol (number of runs, metric distributions) and whether improvements are significant under repeated runs.  
- For FrameNet augmentation: how many instances were added, their class balance, and the estimated noise rate from automatic entity labeling?
