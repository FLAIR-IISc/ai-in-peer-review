- Summary: Proposes supervised attention that explicitly leverages annotated argument information to improve event detection (ED), and evaluates different attention strategies.  
- Contribution: Identifies underutilization of arguments in ED, introduces supervised attention using gold attention vectors, and systematically compares attention strategies.  
- Strength: Novel insight that arguments are underused for ED and can disambiguate triggers, supported with concrete examples.  
- Strength: Well-motivated, elegant supervised attention mechanism with two strategies (S1, S2) and supporting ablation studies showing argument importance.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results—ANN-S2 achieves state-of-the-art on ACE 2005 (71.7% F1) with statistically significant gains and higher precision.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why joint AE/ED models cannot be adapted to use arguments for ED or incorporate the proposed attention.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of how gold attention vectors are constructed in edge cases (multiple arguments, no arguments) and rationale for chosen handling.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to more recent state-of-the-art joint methods beyond Nguyen et al. (2016), e.g., Li et al. (2017).  
- Question: How would the supervised attention integrate with existing joint models—would it need major architectural changes?  
- Question: How are gold attention vectors built for multiple arguments and does the model attend to multiple argument regions simultaneously?  
- Question: Provide details on FrameNet processing—mapping frame elements to ACE arguments and quality of automatically extracted events.  
- Overall assessment: Valuable, well-supported contribution that explicitly uses argument information to improve ED; recommended for acceptance despite the noted weaknesses.
