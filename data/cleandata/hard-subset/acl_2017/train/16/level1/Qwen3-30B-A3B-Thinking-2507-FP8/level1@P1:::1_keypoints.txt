- Proposal: Introduces a supervised attention mechanism that explicitly uses argument information to improve event detection (ED).
- Motivation: Argues that argument words provide strong disambiguating clues for ambiguous trigger words.
- Model: Presents a neural ED model with supervised attention and two strategies (S1, S2) for constructing gold attention vectors from annotated arguments.
- Results: Reports state-of-the-art performance on ACE 2005 (71.7 F1) with statistically significant improvement.
- Contribution 1: Novel use of supervised attention to incorporate argument information into ED.
- Contribution 2: Systematic comparison of two strategies for building gold attention vectors from arguments.
- Contribution 3: Empirical evidence that argument information benefits ED and challenges ignoring arguments in prior models.
- Strength 1: Strong motivation with clear illustrative examples (e.g., ambiguous trigger “fired”).
- Strength 2: Robust experimental gains and statistical significance versus prior feature-based baselines.
- Strength 3: Insightful analysis explaining why joint AE/ED models often fail to improve ED—arguments are used for AE but not ED.
- Strength 4: Method is simple and easily integrated into existing ED architectures.
- Weakness 1: Insufficient evidence/examples to support the claim that existing joint models don’t leverage arguments for ED.
- Weakness 2: Missing comparisons with more recent post-2016 state-of-the-art methods, limiting the SOTA claim.
- Weakness 3: FrameNet augmentation is noted as important but lacks detail on handling mismatches with ACE 2005 event types and arguments.
- Weakness 4: Inadequate analysis of data sparsity and how the proposed method specifically addresses it.
- Question 1: Request for concrete literature examples showing joint models’ attention mechanisms that don’t leverage arguments for ED.
- Question 2: Ask whether comparisons with post-2016 methods were performed and why they were omitted.
- Question 3: Request clarification on how FrameNet frame elements are mapped to ACE event arguments during augmentation.
