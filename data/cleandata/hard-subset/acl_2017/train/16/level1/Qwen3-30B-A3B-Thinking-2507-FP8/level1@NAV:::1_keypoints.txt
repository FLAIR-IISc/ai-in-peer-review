- Proposal: Leverages argument information for event detection via supervised attention, introducing two strategies (S1: argument words only; S2: arguments + context) and evaluating on ACE 2005.

- Contribution: Shows joint methods fail to exploit argument information due to data imbalance/implementation limits.

- Contribution: Introduces supervised attention with gold attention vectors to directly incorporate argument information into ED.

- Contribution: Empirically outperforms prior work, achieving best F1 (71.7%) on ACE 2005.

- Strength: Clear motivating example demonstrating arguments disambiguate ambiguous triggers (e.g., “fired” as End-Position vs. Attack).

- Strength: Ablation studies show gold-attention models (ANN-Gold1/2) substantially outperform models without argument supervision (ANN/ANN-ENT).

- Strength: Method is conceptually simple and ANN-S2 yields statistically significant gains over baselines (p<0.05).

- Strength: Provides thorough analysis explaining why joint approaches fail to improve ED.

- Weakness: Fails to explain why unsupervised attention model (ANN, 64.6 F1) performs much worse than supervised variants.

- Weakness: Omits computational complexity/efficiency analysis, limiting assessment of practical adoption.

- Weakness: Lacks error analysis to diagnose model failure modes and limitations.

- Weakness: Does not adequately explain why S2 achieves higher recall but lower precision than S1.

- Question: How well does the approach scale to datasets with more event types or different linguistic structures beyond ACE 2005?

- Question: What is the computational overhead of the supervised attention mechanism compared to standard ED models?
