- Proposes supervised attention mechanisms to explicitly leverage argument information for event detection (ED).
- Systematically investigates two strategies (S1 and S2) to construct gold attention vectors from annotated arguments.
- Reports state-of-the-art F1 performance on the ACE 2005 dataset, improved further with FrameNet augmentation.
- Novelty: explicitly models arguments to disambiguate triggers and improve ED performance.
- Comprehensive experiments on ACE 2005 with comparisons to pipeline and joint methods.
- Clear analysis showing S1 and S2 trade off precision and recall differently.
- Uses external FrameNet data to mitigate data sparsity and strengthen robustness.
- Limited generalization: evaluation confined to ACE 2005, no tests on other datasets or domains.
- Relies on annotated arguments, limiting applicability where such annotations are unavailable or incomplete.
- Introduces additional model complexity via supervised attention without analysis of computational overhead or scalability.
- Asks whether the method generalizes to other or low-resource datasets.
- Questions the impact of noisy or incomplete argument annotations on performance.
- Suggests exploring unsupervised or semi-supervised adaptations to reduce dependence on annotations.
- Recommends acceptance contingent on addressing generalization, annotation dependency, and scalability concerns.
