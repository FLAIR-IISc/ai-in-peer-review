- Novel neural model that uses supervised attention guided by gold attention distributions from annotated arguments to improve event detection.
- Two strategies for generating gold attention and experiments on ACE 2005 showing a new state-of-the-art F1 of 71.7 and high precision (78.0).
- Thorough ablations and analyses validating that argument information is key and comparing supervision strategies.
- Well-motivated critique of prior joint ED/AE work and a clever supervised-attention solution to force focus on argument-driven context clues.
- Strong empirical results demonstrating that argument information reduces false positives and yields compelling gains.
- Experiments include oracle gold-attention comparisons, show S2 (softer attention) improves recall, and integrate FrameNet augmentation successfully.
- Baseline ANN encoder is relatively weak (64.6 F1 vs. Skip-CNN 71.3), so part of the reported improvement may compensate for the simple context encoder.
- Method depends on rich event-argument annotations during training, limiting applicability to corpora that lack argument labels.
- Paper critiques joint models but evaluates a pipeline model, so it does not directly demonstrate how supervised attention would fix joint-model shortcomings.
- Question: Would supervised attention produce similar gains when applied to stronger base architectures (e.g., BiLSTM or Skip-CNN)?
- Question: How noisy are the automatically labeled FrameNet annotations and how does that noise affect precision and the supervised-attention training?
- Question: Can the approach be adapted to settings with only trigger annotations (e.g., via distant or semi-supervised methods for generating gold attention)?
