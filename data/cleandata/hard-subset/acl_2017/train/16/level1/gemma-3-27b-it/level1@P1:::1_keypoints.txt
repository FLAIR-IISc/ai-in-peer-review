- Paper highlights that existing joint ED/AE models underutilize argument information due to data imbalance and indirect argument usage.
- Proposes a supervised attention mechanism that uses annotated arguments to guide event detection attention.
- Explores two strategies (S1 and S2) for constructing gold attention vectors to integrate argument clues.
- Reports state-of-the-art F1 on ACE 2005, with improvements shown to be statistically significant.
- Clear motivation and illustrative example demonstrating how arguments disambiguate triggers.
- Sound, well-defined technical approach using gold attention supervision to focus on relevant sentence parts.
- Comprehensive experiments including comparisons to SOTA, ablation studies (ANN, ANN-S1, ANN-S2), and FrameNet augmentation.
- Augmenting with FrameNet data further strengthens empirical evaluation.
- Paper is well-written and organized, making methods and results easy to follow.
- Core attention mechanism lacks novelty; main novelty is supervising attention with argument information.
- Performance differences between ANN-S1 and ANN-S2 and varying lambda values indicate hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Hyperparameter tuning process is under-described and needs more detailed analysis.
- FrameNet augmentation process, especially the use of RPI to add entities, is described briefly and lacks detail.
- Potential errors from RPI entity labeling and their impact on results are not discussed.
- Lacks a detailed error analysis identifying difficult event types or triggers.
- Questioning the broader advance beyond applying a known attention technique to a new task.
- Minor issues: inconsistent citation style across the paper.
- Concluding paragraph is repetitive and could be more concise or forward-looking.
