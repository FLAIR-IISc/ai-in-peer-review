- Paper addresses event detection and argues existing methods underutilize argument information, proposing supervised attention to leverage arguments.  
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that explicitly incorporating argument information via supervised attention improves ED performance.  
- Secondary contribution: systematic comparison of two attention strategies (S1 & S2) and analysis of their precision/recall trade-offs.  
- Experimental result: achieves state-of-the-art on ACE 2005 and shows further improvement using FrameNet data augmentation.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on a well-established ED benchmark supporting the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: clear motivation explaining limitations of prior joint models in utilizing argument information.  
- Strength: systematic investigation of attention strategies provides insights into integrating argument information.  
- Strength: data augmentation with FrameNet addresses data sparsity and demonstrates method adaptability.  
- Weakness: core neural architecture is standard; novelty mainly in supervised attention for arguments and needs deeper architectural discussion.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameter λ differs between S1 and S2, implying tuning required and lacking discussion of robustness.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis/visualization of learned attention weights to confirm the model focuses on relevant argument information.  
- Question: can the authors provide attention-weight examples showing focus on argument words during ED?  
- Question: how does the model handle cases where the trigger is an entity (10% of ACE 2005 triggers) and how does that affect performance?  
- Question: what is the computational overhead of the supervised attention mechanism versus a baseline without attention?  
- Additional: define “core elements” in FrameNet more clearly when discussing data augmentation.  
- Additional: include discussion of limitations and future directions (other attention mechanisms, extension to argument extraction).
