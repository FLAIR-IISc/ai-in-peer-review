- Proposes a supervised attention-based event detection (ED) model that explicitly leverages annotated argument information.  
- Claims existing joint ED + argument extraction methods fail to effectively use argument information for ED.  
- Contribution: introduces a supervised attention ED model using argument information to boost ED performance.  
- Contribution: investigates two strategies to construct gold attention signals from annotated arguments.  
- Contribution: reports state-of-the-art results on the ACE 2005 dataset.  
- Strength: provides a thorough analysis of limitations in existing joint methods and proposes a novel remedy.  
- Strength: experimental results show significant improvement over prior state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: augmentation with FrameNet event data further enhances performance.  
- Weakness: approach depends heavily on the quality of annotated arguments, which may be noisy or inaccurate.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other attention-based methods, obscuring novelty assessment.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to ACE 2005, leaving generalizability to other datasets unclear.  
- Question: how will the authors handle noisy or inaccurate annotated arguments that could hurt performance?  
- Question: please provide more details on hyper-parameter tuning, especially the trade-off parameter λ in the joint loss.  
- Question: how can the approach be extended to other event extraction tasks like argument extraction and event coreference?
