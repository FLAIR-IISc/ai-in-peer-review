- Tackles event detection by explicitly exploiting event arguments via supervised attention, achieving top F1 on ACE 2005.  
- Primary contribution: explicit incorporation of argument information into ED with supervised attention, improving event detection beyond joint models.  
- Secondary contribution: systematic comparison of attention supervision strategies (S1, S2) and insights on how to leverage arguments.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art performance on ACE 2005 with significant gains over strong baselines.  
- Ablation studies validate the importance of argument information for the model’s performance.  
- Clear, straightforward architecture with well-explained supervised attention and reproducible implementation details.  
- Thorough evaluation including baselines, ablations, and augmentation with FrameNet data.  
- Demonstrated ability to leverage external knowledge (FrameNet), suggesting broader applicability.  
- Weakness: attention vector computed by naïvely summing word and entity vectors, possibly missing complex interactions.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of more sophisticated or semantically richer attention strategies beyond S1 and S2.  
- Weakness: evaluation confined to ACE 2005, a relatively small benchmark, limiting claims of generality.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed error analysis to identify and explain common failure modes.  
- Question: why sum word and entity attention vectors instead of using a more complex interaction mechanism?  
- Question: were other gold-attention strategies considered, and if so, why were they not chosen?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to hyperparameters (especially λ) and was an extensive search performed?  
- Question: can the authors provide concrete error examples and explanations for misclassified event types?  
- Additional comment: citation style is inconsistent and should be fixed.  
- Additional comment: paper would benefit from discussion of computational complexity.  
- Additional comment: visualizing attention weights would provide insight into how arguments are used.  
- Overall assessment: novel and effective approach with convincing experiments; recommend acceptance.
