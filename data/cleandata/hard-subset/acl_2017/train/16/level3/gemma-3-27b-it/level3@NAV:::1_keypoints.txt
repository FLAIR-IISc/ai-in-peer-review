- Paper proposes using argument information with supervised attention to improve event detection, showing SOTA on ACE 2005.
- Main contribution: demonstrates explicit incorporation of arguments into attention-based ED yields substantial performance gains over joint methods.
- Presents a clear, simple neural framework that is easy to understand and adapt.
- Systematically investigates different attention strategies (S1 & S2) to leverage argument cues.
- Strong empirical results: significantly outperforms baselines and achieves new F1 state-of-the-art on ACE 2005.
- Supervised attention effectively guides the model to focus on relevant argument words, improving ED accuracy.
- Experimental setup is rigorous with clear baselines and a defined development set for hyperparameter tuning.
- Analysis of attention strategies provides useful insights into how argument information is utilized.
- Weakness: combining two part-wise attention vectors by summation is simplistic and may limit modeling capacity.
- Weakness: attention strategies explored are relatively limited; more complex context- or dependency-aware methods might help.
- Weakness: results are demonstrated only on ACE 2005, leaving generalizability to other datasets unclear.
- Question: why choose summation to combine attention vectors and were alternatives (concatenation, element-wise multiplication) tried?
- Question: what is the computational overhead of supervised attention versus unsupervised/standard attention?
- Question: can the authors analyze learned attention weights to show which argument types most influence event detection?
