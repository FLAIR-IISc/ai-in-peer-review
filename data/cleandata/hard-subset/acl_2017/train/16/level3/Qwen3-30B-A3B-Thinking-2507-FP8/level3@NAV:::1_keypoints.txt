- Proposes a novel ED approach that explicitly leverages argument information, often ignored by existing systems.  
- Contributions: shows argument information disambiguates event triggers, introduces a supervised attention framework using arguments, and compares two attention strategies (S1: argument words; S2: arguments + context).  
- Addresses an important gap by demonstrating arguments help ED, not just argument extraction.  
- The framework is conceptually simple yet effective, achieving state-of-the-art on ACE 2005 (71.7 F1, +1.0 over prior best).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including ablation studies that validate the value of argument information and supervised attention.  
- Method is well-motivated and clearly explained, with analysis attributing unsupervised attention failure partly to data sparsity.  
- Weakness: attention vector fusion is naive (α = αw + αe) and may benefit from more sophisticated fusion methods.  
- Weakness: the two attention strategies are straightforward; more complex mechanisms might improve performance.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis explaining why supervised attention outperforms unsupervised approaches beyond data sparsity.  
- Overall verdict: compelling, well-validated contribution that provides a strong foundation for future refinements; recommend acceptance.
