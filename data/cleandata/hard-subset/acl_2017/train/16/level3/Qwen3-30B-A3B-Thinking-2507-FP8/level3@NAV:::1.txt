

This paper proposes a novel approach to event detection (ED) by explicitly leveraging argument information, which is often ignored in existing ED systems. The main contributions are: (1) demonstrating that argument information provides crucial clues for ambiguous event triggers, (2) introducing a supervised attention mechanism framework that effectively utilizes argument information to improve ED, and (3) systematically investigating two attention strategies (S1: focusing solely on argument words; S2: focusing on arguments and surrounding words).

The paper's strengths are significant. First, it addresses an important gap in the literature by showing that arguments are useful for ED, not just argument extraction, which has been largely overlooked. Second, the proposed framework is conceptually simple yet highly effective, achieving state-of-the-art results on the ACE 2005 dataset (71.7 F1 for ANN-S2, outperforming previous best by 1.0 F1). Third, the experimental evaluation is thorough, with ablation studies demonstrating the value of argument information and the effectiveness of the supervised attention approach. Fourth, the method is well-motivated and clearly explained, with detailed analysis of why unsupervised attention mechanisms underperform due to data sparseness.

The main weaknesses are: (1) the attention vector is naively computed as the sum of two part-wise attention vectors (α = αw + αe), which may not be optimal and could be improved with more sophisticated fusion methods. (2) The two proposed attention strategies are relatively straightforward; more complex attention mechanisms might yield further improvements. (3) The paper lacks detailed analysis of why the supervised attention approach works better than unsupervised alternatives beyond the data sparseness argument.

Overall, the paper presents a compelling solution to an important problem in event detection with solid experimental validation. The framework is effective, the approach is well-justified, and the results are significant. While the attention mechanism could be refined, the current implementation provides a strong foundation for future work. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field and should be accepted.
