- Summary: Proposes an ED method that explicitly leverages event arguments via a supervised attention mechanism applied to annotated argument words, integrating argument information into a simple neural classifier and achieving SOTA on ACE 2005.

- Strength 1: Novel and effective use of argument information—supervising attention on arguments helps the model focus on salient context and yields substantial gains over strong baselines.

- Strength 2: Simplicity and clarity—the approach is an elegant, low-complexity modification to a standard neural classifier, making it easy to understand and reimplement.

- Strength 3: Strong empirical results and analysis—comprehensive experiments and ablations show supervised attention outperforms unsupervised attention and argument-focused attention beats entity-focused attention.

- Weakness 1: Naive attention fusion—the model sums word-based and entity-based attention vectors equally, lacking a learned gating or fusion mechanism to weight sources dynamically.

- Weakness 2: Straightforward supervision strategies—the gold attention constructions (S1 uniform, S2 fixed Gaussian) are heuristic and could be improved with more nuanced or data-driven methods.
