- Task & idea: Supervising attention with annotated event arguments to disambiguate and classify event triggers.  
- Method: Neural framework trains attention weights to match "gold" attention vectors derived from arguments using two proposed strategies.  
- Results: Significant gains on ACE 2005 over unsupervised-attention baseline and state-of-the-art performance.  
- Contribution 1: Novel use of annotated arguments as direct supervision for attention, offering a simpler alternative to complex joint-inference models.  
- Contribution 2: Simple, reproducible neural architecture with strong empirical validation and new SOTA F1 on ACE 2005.  
- Strength: Clear motivation showing prior joint models improve arguments but not ED, justifying targeted argument supervision.  
- Strength: Simple design is effective—supervised attention yields large F1 improvement (ANN 64.6 → ANN-S2 71.7).  
- Strength: Thorough experiments including gold-attention upper-bound, comparisons to strong baselines, and FrameNet augmentation.  
- Strength: High-precision outputs (ANN-S2 78.0, ANN-S1 81.4), beneficial for practical applications.  
- Weakness: Fusion of word and entity attention is a naive unweighted sum; a learned weighting or gating could be better.  
- Weakness: Gold-attention strategies (S1/S2) are heuristic and simplistic; they may miss role importance and need hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Questions for authors: Did you try learned/ gated fusion of αw and αe? How sensitive is S2 to Gaussian/window hyperparameters and did you explore more data-driven supervision?  
- Recommendation: Strong, well-executed paper with convincing results—recommend acceptance.
