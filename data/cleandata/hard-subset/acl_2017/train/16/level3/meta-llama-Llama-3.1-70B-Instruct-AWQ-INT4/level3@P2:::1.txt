This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by leveraging argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that existing joint methods, which tackle ED and argument extraction simultaneously, do not effectively utilize argument information for ED. Instead, they propose a neural network-based approach that focuses on ED and adopts a supervised attention mechanism to exploit argument information.

The main contributions of this work are: (1) the proposal of a novel approach to leverage argument information for ED, (2) the introduction of a supervised attention-based ED model, and (3) the investigation of different attention strategies for the proposed model.

Strengths:

1. Effective use of argument information: The authors demonstrate that argument information is crucial for ED and propose a simple yet effective way to leverage it. This is a significant contribution, as existing methods often ignore or indirectly use argument information.
2. Clear and simple framework: The proposed approach is easy to understand and implement, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
3. Significant performance gains: The experimental results show that the proposed approach outperforms state-of-the-art methods, including feature-based and representation-based methods.
4. Effective use of supervised attention: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of supervised attention mechanisms in improving ED performance.

Weaknesses:

1. Naive attention vector computation: The attention vector is computed as the sum of two part-wise attention vectors, which may not be the most effective way to combine attention information.
2. Simple supervised-attention strategies: The two proposed supervised-attention strategies are straightforward and may not be the most effective way to leverage argument information.

Questions to Authors:

1. How do you plan to improve the attention vector computation to better combine attention information from different sources?
2. Can you explore more complex supervised-attention strategies to further improve ED performance?
3. How do you plan to extend the proposed approach to other tasks, such as argument extraction or event extraction?

Additional Comments:

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
* The experimental results are thorough and convincing.
* The authors provide a clear and concise summary of the proposed approach and its contributions.
* The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of existing methods.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of event detection, and the proposed approach is effective and easy to implement. With some minor improvements, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
