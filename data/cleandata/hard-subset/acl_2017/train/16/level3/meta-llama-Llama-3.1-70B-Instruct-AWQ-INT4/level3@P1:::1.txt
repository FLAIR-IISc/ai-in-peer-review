This paper proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by leveraging argument information via supervised attention mechanisms. The authors argue that existing methods often ignore or indirectly utilize argument information, which is crucial for ED. The proposed approach uses a neural network-based model with a supervised attention mechanism to focus on argument words and improve ED performance.

**Main Contributions:**

1. The authors propose a new approach to explicitly utilize argument information for ED, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The use of a supervised attention mechanism is effective in improving ED performance, and the authors provide a clear and simple framework for implementing this mechanism.
3. The paper presents solid experiments and results, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper addresses an important and underused aspect of ED, namely, the use of argument information.
2. The proposed framework is clear, simple, and easy to implement, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
3. The use of a supervised attention mechanism is effective in improving ED performance, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the results.
4. The paper presents a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The attention vector is naively computed as the sum of two part-wise attention vectors, which may be improved by using more sophisticated methods.
2. The two proposed supervised-attention strategies are straightforward, and more complex strategies might yield better results.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including error analysis and discussion of potential limitations.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. How do the authors plan to extend the proposed approach to more complex event detection tasks, such as multi-event detection or event coreference resolution?
2. Can the authors provide more insights into the choice of hyperparameters and their impact on the performance of the proposed approach?
3. How do the authors plan to address the potential issue of overfitting, given the relatively small size of the ACE 2005 dataset?

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of ED, and the proposed approach shows promising results. With some minor improvements and extensions, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact on the field.
