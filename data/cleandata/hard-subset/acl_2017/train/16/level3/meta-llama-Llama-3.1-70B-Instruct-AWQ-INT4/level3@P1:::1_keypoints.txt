- Proposes explicitly leveraging argument information for event detection using supervised attention.  
- Introduces a clear, simple supervised-attention framework for focusing on argument words.  
- Provides solid experiments demonstrating improved ED performance.  

- Addresses an important, underused aspect of ED: explicit use of argument information.  
- Framework is straightforward and easy to implement.  
- Supervised attention shows effectiveness and is analyzed in the paper.  
- Comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art methods indicating superiority.  

- Attention vector is naively computed by summing two part-wise attention vectors and may be improved.  
- The two supervised-attention strategies are basic; more complex strategies might yield better results.  
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis and discussion of limitations.  

- How will the approach extend to multi-event detection or event coreference resolution?  
- Can the authors provide more insight into hyperparameter choices and their effect?  
- How will the method address potential overfitting on the small ACE 2005 dataset?  

- Overall: valuable and promising contribution that needs minor improvements and extensions.
