- Proposes supervised-attention mechanisms that explicitly leverage annotated argument information to improve event detection.  
- Argues existing joint event-detection/argument-extraction methods fail to utilize argument information effectively, yielding limited gains.  
- Introduces a supervised attention-based event detection model with two components: Context Representation Learning (CRL) and Event Detector (ED).  
- CRL learns contextual word and entity representations via attention; ED assigns event types to candidate triggers using those representations.  
- Supervises attention to bias focus toward annotated argument words.  
- Investigates two strategies to construct gold attentions from annotated arguments and demonstrates both improve performance.  
- Evaluated on ACE 2005, achieving significant gains and outperforming state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strength: clear, simple framework that effectively leverages argument information.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of limitations in existing joint methods and a targeted solution.  
- Strength: well-presented experimental results demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Weakness: attention vector computed naively as the sum of two part-wise attention vectors, which may be suboptimal.  
- Weakness: the supervised-attention strategies are straightforward; more complex methods might perform better.  
- Question: How will the approach extend to more complex event detection tasks with multiple triggers and arguments?  
- Question: Please provide more details on hyperparameter tuning, especially the λ in the joint loss.  
- Question: How do the authors plan to address data sparsity when training data is limited?
