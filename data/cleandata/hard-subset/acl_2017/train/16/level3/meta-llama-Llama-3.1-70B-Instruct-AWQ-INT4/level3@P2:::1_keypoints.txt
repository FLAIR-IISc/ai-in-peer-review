- Proposes a novel approach to improve event detection (ED) by leveraging argument information via supervised attention.
- Claims existing joint ED/argument extraction methods do not effectively utilize argument information for ED.
- Introduces a supervised attention-based ED model and investigates different attention strategies.
- Demonstrates effective use of argument information, showing it is crucial for ED.
- Presents a clear, simple, and easy-to-implement framework.
- Reports significant performance gains over state-of-the-art feature-based and representation-based methods.
- Shows supervised attention mechanisms effectively improve ED performance.
- Weakness: attention vector is computed naively as the sum of two part-wise attention vectors.
- Weakness: the two proposed supervised-attention strategies are straightforward and may be suboptimal.
- Asks how the attention vector computation can be improved to better combine sources.
- Asks whether more complex supervised-attention strategies could further improve ED.
- Asks how the approach could be extended to other tasks like argument extraction or event extraction.
- Notes the paper is well-written, experiments are thorough and convincing, and related work is comprehensive.
- Overall: valuable, effective, and implementable contribution that could have greater impact with minor improvements.
