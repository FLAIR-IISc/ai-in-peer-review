- Presents a comprehensive evaluation and a simple framework for robust HPSG parsing that improves ERG performance.
- Introduces a supervised attention mechanism (adapted from MT) to improve event detection.
- Proposes and evaluates robust parsing techniques: bridging, Pacman, and PCFG approximation across multiple datasets.
- Develops a methodology for evaluating robust parsing that handles missing gold-standard data for unparsable inputs.
- Strength: thorough evaluation and solid experiments supporting the framework’s effectiveness.
- Strength: supervised attention outperforms the baseline, showing promise for parsing improvements.
- Weakness: attention vector calculation is simplistic and could be improved.
- Weakness: proposed supervised-attention strategies are straightforward; more complex strategies may yield better results.
- Overall: effective framework with good performance and solid experiments; candidate for acceptance despite areas for improvement.
- Question: how will the authors address the simplistic attention-vector calculation and what alternatives could be tried?
- Question: can the authors detail potential applications and integration of the framework into existing NLP systems?
- Question: how will the framework be evaluated on datasets strictly outside the coverage of the underlying precision grammar?
