- Paper proposes supervised-attention ED model that guides trigger classification toward argument tokens (S1: arguments-only; S2: arguments+Gaussian neighborhood).  
- Best model (S2) achieves state-of-the-art F1 on ACE 2005 and improves significantly over an unsupervised-attention baseline; gains persist with FrameNet augmentation.  
- Contribution: shows explicit supervised attention over arguments substantially improves ED precision and overall performance.  
- Contribution: presents a simple, transparent ED architecture fusing word and entity-context attention with joint training.  
- Contribution: empirically compares two attention-supervision strategies (arguments-only vs. arguments+neighborhood) and analyzes precision/recall trade-offs.  
- Strength: effective use of underused argument signals, yielding large F1 gains (64.6 → 71.7) and sensible precision/recall differences (S1 higher precision, S2 higher recall).  
- Strength: simple, easy-to-implement model achieves robust results without heavy engineering.  
- Strength: principled construction of gold attention targets and effective joint loss with tunable λ.  
- Strength: solid empirical evaluation using standard ACE 2005 splits and comparisons to strong baselines; FrameNet augmentation validates robustness.  
- Strength: diagnostic experiments (ANN-ENT vs. ANN-Gold) show explicit argument supervision is necessary beyond entity info.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—supervised attention and leveraging arguments have prior work; contribution is incremental.  
- Weakness: attention fusion is naive (α = αw + αe) with no normalization or learned weighting, which may be suboptimal.  
- Weakness: supervision strategies S1/S2 are simplistic heuristics; more expressive or distance/role-aware schemes could improve results.  
- Weakness: unclear whether gold entity mentions/types are assumed at test time, affecting fairness vs. baselines.  
- Weakness: some baselines/ablations are missing or weak (e.g., stronger encoders, αw-only/αe-only, renormalization, λ sensitivity, Gaussian hyperparameters).  
- Question: are gold entities/types used at test time or predicted, and what is the impact of entity prediction accuracy on ED?  
- Question: did you try renormalizing α or learning a gate/scalar to combine αw and αe, and what were the results?  
- Question: are there ablations for αw-only vs. αe-only vs. their sum to quantify each component’s contribution?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to λ, Gaussian window size, and σ, and which event types benefit most from argument supervision?  
- Question: how is attention supervision constructed for negative (NA) candidates in sentences with other event arguments—do you risk misdirecting attention to other events?  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — clear, effective idea that leverages argument information for ED with strong empirical validation despite room for refinement.
