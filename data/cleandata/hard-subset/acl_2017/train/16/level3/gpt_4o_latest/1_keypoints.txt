- Novel framework that explicitly incorporates annotated argument information for event detection, addressing limitations of indirect joint models.
- Introduces a supervised attention mechanism with two strategies for constructing gold attention vectors.
- Comprehensive ACE 2005 experiments showing state-of-the-art performance and demonstrating the utility of argument information.
- Clear, simple, and replicable model architecture with effective integration of supervised attention.
- Significant F1 improvements over prior methods and robustness gains using FrameNet augmentation.
- Novel explicit modeling of argument information to help disambiguate event triggers.
- Thorough evaluation comparing multiple attention strategies and strong baselines.
- Attention vector calculation is simplistic; could benefit from multi-head attention or contextualized embeddings like BERT.
- The two supervised attention strategies (S1, S2) are conceptually straightforward and offer limited novelty.
- Generalizability is unclear since experiments are limited to ACE 2005 and one augmented resource.
- Question: How does the model perform on datasets other than ACE 2005 and across domains?
- Question: Have pre-trained contextualized embeddings (e.g., BERT) been considered for triggers and arguments?
- Question: Can the supervised attention mechanism be extended to overlapping or nested events?
- Overall: Well-executed, clear contribution with SOTA results and suitable for acceptance despite areas for improvement.
