## Review of "Exploiting Argument Information to Improve Event Detection via Supervised Attention Mechanisms"

This paper addresses the event detection (ED) task, arguing that existing methods overlook valuable information present in event arguments. The authors propose a novel approach that explicitly leverages argument information through supervised attention mechanisms, demonstrating significant performance gains on the ACE 2005 dataset. 

The primary contribution of this work lies in the successful integration of argument information into a neural network framework for event detection. While joint event and argument extraction models exist, this paper convincingly demonstrates that *how* argument information is used is crucial. By employing supervised attention, the model learns to focus on relevant arguments, leading to substantial improvements over baseline methods and existing joint models. A second, supporting contribution is the clear and simple framework itself. The model is relatively straightforward to understand and implement, making it a valuable contribution to the field. Finally, the thorough experimental evaluation, including ablation studies and augmentation with FrameNet data, strengthens the validity of the findings.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty and Importance:** The explicit use of argument information for ED, rather than as a byproduct of joint learning, is a valuable and relatively unexplored area. This addresses a clear gap in the literature.
*   **Strong Performance:** The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results on the ACE 2005 dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness. The performance gains are significant and consistently observed across different experimental setups.
*   **Clear Framework:** The architecture is well-defined and easy to follow. The use of supervised attention is a logical and effective approach.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough experimental evaluation, including comparisons to strong baselines and ablation studies to analyze the impact of different components. The augmentation with FrameNet data further validates the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Attention Mechanism Simplicity:** The attention vector is computed as a simple sum of two part-wise attention vectors. While effective, this approach seems somewhat naive and could potentially be improved with more sophisticated attention mechanisms (e.g., incorporating multiplicative interactions).
*   **Limited Strategy Exploration:** The two proposed supervised attention strategies (S1 and S2) are relatively straightforward. Exploring more complex strategies, potentially incorporating contextual information or hierarchical attention, might yield further performance gains.
*   **Dataset Limitation:** Evaluation is limited to the ACE 2005 dataset. While a standard benchmark, exploring performance on other datasets or in different domains would strengthen the generalizability claims.

**Questions to Authors:**

*   Could you elaborate on the computational cost associated with the supervised attention mechanism compared to a standard attention mechanism?
*   Have you considered exploring more sophisticated attention mechanisms beyond the simple summation approach? If so, what were the results?
*   Do you have any insights into *which* arguments are most informative for event detection? Could this information be used to further refine the attention mechanism?
