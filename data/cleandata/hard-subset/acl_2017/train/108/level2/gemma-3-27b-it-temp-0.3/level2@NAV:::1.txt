## Review of "A Multigraph-based Model for Overlapping Entity Recognition"

This paper introduces a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping named entities, addressing a known limitation of traditional sequence labeling approaches like CRFs. The core contribution lies in representing relationships between entities using a multigraph structure, allowing for explicit semantics on edges connecting the same nodes – a key innovation for handling overlaps. The authors demonstrate empirically that their model outperforms previous state-of-the-art methods on standard datasets (ACE2004, ACE2005, GENIA) and theoretically show it avoids the “spurious structures” issue present in the compared hypergraph model while maintaining comparable inference complexity.

The strengths of this submission are significant. The proposed multigraph approach is a conceptually sound way to tackle overlapping entity recognition. The empirical results consistently show improvements over the baseline mention hypergraph model, demonstrating the practical benefit of the approach. The theoretical analysis identifying and resolving the spurious structures issue is a valuable contribution, providing insight into the limitations of previous models and justifying the novelty of the proposed method. The authors also clearly articulate the potential for applying this multigraph framework to other structured prediction problems.

However, the paper also has weaknesses. While the theoretical analysis is insightful, the explanation of “spurious structures” could be more accessible. The description relies heavily on the mention hypergraph model for comparison, and readers unfamiliar with that work might struggle to fully grasp the issue. The feature engineering details, while standard, are not thoroughly explained, making it difficult to assess the impact of specific features on performance. Furthermore, the ablation studies are missing. It would be beneficial to see how different components of the multigraph model contribute to the overall performance. Finally, the discussion of limitations is brief; a more detailed exploration of potential failure cases and areas for future work would strengthen the paper.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more intuitive explanation of “spurious structures” without relying so heavily on the mention hypergraph model as a reference?
2. Can you elaborate on the feature engineering process and provide a rationale for the chosen features?
3. What are the potential failure cases of your model, and what types of overlapping entities does it struggle to identify?
4. How does the performance of your model degrade with increasing sentence length or complexity of overlapping structures?
