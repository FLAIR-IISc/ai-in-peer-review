- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition, claims empirical and theoretical advantages.  
- Shows empirical improvements over previous models on ACE2004, ACE2005, and GENIA.  
- Theoretically demonstrates avoidance of spurious structures present in the mention hypergraph model while claiming same inference time complexity.

- Addresses an important gap by analyzing why linear-chain CRFs and tree models struggle with overlapping entities.  
- Strong theoretical analysis showing the model avoids spurious structures and clarifies differences in normalization terms.  
- Empirical gains are consistent across datasets, with up to +2.1 F1 on ACE2004 and analysis on sentences with/without overlaps.

- Novelty claim is problematic: paper fails to convincingly distinguish multigraphs from hypergraph-based mention models.  
- Performance gains are modest (max +2.1 F1) and the practical significance is not well justified.  
- Does not adequately justify why a multigraph is necessary versus converting to a state-based model; lacks empirical evidence that conversion harms performance.  
- Fails to show that the edge-based vs. state-based distinction will generalize beyond overlapping entity recognition.

- How does the multigraph model handle crossing (non-nested) overlapping entities compared to the mention hypergraph model?  
- Could the model be implemented with the mention hypergraph structure using different features/edge semantics, and what unique advantage does the multigraph representation provide?  
- Please provide a detailed complexity analysis to support the claimed O(nT) inference time for the multigraph model.

- Paper is well-written and experiments are thorough but novelty needs stronger justification.  
- Theoretical analysis of spurious structures is the strongest contribution and should be emphasized.  
- Authors should clarify their definition of "multigraph" and clearly distinguish it from hypergraphs.
