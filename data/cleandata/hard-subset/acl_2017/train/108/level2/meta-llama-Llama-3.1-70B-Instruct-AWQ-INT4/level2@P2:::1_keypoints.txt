- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model to represent and predict overlapping entities in text.  
- Reports higher F1-scores than prior models on multiple standard overlapping and non-overlapping entity datasets.  
- Theoretically shows the model avoids spurious-structure issues in inference and is non-deficient.  

- Introduces a novel multigraph approach distinct from traditional graph/hypergraph methods.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance with improved F1 results on benchmark datasets.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis addressing spurious structure problems.  

- Compares against only a limited set of prior models, lacking broader state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Insufficient explanation of the multigraph model’s internal workings and interpretability.  
- No analysis or discussion of potential overfitting and regularization strategies.  

- Request for a more detailed explanation of how multigraphs and entity separators encode overlapping entities.  
- Ask how the authors will detect or mitigate overfitting in the proposed model.  
- Request for a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art overlapping-entity methods.  

- Paper is well-written and clear overall but has minor formatting and grammar issues to fix.
