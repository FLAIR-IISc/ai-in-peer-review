- Summary: Proposes a multigraph NER model using explicit "entity separator" edges to handle overlapping entities, claims nondeficiency vs. mention hypergraph, uses log-linear training with inside–outside inference, and shows strong results on ACE, GENIA, and CoNLL 2003.
- Contribution 1: Introduces an intuitive multigraph representation that models overlaps via explicit edge semantics (entity separators).
- Contribution 2: Provides a theoretical analysis identifying "spurious structures" in the mention hypergraph and argues the multigraph is nondeficient.
- Contribution 3: Delivers comprehensive empirical validation showing consistent improvements over strong baselines across multiple datasets.
- Strength 1: The model is clear and intuitive, with effective graphical illustrations of the entity-separator concept.
- Strength 2: Theoretical justification (edge- vs. state-based view and spurious structures) gives principled reasons for improved performance.
- Strength 3: Experiments are thorough and fair, including reimplementation of the main baseline with identical features.
- Strength 4: Result analyses are insightful (performance breakdowns for overlapping vs. non-overlapping sentences, convergence analysis).
- Weakness 1: The spurious-structures argument is underexplained in the main text and relies on a dense appendix example.
- Weakness 2: The claimed state- vs. edge-based paradigm distinction may be overstated and is largely a modeling convenience.
- Weakness 3: The approach depends on manual features and lacks discussion or experiments integrating neural representations.
- Question 1: Please clarify precisely how the multigraph avoids spurious structures—does it guarantee a one-to-one mapping between separator paths and entity annotations?
- Question 2: Why does the mention hypergraph slightly outperform on GENIA's overlapping subset, and is the multigraph more sensitive to scarce overlapping examples?
- Question 3: Have you ablated the eight separator types (e.g., rare complex separators like ECS) to measure their frequency and impact on performance?
