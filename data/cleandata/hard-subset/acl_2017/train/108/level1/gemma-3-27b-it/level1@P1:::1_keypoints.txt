- Contribution — Formalization of Edge Semantics: Explicitly assigns semantics to different edge types in a multigraph to better represent overlapping entity structures in structured prediction.
- Contribution — Empirical Validation: Shows improved performance on ACE and GENIA (and generalization to CoNLL-2003) with comparisons to strong baselines.
- Contribution — Spurious Structures Analysis: Theoretically demonstrates the multigraph avoids spurious structures present in the mention hypergraph while keeping comparable inference complexity.

- Strength — Clear Motivation: Clearly articulates limitations of prior methods and motivates the multigraph approach with illustrative overlapping-entity examples.
- Strength — Theoretical Foundation: Strong analysis of edge- vs. state-based paradigms and a focused comparison to the mention hypergraph model.
- Strength — Comprehensive Experiments: Well-designed experiments and ablations, including analysis on sentences with and without overlapping entities.
- Strength — Detailed Model Description: Thorough, easy-to-follow explanation of multigraph structure, edge types, and inference, supported by effective figures.
- Strength — Code Availability: Authors will release code and system to facilitate reproducibility.

- Weakness — Limited Novelty of Multigraphs: Multigraphs as representations are not entirely new; paper needs to better situate novel aspects relative to prior multigraph work.
- Weakness — Feature Engineering Complexity: The large, complex feature set raises questions about how much gain comes from representation versus engineered features.
- Weakness — Potential Overfitting: Many features and small datasets (especially GENIA) raise generalization concerns despite regularization tuning.
- Weakness — Spurious Structures Explanation: The paper relies heavily on an appendix figure for explaining spurious structures; a clearer main-text explanation is needed.

- Question — Relation to Prior Multigraph Work: How does this approach compare to existing multigraph uses in NLP/other fields and what implementation aspects are novel?
- Question — Feature Ablation: Have you conducted feature ablations to identify which feature groups drive performance gains?
- Question — Computational Overhead: What is the detailed memory and training-time overhead of multigraphs versus simpler representations beyond the reported ~12%?
- Question — Intuitive Example for Spurious Structures: Can you provide a concise, intuitive example (not just Figure 8) showing how spurious structures affect the mention hypergraph?
- Question — Model Limitations: What entity types or linguistic constructions does the model struggle with?

- Additional — Writing & Accessibility: Paper is well-written and accessible despite technical complexity.
- Additional — Supplementary Material Placement: Important details (e.g., full feature set) should be more accessible or moved into the main text.
- Additional — Comparative Summary Suggestion: Add a concise table summarizing key differences between this model and the mention hypergraph.
