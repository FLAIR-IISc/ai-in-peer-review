- Proposes a novel multigraph representation for overlapping entity recognition that assigns explicit semantics to multiple edges between nodes.  
- Empirically shows improved F1-scores over prior models (mention hypergraph, CRF) on ACE and GENIA datasets.  
- Presents a theoretical analysis arguing the model avoids "spurious structures" found in the mention hypergraph baseline while keeping similar inference time complexity.  
- Provides a comprehensive empirical comparison and detailed feature/hyperparameter descriptions against strong baselines.  

- Lacks sufficient detail on how the multigraph is automatically constructed from text and how edge types are determined during training and inference.  
- Performance appears to depend heavily on engineered features; it's unclear whether gains come from the multigraph or the feature set (no ablation study).  
- Emphasizes strong results on CONLL2003 (non-overlapping entities), which may be disproportionate to the paper’s core focus on overlapping entities.  
- Some figures (notably Figures 2 and 4) are hard to interpret; notation and visuals need clarification.  

- Request: provide a detailed explanation of multigraph construction from input sentences and edge-type determination during training/inference.  
- Request: perform a feature ablation study to quantify each feature's contribution to performance.  
- Request: clarify practical inference cost of handling multiple edges beyond asymptotic O(nT) complexity and compare real-world speed to the hypergraph model.  
- Request: elaborate how the interpretation process resolves ambiguities in spurious structures to ensure consistent entity extraction.  

- Writing is generally clear but some dense sections (e.g., Section 5.1) should be simplified.  
- Supplementary material (feature details, spurious-structure analysis) is essential and should be made readily available.  
- Authors should clarify the term "deficient" model, as its usage is uncommon in this context.
