1. Novel multigraph model for overlapping NER using semantically-rich directed "entity separator" edges.  
2. Theoretical claim identifying "spurious structures" in the mention hypergraph and that the multigraph is nondeficient.  
3. Empirical results showing state-of-the-art performance on overlapping NER datasets and competitive results on non-overlapping data.  
4. Well-motivated, novel edge-based formulation that cleanly models complex overlapping entity transitions.  
5. Consistent empirical improvements across four datasets and robust performance on both overlapping and non-overlapping sentences.  
6. Sound theoretical argument and practical efficiency: nondeficiency claim, O(nT) inference, and faster convergence in practice.  
7. The "spurious structures" argument is unclear and lacks a demonstrated causal link to observed performance shortfalls.  
8. Evaluation omits comparison or integration with neural models (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF), limiting relevance to contemporaneous neural NER work.  
9. The model may be viewed as an incremental refinement of the mention hypergraph since separators compactly encode similar transitions and gains are modest.  
10. Request for a minimal, intuitive example of spurious structures and how this deficiency directly causes lower recall in the mention hypergraph.  
11. Question whether faster convergence is a consequence of nondeficiency and if there is theoretical justification for easier/stabler optimization.  
12. Ask whether and how sparse features could be replaced by neural encoders (e.g., BiLSTM) in the multigraph and whether that would outperform a similarly neuralized hypergraph.
