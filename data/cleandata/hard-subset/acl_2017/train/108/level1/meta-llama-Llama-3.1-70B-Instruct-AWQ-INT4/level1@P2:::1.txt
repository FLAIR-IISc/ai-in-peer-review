**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The model is capable of handling overlapping entities, which are common in many datasets, including ACE, GENIA, and clinical text datasets. The authors claim that their model outperforms previous models, including the mention hypergraph model, and demonstrate this through extensive experiments on standard datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel multigraph-based model for predicting overlapping entities.
2. Empirical results showing that the model achieves higher F1-scores compared to previous models on multiple datasets.
3. Theoretical analysis showing that the model does not present the spurious structures issue in its inference procedure and is therefore non-deficient.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a new and innovative approach to handling overlapping entities, which is a common problem in many NLP tasks.
2. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the model's strengths and weaknesses, including a comparison with previous models.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the model in recognizing overlapping entities.
4. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the model and its components.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes a high level of background knowledge in NLP and graph theory, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
2. The model's complexity and computational requirements are not thoroughly discussed, which may be a concern for practical applications.
3. The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models, which would be useful for evaluating the model's performance.
4. The paper could benefit from more discussion on the potential applications and implications of the model, beyond just entity recognition.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the computational requirements of the model, including training and inference times?
2. How does the model handle cases where the overlapping entities are not nested, but rather crossing?
3. Can you provide more information on the features used in the model, including the specific features used for each dataset?
4. How does the model's performance compare to other state-of-the-art models in entity recognition, such as deep learning-based models?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and proposes a novel approach to handling overlapping entities. However, it could benefit from more discussion on the model's complexity and computational requirements, as well as a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art models. Additionally, the authors could provide more information on the potential applications and implications of the model beyond just entity recognition.
