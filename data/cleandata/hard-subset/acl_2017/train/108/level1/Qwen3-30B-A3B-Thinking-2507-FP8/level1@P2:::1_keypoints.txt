- Proposes a novel multigraph-based structured prediction model for overlapping entity recognition.  
- Empirically outperforms previous models on standard datasets (ACE2004/2005, GENIA).  
- Theoretically shows the model avoids "spurious structures" in normalization while keeping inference complexity.

- Introduces a useful distinction between state-based and edge-based paradigms, motivating an edge-based approach.  
- Demonstrates consistent, statistically significant empirical gains, especially on ACE datasets with many overlaps.  
- Provides a theoretical explanation of avoiding spurious structures that may account for improved performance.  
- Analyzes model behavior on overlapping and non-overlapping sentences, showing general applicability (e.g., CONLL2003).

- Multigraph architecture may lack strong novelty over the mention hypergraph; core structure is similar.  
- Fails to concretely illustrate how avoiding spurious structures leads to specific correct predictions.  
- Lacks ablation studies to identify which components (multigraph itself vs. specific edge semantics) drive gains.  
- Evaluation limited to NER datasets; no experiments on other overlapping-structure tasks (e.g., relation or event extraction).

- Please provide dataset examples where the mention hypergraph errs due to spurious structures but the multigraph succeeds.  
- How much improvement is attributable to the multigraph representation versus the designed edge semantics (ECS, EC, etc.)?  
- Why does the model perform well on non-overlapping datasets like CONLL2003 despite not being trained on overlapping entities?  
- Why does the model slightly underperform the mention hypergraph on GENIA for overlapping entities; is it due to lower overlap prevalence?

- Overall: meaningful theoretical and empirical contribution, but needs clearer case studies, ablations, and broader evaluations to fully validate novelty and generality.
