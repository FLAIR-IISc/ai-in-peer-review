- Summary: Proposes a multigraph-based NER model to handle overlapping entities, claiming a novel representation, modest F1 improvements on ACE2004/2005/GENIA, and theoretical avoidance of “spurious structures” with linear inference complexity.
- Primary contribution (as reviewer sees it): Exploration of multigraphs to represent overlapping entity structures.
- Secondary contribution: Theoretical comparison with the mention hypergraph model highlighting spurious-structure issues.
- Tertiary contribution: Empirical results that modestly support the approach on standard datasets.
- Strength: Novel multigraph representation allowing multiple semantically distinct edges between node pairs.
- Strength: Comprehensive empirical evaluation across three standard datasets against several baselines.
- Strength: Theoretical analysis identifying and addressing spurious structures in the mention hypergraph model.
- Strength: Maintains linear-time inference comparable to the mention hypergraph baseline.
- Weakness: Empirical gains are small and claims of outperforming prior work are overstated; no statistical significance analysis.
- Weakness: No analysis of practical impact—unclear whether F1 gains yield meaningful downstream improvements or more important entities detected.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to strong/realistic baselines (e.g., dictionary methods or commercial NLP systems).
- Weakness: Unclear emphasis and evaluation on crossing vs. nested entities; lacks explicit analysis for crossing entities.
- Weakness: Insufficient algorithmic detail (edge selection rules, CS-edge, I^k_t node variants), hindering reproducibility.
- Question: Provide detailed error analysis with examples showing successes/failures versus mention hypergraph, especially for crossing entities.
- Question: Quantify practical impact—how many additional or important entities are detected vs baseline?
- Question: What criteria determine the semantics assigned to each multigraph edge, and why were they chosen?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to feature choices—please provide an ablation study by feature type.
- Question: Give concrete examples of the “spurious structures” and show exactly how the multigraph formulation avoids them.
- Additional comment: Define “multigraph” explicitly at first use.
- Additional comment: Paper needs careful proofreading for wording, grammar, and style.
- Additional comment: Clarify terminology (“spurious structures,” “state” vs “node”) and meanings of edge types (CS, EC, etc.).
- Additional comment: Improve figure clarity and annotation (Fig. 2 especially), avoid relying on color.
- Additional comment: Fix notation inconsistencies and reference formatting.
- Overall recommendation: Leaning toward rejection pending clearer algorithmic details, stronger baselines/impact analysis, and clearer empirical justification.
