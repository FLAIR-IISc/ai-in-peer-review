- Overall assessment: Proposes a multigraph model for overlapping NER with modest empirical gains but not a convincing enough contribution; reviewer leans to reject.

- Contribution — multigraph exploration: Introduces multigraphs as a novel representational approach for overlapping entity recognition.

- Contribution — empirical results: Reports modest F1 improvements over baselines on standard datasets.

- Contribution — spurious-structure analysis: Argues the multigraph avoids “spurious structures” present in hypergraph models.

- Strength — problem relevance: Overlapping and crossing entity detection is an important and well-motivated problem area.

- Strength — empirical effectiveness: The proposed technique shows some empirical improvement on tested datasets.

- Strength — efficiency claim: Maintains linear time complexity and discusses efficiency improvements over prior hypergraph systems.

- Strength — writing quality: Generally well-written but needs polishing.

- Weakness — weak motivation for nested entities: Paper fails to convincingly argue why accurately detecting nested entities is practically important.

- Weakness — missing algorithmic details: Lacks clear criteria for edge inclusion, features for edge types, and precise description of I^k_t node variants and connections.

- Weakness — overstated performance claims: Small F1 gains are presented as “outperforming” without effect-size discussion or statistical significance.

- Weakness — lack of practical impact analysis: No analysis on how many new important entities are found or downstream impact of the small gains.

- Weakness — insufficient baseline comparisons: Omits comparisons to simpler/dictionary-based baselines or commercial NLP systems for context.

- Weakness — insufficient focus on crossing entities: Experiments and error analysis do not isolate performance on crossing vs. nested entities.

- Weakness — limited error analysis: Needs more concrete success/failure examples and comparisons to prior systems.

- Question 1: Provide stronger motivation and examples showing downstream benefits of detecting nested entities.

- Question 2: Clarify the decision criteria and features used to include and type edges in the multigraph (S, E, C, etc.).

- Question 3: Specify which I^k_t node variants are used and precisely how they are connected in the graph.

- Question 4: Provide statistical significance tests and discuss effect sizes for reported improvements.

- Question 5: Explain whether and why comparisons to dictionary-based or commercial systems were not included.

- Question 6: Report performance breakdowns for crossing versus nested entities to pinpoint where gains arise.

- Question 7: Supply concrete examples comparing model successes and failures against prior state-of-the-art.

- Minor — define terms: Define “multigraph” on first use and clarify terms like “spurious structures,” “state” vs. “node,” and CS-edge selection.

- Minor — presentation issues: Fix wording, grammar, punctuation, inconsistent notation, unclear figures (esp. Fig. 2 in B/W), and math/text formatting.

- Minor — bibliography and references: Standardize reference formatting and avoid using references as nouns.

- Minor — experimental reporting: Clarify what “significant” means, report exact effect sizes, and detail precision/recall behaviors on datasets.
