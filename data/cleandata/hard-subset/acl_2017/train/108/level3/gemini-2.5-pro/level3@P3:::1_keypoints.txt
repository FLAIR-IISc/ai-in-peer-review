- Problem & approach: Tackles overlapping (nested/crossing) NER with a novel multigraph "edge-based" model using eight edge-level "entity separators" instead of traditional state-based models.

- Theoretical claim: The multigraph is "non-deficient" and avoids the "spurious structures" ambiguity found in the Mention Hypergraph (MH) model.

- Empirical claim: Achieves state-of-the-art results on ACE2004, ACE2005, and GENIA, outperforming the MH baseline (e.g., +2.1 F1 on ACE2004).

- Contribution 1: Proposes a novel, effective model for overlapping NER with consistent empirical F1 improvements across datasets.

- Contribution 2: Offers a theoretical analysis identifying "spurious structures" in MH, motivating the multigraph approach.

- Strength 1: Addresses an important, practical NLP problem common in domains like biomedical text and news.

- Strength 2: Strong and convincing empirical results with useful breakdowns on sentences with/without overlaps.

- Strength 3: Novel conceptualization via explicit edge semantics and an edge-based paradigm that could inspire future work.

- Weakness 1: Motivation is poorly structured — critique of MH and the need for the new model are presented too late.

- Weakness 2: The choice and novelty of the eight entity separators are unclear and lack justification or ablation.

- Weakness 3: The key theoretical argument about MH's "spurious structures" is underdeveloped and lacks a concrete main-text example/diagram.

- Weakness 4: The connection between the theoretical non-deficiency claim and the empirical gains is speculative and not disentangled from other factors.

- Question 1: Provide a simplified, main-text example/diagram showing an MH subgraph that ambiguously represents nested vs. crossing entities and how the multigraph resolves it.

- Question 2: Give more intuition and/or an ablation study for the eight entity separators, especially removing complex ones like ECS, EC, CS.

- Question 3: Present direct analysis linking non-deficiency to improved recall, e.g., error analysis showing MH confuses nested and crossing structures more often.
