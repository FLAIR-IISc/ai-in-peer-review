- Summary: Proposes a multigraph-based model for overlapping NER (nested and crossing entities) claiming efficiency and avoidance of "spurious structures."

- Contribution: Focuses on the under-explored problem of crossing/overlapping entities.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel multigraph structured prediction framework with modest empirical gains over a hypergraph baseline.

- Strength 1: Addresses a significant and relevant limitation of standard NER by targeting overlapping/crossing entities.

- Strength 2: Presents a novel technical approach that yields consistent, small F1 improvements across benchmarks.

- Strength 3: Provides theoretical analysis comparing to prior work and argues a training/convergence advantage via avoiding spurious structures.

- Weakness 1: Experiments do not demonstrate that gains specifically improve crossing-entity detection; improvements may stem from easier nested cases.

- Weakness 2: Empirical gains are marginal (1–2 F1) with no statistical significance testing and missing important baselines for context.

- Weakness 3: Technical clarity and reproducibility are lacking—key algorithmic details, definitions, and clear figures are missing and writing needs polishing.
