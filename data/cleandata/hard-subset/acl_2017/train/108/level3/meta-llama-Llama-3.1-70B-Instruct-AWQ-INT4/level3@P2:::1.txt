**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The model is designed to tackle the problem of overlapping entity extraction, which is common in many existing datasets. The authors introduce a new representation called entity separators, which are used to capture the complex combinations of roles each token can take. The model is trained using a log-linear approach and is evaluated on standard datasets containing overlapping and non-overlapping entities.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel multigraph-based model for predicting overlapping entities, which is the first structured prediction model utilizing multigraphs to predict overlapping structures.
2. The model is able to achieve higher F1-scores compared to previous models on multiple datasets.
3. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the model, showing that it resolves the spurious structures issue associated with a previous state-of-the-art model, while still maintaining the same inference time complexity.

**Strengths**

1. The paper tackles a useful problem of overlapping entity detection, which is common in many existing datasets.
2. The proposed multigraph-based model is a variant of the mention hypergraph (MH) model, but it introduces a new representation called entity separators, which are used to capture the complex combinations of roles each token can take.
3. The empirical results are solid and improve the state-of-the-art on benchmarks.
4. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the model, showing that it resolves the spurious structures issue associated with a previous state-of-the-art model.

**Weaknesses**

1. Section 3 needs to state specific issues with MH that motivate the new model.
2. Section 4 is unclear about why separators are introduced and what they add beyond T/I/O.
3. Section 5.1 fails to demonstrate why the new model is superior.
4. Section 5.2 is too abstract and needs concrete examples of spurious structures.
5. The paper does not provide enough explanation about why and how the new model works better than the previous models.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more explanation about why and how the new model works better than the previous models?
2. Can you provide concrete examples of spurious structures in Section 5.2?
3. Can you clarify why separators are introduced and what they add beyond T/I/O in Section 4?
4. Can you provide more details about the features used in the model and how they are defined?
5. Can you provide more explanation about the theoretical analysis of the model and how it resolves the spurious structures issue associated with a previous state-of-the-art model?
