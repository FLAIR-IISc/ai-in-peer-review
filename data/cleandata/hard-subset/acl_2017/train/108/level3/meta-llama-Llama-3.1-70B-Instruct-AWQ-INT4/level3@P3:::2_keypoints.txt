- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities.
- Demonstrates higher F1-scores on standard datasets via extensive experiments.
- Reports efficiency improvements compared to a previous system.
- Provides an informative discussion of differences and implications relative to prior models.
- Empirical gains are modest (often only a few F1 points) and effect size is not discussed.
- Motivation for detecting nested entities is weak and under-explained.
- Key algorithmic details are missing (e.g., decision criterion for including edges, which I^k_t node variants are used).
- Claims of “outperforming” prior models are overstated without effect-size analysis.
- Lacks analysis of practical impact (how many important new entities found; whether small F1 gains matter to users).
- No baseline comparisons to simple dictionary-based methods or commercial systems to contextualize results.
- Insufficient focus on crossing entities in experiments and error analysis; unclear whether gains come from nested or crossing detection.
- Needs detailed error comparisons with prior systems and concrete success/failure examples.
- Reviewer lukewarm and leans toward rejection because the importance of crossing entities is not convincingly emphasized.
- “Multigraph” should be defined at first use for non-expert readers.
- Numerous wording, grammar, and stylistic issues (typos, awkward phrases, punctuation errors).
- Several unclear terms and notations require clarification (e.g., “spurious structures,” “state” vs. “node,” CS-edge selection).
- Figures are unclear in black-and-white (Figure 2); colors/gray nodes and highlighted edges are unexplained.
- Example goals are not justified and many presentation issues remain in math and text (comma/period usage, equation punctuation).
- Bibliography has inconsistent capitalization and reference formatting; avoid using references as nouns.
- Experimental reporting needs improvement: define “significant” and “better,” report exact effect sizes, and give dataset-specific precision/recall details.
- Questions to authors: clarify edge-inclusion decision criteria and which I^k_t variants are used.
- Questions to authors: quantify effect sizes and practical impact; indicate whether gains are meaningful to users.
- Questions to authors: provide baseline comparisons to dictionary-based and commercial systems.
- Questions to authors: clarify experimental focus and provide analysis separating nested vs. crossing-entity gains.
