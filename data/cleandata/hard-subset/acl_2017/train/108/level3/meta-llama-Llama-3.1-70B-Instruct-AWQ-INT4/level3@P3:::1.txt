**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition, which is capable of recognizing overlapping entities based on multigraphs. The authors demonstrate that their model outperforms previous models through extensive experiments on standard datasets containing overlapping and non-overlapping entities.

**Contributions**

The authors claim three major contributions:

1. A novel multigraph-based model for predicting overlapping entities.
2. Empirical results showing that their model achieves higher F1-scores compared to previous models on multiple datasets.
3. Theoretical analysis showing that their model does not present the spurious structures issue in its inference procedure and is therefore non-deficient.

**Strengths**

1. **Improvement over state-of-the-art**: The authors demonstrate that their model outperforms the mention hypergraph model, a previous state-of-the-art model, on multiple datasets.
2. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel multigraph-based model, which is a significant departure from traditional graph-based models.
3. **Theoretical analysis**: The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their model, including a discussion of spurious structures and how their model resolves this issue.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of clarity in Section 3**: The authors do not clearly state the specific issues with the mention hypergraph model that motivate their new model.
2. **Unclear introduction of separators**: The authors introduce separators in Section 4 without clearly explaining why they are necessary or what they add beyond traditional T/I/O labels.
3. **Insufficient demonstration of superiority**: The authors do not provide a clear demonstration of why their model is superior to the mention hypergraph model in Section 5.1.
4. **Lack of concrete examples**: The authors do not provide concrete examples of spurious structures in Section 5.2, making it difficult to understand the issue.
5. **Unclear explanation of edge-based paradigm**: The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the edge-based paradigm and how it differs from the state-based paradigm.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clearer explanation of the specific issues with the mention hypergraph model that motivate your new model?
2. How do separators add value beyond traditional T/I/O labels, and why are they necessary?
3. Can you provide a more detailed demonstration of why your model is superior to the mention hypergraph model?
4. Can you provide concrete examples of spurious structures and how your model resolves this issue?
5. Can you provide a clearer explanation of the edge-based paradigm and how it differs from the state-based paradigm?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is generally well-written, but there are some minor issues with clarity and organization. The authors could benefit from providing more concrete examples and clearer explanations of their model and its advantages.
