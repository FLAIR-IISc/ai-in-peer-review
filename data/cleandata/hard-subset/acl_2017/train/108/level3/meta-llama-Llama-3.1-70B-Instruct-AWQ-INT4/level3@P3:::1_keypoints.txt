- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition with strong empirical results on standard datasets.
- Claims three contributions: multigraph model, higher F1-scores vs prior models, and theoretical analysis showing non-deficiency (no spurious structures).
- Empirically outperforms the mention hypergraph model and other baselines on multiple datasets.
- Introduces a novel multigraph approach that departs from traditional graph-based models.
- Provides theoretical analysis addressing spurious structures and claiming the model is non-deficient.
- Section 3 lacks clarity on the specific issues with the mention hypergraph model that motivate the new approach.
- The role and necessity of introduced separators (Section 4) are not clearly explained relative to T/I/O labels.
- Section 5.1 does not clearly demonstrate why the proposed model is superior to the mention hypergraph model.
- Section 5.2 lacks concrete examples of spurious structures, making the claimed issue difficult to assess.
- The edge-based paradigm is not clearly explained or contrasted with the state-based paradigm.
- Requests clearer explanation of the specific shortcomings of the mention hypergraph model that motivate this work.
- Requests justification for how separators add value beyond traditional labeling schemes and why they are needed.
- Requests a more detailed empirical/theoretical demonstration of superiority over the mention hypergraph model.
- Requests concrete examples of spurious structures and demonstration of how the new model resolves them.
- Requests a clearer explanation of the edge-based paradigm and its differences from the state-based paradigm.
- Paper is generally well-written but needs improved clarity, organization, and more concrete examples.
- Authors should provide clearer explanations and illustrative examples to better convey model advantages and reasoning.
