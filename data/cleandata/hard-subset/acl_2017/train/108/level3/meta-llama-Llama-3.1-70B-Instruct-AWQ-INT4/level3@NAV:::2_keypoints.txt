- Proposes a multigraph-based model that allows multiple edges between nodes to capture overlapping entities.
- Evaluated on standard datasets and shown to be effective at recognizing overlapping entities.
- Provides theoretical analysis of model properties, including resolving "spurious structures."
- Addresses the relevant problem of overlapping entity detection and shows empirical promise.
- Claims efficiency improvements over a prior system.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear model explanations.
- Motivation for detecting nested entities is weak and under-explained.
- Missing key algorithmic details (edge inclusion criteria, node variant choices).
- Empirical improvements are small and lack analysis of practical significance.
- No comparisons to simple dictionary or commercial baselines.
- Experiments and error analysis insufficiently focus on crossing entities.
- Requests clarification on the decision criterion for including edges in the multigraph.
- Asks how multiple edges between the same nodes with differing weights/labels are handled.
- Requests more analysis of the model's practical impact on overlapping entity recognition.
- Asks how the model performs on datasets with a higher proportion of crossing entities.
- Recommends more detailed error analysis and comparisons with prior systems.
- Notes Figure 2 uses colors/gray nodes without explanation and lacks justification of example goals.
- Points out several unclear terms/notations (e.g., "spurious structures", "state" vs. "node").
- Suggests more consistent reference usage and capitalization in the bibliography.
