**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The model is an extension of the mention hypergraph (MH) model, which is a state-of-the-art approach for entity recognition. The authors argue that the MH model has limitations, such as the presence of spurious structures, which can lead to suboptimal performance. The proposed multigraph-based model addresses these limitations by introducing entity separators, which allow for more accurate representation of overlapping entities. The model is evaluated on several benchmarks, including ACE2004, ACE2005, and GENIA, and shows significant improvements over the MH model.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text.
2. The model is shown to outperform the state-of-the-art MH model on several benchmarks.
3. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the model, highlighting its advantages over the MH model.

**Strengths**

1. The paper tackles a useful problem in natural language processing, namely overlapping entity detection.
2. The proposed model is well-motivated and addresses specific limitations of the MH model.
3. The empirical results are solid and demonstrate significant improvements over the state-of-the-art.
4. The paper provides a clear and concise presentation of the model and its evaluation.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the issues with the MH model that motivate the new approach.
2. The introduction of entity separators in Section 4 is not clearly explained, and it is unclear what they add beyond the existing T/I/O representation.
3. The comparison between the state-based and edge-based paradigms in Section 5.1 is not convincing, and it is unclear why the new model is superior.
4. The discussion of spurious structures in Section 5.2 is too abstract and could benefit from concrete examples.
5. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of why and how the new model works better than the MH model.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the issues with the MH model that motivate the new approach?
2. How do entity separators contribute to the representation of overlapping entities, and what are the advantages of using them?
3. Can you provide more concrete examples of spurious structures and how they are addressed by the new model?
4. How do you plan to extend the model to other structured prediction problems involving complex structures?
5. Can you provide more insights into why the new model works better than the MH model, and what are the key factors contributing to its improved performance?
