- Contribution: Proposes a multigraph structured prediction model with typed edge "entity separators" and inside–outside/Viterbi inference in O(nT) for overlapping entity recognition.
- Contribution: Demonstrates empirical improvements over strong baselines (including mention hypergraph) on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA, and competitive results on CoNLL 2003.
- Contribution: Argues theoretically that the model is nondeficient (avoids spurious structures) compared to mention hypergraph, potentially explaining better calibration/recall.

- Strength: Addresses a significant problem (overlapping entities) common in ACE and GENIA with a general edge-based formulation.
- Strength: Solid empirical gains across datasets, with meaningful F1 increases (e.g., +2.1 on ACE2004) and robustness in non-overlapping settings.
- Strength: Methodological care in feature parity, tuning, and reporting raw vs dev-optimized results for interpretability.
- Strength: Maintains O(nT) complexity like MH, converges in fewer iterations (though iterations are ~12% slower), suggesting practical training advantages.
- Strength: Clear conceptual contribution distinguishing state-based vs edge-based structured modeling.

- Weakness: Motivation versus MH is insufficiently concrete; lacks targeted error analysis showing where MH fails (precision/recall, span lengths, nested vs crossing).
- Weakness: Necessity and contribution of the 8 separator types is unclear; no ablations showing which separators matter.
- Weakness: Spurious-structures/nondeficiency argument is abstract and under-validated; needs concrete minimal example, formal proof sketch, and empirical linkage to learning gains.
- Weakness: Ambiguity about supporting crossing spans — decoding may force nesting; need clarification and assessment of prevalence/impact of crossings.
- Weakness: Reporting gaps and fairness concerns — no statistical significance, differing GENIA preprocessing, tuned mention penalties, and missing wall-clock/memory quantification for 8 separators.

- Question: Provide a crisp minimal example in the main paper showing spurious structures in MH and how the multigraph avoids them with explicit inside/outside computations.
- Question: Do you truly allow crossing entities at inference/decoding? If not, how many gold crossings exist and how would nesting-only decoding affect results?
- Question: What empirical evidence isolates nondeficiency as the cause of gains vs separator-induced feature interactions (e.g., synthetic study or calibration/likelihood analysis)?
- Question: Please add ablations on separator design: fewer types, collapsed state-based variant, and removing the mention penalty — how do these affect F1/recall on overlapping spans?
- Question: Provide statistical significance tests for main tables and wall-clock/memory comparisons vs MH on ACE2004/2005.
- Question: Are cross-type entity interactions modeled, and would modest cross-type features yield further gains?

- Comment: Section 3 should explicitly state specific MH limitations motivating the design and link to later analysis/error breakdowns.
- Comment: Section 4 would benefit from a step-by-step example contrasting MH edges vs the proposed separators on a short sentence.
- Comment: Clean up references, specify dataset splits/tokenization (especially GENIA), and clarify preprocessing differences impacting comparability.
- Comment: Make figures self-contained (separator legends, consistent notation) and add a table mapping separator types to span events for readability.

- Overall: Strong, well-written paper with practical, effective model and results; main weaknesses are clarity of motivation vs MH and need for targeted ablations and concrete validation of the nondeficiency claim.
