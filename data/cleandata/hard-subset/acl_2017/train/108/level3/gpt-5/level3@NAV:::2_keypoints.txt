- Proposes an edge-based multigraph model with explicit “entity separators” to handle overlapping entities while avoiding spurious structures and keeping O(nT) complexity.
- Reports modest F1 improvements over mention hypergraphs on ACE2004/2005 and comparable/better results on GENIA, plus some training-efficiency observations.
- Claimed contributions: (1) conceptual edge-based formulation with nondeficient normalization, (2) empirical gains and efficiency advantages, (3) discussion of state- vs edge-based trade-offs.
- Strength: Tackles the important problem of overlapping/crossing entity detection relevant to ACE/GENIA.
- Strength: Multigraph method is sensible and shows up to ~2 F1 gain on ACE and competitive performance on GENIA.
- Strength: Provides analysis of spurious structures and argues the model is nondeficient while retaining O(nT) complexity.
- Strength: Presents some evidence of faster convergence (fewer L-BFGS iterations) with candid note of per-iteration overhead.
- Strength: Generally clear exposition of the edge-based paradigm and feature design alignment with baselines.
- Weakness: Poor motivation for nested entities and limited discussion of practical utility or significance of small F1 gains.
- Weakness: Crossing-entity handling is not convincingly emphasized and experiments do not isolate crossing-specific improvements.
- Weakness: Key algorithmic details are missing (exact edge inclusion rules, active Ikt variants/states, scoring/decoding mechanics).
- Weakness: Empirical gains are small and lack effect-size reporting, confidence intervals, significance tests, and per-type analyses.
- Weakness: Missing important baselines (gazetteer/dictionary/commercial systems) and ablation studies on features or separator inventory.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—“multigraph” undefined on first use, unclear figures in grayscale, under-specified terms, and style/grammar/reference inconsistencies.
- Reviewer requests: report counts and P/R/F1 for crossing vs nested vs non-overlapping mentions; clarify whether nested-decoding suppresses crossing and possibly report alternative policies.
- Reviewer requests: provide exact criteria for ES/CS/EC/ECS edge inclusion and any pruning heuristics, full DP recurrences and instantiated Ikt variants/states.
- Reviewer requests: include effect sizes with CIs and statistical tests, per-entity-type metrics and error breakdowns, comparisons to gazetteer/commercial baselines, and concrete success/failure examples versus mention hypergraphs.
- Overall verdict: Lukewarm, leaning toward rejection unless crossing-entity contributions are empirically substantiated and missing details/analyses are addressed.
