- Summary: Proposes a novel multigraph-based structured prediction model to recognize overlapping entities, addressing limitations of prior graph/hypergraph approaches.

Contributions:
- Model innovation: Introduces a multigraph framework that assigns semantics to edges to represent overlapping entities.
- Empirical performance: Shows improved F1 over state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., mention hypergraph) on datasets like ACE2004/2005 and GENIA.
- Theoretical analysis: Compares state-based and edge-based paradigms and claims to resolve spurious structures in the mention hypergraph, making the model nondeficient.

Strengths:
- Clear motivation and well-written exposition on the importance of handling overlapping entities.
- Strong empirical results with analysis on overlapping and non-overlapping subsets.
- Valuable theoretical discussion on spurious structures and paradigm distinctions.

Weaknesses:
- Section 3 lacks a detailed explanation of the specific limitations of the mention hypergraph (MH) model.
- Section 4 does not clearly explain the role and mechanics of entity separators in the multigraph.
- Section 5.1 offers mainly theoretical comparison without strong empirical support for edge-based superiority.
- Section 5.2’s discussion of spurious structures is abstract and needs concrete examples.
- Insufficient analysis explaining why the multigraph approach outperforms MH in practice.

Questions to authors:
- Provide a more detailed explanation of MH’s limitations that motivated the multigraph model.
- Explain how entity separators enable handling overlapping entities, with a detailed example.
- Supply empirical evidence that edge-based paradigms outperform state-based paradigms in practice.
- Give concrete examples of spurious structures in MH and their impact on performance.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on addressing the clarity and justification issues outlined above.
