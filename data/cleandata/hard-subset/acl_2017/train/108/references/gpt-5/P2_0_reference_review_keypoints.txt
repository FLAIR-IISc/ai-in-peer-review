- Proposes an edge-based multigraph representation for overlapping NER using explicit “entity separator” edges and an O(nT) dynamic program.  
- Argues the model is nondeficient: its partition function avoids spurious structures present in mention hypergraphs.  
- Shows consistent but modest F1 gains on ACE2004/2005, GENIA, and CoNLL03 versus a reimplementation of mention hypergraphs, with faster convergence.

- Novel framing: cleanly separates state-based vs edge-based modeling and promotes multigraphs for overlapping structures.  
- Efficient and compact: dynamic program is linear in sentence length and types, using 8 separator types per gap for simple inference.  
- Nondeficiency analysis: careful discussion and examples showing mention hypergraph spurious-structure issues; multigraph sums over paths not subgraphs.  
- Fair empirical setup: features aligned with prior work and mention penalty tuned for both systems; improvements match datasets with more overlaps.  
- Practical training: empirical evidence of faster optimization (fewer L-BFGS iterations) when using the multigraph.

- Limited novelty: multigraph may be a reparameterization of mention hypergraphs with similar modeling power; distinctiveness rests on the nondeficiency claim.  
- Weak empirical scope: evaluation uses feature-based, older baselines and lacks comparisons to modern neural nested/overlapping NER systems.  
- Ambiguity on crossing mentions: unclear whether the model truly supports crossing entities or biases to nested decoding; needs clarity and analysis.  
- Modest improvements and missing significance testing; GENIA preprocessing differs from prior work and CoNLL03 experiment is not well-matched to overlapping NER.  
- Reproducibility gaps: code not yet provided and several details missing (evaluation scripts, mention penalty values, decoding constraints, ablations).

- Does the model natively represent and decode crossing mentions, how frequent are crossing cases, and how often does the “resolve as nested” heuristic change outputs?  
- Can the authors provide a formal proof or bijection that entity combinations correspond one-to-one to path collections in the multigraph (nondeficiency)?  
- Please include a linear-chain CRF over separators as a baseline to quantify benefits of the multigraph vs a simpler first-order model.  
- Can you compare against recent neural nested NER systems on ACE/GENIA (with/without external resources) or justify the historical framing?  
- How sensitive are results to the GENIA token-splitting scheme; can you report numbers under standard tokenization?

- Originality: moderate—neat reframing but main novelty hinges on formalizing nondeficiency.  
- Soundness/correctness: generally sound but lacking full proofs/details (complexity and nondeficiency).  
- Clarity: good overall, but ambiguities remain about crossing support and decoding choices.  
- Empirical evaluation: adequate against classic baselines but insufficient vs modern neural systems and lacks significance testing.  
- Reproducibility: partially met; additional details and code release required.
