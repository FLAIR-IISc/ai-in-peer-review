- Proposes an edge-semantic multigraph model with “entity separators” for overlapping (nested/crossing) NER, yielding O(nT) global inference.  
- Claims to avoid mention-hypergraph “spurious structures” via multiedges and globally normalized inference.  
- Empirically shows small but consistent F1 improvements on ACE2004/2005, GENIA, and CoNLL-2003 versus structured baselines.

- Clear and intuitive edge-based formulation that maps token-boundary semantics to overlapping spans while preserving linear-time inference.  
- Provides a conceptual/theoretical clarification of why mention hypergraphs are deficient and gives an illustrative appendix example.  
- Fair empirical comparisons using identical features/training with a reimplementation of mention hypergraphs.  
- Reports convergence behavior (fewer iterations) and practical feasibility while keeping O(nT) complexity.  
- Broadly frames the state- vs. edge-based paradigm discussion that may generalize to other overlapping-structure problems.

- Novelty may be limited because separators could be encoded as linear-chain states; no empirical separator-as-state baseline or analysis is provided.  
- The nondeficiency claim is informal; no formal proof or bijection between valid entity sets and derivations is given.  
- Ambiguity around crossing mentions: paper claims support but decoding seems to favor nested outputs; handling and frequency of this are unclear.  
- Empirical gains are modest (≈1–2 F1 on ACE, ≈0.4 on GENIA) with no statistical significance tests or extensive error/type/length analyses.  
- Missing comparisons to stronger/neural nested NER systems and additional classic baselines (e.g., Semi-CRF, parser-based) across datasets.  
- Reproducibility concerns: preprocessing choices (GENIA tokenization, coordinated entities), CoNLL claims, and promised code not yet available.

- Request a formal proof or proof sketch that each valid entity combination maps to exactly one derivation in the multigraph partition.  
- Ask whether decoding actually supports crossing mentions in practice, how ambiguous cases are resolved, and statistics on decoding-induced nesting.  
- Ask if a separator-as-state linear-chain baseline was implemented and, if not, for theoretical/empirical justification of the edge-based approach.  
- Request statistical significance tests and per-entity-type/length breakdowns for reported improvements.  
- Ask how cross-type dependencies are modeled: are entity types independent given the structure or are there cross-type constraints/features?

- Request clarification of constant factors in the O(nT) claim (e.g., 8 separator edges) and end-to-end runtime/memory per sentence.  
- Suggest improving figure/notation clarity and fully reporting all table numbers (CoNLL, GENIA) with exact values.
