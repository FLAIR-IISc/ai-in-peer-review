- Proposes a multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition with explicit edge semantics (entity separators) and claims higher F1 and avoidance of spurious structures while retaining inference complexity.  
- Main contribution 1: theoretical analysis identifying "spurious structures" flaw in the mention hypergraph model.  
- Main contribution 2: introduces a multigraph model with explicit edge semantics to avoid spurious structures.  
- Main contribution 3: empirical validation showing consistent F1 improvements over prior models.  
- Strength 1: strong, well-supported theoretical proof that mention hypergraph’s normalization includes spurious structures.  
- Strength 2: clear conceptual distinction between state-based and edge-based paradigms, clarifying the role of edges in structured prediction.  
- Strength 3: consistent, statistically significant empirical gains on ACE2004/2005, GENIA, and competitive performance on CONLL2003.  
- Weakness 1: novelty claim overstated—multigraph is a refinement of edge-based approaches (related to mention hypergraph) rather than a wholly new paradigm.  
- Weakness 2: lacks a detailed step-by-step example showing how the multigraph resolves a specific spurious-structure case compared to the mention hypergraph.  
- Weakness 3: inadequate discussion of practical implications and scalability (e.g., 12% extra training time, memory/time scaling for long sequences or many entity types).  
- Question 1: request for a concrete sentence example demonstrating mention hypergraph spurious structures and how the multigraph fixes it.  
- Question 2: ask for explanation of the 12% per-iteration overhead and how it scales with larger datasets or complex entity sets.  
- Question 3: ask why performance on GENIA’s overlapping portion lags and what modifications could help low-overlap datasets.  
- Question 4: request clarification on how this multigraph model differs from other graph-based approaches (e.g., Zhang et al., 2020).  
- Overall assessment: valuable theoretical contribution resolving spurious structures with supportive empirical gains, but novelty and practical analysis need clarification.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (clarify novelty, add detailed spurious-structure example, and discuss scalability/practical trade-offs).
