- Proposes a multigraph-based representation for overlapping entity recognition with explicit edge semantics.
- Claims the model avoids spurious structures from prior hypergraph models while keeping the same inference complexity.
- Shows empirical gains over the hypergraph baseline across multiple datasets.

- Provides a clear theoretical analysis explaining and resolving the hypergraph model’s spurious structures.
- Frames a useful paradigm shift by distinguishing state-based versus edge-based representations for entity structure.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA, and competitive results on non-overlapping CoNLL2003.
- Reports practical implementation details and faster convergence (fewer iterations) than the hypergraph baseline.

- Novelty is limited since the multigraph approach closely relates to the Lu and Roth (2015) hypergraph model and may overstate being “first.”
- Lacks comparisons with recent neural/transformer-based overlapping NER methods (e.g., BERT-based approaches).
- Does not analyze specific failure cases where the model underperforms the hypergraph baseline, e.g., GENIA overlapping sentences.
- No ablation studies to attribute performance gains specifically to the multigraph components versus other factors.
- The “non-deficient” claim and distinction from the hypergraph model are abstract and need clearer exposition in the main text.

- How does the multigraph model fundamentally differ from Lu and Roth (2015) to justify the “first multigraph” claim when edges correspond to hypergraph edge combinations?
- Is the slight GENIA drop on overlapping sentences due to limited overlapping training data or specific limitations of the model for certain overlap types?
- Have you compared to recent neural/transformer-based overlapping NER methods, and why would the multigraph approach be preferable?

- Paper is well-written with strong theoretical foundations, but should better contextualize novelty and include comparisons to modern neural baselines.
