- Summary: Proposes a multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition claiming to avoid mention-hypergraph "spurious structures", retain O(nT) inference, and improve results on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA.

Main contributions:
- Theoretical Analysis of Spurious Structures: Provides a detailed formal analysis showing the mention hypergraph model is "deficient" due to spurious structures in its normalization.
- Edge-Based Paradigm Framework: Distinguishes state-based vs. edge-based paradigms and frames an edge-based approach for overlapping-structure prediction.
- Empirical Validation: Demonstrates statistically significant empirical improvements over the mention hypergraph baseline on standard datasets.

Strengths:
- Significant Theoretical Insight: Offers a rigorous mathematical explanation of the spurious-structures issue with broader implications.
- Clear and Well-Structured Argument: Paper is logically organized and clearly contrasts modeling paradigms.
- Comprehensive Empirical Validation: Thorough experiments including overlap vs. non-overlap analyses and testing on non-overlapping NER.
- Practical Impact: Model applies to standard NER (e.g., CONLL2003) and outperforms linear-chain CRF and a constituency parser.
- Clear Explanation of Methodology: Good visualizations and detailed descriptions of multigraph representation and inference.

Weaknesses:
- Lack of Clear Novelty Distinction: Unclear whether multigraph/entity-separators are truly novel relative to hypergraph formulations.
- Limited Comparison with Recent Work: Fails to compare to more recent overlapping-NER models (e.g., Zhang et al. 2020; Wang et al. 2021; Zhang et al. 2022).
- Superficial Explanation of Spurious Structures: Main paper’s explanation of why spurious structures arise/are avoided is not sufficiently detailed (supplementary handles more).
- Lack of Ablation Studies: Missing analyses to show which components (entity separators vs. multigraph) drive gains.
- Insufficient Implementation Details: Omits key reproducibility details (features, iterations, hyperparameters, computational costs).

Questions to Authors:
- Why is the mention hypergraph model not considered a multigraph-based model, given hypergraphs can be seen as multigraphs?
- Please clarify in detail why the mention hypergraph normalization contains spurious structures and why the multigraph avoids them.
- Why were more recent state-of-the-art overlapping NER models not included in comparisons?
- Is a 2.1 F1 gain on ACE2004 sufficient to justify the added model complexity and ~12% extra training time per iteration?
- Why does a model designed for overlapping entities perform well on strictly non-overlapping datasets like CONLL2003?

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions: solid theoretical contribution and convincing results, but needs clearer novelty claims, comparisons to recent work, deeper explanation of spurious-structure avoidance, ablations, and more implementation details.
