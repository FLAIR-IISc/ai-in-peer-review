- Proposes a multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition, claiming higher F1 and a proof avoiding spurious structures while keeping same inference complexity.

- Strong theoretical analysis identifying and resolving "spurious structures" in the mention hypergraph model, with rigorous proof for the multigraph approach.

- Empirical results show consistent, statistically significant F1 improvements over the hypergraph model on ACE2004, ACE2005, and GENIA, supported by ablation studies.

- Clear problem formulation, well-structured presentation, and helpful visualizations; useful distinction between "state-based" and "edge-based" paradigms.

- Novelty claim appears overstated; multigraph may be a reparameterization of the hypergraph rather than a fundamentally new model.

- No detailed analysis for why the model underperforms on GENIA's overlapping-entity subset; authors' claim of insufficient training data is unsubstantiated.

- Insufficient explanation of computational differences: paper states same O(nT) complexity but reports ~12% slower training without justification.

- Limited discussion of practical limitations such as scalability to longer sequences, handling more complex overlapping patterns, and performance on datasets with high overlap.

- Question: Clarify how the multigraph representation fundamentally differs from the hypergraph beyond a representational viewpoint.

- Question: Provide detailed analysis and examples for GENIA failures on overlapping entities to explain the performance drop.

- Question: Explain the source of the reported 12% extra training time despite identical theoretical complexity.

- Overall recommendation: Accept (Strong Accept); theoretical resolution of spurious structures is a valuable contribution, but paper should temper novelty claims and address GENIA and computational-analysis gaps.
