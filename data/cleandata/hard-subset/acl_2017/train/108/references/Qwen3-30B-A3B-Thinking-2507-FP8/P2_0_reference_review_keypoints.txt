- Novel multigraph model for overlapping NER that uses edges as explicit "entity separators" and claims same inference complexity while avoiding spurious structures.  
- Main theoretical contribution: identifies and resolves the "spurious structures" issue in the mention hypergraph model.  
- Empirical contribution: thorough experiments on ACE2004/ACE2005/GENIA (and CONLL2003) showing consistent F1 improvements and faster convergence.  
- Conceptual contribution: introduces an "edge-based" paradigm for structured prediction as an alternative to state-based models.  
- Strength: clear, well-explained theoretical analysis demonstrating the model is non-deficient, with illustrative examples.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation across datasets and analyses of overlapping vs. non-overlapping performance.  
- Strength: useful conceptual framing (state-based vs. edge-based) that may generalize beyond NER.  
- Weakness: novelty claim feels overstated due to lack of rigorous mathematical comparison between multigraphs and hypergraphs.  
- Weakness: missing ablation studies to isolate the effects of multigraph representation vs. specific edge semantics.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and scalability (handling very long overlaps, many entity types, longer documents).  
- Question: provide formal proof/details showing Zw(x) in their model does not contain spurious structures.  
- Question: why does the model outperform on CONLL2003 (no overlapping entities), and what does this imply about broader applicability?  
- Question: clarify how computational cost scales with number of entity types T and how constant factors change.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen novelty comparison and include ablation studies; reviewer confidence high.
