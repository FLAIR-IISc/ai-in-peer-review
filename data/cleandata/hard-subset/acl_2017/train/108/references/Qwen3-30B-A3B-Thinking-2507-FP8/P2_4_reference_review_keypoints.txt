- Proposes a multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition using "entity separators" and claims three contributions: novel model, higher F1, and theoretical proof avoiding spurious structures with O(nT) complexity.

- Theoretical strength: rigorously identifies and resolves the "spurious structures" issue in the mention hypergraph model while preserving O(nT) inference time.

- Empirical strength: consistent F1 improvements on ACE2004/2005/GENIA (up to +2.1 on ACE2004), with analysis showing gains mainly on sentences with overlapping entities and good generalization to CONLL2003.

- Conceptual strength: clear framework distinguishing state-based vs. edge-based approaches, highlighting under-explored edge-based methods.

- Weakness: core model novelty is limited; appears to rephrase the mention hypergraph and entity separators may be equivalent to hyperedge combinations.

- Weakness: lacks explanation for why empirical gains occur; no ablation or feature analyses tying the no-spurious-structures property to improved performance.

- Weakness: inadequate comparisons—only evaluated against the dated mention hypergraph and omits recent neural overlapping-NER methods (e.g., Lample et al., 2016; Chen et al., 2019).

- Weakness: insufficient empirical analysis of how spurious structures impacted prior models and how the new model remedies that in practice.

- Questions for authors: clarify fundamental differences from the mention hypergraph beyond terminology; provide concrete evidence linking no-spurious-structures to performance gains; explain omission of comparisons to recent neural approaches.

- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution and solid empirical results, but novelty claims need clarification and additional analyses/comparisons are required; suitable for ACL with minor revisions.
