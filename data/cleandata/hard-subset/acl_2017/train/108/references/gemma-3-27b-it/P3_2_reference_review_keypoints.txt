- Proposes a multigraph-based model for overlapping NER that assigns semantics to multiple edges between node pairs.  
- Reports improved F1 scores on ACE and GENIA and claims comparable inference complexity to Lu & Roth (2015).  
- Argues theoretically that the multigraph avoids the "spurious structures" issue present in the Lu & Roth baseline.  
- Main contribution: formalizes overlapping entity structures using a multigraph for more explicit and interpretable representation.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over a strong baseline on standard datasets.  
- Main contribution: provides theoretical analysis highlighting deficiencies in prior hypergraph approaches.  
- Strength: clear motivation and well-defined problem framing for overlapping NER.  
- Strength: novelty of assigning explicit semantics to multigraph edges in structured prediction.  
- Strength: strong empirical results across multiple datasets strengthen the claims.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis of spurious structures increases credibility.  
- Strength: code and system availability for research use.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on feature engineering and lack of ablation studies to isolate contributions.  
- Weakness: multigraph model appears more complex to implement and needs more intuitive explanation and visualizations.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation (only ACE and GENIA) raises generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: some sections are dense and could be more concise (e.g., state-based vs. edge-based discussion).  
- Question: please provide detailed feature sets, feature importance, and ablation results for major feature categories.  
- Question: provide a detailed computational cost breakdown, including memory usage and constant factors, compared to Lu & Roth.  
- Question: clarify scalability to very long sentences or documents and any multigraph-related limitations.  
- Question: give concrete examples of adapting the multigraph framework to other overlapping-structure problems.  
- Additional comment: include error analysis with concrete success/failure cases.  
- Additional comment: add a complete list of features per dataset in the supplementary material.  
- Additional comment: provide a runtime comparison between this implementation and Lu & Roth (2015).  
- Overall assessment: a strong, novel, and promising paper with compelling results; recommend acceptance despite relatively minor concerns.
