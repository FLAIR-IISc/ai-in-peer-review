- Strength: Paper is well-written with minor clarifications needed.  
- Strength: Addresses a useful problem and proposes a valuable multigraph-based variant of the MH model.  
- Strength: Empirical results are solid and improve on prior state of the art.  
- Weakness: Several sections need clearer exposition to improve coherence.  
- Section 3: Explicitly state the issues with the original MH model that motivate the new model.  
- Section 4: Clarify the role of separators and what information they add beyond T/I/O labels.  
- Section 5.1: Provide deeper analysis explaining why the new model outperforms existing approaches.  
- Section 5.2: Make the discussion less abstract by giving concrete examples of spurious structures the new model handles better than MH.  
- General: The paper shows performance gains over MH but should better explain how and why the new model overcomes MH’s limitations.
