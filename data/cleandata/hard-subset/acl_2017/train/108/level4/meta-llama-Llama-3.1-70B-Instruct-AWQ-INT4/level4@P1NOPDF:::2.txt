The paper proposes a multigraph-based approach for detecting potentially overlapping entities. 

Strengths:
The problem addressed in the paper is interesting, particularly when dealing with crossing entities, where the goal is to determine which entity is actually mentioned in a given text. Although the empirical results do not demonstrate a dramatic effect, the technique appears to be effective. The authors have also made an effort to discuss efficiency compared to a previous system, which is a welcome addition. The paper is generally well-written, but some minor polishing is needed in certain areas (see minor remarks below).

Weaknesses:
The problem itself is not well-motivated. It is unclear why detecting "China" as an entity within the entity "Bank of China" (as mentioned in the introduction) is important. While crossing entities are a valid concern, the use case for nested entities is not clearly explained. This lack of motivation makes it challenging for the reader to become invested in the problem. Regarding the approach, some crucial details are missing. For instance, the decision criterion for including an edge in the multigraph is not specified. In lines 229-233, several options for the I^k_t nodes are mentioned, but it is unclear which edges should be present.

The empirical evaluation shows that the achieved results are better than some previous approaches, but the improvement is not substantial. The authors claim that their method "outperforms" previous approaches, but this assertion is not entirely convincing. The effect size of the improvement is not provided, making it difficult to assess its significance. It is also unclear whether the slight improvement in F_1 score (two percentage points) has any practical impact on users. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to compare the performance of the proposed method to that of a simplistic dictionary-based approach or a commercial system like Google's NLP cloud, which could also be used to detect and link entities.

The discussion of the results could be improved by placing more emphasis on actual crossing entities. How does the performance of the proposed method compare to that of previous approaches in this regard? How many more crossing entities are detected, and which ones were missed? Is the performance improvement due solely to better nested entity detection or also due to the detection of crossing entities? A general error discussion comparing the errors made by the proposed system and previous ones would also strengthen this section.

General Discussion:
While the problems related to named entity recognition are interesting, the paper does not convincingly motivate the importance of recognizing nested entities. The evaluation does not shed light on this aspect either. Discussing errors, advantages, and example cases, with an emphasis on the results for crossing entities compared to other approaches, could have made a stronger case for the paper. As it stands, the reviewer is only lukewarm about the paper, with a slight tendency towards rejection. It appears to be just another attempt without emphasizing the important question of crossing entities.
