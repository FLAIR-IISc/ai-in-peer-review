- Proposes a multigraph-based method to identify potentially overlapping entities, addressing intersecting entity cases.  
- Problem is interesting but the significance, especially for nested entities (e.g., "China" in "Bank of China"), is not well motivated.  
- Technique appears effective, but empirical gains over existing methods are marginal.  
- Authors provide a useful discussion of efficiency compared to a prior system.  
- Paper is generally well-written but requires minor polishing and consistency fixes.  
- Key methodological details are missing, notably the decision criterion for including edges.  
- Evaluation lacks focus on crossing entities, which are the most interesting subset of overlapping entities.  
- No detailed error analysis or comparison of errors between the proposed system and prior systems.  
- The paper fails to convincingly argue the practical importance of identifying nested entities.  
- Empirical improvements may not be practically significant; effect size discussion is needed.  
- Readers would benefit from a brief description of multigraphs for unfamiliar audiences.  
- Clarify abbreviations (e.g., "NP") and technical terms (e.g., what constitutes a "state" in hypergraphs).  
- Figures and tables need clearer formatting and better context, especially the selection of edges/nodes in Figure 2.  
- Notation for denoting entities is inconsistent and should be standardized.  
- Footnotes are placed incorrectly (should follow punctuation).  
- Provide more rationale for algorithmic choices, such as selecting the CS-edge.  
- Improve clarity and formatting of equations and tables.  
- Give more context and interpretation for statistics and results presented.  
- Ensure references are formatted consistently, using title case for paper titles.
