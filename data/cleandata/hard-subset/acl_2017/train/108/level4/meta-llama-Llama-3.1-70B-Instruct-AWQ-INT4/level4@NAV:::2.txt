The paper proposes a multigraph-based approach for identifying potentially overlapping entities, which is an interesting problem, particularly in cases where entities intersect.

Strengths:
The technique appears to be effective, although the empirical results do not demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods. The authors' discussion of efficiency compared to a previous system is a welcome addition. The paper is well-written, but some minor polishing is required to address the issues mentioned below.

Weaknesses:
The problem's significance is not adequately motivated. It is unclear why detecting entities like "China" within "Bank of China" is important. While there is a clear use case for intersecting entities, the authors fail to provide a compelling argument for identifying nested entities. The approach lacks crucial details, such as the decision criterion for including edges. The empirical evaluation shows only marginal improvements over previous methods, which may not be practically significant.

Regarding the results discussion, the authors should have focused more on the performance of crossing entities, which is a more interesting subset of overlapping entities. A comparison of errors made by the proposed system and previous ones would have strengthened the discussion.

General Discussion:
The paper's topic is relevant to named entity recognition, but the authors fail to convincingly argue for the importance of identifying nested entities. The evaluation does not provide sufficient insight into the advantages and errors of the proposed approach, particularly with regard to crossing entities.

Minor remarks:
- A brief description of multigraphs would be helpful for readers unfamiliar with the concept.
- Some minor errors and inconsistencies in formatting and notation were noted throughout the paper.
- The use of abbreviations, such as "NP" for noun phrase chunking, should be clarified.
- The paper would benefit from more detailed explanations of technical terms, such as "state" in the context of hypergraphs.
- The formatting of figures and tables could be improved for better clarity.
- The authors should provide more context for the selection of edges and nodes in Figure 2.
- The notation for denoting entities is inconsistent and should be standardized.
- Footnotes should be placed after punctuation marks, not before.
- The paper would benefit from more detailed explanations of algorithmic choices, such as the selection of the CS-edge.
- The language and formatting of equations and tables could be improved for better clarity.
- The authors should provide more context for the statistics and results presented in the paper.
- The formatting of references should be consistent, with paper titles in title case.
- The effect size of the results should be discussed in more detail, particularly with regard to the practical significance of the improvements.
