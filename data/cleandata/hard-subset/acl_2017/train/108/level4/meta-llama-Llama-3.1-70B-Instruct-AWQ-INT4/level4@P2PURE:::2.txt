The paper proposes an approach based on multigraphs, where multiple edges can connect two nodes, for detecting potentially overlapping entities.

Strengths:
The problem of overlapping entities is intriguing, particularly in cases where entities intersect, and the technique appears to be effective. Although the empirical results do not demonstrate a significant improvement, the paper's discussion on efficiency compared to a previous system is appreciated. The overall writing is clear, but some details require further refinement (see minor remarks below).

Weaknesses:
The problem of detecting nested entities, such as identifying "China" within "Bank of China," is not well-motivated. While there is value in addressing crossing entities, the use case for nested entities is unclear. The approach lacks crucial details, including the decision criterion for including edges in the multigraph. The discussion on the I^k_t nodes (lines 229-233) mentions various options but fails to clarify which edges should be present.

The empirical evaluation shows marginal improvements over previous approaches, but the effect size is unclear. It is debatable whether a two percentage point improvement in F_1 score is substantial. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to compare the results to those obtained by a simplistic dictionary-based method or a commercial system like Google's NLP cloud. This would provide a better understanding of the approach's performance in relation to existing systems.

The discussion on the results could be improved by emphasizing the performance on crossing entities, which is a more interesting subset of overlapping entities. It would be valuable to know how many more crossing entities are detected compared to previous methods, which entities were missed, and why. A general error discussion comparing the errors made by the proposed system and previous ones would also strengthen this section.

General Discussion:
While the problem of named entity recognition is relevant, the paper fails to motivate the importance of detecting nested entities. The evaluation does not provide insight into this aspect, and the discussion on errors and advantages could be improved with example cases and an emphasis on crossing entities. As a result, the reviewer remains lukewarm about the paper, with a slight inclination towards rejection, as it seems to be another attempt without adequately addressing the important question of crossing entities.
