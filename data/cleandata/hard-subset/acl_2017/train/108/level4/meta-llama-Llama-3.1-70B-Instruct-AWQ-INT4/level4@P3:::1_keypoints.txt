- Summary: Proposes a novel multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition and shows it outperforms prior models on ACE2004, ACE2005, and GENIA.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a multigraph-based model for predicting overlapping entities.

- Contribution 2: Empirically achieves higher F1 scores than previous models across multiple datasets.

- Contribution 3: Theoretically argues the model is non-deficient and avoids spurious-structure issues in inference.

- Strength 1: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Strength 2: Tackles a useful and relevant problem in NLP.

- Strength 3: Offers a new perspective as a variant of the mention hypergraph (MH) model.

- Strength 4: Solid empirical results demonstrating effectiveness.

- Weakness 1: Needs clearer motivation—Section 3 should explicitly state MH model issues that motivated the new approach.

- Weakness 2: Section 4 needs explanation for separators and what information they add beyond T/I/O labels.

- Weakness 3: Section 5.1’s state-based vs edge-based discussion is unconvincing about why the new model is superior.

- Weakness 4: Section 5.2’s treatment of spurious structures is abstract and lacks concrete examples.

- Weakness 5: Lacks deeper insight into why and how the new model outperforms the MH model.

- Question 1: Provide more details on the specific issues of the MH model that motivated the new model.

- Question 2: Explain how separators in Section 4 contribute to the model’s effectiveness.

- Question 3: Give concrete examples of spurious structures and show how the proposed model avoids them.

- Question 4: How will the model be extended to other structured prediction problems with complex structures?
