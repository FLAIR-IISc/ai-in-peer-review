- Paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model to recognize overlapping entities by representing them as multigraphs and reports strong empirical results on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA.
- Contribution: first structured prediction model using multigraphs to predict overlapping entity structures.
- Contribution: empirically achieves higher F1-scores than prior models across multiple datasets.
- Contribution: theoretically avoids spurious structures in inference and is non-deficient compared to a previous SOTA model.
- Strength: paper is well-written and addresses a useful problem.
- Strength: multigraph approach is a meaningful variant of the mention hypergraph (MH) model with solid empirical support.
- Strength: provides thorough analysis and comparisons with prior models.
- Weakness: Section 3 needs explicit explanation of MH model issues motivating the new model.
- Weakness: Section 4 should clarify why separators are introduced and what information they add beyond T/I/O labels.
- Weakness: Section 5.1 should give more concrete reasons why the new model is superior.
- Weakness: Section 5.2 is too abstract; authors should provide concrete examples of spurious structures.
- Weakness: overall lack of clarity on why the new model is better than MH in the discussion.
- Question: please provide deeper insights into why the multigraph model outperforms the MH model.
- Question: please include concrete examples of spurious structures to illustrate Section 5.2.
- Question: how can the model be extended to other structured prediction problems with complex structures?
- Additional: provide more details on the features used and their definitions.
- Additional: describe hyperparameter tuning procedures and selection for each dataset.
- Additional: detail preprocessing for GENIA, including tokenization handling and discontiguous entity treatment.
