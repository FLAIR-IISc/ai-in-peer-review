- Strengths: The paper is well-written, with a few exceptions noted below. The problem it addresses is useful, and the proposed multigraph-based model, a variant of the Matrix-Tree (MH) model, shows solid empirical results.

- Weaknesses: Several areas require clarification.
1. In Section 3, in addition to describing the previous MH model, it would be beneficial to highlight the specific issues with MH that motivated the proposal of a new model.
2. The introduction of separators in Section 4 is unclear; what additional information do they convey beyond the traditional T/I/O (Token/In/Out) structure?
3. Section 5.1 does not provide useful insights into why the new model is superior to existing approaches.
4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract, making it difficult to understand why the new model outperforms MH; providing concrete examples of spurious structures would be helpful.

- General Discussion: This paper proposes a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text, improving upon the previous state-of-the-art MH model in experiments on several benchmark datasets. However, it remains unclear why and how the new model achieves better results.
