**Strengths:** The paper is well-written, with a few minor exceptions noted below. The problem it addresses is practical and useful. The proposed multigraph-based model, a variant of the Matrix-Tree (MH) approach, offers a solid contribution. The empirical results presented in the paper are also robust.

**Weaknesses:** To enhance the clarity of the paper, several sections require further clarification.

1. **Section 3:** In addition to describing the previous MH model, it would be beneficial to highlight the specific issues with MH that motivated the proposal of a new model. This would provide a clearer justification for the new approach.

2. **Section 4:** The introduction of separators lacks clear explanation. It is not immediately apparent what additional information they convey beyond the T/I/O (Tag/Inside/Outside) information. Providing a more detailed explanation of their purpose and benefits would strengthen this section.

3. **Section 5.1:** This section does not provide sufficient insight into why the new model is superior to existing approaches. More specific examples or analysis would be helpful in demonstrating the advantages of the proposed model.

4. **Section 5.2:** The discussion in this section is somewhat abstract and could be improved with more concrete examples. Specifically, providing instances of spurious structures that the new model can handle better than MH would enhance understanding and appreciation of the model's capabilities.

**General Discussion:** The paper presents a novel model for detecting overlapping entities in text, improving upon the previous state-of-the-art MH model in experiments conducted on several benchmark datasets. However, it is not entirely clear why and how the new model achieves better performance. To strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to provide a more detailed explanation of the model's advantages and how it addresses the limitations of MH.
