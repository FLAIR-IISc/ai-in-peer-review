The paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The model is capable of handling both nested and crossing entities, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on standard datasets.

Main Contributions:

1. The authors propose a new multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities, which is the first structured prediction model to utilize multigraphs for this task.
2. The model is shown to outperform previous models in recognizing overlapping entities, with significant improvements in F1-score.
3. The authors provide a theoretical analysis of the model, demonstrating that it resolves the spurious structures issue associated with a previous state-of-the-art model.

Strengths:

1. The problem of recognizing overlapping entities is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of its importance.
2. The multigraph-based model is well-designed, and the authors provide a thorough explanation of its components and how it works.
3. The experimental results are convincing, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the model's performance.

Weaknesses:

1. The authors could provide more motivation for why recognizing nested entities is important, as this is not as clear as the motivation for recognizing crossing entities.
2. The decision criterion for including an edge in the multigraph is not clearly explained, and the authors could provide more details on this aspect.
3. The empirical evaluation could be strengthened by including more comparisons with other models, such as commercial systems, and by providing more analysis of the model's performance on crossing entities.

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide more details on the decision criterion for including an edge in the multigraph?
2. How do you determine which edges to include in the multigraph, and what is the impact of this decision on the model's performance?
3. Can you provide more analysis of the model's performance on crossing entities, and how it compares to previous models?
4. How does the model's performance change when recognizing nested entities, and what are the implications of this for real-world applications?

Minor Remarks:

1. The first mention of multigraphs could benefit from a short description for readers who may not be familiar with the concept.
2. Some minor formatting issues, such as inconsistent use of commas and periods, could be addressed to improve the overall clarity of the paper.
3. The use of footnotes could be improved by placing them after punctuation marks rather than directly in front of them.
