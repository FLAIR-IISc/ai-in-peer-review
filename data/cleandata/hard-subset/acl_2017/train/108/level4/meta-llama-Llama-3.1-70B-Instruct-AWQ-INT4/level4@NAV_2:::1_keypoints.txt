- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model that represents overlapping entities via multiple edges with explicit semantics.  
- Claims empirical improvement in F1-score over prior state-of-the-art (including mention hypergraph) on several benchmark datasets.  
- Provides a theoretical analysis arguing the model can better handle overlapping entities and has advantages over previous models.  
- Strength: well-written, tackles a useful problem, and presents solid empirical results.  
- Strength: clear explanation of the model and its advantages over previous approaches.  
- Weakness: Section 3 does not clearly state the specific problems with the mention hypergraph model that motivate the new approach.  
- Weakness: Section 4’s introduction of separators is unclear; unclear what extra information they provide beyond T/I/O labels.  
- Weakness: Section 5.1 lacks useful information explaining why the new model is superior.  
- Weakness: Section 5.2 is too abstract and does not convincingly show why the model outperforms the mention hypergraph; lacks examples of spurious structures.  
- Question: Please detail the issues with the mention hypergraph model that motivated your design.  
- Question: Explain how separators convey information beyond T/I/O and why they are necessary.  
- Question: Provide concrete examples illustrating the advantages of the new model over previous models.  
- Question: Give more details on the theoretical analysis demonstrating the model’s ability to handle overlapping entities and its advantages.  
- Additional comment: Experimental improvements are shown but the paper needs clearer explanations and examples to justify why and how the new model works better.
