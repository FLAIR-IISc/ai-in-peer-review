- Addresses an interesting problem, particularly for crossing entities.
- Technique appears effective though empirical gains are modest.
- Includes a useful discussion of efficiency compared to a prior system.
- Paper is generally well-written but needs minor polishing.

- Problem motivation is weak; the need to detect nested entities (e.g., "China" in "Bank of China") is not clearly justified.
- Use case for nested entities is unclear, making reader investment difficult.
- Crucial methodological details are missing, e.g., decision criterion for including edges in the multigraph.
- Unclear which edges should be present for the I^k_t nodes (lines 229-233).

- Reported empirical improvements are small and the claim of "outperforms" previous approaches is unconvincing.
- Effect size is not provided, so practical significance of ~2 percentage points F1 is unclear.
- No comparison to simple baselines (dictionary-based) or commercial systems (e.g., Google NLP) is provided.
- Evaluation does not focus enough on crossing entities—lack of analysis on how many more crossing entities are detected or missed.
- Unclear whether improvements stem from nested or crossing entity detection; error analysis comparing to prior systems is missing.

- Paper does not convincingly motivate importance of recognizing nested entities and the evaluation fails to address this gap.
- More error discussion, examples, and emphasis on crossing-entity results are needed to strengthen the paper.
- Reviewer is lukewarm, with a slight tendency toward rejection.

- First mention of multigraphs should include a brief description for unfamiliar readers.
- Awkward/unclear phrasings throughout (several cited examples).
- Unclear referents (e.g., who "they" are) and many sentences that need clarification.
- Typo: "pervious" → "previous".
- Citation phrasing issues (e.g., "of Lu and Roth~(2015)" should be rephrased).
- "NP chunking" should be expanded to "noun phrase chunking".
- Many unclear technical terms: "spurious structures", "mention hypergraph", "types of states", "child nodes" in hypergraph context.
- Figure 2 is unclear (color dependence, unexplained gray nodes) and its example is not well explained.
- Inconsistent entity notation (sometimes brackets, sometimes not).
- Footnote placement and content issues (footnotes before punctuation; unclear footnote 2 and 3).
- Several algorithmic/implementation choices are left unspecified (how CS-edge is chosen, how separator is determined).
- Formatting/grammar issues: missing commas, numeral vs. word usage, odd phrases like "to find out", "we make two", "used in this experiments".
- Claims of "significantly better" or "outperforms by about 0.4 points" need effect sizes and context.
- Dataset descriptions need clarity (e.g., "In GENIA dataset" → "On the GENIA dataset"; specify which dataset aspects are referred to).
- Clarify labeling scheme usage (BILOU) and what is being highlighted in bold.
- References inconsistent in title capitalization.
