- Proposes a multigraph-based method for detecting overlapping (especially crossing) entities.  
- Technique appears to work but empirical gains are modest rather than dramatic.  
- Paper is generally well-written but needs polishing on several details.  
- Problem motivation is weak, particularly the use case for nested entities (e.g., "China" in "Bank of China").  
- Decision criterion for including edges in the multigraph is unclear and under-specified.  
- Lines 229–233 mention options for I^k_t nodes but do not state which edges should actually be present.  
- Empirical evaluation shows improvements but the claimed "outperforms" is exaggerated given small effect sizes.  
- No comparison to simple baselines (dictionary) or commercial systems (e.g., Google NLP) to contextualize results.  
- Results discussion lacks emphasis on actual crossing-entity performance and how many more crossing entities are detected.  
- Paper omits a general error analysis comparing this system’s errors to prior approaches.  
- Including example cases and detailed error/advantage discussion would strengthen the paper.  
- Minor fixes needed: short definition of multigraph, clarification of unclear sentences/phrases, and correction of typos/formatting.  
- Recommendation: major revisions required to improve motivation, clarify methods, expand evaluations/comparisons, and address minor remarks.
