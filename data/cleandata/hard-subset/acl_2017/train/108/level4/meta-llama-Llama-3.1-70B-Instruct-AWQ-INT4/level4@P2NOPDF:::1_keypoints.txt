- Paper is well-written with minor exceptions.  
- Addresses a practical, useful problem.  
- Proposes a solid multigraph-based variant of the Matrix-Tree (MH) approach.  
- Empirical results are robust across benchmarks.  
- Section 3: needs to state specific shortcomings of MH that motivate the new model.  
- Section 4: separators are not clearly explained—clarify what they add beyond T/I/O information.  
- Section 5.1: lacks sufficient insight or examples showing why the new model is superior.  
- Section 5.2: discussion is abstract and needs concrete examples of spurious structures the new model handles better.  
- General: unclear why/how the new model achieves better performance; provide detailed explanation of its advantages and how it addresses MH limitations.
