The paper proposes an approach based on multigraphs (where several edges may link two nodes) for detecting potentially overlapping entities.

**Summary and Contributions:**
The authors present a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The main contributions of this work are: (1) a new model that can handle overlapping entities using multigraphs, (2) empirical results showing that the model outperforms previous models on standard datasets, and (3) a theoretical analysis of the model's strengths and weaknesses.

**Strengths:**
The problem of recognizing overlapping entities is interesting, especially for crossing entities. The technique seems to work, and the paper is well-written. The authors provide some discussion on efficiency compared to a previous system.

**Weaknesses:**
The problem itself is not well-motivated. The authors should clarify why detecting nested entities is important. The approach lacks some important details, such as the decision criterion for including an edge or not. The empirical evaluation shows only slight improvements over previous approaches, and the effect size is not clear. The authors should discuss the actual effect of the improvements and compare their results to those of commercial systems.

**Questions to Authors:**
1. Can you provide more motivation for detecting nested entities and clarify their importance?
2. How do you decide which edges to include in the multigraph, and what is the decision criterion?
3. Can you provide more discussion on the effect size of the improvements and compare your results to those of commercial systems?
4. How do you handle errors in the system, and what is the error analysis for the results?

**Minor Remarks:**
1. Consider adding a short description of multigraphs for readers who may not be familiar with the concept.
2. Clarify the notion of "state" in a (hyper-)graph and its relation to nodes.
3. Use consistent formatting for paper titles in the references.
4. Avoid using references as nouns; instead, use the author's name and year of publication.
5. Consider adding commas after enumeration items and a period after the last one.
6. Clarify the selection of highlighted edges in Figure 2 and why some nodes/edges are in gray.
7. Provide more discussion on the difference between detecting the long entity and the short entity in the example of Figure 2.
