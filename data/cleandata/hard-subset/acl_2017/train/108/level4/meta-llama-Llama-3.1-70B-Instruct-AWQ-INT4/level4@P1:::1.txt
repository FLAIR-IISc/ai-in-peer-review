**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The model is capable of handling overlapping entities by representing them as a multigraph, which is an extension of the traditional graph-based models. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model through extensive experiments on standard datasets, including ACE2004, ACE2005, and GENIA, and show that it outperforms previous models.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities, which is the first structured prediction model utilizing multigraphs to predict overlapping structures.
2. Empirically, the authors show that their model achieves higher F1-scores compared to previous models in multiple datasets.
3. Theoretically, the authors show that their model does not present the spurious structures issue in its inference procedure and is therefore non-deficient, unlike a previous state-of-the-art model.

**Strengths**

1. The paper is well-written, and the problem it tackles is useful.
2. The proposed approach, multigraph-based model, is a variant of the mention hypergraph (MH) model, and the empirical results are solid.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their model and compare it with previous models.

**Weaknesses**

1. Clarification is needed in several places, including:
	* Section 3: The authors should point out the issues of the MH model that motivate them to propose a new model.
	* Section 4: The authors should explain why separators are introduced and what additional information they convey beyond T/I/O.
	* Section 5.1: The authors should provide more useful information regarding why the new model is superior.
	* Section 5.2: The authors should provide examples of spurious structures to make the discussion more concrete.
2. The discussion in Section 5.2 is too abstract, and it is not clear why the new model is better than the MH model.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more insights into why the multigraph-based model is better than the MH model?
2. Can you provide examples of spurious structures to illustrate the discussion in Section 5.2?
3. How do the authors plan to extend their model to other structured prediction problems involving complex structures?

**Additional Comments**

1. The authors should consider providing more details on the features used in their model and how they are defined.
2. The authors should consider providing more information on the hyperparameter tuning process and how they selected the best values for each dataset.
3. The authors should consider providing more details on the preprocessing steps for the GENIA dataset, including how they handled tokenization issues and discontiguous entities.
