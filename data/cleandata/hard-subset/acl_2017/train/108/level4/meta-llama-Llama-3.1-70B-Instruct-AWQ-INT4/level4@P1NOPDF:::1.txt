- Strengths: The paper is well-written, with only a few areas requiring clarification, as described below. The problem it addresses is useful, and the proposed multigraph-based model, a variant of MH, is a valuable contribution. The empirical results are solid.

- Weaknesses: Several sections require further clarification to enhance the paper's overall coherence.

1. In Section 3, in addition to describing the previous MH model, it would be beneficial to explicitly highlight the issues with MH that motivate the proposal of a new model. This would provide a clearer justification for the work presented in the paper.

2. In Section 4, the introduction of separators is not adequately explained. Specifically, it is unclear what additional information they convey beyond the T/I/O information already presented. Further explanation is necessary to understand the rationale behind incorporating separators into the model.

3. Section 5.1 does not appear to provide useful insights into why the new model is superior to existing approaches. It would be helpful to include more detailed analysis or discussion to support the claims made in this section.

4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is quite abstract, making it challenging to understand the specific advantages of the new model over MH. To improve clarity, consider providing concrete examples of spurious structures that the new model can handle more effectively than MH.

- General Discussion: The paper proposes a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text, which improves upon the previous state-of-the-art MH model in experiments on several benchmark datasets. However, it is not entirely clear why and how the new model achieves better results. To strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to provide a more detailed explanation of the model's advantages and how it addresses the limitations of MH.
