**Strengths:** This well-written paper addresses a useful problem and proposes a multigraph-based model that builds upon existing work in the Maximum Homogeneity (MH) framework. The empirical results presented are solid and demonstrate the model's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:** Several areas require clarification to enhance the paper's overall coherence.

1. **Motivation for the new model**: In Section 3, it would be beneficial to explicitly state the limitations of the MH model that your approach aims to address, in addition to providing a description of the previous model.
2. **Introduction of separators**: The purpose and benefits of introducing separators in Section 4 are unclear. Specifically, it would be helpful to explain what additional information they convey beyond the existing T/I/O framework.
3. **Comparative analysis**: Section 5.1 could be improved by providing more insightful information on why the new model outperforms existing approaches.
4. **Discussion of improvements**: The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract and would benefit from concrete examples illustrating the spurious structures that the new model can identify more effectively than MH.

**General Discussion**: This paper introduces a novel model for detecting overlapping entities in text, demonstrating improvement over the state-of-the-art MH model on several benchmark datasets. However, further clarification is needed to fully understand the underlying reasons and mechanisms behind the new model's superior performance.
