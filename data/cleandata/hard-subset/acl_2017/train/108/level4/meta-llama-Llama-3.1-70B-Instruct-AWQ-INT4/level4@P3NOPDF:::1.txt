This paper presents a well-written and well-motivated approach to detecting overlapping entities in text, building upon the Multigraph-based model (MH). The problem tackled by the authors is a useful contribution to the field, and the empirical results are solid.

The paper has several strengths. The writing is clear and concise, making it easy to follow the authors' argument. The proposed approach, a variant of the MH model, is well-integrated into the existing literature. The empirical results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed model.

However, there are several areas that require clarification. In Section 3, the authors provide a description of the previous MH model, but it would be beneficial to explicitly state the issues with MH that motivated the proposal of a new model. This would help readers understand the rationale behind the new approach.

Additionally, the introduction of separators in Section 4 is not well-motivated. It is unclear what additional information separators convey beyond the traditional T/I/O (Token/Input/Output) framework. The authors should provide a clear explanation for the necessity of separators and how they enhance the model.

Section 5.1 does not provide sufficient insight into why the new model is superior to the existing state-of-the-art. The discussion is lacking in concrete examples or empirical evidence that demonstrates the advantages of the proposed approach.

Furthermore, the discussion in Section 5.2 is too abstract, making it difficult to understand why the new model is better than MH. The authors should provide specific examples of spurious structures that the new model can handle more effectively than MH. This would help readers appreciate the practical implications of the proposed approach.

In general, the paper presents a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text, which improves upon the previous state-of-the-art, MH, in experiments on several benchmark datasets. However, it is not entirely clear why and how the new model works better. To strengthen the paper, the authors should address the above-mentioned issues and provide more concrete explanations and examples to support their claims.
