This paper has several strengths. The writing is clear, except for a few areas that require clarification, as described below. The problem addressed by the paper is useful and relevant. The proposed multigraph-based model is a variant of the Multihop (MH) model, and the empirical results are solid.

However, there are several weaknesses that need to be addressed. Firstly, clarification is needed in several places to improve the paper's overall coherence. Specifically:

1. In Section 3, in addition to describing the previous MH model, the authors should highlight the issues with MH that motivated the proposal of a new model. This would provide a clearer understanding of the improvements offered by the new approach.

2. In Section 4, the introduction of separators is not well justified. It is unclear what additional information separators convey beyond the traditional T/I/O (type/inside/outside) representation. The authors should provide a clear explanation for the necessity of separators.

3. Section 5.1 does not provide useful insights into why the new model is superior to existing approaches. The authors should rephrase or expand this section to better demonstrate the advantages of their model.

4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is too abstract, making it difficult to understand why the new model is better than MH. To address this, the authors should provide concrete examples of spurious structures that their model can handle more effectively than MH.

In general, the paper proposes a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text, which improves upon the previous state-of-the-art MH model in experiments on several benchmark datasets. However, it is not entirely clear why and how the new model achieves better results. The authors should strive to provide a more detailed explanation of their model's strengths and weaknesses to strengthen the paper's contributions.
