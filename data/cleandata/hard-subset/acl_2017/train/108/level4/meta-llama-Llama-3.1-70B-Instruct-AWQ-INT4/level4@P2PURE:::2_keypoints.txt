- Strength: Tackles the intriguing problem of overlapping entities and the multigraph technique appears effective.
- Strength: Paper includes a useful discussion on efficiency compared to a previous system.
- Strength: Writing is generally clear, though some details need refinement.

- Weakness: Motivation for detecting nested entities (e.g., "China" in "Bank of China") is weak and use case unclear.
- Weakness: The paper lacks crucial methodological details, notably the decision criterion for including edges in the multigraph.
- Weakness: Description of I^k_t nodes (lines 229–233) is ambiguous about which edges should be present.
- Weakness: Empirical improvements are marginal and the effect size is not clearly demonstrated.
- Weakness: A ~2 point F1 improvement may not be substantial; statistical/significance details are missing.
- Weakness: No comparison to simple baselines (dictionary-based) or commercial systems (e.g., Google NLP) to contextualize performance.
- Weakness: Evaluation lacks focus on crossing entities; should report how many more crossing entities are detected, which are missed, and why.
- Weakness: Missing general error analysis comparing types of errors between the proposed and prior systems.

- General: Paper fails to motivate the importance of nested entities and does not provide evaluation insight on this aspect.
- General: Reviewer remains lukewarm and slightly inclined to reject due to insufficient emphasis on crossing-entity questions.
