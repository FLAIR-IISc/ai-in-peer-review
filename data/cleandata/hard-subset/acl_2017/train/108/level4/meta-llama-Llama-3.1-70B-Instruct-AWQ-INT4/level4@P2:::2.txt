This paper proposes a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The problem of overlapping entities is indeed interesting, especially for crossing entities, where the model needs to decide which entity is actually mentioned in the text. The technique seems to work, although the empirical results do not show a dramatic effect.

The paper is well-written, but some details need further polishing. The problem itself is not well-motivated, and it is unclear why detecting China as an entity within the entity Bank of China is important. The use case for nested entities is also unclear. 

The approach itself lacks some important details. For instance, the decision criterion for including an edge or not is unclear. In lines 229-233, several options for the I^k_t nodes are mentioned, but it is never clarified which edges should be present.

The empirical evaluation shows better results than some previous approaches, but not by a large margin. The slight improvements are not convincingly presented as "outperforming" previous methods. The effect size is unclear, and it is uncertain whether the improvement of two percentage points in F1 really matters to users. The actual effect of the improvement is also unclear, and it is unknown how many "important" entities are discovered that were not discovered by previous methods.

The paper lacks a comparison with simplistic dictionary-based methods or commercial systems like Google's NLP cloud, which could also detect overlapping entities. The discussion of results focuses on overall performance but lacks emphasis on actual crossing entities. The performance on crossing entities is more interesting than nested entities, and it is unclear how many more crossing entities are detected than were possible before.

The paper could benefit from a more detailed error discussion comparing errors made by the suggested system and previous ones. A general discussion on the importance of recognizing crossing entities would strengthen the paper.

Minor remarks include the need for a short description of the notion of a multigraph, clarification of some sentences, and consistency in formatting and punctuation.

**Summary of the Paper:**
The paper proposes a multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text. The model uses multiple edges between nodes to represent different roles that a token can take, allowing for the detection of overlapping entities. The paper presents an empirical evaluation of the model on several datasets, showing improved results compared to previous approaches. However, the improvement is not dramatic, and the paper lacks a clear motivation for the problem and a detailed discussion of the results.

**Main Contributions:**
1. A novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text.
2. An empirical evaluation of the model on several datasets, showing improved results compared to previous approaches.
3. A discussion of the importance of recognizing crossing entities and the potential applications of the model.
