- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model to recognize overlapping entities in text.  
- Addresses an interesting problem, especially for crossing entities where mention selection is ambiguous.  
- Empirical results show improvements over prior methods but gains are small and not dramatic.  
- Paper is generally well-written but requires polishing and clarification of several details.  
- Motivation is weak; unclear why nested entities (e.g., "China" inside "Bank of China") are important.  
- Key model details are missing: decision criterion for including edges is unclear and I^k_t node options aren’t specified.  
- Unclear which edges should be present in the multigraph representation.  
- Improvement magnitude (e.g., ~2 F1 points) lacks context on practical significance or effect size.  
- Lacks comparison to simple dictionary-based baselines or commercial systems (e.g., Google NLP).  
- Evaluation focuses on overall scores and neglects targeted analysis of crossing-entity performance.  
- No detailed error analysis comparing this system’s errors versus prior approaches.  
- Paper would benefit from general discussion on the importance and use cases for crossing-entity recognition.  
- Minor fixes needed: brief definition of multigraph, sentence clarifications, and consistent formatting/punctuation.
