The paper proposes a multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text, a problem that has been largely ignored in previous work. The authors demonstrate that their model outperforms previous models on standard datasets, and provide an analysis of the differences between their model and previous models.

**Summary of the Paper**

The paper addresses the problem of overlapping entity recognition, where entities in a text may overlap with one another. The authors propose a multigraph-based model that can handle this problem, and demonstrate its effectiveness on standard datasets. The model is compared to previous models, including a mention hypergraph model, and is shown to outperform them.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. The authors propose a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text.
2. The model is demonstrated to outperform previous models on standard datasets.
3. The authors provide an analysis of the differences between their model and previous models, including a discussion of the limitations of previous models.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. The problem of overlapping entity recognition is an important one, and the authors provide a clear motivation for why it is necessary to address this problem.
2. The multigraph-based model proposed by the authors is a novel and effective solution to this problem.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the differences between their model and previous models, which helps to clarify the strengths and limitations of each approach.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. The authors could provide more motivation for why recognizing overlapping entities is important. While they provide some examples of why this is necessary, they could elaborate on this point further.
2. Some details of the model are not clearly explained, such as the decision criterion for including an edge in the multigraph.
3. The empirical evaluation could be more thorough, including a comparison to commercial systems and a discussion of the effect size of the improvements achieved by the authors' model.
