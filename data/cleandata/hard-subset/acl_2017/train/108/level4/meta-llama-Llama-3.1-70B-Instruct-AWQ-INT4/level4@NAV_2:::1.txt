The paper "A Multigraph-based Model for Overlapping Entity Recognition" proposes a novel approach to recognizing overlapping entities in text using a multigraph-based model. The authors claim that their model outperforms previous state-of-the-art models, including the mention hypergraph model, on several benchmark datasets.

**Summary of the paper and main contributions**

The paper introduces a multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition, which represents entities as a combination of multiple edges in a graph. The model is capable of handling overlapping entities by assigning explicit semantics to different edges connecting the same pair of nodes. The authors claim that their model achieves higher F1-scores compared to previous models on multiple datasets and provides a more efficient and effective way of recognizing overlapping entities.

The main contributions of the paper are:

1. A novel multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition.
2. Empirical results showing that the model outperforms previous state-of-the-art models on several benchmark datasets.
3. A theoretical analysis of the model's ability to handle overlapping entities and its advantages over previous models.

**Strengths**

The paper is well-written, and the problem it tackles is useful. The proposed approach is a variant of the mention hypergraph model, and the empirical results are solid. The authors provide a clear explanation of their model and its advantages over previous models.

**Weaknesses**

Several places in the paper require clarification:

1. In Section 3, the authors need to point out the issues with the mention hypergraph model that motivate the proposal of a new model.
2. In Section 4, the introduction of separators is not clearly explained, and it is not clear what additional information they convey beyond T/I/O.
3. Section 5.1 does not provide useful information regarding why the new model is superior.
4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is too abstract, and it is not clear why the new model is better than the mention hypergraph model. Examples of spurious structures would be helpful.

**Questions to authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the issues with the mention hypergraph model that motivated the proposal of a new model?
2. How do separators convey additional information beyond T/I/O, and why are they necessary?
3. Can you provide more concrete examples to illustrate the advantages of the new model over previous models?
4. Can you provide more details on the theoretical analysis of the model's ability to handle overlapping entities and its advantages over previous models?

**Additional comments**

The paper presents a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text and shows that it improves the previous state-of-the-art model in the experiments on a few benchmark datasets. However, it is not clear why and how the new model works better. The authors need to provide more clarification and examples to support their claims.
