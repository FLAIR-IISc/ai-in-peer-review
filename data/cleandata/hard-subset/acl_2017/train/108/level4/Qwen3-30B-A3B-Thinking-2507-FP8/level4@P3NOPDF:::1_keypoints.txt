- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with minor grammar issues and a clear problem statement.
- Strength: Robust empirical results showing improvements over the prior MH model on standard overlapping-entity benchmarks.
- Strength: Proposed multigraph approach is a meaningful extension of MH and experimentally validated thoroughly.
- Weakness: Section 3 fails to state the specific limitations of MH that motivate the new multigraph model.
- Weakness: Section 4 does not justify the need for separators or explain what unique information they convey beyond T/I/O labels.
- Weakness: Section 5.1 reports performance gains but lacks interpretable analysis explaining why the multigraph outperforms MH.
- Weakness: Section 5.2 is abstract and provides no concrete examples or evidence illustrating how the multigraph avoids MH’s pitfalls.
- Core critique: The paper does not connect model design choices (e.g., separators, multigraph edges) to measurable improvements, leaving the rationale opaque.
- Required fix: Explicitly state MH’s shortcomings in Section 3.
- Required fix: Define the unique purpose and functional contribution of separators in Section 4.
- Required fix: Add a subsection with concrete examples of spurious structures MH produces and show how the multigraph resolves them.
- Required fix: Replace abstract superiority claims in Section 5.2 with specific, interpretable explanations tied to examples.
- Overall: Contribution is valuable and results promising, but the paper needs clearer, evidence-based justification for why the model works better to be a principled advance.
