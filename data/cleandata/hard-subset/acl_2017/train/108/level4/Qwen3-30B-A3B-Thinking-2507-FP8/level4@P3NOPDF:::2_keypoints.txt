- Strength: Timely focus on overlapping entities with a novel multigraph structural representation.  
- Strength: Efficiency claim (linear in sentence length) is a valuable advantage over prior work.  
- Strength: Writing is generally clear, aside from minor grammatical and phrasing issues.  

- Major weakness: Insufficient motivation—unclear practical value of nested entities versus genuinely challenging crossing entities; evaluation does not distinguish them.  
- Major weakness: Methodology ambiguous—key design choices (which I^k_t nodes/edges are included) are unspecified, harming reproducibility.  
- Major weakness: Underwhelming empirical gains (e.g., +0.4 F1), so "outperforming" prior work is overstated and of questionable practical significance.  
- Major weakness: Missing comparisons to simple baselines and commercial systems (e.g., dictionary methods, Google Cloud NLP) to contextualize results.  
- Major weakness: Evaluation lacks focused analysis on crossing entities—the paper fails to report how many more crossing entities are detected or detailed error analysis.  
- Major weakness: Claims of improved nested-entity detection are not isolated from crossing-entity performance; improvements may come solely from nested cases.  

- Recommendation: Paper is a marginal incremental advance; recommend rejection without substantial revisions.  
- Suggested fixes: Focus evaluation on crossing entities, add baseline/commercial comparisons, justify or drop nested-entity emphasis, specify edge-inclusion criteria, and include detailed error analysis.  

- Minor remark: Define "multigraph" at first use.  
- Minor remark: Fix typos and wording (e.g., "pervious"→"previous", "BILOU"→"BILOU scheme").  
- Minor remark: Clarify ambiguous terms ("state" in hypergraphs, "child nodes", "CS-edge").  
- Minor remark: Make Figure 2 interpretable in grayscale and justify highlighted edges algorithmically.  
- Minor remark: Improve phrasing (e.g., "we make two"→"we conduct two") and footnote placement.  
- Minor remark: Avoid claiming "outperform" for tiny F1 differences; report effect sizes and standardize reference title capitalization.  
- Overall: Interesting and technically sound idea but under-specified, insufficiently motivated, and empirically weak; not ready for acceptance.
