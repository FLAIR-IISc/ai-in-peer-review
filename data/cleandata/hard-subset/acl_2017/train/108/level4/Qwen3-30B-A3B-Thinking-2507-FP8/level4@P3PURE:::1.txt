

Strengths: The paper is well-written with only minor grammatical issues requiring attention. The problem addressed is significant, and the proposed multigraph-based model represents a meaningful variant of the MH approach. The empirical results are robust and well-supported.

Weaknesses: Several points require clarification to strengthen the contribution:
1. Section 3 must explicitly identify the limitations of the MH model that motivate the proposed approach, beyond merely describing MH.
2. Section 4 lacks justification for introducing separators; it is unclear what additional information they convey beyond the T/I/O components.
3. Section 5.1 fails to provide meaningful insights into why the new model outperforms MH.
4. Section 5.2 presents an overly abstract discussion that does not convey clear reasons for the model's superiority over MH. Please provide concrete examples of spurious structures that the new model resolves.

General Discussion: The paper introduces a novel model for detecting overlapping entities in text, demonstrating improvements over the previous state-of-the-art (MH) on benchmark datasets. However, the review lacks sufficient explanation for both the mechanism and the underlying reasons why the new model achieves better performance.
