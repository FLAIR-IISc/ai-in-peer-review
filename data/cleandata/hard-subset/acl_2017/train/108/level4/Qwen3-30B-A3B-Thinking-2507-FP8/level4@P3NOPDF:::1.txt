

The paper presents a multigraph-based model for overlapping entity detection, addressing a relevant problem in NLP. Strengths include a well-written presentation (with minor grammatical exceptions), a clear problem statement, and robust empirical results demonstrating improvements over the prior MH model on standard benchmarks. The proposed approach constitutes a meaningful extension of MH, and the experimental validation is thorough.

However, several critical clarifications are required to strengthen the paper’s contribution and justification:

1.  **Motivation for the New Model (Section 3):** The paper describes the MH model and the proposed multigraph approach but fails to explicitly articulate the *specific limitations* of MH that necessitate the new model. A clear, concise statement of MH’s shortcomings (e.g., its inability to handle certain structural ambiguities or overlapping patterns) is essential to justify the proposed method. Without this, the novelty of the multigraph model remains underdeveloped.

2.  **Role of Separators (Section 4):** The introduction of separators in the model architecture lacks justification. The paper does not explain *why* separators are necessary beyond the basic token/intermediate/output (T/I/O) structure. What specific information do separators convey that T/I/O alone cannot? Clarifying their unique functional contribution is crucial for understanding the model’s design.

3.  **Insight from Results (Section 5.1):** The results presented in Section 5.1 demonstrate performance gains but fail to provide *interpretable insights* into *why* the multigraph model outperforms MH. The section reports metrics without linking them to specific model behaviors or structural advantages. For instance, it does not show *which types of overlapping entities* the new model handles better, or *how* its multigraph structure resolves ambiguities that MH misses.

4.  **Concrete Justification for Superiority (Section 5.2):** The discussion in Section 5.2 remains abstract, stating the model is better without providing concrete evidence or examples. The authors must illustrate *how* the multigraph model avoids the pitfalls of MH. Specifically, they should provide **examples of spurious structures** that MH produces (e.g., incorrectly splitting a single entity like "John Smith" into two entities due to overlapping spans, or failing to merge related mentions like "Apple Inc." and "Apple" within a sentence) and demonstrate how the multigraph model correctly resolves these cases. Without such examples, the claimed superiority lacks tangible support.

The core weakness lies in the paper’s failure to explicitly connect the model’s *design choices* to *measurable improvements*. While the empirical results show the model works better, the paper does not sufficiently explain *how* or *why* the multigraph structure leads to these gains. The proposed approach is presented as a technical modification, but the *reasoning* behind its effectiveness remains opaque. The authors must provide a clear, evidence-based narrative explaining the causal relationship between the model’s architecture (e.g., separators, multigraph edges) and its performance advantages over MH.

To address these concerns, the authors should:
*   Explicitly state MH’s limitations in Section 3.
*   Define the *unique purpose* of separators in Section 4.
*   Add a subsection in Section 5.1 (or 5.2) with **concrete examples** of spurious structures resolved by the new model, contrasting them with MH’s failures.
*   Replace abstract claims in Section 5.2 with specific, interpretable explanations tied to the examples.

The paper’s core contribution—improving overlapping entity detection—is valuable, and the results are promising. However, without a clear, justified explanation of *why* the proposed model works better, its significance is diminished. The current draft reads as a technical description of a model that *happens* to perform well, rather than a principled advancement. Addressing the above points would transform the paper from a strong empirical study into a compelling theoretical and methodological contribution. The authors are encouraged to revise the manuscript to explicitly articulate the model’s rationale and demonstrate its advantages through concrete evidence.
