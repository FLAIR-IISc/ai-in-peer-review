- Proposes a multigraph-based structured prediction model for overlapping entity recognition, claiming novelty over previous graph-based methods.  
- Claims three contributions: multigraph model, empirical superiority on standard datasets, and theoretical proof avoiding MH's "spurious structures" while keeping O(nT) inference.

- Empirical results are strong and consistent: outperforms MH baseline on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA, with up to +2.1 F1 on ACE2004.  
- Theoretical analysis of "spurious structures" plausibly explains performance gains and shows the model retains MH's inference complexity.  
- Model also performs well on non-overlapping entity recognition (CoNLL2003), indicating broader applicability.

- Paper does not clearly state which specific limitations of the MH model motivated the new approach, weakening motivation.  
- Introduction of "entity separators" is insufficiently justified—unclear what extra information they add beyond T/I/O states.  
- Discussion of state-based vs. edge-based paradigms (Section 5.1) fails to clearly argue why the proposed model is superior or novel.  
- The "spurious structures" discussion (Section 5.2) is abstract and needs concrete examples demonstrating the problem and the model's avoidance.

- Requests concrete examples of spurious structures in MH and demonstrations of how the new model avoids them.  
- Asks for clarification on the exact information entity separators encode that T/I/O cannot, and why that is necessary.  
- Wants a precise comparison of representations between the new model and MH, and explicit limitations of MH addressed by the new approach.

- Overall: interesting and empirically solid work, but weakened by unclear motivation, insufficient design justification, and lack of concrete theoretical examples; clearer articulation and examples would strengthen the paper.
