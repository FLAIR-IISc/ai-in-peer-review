- Interesting problem: detecting overlapping entities (especially crossing entities) is potentially valuable for resolving which entities are referenced in text.  
- Proposed technique appears functional but yields only modest empirical gains.  
- Paper appropriately addresses efficiency compared to prior work.  
- Writing is generally clear but needs minor polishing.

- Insufficient motivation for nested-entity detection (e.g., why detect "China" inside "Bank of China").  
- Critical methodological detail missing: decision criterion for edge inclusion is undefined (Lines 229–233 list options but do not specify chosen edges).  
- Empirical improvements are marginal (≈2-point F1); effect size and practical significance are unclear.  
- Missing comparisons to simple dictionary baselines and commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud NLP) to contextualize results.  
- Paper overemphasizes nested entities; should focus on crossing entities, which are more interesting.  
- Key evaluation questions unanswered: how many additional crossing entities are detected, which specific entities were missed previously, and are gains due to nested or crossing detection?  
- Lacks comparative error analysis against prior systems to explain where improvements come from.  
- Reviewers lean toward rejection due to insufficient emphasis/analysis of crossing entities and limited impact.

- General recommendation: provide stronger motivation for nested entities, deeper analysis of crossing-entity behavior (including error patterns), and clearer positioning to avoid appearing a routine incremental contribution.
