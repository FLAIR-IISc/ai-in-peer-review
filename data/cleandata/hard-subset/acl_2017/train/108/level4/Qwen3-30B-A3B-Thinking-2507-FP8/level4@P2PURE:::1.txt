

- Strengths: The paper is well-written, with only minor issues requiring attention. The problem addressed is valuable. The proposed multigraph-based model constitutes a variant of MH, and the empirical results are robust.
- Weaknesses: Several points require clarification:
  1. Section 3 must explicitly identify the limitations of the prior MH model that necessitate the proposed new model.
  2. Section 4 fails to justify the introduction of separators; it is unclear what additional information they provide beyond T/I/O.
  3. Section 5.1 fails to provide meaningful insights into why the new model outperforms MH.
  4. Section 5.2's discussion is overly abstract and obscures the key insights into why the new model surpasses MH; concrete examples of spurious structures should be provided.
- General Discussion: The paper introduces a novel model for detecting overlapping entities in text, demonstrating improved performance over the prior state-of-the-art (MH) on several benchmark datasets. However, it remains unclear why and how the new model achieves better results.
