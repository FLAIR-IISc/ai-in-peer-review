- Paper proposes a multigraph-based model for overlapping entity recognition and claims three contributions: novel model, higher F1 across datasets, and theoretical analysis avoiding spurious structures with linear-time inference.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical analysis showing the model resolves spurious-structure issues in mention hypergraphs, with valuable discussion of normalization Zw(x) and deficiency.  
- Strength: Empirical efficiency claim that model needs far fewer training iterations than mention hypergraph model, though each iteration is ~12% slower.  
- Strength: Model can handle both nested and crossing entities, an advantage over tree-based nested-only methods.  
- Weakness: Weak motivation for nested entities; example (“China” in “Bank of China”) doesn’t show clear practical value over more common crossing-entity cases.  
- Weakness: Empirical gains are marginal (e.g., +2.1 F1 on ACE2004, +0.4 on GENIA) and not framed to show practical significance; comparison to simple baselines or commercial systems is missing.  
- Weakness: Paper emphasizes nested entities but under-addresses crossing entities; performs worse on GENIA’s overlapping cases and lacks error analysis for crossing-entity failures.  
- Question: Please clarify practical use cases where nested-entity recognition is more valuable than crossing-entity recognition.  
- Question: How exactly does the model handle crossing entities, and why does performance drop on GENIA compared to ACE—dataset effect or model limitation?  
- Question: Provide comparative error analysis versus mention hypergraph models, especially for crossing-entity error types your model fixes or still makes.  
- Comment: Define “spurious structures” early and explain “multigraph” for non-specialist readers.  
- Comment: Clarify or consistently define “non-deficient” (non-standard term) and temper claims of empirical outperformance to reflect modest gains.  
- Overall: Technically solid and resolves a key theoretical issue, but weak motivation for nested entities, insufficient practical framing of marginal gains, and inadequate analysis of crossing entities—major revisions recommended.
