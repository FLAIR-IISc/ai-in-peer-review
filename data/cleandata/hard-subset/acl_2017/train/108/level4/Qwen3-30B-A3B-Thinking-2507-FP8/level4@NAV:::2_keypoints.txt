- Problem is interesting, especially for identifying crossing entities mentioned in text.  
- Proposed technique is functional but empirical gains are limited.  
- Discussion of efficiency relative to prior systems is useful.  
- Paper is well-written but needs minor refinements.

- Paper lacks strong motivation for detecting nested entities (e.g., "China" in "Bank of China").  
- Use case for nested entities is unclear and needs stronger justification.

- Edge inclusion criterion is undefined (lines 229–233 list options for I^k_t nodes but do not specify which edges are present).  
- Missing justification for design choices (e.g., why some edges are omitted — footnote 2).

- Empirical improvements are marginal (≈2-point F1) and not shown to be practically significant.  
- Paper does not report effect size, statistical significance, or number of newly discovered important entities.

- No dictionary-based baseline or comparison to commercial systems (e.g., Google NLP Cloud) to contextualize gains.  
- Evaluation fails to emphasize crossing entities, the most compelling subset of overlaps.

- Missing evaluation details: detection rates for crossing vs nested entities, examples of missed cases and reasons, and whether gains stem from nested or crossing handling.  
- A comparative error analysis is needed to diagnose strengths and failures.

- Overall contribution feels incremental and the paper inadequately addresses the central question of crossing entities; recommendation leans toward rejection.
