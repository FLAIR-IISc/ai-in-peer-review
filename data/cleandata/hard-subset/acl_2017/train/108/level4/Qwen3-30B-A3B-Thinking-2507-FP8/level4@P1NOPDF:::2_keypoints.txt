- Paper proposes a multigraph-based method for detecting overlapping entities (multiple edges between nodes).  
- Strength: Tackles the interesting problem of overlapping entities, particularly crossing entities, with reasonable effectiveness and attention to efficiency.  
- Strength: Writing is generally clear, though some minor refinements are needed.  
- Weakness: Motivation for nested-entity detection is unconvincing; practical utility of detecting nested entities (e.g., "China" inside "Bank of China") is unclear.  
- Weakness: Critical methodological detail missing: the criterion for including edges (I^k_t selection) is unspecified, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Empirical gains are marginal (e.g., +0.4 F1 on GENIA) and the paper overstates “outperformance.”  
- Weakness: No assessment of effect size or real-world impact—how many newly discovered important entities does the method find?  
- Weakness: Missing baseline comparisons to simple dictionary-based methods and to commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud NLP).  
- Weakness: Evaluation omits focused analysis on crossing entities; unclear whether improvements come from nested or crossing cases.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparative error analysis showing which crossing/nested entities are detected or missed and why.  
- General: Paper is technically sound but under-motivated; stronger focus on crossing-entity performance and concrete error cases needed.  
- Verdict: Reviewer leans toward rejection due to weak motivation and lack of meaningful gains.  
- Minor editorial: Define "multigraph" at first mention and clarify whether “n” (linearity) equals sentence length.  
- Minor editorial: Numerous wording, grammar, and phrasing fixes suggested (e.g., word choices, punctuation, bracket/footnote placement).  
- Minor editorial: Figures and examples need clarification (Figure 2 grayscale/color, why both shorter and longer entities are detected).  
- Minor editorial: Clarify ambiguous terminology (e.g., "state" in hypergraphs, "spurious structures," "child nodes") and algorithmic selections (CS-edge).  
- Minor editorial: Standardize citations and reference capitalization; fix typos and inconsistent bracket/footnote usage.
