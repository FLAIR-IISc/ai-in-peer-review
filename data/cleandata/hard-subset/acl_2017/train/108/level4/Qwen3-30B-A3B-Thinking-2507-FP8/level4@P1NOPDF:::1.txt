

Strengths: The paper is well-written overall, with only minor areas requiring clarification. The problem addressed is clearly valuable, and the proposed multigraph-based model represents a meaningful extension of the prior MH approach. The empirical results are robust and well-supported.

Weaknesses: Several key clarifications are needed to strengthen the contribution and justification:

1.  **Section 3 (Model Motivation):** The paper describes the existing MH model but fails to explicitly state the specific limitations or shortcomings of MH that necessitate the proposed multigraph model. The motivation for developing a new approach is insufficiently articulated. Please clearly identify the core issues with MH that your model resolves.

2.  **Section 4 (Separator Purpose):** The introduction of separators within the model is not adequately justified. It is unclear what *additional information* these separators convey beyond the standard token (T), intermediate (I), and output (O) components. Please explicitly state the distinct role or information gain provided by the separators.

3.  **Section 5.1 (Results Analysis):** The analysis of results in Section 5.1 does not provide meaningful insights into *why* the proposed model outperforms MH. The discussion lacks concrete evidence or reasoning linking the model's structure to the observed performance gains. This section fails to establish the superiority of the new model beyond merely reporting numbers.

4.  **Section 5.2 (Model Superiority):** The discussion in Section 5.2 remains overly abstract. The paper claims the model avoids "spurious structures" but provides no concrete examples or clear explanation of *what these structures are* or *how the new model prevents them*. To support the claim of improved performance, please provide specific, illustrative examples of spurious structures present in MH's output that the multigraph model successfully avoids, and explain *how* the model's design addresses them.

General Discussion: The paper presents a new model for overlapping entity detection, demonstrating improvements over the previous state-of-the-art (MH) on several benchmark datasets. However, the core contribution's *mechanism* and *reason for success* remain unclear. While the empirical results are solid, the paper does not sufficiently explain *how* and *why* the multigraph approach leads to better performance compared to MH. The lack of concrete analysis in Sections 5.1 and 5.2, coupled with the insufficient motivation in Section 3, prevents a clear understanding of the model's novel advantages. The work shows promise, but a stronger justification for the model's design and a clearer explanation of its operational benefits are essential for a compelling contribution.
