

Strengths: The paper is well-written with minor issues as noted below. The problem addressed is meaningful. The proposed multigraph-based model constitutes a variant of MH, and the empirical validation is robust.  
Weaknesses: Key clarifications are required:  
1. Section 3 must explicitly state the limitations of MH that necessitate the new model.  
2. Section 4 requires justification for introducing separators—what additional information do they convey beyond T/I/O?  
3. Section 5.1 fails to demonstrate the new model’s superiority over MH.  
4. Section 5.2’s discussion is overly abstract; concrete examples of spurious structures are needed to clarify why the new model outperforms MH.  
General Discussion: While the paper introduces a novel multigraph model for overlapping entity detection and shows improved results over MH on benchmark datasets, the mechanistic rationale for its superiority remains unclear. The experimental gains lack explanatory depth regarding how and why the model performs better.
