- Proposes a multigraph-based method for overlapping entity detection; crossing-entity handling is compelling but nested-entity motivation is underdeveloped.  
- Reported gains are marginal (e.g., +0.4 F1 on GENIA), so the claim of “outperformance” is overstated.  
- Evaluation lacks comparisons to simple baselines and commercial systems (e.g., dictionary-based methods, Google Cloud NLP), making significance unclear.  
- No effect-size analysis or error analysis to judge whether small F1 gains are meaningful to users.  
- Crossing-entity performance is underreported—missing quantitative counts of additional/fewer crossing entities detected and qualitative error examples.  
- Key methodological details are missing: criteria for adding edges to the multigraph (lines 229–233) and rules for including specific I^k_t nodes are unclear.  
- The paper does not define what constitutes a valid multigraph structure, weakening the technical contribution.  
- Strengths: clear, well-structured presentation and a useful discussion of efficiency.  
- The paper emphasizes nested entities without showing practical use cases or justifying why nested detection is prioritized over crossing entities.  
- Recommend refocusing on crossing-entity benefits and providing concrete comparisons (e.g., “detects X additional crossing entities compared to prior work”).  
- Minor fixes needed: define “multigraph” on first mention, correct typos (pervious→previous, BILOU), standardize terminology (state vs. node), fix punctuation, redundant phrasing, citation style, and reference capitalization.  
- Overall verdict: meaningful problem but insufficient motivation, technical transparency, and rigorous evaluation; contribution appears incremental—recommend rejection.
