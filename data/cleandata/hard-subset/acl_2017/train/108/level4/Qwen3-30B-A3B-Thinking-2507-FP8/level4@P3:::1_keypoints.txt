- Summary: Proposes a novel multigraph-based structured prediction model for overlapping entity recognition, claiming empirical gains and theoretical avoidance of spurious structures with no extra inference cost.

- Strength 1: Comprehensive empirical evaluation on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA showing consistent improvements over the mention hypergraph (MH) baseline, with detailed analysis by sentence overlap.

- Strength 2: Strong theoretical analysis demonstrating the model avoids spurious structures present in MH, offering a non-trivial explanation for potential practical gains.

- Strength 3: Well-written paper with clear model descriptions and helpful figures (e.g., Figure 2) that illustrate overlapping-entity representation.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient motivation: paper does not clearly state the specific limitations of MH that the new model targets or why spurious structures harm performance.

- Weakness 2: Entity separators are introduced without justification—no explanation of what extra information the 8 separators provide beyond T/I/O states.

- Weakness 3: Theoretical analysis is abstract and lacks concrete examples of spurious MH structures and how the multigraph model resolves them.

- Weakness 4: Weak linkage between theory and results: the paper doesn’t convincingly connect avoidance of spurious structures to the observed empirical improvements.

- Question 1: Please provide a concrete example of a spurious MH structure that your model avoids and its impact on entity recognition.

- Question 2: Why are entity separators necessary, what information do they add beyond T/I/O, and why not model them as separate states?

- Question 3: Why does your model perform slightly worse than MH on overlapping entities in GENIA, and how might this be addressed given GENIA’s lower overlap rate?

- Additional comment: A clearer, direct comparison of MH’s limitations versus the proposed model’s advantages (and clearer discussion of edge- vs state-based paradigms) would strengthen the paper.
