

Strengths:  
The problem of detecting potentially overlapping entities is potentially interesting, particularly for resolving crossing entities to determine which are referenced in text. The proposed technique appears functional, though empirical results lack dramatic impact. The paper appropriately addresses efficiency relative to prior work. Overall, the writing is clear but requires minor polishing (see specific comments below).

Weaknesses:  
The problem lacks sufficient motivation. Why is detecting "China" as a nested entity within "Bank of China" meaningful? While crossing entities have clear utility, the use case for nested entities remains unconvincing and requires stronger justification. Regarding the approach, critical details are missing: the decision criterion for edge inclusion is undefined. Lines 229–233 enumerate options for $I^k_t$ nodes but never clarify which edges should be present.  

Empirical results show marginal improvements over prior methods (e.g., a 2-point F1 gain), yet the paper claims "outperformance." The effect size is unclear: does a 2-point F1 improvement matter to end users? How many *important* entities were newly discovered? Crucially, the paper omits comparisons with simplistic dictionary-based methods or commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud NLP), which would contextualize the results against existing solutions.  

The discussion should emphasize crossing entities (the more interesting subset of overlapping entities), not nested ones. Key questions remain unanswered: How many more crossing entities are detected? Which were missed and why? Is performance gain due to improved nested detection, crossing detection, or both? A comparative error analysis against prior systems would strengthen this section.  

General Discussion:  
While crossing entities are compelling, the paper fails to justify nested entity detection. A clearer motivation, combined with deeper analysis of crossing entities (including error patterns and comparative results), would have been more persuasive. The paper reads as a routine incremental contribution without adequately addressing the core question of crossing entities. I am moderately positive but lean toward rejection due to insufficient emphasis on the most significant aspect of the problem.
