- Proposes a multigraph-based model that theoretically resolves spurious structures in prior mention-hypergraph models while maintaining identical inference time complexity.

- Empirically validates that the model achieves higher F1 scores than prior approaches across multiple datasets.

- Introduces a novel multigraph representation that gives explicit semantics to different edges between the same node pair.

- Theoretical analysis shows the model is non-deficient (no spurious structures) without increasing inference complexity, addressing a key limitation of prior work.

- Empirical results consistently improve over the prior state-of-the-art on ACE2004, ACE2005, GENIA, and CONLL2003, with up to +2.1 F1 on ACE2004.

- Provides a clear, well-structured comparison of state-based vs edge-based paradigms and an insightful discussion on spurious structures.

- Motivation for nested entity recognition is weak, lacking convincing real-world use cases and comparison to the more common crossing-entity problems.

- Reported improvements are marginal (best +2.1 F1) with no analysis of effect size or practical significance (e.g., how many additional important entities are detected).

- Evaluation omits comparisons with commercial systems and lacks a focused analysis of performance on crossing entities, the more challenging subset.
