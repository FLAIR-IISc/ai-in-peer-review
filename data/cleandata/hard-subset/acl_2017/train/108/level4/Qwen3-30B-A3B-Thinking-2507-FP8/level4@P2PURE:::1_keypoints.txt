- Well-written paper with minor issues.
- Addresses a valuable problem.
- Proposes a multigraph-based model that is a variant of MH.
- Empirical results are robust.

- Section 3 must explicitly state limitations of the prior MH model that motivate the new model.
- Section 4 does not justify introducing separators or explain what extra information they add beyond T/I/O.
- Section 5.1 lacks meaningful explanation of why the new model outperforms MH.
- Section 5.2 is overly abstract and should include concrete examples of spurious structures.

- Introduces a novel model that improves over MH on benchmarks, but it remains unclear why and how the improvements arise.
