- Valuable problem: detecting overlapping entities (nested and crossing) is an important research direction.  
- Multigraph approach: representing entities with a multigraph is a plausible model for complex relationships.  
- Writing and efficiency: paper is generally well-written and addresses efficiency relative to prior work.  

- Weak motivation for nested entities: the paper fails to justify practical value of detecting nested entities (example China in Bank of China is insufficient).  
- Incomplete multigraph construction: criteria for including edges and the final selection of I^k_t nodes/edges (lines 229–233) are not clearly specified.  
- Modest empirical gains: reported improvements are small (e.g., ~2 F1 points) and claims of “outperforming” feel overstated.  
- Missing effect-size discussion: no analysis of practical significance or effect sizes for the reported improvements.  
- No quantification of novel detections: paper does not report how many important previously undetected entities are now found.  
- Missing baseline comparisons: lacks simple baselines (dictionary) and comparisons to commercial NER/linking systems (e.g., Google Cloud NLP).  
- Insufficient focus on crossing entities: paper should report detailed results and examples specifically for crossing-entity detection.  
- Lacks error analysis: needs comparative error analysis against existing methods to explain strengths and failures.  
- Feels incremental: contribution appears incremental rather than fully capitalizing on crossing-entity detection potential.  

- Recommendation: reviewer leans toward rejection pending stronger motivation for nested entities, full multigraph specification, and a deeper empirical evaluation with stronger baselines and analyses.
