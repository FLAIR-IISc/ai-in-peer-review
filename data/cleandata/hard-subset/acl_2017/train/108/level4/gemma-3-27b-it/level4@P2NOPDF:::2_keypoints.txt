- Strength: Tackles valuable problem of overlapping (nested/crossing) entity recognition and shows some effectiveness with an efficiency discussion; overall readable but needs clarification.

- Weakness: Motivation for detecting nested entities is weak—practical use cases beyond crossing entities are not well justified.

- Weakness: Technical details incomplete—criteria for including edges in the multigraph (I^k_t nodes) are not clearly specified.

- Weakness: Empirical gains are modest; effect sizes aren’t reported so practical significance of ~2 pp F1 improvements is unclear.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons to simple baselines (dictionary) and commercial entity-linking systems to contextualize results.

- Weakness: Paper under-emphasizes crossing entities; needs quantitative performance on crossing cases, examples of newly detected entities, and comparative error analysis.

- General assessment: Work feels incremental without a clear impactful contribution; reviewer leans toward rejection.

- Clarity: Define “multigraph” at first mention and clarify terms like “state”, “child nodes”, “spurious structures”, “regarded as a chunk”, and “in the mention hypergraph.”

- Algorithmic detail: Explain algorithmic selection of CS-edges, how separators are determined, and which edges are implemented (lines 229–233); clarify “simple first order assumption.”

- Complexity claim: Clarify what ‘n’ refers to in “linear in n” (e.g., sentence length) and discuss practical significance.

- Evaluation reporting: Report effect sizes, quantify how many important entities the method newly discovers, and qualify “significantly better” claims with effect sizes.

- Baselines & analyses: Add dictionary/commercial baselines, focus analysis on crossing entities, and include an error analysis comparing to prior approaches.

- Figures/tables: Make Figure 2’s hypergraph representation clearer (explain gray nodes/edges, selection criteria), ensure color-independence for print, and justify the value of detecting nested vs. longer entity alone.

- Writing/style fixes: Numerous wording changes and grammar fixes requested (awkward phrases, replace casual verbs, consistent bracket/italic use, spellings like “pervious”→“previous”, and place footnotes after punctuation).

- Footnotes/claims: Justify claims in footnotes (e.g., missing edge in Footnote 2; Footnote 3’s prioritization claim) and smooth constructions where footnotes interrupt flow.

- Consistency & references: Use consistent section references (e.g., “In Section X”), consistent dataset/title phrasing (e.g., “On the GENIA dataset”), consistent citation format (Lu and Roth, 2015), and consistent capitalization in references.

- Experimental details: Clarify BILOU scheme usage, how increased confidence affects recall, convergence comparisons to the mention hypergraph, and correct phrasing for dataset/table captions (e.g., “Dataset statistics are presented in…”).
