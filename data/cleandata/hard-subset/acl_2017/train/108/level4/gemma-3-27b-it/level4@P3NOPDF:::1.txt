## Review for ACL 2017

This paper presents a novel multigraph-based model for detecting overlapping entities in text. The problem addressed is valuable, and the paper is generally well-written, though some sections require clarification. The proposed approach builds upon the existing Mention-based Hierarchical (MH) model and demonstrates solid empirical performance on benchmark datasets, improving upon the current state-of-the-art. However, the reasons *why* this improvement occurs are not fully articulated, hindering a complete understanding of the model’s strengths.

**Strengths:**

The paper is, for the most part, clearly written and easy to follow. The experimental results presented are convincing and demonstrate a tangible improvement over baseline methods. The focus on overlapping entity detection is relevant and contributes to the ongoing research in information extraction.

**Weaknesses:**

Several aspects of the paper require further elaboration to fully appreciate the contribution. 

1. **Motivation (Section 3):** While the paper describes the MH model, it lacks a clear articulation of its limitations which motivate the proposed multigraph approach. A more thorough discussion of the shortcomings of MH – specifically, *why* those limitations exist and *how* they impact performance – is necessary to establish the need for a new model. Detailing these issues will strengthen the rationale for the proposed work.

2. **Separator Introduction (Section 4):** The introduction of separators is not sufficiently justified. The paper needs to clearly explain what additional information these separators contribute beyond the traditional Type, Instance, and Ontology (T/I/O) relationships. What specific linguistic or semantic cues are captured by the separators that are missed by the standard MH representation? A more detailed explanation of their function and benefit is crucial.

3. **Ablation Study (Section 5.1):** Section 5.1, intended to demonstrate the value of the proposed components, currently provides insufficient evidence to support the claim that the new model is superior. The ablation study lacks the depth needed to definitively isolate the contribution of each element. Presenting a more detailed analysis of the results, potentially with statistical significance testing, would strengthen this section.

4. **Qualitative Analysis & Spurious Structures (Section 5.2):** The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract. While the paper suggests that the new model avoids “spurious structures,” it fails to provide concrete examples illustrating these structures in the context of MH and how the multigraph approach mitigates them. Providing illustrative examples of incorrect entity groupings made by MH, and demonstrating how the new model correctly identifies entities in those same cases, would significantly enhance understanding and bolster the claim of improved performance. Specifically, describing the types of spurious structures observed and *why* they arise in MH would be extremely helpful.



**General Discussion and Recommendation:**

This paper presents a promising new model for overlapping entity detection. The empirical results are encouraging, and the model demonstrates an improvement over existing state-of-the-art methods. However, the paper’s impact is diminished by the lack of a clear explanation regarding *why* the proposed approach is more effective. 

To address these concerns, I recommend the authors:

*   Expand Section 3 to explicitly detail the limitations of the MH model.
*   Provide a more compelling justification for the introduction of separators in Section 4.
*   Strengthen the ablation study in Section 5.1 with more detailed analysis and statistical significance.
*   Supplement Section 5.2 with concrete examples illustrating spurious structures and how the multigraph approach avoids them.

Addressing these points will significantly enhance the clarity, rigor, and overall impact of the paper. Currently, I rate this paper as **Borderline Accept / Weak Accept**. Resolving the weaknesses outlined above could easily elevate this paper to a Strong Accept.
