- Proposes a novel multigraph-based structured prediction model for overlapping entity recognition, claiming first use of multigraphs for this task.  
- Represents overlapping roles by assigning explicit semantics to multiple directed edges (entity separators) between the same node pair.  
- Defines eight entity separators (combinations of Start, End, Continue) to encode token-level role transitions.  
- Uses a log-linear model trained with L-BFGS, with inside-outside dynamic programming for inference and max-product decoding for MPE.  
- Maintains linear inference time complexity O(nT), matching the mention hypergraph baseline.  
- The model is non-deficient and avoids the spurious-structures problem that affects the mention hypergraph’s normalization.  
- Frames a conceptual distinction between state-based and edge-based paradigms, categorizing their approach as edge-based.  
- Empirically outperforms prior models on multiple standard datasets, achieving higher F1 scores.  
- Provides theoretical analysis, empirical comparisons, and appendices detailing spurious structures, features, hyperparameters, and preprocessing.  
- Argues the multigraph framework can generalize to other overlapping-structure problems and promises to release code.
