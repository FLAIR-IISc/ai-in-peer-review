- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model for detecting overlapping entities, building on the Mention-based Hierarchical (MH) model.
- Empirical results show solid improvement over baselines and current state-of-the-art on benchmark datasets.
- Paper is generally clearly written and the problem is relevant to information extraction.
- Lacks clear articulation of MH’s limitations to motivate the multigraph approach; need explicit explanation of why MH fails and how that impacts performance.
- Introduction of separators is insufficiently justified; unclear what extra linguistic/semantic information they capture beyond T/I/O relationships.
- Ablation study (Section 5.1) is too shallow to isolate component contributions and lacks statistical significance testing.
- Qualitative analysis (Section 5.2) is overly abstract and fails to provide concrete examples of “spurious structures” produced by MH and corrected by the multigraph model.
- Recommendations: expand Section 3 on MH limitations, justify separators in Section 4, strengthen ablation with detailed analysis/statistics, and add illustrative examples of spurious structures.
- Overall assessment: promising work with convincing results but diminished impact due to lack of explanation for why the approach improves performance; rated Borderline Accept / Weak Accept, could be Strong Accept with revisions.
