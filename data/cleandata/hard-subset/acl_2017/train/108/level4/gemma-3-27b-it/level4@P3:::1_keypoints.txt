- Paper claims three contributions: a novel multigraph model for overlapping entities, improved F1 on several datasets, and a theoretical non-deficiency proof versus mention hypergraphs (MH).
- Reviewer views the main contribution as exploring multigraphs for structured prediction; empirical gains are secondary and the theoretical claim needs clearer justification.

- Strength: Introduces a relatively unexplored multigraph representation for modeling overlapping entity structures.
- Strength: Shows competitive and often superior empirical performance on ACE2004, ACE2005, and GENIA, including overlapping and non-overlapping settings.
- Strength: Provides a comprehensive comparison and positioning relative to the mention hypergraph model.
- Strength: Authors will release code and system, aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness: Rationale for introducing “entity separators” is unclear; the paper doesn’t explain what information separators add beyond T/I/O states.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for why the multigraph approach is fundamentally superior; the state-based vs edge-based distinction is abstract and not tied to performance benefits.
- Weakness: Discussion of “spurious structures” is overly theoretical and lacks concrete examples or intuitive explanation; Appendix figures should be integrated into the main text.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of feature importance and no breakdown of which features contributed most to performance.

- Question: Please clarify the rationale and specific problems addressed by entity separators.
- Question: Provide a concrete sentence/example where the multigraph model succeeds but the MH model fails due to spurious structures.
- Question: What is the computational overhead (training/inference time) of multigraphs versus hypergraphs; provide a detailed comparison.

- Additional comment: Explicitly state MH model limitations in Section 3 to better motivate the approach.
- Additional comment: Add a visual, concrete example in Section 4 showing multigraph representation for an overlapping-entity sentence.
- Additional comment: Clarify the term “non-deficient” in the theoretical analysis.
- Additional comment: Expand the hyperparameter table to explain why chosen values were selected.
