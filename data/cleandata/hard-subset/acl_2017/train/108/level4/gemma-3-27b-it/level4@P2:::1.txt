## Review for ACL 2017 - A Multigraph-based Model for Overlapping Entity Recognition

This paper presents a novel multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities, offering an alternative to existing approaches like hypergraph-based models. The paper is generally well-written and addresses an important problem in named entity recognition. The empirical results demonstrate solid performance gains over existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Novel Multigraph Representation:** The paper introduces a multigraph-based representation for modeling overlapping entities, which allows for explicit semantics to be assigned to edges connecting the same node pair. This offers a distinct approach compared to existing graph-based models.
2. **Empirical Performance:** The proposed model demonstrably achieves higher F1-scores compared to previous state-of-the-art models on standard datasets containing both overlapping and non-overlapping entities.
3. **Theoretical Analysis of Model Deficiency:** The authors identify and theoretically demonstrate that their model avoids the "spurious structures" issue present in the compared hypergraph-based model (Lu & Roth, 2015), while maintaining comparable inference time complexity.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper effectively highlights the importance of addressing overlapping entities in NER, a challenge often overlooked in previous work.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results across multiple datasets (ACE2004, ACE2005, GENIA) consistently show improvements over existing methods, providing convincing evidence of the model’s effectiveness.
3. **Detailed Model Description:** The multigraph-based model is described thoroughly, with clear explanations of the different node types, edge representations (separators), and the inference process. 
4. **Comparative Analysis:** The paper engages in a detailed comparison with the previous state-of-the-art (mention hypergraphs), identifying key differences and theoretically analyzing their implications.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Motivation for Separators is Unclear:** The introduction of “entity separators” (Section 4) lacks sufficient justification. The paper doesn’t clearly articulate *why* these separators are necessary or what specific information they convey beyond traditional BIO/BILOU tagging schemes (T/I/O states). A more intuitive explanation of their role is needed.
2. **Limited Insight from State vs. Edge Paradigm Discussion (Section 5.1):** While the categorization into state-based and edge-based paradigms is interesting, the discussion doesn’t provide substantial insight into *why* the edge-based approach is beneficial. It feels more like a classification exercise than a compelling argument for the chosen design.
3. **Abstract Explanation of Spurious Structures (Section 5.2):** The discussion on spurious structures is highly abstract and difficult to grasp. Providing concrete examples of these structures within a specific sentence, and illustrating how the multigraph model avoids them, would significantly improve clarity.  The current explanation relies heavily on technical details and lacks intuitive understanding.
4. **Missing Ablation Study:** It would be valuable to include an ablation study to assess the contribution of specific components of the model (e.g., the entity separators) to the overall performance. This would help isolate the factors driving the observed improvements.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a concrete example demonstrating the “spurious structures” issue in the mention hypergraph model and explain precisely how your multigraph-based model avoids creating them in the same scenario?
2. Can you elaborate on the intuition behind the entity separators? What specific linguistic information do they capture that isn’t readily available through standard BIO/BILOU tagging?
3. Is there a computational cost associated with the increased number of edges in the multigraph representation compared to the hypergraph representation? If so, how is this addressed?

**Additional Comments:**

*   In section 3, explicitly state the shortcomings of the mention hypergraph model that motivate the development of your approach. This will strengthen the paper’s narrative.
*   Consider providing a visual example illustrating how your model represents a sentence with overlapping entities, highlighting the role of the entity separators. This would significantly aid comprehension.
*   The table of hyperparameter settings (Table 6) is helpful.
