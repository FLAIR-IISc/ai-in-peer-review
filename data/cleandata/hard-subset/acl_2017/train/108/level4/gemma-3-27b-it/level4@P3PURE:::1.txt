This paper presents a well-written contribution, with a few minor areas for improvement as detailed below. The problem addressed – detecting overlapping entities in text – is a valuable one, and the proposed multigraph-based model represents a logical extension of the Markov Hierarchy (MH) approach. The empirical results presented are solid and demonstrate performance improvements.

However, several points require further clarification.

1. In Section 3, while the previous MH model is described, the paper would benefit from explicitly outlining the limitations of MH that motivate the development of the proposed new model. Highlighting these deficiencies will strengthen the rationale for the presented work.

2. In Section 4, the introduction of separators is not sufficiently justified. The paper should clarify what additional information the separators convey beyond the Type, Instance, and Ontology (T/I/O) features already present in the model.

3. Section 5.1 does not provide compelling evidence explaining the superiority of the new model. The analysis presented in this subsection feels insufficient to justify the observed performance gains. 

4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract, and the insights into *why* the new model outperforms MH are unclear. Concrete examples of the “spurious structures” the authors claim to address would significantly improve understanding.

General Discussion: The paper introduces a novel model for detecting overlapping entities, achieving improvements over the state-of-the-art MH model on several benchmark datasets. However, the underlying reasons and mechanisms driving these performance improvements remain unclear. Further elaboration on the benefits of the introduced components (separators and the multigraph structure) and a more detailed analysis of the model’s behavior are needed to fully understand its effectiveness.
