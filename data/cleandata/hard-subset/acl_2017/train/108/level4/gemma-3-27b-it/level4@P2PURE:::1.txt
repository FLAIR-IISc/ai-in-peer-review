The paper is generally well-written, with only minor clarity issues as detailed below. It addresses a valuable problem, and the proposed multigraph-based model represents a worthwhile extension of the existing Markovian Hierarchical (MH) model. The empirical results presented are robust and convincing.

However, several points require further clarification. 

1. Section 3 would benefit from a more explicit discussion of the limitations of MH that motivate the development of the proposed model. Simply describing MH is insufficient; the paper needs to clearly articulate *why* a new approach is necessary.
2. The rationale for introducing separators in Section 4 is unclear. The paper should explain what additional information these separators provide beyond the Type, Inclusion, and Overlap (T/I/O) features.
3. Section 5.1 does not sufficiently justify the observed performance gains of the new model. More detailed analysis is needed to explain *why* it outperforms the baseline.
4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract, and the reasons for the new model’s improvements over MH remain unclear. Providing concrete examples of the spurious structures identified by the model would greatly enhance understanding.

Overall, this paper presents a new model for overlapping entity detection that achieves improvements over the state-of-the-art (MH) on several benchmark datasets. However, the paper lacks a clear and compelling explanation of *how* and *why* the proposed model achieves these gains. Further elaboration on the motivations behind the model's design and a more detailed analysis of its behavior are needed.
