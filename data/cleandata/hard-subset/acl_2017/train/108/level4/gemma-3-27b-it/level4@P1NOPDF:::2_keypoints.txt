- Strength: Addresses an interesting problem of overlapping/crossing entity detection.  
- Strength: Proposed multigraph-based technique appears to work empirically, though gains are modest.  
- Strength: Includes a useful discussion of efficiency compared to a prior system.  
- Strength: Generally well-written but needs polishing in details.

- Weakness: Motivation for nested-entity detection (e.g., detecting “China” inside “Bank of China”) is unclear and not well justified.  
- Weakness: Important methodological details are missing—specifically the decision criterion for including edges and which I^k_t node options are actually used.  
- Weakness: Empirical improvements are small; claims of “outperforming” are overstated without effect size or practical impact analysis.  
- Weakness: No baseline comparisons to simple dictionary-based methods or commercial systems (e.g., Google NLP) to contextualize results.  
- Weakness: Insufficient focus on crossing-entity performance—need results for that subset, counts of additional detected crossing entities, and analysis of missed ones.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether gains come from better nested detection, better crossing detection, or other factors—needs error analysis comparing systems.  
- Recommendation sentiment: Reviewer leans slightly toward rejection due to limited novelty/impact and insufficient emphasis on crossing entities.

- General: Emphasize crossing-entity results and include error analyses with illustrative examples to strengthen the paper’s case.

- Minor: Define “multigraph” at first mention.  
- Minor: Fix awkward phrasing (“previously noted by…many previous”, “Since they set”, etc.) and numerous typos (“pervious” → “previous”).  
- Minor: Clarify referents and tasks (e.g., “Solving this task”, who “they” are).  
- Minor: Remove unnecessary formatting (italic “e.g.”) and correct punctuation/footnote placement.  
- Minor: Clarify technical terms and usage (what is a “state” vs. “node”, “spurious structures”, “simple first order assumption”).  
- Minor: Explain algorithmic choices (how CS-edge is chosen, how missing edges were determined, how separator decisions were made).  
- Minor: Clarify Figure 2 (why hypergraph, meaning of gray nodes/edges, why both entities should be detected) and ensure figures work in B/W.  
- Minor: Quantify claims (“significantly better”, “outperforms by about 0.4 points”) with effect sizes and practical significance.  
- Minor: Report crossing-entity-specific metrics and detail which dataset statistics were used; correct dataset phrasing (e.g., “On the GENIA dataset”).  
- Minor: Consistency issues in references and capitalization, and multiple awkward phrasings to be revised for readability.
