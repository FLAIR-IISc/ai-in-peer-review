- Problem is valuable: overlapping entity recognition (especially crossing entities) and ambiguity resolution matter.  
- Proposed multigraph approach shows improved performance over several prior methods and claims efficiency gains.  
- Paper is generally well-written with a clear method description.  
- Motivation is weak: practical importance of nested entities (vs. crossing entities) is not well established.  
- Focus should be more on crossing entities; current emphasis on nested entities is unclear.  
- Important implementation details are missing, notably criteria for including edges and how I^k_t nodes are selected.  
- Empirical improvements are small and reported without effect sizes, leaving practical significance unclear.  
- No baseline comparisons to simple dictionary approaches or commercial NLP systems to contextualize gains.  
- Results discussion lacks focused analysis on crossing-entity performance and comparative error analysis.  
- Paper feels incremental; limited empirical impact and motivation reduce its contribution (reviewer leans toward rejection).  
- Need more focused error analysis with illustrative examples to strengthen contribution.  
- First mention of “multigraph” should include a brief definition.  
- Numerous unclear or ambiguous phrasings, referents, and formatting/grammar issues across the manuscript.  
- Clarify algorithmic choices (e.g., how CS-edge is chosen, separator decisions) and define technical terms like “state” and “first-order assumption.”  
- Explain figures for monochrome printing, justify highlighted elements, and explain example outcomes (why both entities detected).  
- Address practical significance of claimed time-complexity improvements (linear vs. cubic in sentence length).  
- Report and comment on effect sizes whenever claiming “significant” improvements; qualify small differences in tables.  
- Fix typos, footnote placement, inconsistent citations/phrasing, and ensure consistent capitalization in references.
