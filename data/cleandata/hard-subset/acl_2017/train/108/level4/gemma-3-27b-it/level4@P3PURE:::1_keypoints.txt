- Well-written paper addressing the valuable problem of overlapping entity detection; proposes a logical multigraph extension of the Markov Hierarchy (MH) and reports solid empirical improvements.
- Section 3 should explicitly outline MH’s limitations that motivate the new model to strengthen the rationale.
- Section 4 introduces separators without sufficient justification; clarify what information they add beyond the existing Type/Instance/Ontology (T/I/O) features.
- Section 5.1 lacks compelling analysis to explain the new model’s superiority; provide stronger evidence for observed performance gains.
- Section 5.2 is overly abstract and doesn’t explain why the model outperforms MH; include concrete examples of the claimed “spurious structures.”
- General: although the model improves over MH on benchmarks, the mechanisms behind these gains are unclear—provide deeper analysis of the separators, multigraph structure, and model behavior.
