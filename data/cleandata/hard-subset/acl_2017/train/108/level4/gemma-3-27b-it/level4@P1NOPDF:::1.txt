The paper is generally well-written, though a few areas could benefit from clarification, as detailed below. The problem addressed is valuable, and the proposed multigraph-based model presents a reasonable variant of the Markov Hierarchy (MH) model. The empirical results are solid and demonstrate performance improvements.

However, several points require further explanation.

1. In Section 3, while the previous MH model is described, the paper would be strengthened by explicitly outlining the limitations of MH that motivate the proposed new model. What specific issues with MH does this work aim to address?

2. In Section 4, the rationale for introducing separators is unclear. What additional information do these separators convey beyond the standard Type, Instance, and Operation (T/I/O) tags? The paper needs to justify their inclusion and explain their contribution to the model.

3. Section 5.1 does not convincingly demonstrate *why* the new model performs better. The information presented does not clearly articulate the advantages of the proposed approach.

4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract, making it difficult to understand *how* the new model surpasses MH. Providing concrete examples of “spurious structures” would significantly improve clarity and illustrate the model's benefits. What specific types of incorrect groupings does the new model avoid that MH falls prey to?

General Discussion: This paper introduces a novel model for detecting overlapping entities in text, achieving improvements over the previous state-of-the-art, MH, on several benchmark datasets. However, the underlying reasons for these improvements – specifically *why* and *how* the new model outperforms MH – remain unclear. While the paper demonstrates *that* the new model is better, it falls short of fully explaining *why* it is better, hindering a complete understanding of its contributions.
