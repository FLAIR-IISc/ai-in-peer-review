- Strength: Addresses an interesting problem (crossing and nested entities), with a novel multigraph-based approach that appears to work in practice.
- Strength: Paper is generally well-written and discusses efficiency improvements over prior work.
- Weakness: Motivation for detecting nested entities is weak and practical importance is not clearly justified.
- Weakness: Key methodological details are missing—specifically the criteria for including edges in the multigraph and which I^k_t node options are used.
- Weakness: Empirical gains are modest; the claim of “outperforming” prior work seems overstated without effect-size analysis.
- Weakness: Lacks assessment of practical significance—how many important entities are newly detected and whether small F1 gains matter.
- Weakness: No comparison to simple baselines (e.g., dictionary lookup) or commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud NLP).
- Weakness: Insufficient focus on crossing-entity results; need per-subset performance, counts of newly detected crossing entities, and attribution of improvements (nested vs crossing).
- Weakness: Needs a thorough error analysis comparing types of errors between this system and previous methods, with illustrative examples.
- General: Reviewer is cautiously optimistic but leans toward rejection, viewing the work as incremental without strongly emphasizing crossing-entity benefits.
- Question: Provide a stronger use case for nested-entity detection.
- Question: Specify exact criteria for selecting edges in the multigraph.
- Question: Report effect sizes for observed F1 improvements and their practical impact.
- Question: Provide explicit performance metrics on crossing entities versus prior approaches.
- Question: Include an error analysis comparing error types across systems.
- Question: Report performance of a simple dictionary-based baseline and of commercial entity recognizers on the datasets.
- Comment: Define “multigraph” at first mention for readers unfamiliar with the term.
- Comment: Clarify many ambiguous phrasings and terminology (e.g., “spurious structures,” “state,” “regarded as a chunk,” “first order assumption”).
- Comment: Improve wording and grammar throughout (many suggested rephrasings and typo fixes).
- Comment: Clarify technical choices and algorithmic details (e.g., how CS-edge is chosen, how missing edges are determined).
- Comment: Improve figure clarity (Figure 2 grayscale distinction, highlighted edges, and example justification).
- Comment: Clarify evaluation descriptions and dataset/statistics phrasing (e.g., Table 1, GENIA dataset wording).
- Comment: Explain and justify design/annotation choices (favoring crossing entities, BILOU usage).
- Comment: Report effect sizes and be precise when claiming “significantly better” or “outperforms.”
- Comment: Fix reference and formatting issues (references used as nouns, capitalization consistency, footnote placement).
