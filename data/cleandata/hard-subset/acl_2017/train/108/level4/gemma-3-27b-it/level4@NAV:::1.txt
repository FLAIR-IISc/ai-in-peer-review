## Review:

This paper is generally well-written and addresses a valuable problem. The proposed multigraph-based model represents a logical extension of existing work in Markov homology (MH), and the empirical results presented are robust. However, several areas require further clarification.

Specifically, the paper would benefit from more detailed discussion of its motivations. In Section 3, alongside the description of MH, explicitly outlining the limitations of MH that led to the development of the proposed model would strengthen the rationale. 

Furthermore, the introduction of separators in Section 4 lacks a clear justification. The paper should articulate what novel information these separators provide beyond the traditional Type/Instance/Operation (T/I/O) distinctions.

Regarding the results, Section 5.1 does not convincingly demonstrate the advantages of the new model. Similarly, the discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract; concrete examples of spurious structures identified by the new model – and missed by MH – would significantly improve understanding of its benefits.

Overall, the paper introduces a new model for overlapping entity detection that achieves improved performance over the current state-of-the-art (MH) on standard benchmarks. However, the underlying mechanisms driving these improvements remain unclear, and a more detailed explanation of *why* and *how* the model achieves better results is needed.
