This paper suggests an approach based on multigraphs (multiple edges can connect two nodes) to potentially detect overlapping entities.

**Strengths:**

The problem itself can be quite interesting, especially for crossing entities, to determine which text can actually be referenced. The technique seems to work, although the empirical results do not show some "dramatic" effects. I like that some words are spent on efficiency compared to the previous system. The paper is generally well-written, but some details need some more improvement (see minor comments below).

**Weaknesses:**

The problem itself is not really well-motivated. Why is it important to identify China as an entity within the Bank of China, with the example given in the introduction? I see a point for crossing entities, but what is the use for nested entities? It could be more motivated to create interest for the reader. As for the approach, in my opinion, some important details are missing: what is the criterion for deciding whether to include an edge or not? Lines 229-233 give several different options for I^k_t nodes, but it is never clear which edges should exist!

For empirical evaluation, the results obtained are better than some previous approaches, but not by a very large margin. I would not call the minor improvements "outperform" as the paper does. What is the size of the effect? Does it really matter to some users that there is a slight improvement of a few percentage points in F_1? What is the real effect that one can see? How many "significant" entities have been discovered that were not discovered by previous methods? Also, what performance would some simple dictionary-based method achieve that could also be used to find overlapping things? And in the same direction: what would some commercial system, like Google's NLP cloud, have achieved on datasets that should be able to detect and add entities. Also to put the results in contrast to existing "commercial" systems.

For the results discussion, I would like to emphasize a little more on real crossing entities. What is the performance like there? In my opinion, it is a more interesting subset of overlapping entities than nested entities. How many more crossing entities have been discovered compared to before? Which ones are left out and perhaps why? Is the performance improvement only due to better nested detection or also due to the detection of crossing entities? Some general error discussion that compares the suggested system and the errors made by the previous ones would also strengthen that part.

**General Discussion:**

I like the problems related to named entity recognition and see a point for identifying crossing entities. However, why is anyone interested in nested entities? The current paper does not really motivate the scenario and does not shed any light on that point in the evaluation. Discussing the errors and emphasizing the advantages and results on crossing entities with some example cases might have made me more convinced than other approaches.

Therefore, I am just humming about the paper, perhaps with a slight tendency of rejection. It just seems like another attempt without really emphasizing the important question on crossing entities in my opinion.
