The paper proposes a method based on a multi-graph (where several edges can connect two nodes) to identify potential overlapping entities.

**Strength:**

The problem itself can be quite interesting, especially for crossed entities, to determine which one might be mentioned in a text. The technique seems to work, although empirical results do not show some "dramatic" effects. I like that some word efficiency has been spent compared to previous systems. In general, the paper is well-written, but some detailed information needs further improvement (see small comments below).

**Weaknesses:**

The problem itself is not very well explained. Why is it important to identify China as an entity within the Bank of China, consistent with the example in the role? There is a rationale for crossed entities, but what about the use of nested entities? This could be more motivating for the reader. Speaking of the method, I think some important details are missing: what is the criterion for including an edge? Several different options are mentioned for I^k_t nodes in lines 229-233, but it is never clarified which edges should be present!

In terms of empirical evaluation, the results achieved are better than some previous methods, but not by a very large margin. I would not call the minor improvements "outperforming," as the paper does. What is the size of the effect? Is a slight improvement of two percent points in F_1 really important to some users? What effect can a person actually see? How many "significant" entities have been discovered that were not discovered in previous methods? Furthermore, what performance would some simple dictionary-based methods achieve that could also be used to find overlapping things? And in a similar vein: what could some commercial systems like Google's NLP cloud achieve in datasets that are capable of identifying and connecting entities? Just to compare the results with existing "commercial" systems.

In discussing the results, I would have wanted to see more emphasis on crossed entities. What is the performance like there? In my opinion, it is more interesting in overlapping entities than in nested entities. How many crossed entities have been identified more than before? Which ones are missing and perhaps why? Has the performance improvement only been due to better nested identification or also due to crossed entity identification? That part would be stronger if some common errors were discussed, comparing the proposed system and the errors made by previous systems.

**General Discussion:**

I like the problems related to named entity identification and there is a rationale for crossed entity identification. However, why are nested entities of interest? The current paper does not encourage the situation and does not shed any light on that matter in the evaluation. Discussing the errors and emphasizing the results on crossed entities compared to some examples and other methods might have convinced me more.

So, I am only mildly interested in the paper, perhaps with a slight tendency to reject it. It seems like just another attempt without emphasizing the important question of crossed entities.
