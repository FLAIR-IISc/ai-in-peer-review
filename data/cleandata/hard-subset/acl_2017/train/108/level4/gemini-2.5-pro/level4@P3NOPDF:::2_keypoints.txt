- Proposes a multigraph representation for NER to handle overlapping entities, including nested and crossing mentions, with inference over token-based nodes and multiple edge types.  
- Strength: Targets the important, challenging problem of overlapping and especially crossing entities missed by standard sequence models.  
- Strength: Introduces a novel and intuitive multigraph representation to model non-sequential entity relationships.  
- Strength: Provides an efficiency comparison with prior work, supporting practical viability.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Motivation for detecting nested (sub-span) entities is underdeveloped; downstream benefits are not convincingly argued.  
- Weakness: Key methodological details are missing—unclear which token representation is used and how edges are deterministically added to the graph.  
- Weakness: Empirical gains are small (<2 F1 points on several datasets), making claims of outperforming prior work feel overstated.  
- Weakness: Lacks additional informative baselines (e.g., dictionary-based methods or commercial SOTA tools like Google Cloud NLP or spaCy).  
- Weakness: No separate performance breakdown for nested vs. crossing entities to show where gains arise.  
- Weakness: Missing qualitative error analysis comparing error types between the proposed model and prior systems.  
- Recommendation: Promising approach but, due to weak motivation for nested entities, missing technical details, and shallow evaluation, the paper leans toward rejection pending substantial revisions.  
- Suggestion: Define "multigraph" at first mention and clarify vague terms like "spurious structures" and "child nodes."  
- Suggestion: Be consistent in terminology (e.g., "state" vs. "node") and fix figure clarity (color loss in B/W, explain gray nodes/edges and example intent).  
- Suggestion: Rephrase awkward wording, clarify ambiguous references and tasks, and highlight practical implications of algorithmic complexity (linear vs. cubic).  
- Technical questions: Specify how missing edges are determined (oracle), the heuristic/algorithm for separator decisions, and how CS-edges are chosen.  
- Technical question: Justify the claim that not favoring crossing entities is desirable and explicitly state the "simple first-order assumption."  
- Formatting: Spell out small numbers in prose and normalize capitalization in reference titles; move footnotes after punctuation.
