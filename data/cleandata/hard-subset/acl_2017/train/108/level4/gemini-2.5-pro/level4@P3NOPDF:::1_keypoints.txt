- Tackles the important and practical problem of nested and overlapping named entity recognition.  
- Demonstrates solid empirical improvements over a strong MH baseline across multiple benchmarks.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly structured.  
- Main weakness: unclear motivation and insufficient explanation for why the new multigraph model outperforms MH.  
- Section 3 fails to identify specific limitations or failure modes of the MH model that the new approach addresses.  
- Section 4 does not justify the introduction of "separators" or explain what extra information they capture versus standard tagging schemes.  
- Ablation and analysis (Section 5.1) report results but lack interpretation of why components improve performance.  
- Claims about avoiding “spurious structures” (Section 5.2) are too abstract and lack concrete evidence or examples.  
- Recommend analyzing MH’s errors to motivate design choices before presenting the new model.  
- Recommend clarifying separators’ function with concrete examples and ablation showing consequences of removal.  
- Recommend adding concrete sentence-level examples comparing MH and the multigraph outputs, with side-by-side graph visualizations.  
- Overall: promising, empirically strong work undermined by insufficient motivation and insightful analysis; addressing these would strengthen the contribution.
