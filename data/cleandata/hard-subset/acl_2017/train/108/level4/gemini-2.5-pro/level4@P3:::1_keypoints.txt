- Paper proposes a multigraph NER model using 8 explicit "entity separator" edge types to handle overlapping (nested/crossing) mentions.  
- Evaluated on ACE2004, ACE2005, GENIA (overlapping) and CoNLL2003 (non-overlapping), showing superior F1 (e.g., +2.1 F1 on ACE2004) to the mention hypergraph (MH) and other baselines.  
- Main contribution: thorough empirical validation demonstrating consistent, significant improvements over the MH model across benchmarks.  
- Main contribution: a novel edge-based multigraph formulation that encodes token roles at entity boundaries via entity separators.  
- Main contribution: theoretical critique of the MH model, identifying "spurious structures" and claiming their multigraph is "nondeficient."  
- Strength: state-of-the-art performance and robustness across overlapping and non-overlapping NER tasks.  
- Strength: thorough, insightful evaluation (breakdowns for overlapping vs non-overlapping sentences and training convergence analysis).  
- Strength: clear writing, good organization, and helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 2).  
- Weakness: insufficient front-loaded motivation — multigraph/separator design feels arbitrary until theoretical section.  
- Weakness: core theoretical claim about MH being "deficient" due to "spurious structures" is unclearly explained in the main text and deferred to the supplement.  
- Weakness: the "state-based vs. edge-based" taxonomy is descriptive but does not strongly justify why the proposed edge-based model is superior.  
- Question: please include a brief concrete example in the main paper showing two entity sets that map to the same MH subgraph but are distinguished by your model.  
- Question: can you analyze model probability distributions (e.g., normalization term Zw(x)) to empirically support the claim that nondeficiency yields higher recall/confidence?  
- Question: how were the 8 entity separators chosen — from first principles or empirically — and could a smaller/different set resolve spurious structures while simplifying the model?
