This paper presents a novel multigraph-based model for detecting overlapping entities in text. The paper addresses an important and challenging problem, and the proposed model demonstrates solid empirical improvements over the previous state-of-the-art (MH) on several benchmark datasets. The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.

However, the main weakness of the paper is the lack of a clear explanation for *why* the proposed model outperforms the baseline. While the empirical results are strong, the paper does not provide sufficient motivation for its architectural choices or a clear analysis of the source of the performance gains. The contribution would be much stronger if the authors could address the following points.

**Major Comments**

1.  **Motivation for the Proposed Model:** Section 3 provides a description of the previous MH model, but it does not articulate the specific limitations or shortcomings of this approach. To properly motivate the new model, the authors should clearly state the issues with the MH model that their proposed multigraph-based approach is designed to overcome. What specific types of errors does MH make, and how does the new architecture theoretically address these deficiencies?

2.  **Justification for Separators:** In Section 4, the rationale for introducing separators is unclear. The paper should explicitly state what additional information these separators provide beyond a standard tagging scheme (e.g., T/I/O for "tag," "inside," and "outside"). A clear justification for their necessity, perhaps with a brief example, would help the reader understand this component of the model.

3.  **Analysis of Model Superiority:** The experimental analysis in Section 5.1 does not effectively illustrate *why* the new model is superior. To strengthen the paper, this section should connect the results more directly to the specific architectural contributions of the model. For instance, an ablation study that demonstrates the impact of each novel component would provide valuable insight into the source of the performance gains.

4.  **Clarity of Discussion and Need for Examples:** The discussion in Section 5.2 is too abstract to provide a deep understanding of the model's advantages. The authors claim that the new model avoids "spurious structures" that may affect the MH model, but this claim is not substantiated with concrete examples. Illustrating this point with one or two specific cases from the dataset where the MH model fails but the proposed model succeeds would significantly strengthen the paper's argument and provide valuable intuition for the reader.

**Summary**

Overall, this paper presents a promising approach with strong results. However, its acceptance is conditional on a significant revision to improve clarity regarding the model's motivation and the underlying reasons for its success. By clarifying the limitations of prior work, justifying the new architectural components, and providing concrete examples to support the analysis, the authors can make a much more compelling case for their contribution.
