- Problem of detecting overlapping entities (especially crossing/discontinuous) is compelling and timely.  
- Proposed multigraph technique appears sound and includes a useful efficiency comparison to prior work.  
- Paper is generally well-written but needs polishing for minor clarity issues.  
- Motivation for nested entities is weak; the paper fails to justify why detecting nested entities (e.g., "China" in "Bank of China") is important.  
- Key implementation details are missing: the decision criterion for including edges in the multigraph is unspecified.  
- It is unclear which of the I^k_t node options are actually used to construct edges, leaving the model underspecified.  
- Empirical improvements are marginal (few F1 points) and the term "outperformed" is overstated without effect size analysis.  
- The evaluation lacks qualitative analysis showing which important entities the model finds that prior methods miss.  
- Missing baselines: no comparison to simple dictionary-based methods.  
- Missing practical benchmark: no comparison to state-of-the-art commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud NLP).  
- Results discussion lacks stratified analysis for crossing vs nested entities to show where gains come from.  
- Error analysis is insufficient; need detailed comparison of types of errors, newly detected vs missed crossing entities, and explanations.  
- Overall contribution is weakened by poor motivation for nested entities and lack of focused evaluation on crossing entities.  
- Paper feels incremental and does not sufficiently address the most critical questions in the problem space.  
- Define "multigraph" on first use for reader clarity.  
- Numerous phrasing and clarity issues (awkward sentences, ambiguous antecedents, inconsistent formatting like italicized "e.g.").  
- Several informal or unclear phrases should be reworded to be more formal and precise.  
- Correct typos and wording errors (e.g., "pervious" -> "previous", redundant phrases).  
- Define technical terms on first use (e.g., NP chunking) and clarify what "state" and "child nodes" mean in hypergraphs.  
- Explain the algorithm for choosing the CS-edge used as an entity separator.  
- Clarify footnote claim that the model does not distinguish nested vs crossing entities and justify why this is desirable.  
- Figure 2 is confusing: unclear why it’s a hypergraph, color distinctions ineffective for B/W printing, and selection criteria for highlighted edges are unexplained.  
- Justify the motivation for detecting both nested entities in examples and explain the practical advantage of shorter entities.  
- Tables’ claims of statistical significance or superiority need supporting statistical tests or effect size discussions.  
- Justify claims like the model being "more confident" and explain how that increases recall.  
- Formatting/style fixes needed: punctuation in numbered lists, spell out small numbers, footnote placement, consistent parenthetical formatting, and consistent capitalization in References.
