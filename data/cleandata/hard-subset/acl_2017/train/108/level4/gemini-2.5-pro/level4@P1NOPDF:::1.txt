This paper presents a novel model for detecting overlapping entities in text. The proposed approach is shown to outperform the previous state-of-the-art, the MH model, across several benchmark datasets. However, while the empirical results are promising, the paper's primary weakness is its lack of a clear and convincing explanation for *why* the new model is superior. The core contribution is therefore difficult to fully appreciate.

**Strengths**

*   The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
*   The problem of identifying overlapping named entities is important and well-motivated.
*   The proposed multigraph-based model is presented as an intuitive variant of the existing MH approach.
*   The empirical results are solid and demonstrate a consistent improvement over the state-of-the-art on multiple datasets.

**Weaknesses**

My main concerns relate to the motivation for certain design choices and the analysis of the results. The following points require clarification:

1.  **Motivation:** The motivation for the new model is not clearly established. Section 3 describes the previous MH model, but it does not adequately discuss its limitations or shortcomings. A clearer explanation of the specific issues with MH that the proposed model aims to solve would significantly strengthen the paper's rationale.

2.  **Separators:** The rationale for introducing "separators" in Section 4 is unclear. The paper should explicitly state what additional information these separators are intended to convey that is not already captured by the standard T/I/O tagging scheme.

3.  **Analysis in Section 5.1:** The analysis in Section 5.1 does not provide sufficient insight into the model's behavior. It is not clear how the information presented in this section supports the claim that the proposed model is superior to the baseline.

4.  **Abstract Discussion in Section 5.2:** The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract, which prevents a deep understanding of the model's advantages. For instance, the authors mention that the model avoids "spurious structures" that MH might generate, but this claim is not substantiated. Providing concrete examples of such spurious structures and illustrating how the proposed model correctly handles them would make the argument much more convincing.
