- Summary: Proposes a multigraph-based model with semantically distinct "entity separator" edges to handle overlapping NER, claiming to fix spurious structures in mention hypergraphs and achieving superior F1 on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a provably "nondeficient" multigraph structured-prediction model that avoids the MH model's spurious-structures problem.

- Contribution 2: Provides strong empirical validation showing consistent F1 gains over mention hypergraph and other baselines across multiple datasets, including analysis by overlap presence.

- Strength 1: Novel, theoretically grounded approach—multigraph separators elegantly address overlapping entity complexity and the paper includes a formal analysis of the spurious-structure issue.

- Strength 2: Thorough and convincing experiments demonstrating consistent improvements and robustness (including non-overlapping CoNLL 2003).

- Strength 3: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures that make the core concepts accessible.

- Weakness 1: Motivation and positioning of the theoretical contribution are delayed; the MH model's deficiency should be highlighted earlier to better frame the solution.

- Weakness 2: The spurious-structures argument is abstract in the main text; a small concrete example is deferred to the appendix and should be placed in the paper.

- Weakness 3: Limited justification for the specific set of 8 entity separators and lack of discussion of alternative (e.g., state-based) design choices.

- Question 1: Please provide a minimal, concrete example in the main text illustrating how the MH model maps two distinct entity sets to the same subgraph and how the multigraph resolves this.

- Question 2: Can you elaborate on how being "nondeficient" theoretically leads to the observed recall improvements and tendency to predict more entities at test time?
