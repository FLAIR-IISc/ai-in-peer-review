- Addresses a relevant problem (detection of crossing/overlapping entities) and focuses on an interesting challenge.  
- Proposed multigraph-based technique appears effective, though empirical gains over baselines are small.  
- Paper usefully discusses computational efficiency compared to prior work.  
- Overall writing is good but needs polishing per minor remarks.  
- Primary weakness: insufficient motivation for detecting nested entities (practical importance not well-established).  
- Methodology lacks critical implementation details (e.g., criterion for adding edges to the multigraph).  
- Unclear which I^k_t node configuration is used and how edges are formed for those configurations.  
- Reported performance gains are marginal; calling them "outperformed" overstates the results.  
- Evaluation misses effect-size analysis to show practical significance of small F1 improvements.  
- No analysis of how many important entities the method recovers that prior methods missed.  
- Experiments omit simple baselines (e.g., dictionary lookup) and state-of-the-art commercial systems for context.  
- Results lack a targeted analysis on crossing entities — need per-subset metrics to show where gains come from.  
- Missing comparative error analysis to show which errors are corrected or introduced relative to prior methods.  
- Overall contribution feels incremental without clearer demonstration of advantages for crossing entities; reviewer leans toward rejection.  
- Minor: First mention of "multigraph" needs a brief definition for unfamiliar readers.  
- Minor: Multiple awkward phrasings and unclear antecedents (e.g., "they", "Solving this task") that require rewriting.  
- Minor: Inconsistent or unconventional formatting (italics for e.g., footnote placement, parenthetical formatting).  
- Minor: Several unclear technical terms and usages (e.g., "spurious structures", "chunk", "state" vs "node", "child nodes" for hypergraphs).  
- Minor: Figures (especially Figure 2) are unclear in black-and-white; grayed nodes/edges and selection criteria are unexplained.  
- Minor: Motivation for detecting nested entities in examples (e.g., "Bank of China" vs "China") is not explained.  
- Minor: Algorithmic choices are under-specified (e.g., how CS-edge/entity separator is chosen).  
- Minor: Many grammatical/typo issues and inconsistent citation/title capitalization that need correction.  
- Minor: Experimental descriptions are unclear or awkward (e.g., dataset phrasing, BILOU not defined, ambiguous claims about significance).  
- Minor: Statistical significance and effect-size of reported improvements are not specified and should be reported.
