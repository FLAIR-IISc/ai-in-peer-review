- Strengths: The manuscript is clearly written and well-structured, with some minor exceptions noted below. The paper addresses a significant and relevant problem in the field. The proposed multigraph-based model is a logical extension of the existing MH approach. Furthermore, the empirical evaluation is robust, and the results presented are compelling.

- Weaknesses: Several aspects of the paper require further clarification to strengthen the contribution.
1. In Section 3, the motivation for the new model needs to be more explicit. While the paper describes the previous MH model, it should also detail the specific limitations or shortcomings of MH that necessitate the development of the proposed approach.
2. The rationale for introducing separators in Section 4 is not apparent. The manuscript should explain what additional information these separators provide beyond what is already captured by the T/I/O tagging scheme.
3. The analysis in Section 5.1 does not seem to offer significant insights into the sources of the new model's superior performance.
4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is currently too abstract, making it difficult to grasp the practical advantages of the new model over MH. Providing concrete examples of the "spurious structures" that the proposed method is claimed to avoid would be highly beneficial.

- General Discussion: This paper introduces a novel model for detecting overlapping entities in text, demonstrating improved performance over the state-of-the-art MH model on several benchmark datasets. However, the manuscript's primary weakness is its failure to clearly articulate *why* and *how* the proposed model achieves these superior results. The connection between the architectural innovations and the observed empirical gains is not sufficiently established.
