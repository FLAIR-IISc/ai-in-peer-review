This paper presents a novel multigraph-based model for detecting overlapping entities in text. The proposed approach, a variant of the existing MH model, achieves solid empirical improvements over the state-of-the-art on several benchmark datasets. However, the paper's primary weakness is a lack of clear motivation and in-depth analysis, making it difficult for the reader to understand precisely why and how the new model outperforms its predecessor.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper is generally well-written and addresses the important and practical problem of overlapping entity recognition.
*   The proposed multigraph-based model is an interesting extension of the previous state-of-the-art MH model.
*   The empirical evaluation is solid, demonstrating consistent improvements over strong baselines on benchmark datasets.

**Weaknesses:**
The paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the following points requiring clarification:

1.  **Motivation:** The motivation for the new model is not clearly articulated. While Section 3 describes the previous MH model, it does not sufficiently explain the limitations or specific issues with MH that necessitate the proposed approach. The authors should explicitly state the shortcomings of the existing model to better justify their contribution.

2.  **Rationale for Separators:** The rationale for introducing "separators" in Section 4 is unclear. The paper should clarify what additional information these separators provide beyond what is already captured by the T/I/O tagging scheme.

3.  **Analysis in Section 5.1:** The analysis in Section 5.1 does not seem to provide useful information explaining the new model's superiority. This section should be revised to offer concrete insights drawn from the experiments.

4.  **Abstract Discussion in Section 5.2:** The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract and lacks concrete examples. To help the reader understand the model's advantages, it would be highly beneficial to provide specific examples of the "spurious structures" that the new model successfully handles or avoids, in contrast to the MH model. This would provide valuable intuition about the model's mechanisms.
