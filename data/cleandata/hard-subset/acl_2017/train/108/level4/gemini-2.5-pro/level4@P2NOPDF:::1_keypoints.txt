1. Introduces a novel multigraph-based model for overlapping entity detection with strong empirical gains over prior state-of-the-art (MH) and clear writing.

2. Main weakness: paper lacks a clear explanation of why the proposed model outperforms MH—insufficient motivation for architectural choices and analysis of performance sources.

3. Major comment 1: Explicitly state MH's limitations and the specific error types the multigraph approach is designed to fix, and explain the theoretical rationale.

4. Major comment 2: Justify the need for separators—what information they add beyond standard tagging schemes (e.g., T/I/O), preferably with a short example.

5. Major comment 3: Provide analyses (e.g., ablation studies) linking empirical improvements to each novel component to demonstrate why the model is superior.

6. Major comment 4: Support the claim that the model avoids "spurious structures" with concrete dataset examples where MH fails but the proposed model succeeds.

7. Summary: Acceptance should be conditional on revisions that clarify prior-work limitations, justify architectural components, and add concrete examples/ablation analyses to explain performance gains.
