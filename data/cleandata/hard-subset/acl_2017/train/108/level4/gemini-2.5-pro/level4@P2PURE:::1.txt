**Strengths:**
The paper is generally well-written and addresses the important problem of overlapping entity detection. The proposed multigraph-based model is presented as a novel variant of the existing MH approach, and the empirical results are strong, demonstrating consistent improvements over the baseline.

**Weaknesses:**
The paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing several points that require clarification:
1.  Section 3 describes the previous MH model but does not articulate its specific limitations or shortcomings. A clearer motivation is needed to explain what issues with MH prompted the development of the new model.
2.  In Section 4, the rationale for introducing separators is unclear. The paper should explain what additional information these separators convey beyond the standard T/I/O tags and justify their inclusion in the model.
3.  The analysis in Section 5.1 does not provide sufficient insight into the sources of the proposed model's superior performance.
4.  The discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract, making it difficult to understand precisely why the new model is better than MH. To make the argument more concrete, the authors should provide specific examples of the "spurious structures" that the proposed model successfully handles while MH does not.

**General Discussion:**
This paper introduces a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text and shows that it improves upon the previous state-of-the-art (the MH model) across several benchmark datasets. However, while the empirical results are convincing, the paper's main weakness is its lack of a clear and intuitive explanation for *why* and *how* the proposed model achieves these improvements. The connection between the model's architectural changes and the resulting performance gains is not sufficiently established.
