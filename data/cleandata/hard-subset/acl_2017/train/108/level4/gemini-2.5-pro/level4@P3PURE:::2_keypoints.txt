- Addresses overlapping entity detection, notably crossing entities, and proposes a multigraph-based approach.
- Technique appears sound and shows modest empirical improvement over evaluated baselines.
- Includes a useful discussion of computational efficiency versus a prior system.
- Paper is generally well-written but needs polishing for clarity and precision.

- Lacks clear motivation for nested-entity detection (e.g., utility of finding "China" inside "Bank of China" is unexplained).
- Method description omits crucial reproducibility details, especially the criterion for including edges in the multigraph.
- Unclear which configuration of I^k_t nodes is used in experiments and how edges to those nodes are determined.
- Reported F1 improvements are small; claims of "outperformed" overstate the effect without discussing practical significance.
- Evaluation misses simple and strong baselines (e.g., dictionary-based method, commercial NER APIs) for context.
- Results are not broken down by nested vs. crossing entities, so it's unclear where gains originate.
- Lacks qualitative error analysis comparing the proposed system to baselines to explain what is now correctly or incorrectly detected.
- Overall empirical evaluation is shallow and needs deeper analysis focused on crossing-entity performance.

- Contribution feels incremental without a clear demonstration of practical or theoretical significance, especially for crossing entities.
- Recommend stronger empirical analysis, clearer motivation, and examples to justify design choices.

- First use of "multigraph" should include a brief definition for unfamiliar readers.
- Numerous awkward phrasings and inconsistent citation/formatting styles need revision.
- Inconsistent handling of numbers, punctuation in lists, footnote placement, and reference title capitalization.
- Several technical terms and concepts are undefined or ambiguous (e.g., "spurious structures," "chunk," "state" vs "node," "child nodes," "first order assumption").
- Figure 2 and its hypergraph labeling/selection are unclear and not robust to black-and-white printing; rationale for detecting both entities vs. longest one is missing.
- Several algorithmic choices are unspecified (e.g., how CS-edge is chosen, how separators are determined, justification for missing edges in footnote 2).
- Footnote claim that nested and crossing entities are treated equally needs justification and examples.

- Many typos and grammatical errors throughout (e.g., "pervious" -> "previous," wrong article/number agreement, awkward sentences), and some statistical claims (e.g., "significantly better") are unsupported or misleading.
