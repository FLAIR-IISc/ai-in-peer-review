- Proposes a multigraph-based model to detect overlapping named entities, including nested and crossing spans.
- Addresses an interesting and important problem, especially disambiguating crossing entities.
- Technique appears technically sound and yields modest, consistent empirical improvements.
- Authors discuss model efficiency compared to prior work.
- Paper is generally clear and well-written but needs polishing for minor issues.
- Motivation for detecting nested entities (e.g., "China" inside "Bank of China") is weak and not well justified.
- Key methodological details are missing—specifically how token-internal nodes (I^k_t) and multigraph edges are selected.
- Reported improvements are marginal; claims of “outperforming” are overstated without effect-size discussion.
- Lacks focused analysis on crossing entities; unclear whether gains come from nested or crossing detection.
- Missing broader baselines (e.g., dictionary-based methods or commercial NLP systems) to contextualize results.
- No qualitative error analysis comparing types of errors to prior methods.
- Overall assessment: incremental contribution with insufficient narrative and deep analysis; reviewer leans slightly toward rejection.
- Suggest defining “multigraph” at first use for unfamiliar readers.
- Numerous phrasing issues and awkward sentences that need rewording.
- Multiple formatting/typo errors and inconsistent reference title capitalization must be fixed.
- Claims about algorithmic complexity (e.g., “time linear in n”) should be made precise and justified.
- Terms like “spurious structures,” “NP chunking,” and “types of states” need clear definitions and consistent usage.
- Figure 2 is unclear (color-dependent, unexplained grayed nodes, unclear hypergraph representation) and the example’s motivation is not demonstrated.
- Clarify notions such as “child nodes in a hypergraph,” how the CS-edge is chosen, and why certain edges are missing (footnotes need justification).
- Tables report very small F1 differences without discussion of significance or practical impact.
- Explain links between model confidence and recall increases and justify claims that not favoring crossing over nested entities is desirable.
