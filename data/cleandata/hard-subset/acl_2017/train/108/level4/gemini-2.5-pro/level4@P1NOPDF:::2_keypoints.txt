- Problem of detecting overlapping entities (especially crossing entities) is interesting and relevant.
- Proposed multigraph-based technique appears effective and includes a helpful efficiency comparison to prior work.
- Paper is generally well-written but needs additional polishing.

- Motivation for detecting nested entities (e.g., "China" inside "Bank of China") is weak and underdeveloped.
- Key model details are missing: criterion for including edges in the graph is not defined.
- It is unspecified which of the suggested I^k_t node edge options are used in the final model (lines 229–233).
- Empirical improvements are small; claims of "outperforming" prior work seem overstated without effect-size discussion.
- No analysis of whether small F1 gains translate to meaningful practical improvements (e.g., how many critical entities newly discovered).
- Lacks simpler baselines (e.g., dictionary-based) and comparisons to commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud NLP) for context.
- Results discussion insufficiently focused on crossing entities; need performance breakdown and counts for this subset.
- No qualitative/error analysis comparing types of mistakes vs prior systems to explain where gains come from (nested vs crossing).
- Contribution currently feels incremental due to insufficient exploration of the most compelling aspect (crossing entities).

- Overall: interesting problem and promising method, but weak motivation, missing technical details, limited empirical analysis, and insufficient error/case studies make the paper less convincing.
