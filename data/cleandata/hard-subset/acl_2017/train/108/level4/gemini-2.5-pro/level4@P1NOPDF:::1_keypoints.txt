- Proposes a novel multigraph-based model for overlapping entity detection that outperforms the MH baseline on multiple benchmarks.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- The task of identifying overlapping named entities is important and well-motivated.
- The multigraph model is presented as an intuitive variant of the existing MH approach.
- Empirical results are solid and show consistent improvements over the state of the art.
- Primary weakness: lacks a clear, convincing explanation of why the new model is superior, making the core contribution hard to appreciate.
- Motivation is unclear: the paper does not explain specific limitations of MH that the new model addresses.
- Rationale for introducing "separators" is not explained—unclear what information they add beyond standard T/I/O tags.
- Analysis in Section 5.1 is insufficient and does not demonstrate how results support the claimed superiority.
- Discussion in Section 5.2 is overly abstract and claims (e.g., avoiding “spurious structures”) are unsubstantiated; concrete examples are needed.
