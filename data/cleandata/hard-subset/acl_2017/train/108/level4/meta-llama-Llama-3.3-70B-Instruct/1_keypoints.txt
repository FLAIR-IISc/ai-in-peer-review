- Overall clear writing with some areas needing improvement.
- Addresses a pertinent problem; proposes a multigraph-based variant of MH.
- Empirical results are solid and show improvements over MH on benchmarks.
- Section 3 lacks an explicit discussion of limitations of the previous MH model and motivations for the new approach.
- Section 4’s introduction of separators is unjustified; unclear what extra information they add beyond T/I/O.
- Section 5.1 fails to provide meaningful insights into why the new model is superior.
- Section 5.2 is overly abstract; concrete examples of spurious structures are needed to demonstrate advantages over MH.
- Overall, the paper shows empirical gains but does not clearly explain why or how the new model achieves superiority.
