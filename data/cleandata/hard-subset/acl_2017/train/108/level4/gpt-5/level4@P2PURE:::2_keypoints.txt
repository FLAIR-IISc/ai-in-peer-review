- Proposes a multigraph-based method to detect overlapping (including crossing) entities.  
- Interesting and relevant problem, especially for crossing entities; technique appears to work though gains are modest.  
- Paper discusses efficiency relative to prior systems, which is appreciated.  
- Generally well written but needs additional polishing in multiple places.  

- Motivation for detecting nested entities (e.g., "China" inside "Bank of China") is weak and needs stronger use-case justification.  
- Important methodological details are missing, notably the decision criterion for including edges and which I^k_t node options are used.  
- Empirical improvements are small; claiming to have “outperformed” prior work overstates the effect without effect-size or practical significance analysis.  
- Lacks comparison to simple dictionary baselines and commercial entity-recognition systems to contextualize performance.  
- Evaluation lacks focused analysis on crossing entities—the most interesting case—such as counts of additional detections and remaining failures.  
- Needs a focused error analysis comparing proposed system to prior ones, particularly for crossing vs. nested entity gains.  

- Reviewer is interested in the topic but remains lukewarm and leans slightly toward rejection due to insufficient emphasis on crossing entities and weak motivation for nested entities.  

- First mention of "multigraph" should include a brief definition for readers.  
- Multiple wording/phrasing issues (e.g., “previously noted by ... many previous,” “Solving this task,” italics misuse).  
- Clarify what "n" refers to for time complexity and whether linear vs. cubic distinction is practically important.  
- "Spurious structures" and "regarded as a chunk" are unclear and need explanation.  
- Clarify “NP chunking” vs. “noun phrase chunking” and ambiguous pronouns like “they.”  
- Numerous typos and formatting issues (e.g., “pervious” → “previous,” tilde use, punctuation/footnote placement).  
- Define what a “state” is in (hyper-)graph context and clarify “child nodes” in a hypergraph.  
- Figure 2 is unclear: justify hypergraph labeling, grayscale visibility, gray vs. highlighted nodes/edges, and why both nested entities should be detected.  
- Explain how CS-edge and separator determinations are chosen algorithmically.  
- Specify the decision rule for edge inclusion where footnotes claim missing edges without justification.  
- Clarify the “simple first order assumption” explicitly and fix cross-references like “In the previous section.”  
- Improve phrasing for results reporting (e.g., avoid overstating small F1 differences, report effect sizes/statistical tests).  
- Provide concrete examples and error cases, especially for crossing entities, and quantify how many important entities were newly discovered.  
- Fix dataset and experimental description issues (grammar, citation formatting, feature usage details, “GENIA” phrasing).  
- Table 5 and similar results: acknowledge similarity of F1 scores and avoid overstating superiority; explain mechanisms behind recall or confidence claims.  
- Standardize reference/title capitalization and overall formatting throughout the paper.
