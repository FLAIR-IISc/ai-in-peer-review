- Strengths: The manuscript is clearly written overall, with only a few exceptions noted below. The problem addressed is important. The proposed multigraph-based approach can be viewed as a variant/refinement of MH. The empirical evidence is solid.

- Weaknesses: Several points require clarification.
1. Section 3: Beyond describing MH, the paper should explicitly identify MH’s shortcomings that motivate introducing the new model.
2. Section 4: The rationale for introducing separators is unclear. What additional information do they encode beyond the T/I/O states?
3. Section 5.1: This section does not convincingly explain why the new model is superior.
4. Section 5.2: The discussion is too abstract to yield insight into why the new model improves upon MH. Please provide concrete examples of spurious structures.

- General Discussion: The paper introduces a new model for recognizing overlapping entities and shows improvements over the previous state of the art (MH) on several benchmark datasets. However, the reasons and mechanisms by which the new model outperforms MH remain insufficiently explained.
