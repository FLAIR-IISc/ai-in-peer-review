- Strengths: The paper is generally well written, aside from a few issues noted below. The problem addressed is important. The proposed multigraph-based model is a variant of MH. The empirical results are solid.

- Weaknesses: Several points need clarification.
1. In Section 3, beyond describing the prior model (MH), you should explicitly identify MH’s limitations that motivate introducing a new model.
2. In Section 4, the rationale for introducing separators is unclear. What additional information do they convey beyond T/I/O?
3. Section 5.1 does not provide convincing evidence or explanation for why the new model is superior.
4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is too abstract to yield insight into why the new model outperforms MH. Can you provide concrete examples of spurious structures?

- General Discussion: The paper introduces a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text and improves upon the previous state of the art, MH, on several benchmark datasets. However, it remains unclear why and how the new model achieves better performance.
