- Strength: Addresses an interesting problem of overlapping entities, especially crossing entities and deciding which mention is expressed in text.
- Strength: The proposed multigraph technique appears effective, though empirical gains are modest.
- Strength: Provides a useful discussion of efficiency relative to prior work.
- Strength: Generally well written but needs polishing on several details.

- Weakness: Motivation for nested entities is weak; the Bank of China/China example does not convincingly justify nested-entity importance.
- Weakness: Key approach details are underspecified—decision criteria for including/omitting edges and which I^k_t nodes are instantiated are unclear.
- Weakness: Empirical gains over prior methods are small; claiming to “outperform” seems overstated without effect sizes or practical-impact discussion.
- Weakness: Missing baselines—include a simple dictionary-based baseline and comparisons to strong commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud Natural Language).
- Weakness: Analysis insufficiently focused on crossing entities; report performance specifically on crossing cases, counts of newly detected crossing entities, remaining misses, and whether gains stem from nested vs crossing detection.
- Weakness: Lacks comparative error analysis vs prior systems to clarify strengths and weaknesses.
- General view: Paper is incremental and underemphasizes the central challenge of crossing entities, leaving the reviewer lukewarm with slight inclination to reject.
- Recommendation: Provide more thorough error analysis with illustrative examples, emphasizing crossing-entity results relative to other approaches.
