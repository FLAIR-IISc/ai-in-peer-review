- Summary: Proposes a multigraph variant of MH for overlapping-entity recognition with consistent empirical improvements on benchmarks.

- Strength: Addresses an important, practical problem of detecting overlapping entities.

- Strength: Method is well presented, technically sound, and concretely positioned as a variant of MH.

- Strength: Empirical results are solid and show improvements over MH on multiple datasets.

- Strength: Writing is generally clear, with only a few areas needing clarification.

- Weakness/clarification 1 — Motivation vs MH: Explicitly state MH’s limitations that the multigraph addresses (representational or inference issues).

- Weakness/clarification 1 — Provide concrete examples of overlapping/nested structures MH mishandles (ambiguous segmentations, constraints it cannot encode).

- Weakness/clarification 1 — If issues relate to spurious derivations, independence assumptions, or context sensitivity, state and link them to design choices.

- Weakness/clarification 2 — Rationale for separators: Clarify what separators encode beyond T/I/O and why they’re needed.

- Weakness/clarification 2 — Give illustrative tag-sequence examples with/without separators to show disambiguation benefits.

- Weakness/clarification 2 — Discuss trade-offs: expressiveness, learning signal, spurious ambiguity, decoding complexity, and error propagation.

- Weakness/clarification 3 — Evidence for superiority: Add ablations isolating multigraph vs separators and compare to MH with identical features/training.

- Weakness/clarification 3 — Report statistical significance and ensure fair-comparison protocols for features, hyperparameters, and training.

- Weakness/clarification 3 — Reorganize Section 5.1 to present diagnostic analyses attributing gains to design elements if currently setup-focused.

- Weakness/clarification 4 — Define “spurious structures” precisely in the framework and give token-level/tag-level examples where MH admits them but multigraph avoids or downweights them.

- Weakness/clarification 4 — Quantify effects: counts/proportions of pruned derivations, lattice size changes, decoding ambiguity, and link to precision/recall shifts.

- Suggestion — Theoretical comparison: Briefly contrast expressivity and independence assumptions of MH vs multigraph and what patterns each can/cannot represent.

- Suggestion — Complexity & efficiency: Provide asymptotic and empirical training/decoding time and memory comparisons and mitigation strategies for overhead.

- Suggestion — Error analysis: Break errors down by overlap depth, entity length, and type to show which categories improve.

- Suggestion — Robustness & variance: Report averages over multiple seeds with standard deviations to show stability.

- Suggestion — Data & evaluation: Confirm dataset splits/preprocessing match prior work and report performance by overlap degree.

- Suggestion — Clarity & presentation: Define MH on first mention, keep notation consistent, provide a running example across sections, and make figure/algorithm captions convey intuition.

- Minor writing issues: Edit for concision and parallel structure; fix capitalization and section cross-references.

- Overall assessment: Timely and promising work with clear empirical gains, but needs stronger motivation, separator clarification, targeted ablations, and deeper analysis of spurious structures to be fully convincing.
