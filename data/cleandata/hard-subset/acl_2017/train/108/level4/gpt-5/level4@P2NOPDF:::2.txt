The paper proposes a multigraph-based method (allowing multiple edges between two nodes) for detecting potentially overlapping entities.

Strengths:
- The problem is interesting, particularly for handling crossing entities and deciding which mention is actually expressed in text.
- The technique appears effective, though the empirical gains are modest rather than dramatic.
- I appreciate the discussion of efficiency relative to prior work.
- The paper is generally well written, but some details still need polishing (see minor remarks below).

Weaknesses:
The motivation is weak. The introduction’s example—detecting China within Bank of China—does not clearly justify why nested entities are important. The case for crossing entities is more compelling, but the use cases for nested entities should be explained more convincingly to engage the reader.

Key details of the approach are underspecified. What is the decision criterion for including or omitting an edge? Lines 229–233 list several options for the I^k_t nodes, but the paper never specifies which edges are actually instantiated under which conditions.

The empirical gains over prior methods are small. Describing such improvements as “outperforming” seems overstated. Please report effect sizes and discuss practical impact: Does a 2-point F1 gain matter to downstream users? How many important entities are newly discovered compared to earlier systems? Include a simple dictionary-based baseline that could also capture overlapping mentions. Moreover, benchmarking against widely used commercial systems (e.g., Google Cloud Natural Language) would contextualize the results against strong, practical baselines.

The analysis should focus more on crossing entities. What is the performance specifically on crossing cases, which are arguably the more interesting subset? How many more crossing entities are detected than before, which ones are still missed, and why? Are the improvements mainly from better nested detection, or do they extend to crossing mentions? A comparative error analysis versus prior systems would clarify the method’s strengths and weaknesses.

General Discussion:
I value the focus on named entity recognition with overlapping structures, especially crossing entities. However, the paper does not adequately motivate the need for nested entities, nor does it analyze this distinction in the experiments. A more thorough error analysis with illustrative examples—emphasizing results on crossing entities relative to other approaches—would substantially strengthen the paper. Overall, I am lukewarm, with a slight inclination toward rejection, because the work feels incremental and underemphasizes the central challenge of crossing entities.
