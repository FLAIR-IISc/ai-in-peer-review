- Strength: Paper is well written with few issues.
- Strength: Addresses the important problem of detecting overlapping entities.
- Strength: Proposes a multigraph-based model (variant of MH).
- Strength: Empirical results are solid and show improvements.

- Weakness: Section 3 should explicitly state MH’s limitations and which shortcomings motivate the new model.
- Weakness: Section 4 lacks rationale for introducing separators—what they add beyond T/I/O, how they interact with tagging, and why they’re necessary.
- Weakness: Section 5.1 does not clearly show why the new model is superior; needs evidence or analysis tied to design choices.
- Weakness: Section 5.2 is too abstract and should include concrete examples of spurious structures MH permits and how the new model avoids them.

- General: Overall gains over MH are shown but it’s unclear why; the paper needs explicit articulation of MH’s weaknesses, justification for separators, and concrete examples demonstrating modeling advantages.
