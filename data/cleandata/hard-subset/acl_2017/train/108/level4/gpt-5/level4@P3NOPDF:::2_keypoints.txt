- Summary: Proposes a multigraph-based method for detecting overlapping (nested and crossing) named entities, described clearly, with modest empirical improvements and some efficiency discussion.
- Strength: Addresses the under-explored and valuable problem of overlapping/crossing entities.
- Strength: Model presentation is overall clear and considers computational efficiency versus prior work.
- Strength: Results are competitive with incremental gains and the paper is well written.
- Weakness — Motivation: Practical importance of nested entities is under-motivated; needs concrete downstream use cases and distinction between nested vs. crossing value.
- Weakness — Model specification: Edge inclusion criteria and exact node/edge construction rules (e.g., for I^k_t nodes) are underspecified and need precise definition.
- Weakness — Empirical significance: Report effect sizes and statistical significance; current gains (~0.2–2 F1) may be small and their meaningfulness is unclear.
- Weakness — Comparative context: Include stronger baselines (e.g., dictionary-based overlap-capable methods, commercial NER) to better calibrate practical value.
- Weakness — Error analysis: Provide targeted analysis for crossing entities—how many are recovered vs. missed, and whether improvements stem from nested or crossing cases—with qualitative examples.
- Weakness — Results interpretation: Avoid overclaiming; temper wording like “outperform” unless improvements are statistically and practically significant.
- Weakness — Reproducibility: Clarify hyperparameters, feature choices, heuristics, thresholds, and provide code or pseudocode for multigraph construction.
- Overall evaluation: Interesting and promising approach for crossing entities, but current draft underspecifies modeling details, lacks motivation for nested entities, and provides limited crossing-case analysis; recommendation is borderline/reject.
- Minor — Terminology/clarity: Define terms (multigraph, spurious structures, state vs. node, mention hypergraph), clarify ambiguous phrases, and be consistent in terminology.
- Minor — Model/equations: Explicitly list included edges for I^k_t and decision rules; fix small grammatical/formatting issues around equations.
- Minor — Figures/tables: Improve figure readability (grayscale), explain highlights and choices, report effect sizes/statistics with bolded results, and temper claims for small differences.
- Minor — Motivation/evaluation: Strengthen motivation for nested detection, provide counts/examples of entities newly discovered, and explain how confidence increases lead to higher recall.
- Minor — Style/consistency/typos: Fix awkward phrasing, grammar, punctuation, footnote placement, and typos; standardize parenthesis/notation and runtime implications.
- Minor — References: Ensure consistent capitalization and formatting across references.
