1. Paper proposes an edge-labeled multigraph, log-linear model for overlapping entity recognition using “entity separators” to encode start/end/continue events.  
2. Training/inference use inside–outside and max-product decoding with O(nT) complexity.  
3. Main representational contribution: reformulates overlapping mentions to remove MH’s spurious structures, yielding a nondeficient model.  
4. Empirically outperforms mention hypergraph (MH) and baselines on ACE2004/2005 and GENIA, and improves on non-overlapping CoNLL 2003.  
5. Frames a design axis of state-based vs. edge-based modeling, advocating edge semantics for overlapping structure prediction.  
6. Strength: tackles a practically important problem—efficiently handling frequent overlapping mentions in ACE/GENIA.  
7. Strength: theoretical clarification—identifies spurious structures in MH and argues the multigraph is nondeficient.  
8. Strength: fair, thorough empirical evaluation with identical features, standard splits, and analyses by sentence type and training dynamics.  
9. Strength: training appears more stable (fewer LBFGS iterations) despite slightly slower per-iteration cost.  
10. Strength: approach is conceptually generalizable to other overlapping-structure tasks.  
11. Weakness: insufficient motivation and intuition for the eight entity separator types; needs a step-by-step worked example.  
12. Weakness: abstract explanation of spurious structures in the main text is hard to follow; a compact contrasting example is needed.  
13. Weakness: limited evidence that removing spurious structures causally drives empirical gains—no ablation isolating this factor.  
14. Weakness: unclear expressivity/decoding ambiguity—whether model can produce crossing mentions and how they’re resolved.  
15. Weakness: novelty may seem incremental relative to MH since core machinery (training, inference, features) is similar.  
16. Weakness: missing implementation/efficiency details and constants (wall-clock time, memory, parameter counts).  
17. Question: provide an end-to-end example showing separator assignments, resulting edges, and contrast with MH.  
18. Question: can the model produce crossing mentions at decoding time; if not, revise claims and discuss implications.  
19. Question: are feature templates shared across separator types or separator-specific; any parameter tying?  
20. Question: sensitivity of results to mention-penalty tuning—please provide P/R/F1 curves or sensitivity plots.  
21. Question: quantify training time per epoch, total convergence time, peak memory, and parameter counts for MH vs. multigraph.  
22. Question: since model factors by entity type, did cross-type features help on ACE?  
23. Additional: section 3 should state MH’s deficiencies up front to motivate the new formulation.  
24. Additional: clarify notation/figures in Section 4 and ensure consistency of node/edge types across text and figures.  
25. Additional: tighten the state-vs.-edge discussion and fix bibliography/placeholders and minor typos.  
26. Overall: positive leaning recommendation contingent on clarifying separator motivation, tightening spurious-structure analysis, and clarifying decoding expressivity.
