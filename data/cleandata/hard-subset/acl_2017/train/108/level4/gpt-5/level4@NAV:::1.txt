Review-
- Strengths: The paper is clearly written, with only a few exceptions noted below. The problem addressed is valuable. The proposed multigraph-based model is a variant of MH. The empirical results are strong.
- Weaknesses: Clarification is needed in several areas.
1. In Section 3, beyond describing the prior MH model, you should explicitly identify the issues with MH that motivate proposing a new model.
2. In Section 4, the rationale for introducing separators is unclear. What additional information do they convey beyond T/I/O?
3. Section 5.1 does not appear to provide useful evidence or explanation for why the new model is superior.
4. The discussion in Section 5.2 is so abstract that it does not yield insight into why the new model outperforms MH. Can you provide concrete examples of spurious structures?
- General Discussion: The paper presents a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text. The model improves upon the previous state-of-the-art, MH, on several benchmark datasets. However, it remains unclear why and how the new model achieves better performance.
