- Proposes a multigraph-based method for detecting overlapping (including crossing) entities.  
- Interesting problem, especially for crossing entities; proposed technique appears to work though gains are modest.  
- Good discussion of efficiency relative to prior work and generally well written with some polishing needed.  
- Motivation is weak, especially for nested entities (e.g., detecting “China” inside “Bank of China” lacks clear practical use).  
- Key technical details are missing—unclear decision criterion for including edges and which edges should exist among I^k_t nodes.  
- Empirical improvements are marginal; claims of “outperforming” are overstated without effect sizes or practical impact analysis.  
- Missing important baselines (simple dictionary baseline, commercial systems) to contextualize gains on overlapping cases.  
- Results underemphasize crossing entities; lacking per-case performance, counts of additional crossing detections, and targeted error analysis.  
- Need clearer error analysis showing which nested vs. crossing entities are improved or missed and why.  
- Numerous clarity and writing issues: define “multigraph,” clarify “state”/node terms, fix awkward phrasing and typographical errors.  
- Figures and examples are unclear—Figure 2’s hypergraph depiction, gray nodes/edges, and example justification need clarification.  
- Reporting issues: specify statistical tests/effect sizes for “significant” claims, clarify dataset statistics, and standardize citation/formatting.
