- Strengths: The paper is generally well written, with only a few issues noted below. The problem it addresses—detecting overlapping entities—is important. The proposed approach, a multigraph-based model, is a variant of MH. The empirical results are solid.

- Weaknesses: Several clarifications are needed.
1. Section 3: In addition to describing the prior model MH, please explicitly identify MH’s limitations that motivate your new model. What specific shortcomings does MH have in handling overlapping entities, and how do these issues lead to your design choices?
2. Section 4: I do not see a clear rationale for introducing separators. What additional information do they convey beyond T/I/O? Please explain their role, how they interact with the tagging scheme, and why they are necessary.
3. Section 5.1: This section does not seem to provide useful information about why the new model is superior. Please clarify what evidence or analysis here supports the claimed advantages, and tie it directly to the model’s design.
4. Section 5.2: The discussion is too abstract to provide insight into why the new model outperforms MH. Could you include concrete examples of spurious structures that MH permits (or fails to rule out) and show how your model avoids them?

- General Discussion: The paper presents a new model for detecting overlapping entities in text and reports improvements over the previous state of the art (MH) on several benchmark datasets. However, it remains unclear why and how the new model achieves these gains. The paper would benefit from a more explicit articulation of MH’s weaknesses, a clear justification for separators beyond T/I/O, and concrete examples (e.g., spurious structures) that demonstrate the specific modeling advantages leading to the observed improvements.
