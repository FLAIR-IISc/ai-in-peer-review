- Proposes a multigraph-based formulation for overlapping entity recognition that assigns explicit semantics to multiple edges between the same node pair.
- Provides theoretical analysis claiming nondeficiency (no spurious structures) while retaining linear-time inference (O(nT)).
- Presents empirical comparisons with modest but consistent F1 gains on ACE2004/2005, GENIA, and CoNLL 2003, and reports on training convergence.

- Novel edge-based representation mapping entity separators to edges; well-motivated and connected to prior mention-hypergraph work.
- Clear, practical inference procedures (inside–outside and max-product) with O(nT) complexity.
- Nondeficiency argument addresses known spurious-structure issues of mention hypergraphs.
- Competitive empirical performance: consistent improvements on overlapping datasets and robustness on non-overlapping data.
- Training behavior: faster convergence in iterations despite slightly slower per-iteration time.

- Insufficient motivation for nested entities and unclear practical downstream impact; better application-focused justification needed (e.g., IE, linking, biomedical).
- Missing algorithmic detail on how edges/separators (e.g., Ikt, CS) are selected or instantiated during training and inference.
- Limited analysis of effect size and significance; small F1 gains lack statistical testing, effect-size metrics, or counts of additional correct entities.
- Incomplete baseline coverage: no dictionary/heuristic or commercial-system comparisons, and limited comparison to other structured/span-based baselines (e.g., semi-CRF, span models).
- Limited error analysis and ablation: no breakdown by nested vs. crossing entities, no qualitative error inspection to show where gains arise.

- How are entity separators (CS, EC, etc.) instantiated between tokens algorithmically, and how is ambiguity resolved when multiple separators connect the same state pair?
- What are precision/recall/F1 and absolute counts for crossing entities specifically, and how do these compare to baselines?
- Are the reported gains statistically significant? Please provide significance tests and counts of additional correct mentions, especially for high-value types.
- How would simple dictionary-based approaches or commercial NER/linking systems perform on overlapping/nested spans on these datasets?
- Provide wall-clock efficiency comparisons for realistic sentence lengths to quantify practical differences between O(n) and higher-complexity methods.
