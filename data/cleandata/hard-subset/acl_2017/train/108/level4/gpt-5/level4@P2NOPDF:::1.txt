- Strengths: The paper is generally well written, with only a few issues noted below. The problem it addresses is important. The proposed multigraph-based model is a variant of MH, and the empirical results are strong.

- Weaknesses: Several clarifications are needed.
1. Section 3: Beyond describing the prior MH model, please clearly identify MH’s limitations and how they motivate your new approach.
2. Section 4: The rationale for introducing separators is unclear. What additional information do they convey beyond T/I/O? Please provide intuition and evidence for their benefit.
3. Section 5.1: This section does not convincingly explain why the new model is superior. Strengthen the connection between the analysis and the mechanisms that yield the gains.
4. Section 5.2: The discussion is overly abstract and does not provide insight into why the new model outperforms MH. Please include concrete examples of spurious structures and show how your model mitigates them.

- General Discussion: The paper introduces a new model for detecting overlapping entities that outperforms the previous state of the art (MH) on several benchmark datasets. However, it remains unclear why and how the new model achieves these improvements. Clearer articulation of MH’s shortcomings, justification for separators, and concrete examples or analyses demonstrating how the new model eliminates spurious structures would substantially improve the paper’s clarity and impact.
