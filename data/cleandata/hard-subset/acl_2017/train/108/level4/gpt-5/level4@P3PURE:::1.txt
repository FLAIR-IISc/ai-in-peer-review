Summary:
The paper proposes a multigraph-based model for detecting overlapping entities in text. The approach is a variant of MH and achieves solid empirical improvements over the prior state of the art on several benchmark datasets. Overall, the paper is well written, with a few exceptions noted below. However, it is still unclear why and how the new model works better than MH.

Strengths:
- Generally well written and clearly presented (with a few exceptions).
- The problem addressed is important and useful.
- The proposed multigraph-based model is a plausible variant of MH.
- Empirical results are solid.

Weaknesses and required clarifications:
1. Section 3: In addition to describing the previous model (MH), please explicitly identify the issues or limitations of MH that motivate your new model. What concrete shortcomings of MH does the multigraph formulation address?
2. Section 4: The introduction of separators is not well justified. What additional information do they convey beyond T/I/O? Why are they needed, and how do they affect the hypothesis space, representational capacity, or learning signal?
3. Section 5.1: This section does not provide useful insight into why the new model is superior. Please include analysis that links specific design choices to the observed gains, rather than only restating results.
4. Section 5.2: The discussion is overly abstract and does not provide actionable insight into why the new model improves over MH. Please include concrete examples of spurious structures under MH and demonstrate how the new model avoids them.

General discussion:
The paper introduces a new model for overlapping entity detection that outperforms MH on several benchmarks. Nonetheless, the paper does not yet make clear why and how the new model achieves these gains. Strengthening the motivation by detailing MH’s shortcomings, justifying the role of separators, and enhancing Sections 5.1 and 5.2 with concrete analyses and examples of spurious structures would substantially improve the clarity and impact of the work.
