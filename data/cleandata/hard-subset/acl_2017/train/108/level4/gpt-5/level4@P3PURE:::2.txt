This paper proposes a multigraph-based approach (allowing multiple edges between two nodes) for detecting potentially overlapping entities.

Strengths:
- The problem is interesting, particularly for handling crossing entities and deciding which entity mentions are actually present in text. 
- The technique appears to work, even though the empirical gains are modest rather than dramatic.
- I appreciate the discussion of efficiency relative to a prior system.
- The paper is generally well written, though some details still need polishing (see minor remarks below).

Weaknesses:
- The motivation is not sufficiently developed. For example, why is it important to detect “China” as a standalone entity within “Bank of China,” as in the introduction? I can see a strong case for crossing entities, but the use case for nested entities is not clear. Please strengthen the motivation so readers understand why nested entities matter in practice.
- Methodological details are missing. What is the decision criterion for including an edge in the multigraph? In lines 229–233, several different options for the I^k_t nodes are mentioned, but it is not clarified which edges should be present under which conditions.
- Empirical evaluation: Results are better than some previous approaches but not by a large margin. Describing such small gains as “outperforming” seems overstated. What is the effect size? Would a two-percentage-point gain in F1 matter to end users in practice? How many “important” entities are discovered that were missed by prior methods?
- Baselines and context: What performance would a simple dictionary-based approach achieve for detecting overlapping entities? Also, how would a commercial system (e.g., Google’s Cloud Natural Language) perform on these datasets? Even if not directly comparable, such context would help position the contribution relative to existing “commercial” systems.
- Result analysis: Please emphasize performance on crossing entities specifically. How well does the method perform there, and how many more crossing entities are detected than before? Which crossing entities are still missed, and why? Is the overall improvement driven primarily by better nested-entity detection, or does it also improve detection of crossing entities? A targeted error analysis comparing errors made by the proposed system and by previous ones would strengthen the discussion.

General discussion:
I am interested in NER and see a strong case for recognizing crossing entities. However, the paper does not adequately motivate nested entities, and the evaluation does not shed light on that question. A clearer error analysis, concrete example cases, and an explicit emphasis on results for crossing entities (relative to other approaches) would have been more convincing. Overall, I am lukewarm on the paper, with a slight tendency toward rejection. As it stands, it feels like another iteration that does not sufficiently emphasize the, in my view, more important question of crossing entities.
