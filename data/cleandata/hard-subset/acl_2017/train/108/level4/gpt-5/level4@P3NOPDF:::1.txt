Summary
This paper proposes a multigraph-based model for recognizing overlapping entities in text, presented as a variant of the prior state-of-the-art MH approach. The method is evaluated on several benchmark datasets and achieves consistent empirical improvements over MH.

Strengths
- The paper addresses an important and practically useful problem: detecting overlapping entities.
- The approach is generally well presented and appears technically sound. Positioning it as a variant of MH makes the contribution concrete and comparable.
- The empirical results are solid and show improvements over MH on multiple datasets.
- The writing is clear overall, with only a few areas where additional clarification would strengthen the paper.

Weaknesses and requests for clarification
1) Motivation relative to MH (Section 3)
- In addition to describing MH, please clearly articulate its limitations that motivate your new model. What specific representational or inference issues in MH does the multigraph address?
- Provide concrete examples of overlapping or nested structures that MH mishandles (e.g., ambiguous segmentations, constraints it cannot encode, or error patterns it induces).
- If the problem relates to spurious derivations, independence assumptions, or limited context sensitivity in MH, state this explicitly and connect it to your design choices.

2) Rationale for separators (Section 4)
- The purpose of introducing separators is not sufficiently clear. What additional information do they encode beyond standard T/I/O labeling?
- Please include one or two illustrative sentences showing tag sequences with and without separators, and explain how separators disambiguate structures that T/I/O alone cannot represent or would represent suboptimally.
- Discuss the trade-offs: Do separators increase expressiveness, improve learning signals, or reduce spurious ambiguity? What is their impact on decoding complexity and error propagation?

3) Evidence for superiority (Section 5.1)
- As written, Section 5.1 does not convincingly explain why the new model is superior. Consider adding:
  - Ablations isolating the contribution of the multigraph structure and of separators (vs. MH with identical features/training).
  - Statistical significance tests for key improvements.
  - A fair-comparison protocol ensuring feature sets, hyperparameters, and training regimes match MH as closely as possible.
- If Section 5.1 primarily describes setup, consider moving that material earlier and use Section 5.1 to present diagnostic analyses that attribute gains to specific design elements.

4) Insights and spurious structures (Section 5.2)
- The discussion is too abstract to yield actionable insight. Please:
  - Define “spurious structures” precisely in your framework.
  - Provide concrete token-level examples (ideally with diagrams or tag-level walkthroughs) where MH admits spurious derivations and the multigraph either disallows them or assigns them lower score.
  - Quantify effects, e.g., counts or proportions of pruned derivations, changes in lattice size, or decoding ambiguity.
  - Link these differences to precision/recall shifts (e.g., fewer false positives due to reduced spurious overlaps).

Suggestions to strengthen the paper
- Theoretical comparison: Briefly contrast the expressivity and independence assumptions of MH vs. the multigraph model. What classes of overlapping entities or dependency patterns can your model represent that MH cannot (or can represent only with ambiguity)?
- Complexity and efficiency: Provide asymptotic and empirical complexity comparisons (training/decoding time and memory). If the multigraph introduces overhead, explain mitigation strategies or justify the cost with observed gains.
- Error analysis: Break down errors by overlap depth, entity length, and type. Identify which error categories shrink with your model and which persist.
- Robustness and variance: Report averages over multiple random seeds with standard deviations to ensure improvements are stable.
- Data and evaluation details: Confirm that dataset splits and preprocessing match prior work; note any deviations. Consider reporting performance by overlap degree to substantiate that improvements specifically target overlapping cases.
- Clarity and presentation:
  - Define MH on first mention and keep notation consistent across sections.
  - Add a running example carried through Sections 3–4 to ground the formalism.
  - Ensure figure/algorithm captions convey intuition, not just formal details.

Minor writing issues
- A light edit for concision and parallel structure would improve readability.
- Capitalize sentence starts consistently (e.g., “What additional info...” rather than “what”).
- Ensure section cross-references are correct and specific.

Overall assessment
This is a timely and promising paper that addresses a meaningful gap in overlapping entity recognition and demonstrates clear empirical gains over MH. However, the current presentation does not make it sufficiently clear why and how the proposed multigraph model outperforms MH. Strengthening the motivation (limitations of MH), clarifying the role of separators with concrete examples, providing targeted ablations, and deepening the analysis of spurious structures would substantially improve the paper’s clarity and impact. With these additions, the contribution would be much easier to assess and likely compelling.
