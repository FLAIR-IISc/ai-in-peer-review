- Proposes a multigraph-based variant of MH for overlapping entity detection that yields solid empirical improvements on benchmarks.  
- Paper is generally well written; the problem is important and the proposed model is a plausible MH variant with strong results.  
- Unclear why and how the new model outperforms MH — the paper lacks explicit motivation tied to MH’s concrete shortcomings.  
- Section 3 should identify specific limitations of MH that the multigraph formulation addresses.  
- Introduction of separators (Section 4) is insufficiently justified: what do they add beyond T/I/O, why are they needed, and how do they affect hypothesis space, capacity, or learning?  
- Section 5.1 restates results but does not analyze which design choices produce the observed gains; needs component-level analysis.  
- Section 5.2 is overly abstract and must include concrete examples of spurious structures under MH and demonstrate how the new model avoids them.  
- Strengthening motivation, separator justification, and adding concrete analyses/examples would substantially improve clarity and impact.
