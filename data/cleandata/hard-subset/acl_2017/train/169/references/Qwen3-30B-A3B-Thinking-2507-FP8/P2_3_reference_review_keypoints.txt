- Proposes a two-step, rule-based method for automatic error-type annotation in GEC: linguistically-enhanced edit extraction + rule-based edit classification.  
- Main contributions: dataset-agnostic rule-based annotation framework, enables detailed error-type analysis of GEC systems, and a publicly released annotation tool.  
- Addresses a significant evaluation gap: current overall scores hide system strengths/weaknesses on specific error types.  
- Justifies rule-based approach over ML due to transparency, determinism, and avoidance of training-corpus bias.  
- Manual evaluation with 5 experts on 200 edits shows >95% acceptable/good annotations and high inter-annotator agreement (κfree=0.724).  
- Valuable demonstration on CoNLL-2014 revealing system-specific strengths/weaknesses (e.g., many teams fail to correct unnecessary token errors).  
- Core components are not highly novel—builds on prior edit extraction and classification work without clear distinction from past methods.  
- Generalizability is under-evaluated: validated only on CoNLL-2014 and FCE-test despite claims of dataset-agnosticism.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to other possible error-type evaluation methods in the literature.  
- Limited analysis of how the new evaluation affects overall system rankings compared to standard metrics like M2.  
- Question: need more evidence of performance on datasets with different error distributions and linguistic traits.  
- Question: request examples showing whether system rankings change significantly under the new metric vs. M2.  
- Question: unclear handling of ambiguous edits that could map to multiple error types (e.g., tense vs. modal).  
- Overall recommendation: solid, useful contribution with sound methodology and evaluation; recommend acceptance despite limited novelty and generalizability concerns.
