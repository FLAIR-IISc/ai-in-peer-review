- Proposes an automatic, dataset-agnostic two-step method for annotating GEC error types: linguistically-enhanced edit extraction + rule-based typing using automatic linguistic features.  
- Claims >95% agreement with human raters and provides the first comprehensive error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems.  

Strengths:
- Identifies a genuine evaluation gap in GEC by enabling per-error-type analysis beyond aggregate metrics like F0.5.  
- Uses a dataset-agnostic, rule-based typing approach that avoids dataset-specific ML biases and works without retraining.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by revealing concrete system-level insights on the CoNLL-2014 shared task.  
- Presents a modular framework allowing flexible, fine-grained analyses (e.g., by edit or POS class).  

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient analysis of edit-extraction errors and their impact on downstream error-typing reliability.  
- No direct comparative evaluation against prior methods (e.g., Swanson & Yamangil 2012, Xue & Hwa 2014).  
- Limited discussion of applicability to non-English languages or UD tagset limitations.  
- Unclear how the framework addresses fluency-related errors and multi-token corrections relevant to fluency-focused GEC.  

Questions for authors:
- How does the rule-based system handle ambiguous cases humans would discuss (e.g., "He has a cat" → "He has cats": NOUN:NUM vs NOUN:INFL)?  
- What specific unnecessary-token error patterns did the five teams miss, and why were they challenging?  
- How does your edit comparison avoid M2 scorer’s tendency to overestimate performance and dynamic edit-boundary issues?  

Recommendation:
- Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address edit-extraction error analysis and comparative evaluation; releasing the tool will benefit the GEC community.
