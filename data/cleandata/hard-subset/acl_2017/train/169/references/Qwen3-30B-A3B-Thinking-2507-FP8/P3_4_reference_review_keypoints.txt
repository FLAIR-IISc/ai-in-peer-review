- Summary: Proposes automatic two-step method (linguistically-enhanced edit extraction + rule-based error classification) to annotate GEC outputs with error types, applied to CoNLL-2014.

- Contribution: Dataset-agnostic, rule-based error-typing framework requiring no labeled training data.

- Contribution: A practical tool for automatic error-type annotation enabling fine-grained analysis.

- Contribution: First comprehensive error-type analysis of all CoNLL-2014 systems across 25 categories.

- Strength: Fulfills a genuine evaluation gap by revealing system-specific strengths/weaknesses hidden by overall scores.

- Strength: Innovative, dataset-independent rule-based approach with human evaluation >95% "Good" or "Acceptable."

- Strength: Practical insights from CoNLL-2014 analysis (e.g., complementary system strengths; five teams failed unnecessary token errors).

- Weakness: Limited justification for choosing these 25 error types and some arbitrary labeling decisions (e.g., modal vs. tense).

- Weakness: Insufficient controlled comparison to prior ML-based error-typing methods and metrics.

- Weakness: Overstated claim that the M2 scorer overestimates performance, supported only by a single example.

- Question: How to adapt the framework for learner-population-specific error types (e.g., Chinese vs. Spanish L2 writers)?

- Question: How robust is the claimed dataset-independence beyond FCE-test and CoNLL-2014 given varying error distributions?

- Question: How to prevent the framework becoming unwieldy when adding fine-grained categories and what criteria justify new categories?

- Overall: Valuable, practical contribution with strong validation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address justification, comparisons, and evidence concerns.
