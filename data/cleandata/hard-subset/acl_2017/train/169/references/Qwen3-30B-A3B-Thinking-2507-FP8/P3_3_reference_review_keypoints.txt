- Identifies a gap: system outputs in GEC lacked error-type annotations, preventing detailed error analysis.
- Proposes an automatic annotation method using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based error-type classifier.
- Rule-based classifier is dataset-agnostic, relying only on POS tags and lemmas rather than labeled training data.
- Demonstrates utility on CoNLL-2014, revealing system-specific strengths and weaknesses by error type.
- Example findings: CAMB excels in many categories; CUUI outperforms others on ORTH errors; some teams never correct unnecessary token errors.
- Validates approach with a small human evaluation showing 86.3% "Good" or "Acceptable" agreement.
- Tool is practical, reproducible, and builds on established NLP techniques (e.g., Felice et al., 2016).
- Human rater comments indicate many "Bad" cases trace to POS tagger errors, suggesting robustness of the approach.
- Lacks comparison with alternative automatic methods or trained classifiers to quantify improvement over prior work.
- Human evaluation is limited in scale (5 raters, 200 examples) and may not reflect performance on diverse/challenging errors.
- Paper does not convincingly demonstrate how detailed error-type analysis will change GEC system development or evaluation practice.
- Claims about M2 scorer being overestimating performance are overstated and not empirically shown to be improved upon.
- Overall: sound, well-validated, and practically useful contribution that warrants acceptance, though could be strengthened.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
