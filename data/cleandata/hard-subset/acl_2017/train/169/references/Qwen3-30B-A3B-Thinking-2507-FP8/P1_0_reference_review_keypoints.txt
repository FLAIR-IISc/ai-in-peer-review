- Paper addresses GEC evaluation gap by proposing a two-stage method (linguistically-enhanced edit extraction + rule-based error typing) to enable error-type analysis.  
- Main contribution: dataset-independent, rule-based error typing that requires no labeled training data.  
- Provides a practical automatic annotation tool enabling detailed error-type analysis previously impossible due to inconsistent annotations.  
- Demonstrates approach on CoNLL-2014, producing comprehensive error-type analyses of all participating systems.  
- Method is methodologically sound and validated via a small manual evaluation (>85% “Good”/“Acceptable”).  
- Impactful demonstration: reveals nuanced system strengths/weaknesses (e.g., CAMB excels broadly; five teams failed on unnecessary token errors).  
- Promises to release the tool and offers a hierarchical operation+error-type framework for flexible analysis.  
- Weakness: insufficient direct comparison to prior automatic annotation methods, including other rule-based approaches (e.g., Swanson & Yamangil 2012, Xue & Hwa 2014).  
- Weakness: limited evaluation of the complete pipeline—unclear how final automated annotations compare to human annotations or the pipeline error rate.  
- Weakness: underdeveloped analysis of why specific systems perform well on particular error types (lack of causal/system-design explanation).  
- Weakness: claim that M2 "overestimates" performance is inadequately supported by systematic evidence.  
- Question: How specifically does this framework improve on Swanson & Yamangil (2012) and address their limitations?  
- Question: Will the authors provide a gold-standard comparison of final annotated output against human annotations (e.g., subset of CoNLL-2014)?  
- Question: How does the framework handle semantic-level errors that go beyond POS-based rules (e.g., “I like to eat” vs. “I like eating”)?  
- Question: What design choices enabled CAMB’s broad coverage of error types compared to other teams?  
- Overall recommendation: valuable, well-designed contribution; accept with minor revisions contingent on deeper comparisons and analyses.
