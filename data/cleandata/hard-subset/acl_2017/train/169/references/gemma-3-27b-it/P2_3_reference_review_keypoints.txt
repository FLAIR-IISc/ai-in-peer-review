- Presents a rule-based, dataset-independent method for automatically annotating GEC outputs with error types using POS, lemmas, and stemming.  
- Demonstrates the tool by re-analyzing CoNLL-2014 systems to provide detailed error-type and operation breakdowns.  
- Core contribution: a training-data-free error type annotation framework enabling cross-dataset comparison.  
- Contribution: delivers a richer, fine-grained analysis of CoNLL-2014 GEC systems beyond overall scores.  
- Contribution: promises a publicly released tool to support reproducibility and community use.  
- Strength: tackles the lack of standardized, detailed error-type evaluation in GEC.  
- Strength: strong argument for a transparent, deterministic rule-based approach over data-dependent ML methods.  
- Strength: manual evaluation and bootstrap testing provide reasonable validation of automatic annotations.  
- Strength: practical impact through public tooling and actionable error-type breakdowns for future research.  
- Weakness: the 25 error types may lack sufficient granularity to capture GEC complexity.  
- Weakness: performance depends on accuracy of POS tagging, lemmatization, and stemming, with limited analysis of those failures.  
- Weakness: the proposed edit comparison method is somewhat simplistic and may ignore partial or subtle correction matches.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of interactions between multiple simultaneous error types.  
- Question: request for detailed error analysis tracing mistakes to POS tagger or other NLP tool inaccuracies and mitigation strategies.  
- Question: ask for rationale and alternatives considered for the strict edit comparison approach (e.g., partial/fuzzy matching).  
- Question: inquire about plans to expand error-type granularity and how to balance informativeness vs. practicality.  
- Question: ask whether the authors will explore error-type interactions in future work.  
- Additional comment: Table 9's listing of error code combinations is helpful for understanding framework granularity.  
- Additional comment: results and tables are clearly presented and easy to follow.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, well-motivated, and robustly evaluated contribution to GEC that merits acceptance with minor revisions.
