- Proposes a dataset-agnostic, rule-based pipeline for automatic error-type annotation in GEC outputs using linguistically-enhanced edit extraction.
- Provides a practical, reusable tool for post-hoc error analysis that enables more granular evaluation than prior CoNLL-style metrics.
- Demonstrates utility by re-evaluating CoNLL-2014 systems and offering detailed error-type breakdowns as a benchmark.

- Addresses a significant gap in GEC evaluation by enabling error-type specific feedback.
- Builds on established edit-extraction methods and justifies a transparent, dataset-independent rule-based approach.
- Conducts a thorough re-evaluation of CoNLL-2014 systems with insightful comparisons across error-type granularities.
- Commits to releasing the tool, supporting reproducibility and community adoption.
- Presents the paper clearly and coherently.

- Framework depends heavily on accuracy of initial edit extraction; extraction errors may propagate and are not fully quantified.
- Rule-based classifier evaluation is limited (200 edits, focused on appropriateness) and lacks precision/recall against a gold standard.
- Rule-based approach may oversimplify nuanced errors requiring deeper linguistic understanding.
- Limited discussion of design choices for the error-type schema and alternatives.

- Please quantify errors introduced by the edit-extraction step and their impact on downstream typing.
- Clarify manual evaluation procedures: rater guidelines and inter-rater reliability beyond the reported κ-free score.
- Explain how ambiguous edits that map to multiple error types are resolved and what tie-breaking rules are used.
- Have you considered a hybrid approach incorporating machine learning to handle complex or subtle error types?

- Tables effectively summarize results and aid quick understanding.
- Reprocessing references with the automatic annotator to standardize evaluation is a useful decision.
- The critique of M2 scorer limitations is informative.

- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
