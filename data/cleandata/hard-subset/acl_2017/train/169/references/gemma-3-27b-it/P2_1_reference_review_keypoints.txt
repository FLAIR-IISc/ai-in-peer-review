- Summary: Proposes a dataset-agnostic, linguistically-enhanced alignment plus rule-based classifier to automatically assign error types to GEC system edits and re-analyzes CoNLL-2014 outputs.

- Contribution 1: Practical method for automatic fine-grained error-type annotation of GEC system outputs.

- Contribution 2: Rule-based, dataset-agnostic classifier that avoids labeled training data and diverse annotation schemes.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates tool utility by re-evaluating CoNLL-2014 to reveal system strengths across error types.

- Strength 1: Addresses the critical need for detailed error analysis in GEC to guide system improvements.

- Strength 2: Technically strong approach combining linguistically-informed alignment (building on prior work) with rule-based classification.

- Strength 3: Rigorous evaluation and insightful analysis, including comparisons to M2 and actionable observations about system complementarities.

- Strength 4: Committed tool release, increasing reproducibility and impact for the GEC community.

- Strength 5: Small-scale human evaluation supports classifier accuracy and reliability despite noisy edit extraction.

- Weakness 1: Human evaluation is small (200 edits) and does not assess alignment/edit-boundary accuracy, a crucial component.

- Weakness 2: Rule-based system complexity could hinder maintenance and extension as new error types emerge.

- Weakness 3: High dependence on POS tagger accuracy; tagging errors are a frequent misclassification source.

- Weakness 4: Lacks direct empirical comparison to existing error-type prediction methods in accuracy and efficiency.

- Question 1: What is the computational cost and scalability of the annotation process (time per sentence, large dataset feasibility)?

- Question 2: Have you considered techniques to improve edit alignment for multi-token errors?

- Question 3: Which specific tagging errors most often cause misclassification, and what mitigation strategies are planned?

- Question 4: How does the human-annotated error-type distribution compare to the automatic annotations?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written; Table 9 and the CoNLL-2014 testbed choice are helpful.

- Overall assessment: Recommend accept with minor revisions—expand human evaluation to include boundary accuracy and add direct comparisons to existing methods.
