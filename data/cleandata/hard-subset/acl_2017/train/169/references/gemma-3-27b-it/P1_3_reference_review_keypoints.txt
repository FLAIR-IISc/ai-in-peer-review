- Paper proposes a rule-based, dataset-agnostic method to automatically annotate GEC outputs with error types using linguistically-enhanced edit alignment and POS/operation rules.
- Authors re-evaluate CoNLL-2014 systems to provide error-type-specific performance breakdowns and show systems often detect errors better than they correct them.

- Contribution: Practical, reproducible, dataset-agnostic automatic error-typing tool with planned public release.
- Contribution: Detailed re-evaluation of CoNLL-2014 systems highlighting strengths/weaknesses by error type.
- Contribution: Empirical finding that many GEC systems detect errors more reliably than they perform corrections.

- Strength: Novel, practical rule-based approach using readily available linguistic features, avoiding reliance on expensive annotated training data.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation including manual validation, bootstrap significance testing, and comparison with M2 scorer.
- Strength: Clear presentation and strong motivation situated within GEC literature.
- Strength: Commitment to releasing the tool for replication and extension.

- Weakness: Manual evaluation limited to 200 edits from two datasets and a coarse "Good/Acceptable/Bad" scale; inter-annotator agreement could be deeper.
- Weakness: Pipeline depends on accuracy of initial edit extraction (Felice et al., 2016); impact of extraction errors on results is not fully quantified.
- Weakness: Rule-based, dataset-agnostic scheme simplifies some error categories, lumping distinctions like modal vs. tense errors.

- Question: What is the F1 score of the edit extraction algorithm on the CoNLL-2014 test set?
- Question: Can you provide the distribution of error types on CoNLL-2014 after applying the automated annotations?
- Question: Please elaborate with examples on how the M2 scorer overestimates performance in CoNLL-2014.
- Question: Have you tried hybrid approaches combining rule-based typing with specialist ML classifiers for hard-to-handle error types?

- Comment: Table 9 (error codes) should be reformatted for readability.
- Comment: The discussion on multi-token errors is insightful and valuable.
- Comment: Paper should discuss limitations of the rule-based approach for stylistic/semantic nuances.
- Comment: An appendix with the rule-based classifier code would be beneficial.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-evaluated, practical contribution to GEC evaluation deserving acceptance with minor revisions.
