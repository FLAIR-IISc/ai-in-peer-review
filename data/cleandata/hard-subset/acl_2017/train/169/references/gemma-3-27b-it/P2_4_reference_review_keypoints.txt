- Proposes a pipeline for automatic GEC error-type annotation using linguistically-enhanced edit alignment plus a rule-based classifier.  
- Rule-based classifier is dataset-agnostic, reducing domain dependence of prior ML approaches.  
- Demonstrates the tool on CoNLL-2014, producing a detailed per-error-type analysis of participating systems.  
- Fills a real need for granular GEC evaluation and the authors release the tool.  
- Methodology is sound; reported high agreement with human raters (~95% “Good” or “Acceptable”).  
- Analysis reveals useful patterns (e.g., systems struggle with unnecessary-token and multi-token errors) and complementary system strengths.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and well-written.  
- Individual components lack novelty; novelty is primarily their integration and application to GEC evaluation.  
- Heavy reliance on POS tags/lemmatization/stemming; errors in these upstream tools cause misclassifications.  
- Manual evaluation is small (200 edits) and measures appropriateness rather than precision/recall; stronger gold-standard evaluation is needed.  
- Rationale for the chosen 25 error categories could be expanded and better justified.  
- Reviewer asks for quantified analysis of misclassifications due to POS/lemma errors, challenges/trade-offs for adding categories, robustness across domains/languages, and broader applications (e.g., data augmentation or learner feedback).  
- Minor positives: Table 9 of error codes, complete references, and concise writing.  
- Overall: strong, practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen evaluation and category-design discussion.
