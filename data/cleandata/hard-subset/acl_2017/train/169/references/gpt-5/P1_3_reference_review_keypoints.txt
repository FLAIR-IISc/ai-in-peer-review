- Contribution: Deterministic, dataset-agnostic error-typing framework/tool that assigns operation-aware error types using only automatic linguistic markup.
- Contribution: Empirical validation showing automatically reprocessed references give team-level scores statistically indistinguishable from gold, and analysis contrasting fixed-span matching with M2.
- Contribution: Comprehensive error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 outputs across 24+ categories, multi-token edits, and detection vs. correction.

- Strength: Practical, deterministic, interpretable, requires no labeled training data and supports flexible operation-tier diagnostics.
- Strength: Small human study (5 experts, 200 edits) reports 86.3% Good, κ_free=0.724, with most “Bad” cases due to POS tagging errors.
- Strength: Reprocessing references simplifies scoring/alignment and bootstrap tests show no significant team-level differences versus gold.
- Strength: Rich per-type/operation analyses reveal actionable system strengths/weaknesses (e.g., classifiers excel at VERB:FORM, MT on multi-token/NOUN).
- Strength: Framework is by-design dataset-agnostic and extensible to new error categories/configurations.

- Weakness: Limited and indirect evaluation of type-level accuracy—small sample, “appropriateness” metric, no mapping to human taxonomies or large-scale validation.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on upstream POS/lemma/dependency/Hunspell preprocessing with no ablation or robustness analysis across taggers/domains.
- Weakness: Taxonomy design choices are under-justified and sometimes coarse (e.g., modality under VERB:TENSE); limited comparison/interoperability with existing schemes.
- Weakness: Reprocessing references trades annotation fidelity for comparability and may obscure multi-annotator variation; score parity ≠ annotation quality parity.
- Weakness: Per-type statistical reporting lacks confidence intervals, multiple-comparison controls, effect sizes, and some presentation issues.

- Question: Can you map your labels to existing human-annotated taxonomies (e.g., NUCLE/FCE) and report type-level agreement/confusion matrices?
- Question: How sensitive is the classifier to choice/quality of POS tagger, lemmatizer, and parser, and can you provide ablation results?
- Question: How often do edits fall into broad categories like MORPH/OTHER and what are the most frequent confusions or “Bad” cases?
- Question: How are multi-annotator references handled when reprocessing, and can partial-credit across annotators be accommodated?
- Question: Will the released tool include the full ruleset, taxonomy configurations, reprocessing scripts, and documentation for extending to new languages/domains?

- Overall: Useful and timely practical contribution with insightful analyses, but needs stronger evaluation rigor, robustness checks, and clearer taxonomy justification.
