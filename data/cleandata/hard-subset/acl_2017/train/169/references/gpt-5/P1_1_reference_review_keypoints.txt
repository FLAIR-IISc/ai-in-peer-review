- Paper presents an automatic pipeline to annotate GEC corrections with explicit edit spans and error types for per-type evaluation.  
- Method: linguistically-informed alignment (Damerau–Levenshtein with POS/lemma-aware costs and rules) followed by a deterministic, rule-based type classifier using POS/lemmas/stems/deps/Hunspell.  
- Proposes a hierarchical, dataset-agnostic error taxonomy (operation: Missing/Unnecessary/Replacement; token/morph tiers; 55 valid combinations).  
- Small manual validation (200 edits, 5 raters) reported 96.3% Good/Acceptable and κ_free=0.724.  
- Applied the tool to CoNLL-2014 system outputs and references, finding negligible differences vs gold under M2 and an exact-match scorer (bootstrap p>.05).  
- Tool enables the first detailed per-type, per-operation, multi-token, and detection-vs-correction analyses across systems and will be released.  
- Contribution: a practical, deterministic, dataset-agnostic resource for aligning and typing edits to enable per-error-type evaluation.  
- Contribution: a modular error taxonomy engineered for automatic assignment (operation/token/morph tiers).  
- Contribution: empirical insights from comprehensive type-level analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems exposing complementary strengths and persistent weaknesses.  
- Strength: addresses a real evaluation gap by standardizing hypotheses and references into the same annotation space for exact-match scoring.  
- Strength: explainable, auditable classifier that avoids corpus-specific training and uses only automatic annotations.  
- Strength: empirical evidence that auto-annotated references can suffice for evaluation, simplifying scoring and removing alignment-format mismatches.  
- Strength: generates actionable system-level findings (e.g., failures on Unnecessary tokens, system-specific strengths on Missing/Replacement/ORTH).  
- Strength: reasonable validation of the rule-based classifier given noisy inputs (high Good/Acceptable rate and acceptable inter-rater agreement).  
- Weakness: intrinsic evaluation is limited (small sample, coarse labels, no per-type accuracy or confidence intervals), raising concerns about long-tail types.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on upstream NLP quality and English-only experiments; no ablation or portability analysis across taggers/parsers/languages.  
- Weakness: taxonomy design and coverage lack empirical validation, include broad catch-alls (MORPH/OTHER) and unclear mapping to existing schemes.  
- Weakness: reprocessing gold references might mask true mismatches and could institutionalize alignment/type biases without granular difference analysis.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to learning-based type classifiers or alternative alignment strategies and no controlled ablations of rules’ contributions.  
- Questions for authors: provide per-type confusion stats/CIs and causes of “Bad” labels; report sensitivity to upstream tools and tagsets; release mapping to existing schemes and fraction of MORPH/OTHER edits.  
- Additional comment: a small quantitative experiment would strengthen the M2 overestimation claim; releasing code and auto-annotated outputs will maximize impact.  
- Recommendation: practical and impactful paper; accept if strengthened with deeper intrinsic evaluation and sensitivity analyses.
