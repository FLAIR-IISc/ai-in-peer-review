- Proposes a dataset-agnostic pipeline to annotate GEC outputs with explicit edit boundaries and error types for fine-grained evaluation.  
- Two-step method: POS/lemma-aware Damerau-Levenshtein edit extraction with merging rules, then deterministic rule-based error-type classification (25 base categories, 55 ops) using automatic markup.  
- Validates classifier with a small human study and demonstrates utility by re-evaluating CoNLL-2014 systems across types, operations, multi-token edits, and detection vs. correction.

- Contribution: practical, deterministic, dataset-agnostic tool for typing edits in both references and hypotheses to enable consistent error-type evaluation.  
- Contribution: empirical, type-level analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems highlighting system-specific strengths and weaknesses.  
- Contribution: a simple evaluation protocol using fixed spans + exact-match scoring to decouple alignment from scoring and enable transparent type-aware metrics.

- Strength: fills a clear gap by enabling automated, like-for-like error-type evaluation for hypotheses and references, with immediate practical value.  
- Strength: deterministic, auditable design based solely on automatic linguistic markup supports cross-corpus comparability and reproducibility.  
- Strength: empirical utility demonstrated via extensive analyses on a major benchmark revealing actionable system differences.  
- Strength: sensible separation of alignment and scoring that clarifies metric confounds and M2’s dynamic boundary effects.  
- Strength: initial human validation shows high Good/Acceptable rates and reasonable inter-rater agreement.

- Weakness: validation is limited — small, subjective study without gold-typed corpus benchmarking or per-type precision/recall and lacking ablations.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on upstream POS/lemma/parse/Hunspell quality without quantified impact or tagger-robustness experiments.  
- Weakness: error-type scheme choices lack deeper justification, mapping to existing taxonomies, and per-type distribution statistics (OTHER/UNK usage unclear).  
- Weakness: strict exact span+correction matching may unfairly penalize legitimate alternative corrections and paraphrases; metric trade-offs aren’t validated against human judgments or other metrics.  
- Weakness: reprocessing gold references automatically may shift boundaries/labels and type-level implications aren’t quantified.

- Q1: Will you release the full executable rule set, alignment parameters, and versioned dependencies to ensure reproducibility?  
- Q2: How sensitive is classification to POS/lemma/parse errors—can you provide ablations (POS-only vs. POS+lemma+dep) and tagger-swap experiments?  
- Q3: Can you map your type scheme to established taxonomies (FCE/NUCLE) and report frequencies/causes of OTHER/UNK?  
- Q4: Can you report correlations between your exact-match type-aware scores and human judgments and contrast with M2/GLEU/I-measure? How are multi-reference matches aggregated?  
- Q5: Have you applied the tool to other corpora (FCE, JFLEG) or non-English languages and what are preliminary results?  
- Q6: How often do boundary disagreements on multi-token edits drive failures, and would alternative matching strategies change conclusions?

- Overall: Timely, practical contribution with clear utility; needs stronger validation, ablations, and scheme/metric clarifications to fully solidify impact.
