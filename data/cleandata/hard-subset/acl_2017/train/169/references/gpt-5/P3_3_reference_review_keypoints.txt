- Proposes a rule-based, dataset-agnostic framework that types GEC edits using only automatic linguistic markup (POS, lemmas, deps, spelling).
- Implements a unified pipeline: linguistically-enhanced edit alignment + modular classifier (55 types) applied to both hypotheses and references for exact-match comparison.
- Provides empirical analysis on CoNLL-2014 with per-type, operation-tier, multi-token, and detection-vs-correction breakdowns; small human study shows high perceived appropriateness.
- Fills an evaluation gap by enabling per-error-type diagnostics for GEC systems where standard scorers lack robust breakdowns.
- Deterministic, markup-driven classifier avoids corpus-specific training, improving interpretability, reproducibility, and reducing domain-mismatch classifier bias.
- Separation of edit extraction and evaluation (fixed spans, exact-match) makes evaluation auditable and comparable; auto-processed references give similar system-level scores to gold via bootstrap.
- Per-type breakdowns reveal complementary system strengths, common weaknesses (Unnecessary deletions, multi-token edits), and detection–correction gaps to guide future system design.
- Tool release would likely standardize fine-grained GEC reporting and have high community impact.
- Validation is limited: manual eval of 200 edits measures perceived appropriateness not accuracy against gold error-type labels, with no per-category IAA or mapped-corpus comparison.
- Rule set and decision procedure are under-specified: missing rule ordering, conflict resolution, tie-breaking, and detailed criteria for categories like Word Order and Morphology.
- Heavy dependence on upstream NLP quality with no sensitivity analysis across taggers/parsers or confidence-aware handling; learner text noise may skew categories.
- Evaluation evidence is narrow: equivalence of gold vs auto references shown only at overall F0.5 on one dataset, lacking per-type correlations, rank correlations, and CIs for per-type scores.
- Claimed dataset-independence is English-centric (Penn→UD mapping, Hunspell GB, spaCy v1.6); rare categories suffer sparsity and stability under class imbalance is unaddressed.
- Request for precise rule specification and release including precedence, conflict resolution, and fallback behaviors.
- Ask for sensitivity analysis to different taggers/lemmatizers/parsers and stability metrics (e.g., Cohen’s kappa).
- Request deeper validation: per-category human ratings, IAA, comparison to human-typed corpora, and per-type system-ranking correlations between gold and auto refs.
- Question about span strictness: whether relaxed matching (partial span/char overlap) was tested and its effects on system/per-type conclusions.
- Ask about language portability: what adaptations are needed to transfer beyond English (tagset mapping, lexicons).
- Suggest including confidence intervals for per-type and multi-token scores due to sparse cells.
- Request clarification of bootstrap procedure (sampling unit, paired/unpaired, number of references).
- Note references formatting inconsistencies and request fixes for camera-ready.
- Strongly recommend releasing the tool with comprehensive documentation, rule files, and reproduction scripts to maximize impact.
- Overall recommendation: useful, timely contribution with moderate novelty; recommend acceptance after addressing validation depth and rule/specification clarity.
