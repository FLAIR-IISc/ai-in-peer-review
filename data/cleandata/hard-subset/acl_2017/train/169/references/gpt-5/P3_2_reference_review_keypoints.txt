- Proposes an automatic two-stage pipeline: edit extraction via enhanced Damerau–Levenshtein alignment and deterministic rule-based error typing using automatic linguistic mark-up.  
- Maps labels to a compact, dataset-agnostic error scheme (~25 base types with M/R/U prefixes and morphological subtypes; 55 combinations).  
- Validates the annotator with a small expert study (200 edits: 86% “Good”, 10% “Acceptable”) and releases the tool.  

- Contribution: deterministic, interpretable, dataset-agnostic error-typing framework enabling per-type evaluation at multiple granularities.  
- Contribution: comprehensive type-level analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems (operation-tier, multi-token edits, detection vs. correction).  
- Contribution: practical evaluation protocol separating edit extraction from scoring and showing auto-processed references give comparable overall scores to gold on CoNLL-2014.  

- Strength: provides practical, impactful tooling filling the gap of standardized error-type annotations for system outputs.  
- Strength: dataset-agnostic scheme tied to UD/POS/lemma features avoids corpus-specific bias and offers rule traceability.  
- Strength: empirical utility shown via extensive CoNLL-2014 analyses revealing actionable insights (e.g., weaknesses on deletions, multi-token edits, detection–correction gap).  
- Strength: sensible decoupling of extraction and evaluation with transparent exact edit matching and critique of M2’s score inflation.  
- Strength: initial human validation with encouraging inter-rater agreement (κfree=0.724).  

- Weakness: validation is limited—200 edits insufficient for per-type accuracy, no quantitative comparison to a gold error-typing standard or ablation of feature contributions.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on noisy automatic analyses (POS/UD/lemmas/Hunspell) without sensitivity/robustness studies across taggers or settings.  
- Weakness: “auto reference ≈ gold reference” claim is narrowly supported by overall F0.5 on one dataset and lacks per-type, ranking, or statistical significance analyses.  
- Weakness: error scheme has ambiguity and coverage issues (linguistically distinct phenomena collapsed; large OTHER/UNK) with no per-type distributions or confusion rates reported.  
- Weakness: scope limited to CoNLL-2014 and English resources, so “dataset-agnostic” should be qualified as English-focused.  

- Q1: Provide per-type confusion diagnostics from expert study indicating common misassignments and error sources.  
- Q2: Report label sensitivity to choice of tagger/parser/dictionary (e.g., swap spaCy, remove dependencies) and downstream per-type F0.5.  
- Q3: Show whether team rankings and pairwise significance remain stable per error type/operation tier under auto vs. gold references (Kendall’s τ/ρ).  
- Q4: Explain handling of multiple-reference evaluation under exact-edit matching (union-of-edits, conflict resolution).  
- Q5: Detail rule precedence and coverage, and tie-breaking/back-off strategies for ambiguous cases.  
- Q6: Clarify handling of spelling dialect variants and capitalization interactions with Hunspell and potential biases.  

- Additional: release code/rules with license and versioning (spaCy model, UD mapping) and specify whether re-annotated CoNLL-2014 outputs will be shared.  
- Additional: fix malformed references and clean citations.  
- Additional: report per-type frequency distributions and proportion of OTHER/UNK to assess coverage.  
- Additional: include ablation on multi-token edit alignment behavior to contextualize low F0.5 on multi-token phenomena.
