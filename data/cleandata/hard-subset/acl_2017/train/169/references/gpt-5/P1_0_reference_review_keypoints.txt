- Presents a deterministic two-step pipeline (Damerau–Levenshtein aligner + rule-based classifier) to auto-label GEC edits with spans and error types without manual labels.
- Pipeline assigns edits to a dataset-agnostic taxonomy (25 base types; 55 type-operation combos) using automatic linguistic markup.
- Small human study (5 raters, 200 edits) rates assigned types Good/Acceptable in >95%; inter-annotator κ_free=0.724.
- Applied tool to CoNLL-2014 systems for per-type, operation-tier, multi-token, and detection vs. correction analyses.
- Reprocessing references with the automatic aligner yields overall scores statistically indistinguishable from gold; argues M2 scorer inflates performance via dynamic edit boundaries.
- Contribution: practical, deterministic, reproducible tooling enabling per-type evaluation of unannotated GEC outputs without supervised training.
- Contribution: comprehensive retrospective per-type and operation-tier analysis of CoNLL-2014 system outputs revealing actionable system strengths/weaknesses.
- Contribution: empirical claim and critique that auto-reannotation aligns with gold scoring and that M2’s alignment behavior can inflate scores.
- Strength: immediate practical impact—tool can diagnose systems and allow richer shared-task metrics without retraining.
- Strength: deterministic, transparent rules avoid supervised biases and improve explainability and standardization.
- Strength: human validation provides stronger evidence than purely automatic proxies for rule quality.
- Strength: insightful analyses surface meaningful differences across approaches (classifiers vs MT, multi-token weaknesses, detection vs correction).
- Strength: clear separation of alignment and scoring, with empirical tests and critique of M2 behavior.
- Weakness: human validation is small and measures appropriateness not accuracy against established gold error-type corpora.
- Weakness: incomplete disclosure of rule set, decision criteria, and taxonomy mapping hampers reproducibility and auditing of edge cases.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on automatic POS/lemma/dep preprocessing without sensitivity analysis to tagger/parser choice or domain shifts.
- Weakness: no statistical significance or robustness checks reported for per-type analyses, risking overinterpretation of some findings.
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is incremental—the main advance is engineering/standardization rather than new algorithms.
