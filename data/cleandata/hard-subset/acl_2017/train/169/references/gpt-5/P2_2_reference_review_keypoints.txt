- Presents a pipeline to annotate GEC outputs with edit spans and error types using a linguistically-enhanced Damerau–Levenshtein aligner and a new rule-based typing scheme (POS, lemmas, morphology, dependencies, spell dictionary).
- Reprocesses references and hypotheses to evaluate by direct edit comparison with breakdowns by operation (Missing/Unnecessary/Replacement), fine-grained type, multi-token edits, and detection vs. correction.
- Shows auto-reprocessing of gold references yields near-identical overall scores to original gold references with M2 and argues M2 may overestimate performance.
- Small manual evaluation (200 edits, 5 raters) reports ~96% Good/Acceptable judgments for rule-based types; authors plan to release the tool.
- Contribution 1: A practical, deterministic, dataset-agnostic error-typing framework and tool enabling per-type evaluation without supervised training.
- Contribution 2: A comprehensive CoNLL-2014 analysis by error type, operation tier, multi-token edits, and detection vs. correction revealing complementary strengths across systems.
- Contribution 3: A transparent evaluation protocol that separates edit extraction from scoring and shows auto reprocessing doesn’t materially change overall system scores.
- Strength: Fills an unmet need for error-type-specific evaluation with fine-grained, interpretable, and aggregable categories useful to researchers and practitioners.
- Strength: Deterministic, transparent, and dataset-agnostic rules grounded in automatic linguistic markup increase reproducibility and robustness versus ML-based typers.
- Strength: Multi-view evaluation offers actionable insights (neglect of Unnecessary edits, MT vs classifier strengths, weaknesses on complex fluency/multi-token edits, detection vs correction gaps).
- Strength: Sensible scoring setup aligning and typing both refs and hypotheses provides clear TP/FP/FN semantics and avoids hidden dynamic edit boundary optimizations; critiques M2 inflation.
- Strength: Resource contribution—committed release of a standardized annotation and evaluation tool.
- Weakness: Limited validation of typing accuracy—small subjective study, no evaluation against a gold human-typed schema or comparison to existing automatic typers, raising reliability concerns for rare categories.
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on automatic markup without sensitivity analysis to tagger/parser choice; no quantification of error propagation effects.
- Weakness: Claim that auto annotations equal human annotations is overstated—similar overall F0.5 doesn’t validate edit boundaries or type fidelity.
- Weakness: Insufficient methodological detail on the rule set (ordering, precedence, disambiguation) and missing ablation studies to assess component importance.
- Weakness: Scope restricted to CoNLL-2014; lacks cross-dataset validation (e.g., FCE, JFLEG) to demonstrate generality across domains and guidelines.
- Question: How sensitive are per-type F-scores to the choice of tagger/parser or domain-adapted models?
- Question: Is there any evaluation against a human-typed error scheme to estimate precision/recall per category and identify most/least reliable categories?
- Question: Can you detail the rule engine’s ordering, tie-breaking, handling of multi-label ambiguities, and whether a confidence score is produced?
- Question: How often do auto edit boundaries disagree with human boundaries, and what is the effect on per-type evaluation (single-token vs multi-token mismatch stats)?
- Question: Have you applied the tool to other corpora (FCE, JFLEG) and observed similar stability in overall scores and per-type patterns?
- Overall assessment: Originality moderate (aligner prior work; novelty in typing framework), soundness reasonable but limited typing validation, high practical significance, generally clear writing; recommendation leans toward acceptance conditional on releasing the tool and clarifying validation and rule details.
