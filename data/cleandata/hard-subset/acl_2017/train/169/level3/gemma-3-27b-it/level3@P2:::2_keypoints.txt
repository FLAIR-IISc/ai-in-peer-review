- Proposes a novel pipeline for automatic annotation and precision-based evaluation of GEC by error type using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier.  
- Applies the method to CoNLL-2014 submissions and publicly releases an implementation.  
- Enables precision (not just recall) measurement per error type, allowing finer-grained system analysis.  
- Uses a dataset-independent, rule-based classifier relying on automatic POS/lemma markup to improve generalizability.  
- Reports high agreement with human raters (>95% rated Good or Acceptable), supporting the method’s validity.  
- Major weakness: the paper omits a detailed specification of the classification rules, hindering replication and understanding.  
- Evaluation of the classifier is limited and lacks a systematic error analysis explaining “Bad” predictions beyond POS tagging errors.  
- Scope is limited to English; the paper does not address adaptation challenges for other languages or tagsets.  
- Requests: provide a comprehensive description of rule structure and handling of linguistic contexts.  
- Requests: report additional factors and concrete examples behind human-identified “Bad” predictions.  
- Requests: discuss challenges and necessary modifications for multilingual adaptation.  
- Requests: clarify whether statistical significance tests were run on F0.5 scores across systems and report results.  
- Suggests clarifying whether gold vs. auto comparisons include error-type classification beyond edit boundaries.  
- Recommends including raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts (e.g., for Table 6) to better assess performance and significance.  
- Suggests acknowledging prior GEC systems that use error tagging to contextualize novelty.  
- Notes reference formatting/completeness issues (missing page numbers/URLs).  
- Overall conclusion: promising and useful contribution but requires rule transparency and broader/more detailed evaluation before acceptance.
