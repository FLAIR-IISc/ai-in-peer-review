- Paper proposes an automatic pipeline using linguistically-enhanced alignment plus a rule-based classifier to annotate grammatical error types in GEC outputs.  
- Primary contribution: a tool/pipeline for automatically annotating GEC outputs with error-type information to enable fine-grained evaluation.  
- Secondary contribution: a proposed dataset-independent error tagset.  
- Demonstration: re-evaluation of CoNLL-2014 systems using the proposed pipeline.  

Strengths:
- Practical utility: fills a real need by enabling automatic error-type annotation for GEC system analysis.  
- Clear motivation: well-defined problem statement and rationale for moving beyond recall-only evaluation.  
- Human validation: manual evaluation shows >95% agreement between raters and the automatic classifier.  
- Detailed analysis: extensive CoNLL-2014 re-evaluation provides comprehensive per-error-type system comparisons.  

Weaknesses:
- Superficial system description: lacks concrete rule examples, rule counts, complexity, and ordering details.  
- Overemphasis on CoNLL-2014: results section is repetitive and often restates table-obvious findings.  
- Evaluation methodology concerns: relies on rater averages without distributions or a gold-standard test set; test-data composition and coverage are unclear.  
- Vague on edit boundaries: admits “unusual” edit boundaries but gives no examples or impact analysis.  
- Questionable domain independence: rule-based approach and external resources (e.g., Hunspell) plus student-essay corpora limit generalizability.  
- Unclear focus: unclear whether main contribution is the system, tagset, or evaluation methodology.  

Questions for authors:
- Provide detailed description of the rule-based classifier: example rules, total rules, and ordering logic.  
- Report distribution of “bad” ratings and rater disagreement breakdown from human evaluation.  
- Provide test-data composition: proportion of examples per error category in the tagset.  
- Elaborate on examples and impact of “unusual” edit boundaries from alignment.  
- Explain why a new error tagset was developed instead of using/extending existing tagsets (e.g., CoNLL-2014).  

Additional comments:
- Paper contains minor formatting inconsistencies and typos (e.g., citation styles, “M^2” vs “M2”) and needs proofreading.
