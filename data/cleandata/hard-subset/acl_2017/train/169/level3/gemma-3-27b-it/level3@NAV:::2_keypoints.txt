- Presents a promising method for automatic annotation and error-type evaluation of GEC using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier.  
- Demonstrates application to CoNLL-2014 data and provides novel, detailed system-level analyses beyond recall-based metrics.  
- Validation shows high agreement with experts (>95%), indicating strong potential for large-scale automated evaluation.  
- Releases the associated tool, which is a valuable resource for the community.  
- Critical reproducibility flaw: rules used to derive error types are not described, preventing replication.  
- Classifier evaluation is superficial and needs detailed error analysis (e.g., confusion matrix) to identify weaknesses.  
- Study is limited to English with no discussion of adapting to morphologically rich or structurally different languages.  
- Unclear provenance of error categories—whether they follow established taxonomies or are ad-hoc.  
- The method’s handling of discontinuous edits is not addressed and may be problematic.  
- Significance testing setup is unclear, and reliance on F0.5 as the primary discriminatory metric is questionable.  
- Strength: provides a granular, precision-focused evaluation approach that moves beyond recall.  
- Strength: rule-based classifier does not require labeled training data, enabling dataset independence.  
- Strength: method shown to work across multiple CoNLL-2014 systems, demonstrating applicability.  
- Requests from reviewer: provide full rule descriptions, confusion matrix/detailed error analysis, anticipated performance on languages with discontinuous morphology, relation of categories to existing taxonomies, and specifics of significance-test parameters.  
- Additional issues: inconsistent reference formatting, missing page numbers/URLs in citations, and lack of acknowledgement of GEC systems with built-in error tagging.
