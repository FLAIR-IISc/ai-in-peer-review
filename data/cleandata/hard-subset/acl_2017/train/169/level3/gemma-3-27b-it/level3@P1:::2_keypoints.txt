- Contribution: introduces a dataset-independent, linguistically-enhanced alignment plus rule-based classifier to automatically annotate grammatical error types in GEC outputs, enabling precision/F1 by error type.
- Validation: applied to CoNLL-2014 systems and achieved high agreement with human experts (over 95% “Good” or “Acceptable”).
- Strength: enables error-type precision and F-score evaluation, moving beyond recall-only metrics.
- Strength: removes dependence on manually annotated data, lowering annotation cost and enabling rapid evaluation.
- Strength: demonstrates practical scalability and utility via comprehensive analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems and planned tool release.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed, exhaustive description of the rule-based classifier, harming reproducibility and interpretability.
- Weakness: limited error analysis — aggregate ratings dominate and there is no systematic breakdown of why the classifier fails (especially “Bad” cases).
- Weakness: evaluation only on English, raising concerns about cross-linguistic applicability and likely language-specific rules.
- Question: authors must provide the complete set of rules used for error type assignment.
- Question: request for detailed analysis of classifier errors—what specific error types cause “Bad” ratings and are there systematic patterns?
- Question: ask authors to discuss adapting the approach to other languages and what modifications would be required.
- Question: how sensitive are results to choice of linguistic tools (e.g., spaCy); an ablation study is requested.
- Additional concerns: clarify significance testing setup and expand comparison between gold and auto references.
- Additional concerns: acknowledge and compare to existing systems with error-tagging capability and fix reference formatting/citation completeness.
