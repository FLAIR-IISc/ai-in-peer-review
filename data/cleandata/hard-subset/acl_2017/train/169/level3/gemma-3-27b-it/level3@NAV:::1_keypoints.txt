- Proposes an automated annotation pipeline combining linguistically-enhanced alignment with a rule-based error-type classifier to enable fine-grained GEC evaluation.  
- Applies the pipeline to CoNLL-2014 outputs to provide detailed per-error-category system analyses.  
- Practical utility: tool could help teachers, learners, and researchers identify system strengths and weaknesses.  
- Promised tool release increases potential community adoption and reproducibility.  
- Raises important concerns about inconsistencies in existing GEC evaluation metrics (e.g., M2).  
- Core rule-based classifier description is superficial, lacking concrete rules, statistics, and conflict-resolution details.  
- No examples or counts of rule application, making robustness and generalizability unclear.  
- Overemphasis on a large CoNLL-2014 results table with repetitive observations and insufficient interpretation.  
- Evaluation methodology is weak: human ratings were used without creating an independent gold standard for the test sentences.  
- Aggregating rater scores by simple averaging is misleading; distribution of "bad" ratings and disagreements aren’t reported.  
- Missing information on test data composition and error-type distribution limits result interpretability.  
- Claim that “edit boundaries might be unusual” is unsupported by specific examples or impact analysis.  
- Claim of low domain dependence is unsubstantiated given dependence on manual rules, Hunspell vocabularies, and student-essay-only test data.  
- Unclear main contribution—system, tagset, or evaluation methodology—and lacks a clear framing statement of novelty and significance.  
- Numerous typos and formatting errors in references reduce the paper’s professionalism.  
- Requests for authors: provide 5–10 concrete rule examples and conflict-resolution details.  
- Requests for authors: report the distribution of error types within the CoNLL-2014 test outputs.  
- Requests for authors: give a detailed breakdown of "bad" human ratings by error type and nature of disagreements.  
- Requests for authors: explain steps taken to mitigate rater bias in evaluation.  
- Requests for authors: elaborate on limitations handling non-student-essay domains.
