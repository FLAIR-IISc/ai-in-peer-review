- Presents an automatic pipeline using linguistically-enhanced alignment plus a rule-based error-typing framework to annotate grammatical edits in corrected text.  
- Applies the pipeline to CoNLL-2014 outputs to enable per-error-type evaluation of GEC systems.  

Strengths:
- Practical utility: enables automatic, fine-grained evaluation and learner/teacher feedback beyond recall-based metrics.  
- Dataset-independent aim: rule-based classifier designed to avoid corpus-specific bias and reduce need for manual annotation.  
- Detailed CoNLL-2014 analysis: provides previously unavailable error-type breakdowns across systems.  

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient classifier description: lacks rules, examples, rule counts, complexity, ordering, and conflict-resolution details.  
- Overemphasis on tables: paper reiterates tabular CoNLL-2014 results rather than focusing discussion on key findings and implications.  
- Questionable evaluation: human raters judged pipeline outputs (not an independent gold standard); no full report of “Bad” rating counts, undermining the 95% claim.  
- Limited data characterization: missing distribution of error categories in the test data and representation of rare error types.  
- Vague/unsubstantiated claims: “unusual” edit boundaries and lower domain dependence are asserted without concrete examples or evidence; rule-based tools and Hunspell are domain-dependent.  
- Unclear motivation/contribution: unclear whether main contribution is a GEC system, a new tagset, or an evaluation framework.  

Questions for authors:
- Provide detailed description of the rule-based classifier: example rules, number/complexity, ordering, and conflict handling.  
- Report the distribution/count of “Bad” ratings from human evaluation.  
- Provide the distribution of error types in the CoNLL-2014 test data used.  
- Give examples of the “unusual” edit boundaries and discuss their impact on evaluation.  
- Explain why a new error tagset was created instead of using established tagsets (e.g., CoNLL-2014, Nicholls) and what limitations motivated it.  

Additional comment:
- Paper contains typos and inconsistent reference formatting (e.g., “c.f.” → “cf.”, M2 notation); needs careful proofreading.
