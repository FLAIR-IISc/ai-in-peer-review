- Proposal of an annotation-free framework that annotates and evaluates GEC outputs by error type, yielding precision and recall per type.
- Uses linguistically enhanced alignment plus a rule-based, dataset-agnostic classifier to assign error tags and analyze multi-token edits.
- Demonstrated on CoNLL-2014 systems to reveal type-specific strengths and weaknesses across diverse system families.
- Practical contribution: fills a long-standing gap by providing precision- and recall-by-error-type for diagnostic GEC evaluation.
- Broad empirical analysis including operation-tier and multi-token performance shows applicability across MT, classifiers, and rule systems.
- Rule-based error tagger achieved high expert acceptability (~95% Good/Acceptable) in human validation.
- Enables actionable, type-specific diagnostics (e.g., DET vs VERB:FORM) that support model development.
- Annotation independence and dataset-agnostic design allow reuse without domain-specific training.
- Proper handling of multi-token edits and clear separation of alignment vs evaluation exposes weaknesses in dynamic-boundary scorers.
- Validation on CoNLL-2014 outputs demonstrates broad applicability and complementary strengths across systems.
- Reproducibility concern: rules and alignment/preprocessing dependencies (tagger, lemmatizer, parser, merging) are under-specified.
- Evaluation depth concern: classifier error analysis is cursory, rating bias and noisy boundaries are untested, and gold vs auto comparisons are limited.
- Generalization concern: evaluated only on English; discontinuous spans and morphologically rich languages are not addressed.
- Statistical/reporting gaps: unclear significance-testing setup, missing per-type TP/FP/TN/FN counts, and lack of confidence intervals or warnings for small per-type samples.
- Interpretation limits: some system "failures" may reflect design choices or data/training constraints rather than evaluation flaws; multi-token results need deeper analysis.
- Question: will you release full rule set, code, and exact alignment/markup configurations, and report stability to these choices?
- Question: can you provide per-type TP/FP/TN/FN counts, confidence intervals, and clarify which metric was used in significance testing (F0.5?)?
- Question: how will discontinuous edits be handled and how will the method be adapted beyond English?
- Question: can you rerun the human study with manual boundaries and finer-grained rating scales?
- Question: can you compare automatic vs gold classification (not just boundary matching) to substantiate equivalence claims?
