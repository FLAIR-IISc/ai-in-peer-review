- Proposes an automatic pipeline to annotate GEC system hypotheses with explicit edit spans and error types using linguistically enhanced alignment plus a deterministic rule-based classifier.
- Enables per-error-type precision and recall (not just recall), analysis of multi-token edits, and separation of detection vs. correction.
- Validates the classifier with a small human study (≥95% Good/Acceptable claimed) and applies the method to all CoNLL-2014 systems for broad per-type analysis.
- Shows that automatically reprocessed references yield comparable overall F0.5 scores to gold references.
- Promises a practical tool release to standardize error-type evaluation and enable finer-grained system analysis.
- Strength: fills gap by enabling precision-aware, per-type evaluation and granular operation/linguistic-tier breakdowns for diagnosing systems.
- Strength: dataset-agnostic, transparent, deterministic rule set relying on automatic markup (POS/lemma/parse/stemming/Hunspell) improves interpretability and consistency.
- Strength: demonstrates broad applicability and scalability across CoNLL-2014 outputs, uncovering actionable insights (e.g., issues with Unnecessary edits, multi-token weaknesses).
- Strength: separates alignment from evaluation by fixing edit spans before scoring, avoiding some dynamic-boundary metric pathologies and clarifying TP/FP/FN attribution.
- Strength: provides useful analyses beyond headline scores (operation-tier breakdowns, per-type tables, multi-token and detection analyses) that suggest directions for hybrid systems.
- Weakness: rule inventory, precedence, and decision logic are under-specified, harming reproducibility and extensibility.
- Weakness: sensitivity to preprocessing (POS/lemma/parse) is noted but not systematically analyzed; many Bad cases attributed to POS errors without quantification.
- Weakness: choice and design of the 25 categories (55 operation/type combos) and their mapping to existing schemes (M2/ERRANT/FCE) are unclear, limiting comparability and coverage assessment.
- Weakness: human validation is small (200 edits), conflates boundary and type errors, and lacks confusion analyses, ablations, and robustness checks across taggers/parsers.
- Weakness: demonstrated only for English; method may struggle with language-specific morphology, discontinuous edits, separable verbs, clitics, and non-space-delimited scripts.
- Weakness: statistical testing details (resampling unit, stratification, metric, per-team vs. pooled) are insufficiently specified.
- Weakness: per-type tables omit raw TP/FP/FN counts and do not flag sparse categories, reducing interpretability and reliability of "best" results.
- Weakness: SPELL category depends on a specific GB Hunspell dictionary without discussion of dictionary bias.
- Q: Provide the full rule set, precedence/exception handling, conflict resolution, and dependency features used in implementation.
- Q: Report exact preprocessing models/versions (spaCy/UD), quantify performance changes with alternative taggers/parsers, and measure how often preprocessing errors cause misclassification by type.
- Q: Explain how error categories were selected and provide a mapping to existing annotation schemes (M2/FCE/ERRANT), noting mismatches and trade-offs.
- Q: When reprocessing references, report per-category agreement between automatic classifier types and human labels.
- Q: Specify bootstrap testing details: resampling unit (sentence vs. edit), number of samples, metric used, and whether tests are per team or pooled; include confidence intervals.
- Q: Add TP/FP/FN counts per team/type, flag categories with <20 gold instances, and provide detection-vs-correction breakdowns by type.
- Q: Describe what's required to port the approach to other languages and how to handle non-contiguous edits, clitics, or script differences; any preliminary non-English results?
- Q: Rerun a subset of the human study with manual boundaries fixed to test whether “Bad” judgments remain and provide confusion analyses by type.
- Comment: Discuss how internal error labels from GEC pipelines (e.g., classifier-based subsystems) relate to this method to strengthen novelty claims.
- Comment: Publish tool, versioning, configuration files, and dictionary versions (SPELL) to ensure replicability.
- Minor: fix citation formatting (authors, venues, years, URLs/page ranges).
