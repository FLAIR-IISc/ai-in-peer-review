- Paper proposes a practical pipeline to auto-annotate GEC outputs with edit spans and 55 error-type labels, enabling per-type precision/recall and F0.5.
- Method: linguistically enhanced edit extraction (Damerau–Levenshtein + POS/lemma) plus a deterministic, dataset-agnostic rule-based classifier.
- Contribution 1: Standardized evaluation tool/protocol for per-type scoring on unannotated hypotheses, supporting multi-token and detection-vs-correction analyses.
- Contribution 2: Large-scale per-error-type analysis across all CoNLL-2014 systems, revealing complementary system strengths/weaknesses and persistent challenges (e.g., multi-token edits).
- Contribution 3: Deterministic, dataset-independent error-type classifier with high expert acceptability (>95% Good/Acceptable on a 200-edit sample).
- Strength: Enables per-type precision/recall on hypotheses, revealing insights not visible in aggregate scores (e.g., DET/VERB:FORM differences, unnecessary deletions).
- Strength: Rule-based, transparent, and interpretable classifier that avoids dataset-specific training biases and shows high human acceptability despite noisy boundaries.
- Strength: Broad applicability demonstrated across diverse CoNLL-2014 system architectures and detailed diagnostic breakdowns (operation tier, multi-token, detection vs. correction).
- Strength: Simple, modular scoring via fixed-span edit comparison; auto-processed references yield results comparable to gold and clarify M2 metric inflation.
- Weakness: Reproducibility hindered by missing rule inventory/pseudocode for mapping markup to 55 error codes (precedence, conflicts, MWE handling).
- Weakness: Lack of systematic sensitivity analysis to POS/tagger/parser errors, alignment quality, Hunspell choice, and other preprocessing decisions.
- Weakness: Human evaluation limited (200 English-only edits), raters told to ignore boundaries, no per-type/confusion/size stratified analysis, and weak evidence linking "Bad" labels to POS errors.
- Weakness: Evaluated only on English; no discussion of adapting inventory/rules to morphologically rich or non-configurational languages or handling discontinuous edits/reordering.
- Weakness: Statistical reporting under-specified (bootstrap unit/statistic unclear), missing TP/FP/FN counts and per-type support sizes, reducing interpretability of scores.
- Question: Please provide the full rule inventory or pseudocode, including precedence, tie-breaking, and dependencies on spaCy/UD/lemmatizer/stemmer/speller.
- Question: How does per-type performance change with different taggers/parsers or with gold POS, and what fraction of “Bad” ratings are due to tagging vs. rule vs. boundary errors?
- Question: Can the alignment/typing represent non-contiguous/discontinuous edits (e.g., separable particles, reordering), and if not, how to extend it?
- Question: What was the resampling unit and statistic for the bootstrap tests, and were both boundary-only and boundary+type matches tested?
- Question: Will you release TP/FP/FN and support sizes per system/category, indicate low-support categories, and state any filters applied?
- Question: What are concrete steps to adapt the inventory and rules to other languages, and any preliminary non-English experiments?
- Additional: Acknowledge that some GEC systems output internal error tags; emphasize the contribution is standardizing evaluation across systems without those outputs.
- Additional: Provide tool URL, license, environment details, and explicit dependency versions (e.g., spaCy) to ensure reproducibility.
- Additional: Improve and fix references, venues, pages, URLs, and formatting inconsistencies.
- Overall assessment: Promising and practically useful contribution for per-type GEC analysis but needs rule specifications, clearer statistics/reporting, and sensitivity/cross-lingual work before being ready.
