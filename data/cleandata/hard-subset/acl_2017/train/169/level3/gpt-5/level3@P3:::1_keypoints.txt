- Summary: Presents a pipeline that extracts edits via linguistically-enhanced alignment and assigns error types with a deterministic rule-based classifier, validated by a small 200-edit, 5-rater study and applied to re-annotate CoNLL-2014 for per-type and operation-tier analyses.

- Contribution: Provides a practical, training-free per-error-type evaluation tool for GEC enabling fine-grained analysis for researchers, teachers, and learners.

- Contribution: Separates alignment from scoring and normalizes hypothesis/reference annotations for transparent edit matching and detailed analyses (operation tier, multi-token, detection vs. correction).

- Contribution: Offers a large-scale per-type comparative analysis of CoNLL-2014 system outputs showing system specialization across error categories.

- Strength: Enables clear, actionable fine-grained comparisons (per-type, per-operation, multi-token) that current metrics lack.

- Strength: Uses transparent, deterministic, rule-based classification that is traceable and debuggable, avoiding black-box bias.

- Strength: Reprocessing references with the same pipeline simplifies evaluation and the bootstrap shows no significant F0.5 difference between gold and auto references.

- Strength: Highlights under-reported dimensions like detection vs. correction and multi-token performance, refining understanding of system behavior.

- Strength: Tool has strong potential community impact if released, standardizing per-type reporting and aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness: Rule-based classifier is under-specified—no inventory, examples, precedence/conflict policy, or UNK/fallback statistics are provided.

- Weakness: Evaluation design lacks validation against an independent gold with fixed boundaries and labels, conflating boundary and type errors and precluding per-type P/R.

- Weakness: Paper over-emphasizes CoNLL-2014 table narration and repeats observations, crowding out critical method details and rationale.

- Weakness: Claims of dataset/domain independence are unsubstantiated given reliance on English POS/lemma/UD mappings, GB Hunspell, and handcrafted rules without cross-domain or cross-language validation.

- Weakness: New tagset motivation and compatibility are unclear—no explanation why existing schemes are inadequate or mappings to Nicholls/CoNLL are provided.

- Weakness: Overall motivation and positioning are ambiguous—unclear whether main contribution is the pipeline, the tagset, or the evaluation protocol.

- Weakness: “Edit boundary” caveat is vague and needs definition, examples, quantification of mismatch rates, and impact on labeling/scoring.

- Question: How many rules per category, what are representative rule templates, and what is the exact matching/precedence/conflict-resolution scheme?

- Question: What fraction of edits fall into UNK or OTHER by corpus, and which categories are most error-prone per the rater study?

- Question: Did you aggregate rater results via averaging per-rater percentages or micro-averaging over items; please report CIs/SEs and “Bad” distribution by category/operation.

- Question: Can you release a small gold standard (the 200 sentences) with fixed boundaries and gold labels to report per-type P/R and boundary error rates?

- Question: What is the effect of swapping Hunspell-GB for other lexicons or applying the classifier to non-essay domains or different English varieties?

- Question: Why introduce a new tagset—please provide mappings to Nicholls/CoNLL, design principles, granularity rationale, and known limitations.

- Question: Define “unusual” edit boundaries, quantify auto vs. gold boundary mismatch rates, and show effect sizes on P/R/F.

- Additional: Reorganize exposition to move method details (alignment parameters, rule inventory, tagset rationale) earlier and move large per-type tables to the appendix.

- Additional: Report counts of edits per category in the 200-sentence sample and in CoNLL-2014 and indicate coverage of categories.

- Additional: Fix notation/style issues (cf., M^2 consistency, capitalization, references) and ensure ACL-style formatting.

- Additional: Improve reproducibility by releasing code, rule files, tagset definition and mappings, specifying spaCy/UD/Hunspell versions, and stating license.
