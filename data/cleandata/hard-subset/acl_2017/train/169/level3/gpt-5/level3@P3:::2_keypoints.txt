- Proposes an automatic, dataset-agnostic pipeline to extract and type GEC edits via linguistically enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier, enabling per-error-type precision as well as recall.  
- Demonstrates method on CoNLL-2014 system outputs and reports the first broad per-type analysis across systems, including multi-token edits and detection vs. correction.  
- Small human validation study rates predicted types Good/Acceptable in ≥95% of cases.  

- Contribution: practical pipeline for automatic extraction and typing of edits to enable per-type precision/recall/F0.5 and size-based analysis.  
- Contribution: empirical study showing applicability across many systems and error types, with human validation of tag quality.  
- Contribution: an evaluation protocol separating edit extraction from scoring to facilitate comparisons and new analyses.  

- Strength: moves the field beyond recall-only per-type reports by enabling precision and F0.5 on hypotheses.  
- Strength: dataset independence and rule-based typing avoid training-data bias and permit use on legacy outputs.  
- Strength: explicitly handles multi-token edits and fixed spans, enabling clearer size-based analysis.  
- Strength: broad coverage across many CoNLL-2014 systems and many error types, surfacing informative findings (e.g., systems’ relative strengths).  
- Strength: human validation with reasonable inter-rater agreement supports tag quality claims.  

- Weakness: rules mapping edits to types are insufficiently described and the full ruleset is not released, threatening reproducibility.  
- Weakness: classifier evaluation lacks detailed error analysis, ablations, and systematic evidence for claimed failure modes.  
- Weakness: demonstrated only for English with no multilingual adaptation plan; potential issues with discontinuous edits, clitics, and morphology.  
- Weakness: statistical-testing setup is underspecified (what metrics/unit of resampling, per-type testing, multiple-comparison control).  
- Weakness: reporting omissions (no TP/FP/FN/TN counts, unflagged small per-type sample sizes) and lack of context for multi-token/system design differences.  

- Request: release the complete ruleset with exact decision order, feature sets, thresholds, and alignment/merging parameters for replication.  
- Request: report sensitivity/ablation studies showing dependence on alignment parameters, tokenizer, POS/lemma/parser choices.  
- Request: explain how error categories were designed and how they align with or diverge from prior schemes (e.g., FCE, CoNLL).  
- Request: clarify significance-testing details (which metrics tested, unit of resampling, per-type vs. overall tests, multiple-test control).  
- Request: provide per-type TP/FP/FN (and ideally TN) counts for key tables to assess sparsity and enable recomputation of intervals/measures.  
- Request: re-run or extend human study with manually curated boundaries and a finer rating scheme (or pairwise comparisons) to quantify boundary bias and rating granularity.  
- Request: outline/adapt the method for languages with discontinuous/non-projective edits and demonstrate on at least one additional language or give a concrete plan.  
- Request: provide systematic analysis of rater choices across Good/Acceptable/Bad and detailed confusion matrices for Bad cases.  
- Request: disaggregate multi-token edit results by pattern (insertions, deletions, reorderings) and relate to system architectures/training.  
- Request: verify whether zero scores in some categories reflect systems not attempting those operations versus evaluation misses.  

- Additional comment: clarify whether gold vs. auto comparisons evaluate type classification consistency and not just boundary alignment.  
- Additional comment: acknowledge that some GEC pipelines can expose internal error tags and discuss implications.  
- Additional comment: fix reference formatting and add missing page numbers/URLs.
