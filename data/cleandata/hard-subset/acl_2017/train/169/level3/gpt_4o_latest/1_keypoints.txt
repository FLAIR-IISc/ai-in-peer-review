- Introduces a rule-based, dataset-independent method to annotate GEC outputs with edit spans and error types.  
- Relies solely on automatically obtained linguistic features (POS tags, lemmas), avoiding labeled training data.  
- Provides a publicly available tool and a fine-grained evaluation of CoNLL-2014 GEC system outputs.  

- Practical utility for teachers, learners, and researchers by enabling actionable, error-type-level analysis.  
- Dataset-agnostic design offers adaptability and scalability compared to ML approaches requiring domain-specific training.  
- Deterministic rules ensure transparent, consistent annotations and avoid human/ML annotation biases.  

- Paper lacks sufficient detail on the specific rules, their design rationale, and linguistic foundations.  
- Evaluation methodology is weak: gold-standard creation is under-documented, raters averaged (subjectivity concerns), and test-data details are sparse.  
- Manual rules and test data likely introduce domain-specific biases, undermining claims of dataset independence.  
- Introducing a new error-category set is insufficiently justified given existing tagsets.  
- Unclear primary motivation — focus on system, categories, or CoNLL-2014 evaluation is ambiguous.  
- Presentation issues (typos, unclear notations, inconsistent references) reduce readability and professionalism.  

- Requests more details on rule design and linguistic principles guiding them.  
- Asks justification and evidence for adopting a new error-category set over existing frameworks.  
- Seeks demonstrations of framework performance across diverse datasets to address domain-dependency concerns.  

- Recommendation: major revision required to address methodological clarity, evaluation rigor, and presentation before acceptance.
