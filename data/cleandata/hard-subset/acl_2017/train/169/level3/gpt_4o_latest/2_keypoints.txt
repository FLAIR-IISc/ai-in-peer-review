Contrib 1: Proposes a dataset-independent, rule-based classifier that removes the need for labeled training data and yields deterministic, transparent error-type labels.  
Contrib 2: Enables detailed, granular error-type evaluation including multi-token errors previously underexplored.  
Contrib 3: Human validation shows >95% of classifier outputs rated "Good" or "Acceptable," indicating high reliability.  
Contrib 4: Applied the method to CoNLL-2014 systems to provide the first detailed error-type analysis for that shared task.

Strength 1: Significant advance in GEC evaluation by enabling precision-by-error-type without manual annotation.  
Strength 2: Comprehensive evaluation across many GEC systems, revealing strengths and weaknesses by error type.  
Strength 3: Robust handling of multi-token errors, a challenging aspect of GEC.  
Strength 4: High human approval rates lend credibility to the method.

Weakness 1: Rules for deriving error types are insufficiently described, harming reproducibility and adaptation.  
Weakness 2: Classifier evaluation lacks detailed error analysis, especially for cases labeled "Bad" or "Appropriate."  
Weakness 3: Evaluation limited to English; adaptability to languages with discontinuous spans (e.g., German separable verbs) is unclear.  
Weakness 4: Gold vs. auto reference comparison focuses on boundary alignment but omits effects on classification accuracy and uses poorly documented significance testing.  
Weakness 5: Missing raw TP/FP/TN/FN metrics, limiting interpretability of system comparisons.

Question 1: Please provide a detailed description of the classification rules and the linguistic principles behind them.  
Question 2: How does the method handle discontinuous error spans (e.g., German separable verbs) and other morphological complexities?  
Question 3: What steps are needed to adapt the approach to non-English languages, and have you tested other datasets?  
Question 4: Elaborate the methodology and robustness of the significance testing used in gold vs. auto reference comparisons.  
Question 5: Provide raw TP, FP, TN, and FN values for evaluated systems to enable deeper comparison.

Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on addressing rule transparency, expanded classifier evaluation, and clarification of cross-lingual generalizability and metrics.
