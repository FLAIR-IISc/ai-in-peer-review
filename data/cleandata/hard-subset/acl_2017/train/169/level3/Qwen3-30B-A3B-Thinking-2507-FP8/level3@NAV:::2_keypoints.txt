- Proposes a novel method for automatic GEC evaluation by error type, adding precision metrics in addition to recall.  
- Introduces a linguistically-enhanced alignment to extract edits and a rule-based classifier to categorize errors without manual annotation.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across multiple GEC systems with high human agreement (95% "Good" or "Acceptable") on automatic labels.  
- Primary contribution: a dataset-independent evaluation framework enabling detailed error-type analysis for GEC systems.  
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems revealing nuanced strengths and weaknesses across 24 error categories.  
- Strength: handles multi-token errors without requiring manual annotation.  
- Strength: independent from labeled training data, increasing applicability.  
- Strength: evaluation across multiple systems shows practical utility for GEC research.  
- Major weakness: rules for deriving error types are unspecified, preventing replication.  
- Weakness: error categories lack justification and connection to prior work or linguistic validity.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to English despite claimed generalizability to other languages.  
- Weakness: classifier evaluation lacks detailed error analysis and does not explain causes of "Bad" ratings.  
- Weakness: paper omits raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts and provides an unclear significance-testing setup.  
- Weakness: fails to distinguish between system design limitations and evaluation failures, especially for multi-token and discontinuous edits.  
- Conclusion: promising approach but requires substantial revisions on reproducibility and language generalization before acceptance.
