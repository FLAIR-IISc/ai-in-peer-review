- Introduces a rule-based, dataset-independent error-typing framework for GEC that requires no labeled training data.  
- Automatically annotates GEC system outputs with error types, enabling detailed error-type analysis.  
- Provides a comprehensive error-type analysis of all CoNLL-2014 systems across 25 categories.

- Addresses a critical gap in GEC evaluation by enabling granular analysis of system strengths and weaknesses.  
- Offers a transparent, interpretable approach with validation showing 95% of predicted types rated “Good” or “Acceptable.”  
- Produces actionable insights about complementary system strengths that could guide future system combination or development.

- Paper gives only a superficial description of the rule set, lacking concrete examples, counts, complexity, and ordering details.  
- Classifier evaluation is flawed: ratings were done on system output without an independent gold standard and aggregation methods obscure bad ratings.  
- The claim of dataset independence is unsubstantiated since tools like Hunspell and manual rules may be domain- or language-dependent and testing is limited to student essays.

- Provide concrete examples of rules, their counts, complexity, ordering, and handling of edge cases.  
- Explain how evaluation bias from using system output (rather than an independent gold standard) and erroneous edit boundaries was addressed.  
- Share the full distribution of rater scores (not just aggregated percentages) to assess rater consistency.  
- Clarify or demonstrate how the framework performs on other domains or text types beyond student essays.
