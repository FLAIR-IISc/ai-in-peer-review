- Proposal: dataset-independent, rule-based framework to annotate GEC system outputs with error types requiring no labeled training data.  
- Contribution: detailed error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 GEC systems previously impossible due to lack of annotated system outputs.  
- Contribution: practical tool for fine-grained error analysis to help teachers and learners identify specific error patterns.  
- Strength: enables detailed, system-specific error analyses that reveal strengths and weaknesses important for practitioners.  
- Strength: rule-based classifier is motivated as avoiding machine-learning domain dependence.  
- Strength: human evaluation reports 95%+ agreement with expert ratings, supporting reliability of predicted error types.  
- Strength: demonstrates concrete system differences (e.g., CAMB excels at DET errors; CUUI at NOUN:NUM).  
- Weakness: classifier description is poor—insufficient detail on rules, examples, counts, complexity, and ordering.  
- Weakness: paper overemphasizes CoNLL-2014 with repetitive tables that add little deeper insight.  
- Weakness: human evaluation methodology is flawed—raters judged system output rather than creating an independent gold standard.  
- Weakness: aggregation by averaging percentages is misleading; distribution of “bad” ratings across raters is not reported.  
- Weakness: claim of “dataset independence” is unsubstantiated because rules rely on POS tagging and Hunspell and evaluation used only student essays.  
- Weakness: unclear motivation for a new 25-category error tagset versus adopting existing standards like CoNLL-2014.  
- Weakness: claim that “edit boundaries might be unusual” lacks precise definition or examples and unquantified impact.  
- Technical shortcoming: no explanation of how rules interact or resolve conflicts.  
- Technical shortcoming: 25-category taxonomy appears overly granular without justification.  
- Technical shortcoming: multi-token error analysis is underdeveloped and does not explain why MT approaches perform better.  
- Technical shortcoming: disproportionate emphasis on CoNLL-2014 results despite method’s general applicability.  
- Technical shortcoming: multiple typos and formatting inconsistencies suggest poor proofreading.  
- Question: provide concrete examples demonstrating classifier operation and handling of ambiguous cases.  
- Question: explain how the error tagset differs from existing standards and why this design was chosen.  
- Question: how is domain dependence from POS tagging and Hunspell addressed?  
- Question: provide the full rule set and implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Question: supply evidence quantifying the claim that “edit boundaries might be unusual” and its effect on classification.
