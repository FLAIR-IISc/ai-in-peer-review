1. Proposes a linguistically-enhanced alignment algorithm and rule-based classifier for automatic annotation and evaluation of GEC error types.  
2. Approach extracts multi-token edits from parallel original and corrected sentences without manual annotation.  
3. Demonstrates the method on CoNLL-2014 data and analyzes system error-type performance.  
4. Introduces a novel evaluation method enabling per-error-type measurement including precision as well as recall.  
5. Presents an automatic rule-based classifier to label system hypothesis corrections with error types.  
6. Provides detailed error-type analyses comparing strengths and weaknesses of different GEC systems.  
7. Evaluation method yields more granular and effective measurement of system performance by error type.  
8. Automatic annotation reduces reliance on costly manual error labeling.  
9. The paper’s detailed analysis highlights system-specific performance patterns.  
10. Rules for deriving error types are not described, harming reproducibility.  
11. Evaluation is limited to English; no discussion on adapting the approach to other languages.  
12. Classifier evaluation lacks in-depth error analysis and actionable improvement directions.  
13. Requests: provide detailed rule descriptions and development process, plans for multilingual adaptation, and more concrete classifier error analysis and improvement guidance.
