- Proposes a method for automatic annotation and evaluation of GEC error types using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier, independent of manual annotation and handling multi-token errors.  
- Applies the approach to CoNLL-2014 system outputs and provides detailed system-level error-type analyses.  
- Contribution: introduces a novel evaluation method for GEC by error type that includes precision as well as recall.  
- Contribution: provides automated annotation of edits with explicit spans and assigned error types.  
- Contribution: enables detailed per-error-type analysis including precision, recall, and F-score.  
- Strength: demonstrates broad applicability across multiple systems.  
- Strength: automatically assigned tags receive high human approval, indicating effectiveness.  
- Strength: yields improved, fine-grained evaluation that helps identify system strengths and weaknesses.  
- Weakness: rules for deriving error types are not described, threatening reproducibility.  
- Weakness: classifier evaluation lacks in-depth error analysis and concrete improvement directions.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to English and do not discuss adaptation to other languages.  
- Question: please provide a detailed description of the rules and how they were developed.  
- Question: how will the approach be adapted or generalized to languages other than English?  
- Question: provide a more detailed classifier performance analysis, including error analysis and suggestions for improvement.  
- Additional: minor reference formatting and missing citation details; reviewer finds the approach promising but not ready until rules are described and multilingual generalization is addressed.
