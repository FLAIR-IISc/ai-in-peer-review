- Summary: Proposes automatic annotation and evaluation of GEC error types via linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based, dataset-independent classifier.  
- Contribution: Enables fine-grained comparison of GEC systems through automatic error-type annotation.  
- Contribution: Introduces a linguistically-enhanced alignment algorithm to extract edits between original and corrected sentences.  
- Contribution: Presents a rule-based, dataset-independent classifier relying on automatic markup (POS tags, lemmas).  
- Strength: Practical application for teachers and learners to compare GEC systems at a fine-grained level.  
- Strength: Provides a detailed evaluation of GEC system performance on CoNLL-2014.  
- Weakness: System description is superficial, lacking examples, rule counts, complexity, and ordering details.  
- Weakness: Overemphasizes CoNLL-2014 results and repeats facts already visible in tables.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses rater judgments on system output instead of an independent gold standard for the 200 test sentences.  
- Weakness: Aggregation of rater scores is misleading—averages reported without distributions of "bad" ratings.  
- Weakness: No information about test data composition, number of error categories, or coverage of categories.  
- Weakness: Vague claim about "unusual" edit boundaries—needs precise definition, examples, and discussion of impact.  
- Weakness: Overall motivation is unclear and important details are omitted.  
- Question: Provide detailed core rule set information, including examples, counts, complexity, and ordering.  
- Question: Justify creating a new error tagset and explain why existing tagsets (Nicholls, CoNLL-2014) were not used.  
- Question: Provide full test-data composition details, including number and coverage of error categories.  
- Question: Define and exemplify unusual edit boundaries and discuss their potential impact on results.  
- Question: Clarify the main contribution—new system, new tagset, or new evaluation methodology.
