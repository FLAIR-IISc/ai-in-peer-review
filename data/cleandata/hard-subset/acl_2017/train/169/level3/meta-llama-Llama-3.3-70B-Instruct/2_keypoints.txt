- Novel evaluation approach for GEC systems enabling error-type-level assessment of recall and precision.  
- Thorough comparison of five methods to increase robustness of parsing with the English Resource Grammar (ERG).  
- Introduction of a hybrid approach combining precision grammar and probabilistic parsing.  
- Enables fine-grained analysis of GEC performance to better identify system strengths and weaknesses.  
- Provides valuable insights into trade-offs between different techniques for ERG robustness.  
- Hybrid approach shows promise in balancing precision and robustness.  
- Rules for deriving error types are not described, hindering replication and adaptation.  
- Classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and guidance for improvement.  
- Evaluation was performed only for English; cross-lingual applicability is unclear.  
- How will the authors address language dependence of their approach?  
- Can the authors provide details on the rules for deriving error types and their development?  
- How do the authors plan to improve classifier evaluation and provide more thorough error analysis?
