- Summary: Proposes a rule-based method and new tagset to automatically annotate edits in original/corrected sentence pairs using linguistic features, validated by human raters and applied to CoNLL-2014 outputs.
- Contribution: Provides a methodology for fine-grained comparative analysis of GEC systems to reveal error-type-specific strengths and weaknesses.
- Contribution: Offers a detailed, extensive analysis of CoNLL-2014 system outputs as a case study and community resource.
- Strength: High practical utility for the GEC community by enabling detailed, multi-faceted comparisons beyond aggregate scores.
- Strength: Enables novel analyses (e.g., detection vs. correction, multi-token edits) that standard metrics obscure.
- Weakness: Lacks methodological detail and reproducibility—rule set is a black box with no examples, counts, ordering, or conflict-resolution strategy.
- Weakness: Motivation and justification are unclear—uncertain whether the main contribution is the system, the tagset, or the evaluation, and no rationale for replacing existing tagsets.
- Weakness: Classifier evaluation is flawed—rater judgments are not a substitute for an independent gold standard, averaging percentages is misleading, and “Bad” rating distributions are unreported.
- Weakness: The claim of dataset-independence is unsubstantiated since the system depends on domain-/language-specific tools and is evaluated only on student essays.
- Weakness: Poor presentation—overlarge, repetitive tables and frequent typos/formatting errors waste space that should detail the core methodology.
