- Summary: Proposes automatic annotation of GEC edits via linguistically-enhanced alignment plus a rule-based classifier and applies it to CoNLL-2014 system outputs for error-type-level evaluation.
- Contribution 1: Releases a tool to label GEC edits with fine-grained error types, enabling per-category performance analysis.
- Contribution 2: Provides a comprehensive re-evaluation of CoNLL-2014 systems, revealing which architectures excel at which error categories and common weaknesses like multi-token edits.
- Strength 1: Well-motivated work that fills a clear need in GEC evaluation and can guide targeted research improvements.
- Strength 2: Strong empirical demonstration with insightful results (e.g., MT systems better at multi-token edits; many teams failed on "Unnecessary" tokens).
- Strength 3: Human evaluation (5 experts, 200 edits) found >95% of classifier labels "Good" or "Acceptable," supporting label reliability.
- Weakness 1: Core methodology (rule-based classifier) is described superficially—no example rules, rule counts, complexity, or conflict-resolution details—making reproduction difficult.
- Weakness 2: Classifier evaluation is methodologically weak: raters judged appropriateness of system labels rather than comparing to an independent gold standard, and aggregation of rater scores is unconventional.
- Weakness 3: Design choices are unjustified—introduces a new 25-category tagset without comparing to existing schemes and overclaims dataset independence despite reliance on domain-varying tools (spaCy, Hunspell).
- Weakness 4: Paper overemphasizes CoNLL-2014 results at the expense of detailing the method and justifying choices.
- Question 1: Provide representative classifier rules and explain rule ordering/conflict resolution (e.g., for VERB:TENSE vs VERB:SVA vs VERB:INFL).
- Question 2: Justify the new 25-category tagset and show how it maps to or improves on existing taxonomies like CoNLL-2014.
- Question 3: Clarify the claim of dataset independence and report any robustness tests on out-of-domain text given dependencies on POS/tagger/dictionary tools.
- Question 4: Do you plan to create a manually annotated gold test set to measure the classifier’s absolute accuracy with standard metrics?
