- Two-stage method: linguistically-enhanced alignment extracts edits, then a rule-based, dataset-independent classifier assigns error types, enabling per-error-type precision/recall and F-scores; validated by a human study and applied to CoNLL-2014 across 25 categories.

- Contribution: introduces a comprehensive per-error-type GEC evaluation pipeline that adds precision to prior recall-only analyses.

- Contribution: proposes a dataset-independent, rule-based classifier using automatic linguistic markup (POS, lemmas) that requires no labeled training data.

- Contribution: provides an in-depth, system-level analysis of CoNLL-2014 outputs revealing complementary strengths and weaknesses across architectures.

- Strength: significant advance by enabling fine-grained precision and recall measurement for specific error types, uncovering insights invisible to monolithic scores.

- Strength: broad applicability demonstrated across diverse CoNLL-2014 systems and practical portability due to no need for annotated training data; handles multi-token edits.

- Strength: high-quality automatic annotations with 96.3% of assigned categories judged "Good" or "Acceptable" in human evaluation.

- Weakness: complete omission of the classifier rules and insufficient description of category design makes the work non-reproducible and opaque.

- Weakness: limited scope—developed and tested only on English with no discussion of adaptation challenges for morphologically rich or free-word-order languages.

- Weakness: superficial analysis of classifier failures, potential bias in the rating task, unclear significance-testing procedures, and missing raw TP/FP/FN counts.

- Question: did you perform a systematic error analysis and evaluate classifier accuracy on gold-standard edit boundaries to separate alignment from classification errors?
