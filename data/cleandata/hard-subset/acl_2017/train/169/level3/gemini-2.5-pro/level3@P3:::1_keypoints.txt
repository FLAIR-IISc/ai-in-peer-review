- Proposes a method to auto-annotate GEC system outputs by extracting edits via linguistically-enhanced alignment and classifying them with a rule-based classifier using automatic linguistic markup.  
- Evaluates the classifier with a small human rating study and reports high "Good" or "Acceptable" ratings.  
- Applies the tool to CoNLL-2014 system outputs to produce a detailed per-error-type analysis (operation type, error category, edit length, detection vs. correction).  
- Contribution: provides a tool and methodology for fine-grained, error-type-level GEC evaluation enabling diagnostic analysis beyond aggregate scores.  
- Contribution: delivers the first comprehensive per-error-type comparative analysis of all CoNLL-2014 systems, linking architectures to strengths on specific error types.  
- Strength: high potential impact for GEC research and language pedagogy by enabling targeted diagnostics and hybrid-system design.  
- Strength: enables novel, insightful analyses (operation types, multi-token edits, detection vs. correction) that reveal important system weaknesses like multi-token edits.  
- Weakness: the rule-based classifier is underspecified and treated as a black box—missing rule examples, counts, application order, conflict resolution, and complexity details.  
- Weakness: classifier evaluation is flawed—no independent gold standard, non-standard aggregation of rater scores, and lack of item-level rating distributions.  
- Weakness: evaluation sample details are missing—no breakdown of the 200-edit set by error category, so results may be biased by easy error types.  
- Weakness: unclear motivation for the new 25-type tagset and lack of mapping to existing, standard tagsets, weakening comparability to prior work.  
- Weakness: presentation issues obscure insights—overlong CoNLL-2014 result reporting, unsubstantiated claims (domain-independence), and minor formatting/typo problems.  
- Question: provide concrete examples, counts, and application logic/order of rules used in the classifier for reproducibility.  
- Question: clarify how "OVERALL" percentages in Table 3 were computed and provide distribution of ratings per item (e.g., percent of items with any "Bad" rating).  
- Question: report the distribution of error types in the 200-edit manual evaluation set and compare it to the CoNLL-2014 test set.  
- Question: justify the new error tagset choice instead of using or mapping to existing tagsets (e.g., CoNLL-2014) to improve comparability.
