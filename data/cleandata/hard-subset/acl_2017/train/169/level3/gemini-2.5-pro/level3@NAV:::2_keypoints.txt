- Introduces a rule-based method to annotate GEC outputs with error types, enabling per-category precision, recall, and F-score, demonstrated on CoNLL-2014 systems.  
- Main contribution: an evaluation framework that measures both precision and recall by error type, moving beyond recall-only metrics.  
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive error analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems that yields new insights and a resource for the community.  
- Strength: Enables distinguishing cautious/accurate vs aggressive/noisy systems through per-error-type precision.  
- Strength: Dataset-independent approach using standard NLP tool outputs rather than manual training data, increasing applicability.  
- Strength: Human validation shows over 95% of automatic labels rated “Good” or “Acceptable,” supporting label quality.  
- Weakness: Classification rules are completely omitted, making the method a non-reproducible black box.  
- Weakness: Unclear how the 25 error categories were designed or whether they align with established annotation schemes.  
- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to English with no discussion of adapting the rule-based system to other languages or handling discontinuous edits.  
- Weakness: Superficial treatment of human “Bad” ratings, lacking systematic breakdown of error sources beyond blaming the POS tagger.  
- Weakness: Raw TP/FP/FN counts are not reported, preventing assessment of statistical significance for rare error types.  
- Weakness: No analysis on whether poor performance in some categories (e.g., Unnecessary tokens) stems from system architecture versus correction strategy.  
- Overall judgment: Promising work but not ready for publication—must publish full rules and address generalization to other languages.
