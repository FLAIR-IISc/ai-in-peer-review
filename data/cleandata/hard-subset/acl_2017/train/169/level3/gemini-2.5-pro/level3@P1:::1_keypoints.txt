- Paper proposes a two-stage method: linguistically-enhanced alignment to extract edits, then a rule-based, dataset-independent classifier to label grammatical error types.  
- Authors report a small-scale human rating showing >95% of classifications rated "Good" or "Acceptable."  
- Main application: fine-grained analysis of CoNLL-2014 GEC system outputs across 25 error categories.  
- Authors promise to release the annotation tool publicly.  

- Contribution: provides a novel, detailed fine-grained analysis revealing strengths/weaknesses of GEC architectures by error type.  
- Contribution: offers a publicly available automatic error-annotation tool to enable standardized, targeted evaluations.  

- Strength: high potential impact and utility for GEC research and CALL through nuanced diagnostics and learner feedback adaptation.  
- Strength: thorough, multi-angle experimental analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems (operation type, linguistic categories, edit size, detection vs. correction).  
- Strength: fair-evaluation approach by reprocessing gold references with the same automatic tool, shown not to change system rankings significantly.  

- Weakness: core rule-based classifier is described superficially, lacking concrete rules, precedence, conflict resolution, and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: human evaluation design is flawed and prone to confirmation bias, uses unsound aggregation, and lacks raw rating distributions and test-set composition.  
- Weakness: claim of "dataset independence" is overstated since the method depends on language-specific NLP tools and manually developed rules that likely embed corpus biases.  
- Weakness: introducing a new 25-type error schema is insufficiently justified and risks fragmenting existing annotation standards.  

- Question: provide concrete examples of classifier rules and how rule application order and conflict resolution are handled (e.g., VERB:TENSE vs VERB:SVA).  
- Question: provide raw counts for "Good/Acceptable/Bad" across the 1000 judgments and the error-category distribution in the 200-edit set.  
- Question: give examples of unusual or incorrect edit boundaries and discuss their impact on classification accuracy.  
- Question: justify creating a new error annotation schema rather than adapting the CoNLL-2014 schema or providing a direct mapping.  

- Minor: fix typos/formatting issues (e.g., "c.f." → "cf.", "M2 scorer" → "M² scorer", inconsistent reference formatting).
