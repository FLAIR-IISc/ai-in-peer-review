- Proposes a pipeline that automatically annotates grammatical errors in corrected text using linguistically-enhanced alignment plus a rule-based classifier.  
- Introduces a dataset-independent, rule-based error type classifier that avoids training on corpus-specific annotation schemes.  
- Applies the pipeline to CoNLL-2014 to provide detailed error-type analysis of participating GEC systems.  
- Fills a major gap in GEC research by enabling detailed error-type evaluation of system outputs beyond overall metrics.  
- Manual validation shows >95% agreement with human raters, indicating high reliability of automated annotations.  
- Authors plan to release the tool and describe the method clearly, making the work practical and reproducible.  
- The CoNLL-2014 analysis reveals strengths and weaknesses of different GEC approaches across specific error types.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, facilitating understanding and replication.  
- Core components (enhanced alignment and rule-based classifier) largely build on existing work; novelty is in their integration and application.  
- Classifier depends on POS tagging accuracy, making it vulnerable to POS tagger errors.  
- Re-processing gold references for comparison is somewhat contrived and needs stronger justification and analysis of its impact.  
- The 25 error categories may miss finer-grained grammatical nuances and could be expanded.  
- Needs analysis of how sensitive classifier performance is to POS tagging errors and error attribution frequency.  
- Authors should clarify the rationale and consequences of re-processing gold references for comparability.  
- Provide computational cost estimates for running the annotation pipeline to assess scalability.  
- Discuss limitations of the rule-based approach and which error types are handled poorly and how to address them.  
- Clarify table notation (e.g., ‘M:’, ‘U:’, ‘R:’) in the error-type listing.  
- Temper the “dataset-agnostic” claim by acknowledging potential loss of dataset-specific nuances.  
- Define the ‘UNK’ error category clearly in the text.
