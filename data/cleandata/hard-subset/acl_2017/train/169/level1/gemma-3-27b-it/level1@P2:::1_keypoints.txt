- Proposes a dataset-agnostic rule-based error-typing classifier using linguistically-enhanced aligned edits to automatically annotate grammatical errors.
- Applies the classifier to re-evaluate CoNLL-2014 systems, giving a more granular breakdown by error type, operation (missing/unnecessary/replacement), and detection vs correction.
- Releases the annotation tool to the GEC community.

- Addresses a major bottleneck in GEC evaluation by enabling automatic error-type annotations and reducing reliance on manual labeling.
- Uses only automatic linguistic features (POS, lemmas) to avoid dataset bias and reports 95%+ agreement with human raters.
- The CoNLL-2014 analysis reveals complementary strengths of different systems and suggests directions for combining approaches.
- Public tool release will facilitate broader adoption and further research.

- Individual components (linguistically-informed alignment, rule-based typing) are not novel; main novelty is their integration and dataset-agnostic outcome.
- Overall performance is highly dependent on alignment quality, but the paper lacks a detailed analysis of alignment-induced errors.
- The 25 error-type taxonomy may be too coarse for some needs (e.g., modal vs. tense distinctions), limiting finer-grained diagnostics.
- No direct comparison with other automatic annotation methods is provided to establish relative performance.

- What is the impact of alignment errors on error-type classification, and can you provide an analysis of misalignment-driven mistakes?
- Have you considered adding more sophisticated linguistic features (e.g., dependency relations, semantic info) to the rule set?
- Will the rule-based classifier be released as code to allow customization and extension?
- Are there plans to apply the tool to newer GEC datasets or systems beyond CoNLL-2014?
