- Proposes a dataset-independent, rule-based pipeline to automatically annotate GEC system outputs with detailed error types.  
- Uses linguistically-enhanced alignment plus POS/lemma features to extract edits and classify error types.  
- Validates the classifier with high agreement (>95% “Good” or “Acceptable”) against human raters.  
- Applies the tool to re-evaluate CoNLL-2014 systems, producing detailed error-type breakdowns and insights.  
- Shows complementary strengths of MT vs. classifier-based GEC systems through the error-type analysis.  
- Releases the annotation tool as open-source to improve reproducibility and community impact.  
- Limitation: analysis is performed on a single dataset (CoNLL-2014), limiting demonstrated generalizability.  
- Limitation: rule-based approach may be brittle and miss nuanced or rare error categories as rules scale.  
- Limitation: classifier performance depends heavily on POS tagger accuracy, affecting classification reliability.  
- Question: potential to extend the rule-based classifier with richer linguistic features or ML components?  
- Question: sensitivity of classifier performance to different POS taggers and variation in results?  
- Question: can you quantify M2 scorer’s tendency to overestimate performance and its implications for past GEC evaluations?
