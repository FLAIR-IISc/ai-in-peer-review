- Novel method for automatic error-type annotation of GEC outputs enabling detailed error-type analysis.
- Dataset-independent rule-based classifier that uses only automatically obtained properties and requires no labeled training data.
- Comprehensive error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems revealing system strengths and weaknesses.

- Addresses a critical GEC evaluation gap by enabling per-error-type performance measurement.
- Rigorous validation: manual evaluation by 5 experts on 200 edits with 95%+ rated "Good" or "Acceptable."
- Insightful granular analysis showing which systems excel at which error types (e.g., CAMB vs CUUI).
- Practical implementation with planned tool release, increasing immediate community impact.
- Clear explanation of why the M2 scorer can overestimate performance.

- Evaluation scope limited to CoNLL-2014; generalizability across datasets, error types, or languages not demonstrated.
- Insufficient direct comparison with machine-learning approaches on the same data.
- No statistical significance testing reported for system differences across error types.
- Rule-based classifier limitations and failure modes not systematically analyzed (e.g., Word Order).
- Limited discussion of applicability to recent neural GEC systems.

- How does the method handle error types not well-characterized by POS tags (e.g., Word Order), and what are the limitations?
- Has the method been applied to more recent GEC datasets/systems (e.g., CoNLL-2015/2016), and how well does it generalize?
- Can you provide statistical significance tests for the reported error-type performance differences?

- Overall: strong, novel, well-validated contribution that enables detailed error-type analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to broaden evaluation and add statistical analysis.
