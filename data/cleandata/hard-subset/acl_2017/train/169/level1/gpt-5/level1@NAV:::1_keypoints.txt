- Presents a pipeline to annotate untyped GEC hypotheses with explicit edit spans and error types using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a deterministic rule-based classifier.
- Small manual study shows high perceived quality of predicted types (>95% Good/Acceptable per rater; overall 86.3% Good, 10% Acceptable; κfree=0.724).
- Applies the tool to CoNLL-2014 to deliver the first large-scale operation-/type-/multi-token-/detection-vs-correction error-type analysis across systems.
- Shows auto-processed references produce scores statistically indistinguishable from gold via bootstrap testing and argues M2 can inflate scores.

- Contribution: A practical, deterministic, dataset-agnostic rule-based error-type classifier coupled with linguistically-informed edit extraction to type system hypotheses.
- Contribution: A comprehensive empirical error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems that surfaces actionable strengths and weaknesses (e.g., poor handling of unnecessary tokens, multi-token edits).
- Contribution: An evaluation protocol demonstrating auto-referenced scoring comparability to gold and highlighting limitations of the M2 scorer.

- Strength: High practical utility and reproducibility—released deterministic tool fills a long-standing gap without requiring training data.
- Strength: Empirical human validation supports type assignments and allows tracing errors to POS/tagging problems.
- Strength: Comprehensive analysis yields clear, actionable insights for system design (e.g., VERB:FORM robustness, MT strengths on multi-token edits).
- Strength: Sound experimental methodology—separates alignment from scoring, reprocesses references, uses bootstrap significance testing, and provides a pointed critique of M2.

- Weakness: Limited validation scale—only 200 edits and no per-type precision/recall against harmonized gold reduces confidence in fine-grained accuracy.
- Weakness: Pipeline brittleness and English dependence—reliance on specific taggers/parsers/stemmers and Hunspell may limit robustness and portability; POS errors propagate.
- Weakness: Scheme design choices (e.g., collapsing modals into tense) introduce ambiguity and may obscure important distinctions and downstream impacts.
- Weakness: Boundary sensitivity—automatic edit spans can misalign with human edits, affecting type counts and F-scores; more ablations on alignment needed.
- Weakness: Generalization not demonstrated beyond CoNLL/FCE and non-English datasets.

- Question: Can you provide ablations on features (POS vs lemma vs dependencies) and alignment parameters to quantify sensitivity?
- Question: Is there a coarse mapping to shared schemes to enable per-type precision/recall against gold-typed corpora?
- Question: How portable is the framework to other languages using UD tags; any preliminary results?
- Question: Will the tool include diagnostics to flag low-confidence types (e.g., when POS disagreement is detected)?
