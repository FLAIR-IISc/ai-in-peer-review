- Summary: Proposes an automatic pipeline to annotate unlabelled GEC outputs with edit spans and error types for per-error-type evaluation using alignment-based edit extraction plus rule-based, dataset-agnostic classification, validated by expert judgments and applied to CoNLL-2014.

- Contribution 1: Presents a practical, deterministic, dataset-agnostic rule-based error-typing framework that enables per-type evaluation without labeled training data.

- Contribution 2: Provides a large-scale, detailed error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems, revealing complementary strengths/weaknesses and difficulties like multi-token edits and detection vs. correction gaps.

- Contribution 3: Proposes a methodological protocol that reprocesses gold references with the same pipeline to fix edit spans, making edit-level comparisons straightforward and reproducible with comparable aggregate scores to M2.

- Strength 1: Fills a gap in GEC evaluation by offering actionable, per-type diagnostics that can guide system design and research priorities.

- Strength 2: Uses deterministic, interpretable rules relying on POS/lemma/parse and simple lexica, increasing transparency, consistency, and portability without needing training data.

- Strength 3: Employs consistent processing of hypothesis and reference to reduce boundary effects; shows no significant aggregate score differences versus gold/M2 across teams.

- Strength 4: Produces empirically grounded insights (e.g., weakness on multi-token edits, detection–correction gap, operation-tier variability) likely to influence future work.

- Strength 5: Commits to releasing the tool, facilitating reproducibility and community adoption.

- Weakness 1: Validation is limited and coarse (200 edits, acceptability labels) without per-type precision/recall or confusion analyses against a gold error-type standard.

- Weakness 2: Method depends on English-specific POS/tagging/UD mapping/Hunspell and may be sensitive to upstream annotation quality and domain differences; portability is untested.

- Weakness 3: Using the same alignment/merging rules for hypothesis and reprocessed reference could bias category-level distributions and multi-token boundaries; sensitivity analysis is lacking.

- Weakness 4: Claims about M2 “overestimation” are not quantified; the paper lacks controlled studies showing when and how M2 inflates scores relative to the fixed-span protocol.

- Weakness 5: Rule set and taxonomy details (full rules, precedence, tie-breaking) are under-specified, hindering reproducibility and community extension.

- Question 1: Will the authors release the complete rule set, alignment cost/merging parameters, and scripts to reproduce CoNLL-2014 analyses?

- Question 2: How does classifier performance vary by error type and under POS/parsing errors; can the authors provide larger, stratified per-type manual validation?

- Question 3: How robust are results to different POS taggers/parsers or removing dependency parses; are there any ablation/sensitivity results?

- Question 4: Can the approach generalize to other English domains or other languages with appropriate taggers/dictionaries; is there any preliminary evidence?

- Question 5: For the M2 critique, can the authors quantify average inflation and show cases where their protocol under- or over-estimates relative to human-aligned spans?

- Additional Comment 1: Fix reference formatting and minor typographical issues.

- Additional Comment 2: Specify spaCy model version/configuration, UD mapping, Hunspell version, and any non-default alignment parameters.

- Additional Comment 3: Consider releasing per-type detection breakdowns and multi-token length distributions to complement aggregates.

- Overall evaluation: Practical, impactful contribution that meaningfully improves GEC evaluation with interpretable methods and useful analyses, but requires stronger validation and more exhaustive methodological documentation; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
