- Proposal: Practical pipeline to auto-annotate parallel original/corrected sentences with explicit edit spans and error types for type-wise GEC evaluation, including on unannotated hypotheses.

- Method: Combines linguistically-enhanced alignment (Damerau–Levenshtein with POS/lemma costs) and a deterministic, dataset-agnostic rule-based classifier mapping edits to a POS-centered error taxonomy with operation prefixes (Missing, Unnecessary, Replacement).

- Validation & use: Small human study shows ≥95% Good/Acceptable ratings per rater; tool used to analyze CoNLL-2014 system outputs by error type.

- Main contribution 1: Usable, dataset-agnostic deterministic error-type annotation pipeline enabling detailed, comparable error-type evaluation (operation tier, multi-token, detection vs. correction).

- Main contribution 2: Error-type framework and rule-based classifier relying only on automatic linguistic markup with encouraging manual validation.

- Main contribution 3: Novel, comprehensive analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems by error category, revealing complementary strengths and performance gaps.

- Strength: Fills an important practical evaluation gap by enabling edit-level, per-type diagnostics and consistent reprocessing of references/hypotheses.

- Strength: Simple, transparent, deterministic, and dataset-agnostic method that avoids supervised training bias and supports interpretability and debugging.

- Strength: Empirical validation is encouraging (96.3% Good/Acceptable overall; κfree = 0.724) compared to prior ML classifiers.

- Strength: Insightful per-type analysis yields actionable takeaways about system strengths/weaknesses (e.g., multi-token edits weak, Unnecessary deletions often missed, spellchecking underused).

- Strength: Thoughtful discussion of reference processing and metric behavior, including commentary on M2’s dynamic boundary effects.

- Weakness: Validation is limited and subjective — small 200-edit study, no gold label set, no per-category accuracy or confusion analysis, and raters asked to ignore boundary issues.

- Weakness: Error scheme and rule set under-specified; broad categories may mask distinctions and rules may be brittle to tagging errors; “OTHER”/“UNK” could become sinks.

- Weakness: Dependence on automatic alignment and exact-span matching may bias evaluation toward chosen alignment heuristic and penalize segmentation mismatches; no per-type sensitivity or alternative alignment analysis.

- Weakness: Reproducibility and scope details missing — rules, thresholds, UD mapping, handling of contractions/orthography, dependency on specific taggers/parsers/spelling lexicons not provided; presented for English only.

- Weakness: Statistical rigor on per-category analysis is light — many per-type comparisons lack confidence intervals or significance testing.

- Question: Provide full rule inventory and decision procedure (precedence, fallbacks, examples) and an error-type confusion analysis from the human study; which categories drive “Bad” ratings?

- Question: How sensitive are assignments to tagging/parsing errors; have alternative taggers/parsers or UD mappings been tested and mitigations considered?

- Question: How portable is the framework to other languages; which components are language-specific and what is required to adapt?

- Question: Can you report per-type reliability, manual spot-checks per category, and an ablation showing impact of the linguistically-enhanced alignment vs. simpler alignment?

- Question: Will the scorer and fixed dependency versions/scripts be released to ensure exact reproducibility of CoNLL-2014 reprocessing?

- Overall verdict: Timely, useful, and implementable evaluation paper with practical utility and compelling benchmark application, meriting acceptance pending clarifications and release of tool and rules.
