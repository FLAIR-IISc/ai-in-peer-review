- Proposes a pipeline to automatically annotate GEC outputs with edit spans and error types for per-error-type precision/recall evaluation.  
- Uses linguistically-informed Damerau–Levenshtein alignment and a deterministic, dataset-independent rule-based classifier (POS/lemma/UD/Hunspell) to assign error types.  
- Conducts a small human study showing ≥95% Good/Acceptable ratings for predicted types and re-annotates CoNLL-2014 references/outputs for a comprehensive error-type breakdown.  

Primary contributions:  
- Practical, dataset-agnostic rule-based error-type classification tool enabling consistent annotation of hypotheses and references.  
- Detailed empirical error-type analysis across CoNLL-2014 systems, revealing complementary system strengths and weaknesses and operation-tier differences.  
- Evidence that automatically reprocessed references yield similar aggregate scores to gold and a critique that M2 may overestimate via dynamic boundaries.  

Strengths:  
- Clear, actionable improvement to evaluation practice allowing transparent span+correction matching and type-wise diagnostics.  
- Linguistically motivated engineering that leverages robust signals and avoids dataset-specific training, with a balanced taxonomy.  
- Large-scale analysis yielding novel, informative system-level insights, especially about multi-token edits.  
- Human validation shows high agreement with expert intuition and attributes many errors to tagging mistakes.  
- Useful critique of M2 and demonstration that reprocessed references can reproduce aggregate scores.  

Weaknesses:  
- Classifier evaluation is limited (200 edits, perceived appropriateness only), lacking per-type accuracy, robustness tests, and ML comparisons.  
- Potential brittleness to preprocessing: reliance on spaCy/Hunspell may propagate tagging/dialectal biases (e.g., British vs. American spelling).  
- Some schema choices (broad MORPH/OTHER, modals→TENSE) and lack of full rule-ordering/conflict transparency hinder adaptability.  
- Equivalence claim between auto and gold references is under-evidenced: no per-type/operation analyses or boundary-level agreement reported.  
- Trade-off with M2 not fully resolved: strict span+correction matching can be brittle and tolerant/type-level matching is not explored.  

Questions for authors:  
- Provide details on rule engine: ordering, conflict resolution, and features per major rule family; configurability for alternative taxonomies?  
- How sensitive is the classifier to tagging/parsing errors; any per-type accuracy with gold POS/UD or ablations (POS vs. lemma vs. deps)?  
- How are dialectal spelling variants handled; can lexicons be switched and does that change type distributions or rankings?  
- Can you report per-type/per-operation comparisons and boundary-level agreement (e.g., IoU) between gold and auto references?  
- Has the tool been tested on other corpora (e.g., JFLEG) and how does it handle paraphrastic/fluency edits that may map to OTHER/MORPH?  

Overall recommendation:  
- Solid, practical contribution with valuable empirical insights; recommend acceptance contingent on stronger classifier validation and clearer rule documentation.
