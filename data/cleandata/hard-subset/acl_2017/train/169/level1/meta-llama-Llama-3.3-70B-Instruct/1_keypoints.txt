- Proposes an automatic method to extract edits between original and corrected sentences and classify them with a dataset-independent rule-based classifier.  
- Enables dataset-independent, fine-grained error-type evaluation of system performance not possible with existing metrics.  
- Plans to release a tool for automatic annotation to standardize corpora and facilitate detailed error-type evaluation.  
- Novel, dataset-independent framework for error-type evaluation relying only on automatically obtained information.  
- Presents the first detailed error-type performance evaluation for all teams in the CoNLL-2014 GEC shared task.  
- Release of a practical tool that other researchers can use to standardize corpora and perform detailed evaluations.  
- Classifier evaluation is limited to a small-scale manual study, raising concerns about its robustness and generality.  
- No comparison is provided between the proposed approach and existing error-type evaluation metrics.  
- Analysis of the presented results is limited; some findings are not fully explored.  
- How will the classifier be evaluated at larger scale to demonstrate effectiveness?  
- Can the authors compare their approach quantitatively to existing error-type evaluation metrics?  
- How do the authors plan to address the limited analysis and other evaluation shortcomings in the current work?
