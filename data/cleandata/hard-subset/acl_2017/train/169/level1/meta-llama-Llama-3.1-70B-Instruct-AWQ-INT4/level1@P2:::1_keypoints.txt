- Summary: Proposes automatic annotation of parallel error-correction data with explicit edit spans and error types via linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier, evaluated on CoNLL-2014 system outputs.

- Contribution: Automatic annotation and evaluation method enabling more detailed system performance analysis.

- Contribution: Dataset-independent rule-based classifier that uses only automatically obtained POS tags and lemmas.

- Contribution: First detailed error-type analysis of all CoNLL-2014 shared-task systems.

- Strength: Novel method enables fine-grained, automated error-type evaluation.

- Strength: Classifier’s dataset-independence increases applicability across datasets.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across CoNLL-2014 teams demonstrates practical utility.

- Weakness: Evaluation relies on a small-scale manual check, limiting confidence in generalizability.

- Weakness: Limited comparison to other metrics (e.g., M2 scorer) leaves relative strengths unclear.

- Weakness: Rule-based classifier may fail to capture nuanced human error-type judgments, risking inconsistencies.

- Question: How will the authors address the small-scale manual evaluation to validate the classifier on larger datasets?

- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison to other metrics like the M2 scorer?

- Question: How do the authors plan to refine the rule-based classifier to better model nuanced human error-type classification?
