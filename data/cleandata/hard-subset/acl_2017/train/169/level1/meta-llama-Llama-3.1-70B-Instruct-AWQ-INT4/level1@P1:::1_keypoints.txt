- Proposes a linguistically-enhanced alignment plus rule-based, dataset-independent classifier for automatic annotation and evaluation of GEC error types.  
- Claims a novel method providing more detailed and accurate automatic error-type annotation than previous approaches.  
- Uses a linguistically-enhanced alignment algorithm to extract edits between original and corrected sentences more effectively.  
- Introduces a rule-based classifier that relies only on automatically obtained POS tags and lemmas, making it dataset-independent and practical.  
- Reports strong performance: overall F-score 86.3% and inter-annotator agreement κfree = 0.724.  
- Enables detailed analysis of system error-type performance, useful for improving GEC systems.  
- Evaluation is limited to the CoNLL-2014 shared task dataset, so generalizability is uncertain.  
- Dependence on POS tags and lemmas may be problematic when those annotations are inaccurate or unavailable.  
- May have limited ability to handle complex errors involving multiple tokens or sentences.  
- Asks how the method can be extended to handle multi-token or cross-sentence complex errors.  
- Requests more details on handling errors not well-characterized by POS tags and lemmas.  
- Suggests evaluating the approach on additional datasets and diverse GEC systems.  
- Overall: significant contribution with impressive results, but should be extended to complex errors and broader evaluations.
