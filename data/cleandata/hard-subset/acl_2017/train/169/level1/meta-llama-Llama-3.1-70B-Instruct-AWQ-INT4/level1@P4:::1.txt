**Summary**

The paper proposes a method for automatic annotation and evaluation of error types for Grammatical Error Correction (GEC) systems. The approach consists of two main steps: edit extraction using a linguistically-enhanced alignment algorithm, and error typing using a new rule-based framework. The authors evaluate their approach by applying it to the system output produced in the CoNLL-2014 shared task and demonstrate its value by carrying out a detailed analysis of system error type performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel method for automatic annotation and evaluation of error types for GEC systems, which overcomes the limitation of existing methods that require human-annotated data.
2. A new rule-based framework for error typing that is dataset-independent and relies only on automatically obtained information such as POS tags and lemmas.
3. A detailed evaluation of system error type performance for the first time for all teams in the CoNLL-2014 shared task on Grammatical Error Correction.

**Strengths**

1. The proposed method is able to automatically annotate system hypothesis corrections with the same alignment and error type information as the reference, enabling a more detailed evaluation.
2. The rule-based framework for error typing is dataset-independent and does not require labelled training data, making it a more practical solution for GEC evaluation.
3. The evaluation of system error type performance for the CoNLL-2014 shared task provides valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of different GEC systems.

**Weaknesses**

1. The evaluation of the proposed method is limited to a small-scale manual evaluation, which may not be representative of the entire dataset.
2. The authors do not provide a direct comparison with other existing methods for GEC evaluation, making it difficult to assess the relative performance of their approach.
3. The paper assumes that the edit extraction algorithm is accurate, but does not provide any evaluation of its performance.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the evaluation of the edit extraction algorithm and its impact on the overall performance of the proposed method?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of annotator bias in the manual evaluation of the proposed method?
3. Can you provide a more detailed comparison with other existing methods for GEC evaluation, including their strengths and weaknesses?
