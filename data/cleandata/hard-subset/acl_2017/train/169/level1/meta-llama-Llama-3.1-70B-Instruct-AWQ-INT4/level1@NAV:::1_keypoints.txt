- Summary: Proposes a method to automatically annotate parallel error-correction data with explicit edit spans and error types via linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier, evaluated on CoNLL-2014.

- Contribution 1: Automatic annotation of error-correction corpora with edit spans and error-type labels to standardize datasets and enable detailed evaluation.

- Contribution 2: A dataset-independent, rule-based framework that classifies edits into 25 error types using only automatic linguistic information (POS, lemmas).

- Contribution 3: First detailed, per-error-type evaluation of all CoNLL-2014 shared task teams to illustrate the method’s analytical value.

- Strength 1: High annotation quality—95% of predicted error types judged "Good" or "Acceptable" by human evaluators.

- Strength 2: Enables fine-grained analysis of system strengths and weaknesses across error types.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates practical applicability by analyzing real system outputs from the CoNLL-2014 task.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (CoNLL-2014), raising concerns about generalizability.

- Weakness 2: Rule-based approach may be less effective than machine-learning methods for some error types or domains.

- Weakness 3: Analysis of multi-token errors is limited and requires further investigation.

- Question 1: How will the approach be extended to handle more complex error types and diverse datasets?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide more detail on the rule-based framework’s design, rules, and development process?

- Question 3: How can this annotation approach be integrated into practical applications such as language learning or writing assistance?
