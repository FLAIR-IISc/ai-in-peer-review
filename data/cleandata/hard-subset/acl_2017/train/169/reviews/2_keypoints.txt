- Novel evaluation method enables measuring GEC performance by error type including precision, not just recall.  
- Approach is independent of manual error annotation and can handle multi-token errors.  
- Evaluation covers a variety of systems, showing broad applicability.  
- Automatically selected error tags are largely approved by human experts.  

- The rules used to derive error types are not described, threatening reproducibility.  
- Classifier evaluation lacks detailed error analysis and concrete directions for improvement.  
- The evaluation is performed only on English and the paper does not address adapting the approach to other languages.  

- It is unclear how the error categories were devised or whether they follow prior work.  
- The rules for assigning error tags likely depend on alignment/preprocessing, but this dependence is not detailed.  
- The method may struggle with discontinuous edit spans (e.g., separable verb prefixes in German) and this is not addressed.  
- Human rating of automatic tags may be biased by noisy boundaries and lack of intermediate rating options; authors should test ratings with manual boundaries.  
- The classifier’s “Bad” labels are only cursorily attributed to POS errors; there is no systematic analysis of when and why raters chose each judgment.  

- The significance test setup is unclear (e.g., whether F0.5 was tested) and F0.5 may be a weak basis for discrimination.  
- Comparison between gold and auto references appears limited to boundaries and not classification, despite claims otherwise.  

- Some systems’ failure on certain error types (e.g., unnecessary token) may reflect system design limits rather than evaluation failure, but the paper does not analyze this.  
- The paper does not report raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts (Table 6), which are needed to assess sample sizes and compute other measures.  
- Results for some systems/error types may be based on very few instances; this is not clarified.  
- Multi-token error results need more interpretation given system design and training data differences.  

- The paper should acknowledge that some GEC systems can already produce error-tagged outputs via submodules, even if not straightforward.  
- Minor issues: reference formatting and missing page/URL details in citations.  

- Author-response summary: reviewer agrees the approach is promising but the paper is not ready; rules must be described for replication and generalization to other languages must be addressed.
