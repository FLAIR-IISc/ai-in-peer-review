- Strength: Useful application for teachers and learners and enables fine-grained comparison of GEC systems.  
- Weakness: System description is highly superficial; core rule set lacks examples, counts, complexity, and ordering details.  
- Paper overemphasizes CoNLL-2014 system results (large table) and repeats facts that are visible from the table.  
- Evaluation used raters to judge system output rather than creating an independent gold standard for the 200 test sentences.  
- Aggregation of rater scores is misleading (averaging individual rater percentages); distribution of “bad” ratings not reported.  
- No information about test data composition (number of error categories or which categories are covered).  
- Vague claim that “edit boundaries might be unusual” needs precise definition and examples and potential impact discussed.  
- Claim of lower domain dependence is unsubstantiated; manual rules and Hunspell vocabularies can be domain- and language-dependent, and test corpora are limited to student essays.  
- Unclear why a new error-tagset was designed; existing tagsets (Nicholls, CoNLL-2014) could have been used or justified.  
- Overall motivation is unclear: is the contribution a new system, a new tagset, or an evaluation? important details are omitted.  
- Typos and reference formatting issues (e.g., "c.f." → "cf.", "M2" → "M^2", inconsistent casing in references).
