- Automated method to annotate GEC system outputs with detailed error types.  
- Dataset-independent, rule-based classifier designed to avoid dataset-specific biases.  
- Applied method to CoNLL-2014 to produce a detailed error-type analysis of participating systems.  
- Fills a critical gap in GEC evaluation by enabling nuanced, informative performance analysis.  
- Human evaluation shows >95% agreement with the classifier, supporting its accuracy and reliability.  
- Authors plan to release the tool, promoting reproducibility and practical adoption.  
- Provides breakdowns by error type and operation (missing, unnecessary, replacement) for deeper insights.  
- Thoughtful comparison to the M2 scorer highlights potential biases in existing evaluation methods.  
- Evaluation is limited to CoNLL-2014 and a specific set of systems, leaving generalizability untested.  
- Classifier depends heavily on POS tagging, stemming, and lemmatization; impact of errors here is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- The 25 error types may be too granular for some uses; need exploration of collapsing/aggregating types.  
- Handling of complex, multi-issue errors is not fully explored or validated.  
- No direct comparative evaluation against prior automated error-typing approaches (e.g., Felice et al., 2016).  
- How sensitive is classifier performance to POS/stemming/lemmatization errors; request for an ablation study.  
- How does the system resolve ambiguities when multiple error types are plausible; could probabilistic methods help?  
- What are the computational costs (time/resources) for annotating large GEC corpora?  
- Have you experimented with collapsing error types to coarser granularity; what was the impact on analysis?  
- Table 2 is too wide and hard to read; consider breaking or reformatting.  
- Reference list contains incomplete/incorrect entries and needs proper formatting.  
- The term "dataset-agnostic" should be qualified, since the method relies on linguistic preprocessing that may vary across languages/domains.
