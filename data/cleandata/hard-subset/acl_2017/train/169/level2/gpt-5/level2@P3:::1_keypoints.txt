- Summary: Proposes a two-stage pipeline (linguistically enhanced alignment + deterministic, rule-based, dataset-agnostic error-type classifier) and shows ≥95% “Good”/“Acceptable” per rater on a small human eval; applied to CoNLL-2014 for the first comprehensive type-level analysis.
- Contribution: A transparent, reproducible, dataset-independent rule-based error typing tool with flexible granularity (operation tier + 25+ categories).
- Contribution: A practical evaluation methodology that reprocesses references to match hypothesis alignment/classification, with evidence that auto-processed references do not significantly change overall scores, plus a simple edit-comparison scorer and critique of M2 boundary effects.
- Contribution: The first extensive error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 system outputs, revealing actionable strengths/weaknesses by type, operation, and edit span.
- Strength: Provides a practical, interpretable, and reproducible solution that avoids reliance on heterogeneous annotated training data.
- Strength: Empirical validation includes a 200-edit human rating study and bootstrap testing showing stability of auto-vs.-gold reference processing.
- Strength: Produces rich, fine-grained analysis (operation categories, multi-token edits, detection vs. correction) with clear takeaways for system development and combination.
- Strength: Clean separation of alignment and scoring clarifies evaluation mechanics and highlights how dynamic-boundary scoring (M2) can distort results.
- Strength: Plans to release the tool, enabling reproducibility and community uptake.
- Weakness: Classifier evaluation is limited (200 edits) and lacks per-type accuracy, confusion matrices, and operation-tier reliability.
- Weakness: Framework is sensitive to upstream NLP errors (spaCy v1.6, GB-centric dictionary); no sensitivity analysis to alternative taggers/parsers or domains.
- Weakness: Some design/category choices conflate nuanced phenomena and lack documentation on OTHER/UNK frequency, rule precedence, and handling of multi-label/composite edits.
- Weakness: Main empirical application is limited to CoNLL-2014; further validation on other corpora and tolerance analyses for the simple scorer are lacking.
- Weakness: Spelling-variant policy (Hunspell GB-large) may mislabel variants like color/colour as SPELL without discussion of cross-variant implications.
- Question: How many rules are there, what is their precedence, and how are conflicts and fallbacks (OTHER/UNK) handled?
- Question: How are multi-token heterogeneous edits assigned a single type when POS categories differ, and is there evidence of inter-rule consistency?
- Question: What proportions of edits are labeled OTHER or UNK overall and by dataset, and which categories are prone to misclassification in human ratings?
- Question: How does performance change with different taggers/parsers or dictionaries (US vs GB), and what ablations isolate POS-only vs dependency features?
- Question: Do error-type scores remain stable when using gold vs. auto references beyond overall F0.5?
- Suggestion (Essential): Expand human evaluation to a larger, stratified sample with per-type breakdowns, confusion matrices, operation-tier reliability, and examples of frequent misclassifications with root causes.
- Suggestion (Essential): Document full rule inventory, precedence, decision logic, and statistics on OTHER/UNK usage in an appendix.
- Suggestion (Essential): Add robustness checks quantifying sensitivity to upstream NLP errors (alternate tagger or POS noise injection).
- Suggestion (Nice-to-have): Provide per-type stability analysis comparing gold vs. auto references.
- Suggestion (Nice-to-have): Offer configurable dictionary/variant spelling policies and discuss implications for cross-variant English.
- Suggestion (Nice-to-have): Explore a tolerance variant for the simple scorer (e.g., boundary slack) and report whether main findings persist.
- Overall assessment: Important, practical, and interpretable contribution to GEC evaluation that would be strengthened by larger, per-type reliability evidence and clearer rule/robustness documentation.
