- Summary: Proposes a two-step pipeline for automatic GEC annotation—linguistically enhanced alignment for edit extraction and a rule-based, dataset-independent error type classifier using POS/lemmas/dependencies—and re-annotates CoNLL-2014 to enable per-error-type analysis.  
- Contribution: Presents a practical, dataset-agnostic rule-based error-typing framework with an operational taxonomy across operation tiers and POS/morphology categories.  
- Contribution: Introduces an evaluation methodology that decouples edit extraction from scoring and shows auto-annotated references yield scores statistically indistinguishable from gold via bootstrap tests.  
- Contribution: Provides a comprehensive error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems, revealing system strengths, detection vs. correction differences, and multi-token edit issues.  
- Strength: High practical impact—enables per-type precision/recall and detection analyses for diagnosing and improving GEC systems, with detailed category breakdowns.  
- Strength: Methodologically clear and reproducible intent—pipeline uses standard NLP tools, deterministic rule design, and promised code release.  
- Strength: Empirical validation indicates auto-annotated references preserve system rankings and scores (no significant differences across teams on 1,000-sample bootstrap).  
- Strength: Small human validation shows labels judged Good/Acceptable in ≥95% per rater (aggregate 86.3% Good, 10% Acceptable) with transparent error attribution.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—the alignment builds on prior work and main novelty is rule-based labeling; lacks comparison to learned classifiers.  
- Weakness: English-centric and small validation—200 edits, 5 raters, reliance on spaCy/Hunspell; cross-dataset and multilingual generalizability untested.  
- Weakness: Rule inventory and decision logic not fully specified; missing ablations (e.g., POS vs. dependencies) and robustness checks to different taggers/parsers.  
- Weakness: Potential circularity from reprocessing gold references to match hypothesis extraction scheme; need complementary evaluation against untouched gold, especially for multi-token boundaries.  
- Question: How many rules are in the classifier and what is their per-category coverage—will you release the full rule set and ablations?  
- Question: How does performance vary with different taggers/parsers or on other GEC corpora and languages?  
- Question: Can you provide larger human validation and a detailed error analysis quantifying failure modes (boundary vs. POS errors)?  
- Question: Do conclusions about M2’s overestimation and system-specific strengths generalize beyond CoNLL-2014?
