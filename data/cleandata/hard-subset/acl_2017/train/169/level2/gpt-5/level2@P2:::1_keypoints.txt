- Proposes a deterministic pipeline (linguistically enhanced alignment + rule-based classifier) to annotate GEC outputs with edit spans and 55 error-type labels for type-level precision/recall/F evaluation.
- Uses automatic linguistic markup (POS, lemmas, dependencies, stemming, dictionary) and reports ≥95% Good/Acceptable label ratings in a small human study.
- Re-annotates CoNLL-2014 systems to deliver the first detailed cross-system error-type analysis (operation tiers, POS categories, multi-token edits, detection vs. correction).
- Contribution 1: Practical, dataset-agnostic pipeline enabling type-level diagnostic evaluation for GEC.
- Contribution 2: Comprehensive empirical analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems by error type, operation, and edit size with actionable insights.
- Contribution 3: Examination of evaluation choices showing auto-processing references preserves system rankings while simplifying evaluation.
- Strength: High practical value and likely community impact by filling a long-standing diagnostic evaluation gap.
- Strength: Sensible, transparent design relying on deterministic rule-based classification and automatic features avoids corpus-specific training.
- Strength: Human assessment, though small, supports that the rule set produces mostly reasonable labels.
- Strength: Insightful analysis of evaluation artifacts (M2 matching issues) supported by bootstrap testing.
- Strength: Rich empirical findings highlighting system strengths/weaknesses (e.g., weak Unnecessary edits, multi-token edit difficulties).
- Weakness: Limited validation — small (200-edit) subjective study; no large-scale quantitative mapping to annotated corpora like FCE/NUCLE.
- Weakness: No robustness/sensitivity analysis to different taggers/parsers/dictionaries or ablations of linguistic features.
- Weakness: Rule coverage, complexity, conflict resolution, and error-category breakdowns are not reported or analyzed.
- Weakness: External validity limited to CoNLL-2014 systems; generality to other datasets (JFLEG, FCE) not shown.
- Weakness: Reprocessing references may mask boundary errors; no study of divergence between auto and human span boundaries by type.
- Questions: Can you quantitatively evaluate type assignment against an existing human-typed corpus via mapping (e.g., FCE/NUCLE)?
- Questions: How many rules exist, how are conflicts/resolutions handled, and how are ambiguous cases treated?
- Questions: How sensitive are results to choice of tagger/parser/dictionary and have ablations been run?
- Questions: For multi-token edits, what proportion of Bad labels come from span errors vs. classification, and what is boundary agreement with gold?
- Questions: Will the released tool include scripts to reprocess references and a stable interface for reproducibility?
- Suggestions: Provide an error-analysis taxonomy of misclassifications with examples and Good vs. Acceptable by type.
- Suggestions: Add confidence/ambiguous flags instead of forcing deterministic labels when evidence conflicts.
- Suggestions: Clarify UNK handling and whether it conflates detection failures and boundary errors.
- Suggestions: Include runtime/throughput stats to aid adoption at scale.
- Suggestion: Clarify language scope (current English-specific tools) and requirements for multilingual support.
- Overall assessment: Timely, useful contribution likely to be adopted, but would be strengthened by larger-scale validation, robustness studies, and richer error analysis.
