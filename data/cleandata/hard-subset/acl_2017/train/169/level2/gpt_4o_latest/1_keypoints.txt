- Two-step, dataset-independent method: extract edits with linguistically-enhanced alignment, then classify with a rule-based framework.  
- Main contribution: deterministic, transparent, reproducible error-classification system released as a public tool.  
- Validation: manual evaluation shows >95% agreement on error-type appropriateness.  
- Application: first detailed error-type analysis of GEC systems on CoNLL-2014 outputs.  
- Strength: addresses ML limitations by being dataset-agnostic, interpretable, and practically useful.  
- Strength: provides comprehensive CoNLL-2014 analysis highlighting system strengths and weaknesses.  
- Strength: high inter-rater agreement in validation supports reliability.  
- Strength: public release increases adoption and impact.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of reliance on automatic linguistic mark-up (e.g., POS tagger) and lack of mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness: manual evaluation limited to 200 edits, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: 25 error types with many subcategories may be overly complex for practical use.  
- Weakness: evaluation focused only on CoNLL-2014; broader dataset testing is needed for robustness.  
- Question: how does the rule-based classifier compare to state-of-the-art ML classifiers at larger scale?  
- Question: have you assessed or planned to mitigate errors from linguistic mark-up tools?  
- Question: can the framework be simplified or adapted for real-time applications (e.g., educational tools)?  
- Conclusion: valuable, practical contribution that should address evaluation limitations and extend analysis to other datasets.
