- Paper proposes automatic annotation/evaluation of GEC error types using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier, evaluated on CoNLL-2014.
- Contribution: automatic annotation method to standardize corpora and enable detailed error-type evaluation.
- Contribution: dataset-independent, rule-based classifier relying only on automatic features (POS tags, lemmas).
- Contribution: demonstrates detailed error-type analysis of GEC systems on CoNLL-2014 data.
- Strength: approach effectively annotates error types with high agreement between humans and the tool.
- Strength: provides detailed error-type analyses that reveal system strengths and weaknesses.
- Strength: dataset-independence of the classifier makes it a useful resource for the GEC community.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to CoNLL-2014, so generalization to other datasets is unclear.
- Weakness: rule-based classifier may struggle to capture complex error types and linguistic nuances.
- Weakness: no comparison to state-of-the-art GEC systems or existing error-annotation tools.
- Question: how will the method be extended to handle more complex error types and language nuances?
- Question: request for more details on the rule-based classifier design and development.
- Question: how will the authors evaluate the approach on other datasets or in different contexts?
- Additional comment: valuable contribution but requires broader evaluation and comparisons to fully assess effectiveness.
