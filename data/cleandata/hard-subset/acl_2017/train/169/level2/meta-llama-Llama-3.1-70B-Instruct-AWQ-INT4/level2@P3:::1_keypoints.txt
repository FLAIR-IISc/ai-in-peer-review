- Summary: Proposes automatic annotation of parallel GEC data with explicit edit spans and error types via linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based typing framework using POS tags and lemmas.

- Contribution 1: An automatic annotation method to standardize error-correction corpora and enable detailed error-type analyses.

- Contribution 2: A dataset-independent, rule-based error-typing framework that relies only on automatically obtained information (POS, lemmas).

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates a detailed error-type evaluation for all systems in the CoNLL-2014 GEC shared task.

- Strength 1: The approach is novel and distinct from existing annotation methods.

- Strength 2: Enables detailed evaluation of system error-type performance not possible with prior methods.

- Strength 3: The typing framework is dataset-independent and does not require labeled training data.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to CoNLL-2014 data, which may not generalize to other GEC datasets.

- Weakness 2: Rule-based error typing may be less effective than machine-learning approaches in some cases.

- Weakness 3: The method heavily depends on POS tagging accuracy, which can be unreliable.

- Question 1: How will the authors evaluate the approach on other error-correction datasets?

- Question 2: Please provide more details on the rule-based framework, including specific rules and decision-making logic.

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to address limitations of the rule-based framework, especially POS-tag dependence?

- Additional comment: Promising and useful work but should be validated on additional datasets and strengthened against POS/rule-based limitations.
