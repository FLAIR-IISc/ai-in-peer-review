- Summary: Proposes automatic annotation of GEC outputs using linguistically-enhanced alignment plus a rule-based classifier to enable error-type analysis of system outputs.

- Contribution 1: Method to extract edits via enhanced alignment and assign error types with a rule-based classifier.

- Contribution 2: Performs a detailed error-type analysis of all CoNLL-2014 teams, revealing fine-grained system strengths and weaknesses.

- Strength 1: Fills a significant gap in GEC evaluation by enabling per-error-type analysis of system outputs.

- Strength 2: Practical, dataset-independent rule-based approach relying on POS/lemmas with human evaluation showing 95%+ acceptable labels.

- Strength 3: CoNLL-2014 analysis yields useful insights about complementary system behaviors (e.g., CAMB vs CUUI).

- Weakness 1: Paper does not explain why this problem was not addressed earlier and poorly situates itself relative to prior work (e.g., Swanson & Yamangil).

- Weakness 2: Evaluation is limited—human validation covers only 200 edits from two datasets, undermining the dataset-independence claim.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient direct comparison to previous ML approaches on the same datasets/metrics despite claiming superiority.

- Weakness 4: Fails to adequately analyze limitations, especially how POS-tagging errors propagate and how to mitigate them.

- Question 1: How were the 25 error types selected and validated for coverage and usefulness?

- Question 2: How do the rule-based classifications map to existing human annotation guidelines used in prior GEC datasets?

- Question 3: Why does CUUI outperform CAMB on Replacement Determiner errors while CAMB has higher overall scores, and what does that imply about error-type vs overall performance?
