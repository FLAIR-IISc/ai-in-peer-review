- Proposes a dataset-independent automatic method to annotate error types in GEC outputs using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier.  
- Method uses only automatically obtained linguistic features (POS tags, lemmas, etc.) to assign error types.  
- Applied to CoNLL-2014 to produce the first detailed error-type analysis of GEC systems.  
- Addresses an important gap in GEC evaluation by enabling error-type performance analysis of system outputs.  
- Human evaluation shows high classifier quality (95% rated "Good" or "Acceptable").  
- Analysis reveals nuanced system strengths and weaknesses across 25 error types, demonstrating practical value.  
- Dataset independence is a notable advantage over prior ML-based methods that need dataset-specific training.  
- Insufficient comparison with prior work (e.g., Swanson & Yamangil 2012; Xue & Hwa 2014) and lacks direct comparative metrics.  
- Claim of dataset independence is under-validated, being tested only on CoNLL-2014 and FCE-test.  
- Limitations underaddressed: POS tagging errors can propagate to misclassification and multi-token edits show poor performance.  
- Recommendations: provide direct comparisons to prior methods, validate on more diverse GEC datasets, and analyze POS-dependent and multi-token limitations more thoroughly.  
- Overall, the work enables valuable new insights into GEC evaluation and represents a significant methodological contribution despite noted weaknesses.
