- Proposes a two-stage method (linguistically-enhanced alignment + rule-based classifier) to auto-annotate GEC outputs and compute per-error-type precision/recall/F-score.
- Releases a dataset-independent, rule-based annotation tool that uses automatic linguistic features for standardized, transparent evaluation.
- Delivers a detailed analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems with a 25-error-type breakdown, exposing system-specific strengths and weaknesses.
- High practical impact: enables fine-grained F-scores and diagnostic evaluation; tool is publicly released.
- Methodologically sound: rule-based approach avoids dataset-specific learned models; manual evaluation rated >95% of annotations as Good/Acceptable.
- Novel insights: reveals poor handling of "Unnecessary" token errors and widespread failure on multi-token edits.
- Limited classifier evaluation: manual sample is small (200 edits) and lacks quantitative analysis of how upstream POS/tagging errors affect annotations.
- Dependence on automatic "gold" references: reprocessing human references may discard nuances and the paper lacks analysis of disagreements with original gold annotations.
- Unclear treatment of complex/ambiguous edits: the "OTHER" category is a catch-all without discussion of its prevalence or typical contents.
