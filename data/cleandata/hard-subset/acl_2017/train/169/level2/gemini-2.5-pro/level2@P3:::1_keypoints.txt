- Introduces a novel fully automatic end-to-end pipeline (linguistically-enhanced alignment + dataset-independent rule-based classifier) for annotating GEC edits with fine-grained error types, enabling F-score evaluation per error category.  
- Applies the tool to re-evaluate CoNLL-2014 systems, providing detailed analyses across operation types, linguistic categories, multi-token edits, and detection vs. correction.  
- High impact and practical utility: fills a major gap in GEC evaluation by enabling precision/F-score diagnostics per error type and releasing a useful analysis tool.  
- Thorough and novel analysis: the CoNLL-2014 re-analysis uncovers actionable insights (e.g., many systems fail on "Unnecessary" tokens; architectures differ in error strengths), guiding future research and hybrids.  
- Methodologically sound: re-processing the gold reference with the same pipeline ensures fair comparisons and shows no significant score differences versus original gold.  
- Convincing classifier reliability: manual evaluation by five experts rated over 95% of classifier outputs as "Good" or "Acceptable."  
- Weakness — limited detail on classifier rules: the paper lacks illustrative rule examples (e.g., distinguishing VERB:SVA vs. VERB:TENSE vs. VERB:FORM), limiting clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness — unclear extensibility: the paper does not discuss how easy it is to add or refine error categories in the rule-based typology or the engineering effort required.  
- Weakness — unquantified impact of edit extraction errors: classifier evaluation ignores span correctness, leaving unclear how extraction mistakes (e.g., merged edits) may bias error-type distributions.
