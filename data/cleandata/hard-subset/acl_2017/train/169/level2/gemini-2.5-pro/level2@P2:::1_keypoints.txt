- Introduces an automatic error-type annotation method for GEC using linguistically-enhanced alignment plus a rule-based classifier over POS/lemmas.

- Contribution: a practical tool/methodology that annotates hypotheses and references consistently to compute precision/recall/F-scores per error category (tool to be released).

- Contribution: the first detailed 25-way error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014, revealing strengths/weaknesses of different GEC architectures (e.g., MT vs classifier).

- Contribution: a dataset-agnostic, rule-based error-classifier requiring no training; human evaluation rated 95% of outputs as Good or Acceptable.

- Strength: high practical impact and clear motivation—fills a critical gap in fine-grained GEC evaluation and yields new insights via CoNLL-2014 analysis.

- Strength: thorough experimental analysis with breakdowns by operation type, 25 error types, multi-token edits, and detection vs correction, exposing concrete system patterns.

- Strength: sound methodology—re-processing gold to create an automatic reference makes comparisons fair; Table 4 shows overall rankings remain stable.

- Strength: novel and transparent choice of a rule-based classifier that avoids corpus-specific scheme issues and is deterministic; manual eval supports quality.

- Weakness: insufficient detail on the classifier rules—no examples provided, hurting reproducibility and weakening the claimed transparency.

- Weakness: criticism of the M2 scorer is overstated—switching to a fixed automatic annotation shifts alignment complexity and potential mismatches.

- Weakness: comparison to ML-based classifiers is unsubstantiated because evaluation metrics differ; no direct F1-style comparison against a gold standard.

- Question: provide example rules that distinguish related verb error categories (VERB:FORM, VERB:SVA, VERB:TENSE) for clarity and reproducibility.

- Question: does the stability observed when switching to the automatic reference hold at the individual error-category level, or does the annotator systematically disagree on certain types (e.g., OTHER)?
