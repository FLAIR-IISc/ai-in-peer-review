- Summary: Proposes a two-stage method (linguistically-enhanced alignment + rule-based classifier) for automatic, fine-grained error-type annotation and evaluation of GEC systems, applied to re-analyze CoNLL-2014.

- Contribution 1: A dataset-agnostic, rule-based classifier that enables per-error-type precision/recall/F without needing manually annotated training data.

- Contribution 2: A detailed, fine-grained re-analysis of CoNLL-2014 revealing system-specific strengths and weaknesses across error categories.

- Strength 1: Enables deeper diagnostics beyond a single F-score (per-error precision/recall/F, multi-token handling, detection vs correction).

- Strength 2: Practical, high-quality approach using standard preprocessing tools; human evaluation rates >95% of automatic labels as Good/Acceptable.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive empirical demonstration with insightful findings (e.g., many systems fail on "Unnecessary" tokens; MT systems handle multi-token edits better).

- Weakness 1: Core rule-based classifier is treated as a black box with no example rules, rule-set size, or development process, harming reproducibility and critique.

- Weakness 2: Scope limited to English; unclear how to adapt to other languages, different tagsets, or discontinuous edits (e.g., separable verbs).

- Weakness 3a: Superficial classifier evaluation lacking in-depth error analysis of "Bad" vs "Acceptable"/"Good" cases and hardest error types.

- Weakness 3b: Superficial CoNLL-2014 analysis that doesn’t sufficiently consider original system designs when interpreting zero scores or unexpected failures.

- Question 1: Please provide example rules or descriptions (e.g., how R:VERB:FORM is distinguished from R:VERB:TENSE).

- Question 2: What are the main challenges in adapting the framework to languages with different morphology/syntax or discontinuous constituents?

- Question 3: For CUUI’s 0.0 on Word Order, is this due to the system’s design (no reordering) or potential misclassification by your tool?

- Question 4: On what data were the bootstrap resampling significance tests performed (system-level F0.5, sentence-level, or document-level)?
