- Two-stage method: linguistically-enhanced alignment extracts edits, then a rule-based classifier labels error types; applied to CoNLL-2014 system outputs for fine-grained evaluation.

- Contribution: provides detailed performance breakdown across 25 error categories for CoNLL-2014 systems.

- Contribution: releases a tool for automatic GEC annotation useful to researchers, educators, and learners.

- Strength: addresses a practical need by enabling error-type feedback beyond corrected text.

- Strength: enables nuanced, error-type-specific comparisons that reveal complementary strengths of different GEC architectures.

- Weakness: rule-based classifier is severely underspecified—no rule examples, counts, structure, or ordering, hindering scrutiny and reproducibility.

- Weakness: classifier evaluation is methodologically weak—raters judged pre-annotated output (inflating agreement), reporting masks disagreement, and test-set composition is unspecified.

- Weakness: unclear primary contribution—insufficient justification for new typology, lack of comparison to established tagsets (e.g., CoNLL-2014), and analysis is largely descriptive rather than explanatory.

- Weakness: dataset-independence claim is questionable because of reliance on tools (e.g., Hunspell) and manually tuned, domain/language-dependent rules.

- Question: provide technical details about the rule-based classifier—sample rules, total number, and rule application/ordering/conflict resolution.

- Question: give examples of "unusual" edit boundaries and discuss their potential negative impact on evaluation utility.

- Question: explain rationale for creating a new error typology instead of adopting existing schemes like CoNLL-2014.

- Question: justify why raters judged system output instead of producing an independent gold standard for evaluation.
