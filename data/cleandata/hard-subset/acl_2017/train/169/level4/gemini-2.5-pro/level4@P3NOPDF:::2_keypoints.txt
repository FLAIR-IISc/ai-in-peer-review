- Contribution: Proposes an automated framework to evaluate GEC systems by error type, computing both precision and recall without requiring manual annotations and handling multi-token errors.  
- Strength: Enables per-error-type precision, operates without manual output annotations, and supports multi-token edits.  
- Strength: Demonstrated utility across diverse GEC systems and supported by human validation showing experts approved most automatic tags.  
- Major weakness: No description of the rules used to derive error types, preventing replication or assessment of rule complexity and alignment dependence.  
- Major weakness: Classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and may be biased by the human validation setup.  
- Major weakness: No discussion of applicability or adaptation challenges for languages other than English.  
- Detail: Paper omits basis/examples of rule set, making it unclear if rules derive from prior work or depend on alignment algorithm.  
- Detail: Language generalization is unaddressed; assumptions like continuous error spans fail for languages with discontinuous edits (e.g., German separable verbs).  
- Detail: Human validation may be biased by noisy automatic edit boundaries and binary rating choices; evaluation should use manual boundaries to isolate classifier errors.  
- Detail: Classifier error analysis is shallow—beyond blaming POS errors, the paper does not analyze failure modes or conditions of breakdown.  
- Detail: System performance analysis lacks clarity on significance testing (which metric used) and can be superficial or misleading without considering system design.  
- Detail: Reported zero scores (e.g., CUUI on word order) may indicate classification errors that are not investigated.  
- Suggestion: Report raw TP/FP/FN counts (e.g., in an appendix) for transparency and reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: Contextualize complementary strengths of systems with prior literature that observed similar complementarities.  
- Suggestion: Acknowledge prior modular/rule-based systems that could output error tags to better position the contribution.  
- Suggestion: Discuss how the method handles or could be extended to discontinuous edits.  
- Suggestion: Fix reference formatting issues (capitalization, page info, URL wrapping).
