- Paper proposes automatic annotation of GEC outputs with specific error types, a useful idea for learners and system comparison.
- Strength: Provides explicit error-type feedback valuable for pedagogy and missing in many GEC systems.
- Strength: Enables fine-grained, category-level comparison of GEC system performance beyond overall precision/recall.
- Major weakness: System description is critically underspecified; core rule set lacks details (creation process, size, complexity, examples).
- Major weakness: No explanation of rule ordering/prioritization or how conflicting rules are resolved.
- Evaluation is flawed: annotators rated pre-annotated output instead of independently creating a gold standard, inflating agreement.
- Evaluation reporting is misleading: averages hide possible high disagreement; no standard inter-annotator metrics (e.g., Fleiss' Kappa) reported.
- Evaluation lacks test-set characterization: no distribution of error categories or coverage information for the 200-sentence set.
- Terminology is vague (e.g., "edit boundaries might be unusual") and needs concrete examples and discussion of impact.
- Unsupported claim: System asserted to be less domain-dependent than trained systems without evidence or evaluation across domains.
- Unclear motivation for a new error tagset and no comparison to established tagsets (e.g., Nicholls 2003 or CoNLL-2014), missing justification.
- Central contribution is ambiguous—insufficient detail on system, tagset, or the value of the CoNLL-2014 analysis.
- Minor issues/typos: incorrect "c.f." usage, unclear "50-70 F1" notation, formatting of "M2" scorer, and inconsistent reference casing (e.g., "fleiss, kappa", "esl").
