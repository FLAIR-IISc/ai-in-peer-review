- Useful application for language teachers and learners.
- Enables more fine-grained comparisons of GEC systems by annotating error types.
- Core system description is highly superficial and lacks technical detail.
- The paper fails to provide examples, number, complexity, or interaction/order of the rules.
- Excessive space is devoted to re-evaluating CoNLL-2014 systems with redundant presentation of results.
- Evaluation relies on raters judging pre-annotated outputs instead of an independent gold-standard annotation.
- Rater agreement reporting is misleading (averaging individual scores hides per-edit disagreement).
- No information about test data distribution or which error categories were covered by positively rated annotations.
- The claim about unusual "edit boundaries" is unexplained and lacks examples or analysis of impact.
- Claim of domain-independence is unconvincing; rule-based components and resources (e.g., Hunspell) and language-specific rules introduce dependencies.
- All test data comes from a single, narrow domain (student essays), undermining generality claims.
- Motivation for a new error-category set is unclear given existing tagsets (e.g., Nicholls 2003) and omission of CoNLL-2014 tagset comparison.
- Main contribution is unclear: unclear whether it’s a new system, new tagset, or superficial re-evaluation.
- Minor issues/typos: use "cf." not "c.f.", write M² (superscript), clarify "50-70 F1" as percent, and fix reference capitalization (e.g., ESL, Fleiss, Kappa).
