- Proposes a two-stage pipeline (linguistically-enhanced alignment + rule-based, dataset-independent classifier) to automatically annotate GEC system output and analyze CoNLL-2014 per-error-type precision/recall/F.

- Contribution: first fine-grained, per-error-type precision and F-score analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems.

- Contribution: a method and planned public tool for automatic error annotation of GEC outputs.

- Strength: addresses a clear evaluation gap by enabling error-type precision and F-scores, giving a more nuanced assessment than aggregate scores.

- Strength: provides actionable insights into system strengths/weaknesses (e.g., systems excelling at specific error types) and highlights multi-token edit shortcomings.

- Strength: commitment to release the annotation tool enables replication and wider use.

- Weakness: core method is underspecified—no example rules, rule counts, ordering, or conflict-resolution details, harming reproducibility.

- Weakness: classifier evaluation protocol is weak because pre-annotated judgments inflate agreement; an independently annotated gold standard would be preferable.

- Weakness: reporting is potentially misleading—averaging raters’ “Good/Acceptable” hides that up to ~18.5% of edits might have been judged “Bad” by at least one rater.

- Weakness: insufficient justification for introducing a new 25-category error taxonomy instead of using/adapting the CoNLL-2014 tagset, which would permit direct validation against the human-annotated gold standard.

- Question: please provide representative rules, approximate rule count, and how rule ordering/conflicts are handled in the classifier.

- Question: report the percentage of edits marked “Bad” by at least one annotator.

- Question: why develop a new error schema instead of using or adapting the CoNLL-2014 schema to enable direct automatic evaluation against the original gold standard?
