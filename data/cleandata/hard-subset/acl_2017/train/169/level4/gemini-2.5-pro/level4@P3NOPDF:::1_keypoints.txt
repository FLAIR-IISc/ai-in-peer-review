- Useful application: the tool addresses a clear need for error-type feedback in GEC for learners and teachers.  
- Enables finer-grained analysis: tagging edits with error types allows diagnostic comparison of GEC systems beyond aggregate metrics.  

- Superficial system description: the rule-based core is under-described, preventing understanding or reproducibility.  
- Unknown rule provenance: unclear whether rules were created manually or semi-automatically and what resources/expertise informed them.  
- Missing rule examples: no concrete rule examples to show format, complexity, or linguistic basis.  
- Undocumented rule set details: total number, structure, ordering/priorities, and potential rule interactions/blocking are not reported.  
- Poor paper organization: excessive space devoted to re-evaluating CoNLL-2014 systems instead of explaining the authors’ system.  

- No independent gold standard: annotators rated pre-annotated output rather than producing independent annotations, likely inflating agreement.  
- Incorrect reporting of rater agreement: averaging "Bad" ratings is statistically unsound; proper inter-annotator metrics (e.g., Fleiss’ Kappa) are needed.  
- Insufficient test-data detail: the 200-sentence test set’s error-category distribution and coverage are not reported.  
- Vague terminology: terms like “unusual edit boundaries” are undefined and unsupported with examples.  

- Unjustified new tagset: the authors fail to motivate why existing tagsets (e.g., Nicholls 2003, CoNLL-2014) are insufficient.  
- Questionable domain-independence claim: reliance on tools like Hunspell and manually crafted, language-tuned rules undermines the claim, and evaluation is limited to one domain.  

- Conclusion: the main contribution is unclear (poorly described system, weakly motivated tagset, superficial re-evaluation); recommend rejection with major revision for better description and rigorous evaluation.  

- Minor issues/typos: fix "c.f."→"cf.", clarify "50-70 F1", write M² for M2, and correct reference capitalization (e.g., "Fleiss, Kappa", "ESL").
