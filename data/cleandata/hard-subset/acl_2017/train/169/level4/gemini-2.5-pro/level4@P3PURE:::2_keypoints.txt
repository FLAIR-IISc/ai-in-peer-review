- Novel evaluation framework enables assessing GEC performance by error type in both precision and recall.
- Framework is an important contribution for more fine-grained analysis of GEC behavior.
- Paper provides broad evaluation across a variety of existing GEC systems.
- Method advantages: enables precision by error type, independent of manual error annotation, and handles multi-token errors.
- Human evaluation indicates automatically assigned error tags for pre-computed edit spans are largely considered appropriate.
- The rules used to derive error types are not described, a major omission.
- Classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and offers limited guidance for improving the classifier.
- The taxonomy basis is unclear; authors should state whether it builds on prior work.
- Classification rules likely depend on the aligner, but the paper gives no details or examples of these rules.
- Without rule details (number, complexity), readers cannot estimate effort to adapt approach to other languages.
- Evaluation is confined to English and does not discuss adapting the method to other languages.
- Pre-computed edit spans are continuous and may not handle discontinuous edits (e.g., separable verbs in German).
- Human evaluation may be biased (raters shown system tag, binary scale) and should include judgments on gold-standard boundaries.
- The paper lacks analysis of conditions leading to "Bad" vs. "Appropriate" ratings and of classifier failure modes (e.g., POS tagging errors).
- It is unclear what data were used for significance tests; relying on F0.5 may be problematic due to weak discriminative power.
- Comparison between automatic and gold references appears to address boundaries but not classification labels.
- Some systems’ failure on certain error types (e.g., unnecessary tokens, word order) may reflect system design rather than evaluation errors, which the paper overlooks.
- The analysis should report raw TP/FP/FN counts (e.g., in an appendix) to clarify instance counts and enable alternate metrics.
- The finding that different systems excel in different categories aligns with prior work and should be noted.
- Multi-token error analysis is useful but needs interpretation acknowledging some systems cannot correct such errors and training data limitations.
- The paper should acknowledge that modular systems could already produce error-type annotations; its main advantage is providing a unified framework for all system types.
- References and citation formatting/page info need fixing.
- Reviewer: core idea is strong but paper not ready; omission of classification rules and lack of language-generalization discussion are fatal shortcomings for reproducibility and portability.
