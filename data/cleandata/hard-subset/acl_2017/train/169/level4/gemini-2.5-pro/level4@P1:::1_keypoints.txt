- Two-step method: linguistically-enhanced alignment to extract edits, then a rule-based, claimed dataset-independent classifier to label GEC errors.  
- Evaluated via a small-scale human rating study and applied to CoNLL-2014 system outputs for the first error-type-specific analysis.  
- Main contributions: a tool for fine-grained GEC evaluation and a detailed per-error analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems.  
- Strength: High practical utility for system developers and end-users (learners/teachers) by providing explicit error-type feedback.  
- Strength: Enables deeper system comparison by reporting per-category precision/recall, revealing complementary strengths across systems.  
- Weakness: Poor description of the rule-based classifier—no examples, count, complexity, or rule ordering/conflict-resolution explained.  
- Weakness: Evaluation methodology is weak—raters rated pre-annotated labels instead of independently annotating a gold standard, likely inflating agreement.  
- Weakness: Presentation of human agreement is misleading; averages hide per-edit disagreement (possible substantial minority of “Bad” ratings).  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on test data—no distribution of error categories in the 200-item sample.  
- Weakness: Claim of dataset-independence is unsupported—relies on external resources (e.g., Hunspell), manual rules, and evaluation on a single domain (student essays).  
- Weakness: Introduction of a new error schema is unjustified; using CoNLL-2014 tags would enable direct comparison to gold standard.  
- Weakness: Unclear primary contribution (the system, the new schema, or the analysis) given shallow method description and weak motivation for the schema.  
- Question: Provide concrete details/examples of rules, total number, and how rule conflicts are resolved.  
- Question: Explain rationale for creating a new tagset instead of using CoNLL-2014’s tagset.  
- Question: Clarify what makes automatic edit boundaries “unusual,” give examples, and discuss practical implications.  
- Miscellaneous: Fix minor typos/formatting (use “cf.”, M² notation), clarify “50-70 F1” ambiguity, and normalize reference capitalization.
