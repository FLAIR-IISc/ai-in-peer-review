- Introduces a novel method to evaluate GEC systems by error type measuring both recall and precision.  
- Overcomes prior inability to evaluate precision by error type without error-category annotations in outputs.  
- Strength: important step forward for analyzing GEC system behavior.  
- Strength: evaluates a variety of GEC systems, giving a comprehensive overview.  
- Strength: computes precision by error type, is independent of manual annotation, and handles multi-token errors.  
- Strength: automatically selected error tags for pre-computed spans are largely approved by human experts.  
- Weakness: rules for deriving/assigning error types are not described, harming replicability and adaptability.  
- Weakness: classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis to guide future improvements.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to English and lacks discussion of cross-lingual applicability or adaptation effort.  
- Concern: rules likely depend on alignment algorithm though claimed independence; transparency on this dependency is missing.  
- Limitation: pre-computed error spans appear continuous and may miss discontinuous edits (e.g., separable verbs in German).  
- Human evaluation may be biased by noise from automatic edit extraction; need assessment with manually created boundaries.  
- Comparison between gold and auto references is unclear and seems limited to boundary agreement rather than classification differences.  
- Results reporting lacks raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts (e.g., Table 6) needed for alternative measures and clearer comparison.  
- Interpretation gaps: some systems’ failures on certain error types may reflect design choices; multi-token error analysis needs more interpretation.  
- Clarification: some systems could previously provide error annotations (e.g., submodules), but the proposed method enables unified comparison across systems.  
- Minor issues: reference formatting errors and missing citation details.  
- Recommendation: not ready for publication until error-type rules and cross-lingual generalization are fully described and evaluated.
