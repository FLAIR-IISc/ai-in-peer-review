- Contribution: Proposes a dataset-independent framework to automatically annotate GEC system outputs with error types using alignment + rule-based classification.  
- Contribution: Validates the automatic annotation method on CoNLL-2014 shared task data.  
- Contribution: Provides a detailed error-type analysis of GEC systems, revealing per-category performance differences.  

- Strength: Novel evaluation capability—enables error-type-specific analysis across 25 categories, overcoming prior recall-only evaluation.  
- Strength: Dataset independence—the rule-based classifier uses automatic features (POS, lemmas) and requires no labeled training data.  
- Strength: Strong validation—human evaluation (5 experts, 200 edits) shows 95%+ agreement and κ = 0.724.  

- Weakness: Superficial system description—no concrete examples, counts, complexity, or ordering/conflict-resolution logic for rules.  
- Weakness: Flawed human evaluation—raters judged predicted labels rather than producing an independent gold standard, inflating agreement and omitting "Bad" rating distributions.  
- Weakness: Unclear motivation for new 25-category framework—no clear justification or comparison to existing tagsets (e.g., CoNLL-2014, Nicholls 2003).  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—validated only on student essays; reliance on Hunspell and standard POS taggers may not generalize to other domains.  

- Question: Provide concrete rule examples, total rule count, and how rule ordering/conflicts are resolved.  
- Question: Why create a new error-type framework instead of adapting existing ones, and how is it an improvement?  
- Question: How is "dataset-independence" justified given domain-specific resources (Hunspell, POS taggers), and how would the system perform on non-learner text?  

- Comment: Typos—use "cf." instead of "c.f."; clarify "F1" as "F1-score (50–70%)."  
- Comment: Unsupported claim—paper asserts M2 scorer "overestimates performance" but Table 4 does not support this.  
- Comment: Redundancy—the "operation tier" analysis overlaps with Table 5 and should be condensed.  

- Overall: Addresses an important evaluation gap but lacks sufficient technical detail, has methodological weaknesses, and limited validation; needs substantial revision to meet ACL standards.
