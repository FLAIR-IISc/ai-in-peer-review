- Summary: Proposes automatic annotation of GEC system outputs with error types to enable precision-based, fine-grained evaluation.
- Contribution 1: Linguistically-enhanced alignment method for automatic edit extraction.
- Contribution 2: Dataset-independent, rule-based classifier that assigns error types using only automatically derived linguistic properties.
- Contribution 3: First comprehensive error-type analysis of systems from the CoNLL-2014 shared task.
- Strength: Enables precision-based and granular error-type evaluation that reveals system-specific strengths/weaknesses beyond overall scores.
- Strength: Rule-based classifier shows strong human agreement (95%+ "Good" or "Acceptable") without requiring labeled training data.
- Strength: Supports multi-dimensional analysis (operation type, error category, combinations) for nuanced insights.
- Weakness: Error-classification rules are under-described, hindering reproducibility and understanding of rule complexity or edge-case handling.
- Weakness: Human evaluation used automatically extracted edit boundaries, introducing noise and potential bias toward system outputs.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to English with no discussion of adaptation needs or generalizability to other morphosyntactic systems.
- Question: Provide concrete examples of error classification rules, especially for multi-step or complex error types.
- Question: How would the method handle discontinuous error spans (e.g., separable verb prefixes in German)?
- Question: Which linguistic properties must be modified to apply the method to languages with different morphology or syntax?
