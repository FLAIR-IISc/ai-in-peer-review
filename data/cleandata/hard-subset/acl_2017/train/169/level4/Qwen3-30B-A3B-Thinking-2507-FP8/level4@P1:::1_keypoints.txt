- Introduces a two-stage method to automatically annotate GEC system outputs with error types (linguistically-enhanced alignment + rule-based classifier) and analyzes CoNLL-2014 systems.  
- Contributions: practical automatic error-type annotation, dataset-independent rule-based classifier, first detailed error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014.  

- Strength: solves a real GEC evaluation gap by enabling error-type level analysis of system outputs.  
- Strength: manual evaluation (5 raters, 200 edits) reports >95% predicted types rated Good/Acceptable, suggesting classifier effectiveness.  
- Strength: practical impact—enables actionable per-error-type comparisons across systems (demonstrated on CoNLL-2014).  

- Weakness: rule-based classifier is described superficially—missing number/complexity of rules, ordering logic, examples, and ambiguity handling (e.g., "ring → rings").  
- Weakness: flawed evaluation protocol—raters judged predictions rather than creating gold labels, likely inflating results and possibly masking up to ~18.5% problematic edits.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for the chosen 25-category tagset and lack of comparison to existing tagsets (e.g., CoNLL-2014) that would enable direct gold-standard comparisons.  
- Weakness: limited domain coverage—evaluation only on student essays and reliance on resources like Hunspell call dataset-independence into question.  

- Question: provide concrete rule examples (e.g., rule for VERB:SVA) and clarify rule ordering and conflict resolution.  
- Question: why not use CoNLL-2014’s tagset to allow direct comparison with gold annotations?  
- Question: how does the system generalize to non-student domains given reliance on domain-specific resources like Hunspell?  

- Additional: CoNLL-2014 result discussion is repetitive and could synthesize insights better.  
- Additional: typos and notation issues noted (e.g., "c.f." → "cf.", "M2" → "M²", clarify "50-70 F1" as "%").  
- Additional: claim that "M2 scorer overestimates performance" is interesting but needs stronger evidence (direct comparison with gold edit boundaries).  

- Overall: tackles an important problem with a practical approach, but opaque method description and a flawed evaluation weaken confidence; with rule details and gold-standard evaluation this could be a strong contribution, but currently weaknesses outweigh strengths.
