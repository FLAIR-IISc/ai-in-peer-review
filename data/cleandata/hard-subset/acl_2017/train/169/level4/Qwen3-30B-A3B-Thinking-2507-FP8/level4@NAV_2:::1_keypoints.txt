- Presents a useful method for automatically annotating error types in GEC outputs with clear utility for education and error-type-specific system comparison.  
- Core system description is overly superficial, especially the rule-based classifier.  
- Paper omits essential rule details: examples, total number, complexity, and rule ordering.  
- Excessive space devoted to CoNLL-2014 results (large table) without substantive interpretation.  
- Evaluation should have used a gold standard (annotators labeling sentences) instead of only rating system outputs.  
- Rater results are misleading: authors report averaged scores instead of inter-annotator agreement, masking disagreement (potentially 18.5% of edits flagged "bad" by at least one annotator).  
- No information on test data characteristics (number of error categories covered or which categories were evaluated).  
- Claim that "edit boundaries might be unusual" is vague and needs concrete examples.  
- Claim that the system is less domain-dependent than ML approaches is questionable given Hunspell coverage variability and language-dependence of manual rules; test data is limited to student essays.  
- Motivation for a new error category set is inadequately justified; authors do not compare or justify superiority over existing taxonomies (e.g., Nicholls 2003, CoNLL-2014).  
- Overall contribution is unclear: not convincingly a novel system, a justified new taxonomy, or a substantive CoNLL-2014 evaluation.
