- Proposes enriching GEC outputs with explicit error types—useful for educators and fine-grained benchmarking.  
- System description is superficial: omits number/complexity/ordering/conflict-resolution of rules and concrete examples; wastes space on redundant CoNLL-2014 results.  
- Evaluation lacks a gold standard: annotators rated system outputs instead of independently annotating test sentences, likely inflating agreement.  
- Inter-annotator agreement is misreported: average metrics hide that 18.5% of edits were marked "bad" by at least one rater, with no disagreement distribution provided.  
- Test data specification is insufficient: no error-category distribution, no list of covered categories, and no per-category quality rates, so evaluation scope is unclear.  
- Ambiguous phrasing ("edit boundaries might be unusual") is undefined and unsupported by examples or impact analysis.  
- Claim of being "less domain dependent" is unsubstantiated: manually crafted rules are domain-sensitive and only student-essay data were evaluated.  
- Motivation for a new error tagset is weak: existing tagsets (e.g., Nicholls, CoNLL-2014) could serve the stated goals and enable standard evaluation.  
- Main contribution is unclear: unclear whether the paper offers a novel system, a new tagset, or a superficial CoNLL-2014 evaluation.  
- Reproducibility and technical assessment are undermined by missing system details and flawed evaluation methodology.  
- Minor typos/formatting issues need correction (e.g., "c.f."→"cf.", "M2"→"M²", "50-70 F1"→"50-70% F1", consistent reference casing).
