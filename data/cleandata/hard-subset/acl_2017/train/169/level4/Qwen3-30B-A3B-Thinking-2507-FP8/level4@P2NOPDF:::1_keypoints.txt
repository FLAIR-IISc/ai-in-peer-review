- Addresses a valuable need by automatically enriching GEC outputs with error types, aiding teachers and learners.  
- Enables finer-grained comparison of GEC systems via error-type-specific precision and recall.  
- System description is insufficiently detailed; core rule-based approach lacks examples, rule count, complexity, ordering logic, and creation process.  
- Evaluation methodology is flawed and lacks necessary rigor and contextual information.  
- Gold standard issue: annotators rated the system’s output instead of creating an independent gold standard, likely inflating agreement.  
- Rater aggregation is mishandled: averaging scores misrepresents disagreement and hides that many edits had at least one "bad" rating.  
- Test data ambiguity: no information on error category definitions, coverage, or distribution to interpret results.  
- Unclear terminology (e.g., "edit boundaries might be unusual") without examples or explanation.  
- Unsupported claim of being "less domain-dependent": manual rules are inherently domain- and language-dependent, and evaluation used only student-essay data.  
- Motivation for a new error category set is unclear and unjustified; deviating from established tagsets prevents direct comparison to existing gold standards.  
- Core contribution is ambiguous—unclear whether the paper presents a novel system, a new tagset, or an evaluation of CoNLL-2014 systems.  
- Presentation issues: excessive space spent rehashing CoNLL-2014 results and redundant text.  
- Typos/formatting errors need fixing (examples: "c.f." → "cf.", "M2" → "M²", "50-70 F1" → "50–70% F1", correct reference casing and Fleiss’ κ).
