- Introduces a method to automatically annotate GEC system outputs with error-type information using linguistic alignment and rule-based typing.  
- Combines a linguistically-enhanced alignment for edit extraction with a dataset-independent rule-based classifier relying on POS tags and lemmas.  
- Validates the classifier via human evaluation (95%+ "Good" or "Acceptable") and applies it to analyze all CoNLL-2014 systems.  

Main contributions:
- Novel automatic annotation method enabling detailed error-type analysis beyond recall metrics.  
- Dataset-independent, transparent rule-based error-typing framework using only automatic linguistic features.  
- First comprehensive error-type analysis of all CoNLL-2014 shared-task systems, revealing distinct system strengths.  

Strengths:
- Fills a gap in GEC evaluation by enabling precision-based error-type analysis from system outputs.  
- Rule-based approach is transparent, training-data-free, and potentially more generalizable than black-box ML methods.  
- Strong human validation with high rater agreement despite noisy automatic edit extraction.  

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient detail on the error classification rules (derivation, examples, number), harming replicability and adaptation.  
- Superficial classifier evaluation: lacks analysis of error causes, failure modes, and concrete improvement suggestions.  
- Limited to English with no discussion of cross-linguistic generalization or required adaptations.  
- Edit/span extraction appears continuous and may fail for discontinuous phenomena (e.g., German separable verbs) with no mitigation discussed.  
- Potential human-evaluation bias: raters saw system outputs, no neutral middle rating, possibly inflating positive assessments.  

Questions to authors:
- Please provide concrete examples of error-classification rules to show their structure and complexity.  
- How would the approach handle discontinuous error spans (e.g., German separable verbs) and what adaptations are needed?  
- Were systems like UFC and AMU designed to handle the noted unnecessary-token errors, and can you clarify reasons for their failures?  
- Can you provide raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts for Table 6 to allow alternative metrics, especially for low-count error types?
