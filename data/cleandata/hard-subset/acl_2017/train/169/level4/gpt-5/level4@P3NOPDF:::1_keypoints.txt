- Motivation: Labeling error types for GEC is timely and useful for pedagogy and fine-grained system analysis.  
- Practical value: Post-hoc labeling can enable comparisons across systems beyond aggregate precision/recall or F-scores.  
- Strength: Focused on broadly useful post-processing of existing GEC outputs.

- Weakness: Method description is too superficial to assess validity or reproduce results.  
- Weakness: Evaluation design is unconvincing—no independent gold standard, limited inter-annotator agreement reporting, and unclear rating aggregation.  
- Weakness: Claims of domain and language independence are unsubstantiated.  
- Weakness: Rationale for a new tagset is underdeveloped and lacks alignment with existing tagsets.  
- Weakness: Excessive space devoted to CoNLL-2014 system tables with little analytical insight; core method details omitted.

- Method reproducibility: Provide number/creation of rules, examples, rule complexity, coverage by error type, and release rules/mappings/scripts.  
- Rule interactions: Describe rule ordering, conflict resolution, overlapping matches, tie-breaking, ambiguity handling, and defaults.  
- Resources and preprocessing: Specify tokenizers, POS taggers, parsers, spellcheckers (e.g., Hunspell) with versions/configs and lexicon coverage effects.

- Evaluation design: Create an independently annotated gold standard (e.g., for 200 sentences) and report per-type precision/recall.  
- Inter-annotator agreement: Report IAA for labels and edit boundaries (e.g., Fleiss’ kappa or Krippendorff’s alpha) with confidence intervals.  
- Aggregation transparency: Report per-edit rater distributions (counts of Good/Acceptable/Bad), proportion with at least one Bad, per-type breakdowns, and confusion matrices.  
- Test set characterization: Report error-category distribution, edits per type, coverage, and treatment of long-tail categories.  
- Baselines and ablations: Include simple baselines (alignment heuristics/frequency defaults) and ablation studies to show component contributions.  
- Error analysis: Provide qualitative examples of correct/incorrect label assignments and typical failure modes.

- Edit boundaries: Clarify what “unusual” edit boundaries mean with examples and quantify impacts versus M2/minimal-edit conventions.  
- Domain dependence: Substantiate domain-independence claims with cross-domain evaluation or qualify claims and discuss lexicon coverage effects.  
- Language portability: Discuss effort and design choices needed to port rules to other languages and the extent of language dependence.

- Tagset design: Justify the new tagset versus existing schemes, explain granularity trade-offs, and provide explicit mappings to prior tagsets (with examples).  
- Use of space: Move exhaustive CoNLL-2014 tables to appendix and focus main text on substantive insights enabled by labels (per-type strengths/weaknesses).  
- Contributions and clarity: Clearly state whether the contribution is the labeling system, tagset, or an evaluative study and align claims with supported evidence.  
- Minor issues: Fix typos, formatting (cf., M^2), clarify ambiguous percentages, and standardize capitalization in references and tag/resource names.
