- Contribution: automatic, rule-based method to label GEC edits with error types for per-type evaluation and feedback.
- Contribution: large-scale application to CoNLL-2014 outputs showing system behavior across error categories.

- Strength: practical educational value—labels make system errors actionable for teachers and learners.
- Strength: enables fine-grained system comparison (precision/recall by type, operation-level analysis).
- Strength: claims broad applicability since classifier uses automatic POS/lemma markup and no dataset-specific training.

- Weakness: rule set is under-described—no concrete examples, counts, complexity, ordering, conflict resolution, or coverage metrics.
- Weakness: evaluation lacks an independent gold standard—raters judged system labels rather than producing labels from scratch.
- Weakness: rater-aggregation reporting is misleading; per-rater summaries hide that many edits could be marked “Bad” by at least one rater.
- Weakness: missing test-data details—no report of how many categories appear in the 200-edit sample or category coverage among “Good” ratings.
- Weakness: “unusual” edit boundaries are mentioned but not exemplified, quantified, or analyzed for impact on labeling/matching.
- Weakness: domain-dependence claims are weak—Hunspell coverage, manual rules, and language dependence are not addressed and both eval sets are student essays.
- Weakness: rationale for a new taxonomy is unclear—existing schemes (CoNLL, Nicholls) might suffice and enable direct comparison.
- Weakness: unclear focus—uncertain whether the main contribution is the system, the taxonomy, or CoNLL-2014 analysis; paper repeats tables instead of deeper insights.

- Question: provide representative rules, number of rules, their complexity, ordering/priority, conflict resolution, and coverage diagnostics.
- Question: why not create a small gold standard for the 200 edits; report per-rater distributions and sampling across categories.
- Question: give concrete examples/frequencies of “unusual” edit boundaries and analyze their impact on type assignment and evaluation bias.
- Question: analyze domain and language dependence—how do Hunspell and POS/dependency errors affect labels, and what is needed to adapt rules?
- Question: justify new taxonomy vs. existing ones and provide mappings to CoNLL/Nicholls to enable comparison.
- Question: beyond restating tables, what are the main empirical takeaways from the CoNLL-2014 analysis that inform system design?

- Minor: fix “c.f.” → “cf.”, M2 typesetting, clarify “50–70 F1” as percent, and check capitalization/formatting in references and acronyms.
