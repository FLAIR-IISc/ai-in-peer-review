- Proposal: pipeline that extracts edits via linguistically informed alignment and assigns error categories with a dataset-agnostic rule-based classifier, enabling per-error-type precision and recall.
- Application: method applied to CoNLL-2014 outputs to deliver the first broad per-type analysis across systems, including operation tier, multi-token edits, and detection vs. correction.
- Human validation: a small study reports predicted error types are acceptable in ≥95% of cases.

Strengths
- Enables per–error-type precision, filling a gap beyond overall scores and recall-only analyses.
- Broad empirical application across many CoNLL-2014 systems yields actionable, system-specific insights.
- Practical: training-free, dataset-independent, automatic markup that fixes edit spans for multi-token and detection/correction analyses.
- Human validation supports category appropriateness despite alignment noise.
- Transparent evaluation design by decoupling edit extraction from scoring, highlighting differences from M2.

Weaknesses
- Insufficient detail on classifier rules (coverage, structure, examples, priority/conflict resolution), hindering reproducibility and critique.
- Classifier evaluation is limited and under-analyzed; human study lacks breakdown by error type and failure source.
- Unclear language generalization: portability to other tag sets, morphologies, tokenization, and discontinuous phenomena is under-specified.
- Mismatch analyses and significance testing are unclear (possible reliance on F0.5 only); divergence in P/R should be tested more informatively.
- Per-type result interpretation lacks context: must distinguish “not attempted” from “attempted but failed” and report raw TP/FP/FN counts to show sparsity.

Questions for authors
- Rule set: How many rules overall/per category, and what is their structure, priority, and examples?
- Dependencies: How tied are rules to specific POS/taggers/lemmatizers/parsers/Hunspell, and how hard is porting?
- Error scheme: Which categories are from prior schemes versus new, and how was granularity decided?
- Discontinuous edits: Can alignment/classifier handle non-contiguous spans, and if not, how to extend for such languages?
- Human study: Please report category-wise and failure-source breakdowns, and replicate with gold edit boundaries to measure alignment noise effects.

Additional comments
- Some systems’ zero scores on Unnecessary reflect design choices; analysis should reflect system intent and cite prior explanations.
- Provide TP/FP/FN counts per team/type (appendix) to expose low-support categories and enable re-analysis.
- Clarify which architectures are inherently limited on multi-token edits and note which systems were not trained for fluency rewrites.
- Emphasize the value of enabling unified cross-system tagging without modifying systems.
- Clean up references (capitalization, URLs, missing pages).

Post-rebuttal recommendations
- Publish substantive rule-set description (structure, representative rules, examples) to ensure replicability and extensibility.
- Explicitly address language generalization: which components are language/pipeline dependent and what redesign is needed for discontinuous constructions.
- Qualify claims about systems’ coverage of error types and separate “not attempted” from “attempted but failed” using system descriptions and annotations.
