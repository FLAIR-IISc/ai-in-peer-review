- Proposes an automatic pipeline to annotate unlabelled GEC outputs with edit spans and error types using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a deterministic rule-based classifier.  
- Validates the classifier with a small human study (>95% Good/Acceptable) and applies it to CoNLL-2014 for per-type precision/recall analysis including multi-token edits and detection vs correction.  
- Finds no significant difference when evaluating with gold vs automatically reprocessed references.  

- Contribution: dataset-independent pipeline enabling per-error-type precision and recall, including multi-token edits and a transparent edit-comparison scorer.  
- Contribution: comprehensive error-type analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems, exposing complementary strengths and under-addressed categories.  
- Contribution: deterministic, interpretable rule-based error-type classifier that avoids in-domain training.  

- Strength: enables per-type precision, allowing finer-grained diagnosis and fairer system comparisons.  
- Strength: dataset-agnostic, interpretable rules relying only on automatic tags/lemmas/parses and a spelling dictionary.  
- Strength: broad empirical analysis yields actionable findings about system strengths and weaknesses.  
- Strength: handles multi-token edits and separates detection from correction for deeper error diagnosis.  

- Weakness: rule set is under-specified—no details on rule inventory, priority, conflict resolution, or representative patterns.  
- Weakness: limited classifier evaluation—small human study conflates span noise and type accuracy; lacks confusion matrix and breakdowns by operation/edit length.  
- Weakness: unclear language portability—dependence on POS/lemma/parse/spellcheck not discussed for morphologically rich or non-Latin languages and discontinuous spans.  
- Weakness: evaluation methodology details missing—bootstrap test on F0.5 unclear, and raw per-type TP/FP/FN counts are not reported.  
- Weakness: interpretation sometimes ignores system design constraints, potentially misattributing failures to algorithmic difficulty.  

- Question: provide a compact description of the rule inventory, hierarchy, features used (tagsets, lemmas, deps, Hunspell), representative rules, tie-breaking, and sensitivity to tagger/parser.  
- Question: what modifications and engineering effort are needed to port the classifier to other languages, and how handle discontinuous edits or rich morphology?  
- Question: can the human study be rerun on manually segmented edits and provide a confusion matrix and analysis of mislabels by cause?  
- Question: clarify the bootstrap procedure—what was bootstrapped, whether types were reclassified in both gold/auto conditions, and provide raw TP/FP/FN per team/type.  
- Question: provide per-type detection metrics and compare them to correction metrics to show where systems fail.  

- Additional: clarify positioning relative to systems that could output their own error tags and emphasize the tool’s unifying evaluation advantage.  
- Additional: include raw counts (TP/FP/FN) in appendices for tables to support alternative analyses and reveal small-sample effects.  
- Additional: cross-reference surprising failures with system design and prior work; fix minor editorial/reference issues.  

- Overall: valuable and timely contribution for richer GEC evaluation, but needs substantially more rule detail, stronger validation/error analysis, and clearer portability discussion to be fully convincing.
