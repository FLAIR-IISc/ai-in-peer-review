- Presents a method to add error-type labels to GEC outputs, useful for teachers and learners.
- Enables fine-grained evaluation of GEC systems, including error-type-specific precision and recall.
- Practical and potentially impactful application for education and system comparison.
- System description is superficial: crucial implementation details (rule examples, number, complexity, ordering) are missing.
- Paper devotes excessive space to CoNLL-2014 system results rather than core system details.
- Evaluation should have used an independent gold standard for the 200 test sentences instead of only rating system outputs.
- Rater-aggregation is flawed: averaging individual rater acceptability masks that up to 18.5% of edits may be judged “bad” by at least one annotator.
- Test data characterization is missing: no breakdown of error categories present or covered by the system.
- The claim about “unusual” edit boundaries is vague and unsupported by precise definitions or examples.
- Domain-dependence claims are unconvincing: manual rules and resources like Hunspell can be domain- and language-dependent, and evaluation used only student-essay data.
- Motivation for introducing a new error category set is unclear; existing tagsets (Nicholls 2003 or CoNLL-2014) could have sufficed and enabled direct comparison to gold standards.
- Overall contribution is unclear: missing technical details if the contribution is a system, weak justification if it’s a tagset, and superficial presentation if it’s an evaluation.
- Minor issues and typos throughout (e.g., “c.f.” → “cf.”, “M2” → “M^2”, unclear F1 percent, reference capitalization errors).
