- Strength: Practical tool for educators and learners, providing explicit error-type annotations missing from many GEC systems.  
- Strength: Enables fine-grained comparisons of GEC systems, including overall and error-type-specific precision/recall.  
- Weakness: System description is overly shallow and lacks essential implementation details.  
- Missing details: No examples, counts, complexity, or ordering of the (manually?) crafted rules are provided.  
- Presentation critique: Paper over-focuses on tabulated CoNLL-2014 system results (Table 6) instead of core method details.  
- Evaluation flaw: Annotators judged system output rather than independently creating a gold standard for the 200 test sentences.  
- Rater reporting issue: Treating raters “individually” and averaging is nonstandard and may hide high disagreement; reported averages are misleading.  
- Data description gap: Test data lacks description of how many/error categories present and which categories were covered.  
- Ambiguity: “Edit boundaries might be unusual” needs clarification and examples to assess practical impact.  
- Domain-dependence claim questionable: Hand-authored rules, Hunspell lexicon, and language-specific rules likely make the system domain- and language-dependent; test sets are limited to student essays.  
- Tagset justification weak: Unclear why a new error tagset was introduced instead of using Nicholls (2003) or CoNLL-2014 for comparison and gold-standard leverage.  
- Paper goal ambiguous: Unclear whether contribution is a new system, a new taxonomy, or an evaluation study.  
- Typos and casing errors: Several instances (e.g., “c.f.” -> “cf.”, “M2” -> “M^2”, “50–70 F1” ambiguity, “esl” -> “ESL”, “fleiss, kappa” -> “Fleiss, Kappa”).
