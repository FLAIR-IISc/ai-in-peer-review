- Proposes a framework to evaluate GEC systems by error type reporting precision and recall without manual error-tagged outputs.  
- Method enables precision-by-type evaluation and handles multi-token errors without manual annotation.  
- Evaluation across multiple systems yields informative cross-system comparisons and human judgments largely support automatic tags.  
- Core rules for deriving error types are not described, reducing reproducibility and interpretability.  
- Classifier evaluation lacks detailed error analysis and concrete improvement strategies.  
- Method is evaluated only on English and does not address porting to other languages or morphologically rich/free-word-order scenarios.  
- Treatment of edit spans appears limited to continuous spans, potentially failing for languages requiring discontinuous edits.  
- Some analyses conflate evaluation artifacts with system capabilities and ignore systems’ intended design targets.  
- Paper fails to explain how error categories were devised or whether grounded in prior taxonomies, and omits representative rules and coverage stats.  
- Rules likely depend on alignment and preprocessing; the pipeline dependency is not clarified.  
- No discussion or estimate of effort needed to adapt the method to other languages or handle discontinuous edits.  
- Human judgment study may be biased by noisy boundaries and a skewed rating scale; inter-annotator agreement and adjudication are missing.  
- Classifier error analysis should identify which categories are error-prone, common confusions, and boundary vs feature error sources, with concrete fixes suggested.  
- Significance testing is unclear; recommendation to test per-type precision/recall with bootstrapped CIs rather than conflated F0.5 tests.  
- Results should account for system scope (design targets) and provide raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts by system and error type to reveal small-sample effects.  
- Multi-token analysis is useful but needs discussion of how training data and model classes limit system performance on such edits.  
- Acknowledge that some modular systems can output error-tagged corrections; the paper’s value is a unified, system-agnostic comparison method.  
- Presentation needs clearer rule examples, preprocessing dependencies, and reference/formatting fixes.  
- Overall: promising and important contribution but requires substantial revisions (rule documentation, stronger human eval, clear significance testing, raw counts, multilingual scope) before acceptance.
