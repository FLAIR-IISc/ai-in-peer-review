- Paper proposes automatic annotation of parallel GEC data with explicit edit spans and error-type labels to enable per-error-type evaluation.
- Main contributions: automatic annotation method, detailed error-type evaluation, and analysis of GEC system performance by error type.
- Strength: useful stepping stone for analyzing GEC system behavior.
- Strength: evaluation covers a variety of systems.
- Strength: method advantages—computes precision by error type, removes need for manual annotation, and handles multi-token errors.
- Strength: auto-selected error tags largely validated by human experts.
- Weakness: rules used to derive error types are not described.
- Weakness: classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and guidance for improvement.
- Weakness: experiments are only in English; portability to other languages is unclear.
- Classifier critique: unclear whether error-type rules depend on alignment algorithm; examples of rules are missing.
- Gold vs. auto-reference critique: unclear which data were used for significance tests; comparison focuses on boundaries not classification.
- Error-type evaluation critique: authors do not relate results to system design; raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts should be reported (e.g., for Table 6).
- Multi-token error analysis critique: results need interpretation—some systems may be inherently unable to correct such errors due to training data.
- Other note: authors should acknowledge some GEC approaches could already produce annotations; proposed method enables unified comparison.
- Questions for authors requested: detailed rules, adaptation plan for other languages, raw confusion counts for Table 6, interpretation of multi-token results, and discussion of limitations/future work.
