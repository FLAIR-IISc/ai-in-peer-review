- Paper proposes a novel method to evaluate GEC systems by error type with both recall and precision, avoiding manual annotation.
- Strength: method advances analysis of GEC system behavior and includes comprehensive evaluation across systems.
- Strength: approach computes precision by error type, handles multi-token errors, and is independent of manual error annotation.
- Strength: automatically selected error tags for pre-computed spans are largely approved by human experts.
- Major weakness: rules for deriving and assigning error types are not described in sufficient detail for replication.
- Major weakness: classifier evaluation lacks a thorough error analysis to identify failure modes and improvement directions.
- Major weakness: evaluation is limited to English and unclear how much effort is needed to adapt the approach to other languages.
- Unclear basis for error categories and whether they derive from prior work; possible dependence of rules on the alignment algorithm is not detailed.
- Need explicit information on rules complexity and number of rules per error type to estimate adaptation effort for other languages.
- Pre-processing computes continuous error spans but cannot clearly handle discontinuous spans (e.g., German separable verbs).
- Human rater validation may be biased by noisy automatic boundaries, exposure to system outputs, and lack of intermediate rating options.
- Authors should evaluate classifier ratings using manually created boundaries to remove boundary-noise bias.
- Classifier "Bad" judgments are attributed to POS errors but require deeper analysis of when and why raters pick "Bad" vs "Appropriate."
- Significance testing methods are unclear (which data were tested); using F0.5 may mask differences in recall vs precision.
- Comparison between gold and auto reference focuses on boundaries, not classification, leaving mismatch sources underexplored.
- Some systems failing on certain error types (e.g., unnecessary tokens) may reflect system design limits, but the paper does not analyze this.
- Authors should report raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts (e.g., for Table 6) to allow alternative measures and reveal sparsity in some cells.
- Multi-token error analysis is useful but needs more interpretation, considering system training data limitations.
- Paper should acknowledge prior possibilities for error-tagged outputs from systems with specialized submodules and clarify unified comparison benefits.
- Minor issues: reference formatting and missing bibliographic details need fixing.
- Reviewer conclusion/author response: reviewer agrees approach is promising but not ready for publication until rules are fully specified, generalization is addressed, and analyses are strengthened.
