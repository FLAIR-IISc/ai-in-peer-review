- Proposes a novel automatic method to annotate parallel GEC data with edit spans and error types to evaluate system performance by error type (precision and recall) using linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier.

- Contribution: Automatic annotation of error types enabling error-type-specific evaluation of GEC outputs.

- Contribution: Dataset-independent evaluation that does not require manual error annotation and handles multi-token errors.

- Contribution: Demonstrates detailed error-type performance analysis across all CoNLL-2014 GEC systems.

- Strength: Provides an important step forward for analyzing GEC system behavior.

- Strength: Evaluation covers a variety of systems, showing broad applicability.

- Strength: Offers advantages over prior work, including per-type precision, independence from manual tags, and multi-token error assessment.

- Strength: Automatically selected error tags for pre-computed spans are largely endorsed by human experts.

- Weakness: Paper lacks a detailed description of the rule set used to assign error types, hindering reproducibility and understanding of limits.

- Weakness: Classifier evaluation is limited and missing thorough error analysis or concrete improvement directions.

- Weakness: Unclear how the approach generalizes or can be adapted to other languages.

- Weakness: Missing raw contingency values (TP/FP/TN/FN) in the appendix, reducing transparency and comparability.

- Question: Request for detailed rules used to assign error types and their development process.

- Question: How will the classifier be improved and what are the current approach’s limitations?

- Question: How can the method be adapted to other languages and what challenges are anticipated?

- Question: Request to provide raw TP, FP, TN, and FN values in the appendix for deeper comparison.
