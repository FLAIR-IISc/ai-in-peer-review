- Novel evaluation approach for GEC that measures precision and recall by error type without requiring manual error annotation.  
- Can assess multi-token errors and provides advantages over prior work.  
- Evaluations include multiple systems and automatically selected error tags are mostly approved by human experts.  
- Key rules for deriving/assigning error types are not described, reducing transparency.  
- Basis and derivation of error categories are unclear, making adaptation to other languages hard to estimate.  
- Classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and concrete directions for improvement.  
- Classifier may struggle with discontinuous error spans (e.g., separable-prefix verbs in German).  
- Human rater evaluation may be biased (raters saw system output) and lacked a neutral/middle rating option.  
- Authors did not evaluate classifier performance on manually created boundaries to isolate boundary-noise effects.  
- The paper only evaluates English; portability to other languages is not assessed.  
- Comparison between gold and auto references is unclear and seems limited to boundary alignment, not classification.  
- Significance testing and use of F0.5 scores are unclear or potentially inappropriate due to weak discriminative power.  
- Error-type results are not analyzed with respect to whether systems were designed to handle those error types.  
- Authors should report raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts (e.g., for Table 6) and address the above issues before publication.
