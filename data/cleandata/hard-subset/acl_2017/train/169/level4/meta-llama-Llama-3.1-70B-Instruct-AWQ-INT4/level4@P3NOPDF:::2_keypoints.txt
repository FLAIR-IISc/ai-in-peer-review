1. Proposes a novel GEC evaluation that measures precision and recall by error type, is independent of manual annotation, and handles multi-token errors.  
2. Evaluated on multiple systems; automatically selected error tags are largely approved by human experts.  
3. Rules for deriving error types are not described in sufficient detail, harming reproducibility and assessment.  
4. Classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and guidance for how to improve the classifier.  
5. Evaluation is limited to English; adaptability to other languages and needed taxonomy changes are unclear.  
6. Method does not address discontinuous error spans (e.g., in German), which may reduce accuracy for some languages.  
7. Human ratings of auto tags may be overly optimistic due to coarse rating options and tolerance for noisy boundaries.  
8. Authors should also evaluate classifier output when boundaries are manually created to validate tagging robustness.  
9. Comparison between gold and automatic references is unclear and currently focuses on boundaries rather than classification.  
10. Error-type results (e.g., Table 6) lack discussion of whether systems were designed to handle those error types.  
11. Raw TP/FP/TN/FN values should be provided (e.g., in the appendix) to enable alternative comparisons and metrics.  
12. The finding that different GEC approaches complement different error types aligns with prior work and should be acknowledged.  
13. Multi-token error analysis is promising but needs deeper interpretation; some systems may be inherently unable to correct certain errors and training data differences matter.  
14. Minor: some earlier GEC approaches could produce error annotations (e.g., modular systems) and this should be noted.  
15. Minor: references contain formatting errors (capitalization, missing page info) that must be fixed.  
16. Overall: approach is valuable but the paper needs more methodological detail, clearer analyses, and language-adaptability discussion before publication.
