- Proposes automatic annotation of GEC system outputs with error types via linguistically-enhanced alignment and a rule-based classifier, applied to CoNLL-2014 outputs.

- Contribution: novel method enabling fine-grained comparison of GEC systems.

- Contribution: dataset-independent rule-based classifier using only automatically obtained info (POS, lemmas).

- Contribution: detailed error-type evaluation of CoNLL-2014 system outputs.

- Strength: useful for teachers and learners by providing explicit error-type information.

- Strength: enables finer-grained comparisons to identify system strengths and weaknesses.

- Weakness: system description is superficial—missing details on manually created rules, their complexity, and ordering.

- Weakness: evaluation lacking a gold standard—annotators did not independently annotate test sentences.

- Weakness: annotator ratings were not correctly combined.

- Weakness: insufficient information about test data, including number of error categories and coverage.

- Weakness: claim of reduced domain dependence is unconvincing given manual rules and Hunspell vocabulary.

- Weakness: unclear motivation for a new set of error categories and insufficient comparison with existing tagsets.

- Question: provide details, examples, complexity, and ordering of the manual rules.

- Question: how will you create a proper gold standard and correctly aggregate annotator ratings?

- Question: provide full details on test data, number of categories, and which categories are covered.

- Question: respond to the domain-dependence criticism despite not requiring training data.

- Question: justify the new error-category design and compare it to existing tagsets.
