- Presents a novel evaluation for GEC that measures performance by error type and computes precision as well as recall.  
- Important step forward for analyzing GEC system behavior.  
- Evaluation includes a variety of systems, showing practical applicability.  
- Advantages: computes precision by error type, is independent of manual annotation, and handles multi-token errors.  
- Automatically assigned error tags were largely approved by human experts.  

- Core rules for deriving error types are not described in sufficient detail.  
- Classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and actionable suggestions for improvement.  
- Evaluation is English-only; adaptation effort and language-dependence are not discussed.  
- Precomputed error spans are continuous, which may fail to capture discontinuous edits (e.g., separable-prefix verbs).  
- Human judgment of tag appropriateness may be biased by noisy boundaries and lack of finer rating options.  
- No evaluation of classifier tags with manually created boundaries to isolate boundary noise effects.  
- Unclear how error categories were devised and whether they depend on alignment/preprocessing choices.  
- Significance testing details are unclear; using F0.5 for significance is questionable due to aggregation of precision/recall.  
- Comparison between gold and auto references appears limited to boundaries, not classification.  
- No analysis of whether systems were designed to handle specific error types when interpreting failures.  
- Table 6 lacks raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts and may be based on very small sample sizes for some cells.  
- Multi-token error results need more interpretation, given some systems may be inherently unable to correct them.  
- Some “failures” (e.g., unnecessary token errors) have known system-specific explanations that are not discussed.  
- Suggest noting that some prior systems could output error annotations but the proposed method unifies comparison across systems.  
- References and citation formatting contain issues (capitalization, URLs, missing page info).  
- Recommendations: provide detailed rule descriptions with examples, discuss cross-lingual generalizability, and perform an in-depth classifier error analysis to guide future work.
