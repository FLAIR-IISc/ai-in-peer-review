- Presents a dataset-independent, rule-based framework to annotate GEC system outputs with error-type information, useful for pedagogical use and fine-grained GEC evaluation.
- Primary contribution: a rule-based, training-data-free edit classification framework that avoids ML training bias.
- Secondary contribution: application of the framework to analyze CoNLL-2014 systems with error-type-specific evaluation.
- Strength: Novelty — rule-based classification using automatic linguistic markup (POS, lemmas) increases portability and avoids labeled data requirements.
- Strength: High reported rater agreement (>95% “Good” or “Acceptable”) suggests the approach is reasonably effective.
- Strength: Detailed CoNLL-2014 analysis provides more granular insight into system strengths and weaknesses than overall scores.
- Strength: Practical potential — automatic error-type annotation can provide targeted feedback for learners and educators.
- Weakness: System description is superficial — lacks examples, rule count, complexity, ordering, and interactions, limiting reproducibility and assessment.
- Weakness: Evaluation methodology is questionable — relies on expert ratings of system annotations rather than a gold-standard comparison, and averaging masks distribution of poor ratings.
- Weakness: Analysis scope is limited and descriptive — results (e.g., Table 6) and discussion of multi-token edits lack deeper synthesis and insight.
- Question: Provide representative rule examples to show complexity and interaction.
- Question: Report the distribution of “bad” ratings across the 200 test sentences.
- Question: How representative are FCE-test and CoNLL-2014 of other learner domains and what ensures generalizability?
- Question: Quantify the impact of potentially incorrect automatically extracted edit boundaries on evaluation.
