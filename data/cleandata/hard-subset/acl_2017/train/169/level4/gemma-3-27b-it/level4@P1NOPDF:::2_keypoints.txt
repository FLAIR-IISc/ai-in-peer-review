- Proposes a novel evaluation for GEC that measures performance by error type, capturing both precision and recall.  
- Important step forward for analyzing GEC system behavior across error types.  
- Evaluated across a variety of systems, demonstrating broad applicability.  
- Advantages claimed: computes precision by error type, is independent of manual annotation, and handles multi-token errors.  
- Automatic error-tag assignments were largely confirmed by human experts.  

- The rules used to derive error types are not described, undermining reproducibility.  
- The basis and grounding of error categories in prior work are unclear.  
- Rules likely depend on alignment/alignment algorithm, but the paper lacks details or examples of such rules.  
- No information on the number or complexity of rules, making cross-language adaptation effort unknown.  
- Evaluation conducted only on English; applicability to other languages is not demonstrated or discussed.  
- Continuous edit-span assumption is limiting; discontinuous edits (e.g., German separable verbs) may not be handled.  
- Human rater validation may be biased by noisy automatic boundaries and limited rating options; needs validation with manual boundaries.  
- Classifier evaluation lacks thorough error analysis and actionable directions for improvement; “Bad” ratings are not analyzed in depth.  
- Unclear on what data/statistic the significance test was performed; using F0.5 may be inappropriate for some comparisons.  
- Comparison between gold and auto references appears limited to boundaries and not classification.  
- No analysis of whether systems were designed to handle the evaluated error types, confounding interpretation of zero scores (e.g., CUUI).  
- Some apparent failures may be classification errors rather than true system failures, but this is not investigated.  
- Request for raw TP/FP/TN/FN counts (e.g., for Table 6) to enable other metrics and clarify small-sample issues.  
- Multi-token error results need further interpretation since some systems cannot correct such errors or lacked appropriate training data.  
- Paper should acknowledge that some GEC systems can produce error annotations via submodules, while noting the proposed method offers a unified comparison.  
- References and formatting issues: missing capitalization, malformed URL, and missing page info in citations.  

- Author response concedes the approach is sound but reiterates that missing rule descriptions and language-generalization issues make the paper not ready for publication.
