- Paper proposes an automatic method to annotate GEC system outputs with error types using linguistically-enhanced alignment plus a rule-based classifier.  
- Core contributions: dataset-independent rule-based error typing framework, tool release, and detailed CoNLL-2014 system analysis.  
- Strength: Practical utility—automated error-type annotation is useful for educators, learners, and system developers.  
- Strength: Dataset-independence goal—rule-based approach aims to avoid classifier bias from corpus-specific training.  
- Strength: Detailed analysis of CoNLL-2014 systems provides insights into system strengths/weaknesses by error type.  
- Weakness: Superficial system description—missing example rules, rule count, complexity, and rule application order.  
- Weakness: Flawed evaluation—relies on ratings of system outputs instead of a gold-standard annotation, inflating agreement and misrepresenting reliability.  
- Weakness: Weak motivation for new error tagset and puzzling decision not to use the CoNLL-2014 tagset, hindering direct comparison to gold standard.  
- Weakness: Domain-dependence concerns—use of Hunspell vocabulary and manual rules and limited test data (student essays) may limit generalizability.  
- Question: Provide representative sample rules to illustrate rule complexity and interactions.  
- Question: Why was a gold-standard annotation not created for a subset of test data instead of rating outputs?  
- Question: Why not use the CoNLL-2014 tagset, and what clear advantage does the proposed scheme offer?  
- Question: How does the system handle inaccurate or “unusual” automatic edit boundaries and their impact on classification?  
- Question: Please elaborate on domain dependence of the Hunspell dictionary and manually created rules.  
- Additional: Correct typographical issues (e.g., "c.f."→"cf.", "M2"→"M^2", clarify "50-70 F1", reference casing).  
- Additional: Table 6 is overly verbose—summarize more concisely.  
- Additional: Strengthen the connection between the method and broader goals for fluency correction.
