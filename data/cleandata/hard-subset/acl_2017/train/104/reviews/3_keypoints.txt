- Strength: Proposes a good idea integrating entity, mention and sense modeling within the same neural language modeling technique.  
- Strength: Uses a simple neural learning and training procedure that supports a large set of applications.  
- Strength: Formal presentation is clear in places and empirical evaluation is solid, even if improvements are not large.  
- Weakness: Amount of genuinely novel content is limited; improvements are not striking.  
- Weakness: Prose and exposition are inconsistent; Section 3 and parts of Figure 2 lack clarity and detail.  
- Weakness: Terminology is redundant/confusing (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” vs text anchors).  
- Weakness: Paper is quite close to Yamada et al. (2016) and does not sufficiently outline differences.  
- Suggestion: Evaluate the learned word embeddings on other NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they are not weaker than simpler wordspaces.
