This paper proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model to address ambiguity in entity linking by learning multiple sense embeddings for each mention, leveraging both textual contexts and knowledge base entities. A language model is then used for sense disambiguation. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on an entity linking benchmark, and provide analysis of the smoothing parameter used in their approach.

The primary contribution of this work, in my view, is the exploration of multi-prototype mention embeddings within the context of entity linking. While the idea of multiple embeddings per mention isn’t entirely novel (as evidenced by citations like Huang et al., 2012, and Neelakantan et al., 2014), the specific joint modeling approach and its application to entity linking, coupled with the smoothing parameter analysis, offer a valuable contribution. A secondary contribution lies in the direct comparison to a related approach, providing a useful benchmark within a similar experimental setup.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the entity linking task, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed model.
2. **Insightful Parameter Analysis:** The analysis of the smoothing parameter provides a clear understanding of the trade-off between popularity bias and contextual relevance in entity linking. This is a practical and valuable insight for researchers in the field.
3. **Direct Comparison:** The inclusion of a direct comparison with a closely related method strengthens the validity of the results and allows for a more nuanced evaluation of the proposed approach.
4. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed model and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incomplete Comparison to ALIGN:** The comparison to ALIGN is confounded by differing hyperparameters (context window size and vector dimensions). This makes it difficult to definitively conclude that the proposed method *outperforms* ALIGN, as the performance difference could be attributable to these factors.
2. **Ambiguous Graph Definition:** The definition of N(e_j) is unclear, and it’s crucial to know whether the Wikipedia graph is treated as directed or undirected. This significantly impacts the construction of entity contexts and, consequently, the learned embeddings.
3. **Mention Embedding Clarity:** The terminology surrounding “mention” is ambiguous. It’s unclear if embeddings are generated for string types or specific document mentions. The statement that "mention embedding" refers to mention *senses* needs further clarification.
4. **Limited Comparison to Unsupervised Methods:** The lack of comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods makes it difficult to assess the true impact of the sense disambiguation component. Is the performance gain primarily due to the multi-prototype embeddings or the disambiguation process?



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you please clarify the definition of N(e_j) and explicitly state whether the Wikipedia graph is treated as directed or undirected?
2.  Please elaborate on the distinction between string-type mention embeddings and document-specific mention embeddings.
3.  Would you consider adding a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods to better isolate the contribution of your sense disambiguation component?
