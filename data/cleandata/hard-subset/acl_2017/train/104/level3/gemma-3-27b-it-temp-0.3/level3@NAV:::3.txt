This paper proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model to address ambiguity in entity mentions within a unified semantic space. The core idea is to learn multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and entities from a knowledge base, coupled with a language model for disambiguation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through entity linking experiments, achieving state-of-the-art results.

The primary contribution lies in the integrated approach to entity, mention, and sense modeling within a neural language modeling framework. This allows for a more nuanced representation of mentions, acknowledging their inherent ambiguity. A second contribution is the simplicity of the learning and training procedure, which makes the model potentially applicable to a wide range of downstream tasks. Finally, the empirical evaluation, while not groundbreaking, is solid and provides evidence of the model’s effectiveness in the entity linking task.

However, the novelty of this work is limited. The proposed method builds heavily on existing techniques, and the improvements over existing state-of-the-art are not substantial. The paper lacks a clear differentiation from Yamada et al. (2016), which also explores multi-prototype embeddings, and this connection isn’t adequately addressed. Furthermore, the presentation suffers from inconsistencies. Section 3 and parts of Figure 2 are unclear and lack sufficient detail to fully understand the model’s architecture and training process. The terminology is also problematic, with redundant and confusing terms like “dictionary of mentions” and “text anchors” hindering clarity. 

The authors’ response clarifies some points, but doesn’t fully address the concerns regarding novelty and clarity. To further demonstrate the value of the learned embeddings, I recommend evaluating them on other NLP tasks such as parsing, coreference resolution, and semantic role labeling. This would demonstrate whether the embeddings offer benefits beyond entity linking and are not simply optimized for that specific task.
