- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings by jointly modeling textual contexts and KB entities, applied to entity linking with state-of-the-art results.
- Main contribution: showing multi-prototype mention embeddings informed by both text and a knowledge base improve entity linking.
- Secondary contribution: analysis of the smoothing parameter revealing trade-offs between context and entity popularity.
- Provides a direct (but partially confounded) comparison to ALIGN as a benchmark.
- Strength: Strong empirical results achieving significant improvements on entity linking benchmarks.
- Strength: Addresses a relevant, well-motivated problem of mention/entity ambiguity.
- Strength: Clear, readable presentation and mathematical formulation of the model and disambiguation process.
- Strength: Insightful analysis of the smoothing parameter and its impact on model behavior.
- Strength: Explicit comparison to related work (ALIGN) and acknowledgment of comparison limitations.
- Weakness: Comparison to ALIGN is confounded by differing hyperparameters (context window and vector dimensionality).
- Weakness: Ambiguity in terminology—unclear whether embeddings are for string types, document mentions, or mention senses.
- Weakness: Unclear definition of N(e_j) and whether knowledge graph adjacency is treated as directed or undirected.
- Weakness: Limited comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods, leaving unclear the independent effect of multi-prototype embeddings versus disambiguation.
- Weakness: Reference list contains many duplicated entries.
- Question: Clarify whether learned mention embeddings target string types, specific document mentions, or mention senses.
- Question: Specify if knowledge graph adjacency relationships are treated as directed or undirected when defining entity context.
- Question: Explain the rationale for ALIGN hyperparameter choices and whether matching hyperparameters would change results.
- Question: Provide comparisons to unsupervised entity linking methods (e.g., Pershina et al. 2015; Cucerzan 2007).
- Additional comment: Clean up repeated references in the final version.
- Additional comment: Include ablation studies to isolate contributions of multi-prototype embeddings versus disambiguation.
