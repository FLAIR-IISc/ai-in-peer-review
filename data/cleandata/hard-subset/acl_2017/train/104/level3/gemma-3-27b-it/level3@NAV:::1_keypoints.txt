- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by integrating textual context and KB entities, claiming state-of-the-art entity linking performance and analyzing a smoothing parameter.  
- Main contribution: explicit modeling of multiple mention senses in a joint text–knowledge embedding space.  
- Secondary contributions: analysis of the smoothing parameter (trade-off with popularity bias) and direct comparison to a related method.  
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on an entity linking benchmark.  
- Strength: clear motivation addressing mention ambiguity with a directly relevant solution.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of the smoothing hyperparameter and its impact.  
- Strength: direct comparison against a closely related approach for focused evaluation.  
- Weakness: comparison to ALIGN is confounded by different hyperparameters (context window and embedding size), preventing a fair conclusion of superiority.  
- Weakness: unclear definition of N(e_j) and whether the Wikipedia adjacency graph is treated as directed or undirected.  
- Weakness: ambiguous use of the term “mention embedding” (string-level, document-specific, or sense embeddings).  
- Weakness: limited baselines—missing comparisons to a broader set of unsupervised/entity-linking methods (e.g., graph-based approaches).  
- Question: please clarify the definition of N(e_j) and graph directionality for the Wikipedia adjacency graph.  
- Question: clarify the scope of “mention embedding” (string type, document mention, or sense embedding).  
- Question: consider adding comparisons against a broader set of unsupervised entity linking methods to contextualize the sense disambiguation impact.  
- Question: can you provide ALIGN results using the same hyperparameters as your model (window=10, dims=500) for a direct comparison?
