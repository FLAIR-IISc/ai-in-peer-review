- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and KB entities.  
- Integrates a language model for disambiguation and is evaluated on entity linking, achieving state-of-the-art results (modest but statistically significant improvements).  
- Contributions: practical multi-prototype embeddings leveraging text and KB, a straightforward neural training procedure, and strong empirical entity linking evaluation.  
- Strength: well-motivated unified framework for modeling words, entities, and mentions addressing mention ambiguity.  
- Strength: simple, standard neural objective and training procedure that’s easy to adapt to other tasks.  
- Strength: solid empirical results with qualitative analysis supporting effectiveness.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—core ideas overlap with prior work (e.g., Huang et al., Chen et al., Mancini et al.).  
- Weakness: improvements are not large, raising questions about overall impact.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—Section 3 lacks detail, Figure 2 is hard to interpret, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: confusing and inconsistent terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs. “text anchors”).  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation and comparison to closely related work, especially Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Question: please clarify the model architecture and interactions between embedding layers and disambiguation, ideally with a clearer diagram.  
- Question: provide a point-by-point comparison to Yamada et al. (2016) highlighting novel aspects.  
- Question: have the learned embeddings been evaluated on other NLP tasks (parsing, coref, SRL); if not, why?  
- Additional comment: reference list contains duplicates and needs cleaning.
