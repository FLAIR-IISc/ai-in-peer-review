- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model to handle mention ambiguity by learning multiple sense embeddings per mention.  
- Jointly models textual contexts and knowledge-base entities within a unified semantic space, using a language model for disambiguation.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via entity linking, reporting state-of-the-art results on a benchmark dataset.  
- Primary contribution: straightforward integration of entity, mention, and sense modeling in a neural language modeling framework enabling joint learning and disambiguation.  
- Secondary contribution: practical application to entity linking with solid empirical performance.  
- Simplicity of the method is a practical advantage for broader NLP applications.  
- Novelty is limited: approach builds heavily on existing embedding and knowledge-integration techniques and gains are not substantial.  
- Presentation issues: prose is inconsistent and parts of Section 3 and Figure 2 are unclear and under-detailed.  
- Terminology is confusing and redundant—“dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity-mention pairs,” and “text anchors” are not well-defined.  
- Fails to adequately differentiate itself from Yamada et al. (2016); authors’ response does not sufficiently address this comparison.  
- Suggests further validation by evaluating embeddings on downstream tasks (parsing, coreference resolution, semantic role labeling) to show benefits beyond entity linking.  
- Question 1: Please detail differences from Yamada et al. (2016), especially in learning objective and disambiguation.  
- Question 2: Please clarify distinctions among “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity-mention pairs,” and “text anchors.”  
- Question 3: Which architectural choices and hyperparameters were most critical for the reported performance, and why?
