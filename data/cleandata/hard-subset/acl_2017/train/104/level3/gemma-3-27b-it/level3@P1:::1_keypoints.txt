- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and knowledge-base entities, with a language-model disambiguation step.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art entity linking performance, outperforming ALIGN.

- Primary contribution: improved entity linking via multi-prototype embeddings combined with LM-based disambiguation.  
- Secondary contribution: analysis of smoothing parameter showing entity popularity effects.  
- Provides a direct benchmarked comparison to ALIGN to demonstrate effectiveness.

- Strong empirical results with clear quantitative improvements over ALIGN on entity linking benchmarks.  
- Insightful analysis of the smoothing parameter and its impact on disambiguation.  
- Valuable direct comparison to a closely related text-knowledge integration method (ALIGN).  
- Clear problem formulation and well-motivated approach addressing mention ambiguity.

- Comparison to ALIGN is potentially confounded by differing hyperparameters (context window size, vector dimension), making fairness unclear.  
- Unclear description of the Wikipedia graph (directed vs. undirected) and adjacency treatment for entity contexts.  
- Ambiguous definition of “mention”: unclear if embeddings are for string types, document-specific mentions, or mention senses.  
- Limited comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking baselines, making it hard to isolate the contribution of the disambiguation step versus multi-prototype embeddings.
