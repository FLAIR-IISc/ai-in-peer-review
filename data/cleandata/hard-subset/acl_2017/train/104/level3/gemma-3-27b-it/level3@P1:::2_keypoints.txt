- Proposes MPME: multi-prototype mention embeddings that learn multiple sense embeddings per mention and jointly model text and KB entities for entity disambiguation.  
- Introduces a language model–based component to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art empirical performance on entity linking benchmarks, outperforming recent baselines.  
- Method is clear, well-defined, and grounded in established embedding techniques with sensible LM integration.  
- Provides practical implementation details (e.g., smoothing parameter) that aid reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written and experimentally transparent.  
- Lacks theoretical insight into why mention-sense embeddings improve over direct mention→entity mappings.  
- Omits relevant baselines/datasets, notably TAC KBP EDL evaluations, weakening comparative claims.  
- Unclear how mention senses are created and differentiated (number per mention, mapping to KB entities, seeding process).  
- Framing overclaims by presenting the work as broadly "bridging text and knowledge" despite a narrower EDL focus.  
- Requests clarification on motivation for mention-sense embeddings and what unique signal they capture.  
- Asks for explicit description of sense-creation process, sense count per mention, and correspondence to KB entities.  
- Questions why TAC KBP baselines were excluded from experiments.  
- Seeks confirmation of assumed configurations (entity profiles, word distributions, mention sense representations).  
- Notes that the smoothing parameter detail is useful and that stronger justification and KBP comparisons would strengthen the paper.
