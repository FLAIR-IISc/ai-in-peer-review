- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention using textual context and a KB, disambiguated via a language model, achieving SOTA on entity linking.

- Contribution: unified framework jointly learning word, entity, and mention embeddings to explicitly address mention ambiguity.

- Contribution: practical, simple neural learning procedure applicable to multiple NLP tasks.

- Strength: integrated modeling of entity, mention, and sense within a single neural language model captures mention ambiguity.

- Strength: simplicity and straightforward training improve practicality and adaptability to downstream applications.

- Strength: solid empirical evaluation with competitive SOTA results on entity linking and supportive qualitative analysis.

- Weakness: limited novelty—multi-prototype and joint text+KB learning are incremental beyond prior work, with modest gains.

- Weakness: clarity and exposition problems, especially in Section 3 and parts of Figure 2, make the model hard to follow.

- Weakness: confusing and redundant terminology (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" vs "text anchors").

- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from Yamada et al. (2016); lacks detailed comparison of methods and advantages.

- Question: please detail differences from Yamada et al. (2016), particularly in the language modeling component and entity handling.

- Question: please provide a precise mathematical formulation of the training objective used in Section 3.

- Question: please define "text anchor" clearly and explain its relation to other terminology.

- Additional: area chairs should review the authors’ response to verify addressed concerns.

- Additional: recommend evaluating learned embeddings on other downstream NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to demonstrate generalizability.
