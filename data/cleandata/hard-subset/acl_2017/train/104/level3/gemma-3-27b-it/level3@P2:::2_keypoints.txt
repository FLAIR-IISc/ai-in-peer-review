- Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context and knowledge-base entities.
- Introduces an intermediate "mention sense" layer to capture ambiguity and improve entity linking.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art entity linking performance and introduces a smoothing technique for language model training.

- Reports strong empirical results showing improvement over strong baselines.
- Methodologically sound: joint text–knowledge learning, clear architecture, and sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and experiments are clearly described.
- Smoothing parameter in LM training is a useful idea to address data sparsity and improve generalization.

- The paper does not convincingly explain why the intermediate mention-sense layer provides information beyond entity or contextual embeddings.
- Lacks crucial details on mention-sense creation: how many senses per mention, whether senses are predefined or learned, and mapping to KB entities.
- Incomplete baseline comparison—fails to acknowledge or compare with TAC KBP EDL evaluations and related prior work.
- Claims to “bridge text and knowledge” but practically focuses narrowly on entity disambiguation, overstating scope.

- What information does the mention-sense layer capture that entity or contextual embeddings do not?
- How are mention senses created and represented; how many per mention and are they learned or predefined?
- How does the number of senses affect performance; were different counts experimented with?
- What signal exists at the mention-sense level not present in corresponding entity pages, and can it be merged into entity representations?
- Please provide comparative analysis against EDL systems evaluated on TAC KBP datasets.

- Update Section 5 to explicitly state the role and novelty of mention senses and consider visualizing the embedding space.
- Clean and deduplicate the references for clarity.
