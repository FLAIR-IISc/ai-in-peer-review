- Empirical performance: Reports improved EDL results and claims state-of-the-art vs direct mention→entity approaches (e.g., ALIGN/Yamada et al.).
- Multi-prototype mention embeddings: Introduces an intermediate “mention sense” layer learned jointly with words and entities; smoothing helps rare senses.
- Systematic evaluation: Provides qualitative analysis and ablation studies to probe model behavior.
- Methodological soundness: Builds on established embedding techniques and follows accepted practices.
- Clear presentation: Paper is well-written with a clear, understandable pipeline.
- Effective smoothing: The smoothing parameter (Sec 6.4.2) practically improves performance for infrequent senses.
- Comprehensive experimentation: Multiple experiments and ablations offer insight into component contributions.
- Overstated claims: Framing as “bridging text and knowledge” is misleading — the work is narrowly focused on EDL.
- Lack of theoretical justification: No clear explanation why an intermediate mention-sense layer adds signal beyond entity embeddings.
- Missing implementation details: Insufficient information on how mention senses are generated, how many per mention, and their mapping to entities.
- Incomplete baselines: Fails to compare against key prior work such as TAC KBP EDL tasks.
- Limited validation: Evaluation is largely on embedding quality and EDL; no downstream application tests.
- Question — mention sense creation: Request details on generation and criteria for number of senses per mention.
- Question — mapping to entities: Ask whether mention senses map bijectively to KB entities and how ambiguity is resolved.
- Question — novelty of senses: Ask what unique signal mention senses provide beyond entity pages and whether it could be absorbed into entity representations.
- Question — justification for two-step approach: Request deeper explanation of why mention→sense→entity outperforms direct mapping and what direct mapping fails to capture.
- Question — KBP evaluation: Suggest evaluating on TAC KBP to compare with established systems.
- Additional comments: References are poorly formatted and repeated.
- Additional comments: Recommend a more detailed error analysis to identify error types and improvement areas.
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and well-executed but contributions are overstated, lack theoretical grounding, and missing details and baselines limit impact.
