- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly learns multiple sense embeddings by integrating textual contexts and knowledge-base entities.  
- Introduces a language model–based sense disambiguation method to map mentions to specific senses and reports state-of-the-art results on a benchmark entity linking task.  
- Claims improved entity linking performance over ALIGN in supervised settings, demonstrating better joint text–knowledge representations.  
- Provides analysis of a smoothing parameter to address entity popularity bias in entity linking.  
- Offers a direct comparative evaluation with ALIGN using similar input data to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Demonstrates superior quantitative and qualitative evaluation results, supporting the effectiveness of multi-prototype mention embeddings.  
- Novel handling of popularity bias via smoothing analysis is a standout contribution.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the evaluated benchmark, indicating practical effectiveness.  
- Comparison with ALIGN is unclear due to differing experimental parameters (content window size, vector dimensions, graph adjacency), making attribution of gains uncertain.  
- The term “mention” is used ambiguously in parts of the paper, reducing clarity and readability.  
- The impact of sense disambiguation order is under-explored, and the work lacks comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.  
- Requests clarification on how parameter differences versus ALIGN may have influenced results.  
- Asks for comparisons of the sense disambiguation method to other unsupervised approaches in accuracy and efficiency.  
- Requests a precise definition of “mention” and consistent usage throughout the paper.  
- Concludes that clearer comparisons and more precise terminology would strengthen the paper’s overall impact.
