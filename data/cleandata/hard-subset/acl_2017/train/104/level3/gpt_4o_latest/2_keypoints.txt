- Proposes "mention senses" as an intermediate layer between textual mentions and KB entities, with a two-step mapping and jointly trained embeddings; claims state-of-the-art EDL results.  
- Contribution: introduces mention senses (novel idea) but their added value over direct mention-to-entity mapping is unclear.  
- Contribution: joint training of word, mention-sense, and entity embeddings is technically sound and consistent with deep learning practices.  
- Contribution: reports improved empirical performance on a benchmark entity linking dataset.  
- Strength: model design is technically sound and includes a useful smoothing parameter to improve robustness.  
- Strength: empirical validation shows performance gains on a benchmark, indicating practical utility.  
- Strength: explicitly tackles mention ambiguity, an important problem for text-to-knowledge integration.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for why mention senses are needed or what unique role they serve compared to entities.  
- Weakness: inadequate description of how mention senses are created, differentiated, and how many unique words they add beyond entity profiles.  
- Weakness: incorrect novelty claim—prior work (e.g., TAC KBP tasks) has addressed related mention-ambiguity problems; dismissal of this prior work is unsatisfactory.  
- Weakness: evaluation emphasizes embedding quality rather than demonstrating clear downstream task benefits.  
- Question: clarify the specific added value and representational/functional differences of mention senses versus entities.  
- Question: explain the process, criteria, and counts for creating and differentiating mention senses.  
- Question: justify the two-step mapping design and specify what challenges the intermediate layer solves versus direct mention-to-entity mapping.  
- Additional: paper has potential but needs stronger justification of core contributions and proper positioning with respect to prior TAC KBP and related work.
