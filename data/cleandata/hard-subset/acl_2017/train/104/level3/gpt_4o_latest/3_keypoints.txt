- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention.  
- Integrates textual context and knowledge base into a unified semantic space with a language-model–based disambiguation mechanism.  
- Simple, broadly applicable training procedure and reported state-of-the-art performance on an entity linking benchmark.

- Innovative integration of multi-sense mention embeddings within a unified entity/mention/sense framework.  
- Training is simple, efficient, and scalable across tasks and datasets.  
- Strong empirical validation: competitive/SOTA entity linking results and supportive qualitative analysis.  
- Approach has potential applicability beyond entity-focused tasks.

- Novelty concerns: method appears similar to Yamada et al. (2016) without a clear, detailed distinction.  
- Clarity problems: Section 3 and Figure 2 are underexplained and some terminology is redundant.  
- Limited evaluation: experiments focus mainly on entity tasks, lacking tests on broader semantic tasks.  
- Incremental gains: performance improvements, while positive, are not substantially large.

- How does the method fundamentally differ from Yamada et al. (2016), conceptually and empirically?  
- Can the embeddings be effectively applied to other tasks (e.g., semantic similarity, relation extraction), and if so why were they not evaluated?  
- Please clarify Figure 2 and provide more detailed explanation for Section 3.

- Overall promising approach but needs clearer presentation, stronger novelty justification, and broader evaluation to demonstrate wider impact.
