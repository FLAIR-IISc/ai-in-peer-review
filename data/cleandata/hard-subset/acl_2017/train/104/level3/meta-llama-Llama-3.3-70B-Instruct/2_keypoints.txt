- Proposes Neural Symbolic Machine (NSM): a seq2seq neural "programmer" integrated with a symbolic non-differentiable Lisp interpreter using key-variable memory.  
- Uses interpreter code assistance to provide a friendly neural computer interface supporting compositionality.  
- Trains NSM with iterative maximum likelihood combined with reinforcement learning to directly optimize task reward.  
- Introduces the Manager-Programmer-Computer framework for neural program induction combining neural networks with symbolic non-differentiable computers.  
- Develops NSM that integrates a seq2seq programmer, Lisp interpreter, and key-variable memory.  
- Applies iterative maximum likelihood plus reinforcement learning for end-to-end reward optimization under weak supervision.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on WEBQUESTIONSSP with weak supervision, significantly closing the gap to full supervision.  
- Trained end-to-end without feature engineering or domain-specific knowledge.  
- Symbolic non-differentiable computer enables abstract, scalable, and precise operations and a friendly interface.  
- Relies on a large knowledge base and may not generalize to smaller or different KBs.  
- Training process is complex and requires careful hyperparameter tuning.  
- May struggle with complex queries that require multiple reasoning steps.
