- Proposes MPME: learn multiple "sense" embeddings per mention to handle ambiguity.  
- Uses a two-step pipeline: disambiguate mention→sense, then link sense→KB entity; jointly learns words, entities, and senses from text and KG.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on a standard entity linking benchmark.  
- Novel, intuitive intermediate mention-sense layer modeling one-to-many mention→entity relations.  
- Empirically strong: outperforms direct mention→entity baselines including Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Simple, effective context-aware disambiguation using a language-model-like scorer with smoothing.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for why an intermediate mention-sense layer is necessary versus richer entity/context embeddings.  
- Omits critical methodological details (number of senses, initialization, training incentives for divergence, mapping to entities), hurting reproducibility.  
- Incomplete comparison to prior work and benchmarks, notably missing TAC KBP EDL / related literature.  
- Overstates scope as general "text and knowledge" integration while evaluation is narrowly on Wikipedia entity linking.  
- Asks authors to clarify the unique information captured by mention-sense embeddings beyond entity embeddings.  
- Requests details on how the number of senses is determined and how senses are initialized and differentiated.  
- Queries whether learned senses map one-to-one to candidate entities and how this differs from context-sensitive entity embeddings.  
- Asks why TAC KBP EDL was not included and urges discussion/comparison to that benchmark.
