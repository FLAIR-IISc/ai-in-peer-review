- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding: learn multiple sense-specific vectors per ambiguous mention within a joint text–KB embedding framework.
- Jointly trains word, entity, and mention-prototype embeddings using a skip-gram–style objective that leverages corpus context and KB structure.
- Uses a language-model–based disambiguation method to map mentions in context to the most likely prototype/entity and reports SOTA on an entity linking benchmark.

- Elegantly combines multi-prototype word embedding ideas with joint text–KB representation learning to directly address mention ambiguity.
- Keeps the method simple and scalable by building on a modified skip-gram objective, enabling efficient end-to-end training on large data.
- Provides solid empirical evidence (quantitative and qualitative) showing consistent improvements over strong baselines.

- Novelty is limited: mainly a combination of existing techniques (multi-prototype embeddings + joint text–KB models) with incremental performance gains.
- Exposition is unclear: key model details, objective formulation, and component relationships (e.g., Figure 2) are insufficiently explained.
- Terminology is inconsistent/confusing (e.g., “dictionary of mentions,” “entity-mention pairs,” “text anchors” used without clear distinctions).
- Insufficient comparison to closely related work (notably Yamada et al., 2016); lacks a clear discussion of differences and contributions versus that prior work.

- Question: Have you evaluated the learned word embeddings on standard intrinsic/extrinsic tasks to ensure joint training does not degrade word vector quality?
