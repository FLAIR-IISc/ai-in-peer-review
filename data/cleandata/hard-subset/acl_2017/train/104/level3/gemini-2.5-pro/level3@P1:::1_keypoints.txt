- Contribution: Proposes a multi-prototype embedding model that jointly learns words, entities, and mention-sense embeddings from text and a KB to handle ambiguous mentions.
- Contribution: Empirically shows state-of-the-art supervised entity linking performance, outperforming the ALIGN baseline.
- Contribution: Analyzes a smoothing parameter that trades off contextual evidence and entity popularity, providing insight into popularity bias.

- Strength: Strong empirical results on supervised entity linking, convincingly outperforming a relevant recent baseline.
- Strength: Direct, relevant comparison to ALIGN using similar data sources, making the architectural comparison meaningful.
- Strength: Useful analysis of the smoothing parameter α that illuminates the popularity vs. context trade-off.

- Weakness: Comparison with ALIGN is confounded by different hyperparameters (window size and embedding dimension), so gains may stem from larger capacity rather than the new architecture.
- Weakness: Definition of an entity’s neighborhood N(e_j) is unclear — paper does not state whether the Wikipedia link graph is treated as directed or undirected.
- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology around “mention” — unclear if embeddings are for mention types (surface strings) or per-token mention instances; “mention embedding” vs. “mention sense embedding” used interchangeably.
- Weakness: The language-model-based disambiguation component is not evaluated in isolation and lacks comparisons to unsupervised/lightly-supervised methods; no analysis of how sequential ordering of disambiguation affects performance.

- Question: Can you re-run or provide results using ALIGN’s hyperparameters (200 dim, window 5) or run ALIGN with your hyperparameters (500 dim, window 10) to isolate architectural gains?
- Question: How exactly is N(e_j) constructed — do you use directed outlinks, inlinks, or undirected (both) edges from the Wikipedia link graph?
- Question: Precisely define “mention embedding”: are there per-surface-string prototype sets or per-occurrence representations conditioned on document context?
- Question: Have you studied the impact of mention disambiguation order within a document (e.g., left-to-right vs. ambiguity/confidence-based ordering)?
