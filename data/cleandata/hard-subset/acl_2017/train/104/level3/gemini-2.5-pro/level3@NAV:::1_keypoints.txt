- Introduces a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly learns words, entities, and multiple mention senses using text + a knowledge base, with a language-model-based disambiguation step.
- Contribution: Shows a multi-prototype mention-sense model can outperform the single-prototype ALIGN baseline on supervised entity linking, arguing for modeling ambiguity in the joint embedding space.
- Contribution: Provides analysis of a smoothing parameter that balances contextual evidence and entity popularity.
- Strength: Superior performance over ALIGN on supervised EL, supporting the benefit of jointly learned multi-prototype representations.
- Strength: Direct comparison with ALIGN using similar input data offers a focused, relevant evaluation.
- Strength: Smoothing-parameter analysis gives practical insights for tuning and understanding model behavior.
- Weakness: Comparison with ALIGN is confounded by different hyperparameters (context window and embedding dimensions), so gains may be due to settings rather than architecture.
- Weakness: Key methodological details are ambiguous (e.g., whether N(e_j) uses a directed or undirected Wikipedia link graph; unclear whether “mention” embeddings are for types or senses), harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: Disambiguation evaluation is limited—no comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods to gauge the proposed ordering strategy’s standalone effectiveness.
- Question: Why were different hyperparameters used for the proposed model and ALIGN—can you report results with matched hyperparameters (e.g., same context window and embedding size)?
- Question: Is the entity neighborhood N(e_j) derived from a directed or undirected view of the Wikipedia link graph?
