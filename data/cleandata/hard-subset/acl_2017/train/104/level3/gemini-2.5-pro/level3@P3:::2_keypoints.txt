- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking to Wikipedia via a two-step pipeline: mention → mention-sense → entity using jointly learned embeddings (MPME).  
- Core model (MPME) learns multiple sense embeddings per ambiguous mention and is trained with word- and entity-context objectives.  
- Empirical claim: MPME achieves state-of-the-art performance on a standard EDL benchmark, outperforming Yamada et al. (ALIGN).  

Contributions:
- Introduces an intermediate "mention sense" representation and a two-step linking process shown to outperform direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Proposes joint learning of words, multi-prototype mention senses, and entities in a unified vector space.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, surpassing a recent strong baseline.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical improvement over a relevant baseline (ALIGN/Yamada et al.).  
- Methodologically sound: clear model description and well-defined training objectives (skip-gram-style negative sampling).  
- Conceptually appealing: intermediate sense layer reflects real ambiguity in surface mentions.  
- Practical detail: uses a smoothing parameter λ to effectively combine mention-sense and entity scores.

Weaknesses:
- No conceptual explanation for why the intermediate sense layer outperforms richer entity representations.  
- Key details about mention senses are missing: initialization, how number of senses is chosen, and relation to KB entities.  
- Framing overclaims impact by positioning work as broadly "bridging text and knowledge" while focusing narrowly on Wikipedia EDL.  
- Incomplete related work/evaluation: omits discussion and comparison to TAC KBP EDL shared-task systems and benchmarks.

Questions for authors:
- What specific information do mention-sense embeddings capture that entity embeddings do not, and why is a two-step model better?  
- How are mention senses generated, how is their number determined, and is there a one-to-one mapping between senses and candidate entities?  
- Please confirm embedding sources and clarify what novelty the sense-level signal provides beyond entity/context training.  
- Why were TAC KBP EDL evaluations not discussed or compared against, given their relevance for end-to-end EDL performance?
