- Paper tackles mention ambiguity by jointly embedding text and KBs and proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding to learn multiple sense vectors per ambiguous mention.
- Introduces a language-model-based method to disambiguate mentions to the most likely learned sense in context.
- Evaluates on supervised entity linking and reports state-of-the-art results, outperforming ALIGN.
- Main contribution: a novel multi-sense embedding method that models mention polysemy in joint text-KB representations.
- Main contribution: strong empirical evidence that multi-prototype embeddings improve entity linking performance.
- Main contribution: analysis of a smoothing parameter that balances contextual similarity with entity popularity.
- Strength: achieves SOTA on a key downstream task, suggesting higher-quality representations for disambiguation.
- Strength: direct, appropriate comparison to ALIGN using similar Wikipedia text and link-graph data.
- Strength: thoughtful analysis of the popularity smoothing parameter α and its effect on performance.
- Weakness: comparison to ALIGN may be confounded by different hyperparameters (context window 10 vs 5, dims 500 vs 200), undermining attribution of gains to architecture.
- Weakness: key methodological details are underspecified—definition of entity neighborhood N(e_j) (in-links, out-links, or undirected) is unclear.
- Weakness: ambiguous terminology around “mention”—unclear whether a surface-form embedding is learned separately from sense/entity embeddings.
- Weakness: disambiguation component is only evaluated as part of the full system and lacks comparisons to standard unsupervised or simple baselines.
- Questions for authors: clarify hyperparameter differences and provide results with ALIGN-matching settings (e.g., window=5, dim=200).
- Question: how is N(e_j) constructed from the Wikipedia link graph (in-links, out-links, or undirected)?
- Question: does the model learn a distinct embedding for the surface string separate from its sense/entity embeddings?
- Question/suggestion: compare the proposed disambiguation method to standard strategies (cosine similarity, graph-based methods) using the pre-trained multi-prototype embeddings.
