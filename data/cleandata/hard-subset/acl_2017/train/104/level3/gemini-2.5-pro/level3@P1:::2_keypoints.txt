1. Proposes MPME: joint embeddings of words, KB entities, and multi-prototype mention senses with a two-step mention→sense→entity pipeline, trained on text and a knowledge graph and claiming SOTA results.

2. Empirically shows the two-step pipeline outperforms direct mention→entity mapping on a standard EDL benchmark.

3. Introduces a clear, methodologically sound MPME model that extends multi-prototype embeddings to entity linking and joint text–KB training.

4. Strong empirical performance: achieves state-of-the-art on a widely used entity linking dataset, beating recent competitive baselines (e.g., Yamada et al., ALIGN).

5. Tackles a core NLP problem by explicitly modeling mention ambiguity with sense-specific vectors (e.g., different senses of “Apple”).

6. Methodology is clear and reproducible, with sensible training choices and implementation details (e.g., smoothing parameter).

7. Fails to provide a conceptual justification for why an intermediate mention-sense layer adds information beyond entity embeddings.

8. Omits critical methodological details about mention senses: number per mention, whether fixed or learned, initialization, and how senses are differentiated during training.

9. Incomplete related-work positioning and overclaiming: ignores TAC KBP and other major EDL benchmarks and comparisons.

10. Requests conceptual explanation of what mention-sense embeddings capture that entity embeddings do not and why two-step mapping is fundamentally superior.

11. Asks for clarification on sense generation/training: how many senses, how initialized/differentiated, and whether senses map one-to-one to candidate entities.

12. Seeks confirmation that entities are learned from KB graph, words from text, and mention-senses from disambiguated contexts, and asks why not directly enrich entity representations instead.

13. Requests that Section 5 explicitly state the unique novelty and role of the mention-sense layer.

14. Asks why TAC KBP shared tasks were not discussed or used as benchmark comparisons.
