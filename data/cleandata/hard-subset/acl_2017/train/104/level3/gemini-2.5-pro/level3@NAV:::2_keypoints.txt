- Introduces a novel two-step EDL pipeline mapping mentions → mention senses → KB entities to address lexical ambiguity.  
- Empirical results: two-step models (MPME/SPME) outperform strong direct mention→entity baselines (e.g., Yamada et al.).  
- Methodology is clearly described and includes a smoothing parameter that likely improves robustness.  
- Fails to explain why the intermediate mention-sense layer improves performance or what unique information senses capture.  
- Missing methodological/reproducibility details: how senses are created/differentiated, number of senses per mention, and sense↔entity mapping specifics.  
- Overclaims scope (“bridging text and knowledge”) and omits citations to prior EDL work (e.g., TAC KBP), lacking evaluation against established benchmarks.
