- Summary: Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding that jointly models text and KB to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention and uses an LM-based disambiguation method, achieving SOTA entity linking results.

- Contribution 1: Improved joint text-knowledge representations—model outperforms ALIGN in supervised entity linking.

- Contribution 2: Efficient language model–based disambiguation approach for mapping mentions to specific senses.

- Contribution 3: Analysis of a smoothing parameter, offering insights into the role of entity popularity in linking.

- Strength 1: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the benchmark.

- Strength 2: Disambiguation method is computationally efficient and effective.

- Strength 3: Provides useful empirical analysis of the smoothing/popularity effect.

- Weakness 1: Comparison with ALIGN is incomplete/confounded by differing hyperparameters, limiting fairness of evaluation.

- Weakness 2: Definition of N(e_j) is unclear and it’s unspecified whether Wikipedia outlink adjacency is treated as directed or undirected.

- Weakness 3: "Mention" terminology is ambiguous—unclear if embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions.

- Question 1: Please provide detailed hyperparameters used in the ALIGN comparison.

- Question 2: Please clarify the definition of N(e_j) and how adjacency is handled in the Wikipedia outlink graph.

- Question 3: Please clarify the meaning of "mention" and whether embeddings are per surface string or per contextual/document-specific mention.
