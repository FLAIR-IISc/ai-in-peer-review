**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model to tackle entity disambiguation and linking (EDL) to Wikipedia. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. The authors also design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms the state-of-the-art methods in entity linking tasks.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding Model**: The authors propose a novel MPME model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention, which is a significant contribution to the field of entity disambiguation and linking.
2. **Joint Modeling of Text and Knowledge**: The model jointly models words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base, which is a key innovation in this work.
3. **Efficient Language Model-Based Disambiguation**: The authors design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense, which is a practical contribution to the field.

**Strengths**

1. **Methodological Clarity**: The paper is methodologically clear, and the authors follow standard embedding best practices.
2. **State-of-the-Art Performance**: The experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms the state-of-the-art methods in entity linking tasks.
3. **Innovative Idea**: The idea of learning multiple sense embeddings for each mention is innovative and has the potential to improve entity disambiguation and linking tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Overclaiming**: The title and introduction overclaim the broader "bridging text and knowledge" while focusing narrowly on EDL.
2. **Lack of Explanation**: The authors do not explain why the two-step MPME/SPME outperforms direct mention→entity mapping (ALIGN/Yamada et al.).
3. **Missing Details**: The paper lacks details on how mention senses are created/differentiated, how many senses per mention, and whether senses map bijectively to KB entities.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a concrete explanation of what additional information mention senses provide over entities and why that justifies an intermediate layer?
2. Can you confirm or correct the assumed setups (entity profiles from neighboring-entity graph, words from distributional profiles, mention senses as context-disambiguated distributional profiles)?
3. How do you create/differentiate mention senses, and how many senses per mention do you use?
4. Can you provide more details on the smoothing parameter idea in Section 6.4.2?
