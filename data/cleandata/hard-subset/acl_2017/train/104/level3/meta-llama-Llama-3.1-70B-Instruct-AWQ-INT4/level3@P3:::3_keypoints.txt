- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling within a single neural language modeling framework.
- Learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context words and KB-derived entities.
- Introduces an efficient language model–based disambiguation method to assign mentions to specific senses.
- Evaluated on entity linking, showing high-quality word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings.
- Strength: conceptually strong idea addressing ambiguity by unifying entity, mention, and sense representations.
- Strength: simple neural learning and training procedure that is practical for many applications.
- Strength: solid empirical evaluation demonstrating embedding quality.
- Weakness: limited novelty — improvements over prior work are modest and not highly striking.
- Weakness: inconsistent prose and unclear exposition in Section 3 and parts of Figure 2.
- Weakness: redundant and confusing terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” vs text anchors).
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from closely related work, particularly Yamada et al. (2016).
- Suggestion: evaluate learned word embeddings on additional NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to demonstrate broader utility.
- Additional comment: reviewer appreciates authors’ response but remains concerned about novelty and lack of clear distinction from previous work.
