- Summary: Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling text and KB entities, with an LM-based disambiguation method that outperforms prior entity linking approaches.

- Contribution: Introduces a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention.

- Contribution: Jointly models words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base.

- Contribution: Designs an efficient language model–based approach for disambiguating mentions to specific senses.

- Strength: Paper is methodologically clear and follows standard embedding best practices.

- Strength: Experimental results report state-of-the-art performance on entity linking tasks.

- Strength: The multi-sense mention embedding idea is innovative and promising for EDL.

- Weakness: Title and introduction overclaim “bridging text and knowledge” despite a narrow focus on EDL.

- Weakness: No explanation for why the two-step MPME/SPME approach outperforms direct mention→entity mapping (ALIGN/Yamada et al.).

- Weakness: Lacks details on how mention senses are created/differentiated, how many senses per mention, and whether senses map bijectively to KB entities.

- Question: What additional information do mention senses provide over entities, and why is an intermediate layer justified?

- Question: Please confirm or correct assumed setups (entity profiles from neighbor-entity graph, words from distributional profiles, mention senses as context-disambiguated distributional profiles).

- Question: How are mention senses created/differentiated, and how many senses per mention are used?

- Question: Provide more details on the smoothing parameter idea in Section 6.4.2.
