**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates text and knowledge into a unified semantic space to address the ambiguity of entity mentions. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. An efficient language model-based approach is also designed to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The authors evaluate their model on the benchmark dataset and achieve state-of-the-art results in entity linking.

**Contributions**

1. **Improved joint text-knowledge representations**: The proposed Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model outperforms ALIGN in supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint text-knowledge representations.
2. **Efficient language model-based disambiguation approach**: The authors design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense, which is a significant contribution to the field of entity linking.
3. **Analysis of smoothing parameter**: The authors analyze the smoothing parameter and provide useful insights into the impact of popularity in entity linking.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved performance**: The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results in entity linking, outperforming ALIGN in supervised entity linking.
2. **Efficient disambiguation approach**: The language model-based disambiguation approach is efficient and effective in disambiguating entity mentions.
3. **Insights into smoothing parameter**: The analysis of the smoothing parameter provides useful insights into the impact of popularity in entity linking.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Incomplete comparison with ALIGN**: The comparison with ALIGN is incomplete and confounded by different hyperparameters, making it difficult to assess the true performance of the proposed model.
2. **Unclear definition of N(e_j)**: The definition of N(e_j) is unclear, and it is not specified whether adjacency treats the directed Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected.
3. **Ambiguity around "mention" terminology**: The terminology "mention" is ambiguous, and it is unclear if embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the comparison with ALIGN?
2. Can you clarify the definition of N(e_j) and how adjacency is treated in the directed Wikipedia outlink graph?
3. Can you provide more insights into the "mention" terminology and how it is used in the proposed model?
