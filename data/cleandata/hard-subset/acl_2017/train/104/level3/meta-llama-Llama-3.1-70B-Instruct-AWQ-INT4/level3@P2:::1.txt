**Paper Summary**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model to address the ambiguity of entity mentions in a unified semantic space. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. Additionally, an efficient language model-based approach is designed to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The authors evaluate their method using entity linking as a study case on a benchmark dataset and achieve state-of-the-art performance.

**Contributions**

Based on the paper, I identify the following contributions, in order of strength:

1. **Improved joint text-knowledge representations**: The proposed model outperforms a closely related approach (ALIGN) in supervised entity linking, suggesting that the joint text-knowledge representations are more effective.
2. **Novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model**: The authors propose a new model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention, which is a significant contribution to the field of entity linking.
3. **Efficient language model-based disambiguation approach**: The authors design an efficient approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved performance**: The proposed model outperforms a closely related approach (ALIGN) in supervised entity linking, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the joint text-knowledge representations.
2. **Direct comparison with a closely related approach**: The authors provide a direct comparison with a closely related approach using very similar input data, which helps to establish the superiority of their method.
3. **Useful insight into the impact of popularity in entity linking**: The authors analyze the smoothing parameter, which yields useful insight into the impact of popularity in entity linking.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Incomplete comparison with ALIGN**: The comparison with ALIGN is incomplete/confounded by different hyperparameters (context window 10 vs 5, vector dims 500 vs 200), which makes it difficult to assess the true impact of the proposed model.
2. **Unclear definition of N(e_j) and adjacency**: The definition of N(e_j) and whether adjacency treats the directed Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected are unclear, which could alter entity contexts and vectors.
3. **Ambiguity around "mention" terminology**: The terminology around "mention" is ambiguous, making it unclear if embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions; "mention embedding" appears to be for mention senses only.
4. **Lack of comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods**: The impact of sense disambiguation order is hard to assess due to the lack of comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify the definition of N(e_j) and whether adjacency treats the directed Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected?
2. Can you provide more details on the "mention" terminology and clarify whether embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions?
3. Can you provide a comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods to assess the impact of sense disambiguation order?
4. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameters used in the comparison with ALIGN and justify the choice of different hyperparameters?
