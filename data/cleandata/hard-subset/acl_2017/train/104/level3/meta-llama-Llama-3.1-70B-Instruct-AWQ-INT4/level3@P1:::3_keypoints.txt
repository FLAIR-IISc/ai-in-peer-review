- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model integrating text and knowledge to handle mention ambiguity.

- Learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context words and KB entities.

- Introduces an efficient language-model-based disambiguation method to assign mentions to specific senses.

- Presents a simple neural learning and training procedure applicable to many downstream tasks.

- Provides qualitative and quantitative evaluations showing high-quality word, entity, and mention embeddings.

- Strength: Integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within one neural language modeling framework.

- Strength: Learning/training procedure is simple and efficient, suitable for broad applications.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation and analysis are thorough, despite modest performance gains.

- Weakness: Limited genuine novelty and only small improvements over prior methods.

- Weakness: Inconsistent prose and unclear exposition in parts (e.g., Section 3, Figure 2).

- Weakness: Redundant/confusing terminology (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "entity-mention pairs" vs anchors).

- Weakness: Method is very close to Yamada et al. (2016) without clearly outlining differences.

- Suggestion: Evaluate learned word embeddings on more NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show broader utility.

- Suggestion: Provide a more detailed comparison with Yamada et al. (2016) to highlight unique contributions.

- Additional comment: Reviewer appreciated the authors' response but still urges clearer, more consistent exposition in revisions.
