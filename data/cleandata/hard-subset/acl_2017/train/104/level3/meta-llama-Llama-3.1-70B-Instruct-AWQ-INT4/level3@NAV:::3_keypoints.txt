- Summary: Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities, with an LM-based disambiguation method and SOTA entity linking results.

- Contribution 1: Integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling into a single neural language modeling framework.

- Contribution 2: Introduces an efficient language-model–based disambiguation approach to assign mentions to specific senses.

- Contribution 3: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on entity linking benchmarks.

- Strength 1: Presents a compelling idea to unify entity, mention, and sense representations.

- Strength 2: Uses a simple, scalable neural training procedure applicable to many tasks.

- Strength 3: Provides solid empirical evaluation with quantitative and qualitative analyses.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty; improvements over prior work are modest.

- Weakness 2: Prose and exposition are inconsistent, with unclear sections (e.g., Section 3, parts of Figure 2).

- Weakness 3: Terminology is redundant and confusing (e.g., different terms for the same concept).

- Weakness 4: Close similarity to Yamada et al. (2016) without clear delineation of differences.

- Question 1: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary words and entities?

- Question 2: How does the model perform on other NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL)?

- Question 3: How can the model be adapted or applied to other domains and applications?
