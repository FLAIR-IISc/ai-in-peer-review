- Proposes a two-step EDL pipeline that maps mentions to senses then links senses to KB entities.
- Introduces MPME, a joint embedding model learning multiple sense embeddings per mention from text and KB entities.
- Claims improved, state-of-the-art performance on a benchmark compared to direct mention-to-entity methods.
- Methodologically clear and follows embedding best practices.
- Demonstrates empirical performance gains on the EDL task.
- Novel idea of using mention senses as an intermediate representation between mentions and entities.
- Title and introduction overclaim the work’s broader impact while the focus is narrowly on EDL.
- No clear explanation of what extra information mention senses provide beyond entities to justify an intermediate layer.
- Missing details on how mention senses are created, differentiated, and mapped to entities.
- Incorrectly claims novelty without citing prior TAC KBP EDL-related work as baselines.
- Ask for clarification on what additional information mention senses provide beyond entities.
- Ask to confirm assumed setups: entity profiles from neighboring-entity graphs, words from distributional profiles, mention senses as context-disambiguated distributional profiles.
- Ask how mention senses are created, how they’re differentiated, and the typical number of senses per mention.
- Ask whether mention senses map bijectively to KB entities, and if not, why.
- Ask what signal at the mention-sense level is not already present in entity pages and whether it could be merged into entity representations.
- Reviewer liked the smoothing parameter idea in Section 6.4.2.
- Reviewer objects to dismissal of KBP evaluation and insists embeddings must be validated via application performance, not just conceptual appeal.
