- Proposes MPME: a multi-prototype mention embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention for entity disambiguation/linking to Wikipedia.  
- Jointly embeds textual context words and knowledge-base entities into a unified semantic space.  
- Claims to outperform direct mention→entity mapping baselines like ALIGN and Yamada et al.  

- Contribution: novel MPME model introducing multiple sense embeddings per mention.  
- Contribution: integration of text and knowledge into a shared embedding space.  
- Contribution: thorough experimental evaluation with qualitative and quantitative analyses.  

- Strength: paper is well-written with clear methodological exposition.  
- Strength: follows standard embedding best practices for robustness.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating model effectiveness.  
- Strength: introduces a novel smoothing parameter idea (Section 6.4.2).  

- Weakness: title/introduction overclaim broader impact by asserting it “bridges text and knowledge” while focusing on EDL.  
- Weakness: lacks clear explanation for why the two-step MPME/SPME approach outperforms direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Weakness: missing critical details on how mention senses are created/differentiated, number of senses per mention, and whether senses map bijectively to KB entities.  
- Weakness: incorrect or overstated novelty claim—prior TAC KBP EDL evaluations and related baselines are not cited.  

- Question: what additional information do mention senses provide over entities, and why is an intermediate mention-sense layer justified?  
- Question: can the authors confirm the assumed setups (entity profiles from neighboring-entity graph, words from distributional profiles, mention senses as context-disambiguated distributional profiles)?  
- Question: provide details on how mention senses are created/differentiated, how many senses per mention, and whether senses map bijectively to KB entities.  
- Question: if mention senses are seeded from entity text, what signal exists at the mention-sense level that is not already present in entity pages, and could that signal be merged into entity representations instead?
