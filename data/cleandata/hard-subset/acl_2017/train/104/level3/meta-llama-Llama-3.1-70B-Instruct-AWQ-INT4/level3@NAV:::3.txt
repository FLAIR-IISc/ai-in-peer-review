**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within the same neural language modeling technique. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. The authors also design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The proposed model is evaluated on entity linking tasks and achieves state-of-the-art results.

**Contributions of this Work**

1. **Integration of entity, mention, and sense modeling**: The paper proposes a novel approach to integrate entity, mention, and sense modeling within the same neural language modeling technique, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **Efficient language model-based approach**: The authors design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense, which is a valuable contribution to the field of entity disambiguation.
3. **State-of-the-art results on entity linking tasks**: The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results on entity linking tasks, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Good idea**: The paper proposes a good idea integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling within the same neural language modeling technique.
2. **Simple neural learning and training procedure**: The authors use a simple neural learning and training procedure that supports a large set of applications.
3. **Solid empirical evaluation**: The paper presents a solid empirical evaluation of the proposed model, including both qualitative and quantitative analysis.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited novel content**: The amount of genuinely novel content in the paper is limited, and the improvements are not striking.
2. **Inconsistent prose and exposition**: The paper's prose and exposition are inconsistent, and some sections, such as Section 3 and parts of Figure 2, lack clarity and detail.
3. **Redundant/confusing terminology**: The paper uses redundant and confusing terminology, such as "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" vs text anchors.
4. **Similarity to previous work**: The paper is quite close to Yamada et al. (2016) and does not sufficiently outline the differences.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the proposed model handles out-of-vocabulary words and entities?
2. How does the proposed model perform on other NLP tasks, such as parsing, coreference, and semantic role labeling?
3. Can you provide more insights on how the proposed model can be applied to other domains and applications?
