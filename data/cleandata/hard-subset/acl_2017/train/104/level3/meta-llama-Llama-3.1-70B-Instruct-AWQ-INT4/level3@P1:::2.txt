**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to Wikipedia by integrating text and knowledge into a unified semantic space. The authors introduce a two-step pipeline that first maps a mention to its sense and then links the sense to an entity in the knowledge base. They employ a joint embedding model, Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME), which learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. The authors claim that their approach outperforms direct mention-to-entity mapping methods and achieves state-of-the-art results on a benchmark dataset.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Two-step pipeline for EDL**: The authors propose a novel two-step pipeline for EDL, which first maps a mention to its sense and then links the sense to an entity in the knowledge base.
2. **Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model**: The authors introduce a joint embedding model, MPME, which learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base.
3. **Improved performance on EDL task**: The authors claim that their approach outperforms direct mention-to-entity mapping methods and achieves state-of-the-art results on a benchmark dataset.

**Strengths**

1. **Methodologically clear and well-executed**: The authors follow standard embedding best practices, and their approach is well-executed and easy to understand.
2. **Improved performance on EDL task**: The authors demonstrate improved performance on the EDL task, which is a significant contribution to the field.
3. **Novel idea of using mention senses**: The authors introduce the idea of using mention senses as an intermediate layer between mentions and entities, which is a novel approach to EDL.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Overclaiming in title and introduction**: The title and introduction overclaim the broader impact of the work, while the paper focuses narrowly on EDL.
2. **Lack of explanation for the additional information provided by mention senses**: The authors do not provide a clear explanation of what additional information mention senses provide over entities and why that justifies an intermediate layer.
3. **Missing details on mention sense creation and differentiation**: The authors do not provide details on how mention senses are created, differentiated, and mapped to entities, which is a crucial aspect of their approach.
4. **Incorrect claim of novelty**: The authors claim novelty for their approach, but prior work on TAC KBP EDL evaluations exists and should be cited as related baselines.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear explanation of what additional information mention senses provide over entities and why that justifies an intermediate layer?
2. Can you confirm or correct the assumed setups outlined in the review, including entity profiles from neighboring-entity graphs, words from distributional profiles, and mention senses as context-disambiguated distributional profiles?
3. How are mention senses created and differentiated, and how many senses are there per mention?
4. Do mention senses map bijectively to KB entities, and if not, why not?
5. If mention senses are seeded from entity text, what signal exists at the mention-sense level that is not already present in entity pages, and could that be merged into entity representations instead?

**Additional Comments**

1. The reviewer likes the smoothing parameter idea in Section 6.4.2, which is a nice touch to the model.
2. The reviewer is unsatisfied with the authors' dismissal of KBP evaluation and argues that embeddings must be validated via application performance, not just conceptual elegance.
