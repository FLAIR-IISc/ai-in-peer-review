- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling text and KB entities, with an LM-based disambiguation step and SOTA EL results.

- Contribution: Improved joint text-knowledge representations that outperform ALIGN in supervised entity linking.

- Contribution: Novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model learning multiple sense embeddings for each mention.

- Contribution: Efficient language model–based disambiguation approach to assign mentions to senses.

- Strength: Demonstrates improved performance over a closely related method (ALIGN).

- Strength: Includes a direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar input data.

- Strength: Analyzes the smoothing parameter, offering insight into the effect of popularity in entity linking.

- Weakness: ALIGN comparison is confounded by different hyperparameters (context window 10 vs 5, vector dims 500 vs 200).

- Weakness: Definition of N(e_j) and whether adjacency treats the Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected is unclear.

- Weakness: “Mention” terminology is ambiguous—unclear if embeddings represent string types or specific document mentions.

- Weakness: No comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods, limiting assessment of sense disambiguation order effects.

- Question: Please clarify the definition of N(e_j) and whether adjacency treats the outlink graph as directed or undirected.

- Question: Please clarify the “mention” terminology and whether embeddings are for types or document-specific mentions.

- Question: Can you provide comparisons with other unsupervised entity linking methods to evaluate disambiguation order impact?

- Question: Provide detailed hyperparameters used for the ALIGN comparison and justify the choice of differing hyperparameters.
