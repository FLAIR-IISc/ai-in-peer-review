- Proposes MPME: a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention for entity disambiguation/linking to Wikipedia.  
- Designs an efficient language-model-based disambiguation method and reports state-of-the-art results on a benchmark dataset.

Contributions:
- Novel MPME model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention.  
- Efficient language model–based approach for mapping mentions to specific senses.  
- Empirical evaluation showing SOTA performance on a benchmark dataset.

Strengths:
- Method is clearly described and follows standard embedding best practices, facilitating reproducibility.  
- MPME offers a novel multi-sense representation for mentions.  
- The LM-based disambiguation approach is practically efficient.

Weaknesses:
- Title and introduction overclaim broader "bridging text and knowledge" while the work focuses narrowly on EDL.  
- No explanation for why the two-step MPME/SPME pipeline outperforms direct mention→entity mapping approaches.  
- Insufficient details on how mention senses are created, differentiated, how many per mention, and their relation to KB entities.  
- Incorrect novelty claim: prior TAC KBP EDL evaluations (e.g., KBP 2016) are not cited as related baselines.

Questions to authors:
- What additional information do mention senses provide over entities, and why is an intermediate mention-sense layer justified?  
- Can you confirm/correct the assumed setups: entity profiles from neighboring-entity graph, words from distributional profiles, and mention senses as context-disambiguated distributional profiles?  
- How are mention senses created/differentiated, and how many senses are used per mention?  
- Do mention senses map bijectively to KB entities?  
- If mention senses are seeded from entity text, what signal at the mention-sense level is not already present in entity pages, and could it be merged into entity representations?  
- Can Section 5 be updated to explicitly state the role and novelty of mention senses if the reviewer’s understanding is correct?

Additional comments:
- Reviewer appreciates the smoothing-parameter idea in Section 6.4.2.  
- Reviewer objects to dismissing KBP evaluation and urges validating embeddings via application performance, not just conceptual arguments.
