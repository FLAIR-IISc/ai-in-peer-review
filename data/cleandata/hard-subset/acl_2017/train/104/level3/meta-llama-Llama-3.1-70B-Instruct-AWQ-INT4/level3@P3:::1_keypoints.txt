- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities, applied to entity linking and claiming state-of-the-art results.
- Contribution: a novel multi-prototype mention embedding model learning multiple sense embeddings per mention.
- Contribution: an efficient language model–based approach to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.
- Contribution: experimental results showing high-quality word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings.
- Strength: outperforms ALIGN in supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint text-knowledge representations.
- Strength: provides a direct comparison with ALIGN using similar input data for a fairer evaluation.
- Strength: analyzes the smoothing parameter’s impact, offering insights into the role of entity popularity.
- Strength: disambiguation approach is efficient and effective at assigning mentions to senses.
- Weakness: ALIGN comparison is incomplete/difficult to interpret due to different hyperparameters (context window and vector dimensions).
- Weakness: unclear definition of N(e_j) and whether adjacency treats the Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected.
- Weakness: ambiguous use of “mention” — unclear if embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions; “mention embedding” seems used only for mention senses.
- Weakness: lacks comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods, so the impact of sense disambiguation order is unknown.
- Question: please define N(e_j) and clarify whether adjacency treats Wikipedia outlinks as directed or undirected.
- Question: clarify the definition of “mention” and whether embeddings represent string types or document-specific mentions.
- Question: provide comparisons with other unsupervised entity linking methods to assess sense disambiguation order effects.
- Question: how will you address the incomplete ALIGN comparison and which hyperparameters would you use for a fair comparison?
