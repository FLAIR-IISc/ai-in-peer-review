**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates text and knowledge into a unified semantic space. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. An efficient language model-based approach is also designed to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The authors evaluate their model on entity linking tasks and achieve state-of-the-art performance.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Improved Joint Text-Knowledge Representations**: The proposed model outperforms the ALIGN model in supervised entity linking tasks, indicating that the joint text-knowledge representations are more effective.
2. **Direct Comparison with a Closely Related Approach**: The authors provide a direct comparison with a closely related approach, allowing for a more accurate evaluation of the proposed model's performance.
3. **Insight into the Impact of Popularity in Entity Linking**: The analysis of the smoothing parameter provides useful insight into the impact of popularity in entity linking, which can inform future research in this area.

**Strengths of the Submission**

1. **Effective Joint Text-Knowledge Representations**: The proposed model demonstrates improved performance in entity linking tasks, indicating that the joint text-knowledge representations are more effective.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of their model, including a comparison with a closely related approach and an analysis of the smoothing parameter.
3. **Novel Approach to Sense Disambiguation**: The proposed language model-based approach to sense disambiguation is novel and effective, allowing for more accurate entity linking.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

1. **Incomplete Comparison with ALIGN**: The comparison with ALIGN is incomplete and confounded by different hyperparameters, making it difficult to accurately evaluate the proposed model's performance.
2. **Unclear Definition of N(e_j)**: The definition of N(e_j) is unclear, and it is unclear whether the adjacency treats the directed Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected, which could alter entity contexts and vectors.
3. **Ambiguity around "Mention" Terminology**: The terminology around "mention" is ambiguous, making it unclear whether the embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the definition of N(e_j) and how it is used in the proposed model?
2. How do you address the ambiguity around "mention" terminology, and can you provide more clarity on whether the embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions?
3. Can you provide a comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods to assess the impact of sense disambiguation order?
