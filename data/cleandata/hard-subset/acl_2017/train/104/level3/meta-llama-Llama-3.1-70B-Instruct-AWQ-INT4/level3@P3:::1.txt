**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. The model is designed to deal with the ambiguity of entity mentions and is applied to the task of entity linking. The authors claim that their model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the benchmark dataset.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention.
2. An efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense.
3. Experimental results demonstrating the high quality of the word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved performance on entity linking**: The proposed model outperforms ALIGN in supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint text-knowledge representations. This is a significant contribution, as entity linking is a challenging task that requires effective integration of text and knowledge.
2. **Direct comparison with a closely related approach**: The authors provide a direct comparison with ALIGN, a closely related approach that uses similar input data. This allows for a fair evaluation of the proposed model's performance.
3. **Insightful analysis of the smoothing parameter**: The authors analyze the impact of the smoothing parameter on entity linking performance, providing useful insights into the role of popularity in entity linking.
4. **Efficient language model-based disambiguation approach**: The proposed disambiguation approach is efficient and effective, allowing for accurate disambiguation of mentions to specific senses.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Incomplete comparison with ALIGN**: The comparison with ALIGN is incomplete, as the authors use different hyperparameters (context window 10 vs 5, vector dimensions 500 vs 200). This makes it difficult to determine the true effectiveness of the proposed model.
2. **Unclear definition of N(e_j) and adjacency**: The authors do not provide a clear definition of N(e_j) and whether adjacency treats the directed Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected. This could alter entity contexts and vectors, affecting the model's performance.
3. **Ambiguity around "mention" terminology**: The authors use the term "mention" ambiguously, making it unclear whether embeddings are for string types or specific document mentions. The term "mention embedding" appears to be used only for mention senses.
4. **Lack of comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods**: The authors do not compare their model with other unsupervised entity linking methods, making it difficult to assess the impact of sense disambiguation order.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear definition of N(e_j) and clarify whether adjacency treats the directed Wikipedia outlink graph as directed or undirected?
2. How do you define a "mention," and are embeddings for string types or specific document mentions?
3. Can you provide a comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods to assess the impact of sense disambiguation order?
4. How do you plan to address the incomplete comparison with ALIGN, and what hyperparameters would you use for a fair comparison?
