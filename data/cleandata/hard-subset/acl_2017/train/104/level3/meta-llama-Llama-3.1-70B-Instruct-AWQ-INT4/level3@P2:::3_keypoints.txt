- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly integrates textual context and KB entities into a unified semantic space.  
- Learns multiple sense embeddings per mention and uses a language-model-based disambiguation to assign mentions to senses.  
- Claims three main contributions: unified neural language modeling of entities/mentions/senses, a simple scalable training procedure, and solid empirical comparisons.  
- Strength: Integration of entity, mention, and sense modeling in one neural LM is a promising idea for improving NLP tasks.  
- Strength: Learning and training procedure is simple, efficient, and practical for many applications.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation is thorough and compares to state-of-the-art, though gains are modest.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—builds on prior work and improvements are not striking.  
- Weakness: Prose and exposition are inconsistent; Section 3 and parts of Figure 2 lack clarity and detail.  
- Weakness: Terminology is redundant and confusing (e.g., inconsistent use of “dictionary of mentions,” “entity-mention pairs,” “text anchors”).  
- Weakness: Paper is very similar to Yamada et al. (2016) and does not clearly delineate differences.  
- Question: Provide more details on the model architecture and training procedure, especially Section 3 and Figure 2.  
- Question: How will the authors resolve redundant and confusing terminology?  
- Question: Provide a detailed comparison with Yamada et al. (2016) and highlight unique contributions.  
- Question: Have the learned word embeddings been evaluated on other NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to ensure they are not weaker than simpler word spaces?  
- Additional comment: Authors are thanked for their response, but paper still needs more innovation, clearer presentation, and consistency.
