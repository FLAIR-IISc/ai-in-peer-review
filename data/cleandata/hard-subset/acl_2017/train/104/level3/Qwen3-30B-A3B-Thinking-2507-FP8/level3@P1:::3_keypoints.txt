- Summary: Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities, with a language-model-based disambiguation method and claimed state-of-the-art entity linking results.

- Contribution 1: Unified framework that integrates textual context, entity knowledge, and sense disambiguation into one neural training procedure for multi-sense embeddings.

- Contribution 2: Practical disambiguation approach leveraging learned embeddings, demonstrated with strong empirical results on benchmark entity linking tasks.

- Contribution 3: Comprehensive qualitative and quantitative validation of embedding quality, showing utility beyond the primary task.

- Strength 1: Strong conceptual integration—joint modeling embeds disambiguation into learning, avoiding ad-hoc modules.

- Strength 2: Practical and scalable design—uses standard LM objectives and an efficient disambiguation method suitable for large-scale use.

- Strength 3: Rigorous empirical validation—intrinsic analyses, downstream performance gains (1.2–1.8% F1), and ablations isolating multi-prototype contributions.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—approach closely parallels Yamada et al. (2016) and differences are not clearly articulated.

- Weakness 2: Terminology and exposition flaws—inconsistent terms and missing details (e.g., prototype selection/updates, Figure 2 omissions) hinder reproducibility.

- Weakness 3: Modest empirical gains—reported improvements are small (e.g., 1.2% F1), making the SOTA claim feel overstated.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient embedding evaluation—embeddings are only validated on entity linking, not on broader NLP tasks to support generality claims.

- Question 1: How does MPME specifically differ from Yamada et al. (2016) in architecture, training, or disambiguation?

- Question 2: Will the authors evaluate multi-prototype embeddings on additional NLP tasks (e.g., parsing, coreference) to demonstrate broader utility?

- Question 3: Please precisely define terms like "text anchors" and "entity-mention pairs" and resolve inconsistencies in Section 3 and Figure 2.
