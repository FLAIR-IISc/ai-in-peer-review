- Proposal: MPME learns multiple sense embeddings per mention via joint modeling of textual contexts and KB entities to resolve mention ambiguity.  
- Core contribution: framework that generates context-aware, sense-specific embeddings to improve entity linking.  
- Secondary contribution: an efficient language model for disambiguating mentions to specific senses, validated on benchmarks.  
- Tertiary contribution: analysis of smoothing parameter α, showing how entity popularity affects disambiguation performance.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming ALIGN by 1.8–3.2 F1 across datasets, supporting the multi-prototype approach.  
- Strength: careful analysis finds α=0.5 optimal, offering practical guidance on entity popularity effects and model tuning.  
- Strength: direct comparison with ALIGN (a closely related recent model) helps isolate the impact of the multi-prototype mechanism.  
- Weakness: ALIGN comparison is confounded by different hyperparameters (context window and vector dimensionality); needs re-run with identical settings.  
- Weakness: definition of N(e_j) is ambiguous—unclear if Wikipedia outlink graph is treated as directed or undirected, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: inconsistent terminology—“mention embeddings” vs “sense embeddings” blurs standard NLP meanings and confuses the contribution.  
- Question: clarify whether the Wikipedia outlink graph for N(e_j) is directed or undirected and how that choice affects entity contexts.  
- Question: how are MPME "mention sense" embeddings trained on document-level mentions applied to new mentions—does disambiguation operate at document or mention-instance level?
