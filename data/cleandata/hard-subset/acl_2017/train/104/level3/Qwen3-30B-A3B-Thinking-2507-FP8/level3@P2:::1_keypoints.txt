- Proposes MPME: a multi-prototype mention embedding framework that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention via joint modeling of textual contexts and knowledge-base entities, plus a language-model-based disambiguation module.  
- Empirically analyzes the smoothing parameter λ’s impact on entity linking and claims state-of-the-art results (though this claim is questioned).  
- Novelty: principled multi-prototype embedding approach that captures distinct contextual senses, supported by qualitative analysis.  
- Insightful smoothing analysis showing trade-offs: higher λ favors popular entities improving common-mention accuracy but degrades rare-entity disambiguation.  
- Strong empirical performance on benchmarks (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL) and better supervised results than ALIGN in the reported experiments.  
- Confounded comparison with ALIGN due to inconsistent hyperparameters (context window, embedding dimensionality), undermining the SOTA claim.  
- Ambiguous technical definitions (unclear neighborhood N(e_j) semantics and ambiguous use of “mention” vs. sense), obscuring methodology.  
- Lack of evaluation against unsupervised baselines and missing analysis of disambiguation order (pre- vs. post-embedding), leaving the disambiguation module’s advantage unverified.  
- Request to clarify ALIGN hyperparameters and provide fair comparisons with identical settings.  
- Request to define N(e_j) precisely (directed vs. undirected outlink graph) and discuss its effect on entity embeddings.  
- Request to justify omission of unsupervised baselines and analyze how disambiguation order affects performance.  
- Clarify terminology: whether embeddings represent senses (not textual mention spans) and justify using “mention embedding.”  
- Additional issues: bibliography contains typos/duplicates, smoothing analysis needs further ablations by entity type, and claimed computational efficiency/inference-time comparisons (e.g., vs. ALIGN) are missing.
