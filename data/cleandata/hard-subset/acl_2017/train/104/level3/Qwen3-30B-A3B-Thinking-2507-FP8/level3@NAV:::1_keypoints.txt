- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model claiming SOTA by learning multiple sense embeddings per mention and disambiguating via a language model.  
- Primary contribution: smoothing parameter analysis revealing popularity’s critical impact on entity linking performance.  
- Secondary contribution: direct comparison with a closely related approach using identical input data, but this is undermined by methodological flaws.  
- Embedding qualitative/quantitative analysis shows high-quality representations but the core approach lacks novelty.  
- Strength: useful smoothing parameter insights (Section 4.3).  
- Strength: clear setup for input data comparison.  
- Weakness: Confounded comparison with ALIGN due to uncontrolled hyperparameters (context window 10 vs. 5, vector dims 500 vs. 200), invalidating the SOTA claim.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology (undefined N(e_j)) and unclear treatment of the Wikipedia outlink graph (directed vs. undirected), jeopardizing reproducibility.  
- Weakness: No comparison to unsupervised baselines (e.g., Huang et al. 2012; Reisinger & Mooney 2010), so the effect of the proposed disambiguation order is unassessed.  
- Weakness: Confused definition of “mention,” conflating string-level mentions with document-specific instances and obscuring whether embeddings are general or context-dependent.  
- Questions to authors: clarify Wikipedia graph adjacency choice, provide ALIGN hyperparameters (context window, vector dims), and include at least one unsupervised sense-disambiguation baseline.  
- Conclusion: Core innovation is obscured by methodological flaws and unclear presentation; smoothing analysis is valuable but the evaluation’s validity is compromised.  
- Additional issue: Reference list appears corrupted (repeated entries, typos like “Efficie t”), suggesting insufficient preparation.
