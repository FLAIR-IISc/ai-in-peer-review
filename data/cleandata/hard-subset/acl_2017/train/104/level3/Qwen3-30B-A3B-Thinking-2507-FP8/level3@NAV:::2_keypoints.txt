- Proposes a two-step EDL pipeline (mention → mention-sense → entity) with joint multi-prototype embeddings (MPME/SPME) claiming to bridge text and knowledge.  
- Core contribution is narrowly focused on EDL, conflicting with the paper’s broader title/introduction.  
- Main technical novelty is the two-step embedding model (MPME/SPME), but its novelty is questionable.  
- Secondary contribution: smoothing parameters (Section 6.4.2), methodologically sound yet minor.  
- Strength: Methodology is clear and follows standard embedding practices with solid empirical evaluation.  
- Strength: The smoothing parameter is a practical, thoughtful improvement for disambiguation.  
- Strength: Strong quantitative performance, achieving state-of-the-art on EDL benchmarks.  
- Weakness: Fails to cite TAC KBP 2016 and omits standard KBP baselines, invalidating novelty claims.  
- Weakness: No justification for why the intermediate mention-sense layer outperforms direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Weakness: Critical details missing on how mention senses are created, how many per mention, and their mapping to KB entities.  
- Question: How are mention senses defined/differentiated, and do they map bijectively to KB entities?  
- Question: What specific signal does the mention-sense layer capture that entity representations (e.g., Wikipedia pages) do not?  
- Question: Why were TAC KBP 2016 EDL results omitted despite being the standard evaluation?  
- Conclusion: Empirical gains are compelling but unaddressed theoretical gaps and misaligned claims undermine the paper’s conceptual novelty; smoothing alone is insufficient.
