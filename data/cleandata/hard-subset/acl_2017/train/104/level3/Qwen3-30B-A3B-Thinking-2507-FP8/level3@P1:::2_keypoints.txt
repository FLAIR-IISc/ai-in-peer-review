- Proposes MPME: a multi-prototype mention embedding model learned by joint text–knowledge optimization.  
- Presents a two-step disambiguation pipeline (mention → mention-sense → entity) mapping learned senses to KB entities.  
- Claims state-of-the-art empirical results on standard EDL datasets and provides qualitative embedding analysis.  

- Methodology is sound and clearly designed, using justified components like a context word prediction objective.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements on benchmarks (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL) over prior mention→entity methods.  
- Practical smoothing strategy for sparse contexts improves robustness and is well-executed.  

- Title/intro overclaim broader text–knowledge integration while work is narrowly focused on EDL, misrepresenting scope.  
- Evaluation omits key baselines (e.g., TAC KBP 2016), undermining the validity of the “state-of-the-art” claim.  
- Unclear what mention senses add beyond entity embeddings: creation process, number per mention, and mapping to entities are not explained.  
- Novelty is overstated given prior multi-prototype embedding work (e.g., Huang et al. 2012; Neelakantan et al. 2014) and lacks proper contextualization.  

- How are mention senses created: seeded from entity text or derived differently, and what unique signal do senses provide beyond entity representations?  
- How many senses per mention are assigned and is there a one-to-one mapping between senses and KB entities?  
- Why does the two-step mention→sense→entity pipeline outperform direct mention→entity mapping if senses mirror contextualized entity profiles?  

- Section 5 must explicitly state the role and novelty of mention senses or revise novelty claims if senses simply mirror entity profiles.  
- Omitting KBP 2016 baselines is a critical oversight; embedding efficacy should be validated on standard EDL evaluations to support SOTA claims.
