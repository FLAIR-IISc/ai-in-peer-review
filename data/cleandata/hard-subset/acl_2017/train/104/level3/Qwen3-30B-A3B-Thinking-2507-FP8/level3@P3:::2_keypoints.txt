- Claims a two-step EDL pipeline (mention → mention-sense → entity) with multi-prototype mention embeddings (MPME) but novelty is overstated relative to prior work (e.g., Huang et al., 2012).  
- Methodological clarity: implements a standard joint word/entity embedding pipeline with a useful smoothing parameter (Section 6.4.2).  
- Empirical validation: MPME/SPME shows improved entity linking on the single benchmark dataset used, with strong qualitative analysis (Section 5.1).  
- Technical execution is solid: joint modeling and smoothing improve robustness and adhere to embedding best practices.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms direct mention→entity methods (ALIGN/Yamada et al.) on the reported benchmark.  
- Clear presentation and reproducibility: methodology (Sections 3–4) is well-structured and EDL focus grounds the work.  
- Overclaimed scope: framing as "bridging text and knowledge" is misleading because the method is narrowly applied to EDL.  
- Unexplained mention-sense layer: paper fails to justify how senses are created, how many exist, whether they map to entities, or what unique signal senses provide beyond entity embeddings.  
- Intermediate layer may be redundant if senses are simply seeded from entity text; no explanation why senses add value over entity profiles.  
- Critical citation omission: KBP 2016 and related EDL benchmarks/baselines are not cited or evaluated, weakening empirical rigor.  
- Dismissal of KBP evaluation is unjustified: application-level performance on standard EDL tasks is the proper validation for embeddings.  
- Requests to authors: clarify mention-sense generation, mapping to entities, and unique signal of senses versus entities.  
- Requests to authors: justify omission of KBP 2016, cite it as a baseline, and evaluate to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Conclusion: technically sound with a useful smoothing contribution, but unexplained intermediate layer and omission of KBP make the claimed novelty unsubstantiated; acceptance should require justification of mention-sense layer and inclusion of KBP.
