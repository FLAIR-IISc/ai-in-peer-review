

This paper proposes a two-step entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) pipeline (mention → mention-sense → entity) using joint embeddings, claiming to "bridge text and knowledge" via multi-prototype mention embeddings. However, the core contribution is narrowly focused on EDL, contradicting the overreaching title/introduction. The primary contribution is the two-step embedding model (MPME/SPME), though its novelty is questionable. The secondary contribution—smoothing parameters in Section 6.4.2—is methodologically sound but minor.

**Strengths:**  
1. The pipeline is methodologically clear and adheres to standard embedding practices, with solid empirical results on benchmark datasets.  
2. The smoothing parameter technique (Section 6.4.2) is a thoughtful, practical improvement for disambiguation.  
3. The paper demonstrates strong quantitative performance on EDL tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results.  

**Weaknesses:**  
1. **Novelty claim is invalid:** The paper fails to cite TAC KBP 2016 (a standard EDL benchmark), misrepresenting the novelty. Prior KBP evaluations are direct baselines that must be addressed.  
2. **Lack of justification for core innovation:** The authors claim MPME/SPME outperforms direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN) but provide *no explanation* for why the intermediate "mention-sense" layer adds value. This is the paper’s central weakness.  
3. **Critical missing details:** The paper omits how mention senses are created (e.g., source of sense definitions, number per mention, bijectivity to KB entities). If senses are seeded from entity text, the authors must clarify *what signal exists at the sense level not already in entity representations*—otherwise, the layer is redundant.  

**Questions to Authors:**  
1. How are mention senses defined/differentiated? Do they map bijectively to KB entities?  
2. What specific signal does the "mention-sense" layer capture that is absent in entity representations (e.g., from Wikipedia pages), justifying its inclusion?  
3. Why were TAC KBP 2016 EDL results omitted despite being the standard evaluation for this task?  

The paper’s empirical results are compelling but undermined by unaddressed theoretical gaps and misaligned claims. The authors must rigorously justify the mention-sense layer’s necessity and correct the novelty assertion. Without these, the contribution lacks conceptual novelty beyond incremental improvements to embedding pipelines. The smoothing parameter is a positive note, but insufficient to offset the core weaknesses.
