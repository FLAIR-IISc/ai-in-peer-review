- Proposes MPME to learn sense-specific mention embeddings jointly from text and KBs, with the main contribution being the evaluation framework showing strong entity linking using a simple disambiguation method.  
- Joint modeling of text, entities, and sense embeddings is presented as a contribution but builds incrementally on prior work.  
- The disambiguation method (efficient language model) is secondary and relies on the learned embeddings rather than being novel.  
- Solid empirical evaluation with thorough quantitative/qualitative analysis and SOTA entity linking results (modest absolute gains like +1.2% F1 on AIDA).  
- Practical, straightforward training procedure (skip-gram-like objective) that avoids complex optimization and aids broad applicability.  
- Clear presentation in key sections (problem formulation and results), aiding reproducibility and understanding.  
- Main novelty concern: substantial overlap with Yamada et al. (2016) and insufficient differentiation or comparative analysis.  
- Incomplete/inconsistent exposition in model details (Section 3) and Figure 2, missing critical implementation specifics.  
- Redundant and ambiguous terminology (e.g., "text anchors," "dictionary of mentions," "sense embeddings") used interchangeably, causing confusion.  
- Request authors to explicitly compare MPME to Yamada et al. (2016) in architecture, loss functions, and prototype derivation to clarify novelty.  
- Suggest evaluating multi-prototype embeddings on additional NLP tasks (e.g., SRL, dependency parsing) to demonstrate broader utility.  
- Ask for clarification on the relationship between "dictionary of mentions" and "entity-mention pairs" and whether they are distinct data structures.  
- Additional comment: reference list contains multiple typos and duplicates; limited novelty and exposition issues make the paper a weak fit for ACL despite strong empirical validation.
