- Proposal: Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly models entity, mention, and sense, learning multiple sense embeddings per mention and using a simple language-modeling disambiguation, evaluated on entity linking.
- Strength: Integrated entity-mention-sense modeling is well-motivated and addresses ambiguity in semantic spaces.
- Strength: Training procedure is straightforward and broadly applicable across NLP tasks.
- Strength: Empirical evaluation is robust with consistent (though modest) improvements over baselines and useful qualitative analysis.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—method closely mirrors Yamada et al. (2016) with insufficient differentiation.
- Weakness: Inconsistent terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions,” “entity-mention pairs,” “text anchors”) creates confusion.
- Weakness: Key sections (Section 3, Figure 2) lack clarity and detail, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: Paper does not convincingly justify incremental advances over prior work, problematic for a novelty-focused venue.
- Question: Request detailed comparison showing how disambiguation differs from Yamada et al. (2016) beyond implementation details.
- Question: Suggest evaluating embeddings on other tasks (e.g., dependency parsing, coreference) to show they are not inferior to simpler word embeddings.
- Overall: Sound method with solid evaluation, but limited novelty and unclear exposition significantly weaken the contribution; author response did not fully resolve the novelty gap.
