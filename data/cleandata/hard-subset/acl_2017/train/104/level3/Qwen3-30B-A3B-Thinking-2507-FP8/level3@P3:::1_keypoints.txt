- Paper proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly models textual contexts and KB entities to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention.

- Claimed contributions: (1) multi-prototype approach for mention ambiguity, (2) efficient disambiguation linking mentions to entity senses, (3) analysis of smoothing parameter’s impact on linking.

- Reviewer ranks contribution (3) smoothing analysis as most significant, (2) direct comparison as second, and (1) multi-prototype approach as least novel given prior multi-sense work.

- Strength: smoothing parameter analysis gives actionable insight on how entity popularity affects linking and helps handle popular and rare entities.

- Strength: direct comparison to a closely related method using similar input data isolates benefits of the proposed approach.

- Strength: qualitative embedding analysis shows multi-prototype embeddings capture meaningful semantic distinctions between senses.

- Weakness: comparison with ALIGN is confounded by different hyperparameters (window 10 vs 5, dims 500 vs 200), so performance differences may be due to parameters, not method.

- Weakness: unclear definition of N(e_j) and whether Wikipedia outlink graph adjacency is treated as directed or undirected, leaving graph-based entity contexts ambiguous.

- Weakness: unclear "mention" terminology—uncertain if embeddings represent string types (e.g., “Paris” concept) or specific document mentions—obscures scope and applicability.

- Weakness: no comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods, so impact of their disambiguation order is not properly assessed.

- Q1: Clarify whether Wikipedia outlink adjacency is treated as directed or undirected and how that choice affects entity context vectors.

- Q2: Specify what “mention” refers to—general string types or specific document mentions.

- Q3: Provide a fair ALIGN comparison using the same hyperparameters (context window 5, vector dim 200).

- Q4: Explain the interpretation of the smoothing parameter’s relationship with entity popularity and implications for entity linking system design across domains.
