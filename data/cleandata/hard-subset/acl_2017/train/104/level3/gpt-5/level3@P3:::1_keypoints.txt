- Summary: Proposes multi-prototype mention embeddings learned jointly from text and KB entities with an LM-based disambiguator; achieves SOTA supervised entity linking and analyzes popularity smoothing.

- Contribution (primary): Introduces joint text–knowledge multi-prototype mention representations that improve supervised EL over a strong baseline (ALIGN) with comparable inputs.

- Contribution (secondary): Provides a simple, efficient LM-based sense disambiguation mechanism for inference.

- Contribution (secondary): Empirically analyzes a popularity-smoothing parameter showing trade-offs between context and popularity priors.

- Strength: Demonstrates strong empirical gains over ALIGN under comparable training inputs, isolating benefit of multi-prototype mention representations.

- Strength: Direct comparison using similar input data increases credibility that gains come from modeling choices rather than data differences.

- Strength: Controlled analysis of popularity smoothing clarifies how priors interact with contextual signals in EL.

- Strength: Conceptual clarity in focusing multi-prototype representations on mentions, with qualitative analyses improving interpretability.

- Strength: Disambiguation mechanism is simple and efficient, making the approach practical at inference time.

- Weakness: Comparison to ALIGN is confounded by mismatched hyperparameters (e.g., context window size, embedding dimensionality), weakening attribution of gains.

- Weakness: Paper fails to precisely define N(ej) and whether the Wikipedia link graph is directed or symmetrized, harming reproducibility.

- Weakness: Terminology around “mention embeddings” is unclear—uncertain whether they are type-level, occurrence-level, or sense-tied—creating ambiguity about generalization and usage.

- Weakness: Limited assessment of the disambiguation strategy versus strong unsupervised EL baselines or alternative inference orders.

- Weakness: Missing key ablations (single- vs multi-prototype, window size, dimensionality, entity–entity graph, simpler scoring) needed to isolate component contributions.

- Question: Precisely define N(ej): directed vs symmetrized graph, handling of redirects/disambiguation pages, and how neighborhood enters the objective.

- Question: Clarify what a “mention embedding” is (type-level, occurrence-level, or sense-tied) and how unseen surface forms are handled at test time.

- Question: Re-run ALIGN comparison with matched hyperparameters or provide sensitivity analysis to show robustness of gains.

- Question: Describe LM disambiguator training and scoring details (context window, vocabulary, smoothing) and report inference complexity relative to candidate set size.

- Question: Provide ablations isolating multi-prototype vs single-prototype contributions and the effect of the entity–entity graph, or preliminary results if available.

- Additional: Clean up duplicated/corrupted references in the bibliography for the camera-ready.

- Additional: Report training data statistics and preprocessing details (tokenization, stopwords, min-counts) to improve reproducibility.
