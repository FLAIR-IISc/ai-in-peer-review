- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per surface mention using text and KB signals, plus an LM-based disambiguation, and reports modest state-of-the-art EL gains.  
- Contribution: joint training framework inducing multiple embeddings per mention to reduce surface-form ambiguity.  
- Contribution: efficient local disambiguation using a language-model-based scorer to select mention senses/entities without heavy global inference.  
- Contribution: empirical and qualitative analyses showing coherent word, entity, and mention-sense embeddings and competitive EL performance.  
- Strength: conceptually integrates mentions, entities, and senses in one neural framework, improving interpretability of embedding neighborhoods.  
- Strength: simple, scalable training (negative sampling/contrastive style) and lightweight LM-based disambiguation suitable for practical deployment.  
- Strength: consistent empirical gains on EL benchmarks with ablations and qualitative visualizations supporting the method.  
- Strength: parts of the formalization (objective and training signals) are presented clearly, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited novelty given prior multi-prototype and joint text–KB embedding work; improvements appear incremental.  
- Weakness: insufficient, apples-to-apples comparison and differentiation from closest prior (Yamada et al., 2016).  
- Weakness: exposition and terminology issues (Section 3, Figure 2) leave sense creation/update, prototype counts, and LM details unclear; references contain errors.  
- Weakness: narrow evaluation — lacks intrinsic embedding tests and downstream tasks, and comparisons to strong global/collective EL systems.  
- Weakness: missing analysis of computational/memory costs, sensitivity to hyperparameters, and performance on rare/long-tail mentions.  
- Question: How are multiple prototypes induced/maintained — fixed or dynamic, and what split/merge criteria are used?  
- Question: Is the LM-based disambiguation trained jointly or post hoc, what is the exact scoring function, and its per-candidate complexity?  
- Question: Can you provide a controlled comparison with Yamada et al. (2016) under identical data, candidate generation, and evaluation?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to number of prototypes, context window, and negative sampling — please include sensitivity plots?  
- Question: Do the learned word embeddings remain competitive on intrinsic tests or downstream tasks, and what trade-offs occur?  
- Question: What is the coverage of the mention/anchor dictionary and KB, and how are NIL/unseen surface forms handled at test time?  
- Author response: Authors clarified some training and comparison details but did not fully resolve novelty and clarity concerns; stronger comparative study and clearer Section 3 needed.
