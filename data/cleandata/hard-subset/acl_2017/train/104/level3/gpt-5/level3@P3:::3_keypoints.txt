- Summary: Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly learns words, mention senses, and entities in one space and uses an LM-based scorer for disambiguation, reporting strong EL results but with concerns about novelty and exposition.

Contributions:
- Unified framework learning multi-prototype mention vectors aligned with words and entities for sense-specific representations.
- Simple, efficient LM-based disambiguation that uses the learned space for entity linking without heavy feature engineering.
- Empirical study showing competitive-to-SOTA EL performance and qualitative nearest-neighbor analyses.

Strengths:
- Conceptually clean joint modeling of entities, mentions, and senses that directly addresses mention ambiguity.
- Simple, scalable training and inference with straightforward objectives and a lightweight disambiguation procedure.
- Solid empirical EL evaluation demonstrating competitive performance using primarily learned embeddings and a simple resolver.
- Parts of the modeling are precisely formalized, aiding reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: method is incremental relative to prior joint word–entity and multi-sense embedding work, especially Yamada et al.
- Exposition gaps: key procedural details (sense induction, prototype counts, disambiguation integration) are under-specified.
- Confusing terminology: overlapping terms (“dictionary of mentions,” “entity–mention pairs,” “text anchors”) obscure data flow and supervision.
- Insufficient direct comparison to Yamada et al.: lacks crisp technical contrasts and controlled ablations isolating differences.
- Unclear impact on pure word representations; no downstream evaluations to show word-space quality.

Questions to authors:
- Precisely what technical differences exist between your objective/training pipeline and Yamada et al. (2016/2017)? Provide side-by-side comparison and ablation.
- How is the number of prototypes per mention determined, initialized, assigned, and updated, and how do you prevent collapse/proliferation?
- What exact LM-based disambiguation score is computed, what context window is used, how are priors incorporated, and is selection local or global?
- Define “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity–mention pairs,” and “text anchors”: which supervise training, which generate candidates, and how are they constructed?
- What are training time, memory usage, corpus scale, and how does complexity scale with mentions/senses; any engineering/tricks used?
- Can you report downstream evaluations (e.g., parsing, SRL, coreference, NER) to validate word-embedding quality versus baselines?

Additional comments:
- Improve Section 3 and Figure 2 with a clear training-loop diagram, parameter matrices, and aligned symbols/equations.
- Provide stronger ablations: remove entity signals, use single-prototype mentions, vary priors, and test without the LM scorer.
- Clarify hyperparameters and training details: negative sampling, frequency thresholds, handling of rare mentions/entities, and sense pruning/merging.
