- Proposes a multi-prototype mention embedding model that jointly learns word, mention, and entity representations in a shared space.  
- Assigns multiple sense embeddings per surface mention and uses a lightweight language-model objective to disambiguate mentions in context.  
- Evaluated mainly on entity linking, showing modest but consistent quantitative improvements and qualitative evidence of coherent senses.  
- Contribution: a unified neural framework integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling with an LM-based disambiguation mechanism.  
- Contribution: a simple, scalable training procedure that is broadly applicable to mention/entity disambiguation tasks.  
- Contribution: empirical study with competitive entity linking results and analyses supporting learned representation quality.  
- Strength: integrative modeling that targets ambiguity and bridges text and KB signals without heavy feature engineering.  
- Strength: simple, practical training likely easy to reproduce and extend.  
- Strength: solid empirical evaluation with consistent gains and qualitative case studies; parts of the formalization are clear.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—closely related to prior joint text–KB and multi-sense embedding work (notably Yamada et al. 2016) with insufficient articulation/ablation of differences.  
- Weakness: exposition gaps (details on sense induction/selection, negative sampling, parts of Figure 2) and errors/duplications in references.  
- Weakness: confusing terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity–mention pairs,” “text anchors”) that obscures data assumptions and construction.  
- Weakness: lack of demonstrated generality—no transfer evaluations of the learned word embeddings on other NLP tasks (parsing, coref, SRL).  
- Questions for authors: explicitly delineate differences from Yamada et al. (2016) and provide ablations; clarify construction/role of dictionaries and anchors; detail sense induction, disambiguation LM, and negative sampling; report transfer evaluations on parsing/coref/SRL.  
- Reviewer note: authors’ response was helpful but did not fully resolve novelty and clarity concerns.
