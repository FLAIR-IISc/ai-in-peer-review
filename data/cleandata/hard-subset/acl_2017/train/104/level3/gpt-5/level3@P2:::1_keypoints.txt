- Contribution: Shows empirical gains over ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint text–KB representations.
- Contribution: Proposes multi-prototype (sense) embeddings for surface-form mentions that couple textual contexts and KB neighbor contexts.
- Contribution: Analyzes a smoothing parameter controlling popularity priors, illuminating the popularity–context trade-off.

- Strength: Consistent empirical improvements over a closely related baseline (ALIGN), strengthening the claim of better mention–entity semantics.
- Strength: Direct, focused comparison setup aligned to ALIGN reduces confounders and makes gains more believable.
- Strength: Useful analysis of popularity smoothing that gives practical deployment guidance and explains error modes.
- Strength: Conceptually well-motivated multi-prototype design that naturally addresses mention polysemy by joint text–KB modeling.
- Strength: Disambiguation method is simple and seemingly efficient, making the approach practical for large-scale EL.

- Weakness: ALIGN comparison may be unfair due to differing hyperparameters (context window size and vector dimensionality) that affect results.
- Weakness: Graph neighborhood N(ej) is underspecified (directed vs. undirected, incoming vs. outgoing links, handling of redirects/disambiguation pages).
- Weakness: “Mention” terminology and granularity are ambiguous—unclear if prototypes are corpus-wide or token-level, and what is learned vs. inferred.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation of the disambiguation component and ordering; missing comparisons to other unsupervised/lightly supervised EL methods.
- Weakness: Missing ablations (single vs. multi-prototype, text-only vs. KB-only vs. joint, number of prototypes, smoothing robustness) to isolate contributions.

- Question: Precisely define N(ej): is the Wikipedia graph directed, which link types are included, and how are redirects/disambiguations/templates handled?
- Question: Clarify “mention”: are sense prototypes per surface-form type or per document, is there a sense-agnostic embedding, how are prototype counts chosen and initialized?
- Question: Can ALIGN be re-run with matched context window and vector dimensionality (and vice versa) or show sensitivity plots to rule out hyperparameter effects?
- Question: What is the computational complexity and runtime of disambiguation per mention/document, does the method use collective/global reasoning, and how sensitive are results to ordering?
- Question: Can the disambiguation module be compared to standard unsupervised EL baselines (prior-only, prior+context, PPR) while holding embeddings fixed?
- Question: How stable is the smoothing parameter across datasets/domains, and is it tuned per dataset?

- Comment: Writing/notation needs clarification (e.g., define N(ej) and “mention” early; consider renaming “mention embedding” to “surface-form sense embedding”).
- Comment: For reproducibility, release code, pre-trained embeddings, and detailed hyperparameters/preprocessing (vocab sizes, graph stats, windowing, negatives).
- Comment: Clean the bibliography to remove duplicates and typos.
