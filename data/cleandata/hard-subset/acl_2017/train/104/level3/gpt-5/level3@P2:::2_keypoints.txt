- Proposes a two-step EDL pipeline (mention → mention-sense → entity) with Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) learning joint embeddings for words, entities, and multiple mention prototypes.  
- Empirical claim: adding an intermediate mention-sense layer and multi-prototype embeddings improves EDL accuracy over direct mention→entity baselines.  
- Method: joint embedding framework + language-model-based sense assignment and a smoothing strategy.  
- Analysis: qualitative evidence shows coherent, interpretable embeddings across words, entities, and mention-senses.  
- Strength: Clear, standard modeling recipe building on well-known embedding techniques, making it reproducible and efficient.  
- Strength: Demonstrates empirical gains over strong direct-alignment baselines, supporting the value of an intermediate sense layer.  
- Strength: Qualitative analyses increase confidence that learned embeddings capture meaningful cross-modal relations.  
- Strength: Practical smoothing parameter is a useful, well-motivated robustness improvement.  
- Weakness: Paper overclaims scope by framing work as broadly “bridging text and knowledge” while evaluating narrowly on EDL.  
- Weakness: Missing core methodological details on how mention-senses are created, how many prototypes per mention, and how proliferation is controlled.  
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation for why the intermediate sense layer helps; lacks causal ablations (e.g., with/without sense layer, varying prototypes).  
- Weakness: Incomplete evaluation—does not compare to or contextualize strong TAC KBP EDL baselines or handle NIL/cluster metrics.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of design alternatives (e.g., clustering algorithms, sense priors, contrastive vs LM disambiguation, entity profile construction).  
- Questions: Confirm the exact pipeline sequence and detail how mention-senses are initialized, counted, and mapped to entities (one-to-one or many-to-one).  
- Questions: Explain what unique signal mention-senses capture beyond entity embeddings, with concrete examples.  
- Questions: Clarify entity profile construction, context sampling/weighting, and negative sampling strategy.  
- Questions: Provide ablation/sensitivity results for removing the sense layer, single vs multi-prototype, varying prototype counts, smoothing values, and LM vs simpler heuristics.  
- Additional requests: Revise title/intro to match EDL scope, clean duplicate/typo references, and report training/inference costs and test-time complexity added by the sense layer.  
- Post-note: If TAC KBP evaluation is omitted, authors must justify or provide comparative downstream results—joint embeddings need downstream validation, not just intrinsic analyses.
