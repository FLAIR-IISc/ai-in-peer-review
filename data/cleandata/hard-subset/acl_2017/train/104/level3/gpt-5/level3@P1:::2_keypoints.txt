- Summary: Proposes a two-step EDL pipeline that learns multi-prototype mention embeddings (MPME) and disambiguates mentions to senses then to KB entities, with LM-based disambiguation and reported gains over direct mention→entity baselines.

- Contribution: Shows that an explicit mention-sense layer (MPME/SPME) improves EDL accuracy compared to direct mapping baselines.

- Contribution: Introduces an efficient language-model-based disambiguator with a smoothing scheme for robust sense selection in context.

- Contribution: Presents a joint training setup producing compatible word, entity, and mention-sense embeddings enabling fast scoring at test time.

- Strength: Methodologically clean joint embedding framework following best practices, easy to reproduce and adopt.

- Strength: Empirical improvements over direct mention→entity mapping, with useful ablation between single- and multi-prototype variants.

- Strength: Simple, effective disambiguator; smoothing stabilizes decisions with sparse/noisy context.

- Strength: Joint embedding space appears mutually consistent across words, entities, and mention-senses, suggesting downstream utility.

- Strength: Clear presentation and a crisp separation between space learning and disambiguation.

- Weakness: Lacks convincing explanation for why MPME/SPME outperform direct mapping—capacity vs. inductive bias vs. data use is unresolved.

- Weakness: Insufficient details about the sense inventory (how senses are created, counts per mention, fixed vs. data-driven, mapping to entities).

- Weakness: Unclear what unique signal mention-senses capture beyond entity representations; could be redundant or merely mirror entities.

- Weakness: Overclaims scope as “bridging text and knowledge” while evaluation is narrowly focused on EDL.

- Weakness: Related work and evaluation coverage incomplete; stronger EDL baselines (e.g., TAC KBP) not clearly compared under matched settings.

- Question: How is the number of senses per mention determined and what is the distribution of senses per mention?

- Question: Do mention senses correspond 1–1 with entities, aggregate multiple entities, or allow many–many mappings?

- Question: What supervises mention-sense vectors—context words, linked entity signals, or both—and how are signals weighted?

- Question: Are senses initialized from entity text/anchors or via unsupervised clustering of contexts?

- Question: Can you provide capacity-controlled ablations (wider entity embeddings, entity sub-prototypes) to isolate the sense-layer effect?

- Question: Any analysis of what mention senses capture beyond entities (e.g., sense–entity similarities, examples where sense ≠ entity)?

- Question: Provide details of the language model: vocabulary, context window, smoothing formula, complexity, and sensitivity to the smoothing hyperparameter.

- Question: How are sense selection and candidate entity selection combined, and is there a fallback when a best sense has no mapped candidate?

- Question: Which benchmark(s) are used, how are NIL entities handled, and why are TAC KBP EL baselines omitted or not matched?

- Additional comment: Clarify in Section 5 whether mention senses are surrogates for entities or a distinct representation level.

- Additional comment: Calibrate novelty claims, cite TAC KBP EL work, and clean duplicated/typoed references.

- Additional comment: The smoothing idea is a highlight—consider ablations across context sparsity regimes to showcase its effect.
