- Paper proposes MPME: multi-prototype mention embeddings learned jointly with words and KB entities, plus an LM-based disambiguation for mapping occurrences to prototypes.
- Main claim: multi-prototype mention embeddings improve supervised entity linking over a strong joint text–KB baseline (ALIGN).
- Contribution: efficient language-model-based disambiguation that uses multi-sense mention embeddings at inference.
- Contribution: empirical analysis of a popularity-smoothing parameter showing trade-offs between context and prior frequency in EL.
- Strength: clear empirical gains over a closely related baseline (ALIGN) using similar resources, isolating effect of multi-prototype mention modeling.
- Strength: practical, actionable analysis of popularity smoothing with performance trends across the parameter.
- Strength: sensible extension of multi-sense embeddings to mention strings, with joint optimization encouraging EL-discriminative sense clusters.
- Strength: simple, likely efficient inference method that integrates with standard EL scoring without heavy global inference.
- Strength: both qualitative and quantitative evaluations of embedding quality, beyond task-only metrics.
- Weakness: comparison with ALIGN is confounded by differing hyperparameters (window size, embedding dimension), making attribution uncertain.
- Weakness: graph adjacency N(e_j) is underspecified—unclear if Wikipedia links are directed, symmetrized, weighted, or how categories/redirects are handled.
- Weakness: ambiguous use of “mention” (type-level vs. token-level) and unclear prototype granularity and reusability across documents.
- Weakness: limited positioning versus unsupervised EL and standard collective methods; no direct comparison isolating disambiguation strategy benefits.
- Weakness: missing ablations isolating multi-prototype vs. single-prototype, word–entity vs. entity–entity contexts, and sensitivity to number of prototypes.
- Question: precisely define N(e_j)—which neighbors are included, directed vs. undirected, weights, handling of categories/redirects?
- Question: what is a “mention” in embedding space—are prototypes tied to surface forms (type-level) or document-specific tokens, and how are aliases handled?
- Question: how is the number of prototypes per mention determined (fixed K, nonparametric, clustering), and how sensitive is EL to K and initialization?
- Question: please provide controlled comparisons with ALIGN using matched hyperparameters and/or a grid to show robustness of gains.
- Question: what is the computational cost of the disambiguation step (time per mention) and how does it scale with candidate set size and smoothing?
- Question: what candidate generation is used and is it identical across systems; report coverage (recall@K) to rule out upstream differences?
- Question: do you evaluate with and without supervision to isolate embedding and LM-disambiguation contributions from task-specific training?
- Additional comment: include an ablation with a single-prototype model to quantify the value of multi-prototype learning.
- Additional comment: clarify training data overlap with evaluation (Wikipedia snapshot, anchor coverage) and potential test leakage via popularity stats.
- Additional comment: consider reporting standard collective EL baselines under the same candidate generator to better position the method beyond ALIGN.
- Minor: references list contains duplicates and typos—clean for camera-ready.
