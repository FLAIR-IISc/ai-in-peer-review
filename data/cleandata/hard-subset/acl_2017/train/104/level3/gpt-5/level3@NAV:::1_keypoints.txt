- Proposal: Multi-prototype mention embedding model that jointly learns word, entity, and multiple mention-sense representations from text and KB signals.  
- Disambiguation: Uses a simple language-model–based mechanism to select a sense for each mention.  
- Results: Reports state-of-the-art performance on supervised entity linking and direct comparison to ALIGN.  
- Analysis: Studies a smoothing/popularity parameter that balances prior popularity and contextual evidence.  
- Primary contribution: Empirical gains over ALIGN on supervised EL with similar inputs, indicating better handling of ambiguous mentions.  
- Secondary contribution: Actionable analysis of how popularity priors affect EL accuracy across mention frequencies.  
- Tertiary contribution: Provides an apples-to-apples baseline setup against ALIGN for clearer attribution of gains.  
- Strength — empirical: Outperforms ALIGN, suggesting multi-prototype mention modeling improves disambiguation for ambiguous mentions.  
- Strength — analysis: Smoothing parameter study clarifies trade-offs between priors and context, useful for tail entities and noisy contexts.  
- Strength — comparative framing: Direct ALIGN comparison reduces confounds and strengthens empirical claims.  
- Strength — practicality: Efficient disambiguation mechanism that integrates naturally with learned mention senses, aiding deployment.  
- Weakness — fairness: ALIGN comparison uses different hyperparameters (context window, vector dims), leaving gains possibly due to capacity/context breadth.  
- Weakness — graph/context ambiguity: N(e_j) is undefined and unclear whether the Wikipedia outlink graph is directed, undirected, or symmetrized, affecting reproducibility.  
- Weakness — terminology: “Mention” is ambiguous—unclear if embeddings represent surface-form types, token-level mentions, or only senses and how they link.  
- Weakness — limited positioning: Hard to assess the LM disambiguation impact without comparisons to unsupervised EL baselines or ablations on disambiguation order.  
- Questions for authors: Provide ALIGN results with matched hyperparameters or sensitivity curves for both methods.  
- Questions for authors: Precisely define N(e_j) and state how the outlink graph is treated (directed/undirected/symmetrized) and its effect.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify what is embedded (surface-form types, token-level mentions, or only mention senses) and how senses map to surface forms.  
- Questions for authors: Compare LM-based disambiguation to standard unsupervised EL baselines or provide ablations on disambiguation order.
