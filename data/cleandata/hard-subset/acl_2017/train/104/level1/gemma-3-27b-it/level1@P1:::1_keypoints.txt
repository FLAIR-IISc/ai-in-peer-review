- Multi-prototype mention embeddings to capture different senses from text and KB.
- Joint modeling of textual context and knowledge base for sense-specific embeddings.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on an entity linking benchmark.

- Clear problem definition and strong motivation for multi-prototype approach.
- Solid experimental evaluation demonstrating effectiveness.
- Effective integration of text and KB information for robust representations.
- Efficient language-model-based disambiguation method.
- Comprehensive related work coverage.

- Core idea of multiple embeddings per mention is not novel relative to prior work.
- Lacks ablation study to quantify each component’s contribution.
- Evaluation on a single dataset limits generalizability claims.
- No discussion of computational cost or scalability.

- How does this model compare directly to prior multi-prototype methods on the same task?
- What are the training/inference costs and how does prototype count affect performance?
- What are the limitations of the KB used and how would the model perform with different KBs?
- Is the disambiguation language model pre-trained or trained jointly with the embeddings?

- References contain duplicates and formatting inconsistencies needing cleanup.
- Abstract is well-written and concise.
- Paper is generally well-organized and easy to follow.
