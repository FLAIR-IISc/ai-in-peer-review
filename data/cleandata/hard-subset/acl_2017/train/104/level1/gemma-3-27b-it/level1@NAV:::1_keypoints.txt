- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention using textual context and a knowledge base, with a language model for disambiguation, achieving state-of-the-art entity linking results.  
- Main contribution: applying multi-prototype embeddings to jointly model textual contexts and KB entities for mention disambiguation.  
- Secondary contribution: a language model–based disambiguation method, though its novelty is not fully clarified.  
- Empirical strength: strong SOTA performance on a standard entity linking benchmark, demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Methodological strength: joint modeling of text and KB improves handling of mention ambiguity versus treating sources separately.  
- Presentation strength: paper is generally well-written and clearly structured.  
- Novelty concern: multi-prototype idea is not entirely new; prior multi-sense embedding work (e.g., Huang et al. 2012; Reisinger & Mooney 2010; Tian et al. 2014) requires clearer differentiation.  
- Disambiguation detail gap: the language model’s architecture, training procedure, and distinctions from existing disambiguation techniques are underspecified.  
- Reference issues: bibliography is messy with repetitions and formatting inconsistencies, detracting from professionalism.  
- Question: provide a detailed comparison of the LM-based disambiguation with graph-ranking and deep-learning baselines.  
- Question: how does the approach handle cold-start / KB-sparse or unseen entities?  
- Question: clarify computational complexity of training and inference compared to single-embedding approaches.
