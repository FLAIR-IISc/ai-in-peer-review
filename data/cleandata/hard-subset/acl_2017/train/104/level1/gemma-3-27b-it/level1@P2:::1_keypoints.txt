- Contribution: Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and KB entities, offering a reasonable extension of prior multi-prototype ideas.
- Contribution: Introduces a language-model-based disambiguation component to select the appropriate sense embedding for a mention.
- Contribution: Achieves state-of-the-art results on a standard entity linking benchmark, demonstrating practical effectiveness.

- Strength: Clearly states the problem of ambiguity in unified semantic spaces and motivates multi-sense representations.
- Strength: Solid experimental evaluation on a standard benchmark with results surpassing existing systems.
- Strength: Well-structured and clearly written presentation with understandable technical details.
- Strength: Provides a comprehensive and relevant overview of related work.

- Weakness: Multi-prototype idea lacks novelty; paper does not clearly delineate specific innovations beyond joint KB modeling compared to prior work.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail about the disambiguation language model (architecture, training, features, and advantages).
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of multi-prototype embeddings, joint KB modeling, and the disambiguation LM.
- Weakness: Reference list contains formatting errors and repetitions, reducing professionalism.
