- Summary: Introduces Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings (MPME) to learn multiple sense vectors per surface mention by jointly modeling text and KB entities, with an LM-based disambiguator and EL evaluation claiming SOTA.
- Contribution 1: Multi-prototype embedding model for mention strings jointly learned with word and entity embeddings, tying senses to KB entities.
- Contribution 2: Efficient LM-style disambiguation mechanism that selects mention-sense from context at inference.
- Contribution 3: Empirical study showing MPME improves intrinsic embeddings and yields competitive/state-of-the-art EL results.

- Strength 1: Targets the core EL ambiguity by modeling multiple senses for mentions rather than only words or entities.
- Strength 2: Joint text–KB training plausibly improves compatibility between context words and candidate entities.
- Strength 3: Practical, efficient disambiguation approach that avoids heavy global inference and integrates with candidate pipelines.
- Strength 4: Reported EL gains on a standard dataset suggest practical applicability beyond intrinsic evaluation.
- Strength 5: Potentially low-supervision training using hyperlinks/anchors and KB alignments, making it scalable.

- Weakness 1: Presentation is messy with formatting issues and truncated/missing methodological details, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness 2: Unclear how many prototypes per mention, how senses are induced, and whether assignments are hard/soft or fixed/adaptive.
- Weakness 3: Novelty is incremental relative to prior multi-sense and joint word–entity embedding work; stronger positioning needed.
- Weakness 4: Evaluation lacks clarity on datasets, metrics, and strong baselines, and misses necessary ablations to attribute gains.
- Weakness 5: Purely local LM-based disambiguation may fail where global coherence matters; no comparison to collective models shown.
- Weakness 6: Runtime, memory, and scalability costs for training and inference are not reported.

- Question: What is the exact training objective and optimization procedure (e.g., skip-gram/negative sampling variants and how entities are incorporated)?
- Question: How many prototypes per mention are used (fixed K or adaptive), how are contexts assigned to prototypes, and what prevents sense proliferation?
- Question: What is the precise LM scoring function at inference and how are candidates enumerated?
- Question: Which EL benchmarks, metrics, and baselines were used, and are candidate generation/mention detection standardized?
- Question: Are there ablations isolating multi-prototypes vs single vector, text+KB vs text-only, and LM disambiguation vs cosine/classifier?
- Question: What are training time, inference latency per mention, and memory footprint as a function of vocab and prototypes?

- Additional comment: Reference list needs cleaning (duplicates/corruptions) and proper citations for compared methods.
- Additional comment: Include qualitative examples of different sense prototypes and nearest neighbors to support interpretability claims.

- Overall recommendation: Interesting and practically relevant idea but currently underspecified with incomplete evaluation; lean towards rejection pending substantial revisions and clearer experiments.
