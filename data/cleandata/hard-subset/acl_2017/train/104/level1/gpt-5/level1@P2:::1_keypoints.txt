- Summary: Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings (MPME) that learn multiple sense-specific vectors per surface form by jointly training on textual contexts and KB-linked entities, plus a fast LM-based disambiguator for downstream use.  
- Contribution 1: Joint framework inducing multiple sense embeddings per mention by integrating word context signals with KB entity supervision.  
- Contribution 2: A lightweight language-model-style disambiguation method that assigns contexts to mention senses for inference.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical study on entity linking claiming strong/SOTA performance with qualitative analyses of learned embeddings.  

- Strength: Tackles mention-level ambiguity in joint text–KB embeddings by separating surface forms from entities and giving mentions multiple sense vectors.  
- Strength: Joint text+KB supervision likely yields better sense separation than text-only multi-sense models, helping disambiguation in hard cases.  
- Strength: Reported empirical gains on EL datasets suggest practical impact if SOTA claims hold.  
- Strength: The proposed disambiguator is computationally light, which is appealing for latency-sensitive EL.  
- Strength: Qualitative analyses (visualizations/nearest neighbors) improve interpretability of learned senses.

- Weakness: Novelty seems incremental—extends well-known multi-prototype and joint text–KB ideas to mentions without a clearly substantial algorithmic advance.  
- Weakness: The LM-based disambiguation is under-specified (model form, priors, joint vs post-hoc training unclear).  
- Weakness: Evaluation may lack comparisons to strong contemporaneous EL baselines and clear reporting of datasets, splits, and metrics.  
- Weakness: Missing key ablations (single vs multi-prototype, text-only vs text+KB, number of prototypes, LM vs simple scoring) to isolate contributions.  
- Weakness: Presentation and references are messy/duplicated, raising reproducibility and clarity concerns.

- Q: How are mention senses parameterized (fixed K, nonparametric, gating) and how do you prevent proliferation for rare mentions?  
- Q: What is the exact training objective (terms for word/mention/entity, negative sampling/NCE, use of KG relations)?  
- Q: Precisely define the LM-based scoring (context window, word weighting, priors) and whether disambiguation is joint or post-hoc.  
- Q: How are candidate entities generated and what is candidate recall vs final accuracy—could gains stem from better candidates?  
- Q: Which datasets/splits/metrics are used (AIDA-CoNLL, TAC, etc.), and do you use global coherence or only local disambiguation?  
- Q: Please provide ablations for single vs multi-prototype, text-only vs text+KB, and sensitivity to number of prototypes and mention frequency.  
- Q: Report training/inference time and memory compared to baselines as a function of senses per mention.

- Overall: Important and promising idea but currently borderline due to moderate novelty, missing methodological/detail clarity, insufficient baselines/ablations, and presentation issues.
