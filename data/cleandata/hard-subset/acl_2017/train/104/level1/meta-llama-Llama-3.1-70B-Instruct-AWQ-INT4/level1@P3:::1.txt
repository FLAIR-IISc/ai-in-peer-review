**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model to address the ambiguity of entity mentions in a unified semantic space. The MPME model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. An efficient language model-based approach is also designed to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The authors evaluate their model on entity linking tasks and achieve state-of-the-art results on a benchmark dataset.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding Model**: The MPME model is a novel approach to address the ambiguity of entity mentions in a unified semantic space. The model's ability to learn multiple sense embeddings for each mention is a significant contribution.
2. **Efficient Language Model-Based Disambiguation Approach**: The authors' approach to disambiguating each mention to a specific sense using a language model is efficient and effective.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results on Entity Linking Tasks**: The authors' evaluation on entity linking tasks demonstrates the high quality of the MPME model and its disambiguation approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel Approach to Addressing Ambiguity**: The MPME model is a novel approach to addressing the ambiguity of entity mentions in a unified semantic space. The model's ability to learn multiple sense embeddings for each mention is a significant contribution.
2. **Effective Disambiguation Approach**: The authors' approach to disambiguating each mention to a specific sense using a language model is efficient and effective.
3. **Strong Evaluation Results**: The authors' evaluation on entity linking tasks demonstrates the high quality of the MPME model and its disambiguation approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Over-Reliance on Knowledge Base**: The MPME model relies heavily on a knowledge base to derive entities. This may limit the model's applicability to domains where a knowledge base is not available.
2. **Limited Analysis of Model Parameters**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the model's parameters and their impact on the results.
3. **Comparison to Baseline Models**: The authors do not provide a detailed comparison of their model to baseline models, making it difficult to assess the model's performance in relation to existing approaches.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the MPME model handles cases where the knowledge base is incomplete or inaccurate?
2. How do you plan to extend the MPME model to handle more complex entity disambiguation tasks, such as those involving multiple entities and relationships?
3. Can you provide more analysis on the impact of the model's parameters on the results, such as the number of sense embeddings and the choice of language model?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the MPME model and its disambiguation approach. However, the paper could benefit from more analysis and discussion of the model's limitations and potential extensions.
