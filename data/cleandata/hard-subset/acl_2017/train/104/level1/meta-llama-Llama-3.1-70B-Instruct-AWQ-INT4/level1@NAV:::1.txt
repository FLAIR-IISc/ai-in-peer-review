**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates text and knowledge into a unified semantic space to address the ambiguity of entity mentions. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. Additionally, the authors design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The proposed method is evaluated on the benchmark dataset for entity linking and achieves state-of-the-art results.

**Contributions**

1. **Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model**: The authors propose a novel model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **Efficient language model-based disambiguation approach**: The authors design an efficient approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense, which is a crucial step in entity linking.
3. **State-of-the-art results on entity linking**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method by achieving state-of-the-art results on the benchmark dataset for entity linking.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to entity mention disambiguation**: The authors propose a novel approach to entity mention disambiguation, which is a challenging task in natural language processing.
2. **Effective use of knowledge base**: The authors effectively utilize a knowledge base to derive entities and learn multiple sense embeddings for each mention.
3. **State-of-the-art results**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed method by achieving state-of-the-art results on the benchmark dataset for entity linking.
4. **Well-written and organized paper**: The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed method and its contributions.
5. **Thorough evaluation**: The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their proposed method, including qualitative and quantitative analysis.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited discussion on model complexity**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion on the complexity of their proposed model, which could be a limitation for practical applications.
2. **No comparison with other disambiguation approaches**: The authors do not compare their proposed disambiguation approach with other existing approaches, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of its effectiveness.
3. **No discussion on handling out-of-vocabulary words**: The authors do not discuss how their proposed model handles out-of-vocabulary words, which could be a limitation for real-world applications.
