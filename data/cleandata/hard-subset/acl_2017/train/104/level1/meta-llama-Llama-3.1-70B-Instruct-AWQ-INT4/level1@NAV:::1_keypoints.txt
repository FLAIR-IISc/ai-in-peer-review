- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly maps textual context and knowledge-base entities into a unified space to model mention ambiguity and disambiguation.  
- Learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by combining words from context and KB-derived entities.  
- Designs an efficient language model–based method to assign each mention to a specific sense.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on a benchmark entity linking dataset.  
- Novel approach to entity mention disambiguation addressing mention ambiguity with multi-prototype embeddings.  
- Effectively leverages a knowledge base to derive entities and inform sense embedding learning.  
- Achieves strong empirical results (SOTA) validating the method’s effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, facilitating understanding of the method and experiments.  
- Provides thorough evaluation including both qualitative and quantitative analyses.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of model complexity and computational cost for practical deployment.  
- No comparison of the proposed disambiguation approach with alternative existing disambiguation methods.  
- Does not address how out-of-vocabulary words are handled, limiting assessment for real-world use.  
- How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary words and what are the real-world implications?  
- Please provide detailed analysis of model complexity and its practical implications.  
- How does the disambiguation approach compare empirically and conceptually with existing methods, including pros and cons?
