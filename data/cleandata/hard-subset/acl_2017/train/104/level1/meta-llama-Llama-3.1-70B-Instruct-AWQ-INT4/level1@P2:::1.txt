**Summary and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model to deal with the ambiguity of entity mentions in a unified semantic space. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. An efficient language model-based approach is also designed to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The contributions of this work can be summarized as follows:

1. **Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model**: The proposed model is a significant contribution, as it addresses the ambiguity of entity mentions in a unified semantic space. The model's ability to learn multiple sense embeddings for each mention is a novel approach to entity disambiguation.
2. **Efficient language model-based disambiguation approach**: The designed approach is a valuable contribution, as it provides an efficient way to disambiguate entity mentions in text.
3. **State-of-the-art results on entity linking task**: The paper reports state-of-the-art results on the entity linking task using the proposed model and disambiguation approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to entity disambiguation**: The proposed Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model is a novel approach to entity disambiguation, which addresses the ambiguity of entity mentions in a unified semantic space.
2. **Efficient disambiguation approach**: The designed language model-based disambiguation approach is efficient and provides a valuable contribution to the field of entity disambiguation.
3. **State-of-the-art results**: The paper reports state-of-the-art results on the entity linking task, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed model and disambiguation approach.
4. **Well-written and clear presentation**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear presentation of their proposed model, disambiguation approach, and experimental results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of theoretical analysis**: The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the proposed model and disambiguation approach. A more in-depth analysis of the model's properties and limitations would strengthen the paper.
2. **Limited evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the proposed model and disambiguation approach on the entity linking task. A more comprehensive evaluation on other tasks, such as question answering or text classification, would provide a more complete picture of the model's effectiveness.
3. **Dependence on knowledge base**: The proposed model relies heavily on a knowledge base, which may not always be available or up-to-date. A discussion on how to handle cases where the knowledge base is incomplete or outdated would be beneficial.
4. **Comparison to other models**: The paper only compares the proposed model to a few baseline models. A more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art models would provide a better understanding of the model's strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions to Authors**
