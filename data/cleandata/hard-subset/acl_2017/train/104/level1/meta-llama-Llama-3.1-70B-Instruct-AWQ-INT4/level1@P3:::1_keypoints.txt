- Proposes MPME, a model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities, with an LM-based disambiguation, showing SOTA on an entity linking benchmark.  
- Contribution: Introduces the Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model to capture mention ambiguity via multiple sense embeddings.  
- Contribution: Presents an efficient language model–based method to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on entity linking tasks.  
- Strength: Novel approach that explicitly models mention ambiguity in a unified semantic space.  
- Strength: Effective and efficient disambiguation using a language model.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results on benchmark entity linking evaluations.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on a knowledge base, limiting applicability in KB-poor domains.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of model hyperparameters and their effects.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to baseline models, hindering relative assessment.  
- Question: How does MPME handle incomplete or inaccurate knowledge bases?  
- Question: How will MPME be extended to handle more complex disambiguation involving multiple entities and relations?  
- Question: Can you provide analysis on the impact of parameters like number of sense embeddings and choice of language model?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but would benefit from more analysis of limitations and extensions.
