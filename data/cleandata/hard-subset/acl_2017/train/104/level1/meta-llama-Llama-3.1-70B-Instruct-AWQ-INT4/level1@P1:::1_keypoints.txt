Summary: Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding that jointly models textual contexts and KB entities to learn multiple sense embeddings and uses an LM-based disambiguation, evaluated on entity linking with strong results.

Main contribution 1: Introduces a novel multi-prototype mention embedding model that handles mention ambiguity by jointly modeling words and knowledge-base entities.

Main contribution 2: Designs an efficient language model–based approach to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.

Main contribution 3: Reports state-of-the-art performance on benchmark entity linking datasets.

Strength 1: Novel approach addressing ambiguity in a common text–knowledge embedding space.

Strength 2: Demonstrates effectiveness via strong empirical results on entity linking.

Strength 3: Clear and well-organized presentation of the method.

Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to entity linking, raising questions about generalizability.

Weakness 2: No comparison to other disambiguation approaches, making relative effectiveness unclear.

Weakness 3: Heavy focus on technical details may reduce accessibility for non-expert readers.

Question 1: How were model hyperparameters selected?

Question 2: How can the approach be extended to tasks beyond entity linking?

Question 3: Why is the LM-based disambiguation more effective than alternative disambiguation methods?
