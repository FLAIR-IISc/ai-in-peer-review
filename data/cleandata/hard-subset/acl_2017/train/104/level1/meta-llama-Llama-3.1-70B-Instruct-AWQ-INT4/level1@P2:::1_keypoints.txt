- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention to resolve entity ambiguity in a unified semantic space.
- Designs an efficient language model–based disambiguation method to map mentions to specific senses.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on the entity linking task.

- Novel multi-prototype approach effectively addresses mention ambiguity.
- Efficient LM-based disambiguation is practical and valuable.
- Empirical results demonstrate SOTA performance on entity linking.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.

- Lacks theoretical analysis of the model’s properties and limitations.
- Evaluation is limited to entity linking and omits other tasks (e.g., QA, text classification).
- Relies heavily on a knowledge base without addressing incompleteness or staleness.
- Compares to only a few baselines rather than a broad set of SOTA models.
