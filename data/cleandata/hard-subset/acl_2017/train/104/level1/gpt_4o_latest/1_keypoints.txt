- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and knowledge-base entities.  
- Introduces a language model–based disambiguation method to assign mentions to specific senses.  
- Reports state-of-the-art empirical performance on entity linking benchmarks with qualitative and quantitative analyses.  
- Novelty: multi-prototype embeddings effectively capture mention polysemy and integrate textual and knowledge contexts.  
- Strong empirical results: thorough evaluation demonstrating practical effectiveness on benchmarks.  
- Relevance: tackles an important problem in NLU and knowledge representation.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written with clear methodology and experimental setup.  
- Weakness: disambiguation method appears to be a straightforward application of existing language-modeling techniques with limited novelty.  
- Weakness: potential scalability issues due to computing multiple embeddings per mention for large-scale datasets/KBs.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of model components (multi-prototype vs disambiguation).  
- Question: how does the model handle entities lacking sufficient KB information?  
- Question: can scalability be improved for large-scale applications and were any optimizations considered?  
- Question: how were hyperparameters for the multi-prototype embeddings selected?
