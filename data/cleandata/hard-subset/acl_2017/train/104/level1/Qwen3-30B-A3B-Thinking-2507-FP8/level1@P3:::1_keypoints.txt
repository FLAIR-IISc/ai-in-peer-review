- Paper proposes MPME: multi-prototype mention embeddings learned via joint text–KB modeling, plus an LM-based disambiguation, claiming SOTA entity linking.  
- Multi-prototype embedding idea is not novel; it closely builds on Huang et al. (2012), Neelakantan et al. (2014), Mancini et al. (2016).  
- SOTA claim is weakly supported due to missing comparisons to key baselines (e.g., Hoffart et al. 2011, Kulkarni et al. 2009) and no statistical significance.  
- The “efficient language model” for disambiguation is vague, likely standard, and lacks demonstrated technical novelty.  
- Strength: clear framing of mention ambiguity as a core text–KB integration challenge, consistent with prior work.  
- Strength: qualitative analyses (nearest-neighbor/sense clusters) show MPME captures semantic distinctions (e.g., “Apple” company vs. fruit).  
- Strength: integration of MPME with a simple LM is a practical disambiguation pipeline that may help downstream tasks.  
- Major weakness: severe lack of novelty—methodology largely derives from prior multi-prototype and joint modeling work without meaningful extension.  
- Major weakness: unsupported SOTA claim—missing comparisons to strong and recent baselines and absent uncertainty reporting.  
- Major weakness: reference list contains many typos, repeated/corrupted entries, and omitted key citations (e.g., Huang et al. 2012), undermining credibility.  
- Major weakness: methodological details about the proposed LM are insufficient, preventing proper evaluation and replication.  
- Major weakness: claims about high-quality embeddings are overstated without validation on downstream NLP tasks.  
- Questions to authors: Why was Huang et al. (2012) omitted and how does MPME extend it?  
- Questions to authors: Please fix the corrupted reference list and confirm whether errors are submission issues.  
- Questions to authors: What specific baselines were used to support the SOTA claim, and why were classic/strong baselines omitted?  
- Overall recommendation: Reject—lack of novelty, critical reference errors, and unsubstantiated claims outweigh the empirical embedding validation.
