- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) to model multiple sense embeddings for entity mentions by jointly using textual context and KB entities.
- Claims empirical improvement in entity linking using the proposed embeddings and an efficient disambiguation language model.
- Presents qualitative and quantitative analyses showing high-quality word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings.

- Addresses the important problem of entity mention ambiguity with clear motivation and practical relevance.
- Reports state-of-the-art improvements on entity linking benchmarks, showing effectiveness of the method.
- Joint modeling of textual context and knowledge base entities with thorough qualitative analysis supports representation quality.

- Novelty is questionable: approach appears to be a straightforward extension of existing multi-prototype embedding work without clearly articulated differences.
- Lacks adequate comparison with recent entity disambiguation methods (e.g., Huang et al., 2015; Hoffart et al., 2011), making the state-of-the-art claim hard to assess.
- Evaluation is limited to entity linking and does not demonstrate broader applicability to other NLP tasks (e.g., relation extraction, QA).
- Paper exhibits poor quality control with duplicated and misformatted references, undermining professionalism.

- How does MPME differ technically from prior multi-prototype word embedding methods and what novel techniques address entity-mention-specific challenges?
- How does the method compare quantitatively and qualitatively to recent KB- and language-model-based disambiguation approaches (e.g., Huang et al., 2015; Hoffart et al., 2011)?
- Can the authors demonstrate or provide examples of how these embeddings would improve other NLP tasks such as relation extraction or question answering?
