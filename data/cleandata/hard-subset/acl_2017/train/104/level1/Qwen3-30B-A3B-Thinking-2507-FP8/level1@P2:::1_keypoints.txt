- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings for mentions by jointly modeling textual context and knowledge-base entities.  
- Introduces an efficient language-model–based approach for disambiguating mentions to specific senses and claims state-of-the-art entity linking results.

- Applies multi-prototype embedding techniques specifically to entity mentions to address mention ambiguity.  
- Jointly models textual context and KB entities to produce richer mention embeddings.

- Addresses an important problem (entity ambiguity) relevant to entity linking and KB population.  
- Provides empirical validation on a standard entity linking benchmark reporting strong performance.  
- Joint modeling of text and KB is a reasonable idea likely to capture nuanced semantics beyond text-only or KB-only methods.

- Fails to clearly distinguish novelty from prior multi-prototype embedding work (e.g., Huang et al. 2012; Neelakantan et al. 2014).  
- Provides insufficient comparison and specific metrics against recent state-of-the-art methods to substantiate the SOTA claim.  
- References contain numerous typos/inconsistencies, indicating poor proofreading.  
- Lacks technical details about the claimed “efficient” language-model disambiguation, making efficiency and novelty unclear.

- How specifically does your multi-prototype mention method differ from prior multi-prototype embedding approaches, and what innovations target mentions?  
- Please provide exact performance metrics and comparisons to recent ACL/EMNLP (2016) state-of-the-art entity linking systems.  
- Clarify how the proposed language-model–based disambiguation differs from standard LM techniques and why it is more efficient.

- Overall: relevant problem and reasonable approach but insufficient novelty, comparisons, and manuscript quality; recommend rejection unless revised to clarify novelty, add thorough comparisons, and fix presentation.
