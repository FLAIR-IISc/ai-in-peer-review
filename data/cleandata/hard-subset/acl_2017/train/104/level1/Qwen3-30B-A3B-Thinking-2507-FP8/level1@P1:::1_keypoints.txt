- Proposes MPME: multi-prototype mention embeddings that jointly model textual context and KB entities for sense-specific mention embeddings and disambiguation.  
- Claims state-of-the-art entity linking results using the learned embeddings and an efficient language-model disambiguation.  
- Contribution: applies multi-prototype embeddings to entity mentions with joint text-knowledge modeling.  
- Contribution: provides qualitative and quantitative validation of word, entity, and multi-prototype embeddings.  
- Contribution: introduces an efficient disambiguation method suitable for scalable entity linking.  
- Core method lacks novelty: multi-prototype learning closely follows Huang et al. (2012) and Neelakantan et al. (2014).  
- Paper fails to clearly differentiate its entity-focused approach from prior multi-prototype work.  
- Inadequate baselines: omits direct comparisons to Huang (2012) and Neelakantan (2014), undermining the SOTA claim.  
- Evaluation is rigorous with qualitative analyses, quantitative benchmarks, and ablation studies supporting embedding quality.  
- Practical efficiency is emphasized, claiming a scalable alternative to expensive graph-based disambiguation.  
- Writing and references are poor: duplicated/corrupted citations and grammatical errors indicate lack of rigor.  
- Requests clarification on how the method specifically differs from Huang (2012) and how entity mentions pose unique challenges.  
- Asks authors to include missing baselines (Huang, Neelakantan) to substantiate SOTA claims.  
- Notes missing statistical significance tests and lacking details on computational/inference costs.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak Reject, pending clearer methodological distinction, complete baselines, and correction of reference/writing errors.
