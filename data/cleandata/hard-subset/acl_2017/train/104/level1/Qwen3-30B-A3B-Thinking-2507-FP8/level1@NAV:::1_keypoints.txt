- Paper proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding for mention ambiguity and a disambiguation module for entity linking, claiming three contributions including SOTA results.  
- Core methodological claims are undermined: the multi-prototype approach closely resembles prior work (e.g., Neelakantan et al., 2014; Chen et al., 2014) with no clear distinction.  
- The disambiguation method lacks novelty, appearing similar to standard contextual classification.  
- Empirical results partially hold: reported SOTA entity linking improvements, but presented without sufficient detail.  
- Strength: empirical validation shows improved downstream entity linking on benchmarks (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL).  
- Strength: clear task application—focus on entity linking provides a concrete evaluation context.  
- Strength: method is straightforward to implement, offering good replicability potential.  
- Weakness: severe reference corruption (misspellings, duplicates, wrong author names) indicating poor academic rigor.  
- Weakness: lack of novelty and no ablation studies or direct comparisons to established multi-sense embedding methods.  
- Weakness: unsubstantiated "state-of-the-art" claims—missing precise metrics, baselines, and statistical significance.  
- Question: How do the proposed embeddings differ quantitatively from Neelakantan et al. (2014) or Chen et al. (2014)?  
- Request: please correct and verify the reference list and provide exact performance metrics (e.g., F1 on AIDA-CoNLL) against prior SOTA methods.
