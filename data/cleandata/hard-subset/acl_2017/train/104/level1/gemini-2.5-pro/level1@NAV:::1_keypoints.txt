- Paper tackles mention ambiguity by proposing Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings that learn multiple sense-specific vectors via joint text and KB training, plus a language-model-based disambiguation; evaluated on entity linking with claimed SOTA.  
- Contribution: novel method to learn multi-prototype embeddings by jointly leveraging unstructured text and a structured knowledge base.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration of effectiveness with claimed state-of-the-art results on a standard entity linking benchmark.  
- Strength: Addresses an important, widely-recognized problem (mention ambiguity) relevant to entity linking and relation extraction.  
- Strength: Approach is intuitive and technically plausible, extending multi-prototype embeddings to a knowledge-aware setting.  
- Strength: Reported SOTA results provide practical evidence of the method’s effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Novelty is unclear—multi-prototype embeddings and joint text+KB embedding exist in prior work and the paper must better situate its technical novelty.  
- Weakness: Abstract and description are vague about model architecture, objective function, and the LM-based disambiguation mechanism, hindering assessment of technical contribution.  
- Weakness: Manuscript quality issues (duplicated, typo-ridden, badly formatted bibliography) raise concerns about care in experiments and analysis.  
- Question: Authors should clarify the specific architectural novelty and how the joint objective meaningfully differs from applying existing multi-prototype methods to standard text+KB embeddings.  
- Question: Can the authors confirm they will correct the bibliography and manuscript formatting in the final version?
