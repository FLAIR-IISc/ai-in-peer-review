- Addresses mention ambiguity by learning multiple sense-specific embeddings for ambiguous mentions jointly from text and KB structure.  
- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that induces sense embeddings using both textual context (skip-gram style) and KB relational signals.  
- Introduces a language-model–based disambiguation method at inference to select the appropriate sense for a mention in context.  
- Evaluated intrinsically and extrinsically (entity linking) and claims state-of-the-art results on a standard benchmark.

- Novel joint model that learns multiple prototypes per mention by combining unstructured text and structured KB signals.  
- Contextual disambiguation mechanism using a language-model approach to pick the correct prototype at inference.  
- Empirical claim of state-of-the-art performance on entity linking, demonstrating practical benefit.

- Tackles a timely and important limitation of joint text-KB embeddings by modeling mention ambiguity.  
- Technically sound approach combining local textual co-occurrence and global KB relational information.  
- Strong empirical results validating the method on a downstream entity linking task.  
- Comprehensive evaluation plan including qualitative and quantitative analyses.

- Need clearer distinction of novelty versus simple pipeline alternatives (e.g., existing multi-prototype embeddings + KB alignment).  
- Disambiguation component is vaguely described—architecture, training regime, and efficiency are unclear.  
- Scalability concerns: training many prototypes for millions of mentions may be computationally expensive and is not addressed.  
- Presentation issues: references contain many typographical errors and garbled text, suggesting poor proofreading.

- Please clarify the exact objective function and how textual and KB losses are combined to separate prototypes.  
- Provide detailed description of the language-model–based disambiguation: architecture, training (joint or separate), and computational cost relative to prototypes/context size.  
- How is the number of prototypes per mention determined—global hyperparameter, KB-based per-mention setting, or learned non-parametrically?
