- Problem and approach: addresses mention ambiguity by learning multi-prototype mention embeddings that integrate textual context with KB entity information.
- Disambiguation method: introduces a language model–based disambiguation procedure that selects the context-appropriate prototype.
- Empirical claim: demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on an entity linking benchmark using the learned embeddings and disambiguation method.
- Contribution 1: novel multi-prototype embedding model that uses KB entities to guide sense induction for mentions.
- Contribution 2: efficient LM-based mention disambiguation leveraging the learned prototypes.
- Contribution 3: empirical validation with quantitative and qualitative analyses showing strong EL results.
- Strength 1: well-motivated and novel extension of multi-prototype embeddings by incorporating KB guidance for clustering.
- Strength 2: technically sound joint learning framework where textual context and KB structure reinforce each other.
- Strength 3: strong empirical results with SOTA claims and sensible downstream evaluation (entity linking).
- Weakness 1: unclear technical details of sense induction and how mention instances are assigned to prototypes during training.
- Weakness 2: unclear handling of the number of prototypes per mention and lack of sensitivity/scalability analysis.
- Weakness 3: insufficient comparison to strong pipeline-based entity linking baselines to isolate the benefit of joint learning.
- Question 1: please clarify the training objective, prototype assignment mechanism, and integration of textual and KB losses.
- Question 2: how is the number of prototypes chosen (global K vs. per-mention) and how sensitive is performance to this choice?
- Question 3: do baselines include state-of-the-art pipeline EL systems (disambiguate first, then learn embeddings) for fair comparison?
