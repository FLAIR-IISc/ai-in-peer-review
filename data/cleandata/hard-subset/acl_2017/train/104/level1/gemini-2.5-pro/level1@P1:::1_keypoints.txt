- Paper summary: Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense vectors per mention by jointly modeling text and a KB, plus an LM-based disambiguation method, evaluated intrinsically and on entity linking with claimed SOTA results.

- Contribution 1: Introduces multi-prototype mention embeddings extending multi-sense word embeddings to joint text–KB representation learning.

- Contribution 2: Presents a contextual disambiguation method to select the appropriate prototype for a mention in context.

- Contribution 3: Reports strong empirical results, claiming state-of-the-art performance on an entity linking benchmark.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated and conceptually novel synthesis of multi-sense embeddings and joint text–KB modeling to handle mention ambiguity.

- Strength 2: Complete end-to-end system that includes both representation learning and an efficient disambiguation component.

- Strength 3: Empirical validation via top results on a competitive entity linking task, demonstrating practical effectiveness.

- Weakness 1: Core model details are unclear—architecture, exact training objective, and how text and KB signals are combined are not specified.

- Weakness 2: Disambiguation method is vague—unspecified whether it’s a post-processing or integrated step and how the LM scores prototypes.

- Weakness 3: Possible lack of critical analyses—no clear ablation against a single-prototype baseline or detailed qualitative mapping of prototypes to interpretable senses.

- Question 1: What is the concrete joint objective formulation and how are text and KB losses balanced?

- Question 2: How is the number of prototypes per mention determined—fixed, KB-derived, or learned non-parametrically?

- Question 3: Please detail the LM-based disambiguation: input context window, scoring function, and integration with training/inference.
