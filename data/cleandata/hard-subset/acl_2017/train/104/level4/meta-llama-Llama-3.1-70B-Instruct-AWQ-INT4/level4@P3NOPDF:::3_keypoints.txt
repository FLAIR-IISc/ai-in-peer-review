- Proposes a neural method that integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling in a single language-modeling framework with a simple training procedure and broad applicability.  
- Paper is formally well-structured but the discussion lags behind the technical ideas in places and needs improvement.  
- Strengths: good ideas, straightforward neural learning approach, and respectable (though not outstanding) empirical performance.  
- Method has notable potential for application across multiple tasks.  
- Novelty is limited: the work appears primarily an extension of Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016) and needs clearer articulation of new contributions.  
- Clarity issues in Section 3 that require more explanation and elaboration.  
- Figure 2 and related terminology are insufficiently explained—e.g., the difference between “dictionary of mentions” and “dictionary of entity-mention pairs,” and their relation to “text anchors” and “types for annotated text anchors.”  
- Evaluation is narrow: MPME is only tested on entity linking/disambiguation; authors should show that the produced word embeddings are competitive on non-entity semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) involving mentions.  
- Authors should explicitly address these concerns in the rebuttal to improve the paper’s clarity, novelty claims, and empirical validation.
