**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates text and knowledge into a unified semantic space. The model addresses the ambiguity of entity mentions by learning multiple sense embeddings for each mention, jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. The authors also design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The proposed method is evaluated on the entity linking task, achieving state-of-the-art performance.

**Main Contributions**

Based on the paper, I identify the following main contributions, in order of strength:

1. **Novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model**: The proposed model addresses the ambiguity of entity mentions by learning multiple sense embeddings for each mention, jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base.
2. **Efficient language model-based approach for sense disambiguation**: The authors design an efficient approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense, leveraging the learned mention embeddings.
3. **State-of-the-art performance on entity linking task**: The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art performance on the entity linking task, demonstrating its effectiveness in integrating text and knowledge.

**Strengths**

1. **Outperforms ALIGN in supervised entity linking task**: The proposed framework improves representations of text and knowledge that are learned jointly, outperforming ALIGN in the supervised entity linking task.
2. **Direct comparison with closely related approach**: The authors provide a direct comparison with a closely related approach, using very similar input data.
3. **Analysis of the smoothing parameter**: The analysis of the smoothing parameter provides useful insights, as the impact of popularity is a persistent issue in entity linking.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Comparison with ALIGN could be better**: The comparison with ALIGN could be improved by clarifying the differences in input data, such as content window size and vector dimension. Additionally, it is unclear whether N(ej) includes only entities that link to ej, and how the adjacency is defined in the directed graph.
2. **Ambiguity in terminology**: The paper sometimes uses "mention" to refer to a string type, and sometimes to a particular mention in a particular document. This ambiguity makes it difficult to follow the discussion.
3. **Lack of comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods**: The paper does not provide a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods, making it difficult to determine the impact of sense disambiguation order.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify the differences in input data between the proposed method and ALIGN, and how these differences may impact the learned representations?
2. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of how the adjacency is defined in the directed graph, and how this affects the learned mention embeddings?
3. Can you provide a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods to better understand the impact of sense disambiguation order?
