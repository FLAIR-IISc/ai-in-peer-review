**Summary of the Paper**

This paper addresses the problem of disambiguating/linking textual entity mentions into a given background knowledge base (English Wikipedia). The proposed approach involves an intermediate layer of representation, namely mention senses, which follows two steps: (1) mention to mention sense, and (2) mention sense to entity. Various embedding representations are learned for words, mention senses, and entities, which are jointly trained to maximize a single overall objective function.

**Contributions**

1. **Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model**: The paper proposes a novel MPME model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base.
2. **Efficient language model-based approach**: The authors design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense.
3. **State-of-the-art results on entity linking**: The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art results on the benchmark dataset for entity linking.

**Strengths**

1. **Clear and well-structured approach**: The technical approach is fairly clear and conforms to current deep processing fashion and known best practices regarding embeddings.
2. **Novel MPME model**: The proposed MPME model is a novel contribution that addresses the ambiguity of entity mentions.
3. **Effective use of smoothing parameter**: The idea of the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a positive aspect of the paper.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of explanation for two-step process**: The paper does not explain why a two-step process, involving mention senses, is better than a simple direct one-step mapping from word mentions to entities.
2. **Unclear sense disambiguation process**: The sense disambiguation process using a sense-based profile ('language model') from local context words and neighboring mentions is not clearly explained.
3. **Overemphasis on embedding 'goodness'**: The authors' response to the KBP evaluation not being relevant is not satisfactory, as the only way to evaluate the 'goodness' of embeddings is through an application.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear explanation for why a two-step process, involving mention senses, is better than a simple direct one-step mapping from word mentions to entities?
2. How exactly are the senses created and differentiated? Who defines how many senses a mention string can have?
3. Can you provide more details on the sense disambiguation process using a sense-based profile ('language model') from local context words and neighboring mentions?
4. How do you respond to the criticism that the paper does not provide a clear explanation for the two-step process and the sense disambiguation process?
