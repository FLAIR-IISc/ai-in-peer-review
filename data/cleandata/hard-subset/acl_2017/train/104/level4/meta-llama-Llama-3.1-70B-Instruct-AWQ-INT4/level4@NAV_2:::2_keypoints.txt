- Paper addresses entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, focusing on mapping textual mentions into a KB.  
- Method: two-step process—(1) map mentions to mention senses, (2) map mention senses to entities—via jointly trained word, mention-sense, and entity embeddings.  
- Approach is technically clear and follows modern embedding-based practices with a single joint objective.  
- Unclear why the two-step mention-sense approach is superior to a direct one-step mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN by Yamada et al.).  
- Empirically, MPME (and even SPME) outperform the one-step baseline per Table 2, but the reason for this improvement is not explained.  
- Contribution: novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model learning multiple sense embeddings per mention from text and KB entities.  
- Contribution: an efficient language-model-based disambiguation method to assign mentions to specific senses.  
- Contribution: state-of-the-art entity linking performance using the proposed embeddings and disambiguation.  
- Strength: technically sound, well-motivated approach addressing integration of text and knowledge representations.  
- Strength: strong experimental results demonstrating effectiveness and SOTA performance.  
- Strength: introducing a smoothing parameter to balance different embedding influences is innovative and promising.  
- Weakness: lack of clear justification or analysis for why the two-step sense-based pipeline outperforms direct mapping.  
- Weakness: unsatisfactory response regarding the KBP evaluation’s relevance and how embedding quality is evaluated via application.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on how senses are created/differentiated and how definitional profiles for sense collections are constructed.  
- Questions for authors: explain sense creation/differentiation and sense-definitional-profile construction in detail.  
- Questions for authors: respond to criticism about KBP evaluation relevance and outline more meaningful embedding-evaluation plans.  
- Questions for authors: provide more information on the smoothing parameter and its impact on performance.
