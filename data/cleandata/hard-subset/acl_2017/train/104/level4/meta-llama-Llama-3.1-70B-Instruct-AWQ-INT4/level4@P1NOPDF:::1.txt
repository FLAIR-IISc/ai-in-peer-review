- Strengths:
* The proposed framework outperforms ALIGN in the supervised entity linking task, suggesting that it improves the joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
* The direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar input data is a significant strength of the paper.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter provides valuable insights, as the impact of popularity is a persistent issue in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN could be more comprehensive. Notably, ALIGN employed a content window size of 10, whereas this paper uses a size of 5, and a vector dimension of 500, whereas this paper uses 200. Furthermore, it is unclear whether N(ej) only includes entities that link to ej. Given that the graph is directed and consists of Wikipedia outlinks, it is also unclear whether adjacency is defined as it would be for an undirected graph. In ALIGN, the context of an entity is defined as the set of entities that link to that entity. If N(e_j) is defined differently, it is difficult to determine the impact of this change on the learned vectors, which could contribute to the difference in scores on the entity similarity task.
* The terminology used in the paper can be confusing at times. Specifically, the term "mention" is sometimes unclear, referring to either a string type or a particular mention in a specific document. While the phrase "mention embedding" is used, it appears that embeddings are only learned for mention senses.
* It is challenging to assess the impact of sense disambiguation order without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
