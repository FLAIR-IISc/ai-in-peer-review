- The framework outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting better joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
- A direct comparison to a closely related approach using similar input data provides a useful baseline.
- The smoothing-parameter analysis offers insights into popularity bias in entity linking.
- The ALIGN comparison is not tightly controlled: different window sizes and vector dimensions and an unclear definition of N(e_j) and adjacency hinder interpretation.
- Terminology around "mention" is confusing—it's unclear if it denotes a string type or a document-specific occurrence, and "mention embedding" appears to target only mention senses.
- The impact of sense disambiguation order is unclear because the work lacks comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
