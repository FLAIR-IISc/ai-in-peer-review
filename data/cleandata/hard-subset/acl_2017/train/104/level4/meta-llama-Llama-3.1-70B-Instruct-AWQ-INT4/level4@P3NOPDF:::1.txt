**Review of [Paper Title]**

**Strengths:**

This paper has several notable strengths. Firstly, the proposed framework outperforms the ALIGN approach in the supervised entity linking task, suggesting that the joint learning of text and knowledge representations is effective. Additionally, the authors provide a direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar input data, which facilitates a more accurate assessment of the proposed method's performance. The analysis of the smoothing parameter is also a valuable contribution, as it sheds light on the impact of popularity on entity linking, a persistent issue in this field.

**Weaknesses:**

Despite its strengths, the paper has several weaknesses that need to be addressed. One major concern is the comparison with the ALIGN approach. The authors should provide more details on the differences in experimental settings, such as the content window size (10 vs 5) and vector dimension (500 vs 200). Moreover, it is unclear whether N(ej) includes only entities that link to ej, and how the adjacency is defined in the directed graph of Wikipedia outlinks. In the ALIGN approach, the context of an entity is defined as the set of entities that link to it. If N(ej) is defined differently, it is difficult to determine the impact of this change on the learned vectors, which could contribute to the difference in scores on the entity similarity task.

Furthermore, the terminology used in the paper can be confusing at times. The term "mention" is sometimes unclear, referring to either a string type or a particular mention in a specific document. The phrase "mention embedding" is used, but it appears that embeddings are only learned for mention senses. This inconsistency in terminology may lead to confusion for readers.

Lastly, the impact of sense disambiguation order is difficult to determine without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods. The authors should consider including such a comparison to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their approach.

**General Discussion:**

Overall, this paper proposes a promising approach to entity linking by jointly learning text and knowledge representations. While it has several strengths, including outperforming the ALIGN approach and providing a valuable analysis of the smoothing parameter, it also has some weaknesses that need to be addressed. The authors should provide more details on the experimental settings, clarify the terminology used, and include a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods to strengthen their evaluation. With these improvements, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of entity linking.
