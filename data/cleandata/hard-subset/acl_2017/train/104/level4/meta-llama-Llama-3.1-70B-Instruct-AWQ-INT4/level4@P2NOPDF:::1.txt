**Strengths:**

* The proposed framework outperforms the ALIGN approach in the supervised entity linking task, indicating that the joint learning of text and knowledge representations is effective.
* A direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar input data is provided, allowing for a more accurate assessment of the framework's performance.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter offers valuable insights, as the impact of popularity is a persistent issue in entity linking tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

* The comparison with ALIGN could be improved. Notably, the ALIGN approach used a content window size of 10, whereas this paper uses a size of 5, and a vector dimension of 500, whereas this paper uses 200. Furthermore, it is unclear whether N(ej) only includes entities that link to ej. Given that the graph is directed and consists of Wikipedia outlinks, it is also unclear whether adjacency is defined as it would be for an undirected graph. In the ALIGN approach, the context of an entity is defined as the set of entities that link to that entity. If N(ej) is defined differently, it is difficult to determine the impact of this change on the learned vectors, which could contribute to the difference in scores on the entity similarity task.
* The terminology used in the paper can be confusing at times. Specifically, the term "mention" is sometimes unclear, as it is not always evident whether it refers to a string type or a particular mention in a specific document. Additionally, the phrase "mention embedding" is used, but it appears that embeddings are only learned for mention senses.
* The impact of sense disambiguation order is not clearly evaluated, as there is no comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods. This makes it challenging to determine the effectiveness of the proposed approach in this regard.

**General Discussion:**

The paper presents a promising approach to entity linking, with a joint learning framework that improves representations of text and knowledge. However, there are some areas that require clarification and improvement. A more detailed comparison with existing approaches, such as ALIGN, would be beneficial, including a clear explanation of any differences in methodology or parameters. Additionally, the terminology used in the paper could be refined to improve clarity, and further evaluation of the impact of sense disambiguation order would be valuable.
