- Proposes a compelling neural approach for entity disambiguation/linking that jointly models entity, mention, and sense within a unified neural language model.  
- The method is simple, effective, and enables a broad range of applications.  
- The paper is formally clear and supported by good empirical evaluation.  
- The novelty is somewhat limited.  
- Section 3 lacks clarity and some elements in Figure 2 are unexplained.  
- Terminology is redundant/confusing (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs”) and should be streamlined.  
- The paper is similar to Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016) and fails to explicitly highlight key differences.  
- The Multiple Embedding model should be evaluated on semantic tasks not relying on KB entities (parsing, coreference, SRL) to verify MPME word embeddings are competitive with simpler word spaces.  
- With revisions addressing these issues, the paper could make a relevant contribution.
