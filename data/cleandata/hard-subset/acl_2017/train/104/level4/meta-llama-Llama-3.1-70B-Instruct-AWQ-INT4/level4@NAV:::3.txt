**Review**

**Strengths:**
The paper presents a compelling neural learning approach for entity disambiguation and linking, integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling within a unified neural language modeling framework. This simple yet effective method enables a broad range of applications. Additionally, the paper's formal clarity and good empirical evaluation are notable strengths.

**Weaknesses:**
However, the paper's novelty is somewhat limited, and certain sections lack clarity. Specifically, Section 3 requires improvement, with some details in Figure 2 left unexplained. Furthermore, the terminology could be streamlined, as some concepts (e.g., dictionary of mentions, dictionary of entity-mention pairs) seem redundant and warrant clarification.

The paper's similarity to Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016) is also a concern, and the authors should explicitly highlight the differences between the two works. While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed Multiple Embedding model in entity linking/disambiguation tasks, it is essential to evaluate its performance in other semantic tasks that do not directly rely on entities in the knowledge base (KB), such as parsing, coreference, or semantic role labeling. The authors should verify that the word embeddings generated by the proposed MPME method are not inferior to simpler word spaces in these tasks.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting idea, and with some revisions to address the mentioned weaknesses, it has the potential to make a relevant contribution to the field.
