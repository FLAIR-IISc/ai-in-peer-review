- Summary: Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding that jointly embeds text and KB entities, learns multiple sense embeddings per mention, uses an LM-based disambiguation, and reports SOTA on entity linking.  
- Contribution 1: Novel multi-prototype mention embedding model addressing mention ambiguity by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities.  
- Contribution 2: Efficient language model–based approach for disambiguating mentions to specific senses.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical state-of-the-art performance on the entity linking task.  
- Strength 1: Outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, showing improved joint text–knowledge representations.  
- Strength 2: Provides direct comparison to a closely related method using similar input data.  
- Strength 3: Analyzes the smoothing parameter and the effect of entity popularity, offering useful insights.  
- Weakness 1: Comparison with ALIGN is under-specified—missing details on input differences (content window size, vector dimensions, definition of N(ej), graph adjacency).  
- Weakness 2: Terminology ambiguity: “mention” is used interchangeably for a string type and a document-specific instance.  
- Weakness 3: Lacks comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods, leaving the impact of sense disambiguation order unclear.  
- Question 1: Please clarify input-data differences versus ALIGN and how they affect learned representations.  
- Question 2: Please detail how adjacency is defined in the directed graph and its effect on mention embeddings.  
- Question 3: Please provide comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods to assess the role of sense disambiguation order.
