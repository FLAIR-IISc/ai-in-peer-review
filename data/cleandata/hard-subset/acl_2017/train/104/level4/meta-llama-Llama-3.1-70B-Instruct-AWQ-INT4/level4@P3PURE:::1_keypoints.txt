- Framework yields improved joint text+knowledge representations, outperforming ALIGN on supervised entity linking.  
- Paper provides a direct comparison with a closely related method using similar input data for a fairer assessment.  
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter highlights the persistent issue of popularity bias in entity linking.  
- ALIGN comparison is confounded by different hyperparameters (content window 10 vs 5, vector dim 500 vs 200) and unclear whether N(e_j) matches ALIGN’s “entities that link to e_j.”  
- Ambiguity in graph adjacency (directed Wikipedia outlinks vs undirected adjacency) and in the definition of N(e_j) makes impacts on learned vectors and similarity scores hard to interpret.  
- Terminology is confusing: “mention” is used inconsistently (string type vs document-specific mention), and “mention embedding” appears to refer only to mention-sense embeddings.  
- The effect of sense disambiguation order is unclear due to lack of comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods.
