**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model that integrates text and knowledge into a unified semantic space. The model learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. An efficient language model-based approach is also designed to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The authors evaluate the MPME model on entity linking tasks and achieve state-of-the-art performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel MPME model that learns multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base.
2. An efficient language model-based approach for disambiguating each mention to a specific sense.
3. A large-scale evaluation of the MPME model on entity linking tasks, achieving state-of-the-art performance.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a good idea to integrate entity, mention, and sense modeling within the same neural language modeling technique.
2. The simple training procedure connected with the modeling allows for a large set of applications.
3. The empirical evaluation is good, although not striking improvements of performance are reported.
4. The paper adds novel ideas and is of relevant interest, despite being an extension of previous work (Yamada et al., CoNLL 2016).

**Weaknesses**

1. The amount of novel content is limited, as the paper is quite close in nature to Yamada et al. (2016).
2. Clarity in some sections is lacking, particularly in Section 3, where some details of Figure 2 are not explained, and the terminology is redundant.
3. The paper does not demonstrate the effectiveness of the MPME model on other semantic tasks that do not directly depend on entities of the knowledge base, such as parsing, coreference, or semantic role labeling.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify the differences between the dictionary of mentions, the dictionary of entity-mention pairs, text anchors, and types for annotated text anchors?
2. How does the MPME model compare to simpler word spaces in other semantic tasks that do not directly depend on entities of the knowledge base?
3. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup and the evaluation metrics used to assess the performance of the MPME model on entity linking tasks?
