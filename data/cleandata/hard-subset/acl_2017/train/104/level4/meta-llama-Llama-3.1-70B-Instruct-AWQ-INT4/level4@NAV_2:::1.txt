**Summary and Contributions**

This submission proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates text and knowledge into a unified semantic space. The model addresses the challenge of ambiguity in entity mentions by learning multiple sense embeddings for each mention. The authors also design an efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly models words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base.
2. An efficient language model-based approach for disambiguating entity mentions.
3. State-of-the-art performance on the entity linking task using the proposed framework.

**Strengths**

1. The proposed framework outperforms ALIGN in the supervised entity linking task, indicating improved representations of text and knowledge learned jointly.
2. The direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar input data provides a useful baseline.
3. The analysis of the smoothing parameter offers valuable insights into the impact of popularity on entity linking.

**Weaknesses**

1. The comparison with ALIGN could be more comprehensive. The differences in content window size, vector dimension, and adjacency definition may contribute to the performance gap, making it difficult to isolate the impact of the proposed framework.
2. The terminology used in the paper can be confusing, particularly the distinction between "mention" as a string type versus a specific mention in a document. Clarification is needed to ensure that the reader understands the scope of the learned embeddings.
3. The impact of sense disambiguation order is unclear without comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods. Additional experiments or analysis would help to better understand the contribution of this component.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the adjacency definition used in the graph construction, and how it differs from the approach used in ALIGN?
2. How do you distinguish between "mention" as a string type versus a specific mention in a document, and how do these definitions impact the learned embeddings?
3. Can you provide additional experiments or analysis to better understand the impact of sense disambiguation order on the overall performance of the proposed framework?
