- Novel method: proposes a neural learning approach integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling within a single neural language model.  
- Simple training: the training procedure is straightforward and enables many downstream applications.  
- Empirical evaluation: thorough experiments, though reported performance gains are modest.  
- Limited novelty: amount of new content is limited and the work resembles Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Need comparison: authors should clearly outline key differences from Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Clarity/writing issues: some sections (notably Section 3) and parts of Figure 2 lack clear explanation.  
- Terminology confusion: unclear distinctions between “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity-mention pairs,” “text anchors,” and “types for annotated text anchors.”  
- Mismatch in discussion: paper’s discussion sometimes doesn’t match its technical sophistication.  
- Evaluation scope: testing is focused on entity linking/disambiguation; should evaluate embeddings on other semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they aren’t weaker than simpler word spaces.  
- Author response: reviewer read the response and notes it addresses some concerns.
