- Title/intro overstate scope (bridging text and knowledge larger than EDL), but EDL is an important subtask.
- Method uses an intermediate "mention sense" layer and performs linking in two steps: mention→mention-sense and mention-sense→entity.
- Learns joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities with a single unified objective.
- Approach is technically clear and follows current embedding best practices; alternative methods possible but unclear if they'd help.
- Empirical gains (MPME/SPME in Table 2) over direct methods are shown, but the reason for improvement from the two-step design is not explained.
- Major unclear point: how mention senses are created, differentiated, and how many senses a mention can have.
- Potential bijection concern: if senses are derived from the KB, mention senses may simply map to entities, making the intermediate layer redundant.
- Question of what extra information mention senses capture that entity profiles do not, and whether that info could instead be added to entity pages.
- Request to clarify Section 5 on how sense profiles are built, what data they add relative to entity profiles, and statistics (e.g., average distinct words per sense).
- Incorrect claim: prior EDL work (e.g., TAC KBP systems) has addressed mention ambiguity—so the paper’s “first work” statement is inaccurate.
- Positive note: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a useful contribution.
- Authors’ rebuttal about KBP being irrelevant is unconvincing; embedding quality should be validated via application-level evaluations.
