- Proposes MPME: a model that integrates text and knowledge into a unified semantic space, learning multiple sense embeddings per mention and using an LM-based disambiguation; evaluated on entity linking with state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution: novel MPME that jointly models textual context words and KB-derived entities to learn multiple sense embeddings for mentions.

- Contribution: an efficient language-model-based disambiguation method to assign mentions to specific senses.

- Contribution: large-scale evaluation on entity linking tasks showing competitive / state-of-the-art performance.

- Strength: effectively integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within a single neural language modeling framework.

- Strength: simple training procedure that enables broad applicability.

- Strength: solid empirical evaluation, though improvements are modest rather than dramatic.

- Strength: introduces novel ideas of interest despite building on Yamada et al. (2016).

- Weakness: limited novelty — approach is closely related to prior work (Yamada et al., 2016).

- Weakness: clarity issues in parts of the paper (notably Section 3 and Figure 2), with some redundant terminology.

- Weakness: lacks demonstration of effectiveness on semantic tasks not directly tied to KB entities (e.g., parsing, coreference, SRL).

- Question: please clarify differences among the dictionary of mentions, entity-mention pair dictionary, text anchors, and types for annotated text anchors.

- Question: how does MPME compare to simpler word-space models on semantic tasks that don’t rely on KB entities?

- Question: provide more details on experimental setup and evaluation metrics used for the entity linking experiments.
