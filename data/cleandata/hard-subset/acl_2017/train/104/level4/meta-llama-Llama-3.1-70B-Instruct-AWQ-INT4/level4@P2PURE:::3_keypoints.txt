- Proposes a simple, effective neural learning approach for entity disambiguation and linking with broad applicability.  
- Integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within a unified neural language modeling framework—a notable contribution.  
- Novelty is somewhat limited and the paper’s clarity is occasionally compromised.  
- Formal presentation is clear but the discussion does not always match the technical sophistication of the ideas.  
- Empirical evaluation is satisfactory but lacks striking performance improvements.  
- Appears closely related to Yamada et al. (2016); authors should explicitly state differences.  
- Writing needs clarification, especially Section 3; Figure 2 details and several terminology distinctions (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “entity-mention pairs”, “text anchors” vs “types for annotated text anchors”) are unclear or redundant.  
- Paper evaluates MPME mainly on entity tasks but should show that its word embeddings are not inferior to simpler models on other semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) involving entity mentions.
