- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, though title/intro overstate scope.  
- Method introduces an intermediate mention-sense layer and a two-step mapping: mention → mention-sense → entity.  
- Jointly learns embeddings for words, mention-senses, and entities with a single objective that equally optimizes all three.  
- Technical approach is clear and follows embedding best practices; alternative methods may exist but benefits are uncertain.  
- Reviewer questions necessity of the two-step mention-sense pipeline versus direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN), since performance gains are unexplained.  
- Entity profiles are built from neighboring entities via a relatedness graph from Wikipedia definitions; word profiles use standard distributional semantics.  
- Mention-sense profiles use sense-disambiguated local context and neighboring mentions, but the sense-disambiguation method is under-specified.  
- Key omissions: how senses are created, how many senses per mention, whether senses are seeded from entity text, and what unique information senses capture beyond entity profiles.  
- Suggest updating Section 5 to clearly justify and detail the mention-sense contribution.  
- Claim of being the first to address mention ambiguity is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL tasks have addressed this previously against Freebase.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter idea in Section 6.4.2 is a good contribution.  
- Reviewer dissatisfied with authors' rejection of KBP evaluation relevance; argues embedding quality must be validated through downstream applications, not just intrinsic measures.
