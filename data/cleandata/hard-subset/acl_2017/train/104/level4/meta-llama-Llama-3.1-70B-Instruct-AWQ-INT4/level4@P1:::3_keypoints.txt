- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model that integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling for entity disambiguation/linking in a unified neural language model.
- Presents a simple, efficient training procedure that enables many downstream applications.
- Empirical evaluation shows the proposed model is effective for entity linking/disambiguation, though gains are not striking.
- Strength: good idea to jointly model entity, mention, and sense in one framework.
- Strength: training procedure is simple and broadly applicable.
- Strength: formal presentation is clear and the discussion is generally strong.
- Weakness: clarity issues—prose (especially Section 3), unexplained parts of Figure 2, and some redundant terminology.
- Weakness: unclear distinction between the dictionary of mentions and the dictionary of entity-mention pairs.
- Weakness: novelty concerns—approach is quite similar to Yamada et al. (2016); differences are not clearly outlined.
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope—only tested on entity linking/disambiguation, not on other semantic tasks.
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed comparison with related work, notably Yamada et al. (2016).
- Suggestion: evaluate MPME embeddings on broader semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to demonstrate generality and competitiveness.
- Questions to authors: clarify the two dictionary concepts; explain differences and advantages over Yamada et al. (2016); provide broader evaluation results.
