- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) that jointly models words, mentions, and KB entities to address mention ambiguity.
- Contributions: unified neural language modeling for entity/mention/sense, simple training procedure applicable to many tasks, and an efficient LM-based mention disambiguation method.
- Strength: integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within a single neural language modeling framework.
- Strength: simple training approach that enables a wide range of applications.
- Strength: strong empirical evaluation showing high-quality word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings.
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art performance on entity linking tasks.
- Weakness: limited novelty, as the work builds on prior methods (notably Yamada et al., 2016).
- Weakness: clarity issues in parts of the paper (e.g., Section 3) and insufficient explanation of Figure 2 details.
- Weakness: redundant/unclear terminology—need to clarify dictionaries of mentions, entity-mention pairs, text anchors, and annotated anchor types.
- Question: clarify differences from Yamada et al. (2016) and specify the novel ideas introduced here.
- Question: plan to evaluate MPME on semantic tasks beyond KB-dependent entity linking/disambiguation?
- Question: provide more details on experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning for MPME.
- Question: how will MPME be extended to handle out-of-vocabulary entities and mentions?
