- Proposes MPME: a model integrating text and knowledge to learn multi-prototype sense embeddings for mentions.  
- Learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context words and KB-derived entities.  
- Introduces an efficient language model–based method to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.  
- Evaluates word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings with qualitative and quantitative analyses.  
- Applies MPME and disambiguation to entity linking and reports state-of-the-art performance on a benchmark.  
- Main contribution: novel integrated MPME model combining entity, mention, and sense modeling in a neural LM framework.  
- Main contribution: efficient LM-based disambiguation technique.  
- Main contribution: achieves SOTA results on entity linking.  
- Strength: clear, simple neural learning approach that is easy to understand and implement.  
- Strength: performance gains are modest but interesting and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Strength: potential applicability across a large set of tasks.  
- Weakness: method is close to Yamada et al. (2016); differences are not clearly outlined.  
- Weakness: clarity issues in parts of the paper (notably Section 3) and missing explanations for Figure 2 details.  
- Weakness: redundant or unclear terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs”).  
- Question: please detail differences between MPME and Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Question: clarify terminology such as “dictionary of mentions” and “dictionary of entity-mention pairs.”  
- Question: provide more insight into applications beyond entity linking.  
- Additional comment: authors should evaluate whether MPME word embeddings degrade performance on non-entity tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) compared to simpler word spaces.
