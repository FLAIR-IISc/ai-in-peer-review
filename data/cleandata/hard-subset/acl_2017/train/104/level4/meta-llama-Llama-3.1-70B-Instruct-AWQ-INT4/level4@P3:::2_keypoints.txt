- Title/introduction misleadingly imply a broader scope; the paper actually focuses on entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to Wikipedia.
- Proposed method introduces an intermediate layer of "mention senses" with a two-step mapping: mention → mention-sense → entity.
- The model jointly learns embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities with a single unified objective.
- Entity profiles are built from neighboring entities in a relatedness graph derived from word-level relatedness of Wikipedia page definitions, using extended skip-gram embeddings.
- Word profiles use standard distributional semantics without sense disambiguation.
- Mention-sense profiles use distributional semantics plus sense disambiguation via a sense-based profile (language model) from local context words and neighboring mentions.
- Paper lacks explanation why the two-step mention-sense approach is preferable to a direct mention→entity mapping like ALIGN/Yamada et al.
- Table 2 shows MPME/SPME outperform one-step methods, but it is unclear what additional signal mention senses provide beyond entities.
- Unclear how senses are created, how many senses a mention can have, and whether senses are defined from the KB (potentially making them bijective with entities).
- Unclear what information at the mention-sense level is not captured at the entity level and why a separate embedding space is necessary.
- Contribution: MPME learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and KB-derived entities.
- Contribution: Uses an efficient language-model-based method to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.
- Contribution: Presents qualitative and quantitative analyses demonstrating the quality of word, entity, and mention-sense embeddings.
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the evaluated entity linking benchmark.
- Strength: Incorporates a useful smoothing parameter (Section 6.4.2).
- Strength: Clear and well-structured presentation of the method.
- Weakness: No clear justification or theoretical/empirical analysis explaining the need for the two-step process.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on how senses are created, differentiated, and determined per mention string.
- Weakness: Incorrect or overstated claim that this is the first work addressing mention ambiguity in text–knowledge integration.
- Question: Please clarify why the two-step mention-sense process is superior to a direct mention→entity mapping.
- Question: How exactly are senses created, differentiated, and how is the number of senses per mention determined?
- Question: Provide more details on the sense-based profile (language model) used for disambiguation and its training procedure.
- Question: How do the authors respond to prior EDL evaluations (e.g., TAC KBP) and existing systems that perform similar tasks against larger/noisier KBs like Freebase?
