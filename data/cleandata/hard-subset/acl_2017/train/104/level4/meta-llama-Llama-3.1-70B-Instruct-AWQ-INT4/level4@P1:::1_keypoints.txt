- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates text and knowledge to capture mention ambiguity in a unified semantic space.
- Learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context words and knowledge-base entities.
- Introduces an efficient language-model–based method to disambiguate mentions to specific senses and applies it to entity linking with state-of-the-art benchmark results.
- Thorough evaluation with qualitative and quantitative analyses showing high-quality word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings.
- Direct comparison to ALIGN provides a useful baseline for assessing the model’s effectiveness.
- Analysis of a smoothing parameter gives insight into the role of entity popularity in entity linking performance.
- Comparison with ALIGN lacks experimental-detail transparency (content window size, vector dimension, adjacency definition), hindering interpretation of differences.
- Terminology is sometimes unclear—especially the distinction between a mention type (string) and a specific mention instance—complicating the discussion of mention vs. sense embeddings.
- The effect of sense disambiguation order on performance is underexplored, and no comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods is provided.
- Request for detailed ALIGN experimental settings (content window size, vector dimension, adjacency definition).
- Request clarification on how the paper distinguishes mention types from mention instances and the implications for learning sense embeddings.
- Suggestion to compare with other unsupervised entity linking methods to evaluate the impact of sense disambiguation order.
