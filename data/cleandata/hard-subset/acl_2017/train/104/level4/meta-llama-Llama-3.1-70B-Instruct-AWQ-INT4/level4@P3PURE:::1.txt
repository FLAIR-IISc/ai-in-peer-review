- Strengths:
* The proposed framework demonstrates improved representations of text and knowledge learned jointly, as evidenced by its superior performance in the supervised entity linking task compared to ALIGN.
* A direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar input data is provided, allowing for a more informed assessment of the framework's strengths.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter offers valuable insights, as the impact of popularity is a persistent issue in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN could be improved. Notably, ALIGN employed a content window size of 10 versus the 5 used in this paper, and a vector dimension of 500 versus the 200 used here. Furthermore, it is unclear whether N(ej) includes only entities that link to ej. Given that the graph is directed and consists of Wikipedia outlinks, it is also unclear whether adjacency is defined as it would be for an undirected graph. In ALIGN, the context of an entity is defined as the set of entities that link to that entity. If N(e_j) is defined differently, it is difficult to determine how much impact this change has on the learned vectors, which could contribute to the difference in scores on the entity similarity task.
* The terminology used in the paper can be confusing at times, particularly with regards to the term "mention." It is occasionally unclear whether "mention" refers to a string type or a specific mention in a particular document. The phrase "mention embedding" is used, but it appears that embeddings are only learned for mention senses.
* The impact of sense disambiguation order is difficult to determine without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
