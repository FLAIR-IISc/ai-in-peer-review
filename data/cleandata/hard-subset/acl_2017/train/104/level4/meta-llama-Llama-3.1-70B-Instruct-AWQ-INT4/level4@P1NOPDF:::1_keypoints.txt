- The proposed framework outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint learning of text and knowledge representations.  
- Direct comparison to a closely related approach using similar input data is a noteworthy strength.  
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter yields useful insights into the persistent popularity bias in entity linking.  
- The comparison with ALIGN is incomplete: different context window sizes and vector dimensions were used, making results hard to attribute.  
- The definition of N(e_j) and graph adjacency is unclear (directed Wikipedia outlinks vs. ALIGN’s "entities that link to that entity"), complicating comparisons and interpretation of learned vectors.  
- Terminology is confusing—“mention” is used inconsistently (string type vs. specific document mention), and “mention embedding” seems to actually refer only to mention-sense embeddings.  
- It’s difficult to assess the effect of sense disambiguation order without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
