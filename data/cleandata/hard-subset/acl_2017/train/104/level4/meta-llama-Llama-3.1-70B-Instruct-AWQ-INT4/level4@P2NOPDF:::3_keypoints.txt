- Proposes a novel neural method integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling within a single neural language modeling framework.  
- Employs a simple training procedure that enables a wide range of applications.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and the paper is formally well-presented.  
- Overall novelty is limited; contributions are relevant but not revolutionary.  
- Reported performance improvements are modest and not striking.  
- Strong similarity to Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016) that is not adequately addressed or differentiated.  
- Clarity issues in parts of the paper, particularly Section 3, with insufficient explanation of Figure 2.  
- Terminology is redundant and confusing (e.g., unclear distinctions among dictionaries, entity-mention pairs, text anchors, and types).  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on entity linking/disambiguation; authors should test embeddings on other semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to validate quality.  
- Reviewer has read and considered the authors’ response.
