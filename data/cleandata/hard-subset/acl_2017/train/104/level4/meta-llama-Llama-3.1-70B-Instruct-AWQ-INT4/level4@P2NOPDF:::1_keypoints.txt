- Joint learning of text and knowledge representations outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, showing the framework’s effectiveness.  
- Direct comparison with a closely related method using similar input data enables a more accurate performance assessment.  
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter provides useful insight into popularity bias in entity linking.

- Comparison with ALIGN is insufficiently controlled: differing context window sizes, vector dimensions, and unclear definition of N(ej) (incoming vs. outgoing links) may affect results.  
- Terminology is confusing: "mention" is ambiguous (string type vs. document-specific mention) and "mention embedding" seems misused since only mention-sense embeddings are learned.  
- Lack of evaluation against other unsupervised entity linking methods leaves the impact of sense disambiguation order unclear.

- The paper needs clearer methodological and parameter comparisons with prior work (e.g., ALIGN).  
- Terminology should be refined for clarity, particularly around "mention" and embeddings.  
- Additional evaluation of sense disambiguation order and comparisons to other unsupervised methods would strengthen the claims.
