- Paper addresses EDL linking mentions to Wikipedia, but title/intro misleadingly imply a broader “bridging text and knowledge” goal.  
- Method introduces an intermediate mention-sense layer enabling a two-step mapping: mention → mention sense → entity, with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Technical approach is clear and consistent with embedding best practices; alternate methods might exist but their benefit is uncertain.  
- Core question: why does the two-step mention-sense approach outperform direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN/Yamada et al.) — added value of mention senses is not explained.  
- Entity profiles are neighbor-based (relatedness graph from entity text); mention-sense profiles use local-context sense disambiguation, but the disambiguation mechanism is under-described.  
- Critical missing detail: how mention senses are created, differentiated, and how the number of senses per mention is determined.  
- If mention-sense profiles are seeded from entity text, they may be bijective with entities, so it’s unclear what extra information mention senses add versus enriching entity representations.  
- Paper should clarify (Section 5) how many unique context words per mention sense exist and why a separate mention-sense space is necessary instead of augmenting entity pages.  
- Claim of being the first work to address mention ambiguity in integrated text–KB representations is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL evaluations have addressed this with multiple systems.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a useful idea.  
- Authors’ dismissal of KBP evaluation is unconvincing — embedding “goodness” must be validated through downstream application performance, not just conceptual arguments.
