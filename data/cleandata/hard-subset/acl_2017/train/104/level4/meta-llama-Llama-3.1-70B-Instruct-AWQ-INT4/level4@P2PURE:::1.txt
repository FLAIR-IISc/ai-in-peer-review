- Strengths:
* The proposed framework outperforms the ALIGN system in the supervised entity linking task, suggesting that it effectively improves the joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
* A direct comparison is made with a closely related approach using similar input data, providing a useful baseline for evaluation.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter offers valuable insights, as the impact of popularity is a persistent issue in entity linking tasks.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN could be improved. Notably, ALIGN employed a content window size of 10 versus the 5 used in this paper, and a vector dimension of 500 versus the 200 used here. Furthermore, it is unclear whether N(ej) only includes entities that link to ej. Given that the graph is directed and consists of Wikipedia outlinks, it is also unclear whether adjacency is defined as it would be for an undirected graph. In ALIGN, the context of an entity is defined as the set of entities that link to it. If N(e_j) is defined differently, it is difficult to determine the impact of this change on the learned vectors, which could contribute to the difference in scores on the entity similarity task.
* The terminology used in the paper can be confusing, particularly with regards to the term "mention." It is sometimes unclear whether "mention" refers to a string type or a specific mention in a particular document. While the phrase "mention embedding" is used, it appears that embeddings are only learned for mention senses.
* The impact of the sense disambiguation order is difficult to determine without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods, making it challenging to fully evaluate the effectiveness of this aspect of the proposed framework.
