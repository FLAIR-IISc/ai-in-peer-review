- Framework outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting joint text-knowledge learning is effective.
- Direct comparison to a closely related approach using similar input data is provided.
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter clarifies the impact of popularity on entity linking.
- Comparison with ALIGN omits crucial experimental details (content window size, vector dimension) needed for fair evaluation.
- Definition of N(ej) and adjacency in the directed Wikipedia outlink graph is unclear (whether N(ej) includes only entities linking to ej).
- If entity context is defined differently than in ALIGN, it could explain differences in learned vectors and entity similarity scores.
- Terminology is inconsistent—“mention” and “mention embedding” are ambiguously used (string type vs document-specific mention vs sense embeddings).
- Impact of sense disambiguation order is unclear due to lack of comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods.
- Overall promising approach but needs clearer experimental settings, consistent terminology, and broader baseline comparisons to strengthen the evaluation.
