- Strength: Presents good ideas using a simple neural learning approach with broad potential applications.
- Strength: Integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within a unified neural language modeling technique.
- Strength: Simple training procedure and formally clear presentation.
- Weakness: Novelty is limited — amount of new content is a concern.
- Weakness: Clarity issues in parts of the paper (notably Section 3) and inconsistent technical discussion depth.
- Weakness: Figure 2 lacks explanation and some terminology is redundant/confusing (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs").
- Empirical: Evaluation is satisfactory but performance gains are not striking.
- Relation to prior work: Paper strongly resembles Yamada et al. (2016); authors should explicitly state differences.
- Generalization concern: MPME is mainly tested on entity linking; authors should show its word embeddings perform comparably to simpler wordspaces on non-entity semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL).
