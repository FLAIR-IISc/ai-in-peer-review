- Paper addresses entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, but title/introduction overstate a broader text-knowledge bridging claim.
- Method introduces an intermediate mention-sense layer with a two-step mapping (mention → mention sense → entity) and jointly trained embeddings for words, senses, and entities.
- Technical approach is clear and follows modern embedding practices.
- No clear rationale for the two-step mention-sense design versus a direct one-step mapping; ALIGN’s one-step baseline exists and MPME/SPME’s superiority is unexplained.
- Entity profiles use neighboring entities from a relatedness graph built from entity definitions; word profiles use standard distributional semantics; mention-sense profiles use sense-disambiguated local context and neighboring mentions.
- The process for creating and differentiating senses is under-specified, including how many senses per mention are determined.
- Unclear what unique information mention senses capture beyond entity pages and whether those words could instead be incorporated into the entity space.
- Claim of being the first to address mention ambiguity in text-knowledge integration is inaccurate given TAC KBP EDL tasks linking to Freebase.
- The smoothing parameter idea in Section 6.4.2 is a positive contribution.
- Embedding quality must ultimately be judged by application performance; conceptual elegance alone is insufficient.
