Strengths:
* The proposed framework demonstrates superior performance to ALIGN in the supervised entity linking task, indicating enhanced joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
* A direct comparison with a closely related approach is provided, utilizing similar input data, which facilitates a more accurate assessment.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter offers valuable insights, as the impact of popularity remains a persistent challenge in entity linking tasks.

Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN could be improved by addressing discrepancies in experimental settings, including content window size (10 vs 5) and vector dimension (500 vs 200). Furthermore, clarification is needed on whether N(ej) exclusively includes entities linked to ej, and whether the graph's adjacency is defined as in an undirected graph. Specifically, ALIGN defines an entity's context as the set of entities linking to it, which may differ from N(e_j). This ambiguity obscures the impact of the change on learned vectors, potentially contributing to the difference in entity similarity task scores.
* The terminology "mention" is occasionally ambiguous, referring to either a string type or a specific mention within a document. While "mention embedding" is used, it appears that embeddings are only learned for mention senses, which may lead to confusion.
* The effect of sense disambiguation order on the results is difficult to discern without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods, which would provide a more comprehensive understanding of its impact.
