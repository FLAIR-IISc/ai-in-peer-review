1. Paper addresses Entity Disambiguation and Linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, though title/intro misleadingly imply a broader text-knowledge bridging.

2. Proposed method uses an intermediate mention-sense layer with two steps: mention→mention-sense and mention-sense→entity, learning joint embeddings for words, senses, and entities.

3. Technical approach is clear and follows contemporary embedding best practices.

4. Core question: why is the two-step mention-sense approach superior to a direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN)? Table 2 shows gains but lacks an explanation of what senses add.

5. Entity profiles appear to be neighboring entities from a relatedness graph built via word-level relatedness on Wikipedia definitions using skip-gram embeddings.

6. Word profiles use standard distributional semantics without disambiguation; mention-sense profiles use distributional semantics with sense disambiguation from local context and neighboring mentions.

7. Details are missing on how mention senses are created, differentiated, and how the number of senses per mention is determined.

8. If mention senses are seeded from entity text, this could produce a bijection between senses and entities; the paper must clarify whether senses add unique words not present in entity pages.

9. Need statistics on how many unique context words per mention-sense exist and whether keeping a separate mention-sense space is necessary versus augmenting entity profiles.

10. Section 5 should explicitly state the above mechanism if it is the paper’s primary novel contribution.

11. The claim of being first to address mention ambiguity in integration of text and knowledge is inaccurate given recent TAC KBP EDL evaluations against Freebase.

12. The smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a positive contribution.

13. Authors’ response about KBP evaluation was unsatisfactory; embedding quality should be validated via application-level performance, not just conceptual arguments.
