- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) into Wikipedia, though title/intro may overstate scope.  
- Proposes an intermediate "mention sense" layer with a two-step mapping: mention → mention sense → entity.  
- Learns joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities using a single objective that equally optimizes all three.  
- Method is technically clear and follows modern embedding best practices, though alternative methods are possible.  
- Main unresolved question: no clear explanation why the two-step mention-sense approach outperforms a direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Table 2 shows MPME/SPME beat ALIGN, but the paper does not explain what extra information mention senses add beyond entities.  
- Unclear how mention senses are created, differentiated, or how their number is determined—potentially implying a bijection with entities.  
- Questions whether mention-sense profiles add unique context words not present in entity pages, and if so how many and why they require a separate space.  
- Recommends clarifying Section 5 to explain sense construction and the key novelty (mention-sense information) in detail.  
- Corrects claim of novelty: prior TAC KBP EDL evaluations exist and should be cited as relevant baselines (link provided).  
- Praises the smoothing parameter idea in Section 6.4.2.  
- After authors' response, reviewer remains dissatisfied with dismissal of KBP evaluation—argues embeddings must be validated via downstream applications, not just conceptual claims.
