**Summary and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model to address the ambiguity in entity mentions by jointly modeling words from textual contexts and entities derived from a knowledge base. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A unified neural language modeling technique that integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling.
2. A simple training procedure that supports a large set of applications.
3. An efficient language model-based approach to disambiguate each mention to a specific sense.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a good idea to integrate entity, mention, and sense modeling within the same neural language modeling technique.
2. The simple training procedure connected with the modeling allows for a large set of applications.
3. The empirical evaluation is good, demonstrating the high quality of the word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings.
4. The paper achieves state-of-the-art performance on entity linking tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. The amount of novel content is limited, as the paper builds upon existing work (Yamada et al., CoNLL 2016).
2. The clarity of some sections, such as Section 3, is not always clear, and some details of Figure 2 are not explained.
3. The terminology is sometimes redundant, and the authors should clarify the differences between the dictionary of mentions, dictionary of entity-mention pairs, text anchors, and types for annotated text anchors.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify the differences between your work and Yamada et al. (2016) and outline the novel ideas introduced in this paper?
2. How do you plan to address the limitation of testing the MPME model only against entity linking/disambiguation tasks, and not against other semantic tasks that do not directly depend on entities of the KB?
3. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning for the MPME model?
4. How do you plan to extend the MPME model to handle out-of-vocabulary entities and mentions?
