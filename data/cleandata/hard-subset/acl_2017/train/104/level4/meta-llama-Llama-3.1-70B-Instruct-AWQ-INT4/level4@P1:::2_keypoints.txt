- Paper addresses entity disambiguation/linking to Wikipedia; title/introduction may overstate scope beyond EDL.  
- Proposes a two-step approach with an intermediate "mention sense" layer: mention→mention-sense→entity.  
- Learns joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities with a single objective.  
- Method is technically clear and aligns with current embedding best practices.  
- Reviewer questions why the two-step mention-sense process is better than direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN/Yamada).  
- Table 2 shows MPME/SPME outperform ALIGN, but it's unclear what extra information mention senses capture versus entities.  
- Unclear how mention senses are created, differentiated, and how many senses each mention can have—details are missing.  
- If senses are derived from the KB, a bijection between senses and entities may exist, undermining claimed novelty.  
- Unclear whether mention-sense profiles are seeded from entity page text and what additional context words they add beyond entity pages.  
- Recommendation to clarify Section 5 to explain construction and role of mention senses.  
- Statement claiming first work to handle mention ambiguity in text-knowledge integration is inaccurate given TAC KBP EDL tasks.  
- Smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a positive and welcome idea.  
- Post-response, reviewer remains unconvinced that KBP evaluation is irrelevant and stresses that embedding quality must be shown via application performance.  
- Paper’s stated contributions: multi-prototype mention embedding model; multiple sense embeddings learned jointly with words/entities; language-model-based disambiguation.  
- Strengths noted: sound technical approach, novel multi-sense mention embeddings, strong experimental embedding quality.  
- Weaknesses noted: insufficient justification for two-step vs one-step, poorly explained sense disambiguation/creation, inaccurate novelty claim.  
- Questions for authors: justify two-step approach over direct mapping; detail how senses are created and how many per mention; explain the sense-based language-model disambiguation procedure.
