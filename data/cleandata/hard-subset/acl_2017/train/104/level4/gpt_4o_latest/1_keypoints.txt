- Shows superior performance over ALIGN on supervised entity linking, indicating the framework better joint-learns text and knowledge representations.
- Provides a direct comparison with a closely related method using highly similar input data.
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter is useful, highlighting that popularity bias remains a recurring issue in entity linking.

- Comparison with ALIGN is incomplete: differences in context window (10 vs 5), vector dimensionality (500 vs 200), and unclear definition of N(ej) (incoming vs undirected adjacency) make it hard to attribute performance gaps.
- The term “mention” is ambiguous—unclear if it denotes a string type or a document-specific mention; “mention embedding” appears to actually be for mention senses only.
- The effect of sense disambiguation order is hard to judge because the paper lacks comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
