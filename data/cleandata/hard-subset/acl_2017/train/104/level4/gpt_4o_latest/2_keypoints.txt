- Title and introduction overstate scope; bridging text and knowledge is broader than the EDL task addressed.  
- Paper proposes a two-step approach with an intermediate "mention senses" layer: mention→mention senses→entities.  
- Method learns joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities with a single balanced objective.  
- Technical methodology is clear and consistent with modern embedding practices, though alternatives might exist.  
- Experimental results (Table 2) show two-step MPME/SPME outperforming a direct one-step mapping, but the reason for this improvement is not explained.  
- The paper fails to clearly define how mention senses are created, differentiated, and how many senses per mention are allowed.  
- Unclear whether mention senses map one-to-one to KB entities or use entity text as seeds, and what extra information mention senses capture beyond entity profiles.  
- Suggests verifying and clarifying how entity profiles, word profiles, and mention-sense profiles are constructed and how sense disambiguation is performed.  
- Recommends updating Section 5 to explicitly clarify the novel contribution and the role of mention senses.  
- Claim of no prior baselines is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL evaluations and systems (using Freebase) are relevant and should be cited.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is innovative and valuable.  
- After authors’ response, reviewer remains unconvinced that KBP is irrelevant and stresses embedding quality must be validated via application performance.
