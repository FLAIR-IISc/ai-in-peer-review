- Introduces compelling ideas with a unified neural framework for entity, mention, and sense modeling.  
- Uses a simple neural learning/training approach that enables broad application support.  
- Demonstrates interesting empirical performance, though not substantially better than prior work.  
- Empirical evaluation is solid but lacks significant performance improvements.  
- Paper builds on Yamada et al. (2016) but incorporates some novel ideas and is of notable interest.  
- Limited amount of novel contributions overall.  
- Writing and exposition are inconsistently clear; Section 3 and parts of Figure 2 are insufficiently explained.  
- Terminology is occasionally redundant/confusing (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" vs text anchors).  
- Authors should explicitly highlight differences from closely related work (Yamada et al., 2016).  
- Discussion quality does not consistently match the strength of the technical ideas.  
- Broader concern: need to evaluate the MPME-generated word embeddings on other semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they’re not inferior to simpler embedding spaces.
