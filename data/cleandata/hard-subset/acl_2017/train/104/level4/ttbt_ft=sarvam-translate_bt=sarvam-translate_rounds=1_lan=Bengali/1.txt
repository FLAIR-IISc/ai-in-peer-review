- Strengths:
* The supervised entity connection performs better than ALIGN, indicating that the proposed framework improves the representation of jointly learned text and knowledge.
* Can be directly compared with closely related methods using very similar input data.
* Analysis of smoothing parameters is useful, as the effect of popularity on entity connection is a persistent problem.
- Weaknesses:
* Comparison with ALIGN could be better. ALIGN used a content window size of 10, while this paper uses a size of 5, a vector dimension of 500, whereas this paper uses a size of 200. Also, it is not clear to me whether N(ej) only includes entities that link to ej. The graph consists of directed and Wikipedia outlines, but how adjacency will be defined, as it is for an undirected graph? For ALIGN, the context of an entity is the set of entities linked to that entity. If N(e_j) is different, we cannot say how much this change will affect the learned vectors, and it may increase the difference in the entity similarity task score.
* It is sometimes difficult to follow whether "mention" means a string type or a specific reference within a particular document. The term "mention embedding" has been used, but it seems that embedding is learned only in the sense of a mention.
* It is difficult to determine the effect of the sense disambiguation order without comparing it with other unsupervised entity connection methods.
