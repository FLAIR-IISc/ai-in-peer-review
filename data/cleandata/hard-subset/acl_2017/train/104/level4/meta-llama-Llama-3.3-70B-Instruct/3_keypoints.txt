- Proposes innovative concepts with a simple neural learning approach and demonstrates interesting (if not large) performance gains and broad applicability.
- Introduces a unified neural language-modeling framework that jointly models entities, mentions, and senses for entity disambiguation and linking.
- The simple training procedure and modeling enable support for a wide range of downstream applications.
- Paper is formally well-structured, though some discussions occasionally contradict the technical exposition.
- Empirical evaluation is adequate but reported performance improvements are modest.
- Work appears to build on Yamada et al. (2016) yet contains novel ideas and remains relevant.
- Concerns about the amount of new content and clarity in parts of the manuscript.
- Writing is unclear in places (Section 3 particularly); Figure 2 lacks explanation and terminology is sometimes redundant or confusing.
- Unclear distinction between the “dictionary of mentions” and the “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” and their relation to text anchors/types.
- Strong resemblance to Yamada et al. (2016); authors should explicitly state differences.
- Need to show that MPME word embeddings are competitive with simpler word spaces on other semantic tasks involving entity mentions (e.g., parsing, coreference, SRL).
