- Paper tackles entity disambiguation and linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, though the title/intro overstate scope of "bridging text and knowledge."
- Method introduces an intermediate "mention sense" layer with a two-step mapping: mention → mention sense → entity.
- Jointly learned embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities, optimized with a single objective.
- Technical approach is clear and follows modern embedding best practices; unclear if alternatives would perform better.
- Reviewer questions necessity of the two-step mention-sense design and lacks a clear rationale for superiority over direct mention→entity methods (e.g., ALIGN).
- Table 2 shows MPME/SPME outperform simpler models, but the added value and exact difference provided by mention senses are not well explained.
- Entity profiles are built from neighboring entities in a relatedness graph derived from word-level relatedness in Wikipedia definitions (extended skip-gram embeddings).
- Word profiles use standard distributional semantics without sense disambiguation.
- Mention sense profiles use distributional semantics with sense disambiguation from local context and neighboring mentions, but the sense creation and differentiation process is underexplained.
- Unclear how senses are defined, how many senses a mention can have, and what unique information mention-sense profiles capture beyond entity pages.
- Claim of being first to address mention ambiguity in text–knowledge integration is inaccurate given recent TAC KBP EDL tasks on Freebase.
- Positive contribution: introduction of a smoothing parameter (Section 6.4.2).
- Reviewer remains unsatisfied with authors' dismissal of KBP evaluation; stresses that embedding quality must be validated by empirical application/performance.
