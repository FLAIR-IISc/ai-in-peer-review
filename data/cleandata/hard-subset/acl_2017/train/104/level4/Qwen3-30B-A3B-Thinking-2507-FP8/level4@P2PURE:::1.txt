

- Strengths:
* Outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, demonstrating that the proposed framework enhances jointly learned text and knowledge representations.
* Provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using nearly identical input data.
* Offers valuable analysis of the smoothing parameter, addressing the persistent challenge of popularity bias in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN requires clarification. ALIGN employs a content window size of 10 versus this paper's 5, and a vector dimension of 500 versus 200. Additionally, it is unclear whether N(e_j) includes only entities linking to e_j. Given that the graph is directed (using Wikipedia outlinks), it is ambiguous whether adjacency is defined as in an undirected graph. Since ALIGN defines an entity's context as the set of entities linking to it, a difference in N(e_j) definition prevents isolating the impact of this change on the learned vectors, which may contribute to the observed score differences in the entity similarity task.
* The terminology regarding "mention" is occasionally ambiguous, making it difficult to discern whether it refers to a string type or a specific mention within a document. The term "mention embedding" is used, but embeddings appear to be learned only for mention senses.
* The paper fails to establish the impact of sense disambiguation order, as there is no comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
