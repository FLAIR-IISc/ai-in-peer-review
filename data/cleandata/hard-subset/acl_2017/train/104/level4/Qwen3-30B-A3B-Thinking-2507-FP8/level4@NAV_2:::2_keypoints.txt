- Proposes a two-step EDL framework (mention → sense → entity) with a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model that jointly learns word, mention-sense, and entity embeddings, claiming state-of-the-art entity linking.
- Empirically demonstrates SOTA entity linking performance using multi-prototype mention embeddings.
- Implements a multi-prototype embedding model that leverages context and KB information, though core novelty is limited.
- Achieves robust empirical validation across benchmarks.
- Introduces a smoothing parameter (Section 6.4.2) that improves model robustness and performance.
- The two-step pipeline lacks clear justification or comparison versus direct entity linking approaches (e.g., ALIGN).
- The definition and differentiation of mention senses is inadequately explained, leaving unclear what additional information senses provide beyond entities.
- The claim of “no exact baselines” is incorrect given existing TAC KBP entity linking baselines and evaluations.
- Question: How are the number and definitions of senses per mention determined, and is this KB-dependent while avoiding a bijection with entities?
- Question: Which context words define mention senses that do not appear on the entity’s Wikipedia page, and what is the average count per sense?
- Question: How does the intermediate sense layer capture information not already present in entity embeddings, and why is that information better preserved in a separate sense space?
