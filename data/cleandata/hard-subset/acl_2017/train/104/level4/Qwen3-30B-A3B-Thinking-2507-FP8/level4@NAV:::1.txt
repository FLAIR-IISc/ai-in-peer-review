

Strengths:
* Outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, demonstrating that the proposed framework enhances the joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
* Provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using highly similar input data.
* Offers valuable analysis of the smoothing parameter, addressing the persistent challenge of popularity impact in entity linking.

Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN requires refinement. ALIGN employs a content window size of 10 versus the paper's 5, and a vector dimension of 500 versus 200. It remains unclear whether N(ej) encompasses only entities linking to ej. Given the directed graph (using Wikipedia outlinks), adjacency definition differs from an undirected graph. Since ALIGN defines an entity's context as the set of entities linking to it, ambiguity in N(ej) prevents attributing the score difference on the entity similarity task to specific methodological changes.
* Terminology regarding "mention" is occasionally ambiguous, conflating string types with document-specific mentions. The term "mention embedding" is used, yet embeddings appear learned only for mention senses.
* The impact of sense disambiguation order cannot be assessed without comparison to alternative unsupervised entity linking approaches.
