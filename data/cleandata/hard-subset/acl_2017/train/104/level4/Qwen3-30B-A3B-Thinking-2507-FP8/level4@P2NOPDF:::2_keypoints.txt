- Title/intro overstate scope: EDL is only part of text-knowledge integration, not the whole problem.  
- Task is well-defined; authors propose a two-step pipeline: mention → mention-sense → entity with joint embeddings.  
- Technical approach is sound and consistent with current embedding practices.  
- Paper lacks clear justification for two-step design versus direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN/Yamada et al.).  
- Unclear what unique information the mention-sense layer captures beyond entity embeddings and why it must be a separate space.  
- Missing specification of how mention senses are defined: context-derived or KB-derived, and whether senses map one-to-one to entities.  
- Need empirical/accounting details: what contextual words exist at mention-sense level but not in entity pages, and average counts per sense.  
- Mechanism for “sense-disambiguated” distributional semantics is under-specified, especially if seeded from entity text (blurs distinction).  
- Baseline claim is incorrect: TAC KBP 2015–2016 EDL tasks and multiple systems are relevant and should be cited/evaluated.  
- Positive: smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is well-motivated and useful.  
- Authors’ rebuttal dismissing KBP as irrelevant is unpersuasive; embedding quality must be validated via task performance.  
- Required revisions: (1) clarify the purpose/necessity of mention-sense layer (Section 5), (2) correct/acknowledge TAC KBP baselines (Section 6), (3) tone down/refine title and introduction.  
- Overall: promising two-step framework but novelty and necessity of the intermediate layer are not convincingly demonstrated, risking it being a minor variation.
