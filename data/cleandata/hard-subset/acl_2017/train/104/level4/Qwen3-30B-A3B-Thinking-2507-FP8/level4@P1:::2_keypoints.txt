- Proposes a two-step EDL approach: mention‚Üísense-specific embeddings, then sense‚Üíentity, claiming novelty with MPME.  
- Primary contribution is the empirical evaluation framework for embedding-based EDL, not a fundamentally new technique.  
- Provides rigorous qualitative and quantitative analysis of word, sense, and entity embeddings beyond simple accuracy metrics.  
- MPME achieves new state-of-the-art on a standard EDL benchmark, outperforming prior embedding-based methods.  
- Introduces a context-aware smoothing parameter that improves disambiguation robustness with little added complexity.  
- Fails to justify why the intermediate ‚Äúsense‚Äù layer is superior to direct mention‚Üíentity mapping.  
- Unclear whether mention sense profiles include additional local context words absent from entity pages, and if so how many and why those can't be added to entity embeddings.  
- If senses are derived from entity pages, the sense‚Üíentity mapping may be bijective, making the sense layer redundant.  
- Incorrectly claims no exact baselines exist; prior EDL benchmarks (e.g., TAC KBP, Hoffart et al., Ceccarelli et al.) should be cited and compared.  
- Disambiguation step lacks technical detail: who determines the number of senses per mention and how senses are created (clustering, manual, KB constraints)?  
- Unclear what informational gain is obtained by a separate sense space versus enriching entity embeddings with context words.  
- Asks authors to specify number of prototypes per mention (fixed vs. dynamic) and relation to prior multi-prototype work (e.g., Neelakantan et al.).  
- Asks whether prototype count (not just multi-prototype design) drives improvements and what the optimal count is.  
- Requests details on how entity profiles are built from Wikipedia and whether they exclude words found only in mention local context.  
- Notes title/introduction overstate scope; smoothing parameter is a positive technical detail but does not compensate for weak novelty justification.
