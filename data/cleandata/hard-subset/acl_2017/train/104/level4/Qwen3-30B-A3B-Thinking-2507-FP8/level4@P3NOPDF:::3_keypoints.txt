- Proposes a unified neural language-modeling approach for entity disambiguation and linking (EDL) integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling.  
- Core idea is well-motivated, elegant, and supports a streamlined, efficient training procedure.  
- Demonstrates consistent but modest empirical improvements over baselines.  
- Builds on Yamada et al. (2016) with meaningful extensions, making it relevant to the EDL community.  
- Strength: conceptually sound unification that is practically advantageous for deployment.  
- Strength: applicable across diverse EDL scenarios with clear and rigorous presentation.  
- Weakness: insufficiently differentiated from Yamada et al.; novelty and specific advances are not clearly justified.  
- Weakness: model description (Section 3, Figure 2) and terminology (e.g., "dictionary" vs. "anchors"/"entity-mention pairs") lack clarity for reproducibility.  
- Critical weakness: no evaluation of MPME embeddings on non-EDL tasks, so broader utility beyond EDL is unproven.  
- Overall: technically solid but falls short on novelty articulation, exposition, and cross-task validation.  
- Recommendation: Minor revision—explicitly contrast with Yamada, clarify model details/terminology, and add at least one non-EDL embedding experiment.  
- Remaining concern: novelty and embedding utility issues must be resolved by revisions to substantiate the contribution.
