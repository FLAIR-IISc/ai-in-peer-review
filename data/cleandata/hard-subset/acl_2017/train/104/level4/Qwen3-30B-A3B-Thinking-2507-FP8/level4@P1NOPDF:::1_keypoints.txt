- Model outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, indicating more effective joint text and knowledge representations.  
- Direct comparison with a closely related approach using identical input data strengthens result validity.  
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter is valuable given persistent challenges from entity popularity.  
- ALIGN comparison needs clarification: context window sizes differ (10 vs 5) and vector dimensions differ (500 vs 200), which may confound results.  
- Unclear whether N(e_j) counts only entities linking to e_j (directed) or uses undirected adjacency; this implementation detail could explain the performance gap.  
- Terminology around "mention" is ambiguous—embeddings are learned for mention senses, not raw mention strings or document-specific mentions.  
- No comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods, leaving the impact of sense disambiguation order undetermined.  
- Joint learning is promising, but methodological inconsistencies and missing benchmarks prevent definitive attribution of advantages to the proposed framework.
