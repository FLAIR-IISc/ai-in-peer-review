- Proposes MPME: learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities for disambiguation.  
- Primary contribution: a novel multi-sense representation framework integrating textual and knowledge-based signals; secondary: empirical SOTA results on entity linking.  
- Limitation: authors do not demonstrate or claim usefulness of embeddings beyond entity disambiguation.  

- Strength: Novel framework that resolves mention ambiguity via multiple embeddings, improving over single-vector approaches and heuristic methods.  
- Strength: Joint modeling of text and KB entities is technically sound and directly addresses core semantic representation challenges.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation (e.g., 78.4 F1 on AIDA-CoNLL), qualitative sense distinctions, and ablations showing the necessity of joint modeling.  
- Strength: Clear, straightforward training using language-modeling objectives and practical implementation choices (e.g., Aho-Corasick) that aid reproducibility.  

- Weakness: Insufficient novelty relative to Yamada et al. (2016); the paper does not clearly explain how it advances beyond prior multi-sense EL work.  
- Weakness: Unclear exposition in Section 3—ambiguous "dictionary of mentions", unexplained Figure 2 notation, and redundant terminology confuse readers.  
- Weakness: Narrow evaluation scope—only entity linking is tested, so broader claims about embedding utility remain unproven.  

- Question: Please clarify the specific technical differences and novelty relative to Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Question: Define and disambiguate "dictionary of mentions" vs. "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" and standardize terminology relating to the KB.  
- Question: Provide evaluations of MPME embeddings on non-EL tasks (e.g., coreference, SRL) or temper claims about general applicability.
