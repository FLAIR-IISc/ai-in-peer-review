- Outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, indicating improved joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
- Provides a direct comparison with a closely related method using highly similar input data.
- Offers useful analysis of the smoothing parameter to mitigate popularity bias in entity linking.
- ALIGN comparison is confounded by different context window sizes (10 vs 5), vector dimensions (500 vs 200), and an unclear definition of N(ej) given directed vs undirected graph semantics, so score differences are hard to attribute.
- Terminology around "mention" is ambiguous, conflating string types with document-specific mentions, and "mention embedding" appears misused since embeddings are learned only for mention senses.
- The effect of sense disambiguation order cannot be evaluated without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking approaches.
