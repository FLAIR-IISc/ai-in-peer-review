- Framework outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting joint text and knowledge learning improves representations.  
- Direct comparison using nearly identical input data strengthens result validity.  
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter is informative; entity popularity effects remain a persistent challenge.  
- ALIGN comparison lacks rigor: different context window sizes and vector dimensions may confound results.  
- Unclear definition of N(e_j) (incoming-only vs. outgoing/undirected adjacency) obscures whether gains stem from model or implementation differences.  
- Ambiguous graph handling (directed vs. treated as undirected) further clouds the ALIGN comparison.  
- Terminology around "mention embedding" is inconsistent—paper conflates string-level mentions with sense-specific/contextual embeddings.  
- It is unclear whether embeddings represent lexical forms or context-dependent senses, complicating interpretation.  
- Impact of sense disambiguation order is under-evaluated; no comparisons to other unsupervised disambiguation heuristics.  
- Smoothing analysis is a strength but cannot offset methodological ambiguities that weaken the central claim.  
- Recommended fixes: clarify N(e_j)/graph handling and hyperparameters to match ALIGN, distinguish "mention" vs. "mention sense" terminology, and add unsupervised-baseline comparisons to isolate ordering effects.
