- Task & scope: Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking to English Wikipedia, but title/intro overstate scope — EDL is only one part of text-knowledge bridging.  
- Approach summary: Uses a two-step pipeline via an intermediate "mention senses" layer: (1) mention → mention-sense, (2) mention-sense → entity, with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Technical quality: Method is clearly presented and consistent with modern embedding/deep-learning practices; alternative technical tweaks unlikely to yield large gains.  
- Main conceptual concern: Paper fails to justify why the two-step mention-sense approach is superior to direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN/Yamada et al.) despite better empirical results.  
- Entity profiles: Built from neighboring entities in a relatedness graph using word-level semantic relatedness of Wikipedia pages (skip-gram embeddings).  
- Word profiles: Use standard distributional semantics without any sense disambiguation.  
- Mention-sense profiles: Claimed to use sense-disambiguated distributional semantics from local context and neighboring mentions, but implementation/details are insufficiently described.  
- Critical gap/questions: How are mention senses defined/differentiated and why require a separate embedding space? If senses are KB-derived, how are sense profiles constructed and how do they differ from entity profiles?  
- Specific clarifying questions needed: Are sense profiles seeded from entity page text? What unique words do sense profiles capture that entity pages do not? How many such words per sense on average? Could entity profiles be augmented instead of creating a separate sense space?  
- Required revision: Section 5 must explicitly address the above sense-definition and necessity questions — this is the paper’s key contribution and current weakness.  
- Incorrect novelty claim: Statement that this is the “first” work on mention ambiguity is false — recent TAC KBP EDL evaluations include multiple baselines/systems.  
- Positive point: The smoothing parameter choice in Section 6.4.2 is well thought-out.  
- Rebuttal weakness: Authors’ rebuttal dismissing TAC KBP as irrelevant is inadequate — embedding quality must be justified by downstream application performance, not just conceptual elegance.
