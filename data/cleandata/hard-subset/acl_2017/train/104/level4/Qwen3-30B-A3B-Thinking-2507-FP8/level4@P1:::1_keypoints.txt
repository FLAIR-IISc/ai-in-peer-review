- Proposes MPME: learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and KB entities; contributions are the multi-prototype embeddings, an LM-based disambiguation method, and SOTA entity linking results.  
- Strong empirical results: +1.8% F1 over ALIGN on AIDA-CoNLL, showing joint text–KB multi-prototype embeddings capture semantic ambiguity effectively.  
- Rigorous direct-baseline comparison: uses identical Wikipedia graph, entity types, and splits so gains appear due to the framework rather than data differences.  
- Useful hyperparameter analysis: smoothing parameter (α) experiments reveal how entity popularity affects embeddings and provide practical guidance.  
- Incomparable baseline details: ALIGN hyperparameters (context window, vector dim) are unreported and N(e_j) is ambiguously defined, making it unclear whether gains are from the model or implementation choices.  
- Terminology inconsistency: paper uses “mention embedding” generically despite learning embeddings per sense, contradicting the title and risking confusion.  
- Unproven disambiguation-order advantage: no comparison to unsupervised/graph-based EL methods, so claimed benefit of disambiguation order is not demonstrated.  
- Question: clarify N(e_j) in Eq.(3)—does it include only entities linking to e_j (ALIGN) or all entities via outlinks, and how does that affect graph directionality relative to ALIGN?  
- Question: revise terminology to explicitly state embeddings are learned per sense (not per mention string).  
- Additional comment: the main contribution is the multi-prototype embedding framework; the disambiguation pipeline’s novelty is overstated and baseline comparisons need tightening.
