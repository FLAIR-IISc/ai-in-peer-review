- Novel neural approach integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling within a unified neural language modeling framework.  
- Simple training procedure and broad applicability across a wide set of tasks.  
- Empirical evaluation is sound, but performance improvements are modest rather than striking.  
- Novelty/Comparison: Paper does not clearly articulate differences and contributions relative to Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016).  
- Clarity/Terminology: Section 3 and Figure 2 need clearer exposition; terms like "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" are ambiguous or redundant.  
- Evaluation Scope: MPME is only evaluated on entity linking/disambiguation; need tests showing embeddings are competitive on non-KB semantic tasks (e.g., parsing, coreference, SRL), especially for entity mentions.
