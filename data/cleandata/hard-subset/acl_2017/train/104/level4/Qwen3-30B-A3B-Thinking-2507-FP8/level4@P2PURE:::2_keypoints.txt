- Paper addresses Entity Disambiguation and Linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, but title/introduction overstate scope of "bridging text and knowledge."  
- Method is sound: introduces an intermediate "mention sense" layer and a two-step mapping (mention → mention sense → entity).  
- Learns joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities via a single objective; approach is clear and follows embedding best practices.  
- Main critique: paper fails to justify why a two-step mention-sense intermediate is better than a direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Empirically MPME/SPME outperform direct methods (Table 2), but the paper does not explain the reason for this improvement.  
- Needs to clarify what additional information mention senses provide compared to entities.  
- Must specify how mention senses are defined, differentiated, and constrained (who determines number of senses per mention string).  
- Clarify whether senses derived from the KB are bijectively mapped to entities (one sense per entity).  
- If sense profiles use entity page text as seeds, explain what unique information mention senses capture that entity pages do not.  
- Report how many distinct seed/associated words exist per mention sense on average.  
- Justify necessity of a separate mention-sense space versus augmenting entity representations (e.g., enriching Wikipedia pages).  
- Section 5 should be revised to explicitly address these points, as they constitute the paper’s primary novel contribution.  
- Claiming novelty ("first work") is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL evaluations and multiple systems are relevant baselines.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is well-conceived.  
- Post-response: reviewer remains unconvinced by authors’ dismissal of KBP relevance and stresses that embedding quality must be validated by downstream performance.
