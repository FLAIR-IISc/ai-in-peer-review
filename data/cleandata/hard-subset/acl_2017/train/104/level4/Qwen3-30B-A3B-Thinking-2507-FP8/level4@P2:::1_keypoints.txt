- Summary: Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention via joint text–KB modeling, achieving SOTA entity linking and analyzing smoothing’s effect on popularity bias.
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art on AIDA, outperforming ALIGN by 1.5 F1, demonstrating the effectiveness of joint text–knowledge embeddings.
- Strength: Thorough analysis of smoothing parameter α shows higher α reduces bias toward popular entities.
- Strength: Robust experimental design with direct comparisons to ALIGN on identical AIDA data and qualitative embedding analysis.
- Weakness: Comparison with ALIGN is confounded by differing context window sizes, vector dimensions, and an unclear neighborhood definition N(e_j), so improvements may not be isolated to the method.
- Weakness: Terminology is ambiguous—“mention” is not distinguished between mention type and instance, and “mention embedding” is misleading because the model learns sense embeddings.
- Weakness: Lacks unsupervised baselines; disambiguation is only evaluated within supervised entity linking, limiting assessment of the disambiguation module alone.
- Question: Clarify the definition of neighborhood N(e_j) (entities linked to vs. linking to e_j) and whether the Wikipedia link graph is treated as directed or undirected.
