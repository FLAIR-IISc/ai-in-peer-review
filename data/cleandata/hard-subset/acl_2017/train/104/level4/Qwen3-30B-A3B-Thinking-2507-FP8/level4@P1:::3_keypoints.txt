- Summary: MPME learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities and adds a language-model-based disambiguation, claiming SOTA entity linking.  
- Strength: Novel integration framework that unifies textual context and knowledge-base entities in a neural language-modeling approach to address mention ambiguity.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation on standard entity linking benchmarks with both qualitative sense examples and quantitative precision/recall gains.  
- Strength: Broad applicability and simplicity, easily adaptable to other NLP tasks (e.g., relation extraction, knowledge graph completion).  
- Weakness: Insufficiently distinguished from Yamada et al. (2016); unclear whether the multi-prototype aspect is a fundamental novelty or a minor extension.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology and exposition (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs. “dictionary of entity-mention pairs,” unclear Figure 2), obscuring model mechanics.  
- Weakness: Incomplete evaluation scope—no validation on non-entity tasks (e.g., parsing, coreference, SRL) to demonstrate general embedding utility.  
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to isolate the contribution of multi-prototype embeddings versus joint text–knowledge modeling.  
- Question: How exactly does MPME improve upon Yamada et al. (2016) architecturally and in performance—please clarify key technical distinctions.  
- Question: Can the authors provide results on standard non-entity tasks (e.g., a parser or SRL benchmark) to show embeddings are competitive beyond entity-centric applications?
