- Strength: Presents a sound, unified neural language-modeling idea that integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling.  
- Strength: Simple neural learning approach with a straightforward training procedure that supports many applications.  
- Strength: Broad applicability and solid empirical evaluation, though performance gains are modest.  
- Strength: Formally clear presentation overall and introduces novel elements beyond Yamada et al. (2016).  

- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to prior work and insufficiently distinguished from Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Weakness: Clarity issues in writing, especially Section 3, unexplained details in Figure 2, and redundant/unclear terminology (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs").  
- Weakness: Empirical gains are interesting but not striking.  
- Suggestion: Evaluation is narrow—MPME is only tested on entity linking/disambiguation; authors should show embeddings perform comparably on broader semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) where word embeddings are commonly used.
