- Title/intro overstate scope; paper actually focuses on Entity Disambiguation and Linking (EDL).
- Proposed method: two-step mapping (mentions → mention senses → entities) with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities optimized by a single objective.
- Technical approach is clear and follows embedding-based best practices; alternative architectures unlikely to yield large improvements.
- Primary concern: paper does not explain why a two-step mention-sense approach is superior to direct mention→entity mapping despite empirical gains.
- Missing detail: unclear distinction between mention sense profiles and entity profiles; the "language model" used for sense disambiguation is not described.
- Missing detail: how mention senses are defined and how many senses per mention are determined; is there a one-to-one mapping between senses and entities?
- Missing detail: whether sense profiles are seeded from entity page text and what additional information they capture that entity profiles do not.
- Request quantification: how many words per sense are absent from corresponding entity pages, and how separating into a mention-sense space compares to augmenting entity representations.
- Request to update Section 5 to explicitly address the above representation and design questions.
- Incorrect claim: Section 6’s statement that no exact baselines exist is false—TAC KBP EDL evaluations (recent years) provide relevant baselines.
- Positive note: the smoothing parameter introduced in Section 6.4.2 is appreciated.
- Post-response: reviewer remains unconvinced by authors’ dismissal of TAC KBP and stresses embeddings must be evaluated by application-driven downstream performance.
