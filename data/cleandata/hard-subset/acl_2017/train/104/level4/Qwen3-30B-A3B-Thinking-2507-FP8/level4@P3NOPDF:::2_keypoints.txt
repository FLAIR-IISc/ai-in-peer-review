- Paper addresses EDL by mapping mentions → mention-senses → Wikipedia entities with joint embeddings, but the title/intro overstate its scope (EDL is part of broader text-knowledge integration).  
- Technical approach is sound and consistent with current embedding-based methods.  
- Paper lacks justification for the two-step (mention-sense) approach versus direct one-step mapping; improvement in Table 2 is unexplained.  
- Unclear how mention senses encode information absent from entity-level representations and what unique contextual/linguistic features senses capture.  
- Ambiguity in how senses are defined (fixed number per mention? KB-driven? bijection to entities?).  
- Unclear whether sense profiles exclude words from entity pages, how many such unique words exist per sense, and why they warrant a separate sense space.  
- Authors should explicitly clarify the informational distinction between sense and entity profiles (Section 5).  
- Claim that “there are no exact baselines” is incorrect; TAC KBP 2015–2016 EDL systems provide relevant baselines and should be compared.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a thoughtful, noteworthy contribution.  
- Authors’ rebuttal dismissing KBP as irrelevant is inadequate; embedding quality must be validated on downstream EDL benchmarks.  
- Core gaps remain: why the two-step approach works better and how it outperforms existing EDL systems.  
- Recommendations: define sense vs. entity information clearly, justify the two-step design concisely, and compare results to TAC KBP to contextualize performance.  
- Overall: method is technically plausible but the sense-layer innovation and baseline landscape are obscured, leaving novelty and significance unclear without revisions.
