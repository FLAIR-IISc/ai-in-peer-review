1. Presents a compelling joint text-and-knowledge representation framework that outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking.  
2. Direct comparison with ALIGN using identical input data is a strength, providing a clear baseline.  
3. Analysis of the smoothing parameter offers useful insights for mitigating entity popularity bias.  

4. Comparison with ALIGN is inconsistent due to different window sizes, vector dimensions, and unclear neighborhood (in-neighbors vs out-neighbors) definitions.  
5. The paper ambiguously uses "mention" for both string-level types and document-specific instances, obscuring what "mention embeddings" represent.  
6. The impact of the proposed sense disambiguation order is not evaluated against unsupervised or graph-based baselines, leaving its contribution unquantified.  

7. Re-run ALIGN with identical parameters (window size 10, dim 500) and clarify/justify whether N(e_j) uses in-neighbors or out-neighbors and graph directedness.  
8. Revise terminology to distinguish mention types vs instances and use "sense embeddings" where appropriate.  
9. Add an ablation comparing the sense disambiguation order to simple unsupervised baselines (e.g., random or greedy) to isolate its effect.  

10. Core idea is promising and publication-worthy if methodological inconsistencies and terminology issues are addressed; emphasize the smoothing-parameter analysis in the revision.
