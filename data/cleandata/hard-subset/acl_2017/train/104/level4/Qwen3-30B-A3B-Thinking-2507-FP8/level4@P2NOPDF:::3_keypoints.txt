- Proposes a unified neural framework integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling for entity disambiguation/linking.  
- Uses a simple training procedure that enables broad applicability across tasks.  
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent performance gains, though improvements are incremental.  
- Builds on Yamada et al. (2016) but introduces novel elements that need clearer differentiation.  
- Strength: conceptually sound, scalable, and practically advantageous for deployment.  
- Strength: thorough empirical results that support the method’s efficacy.  
- Weakness: clarity/exposition problems in Section 3 and Figure 2; key terminology is ambiguous (e.g., different “dictionaries,” “text anchors,” “types”).  
- Weakness: insufficient articulation and explicit comparison of novelty relative to Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Weakness: evaluation scope limited to EL/ED tasks; MPME embeddings are not validated on non-EL NLP tasks.  
- Suggestion: demonstrate MPME embeddings on at least one non-EL task involving mentions (e.g., coreference, mention-based classification) and compare to standard embeddings.  
- Revision request: revise Section 3 and Figure 2 to define terms (e.g., dictionaries) and remove redundancy.  
- Revision request: add a dedicated subsection clearly contrasting contributions with Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Note: authors acknowledged need for clearer exposition and Yamada comparison but did not address embedding evaluation.  
- Recommendation: minor revision contingent on clarifications and expanded evaluation to strengthen the paper’s perceived contribution.
