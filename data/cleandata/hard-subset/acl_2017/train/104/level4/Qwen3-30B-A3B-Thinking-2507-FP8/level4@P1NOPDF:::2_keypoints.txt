- Paper tackles entity linking (EL) to English Wikipedia but overstates scope relative to broader text-knowledge integration.  
- Proposes two-step approach: mentions → mention senses → entities, with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Technical approach is well-structured and consistent with embedding best practices.  
- Primary concern: no justification for two-step process over direct one-step mapping (e.g., ALIGN).  
- Empirical result: MPME/SPME outperform direct mapping (Table 2) but paper fails to explain why.  
- Critical gap: unclear definition and construction of mention senses and how many senses per mention string are determined.  
- If senses are derived from the KB, this may create a one-to-one mapping with entities—paper does not address this potential bijection.  
- Unclear whether sense profiles are built from entity page text as seeds and what extra information sense space contributes beyond entity profiles.  
- Question whether words specific to senses but absent from entity pages exist in sufficient quantity to justify a separate mention-sense space.  
- Paper should explicitly clarify the mention-sense mechanism in Section 5; this distinction may be the key novelty but is currently obscured.  
- Section 6’s claim of being the “first” to address mention ambiguity is incorrect—TAC KBP EDL evaluations (2015–2016) addressed this task and should be cited.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a well-motivated contribution.  
- Post-response critique: authors’ focus on embedding quality is insufficient—embeddings must be validated by downstream EL performance (e.g., KBP-style evaluations) to demonstrate practical benefit.
