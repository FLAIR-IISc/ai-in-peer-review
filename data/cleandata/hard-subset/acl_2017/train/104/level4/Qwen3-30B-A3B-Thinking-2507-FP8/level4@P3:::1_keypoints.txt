1. Proposes MPME: multi-prototype mention embeddings that jointly model text and KB entities, plus an LM-based disambiguation method, claiming novelty mainly in the embedding model and SOTA EL results.  
2. Strong empirical validation: MPME outperforms baselines (including ALIGN) and multi-prototype embeddings improve downstream EL (e.g., +2.1% F1 on AIDA).  
3. Smoothing parameter (α) analysis shows reducing popularity bias improves rare-entity performance (e.g., +3.7% F1 for low-frequency entities).  
4. Comparison with ALIGN is generally rigorous (same input data/benchmark) and supports the benefit of joint text-knowledge modeling.  
5. ALIGN comparison is unclear due to setup differences (context window, vector dims) and ambiguous neighborhood definition (incoming vs outgoing edges) that may confound results.  
6. Terminology ambiguity: "mention" is used inconsistently for types vs instances, confusing embedding learning (senses) with disambiguation (instances).  
7. Missing unsupervised/disambiguation baselines and ablations, so it’s unclear whether gains stem from the disambiguation method or improved embeddings.  
8. Question: Clarify N(e_j)—is it entities linking to e_j (incoming) or linked from e_j (outgoing), and how this choice affects embeddings?  
9. Question: Provide results comparing MPME embeddings without disambiguation (e.g., fixed most-frequent sense) to the full pipeline to justify the disambiguation claim.  
10. Suggestion: Better contextualize embedding evaluations against prior multi-prototype work (e.g., Neelakantan et al. 2014).  
11. Minor: Fix typos in references (e.g., "Efficie t" in Aho & Corasick).  
12. Recommendation: Emphasize the smoothing parameter analysis more prominently in the discussion.
