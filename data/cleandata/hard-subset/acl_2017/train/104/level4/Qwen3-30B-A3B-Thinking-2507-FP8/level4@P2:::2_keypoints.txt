- Summary: Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense-specific mention embeddings via joint text+KB modeling and an efficient disambiguation method, achieving SOTA on entity linking benchmarks.

- Strength: Clear technical architecture—joint training of word, mention-sense, and entity embeddings with a modular sense-generation→disambiguation pipeline.

- Strength: Practical disambiguation—context+mention-sense method is efficient and yields strong empirical results (e.g., 86.2% F1 on AIDA-CoNLL), suitable for real-world pipelines.

- Strength: High-quality embeddings—multi-prototype design captures meaningful semantics and resolves ambiguity better than single-prototype baselines.

- Weakness: Misrepresented novelty—claiming to be first to handle mention ambiguity is inaccurate; prior comparable systems (e.g., TAC KBP 2016) and benchmarks are not cited or compared.

- Weakness: Insufficient justification for two-step design—paper does not convincingly show why an intermediate mention-sense layer is necessary over direct mention→entity mapping, nor how senses encode information not present in entity embeddings.

- Weakness: Underdeveloped sense definition—missing details on how senses are generated, number of senses per mention, differentiation criteria, and whether sense profiles contain information absent from entity profiles.

- Question to authors: For a mention like “Paris,” do sense-defining context words (e.g., “Eiffel Tower”) not appear on the Wikipedia entity page; if they do/don’t, how many such context words exist on average and why not integrate them directly into entity embeddings?
