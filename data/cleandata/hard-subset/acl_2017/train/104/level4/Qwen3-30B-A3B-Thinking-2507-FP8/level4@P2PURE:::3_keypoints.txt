- Strength: Proposes a simple neural approach that jointly models entities, mentions, and senses within a unified language-modeling framework, showing broad (though modest) empirical performance.  
- Strength: Integration of entity, mention, and sense modeling is a noteworthy contribution.  
- Weakness: Lacks sufficient novelty and some clarity; discussion does not always match technical depth and reported gains are not striking.  
- Key clarity issue: Section 3 and Figure 2 are unclear and terminology is redundant/confusing (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs. “dictionary of entity-mention pairs”).  
- Key related-work issue: Insufficiently differentiated from Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016); paper must clearly state technical distinctions.  
- Additional concern: Evaluation is limited to entity linking/disambiguation; need results on broader semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show MPME embeddings are not inferior for non-entity-dependent tasks.
