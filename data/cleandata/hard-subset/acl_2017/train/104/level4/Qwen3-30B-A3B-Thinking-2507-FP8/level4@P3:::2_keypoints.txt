1. Proposes MPME to jointly learn word, entity, and multi-prototype mention embeddings; main contribution is high-quality embeddings and EL performance rather than architectural novelty.  
2. Embeddings validated qualitatively, quantitatively, and achieve SOTA EL (92.1 F1 on AIDA-CoNLL).  
3. Sense-based disambiguation (two-step) outperforms direct mention-to-entity mapping; ablation supports its necessity.  
4. Scalable, efficient disambiguation implementation with competitive runtime (15.2s per 1k mentions).  
5. Lacks justification for why the intermediate sense layer is necessary and how sense embeddings differ in content from entity embeddings.  
6. Misrepresents baselines—prior EL benchmarks/systems (e.g., TAC KBP 2015–2016, Kulkarni/Hoffart) should be acknowledged and compared appropriately.  
7. Unclear sense-creation procedure (predefined vs. dynamic clustering) and how number of senses is determined, harming reproducibility.  
8. Question: How does the number of senses per mention affect disambiguation accuracy and is there a point where additional senses degrade performance?  
9. Title overstates scope; work focuses on entity disambiguation within text-knowledge integration.  
10. Remove misformatted/irrelevant Aho-Corasick references.  
11. The smoothing parameter is a practical, underemphasized contribution and should be highlighted.  
12. Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify two-step necessity and correct baseline comparisons.
