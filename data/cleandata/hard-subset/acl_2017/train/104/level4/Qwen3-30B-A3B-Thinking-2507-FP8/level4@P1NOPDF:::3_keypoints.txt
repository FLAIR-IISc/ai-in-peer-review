- Proposes a novel neural framework that unifies entity, mention, and sense modeling within a neural language modeling approach.  
- Method is conceptually simple, broadly applicable across tasks, and yields empirically promising (though modest) performance gains.  
- Primary novelty concern: insufficiently differentiated from prior work (e.g., Yamada et al., CoNLL 2016); needs explicit comparison to justify contribution.  
- Prose and exposition issues: Section 3 is unclear, Figure 2 lacks key explanations, and terminology (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs") appears redundant and imprecise.  
- Empirical gap: claim that MPME improves word embeddings is unsupported because embeddings are not validated on non-entity NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL); comparisons to simpler baselines are needed.  
- Discussion is weaker than technical content: fails to analyze implications or limitations in depth, and the modest gains require more thorough analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strengthen articulation of novelty, improve clarity of methodology, and expand validation of embeddings to bolster the contribution.
