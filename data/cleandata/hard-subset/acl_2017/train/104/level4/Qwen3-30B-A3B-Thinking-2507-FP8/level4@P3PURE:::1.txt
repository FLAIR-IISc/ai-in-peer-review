

- Strengths:
* Outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, suggesting the proposed framework effectively improves jointly learned text and knowledge representations.
* Provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using identical input data.
* Analyzes the smoothing parameter to address the persistent issue of entity popularity bias in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN requires clarification. ALIGN uses a content window size of 10 versus the paper’s 5, and vector dimensions of 500 versus 200. Crucially, it is unclear whether $N(e_j)$ includes only entities linking to $e_j$ (as in ALIGN’s context definition), and whether adjacency in the directed Wikipedia outlink graph is interpreted as undirected. These differences confound the interpretation of performance gains on the entity similarity task.
* Terminology regarding "mention" is ambiguous: it is unclear whether "mention" refers to a string type or a specific instance in a document. The phrase "mention embedding" is used, yet embeddings are learned only for mention *senses*, not for raw mention strings.
* The impact of sense disambiguation order cannot be isolated without comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
