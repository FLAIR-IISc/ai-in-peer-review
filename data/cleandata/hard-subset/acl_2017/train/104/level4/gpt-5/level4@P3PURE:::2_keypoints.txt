- Paper addresses entity disambiguation/linking to Wikipedia but title/introduction overstate scope of "bridging text and knowledge."
- Proposed model: two-step pipeline (mention → mention-sense → entity) with embeddings for words, mention-senses, and entities trained jointly.
- Technical description is clear and follows standard deep-learning/embedding practices.
- Main weakness: paper does not explain why the intermediate mention-sense layer outperforms a direct mention→entity mapping (ALIGN), despite empirical gains in Table 2.
- Reviewer’s interpretation: entities use a graph-based profile from Wikipedia pages; words use standard distributional profiles; mention-senses use context-based profiles, but the paper lacks sufficient detail on this.
- Requests for clarification: how mention-senses are created/differentiated and how the number of senses per mention is determined.
- Asks whether mention-sense↔entity mapping is bijective and whether sense profiles are seeded from entity texts.
- Wants explicit explanation of what information mention-senses capture that entities do not, including whether extra words come from contexts not present on entity pages and average counts of such words.
- Questions necessity and power of a separate mention-sense space versus augmenting entity representations with context words.
- Requests updating Section 5 to make the above mechanisms and assumptions explicit.
- Corrects claim in Section 6: this is not the first work on mention ambiguity integration—TAC KBP EDL evaluations are relevant baselines.
- Positive note: smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a good idea.
- Post-response: reviewer remains unconvinced by authors’ dismissal of KBP relevance and insists application-level evaluation is required to prove embedding “goodness.”
