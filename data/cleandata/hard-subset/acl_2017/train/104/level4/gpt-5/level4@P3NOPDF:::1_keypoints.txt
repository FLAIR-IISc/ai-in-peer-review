- Proposes a joint framework for learning text and knowledge representations, evaluated on supervised entity linking and entity similarity, compared to ALIGN, and analyzes a smoothing parameter for popularity bias.
- Strength: Shows improvements over ALIGN on supervised entity linking, indicating stronger text–knowledge representations for EL.
- Strength: Provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar data, helping contextualize contributions.
- Strength: Includes useful analysis of a smoothing parameter addressing popularity bias and its effect on performance.
- Weakness: Comparison fairness is questionable because key hyperparameters differ (e.g., content window size and embedding dimensionality), complicating attribution of gains.
- Weakness: Unclear definition of graph neighborhood N(ej) (inlinks, outlinks, or symmetrized), which can substantially affect learned representations and comparisons to ALIGN.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in the term “mention” and “mention embedding” — unclear whether embeddings are for surface forms, token occurrences, senses/entities, or multiple types.
- Weakness: Impact of disambiguation order is not well assessed; no ablations or comparisons to show its contribution.
- Clarification request: Specify exactly how N(ej) is defined and whether it matches ALIGN’s context (inlinks vs. outlinks vs. symmetrized).
- Clarification request: Confirm whether adjacency is treated as directed or undirected during training and evaluation.
- Clarification request: Report the content window definition and whether it includes both entity and word tokens, and how it compares to ALIGN’s windowing.
- Clarification request: Clarify what is embedded (surface-form types, token-level mentions, sense-specific mentions, entities) and how multiple embeddings are tied or regularized.
- Clarification request: State whether the same graph and training data as ALIGN are used for entity similarity; if not, enumerate differences and expected effects.
- Suggestion: Match key hyperparameters with ALIGN (window size, embedding dimensionality) and report results for both default and ALIGN-matched settings.
- Suggestion: Provide sensitivity analyses for window size and embedding dimension to disentangle modeling gains from capacity/context effects.
- Suggestion: Run entity similarity evaluations under aligned neighborhood definitions and report ablations for outlinks-only and symmetrized graphs.
- Suggestion: Expand smoothing analysis across datasets or entity-frequency subsets and report precision/recall trade-offs to characterize popularity bias.
- Suggestion: Evaluate disambiguation order via ablations (randomized, heuristic, beam/joint inference) and compare to unsupervised EL baselines.
- Suggestion: Improve clarity and reproducibility by defining neighborhoods, windows, training objectives, detailing preprocessing, and releasing code or pseudo-code.
- Overall: Promising empirical gains and analysis, but unclear comparisons to ALIGN and ambiguous terminology weaken causal claims; addressing neighborhood definition, hyperparameter alignment, and disambiguation ablations would strengthen the paper.
