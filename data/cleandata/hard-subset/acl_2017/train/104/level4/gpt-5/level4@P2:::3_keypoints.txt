- Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings for ambiguous mentions by jointly embedding context words and KB entities in a shared space.
- Introduces an efficient language-model–based disambiguation module to assign mention occurrences to specific sense embeddings at inference.
- Evaluates embeddings qualitatively and quantitatively and applies them to entity linking, reporting competitive and sometimes state-of-the-art results.

- Jointly modeling mentions, entities, and words is a sound idea that addresses surface-form ambiguity and naturally supports downstream entity-aware tasks.
- The training objective and architecture are simple and pragmatic, making the approach easy to implement and scale.
- Produces representations with broad applicability beyond EL (e.g., retrieval, QA, ER), increasing potential impact.
- Provides qualitative analyses and competitive EL results, with some benchmarks reaching state of the art.

- Limited novelty relative to prior work (notably Yamada et al. 2016); technical differences are not clearly delineated and contribution feels incremental.
- Clarity and terminology problems: terms like “dictionary of mentions,” “entity–mention pairs,” “text anchors,” and “types for annotated text anchors” are ill-defined and Figure 2 is underexplained.
- Evaluation scope is narrow (focused on EL); lacks evidence that word embeddings do not degrade on standard NLP tasks or intrinsic/extrinsic benchmarks.
- Magnitude of gains is uneven and not striking; needs more ablations to isolate the impact of multiple prototypes, disambiguation, and KB vs. text contributions.
- Exposition issues hinder readability and reproducibility, especially in the model and disambiguation descriptions.

- Precisely how does this differ from Yamada et al. (2016) in objectives, architectures, training data, negative sampling, and disambiguation—please provide controlled comparisons.
- Define and clarify “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity–mention pairs,” “text anchors,” and “types for annotated text anchors,” and state how they are constructed and related.
- Clarify Figure 2: label components and data flows, explain context sampling, negative generation, gradient flows, and whether the disambiguation model is trained jointly or separately.
- How is the number of sense prototypes per mention chosen (fixed or adaptive), what is the computational overhead, and how does performance vary with this hyperparameter?
- Can you report standard word embedding evaluations or extrinsic task results (e.g., parsing, NER, SRL) to show MPME’s word representations do not regress?
- Provide error analysis: which mentions benefit most from multiple prototypes (highly polysemous vs. rare) and where does the system fail (limited context, tail entities)?
