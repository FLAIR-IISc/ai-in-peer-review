- Proposes MPME: multi-prototype mention embeddings that jointly model textual context and KB entities in a unified vector space.
- Adds a lightweight LM-based disambiguation component that assigns mention occurrences to learned sense prototypes.
- Evaluates qualitatively and quantitatively, reporting state-of-the-art results on entity linking.

- Conceptually clean integration of text and KB supervision, explicitly modeling mention-level polysemy.
- Simple, efficient learning and inference that scales without complex global inference or heavy feature engineering.
- Solid empirical EL performance with consistent improvements despite a simple architecture.
- Qualitative analyses (case studies, visualizations) support that learned prototypes capture mention ambiguity.
- Training objective unifies words, entities, and mentions, making representations broadly reusable across entity-aware tasks.

- Limited novelty relative to prior joint text–KB embedding work (e.g., Yamada et al., 2016); differences not sharply delineated.
- Exposition and terminology are confusing in places (Section 3, Figure 2, multiple overlapping terms), obscuring clarity.
- Narrow evaluation focused on EL; unclear whether joint training harms or preserves general-purpose word embedding quality.
- Modest empirical gains and missing ablations (single vs multi-prototype, text-only vs joint, disambiguation effect, prototype sensitivity).
- Presentation issues in references (duplicates, typos, corrupted citations) reduce polish.

- Precisely compare and contrast with Yamada et al. (2016) and other joint embedding baselines (objective, architecture, negatives, data, disambiguation).
- How is the number of prototypes chosen and initialized; is it fixed, global, or data-driven; how is sense proliferation avoided?
- Clarify disambiguation: exact scoring function, context windowing, inference complexity, and scaling with candidate set size.
- How are rare and unseen mentions handled; is there backoff to single-prototype, surface-form similarity, or priors?
- Provide evaluations showing word embeddings remain competitive on intrinsic benchmarks or an extrinsic non-entity task.
- Add ablations (single vs multi-prototype; text-only vs joint; with/without disambiguation) and an error analysis of failure modes.
- Define precisely “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity–mention pairs,” and “text anchors,” with sources, formats, and roles.

- Overall: solid, practical contribution with competitive results but needs clearer differentiation from related work, stronger ablations, and improved exposition to be a strong accept.
