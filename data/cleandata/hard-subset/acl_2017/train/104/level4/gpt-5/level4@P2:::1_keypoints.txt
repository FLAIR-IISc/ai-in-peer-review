- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense-specific embeddings by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities.  
- Introduces an efficient language-model-based disambiguation procedure to assign mention occurrences to specific sense embeddings.  
- Reports state-of-the-art entity linking results and provides qualitative/quantitative analyses of word, entity, and mention-sense embeddings, including popularity smoothing.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms ALIGN on a standard benchmark, suggesting benefits from joint text–KB representation and multi-prototype mentions.  
- Includes a direct head-to-head comparison with a closely related approach using similar input sources, making the contrast informative.  
- Provides useful analysis of popularity smoothing, illuminating how popularity bias affects entity linking.  
- Fairness of comparison with ALIGN is unclear due to differing hyperparameters and an imprecise definition of the entity neighborhood N(ej), which could affect results.  
- Terminology and representation granularity are ambiguous (mention vs. surface form vs. occurrence vs. sense); mapping between surface forms, sense inventories, and prototypes needs clarification.  
- The disambiguation component’s contribution is not isolated—no comparisons to unsupervised EL/WSD baselines or ablations swapping disambiguation methods.  
- Question: Precisely how is N(ej) defined—inlinks, outlinks, both, and is the graph treated as directed or undirected?  
- Question: Provide a controlled comparison with ALIGN using matched hyperparameters (e.g., window=10, dim=500) and show ALIGN under your settings.  
- Question: Define “mention” in the model (surface string, token occurrence, or sense), whether surface-form embeddings are learned separately, how sense prototypes are induced, how many per form, and whether they’re shared across documents.  
- Question: Can you isolate the disambiguation’s contribution by (a) using your embeddings with a standard prior+context disambiguator and (b) using your disambiguator with ALIGN or other embeddings?  
- Question: Do you compare unsupervised disambiguation to established baselines (prior-based, PageRank/graph-based, WSD-style)? If not, add such comparisons.  
- Suggestion: Report sensitivity curves for popularity smoothing across additional datasets or domains.  
- Suggestion: Clean and de-duplicate corrupted bibliography entries.
