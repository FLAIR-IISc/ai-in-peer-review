Review-
- Strengths:
* Surpasses ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, indicating that the proposed framework enhances jointly learned text and knowledge representations.
* Offers a direct, head-to-head comparison with a closely related method using nearly identical input data.
* The study of the smoothing parameter is informative, as popularity effects are a persistent challenge in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN could be better controlled. ALIGN uses a content window of 10 versus 5 here, and 500-dimensional vectors versus 200 in this paper. It is also unclear whether N(ej) includes only entities that link to ej. The graph is directed and built from Wikipedia outlinks, but is adjacency treated as if the graph were undirected? In ALIGN, an entity’s context is the set of entities that link to it. If N(e_j) differs, the impact on the learned embeddings is hard to isolate and may partly explain the discrepancy on the entity similarity task.
* The term “mention” is sometimes ambiguous: does it denote a surface-form type or a specific occurrence in a document? The phrase “mention embedding” appears, yet embeddings seem to be learned only for mention senses.
* The effect of the sense disambiguation order is difficult to assess without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.

- General Discussion:
