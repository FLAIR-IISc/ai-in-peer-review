- Outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, showing the joint learning framework improves text and knowledge representations.
- Provides a direct, closely matched comparison to a highly related approach using similar input data.
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter is valuable for addressing popularity bias in entity linking.

- Comparison with ALIGN is not fully controlled: different context window (10 vs 5) and embedding dimension (500 vs 200) may confound results.
- Unclear definition of N(e_j) and whether the directed Wikipedia link graph is treated as undirected, making it hard to isolate methodological differences from ALIGN.
- The term “mention” is ambiguous (type vs. occurrence); paper seems to learn only mention-sense embeddings despite referring to “mention embedding.”
- Hard to assess the effect of sense disambiguation order because there are no comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
