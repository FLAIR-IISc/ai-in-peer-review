- Proposes a unified neural LM framework that jointly models entities, mentions (with multiple senses), and words for entity disambiguation/linking.  
- Learns multi-prototype mention embeddings (MPME) by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities.  
- Introduces an efficient language-model–based disambiguation method that uses the learned embeddings.  
- Training procedure is simple and broadly applicable.  
- Contribution: unified framework enabling multi-prototype mention embeddings.  
- Contribution: efficient disambiguation method leveraging the embeddings.  
- Contribution: empirical study showing competitive performance on entity linking/disambiguation.  
- Strength: conceptually sound integration of entities, mentions, and multiple senses that addresses ambiguity.  
- Strength: simple, practical learning setup that is accessible for applications.  
- Strength: solid empirical results that demonstrate viability against strong baselines.  
- Strength: framework has potential broad applicability beyond entity-centric tasks.  
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to prior work (notably Yamada et al., 2016); paper must clearly delineate genuine novelties.  
- Weakness: clarity issues—Section 3 and Figure 2 lack explanations and terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs”) is inconsistent.  
- Weakness: narrow evaluation—need to show that the produced word embeddings are not weaker than standard embeddings on non-entity tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL).  
- Weakness: magnitude of improvement is modest; requires stronger ablations or analysis to contextualize gains and show when the method excels.  
- Question: explicitly enumerate differences from Yamada et al. (modeling, objective, multi-prototype use, disambiguation, data/resources) and provide ablation results.  
- Question: precisely define “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” and relate them to text anchors/annotated anchor types.  
- Question: clarify Figure 2 with detailed captions/text explaining each component and its role in training/disambiguation.  
- Question: provide results or commentary on how MPME word embeddings perform on standard NLP tasks (dependency parsing, coreference, SRL) or plans to evaluate them.  
- Question: provide more error analysis or case studies showing where multi-prototype mention modeling clearly outperforms alternatives.  
- Additional comment: tighten exposition (especially Section 3/Fig. 2); approach is a meaningful extension of prior work though improvements are modest.
