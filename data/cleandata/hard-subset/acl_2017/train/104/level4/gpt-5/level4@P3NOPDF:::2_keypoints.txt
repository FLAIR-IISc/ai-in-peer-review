- Summary: Proposes a two-step EDL pipeline (mention → sense → entity) with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.

- Strength: Clear formulation of the two-step pipeline and joint training.

- Strength: Technically sound use of embedding methods and relatedness graphs.

- Strength: Empirical results indicate MPME/SPME outperform direct mapping (ALIGN).

- Strength: Smoothing parameter is thoughtful and empirically effective.

- Concern: Paper lacks a mechanistic explanation for why the intermediate mention-sense layer yields gains; needs analysis (e.g., sparsity reduction, parameter sharing, denoising).

- Request: Provide ablation/control experiments that vary only the presence of the sense layer while holding candidate generation and data constant.

- Concern: Unclear how mention senses are created, how many senses per surface form, and whether the inventory is induced from text or derived from the KB.

- Request: Clarify whether mention senses biject to entities or map many-to-one/many-to-many, and specify the mapping.

- Request: Describe sense assignment algorithm during training/inference, supervision signals, negative sampling, and regularization.

- Request: Quantify what information mention-sense profiles capture beyond entity pages (e.g., distinctive contextual words per sense and their contribution via ablation).

- Question: If sense profiles use entity text as seeds, justify why entities cannot be augmented instead of creating a separate mention-sense space; propose an experiment replacing the sense layer with enriched entity embeddings.

- Request: Clarify representation learning details: how the entity relatedness graph is constructed/weighted and how word embeddings are trained (corpus, window, settings).

- Request: Provide full training details for mention-sense embeddings, including data generation, context windowing, and handling ambiguous contexts.

- Concern: Explain why the three embedding objectives are weighted equally; provide sensitivity analysis over loss weightings and interaction with the smoothing parameter.

- Evaluation critique: Overlooks relevant TAC KBP EDL work—either compare, map results, or clearly justify infeasibility due to KB/annotation mismatch.

- Request: Provide downstream EDL evaluation with standard P/R/F1 on widely used datasets and error analysis comparing ALIGN vs MPME/SPME (candidate recall, accuracy by mention type/frequency, smoothing impact).

- Reproducibility: Expand Section 5 with details on mention-sense construction, relatedness graph, and training data; release code, embeddings, and preprocessing scripts.

- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer remains unconvinced that excluding TAC KBP is acceptable; stresses embedding quality must be demonstrated on downstream tasks and competitive baselines.

- Overall: Promising and technically competent work centered on the mention-sense idea, but needs clearer justification, detailed methodology, stronger empirical positioning, and additional experiments to substantiate contributions.
