- Proposes MPME: joint learning of multiple sense embeddings per mention with word and entity embeddings, plus a two-step mention→sense→entity disambiguation.  
- Demonstrates improved entity linking performance over ALIGN-style direct mention-to-entity baselines and shows qualitative evidence of coherent representations.  
- Uses a language-model–style disambiguator with smoothing to robustly assign senses from noisy local context.  

Strengths:
- Clear, modular two-step pipeline separating mention-to-sense and sense-to-entity, which is intuitive for handling ambiguity.  
- Strong empirical EL gains for both MPME and SPME variants, supporting multi-prototype modeling.  
- Practical, well-motivated smoothing in the disambiguator that improves robustness.  
- Joint training of words, senses, and entities yields sensible neighborhoods and coherent representations.  
- Good positioning at the intersection of multi-prototype embeddings and knowledge-enhanced embeddings, focusing on mention-level ambiguity.

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient explanation for why the two-step design outperforms one-step approaches; no ablation on capacity vs. architecture.  
- Sense induction/mapping is under-specified: how senses are created, how many per mention, and their relation to entities are unclear.  
- Construction and weighting of the entity relatedness graph and combination of text vs. graph signals are not detailed.  
- Baseline framing is weak: overlooks comparable TAC KBP EDL systems and may understate prior art.  
- Missing implementation details and reproducibility info (objective weights, negative sampling, hyperparams, runtime); references contain duplicates/errors.

Key questions for authors:
- How is the number of senses per mention determined, and can senses map to multiple entities (or vice versa)?  
- What contexts initialize/update sense embeddings and what is the exact disambiguation language-model objective and features?  
- How is the entity relatedness graph constructed and how are graph/text signals combined for entity embeddings?  
- Can you provide ablations isolating the benefit of the sense layer versus matched-parameter one-step models and quantify extra context captured by senses?  
- Can you evaluate or compare to TAC KBP EDL (or justify dataset choices) to better situate results?

Additional comments:
- Recommend reframing to focus on EL with multi-prototype mentions, cleaning duplicate references, and releasing code/embeddings to aid reproducibility.

Overall assessment:
- Promising and empirically effective idea, but needs clearer methodology, more analysis of where gains come from, stronger baselines, and improved reproducibility to be a solid contribution.
