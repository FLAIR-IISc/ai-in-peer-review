- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) but title/introduction overclaim the broader “bridging text and knowledge” scope.  
- Proposed model inserts an intermediate “mention senses” layer, forming a two-stage pipeline: mention → mention-sense → entity.  
- Authors learn joint embeddings for words, mention-senses, and entities under a single objective weighting all three equally.  
- Methodology is well presented and aligns with standard deep-learning/embedding techniques.  
- Main conceptual concern: paper does not justify why a two-step mention-sense pathway should outperform direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN).  
- Empirical results show MPME/SPME outperform ALIGN, but the paper fails to explain what distinct signal mention-senses capture beyond entities.  
- Reviewer’s interpretation: entity profiles are neighbor-based embeddings from a relatedness graph (built from word-level relatedness of Wikipedia pages); please verify and clarify.  
- Reviewer’s interpretation: word profiles are standard distributional vectors, while mention-senses use context-disambiguated profiles — but the paper lacks detail on how senses are generated, distinguished, and how many senses a mention can have.  
- If mention-senses are KB-driven (one sense per entity), clarify what extra information exists at mention-sense level vs. entity level and whether senses are seeded from entity texts.  
- Request statistics/analysis: how many context-discriminative words per mention-sense exist on average, and why maintain a separate mention-sense space instead of augmenting entity representations.  
- Ask authors to explicitly state this interpretation in Section 5 if it reflects the paper’s main novel contribution.  
- Contradiction: claim of “no exact baselines” is incorrect — TAC KBP EDL evaluations and multiple systems exist and should be considered (e.g., KBP 2016).  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a particularly nice idea.  
- Post-rebuttal position: reviewer remains unconvinced that intrinsic embedding quality suffices — downstream task performance is the crucial validation metric.
