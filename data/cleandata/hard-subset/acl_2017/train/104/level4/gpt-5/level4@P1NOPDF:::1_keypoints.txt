- Outperforms ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task, indicating the framework improves jointly learned text and knowledge representations.
- Provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using very similar input data.
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter is useful for addressing popularity bias in entity linking.

- Comparison with ALIGN is not rigorous: different context window sizes (10 vs 5) and embedding dimensionalities (500 vs 200) may confound results.
- Unclear definition of N(ej) and whether it includes only entities that link to ej, complicating interpretation of entity context and learned vectors.
- Ambiguity about graph adjacency (directed vs undirected) and whether ALIGN’s context definition (entities that link to ej) is matched here.
- Terminology ambiguity: “mention” may refer to a string type or a specific document occurrence, and “mention embedding” wording suggests embeddings for mentions though only mention-sense embeddings seem learned.
- Impact of sense disambiguation order is not evaluated against other unsupervised entity linking methods, so its effectiveness is unclear.
- Recommendation: clarify N(ej), align hyperparameters where possible, and compare disambiguation order to unsupervised baselines to strengthen claims.
