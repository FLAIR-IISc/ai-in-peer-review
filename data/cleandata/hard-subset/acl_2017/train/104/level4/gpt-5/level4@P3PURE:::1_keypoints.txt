- Outperforms ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task, suggesting improved joint text–knowledge representations.  
- Provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using very similar input data.  
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter is useful for addressing popularity bias in entity linking.  
- Comparison with ALIGN is not sufficiently controlled: different content window (10 vs 5) and vector dimensionality (500 vs 200) may confound results.  
- Unclear definition of N(e_j) and whether graph adjacency is directed or undirected, making context/neighborhood comparisons with ALIGN ambiguous.  
- Ambiguity in terminology: unclear if “mention” denotes a surface-form type or a specific mention instance; “mention embedding” appears limited to mention senses.  
- Lack of comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods, so the impact of disambiguation order is undetermined.  
- Recommend clarifying N(e_j), graph directionality, the meaning of “mention” vs “mention sense,” and adding unsupervised baselines to improve fairness and interpretability.
