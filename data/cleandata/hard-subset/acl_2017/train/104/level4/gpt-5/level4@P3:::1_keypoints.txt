- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per surface form by jointly modeling text context and KB entities.  
- Uses a language-model-based disambiguation component with a popularity smoothing parameter to assign mention occurrences to sense embeddings and perform entity linking.  
- Evaluates learned word, entity, and mention-sense embeddings qualitatively and quantitatively, showing strong supervised EL performance and outperforming ALIGN.  

- Contribution: joint framework for sense-specific mention embeddings integrating textual context and KB signals.  
- Contribution: simple, efficient LM-based disambiguation with tunable popularity smoothing used for sense induction and EL.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration that the embeddings and disambiguation improve supervised EL over a closely related baseline.  

- Strength: Strong empirical gains over ALIGN on supervised entity linking, using similar supervision sources.  
- Strength: Useful analysis of the popularity smoothing parameter and its effect on disambiguation.  
- Strength: Unified embedding space for words, entities, and mention senses enabling qualitative and quantitative inspection.  
- Strength: Methodological simplicity and implementation efficiency attractive for practitioners.  

- Weakness: Comparison to ALIGN may be unfair due to differing hyperparameters (window size, embedding dimensionality) and unclear impact of capacity/context differences.  
- Weakness: Unclear definition of entity context N(ej) (incoming vs outgoing vs undirected neighborhood) relative to ALIGN.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology between “mention” as surface form type vs token occurrence and unclear what exactly is embedded and when occurrences map to prototypes.  
- Weakness: Missing key ablations (single-prototype vs multi-prototype, with/without LM disambiguation, text-only vs KB-only vs joint training) to isolate component contributions.  
- Weakness: No comparison to unsupervised/graph-based EL or coherence-based disambiguation baselines to contextualize the LM-based method.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility and evaluation details are insufficient—training corpus, candidate generation, mention detection, dictionaries/pruning, and statistical significance tests are not clearly specified.  

- Question: Precisely how is N(ej) defined (incoming links, outgoing links, both, or other), and how does this choice affect results compared to ALIGN?  
- Question: Were ALIGN and your model compared with matched hyperparameters (window size, dimensionality), and can you provide sensitivity analyses?  
- Question: What is the allowed number of sense prototypes per mention (fixed, global, or nonparametric), and are prototypes tied to KB entities or latent senses?  
- Question: When is a mention occurrence assigned to a prototype—during training or post hoc—and does using the same LM signal for both cause circularity?  
- Question: What is the exact evaluation protocol (which supervised EL datasets, mention detection vs gold mentions, candidate generation, use of priors) and how does smoothing interact with priors?  
- Question: Are the improvements over ALIGN and other baselines statistically significant—please report tests and p-values.  

- Suggestion: Add ablations for single- vs multi-prototype mentions and for text-only/KB-only/joint training to clarify contributions.  
- Suggestion: Provide qualitative error analysis showing cases where multi-prototype modeling helps or fails (e.g., “Washington” examples).  
- Suggestion: Clean and deduplicate the references section to fix duplicates, typos, and malformed entries.
