- Strengths:
* Surpasses ALIGN on the supervised entity linking benchmark, indicating the proposed framework yields stronger jointly learned text–knowledge representations.
* Offers a head-to-head comparison with a closely related method using nearly identical input data.
* The exploration of the smoothing hyperparameter is informative, as popularity bias remains a persistent challenge in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison to ALIGN is not fully controlled. ALIGN uses a context window of 10 versus 5 here, and an embedding dimension of 500 versus 200. It is also unclear whether N(e_j) includes only entities that link to e_j. The graph is directed (Wikipedia outlinks), but is adjacency treated as undirected? In ALIGN, an entity’s context is the set of entities that link to it. If N(e_j) is defined differently, the effect on the learned embeddings cannot be isolated, which could influence the entity similarity results.
* The term “mention” is ambiguous: does it refer to a surface form/type or a specific occurrence in a document? Although “mention embedding” is used, it seems embeddings are only learned for mention senses.
* The contribution of the sense disambiguation step is hard to assess without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking approaches.
