- Summary: Proposes an intermediate "mention-sense" layer with jointly learned word, sense, and entity embeddings plus an LM-based disambiguation method, yielding strong Wikipedia EL results.

- Contribution: Introduces multi-prototype mention embeddings (MPME) as an explicit intermediate layer jointly trained with words and entities.

- Contribution: Proposes a simple, efficient language-model-style disambiguation mechanism with a smoothing parameter for robustness.

- Contribution: Demonstrates empirical improvements over a strong direct mention-to-entity baseline (ALIGN) on a standard Wikipedia EL benchmark.

- Strength: Clear, modern joint-embedding modeling that is straightforward to reproduce.

- Strength: Empirical gains (MPME/SPME outperform ALIGN) support the usefulness of an intermediate sense layer.

- Strength: Joint learning of words, mention-senses, and entities is a clean design and yields coherent qualitative embeddings.

- Strength: The LM-based disambiguation with smoothing is a pragmatic idea likely to improve robustness in sparse contexts.

- Strength: The problem is relevant—addressing mention ambiguity is central to text–KB integration.

- Weakness: Insufficient justification for why an intermediate mention-sense layer is better than direct mention-to-entity mapping.

- Weakness: Unclear sense induction—how senses are created, how many per mention, and whether they merely mirror KB entities.

- Weakness: Ambiguity in how entity profiles and relatedness graphs are built and how mention-sense profiles are initialized/updated.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons to broader EDL literature (e.g., TAC KBP systems) to contextualize novelty and performance.

- Weakness: Framing overclaims scope as broader "text–knowledge integration" and incorrectly implies novelty over prior EDL work.

- Question: How is the number of senses per surface form determined—fixed, learned, or tied to KB candidate entities?

- Question: Are mappings between mention-senses and entities one-to-one, many-to-one, or many-to-many in practice?

- Question: How are entity profiles constructed and what defines edges in the entity relatedness graph (links, co-mentions, textual similarity)?

- Question: How are mention-sense profiles initialized and updated—using entity page text, local contexts, or both?

- Question: What textual evidence at the mention-sense level is not already captured at the entity level, and can you quantify non-overlap with entity pages?

- Question: Please provide the full formulation of the LM-based disambiguation (smoothing, context window, neighboring-mention handling).

- Question: What is the computational complexity of sense disambiguation at inference and how does it scale with senses per mention?

- Question: Are the word, mention-sense, and entity embedding components weighted equally in the objective, and how were weights chosen?

- Question: Please add ablations isolating the contributions of mention-sense embeddings, entity relatedness, and the smoothing parameter.

- Question: Can you evaluate or discuss performance on TAC KBP EDL datasets to bolster generality, or justify why not?

- Suggestion: Clarify sense inventory construction, mapping cardinalities, and the distinct evidence captured by mention-sense vs entity embeddings.

- Suggestion: Temper the title/introduction to reflect the paper’s scope as an EDL method using joint embeddings with a sense layer.

- Post-response comment: Reviewer remains unconvinced TAC KBP EDL is irrelevant and emphasizes that embedding quality should be judged by downstream task performance.
