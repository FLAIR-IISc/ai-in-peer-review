- Surpasses ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint text–knowledge representations.  
- Provides a direct head-to-head comparison with a closely related method using nearly identical input data.  
- Studies the smoothing parameter, giving useful insight into mitigating popularity effects in entity linking.  
- ALIGN comparison is not well controlled: different content window (10 vs 5) and vector dimensionality (500 vs 200), and unclear definition/handling of N(e_j) and graph adjacency (directed outlinks vs possibly treated as undirected), which may affect embeddings.  
- The term “mention” is ambiguous (surface-form type vs specific occurrence) and “mention embedding” seems inconsistent since embeddings appear learned only for mention senses.  
- The effect of sense disambiguation order is hard to assess without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
