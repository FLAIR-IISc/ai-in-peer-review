- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking to English Wikipedia via an intermediate "mention sense" representation and a two-step disambiguation (mention→mention-sense→entity) with joint embeddings; reports SOTA on an EDL benchmark.

- Main contributions: (1) empirical finding that two-step multi-prototype mention embeddings (MPME/SPME) outperform direct mention→entity mapping, (2) joint training of word, mention-sense, and entity embeddings under one objective, (3) a simple effective smoothing parameter.

- Strength: Model is technically sound and aligned with embedding best practices.

- Strength: Empirical improvements over a strong direct-mapping baseline (ALIGN).

- Strength: Sensible unified representation of words, mention-senses, and entities learned jointly.

- Strength: The smoothing parameter is an elegant, practical improvement that yields measurable gains.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why the two-step/sense layer helps beyond the empirical delta.

- Weakness: Unclear sense induction/disambiguation procedure — how senses are created, how many per mention, and how neighboring mentions are used.

- Weakness: Potential redundancy between mention-sense and entity profiles; lack of evidence that sense profiles capture discriminative textual signals not already in entity pages.

- Weakness: Inaccurate novelty claim — prior EDL work (e.g., TAC KBP) is relevant and should be cited/compared.

- Weakness: Overstated scope in title/intro — “bridging text and knowledge” overreaches beyond EDL.

- Weakness: Evaluation focuses on embedding quality rather than downstream task performance; needs broader application-based validation.

- Question: Clarify construction of entity profiles and the relatedness graph (how relatedness is computed and from which texts).

- Question: Provide exact features and training/inference details for the language-model-based sense disambiguation and role of neighboring mentions.

- Question: How is the number of senses per mention determined, and are senses KB-derived or mapped one-to-one to entities?

- Question: If senses tie to entities, quantify what novel textual evidence sense profiles capture (e.g., extra context words per sense) and show ablations.

- Question: Why does the two-step pipeline outperform ALIGN — provide ablations isolating the sense layer and sensitivity to sense count.

- Question: Did you explore different weightings in the joint objective (words vs senses vs entities) and how sensitive are results?

- Suggestion: Revise Section 5 to explicitly detail sense creation, differentiation, training, and relation to entities and textual sources.

- Suggestion: Temper novelty claims, cite relevant EDL/KBP work, and clarify differences.

- Suggestion: Validate embedding quality via downstream applications to demonstrate practical gains.

- Post-note: Reviewer remains unconvinced that prior KBP EDL is irrelevant and reiterates that embedding quality should be validated through application performance.
