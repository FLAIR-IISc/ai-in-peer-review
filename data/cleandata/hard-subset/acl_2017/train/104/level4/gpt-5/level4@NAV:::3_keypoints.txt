- Strength: Unifies entity, mention, and sense modeling in a single neural language-modeling framework with a simple training procedure supporting many applications.  
- Strength: Straightforward neural learning and broad range of applications; empirical evaluation is solid though gains are modest.  
- Weakness: Degree of novelty is limited; paper is close to Yamada et al. (2016) and should explicitly outline differences.  
- Weakness: Clarity issues—Section 3 is hard to follow, some Figure 2 details unexplained, and terminology is redundant/confusing (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “entity–mention pairs”).  
- Suggestion: Authors should evaluate MPME word embeddings on non-KB semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they aren’t weaker than simpler word spaces.  
- Observation: Formal presentation is clear, but the discussion does not always match the technical content’s level.  
- Positive: Introduces new ideas extending prior work and is of clear interest to the community.
