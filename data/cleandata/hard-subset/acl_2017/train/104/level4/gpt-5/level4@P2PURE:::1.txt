- Strengths:
* Demonstrates superior performance to ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task, suggesting that the proposed framework improves jointly learned text and knowledge representations.
* Provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using highly similar input data.
* Includes a useful analysis of the smoothing parameter; accounting for popularity is a persistent issue in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN could be stronger. ALIGN used a content window of 10 (vs. 5 here) and 500-dimensional vectors (vs. 200 here). It is also unclear whether N(e_j) includes only entities that link to e_j. The graph is directed and built from Wikipedia outlinks; is adjacency defined as it would be for an undirected graph? In ALIGN, an entity’s context is the set of entities that link to it. If N(e_j) differs, it is difficult to determine how much this change affects the learned vectors, and it may contribute to the difference in scores on the entity similarity task.
* The term “mention” is ambiguous: does it refer to a surface-form type or a specific occurrence in a document? Although “mention embedding” is used, the model appears to learn embeddings only for mention senses.
* The impact of sense disambiguation order is hard to assess without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
