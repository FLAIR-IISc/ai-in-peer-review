- Strength: sound ideas, straightforward neural training, competitive (though not dramatic) results, and broad potential applications.  
- Strength: simple training procedure and modeling choices make the approach applicable to many tasks.  
- Strength: paper is formally well-structured.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—work is quite close to Yamada et al. (2016) and should more clearly delineate differences.  
- Weakness: empirical improvements are solid but not dramatic.  
- Weakness: exposition is uneven and sometimes opaque (Section 3 unclear; parts of Figure 2 underspecified).  
- Weakness: redundant/confusing terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity–mention pairs”; relation to anchors/types unclear).  
- Suggestion: demonstrate that MPME-produced embeddings perform well on non-KB tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show general utility.  
- Overall: extends Yamada et al. with additional ideas and is of clear interest.
