- Paper tackles entity linking to English Wikipedia but the title/intro overclaim broader “text–knowledge integration.”
- Proposes a two-stage pipeline with an intermediate “mention sense” layer: mention → mention sense → entity.
- Model jointly learns embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities under a symmetric objective.
- Method is clear and consistent with embedding-based practice, though many plausible alternatives exist.
- Core modeling choice (introducing mention senses) is not convincingly justified compared to direct mention→entity mapping (ALIGN/Yamada).
- Empirical gains (Table 2: MPME/SPME > ALIGN) are reported but the reason for the improvement is not explained.
- Unclear how mention senses are induced, distinguished, and how the number of senses per mention is determined.
- If mention senses map to KB entities, this implies a bijection—unclear whether senses are seeded from entity text or what new information they add.
- Need clarification on whether mention-sense profiles mainly capture context words absent from entity pages and how many such words occur per sense on average.
- Questioned necessity of a separate mention-sense space versus augmenting entity representations directly with context information.
- Suggest revising Section 5 to make the assumed relationship between senses and entities explicit if that is the central novelty.
- Claim that this is the first work addressing mention ambiguity in joint text–knowledge representations is inaccurate; TAC KBP/E﻿DL prior work should be cited.
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a thoughtful modeling choice.
- Reviewer remains unconvinced after rebuttal and argues embedding quality should be demonstrated via extrinsic task performance (e.g., KBP-style evaluations).
