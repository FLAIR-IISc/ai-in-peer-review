- Strengths:
* Outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, indicating that the proposed joint learning framework improves text and knowledge representations.
* Provides a direct, closely matched comparison with a highly related approach using similar input data.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter is valuable, as popularity bias is a persistent challenge in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison with ALIGN could be more controlled. ALIGN uses a context window of 10 (vs. 5 here) and an embedding dimension of 500 (vs. 200 here). In addition, it is unclear whether N(e_j) includes only entities that link to e_j. The graph is directed (Wikipedia outlinks), but is adjacency treated as if the graph were undirected? In ALIGN, an entity’s context is the set of entities that link to that entity. If N(e_j) is defined differently here, it is difficult to isolate the impact of this change on the learned vectors, which may contribute to the difference on the entity similarity task.
* The term “mention” is ambiguous: it is not always clear whether it refers to a string type or a specific occurrence in a document. Although the paper refers to a “mention embedding,” it appears that embeddings are learned only for mention senses.
* The effect of sense disambiguation order is hard to assess without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
