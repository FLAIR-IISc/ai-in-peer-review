- Strength: Proposes a sound core idea of jointly modeling entities, mentions, and senses within a single neural language modeling framework.  
- Strength: Uses a simple neural training procedure that supports practical implementation.  
- Strength: Shows interesting empirical performance, though gains are not dramatic.  
- Strength: Has a broad range of potential applications.  

- Weakness: Limited amount of genuinely novel content relative to prior work (notably close to Yamada et al. 2016).  
- Weakness: Clarity issues in parts of the exposition, especially Section 3 and parts of Figure 2.  

- Suggestion: Clarify terminology and resources—distinguish “dictionary of mentions” vs. “dictionary of entity–mention pairs,” and explain “text anchors” and “types for annotated text anchors.”  
- Suggestion: Improve explanations for Figure 2 and missing details in Section 3 to match the technical rigor.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly outline differences from Yamada et al. (2016) in modeling assumptions, training data, objectives, capabilities, and quantify each change’s contribution to performance/coverage.  
- Suggestion: Broaden evaluation to test the learned word embeddings on non-KB-dependent NLP tasks (e.g., parsing, coreference, semantic role labeling) to ensure no loss of general-purpose lexical semantics.  

- Overall: Technically sound and potentially useful with a compelling unification approach; clarity, stronger differentiation from prior work, and broader evaluation would strengthen the paper.
