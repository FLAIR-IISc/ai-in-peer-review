- Paper proposes integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling in one neural language-modeling framework with a simple training procedure and broad application potential.
- Formal presentation is clear overall, but the discussion sometimes fails to match the technical level.
- Strength: conceptually good ideas and a simple neural learning approach.
- Strength: empirical performance is interesting though not dramatically better than prior work.
- Strength: method has broad applicability across many tasks.
- Weakness: limited amount of novel content compared to prior work.
- Weakness: clarity issues in several sections and some figures.
- Experiments are solid but do not show striking improvements and appear as an extension of Yamada et al. (2016).
- Section 3 and parts of Figure 2 lack clear explanations and need improved prose.
- Terminology is redundant and confusing (e.g., unclear difference between “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity–mention pairs,” text anchors, and anchor types).
- Paper is close to Yamada et al. (2016); authors should explicitly outline differences.
- Evaluation scope is narrow: authors should show MPME word embeddings perform at least as well as simpler word spaces on non-KB semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL).
