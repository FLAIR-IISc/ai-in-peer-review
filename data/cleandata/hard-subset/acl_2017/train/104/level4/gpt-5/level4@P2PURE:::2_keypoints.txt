- Title/intro overstate scope: bridging text and knowledge is broader than EDL, so framing is misleading.  
- Method: introduces intermediate mention-sense layer yielding a two-step pipeline (mention → sense → entity).  
- Training: learns word, mention-sense, and entity embeddings jointly with a single objective weighting all equally.  
- Technical clarity: approach aligns with standard deep-learning embedding practices and is clearly presented.  
- Core question: paper fails to explain why a two-step mention→sense→entity pipeline should outperform direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Empirical gap: Table 2 shows MPME/SPME outperform ALIGN, but the paper doesn’t diagnose what mention senses capture beyond entities.  
- Proposed understanding to verify: entities use neighbor-based profiles from a relatedness graph; words use standard distributional profiles; mention senses use context-driven sense-disambiguated profiles.  
- Missing details: unclear how mention senses are constructed, differentiated, and how many senses per surface form are allowed/determined.  
- Possible bijection concern: if senses derive from the KB, there may be a one-to-one mapping between mention senses and entities—then it’s unclear what new signal senses add.  
- Initialization question: are mention-sense profiles seeded from entity text, and if so, what additional context words for senses aren’t already in entity pages?  
- Practicality: need statistics (e.g., average number of additional words per mention sense) and justification for maintaining a separate mention-sense space vs augmenting entity representations.  
- Revision request: if the above reading is correct, revise Section 5 to make this key insight explicit and provide diagnostics.  
- Incorrect claim: Section 6’s statement that this is the “first” work handling mention ambiguity in text+KB integration is false; TAC KBP EDL evaluations and multiple systems exist.  
- Positive note: reviewer liked the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2.  
- Post-response stance: reviewer remains unconvinced by authors’ rebuttal about KBP relevance and emphasizes that empirical task-based evaluation is necessary to demonstrate embedding “goodness.”
