Strengths:
- The model outperforms ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task, suggesting that the proposed framework improves jointly learned representations of text and knowledge.
- The paper provides a direct comparison with a closely related approach using very similar input data.
- The analysis of the smoothing parameter is useful, as the impact of popularity is a persistent issue in entity linking.

Weaknesses:
- The comparison with ALIGN could be more controlled. ALIGN uses a content window size of 10 versus this paper’s 5, and a vector dimensionality of 500 versus this paper’s 200. It is also not clear whether N(e_j) includes only entities that link to e_j. The graph is directed and consists of Wikipedia outlinks, but is adjacency defined as it would be for an undirected graph? In ALIGN, the context of an entity is the set of entities that link to that entity. If N(e_j) is defined differently here, it is difficult to isolate how much this change affects the learned vectors, and it could contribute to the differences observed on the entity similarity task.
- It is sometimes difficult to follow whether “mention” refers to a surface-form type or to a particular mention instance in a specific document. The phrase “mention embedding” is used, but it appears that embeddings are learned only for mention senses.
- It is difficult to determine the impact of sense disambiguation order without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods.

General Discussion:
The paper presents promising results and valuable analysis, particularly regarding popularity smoothing. However, the fairness and interpretability of the comparison with ALIGN are undermined by differing hyperparameters and potentially different neighbor/context definitions. Clarifying the definition of N(e_j), the treatment of graph directionality, and the intended meaning of “mention” versus “mention sense,” as well as providing comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods to assess the effect of disambiguation order, would substantially strengthen the paper.
