- Good idea and straightforward neural approach for entity disambiguation and linking.  
- Integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within a unified neural language-modeling framework.  
- Simple, aligned training procedure that enables a broad range of applications.  
- Broad applicability demonstrated, with solid (though not dramatic) empirical performance gains.  
- Formally clear and technically sound, and introduces new ideas building on Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Limited novelty—paper is close in spirit to Yamada et al. and should more explicitly state differences.  
- Exposition and prose issues (Section 3 hard to follow; some details in Figure 2 unexplained).  
- Confusing or redundant terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity–mention pairs” vs text anchors).  
- Performance gains are modest rather than striking.  
- Need to evaluate produced word embeddings on downstream semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they aren’t inferior to simpler word spaces.
