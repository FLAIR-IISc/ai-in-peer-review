Summary: Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings—multiple sense embeddings per mention string—learned jointly with word and entity embeddings and disambiguated per occurrence with a language-model-based method; shows SOTA EL vs ALIGN.

Contributions: (1) Joint framework producing multiple sense embeddings for mentions alongside word/entity embeddings. (2) Efficient LM-based method to assign mention occurrences to sense embeddings. (3) Empirical EL gains over ALIGN and analysis of a popularity-smoothing parameter.

Strengths: (1) Competitive supervised EL performance vs a strong, relevant baseline (ALIGN). (2) Direct comparison using closely matched input resources reduces confounds. (3) Useful analysis of popularity smoothing and its effect on bias between priors and context.

Weaknesses: (1) Comparison to ALIGN may be confounded by differing hyperparameters (window size, embedding dim) and unclear construction of entity neighborhood N(ej). (2) Terminological ambiguity around “mention” (surface type vs occurrence) obscures what is embedded and how mapping to prototypes works. (3) Limited evaluation of the disambiguation component—no ablation or comparisons to alternative unsupervised EL methods to isolate contributions.

Questions for authors: Precisely define N(ej) and whether the entity graph is directed/undirected; match or sensitivity-test ALIGN hyperparameters (window, dim); clarify whether mention embeddings are per surface string, per sense, or both and how tokens map to prototypes; quantify the LM-disambiguation contribution via ablation or baselines; characterize interactions between popularity smoothing and context strength.

Overall: Promising joint modeling and empirical results, but empirical claims would be strengthened by clarifying N(ej), resolving hyperparameter mismatches, clarifying “mention” semantics, and providing disambiguation ablations.
