**Strength:**

*   The supervised entity connection works better than ALIGN, indicating that the proposed framework improves the presentation of jointly learned text and knowledge.
*   Can be directly compared with closely related methods using very similar input data.
*   Analysis of smoothness parameters is useful, as the influence of popularity on entity connection is a persistent problem.
**Weaknesses:**

*   Might be better compared to ALIGN. ALIGN used a content window size of 10, whereas this paper uses a vector size of 5, 500, whereas this paper uses a size of 200. Also, it is not clear to me whether N(ej) only includes entities linked to ej. The graph is indicated and consists of Wikipedia outlines, but how adjacency will be defined, such as for an undirected graph? For ALIGN, the context of an entity is the set of entities linked to that entity. If N(e_j) is different, we cannot say how much this change will affect the learned vectors and whether it can increase the difference in the entity similarity task score.
*   It is sometimes difficult to follow that "mention" means a string type or a specific reference within a specific document. The term "mention embedding" has been used, but it seems that embedding is learned only in the sense of a mention.
*   It is difficult to determine the impact of order of obscurity without comparing with other unsupervised entity connection methods.
