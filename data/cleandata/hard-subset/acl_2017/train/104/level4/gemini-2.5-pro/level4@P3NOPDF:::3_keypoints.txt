- Proposes a unified neural model that jointly represents entities, mentions, and senses for entity disambiguation/linking.  
- Training procedure is simple and elegant, making the method easy to replicate and extend.  
- Produces unified embeddings (MPME) that are versatile and applicable to many downstream tasks.  
- Empirical results are solid and demonstrate effectiveness, though performance gains are not dramatic.  
- Clarity and presentation need improvement—Section 3 and several components in Figure 2 are hard to follow.  
- Terminology is inconsistent/unclear (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" vs "text anchors").  
- Lacks an explicit, detailed comparison to closely related work, especially Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016).  
- Missing evaluation of MPME embeddings on general NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to assess whether specialization harms general-purpose embedding quality.
