- Outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, implying joint learning yields better text+knowledge representations.

- Offers an insightful analysis of the smoothing parameter that tackles popularity bias in entity linking.

- Comparison with ALIGN is confounded by different hyperparameters (content window 10 vs 5, vector dim 500 vs 200).
- Definition of an entity's neighborhood N(e_j) is unclear (directed vs undirected adjacency), complicating comparisons to ALIGN’s context.
- Terminology is ambiguous: "mention" is not clearly defined as a type or token, and "mention embedding" may be misused.
- The benefit of the proposed sense disambiguation ordering is unclear without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking strategies.

- Overall the framework is promising with compelling empirical gains but conclusions are weakened by experimental ambiguities.
- Clarifying hyperparameter differences, the entity context definition, and precise technical terms is necessary to fairly attribute performance improvements.
