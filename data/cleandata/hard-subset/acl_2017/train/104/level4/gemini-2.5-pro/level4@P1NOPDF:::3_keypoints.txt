- Proposes a simple, effective neural method that unifies entity, mention, and sense modeling for entity disambiguation/linking.  
- Uses a simple training procedure that supports a wide range of applications.  
- Empirical results are solid and promising but improvements are not striking or groundbreaking.  
- Formal model presentation is clear, but surrounding discussion quality is weaker.  
- Prose is occasionally unclear (Section 3) and Figure 2 lacks sufficient explanation.  
- Terminology is confusing/redundant; clarify "dictionary of mentions," "dictionary of entity-mention pairs," "text anchors," and their relation to "types."  
- Method is very similar to Yamada et al. (2016); authors should explicitly delineate key differences and novel contributions.  
- Concern about overall degree of novelty relative to prior work.  
- Recommend evaluating the model’s word embeddings on general NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to ensure they are not degraded versus standard embeddings.  
- Reviewer has read the authors' response.
