- Proposes MPME: joint embeddings for words, entities, and intermediate "mention senses" to perform mention->sense->entity linking and reports SOTA on a benchmark.

- Contribution: introduces a multi-prototype framework that models mention ambiguity via multiple sense embeddings per mention string.

- Contribution: demonstrates state-of-the-art entity linking performance, providing empirical support for the multi-prototype approach.

- Strength: strong empirical results outperforming prior methods (including ALIGN) on the entity linking task.

- Strength: technically sound joint embedding objective that integrates textual context and knowledge structure.

- Strength: novel intermediate sense layer with qualitative evidence that distinct senses are learned for ambiguous mentions.

- Weakness: insufficient conceptual justification for why a separate mention-sense layer is needed and what it captures beyond entity embeddings.

- Weakness: methodological details are unclear — how many senses per mention are chosen and how the "language model based" sense disambiguation works is not specified.

- Weakness: incorrect claim about lack of prior baselines; ignores relevant TAC KBP EDL work and benchmarks addressing mention ambiguity.

- Question: clarify the fundamental distinction between a "mention sense" and an "entity" and why senses aren’t redundant with entity embeddings.

- Question: provide details on how senses are identified/enumerated and the architecture/training of the language-model-based sense-selection component.

- Question: explain why TAC KBP EDL systems and benchmarks were not considered relevant baselines or related work.
