1. Proposes MPME: joint model that learns multiple sense-specific mention embeddings from text and KBs with a LM-based disambiguation module.  
2. Claims: unified model for entities/mentions/senses and SOTA performance on a standard entity linking benchmark.  
3. Strength: elegant, cohesive neural language model that jointly models entities, mentions, and senses with a simple training procedure.  
4. Strength: strong empirical results—achieves state-of-the-art on the evaluated entity linking benchmark.  
5. Strength: embeddings have broad potential applicability (relation extraction, QA, KB completion, etc.).  
6. Weakness: unclear presentation—Section 3 hard to follow, terminology (e.g., dictionaries) ambiguous, and Figure 2 underexplained.  
7. Weakness: insufficient differentiation from prior work (notably Yamada et al. 2016); lacks clear comparison of novel contributions.  
8. Weakness: narrow evaluation limited to entity linking; no experiments showing general semantic quality or utility on other intrinsic/extrinsic tasks.  
9. Question: please precisely define "dictionary of mentions" vs. "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" and clarify Figure 2 components/data flow.  
10. Question: provide a point-by-point comparison to Yamada et al. (2016) highlighting technical differences and novelties.  
11. Question: have you assessed the impact on general word embedding quality or other NLP tasks; provide evidence that embeddings remain strong beyond entity-centric tasks?
