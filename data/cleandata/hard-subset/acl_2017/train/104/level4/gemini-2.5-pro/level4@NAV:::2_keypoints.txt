- Paper proposes a two-stage EDL method mapping mentions → "mention senses" → entities using jointly learned embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Title/intro overstate scope; work actually focuses on core Entity Disambiguation and Linking to English Wikipedia.  
- Methodology and embedding training are technically sound and align with modern deep learning practices.  
- Main weakness: insufficient motivation/justification for the two-step mention-sense architecture versus direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Empirical results (Table 2) show two-step models (MPME/SPME) outperform direct mapping, but the paper fails to explain why.  
- Reviewer’s interpretation request: entity profiles from relatedness graph, word profiles from distributional semantics, mention-sense profiles as sense-disambiguated distributional semantics—authors should confirm.  
- Sense-disambiguation mechanism is under-specified: how mention senses are defined, distinguished, and how many senses per mention are allowed.  
- If senses are KB-derived (one-to-one with entities), clarify whether sense profiles are seeded from entity pages and what unique information mention senses capture beyond entities.  
- Paper should analyze the quantity/impact of corpus co-occurrence information not present in Wikipedia and justify a separate mention-sense representation.  
- Claim of being the first to handle mention ambiguity in text–knowledge integration is incorrect; TAC KBP/KBP EDL systems are relevant baselines and should be discussed.  
- Smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a positive, thoughtful inclusion.  
- Post-response critique: authors’ refusal to evaluate on KBP/task benchmarks is unconvincing; embedding quality must be validated on downstream tasks/established benchmarks.
