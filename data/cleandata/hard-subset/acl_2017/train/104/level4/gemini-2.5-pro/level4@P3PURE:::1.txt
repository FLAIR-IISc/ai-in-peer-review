### Strengths
*   The proposed framework outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, which suggests that its joint learning approach yields improved representations of text and knowledge.
*   The paper provides a direct and valuable comparison with ALIGN, a closely related approach, using very similar input data.
*   The analysis of the smoothing parameter is insightful, as it directly addresses the persistent issue of popularity bias in entity linking.

### Weaknesses
*   The comparison with ALIGN could be strengthened, as several differences in the experimental setup confound the results. Specifically, ALIGN used a content window size of 10 and a vector dimension of 500, whereas this paper uses 5 and 200, respectively.
*   Furthermore, the definition of an entity's neighborhood, N(e_j), is unclear. The paper states that the graph consists of directed Wikipedia outlinks, but it does not specify whether adjacency is defined as it would be for an undirected graph. For ALIGN, an entity's context is the set of entities that link *to* it. If the definition of N(e_j) in this work is different, it is difficult to determine the impact of this change on the learned vectors, which in turn could contribute to the performance difference on the entity similarity task.
*   The terminology is sometimes ambiguous. It is difficult to follow whether "mention" refers to a surface string (a type) or a specific instance in a document (a token). The phrase "mention embedding" is used, but the model appears to learn embeddings only for mention senses, not for the mention strings themselves.
*   The impact of the proposed sense disambiguation ordering is difficult to determine without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods that employ different strategies.

### General Discussion
Overall, this paper presents a promising framework for jointly learning representations for entity linking, and the empirical improvements over a strong baseline like ALIGN are compelling. However, the paper's conclusions would be significantly strengthened by addressing the noted ambiguities in the experimental comparison. Clarifying the differences in hyperparameters and, crucially, the definition of entity context is necessary to fairly attribute the performance gains to the proposed model. Additionally, improving the precision of key technical terms would enhance the paper's clarity and contribution.
