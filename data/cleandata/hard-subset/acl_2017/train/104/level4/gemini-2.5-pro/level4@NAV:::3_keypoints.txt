- Strength: Proposes an elegant, straightforward neural training procedure built on strong concepts.  
- Strength: Model achieves promising (though not groundbreaking) performance and supports many applications.  
- Contribution: Introduces a novel unified neural framework integrating entity, mention, and sense representations.  
- Contribution: Simple training methodology enables application across diverse tasks.  
- Presentation: Formal parts are clear, but the accompanying discussion sometimes lacks precision.  
- Results: Experimental results are solid but do not show substantial gains over existing methods.  
- Novelty: Despite similarities to Yamada et al. (2016), the paper introduces valuable new concepts of interest.  
- Weakness: Writing clarity needs improvement—Section 3 is hard to follow, parts of Figure 2 are under-explained, and terminology is redundant/unclear.  
- Weakness: Method is very similar in spirit to Yamada et al. (2016); authors must more explicitly delineate key differences and novel contributions.  
- General concern: MPME evaluated only on entity linking/disambiguation—need tests on other NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to ensure embeddings don't harm general linguistic representation.  
- Misc: Reviewer read the authors’ response.
