- Proposes a two-step EDL method using an intermediate "mention sense" layer: mention → mention sense → KB entity.  
- Jointly trains word, mention-sense, and entity embeddings with a unified objective; technically sound and clearly presented.  
- Introduces a smoothing parameter to balance global and local information (Section 6.4.2).  
- Empirically, two-step models (MPME, SPME) outperform one-step ALIGN (Table 2), but no explanation for why.  
- Main weakness: lack of clear justification or intuition for introducing the intermediate mention-sense layer.  
- Paper omits details on how mention senses are created/enumerated and whether they correspond bijectively to candidate entities.  
- Missing analysis of what distinct information mention-sense embeddings capture beyond word and entity embeddings.  
- Authors should explicitly frame the contribution as separating KB-derived entity information from corpus-derived contextual information and analyze the differential.  
- Incorrect claim of being the first to handle mention ambiguity; should acknowledge prior EDL work (e.g., TAC KBP) and situate comparisons.  
- Post-rebuttal point: reviewer disagrees that embedding “goodness” can be evaluated in isolation—downstream EDL performance is the appropriate measure.
