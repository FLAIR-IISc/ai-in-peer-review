- Summary: Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly learns multiple sense vectors for ambiguous mentions using text and a knowledge graph, plus a LM-based method to disambiguate senses; shows strong entity linking performance.

- Contribution 1: Novel joint text+KG model that learns multi-sense embeddings for ambiguous entity mentions to address mention polysemy in a shared semantic space.

- Contribution 2: Efficient language model–based disambiguation method that selects the appropriate learned mention sense given context.

- Contribution 3: Strong empirical results achieving state-of-the-art performance on a benchmark supervised entity linking dataset, outperforming a relevant baseline.

- Strength 1: Achieves state-of-the-art results and notably outperforms the strong ALIGN baseline, supporting the effectiveness of the multi-prototype approach.

- Strength 2: Provides a direct, comparable evaluation against ALIGN under a similar experimental setup, clarifying advantages over prior joint embedding methods.

- Strength 3: Includes analysis of the smoothing parameter to address entity popularity bias, showing attention to practical robustness beyond raw metrics.

- Weakness 1: Comparison to ALIGN may be confounded by differing hyperparameters (context window, embedding dimension) and ambiguous definition of entity neighborhood N(e_j), making isolation of model effects unclear.

- Weakness 2: Terminology around "mentions" is ambiguous—unclear if embeddings are for surface mention strings or for mention senses—reducing clarity and precision.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation is limited to supervised entity linking; lack of comparisons to unsupervised methods leaves the standalone strength of the disambiguation approach uncertain.

- Question 1: Please clarify differences in hyperparameters (window size, vector dimension) and the exact definition of N(e_j) relative to ALIGN, and how these affect performance comparison.

- Question 2: Please clarify whether the model learns embeddings for ambiguous mention strings directly or only for their distinct senses.

- Question 3: Have you considered evaluating the disambiguation method in an unsupervised setting or comparing it to other unsupervised entity linking baselines?
