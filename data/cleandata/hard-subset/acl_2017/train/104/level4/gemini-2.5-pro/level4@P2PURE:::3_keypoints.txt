- Paper proposes a neural method integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling in a single neural language modeling framework.
- Strength: the integrated modeling idea is novel and of interest to the community.
- Strength: the method is simple, elegant, and has a straightforward training procedure.
- Strength: the model is versatile and applicable to a wide range of tasks.
- Strength: empirical results are solid, though improvements are not dramatic.
- Weakness: writing is occasionally unclear (Section 3 especially) and some figures/details are unexplained.
- Weakness: the novel contribution over prior work (notably Yamada et al. 2016) is not clearly articulated.
- Detailed comment: clarify terminology and distinctions (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "text anchors", and "dictionary of entity-mention pairs" vs "types for annotated text anchors").
- Detailed comment: improve explanation of Figure 2 and other under-explained details.
- Comparison request: explicitly outline key differences and novel contributions relative to Yamada et al. (2016).
- General observation: show that MPME-produced word embeddings do not degrade performance on general NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) compared to standard embeddings.
