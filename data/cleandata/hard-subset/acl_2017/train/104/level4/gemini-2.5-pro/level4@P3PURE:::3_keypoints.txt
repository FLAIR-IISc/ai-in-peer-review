- Proposes a unified neural language-modeling framework that jointly models entities, mentions, and senses for entity disambiguation/linking.  
- Training and modeling are simple and scalable, enabling many downstream applications.  
- Paper is formally clear and experiments show solid, modest improvements (interesting but not striking).  
- Appears related to Yamada et al. (2016) but claims novel ideas of interest to the community.  
- Main weakness: unclear prose—Section 3 and parts of Figure 2 need significant revision for clarity.  
- Terminology is sometimes redundant/ambiguous (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs "text anchors"); authors should clarify terms.  
- Must explicitly delineate differences and novel contributions relative to Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Recommend evaluating the MPME embeddings on general NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they do not degrade performance compared to standard embeddings.
