- Paper frames a broad "bridging text and knowledge" goal but actually tackles Entity Linking; title/intro mismatch.  
- Method: introduces an intermediate "mention sense" layer, decomposing linking into mention→sense and sense→entity, with joint embeddings for words, senses, and entities.  
- Technical approach and embedding-learning setup are sound and consistent with current DL practices.  
- Main concern: paper fails to justify why a two-step mention-sense layer should outperform direct mention→entity mapping (ALIGN baseline).  
- Results show two-step models beat baselines, but there's little intuition or explanation for what extra information mention senses provide.  
- Reviewer’s reconstructed understanding: entity profiles from entity co-occurrence graph, word profiles are standard distributional embeddings, mention-sense profiles are sense-disambiguated distributional embeddings—authors should confirm.  
- Creation of mention senses is poorly described: how senses are defined, how many per mention, and how they’re differentiated is unclear.  
- If senses are derived from KB candidate entities, this risks a bijection with entities—what then makes sense profiles distinct from entity profiles?  
- Hypothesis: sense profiles may include corpus words not on the entity’s Wikipedia page—authors should quantify how much additional textual information senses add on average.  
- Core question: why model supplementary textual evidence in a separate mention-sense space rather than augmenting entity representations directly?  
- Strong recommendation to revise the paper to make the motivation and mechanism of mention senses explicit; this distinction appears to be the core contribution.  
- Claim that this is "first work" addressing mention ambiguity is inaccurate—prior EDL/KBP tracks (e.g., TAC KBP 2015/2016) and systems are relevant baselines.  
- Positive: introduction of the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is thoughtful and interesting.  
- Post-response: reviewer remains unconvinced that KBP is irrelevant; embeddings should be evaluated extrinsically on downstream tasks, and KBP remains a meaningful benchmark.
