- Summary: Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding to learn multiple sense vectors per ambiguous mention from text and KB, with an LM-based disambiguation, evaluated on entity linking with reported SOTA.

- Contribution 1: Novel framework for multi-prototype embeddings that unifies unstructured text and structured KB signals.

- Contribution 2: Introduces a language-model-based disambiguation component that uses the learned multi-sense embeddings.

- Contribution 3: Empirical results show the method outperforms ALIGN on a supervised entity linking benchmark.

- Strength 1: Shows superior performance versus ALIGN, indicating the multi-prototype approach improves joint text–KB representations.

- Strength 2: Direct comparison to ALIGN is informative because ALIGN uses similar inputs, highlighting benefits of modeling mention ambiguity.

- Strength 3: Includes analysis of a smoothing parameter for mitigating entity popularity bias, adding experimental depth and insight.

- Weakness 1: Comparison to ALIGN may be confounded by experiment differences, making it hard to attribute gains to the model.

- Weakness 1a (hyperparameter mismatch): Uses different context window (5 vs 10) and vector dimension (200 vs 500) from ALIGN without analyzing their impact.

- Weakness 1b (entity context definition): Unclear definition of entity neighborhood N(e_j) (in-links vs out-links vs undirected), which affects input signals and reproducibility.

- Weakness 2: Terminology is ambiguous—“mention” and “mention embedding” are not clearly distinguished between strings, instances, and sense embeddings.

- Weakness 3: The disambiguation method’s individual contribution is hard to assess; lacks comparison to other unsupervised or collective entity linking methods.

- Question 1: Please clarify the exact definition of N(e_j): incoming links, outgoing links, or both, and how this compares to ALIGN.

- Question 2: Given hyperparameter differences, can you justify that gains are architectural and/or report results using ALIGN’s hyperparameters?

- Question 3: Does the model learn an embedding for the mention string itself in addition to sense embeddings, and will you refine terminology to distinguish string, instance, and sense embeddings?
