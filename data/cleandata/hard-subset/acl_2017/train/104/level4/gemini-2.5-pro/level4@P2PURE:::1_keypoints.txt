- Framework outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint text-knowledge representations.  
- Direct comparison to ALIGN using similar input data strengthens experimental claims.  
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter addresses popularity bias in entity linking.  
- Comparison to ALIGN is confounded by inconsistent experimental settings (ALIGN used window=10, dim=500; this paper uses window=5, dim=200).  
- Definition of the entity neighborhood N(e_j) is unclear (outlinks vs incoming links / directed vs undirected), which could change learned vectors and explain performance differences.  
- Terminology around "mention" is ambiguous (surface string type vs specific instance), and "mention embedding" appears to refer to candidate-sense embeddings rather than mention-string embeddings.  
- The impact of the proposed sense disambiguation ordering is not assessed due to lack of comparisons with other unsupervised ordering strategies.
