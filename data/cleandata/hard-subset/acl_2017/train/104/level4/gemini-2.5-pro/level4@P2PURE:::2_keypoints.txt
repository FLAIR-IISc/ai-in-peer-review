- Paper tackles entity linking by introducing a two-step pipeline: mention → mention sense → entity with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Technical approach is sound and uses modern deep learning/embedding practices.  
- Central weakness: no clear justification for why the two-step mention-sense approach should outperform a direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Empirical improvement (Table 2) is shown but the paper fails to explain the underlying cause of the gain.  
- Unclear how mention senses are defined and differentiated; authors must specify the mechanism for sense induction.  
- Unclear whether mention senses are derived from the KB and whether that induces a one-to-one mapping to KB entities.  
- If mapping is one-to-one, the paper must explain what extra information mention-sense embeddings capture that entity embeddings do not.  
- If mention senses capture corpus-derived contextual signals absent from KB pages, the paper should quantify this non-KB information.  
- The paper should evaluate whether the amount of unique information justifies a separate mention-sense space versus augmenting entity representations.  
- The distinction between mention senses and entities is central and should be explicitly articulated (notably in Section 5).  
- Claim of being first to handle mention ambiguity is inaccurate; TAC KBP EDL work (e.g., 2016 overview) is relevant and must be cited.  
- Positive: the introduced smoothing parameter (Section 6.4.2) is a thoughtful and valuable contribution.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer remains unconvinced by dismissing KBP — embedding quality must be validated via downstream task performance, not only conceptual arguments.
