- Title/intro overstate scope: paper treats EDL only, while bridging text and knowledge is broader.  
- Method summary: two-step linking via intermediate "mention senses" with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Technical approach is sound and follows current embedding best practices.  
- Core concern: paper fails to justify why an intermediate mention-sense layer outperforms direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Request for clarification: are entity reps learned from a relatedness graph of Wikipedia, word reps from distributional corpora, and mention-sense reps distributional with sense disambiguation?  
- Sense-disambiguation details are underspecified—need clear definition/enumeration of senses for a mention string.  
- Question whether mention senses simply map bijectively to KB entities, and if so what unique information mention senses add beyond entity representations.  
- Ask for quantitative analysis of non-KB contextual words associated with mention senses and whether augmenting entity pages could incorporate this evidence.  
- Incorrect claim noted: prior EDL baselines exist (TAC KBP), so the novelty claim about no exact baselines is wrong.  
- Positive point: smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a thoughtful contribution.  
- Post-rebuttal critique: authors’ focus on intrinsic embedding quality is insufficient—extrinsic task-based evaluation is necessary to demonstrate practical value.
