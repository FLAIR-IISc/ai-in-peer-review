- Model outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, supporting improved joint text-knowledge representations.  
- Direct comparison to a closely related method using similar input data is a notable strength.  
- Provides useful analysis of the smoothing parameter, addressing entity popularity bias in entity linking.  
- Comparison to ALIGN is weakened by experimental discrepancies (context window, vector dimensionality) and an unclear definition of entity neighborhood N(ej), including graph directionality issues.  
- The term "mention" is used ambiguously—unclear if it denotes string types or document-specific instances; model seems to learn only mention-sense embeddings.  
- The effect of sense disambiguation ordering is hard to isolate; needs comparison against other unsupervised entity linking methods.
