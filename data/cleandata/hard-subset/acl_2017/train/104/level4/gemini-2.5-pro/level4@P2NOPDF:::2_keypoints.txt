- Paper proposes a two-stage EDL method introducing an intermediate "mention sense" layer and jointly learns embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities to map mentions to Wikipedia entities.
- Method is technically sound and aligns with embedding-based deep learning practices.
- Main conceptual weakness: no clear justification for why the intermediate sense layer is superior to a direct mention→entity mapping.
- Empirical gains (MPME/SPME over ALIGN) are reported but lack theoretical explanation of what the sense layer adds beyond entity representations.
- Reviewer’s hypothesized construction: entity embeddings from KB relatedness graph, word embeddings from text, and mention-sense embeddings as context-disambiguated distributional vectors—details of the latter are omitted.
- Crucial unanswered questions: how senses are enumerated (sense inventory), whether senses map bijectively to KB entities, and what distinct information senses capture (and its scale).
- Suggests the paper should analyze whether sense-level information could instead be incorporated into entity representations, simplifying the model to one step.
- Criticizes inaccurate claim of novelty: prior work (e.g., TAC KBP EDL systems) addresses mention ambiguity and should be acknowledged and compared.
- Praises the smoothing parameter introduced in Section 6.4.2 as clever and well-motivated.
- Post-rebuttal: rejects authors’ dismissal of KBP relevance and insists extrinsic benchmarks like KBP are necessary to demonstrate embedding utility.
