- Core idea: Introduces a neural Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model that jointly models textual context, KB entities, and multiple mention senses in a unified vector space.  
- Disambiguation: Proposes a simple, efficient method to select the appropriate sense embedding for a mention in context.  
- Empirical claim: Reports state-of-the-art performance on a standard entity linking benchmark.  
- Contribution 1: MPME jointly learns representations for words, entities, and multiple mention senses.  
- Contribution 2: An efficient disambiguation procedure leveraging the trained language model.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical evaluation showing improved entity linking results.  
- Strength: Elegant core idea that directly addresses mention ambiguity via multi-prototype embeddings.  
- Strength: Methodological simplicity and straightforward training procedure that aids reproducibility and extensibility.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance supporting the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Strength: Learned representations could benefit many downstream NLP tasks requiring nuanced entity understanding.  
- Weakness: Unclear novelty relative to prior work (e.g., Yamada et al., 2016); lacks detailed comparison and delineation of technical differences.  
- Weakness: Presentation and clarity issues, especially in Section 3 and Figure 2; confusing terminology (e.g., "dictionary of mentions" vs. "dictionary of entity-mention pairs").  
- Question for authors: Have you evaluated the model’s word embeddings on general NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to ensure entity-aware training does not degrade standard embedding utility?
