### Strengths:

*   A key strength of this work is that the proposed framework outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task. This result suggests that the model learns more effective joint representations of text and knowledge.
*   The authors provide a direct comparison with ALIGN, a closely related approach, using very similar input data. This strengthens the validity of the experimental results.
*   The analysis of the smoothing parameter is a valuable contribution, as it directly addresses the persistent challenge of entity popularity bias in entity linking.

### Weaknesses:

*   The comparison with the ALIGN baseline could be significantly improved. Several key hyperparameter differences between the two experimental setups make a direct comparison less conclusive. For example, ALIGN used a content window size of 10 and a vector dimension of 500, whereas this work uses a window size of 5 and a dimension of 200.
*   This comparison is further complicated by a lack of clarity regarding the definition of an entity's neighborhood, N(e_j). The paper specifies that the graph is directed and based on Wikipedia outlinks, but it is unclear whether adjacency is then defined based on these outlinks, incoming links, or as if the graph were undirected. This is a critical detail, as ALIGN defines an entity's context as the set of entities that link *to* it. If N(e_j) is defined differently here, this could have a substantial impact on the learned vectors and may account for some of the performance difference on the entity similarity task. Without this clarification, it is difficult to isolate the contribution of the proposed model architecture.
*   The paper's terminology is at times ambiguous. It is not always clear whether the term "mention" refers to a surface string type or a specific instance in a document. The phrase "mention embedding" is also used, although it appears that embeddings are learned for mention *senses*, not for the mention strings themselves. Refining this terminology would improve the paper's overall clarity.
*   Finally, it is difficult to assess the impact of the proposed sense disambiguation order. Without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods that employ different ordering strategies, the significance of this modeling choice remains unclear.

### General Discussion:
