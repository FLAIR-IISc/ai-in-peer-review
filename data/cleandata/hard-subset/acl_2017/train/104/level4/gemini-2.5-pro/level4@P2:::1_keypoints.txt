- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense-specific embeddings by jointly modeling textual contexts and knowledge-graph entity contexts, plus a language-model–based disambiguation method.
- Main contribution: a novel model that explicitly represents multiple senses per mention to address ambiguity in joint text–KB embeddings.
- Main contribution: achieves state-of-the-art performance on a supervised entity linking benchmark, outperforming the ALIGN baseline.
- Strength: strong empirical performance indicating multi-prototype embeddings improve joint representations.
- Strength: direct experimental comparison to a closely related baseline (ALIGN) using comparable input data.
- Strength: includes an insightful analysis of a smoothing parameter to mitigate entity popularity bias.
- Weakness: comparison to ALIGN may be unfair due to mismatched hyperparameters (context window 5 and dim 200 vs ALIGN’s 10 and 500).
- Weakness: unclear/ambiguous definition of the entity neighborhood N(e_j) (in-links, out-links, or both), which affects comparability to ALIGN.
- Weakness: imprecise terminology around “mention” — unclear if embeddings are for surface strings or sense-specific instances.
- Weakness: limited evaluation of the LM-based disambiguation component; its contribution isn’t isolated or compared to other unsupervised linking methods.
- Question: please clarify the exact definition of N(e_j) — does it use in-links, out-links, or both, and how does this compare to ALIGN?
- Question: why were different hyperparameters (window size, vector dimension) used vs ALIGN, and were experiments run with matched hyperparameters?
- Question: does the model learn a single embedding for a mention string in addition to multiple sense embeddings, or only learn embeddings for senses?
