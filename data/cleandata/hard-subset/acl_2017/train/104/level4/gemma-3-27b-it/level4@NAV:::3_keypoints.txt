- Presents a neural method for entity disambiguation/linking integrating entity, mention, and sense modeling in a unified neural language model.  
- Simplicity of training is a major strength, enabling broad application support.  
- Empirical performance is interesting but improvements over prior work are modest.  
- Addresses a relevant problem and offers ideas of genuine interest with novel contributions.  
- Paper is well-structured formally, but discussions sometimes lack depth relative to technical sophistication.  
- Empirical evaluation is sound, though reported gains are moderate.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Yamada et al. 2016) but needs clearer explanation of novel differences.  
- Clarity issues: Section 3 and Figure 2 need clearer exposition and more detailed explanations.  
- Terminology is redundant/unclear—distinctions among “dictionary of mentions,” “dictionary of entity-mention pairs,” “text anchors,” and their types should be explicitly defined.  
- Evaluation scope limited: Multiple Embedding model is tested only on entity linking/disambiguation and should be evaluated on broader NLP tasks to ensure embeddings aren’t worse than simpler spaces.
