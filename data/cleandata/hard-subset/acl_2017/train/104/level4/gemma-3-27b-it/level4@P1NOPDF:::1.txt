## Review for ACL 2017

**Strengths:**

* This work outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, suggesting that the proposed framework effectively improves the joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
* The paper provides a direct comparison to a closely related approach, using very similar input data, which strengthens the evaluation.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter is particularly insightful, as accounting for the impact of popularity is a persistent challenge in entity linking.

**Weaknesses:**

* The comparison with ALIGN could be more thorough. ALIGN utilized a content window size of 10, whereas this paper employs a window size of 5, and ALIGN’s vector dimension is 500 compared to 200 in this work. Furthermore, it’s unclear whether N(ej) includes only entities that link *to* ej. The graph structure is based on Wikipedia outlinks, but it’s not explicitly stated whether the adjacency is defined for a directed or undirected graph. ALIGN defines the context of an entity as the set of entities linking to it. If N(e_j) is defined differently, it’s difficult to ascertain the extent to which this change contributes to the observed differences in performance on the entity similarity task.
* The term “mention” is sometimes used ambiguously, referring at times to a string type and at other times to a specific mention within a document. The phrase “mention embedding” is used, but the embeddings appear to be learned only for mention *senses*, rather than mentions themselves.
* Determining the impact of the sense disambiguation order is difficult without a comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods.

**General Discussion:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of entity linking. The reported performance improvements over ALIGN are encouraging, and the analysis of the smoothing parameter offers practical insights. However, several aspects of the experimental setup and presentation could be clarified to strengthen the evaluation and improve understanding. 

Specifically, a more detailed comparison with ALIGN – accounting for differences in hyperparameters and graph construction – would provide a more convincing assessment of the proposed method's advantages. Clarifying the definition of “mention” and its relation to “mention embeddings” would improve the readability and precision of the paper. Finally, situating the results within the broader landscape of unsupervised entity linking methods, including a comparison to other baselines, would help to better understand the significance of the chosen disambiguation order. 

Addressing these points would significantly enhance the paper’s clarity and impact. Overall, the work demonstrates a solid technical approach with promising results, and I believe that addressing the concerns raised above would make it a stronger contribution to the ACL community.
