- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, though title/intro suggest a broader text–knowledge integration.
- Proposed method uses an intermediate mention-sense layer in two stages: mention → mention-sense → entity, with joint embeddings for words, mention-senses, and entities.
- Technical approach is sound and consistent with current deep learning and embedding techniques.
- Main concern: unclear rationale for two-step mention-sense process versus direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN/Yamada), despite Table 2 showing two-step superiority.
- Unclear what extra information mention-senses provide beyond entities and why that improves performance.
- Reviewer’s interpretation of profiles: entity embeddings come from neighboring-entity relatedness graph based on Wikipedia page word-level relatedness.
- Words use standard distributional semantics without sense disambiguation.
- Mention-senses allegedly use sense-disambiguated distributional profiles from local context and neighboring mentions, but the paper lacks details on sense creation and differentiation.
- Key unanswered questions: how senses are determined, how many senses per mention, whether KB imposes senses (one-to-one with entities), and whether sense profiles are initialized from entity text.
- Question whether mention-sense space contains words not on the corresponding Wikipedia page, how many such words exist on average, and whether separate sense space is necessary versus augmenting entity representations.
- If the reviewer’s understanding is accurate, this should be explicitly stated in Section 5 as the paper’s main contribution.
- Claim that this is the first work to handle mention ambiguity in text–knowledge integration is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL evaluations and systems (using Freebase) have addressed this—see TAC KBP 2016.
- Positive note: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a useful contribution.
- After the authors’ response, the reviewer remains unconvinced about dismissing KBP evaluations and stresses that embedding quality must be validated via downstream application performance.
