- Presents strong ideas with a simple neural learning approach, yielding interesting (though not groundbreaking) performance and broad applicability.
- Integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within a unified neural language modeling framework.
- Simplicity of training enables support for many downstream applications.
- Paper is formally clear, but discussion depth is uneven relative to the technical sophistication.
- Empirical evaluation is sound, but reported performance improvements are modest.
- Builds on Yamada et al. (2016) yet does not clearly spell out key differences; authors should explicitly address this relationship.
- Prose and exposition issues: Section 3 and Figure 2 lack clarity, and some terminology is redundant/unclear (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs”; “text anchors” vs “types for annotated text anchors”).
- Recommend demonstrating that MPME-produced word embeddings are not inferior to simpler wordspaces on broader semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL), especially those involving entity mentions.
