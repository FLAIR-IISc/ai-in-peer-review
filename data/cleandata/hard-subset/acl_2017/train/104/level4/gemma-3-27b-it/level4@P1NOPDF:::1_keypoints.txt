- Outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, suggesting better joint learning of text and knowledge representations.
- Direct comparison to a closely related approach using similar input data strengthens the evaluation.
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter is insightful for addressing popularity effects in entity linking.

- Comparison with ALIGN is incomplete: different context window sizes (5 vs 10) and vector dimensions (200 vs 500) are not accounted for.
- Unclear whether N(e_j) includes only entities linking to e_j and whether the adjacency is defined on a directed or undirected graph.
- Ambiguous use of “mention”: sometimes refers to a string type and other times to a document-specific mention; “mention embeddings” appear to be for senses, not mentions.
- No comparison to other unsupervised entity linking methods, making the impact of the disambiguation order difficult to assess.

- Recommend a more detailed ALIGN comparison controlling for hyperparameters and graph construction.
- Recommend clarifying the definition of “mention” and the relationship to “mention embeddings.”
- Recommend situating results among broader unsupervised EL baselines and comparing different disambiguation orders.
