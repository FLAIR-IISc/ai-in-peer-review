- Task: Entity disambiguation and linking (EDL) mapping textual mentions to English Wikipedia entities; paper frames broader text-knowledge integration but core is EDL.  
- Method: Two-step pipeline—mentions → mention senses → entities—with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities optimized by a single objective.  
- Technical soundness: Uses contemporary deep-learning embedding techniques and a unified training objective.  
- Empirical result: MPME/SPME (multi-prototype mention embeddings) outperform a direct one-step baseline (ALIGN) on the benchmark (Table 2).  
- Main gap: The paper does not clearly justify why the intermediate mention-sense step helps beyond a direct mention→entity mapping.  
- Construction: Entity profiles are built from related entities in a KG via word-level relatedness of Wikipedia page text and embedded.  
- Word profiles: Use standard distributional semantics without sense disambiguation.  
- Mention-sense profiles: Use distributional semantics with sense disambiguation via a local-context language model and neighboring mentions, but procedural details are limited.  
- Ambiguity about senses: Unclear how mention senses are created, how many senses per mention are allowed, and what defines distinct senses.  
- Potential redundancy: If mention senses are derived mainly from entities, the sense layer may be nearly bijective with entities, questioning added value.  
- Hypothesis: Mention senses might capture context-specific words not on Wikipedia pages; paper should quantify these differences and their effect.  
- Alternative: If extra words drive gains, simple augmentation of entity profiles might achieve similar improvements—this is not tested or discussed.  
- Prior work omission: Claiming novelty on mention ambiguity is inaccurate—TAC KBP EDL evaluations and systems (e.g., KBP 2015–2016) are relevant baselines.  
- Positive detail: The smoothing parameter introduced in Section 6.4.2 is a practically useful contribution.  
- Contribution 1: Empirical demonstration that multi-prototype mention embeddings plus disambiguation improves entity linking and yields SOTA results.  
- Contribution 2: Joint learning framework that embeds words, entities, and mention senses in a unified space.  
- Contribution 3: A practical language-model-based disambiguation approach for mapping mentions to senses (needs more detail).  
- Strength: Strong quantitative results and clear technical presentation that align with current embedding practices.  
- Strength: A single objective for joint training simplifies learning and enforces cohesive representations.  
- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical/empirical explanation for the two-step architecture’s superiority.  
- Weakness: Lack of reproducibility details for sense disambiguation—creation algorithm and sense count criteria missing.  
- Weakness: Failure to cite and compare to TAC KBP systems undermines positioning against prior work.  
- Weakness: Unclear what unique information mention-sense layer captures beyond entity representations.  
- Questions for authors: Provide a detailed algorithm and criteria for sense creation and counts; quantify unique words captured by mention senses vs. Wikipedia pages; compare to top TAC KBP systems; and give an intuitive explanation why the two-step approach improves over direct mapping.
