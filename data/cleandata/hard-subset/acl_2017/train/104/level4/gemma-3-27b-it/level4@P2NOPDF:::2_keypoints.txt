- Paper is actually focused on entity disambiguation and linking (EDL) despite framing as broadly bridging text and knowledge.  
- Proposed two-step method inserts a “mention sense” layer: (1) mention → mention sense, (2) mention sense → entity, with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Method is technically sound and consistent with current deep learning/embedding practices.  
- Main concern: paper fails to justify why the intermediate mention-sense layer improves over direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN).  
- Empirical gains (Table 2) are shown but the paper does not explain what extra information mention senses capture that entities do not.  
- Critical methodological details about mention-sense creation and differentiation are missing (how senses are generated, how many per mention, initialization).  
- Unclear whether mention senses are derived from the KB and whether they map one-to-one to entities or are meaningfully distinct.  
- Question whether mention-sense profiles simply replicate entity profiles or truly add context words absent from entity pages; could entity profiles be augmented instead.  
- If the mention-sense interpretation is the core novelty, Section 5 should explicitly state and justify it.  
- Claim of being “first to deal with mention ambiguity” is incorrect—TAC KBP EDL tasks and systems have addressed this problem.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a useful contribution.  
- Reviewer remains unconvinced by authors’ rebuttal that KBP evaluations are irrelevant—embedding quality must be validated by downstream task performance.
