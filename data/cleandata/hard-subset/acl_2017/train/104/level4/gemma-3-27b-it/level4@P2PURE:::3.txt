- Strengths:
The paper presents strong ideas, employs a simple neural learning approach, achieves interesting (though not groundbreaking) performance, and demonstrates applicability to a wide range of tasks.
- Weaknesses:
The amount of novel contribution and the clarity of certain sections could be improved. 

This paper introduces a neural learning method for entity disambiguation and linking. A key strength is the integration of entity, mention, and sense modeling within a unified neural language modeling framework. This simplicity in training, coupled with the modeling approach, enables support for a large set of applications.

The paper is formally clear, but the depth of discussion does not consistently match the sophistication of the technical ideas. The empirical evaluation is sound, although the performance improvements reported are not substantial. While building on the work of Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016), the paper introduces novel ideas and is of relevant interest to the community.

The paper’s weaknesses include:
- Instances of unclear prose. Specifically, Section 3 lacked clarity, and details within Figure 2 were not fully explained. The terminology also felt somewhat redundant; for example, the distinctions between “dictionary of mentions” and “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” are unclear, as are their relationships to “text anchors” and “types for annotated text anchors.”
- A close relationship to Yamada et al. (2016), which the authors should explicitly address by outlining the key differences.

A general observation:
The paper evaluates the Multiple Embedding model on entity linking/disambiguation tasks. However, word embeddings are utilized not only for these tasks, but also for tasks less directly tied to a knowledge base, such as parsing, coreference resolution, and semantic role labeling. The authors should demonstrate that the word embeddings produced by the proposed MPME method are not inferior to those from simpler wordspaces on these broader semantic tasks, particularly those involving entity mentions.
