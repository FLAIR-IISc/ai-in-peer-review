- Focus: Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia using a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model with an intermediate mention-sense representation.
- Method: MPME uses a two-step pipeline (mention → mention sense → entity) and jointly trains embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.
- Primary contribution: Introduces multi-prototype mention embeddings to model mention senses as an intermediate representation for entity linking.
- Secondary contribution: Proposes an efficient language-model-based approach for mention sense disambiguation.
- Empirical result: Reports state-of-the-art performance and clear improvements over single-step baselines (e.g., ALIGN) in Table 2.
- Model quality: MPME is technically sound, coherently presented, and uses a reasonable joint training objective.
- Problem framing: Clearly motivates mention ambiguity and justifies the idea of modeling mention senses.
- Implementation detail: Smoothing parameter (Section 6.4.2) is a thoughtful and useful practical innovation.
- Weakness — justification: Paper lacks a clear explanation of why the two-step mention-sense process is inherently superior to direct mention→entity mapping.
- Weakness — sense creation: The sense disambiguation procedure is under-specified (number of senses, data sources, sense boundaries unclear).
- Weakness — novelty claim: Claiming to be first to handle mention ambiguity is inaccurate given prior TAC KBP and related work.
- Weakness — redundancy: Unclear why a distinct mention-sense embedding space is necessary versus enriching the entity space; needs analysis of unique information captured.
- Question 1: How are mention senses created and differentiated, and what determines how many senses a mention has?
- Question 2: What specific information do mention-sense embeddings capture that entity embeddings do not, and can this be quantified?
- Question 3: How exactly are profiles for entities, words, and mention senses constructed—please summarize differences in a table.
- Question 4: How does this work relate to TAC KBP and prior entity-linking efforts, and do Wikipedia vs. Freebase choices justify excluding those baselines?
- Question 5: What language model architecture is used for sense disambiguation, how is it trained, and what is its impact?
- Bibliography issue: References are formatted inconsistently and contain frequent duplicates (e.g., repeated Aho and Corasick).
- Related work: Expand discussion to include more recent advances in entity linking and mention disambiguation.
