- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings by jointly modeling textual contexts and KB entities with an LM-based disambiguation.  
- Contribution: introduces multi-prototype mention embeddings integrating text and knowledge to capture mention ambiguity.  
- Contribution: achieves state-of-the-art entity linking on a benchmark and analyzes the smoothing parameter's impact.  
- Strength: Outperforms ALIGN in supervised entity linking, suggesting improved joint text–KB representations.  
- Strength: Direct, similar-data comparison to ALIGN enables a focused evaluation of advantages.  
- Strength: Analysis of the smoothing parameter addresses popularity bias and offers practical insights.  
- Strength: Qualitative analysis provides additional insight into learned embeddings and disambiguation.  
- Weakness: Comparison to ALIGN is not fully controlled—differences in hyperparameters and neighborhood graph definition weaken the claim.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology for “mention” (type vs. instance); paper actually focuses on mention senses.  
- Weakness: Contribution of the sense disambiguation component is unclear without comparisons to unsupervised EL methods.  
- Question: Clarify N(ej): does it include only entities linking to ej, and is the graph directed or undirected?  
- Question: Provide an ablation study isolating multi-prototype embeddings from the sense disambiguation component.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters, especially window size and embedding dimension?  
- Additional: Reference list contains repetitions and inconsistent formatting—standardize citations.  
- Additional: Provide a more intuitive explanation of the language model used for disambiguation.
