- Presents a unified neural method for entity disambiguation/linking that models entities, mentions, and senses together.
- Training procedure is simple and the approach is conceptually straightforward and easy to adapt.
- Method supports broad applicability and a large set of downstream tasks.
- Empirical evaluation is solid but performance gains are positive yet not substantial.
- Builds upon prior work (Yamada et al., 2016) but the novel contributions over that work are not clearly delineated.
- Major clarity issues: Section 3 needs more detailed explanation of modeling choices and component connections.
- Figure 2 lacks sufficient explanation and requires an expanded caption or text description.
- Terminology is confusing and sometimes redundant (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs. “dictionary of entity-mention pairs”; “text anchors” and “types”).
- Related work discussion is insufficient; must explicitly compare and contrast with Yamada et al. (2016).
- Important missing evaluation: show that MPME-generated word embeddings are competitive with general-purpose embeddings (Word2Vec/GloVe) on broader NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL).
- Author response addressed some points but did not fully resolve concerns about clarity and novelty.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying methodology/terminology, better positioning vis-à-vis Yamada et al., and demonstrating general-purpose embedding utility.
