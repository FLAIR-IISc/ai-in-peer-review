The framework outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, indicating improved joint text-and-knowledge representations.

Direct comparison to a closely related approach using similar input data is provided.

The smoothing-parameter analysis is insightful and addresses popularity bias in entity linking.

Comparison to ALIGN is weakened by differing context window sizes and vector dimensions and by unclear definitions of N(e_j) and adjacency symmetry, obscuring causes of performance differences.

The term “mention” is used ambiguously (string vs. document-specific mention), and “mention embeddings” appear to be learned only for mention senses.

The paper lacks comparisons to other unsupervised entity-linking methods to assess the effect of sense disambiguation order.
