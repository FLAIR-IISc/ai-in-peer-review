- Proposes a joint learning framework that learns representations for text and knowledge-graph entities using local context and global graph structure.
- Demonstrates improved performance over ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task, indicating stronger joint embeddings.
- Direct comparison to ALIGN is useful but fairness is questionable due to differing hyperparameters (window size 10 vs 5, vector dims 500 vs 200).
- Requires sensitivity analyses or ablation studies to isolate effects of hyperparameters and modeling choices.
- Definition of N(ej) is unclear—uncertain if it includes only entities linking to ej or a broader neighborhood.
- Must clarify whether the Wikipedia outlink graph adjacency is treated as directed or undirected.
- Terminology is imprecise: “mention” sometimes means a string and sometimes an instance; “mention embedding” may actually be sense embeddings.
- The contribution of the changed neighborhood definition to entity similarity performance is unclear without clarification.
- Impact of the proposed sense disambiguation order is hard to assess without comparisons to other established unsupervised entity linking methods.
- Analysis of the smoothing parameter and discussion of entity popularity effects are insightful strengths.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking authors to detail hyperparameter choices/sensitivity, clarify N(ej) and adjacency treatment, fix terminology, and add comparisons to more unsupervised baselines.
