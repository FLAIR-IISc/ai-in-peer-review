- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) to Wikipedia, though title/introduction overclaim by suggesting broader "bridging text and knowledge."
- Method uses a two-step intermediate layer of "mention senses": (1) mention → mention sense, (2) mention sense → entity, with joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.
- Technical approach is sound and follows current deep learning and embedding best practices, but alternatives may achieve similar results.
- Improvement of two-step (MPME/SPME) over one-step methods is shown (Table 2) but the rationale for why mention senses help is not explained.
- Details on how mention senses are created and differentiated (sense induction, number of senses per mention) are missing and need clarification.
- Unclear whether mention senses map bijectively to entities and whether sense profiles are initially seeded from entity text; need to explain what unique information mention senses capture beyond entity pages.
- Question whether mention-sense-specific words (absent from entity pages) exist, how many there are on average, and whether their information could instead augment entity representations.
- The brief mention-disambiguation model (local context words and neighboring mentions) is underspecified and should be expanded (esp. before/around Section 4).
- Claim of novelty in Section 6 is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL evaluations and systems provide relevant baselines (see TAC KBP 2016).
- Positive note: the smoothing parameter described in Section 6.4.2 is useful and appreciated.
