This paper addresses the problem of disambiguating/linking textual entity mentions to a knowledge base (English Wikipedia). While the title and introduction suggest a broad contribution to bridging text and knowledge, the work focuses specifically on entity disambiguation and linking (EDL). The method introduces a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model, which operates via an intermediate representation of mention senses, following a two-step process: (1) mention to mention sense, and (2) mention sense to entity. Embeddings are learned for words, mention senses, and entities, and jointly trained to maximize a single objective function.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the exploration of multi-prototype mention embeddings as an intermediate step in entity linking. By explicitly modeling mention senses, the authors aim to capture nuanced meanings that a direct mention-to-entity mapping might miss. The empirical results, showing improvements over a single-step baseline (ALIGN), suggest that this intermediate representation is beneficial. A secondary contribution is the design of an efficient language model-based approach for mention sense disambiguation.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the entity linking task, utilizing the proposed MPME model. Table 2 clearly shows improvements over competitive baselines, including the single-step ALIGN approach. This provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
2. **Well-Defined Model:** The MPME model is technically sound and logically presented. The authors leverage existing techniques like embeddings and language models in a coherent manner. The joint training objective appears appropriately formulated for the task.
3. **Clear Problem Formulation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenge of mention ambiguity in the context of knowledge base integration. The motivation for introducing mention senses as an intermediate representation is well-defined.
4. **Smoothing Parameter Innovation:** The smoothing parameter described in Section 6.4.2 is a clever addition and demonstrates a thoughtful consideration of practical implementation details.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Justification for Two-Step Process:** The paper does not sufficiently explain *why* a two-step process, involving mention senses, is superior to a direct mention-to-entity mapping. While results demonstrate an improvement, the underlying reason for this improvement remains unclear. The authors need to provide a more intuitive explanation of what additional information the mention sense representation captures.
2. **Vague Sense Disambiguation Details:** The description of the sense disambiguation process is insufficient. Specifically, the creation and differentiation of mention senses are not fully explained. Questions regarding the number of senses per mention, the data sources used for sense creation (e.g., knowledge base, contextual data), and the method for defining sense boundaries are left unanswered.
3. **Overstated Novelty:** The claim that this is the "first work to deal with mention ambiguity" in text and knowledge integration is inaccurate. The TAC KBP evaluations have extensively addressed this problem for several years. This oversight diminishes the perceived novelty of the work.
4. **Potential for Redundancy:** The paper does not convincingly demonstrate why keeping the disambiguation information in a separate 'mention sense' space is necessary, rather than simply incorporating it into the entity space. A more thorough analysis of the unique information captured at the mention sense level (compared to the entity level) is needed.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the sense disambiguation process? Specifically, how are mention senses created and differentiated? What determines the number of senses a mention string can have?
2. What specific information is captured at the mention sense level that is *not* already present in the entity representations? Can you quantify, on average, how much new information is added by the mention sense layer?
3. How do the profiles for entities, words, and mention senses differ in construction beyond the sense disambiguation step? A table summarizing the profile creation process for each would be helpful.
4. Given the existence of the TAC KBP evaluations, how does this work relate to those prior efforts? Do the authors believe the focus on Wikipedia versus Freebase justifies ignoring those related baselines?
5. Could you clarify the role and impact of the language model used for sense disambiguation? What architecture is used, and how is it trained?

**Additional Comments:**

* The references are formatted inconsistently. Please ensure uniform formatting throughout.
* The frequent repetition of references (e.g., Aho and Corasick) suggests a potential issue with the bibliography management tool.
* Consider expanding the discussion of related work to include more recent advances in entity linking and mention disambiguation.
