- Framework outperforms ALIGN on supervised entity linking, implying improved joint text–knowledge representations.
- Direct comparison with a closely related approach using similar input data allows for a nuanced evaluation.
- Insightful analysis of the smoothing parameter, addressing the effect of entity popularity in entity linking.
- Comparison with ALIGN is weakened by differing experimental settings (context window size, vector dimensionality) and unclear definition of N(e_j) regarding directed vs. undirected adjacency, which could affect learned vectors and similarity scores.
- Terminology around “mention” is ambiguous—unclear if embeddings are for string-type mentions or specific mention instances; “mention embedding” appears to refer to mention senses only.
- Impact of sense disambiguation order is hard to assess due to lack of comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods.
