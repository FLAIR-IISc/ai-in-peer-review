- Paper tackles entity disambiguation/linking to Wikipedia using a two-step process with an intermediate “mention sense” layer and joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Title/intro overreach: bridging text and knowledge is broader than the presented EDL focus.  
- Methodologically sound and aligned with current deep-learning/embedding practices.  
- Main concern: paper fails to justify why the two-step mention-sense approach is superior to direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN/Yamada et al.).  
- Table 2 shows MPME/SPME outperform one-step baselines, but the reason for improvement is unexplained.  
- Need clarification on what extra information mention senses provide beyond entities.  
- Reviewer’s interpretation: entities = profiles of related entities from a knowledge graph implemented as extended skip-gram embeddings — please confirm.  
- Words = standard distributional embeddings without sense disambiguation — please confirm.  
- Mention senses = distributional representations with sense disambiguation using local context and neighboring mentions, but the creation/differentiation process is under-specified.  
- Key unanswered questions: How are senses created and distinguished? What fixes the number of senses per mention string?  
- If senses map bijectively to entities, why are separate mention senses needed and how are sense profiles initially seeded?  
- What information at the mention-sense level is not already present in entity profiles (e.g., co-occurring words absent from Wikipedia pages), and how many such words exist on average?  
- Need justification for maintaining a separate mention-sense space versus augmenting entity profiles directly; if accurate, emphasize this as the main contribution in Section 5.  
- Claim of being the first to handle mention ambiguity in text+knowledge integration is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL evaluations and systems exist (using Freebase) and should be cited.  
- Positive: smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is appreciated.  
- Post-response: reviewer remains unconvinced by authors’ rebuttal—practical application performance is necessary to validate embedding quality; conceptual claims alone are insufficient.
