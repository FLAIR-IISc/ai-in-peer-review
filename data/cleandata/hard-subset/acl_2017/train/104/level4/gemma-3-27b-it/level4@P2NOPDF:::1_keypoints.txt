- Novel joint learning framework for text and knowledge representations that outperforms ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task.  
- Direct, relevant comparison to a closely related method (ALIGN) using similar input data.  
- Insightful analysis of the smoothing parameter and its effect on mitigating entity popularity bias.  
- Comparison to ALIGN is confounded by differing hyperparameters (content window size 10 vs 5; vector dim 500 vs 200), making it hard to isolate the framework’s contribution.  
- Ambiguous definition of N(e_j); unclear whether it counts only entities linking to e_j and whether adjacency is treated as directed or undirected.  
- Inconsistent terminology around “mention” and “mention embedding” — appears to learn mention senses rather than mention instances, requiring clearer language.  
- Missing comparisons to other established unsupervised entity linking methods, so the benefit of the proposed sense disambiguation order is not fully validated.  
- Recommendation: clarify ALIGN comparison settings, define graph adjacency/N(e_j) explicitly, standardize terminology, and broaden evaluations to strengthen conclusions.
