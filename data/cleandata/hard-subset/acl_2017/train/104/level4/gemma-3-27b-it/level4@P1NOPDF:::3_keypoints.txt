- Strength: Presents good ideas with a simple neural learning approach, showing interesting (though not groundbreaking) performance and many potential applications.  
- Strength: Integrates entity, mention, and sense modeling within a single neural language-modeling framework.  
- Strength: Simplicity in training and modeling enables support for a wide range of applications.  
- Formal clarity is good, but the depth of discussion does not always match the technical sophistication.  
- Empirical evaluation is sound, but performance gains are modest rather than dramatic.  
- The work builds on Yamada et al. (CoNLL 2016) yet introduces novel concepts and is of community interest.  
- Weakness: Amount of novel content is somewhat limited.  
- Weakness: Clarity/presentation issues—Section 3 is hard to follow, Figure 2 lacks explanation, and terminology feels redundant.  
- Specific clarification requested: justify the distinction between “dictionary of mentions” and “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” and how these differ from text anchors/types.  
- Weakness: Related work discussion is insufficient—authors should explicitly outline key differences from Yamada et al. (2016).  
- General suggestion: Demonstrate that MPME-produced word embeddings are not inferior to simpler embeddings on broader semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) involving entity mentions.
