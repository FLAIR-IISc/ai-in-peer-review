- Strengths:
* The proposed framework outperforms ALIGN on the supervised entity linking task, indicating improved joint representations of text and knowledge.
* The paper provides a direct comparison to a closely related approach using similar input data.
* The analysis of the smoothing parameter is insightful, addressing the persistent challenge of popularity bias in entity linking.

- Weaknesses:
* The comparison to ALIGN could be strengthened. ALIGN employed a content window size of 10, while this paper uses 5, and a vector dimension of 500 compared to 200 here. Furthermore, it is unclear whether N(ej) includes only entities linking *to* ej. Given the directed nature of the graph, constructed from Wikipedia outlinks, it is also unclear whether adjacency is defined symmetrically.  ALIGN defines entity context as the set of linking entities; if N(e_j) is defined differently, it is difficult to assess the impact of this change on the learned vector representations, and this may contribute to the observed performance difference in the entity similarity task.
* The terminology regarding “mention” is occasionally ambiguous, shifting between a mention as a string and a specific mention within a document. While the paper uses the phrase “mention embedding,” the embeddings appear to be learned only for mention senses.
* Evaluating the impact of sense disambiguation order requires comparison with other unsupervised entity linking methods, which is currently lacking.
