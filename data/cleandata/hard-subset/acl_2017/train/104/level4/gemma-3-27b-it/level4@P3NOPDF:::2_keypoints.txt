- Paper actually addresses entity disambiguation and linking (EDL), not the broader “bridging text and knowledge” claim in title/introduction.
- Method introduces an intermediate mention-sense layer and decomposes EDL into mention→mention-sense and mention-sense→entity, learning joint embeddings for words, senses, and entities.
- Approach is technically sound and follows current deep-learning embedding practices.
- Primary criticism: paper does not justify why a two-step mention-sense approach is superior to a direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN).
- Table 2 shows MPME/SPME outperforming baselines, but the mechanism driving this improvement is not explained.
- Unclear what mention senses capture that entity representations do not—need explicit description of the additional signal senses provide.
- Reviewer's interpretation: entity profiles use neighboring-entity relatedness, word profiles use standard distributional semantics, and mention-sense profiles use sense-disambiguated local context—but the paper omits details of this pipeline.
- Missing details on sense creation and differentiation: how is the number of senses per mention determined?
- If senses are dictated by the KB, that implies a bijection between mention senses and entities—this implication is not discussed.
- Unclear whether mention-sense profiles are initialized from entity text (“seed words”) and what contextual words are exclusive to mention senses versus entity pages; recommend quantifying average such words per sense.
- Paper needs to justify why a separate mention-sense embedding space is more effective than augmenting entity representations with those context words.
- If the above interpretation is correct, Section 5 should be revised to clearly state this as the core contribution.
- The claim of being “first” to handle mention ambiguity in integrated text/knowledge is incorrect—TAC KBP EDL systems have addressed this (links provided).
- Positive point: the smoothing parameter in Section 6.4.2 is a clever and potentially useful addition.
- Post-response, reviewer remains unconvinced by the authors’ dismissal of TAC KBP relevance and emphasizes that embedding quality must be validated via downstream tasks, not just conceptual arguments.
