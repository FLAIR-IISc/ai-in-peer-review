## Review:

**Strengths:**

* The proposed framework demonstrates performance exceeding that of ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task, indicating successful learning of improved joint text and knowledge representations.
* The paper provides a direct comparative analysis against a closely related methodology, utilizing highly similar input data.
* The investigation into the smoothing parameter’s influence is insightful, addressing a common and significant challenge in entity linking – the impact of entity popularity.

**Weaknesses:**

* The comparison to ALIGN could be strengthened. Discrepancies in hyperparameters (content window size of 10 for ALIGN vs. 5 here, and vector dimensions of 500 vs. 200) are not fully addressed. Furthermore, clarification is needed regarding the composition of N(ej); specifically, whether it encompasses only entities linking *to* ej. Given the directed nature of the Wikipedia outlink graph, the definition of adjacency – whether it reflects an undirected or directed graph structure – requires clarification. The context of an entity in ALIGN is defined by entities linking *to* it; differing definitions of N(e_j) make it difficult to isolate the contribution of this change to observed performance differences on entity similarity.
* The terminology surrounding “mention” occasionally lacks precision, shifting between referring to a string-type mention and a specific mention within a document. While “mention embedding” is used, the learned embeddings appear to be at the mention *sense* level, which should be clarified.
* Assessing the contribution of the sense disambiguation order is challenging without comparison to established unsupervised entity linking baselines.
