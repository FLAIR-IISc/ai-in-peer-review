This paper proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) model to address ambiguity in representing entity mentions within a unified semantic space. The core idea is to learn multiple sense embeddings for each mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and knowledge base entities. An efficient language model is then used for mention disambiguation. The authors demonstrate the quality of their embeddings and disambiguation method through entity linking experiments, achieving state-of-the-art results.

**Contributions:**

1. **Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings:** The primary contribution is the MPME model itself, which effectively learns multiple sense embeddings for entity mentions. This addresses the inherent ambiguity of mentions referring to different entities, a crucial issue in integrating text and knowledge.
2. **Joint Modeling of Text and Knowledge:** The paper presents a concrete approach to jointly modeling textual contexts and knowledge base entities within a single framework. This integration is valuable for improving the representation of mentions and enabling effective disambiguation.
3. **Strong Entity Linking Performance:** The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on a standard entity linking benchmark, demonstrating its practical effectiveness.

**Strengths:**

1. **Solid Technical Approach:** The combination of multiple prototype embeddings with a language modeling approach is a well-motivated and technically sound strategy for tackling mention ambiguity. The method appears relatively simple to implement and train, facilitating broader adoption.
2. **Empirically Validated:** The paper provides empirical evidence, through entity linking results, that supports the effectiveness of the proposed method. While the improvements may not be dramatic, achieving state-of-the-art performance is a positive signal.
3. **Relevance to Current Research:** The problem of integrating textual and knowledge-based information is a central challenge in NLP, making this work relevant and potentially impactful.  The focus on mention disambiguation is a crucial step towards achieving more robust and accurate knowledge integration.
4. **Clear Presentation (Generally):** The paper is largely well-structured and formally clear, aiding understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty:** The work builds heavily on previous approaches, particularly that of Yamada et al. (2016). The authors need to more clearly articulate the specific novel contributions of MPME beyond this existing work. A more detailed comparison would strengthen the paper.
2. **Clarity in Specific Sections:** Section 3 lacks clarity in certain areas. The details of Figure 2 require further explanation, and the terminology used – specifically surrounding “dictionaries of mentions” and “entity-mention pairs” – is somewhat redundant and potentially confusing. Defining the relationship between these concepts and “text anchors” would improve readability.
3. **Narrow Empirical Evaluation:** The evaluation focuses solely on entity linking. While achieving state-of-the-art results is commendable, the authors should consider demonstrating the generalizability of the learned word embeddings to other semantic tasks that *do not* directly rely on entity mentions (e.g., parsing, coreference resolution, semantic role labeling). This would showcase the broader utility of the MPME model.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more detailed comparison to Yamada et al. (2016), explicitly outlining the key differences and innovations introduced by the MPME model?
2. Could you clarify the terminology used in Section 3, specifically the relationship between “dictionaries of mentions,” “dictionaries of entity-mention pairs,” “text anchors,” and “types for annotated text anchors”?
3. What experiments, if any, have you conducted to evaluate the performance of the MPME-generated word embeddings on semantic tasks that are not directly dependent on entity mentions, such as parsing or coreference resolution? If not, would you consider adding such an evaluation?
