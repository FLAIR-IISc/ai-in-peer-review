- Proposes MPME: learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by combining textual context and KB entities.
- Uses a language-model-based disambiguation method to select the appropriate sense for a mention.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on entity linking, validating the approach empirically.

- Strength: conceptually strong integration of textual and knowledge-based signals into multi-prototype embeddings.
- Strength: language-model disambiguation is a natural and effective mechanism for sense selection.
- Strength: achieves SOTA performance on entity linking, providing strong empirical support.
- Strength: paper is generally well-structured with a clear formal and architectural description.
- Strength: training procedure is simple and practically applicable to downstream tasks.

- Weakness: Section 3 lacks clarity; Figure 2 and some explanations are unclear.
- Weakness: Terminology (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs “dictionary of entity-mention pairs”) feels redundant and confusing.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of novelty and explicit comparison to related work, especially Yamada et al. (2016).
- Weakness: Evaluation scope is narrow—focuses mainly on entity linking and not on tasks that don’t rely on KB entities.

- Question: Please clarify the different dictionaries in Section 3 and their relation to text anchors and annotated anchors.
- Question: What are the key distinctions between MPME and Yamada et al. (2016)? Please provide a detailed comparison.
- Question: Have you evaluated the learned embeddings on non-KB tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL)? If not, do you plan to?

- Additional comment: Reference list has inconsistent formatting and repetitions—please standardize and clean references.
