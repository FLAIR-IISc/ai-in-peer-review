- Framework outperforms ALIGN on a supervised entity linking task, showing improved joint text and knowledge representations.  
- Direct comparative analysis against a closely related method using very similar input data.  
- Insightful analysis of the smoothing parameter and its effect on entity popularity in linking.  
- Comparison to ALIGN is weakened by unexplained hyperparameter differences (content window size, vector dimensions) and unclear definition of N(e_j) and graph adjacency (directed vs. undirected).  
- Terminology around “mention” and “mention embedding” is imprecise; embeddings appear to be at the mention sense level and should be clarified.  
- Hard to assess the contribution of sense disambiguation order without comparison to established unsupervised entity linking baselines.
