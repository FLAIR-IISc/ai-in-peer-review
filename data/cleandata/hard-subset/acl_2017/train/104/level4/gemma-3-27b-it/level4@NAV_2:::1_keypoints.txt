- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by combining textual context and KB entities with LM-based disambiguation.
- Main contributions: novel multi-sense embedding approach, empirical state-of-the-art entity linking results, and analysis of a smoothing parameter affecting popularity vs. specificity trade-offs.
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on a benchmark entity linking dataset, indicating effective joint text–knowledge representations.
- Strength: Provides a direct comparison to ALIGN, enabling focused evaluation of advantages over a closely related method.
- Strength: Offers a useful analysis of the smoothing parameter, addressing the persistent issue of entity popularity in entity linking.
- Strength: The multi-sense modeling of mentions is well-motivated and yields a more nuanced representation in a unified semantic space.
- Weakness: Comparison to ALIGN may be unfair due to differing hyperparameters and data handling (context window size, vector dimensionality).
- Weakness: The definition of the neighborhood function N(ej) is unclear, including whether it uses incoming links only and whether the graph is directed or undirected.
- Weakness: Ambiguous use of the term “mention” — unclear if it denotes a string type or a specific document instance — complicating understanding of which embeddings are learned.
- Weakness: Hard to determine the specific impact of the sense disambiguation component without comparisons to other unsupervised entity linking methods.
- Question: Please clarify the precise definition of N(ej) and whether the entity graph is treated as directed or undirected.
- Question: Provide a more detailed ablation study to isolate the contribution of the sense disambiguation module from the multi-prototype embeddings.
- Question: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters like context window size and vector dimension, and would matching ALIGN’s parameters yield a fairer comparison?
