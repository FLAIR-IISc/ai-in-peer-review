- Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings: MPME learns multiple sense embeddings per mention to address mention ambiguity.
- Joint Modeling of Text and Knowledge: The model jointly models textual contexts and KB entities in a unified framework to improve mention representations.
- Strong Entity Linking Performance: The method achieves state-of-the-art results on a standard entity linking benchmark.

- Solid Technical Approach: Combines multi-prototype embeddings with a language model in a simple, implementable framework for disambiguation.
- Empirically Validated: Entity linking experiments support the method’s effectiveness, yielding SOTA performance even if gains are modest.
- Relevant to Current Research: Tackles the core NLP challenge of integrating text and knowledge for robust mention disambiguation.
- Clear Presentation (Generally): The paper is largely well-structured and formally clear.

- Limited Novelty: Heavily builds on prior work (notably Yamada et al., 2016) and needs clearer articulation of what is novel.
- Clarity in Specific Sections: Section 3 and Figure 2 lack clarity; terminology around mention/entity dictionaries and text anchors is confusing.
- Narrow Empirical Evaluation: Evaluation is limited to entity linking and does not demonstrate embedding generalizability to other semantic tasks.

- Comparison to Yamada et al. (2016): Please provide a detailed comparison outlining specific differences and innovations.
- Terminology Clarification: Clarify relationships among “dictionaries of mentions,” “entity-mention pairs,” “text anchors,” and “types for annotated text anchors.”
- Additional Evaluations: Have you evaluated MPME embeddings on non-mention semantic tasks (e.g., parsing, coreference, SRL); if not, consider adding such experiments.
