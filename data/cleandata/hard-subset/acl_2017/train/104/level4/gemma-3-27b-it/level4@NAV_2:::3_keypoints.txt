- Proposes MPME: learn multiple sense embeddings per mention to address ambiguity by jointly modeling text contexts and KB entities.  
- Uses a language-model-based disambiguation method to select the appropriate sense embedding for a mention.  
- Claims contributions: (1) framework for multi, context-aware mention embeddings capturing polysemy.  
- Claims contributions: (2) efficient LM-based disambiguation to pick the correct sense embedding.  
- Claims contributions: (3) state-of-the-art results on entity linking demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Strength: Presents a compelling, well-motivated approach for handling mention ambiguity in text–knowledge integration.  
- Strength: Effectively integrates textual context and KB information within a unified neural framework.  
- Strength: Simple training procedure that supports scalability and broad applicability.  
- Strength: Solid empirical results on a standard benchmark, though improvements are not dramatic.  
- Strength: High potential for improving downstream tasks that use mention representations.  
- Weakness: Presentation lacks clarity—Section 3 and parts of Figure 2 need more detailed explanation.  
- Weakness: Terminology (dictionaries of mentions vs. entity-mention pairs, text anchors) is redundant and confusing.  
- Weakness: Relationship and differences to prior work, especially Yamada et al. (2016), are not clearly delineated.  
- Weakness: No evaluation of learned embeddings on broader semantic tasks (e.g., parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they don’t degrade general performance.  
- Q1: Request more implementation details on the multi-prototype mention embedding learning process (Section 3).  
- Q2: Ask for clarification of “dictionary of mentions” vs. “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” and relation to “text anchors” and “types for annotated text anchors.”  
- Q3: Request explicit outline of key differences from Yamada et al. (2016).  
- Q4: Ask whether authors will evaluate embeddings on tasks beyond entity linking (parsing, coreference) or discuss potential impact on those tasks.
