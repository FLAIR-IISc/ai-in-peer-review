- Proposal of MPME: a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities.  
- Language model–based disambiguation: a practical LM approach to map mentions to specific sense embeddings during entity linking.  
- Strong empirical claim: achieves state-of-the-art results on a standard entity linking benchmark.  

- Novelty: extends joint text–knowledge representation by explicitly modeling multiple mention senses, addressing mention ambiguity.  
- Empirical rigor: thorough evaluation on a standard EL dataset with clear presentation and analysis of quantitative results.  
- Clear motivation: well-formulated problem statement linking mention ambiguity to real-world entity linking needs.  
- Practicality: implementation and training appear straightforward and scalable due to LM framework.  

- Clarity issues: Section 3 and Figure 2 descriptions and certain terminology are confusing and redundant.  
- Insufficient differentiation: lacks a detailed comparison to closely related work (e.g., Yamada et al., 2016) and clearer statement of novel contributions.  
- Narrow evaluation: experiments only cover entity linking; no evidence of embedding utility on other NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL).  
- Missing ablation: no ablation study to quantify each component’s contribution (multi-prototype, KB integration, LM).  

- Clarify Section 3 terminology: distinguish "dictionary of mentions," "dictionary of entity-mention pairs," "text anchors," and "types for annotated text anchors."  
- Explicitly compare to Yamada et al. (2016): detail methodological differences and specific improvements.  
- Evaluate broader utility: test learned embeddings on other NLP tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) or explain barriers.  
- Provide ablation results: quantify the impact of multi-prototype learning, KB integration, and the language model.  

- Fix reference formatting inconsistencies in the bibliography.
