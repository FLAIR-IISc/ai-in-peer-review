- Introduces a neural method for entity disambiguation/linking that unifies entity, mention, and sense modeling in a simple neural language model supporting many applications.  
- Empirical evaluation is sound and shows interesting but not dramatic performance gains.  
- Formal presentation is clear, but the prose and depth of discussion are sometimes insufficient relative to the technical ideas.  
- Novelty is limited overall, though the paper does contribute some new ideas and is relevant to the community.  
- Clarity issues in Section 3 and Figure 2; terminology is sometimes redundant or unexplained (e.g., “dictionary of mentions” vs. “dictionary of entity-mention pairs,” relation to text anchors and types).  
- Strong relation to Yamada et al. (2016); authors should explicitly outline key differences from that work.  
- Multiple Embedding (MPME) is evaluated for entity linking, but authors should show its word embeddings do not perform worse than simpler wordspaces on other semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) that use direct entity mentions.
