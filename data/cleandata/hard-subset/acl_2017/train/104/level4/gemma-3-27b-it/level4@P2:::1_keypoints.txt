- Paper proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding: learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by integrating textual context and KB entities, plus LM-based disambiguation, achieving SOTA entity linking.

Contributions:
- Novel multi-prototype mention embeddings capture different senses using text and knowledge-base information.
- Effective disambiguation component uses a language model to select specific senses.
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art performance on a benchmark entity linking dataset.

Strengths:
- Superior performance over ALIGN on supervised entity linking, indicating meaningful advancement.
- Direct comparison to ALIGN aids understanding of benefits.
- Smoothing-parameter analysis provides useful insight into handling entity popularity.
- Clear presentation of entity linking results.

Weaknesses:
- Comparison to ALIGN is incomplete due to differing hyperparameters and unclear definition of neighborhood N(ej) (incoming vs outgoing links, directed vs undirected).
- Terminology ambiguity: "mention" is used inconsistently (type vs instance), making "mention embedding" unclear.
- Limited benchmarking against other unsupervised entity linking methods, weakening claims about disambiguation novelty.

Questions to authors:
- Clarify definition of N(ej) and whether the Wikipedia graph is treated as directed or undirected.
- How much of the performance difference versus ALIGN is due to hyperparameter choices (window size, vector dimension)? Have you run aligned-hyperparameter experiments?
- Please provide consistent, precise definitions of "mention" throughout the paper.
- How does your disambiguation method compare to other state-of-the-art unsupervised entity linking methods that do not use multi-prototype embeddings?

Additional comment:
- References are inconsistently formatted and contain errors/repetitions; please standardize the bibliography.
