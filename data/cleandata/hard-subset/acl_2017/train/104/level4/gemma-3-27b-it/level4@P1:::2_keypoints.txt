- Paper focuses on entity disambiguation and linking (EDL) to English Wikipedia, despite title/intro implying a broader text–knowledge bridging approach.  
- Method introduces an intermediate "mention senses" layer with a two-step mapping: mention → mention senses → entities, learning joint embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities.  
- Approach is technically sound and consistent with modern deep-learning embedding practices.  
- The paper fails to justify why a two-step mention-sense approach is superior to direct mention→entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN), despite better empirical results in Table 2.  
- Unclear what additional information mention senses provide beyond entity representations and why that information can’t be captured in the entity space.  
- Entity profiles are built from neighboring entities in a relatedness graph (likely from word-level relatedness in Wikipedia pages) and represented via an extended skip-gram model.  
- Word profiles use standard distributional semantics without sense disambiguation.  
- Mention-sense profiles use distributional semantics with sense disambiguation based on local context and neighboring mentions, but the construction/details are not sufficiently explained.  
- Key unanswered questions: how senses are created/differentiated, how many senses per mention, whether sense↔entity mapping is bijective, and whether sense profiles are seeded from entity text.  
- Need empirical evidence on what words are uniquely associated with mention senses but absent from entity pages, average counts per sense, and whether a separate mention-sense space is necessary versus augmenting entity representations.  
- The authors should clarify the mention-sense mechanism in Section 5, as it is the core novel contribution.  
- The claim of being the first to handle mention ambiguity in text–knowledge integration is inaccurate; prior TAC KBP EDL systems (against Freebase) are relevant baselines.  
- Positive: the smoothing parameter introduced in Section 6.4.2 is a useful contribution.
