- Proposes a neural method for entity disambiguation/linking that jointly models entities, mentions, and senses within a language modeling framework.  
- Core idea is sound and training simplicity enables application to many tasks.  
- Empirical results show modest but not groundbreaking performance gains.  
- Builds on prior work (notably Yamada et al. 2016) and introduces some novel elements, keeping the contribution relevant.  
- Paper lacks clarity in parts and contains limited truly novel content relative to prior work.  
- Section 3 needs clarification—Figure 2 has unexplained details and terminology feels redundant.  
- Unclear distinction between “dictionary of mentions” and “dictionary of entity-mention pairs” versus standard anchors/types.  
- Relationship and specific differences to Yamada et al. (2016) should be more fully articulated.  
- Evaluation is narrow; authors should test embeddings on broader semantic tasks (parsing, coreference, SRL) to show they aren’t worse than simpler wordspaces.  
- Reviewer appreciated and noted the authors’ response to initial feedback.
