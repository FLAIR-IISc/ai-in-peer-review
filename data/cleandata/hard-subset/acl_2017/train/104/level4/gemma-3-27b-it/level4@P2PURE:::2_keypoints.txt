- Paper addresses entity disambiguation/linking (EDL) using an intermediate mention-sense layer, decomposing linking into (mention -> mention sense) and (mention sense -> entity).

- Authors jointly learn embeddings for words, mention senses, and entities with a unified objective that equally weights all three types.

- Approach is technically well-defined and consistent with current deep learning and embedding practices.

- Major concern: paper fails to justify why a two-step mention-sense approach is needed versus direct mention->entity mapping (e.g., ALIGN).

- Empirical gains of MPME/SPME are reported, but the paper does not explain what additional information mention senses capture beyond entity-level representations.

- Critical methodological details are missing: how mention senses are created/differentiated, how many senses per mention, and whether senses map bijectively to entities.

- Unclear whether mention-sense profiles are initialized/seeded with entity text and what unique contextual words exist at the sense level but not in entity pages (and their average counts).

- Authors should explicitly state these assumptions and the role of mention senses in Section 5, since this is the paper’s core contribution.

- The claim of being the first work to handle mention ambiguity in text-knowledge integration is incorrect; TAC KBP EDL systems over recent years already address related problems (e.g., Freebase evaluations).

- Positive note: the smoothing parameter introduced in Section 6.4.2 is appreciated.

- After the authors’ response, the reviewer remains unconvinced; embedding quality must be validated via application-based evaluations (like KBP), not just conceptual arguments.
