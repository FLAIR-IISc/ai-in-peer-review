

# Peer Review

## Summary and Main Contributions

This submission appears to be a malformed reference list rather than a research paper. The document consists almost entirely of repeated, corrupted references to existing works, with numerous typos and inconsistencies. There is no actual paper content, methodology, results, or discussion section. The document includes multiple versions of the same references (e.g., Aho and Corasick 1975, Aoe 1989, Han et al. 2016) with varying misspellings of titles, author names, and journal names.

The submission claims no actual contributions as a research paper. The "paper" consists solely of a list of references with intentional or unintentional errors, suggesting it may be a test submission or a malformed attempt at a reference section. There are no research claims, no novel contributions, and no technical content to evaluate.

## Strengths

There are no genuine strengths to this submission as a research paper. The document does not present any original research, methodology, or results. The only potential "strength" would be the identification of a potential issue with reference formatting, but this is not a valid research contribution.

## Weaknesses

1. **Fundamental Submission Error**: This is not a research paper but rather a corrupted reference list. The submission completely lacks a title, abstract, introduction, methodology, experiments, results, or discussion sections - all essential components of a research paper. The document appears to be an attempt to submit a reference list instead of a paper.

2. **Numerous Typos and Inconsistencies**: The reference list contains severe and consistent typos across all entries (e.g., "Efficie t" instead of "Efficient", "doubl -array" instead of "double-array", "Xu H n" instead of "Xu Han"). These are not minor formatting errors but fundamental mistakes that suggest the submission was not properly prepared.

3. **Repeated References**: The document contains multiple identical or near-identical references with different typos (e.g., the Aho and Corasick paper appears 10+ times with different misspellings). This suggests either a copy-paste error or an attempt to artificially inflate references.

4. **Lack of Research Contribution**: There is no original research, no novel algorithms, no experiments, no results, and no analysis. The submission fails to meet the most basic requirements of a research paper submission to ACL.

5. **Invalid Submission Format**: The submission violates all standard academic submission requirements. The document does not follow ACL formatting guidelines, contains no paper content, and appears to be a test or mistake rather than a legitimate submission.

## Questions to Authors

1. Could you please clarify whether this was intended as a legitimate research paper submission or if there was a mistake in the submission process?

2. If this was a mistake, could you please resubmit a properly formatted paper with original research content?

3. Could you explain why the submission contains only a reference list with multiple typos rather than a research paper?

## Additional Comments

This submission does not meet the minimum requirements for consideration as a research paper. The document lacks all essential components of an academic paper and contains only a corrupted reference list with numerous errors. It appears the submission may have been created by copying reference entries without proper formatting or verification.

I recommend rejection of this submission as it does not constitute a valid research contribution. The authors would need to submit a proper research paper with original content, methodology, and results to be considered for publication.

The submission appears to be a test case or a mistake rather than a legitimate research contribution to the field of computational linguistics. The numerous typos and repeated references suggest it was not properly prepared before submission. This is not a submission that can be reviewed as a research paper, as it contains no research content.

This is not an issue of novelty or quality - it's an issue of the submission being fundamentally invalid as a research paper. The authors should be instructed to submit a properly formatted paper with original research content if they wish to have their work considered.
