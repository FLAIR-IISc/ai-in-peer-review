## Weaknesses

1. **Severe reference list formatting errors**: The reference list contains multiple misspellings of the same papers (e.g., "bibliogr phic" instead of "bibliographic", "digit l" instead of "digital", "doub e-array" instead of "double-array", "n et al\." instead of "et al."). These errors are not just minor typos but fundamental mistakes in academic writing standards. For example, the Aho and Corasick reference appears 8 times with different misspellings of the title and author names.

2. **Inclusion of non-reference text in reference list**: The reference list contains extraneous text fragments that should not be part of a reference list, including "Confid tia Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE.", "played it during public", and "Confidential Review Copy, DISTRIBUTE." These are clearly review instructions that were mistakenly included in the reference list, indicating a severe lack of proofreading.

3. **Complete absence of actual paper content**: The submission contains no abstract, introduction, methodology, results, or discussion sections. The only content is the reference list with errors, which is insufficient for an academic submission. The paper appears to be nothing more than a botched reference list, with no research contribution to evaluate.

4. **Inconsistent and unprofessional formatting**: The reference list uses inconsistent punctuation (e.g., periods after "et al." vs. no periods), inconsistent spacing (e.g., "CoRR ., abs/1611.04125." vs. "CoRR, abs/1611.04125."), and strange formatting elements (e.g., "? \Q2017\E" instead of "2017"). The reference list also contains "References" in the title of one entry, which is not standard academic practice.

5. **Evidence of poor proofreading and attention to detail**: The submission demonstrates a lack of basic academic writing skills. The reference list appears to have been generated by a process that introduced errors rather than curated with care. For example, the same paper is cited with multiple inconsistent versions (e.g., "Joint representation learning of text and knowledge for knowledge graph completion" vs. "Joint representation l arning of text and knowledge for knowledge graph completion"), suggesting the authors did not proofread the reference list before submission.
