The references contain numerous typos ("Efficie t" instead of "Efficient," "doubl -array" instead of "double-array," "bibliogr phic" instead of "bibliographic") and repeated entries for the same papers. The paper appears to be applying well-known techniques without demonstrating significant innovation, which is insufficient for ACL publication.

## Additional Comments

The paper's references section is so severely corrupted that it undermines the credibility of the submission. ACL submissions are expected to follow strict formatting guidelines and demonstrate attention to detail. This level of error suggests the submission was not properly prepared for review. 

I strongly recommend rejecting this submission due to the unacceptable quality of the references section, which indicates a lack of professionalism. Without a properly formatted references section, it's impossible to evaluate the paper's contribution or relationship to prior work. The paper also appears to lack novelty, as it seems to build upon well-established string matching techniques without demonstrating a significant advancement.

This submission does not meet the basic standards for publication in ACL, and I would not recommend it for acceptance in its current form. The authors would need to completely rewrite and properly format the submission, and provide a clear demonstration of novelty beyond the referenced foundational work.
