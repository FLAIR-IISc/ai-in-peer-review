**Summary and Contributions**

This paper appears to address the topic of entity disambiguation and knowledge graph completion, likely proposing a joint embedding model, judging by the titles in the reference list (e.g., "Joint representation learning of text and knowledge..."). However, it is impossible to ascertain the specific contributions of the work, as the submission appears to be deeply flawed at a fundamental level. Based on the provided reference section—the only part of the paper available for review—the primary observable characteristic of this work is an extreme lack of care and scholarly rigor. Any potential scientific contribution is entirely overshadowed by the abysmal quality of the manuscript's preparation.

**Strengths**

1.  The selection of cited works appears to be relevant to the presumed topics of entity linking, knowledge graph embeddings, and word representation learning. The authors reference foundational papers (e.g., Aho & Corasick, 1975; Mikolov et al., 2013a,b) as well as more recent and relevant work in the specific subfield (e.g., Han et al., 2016; Wang et al., 2014), suggesting a basic familiarity with the literature landscape.

**Weaknesses**

The manuscript, as evidenced by its reference section, is not ready for publication at a top-tier venue like ACL. The sheer volume and severity of errors are staggering and suggest a profound disrespect for the review process. My arguments for rejection are as follows:

1.  **Pervasive Typographical and Orthographical Errors:** The bibliography is riddled with an unacceptable number of typos. This is not a matter of one or two minor mistakes, but a systematic failure to proofread. Examples are rampant and include:
    *   "bibliogr phic search" instead of "bibliographic search"
    *   "digit l search" instead of "digital search"
    *   "Softw re Engine ring" instead of "Software Engineering"
    *   "doubl -array structure" instead of "double-array structure"
    *   "knowledg for knowledge graph completion" instead of "knowledge for knowledge graph completion"
    *   This pattern of missing or misplaced characters ("efficie t", "algorit m", "usi g", "l arning", "Margar t") appears in nearly every single entry, indicating that the entire section was likely generated by a faulty process and never manually checked.

2.  **Gross Inconsistencies in Formatting and Citation Style:** There is no discernible, consistent citation style. This is a fatal flaw for any academic paper.
    *   **Author Names:** Authors are cited with varying formats and even typos (e.g., "Aho, Margaret J Corasick." vs. "Aho a d Margar t J Corasi k."). The citation key for the same paper inexplicably changes from "Aho and Corasick." to "k." to "Corasick.". Similarly, "Han et al\." becomes "n et al\.". This level of inconsistency is bewildering.
    *   **Venue Names:** The same venue is written in multiple ways, such as "Communications of the ACM" and "Communications of th ACM". Journal names are misspelled ("IEEE Transacti s on Software Eng neering").
    *   **Page Numbers:** The page range "333–340" is at one point rendered as "3 3–340".
    *   **Year Formatting:** Some years are standard numbers (e.g., "1975"), while others are bizarrely formatted (e.g., "\Q2017\E").

3.  **Inclusion of Extraneous and Nonsensical Text:** The reference list contains numerous artifacts that appear to be remnants of a flawed copy-pasting or optical character recognition (OCR) process. This is perhaps the most damning weakness, as it demonstrates a complete failure to review the final manuscript.
    *   The entry for Han et al. (2016) inexplicably contains the phrase "played it during public".
    *   An entry for Yamada (2016) begins with a stray closing parenthesis: ") designs a list of features".
    *   There are multiple lines that are clearly not references, such as "Confid tia Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE. predict the context words by maximizing the following objective functi, Su missio".
    *   The phrase "Confidential Review Copy" and "DO NOT DISTRIBUTE" appears multiple times within the reference list itself.

The state of this bibliography is not a minor issue of presentation. It is symptomatic of a deeply flawed research process. If the authors cannot be trusted to correctly type the title of a seminal paper or to check that their own reference list is free of nonsensical artifacts, how can a reviewer trust their implementation of complex models, their handling of data, their execution of experiments, or their reporting of results? The lack of attention to detail is so profound that it invalidates the credibility of the entire submission.

**Questions to Authors**

1.  Was this manuscript proofread by any of the authors prior to submission? The density of errors, particularly the inclusion of document-processing artifacts like "Confidential Review Copy," strongly suggests it was not.

2.  The reference section appears to have been assembled via a careless and error-prone automated process. Can you provide assurance that the same lack of meticulous oversight did not affect other critical parts of your research, such as data preprocessing scripts, model implementation, and the collation of experimental results? Given the evidence at hand, why should the reviewers trust the technical correctness of the work?
