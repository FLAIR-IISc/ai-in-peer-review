Questions to authors
- What is the precise, novel technical contribution beyond established EL pipelines (dictionary-based mention detection + embedding-based ranking + collective inference)? Please state the key idea in one paragraph and contrast it explicitly with at least Yamada et al. (ACL 2017), Toutanova et al. (2015), and Hoffart et al. (2011).
- How are mentions detected and candidates generated? If you use Aho–Corasick and/or a double-array trie, please provide complexity, memory footprint, and coverage/precision trade-offs compared to alternatives. How do you handle overlapping spans and redirects/aliases?
- Describe the learning objective(s) for your joint text–KG embeddings: training data, loss, negative sampling, alignment between tokens and entities, and how multi-sense phenomena are handled. How does your approach differ from Han et al. (2016), Wang & Li (2016), and Mancini et al. (2016)?
- Detail the collective disambiguation component: graph construction, edge weights (semantic relatedness vs. co-occurrence), initialization, and the role of learned embeddings in the graph. How is PPR parameterized, and what ablations isolate its contribution?
- Datasets and baselines: Which benchmarks (AIDA-CoNLL, MSNBC, AQUAINT, TAC, WNED) and which strong modern baselines are included? Please report standard metrics (micro/macro accuracy/F1, NDCG) and provide statistical significance and error analysis.
- Reproducibility: Will you release code, pretrained models, mention dictionaries, and scripts to reconstruct candidate sets and graphs?
