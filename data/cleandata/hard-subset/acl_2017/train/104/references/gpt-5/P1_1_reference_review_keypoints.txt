- Summary: Paper proposes joint word–entity embeddings using text and KG signals for entity linking, combined with fast dictionary-based mention detection and candidate generation.

- Claimed contribution 1: A joint training objective embedding words and entities in a shared space leveraging text co-occurrence and KG structure for EL.

- Claimed contribution 2: An efficient mention detection and candidate generation pipeline using automata/double-array tries.

- Claimed contribution 3: Empirical improvements over strong EL baselines on standard datasets (ranking metrics and accuracy claimed).

- Strength 1: Joint text–KG representation learning is a compelling direction that can capture semantic relatedness and coherence beyond symbolic features.

- Strength 2: Attention to efficiency (Aho–Corasick, double-array tries) is valuable for practical, high-throughput EL deployments.

- Strength 3: Bibliography shows broad awareness of related EL and representation-learning work, indicating intended comparisons to appropriate baselines.

- Strength 4: Learned word/entity embeddings could be useful beyond EL (relation extraction, knowledge completion) if released.

- Weakness 1: Submission is incomplete/corrupted (mostly references), preventing assessment of methods, experiments, or results.

- Weakness 2: Novelty over prior joint text–KG models is unclear and may be incremental without precise distinctions.

- Weakness 3: Missing experimental details—datasets, baselines, candidate generation, collective inference, ablations, NIL handling, and significance tests.

- Weakness 4: Reproducibility concerns—no code, hyperparameters, preprocessing, or resource release described.

- Weakness 5: Poor and corrupted bibliography with duplicates and garbled entries, indicating lack of polish.

- Question 1: Specify the exact joint objective components, weighting, and how it differs from Wang & Li (2016), Toutanova et al. (2015), Han et al. (2016), etc.

- Question 2: Describe candidate generation and scoring: mention dictionary construction, p(e|m) estimation, pruning, and context-sensitive priors.

- Question 3: Clarify whether collective disambiguation is used, and if so, the graph construction, edge weights, and inference algorithm.

- Question 4: Report evaluated datasets, metrics, and baselines (minimum AIDA-CoNLL-YAGO), and relate ranking metrics (NDCG) to EL accuracy.

- Question 5: Provide ablations: text-only vs KG-only vs joint; with/without collective inference; with/without priors; embedding dimension and training data effects.

- Question 6: Provide efficiency results: throughput, memory footprint, and per-document latency compared to simpler gazetteer baselines.

- Question 7: Include error analysis covering ambiguity, long-tail entities, and NIL mentions.

- Additional comment 1: Fix and clean the bibliography—deduplicate and correct entries to meet ACL style.

- Additional comment 2: Separate gains from embedding model vs gains due to better candidate priors or mention detection to avoid confounded improvements.

- Additional comment 3: Position work against recent neural EL systems and explain complementary advantages if contemporaneous.

- Additional comment 4: Release trained embeddings and code to maximize community impact.

- Recommendation: Paper is not ready for acceptance in its current, corrupted form; invite resubmission with a complete, polished manuscript and rigorous experiments demonstrating clear novelty and strong empirical results.
