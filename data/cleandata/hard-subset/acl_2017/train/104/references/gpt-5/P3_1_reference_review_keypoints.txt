- Paper appears to target NER/EL with an efficient dictionary-based mention detector (Aho–Corasick / double-array trie) plus joint word/entity/KG embeddings for disambiguation.  
- Claimed contributions: engineering (high-throughput mention detection), modeling (joint embeddings for entities/words, possibly multi-prototype), and empirical (ranking gains and efficiency improvements).  

Strengths:
- Practical emphasis on efficiency using compact trie-based mention detection suitable for production pipelines.  
- Integration of text and KG signals via joint embeddings is well-motivated for improving candidate scoring, especially for long-tail entities.  
- References suggest awareness of core EL literature and relevant embedding work.  
- Potentially useful modular EL component for IE, QA, and retrieval applications.

Weaknesses:
- Submission is largely corrupted/incomplete (references duplicated, typos, stray fragments); core sections (problem statement, method, experiments) are missing, making the paper unreviewable.  
- Novelty is unclear: similar joint word–entity and neural EL systems already exist (Yamada, Ganea, Kolitsas, BLINK, etc.); the paper must state what is new.  
- Poor reference quality and integrity (duplicates, corrupted metadata, missing entries) undermines credibility and reproducibility.  
- Evaluation details are missing: datasets, metrics, candidate recall, NIL handling, and statistical significance are not reported.  
- Reproducibility concerns: no training details, hyperparameters, data sources, ablations, error analysis, or code provided.  
- Efficiency claims lack rigorous measurements and comparison against strong baselines.

Questions for authors:
- Is the task end-to-end NER+EL or only EL? Do you handle NIL, coreference, or multilingual cases?  
- Do you use Aho–Corasick / double-array trie only for mention spotting or also for alias lookup/candidate retrieval? What are alias sources and candidate recall@K?  
- How does your joint embedding objective differ from prior work (Yamada, Toutanova)? Do you incorporate KG relations or only text co-occurrence?  
- Are multi-prototype/sense embeddings actually used at inference time for EL?  
- Do you use global coherence/graph-based disambiguation (PPR) or only local scoring? How is any graph optimized efficiently?  
- Which benchmarks and strong baselines (Ganea & Hofmann, Kolitsas, BLINK) are used for comparison, and how are candidate sets standardized?  
- What are the measured latency, throughput, memory, and index-size numbers under realistic loads?  
- Please provide ablations isolating candidate generation, embedding components, global coherence, and KG integration.

Additional comments:
- Fix the references: remove duplicates, correct typos, and add missing seminal works; ensure in-text citations match the bibliography.  
- Remove stray confidentiality/formatting fragments and resubmit a clean manuscript with full sections (Introduction, Method, Experiments, Analysis).  
- If efficiency is a main claim, consider open-sourcing the mention detection/indexing and reporting standardized speed/accuracy trade-offs.

Recommendation:
- In its current corrupted/incomplete form the submission is not evaluable and should be rejected; resubmit a complete, corrected paper with clear novelty, rigorous evaluation, and reproducibility materials.
