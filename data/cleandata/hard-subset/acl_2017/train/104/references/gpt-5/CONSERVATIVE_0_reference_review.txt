Strengths
- Practical topic with clear relevance: Efficient and accurate entity linking remains important in NLP and IR. If the paper genuinely unifies fast candidate generation with strong disambiguation signals, the contribution could be of practical value.
- Awareness of related work: The references cover core strands in EL (e.g., Cucerzan; Hoffart et al.; Pershina et al.; Kulkarni et al.) and joint text–KG embedding (e.g., Wang et al.; Toutanova et al.; Weston et al.; Han et al.), as well as multi-sense embeddings. This suggests the authors aim to position their work within established paradigms.
- Potential scalability angle: Leveraging Aho–Corasick/double-array tries for mention detection is a well-founded engineering choice and, if implemented carefully, can yield strong throughput and memory efficiency, which is useful in production settings.

Questions to authors (to be addressed in the response)
- Please provide a clear, concise description of your method. What are the exact components, and what is novel versus adapted from prior work?
- What datasets do you evaluate on? Which metrics (e.g., micro/macro accuracy, in-KB accuracy, AIDA strong matching, NDCG) do you report? Do you handle NIL detection?
- What are your baselines? Include both classical (e.g., PPR-based, collective EL systems) and recent embedding-based EL methods. How do you ensure fair candidate generation across systems?
- How do you integrate Aho–Corasick/double-array tries? What are the memory footprint, build time, and throughput at inference? How do you handle overlapping mentions, redirects, and surface-form normalization?
- How are embeddings trained and aligned to entities? Which corpora and KG (e.g., Wikipedia/Wikidata/Freebase) are used? Do you use multi-sense word embeddings; if so, how do you map senses to entities?
- Is your disambiguation local, global, or both? What is the graph construction (nodes/edges/weights), and what algorithm do you use for collective inference? Any convergence or complexity guarantees?
- Provide ablations isolating candidate generation, local scoring, and collective inference. What performance drops occur when each component is removed?
- Report statistical significance of improvements and provide an error analysis highlighting common failure modes.
- Clarify reproducibility: Will you release code, pre-trained models, and dictionaries? Specify license and data accessibility.
