- Summary: Paper appears to address entity linking by combining Aho–Corasick + double-array trie candidate generation, joint word–entity skip-gram embeddings, and graph-based global disambiguation, but the main body is largely missing.  
- Contribution 1: Efficient candidate generation using an Aho–Corasick matcher implemented with a double-array trie to scale large mention–entity lexicons.  
- Contribution 2: Joint embedding model for words and KB entities trained with a skip-gram-style objective, possibly augmented with KG signals.  
- Contribution 3: End-to-end EL pipeline integrating dictionary candidates, embedding-based local scoring, and graph-based global inference.  
- Strength: Practical efficiency focus—combining Aho–Corasick and double-array trie could yield high-throughput, compact candidate generation if benchmarked.  
- Strength: Coherent pipeline design aligned with EL best practices (priors, context models, graph coherence).  
- Strength: Appropriate learning signal—word/entity skip-gram objectives have precedent and can help disambiguation if KG structure is integrated.  
- Strength: Potential impact/reproducibility if code and resources for candidate generation and embedding training are released.  
- Weakness: Submission is incomplete/garbled—core model, training, datasets, and results are missing or unreadable, preventing verification.  
- Weakness: Unclear novelty—joint text–KB embeddings and graph-aware disambiguation are well-studied; the paper may be incremental without clear new advances.  
- Weakness: Methodological ambiguity—details missing on how entities are integrated into the objective, KG encoding, multi-sense handling, NIL detection, and priors.  
- Weakness: Missing evaluation protocol—no standard benchmarks, baselines, metrics, ablations, or efficiency measurements are provided.  
- Weakness: Poor writing and presentation with typos, duplicated/corrupted references that undermine credibility.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify precise novelty, objective/negative sampling, candidate generation implementation and recall/throughput, disambiguation model features/training, datasets/metrics, NIL handling, and code release plans.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form due to incomplete/garbled content and unclear novelty; encourage resubmission with a complete, well-edited manuscript and rigorous, reproducible empirical validation.
