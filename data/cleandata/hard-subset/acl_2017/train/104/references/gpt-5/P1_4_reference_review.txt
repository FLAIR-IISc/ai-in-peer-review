Contributions as I see them
Given the absence of content, I cannot identify verifiable contributions. If the above inferences are correct, the primary potential contribution would be a practical, efficient EL system combining fast dictionary-based mention detection with learned entity representations. The secondary potential contribution would be a joint training scheme for words and entities. Both need to be substantiated by a clear method and experimental results.

Strengths
- Important problem area: Entity linking/disambiguation and joint text–KG representation learning are central, high-impact topics in NLP and IR. A contribution that improves efficiency and accuracy here would be of broad interest to ACL.
- Awareness of relevant literature: The references (despite severe corruption) cover key strands: collective EL (Hoffart et al., Kulkarni et al., Pershina et al.), embedding-based EL (Huang et al.), word/entity joint embeddings (Toutanova et al., Wang & Li, Wang et al., Han et al.), multi-sense embeddings (Reisinger & Mooney; Neelakantan et al.; Li & Jurafsky), and classic string-matching structures (Aho–Corasick; Aoe). This suggests the authors are familiar with the landscape, which, if reflected in the body, could lead to a well-contextualized contribution.
- Potential for practical impact: If the work indeed integrates automata-based mention detection (Aho–Corasick) with compact index structures (double-array trie) and strong neural/entity representations, it could yield a system that is both accurate and production-ready, a valuable combination often underrepresented in academic EL work.

Weaknesses
- Incomplete/corrupted submission: The manuscript is effectively absent. Beyond a broken references section and a stray sentence fragment (“predict the context words by maximizing the following objective functi”), there is no content to assess. This precludes evaluation of novelty, correctness, clarity, experimental rigor, or significance.
- Reference quality is unacceptable: Numerous entries are corrupted, duplicated, or malformed (misspellings, missing venues/years, inconsistent formatting, author truncations like “n et al.”). This makes it impossible to trace precise prior work and undermines confidence in scholarship and reproducibility.
- No methodology, datasets, or results: There are no model details (architectures, objectives, training regimes), datasets (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL, TAC-KBP, MSNBC, AQUAINT), or metrics (accuracy, micro/macro F1, MRR/NDCG) described. Without experiments and ablations, claims—if any were present—would be unsubstantiated.
- Novelty cannot be established: The hypothesized ideas (fast dictionary-based mention detection; joint word–entity embeddings) have known precedents (e.g., Yamada et al., Toutanova et al., Wang & Li, Huang et al.). Without a clear statement of what is new, how it improves upon prior art, and empirical evidence, the paper risks being incremental or redundant.
- Reproducibility and ethical considerations missing: There is no mention of code, models, data availability, training corpora (e.g., Wikipedia dump version), preprocessing, or compute budget. Ethical aspects (e.g., biases in KBs, potential harms from EL errors) are not discussed.

Questions to authors
- Please provide the complete manuscript. What problem do you address precisely (EL, mention detection, KG completion, joint embedding), and what is the main technical contribution beyond prior work such as Yamada et al. (joint word–entity embeddings) and Huang et al. (DNN+KG EL)?
- If you propose an efficient mention detection algorithm: What is the exact data structure and time/memory complexity? How does it compare empirically (throughput, latency, memory) to a standard Aho–Corasick implementation and other dictionary/trie variants on large mention lexicons?
- If you propose a joint embedding method: What is the training objective? How are textual and KG signals combined (e.g., skip-gram with entity contexts, relation-aware losses)? How do you handle polysemy and sense/entity disambiguation during training?
- What datasets and evaluation protocols do you use? For EL, please report standard splits (e.g., AIDA-A/B/C) and metrics, compare to strong baselines (Hoffart et al., Pershina et al., Huang et al., Yamada et al.), and include ablations isolating the contribution of each component.
- What error analyses and qualitative studies do you provide (e.g., failure modes by entity frequency, type, mention ambiguity; impact of candidate generation vs. disambiguation; sensitivity to KB coverage)?
- Will you release code, pretrained models, and the mention lexicon? If not, what documentation and hyperparameter details will support reproducibility?

Recommendation
Given the essentially missing manuscript and unusable references, I cannot evaluate the work. Even assuming promising ideas, the current submission does not meet ACL standards for clarity, completeness, or scientific validation. I recommend rejection, with encouragement to resubmit a complete, carefully proofread paper that clearly states the novel contributions, provides thorough empirical comparisons and ablations, and includes clean, complete references.
