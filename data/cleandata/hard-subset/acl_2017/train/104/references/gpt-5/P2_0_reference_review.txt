If the submission indeed advances EL with better joint modeling and/or improves efficiency at candidate generation, it could be impactful for large-scale EL systems.

Questions to authors (to enable a proper review)
- Please provide the complete manuscript (abstract, introduction, method, experimental setup, results, analysis). Without it, meaningful review is impossible.
- What is the core novelty relative to prior joint text–KG embedding approaches (e.g., Wang et al. 2014; Weston et al. 2013; Toutanova et al. 2015; Han et al. 2016; Mancini et al. 2016) and to strong EL systems (Hoffart et al. 2011; Pershina et al. 2015; Alhelbawy and Gaizauskas 2014)?
- How are candidate entities generated? If Aho–Corasick/double-array tries are used, what is new in your design (data structures, memory layout, multilingual coverage) and how much do they reduce latency compared to standard alternatives?
- What datasets and metrics do you use? Please report standard EL datasets (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL) and IR/EL metrics (micro/macro accuracy, F1, NDCG) with clear train/dev/test splits, and compare to strong published baselines under identical settings.
- How is the joint embedding trained? Specify the objective(s), negative sampling, use of KB edges/text corpora, handling of polysemy and NIL entities, and how embeddings are integrated into the disambiguation inference (local vs. global coherence).
- Provide ablations and error analyses: What components contribute most? Where does the system fail (rare entities, NILs, cross-lingual cases)?
- Reproducibility: Will code, data preprocessing scripts, and trained models be released? What compute is required?
