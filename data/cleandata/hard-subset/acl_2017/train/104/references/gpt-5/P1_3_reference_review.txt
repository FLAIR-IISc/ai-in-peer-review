Summary
The submitted file appears to contain only a corrupted and highly duplicated reference list with fragments of sentences (e.g., “predict the context words by maximizing…”) and “Confidential Review Copy” markers. There is no abstract, introduction, method, experiments, results, or conclusions. From the bibliography, the intended topic seems to be named entity disambiguation/linking (NED/EL) with an emphasis on efficiency and joint text–knowledge-base embeddings. Classic string matching (Aho–Corasick; double-array tries), EL systems (Hoffart et al., Pershina et al., Cucerzan, Kulkarni et al.), and joint text–KG representation learning (Wang et al., Toutanova et al., Han et al., Weston et al.) are repeatedly cited, as are multi-sense word embedding papers. However, without the main text, the technical contributions, novelty, and empirical findings cannot be assessed.

- A joint embedding or knowledge-enhanced representation for disambiguation ranking, potentially leveraging multi-sense embeddings and text–KG joint training.
- An evaluation on standard EL benchmarks with ranking metrics (DCG/nDCG implied by citation), demonstrating improved effectiveness and/or speed.

Strengths
- Potentially relevant and timely problem: Efficient, accurate entity linking at scale remains an important challenge in NLP/IR. If the paper indeed integrates fast dictionary matching with strong representation learning for ranking, that could be impactful for real-world systems.
- Literature awareness: The reference list, despite its corruption, encompasses key strands in EL (AIDA, PPR-based methods, collective inference), joint text–KG embeddings, and multi-prototype word representations. This suggests an understanding of the major baselines and methodological options.
- Possible focus on efficiency: The repeated references to Aho–Corasick and double-array tries point to attention to practical scalability, which is often under-emphasized in EL work and would be a contribution if accompanied by rigorous time/memory analyses on large corpora.

Weaknesses
- Incomplete submission: The manuscript body is missing. There is no problem statement, method description, dataset, training details, experiments, results, ablations, or error analysis. Consequently, correctness, novelty, and significance cannot be evaluated.

This fails to meet basic scholarly standards and complicates verification.
- Anonymity breach: Multiple “Confidential Review Copy” lines include author names (e.g., “Ikuya Yamada, Hiroyuki Shindo, …”), which violates double-blind review policy. Self-identifying material must be removed or anonymized per ACL guidelines.
- Lack of novelty and positioning (unverifiable): Even if the intended contribution is a combination of automaton-based gazetteer matching with joint text–KG embeddings for EL, prior work has explored these ingredients extensively. Without evidence of a new objective, architecture, training regime, or a demonstrably superior efficiency–accuracy trade-off, the novelty would be questionable.
- No empirical evidence: There are no experimental results, datasets, metrics, or baselines. Claims of effectiveness and efficiency, if any, cannot be substantiated. No error analysis, ablation, or statistical significance testing is provided. Reproducibility artifacts (code/data) are not indicated.

Questions to Authors
- Please provide the complete anonymized manuscript. What is the precise research problem (entity linking, mention detection, knowledge graph completion, or joint tasks)?
- What is the core technical contribution beyond existing EL pipelines that use gazetteer-based candidate generation and embedding-based ranking? Is there a new model, training objective, or inference strategy?
- What datasets do you evaluate on (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL, TAC-KBP, Wikipedia hyperlinks)? What are your baselines (e.g., AIDA, PPR-EL, recent neural EL models), and how do you ensure fair comparison?
- How do you measure efficiency (throughput, latency, memory) and at what scale? Please report complexity, wall-clock benchmarks, and memory footprint for candidate generation and disambiguation.
- If multi-sense or joint text–KG embeddings are used, how are senses/entities aligned with tokens, and how do you avoid label leakage from Wikipedia hyperlinks?
- What is the candidate generation recall at various mention thresholds, and how does it trade off with downstream disambiguation accuracy? Any analysis of long-tail entities and NIL detection?
- Are code, pretrained models, and dictionaries available to support reproducibility?
