Summary and contributions
From the limited material provided (a heavily corrupted, duplicated References section with scattered hints about objectives and methods), the paper appears to address named entity linking/disambiguation (EL) and/or knowledge graph (KG) completion by combining (i) efficient dictionary/string-matching methods for mention detection (Aho–Corasick; double-array tries) and (ii) joint embedding of text and knowledge (words/senses/entities), with ranking-based evaluation (e.g., NDCG). My interpretation of the main contributions (as I see them) is:

- Contribution 1 (engineering/efficiency): A practical, high-throughput mention detection pipeline using Aho–Corasick and a double-array trie for candidate generation in EL.

- Contribution 2 (modeling): A joint representation learning setup that embeds words/senses/entities (drawing on prior work in multi-prototype word embeddings and joint text–KG training) to score/rank candidates for EL and possibly improve KG completion.

- Contribution 3 (empirical): An evaluation of the combined system on EL benchmarks (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL, TAC-KBP, Wikilinks) with ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG), and comparison to classic graph-based and neural baselines.

Strengths
- Addresses an important problem with practical impact. Efficient and accurate EL remains a core component of many NLP pipelines and downstream tasks (IR, QA, KG population). Leveraging fast dictionary-based mention detection is a sensible choice for real-time or large-scale settings, while joint embeddings can improve disambiguation quality.

- Sensible combination of classic and neural techniques. The pairing of Aho–Corasick/double-array tries (for recall-oriented mention detection and fast candidate retrieval) with learned semantic representations (for precision-oriented ranking) is methodologically sound and, if executed well, often delivers strong accuracy-speed tradeoffs in EL systems.

- Awareness of relevant literature. The references list, despite its severe corruption, includes seminal EL works (Cucerzan; Kulkarni et al.; Hoffart et al.; Pershina et al.), surveys (Shen et al.), multi-sense embedding methods (Reisinger & Mooney; Huang et al.; Neelakantan et al.; Tian et al.; Li & Jurafsky; Mancini et al.), and joint text–KG models (Weston et al.; Wang et al.; Toutanova et al.; Han et al.; Wang & Li; Wu et al.). This coverage suggests the authors position their work in a well-established context.

- Potential to report both accuracy and efficiency. If the paper indeed presents runtime/memory measurements in addition to disambiguation metrics, that would be valuable for practitioners, especially given the emphasis on efficient string matching.

Weaknesses
- Severe submission quality issues in references and likely beyond. The References section is extensively corrupted: duplicated entries, pervasive typos and broken words, inconsistent venues/years/author strings, spurious text (“Confidential Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE”), and misattributions. This raises serious concerns about the care taken in preparing the submission and, potentially, the integrity of other sections. ACL requires professionally prepared submissions; in its current form, the paper does not meet minimal presentation standards.

- Unclear novelty relative to substantial prior art. Joint embeddings of words and entities/senses for EL and KG completion have been widely explored, as have fast dictionary-based mention detection methods. The paper must clearly articulate what is new beyond (a) established Aho–Corasick/double-array engineering, and (b) prior joint text–KG and multi-sense embedding models. Without a precise methodological innovation or a new, demonstrably useful integration, the contribution risks being incremental.

- Insufficient evidence about experimental rigor. The provided excerpt does not list datasets, baselines, metrics, or ablations. Meaningful EL evaluation typically requires: standard benchmarks (AIDA-CoNLL, TAC-KBP, MSNBC/AQUAINT, WikilinksNED), strong baselines (local prior+context, graph coherence methods, recent neural rankers), clear separation of mention detection vs disambiguation, NIL handling, and detailed error analysis. Without these details, it is impossible to assess the validity and significance of the results.

- Reproducibility and transparency are unclear. There is no information on data preprocessing, dictionary construction/coverage, KG versions, negative sampling strategies, optimization details, or code/model release. For a systems paper, the community benefit is limited without reproducible artifacts or at least comprehensive implementation details.

- Potential mismatch between scope and claims. The references suggest both EL and KG completion are targeted. A single paper claiming advances in both often needs especially careful experimental design to avoid over-claiming and to ensure each task is evaluated to community standards. This is not evident from the provided material.

Questions to authors
- Clearly state the main research questions and the specific, novel contributions. What is the key methodological innovation beyond prior joint embedding and EL pipelines?

- Describe the end-to-end system. How are mentions detected (exactly which lexicons, redirects, aliases; handling of overlaps; case/diacritics; morphology)? How are candidates generated and pruned? How is NIL handled?

- Provide the learning objective(s) precisely. If you use a word2vec-style objective, how do you incorporate entities/senses and KG relations? How are multiple senses per word induced and assigned at training/inference time?

- Datasets and splits. Which EL datasets are used? Are you evaluating mention detection and disambiguation separately? Do you use in-KB vs NIL-aware metrics? For KG completion, which benchmarks and protocols?

- Baselines. Which local and collective EL methods do you compare to (e.g., AIDA, PPR-based, modern neural rankers)? Do you include strong joint text–KG baselines (e.g., TransE/DistMult/ComplEx variants with text-enhanced training)?

- Ablations. What is the contribution of the efficient dictionary module vs the embedding model? How do results change when removing graph-based coherence or prior probabilities? How sensitive is performance to candidate set size and dictionary coverage?

- Efficiency. Provide time/memory complexity and wall-clock results for mention detection and disambiguation. How does your approach scale with dictionary size and document length? Is the double-array trie critical vs standard Aho–Corasick?

- Resources. Will you release code, models, and dictionaries? How are the knowledge base and text corpora licensed and preprocessed?

- References. Please thoroughly clean, deduplicate, and correct the entire bibliography. Some entries are malformed or misattributed; ensure proper citation formatting per ACL style.

Recommendation
Given the current state of the submission (serious presentation issues, unclear novelty, and insufficient evidence of rigorous evaluation), my preliminary recommendation is to reject. I encourage the authors to (1) clearly articulate and demonstrate their unique contribution with strong baselines and ablations, (2) substantially improve the writing and references, and (3) release code and resources to substantiate the engineering claims.
