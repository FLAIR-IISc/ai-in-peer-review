## Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of references related to Named Entity Disambiguation (NED) and Knowledge Graph (KG) completion/embedding. It’s not a traditional research paper presenting a novel method, but rather a bibliography compiled for some purpose (perhaps a survey or a dataset creation effort). The repeated entries and formatting inconsistencies suggest this is an unpolished or incomplete document. The references span a significant period, starting with foundational work in string matching (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) and progressing to more recent deep learning approaches for joint representation learning (Han et al., Wu et al.). 

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Comprehensive (though messy) Collection of NED/KG Relevant Literature:** The primary contribution is the gathering of a fairly broad set of references touching upon key techniques in entity linking, disambiguation, knowledge graph embedding, and related areas. This could be valuable *if* curated and presented properly.
2. **Historical Context:** Including foundational papers on string matching alongside recent deep learning work provides a sense of the evolution of techniques relevant to the field. This highlights the connections between early information retrieval methods and modern approaches.
3. **Potential for Dataset/Survey Resource:**  The list *could* serve as a starting point for creating a benchmark dataset or as a basis for a comprehensive survey paper, although significant effort is required.



**Strengths:**

1. **Breadth of Coverage:** The references span several crucial areas within NED and KG representation learning. It includes foundational papers (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) alongside state-of-the-art work (Han et al., Wang and Li). This suggests the compiler has a good understanding of the field's landscape.
2. **Inclusion of Classic Work:**  The inclusion of papers like Schütze (2008) and Järvelin & Kekäläinen (2002) provides important context for understanding the roots of modern techniques.  Many current approaches build on these earlier concepts.
3. **Relevant Recent Publications:** The bibliography includes significant papers from ACL, EMNLP, and other reputable conferences, demonstrating awareness of the current research trends (e.g., Chen et al., Mancini et al., Wu et al.).
4. **Diverse Techniques Represented:** References cover a wide range of techniques including string matching, graph ranking, word embeddings, knowledge graph embeddings, and joint representation learning, offering a varied perspective.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Not a Research Contribution:**  This is fundamentally a list of references. It does not present any new research, methodology, results, or analysis.  It’s not suitable for publication in a research venue like ACL in its current form.
2. **Significant Duplication & Formatting Issues:** The repeated entries (Aho & Corasick, Aoe, Han et al. appear multiple times) and inconsistent formatting (e.g., missing years, differing abbreviations) demonstrate a lack of polish and suggest the list hasn’t been carefully curated.  The formatting errors are extremely distracting.
3. **Lack of Organization/Categorization:** The list is presented without any clear organization or categorization.  It's difficult to quickly identify papers related to specific sub-problems or techniques. A thematic grouping would vastly improve usability.
4. **Missing Key Papers:** While broad, the bibliography is not exhaustive. Important works in areas like distant supervision for NED and recent advancements in pre-trained language models for KG completion are notably absent. This suggests the selection process was not systematic.
5. **Unclear Purpose:**  The document lacks any introduction or explanation of its purpose. Why was this list compiled? What is it intended to be used for? This lack of context makes it difficult to assess its value.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the intended purpose of this compilation of references?** Is it intended as a resource for researchers, a dataset creation effort, or something else?
2. **Why are there so many duplicate entries?** What process was used to collect these references, and why was it not de-duplicated?
3. **What criteria were used to select these references?**  Was it a systematic search, or based on personal knowledge?
4. **Do you plan to curate and organize this list into a more usable format (e.g., by sub-topic)?**  If so, could you outline your plans?
5. **Are you submitting this as a dataset description or a resource paper?**  If so, please clarify and provide details.



**Additional Comments:**

*   The "Confidential Review Copy" watermark is irrelevant for a public ACL submission.
*   The inclusion of random text snippets (e.g., "predict the context words by maximizing the following objective functi") is confusing and should be removed.
*   If the intention is to create a dataset, consider providing a structured format (e.g., BibTeX) and a clear license.




**Overall Assessment:**

This submission, as it stands, is not suitable for acceptance at ACL. It lacks the core elements of a research contribution and is hampered by significant formatting issues and a lack of context.  However, the underlying collection of references has *potential* value if significantly curated, organized, and presented with a clear purpose and justification. I recommend a **reject** decision unless the authors clarify the purpose and dramatically improve the quality and organization of the list.
