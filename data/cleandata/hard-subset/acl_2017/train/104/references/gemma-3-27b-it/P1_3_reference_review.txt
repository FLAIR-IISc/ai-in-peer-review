## Peer Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of bibliographic references related to Named Entity Disambiguation (NED) and knowledge graph embedding. It includes citations spanning from foundational work in string matching (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) to more recent papers on joint representation learning for knowledge graph completion (Han et al., Wu et al.). The list covers a broad range of techniques, including word embeddings, knowledge graph embedding, and probabilistic models. The references encompass publications from major NLP/IR conferences and journals like ACL, EMNLP, NAACL, CoRR, IEEE Transactions, and Communications of the ACM. However, there is no accompanying paper *per se*; it's solely a bibliography.

**Main Contributions (as I perceive them – acknowledging the unusual nature of this submission):**

1. **Comprehensive Resource Compilation:** The primary contribution is the assembly of a relatively comprehensive bibliography of research relevant to NED and knowledge graph embedding. It consolidates a wide array of influential papers in the field, potentially serving as a valuable resource for researchers entering or working in these areas.
2. **Historical Overview:** The inclusion of foundational work like Aho & Corasick and Aoe alongside recent advancements offers a glimpse into the historical evolution of techniques impacting NED and knowledge graph embedding.
3. **Coverage of Diverse Approaches:** The bibliography showcases the breadth of methodologies applied to NED and knowledge graph embedding, including traditional information retrieval methods and modern deep learning approaches.

**Strengths:**

1. **Breadth of Coverage:** The list is impressively broad, covering a significant number of key publications in the field. It demonstrates a good understanding of the relevant literature.
2. **Inclusion of Foundational Work:**  Incorporating classical papers like Aho & Corasick and Aoe provides valuable context and acknowledges the roots of modern techniques. This is often missing in more focused literature reviews.
3. **Relevance to Current Research:** The inclusion of recent papers (e.g., Han et al., Wu et al.) ensures the bibliography remains up-to-date and relevant to ongoing research in NED and knowledge graph embedding.
4. **Potential Utility:** For researchers new to the field, or those looking for a comprehensive overview, this bibliography could be a valuable starting point. It saves the effort of independently tracking down these resources.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Missing Context & Analysis:** The bibliography lacks any accompanying text providing context, analysis, or synthesis of the included papers. It's simply a list. A useful review or survey would offer insights into how these papers relate to each other, identify key trends, and highlight open problems.
2. **Formatting and Redundancy:** The formatting is inconsistent and contains numerous repetitions of the same references. This suggests a lack of careful curation and editing.  There are also apparent OCR errors within some entries (e.g., “digit l”, “knowledg”).
3. **Lack of Clear Purpose/Novelty:** As it stands, this submission isn't a research contribution in the traditional sense.  It's a resource compilation, but its value hinges on *how* that compilation is presented and utilized.  The current submission doesn’t articulate a clear purpose beyond “here are some papers”.  Is it intended as a dataset? A starting point for a larger study? Without this clarification, its relevance to ACL is questionable.
4. **Non-Standard Submission:** Submitting a bibliography as a standalone ACL paper is highly unusual. ACL primarily seeks original research contributions, not simply curated lists of references.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the primary *purpose* of this submission?** Is it intended to be a dataset, a resource for researchers, or something else?  Please clearly articulate the intended use case.
2. **Do you plan to accompany this bibliography with any analysis or commentary?**  For example, a discussion of key trends, a categorization of papers by approach, or an identification of open research questions.
3. **Can you address the formatting inconsistencies and redundancies within the bibliography?** A clean and well-organized list would significantly enhance its usability.
4. **Why do you believe this submission is appropriate for publication at ACL?** Please justify its relevance to the conference’s focus on computational linguistics and natural language processing.

**Additional Comments:**

* The string "Confidential Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE." appears in the text, which is confusing and inappropriate for a submission to a public venue like ACL.
* The frequent mentions of "\Qyear\E" (e.g., \Q2017\E) suggest these are placeholder values that have not been properly replaced.
