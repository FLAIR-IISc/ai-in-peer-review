This paper presents a bibliography of references related to named entity disambiguation (NED) and knowledge graph completion, seemingly compiled for a specific project (indicated by "Confidential Review Copy"). The document primarily consists of a long list of research papers with associated author names and publication venues.

The main contribution, as I see it, is the **compilation of a reasonably comprehensive bibliography** focused on the intersection of knowledge graphs, text representation, and entity disambiguation. While not a novel research contribution in itself, such curated lists can be valuable resources for researchers entering the field or seeking a broad overview of related work. A secondary contribution is the **demonstration of the breadth of research** within this area, covering papers from the early 2000s to 2017.

**Strengths:**

1. **Resource Compilation:** The bibliography is a useful collection of papers. It covers core publications like Aho & Corasick (string matching foundations relevant to search), Aoe (double-array structure used in indexing), and more recent work on joint embedding models (Han et al., Wang et al., Wu et al.). This saves time for researchers needing to survey the literature.
2. **Coverage of Foundational Work:**  Including references like Schütze’s “Introduction to Information Retrieval” grounds the topic in information retrieval basics and provides context for more specialized NED research. The inclusion of earlier work (Cucerzan, 2007) illustrates the historical development of the field.
3. **Relevance to Current Research:** The bibliography includes important papers on recent advances in knowledge graph embeddings and joint representation learning, demonstrating an awareness of contemporary research trends.  Papers from ACL, EMNLP, and related conferences are well represented.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty:**  A bibliography, in and of itself, does not constitute a novel research contribution. The document lacks any original analysis, synthesis, or insight beyond simply listing relevant publications.  Submission to ACL is questionable given this fundamental limitation.
2. **Formatting and Consistency Issues:** The reference formatting is inconsistent and contains numerous errors (e.g., missing punctuation, abbreviated venue names with question marks, inconsistent author formatting). The repeated entries with slightly different formatting suggest a manual compilation process prone to errors. This undermines the usability of the resource.
3. **Missing Context & Organization:** The bibliography is presented as a simple list, without any categorization or annotation. There's no indication of *why* these papers were selected, what specific aspects of NED or KG completion they address, or how they relate to each other. This makes it difficult to use effectively. A simple categorization (e.g., by task – entity linking, relation extraction, knowledge graph completion; or by method – embedding models, graph-based methods) would be highly beneficial.
4. **Incomplete Information:** Some entries lack complete information. For example, several entries simply state "\Q2017\E" or "\Q2016\E" without further details. This makes it difficult to locate the original sources.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. What was the specific motivation for compiling this bibliography?  Was it for a specific project or literature review?  Understanding the intended purpose could clarify its value.
2. Do you plan to provide any further analysis or organization of this bibliography, such as categorization or summarization of key themes?
3. Will you be addressing the numerous formatting inconsistencies and errors in the references? A standardized format is crucial for a usable resource.



**Additional Comments:**

The frequent repetition of the same references with minor formatting variations indicates a potential issue with the compilation process. Consider using a reference management tool (e.g., BibTeX, Zotero, Mendeley) to ensure consistency and accuracy.



**Overall Assessment:**

Reject. While the compiled bibliography represents a potentially useful *resource*, it does not meet the standards for publication in a research conference like ACL. The lack of novelty, formatting issues, and absence of contextual information significantly diminish its value. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above – particularly improving the formatting, providing organization and annotations, and possibly presenting the bibliography as a supporting material for a more substantial research contribution – could improve its potential.
