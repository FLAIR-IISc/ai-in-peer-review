## Peer Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of references related to named entity disambiguation (NED), knowledge graph completion, and related areas like string matching, information retrieval, and representation learning. The provided text consists almost entirely of bibliographic entries, often repeated with minor variations in formatting and author naming. It does *not* present a novel research paper with a clearly defined methodology, experiments, or results. It reads as a raw compilation of citations.

**Main Contributions (as claimed - though difficult to ascertain definitively):**

Given the nature of the submission, identifying "contributions" is problematic. If forced to interpret, the implied contribution is the creation of a (very messy) bibliography focused on NED and related topics. However, even this is not presented in a readily usable format (e.g., BibTeX).  There are no novel ideas or analyses presented. The strongest 'contribution' would be a demonstration of the breadth of research in the field, but this is hampered by the repetition and inconsistent formatting.



**Strengths:**

1. **Breadth of Coverage (potentially):** The list touches upon many influential papers in the NED and knowledge graph space. If organized and cleaned, this collection *could* serve as a starting point for researchers entering the field. However, as it stands, its utility is limited.
2. **Relevance to Core NLP Topics:** The references included are highly relevant to the ACL community, covering key areas like entity linking, knowledge representation, and natural language understanding.
3. **Historical Perspective (limited):** The inclusion of older papers (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) demonstrates a historical understanding of the foundational work that informs modern approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Not a Research Paper:** This is the most significant weakness. The submission *is not* a research paper. It lacks a hypothesis, methodology, experiments, results, and discussion. It's simply a list of references.  Submitting a list of references as a research paper is inappropriate for a venue like ACL.
2. **Extreme Redundancy & Inconsistent Formatting:** The same references are repeated multiple times, sometimes with minor variations in punctuation, capitalization, or author naming. This makes the list difficult to parse and use. The formatting is inconsistent throughout (e.g., use of "\QYYYY\E" for years, varying abbreviation styles).
3. **Lack of Organization/Analysis:** The references are not organized in any logical way (chronological, topical, etc.). There is no analysis or synthesis of the cited works; it's merely a compilation.  It doesn’t provide any insights or a novel viewpoint.
4. **Missing Key Information:**  While many references *appear* to be complete, some are truncated or lack crucial details like venue names or page numbers. The "Confidential Review Copy" tags and other extraneous text further clutter the submission.
5. **Poor Presentation:** The text is littered with errors (e.g., "digit l" instead of "digital", "knowledg" instead of "knowledge"), making it appear unedited and unprofessional.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the intended contribution of this submission?**  Please explicitly state the research question(s) addressed or the novel insights presented.
2. **If the intention is to present a bibliography, why was this submitted to ACL as a research paper?**  ACL is not a venue for simply listing references.
3. **Can you explain the rationale for the repeated references and the inconsistencies in formatting?** 
4. **If you intend to present a synthesis or analysis of these works, please provide that analysis.** Where is the discussion of how these papers relate to each other and to the field?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The inclusion of "Confidential Review Copy" and "DO NOT DISTRIBUTE" is concerning and suggests an inappropriate submission.
*   The formatting issues suggest the text may have been extracted from a PDF with errors during optical character recognition (OCR).
*   This submission, as is, is not suitable for publication at ACL.  It requires substantial revision, including a clear statement of research contribution and a complete overhaul of the formatting and organization. It needs to be a *paper* with a *method* and *results*.
