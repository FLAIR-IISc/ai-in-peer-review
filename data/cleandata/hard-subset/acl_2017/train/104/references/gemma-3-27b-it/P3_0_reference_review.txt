## Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This paper appears to be a collection of bibliographic references related to Named Entity Disambiguation (NED), Knowledge Graph Completion, and related areas of Information Retrieval and Natural Language Processing. It doesn’t present a novel research contribution *per se*, but rather serves as a comprehensive list of relevant publications, potentially for a survey or literature review. The documents have numerous formatting errors and inconsistencies.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Compilation of Relevant Literature:** The primary contribution is the aggregation of a substantial number of references pertaining to NED, knowledge graph embeddings, and related techniques. This could be a valuable resource for researchers entering the field.
2. **Demonstration of Breadth:** The included papers cover a diverse range of approaches, spanning traditional information retrieval techniques (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) to recent deep learning models (Han et al., Wang & Li). This indicates a broad understanding of the research landscape.

**Strengths:**

1. **Potential Value as a Resource:** A well-organized and curated list of references is always useful, especially in rapidly evolving fields like NLP.  If cleaned up and properly formatted, this list could save researchers significant time in literature searches.
2. **Coverage of Foundational Work:** The inclusion of seminal papers like Aho & Corasick and Aoe demonstrates an awareness of the historical roots of string matching and indexing techniques that underpin many NED systems.
3. **Representation of Recent Advances:** The list incorporates recent publications (up to 2017) showcasing the application of deep learning and knowledge graph embeddings to the problem of entity disambiguation. This suggests the compilation is relatively up-to-date.
4. **Breadth of Topics:** The references cover a fairly extensive spectrum of relevant sub-topics, from entity linking and disambiguation to knowledge graph completion and representation learning.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty:**  The core “contribution” is simply a collection of existing work.  ACL is a research conference, and this submission does not present any new research findings, methods, or analysis. It is more suitable for a resource repository or perhaps a workshop focused on literature review methodology.
2. **Formatting and Consistency Issues:** The reference list is riddled with inconsistencies in formatting (e.g., different capitalization styles, missing fields, varying use of "et al."), and contains numerous errors (e.g. repeated references, broken/incomplete citations). This severely detracts from the list’s usability and professionalism. Many titles have unreadable characters due to encoding issues.
3. **Missing Context & Organization:** The references are presented as a flat list with no discernible organization.  It lacks any categorization (e.g., by task, method, or year) or annotations that would help users navigate the list effectively. There's no indication of *why* these specific papers were selected.
4. **Unclear Purpose:** The "Confidential Review Copy" and "DO NOT DISTRIBUTE" watermarks are confusing. The purpose of submitting this to ACL is unclear – is it intended as a resource to be made publicly available?  If so, the current state is unacceptable. If not, it's unclear what the submission intends to achieve.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the intended purpose of this submission?** Is it intended to be a publicly available resource, a preliminary step towards a larger survey, or something else entirely?
2. **If the intent is to create a usable resource, what steps will be taken to address the significant formatting and consistency issues?** Specifically, how will you ensure a uniform citation style and correct encoding errors?
3. **What criteria were used to select these references?**  A brief explanation of the inclusion/exclusion criteria would be helpful.
4. **Do you plan to add any structure or organization to the list (e.g., categories, annotations)?**  If so, please describe your proposed organization scheme.
5. **Why submit this as a conference paper?** A resource listing like this would be better suited to a different venue.



**Additional Comments:**

*   The repeated references should be removed.
*   The encoding errors need to be resolved to ensure all titles and authors are readable.
*   Consider adding DOIs to all references where available.
* The reference to "\Q2017\E" and "\Q2016\E" is not standard and needs to be corrected.



**Overall Assessment:**

This submission, in its current form, is **not suitable for acceptance to ACL**. It lacks novelty and suffers from significant formatting and organizational deficiencies.  However, with substantial revisions and a clear articulation of its purpose, it *could* potentially be repurposed as a valuable resource.  The authors need to address the weaknesses outlined above and clarify the motivation for submitting this work to a research conference.
