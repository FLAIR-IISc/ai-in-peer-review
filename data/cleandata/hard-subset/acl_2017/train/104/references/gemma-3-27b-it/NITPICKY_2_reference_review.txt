This submission presents a bibliography of references related to named entity disambiguation, knowledge graph completion, and related areas of natural language processing. The core of the submission *is* the bibliography itself. There is no novel research presented; rather, it’s a collection of papers.

**Contributions (as I see them):**

1.  A reasonably comprehensive, though deeply flawed (see weaknesses), collection of references relevant to a specific subfield of NLP. The potential benefit lies in providing a single point of access to many key papers.
2.  The attempt to categorize these references, implicitly demonstrating understanding of the relationships between different approaches within the field. (Though, this is hindered by the errors, discussed below).

**Strengths:**

1.  **Breadth of Coverage:** The bibliography includes many seminal papers in named entity disambiguation, knowledge graph embedding, and related techniques. It touches upon significant contributions from Aho and Corasick to more recent works.
2.  **Relevance:** The included papers are, generally, pertinent to the stated areas of focus – entity disambiguation, knowledge graph completion, and text representation.
3.  **Potential Utility:** For researchers new to the field, or looking for a starting point for literature review, this collection *could* be helpful… if it were accurate.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Formatting Inconsistency & Errors:** This is the most significant and pervasive issue. The formatting of each entry is wildly inconsistent. Author names are sometimes inverted, sometimes not. Journal/conference names are abbreviated inconsistently. Year formatting varies (“? 1975”, “\Q2017\E”). Some entries are truncated. Multiple entries are *identical* (Aho & Corasick appears several times verbatim). This lack of rigor undermines the entire utility of the bibliography.
2.  **Data Quality Issues:** Several entries contain extraneous text *within* the reference itself ("played it during public", "Confid tia Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE."). These additions appear as part of the reference string and are unacceptable.
3.  **Incomplete Information:** The presence of “?” as a placeholder for publication details is unprofessional and suggests a lack of thoroughness. While some papers may be preprints (e.g. CoRR entries), the missing data should be investigated and rectified if possible.
4.  **Lack of Novelty:** A bibliography, in and of itself, is not a research contribution. While compiling a curated list *can* be valuable, this submission lacks any original analysis, synthesis, or insight beyond the simple act of collection. This isn't a research paper; it's a list. The attempt to present it *as* a paper is problematic.
5.  **Arbitrary Inclusion Criteria:** It's unclear what criteria were used to select these references. Some highly relevant papers might be missing, while others appear without a clear justification.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  What was the specific methodology used to compile this bibliography? What search terms, databases, or inclusion/exclusion criteria were employed?
2.  Can you explain the source of the extraneous text found within several of the reference entries (e.g., “played it during public”)?
3.  What steps have been taken to ensure the accuracy and completeness of the bibliographic information for each entry? Specifically, how will you address the numerous formatting inconsistencies and missing data?
4.  Do you contend that a bibliography, without additional analysis or original contribution, constitutes a viable submission to ACL?

**Additional Comments:**

The repeated errors suggest a lack of automated processing or careful manual review. A simple script to enforce a consistent BibTeX or similar formatting standard would address many of the issues. This needs significant revision before it could be considered for acceptance. The current state is unacceptable for a venue like ACL.



**Recommendation:** **Reject.** The numerous flaws, particularly the formatting inconsistencies and data quality issues, severely detract from the potential utility of this bibliography. While the collection *could* be a helpful resource, it is currently presented in a manner that is unprofessional and undermines its credibility. The lack of original research contribution further weakens the submission.
