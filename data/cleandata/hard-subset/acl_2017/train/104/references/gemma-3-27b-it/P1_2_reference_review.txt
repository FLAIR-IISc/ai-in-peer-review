## Peer Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of references related to named entity disambiguation (NED), knowledge graph completion, and related areas of natural language processing. It primarily lists research papers spanning from foundational work in string matching (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) to more recent advancements in joint representation learning and knowledge graph embedding (Han et al., Wang & Li, Wu et al.). The "paper" itself doesn't present a novel research contribution; instead, it functions as a bibliography. The references cover a broad range of techniques, including word embeddings, knowledge graphs, probabilistic models, and deep learning approaches for NED and knowledge base completion. The format is rather unusual, resembling a raw list of citations with varying degrees of formatting consistency.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Comprehensive Bibliography:** The primary contribution is the assembly of a relatively comprehensive bibliography on the topics of NED and knowledge graph completion. This could be valuable to researchers entering the field, offering a starting point for literature review.
2. **Historical Context:** Inclusion of foundational papers like Aho & Corasick and Aoe provides valuable historical context, illustrating the evolution of techniques relevant to string matching and information retrieval that underpin modern approaches.
3. **Breadth of Coverage:** The list spans a considerable time period and incorporates papers from various venues (ACL, EMNLP, KDD, IEEE Transactions, CoRR), showcasing a wide range of approaches and perspectives.

**Strengths:**

1. **Potential Utility as a Resource:** For researchers new to the field, this bibliography could serve as a useful entry point, saving them time in initial literature searches. The sheer volume of citations is a benefit.
2. **Inclusion of Foundational Work:** Recognizing and including seminal papers like Aho & Corasick is commendable. It demonstrates an understanding of the roots of the field and how modern techniques build upon earlier research.
3. **Coverage of Diverse Approaches:** The bibliography is not limited to a single methodology (e.g., only deep learning). It encompasses a variety of techniques, providing a more holistic view of the research landscape.
4. **Timeliness (in some areas):** The inclusion of papers from 2016 and 2017 (Han et al., Yamada et al., Wu et al.) suggests an attempt to capture relatively recent advancements.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Not a Research Contribution:** This submission *is* a bibliography, not a research paper. It lacks any novel analysis, synthesis, or experimental evaluation. This is a fundamental issue for ACL, which focuses on original research.
2. **Poor Formatting and Consistency:** The formatting of the references is inconsistent (e.g., varying abbreviation styles, differing handling of "et al.", inconsistent date formats). This significantly detracts from its usability as a resource. Many entries are missing key information such as page numbers. There are duplicate entries.
3. **Lack of Organization/Categorization:** The bibliography is presented as a flat list. It lacks any organization, such as thematic categories, chronological order, or classification by technique. This makes it difficult to navigate and extract relevant papers.
4. **Data Quality Issues:** The repeated citations and typographical errors (e.g., “knowledg” instead of “knowledge,” inconsistent spacing) raise concerns about the attention to detail and reliability of the list. The phrases "Confidential Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE." and "played it during public" are misplaced and suggest the document has been copied and pasted from other sources.
5. **Missing Key Information:** Several entries lack crucial details like conference/journal names or page numbers, hindering their proper citation.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the intended contribution of this submission?**  Given that it is a bibliography, how does it meet the ACL's requirement for original research?
2. **What criteria were used for selecting the papers included in the bibliography?**  Was there a specific focus or scope beyond NED and knowledge graph completion?
3. **Do you plan to improve the formatting and consistency of the references?** Specifically, will you ensure all entries are complete, properly formatted, and free of typographical errors?
4. **Do you intend to add any organizational structure to the bibliography?** For example, categorization by technique, topic, or year of publication.

**Additional Comments:**

*   The submission's current format is unsuitable for publication in ACL.
*   If the authors intend to present this as a resource (e.g., a supplementary material for a tutorial), they should significantly improve the formatting and organization.
*   Consider exploring existing bibliographic databases (e.g., Semantic Scholar, Google Scholar) to see how NED and KG completion papers are already organized and presented.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Reject**. This submission, in its current form, does not meet the standards for acceptance at ACL due to its lack of original research contribution and significant formatting/quality issues. It would require substantial revision and a clear justification of its purpose to be considered further.
