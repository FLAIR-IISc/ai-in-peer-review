## Review: References - A Compilation of Works

**Summary:**

This submission is not a research paper but rather a collection of bibliographic references related to named entity disambiguation, knowledge graphs, and related areas of Natural Language Processing. The list includes foundational works on string matching (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) alongside more recent papers on joint representation learning, entity linking, and knowledge graph completion. The formatting of the references is inconsistent and contains numerous errors (see Weaknesses).

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Compilation of Relevant Literature:** The primary contribution, albeit a basic one, is the aggregation of a reasonably broad set of papers pertaining to a specific research area. This *could* be useful to someone entering the field.
2. **Historical Context:** The inclusion of seminal works like Aho & Corasick and Aoe alongside contemporary research provides a glimpse into the historical development of techniques used in these areas.



**Strengths:**

1. **Broad Coverage:** The list includes papers from a variety of relevant subfields – string matching, knowledge graph embedding, entity linking, and word sense disambiguation. This breadth suggests a certain level of awareness of the research landscape.
2. **Inclusion of Foundational Work:**  Referencing classic papers like Aho & Corasick demonstrates an understanding of the underlying principles that have informed more recent work. These provide the foundation for modern techniques.
3. **Relevance to Current Research:** Many of the listed papers deal with topics that are currently active areas of research within the NLP community, such as knowledge graph completion and joint embedding models.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Formatting Inconsistency & Errors:** The most significant weakness is the incredibly inconsistent and error-ridden formatting of the references.  Year formats vary ("? 1975", "\Q2016\E", "? 2007"), author names are inconsistently formatted (e.g., "Xu Han" vs. "Han et al\."), and there are numerous typographical errors (e.g., "digita" instead of "digital", "knowledg" instead of "knowledge"). This makes the list almost unusable for anyone needing accurate citations.
2. **Lack of Organization:** The references are presented in a seemingly random order.  There is no discernible organization (e.g., chronological, alphabetical, by topic). This makes it difficult to navigate and find specific papers.
3. **Not a Research Contribution:** This is fundamentally *not* a research paper. It’s a list of references. While compiling a useful bibliography *can* be valuable, this submission does not present any new research, analysis, or insight.  It doesn't meet the criteria for ACL publication.
4. **Redundancy:** Several references are repeated multiple times with minor variations in formatting (e.g., Aho & Corasick appears numerous times). This further highlights the lack of careful curation.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What was the intended purpose of compiling this list?** Was this intended as a resource for others, or as part of a larger research project?
2. **If this is intended as a resource, what criteria were used for selecting the included papers?**
3. **Will you be able to correct all formatting errors and inconsistencies?**  If so, please provide a sample of the corrected formatting.

**Additional Comments:**

This submission, as it stands, is not suitable for publication at ACL.  It requires substantial effort to clean up and organize the references before it could even be considered a useful resource. It does not constitute a research contribution.
