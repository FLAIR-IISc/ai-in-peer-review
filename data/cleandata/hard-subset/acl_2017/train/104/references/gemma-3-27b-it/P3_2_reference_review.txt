## Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of references related to named entity disambiguation (NED), knowledge graph completion, and related areas of natural language processing. It’s essentially a bibliography, rather than a novel research paper presenting new methods or findings. The provided "text" seems to be fragmented notes or extracted sentences from various papers, including mentions of objective functions and evaluation metrics, rather than a cohesive argument. The document lacks a clear thesis, methodology, experiments, or results section. It repeatedly lists the same references with minor formatting variations.

**Main Contributions (as perceived):**

Given the nature of the document, identifying contributions is challenging. The primary "contribution" is the *compilation* of a reasonably extensive list of relevant publications in the fields of knowledge graph embedding, entity linking, and related areas. This could be useful as a starting point for researchers entering the field. However, the repetition and lack of organization significantly diminish this benefit.



**Strengths:**

1. **Broad Coverage:** The bibliography touches upon many prominent papers in NED and KG completion, including key works by Aho & Corasick (string matching foundation), Aoe (double-array structures), Han et al., Mikolov et al. (word embeddings), and Weston et al.  It demonstrates awareness of the foundational work and current research trends in the field.
2. **Relevance to Key Areas:** The selected papers are demonstrably relevant to the core challenges of linking text to knowledge bases, representing entities and relationships, and leveraging knowledge for improved NLP tasks.
3. **Potential as a Starting Point:**  For someone unfamiliar with the field, this list *could* serve as a rough guide to essential literature. (Though heavily needing curation – see weaknesses).

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty/Originality:**  This is not a research paper. It’s a bibliography. Simply compiling a list of references, even a relevant one, does not constitute original research worthy of publication at ACL. The core purpose of ACL is to present *new* contributions to the field.
2. **Extreme Redundancy and Disorganization:** The same references appear *multiple* times, with slight formatting inconsistencies. This redundancy makes the document difficult to navigate and indicates a lack of careful curation. The lack of any clear organization or categorization (e.g., by task, methodology, or year) further hinders usability.
3. **Incoherent Text/Missing Components:** The interspersed fragments of text (e.g., "predict the context words...", "Joint representation learning...") are disjointed and lack context.  There’s no explanation of why these snippets are included or how they relate to the overall purpose of the document (assuming there is one).  Critically, there is a complete absence of a research question, methodology, experimental setup, results, or analysis.  The presence of "Confidential Review Copy" suggests this might be an internal document mistakenly submitted.
4. **Formatting Issues:** Numerous typographical errors and inconsistencies throughout the reference list (e.g., variations in author names, journal abbreviations) demonstrate a lack of attention to detail.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the primary research question or thesis being addressed by this submission?**  If the goal is to present a novel method or analysis, please clearly articulate it.
2. **Can you clarify the purpose of the interspersed text fragments?** How do these relate to the listed references, and to a broader research agenda?
3. **What specific contribution does this work make to the field of NLP, beyond simply listing existing publications?**
4. **If this is intended as a bibliography, why is it being submitted to a research conference like ACL?** Bibliographies are generally not appropriate submissions for peer-reviewed conferences.
5. **Please explain the source of the "Confidential Review Copy" tag.**



**Additional Comments:**

*   The repeated references suggest a possible error in data processing or compilation.
*   The inconsistent formatting of references needs to be addressed regardless of the submission's eventual outcome.
*   If the authors intend to submit a proper research paper, they must significantly expand upon this document to include a clear research question, methodology, experiments, and analysis.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Reject**.  This submission, as it stands, is not suitable for publication at ACL. It lacks the fundamental characteristics of a research paper and consists primarily of a poorly organized and redundant bibliography.  The authors need to either significantly revise the submission to present original research or withdraw it.
