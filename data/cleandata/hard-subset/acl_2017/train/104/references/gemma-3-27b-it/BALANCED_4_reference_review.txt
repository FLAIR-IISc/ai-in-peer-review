This paper presents a bibliography of references related to named entity disambiguation, knowledge graph embedding, and related areas. It appears to be a collection of papers relevant to a specific research project, potentially for a literature review or as supporting material for a larger work.

**Contributions:**

1. **Compilation of Relevant Literature:** The primary contribution is the aggregation of a substantial list of papers pertaining to entity disambiguation, knowledge graph completion, and related techniques. This serves as a valuable resource for researchers in these areas.
2. **Identification of Key Works:** The collection includes many seminal papers in the field, such as Aho and Corasick's work on string matching, Aoe’s double-array structure, Mikolov et al.’s word embeddings, and Han et al.’s joint representation learning. This indicates a good understanding of the foundational research.
3. **Focus on Joint Representation Learning:** The bibliography demonstrates a particular interest in approaches that combine textual information with knowledge graphs, reflecting a current trend in the field.

**Strengths:**

1. **Comprehensive Coverage:** The list is remarkably extensive, covering a wide range of techniques and approaches within the specified domains. It includes papers from various conferences (ACL, EMNLP, KDD, etc.) and journals (IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering, ACM Transactions on Information Systems).
2. **Inclusion of Foundational Work:** The bibliography doesn’t solely focus on recent publications; it incorporates seminal papers that laid the groundwork for current research, providing historical context.  Including Aho-Corasick and Aoe demonstrates an awareness of underlying algorithms.
3. **Relevance to Current Research Trends:**  The prominent inclusion of papers on joint representation learning (Han et al., Wang et al., Wu et al.) suggests a focus on state-of-the-art techniques.
4. **Potential Usefulness as a Resource:** This bibliography could be incredibly valuable to researchers entering the field or needing a comprehensive overview of relevant literature.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Analysis/Synthesis:** The document *is* a bibliography, and does not present any new research, analysis, or synthesis of the included works. It's a collection, not a contribution to scientific knowledge itself.  A simple list, while useful, does not meet the standard for ACL publication.
2. **Formatting Inconsistencies & Errors:** The formatting of the references is inconsistent and contains numerous errors (e.g., missing information, truncated titles, inconsistent use of et al., inconsistent date formatting, strange characters like "\Q2017\E"). This significantly detracts from its usability and appears careless.
3. **Redundancy:** Several references are repeated multiple times throughout the list.  This doesn't add value and indicates a lack of careful curation.
4. **Missing Context:** There is no explanation of the criteria used to select these papers or any indication of the specific research questions the bibliography is intended to support.  Is it exhaustive? Targeted?

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  What is the intended *purpose* of this bibliography? Is it intended to accompany a larger research work, or is it intended as a standalone resource?
2.  Could you clarify the selection criteria used to compile this list? What principles guided the inclusion (or exclusion) of specific papers?
3.  Will you undertake a thorough formatting review and correction of the references to ensure consistency and accuracy?
4.  Are you planning to add any analytical or synthetic content (e.g., summarizing key trends, identifying research gaps) to elevate this work beyond a simple list of references?



**Additional Comments:**

The numerous errors in formatting and the lack of any added value beyond a simple collection of references make this submission unsuitable for publication at ACL in its current state. While the list itself may be a useful resource *to the authors*, it doesn’t represent a contribution to the field in and of itself. A significant revision, addressing the weaknesses outlined above, is required for consideration.  Consider submitting this as a resource on a platform like GitHub if the primary goal is dissemination.
