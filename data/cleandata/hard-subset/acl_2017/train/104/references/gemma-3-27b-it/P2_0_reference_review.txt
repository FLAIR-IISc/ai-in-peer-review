## Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission consists entirely of a list of bibliographic references related to Named Entity Disambiguation (NED) and related fields like knowledge graph embedding, word sense disambiguation, and information retrieval. There is no paper *per se* – no methodology, experiments, results, or analysis. The list appears to be a collation of papers frequently cited in the NED literature. The document includes many instances of repeated entries with minor formatting differences (e.g., "Aho and Corasick." vs "Aho and Corasick.,").

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

Given the nature of the submission, framing "contributions" is difficult. However, if forced, I would list:

1. **Compilation of Relevant Literature:** The document *attempts* to compile a collection of foundational and important papers in NED and related areas. This *could* be useful as a starting point for researchers new to the field.
2. **Demonstration of Citation Patterns (Implicit):** The frequent repetition of certain papers suggests (though does not explicitly demonstrate) the core papers that heavily influence this research area.

**Strengths:**

1. **Potential Utility as a Starting Point:** For someone completely unfamiliar with NED, this list *could* provide an initial overview of key papers. It covers a broad range of relevant topics, including string matching (Aho-Corasick), knowledge graph embedding, and traditional IR techniques.
2. **Coverage of Foundational Work:** The list includes seminal papers like Aho and Corasick’s string matching algorithm, which is a fundamental technique often used in preprocessing steps for NED. The inclusion of Schütze’s IR textbook also indicates a foundational approach.
3. **Breadth of Topics:** The collection isn't limited to just NED; it touches on related areas like word sense disambiguation, entity linking, and knowledge graph completion. This demonstrates an understanding of the broader context of the field.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Not a Research Paper:** This is the most significant weakness.  ACL is a research conference, and this submission is simply a bibliography. It lacks any original research, analysis, or novel contribution. It doesn’t meet the basic criteria for an ACL submission.
2. **Poor Formatting & Redundancy:** The list is riddled with formatting inconsistencies and repeated entries. This suggests a lack of careful preparation and detracts from any potential usefulness.  Multiple entries exist with minor variations and unclear reasoning.
3. **Lack of Organization/Analysis:** The list is not organized in any meaningful way (e.g., chronologically, thematically, by impact). There is no analysis of the papers, no identification of research trends, and no discussion of open problems. It’s simply a dump of references.
4. **Missing Critical Information:** While many entries *attempt* to include venue and year, this is incomplete. Some entries lack this crucial information hindering their usability. Some years are questionably represented as "\Q2017\E".
5. **Unclear Purpose:** It's unclear *why* this list was compiled. Is it intended to be a comprehensive bibliography? A curated list of key papers? Without a clear purpose, it’s difficult to evaluate its value.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the primary research question or hypothesis this submission addresses?**  (Given the lack of a discernible research contribution, this is critical.)
2. **What is the rationale behind the selection of these specific papers?**  What criteria were used to include or exclude papers from the list?
3. **What is the intended audience for this list, and how is it different from existing bibliographies or survey papers in the field?**
4. **Why is this compilation presented as a research submission to ACL?**



**Overall Assessment:**

This submission is **strongly reject**. It does not meet the fundamental requirements of a research paper for a conference like ACL. While the compilation of references *could* be a useful resource, it is presented without any analysis, organization, or justification. The poor formatting and redundancy further detract from its value. This submission should not be considered for acceptance in its current form.
