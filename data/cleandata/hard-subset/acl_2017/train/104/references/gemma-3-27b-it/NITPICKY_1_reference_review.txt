This submission consists solely of a bibliography. There is no paper to review; only a list of references. The formatting of the references is inconsistent and riddled with errors.

**Summary & Contributions:**

There are no contributions to assess beyond the (poorly executed) compilation of a bibliography related to Named Entity Disambiguation (NED) and related fields. The purported "paper" merely lists publications.

**Strengths:**

It is difficult to identify strengths in a list of references presented in such a flawed manner. One could argue that the breadth of references *attempts* to demonstrate familiarity with the relevant literature. However, this is undermined by the numerous errors.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Formatting Inconsistencies:** The most glaring issue is the rampant inconsistency in formatting. Author names are presented in different orders (e.g., "Xu Han, Zhiyuan Liu, Maosong Sun" vs. "Alfred V Aho, Margaret J Corasick"). Some entries include middle initials, others do not. The use of et al. is inconsistent. Publication venues are sometimes abbreviated, sometimes fully spelled out, and sometimes a mixture (e.g., "ACL (2)." is jarring).
2. **Typographical Errors:** The list is littered with typos. “Efficie t” appears repeatedly. “digit l” and “doubl -array” are also present. “knowledg” appears numerous times. These errors significantly detract from the credibility of the compilation.
3. **Unclear Citation Keys:** The assigned citation keys (“Aho and Corasick.”, “Aoe.”, “Han et al\.”, etc.) are often redundant and incomplete. The inclusion of a question mark after the year (“? 1975”, “? 2016”) is particularly perplexing and unprofessional.  The presence of "\Q2017\E" is also unusual and suggests an improper encoding or data source.
4. **Redundancy:** Many references are duplicated verbatim. This adds unnecessary length and highlights the lack of quality control.
5. **Missing Information:** Some entries lack complete publication details. For example, “Confidential Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE.” is not a valid bibliographic entry. The same issue appears with  “Confidential Review Copy, DISTRIBUTE.”
6. **Inappropriate Inclusion:** Including "Confidential Review Copy" entries in an academic bibliography is entirely inappropriate.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. What is the purpose of submitting *only* a bibliography as a research paper?
2. If the intention was to create a bibliography for a larger project, why was it submitted in this error-filled state?
3. What criteria were used for selecting the references, and how does this selection process contribute to the field of NLP? (Given that the submission offers no analysis or novel insight beyond the list itself.)

**Additional Comments:**

The submission, as it stands, is not suitable for publication in ACL. It demonstrates a lack of attention to detail and a fundamental misunderstanding of what constitutes a scholarly work. The authors should revise the document meticulously, adhering to a consistent bibliographic style (e.g., BibTeX) and ensuring the accuracy of all entries. I recommend **rejection**. Even after substantial revision, I question the value of publishing a mere bibliography without accompanying research.
