This paper presents a bibliography of references related to named entity disambiguation and knowledge graph embedding. It is essentially a list of papers with some formatting inconsistencies in the citation style.

The primary contribution is the compilation of a relatively comprehensive list of relevant publications in the field. A secondary, weaker contribution is the implicit highlighting of key research areas within the broader topic of knowledge graph interaction with natural language processing. 

**Strengths:**

1. **Comprehensive Coverage:** The bibliography includes many seminal and recent papers in named entity disambiguation, knowledge graph completion, and related areas. It captures a significant body of work, potentially serving as a useful starting point for researchers new to the field.
2. **Relevance:** The papers included are almost entirely focused on the stated topics, indicating a clear scope and purpose for the compilation.
3. **Potential Utility:**  A consolidated list of references can be helpful for literature reviews, identifying research trends, and understanding the evolution of the field.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty:** The core activity – compiling a list of papers – is not research in itself.  Bibliographies are useful *tools*, but they do not constitute original contributions to the scientific literature.
2. **Inconsistent Formatting:** The citation formatting is highly inconsistent (e.g., differing use of "abs/", "\Q2017\E", variations in author lists, and capitalization). This inconsistency significantly reduces the usability of the bibliography. While the information *is* present, extracting it for use in a BibTeX file or similar is unduly burdensome.
3. **Missing Metadata:** Beyond the basic citation information, the bibliography lacks any additional metadata, such as abstracts, keywords, or brief summaries of each paper.  This limits its usefulness as a research tool.
4. **Redundancy:** Several references are repeated multiple times with slight variations in formatting. This redundancy adds noise and further highlights the formatting issues.
5. **No Clear Selection Criteria:** It is unclear what criteria were used to select the included papers. Was it based on citation count, relevance to a specific sub-problem, or simply a subjective collection?  A clear rationale for inclusion would strengthen the value of the bibliography.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. What specific criteria were used to determine which papers to include in the bibliography?
2. What is the intended use case for this bibliography? (e.g., a resource for students, a starting point for a systematic review, etc.)
3.  Do you plan to address the inconsistencies in citation formatting? If so, what style guide will you follow?
4.  Will you consider adding additional metadata (e.g., abstracts, keywords) to enhance the utility of the bibliography?



**Additional Comments:**

* The inclusion of "Confidential Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE." is puzzling and irrelevant to the scientific content of the bibliography.
* The frequent inclusion of the phrase "played it during public" next to a citation is unclear and should be removed.
