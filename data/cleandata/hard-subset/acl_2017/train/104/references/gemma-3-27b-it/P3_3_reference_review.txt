## Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of references related to Named Entity Disambiguation (NED) and knowledge graph completion. It's not a research paper presenting novel findings, but rather a bibliography. The references span a significant period (1975-2017) and cover foundational work in string matching (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) to more recent advancements in joint representation learning for knowledge graphs (Han et al., Wu et al.).  The repeated citation entries suggest potential issues with reference management.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Compilation of Relevant Literature:** The primary contribution is the gathering of a broad range of references relevant to NED and related areas. This could be useful to researchers entering the field, although its usefulness is limited by the issues described below.
2. **Historical Perspective:** The inclusion of older papers (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) provides a historical context for the field, highlighting the evolution of techniques used in string matching and information retrieval – fundamental components of NED.
3. **Breadth of Coverage:** The bibliography covers a variety of approaches to NED, including knowledge-based methods, embedding-based methods, and those leveraging deep learning.



**Strengths:**

1. **Broad Coverage of Key Areas:** The bibliography touches upon critical areas within NED and knowledge graph completion, including entity linking, word sense disambiguation, and representation learning. A quick scan reveals inclusion of pivotal papers like Mikolov et al. (2013a, 2013b), Weston et al. (2013), and Toutanova et al. (2015).
2. **Inclusion of Foundational Work:** Recognizing the importance of underlying string matching algorithms by including references like Aho & Corasick and Aoe is a valuable aspect.  These papers provide a solid basis for understanding the computational challenges inherent in NED.
3. **Potential Utility as a Starting Point:** For researchers unfamiliar with the field, the bibliography *could* serve as a starting point for literature review.  However, this is significantly undermined by the issues discussed in the "Weaknesses" section.
4. **Demonstrates Awareness of the Field:** The collection of references suggests the compiler has a good understanding of the landscape of NED research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Repetitive and Poorly Organized References:** The most significant weakness is the excessive repetition of references.  The same paper (e.g., Aho & Corasick, Aoe, Han et al.) is listed multiple times, sometimes with slight variations in formatting. This is extremely unprofessional and suggests a lack of care in compiling the bibliography.  A proper bibliography management tool would easily avoid this.
2. **Lack of Context/Annotation:**  The bibliography is simply a list of references. There's no annotation or categorization of the papers.  A useful bibliography would ideally group papers by topic, provide brief summaries, or highlight key contributions of each work.
3. **Not a Research Contribution:** This is not a research paper; it's a list of references.  As such, it doesn't present any novel research findings or analysis.  It's unclear what the submission aims to contribute to ACL beyond a raw list.  Is this intended as a resource for the community? If so, it needs significant improvement.
4. **Inconsistent Formatting:**  There's inconsistency in the formatting of the references.  Some include full publication details, while others are abbreviated. This further diminishes the utility of the bibliography.  The presence of "\Q2017\E" and similar artifacts suggests copy-paste errors.
5. **Missing DOIs/URLs:** Many entries lack Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs) or URLs, making it more difficult to locate the referenced papers.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the intended contribution of this submission to the ACL conference?**  Is it intended to be a community resource, and if so, why is it being submitted as a full paper?
2. **Can you explain the rationale behind the repeated entries in the bibliography?**
3. **Do you plan to implement a systematic organization and annotation of these references to enhance their usability (e.g., categorization by topic, brief summaries)?**
4. **Will you add DOIs or URLs to all references to facilitate access?**
5. **Will you ensure consistent formatting throughout the bibliography?**




**Additional Comments:**

*   The file name “Confidential Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE." seems irrelevant to the content and potentially misleading.
*   The excerpt "predict the context words by maximizing the following objective functi" appears to be from within a paper, not a bibliographic entry.



**Overall Assessment:**

In its current state, this submission is **not suitable for acceptance** to ACL. It lacks the core characteristics of a research contribution and suffers from significant issues with organization, formatting, and quality control.  If the authors clarify its purpose and substantially improve the bibliography’s usability, it *might* be considered as a resource for the community, but even then, it would be more appropriate for a different venue (e.g., a dedicated resource website).  The current submission requires a complete overhaul.
