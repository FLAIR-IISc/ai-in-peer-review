## Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of bibliographic references related to named entity disambiguation, knowledge graph completion, and related areas of natural language processing and information retrieval. It includes papers spanning from the 1970s to 2017, covering foundational work in string matching (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) to more recent advances in joint representation learning and knowledge graph embedding. The formatting is extremely poor with numerous errors (typos, inconsistent formatting, question marks where dates should be) and repetition of entries. 

**Main Contributions (as I perceive them – though severely hampered by the presentation):**

1. **Compilation of Relevant Literature:** The collection *attempts* to provide a broad overview of key publications in the field of entity disambiguation and knowledge integration. This could be valuable *if* the compilation was curated thoughtfully and presented cleanly.
2. **Historical Context:** Including seminal papers like Aho & Corasick alongside more recent work provides a glimpse into the evolution of relevant techniques, highlighting how fundamental algorithms have influenced contemporary NLP approaches. (Again, this is conditional on actually being able to *use* the list).



**Strengths:**

1. **Breadth of Coverage:** The list does touch on a significant range of influential papers. It includes works on string matching, word embeddings, knowledge graph embeddings, entity linking, and disambiguation. This suggests the compiler has some awareness of the core literature.
2. **Inclusion of Foundational Work:** The presence of classic papers like Aho & Corasick demonstrates an understanding that modern techniques build upon established computational foundations.
3. **Potential for Resource (If Fixed):** A well-formatted, curated bibliography of this scope *could* serve as a valuable resource for researchers entering the field or seeking a comprehensive overview.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Terrible Formatting and Consistency:** This is the most significant weakness. The reference list is riddled with errors: typos, inconsistent author name formatting (e.g., "n et al."), missing information, extraneous characters (question marks), and repeated entries. This makes the list almost unusable.
2. **Lack of Curation/Annotation:** The list appears to be a simple concatenation of references without any organization, categorization, or annotation. There's no indication of the relevance of each paper to specific sub-problems within entity disambiguation, or any indication of key ideas/contributions within each paper. 
3. **Missing Key Information:** Dates are often unclear or replaced with question marks.  Venue information is sometimes incomplete. This severely limits the usability of the references.
4. **No Clear Contribution:** As it stands, this is simply a list of references. There is no novel method, analysis, or insight presented. Simply collecting papers doesn’t constitute a research contribution to ACL.
5. **Repetitive Entries:** Many of the same papers are listed multiple times, further cluttering the list and suggesting a lack of careful review.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the core research question or contribution of this work?**  Simply listing references is not a research contribution. What specific problem are you addressing, and how does this collection of references contribute to solving it?
2. **What criteria were used to select these references?**  Was there a systematic approach to identify relevant papers, or was this a random selection?
3. **Can you provide a detailed explanation of the formatting errors and what steps have been taken to correct them?** The current state is unacceptable for an ACL submission.
4. **What is the intended use of this bibliography?** Is it intended to be a resource for researchers, a starting point for a literature review, or something else?



**Additional Comments:**

*  The "Confidential Review Copy" tag is odd.
* The phrase “played it during public” is present in one entry and appears to be unrelated to the paper itself.
* The submission requires a *complete* overhaul of its formatting and presentation to be considered even remotely acceptable.  A simple copy-paste into a bibliography management tool (BibTeX, Zotero, etc.) would be a significant improvement.
* Without a clear purpose, organization, or correction of the myriad errors, this submission does not meet the standards for acceptance at ACL.



**Overall Score:**  Strong Reject.  The current state of this submission is significantly below the quality expected for an ACL publication. While the underlying idea of compiling a resource *could* be valuable, the execution is severely flawed.
