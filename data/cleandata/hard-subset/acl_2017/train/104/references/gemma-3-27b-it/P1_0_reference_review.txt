## Peer Review for ACL Submission

**Summary:**

This submission appears to be a collection of references related to Named Entity Disambiguation (NED), Knowledge Graph Completion, and related areas like word embeddings and information retrieval. It’s essentially a bibliography, citing papers from conferences like ACL, EMNLP, KDD, and journals like IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering and ACM Transactions on Information Systems. The references span a significant period, from the 1970s (Aho & Corasick) to 2017. The formatting of the reference list is inconsistent and contains numerous errors (typos, missing information, inconsistent abbreviation of venues).

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Compilation of Relevant Literature:** The primary contribution is the attempt to gather a reasonably broad collection of papers relevant to the field of NED and related topics. This *could* be useful as a starting point for researchers entering the field. However, its current state severely limits this utility.
2. **Historical Perspective:** The inclusion of older papers (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) provides a historical context for string matching techniques that underpin many NED approaches. This demonstrates awareness of the foundational work.

**Strengths:**

1. **Breadth of Topics:** The list covers a fairly wide range of techniques used in NED, including knowledge graph embeddings, word sense disambiguation, and traditional information retrieval methods. This suggests a reasonable grasp of the field's diverse approaches.
2. **Inclusion of Foundational Work:**  The inclusion of classic papers on string matching (Aho & Corasick, Aoe) is a positive aspect, grounding the work in established computer science principles.
3. **Coverage of Recent Approaches:** The list includes papers from as recent as 2017, demonstrating awareness of current research trends.



**Weaknesses:**

1. **Formatting and Accuracy Issues:** This is the most significant weakness. The reference list is riddled with errors. There are typos in author names, missing information (e.g., full conference/journal names), inconsistent abbreviations, and repeated entries.  The formatting is highly inconsistent. This makes the list unreliable and difficult to use.  For example, multiple entries use "\Q2017\E" which is not standard bibliographic formatting.
2. **Lack of Organization/Categorization:** The references are presented in a seemingly random order.  There's no clear categorization or grouping of papers by topic, technique, or year. This hinders usability and makes it difficult to find relevant papers.
3. **Missing Context & Annotations:**  The list is simply a collection of references *without any context*. There is no indication of *why* these papers were chosen, what their key contributions are, or how they relate to each other. A simple categorization, or even brief annotations for each paper, would dramatically improve the value of this resource.  
4. **Not a Research Contribution:**  As it stands, this is *not* a research contribution suitable for a conference like ACL.  It's a preliminary step – a bibliography – that requires significant refinement and value-added components to be considered a substantial piece of work. It lacks novelty and doesn’t present any new analysis, insights, or methods.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **What is the intended purpose of this collection of references?**  Is it meant to be a tutorial resource, a starting point for a literature review, or something else?
2. **What criteria were used to select these references?** What makes this collection distinct or more valuable than a standard Google Scholar search on NED?
3. **What steps have been taken to ensure the accuracy and completeness of the reference list?**  Please detail the process used to verify the information.
4. **Do you plan to add any organizational structure (e.g., categorization, thematic grouping) or annotations to the references to enhance their usability?**
5. **Do you plan to expand this into a survey paper or a more substantive contribution?** If so, please outline the planned additions.




**Additional Comments:**

*   The repeated references should be removed.
*   The formatting should be standardized according to a widely accepted bibliographic style (e.g., BibTeX, ACM style).
*   Consider adding DOI’s where available to ensure easy access to the papers.
*   The submission title is missing.

**Overall Assessment:**

This submission, in its current form, is not suitable for acceptance at ACL. The significant errors and lack of organization make it an unreliable and unhelpful resource. While the effort to compile a list of relevant papers is commendable, substantial revisions are needed to address the identified weaknesses.  If the authors can significantly improve the formatting, accuracy, and usability of the reference list, and potentially expand it into a more substantive contribution (like a well-annotated survey), it *might* be reconsidered. However, as it stands, it requires substantial work before it can be considered a valuable contribution to the field.
