This paper presents a bibliography of research papers related to named entity disambiguation, knowledge graph completion, and related areas of natural language processing. The list appears comprehensive, covering key publications from the mid-2000s to 2017. The primary contribution of this submission is the curated collection of references itself, valuable for researchers entering or working within these fields. A secondary contribution is the demonstration of a functional (though imperfect, see weaknesses) citation management system capable of assembling such a bibliography.

**Strengths:**

1. **Comprehensive Resource:** The bibliography is extensive, including many seminal papers in named entity disambiguation (NED), knowledge graph completion (KGC), and related areas like word sense disambiguation and representation learning. It provides a valuable starting point for researchers seeking to understand the historical development and current state-of-the-art in these fields.
2. **Breadth of Coverage:** The references cover a range of techniques, including early statistical methods, knowledge-based approaches, and recent deep learning models. The inclusion of papers from various venues (ACL, EMNLP, KDD, etc.) indicates a wide-ranging search strategy.
3. **Potential for Automation:** The underlying system capable of producing this bibliography (implied by the repeated formatting and structure) suggests the potential for automated literature surveys and knowledge base construction, a valuable research direction.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Formatting Inconsistencies & Errors:** The most significant weakness is the inconsistent and often incorrect formatting of the references. Authors’ names are sometimes abbreviated, publication years are represented with question marks or in a non-standard format ("\Q2017\E"), and venue information is incomplete or ambiguous. There are numerous typographical errors throughout the list.  This reduces the usability of the bibliography.
2. **Lack of Novelty in Bibliography Itself:** While the *collection* of references is valuable, the *selection* of references doesn't demonstrate substantial novelty. It’s a strong collection of known important papers, but doesn't present overlooked or emerging work. It feels like a standard literature review output, not a curated, insightful contribution.
3. **Unclear System Description:** The paper itself consists *only* of the bibliography. There is no description of the methods used to compile it (e.g., search terms, inclusion/exclusion criteria, citation network analysis).  It is unclear if a specific algorithm or a manual process was used, hindering reproducibility and assessment of the underlying methodology.  The lack of a ‘Methods’ section is a major shortcoming.
4. **Redundancy:** The bibliography contains numerous duplicate entries for the same paper, sometimes with slightly different formatting. This further diminishes its usability.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  What methodology was used to construct this bibliography? Please describe the search terms, databases utilized, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and any automated processes involved.
2.  What is the intended application of the underlying system that generated this bibliography? Is it meant to be a general-purpose citation manager, or specifically targeted at research in NED and KGC?
3.  How were the formatting errors and redundancies addressed?  Will the underlying system be improved to consistently and accurately format references in a standard style (e.g., BibTeX)?
4.  What distinguishes this bibliography from readily available literature surveys or citation lists found on platforms like Google Scholar or Semantic Scholar?



**Additional Comments:**

The reference to "Confidential Review Copy. DO NOT DISTRIBUTE." is unusual and raises questions about the source and intended use of this work. It’s unclear why this bibliography is being submitted to a conference. The repeated presence of “played it during public” in one reference is a clear error and highlights the need for thorough review and cleaning of the data.
