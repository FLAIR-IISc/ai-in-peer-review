- Summary: Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings from text and KB entities and uses an LM-based disambiguation to achieve SOTA on an entity linking benchmark.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a multi-prototype mention embedding model to capture mention ambiguity.

- Contribution 2: Designs an efficient language model–based method to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on an entity linking benchmark.

- Strength 1: Novel approach that effectively models mention ambiguity.

- Strength 2: Disambiguation method is efficient and practical for entity linking.

- Strength 3: Empirical results show strong, SOTA performance.

- Strength 4: Evaluation uses a benchmark dataset enabling fair comparison.

- Strength 5: Paper is clearly written and well organized.

- Weakness 1: Related work discussion is limited, obscuring novelty relative to prior methods.

- Weakness 2: No detailed analysis of the learned embeddings to explain model behavior.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation is limited to entity linking, so generalizability is unclear.
