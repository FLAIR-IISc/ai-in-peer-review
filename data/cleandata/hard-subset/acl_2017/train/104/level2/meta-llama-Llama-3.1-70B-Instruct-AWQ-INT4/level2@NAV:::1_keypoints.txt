- Summary: Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that integrates textual context and KB entities to learn multiple sense embeddings and uses an LM-based disambiguator; reports SOTA on an entity linking benchmark.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a multi-prototype mention embedding to handle mention ambiguity by learning multiple sense embeddings.
- Contribution 2: Designs an efficient language model–based method to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.
- Contribution 3: Achieves state-of-the-art entity linking results on a benchmark dataset.
- Strength 1: Addresses the important NLP problem of mention ambiguity with a novel approach.
- Strength 2: Provides thorough evaluation on entity linking and demonstrates strong empirical performance.
- Strength 3: Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of the model and components.
- Weakness 1: Heavy reliance on prior work with limited discussion of differences from existing approaches.
- Weakness 2: Evaluation is confined to entity linking; generalization to other tasks (QA, text classification) is not shown.
- Weakness 3: Lacks details on computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.
- Question 1: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words and are there plans to extend OOV handling?
- Question 2: How does the model deal with homographs (same spelling, multiple meanings)?
- Question 3: How does the model perform with limited training data, and are few-shot or transfer learning approaches considered?
