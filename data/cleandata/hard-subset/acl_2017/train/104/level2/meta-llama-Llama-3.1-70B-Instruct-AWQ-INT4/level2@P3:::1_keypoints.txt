Paper proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that jointly learns multiple sense embeddings and uses a language-model-based disambiguation to achieve SOTA on an entity linking benchmark.
Novel multi-prototype embedding to handle mention ambiguity in a unified semantic space.
Joint learning of textual context words and KB-derived entities for richer mention representations.
Efficient LM-based disambiguation method to assign mentions to specific senses.
Strength: novel approach that integrates knowledge bases into mention disambiguation.
Strength: thorough evaluation demonstrating state-of-the-art performance.
Strength: clear, well-written presentation of the method.
Weakness: limited discussion and comparison with related work.
Weakness: insufficient analysis of the model’s limitations and failure cases.
Weakness: lack of qualitative examples illustrating correct and incorrect disambiguations.
Question: how will the model scale and remain computationally efficient on large datasets?
Question: please clarify hyperparameter choices and their impact on performance.
Question: how can the approach be extended to other tasks like NER or relation extraction?
Additional comment: solid paper overall but should address the above weaknesses to strengthen the submission.
