- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention to address mention ambiguity.  
- Jointly models textual context words and knowledge-base entities to learn mention senses in a unified semantic space.  
- Introduces an efficient language-model–based disambiguation method to assign mentions to specific senses.  
- Evaluated on entity linking tasks and reports state-of-the-art performance.  
- Contribution: novel multi-prototype embedding approach for mention sense modeling.  
- Contribution: efficient LM-based disambiguation strategy for mapping mentions to senses.  
- Contribution: empirical SOTA results on entity linking benchmarks.  
- Strength: model novelty with promising results on ambiguity handling.  
- Strength: disambiguation method is efficient and practically useful.  
- Strength: comprehensive benchmark evaluation and analysis.  
- Weakness: unclear discussion of model scalability for real-world deployment.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art models.  
- Weakness: focus on entity linking may limit demonstrated applicability to other NLP tasks.  
- Question: how are out-of-vocabulary entities handled and can performance be improved for them?  
- Question: please provide detailed computational complexity analysis of the model and disambiguation procedure.
