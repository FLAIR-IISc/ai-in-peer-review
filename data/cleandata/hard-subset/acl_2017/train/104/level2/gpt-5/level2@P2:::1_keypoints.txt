- Proposal: Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings (MPME) learns multiple sense-specific embeddings per surface form by jointly modeling context words and KB entities, with an LM-based disambiguation to pick a sense at test time.  
- Claimed contributions: joint framework inducing multiple mention senses, an efficient LM-based disambiguation procedure, and empirical EL gains.  

- Strength: Clearly targets mention ambiguity by modeling surface forms with multiple prototypes rather than monosemous mentions.  
- Strength: Joint embedding of words, mentions, and entities could improve candidate ranking and contextual scoring in a unified space.  
- Strength: The LM-based disambiguation is simple, efficient, and easy to integrate into existing EL pipelines.  
- Strength: Reported state-of-the-art EL results, if validated on standard benchmarks and baselines, would be significant.  
- Strength: Multi-prototype mention representations may generalize to related tasks (NER+linking, QA, query understanding).  

- Weakness: Novelty unclear given prior multi-prototype and joint word/entity embedding literature; paper must clarify technical novelties and include ablations.  
- Weakness: Disambiguation appears local-only and may ignore document-level coherence that strong EL systems exploit.  
- Weakness: Empirical setup under-specified — datasets, splits, metrics, and baselines are not clearly reported.  
- Weakness: Unclear how many prototypes per mention (fixed K, frequency-dependent, or nonparametric) and how tail mentions/NILs are handled.  
- Weakness: Training objective and optimization details (which co-occurrences, negative sampling, pretraining) are insufficiently specified for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues — duplicated/garbled references and typos hinder clarity and positioning vs prior work.  

- Question: How is the number of prototypes per mention determined and what hyperparameters control sense splitting/merging?  
- Question: Are prototypes explicitly tied to KB entities or are they latent clusters that may span multiple entities, and how are prototypes mapped to entities at test time?  
- Question: Please provide the exact training objectives: which pairs/triples are optimized, sampling scheme, and context construction; is any component pretrained?  
- Question: What is the concrete “language model” used for disambiguation, and does the method incorporate or allow incorporation of document-level coherence?  
- Question: Which datasets, metrics, and baselines are evaluated (e.g., AIDA-CoNLL, TAC KBP, Hoffart/Yamada/Pershina), and are significance tests reported?  
- Question: Ablations requested — gains from multi-prototype vs single-prototype, from joint training vs text-only, and from LM-based scoring vs standard scoring.  
- Question: What are training/inference complexities and wall-clock times, and how does candidate generation work and affect recall?  

- Additional: Fix and deduplicate references, correct formatting, and add missing recent EL baselines.  
- Additional: Include error analysis showing when multi-prototype helps or fails (ambiguous mentions vs rare/NILs).  
- Additional: Consider adding a lightweight document-level coherence module to complement the local scorer.  
- Additional: Release code/embeddings to facilitate reproducibility.  

- Overall: Important, practical idea with potential impact, but needs clearer novelty claims, full methodological details, comprehensive evaluations (including collective baselines and ablations), and improved presentation to be convincing.
