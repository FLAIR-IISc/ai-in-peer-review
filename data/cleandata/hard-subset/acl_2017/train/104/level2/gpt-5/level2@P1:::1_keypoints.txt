- Proposes MPME: multi-prototype mention embeddings that jointly model textual context and KB entities for mention-level senses.  
- Introduces a lightweight LM-based disambiguator to select mention senses at inference.  
- Reports state-of-the-art entity linking (EL) performance with qualitative analyses of learned spaces.

- Clear focus on modeling ambiguity at the mention level, filling a practical EL gap versus prior word-level multi-sense or joint text–KB work.  
- Empirical gains on standard EL benchmarks, especially for highly ambiguous mentions, with competitive baselines and favorable speed-accuracy tradeoffs.  
- Joint training ties context words to candidate entities, producing sense clusters anchored to real-world entities.  
- Efficient, simple inference via the LM disambiguator attractive for deployment compared to heavy collective methods.  
- Design is extensible and compatible with different candidate generation strategies and global priors.

- Novelty relative to prior multi-prototype and joint embedding work is not sharply positioned; technical differences need clearer articulation.  
- Sense inventory learning is under-specified: fixed vs adaptive K, mechanisms to avoid collapse or proliferation, and regularization/priors are unclear.  
- Details of the LM disambiguator and handling of global/document-level coherence are insufficiently described.  
- Ablations and diagnostics are lacking (e.g., single vs multi-prototype, text-only vs KB, number of senses, role of LM, candidate generation).  
- Evaluation limited to EL; broader transfer to tasks like WSD or typing would strengthen claims.  
- Presentation issues: duplicated/garbled references and formatting need cleanup.

- How is the number of senses per mention determined and what stops new sense creation?  
- What is the exact training objective and how are entities incorporated to prevent sense collapse?  
- Which candidate generation and mention detection resources/algorithms are used, and what are their recall/precision tradeoffs?  
- What is the LM disambiguator’s architecture, context windowing, smoothing, negative sampling, and use of entity priors?  
- Is document-level coherence modeled and how does performance compare to collective systems?  
- Which EL datasets, metrics (micro/macro, NIL handling), and baselines were used, and were results significance-tested?  
- What are the resource footprints: number of senses learned, memory, and training/inference time; how are rare/OOV mentions handled?

- Request more ablations and error analysis to show when multi-prototype mentions help (ambiguity buckets, head vs tail, NIL cases).  
- Suggest testing transfer to non-Wikipedia text to assess robustness.  
- Recommend cleaning references/formatting and ensuring all baselines are properly cited.  
- Encourages releasing code and pretrained embeddings to increase impact.

- Overall: well-motivated and practically useful with promising EL results; recommend acceptance conditional on clearer positioning, stronger ablations, and improved presentation.
