- Proposal: Multi-Prototype Mention Embeddings (MPME) — learn multiple sense-specific embeddings per surface mention by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities to handle mention ambiguity.  
- Disambiguation: Introduces an efficient language-model-based resolver that maps mention occurrences to learned prototypes using surrounding context.  
- Evaluation claim: Case study on entity linking with qualitative/quantitative analyses and claimed state-of-the-art results.

Primary contributions:
- Mention-centric multi-prototype embedding formulation integrating word context and entity supervision in a unified space.  
- Practical, efficient local disambiguation mechanism leveraging a language model over the learned space.  
- Empirical EL study demonstrating the representation’s utility (details pending).

Strengths:
- Clear motivation and novel angle: modeling ambiguity at the surface-mention level fills a gap between word-sense and entity embeddings.  
- Joint embedding of words, entities, and mention-senses could improve robustness for ambiguous mentions with limited context.  
- Efficiency focus: prototype-based LM resolver is practically appealing compared to heavier collective methods.  
- Potential empirical impact if validated on standard EL benchmarks against strong baselines.

Weaknesses:
- Methodological clarity lacking: training objective, prototype induction (fixed K vs nonparametric), and supervision details are unspecified.  
- Disambiguation model underspecified: unclear what “language model based” concretely means and how priors are integrated.  
- Evaluation details unclear: datasets, metrics, splits, baselines, and claims of SOTA are not specified.  
- Relation to related work needs sharpening: differences vs joint word-entity embeddings, multi-sense methods, and neural EL models are not well articulated.  
- Presentation issues: duplicate/malformed references undermine clarity and verifiability.

Questions for authors:
- How is the number of prototypes determined and how are senses initialized/updated to avoid collapse or proliferation?  
- What is the exact training objective, negative sampling strategy, and source of entity supervision (anchors/Wikilinks, KB version)?  
- What precise language model is used for sense selection and how are mention→entity priors incorporated?  
- Which datasets, splits, and metrics are used and which baselines (with numbers) support the SOTA claim?  
- Please provide ablations: single vs multi-prototype, with/without entity supervision, effect of prototype count, runtime/memory, and performance on highly ambiguous mentions.  
- How does the model handle unseen or rare mentions at test time?

Suggestions for improvement:
- Formalize the model and disambiguation procedure with equations, algorithms, and complexity analysis.  
- Strengthen empirical evaluation with standard EL benchmarks, rigorous baselines, comprehensive ablations, and targeted error analysis.  
- Clarify positioning vs multi-sense and joint word-entity embeddings and argue advantages of mention-level prototypes.  
- Release code and pretrained embeddings and document datasets/preprocessing for reproducibility.  
- Thoroughly revise and correct the references and presentation.

Overall assessment:
- Promising and well-motivated idea that could make a solid contribution if methodological details, rigorous comparisons, and presentation issues are resolved.
