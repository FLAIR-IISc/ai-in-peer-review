- Summary: Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings for mentions by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities with a disambiguation module for entity linking.

- Contribution 1: Multi-prototype embedding approach integrating text and knowledge-base signals, though novelty appears limited relative to prior work.

- Contribution 2: An efficient disambiguation method that leverages language models, but the abstract provides no empirical validation.

- Contribution 3: Claims state-of-the-art entity linking performance, which is unverified in the provided abstract.

- Strength 1: The entity ambiguity problem is well-motivated and the text+KB integration aligns with current NLP trends.

- Strength 2: The multi-prototype embedding framework is conceptually clear and could benefit downstream tasks like entity linking.

- Weakness 1: Unsupported "state-of-the-art" claim with no quantitative results, benchmarks, or comparisons presented.

- Weakness 2: Questionable novelty due to close resemblance to Huang et al. (2012) and Neelakantan et al. (2014) and inadequate differentiation.

- Weakness 3: Severe bibliographic issues—numerous duplicate entries, typos, and omission of recent relevant work (e.g., Wang et al. 2020).

- Question 1: Provide specific entity linking results (e.g., on AIDA/ACE) with baseline comparisons to substantiate SOTA claims.

- Question 2: Clarify how MPME significantly differs from Huang et al. (2012) and Neelakantan et al. (2014), particularly in KB usage.

- Question 3: Explain why key recent works (e.g., Mancini et al. 2016, Wang et al. 2020) are omitted from related work.

- Additional comment: The idea has potential but the lack of experiments, weak novelty justification, and bibliographic errors are serious shortcomings that must be addressed.
