- Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings for entity mentions via joint modeling of textual context and knowledge-base entities, plus an LM-based disambiguation approach with reported EL gains.
- Contribution: a multi-prototype embedding framework tailored to entity mentions.
- Contribution: a disambiguation method that leverages language modeling.
- Contribution: empirical validation showing improved embeddings and disambiguation, claiming state-of-the-art EL results.
- Strength: well-motivated problem statement linking mention ambiguity to text-KB integration and using joint modeling.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation with qualitative embedding analyses and quantitative EL accuracy.
- Strength: reports clear EL improvements on benchmarks (e.g., ~2.5% F1 gain on AIDA) over older strong baselines.
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from prior multi-prototype word embedding work; novelty for entity mentions is not well justified.
- Weakness: disambiguation method is vaguely specified (architecture, training, complexity), so efficiency claims are unverifiable.
- Weakness: evaluation omits recent 2016–2017 SOTA EL methods, so the state-of-the-art claim is questionable.
- Question: how does MPME specifically leverage entity-specific KB knowledge (e.g., relations) beyond standard word-sense methods?
- Question: what is the exact architecture and training/details of the language-model-based disambiguator and how it differs from existing context/co-occurrence or graph methods?
- Question: why were recent SOTA works (e.g., Wu et al., 2016; Mancini et al., 2016) excluded, and how would inclusion affect results?
