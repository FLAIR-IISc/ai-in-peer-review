- Summary: Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings by jointly modeling textual context and KB entities, plus an efficient disambiguation model, evaluated on entity linking.

- Strength: Multi-prototype approach directly addresses mention ambiguity and yields a 1.8% absolute F1 gain over Han et al. (2016) on AIDA.

- Strength: Disambiguation module is efficient (20% faster inference than Hoffart et al., 2011) and ablations show both embedding and disambiguation components are important.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation with qualitative cluster visualizations and quantitative benchmarks on AIDA and ACE05, avoiding reliance on a single metric.

- Strength: Method integrates easily into existing entity linking pipelines without major architectural changes.

- Weakness: Novelty claim is weak—insufficiently differentiated from prior multi-prototype or KB-integrating work (e.g., Mancini et al., 2016).

- Weakness: "State-of-the-art" claim is unsupported—paper’s 85.2% F1 on AIDA is lower than Zhang et al. (2016)’s 86.7%, and recent SOTA comparisons are missing.

- Weakness: No clear explanation or analysis of how the number of prototypes per mention is chosen (fixed vs. dynamic) or its impact on performance.

- Question: How does MPME select the number of prototypes per mention and are there ablation results for different prototype counts?

- Question: Why was Zhang et al. (2016) omitted from comparisons, and does the disambiguation module’s efficiency come at a cost to accuracy versus such methods?
