- Proposes MPME to learn multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly modeling textual contexts and KB entities.  
- Introduces an efficient language-model-based method to disambiguate mentions to specific senses.  
- Claims state-of-the-art results on entity linking with qualitative/quantitative validation of embeddings.

- Novel integration of text and KB to learn multi-prototype embeddings, avoiding separate-modality pitfalls.  
- Clear, effective disambiguation framework demonstrated on standard entity linking tasks.  
- Strong qualitative examples showing embeddings capture context-specific senses (e.g., “Apple” as company vs. fruit).

- Overstates novelty and omits critical comparisons to prior multi-prototype work (Huang et al. 2012; Tian et al. 2014), weakening the SOTA claim.  
- Reference list contains repeated typos and duplicate entries, suggesting poor editing and undermining credibility.  
- Insufficient analysis of limitations (failure cases, rare mentions, KB coverage, scaling) and limited quantitative ablation of embedding quality.

- How does MPME perform relative to Huang et al. (2012) and Tian et al. (2014) on the same benchmarks?  
- Are the multiple reference errors typos in the submission or errors in the cited works (e.g., Aho and Corasick; Han et al.)?  
- Please provide absolute F1/error rates and baseline numbers (e.g., Shen et al. 2015; Hoffart et al. 2011) to substantiate the SOTA claim.
