- Proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple sense embeddings per mention by combining textual context and knowledge-base structure.
- Introduces an efficient language model–based disambiguation method that uses the learned prototypes and reports state-of-the-art performance on an entity linking task.
- Tackles the important problem of mention ambiguity with a well-motivated synthesis of multi-prototype and joint text–KB embedding ideas.
- Offers a novel, technically sound approach to learn distinct, context-aware sense embeddings instead of single averaged mention vectors.
- Presents comprehensive evaluation including intrinsic analyses and extrinsic entity linking results showing practical utility.
- Abstract omits crucial architectural and training details (e.g., how prototype counts are chosen and the joint objective), hindering reproducibility and assessment.
- The disambiguation method is described as “efficient” but lacks explanation of its mechanism and novelty separate from the embedding model.
- The claim of state-of-the-art is unclear because the compared baselines and contemporaneous models are not specified.
- Question: Please detail the language model architecture used for disambiguation and how it incorporates the multi-prototype embeddings.
- Question: How is the number of prototypes per mention determined — fixed hyperparameter or learned from data/KB links?
