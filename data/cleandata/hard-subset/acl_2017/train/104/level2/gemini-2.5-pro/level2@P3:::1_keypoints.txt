- Problem: Models that use a single vector per mention conflate multiple entity senses (e.g., "apple" = company vs fruit), hurting joint text-KB embeddings.  
- Proposal: Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model that learns multiple vector prototypes per ambiguous mention by jointly training on text and a knowledge base.  
- Disambiguation: A language model–based method is used to select the appropriate prototype for a mention in context.  
- Empirical claim: Learned embeddings and disambiguation achieve state-of-the-art results on a benchmark entity linking task and show intrinsic embedding quality.  

- Strength: Addresses a significant problem with an intuitive, multi-prototype approach extending word-sense methods to joint text-KB embeddings.  
- Strength: Comprehensive joint learning framework that leverages both local textual context and global KB relational structure.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation with extrinsic entity linking gains supporting practical utility.  
- Strength: Two-pronged evaluation (intrinsic qualitative/quantitative + extrinsic task) suggests a thorough experimental setup.  

- Weakness: Disambiguation component is described too vaguely—details on architecture, inputs/outputs, and scoring are missing for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Method for choosing the number of prototypes per mention is unclear; sensitivity and whether it’s fixed or dynamic are not addressed.  
- Weakness: Lacks ablation analyses to disentangle gains from multi-prototype modeling versus joint text-KB learning (e.g., single-prototype or text-only baselines).  

- Question: Please provide a detailed description of the "language model based approach," including objective and scoring for candidate senses.  
- Question: How is the number of prototypes per mention determined, and what impact does this choice have on entity linking performance?  
- Question: Can you compare the full model to a single-prototype variant (and to text-only multi-prototype models) to isolate the source of performance gains?
