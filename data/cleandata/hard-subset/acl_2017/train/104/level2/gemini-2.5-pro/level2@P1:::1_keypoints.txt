- Tackles mention ambiguity by learning multiple sense (prototype) embeddings for ambiguous mentions jointly with word and knowledge-base entity embeddings.  
- Proposes a language-model–based disambiguation component to select the appropriate mention prototype in context.  
- Evaluated both intrinsically (embedding quality) and extrinsically on entity linking, claiming state-of-the-art results.  
- Contribution: novel adaptation of multi-prototype embeddings to entity mentions, integrating unstructured text with a structured KB.  
- Contribution: practical disambiguation mechanism that applies the learned prototypes in downstream tasks.  
- Contribution: strong empirical evidence via state-of-the-art entity linking performance.  
- Strength: addresses an important, well-motivated problem relevant to many NLP tasks.  
- Strength: conceptually elegant model that lets KB information guide sense separation for mentions.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation strategy combining intrinsic and extrinsic analyses.  
- Weakness: novelty over prior multi-prototype word embedding work is not clearly articulated.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on the disambiguation model’s design, objective, and computational costs.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of how the number of prototypes is chosen and model sensitivity to that hyperparameter.  
- Question: How is the number of sense prototypes per mention determined (fixed vs. dynamic) and how does it affect performance?  
- Question: What is the specific objective and mechanism of the “language model based” disambiguation?  
- Question: How much do text-only vs. KB-enhanced training contribute to final performance (ablation analysis)?
