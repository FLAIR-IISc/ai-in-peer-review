This paper proposes a Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding model to address the ambiguity of entity mentions when integrating text and knowledge. The core idea is to learn multiple sense embeddings for each mention by combining textual context and knowledge base entities, followed by a language model-based disambiguation approach. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through entity linking experiments, achieving state-of-the-art results on a benchmark dataset.

The primary contribution is the novel combination of multi-prototype embeddings with a language model for mention disambiguation. This allows for a more nuanced representation of mentions with multiple senses, improving the accuracy of entity linking. A secondary contribution is the demonstration of strong performance on a standard entity linking task, establishing a new baseline for future research. Finally, the paper provides a clear and well-structured approach to jointly modeling textual and knowledge-based information for improved entity representation.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on entity linking, providing concrete evidence of the method's effectiveness.
2.  **Clear Problem Definition:** The issue of mention ambiguity is well-defined and motivated, highlighting the importance of the proposed solution.
3.  **Sound Methodology:** The combination of multi-prototype embeddings and a language model is a logical and well-justified approach to address the problem.
4.  **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The use of a benchmark dataset and qualitative analysis provides a thorough evaluation of the model.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Limited Novelty of Components:** While the *combination* is novel, both multi-prototype embeddings and language model-based disambiguation have been explored independently in prior work. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how their approach differs significantly from existing methods.
2.  **Lack of Ablation Study:** The paper doesn’t include an ablation study to demonstrate the contribution of each component (multi-prototype embeddings vs. the language model). It’s unclear how much each part contributes to the overall performance.
3.  **Reference Formatting Issues:** The reference list is poorly formatted and contains many inconsistencies and repetitions. This detracts from the paper's professionalism and makes it difficult to verify claims.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed comparison of your approach to existing multi-prototype embedding methods (e.g., Huang et al., 2012; Neelakantan et al., 2014)? What are the key differences and advantages of your model?
2.  Would you consider adding an ablation study to evaluate the individual contributions of the multi-prototype embeddings and the language model?
3.  Please revise the reference list to ensure consistent formatting and remove duplicate entries.
