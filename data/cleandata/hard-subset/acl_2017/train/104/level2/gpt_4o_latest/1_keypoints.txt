- Proposes Multi-Prototype Mention Embedding (MPME) that generates multiple sense embeddings per mention by jointly leveraging textual context and a knowledge base.  
- Introduces a language model–based disambiguation method to map mentions to specific senses.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on a benchmark entity linking dataset with qualitative and quantitative experiments.  
- Novel and relevant: multi-prototype embeddings address mention ambiguity and integrate textual and KB signals.  
- Strong empirical evidence: achieves SOTA results on entity linking tasks.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation with detailed methodological explanations.  
- Comprehensive evaluation including qualitative analyses, multiple metrics, and comparisons with strong baselines.  
- Limited discussion of limitations, notably scalability and computational overhead for large KBs.  
- Disambiguation method appears straightforward and lacks strong novelty or theoretical grounding.  
- Reproducibility concerns due to missing hyperparameter settings, training details, and code access.  
- Lacks broader impact analysis, including applicability to other NLP tasks and potential biases.  
- Question: How does the model scale to very large knowledge bases and has efficiency been tested?  
- Question: Can you provide detailed hyperparameter settings and training process for reproducibility?  
- Question: How sensitive is disambiguation performance to the quality of the knowledge base?  
- Overall: Promising and well-evaluated contribution but needs revisions on scalability, reproducibility, and disambiguation novelty.
