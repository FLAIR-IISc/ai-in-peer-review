- Paper proposes a rule-based method for time expression extraction.  
- Key insight: time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token.  
- Time-token recognition uses dictionary lookup, regex matches, and POS tagging.  
- Time segments are expanded bidirectionally from the time token using heuristic rules.  
- Time segments are merged into single expressions using another rule set.  
- Evaluation on three datasets vs. rule-based and ML baselines shows significant improvements.  
- Strength: well written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: rules are motivated by empirical observations and supported by evaluation results.  
- Weakness: underspecified details hinder reproducibility.  
- Question (Sec 4.1): what are the "5 seasons" and how are things like Ramadan or holiday seasons handled?  
- Issue (Sec 5.1): text says "two benchmark datasets" but paper uses three datasets.  
- Request (Sec 5.2): provide an example of a time expression without a time token.  
- Question (Sec 5.2): with 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, how can performance be further improved?  
- Request: will the full set of rules/software be released?
