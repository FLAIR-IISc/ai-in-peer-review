This paper proposes a method for identifying temporal expressions from text. It is a simple rule-based method, which has a strong advantage as an analysis tool, because temporal expression recognition should be a fundamental process in applications.

The test results show that the proposed method performs better than state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based methods for temporal expression recognition.

That's great, but my concern is the generality of the method. The rules in the method were designed according to corpora observations, which are also used for evaluation. So I fear that the rules have become too over-fitted to these corpora. Similarly, the domains of these corpora may influence the rule design.

There are no statistics or discussions to show overlap in temporal expressions in the observed corpora. If it could be shown that temporal expressions in these corpora overlap in most cases, this information would support the generality of the rules.

However, the tests were conducted using a new corpus that was separate from the rule design process, so that it could be shown that the proposed method is widely effective.
