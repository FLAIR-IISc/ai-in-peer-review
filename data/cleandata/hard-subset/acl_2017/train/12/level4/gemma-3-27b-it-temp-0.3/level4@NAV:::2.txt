The paper introduces a rule-based approach for time expression recognition from text. A key strength of this method lies in its simplicity, making it a valuable analytical tool for applications where time expression recognition is a foundational step. Experimental results demonstrate superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning techniques. 

However, a concern regarding the method’s generalizability arises from the fact that the rules were developed based on the same corpora used for evaluation. This raises the possibility of overfitting to these specific datasets. Furthermore, the characteristics of the corpora’s domains may have influenced the rule design. The paper would benefit from an analysis quantifying the overlap of time expressions across the corpora used; demonstrating substantial overlap would strengthen the argument for the rules’ broader applicability. Ideally, evaluating the method on a novel, independent corpus – one not involved in the rule creation process – would provide stronger evidence of its widespread effectiveness.
