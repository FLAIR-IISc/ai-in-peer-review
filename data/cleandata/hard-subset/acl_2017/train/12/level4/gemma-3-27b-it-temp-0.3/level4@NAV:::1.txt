This paper presents a rule-based system for time expression extraction, grounded in the observation that time expressions are generally concise and include at least one identifiable time token. The method initially identifies time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and part-of-speech tagging. It then expands these tokens into complete time segments via a set of heuristic rules, operating bidirectionally from the initial token. Finally, these segments are consolidated into unified time expressions using a further set of rules. The proposed approach demonstrates substantial improvements over both rule-based and machine learning-based systems when evaluated across three datasets.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-written and clearly structured. The rules employed are demonstrably motivated by empirical analysis of the data and appear well-justified, as supported by the reported evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**

Certain aspects of the paper lack sufficient detail to enable straightforward reproduction of the reported results. Specific concerns are detailed below.

**General Discussion:**

* Section 4.1: The categorization of seasons as five requires clarification. The treatment of culturally-specific time expressions, such as Ramadan or the Holiday Season, should also be addressed.
* Section 5.1: The text refers to “two benchmark datasets,” while the evaluation appears to utilize three datasets. This discrepancy should be resolved.
* Section 5.2: Including an example of a time expression *without* a readily identifiable time token would be beneficial for understanding the system’s behavior.
* Section 5.2: Given that the approach appears to approach performance limits – with 93% of expressions containing a time token and the system achieving 92% recall – what strategies are envisioned for further performance gains?
* Finally, are there plans to publicly release the complete rule set and/or the software implementation used in this work?
