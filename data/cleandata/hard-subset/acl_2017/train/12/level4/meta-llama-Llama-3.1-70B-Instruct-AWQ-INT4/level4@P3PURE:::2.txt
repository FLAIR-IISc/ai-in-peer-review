The paper proposes a method to recognize time expressions from text, which has the advantage of being a simple rule-based approach. This simplicity is beneficial as an analysis tool, as time expression recognition should be a fundamental process in various applications. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based methods for time expression recognition.

However, my primary concern is the generality of the method. The rules in the method were designed based on observations of the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises the risk of overfitting to these specific corpora. Similarly, the domains of these corpora may have influenced the rule design, potentially limiting the method's applicability to other domains. Unfortunately, the paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion on the overlap of time expressions in the observed corpora. If the authors had shown that time expressions in these corpora exhibit significant overlap, this would have provided evidence to support the generality of the rules. Ideally, conducting experiments on a new, distinct corpus that was not involved in the rule design process would have strengthened the claim that the proposed method is widely effective.
