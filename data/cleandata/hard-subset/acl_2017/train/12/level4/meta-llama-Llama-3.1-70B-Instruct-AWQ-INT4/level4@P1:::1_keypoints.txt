- Paper presents a rule-based time-expression extractor that finds a time token (via dictionary, regex, POS), expands around it with heuristics, and merges segments into final expressions.  
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvements over prior rule-based and ML systems.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: rules are empirically motivated and justified by evaluation.  
- Weakness: important rule details (time token identification, segment expansion, merging) are underspecified, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed error analysis to identify failure modes and improvement areas.  
- Contribution: analysis of time expressions across four datasets, finding they are typically short and contain at least one time token.  
- Contribution: a motivated rule-based extraction approach derived from empirical observations.  
- Contribution: empirical evaluation demonstrating substantial performance gains on three datasets.  
- Question: please explain how the 15 time-token types in Section 4.1 were chosen and the criteria used.  
- Question: provide full dataset details (size, composition, preprocessing) for the three evaluation sets in Section 5.1.  
- Question: report error types and analysis for the high-recall results in Section 5.2 and plans to improve performance.  
- Question: will the full rule set and software be released to enable reproduction and further research?
