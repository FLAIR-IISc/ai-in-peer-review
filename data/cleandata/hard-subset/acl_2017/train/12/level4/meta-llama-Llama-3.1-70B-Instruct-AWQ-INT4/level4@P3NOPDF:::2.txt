The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions from text, leveraging a simple rule-based approach. This simplicity is a significant advantage, as time expression recognition should be a fundamental process in various applications. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based methods for time expression recognition.

However, my primary concern lies in the generality of the method. The rules were designed based on observations of the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises concerns about overfitting. It is possible that the rules are too closely tailored to these specific corpora, limiting their applicability to other domains. Furthermore, the domains of the corpora used for rule design may have had a significant impact on the resulting rules. To alleviate these concerns, it would be beneficial to provide statistical analysis or discussion on the overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora. If the time expressions in these corpora were found to be largely overlapping, this would lend support to the generality of the rules.

It would have been more convincing if the experiments had been conducted using a new corpus that was distinct from the rule design process, thereby demonstrating the method's effectiveness across different datasets. Nevertheless, the results presented are promising, and with further evaluation on diverse corpora, the proposed method's robustness and generalizability could be more comprehensively established.
