- Proposes a rule-based time expression extraction method based on two insights: expressions are short and contain at least one time token.  
- Method: detect time tokens via dictionary/regex/POS, expand segments with heuristic rules, then merge segments into final expressions.  
- Evaluation: reports significant improvements over both rule-based and ML systems on three datasets.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: rules are empirically motivated and justified by evaluation results.  
- Weakness: paper has underspecifications that hinder reproducibility.  
- Issue (4.1): unclear what "5 seasons" refers to and how non-traditional seasons (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) are handled.  
- Issue (5.1): inconsistency—text mentions “two benchmark datasets” while evaluation uses three.  
- Issue (5.2): missing example of a time expression without a time token to illustrate method limitations.  
- Question (5.2): system is near ceiling (93% expressions contain time tokens; 92% recall)—how do authors plan to further improve performance?  
- Request: clarify whether the full rule set or software will be released to enable replication and extension.
