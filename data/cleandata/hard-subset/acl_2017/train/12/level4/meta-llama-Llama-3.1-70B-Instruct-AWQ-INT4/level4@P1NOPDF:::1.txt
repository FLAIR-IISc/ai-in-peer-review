This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction, leveraging two key insights: time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The approach first identifies time tokens through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. It then expands the time segment from either direction of the time token, guided by a set of heuristic rules, until it reaches a stopping point. Finally, it merges the time segments into a single time expression based on another set of rules. Evaluation of this approach against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems on three datasets shows significant improvements.

Strengths:
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear to be well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

Weaknesses:
There are some underspecifications in the paper that make it difficult to reproduce the results. See below for details.

General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: The paper mentions "5 seasons," but it is unclear what these seasons are. How do the authors handle cases such as Ramadan or Holiday Season, which may not fit into the traditional notion of seasons?
* Section 5.1: The text refers to "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation is actually performed on three datasets. It would be helpful to correct this inconsistency.
* Section 5.2: Providing an example without a time token would be helpful in illustrating the approach's capabilities.
* Section 5.2: Given that this approach is close to the ceiling of performance, with 93% of expressions containing time tokens, and the system has achieved 92% recall, it would be interesting to know how the authors plan to further improve the system's performance.
* Are there any plans to release the full set of rules or software used in the approach, to facilitate replication and extension of the work?
