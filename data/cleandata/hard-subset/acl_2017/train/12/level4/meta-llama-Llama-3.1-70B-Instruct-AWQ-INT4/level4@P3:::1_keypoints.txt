- Paper proposes SynTime, a type-based method that defines three syntactic token types and simple boundary-expansion heuristics to recognize time expressions, showing strong gains over baselines.  
- Contribution: empirical analysis of time expressions across four datasets, noting short length, limited time keywords, and similar syntactic behavior (least-effort principle).  
- Contribution: SynTime—defines token types (time token, modifier, numeral) and uses simple heuristic rules for tag/ boundary expansion.  
- Contribution: Evaluation on three datasets shows SynTime outperforms both rule-based and ML time taggers.  
- Strength: Well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: Rules are empirically motivated and justified by evaluation results.  
- Strength: Simple, efficient method that runs in real time.  
- Strength: Demonstrates significant performance improvements over state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Weakness: Key definitions (syntactic types, heuristics) are underspecified, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Paper needs more examples and illustrations to clarify the approach and its application.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of limitations and future improvement avenues.  
- Question: Provide detailed definitions of syntactic types and the heuristic rules used in SynTime.  
- Question: How do you plan to further improve SynTime’s performance given its high recall?  
- Question: Will you release the complete rule set and software for SynTime?  
- Question: Provide examples of time expressions without time tokens and explain how SynTime handles them.  
- Question: How does SynTime perform on other datasets/domains and are there plans for broader evaluation?
