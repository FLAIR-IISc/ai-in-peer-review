The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions from text, leveraging a simple rule-based approach that is advantageous as an analysis tool, given that time expression recognition should be a fundamental process in various applications. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based methods for time expression recognition.

However, my primary concern lies in the generality of the method. The rules were designed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises the possibility that the rules may overfit to these specific corpora. Moreover, the domains of these corpora may have influenced the rule design, potentially limiting the method's applicability. Unfortunately, the paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion on the overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora. If the overlap were significant, it would have provided evidence supporting the generality of the rules. Ideally, conducting experiments on a new, distinct corpus that was not involved in the rule design process would have better demonstrated the proposed method's broader effectiveness.
