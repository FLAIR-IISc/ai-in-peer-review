This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction, leveraging the key insights that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The approach first identifies the time token through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. It then expands the time segment from either direction of the time token until it reaches a boundary based on a set of heuristic rules. Finally, it merges the time segments into a single time expression based on another set of rules. Evaluation of this approach against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems on three datasets shows significant improvements.

Strengths:
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear to be well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

Weaknesses:
There are some underspecifications in the paper that make it difficult to reproduce the results. See below for details.

General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: The paper mentions five seasons, but it is unclear what these seasons are. How do the authors handle cases such as Ramadan month or Holiday Season, which may not fit into the traditional definition of seasons?
* Section 5.1: The text refers to "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation is actually performed on three datasets. It would be helpful to correct this inconsistency.
* Section 5.2: Providing an example without a time token would be helpful in illustrating the approach's capabilities.
* Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system has achieved 92% recall, the authors are close to the ceiling of performance. How do they plan to further improve the system's performance?
* Are there any plans to release the full set of rules or software used in the approach to facilitate reproducibility and further research in this area?
