- Presents a rule-based time-expression extraction method that detects time tokens, expands segments with heuristics, and merges segments into expressions.  
- Uses dictionary lookup, regex matching, and POS tagging to identify time tokens.  
- Expansion and boundary determination rely on heuristic rules applied bidirectionally from the time token.  
- Merges segments into single expressions using a second rule set.  
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvements over prior rule-based and ML systems.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented with empirically motivated, justified rules.  
- Underspecified implementation and evaluation details hinder reproducibility.  
- Section 4.1 ambiguously references “five seasons” and omits handling of other time concepts (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season).  
- Section 5.1 inconsistently states “two benchmark datasets” then mentions three; clarification needed.  
- Section 5.2 should include an example lacking a time token to illustrate limits/capabilities.  
- With 93% of expressions containing time tokens and system recall at 92%, discuss avenues for further improvement given near-ceiling performance.  
- Recommend releasing the full rule set and software to improve reproducibility and enable further research.
