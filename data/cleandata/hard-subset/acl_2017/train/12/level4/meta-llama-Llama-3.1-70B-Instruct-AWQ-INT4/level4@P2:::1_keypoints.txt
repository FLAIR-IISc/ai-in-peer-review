- Proposes SynTime, a type-based method that recognizes time expressions using syntactic types and simple heuristic rules informed by dataset analysis.
- Analyzes four datasets and finds most time expressions are short, use a small set of keywords, and share similar syntactic behavior.
- Defines three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) to guide recognition and rule design.
- Evaluates SynTime against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets, showing significant improvements.
- Contribution: empirical evidence supporting a “least effort” principle in time expression usage.
- Contribution: design of a simple, interpretable type-based tagger (SynTime) with heuristic rules.
- Contribution: empirical demonstration of SynTime’s effectiveness across multiple datasets.
- Strength: paper is well-written with rules clearly motivated by empirical observations.
- Strength: evaluation shows strong performance gains over baselines.
- Weakness: underspecified details (exact definitions of the 15 time token types and 5 modifier types) hinder reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited discussion of approach limitations and avenues for future improvement.
- Question: request for detailed definitions of the 15 time token types and 5 modifier types.
- Question: how will the authors further improve the approach given high coverage (93% expressions contain time tokens)?
- Question: do the authors plan to release the full rule set and software for SynTime?
- Comment: clarify definitions of the 15 time token types (e.g., seasons, Ramadan, Holiday Season) in Section 4.1.
- Comment: correct wording in Section 5.1 to “three datasets” instead of “two benchmark datasets.”
- Comment: include an example without a time token in Section 5.2 to illustrate robustness.
- Comment: add more discussion on implications, limitations, and potential applications of SynTime.
