- Paper proposes a rule-based time expression extractor that identifies a central time token (via dictionary lookup, regex, POS), expands boundaries with heuristics, and merges segments, showing strong improvements on three datasets.
- Evaluation compares favorably to both rule-based and machine-learning baselines, demonstrating significant performance gains.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented, making the method easy to follow.
- Proposed rules are grounded in empirical observations and are justified by the evaluation results.
- Key methodological details are underspecified, making reproduction of results challenging.
- Section 4.1: reference to “five seasons” is unclear and the handling of other time-related concepts (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) needs clarification.
- Section 5.1: inconsistency—text refers to “two benchmark datasets” but evaluation uses three.
- Section 5.2: recommend adding an example lacking a time token to illustrate method behavior in such cases.
- Given the near-ceiling scenario (93% expressions contain time tokens; system achieves 92% recall), authors should discuss plans to further improve performance.
- Request for release of the full rule set or software to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
