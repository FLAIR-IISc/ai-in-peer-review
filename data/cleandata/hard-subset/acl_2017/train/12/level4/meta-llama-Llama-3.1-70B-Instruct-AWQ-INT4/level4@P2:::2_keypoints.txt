- Proposes SynTime, a method for recognizing time expressions using a syntactic type system and heuristic rules.  
- Observes four properties of time expressions: short length, small vocabulary, at least one time token, and similar syntactic behavior.  
- Designs simple, lightweight heuristic rules based on those observations for real-time applicability.  
- Evaluates SynTime against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets, showing improved performance.  
- Contribution: thorough analysis of time expressions across four datasets supporting a "least effort" principle.  
- Contribution: formulation of a syntactic type system and rule-based SynTime approach for time expression recognition.  
- Contribution: multi-dataset evaluation demonstrating robustness.  
- Strength: Outperforms baselines, indicating effectiveness.  
- Strength: Simple and lightweight implementation suitable for real-time use.  
- Strength: Easily extensible by adding rules and types for new domains.  
- Weakness: Rules may be overfitted to the evaluation corpora, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: Domain dependence of corpora could have biased rule design and applicability.  
- Weakness: Lacks statistical analysis of overlap in time expressions across corpora to support rule generality.  
- Questions: How will you mitigate potential overfitting of rules to the evaluated corpora?  
- Questions: Can you provide statistical analysis of time-expression overlaps across datasets to support generality?  
- Questions: How will SynTime be adapted and validated for other domains and datasets?
