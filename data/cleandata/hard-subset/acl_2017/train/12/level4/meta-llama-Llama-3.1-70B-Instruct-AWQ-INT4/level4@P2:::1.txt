**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a type-based approach, SynTime, for recognizing time expressions in free text. The authors analyze time expressions from four datasets and observe that most time expressions are short, contain a small group of keywords, and demonstrate similar syntactic behaviors. Based on these observations, SynTime defines three main syntactic types (time token, modifier, and numeral) and designs simple heuristic rules to recognize time expressions. The approach is evaluated against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets, showing significant improvements.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors analyze time expressions from four datasets and make four observations that provide evidence for the principle of least effort in time expression usage.
2. They propose a type-based time expression tagger, SynTime, that defines syntactic types and designs simple heuristic rules for time expression recognition.
3. They evaluate SynTime against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.

**Strengths**

1. The paper is well-written and clearly presented, with rules motivated by empirical observations of the data.
2. The evaluation shows significant improvements over state-of-the-art baselines.

**Weaknesses**

1. There are some underspecifications in the paper that make it difficult to reproduce the results, such as the exact definition of the 15 time token types and the 5 modifier types.
2. The paper could benefit from more discussion on the limitations of the approach and potential future improvements.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the definition of the 15 time token types and the 5 modifier types?
2. How do you plan to improve the approach further, given that it is already close to the ceiling of performance since 93% expressions contain time tokens?
3. Are there any plans to release the full set of rules and software used in SynTime?

**Additional Comments**

* In Section 4.1, it would be helpful to clarify the definition of the 15 time token types, including the 5 seasons and other special cases such as Ramadan month or Holiday Season.
* In Section 5.1, it would be more accurate to say "three datasets" instead of "two benchmark datasets".
* In Section 5.2, an example without a time token would be helpful to illustrate the approach.
* The paper could benefit from more discussion on the implications of the results and potential applications of SynTime.
