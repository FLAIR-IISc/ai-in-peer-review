- Proposes a simple rule-based method for recognizing time expressions from text.  
- Simplicity is an advantage because time expression recognition is a fundamental task for many applications.  
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms both state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning approaches.  
- Major concern: rules were designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, risking overfitting.  
- Domain-specific corpora may have biased rule design, limiting applicability to other domains.  
- No statistical analysis or discussion of overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora.  
- Suggestion: test on a new, independent corpus to better demonstrate generality and robustness.
