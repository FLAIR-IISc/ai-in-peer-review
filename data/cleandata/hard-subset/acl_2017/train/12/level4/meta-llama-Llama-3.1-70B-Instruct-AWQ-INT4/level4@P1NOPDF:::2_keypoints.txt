- Proposes a simple rule-based method for recognizing time expressions from text.
- Simplicity is a major advantage since time expression recognition is a fundamental task for many applications.
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning approaches.
- Main concern: rules were designed using the same corpora as evaluation, raising overfitting risk.
- The specific domains of the evaluation corpora may have biased rule design and limit generality.
- Paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion of overlap of time expressions across the corpora.
- Demonstrating significant overlap would support the claim of rule generality.
- Running experiments on a new corpus distinct from the rule-design data is needed to prove broad effectiveness.
