- Paper proposes a rule-based time-expression extractor that assumes expressions are concise and contain at least one time token.
- Time tokens are identified via dictionary lookup, regular expressions, and POS-tag information.
- Time segments are expanded from the identified token using heuristic rules and merged into full expressions with additional rules.
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvement over prior rule-based and ML systems.
- Strength: well-written, clearly presented, with empirically motivated and justified rules supported by evaluation.
- Weakness: underspecified details hinder reproducibility of results.
- Clarify how the "5 seasons" definition handles other seasonal periods (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season).
- Fix wording in Section 5.1 from "two benchmark datasets" to "three datasets."
- Add an example of a case without a time token to improve understanding.
- Discuss avenues for improvement given near-ceiling performance (93% expressions have time tokens; system recall 92%).
- State whether the full rule set or software will be released to enable replication and further research.
