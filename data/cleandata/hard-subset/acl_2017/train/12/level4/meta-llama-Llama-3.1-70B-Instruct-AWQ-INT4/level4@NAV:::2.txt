The paper presents a novel approach for identifying time expressions in text, leveraging a straightforward rule-based methodology that offers a significant advantage as an analytical tool, given that time expression recognition is a fundamental process in various applications. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method surpasses the performance of existing state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based approaches for time expression recognition.

However, a concern arises regarding the method's generality. The rules underlying the method were developed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising the possibility that these rules may be overfitted to these specific corpora. Furthermore, the domains of these corpora may have influenced the design of the rules. Notably, the paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion on the overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora. If the overlap were substantial, it would have provided evidence supporting the rules' generality. Nevertheless, conducting experiments on a distinct corpus, separate from the rule design process, would have strengthened the claim that the proposed method is widely effective.
