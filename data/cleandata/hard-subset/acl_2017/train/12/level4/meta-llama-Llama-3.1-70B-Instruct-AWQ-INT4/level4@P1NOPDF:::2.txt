The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions from text, which is a simple rule-based approach. This simplicity is a significant advantage, as time expression recognition should be a fundamental process in various applications. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based methods for time expression recognition.

However, my primary concern is the generality of the method. The rules in the method were designed based on observations of the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises the risk of overfitting to these specific corpora. Moreover, the domains of these corpora may have influenced the rule design, potentially limiting its applicability. Unfortunately, the paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion on the overlap of time expressions in the observed corpora. If the authors had shown that time expressions in these corpora exhibit significant overlap, it would have strengthened the case for the generality of the rules.

Conducting experiments using a new corpus, distinct from the one used in the rule design process, would have been a more effective way to demonstrate the proposed method's broad effectiveness.
