This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction, leveraging the insights that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The approach first identifies time tokens through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and part-of-speech (POS) tagging information. It then expands the time segment in both directions from the time token, using a set of heuristic rules to determine the boundaries. Finally, the approach merges the time segments into a single time expression based on another set of rules. The evaluation of this approach, compared to both rule-based and machine learning-based systems, on three datasets demonstrates significant improvements.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules proposed in the paper are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear to be well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**

There are some underspecifications in the paper that make it challenging to reproduce the results. Specifically, some details regarding the implementation and evaluation could be further clarified.

**General Discussion:**

* In Section 4.1, the authors mention five seasons, but it is unclear what these seasons are. Additionally, the paper does not discuss how the approach handles other time-related concepts, such as Ramadan month or Holiday Season.
* In Section 5.1, the authors refer to "two benchmark datasets," but later mention three datasets. It would be helpful to clarify this inconsistency.
* In Section 5.2, providing an example without a time token would be beneficial for illustrating the approach's capabilities.
* Given that the approach is close to the ceiling of performance, with 93% of expressions containing time tokens, and the system has achieved 92% recall, it would be interesting to discuss potential avenues for further improvement.
* The authors may consider releasing the full set of rules and software used in the approach to facilitate reproducibility and further research in this area.

Overall, the paper presents a well-motivated and effective approach to time expression extraction. Addressing the mentioned underspecifications and providing additional details would strengthen the paper and enhance its contribution to the field.
