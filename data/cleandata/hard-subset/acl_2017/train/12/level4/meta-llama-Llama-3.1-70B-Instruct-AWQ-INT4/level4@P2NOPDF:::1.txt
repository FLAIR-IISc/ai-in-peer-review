This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction, leveraging the insights that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The proposed method first identifies the time token through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. It then expands the time segment in both directions from the time token, relying on a set of heuristic rules to determine the boundaries. Finally, it merges the time segments into a single time expression based on another set of rules. The evaluation of this approach, comparing it to both rule-based and machine learning-based systems on three datasets, demonstrates significant improvements.

Strengths:

The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it easy to follow. The rules proposed are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear to be well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

Weaknesses:

There are some underspecifications in the paper that make it challenging to reproduce the results. These will be discussed in more detail below.

General Discussion:

* In Section 4.1, the authors mention five seasons, but do not provide a clear explanation. It would be helpful to clarify how the approach handles other time-related concepts, such as Ramadan month or Holiday Season.
* In Section 5.1, the text refers to "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation is actually performed on three datasets. It would be beneficial to correct this inconsistency.
* In Section 5.2, providing an example without a time token would help to illustrate the approach's capabilities in such cases.
* Given that the approach is close to the ceiling of performance, with 93% of expressions containing time tokens, and the system achieving 92% recall, it would be interesting to know how the authors plan to further improve the system's performance.
* Are there any plans to release the full set of rules or software used in the approach, to facilitate reproducibility and further research in this area?
