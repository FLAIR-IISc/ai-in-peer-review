The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions from text, utilizing a simple rule-based approach. This simplicity is a significant advantage, as time expression recognition should be a fundamental process in various applications. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based methods for time expression recognition.

However, my primary concern lies in the generality of the method. The rules were designed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising concerns about overfitting to these specific datasets. Furthermore, the domains of these corpora may have influenced the rule design, potentially limiting the method's applicability to other domains. The authors do not provide any statistical analysis or discussion on the overlap of time expressions in the observed corpora. If the time expressions in these corpora were found to be largely overlapping, this would have supported the generality of the rules.

Conducting experiments on a new corpus, distinct from the one used in the rule design process, would have strengthened the claim that the proposed method is widely effective. This would have provided a more robust evaluation of the method's performance and generalizability.
