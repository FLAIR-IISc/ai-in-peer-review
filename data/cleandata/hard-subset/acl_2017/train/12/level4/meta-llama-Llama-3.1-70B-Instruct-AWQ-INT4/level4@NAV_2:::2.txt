This paper proposes a method to recognize time expressions from text. It is a simple rule-based method, which is a strong advantage as an analysis tool since time expression recognition should be a basic process in applications. Experiments results show that the proposed method outperforms the state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning based method for time expression recognition.

The paper's main contributions are:
1. The authors analyze the time expressions from four datasets and make four observations, which provide evidence for the principle of least effort in terms of time expression. 
2. The authors propose a type-based time expression tagger, SynTime, that defines syntactic types and designs simple heuristic rules for time expression recognition.
3. The authors conduct experiments on three datasets and compare SynTime with three state-of-the-art baselines.

The strengths of this paper are:
1. The proposed method is simple and rule-based, making it a strong analysis tool.
2. The method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning based method for time expression recognition.
3. The experiments are conducted on three datasets, showing the method's effectiveness.

The weaknesses of this paper are:
1. The generality of the method is a concern. The rules in the method were designed based on observations of corpora that are used for evaluation as well, which may lead to over-fitting to these corpora.
2. The domains of these corpora may have affected the rule design, and there is no statistic nor discussion to show overlaps in time expressions in the observed corpora.
3. The experiments could have been more convincing if they were conducted using a new corpus that was distinct from the rule design process.

Questions to Authors:
1. How do you plan to address the concern of generality of the method?
2. Can you provide more discussion on the overlaps in time expressions in the observed corpora?
3. Are there any plans to conduct experiments using a new corpus that is distinct from the rule design process?
