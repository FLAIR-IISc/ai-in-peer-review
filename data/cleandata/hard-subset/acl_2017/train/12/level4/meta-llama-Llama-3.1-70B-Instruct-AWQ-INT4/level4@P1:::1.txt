This paper describes a rule-based approach to time expression extraction. Its key insights are that time expressions typically are short and contain at least one time token. It first recognizes the time token through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression match with POS tagging information. It then expands the time segment from either direction of the time token until it reaches a boundary based on a set of heuristic rules. Finally, it merges the time segments into a single time expression based on another set of rules. Evaluation of this approach with both rule-based and machine learning-based systems on three datasets shows significant improvements.

Strengths:
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and seem to be well-justified as evidenced by the evaluation.

Weaknesses:
There are some underspecifications in the paper that make it difficult to reproduce the results. For instance, the paper does not provide enough details about the specific rules used for time token identification, time segment expansion, and time expression extraction. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the errors made by the system, which would help to identify potential areas for improvement.

Contributions:
The main contributions of this paper are:
1. An analysis of time expressions from four datasets, which reveals that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token.
2. A rule-based approach to time expression extraction, which is motivated by empirical observations of the data.
3. An evaluation of the approach on three datasets, which shows significant improvements over both rule-based and machine learning-based systems.

Questions to Authors:
* In Section 4.1, the paper defines 15 types for time tokens, but it does not provide enough details about how these types were chosen. Can you provide more information about the criteria used to define these types?
* In Section 5.1, the paper mentions that the system was evaluated on three datasets, but it does not provide enough details about the datasets. Can you provide more information about the datasets, such as their size, composition, and any preprocessing that was performed?
* In Section 5.2, the paper mentions that the system achieved high recall on the three datasets, but it does not provide enough details about the errors made by the system. Can you provide more information about the types of errors that were made, and how you plan to improve the system's performance in the future?
* Are there any plans to release the full set of rules and software used in the system, to facilitate reproduction of the results and further research in this area?
