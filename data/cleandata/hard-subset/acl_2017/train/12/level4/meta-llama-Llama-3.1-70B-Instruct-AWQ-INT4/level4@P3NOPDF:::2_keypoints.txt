- Proposes a simple rule-based method for recognizing time expressions from text.  
- Simplicity is a significant advantage since time expression recognition is a fundamental task.  
- Experimental results show the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning approaches.  
- Major concern: rules were designed using the same corpora as evaluation, raising overfitting risks.  
- Rules may be overly tailored to specific corpora and thus not generalize well to other domains.  
- Corpus domain characteristics could have strongly influenced the derived rules.  
- Suggest providing statistical analysis or discussion of overlap in time expressions across corpora to support rule generality.  
- Recommend evaluating on a new, distinct corpus and diverse datasets to confirm robustness and generalizability.
