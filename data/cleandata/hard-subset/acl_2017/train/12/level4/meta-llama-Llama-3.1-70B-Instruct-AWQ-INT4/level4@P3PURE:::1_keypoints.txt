- Paper proposes a rule-based time-expression extractor that assumes expressions are short and contain at least one time token.
- Time tokens are detected using dictionary lookup, regex matching, and POS tagging; segments are expanded with heuristic boundary rules and merged via additional rules.
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvements over prior rule-based and ML systems.
- Strength: well-written and clearly presented methodology.
- Strength: rules are empirically motivated and justified by evaluation results.
- Weakness: underspecified details hinder reproducibility.
- Section 4.1: unclear what the "five seasons" are and how non-standard seasons (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) are handled.
- Section 5.1: inconsistency—text refers to "two benchmark datasets" while evaluation uses three.
- Section 5.2: request for an example of an expression without a time token to illustrate capabilities.
- Section 5.2: with 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, authors are near a performance ceiling—how will they improve further?
- Request for release of full rules or software to enable reproducibility and further research.
