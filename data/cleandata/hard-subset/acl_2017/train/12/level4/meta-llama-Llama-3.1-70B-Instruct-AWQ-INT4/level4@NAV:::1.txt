This paper proposes a rule-based method for extracting time expressions, primarily based on two key observations: time expressions are generally concise and contain at least one time token. The approach involves identifying time tokens through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. Subsequently, the time segment is expanded from the time token in both directions, adhering to a set of heuristic rules. Finally, the time segments are merged into a single time expression based on an additional set of rules. The evaluation of this method against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems across three datasets demonstrates significant improvements.

- Strengths:
The paper is well-written and clearly presented, with rules that are empirically motivated and well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

- Weaknesses:
However, there are some underspecifications in the paper that hinder the reproducibility of the results, which are detailed below.

- General Discussion:
* In Section 4.1, the concept of '5 seasons' is introduced. It would be beneficial to clarify how this definition accommodates other seasonal periods, such as Ramadan or the Holiday Season.
* In Section 5.1, the text refers to 'two benchmark datasets,' which should be corrected to 'three datasets' for consistency.
* An example in Section 5.2 illustrating a scenario without a time token would enhance understanding.
* Given that the approach is near the ceiling of performance, with 93% of expressions containing time tokens and the system achieving 92% recall, it would be valuable to discuss potential avenues for further improvement.
* Are there plans to release the complete set of rules or software used in the study to facilitate further research and replication?
