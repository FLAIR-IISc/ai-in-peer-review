This paper proposes a rule-based approach for extracting time expressions, primarily based on the insights that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The approach involves recognizing time tokens through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. It then expands the time segment in both directions from the time token, based on a set of heuristic rules, until a stopping criterion is met. Finally, it merges the time segments into a single time expression using another set of rules. The evaluation of this approach against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems on three datasets demonstrates significant improvements.

Strengths:
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear to be well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

Weaknesses:
However, there are some underspecifications in the paper that make it difficult to reproduce the results, as detailed below.

General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: The paper mentions "5 seasons," but it is unclear what these seasons are. The authors may want to consider including more specific examples, such as Ramadan month or Holiday Season, to provide a clearer understanding of their approach.
* Section 5.1: The text refers to "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation is actually performed on three datasets. It would be helpful to correct this inconsistency.
* Section 5.2: Providing an example without a time token would be beneficial in illustrating the approach's capabilities.
* Section 5.2: Given that the approach is close to the ceiling of performance, with 93% of expressions containing time tokens and the system achieving 92% recall, it would be interesting to know how the authors plan to further improve the system's performance.
* Are there any plans to release the full set of rules or software used in the approach, to facilitate reproducibility and further development?
