- Presents SynTime, a rule-based time expression extractor that uses dictionary lookup, regex, and POS tags, expanding from identified time tokens with heuristic rules and merging segments.
- Empirical insight: time expressions are typically short, use a small vocabulary, and tokens show similar syntactic behavior.
- Proposes a type-based tagger defining syntactic token types and simple boundary-expansion heuristic rules.
- Reports experimental results where SynTime outperforms both rule-based and machine-learning baselines.
- Strength: well-written, with rules clearly motivated by empirical data and strong evaluation evidence.
- Strength: provides a thorough analysis of time expressions across four datasets.
- Weakness: underspecified components hinder reproducibility, including missing lists of token/modifier/numeral types.
- Weakness: heuristic rules for segment expansion and merging are not fully specified, making replication difficult.
- Question: request for detailed explanation of the 15 time token types, 5 modifier types, and 1 numeral type.
- Question: asks how authors plan to further improve SynTime given a high recall (92%).
- Question: asks whether the authors will release the full rule set and software to enable reproducibility.
- Question: requests an example of a time expression without a time token to illustrate SynTime’s handling.
- Question: asks how the authors will address underspecification, particularly regarding the heuristic rules.
