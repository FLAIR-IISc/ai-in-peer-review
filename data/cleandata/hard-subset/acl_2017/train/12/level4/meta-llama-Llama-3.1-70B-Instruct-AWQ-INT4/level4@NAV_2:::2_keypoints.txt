- Proposes SynTime, a simple rule-based type-based tagger for time expression recognition that outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and ML methods.
- Analyzes time expressions from four datasets and claims observations supporting a "principle of least effort" for time expressions.
- Defines syntactic types and designs simple heuristic rules for time expression recognition.
- Conducts experiments on three datasets and compares SynTime with three state-of-the-art baselines.

- Strength: Simple, rule-based method is a strong analysis tool.
- Strength: Empirical results show SynTime outperforms existing rule-based and ML methods.
- Strength: Evaluation performed across three datasets demonstrating effectiveness.

- Weakness: Generality is questionable because rules were designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, risking overfitting.
- Weakness: Potential domain bias in corpora may have influenced rule design; no statistics or discussion on corpus overlap in time expressions.
- Weakness: Lacks experiments on a new, independent corpus to more convincingly demonstrate generality.

- Question: How will the authors address the method’s generality concerns?
- Question: Can the authors provide more discussion or statistics on overlaps in time expressions across the observed corpora?
- Question: Are there plans to conduct experiments on a new corpus distinct from the rule design process?
