- Proposes a simple rule-based method for recognizing time expressions from text.  
- Simplicity is valuable as an analysis tool and because time expression recognition is a fundamental task for many applications.  
- Experiments claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning approaches.  
- Major concern: rules were designed using the same corpora used for evaluation, risking overfitting.  
- The corpora’s domain may have biased rule design, potentially limiting cross-domain applicability.  
- The paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion of overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora.  
- Demonstrating significant overlap in time expressions would help justify the rules’ generality.  
- Stronger validation would come from experiments on a new, distinct corpus not used in rule design.
