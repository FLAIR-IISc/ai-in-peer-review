The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions in text. A key strength of this approach is its simplicity – it’s a rule-based method, which is advantageous as an analytical tool, given that time expression recognition represents a fundamental process in many applications. 

The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both state-of-the-art rule-based methods and a machine learning-based method for time expression recognition. This is a significant achievement.

However, my primary concern lies with the generality of the method. The rules were designed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation. This raises the possibility that the rules are overfit to these specific datasets. Furthermore, the domains represented in these corpora may have influenced the rule design process. The paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion regarding the overlap of time expressions *across* the observed corpora. Demonstrating a high degree of overlap would have strengthened the argument for the rules’ generality. 

Ideally, the method would have been evaluated on a novel corpus, independent of the rule design process, to provide stronger evidence of its broad effectiveness.
