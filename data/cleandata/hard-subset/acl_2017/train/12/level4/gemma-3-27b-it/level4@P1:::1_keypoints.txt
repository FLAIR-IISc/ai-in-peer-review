- Paper proposes a rule-based time expression extractor (SynTime) that finds time tokens, expands segments via heuristics, and merges segments into expressions.
- Key insight: time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token, motivating a lightweight rule-based method.
- Time tokens are identified via dictionary lookup, regex matching, and POS tagging before rule-based expansion and merging.
- Evaluation on multiple datasets (TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, Tweets) shows significant improvements over prior rule-based and ML systems.
- SynTime achieves particularly high recall on Tweets and overall strong real-time, lightweight performance.
- Paper is well-written, with empirically motivated rules and insightful cross-dataset analysis supporting the method.
- Lack of sufficient detail in Section 4.2 about the heuristic rules hinders reproducibility; concrete rule examples are needed.
- Expansion process and keyword selection from training data (Section 4.3) are under-specified and require clearer explanation.
- Implementation details, including POS tagger choice and preprocessing steps, are not fully described.
- Clarify what the five "SEASON" tokens are and how culturally specific events (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) are handled.
- Resolve the discrepancy where Section 5.1 mentions two benchmark datasets but discusses three.
- Provide an example of a time expression without a time token to illustrate system handling of such cases.
- Explain strategies for addressing the ~7% of time expressions lacking time tokens and further improving performance.
- Indicate whether the full rule set and software will be released to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
- Additional positive note: the dataset analysis (length and limited vocabulary of time expressions) is compelling and ties well to theoretical principles like Zipf’s least effort.
