This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction. The core idea is that time expressions are typically short and include at least one identifiable time token. The method first identifies time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging. It then expands these initial segments in both directions, guided by a set of heuristic rules, to encompass the complete time expression. Finally, it merges overlapping segments based on a further set of rules. Evaluation against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems across three datasets demonstrates significant performance improvements.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are empirically motivated and appear well-justified, as supported by the evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**

The paper contains some underspecification which hinders reproducibility of the reported results. Specific concerns are detailed below.

**General Discussion:**

* Section 4.1: The description of seasons is unclear – what constitutes the “five seasons”? How are culturally specific time periods, such as Ramadan or the Holiday Season, handled?
* Section 5.1: The text refers to “two benchmark datasets,” but the evaluation actually uses three datasets.
* Section 5.2: Providing an example of a time expression *without* a readily identifiable time token would be beneficial for understanding the system’s behavior.
* Section 5.2: Given that this approach appears to be nearing the limit of possible performance – as 93% of expressions contain a time token, and the system already achieves 92% recall – what strategies are planned for further improvement?
* Is there any intention to release the complete rule set and/or software used in this work?
