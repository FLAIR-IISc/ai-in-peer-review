- Presents a rule-based method that detects time tokens (via dictionary, regex, POS), expands outward with heuristics, and merges segments to extract time expressions.  
- Evaluated against rule-based and ML systems on three datasets, showing significant improvements.  
- Paper is very clearly written and the rules are empirically motivated and well-justified.  
- Approach is intuitive and pragmatically addresses temporal information extraction.  
- Paper is underspecified: implementation details and rule/data processing descriptions are insufficient for reproducibility.  
- Section 4.1: unclear reference to “five seasons” and needs explanation; also unclear handling of culturally-specific expressions (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season).  
- Section 5.1/5.2 discrepancy: text mentions two benchmark datasets but evaluation uses three—needs correction.  
- Request example and analysis of time expressions without an identifiable time token to show system behavior on harder cases.  
- Given near-ceiling recall (92% vs 93% token-containing cases), authors should discuss limitations and avenues for improvement beyond adding/refining rules.  
- Strong overall contribution; recommend acceptance if authors address reproducibility, clarify the noted points, and (preferably) release full rules and code.
