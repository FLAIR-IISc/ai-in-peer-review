- Paper proposes a rule-based time expression extractor that finds time tokens (dictionary, regex, POS), expands segments with heuristics, and merges overlaps; shows significant gains vs baselines on three datasets.  
- Strength: well-written paper with empirically motivated, justified rules and strong evaluation support.  
- Weakness: underspecified details impede reproducibility of results.  
- Clarify “five seasons”: what constitutes them and how culturally specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) are handled.  
- Fix dataset count inconsistency: Section 5.1 refers to “two benchmark datasets” despite using three.  
- Provide an example of a time expression lacking an identifiable time token to illustrate system behavior on such cases.  
- Given 93% of expressions contain time tokens and system recall is 92%, state planned strategies for further improvement.  
- Indicate whether the complete rule set and/or software will be released.
