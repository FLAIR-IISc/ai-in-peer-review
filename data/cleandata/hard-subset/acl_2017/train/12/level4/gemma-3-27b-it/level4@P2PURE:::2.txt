This paper presents a rule-based method for time expression recognition. A key strength of this approach is its simplicity, which makes it a valuable analytical tool for applications where time expression recognition is a foundational step. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method surpasses both state-of-the-art rule-based systems and a machine learning-based approach in terms of performance on time expression recognition.

However, a primary concern is the potential lack of generality. The rules were developed with direct reference to the corpora used for evaluation, raising the possibility of overfitting. Furthermore, the characteristics of these corpora likely influenced the rule design process. The paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion regarding the overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora; demonstrating substantial overlap would have bolstered the argument for the rules’ generalizability. Ideally, evaluation on a corpus independent of the rule design process would have provided stronger evidence of the method’s broad effectiveness.
