- Proposes SynTime: a simplified rule-based system that finds time tokens, expands segments bidirectionally with heuristics, and merges segments into time expressions.  
- Main contribution: a type-based rule approach leveraging brevity and a limited set of time tokens, backed by empirical analysis across datasets.  
- Empirical contribution: analysis of time expressions over four datasets to justify design choices.  
- Performance: reports significant improvements over rule-based and ML baselines, notably on the Twitter dataset.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated design grounded in dataset observations.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results with notable gains in precision and recall.  
- Strength: Lightweight and efficient, suitable for real-time use and easy to extend by adding keywords.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns due to insufficient detail on heuristic implementations and baseline configurations.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis describing specific failure modes and problematic expression types.  
- Weakness: Potential performance ceiling from reliance on time tokens; limited discussion of strategies to overcome it.  
- Question: Request full details of the regular expressions used for each of the 15 time token types.  
- Question: Ask for explicit specification and examples of the heuristic rules for time segment identification.  
- Question: What strategies are planned to capture the ~7% of time expressions lacking identifiable time tokens?  
- Question: Will the authors release code and the complete rule set to enable reproducibility?  
- Comment: Clarify the unusual definition of “season” with five types and consider adding culturally relevant periods (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season).  
- Comment: Correct the manuscript’s dataset count — three benchmark datasets are used, not two.  
- Comment: Provide an example of a time expression without an identifiable time token to illustrate handling of complex cases.
