This paper introduces a rule-based approach for time expression recognition from text. A key strength of this method lies in its simplicity, making it a valuable analytical tool for applications where time expression recognition serves as a foundational step. 

The experimental results demonstrate superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning techniques for this task.

However, a potential limitation concerns the method's generalizability. The rules were developed with close reference to the corpora used for evaluation, raising the possibility of overfitting. Furthermore, the characteristics of these specific corpora may have influenced rule design. The paper would benefit from a statistical analysis and discussion of the overlap in time expressions across the observed corpora; demonstrating substantial overlap would strengthen the argument for the rules’ wider applicability. Ideally, evaluating the method on a corpus independent of the rule creation process would provide stronger evidence of its broad effectiveness.
