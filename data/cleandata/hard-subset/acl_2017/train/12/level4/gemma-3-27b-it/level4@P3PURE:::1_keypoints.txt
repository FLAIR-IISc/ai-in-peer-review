- Paper proposes a rule-based time expression extraction method that recognizes time tokens, expands segments around them, and merges segments into final expressions.
- Key insight: time expressions are typically short and usually contain at least one identifiable time token.
- Time-token recognition combines dictionary lookup, regular expressions, and POS-tag information.
- Segment expansion is performed bidirectionally from the time token using heuristic rules.
- Segment merging uses an additional rule set to form a single time expression.
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvements over existing rule-based and machine-learning systems.
- Strength: paper is well-written and rules are empirically motivated and justified by results.
- Weakness: some methodological details are underspecified, hindering reproducibility.
- Clarification needed on the reference to “five seasons” and handling of non-traditional time concepts (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season).
- Correction needed: text claims “two benchmark datasets” but experiments use three datasets.
- Request for an example of a time expression without a clear time token to illustrate system behavior.
- Question about plans to improve further given near-ceiling performance (93% of expressions contain a time token; system achieves ~92% recall).
- Ask whether the authors will release the full rule set and/or software to facilitate reproducibility and further research.
