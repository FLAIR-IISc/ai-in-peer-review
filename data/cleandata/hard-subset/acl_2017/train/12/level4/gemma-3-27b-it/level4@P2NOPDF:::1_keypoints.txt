- Paper proposes a rule-based time expression extraction method: identify time tokens via dictionary/regex/POS, expand with bidirectional heuristics, merge overlaps, and report strong gains on three datasets versus rule-based and ML baselines.

- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology.

- Strength: Rules are empirically motivated and appear well-justified by evaluation results.

- Weakness: Underspecified details hinder reproducibility.

- Section 4.1 concern: The paper lists five seasons—question whether the approach can handle culturally specific or less common temporal divisions (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season).

- Section 5.1 discrepancy: Text refers to “two benchmark datasets” but the experiments use three—this should be corrected.

- Section 5.2 request: Provide an example of a time expression without a clear time token to illustrate system behavior on harder cases.

- Section 5.2 question: With 93% of expressions containing a time token and 92% recall, what are the authors’ plans or avenues for further improving performance?

- Reproducibility request: Will the full rule set and/or implementation be released publicly to enable replication?
