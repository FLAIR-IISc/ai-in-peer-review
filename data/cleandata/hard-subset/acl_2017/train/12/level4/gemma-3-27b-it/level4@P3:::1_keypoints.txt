- Presents SynTime, a rule-based time expression extractor using syntactic types and heuristic boundary expansion, with strong results on three datasets.  
- Contribution: a simplified type-based rule system (time token, modifier, numeral) and heuristic expansion that outperforms more complex approaches.  
- Contribution: empirical analysis of time expressions across four datasets motivating design choices.  
- Contribution: consistently high recall, notably on the challenging Twitter dataset.  
- Strength: Strong empirical motivation—dataset observations directly inform system design.  
- Strength: Competitive performance—outperforms HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime with high F1 and recall.  
- Strength: Lightweight and efficient—real-time processing and easy to adapt to new domains.  
- Strength: Clear, well-written, and well-structured presentation.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—regular expressions, exact rules, and POS details are underspecified and code/rules are not provided.  
- Weakness: Limited error analysis—insufficient breakdown of false positives, misses, and their causes.  
- Weakness: Dependence on POS tagging—potential failure mode and unclear robustness to tagging errors.
