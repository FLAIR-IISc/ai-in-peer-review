- Paper presents a rule-based time expression extractor that detects time tokens (dictionary/regex/POS), expands around them with heuristics, and merges segments with rules.
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvements over other rule-based and ML systems.
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Strength: rules are empirically motivated and evaluation supports their effectiveness.
- Weakness: underspecified details hinder reproducibility.
- Clarify which five “seasons” are meant and how seasonal or cultural expressions like “Ramadan month” or “Holiday Season” are handled.
- Clarify the dataset count: text says “two benchmark datasets” but evaluation uses three.
- Provide an example of a time expression that does not contain a time token to illustrate system limits.
- Note that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system attains ~92% recall, suggesting a performance ceiling—ask about plans for further improvements.
- Ask whether the full rule set and/or software will be publicly released.
