This paper describes a rule-based approach to time expression extraction. Its key insight is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The approach first identifies time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. It then expands the time segment from either side of the time token, guided by a set of heuristic rules. Finally, it merges these time segments into a single time expression, again using a defined set of rules. Evaluation of this approach, alongside rule-based and machine learning-based systems, on three datasets demonstrates significant improvements.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**

There is some underspecification in the paper that makes reproducing the results difficult. Details are provided below.

**General Discussion:**

* Section 4.1: The paper mentions five seasons. Could you clarify which seasons are being referred to? Also, how does the system handle time expressions such as “Ramadan month” or “Holiday Season”?
* Section 5.1: The text refers to “two benchmark datasets,” but the evaluation details indicate the use of three datasets. Please clarify.
* Section 5.2: Providing an example of a time expression that *doesn’t* contain a time token would be helpful for understanding the system’s capabilities.
* Section 5.2: Given that the approach appears to be approaching the performance ceiling – since 93% of expressions contain a time token – and the system has already achieved 92% recall, what are the plans for future improvements?
* Is there any intention to release the full set of rules and/or the software used in this work?
