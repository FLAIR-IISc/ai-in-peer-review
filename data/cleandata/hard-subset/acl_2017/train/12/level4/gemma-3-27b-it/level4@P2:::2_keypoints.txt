- Proposes SynTime, a simple rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and heuristics, valuable as an analysis tool.
- SynTime reportedly outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine-learning methods on multiple datasets.
- Main contribution: a novel type-based rule approach that defines types by syntactic behavior instead of semantic definitions.
- Secondary contribution: cross-dataset analysis showing time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, often contain time tokens, and have predictable syntactic structure.
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance, particularly strong on the Twitter dataset.
- Major limitation: rules were developed from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising concerns about overfitting and generalizability.
- Paper lacks quantitative analysis of overlap in time expressions across the evaluated corpora, which would support claims of generality.
- No evaluation on a fully independent corpus outside the rule-design datasets to confirm widespread effectiveness.
- Domain effects are mentioned but not thoroughly explored; impact of domain-specific language on performance is unclear.
- Insufficient detail on the expansion strategy and the human effort required to adapt SynTime to new domains.
- Reviewer asks for a quantitative overlap analysis across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets.
- Reviewer asks for an estimate of the effort (e.g., annotation time) needed to adapt SynTime to a new domain.
- Reviewer asks how SynTime handles more complex or ambiguous time expressions that deviate from the observed syntactic patterns.
- Additional comment: reference list is extensive; recommend briefly comparing SynTime to other recent work beyond the experiment baselines.
