This paper presents a rule-based method for time expression recognition from text. A key strength of this approach is its simplicity and interpretability, making it a valuable analytical tool for downstream applications where robust time expression identification is a fundamental requirement. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method surpasses both state-of-the-art rule-based systems and a machine learning-based baseline in terms of time expression recognition accuracy.

While the performance gains are encouraging, a primary concern relates to the generality and potential overfitting of the rules. The rules were developed based on observations derived directly from the evaluation corpora. Consequently, it’s possible that the rules are tailored specifically to the characteristics of these datasets, rather than representing a broadly applicable solution. This concern is exacerbated by the potential influence of the corpora’s domains on the rule design process. 

The paper would be significantly strengthened by a discussion of the overlap in time expressions observed across the utilized corpora. Demonstrating a high degree of commonality would provide evidence supporting the generality of the learned rules. However, the absence of such analysis raises legitimate questions about the portability of the system to unseen data. 

Ideally, a more convincing demonstration of the method’s widespread effectiveness would involve evaluation on a completely independent corpus – one not used during the rule design phase. Such an evaluation would provide greater confidence in the method’s ability to generalize beyond the specific characteristics of the training and testing data. 

In summary, this paper presents a promising approach to time expression recognition. Its simplicity and strong performance are notable advantages. However, the potential for overfitting and the lack of evaluation on a truly independent dataset limit the claims regarding the method's generality. Addressing these points would substantially improve the paper’s contribution.
