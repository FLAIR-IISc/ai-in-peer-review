The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions in text. A key strength of this approach is its simplicity – a rule-based method offers a valuable analytical tool, as time expression recognition represents a fundamental process in many applications. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both state-of-the-art rule-based methods and a machine learning-based method for time expression recognition. 

While these results are promising, a concern remains regarding the generality of the method. The rules were designed based on observations from the corpora used for evaluation, raising the possibility of overfitting. Furthermore, the specific domains of these corpora may have influenced rule design. The paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion regarding the overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora. Demonstrating a high degree of overlap would have strengthened the argument for the rules’ generalizability. Ideally, evaluation on a corpus distinct from the one used to inform rule design would provide more convincing evidence of the method’s broad effectiveness.
