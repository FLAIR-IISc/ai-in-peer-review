This paper describes a rule-based approach to time expression extraction. Its key insights are that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The method first recognizes time tokens through a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. It then expands the time segment from either direction of the time token, guided by a set of heuristic rules. Finally, it merges these time segments into a single time expression using another set of rules. Evaluation of this approach, alongside rule-based and machine learning-based systems, on three datasets demonstrates significant improvements.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**

There are some areas of underspecification in the paper that make reproducing the results difficult. See the “General Discussion” section below for details. 

**General Discussion:**

* Section 4.1: The paper refers to "five seasons." Could the authors clarify this? What about time-related concepts such as Ramadan or the Holiday Season, which aren't traditional seasons?
* Section 5.1: The text states "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation utilizes three datasets. This should be corrected.
* Section 5.2: An example of a time expression *without* a clear time token would be helpful for illustrating the system’s behavior in such cases.
* Section 5.2: Given that this approach appears to be nearing the ceiling of performance (since 93% of expressions contain a time token) and the system has already achieved 92% recall, what are the authors’ plans for further improvement?
* Is there any plan to release the full set of rules and/or the software used in this work to facilitate reproducibility and further research?
