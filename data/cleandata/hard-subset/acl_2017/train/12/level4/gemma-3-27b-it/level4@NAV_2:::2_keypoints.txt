- Proposes a rule-based method (SynTime) for time expression recognition that is transparent and interpretable.  
- SynTime outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine-learning methods on benchmark datasets and Twitter data.  
- Primary contribution: a novel type-based rule design grounded in corpus observations (consistent patterns in length, vocabulary, syntactic behavior).  
- Resulting rule set is simplified and effective.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical evaluation demonstrating competitive performance, notably strong recall.  
- SynTime is lightweight, enabling real-time processing and easy extension.  
- Major concern: rules may be overfitted to the corpora used for both observation and evaluation, raising generalizability questions.  
- Paper lacks a quantitative analysis of overlap in time expressions across the four datasets; demonstrating overlap would strengthen the claims.  
- Evaluation should include a corpus completely independent of the rule-design process to validate performance on unseen data.  
- Reviewer requests: provide quantitative overlap analysis across datasets and report SynTime’s performance on a dataset not used during rule creation.
