This paper presents a rule-based system for time expression extraction, grounded in the observation that time expressions are generally concise and include at least one identifiable time token. The method initially identifies time tokens using a hybrid approach of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging. Subsequently, it expands these initial tokens into complete time segments using a set of heuristic rules, operating bidirectionally. Finally, these segments are consolidated into unified time expressions based on further defined rules. The evaluation demonstrates substantial performance gains when compared to both rule-based and machine learning-based systems across three datasets.

**Strengths:**

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly structured. The defined rules are demonstrably linked to empirical data analysis, and the evaluation results strongly support their effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**

Certain aspects of the methodology lack sufficient detail, hindering complete reproducibility of the reported results (see specific comments below).

**General Discussion:**

* Section 4.1: The categorization of “five seasons” warrants clarification. The treatment of culturally specific time expressions, such as Ramadan or Holiday Season, is also not addressed.
* Section 5.1: The text refers to “two benchmark datasets,” while the evaluation appears to utilize three. This discrepancy should be resolved.
* Section 5.2: Including an illustrative example of a time expression *without* a discernible time token would greatly enhance understanding.
* Section 5.2: Given that the approach appears to be nearing peak performance – recognizing that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall – what strategies are envisioned for further improvement?
*  Will the complete rule set and associated software be made publicly available?
