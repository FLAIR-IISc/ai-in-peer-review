- Proposes a rule-based time-expression extractor assuming expressions are concise and contain at least one identifiable time token; uses dictionary lookup, regex, and POS tagging to find tokens, then bidirectional heuristic expansion and consolidation.  
- Evaluation shows substantial performance gains over prior rule-based and machine-learning systems on three datasets.  
- Strength: Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly structured.  
- Strength: Rules are empirically motivated and evaluation results strongly support their effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Methodological details are insufficient for full reproducibility.  
- Section 4.1: The “five seasons” categorization needs clarification and the handling of culturally specific expressions (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) is not addressed.  
- Section 5.1: Inconsistency between referring to “two benchmark datasets” while using three in evaluation.  
- Section 5.2: Reviewer requests an illustrative example of a time expression lacking a discernible time token.  
- Section 5.2: Given near-peak performance (93% expressions contain a time token; system reaches 92% recall), the paper should discuss potential avenues for further improvement.  
- Reproducibility/availability: Reviewer asks whether the full rule set and software will be made publicly available.
