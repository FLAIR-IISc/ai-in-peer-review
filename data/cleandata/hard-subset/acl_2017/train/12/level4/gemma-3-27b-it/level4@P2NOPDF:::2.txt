This paper presents a rule-based method for time expression recognition. A key strength of this approach is its simplicity, making it a valuable analysis tool for downstream applications where accurate time expression identification is fundamental. The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance, exceeding that of existing state-of-the-art rule-based systems and even a machine learning-based method on the task of time expression recognition.

However, a primary concern is the potential lack of generalizability. The rules were developed based on observations derived *from* the same corpora used for evaluation. This raises the possibility of overfitting to the specific characteristics of these datasets. Furthermore, the domains represented within these corpora likely influenced the rule design process. The paper lacks any statistical analysis or discussion regarding the overlap of time expressions across the constituent corpora. Demonstrating substantial overlap would have provided evidence supporting the generality of the developed rules. 

Ideally, the method's robustness would have been more convincingly demonstrated through evaluation on a held-out corpus, entirely independent of the data used to inform rule creation. Such an evaluation would offer stronger evidence that the approach is broadly effective and not simply tailored to the training data. While the current results are promising, this additional experiment would significantly strengthen the paper’s claims regarding generalizability.
