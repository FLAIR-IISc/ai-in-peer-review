This paper proposes a method for identifying time expressions from text. It is a simple rule-based method, which has a strong advantage as an analysis tool because identifying time expressions should be a fundamental process in applications.

The results of the experiments show that the proposed method performs better than state-of-the-art rule-based methods and machine learning-based methods for identifying time expressions.

This is great, but my concern is the generality of the method. The rules in the method were designed based on corpora observations, which are also used for evaluation. Therefore, I fear that the rules may overfit these corpora. Similarly, the domain of these corpora may have influenced the rule design.

There are no statistics or discussions to show the overlap in time expressions in the observed corpora. If it was shown that the time expressions in these corpora are mostly overlapped, then the fact should have supported the generality of the rules.

Anyway, it would have been better if the experiments were conducted using a new corpus that was separate from the rule design process to show that the proposed method is widely effective.
