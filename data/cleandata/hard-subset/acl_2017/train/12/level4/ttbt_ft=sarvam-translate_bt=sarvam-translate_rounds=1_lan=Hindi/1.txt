This paper describes a rule-based approach for extracting time expressions. Its key points are that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. It first identifies time tokens through a combination of dictionary lookup, POS tagging information, and regular expression matching. Then, it expands the time segment from both directions of the time token until it reaches a set of heuristic rules. Finally, it merges the time segments into a single time expression based on another set of rules. Evaluation of this approach with both rule-based and ML-based systems on 3 datasets shows significant improvements.

- Strengths:
It is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are inspired by empirical observations of data and appear well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation.
- Weaknesses:
The paper has some specific limitations that make it difficult to reproduce the results. See below for details.
- General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: What are the 5 seasons? What about things like the month of Ramadan or the holiday season?
* Section 5.1: "Two Benchmark Datasets" => "Three Datasets"?
* Section 5.2: An example without time tokens would be helpful.
* Section 5.2: Given that this approach is close to the limit of performance because 93% of expressions contain time tokens, and the system achieved 92% recall, how do you plan to improve it further?
* Is there any plan to release the complete set of rules/software used?
