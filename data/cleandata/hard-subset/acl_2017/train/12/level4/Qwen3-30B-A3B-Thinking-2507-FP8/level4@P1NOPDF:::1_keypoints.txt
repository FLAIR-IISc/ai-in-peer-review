- Paper proposes a rule-based time expression extractor that finds time tokens (dictionary, regex, POS), expands segments bidirectionally with heuristics, merges overlaps, and outperforms baselines on three datasets.  
- Strength: Well-written and clearly structured presentation.  
- Strength: Rule design is empirically motivated and justified by data analysis and evaluation results.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility is limited by insufficient implementation details and missing specifics.  
- Weakness: Claim of "five seasons" in Section 4.1 needs clarification and treatment of culturally specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, holiday seasons).  
- Weakness: Section 5.1 incorrectly refers to "two benchmark datasets" instead of three as shown in Table 1.  
- Weakness: Section 5.2 should include a concrete example of a time expression lacking a clear time token.  
- Weakness: Given 93% of expressions contain a time token and system recall is 92%, authors must explain how they will address the remaining ~8% gap and near-ceiling performance.  
- Weakness: Authors should clarify whether they will release the full rule set and implementation code.  
- Overall: These issues hinder reproducibility and limit the paper’s impact; addressing them would significantly strengthen the contribution.
