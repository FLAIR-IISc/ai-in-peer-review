- Proposes a rule-based time expression extractor: identify time tokens (dictionary, regex, POS), expand bidirectionally with heuristics, then merge segments with rules.  
- Evaluated against rule-based and ML systems on three datasets, showing significant performance gains.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Rule design is empirically motivated and convincingly supported by evaluation.  
- Lacks reproducibility details: tokenization boundaries, heuristic thresholds, and merging criteria are underspecified.  
- Needs clarification of the term "five seasons" and how culturally/context-specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) are handled.  
- Inconsistency in dataset reporting: Section 5.1 says "two benchmark datasets" but the paper elsewhere mentions three.  
- Should provide an example of handling expressions without a time token to clarify the expansion phase.  
- Given 93% of expressions contain a time token and system recall is 92%, the approach may be near a performance ceiling; authors should address the remaining 7% lacking tokens.  
- Clarify whether the complete rule set and implementation code will be publicly released for reproducibility and community use.
