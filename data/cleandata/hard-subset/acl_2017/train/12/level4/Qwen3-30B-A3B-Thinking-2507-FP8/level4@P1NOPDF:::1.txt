

This paper presents a rule-based approach for time expression extraction, leveraging the insight that such expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The system first identifies time tokens using dictionary lookups, regular expressions, and POS tagging. It then expands the time segment bidirectionally from the token boundary using heuristic rules, before merging overlapping segments into cohesive expressions via additional rules. Evaluation on three datasets demonstrates significant improvements over both rule-based and ML-based baselines.

*Strengths:*  
The paper is well-written and clearly structured. The rule design is empirically motivated and well-justified through data analysis, as convincingly shown in the evaluation.

*Weaknesses:*  
The paper lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility. Specific gaps include:  
- Section 4.1: The claim of "five seasons" requires clarification. How does the system handle culturally specific periods like Ramadan or holiday seasons?  
- Section 5.1: The reference to "two benchmark datasets" should be corrected to "three datasets" (as three are cited in Table 1).  
- Section 5.2: A concrete example of a time expression without a clear time token would strengthen the explanation.  
- Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain at least one time token and the system achieves 92% recall, how does the authors plan to address the remaining 8% gap? The approach appears close to the performance ceiling.  
- The authors should clarify whether they intend to release the full rule set and implementation code.  

These issues collectively hinder reproducibility and limit the paper’s impact. Addressing them would substantially strengthen the contribution.
