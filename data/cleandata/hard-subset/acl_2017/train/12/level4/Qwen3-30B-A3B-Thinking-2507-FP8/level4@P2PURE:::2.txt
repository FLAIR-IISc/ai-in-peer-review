

The paper proposes a simple rule-based method for recognizing time expressions in text, which serves as a strong advantage as an analysis tool given that time expression recognition constitutes a fundamental process in applications. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms both state-of-the-art rule-based approaches and machine learning-based methods for time expression recognition.  

However, I have concerns regarding the generality of the method. The rules were developed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising the risk that the rules overfit to these specific corpora. Furthermore, the domains represented in these corpora may have influenced the rule design. The paper provides no statistical analysis or discussion of overlaps in time expressions across the observed corpora. If such overlaps were shown to be substantial, this would have supported the generality of the rules. To establish the method's broader effectiveness, the experiments should have employed a new corpus distinct from the rule design process.
