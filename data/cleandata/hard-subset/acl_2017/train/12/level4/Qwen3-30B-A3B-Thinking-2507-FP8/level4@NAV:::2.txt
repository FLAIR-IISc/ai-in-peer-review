

The paper introduces a rule-based approach for time expression recognition, presenting a notable strength as a fundamental prerequisite for time-related applications due to its simplicity. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior performance compared to both state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning-based approaches for time expression recognition.  

However, I remain concerned about the method's generality. The rules were developed through analysis of the same corpora used for evaluation, raising the risk that the rules may be overfitted to these specific datasets. Additionally, domain-specific characteristics within the evaluation corpora likely influenced rule formulation. Crucially, the paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion regarding temporal expression overlap across the observed corpora. Demonstrating minimal overlap would have strengthened the argument for rule generalizability.  

To conclusively establish the method's broader applicability, experiments should have employed an independent corpus, distinct from the data used to develop the rules. This would better validate the proposed approach's effectiveness beyond the specific evaluation domains.
