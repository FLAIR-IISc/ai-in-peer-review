- Paper introduces SynTime, a syntactic type-based rule tagger for time expression recognition.  
- Empirical observations across four datasets: 80% of time expressions ≤3 tokens; small time-related vocabulary; ≥93% contain at least one time token; words in time expressions show similar syntactic behavior.  
- SynTime defines three syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and uses heuristic boundary expansion rules.  
- Contributions: thorough empirical analysis supporting the principle of least effort; a novel syntactic type system distinct from semantic approaches; a lightweight rule-based tagger that outperforms state-of-the-art on benchmarks and tweets.  
- Strength: Empirical analysis is strong, consistent across diverse datasets, and well-motivated.  
- Strength: SynTime’s design is simple, principled, and syntactically driven, enabling intuitive heuristic rules.  
- Strength: Evaluation shows significant improvements (e.g., TimeBank 92.09% strict F1, Tweets 91.74% strict F1) and very high recall.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written with thorough comparisons to existing methods.  
- Weakness: Insufficient implementation detail for reproducibility, especially unclear definition/handling of the “5 seasons” and cultural time expressions.  
- Weakness: Factual error in Section 5.1 claiming “two benchmark datasets” when three are used.  
- Weakness: No example provided of a time expression without a time token to illustrate edge-case handling.  
- Weakness: Discussion omits strategies to improve beyond current ceiling for the ~1% of expressions lacking time tokens.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether the authors will release the full rule set or software for SynTime.  
- Question: Please clarify the definition of the “5 seasons” and handling of culturally specific expressions (e.g., “Ramadan month”).  
- Question: Correct the “two benchmark datasets” statement and specify which datasets are benchmarks.  
- Question: Provide an example of a time expression without a time token and show how SynTime handles it.  
- Question: How will the authors address the remaining ~1% of time expressions that lack time tokens?  
- Question: Will the authors release the full set of rules and software for SynTime?
