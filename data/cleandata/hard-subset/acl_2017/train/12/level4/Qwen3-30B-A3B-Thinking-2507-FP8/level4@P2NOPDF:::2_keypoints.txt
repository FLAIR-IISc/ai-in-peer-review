- Proposes a straightforward rule-based method for time expression recognition, useful as a preprocessing tool for temporal analysis.  
- Reports experimental results showing superior performance over state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning approaches.  
- Claims a notable contribution, but a critical limitation is the method’s generality.  
- Rules were derived from the same corpora used for evaluation, creating a risk of overfitting.  
- Observed performance may not generalize to new domains or texts beyond the evaluation corpora.  
- Authors do not assess whether time expressions overlap or are redundant across the corpora.  
- Absence of statistical analysis of corpus overlap (e.g., shared expressions) weakens the generality claim.  
- Experiments should use a distinct corpus not involved in rule design to rigorously test generalization.  
- Recommend conducting experiments on an independent dataset to substantiate broad applicability.  
- Suggest adding analysis of time expression diversity (e.g., Jaccard similarity or shared-frequency metrics).  
- Overall: method is interpretable and empirically competitive, but validation on unseen data is needed to confirm real-world impact.
