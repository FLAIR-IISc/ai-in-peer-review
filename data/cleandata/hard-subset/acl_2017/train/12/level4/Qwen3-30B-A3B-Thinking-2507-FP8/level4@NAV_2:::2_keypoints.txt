- SynTime: a syntactic-type, rule-based method for time expression recognition using three types (time token, modifier, numeral) derived from analysis of four datasets.

- Key observations: time expressions are very short (80% ≤3 tokens), use a small time-related vocabulary, contain a time token in >93% cases, and show similar syntactic behavior.

- Contributions: comprehensive multi-dataset analysis supporting the principle of least effort; novel syntactic-type grouping; simple heuristics with strong empirical results vs. SOTA.

- Strength: Diverse-dataset analysis provides compelling evidence for short expressions, small vocabulary, high time-token occurrence, and syntactic consistency.

- Strength: Lightweight design with real-time performance suitable for immediate-processing applications.

- Strength: Exceptional recall (over 95% relaxed) and strong overall performance, notably 95.87% F1 on Tweets.

- Weakness: Rules were developed using the same corpora as evaluation, risking overfitting to those datasets.

- Weakness: No analysis of overlap between analysis and evaluation datasets, so unclear if rules capture domain-specific patterns.

- Weakness: Lack of experiments on an external/new corpus prevents assessment of broader applicability beyond studied domains.

- Question: Please provide overlap statistics between time expressions in analysis vs. evaluation datasets to assess potential overfitting.

- Question: How would SynTime perform on domains not covered in the analysis (e.g., medical or legal texts) with different time-expression patterns?

- Question: Have you tested SynTime on a corpus not used during analysis or rule design to demonstrate generalizability?
