- Summary: Analyzes time expressions across four datasets, identifies four properties (short length, small keyword vocab, high token overlap, similar syntactic behavior) and proposes SynTime, a syntactic type-based rule system for recognition.
- Contribution 1: Empirical analysis supporting the principle of least effort in time expressions across multiple corpora.
- Contribution 2: Novel SynTime approach using syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) for flexible, extensible rule-based recognition.
- Contribution 3: Empirical validation showing SynTime outperforms baselines with strong F1 and very high recall, especially on Twitter data.
- Strength 1: Comprehensive, systematic dataset analysis with well-supported observations forming a solid foundation.
- Strength 2: Conceptual shift to syntactic-type rules yields greater flexibility and ease of domain expansion by adding keywords.
- Strength 3: Strong experimental results, highest F1 on TimeBank and Tweets and recall >92% (relaxed) across datasets.
- Strength 4: Practical advantages highlighted—lightweight, real-time operation, and easy expandability for real-world use.
- Weakness 1: Risk of overfitting because SynTime rules were developed on the same corpora used for evaluation; no evidence of low dataset overlap.
- Weakness 2: Insufficient treatment of prepositional or descriptive time phrases (e.g., "the time Arnold reached Quebec City") noted in WikiWars.
- Weakness 3: Claim of a small time-expression vocabulary lacks broad empirical support; manual keyword addition may not scale to diverse domains.
- Weakness 4: No quantitative comparison of computational efficiency versus baselines despite real-time claims.
- Question 1: Provide evidence of overlap between time expressions across the four datasets to assess generalizability.
- Question 2: How would SynTime handle prepositional/descriptive time expressions and what modifications are needed?
- Question 3: Provide quantitative processing-speed comparisons between SynTime and baselines to support real-time claims.
- Question 4: How will manual keyword expansion scale to domains with highly diverse time-expression patterns?
