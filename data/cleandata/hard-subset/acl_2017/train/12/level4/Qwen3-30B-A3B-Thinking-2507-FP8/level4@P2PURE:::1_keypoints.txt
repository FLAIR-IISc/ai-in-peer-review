- Paper proposes a rule-based time-expression extractor that finds time tokens via dictionary/regex/POS, expands spans heuristically, merges overlaps, and reports strong improvements on three datasets.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: Rules are empirically motivated and justified by evaluation results.  
- Weakness: Several implementation details are underspecified, harming reproducibility.  
- Clarification needed: What are the "five seasons" and how are non-standard periods (e.g., Ramadan, holiday seasons) handled?  
- Inconsistency: Section 5.1 refers to "two benchmark datasets" despite evaluation on three datasets.  
- Request: Provide an example of a time expression that contains no time token.  
- Question: With 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, how will the authors address the remaining performance gap?  
- Request: Do the authors plan to release the full rule set and software for replication?
