- Paper proposes a rule-based method for time expression extraction based on the insight that expressions are short and include at least one time token.  
- System identifies time tokens via dictionary lookup, regular expressions, and POS tagging.  
- It expands a time segment bidirectionally from the time token using heuristic rules until a boundary is reached.  
- Time segments are merged into single expressions using additional merging rules.  
- Evaluation against rule-based and ML baselines on three datasets shows significant performance improvements.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: rules are empirically motivated and supported by evaluation results.  
- Weakness: underspecified details hinder reproducibility.  
- Clarification needed (Sec 4.1): what are the "5 seasons" and how are expressions like "Ramadan month" or "Holiday Season" handled?  
- Correction (Sec 5.1): phrase "Two benchmark datasets" should be "three datasets."  
- Clarification requested (Sec 5.2): provide an example of an expression without a time token to illustrate the approach.  
- Question (Sec 5.2): with 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, how can performance be further improved?  
- Practical question: will the complete rule set and software be released?
