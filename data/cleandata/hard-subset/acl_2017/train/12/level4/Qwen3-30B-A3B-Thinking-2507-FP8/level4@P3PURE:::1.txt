

This paper presents a rule-based approach for time expression extraction. The key insight is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The method first identifies time tokens via dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging. It then expands the time span bidirectionally from the token using heuristic rules until reaching a boundary, and finally merges overlapping segments into a single expression using additional rules. Evaluation against both rule-based and ML-based systems across three datasets demonstrates significant improvements.

- **Strengths:** The paper is clearly written and well-structured. The rules are empirically motivated and well-justified by the evaluation results.

- **Weaknesses:** The paper contains underspecifications that hinder reproducibility (detailed below).

- **General Discussion:**  
  * Section 4.1: The definition of "5 time tokens" requires clarification. How are non-standard temporal references (e.g., "Ramadan month" or "Holiday Season") handled?  
  * Section 5.1: The phrase "two benchmark datasets" should be corrected to "three datasets" to align with the evaluation.  
  * Section 5.2: An example of an extraction *without* a time token would clarify the method's limitations.  
  * Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, how does the authors plan to address the remaining gap?  
  * The authors should clarify whether the full rule set and software will be released.
