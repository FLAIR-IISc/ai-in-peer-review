

This paper presents a rule-based methodology for time expression extraction. Its core insight is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The approach first identifies time tokens via dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and POS tagging. It then expands the time segment bidirectionally from the time token using heuristic rules until reaching a boundary. Finally, time segments are merged into a single expression using additional rules. Evaluation against both rule-based and ML systems across three datasets demonstrates significant performance improvements.  
- Strengths:  
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are empirically motivated and well-justified by the evaluation results.  
- Weaknesses:  
The paper contains underspecifications that hinder reproducibility; details are provided below.  
- General Discussion:  
* Section 4.1: What constitutes the "5 seasons"? How are expressions like "Ramadan month" or "Holiday Season" handled?  
* Section 5.1: "Two benchmark datasets" should be corrected to "three datasets".  
* Section 5.2: An example without a time token would clarify the approach.  
* Section 5.2: Given 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, how will performance be further improved?  
* Is there a plan to release the complete rule set and software?
