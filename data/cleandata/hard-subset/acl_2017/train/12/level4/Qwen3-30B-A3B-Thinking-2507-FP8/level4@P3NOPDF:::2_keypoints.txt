- Presents a simple, interpretable rule-based method for time expression recognition that serves as a useful foundation for downstream applications.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, outperforming state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning systems on standard evaluation corpora.
- The method’s transparency combined with performance is a notable contribution to the field.
- Major methodological flaw: rules were explicitly designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, creating high risk of overfitting.
- Authors provide no evidence that the evaluation corpora are diverse or representative of broader domains.
- No analysis of overlap between corpora used for rule development and evaluation (e.g., shared time expression patterns or domain coverage).
- Core issue: lack of a distinct development vs. evaluation split prevents claims of generality.
- Recommended fixes: derive rules from a separate corpus/subset and test on an independent dataset to demonstrate generalization.
- Additional recommended analyses: statistical measures of time expression diversity/overlap across corpora and experiments on a domain-diverse test set.
- Despite evaluation concerns, the approach is well-motivated and results are compelling within the studied corpora; current evaluation does not support broad robustness claims.
