

This paper presents a rule-based approach for time expression extraction. Its core insight is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The method first identifies time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookup, regular expression matching, and part-of-speech tagging. It then expands the time span in both directions from the time token using heuristic rules until a boundary condition is met. Finally, overlapping or adjacent time spans are merged into a single expression using additional rules. Evaluation against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems across three datasets demonstrates significant improvements.

- Strengths:  
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The rules are empirically motivated and well-justified, as evidenced by the evaluation results.

- Weaknesses:  
The paper contains underspecified details that hinder reproducibility. See specific concerns below.

- General Discussion:  
* Section 4.1: What constitutes the "five seasons"? How are non-standard periods like Ramadan or holiday seasons handled?  
* Section 5.1: The phrase "two benchmark datasets" appears inconsistent with the evaluation on three datasets.  
* Section 5.2: An example of an expression without a time token would be helpful.  
* Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, how does the authors plan to address the remaining performance gap?  
* Is there a plan to release the complete set of rules and software?
