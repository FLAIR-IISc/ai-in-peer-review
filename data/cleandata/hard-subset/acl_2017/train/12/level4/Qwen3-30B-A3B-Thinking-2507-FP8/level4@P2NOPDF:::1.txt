

This paper presents a rule-based approach for time expression extraction, leveraging two key observations: time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The method first identifies time tokens via dictionary lookup, regular expressions, and POS tagging. It then expands segments bidirectionally from the token using heuristic rules until boundary conditions are met, before merging segments into complete expressions through additional rules. Evaluation against both rule-based and machine learning systems across three datasets demonstrates significant performance gains.

**Strengths:**  
The paper is clearly written and well-structured. The rule design is empirically motivated and convincingly justified by the evaluation results, enhancing the method’s credibility.

**Weaknesses:**  
The paper lacks sufficient detail to enable reproducibility. Critical implementation specifics—such as exact tokenization boundaries, heuristic thresholds, and merging criteria—are underspecified, hindering independent verification of the reported results.

**General Discussion:**  
*Section 4.1:* The definition of "five seasons" requires clarification. How are culturally or context-specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) handled within this framework?  
*Section 5.1:* The phrase "two benchmark datasets" conflicts with the subsequent mention of three datasets; this should be harmonized.  
*Section 5.2:* An example demonstrating the system’s handling of expressions *without* a time token would strengthen the explanation of the expansion phase.  
*Section 5.2:* Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, the approach appears near the performance ceiling. How might the authors address the remaining 7% of expressions lacking tokens to further improve recall?  
*Reproducibility:* The authors should clarify whether the complete rule set and implementation code will be publicly released to facilitate community validation and extension.
