- Presents a rule-based method for time expression extraction that finds time tokens, expands segments with heuristics, and merges them, showing large gains over baselines.  
- Evaluation on three datasets demonstrates significant improvement versus both rule-based and ML baselines (Table 3).  
- Paper is very well-written, logically structured, and motivates rules from empirical analysis.  
- Strong evaluation design that supports the claimed improvements.  
- Focus on practical, interpretable rules is valuable for real-world NLP use.  
- Implementation details are insufficient, limiting reproducibility.  
- Ambiguity in the exact criteria and scope for "time tokens" (e.g., multiword tokens like "next Monday").  
- Unclear boundary conditions for segment expansion (e.g., punctuation/conjunction handling).  
- Ambiguous merging rules for overlapping segments (e.g., "July 4th and 5th").  
- Specific issues: unclear handling of culturally specific periods (e.g., "Ramadan"), discrepancy in dataset count (text says two vs. Table 1 listing three), and missing examples for tokenless expressions.  
- Near-ceiling performance (93% token coverage, 92% recall) raises the question of how to handle the remaining ~8%; authors should consider extra rules or hybrid approaches.  
- Reproducibility request: release of full rule set, dictionary, and code strongly encouraged.  
- Overall: strong, empirically supported contribution that requires clearer implementation details and examples to maximize impact and adoption.
