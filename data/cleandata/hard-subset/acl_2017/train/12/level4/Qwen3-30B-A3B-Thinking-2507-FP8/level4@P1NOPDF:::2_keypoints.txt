- Proposes a simple, well-motivated rule-based method for time expression recognition serving as a strong base for applications.  
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning approaches.  
- Technical execution appears sound.  
- Major concern: rules were developed on the same corpora used for evaluation, raising overfitting concerns.  
- The specific domains of the corpora may have biased rule design.  
- No statistical analysis or discussion of overlap in time expressions across corpora is provided.  
- If corpora overlap significantly, reported gains may reflect dataset adaptation rather than true generality.  
- Demonstrating minimal overlap would strengthen the generality claim; its absence leaves robustness uncertain.  
- A stronger evaluation would test the rules on an independent corpus not used for rule development.  
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge this limitation and consider re-evaluation on independent, domain-diverse data.
