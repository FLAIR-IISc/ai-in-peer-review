- Paper presents SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer and an empirical analysis of time expressions across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets.

- Contributions: empirical evidence that time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, nearly always contain a time token, and show similar syntactic behavior.

- Contributions: a syntactic type system (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) enabling a lightweight rule-based recognition approach.

- Contributions: SynTime implementation that outperforms state-of-the-art methods on multiple datasets, notably Tweets.

- Strength: Thorough cross-dataset analysis supports the principle of least effort (80% ≤3 tokens; 64–80 distinct time tokens; 93–98% contain time tokens).

- Strength: SynTime’s syntactic-type and heuristic boundary expansion design is simple, flexible, and less reliant on fixed-length patterns or heavy training data.

- Strength: Evaluation shows clear improvements and high recall (92–95.87%), with especially strong performance on informal text (Tweets).

- Strength: Convincing explanation for success on informal text—formal time words dominate usage even on Twitter.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail to reproduce the system—heuristic rules for time segment identification lack concrete examples and implementation specifics.

- Weakness: Inadequate handling/explanation of time expressions without explicit time tokens and unclear POS-based filtering behavior.

- Weakness: Expansion process is underspecified—criteria for adding keywords and the manual addition workflow are not explained or justified.

- Weakness: Evaluation methodology lacks clarity (e.g., how SUTime miss rate on tweets was computed and annotation/sample details).

- Questions: Request examples of time expressions with no time tokens and how SynTime handles them.

- Questions: Ask for clarification of the Twitter frequency claim and the scale/numbers behind the "today" vs. "2day" comparison.

- Questions: Ask how SynTime handles descriptive WikiWars expressions (e.g., "the time Arnold reached Quebec City") and what improvements are needed.

- Questions: Request examples of tokens added for WikiWars (16 time tokens, 19 modifiers) and why they were necessary there but not in other datasets.
