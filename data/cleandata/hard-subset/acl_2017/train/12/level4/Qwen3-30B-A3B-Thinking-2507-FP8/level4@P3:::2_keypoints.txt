- Observation: Time expressions are typically short — ~80% contain ≤3 tokens.  
- Observation: Time expressions use a small, consistent vocabulary across domains.  
- Observation: Over 93% of time expressions contain at least one “time token” (e.g., month names).  
- Observation: Words in time expressions show similar syntactic behavior.  
- Method: SynTime — a rule-based recognizer with three syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and simple boundary-expansion heuristics.  
- Contribution: Empirical evidence supporting a “least effort” principle in time expression usage.  
- Contribution: A linguistically grounded syntactic type system for time expressions.  
- Contribution: A lightweight, expandable rule-based system that outperforms prior methods on benchmarks and tweets.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation across diverse datasets (news, Wikipedia, tweets).  
- Strength: Practical efficiency — real-time, high recall (≥92% relaxed) without training data.  
- Strength: Domain adaptability demonstrated by minimal expansion for tweets, achieving high performance.  
- Strength: Clear theoretical grounding using POS-based syntactic types rather than ad-hoc semantic rules.  
- Weakness: Potential overfitting — rules were derived and evaluated on the same datasets without overlap analysis or cross-validation.  
- Weakness: Limited evidence of generalization to truly new domains (e.g., medical or legal text).  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of robustness to tokenization/POS-tagging errors and their impact on performance.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous novelty claims relative to prior systems (e.g., SUTime) lacking rigorous comparative justification.
