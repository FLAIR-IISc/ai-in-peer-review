

The paper presents a rule-based method for time expression recognition, which is advantageous as a straightforward analysis tool given that time expression recognition constitutes a fundamental preprocessing step in many applications. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior performance compared to both state-of-the-art rule-based approaches and machine learning-based methods for time expression recognition.  

While the approach is promising, a key concern pertains to the method's generality. The rules were derived from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising the risk of overfitting to these specific datasets. Furthermore, the domains represented in these corpora may have inadvertently influenced rule design. Crucially, the paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion regarding the overlap of time expressions across the evaluation corpora. If such overlap were substantial, it would have provided stronger evidence for the rules' generalizability. To rigorously establish the method's broad applicability, the experiments should have employed a distinct corpus for evaluation—one not involved in rule design.
