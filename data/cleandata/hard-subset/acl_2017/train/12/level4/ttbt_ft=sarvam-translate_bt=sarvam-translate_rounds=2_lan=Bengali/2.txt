This paper proposes a method for identifying temporal expressions from text. It is a simple rule-based method that has a strong advantage as an analysis tool, as temporal expression recognition should be a fundamental process in applications.

The test results show that the proposed method performs better than state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning-based methods for temporal expression recognition.

That's great, but my concern is the generality of the method. The rules of the method were designed according to corpora, which are also used for evaluation. So I fear that the rules have become too overfitted for these corpora. Similarly, the domains of these corpora may influence the rule design.

There are no statistics or discussions to show that the observed corpora show overlap in temporal expressions. If it could be shown that temporal expressions in these corpora overlap in most cases, this information would support the generality of the rules.

However, the tests were conducted using a new corpus that was separate from the rule design process, so as to show that the proposed method is widely effective.
