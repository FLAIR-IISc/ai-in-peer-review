This paper presents a method for recognizing temporal expressions in text. The proposed method is rule-based and notable for its simplicity. This is a significant advantage, as temporal expression recognition is a foundational component in many NLP applications, where a straightforward and reliable tool is often preferred. Experimental results indicate that the proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art systems, including both rule-based and machine learning-based approaches.

While the results are strong, my primary concern is the method's generalizability. The rules were designed based on observations from the same corpora that were subsequently used for evaluation. Consequently, it is likely that the rules have over-fitted to these specific corpora. Similarly, the particular domains of these corpora may have unduly influenced the rule design, potentially limiting their applicability to other domains.

The paper lacks any statistical analysis or discussion regarding the overlap of temporal expression types across the development corpora. If the authors had demonstrated that these corpora contain a largely overlapping set of temporal phenomena, this would have strengthened the argument for the rules' generality.

Ultimately, the claim of generalizability would have been far more convincing if the experiments had included evaluation on a held-out corpus, one completely unseen during the rule design process. Such an experiment would be necessary to demonstrate that the proposed method is broadly effective.
