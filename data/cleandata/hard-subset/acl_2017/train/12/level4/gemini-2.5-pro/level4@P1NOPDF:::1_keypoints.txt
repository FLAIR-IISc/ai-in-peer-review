- Presents a rule-based time-expression extraction method assuming expressions are short and contain at least one time-related token.  
- Token recognition uses dictionary lookups, regex matching, and POS-tagging.  
- Time segments are expanded bidirectionally from identified tokens using heuristic boundary rules.  
- Adjacent or related segments are merged via additional heuristic rules to form complete expressions.  
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvements over prior rule-based and ML systems.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: Heuristic rules are empirically motivated and supported by strong evaluation results.  
- Weakness: Missing specific details required for reproducibility.  
- Question: Clarify rationale for using “five seasons” and handling of culturally specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, “Holiday Season”).  
- Question: Resolve discrepancy between mentioning “two benchmark datasets” and evaluating on three.  
- Suggestion: Provide examples of time expressions that lack a time token and explain current system’s failure modes.  
- Question: Given 93% of expressions contain a time token and system achieves 92% recall, what avenues exist to close the remaining gap?  
- Request: Will the authors release the full rule set or software implementation for replication?
