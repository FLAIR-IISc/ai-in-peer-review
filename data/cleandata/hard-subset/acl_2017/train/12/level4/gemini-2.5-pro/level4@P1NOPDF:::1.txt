This paper presents a rule-based approach for time expression extraction. The approach is based on the key observation that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time-related token. The system first recognizes these time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookups, regular expression matching, and part-of-speech (POS) tagging information. Next, it expands a time segment in both directions from the identified token, using a set of heuristic rules to define the boundaries. Finally, another set of rules is used to merge adjacent or related time segments into a single, complete time expression. An evaluation on three datasets shows that this approach achieves significant improvements over both existing rule-based and machine learning-based systems.

**Strengths:**
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The proposed heuristic rules are well-motivated by empirical observations from the data, and their effectiveness is justified by the strong evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**
The paper lacks some specific details that would be necessary to reproduce the results, as noted in the discussion below.

**General Discussion:**
*   Section 4.1: The paper mentions five seasons. Could the authors clarify the reasoning for this specific number? For instance, how would the system handle culturally specific time periods like the month of Ramadan or the "Holiday Season"?

*   Section 5.1: The text mentions "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation appears to be conducted on three. Please clarify this discrepancy.

*   Section 5.2: To better illustrate the system's limitations, it would be helpful to include an example of a time expression that does not contain a time token and discuss how the current system handles (or fails to handle) it.

*   Section 5.2: The paper notes that 93% of time expressions in the data contain a time token, which sets a rough performance ceiling for this approach. Given that the system already achieves 92% recall, what are the potential avenues for further improvement? How can the remaining gap be closed?

*   Do the authors plan to release the full set of rules or the software implementation to facilitate future research and replication?
