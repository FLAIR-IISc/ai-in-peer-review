- Presents a simple rule-based method for temporal expression recognition, claiming simplicity as an advantage for downstream NLP tasks.  
- Experimental results are strong, reportedly outperforming prior rule-based and machine-learning methods.  
- Primary concern: generalizability—rules were designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising overfitting risk.  
- Overfitting risk specifics: rules may be overly tuned to dataset-specific characteristics and idiosyncrasies.  
- Twofold generalization worry: (1) degraded performance on unseen data, (2) domain-specific rule design limiting cross-domain applicability.  
- Paper lacks statistical analysis of the development corpora (e.g., overlap of temporal expression types) to support rule generality.  
- Suggested mitigation: include analysis of corpus diversity/overlap to justify rule coverage.  
- Stronger validation recommended: evaluate on a held-out test set from a corpus not used in rule design to demonstrate robustness.
