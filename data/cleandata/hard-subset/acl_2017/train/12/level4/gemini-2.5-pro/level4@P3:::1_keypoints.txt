- Proposes SynTime, a lightweight, rule-based time expression recognizer using an "anchor-and-expand" strategy on POS-tagged text.
- Method operates in three stages: identify time tokens/modifiers/numerals, heuristically expand boundaries to form segments, then merge adjacent/overlapping segments.
- Evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and a Tweets corpus; outperforms several baselines and achieves very high recall (relaxed-match recall >92%).
- Contribution: a simple, effective, interpretable system that avoids complex grammars or heavy ML features.
- Contribution: a corpus analysis quantifying time expression properties (length, vocab size, prevalence of core tokens) that motivates the design.
- Strength: strong empirical performance and highest recall across datasets, validating the core-token hypothesis.
- Strength: well-motivated, intuitive, and interpretable design directly tied to empirical findings.
- Strength: highly extensible and practical—small keyword additions improve domain performance; system is lightweight and fast.
- Weakness: underspecified heuristic rules for expansion and merging, making reproduction difficult (stopping conditions, precedence, logic not detailed).
- Weakness: the keyword/regex resources used to initialize syntactic types are not provided, preventing replication.
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases, especially the ~7% of time expressions without a recognized time token.
- Weakness: ambiguity resolution via POS tags is underspecified and robustness to POS tagging errors is not analyzed.
- Question: please provide exact heuristic rules for time segment expansion and merging (stopping conditions, merge logic).
- Question: will you publicly release code and the full keyword/regex lists used to initialize SynTime?
- Question: provide examples and analysis of time expressions that lack predefined time tokens and how SynTime handles them.
- Question: given a theoretical recall ceiling (~93–97%), what are the best paths to further improve precision and overall F1?
