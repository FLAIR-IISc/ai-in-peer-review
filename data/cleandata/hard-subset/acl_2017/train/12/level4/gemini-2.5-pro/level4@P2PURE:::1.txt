This paper presents a rule-based approach for time expression extraction. The approach is based on the key observation that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one "time token." The methodology consists of three stages. First, time tokens are identified using a combination of dictionary lookups, regular expression matching, and POS tagging information. Second, the system expands these initial segments bidirectionally from the time token according to a set of heuristic rules that define the expression's boundaries. Finally, adjacent time segments are merged into a single expression using another set of rules. A comparative evaluation against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems on three datasets demonstrates significant performance improvements.

**Strengths:**
The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The proposed heuristic rules are well-motivated by empirical observations from the data, and their effectiveness is substantiated by the strong evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**
The paper lacks certain details in its description, which could make it difficult to reproduce the results. See below for specific points.

**General Discussion:**
*   In Section 4.1, the authors list five seasons. What is the rationale for this specific set? For instance, how would the system handle concepts like "Ramadan month" or "the holiday season"?
*   Section 5.1 mentions "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation appears to use three. Could the authors clarify this discrepancy?
*   In Section 5.2, the authors note that 7% of expressions do not contain a time token. An example of such an expression would be helpful to illustrate these cases.
*   The proposed approach achieves an impressive 92% recall. Given that the authors' analysis shows that 93% of time expressions contain a time token, the system appears to be approaching the performance ceiling for this method. What are the potential avenues for further improvement?
*   Do the authors plan to release the complete set of rules or the software implementation to facilitate future research and replication?
