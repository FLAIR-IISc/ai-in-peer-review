- Proposes a rule-based time expression extractor using the insights that time expressions are short and usually contain a “time token.”
- Method: identify time tokens via dictionary lookups, regex, and POS tags; expand segments heuristically; merge adjacent segments into full expressions.
- Evaluated on three datasets and shows significant improvements over prior rule-based and ML systems.
- Strength: well-written, clearly presented; heuristic rules are data-motivated and supported by strong evaluation results.
- Weakness: underspecification in the paper could impede reproducibility.
- Clarification needed: Section 4.1 mentions “five seasons” — specify what they are and how culturally specific/event-based periods (e.g., Ramadan, Holiday Season) are handled.
- Clarification needed: Section 5.1 refers to “two benchmark datasets” but experiments use three — resolve this discrepancy.
- Request example: Section 5.2 notes 7% of time expressions lack a time token — provide an example of such expressions.
- Ceiling concern: 93% of expressions contain a time token (performance ceiling); given 92% recall, discuss possible avenues for further improvement.
- Reproducibility question: will the authors release the full rule set or software implementation to facilitate future research?
