- Presents SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using an "identify-and-expand" heuristic around core time tokens.
- Motivated by analysis of four datasets (TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, Tweets) showing time expressions are short, use limited vocabulary, and contain a core time token.
- Defines three syntactic word types for time expressions: time tokens, modifiers, and numerals, and uses a three-step heuristic: detect tokens, expand boundaries, merge segments.
- Evaluates two versions: SynTime-I (bootstrapped from SUTime keywords) and SynTime-E (expanded with keywords learned from training data).
- SynTime outperforms several baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime) on TimeBank and Tweets, and achieves comparable results on WikiWars, with notably high recall.
- Primary contribution: a minimalist, effective rule-based tagger that avoids complex cascaded rules in favor of a simple syntactic heuristic.
- Secondary contribution: systematic cross-genre analysis of time expressions that grounds the system design empirically.
- Strength: conceptual simplicity and efficiency, making the system lightweight, fast, and easier to maintain or adapt.
- Strength: strong empirical performance, especially on noisy Tweets, with substantial F1 and recall improvements over baselines.
- Strength: approach is well-motivated by dataset analyses linking design choices to observed properties of time expressions.
- Weakness: concerns about generality and overfitting since keyword lists and SynTime-E were developed using the same corpora used for evaluation.
- Weakness: lack of analysis on lexical overlap of time tokens across datasets to support claims of a core, domain-independent vocabulary.
- Weakness: limited discussion and error analysis of ambiguity resolution and failure cases beyond blaming POS tagging errors.
- Questions: how would untuned SynTime-I perform on unseen domains (e.g., legal or medical texts)?
- Questions: what specific SUTime token lists/regexes were used and which SUTime components were discarded?
- Questions: can the authors provide statistics on lexical overlap of time tokens across the four analyzed corpora?
