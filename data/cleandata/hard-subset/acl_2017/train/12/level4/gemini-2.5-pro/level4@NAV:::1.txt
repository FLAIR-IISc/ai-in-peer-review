This paper presents a rule-based methodology for the task of time expression extraction. The approach is built on the core insights that temporal expressions are typically short and contain at least one time-related token. The system operates in three stages. First, it identifies temporal tokens through a combination of dictionary lookups, regular expression matching, and Part-of-Speech information. Second, it expands the segment around the identified token in both directions based on a series of heuristic rules. Finally, a separate set of rules is used to merge these segments into a single, complete time expression. The evaluation, conducted on three datasets against both rule-based and machine learning competitors, shows that this approach yields substantial improvements.

- Strengths:
The paper is well-written and the methodology is presented with clarity. The rules are grounded in empirical observations from the data, and their effectiveness is well-justified by the strong evaluation results.

- Weaknesses:
The paper lacks certain details, which could hinder the reproducibility of the reported results. Please see the detailed comments below.

- General Discussion:
*   Section 4.1: Could the authors clarify the reasoning for defining five seasons? The discussion could also benefit from addressing how the system handles cultural or event-based temporal expressions like "Ramadan" or "the holiday season."
*   Section 5.1: The text mentions "two benchmark datasets," but the evaluation appears to use three. Please clarify or correct this discrepancy.
*   Section 5.2: To better illustrate the system's limitations, it would be helpful to include an example of an expression that does not contain a temporal token.
*   Section 5.2: The system achieves an impressive 92% recall on a dataset where 93% of expressions contain a time token, suggesting it is approaching the performance ceiling for this method. What are the authors' plans for future improvements to close this remaining gap?
*   Will the complete set of rules and/or the software implementation be made publicly available to support future research?
