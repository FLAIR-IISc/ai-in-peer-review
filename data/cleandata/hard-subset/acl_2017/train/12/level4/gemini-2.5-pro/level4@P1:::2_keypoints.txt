- Proposes SynTime, a lightweight, rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic word types and simple heuristics.
- Method: identify core "time tokens," expand boundaries with adjacent modifiers/numerals to form segments, then merge segments into complete expressions.
- Motivated by corpus analysis showing time expressions are short, use limited vocabulary, and contain a core time token.
- Evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets; claims SynTime outperforms several state-of-the-art systems.
- Primary contribution: an intuitive, efficient rule-based tagger (SynTime) based on core token anchoring and boundary-expansion rules.
- Secondary contribution: empirical analysis of time expressions across four datasets supporting the design and a "least effort" principle.
- Strength: simplicity and efficiency—no complex training pipeline, suitable as a fast preprocessing tool.
- Strength: strong experimental results, especially high recall, suggesting effective core-token anchoring.
- Strength: extensibility—adding a few keywords improves domain adaptation (e.g., WikiWars).
- Weakness: generality not convincingly demonstrated; rules were designed using the same corpora used for evaluation, risking overfitting.
- Weakness: lacks inter-corpus statistical analysis to show consistency of time-token/vocabulary patterns across domains.
- Questions for authors: clarify rule development workflow and measures taken to avoid implicit overfitting.
- Request for cross-domain evaluation: report performance of SynTime-I (trained on TimeBank only) on WikiWars and Tweets without expansion to assess out-of-the-box generality.
