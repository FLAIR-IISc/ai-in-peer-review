This paper proposes a simple, rule-based method for recognizing time expressions in text. The simplicity of the approach is a strong advantage, as time expression recognition is a foundational task for many applications. Experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning-based methods.

While the results are strong, my primary concern is the method's generality. The rules were developed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation. This creates a significant risk that the rules are overfit to these specific datasets. Furthermore, the domains of these corpora may have influenced the rule design, potentially limiting the method's applicability.

The paper provides no statistics or discussion on the overlap of temporal expressions across the observed corpora. An analysis showing significant overlap would have helped to support the generality of the rules. To convincingly demonstrate that the proposed method is widely effective, the experiments should have been conducted on a new corpus, entirely distinct from the data used during the rule design process.
