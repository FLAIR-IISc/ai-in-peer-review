This paper presents a rule-based approach for time expression extraction. The approach is based on two key insights: time expressions are typically short and almost always contain at least one "time token." The method first identifies these time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookups, regular expression matching, and Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging information. Subsequently, it expands the time segment from both directions of the identified time token based on a set of heuristic rules. Finally, another set of rules is applied to merge adjacent time segments into complete time expressions. The proposed approach is evaluated on three datasets and demonstrates significant improvements over both rule-based and machine learning-based systems.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-written and clearly presented. The heuristic rules are motivated by empirical observations from the data, and their effectiveness is well-justified by the strong evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**

The paper suffers from some underspecification, which could make it difficult for others to reproduce the results. Specific points are detailed below.

**General Discussion:**

*   In Section 4.1, the authors mention five seasons. Could they clarify what these are? Additionally, how does the system handle culturally specific or event-based time periods, such as "Ramadan" or the "Holiday Season"?
*   In Section 5.1, the text mentions "two benchmark datasets," but the experiments appear to use three. Please clarify this discrepancy.
*   In Section 5.2, the authors note that 7% of time expressions do not contain a time token. It would be helpful to include an example of such an expression to better understand these cases.
*   According to Section 5.2, 93% of time expressions contain a time token, which represents a performance ceiling for the current approach. Given that the system already achieves 92% recall, what are the potential avenues for further improvement?
*   Are there any plans to release the full set of rules or the software implementation to facilitate future research and reproducibility?
