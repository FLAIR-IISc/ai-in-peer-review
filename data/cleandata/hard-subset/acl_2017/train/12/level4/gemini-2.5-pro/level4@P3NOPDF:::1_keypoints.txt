- Paper introduces a rule-based temporal expression extractor based on two observations: temporal expressions are short and usually contain a "time token," implemented in a three-stage pipeline (token identification, bi-directional expansion, merging).  
- System identifies time tokens via a hybrid of dictionaries, regexes, and POS tags, then uses heuristic boundary and merging rules.  
- Evaluated against rule-based and ML baselines on multiple datasets, showing significant performance improvements.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clear; methodology is simple, intuitive, and effective.  
- Strength: heuristic rules are empirically motivated and the system outperforms more complex alternatives, demonstrating strong engineering.  
- Weakness: key details for reproducibility are missing—dictionaries and full heuristic rules are not fully enumerated.  
- Request: specify the "five seasons" in the dictionary and consider culturally/religiously specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, "Holiday Season").  
- Request: release the full set of rules and/or software implementation to enable reproducibility.  
- Analysis request: provide examples of the ~7% of temporal expressions that lack a time token and discuss identification challenges.  
- Future-work question: given 92% recall vs a 93% token-containing ceiling, discuss approaches to capture the remaining expressions—extend rules or adopt a hybrid ML approach?  
- Minor correction: clarify the inconsistency between claiming evaluation on "two benchmark datasets" while tables/descriptions cover three.
