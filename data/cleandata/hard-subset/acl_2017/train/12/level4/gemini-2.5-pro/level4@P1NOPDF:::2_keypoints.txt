- Proposes a simple, rule-based method for temporal expression recognition.  
- Simplicity is a significant advantage for foundational NLP applications.  
- Experimental results claim to outperform existing state-of-the-art (both rule-based and ML).  
- Primary concern: rules were designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, risking overfitting.  
- Domain-specific corpora may have biased rule design, limiting applicability to other domains.  
- Paper lacks statistical analysis or discussion of overlap in temporal expression types across development corpora.  
- Demonstrating corpus overlap would strengthen the argument for rule generality.  
- Recommends evaluation on a held-out, unseen corpus to convincingly demonstrate generalizability.
