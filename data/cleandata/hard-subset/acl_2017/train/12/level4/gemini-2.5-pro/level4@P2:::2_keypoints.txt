- Introduces SynTime: a lightweight, rule-based method for recognizing time expressions based on linguistic analysis of four corpora.  
- Observes time expressions are typically short, use a small vocabulary, and center on a core "time token."  
- Defines three syntactic word types for time expressions: time token, modifier, and numeral.  
- Recognition pipeline: identify time tokens (string/regex + POS), expand boundaries for modifiers/numerals, then merge segments.  
- Evaluated against state-of-the-art systems on three datasets and shows superior performance, especially on noisy Twitter data.  

- Contribution: a simple, syntactic-type-based rule method (SynTime) that is effective and computationally lightweight.  
- Contribution: a corpus-driven analysis across four corpora that motivates design choices and grounds the method in linguistic observations.  

- Strength: strong empirical performance with high recall and state-of-the-art or competitive results across datasets.  
- Strength: conceptual simplicity and extensibility—easy to implement, debug, and adapt by adding keywords.  
- Strength: particularly effective on informal/social media text (high F1 on Tweets).  

- Weakness: potential overfitting and questionable generality because rules were designed from and evaluated on largely the same corpora.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of lexical/phenomenon overlap across corpora to support claims of a small, generalizable keyword set.  
- Weakness: dependence on upstream tokenizer/POS tagger; no quantified error analysis showing impact of POS/tagging errors.  

- Question: have you performed cross-domain experiments (e.g., train rules on some corpora and test on a held-out corpus) to assess out-of-the-box generalization?  
- Question: can you provide statistics on lexical overlap of identified time tokens and modifiers across TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets to support claims of generality?
