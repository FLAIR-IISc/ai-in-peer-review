- Presents a rule-based, three-stage method for time expression extraction based on short expressions containing a "time token."  
- Method: identify time tokens (dictionary/regex/POS), expand segments bidirectionally via heuristics, then merge adjacent segments.  
- Evaluated on three datasets vs rule-based and ML systems, showing significant performance improvements.  
- Strength: clear writing and well-motivated heuristic rules supported by strong empirical results.  
- Weakness: paper omits details that hinder reproducibility.  
- Question: rationale for listing five seasons and handling of concepts like "Ramadan month" or "the holiday season."  
- Question: clarify discrepancy between "two benchmark datasets" mentioned and the three used in evaluation.  
- Request: provide an example of the ~7% of expressions that lack a time token.  
- Concern: 92% recall is near the 93% ceiling implied by the time-token assumption—what avenues remain for improvement?  
- Request: will the authors release the full rule set or software to enable replication and future work?
