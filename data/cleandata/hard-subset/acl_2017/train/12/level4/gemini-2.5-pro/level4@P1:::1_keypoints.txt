- Summary: SynTime is a rule-based, three-stage system that anchors on "time tokens" and expands/merges with heuristics to recognize time expressions; evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets with strong gains, especially in recall.  
- Main contributions: a simple, effective anchor-based rule method and extensive evaluation showing state-of-the-art performance, notably on informal text.  
- Strength: Simplicity and effectiveness — anchor + boundary expansion heuristics capture most time expressions without complex rules or heavy feature engineering.  
- Strength: Strong empirical grounding — design motivated by dataset analysis showing brevity of expressions and near-ubiquity of time tokens.  
- Strength: State-of-the-art performance — outperforms HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime, with very high recall (often >92%), especially on noisy tweets.  
- Strength: Clear presentation — paper is well-written and logically structured.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail for reproducibility — key regexes, keyword lists, type mappings, and merging rule logic are underspecified.  
- Weakness: Lack of error analysis — missing analysis of failure cases, especially expressions lacking time tokens and other systematic errors.  
- Question: Provide examples of valid time expressions in datasets that do not contain a time token and explain how SynTime handles them.  
- Question: Is the path to further improvement focused on capturing expressions without time tokens or on improving precision for current errors?  
- Question: Clarify type definitions (e.g., which tokens form SEASON) and handling of culturally specific or vague periods (e.g., "Ramadan", "the holiday season").  
- Question: Will you release SynTime source code and the full rule/token lists to support reproducibility?
