- Paper proposes a three-stage rule-based method for temporal expression extraction: identify time tokens, expand boundaries, then merge segments.  
- Method is motivated by two insights: temporal expressions are typically short and usually contain at least one “time token.”  
- Time-token identification uses dictionary lookups, regex matching, and POS tagging; boundary expansion and merging use heuristic rules.  
- Evaluated against rule-based and ML systems on three datasets and shows significant performance improvements.  
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear presentation and well-motivated heuristic rules.  
- Strength: empirical results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed rules.  
- Weakness: paper lacks specificity in places, which may hinder reproducibility.  
- Section 4.1: unclear what the “five seasons” are and how culturally specific or non-standard seasonal expressions (e.g., “Ramadan,” “Holiday Season”) are handled.  
- Section 5.1: discrepancy between text (mentions two benchmark datasets) and experiments (appear to use three datasets) needs clarification.  
- Section 5.2: request for examples and analysis of the ~7% of temporal expressions that lack a time token to show system behavior on these cases.  
- Section 5.2: given 93% of expressions contain a time token and system recall is 92%, authors should discuss plans to handle the remaining cases and improve beyond this apparent ceiling.  
- Reproducibility: ask whether source code, full rule set, and dictionaries will be released to facilitate future research.
