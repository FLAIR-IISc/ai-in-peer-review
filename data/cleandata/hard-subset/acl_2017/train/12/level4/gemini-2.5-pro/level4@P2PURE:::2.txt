This paper presents a simple rule-based method for recognizing temporal expressions in text. The simplicity of this approach is a significant advantage, as temporal expression recognition is a foundational task that often serves as a preliminary step in larger NLP applications. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed system outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning-based methods on this task.

While these results are impressive, my primary concern is the method's generalizability. The rules were designed based on observations from the same corpora that were also used for evaluation. This raises a significant concern that the rules may be over-fitted to the specific characteristics and domains of these test sets. The paper does not provide a statistical analysis or discussion of the overlap in temporal expressions across the development corpora; evidence of significant overlap would have helped to support the claim that the derived rules are general.

Ultimately, a more convincing evaluation would have involved testing the system on a held-out corpus that was completely unseen during the rule design process. Such an experiment would be necessary to truly validate that the proposed method is widely effective.
