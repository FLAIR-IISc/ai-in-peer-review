This paper presents a rule-based approach for temporal expression extraction. The approach is motivated by two key insights: that temporal expressions are typically short and that they almost always contain at least one "time token." The system operates in three stages. First, it identifies time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookups, regular expression matching, and Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging. Next, it expands the boundaries around these seed tokens in both directions, based on a set of heuristic rules, to form candidate time segments. Finally, another set of rules merges these segments into complete temporal expressions. The proposed method is evaluated against both rule-based and machine learning-based systems on three datasets and demonstrates significant performance improvements.

**Strengths:**
The paper is well-written and the proposed approach is presented with excellent clarity. The heuristic rules are well-motivated by empirical observations from the data, and their effectiveness is convincingly demonstrated by the strong evaluation results.

**Weaknesses:**
The primary weakness of the paper is a lack of specificity in some areas, which could hinder the reproducibility of the presented results. Specific points requiring clarification are detailed below.

**Detailed Comments:**

*   **Section 4.1:** The authors mention using a list of five seasons. Could you clarify what these five seasons are? Furthermore, how does the system handle culturally specific or non-standard seasonal expressions, such as "Ramadan" or the "Holiday Season"?

*   **Section 5.1:** The text in this section refers to "two benchmark datasets," but the experiments appear to be conducted on three. Please clarify this discrepancy.

*   **Section 5.2:** The paper notes that 7% of temporal expressions do not contain a time token. To better illustrate how the system handles (or fails to handle) these cases, it would be beneficial to include an example of such an expression and discuss the system's output.

*   **Section 5.2:** The paper reports that 93% of temporal expressions contain a time token, which forms the basis of this approach. With an achieved recall of 92%, the system is already performing close to the theoretical ceiling for this method. What are the authors' plans for future work to address the remaining cases and improve performance further?

*   **Reproducibility:** For the benefit of the community and to facilitate future research, are there any plans to release the source code, the complete set of rules, or the dictionaries used in this work?
