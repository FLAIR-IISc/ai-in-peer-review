This paper presents a rule-based method for the recognition of temporal expressions in text. The simplicity of the approach is a notable advantage, positioning it as a strong analysis tool for applications where time expression recognition is a fundamental process.

Experimental results indicate that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art techniques, including both rule-based and machine learning-based systems for this task.

While the performance is impressive, my main concern relates to the method's generalizability. The rules were crafted based on observations from the same corpora that were subsequently used for evaluation. Consequently, there is a risk that the rules are overfitted to these specific datasets. In a similar vein, the domains represented in these corpora might have biased the rule design.

The paper does not provide statistics or a discussion on the degree of overlap in temporal expressions across the observed corpora. If it could be shown that these expressions are largely consistent across the datasets, this would lend support to the generality of the rules.

Ultimately, the experiments would have been more convincing in demonstrating the method's broad effectiveness if they had been conducted on a new corpus, entirely separate from the one used during the rule design process.
