- SynTime: a lightweight, rule-based time expression recognizer using an "identify and expand" mechanism with three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral).
- Contribution: a simple, effective rule-based system that is interpretable, lightweight, and achieves high recall via syntactic-type-based boundary expansion.
- Contribution: a linguistic analysis of time expressions across news, encyclopedic, and social media texts that motivates the system design.
- Strength: strong empirical performance—state-of-the-art or highly competitive results, significantly outperforming baselines on TimeBank and Tweets.
- Strength: simplicity and efficiency—intuitive heuristics avoid large fixed-pattern rule cascades and enable real-time operation.
- Strength: robust on informal text—large F1 improvement on Tweets, showing resilience to abbreviations and non-standard syntax.
- Weakness: potential overfitting—analysis corpora overlap with evaluation sets, raising concerns that rules were tailored to test data.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of cross-corpus generality—no statistical measures of vocabulary/structure overlap to support claimed universality.
- Weakness: reliance on upstream NLP tools (tokenization, POS); paper lacks error analysis on sensitivity to those tool failures.
- Question: clarify the extent of dataset overlap between the linguistic analysis and final evaluation—was strict separation enforced?
- Question: quantify how much performance gain comes from SUTime-derived initial keyword lists versus the novel heuristic expansion rules.
- Question: provide quantitative overlap metrics (shared time tokens/patterns) across TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets to assess generalizability.
