- Proposes a simple, rule-based method for recognizing time expressions in text.  
- Simplicity is a strong advantage because time expression recognition is foundational for many applications.  
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and machine learning methods.  
- Primary concern: rules were developed from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising significant risk of overfitting.  
- Domain-specific characteristics of the evaluation corpora may have biased rule design, limiting applicability to other domains.  
- Paper lacks statistics or discussion on overlap of temporal expressions across the corpora.  
- An overlap analysis would help support claims of generality.  
- Stronger validation would require experiments on a new, independent corpus not used during rule development.
