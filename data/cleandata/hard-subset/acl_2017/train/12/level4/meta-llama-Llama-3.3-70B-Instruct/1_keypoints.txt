- Proposes a rule-based method for extracting time expressions based on the assumption that such expressions are concise and contain at least one time token.  
- Uses a multi-step pipeline: identify time tokens (dictionary lookup, regex, POS), expand outward via heuristic rules, then consolidate segments into a single expression.  
- Evaluation on three datasets against rule-based and ML baselines reports significant performance improvements.  
- Strength: manuscript is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Strength: rules are empirically grounded and supported by evaluation results.  
- Weakness: underspecified details hinder reproducibility.  
- Issue: unclear reference to "5 seasons" in Section 4.1; recommend considering seasonal references like Ramadan or holiday season.  
- Issue: Section 5.1 incorrectly refers to "two benchmark datasets" rather than three.  
- Suggestion: include an illustrative example that lacks a time token in Section 5.2 for clarity.  
- Concern: with 93% of expressions containing time tokens and 92% recall, authors should explain how to improve beyond this apparent performance ceiling.  
- Request: clarify whether the full rule set or software will be made publicly available.
