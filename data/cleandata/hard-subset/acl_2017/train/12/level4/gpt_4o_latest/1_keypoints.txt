- Paper proposes a rule-based time-expression extractor that finds time tokens (dictionary/regex/POS), expands segments bidirectionally with heuristics, consolidates them, and outperforms baselines on three datasets.
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and the rules are empirically grounded and justified by evaluation results.
- Weakness: methodology is underspecified in places, making reproduction difficult.
- Query (Sec 4.1): rationale for using five “seasons” and how non-standard periods (e.g., Ramadan, “holiday season”) are handled.
- Correction (Sec 5.1): the text should refer to three datasets, not two.
- Request (Sec 5.2): include an example of a time expression that lacks a time token.
- Evaluation concern (Sec 5.2): with 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, how can the approach improve further given an apparent performance ceiling?
- Reproducibility question: will the authors release the full rule set or the software used?
