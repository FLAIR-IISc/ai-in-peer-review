This paper presents a rule-based method for time expression extraction. Its core assumption is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The system first identifies time tokens through a combination of dictionary lookup and regular-expression matching augmented with POS-tag information. It then expands the segment bidirectionally from the seed token according to heuristic boundary rules. Finally, overlapping or adjacent segments are merged into a single time expression using an additional rule set. Experiments against both rule-based and ML systems on three datasets show significant improvements.

- Strengths:
The paper is clearly written and well organized. The rules are grounded in empirical observations of the data and appear well justified by the evaluation results.

- Weaknesses:
Some details are underspecified, which hampers reproducibility. See below for specific questions.

- General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: Why are there five seasons? How do you treat culturally or domain-specific periods such as Ramadan or the “holiday season”?
* Section 5.1: The text refers to “two benchmark datasets,” but results are reported on three datasets. Please clarify.
* Section 5.2: An example of an expression that lacks a time token would be helpful to illustrate how the system behaves in such cases.
* Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, the method appears close to a performance ceiling. How do you plan to improve further?
* Do you plan to release the full set of rules and/or software to facilitate replication?
