- Paper proposes a rule-based temporal expression extractor based on the insight that time expressions are short and usually contain an explicit time token.  
- Method: detect time tokens via dictionary/regex + POS tagging, expand spans with heuristic rules, then merge segments with merging rules.  
- Evaluation on three datasets vs rule-based and ML baselines shows sizable improvements.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Strength: rules are empirically grounded and supported by reported results.  
- Weakness: several implementation/details are under-specified, hindering reproducibility.  
- Question (Sec 4.1): why are there exactly five seasons and how are categories like “Ramadan month” or “holiday season” handled?  
- Error/correction (Sec 5.1): “two benchmark datasets” should read “three datasets.”  
- Request (Sec 5.2): provide an example of a detected expression that lacks a time token.  
- Question (Sec 5.2): given ~93% of expressions contain a time token and system recall ≈92%, how will performance be further improved?  
- Request: will the authors release the complete rule set and/or software implementation?
