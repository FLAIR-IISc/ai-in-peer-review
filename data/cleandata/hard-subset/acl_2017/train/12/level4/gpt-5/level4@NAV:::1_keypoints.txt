- Paper proposes a rule-based time-expression extractor based on the insight that such expressions are short and contain at least one time token.
- Method: identify a time token via dictionary lookup + regex + POS tagging, expand span outward using heuristic stopping rules, then merge spans with additional rules.
- Evaluation reports significant improvements over both rule-based and ML baselines on three datasets.
- Strength: clearly written, well organized, with rules grounded in empirical observations and supported by evaluation.
- Weakness: underspecified details that make reproduction difficult.
- Question (Sec 4.1): Why are there five seasons and how are cases like Ramadan or “holiday season” handled?
- Issue (Sec 5.1): Text says “two benchmark datasets” but evaluation uses three — clarify.
- Request (Sec 5.2): Provide an example of a time expression without a time token.
- Concern: With 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall of 92%, the approach may be near a performance ceiling—how to further improve?
- Question: Is there a plan to release the full rule set and/or the software?
