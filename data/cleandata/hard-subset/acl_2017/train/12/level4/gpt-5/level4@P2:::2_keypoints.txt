- Summary: SynTime is a lightweight, rule-based system using a small set of syntactic types and position-sensitive heuristics to expand time tokens into full time expressions; it outperforms HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime on multiple datasets, especially in recall and on tweets.

- Contribution — practical rule system: Presents a simple, interpretable, fast type-based rule system for time expression recognition.

- Contribution — empirical results: Reports strong improvements over rule-based and learning baselines across TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets, notably higher recall.

- Contribution — analysis-driven design: Provides an analysis of time expressions motivating a small vocabulary and consistent syntactic behavior that informs rule design.

- Strength — simplicity & interpretability: Rules are easy to understand, modify, and use as preprocessing in downstream tasks.

- Strength — empirical performance: Demonstrates superior results on two datasets and competitive results on WikiWars, with excellent tweet performance.

- Strength — practicality: Lightweight, real-time, and extendable via keywords, facilitating deployment and domain adaptation.

- Strength — clear methodology: Type system and boundary-expansion heuristics are clearly described, aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness — potential overfitting/domain leakage: Rules were designed using the same corpora as evaluation, risking overfitting and limiting generality claims.

- Weakness — lack of cross-corpus overlap analysis: Paper does not quantify overlap of time tokens/modifiers/patterns across datasets to support generality claims.

- Weakness — missing truly held-out evaluation: No leave-one-corpus-out or fully held-out corpus evaluation to assess robustness and avoid circularity.

- Question — rule provenance: Which datasets informed initial rule design vs. rule expansion, and how many rules/keywords came from each corpus?

- Question — overlap statistics: What are token/type/pattern overlap measures (e.g., Jaccard) across TimeBank, WikiWars, Gigaword, and Tweets?

- Question — held-out validation: Can you provide leave-one-corpus-out results or tests on a new corpus to demonstrate generality?

- Question — ablations: Can you show performance without POS constraints or without specific modifier types to assess heuristic sensitivity?

- Overall assessment: Elegant, practical approach with convincing gains, but generality claims are weakened by lack of disjoint evaluation and quantitative overlap analysis; held-out cross-domain validation would strengthen the paper.
