The paper presents a method for identifying time expressions in text. The approach is a simple rule-based system, which is advantageous as an analytical tool because time expression recognition is a foundational step in many applications. The experimental results indicate that the proposed method surpasses state-of-the-art rule-based systems and a machine learning–based method for time expression recognition.

The work is strong, but I am concerned about its generality. The rules were crafted based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises the risk of overfitting to those datasets. Likewise, the domains represented in these corpora may have shaped the rule design. The paper does not provide statistics or discussion quantifying the overlap of time expressions across the analyzed corpora. Showing that time expressions largely overlap across these datasets would have supported the rules’ generality. Ideally, the experiments would also include a new corpus not involved in rule development to demonstrate the method’s broader effectiveness.
