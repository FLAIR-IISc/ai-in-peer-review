This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction. The key insight is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The system first identifies time tokens using a combination of dictionary lookup and regular-expression matching augmented with POS-tagging information. It then expands the span in both directions from the time token, guided by a set of heuristic rules, until boundaries are reached. Finally, it merges the resulting segments into a single time expression using another set of rules. Evaluation on three datasets, alongside both rule-based and ML-based systems, shows significant improvements.

- Strengths:
The paper is well written and clearly presented. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and seem well justified, as evidenced by the evaluation.

- Weaknesses:
There are some underspecified details that make it difficult to reproduce the results. See below for details.

- General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: Why are there five seasons? What about periods such as the month of Ramadan or the Holiday Season?
* Section 5.1: should “two benchmark datasets” be “three datasets”?
* Section 5.2: An example without a time token would be helpful.
* Section 5.2: Given that this approach is close to the performance ceiling—since 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system has achieved 92% recall—how do you plan to improve further?
* Is there a plan to release the full set of rules/software used?
