The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions in text. The approach is a simple, rule-based system, which is a strong advantage as an analysis tool, since time expression recognition is a fundamental preprocessing step in many applications. The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based systems as well as a machine-learning-based method for time expression recognition.

This is promising, but I am concerned about the method’s generality. The rules were designed based on observations drawn from the same corpora used for evaluation. As a result, I worry that the rules may overfit these corpora. Similarly, the domains represented in these corpora may have influenced the rule design, limiting the method’s applicability to other domains.

There is no statistical analysis or discussion of the overlap in time expression types across the observed corpora. If the authors could demonstrate that the time expressions in these corpora largely overlap, that would support the generality of the rules and mitigate concerns about overfitting to corpus-specific phenomena.

Ideally, the experiments should have been conducted on an additional, held-out corpus that was not used in the rule-design process, to demonstrate the method’s effectiveness more broadly. A clear separation between the data used to derive the rules and the data used to evaluate them would strengthen the claims of generality and robustness.
