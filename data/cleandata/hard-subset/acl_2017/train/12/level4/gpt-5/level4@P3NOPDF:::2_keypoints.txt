- Proposes a simple, transparent rule-based method for recognizing time expressions in text.
- Strength: design is clear and interpretable, aiding error analysis and pipeline integration.
- Strength: empirical results reportedly outperform state-of-the-art rule systems and strong ML baselines.
- Strength: practical relevance—robust, interpretable time recognition is valuable for NLP tasks.
- Concern: rules may be overfit to the corpora used for design, limiting domain-independence and portability.
- Concern: paper lacks quantitative overlap/coverage analysis of time expression types across corpora.
- Concern: experimental design does not evaluate on a corpus uninvolved in rule development, risking inflated results.
- Suggestion: perform leave-one-corpus-out experiments to test generalization across datasets.
- Suggestion: evaluate on an external, truly unseen test set from a different domain.
- Suggestion: report coverage/overlap stats and per-category precision/recall (dates, times, durations, sets).
- Suggestion: provide error analysis on out-of-domain, rare, and ambiguous cases to guide robust extensions.
- Suggestion: clarify rule development protocol to avoid data leakage (e.g., rule templates, corpus lineage, tuning logs).
- Suggestion: test robustness under domain shift (noise, tokenization, punctuation variations).
- Overall: solid, interpretable approach with strong in-domain results, but needs rigorous cross-domain evaluation and analyses to support generality claims.
