- Proposes a simple rule-based method for recognizing time expressions, valuable as an analysis tool and preprocessing step.  
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based systems and a machine-learning baseline.  
- Major concern about generality: rules were designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, risking overfitting.  
- Domains represented in those corpora may have biased rule design, limiting cross-domain applicability.  
- No statistical analysis of overlap in time-expression types across corpora; such analysis could support rule generality.  
- Recommends testing on an additional held-out corpus not used in rule design to validate broader effectiveness.  
- Calls for a clear separation between data used to derive rules and data used for evaluation to strengthen claims of robustness.
