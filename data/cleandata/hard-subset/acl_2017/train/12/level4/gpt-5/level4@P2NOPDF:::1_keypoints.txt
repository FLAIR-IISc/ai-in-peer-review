- Paper proposes a rule-based time-expression extractor that assumes expressions are short and contain a time token; it detects seed tokens via dictionary/regex+POS, expands bidirectionally with boundary heuristics, and merges adjacent/overlapping segments.

- Experiments report significant improvements over both rule-based and ML baselines on three datasets.

- Strength: paper is clearly written and rules are empirically grounded and supported by evaluation.

- Weakness: several implementation/details are underspecified, hindering reproducibility.

- Question: Why are there exactly five seasons in the model, and how are culturally or domain-specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, “holiday season”) handled?

- Clarification needed: text refers to “two benchmark datasets” but results are reported on three—please reconcile.

- Request: provide an example of an expression that lacks a time token to illustrate system behavior in such cases.

- Concern: with 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, the method may be near a performance ceiling—how will improvements be achieved?

- Ask: do the authors plan to release the full rule set and/or software to enable replication?
