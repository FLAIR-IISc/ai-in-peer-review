- Proposes a simple rule-based method for time expression recognition; simplicity aids analysis and practical use.
- Time expression recognition is a fundamental preprocessing step for many applications.
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based systems and a machine-learning baseline.
- Main concern: rules were designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising generality questions.
- This creates a risk of overfitting to the evaluated datasets.
- The specific domains of the corpora may have biased rule design.
- The paper provides no statistics or discussion on overlap of time expressions across the corpora.
- Showing high overlap would strengthen the claim of generality.
- Recommendation: run experiments on a new, independent corpus to validate broad effectiveness.
