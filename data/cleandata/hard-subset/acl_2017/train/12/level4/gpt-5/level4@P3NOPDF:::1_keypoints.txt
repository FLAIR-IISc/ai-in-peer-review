- Summary: Proposes a rule-based time expression extractor that identifies salient “time tokens,” expands spans heuristically, and merges overlaps, showing strong gains on three datasets.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and simple, interpretable pipeline grounded in empirical properties of temporal mentions.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results with significant improvements across multiple datasets, suggesting robustness.  
- Strength: Practical and computationally light approach emphasizing high-precision token detection followed by constrained expansion.  
- Strength: Generally well written and presented.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns from under-specified components (lexicons, regexes, POS tagger choice, exact heuristics).  
- Weakness: Unclear coverage for seasonal and culturally specific temporal expressions (e.g., Ramadan, holiday season).  
- Weakness: Potential ceiling effects—paper lacks analysis of the remaining ~1% gap between token-containing gold mentions (~93%) and system recall (~92%).  
- Weakness: Minor inconsistency in reporting dataset count (mentions both two and three datasets).  
- Seasons question: Clarify why five seasons are listed, how seasonal expressions are defined/normalized, and whether culturally/domain-specific seasons are covered in lexicons/rules.  
- Datasets question: Reconcile “two” vs “three” datasets, list datasets with sizes/domains/splits, and state whether a development set was used for tuning.  
- Examples request: Include annotated examples of correct identifications and at least one gold mention lacking a canonical time token to illustrate boundaries and failure modes.  
- Error/ceiling analysis: Explain residual errors (boundary, missed token, merge) and strategies to handle token-less mentions (lexicon expansion, character/subword patterns, lightweight learned boundary classifier, contextual cues).  
- Rules/merging demand: Provide precise descriptions or pseudocode for expansion stopping criteria, conflict resolution, merge priorities, and handling nested/ coordinated/prepositional structures.  
- Evaluation details: Specify matching metrics (strict/relaxed), micro/macro reporting, per-dataset precision/recall/F1, per-category breakdown, normalization vs. span-only evaluation, ablations, and statistical significance tests.  
- Practical aspects: Report domain transferability (news/clinical/social media), whether dictionaries were domain-tuned, runtime/throughput, and an error categorization for actionable insights.  
- Reproducibility/release: Release regexes, dictionaries (sources/versions), POS tagger/version, preprocessing, rule order, dataset splits, tuning procedure, or at least a small dev config/seed rules.  
- Licensing question: State whether and under what license the rules/software will be released, and what documentation will accompany it.  
- Overall assessment: Well-motivated, effective, and practical rule-based system that needs clearer specification, error analysis, ablations, and handling of culturally specific expressions to increase impact and reproducibility.
