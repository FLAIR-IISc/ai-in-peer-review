- Paper proposes a rule-based method: detect a “time token” (dictionary/regex+POS), expand left/right with heuristics, then merge adjacent segments into time expressions; shows significant improvements on three datasets.
- Strength: paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Strength: rules are motivated by empirical observations and supported by evaluation results.
- Weakness: some implementation and rule details are underspecified, hindering reproducibility.
- Question: Why assume five seasons—what are they and how are culturally/contextually defined periods (e.g., Ramadan, “Holiday Season”) handled?
- Issue: Section 5.1 refers to “two benchmark datasets,” but the paper elsewhere mentions three—needs consistency/clarification.
- Request: provide an illustrative example of a time expression that does not contain a time token.
- Concern: with 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, the approach may be near a performance ceiling—how can it be improved further?
- Reproducibility ask: will the authors release the full rule set and/or software?
