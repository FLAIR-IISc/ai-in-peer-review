This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction. The key insight is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The system first identifies a time token using a combination of dictionary lookup and regular-expression matching augmented with POS-tagging information. It then expands the time segment in either direction from the token until stopping conditions defined by a set of heuristic rules are met. Finally, it merges time segments into a single time expression using another set of rules. Evaluation comparing this approach with both rule-based and ML-based systems on three datasets shows significant improvements.

- Strengths:
  - The paper is well written and clearly presented.
  - The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear well justified, as evidenced by the evaluation.

- Weaknesses:
  - Some aspects are underspecified, making it difficult to reproduce the results. See below for details.

- General Discussion:
  - Section 4.1: Why are there five seasons? How are cases such as “Ramadan” or “holiday season” handled?
  - Section 5.1: Should “two benchmark datasets” be “three datasets”?
  - Section 5.2: An example of a correctly extracted expression that does not contain a time token would be helpful.
  - Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, this approach appears close to the performance ceiling. How do you plan to improve further?
  - Is there a plan to release the full set of rules and/or the software used?
