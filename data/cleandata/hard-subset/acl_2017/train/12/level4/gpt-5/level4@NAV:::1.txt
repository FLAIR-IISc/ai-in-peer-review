Review- This paper presents a rule-based method for extracting time expressions. Its central insight is that such expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The system first identifies a time token using a combination of dictionary lookup and regular-expression matching augmented with POS tagging. It then expands the candidate span outward from the token in both directions according to heuristic rules that determine stopping conditions. Finally, it merges the resulting spans into a single time expression using another set of rules. Evaluation against both rule-based and ML-based systems on three datasets shows significant improvements.

- Strengths:
It is clearly written and well organized. The rules are grounded in empirical observations of the data and appear well justified by the evaluation.

- Weaknesses:
There are underspecifications that make reproducing the results difficult. See details below.

- General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: Why are there five seasons? How are cases like the month of Ramadan or the “holiday season” handled?
* Section 5.1: “two benchmark datasets” => “three datasets”?
* Section 5.2: An example without a time token would be helpful.
* Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, this approach may be near a performance ceiling; how do you plan to improve further?
* Is there a plan to release the full set of rules and/or the software?
