The paper presents a method to recognize time expressions in text. It is a straightforward rule-based approach, which is a strong advantage as an analysis tool since time expression recognition should be a fundamental process in applications. Experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based methods and a machine-learning-based method for time expression recognition.

The work is strong, but I am concerned about the method’s generality. The rules were crafted based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises the risk that the rules overfit these corpora. Similarly, the domains represented by these corpora may have influenced the rule design. There is no statistical analysis or discussion demonstrating the degree of overlap in time expressions across the observed corpora. If it were shown that time expressions in these corpora mostly overlap, that evidence would support the generality of the rules.

Ideally, the experiments should have been conducted on a new corpus independent of the rule design process to demonstrate that the proposed method is broadly effective.
