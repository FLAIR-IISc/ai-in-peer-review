This paper presents a method for recognizing temporal expressions in text. The approach is a straightforward rule-based system, which is appealing as an analysis tool because temporal expression recognition is a fundamental preprocessing step in many applications. The reported experimental results indicate that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based systems as well as a machine-learning-based baseline.

While the results are encouraging, I have concerns about the method’s generality. The rules appear to have been designed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises the risk of overfitting to those datasets and their specific domains. The paper does not provide statistics or discussion on the degree of overlap in temporal expression types across the examined corpora. Demonstrating substantial overlap would strengthen the claim that the rules capture broadly applicable patterns rather than corpus-specific idiosyncrasies.

To better support claims of broad effectiveness, I recommend evaluating the system on a new, held-out corpus that was not involved in rule development, preferably from a different domain. This would more convincingly demonstrate the method’s robustness and generalizability.
