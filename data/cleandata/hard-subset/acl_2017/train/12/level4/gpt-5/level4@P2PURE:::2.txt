The paper proposes a method for recognizing time expressions in text. The approach is simple and rule-based, which is a strong advantage as an analysis tool, since time expression recognition should be a basic process in applications. The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based methods and a machine learning-based method for time expression recognition.

The results are promising, but my main concern is the method’s generality. The rules were designed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising the risk of overfitting to these corpora. Likewise, the domains of these corpora may have influenced the rule design. There is no statistical analysis or discussion of the overlap in time expressions across the observed corpora. Demonstrating that time expressions in these corpora largely overlap would have supported the generality of the rules. In any case, it would have been better to conduct experiments on a new corpus that was not used during rule design to show that the proposed method is broadly effective.
