- Paper proposes a rule-based time expression extractor based on the insight that time expressions are short and usually contain at least one time token.
- System pipeline: identify a time token via dictionary+regex augmented with POS tags, expand left/right using heuristic stopping rules, then merge segments with additional rules.
- Evaluation on three datasets shows significant improvements over prior rule-based and ML systems.
- Strength: paper is well written and clearly presented.
- Strength: rules are empirically motivated and justified by evaluation results.
- Weakness: some aspects are underspecified, making reproduction difficult.
- Question: why are there five seasons defined, and how are cases like “Ramadan” or “holiday season” handled?
- Question: Section 5.1 may misreport dataset count — should “two benchmark datasets” be “three datasets”?
- Request: provide an example of a correctly extracted expression that does not contain a time token.
- Concern: with 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, this approach may be near a performance ceiling — how will improvements be achieved?
- Request: will the full rule set and/or software be released for replication?
