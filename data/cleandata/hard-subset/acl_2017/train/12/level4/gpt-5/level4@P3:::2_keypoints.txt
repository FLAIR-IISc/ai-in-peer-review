- Introduces SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using a small set of syntactic types and boundary-expansion heuristics.  
- Contributions: type-based syntactic design with position-sensitive heuristics enabling flexibility and real-time performance.  
- Contributions: empirical multi-corpus analysis supporting a “least-effort” hypothesis for time expression use.  
- Contributions: strong empirical results, outperforming or matching baseline systems on several benchmarks with high recall.  
- Strength: simple, linguistically grounded, transparent design with intuitive type definitions and inspectable rules.  
- Strength: strong and consistent performance, notably very high recall and robustness on informal text (Tweets).  
- Strength: lightweight, fast, and extensible; small targeted expansions (SynTime-E) improve recall.  
- Strength: thorough comparisons to HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime with clear reporting of strict/relaxed metrics.  
- Weakness: potential overfitting—rules and expansions derived from the same datasets used for evaluation, risking tuned results.  
- Weakness: Tweets dataset likely biased by SUTime pre-selection, which may underrepresent hard cases and affect fairness.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis and ablation studies—insufficient insight into failure modes and contribution of individual heuristics.  
- Weakness: dependence on POS tagging with little quantification of its impact or exploration of mitigations.  
- Weakness: practical scope limited by absence of normalization component or interface to normalizers.  
- Question: Were tokens/expansions added by inspecting the same data used for evaluation, and can held-out-split results be provided?  
- Question: Can cross-domain generalization be demonstrated with an additional corpus or leave-one-corpus-out evaluation?  
- Question: Please report vocabulary overlap across datasets and proportion of test expressions covered by token sets learned elsewhere.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to POS tagging errors, and do Twitter-specific taggers or POS-agnostic fallbacks change performance?  
- Question: What are the contributions of each modifier type and boundary-expansion rule—can you provide an ablation?  
- Question: Provide runtime/memory numbers, release plans for code/lexicons/expansions, and clarify licensing for reused SUTime regexes.
