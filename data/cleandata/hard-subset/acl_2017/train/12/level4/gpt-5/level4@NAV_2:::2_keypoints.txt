- Presents a simple rule-based method for identifying time expressions, useful as an analytical/foundational tool.
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based systems and a machine-learning baseline.
- Strength of the work acknowledged, but concern about generality due to rules crafted from the same corpora used for evaluation (risk of overfitting).
- Rule design may be biased by the domains represented in the evaluation corpora.
- Paper lacks statistics or discussion quantifying overlap of time expressions across the analyzed corpora, which would support generality.
- Experiments should ideally include a new, held-out corpus not used in rule development to demonstrate broader effectiveness.
