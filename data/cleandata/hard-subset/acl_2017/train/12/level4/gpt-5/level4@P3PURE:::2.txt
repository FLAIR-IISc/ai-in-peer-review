This paper proposes a simple rule-based method for recognizing time expressions in text. Its simplicity is an advantage for analysis and practical use, given that time expression recognition is a fundamental preprocessing step in many applications. The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based approaches and a machine-learning-based method for time expression recognition.

My main concern is the method’s generality. The rules were designed based on observations from the same corpora used for evaluation, which raises the risk of overfitting to these datasets. The specific domains of these corpora may also have influenced the rule design. Moreover, the paper provides neither statistics nor discussion regarding the overlap of time expressions across the observed corpora. If it were shown that time expressions in these corpora largely overlap, that would strengthen the claim that the rules are general.

It would be preferable to include experiments on a new corpus, distinct from those used during rule design, to demonstrate that the proposed method is broadly effective beyond the observed datasets and domains.
