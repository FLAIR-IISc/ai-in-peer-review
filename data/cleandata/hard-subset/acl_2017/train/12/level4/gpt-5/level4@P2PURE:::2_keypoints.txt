- Paper proposes a simple, rule-based method for recognizing time expressions in text.
- Simplicity and rule-based design are strengths for use as an analysis tool.
- Experimental results claim the method outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and a machine-learning baseline.
- Results are promising but main concern is the method’s generality.
- Rules were designed from the same corpora used for evaluation, creating a risk of overfitting.
- Corpus domains may have biased rule design; no analysis of domain influence is provided.
- No statistical analysis or discussion of overlap in time expressions across corpora to support generality.
- Authors should test on a new, unseen corpus to demonstrate broader effectiveness.
