This paper presents a rule-based approach to time expression extraction. The core insight is that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one “time token.” The system first identifies a time token via a combination of dictionary lookup and regular-expression matching augmented with POS tags. It then expands the segment in both directions from the time token until heuristic stopping conditions are met. Finally, it merges adjacent segments into a single time expression using an additional set of rules. Evaluations against both rule-based and ML-based systems on three datasets show significant improvements.

- Strengths:
The paper is clearly written and well organized. The rules are motivated by empirical observations of the data and appear well justified by the evaluation results.

- Weaknesses:
Some aspects are underspecified, which makes reproducing the results difficult. See details below.

- General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: Why do you assume five seasons? What exactly are they? How do you handle culturally or contextually defined periods such as Ramadan or the “Holiday Season”?
* Section 5.1: You refer to “two benchmark datasets.” Should this be “three datasets,” consistent with the rest of the paper?
* Section 5.2: An illustrative example of an expression that does not contain a time token would be helpful.
* Section 5.2: Given that 93% of expressions contain a time token and the system achieves 92% recall, this seems near a performance ceiling. How do you plan to improve further?
* Do you plan to release the full set of rules and/or the software to facilitate reproducibility?
