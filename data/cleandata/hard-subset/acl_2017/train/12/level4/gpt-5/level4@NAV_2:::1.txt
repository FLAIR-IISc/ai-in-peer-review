This paper presents a rule-based method for extracting temporal expressions. Its central insight is that time expressions are usually short and typically include at least one explicit time token. The approach first detects a time token via dictionary/regex matches augmented with POS tagging, then expands the token’s span in both directions using simple heuristic rules, and finally merges the resulting segments into complete time expressions using a separate set of merging rules. Evaluation against both rule-based and ML-based systems on three datasets demonstrates sizable improvements.

- Strengths:
It is clearly written and easy to follow. The rules are grounded in empirical analysis of the data and are well supported by the reported results.

- Weaknesses:
Certain aspects are under-specified, making it hard to reproduce the findings. See details below.

- General Discussion:
* Section 4.1: Why are there five seasons? How are categories like “Ramadan month” or “holiday season” treated?
* Section 5.1: “two benchmark datasets” should read “three datasets”?
* Section 5.2: An example of a detected expression that lacks a time token would be informative.
* Section 5.2: Given that ~93% of expressions contain a time token and the system’s recall is ~92%, how do you intend to push performance further?
* Will you release the complete rule set and/or software implementation?
