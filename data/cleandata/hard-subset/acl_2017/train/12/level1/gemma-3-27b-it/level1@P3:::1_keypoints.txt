- Contribution: Empirical analysis showing time expressions are short, low-vocabulary, and follow syntactic patterns across datasets.  
- Contribution: Type-based rule design using syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple positional heuristics.  
- Contribution: Strong, competitive performance on benchmarks and notably improved results on the challenging Tweets dataset.

- Strength: Clear motivation and justification linked to Zipf’s principle, supporting a lightweight rule-based approach.  
- Strength: Simple, transparent, and explainable system that is easy to understand and extend.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results with both strict and relaxed match evaluations; especially robust on Tweets.  
- Strength: Efficient, real-time implementation suitable for streaming or immediate-processing applications.  
- Strength: Well-structured, clear presentation of method and experiments.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—still a rule-based system with inherent brittleness and generalization limits.  
- Weakness: Heavy dependence on POS tagging accuracy; impact of POS errors is not quantified.  
- Weakness: Expansion strategy’s practical impact and scalability unclear given few added rules.  
- Weakness: Insufficient error analysis—lack of detailed breakdown of false positives/negatives and failure patterns.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to recent neural sequence-labeling baselines adapted for time expression recognition.

- Q: Please quantify the impact of POS tagging errors on SynTime’s performance (e.g., with artificial POS noise).  
- Q: What criteria and human effort are required to select keywords/rules during expansion and for new domains?  
- Q: What specific types of errors does SynTime make—examples of common misses and false positives?  
- Q: How does SynTime compare to recent neural sequence-labeling models for time expression recognition?  
- Q: Why was Gigaword excluded—please justify the decision about its label accuracy.

- Additional: Reference list is extensive.  
- Additional: SUTime GitHub link is helpful.  
- Additional: Add a table summarizing regular expressions for each syntactic type to aid reproducibility.

- Overall: Solid contribution with strong empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
