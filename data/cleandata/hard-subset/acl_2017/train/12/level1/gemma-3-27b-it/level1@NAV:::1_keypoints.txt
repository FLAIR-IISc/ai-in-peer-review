- SynTime is a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple heuristics, shifting from semantic to syntactic typing.  
- Authors observe time expressions are typically short, use limited vocabulary, and show consistent syntactic behavior across datasets.  
- SynTime outperforms baselines on TimeBank and especially Tweets, and is comparable on WikiWars, indicating robustness on noisy text.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, notably on real-world noisy Tweets.  
- Strength: insightful data analysis and novel use of the “least effort” principle to motivate design.  
- Strength: lightweight, expandable rule-based design with easy keyword-based adaptation and real-time performance.  
- Strength: clear, well-articulated contribution in moving from semantic to syntactic typing.  
- Weakness: limited novelty concern since approach remains rule-based and may be brittle compared to deep learning methods.  
- Weakness: dependence on accurate POS tagging creates a potential bottleneck; impact not quantified.  
- Weakness: missing detailed error analysis—no breakdown of frequent failure modes or difficult expression types.  
- Question: quantify the impact of POS tagging errors (e.g., controlled noisy POS experiments).  
- Question: what strategies beyond keyword addition can improve robustness to language style and grammar variation?  
- Question: how does the rule-set complexity scale with increasing size and diversity of data?
