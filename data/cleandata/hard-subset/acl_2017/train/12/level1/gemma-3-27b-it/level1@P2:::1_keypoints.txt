- Dataset analysis finds time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, and show consistent syntactic behavior, justifying simplification.
- Proposes a type-based rule design (time token, modifier, numeral) as an alternative to semantic-based rules.
- SynTime reports competitive performance, outperforming prior methods on TimeBank and Tweets.

- Strong empirical motivation from the dataset analysis supporting design choices.
- Methodology is clearly and concisely explained, especially the type-based vs semantic-based distinction.
- Simple heuristic rules enable real-time performance and easy extensibility.
- Evaluation is comprehensive across TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets, compared against strong baselines.
- Practical discussion highlights limited need for complex ML features and easy expansion.

- Rule-based approach has limited novelty beyond the type-based framing and needs clearer differentiation from prior systems and boundary expansion specifics.
- Reliance on POS tagging is a potential weakness; sensitivity to tagging errors is not fully investigated.
- Manual keyword expansion may become a bottleneck for new domains; lacks automated keyword discovery.
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis and examples of common failure modes.

- Please clarify advantages and failure cases of the boundary expansion heuristic versus other expansion techniques.
- How sensitive is SynTime to POS tagger errors, and have mitigation strategies been tried?
- How were SynTime-E expansion keywords selected, what made them “generic,” and could selection be biased?
- Provide example errors on test sets and explanations for these mistakes.

- Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Table 4 is hard to read; highlight best results (e.g., bold) for clarity.

- Overall: solid, well-presented paper with valuable contributions; recommended for acceptance despite noted limitations.
