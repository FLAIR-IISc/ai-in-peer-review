- Paper proposes SynTime, a type-based rule system for time expression recognition that leverages short length, limited vocabulary, and consistent syntactic behavior, defining three types and heuristic rules, and reports competitive results on three datasets.

Contributions:
- Empirically analyzes time-expression characteristics (length, vocabulary, syntax) across datasets to motivate the approach.
- Introduces a syntactic type system (time token, modifier, numeral) to simplify rule design by focusing on type relations.
- Presents an effective, lightweight, real-time system (SynTime) that achieves competitive or superior performance on benchmarks.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated design with clear rationale linking observations to the method.
- Solid experimental evaluation across multiple datasets including informal Tweets and an ablation study.
- Clear, easy-to-follow methodology and illustrative figures explaining rule construction and boundary expansion.
- Practical emphasis on easy expansion and real-time applicability for different domains.
- Demonstrates competitive results, notably strong on the challenging Tweet dataset.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: still fundamentally rule-based and may struggle with unseen variations despite type-based simplification.
- Relies on Stanford POS Tagger, introducing error propagation and portability concerns.
- Lacks detailed error analysis identifying specific failure modes.
- Tweet dataset is small, raising concerns about generalizability of Tweet-domain results.
- Missing comparisons to recent deep-learning-based time expression recognition methods.

Questions to authors:
- Provide a detailed error analysis identifying common error types and their causes.
- How sensitive is SynTime to POS tagging errors, and have alternative taggers or integrated tagging been evaluated?
- Explain the process and criteria for selecting expansion keywords and avoiding added ambiguity.
- How does SynTime compare experimentally to recent deep-learning models for time expression recognition?
- Does the type-based approach generalize to languages other than English, and what adaptations are needed?

Additional comments:
- Figure 3 notation is helpful for understanding extraction.
- Reference list is comprehensive and well-formatted.
- Description of Twitter word clusters strengthens the motivation for handling informal text.
