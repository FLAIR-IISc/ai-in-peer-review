- Proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using three syntactic types and boundary expansion heuristics.  
- Achieves strong, efficient performance by framing the task around syntactic types rather than complex semantics, and is easily extensible.  
- Provides empirical validation across diverse datasets (TimeBank, Tweets) and a linguistic analysis linking design to the "principle of least effort."  

- Elegant simplicity: simple heuristics and syntactic framing yield state-of-the-art or competitive results (e.g., 92.47% strict F1 on TimeBank).  
- Robust on informal text: strong performance on Tweets (91.77% strict F1), showing resilience to noisy, short social media language.  
- High recall and extensibility: consistently high recall across datasets and easy gains by adding keywords (SynTime-E vs SynTime-I).  

- Novelty is overstated: initialization relies on SUTime regexes, making it unclear how much improvement comes from new rule logic versus reused curated resources.  
- Limited on complex expressions: simple boundary expansion struggles with descriptive or preposition-heavy temporal phrases (weaker on WikiWars).  
- Dependent on upstream POS tagging accuracy: success hinges on an external POS tagger, but the impact is not quantified or analyzed.
