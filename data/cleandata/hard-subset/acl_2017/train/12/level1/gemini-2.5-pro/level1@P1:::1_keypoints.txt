- SynTime is a lightweight, rule-based time expression recognizer that finds core "time tokens" (via regex/POS), heuristically expands boundaries, and merges segments to produce final time expressions.  
- Main contributions: a simple, transparent TER system achieving strong results, and an empirical analysis showing time expressions are short, use a small core vocabulary, and almost always contain a time token.  
- Excellent empirical performance: SynTime-E achieves state-of-the-art F1 (e.g., 92.47 on TimeBank, 91.77 on Tweets) with very high recall.  
- Simplicity, efficiency, and extensibility: three intuitive stages, real-time operation, and easy domain adaptation by adding few keywords.  
- Robust performance on informal text: strong results on Tweets demonstrate robustness to noisy social media data.  
- Limited methodological novelty: approach follows established anchor-keyword + rule expansion patterns, and the "syntactic types" distinction may be superficial compared to semantic typing.  
- Heavy reliance on SUTime resources: initial token lists/regex for SynTime-I were collected from SUTime, which complicates claims of originality.  
- Lack of in-depth error analysis: paper only mentions generic tokenization/POS errors and lacks detailed failure-mode analysis (e.g., complex compositional expressions).  
- Question: clarify how "syntactic types" differ in practice and why they lead to better rule design than semantic token types used by systems like SUTime.  
- Question: quantify the extent of borrowing from SUTime (were time token and modifier lists taken directly, and how much lexicon was reused?).  
- Question: describe the Tweets dataset annotation process, annotator count, and whether inter-annotator agreement was measured.
