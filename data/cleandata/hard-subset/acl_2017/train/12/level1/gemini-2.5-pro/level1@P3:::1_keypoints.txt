- SynTime is a lightweight, rule-based system that recognizes time expressions using an "anchor-and-expand" heuristic based on syntactic token types.  
- It labels tokens by syntactic function (PREFIX, SUFFIX, etc.), finds anchor time tokens, expands boundaries to include modifiers/numerals, and merges segments.  
- Evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets, SynTime outperforms rule-based and ML baselines, especially on noisy Tweets with higher recall.

- Contribution: a simple, effective rule-based architecture showing anchor-and-expand outperforms more complex systems.  
- Contribution: formalizes syntactic token types for rules, simplifying rule creation and increasing flexibility.  
- Contribution: presents strong empirical results across news, encyclopedic, and social media domains, notably state-of-the-art on Tweets.

- Strength: very strong empirical performance (high F1 and recall), especially on informal text.  
- Strength: simple, elegant, efficient method that is easy to implement and runs in real-time.  
- Strength: design is data-driven, justified by corpus analysis showing short, anchored time expressions.  
- Strength: generalizes well to noisy, informal text where other systems and ML struggle.

- Weakness: potential unfairness since SynTime-I uses token regexes collected from SUTime, conflating lexicon and engine contributions.  
- Weakness: claimed novelty of syntactic types may be overstated because prior systems already use positional/token-sequence rules.  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative error analysis to explain lower precision in some settings and failure modes of boundary expansion.  
- Weakness: SynTime-E’s manual expansion process is subjective and under-specified, harming replicability and generality claims.

- Question: can the authors isolate the rule-engine contribution by building a lexicon from TimeBank training data and comparing to SUTime?  
- Question: can the authors provide example errors, especially those causing lower precision in relaxed matching, to illustrate failure modes?  
- Question: please clarify the intuition and examples for the COMMA-merge heuristic and why its three conditions are necessary.
