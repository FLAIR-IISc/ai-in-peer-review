- SynTime: a rule-based system for time expression recognition using syntactic token types (Time Token, Modifier, Numeral) and a three-step pipeline (identify anchors, expand boundaries, merge segments).
- Evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and a Twitter dataset, outperforming prior rule-based and ML baselines, especially in recall.
- Contribution: a simple, effective rule-based architecture combining syntactic typing with heuristic boundary-expansion rules.
- Contribution: an empirical analysis of time-expression properties (short length, small vocabulary, anchor tokens) motivating the design.
- Contribution: strong performance on informal text (Twitter), showing robustness beyond newswire.
- Strength: achieves high empirical results and state-of-the-art F1 on standard benchmarks with consistently high recall.
- Strength: conceptually simple, transparent, efficient, and likely easy to debug, maintain, and extend.
- Strength: design is well-motivated by data-driven observations about time expression structure.
- Strength: novel rule-based design that defines token types by syntactic behavior rather than semantic classes.
- Weakness: SynTime-I is bootstrapped from SUTime’s token regexes, making it hard to isolate the novel rule engine’s contribution.
- Weakness: implementation details of segment identification and merging are described informally, reducing reproducibility.
- Weakness: scope limited to recognition only (no normalization), so comparisons to end-to-end systems are incomplete.
- Weakness: SynTime-E’s manual lexicon expansion is unsystematic and subjective, with no detailed error analysis of what was missed then fixed.
- Question: quantify reliance on SUTime (what fraction of initial token regexes came from SUTime) and performance with a basic lexicon.
- Question: provide examples of tokens added in SynTime-E (e.g., the 16 time tokens and 19 modifiers for WikiWars) and the expressions they enabled.
