- Observation: Time expressions are very short (80% ≤3 tokens), use a small time-related vocabulary, ≥93% contain a time token, and show similar syntactic behavior across datasets.  
- Contribution: Systematic cross-dataset analysis supporting a "least effort" principle in time expression usage.  
- Contribution: Introduces a syntactic type system (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) distinct from prior semantic-type rule systems.  
- Contribution: Proposes SynTime, a simple rule-based recognizer yielding high recall and strong empirical results, especially on Tweets.  
- Strength: Thorough, statistically supported analysis across four diverse datasets with an insightful link to Zipf's principle.  
- Strength: Novel syntactic-type approach and simple boundary-expansion heuristics that improve flexibility over prior rule systems.  
- Strength: Strong experimental performance (92–95% recall), significant F1 gains on Tweets, and effective small-domain expansion.  
- Strength: Practical and lightweight method claimed to run in real time and easy to adapt with few additional tokens.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty overall since the approach remains rule-based and differs mainly in syntactic vs. semantic typing.  
- Weakness: Evaluation scope/depth is limited (three datasets, small Tweets set), lacks comparisons to recent methods and runtime measurements.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of robustness to tokenization/POS errors and missing discussion of failure cases (complex/ambiguous expressions).  
- Weakness: Connection to Zipf's principle is superficial and not developed into deeper theoretical insights or future directions.  
- Question: Please provide runtime measurements and baseline comparisons to substantiate the "real time" claim.  
- Question: Clarify annotation consistency with TimeML for the small Tweets dataset and whether its characteristics inflated performance.  
- Question: Provide broader evidence that "formal words" prevalence (e.g., "today") generalizes across other time expressions.  
- Overall: Strong empirical and practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen evaluation, runtime reporting, and discussion of limitations.
