- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic patterns across datasets.
- Analyzes TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets to derive empirical patterns.
- Observation 1: Time expressions are typically very short (80% ≤ 3 tokens).
- Observation 2: Time expressions use a small, consistent vocabulary across domains.
- Observation 3: Over 93% of time expressions contain explicit time tokens.
- Observation 4: Words in time expressions show similar syntactic behaviors.
- Defines three syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and uses boundary expansion heuristics.
- Contribution: Empirical analysis supporting “least effort” consistency in time expressions.
- Contribution: SynTime — a lightweight, real-time rule-based tagger that outperforms SOTA on TimeBank and Tweets (91.74% F1 on Tweets).
- Contribution: Shows small manual keyword additions (3–16) can boost domain-specific performance.
- Strength: Compelling cross-domain empirical analysis revealing consistent patterns.
- Strength: Strong performance on informal Twitter data, with 95.87% recall and real-time efficiency.
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of POS tagging errors and their impact (e.g., Stanford POS mistakes).
- Weakness: Limited baseline comparisons (only three methods), missing newer approaches.
- Weakness: Claim of simplicity is undermined by manual configuration of 21 syntactic types.
- Question: How does SynTime handle complex expressions like “the day after tomorrow” that defy observed patterns?
- Question: What challenges arise when applying this syntactic-rule approach to languages with different syntax?
