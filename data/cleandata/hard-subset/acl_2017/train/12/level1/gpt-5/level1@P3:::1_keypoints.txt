- Paper proposes SynTime: a lightweight rule-based temporal tagger using “syntactic types” and boundary-expansion heuristics, evaluated on TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets.
- Main contribution 1: Simple boundary-expansion rules that yield strong recall and competitive SOTA performance, especially on tweets.
- Main contribution 2: Empirical analysis of temporal expressions across four corpora motivating short-span, small-lexicon, syntactic-regularity design choices.
- Main contribution 3: Practical, extensible rule/lexicon resource (based on SUTime plus small manual additions) intended for real-time use.
- Strength: Achieves top or near-top F1 and consistently highest recall across datasets, with exceptional tweet performance (strict F1 ~91.7).
- Strength: Transparent, easy-to-implement heuristics and merging logic that handle many common real cases (commas, ranges).
- Strength: Sensible syntactic-type factoring (time tokens, PREFIX/SUFFIX/LINKAGE, numerals) that generalizes across domains.
- Strength: Low manual expansion cost and demonstrated portability (improvements from SynTime-I to SynTime-E on WikiWars).
- Strength: Empirical analyses (length distribution, token coverage, POS) support the token-first plus local-expansion approach.
- Weakness: Novelty is limited relative to SUTime/HeidelTime and not rigorously demonstrated; distinction of “syntactic types” is not quantified.
- Weakness: Comparisons may be unfair because SynTime uses SUTime token regexes plus additions while baseline SUTime/HeidelTime were not equivalently tuned.
- Weakness: Tweet dataset selection prefiltered by SUTime, risking bias by underrepresenting expressions SUTime misses.
- Weakness: “Real-time” and “light-weight” claims lack runtime, memory, or throughput evidence.
- Weakness: Limited systematic error analysis and no ablation quantifying contributions of POS filtering, expansion, and merging rules.
- Question: Provide ablation quantifying effects of POS filtering, left/right expansion, and merging (commas/linkages) on recall/precision.
- Question: How does SynTime compare to SUTime when both use the same lexicon and tweet adaptations?
- Question: What tokenizer/POS tagger was used for tweets, what is POS accuracy on the tweet set, and how sensitive is SynTime to tagging errors?
- Question: Report runtime (docs/sec) and memory usage vs. SUTime/HeidelTime on datasets.
- Question: What are tweet annotation details—inter-annotator agreement, guidelines, handling of prepositions, hashtags, mentions, and informal tokens like “2day/tmrw”?
- Question: Do rules cover multi-token constructs (“last Monday morning,” “the end of next month,” coordinated ranges), and what fraction of errors are these types?
- Additional comment: Temper the “least effort” framing or add statistical tests for cross-domain claims.
- Additional comment: Release code, rules, and expanded lexicons for reproducibility.
- Additional comment: Clarify dataset splits and whether evaluation is recognition-only (not normalization) for fair comparison.
