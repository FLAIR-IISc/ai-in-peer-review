- Paper introduces SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and boundary-expansion heuristics.
- Authors analyze four corpora (TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, Tweets) to motivate design choices (short spans, small vocabulary, frequent time tokens, POS tendencies).
- SynTime initializes lexicon from SUTime regexes, filters with POS tags, expands around time tokens, and merges segments with simple heuristics.
- Main contribution: a high-recall, simple rule-based recognizer (SynTime) with strong cross-dataset performance, especially on tweets.
- Contribution: a cross-corpus descriptive analysis of time expressions that informs practical design decisions.
- Contribution: a pragmatic, type-based rule design emphasizing expandability and runtime simplicity.
- Strength: strong empirical performance and recall (best/second-best F1 in many settings; relaxed recall >92% across datasets; large gains on tweets).
- Strength: clear, reproducible, and intuitive heuristics with explicit type taxonomy and implementation details provided.
- Strength: practical expandability and low engineering cost—few additions needed to reach near-ceiling performance on some datasets.
- Strength: sensible, data-driven motivation—find time token first then expand is justified by distributional analyses.
- Weakness: modest novelty over existing rule systems and heavy reliance on SUTime’s lexicon; missing ablation disentangling lexicon vs heuristics.
- Weakness: tweet dataset construction likely biased (preselected with SUTime), excluding negatives and affecting ecological validity.
- Weakness: limited evaluation rigor—no statistical significance tests, no measured runtimes, and some baselines borrowed (potentially incomparable).
- Weakness: schema mismatches and scope limits—excludes prepositions to match TimeML but struggles on WikiWars and does not address normalization.
- Weakness: missing detailed error analysis and deeper ablations (POS filtering impact, modifier contributions, failure modes).
- Question: provide an ablation separating SUTime token lexicon effects from SynTime boundary-expansion rules (e.g., reduced lexicon experiments).
- Question: confirm comparability of borrowed baseline numbers or re-run baselines under the same pipeline (tokenization, POS tagger, versions).
- Question: for Tweets, release or report results on a fully random, fully annotated sample (including negatives) and per-document/mention metrics; report sensitivity to tweet POS tagger.
- Question: provide statistical significance tests for F1 gains and concrete runtime measurements (tokens/sec) versus SUTime/HeidelTime.
- Question: clarify handling of cross-sentence expressions and complex ranges (“between … and …”, “from … through …”) and quantify related failures.
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject—method is simple and effective but limited novelty, biased tweet dataset, and insufficient evaluation/ablations.
