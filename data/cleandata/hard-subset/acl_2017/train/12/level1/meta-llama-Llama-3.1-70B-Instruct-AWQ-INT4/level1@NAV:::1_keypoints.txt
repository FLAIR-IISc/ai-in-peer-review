- Proposes SynTime, a type-based method to recognize time expressions from free text.  
- Analyzes four datasets and observes: time expressions are short; use a small set of keywords; usually contain a time token; words show similar syntactic behaviors.  
- Defines three syntactic token types (time token, modifier, numeral) and uses simple heuristic rules for recognition.  
- Evaluated against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets and outperforms them on precision, recall, and F1.  
- Contribution: thorough empirical analysis of time expressions across datasets.  
- Contribution: novel type-based approach that defines syntactic types and heuristics for recognition.  
- Contribution: a simple, lightweight, and effective method for time expression recognition.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis supports method design.  
- Strength: presents a novel perspective with the type-based approach.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation is strong (multiple baselines and datasets).  
- Weakness: narrow scope—focuses only on recognition and not broader applications.  
- Weakness: assumes correct tokenization and POS-tagging, which may fail in real-world settings.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with other machine learning–based methods.  
- Question: How will SynTime be extended to other languages?  
- Question: Can the authors detail expansion to different domains?  
- Question: How will limitations like reliance on correct tokenization/POS-tagging be addressed?
