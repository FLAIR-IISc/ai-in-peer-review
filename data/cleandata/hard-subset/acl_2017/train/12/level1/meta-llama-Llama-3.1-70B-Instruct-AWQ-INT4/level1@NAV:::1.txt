**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a type-based approach, named SynTime, to recognize time expressions from free text. The authors analyze time expressions from four datasets and make four observations: (1) time expressions are short, (2) a small group of time-related keywords are used, (3) most time expressions contain at least one time token, and (4) words in time expressions demonstrate similar syntactic behaviors. Based on these observations, SynTime defines three main syntactic types (time token, modifier, and numeral) and designs simple heuristic rules to recognize time expressions. The authors evaluate SynTime against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets and show that SynTime outperforms the baselines in terms of precision, recall, and F1 score.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Analysis of time expressions**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of time expressions from four datasets, which leads to a better understanding of the characteristics of time expressions.
2. **Type-based approach**: SynTime's type-based approach is a novel contribution, which defines syntactic types for tokens and designs simple heuristic rules to recognize time expressions.
3. **Simple and effective method**: SynTime's method is simple, lightweight, and effective, making it a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Thorough analysis**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of time expressions, which is essential for developing an effective method for recognizing time expressions.
2. **Novel approach**: SynTime's type-based approach is a novel contribution, which provides a new perspective on recognizing time expressions.
3. **Effective evaluation**: The authors evaluate SynTime against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the method.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited scope**: The paper focuses on recognizing time expressions, which is a limited scope. The authors could have explored other applications of SynTime.
2. **Assumptions**: The authors assume that words are tokenized and POS-tagged correctly, which may not always be the case in real-world scenarios.
3. **Comparison with other methods**: The authors could have compared SynTime with other machine learning-based methods to demonstrate its effectiveness.
