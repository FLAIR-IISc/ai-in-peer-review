**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel approach, SynTime, for recognizing time expressions in free text. The authors analyze time expressions from four datasets and identify four key observations: time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, contain at least one time token, and exhibit similar syntactic behavior. Inspired by part-of-speech, the authors define a syntactic type system for time expressions and design simple heuristic rules for recognition. The proposed approach is evaluated on three datasets and compared to three state-of-the-art baselines, demonstrating superior performance.

**Contributions**

1. **Analysis of time expressions**: The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of time expressions from four datasets, revealing key characteristics that inform the design of SynTime.
2. **SynTime: A type-based time expression tagger**: The authors propose a novel approach that defines syntactic types for tokens and uses simple heuristic rules for recognition, outperforming state-of-the-art baselines.
3. **Evaluation on diverse datasets**: The authors evaluate SynTime on three datasets, including a manually annotated Twitter dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in various contexts.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: SynTime's type-based approach and simple heuristic rules offer a fresh perspective on time expression recognition, departing from traditional rule-based and machine learning-based methods.
2. **Comprehensive analysis**: The authors' analysis of time expressions from four datasets provides valuable insights into the characteristics of time expressions, informing the design of SynTime.
3. **Strong evaluation**: The authors evaluate SynTime on three datasets, including a manually annotated Twitter dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in various contexts.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumes correct tokenization and POS tagging**: SynTime relies on accurate tokenization and POS tagging, which may not always be the case in real-world scenarios.
2. **Limited handling of descriptive time expressions**: SynTime may struggle with descriptive time expressions, as seen in the WikiWars dataset, which may require additional rules or modifications.
3. **Dependence on manual annotation**: SynTime's expansion requires manual annotation of additional words, which may be time-consuming and labor-intensive.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the issue of incorrect tokenization and POS tagging, which may affect SynTime's performance?
2. Can you provide more details on how SynTime handles descriptive time expressions, and whether you plan to extend its capabilities in this area?
3. How do you envision SynTime being used in real-world applications, and what are the potential benefits and challenges of deploying this approach?
