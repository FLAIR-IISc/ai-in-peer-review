- Proposes SynTime, a syntactic type–based, rule-driven method for time expression recognition.
- Analysis of four datasets shows time expressions are typically short, use a small vocabulary, and have similar syntactic behavior.
- Provides a thorough dataset analysis that informs the method’s design.
- Introduces a syntactic type system and a type-based recognition approach for time expressions.
- Implements SynTime, a lightweight rule-based tagger realizing the type-based approach.
- Analysis yields actionable insights into the structure and syntax of time expressions.
- The type-based approach is claimed to be more flexible and efficient than prior rule- or ML-based methods.
- SynTime outperforms three state-of-the-art baselines on three evaluated datasets.
- SynTime is lightweight and capable of real-time operation.
- Evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets, raising questions about generalizability.
- SynTime depends on POS tagging, which may hurt performance when POS tags are noisy.
- May have limited ability to handle complex time expressions with multiple tokens or modifiers.
- Question: How will SynTime be extended to handle multi-token and modifier-heavy time expressions?
- Question: How will robustness to POS tagging errors be improved or evaluated?
- Question: What is SynTime’s time complexity versus other methods, and how will its generalizability be tested on more diverse datasets?
