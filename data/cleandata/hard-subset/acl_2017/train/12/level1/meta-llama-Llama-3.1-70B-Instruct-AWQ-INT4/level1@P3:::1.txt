**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a novel approach to time expression recognition, called SynTime, which leverages syntactic types and simple heuristic rules to identify time expressions in free text. The authors analyze four datasets and observe that time expressions are typically short, use a small vocabulary, and exhibit similar syntactic behavior. Based on these observations, they define a syntactic type system for time expressions and propose a type-based approach to recognize time expressions. The proposed method is evaluated against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets and demonstrates superior performance.

**Contributions**

1. **Analysis of time expressions**: The authors conduct a thorough analysis of time expressions in four datasets, providing insights into their structure and syntax. This analysis is a significant contribution, as it provides a deeper understanding of time expressions and informs the development of the proposed method.
2. **Type-based approach to time expression recognition**: The authors propose a novel type-based approach to time expression recognition, which leverages syntactic types and simple heuristic rules. This approach is a significant contribution, as it provides a more flexible and efficient way to recognize time expressions compared to existing rule-based and machine learning-based methods.
3. **SynTime: A lightweight rule-based tagger**: The authors propose SynTime, a lightweight rule-based tagger that implements the type-based approach. SynTime is a significant contribution, as it provides a simple and efficient way to recognize time expressions in free text.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective analysis of time expressions**: The authors' analysis of time expressions provides valuable insights into their structure and syntax, which informs the development of the proposed method.
2. **Novel type-based approach**: The authors' type-based approach to time expression recognition is a significant innovation, as it provides a more flexible and efficient way to recognize time expressions compared to existing methods.
3. **Superior performance**: The proposed method, SynTime, demonstrates superior performance compared to three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets.
4. **Lightweight and efficient**: SynTime is a lightweight rule-based tagger that can run in real-time, making it a practical solution for time expression recognition.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited generalizability**: The authors' analysis and proposed method are based on a limited number of datasets, which may not be representative of all types of text. Further evaluation on more diverse datasets is necessary to demonstrate the generalizability of the proposed method.
2. **Dependence on POS tagging**: SynTime relies on POS tagging, which may not always be accurate. The authors should investigate ways to improve the robustness of SynTime to POS tagging errors.
3. **Limited handling of complex time expressions**: SynTime may not be able to handle complex time expressions, such as those involving multiple time tokens or modifiers. The authors should investigate ways to extend SynTime to handle more complex time expressions.
