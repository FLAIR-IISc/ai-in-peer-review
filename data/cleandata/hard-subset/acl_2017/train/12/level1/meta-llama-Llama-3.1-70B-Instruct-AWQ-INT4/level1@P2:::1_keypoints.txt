- Paper proposes SynTime: a type-based, rule-driven system for recognizing time expressions using three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) informed by analysis of four datasets; claims state-of-the-art results on three datasets.

- Strength: Novel, simple, and flexible type-based approach offering an attractive alternative to existing methods.

- Strength: Thorough analysis of time expressions across four datasets that motivated SynTime's design.

- Strength: Strong experimental performance, notably excellent results on the Tweets dataset.

- Strength: Lightweight, efficient, and scalable rule-based design suitable for real-time use.

- Weakness: Limited generalizability — weaker performance on the WikiWars dataset suggests domain sensitivity.

- Weakness: Assumes perfect tokenization and POS tagging, which may not hold in real-world settings.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to machine learning–based methods commonly used in NLP.

- Weakness: No error analysis provided to pinpoint frequent failure modes and guide improvements.

- Question: How can SynTime's type-based approach be adapted to other languages or domains?

- Question: How will the method handle imperfect tokenization and POS tagging in real-world scenarios?

- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed comparison against machine learning–based time expression recognizers?

- Question: What are the most common errors SynTime makes, and how do the authors plan to address them?
