**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach, SynTime, for recognizing time expressions in free text. The authors analyze time expressions from four datasets and observe that they are typically short, use a small vocabulary, and exhibit similar syntactic behavior. Based on these observations, they define a syntactic type system for time expressions and design simple heuristic rules for recognition. The approach is evaluated on three datasets and compared to three state-of-the-art baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Analysis of time expressions**: The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of time expressions from four datasets, revealing their characteristics and patterns.
2. **SynTime approach**: The authors propose a novel type-based approach, SynTime, which defines syntactic types for time expressions and designs simple heuristic rules for recognition.
3. **Evaluation and comparison**: The authors evaluate SynTime on three datasets and compare its performance to three state-of-the-art baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: SynTime offers a fresh perspective on time expression recognition, leveraging syntactic types and heuristic rules to achieve high performance.
2. **Comprehensive analysis**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of time expressions, shedding light on their characteristics and patterns.
3. **Efficient and effective**: SynTime demonstrates high performance and efficiency, making it a promising approach for real-time applications.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited generalizability**: The authors acknowledge that SynTime may not perform well on time expressions that do not follow the observed patterns.
2. **Dependence on POS tagging**: SynTime relies on accurate POS tagging, which may not always be available or accurate.
3. **Limited handling of prepositions**: SynTime does not fully recognize time expressions with prepositions, which may limit its applicability.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limited generalizability of SynTime, particularly for time expressions that do not follow the observed patterns?
2. Can you discuss the potential impact of POS tagging errors on SynTime's performance and how you plan to mitigate this issue?
3. How do you envision extending SynTime to handle time expressions with prepositions and other complex cases?
