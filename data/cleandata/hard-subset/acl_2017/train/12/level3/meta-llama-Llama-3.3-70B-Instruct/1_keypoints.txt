- Proposes a new task: concept-map-based multi-document summarization to represent key content as a concept map.
- Introduces "low-context importance annotation," a crowdsourcing scheme to score proposition importance efficiently and reliably.
- Creates a new benchmark corpus of 30 topics (~40 documents each) that is large, heterogeneous, and linguistically varied.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented with organized explanations of task, annotation, and corpus creation.
- Makes significant contributions: new task formulation, novel crowdsourcing method, and a larger, more diverse corpus.
- Provides a thorough evaluation including a pilot study, quality control, and corpus analysis.
- Lacks detailed documentation on corpus creation specifics, such as annotation guidelines and document selection criteria.
- Analysis is limited; the paper could further examine the generated concept maps and their properties.
- Baseline method is simplistic, with weak concept extraction and relation identification approaches.
- Requests more details on the annotation guidelines and document selection criteria.
- Asks how authors will address underspecification and provide fuller documentation of corpus creation.
- Requests additional analysis of the corpus, specifically deeper examination of concept maps and their characteristics.
