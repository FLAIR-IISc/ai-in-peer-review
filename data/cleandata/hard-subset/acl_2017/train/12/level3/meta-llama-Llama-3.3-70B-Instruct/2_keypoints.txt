- Proposes concept-map-based multi-document summarization (MDS) to represent key content of document clusters as concept maps.
- Introduces "low-context importance annotation," a crowdsourcing scheme to label proposition importance efficiently.
- Builds a new benchmark corpus for concept-map-based MDS: 30 topics with ~40 heterogeneous documents each.
- Novel task extends traditional summarization by using concept maps as summaries.
- Low-context annotation is claimed to be more efficient and effective than traditional crowdsourcing.
- Created corpus is larger and more heterogeneous than typical summarization datasets, increasing task difficulty.
- Evaluation is limited to the authors' baseline system without comparisons to state-of-the-art summarizers.
- Paper lacks detailed analysis of the corpus (e.g., distributions of concepts/relations and document characteristics).
- Applicability may be limited across domains/genres, and potential limitations are not discussed.
- How will the authors evaluate effectiveness of concept-map MDS across different domains/genres?
- Can the authors provide detailed corpus statistics (concept/relation distributions, document characteristics)?
- How do the authors plan to address potential limitations and limited applicability of their approach?
