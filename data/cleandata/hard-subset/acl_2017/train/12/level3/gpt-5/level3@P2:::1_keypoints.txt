- Paper presents SynTime, a lightweight rule-based system for time expression recognition using token-first, type-based boundary expansion and merging.  
- Empirical analysis across news, Wikipedia, and Twitter motivates the approach: time expressions are short, use a small lexicon, and usually contain a time token.  
- Contributions: (1) cross-domain characterization of time expressions, (2) practical type-based rule system (SynTime) with heuristic expansion, (3) evaluation including a new annotated Tweets dataset.  
- SynTime achieves strong empirical performance with high recall and competitive/SOTA strict F1 on TimeBank and Tweets.  
- Design is simple, well-motivated, easy to extend, and runs in real time with minimal engineering cost.  
- Clear articulation of the type inventory (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and boundary-expansion rationale.  
- Cross-corpus analyses (length, token coverage, POS tendencies) provide useful insights for future taggers.  
- Achieves competitive WikiWars performance with limited tuning; expansion improves recall, indicating easy domain adaptation via lexicon additions.  
- Reproducibility is limited by underspecified implementation details (regexes, full modifier/num lists, POS constraints, disambiguation, merging criteria).  
- Paper lacks qualitative error analysis and ablations to quantify contributions of POS filtering, boundary expansion, merging, and SUTime initialization.  
- Novelty is moderate since SynTime builds on SUTime resources; main novelty is syntactic-type orientation and heuristic expansion.  
- Evaluation fairness concerns: UWTime training data differences, TimeML vs corpus annotation conventions, and tweet annotation protocol/clash need clarification.  
- Missing concrete examples for key claims (e.g., the ~7% of expressions without time tokens) and how SynTime handles them.  
- Questions: clarify SEASON type definitions and handling of religious/holiday/overlapping season categories.  
- Questions: provide examples of non-token time expressions and explain SynTime’s coverage and misses.  
- Questions: detail main residual recall errors and which fixes would most improve recall (POS/tokenization, missing tokens, merging).  
- Questions: will the full rule inventory and code or a complete rulespec be released to enable replication?  
- Questions: robustness to noisy POS tagging (e.g., Twitter), effect of POS-agnostic fallback, and impact on recall/precision.  
- Questions: provide ablation results for POS filtering, boundary expansion, and segment merging.  
- Editorial/additional comments: fix dataset count inconsistency (says two benchmark datasets but evaluates three).  
- Additional comments: explicitly list which SUTime resources were imported/excluded and provide counts per type for transparency.  
- Additional comments: report confidence intervals and annotation agreement for the Tweets sampling and SUTime miss-rate estimate.  
- Suggest discussing limitations and feasibility of porting the type system and heuristics to non-English languages.
