- Paper proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using a small set of syntactic types and boundary-expansion heuristics, initialized from SUTime token regexes and using POS tags to disambiguate tokens.  
- Evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and a new Tweets dataset, SynTime reports strong performance (high recall, often outperforming HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime) and is simple, fast, and easy to extend.

- Contribution: a practical, type-based rule design with minimal position-aware heuristics that yields strong multi-dataset performance.  
- Contribution: an empirical characterization of time expressions (short spans, small vocabulary, syntactic regularity) that motivates the design.  
- Contribution: a new Tweets dataset and evaluation demonstrating robust performance on informal text.

- Strength: strong empirical performance and high recall (e.g., strict F1 ≈92 on TimeBank and Tweets; relaxed recall >92% across datasets).  
- Strength: simple, interpretable, and practical system design that is easy to analyze, debug, and adapt.  
- Strength: measured use of POS tags to reduce false positives without overfitting to brittle features.  
- Strength: lightweight extensibility—small lexicon additions yield large recall gains with little annotation cost.  
- Strength: clear corpus analysis supporting linguistic regularities that explain effectiveness, particularly on tweets.

- Weakness: potential overfitting because rules and type lists were designed/tuned on the same corpora used for evaluation.  
- Weakness: domain and annotation-policy bias across corpora (e.g., TimeML span conventions vs. WikiWars) may affect comparability and advantage SynTime in some settings.  
- Weakness: lack of quantified token/type overlap or OOV rates across corpora to support claims of small vocabulary/generalizability.  
- Weakness: limited ablations and no statistical significance testing to isolate contributions of SUTime regexes, POS filtering, and merging heuristics.  
- Weakness: fairness concern from inheriting SUTime token regexes, which may advantage SynTime relative to SUTime; impact not discussed.

- Question: Which corpora were used for developing/tuning core rules (boundary expansion, comma/linkage merging) versus held-out testing?  
- Question: What are token/type overlaps and OOV rates across TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets, and performance on OOV expressions?  
- Question: Can you provide leave-one-corpus-out experiments to demonstrate domain transfer (e.g., design on TimeBank, test on WikiWars/Tweets)?  
- Question: How sensitive is SynTime to POS tagging errors—please provide ablation with/without POS filtering or with noisy POS tags.  
- Question: Please report runtime (docs/sec) and memory usage to substantiate real-time claims.

- Additional: add statistical significance testing (e.g., paired bootstrap) for reported F1 gains over baselines.  
- Additional: include an ablation table for POS filtering, comma/linkage merge conditions, SUTime-only tokens vs. expansions, and search window sizes.  
- Additional: provide error analysis by time type (DATE/TIME/DURATION) and by span length to show strengths and failures.  
- Additional: evaluate on a truly out-of-domain corpus (e.g., clinical, forum, historical) to address generality concerns.  
- Additional: clarify whether Tweets dataset and code will be released to support reproducibility.
