1. SynTime: a lightweight, rule-based time expression recognizer that groups tokens into syntactic types and uses boundary-expansion heuristics, showing strong, high-recall performance on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets.

2. Contribution: interpretable type-based heuristic framework achieving competitive or superior accuracy to established rule-based and ML systems.

3. Contribution: engineering efficiency—a minimal rule set that runs in real time and is easy to extend via small keyword additions.

4. Contribution: empirical analysis highlighting short lengths, small vocabulary, and syntactic regularities of time expressions that motivate the approach.

5. Strength: strong empirical performance—matches/exceeds HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime, with best F1/recall on tweets.

6. Strength: simplicity and interpretability—avoids complex grammars/training, facilitating analysis, debugging, and adaptation.

7. Strength: real-time efficiency and low-cost extensibility—few per-domain token additions indicate portability and transparent maintenance.

8. Strength: clear methodological framing—the syntactic-type and boundary-expansion strategy is well articulated and intuitive.

9. Weakness: generality/overfitting risk—rule design and expansions derived from the same corpora used for evaluation, lacking a held-out unseen domain.

10. Weakness: possible domain bias—dataset-specific analysis may have biased keyword/pattern selection and inflated results.

11. Weakness: missing cross-corpus overlap statistics—the “small, shared vocabulary” claim isn’t supported by overlap/OOV rates across corpora.

12. Weakness: insufficient ablations and error analysis—the contributions of POS filtering, boundary merging, and expansions aren’t isolated, and POS reliance is unquantified.

13. Question: were any keyword additions or rule refinements derived from documents later used in TimeBank/WikiWars evaluation; can results on an entirely unseen corpus be reported?

14. Question: please report cross-corpus token overlap and OOV rates (e.g., proportion of time expressions in each test set covered by tokens learned elsewhere).

15. Question: can you add an evaluation on a distinct domain (e.g., clinical, legal, or narrative fiction) with no prior expansion?

16. Question: provide ablations (no POS filtering; no boundary expansion; no expansions), timing benchmarks, and confirm whether rules/code will be released.
