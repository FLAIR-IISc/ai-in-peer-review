- Paper proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and boundary-expansion heuristics.
- Authors motivate the design via corpus analysis showing time expressions are short, use a small lexicon, almost always contain a time token, and follow regular syntactic patterns.
- SynTime initializes its lexicon from SUTime regexes and can be expanded manually with a few domain-specific keywords.
- Experiments on TimeBank, WikiWars, and a Twitter dataset report high recall and competitive F1, outperforming or matching HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime on most settings.
- Contribution 1: Simple type-based formulation with heuristic boundary expansion achieving high recall and robust cross-domain performance.
- Contribution 2: Empirical multi-corpus analysis supporting a time-token-centered, small-vocabulary design.
- Contribution 3: Lightweight, extensible, real-time implementation requiring no training and minimal resources.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance, with relaxed recall >92% and strict F1 ≈92 on TimeBank and ≈91.7–91.8 on Tweets.
- Strength: Simplicity and extensibility—few added keywords per domain and easy-to-extend rules.
- Strength: Clear, data-driven motivation from corpus analysis, with pragmatic POS use for disambiguation.
- Strength: Practical deployability and interpretability for real-time applications without heavy ML pipelines.
- Weakness: Possible data leakage/overfitting if SynTime-E expansions were derived from the evaluated corpora; expansion protocol unclear.
- Weakness: Twitter dataset selection bias—tweets pre-filtered by SUTime may underrepresent cases SUTime misses.
- Weakness: Baseline configuration comparability unclear; unclear whether HeidelTime/SUTime/UWTime were equally tuned per domain.
- Weakness: Limited error analysis and ablation studies; missing breakdowns by expression type and unreported runtime numbers.
- Weakness: Claims of generality not fully substantiated; token inventory overlap across datasets and truly unseen-domain tests are missing.
- Question: Which texts were used to derive SynTime-E expansions for TimeBank and WikiWars, and are they disjoint from evaluation data?
- Question: Can results be provided on a randomly sampled, fully annotated tweet set (no SUTime pre-filtering) or full annotation protocol and IAA for the 4,000-tweet sample?
- Question: How were HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime configured/tuned for each dataset, and what resources/training did UWTime use?
- Question: What are ablation results (no POS disambiguation, disabling specific heuristics, or using only time-token anchoring)?
- Question: What are runtime/throughput and memory measurements to substantiate real-time claims?
- Question: Can you report lexicon overlap statistics (e.g., Jaccard) across datasets and how much expansion improves coverage?
- Additional comment: Recommend an out-of-domain test (e.g., newswire, clinical notes, forum text) to demonstrate generality and reduce overfitting concerns.
- Additional comment: Provide an error taxonomy and qualitative examples, especially for WikiWars-related failures.
- Additional comment: Fix minor typos/formatting, clarify type counts, and ideally release code and lexicons for reproducibility.
