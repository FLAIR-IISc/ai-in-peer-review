- Presents SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using token types and boundary-expansion heuristics driven by corpus analysis.
- Claims main contributions: type-based boundary-expansion method with strong recall and competitive F1; empirical analysis motivating the design; low-effort domain extensibility via small lexicon additions.
- Strong empirical performance: very high strict/relaxed F1 on TimeBank and Tweets and recalls >92% across datasets, outperforming HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime on several metrics.
- Simple, well-motivated three-stage pipeline (detect time tokens, expand left/right, merge) with real-time runtime and minimal components.
- Demonstrates easy extensibility: adding a handful of keywords improved domain recall (e.g., WikiWars gains; few additions needed for TimeBank/Tweets).
- Data-driven design: analysis shows time expressions are short, use a compact lexicon, and typically contain time tokens and POS cues, supporting the token-first approach.
- Clear presentation of core rules, boundary expansion, and merge conditions with examples.
- Reproducibility concerns: full token lists/regexes, exact POS constraints, and complete expansion/merge rules are not provided; code/rules not released.
- Incremental novelty: prior systems (SUTime/HeidelTime) also use token classes/POS and rule expansion; paper lacks ablations to show which components drive gains.
- Evaluation design issues: Tweets dataset seeded by SUTime risks selection bias, small annotated test set (200 tweets), missing inter-annotator agreement, and confusing dataset-count wording.
- Inconsistent coverage tied to annotation choices: following TimeML reduces coverage of descriptive expressions, causing lower performance in WikiWars; system addresses recognition only, not normalization.
- Limited error analysis: lacks systematic breakdown of failures (e.g., expressions without time tokens, POS tagging errors, error types by length/type).
- Question on SEASON type: clarify what the five seasons are and how Ramadan, Lent, “holiday season,” or domain-specific seasons are categorized and included in lexicon.
- Clarify dataset reporting: explain “two benchmark datasets” vs. three evaluated, and confirm baseline versions/configurations and training/tuning comparability.
- Request examples and proportions of time expressions without time tokens and how the dependent-segment mechanism handles them.
- Ask for dominant failure modes explaining why recall ≈92% despite ≥93% expressions containing time tokens, and which enhancements would most improve recall.
- Request release of full rule set, lexicons/regexes (including dataset-specific additions), and code, or alternatively an appendix detailing rules, POS constraints, and added tokens for reproducibility.
- Request ablation studies (without POS filtering, merging, expansion) and runtime/memory measurements across datasets to validate simplicity, speed, and component contributions.
