- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer motivated by dataset-driven observations.  
- Empirical analysis across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets shows 80% of time expressions are very short (≤3 tokens).  
- Analysis finds time expressions use a small, limited vocabulary of time-related keywords.  
- Reports that ≥93% of time expressions contain at least one time token.  
- Finds words within time expressions exhibit similar syntactic behaviors, supporting the principle of least effort.  
- SynTime defines syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and uses simple heuristic rules that expand segments bidirectionally from time tokens.  
- Main contributions: thorough multi-dataset empirical analysis, the SynTime system, and comprehensive evaluation showing strong performance.  
- Strength: rigorous, well-supported empirical analysis across diverse datasets that directly informs system design.  
- Strength: elegant, lightweight heuristic design suitable for real-time applications.  
- Strength: strong quantitative results (e.g., Tweets: 91.74% F1 strict, 95.87% F1 relaxed) with notable improvements over baselines.  
- Weakness: paper incorrectly claims "two benchmark datasets" while evaluating on three, misrepresenting the experimental setup.  
- Weakness: implementation details are underspecified (e.g., unclear “5 seasons,” handling of culturally specific expressions), hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: discrepancy between the 93% claim and reported 92% recall; lacks examples of time expressions without time tokens.  
- Evaluation summary: SynTime achieves best results on TimeBank and Tweets, comparable on WikiWars; SynTime-E yields modest domain-specific gains.  
- Questions for authors: clarify the “5 seasons,” handling of cultural time expressions, dataset-count discrepancy, example of a no-time-token expression, sources of remaining errors despite high recall, and plans to release rules/software.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, well-written, and effective approach with minor clarifications needed—recommend acceptance pending improved methodological and reproducibility details.
