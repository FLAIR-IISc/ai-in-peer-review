- Paper proposes SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple heuristics.  
- Four dataset observations: time expressions are short (80% ≤3 tokens).  
- Observation: a small vocabulary of time-related keywords is used.  
- Observation: >93% of expressions contain at least one time token.  
- Observation: words in time expressions show similar syntactic behavior, motivating three syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals).  
- Main contribution: syntactic type system providing a linguistically grounded approach to time recognition.  
- Main contribution: shows simple rule-based methods can outperform prior rule-based and ML baselines on benchmarks.  
- Main contribution: provides evidence supporting Zipf’s principle of least effort in time expression usage.  
- Strength: method is simple, interpretable, and real-time, suitable for basic applications.  
- Strength: strong empirical results (e.g., 92.09% strict F1 on TimeBank) compared to baselines.  
- Primary weakness: rules were designed using the same corpora as evaluation, risking overfitting and undermining generalizability.  
- Missing analyses: no statistics on overlap between training and evaluation corpora and potential domain bias of datasets.  
- Additional weaknesses: insufficient explanation of why SynTime outperforms prior rule-based methods and no analysis of handling out-of-lexicon words.  
- Limitation: no evaluation on non-English data.  
- Recommendation: test SynTime on an independent corpus and report corpus-overlap statistics to justify generalizability; otherwise contribution may be domain/dataset-limited.
