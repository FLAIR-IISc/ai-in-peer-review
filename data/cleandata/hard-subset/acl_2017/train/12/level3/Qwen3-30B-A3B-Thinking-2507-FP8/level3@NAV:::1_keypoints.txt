- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based time expression extractor that uses four empirical observations (short length, small vocabulary, presence of time tokens, similar syntax) to detect and expand time segments via dictionary/regex/POS and heuristic expansion/merging.  
- Contribution: Large empirical analysis across four datasets revealing fundamental properties of time expressions supporting the method’s design.  
- Contribution: SynTime’s simple, syntactic-type rule approach achieves state-of-the-art results on three datasets, outperforming rule-based and ML baselines.  
- Contribution: Lightweight design enables real-time performance and easy domain adaptation by extending rules.  
- Strength: Empirical observations are well validated across multiple datasets and justify the method.  
- Strength: Strong evaluation results (e.g., 95.87% relaxed F1 on Tweets; ≥92% recall across datasets).  
- Strength: Paper is clearly and well written, with transparent presentation of methodology and results.  
- Strength: Simplicity and real-time capability offer practical advantages over complex ML models.  
- Weakness: Important implementation details are underspecified (exact rule sets, POS thresholds), harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent reporting—claims “two benchmark datasets” but evaluates on three (TimeBank, WikiWars, Tweets).  
- Weakness: Fails to explain handling of holiday/seasonal expressions (e.g., Ramadan, “5 seasons”), limiting real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: No example given for the minority of time expressions without time tokens despite claiming 93%+ contain them.  
- Weakness: Unclear how to further improve performance given already high recall and reliance on time-token presence.  
- Question: Will the authors release the full rule set and software?  
- Question: Please provide an example of a time expression without a time token.  
- Question: How does the system handle holiday-specific expressions like “Ramadan 2023”?
