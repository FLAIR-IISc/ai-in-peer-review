- Primary contribution: SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and heuristics that leverages brevity, small vocabulary, and time tokens.  
- Secondary contribution: Empirical analysis of time expressions across four datasets showing short length, small vocab, high time-token presence, and consistent syntactic behavior.  
- Practical contribution: A lightweight, real-time implementation suitable for low-latency applications.  

- Strength: Strong empirical motivation—data analysis convincingly motivates design choices.  
- Strength: Clear, concise presentation with helpful diagrams and straightforward rule explanations.  
- Strength: Competitive performance, especially high recall and strong results on a challenging Twitter dataset.  
- Strength: Efficient and practical compared to heavier machine-learning approaches.  

- Weakness: Lack of reproducibility—missing exact regexes, full heuristic rule set, and POS-tagging details (subset of SUTime unclear).  
- Weakness: Limited error analysis—insufficient breakdown of false positives and missed expressions.  
- Weakness: Potential overgeneralization using Zipf’s principle; needs a more nuanced discussion of contributing factors.  

- Question: What are the “5 seasons” in Section 4.1 and how are cultural/regional seasonal variations handled?  
- Question: Provide an example of a time expression detected that contains no time token.  
- Question: Given high recall (>92%), what avenues remain to further improve recall and reduce missed expressions?  
- Question: Will the code and complete rule set for SynTime be publicly released to enable reproducibility?  

- Additional comment: Clarify the discrepancy where Section 5.1 refers to “two benchmark datasets” but evaluates three (TimeBank, WikiWars, Tweets).  
- Additional comment: References are comprehensive and appropriate.  

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated paper with competitive results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve reproducibility and error analysis.
