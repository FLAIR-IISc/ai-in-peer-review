- Primary contribution: SynTime simplifies rule-based time expression recognition by using three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple heuristic boundary-expansion rules.

- Secondary contribution: Empirical analysis across four datasets showing time expressions are short, use a limited vocabulary, and follow consistent syntactic behavior (principle of least effort).

- Empirical result: SynTime achieves strong performance, outperforming state-of-the-art on TimeBank and Tweets and matching performance on WikiWars.

- Design benefit: Lightweight, efficient, and real-time capable due to simple rules and type-based approach, making it adaptable across domains.

- Presentation: Paper is clear and well-structured, with helpful architectural overview (Figure 2).

- Weakness — generalizability: Risk of overfitting; unclear overlap between datasets used for analysis, rule creation, and evaluation, raising questions about rule specificity.

- Weakness — error analysis: Lacks detailed error analysis to reveal which time-expression types cause most errors and limitations of the approach.

- Weakness — preprocessing reliance: Heavy dependence on accurate POS tagging without sensitivity analysis on how POS errors affect performance.

- Weakness — expansion benefit: Manual expansion phase added few tokens and showed modest gains, so the cost-benefit of expansion is not well justified.

- Question: Provide statistics on overlap of time expressions/components across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets to assess tailoring to corpora.

- Question: Provide a detailed error analysis describing common SynTime error types.

- Question: Report any investigations or results on sensitivity to POS tagging errors.

- Question: Estimate expected time investment and likely performance gain when expanding SynTime to a new domain.

- Additional: Reference list appears incomplete (e.g., Alonso et al. 2011 missing details).

- Additional: Suggest comparing SynTime to more recent state-of-the-art time expression systems beyond those cited.

- Additional: Clarify the use of the term "type" to avoid confusion between syntactic types and other senses (e.g., dataset type).

- Overall recommendation: Solid, novel, and effective contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing generalizability and analysis concerns.
