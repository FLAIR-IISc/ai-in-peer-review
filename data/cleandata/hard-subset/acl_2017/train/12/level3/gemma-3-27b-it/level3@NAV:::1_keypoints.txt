- Presents SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple heuristics.  
- Core insight: time expressions are typically short and contain at least one identifiable “time token.”  
- SynTime defines syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and uses boundary expansion/merging heuristics.  
- Empirical results show significant improvements over rule-based and ML baselines on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets.  
- Strong contributions: well-motivated simplification, lightweight design, clear method presentation, and positive empirical support.  
- Additional contribution: analysis showing prevalence of short expressions with time tokens that motivates the approach.  
- Main weakness: reproducibility concerns due to underspecified rule details and implementation.  
- Requests for clarification: which “5 seasons” are used and handling of culturally specific periods (e.g., Ramadan, holidays).  
- Dataset discrepancy: paper mentions “two benchmark datasets” but experiments use three—please clarify.  
- Ask for examples of time expressions lacking a time token and how SynTime handles them.  
- Question about future improvements given 93% token-containing expressions and SynTime’s 92% recall.  
- Request to release full rule set and/or software to facilitate validation and further research.
