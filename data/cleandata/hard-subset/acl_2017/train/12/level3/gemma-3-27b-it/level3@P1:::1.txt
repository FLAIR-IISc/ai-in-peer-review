This paper presents SynTime, a novel rule-based approach to time expression recognition. The authors motivate their work through analysis of four datasets, observing that time expressions are generally short, utilize a small vocabulary, and frequently contain identifiable "time tokens."  SynTime leverages these observations by defining syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and employing simple heuristic rules to identify and merge time segments into complete expressions.  The system is evaluated on three datasets, demonstrating competitive and often superior performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the insightful analysis of time expressions and the subsequent design of a system specifically tailored to exploit those characteristics. While rule-based approaches aren't novel, the observed properties – brevity, limited vocabulary, reliance on time tokens – provide a strong justification for the chosen method. The type-based approach, contrasting with semantically driven rule systems, offers a streamlined and potentially more robust method for time expression identification. A second, related contribution is the development and demonstration of a surprisingly effective system leveraging these principles, achieving strong performance on benchmark datasets, especially on the challenging Twitter data.

The strengths of this submission are numerous. First, the paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented, making the methodology and results easy to understand. The motivation stemming from the dataset analysis is convincing, and the rationale behind the design choices is sound. Second, the empirical evaluation provides strong support for the effectiveness of SynTime. The consistent outperformance of baseline methods across multiple datasets, and especially the high recall scores, suggests a valuable contribution.  The demonstration of strong results on Twitter data is particularly noteworthy, as this dataset often presents unique challenges for NLP systems.  Finally, the light-weight nature of the system and the claim of real-time performance are attractive features.

Despite these strengths, several weaknesses need addressing. The most significant is a lack of detail hindering reproducibility. While the core ideas are clear, the specific regular expressions and heuristic rules are not fully described.  Providing a more comprehensive listing of rules, or at least representative examples, would greatly increase the paper's impact. A second weakness concerns the ambiguity around certain definitions. For example, in Section 4.1, the "5 seasons" are not defined, and it's unclear how holidays that aren’t explicitly listed would be handled. Clarifying these aspects is crucial. Furthermore, a minor inconsistency exists in Section 5.1 where the authors refer to "two benchmark datasets" despite evaluating on three. This needs to be corrected.  Finally, while the authors acknowledge the importance of accurate tokenization and POS tagging, they do not discuss the potential impact of errors in these preprocessing steps on the performance of SynTime.

To facilitate a more thorough evaluation, I have several questions for the authors:

1. **(Section 4.1):** Could you please elaborate on the "5 seasons" mentioned in the definition of the SEASON type? How are less common or culturally specific seasonal events handled?
2. **(Section 5.2):** You mention that 93% of time expressions contain a time token.  Can you provide an example of a time expression recognized by SynTime that *does not* contain a time token, and explain how it is identified?
3. **(Section 5.2):** Given the high recall already achieved (around 92-98%), what strategies are being considered to further improve performance, particularly in precision? Is there a potential for incorporating machine learning to refine the rules or address edge cases?
4. Will the full source code and a complete listing of the regular expressions and rules used in SynTime be publicly released to enable reproducibility and further research?



In conclusion, this is a promising paper presenting a well-motivated and effective approach to time expression recognition. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing more detailed implementation information, would significantly strengthen the submission. The combination of insightful analysis, strong empirical results, and the system’s lightweight nature makes this work a valuable contribution to the field.
