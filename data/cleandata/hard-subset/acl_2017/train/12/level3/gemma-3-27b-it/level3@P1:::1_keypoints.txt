- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based system for time expression recognition motivated by dataset analysis showing brevity, small vocabulary, and frequent "time tokens."  
- Introduces syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple heuristic rules to merge segments into complete time expressions.  
- Evaluated on three datasets, often outperforming state-of-the-art baselines and showing especially strong results on noisy Twitter data.  
- Main contribution: insightful analysis of time expressions and a streamlined type-based rule design tailored to those properties.  
- System is lightweight, claims real-time performance, and achieves high recall (around 92–98%).  
- Strength: paper is clearly written with convincing motivation and strong empirical support across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation detail for reproducibility—specific regular expressions and heuristic rules are not fully listed.  
- Weakness: ambiguous definitions (e.g., unspecified "5 seasons") and unclear handling of holidays not explicitly listed.  
- Weakness: minor inconsistency referring to "two benchmark datasets" despite evaluating on three.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of the impact of tokenization and POS tagging errors on SynTime’s performance.  
- Questions for authors: clarify the "5 seasons" and handling of cultural/rare seasons; provide an example of a time expression without a time token; outline strategies to improve precision and potential ML integration; and confirm release of full source code and rule listings.  
- Conclusion: Promising and effective approach; addressing implementation and clarity issues would significantly strengthen the paper.
