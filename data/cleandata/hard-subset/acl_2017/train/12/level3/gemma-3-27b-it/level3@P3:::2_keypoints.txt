- Empirical analysis of time expressions across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets reveals short length, small vocabulary, reliance on time tokens, and consistent syntactic behavior.
- SynTime: a rule-based system using syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple boundary-expansion heuristics to identify time expressions.
- SynTime achieves superior performance to state-of-the-art rule-based and ML methods on benchmark datasets and notably on Tweets.
- Clear motivation: observations about time expression properties directly inform SynTime’s design choices.
- SynTime is simple, interpretable, and easily expandable by adding keywords or rules.
- Strong empirical results with consistent outperformance of baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime).
- Practical relevance: SynTime offers real-time performance suitable for speed-critical applications.
- Thorough evaluation using standard metrics (Precision, Recall, F1) and established datasets.
- Major weakness: potential overfitting because rules were designed and evaluated on the same corpora, raising generalization concerns.
- Corpus bias: rule design may have been influenced by dataset-specific characteristics without clear mitigation.
- Lack of inter-corpus statistical analysis to show overlap or consistency of time expressions across datasets.
- Limited error analysis: insufficient detail on failure modes or specific challenging expression types.
