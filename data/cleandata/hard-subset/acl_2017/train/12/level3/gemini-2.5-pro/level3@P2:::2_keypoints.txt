- Introduces SynTime, a rule-based system for time expression recognition.  
- Corpus analysis (TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, Tweets) finds time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, and include core "time tokens."  
- Proposes a three-step method: identify core time tokens, expand boundaries via nearby modifiers/numerals, merge adjacent/overlapping segments.  
- Evaluated on three datasets and reports strong performance against state-of-the-art systems.  
- Contribution: a simple, effective rule-based method achieving high recall and competitive F1.  
- Contribution: empirical analysis that motivates the identify-and-expand design.  
- Strength: impressive empirical results (e.g., SynTime-E 92.47 strict F1 on TimeBank vs next best 83.10).  
- Strength: high interpretability and ease of error analysis due to simple syntactic rules and token lists.  
- Strength: efficient, training-free, and easily extensible to new domains with small additions.  
- Weakness: potential overfitting—rules and keyword lists may be tuned to the corpora used in analysis and evaluation.  
- Weakness: lacks dataset-comparison statistics (e.g., vocabulary overlap) to support cross-domain generality claims.  
- Weakness: the claimed novelty of "syntactic types" over prior semantic types seems overstated; main novelty is simplicity of rules.  
- Question: can the unmodified SynTime-I be evaluated on a held-out domain not used in analysis to test generalizability?  
- Question: can you provide statistics on vocabulary overlap of time tokens/modifiers across TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets?  
- Question: have you quantified how many errors stem from upstream POS tagger mistakes versus rule/keyword limitations?
