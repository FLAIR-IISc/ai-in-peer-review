- Paper proposes SynTime, a rule-based three-step pipeline (identify time tokens, expand boundaries, merge segments) for time expression recognition, evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and a Tweets corpus with high recall.
- Contribution: a simple, effective, data-driven "identify–expand–merge" heuristic that outperforms stronger rule-based and ML baselines.
- Contribution: concise statistical analysis showing time expressions are typically short, use a small recurring vocabulary, and almost always contain a core time token.
- Strength: strong empirical performance, achieving state-of-the-art results on two datasets and >92% relaxed recall across datasets.
- Strength: method is clear, simple, and well-explained, offering an intuitive alternative to opaque ML or brittle grammar systems.
- Strength: approach is well-motivated and directly grounded in empirical observations linking data analysis to system design.
- Weakness: implementation details of heuristic rules (expansion/merging and type dictionaries) are underspecified, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: ambiguity in syntactic type definitions and limited discussion of culturally specific or nonstandard temporal expressions (e.g., Ramadan, holiday seasons).
- Weakness: minor textual inconsistencies and missing analysis, notably lack of examples/analysis for the ~7% of time expressions without a time token.
- Question: will the authors release SynTime source code, token lists, and full heuristic rules to aid reproducibility?
- Question: please clarify the five `SEASON` tokens and how culturally/religiously significant periods are categorized (e.g., `HOLIDAY` vs. season).
- Question: provide an example of a valid time expression from the datasets that lacks a time token.
- Question: what are the most promising directions to close the remaining error gap—POS tagger errors, boundary expansion faults, or expressions missing time tokens?
