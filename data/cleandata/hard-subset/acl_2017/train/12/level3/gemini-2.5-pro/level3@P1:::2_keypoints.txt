- Paper introduces SynTime, a lightweight rule-based system that recognizes time expressions by identifying core time tokens and expanding with adjacent modifiers/numerals.  
- Main contribution: a simple, effective heuristic method for time expression recognition that challenges more complex systems.  
- Strength: Simplicity and efficiency — transparent, training-free, fast, and easy to debug/integrate.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance — outperforms baselines (e.g., strict F1 ~92.47% on TimeBank, ~91.77% on Tweets) with high recall.  
- Strength: Extensibility and domain adaptability — small curated keyword additions (SynTime-E) boost recall on domain-specific data (e.g., WikiWars).  
- Weakness: Risk of overfitting and limited generality because system design and SynTime-E keyword expansion used the same corpora as evaluation.  
- Weakness: No true cross-domain evaluation — final system not tested on a completely unseen domain to prove robustness.  
- Weakness: Analysis supporting general principles is largely qualitative; lacks quantitative measures (e.g., vocabulary overlap across corpora).  
- Question/request: Report performance of the final system on a completely distinct domain dataset (e.g., scientific, legal, financial) to demonstrate generality.
