- Introduces SynTime: a lightweight rule-based system that finds time tokens, expands them bidirectionally, and merges segments to extract time expressions, evaluated on three datasets with strong gains.
- Contribution: proposes a simple, effective heuristic method anchored on identifiable time tokens with expansion and merging as an alternative to complex rule-based or feature-heavy ML systems.
- Contribution: provides data-driven analysis across four datasets validating that time expressions are short and almost always contain a time token.
- Contribution: demonstrates strong empirical performance, outperforming several state-of-the-art systems and achieving very high recall, especially on informal text like tweets.
- Strength: paper is exceptionally well-written and the method is presented clearly with helpful figures illustrating the process.
- Strength: methodology is strongly motivated by preliminary analysis; design choices follow the observation that >93% of time expressions contain a time token.
- Strength: impressive experimental results showing that a lightweight, interpretable rule-based system can achieve state-of-the-art recall.
- Weakness: lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility—full expansion and merging rules are not provided.
- Weakness: some definitions and claims are underspecified (e.g., what the "5 seasons" are) and there is a minor inconsistency about the number of benchmark datasets.
- Question: please clarify the "5 seasons" and how culturally specific or non-standard time expressions (e.g., Ramadan, the holiday season) are handled.
- Question: provide an example of a time expression that does not contain a time token which the system might miss.
- Question: what are the primary sources of the remaining errors given recall near the estimated ceiling, and what are promising avenues for improvement?
- Question: do the authors plan to release the full set of rules and/or the SynTime implementation to address reproducibility?
