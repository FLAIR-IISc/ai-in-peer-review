- Proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based system for time expression recognition that uses three syntactic types and simple merging heuristics, claiming state-of-the-art results and motivated by corpus analysis.
- Empirical results: SynTime significantly outperforms baselines (SUTime, HeidelTime) on TimeBank and Tweets, with particularly high recall supporting the "time token" observation.
- Simplicity and interpretability: the method is easy to understand, debug, and runs in real time, making it attractive for practical applications.
- Well-motivated design: preliminary analysis of four corpora directly informs the syntactic types and boundary-expansion rules, creating a clear link between analysis and system design.
- Generalizability/overfitting risk: rules appear to be derived from the same corpora used for evaluation, raising concerns that performance may be overfit to those datasets.
- Lack of cross-domain validation: no analysis of vocabulary/pattern overlap across domains and no evaluation on an unseen, distinct-domain corpus to demonstrate broad applicability.
