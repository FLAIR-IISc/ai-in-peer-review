- Proposes SynTime, a simple, lightweight rule-based method for recognizing time expressions in text.  
- Corpus analysis shows time expressions are typically short, use a small vocabulary, and >93% contain a core "time token," motivating an identify-then-expand strategy.  
- SynTime defines three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and uses heuristic rules to identify core tokens and expand boundaries.  
- Evaluated on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets, SynTime outperforms several state-of-the-art rule-based and machine-learning systems.  
- Main contribution: an effective, transparent, computationally inexpensive heuristic method that achieves strong performance.  
- Main contribution: an empirical analysis of structural properties of time expressions that grounds the system design.  
- Strength: achieves impressive performance with a simple model (e.g., >91% strict F1 on TimeBank and Tweets).  
- Strength: consistently high recall across all three datasets for both strict and relaxed matching.  
- Strength: highly extensible and efficient (easy to adapt via SynTime-E, real-time tagging, no training phase).  
- Weakness: evaluation methodology risks overfitting because rules/types were developed using the same corpora used for evaluation, and SynTime-E uses training-set keywords.  
- Weakness: claims of generality are unsupported because evaluation is limited to three specific domains and no held-out domain was tested.  
- Weakness: lacks cross-corpus analysis of keyword/type overlap to validate that the type system is general rather than domain-specific.  
- Question: please test the frozen SynTime-I on a completely new, held-out dataset to demonstrate generality.  
- Question: clarify whether the initial SynTime-I token lists (from SUTime) were manually filtered, added to, or re-categorized based on TimeBank/WikiWars/Tweets before creating SynTime-I.  
- Question: provide lexical overlap statistics for time tokens and modifiers across corpora (shared vs. unique keywords between TimeBank, WikiWars, Tweets).
