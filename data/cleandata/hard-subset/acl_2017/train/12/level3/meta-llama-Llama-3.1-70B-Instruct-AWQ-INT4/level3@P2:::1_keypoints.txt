- Summary: SynTime is a type-based method that defines three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple heuristic rules to recognize short, keyword-driven time expressions; it outperforms baselines on three evaluated datasets.

- Contribution — Analysis: Provides a thorough analysis of time expressions across four datasets, showing they are typically short, use a small set of keywords, and share syntactic behavior.

- Contribution — Type-based approach: Introduces a novel syntactic-type framework (time token, modifier, numeral) and positional rules distinct from semantic rule-based taggers.

- Contribution — Heuristic rules: Designs simple, empirically motivated heuristic rules for recognizing time expressions that are flexible and extensible.

- Strength: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.

- Strength: Rules and approach are motivated by empirical observations and validated by evaluation results.

- Strength: Demonstrates significant performance improvements over state-of-the-art baselines.

- Weakness: Some implementation details (e.g., rules for time segment expansion and merging) are underspecified, hindering reproducibility.

- Weakness: Limited treatment and examples of edge cases, especially expressions lacking a time token.

- Question: How does SynTime handle holiday-season or multi-day expressions (e.g., Ramadan, Christmas) that may not fit the defined syntactic types?

- Question: With 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, what strategies can further improve performance?

- Question: Will the authors release the full rule set and software to facilitate reproducibility and further development?

- Additional comment: Clarify the discrepancy where the text mentions "two benchmark datasets" but evaluation uses three datasets.

- Additional comment: Provide an example of a time expression without a time token and explain how the system handles it.
