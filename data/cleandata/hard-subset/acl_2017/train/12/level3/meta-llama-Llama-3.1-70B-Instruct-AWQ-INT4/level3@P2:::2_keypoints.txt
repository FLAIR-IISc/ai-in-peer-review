- Proposal: SynTime — a type-based method recognizing time expressions using three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple heuristic rules.
- Dataset analysis: authors analyze four corpora and find a small set of words expressing time and similar syntactic behavior guiding design.
- Contribution 1: thorough analysis of time expressions and argument for the principle of least effort.
- Contribution 2: SynTime’s syntactic-type framework and heuristic rule-based recognition.
- Contribution 3: Empirical results showing SynTime outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets.
- Strength: method simplicity makes it easy to analyze, understand, and implement.
- Strength: experimental results demonstrate effectiveness against both rule-based and ML time taggers.
- Strength: cross-dataset analysis provides valuable insights into time-expression characteristics.
- Weakness: risk of overfitting because rules were designed and evaluated on the same corpora, with no analysis of cross-corpus overlap.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on POS tagger quality; token/POS errors can degrade performance.
- Weakness: scaling requires manual annotation/expansion of word lists, which is time-consuming and may not scale.
- Question: how will authors address overfitting and ensure generality across domains?
- Question: provide details on SynTime’s expansion process, word selection criteria, and manual annotation workflow.
- Question: how will robustness to incorrect tokens and POS tags be improved?
- Additional: paper is well-written, figures/tables are helpful, and related work review is comprehensive.
