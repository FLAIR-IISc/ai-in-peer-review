- Paper proposes SynTime, a type-based heuristic method to recognize time expressions from text and reports higher precision/recall/F1 than three baselines on three datasets.  
- Authors analyze four corpora and observe time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, and show similar syntactic behavior.  
- SynTime defines three syntactic token types (time token, modifier, numeral) and uses simple heuristic rules for recognition.  
- Main contributions: corpus analysis supporting least-effort principle, the SynTime rule-based method, and comparative experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: simplicity — lightweight, easily extended by adding keywords for types/domains.  
- Strength: effectiveness — outperforms state-of-the-art rule-based and ML baselines on evaluated datasets.  
- Strength: flexibility — applicable to varied text types, including informal text like tweets.  
- Weakness: potential overfitting because rules were designed using the same corpora as evaluation.  
- Weakness: domain bias — rule design may be biased by evaluation corpora and may not generalize to other domains.  
- Weakness: lack of statistical analysis on overlap of time expressions across corpora to support generality claims.  
- Question: How will the authors address overfitting and ensure SynTime generalizes to other corpora?  
- Question: Can the authors provide overlap statistics across corpora and results on a new, distinct corpus?  
- Question: How will SynTime be extended to handle more complex time expressions involving prepositions or descriptive phrases?
