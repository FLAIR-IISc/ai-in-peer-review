**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel rule-based approach, SynTime, for recognizing time expressions in free text. The method is based on the analysis of time expressions from four datasets, which reveals that time expressions are typically short, contain a small group of keywords, and demonstrate similar syntactic behavior. SynTime defines three main syntactic types (time token, modifier, and numeral) and uses simple heuristic rules to recognize time expressions. The approach consists of three steps: time token identification, time segment identification, and time expression extraction. The evaluation on three datasets shows that SynTime outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, including rule-based and machine learning-based methods.

**Contributions**

1. **Analysis of time expressions**: The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of time expressions from four datasets, which reveals insights into the structure and syntax of time expressions.
2. **SynTime approach**: The paper proposes a novel rule-based approach, SynTime, which defines syntactic types and uses simple heuristic rules to recognize time expressions.
3. **Evaluation results**: The paper presents evaluation results on three datasets, which demonstrate the effectiveness of SynTime against state-of-the-art baselines.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-written and clearly presented**: The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it easy to follow and understand.
2. **Rules motivated by empirical observations**: The rules used in SynTime are motivated by empirical observations from the analysis of time expressions, which adds credibility to the approach.
3. **Significant improvements over baselines**: The evaluation results show significant improvements over state-of-the-art baselines, which demonstrates the effectiveness of SynTime.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Underspecified details**: Some details of the approach, such as the exact rules used for time segment identification and time expression extraction, are not fully specified, which hinders reproducibility.
2. **Limited handling of special cases**: The paper does not provide clear explanations of how special cases, such as Ramadan or holiday seasons, are handled.

**Questions to Authors**

1. **Handling of special cases**: How are special cases, such as Ramadan or holiday seasons, handled in SynTime?
2. **Example of time expression without time token**: Can you provide an example of a time expression that does not contain a time token?
3. **Further improvement of performance**: With 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, how can performance be further improved?

**Additional Comments**

1. **Release of full set of rules/software**: Will the full set of rules and software be released to facilitate reproducibility and further development of SynTime?
2. **Clarification of dataset usage**: The text mentions "two benchmark datasets" but uses three datasets. Please clarify the usage of datasets in the evaluation.
