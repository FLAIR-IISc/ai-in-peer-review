- Summary: Proposes SynTime, a simple type-based rule-driven tagger for time expressions informed by dataset analysis, claiming better performance than prior rule-based and ML methods on benchmarks and tweets.

- Contribution — Analysis: Comprehensive analysis of time expressions from four datasets revealing common short length, small vocabulary, and similar syntactic behavior.

- Contribution — SynTime: Defines a syntactic type system for time expressions and implements simple heuristic rules for recognition.

- Contribution — Experiments: Evaluates SynTime on three datasets and compares it to state-of-the-art baselines, showing improved performance.

- Strength — Simplicity/flexibility: Lightweight, easily expanded and adapted rule-based approach.

- Strength — High performance: Outperforms state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets and tweets.

- Strength — Insights: Analysis offers valuable insights into time expression structure and syntax for future work.

- Weakness — Generality: Rules were designed using the same corpora as evaluation, risking overfitting and limited generality.

- Weakness — Domain bias: Possible corpus-driven bias with no statistics on overlap of time expressions across datasets to justify generality.

- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Only three datasets evaluated, unclear how method performs in other domains.

- Question: How will you address generality concerns and potential overfitting of rules to the evaluation corpora?

- Question: Can you provide overlap statistics across corpora and analyze how domain differences affect SynTime's performance?

- Question: Have you considered evaluating SynTime on additional datasets or domains to demonstrate broader effectiveness?

- Additional comment: Analysis and simple rule-based approach are promising, but robustness and generality need further evidence.
