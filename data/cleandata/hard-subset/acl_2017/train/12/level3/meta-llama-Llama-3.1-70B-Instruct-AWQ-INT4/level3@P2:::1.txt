**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a type-based approach, named SynTime, for recognizing time expressions in free text. The authors analyze time expressions from four datasets and observe that they are typically short, contain a small group of time-related keywords, and demonstrate similar syntactic behavior. Based on these observations, SynTime defines three main syntactic types - time token, modifier, and numeral - and designs simple heuristic rules to recognize time expressions. The approach is evaluated on three datasets and compared to three state-of-the-art baselines, showing significant improvements in performance.

**Contributions**

1. **Analysis of time expressions**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of time expressions from four datasets, revealing insights into their structure and behavior. This analysis is a significant contribution to the field of temporal information extraction.
2. **Type-based approach**: SynTime's type-based approach is a novel contribution to the field, as it defines syntactic types for tokens and designs rules based on their relative positions in time expressions. This approach is distinct from other rule-based taggers, which define types based on semantic meaning.
3. **Simple heuristic rules**: The authors design simple heuristic rules to recognize time expressions, which are motivated by empirical observations and supported by evaluation results. These rules are a significant contribution to the field, as they provide a flexible and expansible approach to time expression recognition.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-written and clearly presented**: The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it easy to follow and understand.
2. **Rules motivated by empirical observations**: The authors' approach is motivated by empirical observations and supported by evaluation results, which adds credibility to their method.
3. **Significant improvements in performance**: The evaluation results show significant improvements in performance compared to state-of-the-art baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of SynTime.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Underspecified details**: Some details, such as the specific rules used for time segment expansion and merging, are not fully specified, which hinders reproducibility.
2. **Limited handling of edge cases**: The authors do not provide examples of time expressions without a time token, which raises questions about how the system handles such cases.

**Questions to Authors**

1. **Handling of holiday seasons**: How does SynTime handle holiday seasons, such as Ramadan or Christmas, which may not fit into the defined syntactic types?
2. **Improving performance**: With 93% of expressions containing a time token and system recall at 92%, how can performance be further improved?
3. **Release of full set of rules/software**: Will the full set of rules and software be released to facilitate reproducibility and further development?

**Additional Comments**

1. **Clarification on datasets**: The text mentions "two benchmark datasets" but uses three datasets in the evaluation. Please clarify this discrepancy.
2. **Example of time expression without a time token**: Please provide an example of a time expression without a time token to illustrate how the system handles such cases.
