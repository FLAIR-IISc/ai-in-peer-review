- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based time-expression recognizer using a syntactic type system (time token, modifier, numeral).  
- Analyzes four datasets and reports four observations supporting the principle of least effort in time-expression usage.  
- Designs simple heuristic rules that outperform state-of-the-art rule-based and ML methods on three datasets.  
- Contribution: introduces a type-based approach to simplify rule-based time taggers.  
- Contribution: provides corpus analysis of time expressions across datasets.  
- Contribution: evaluates SynTime against three baselines, showing effectiveness.  
- Strength: lightweight and simple, suitable for real-time/basic applications.  
- Strength: flexible and extendable by adding time-related token regular expressions from training text.  
- Strength: achieves high recall across evaluated datasets.  
- Strength: performs particularly well on informal, noisy text (tweets).  
- Weakness: rules were designed using the same corpora as evaluation, risking overfitting.  
- Weakness: lacks statistics on cross-corpus overlap of time expressions to justify generality.  
- Weakness: depends on correct tokenization and POS tagging, which may not hold in practice.  
- Question: How will the authors address overfitting and ensure generalizability to other datasets?  
- Question: Can the authors detail how SynTime can be expanded and adapted to other domains and languages?  
- Question: How will SynTime handle tokenization/POS tagging errors to remain robust?
