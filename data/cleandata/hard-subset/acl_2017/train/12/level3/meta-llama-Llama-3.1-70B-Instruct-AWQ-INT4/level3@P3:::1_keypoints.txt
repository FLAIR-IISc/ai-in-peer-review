- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based time-expression extractor premised on time expressions being short and containing at least one time token.
- Analyzes four datasets and derives four observations supporting the central insight.
- Defines a syntactic type system: 15 time-token types, 5 modifier types, and 1 numeral type.
- Implements simple heuristic, boundary-expansion rules based on the type system.
- Main contributions: dataset analysis, the type-based tagger SynTime, and an evaluation against three baselines on three datasets.
- Evaluation results: outperforms baselines on TimeBank and Tweets; comparable performance on WikiWars.
- Strength: clear presentation of the idea and method.
- Strength: thorough, detailed analysis that justifies SynTime’s design choices.
- Strength: strong performance in informal text (tweets).
- Weakness: insufficient detail about the “5 seasons” in Section 4.1 and unclear handling of Ramadan/holiday seasons.
- Weakness: inconsistency in text claiming “two benchmark datasets” while using three.
- Weakness: claim that SynTime can recognize time expressions without time tokens lacks an illustrative example.
- Weakness: reports 92% recall and notes 93% of expressions have time tokens, but does not explain avenues for further improvement.
- Recommendation: release the full rule set and software to enable reproducibility and further research.
- Reviewer priorities: (1) SynTime tagger, (2) dataset analysis, (3) evaluation against baselines.
- Key questions for authors: clarify seasons handling, dataset-count inconsistency, provide tokenless-expression example, explain how to improve performance, and state plans to release rules/software.
