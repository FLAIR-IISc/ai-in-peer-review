**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a rule-based method for time expression extraction, called SynTime, which is based on syntactic types and simple heuristic rules. The method is motivated by empirical observations of time expressions in four datasets, which show that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token. The authors define a syntactic type system for time expressions, which includes 15 types for time tokens, 5 types for modifiers, and 1 type for numerals. The method uses dictionary lookup, regex matches, and POS tagging to recognize time tokens, and then expands the time tokens bidirectionally to form time segments, which are then merged into single time expressions using another set of rules. The authors evaluate SynTime on three datasets and compare it with three state-of-the-art baselines, showing significant improvements in performance.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Proposed Method**: The paper proposes a novel rule-based method for time expression extraction, called SynTime, which is based on syntactic types and simple heuristic rules.
2. **Empirical Observations**: The authors provide empirical evidence for the principle of least effort in time expression usage, which motivates the design of SynTime.
3. **Evaluation**: The authors evaluate SynTime on three datasets and compare it with three state-of-the-art baselines, showing significant improvements in performance.
