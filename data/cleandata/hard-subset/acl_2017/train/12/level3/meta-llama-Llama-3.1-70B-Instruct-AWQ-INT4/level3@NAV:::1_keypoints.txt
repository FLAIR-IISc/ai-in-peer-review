- Proposes SynTime: a rule-based time expression extractor using syntactic types and simple heuristic rules.  
- Motivated by empirical finding that time expressions are typically short and contain at least one time token (principle of least effort).  
- Defines a syntactic type system: 15 time-token types, 5 modifier types, and 1 numeral type.  
- Recognition pipeline uses dictionary lookup, regex, and POS tagging, expands tokens bidirectionally, then merges segments with rules.  
- Evaluated on three datasets against three state-of-the-art baselines, showing significant performance improvements.  
- Main contributions: novel rule-based method, empirical evidence motivating design, and evaluation demonstrating improvements.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Strength: method is motivated by empirical observations of time expression usage.  
- Strength: demonstrates significant improvement over baselines.  
- Weakness: some implementation details and specific rules for segment identification/extraction are underspecified.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope—only three datasets and no broader generalization analysis.  
- Question: provide more details on the exact rules for time segment identification and extraction.  
- Question: how are cases handled where time tokens are not explicitly mentioned (implicit times)?  
- Question: provide more dataset information, e.g., number of time expressions and their distribution.  
- Additional comment: novel and effective approach but needs clearer methodological specification and broader evaluation.
