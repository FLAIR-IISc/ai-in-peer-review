- Empirical analysis: time expressions are very short (80% ≤3 tokens).  
- Empirical analysis: time-expression vocabulary is very small.  
- Empirical analysis: ≥93% of time expressions contain at least one time token.  
- Empirical analysis: words in time expressions show similar syntactic behaviors.  
- Proposal: SynTime — a rule-based recognizer using syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and boundary-expansion heuristics.  
- Contribution: Provides a theoretical foundation linking findings to Zipf’s principle of least effort.  
- Contribution: Presents a lightweight, real-time, training-free system that is easy to extend.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates strong performance across TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets, notably high F1 on Tweets.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical grounding and well-supported cross-domain analysis.  
- Strength: Simple, effective, and practical methodology focusing on syntactic types.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation showing consistent improvements over HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime.  
- Strength: Clear writing and good figures that link observations to the method.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison with recent neural/transformer-based time-expression methods.  
- Weakness: Insufficient error analysis and limited insight into specific failure modes or why it outperforms baselines.  
- Weakness: Overstates the syntactic vs. semantic types distinction without fully justifying its practical significance.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations (robustness to tokenization/POS errors, non-standard syntax, other languages).  
- Weakness: No ablation study to isolate the impact of design choices (types, heuristics, expansion).  
- Question: How many additional keywords are needed to adapt SynTime to a new domain (e.g., medical) compared to other rule systems?  
- Question: Can you provide evidence that POS tagging cannot reliably distinguish time expressions from common words?  
- Question: How robust is SynTime to incorrect tokenization or POS tagging in practice?  
- Question: Provide examples of WikiWars prepositional/descriptive time expressions that SynTime misses and why.  
- Question: How exactly does boundary expansion outperform fixed-length rules, and which patterns benefit most?
