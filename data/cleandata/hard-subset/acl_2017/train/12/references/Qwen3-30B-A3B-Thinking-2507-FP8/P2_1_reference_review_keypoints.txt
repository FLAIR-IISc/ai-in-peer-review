- Summary: Paper analyzes time expressions across four datasets and proposes SynTime, a rule-based recognizer using syntactic types and heuristic boundary expansion.

- Contribution 1: Thorough cross-domain linguistic analysis revealing short expressions, small keyword vocabulary, high presence of time tokens, and similar syntactic behavior.

- Contribution 2: Introduces a novel syntactic-type system (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) as an alternative to semantic types for time expression recognition.

- Contribution 3: Presents SynTime, a lightweight, real-time rule-based system that outperforms baselines on two datasets.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical foundation with convincing statistics across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets.

- Strength 2: Clear conceptual distinction from prior rule-based systems by focusing on syntactic behavior and boundary expansion heuristics.

- Strength 3: Strong experimental results, including state-of-the-art performance and high recall on TimeBank and Tweets.

- Strength 4: Practical value emphasized—system is lightweight and runs in real time compared to heavier ML approaches.

- Strength 5: Provides linguistic grounding linked to Zipf’s principle of least effort.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—approach remains fundamentally rule-based and may not materially differ from existing systems like SUTime.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison with more recent neural or transformer-based time expression methods.

- Weakness 3: Does not analyze sensitivity to tokenization or POS-tagging errors, leaving real-world robustness unclear.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient analysis and examples of failure cases (notably on WikiWars) to explain limitations.

- Weakness 5: No ablation studies to isolate the contributions of syntactic types, heuristics, and boundary expansion.

- Question 1: Provide examples of SynTime failures (especially from WikiWars) and how to modify the system to handle them.

- Question 2: Why not compare with recent neural approaches (e.g., BERT-based), and how would SynTime fare against them?

- Question 3: Provide quantitative runtime and memory comparisons to substantiate real-time, lightweight claims.

- Question 4: How sensitive is SynTime to different POS taggers (e.g., spaCy vs. Stanford); any evaluations?

- Question 5: Explain how the expanded token set improved performance on Tweets and the role of informal variants.

- Recommendation: Weak accept (3/5) — solid empirical contribution and practical results but limited novelty and insufficient comparisons/analyses.
