- Paper proposes SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and heuristic boundary-expansion rules, evaluated on TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets.

- Key dataset observations: time expressions are very short (80% ≤3 tokens), use a small vocabulary, ≥93% contain time tokens, and words show similar syntactic behaviors.

- Contribution: insightful linguistic analysis of time expressions grounded in Zipf’s principle of least effort.

- Contribution: a syntactic type system (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) for time expression recognition as an alternative to semantic type systems.

- Contribution: strong empirical results, notably 95.87% relaxed F1 on Tweets and high recall (92%+) across datasets.

- Strength: comprehensive, data-driven linguistic analysis with solid statistical support (tables/figures).

- Strength: well-motivated, linguistically grounded syntactic type system distinguishing modifier positions and behaviors.

- Strength: empirically strong performance, especially on noisy Twitter data, aligning with the paper’s analysis.

- Weakness: novelty overstated — boundary expansion and heuristic rules are similar to prior rule-based systems (e.g., SUTime).

- Weakness: limited evaluation — only three datasets used, omission of standard TempEval benchmarks and recent TempEval systems.

- Weakness: unsubstantiated runtime claims — no runtime measurements or comparisons provided.

- Weakness: some related work is mischaracterized (e.g., SUTime’s approach and flexibility are misrepresented).

- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure cases and limitations, lacking examples of problematic expressions or ambiguous cases.

- Question: provide a detailed comparison between SynTime’s heuristic rules and those in SUTime/HeidelTime — what specifically is simpler or different?

- Question: supply runtime measurements (time per sentence/document) and comparisons to baselines to support “light-weight” and “real-time” claims.

- Question: show concrete examples where the syntactic type system outperforms semantic categorization.

- Question: explain impact of excluding prepositions (e.g., “on”) from time expressions on performance with TimeML/TimeBank conventions.

- Question: clarify handling of informal variants in tweets (e.g., “2day”) and their effect on SynTime’s performance.

- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions to clarify novelty, add runtime data, and strengthen comparisons and failure analysis.

- Rating: acceptable with major revisions.
