- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer analyzed across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets.  
- Four empirical observations: most time expressions are very short, use a small time-related vocabulary, ≥93% contain time tokens, and tokens show similar syntactic behavior.  
- Introduces a syntactic type system (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) inspired by POS tagging and uses position-based heuristic rules for boundary expansion.  
- Evaluated against HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime; outperforms baselines on TimeBank and Tweets and is comparable on WikiWars.  
- Main contribution: thorough multi-dataset empirical analysis revealing consistent patterns in time expressions.  
- Main contribution: conceptual shift to syntactic types for time expressions rather than semantic typing.  
- Main contribution: simple, lightweight, training-free rule-based system with strong performance and high recall.  
- Strength: comprehensive and data-supported empirical analysis linked to Zipf’s principle of least effort.  
- Strength: clear distinction between syntactic and semantic type approaches for temporal expressions.  
- Strength: strong experimental results with especially high recall (≥92% relaxed match) and excellent Tweet performance.  
- Strength: practical advantages—real-time, no training, low resource requirements.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with supportive figures and tables.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—implementation refines existing rule-based systems and shares similar heuristics with SUTime.  
- Weakness: no direct experimental comparison between syntactic- and semantic-type systems to support claimed superiority.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to three datasets (Gigaword excluded), raising generalizability concerns and handling of atypical/complex expressions.  
- Weakness: dependency on correct POS tagging not thoroughly analyzed or mitigated, a real-world limitation.  
- Weakness: overstates link to Zipf’s principle without conclusive evidence that patterns stem from that principle.  
- Question: clarify token selection during SynTime expansion and its scalability to domains with different patterns.  
- Question: how would SynTime handle atypical/verbose time expressions (e.g., in WikiWars) and what modifications are needed?  
- Question: can the authors implement a semantic-type variant of SynTime (e.g., SUTime-style) for direct comparison?  
- Question: how do findings about formal word use in tweets affect handling of creative/abbreviated time expressions in other informal contexts?  
- Question: provide examples of tokens added by SynTime-E (notably for WikiWars) and explain their omission from the base system.  
- Recommendation: Accept — empirical analysis and syntactic type system are significant, experimental results strong despite some limitations.
