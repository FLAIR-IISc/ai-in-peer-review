- Observation: Time expressions are very short (80% ≤ 3 tokens).  
- Observation: Time expressions use a small, consistent vocabulary of time-related keywords.  
- Observation: At least 93% of time expressions contain at least one explicit time token.  
- Observation: Words in time expressions show similar syntactic behaviors supporting a syntactic-type approach.

- Contribution: Thorough cross-dataset empirical analysis supporting the principle of least effort in time expressions.  
- Contribution: Novel syntactic type-based definition for time-related tokens (vs. semantic typing).  
- Contribution: Lightweight rule-based SynTime with boundary-expansion heuristics achieving strong, real-time performance without training data.

- Strength: Strong empirical evidence and analyses (Tables/Figures) across four datasets.  
- Strength: Compelling experimental results, high recall and strong F1—especially on Tweets.  
- Strength: Practical simplicity and efficiency; real-time rule-based system requiring minimal manual expansion.

- Weakness: Methodological novelty is limited; syntactic-typing claim may be mostly semantic framing.  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons — missing recent deep learning/transformer-based baselines.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of limitations (e.g., handling prepositional/descriptive time phrases).  
- Weakness: No ablation study to quantify the contributions of components (syntactic types vs. boundary expansion).

- Question: How does SynTime’s syntactic type system fundamentally differ from SUTime’s implicit syntactic/POS-based rules?  
- Question: How are informal variants (e.g., “2day”) handled—was the small token expansion in Table 5 sufficient?  
- Question: How does SynTime overcome POS limitations for ambiguous words (e.g., “May”) while still using POS for filtering?  
- Question: Why were Gigaword labels excluded; what is the quality of those automatically generated labels and their suitability for evaluation?

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — solid empirical contribution, practical value, needs clearer distinction from prior work and broader comparisons.
