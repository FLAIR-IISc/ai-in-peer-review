- Paper analyzes time expressions across four datasets and observes they are short, use a small vocabulary, almost always contain a time token, and share syntactic behaviors; proposes SynTime, a rule-based recognizer using syntactic types and heuristic boundary rules.  
- Contribution 1: Comprehensive, data-driven analysis showing consistent patterns across news, Wikipedia, and tweets, linked to Zipf's principle.  
- Contribution 2: Novel syntactic type system for time expressions that defines types by syntactic behavior rather than semantics.  
- Contribution 3: Lightweight, real-time rule-based system (SynTime) achieving strong performance without training data.  
- Strength 1: Strong empirical analysis with tables/figures supporting four key observations across diverse datasets.  
- Strength 2: Practical system design that attains high recall (notably on TimeBank and Tweets) by leveraging the high prevalence of time tokens.  
- Strength 3: Thorough experimental evaluation against three baselines on three datasets, including strict/relaxed metrics and an expanded SynTime-E variant.  
- Weakness 1: Limited novelty — syntactic types + heuristics are similar to prior rule-based systems (e.g., SUTime), and the syntactic vs. semantic distinction is not convincingly justified.  
- Weakness 2: Insufficient comparison to related rule-based work and lack of detailed differentiation from systems that might use syntactic features.  
- Weakness 3: Lacks in-depth error analysis explaining why SynTime outperforms baselines or concrete examples of successes/failures tied to the observations.  
- Weakness 4: Limited discussion of limitations — reliance on correct tokenization/POS, handling of non-standard expressions, and generalization to other languages/domains are underexplored.  
- Question 1: Request examples where syntactic-type approaches succeed but semantic-type approaches fail, and explanations why.  
- Question 2: Clarify what constitutes a "small" vocabulary given 60–80 distinct time tokens and reconcile this with the claim of small size.  
- Question 3: Provide examples of tokens added in SynTime-E for each dataset and rationale for additions.  
- Question 4: Explain handling of informal/tweet-specific abbreviations (e.g., "2day") and whether such tokens were included in the time token list.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen novelty claims and provide deeper analysis of strengths, limitations, and relation to prior work; overall score 7/10.
