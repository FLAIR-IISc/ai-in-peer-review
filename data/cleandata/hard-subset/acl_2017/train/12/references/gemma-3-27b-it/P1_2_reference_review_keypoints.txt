- Summary: SynTime is a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple heuristics, evaluated across multiple datasets with strong Twitter performance.

- Contribution: Empirical analysis of time expressions across datasets revealing shortness, limited vocabulary, high time-token presence, and consistent syntactic behavior.

- Contribution: Proposal of a syntactic type-based rule system (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) as an alternative to semantic-based rules like SUTime.

- Contribution: Experimental validation showing competitive results with state-of-the-art methods and notable improvements on noisy Twitter data.

- Strength: Motivating analysis is thorough and links observations to linguistic principles (e.g., Zipf’s law).

- Strength: Approach is clearly presented; syntactic types and simple heuristic rules are easy to understand and follow.

- Strength: Boundary expansion heuristic effectively captures variations in time expression structures.

- Strength: SynTime is lightweight and fast, making it suitable for real-time or resource-constrained applications.

- Strength: Thorough comparison against strong baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime) demonstrates relative strengths.

- Weakness: Fundamental approach remains rule-based with limited novelty and inherent constraints for unseen variations and domain shifts.

- Weakness: Exclusion of Gigaword reduces evaluation scope; Twitter dataset size (1,127 expressions) is small given variability.

- Weakness: Paper lacks detailed error analysis to explain specific failure modes and differences from baselines.

- Weakness: Reliance on POS tagging is a vulnerability; impact of POS errors is not empirically explored.

- Question: How scalable is SynTime to very different domains (e.g., medical or legal) and what adaptation effort is required?

- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed error analysis categorizing SynTime’s errors versus baseline errors?

- Question: How sensitive is SynTime to POS tagging quality and have experiments quantified this effect?

- Question: Could syntactic types and heuristics be used to bootstrap or integrate learning-based components to refine rules?

- Additional: Paper is well-written, provides code, has comprehensive references, and would benefit from a comparison table versus baseline rule systems.
