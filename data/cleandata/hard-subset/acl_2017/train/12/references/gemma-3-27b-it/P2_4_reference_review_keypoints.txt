- Overall score: 7/10 — Strong Accept with Minor Revisions.

- Summary: SynTime is a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and heuristic boundary expansion, motivated by empirical analysis across four datasets and showing strong recall and competitive performance.

- Contribution 1: Empirical analysis of time expressions across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets, revealing short length, small vocabulary, and syntactic consistency.

- Contribution 2: Novel type-based rule design that classifies tokens syntactically (time token, modifier, numeral) and uses boundary expansion instead of semantic typing.

- Contribution 3: Strong empirical results—outperforming baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime) on three datasets, especially high recall on Twitter data.

- Strength: Well-motivated approach grounded in dataset analysis and Zipf’s principle of least effort.

- Strength: Clear, concise presentation with helpful visuals and logical methodology.

- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation including comparisons to established baselines and an ablation study (SynTime-I vs SynTime-E).

- Strength: Practical, lightweight design suited for real-time, efficient deployment.

- Strength: Addresses some limitations of existing rule-based and ML methods, offering a viable alternative.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—rule-based approach itself is not new; need clearer differentiation from prior rule systems.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on SUTime regular expressions for initialization raises questions about originality of token recognition.

- Weakness: Limited error analysis—paper lacks detailed qualitative breakdown of failure modes and problematic expression types.

- Weakness: Dataset selection/exclusion (e.g., Gigaword) justification feels slightly weak and may introduce selection bias concerns.

- Question: Please elaborate trade-offs between semantic vs. syntactic typing and challenges designing semantic types.

- Question: Quantify how much performance comes from SUTime’s initial regexes versus SynTime’s type-based rules and boundary expansion.

- Question: Provide concrete examples of SynTime errors and explanations for failures.

- Question: Explain the process and safeguards for manually adding keywords to expand the system without introducing ambiguity.

- Additional comment: Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.

- Additional comment: Recommend adding discussion of computational complexity.

- Additional comment: Suggest brief comparison to recent neural approaches for context.

- Conclusion: Well-executed paper offering a fresh, effective type-based rule approach with strong results; addressing noted weaknesses will strengthen its contribution.
