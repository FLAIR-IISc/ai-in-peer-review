- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; solid, well-motivated, empirically sound work with strong results on noisy Twitter data.
- Contribution: Empirical analysis of time expressions across datasets highlighting short length, small vocabulary, and consistent syntactic behavior.
- Contribution: SynTime — a simplified, type-based rule-based tagger using syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple heuristic rules.
- Contribution: Strong empirical results showing SynTime outperforms or matches baselines, notably on Twitter.
- Strength: Strong motivation and justification grounded in data-driven observations and the principle of least effort.
- Strength: Clear and concise presentation; explanations and figures (e.g., Figure 2) effectively illustrate the method.
- Strength: Competitive performance across multiple datasets, with notable robustness on noisy data.
- Strength: Practicality and efficiency — lightweight, real-time, and easier to implement than complex ML approaches.
- Strength: Thorough experimentation using standard datasets and both strict and relaxed matching metrics.
- Weakness: Core idea lacks strong novelty; rule-based and syntactic approaches are not entirely new.
- Weakness: Dependence on POS tagging introduces potential failure modes tied to tagger accuracy.
- Weakness: Limited error analysis; paper does not detail common failure cases or patterns missed by SynTime.
- Weakness: Dataset choice concerns — Gigaword excluded for label quality; broader discussion of noisy automatic labels is lacking.
- Question: Provide a detailed comparison of SynTime’s rule complexity (number/length of rules) versus SUTime and HeidelTime.
- Question: Quantify SynTime’s sensitivity to POS tagger accuracy; any experiments on this sensitivity?
- Question: Give examples of time expressions SynTime frequently fails on and propose strategies to address them.
- Question: What strategies could mitigate noisy labels in datasets like Gigaword for evaluation or training?
- Additional: Reference list is comprehensive.
- Additional: Code availability on GitHub is a significant strength.
- Additional: Paper is well-polished and requires only minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
