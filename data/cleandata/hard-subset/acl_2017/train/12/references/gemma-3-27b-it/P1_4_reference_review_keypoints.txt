- Summary: SynTime is a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and simple heuristics, evaluated against HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime with strong recall, especially on Tweets.
- Contribution 1: Empirical analysis of time expressions across four datasets, quantifying length, vocabulary, and syntactic behavior.
- Contribution 2: Novel framing of rules around syntactic types rather than semantic categories to simplify rule creation and expansion.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates robust performance on noisy, informal Tweets, showing practical applicability.
- Strength 1: Well-motivated design linking observations to system choices, supported by Zipf’s principle rationale.
- Strength 2: Clear, concise presentation with detailed architecture and helpful figures (e.g., Figure 2).
- Strength 3: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and strong baseline comparisons.
- Strength 4: Focus on high recall to avoid missing time expressions, beneficial for downstream tasks.
- Strength 5: Lightweight and fast, suitable for real-time applications.
- Weakness 1: Limited novelty since the core approach remains rule-based compared to learning-based methods.
- Weakness 2: Heavy dependence on accurate POS tagging and tokenization, with robustness to noisy preprocessing unquantified.
- Weakness 3: Limited error analysis; failure modes and problematic expression types are not deeply examined.
- Weakness 4: Expansion methodology (Section 4.3) under-specified; lacks concrete examples of added tokens/modifiers.
- Weakness 5: No ablation study to attribute gains to the type system versus heuristic rules.
- Question 1: Provide a detailed breakdown of SynTime’s error types across datasets and consistently misidentified classes.
- Question 2: Quantify sensitivity to POS/tagging/tokenization errors, e.g., via experiments with noisy preprocessing.
- Question 3: Clarify criteria and examples for selecting new time tokens and modifiers in the expansion phase.
- Question 4: Report computational cost scaling (e.g., processing time per sentence) with input size and rule count.
- Question 5: Compare SynTime-I to UWTime trained only on TimeBank to enable a fair learning vs rule-based comparison.
- Additional comment 1: References are lengthy but useful.
- Additional comment 2: Excluding Gigaword due to label quality is reasonable.
- Additional comment 3: Availability of a GitHub repository is a positive aspect.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-motivated paper with strong results (recommend acceptance with minor revisions).
