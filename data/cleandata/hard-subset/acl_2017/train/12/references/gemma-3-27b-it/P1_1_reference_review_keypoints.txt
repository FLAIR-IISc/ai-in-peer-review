- SynTime is a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple heuristics; it outperforms baselines on TimeBank and Tweets, is comparable on WikiWars, and achieves high recall.

- Contribution: Empirical analysis of time expression length, vocabulary, and syntactic patterns across datasets that motivates the method.

- Contribution: Novel rule design based on syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) rather than semantic categories.

- Contribution: Strong experimental results, especially large improvements on the noisy Tweet dataset.

- Strength: Solid empirical motivation grounded in observed linguistic properties and the principle of least effort.

- Strength: Simplicity and efficiency—uses lightweight heuristic rules and reports real-time performance.

- Strength: Robust performance on Tweets, indicating resilience to social-media noise and informality.

- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation that makes the method and evaluation easy to follow.

- Strength: Thorough benchmarking against strong baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime).

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—the approach remains fundamentally rule-based beyond the syntactic-typing twist.

- Weakness: Dependence on POS tagging accuracy is acknowledged but not quantified or mitigated.

- Weakness: Manual rule expansion may not scale to new domains or languages; automated adaptation strategies are not developed.

- Weakness: Lacks a detailed error analysis to identify systematic failure modes and difficult expression types.

- Question: Can the authors quantify the impact of POS tagging errors and report experiments with different taggers or noise-handling methods?

- Question: What concrete strategies for automated rule adaptation (beyond manual keyword addition) do the authors envision?

- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed error analysis outlining which time-expression types cause failures and common error patterns?

- Question: For the Tweet dataset, what was SUTime’s initial error rate and how many manual corrections were made to produce the ground truth?

- Question: How does SynTime-I compare to SUTime on non-Tweet datasets to clarify the contribution of syntactic typing outside social media?
