- Paper presents SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognition system driven by syntactic types and simple heuristics.  
- Empirical analysis shows time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, and exhibit consistent syntactic behavior across datasets.  
- Authors propose a type-based approach (syntactic roles: time token, modifier, numeral) instead of semantic categories, motivated by Zipf’s principle.  
- SynTime uses simple heuristic rules and boundary expansion, initialized in part from SUTime rules.  
- Evaluated against three baselines on three datasets, SynTime is competitive and achieves notably high recall on the Tweets dataset.  
- Contribution: concise multi-dataset analysis that supports the design choices.  
- Contribution: syntactic typing offers a different, practical perspective for rule definition.  
- Contribution: strong empirical performance on noisy social media text, indicating robustness.  
- Strength: clear motivation and theoretical grounding linking observations to design.  
- Strength: sound, well-documented methodology and reasonable experimental setup.  
- Strength: practical, efficient, and simple design suitable for real-time use.  
- Strength: detailed description of type definitions and rules enabling reproducibility.  
- Strength: emphasis on high recall, appropriate for downstream tasks like event extraction.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—rule-based approach is incremental and not fundamentally new.  
- Weakness: insufficient error analysis and lack of illustrative failure cases (e.g., WikiWars descriptive expressions).  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on SUTime resources without clear demonstration of substantive building upon them.  
- Weakness: no ablation study to quantify the contribution of syntactic typing versus other components.  
- Weakness: dependency on POS tagging introduces risk; impact of tagging errors is not quantified.  
- Question: provide quantitative comparison of rule counts in SynTime versus SUTime (pre/post initialization).  
- Question: give concrete examples of time expressions SynTime misses and explain why.  
- Question: how well would SynTime generalize to other domains (legal, scientific) without adaptation?  
- Question: why was no ablation study performed, or can one be provided to isolate component contributions?  
- Question: have you measured the impact of POS tagging errors on performance, and can those results be shared?  
- Additional comment: fix incomplete references (e.g., "Alonso et al., 2011").  
- Additional comment: avoid informal phrasing like "extremely light-weight" in the paper.  
- Additional comment: expand related work discussion to include more recent (especially deep learning) approaches.  
- Overall assessment: solid, practical contribution with promising results (especially on Twitter); recommend acceptance if weaknesses—error analysis, originality relative to SUTime, and ablation/POS robustness—are addressed.
