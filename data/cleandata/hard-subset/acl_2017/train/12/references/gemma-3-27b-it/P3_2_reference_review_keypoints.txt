- Summary: SynTime is a rule-based time expression recognizer built from an empirical analysis across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets, using syntactic types and simple heuristics, and it outperforms baselines especially on Tweets.

- Contribution 1: Empirical analysis showing time expressions are short, use limited vocabulary, and have consistent syntactic structure across datasets.

- Contribution 2: A lightweight type-based rule system (time token, modifier, numeral) with boundary-expansion heuristics for recognition.

- Contribution 3: Strong empirical results demonstrating competitive or superior performance to HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime, notably on noisy Twitter data.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated by data-driven insights linking Zipf’s principle to system design.

- Strength 2: Clear, concise presentation with helpful examples and understandable system architecture.

- Strength 3: Empirically validated with strong recall and competitive overall performance across datasets.

- Strength 4: Lightweight and efficient, suitable for real-time processing.

- Strength 5: Practical expansion strategy via keyword addition without heavy retraining.

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty — rule-based recognition is well-known and differences from existing systems (SUTime/HeidelTime) are not fully articulated.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient error analysis; paper lacks detail on common failure modes and ambiguous cases.

- Weakness 3: Reliance on POS tagging raises robustness concerns; impact of noisy tags is not deeply evaluated.

- Weakness 4: Comparison to UWTime is incomplete/mischaracterized given UWTime’s rule-based components.

- Weakness 5: Dataset selection rationale (excluding Gigaword) and potential biases from only using manual annotations need stronger justification.

- Question 1: Provide a detailed rule-level comparison between SynTime and SUTime, with examples of complements and gaps.

- Question 2: Present a brief error analysis identifying common error types and potential fixes.

- Question 3: How sensitive is SynTime to POS tagger accuracy; were different taggers or mitigation strategies tested?

- Question 4: Clarify the concrete differences between SynTime and UWTime and why SynTime outperforms it.

- Question 5: Why were the specific train/test splits chosen and were they checked for representativeness?

- Additional comments: References are comprehensive and code is available on GitHub; consider caveats about using the "principle of least effort" as a universal linguistic guide.

- Overall: Solid paper with pragmatic design and strong results; recommended for acceptance pending addressing the listed weaknesses.
