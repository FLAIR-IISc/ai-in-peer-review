- Paper introduces SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple heuristics.
- Authors analyze time expressions across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets, finding they are short, use limited vocabulary, often contain time tokens, and show consistent syntactic behavior.
- Proposal: a type-based approach (time token, modifier, numeral) plus boundary-expansion heuristics for recognition.
- Evaluation vs. HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime shows SynTime outperforms on TimeBank and Tweets and is comparable on WikiWars, especially in recall.
- Empirical analysis of time-expression characteristics across datasets is a valuable contribution and motivates the approach.
- Framing recognition via syntactic typing (rather than purely semantic) is a noteworthy conceptual shift.
- Demonstrates that a simple, type-based rule system can be competitive and efficient, with strong recall and good real-time performance.
- Methodology is clearly described, with an effective diagram and sensible heuristics (boundary expansion).
- Strong empirical motivation linking observations to design choices strengthens the paper.
- Competitive results on the challenging Tweets dataset highlight robustness in noisy domains.
- Efficiency and real-time performance are significant practical advantages over heavier methods.
- Good choice of baseline systems (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime) for comparison.
- Core approach has limited novelty since it remains fundamentally rule-based without new parsing techniques.
- Paper lacks a detailed qualitative error analysis to show where SynTime fails and why.
- Reliance on an external POS tagger (Stanford POS Tagger) is a potential failure point not quantitatively evaluated.
- Performance appears to require domain-specific adaptation (e.g., expansion for WikiWars), limiting out-of-the-box generalizability.
- Excluding Gigaword due to label quality concerns is insufficiently justified; including it or validating labels would be preferable.
- Reviewer requests a detailed error analysis identifying common failure causes (POS errors, rule limits, ambiguity).
- Reviewer asks for sensitivity analysis to POS-tagging errors and experiments with alternative or robust tagging approaches.
- Reviewer requests details on the cost and process of expanding SynTime to new domains, including manual annotation effort.
- Reviewer asks for clarification and validation regarding the Gigaword exclusion and label quality concerns.
- Reviewer requests computational resource comparisons (memory, CPU/time) between SynTime and baselines.
- References, writing quality, and the Zipf’s principle connection are noted positively.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on adding error analysis and discussion of generalizability and POS-tag dependencies.
