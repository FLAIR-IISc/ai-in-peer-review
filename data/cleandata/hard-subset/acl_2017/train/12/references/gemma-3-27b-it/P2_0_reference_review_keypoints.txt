- Paper presents SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple heuristic rules, evaluated against baselines on three datasets.  
- Empirical analysis shows time expressions are short, use limited vocabulary, have high token overlap, and exhibit consistent syntactic behavior.  
- Main contribution: dataset-driven empirical analysis motivating system design.  
- Main contribution: syntactic typing (time token, modifier, numeral) as a basis for recognition rather than semantic categories.  
- Main contribution: a lightweight, expandable heuristic-rule system with strong performance, especially on noisy Tweets.  
- Strength: strong motivation tying design to observed linguistic properties and principle of least effort.  
- Strength: competitive experimental performance, notably high recall on the Tweet dataset.  
- Strength: simplicity, expandability, and real-time efficiency of the rule-based approach.  
- Strength: solid baseline comparisons with HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime.  
- Strength: well-written, clearly organized presentation.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—rule-based approach is not fundamentally new beyond the type definition.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis identifying common failure modes.  
- Weakness: dependency on POS tagger quality is acknowledged but not quantified or experimentally explored.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to standard datasets, raising questions about generalizability to more diverse domains.  
- Question: why prioritize syntactic types over semantic types, and what challenges of semantic approaches does this address?  
- Question: can authors provide a brief error analysis highlighting common SynTime errors?  
- Question: how sensitive is SynTime to POS tagging accuracy and have different taggers or error impacts been tested?  
- Question: why were Gigaword labels deemed unreliable and thus excluded?  
- Additional comment: reference list formatting contains inconsistencies.  
- Additional comment: Figure 2 (SynTime overview) is helpful for understanding system architecture.  
- Overall assessment: recommend acceptance with minor revisions, urging more error analysis and clearer comparison to semantic methods.
