- Overall: Well-written rule-based system (SynTime) with modest improvements, especially on Twitter, but not sufficiently novel for strong acceptance.  
- Claimed contributions: Analysis of time expression properties, proposal of SynTime using syntactic types and heuristics, and empirical evaluation against baselines.  
- Contribution assessment: Empirical evaluation is useful but typing scheme and analysis are incremental rather than a major theoretical advance.  
- Strength — Clear motivation: Paper is well-structured and ties observations to Zipf’s principle of least effort.  
- Strength — Strong Twitter results: Notable performance gains on noisy, informal text where simple rules excel.  
- Strength — Practicality: Lightweight, real-time, no training phase, suitable for resource-limited settings.  
- Strength — Baseline comparison: Thorough evaluation against HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness — Limited novelty: Rule-based approach and typing scheme are incremental and not clearly distinct from prior work.  
- Weakness — Reliance on SUTime resources: Heavy use of SUTime regexes raises questions about the true source of performance gains.  
- Weakness — Limited error analysis: Paper lacks detailed breakdowns of failure modes and complex-case behavior.  
- Weakness — Potential dataset bias: Tweet dataset construction using SUTime pre-tagging and manual correction may introduce bias.  
- Question — Ablation: Provide ablation comparing SynTime with its own regexes only versus using SUTime expressions.  
- Question — Error analysis: Supply a detailed error analysis categorizing missed expressions, boundary errors, and misclassifications.  
- Question — Typing justification: Explain rationale for choosing the three types and discuss possible alternative type systems.  
- Question — Tweet dataset handling: Clarify how initial SUTime tagging was used and how manual annotation avoided bias.  
- Question — Scalability & maintenance: Describe how easily rules adapt to new domains/languages and the process for updating rules.  
- Additional comment — References and reproducibility: Reference list is solid and GitHub link aids reproducibility.  
- Additional comment — Limitations: Consider discussing inherent limits of rule-based approaches for evolving language.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid execution and useful Twitter results but limited novelty and methodological concerns.
