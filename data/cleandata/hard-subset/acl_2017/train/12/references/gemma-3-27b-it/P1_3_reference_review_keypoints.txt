- Paper introduces SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and boundary expansion heuristics.
- Empirical analysis shows time expressions are short, use limited vocabulary, often contain time tokens, and have consistent syntax across datasets.
- SynTime defines syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and applies simple heuristic rules for recognition.
- SynTime outperforms several baselines on TimeBank and Tweets and matches performance on WikiWars, with consistently high recall.
- Contribution: thorough cross-dataset analysis of time expression characteristics that motivates the design.
- Contribution: principled syntactic-type-based rule design as an alternative to semantic rule sets.
- Contribution: strong empirical performance on informal text (Tweets) demonstrating robustness.
- Strength: solid motivation and theoretical grounding (Zipf’s Principle) for the approach.
- Strength: clear, concise presentation and well-explained architecture (illustrated by Figure 2).
- Strength: competitive performance, notably high recall and wins over HeidelTime and SUTime on some datasets.
- Strength: practical, lightweight, and real-time capable system with efficient heuristic rules.
- Strength: robust evaluation using multiple datasets and established baselines.
- Weakness: limited novelty since approach remains fundamentally rule-based and doesn’t fully address rule-based limitations.
- Weakness: reliance on POS tagging accuracy could be a bottleneck; impact not fully evaluated.
- Weakness: exclusion of Gigaword dataset is weakly justified and tweets training set is small (742 examples), raising generalization concerns.
- Weakness: lack of detailed error analysis to show common failure modes and difficult expression types.
- Question: please elaborate on limitations from POS tagging dependence and mitigation strategies.
- Question: justify excluding Gigaword and provide quick analysis of its label quality.
- Question: provide example cases where SynTime succeeds and SUTime fails (and vice versa).
- Question: quantify SynTime’s computational cost compared to baselines on larger corpora.
- Question: discuss generalization to other domains (medical, financial) and possible domain adaptation approaches.
- Additional: reference list needs consistent formatting.
- Additional: include a table summarizing syntactic types and their regexes.
- Additional: provide a more detailed, step-by-step example of the boundary expansion heuristic.
- Overall: well-written, well-motivated paper with valuable analysis and strong results, but would benefit from deeper discussion of trade-offs, error analysis, and dataset choices.
