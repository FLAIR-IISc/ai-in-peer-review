- SynTime: a lightweight rule-based timex recognizer using a small set of syntactic token types and boundary-expansion heuristics, initialized from SUTime regexes and filtered with POS.
- Empirical analysis: cross-corpus study (TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, Tweets) motivating short lengths, small vocabulary, and prevalence of “time tokens.”
- Evaluation: comparative results vs HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime showing strong recall and F1, especially on tweets.

- Strong empirical performance: achieves top or near-top F1 in most settings with relaxed recall >92% and very high tweet performance.
- Interpretable rules: concise, linguistically motivated type system and merging heuristics that handle dependencies and linkers practically.
- Informative corpus study: cross-corpus statistics explain why a small-lexicon, token-first approach is effective.
- Domain portability: modest manual lexicon additions yield good domain transfer, suggesting lightweight adaptation.
- Practicality: no training needed, easy POS integration, and simple extensibility for real-world use.

- Limited novelty: approach reuses SUTime token inventory and a similar token-first expansion paradigm, with unclear gains attributable to the new typing/merging design.
- Tweet dataset bias and size concerns: test set prefiltered by SUTime likely introduces selection bias; main test is small (200 tweets) and lacks inter-annotator agreement.
- Reproducibility and comparability gaps: some baselines taken from papers, missing tool/version/configuration details, and no statistical significance reported or clear resource release.
- Missing ablations and error analysis: no component-level ablations (POS filtering, merging rules, manual expansions) and limited error typology for failure modes.
- Unsubstantiated efficiency/scope claims: “real-time” claims lack runtime/memory benchmarks and the system only addresses recognition, not normalization or end-to-end integration.

- Request tweet dataset without SUTime pre-filtering and release of annotations and added token lists.
- Clarify baseline versions/configurations (HeidelTime, SUTime) and UWTime training/feature details, and rerun baselines under identical evaluation with significance testing.
- Specify exact POS tags used for disambiguation and quantify precision/recall trade-offs with/without POS filtering.
- Provide ablation results for comma/link merging, left vs right expansion rules, and removal of manual expansions.
- Report proportion of gold timex lacking an explicit “time token” and how SynTime handles them; note potential recall ceiling.
- Supply runtime and memory metrics (including POS tagging) on test sets and compare throughput to HeidelTime/SUTime.
