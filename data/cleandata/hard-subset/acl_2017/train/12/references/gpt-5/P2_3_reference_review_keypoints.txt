- SynTime: a simple rule-based time expression recognizer using a small set of token types, boundary-expansion heuristics, and optional manual lexicon expansion (reuses SUTime regexes).
- Empirical analysis across four corpora showing time expressions are short, covered by a small vocabulary, >93% contain at least one time token, and POS alone doesn't separate temporal spans from non-temporal spans.
- Evaluation on TimeBank, WikiWars, and a Tweets set against HeidelTime, SUTime, and UWTime showing high recall and strong F1 (especially on TimeBank and Tweets).
- Strength: strong empirical performance and high recall (best recall across datasets; top F1 on TimeBank and Tweets).
- Strength: practical, lightweight, minimal supervision/no training with small, interpretable rule set and low domain adaptation cost.
- Strength: clear, testable design motivated by corpus evidence (short spans and token prevalence) with straightforward heuristics.
- Strength: broad baselines and metrics (rule-based and learned systems; strict and relaxed TempEval metrics).
- Strength: inclusion of social media (Tweets) evaluation addressing noisy-text scenarios where many systems struggle.
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to SUTime and similar rule-based systems; main ideas are incremental and token types are semantic rather than purely syntactic.
- Weakness: insufficient methodological detail and no ablation studies to attribute gains to specific components (token detection vs boundary rules vs lexicon).
- Weakness: potential selection bias in Tweets dataset due to SUTime-based prefiltering and small remainder sampling, which may distort precision/recall comparisons.
- Weakness: reproducibility and fairness concerns—initialization from SUTime resources, unclear code/lexicon release, baseline numbers from prior papers, no significance tests, and unsupported real-time claims.
- Weakness: theoretical framing (principle of least effort/Zipf) is suggestive but underdeveloped and not rigorously tested.
- Key questions: clarify how SynTime’s boundary-expansion differs from SUTime with side-by-side examples of failures/successes.
- Key questions: provide full algorithmic specification or code for boundary-expansion/merging and handling of punctuation, overlaps, and dependent segments.
- Key questions: run ablations for modifier-position types, initialization without SUTime regexes, and disabling added lexicon (SynTime-E).
- Key questions: clarify Tweets selection pipeline, address SUTime bias, and provide a random fully annotated subset or re-evaluate on an existing Twitter temporal corpus.
- Key questions: report statistical significance and runtime/memory comparisons versus SUTime and HeidelTime.
- Key questions: state whether SynTime can be configured to match WikiWars-style spans that include prepositions and sensitivity to TimeML annotation choices.
- Overall assessment: method is sound and useful but underspecified; evaluation is broadly convincing but weakened by Tweets bias and missing ablations/significance tests.
- Novelty assessment: incremental over established rule-based systems; clearer differentiation required.
- Impact assessment: potentially valuable for practitioners needing a simple, high-recall recognizer, especially for social media, contingent on released code/resources.
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger novelty evidence, clearer algorithmic specification, ablations, unbiased Twitter evaluation, significance tests, and released code/data; could become a solid accept with these fixes.
