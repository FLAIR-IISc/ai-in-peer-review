- Paper proposes SynTime: a lightweight, rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic token types and boundary-expansion heuristics, motivated by corpus analysis; shows strong recall and competitive F1, especially on tweets.

- Main contribution 1: practical, simple, training-free recognizer with very high recall and strong F1 across domains.

- Main contribution 2: empirical characterization of time expressions across corpora (short spans, small lexicon, token-centric patterns).

- Main contribution 3: a clear, type-oriented formulation (syntactic token types and positional modifier classes) that simplifies rule writing.

- Strength: achieves best recall on all datasets and top F1 on TimeBank and Tweets, supporting the token-first, boundary-expansion approach.

- Strength: simplicity and practicality — compact type set and few heuristics make the method easy to maintain, adapt, and integrate into real-time pipelines.

- Strength: data-driven design — corpus statistics (e.g., >93% spans containing time tokens) directly motivate the pipeline choices.

- Strength: clear, reproducible algorithmic description with illustrative examples.

- Weakness: limited novelty relative to prior rule-based systems (SUTime/HeidelTime); syntactic/type reorganization may be incremental without ablation evidence.

- Weakness: evaluation fairness concerns — SynTime initializes from SUTime lexical regexes and SynTime-E adds manual tokens while baselines may not have equivalent domain adaptation.

- Weakness: Tweets dataset likely biased — sampled based on SUTime detections with a small set of added misses, reducing generalizability.

- Weakness: insufficient experimental detail and controls — unclear data splits, potential leakage, unspecified baseline configurations/versions, and no statistical significance testing.

- Weakness: missing ablations and error analysis — no quantitative isolation of POS filtering, type inventory, merging heuristics, or manual expansion effects; failure modes not analyzed.

- Weakness: some claims unsubstantiated — no runtime benchmarks for "real-time" claim and normalization not addressed.

- Question: clarify data splits for TimeBank/WikiWars and confirm SynTime-E additions were derived only from training data to avoid leakage.

- Question: detail how HeidelTime and SUTime were configured/tuned per domain and whether baselines received comparable domain-specific resources.

- Question: provide ablations isolating POS filtering, syntactic modifier types versus undifferentiated modifiers, and merging rules versus simpler strategies.

- Question: release the Tweets annotations and supply an evaluation set sampled independently of SUTime to avoid selection bias.

- Question: report runtime throughput and latency comparisons with SUTime/HeidelTime on standard hardware.

- Overall: clear, useful, and effective rule-based recognizer with strong empirical results, but incremental novelty and evaluation/ablation issues mean the paper is borderline-positive pending clarifications and additional experiments.
