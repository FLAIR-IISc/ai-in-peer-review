- Paper introduces SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple boundary-expansion heuristics, initialized from SUTime regexes and disambiguated with POS.

- Main contributions claimed: simple type-driven heuristic method with strong recall, corpus analysis motivating design, and lightweight domain adaptation via small manual additions.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance with high recall, especially on noisy tweets where SynTime substantially outperforms baselines.

- Strength: Methodological simplicity and interpretability—easy to implement, fast, low-resource, and maintainable compared to complex grammars or learned parsers.

- Strength: Clear design rationale tied to corpus patterns (compact mentions, small lexicon, POS regularities) and pragmatic POS disambiguation for ambiguous tokens.

- Strength: Small domain adaptation cost—few manual additions substantially improve performance on new domains like WikiWars.

- Strength: Coverage-focused error profile—high recall benefits downstream temporal tasks even if boundary precision is weaker.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—largely reuses SUTime regexes and resembles prior rule-based chunk-expansion methods, so conceptual contribution is modest.

- Weakness: Potential data leakage—unclear whether tokens/modifiers were added using the same test corpora (TimeBank/WikiWars), risking overfitting.

- Weakness: Tweets dataset selection bias—filtering tweets by SUTime detections and lack of inter-annotator agreement may favor dictionary methods and distort recall estimates.

- Weakness: Missing ablations and error analysis—no breakdown of contributions from dictionaries, POS, expansion rules, or detailed failure categories.

- Weakness: No runtime, memory, or complexity measurements despite claims of being lightweight and real-time.

- Weakness: Comparative setup and statistical rigor unclear—baseline configurations, document time settings, and significance testing are not fully aligned or reported.

- Question: Clarify train/test protocol and whether Table 5 additions used the test sets or held-out data.

- Question: Provide detailed Tweets dataset construction, sampling approach, proportion annotated independently of SUTime, inter-annotator agreement, and release plans.

- Question: Confirm whether UWTime/HeidelTime/SUTime were run under unified settings (document time, options) or why some numbers are taken from prior work.

- Question: Provide ablation experiments (remove POS filtering, merging, dictionary additions) and a categorical error analysis.

- Question: Report runtime throughput and memory footprint compared to SUTime and HeidelTime.

- Additional comment: The “principle of least effort” framing is interesting but speculative and should be tempered or supported with citations.

- Additional comment: Paper needs proofreading—many typos and reference inconsistencies.

- Additional comment: Consider broader evaluation on more domains (e.g., clinical notes, forums) to strengthen portability claims.
