- Contribution: Proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic typing (time token, modifier, numeral) and boundary-expansion heuristics.
- Contribution: Presents cross-corpus analysis showing time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, usually contain explicit time tokens, and exhibit consistent syntactic behavior.
- Contribution: Shows that minimal, targeted lexicon expansion enables robust domain adaptation (e.g., WikiWars) with little engineering.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance and high recall across datasets, especially on tweets (strict F1 ≈ 91.7, relaxed F1 ≈ 95.9).
- Strength: Simple, transparent, inspectable rules motivated by corpus observations, suitable for resource-constrained or real-time needs.
- Strength: Minimal supervision/engineering for domain transfer — small lexicon additions substantially improve recall.
- Strength: Evaluation against sensible baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime) using TempEval-3 toolkit and strict/relaxed matches.
- Strength: Useful corpus-level quantitative analysis (length distributions, type coverage, POS tendencies) that explains performance patterns.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty; heavy reliance on SUTime’s token regexes and semantic-type inventory, with novelty mainly in positional modifier types and merge rules.
- Weakness: Potential selection bias in Tweets dataset due to SUTime-based prefiltering, possibly inflating apparent generalization.
- Weakness: Annotation/evaluation mismatch on WikiWars (prepositions/descriptive expressions excluded by TimeML guidelines) complicates interpretation and may penalize SynTime.
- Weakness: No ablation studies or statistical significance testing to attribute gains to specific design choices or lexicon expansion.
- Weakness: Claims about efficiency and real-time suitability are unsupported by runtime or memory measurements.
- Weakness: Presentation issues (typos, citation formatting) and lack of released code/rules/lexicons hinder reproducibility.

- Question: Please provide runtime and memory comparisons (throughput, latency) vs. HeidelTime and SUTime on the three datasets.
- Question: How sensitive is SynTime to POS tagging errors; provide ablations for POS filtering and alternative taggers.
- Question: Can you release the exact added tokens/modifiers for SynTime-E and the final rule set for replication?
- Question: For Tweets, can you evaluate on a truly random sample or more thoroughly estimate SUTime’s miss rate to address selection bias?
- Question: For WikiWars, can you adapt rules to include prepositions per its guidelines or provide an error breakdown by phenomenon?

- Overall: Solid, pragmatic systems paper with convincing results and usable design, but modest novelty and concerns about dataset bias, ablations, efficiency data, and reproducibility.
