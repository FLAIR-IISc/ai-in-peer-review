- Paper proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer using a small set of syntactic token types and position-based boundary expansion heuristics.
- Authors analyze four corpora and use SUTime’s token regexes, POS gating, boundary expansion, and simple merging rules to extract time expressions.
- Main contribution: a simple type- and position-aware rule framework that achieves strong recall and competitive F1 across datasets, especially on Tweets.
- Contribution: cross-corpus descriptive analysis of time expression length, vocabulary size, and POS patterns motivating the design.
- Contribution: a small manually annotated Tweets dataset demonstrating robustness on informal text.
- Strength: strong empirical performance with very high recall and competitive/leading F1, notably large gains on Tweets.
- Strength: simplicity and efficiency—rule-based, training-free, easy to extend and suitable for low-resource or real-time deployment.
- Strength: clear, reproducible high-level algorithm and pipeline description facilitating reimplementation.
- Strength: cross-domain evaluation (news, Wikipedia, tweets) showing practical robustness.
- Strength: qualitative analysis aligns with intuition and supports centering rules on time tokens and local structure.
- Weakness: limited novelty—approach is incremental relative to existing rule-based systems (e.g., SUTime, HeidelTime) and reuses SUTime’s lexicon.
- Weakness: potential selection bias in Tweets dataset because tweets were preselected using SUTime, risking unfair comparison and limited representativeness.
- Weakness: unclear baseline comparability and tuning—mixing reported numbers and runs may introduce confounds from preprocessing, tokenization, POS tagging, or annotation conventions.
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—no clear statement about releasing code, exact rules, POS constraints, or added token lists.
- Weakness: limited analysis and ablations—no detailed error analysis or experiments isolating contributions of POS gating, merging rules, or manual expansions.
- Question: did the authors run HeidelTime/SUTime under identical preprocessing and evaluation scripts, and can they provide fully controlled comparisons?
- Question: can the authors quantify SUTime-induced selection bias in the Tweets collection and show results on an independently sampled tweet test set with per-type performance?
- Question: will the SynTime code, full rule set, POS/type constraints, and list of added tokens/modifiers be released for reproducibility?
- Question: can the authors provide ablations for removing POS gating, disabling specific merging rules, and using only SUTime’s token regex to isolate gains?
- Question: can SynTime be extended to include prepositions per TimeBank/WikiWars conventions to recover recall without losing precision, and what are the trade-offs?
