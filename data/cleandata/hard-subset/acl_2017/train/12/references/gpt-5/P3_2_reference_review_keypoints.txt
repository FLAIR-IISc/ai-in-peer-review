- Proposal: SynTime is a lightweight rule-based time expression tagger using token-type grouping and boundary-expansion heuristics initialized largely from SUTime regexes.
- Contribution 1: Type-based boundary-expansion approach achieving high recall and strong F1 without statistical training.
- Contribution 2: Empirical cross-corpus analysis showing short spans, small lexicon, and high coverage by time tokens.
- Contribution 3: Low-cost domain adaptation via small lexicon additions that improve recall (e.g., WikiWars).
- Strength: Strong empirical performance—high strict F1 and very high relaxed-match recall on TimeBank and Tweets.
- Strength: Lightweight, fast, single-pass, interpretable design that is easy to implement and debug.
- Strength: Clear, precise specification of token/modifier types and merging conditions enhancing reproducibility.
- Strength: Cross-corpus quantitative analysis that justifies focusing on time token detection and local expansion.
- Strength: Simple manual lexicon expansion yields measurable domain gains.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty relative to existing rule-based systems (SUTime/HeidelTime) and reliance on SUTime regexes blurs originality.
- Weakness: Tweets dataset construction used SUTime preselection, risking evaluation bias in favor of SUTime-like systems.
- Weakness: Inconsistent extraction scope across datasets (prepositions included/excluded) undermines fair comparison and clarity.
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies and detailed error analysis to quantify the impact of design choices and failure modes.
- Weakness: Theoretical claims (e.g., “least effort”) are anecdotal and not statistically supported.
- Weakness: Runtime/real-time performance claims lack empirical timing, memory, or throughput evidence.
- Question: How many tweets were annotated end-to-end without SUTime preselection, and can a fully random annotated test set be released?
- Question: Are extracted spans aligned with each gold standard’s scope (e.g., prepositions); can scores be reported with guideline alignment?
- Question: Which SUTime regexes were imported/modified, and how does SynTime perform without SUTime-initialized resources?
- Question: Please provide ablations removing POS disambiguation, comma/linker rules, dependent-segment handling, and manual expansion, plus error category breakdowns.
- Question: Provide runtime benchmarks (throughput, memory) for SynTime vs SUTime/HeidelTime with hardware/software details.
- Question: Will you release code, expanded lexicons, Tweets annotations, and report inter-annotator agreement?
