- SynTime: a rule-based time expression tagger using syntactic types and heuristic rules to recognize time expressions in free text.  
- Analysis across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets shows time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, and have consistent syntactic behavior.  
- SynTime defines three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and merges segments via heuristic boundary expansion.  
- System is lightweight, expandable, and capable of real-time performance.  
- Empirical results: SynTime outperforms baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime), with particularly high recall and strong performance on informal text (tweets).  
- Contribution 1: Detailed cross-dataset analysis supporting the principle of least effort in time expression use.  
- Contribution 2: Introduction of a type-based, simplified rule approach (SynTime) for time recognition.  
- Contribution 3: Comprehensive experiments demonstrating superior recall across diverse datasets.  
- Strength: Novel and simple type-based approach that departs from semantic-heavy rule systems.  
- Strength: Strong experimental results, notably recall >92% in relaxed match and robustness on tweets.  
- Strength: Practicality—lightweight, real-time, and easily expandable via keyword additions.  
- Strength: Thorough analysis that motivates the approach.  
- Strength: Clear and well-structured presentation.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on tokenization and POS tagging, which are error-prone in noisy text and lack mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness: Manual keyword expansion for domain-specific text may limit scalability in diverse or evolving domains.  
- Weakness: No exploration of hybrid rule + machine-learning approaches that might address remaining weaknesses.  
- Weakness: Focuses only on recognition and excludes normalization, limiting downstream applicability.  
- Questions: How are ambiguous tokens (e.g., “May”) handled when POS tagging fails—are there fallback mechanisms?  
- Questions: What is SynTime’s computational efficiency compared to baselines on large-scale datasets?  
- Questions: Have the authors considered integrating SynTime with ML models to better handle ambiguous or descriptive expressions?  
- Conclusion: Well-motivated, innovative, and practical approach; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability, ambiguity handling, and integration/normalization concerns.
