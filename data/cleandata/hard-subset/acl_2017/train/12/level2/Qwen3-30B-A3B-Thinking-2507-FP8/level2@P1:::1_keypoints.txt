Summary: SynTime is a rule-based time-expression recognizer that defines three syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and simple heuristics, evaluated on four datasets with strong results, especially on Tweets.

Contribution: comprehensive cross-dataset analysis revealing short, small-vocabulary, token-rich, syntactically consistent time expressions supporting Zipf's principle.

Contribution: proposal of a novel syntactic-type system for time expressions that departs from semantic-based approaches.

Contribution: a lightweight, easily expandable rule-based tagger (SynTime) that achieves competitive performance.

Strength: thorough, data-driven analysis across multiple sources with clear empirical support and connection to Zipf's principle.

Strength: strong empirical performance, notably 91.74% F1 on Tweets, showing efficacy in informal text with small vocabularies.

Strength: clear step-by-step method description with examples and a practical, low-effort expansion mechanism.

Strength: honest discussion of limitations, including dependence on tokenization/POS tagging, with plausible explanations for Tweet performance.

Weakness: novelty vs. existing rule-based systems (e.g., SUTime) is not clearly differentiated or explicitly contrasted.

Weakness: lack of runtime measurements to substantiate the "real time" and lightweight efficiency claims.

Weakness: weaker performance on WikiWars (80.11% F1) without detailed error analysis to explain failures.

Weakness: insufficient discussion of handling atypical or complex time expressions (e.g., "the third Wednesday of next month").

Question: how does SynTime resolve overlapping or nested time segments (e.g., "the first week of January 2020") in practice?

Question: how is the claimed easy expansion by adding keywords performed in concrete terms, especially for specialized domains like medical text?

Question: can the authors quantify how POS-tagging errors at different rates impact SynTime's accuracy?
