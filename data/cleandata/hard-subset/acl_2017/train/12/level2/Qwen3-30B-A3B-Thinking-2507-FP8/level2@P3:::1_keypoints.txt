- Time expressions are very short (80% have ≤3 tokens).  
- Time expressions use a small, repeatable vocabulary of time-related keywords.  
- At least 93% of expressions contain explicit time tokens.  
- Words in time expressions show similar syntactic behavior.  
- SynTime: a rule-based recognition system defining three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) using simple boundary-expansion heuristics; designed to be lightweight, real-time, and easily expandable.

- Contribution: detailed multi-dataset analysis supporting the principle of least effort in time expressions.  
- Contribution: novel syntactic-type (rather than semantic) rule-based approach (SynTime) for flexible, lightweight recognition.  
- Contribution: consistent experimental improvements over baselines, with exceptional performance on Tweets.

- Strength: thorough, cross-dataset analysis with compelling empirical support for observed properties.  
- Strength: genuinely novel syntactic-type formulation and simple, effective heuristic rules.  
- Strength: strong empirical results vs. HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime, especially on informal text.  
- Strength: practical expandability demonstrated via simple keyword additions.  
- Strength: clear explanation linking SynTime’s strengths to observed properties of informal text.

- Weakness: limited comparison set (only three baselines); missing recent neural or alternative methods.  
- Weakness: claim of “real time” lacks concrete runtime measurements or system comparisons.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of how many keywords are needed per domain and how performance scales with additions.  
- Weakness: inadequate treatment of ambiguous cases (e.g., “May” month vs. modal verb); POS-tagging mention is insufficient.  
- Weakness: syntactic-behavior analysis (Observation 4) could be deeper to better justify the type system design.

- Question: provide specific runtime measurements for SynTime versus baselines to justify “real-time.”  
- Question: how many additional keywords are needed for domain-specific datasets and what is the marginal benefit of each?  
- Question: how does SynTime handle ambiguous tokens (e.g., “May”) and what is its performance on such cases?
