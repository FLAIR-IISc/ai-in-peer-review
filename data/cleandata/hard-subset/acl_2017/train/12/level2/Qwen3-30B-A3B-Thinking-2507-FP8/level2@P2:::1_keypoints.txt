- Paper analyzes time expressions across TimeBank, Gigaword, WikiWars, and Tweets and observes they are very short, use small vocabulary, predominantly contain time tokens, and show similar syntactic behavior.  
- Proposes SynTime, a rule-based recognizer using syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and simple heuristics, claiming SOTA performance and strong results on Tweets.  
- Contribution: provides a comprehensive multi-domain analysis supporting the principle of least effort in time expression usage.  
- Contribution: introduces SynTime, which leverages syntactic rather than semantic types for rule-based time expression recognition.  
- Contribution: demonstrates high recall (92%+) and competitive precision on benchmark datasets.  
- Strength: thorough, data-supported analysis yielding four clear observations that underpin the method.  
- Strength: connects findings to Zipf’s principle of least effort, offering a compelling theoretical justification.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, notably >92% relaxed recall across datasets and 95.87% F1 on Tweets.  
- Strength: SynTime is lightweight and real-time, advantageous for fast processing applications.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance tests reported for claimed performance improvements versus baselines.  
- Weakness: Paper does not fully explain poorer SynTime performance on WikiWars despite noting its different expression patterns.  
- Weakness: Limited baseline comparison (only three systems) and omission of other relevant systems (e.g., TempEval-3 top performers).  
- Weakness: Claim that SynTime “can be easily expanded” lacks analysis of required effort and impact on performance beyond limited examples.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of handling ambiguous cases (e.g., “May” as month vs. modal), a key challenge for TIMEX recognition.  
- Question: Please provide statistical significance tests comparing SynTime to the baselines.  
- Question: Please elaborate on specific challenges WikiWars poses and how SynTime could be improved for it.  
- Question: Why were other state-of-the-art systems excluded from comparison, and how would their inclusion affect conclusions?
