- Observation: Time expressions are short (≈80% are ≤3 tokens).
- Observation: Time expressions use a small, consistent vocabulary of time-related keywords.
- Observation: Time tokens appear in ≥93% of time expressions.
- Observation: Time expressions exhibit similar syntactic behaviors across datasets.
- Method: SynTime is a rule-based recognizer that defines three syntactic types (time tokens, modifiers, numerals) and uses heuristic boundary expansion for recognition.
- Contribution: Empirical analysis of time expressions supporting Zipf’s principle of least effort.
- Contribution: Introduction of a syntactically motivated type system for time expression recognition, distinct from semantic approaches.
- Contribution: Lightweight SynTime that achieves state-of-the-art performance on benchmark datasets.
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence for the four observations and a theoretical link to Zipf’s principle.
- Strength: Large performance improvement on Tweets (91.74% F1), matching observations about informal text.
- Strength: System simplicity and real-time performance compared to learning-based methods.
- Weakness: Limited novelty, closely building on existing rule-based methods like SUTime.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on heuristic boundary expansion rules, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited—Gigaword excluded from evaluation and missing comparisons to recent neural methods.
