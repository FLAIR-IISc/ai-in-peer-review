## Review of "Time Expression Analysis and Recognition Using Syntactic Types and Simple Heuristic Rules"

This paper presents SynTime, a novel rule-based approach to time expression recognition. The authors begin with an insightful analysis of time expressions across four datasets, observing their short length, limited vocabulary, and consistent syntactic behavior. Leveraging these observations, SynTime defines syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and employs simple heuristic rules for recognition, achieving competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods.

The primary contribution of this work is the type-based approach to rule design, shifting focus from semantic meaning (as in SUTime) to syntactic behavior. This simplification, combined with a boundary expansion heuristic, results in a lightweight and efficient tagger capable of real-time performance. A secondary contribution lies in the thorough analysis of time expression characteristics, providing a strong rationale for the proposed method. Finally, the experimental results, demonstrating superior recall and competitive F1 scores, particularly on the challenging Twitter dataset, are a valuable addition to the field.

The strengths of this paper are its clear motivation, well-defined methodology, and strong empirical validation. The analysis of time expression data is compelling and effectively justifies the design choices in SynTime. The comparison against established baselines (HeidelTime, SUTime, UWTime) provides a solid benchmark for evaluating the proposed approach. The reported performance gains, especially in recall, are significant.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. The reliance on pre-existing POS tagging introduces a potential source of error, which the authors acknowledge but could be further investigated. The expansion process, while efficient, relies on manual annotation of keywords, which may not scale well to different domains. The paper also lacks a detailed error analysis, which would provide valuable insights into the limitations of SynTime and potential areas for improvement. Furthermore, the discussion of related work could be more comprehensive, particularly regarding recent advancements in neural network-based time expression recognition.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is SynTime’s performance to the accuracy of the POS tagger? Have you experimented with different POS taggers or considered incorporating POS tagging as part of the training process?
2.  Could you elaborate on the criteria used for selecting keywords during the expansion phase? What strategies are employed to minimize ambiguity when adding new keywords?
3.  What types of errors does SynTime commonly make? A detailed error analysis would help understand the limitations of the approach and guide future research.
