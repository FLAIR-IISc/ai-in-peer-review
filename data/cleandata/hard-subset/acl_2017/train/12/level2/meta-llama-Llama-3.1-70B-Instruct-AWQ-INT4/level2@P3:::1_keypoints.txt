- Proposes SynTime: a syntactic-type-based, heuristic boundary-expansion method for time expression recognition.
- Analyzes four datasets and observes time expressions are very short.
- Finds only a small group of time-related keywords are used across datasets.
- Reports >93% of time expressions contain at least one explicit time token.
- Observes words within time expressions show similar syntactic behavior, motivating a syntactic type system.
- Defines a syntactic type system inspired by POS tags and uses simple heuristic rules for boundary expansion.
- Evaluates SynTime against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets and reports superior performance to both rule-based and ML taggers.
- Contribution: empirical evidence supporting a “least effort” principle in time expression recognition.
- Contribution: introduces a novel syntactic-type approach (SynTime) for time expression recognition.
- Contribution: provides a concrete syntactic type system and heuristic rule set for boundary expansion.
