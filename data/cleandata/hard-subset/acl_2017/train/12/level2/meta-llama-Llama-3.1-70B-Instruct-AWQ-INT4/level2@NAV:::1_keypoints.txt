- Paper proposes SynTime, a type-based method that uses three syntactic types and simple heuristics to recognize time expressions, evaluated on multiple datasets.

- Contribution — Analysis: provides a comprehensive analysis of time expressions across four datasets revealing common characteristics.

- Contribution — SynTime: defines syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and designs heuristic rules for recognition.

- Contribution — Evaluation: demonstrates SynTime’s effectiveness on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets.

- Strength — Novelty: presents a novel syntactic-type approach distinct from existing rule-based and ML methods.

- Strength — Performance: outperforms state-of-the-art methods on multiple datasets.

- Strength — Lightweight: extremely lightweight, real-time, and easily extensible by adding keywords.

- Weakness — Scope: focuses only on recognition and does not address time expression normalization.

- Weakness — Assumptions: assumes perfect tokenization and POS tagging, which may not hold in practice.

- Weakness — Generalization: not evaluated on other languages, so cross-linguistic generalization is unclear.

- Question: How will SynTime be extended to address time expression normalization?

- Question: How will the approach handle imperfect tokenization and POS tagging?

- Question: Have the authors evaluated SynTime on other languages or plan to, and what are the results?
