- Summary: Proposes SynTime, a type-based heuristic method that uses three syntactic token types and boundary-expansion rules to recognize time expressions, evaluated on multiple datasets with strong results.

Main contributions:
- Introduces SynTime, defining syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and heuristic boundary-expansion rules for time expression recognition.
- Analyzes four datasets and reports four observations supporting a “least effort” principle for time expressions.
- Empirically evaluates SynTime against three state-of-the-art baselines on three datasets, showing competitive or superior performance.

Strengths:
- Thorough analysis of time expressions across four datasets providing useful insights.
- Simple, effective approach with performance comparable to or better than SOTA baselines.
- Clear, detailed description of SynTime’s construction, recognition, and expansion procedures.
- Robust evaluation showing effectiveness across multiple datasets.

Weaknesses:
- Assumes correct tokenization and POS tagging, which may not hold in real-world settings.
- Lacks detailed comparison with machine learning–based methods that may perform differently on some datasets.
- Does not discuss limitations, e.g., inability to recognize descriptive time expressions.
- No clear explanation of how the approach generalizes to other languages or domains.

Questions to authors:
- How will SynTime be extended to other languages or domains?
- Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with ML-based time-expression methods?
- How do the authors plan to address limitations such as recognizing descriptive time expressions?
- Please provide more details on the manual annotation process for the Tweets dataset.
