- Proposes SynTime, a syntactic type–based tagger for recognizing time expressions in free text.  
- Analyzes four datasets and finds time expressions are short, use a small vocabulary, and share syntactic patterns.  
- SynTime applies simple heuristic rules and achieves state-of-the-art results on three benchmarks.

- Provides a comprehensive analysis of time expressions across four datasets.  
- Introduces a novel type-based recognition approach inspired by part-of-speech tagging.  
- Demonstrates SynTime outperforming existing baselines on three benchmark datasets.

- Thorough dataset analysis is a valuable contribution to NLP.  
- SynTime is simple, efficient, and suitable for real-time use.  
- Experimental results show significant improvement over prior methods.

- Assumes correct tokenization and POS-tagging, which may not hold in real-world settings.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with other machine learning–based approaches.  
- Does not discuss limitations like handling out-of-vocabulary or domain-specific time expressions.

- How will you handle incorrect tokenization and POS-tagging that could degrade SynTime’s performance?  
- Can you provide a more detailed comparison with machine learning–based methods, highlighting relative strengths and weaknesses?  
- How do you plan to extend SynTime to handle out-of-vocabulary words and domain-specific time expressions?

- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the approach.  
- Experimental results are impressive and convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Needs clearer discussion of limitations and potential application scenarios.
