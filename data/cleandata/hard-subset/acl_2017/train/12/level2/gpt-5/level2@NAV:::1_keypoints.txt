- Proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based recognizer grouping tokens into syntactic types and using boundary-expansion heuristics.  
- Claims: time expressions are short, use small vocabularies, and share syntactic behaviors.  
- Claims: a type-based (syntactic) design and state-of-the-art recognition (best recall) on TimeBank and Tweets.

- Contribution: simple, pragmatic rule architecture achieving strong recall and competitive F1, especially on Tweets.  
- Contribution: empirical characterization of time expressions across four corpora motivating a high-recall, type-first strategy.  
- Contribution: extensible lexicon+heuristic framework allowing small targeted additions for domain adaptation.

- Strength: strong empirical performance with best recall and top-tier F1 on TimeBank and large gains on Tweets.  
- Strength: simple, practical, real-time method requiring minimal training data, suitable for low-resource domains.  
- Strength: clear, data-driven motivation from cross-corpus analysis (length, vocab, POS).  
- Strength: demonstrated extensibility—small lexicon additions improve recall (e.g., WikiWars).

- Weakness: limited novelty—refines established rule-based paradigms with incremental conceptual advance.  
- Weakness: evaluation threats—Tweets dataset self-annotated; sparse details on IAA and baseline tuning; no significance tests or ablations.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—full rules, lexicons, and expansion lists not released; runtime claims lack quantitative benchmarks.  
- Weakness: scope limited to recognition (not normalization); reliance on POS tagging may be brittle; exclusion of prepositions may undercount expressions.

- Q: Provide ablations comparing (a) without syntactic typing, (b) only SUTime tokens, (c) without POS filtering—what drives recall?  
- Q: How were HeidelTime/SUTime configured for Tweets and was any domain adaptation applied?  
- Q: What are runtime measurements (docs/sec) and memory footprint?

- Q: Will code, rules, and expanded lexicons be released for reproducibility?
