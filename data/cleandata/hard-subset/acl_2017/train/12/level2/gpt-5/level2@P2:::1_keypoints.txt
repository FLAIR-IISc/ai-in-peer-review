- SynTime: a lightweight rule-based system that detects time tokens then expands boundaries using position-based modifier types and local heuristics.  
- Corpus analysis: four corpora analyzed with observations that time expressions are short, dominated by a small vocabulary, usually contain a time token, and have similar POS distributions.  
- Empirical results: high recall and strong F1 on TimeBank, WikiWars, and Tweets; outperforms HeidelTime/SUTime/UWTime on TimeBank and Tweets and is competitive on WikiWars.  
- No training and claims real-time speed, compatible with standard NLP preprocessing.

- Strength: achieves best recall across datasets and very high F1 on Tweets, supporting the token-first expansion approach.  
- Strength: simple, transparent, and adaptable method (position-based merging) that is easy to extend to new domains.  
- Strength: corpus-driven design with quantified observations motivating the approach.  
- Strength: clear comparison to prior rule-based and learning-based systems and distinct positioning.  
- Strength: practical for deployment—no training required and real-time-oriented design.

- Weakness: limited novelty—largely reuses SUTime lexicons/regexes and mainly refines grouping and expansion heuristics.  
- Weakness: potential selection bias in Tweets corpus due to SUTime pre-filtering, risking inflated comparisons.  
- Weakness: incomplete experimental reporting—unspecified baseline configurations, no significance testing, and no ablation or detailed failure analysis.  
- Weakness: real-time claim unsubstantiated—no runtime, memory, or throughput benchmarks provided.  
- Weakness: scope/generalization concerns—TimeML adherence excludes prepositions causing underperformance on domains like WikiWars; cross-lingual applicability untested.
