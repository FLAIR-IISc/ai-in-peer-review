- Presents SynTime, a rule-based time expression recognizer using syntactic types and simple heuristics.  
- Analysis across four datasets shows time expressions are short, use limited vocabulary, and have consistent syntactic behavior.  
- SynTime defines three syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral) and applies a boundary expansion heuristic for recognition.  
- Core contribution: shifts from semantic-based rules to syntactic type–based rules aiming for simplicity, flexibility, and speed.  
- Strength: thorough empirical analysis that motivates the design choices.  
- Strength: competitive experimental results, outperforming state-of-the-art on TimeBank and Tweets, particularly in recall.  
- Strength: lightweight implementation enabling real-time processing; paper is well-written and clear.  
- Weakness: novelty is limited—approach aligns with established linguistic grouping by syntactic behavior.  
- Weakness: performance gains are modest and require more detailed ablation studies to isolate component effects.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive error analysis to show where and why SynTime fails.  
- Weakness: relies on accurate POS tagging; robustness to POS-tagging errors is not evaluated.  
- Weakness: depends on initial regular expressions borrowed from SUTime, raising questions about independence.  
- Weakness: efficiency claims need more detailed processing-time comparisons with existing methods.  
- Question: provide a detailed error analysis categorizing error types and proposing improvements.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to POS-tagging errors and have different POS taggers been tested?  
- Question: how dependent are results on SUTime-derived regexes and would independently derived rules change performance?
