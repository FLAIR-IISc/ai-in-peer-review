- Presents SynTime, a type-based rule system for time expression recognition leveraging short length, small vocabulary, time-token prevalence, and syntactic consistency.
- Empirical analysis across four datasets supports observations about expression length, vocabulary size, and syntactic consistency.
- Introduces a syntactic type system (time token, modifier, numeral) enabling flexible, extensible rule definitions.
- Uses simple heuristic rules for recognition, producing a lightweight, real-time system.
- Outperforms state-of-the-art on TimeBank and Tweets, and is comparable on WikiWars.
- Strong motivation grounded in observed linguistic properties and the principle of least effort.
- Method is clearly explained with helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 2).
- Evaluation is comprehensive, covering benchmark corpora and real-world Tweets with strong baselines.
- Practical advantages include lightweight design, real-time performance, and easy extensibility.
- Related work is well-discussed, highlighting differences from prior approaches.
- Limited novelty overall due to fundamentally rule-based approach, which may hinder generalization to new domains.
- System depends on POS tagging; impact of POS errors is acknowledged but not thoroughly studied.
- Paper lacks detailed error analysis identifying consistent failure modes.
- Keyword expansion process is under-specified; methodology for selecting keywords isn’t well explained.
- Decision to discard Gigaword over label quality is not fully justified or detailed.
- Question: provide more details on the keyword selection methodology for expanding SynTime.
- Question: how sensitive is performance to POS tagger errors; any experiments on this?
- Question: provide a detailed error analysis identifying time expressions SynTime fails on.
- Question: provide computational cost comparisons versus baselines beyond “real time.”
- Additional comments: reference list is extensive; some heuristic rule explanations need more detail; consider adding a comparison table versus baselines.
