- Summary: SynTime is a simple rule-based system that finds core "time tokens" via a lexicon and POS tags, expands boundaries with heuristics, merges segments, and outperforms several baselines on newswire, Wikipedia, and noisy Twitter data.

- Contribution 1: SynTime — a lightweight, efficient rule-based time expression recognizer with high recall and strong results (e.g., TimeBank strict F1 92.47, Tweets strict F1 91.77).

- Contribution 2: A data-driven analysis of time-expression properties across genres motivating the system design (short expressions, small vocabulary, anchored by a time token).

- Strength 1: Impressive empirical performance against strong baselines, validating the core token-anchored design.

- Strength 2: Robust effectiveness on informal/noisy Twitter text, demonstrating practical utility beyond formal corpora.

- Strength 3: Conceptual simplicity and computational efficiency; intuitive seed-token plus boundary-expansion heuristic.

- Strength 4: Well-motivated architecture grounded in cross-genre empirical observations.

- Weakness 1: The claimed novelty of "syntactic types" is overstated and may be mainly a relabeling rather than a fundamentally new typing approach.

- Weakness 2: Heavy dependence on SUTime’s curated lexicon makes it hard to separate gains from the lexicon versus SynTime’s heuristics; ablation needed.

- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed qualitative error analysis and discussion of failure modes (e.g., complex/conjoined expressions).

- Weakness 4: Oversimplifies critiques of learning-based methods; sequence models with standard features can generalize many of the cited patterns.

- Question 1: Clarify how your "syntactic types" fundamentally differ from token-level categories in systems like SUTime and whether the main novelty is the boundary-expansion mechanism.

- Question 2: How crucial is bootstrapping the lexicon from SUTime to performance, and could initial token sets be learned from annotated data?

- Question 3: Which specific time-expression types does SynTime handle better than learning-based systems like UWTime, and why?
