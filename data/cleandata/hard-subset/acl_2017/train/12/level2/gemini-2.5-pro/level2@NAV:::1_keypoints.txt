- Summary: Proposes SynTime, a lightweight rule-based time expression recognizer that identifies core "time tokens" and expands boundaries using syntactic types (time token, modifier, numeral).
- Contribution: Introduces a simple, effective heuristic method using syntactic roles and boundary-expansion rules as a fast alternative to complex rule systems or ML models.
- Contribution: Provides empirical analysis of time-expression structure across news, Wikipedia, and tweets, justifying design choices and linking to linguistic patterns (e.g., Zipf's law).
- Strength: Excellent empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art or superior performance on TimeBank and Tweets with especially high recall.
- Strength: Novel and elegant simplification—syntactic-role-based expansion makes the system lightweight, fast, and easily extensible.
- Strength: Strong methodological grounding—design directly supported by insightful dataset analysis (lengths, vocabulary, component roles).
- Weakness: Relies on SUTime lexical resources (token regexes), making it hard to separate gains from novel heuristics versus pre-existing lexicons.
- Weakness: Inconsistent performance—notably lower strict F1 on WikiWars with only a brief explanation for failures on descriptive/domain-specific expressions.
- Weakness: SynTime-E requires manual keyword additions from training data, which is subjective and not scalable to new domains.
- Question: Please provide an ablation or comparison (e.g., simplified SUTime using only its lexicons with basic matching) to disentangle the performance contribution of your heuristics versus the borrowed SUTime resources.
