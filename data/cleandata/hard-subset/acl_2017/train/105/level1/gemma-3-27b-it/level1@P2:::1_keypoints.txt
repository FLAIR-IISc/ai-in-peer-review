- Introduces a hard attention model enforcing near-monotonic character alignments for morphological inflection, offering a more interpretable/efficient alternative to soft attention.  
- Achieves strong empirical results, setting state-of-the-art on several datasets and especially excelling in low-resource CELEX, outperforming prior latent-variable WFSTs.  
- Provides analysis comparing learned representations of hard vs. soft attention, revealing different ways morphological information is encoded.  

- Novelty & motivation: well-justified adaptation addressing near-monotonic alignment and overfitting in low-data regimes.  
- Strong CELEX performance demonstrates effective generalization with scarce training data.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, including multiple languages.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful visualizations (Figure 1).  
- Insightful analysis, including discussion of positional encoding effects.  

- Relies on pre-computed character-level alignments, raising questions about robustness to alignment errors.  
- Sparse description of the Sudoh et al. (2013) alignment model and its limitations.  
- SIGMORPHON2016 results are mixed: strong on suffixing languages but lagging on others, needing deeper linguistic analysis and comparison to MED.  
- Model architecture is somewhat complex (bi-RNN, hard attention, recurrent decoder with feedback); possible simplifications not fully explored.  

- How sensitive is performance to quality/noise in pre-computed alignments?  
- Why choose Sudoh et al. (2013) for alignment; were alternatives considered?  
- Which linguistic properties explain the SIGMORPHON2016 performance differences versus soft attention?  
- Have simpler variants (e.g., removing decoder feedback) been tested and what was the impact?  

- GitHub reference supports reproducibility but was removed for anonymization.  
- Related work discussion is thorough and provides good context.  

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing alignment dependency and the SIGMORPHON2016 performance analysis.
