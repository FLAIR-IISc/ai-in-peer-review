- Contribution: Novel hard-attention model tailored to morphological inflection exploiting near-monotonic input–output alignment.
- Contribution: Strong empirical results — state-of-the-art on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, with notable gains on CELEX.
- Contribution: Analysis comparing hard vs soft attention with visualizations, offering insight into learned representations.

- Strength: Directly addresses data sparsity; hard attention improves performance on small-data CELEX, showing value of monotonic inductive bias.
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across three datasets and comparisons to neural and non-neural baselines.
- Strength: Insightful representation analysis showing hard attention encodes less explicit positional info and relies on the attention mechanism.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured paper and effective illustration of the hard attention mechanism.

- Weakness: Hard attention mechanism itself is not novel; novelty mainly in application to morphological inflection.
- Weakness: Dependence on pre-computed character-level alignments introduces an external dependency and may limit end-to-end learnability and generalization.
- Weakness: Analysis scope limited — lacks exploration of non-monotonic/long-range interactions and a detailed error analysis by error type.
- Weakness: SIGMORPHON comparison incomplete — potential confounds like hyperparameter tuning, model size, and training length are not thoroughly discussed.

- Question: What is the computational cost of hard attention versus soft attention in training and inference?
