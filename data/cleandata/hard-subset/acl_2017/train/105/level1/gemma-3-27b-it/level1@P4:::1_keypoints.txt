- Proposes a hard attention mechanism tailored to the nearly-monotonic alignment in morphological inflection.
- Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, achieving state-of-the-art results in several setups.
- Strong performance on low-resource CELEX dataset, outperforming prior models including finite-state approaches.
- Provides analysis comparing learned representations of hard vs. soft attention models.
- Hard attention is well-motivated by the task-specific monotonic alignment property.
- Consistent outperformance on CELEX is a significant empirical result for sparse data settings.
- Evaluation across three diverse datasets demonstrates generalization ability.
- Model description, training procedure, and diagrams (e.g., Figure 1) are clear and helpful.
- Analysis of attention patterns gives insight into how models capture morphological information.
- Core architecture uses established encoder-decoder with attention components, so novelty is limited.
- Training depends on externally pre-trained character-level alignments, reducing end-to-end learning.
- Impact of alignment quality on final performance is not fully analyzed.
- Analysis could be deeper (e.g., decoder activations, example attention visualizations).
- No thorough hyperparameter sensitivity study, limiting assessment of robustness.
- Soft attention outperforms hard attention on SIGMORPHON, suggesting monotonicity may not hold for all languages.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to alignment quality and different alignment methods?
- Question: Can differences on SIGMORPHON be explained by varying degrees of non-monotonicity, and can monotonicity be quantified per language?
- Question: What is the computational cost of hard vs. soft attention for training and inference?
- Question: How were hyperparameters chosen and was a systematic search performed?
- Additional: GitHub link removed for anonymity (appropriate).
- Additional: Paper is generally well-written and easy to understand.
- Minor: “RNN” is used without definition in some places.
