- Novel neural model using hard monotonic attention with explicit step/write control to enforce near-monotonic alignments for morphological inflection.
- Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive results and strong performance in low-data settings.
- Comparative analysis of hard vs. soft attention provides insights into differing learned representations and encoding strategies.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results, particularly notable improvement on low-resource CELEX.
- Hard attention is well-motivated, yields more interpretable alignments, and appears to reduce overfitting.
- Comprehensive evaluation against neural and non-neural baselines across varied datasets.
- Insightful qualitative analysis illuminating model behaviors and representation differences.

Weaknesses:
- Relies on pre-computed character-level alignments, introducing an external dependency and limiting end-to-end training.
- Limited error analysis; lacks detailed identification of specific failure cases or problematic morphological processes.
- SIGMORPHON comparison is complicated because the implemented soft-attention baseline is stronger than the published MED system.

Questions to authors:
- How sensitive is performance to the quality of precomputed alignments and have different aligners been tried?
- Can you provide a more detailed error analysis by type of morphological error?
- What is the computational cost and scalability of hard attention compared to soft attention with increasing sequence length?
