- Proposes a hard-attention encoder–decoder for morphological inflection that uses a control mechanism to write or advance a pointer for nearly-monotonic alignments.
- Evaluated on three morphological inflection datasets and reports state-of-the-art results versus prior neural and non-neural methods.
- Main contribution: a hard-attention model tailored for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning.
- Main contribution: achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple inflection generation setups.
- Main contribution: analyzes and compares learned representations of hard vs. soft attention models.
- Strength: improved performance, particularly in low-resource settings.
- Strength: training is simplified by using independently learned alignments from the start.
- Strength: provides insightful analysis illuminating strengths and weaknesses of hard vs. soft attention.
- Weakness: may not generalize well to sequence-to-sequence tasks without nearly-monotonic alignments.
- Weakness: performance depends on the quality of independently learned alignments, which can be imperfect.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis explaining the hard-attention model’s properties and limits.
- Question: how will the model be extended to non-monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks?
- Question: request for more details on the character-level alignment process and its impact on performance.
- Question: how to mitigate dependence on alignment quality and improve alignment accuracy?
