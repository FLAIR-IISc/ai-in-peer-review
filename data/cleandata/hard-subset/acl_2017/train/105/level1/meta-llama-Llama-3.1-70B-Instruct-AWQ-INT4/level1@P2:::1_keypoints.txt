- Proposes a neural hard-attention model for nearly-monotonic morphological inflection, evaluated on three datasets and achieving state-of-the-art in several setups, with analysis of learned representations.

- Introduces hard attention for nearly-monotonic seq2seq learning tailored to morphological inflection.

- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance across three previously studied morphological inflection datasets.

- Analyzes and compares continuous representations learned by hard and soft attention models to reveal extracted features.

- Outperforms prior neural and non-neural approaches on the small CELEX dataset, benefiting low-resource scenarios.

- Shows robustness to varying dataset sizes, performing well on both small (CELEX) and large (Wiktionary) datasets.

- Provides a detailed analysis of learned representations, offering insights into model-extracted features.

- Provides limited linguistic analysis; deeper exploration of linguistic implications is lacking.

- Compares to only a few other models; broader baseline comparisons would better clarify strengths and weaknesses.

- Notes overfitting of the soft attention model on CELEX but lacks detailed analysis of overfitting in the proposed model.

- Requests more detail on the linguistic implications of the learned representations and their relation to morphological structure.

- Asks how the hard attention model handles non-monotonic alignments in languages with complex morphology.

- Requests details on the training procedure, including hyperparameters and optimization algorithm.

- Overall: clear, well-written, novel use of hard attention that improves small-data performance and is suitable for top-tier NLP publication.
