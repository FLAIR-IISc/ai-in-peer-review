- Proposes a hard-attention neural model for morphological inflection that models nearly-monotonic character alignments.  
- Evaluated on three prior inflection-generation datasets and reports state-of-the-art results in several settings.  
- Analyzes continuous representations learned by hard and soft attention models to reveal extracted features.  
- Strength: effectively captures nearly-monotonic input-output alignments, yielding strong performance.  
- Strength: robust performance with small training sets.  
- Strength: representation analysis offers valuable insights into model behavior.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016 neural-weighted FST).  
- Weakness: character-level alignment approach may not generalize to languages or phenomena without near-monotonic alignments.  
- Weakness: no detailed analysis of the model’s computational complexity for practical use.  
- Question: please provide a more detailed empirical comparison to other SOTA models like the neural-weighted FST.  
- Question: how does the model handle languages or morphological processes that are non-monotonic?  
- Question: can you provide a detailed computational-complexity analysis and comparison to alternatives?  
- Additional: paper is well-written, explanations are clear, and results/analysis are impressive, but would benefit from more comparisons and complexity analysis.
