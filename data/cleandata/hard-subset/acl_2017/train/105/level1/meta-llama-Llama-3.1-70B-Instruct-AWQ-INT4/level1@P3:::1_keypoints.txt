- Proposes a hard-attention neural model for morphological inflection generation, claiming state-of-the-art results on three datasets and strong performance with small training sets.  
- Contribution: a hard-attention model for nearly-monotonic seq2seq learning tailored to morphological inflection.  
- Contribution: analysis of continuous representations learned by hard and soft attention models.  
- Contribution: reported state-of-the-art results on three previously studied morphological inflection datasets.  
- Strength: outperforms prior approaches on the small CELEX dataset, showing robustness to limited training data.  
- Strength: better performance than soft-attention baselines on suffixing + stem-change languages (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Strength: training simplified by using independently learned alignments to guide attention and a standard cross-entropy loss.  
- Strength: provides insightful analysis of learned representations, clarifying extracted features for inflection generation.  
- Weakness: slightly lower performance on languages with context-sensitive morphology (vowel harmony, consonant harmony).  
- Weakness: relies on pre-computed alignments, which may be unavailable or inaccurate for some languages/tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons against other neural models with different attention mechanisms or architectures.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of error types, reducing insight into specific model failures and improvement areas.  
- Question: How will the model be extended to handle complex morphological phenomena like vowel and consonant harmony?  
- Question: Can the authors provide more details on the character-level alignment process used to guide attention?  
- Question: How do the authors plan to address the dependence on pre-computed alignments when they are unavailable or noisy?  
- Question: Can the authors provide deeper analysis of error types and plans to mitigate those errors in future work?
