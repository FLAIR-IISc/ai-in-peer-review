- Proposes a neural hard-attention model for morphological inflection that leverages nearly-monotonic character alignment.
- Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016 and reports state-of-the-art results in multiple setups.
- Provides analysis of continuous representations learned by hard vs. soft attention to illuminate extracted features.
- Novel hard-attention approach well-suited to nearly-monotonic alignment tasks.
- Thorough evaluation across multiple datasets demonstrating effectiveness and robustness.
- Representation analysis yields insights into strengths and weaknesses of attention types.
- Relies on the nearly-monotonic alignment assumption, which may not hold for all languages or phenomena.
- Does not include a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art approaches, e.g., the Neural Transducer.
- Representation analysis is limited to a small data subset and may not be representative of the whole dataset.
- Question: How does the model perform on languages with non-monotonic alignment (e.g., vowel or consonant harmony)?
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other SOTA approaches, including Neural Transducer?
- Question: How do the learned representations change with larger datasets or different hyperparameters?
