- Proposes a seq2seq model for morphological inflection with hard monotonic attention where the decoder either WRITEs a character or STEPs to the next input character.  
- Uses external character aligner to precompute gold action sequences and trains the model to predict these actions with cross-entropy loss.  
- Shows state-of-the-art results on CELEX (low-resource) and competitive performance on Wiktionary and SIGMORPHON 2016 benchmarks.  
- Empirical gains are largest in low-data settings, outperforming neural and strong WFST baselines.  
- Analysis indicates hard attention learns cleaner character-identity representations while soft attention encodes more positional information.  
- Elegant inductive bias that reduces search space and improves data efficiency for nearly-monotonic transduction tasks.  
- Thorough evaluation across low-, high-resource, and typologically diverse datasets bolsters robustness claims.  
- Clear writing, simple implementable design, and promised code release enhance reproducibility.  
- Relies on an external aligner, creating a pipeline dependency and sensitivity risk not analyzed in the paper.  
- Limited applicability to non-monotonic or non-concatenative morphologies (e.g., templatic Semitic systems), where soft attention can outperform it.  
- Optimization targets the most likely action sequence rather than the most likely output string, a theoretical approximation not fully examined.  
- Requests clarification on sensitivity to alignment quality (e.g., simpler aligners) and empirical impact of the inference approximation (how often top output string differs from top derivation).
