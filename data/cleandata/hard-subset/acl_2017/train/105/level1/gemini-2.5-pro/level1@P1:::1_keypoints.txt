- Novel hard monotonic attention: decoder restricted to WRITE/STEP actions to enforce monotonic alignments for morphological inflection.
- Empirical SOTA: thorough evaluation on CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON with especially strong low-resource performance.
- Representation analysis: SVD visualizations argue hard attention offloads positional info to STEP, explaining data efficiency.
- Excellent low-resource behavior: outperforms a strong latent-variable FST on CELEX and shows reduced overfitting.
- Elegant/simple design: decouples alignment via precomputed oracle and trains with a standard cross-entropy objective.
- Thorough evaluation and analysis: typological breakdown and representation study highlight where the model succeeds and fails.
- Dependence on external aligner: training requires pre-computed alignments and no sensitivity analysis to aligner quality is provided.
- Limited for non-monotonic phenomena: architecture is ill-suited to metathesis, reduplication, templatic morphology, and other non-local processes.
- Best-derivation approximation: training on a single gold action sequence ignores marginalization over multiple valid derivations and may penalize valid alternatives.
- Question on aligner robustness: how sensitive are results to aligner choice and alignment errors?
- Question on action sequence generation: clarify how multi-character mappings (e.g., o -> ie) and complex infixation are converted to WRITE/STEP sequences.
- Question on architecture variants: have you considered STEP_BACKWARD or other modifications to better handle prefixing or circumfixing languages?
