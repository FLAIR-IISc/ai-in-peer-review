- Introduces a neural encoder–decoder with hard monotonic attention that predicts sequences of WRITE and STEP actions for morphological inflection.
- Decouples alignment and transduction by using an external character aligner to produce oracle action sequences for supervised training.
- Reports strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive performance on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016, especially in low-resource settings.
- Simple, interpretable model formulation that provides a strong inductive bias for near-monotonic morphological processes.
- Demonstrates excellent empirical generalization and regularization benefits of monotonic constraints, notably reducing overfitting on small datasets.
- Replaces soft-attention weighted context with a pointer mechanism, making decisions inspectable via STEP/WRITE sequences and training simpler (cross-entropy on oracle).
- Provides thorough evaluation and insightful analysis, including alignment visualizations and SVD of encoder representations to explain why hard attention helps.
- Performance depends critically on the quality of the external aligner; the model cannot recover from alignment errors and sensitivity is not analyzed.
- Architecturally limited to strictly monotonic transduction, making it ill-suited for non-monotonic phenomena like infixation, metathesis, or reduplication.
- Paper could better situate its contribution within prior hard/monotonic attention work (e.g., RNN-T) and clarify novelty as the oracle-based supervised training strategy.
- Asks authors to report robustness to aligner quality and experiments with simpler aligners (e.g., Levenshtein-based) to assess practical applicability.
- Asks whether the framework can be extended to non-monotonic operations (e.g., JUMP_BACK, COPY) and how such extensions could be trained.
- Queries the impact of optimizing the most likely derivation vs. marginalizing outputs: how often beam search yields high-probability derivations that produce incorrect strings and whether this is a significant error source.
