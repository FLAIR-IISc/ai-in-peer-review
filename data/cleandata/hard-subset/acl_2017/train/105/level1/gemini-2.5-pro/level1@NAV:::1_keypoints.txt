Paper: neural morphological inflection model using hard monotonic attention with an explicit `step` action; trained on action sequences from pre-computed alignments with cross-entropy loss.

Main contribution: hard monotonic attention provides a strong inductive bias for sequential transduction, effective in low-resource settings and reducing overfitting.

Main contribution: strong empirical evaluation achieving new state-of-the-art results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON benchmarks.

Main contribution: analysis comparing hard and soft attention clarifies how the model explicitly encodes positional information.

Strength: excellent empirical performance, notably the first neural model to beat a latent-variable FST on low-resource CELEX.

Strength: conceptually simple and elegant design that decouples alignment from transduction, simplifying training objectives.

Strength: insightful visualizations (Section 5) showing the `step` action lets the encoder focus on character identity rather than position.

Weakness: near-monotonicity assumption limits applicability to non-concatenative/templatic morphological systems, reflected in some SIGMORPHON results.

Weakness: depends on an external character aligner with unexplored sensitivity, reducing end-to-end robustness.

Weakness: authors' soft-attention baseline outperforms the model on several complex languages, tempering SOTA claims and suggesting benefits are domain-specific.
