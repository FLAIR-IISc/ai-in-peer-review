- Proposes a character-level encoder–decoder with hard monotonic attention that emits write/step actions for morphological inflection.
- Trains via externally derived character alignments to produce gold action sequences and uses simple cross-entropy training.
- Evaluated on CELEX (low-resource), Wiktionary (high-resource), and SIGMORPHON 2016 (10 languages) against neural soft-attention and WFST baselines.
- Contribution: a simple hard monotonic transducer that is data-efficient and matches the task’s inductive bias.
- Contribution: strong empirical results, especially in low-resource settings, and competitive performance on larger datasets.
- Contribution: qualitative analyses comparing hard vs. soft attention encoders, alignment behavior, and encoder representations.
- Strength: excels in low-resource regimes by regularizing learning for suffixing and stem-change morphology, reducing overfitting vs. soft attention.
- Strength: clean training pipeline by decoupling alignment from transduction avoids marginalization and yields stable optimization.
- Strength: broad, careful evaluation across benchmarks and morphological typologies supporting the inductive-bias argument.
- Strength: model interpretability via explicit step/write actions and visualized alignments/SVD analyses.
- Strength: practical applicability to other nearly-monotonic character transduction tasks (e.g., transliteration, G2P).
- Weakness: depends on external alignments and optimizes a single oracle derivation, risking bias to the chosen aligner and ignoring marginalization.
- Weakness: limited advantage on non-monotonic phenomena (harmony, templatic morphology) where soft attention can outperform.
- Weakness: potential unfairness in comparisons (different training epochs for baselines), lack of statistical significance reporting, and missing decoding details (beam/greedy).
- Weakness: incomplete ablations—contributions of specific components (embeddings, encoder type, step design) are not quantified.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to exact-match accuracy without edit-distance or per-operation error analyses.
- Questions: sensitivity to choice and quality of external aligner and correlation with downstream accuracy.
- Questions: decoding strategy used (greedy vs. beam), beam size effects, and whether beam mitigates best-derivation approximation.
- Questions: enforcement of legality constraints during decoding and handling of insertions at input boundaries.
- Questions: requests for statistical significance tests across CELEX folds and languages.
- Questions: requested ablations removing feature embeddings, previous-output feedback, using uniLSTM, or replacing step with learned transitions.
- Questions: whether hybrid hard/soft attention or location-aware mechanisms could close gaps on harmony/templatic languages.
- Questions: request for edit-distance metrics and error distributions by operation type to better understand near-misses.
- Overall: a well-motivated, simple, and effective model advancing low-resource inflection with competitive high-resource performance; leans toward acceptance but needs stronger ablations, significance testing, and alignment dependence analysis.
