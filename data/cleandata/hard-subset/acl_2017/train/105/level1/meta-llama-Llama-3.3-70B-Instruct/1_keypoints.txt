- Proposes a hard-attention neural model that directly models nearly-monotonic alignments between input and output character sequences for morphological inflection.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016 across various setups compared to prior neural and non-neural methods.
- Analyzes continuous representations learned by hard and soft attention models, revealing differences in extracted features for inflection generation.
- Demonstrates strong performance on small training sets, outperforming previous neural models in low-resource conditions.
- Shows robust and competitive performance on large training sets as well.
- Provides insightful, detailed analysis of learned representations to explain model behavior.
- Comparison set is incomplete—does not benchmark against all existing models to fully position contributions.
- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining underlying mechanisms and limitations of the hard-attention approach.
- Evaluation is limited to morphological inflection tasks and does not test generalization to other sequence-to-sequence tasks.
- Asks how the model can be extended to handle non-monotonic alignments common in other seq2seq tasks.
- Requests more details on training procedure and hyperparameters used for reproducibility.
- Questions how the model could be adapted for tasks like machine translation or abstractive summarization and what modifications would be needed.
