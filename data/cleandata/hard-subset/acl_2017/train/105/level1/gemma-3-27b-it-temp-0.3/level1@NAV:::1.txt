## Review of "Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention"

This paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation leveraging a hard attention mechanism designed to capture the near-monotonic alignment inherent in this task. The authors evaluate their model on three standard datasets, demonstrating state-of-the-art performance and providing an analysis of the learned representations.

The primary contribution of this work is the introduction of a hard attention mechanism specifically tailored for morphological inflection. While attention mechanisms are common in sequence-to-sequence models, the explicit modeling of a monotonic alignment, enforced through the hard attention, appears to be a significant advantage, particularly in low-resource scenarios. A second contribution lies in the strong empirical results achieved on the CELEX dataset, surpassing previous latent variable WFST models – a notable accomplishment given the dataset’s small size. Finally, the analysis comparing the learned representations of the hard and soft attention models provides valuable insights into the behavior of both approaches.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Performance:** The model achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on all three datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness across different morphological complexities. The significant improvement on the CELEX dataset is particularly compelling.
2. **Novel Hard Attention Mechanism:** The proposed hard attention mechanism is well-motivated by the characteristics of morphological inflection and offers a clear advantage over standard soft attention, especially when data is limited.
3. **Thorough Evaluation:** The authors conduct a comprehensive evaluation, comparing their model to a range of strong baselines, including both neural and non-neural approaches.
4. **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of the learned representations provides valuable understanding of how the model functions and highlights the differences between hard and soft attention. This adds to the scientific value of the work beyond just performance gains.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Architecture:** The underlying encoder-decoder architecture is relatively standard. The primary innovation lies in the attention mechanism, and the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of how this mechanism fundamentally differs from existing hard attention approaches in other domains.
2. **Dependence on Pre-trained Alignments:** The reliance on independently learned character-level alignments for training, while simplifying the process, introduces an external dependency. The impact of the quality of these alignments on the final performance isn’t fully explored.
3. **SIGMORPHON Results Nuance:** The authors acknowledge that their model doesn’t outperform the soft attention baseline on all languages in the SIGMORPHON dataset. A more in-depth discussion of why this occurs, beyond the linguistic typology explanation, would be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the model's performance to the quality of the pre-trained character-level alignments? Have you experimented with different alignment methods or noisy alignments?
2.  Could you elaborate on the specific differences between your hard attention mechanism and other existing hard attention approaches, particularly in terms of training and inference?
3.  For the SIGMORPHON dataset, could you provide a more detailed error analysis to understand the types of morphological phenomena where the soft attention model excels and the hard attention model struggles?
