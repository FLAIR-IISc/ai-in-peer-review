- Novelty: proposes hard monotonic attention, a new approach substantially different from prior methods.
- Method: uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.
- Contrast to prior work: differs from soft-attention models that jointly learn alignment and conversion.
- Writing and experiments: paper is clearly written and presents comprehensive experiments.
- Empirical strengths: effective with small training datasets (e.g., CELEX).
- Empirical limitations: works well when alignments are mostly monotonic and phenomena are less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- Not universally best: method is not state-of-the-art under all conditions, especially when alignments are non-monotonic or highly context-dependent.
- Missing analysis: lacks discussion of practical merits like ease of implementation and computational cost.
