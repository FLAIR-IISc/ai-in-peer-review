- Proposes a neural seq2seq model with a novel hard monotonic attention using discrete WRITE/STEP actions to model near-monotonic morphological alignment.
- Trains on gold action sequences derived from pre-computed character alignments, reducing learning to a standard cross-entropy objective.
- Demonstrates strong, data-efficient performance in low-resource settings, notably outperforming prior models on CELEX.

- Novel, well-motivated WRITE/STEP formulation that bakes monotonicity into the model and acts as an effective regularizer.
- Strong empirical results on CELEX (avg accuracy 89.44%) with better generalization than soft attention in low-data regimes.
- Thorough experimental evaluation across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016, with useful visualizations of alignments and encoder representations.

- Not universally state-of-the-art: underperforms soft attention on languages with non-local phenomena (vowel harmony, templatic morphology).
- Depends on an external aligner (Sudoh et al., 2013) without analysis of sensitivity to alignment quality or aligner errors.
- Claims of simplicity are unsupported by practical comparisons (no reporting of training time, inference speed, or parameter counts).

- How sensitive is the model to the quality of pre-computed alignments; any experiments with simpler aligners or alignment-error analysis?
- Can the authors provide practical trade-offs versus soft attention (training time, inference speed, parameter numbers)?
- How could the model be extended to better handle non-local phenomena (e.g., vowel harmony, templatic morphology)?
