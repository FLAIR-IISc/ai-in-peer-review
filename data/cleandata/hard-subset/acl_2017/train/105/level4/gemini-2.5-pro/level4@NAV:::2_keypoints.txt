- Strength: Introduces a novel encoder-decoder architecture that explicitly enforces monotonicity via "hard monotonic attention."

- Strength: Well-written paper with clear presentation and justified motivation for monotonicity in certain seq2seq tasks.

- Weakness: Model may be equivalent to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignment and transduction, raising questions about novelty.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to morphological tasks and omits other monotonic sequence-to-sequence domains.

- Clarification needed: The nature of alignments is unclear—whether the model uses 1-to-many, many-to-1, many-to-many, or a mix.

- Baseline concern: A simpler two-stage approach (monotonic 1-to-many alignment tool + standard sequence tagger) is not compared; need explanation and empirical comparison.

- Question (2a): How does the proposed architecture fundamentally differ from the two-stage alignment-then-tagger method?

- Question (2b): Why was the two-stage method not included as an experimental baseline?

- Evaluation scope: Should test on other monotonic seq2seq tasks beyond morphology to better demonstrate effectiveness.

- Wording: Rephrase "on par or better" for objectivity; current phrasing may overstate equivalence.

- Feature details missing: Need source of linguistic features (e.g., POS) shown in Figure 1.

- Ablation concern: Could performance gains be due to added linguistic features rather than monotonicity constraints?

- Minor formatting/typos: NN in Eq.(3) should be non-italic; decapitalize "Where" (l.231); fix sequence notation consistency (commas or centered dots); typo at l.437 ("these").

- Figure issue: Example uses a Cyrillic font that hinders accessibility—use a standard script.

- After author response: Authors state alignments use 1-0, 0-1, and 1-1 to build many-to-many mappings (clarification noted).

- After author response: Reviewer reiterates need for comparison to Schnober et al.-style baseline (1-to-x alignments + BiLSTM tagger).

- AC notes: Cohn et al. (2016) is relevant and should be considered.

- AC note: Architecture may relate to Stack LSTM approaches that predict action sequences modifying inputs.

- AC question: Does using greedy alignment lose anything compared to methods that sum over all alignments (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016)?
