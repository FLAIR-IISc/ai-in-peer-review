- Proposes a neural model for morphological inflection using hard attention with pre-computed alignments from a Bayesian transliteration model, decoupling alignment and transduction.
- Novel approach distinct from soft-attention paradigms; idea is sound and clearly explained.
- Experiments are comprehensive, evaluating multiple languages and data conditions.
- Main strength: novelty—separates "where to edit" (alignment) from "how to edit" (transduction).
- Performs well in low-resource settings (e.g., CELEX).
- Effective on languages with largely monotonic character alignments (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- Weakness: mixed results overall; does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance.
- Applicability seems limited to certain linguistic typologies or data scenarios.
- Practical motivation is weak—paper lacks discussion of implementation advantages like training stability, convergence speed, or inference cost.
- Suggests quantifying training/inference speed and computational trade-offs versus soft-attention to clarify when the method is most suitable.
- Well-written paper that would benefit from explicit discussion of trade-offs to guide practical adoption.
