- Proposal: encoder-decoder with hard monotonic attention that decouples alignment and transduction, evaluated on three morphological reinflection datasets.
- Strength: novel explicit modeling of monotonicity as a useful inductive bias for linguistically monotonic seq2seq tasks; clear and well-written presentation.
- Ambiguity in alignment description: unclear whether model supports 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many mappings and how these are formally implemented.
- Missing simple monotonic baseline: no comparison to an align-then-tag approach (e.g., standard monotonic aligner + BiLSTM tagger) to justify added model complexity.
- Question: how does the proposed architecture fundamentally differ from or improve upon the simpler align-then-tag paradigm, and why was that baseline omitted?
- Limited evaluation scope: tested only on morphological reinflection; should include other monotonic tasks (grapheme-to-phoneme, transliteration, lemmatization) to demonstrate generality.
- Potential confound from linguistic features: model appears to use POS/linguistic features whose source and contribution are unspecified; ablation needed to isolate model vs feature effects.
- Insufficient discussion of related work: need to compare trade-offs with Rastogi et al. (2016) (sum-over-alignments vs greedy), Cohn et al. (2016), and action-sequence/Stack LSTM–style models.
- Design choice trade-offs: clarify why greedy alignment is chosen over marginalizing alignments and implications for performance.
- Phrasing of results: avoid vague claims like "on par or better"; report objective comparisons and discuss subsets where the model underperforms.
