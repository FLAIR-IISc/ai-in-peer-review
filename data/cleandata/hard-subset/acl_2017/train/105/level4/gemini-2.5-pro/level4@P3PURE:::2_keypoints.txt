- Proposal: novel encoder-decoder with a hard monotonic attention mechanism, evaluated on three morphological datasets.  
- Writing: generally clear and well-motivated—monotonicity is appropriate for morphological tasks.  
- Core mechanism: decouples alignment and transduction to enforce monotonic output generation, but description lacks clarity on key details.  
- Strength: explicitly models and enforces monotonicity, a sensible inductive bias for many seq2seq tasks.  
- Weakness: novelty is debatable—may amount to a BiRNN with decoupled alignment/transduction rather than a fundamentally new architecture.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation limited to morphology, missing broader monotonic seq2seq tasks to show generality.  
- Q1 (Alignment type): Unclear whether model supports 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many alignments—figure and text appear contradictory.  
- Q2 (Baseline): Missing comparison to a strong two-stage baseline (monotonic 1-to-many alignment tool + sequence tagger like BiLSTM); need explanation of differences and why not included.  
- Q3 (Task diversity): Should evaluate additional monotonic tasks (as in prior work) to better demonstrate the model’s advantages.  
- Q4 (Result interpretation): Phrasing “on par or better” is imprecise and may mask slight underperformance; claims should be more objective.  
- Q5 (Linguistic features): Source and role of linguistic features (e.g., POS) unclear—possible confounder for gains; an ablation study is requested.  
- Minor: Equation (3) format NN as non-italic operator; line edits: “Where”→“where”, sequence typesetting conventions, Figure 1 uses Cyrillic—use Latin example, l.437 “this”→“these”.
