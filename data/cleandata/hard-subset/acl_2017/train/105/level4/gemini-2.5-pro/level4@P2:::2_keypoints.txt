- Proposes a neural encoder-decoder with hard monotonic attention that generates inflections via WRITE and STEP actions.  
- Trains the model using pre-computed character-level alignments converted to gold action sequences and cross-entropy loss.  
- Empirically evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive results.  
- Particularly strong performance in low-resource settings (CELEX), outperforming prior neural and specialized WFST approaches.  
- Architecture explicitly enforces monotonicity, providing a principled inductive bias that reduces overfitting in low-data regimes.  
- Decoupling alignment and transduction lets the model leverage high-quality alignments from the start of training.  
- Analysis comparing hard vs soft attention shows hard attention uses explicit step mechanism for position while soft attention encodes positional info into character representations.  
- Main novelty concern: method may be similar to a simpler two-stage align-then-tag baseline, and no such baseline is compared.  
- Evaluation scope limited to morphological inflection, so broader claims about “nearly-monotonic” seq2seq tasks are unsubstantiated.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion and positioning relative to related transition-based architectures (e.g., Stack LSTMs) and monotonic attention work (e.g., Rastogi et al.).  
- Question: need clarification and empirical comparison to a 1-to-many alignment + sequence tagger baseline.  
- Question: potential risks of training on a single pre-computed alignment path instead of marginalizing over alignments.  
- Question: origin and contribution of morpho-syntactic feature embeddings (POS, gender, etc.) and their impact on performance.
