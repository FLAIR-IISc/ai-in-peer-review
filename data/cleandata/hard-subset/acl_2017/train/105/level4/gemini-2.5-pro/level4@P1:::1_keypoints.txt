- Core contribution: Proposes a neural seq2seq model with hard monotonic attention that uses pre-computed character alignments and predicts explicit write/step actions, yielding strong results on morphological inflection.
- Novelty: Introduces a linguistically motivated inductive bias for monotonicity that decouples alignment from transduction and simplifies training with cross-entropy.
- Strong low-resource performance: Outperforms prior neural and task-specific models on small datasets (CELEX) and shows robustness to overfitting versus soft-attention.
- Empirical breadth and analysis: Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016; includes alignment comparisons and SVD analyses explaining model behavior.
- Limitation — limited applicability: Model underperforms soft-attention on languages with non-monotonic or non-local morphological processes (e.g., vowel harmony, templatic morphology).
- Limitation — missing practical evaluation: Paper lacks comparison of computational costs, training/inference speed, parameter counts, and the overhead of the separate alignment step.
- Question a: How do hard- and soft-attention models compare in training and inference speed?
- Question b: How do they compare in number of parameters and memory footprint?
- Question c: Is generating the initial alignments a significant computational overhead in the overall workflow?
