- Introduces a novel hard monotonic attention mechanism that departs significantly from existing approaches.  
- Experimental results are mixed: strong in low-data settings and on languages with largely monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments.  
- Effective particularly on CELEX and languages like Russian, German, and Spanish where alignment is mostly monotonic.  
- Model uses hard attention based on externally obtained character alignments from a Bayesian transliteration method, unlike soft attention that jointly learns alignment.  
- Core idea is novel, technically sound, and the paper is clearly presented with comprehensive experiments.  
- Main concern: the method does not achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions.  
- Paper would be stronger with discussion of practical merits—ease of implementation and computational cost comparisons to soft attention—to clarify trade-offs.
