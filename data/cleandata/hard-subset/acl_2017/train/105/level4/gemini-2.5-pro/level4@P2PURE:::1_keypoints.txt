- Novel core idea: employing hard monotonic attention is novel and a significant departure from prior approaches.  
- Hard attention implemented using character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.  
- Substantially different from prior state-of-the-art soft attention, which jointly infers alignment and conversion.  
- Paper is clearly written and the experiments are comprehensive.  
- Experimental results are mixed: strong performance on smaller datasets (e.g., CELEX).  
- Performs well for languages with predominantly monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Overall effectiveness appears conditional on training-data size and alignment monotonicity.  
- Does not achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions.  
- Would be strengthened by discussion of practical merits (ease of implementation, computational cost) compared to soft-attention models.
