- Novel concept of hard monotonic attention is a significant and original contribution.
- Empirical results are inconsistent; the model performs best in low-resource settings and on languages with mostly monotonic alignments (e.g., CELEX, Russian, German, Spanish).
- Core innovation: hard attention using character alignments derived independently via a Bayesian transliteration model.
- This approach fundamentally differs from prior soft-attention methods that jointly learn alignment and conversion.
- Paper is innovative, technically sound, clearly written, and experimentally thorough.
- Main reservation: the method does not consistently outperform state-of-the-art across all conditions.
- Best suited to tasks with highly monotonic alignments and limited context-sensitive transformations.
- Lacks analysis of practical benefits such as computational cost or implementation ease, which would strengthen the argument.
