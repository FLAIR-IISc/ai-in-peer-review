- Strength: Proposes a novel encoder-decoder architecture that explicitly models and enforces monotonicity.  
- Strength: Well-written and clearly motivated—monotonicity is a sensible constraint for many seq2seq tasks.  
- Weakness: Novelty unclear relative to a standard BiRNN plus a decoupled alignment step.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to morphological tasks despite claimed broader applicability.  
- Core idea: Decouple alignment and transduction and use "hard monotonic attention" for monotonic output generation.  
- Unclear alignment specification: Reviewer asks whether alignments are 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many.  
- Baseline concern: A simpler monotonic pipeline (monotonic aligner + sequence tagger/BiLSTM) is not compared and may be conceptually similar.  
- Experimental gap: Why the simpler 1-to-x alignment + tagger baseline (e.g., Schnober et al., 2016) was not included?  
- Scope criticism: Should evaluate on additional monotonic tasks beyond morphology to support generality.  
- Result wording: Phrase "on par or better" is imprecise and appears biased; request for more balanced reporting.  
- Feature confound: Source and role of linguistic features (e.g., POS) are unclear and might explain some performance gains.  
- Minor corrections: Formatting for NN, lowercase "where", sequence notation consistency, use Latin alphabet in Figure 1, and replace "this" with "these" at l.437.  
- After response: Alignment description in response was garbled; reviewer interprets model as composing 1-0, 0-1, and 1-1 to form many-to-many mappings.  
- After response reiteration: Emphasis that a direct BiLSTM tagger baseline using 1-to-x alignments is still missing and needed for comparison.
