- Proposes a novel hard monotonic attention encoder-decoder that predicts discrete write/step actions to model near-monotonic morphological transduction.  
- Trains on oracle action sequences from an external aligner using standard cross-entropy loss.  
- Achieves strong empirical results, setting new SOTA on several benchmarks and outperforming a WFST on low-resource CELEX.  
- Provides comparative analysis showing hard attention learns more interpretable, position-explicit alignments versus soft attention.  
- Novel architecture is principled, interpretable, and well-motivated for near-monotonic tasks.  
- Demonstrates excellent data efficiency and low-resource performance, notably on CELEX.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016 with strong baselines.  
- Insightful representational analysis (SVD visualizations) explains why hard attention generalizes better with limited data.  
- Performance is not uniformly superior: soft attention outperforms on languages with non-concatenative or long-distance morphology.  
- Relies on an external pre-alignment step, raising concerns about dependency on aligner quality and error propagation.  
- Lacks discussion of practical trade-offs (training time including pre-alignment, inference speed, memory) versus soft attention.  
- Requests clarification on practical trade-offs (runtime/memory) and sensitivity to alignment quality or using simpler/noisier aligners.
