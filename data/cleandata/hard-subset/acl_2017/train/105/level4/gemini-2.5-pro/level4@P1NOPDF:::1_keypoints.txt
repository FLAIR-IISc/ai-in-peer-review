- Introduces a novel hard monotonic attention mechanism, a significant departure from prior approaches.
- Model obtains character alignments separately via a Bayesian transliteration method rather than joint soft-attention alignment.
- Paper is clearly written, methodologically sound, and experiments are comprehensive.
- Performance is strong on smaller datasets and languages with mostly monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments (e.g., CELEX, Russian, German, Spanish).
- Results are mixed overall: the model is less competitive under non-monotonic or more context-sensitive conditions.
- Main concern: does not achieve state-of-the-art across all settings and seems best suited to specific scenarios.
- Recommendation: discuss practical merits (ease of implementation, computational cost) versus soft-attention models to strengthen adoption arguments.
