- Novelty: Hard monotonic attention is a novel approach that fundamentally differs from prior methods.
- Method: Uses "hard attention" with character alignments precomputed via a Bayesian transliteration method.
- Contrast: Differs from prior state-of-the-art neural models that use soft attention to jointly align and convert.
- Empirical: Experimental results are mixed overall.
- Low-data strength: Model is effective when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX).
- Monotonic-alignments strength: Works well when alignments are predominantly monotonic with low context sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- Limitation: Does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions.
- Presentation and evaluation: Paper is clearly written and experimentally comprehensive.
- Recommendation: Would be strengthened by elaborating practical advantages like implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
