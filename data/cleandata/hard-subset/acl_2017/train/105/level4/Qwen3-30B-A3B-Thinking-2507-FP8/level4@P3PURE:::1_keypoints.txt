- Novel concept: hard monotonic attention substantially distinct from existing methods.  
- Model uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.  
- Fundamentally differs from prior state-of-the-art neural models that use soft attention and joint alignment-conversion.  
- Core idea is novel, well-motivated, and the paper is clearly written with comprehensive experiments.  
- Experimental results for morphological inflection generation are mixed.  
- Model is effective in low-data settings (e.g., CELEX).  
- Model works well when alignments are predominantly monotonic and have low context sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Primary limitation: does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art across all conditions; best suited for monotonic, low-context tasks.  
- Suggestion: discuss practical merits such as implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
