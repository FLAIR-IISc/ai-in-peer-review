- Introduces a novel hard monotonic attention approach for morphological inflection; well-motivated conceptual shift.  
- Employs precomputed character alignments via a Bayesian alignment method (not transliteration), contrasting with soft-attention that jointly optimizes alignment.  
- Core idea is novel and theoretically sound; paper is well-structured and experiments are thorough.  
- Experimental results are mixed, showing clear advantages with limited training data (e.g., CELEX).  
- Efficacy diminishes when alignments are non-monotonic or when tasks are context-sensitive.  
- Method excels on languages with predominantly monotonic, low-context alignments (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art across all conditions, limiting general applicability.  
- Limitations in more challenging scenarios should be explicitly acknowledged.  
- Authors should clarify practical advantages (implementation simplicity, training efficiency, computational cost) over soft-attention alternatives.  
- Without clearer practical benefits, the paper’s broader impact remains underemphasized.
