

Strengths:
The concept of hard monotonic attention presents a novel and substantially distinct approach compared to existing methods.

Weaknesses:
The experimental results for morphological inflection generation exhibit mixed performance. The proposed model demonstrates effectiveness under two specific conditions: when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX), and when alignments are predominantly monotonic with reduced context sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

General Discussion:
The authors introduce a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation that employs "hard attention" with character alignments independently derived via a Bayesian transliteration method. This approach fundamentally differs from prior state-of-the-art neural models for this task, which utilize "soft attention" by jointly solving alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework. The idea is both novel and well-founded, the paper is clearly written, and the experimental evaluation is thorough. The sole reservation is that the method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all scenarios; it is particularly suited for tasks featuring predominantly monotonic alignments and minimal context sensitivity. The paper would be strengthened by elaborating on practical advantages of the proposed method, such as implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
