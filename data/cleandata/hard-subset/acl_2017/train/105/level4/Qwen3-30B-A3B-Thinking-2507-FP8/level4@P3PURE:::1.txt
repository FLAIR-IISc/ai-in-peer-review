

Strengths:  
The concept of hard monotonic attention represents a novel approach that is substantially distinct from existing methods.  

Weaknesses:  
The experimental results for morphological inflection generation exhibit mixed performance. The proposed model demonstrates effectiveness under two specific conditions: (1) when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX), and (2) when alignments are predominantly monotonic and exhibit minimal context sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).  

General Discussion:  
The authors introduce a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation that employs "hard attention" with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian method for transliteration. This framework fundamentally differs from prior state-of-the-art neural models for this task, which utilize "soft attention" by jointly solving character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework. The core idea is both novel and well-motivated, and the paper is clearly written with comprehensive experimental evaluation. The primary limitation is that the method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art results across all conditions; it is particularly suited for tasks with predominantly monotonic alignments and reduced context sensitivity. To strengthen the paper, the authors should further discuss the practical merits of their approach, such as implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
