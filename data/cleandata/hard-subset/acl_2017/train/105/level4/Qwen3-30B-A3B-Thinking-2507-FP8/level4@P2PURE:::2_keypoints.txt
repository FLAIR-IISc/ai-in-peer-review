- Strength: Proposes a novel encoder-decoder model that explicitly enforces monotonicity via "hard monotonic attention."  
- Weakness: Model may be equivalent to a BiRNN with decoupled alignments rather than a genuinely new architecture.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to morphology datasets and does not test other monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.  
- Contribution: Clear rationale that many sequence-to-sequence tasks are inherently monotonic and standard encoder-decoder models may be suboptimal.  
- Method: Decouples alignment and transduction by first establishing monotonic input-output alignments, then training generation consistent with them.  
- Ambiguity: Alignment types are unclear—paper alternately suggests 1-to-many and many-to-one alignments; need explicit specification (1-0, 0-1, 1-1, many-to-many?).  
- Baseline omission: A simpler monotonic pipeline (monotone aligner + sequence tagger like BiLSTM, per Schnober et al. 2016) exists and was not compared; request explanation of differences and omission.  
- Evaluation gap: Should test on other monotonic tasks (phonetic transcription, spelling correction) to better demonstrate advantages.  
- Wording: Replace vague “on par or better” phrasing with precise performance descriptions.  
- Feature confound: Linguistic features (e.g., POS) are under-specified—report source and assess whether they drive gains rather than monotonic constraints.
