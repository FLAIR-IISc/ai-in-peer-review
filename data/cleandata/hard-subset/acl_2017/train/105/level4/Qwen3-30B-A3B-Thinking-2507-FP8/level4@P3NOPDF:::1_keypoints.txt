- Introduces a novel hard monotonic attention model that separates character alignment (from a Bayesian transliteration method) from morphological conversion.  
- Conceptual novelty is strong and theoretically sound, differing from prior soft-attention joint optimization approaches.  
- Method is clearly explained with a logical flow from problem definition to implementation.  
- Evaluation is broad, covering multiple languages (English, Russian, German, Spanish, Turkish) and datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, etc.).  
- Shows clear strengths in low-resource settings and when alignments are monotonic and context-independent.  
- Underperforms on tasks with non-monotonic, context-sensitive alignment (notably Turkish and some English Wiktionary data).  
- Results indicate the method is specialized rather than a universally superior replacement for soft-attention models.  
- Paper lacks quantification of practical trade-offs (training time, inference latency, memory usage) versus soft-attention baselines.  
- Needs deeper analysis explaining why performance degrades on Turkish/English (e.g., alignment errors, context sensitivity).  
- Should explicitly clarify the method’s scope, emphasizing suitability for languages with monotonic, low-context variation.  
- Recommend adding computational cost comparisons to substantiate claims of practical benefits.  
- Overall: technically solid and well-written, but should be reframed as a targeted solution with clarified limitations to strengthen impact.
