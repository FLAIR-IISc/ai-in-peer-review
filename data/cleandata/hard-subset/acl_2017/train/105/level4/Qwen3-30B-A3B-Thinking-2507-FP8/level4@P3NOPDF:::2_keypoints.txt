- Proposes encoder-decoder with hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection, addressing tasks that exhibit inherent monotonicity.  
- Well-written, clear technical description; conceptually sound and well-motivated for morphological tasks.  
- Major weakness: novelty questionable — method may reduce to BiRNN with decoupled alignment training; authors omit comparison to simpler monotone-aligner + neural tagger baselines (e.g., Jiampojamarn & Kondrak, Schnober et al.).  
- Major weakness: narrow evaluation — experiments only on morphology (Turkish, German, English); no tests on other monotonic seq2seq tasks (phonetic transcription, text normalization, chemical name generation) to show broader applicability.  
- Clarification needed on alignment mechanics: is alignment strictly 1-to-many, or are many-to-1 / many-to-many allowed; how do 1-0, 0-1, 1-1 alignments combine into final output?  
- Clarification needed on linguistic features: source of POS/other features and whether they, rather than monotonicity, drive performance gains.  
- Minor issues: vague phrasing ("on par or better" should use precise metrics); several formatting/typo fixes suggested (Eq.3 NN→\text{NN}, punctuation in sequences, Cyrillic fonts in Fig.1, small typos).  
- Conclusion/recommendation: contribution is promising but currently overstated due to missing baseline and limited evaluation; recommendation is reject pending substantial revision and additional experiments.
