- Novel encoder-decoder with "hard monotonic attention" is well-motivated and clearly written, explicitly enforcing monotonicity for morphological generation.
- Paper may reduce to a standard BiRNN with alignments decoupled from transduction, limiting architectural novelty.
- Evaluation is restricted to morphology, omitting other monotonic seq2seq tasks (e.g., phonological transcription, code generation) needed to show broader utility.
- Alignment description is ambiguous: unclear whether alignments are 1-to-many, many-to-one, or many-to-many.
- Lacks comparison to a simple baseline that uses a monotone aligner + standard sequence tagger, which also enforces monotonicity.
- Paper should explicitly differentiate its method from the monotone-aligner+tagger baseline and include that baseline in experiments.
- Broader evaluation on other monotonic tasks (phoneme-grapheme, text normalization, etc.) is required to validate claims.
- Reporting phrase "on par or better" is imprecise; use clearer language like "comparable or superior."
- Figure 1 implies use of POS/features but does not state their source or rule out that features—not monotonicity—drive gains.
- Clarify where linguistic features come from and whether they confound performance improvements.
- Minor issues: notation and typographical fixes (Equation (3) NN formatting, capitalization at line 231, commas in variable lists, avoid Cyrillic font in Figure 1, typo at line 437).
- Author response appears inconsistent about alignment construction; core question remains why not compare against monotone-aligner+tagger baseline to isolate architectural contribution.
- AC note: relevant prior work (Cohn et al. 2016, Stack-LSTM/action-sequence models) should be discussed.
- Greedy alignment reliance may limit expressiveness versus approaches that sum over alignments (e.g., Rastogi et al.); this connection should be addressed.
