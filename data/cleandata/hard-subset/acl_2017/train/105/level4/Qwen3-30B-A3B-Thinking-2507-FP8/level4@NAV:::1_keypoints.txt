- Novelty: Introduces hard monotonic attention as a novel and substantially different approach from existing methods.
- Mixed results: Experimental performance on morphological inflection is inconsistent across datasets.
- Effective niches: Model is effective with limited training data (e.g., CELEX) and for languages with predominantly monotonic, low-context alignments (Russian, German, Spanish).
- Model detail: Employs hard attention using character alignments obtained independently via a Bayesian transliteration method.
- Contrast to prior work: Fundamentally differs from prior SOTA neural models that use soft attention and jointly learn alignments and conversion.
- Paper quality: Idea is well-founded, clearly written, and accompanied by thorough experimental evaluation.
- Limitation: Does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance and is best suited for monotonic, low-context tasks.
- Recommendation: Would be strengthened by discussing practical advantages such as implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
