- Strength: Proposes an encoder-decoder with hard monotonic attention for strictly ordered input-output alignment; clear, well-motivated writing.  
- Novelty concern: Architecture appears equivalent to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignment and generation, so technical novelty is limited.  
- Baseline omission: Fails to compare against a simple strong baseline—generate monotonic 1-to-many alignments (e.g., Jiampojamarn & Kondrak) and train a sequence tagger (e.g., BiLSTM).  
- Alignment inconsistency: Paper shows 1-to-many alignments (Fig.1) but text implies many-to-1; alignment types need clarification.  
- Evaluation scope limited: Experiments only on morphology; should evaluate other monotonic tasks (phonetic transcription, phoneme-to-sound, code generation) to demonstrate broader impact.  
- Ambiguous claims: Phrase "on par or better" is imprecise and risks misrepresenting results by downplaying worse outcomes.  
- Contribution undermined: Alignment mechanism inconsistency and lack of strong baseline make it unclear whether monotonicity constraint drives gains.  
- Linguistic feature confound: Use of linguistic features (e.g., POS) is not justified or sourced and may confound performance attribution.  
- Related work missing/undiscussed: Connections to action-based models like Stack LSTM and the trade-off of greedy alignment vs. sum-over-alignments (Rastogi et al.) are not addressed.  
- Author follow-up: Authors clarified alignments can be 1-to-0, 0-to-1, 1-to-1, many-to-many, but still did not compare to the simpler sequence-tagging baseline.  
- Minor issues: Formatting/typos—Equation (3) NN formatting, "Where" capitalization, ellipsis commas in math, Cyrillic font in Fig.1 example, typo on line 437.  
- Recommendation: Reject—overstated technical contribution, inadequate baselines, narrow evaluation, alignment inconsistency, and potential feature confounds; suggested fixes: clarify alignments, add sequence-tagging baseline, broaden evaluation, and address feature sources.
