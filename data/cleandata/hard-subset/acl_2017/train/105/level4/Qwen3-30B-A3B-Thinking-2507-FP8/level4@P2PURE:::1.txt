

- Strengths:
The concept of hard monotonic attention represents a novel approach that fundamentally differs from prior methods.

- Weaknesses:
The experimental results for morphological inflection generation are mixed. The proposed model demonstrates effectiveness under two specific conditions: when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX), and when alignments are predominantly monotonic with minimal context sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

- General Discussion:
The authors introduce a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation that employs "hard attention" with character alignments precomputed separately via a Bayesian transliteration method. This approach fundamentally differs from prior state-of-the-art neural models for the task, which utilize "soft attention" to jointly solve character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework. The idea is novel and sound, the paper is clearly written, and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive. The primary concern is that the proposed method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions; it is most suitable for tasks with predominantly monotonic alignments and reduced context sensitivity. The paper would be strengthened by elaborating on the practical advantages of the method, such as implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
