**Strengths:**
The concept of hard monotonic attention represents a novel and significant departure from existing approaches.

**Weaknesses:**
Experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat inconsistent. The model demonstrates effectiveness when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX) and when alignment is predominantly monotonic and exhibits limited contextual sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

**General Discussion:**
This paper introduces a new neural model for morphological inflection generation, leveraging “hard attention” and character alignments determined independently via a Bayesian method for transliteration. This contrasts with prior state-of-the-art neural models employing “soft attention,” which jointly address character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework. 

The core idea is both innovative and well-reasoned. The paper is clearly presented, and the experimental evaluation is thorough. However, the method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions, appearing particularly well-suited to tasks with largely monotonic alignment and minimal contextual dependencies. The paper would benefit from a discussion of the practical advantages of this approach, such as ease of implementation or computational efficiency.
