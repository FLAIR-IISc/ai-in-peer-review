- Innovative concept: introduction of hard monotonic attention, distinct from existing approaches.  
- Methodology: uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.  
- Contrast with prior work: differs from prior SOTA soft-attention models that jointly learn alignment and conversion in a probabilistic framework.  
- Empirical results: mixed—stronger performance in low-resource settings and tasks with predominantly monotonic alignments.  
- Effective cases: particularly effective on CELEX and languages/phenomena with monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Limitations: does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art across all conditions and is less suitable for context-sensitive, non-monotonic tasks.  
- Presentation and evaluation: idea is original and well-founded; paper is well-written with thorough experiments.  
- Suggested improvement: should discuss practical advantages such as ease of implementation and computational efficiency.
