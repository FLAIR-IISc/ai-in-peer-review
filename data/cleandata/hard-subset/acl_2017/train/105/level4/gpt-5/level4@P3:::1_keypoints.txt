- Proposal: a hard monotonic seq2seq model for morphological inflection using ADVANCE/WRITE actions derived from external character alignments and trained with cross-entropy on oracle action sequences.

- Contribution 1: Simple, effective hard monotonic attention that decouples alignment from decoding and leverages near-monotonicity of inflectional processes.

- Contribution 2: Thorough empirical evaluation on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON-2016 showing state-of-the-art or competitive results, especially in low-resource and suffixing/stem-change languages.

- Contribution 3: Analysis comparing learned alignments and encoder representations of hard vs. soft attention, providing insight into encoded features.

- Strength: Strong low-resource performance (e.g., CELEX best average accuracy), better generalization and reduced overfitting compared to soft attention.

- Strength: Competitive/state-of-the-art results on Wiktionary and robust performance on suffixing + stem-change languages in SIGMORPHON.

- Strength: Simple, interpretable training via external alignments and explicit ADVANCE/WRITE control that maps naturally to edit operations.

- Strength: Insightful analysis showing soft attention often becomes monotonic and hard attention encodes character identity more distinctly.

- Strength: Clear presentation, comprehensive benchmarks, strong baselines, and qualitative illustrations.

- Weakness: Underperforms soft attention for languages with long-distance dependencies and non-concatenative phenomena (e.g., Finnish, Turkish, Hungarian, Arabic, Navajo), limiting generality.

- Weakness: Dependence on a specific CRP-based external aligner with no sensitivity analysis or comparison to alternative aligners.

- Weakness: Objective mismatch—model optimizes a single derivation instead of marginalizing over equivalent derivations, with no estimate of impact.

- Weakness: Unclear baseline parity and tuning fairness (different training epochs cited), complicating comparisons to soft-attention baselines.

- Weakness: Missing empirical analysis of efficiency, training/inference time, memory footprint, and parameter counts relative to soft attention.

- Questions for authors: How sensitive is performance to aligner choice or noise, and can alignments be fine-tuned during training?

- Questions for authors: What decoding strategy (greedy/beam) and beam width were used, and what are training/inference speed and memory comparisons versus soft attention?

- Questions for authors: Is the pointer strictly monotone or can it move backward, and how does the model handle circumfixation, templatic patterns, or reordering?

- Questions for authors: How common are multiple derivations for the same output, and were marginalization or constrained DP approaches attempted?

- Questions for authors: What do ablations show about feature embeddings, feedback connections, and encoder directionality?

- Additional comments: Request to report exact parameter counts, training schedules, early-stopping criteria, statistical significance tests, and a systematic error analysis by transformation type.

- Overall assessment: A solid, well-executed, and practically valuable contribution for near-monotonic inflection tasks; addressing alignment sensitivity, efficiency, and baseline tuning parity would strengthen the paper; reviewer leans positive.
