- Strength: Introduces a novel encoder-decoder architecture that explicitly enforces monotonicity via hard monotonic attention.  
- Strength: Competently written and presents a fresh idea for modeling monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks like morphology.  
- Weakness: Method may effectively reduce to a standard BiRNN with alignments handled separately, questioning novelty.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to morphological datasets and does not test other monotone seq2seq tasks.  
- Main idea: Decouples alignment and transduction by obtaining a monotone input–output alignment first, then training the model to generate outputs consistent with that alignment.  
- Unclear detail: The exact alignment scheme is ambiguous—paper gives conflicting indications about 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many alignments.  
- Baseline concern: A simple baseline (monotone 1-to-many alignment + standard sequence tagger/BiLSTM) is not included and might be competitive.  
- Baseline question 2a: Need clarification on how the proposed method differs from the straightforward alignment-then-tagger approach.  
- Baseline question 2b: Need explanation why the simple tagger baseline was not evaluated.  
- Evaluation scope: Authors should evaluate on additional monotone seq2seq tasks to better demonstrate advantages of explicit monotonicity modeling.  
- Reporting phrasing: The claim “on par or better” should be revised to avoid conflating worse and better results.  
- Feature confound: Limited information on linguistic features (e.g., POS); need sources and analysis whether features drive improvements rather than monotonicity constraints.  
- Minor typographical issues: Equation (3) formatting (NN), “Where” capitalization (l.231), comma/dots in x1 … xn (l.237), Fig.1 Cyrillic font visibility, and l.437 wording (“these”).  
- After author response: Clarified alignment allows 1-0, 0-1, 1-1 and composes into many-to-many, but description remains muddled.  
- Further comparison requested: Why not adopt Schnober et al.’s 1–x alignments and train a neural tagger to compare against the proposed method?  
- AC notes / related work: Highly relevant prior work includes Cohn et al. (2016) and Stack LSTM-style action-sequence models; authors should cite and position relative to these.  
- Marginalization concern: Does using a greedy single-best alignment sacrifice performance versus approaches that marginalize over all alignments (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016)?
