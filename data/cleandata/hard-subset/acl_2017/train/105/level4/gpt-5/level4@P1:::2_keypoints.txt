- Proposes a hard monotonic attention encoder–decoder for morphological inflection using discrete STEP/WRITE actions trained from induced alignments.
- Contribution: decouples alignment and transduction to allow simple cross-entropy training with a monotonic inductive bias.
- Contribution: strong empirical performance, notably superior in low-resource CELEX and competitive on larger datasets.
- Contribution: analysis comparing hard vs. soft attention alignments and encoder representations, highlighting positional encoding and character clustering differences.
- Strength: model’s STEP/WRITE inductive bias matches dominant monotone inflectional patterns and is supported by alignment visualizations.
- Strength: excels in low-resource settings and shows reduced overfitting compared to soft attention.
- Strength: competitive or best average accuracy on Wiktionary, with gains on several language/pos subsets.
- Strength: informative analysis showing soft attention encodes position more strongly while hard attention externalizes position via STEP.
- Weakness: limited novelty/positioning relative to prior hard/monotonic attention, transducer, neural WFST, and transition-based action models.
- Weakness: missing a simple monotone 1-to-x BiLSTM tagging baseline to isolate the control mechanism’s value.

- Weakness: training on a single external alignment (best derivation) rather than marginalizing over alignments may lose robustness and probability mass.
