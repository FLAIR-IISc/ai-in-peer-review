- Strength: Proposes a novel encoder–decoder with hard monotonic attention that explicitly enforces monotonicity.
- Weakness: Model may effectively reduce to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignments.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to morphological inflection; no other monotone seq2seq tasks considered.
- Contribution: Decouples alignment and transduction by deriving monotone input–output alignments then training the model to generate outputs consistent with them.
- Alignment clarity: Unclear whether alignments are 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many and at which stage each type is used.
- Baseline omission: Reviewer proposes a simple monotone baseline (produce 1-to-many alignments then train a BiLSTM tagger) and asks why this was not included.
- Baseline questions: Request clarification how the proposed approach substantively differs from a tagger-based monotone pipeline and why that baseline was omitted.
- Evaluation scope: Recommends broader evaluation on other monotone seq2seq tasks to better demonstrate benefits.
- Reporting phrasing: Suggests more neutral wording than “on par or better” to avoid bias in reporting small differences.
- Linguistic features: Asks source of linguistic features (e.g., POS) used and whether they might explain gains independent of monotonicity constraints.
