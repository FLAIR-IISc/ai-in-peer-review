- Novelty: hard monotonic attention is a novel idea and markedly different from prior work.
- Method: model uses “hard attention” with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method, unlike joint soft-attention approaches.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and includes comprehensive experiments.
- Mixed results: experimental performance is inconsistent across tasks.
- Low-data strength: model performs well with limited training data (e.g., CELEX).
- Monotonic-strength: effective when alignments are largely monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- Limitation: not consistently state-of-the-art for tasks with non-monotonic or context-sensitive phenomena.
- Missing analysis: paper should discuss practical merits such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
