- Introduces a novel hard, monotonic attention mechanism distinct from prior approaches.  
- Uses externally induced character alignments via a Bayesian transliteration-style aligner.  
- Contrasts with prior soft-attention neural methods that learn alignment and transduction jointly.  
- Paper is clearly written and presents a thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Empirical results are mixed: strong performance with limited training data and largely monotonic alignments (e.g., CELEX, Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Weaker performance when alignment is less monotonic or requires stronger context sensitivity.  
- Main reservation: approach does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art across all settings.  
- Would be more compelling with concrete discussion of practical benefits such as implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
