- Strengths:
The concept of hard, monotonic attention is novel and markedly different from prior approaches.

- Weaknesses:
Results on morphological inflection generation are mixed. The model performs well with limited training data (e.g., CELEX) and when alignments are largely monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

- General Discussion:
The paper presents a neural model for morphological inflection generation that uses hard attention, relying on character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method. This contrasts with prior state-of-the-art neural models that use soft attention and jointly learn alignment and transduction within a probabilistic framework. The idea is sound, the writing is clear, and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive. The main concern is that the method does not achieve state-of-the-art performance in all conditions; it is best suited to settings with predominantly monotonic alignments and limited context sensitivity. The paper would be more compelling if it articulated the practical benefits of the approach—such as ease of implementation and computational cost—to encourage adoption even when accuracy improvements are not universal.
