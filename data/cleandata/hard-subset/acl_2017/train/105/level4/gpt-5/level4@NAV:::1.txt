- Strengths:
The concept of hard monotonic attention is novel and markedly distinct from prior approaches.

- Weaknesses:
The experimental outcomes on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. The proposed model performs well when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX) and when alignments are largely monotonic with low context sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

- General Discussion:
The authors present a new neural model for morphological inflection generation that uses hard attention, with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method. This stands in clear contrast to previous state-of-the-art neural models based on soft attention, where alignment and conversion are addressed jointly within a probabilistic framework. The idea is original and sound, the paper is clearly written, and the experiments are comprehensive. The primary concern is that the method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all settings. It is best suited to scenarios with predominantly monotonic alignments and less context-sensitive phenomena. The paper would be more persuasive if it discussed practical advantages such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
