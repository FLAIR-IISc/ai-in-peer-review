- Strengths:
The concept of hard monotonic attention is novel and substantially different from prior approaches.

- Weaknesses:
The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. The proposed model is effective when the amount of training data is small (e.g., CELEX). It also performs well when alignments are mostly monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

- General Discussion:
The authors propose a neural model for morphological inflection generation that uses hard attention, with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian method for transliteration. This is substantially different from prior state-of-the-art neural models for the task that use soft attention, where character alignment and conversion are learned jointly in a single probabilistic model.

The idea is novel and sound. The paper is clearly written, and the experiments are comprehensive. The main concern is that the proposed method does not achieve state-of-the-art performance in all conditions. It is best suited to settings with mostly monotonic alignment and limited context sensitivity. The paper would be more convincing if it discussed the practical merits of the approach, such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
