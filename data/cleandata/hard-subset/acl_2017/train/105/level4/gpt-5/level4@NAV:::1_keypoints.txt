- Novelty: Introduces hard monotonic attention that is novel and clearly distinct from prior work.  
- Empirical limitations: Experimental results are mixed — strong in low-data settings and for largely monotonic, low-context languages.  
- Applicability: Works well for datasets like CELEX and languages such as Russian, German, and Spanish where alignments are monotonic.  
- Method: Uses hard attention with separately obtained character alignments via a Bayesian transliteration method, contrasting with joint soft-attention approaches.  
- Presentation: Idea is original and sound; paper is clearly written and experiments are comprehensive.  
- Main concern: Does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art across all settings and is best suited for monotonic, less context-sensitive phenomena.  
- Missing discussion: Lacks analysis of practical advantages (ease of implementation, computational cost) that would strengthen the paper.
