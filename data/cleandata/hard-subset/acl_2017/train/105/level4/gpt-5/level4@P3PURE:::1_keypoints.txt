- Novelty: Introduces hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection, a substantially different and novel approach compared to prior work.  
- Method: Uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.  
- Contrast to prior work: Differs from state-of-the-art neural models that use soft attention and learn alignments jointly.  
- Writing and experiments: Paper is clearly written and experiments are comprehensive.  
- Strength: Effective in low-resource settings (e.g., CELEX).  
- Strength: Performs well when alignments are mostly monotonic and conversions are less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Weakness: Experimental results are mixed and the model does not achieve state-of-the-art across all conditions.  
- Limitation: Best suited to mostly monotonic, limited-context tasks rather than highly context-sensitive cases.  
- Suggested improvement: Should discuss practical merits such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
