- Strength: Introduces a new encoder–decoder model that explicitly enforces monotonicity.  
- Strength: Well written and mostly clear; proposes a genuinely new idea for morphological tasks.  
- Motivation: Many Seq2Seq problems are monotonic, so enforcing monotonicity by decoupling alignment and transduction is sensible.  
- Method: First produce a monotonic alignment, then train a model to generate outputs consistent with those alignments.  
- Weakness: Method may reduce to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignments.  
- Weakness: Evaluated only on morphological datasets; lacks experiments on other monotonic Seq2Seq tasks.  
- Unclear: Exact form of alignments is ambiguous—are they 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many?  
- Baseline missing: No comparison to a simple monotone pipeline (1-to-many aligner + standard sequence tagger).  
- Question: How does the proposed method differ from the simple 1-to-many aligner + tagger baseline?  
- Question: Why was that simple baseline not included in experiments?  
- Scope: Should evaluate on broader monotonic Seq2Seq tasks to demonstrate generality.  
- Wording: The claim “on par or better” is vague and should be revised.  
- Features: Linguistic feature details are sparse—source of features unclear.  
- Confound: Gains might be due to linguistic features rather than monotonicity constraints.  
- Minor: Equation (3) should format NN as text (e.g., \text{NN}).  
- Minor: Line 231 “Where” should be lowercase.  
- Minor: Notation x1…xn should use commas and aligned dots (x1, …, xn).  
- Minor: Figure 1 uses Cyrillic font—example unreadable without fonts.  
- Minor: Line 437 typo—should be “these.”
