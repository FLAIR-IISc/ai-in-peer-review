- Strengths:
The introduction of hard, monotonic attention is novel and substantially different from prior approaches.

- Weaknesses:
The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. The proposed model is effective when the amount of training data is small (e.g., CELEX). It is also effective when alignments are mostly monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

- General Discussion:
The authors propose a neural model for morphological inflection generation that uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration model. This is substantially different from prior state-of-the-art neural models for the task, which use soft attention and learn character alignment and conversion jointly within a single probabilistic framework.

The idea is novel and sound. The paper is clearly written, and the experiments are comprehensive. The main concern is that the proposed method is not state of the art under all conditions. It appears particularly well-suited to settings with mostly monotonic alignments and less context-sensitive phenomena. The paper would be more convincing if it explicitly described the practical merits of the approach, such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
