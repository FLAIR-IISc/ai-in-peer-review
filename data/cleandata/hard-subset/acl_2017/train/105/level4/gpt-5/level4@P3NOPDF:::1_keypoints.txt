- Proposal: neural model for morphological inflection using hard, monotonic attention guided by alignments from a Bayesian transliteration aligner, decoupling alignment and transduction.
- Novelty: hard monotonic attention is a clear departure from standard soft-attention models.
- Methodology: well motivated and clearly described.
- Experiments: comprehensive evaluation across multiple languages.
- Strength: performs well in low-resource settings (e.g., CELEX).
- Strength: effective for languages with largely monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- Mixed results: not consistently state-of-the-art, especially when alignments are non-monotonic or morphology is context-sensitive.
- Dependence on external alignments: reliance on a separate Bayesian aligner may propagate errors; sensitivity to alignment quality is underanalyzed.
- Practical aspects underexplored: paper omits implementation complexity, computational cost, and decoding speed comparisons to soft-attention baselines.
- Non-monotonic phenomena: limited analysis of how the model handles insertions, deletions, or long-distance/non-monotonic edits.
- Suggestion — efficiency: report training/inference time, memory, FLOPs, beam width, and ease of implementation.
- Suggestion — alignment sensitivity: evaluate multiple aligners, vary alignment noise, and compare to a joint-learning variant.
- Suggestion — characterize regimes: include learning curves by data size and per-language/per-phenomenon error analyses.
- Suggestion — clarify decoding: describe the action space (skip/emit) and any support for back-off or relaxed constraints when monotonicity is violated.
- Suggestion — rigor & interpretability: report statistical significance and variance across seeds, use strong tuned baselines, and add alignment visualizations and example success/failure cases.
- Overall assessment: novel and promising with clear benefits in certain regimes but empirically mixed; borderline accept, leaning toward acceptance if practical and analytical gaps are addressed.
