- Novelty: Introduces hard, monotonic attention that is substantially different from prior approaches.
- Method: Uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration model.
- Contrast to prior work: Differs from state-of-the-art neural models that use soft attention and learn alignments jointly.
- Writing and experiments: Paper is clearly written and experiments are comprehensive.
- Strengths in results: Highly effective with small training data (e.g., CELEX).
- Strengths in languages: Works well when alignments are mostly monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- Weaknesses in results: Experimental outcomes are mixed and the method is not state of the art under all conditions.
- Applicability concern: Particularly suited to monotonic, less context-sensitive phenomena, limiting generality.
- Missing discussion: Lacks explicit details on practical merits such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
