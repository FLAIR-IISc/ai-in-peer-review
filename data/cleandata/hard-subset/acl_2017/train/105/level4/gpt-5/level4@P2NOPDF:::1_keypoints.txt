- Novelty: Introduces hard, monotonic attention, a novel and markedly different approach from prior methods.

- Method: Neural model uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.

- Contrast: Differs from prior soft-attention neural models that jointly learn alignment and transduction in a probabilistic framework.

- Empirical: Results are mixed—strong performance with limited training data and largely monotonic, low-context-sensitive alignments (e.g., CELEX, Russian, German, Spanish).

- Limitation: Does not achieve state-of-the-art across all conditions; best suited for predominantly monotonic alignments and limited context sensitivity.

- Recommendation: Paper is sound, clearly written, and thoroughly evaluated but would be more compelling if it highlighted practical benefits (ease of implementation, computational cost) to encourage adoption despite not always improving accuracy.
