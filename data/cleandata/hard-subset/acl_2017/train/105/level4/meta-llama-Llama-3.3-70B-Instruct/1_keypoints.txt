- Introduces hard monotonic attention, offering a fresh and distinct perspective from existing approaches.
- Proposes a novel neural architecture for morphological inflection using hard attention and character alignments from a Bayesian transliteration method.
- Diverges substantially from prior state-of-the-art soft-attention models that jointly learn alignment and conversion.
- The core idea is innovative and theoretically well-founded.
- The paper is clear and contains comprehensive experimentation.
- Experimental results are mixed overall.
- The model shows clear benefits with limited training data (e.g., CELEX).
- It performs well when alignments are predominantly monotonic and less context-dependent (Russian, German, Spanish).
- Key concern: the method may not universally outperform existing approaches and is best suited to specific task types.
- Recommendation: better emphasize practical advantages, including implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
