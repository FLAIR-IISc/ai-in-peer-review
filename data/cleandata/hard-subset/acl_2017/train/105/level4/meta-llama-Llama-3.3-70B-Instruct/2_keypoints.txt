- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder architecture with hard monotonic attention to explicitly model monotonicity in seq2seq tasks — significant strength.
- Potentially equivalent to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignments, raising questions about novelty.
- Evaluation is limited to morphology tasks, so generality to other monotonic seq2seq tasks is untested.
- Paper is well-written and motivation for enforcing monotonicity is clear.
- Core idea: decouple alignment and transduction by first producing monotone alignments then training generation to follow them.
- Description of the alignment process is unclear and raises multiple questions.
- Ambiguity about alignment types used (1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many) needs clarification.
- A simpler baseline (monotone aligner + standard sequence tagger) is not included but should be.
- Reporting that the model is “on par or better” is misleading when some results are worse; clearer presentation needed.
- Missing details about linguistic features (source and impact) that may affect performance.
- Minor issues: confusing notation in Eq. (3), formatting errors, and unnecessary Cyrillic font in Figure 1.
- After author response, alignment generation remains unclear; comparison to a 1-x alignment + neural tagger baseline is still needed.
- Related work connections (e.g., Stack LSTM) should be discussed more explicitly.
- Use of greedy alignment is a limitation because it ignores summing over possible alignments, unlike other models.
