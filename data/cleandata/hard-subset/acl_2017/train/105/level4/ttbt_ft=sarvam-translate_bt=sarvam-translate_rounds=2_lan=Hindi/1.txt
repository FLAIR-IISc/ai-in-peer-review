Strengths:
The idea of "strict attention" is new and quite different from others.
Weaknesses:
The results of using morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. The proposed model is effective if the amount of training data is small (e.g., CELEX). It is also effective if the alignment is mostly monotonous and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).
General Discussion:
The authors have proposed a new neural model for morphological inflection generation that uses "strict attention," where different character alignments are obtained using a Bayesian method for transcription. This is quite different from the previous state of the art for this task, which uses "soft attention," where character alignment and transcription are jointly solved in a probabilistic model.
The idea is new and solid. The paper is clearly written. The experiment is extensive. The only concern is that the proposed method is not the state of the art in all circumstances. It is mostly suitable for tasks with monotonous alignments and less context-sensitive phenomena. The paper would be more reliable if it described the practical qualities of the proposed method, such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
