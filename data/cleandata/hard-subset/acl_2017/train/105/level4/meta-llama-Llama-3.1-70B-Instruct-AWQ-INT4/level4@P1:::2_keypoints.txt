- Proposes a hard-attention neural model for nearly-monotonic morphological inflection generation and reports state-of-the-art results.  
- Contribution: introduces a hard attention model tailored for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.  
- Contribution: evaluates the model on three morphological inflection datasets, establishing new state-of-the-art performance.  
- Contribution: analyzes and compares hard-attention and soft-attention models to reveal their differing feature extraction behaviors.  
- Strength: novel, effective approach that explicitly models monotonicity in inflection generation.  
- Strength: achieves strong empirical results across multiple datasets and settings.  
- Strength: provides thorough analysis contrasting hard and soft attention, clarifying strengths and weaknesses.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to morphology tasks, unclear generalizability to other monotonic seq-to-seq problems.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear explanation of how the hard-attention mechanism differs from simpler monotonic alignment plus sequence tagging.  
- Weakness: missing baseline comparisons with other monotonic seq-to-seq models (e.g., Stack LSTM).  
- Question: please clarify how hard attention differs from monotonic alignment followed by a standard sequence tagger.  
- Question: why were other monotonic seq-to-seq baselines like Stack LSTM not included?  
- Question: provide details on linguistic features used (e.g., POS tags) and how they were obtained.
