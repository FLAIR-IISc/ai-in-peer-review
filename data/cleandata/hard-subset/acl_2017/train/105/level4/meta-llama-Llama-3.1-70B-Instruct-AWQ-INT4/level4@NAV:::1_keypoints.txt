- Introduces hard monotonic attention, a notable and original departure from existing approaches.  
- Proposes a neural model combining hard attention with character alignments from Bayesian transliteration.  
- Method contrasts with state-of-the-art soft-attention models that jointly learn alignment and conversion probabilistically.  
- Paper is innovative, well-founded, clearly written, and well-organized.  
- Experimental results are mixed: strong when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX) and when alignment is largely monotonic (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Performance is not consistently superior and appears better suited to monotonic, less context-sensitive tasks.  
- Recommendation: clarify practical advantages, especially implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
