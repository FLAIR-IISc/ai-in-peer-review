- Novel introduction of hard monotonic attention that is substantially different from existing soft-attention methods.  
- Uses character alignments from a Bayesian transliteration method to drive the hard-attention model.  
- The idea is novel, sound, and the paper is clearly written.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across multiple scenarios.  
- Results are mixed: strong performance with limited training data (e.g., CELEX) and for languages with mostly monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Method is not necessarily state-of-the-art in all conditions and seems limited to monotonic, less context-sensitive tasks.  
- Concern about generalization to context-sensitive or non-monotonic phenomena.  
- Recommend adding discussion of practical advantages such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
