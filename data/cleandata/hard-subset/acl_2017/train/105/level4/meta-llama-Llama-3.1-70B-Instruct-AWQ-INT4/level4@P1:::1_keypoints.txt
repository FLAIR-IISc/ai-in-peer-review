- Proposes a novel hard-attention neural model for morphological inflection using separately obtained Bayesian character alignments; evaluated on three datasets and achieves SOTA in some setups.
- Contributions: (1) hard monotonic attention model, (2) evaluation establishing new SOTA on three datasets, (3) analysis comparing hard-attention to soft-attention.
- Strength: hard monotonic attention idea is novel and substantially different from prior work.
- Strength: paper is clearly written with comprehensive experiments.
- Strength: model is effective with small training data (CELEX) and when alignments are mostly monotonic (Russian, German, Spanish).
- Weakness: results are mixed and the model is not SOTA in all conditions.
- Weakness: lacks thorough discussion of practical merits like ease of implementation and computational cost.
- General: paper is sound and well-written but would benefit from discussing practical advantages and why it works better under monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments.
- Question: What is the computational cost compared to soft-attention models?
- Question: How does the model handle non-monotonic alignments and are there plans to extend it?
- Question: How easy is implementation compared to soft-attention models?
