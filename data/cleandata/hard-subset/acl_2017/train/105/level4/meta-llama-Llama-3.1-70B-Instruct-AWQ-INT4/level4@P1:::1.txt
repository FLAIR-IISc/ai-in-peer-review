The paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, which employs a hard attention mechanism. This approach is substantially different from previous state-of-the-art neural models for the task, which use soft attention, where character alignment and conversion are solved jointly in the probabilistic model.

**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions:**

The authors propose a hard attention model for morphological inflection generation, which uses character alignments obtained separately through a Bayesian method for transliteration. The model is evaluated on three previously studied morphological inflection generation datasets and achieves state-of-the-art results in various setups. The main contributions of the paper are: (1) a hard attention model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning, (2) an evaluation of the model on the task of morphological inflection generation, establishing a new state of the art on three previously studied datasets, and (3) an analysis and comparison of the proposed model and the soft-attention model, shedding light on the features extracted by such models for the inflection generation task.

**Strengths:**

1. The idea of hard monotonic attention is novel and substantially different from others.
2. The paper is clearly written, and the experiment is comprehensive.
3. The proposed model is effective in certain conditions, such as when the amount of training data is small (e.g., CELEX) or when the alignment is mostly monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

**Weaknesses:**

1. The experiment results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed, and the proposed model is not necessarily the state of the art in all conditions.
2. The paper does not provide a thorough discussion of the practical merits of the proposed method, such as the ease of implementation and computational cost.

**General Discussion:**

The authors' proposal is sound, and the paper is well-written. However, to make the paper more convincing, it would be beneficial to discuss the practical advantages of the proposed method, such as its ease of implementation and computational cost. Additionally, the authors could provide more insights into why the proposed model is more effective in certain conditions, such as when the alignment is mostly monotonic and less context-sensitive.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on the computational cost of the proposed model compared to the soft-attention model?
2. How does the proposed model handle non-monotonic alignments, and are there any plans to extend the model to handle such cases?
3. Can you discuss the ease of implementation of the proposed model, particularly in comparison to the soft-attention model?
