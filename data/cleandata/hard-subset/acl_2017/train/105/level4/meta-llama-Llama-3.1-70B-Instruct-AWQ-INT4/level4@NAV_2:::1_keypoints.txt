- Paper introduces a novel neural model for morphological inflection using hard attention to exploit nearly-monotonic character alignments.
- Main contributions: (1) a hard-attention model for nearly-monotonic seq2seq learning, (2) new state-of-the-art on three morphological inflection datasets, (3) analysis comparing hard- and soft-attention models.
- Hard attention jointly aligns and transduces with a focused representation at each step, making it well-suited for character-level inflection.
- Strength: The hard monotonic attention idea is novel and offers a fresh perspective on sequence-to-sequence learning.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across three previously studied datasets demonstrates the model’s effectiveness in multiple settings.
- Strength: Comparative analysis with soft-attention provides useful insights into what features each model extracts for inflection generation.
- Weakness: Results are mixed—strong performance primarily in certain conditions (small training sets or mostly monotonic alignments).
- Weakness: Limited discussion of practical merits such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
- Question: How will the authors address limitations when alignments are non-monotonic or when context-sensitive phenomena occur?
- Question: Can the authors provide more details on the computational cost and implementation complexity versus existing state-of-the-art models?
- Question: How do the authors envision applying the hard-attention model to other nearly-monotonic align-and-transduce tasks (e.g., abstractive summarization, machine translation)?
