- Strengths:
The introduction of hard monotonic attention is a novel concept that significantly deviates from existing approaches.

- Weaknesses:
The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat inconsistent. The proposed model appears to be effective under certain conditions, specifically when the training data is limited (e.g., CELEX) or when the alignment is predominantly monotonic and less context-dependent (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

- General Discussion:
The authors propose a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, leveraging "hard attention" and character alignments obtained through a Bayesian method for transliteration. This approach diverges substantially from the state-of-the-art neural models for this task, which rely on "soft attention" and jointly solve character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework.
The idea is both novel and well-founded. The paper is clearly written, and the experiments are comprehensive. However, the proposed method may not necessarily represent the state of the art under all conditions. It appears to be better suited for tasks with predominantly monotonic alignments and less context-sensitive phenomena. To further strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to discuss the practical advantages of the proposed method, such as its ease of implementation and computational cost.
