**Strengths:**

The proposed "hard monotonic attention" mechanism represents a novel and substantially different approach from existing methods, offering a fresh perspective on morphological inflection generation.

**Weaknesses:**

The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are mixed. While the proposed model demonstrates effectiveness with limited training data (e.g., CELEX) and in cases where the alignment is predominantly monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish), its performance is not consistently superior across all conditions.

**General Discussion:**

The authors introduce a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, leveraging "hard attention" and character alignments obtained through a Bayesian method for transliteration. This approach diverges from the state-of-the-art neural models, which employ "soft attention" and jointly solve character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework. The idea is well-conceived, and the paper is clearly written, with comprehensive experiments.

However, the proposed method may not necessarily represent the state-of-the-art in all scenarios. Its suitability is more pronounced in tasks with predominantly monotonic alignments and less context-sensitive phenomena. To strengthen the paper, the authors could provide a more detailed discussion on the practical advantages of their proposed method, including its ease of implementation and computational cost. This would enhance the overall convincingness of the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's benefits.
