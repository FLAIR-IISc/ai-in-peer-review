- Introduces hard monotonic attention as a novel, substantially different approach for morphological inflection generation.  
- Uses hard attention with Bayesian-derived character alignments (from transliteration), diverging from soft-attention neural models that jointly align and convert.  
- Shows strong performance with limited training data (e.g., CELEX) and for languages/tasks with mostly monotonic, less context-sensitive alignments (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Experimental results are mixed; the method does not consistently outperform existing approaches across all conditions.  
- Model appears particularly well-suited to monotonic alignments and less context-sensitive phenomena, limiting generality.  
- Paper is innovative, well-founded, and clearly written.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering various scenarios and datasets.  
- Main concern: lack of consistent superiority and potential robustness issues compared to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Recommendation to add discussion and analysis of practical advantages (ease of implementation, computational cost) to strengthen the paper.  
- Reviewer left rating, confidence, and recommendation unspecified.
