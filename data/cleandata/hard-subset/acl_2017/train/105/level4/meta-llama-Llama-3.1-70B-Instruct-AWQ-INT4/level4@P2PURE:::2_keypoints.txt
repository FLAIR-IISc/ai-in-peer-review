- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder model that explicitly enforces monotonicity for sequence-to-sequence tasks.
- Introduces "hard monotonic attention" by decoupling alignment and transduction.
- Model may be equivalent to an ordinary bidirectional RNN with decoupled alignments.
- Evaluation is restricted to morphology datasets and omits other monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.
- Alignment procedure is unclear; description conflicts between 1-to-many and many-to-1 interpretations.
- A simpler baseline (monotone 1-to-many aligner + standard sequence tagger) exists and resembles prior work (Schnober et al., 2016).
- Authors should clarify how their approach differs from the simpler aligner+tagger idea and why that baseline was not included.
- The claim "on par or better" may be misleading if it masks cases where the system performs worse; report raw results instead.
- Limited information on linguistic features (e.g., POS tags); source and contribution of these features to performance are unclear.
- After author response, alignments appear mixed (1-0, 0-1, 1-1, many-to-many); reviewer questions why not restrict to 1-x alignments and use a tagger baseline.
- Use of greedy alignment rather than summing over all alignments may be a methodological limitation.
- Related work and connections (Cohn et al. 2016, Nicolai et al. 2015, Stack LSTM/action-prediction models) are not sufficiently discussed.
- Minor presentation issues: inconsistent font in Equation (3), capitalization at line 231, math dot alignment, unnecessary Cyrillic font in Fig. 1, and a typo at line 437.
