- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder that enforces monotonicity for seq2seq tasks; may be equivalent to a BiRNN with decoupled alignments.  
- Well-written paper introducing "hard monotonic attention" and evaluated on three morphology datasets.  
- Method decouples alignment and transduction by first producing monotone alignments and then training generation, but alignment procedure is unclear.  
- Ambiguity in alignment type: figure suggests 1-to-many, text implies many-to-1.  
- Reviewer suggests a simpler baseline (monotone aligner + standard sequence tagger); asks how the proposed method differs and why that baseline was omitted.  
- Evaluation restricted to morphology; should include other monotonic seq2seq tasks to demonstrate broader applicability.  
- Claiming results are "on par or better" is vague and may reflect bias; wording problematic.  
- Insufficient detail about linguistic features (e.g., POS): source unclear and may explain performance gains instead of monotonicity.  
- Minor presentation issues: revise Eq.(3) notation, lowercase "where" at line 231, standardize x1…xn notation, remove Cyrillic font in Fig.1, correct pronoun at line 437.  
- After authors’ response, alignments appear to include 1-0, 0-1, 1-1 and many-to-many cases; authors still did not justify not using the simpler neural tagger baseline.  
- Architecture is related to Stack LSTM/action-sequence methods; using greedy alignment may be a limitation versus approaches that sum over alignments (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016).
