- Proposes a novel encoder–decoder with "hard monotonic attention" to explicitly model monotonicity by decoupling alignment and transduction.  
- Paper is well-written and presents a clear core idea of monotone alignment followed by constrained generation.  
- Alignment description is unclear/contradictory: unclear whether alignments are 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many.  
- After author response, alignments involve 1-0, 0-1, 1-1, and many-to-many, but this complexity raises why simpler schemes weren’t used.  
- Missing a simple baseline: monotone aligner (1-to-many) + standard sequence tagger was not included despite being a natural comparison (e.g., Schnober et al. 2016).  
- Evaluation is limited to morphology tasks; broader monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks are needed to demonstrate generality.  
- Claiming results are "on par or better" is misleading—model performs better on some datasets and worse on others.  
- Insufficient information about linguistic features (e.g., POS tags): source, usage, and contribution to performance are unclear.  
- Architecture relates to other approaches (e.g., Stack LSTM) and this connection should be discussed more.  
- Use of greedy alignment is a limitation; model does not sum over all alignments like Rastogi et al. (2016).  
- Minor issues: notation problems (use of "NN" in Eq.3) and inconsistent punctuation in mathematical expressions.  
- Recommendation: clarify alignment formulation, add simpler baselines, report feature details, broaden evaluations, and temper experimental claims.
