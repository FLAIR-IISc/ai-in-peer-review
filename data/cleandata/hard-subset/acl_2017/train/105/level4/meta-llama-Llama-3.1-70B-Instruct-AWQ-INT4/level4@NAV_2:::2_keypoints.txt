- Proposes a neural model with hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection generation.  
- Evaluated on three established datasets and reports state-of-the-art results in various setups.

Main contributions:
- Introduces a hard attention model tailored for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning.  
- Establishes new state-of-the-art performance on three morphological inflection datasets.  
- Provides analysis comparing the hard-attention model to soft-attention, highlighting extracted features.

Strengths:
- Novel encoder-decoder architecture that explicitly encodes monotonicity.  
- Empirical performance matches or outperforms prior neural and non-neural methods.  
- Thorough comparative analysis with soft-attention, clarifying model behaviors.

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation limited to morphology; unclear generalization to other monotonic seq2seq tasks.  
- Lacks clear distinction from a simpler sequence-tagging approach for 1-to-many alignments.  
- Insufficient detail on the linguistic features used and their acquisition.

Questions to authors:
- What alignment types are produced (1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many)?  
- How does this method differ from using a standard sequence tagger for 1-to-many alignments?  
- Why was the simpler sequence-tagger baseline not included?  
- Please provide more details on the linguistic features and how they were obtained.  
- Could those linguistic features, rather than monotonicity constraints, explain some performance gains?
