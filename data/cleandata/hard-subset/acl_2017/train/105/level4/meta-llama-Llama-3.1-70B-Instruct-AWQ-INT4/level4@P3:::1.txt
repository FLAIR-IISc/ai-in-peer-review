**Summary and Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, which employs a hard attention mechanism inspired by the nearly-monotonic alignment commonly found between characters in a word and its inflection. The model is evaluated on three morphological inflection generation datasets and achieves state-of-the-art results in various setups. The authors also analyze and compare the learned representations of the hard attention model with the soft attention model, shedding light on the features extracted by these models for the inflection generation task.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. A hard attention model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning, specifically designed for morphological inflection generation.
2. State-of-the-art results on three previously studied morphological inflection generation datasets, outperforming both neural and non-neural approaches.
3. An analysis of the learned representations of the hard attention model, providing insights into the features extracted by the model for the inflection generation task.

**Strengths**

The paper presents several strengths:

1. **Novel approach**: The hard attention mechanism is a new and substantially different approach from previous state-of-the-art neural models for morphological inflection generation.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The model is evaluated on three datasets, providing a thorough assessment of its performance in various settings.
3. **In-depth analysis**: The authors provide a detailed analysis of the learned representations of the hard attention model, offering insights into its strengths and weaknesses.

**Weaknesses**

The paper also has some weaknesses:

1. **Mixed results**: The experiment results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed, with the proposed model being effective only in certain conditions (e.g., small training data or mostly monotonic alignment).
2. **Limited generalizability**: The model's performance is not consistently better than state-of-the-art approaches across all datasets and conditions, raising concerns about its generalizability.

**General Discussion**

The authors propose a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, which uses "hard attention" and character alignments separately obtained by a Bayesian method for transliteration. The idea is novel and sound, and the paper is clearly written. The experiment is comprehensive, but the proposed method is not necessarily the state of the art in all conditions. The paper would be more convincing if it described the practical merits of the proposed method, such as the ease of implementation and computational cost.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the computational cost and ease of implementation of the proposed method compared to state-of-the-art approaches?
2. How do you plan to address the limited generalizability of the model, and what future work do you propose to improve its performance across different datasets and conditions?
