- Strengths:
The introduction of hard monotonic attention presents a novel and substantially different approach from existing methods.

- Weaknesses:
The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. The proposed model demonstrates effectiveness when the training data is limited (e.g., CELEX) and when the alignment is predominantly monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

- General Discussion:
The authors propose a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, leveraging "hard attention" and character alignments obtained through a Bayesian method for transliteration. This approach diverges significantly from the state-of-the-art neural models, which employ "soft attention" and jointly solve character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework.

The idea is both novel and sound, and the paper is clearly written. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering a range of scenarios. However, a concern remains that the proposed method may not necessarily represent the state-of-the-art in all conditions. Its suitability appears to be limited to tasks characterized by mostly monotonic alignment and less context-sensitive phenomena. To strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to discuss the practical advantages of the proposed method, such as its ease of implementation and computational cost.
