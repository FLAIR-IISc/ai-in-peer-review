- Proposes a novel hard-attention neural model for morphological inflection that leverages nearly-monotonic input–output alignments and reports state-of-the-art results on three datasets.  
- Contribution: introduces a hard-attention model tailored for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning.  
- Contribution: evaluates the model on three established morphological inflection datasets and achieves new state-of-the-art performance.  
- Contribution: analyzes and compares the model to soft-attention variants, offering insights into learned features and alignments.  
- Strength: method is novel and well-motivated by the monotonic nature of inflection tasks.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation with comparisons to several strong baselines.  
- Strength: provides detailed analysis of learned alignments and internal representations.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of how the approach differs from a simple sequence tagger using monotone alignments.  
- Weakness: evaluation is restricted to morphology datasets and does not test other monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons to other monotonic seq2seq models (e.g., Stack LSTM).  
- Question: please clarify how this approach differs from a simple sequence tagger with monotone alignments.  
- Question: why was a simple sequence tagger not included as a baseline?  
- Question: how would the model generalize to other monotonic tasks like abstractive summarization or machine translation?  
- Question: provide details on how linguistic features were obtained and whether they drive the model’s improved performance.  
- Question: does using greedy alignment lose advantages compared to approaches (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016) that sum over all alignments?
