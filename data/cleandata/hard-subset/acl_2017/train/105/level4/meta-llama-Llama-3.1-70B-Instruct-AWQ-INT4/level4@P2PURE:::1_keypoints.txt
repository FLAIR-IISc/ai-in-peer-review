- Strength: Introduction of hard monotonic attention is a novel and significant departure from existing approaches.
- Weakness: Experimental results on morphological inflection generation are inconsistent across conditions.
- Weakness: The proposed model is effective mainly with limited training data (e.g., CELEX).
- Weakness: The model performs well when alignments are predominantly monotonic and less context-dependent (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- General: The paper proposes a novel neural model using hard attention and Bayesian-derived character alignments, differing substantially from soft-attention SOTA methods.
- General: The idea is novel, well-founded, clearly written, and evaluated with comprehensive experiments.
- General: The method may not be state-of-the-art in all settings and is better suited for monotonic, less context-sensitive tasks.
- Recommendation: The paper should discuss practical advantages of the approach, such as ease of implementation and computational cost.
