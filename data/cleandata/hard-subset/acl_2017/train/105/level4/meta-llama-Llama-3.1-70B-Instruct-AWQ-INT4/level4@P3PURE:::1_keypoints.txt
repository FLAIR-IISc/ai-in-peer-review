- Proposes a novel hard monotonic attention mechanism that is substantially different from existing methods.  
- Offers a fresh perspective on morphological inflection generation.  
- Uses hard attention with character alignments obtained via a Bayesian transliteration method instead of joint soft-attention models.  
- Paper is well-written and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive.  
- Experimental results are mixed: strong in low-data settings (e.g., CELEX) and languages with mostly monotonic alignments (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Not consistently state-of-the-art; limited suitability for non-monotonic or highly context-sensitive phenomena.  
- Recommend adding discussion of practical merits, including ease of implementation and computational cost.
