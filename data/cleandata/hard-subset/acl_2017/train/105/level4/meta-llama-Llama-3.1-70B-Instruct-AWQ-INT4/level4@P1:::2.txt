**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, which employs a hard attention mechanism to model the nearly-monotonic alignment between input and output character sequences. The model is evaluated on three morphological inflection generation datasets and achieves state-of-the-art results in various settings compared to previous neural and non-neural approaches.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper presents a hard attention model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning, which is particularly suitable for morphological inflection generation tasks.
2. The model is evaluated on three previously studied morphological inflection generation datasets, establishing a new state of the art on these datasets.
3. The paper performs an analysis and comparison of the hard attention model and the soft-attention model, shedding light on the features extracted by these models for the inflection generation task.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel and effective approach to modeling morphological inflection generation, which explicitly takes into account the monotonicity of the task.
2. The model achieves state-of-the-art results on three morphological inflection generation datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in various settings.
3. The paper provides a thorough analysis and comparison of the hard attention model and the soft-attention model, which helps to understand the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. The model is only evaluated on morphology tasks, and it is unclear whether it can be applied to other monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.
2. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the hard attention mechanism differs from other approaches, such as the simple idea of aligning input and output characters monotonically and then training a standard sequence tagger.
3. The paper does not include a baseline comparison with other monotonic sequence-to-sequence models, such as the Stack LSTM.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the hard attention mechanism is different from other approaches, such as the simple idea of aligning input and output characters monotonically and then training a standard sequence tagger?
2. Why did you not include a baseline comparison with other monotonic sequence-to-sequence models, such as the Stack LSTM?
3. Can you provide more information on the linguistic features used in the model, such as POS tags, and how they are obtained?
4. Do you think that the use of greedy alignment in the model may be a limitation, compared to approaches that sum over all alignments, such as Rastogi et al. (2016)?
