- Summary: Proposes a neural hard-attention model for nearly-monotonic morphological inflection and achieves state-of-the-art results on three existing datasets.

- Contribution: Introduces a hard attention model tailored for nearly-monotonic seq2seq learning common in morphology.

- Contribution: Empirically establishes new state-of-the-art performance on three morphological inflection datasets.

- Contribution: Provides analysis comparing the hard-attention model to soft-attention, highlighting extracted features.

- Strength: Novel encoder–decoder architecture with explicit hard monotonic attention for morphology tasks.

- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation showing SOTA results across multiple datasets and setups.

- Strength: Clear and well-written description of model and experiments.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to morphology tasks, leaving generalizability to other monotonic seq2seq tasks unclear.

- Weakness: Lacks clear explanation of how it differs from a simpler sequence tagger predicting 1-to-many alignments.

- Weakness: Does not discuss limitations of using a greedy alignment versus methods that sum over alignments (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016).

- Question: Provide details on alignment types used—1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many?

- Question: How does this approach differ from a standard sequence tagger for predicting 1-to-many alignments?

- Question: Why was the simpler sequence-tagger baseline not included in the evaluation?

- Question: Can you discuss potential limitations of greedy alignment compared to methods that sum over all alignments?

- Additional comment: Provide a more detailed explanation of the linguistic features used (e.g., POS tags).

- Additional comment: Consider evaluating on other monotonic seq2seq tasks to demonstrate generalizability.

- Additional comment: Expand related-work discussion to include Cohn et al. (2016) and Stack LSTM architectures.
