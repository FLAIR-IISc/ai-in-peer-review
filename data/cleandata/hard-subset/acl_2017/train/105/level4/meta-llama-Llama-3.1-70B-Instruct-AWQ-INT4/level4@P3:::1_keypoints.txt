- Proposes a novel hard-attention neural model for nearly-monotonic morphological inflection generation.
- Uses externally obtained character alignments (Bayesian transliteration method) to guide hard attention.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on three morphological inflection datasets in several setups.
- Provides an analysis comparing learned representations of hard-attention vs. soft-attention models.
- Contribution: a hard-attention architecture tailored for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.
- Contribution: empirical improvements over prior neural and non-neural approaches on multiple datasets.
- Contribution: interpretive analysis offering insights into features learned by the hard-attention model.
- Strength: introduces a novel and substantially different approach from prior neural models.
- Strength: evaluates the model comprehensively across three datasets and varied settings.
- Strength: delivers in-depth representation analysis highlighting model strengths and weaknesses.
- Weakness: mixed experimental results—effective mainly with small training data or mostly monotonic alignments.
- Weakness: limited generalizability—not uniformly better than state-of-the-art across all datasets/conditions.
- General note: paper is novel, sound, and clearly written but lacks discussion of practical merits like implementation ease and computational cost.
- Questions: request for details on computational cost and ease of implementation versus prior methods.
- Questions: ask how authors will address limited generalizability and proposed future work to improve cross-dataset performance.
