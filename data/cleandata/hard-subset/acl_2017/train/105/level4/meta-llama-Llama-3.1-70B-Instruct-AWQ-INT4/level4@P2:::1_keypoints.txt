- Proposes a hard-attention neural model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence morphological inflection.
- Evaluated on three morphological inflection datasets and achieves state-of-the-art results in several setups.
- Introduces a hard monotonic attention mechanism specifically suited for morphological inflection generation.
- Empirically compares the proposed model to prior neural and non-neural approaches on benchmark datasets.
- Analyzes and contrasts the features learned by hard-attention versus soft-attention models for inflection tasks.
- Novel idea: hard monotonic attention substantially differs from previous approaches.
- Paper is clearly written with a comprehensive experimental setup.
- The proposed model outperforms previous methods, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Effectiveness is mixed: best in small-data regimes, mainly monotonic alignments, and less context-sensitive phenomena.
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical merits such as implementation simplicity and computational cost.
- Requests clarification on practical advantages (ease of implementation, computational cost) versus soft-attention.
- Asks how the model could be extended to handle non-monotonic alignments and context-sensitive transformations.
- Seeks more details on the character-level alignment process and its impact on performance.
