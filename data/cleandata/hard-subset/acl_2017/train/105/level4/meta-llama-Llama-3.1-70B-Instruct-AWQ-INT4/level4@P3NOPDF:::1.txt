**Strengths:**

The introduction of hard monotonic attention presents a novel and substantially different approach from existing methods, offering a fresh perspective on the task of morphological inflection generation.

**Weaknesses:**

The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. While the proposed model demonstrates effectiveness with limited training data (e.g., CELEX) and when alignments are primarily monotonic and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish), its performance is not consistently superior across all conditions.

**General Discussion:**

The authors propose a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, leveraging "hard attention" and character alignments obtained through a Bayesian method for transliteration. This approach diverges significantly from the state-of-the-art neural models, which rely on "soft attention" and jointly solve character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework.

The idea is innovative and well-founded, and the paper is clearly written. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering various scenarios and datasets. However, the primary concern is that the proposed method may not necessarily outperform existing approaches under all conditions. The model appears to be particularly suited for tasks involving mostly monotonic alignments and less context-sensitive phenomena.

To further strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to discuss the practical advantages of the proposed method, such as its ease of implementation and computational cost. Providing a more detailed analysis of these aspects would make the paper more convincing and enhance its overall impact.
