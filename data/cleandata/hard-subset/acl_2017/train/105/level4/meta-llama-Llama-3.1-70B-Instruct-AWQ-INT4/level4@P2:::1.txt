**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, employing a hard attention mechanism inspired by the nearly-monotonic alignment commonly found between characters in a word and its inflection. The model is evaluated on three morphological inflection generation datasets and achieves state-of-the-art results in various setups, outperforming previous neural and non-neural approaches.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper presents a hard attention model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning, suitable for morphological inflection generation.
2. The model is evaluated on three previously studied morphological inflection generation datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results in various setups.
3. The paper provides an analysis and comparison of the hard attention model and the soft-attention model, shedding light on the features extracted by these models for the inflection generation task.

**Strengths**

1. The idea of hard monotonic attention is novel and substantially different from previous approaches.
2. The paper is clearly written, and the experiment is comprehensive.
3. The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results in various setups, outperforming previous neural and non-neural approaches.

**Weaknesses**

1. The experiment results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed, with the proposed model being effective only in certain conditions (e.g., small training data, mostly monotonic alignment, and less context-sensitive phenomena).
2. The paper does not provide a thorough discussion on the practical merits of the proposed method, such as ease of implementation and computational cost.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more insights on the practical advantages of the hard attention model, such as its ease of implementation and computational cost, compared to the soft-attention model?
2. How do you plan to extend the proposed model to handle more complex morphological phenomena, such as non-monotonic alignments and context-sensitive transformations?
3. Can you provide more details on the character-level alignment process used in the paper, and how it affects the performance of the proposed model?
