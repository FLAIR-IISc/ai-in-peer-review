**Strengths:**

The concept of "hard attention" is new and significantly different from others.

**Weaknesses:**

The results of the morphological segmentation test are somewhat mixed. The proposed model is effective if the amount of training data is low (e.g., CELEX). It is effective if the alignment is mostly one-way and less context-sensitive (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

**General Discussion:**

The authors propose a new neural model for morphological segmentation that uses "hard attention," where character alignment is obtained separately using a Bayesian method for transcription. This is significantly different from previous state-of-the-art neural models that use "soft attention," where character alignment and transcription are jointly solved in the model.

**Concept:**

The concept is new and reliable. The paper is clearly written. The test is comprehensive. The only concern is that the proposed method may not be state-of-the-art in all situations. It is suitable for tasks with mostly one-way alignment and low context sensitivity. The paper will be more credible if the practical advantages of the proposed method, such as implementation and computational costs, are described.
