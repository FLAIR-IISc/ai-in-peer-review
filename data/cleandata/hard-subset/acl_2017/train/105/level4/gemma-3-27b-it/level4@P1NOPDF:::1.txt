## Review

**Strengths:**

The idea of hard monotonic attention is novel and represents a significant departure from existing approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. The proposed model performs well when training data is limited (as seen with CELEX), and also proves effective for languages with largely monotonic alignment and less context-sensitive morphology (such as Russian, German, and Spanish). 

**General Discussion:**

The authors present a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation that utilizes “hard attention,” leveraging character alignments separately obtained via a Bayesian method for transliteration. This approach is substantially different from previous state-of-the-art neural models for this task, which typically employ “soft attention” and jointly model character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework.

The core idea is both novel and well-reasoned. The paper is clearly written, and the experiments are comprehensive. My primary concern is that the proposed method doesn’t consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions. It appears particularly well-suited to tasks with mostly monotonic alignment and limited contextual dependencies. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of the practical advantages of the proposed method, such as ease of implementation or computational efficiency. Providing this information would make the contribution more compelling.
