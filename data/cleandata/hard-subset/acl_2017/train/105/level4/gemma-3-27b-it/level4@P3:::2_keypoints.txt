- Contribution: Proposes a novel hard monotonic attention model that decouples alignment and transduction using pre-computed alignments.  
- Contribution: Achieves state-of-the-art results on three morphological inflection datasets, notably improving performance on small CELEX data.  
- Strength: Explicitly modeling monotonicity is well-motivated for morphological inflection.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance, especially in low-data settings.  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation across three datasets with baselines and ablation studies.  
- Strength: Analysis comparing learned representations of hard vs. soft attention provides useful insights.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and includes a helpful explanatory diagram.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to morphology; applicability to other monotonic seq2seq tasks is not tested.  
- Weakness: No comparison to a simpler baseline that pre-aligns monotonically and trains a sequence tagger.  
- Weakness: Alignment generation is under-specified and relies on a greedy strategy that may be suboptimal; probabilistic/marginalized alignments were not considered.  
- Weakness: The impact of the input linguistic features is not isolated; feature contribution is unclear.  
- Question: What exact alignment types are produced (1-1, 1-many, many-1, many-many) and how are ambiguities resolved?  
- Question: Why was the pre-align+sequence-tagger baseline omitted, and how would results likely differ?  
- Question: Have you considered probabilistic alignment or summing over multiple alignments instead of greedy alignment?  
- Question: What are the sources of the linguistic features and have you performed ablations to measure their individual effects?  
- Comment: Formatting/notation fixes recommended (Equation 3 NN formatting, lowercase "where", use x_1,…,x_n).  
- Comment: Figure 1’s Cyrillic font hinders readability for some reviewers.  
- Comment: Minor typos (e.g., Line 437 “these”) and relevant references (Schnober et al. 2016, Cohn et al. 2016 / Stack LSTMs) should be considered.
