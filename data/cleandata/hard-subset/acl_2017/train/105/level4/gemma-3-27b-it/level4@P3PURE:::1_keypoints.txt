- Novel idea: hard monotonic attention is novel and substantially different from existing approaches.  
- Method: employs hard attention using character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.  
- Contrast to prior work: differs from prior neural SOTA that use soft attention and jointly model alignment and conversion.  
- Experimental outcome: results are somewhat mixed across datasets and conditions.  
- Low-data strength: performs effectively when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX).  
- Language suitability: effective for languages with largely monotonic alignment and limited context sensitivity (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Presentation: core idea is well-motivated and the paper is clearly written.  
- Evaluation: experimental evaluation is comprehensive.  
- Main concern: does not consistently reach state-of-the-art performance across all settings and is best suited to mostly monotonic, low-context tasks.  
- Recommendation: include discussion of practical advantages (ease of implementation, computational cost) to strengthen the paper.
