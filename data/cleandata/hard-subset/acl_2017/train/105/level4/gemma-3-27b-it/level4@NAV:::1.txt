**Strengths:**
The concept of hard monotonic attention represents a significant departure from existing approaches and offers a novel perspective.

**Weaknesses:**
Experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat inconsistent. The model demonstrates effectiveness when training data is limited (e.g., CELEX) and when alignment is primarily monotonic and exhibits low contextual sensitivity (e.g., Russian, German, and Spanish).

**General Discussion:**
This paper introduces a new neural model for morphological inflection generation employing “hard attention,” leveraging character alignments independently derived through a Bayesian transliteration method. This approach fundamentally differs from prior state-of-the-art neural models utilizing “soft attention,” which jointly model character alignment and conversion within a probabilistic framework. 

The core idea is both novel and well-reasoned. The paper is clearly presented, and the experimental evaluation is thorough. However, the method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all tested conditions, appearing best suited for tasks with largely monotonic alignment and limited contextual dependencies. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of the practical advantages of this approach, such as simplicity of implementation or computational efficiency.
