- Paper is novel, well-written, and motivates enforcing monotonicity for morphology tasks.  
- Method: decouple alignment and transduction to enforce hard monotonic attention, but several points are unclear.  
- Unclear alignment specification: are alignments 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many?  
- Missing baseline: why not use simple monotone 1-to-many alignment plus a standard sequence tagger (e.g., BiLSTM) as a baseline and compare?  
- After author response alignment confusion remains; authors appear to create many-to-many from 1-0,0-1,1-1 but question of using 1-x alignments persists.  
- Limited evaluation: testing only on morphology; broader monotone seq2seq tasks would strengthen claims.  
- Wording issue: phrase “on par or better” is optimistic/misleading and should be corrected.  
- Feature transparency: unclear what linguistic features (e.g., POS) were used, their source, and whether they drive improvements rather than monotonicity.  
- Related work: relation to Cohn et al. (2016) and stack/transition-based models (e.g., Stack LSTM) should be discussed.  
- Greedy alignment concern: does using greedy alignments lose performance compared to methods that sum over alignments (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016)?  
- Minor editorial/math points: rewrite NN in equation (3) as text, lower-case “Where”, use x_1,\ldots,x_n notation, fix typo “these”.  
- Presentation issue: avoid Cyrillic font in Fig.1 to ensure accessibility.
