- Hard monotonic attention mechanism is novel and a significant departure from existing approaches.
- Experimental results are mixed across datasets and settings.
- Model is effective with limited training data (e.g., CELEX).
- Model performs well when alignment is predominantly monotonic with minimal contextual dependencies (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).
- Introduces a neural model using “hard attention” with character alignments derived independently via a Bayesian transliteration method.
- Contrasts with prior state-of-the-art “soft attention” models that jointly model alignment and conversion.
- Core idea is novel and well-justified.
- Paper is clearly written and presents comprehensive experiments.
- Method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions.
- Particularly well-suited for tasks with largely monotonic alignment and low context sensitivity.
- Paper would benefit from discussion of practical advantages (ease of implementation, computational efficiency).
