- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder with "hard monotonic attention", evaluated on three morphology datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, mostly clear, and introduces the novel idea of modeling monotonicity for morphological tasks.  
- Motivation: many seq2seq tasks are monotone, so explicitly enforcing monotonic output generation is sensible; authors decouple alignment and transduction using monotone alignments.  
- Unclear alignment specification: need clarification whether alignments are 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many (conflict between text and Fig.1).  
- Missing obvious baseline: monotone 1-to-many alignment + standard sequence tagger (e.g., BiLSTM) as in Schnober et al. (2016); ask how the proposed method differs and why this baseline was not included.  
- Limited evaluation scope: only tested on morphology; should evaluate other monotonic seq2seq tasks to demonstrate broader advantages.  
- Results wording: phrase “on par or better” is biased/overly optimistic and should be revised for accuracy.  
- Insufficient detail on linguistic features (e.g., POS): unclear source of features and whether feature usage rather than monotonicity drives performance gains.  
- Minor technical/formatting issues: Eq.(3) use proper NN typesetting, lowercase "where" at l.231, use x1,\ldots,xn notation.  
- Figure 1 accessibility: unnecessary use of Cyrillic font hinders readability.  
- Typo: l.437 should be "these".  
- Includes citation recommendation: Schnober et al., COLING 2016.
