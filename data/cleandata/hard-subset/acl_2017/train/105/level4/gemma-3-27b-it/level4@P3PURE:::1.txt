The experimental results on morphological inflection generation are somewhat mixed. The proposed model performs effectively when the amount of training data is limited (e.g., CELEX). It also demonstrates effectiveness for languages exhibiting largely monotonic alignment and limited context sensitivity, such as Russian, German, and Spanish. 

This paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation that employs “hard attention,” leveraging character alignments separately obtained via a Bayesian method for transliteration. This approach is substantially different from previous state-of-the-art neural models for this task, which typically utilize “soft attention” and solve character alignment and conversion jointly within a probabilistic model. 

The core idea is novel and well-motivated. The paper is clearly written and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive. A primary concern is that the proposed method does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art performance across all conditions; it appears particularly well-suited to tasks with predominantly monotonic alignment and minimal context-sensitive phenomena. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of the practical advantages of this method, such as its ease of implementation and computational cost.
