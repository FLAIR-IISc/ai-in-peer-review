- Novelty: Introduces hard monotonic attention, a significant departure from existing soft-attention approaches.  
- Method: Uses hard attention with character alignments obtained separately via a Bayesian transliteration method.  
- Contrast: Differs from prior work that jointly models character alignment and conversion with soft attention.  
- Writing/experiments: Paper is clearly written and presents comprehensive experiments.  
- Strength: Performs well with limited training data (e.g., CELEX).  
- Strength: Effective for languages with largely monotonic alignments and less context-sensitive morphology (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Weakness: Experimental results are mixed and do not consistently achieve state-of-the-art across all conditions.  
- Concern: Method seems best suited to tasks with monotonic alignment and limited contextual dependencies.  
- Suggestion: Paper would be stronger with discussion of practical advantages (ease of implementation, computational efficiency).
