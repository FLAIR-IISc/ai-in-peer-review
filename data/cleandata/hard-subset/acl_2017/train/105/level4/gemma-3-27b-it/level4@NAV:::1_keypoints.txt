- Introduces hard monotonic attention as a novel and significant departure from existing approaches.  
- Employs a new neural model for morphological inflection using hard attention with character alignments from a Bayesian transliteration method.  
- Approach fundamentally differs from prior soft-attention models that jointly learn alignment and conversion.  
- Core idea is novel, well-reasoned, clearly presented, and evaluated thoroughly.  
- Experimental results are inconsistent and the method does not uniformly achieve state-of-the-art performance.  
- Method works best with limited training data and largely monotonic, low-contextual-sensitivity alignments (e.g., CELEX, Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Paper would benefit from discussing practical advantages such as implementation simplicity and computational efficiency.
