- Strength: Introduces a novel encoder-decoder architecture that explicitly enforces monotonicity via "hard monotonic attention."

- Weakness: The model may be conceptually similar to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignments, raising questions about novelty.

- Weakness: Evaluation is confined to morphology tasks, omitting other monotone sequence-to-sequence scenarios that would better demonstrate generality.

- Major clarity issue: The nature of alignments is unclear—whether they are 1-to-many, many-to-1, many-to-many, or a mix.

- Proposed baseline omitted: A simple pipeline (monotone 1-to-many aligner + neural sequence tagger, e.g., BiLSTM) is not compared, despite being a strong, conceptually similar alternative.

- Specific unanswered queries: How the proposed approach differs from the simpler align-then-tag baseline, and why that baseline was not evaluated.

- After author response: Authors clarified alignments involve 1-0, 0-1, 1-1 steps followed by many-to-many, but the reviewer remains unconvinced why the simpler 1-x + tagger baseline wasn't implemented.

- Scope limitation: Focusing only on morphological datasets weakens the claim that explicit monotonicity modeling is broadly beneficial; more monotone tasks should be tested.

- Reporting language: The phrase “on par or better” is potentially biased and should be replaced with a more precise summary of experimental results.

- Feature confound: Description and source of linguistic features (e.g., POS tags) are sparse, raising the possibility that features, not monotonicity constraints, drive improvements.

- Experimental control concern: Need to evaluate whether performance gains stem from monotonicity constraints or from added linguistic features.

- Minor editorial issues: Notational/formatting fixes needed (Equation (3) NN formatting, lowercase "where" at l.231, punctuation for sequences x_1,...,x_n).

- Presentation issue: Figure 1 uses a Cyrillic font that hinders readability and prevents close examination of the example.

- Typo: l.437 should read "these."

- Related work missing/underemphasized: Cohn et al. (2016) and connections to Stack LSTMs and other action-sequence models should be discussed.

- Methodological concern: Does using a greedy alignment approach introduce drawbacks compared to methods (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016) that sum over all possible hard monotonic alignments?
