- Proposes a novel neural model for morphological inflection using hard monotonic attention with Bayesian-derived character alignments.  
- Represents a substantial departure from prior soft-attention, jointly-aligned probabilistic models.  
- Core idea is novel, theoretically sound, and clearly articulated.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, covering multiple languages and datasets.  
- Strong performance in low-resource settings (CELEX) and when source–target alignments are largely monotonic (e.g., Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Weaker or less compelling performance in tasks requiring complex, context-aware (non-monotonic) alignment.  
- Main concern: model does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art across all evaluated conditions.  
- Recommend explicitly discussing limitations due to the monotonic alignment assumption and specifying morphological phenomena where the model excels.  
- Suggest adding practical analyses: ease of implementation, computational cost (training/inference), model complexity, and training efficiency.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted limitations and practical considerations.
