- Strength: Proposes a novel encoder–decoder model that explicitly enforces hard monotonic attention.  
- Weakness: Model may be functionally similar to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignments.  
- Evaluation is limited to morphological tasks, lacking broader assessment on other monotone seq2seq problems.  
- Unclear alignment semantics: paper mixes examples suggesting 1-to-many with text implying many-to-1 alignments; needs explicit clarification.  
- Missing simple monotone baseline: no comparison to a 1-to-many monotone aligner followed by a neural sequence tagger (e.g., BiLSTM).  
- Question 2a: How does the proposed method fundamentally differ from the monotone-align-then-tagger approach?  
- Question 2b: Why wasn’t the monotone-align-then-tagger baseline included in experiments?  
- Need to evaluate on other monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks to substantiate claimed benefits of explicit monotonicity modeling.  
- Wording concern: phrase “on par or better” is biased and should be revised.  
- Insufficient detail on linguistic features (e.g., POS): source and role unclear.  
- Risk that added linguistic features, not monotonicity constraints, explain performance gains.  
- Minor editorial issues: Equation (3) formatting for NN, capitalization of “Where,” variable notation x1…xn, Figure 1 uses Cyrillic font, typo at l.437.  
- After author response: alignment process clarified as starting with 1-0/0-1/1-1 then many-to-many, but still lacks comparison to a direct 1-x monotone baseline.  
- AC notes: relevant prior work (Cohn et al. 2016) should be considered; relate architecture to Stack LSTM/action-sequence methods.  
- Concern about using greedy alignments: authors should discuss drawbacks versus approaches that sum over alignments (e.g., Rastogi et al. 2016).
