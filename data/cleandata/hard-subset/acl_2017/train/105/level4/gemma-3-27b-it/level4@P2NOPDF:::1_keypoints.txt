- Introduces a novel neural model for morphological inflection using hard monotonic attention with Bayesian-derived character alignments.  
- Approach departs from prior soft-attention methods by decoupling alignment (Bayesian) and conversion (neural).  
- Core idea is innovative and well-motivated.  
- Paper is clearly written and presents comprehensive experimental evaluation.  
- Model shows strong effectiveness in low-resource settings (e.g., CELEX) and languages with monotonic, low-context inflection (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Overall results are mixed: it does not consistently outperform existing approaches across all datasets.  
- Authors should better detail practical advantages (ease of implementation, training speed, memory usage).  
- Recommend a more nuanced analysis of the specific scenarios where hard monotonic attention excels and explanations for why.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with insightful identified conditions for success, but needs clearer practical benefits to increase impact.
