- Proposes a neural sequence transducer for morphological inflection enforcing hard monotonic attention via a single forward pointer with STEP and WRITE(c) actions.  
- Uses external character-level alignments converted deterministically to oracle action sequences for cross-entropy training.  
- Model architecture: character biLSTM encoder, recurrent decoder conditioned on last output, and morphological feature embeddings.  
- Main claim: simple hard-monotonic transducer matches or exceeds prior neural and non-neural SOTA across benchmarks, with strong low-resource gains (CELEX).  
- Presents a careful empirical study across datasets and morphological typologies, including alignment visualizations and encoder representation projections.  
- Argues that monotonic inductive bias plus supervised alignments reduces overfitting and improves data efficiency versus soft attention.  
- Strength: well-motivated inductive bias and simple training that avoids expensive marginalization and yields good generalization in low-resource settings.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, including first neural model to surpass Dreyer et al.’s latent WFST on CELEX and competitive/SOTA performance on other datasets.  
- Strength: interpretable attention trajectories and SVD analysis give insight into character identity and position encoding.  
- Strength: practical and reproducible approach, simple to implement, uses standard aligner, and promises code release.  
- Weakness: relies on external alignments and best-derivation training without ablation on alignment quality or comparison to learned alignments.  
- Weakness: strictly forward-only pointer cannot handle non-monotonic phenomena (prefixation, infixation, templatic reordering); limited exploration of extensions.  
- Weakness: comparative fairness concerns—different training budgets/selection for baselines and lack of statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: unclear decoding/search details (greedy vs beam, termination criteria), leaving inference robustness uncertain.  
- Weakness: analyses are mostly qualitative; lacks quantitative metrics (monotonicity, entropy, confusion matrices, per-tag accuracies).  
- Key question: How sensitive is performance to alignment quality, and can the model be fine-tuned without alignments?  
- Key question: What decoding strategy and termination criteria are used, and does beam search help accuracy?  
- Key question: Can the model naturally handle prefixation (WRITE before STEP) and outputs dependent on future input characters?  
- Key question: Were latent-alignment training alternatives (forward-backward, RNN-T-style marginalization) tried to reduce reliance on a single oracle?  
- Key question: For SIGMORPHON results, provide significance tests and per-feature/per-paradigm breakdowns using official metrics.  
- Additional comments: fix reference formatting, clarify hyperparameters, ensembling/model selection, feature-embedding construction, and include ablation studies (no features, no previous-output embedding, no biLSTM).  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with clear idea, strong low-resource gains, and good analysis; recommend acceptance despite alignment reliance and analysis gaps.
