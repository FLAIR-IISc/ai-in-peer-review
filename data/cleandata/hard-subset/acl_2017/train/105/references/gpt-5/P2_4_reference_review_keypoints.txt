- Summary: Proposes a hard monotonic attention encoder–decoder for morphological inflection that uses WRITE/STEP actions from external alignments to enable XE training, showing strong low-resource gains and competitive large-data performance.
- Contribution: Introduces a simple hard monotonic attention architecture trained from induced alignments via explicit action sequences.
- Contribution: Provides empirical evidence that monotonic inductive bias improves generalization in low-resource settings while remaining competitive at scale.
- Contribution: Offers analysis contrasting hard vs soft attention in alignments and encoder representation geometry.
- Strength: Clear task-matched inductive bias with compelling CELEX low-resource gains and learning-curve evidence of reduced overfitting versus soft attention.
- Strength: State-of-the-art or competitive performance on Wiktionary and several sub-tasks, outperforming non-attention and some joint alignment+transduction baselines.
- Strength: Simple, efficient training and decoding by converting alignments to gold action sequences and constraining monotonic pointer movement.
- Strength: Analysis shows linguistically plausible operations and different positional encoding between hard and soft attention, supporting the design rationale.
- Strength: Method is broadly relevant to monotonic string transduction tasks and is easy to implement and reason about.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on external alignments (Sudoh et al. CRP aligner) with no sensitivity analysis to alignment quality or comparisons to other aligners.
- Weakness: Uses a best-derivation approximation without ablation or investigation of errors from not marginalizing over derivations.
- Weakness: Limited baseline comparisons — omits monotonic or location-aware attention variants and pointer/transition-based baselines trained on same alignments.
- Weakness: Mixed SIGMORPHON results on typologically diverse languages without targeted error analysis to substantiate typological explanations.
- Weakness: Some implementation and reproducibility details are scattered or deferred to the supplement; anonymized code link removed.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to alignment source, and have alternative aligners or alignment fine-tuning been tried?
- Question: What decoding strategy is used (greedy vs beam), beam width, and impact on accuracy/latency; any exposure-bias mitigation like scheduled sampling?
- Question: Ablations requested — remove/randomize feature embeddings, remove previous output embedding, and replace hard attention with location-aware soft attention; impacts on CELEX vs SIGMORPHON?
- Question: Can the model perform pure insertions (multiple writes before step), how is termination constrained, and how are prefixing/circumfix cases handled?
- Question: Were reported gains tested for statistical significance, particularly on CELEX and Wiktionary averages?
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-motivated paper with clear low-resource benefits and thoughtful analysis, but needs robustness/ablation studies and broader baseline comparisons; reviewer leans positive.
