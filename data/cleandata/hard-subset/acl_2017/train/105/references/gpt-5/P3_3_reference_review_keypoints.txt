- Methodological contribution: proposes a simple pointer-controlled hard-monotonic attention transducer trained from external character alignments.
- Empirical contribution: strong results across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016, especially in low-resource settings, outperforming WFST and some neural baselines.
- Analysis contribution: qualitative analyses showing learned alignments are largely monotonic and differences in how hard vs. soft models encode position.

- Strength: excellent low-resource performance owing to inductive bias from monotonic alignments.
- Strength: simple, interpretable architecture and straightforward cross-entropy training using external alignments.
- Strength: broad empirical coverage with competitive or state-of-the-art accuracy on multiple benchmarks.
- Strength: insightful alignment and representation analyses suggesting hard attention relies less on positional encoding.
- Strength: clear positioning relative to prior work (soft attention, WFSTs, RNN-T/neural transducers).

- Weakness: training maximizes a best-derivation approximation and depends on external alignments, risking bias when alignments are noisy or multiple derivations exist.
- Weakness: strictly forward pointer cannot handle non-monotonic or long-distance morphology (harmony, templatic, circumfixing) well.
- Weakness: experimental comparability concerns—different training regimes/hyperparameters and unclear preprocessing/splits limit fairness of comparisons.
- Weakness: limited evaluation metrics (only exact match); lacks edit-distance, per-feature, or typology-conditioned error analysis.
- Weakness: decoding/search and efficiency details (beam/greedy, runtime, memory) are not reported.

- Question: sensitivity to alignment quality—ablation with different aligners or alignment noise, and comparisons to joint/alignment-learning variants?
- Question: what decoding strategy is used (greedy vs. beam), and does beam search help; typical action-sequence length vs. output length?
- Question: can you report edit distance, per-feature accuracies, and a typology-aware error analysis?
- Question: were hard and soft attention models trained under identical sizes, dropout, early stopping, and ensembling for fair comparison?
- Question: what are runtime/memory footprints at train/inference and observed speedups from hard attention?
- Question: were simple extensions for non-monotonicity (limited backward moves, COPY action) tried and with what effect?

- Additional comment: include quantitative probing (linear classifiers) of encoder states to strengthen representation claims.
- Additional comment: add learning-curve studies on Wiktionary subsamples to generalize low-resource findings beyond CELEX.
- Additional comment: formally specify the mapping from many-to-one/one-to-many alignments to step/write actions for reproducibility.
- Additional comment: release code and alignment scripts and clarify data splits to aid future comparisons.

- Overall: a well-motivated, practically useful hard-attention transducer with strong low-resource gains; needs robustness checks to alignment quality, tighter experimental controls, and expanded analyses to strengthen the paper.
