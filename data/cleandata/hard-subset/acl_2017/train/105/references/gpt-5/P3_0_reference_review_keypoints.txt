- Proposes a seq2seq model with hard monotonic attention and an explicit WRITE/STEP control mechanism using a single left-to-right attention pointer.  
- Training relies on externally computed character alignments (Sudoh et al., 2013) to derive oracle action sequences optimized with cross-entropy.  
- Evaluated broadly on CELEX (low-resource), Wiktionary (high-resource), and SIGMORPHON 2016 (10 languages) with typology-aware analysis.  

- Strong low-resource performance: new SOTA on CELEX and improved dev generalization versus soft attention.  
- Conceptually simple and easy to debug: eliminates joint alignment marginalization by reducing learning to supervised action prediction.  
- Empirical breadth and interpretation: links performance differences to morphological typology (suffixing/stem-change vs harmony/templatic).  
- Meaningful qualitative analyses: compares learned alignments and encoder representations between hard and soft attention.  
- Efficient decoding implication: linear-time decoding with a single pointer and no soft-attention computation.  

- Heavy reliance on external alignments without ablations, risking bias from misalignments and domain sensitivity.  
- Objective mismatch: optimizes a best-derivation approximation (no marginalization) and does not quantify its effect.  
- Decoding/search details under-specified (greedy vs beam, beam widths, tie-breaking), reducing reproducibility and possibly masking search errors.  
- Limited handling of non-monotonic phenomena (harmony/templatic systems); no attempted mitigations like bounded backward moves or chunkwise monotonicity.  
- Comparisons may be unfair: differing training budgets/epochs across baselines and no statistical significance testing reported.  
- Missing implementation/training details in the main text (optimizer, regularization, feature sizes, per-type vs joint training) and lack of ablations for morpho-syntactic embeddings.  

- How was decoding performed (greedy or beam), what beam sizes were used, and were search errors observed for multiple-derivation cases?  
- How sensitive are results to external alignment quality; were alternative aligners or noise injections tried?  
- What is the impact of removing external alignments (latent/REINFORCE-based training) or using learned/softened alignments, and of morpho-syntactic embeddings vs control mechanism?  
- Were experiments run to allow bounded backward moves or chunkwise monotonicity to address harmony/templatic languages, and do errors suggest such fixes?  
- Can you report training-budget parity across baselines (epochs, early stopping, hyperparameter search) and provide significance tests for improvements?  

- Overall: Solid, simple, and empirically strong contribution—especially in low-resource settings—but needs alignment-sensitivity ablations, clearer decoding details, and reproducibility/fairness clarifications; leaning toward acceptance.
