- Proposes a neural morphological inflection model with hard monotonic attention that either writes an output symbol or advances the input pointer, claiming SOTA especially on small datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON 2016).  
- Contribution: novel approach yielding strong empirical gains on small-data settings, outperforming prior WFST and neural baselines on CELEX.  
- Contribution: insightful analysis contrasting hard vs. soft attention using SVD visualizations to explain positional encoding differences and generalization.  
- Contribution: robust, competitive performance across three diverse morphological datasets.  
- Strength: significant improvement in low-resource CELEX setting, surpassing non-neural LAT and neural baselines.  
- Strength: strong, informative analysis of learned representations that explains model behavior beyond accuracy metrics.  
- Strength: clear, reproducible evaluation across multiple datasets demonstrating robustness.  
- Strength: straightforward training using cross-entropy on precomputed alignments and provided implementation/code.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—monotonic alignment relates closely to prior work (Graves 2012; Yu et al. 2016) and the distinction (precomputed alignments) is not fully justified.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure cases (e.g., languages with vowel/consonant harmony) and lack of concrete examples.  
- Weakness: missing ablation studies to quantify the contribution of components (bi-directional encoder, control/step operations).  
- Weakness: reliance on precomputed alignments (Sudoh et al., 2013) undermines end-to-end claims and lacks analysis of alignment errors’ impact.  
- Question: How does performance compare to Dreyer et al. (2008) when using the same feature set rather than the morpho-syntactic features used here?  
- Question: Provide specific failure examples for vowel-harmony languages and explain why monotonic alignment fails and how the model could be extended.  
- Question: Give examples showing how the bi-directional encoder handles non-monotonic alignments and relate this to the claim of reduced positional sensitivity.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with valuable empirical and analytical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty, failure-case analysis, and ablation studies.
