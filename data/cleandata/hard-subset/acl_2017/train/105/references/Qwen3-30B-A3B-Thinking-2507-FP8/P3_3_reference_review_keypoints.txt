- Proposes a hard monotonic attention model (bi-RNN encoder, decoder that writes or advances pointer) that outperforms prior models on small datasets and performs well on larger ones.

- Contribution: demonstrates that simple hard attention with explicit monotonic alignments can outperform soft attention on low-resource morphological inflection.

- Contribution: provides detailed analysis of learned alignments and representations, showing different positional encoding between hard and soft attention.

- Contribution: claims new state-of-the-art on three datasets and the first neural model to beat Dreyer et al. (2008) on CELEX.

- Strength: practically important for low-resource, morphologically rich languages—neural model beats WFST on CELEX with only ~500 examples.

- Strength: clear methodology and insightful comparative analysis (alignment and representation visualizations).

- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016 with consistent results.

- Weakness: limited novelty—approach closely resembles RNN Transducer/Neural Transducer and lacks a compelling distinction or justification for independent alignments.

- Weakness: overstates the “first neural model” claim without clear comparison to Rastogi et al. (2016) on equivalent data.

- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why hard attention generalizes better on small datasets; soft-attention overfitting claim is under-supported.

- Question: How specifically does this model differ from the RNN Transducer (Graves, 2012) and which architectural elements are critical for gains?

- Question: Does the model outperform Rastogi et al. (2016) on CELEX with the same training data; clarify data requirements and comparisons.

- Question: Given weaker positional encoding in hard attention, how does the model handle non-monotonic morphology and is this a limitation?

- Overall: Valuable empirical contribution for low-resource inflection generation with strong results, but needs clearer positioning against prior transducer work and deeper analysis to solidify claims.
