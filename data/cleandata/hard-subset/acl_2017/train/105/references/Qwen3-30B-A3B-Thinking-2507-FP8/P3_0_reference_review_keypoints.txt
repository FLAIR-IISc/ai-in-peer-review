- Proposal of a neural model with hard monotonic attention that explicitly models nearly monotonic character alignments via step/write actions.
- Demonstration that this simple hard-attention model outperforms prior neural models on the small CELEX dataset (first neural model reported to surpass Dreyer et al. (2008) WFST on CELEX).
- Analysis showing the hard-attention model encodes character identity more distinctly and position information differently than soft-attention models.
- Robust empirical performance across multiple datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON2016).

- Significant improvement on small-data settings (CELEX), achieving statistically better exact-match accuracy than prior models.
- Thorough representation analysis (using SVD) that provides theoretical insight into what hard vs soft attention models learn.
- Clear qualitative evidence that learned alignments are linguistically meaningful and capture monotonic morphological phenomena.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation with comparisons across datasets and language types.

- Insufficient distinction of novelty from prior work (e.g., Graves 2012, Yu et al. 2016); the main novelty (precomputed alignments vs joint learning) is not clearly argued.
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of components (bi-directional encoder, hard vs soft attention, alignment method).
- Overstated claim of being “first to surpass” Dreyer et al. without clear comparison to Rastogi et al. (2016) and data-efficiency justification.
- Limited analysis of failure cases, especially where the model underperforms (e.g., languages with vowel/consonant harmony).

- Please clarify exact data requirements and why your model surpasses Dreyer et al. with less data compared to Rastogi et al. (2016).
- Provide specific failure-case examples (e.g., Arabic, Maltese) and explain why hard attention struggles on those languages.
- Explain scalability when character-level alignments are hard to obtain and estimate the computational cost of the alignment preprocessing.
- Clarify how the decoder’s feedback differs from standard encoder-decoder architectures with attention.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address novelty clarification, ablations, and failure-case analysis.
