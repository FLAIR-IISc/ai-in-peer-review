- Summary: Proposes a neural seq2seq model with hard monotonic attention that either writes a symbol or advances the input pointer; evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016 datasets.

- Contribution: Introduces a hard monotonic attention mechanism for modeling nearly monotonic character alignments in morphological inflection.

- Contribution: Achieves state-of-the-art results on the small-data CELEX benchmark, surpassing a prior latent-variable WFST model.

- Contribution: Provides analysis comparing hard and soft attention, showing similar alignments but different positional encoding behaviors.

- Strength: Strong empirical gains in low-resource settings, notably on CELEX (≈500 examples per type).

- Strength: Insightful analysis of learned alignments and representations (including SVD visualizations) that goes beyond accuracy metrics.

- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful visualizations and justified design choices.

- Weakness: Core mechanism has limited novelty relative to prior explicit-alignment work (e.g., Graves' RNN Transducer, Yu et al. 2016); differences not fully differentiated.

- Weakness: Insufficient explanation of why the monotonic constraint yields better data efficiency and generalization on small datasets.

- Weakness: Incomplete comparison to more recent neural seq2seq approaches and improvements addressing small-data performance.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of failure modes and why the model underperforms on certain languages (e.g., those exhibiting vowel harmony).

- Question: How does the hard monotonic attention compare directly to RNN Transducer variants in performance and training efficiency?

- Question: Can authors elaborate whether improved generalization stems specifically from the monotonic alignment constraint versus other regularization methods?

- Question: Is the poorer performance on complex morphological phenomena (e.g., vowel harmony) a fundamental limitation of monotonicity, or can the model be adapted to handle it?

- Overall: Strong empirical and analytical contribution, particularly for small-data morphological inflection; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty comparisons and the data-efficiency explanation.
