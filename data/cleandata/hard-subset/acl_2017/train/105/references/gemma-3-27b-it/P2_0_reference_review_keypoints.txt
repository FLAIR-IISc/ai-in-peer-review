- Proposes using hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection generation, enforcing near-monotonic input-output alignments.  
- Shows strong empirical results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, achieving SOTA overall and especially strong on low-resource CELEX.  
- Main contribution: effective application of hard attention to inflection generation where monotonicity is a strong prior.  
- Demonstrates data efficiency and superior low-resource performance compared to prior neural methods.  
- Provides comparative analysis against soft attention, including ablations and representation inspection.  
- Clear motivation and problem definition, especially for morphologically rich languages.  
- Well-explained model architecture and justification for decoupling alignment from training.  
- Insightful analysis (ablation study, learned representations) that helps explain model behavior.  
- Mentions code availability, aiding reproducibility (though link is a placeholder).  
- Limited novelty: core components (RNNs, attention) are standard; contribution is primarily an application rather than a new architecture.  
- SIGMORPHON results show inconsistencies: soft attention outperforms on some languages; current linguistic-typology explanation is insufficiently analyzed.  
- Key training hyperparameters are omitted from the main text, hindering assessment of robustness and sensitivity.  
- Insufficient discussion of the quality and impact of pre-computed character-level alignments and potential negative effects of noisy alignments.  
- Requests: more detailed language-wise SIGMORPHON analysis and tuning attempts; alignment accuracy and handling of noise; reporting of training hyperparameters; and clarification of computational cost of pre-computing alignments.  
- Additional notes: paper is well-written, related work and references are comprehensive, and formatting is good (remove GitHub placeholder).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address analysis, alignment, and training-detail concerns.
