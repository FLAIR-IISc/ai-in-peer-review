## Review for "Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation that leverages a hard monotonic attention mechanism. The core idea is to explicitly model the nearly monotonic alignment between input and output character sequences, common in morphologically rich languages. The authors evaluate their model on three datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016), achieving state-of-the-art results, particularly on smaller datasets where data efficiency is crucial. They also provide an analysis comparing their hard attention model with a soft attention counterpart, offering insights into the learned representations.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Hard Attention for Morphological Inflection:** The successful application of a hard monotonic attention mechanism to morphological inflection is a significant contribution. This provides a strong inductive bias tailored to the task, improving performance, particularly in low-resource scenarios.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates compelling empirical results on standard benchmarks, surpassing previous state-of-the-art models on CELEX and achieving competitive performance on Wiktionary and SIGMORPHON2016. The ability to outperform the Dreyer et al. (2008) latent variable WFST model on CELEX with limited data is particularly noteworthy.
3. **Insightful Analysis:** The comparison with the soft attention model, focusing on the learned alignments and representations, is valuable. It helps understand the strengths of the proposed approach and provides a deeper understanding of how these models tackle the task.



**Strengths:**

1. **Addresses a Key Limitation of Neural Models:** The paper directly tackles the data hunger issue of standard sequence-to-sequence models, a significant hurdle for morphological inflection in many languages where labeled data is scarce. The hard attention mechanism demonstrably improves performance in low-resource settings, a crucial practical benefit.
2. **Sound Methodology & Clear Presentation:** The model architecture is well-defined, and the training procedure is clearly explained. The use of pre-trained alignments to guide training is a clever design choice, simplifying the learning process and enforcing a reasonable inductive bias. The presentation is generally clear and accessible, with helpful diagrams.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The evaluation across three diverse datasets provides strong evidence for the generalizability of the proposed approach.  The choice of datasets covering different languages and morphological complexities is appropriate. 
4. **Novelty and Relevance:** While attention mechanisms are well-established, the specific application of *hard* monotonic attention to morphological inflection, combined with the pre-alignment strategy, is a novel and relevant contribution. The problem itself – morphological inflection – remains important for downstream NLP tasks, especially in morphologically rich languages.
5. **Thorough Analysis:**  The analysis of the learned representations and comparison to the soft attention model demonstrate a good understanding of the model’s behavior and offer valuable insights for future research. The visualizations are particularly helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:** While the paper reports strong overall performance, a more detailed error analysis would be beneficial.  What types of inflection errors does the model still struggle with? Are there specific morphological patterns that are particularly challenging? This would help identify areas for future improvement.
2. **Dependence on Pre-Alignment:**  The reliance on pre-computed character-level alignments, while simplifying training, introduces a dependency on the quality of those alignments. The paper mentions using Sudoh et al. (2013) but doesn't fully discuss the potential impact of alignment errors on the final performance. How robust is the model to noisy alignments?
3. **SIGMORPHON Results Not Dominant:** While the results on SIGMORPHON are competitive, they don’t definitively surpass the soft attention baselines across all languages. The authors attribute this to linguistic typology, but a more detailed breakdown of performance by language family or morphological type would provide a more nuanced understanding.
4. **Lack of Ablation Studies:**  The paper could be strengthened by ablation studies examining the contribution of different components of the model. For instance, what is the impact of the bi-directional encoder vs. a unidirectional encoder? What is the effect of the feedback connection in the decoder?




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the potential impact of errors in the pre-computed character-level alignments on the performance of the model? Have you experimented with different alignment methods or error mitigation techniques?
2.  Would you be willing to share a more detailed error analysis, identifying common types of inflection errors made by the model?
3.  Could you provide a more granular breakdown of the SIGMORPHON results, perhaps by language family or morphological type, to support the claim that the model performs better on suffixing languages?
4.  Have you considered exploring other methods for incorporating morphological information into the model, beyond the pre-alignment step, such as explicit feature embeddings?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
*   The inclusion of implementation details in the supplementary material is appreciated.
*   Consider including a discussion of computational complexity. How does the training and inference time of the proposed model compare to other approaches?




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of morphological inflection generation. The proposed hard attention mechanism is a promising approach for improving performance in low-resource settings, and the empirical results are compelling. While there are some areas for improvement, the paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and provides insightful analysis.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
