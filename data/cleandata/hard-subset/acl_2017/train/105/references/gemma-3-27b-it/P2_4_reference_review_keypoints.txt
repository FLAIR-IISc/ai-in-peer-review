- Summary: Proposes a neural seq2seq model with hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection, achieving state-of-the-art results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016 and analyzing differences from soft attention.  
- Main contribution 1: Demonstrates the effectiveness of a hard monotonic attention mechanism that enforces forward-only alignments suited to morphological processes.  
- Main contribution 2: Shows strong empirical performance, especially in low-resource settings, outperforming prior methods on several benchmarks.  
- Main contribution 3: Provides analysis comparing hard vs. soft attention, including SVD visualizations, to explain learned representations and benefits of hard attention.  
- Strength 1: Directly addresses morphological inflection challenges, especially for morphologically rich and low-resource languages.  
- Strength 2: Presents a clear, well-motivated model design aligned with linguistic sequence properties.  
- Strength 3: Offers comprehensive evaluation across three diverse datasets and strong baselines.  
- Strength 4: Particularly strong results on the low-resource CELEX dataset demonstrate effective learning from limited data.  
- Strength 5: Analysis of learned representations (e.g., SVD plots) gives valuable insight beyond performance metrics.  
- Weakness 1: Core components (RNN encoder-decoder, attention) are established; novelty is mainly in application/adaptation rather than new algorithms.  
- Weakness 2: Relies on pre-computed character-level alignments, introducing an external dependency that may limit generality.  
- Weakness 3: Discussion of why soft attention fails in low-data regimes could be deeper (e.g., diffuseness of attention weights, loss of sequentiality).  
- Weakness 4: Paper presentation is somewhat long and could be more concise, especially background and related work.  
- Question 1: How sensitive is performance to the quality of pre-computed character-level alignments and different alignment methods?  
- Question 2: Have you tried end-to-end training that learns alignments jointly, and how does it compare?  
- Question 3: Will the pre-computed alignment approach generalize to languages with very different morphology or where alignments are hard to obtain?  
- Question 4: Can you provide an ablation quantifying the hard attention contribution versus soft attention within the same architecture?  
- Additional comment 1: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Additional comment 2: Figure 1’s visualization of the hard attention mechanism is helpful.  
- Additional comment 3: The anonymized source code link is appreciated.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution with compelling results and analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
