## Review for "Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a novel neural network architecture for morphological inflection generation. The core idea is to utilize a "hard monotonic attention" mechanism, enforcing a nearly-monotonic alignment between the input and output character sequences. This contrasts with standard soft attention mechanisms. The authors demonstrate strong performance on three benchmark datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON2016), achieving state-of-the-art results, particularly on the low-resource CELEX dataset, and provide an analysis comparing their model to soft attention approaches.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Hard Attention for Morphological Inflection:** The primary contribution is demonstrating the effectiveness of a hard monotonic attention mechanism for this specific task. While hard attention isn’t novel in itself, its application and success within the context of morphological inflection, where monotonic alignment is a strong prior, is a valuable finding.
2. **Strong Performance on Low-Resource Data:** The model demonstrably outperforms existing methods, including other neural approaches, on the extremely limited CELEX dataset. This suggests a significant advantage in data efficiency.
3. **Detailed Analysis:** The paper provides a solid comparative analysis against soft attention models, shedding light on the learned representations and highlighting the benefits of the hard attention approach, particularly related to explicit positional encoding.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the importance of morphological inflection generation, especially for morphologically rich languages, and the limitations of existing methods, particularly with limited data.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The results across the three datasets are compelling. Achieving SOTA on SIGMORPHON and significantly outperforming others on CELEX provides strong evidence of the model’s effectiveness. The consistent performance across different datasets also suggests good generalizability.
3. **Well-Designed Model & Justification:** The architecture is clearly explained, and the choice of hard monotonic attention is well-justified given the inherent structure of the task. The reasoning behind decoupling alignment from the training process is also sound.
4. **Insightful Analysis:** The ablation study and analysis of learned representations (Figures 4) provide valuable insights into *why* the model performs well, beyond simply showing *that* it performs well. This is a strong point, helping the community understand the mechanisms at play.
5. **Reproducibility:**  Mentioning source code availability (even with a placeholder) is a plus for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the *combination* of techniques is effective, the individual components (RNN encoders, decoders, attention mechanisms) are not novel.  The core contribution hinges on the application of hard attention to this specific problem. The paper would benefit from more clearly framing this as an *application* of a known technique, rather than a completely new architectural innovation.
2. **SIGMORPHON Performance Nuances:** The SIGMORPHON results are good overall, but the authors acknowledge being outperformed by their own soft attention implementation on some languages.  This discrepancy warrants further investigation and discussion. While the linguistic typology explanation is reasonable, it feels a bit *post-hoc* and lacks deeper analysis. A more systematic exploration of language-specific characteristics and model performance would be beneficial.
3. **Training Details:** While the paper mentions implementation details in the supplementary material, key training hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size, regularization) should be mentioned in the main body.  This makes it harder to assess the robustness and sensitivity of the results.
4. **Alignment Quality Discussion:**  The paper discusses using independently learned alignments. While this simplifies training, a discussion of the *quality* of these initial alignments and their potential impact on final performance would be valuable. Are there cases where incorrect initial alignments hurt performance?



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Regarding the SIGMORPHON results: Could you provide a more detailed analysis of *which* languages your hard attention model struggled with, and why you believe the language typology explanation is sufficient?  Did you try any specific adjustments or hyperparameter tuning to improve performance on these languages?
2. Can you elaborate on the quality of the character-level alignments used during training? What is the accuracy of the alignment model, and how does the model handle potentially noisy or inaccurate alignments?
3. Could you provide the key training hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size, regularization, optimizer) used for all experiments in the main body of the paper?
4.  While you highlight the simplicity of the training procedure, what are the computational costs associated with pre-computing the alignments? Does this pre-processing step introduce a significant bottleneck?




**Additional Comments:**

* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
* The related work section is comprehensive.
* The reference list is extensive.
*  The formatting is good (though the placeholder for the GitHub link should be removed before final submission).



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of morphological inflection generation. The proposed hard attention mechanism proves to be effective, particularly in low-resource settings. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above – specifically, providing more detailed analysis of the SIGMORPHON results, elaborating on the alignment process, and clarifying training details – will further strengthen the paper. The strengths of the work, particularly the strong empirical results and insightful analysis, outweigh the relatively minor weaknesses.
