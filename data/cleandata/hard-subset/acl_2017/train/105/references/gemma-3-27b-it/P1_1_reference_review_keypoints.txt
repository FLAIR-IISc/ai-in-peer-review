- Proposes a hard monotonic attention neural architecture for morphological inflection that explicitly controls when to write or advance an input pointer.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results, outperforming prior methods (including WFSTs) on CELEX and showing competitive SOTA on Wiktionary and SIGMORPHON2016, especially in low-resource settings.  
- Provides a comparative analysis between hard and soft attention, with visualizations and discussion of learned alignments and representations.  
- Strength: effective in low-resource scenarios, leveraging near-monotonic input-output alignments.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated design grounded in morphological characteristics.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across diverse datasets (small, large, multilingual).  
- Strength: detailed analysis of learned alignments and representations adds interpretability.  
- Strength: claims reproducibility through code availability.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—combines existing components (RNNs, hard attention) rather than introducing fundamentally new architectures.  
- Weakness: relies on pre-computed character alignments, risking sensitivity to alignment quality and potential propagation of alignment errors.  
- Weakness: not SOTA on SIGMORPHON2016; lacks deeper analysis of failures on complex morphological phenomena.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion or quantification of computational cost compared to soft attention (training/inference time).  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the quality of pre-computed alignments and have alignment-noise experiments been run?  
- Question: request examples and analysis of errors for languages with complex morphology and suggestions for addressing long-range dependencies.  
- Question: ask for a rough training/inference time comparison between hard and soft attention models.  
- Question: request ablation studies isolating contributions of the bi-directional encoder, hard attention, and decoder feedback.  
- Additional: paper is well-written with helpful figures; references comprehensive; minor equation numbering issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses and author questions.
