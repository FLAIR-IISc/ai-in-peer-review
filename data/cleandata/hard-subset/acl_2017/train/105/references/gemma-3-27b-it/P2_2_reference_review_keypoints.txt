- Proposes a neural model using hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection, leveraging the task’s inherent monotonic input-output alignment and achieving strong empirical results.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, with particularly strong gains in low-resource settings.
- Shows that hard attention outperforms prior methods on small datasets and compares favorably to tailored latent-variable FSTs.
- Core contribution: effective application of hard monotonic attention to morphological inflection, exploiting task structure.
- Core contribution: superior performance in low-resource scenarios, addressing data scarcity limitations of prior neural approaches.
- Core contribution: comparative analysis of learned representations and alignments between hard and soft attention models.
- Strength: consistently strong empirical results with clear, well-presented result tables.
- Strength: theoretically motivated justification for hard attention due to monotonic alignments in morphology.
- Strength: clear, thorough presentation of model architecture and experimental setup, aided by helpful visualizations.
- Strength: insightful analysis comparing hard vs. soft attention beyond accuracy numbers, including learning curves.
- Strength: directly addresses the challenge of data scarcity in morphological inflection.
- Weakness: limited novelty in core components—bidirectional RNNs and attention are not new; novelty is mainly in application/combination.
- Weakness: relies on pre-computed character alignments for training, which feels like preprocessing rather than end-to-end learning.
- Weakness: analysis of learned representations is somewhat superficial; lacks mapping of dimensions to linguistic features.
- Weakness: explanation for SIGMORPHON underperformance is hand-wavy and lacks detailed analysis of failure cases.
- Question: Have you tried learning alignments end-to-end rather than using pre-computed alignments, and what were the outcomes?
- Question: Can you provide concrete examples showing how the “step” action encodes and uses positional information?
- Question: Can you analyze specific representation dimensions and link them to linguistic features (gender, number, tense)?
- Question: Please provide specific input-output examples where hard attention fails on SIGMORPHON and explain the failure modes.
- Additional comment: Reference list is extensive.
- Additional comment: Claim that the model is "much simpler" than soft attention needs further justification given overall architectural complexity.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contributions and state-of-the-art results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
