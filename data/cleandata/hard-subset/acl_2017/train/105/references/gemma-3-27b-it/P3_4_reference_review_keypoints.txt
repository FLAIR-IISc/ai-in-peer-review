- Summary: Proposes a neural model using hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection, evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016 with strong results and analysis versus soft attention.
- Contribution 1: Introduces an effective hard monotonic attention mechanism that encodes a strong inductive bias for nearly monotonic input-output character alignments.
- Contribution 2: Shows strong empirical performance, outperforming prior work on CELEX and achieving competitive results on other datasets, especially in low-resource settings.
- Contribution 3: Provides a comparative analysis with soft attention, offering insights into learned alignments and representations.
- Strength 1: Directly addresses data hunger of seq2seq models and improves low-resource performance.
- Strength 2: Methodology and training procedure are clearly described, using pre-trained alignments to guide learning.
- Strength 3: Evaluation is comprehensive across diverse datasets and language types.
- Strength 4: The hard attention + pre-alignment combination is a novel, relevant contribution to morphological inflection.
- Strength 5: Analysis and visualizations of learned representations are thorough and informative.
- Weakness 1: Error analysis is limited; paper lacks detail on specific error types and challenging morphological patterns.
- Weakness 2: Model depends on pre-computed character alignments and does not assess robustness to alignment noise.
- Weakness 3: SIGMORPHON results are competitive but do not consistently beat soft-attention baselines across languages.
- Weakness 4: Missing ablation studies to quantify contributions of components like bi-directional encoder and decoder feedback.
- Question 1: How do errors in pre-computed alignments affect model performance, and were alternative alignment methods tried?
- Question 2: Will the authors provide a detailed error analysis identifying common inflection errors?
- Question 3: Can the SIGMORPHON results be broken down by language family or morphological type to support typology claims?
- Question 4: Have the authors considered other ways to incorporate morphological information (e.g., explicit feature embeddings) beyond pre-alignment?
- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Additional comment 2: Supplementary implementation details are appreciated.
- Additional comment 3: Consider adding discussion of computational complexity and training/inference time comparison.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with a promising hard-attention approach that merits acceptance with minor revisions.
