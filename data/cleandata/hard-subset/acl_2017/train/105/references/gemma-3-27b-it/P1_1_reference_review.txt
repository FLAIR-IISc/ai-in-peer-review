## Peer Review: Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel neural network architecture for morphological inflection generation, utilizing a "hard monotonic attention" mechanism. The core idea is to model the inherent nearly-monotonic alignment between input and output character sequences common in morphologically rich languages. The model explicitly controls attention, deciding at each step whether to write a character or advance the attention pointer, guided by independently learned alignments. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on three datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON2016), particularly highlighting improved performance in low-resource scenarios compared to existing neural methods and even surpassing tailored WFST models on the CELEX dataset.  The paper also includes a detailed analysis comparing the proposed hard attention model to a soft attention baseline, examining learned alignments and representations.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Hard Attention Mechanism for Morphological Inflection:** The core contribution is the demonstrated effectiveness of the proposed hard monotonic attention mechanism in the context of morphological inflection. It provides a compelling alternative to soft attention, especially when data is limited, by explicitly enforcing alignment and reducing the need to learn it jointly with the transduction.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents a comprehensive empirical evaluation demonstrating competitive or state-of-the-art performance across three benchmark datasets, including a notable result of surpassing the latent variable WFST model on CELEX in low-resource settings.
3. **Insightful Analysis of Learned Representations:** The comparative analysis between hard and soft attention provides valuable insights into how each mechanism learns to encode morphological information, contributing to a better understanding of the task.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Performance in Low-Resource Settings:** The ability to outperform existing models, including specialized WFSTs, on the CELEX dataset (which is notoriously small) is a significant strength. This demonstrates the model’s data efficiency and ability to leverage the inherent structure of morphological processes.
2. **Clear and Well-Motivated Design:** The paper provides a clear rationale for the choice of a hard monotonic attention mechanism, grounding it in the characteristics of morphological inflection. The connection to existing sequence transduction approaches is well-explained.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The evaluation on three diverse datasets provides strong evidence of the model’s generalizability. The inclusion of both large (Wiktionary) and small (CELEX) datasets, as well as a multi-lingual task (SIGMORPHON), is commendable.
4. **Detailed Analysis:** The analysis of learned alignments and representations is a valuable addition, providing insights beyond just the quantitative results. The visualizations and discussions are helpful in understanding the model’s behavior.
5. **Reproducibility:**  The authors mention code availability, a crucial factor for the community’s ability to build upon this work.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of the Core Components:** While the *combination* of hard attention and the application to morphological inflection is novel, the individual components (RNN encoders/decoders, hard attention) are well-established.  The paper doesn't introduce groundbreaking architectural innovations. The core novelty relies on the effectiveness of adapting existing techniques to this specific task.
2. **Dependence on Pre-Computed Alignments:** The reliance on independently learned character-level alignments for training, while simplifying the learning process, introduces a potential dependency on the quality of those alignments. The paper doesn’t thoroughly investigate the impact of alignment errors on the model's performance.
3. **SIGMORPHON Performance Not Leading:** While competitive, the model is not the state-of-the-art on SIGMORPHON 2016, and the authors attribute this to the morphological complexity of some languages. Further analysis to understand *why* the model struggles with those languages would strengthen the work. It would be valuable to explore strategies to improve performance on these more complex cases.
4. **Discussion of Computational Cost:** The computational cost of the hard attention mechanism compared to soft attention is not discussed.  While the paper emphasizes simplicity, it would be useful to quantify any potential performance tradeoffs.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Alignment Sensitivity:**  How sensitive is the model’s performance to the quality of the pre-computed alignments? Have you experimented with different alignment models or introduced noise to the alignments to assess robustness?
2. **Languages where Hard Attention Underperforms:** You mention the model struggles with languages exhibiting more complex morphology (e.g., vowel/consonant harmony). Could you elaborate on specific examples of incorrect inflections produced by the model for these languages? What modifications could be explored to address these issues (e.g., incorporating longer-range dependencies)?
3. **Computational Cost Comparison:**  Can you provide a rough comparison of the training and inference time for your hard attention model versus a comparable soft attention model?
4. **Ablation Study:** Have you performed an ablation study to quantify the individual contributions of the bi-directional encoder, the hard attention mechanism, and the recurrent feedback connection in the decoder?

**Additional Comments:**

*  The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
* The references are comprehensive.
*  The visualizations (especially Figure 1) are very helpful for understanding the model architecture.
*  Minor: Some of the equation numbering seems a little off (e.g., equation 2 and its relation to equation 1).



Overall, this is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of morphological inflection generation. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the effective combination of these components, along with the strong empirical results and insightful analysis, make it a worthy submission. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses and questions raised above.
