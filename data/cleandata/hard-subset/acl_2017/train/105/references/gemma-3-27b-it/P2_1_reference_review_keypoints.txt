- Novel neural model using hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection generation, enforcing largely monotonic input-output alignments.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, especially strong in low-resource settings.
- Demonstrates that hard monotonic attention effectively exploits linguistic sequentiality, improving performance and interpretability.
- Empirical gains notable, including surpassing a latent-variable WFST model on CELEX.
- Provides analysis comparing hard vs. soft attention, offering insights into learned representations and trade-offs.
- Uses pre-trained alignments, which aids alignment modeling but creates dependency on alignment quality.
- Limited qualitative error analysis; paper lacks concrete failure examples and detailed discussion of remaining challenges.
- SIGMORPHON results need deeper contextualization, since soft attention outperforms on most languages; more language-specific analysis required.
- Question on sensitivity: how robust is performance to alignment quality and different/noisy alignment methods?
- Question on joint learning: have the authors tried or considered jointly learning alignments and transduction?
- Question on computational cost: how does training/inference cost compare between hard and soft attention?
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 1).
- Strong experimental validation across diverse datasets and solid comparisons to neural and traditional baselines.
- References are comprehensive and code availability is a positive contribution.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address questions and analyses.
