- Proposes a neural model using hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection, leveraging inherent input-output alignments to improve performance, especially in low-resource settings.

Main contributions:
- Applies hard monotonic attention effectively to morphological inflection, exploiting task-specific alignments.
- Achieves strong empirical results and state-of-the-art or competitive performance on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016.
- Provides detailed analysis comparing learned alignments and representations between hard and soft attention models.

Strengths:
- Addresses data scarcity by improving low-resource performance compared to standard seq2seq models.
- Well-motivated design: hard monotonic attention suits the aligned nature of morphological inflection.
- Comprehensive evaluation across three diverse datasets and multiple baselines.
- Clear, thorough presentation with helpful visualizations (e.g., Figure 1).
- Insightful analysis of learned alignments and representation differences.

Weaknesses:
- Hard attention mechanism is not fundamentally novel—its novelty is mainly in this application.
- Reliance on pre-computed character-level alignments limits end-to-end learning and raises sensitivity concerns.
- SIGMORPHON2016 results are mixed; not uniformly superior across languages.
- Lacks ablation studies to isolate the contributions of model components (bi-directional encoder, recurrent decoder, etc.).

Questions to authors:
- How sensitive is performance to errors or noise in the pre-computed alignments, and can this be mitigated?
- What is the computational cost of hard attention versus soft attention in training and inference?
- What are the trade-offs of optimizing the “best-derivation” objective versus full sequence probability?
- Can differences between soft and hard attention representations (Figure 4) be quantified and tested for significance?

Additional comments:
- Reference list is extensive and well-maintained.
- Code availability is a significant positive.
- Related work discussion is comprehensive.

Overall recommendation:
- Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
