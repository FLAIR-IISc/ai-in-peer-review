## Peer Review: Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation. The core idea is to enforce a nearly-monotonic alignment between the input (lemma) and output (inflected form) character sequences using a hard attention mechanism. This contrasts with previous sequence-to-sequence approaches that rely on soft attention and require larger training datasets. The authors demonstrate strong performance on three benchmark datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON2016), achieving state-of-the-art results, particularly on the low-resource CELEX dataset. The paper also includes an analysis comparing the learned alignments and representations of the proposed model with a soft attention baseline.

**Main Contributions (as perceived by reviewer):**

1. **Effective Hard Attention for Morphological Inflection:** The successful application of a hard attention mechanism to morphological inflection generation is a significant contribution. The model demonstrably addresses the data sparsity issues that plague neural sequence-to-sequence models in this task, outperforming previous approaches, particularly in low-resource scenarios.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The comprehensive experiments on three datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results on CELEX and competitive results on Wiktionary and SIGMORPHON, provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
3. **Insightful Analysis:** The comparison of hard and soft attention models, including analysis of learned alignments and representations, provides valuable insights into the behavior of these models and the features they learn for morphological inflection.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addresses a Key Challenge:** Morphological inflection generation in morphologically rich languages is inherently challenging due to data sparsity. This work directly addresses this challenge with a model designed to leverage the strong monotonic tendencies in the task.
2. **Superior Performance on CELEX:** The model's ability to outperform both neural and non-neural baselines on the small CELEX dataset is particularly compelling. This demonstrates its effectiveness when data is limited, a common scenario in many morphologically rich languages.
3. **Well-Designed Model:** The architecture, combining a bi-directional encoder, hard attention, and decoder with a feedback connection, appears well-motivated and effectively captures the relevant characteristics of the task.
4. **Thorough Evaluation:** The experiments are comprehensive, utilizing multiple datasets and comparing against a wide range of baselines. The inclusion of SIGMORPHON2016 provides evaluation across a variety of languages and morphological types.
5. **Clear and Insightful Analysis:** The analysis section provides a good understanding of the model’s behavior and its differences from soft attention models, providing valuable insights for future research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Hard Attention:** While the *application* of hard attention to morphological inflection is novel, hard attention itself is not a new concept. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how this specific implementation of hard attention is tailored to and optimized for the task of morphological inflection. A deeper justification of design choices (e.g., why hard attention over other techniques) would be valuable.
2. **Dependence on Pre-Computed Alignments:** The reliance on pre-computed character-level alignments for training, while simplifying the training process, feels like a potential limitation. It introduces a dependency on the accuracy of the alignment model (Sudoh et al., 2013) and may limit the model’s ability to learn alignments directly from data. A discussion of the potential impact of alignment errors would be beneficial.
3. **SIGMORPHON Results Less Convincing:** While competitive, the results on SIGMORPHON2016 are not as dominant as on CELEX. The authors attribute this to differences in morphological typology. More detailed analysis comparing performance across different language families and morphological types within SIGMORPHON would strengthen this claim.
4. **Lack of Ablation Studies:** While the comparison with soft attention is helpful, the paper would benefit from more ablation studies to understand the contribution of individual components of the model (e.g., the feedback connection in the decoder).

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  The paper mentions that the model solves a different objective than maximizing the sequence probability (Eq 1).  Could you elaborate on the trade-offs involved in optimizing for the "best derivation" (Eq 2) versus the sequence probability, and any potential implications for the model's generalization ability?
2.  How sensitive is the model's performance to the quality of the pre-computed character-level alignments? Have you experimented with different alignment methods or explored ways to mitigate the impact of alignment errors?
3.  You mention that the hard attention mechanism harnesses the monotonic alignments found in the data.  What specific design choices in the hard attention implementation contribute most to this ability, and how do they differ from standard hard attention mechanisms?
4.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the error cases made by the model, particularly on the SIGMORPHON dataset?  Are there specific morphological phenomena that the model consistently struggles with?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized.
*   The provided link to the source code was removed, which is appropriate for anonymization.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the computational cost of the hard attention mechanism compared to soft attention.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of morphological inflection generation. The proposed hard attention model effectively addresses the data sparsity problem and achieves state-of-the-art results on multiple datasets. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
