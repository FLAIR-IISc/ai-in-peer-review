## Peer Review: Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation. The core idea is to leverage a hard monotonic attention mechanism within an encoder-decoder framework, directly modeling the typically aligned relationship between input (lemma) and output (inflected form) character sequences. The authors argue this approach addresses limitations of existing sequence-to-sequence models, particularly their data hunger and tendency to overfit on smaller datasets. They demonstrate strong performance on three benchmark datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016), achieving state-of-the-art results, especially in low-resource settings.  A detailed analysis is provided comparing the learned alignments and representations of the proposed model with those of a soft attention baseline.

**Main Contributions (as perceived by this reviewer):**

1. **Effective Hard Attention Mechanism for Morphological Inflection:**  The successful application of a hard monotonic attention mechanism to morphological inflection is a significant contribution.  The premise of exploiting the inherent alignment in this task to guide attention is sound and demonstrably effective, especially on smaller datasets.
2. **Strong Empirical Results & Competitive Performance:** The paper presents compelling empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive performance on three standard datasets. The particularly strong performance on CELEX, where the model surpasses previous methods, is noteworthy.
3. **Detailed Analysis & Insights:** The analysis comparing the hard and soft attention models, particularly the examination of learned representations, provides valuable insights into how these models approach the task and highlights the advantages of explicitly modeling alignment.



**Strengths:**

1. **Addressing a Key Limitation:** The authors directly tackle the issue of data scarcity in morphological inflection, a known weakness of standard neural sequence-to-sequence models.  The hard attention mechanism demonstrably improves performance in low-resource settings, as evidenced by the CELEX results. This is a substantial practical benefit.
2. **Well-Motivated Design:** The choice of a hard monotonic attention mechanism is well-justified by the characteristics of morphological inflection.  The inherent alignment between input and output makes this a natural fit, and the authors clearly articulate this rationale.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:**  The model is rigorously evaluated on three diverse datasets, ensuring the robustness of the findings.  The comparison with a range of baselines, including both neural and non-neural approaches, provides a strong benchmark.
4. **Clear and Thorough Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly presents the model architecture, training procedure, and experimental results. The inclusion of a visual illustration of the hard attention mechanism (Figure 1) is particularly helpful.
5. **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of learned alignments and representations provides valuable understanding of how the model functions and contrasts with the soft attention approach. This adds significantly to the contribution beyond simply reporting performance numbers.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Hard Attention:** While the *application* of hard attention to morphological inflection is novel, the hard attention mechanism itself isn’t a fundamentally new contribution.  The core idea has been explored in other sequence-to-sequence tasks.  The paper would benefit from a more nuanced discussion of the specific challenges and advantages of applying this technique to morphological inflection compared to other domains.
2. **Dependence on Pre-Computed Alignments:** The reliance on independently learned character-level alignments for training introduces a potential dependency and limits end-to-end learning. While the authors argue this simplifies training and provides accurate alignments, it's a constraint that could be further explored. What is the sensitivity to errors in those initial alignments?
3. **SIGMORPHON Results Not Overwhelming:**  While competitive, the results on SIGMORPHON2016 are not a clear win across all languages. The authors attribute this to language typology, which is reasonable, but it suggests the model may not be universally superior. A deeper dive into the specific cases where it underperforms might be valuable.
4. **Lack of Ablation Studies:** The paper would be strengthened by ablation studies investigating the contribution of different components of the model, such as the bi-directional encoder or the recurrent decoder with feedback connection. This would help to isolate the specific factors driving the improved performance.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the model’s performance to the quality of the pre-computed character-level alignments used during training?  Have you explored strategies to mitigate potential errors or noise in these alignments?
2.  Could you elaborate on the computational cost of the hard attention mechanism compared to soft attention? Is there a significant difference in training or inference time?
3.  You mention the model solves a different objective than maximizing the sequence probability (Equation 1). Could you discuss the potential trade-offs between this “best-derivation” approximation and optimizing for the full sequence probability?
4.  Regarding the analysis of learned representations (Figure 4), could you quantify the differences between the soft and hard attention models? Are the differences statistically significant?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The reference list is extensive and well-maintained.
*   The code availability is a significant positive aspect of this submission.
*   The discussion of related work is comprehensive.




Overall, this is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field of morphological inflection generation. The proposed model is well-motivated, thoroughly evaluated, and provides valuable insights into the task. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper and enhance its impact. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
