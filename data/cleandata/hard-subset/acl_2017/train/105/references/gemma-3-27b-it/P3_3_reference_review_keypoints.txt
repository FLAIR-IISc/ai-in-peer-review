- Presents a hard monotonic attention model for morphological inflection that enforces near-monotonic input-output character alignment using pre-trained character alignments.  
- Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, showing competitive or state-of-the-art performance, especially in low-resource settings.  

Main contributions:
- Strong low-resource performance, notably outperforming baselines on CELEX.  
- Principled application of hard monotonic attention guided by pre-trained alignments to incorporate linguistic priors.  
- Detailed analysis comparing learned alignments and representations with soft-attention approaches.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results across multiple datasets, with compelling gains in low-resource scenarios.  
- Well-motivated approach and reasonable decoupling of alignment learning from model training.  
- Thorough evaluation against comprehensive baselines (neural and non-neural).  
- Insightful visualization and analysis of alignments and representations.  
- Clear, well-written presentation of methods and experiments.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty in the core encoder-decoder architecture; novelty mainly in the control mechanism and alignment integration.  
- Dependence on external pre-trained alignments raises sensitivity concerns to alignment quality.  
- SIGMORPHON result discussion lacks deep analysis of linguistic factors and over-attributes differences to training duration.  
- No qualitative error analysis showing failure cases and causes.  

Questions for authors:
- How sensitive is performance to the quality or noise in pre-trained alignments and have alternative aligners been tested?  
- Can you analyze linguistic features that predict when hard vs. soft attention performs better?  
- Please provide example failure cases and explanations for those errors.  
- How do training and inference computational costs compare to comparable soft-attention models?  

Additional comments:
- Discuss potential applications beyond inflection (e.g., transliteration, code-switching).  
- Include more implementation details and hyperparameters in the supplementary material.  

Overall recommendation:
- Strong paper with valuable contributions and analyses; recommended for acceptance.
