- Paper proposes a hard monotonic attention neural model for morphological inflection generation, outperforming soft-attention seq2seq in low-resource settings.  
- Core contribution: effective application of hard attention to exploit near-monotonic input-output alignments for inflection.  
- Empirical contribution: state-of-the-art on CELEX and competitive results on Wiktionary and SIGMORPHON2016.  
- Analysis contribution: comparison of learned alignments and representations between hard and soft attention models.  
- Strength: directly addresses data sparsity in morphologically rich languages by leveraging monotonicity.  
- Strength: notably superior performance on small CELEX dataset demonstrates low-resource effectiveness.  
- Strength: well-motivated architecture combining bidirectional encoder, hard attention, and decoder feedback.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation across multiple datasets and baselines, including SIGMORPHON2016.  
- Strength: clear, insightful analysis of model behavior and differences from soft attention.  
- Weakness: hard attention itself is not novel; paper needs more justification of design choices tailored to inflection.  
- Weakness: reliance on pre-computed character alignments may limit learning and depends on alignment quality.  
- Weakness: less dominant results on SIGMORPHON2016; more analysis across language families/morphological types needed.  
- Weakness: lacks ablation studies to quantify contributions of individual components (e.g., decoder feedback).  
- Question: clarify trade-offs between optimizing for "best derivation" versus maximizing sequence probability and effects on generalization.  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to alignment quality; were alternative alignment methods or mitigations tried?  
- Question: which specific design choices enable the hard attention to harness monotonic alignments versus standard hard attention?  
- Question: provide detailed error analysis on SIGMORPHON—what morphological phenomena cause failures?  
- Additional comment: paper is well-written and organized.  
- Additional comment: removal of code link is appropriate for anonymization.  
- Additional comment: consider discussing computational cost of hard attention vs soft attention.  
- Overall: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
