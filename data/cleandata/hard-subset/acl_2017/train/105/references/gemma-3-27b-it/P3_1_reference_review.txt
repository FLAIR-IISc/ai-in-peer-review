## Review for "Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation utilizing a hard monotonic attention mechanism. The core idea is to enforce a nearly-monotonic alignment between input and output character sequences, simplifying the sequence-to-sequence learning process and improving performance, particularly in low-resource scenarios. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art results on three benchmark datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON2016) and provide an insightful analysis comparing their model to soft-attention approaches.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Hard Attention for Morphological Inflection:** The successful application of hard monotonic attention to morphological inflection is the most significant contribution. It demonstrates a valuable architectural choice for tasks where inherent alignment structure exists, leading to improved performance and robustness, especially with limited data.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper achieves competitive or state-of-the-art results across multiple datasets, importantly surpassing previous methods on the challenging CELEX dataset, and maintaining strong performance on larger datasets.
3. **Analysis of Learned Representations:**  The analysis comparing hard and soft attention models provides valuable insights into how these models represent morphological information.  This contributes to a better understanding of the learned features and the impact of architectural choices.



**Strengths:**

1. **Addressing a Key Challenge:** The paper directly tackles the data scarcity problem in morphological inflection generation, which is particularly relevant for morphologically rich languages. The hard attention mechanism demonstrably mitigates this issue, providing a practical advantage over data-hungry soft attention models. The results on CELEX specifically highlight this strength.
2. **Well-Motivated Design:** The choice of a hard monotonic attention mechanism is strongly justified by the nature of morphological inflection, where a largely sequential transformation is common. The authors clearly articulate the reasoning behind their architectural decisions.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The experiments are thorough, covering multiple datasets with varying sizes and linguistic characteristics. The comparison to a diverse set of baselines (neural and non-neural) provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed model.
4. **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of learned representations is a valuable addition. Identifying how the hard and soft attention models encode positional information and character relationships enriches our understanding of their behaviour and the task itself.
5. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the model architecture, training procedure, and experimental setup. Figure 1 effectively visualizes the hard attention mechanism.



**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:**  While the *combination* of hard attention and the specific task application is valuable, the individual components (bi-directional RNNs, attention mechanisms) are well-established. The novelty primarily lies in applying and adapting these components to the morphological inflection problem. This isn't necessarily a fatal flaw, but it does somewhat temper the overall impact.
2. **Alignment Pre-processing:** Relying on an independently learned alignment process (Sudoh et al., 2013) as a prerequisite for training introduces an external dependency and potential for error propagation.  The paper acknowledges this as a simplification, but further investigation into end-to-end alignment learning within the model would be beneficial. How sensitive is the model to errors in the pre-computed alignments?
3. **SIGMORPHON Results Nuance:** The results on SIGMORPHON, while competitive, show a slight disadvantage compared to the soft attention baseline for some languages.  The explanation offered (relating to complex morphological phenomena) is reasonable, but a more detailed linguistic analysis of the failure cases would be helpful. Why does the soft attention model do better in those languages?
4. **Ablation Studies Limited:** While the comparison to soft attention is valuable, more targeted ablation studies exploring the impact of *specific* components (e.g., the bi-directional encoder, the feedback connection in the decoder) would further strengthen the analysis and provide deeper insights into the model's behaviour.




**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Sensitivity to Alignment Errors:** How sensitive is the model's performance to errors or inaccuracies in the pre-computed character alignments used during training? Have you investigated the impact of using noisy or imperfect alignments?
2. **End-to-End Alignment:**  Have you explored incorporating an end-to-end alignment learning component into the model, rather than relying on the external alignment process? What challenges did you encounter, and why did you ultimately choose to decouple the alignment and transduction stages?
3. **SIGMORPHON Failure Cases:** Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the specific linguistic features or morphological phenomena in the languages where the soft attention model outperforms your hard attention model? Are there specific types of inflections where the hard attention mechanism struggles?
4. **Computational Cost:**  How does the computational cost (training and inference time) of your model compare to the soft attention baseline? Does the hard attention mechanism offer any significant efficiency advantages?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The references are comprehensive and relevant.
*   The supplementary material is essential for understanding the LSTM implementation details.
*   The code availability is a strong positive aspect.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of morphological inflection generation. The proposed hard attention mechanism is a well-motivated and effective approach, particularly for low-resource scenarios. While the novelty of the individual components may be limited, the combination and application are compelling. The thorough evaluation and insightful analysis further strengthen the work.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
