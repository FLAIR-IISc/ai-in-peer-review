- Presents a neural model for morphological inflection using hard monotonic attention in an encoder-decoder framework.  
- Demonstrates strong, often state-of-the-art results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, especially in low-resource settings.  
- Core contribution: effective low-resource performance, outperforming prior models on the small CELEX dataset.  
- Core contribution: introduction of a hard monotonic attention mechanism tailored to near-monotonic alignments in inflection.  
- Core contribution: thorough analysis comparing learned alignments and representations of hard vs. soft attention.  
- Strength: strong empirical results across multiple datasets, with clear comparisons to prior work.  
- Strength: well-motivated architecture that leverages linguistic insight about monotonicity.  
- Strength: detailed analysis and helpful visualizations (Figures 3 & 4) illuminating model behavior.  
- Strength: clear, well-written paper with thorough technical explanations.  
- Strength: practical—implementation details provided and code released for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—hard attention itself is not entirely new; specifics of this implementation need clearer differentiation.  
- Weakness: reliance on pre-computed alignments (Sudoh et al., 2013) makes performance dependent on alignment quality.  
- Weakness: SIGMORPHON results discussion is shallow; lacks detailed analysis of non-monotonic transformations that cause errors.  
- Weakness: limited qualitative error analysis—more concrete failed examples would clarify failure modes.  
- Question: how does this hard attention differ from other hard attention implementations, and which design choices target morphological inflection?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to the quality of Sudoh et al. alignments and were alternative alignment methods tested?  
- Question: can the authors provide specific SIGMORPHON examples where the model fails on non-suffixing/stem-changing languages?  
- Question: please provide examples of incorrect predictions with explanations of the model’s errors.  
- Additional: reference list is comprehensive and supplementary material is important for LSTM implementation details.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance; addressing weaknesses would further strengthen impact.
