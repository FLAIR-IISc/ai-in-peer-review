- Proposes a novel hard-attention neural model for morphological inflection generation that attends to a single input state and either writes an output symbol or advances the pointer.  
- Uses an encoder–decoder with a dedicated control mechanism enabling near-monotonic alignment behavior.  
- Evaluated on three morphological inflection datasets and reports state-of-the-art results against prior neural and non-neural methods.  
- Main contributions: the hard-attention model, state-of-the-art empirical results, and analysis of learned continuous representations.  
- Strength: novel, task-suited hard-attention approach.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance across datasets.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to morphology; no tests on other monotone sequence-to-sequence tasks.  
- Weakness: unclear what alignment type the model implements (1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many).  
- Weakness: missing baseline comparison to a simple 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger.  
- Question: please clarify the alignment type used.  
- Question: how does this method differ from an align-then-tag approach, and why was that baseline omitted?  
- Question: can the model be evaluated on other monotone string-translation tasks to show broader advantage?
