- Proposes a hard monotonic attention neural model for morphological inflection to directly model near-monotonic character alignments.
- Uses a bi-directional RNN encoder and a decoder with a dedicated control mechanism trained on input-output character pairs.
- Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016 and claims state-of-the-art performance over neural and non-neural baselines.
- Main contribution: novel encoder-decoder architecture employing hard monotonic attention to model input-output alignments.
- Main contribution: reports state-of-the-art results on three morphological inflection datasets.
- Main contribution: analyzes learned alignments and representations and compares them to soft attention models.
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presents the hard monotonic attention idea.
- Strength: approach is novel compared to prior soft-attention or other techniques.
- Strength: empirical results are strong and claim SOTA performance.
- Weakness: model may be equivalent to a simpler BiRNN with decoupled alignments, questioning novelty/efficiency.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to morphological inflection and not other monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to a simple baseline like a 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger.
- Question: unclear what alignment type the model uses (1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many).
- Question: how does the proposed method differ from an align-then-tag baseline and why wasn’t that comparison included?
- Question: why was the simple baseline omitted from evaluation—was it considered?
- Additional comment: minor formatting and notation fixes recommended (consistent NN notation, ellipsis spacing).
