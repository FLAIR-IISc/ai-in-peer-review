- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder with explicit hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection generation.  
- Main contributions: hard-attention model for nearly-monotonic seq2seq, evaluation on morphological inflection, analysis vs. soft-attention.  
- Strength: model design is novel and well-suited to monotonic morphology tasks.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Strength: empirical results on three datasets match or outperform prior neural and non-neural methods.  
- Weakness: model might be equivalent to a simpler BiRNN with decoupled alignments, questioning added complexity.  
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to morphology; broader monotone seq2seq tasks not tested.  
- Weakness: alignment type (1-to-many, many-to-1, many-to-many) is not clearly explained.  
- Weakness: lacks a simple 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger baseline for comparison.  
- Question: clarify the exact alignment type used and rationale for that choice.  
- Question: how does this method differ from an align-then-tag approach, and why was that baseline omitted?  
- Question: will the authors test the model on other monotone seq2seq tasks to show generality?  
- Additional comment: formatting/notation issues (ellipsis spacing, typo at line 437) need fixing.  
- Additional comment: Figure 1’s Cyrillic font is unnecessary and should use standard fonts.  
- Additional comment: provide more detail on linguistic features (e.g., POS) and analyze their contribution.  
- After author response: alignments appear mixed and later combined into many-to-many—needs clearer explanation.  
- After author response: reviewer suggests using 1-x alignments and a neural tagger baseline to measure model benefit.  
- After author response: concern that greedy alignments may lose performance versus methods summing over alignments.
