- Proposes a novel hard monotonic attention model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning in morphological inflection.  
- Evaluates the model on three previously-studied morphological inflection datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results in various setups.  
- Demonstrates strong performance with small training datasets (e.g., CELEX) and competitiveness with dedicated models using few examples.  
- Provides a comparison and analysis between the hard-attention model and soft-attention models, investigating learned features and alignments.  
- Paper is clearly written and presents comprehensive experiments and result analyses.  
- Novelty: hard attention mechanism is substantially different from prior methods and conditions outputs on the entire input sequence.  
- Empirical strength: comprehensive experiments across datasets validate the model’s effectiveness.  
- Limitation: lacks discussion of practical merits such as ease of implementation and computational cost.  
- Weakness: performance degrades when alignments are non-monotonic or highly context-dependent, so not universally best.  
- Weakness: analysis of learned representations and alignments is present but not thorough enough.  
- Weakness: no detailed analysis of computational resources required to train and evaluate the model.  
- Question: request detailed analysis of computational resources needed for training and evaluation.  
- Question: request discussion of implementation complexity, code complexity, and runtime requirements.  
- Question: request a more thorough analysis of the specific features the model extracts for inflection generation.
