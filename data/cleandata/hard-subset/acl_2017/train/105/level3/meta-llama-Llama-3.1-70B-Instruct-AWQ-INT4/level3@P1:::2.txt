This paper proposes a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation, which employs a hard monotonic attention mechanism. The model is designed to directly model the nearly-monotonic alignment between the input and output character sequences, commonly found in morphological inflection generation tasks. The authors claim that their model provides state-of-the-art results on three previously studied morphological inflection generation datasets, outperforming both neural and non-neural approaches.

**Summary of the Paper**

The paper introduces a hard attention model for morphological inflection generation, which uses a bi-directional RNN encoder and a decoder with a dedicated control mechanism. The model is trained on a dataset of input-output pairs, where the input is a word and the output is its inflected form. The model learns to attend to the relevant input characters and generate the corresponding output characters. The authors evaluate their model on three datasets: CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, and show that it outperforms previous state-of-the-art models.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel encoder-decoder architecture**: The authors propose a novel encoder-decoder architecture that uses a hard monotonic attention mechanism to directly model the alignment between the input and output character sequences.
2. **State-of-the-art results**: The authors show that their model provides state-of-the-art results on three previously studied morphological inflection generation datasets.
3. **Analysis of learned alignments and representations**: The authors analyze the alignments and representations learned by their model and compare them to those learned by a soft attention model.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-written and clear presentation**: The paper is well-written and clearly presents the novel idea of using a hard monotonic attention mechanism for morphological inflection generation.
2. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to morphological inflection generation, which is different from previous approaches that use soft attention or other techniques.
3. **State-of-the-art results**: The authors show that their model provides state-of-the-art results on three previously studied datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Model may be equivalent to an ordinary BiRNN with alignments decoupled**: The reviewer suggests that the model may be equivalent to an ordinary BiRNN with alignments decoupled, which could be a simpler and more efficient approach.
2. **Evaluation limited to morphology**: The authors only evaluate their model on morphological inflection generation tasks, and do not test it on other monotone sequence-to-sequence tasks.
3. **Lack of comparison to simple baseline**: The authors do not compare their model to a simple baseline, such as a 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger, which could provide a more accurate assessment of the model's performance.

**Questions to Authors**

1. **What alignment type is used?**: The reviewer is unclear about the type of alignment used in the model, and asks the authors to clarify whether it is 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many.
2. **How does the proposed method differ from the simple align-then-tag approach?**: The reviewer asks the authors to explain how their model differs from a simple 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger baseline, and why this baseline was not included in the evaluation.
3. **Why was the simple baseline omitted?**: The reviewer asks the authors to explain why they did not include a simple baseline in their evaluation, and whether they considered using it as a comparison point.
