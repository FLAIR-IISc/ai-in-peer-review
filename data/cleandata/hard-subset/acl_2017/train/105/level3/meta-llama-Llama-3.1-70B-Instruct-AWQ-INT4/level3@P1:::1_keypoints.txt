Summary: Proposes a hard monotonic attention model for morphological inflection that models near-monotonic character alignments and reports state-of-the-art results on three datasets.

Contribution — Novel approach: Introduces a hard monotonic attention mechanism substantially different from prior methods.

Contribution — Small-data effectiveness: Demonstrates strong performance on small training sets (e.g., CELEX) where other neural models overfit.

Contribution — Robustness: Shows good scalability and performance with large amounts of training data.

Strength — Clear writing: Paper is clearly written with well-presented experiments.

Strength — Comprehensive evaluation: Evaluated thoroughly on three morphological inflection datasets with comparisons to prior work.

Strength — Low-resource suitability: Model is particularly effective for low-resource language settings.

Weakness — Monotonic limitation: Model may not perform well for non-monotonic or highly context-dependent alignments.

Weakness — Missing practical analysis: Paper lacks discussion of ease of implementation and computational cost.

Question — Non-monotonic alignments: How does the model handle non-monotonic cases and are extensions planned?

Question — Computational details: Can authors provide more details on computational cost and implementation ease versus other approaches?

Question — Complex morphology: How does the model handle languages with phenomena like vowel harmony and consonant harmony?
