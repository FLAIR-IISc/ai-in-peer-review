This paper proposes a novel encoder-decoder model with explicit hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection generation. The model is well-written and clearly presented, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on three previously studied datasets. The main contributions of this work are three-fold: (1) proposing a hard attention model for nearly-monotonic sequence to sequence learning, (2) evaluating the model on the task of morphological inflection generation, and (3) performing an analysis and comparison of the proposed model and the soft-attention model.

The strengths of this submission are:

* The authors propose a novel encoder-decoder model with explicit hard monotonic attention, which is well-suited for morphological inflection generation tasks.
* The model is well-written and clearly presented, making it easy to understand and follow.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on three previously studied datasets, showing that it performs on par or better than previous neural and non-neural approaches.

However, there are also some weaknesses to this submission:

* The model may be equivalent to an ordinary BiRNN with alignments decoupled, which could be a simpler and more efficient approach.
* The evaluation is limited to morphology, and it would be beneficial to test the model on other monotone sequence-to-sequence tasks to demonstrate its broader applicability.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the alignment type used in their model, which could be 1-to-many, many-to-1, or many-to-many.
* A simple 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger baseline is not included or compared, which would be a useful addition to the evaluation.

Some questions to the authors:

* Can you clarify what alignment type is used in your model and why you chose this approach?
* How does your proposed method differ from a simple align-then-tag approach, and why was this approach omitted from the evaluation?
* Would you consider testing your model on other monotone sequence-to-sequence tasks to demonstrate its broader applicability?

Additional comments:

* The formatting and notation could be improved in some places, such as using proper ellipsis spacing and fixing the typo at line 437.
* The use of Cyrillic font in Figure 1 is unnecessary and could be changed to a more standard font.
* The authors could provide more details on the linguistic features used in their model, such as POS tags, and analyze whether these features drive the improvements in the model's performance.

After the author response:

* The authors clarify that the alignments appear mixed (1-0, 0-1, 1-1) and are later combined into many-to-many, which needs further clarification.
* The reviewer suggests using 1-x alignments and a neural tagger baseline to assess how much the complex model adds.
* The reviewer also raises a concern about using greedy alignments, which may lose performance compared to methods that sum over alignments.

Overall, this is a well-written and clearly presented paper that proposes a novel encoder-decoder model with explicit hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection generation. While there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement, the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on three previously studied datasets, and the paper is a valuable contribution to the field.
