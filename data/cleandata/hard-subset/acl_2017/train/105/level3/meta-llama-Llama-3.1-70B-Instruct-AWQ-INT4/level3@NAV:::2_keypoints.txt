- Summary: Proposes an encoder-decoder with explicit hard monotonic attention for morphological inflection, using a bi-LSTM encoder, a control write/advance decoder, cross-entropy training, and independent char-level alignments, reporting SOTA on three datasets.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel encoder-decoder model with explicit hard monotonic attention for morphology.

- Contribution: Proposes a simple training procedure guided by independently learned character-level alignments.

- Contribution: Shows state-of-the-art results on three morphological inflection datasets.

- Strength: Presents a novel, well-written model idea tailored to morphology.

- Strength: Model matches or outperforms prior neural and non-neural state-of-the-art.

- Strength: Handles small training sets effectively.

- Strength: Provides a thorough analysis of learned alignments and representations.

- Strength: Model is simple and easy to train.

- Weakness: Model might be equivalent to a standard BiRNN with decoupled alignments.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to morphology; no other monotone seq2seq tasks tested.

- Weakness: Alignment type (1-to-many, many-to-1, many-to-many) is not clearly explained.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to a simple 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger baseline.

- Weakness: Does not clearly distinguish the proposed method from a simple align-then-tag approach.

- Question: What exact alignment type is used in the model?

- Question: How does the method differ from the simple align-then-tag approach?

- Question: Can you compare against a 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM sequence tagger baseline?

- Question: Can the model be evaluated on other monotone sequence-to-sequence tasks?

- Question: Can you provide deeper analysis of learned representations and their relation to inputs?

- Additional comment: Paper formatting and notation need improvement.

- Additional comment: Use more precise result wording (e.g., "significantly better" if supported).

- Additional comment: Clarify the linguistic features used and their relation to inputs.

- Additional comment: Consider using more robust evaluation metrics (e.g., BLEU or METEOR) instead of only exact match accuracy.
