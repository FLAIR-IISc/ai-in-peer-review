- Introduces concept-map-based multi-document summarization (MDS) to create structured concept maps representing key content across related documents.
- Proposes a new crowdsourcing scheme, low-context importance annotation, to assess proposition importance in document clusters.
- Builds and releases a large-scale corpus of concept maps derived using the new annotation scheme, plus a baseline system and evaluation protocol.
- Contribution: novel task formulation offering more structured and interpretable summaries than text-based MDS.
- Contribution: development of an efficient, purportedly reliable low-context importance annotation method for crowdworkers.
- Contribution: creation of a publicly available large-scale benchmark corpus for concept-map-based MDS.
- Strength: novel task and crowdsourcing approach that address challenges in traditional summarization.
- Strength: substantial corpus creation provides a valuable resource for the community.
- Strength: release of a baseline system and evaluation protocol facilitates comparison and future work.
- Weakness: no evaluation on other monotonic seq2seq tasks, limiting claims of generalizability.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on the implementation of the crowdsourcing scheme, raising reliability concerns.
- Weakness: limited analysis of the linguistic features used in the model, reducing clarity on model behavior.
- Questions: how will content selection and relation extraction challenges be addressed in concept-map-based MDS?
- Questions: can the authors provide more implementation details and reliability evidence for low-context importance annotation?
- Questions: what evaluation strategy and metrics will be used to assess and compare concept-map-based MDS systems?
