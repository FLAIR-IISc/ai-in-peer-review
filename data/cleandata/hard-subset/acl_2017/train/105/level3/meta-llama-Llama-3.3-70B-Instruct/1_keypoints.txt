- Proposes concept-map-based multi-document summarization (MDS) to produce concept maps as summaries of document clusters.  
- Introduces "low-context importance annotation," a crowdsourcing scheme to assess proposition importance in clusters.  
- Builds a gold-standard corpus of 30 topics (≈40 documents each) with corresponding human-authored concept maps.  
- Provides a baseline system and an evaluation protocol to support future research on the task.  
- Novel task that meaningfully extends traditional MDS by outputting concept maps rather than linear summaries.  
- Crowdsourcing scheme appears effective at collecting reliable importance annotations.  
- Corpus is high-quality, sizable, and carefully annotated.  
- Baseline and evaluation protocol are useful resources for the community.  
- Evaluation is limited in scale (small number of topics/documents used).  
- Lacks direct comparison to related tasks (e.g., traditional MDS, keyphrase extraction).  
- Paper needs deeper analysis of baseline errors and result types.  
- How will the task/general approach be extended to other domains or genres?  
- Please provide more details on the annotation process and annotation guidelines.  
- How will the authors address evaluation limitations such as limited topic/document coverage?
