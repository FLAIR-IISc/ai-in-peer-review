- Summary: Proposes an encoder-decoder with hard monotonic attention that uses external alignments and a decoder predicting WRITE/STEP actions to generate inflections, reporting SOTA on three datasets, especially in low-resource settings.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a neural sequence-to-sequence architecture enforcing strict monotonicity via an action-based decoder (STEP/WRITE).

- Contribution 2: Empirically demonstrates strong performance in morphological inflection, achieving state-of-the-art results on standard benchmarks.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated and conceptually elegant for largely monotonic tasks, providing a principled inductive bias effective in low-data regimes.

- Strength 2: Clear, well-written presentation that makes the action-decomposition mechanics easy to understand.

- Strength 3: Strong empirical evidence, notably large gains on the small CELEX dataset, outperforming prior neural and non-neural methods.

- Weakness 1: Omits a crucial simple baseline (align-then-tag with 1-to-many alignment + BiLSTM tagger), leaving it unclear if the complex decoder is necessary.

- Weakness 2: Limited evaluation scope (only morphological inflection); generalizability to other monotonic tasks like transliteration or G2P is not shown.

- Weakness 3: Methodological ambiguities hurt reproducibility and interpretation (unspecified alignment types, unclear contribution of linguistic features, and imprecise reporting).
