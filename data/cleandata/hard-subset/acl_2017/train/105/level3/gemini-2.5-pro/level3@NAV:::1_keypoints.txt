- Summary: Proposes a hard monotonic attention model that decouples alignment and transduction, trained on step/write actions from pre-computed Bayesian alignments.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel hard monotonic attention architecture that explicitly models strong monotonicity in morphological inflection.

- Contribution 2: Shows the constrained model can outperform standard neural models in low-resource settings (e.g., CELEX).

- Strength: Effective in low-data regimes by avoiding overfitting through enforced monotonic constraints.

- Strength: Novel and simple design—decoupling alignment simplifies the learning problem and focuses the network on transduction.

- Strength: Clear writing and rigorous evaluation across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON with strong baselines.

- Weakness: Limited applicability—performs poorly on languages with non-monotonic phenomena (e.g., circumfixing, vowel harmony).

- Weakness: Lacks practical analysis of computational cost, inference speed, memory, and implementation complexity.

- Weakness: Depends on an external Bayesian aligner with no analysis of sensitivity to alignment errors or robustness to different aligners.
