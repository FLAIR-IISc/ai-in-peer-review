- Proposes a hard monotonic seq2seq decoder (STEP/WRITE) for morphological inflection trained from externally induced character alignments.  
- Uses a Bayesian CRP-based transliteration aligner to derive oracle action sequences rather than learning alignments jointly.  
- Trained with cross-entropy on oracle sequences and achieves strong results across three benchmarks.  
- Especially effective in low-resource settings (CELEX) and languages with largely monotonic inflection (Russian, German, Spanish).  
- Provides comparative analysis of learned representations and alignments between hard and soft attention models.  
- Novelty: clean, pointer-based decoder formulation that departs from soft-attention joint alignment/transduction.  
- Low-resource strength: outperforms prior neural and non-neural baselines on CELEX and shows better generalization than soft attention.  
- Competitive at scale: best averages on Wiktionary and strong results on SIGMORPHON for suffixing+stem-change languages.  
- Simpler, more stable training by decoupling alignment induction and avoiding marginalization over alignments.  
- Clear writing and thorough experiments, including qualitative analyses (alignment visualizations, SVD of encoder states).  
- Limitation: reliance on an external aligner may hurt portability and robustness when alignments are noisy or mis-specified.  
- Limitation: underperforms soft attention on non-monotonic or long-range morphological phenomena (harmony, templatic, agglutinative).  
- Limitation: trains to a single oracle derivation rather than marginalizing over multiple derivations, with no analysis of when this fails.  
- Limitation: lacks reported computational metrics (training/inference time, memory, decoding speed) and implementation/tradeoff discussion.  
- Limitation: limited ablations on model components and decoding strategies, leaving unclear what drives gains.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to induced alignment quality; can alternative aligners or noisy alignments be evaluated?  
- Question: What are training/inference time, memory costs, and alignment induction overhead compared to soft attention?  
- Question: Do you use greedy decoding or beam search for actions; what beam sizes and speed/accuracy tradeoffs were observed?  
- Question: Have you tried marginalizing over n-best derivations or scheduled sampling to improve robustness in non-monotonic cases?  
- Question: Can the control space be extended (e.g., JUMP(k), COPY spans) to handle non-monotonic phenomena while keeping simplicity?  
- Question: Can the model be warm-started with external alignments and then fine-tuned jointly to improve coverage for typologies like Turkish or Arabic?
