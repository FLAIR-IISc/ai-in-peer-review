- Introduces a hard monotonic attention model for morphological inflection that decodes via WRITE/STEP actions using externally induced character alignments, claiming better low-resource generalization.
- Proposes a simple transition-system trained with cross-entropy on oracle action sequences as a clear alternative to soft attention that jointly learns alignment and transduction.
- Provides comparative analyses of alignments and encoder representations to contrast what hard versus soft attention models learn.

- Strong low-resource performance: outperforms prior neural and non-neural baselines (e.g., latent-variable WFST) on CELEX, reducing overfitting via near-monotonic write/step control.
- Broad, careful evaluation across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON showing consistent gains in suffixing/mostly monotonic languages and competitive results at scale.
- Conceptual simplicity and training stability from using external alignments, enabling straightforward cross-entropy training without marginalizing latent alignments.
- Clear writing and useful qualitative analyses (alignment visualizations, SVD of encoder states) that support the monotonicity assumption.

- Depends on external alignments; lacks ablations on alignment source, noise sensitivity, or oracle vs. learned alignment quality, so robustness is unclear.
- Limited applicability in non-monotonic or highly context-dependent morphological phenomena (e.g., harmony, templatic morphology), constraining generality.
- Objective mismatch: trains on best derivations rather than sequences, with no empirical study of the impact or whether marginalizing derivations would help.
- Missing practical analysis of computational cost, wall-clock time, implementation complexity, differing training schedules, and absence of statistical significance testing.

- How sensitive is performance to alignment noise and degree of non-monotonicity; please ablate different aligners and synthetic noise.
- What are the training/inference speed and memory footprints compared to soft attention and WFST baselines?
- Does beam search over action sequences reduce the derivation-vs-sequence gap?
- Can the model adaptively relax monotonicity (e.g., limited backward steps) to better handle harmony and templatic morphology?
