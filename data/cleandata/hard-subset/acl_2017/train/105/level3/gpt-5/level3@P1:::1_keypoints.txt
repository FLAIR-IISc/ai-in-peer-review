- Introduces a neural encoder–decoder with hard monotonic attention using WRITE and STEP actions, trained on oracle action sequences from external alignments.
- Contribution: simple, effective hard-monotonic attention architecture modeling pointer movement and write actions, using best-derivation approximation.
- Contribution: Strong empirical gains in low-resource settings (CELEX) and competitive or superior performance on Wiktionary and parts of SIGMORPHON 2016.
- Contribution: Comparative analysis of learned alignments and character-in-context representations versus soft attention.
- Strength: Well-motivated inductive bias exploiting near-monotonicity; alignment visualizations validate the modeling choice.
- Strength: Clear low-resource advantage, outperforming prior neural and non-neural baselines and showing reduced overfitting.
- Strength: Competitive across languages and scales, especially for suffixing-with-stem-change languages.
- Strength: Simpler training pipeline via external alignments enabling straightforward cross-entropy training.
- Strength: Informative representational analysis suggesting differences in positional encoding between hard and soft attention.
- Weakness: Reliance on external aligner without quantifying sensitivity to alignment noise or comparing aligners.
- Weakness: Not universally state-of-the-art; lags on languages with strong non-local dependencies (e.g., harmony).
- Weakness: Trains on highest-probability derivation rather than marginalizing over derivations; no analysis of this approximation’s cost.
- Weakness: Missing practical details on computational cost, training/decoding speed, memory, and beam vs. greedy settings.
- Weakness: Uneven baseline comparisons and limited ablations isolating effects of alignments, action set, and embeddings.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to external aligner quality and have simpler aligners been tried?
- Question: What decoding strategy is used and how do beam size and decoding affect accuracy/speed?
- Question: Can the model handle limited non-monotonicity or be extended with jumps or COPY actions?
- Question: Would marginalizing over multiple plausible oracle derivations improve results versus a single oracle?
- Question: What are training and inference runtimes and memory usage compared to soft-attention baselines?
- Question: How much do morpho-syntactic feature embeddings contribute—any ablation (no features vs. features)?
- Additional: Paper is clearly written; more per-language error analysis would strengthen typology claims.
- Additional: Report variance across runs (especially on CELEX) to assess stability.
