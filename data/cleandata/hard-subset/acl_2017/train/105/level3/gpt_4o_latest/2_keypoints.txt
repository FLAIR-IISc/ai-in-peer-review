- Novel encoder–decoder with hard attention that enforces monotonicity and decouples alignment from transduction.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016, especially in low-resource settings.  
- Provides detailed analysis of learned alignments and representations, comparing hard attention to soft attention.  
- Novel architecture: hard attention + monotonicity is a natural fit for morphological inflection and simplifies training.  
- Strong low-resource performance, outperforming neural and non-neural baselines on CELEX.  
- Broad evaluation across three diverse datasets supports general applicability claims.  
- Insightful analysis showing how the model captures linguistic features and positional information.  
- Limited task scope: evaluated only on morphological inflection, not other monotonic Seq2Seq tasks.  
- Missing comparisons to simpler monotonic baselines (e.g., neural taggers, Schnober et al.), hindering assessment of trade-offs.  
- Greedy alignment during training may restrict handling of complex alignments; no comparison to marginalization approaches.  
- Unclear explanation of the role and necessity of linguistic features (e.g., POS tags) in model performance.  
- Clarity and formatting issues in equations, figures, and training/control descriptions that may impede reproducibility.  
- Question: How does the model perform on other monotonic Seq2Seq tasks (transliteration, summarization)?  
- Question: Why were simpler monotonic baselines omitted and how does the model compare in complexity and performance?  
- Question: Could greedy alignment be replaced by marginalizing over alignments, and what would be the impact on performance/training complexity?  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions, contingent on addressing evaluation scope and baseline comparison issues.
