Novel hard monotonic attention mechanism that uses pre-learned character alignments to avoid joint alignment+conversion learning.

Demonstrates strong empirical gains on small datasets (CELEX), outperforming prior neural models and Dreyer et al.'s WFST.

Well-written with comprehensive experiments across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON, showing robustness for monotonic alignment cases.

Provides useful analysis comparing hard and soft attention and how they encode linguistic features.

Major limitation: reliance on monotonic alignments leads to poor performance on non-monotonic or high-context languages (e.g., Hungarian, Arabic).

Missing practical implementation discussion, including computational cost and ease of implementation versus standard approaches.

Insufficient clarification of the specific conditions where the method is superior, complicating assessment of general applicability.

Promising contribution for targeted morphological tasks but needs revisions to address alignment constraints and dependency on external alignment procedures.
