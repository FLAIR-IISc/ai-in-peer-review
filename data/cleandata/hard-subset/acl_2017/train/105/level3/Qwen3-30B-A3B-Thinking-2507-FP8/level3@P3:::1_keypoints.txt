- Introduces a neural model with hard monotonic attention using independently learned character alignments for morphological inflection.
- Hard attention explicitly models nearly-monotonic alignments, unlike prior soft-attention joint alignment-transduction approaches.
- Uses a Bayesian transliteration method to produce alignments that guide an encoder-decoder with control actions.
- Achieves state-of-the-art on CELEX and excels on small training datasets, while remaining strong on Wiktionary and SIGMORPHON.
- Contribution: a novel hard monotonic attention mechanism tailored to morphological inflection tasks.
- Contribution: comprehensive empirical validation showing neural model outperforms previous methods in low-data regimes.
- Contribution: analysis of learned representations revealing differences in how hard vs soft attention encode character type and position.
- Strength: effectively mitigates overfitting on small datasets by leveraging monotonic alignment bias.
- Strength: thorough experiments across multiple datasets with appropriate baseline comparisons.
- Strength: insightful visualization/analysis that increases understanding of model behavior.
- Weakness: limited applicability—underperforms on languages with non-monotonic or context-dependent morphology (e.g., Turkish, Arabic, Navajo).
- Weakness: no discussion of computational cost, implementation complexity, or training/inference speed versus soft-attention.
- Weakness: incomplete comparison to other external-alignment methods and lack of detailed training-procedure contrasts.
- Question: provide timing comparisons between this model and soft-attention baselines on the same hardware.
- Question: clarify which linguistic features favor the approach and how to extend it to handle non-monotonic alignments.
- Question: discuss robustness to alignment errors from the Bayesian transliteration method and ways to mitigate them.
- Overall: valuable and novel contribution with strong empirical results, but needs clearer scope and practical/implementation clarifications.
