- Introduces a neural hard-attention model for nearly-monotonic morphological inflection generation.  
- Claims three contributions: hard attention model, SOTA on three datasets, and analysis comparing hard vs. soft attention.  
- Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016, showing strong low-resource and competitive high-resource performance.  
- Strength: novel hard-attention mechanism addressing soft-attention limits in low-resource morphological tasks.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across diverse datasets and resource settings.  
- Strength: insightful analysis and visualizations of learned representations and alignments.  
- Strength: practical impact for morphologically rich languages with scarce annotated data.  
- Weakness: unclear generalization to non-morphological seq2seq tasks like MT or summarization.  
- Weakness: dependence on pre-computed external alignments may limit applicability and robustness.  
- Weakness: insufficient exploration of scenarios where soft attention could outperform hard attention.  
- Weakness: paper lacks reproducibility details (hyperparameters, training time, compute).  
- Question: how does the model handle non-monotonic/templatic morphological alignments?  
- Question: can alignment learning be integrated to remove dependence on pre-computed alignments, and with what effect?  
- Question: has the hard-attention approach been tested on other seq2seq tasks (e.g., transliteration, abstractive summarization)?  
- Conclusion: promising and impactful for low-resource morphology but requires broader evaluation and mitigation of alignment/reproducibility limitations.
