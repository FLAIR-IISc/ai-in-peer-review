- Introduces a novel hard attention mechanism enforcing near-monotonic alignments tailored for morphological inflection.
- Shows state-of-the-art results on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, notably outperforming prior models on smaller datasets.
- Provides comparative analysis of learned representations between hard and soft attention models.

- Strong empirical performance across diverse datasets with clear improvements over baselines.
- Hard attention is well-motivated by morphological processes and logically justified.
- Thorough evaluation including multiple baselines and representation analysis.
- Clear, concise writing and well-documented experimental setup.
- Ablation-style comparison to soft attention isolates the effect of hard attention.

- Relies on pre-learned character-level alignments, introducing an external dependency and unexplored sensitivity to alignment quality.
- Lacks detailed error analysis to pinpoint specific morphological weaknesses.
- Slightly worse than soft attention on SIGMORPHON overall; needs deeper language-level investigation.
- No discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, which affects reproducibility.

- How sensitive is performance to the quality or method of pre-learned alignments?
- Can you provide a detailed error analysis highlighting common morphological failure modes?
- Which specific languages on SIGMORPHON drive the hard vs. soft attention performance differences?
- What are the training and inference computational costs compared to soft attention?

- References and related work are comprehensive and well-formatted.
- Recommend releasing source code to aid reproducibility.
- Suggest adding an intuitive example in the introduction illustrating monotonic alignment benefits.
- Overall: strong paper with solid contributions; recommend acceptance.
