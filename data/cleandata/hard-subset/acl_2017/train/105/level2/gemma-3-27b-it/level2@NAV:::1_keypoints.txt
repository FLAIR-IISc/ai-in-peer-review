- Introduces a novel hard monotonic attention mechanism tailored to morphological inflection generation.  
- Evaluated on three standard datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results and especially strong performance in low-resource settings.  
- Outperforms previous models notably on the CELEX dataset with limited training data.  
- Provides comparative analysis between hard and soft attention, offering insights into differing learned representations.  
- Key contributions: task-motivated hard attention, strong empirical performance, and analysis of alignment modeling benefits.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, rigorous experiments, insightful analysis, effective ablation studies, and well-written presentation.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of hard attention’s limitations for languages with non-monotonic morphology.  
- Weakness: SIGMORPHON results show underperformance on some language families, discussed only briefly.  
- Weakness: main paper lacks clarity on experimental setup details (notably hyperparameter tuning), which are relegated to supplementary material.  
- Weakness: reliance on pre-trained alignments may be limiting; end-to-end training alternatives were not explored.  
- Question: How would the hard attention mechanism handle languages with significant non-monotonic morphological processes, and what extensions could address this?  
- Question: For languages where the model underperforms on SIGMORPHON, can the authors provide a detailed error analysis identifying problematic morphological patterns?  
- Question: How sensitive is overall performance to the quality of pre-trained alignments, and were different alignment methods or end-to-end training approaches tried?
