- Proposes a neural model for morphological inflection that uses a hard attention mechanism to model near-monotonic character alignments with a controllable attention/control mechanism.  
- Evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016, reporting state-of-the-art results and analysis of learned representations.  
- Main contributions: novel hard-attention model tailored to inflection, strong empirical SOTA on several datasets, and analysis comparing to soft attention.  
- Strong empirical performance, notably SOTA on CELEX and robust behavior in low-resource settings.  
- Well-motivated hard attention design that decouples alignment and transduction and explicitly models monotonicity.  
- Comprehensive evaluation and ablation-style comparisons, including diverse languages in SIGMORPHON.  
- Depends on pre-trained character-level alignments, introducing an external dependency that may limit claims about the model itself.  
- Paper lacks detailed discussion/analysis of the pre-trained alignment model’s limitations, biases, or error impact.  
- Presentation could be improved by showing percentage improvements and including statistical significance testing.  
- Request: report sensitivity analysis on alignment quality and alignment error impact.  
- Request: discuss or experiment with end-to-end training strategies that encourage monotonicity (e.g., regularization).  
- Request: provide statistical significance testing for reported improvements.  
- Suggestion: include implementation details of the soft-attention baseline in the supplementary material for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: expand related work to cover more recent sequence transduction and attention advances.  
- Suggestion: further elaborate the connection between the “step” action and transition-system concepts.
