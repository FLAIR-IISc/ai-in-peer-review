- Proposes a neural encoder-decoder with hard monotonic attention that generates inflections via WRITE/STEP actions and is trained on deterministic action sequences derived from an external aligner using cross-entropy.

- Contribution: Introduces a simple, effective hard monotonic attention architecture achieving state-of-the-art results, especially in low-resource settings.

- Contribution: Demonstrates that a strong monotonic prior improves generalization and reduces overfitting compared to soft-attention models.

- Contribution: Provides comparative analysis of representations and alignments that explains why hard attention performs strongly.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance across CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON datasets, notably outperforming specialized WFSTs on small data.

- Strength: Well-motivated, elegant design—WRITE/STEP formalizes linguistic monotonicity and acts as an effective regularizer against overfitting.

- Strength: Simple, stable training paradigm by decoupling alignment and transduction, avoiding RL or latent-alignment marginalization.

- Strength: Insightful analysis (qualitative alignments and SVD of encoder representations) that plausibly explains model behavior.

- Weakness: Strict monotonic assumption limits generality for non-monotonic phenomena (e.g., templatic morphology, circumfixing); outperformed by soft attention on such languages.

- Weakness: Dependence on an external aligner without analysis of sensitivity to aligner quality or comparisons to simpler aligners.

- Weakness: Could better position training approach relative to alternative hard-attention methods (e.g., end-to-end RL) and discuss trade-offs.
