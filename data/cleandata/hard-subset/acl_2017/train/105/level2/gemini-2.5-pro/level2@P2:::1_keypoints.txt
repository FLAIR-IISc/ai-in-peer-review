- Model: seq2seq with hard monotonic attention predicting write/step actions; uses an external character aligner to produce gold action sequences and trains with cross-entropy.  
- Contribution: achieves new state-of-the-art on CELEX, Wiktionary, SIGMORPHON 2016 and is first neural model to beat a specialized FST on low-resource CELEX.  
- Analysis: compares hard vs soft attention and shows hard attention improves data efficiency by shifting positional encoding to explicit step actions.  
- Strength: excellent empirical performance, especially in low-resource settings (CELEX 89.44% avg), outperforming strong neural and FST baselines.  
- Strength: well-motivated, elegant design that bakes monotonicity into a simple step/write paradigm and simplifies training by decoupling alignment.  
- Strength: strong internal analysis (SVD projections, qualitative alignments) that explains why hard attention uses representational capacity more effectively.  
- Weakness: depends on an external alignment tool; sensitivity to aligner quality and robustness to noisier/simpler aligners not evaluated.  
- Weakness: limited applicability to non-monotonic morphological processes—worse than soft attention on templatic/circumfixing/agglutinative languages.  
- Weakness: inadequate positioning relative to prior hard/monotonic attention literature (e.g., RNN-T, Chorowski); differences and relations not fully discussed.  
- Question: how sensitive is the model to aligner quality—any experiments with simpler aligners like Levenshtein?  
- Question: does optimizing argmax over derivations (s) instead of outputs (y) cause errors when multiple s map to the same y?  
- Question: what decoding strategy was used at test time (greedy vs beam search), what beam size, and how much does beam search help?
