- Paper presents a hard-attention neural model for morphological inflection generation that conditions on source word and morpho-syntactic attributes.  
- Evaluated on three established datasets, claiming state-of-the-art results across settings.  
- Contribution: proposes a hard-attention approach for nearly-monotonic seq2seq learning tailored to inflection generation.  
- Contribution: establishes new state-of-the-art performance on three morphological inflection datasets.  
- Contribution: analyzes and compares hard-attention vs. soft-attention models to reveal learned features.  
- Strength: handles small training sets well and avoids overfitting, outperforming prior neural models on low-resource CELEX.  
- Strength: hard attention can condition on the entire output history, offering advantages over FST models.  
- Strength: robust performance on large-data Wiktionary experiments, beating prior neural and non-neural baselines.  
- Strength: analysis of learned alignments and representations provides insight into model behavior.  
- Weakness: slightly worse accuracy than soft-attention on languages with complex phenomena (e.g., vowel/consonant harmony).  
- Weakness: relies on pre-trained character-level alignments, which may be unavailable or noisy.  
- Weakness: model simplicity might limit capturing nuanced morphological patterns.  
- Questions: how will authors address reduced performance on languages with complex morphology?  
- Questions: request more details on the character-level alignment process and its role in training.  
- Questions: how can the model generalize to other nearly-monotonic align-and-transduce tasks (e.g., abstractive summarization, MT)?
