- Proposes a hard-attention neural model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning for morphological inflection.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on three morphological inflection datasets, outperforming prior neural and non-neural methods.
- Analyzes continuous representations from hard and soft attention models to reveal features learned.
- Hard attention excels on small-data settings, outperforming prior neural models on the CELEX dataset.
- Model is robust on large datasets, achieving SOTA on the Wiktionary dataset.
- Provides a thorough analysis of learned representations, yielding insights into model behavior.
- Evaluation focuses mainly on suffixing languages, so results may not generalize to languages with complex morphology.
- Comparison uses a soft-attention baseline that is not the most recent or advanced, which may weaken the comparison.
- Paper lacks a detailed error analysis, limiting understanding of failure modes and improvement areas.
- Asks how the model could be extended to handle phenomena like vowel harmony and consonant harmony.
- Requests more details on the character-level alignment process used to derive gold transduction and control sequences.
- Queries how results would change if the soft-attention baseline were replaced by a more recent/advanced model.
- Overall assessment: well-designed and effective contribution with valuable analyses but limited typological coverage and baseline/error-analysis shortcomings.
