- Proposes a neural encoder-decoder for morphological inflection using hard attention with a control mechanism to write or advance the attention pointer.  
- Evaluated on three prior datasets and reports state-of-the-art results across setups.  
- Analyzes continuous representations learned by hard vs. soft attention models.  
- Contribution: introduces a hard-attention model tailored for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence tasks.  
- Contribution: establishes new state-of-the-art performance on three morphological inflection datasets.  
- Contribution: provides comparative analysis of features extracted by hard and soft attention models.  
- Strength: Outperforms prior neural and non-neural approaches, including on the low-resource CELEX dataset.  
- Strength: Demonstrates robustness with very small training sets, improving over prior neural models.  
- Strength: Offers insightful analysis of learned representations for the task.  
- Weakness: Model is specialized for morphological inflection and may not generalize to other seq2seq tasks.  
- Weakness: Depends on pre-trained character-level alignments, which may be unavailable or error-prone.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to other neural architectures, e.g., transformer-based models.  
- Question: How does the model perform on other seq2seq tasks like machine translation or summarization?  
- Question: Can the model be adapted to learn alignments from scratch instead of using pre-trained alignments?  
- Question: How does performance change with different attention types (self-attention, hierarchical attention, etc.)?
