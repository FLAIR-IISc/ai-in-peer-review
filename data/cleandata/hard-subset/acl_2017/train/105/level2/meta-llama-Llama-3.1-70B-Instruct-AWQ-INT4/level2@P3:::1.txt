**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural model for morphological inflection generation, which employs a hard attention mechanism inspired by the nearly-monotonic alignment commonly found between the characters in a word and its inflection. The model consists of an encoder-decoder neural network with a dedicated control mechanism that determines whether to write an output symbol or advance the attention pointer to the next input state. The model is evaluated on three previously studied morphological inflection generation datasets and shows state-of-the-art results in various setups compared to previous neural and non-neural approaches. The paper also presents an analysis of the continuous representations learned by both the hard and soft attention models for the task.

**Main Contributions**

1. A hard attention model for nearly-monotonic sequence-to-sequence learning, as common in the morphological inflection setting.
2. Evaluation of the model on the task of morphological inflection generation, establishing a new state of the art on three previously studied datasets for the task.
3. Analysis and comparison of the hard attention model and the soft-attention model, shedding light on the features such models extract for the inflection generation task.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved performance**: The proposed model outperforms previous neural and non-neural approaches on three morphological inflection generation datasets, including the CELEX dataset, which is a challenging low-resource setting.
2. **Robustness to small training sets**: The model performs well even with very few training examples, which is a significant advantage over previous neural models that require large training sets.
3. **Analysis of learned representations**: The paper provides an insightful analysis of the continuous representations learned by both the hard and soft attention models, which sheds light on the features such models extract for the inflection generation task.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited generalizability**: The model is specifically designed for morphological inflection generation and may not generalize well to other sequence-to-sequence learning tasks.
2. **Dependence on pre-trained alignments**: The model relies on pre-trained character-level alignments, which may not always be available or accurate.
3. **Lack of comparison to other neural models**: The paper only compares the proposed model to soft-attention models and does not evaluate its performance against other neural models, such as transformer-based models.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the model perform on other sequence-to-sequence learning tasks, such as machine translation or text summarization?
2. Can the model be adapted to learn alignments from scratch, rather than relying on pre-trained alignments?
3. How does the model's performance change when using different types of attention mechanisms, such as self-attention or hierarchical attention?
