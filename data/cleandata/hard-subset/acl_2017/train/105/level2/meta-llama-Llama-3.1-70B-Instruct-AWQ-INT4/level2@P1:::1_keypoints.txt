- Proposes a neural hard-attention model for morphological inflection that exploits nearly-monotonic character alignments.
- Evaluated on three established datasets and reports state-of-the-art performance across multiple setups.

- Contribution: a hard-attention seq2seq model tailored to nearly-monotonic sequence mapping.
- Contribution: empirical SOTA results on three morphological inflection datasets.
- Contribution: analysis comparing hard-attention and soft-attention models and their learned features.

- Strength: novel approach that outperforms prior models on multiple datasets.
- Strength: thorough analysis including learned alignments, representations, and soft-attention comparison.
- Strength: well-written and clearly explained model and components.

- Weakness: heavy reliance on the nearly-monotonic alignment assumption that may not hold for all languages or phenomena.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other recent neural approaches (e.g., Kann et al., 2016).
- Weakness: limited discussion of implications and limitations of the hard-attention mechanism.

- Question: request more details on the character-level alignment method used to guide training.
- Question: how will the model be extended to handle complex phenomena like vowel or consonant harmony?
- Question: please provide a more detailed comparison with other recent neural morphological inflection models.

- Additional comment: paper is a significant contribution but would benefit from deeper discussion of hard-attention implications and broader comparisons.
