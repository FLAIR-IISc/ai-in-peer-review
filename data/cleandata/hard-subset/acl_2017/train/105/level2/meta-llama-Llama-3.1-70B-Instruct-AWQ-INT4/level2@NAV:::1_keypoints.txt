- Summary: Proposes a neural hard-monotonic attention model for character-level morphological inflection, achieves state-of-the-art on three datasets, and analyzes learned alignments and representations.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a hard attention model to capture near-monotonic input–output character alignments.

- Contribution 2: Evaluates the model across three established morphological inflection datasets, reporting strong/SOTA results in multiple settings.

- Contribution 3: Presents analysis comparing learned alignments and internal representations of hard vs. soft attention models.

- Strength 1: Demonstrates improved performance over prior neural and non-neural methods, notably in low-resource scenarios.

- Strength 2: Applies a novel hard attention mechanism appropriate for largely monotonic character alignments.

- Strength 3: Provides a comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple datasets for robust comparison.

- Weakness 1: Analysis of learned alignments and representations is limited to a small subset of data.

- Weakness 2: Lacks direct comparisons with other neural attention variants (e.g., soft attention alternatives).

- Weakness 3: Does not discuss method limitations, such as risk of overfitting in low-resource settings.

- Question 1: How would this approach be extended to tasks with non-monotonic alignments?

- Question 2: Can the authors give deeper insights into specific features learned by the hard attention representations for morphological changes?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to mitigate potential limitations like overfitting in low-resource conditions?
