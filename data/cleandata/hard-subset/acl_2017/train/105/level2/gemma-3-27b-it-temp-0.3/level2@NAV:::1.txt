## Review of "Morphological Inflection Generation with Hard Monotonic Attention"

This paper presents a novel neural model for morphological inflection generation utilizing a hard attention mechanism. The core idea is to leverage the near-monotonic alignment commonly found in morphologically rich languages, guiding the model to attend to relevant input characters before generating output characters. The authors evaluate their model on three datasets (CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON2016) and report state-of-the-art results, particularly demonstrating improved performance on the low-resource CELEX dataset.

The primary contribution of this work is the successful application of a hard attention mechanism to morphological inflection, offering a competitive alternative to soft attention models. The model’s ability to surpass previous state-of-the-art results on multiple datasets, especially in low-resource scenarios, is a significant achievement. A secondary contribution lies in the insightful analysis comparing the hard and soft attention models, shedding light on the features each learns and their respective strengths. This analysis provides valuable understanding of how these models approach the task.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The model consistently achieves competitive or state-of-the-art performance across all three datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.
*   **Effective Handling of Low-Resource Data:** The model’s superior performance on the CELEX dataset, a notoriously low-resource benchmark, is a key strength. This suggests the hard attention mechanism is less prone to overfitting than other approaches.
*   **Clear and Well-Motivated Approach:** The paper clearly articulates the motivation behind using hard attention, connecting it to the inherent properties of morphological inflection.
*   **Detailed Analysis:** The comparative analysis of hard and soft attention models provides valuable insights into the learned representations and the behavior of each approach.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Architecture:** While the application of hard attention to this specific task is novel, the underlying encoder-decoder architecture with RNNs is relatively standard.
*   **Dependency on Pre-trained Alignments:** The reliance on independently learned character-level alignments for training, while simplifying the process, introduces an external dependency and potential source of error. The impact of alignment quality on performance isn't thoroughly investigated.
*   **SIGMORPHON Results Nuance:** The authors acknowledge the soft attention model outperforms on some SIGMORPHON languages. A more detailed discussion of *why* this occurs, beyond linguistic typology, would be beneficial.
*   **Ablation Studies Missing:** The paper lacks ablation studies to isolate the contribution of specific components (e.g., bi-directional encoder, feedback connection) within the proposed model.

**Questions to Authors:**

*   How sensitive is the model’s performance to the quality of the pre-trained character-level alignments? Could the alignment process be integrated into the end-to-end training?
*   Could you elaborate on the specific architectural choices that contribute to the improved performance on low-resource datasets compared to the soft attention baseline?
*   What is the computational cost of the hard attention mechanism compared to soft attention, and how does this impact scalability?
