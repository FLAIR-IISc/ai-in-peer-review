- Proposal: Sequence-to-sequence morphological inflection model using hard, nearly monotonic attention via step/write actions trained from external character alignments.

- Contribution 1: A simple externally-aligned hard monotonic attention transducer trained with cross-entropy that generalizes well in low-resource settings.

- Contribution 2: Comprehensive empirical evaluation showing new SOTA on CELEX and competitive/best results on several Wiktionary and SIGMORPHON suffixing language settings.

- Contribution 3: Comparative analysis of learned alignments and encoder representations explaining when hard vs soft attention is advantageous.

- Strength: Strong low-resource performance and reduced overfitting, with best average accuracy on CELEX and favorable learning curves.

- Strength: Broad, careful evaluation across multiple benchmarks, languages, typologies, and both neural and non-neural baselines.

- Strength: Simple, interpretable step/write control mechanism yielding stable training and clearer alignment behavior.

- Strength: Insightful analyses (SVD, alignment visualizations) that plausibly explain why hard attention overfits less.

- Weakness: Limited generality for languages with non-local dependencies (e.g., vowel/consonant harmony) where soft attention can outperform.

- Weakness: Dependence on an external aligner with no ablation on aligner choice, quality, or robustness to alignment errors.

- Weakness: Unclear search/decoding details and no quantification of impact from not marginalizing over derivations (greedy vs beam, runtime vs accuracy).

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing and potential unfairness from under-optimized baselines, risking inflated comparative gains.

- Weakness: Key hyperparameters and training settings are relegated to the supplement rather than summarized in the main text.

- Question: How sensitive are results to the alignment model and its hyperparameters—please provide ablations with different/noisy aligners?

- Question: What decoding strategy (beam size, runtime) was used and does beam search materially change accuracy?

- Question: Could a hybrid or learned monotonic/limited-context soft attention mitigate deficits on harmony-heavy languages?

- Question: Are results stable across random seeds, and are improvements statistically significant on CELEX/SIGMORPHON?
