- Paper proposes a neural encoder–decoder for morphological inflection with a hard monotonic STEP/WRITE attention trained from external character alignments and evaluated on CELEX, Wiktionary, and SIGMORPHON 2016.

- Contribution: simple, effective hard-attention transduction leveraging monotonic alignments that excels in low-resource settings.

- Contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation across three benchmarks with strong CELEX gains and competitive/ best averages on Wiktionary.

- Contribution: qualitative analyses contrasting hard vs. soft attention, including alignment visualizations and representation studies.

- Strength: strong low-resource performance on CELEX, outperforming prior neural and latent-variable WFST baselines.

- Strength: conceptual clarity and simplicity by framing generation as a monotonic transition system trained with cross-entropy on oracle action sequences.

- Strength: broad, fair empirical coverage against strong neural and non-neural baselines showing robustness across conditions.

- Strength: insightful analyses showing linguistically plausible one-to-many/deletion behaviors and differing encoding emphases between models.

- Weakness: reliance on external CRP-based alignments and best-derivation training introduces potential error propagation and train–test objective mismatch.

- Weakness: fairness and rigor concerns in comparisons (SIGMORPHON training disparity with MED, no significance testing, mixed-capacity Wiktionary comparisons).

- Weakness: limited generality—performance drops on non-monotonic phenomena (templatic Arabic, harmony), and no explored hybrid/extensions for non-monotonicity.

- Weakness: limited evaluation breadth—only exact-match accuracy reported, no edit-distance/paradigm metrics, and unclear decoding/search and efficiency details.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to alignment errors; can you ablate with perturbed alignments or compare joint training that refines alignments?

- Question: What decoding strategy is used (greedy vs. beam) and does beam search improve accuracy on complex languages?

- Question: Can you provide edit-distance metrics or error typologies (suffix vs. stem vs. deletion), especially where the hard model underperforms?

- Question: On SIGMORPHON, can you re-run MED/soft baselines with matched training regimens and report per-language hyperparameter tuning for Wiktionary?

- Question: Do you anticipate gains from marginalizing over derivations or training with expected risk, and are there preliminary results?

- Suggestion: Consider a hybrid controller with limited learned non-monotone actions (e.g., JUMP-k, COPY-block) to handle templatic/harmony systems.

- Suggestion: Report runtime and parameter counts to quantify the simplicity/efficiency advantage.

- Suggestion: Include ablations for morphosyntactic tag embeddings and previous-symbol feedback to clarify their contributions.

- Suggestion: Discuss/apply the approach to related near-monotonic tasks (transliteration, G2P) or provide small-scale evidence.

- Suggestion (minor): Clarify preprocessing (normalization, diacritics) and release alignment scripts and hyperparameters for reproducibility.

- Overall: Solid, well-motivated empirical paper leaning toward acceptance contingent on addressing fairness, alignment-sensitivity, and analysis concerns.
