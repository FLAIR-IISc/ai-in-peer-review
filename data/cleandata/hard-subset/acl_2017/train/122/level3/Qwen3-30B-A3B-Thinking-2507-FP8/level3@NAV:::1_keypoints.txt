- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT): uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes utterance/context representations instead of delexicalization.  
- Main contributions claimed: novel embedding-based DST, significant improvements over delexicalization baselines, and showing importance of semantic-specialized vectors.  
- Strength: Well-motivated core idea to use pre-trained embeddings to handle lexical variation.  
- Strength: Shows Paragram-SL999 (semantic-specialized) vectors outperform GloVe and random initializations, highlighting semantic quality importance.  
- Strength: Experimental results are robust and statistically significant across two datasets.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to two restaurant-domain datasets, raising generalization concerns to more complex domains.  
- Weakness: No comparison to separate slot-tagging architectures, a standard alternative approach.  
- Weakness: Similarity computations restricted to slot-value spans, which could bias results and affect fairness of comparisons.  
- Weakness: Introductory example (restaurant_name) is misleading and does not address coreference resolution, suggesting potential model limitations.  
- Recommendation: Authors should evaluate on more diverse, complex dialogue domains to test generality.  
- Recommendation: Authors should compare with slot-tagging approaches and clarify handling of coreference to strengthen claims.
