- Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), a framework for dialogue state tracking using pre-trained word vectors instead of hand-crafted semantic lexicons.
- Contributions: neural SLU/DST coupling without domain-specific dictionaries.
- Contributions: effective representation learning for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries via pre-trained word vectors.
- Contributions: two variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) that outperform delexicalization-based baselines.
- Strength: shows NBT scales to larger domains and matches baselines that rely on hand-crafted dictionaries.
- Strength: significantly outperforms baselines when semantic dictionaries are unavailable, addressing labor-intensive dictionary creation.
- Strength: strong experimental results on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 with significant joint goal and request accuracy gains.
- Strength: demonstrates semantic-specialized vectors (Paragram-SL999) improve DST performance over generic vectors.
- Strength: provides novel evidence linking word vector semantics to downstream dialogue state tracking performance.
- Strength: evaluation compares against both delexicalization and lexicon-enhanced baselines, giving a comprehensive assessment.
- Weakness: paper organization and presentation are confusing; key terms and notation are introduced without definition.
- Weakness: claim of "joint" SLU/DST is misleading and not clearly distinguished from prior joint models.
- Weakness: lacks a clear architecture diagram showing NBT’s relation to the broader dialogue system.
- Weakness: insufficient detail about the baseline semantic dictionary and the manual effort to create it.
- Weakness: no concrete utterance examples where NBT succeeds and the baseline fails, weakening the empirical argument.
- Weakness: does not justify why RNNs/LSTMs were not explored for sequential utterance modeling.
- Question: provide statistics on corrections made to the DSTC2 dataset and their significance.
- Question: clarify manual effort (human hours) to create the baseline semantic dictionary for 100 slot-value pairs.
- Question: supply specific examples where NBT correctly identified slot-value pairs missed by the baseline, especially non-standard phrasing.
- Question: explain why RNN/LSTM architectures were not explored and how they might compare to the proposed models.
