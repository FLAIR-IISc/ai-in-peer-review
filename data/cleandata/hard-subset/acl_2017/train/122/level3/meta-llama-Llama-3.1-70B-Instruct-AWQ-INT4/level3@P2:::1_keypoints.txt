- Proposes a Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values and compose distributed utterance and context representations.  
- Evaluated on two datasets, showing consistent and significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines.  
- Compares different embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999) and analyzes their impact on performance.  
- Demonstrates improved DST performance relative to previous delexicalization approaches.  
- Introduces a novel use of pre-trained embeddings for slot-value representation and context composition.  
- Provides a thorough evaluation including embedding pre-training comparisons.  
- Datasets are limited, raising concerns about generalization to more complex dialogue domains.  
- No comparison with a separate slot-tagging component to contextualize NBT performance.  
- Unclear how the model handles coreference resolution, an important DST aspect.  
- Requests clarification on plans to evaluate generalization to more complex domains.  
- Asks for a direct comparison between NBT and a separate slot-tagging approach.  
- Seeks explanation of how coreference resolution is handled and its implications for DST.
