- Summary: Proposes a neural belief tracking (NBT) framework that uses pre-trained word vectors to couple SLU and DST, removing hand-crafted lexicons and showing improvements over delexicalization baselines on two datasets.

- Contribution 1: Novel DST method representing slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composing distributed utterance and context representations.

- Contribution 2: Empirical improvements: NBT models yield consistent, significant gains over delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.

- Contribution 3: Examines alternative embedding pre-training (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999) and shows semantic specialization improves performance.

- Strength 1: Strong, promising idea—leveraging pre-trained embeddings to improve language understanding in belief tracking.

- Strength 2: Significant quantitative performance improvements demonstrating method effectiveness.

- Strength 3: Robust evaluation across two datasets and multiple baselines provides a thorough assessment.

- Weakness 1: Limited evaluation—only two datasets, may not generalize to more complex dialogue domains.

- Weakness 2: Missing comparison to approaches using a separate slot-tagging component.

- Weakness 3: Introductory example may mislead about handling of coreference (e.g., unresolved "restaurant_name").

- Question 1: How will the approach handle coreference resolution for entities referenced earlier in the conversation?

- Question 2: How do the authors plan to extend the method to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization fails?

- Question 3: Have the authors considered evaluation on additional datasets, multi-domain settings, or non-English languages?

- Additional comment: Paper would benefit from more discussion of limitations and extensions and more implementation details (hyperparameters, training procedure).
