**Paper Summary and Contributions**

This paper proposes a neural belief tracking (NBT) framework for dialogue state tracking, which estimates a user's goal at every step of the dialogue. The NBT model learns to represent user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors, and combines these representations to make binary decisions for slot-value pairs. The main contributions of this work are: (1) a novel neural-network framework for dialogue state tracking that leverages pre-trained word vectors, (2) the development of two utterance representation models (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN), and (3) experimental results showing significant improvement over a delexicalized baseline.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new approach to dialogue state tracking that leverages pre-trained word vectors, which is a significant departure from traditional methods that rely on hand-crafted semantic lexicons.
2. **Improved performance**: The experimental results show that the NBT model outperforms a delexicalized baseline, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
3. **Clear goals and ideas**: The paper has a clear and well-defined goal, and the authors present a reasonable and well-motivated idea for improving dialogue state tracking.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Paper organization and readability**: The paper organization and readability are weak, making it difficult for non-experts to follow the details of the proposed approach.
2. **Lack of clarity on model capabilities**: The paper does not clearly explain whether the model can perform separate SLU, and the term "joint" may be misleading if not.
3. **Insufficient notation definitions**: Notations (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) are introduced before being defined, which can cause confusion for readers.
4. **Limited comparison to other DSTC2 entries**: The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison to other DSTC2 entries, which would be useful for evaluating the performance of the proposed approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a formal definition of DST and its relationship to the proposed NBT model?
2. How does the proposed model handle separate SLU, and can you clarify the term "joint" in this context?
3. Can you provide statistics on the corrected DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other DSTC2 entries if corrections are small?
4. Have you considered exploring RNNs/LSTMs for sequential utterance modeling, given their success in SLU?
5. Can you provide more details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to indicate manual cost?
6. Can you provide example cases where the proposed model fixes baseline errors, which would help to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach?

**Additional Comments**

The paper has a clear and well-defined goal, and the authors present a reasonable and well-motivated idea for improving dialogue state tracking. However, the paper organization and readability are weak, and some notations are introduced before being defined. The authors should consider providing a formal definition of DST, clarifying the term "joint", and providing more details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline. Additionally, the authors may want to consider exploring RNNs/LSTMs for sequential utterance modeling and providing example cases where the proposed model fixes baseline errors.
