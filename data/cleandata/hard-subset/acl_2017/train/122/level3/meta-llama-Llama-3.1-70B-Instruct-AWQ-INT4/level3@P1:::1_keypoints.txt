- Proposes Neural Belief Tracking (NBT) for DST that represents slot values with pre-trained word embeddings and composes utterance/context representations.  
- Evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, showing consistent and significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines.  
- Evaluates alternative embedding pre-training approaches (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999).  
- Demonstrates effective use of pre-trained embeddings to capture semantics and improve DST performance.  
- Presents a novel approach that moves beyond hand-crafted semantic lexicons and separate SLU modules.  
- Limited evaluation restricted to two datasets, which may not reflect performance in more complex dialogue domains.  
- Lacks comparison with a separate slot-tagging component, leaving a key baseline unaddressed.  
- Intro example may be misleading about dialogue state and does not clarify coreference resolution handling.  
- Asks how the model will be extended to complex domains, how it handles coreference, and how it will be compared to slot-tagging approaches.  
- Overall promising and novel contribution but needs more diverse datasets and additional baseline comparisons to strengthen claims.
