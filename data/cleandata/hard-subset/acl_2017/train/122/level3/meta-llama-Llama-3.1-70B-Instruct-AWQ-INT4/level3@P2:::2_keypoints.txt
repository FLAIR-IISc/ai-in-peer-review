- Proposes a Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) for dialogue state tracking using pre-trained word vectors, with binary slot-value decisions and two utterance encoders (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN); reports improvements over a delexicalized baseline.
- Novel approach: leverages pre-trained word vectors instead of hand-crafted semantic lexicons.
- Improved performance: NBT outperforms the delexicalized baseline in experiments.
- Clear goals and motivation: paper presents a well-defined objective and reasonable idea for DST improvement.
- Poor organization and readability: hard for non-experts to follow technical details.
- Unclear model capabilities: ambiguous whether the model performs separate SLU and whether “joint” is accurate.
- Notation issues: symbols (e.g., tq, ts, tv) are introduced before being defined, causing confusion.
- Limited experimental comparison: lacks comprehensive comparison to other DSTC2 entries.
- Request for formal definition: ask authors to formally define DST and its relation to NBT.
- Clarify SLU handling and the meaning of “joint” in the model’s context.
- Provide statistics on the corrected DSTC2 dataset and comparisons if corrections are minor.
- Consider exploring RNNs/LSTMs for sequential utterance modeling given their SLU success.
- Provide details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to quantify manual effort.
- Supply example cases where NBT corrects baseline errors to illustrate practical gains.
