- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) using pre-trained word vectors to handle lexical/morphological ambiguity, coupling SLU and DST without hand-crafted lexicons.
- Evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, outperforming delexicalisation-based models with and without semantic dictionaries.
- Contribution: Novel DST method representing slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composing distributed utterance and context representations.
- Contribution: Demonstrated consistent performance improvements over previous delexicalisation-based models.
- Contribution: Evaluated different word vector pre-training approaches (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999).
- Strength: Strong idea—leveraging pre-trained embeddings to improve language understanding in belief tracking.
- Strength: Consistent and significant empirical improvements across datasets.
- Strength: Robust evaluation on two datasets with different characteristics, suggesting generalizability.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—only two datasets, may not reflect more complex dialogue domains.
- Weakness: No comparison with a separate slot-tagging component as a baseline.
- Weakness: Introductory example may be misleading about dialogue state and raises questions on coreference handling.
- Question: How will the NBT model be adapted to more complex dialogue domains?
- Question: How does the NBT model handle coreference resolution in practice?
- Question: How do the authors plan to evaluate NBT on more diverse datasets in the future?
