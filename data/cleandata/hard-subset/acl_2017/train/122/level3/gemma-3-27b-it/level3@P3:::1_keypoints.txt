- Proposes a Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values and compose utterance/context representations, replacing delexicalization with semantic matching.  
- Core idea—moving from exact matching/delexicalization to semantic reasoning via pre-trained embeddings—is a strong, scalable contribution.  
- Demonstrates consistent, statistically significant improvements over delexicalization baselines on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, especially when lexicons are unavailable.  
- Ablation study shows semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) outperform GloVe and random initialization, highlighting embedding importance.  
- Provides a clear problem statement arguing existing DST methods rely on hand-crafted lexicons and separate SLU modules, motivating the work.  
- Uses standard DSTC metrics (joint goal accuracy, request accuracy), enabling direct comparison and reproducibility.  
- Evaluation is limited to two datasets in the restaurant domain, so generalizability to more complex domains is unproven.  
- Lacks comparison to a pipeline with an explicit slot-tagging component, leaving unclear whether gains come from better slot-filling or belief updating.  
- Introductory example implying coreference resolution may be misleading because the paper doesn’t explain whether NBT handles coreference.  
- Context modeling only uses the last system utterance, which may limit performance on dialogues requiring longer-range context.  
- Paper lacks a detailed qualitative error analysis to explain common failure modes and guide future improvements.  
- Requests clarification on how NBT handles coreference—whether internally or relying on ASR/other components.  
- Asks whether authors plan or have evaluated NBT on other domains (e.g., travel, technical support) and what those results are.  
- Seeks elaboration on limitations of the current context model and plans to incorporate longer-range context.  
- Requests a more detailed error analysis showing frequent misclassification sources.  
- Suggests clarifying context-modeling equations (9 and 10) for better intuition.  
- Recommends reporting computational costs (training and inference time) compared to baselines.
