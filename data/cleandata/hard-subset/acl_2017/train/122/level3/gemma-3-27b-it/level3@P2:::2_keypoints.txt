- Novel NBT framework that leverages pre-trained word vectors to reduce reliance on large SLU annotations and hand-crafted lexicons.  
- Proposes NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN models that learn representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries to predict slot–value presence.  
- Significant empirical improvements over a delexicalization baseline on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, showing robustness to ASR noise and complex language.  
- NBT-CNN shows benefits of convolutional architectures for capturing contextual information compared to NBT-DNN.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation across two datasets and multiple embeddings increases generalizability of findings.  
- Analysis demonstrates semantically specialized vectors (Paragram-SL999) outperform distributional (GloVe) or random vectors for this task.  
- Strong, statistically significant gains in goal and request accuracy across datasets.  
- Tackles a key DST bottleneck by reducing need for manual semantic dictionaries and extensive annotated data.  
- Paper organization and presentation are unclear; foundational DST concepts are not defined early, reducing accessibility.  
- Notational clarity issues: symbols introduced before definitions causing reader confusion.  
- Ambiguity around "joint SLU/DST": unclear if model performs SLU as a separate component or fully integrates SLU and DST.  
- DSTC2 dataset spelling corrections are mentioned but lack statistics and comparison to performance on the original dataset.  
- Limited exploration of sequential models (RNNs/LSTMs) for utterance representation is a missed opportunity.  
- Request for a formal DST task definition and clear placement of NBT within a typical spoken dialogue system architecture.  
- Request for details on DSTC2 corrections: number of errors fixed and performance on uncorrected data versus existing entries.  
- Request for concrete examples where NBT handles lexical variation that the baseline misses.  
- Request to quantify manual effort (entries, person-hours) for building the semantic dictionary used by the baseline.  
- Ask whether RNNs/LSTMs were considered for NBT and reasons for excluding them if not.  
- Overall assessment: promising and empirically strong approach, but needs clearer presentation and exploration of alternative architectures to strengthen impact.
