- Summary: Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses semantically specialized pre-trained word vectors to perform lexicon-free joint SLU/DST, compares two utterance encoders, adds an ASR N-best belief update, and shows significant gains on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0.

- Contribution: Empirically shows specialized pre-trained embeddings enable lexicon-free DST with accuracy gains in noisy and clean settings.

- Contribution: Presents a simple, modular joint SLU/DST architecture combining utterance, ontology, and system-act representations via context gating and element-wise interactions.

- Contribution: Compares sentence encoders (DNN vs CNN) and demonstrates that fixed, specialized embeddings are critical for performance.

- Strength: Strong, statistically significant improvements over competitive delexicalized baselines across two datasets with differing noise profiles.

- Strength: Eliminates need for hand-crafted lexicons while maintaining or improving accuracy, supporting scalability to larger ontologies.

- Strength: Explicit modeling of system context (request/confirm) via gating captures pragmatic phenomena like yes/no confirmations effectively.

- Strength: Sensible parameter sharing and fixed embeddings promote generalization to unseen paraphrases and reduce data/engineering burden.

- Weakness: Organization and readability issues — key notions and DST formalism presented too late, making the paper hard for non-experts.

- Weakness: Notation introduced before definition (e.g., tq, ts, tv), hindering comprehension; symbols should be declared upfront.

- Weakness: The term “joint” is potentially misleading — unclear if NBT can be used as a standalone SLU or only within the DST loop.

- Weakness: Limited exploration of sequential utterance modeling; no RNN/LSTM encoder comparisons despite their effectiveness in SLU.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail on the corrected DSTC2 set (extent of corrections) and on the baseline’s semantic dictionary (size, construction cost); leaderboard positioning unclear.

- Weakness: Lack of qualitative error analysis and concrete examples illustrating where NBT fixes delexicalization errors.

- Question: Provide statistics on the DSTC2 corrections and report performance versus prior DSTC2 entries to contextualize reported gains.

- Question: Clarify whether NBT can operate as a separate SLU module or is exclusively a joint tracker.

- Question: Report sizes and creation costs of the semantic dictionaries used by baseline systems.

- Question: Explain why RNN/LSTM utterance encoders were not explored and whether they might help on longer utterances.

- Question: Include concrete examples where NBT corrects delexicalization errors and report sensitivity to λ and N-best depth.
