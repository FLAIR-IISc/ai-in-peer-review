- Summary: Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word vectors to perform dialogue state tracking without SLU modules or hand-crafted lexicons, classifying each slot-value pair via learned utterance, context, and candidate representations (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN).

- Contribution 1: Proposes a data-driven DST framework that replaces brittle delexicalization and semantic dictionaries by leveraging pre-trained word embeddings to handle lexical variation.

- Contribution 2: Provides comprehensive empirical validation on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, including ablations showing higher-quality embeddings (GloVe/Paragram) improve performance.

- Strength 1: Addresses scalability by removing the need for manual semantic dictionaries, making DST more deployable across domains.

- Strength 2: Strong experimental design and results showing NBT matches or outperforms state-of-the-art, especially when lexicons are unavailable.

- Strength 3: Clear, interpretable architecture that separates utterance, candidate, and context representations and models their interactions explicitly.

- Weakness 1: Paper clarity and organization need improvement; task and some notation are introduced late or unclearly.

- Weakness 2: Limited exploration of sequential encoders—no RNN/LSTM variant evaluated despite their relevance for sequence modeling.

- Weakness 3: “Joint SLU/DST” label may be misleading since the model discriminatively classifies ontology slot-values rather than producing full SLU frames.

- Question 1: Did you try an RNN/LSTM utterance encoder and how would it compare to the CNN, especially on WOZ 2.0?

- Question 2: How large is the baseline semantic dictionary (e.g., number of unique paraphrases) to quantify manual effort avoided?

- Question 3: How extensive were the manual spelling corrections in DSTC2 (turns/dialogues affected), and are baseline comparisons on the uncorrected dataset comparable?

- Question 4: Can you provide qualitative test examples where the baseline without a semantic dictionary fails but NBT succeeds?
