- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT): a DST method that uses pre-trained word embeddings to match utterances to slot-value pairs, avoiding delexicalization and hand-crafted lexicons.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, outperforming delexicalization baselines and matching dictionary-augmented baselines.  
- Analyzes different pre-trained embeddings and finds semantically specialized vectors (Paragram-SL999) give the best results.  
- Strength: Replacing brittle delexicalization with learned embeddings addresses a major scalability bottleneck for task-oriented dialogue.  
- Strength: Rigorous experiments convincingly show NBT outperforms standard baselines and learns semantic relationships without manual dictionaries.  
- Strength: Ablation study on embedding spaces strengthens conclusions and offers practical guidance on embedding choice.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to two simple restaurant datasets, leaving scalability to larger, more complex ontologies untested.  
- Weakness: Missing comparison to a plausible slot-tagging + embedding-similarity baseline that first extracts candidate spans then compares embeddings.  
- Weakness: Context/coreference handling is unclear—described context module may be insufficient for resolving pronouns or multiple entities.  
- Question: Please clarify whether and how NBT performs coreference resolution (e.g., resolving “it” to a previously mentioned restaurant) or if that capability is out of scope.
