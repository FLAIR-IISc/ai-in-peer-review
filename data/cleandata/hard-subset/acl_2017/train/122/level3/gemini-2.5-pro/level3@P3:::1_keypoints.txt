- Proposes the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), a DST model that uses pre-trained word embeddings instead of delexicalization; presents NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN and evaluates on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, matching or beating baselines (especially without hand-crafted lexicons).  
- Main contribution: a neural DST framework that composes pretrained embeddings to compare utterances with ontology slot-value pairs, avoiding manual delexicalization and lexicons.  
- Main contribution: empirical evidence that semantically-specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) substantially improve DST performance over GloVe or random vectors.  
- Strength: elegant, scalable design that replaces brittle hand-crafted features with learned semantic representations suitable for linguistic variation.  
- Strength: convincing experiments showing NBT outperforms baselines without lexicons and matches lexicon-augmented baselines, validating the core hypothesis.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of embedding types, demonstrating the importance of semantically-specialized vectors for this task.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to two small restaurant-finding datasets with closed slot values, so scalability claims to complex or open-ended domains are speculative.  
- Weakness: context/coreference handling appears limited (e.g., grounding entity mentions like “The House” after “Where is it?”), suggesting insufficiency for multi-entity dialogues.  
- Weakness: no comparison to alternative architectures such as sequence tagging + span-level similarity, so the single-vector utterance representation may be suboptimal or noise-sensitive.  
- Question: How does NBT handle tracking specific entity names introduced during dialogue (coreference resolution for entities not in predefined slots)?  
- Question: Have you considered a two-stage approach (sequence tagging to identify value spans, then semantic comparison) as a baseline or alternative?  
- Question: Did you try fine-tuning embeddings on task data to capture domain-specific synonyms, and if so, what were the effects versus keeping embeddings fixed?
