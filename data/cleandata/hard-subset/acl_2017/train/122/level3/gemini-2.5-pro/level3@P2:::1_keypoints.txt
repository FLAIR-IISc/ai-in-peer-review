- Summary: Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), a lexicon-free DST using pre-trained word embeddings to represent utterances, system acts, and slot-value pairs; presents NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN and evaluates on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, matching or exceeding lexicon-based baselines.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel lexicon-free DST framework that composes continuous embeddings instead of relying on hand-crafted semantic lexicons or delexicalization.

- Contribution 2: Provides strong empirical validation showing state-of-the-art performance on two datasets and consistent gains over delexicalization baselines.

- Contribution 3: Analyzes impact of embedding quality (random vs GloVe vs Paragram-SL999), showing semantically specialized vectors significantly improve DST performance.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated and novel approach that shares parameters across slots and leverages embedding semantics to reduce dependency on handcrafted resources.

- Strength 2: Convincing, comprehensive experiments across datasets with different characteristics and appropriate baselines; results are statistically significant.

- Strength 3: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful diagrams and a solid literature contextualization.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation limited to a simple restaurant domain with only three informable slots, so scalability claims to large domains with huge ontologies are not fully substantiated.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison to span-based slot-tagging pipeline models (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF) that also use embeddings and might offer more precise local extraction.

- Weakness 3: Unclear whether NBT handles entity-centric tracking and coreference (e.g., resolving "it" to a specific restaurant), potentially oversimplifying dialogue state.

- Question 1: How does iterating over every slot-value pair scale to domains with hundreds of slots or thousands of values, and is the computational cost prohibitive?

- Question 2: Does NBT track specific entities and resolve coreference (e.g., restaurant names) or is it limited to predefined slot-value constraints?

- Question 3: Why was a pipeline approach with a modern neural sequence tagger for slot filling not included as a baseline, given its potential advantages?
