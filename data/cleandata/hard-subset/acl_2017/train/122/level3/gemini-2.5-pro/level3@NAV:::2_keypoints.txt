- Paper: Proposes the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), which uses pre-trained word vectors to represent utterances, system acts, and ontology entries and classifies slot-value pairs (models: NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN).

- Strength: Improved scalability — avoids hand-crafted semantic dictionaries by leveraging word-vector semantic similarity for domain adaptation.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance — significantly outperforms a delexicalization baseline on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 and matches a baseline with a semantic dictionary.

- Strength: Novel and sound framework — learns separate representations for user input, system context, and candidate slot-value pairs before combining them for decisions.

- Weakness: Poor readability and organization — lacks a formal DST definition and introduces key notation before defining it, which hinders comprehension.

- Weakness: Lack of model exploration — does not evaluate RNNs/LSTMs for utterance encoding, omitting comparison to strong sequential models.

- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative analysis — no example dialogues showing cases where NBT succeeds and the delexicalized baseline fails.

- Q: Clarify "joint SLU/DST" — can the model perform sequence labeling/value extraction or only classification over predefined slot-value pairs?

- Q: Provide statistics on DSTC2 corrections — how extensive were the dataset corrections and how do results compare on the original test set?

- Q: Describe the baseline semantic dictionary — what is its size and how much manual effort was required to create it?
