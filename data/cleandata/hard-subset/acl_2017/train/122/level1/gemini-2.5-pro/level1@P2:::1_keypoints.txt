- Proposes the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), a DST model that classifies every slot-value pair per turn using pre-trained word embeddings to avoid delexicalisation and SLU labels.  
- Main contribution: a scalable, data-driven DST architecture that replaces hand-crafted semantic dictionaries with learned representations.  
- Shows that semantically specialized word embeddings (Paragram-SL999) improve DST performance over standard embeddings and random initializations.  
- Empirical validation: NBT matches or exceeds strong baselines on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, and outperforms them when lexicons are removed.  
- Strength: high novelty and significance by moving away from explicit delexicalisation toward learned semantic representations.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation using two diverse datasets (DSTC2 with ASR noise, WOZ 2.0 with typed input) and strong baselines with/without semantic dictionaries.  
- Strength: insightful analysis showing the model’s success depends on high-quality word vectors, not just architecture.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with helpful diagrams and thoughtful interpretation of results.  
- Weakness: scalability bottleneck since the model requires a separate forward pass for every slot-value pair, which is impractical for very large ontologies.  
- Weakness: utterance encoders (sum of n-grams and CNN) discard sequential/positional information and omit RNN-based encoders like LSTMs/GRUs.  
- Weakness: belief update is a non-learned linear interpolation, failing to model dialogue dynamics or corrections across turns.  
- Question: how would the approach scale to domains with thousands of values per slot, and have alternative output layers (e.g., generation or single-slot classification) been considered?  
- Question: were RNN encoders (LSTM/GRU) tried or considered for better sequential modeling, and how might they compare to NBT-CNN?  
- Question: in system-request gating, why does the gate use only the candidate slot (cs) and not the candidate value (cv), potentially treating different values for the same slot identically?
