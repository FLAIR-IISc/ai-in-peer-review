- Proposes NBT that uses pre-trained word vectors to handle lexical/morphological variation and eliminate hand-crafted semantic lexicons.  
- Couples SLU and DST so the model jointly reasons about user intent and updates belief state.  
- Demonstrates matching state-of-the-art performance with lexicons and outperforming them when lexicons are absent.  
- Robustly handles lexical variation via pre-trained embeddings.  
- Eliminates manual semantic lexicons, improving scalability to larger/complex domains.  
- Shows improved performance on noisy ASR-containing data (e.g., DSTC2).  
- Performance depends on quality and availability of pre-trained word vectors, which may be lacking for some domains/languages.  
- Evaluation is limited to only two datasets, raising concerns about generalizability.  
- No comparison to other neural models (e.g., attention-based or memory network approaches).  
- Asks how authors will handle unavailable or domain-specific/poor-quality pre-trained vectors.  
- Requests more details on hyperparameter tuning (optimizer, learning rate, number of hidden layers).  
- Asks how NBT can be extended to multi-domain or morphologically complex dialogue scenarios.
