- Proposes NBT: a neural belief-tracking framework that composes pre-trained word vectors into utterance and context representations, coupling SLU and DST.  
- Eliminates reliance on hand-crafted semantic lexicons by learning semantic composition from data.  
- Learns efficiently, handling variation without large annotated datasets or manual semantic dictionaries.  
- Matches or outperforms state-of-the-art models on downstream tasks, especially when lexicons are unavailable.  
- Novel perspective leveraging pre-trained embeddings and composition for belief tracking.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on two datasets, surpassing delexicalisation-based approaches.  
- Data-efficient: reduces need for extensive annotation and manual lexicon construction.  
- Flexible and scalable across dialogue domains and robust to linguistic variation.  
- Limited evaluation scope: tested on only two datasets, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Narrow baselines: comparisons are only to delexicalisation-based models, not to RNNs or transformers.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of learned representations and hyperparameter impacts.  
- Question: How are out-of-vocabulary words or words absent from pre-trained vectors handled?  
- Question: Can NBTs handle domains/languages with rich morphological variation and long multi-turn dialogue context?
