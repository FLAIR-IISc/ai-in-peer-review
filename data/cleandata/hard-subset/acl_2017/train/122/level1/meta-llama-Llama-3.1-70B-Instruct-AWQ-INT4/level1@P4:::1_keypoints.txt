- Summary: Proposes Neural Belief Tracking (NBT) that composes pre-trained word vectors into utterance/context representations, matching SOTA with lexicons and outperforming it without them.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel NBT framework that leverages pre-trained word vectors for dialogue state tracking.

- Contribution 2: Proposes efficient learning via semantic information from embeddings and shared parameters across ontology values.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates robust performance on two datasets, handling semantic/linguistic variation without hand-crafted lexicons.

- Strength 1: Improved language understanding from pre-trained embeddings yields better DST performance.

- Strength 2: Efficient learning and parameter sharing make the approach practical for real-world use.

- Strength 3: Robust to semantic relations and linguistic variation, reducing reliance on manual lexicons.

- Weakness 1: Heavy dependence on the availability and quality of pre-trained word vectors across languages/domains.

- Weakness 2: Limited evaluation only on two datasets, raising questions about generalizability.

- Weakness 3: Lacks comparison to other contemporary deep learning models for dialogue state tracking.

- Question 1: How will the NBT framework be extended to multi-domain systems and non-English languages?

- Question 2: What specific pre-trained word vectors were used and how were they trained?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to address dependence on embeddings and expand evaluation beyond two datasets?
