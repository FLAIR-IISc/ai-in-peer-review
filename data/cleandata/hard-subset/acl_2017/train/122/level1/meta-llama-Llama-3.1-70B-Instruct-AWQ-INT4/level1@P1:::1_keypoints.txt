- Proposes a Neural Belief Tracking (NBT) framework that couples SLU and DST using pre-trained word vectors to handle lexical/morphological variation and remove hand-crafted semantic lexicons.
- Claims matching or outperforming state-of-the-art models on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, especially when semantic lexicons are unavailable.
- Introduces a novel NBT framework integrating SLU and DST with pre-trained embeddings to eliminate hand-crafted lexicons.
- Demonstrates improved performance on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 versus models that rely on semantic lexicons.
- Argues the NBT model is scalable to larger, more complex dialogue domains where manual lexicons are infeasible.
- Effectively leverages pre-trained word vectors to handle lexical/morphological variation and remove semantic lexicons.
- Shows empirical performance gains on two benchmark datasets compared to lexicon-dependent baselines.
- Claims scalability to new domains and languages, easing deployment in larger systems.
- Exhibits robustness to ASR errors, as evidenced by DSTC2 results.
- Offers flexibility for adaptation across different dialogue domains and languages.
- Evaluation is limited to only two datasets (DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0), which may not generalize across domains.
- Heavy dependence on pre-trained word vectors could limit applicability in languages or domains lacking quality embeddings.
- Lacks detailed ablation or analysis of individual NBT components to attribute performance gains.
- Compares against a limited set of baselines, omitting broader state-of-the-art alternatives.
- Provides little discussion of concrete real-world applications or deployment scenarios.
