- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word vectors to improve dialogue state tracking without hand-crafted lexicons.
- NBT couples Spoken Language Understanding (SLU) and Dialogue State Tracking (DST) within one framework.
- NBT leverages pre-trained word embeddings to handle lexical and morphological variation.
- Eliminates the need for hand-crafted semantic lexicons, improving scalability to larger domains.
- Demonstrates improved or matching performance on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 compared to baselines.
- Shows robustness to different linguistic expressions due to use of pre-trained embeddings.
- More scalable since it does not require manual lexicon construction.
- Performance depends heavily on the quality of the pre-trained word vectors.
- Evaluation is limited to only two datasets, which may not generalize across domains.
- Lacks comparisons to a broader set of state-of-the-art models.
- How will NBT be extended to multi-domain dialogue systems?
- Please provide more details on the pre-training process for the word vectors.
- How will the model handle out-of-vocabulary words?
