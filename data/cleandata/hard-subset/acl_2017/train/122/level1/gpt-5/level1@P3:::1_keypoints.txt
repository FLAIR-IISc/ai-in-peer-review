- Presents Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), replacing delexicalisation/dictionaries with reasoning over pre-trained word vectors, using NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN to score slot–value candidates per turn with simple context gating and an ASR N-best convex update.

- Contribution: Empirically shows semantically-specialised word vectors enable lexicon-free joint SLU/DST matching or exceeding delexicalisation baselines on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0.

- Contribution: Proposes a simple, scalable architecture sharing parameters across slot values with element-wise utterance–candidate interaction and lightweight last-system-act context gating.

- Contribution: Demonstrates that Paragram-SL999 semantic specialisation improves DST performance, provides cleaned DSTC2 transcripts and expanded WOZ 2.0 data.

- Strength: Strong, consistent gains over delexicalisation baselines without hand-crafted lexicons, especially on richer WOZ language.

- Strength: Architectural choices (fixed embeddings, single classifier, r ⊙ c interaction) encourage paraphrase robustness and efficient parameter sharing.

- Strength: Valuable analysis showing semantic-specialised embeddings (Paragram) outperform GloVe/random in noisy ASR settings.

- Strength: Practical, simple, reproducible system with two encoder variants offering speed/expressivity trade-offs and a tunable ASR N-best integration.

- Weakness: Very limited context modeling (only last system act), hindering handling of cross-turn references, corrections, long-distance dependencies, and complex negation.

- Weakness: ASR robustness concerns—large gap to oracle transcripts and simplistic N-best fusion (hand-tuned λ) with no token-level confidences or learned update dynamics.

- Weakness: Scalability issues—linear iteration over all slot–value pairs, no candidate pruning or hierarchical modeling, and unclear handling of class imbalance from many negatives.

- Weakness: Potential brittleness to OOVs, ASR variants, and multiword values (simple summation), and unclear treatment of special values like dontcare/none.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons/ablations versus attention/memory-based trackers and limited analysis isolating context gates versus semantic decoding or slot-specific parameter effects.

- Question: How does runtime scale with ontology size and utterance length for NBT-DNN vs NBT-CNN, and is any candidate pruning used at inference?

- Question: How are dontcare/none and negation handled beyond Confirm acts—are there special embeddings or rules?

- Question: What is the OOV strategy for ASR/domain-specific terms—subword/char backoff or unknown token averaging—and sensitivity to OOV rate?

- Question: How are request predictions represented and trained (REQ=slot), and do requests share the same architecture as goals?

- Question: Please provide ablations for (i) context gates removed, (ii) learned c vs raw (cs+cv), (iii) fixed vs fine-tuned embeddings, and (iv) slot-specific vs shared parameters.

- Question: Did you try learned belief updates (e.g., RNN over turns) or token-level ASR features (confusion networks), and how much headroom remains versus the λ rule?

- Additional: A low-rank bilinear interaction could offer more capacity than element-wise product without many parameters.

- Additional: Scalability claims would be stronger with experiments on a larger or multi-domain ontology, even if synthetic.

- Additional: Clarify significance testing procedure (dialogue-level vs turn-level and multiple comparisons correction).
