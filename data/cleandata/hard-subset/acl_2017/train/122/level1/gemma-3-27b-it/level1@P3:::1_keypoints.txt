- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word vectors to couple SLU and DST without SLU annotations or hand-crafted lexicons.  
- Introduces two NBT variants (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN), with the CNN variant performing particularly well.  
- Demonstrates competitive results on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, especially strong on WOZ 2.0 when lexicons are unavailable.  
- Contribution: novel framework that bypasses explicit SLU and delexicalization by reasoning over utterance semantics with word vectors.  
- Contribution: provides two architectural implementations showing the approach is realizable with different models.  
- Contribution: empirical validation across two datasets, highlighting generalization to more natural language (WOZ 2.0).  
- Strength: addresses major scalability challenge by reducing reliance on annotated data and manual lexicons.  
- Strength: strong experimental results and ablation on word vector types supporting claims.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful architecture diagrams and concise results.  
- Strength: insightful analysis on the benefits of semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999).  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis identifying failure cases or difficult utterance types.  
- Weakness: simplistic context modelling relying only on the previous system utterance rather than richer dialogue history.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons — does not evaluate against recent end-to-end/transformer-based dialogue models.  
- Weakness: no discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity or tuning details for reproducibility.  
- Question: request for detailed error analysis specifying utterance types where NBT fails.  
- Question: ask whether more sophisticated context models (recurrent/attention) were tried and their results.  
- Question: ask for comparison of NBT performance to recent end-to-end dialogue models on these datasets.  
- Question: ask about sensitivity of NBT performance to hyperparameters (learning rate, embedding dimension, etc.).  
- Additional: include a table summarizing hyperparameters used in experiments.  
- Additional: discuss potential benefits of fine-tuning embeddings versus keeping them fixed.
