- Novel end-to-end neural DST that uses pre-trained word embeddings to avoid delexicalisation and hand-crafted lexicons.  
- Effective use of semantically-specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) to handle lexical variation in DST.  
- Strong empirical results: NBT-DNN/CNN outperform strong baselines on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 with statistical significance.  
- Tackles reliance on manual resources and separate SLU, addressing a key scalability challenge for new domains.  
- Well-motivated approach that clearly articulates drawbacks of prior methods and positions NBT as a viable solution.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on established datasets with comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Ablation study shows the importance of semantic information in pre-trained word vectors.  
- Well-written, detailed model description covering representation learning and context modeling.  
- Paper organization and accessibility issues reduce readability, especially for readers new to DST.  
- Lacks a high-level system diagram showing DST’s role within a complete dialogue system (ASR, SLU, DST, Policy).  
- Notational clarity problems: several symbols introduced before being defined.  
- The term “Joint SLU/DST” may be misleading; unclear if the model can perform standalone SLU.  
- Limited error analysis; paper should include specific examples of errors corrected by NBT vs baseline.  
- Question: Can the proposed model be used for separate SLU tasks or is it inherently tied to DST?  
- Question: How many errors were corrected in the original DSTC2 dataset and how does that affect prior comparisons?  
- Question: Were sequential models (RNNs/LSTMs) explored for utterance representation, and what were the results?  
- Question: Please provide details on the size and content of the semantic dictionary used by baseline models.  
- Suggestion: Provide a more intuitive explanation of the element-wise multiplication (Equation 8) and its role.  
- Suggestion: Add a table summarizing key differences between NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN to highlight respective strengths.
