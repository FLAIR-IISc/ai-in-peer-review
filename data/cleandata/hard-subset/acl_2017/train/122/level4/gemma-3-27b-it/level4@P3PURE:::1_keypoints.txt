- Proposes dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained embeddings for slot values and composing them into distributed utterance and context representations.
- Experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline.
- Authors compare multiple pre-training strategies for embeddings, including XAVIER, GloVe, and Program-SL999.
- Datasets appear limited in scope, raising concerns about generalization to more complex dialogue domains.
- Unclear how the method performs with or without a separate slot-tagging component in complex dialogues.
- Suggests constraining similarity computation to spans of potential slot values when estimating utterance–value similarity, useful even without direct matches.
- Intro examples are misleading and the shown dialogue state is incomplete (e.g., missing restaurant_name=The House).
- Questions remain about the impact of coreference resolution on task performance.
- Overall assessment: using pre-trained embeddings is a strong, promising approach.
