**Strengths:**

This work introduces a new dialogue state tracking method leveraging pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values, and a learning mechanism to combine these into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. The experimental results, conducted on two datasets, demonstrate clear and statistically significant gains over existing delexicalization-based baseline methods. The investigation of different pre-training techniques for the word embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999) is also a positive aspect of this research.

**Weaknesses:**

While the use of embeddings is motivated by improved generalization to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization strategies may struggle, the evaluation currently relies on relatively limited datasets. It would be valuable to assess the performance of this approach – with and without a dedicated slot tagging component – on more challenging and realistic dialogue scenarios. For instance, similarity calculations between utterances and slot value pairs could be refined by focusing solely on the span of the identified slot values, even in cases of mismatched values. 

Additionally, the example provided in the introduction appears incomplete; a complete dialogue state should arguably include "restaurant_name=The House." This observation raises a further question concerning the impact of coreference resolution on the accuracy of this task.

**General Discussion:**

Overall, the incorporation of pre-trained word embeddings is a strong conceptual advance, and the specific methodology developed here for utilizing them is promising.
