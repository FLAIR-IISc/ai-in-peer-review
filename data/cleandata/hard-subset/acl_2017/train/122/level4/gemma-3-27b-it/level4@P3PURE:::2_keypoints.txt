- Proposes a neural network framework for dialogue state tracking that learns representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.  
- Compares two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.  
- Combines learned representations in a downstream network to make binary decisions for each slot–value pair.  
- Reports significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall assessment: quality work with clear goal, reasonable approach, and improved results.  
- Paper organization and clarity need improvement, especially for readers unfamiliar with DST.  
- Recommend adding a formal definition of dialogue state tracking and a general dialogue system architecture early in the paper.  
- Notations (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) are introduced before being defined; suggest defining all symbols earlier.  
- Question whether the model can perform separate SLU—clarify use of the term “joint.”  
- Request statistics on how many DSTC2 dataset errors were corrected and comparisons to other published DSTC2 entries if corrections are few.  
- Suggest exploring RNNs/LSTMs for modeling sequential aspects of utterances given their SLU success.  
- Ask for more details on the semantic dictionary used by the baseline to illustrate manual cost.  
- Recommend including example instances where the baseline fails but the proposed models succeed.
