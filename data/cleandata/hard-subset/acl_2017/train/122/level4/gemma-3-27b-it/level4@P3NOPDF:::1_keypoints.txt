- Proposes using pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values and compose them into utterance/context representations for dialogue state tracking.  
- Core idea of leveraging embeddings to capture semantic similarity and improve generalization is a strong contribution.  
- Shows consistent, significant improvements over a strong delexicalization baseline on two datasets.  
- Thorough comparison of different pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Concern about generalizability: evaluated datasets are relatively limited and may not reflect complex dialogue domains.  
- Recommends evaluation on more challenging datasets with varied slot types, dialogue acts, and contextual dependencies.  
- Questions benefit of adding a dedicated slot tagging component, especially for complex dialogues and span-restricted similarity estimation.  
- Suggests restricting similarity calculation to slot value spans could improve accuracy when direct matches are absent.  
- Example in the paper is incomplete—dialogue state should explicitly include slot assignments (e.g., restaurant_name=The House).  
- Paper does not address coreference resolution and its impact on tracking accuracy; this is a notable omission.  
- Overall assessment: promising, well-motivated method with substantial gains, but needs expanded evaluation and clarifications to strengthen impact.  
- Recommended actions: expand evaluation, investigate slot tagging, address coreference handling, and clarify examples.
