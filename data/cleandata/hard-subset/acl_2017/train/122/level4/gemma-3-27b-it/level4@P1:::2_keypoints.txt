- Proposes a neural-network framework for dialogue state tracking that learns representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries from pre-trained word vectors.
- Compares two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.
- Combines learned representations to make binary decisions for each slot–value pair.
- Reports significant performance gains over a delexicalization-based baseline.
- Overall assessment: quality work with a clear goal, reasonable approach, and improved results.
- Paper organization needs improvement to better convey details, especially for non-experts.
- Recommend providing an earlier, formal definition of DST and a general dialogue system architecture showing relationships with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
- Suggest defining all notation earlier (symbols like t_q, t_s, t_v are used before being explained).
- Question whether the model can perform SLU separately; calling it “joint” may be misleading if it cannot.
- Ask for statistics on how many errors were corrected in the cleaned DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other published work on the same cleaned set.
- Recommend evaluating recurrent models (RNNs/LSTMs) to capture sequential aspects of utterance representations.
- Request more details about the semantic dictionary used with the baseline to illustrate manual construction costs.
- Suggest providing qualitative examples where the baseline fails but the proposed models succeed.
