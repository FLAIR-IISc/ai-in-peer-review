- Proposes a novel dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained embeddings for slot values and composing them into utterance/context representations.  
- Experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant gains over a delexicalization-based baseline.  
- Investigates several pre-training strategies for embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Concern that datasets are limited, weakening the claim about generalization to more complex dialogue domains.  
- Questions performance differences with and without a separate slot-tagging component on intricate dialogues.  
- Suggests constraining similarity computation to the span of slot values when comparing utterances and slot-value pairs.  
- Notes introduction examples may be misleading (e.g., missing “restaurant_name=The House” in the dialogue state).  
- Raises concern about how coreference resolution affects task performance.  
- Overall endorsement: using pre-trained embeddings and the chosen integration method is compelling.
