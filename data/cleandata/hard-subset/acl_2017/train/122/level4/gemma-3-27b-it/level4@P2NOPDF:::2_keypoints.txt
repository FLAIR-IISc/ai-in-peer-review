- Introduces a neural network framework for dialogue state tracking (DST) using pre-trained word vectors to learn representations of user utterances, system responses, and ontology entries.
- Evaluates two utterance representation architectures: Neural Bag-of-Tokens with DNN (NBT-DNN) and with CNN (NBT-CNN).
- Combines learned representations in a downstream network to predict binary slot-value pairs and shows significant gains over a delexicalized baseline.
- Work is sound with a clear objective, reasonable approach, and demonstrable improvements.
- Paper organization and clarity need improvement, especially for readers unfamiliar with the field.
- Needs a formal definition of DST early (Section 1) and a general dialogue system architecture overview to distinguish DST from SLU, ASR, and policy learning.
- Notations (e.g., t_q, t_s, t_v) should be defined earlier to avoid confusion.
- Clarify whether the model supports performing SLU independently or whether the term “joint” is misleading.
- Provide error-analysis statistics, including the number of corrections relative to DSTC2 and comparisons to other published DSTC2 results.
- Consider sequential models (RNNs/LSTMs) for capturing utterance sequence information, given their SLU success.
- Give more details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to illustrate manual resource cost.
- Include qualitative examples of dialogue turns fixed by the proposed models but missed by the baseline to show concrete improvements.
