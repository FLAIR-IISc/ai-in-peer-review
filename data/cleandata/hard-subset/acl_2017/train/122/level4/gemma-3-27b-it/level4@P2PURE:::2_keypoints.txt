- Proposes a neural framework for dialogue state tracking that learns representations for user utterances, system responses, and ontology entries, initialized with pre-trained word vectors.
- Compares two utterance-encoding architectures (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN) and combines their representations to make binary slot–value decisions.
- Shows significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline.
- Overall assessment: solid, clear objective, reasonable approach, and empirical improvement over prior work.
- Major organization issue: paper should more clearly define dialogue state tracking (DST) up front and separate it from semantic language understanding (SLU).
- Recommend presenting a general dialogue system architecture and formally defining DST and its relation to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
- Notations appear before definitions (e.g., tq, ts, t_v); define all symbols earlier to improve readability.
- Question whether the model performs separate SLU — calling it “joint” may be misleading if it cannot handle SLU independently.
- Ask for statistics on how many errors were corrected in the original DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other DSTC2 submissions if corrections are few.
- Suggest considering RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential information in utterance representations given their SLU success.
- Request more details about the semantic dictionary used by the baseline to clarify manual effort required.
- Recommend including example cases where the baseline fails but the proposed models succeed.
