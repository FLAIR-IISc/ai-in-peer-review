- Introduces a new dialogue state tracking method that uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values.  
- Proposes a learning mechanism to combine these embeddings into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context.  
- Experiments on two datasets show clear, statistically significant gains over delexicalization-based baselines.  
- Investigates different embedding pre-training techniques (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Evaluation is limited to relatively small datasets and should be tested on more challenging, realistic dialogue scenarios.  
- Recommends assessing performance both with and without a dedicated slot tagging component.  
- Suggests refining similarity calculations by focusing on the span of identified slot values, especially in mismatched cases.  
- Notes the introduction example appears incomplete (missing restaurant_name=The House) and raises concerns about coreference resolution effects.  
- Concludes that incorporating pre-trained word embeddings is a strong conceptual advance and the proposed methodology is promising.
