- Proposes a novel dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values.  
- Composes embeddings into distributed representations that capture user utterances and dialogue context.  
- Shows consistent, significant improvements over a strong delexicalization-based baseline on two datasets.  
- Evaluates different embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Evaluation datasets are relatively constrained and may not reflect complex dialogue domains.  
- Lacks a comparison of performance with and without a dedicated slot tagging component, especially for harder dialogues.  
- Suggests refining similarity computations to focus on span-level comparisons between utterances and slot-value pairs.  
- Intro example is unclear (dialogue state should arguably include “restaurant_name=The House”), raising questions about coreference handling.  
- Needs analysis of how coreference resolution affects state-tracking accuracy; addressing these issues would strengthen the paper.
