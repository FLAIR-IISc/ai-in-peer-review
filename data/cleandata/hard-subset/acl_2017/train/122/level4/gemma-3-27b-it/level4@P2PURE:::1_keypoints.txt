- Introduces a novel dialogue state tracking approach using pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and compose distributed representations of utterances and context.  
- Shows consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline on two datasets.  
- Evaluates impact of different embedding initializations (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Concerns that the evaluation datasets are limited in scope and may not reflect more complex dialogue domains.  
- Recommends testing performance on more challenging dialogues, both with and without a dedicated slot-tagging component.  
- Suggests restricting similarity calculations to the span of slot values (even when mismatched) could improve performance.  
- Notes introduction examples may be misleading; dialogue state should arguably include "restaurant_name=The House".  
- Raises the related question of how coreference resolution affects the task and the proposed method.  
- Concludes pre-trained word embeddings are a strong contribution and the methodology is promising.
