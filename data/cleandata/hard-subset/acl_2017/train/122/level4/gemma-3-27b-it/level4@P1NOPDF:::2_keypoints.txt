- Paper proposes a neural network framework for dialogue state tracking (DST) that learns representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.  
- Authors compare two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN, and feed combined representations into a classifier that makes binary decisions for each slot-value pair.  
- Experiments show significant improvements over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with clear objective, reasonable approach, and improved results.  
- Main organizational criticism: paper needs clearer presentation, especially for readers unfamiliar with DST.  
- Recommend adding a formal definition of DST and a general dialogue system architecture early in the paper to clarify relationships with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Notations (e.g., t_q, t_s, t_v) are used before being defined; suggest defining all symbols in an earlier section.  
- Question whether the model can perform SLU separately—if not, calling it “joint” may be misleading.  
- Request statistics on the number of corrected errors in the DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other published DSTC2 submissions if corrections are few.  
- Suggest considering RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential information for utterance representations given their success in SLU.  
- Ask for more details about the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to illustrate manual resource costs.  
- Recommend adding example cases where the baseline fails but the proposed models succeed to illustrate strengths.
