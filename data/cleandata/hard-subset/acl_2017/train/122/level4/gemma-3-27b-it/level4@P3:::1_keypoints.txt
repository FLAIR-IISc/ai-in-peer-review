- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) using pre-trained word embeddings to reduce reliance on annotated data and hand-crafted lexicons.  
- Composes pre-trained word vectors to represent utterances and context, reasoning via semantic similarity instead of exact matching.  
- Jointly models SLU and DST without delexicalization, avoiding a known scalability bottleneck.  
- Evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, achieving competitive or superior performance to delexicalization baselines, especially when lexicons are unavailable.  
- Thorough empirical comparison of embedding strategies (random, GloVe, Paragram-SL999) showing benefits of semantically-specialized embeddings.  
- Strong empirical results with consistent improvements in low-resource settings.  
- Novel composition and interaction mechanisms for leveraging dialogue context are well-motivated and effective.  
- Ablation study gives useful insights into the impact of embedding choice, highlighting Paragram-SL999.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful architectural diagrams.  
- Datasets (DSTC2, WOZ 2.0) are limited in complexity and scale; generalization to larger, more complex domains is uncertain.  
- Current similarity computation uses the whole utterance and could benefit from slot-focused attention to target relevant spans.  
- Example dialogue state omits explicit entity (restaurant_name), raising questions about coreference handling.  
- Limited analysis of error cases; more detailed breakdown of failure modes is needed.  
- Question: How does NBT perform on larger ontologies and more complex dialogue domains, and what challenges are anticipated?  
- Question: Have the authors experimented with attention mechanisms to focus on relevant utterance spans for slot-value matching?  
- Question: How does the model handle coreference resolution and its effect on DST accuracy?  
- Question: Can authors provide a detailed error analysis contrasting NBT and delexicalization-based errors?  
- Suggestion: Notation could be more consistent (use subscripts to differentiate vectors vs. matrices).  
- Suggestion: Explore richer context modeling beyond only the previous system utterance.
