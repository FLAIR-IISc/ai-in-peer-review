- Introduces a neural network framework for dialogue state tracking (DST) that learns representations for user utterances, system responses, and ontology entries initialized with pre-trained word vectors.  
- Compares two architectures for utterance representation: Neural Bag-of-Tokens DNN (NBT-DNN) and CNN (NBT-CNN).  
- Combines learned representations to predict binary values for each slot-value pair in DST.  
- Demonstrates significant improvement over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall contribution is sound but paper organization reduces clarity for readers unfamiliar with DST.  
- Recommend restructuring: begin with a goal-oriented dialogue system overview, then a formal DST problem definition and its relation to ASR, SLU, and policy.  
- Add a comprehensive notation list early; several symbols (e.g., t_q, t_s, t_v) are used before being defined.  
- Clarify what “joint” modeling means—whether the model can perform SLU separately or truly handles both tasks independently.  
- Provide error analysis and statistics on corrections in the DSTC2 dataset, and compare to other DSTC2 publications if corrections are limited.  
- Consider exploring sequential models (RNNs/LSTMs) instead of bag-of-tokens to capture utterance order and potentially improve performance.  
- Give more detail on the baseline semantic dictionary, including the manual effort and resource cost to build it.  
- Include qualitative examples where the proposed model fixes baseline errors to illustrate practical benefits.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions after addressing the above points.
