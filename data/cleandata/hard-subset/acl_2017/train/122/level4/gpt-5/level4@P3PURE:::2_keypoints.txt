- Presents a neural-network DST framework that learns representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries from pre-trained word vectors; compares NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN and uses these to make binary slot–value decisions, showing significant gains over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall a solid contribution with clear goals, reasonable ideas, and improved results over prior work.  
- Paper organization and presentation are unclear; needs an earlier formal definition of DST and clearer placement of dialogue system architecture to clarify relationships with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Notations are introduced too late (e.g., t_q, t_s, t_v); define symbols early to improve readability.  
- Clarify whether SLU can be handled as a separate component—using “joint” may be misleading if the model does not address SLU separately.  
- Report statistics on how many errors were corrected in the DSTC2 dataset and, if few, compare to other published DSTC2 results.  
- Discuss pros/cons of using RNNs or LSTMs to capture sequential information in utterance representations given their success in SLU.  
- Provide more details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline, including the manual effort required to build it.  
- Include qualitative examples where the baseline mispredicts but the proposed models succeed to illustrate the source of performance gains.
