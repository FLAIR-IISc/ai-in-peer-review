- Strengths:
This paper presents a novel approach to dialogue state tracking that represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and learns to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. Experiments on two datasets show consistent and significant improvements over a prior delexicalization-based baseline. The paper also investigates alternative approaches for pre-training word embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).

- Weaknesses:
Although a key motivation for using embeddings is to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale, the datasets used appear limited. I wonder how the approach would perform with and without a separate slot-tagging component on more complex dialogues. For example, when computing similarity between the utterance and slot–value pairs, one could restrict the estimation to the span corresponding to the slot values; this should be applicable even when the values do not match. I also find the examples in the introduction misleading—shouldn’t the dialogue state include “restaurant_name=The House”? This raises another question: how does coreference resolution affect this task?

- General Discussion:
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a strong idea, and the specific approach is promising.
