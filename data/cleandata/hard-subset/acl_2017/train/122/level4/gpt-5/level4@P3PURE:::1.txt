- Strengths:
This paper proposes a novel approach to dialogue state tracking that leverages pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and learns to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. Experiments on two datasets show consistent and significant improvements over a prior delexicalization-based baseline. The authors also investigate alternative approaches for pre-training word embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).

- Weaknesses:
Although one of the main motivations for using embeddings is to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale, the datasets used here seem limited. I wonder how the approach would compare with and without a separate slot tagging component on more complex dialogues. For example, when computing similarity between the utterance and slot–value pairs, one could limit the estimation to the span of the slot values; this should be applicable even when the values do not match. I also find the examples in the introduction somewhat misleading: shouldn’t the dialogue state also include “restaurant_name=The House”? This raises another question: how does the resolution of coreferences impact this task?

- General Discussion:
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a great idea, and the specific approach to leveraging them is exciting.
