- Novel approach: represent slot values with pre-trained embeddings and compose them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.  
- Empirical gains: consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline on two datasets.  
- Ablations: paper compares alternative pre-training methods for embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Dataset limitation: evaluated datasets appear limited, leaving unclear performance in more complex dialogue domains.  
- Slot-tagging question: unclear how the model performs with or without a separate slot-tagging component on complex dialogues.  
- Span-restriction idea: similarity computation could be restricted to spans corresponding to slot values, which might improve comparisons even when values differ.  
- Example accuracy/coreference: introductory examples may be misleading (missing restaurant_name=The House) and raise questions about handling coreference.  
- Overall verdict: using pre-trained word embeddings is a strong, promising idea.
