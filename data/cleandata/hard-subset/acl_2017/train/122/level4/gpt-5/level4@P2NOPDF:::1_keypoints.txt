- Novel DST method that represents slot values with pretrained embeddings and composes them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context.
- Consistent, statistically significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline across two datasets.
- Paper examines different embedding pretraining choices (e.g., XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).
- Evaluation datasets appear limited in scope relative to the claim of generalizing to more complex dialogue domains.
- Lacks comparison of variants with and without an explicit slot-tagging component on more complex dialogues.
- Restricting similarity computation to the span corresponding to the slot mention could be informative, even when surface forms differ.
- Introductory example seems misleading—dialogue state should include “restaurant_name=The House.”
- Unclear how coreference resolution influences performance and how it should be handled within the framework.
- Overall assessment: leveraging pretrained word embeddings for dialogue state tracking is a strong, promising idea.
