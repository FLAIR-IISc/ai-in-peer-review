- Strengths:
The paper presents a novel approach to dialogue state tracking that represents slot values with pretrained embeddings and learns to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. Across two datasets, the method delivers consistent and statistically significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline. The authors also examine different embedding pretraining choices (e.g., XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).

- Weaknesses:
While a key motivation is to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization does not scale, the evaluation datasets appear limited in scope. It would be valuable to compare variants with and without an explicit slot-tagging component on more complex dialogues. For example, when computing similarity between utterances and slot–value pairs, restricting the computation to the span corresponding to the slot mention could be informative, even when surface forms do not match. In addition, the introductory example seems misleading: shouldn’t the dialogue state also include “restaurant_name=The House”? This also raises the broader question of how coreference resolution influences performance and should be handled within the framework.

- General Discussion:
Overall, leveraging pretrained word embeddings for dialogue state tracking is a strong idea, and the specific integration proposed here is promising.
