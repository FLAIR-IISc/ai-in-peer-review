- Paper introduces a neural network framework for dialogue state tracking (DST).  
- Core contribution: learning representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries from pre-trained word vectors.  
- Two utterance-encoding architectures compared: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.  
- Representations are combined and fed to a downstream network making binary decisions for each slot–value pair.  
- Experiments show significant improvements over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall solid work with clear objective, reasonable approach, and better results than prior studies.  
- Paper organization and clarity are poor, especially for readers unfamiliar with the area.  
- Request to provide a formal definition of DST early in the paper.  
- Recommend presenting a general dialogue-system architecture in Section 1 and a precise DST problem definition highlighting relations with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Notations are introduced before being defined (e.g., tq, ts, t_v); define symbols earlier.  
- Question whether the model can perform separate SLU; “joint” may be misleading if it does not.  
- Ask for statistics on how many errors were corrected from the original DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other published DSTC2 entries.  
- Suggest exploring RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential aspects in utterance representations.  
- Request more details about the semantic dictionary baseline to clarify manual construction cost.  
- Ask to include example cases where the baseline mispredicted but the proposed models correctly resolved.
