- Proposes a novel DST method that represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.  
- Shows consistent and significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline on two datasets.  
- Explores alternative pre-training methods for word embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Datasets appear limited, raising questions about generalization to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale.  
- Lacks comparison with a separate slot-tagging component; restricting similarity computations to slot-value spans could improve performance and should be evaluated.  
- Introductory examples may be misleading (e.g., omission of "restaurant_name=The House"), and the impact of coreference resolution on the task is not addressed.  
- Overall, leveraging pre-trained embeddings is a strong and promising direction.
