- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT): a data-driven DST that reasons over pre-trained word embeddings and makes binary decisions per slot–value each turn.  
- Compares two variants: NBT-DNN (sum of n‑gram embeddings + shallow network) and NBT-CNN (convolutional filters over n‑grams).  
- Contribution: joint SLU/DST framework removing delexicalization/semantic dictionaries by operating in a semantically specialized embedding space with semantic decoding and context gating.  
- Contribution: context model conditioning on preceding system acts via learned similarity gates to handle implicit confirmations and pronominal/elliptical responses.  
- Contribution: strong empirical validation on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 with significant gains over delexicalized baselines and evidence that Paragram‑SL999 embeddings outperform GloVe/random.  
- Strength: reduces manual effort and improves scalability by eliminating semantic dictionaries, matching lexicon-enhanced models and outperforming when lexicons are absent.  
- Strength: well-motivated architecture decomposing representation learning, semantic decoding, and context modeling to address lexical variation and implicit feedback.  
- Strength: solid empirical evidence—statistically significant improvements in joint goal and request accuracy, especially on WOZ (richer language, no ASR noise).  
- Strength: practical training choices (fixed embeddings, shared parameters) improve generalization to unseen paraphrases and lower data requirements.  
- Weakness: organization and formalism are weak—DST task, SDS interface, and notation (e.g., tq, ts, tv) should be defined earlier and consolidated.  
- Weakness: ambiguity about what “joint” SLU/DST means—unclear if NBT can function as a standalone SLU or only as integrated DST with belief updates.  
- Weakness: manual corrections to DSTC2 transcriptions are mentioned but not quantified, hindering comparability to prior DSTC2 results.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of sequential models (RNN/LSTM) for utterance encoding despite their relevance for long-range dependencies and compositionality.  
- Weakness: insufficient cost/benefit analysis of semantic dictionaries in baselines and lack of qualitative error analysis illustrating specific gains or failure modes.  
- Question: Can NBT be used as a standalone SLU outputting per-turn slot–value detections without belief aggregation, or is it tied to DST?  
- Question: Report statistics on DSTC2 transcription corrections (number/types of fixes) and sensitivity of results to these corrections; compare to uncorrected data if possible.  
- Question: Why were RNN/LSTM encoders not explored—any preliminary experiments, data/training constraints, or expectations about their utility?  
- Question: Provide details about the semantic dictionaries used by baselines (size per slot, example paraphrases, construction effort) to quantify manual cost avoided.  
- Question: Include illustrative error cases where NBT corrects baseline failures (and cases where NBT fails) to clarify remaining challenges (e.g., ASR confusions, rare paraphrases).  
- Question (minor): Clarify belief update coefficient λ selection and sensitivity; summarize critical hyperparameters for reproduction (filter counts, hidden sizes, thresholds).
