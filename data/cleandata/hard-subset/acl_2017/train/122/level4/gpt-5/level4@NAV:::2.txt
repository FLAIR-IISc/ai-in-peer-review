This paper introduces a neural network–based framework for dialogue state tracking.
The core contribution lies in learning representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries, all derived from pre-trained word vectors.
For utterance representation in particular, the authors compare two neural architectures: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.
The learned representations are combined and then passed to a downstream network that makes a binary decision for a given slot–value pair.
Experiments show that the proposed framework achieves significant improvements over a delexicalized baseline.
Overall, it is a solid piece of work with a clear objective, a reasonable approach, and better results than prior studies.
However, the paper does not appear to be well organized to communicate details effectively, especially for readers unfamiliar with the area.
First, a more formal definition of DST should be provided at the beginning of the paper.
The current description is not sufficiently clear and becomes more confusing when coupled with SLU.
I recommend presenting a general dialogue system architecture in Section 1 rather than Section 2, followed by a precise problem definition of DST that highlights its relationships with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
Readability would also improve if all notations used throughout the paper were defined earlier.
Some symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) are introduced much earlier than their explanations.

Below are other comments or questions:
- Is it possible to perform separate SLU with this model? If not, the term “joint” could be misleading by implying the model handles both tasks.
- Could you report statistics on how many errors were corrected from the original DSTC2 dataset? If the number is not large, the experiments could also include comparisons with other published work, including DSTC2 entries, using the same dataset.
- What are your thoughts on employing RNNs or LSTMs to capture sequential aspects when learning utterance representations? Given recent successes of these recurrent models in SLU, they might be effective for DST as well.
- Additional details about the semantic dictionary used by the baseline would help clarify the manual cost of constructing such resources.
- It would be helpful to include examples that the baseline mispredicted but your proposed models correctly resolved.
