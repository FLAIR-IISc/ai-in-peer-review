- Introduces a novel dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and compose utterance/context representations.
- Demonstrates consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline on two datasets.
- Evaluates multiple embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).
- Datasets appear limited, raising concerns about generalization to more complex dialogue domains.
- Lacks comparison of the approach with and without a separate slot-tagging component on complex dialogues.
- Suggests similarity estimation could be improved by restricting comparisons to spans corresponding to slot values.
- Introductory example may be misleading—dialogue state should include "restaurant_name=The House"—and coreference resolution implications are not addressed.
- Overall, leveraging pre-trained embeddings is a strong, compelling idea.
