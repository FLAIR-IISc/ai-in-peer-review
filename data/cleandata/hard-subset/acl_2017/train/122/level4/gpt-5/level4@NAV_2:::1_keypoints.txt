- Proposes a new dialogue state tracking method that uses pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.  
- Shows consistent, statistically significant improvements over prior delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.  
- Evaluates multiple embedding initializations and types (Xavier/random, GloVe, Paragram-SL999).  
- Evaluation domains are limited despite claims about scaling to complex domains.  
- Missing comparison of the method with and without a dedicated slot-tagging component on more complex dialogues.  
- Suggests constraining similarity computation to utterance spans likely to contain slot values to improve estimation even without exact matches.  
- Introductory example may be misleading (omits a restaurant_name slot) and raises concerns about the role of coreference resolution in performance.  
- Overall, leveraging pre-trained embeddings is compelling and the modeling approach is promising.
