- Novel method: uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context for dialogue state tracking.

- Empirical gains: experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline.

- Embedding variants: authors explore alternative pre-training/initialization strategies (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).

- Dataset limitation: chosen datasets seem limited and may not demonstrate advantages in more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization fails.

- Missing comparisons: reviewer asks for evaluation with and without a separate slot-tagging component on more complex dialogues.

- Span-based similarity: suggests limiting similarity computation to the slot-value span (rather than entire utterance) and questions applicability when values do not match.

- Example/annotation issues: introduction examples may be misleading (e.g., should include restaurant_name=The House), raising questions about handling of coreference.

- Overall verdict: using pre-trained word embeddings is a strong idea and the proposed approach is promising.
