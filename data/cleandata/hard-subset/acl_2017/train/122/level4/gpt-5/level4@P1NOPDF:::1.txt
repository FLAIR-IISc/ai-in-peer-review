- Strengths:
This paper presents a novel approach to dialogue state tracking that represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and learns to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. Experiments on two datasets show consistent and significant improvements over a baseline delexicalization-based approach. The paper also investigates alternative methods for pre-training word embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).

- Weaknesses:
Although a main motivation for using embeddings is to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale, the datasets used here seem limited. I wonder how the approach would compare with and without a separate slot-tagging component on more complex dialogues. For example, when computing similarity between the utterance and slot–value pairs, one could restrict the estimation to the span corresponding to the slot value. This should be applicable even when the values do not match exactly.

I also find the examples in the introduction potentially misleading: shouldn’t the dialogue state include “restaurant_name=The House”? This raises another question: how does coreference resolution impact this task?

- General Discussion:
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a strong idea, and the specific way the paper leverages them is promising.
