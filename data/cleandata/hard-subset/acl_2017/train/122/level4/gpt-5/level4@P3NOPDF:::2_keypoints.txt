- Proposed approach: neural DST framework that learns representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries from pre-trained embeddings, using NBT-DNN/NBT-CNN encoders and a binary classifier per slot–value, with gains on DSTC2.
- Strength: Clear goal to reduce reliance on hand-engineered semantic dictionaries and leverage pre-trained embeddings for better generalization.
- Strength: Sensible, modular design—separate encoders for utterances, system acts, and ontology entries followed by a simple binary decision layer.
- Strength: Empirical improvement over a strong delexicalized baseline on DSTC2, demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Strength: Comparison between DNN and CNN encoders provides useful insight into representation choices for DST.
- Major concern: Paper needs an early formal definition of DST, a high-level architecture diagram, and clarification of interfaces with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
- Major concern: The term “joint” is potentially misleading—clarify whether it implies multi-task SLU+DST or only joint modeling within one network.
- Major concern: Notation is introduced inconsistently (symbols like tq, ts, tv appear before definition); define all symbols early and use them consistently.
- Major concern: Dataset corrections to DSTC2 require statistics (number/type of corrections), results on the original dataset, and clarification on public availability of corrected files.
- Major concern: Baseline semantic dictionary needs description (coverage, construction cost, examples) to quantify the manual effort avoided by the proposed method.
- Major concern: Discuss or compare alternative encoders (RNNs/LSTMs) given their strength on sequential data; if not included, justify preferring DNN/CNN (speed, data size, overfitting).
- Major concern: Add qualitative examples showing cases where the baseline fails but the proposed models succeed (and vice versa) to illuminate learned representation behavior.
- Major concern: Improve experimental clarity and reproducibility by specifying pre-trained embedding source, fine-tuning, optimization/regularization/early-stopping, negative construction, handling of “none”/“don’t care”, and per-turn slot–value sets.
- Specific question: Can the framework be adapted to perform SLU separately from DST, or clarify that “joint” does not imply SLU capability?
- Specific question: How many labels/transcripts were corrected in DSTC2, which categories were affected, and are corrected files publicly available?
- Specific question: What is the size/coverage of the semantic dictionary used in the delexicalized baseline and the estimated manual effort to build it for a new domain?
- Specific question: Were RNN/LSTM encoders tried for utterances/system outputs; if so, what were the results, and if not, what trade-offs motivated DNN/CNN?
- Specific question: Provide representative examples where the proposed models correct baseline errors to aid interpretability.
- Overall assessment: Solid and timely contribution framing DST as binary slot–value decisions with learned representations; main issues are exposition and missing experimental details rather than the core idea.
