- Proposes a neural DST framework using pre-trained word embeddings to represent user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries.
- Compares two utterance-encoding variants: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN, feeding composed representations to a binary slot-value classifier.
- Shows significant performance gains over a delexicalization-based baseline and improved results over prior work.
- Paper has a clear objective and sound ideas but overall organization hampers readability, especially for DST novices.
- Recommend a formal, self-contained DST definition up front and moving the high-level dialogue system architecture to Section 1.
- Notation is introduced inconsistently (symbols like tq, ts, tv used before definition); consolidate and define symbols early.
- Clarify the role of SLU: whether SLU is handled separately or implicitly within the DST model, and whether “joint” is an accurate description.
- Report detailed statistics on corrections made to the DSTC2 dataset and compare to results on the original uncorrected dataset and leaderboard entries.
- Justify the choice of DNN/CNN over recurrent models (RNNs/LSTMs) for capturing sequence information, or provide empirical comparisons.
- Provide more details on the semantic dictionary baseline (construction, size, coverage) to assess manual effort and domain expertise required.
- Include qualitative examples and error analysis showing cases where the baseline fails but the proposed models succeed, and remaining failure modes.
