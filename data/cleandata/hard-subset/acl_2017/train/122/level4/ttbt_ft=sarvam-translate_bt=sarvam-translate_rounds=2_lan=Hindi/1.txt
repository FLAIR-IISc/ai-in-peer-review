- Strengths:
This paper proposes a new approach to dialog state tracking that benefits from learning to represent slot values with pre-trained embedding and combine them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialog context. Experiments conducted on two datasets show consistent and significant improvements over the previous delexicalization-based approach. Alternative approaches to pre-trained word embedding (i.e., XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999) have been investigated.
- Weaknesses:
Although one of the main reasons for using embedding is to generalize to more complex dialog domains where delexicalization may not be possible, the datasets used seem limited. I wonder how this approach would be compared with and without a separate slot tagging component on more complex dialogs. For example, when computing similarity between utterances and slot value pairs, one can actually limit prediction to the range of slot values. This should also apply when the values do not match. I think the examples given in the introduction are misleading; shouldn't "restaurant_name=The House" also be included in the dialog state? This raises another question: how does the resolution of coreference affect this task?
- General Discussion:
Overall, using pre-trained word embedding is a very good idea, and the specific approach for using them is exciting.
