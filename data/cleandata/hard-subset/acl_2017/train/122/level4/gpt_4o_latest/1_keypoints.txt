- Introduces an innovative dialogue state tracking method that uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and combine them into distributed utterance and context representations.  
- Experiments on two datasets show consistent and significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline.  
- Authors compare alternative pre-training methods for embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Datasets used are somewhat limited, raising concerns about scalability to more complex dialogue domains.  
- It is unclear how performance changes with or without a dedicated slot-tagging component in complex scenarios.  
- Suggestion to restrict similarity calculations to slot-value spans when possible, which may help even with non-matching values.  
- Introductory examples may be misleading (e.g., missing restaurant_name=The House), indicating clarity issues.  
- Unclear how coreference resolution affects model performance and dialogue state accuracy.  
- Overall assessment: using pre-trained embeddings is an excellent, promising, and compelling approach.
