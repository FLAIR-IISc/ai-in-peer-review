- Introduces a neural network framework for dialogue state tracking (DST) that learns representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.
- Evaluates two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.
- Learned representations are combined and fed into a downstream network to make binary decisions for slot-value pairs.
- Shows significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline.
- Overall solid work with a clear objective, sound methodology, and improved results.
- Paper organization needs improvement to better convey details for readers unfamiliar with the domain.
- Recommend providing a formal definition of DST early and moving the general dialogue system architecture to Section 1 to clarify relationships with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
- Notations (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) should be defined earlier to avoid reader confusion.
- Clarify whether the model can perform separate SLU; using the term “joint” may be misleading if it does not handle both tasks simultaneously.
- Provide statistics on the number of corrections in the DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other published DSTC2 results if corrections are minimal.
- Consider using RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential aspects of utterance representations, given their success in SLU tasks.
- Include more details about the semantic dictionary baseline to clarify manual effort.
- Add example cases where the baseline failed but the proposed models succeeded to illustrate strengths.
