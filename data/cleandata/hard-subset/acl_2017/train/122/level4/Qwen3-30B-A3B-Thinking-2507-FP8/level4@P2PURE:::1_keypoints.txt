- Novel DST approach that uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.  
- Empirical results on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines.  
- Authors compare multiple pre-trained embedding methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Datasets used are limited, raising questions about scalability to more complex dialogue domains.  
- Need to evaluate performance with and without a dedicated slot tagging component in complex dialogues.  
- Similarity computation should consider restricting estimation to the slot-value span; applicability when values do not match needs clarification.  
- Introductory example is misleading (dialogue state should include "restaurant_name=The House"), highlighting concerns about coreference resolution’s impact.  
- Overall, using pre-trained word embeddings is a promising, innovative direction.
