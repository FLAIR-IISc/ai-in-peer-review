

Strengths:  
This paper introduces an innovative methodology for dialogue state tracking by leveraging pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and learning to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. Experimental results on two datasets demonstrate consistent and significant performance gains over prior delexicalization-based baselines. The authors also rigorously evaluate alternative pre-trained word embedding approaches (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  

Weaknesses:  
While the use of embeddings aims to enhance generalization across complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may scale poorly, the evaluated datasets appear insufficient for this claim. It is unclear how the approach would perform with and without a dedicated slot tagging component in more complex dialogues. Specifically, when computing similarity between utterance and slot value pairs, restricting the estimation to the slot value spans (even for mismatched values) could be a viable optimization. Additionally, the introductory example is potentially misleading—shouldn't the dialogue state include "restaurant_name=The House"? This raises further questions about how coreference resolution impacts the task.  

General Discussion:  
Overall, the utilization of pre-trained word embeddings represents a promising direction, and the proposed method for integrating them is well-executed and compelling.
