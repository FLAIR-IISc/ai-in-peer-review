- Introduces an innovative dialogue state tracking method that uses pre-trained embeddings for slot values and composes them into utterance and context representations.  
- Experimental results on two datasets show consistent and significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines.  
- Provides a rigorous comparison of different pre-trained embedding choices (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- The evaluated datasets are likely insufficient to support claims about scaling/generalization to more complex dialogue domains.  
- Unclear how the method performs with or without a dedicated slot-tagging component in complex dialogues.  
- Suggests a possible optimization of computing similarity only over slot-value spans, but its impact is not explored.  
- The introductory example may be misleading (e.g., whether dialogue state should include restaurant_name=The House), raising questions about coreference handling.  
- Overall, the embedding-based approach is promising and the integration is well-executed.
