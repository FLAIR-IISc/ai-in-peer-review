- Paper proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word vectors to handle lexical variation without hand-crafted semantic dictionaries.
- Main contribution: showing learned representations from pre-trained vectors remove need for domain-specific semantic lexicons while matching delexicalization performance.
- Secondary contribution: two model variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) for utterance representation learning.
- Shows semantic specialization of word vectors (e.g., Paragram-SL999) significantly improves tracking performance.

- Strength: NBT matches delexicalization-based models and outperforms them when semantic dictionaries are unavailable, addressing scalability.
- Strength: Experiments on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 show statistically significant improvements in goal and request accuracy, with NBT-CNN strongest.
- Strength: Demonstrates NBT handles semantic variation without explicit lexicons and provides useful comparison across different word vector types.

- Weakness: Paper organization is poor for readers new to dialogue systems; key DST concepts should be defined up front and architecture overview provided.
- Weakness: Critical notation (e.g., tq, ts, tv) is used without early formal definitions, causing reader confusion.
- Weakness: Claim of being a "joint" model is misleading because it does not address standalone SLU tasks; authors should clarify the scope of "joint".
- Weakness: Lacks meaningful comparison with other published DSTC2 entries; evaluation context is incomplete.
- Weakness: Does not justify why RNNs/LSTMs were not used for sequential modeling despite their success in SLU tasks.

- Question: Can NBT be adapted to handle separate SLU tasks, or is the "joint" claim misleading—clarify if it can be used as standalone SLU.
- Question: How many errors were corrected in the DSTC2 dataset, and can results be compared to other published DSTC2 entries for context?
- Question: Why were RNNs/LSTMs not considered for learning sequential utterance representations, and what limitations prevent their use here?
- Question: What was the cost and effort to create the semantic dictionary used with the baseline model to quantify the scalability benefit?
- Question: Please provide concrete examples where NBT correctly identified slot-value pairs that the baseline missed to illustrate strengths.
