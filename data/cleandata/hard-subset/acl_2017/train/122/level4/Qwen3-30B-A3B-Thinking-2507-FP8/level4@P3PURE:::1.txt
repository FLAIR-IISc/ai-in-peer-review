

- Strengths:  
This paper proposes a novel approach for dialogue state tracking that leverages pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and learns to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. Experiments on two datasets demonstrate consistent and significant improvements over prior delexicalization-based baselines. The authors also investigate alternative pre-training methods (e.g., XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999) for word embeddings.

- Weaknesses:  
While the motivation for using embeddings is to generalize to complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale, the evaluation datasets appear limited. The authors should clarify how the approach compares with and without a separate slot tagging component in more complex dialogues. Specifically, when computing similarity between utterances and slot value pairs, the estimation could be restricted to slot value spans—a strategy applicable even when exact value matches are absent. Additionally, the introductory example seems misleading: the dialogue state should include "restaurant_name=The House," raising questions about how coreference resolution impacts the task.  

- General Discussion:  
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a promising approach, and the specific methodology for integrating them is exciting.
