- Contribution: Proposes a neural DST framework using pre-trained word vectors to represent user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries, comparing NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN and using a downstream binary slot-value classifier.  
- Results: Demonstrates significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline and clear improvement over prior work.  
- Organization: DST should be formally defined in Section 1 with a high-level dialogue system architecture and explicit relations to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Notation: Key symbols (e.g., t_q, t_s, t_v) are introduced before being defined; all notation should be presented early and consistently.  
- "Joint" terminology: Calling the model "joint" is misleading since it addresses DST only, not SLU; clarify whether joint SLU/DST or separate SLU is possible.  
- DSTC2 dataset: Provide statistics on DSTC2 error corrections and, if correction rate is low, compare results to other DSTC2 entries using the same corrected dataset.  
- Sequential modeling: Consider and justify why RNNs/LSTMs were not used for utterance representations to better capture sequential dependencies.  
- Baseline effort: Elaborate on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to contextualize the manual/resource cost.  
- Error analysis: Include concrete examples showing where the proposed model corrects baseline errors (specific slot-value pairs).  
- Overall assessment: Valid contribution with strong empirical results; addressing organizational and explanatory gaps would substantially improve clarity and impact.
