- Novel DST method uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.
- Experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over prior delexicalization-based baselines.
- Authors rigorously compare alternative embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL99), strengthening the technical contribution.
- Evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets, weakening claims about generalization to complex dialogue domains where delexicalization fails.
- The paper should compare performance with and without a dedicated slot tagging component on more complex dialogues.
- The model could improve by restricting similarity computations to spans likely containing slot values, even when exact matches are absent.
- The introductory example appears misleading (e.g., missing restaurant_name=The House), raising concerns about handling coreference.
- Coreference resolution (ambiguous references like "it" or "that") is a critical open question for the proposed approach.
- Overall, using pre-trained embeddings and the proposed composition method is well-motivated and promising for DST.
