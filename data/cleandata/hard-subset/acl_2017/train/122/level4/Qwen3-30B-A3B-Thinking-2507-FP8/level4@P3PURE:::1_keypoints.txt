- Proposes a novel dialogue state tracking method that uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and context.  
- Demonstrates consistent and significant improvements over prior delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.  
- Explores multiple embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Evaluation datasets are limited given the claimed scalability benefits over delexicalization for complex domains.  
- Needs clarification on how the approach performs with and without a separate slot-tagging component in more complex dialogues.  
- Suggests restricting similarity computation to slot-value spans as an alternative estimation strategy, which the paper should discuss/compare.  
- Introductory example appears misleading: the dialogue state should include "restaurant_name=The House," raising concerns about how coreference resolution is handled.  
- Overall, using pre-trained word embeddings and the proposed integration method is a promising and exciting direction.
