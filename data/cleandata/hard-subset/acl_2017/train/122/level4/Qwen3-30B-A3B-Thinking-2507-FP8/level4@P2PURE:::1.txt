

- Strengths:  
This paper introduces a novel dialogue state tracking approach that leverages pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations for user utterances and dialogue context. Experiments on two datasets demonstrate consistent and significant improvements over prior delexicalization-based baselines. The authors also evaluate alternative pre-trained word embedding methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  

- Weaknesses:  
While the use of embeddings aims to generalize to complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale, the datasets employed remain limited. The authors should address how the approach performs with and without a dedicated slot tagging component in more complex dialogues. Specifically, when computing similarity between utterance and slot value pairs, the estimation could be restricted to the slot value span—this approach should remain applicable even when values do not match. Additionally, the introductory example appears misleading: the dialogue state should include "restaurant_name=The House." This raises a further question about how coreference resolution impacts the task.  

- General Discussion:  
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings represents a strong and promising direction, and the specific method proposed is innovative.
