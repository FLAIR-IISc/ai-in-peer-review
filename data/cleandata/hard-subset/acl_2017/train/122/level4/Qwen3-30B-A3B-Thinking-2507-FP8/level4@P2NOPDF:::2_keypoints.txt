- Proposes a neural DST framework using pre-trained word vectors and compares two utterance encoders (NBT-DNN vs NBT-CNN) fused into a slot-value binary classifier.  
- Demonstrates significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline and provides clear empirical validation.  
- Paper is well-motivated and technically sound, but presentation and organization reduce clarity.  
- Section ordering confusing: DST is introduced without first situating it in a dialogue system architecture linking ASR, SLU, and policy.  
- Notation issues: symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) appear before being defined; propose an early notation section.  
- Terminology ambiguity: the term "joint" is misleading regarding SLU integration—clarify whether SLU is part of the model.  
- Dataset concerns: report how many DSTC2 errors were corrected and compare against all published DSTC2 results using the same data.  
- Model choices: RNNs/LSTMs for sequential modeling are not explored—justify omission or evaluate them.  
- Baseline transparency: provide details about the semantic dictionary (size, source, manual effort).  
- Error analysis lacking: include concrete examples where the proposed model corrects baseline failures.  
- Overall verdict: strong technical contribution with editorial/organizational fixes needed to better explain why the method outperforms prior work.
