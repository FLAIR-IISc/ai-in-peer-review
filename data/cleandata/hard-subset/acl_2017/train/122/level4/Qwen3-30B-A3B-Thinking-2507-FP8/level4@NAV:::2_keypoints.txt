- Proposes a neural DST framework that learns unified representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.  
- Evaluates two utterance representation architectures (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN) and uses them to make binary slot-value decisions.  
- Shows significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline and improved results versus prior work.  
- Paper is well-motivated and logically structured but organization could be improved for clarity.  
- Recommend moving a formal DST definition and high-level dialogue system architecture to Section 1 and clarifying DST’s relation to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Notations (e.g., t_q, t_s, t_v) appear before being defined and should be introduced earlier to avoid confusion.  
- Clarify whether the model handles SLU independently; calling it "joint" may mislead if SLU is not addressed.  
- Report the error correction rate on the DSTC2 dataset and compare with other DSTC2 submissions using the same data for stronger evaluation.  
- Explain why RNNs/LSTMs were not considered for modeling sequential utterance aspects given their SLU success.  
- Provide details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline, including manual resource costs.  
- Include concrete examples where the baseline failed but the proposed model succeeded to illustrate practical advantages.
