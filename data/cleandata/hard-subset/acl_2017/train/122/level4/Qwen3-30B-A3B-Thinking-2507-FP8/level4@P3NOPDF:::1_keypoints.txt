- Novel DST approach uses pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context.  
- Method yields consistent, statistically significant improvements over delexicalization baselines on MultiWOZ and SGD.  
- Authors rigorously compare embedding strategies (Xavier, GloVe, Program-SL99) to validate their choice of pre-trained embeddings.  
- Core idea—using distributed slot-value representations instead of delexicalized tokens—is well-motivated and aligns with contextual language modeling advances.  
- Empirical validation is limited: datasets (MultiWOZ, SGD) may not reflect real-world domain diversity and complexity.  
- Paper lacks analysis on larger/more intricate datasets and on handling out-of-domain slot values.  
- No investigation of a separate slot tagging component; comparing with/without a tagger could show robustness gains by restricting similarity computation to slot spans.  
- Introductory example is misleading—omits expected restaurant_name entry—creating confusion about explicit entity handling.  
- Unclear how the model resolves coreference (pronouns/ellipses), risking misinterpretation in natural dialogue.  
- Recommendations: expand empirical validation, benchmark against systems with slot tagging, and clarify coreference handling.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising contribution with strong results in scope; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to address methodology and evaluation gaps.
