- Proposes a neural network approach to dialogue state tracking (DST) learning representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.
- Evaluates two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.
- Combines learned representations to make binary decisions for slot–value pairs in the downstream network.
- Experimental results show significant gains over the delexicalized baseline.
- Overall high-quality work with clear objectives, reasonable methodology, and improved outcomes.
- Paper organization could be improved to better convey details to non-expert readers.
- Recommend providing a formal definition of DST early to clarify its relation to SLU.
- Suggest introducing a general dialogue system architecture and a DST problem definition linking ASR, SLU, and policy learning in Section 1.
- Recommend defining all notation earlier, as symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) appear before explanation.
- Ask whether the model can perform separate SLU or if the term “joint” is misleading.
- Request statistics on the number of errors corrected in the DSTC2 dataset and comparisons to other published work if corrections are few.
- Suggest exploring RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential utterance aspects given their SLU success.
- Ask for more details on the semantic dictionary used for the baseline to understand manual resource cost.
- Request examples of instances mispredicted by the baseline but correctly resolved by the proposed models.
