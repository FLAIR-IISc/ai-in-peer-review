- Proposes an innovative dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained embeddings for slot values and distributed representations of utterances and context.  
- Shows consistent, substantial improvements over delexicalization baselines on two datasets.  
- Evaluates multiple pre-trained embedding variants (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Datasets appear limited, so claimed generalizability to complex domains is not fully validated.  
- Needs evaluation of the approach with and without a separate slot-tagging component in more complex dialogues.  
- Suggests computing similarity restricted to slot-value spans to handle non-exact matches between utterances and slot values.  
- Introductory examples may be misleading (e.g., missing "restaurant_name=The House"), highlighting concerns about coreference resolution.  
- Overall, the use of pre-trained embeddings is promising and the method is a valuable contribution to dialogue state tracking.
