## Review:

This paper introduces a promising new dialogue state tracking method leveraging pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values, and subsequently composing these into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. The experimental results on two datasets demonstrate clear and statistically significant gains over a strong delexicalization-based baseline. The authors thoughtfully explore the impact of different pre-training methods for the word embeddings, including XAVIER, GloVe, and Program-SL999.

However, the evaluation datasets appear somewhat constrained, given the stated motivation of improved generalization to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization struggles. It would be valuable to see a comparison of the approach with and without a dedicated slot tagging component, particularly when applied to more challenging dialogues. Specifically, restricting similarity calculations between utterances and slot value pairs to the span of the slot values themselves – even in cases of non-matching values – could prove beneficial. Furthermore, the example provided in the introduction appears incomplete; the dialogue state should arguably include “restaurant_name=The House,” which raises a broader question regarding the handling of coreference resolution within this framework. 

Overall, the application of pre-trained word embeddings to dialogue state tracking is a compelling idea, and the proposed method represents an exciting contribution to the field.
