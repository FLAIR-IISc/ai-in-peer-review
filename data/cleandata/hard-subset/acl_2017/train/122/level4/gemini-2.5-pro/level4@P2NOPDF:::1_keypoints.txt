- Introduces a novel dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained word embeddings for slot values and compositional representations of utterances and context.  
- Moves away from brittle, hard-to-scale delexicalization techniques.  
- Shows strong empirical gains on two standard datasets compared to a delexicalization baseline.  
- Provides thorough analysis by comparing several embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Evaluation datasets may be too simple to demonstrate claimed scalability benefits for complex domains.  
- Lacks comparison or analysis with pipeline models that use slot-tagging to constrain similarity computation to text spans.  
- Omitting analysis of interaction between span-constrained slot-tagging and the proposed method leaves questions about potential efficiency and accuracy gains.  
- Intro example appears misleading/incomplete (missing restaurant_name=The House), calling into question handling of tracking errors.  
- Does not discuss handling of coreference, pronouns, or other anaphoric phenomena crucial for multi-turn state maintenance.  
- Overall: well-motivated, innovative, and empirically effective approach that needs further evaluation on complex dialogues and linguistic phenomena.
