- Proposes a neural DST framework that learns distributed representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries from pre-trained word vectors.  
- Evaluates two utterance encoders: a feed-forward NBT-DNN and a convolutional NBT-CNN.  
- Combines learned representations to perform binary classification per candidate slot-value pair and significantly outperforms a delexicalized baseline on DSTC2.  
- Paper has a clear objective, well-motivated approach, and strong empirical results.  
- Presentation and organization need improvement to be accessible to non-experts.  
- Suggests formally defining the DST task upfront and moving the dialogue system architecture into the introduction.  
- Recommends consolidating and defining all notation earlier (e.g., t_q, t_s, t_v are used before definition).  
- Asks to clarify the term “joint” modeling and whether the model can perform SLU as a separate task.  
- Requests statistics on how many DSTC2 errors were corrected and comparisons with results on the original (uncorrected) DSTC2.  
- Suggests considering RNNs/LSTMs for utterance modeling given language’s sequential nature.  
- Requests more details about the semantic dictionary used by the baseline to illustrate manual effort and cost.  
- Recommends adding qualitative examples showing cases the baseline fails but the NBT models handle correctly.
