- Proposes a novel dialogue state tracking approach that leverages pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values.  
- Model composes slot-value embeddings into distributed representations of the user utterance and dialogue context.  
- Experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over a strong delexicalization-based baseline.  
- Authors thoroughly compare alternative embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Main motivation—better generalization to complex domains where delexicalization may not scale—is not fully validated due to limited datasets.  
- Would benefit from comparisons with and without a separate slot-tagging component, particularly on complex dialogues.  
- Recommends restricting similarity computations to utterance spans likely to contain slot values to handle non-exact phrasing.  
- Introductory example may be misleading (misses restaurant_name=The House) and raises concerns about how the model handles coreference.  
- Overall, using pre-trained embeddings for DST is a promising, novel, and well-motivated direction.
