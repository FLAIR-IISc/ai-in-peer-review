- Proposes a neural framework for dialogue state tracking that learns vector representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries initialized from pre-trained word vectors.
- Evaluates two utterance encoders (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN) whose representations are combined and fed to a final binary classifier per candidate slot-value pair.
- Empirical results show the proposed approach significantly outperforms a delexicalized baseline and advances the state of the art.
- Paper is well-motivated with sound methodology and strong empirical results.
- Paper organization and exposition hinder clarity, especially for readers unfamiliar with the field.
- Introduction needs a formal definition of Dialogue State Tracking (DST) and clearer relation to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
- Suggest moving the general dialogue system architecture to the introduction and defining all notation at first use (e.g., tq, ts, t_v currently undefined initially).
- The term “joint” may be misleading—clarify whether the model can perform SLU as a standalone task or why “joint” is used.
- Authors should report statistics on corrections made to the DSTC2 dataset and compare with prior work using the original dataset if modifications are limited.
- Consideration/discussion of RNNs/LSTMs for modeling sequential utterances is missing and should be addressed given their success in SLU.
- Provide more details about the semantic dictionary baseline, including the effort required to build it, to contextualize the proposed approach’s advantages.
- Include qualitative analysis/examples showing specific cases where the baseline fails but the proposed models succeed to make improvements more concrete.
