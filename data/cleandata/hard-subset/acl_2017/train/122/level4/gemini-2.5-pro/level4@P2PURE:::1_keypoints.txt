- Novel method: represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composes them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context.  
- Strong empirical gains: experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline.  
- Ablation on embeddings: authors analyze effects of different pre-trained word embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Evaluation mismatch: motivation to scale to complex domains conflicts with experiments on relatively simple datasets.  
- Missing analysis of slot tagging: unclear how performance changes with/without the slot tagging component, especially in complex dialogues.  
- Localized matching suggestion: similarity computations could be restricted to likely text spans to better handle unseen values.  
- Incomplete example and coreference concerns: introductory example omits a slot-value and raises unanswered questions about coreference resolution handling.  
- Overall assessment: using pre-trained embeddings for DST is well-motivated and the proposed method is novel and promising.
