- Proposes a novel DST method that represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.  
- Shows consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline on two datasets.  
- Evaluates multiple embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Evaluation datasets may be too simple to demonstrate the proposed method’s advantage in complex dialogue domains where delexicalization fails.  
- Lacks comparison to models that use a separate slot tagger to constrain similarity computations to tagged spans, which could help with out-of-ontology values.  
- Intro example omits a coreference case (restaurant_name=The House), raising concerns about how the model handles coreference resolution.  
- Overall, using pre-trained embeddings for DST is well-motivated and the proposed composition method is a solid, novel contribution.
