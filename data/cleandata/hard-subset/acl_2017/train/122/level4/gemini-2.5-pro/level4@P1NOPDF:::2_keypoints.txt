- Proposes a neural framework for dialogue state tracking (DST) that learns representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries from pre-trained word vectors.
- Compares two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.
- Combines learned representations and predicts slot-value pairs with a downstream binary classifier.
- Experiments show significant improvements over a delexicalized baseline.
- Overall high-quality, well-motivated work with clear objectives and improved results.
- Paper organization and presentation of technical details need improvement for non-expert readers.
- Introduction lacks a formal definition of DST and misplaces the general dialogue system architecture; recommend moving architecture to Section 1 and clarifying DST’s relation to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
- Mathematical notation is introduced out of order; recommend consolidating and defining symbols early to improve readability.
- Clarify the use of the term “joint” — does the model perform SLU standalone or only use SLU-style features for DST?
- Request statistics on how many errors were corrected in the DSTC2 dataset and comparisons to other published/original DSTC2 results.
- Suggest exploring RNNs/LSTMs for sequential utterance modeling given their SLU success.
- Ask for more details about the semantic dictionary baseline to clarify manual effort and costs.
- Recommend qualitative error analysis with examples showing baseline errors corrected by the proposed models.
