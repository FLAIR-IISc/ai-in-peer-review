- Introduces the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT): an end-to-end DST that uses pre-trained word vectors to decide if a user has expressed a specific slot-value given the utterance, system act, and candidate pair.
- Proposes two NBT variants (DNN, CNN) that match lexicon-enhanced state-of-the-art and outperform baselines when hand-crafted lexicons are absent (results on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0).
- Contribution: a scalable framework replacing manual delexicalization with learned embeddings, reducing reliance on domain-specific semantic dictionaries.
- Contribution: strong empirical validation that reasoning over semantic vector spaces is viable and effective for dialogue state tracking.
- Contribution: analysis showing semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) yield substantial gains over random or standard distributional vectors.
- Strength: novel approach handling lexical variation (e.g., “cheap” vs “inexpensive”) without hand-crafted dictionaries, improving portability.
- Strength: thorough experiments on datasets with and without ASR noise that convincingly support the model’s claims.
- Strength: well-designed architecture separating utterance representation, context modeling, and semantic decoding; useful DNN vs CNN comparison.
- Weakness: presentation could better define DST and its relation to ASR/SLU/policy earlier for non-expert readers.
- Weakness: lacks discussion or comparison with recurrent architectures (RNN/LSTM/GRU) as alternatives for utterance encoding.
- Weakness: manually corrected DSTC2 dataset complicates direct quantitative comparisons to prior published results; corrections not quantified.
- Question: clarify whether “Joint SLU/DST” means NBT produces standalone SLU frames or only implicitly performs SLU-like functions for tracking.
- Question: provide details on the hand-crafted semantic dictionary used by baselines (approximate size and annotation effort) to contextualize benefits.
- Question: have you tried or considered LSTMs/GRUs for utterance representations, and how might they compare to the CNN approach?
- Question: include qualitative examples where NBT succeeds but delexicalization-based baselines fail to illustrate the advantages of vector-space reasoning.
