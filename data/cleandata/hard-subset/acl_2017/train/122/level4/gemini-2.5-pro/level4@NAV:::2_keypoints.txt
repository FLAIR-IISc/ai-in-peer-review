- Proposes a neural network approach for dialogue state tracking that learns distributed representations for user utterances, system responses, and ontology entries using pre-trained word embeddings.
- Compares two architectures for user utterance representation: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN, feeding learned representations to a downstream binary classifier for slot-value pairs.
- Empirical results show significant improvement over a delexicalized baseline.
- Overall solid contribution with clear objective, sound methodology, and superior empirical performance.
- Paper organization and clarity are poor; readers unfamiliar with the domain will struggle with technical details.
- Recommend providing a formal definition of Dialogue State Tracking (DST) early and presenting the overall dialogue system architecture in Section 1.
- Suggest defining all mathematical notation (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) in a dedicated early section to improve readability.
- Ask whether the model can perform SLU as a standalone task and caution that calling it “joint” may be misleading if it cannot.
- Request statistics on the number of corrections made in the DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with published/original DSTC2 results if corrections are few.
- Suggest considering RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential utterance structure, given their success in SLU.
- Ask for more details about the semantic dictionary used by the baseline to clarify manual effort and cost.
- Recommend adding qualitative examples showing cases the baseline missed but the proposed models resolved to illustrate model advantages.
