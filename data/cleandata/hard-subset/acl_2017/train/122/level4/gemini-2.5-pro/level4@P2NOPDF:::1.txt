### Strengths

This paper introduces a novel and effective approach to dialogue state tracking. The key contribution is the use of pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values, which are then composed to create distributed representations of the user utterance and the broader dialogue context. This method moves away from traditional delexicalization techniques, which can be brittle and difficult to scale.

The empirical results are strong. Experiments conducted on two standard datasets demonstrate consistent and significant performance gains over a delexicalization-based baseline. Furthermore, the authors strengthen their work by commendably investigating several alternative pre-training methods for the embeddings, including XAVIER, GloVe, and Program-SL999, which provides a more thorough analysis of the proposed architecture.

### Weaknesses

One of the primary motivations for using embeddings is to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale effectively. However, the datasets used for evaluation, while standard, seem somewhat limited in complexity and may not fully showcase the scalability advantages of the proposed method.

This raises the question of how the approach would perform on more challenging dialogues, particularly in comparison to a pipeline model that incorporates a separate slot-tagging component. For example, when computing the similarity between an utterance and candidate slot-value pairs, the estimation could be constrained to specific text spans identified by a slot tagger. This could potentially improve both efficiency and accuracy, even when the surface forms of the values do not perfectly match an ontology. An analysis of this interaction would strengthen the paper.

Additionally, the example provided in the introduction appears to be misleading or incomplete. In the given dialogue, the final dialogue state seemingly omits "restaurant_name=The House," which is a critical piece of tracked information. This raises a broader question about how the model handles more complex phenomena like coreference resolution. The paper does not discuss the impact of pronouns or other anaphoric references on the task, which is a crucial aspect of maintaining state in realistic, multi-turn conversations.

### Overall Assessment

Overall, this paper presents a well-motivated and promising direction for dialogue state tracking. The application of pre-trained word embeddings to move beyond delexicalization is a significant contribution to the field. The specific compositional approach proposed by the authors is innovative and, as demonstrated by the strong empirical results, highly effective. While there are some questions regarding its performance in more complex scenarios and its handling of linguistic phenomena like coreference, the core idea is sound and the work represents a valuable step forward for dialogue systems research.
