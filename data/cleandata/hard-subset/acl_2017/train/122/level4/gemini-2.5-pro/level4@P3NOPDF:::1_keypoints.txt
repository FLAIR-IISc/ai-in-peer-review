- Proposes a novel DST method using pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values and compose utterance/context representations, moving beyond delexicalization.  
- Experimental results on two datasets show significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines.  
- Thorough empirical analysis includes studying different embedding pre-training methods (e.g., Xavier, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Claims about scalability and generalization are not fully substantiated because evaluations use relatively simple datasets.  
- The paper should evaluate on more complex, multi-domain, or open-ended dialogue tasks to support scalability claims.  
- Lacks discussion of interaction between the end-to-end model and modular slot tagging; a hybrid approach (span detection + embedding similarity) might improve precision.  
- Unclear how the model handles nuanced linguistic phenomena like coreference and entity continuation across turns.  
- Introductory example omission (missing restaurant_name) raises concerns about clarity and handling of implicit or referred slot values.  
- Overall: technically strong and promising contribution, but needs broader evaluation and discussion of limitations/extensions.
