- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) for dialogue state tracking that leverages pre-trained word vectors and couples SLU with DST without hand-crafted resources.
- NBT evaluated on two datasets, matching state-of-the-art with semantic lexicons and outperforming them when lexicons are absent.
- Claims novelty as the first belief-tracking framework to successfully use pre-trained word-vector spaces for language understanding in this task.
- Introduces two variants, NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN, which differ in how user utterance vectors are produced.
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on two datasets, outperforming delexicalization-based baselines.
- Strength: represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.
- Strength: experiments show consistent and significant improvements over previous delexicalization approaches across two datasets.
- Strength: investigates alternative pre-training approaches for embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999).
- Weakness: datasets appear limited, so generalization to more complex dialogue domains and comparison with a separate slot tagging component is unclear.
- Weakness: paper does not address how coreference resolution is handled or its impact on DST performance.
- Weakness: introductory examples may be misleading by omitting a slot value (e.g., "restaurant_name=The House") from the dialogue state.
- Question: how will the NBT framework be extended to handle more complex domains with multiple intents or slot values?
- Question: request for more details on how NBT handles coreference resolution and its performance impact.
- Question: whether other pre-trained embeddings (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa) were considered and how they compare to Paragram-SL999.
