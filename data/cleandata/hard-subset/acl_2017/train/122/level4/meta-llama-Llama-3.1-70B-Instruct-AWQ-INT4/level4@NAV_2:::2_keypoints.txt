- Presents Neural Belief Tracking (NBT) framework for dialogue state tracking using pre-trained word vectors for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries.  
- Compares two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.  
- Combines learned representations to make binary slot-value decisions and outperforms the delexicalized baseline.  
- Main contributions: representation learning from pre-trained embeddings, two NBT models, and significant performance improvements.  
- Strength: leverages semantic information from pre-trained vectors to overcome limitations of prior belief trackers.  
- Strength: couples SLU and DST without requiring hand-crafted semantic lexicons.  
- Strength: shows robustness to linguistic variation and ASR errors with thorough evaluation on two datasets.  
- Weakness: organization and notation are unclear; formal DST definition and earlier notation would improve readability.  
- Weakness: symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) appear before being defined, causing confusion.  
- Weakness: lacks details on the baseline semantic dictionary and the manual cost of building it.  
- Weakness: missing example cases where the baseline failed but the proposed model succeeded.  
- Question: Can this model perform separate SLU, or is the term “joint” misleading?  
- Question: Provide statistics on how many DSTC2 errors were corrected and comparisons to other published DSTC2 entries.  
- Question/Suggestion: Consider using RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential aspects of utterance representations.
