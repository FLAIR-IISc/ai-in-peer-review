- Presents a neural-network framework for dialogue state tracking (DST) using pre-trained word-vector representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries.  
- Compares two utterance representation models (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN) and combines learned representations for binary slot-value decision making.  
- Shows significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline on experiments.  
- Overall quality is good with a clear goal and improved results, but paper organization hampers accessibility for non-experts.  
- Requests a formal DST definition and a general dialogue system architecture early in the paper to clarify relationships with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Recommends defining all notation earlier since symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) appear before explanation.  
- Asks whether the model can perform SLU separately and cautions that calling it “joint” may be misleading.  
- Requests statistics on how many errors were corrected in the DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other published DSTC2 entries if corrections are few.  
- Suggests considering RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential aspects of utterance representations given their success in SLU.  
- Wants more details about the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to assess manual resource costs.  
- Recommends including sample cases where the baseline failed but the proposed models succeeded to illustrate practical improvements.
