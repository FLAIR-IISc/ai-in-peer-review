- Proposes a neural framework for DST that learns representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries from pre-trained word vectors.
- Compares two utterance representation models (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN) and combines representations to make binary slot-value decisions.
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements over a delexicalized baseline on experiments.
- Overall quality is good, but the paper needs better organization and clearer presentation for non-expert readers.
- Recommend adding a formal DST definition and a general dialogue system architecture early to clarify relations with ASR, SLU, and policy learning.
- Suggest defining all notation (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) earlier, as some symbols are used before explanation.
- Ask whether the model can perform separate SLU—if not, avoid using the term “joint” which may be misleading.
- Request statistics on how many DSTC2 errors were corrected and comparisons with other published DSTC2 entries if improvements are small.
- Propose considering RNNs/LSTMs for modeling sequential utterance information given their success in SLU.
- Ask for more details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to illustrate manual resource costs.
- Recommend including example utterances the baseline failed on but the proposed models corrected to demonstrate qualitative gains.
