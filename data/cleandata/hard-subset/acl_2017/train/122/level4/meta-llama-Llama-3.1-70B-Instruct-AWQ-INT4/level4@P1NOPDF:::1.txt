Strengths:
This paper proposes a novel approach to dialogue state tracking by leveraging pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and learning to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. The experiments conducted on two datasets demonstrate consistent and significant improvements over the baseline of the previous delexicalization-based approach. Furthermore, the authors have explored alternative approaches to pre-training word embeddings, including XAVIER, GloVe, and Program-SL999.

Weaknesses:
Although one of the primary motivations for using embeddings is to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not be scalable, the datasets used in this study seem limited. It would be beneficial to investigate how the approach performs with and without a separate slot tagging component in more complex dialogues. For instance, when computing similarity between the utterance and slot value pairs, it is possible to limit the estimation to the span of the slot values, which should be applicable even when the values do not match. Additionally, the examples provided in the introduction may be misleading, as the dialogue state should also include "restaurant_name=The House". This raises another question regarding the impact of coreference resolution on this task.

General Discussion:
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a promising idea, and the specific approach proposed in this paper is exciting.
