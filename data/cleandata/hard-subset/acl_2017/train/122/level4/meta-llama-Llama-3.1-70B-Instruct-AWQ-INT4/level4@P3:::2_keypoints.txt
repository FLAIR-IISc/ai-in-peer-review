- Proposes a neural belief tracking framework that learns representations of user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.
- Introduces and compares two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.
- Shows significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline in experiments.
- Clear and reasonable idea leveraging pre-trained word vectors to improve DST performance.
- Experimental results demonstrate substantial improvements versus the baseline.
- Provides thorough analysis and discussion of results and implications.
- Lacks a formal definition of DST at the paper’s start for unfamiliar readers.
- Paper organization could be improved with clearer separation of intro, problem definition, and solution.
- Some notations are introduced before being defined, causing possible confusion.
- More detail needed on the semantic dictionary used for the baseline to clarify manual cost.
- Would benefit from examples of instances corrected by the proposed models but missed by the baseline.
- Question: Can the model perform separate SLU, or is the term “joint” misleading?
- Question: Please provide statistics on errors corrected from the original DSTC2 dataset.
- Question: Have the authors considered RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential utterance aspects?
- Question: Request for more details about the semantic dictionary used with the baseline.
- Question: Please supply sample dialogues that the baseline mispredicted but the proposed models resolved.
