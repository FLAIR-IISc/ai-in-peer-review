**Strengths:**

This paper presents a novel approach to dialogue state tracking by leveraging pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and learning to compose them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. The experimental results on two datasets demonstrate consistent and significant improvements over the baseline of the previous delexicalization-based approach. Furthermore, the authors have explored alternative approaches (XAVIER, GloVe, and Program-SL999) for pre-training word embeddings, showcasing the versatility of their method.

**Weaknesses:**

Although the use of embeddings is motivated by the desire to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not scale, the datasets used in the experiments appear limited. It would be beneficial to investigate how the approach performs with and without a separate slot tagging component on more complex dialogues. For instance, when computing similarity between the utterance and slot value pairs, the estimation could be limited to the span of the slot values, making it applicable even when the values do not match. Additionally, the examples provided in the introduction may be misleading, as the dialogue state should also include "restaurant_name=The House". This raises questions about the impact of coreference resolution on this task.

**General Discussion:**

Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a promising idea, and the specific approach proposed in this paper is exciting. The authors have demonstrated the potential of their method, and with further experimentation on more complex dialogue domains and exploration of coreference resolution, this approach could lead to significant advancements in dialogue state tracking.
