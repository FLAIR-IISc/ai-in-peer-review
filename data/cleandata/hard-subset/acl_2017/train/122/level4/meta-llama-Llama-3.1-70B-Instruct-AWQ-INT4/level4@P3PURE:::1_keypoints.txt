- Proposes a novel dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained embeddings for slot values and composing them into distributed utterance and context representations.
- Experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over previous delexicalization-based baselines.
- Authors compare alternative embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).
- Datasets used appear limited; approach should be evaluated on more complex dialogue domains.
- Recommend evaluating the method with and without a separate slot-tagging component.
- Suggest limiting similarity computation to slot-value spans to handle mismatched values and more complex cases.
- Introduction examples may be misleading (e.g., missing restaurant_name=The House) and highlight the need to consider coreference resolution.
- Overall, using pre-trained word embeddings is promising and the proposed approach is exciting.
