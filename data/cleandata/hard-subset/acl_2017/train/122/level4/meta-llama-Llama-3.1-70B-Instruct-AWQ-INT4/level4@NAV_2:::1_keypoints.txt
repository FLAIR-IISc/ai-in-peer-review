- Proposes a novel neural belief tracking (NBT) framework that leverages pre-trained word vectors to compose slot values, user utterances, and dialogue context into distributed representations.  
- Shows consistent and significant improvements over previous delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.  
- Investigates alternative pre-training methods for word embeddings (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999) and analyzes their impact on NBT performance.  
- Strength: Represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composes them into utterance/context representations.  
- Strength: Empirical validation demonstrates consistent, significant gains on two datasets.  
- Strength: Provides useful analysis of different pre-trained embedding choices.  
- Weakness: Experiments use limited datasets, so generalization to more complex dialogue domains is unclear.  
- Weakness: Paper does not discuss the role or impact of coreference resolution on dialogue state tracking.  
- Weakness: Introductory examples may be misleading because they omit coreference resolution.  
- Question: How will the authors address dataset limitations and evaluate NBT on more complex dialogue domains?  
- Question: How does the NBT model handle coreference resolution and what is its impact on tracking accuracy?  
- Question: How do the authors plan to extend NBT to handle more complex scenarios (multiple intents or multiple slot values)?
