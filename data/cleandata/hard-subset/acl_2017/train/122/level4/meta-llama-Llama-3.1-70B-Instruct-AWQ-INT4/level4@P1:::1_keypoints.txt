- Proposes a Neural Belief Tracker using pre-trained word vectors to represent slot values and compose utterance and context representations.
- Presents a neural architecture that integrates SLU and DST without hand-crafted semantic lexicons.
- Reports significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.
- Strength: embedding-based slot representations improve generalization and performance.
- Strength: evaluates multiple pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999) for embeddings.
- Weakness: datasets used are limited, raising questions about scalability to more complex dialogue domains.
- Weakness: unclear comparison with/without a separate slot tagging component for complex dialogues.
- Weakness: similarity computation could/should be limited to slot-value spans; applicability when values do not match is unclear.
- Weakness: introduction examples are misleading (missing restaurant_name), and coreference resolution handling is not addressed.
- Overall: promising and well-evaluated approach but needs more work on complex domains and coreference handling.
- Q: How will you address limitations on complex dialogue domains?
- Q: How does coreference resolution impact dialogue state tracking in your approach?
- Q: Have you considered character- or subword-based pre-trained embeddings?
