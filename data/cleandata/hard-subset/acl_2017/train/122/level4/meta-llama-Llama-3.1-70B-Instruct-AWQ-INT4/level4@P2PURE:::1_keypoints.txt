- Novel approach to dialogue state tracking using pre-trained embeddings for slot values and composing them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.  
- Demonstrates consistent, significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.  
- Explores alternative embedding pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Limited datasets raise concerns about scalability and performance in more complex dialogue domains.  
- Lacks comparison of the method with and without a separate slot tagging component in complex scenarios.  
- Suggests computing similarity restricted to slot-value spans to handle mismatched values and broaden applicability.  
- Intro example is misleading (omits restaurant_name=The House), highlighting unclear handling of coreference resolution.  
- Overall, using pre-trained word embeddings for DST is promising and the proposed approach is interesting.
