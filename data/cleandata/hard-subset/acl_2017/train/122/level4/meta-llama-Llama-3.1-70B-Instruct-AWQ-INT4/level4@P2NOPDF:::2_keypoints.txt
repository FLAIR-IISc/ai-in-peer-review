- Presents a neural DST framework using pre-trained word vectors, compares NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN for utterance representation, and makes binary slot-value decisions.  
- Demonstrates significant performance gains over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall solid work with a clear goal and reasonable ideas, but paper organization needs improvement.  
- Add a formal DST definition and a general dialogue system architecture early to clarify relations to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Define all notations earlier—symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) appear before they are described.  
- Clarify the term “joint”: can the model perform separate SLU or does it only handle DST?  
- Provide statistics on the number of corrections made to the DSTC2 dataset and compare with other published DSTC2 results if corrections are minor.  
- Consider using RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential information in utterance representations.  
- Give more details on the semantic dictionary used by the baseline to indicate manual construction cost.  
- Include sample dialogues that the baseline failed on but the proposed models solved.  
- Reorganize the paper to improve clarity and accessibility for readers unfamiliar with the area.
