- Strengths:
This paper presents a novel approach to dialogue state tracking by leveraging pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and learning to compose these into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. The experimental results on two datasets demonstrate consistent and significant improvements over the baseline of previous delexicalization-based approaches. Furthermore, the authors have explored alternative methods for pre-training word embeddings, including XAVIER, GloVe, and Program-SL999.

- Weaknesses:
One of the primary motivations for using embeddings is to enable generalization to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not be scalable. However, the datasets used in this study appear to be limited, raising questions about the approach's performance on more complex dialogues. Specifically, it would be valuable to investigate how the approach compares with and without a separate slot tagging component in such scenarios. For instance, when computing similarity between utterances and slot value pairs, the estimation could be restricted to the span of the slot values, making it applicable even when the values do not match. Additionally, the example provided in the introduction seems misleading, as the dialogue state should also include "restaurant_name=The House." This oversight raises further questions about how coreference resolution impacts this task.

- General Discussion:
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a promising idea, and the specific approach proposed in this paper is exciting.
