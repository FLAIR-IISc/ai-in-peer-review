- Proposes a neural DST framework that learns representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.  
- Evaluates two utterance representation models (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN) and combines them to make binary slot-value decisions in downstream network.  
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall solid contribution with clear goal and empirical gains, but paper organization hampers accessibility for readers unfamiliar with the area.  
- Recommend adding a formal DST definition up front and a general dialogue-system architecture showing relations to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.  
- Ask that all notations be defined earlier; some symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) are introduced before explanation.  
- Question whether the model can perform separate SLU; the term “joint” may be misleading if it does not handle SLU separately.  
- Request statistics on corrected errors from the original DSTC2 dataset and comparisons to other published results if corrections are few.  
- Suggest exploring RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential aspects of utterances, given their SLU success.  
- Ask for more details on the semantic dictionary used by the baseline to clarify manual effort and for example inputs where the proposed models fixed baseline errors.
