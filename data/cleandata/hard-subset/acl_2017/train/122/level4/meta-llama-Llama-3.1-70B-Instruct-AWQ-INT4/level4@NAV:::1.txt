Strengths:
This paper presents an innovative approach to dialogue state tracking by leveraging pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values, which are then composed into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context. The experimental results on two datasets consistently outperform the baseline of previous delexicalization-based methods, demonstrating significant improvements. Furthermore, the authors have explored alternative pre-trained word embedding approaches, including XAVIER, GloVe, and Program-SL999.

Weaknesses:
While the use of embeddings is motivated by the need to generalize to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization may not be scalable, the datasets used in this study appear limited. It would be beneficial to investigate how the proposed approach compares to alternatives with and without a separate slot tagging component in more complex dialogue scenarios. For instance, when calculating similarity between utterances and slot value pairs, it may be possible to restrict the estimation to the span of the slot values, even when the values do not match. Additionally, the introductory examples may be misleading, as the dialogue state should also include "restaurant_name=The House." This raises further questions about the impact of coreference resolution on this task.

General Discussion:
Overall, the use of pre-trained word embeddings is a promising idea, and the specific approach proposed in this paper is exciting.
