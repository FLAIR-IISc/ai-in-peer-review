- Neural DST framework learns representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries using pre-trained word vectors.

- Paper compares two utterance representation models (NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN) whose combined representations make binary slot-value decisions.

- Proposed approach yields significant performance gains over the delexicalized baseline.

- Overall solid contribution with clear goal and improved results, but paper organization and clarity need improvement.

- Provide a formal DST definition early; current framing is unclear and conflates DST with SLU.

- Include a general dialogue-system architecture and explicit problem definition showing DST’s relation to ASR, SLU, and policy learning.

- Define all notation earlier — symbols (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) are introduced before explanation.

- Clarify whether the model can perform standalone SLU; the term “joint” may be misleading if it does not.

- Report statistics on how many DSTC2 errors were corrected and compare to other DSTC2 entries if corrections are few.

- Discuss potential use of RNNs/LSTMs to capture sequential aspects of utterances and justify model choice.

- Provide details on the semantic dictionary used for the baseline to illustrate manual cost, and give example cases where the proposed model fixes baseline errors.
