- Novel method leverages pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context.  
- Experiments on two datasets show consistent, significant improvements over a delexicalization-based baseline.  
- Authors evaluated multiple pre-training strategies (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999), demonstrating the approach's versatility.  
- Datasets used are limited; evaluation on more complex dialogue domains is needed to validate scalability.  
- Should assess performance with and without a separate slot-tagging component on more complex dialogues.  
- Proposes restricting similarity computation to slot-value spans to handle non-exact value matches.  
- Intro examples may be misleading—dialogue state should include entries like restaurant_name=The House, raising annotation concerns.  
- Coreference resolution's impact on dialogue state tracking requires further investigation.  
- Overall the approach is promising but needs broader experimentation and coreference-aware modeling to realize its potential.
