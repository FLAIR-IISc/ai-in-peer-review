- Novel approach: uses pre-trained embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed representations of utterances and dialogue context.  
- Empirical gains: shows consistent, significant improvements over prior delexicalization-based baseline on two datasets.  
- Embedding variants: explores alternative pre-training methods (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999).  
- Dataset limitation: current datasets are limited and may not reflect more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization fails.  
- Slot-tagging evaluation: should evaluate performance with and without a separate slot tagging component in more complex dialogues.  
- Span-limited similarity: propose limiting similarity computation to the span of slot values, which may help when values do not match exactly.  
- Example/annotation issue: introduction examples may be misleading—dialogue state should include explicit slot like "restaurant_name=The House."  
- Coreference concern: unclear how coreference resolution affects performance and the proposed approach.  
- Overall judgment: using pre-trained embeddings is promising and the proposed method is exciting.
