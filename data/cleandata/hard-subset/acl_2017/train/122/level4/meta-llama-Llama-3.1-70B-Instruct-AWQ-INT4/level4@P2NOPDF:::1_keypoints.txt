- Novel method: uses pre-trained embeddings for slot values and composes them into distributed representations of user utterances and dialogue context.  
- Empirical gains: shows consistent, significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.  
- Ablations/pre-training: investigates alternative embedding pre-training strategies (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Dataset limitation: datasets used are limited, raising concerns about scalability to more complex dialogue domains.  
- Missing comparisons: should compare performance with and without a separate slot-tagging component for complex scenarios.  
- Similarity computation issue: similarity between utterance and slot values could be constrained to slot-value spans to handle non-exact matches.  
- Intro/example oversight: example omission (missing restaurant_name=The House) may mislead and indicates unclear treatment of dialogue state.  
- Coreference concern: the paper does not address how coreference resolution affects dialogue state tracking.  
- Overall: approach is promising and exciting but needs further development and testing on more complex datasets.
