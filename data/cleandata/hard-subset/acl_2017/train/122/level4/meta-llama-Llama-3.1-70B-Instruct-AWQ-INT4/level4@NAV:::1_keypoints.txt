- Proposes an innovative dialogue state tracking method using pre-trained embeddings for slot values and composing them into distributed utterance/context representations.  
- Achieves consistent, significant improvements over delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.  
- Evaluates multiple pre-trained embedding variants (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Datasets used seem limited; need evaluation on more complex dialogue domains to validate scalability.  
- Lacks comparisons to approaches with and without a separate slot-tagging component in harder scenarios.  
- Could improve similarity estimation by restricting comparisons to spans corresponding to slot values even when values differ.  
- Introductory examples may be misleading (e.g., missing restaurant_name=The House), highlighting potential issues with coreference resolution.  
- Overall, using pre-trained word embeddings for this task is promising and the proposed approach is compelling.
