- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word embeddings to represent slot values and composes them into distributed utterance and context representations.  
- Aims to overcome limitations of delexicalization and hand-crafted semantic lexicons.  
- Evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, showing significant improvements over delexicalization baselines.  
- Compares embedding initializations (XAVIER, GloVe, Paragram-SL999) and finds semantically specialized embeddings perform best.  
- Strength: embedding-based slot representation with learned composition for utterance/context.  
- Strength: consistent, significant experimental improvements on two datasets.  
- Strength: empirical investigation of different pre-trained embeddings.  
- Weakness: datasets are limited; unclear generalization to more complex dialogues.  
- Weakness: does not address coreference resolution and its effect on tracking.  
- Weakness: introductory examples are misleading; dialogue state should include additional info (e.g., restaurant name).  
- Question: How does the approach perform on more complex dialogues with multiple slots or nuanced language?  
- Question: How is coreference resolution handled, and what is its impact?  
- Question: Can authors detail dataset limitations and plans to address them in future work?
