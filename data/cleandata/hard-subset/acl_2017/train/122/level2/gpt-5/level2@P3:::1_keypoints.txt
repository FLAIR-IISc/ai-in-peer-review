- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), an embedding-driven SLU/DST that jointly models utterance, system-act context, and slot–value candidates without hand-crafted lexicons.  
- Explores two architectures (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) and uses a simple rule-based belief update to fuse ASR N-best lists with prior state.  
- Demonstrates that semantically specialized embeddings improve DST performance and removes reliance on semantic dictionaries.

- Strong empirical gains with statistical significance on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 benchmarks compared to delexicalization baselines.  
- Robust performance without hand-crafted lexicons, matching or surpassing systems that use domain-specific dictionaries.  
- Clear, modular architecture with principled context gating that targets confirmations and elliptical replies.  
- Ablation shows semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) improve downstream DST, especially under ASR noise.  
- Practical handling of ASR via transparent N-best fusion with tunable λ and promises to release cleaned/extended datasets.

- Experiments are limited to a small, single-domain ontology (Cambridge restaurants) with no evidence on larger ontologies, multi-domain scaling, or runtime/throughput.  
- Performance degrades noticeably under ASR noise versus gold transcriptions, with limited ASR-specific strategies explored.  
- Belief update is rule-based and context modeling is short-horizon (last system act), potentially missing long-range dependencies like corrections/negations.  
- Lacks empirical comparisons to recent memory/attention-based DST approaches on the same datasets.  
- Insufficient ablations and failure analysis: missing context-gating ablation, decoder composition choices, slot parameterization tests, and qualitative error analysis.

- How does NBT scale computationally with larger ontologies (per-turn inference cost and throughput)?  
- What is the class balance for candidate pairs during training, and are sampling or loss reweighting strategies used?  
- Did parameter sharing across slots hurt performance, and are there results for partial sharing?  
- How sensitive are results to λ and N-best list size/quality; any experiments with confusion networks?  
- How does NBT handle negation and user corrections when not signaled by system Confirm acts?  
- What is Paragram-SL999’s OOV coverage on DSTC2/WOZ and how are OOVs handled; any benefit from subword features?

- Writing is clear and architectural choices are well motivated.  
- Including context-module ablation and runtime/memory analysis would strengthen the paper.  
- Releasing code alongside cleaned/extended datasets would enhance reproducibility and impact.
