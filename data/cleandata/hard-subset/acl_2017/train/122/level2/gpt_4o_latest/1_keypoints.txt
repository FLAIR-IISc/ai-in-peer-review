- Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) to scale belief tracking without hand-crafted semantic lexicons using pre-trained word vectors.  
- Proposes two variants, NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN, with CNNs for richer utterance representations.  
- NBT matches or outperforms delexicalisation-based models on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, especially when semantic dictionaries are unavailable.  
- Demonstrates that semantically specialised word vectors significantly improve belief tracking performance.  
- Strength: Eliminates need for hand-crafted lexicons, improving scalability and practicality for diverse domains.  
- Strength: Consistent, statistically significant empirical gains in joint goal accuracy and request tracking.  
- Strength: Innovative integration of representation learning and belief tracking to handle linguistic variation and noisy inputs.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on both noisy (DSTC2) and clean (WOZ 2.0) datasets.  
- Strength: Emphasises the importance of semantic quality in word vector spaces for downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of other limitations (e.g., computational cost, multi-domain applicability) beyond ASR compensation.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns due to missing implementation details such as hyperparameters and training times.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to two restaurant-domain datasets, raising generalization concerns across domains and languages.  
- Weakness: Context modelling uses a Markovian assumption (only last system act), which may oversimplify multi-turn dialogues.  
- Question: How does NBT's computational cost compare to delexicalisation-based approaches for real-time applications?  
- Question: Can the NBT framework be extended to multi-domain dialogue systems, and what challenges are anticipated?  
- Question: Could the Markovian context assumption be relaxed to incorporate longer dialogue history, and how would that affect performance?  
- Overall: Significant, well-motivated advancement with strong empirical results, but would benefit from broader evaluation and more implementation details.
