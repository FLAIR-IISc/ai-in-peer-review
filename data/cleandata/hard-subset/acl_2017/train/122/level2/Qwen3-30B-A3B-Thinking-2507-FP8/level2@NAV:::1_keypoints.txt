- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that removes hand-crafted semantic lexicons by using pre-trained word vectors for dialogue state tracking.  
- Key contributions: distributed representations of utterances/context, matching or outperforming lexicon-based models, and highlighting need for semantically-specialized word vectors.  
- Addresses a significant practical limitation for scaling dialogue systems by avoiding domain-specific lexicons.  
- Evaluation on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 shows statistically significant improvements (p<0.05) over baselines.  
- Ablation study (Paragram-SL999, GloVe, random) convincingly shows semantic quality of word vectors is critical.  
- Lacks discussion of scalability to large ontologies with thousands of slot-value pairs, a major deployment concern.  
- ASR robustness is underdeveloped: large performance gap between clean text and ASR outputs (91.6% vs. 73.4% goal accuracy) with no concrete mitigation strategies.  
- Comparison to recent neural DST models using attention/transformers is incomplete, weakening the novelty claim.  
- Well-written and promising, but needs further work on scalability and ASR robustness before practical adoption.
