- Proposal: Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) framework (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) using pre-trained word vectors to eliminate hand-crafted semantic lexicons for dialogue state tracking.

- Contribution: Data-driven belief tracking that avoids manual lexicons.

- Contribution: Shows semantic word vectors effectively handle lexical variation.

- Contribution: Empirical results show NBT matches or outperforms lexicon-based methods, especially when lexicons are unavailable.

- Strength: Addresses a major practical scalability limitation of lexicon-dependent dialogue systems with clear real-world motivation.

- Strength: Thorough evaluation on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 with statistically significant improvements (e.g., NBT-CNN 73.4% vs 72.9% on DSTC2; 84.2% vs 83.7% on WOZ).

- Strength: Ablation study demonstrates Paragram-SL999 vectors significantly outperform GloVe and random initialization, highlighting semantic quality importance.

- Strength: Advances understanding of leveraging pre-trained semantic representations in downstream tasks with practical benefits.

- Weakness: Lacks sufficient detail and analysis on handling out-of-vocabulary words that are semantically unrelated to known slot values.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to the restaurant domain, raising questions about generalizability to more complex domains and larger ontologies.

- Weakness: Computational cost, inference time, and memory requirements—especially for NBT-CNN with 300 filters—are not addressed.

- Weakness: No discussion of scalability to very large ontologies (hundreds of slots, thousands of values).

- Question: Can you provide details/evidence on how the model handles OOV words unrelated to slot values and whether this is a practical limitation?

- Question: Have you tested the model on domains with larger ontologies to assess scalability beyond restaurant search?

- Question: How do the computational requirements of NBT compare to traditional lexicon-based approaches for real-time deployment?

- Overall: Solid, novel, and well-evaluated contribution with clear practical implications; would be strengthened by addressing OOV handling, domain generalizability, and computational/scalability concerns.
