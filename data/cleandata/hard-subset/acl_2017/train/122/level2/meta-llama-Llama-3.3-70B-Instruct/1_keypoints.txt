- Proposes a Neural Belief Tracking (NBT) framework using pre-trained word vectors to handle lexical and morphological variation in dialogue state tracking.  
- NBT couples Spoken Language Understanding (SLU) and Dialogue State Tracking (DST) without relying on hand-crafted semantic lexicons.  
- Evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, matching lexicon-based state-of-the-art and outperforming them when lexicons are unavailable.  
- Shows improved performance overall, especially on WOZ 2.0 with longer, richer sentences.  
- More scalable to larger and more complex dialogue domains due to elimination of manual lexicons.  
- Flexible to different word vector collections; semantically specialized vectors yield notable gains.  
- Depends on availability and quality of pre-trained word vectors, which may be limited in some languages or domains.  
- Evaluation is limited to two datasets; broader and multilingual testing is needed.  
- Compares to only a few baselines; more comparisons with other state-of-the-art models are required.  
- Asks how authors will address dependence on pre-trained vectors in low-resource languages.  
- Requests additional evaluations on other datasets and different languages.  
- Queries how NBT can be extended to multi-domain dialogue systems and what challenges/benefits that entails.
