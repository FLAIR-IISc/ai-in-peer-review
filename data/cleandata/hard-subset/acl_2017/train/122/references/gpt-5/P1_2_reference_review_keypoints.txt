- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), neural SLU/DST models operating in pre-trained word-vector space without delexicalization or hand-crafted dictionaries.
- Presents two NBT variants: NBT-DNN (summed n-grams + MLP) and NBT-CNN (convolutions over 1–3-grams + max pooling).
- Uses a semantic decoder computing interaction r ⊙ c between utterance and candidate slot–value, plus a simple context-gating module for last-turn system acts.
- Aggregates N-best ASR hypotheses and prior belief with a rule-based belief update.
- Empirical results on DSTC2 and expanded WOZ 2.0 show NBT matches or outperforms delexicalization-based baselines, especially without semantic dictionaries.
- Demonstrates semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) consistently improve DST performance over GloVe and random vectors.
- Methodologically simple, modular, and reproducible architecture trained with standard cross-entropy.
- Leverages fixed pre-trained vectors and parameter sharing to generalize to paraphrases and lexical variants without word-level annotations.
- Sensible handling of ASR uncertainty (N-best weighting) and last-turn context (request/confirm gating); identifies ASR errors as main failure mode.
- Limited context modeling: only uses previous system acts and ignores longer-range dialogue dynamics like anaphora and corrections.
- Scalability concerns: iterates over all slot–value pairs, uses per-slot parameters, and lacks complexity/latency analysis or experiments on large ontologies.
- Belief update is heuristic and decoupled from the classifier (single λ), with no learned joint temporal dynamics or calibration analysis.
- Evaluation is narrow (single-domain Cambridge restaurant tasks), with modest DSTC2 gains and limited comparisons to contemporary end-to-end/attention-based models.
- Ablations and error analysis are limited beyond embedding comparisons; contributions of semantic decoder, context gating, CNN vs DNN, and negation handling are unclear.
- Raises distributional antonymy/negation issues—specialized vectors help but may not fully resolve contradictions like “not expensive.”
- Asks whether multi-turn context (attention/RNN), shared vs per-slot parameters, and learned or slot-specific λ were tried and how they affect performance.
- Requests analysis of computational costs and inference time as ontology size grows, and whether candidate pruning is used.
- Requests robustness checks: mean/std over multiple runs to substantiate significance and sensitivity to random initialization.
- Asks for targeted evaluation/examples on negation/polarity handling and whether explicit modeling was attempted.
- Suggests releasing cleaned DSTC2 transcriptions and expanded WOZ 2.0, plus qualitative error analysis on paraphrases, antonyms, and confirm/request cases.
