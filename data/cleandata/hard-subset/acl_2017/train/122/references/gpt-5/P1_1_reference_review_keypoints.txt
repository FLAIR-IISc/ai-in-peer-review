- Proposal: Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), joint SLU/DST models that avoid delexicalization by operating in semantically specialized embedding space and matching utterances to candidate slot-value pairs.  
- Architecture: Two variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) differ in utterance encoding and use simple context gating based on the last system act.  
- Contribution 1: A simple, effective DST approach that dispenses with hand-crafted semantic dictionaries via pre-trained specialized word vectors and candidate-pair matching.  
- Contribution 2: Empirical results on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 showing parity or improvement over lexicon-augmented baselines and strong gains when lexicons are unavailable, with analysis on embedding quality.  
- Contribution 3: Lightweight context modeling (dot-product gate with last system act) to handle confirmations/requests without complex sequence models.  
- Strength: Removes reliance on semantic dictionaries while maintaining or improving accuracy, addressing a practical DST scalability issue.  
- Strength: Interpretable, parameter-sharing architecture (candidate-pair formulation) that is data-efficient and avoids per-value models.  
- Strength: Minimal, effective context handling aligned with common SDS pipelines.  
- Strength: Careful comparison of embeddings (random vs GloVe vs Paragram-SL999) highlighting the need for semantic specialization.  
- Strength: Broad evaluation on both ASR (DSTC2) and typed (WOZ 2.0) datasets with joint-goal/request reporting and significance testing.  
- Weakness: Incremental novelty — combines standard components (pre-trained embeddings, shallow composition, CNN/DNN) rather than introducing fundamentally new techniques.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns — O(|S||V|) per-turn computation not quantified and may be problematic for large ontologies.  
- Weakness: Limited context modeling — relies only on last system act, ignoring longer dialogue history and lacking ablation.  
- Weakness: Shallow ASR handling — rule-based belief update over N-best and no evaluation on lattices or deeper ASR integration.  
- Weakness: Missing training details — class imbalance, negative sampling, weighting, batch composition, and regularization are not described, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Dependency on Paragram-SL999 — weaker gains with GloVe and no exploration of other specialized embeddings or fine-tuning; multi-word value representation by summation is simplistic.  
- Weakness: Limited ablations — missing component-wise studies (context gating, interaction choices, fixed vs fine-tuned embeddings, encoder comparisons).  
- Question: Please provide ablation of context modeling (remove dr/dc; request vs confirm separately) and quantify impact on affirmations/requests.  
- Question: How is class imbalance handled during training — negative sampling, class weighting, threshold tuning beyond 0.5?  
- Question: What is the computational cost per turn as a function of ontology size and are there batching/pruning strategies?  
- Question: Report sensitivity of belief update hyperparameter λ with a small sweep and performance curves.  
- Question: Did you try fine-tuning embeddings (low LR) vs keeping fixed, and effects on unseen synonyms/generalization?  
- Question: How many random seeds/runs were used, and what are variances/significance vs dictionary-augmented baselines?  
- Question: For multi-word slot values, did you compare sum vs mean vs CNN/attention, and is there degradation for longer values?  
- Question: Clarify which parameters are shared across slots/values and how much sharing is used in reported models.  
- Additional comment: Emphasize “no lexicon” setting as the primary contribution given modest gains over dictionary-augmented baselines.  
- Additional comment: Clarify use of cleaned DSTC2 transcriptions to ensure fair comparison to prior work.  
- Additional comment: A qualitative error analysis (synonyms, antonyms, long-distance references) would add insight.  
- Additional comment: Releasing code and cleaned data would materially improve reproducibility.
