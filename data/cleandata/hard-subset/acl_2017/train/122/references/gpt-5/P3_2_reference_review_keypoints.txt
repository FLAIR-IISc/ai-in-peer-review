- Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT), a joint SLU/DST model that avoids delexicalisation by operating in pre-trained embedding space; proposes NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN variants.
- Adds a lightweight context module conditioning on last system acts via gating and a semantic decoder aligning utterance and candidate slot–value embeddings.
- Shows NBT matches or outperforms strong delex baselines on DSTC2 and expanded WOZ 2.0; Paragram embeddings outperform GloVe and random in ablation.
- Proposes a simple belief update fusing ASR N-best scores with past belief via a tuned λ and will release cleaned DSTC2 and expanded WOZ 2.0 data.
- Contribution: practical, dictionary-free DST approach that jointly scores candidate pairs with a small context gate, yielding strong benchmark results.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) provide measurable DST gains over generic vectors.
- Contribution: data and reproducibility contributions — cleaned DSTC2 transcripts, expanded WOZ 2.0, and transparent training/evaluation.
- Strength: removes reliance on semantic dictionaries while matching or improving accuracy compared to lexicon-based delexicalised systems.
- Strength: methodologically simple and clear architecture with a favorable accuracy/complexity trade-off versus heavier end-to-end models.
- Strength: careful analysis showing semantic specialization of embeddings (Paragram > GloVe > random) is a key factor, especially on noisy ASR data.
- Strength: robustness to paraphrase and lexical variation through fixed pre-trained vectors and shared parameters across values.
- Strength: practical ASR integration via N-best fusion; core semantic model performs strongly on clean transcripts.
- Weakness: limited contextual modeling — only last system acts considered; lacks handling for corrections, co-reference, implicit negation, and multi-turn changes.
- Weakness: scalability concerns — iterates over all slot–value pairs per turn, no runtime/memory analysis or large-ontology experiments.
- Weakness: evaluation scope limited to single-domain Cambridge restaurant datasets and lacks empirical comparison to recent non-delex neural baselines.
- Weakness: ad hoc belief update with hand-tuned λ and fixed threshold; ignores global consistency and may mishandle negation and goal revision.
- Weakness: tension between claimed parameter sharing and per-slot parameters in NBT-DNN; unclear whether per-slot params drive gains.
- Question: What is the quantitative contribution of the context gating vs the semantic decoder, and what if more than the last system act is used?
- Question: What are runtime per-turn and memory footprints as ontology size grows, and have you tried multi-domain experiments to test sharing?
- Question: How does the model handle negation/corrections (“not X”, “anything but X”, mid-dialogue goal changes); any targeted analysis?
- Question: Which parameter-sharing configuration yielded reported results, and what is the performance gap to full sharing?
- Question: What fraction of tokens are OOV with Paragram in DSTC2/WOZ and how sensitive are results to embedding coverage or domain shift?
- Question: What is the unit of analysis for significance testing (dialogue vs turn) and how were multiple comparisons controlled?
- Overall recommendation: Solid, practical contribution that removes dependence on semantic dictionaries and demonstrates value of specialized embeddings; acceptable despite contextual and evaluation limitations.
