- Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained semantically specialized embeddings (no delexicalization/dictionaries) with two variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN).  
- Composes utterance n-grams, performs semantic decoding via element-wise interactions with slot–value vectors, and uses lightweight context gating on last system acts.  
- Includes a simple belief-state update aggregating ASR N-best lists and smoothing with the previous belief.  
- Empirically matches or exceeds delexicalization-based baselines on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, especially with Paragram-SL999 embeddings.  

Strengths:
- Convincing reframing that high-quality semantic vectors can replace costly lexicons, improving scalability and paraphrase robustness.  
- Simple, well-motivated architecture (n-gram composition, element-wise interaction, context gating) that is computationally straightforward and effective.  
- Strong empirical evidence on two datasets, outperforming baselines without handcrafted dictionaries and showing notable gains on WOZ.  
- Insightful analysis showing semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) improve downstream DST performance, especially under noise.  
- Model and data preprocessing are described clearly, aiding reproducibility; authors plan to release cleaned data.

Weaknesses:
- Context modeling is limited to the last system act, ignoring broader dialogue history and discourse phenomena (corrections, negation, coreference).  
- Scalability/parameter-sharing claims are questionable: slot-specific parameters and iteration over all slot–value pairs may not scale to large ontologies.  
- ASR robustness/fairness concerns: trained on transcripts but evaluated on ASR hypotheses without training on ASR N-best/confusion networks; belief update uses tuned λ with no sensitivity analysis.  
- Sparse ablations and error analysis: limited component-level ablation, no qualitative error analysis of failure modes (antonymy, negation, rare values).  
- Limited evaluation scope: single small domain, near-ceiling request accuracy, no multi-domain/large-ontology or zero-shot tests.

Questions for authors:
- Did you train on ASR N-best/hypotheses or only on cleaned transcripts; what are results when training on ASR inputs?  
- What is the impact of removing dr/dc or using only one context gate; any experiments beyond the last-act Markov assumption?  
- Can you report results for other interaction functions (bilinear term, attention) to justify element-wise product choice?  
- How many parameters are slot-specific vs shared, and can you evaluate zero-shot generalization to unseen values/slots?  
- What is the positive:negative label ratio per turn/slot–value, and were loss weighting or sampling strategies used to address imbalance?  
- How sensitive are results to the belief smoothing coefficient λ and the 0.5 decision threshold; any calibration analysis?

Additional suggestions:
- Provide qualitative analyses (nearest-neighbor paraphrases captured, failure cases like negation/polysemy).  
- Report per-slot performance and breakdowns by turn length to clarify where CNN vs DNN helps.  
- Compare fixed embeddings to light fine-tuning with a semantic regularizer to assess benefits of fixing embeddings.
