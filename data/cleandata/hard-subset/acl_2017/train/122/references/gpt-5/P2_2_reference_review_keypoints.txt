- Primary contribution: Proposes the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that reasons over pre-trained semantically-specialized word vectors to avoid delexicalisation and hand-crafted semantic dictionaries.
- Secondary contributions: Presents two NBT instantiations (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN), a simple context-gating mechanism for last system acts, and a lightweight rule-based belief update over ASR N-best lists.
- Tertiary contribution: Empirical results on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 show NBT matches or outperforms delexicalisation baselines and benefits from Paragram-SL999 embeddings.

- Strength: Eliminates dependency on semantic dictionaries without losing accuracy, addressing a practical scalability concern.
- Strength: Simple, interpretable architecture (fixed pre-trained vectors, summed n-grams/CNNs, element-wise interaction, multiplicative context gating) that generalizes to unseen phrasings.
- Strength: Solid empirical evaluation with comparisons on noisy ASR and text data, joint goal/request metrics, and statistical testing.
- Strength: Useful ablation on word vector choice showing semantic specialization (Paragram) matters for DST performance.
- Strength: Commits to releasing cleaned/transcribed datasets, aiding reproducibility and community resources.

- Weakness: Limited domain and ontology scale; does not measure runtime/latency, memory, or test on larger multi-domain ontologies despite scalability claims.
- Weakness: Shallow context modeling using only the last system act; lacks ablations and analysis on longer-range dependencies, corrections, and coreference.
- Weakness: Training/evaluation mismatch on ASR (trained on transcriptions, tested on ASR hypotheses); simplistic ASR handling and belief update with no sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Limited empirical comparison to newer attention/memory-based end-to-end trackers that might also exploit pre-trained embeddings.
- Weakness: Incomplete ablations and analyses (no quantitative comparison of interaction methods, per-slot breakdown, error types, or embedding fine-tuning effects).

- Question: What is computational complexity and per-turn latency/memory as a function of ontology size (|S||V|), and are there bottlenecks for multi-domain settings?
- Question: Were ablations run removing context gating or using more than the last system act, and how much do confirms/requests benefit specifically?
- Question: Did you try fine-tuning pre-trained vectors, and how sensitive are results to freezing vs fine-tuning and to OOV handling (subword/char models)?
- Question: Were models trained on ASR hypotheses (N-best augmentation), does that close the DSTC2 gap, and how sensitive are results to the λ belief-update parameter?
- Question: How does NBT handle negation, corrections, and multi-value mentions within a turn, and can you provide qualitative error analysis?
- Question: Will you release code in addition to the cleaned DSTC2 and expanded WOZ 2.0 datasets?

- Overall: Well-motivated, clearly written, and empirically supported work that meaningfully reduces reliance on lexicons but is limited by scalability experiments and incomplete ablations.
