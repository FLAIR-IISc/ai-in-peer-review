- Proposes NBT: joint SLU/DST without delexicalization using pre-trained word embeddings; two variants NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.  
- Adds a simple interpretable context module gating utterance with last system act and a semantic decoder scoring utterance–candidate via element-wise product.  
- Empirical results on DSTC2 (ASR) and WOZ 2.0 (typed) show parity or gains over delexicalized baselines and benefit from semantically-specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999).  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated move from lexicon-based delexicalization to vector-space matching with an effective, lightweight semantic decoder.  
- Strength: solid empirical performance—large gains on WOZ 2.0 and modest but significant improvements on DSTC2, highlighting ASR robustness issues.  
- Strength: careful analysis showing semantic specialization of embeddings substantially helps DST performance.  
- Strength: simple, interpretable architecture (utterance encoder, candidate encoder, context gates, decision layer) with transparent last-system-act gating.  
- Strength: practical approach replacing hand-crafted lexicons with public embeddings and providing cleaned datasets for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—main change is replacing lexicons with pre-trained paraphrastic embeddings and a simple gating mechanism.  
- Weakness: shallow context modeling—only uses last system act, inadequate for corrections, coreference, multi-turn goal changes, or longer user history.  
- Weakness: reliance on external semantic resources (Paragram/PPDB) raises fairness/resource-parity concerns versus delexicalized baselines.  
- Weakness: scalability and inference cost not addressed—iterates over all slot–value pairs per turn and unclear parameter-sharing claims affect large/multi-domain applicability.  
- Weakness: heuristic belief update and calibration (hand-tuned interpolation λ, fixed threshold, simple rules) departs from principled Bayesian updates and lacks robustness analysis.  
- Question: clarify parameter sharing—did reported experiments use per-slot parameters or full sharing, and are results for full sharing available?  
- Question: provide ablations quantifying contributions of context gates, semantic decoder variants (element-wise vs bilinear/cosine), and CNN vs DNN, plus effect of removing/replacing context module.  
- Question: report a delexicalized baseline augmented with PPDB (or similar) to match Paragram resource use and isolate continuous vs discrete paraphrase benefits.  
- Question: how are OOV tokens handled at test time (UNK, subword modeling); provide OOV rates and their impact given emphasis on morphology.  
- Question: provide runtime per turn as a function of number of slot–value pairs, results on larger ontologies/multi-domain setups, and whether approximate nearest-neighbor over value embeddings could reduce enumeration.  
- Question: clarify significance testing—how p-values for joint goal accuracy were computed (per-turn vs per-dialogue) and whether multiple-comparison corrections were applied.  
- Additional comment: compare interpolation-based state update to Bayesian/HMM-style updates or a learned recurrent update to strengthen claims.  
- Additional comment: include per-slot breakdowns and case studies (paraphrase coverage, confirm/request handling) to illustrate where NBT provides the most benefit.
