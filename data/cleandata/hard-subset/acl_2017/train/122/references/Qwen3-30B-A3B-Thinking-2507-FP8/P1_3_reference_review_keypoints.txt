- Introduces the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) family for DST that removes dependence on hand-crafted semantic dictionaries by using pre-trained word vectors.
- NBT integrates SLU and DST into a single framework and leverages semantic vectors to recognize paraphrases and lexical variation.
- Main contributions: practical DST framework without semantic dictionaries, showing Paragram-SL999 vectors improve performance, and matching/deceeding delexicalization baselines depending on dictionary availability.

- Practical contribution: addresses infeasibility of creating semantic dictionaries for complex domains, improving scalability of dialogue systems.
- Strong empirical validation: experiments on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 with multiple baselines and ablations show Paragram-SL999 vectors notably boost performance, especially on noisy ASR data.
- Clear technical exposition: well-justified NBT architecture with explained representation learning, semantic decoding, context modeling, and NBT-DNN vs NBT-CNN design choices.

- Limited novelty: core use of CNNs/DNNs for sentence representation is not highly novel in NLP and the paper does not clearly differentiate its DST-specific innovations.
- Modest performance gains: only 1–3% absolute improvement over delexicalization baselines, raising questions about practical significance for real-world systems.
- Limited evaluation of OOV handling: lacks concrete examples or analysis of handling unseen slot values, ASR errors, and completely new slot values at test time.
- No computational analysis: paper omits training/inference time and complexity comparisons with baseline approaches, hindering assessment of deployment trade-offs.

- Q1: Clarify the practical significance of NBT "matching" delexicalization baselines given minimal differences in goal accuracy.
- Q2: Explain how NBT handles user expressions that don't exactly match ontology entries — ignore or map to closest value?
- Q3: Recommend strategies for domains where creating a semantic dictionary is possible but difficult versus impossible.
- Q4: How to adapt NBT when pre-trained vectors do not capture domain-specific semantics (e.g., specialized medical terminology)?

- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational complexity and provide concrete OOV handling examples; paper is well-written, technically sound, and practically valuable.
