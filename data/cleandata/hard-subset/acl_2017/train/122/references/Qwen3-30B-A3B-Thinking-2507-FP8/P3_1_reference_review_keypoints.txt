- Main contribution 1: Shows pre-trained semantic word vectors can replace hand-crafted semantic dictionaries for DST, matching or improving performance without manual lexicons.  
- Main contribution 2: Proposes simple, effective NBT architectures (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) that integrate semantic understanding into belief tracking and avoid separate SLU.  
- Main contribution 3: Validates NBT on two datasets (DSTC2, WOZ 2.0), showing consistent improvements, especially on cleaner/richer language data.

- Strength 1: Addresses a real scalability problem by removing the need for domain-specific semantic dictionaries.  
- Strength 2: Strong ablation evidence that high-quality semantic vectors (Paragram-SL999) matter and outperform GloVe/random initialization.  
- Strength 3: Empirical results are strong and statistically significant across datasets, with larger gains on WOZ 2.0.  
- Strength 4: Provides practical implementation details, including ASR N-best handling useful for deployment.  
- Strength 5: Clear, well-organized presentation with effective figures illustrating the model.

- Weakness 1: Novelty claim is overstated; prior work has used word embeddings for DST and should be acknowledged (e.g., Rocktäschel et al., Mesnil et al.).  
- Weakness 2: Baseline comparisons omit more recent, competitive neural DST models, so positioning versus state-of-the-art is inadequate.  
- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis of ASR robustness—model appears highly sensitive to ASR errors but lacks detailed investigation or mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness 4: Lacks detailed ablation studies to justify architectural choices (CNN vs DNN, context module importance).  
- Weakness 5: Limited analysis of failure cases and example-level comparisons to explain where and why NBT succeeds or fails.

- Question 1: Clarify how this approach differs from prior work that used pre-trained embeddings for DST and why those works are not equivalent.  
- Question 2: Provide results using actual DSTC2 ASR outputs (not just transcriptions) to evaluate ASR robustness.  
- Question 3: Explain why recent neural DST models (e.g., Mrkšić et al., Wang et al., Peng et al.) were not compared and whether NBT would outperform them.  
- Question 4: Clarify what is meant by "semantic dictionary" in comparisons and how it differs from the baseline delexicalization used.  
- Question 5: Explain the WOZ 2.0 experimental setup, specifically how comparisons to "models with semantic dictionary" were made given WOZ lacks such dictionaries.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept conditional on revisions to acknowledge prior work and include stronger comparisons to recent neural DST baselines.
