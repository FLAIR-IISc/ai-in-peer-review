- Paper introduces the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) that uses pre-trained word vectors to remove need for hand-crafted semantic dictionaries in dialogue state tracking.
- Proposes two NBT variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) that jointly perform spoken language understanding and belief tracking by comparing utterances, system acts, and slot-value candidates.
- Evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 showing NBT matches dictionary-based delexicalisation and outperforms when dictionaries are unavailable.
- Major contribution: high-quality semantic word vectors can replace manual semantic dictionaries, improving scalability across domains.
- Contribution: a well-structured architecture with semantic decoding and context modeling mechanisms that leverage word vector spaces.
- Contribution: empirical validation demonstrating NBT’s practical value and performance parity or gains over baselines.
- Strength: eliminates manual lexicon construction, addressing a key deployment bottleneck for dialogue systems.
- Strength: clear experimental setup with appropriate baselines, two complementary datasets, and standard metrics.
- Strength: insight that semantic vector quality (e.g., Paragram-SL999) strongly impacts DST performance, especially with noisy ASR.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to more recent state-of-the-art DST models, limiting positioning against current work.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure cases and limitations, particularly on ASR-noisy data like DSTC2.
- Weakness: no discussion of computational efficiency or scalability in training/inference and with larger ontologies.
- Question: provide comparisons with newer DST models (e.g., DSTC4/DSTC5 or Mrkšić et al. work) to contextualize results.
- Question: clarify how parameter sharing across slot values and across slots is implemented and its effect on performance.
- Question: quantify how much performance gain comes from semantic specialization versus general pre-training of word vectors.
- Question: detail effects of sharing parameters across slots on performance, model size, and training time.
- Question: discuss potential impact of fine-tuning pre-trained word vectors during training and trade-offs (data, overfitting).
- Overall recommendation: valuable and practical contribution with strong experiments, but needs comparisons to recent baselines and deeper analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
