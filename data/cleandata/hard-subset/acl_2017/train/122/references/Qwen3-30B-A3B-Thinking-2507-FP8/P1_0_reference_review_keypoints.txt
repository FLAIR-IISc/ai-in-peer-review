- Introduces the Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) for dialogue state tracking that uses pre-trained word vectors to avoid manual semantic dictionaries.  
- Claims NBT couples SLU and DST using vector representations of utterances, system acts, and candidate slot-value pairs.  
- Contribution: a DST framework that removes the need for hand-crafted lexicons by leveraging pre-trained embeddings.  
- Contribution: empirical results showing NBT matches or outperforms delexicalisation-based models, especially without semantic dictionaries.  
- Contribution: demonstrates that semantically-specialized vectors (Paragram-SL999) improve DST performance.  
- Strength: Addresses the practical deployment issue of scaling semantic dictionaries in dialogue systems.  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 with appropriate baselines and statistical significance testing.  
- Strength: Clear evidence that the semantic quality of word vectors affects downstream DST performance.  
- Strength: Simple, well-explained NBT architecture with sensible use of pre-trained vectors and model variants.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a small restaurant domain (three slots), so scalability claims are unproven.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to more recent neural DST methods (e.g., attention/transformer-based models).  
- Weakness: No analysis of handling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words common in real-world dialogues.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of computational efficiency, including training/inference times and model size.  
- Weakness: Limited error analysis—no slot-level breakdown or examples of common failure modes.  
- Question: Can NBT generalize to unseen slot values (e.g., new price ranges) via semantic similarity?  
- Question: Would contextualized embeddings (BERT, RoBERTa) further improve NBT and how would they integrate?  
- Question: How to create semantically-specialized vectors like Paragram-SL999 for new domains without manual effort?  
- Question: How does the total human effort for lexicon creation compare to training NBT in real-world deployment?  
- Additional: Paper is well-written and shows value of semantic vectors, but needs larger-ontology evaluation and integration discussion with other dialogue system components.
