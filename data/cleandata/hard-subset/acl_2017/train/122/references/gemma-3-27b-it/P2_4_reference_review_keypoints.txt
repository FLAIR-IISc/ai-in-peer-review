- Overall score: 8/10 (Strong Accept).

- Summary: Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) that use pre-trained word vectors to replace delexicalization, achieving strong DST performance on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, especially in low-resource settings.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates effective use of pre-trained word vectors to capture semantic similarity and reduce reliance on hand-crafted lexicons for DST.

- Contribution 2: Shows competitive or superior empirical performance to delexicalization-based methods, notably when lexical resources are absent.

- Contribution 3: Presents the approach clearly with helpful architecture diagrams.

- Strength 1: Directly addresses the scalability bottleneck from hand-crafted lexicons for new domains.

- Strength 2: Robust results and ablation study show clear benefit of semantically specialized vectors (Paragram-SL999).

- Strength 3: Jointly models SLU and DST, reducing error propagation from pipelined systems.

- Strength 4: Incorporates system-act context (request/confirm) for improved state tracking.

- Strength 5: Commits to releasing cleaned datasets, aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Lacks a detailed error analysis identifying failure modes or challenging slot types/phenomena.

- Weakness 2: Omits discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and stability of results.

- Weakness 3: Comparisons are against somewhat dated baselines; lacks evaluation versus more recent DST architectures (e.g., Transformer-based models).

- Questions for authors: Provide detailed error analysis and common failure cases?

- Questions for authors: How sensitive is performance to the choice of pre-trained embeddings and were other vectors tried beyond Paragram-SL999 and GloVe?

- Questions for authors: Have you explored more complex context modeling, such as longer dialogue history?

- Additional comments: Figure 1 effectively illustrates delexicalization; belief state update is clearly explained; writing quality is excellent.
