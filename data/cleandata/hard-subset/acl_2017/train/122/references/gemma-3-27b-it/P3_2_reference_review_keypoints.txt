- Overall: Strong, well-motivated approach to DST that removes reliance on manual lexicons; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Contribution: Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) family to jointly perform SLU and DST without hand-crafted semantic dictionaries.
- Contribution: Demonstrates NBTs match or outperform delexicalization baselines and excel when lexical resources are limited.
- Contribution: Shows semantically specialized word vectors (not just generic pre-trained embeddings) are critical for performance.
- Strength: Tackles a significant scalability problem in DST by removing manual lexicon dependence.
- Strength: Strong empirical results on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, with particularly convincing gains on WOZ 2.0.
- Strength: Clear, well-explained methodology with helpful diagrams for representation learning, semantic decoding, and context modeling.
- Strength: Valuable ablation on word vector types underscoring the importance of semantic specialization.
- Strength: Commits to releasing cleaned datasets, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: Lacks qualitative/error analysis showing where and why NBTs fail, especially differences between DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 performance.
- Weakness: Context modeling is simplistic (only uses the immediately preceding system act); more sophisticated history encoders could help.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, which impacts reproducibility.
- Question: Please provide qualitative examples of NBT failures and explanations for those errors.
- Question: Is the smaller DSTC2 gain primarily due to ASR noise, and were ASR-robustness techniques (e.g., confidence scores) tried?
- Question: What was the hyperparameter tuning process and how sensitive are results to key hyperparameters (learning rate, embedding size, hidden size)?
- Question: What are the computational costs and inference-time differences between NBT-CNN and NBT-DNN?
- Additional: Consider discussing transformer-based DST related work.
- Additional: Clarify notation (e.g., what '⊗' denotes in Equations 9 and 10).
- Additional: Improve Table 2 formatting for readability (highlight best-performing models).
