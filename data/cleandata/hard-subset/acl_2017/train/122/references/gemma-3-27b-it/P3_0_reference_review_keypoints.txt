- Summary: Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that jointly learn SLU and DST using pre-trained word vectors to avoid hand-crafted delexicalisation; evaluates NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates effective end-to-end DST using pre-trained word vectors as an alternative to manual delexicalisation.  
- Contribution: Shows NBTs achieve competitive performance without semantic dictionaries, improving scalability to new domains.  
- Contribution: Ablation study reveals semantic-specialized vectors (e.g., Paragram-SL999) outperform standard distributional embeddings.  
- Strength: Tackles a major practical bottleneck (manual delexicalisation), making DST more deployable.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results, notably on WOZ 2.0 where lexical variation is high.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful architecture diagrams.  
- Strength: Thorough experimentation across datasets (ASR-noisy and clean) and useful ablation on embeddings.  
- Strength: Emphasizes and evidences the importance of semantic specialization in word vectors.  
- Weakness: Individual model components (DNN/CNN/pretrained vectors) lack novelty; novelty is primarily in their integration.  
- Weakness: Context modelling is simplistic (dot-product with system acts, Markovian); potential performance loss is unclear.  
- Weakness: No detailed error analysis to show which slot-values or utterance types cause failures.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to recent transformer-based DST models (e.g., TRADE, SOM-DST).  
- Question: What is the computational cost of NBT-CNN vs NBT-DNN, and is the performance gain worth it?  
- Question: Have you experimented with richer dialogue-history modeling (e.g., RNNs), and what were the results?  
- Question: Can you provide an error analysis highlighting common failure modes and challenging slot-value pairs?  
- Question: Have you considered incorporating attention mechanisms in representation learning or semantic decoding?  
- Question: Which real-world domains lack delexicalisation resources, and how would NBT perform there compared to existing methods?  
- Comment: The belief state update mechanism needs more analysis, particularly the impact of the λ parameter.  
- Comment: The term "semantic specialisation" is overused; consider a clearer phrasing.  
- Comment: Include model sizes (number of parameters) in experimental results.  
- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper addressing an important problem; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
