- Proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that couple SLU and DST and replace manual delexicalization with pre-trained word vectors.  
- Presents two NBT variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) and shows comparable or better DST performance on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, especially without semantic dictionaries.  
- Offers a practical, scalable alternative to lexicon-based DST that removes the manual lexicon bottleneck.  
- Demonstrates that pre-trained word vectors effectively capture lexical variation and improve DST, especially under limited resources or noisy ASR.  
- Provides a clear, reproducible description of architecture and experiments, and suggests releasing code/datasets would help reproducibility.  
- Addresses an important real-world limitation of DST by removing reliance on manually crafted lexicons.  
- Shows strong empirical results with statistical significance, notably on WOZ 2.0 where no lexical resources exist.  
- Justifies design choices (semantically specialized vectors, architecture) clearly, improving understandability.  
- Conducts thorough experiments across datasets and noise levels, including an ablation highlighting word vector quality.  
- Paper is well written and organized, with informative figures and tables.  
- Individual components (CNNs, DNNs, embeddings) are standard, so novelty lies in their combination rather than new model primitives.  
- Lacks comparison to more recent DST baselines (e.g., transformer-based methods like TRADE or SOM-DST).  
- Uses a simplistic belief state update (averaging); more sophisticated methods or deeper discussion of the λ parameter are missing.  
- Provides limited error analysis; common failure modes and problematic slot-value or utterance types are not explored.  
- Requests clarification on computational cost: training and inference time vs. baselines.  
- Asks about sensitivity to choice of pre-trained vectors and whether alternatives (fastText, BERT) were tested.  
- Requests more training hyperparameter details (learning rate, batch size, epochs).  
- Questions the claim of being the first to use pre-trained vectors for belief tracking and asks for prior related work discussion.  
- Asks about the impact of ASR errors and whether ASR error correction was considered for the NBT framework.  
- Recommends releasing code and datasets to aid reproducibility.  
- Suggests expanding related work to include more recent DST advances.  
- Suggests adding a table summarizing differences between NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.
