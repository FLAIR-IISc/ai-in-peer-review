- Paper introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that jointly perform SLU and DST without hand-crafted lexicons by leveraging pre-trained word vectors.  
- NBTs match delexicalization-based methods and outperform them when no lexicon is available on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates pre-trained, semantically-specialized word vectors can replace manual lexical resources in DST.  
- Contribution: Proposes a joint SLU/DST architecture using continuous vector-space reasoning instead of discrete feature engineering.  
- Contribution: Provides empirical validation on multiple datasets (DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0), showing robustness.  
- Strength: Directly addresses the scalability bottleneck of hand-crafted lexicons for DST across domains and languages.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results, notably in low-resource/no-lexicon scenarios; ablation shows importance of semantic specialization.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with understandable architecture diagrams and explanations.  
- Strength: Authors plan to release cleaned dataset and code, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis identifying specific failure cases (e.g., ambiguity, complex utterances, ASR errors).  
- Weakness: Context modeling is simplistic (only last system act); rationale and alternatives not fully explored.  
- Weakness: No discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity or robustness to different settings (vector size, learning rate, etc.).  
- Weakness: Treatment of ASR robustness is brief; more experiments/analysis on noisy ASR input are needed.  
- Question: Please provide a detailed error analysis outlining common error types and challenging utterances/states.  
- Question: Why model context using only the last system act, and have longer-history or attention-based approaches been tried?  
- Question: How sensitive are NBTs to hyperparameter choices and what guided the selected settings?  
- Question: What experiments were done to mitigate ASR errors (e.g., data augmentation, N-best lists)?  
- Additional comment: Unify formatting of Figure 3 and Figure 4 for consistency.  
- Additional comment: Make notation consistent (e.g., the meaning of 'r' varies across sections).  
- Overall: Strong, relevant paper that advances scalable DST; recommended for acceptance.
