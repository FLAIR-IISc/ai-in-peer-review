- Overall assessment: Solid, well-motivated contribution to DST; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Core idea: Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) family jointly learns SLU and DST using pre-trained word embeddings instead of hand-crafted lexical resources.  
- Contribution 1: Novel DST approach that removes delexicalization and leverages embeddings to generalize to unseen paraphrases.  
- Contribution 2: Effective joint SLU/DST architecture with modules for representation learning, context modelling, and semantic decoding; DNN and CNN variants explored.  
- Contribution 3: Strong empirical validation on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, showing advantage when lexical resources are unavailable and importance of semantically specialized vectors.  
- Strength: Tackles critical limitation of hand-crafted lexicons, improving scalability and adaptability to new domains.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated model design with good explanation of context modelling.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on two datasets, covering ASR noise and clean text scenarios.  
- Strength: Insightful ablation study demonstrating the value of specialized word vectors (random, GloVe, Paragram-SL999).  
- Strength: Commitment to reproducibility via releasing cleaned datasets.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed qualitative/error analysis explaining where and why NBT fails (ambiguous utterances, complex context).  
- Weakness: Belief state update is simplistic and rule-based (lambda=0.55); more advanced strategies (e.g., Bayesian filtering) were not explored.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to recent state-of-the-art neural DST models (transformers, advanced attention mechanisms).  
- Weakness: No discussion of computational cost or efficiency trade-offs versus delexicalization-based approaches.  
- Question: Please provide example error cases and explanations for NBT failures.  
- Question: Have alternative belief state update mechanisms been tried, and what were the results?  
- Question: How do training and inference computational costs compare to delexicalization-based systems?  
- Question: Were other encodings for system dialogue acts explored beyond dot-product similarity?  
- Additional comment: Figure 2 needs more detailed labeling for “Semantic Decoding” and “Context Modeling.”  
- Additional comment: Define the notation 'ku' earlier for clarity.
