- Summary: Introduces Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that jointly perform SLU and DST using pre-trained word vectors to avoid hand-crafted semantic lexicons; two variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) achieve competitive results on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates effective DST without manual semantic lexicons, improving scalability and adaptability.

- Contribution 2: Successfully integrates pre-trained word vectors to capture semantic similarity for robust belief tracking.

- Contribution 3: Provides comprehensive experiments on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, comparing vector types and baselines.

- Strength 1: Addresses a core DST bottleneck (lexicon dependence), making the approach highly relevant and impactful.

- Strength 2: Empirical results match or outperform delexicalisation baselines, especially when lexicons are unavailable.

- Strength 3: Ablation on word vectors (random, GloVe, Paragram-SL999) highlights the importance of semantically specialized embeddings.

- Strength 4: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful diagrams explaining architecture and setup.

- Strength 5: Promises to release cleaned datasets, supporting reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Architectural components (DNNs, CNNs, embeddings) are well-known; novelty lies in integration rather than new architectures.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison to more recent SOTA DST approaches (e.g., Transformers, span prediction), limiting assessment of competitiveness.

- Weakness 3: Belief state update is simplistic (averaging probabilities) and could benefit from more sophisticated methods.

- Weakness 4: Context modeling is limited to the previous system act and may miss longer-range dialogue dependencies.

- Question 1: Request for detailed comparison of computational cost (training/inference) between NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN and any efficiency trade-offs.

- Question 2: How sensitive is performance to pre-trained embedding dimensionality and were different sizes evaluated?

- Question 3: How could ASR confidence scores or N-best lists be incorporated into NBT to handle ASR errors?

- Question 4: How do NBT models handle zero-shot or few-shot scenarios with new slots/values unseen during training?

- Additional comment 1: The paper would benefit from an error analysis describing remaining error types and causes.

- Additional comment 2: Recommend adding a table summarizing hyperparameters for each model variant.

- Additional comment 3: Suggest improving formatting/clarity of some equations.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with promising results and practical impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
