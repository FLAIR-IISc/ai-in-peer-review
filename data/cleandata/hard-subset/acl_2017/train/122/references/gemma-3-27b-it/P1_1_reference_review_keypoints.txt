- Paper proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that jointly learn SLU and DST using pre-trained word vectors to avoid delexicalization.
- NBTs achieve competitive or superior performance to delexicalization-based methods, especially when semantic lexicons are unavailable.
- Key contribution: demonstrates effective use of pre-trained embeddings (e.g., Paragram-SL999) improves DST performance and scalability.
- Key contribution: provides a viable joint SLU/DST approach that reduces reliance on manual lexicons.
- Key contribution: includes comprehensive experiments on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0 with strong baselines.
- Strength: strong empirical results, notably on WOZ 2.0 with high lexical variation.
- Strength: directly addresses scalability limitations of delexicalization for new domains.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful diagrams and dataset/experiment descriptions.
- Strength: insightful ablation study showing importance of embedding choice.
- Strength: implicit robustness to ASR noise suggested by training on DSTC2 ASR outputs.
- Weakness: core architecture (CNNs/DNNs + word vectors) lacks fundamental novelty; primary novelty is application and evaluation.
- Weakness: simplistic context modeling (dot-product similarity, only last system utterance); more advanced context methods might help.
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis identifying common model failure modes.
- Weakness: no sensitivity analysis on hyperparameters, embedding dims, learning rates, or architecture choices.
- Questions: please clarify specific architectural innovations that drive improvements beyond standard CNN/DNN+embeddings.
- Questions: have you tried incorporating broader dialogue history into context modeling and what were results?
- Questions: please provide examples of common errors and suggested strategies to fix them.
- Questions: how well will the approach scale to domains with much larger ontologies and what are the computational bottlenecks from iterating over all slot-value pairs?
- Additional: release code and trained models for reproducibility.
- Additional: consider citing work on contrastive/metric learning for embeddings as relevant to Paragram-SL999’s success.
- Additional: improve table formatting for readability.
- Overall: solid, well-executed paper with convincing results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
