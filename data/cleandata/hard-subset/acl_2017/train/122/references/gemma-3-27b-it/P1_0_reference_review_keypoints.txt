- Paper proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that replace delexicalization with pre-trained word vectors for dialogue state tracking.  
- Two NBT variants (NBT-DNN, NBT-CNN) jointly model SLU and DST.  
- Evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, showing comparable performance to delexicalization methods and superior performance when lexicons are unavailable.  
- Contribution: demonstrates pre-trained word embeddings are a viable alternative to hand-crafted lexicons for DST, aiding domain scalability.  
- Contribution: presents a joint SLU/DST model that eliminates the delexicalization step, simplifying the pipeline.  
- Contribution: provides comprehensive experiments and analysis, including impact of different word vector types.  
- Strength: addresses the practical bottleneck of expensive hand-crafted lexicons for DST.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, especially in low-resource/lexicon-free settings.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful architecture diagrams.  
- Strength: thorough ablation showing importance of semantically-specialized word vectors.  
- Strength: authors commit to releasing code and cleaned datasets for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: core architectural components (DNNs, CNNs, pre-trained vectors) are not highly novel—novelty is mainly in application.  
- Weakness: context modeling uses only the last system utterance (Markovian assumption), which may be limiting.  
- Weakness: lacking comparison with more recent DST models (e.g., Transformer-based approaches).  
- Weakness: belief state update is a simple rule-based mechanism with fixed lambda and may be ad-hoc.  
- Question: How sensitive are NBT models to the choice of pre-trained embeddings, and have contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT) been tried?  
- Question: Clarify the claim of being the first to use pre-trained word vector spaces for belief tracking and how this differs from prior embedding-based DST work.  
- Question: Why was only the last system utterance used for context, and were experiments with longer dialogue history performed?  
- Question: What are the computational costs (training/inference) of NBT models compared to delexicalization baselines?  
- Additional: Paper should discuss limitations and future work in more detail.  
- Additional: Including a sample dialogue with predicted belief states would help illustrate model behavior.  
- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper with practical impact; recommend Accept with minor revisions addressing weaknesses and questions.
