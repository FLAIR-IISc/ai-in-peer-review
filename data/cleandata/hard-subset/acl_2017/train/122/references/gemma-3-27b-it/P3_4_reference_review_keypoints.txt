- Paper proposes Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that use pre-trained word vectors to perform end-to-end dialogue state tracking without hand-crafted delexicalization.
- NBT jointly learns SLU and DST and is evaluated on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, matching or outperforming delexicalization baselines.
- Main contribution: shows pre-trained semantic word vectors can replace manual semantic lexicons, improving scalability of DST.
- Main contribution: provides a strong delexicalization baseline and thorough evaluation including with/without semantic dictionaries.
- Main contribution: analyzes importance of semantically specialized embeddings (Paragram-SL999) for DST performance.
- Strength: addresses a key scalability bottleneck (manual delexicalization) in DST.
- Strength: strong empirical results, especially on WOZ 2.0 where semantic understanding is clearer.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful architecture figures.
- Strength: insightful ablation study demonstrating the value of specialized word vectors.
- Strength: practical considerations discussed (belief state update, ASR error handling).
- Weakness: architecture itself (DNN/CNN + element-wise multiplication) lacks strong novelty; novelty is mainly in embedding use.
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent neural DST SOTA (e.g., transformer- or attention-based models).
- Weakness: insufficient error analysis — missing breakdowns for failure cases like negation or complex queries.
- Weakness: simplistic context modeling using only the previous system act; more history-aware models might improve performance.
- Question: please justify architectural choices (e.g., element-wise multiplication vs. attention mechanisms).
- Question: how does NBT compare to recent transformer/attention-based DST models; any experiments or results?
- Question: provide a detailed error analysis highlighting common failure cases (negation, implicit constraints).
- Question: have you tried richer context modeling (e.g., hierarchical recurrent encoders) and what were the outcomes?
- Question: what is the computational cost of NBT vs. baselines, considering pre-trained vectors and CNNs?
- Additional comment: discuss limitations such as embedding biases and out-of-vocabulary handling.
- Additional comment: releasing code and trained models would improve reproducibility.
- Recommendation: overall positive — recommend acceptance contingent on addressing raised questions and weaknesses.
