- Presents Neural Belief Tracker (NBT) models that jointly learn SLU and DST using pre-trained word vectors to avoid hand-crafted semantic lexicons.
- Evaluates two NBT variants (DNN, CNN) on DSTC2 and WOZ 2.0, showing competitive performance with delexicalization baselines and improvements when lexicons are unavailable.
- Key contribution: effective integration of pre-trained word vectors into joint SLU/DST to handle lexical variation without explicit delexicalization.
- Shows NBTs match or outperform strong baselines, especially in low-resource settings lacking handcrafted lexicons.
- Provides a clear, thorough evaluation with ablation studies highlighting the importance of semantically-specialized word vectors.
- Addresses the critical limitation of reliance on hand-crafted lexicons in traditional DST approaches.
- Experimental results are strong and consistent across datasets, with a larger advantage where lexicon resources are sparse.
- Design choices (e.g., specialized vectors, element-wise context modeling) are well-motivated and empirically justified.
- Paper is well-written, with helpful architecture diagrams and claimed dataset release aiding reproducibility.
- Individual components (DNNs, CNNs, pre-trained vectors) are not novel; main novelty is their integration and application to DST.
- Context modeling is simplistic, relying only on the last system act rather than richer dialogue history representations.
- Lacks detailed error analysis identifying specific failure modes or cases where lexicon-based models succeed but NBT fails.
- Scalability claims are untested on large, complex ontologies beyond the small restaurant domain used.
- Requests more analysis of performance across varying levels of lexicon availability (small/medium/large).
- Asks whether incorporating more dialogue history was tested and what the outcomes were.
- Requests clarification on computational complexity and how NBT scales with ontology size and dialogue length.
- Seeks details on the most frequent error types and problematic slot/value combinations for NBT.
- Notes some notation could use clearer variable names and suggests discussing real-world deployment challenges (e.g., noisy ASR).
- Overall: solid, valuable contribution with strong results; recommendation for acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
