- Presents a neural-network framework for dialogue state tracking using pre-trained word vectors.  
- Main contribution: learning representations for user utterances, system outputs, and ontology entries.  
- Compares two utterance representation models: NBT-DNN and NBT-CNN.  
- Combines learned representations to make binary decisions for slot-value pairs.  
- Experimental results show significant improvement over a delexicalized baseline.  
- Overall quality is good, with clear goals and reasonable ideas leading to improved results.  
- Paper organization and readability are weak; details are not delivered effectively for non-experts.  
- Requests a formal definition of DST early and clearer placement of general dialogue system architecture.  
- Notations (e.g., tq, ts, t_v) are introduced before being defined; suggest defining symbols earlier.  
- Clarify whether the model can perform separate SLU—term "joint" may be misleading if not.  
- Ask for statistics on corrected DSTC2 dataset and comparisons with other DSTC2 entries if corrections are small.  
- Suggest exploring RNNs/LSTMs for sequential utterance modeling given their SLU success.  
- Request more details on the semantic dictionary used in the baseline to indicate manual cost.  
- Recommend providing example cases where the proposed model fixes baseline errors.
