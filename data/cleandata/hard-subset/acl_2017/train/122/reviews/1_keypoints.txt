- Proposes a novel DST method that represents slot values with pre-trained embeddings and composes them into distributed utterance and context representations.  
- Shows consistent, significant improvements over previous delexicalization-based baselines on two datasets.  
- Evaluates alternative embedding pre-training approaches (XAVIER, GloVe, Program-SL999).  
- Datasets appear limited, raising concerns about generalization to more complex dialogue domains where delexicalization fails.  
- Lacks comparison with a separate slot-tagging component; computing similarity restricted to slot-value spans could change results.  
- Intro example may be misleading about dialogue state (e.g., restaurant_name should be resolved), prompting questions about coreference resolution handling.  
- Overall assessment: using pre-trained word embeddings is a strong and exciting idea.
