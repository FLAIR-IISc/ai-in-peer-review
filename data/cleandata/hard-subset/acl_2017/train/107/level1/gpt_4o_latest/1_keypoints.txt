- Paper proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via mapping target embeddings into English space.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes (rank-based and confidence-based) to combine outputs of the two approaches.  
- Evaluated on in-house and CoNLL datasets across multiple languages, showing significant improvements over prior state-of-the-art.  
- Heuristic data selection for annotation projection effectively filters noisy projection labels and yields substantial F1 gains, especially for low-alignment languages (e.g., Korean, Japanese).  
- Representation projection enables direct model transfer using cross-lingual embeddings and avoids retraining on target languages.  
- Co-decoding schemes effectively combine complementary strengths of both methods, achieving state-of-the-art results.  
- Methods achieve strong performance often approaching supervised learning baselines.  
- The annotation projection approach is robust to noisy data thanks to the heuristic filtering.  
- Representation projection is scalable and practical, requiring only monolingual embeddings and a small bilingual dictionary.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, covering multiple languages/datasets and comparisons with supervised and prior cross-lingual methods.  
- The representation projection method has limited methodological novelty, largely building on existing cross-lingual embedding techniques.  
- Annotation projection performance depends on alignment model quality and can struggle for languages with poor alignments despite filtering.  
- Paper lacks detailed qualitative error analysis to diagnose system limitations.  
- Discussion is limited on computational costs and scalability of training embeddings and alignment models for new languages.  
- Questions raised: sensitivity to bilingual dictionary size/quality for representation projection.  
- Question on whether co-decoding can incorporate additional weak/unsupervised NER systems and its expected impact.  
- Question on applicability to non-standard scripts or languages with limited monolingual corpora and associated challenges.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contributions and compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor reservations about error analysis and scalability discussion.
