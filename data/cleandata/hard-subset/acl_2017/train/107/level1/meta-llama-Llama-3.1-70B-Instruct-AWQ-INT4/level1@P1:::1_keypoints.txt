- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with heuristic data selection, and representation projection mapping embeddings to English, plus two co-decoding schemes to combine them.
- Heuristic data selection for annotation projection to filter noisy projected labels and improve NER when alignment is poor.
- Representation projection that maps target-language word embeddings into English so a single English NER model can be used across languages.
- Two co-decoding schemes that combine outputs from both projection approaches to achieve higher accuracy than either alone.
- Novel approach reducing the need for human annotation in target languages.
- Thorough evaluation on multiple in-house and public NER datasets across several target languages.
- Detailed analysis of each approach’s strengths and weaknesses, including effects of alignment quality and data selection.
- Demonstrated effectiveness of co-decoding in improving performance over individual systems.
- Heavy reliance on the quality of comparable corpora and source-target alignment.
- Assumes accurate mapping of target-language embeddings into the English vector space, which may not always hold.
- No detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale NER applications.
- No comparisons to other state-of-the-art NER systems or modern deep-learning architectures.
- How will the methods be extended to handle nested entities or multi-type entities?
- Request for more details on training data and hyperparameters for the representation projection neural models.
- How are out-of-vocabulary target-language words handled when they lack corresponding English embeddings?
- Seek more insight on trade-offs between annotation projection and representation projection across different scenarios.
