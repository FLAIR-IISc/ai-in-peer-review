**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) with no human annotation in the target language. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic data selection scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word embeddings in the target language are projected into the English vector space, allowing a single English NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training. Two co-decoding schemes are also proposed to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches.

**Contributions**

1. **Effective annotation projection approach**: The paper proposes a heuristic data selection scheme that can significantly improve the accuracy of the target-language NER system by selecting good-quality projection-labeled data from noisy comparable corpora.
2. **Representation projection approach**: The paper presents a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection, which allows a single English NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training.
3. **Co-decoding schemes**: The paper proposes two co-decoding schemes that can combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches to produce an output that is more accurate than the outputs of individual systems.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to cross-lingual NER**: The paper proposes a new approach to cross-lingual NER that does not require human annotation in the target language, which is a significant challenge in building NER systems for multiple languages.
2. **Effective use of comparable corpora**: The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of using comparable corpora for annotation projection, which is a valuable resource for cross-lingual NER.
3. **Improved performance**: The paper shows that the combined system achieves higher accuracy than both individual systems and outperforms two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The paper evaluates the proposed approaches on a limited number of languages (Japanese, Korean, German, Portuguese, Spanish, and Dutch) and datasets (in-house and CoNLL NER data).
2. **Dependence on alignment accuracy**: The paper notes that the quality of the projection-labeled data depends on the alignment accuracy of the comparable corpora, which may not always be high.
3. **Limited analysis of co-decoding schemes**: The paper proposes two co-decoding schemes but does not provide a detailed analysis of their strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the proposed approaches perform on languages with limited resources or no comparable corpora?
2. Can the proposed approaches be extended to other NLP tasks, such as part-of-speech tagging or dependency parsing?
3. How do the co-decoding schemes perform on datasets with different characteristics, such as varying entity types or sentence lengths?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and clearly presents the proposed approaches and their evaluation. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the co-decoding schemes and their strengths and weaknesses. Additionally, the paper could provide more insights into the limitations of the proposed approaches and potential future work.
