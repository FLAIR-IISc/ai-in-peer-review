- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods without target annotations—annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping—and two co-decoding schemes to combine them.

- Contribution 1: Heuristic data selection for annotation projection that filters comparable-corpus projections to improve target-language NER accuracy.

- Contribution 2: Representation projection that maps target-language embeddings into the source space so a source NER model can be applied without retraining.

- Contribution 3: Two co-decoding schemes that combine the annotation- and representation-projection outputs and yield higher accuracy than either alone.

- Strength 1: The heuristic selection meaningfully improves quality of noisy projection-labeled data and downstream NER performance.

- Strength 2: Representation projection is a novel, practical model-transfer approach enabling zero-shot application of a source NER system.

- Strength 3: Combined system shows strong empirical results, outperforming two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER baselines.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to only a few datasets, raising questions about generality.

- Weakness 2: No comparison with alternative or existing co-decoding schemes to contextualize the proposed decoders.

- Weakness 3: Lacks analysis of the effect of parameter tuning on method performance.

- Question 1: How do the methods perform across additional languages and datasets?

- Question 2: Can the co-decoding schemes be generalized to other NLP tasks?

- Question 3: How are out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words handled in both approaches?

- Question 4: Can representation projection be applied to other tasks (e.g., MT, text classification)?

- Question 5: How do the approaches cope when source and target languages differ in script or grammatical structure?
