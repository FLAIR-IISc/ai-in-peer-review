- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping, plus two co-decoding schemes.  
- Annotation projection contribution: a heuristic data selection scheme that filters noisy comparable-corpus projections to improve target-language NER accuracy.  
- Representation projection contribution: maps target-language embeddings into English space to apply a single English NER model without re-training.  
- Co-decoding contribution: two schemes to combine outputs of the projection methods for improved accuracy over individual systems.  
- Novelty: addresses cross-lingual NER without target-language human annotation, a notable challenge for multilingual NER.  
- Effective use of comparable corpora: demonstrates that comparable corpora can yield useful projection-labeled data when filtered.  
- Empirical improvement: combined system outperforms each individual method and two state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Limited evaluation: experiments cover only six languages and a small set of datasets (in-house and CoNLL).  
- Alignment dependence: projection-labeled data quality relies on comparable-corpus alignment accuracy, which can be unreliable.  
- Insufficient co-decoding analysis: paper lacks detailed examination of the strengths and weaknesses of the two co-decoding schemes.  
- Open question on low-resource applicability: unclear how methods perform for languages with scarce or no comparable corpora.  
- Generalization question: unclear if approaches extend to other tasks like POS tagging or dependency parsing.  
- Robustness question: unclear how co-decoding performs across datasets with different entity types or sentence-length characteristics.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written but would benefit from deeper analysis of co-decoding, limitations, and future work.
