**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) with no human annotation in the target language. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic data selection scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word embeddings are projected from a target language to a source language, allowing a source-language NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training. Two co-decoding schemes are also proposed to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection**: The paper develops a heuristic scheme to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora, which significantly improves the accuracy of the target-language NER system.
2. **Representation projection approach**: The paper proposes a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection, which projects word embeddings from a target language to a source language, allowing a source-language NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training.
3. **Co-decoding schemes**: The paper proposes two co-decoding schemes to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches, which achieve higher accuracy than both individual systems.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective annotation projection approach**: The paper's heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection is effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora, which improves the accuracy of the target-language NER system.
2. **Novel representation projection approach**: The paper's representation projection approach is a novel contribution to the field of cross-lingual NER, which allows a source-language NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training.
3. **Strong experimental results**: The paper's experimental results show that the combined system achieves higher accuracy than both individual systems and outperforms two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation datasets**: The paper's evaluation is limited to a few datasets, which may not be representative of all possible scenarios.
2. **No comparison with other co-decoding schemes**: The paper only proposes two co-decoding schemes and does not compare them with other existing co-decoding schemes.
3. **No analysis of the impact of parameter tuning**: The paper does not provide an analysis of the impact of parameter tuning on the performance of the proposed approaches.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the proposed approaches perform on other languages and datasets?
2. Can the proposed co-decoding schemes be applied to other NLP tasks?
3. How do the proposed approaches handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words?
4. Can the proposed representation projection approach be used for other NLP tasks, such as machine translation or text classification?
5. How do the proposed approaches handle the case where the source language and target language have different writing systems or grammatical structures?
