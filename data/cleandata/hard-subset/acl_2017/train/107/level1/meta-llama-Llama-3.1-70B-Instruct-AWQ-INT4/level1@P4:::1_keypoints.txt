- Proposes two no-target-annotation cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection of embeddings.
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes that combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs.
- Combined system outperforms two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches on evaluated datasets.
- Main contribution: a heuristic data selection scheme that filters high-quality weakly labeled data for annotation projection.
- Main contribution: a representation projection method that maps target-language embeddings to the source language for direct model transfer without re-training.
- Main contribution: two co-decoding strategies that improve accuracy by intelligently merging the two projection outputs.
- Strength: heuristic data selection substantially boosts annotation projection performance when alignment is noisy.
- Strength: representation projection is competitive with annotation projection and complements it via co-decoding.
- Strength: representation projection offers flexibility and scalability for adding new languages without pairwise embeddings.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a small set of languages and datasets, raising questions about generalizability.
- Weakness: annotation projection remains dependent on the quality of alignment systems.
- Weakness: insufficient in-depth analysis of the co-decoding schemes and their behavior across scenarios.
- Question: How do the methods perform for truly low-resource languages or with low-quality comparable corpora?
- Question: Can the representation projection technique be extended to other tasks like POS tagging or dependency parsing?
- Question: How do the co-decoding schemes scale or perform when combining more than two projection-based approaches?
- Overall: clear and well-motivated work but needs broader evaluation and deeper analysis to demonstrate robustness.
