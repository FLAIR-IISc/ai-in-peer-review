**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents two approaches for weakly supervised cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) with no human annotation in the target language. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word embeddings are projected from a target language to a source language, allowing a single NER system to be applied to multiple languages without re-training. Two co-decoding schemes are also proposed to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches. The paper evaluates the performance of the proposed approaches on both in-house and open NER data sets for several target languages.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Effective annotation projection approach**: The paper develops a heuristic scheme to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora, which significantly improves the accuracy of the target-language NER system.
2. **Representation projection approach**: The paper proposes a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection, which allows a single NER system to be applied to multiple languages without re-training.
3. **Co-decoding schemes**: The paper designs two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches, which achieve higher accuracy than both individual systems.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved accuracy**: The proposed approaches achieve higher accuracy than state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches on several target languages.
2. **Flexibility**: The representation projection approach allows for easy addition of new languages without requiring separate embeddings for each language pair.
3. **Robustness**: The co-decoding schemes can handle noisy data and improve the overall performance of the system.
4. **Efficiency**: The annotation projection approach can reduce the need for human annotation in the target language, making it more efficient.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the performance of the proposed approaches on a limited number of target languages and data sets.
2. **Dependence on alignment accuracy**: The annotation projection approach relies on the accuracy of the alignment system, which can be a limitation.
3. **Limited analysis**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by the proposed approaches.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the training data used for the annotation projection approach and the representation projection approach?
2. How do you plan to extend the proposed approaches to other NLP tasks, such as relation extraction or entity linking?
3. Can you provide more insights on the errors made by the proposed approaches and how to improve their performance?
