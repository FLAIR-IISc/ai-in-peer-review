- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping, plus two co-decoding schemes to combine them.  
- Contribution: effective annotation projection that selects high-quality projected NER labels from comparable corpora, boosting target-language accuracy.  
- Contribution: representation projection enabling direct NER model transfer by projecting target embeddings to a source space, allowing one model for multiple languages.  
- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes that combine annotation- and representation-based outputs to outperform either approach alone.  
- Strength: achieves higher accuracy than prior cross-lingual NER methods on several target languages.  
- Strength: representation projection offers flexibility to add new languages without retraining separate models.  
- Strength: co-decoding schemes improve robustness to noisy data and overall performance.  
- Strength: annotation projection reduces reliance on human annotation, improving efficiency.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a small set of target languages and datasets.  
- Weakness: annotation projection depends on alignment quality, which may limit effectiveness.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis of the proposed methods.  
- Question: request for more details on the training data used for both projection approaches.  
- Question: how the approaches could be extended to other NLP tasks like relation extraction or entity linking.  
- Question: ask for deeper insights into the errors observed and ways to improve performance.
