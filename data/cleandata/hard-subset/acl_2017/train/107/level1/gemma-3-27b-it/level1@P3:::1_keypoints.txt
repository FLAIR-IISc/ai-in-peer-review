1. Proposes a heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that improves alignment quality and weak label accuracy, yielding large gains for low-resource languages (e.g., Japanese, Korean).  
2. Introduces co-decoding schemes (notably rank-based) to combine annotation and representation projection, leveraging annotation projection’s precision and representation projection’s recall.  
3. Provides comprehensive evaluation across in-house and CoNLL datasets and multiple target languages, outperforming prior state-of-the-art.  
4. Addresses a highly practical cross-lingual NER problem with a viable solution for languages without target annotations.  
5. Presents a clear, well-explained methodology with equations and diagrams that improve reproducibility and understanding.  
6. Shows strong empirical results with consistent improvements over baselines and notable language-specific gains tied to data selection.  
7. Demonstrates thoughtful system integration by considering the complementary characteristics of each projection method.  
8. Situates the work within related literature with a thorough review of annotation projection and model transfer approaches.  
9. Lacks detailed analysis of cross-lingual embedding mapping quality; metrics like correlation or nearest-neighbor retrieval are missing.  
10. Method remains sensitive to alignment quality and depends on comparable corpora with some alignment signal, which limits robustness.  
11. The choice of rank-based co-decoding is not fully justified; more analysis of why it outperforms confidence-based fusion is needed.  
12. Feature templates for CRFs/MEMMs are insufficiently specified, harming reproducibility and understanding of feature impact.  
13. Error analysis is limited; qualitative breakdowns of remaining error types are not provided.  
14. Requests quantitative metrics to evaluate embedding alignment (e.g., correlation, nearest-neighbor retrieval).  
15. Asks how sensitive annotation projection performance is to the quality of the English NER system used.  
16. Requests further rationale and analysis supporting the rank-based co-decoding choice, including examination of system confidence scores.  
17. Requests a brief description of feature templates used in CRFs and MEMMs.  
18. Suggests renaming “co-decoding” to a clearer term like “output combination” or “ensemble method.”  
19. Recommends briefly mentioning challenges for morphologically rich languages in the introduction.  
20. Suggests adding a discussion of the computational complexity of each proposed approach.
