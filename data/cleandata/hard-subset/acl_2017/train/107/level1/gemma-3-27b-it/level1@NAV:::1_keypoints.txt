- Core idea: Propose annotation projection with a frequency-based data selection and representation projection via cross-lingual embeddings, plus co-decoding to combine them.  
- Primary contribution: Novel data selection scheme for annotation projection that filters noisy alignments and improves performance across languages.  
- Secondary contribution: Empirical comparison and combination of annotation and representation projection using co-decoding strategies.  
- Strength: Addresses practical challenge of NER for low-resource languages with clear motivation.  
- Strength: Data selection scheme is practical and consistently improves projected training data quality.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on in-house and CoNLL datasets with comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: Builds on solid baselines (CRFs, neural models) for credible evaluation.  
- Strength: Experimental details (training, tuning, preprocessing) are sufficiently documented for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Core techniques (annotation and representation projection) are not novel; contribution is mainly application/refinement.  
- Weakness: Annotation projection still heavily depends on alignment and comparable corpora quality, with limited error analysis on this.  
- Weakness: Data selection performance depends on tuned thresholds ('q' and 'n'); parameter sensitivity analysis is insufficient.  
- Weakness: Lacks in-depth error analysis of each approach and the combined system.  
- Question: What is the computational cost of the data selection scheme on very large comparable corpora?  
- Question: How does combined-system performance degrade with lower-quality alignments, and will alignment error estimation be integrated into selection?  
- Question: Were character-level or other language-independent features tried to improve neural cross-lingual transfer?
