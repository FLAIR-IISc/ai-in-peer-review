- Paper proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with data selection and representation projection, plus two co-decoding schemes to combine them.  
- Main contribution: a frequency-based heuristic to select high-quality projected annotation data, improving noisy-label issues from alignment errors.  
- Secondary contribution: demonstration that word-embedding-based representation projection is a practical complementary cross-lingual transfer approach.  
- Co-decoding schemes provide a sensible, if simple, method to integrate outputs from both projection approaches.  

Strengths:
- Tackles a practical low-resource NER problem where labeled data is scarce.  
- Data selection heuristic is intuitive, well-motivated, and empirically effective (improves results, esp. with poor alignments).  
- Comprehensive experiments on in-house and CoNLL datasets across multiple target languages strengthen the findings.  
- Comparisons to prior state-of-the-art (Täckström 2012, Tsai 2016) show clear improvements.  
- Models (CRFs, MEMMs, NNs) are clearly described, aiding reproducibility.  

Weaknesses:
- Individual components (annotation projection, embedding projection) lack fundamental novelty; novelty mainly in their combination and the heuristic.  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning for data selection (q, n) and neural network architectures/hyperparameters.  
- Missing in-depth error analysis to show where systems fail by entity type or language.  
- Co-decoding methods are relatively simple; more sophisticated fusion (e.g., learned weighting) not explored.  
- Experimental result presentation is somewhat repetitive and could be consolidated for readability.  

Questions for authors:
- How were parameters q and n tuned per language—development set and optimization metrics?  
- What error patterns occur for the representation projection approach (specific entity types or languages)?  
- Did you try more complex co-decoding/fusion methods, and what were the outcomes?  
- Please provide detailed NN architecture and training hyperparameters (layer sizes, activations, learning rate, optimizer).  

Additional comments:
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Add discussion of limitations for typologically different languages (word order, morphology).  
- Discuss computational cost, particularly of embedding projection.
