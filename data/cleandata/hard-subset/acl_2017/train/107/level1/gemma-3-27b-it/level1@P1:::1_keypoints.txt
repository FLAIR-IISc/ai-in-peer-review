- Heuristic data selection filters noisy projection-labeled data based on entity label frequency, improving annotation projection especially in low-resource/poor-alignment settings.
- Representation projection uses cross-lingual word embeddings to transfer NER knowledge across languages.
- Two co-decoding schemes (including a rank-based method that prioritizes annotation projection) combine annotation and representation projections and yield consistent gains.
- Extensive multilingual evaluation (in-house and CoNLL) shows improvements over existing cross-lingual NER methods and establishes a new baseline.
- Clear problem statement and strong motivation for weakly supervised cross-lingual NER in low-resource languages.
- The data selection heuristic is simple, practical, and shown via ablation to effectively reduce noisy projections.
- Comprehensive experiments compare approaches and co-decoding schemes with established baselines.
- The paper is well-structured, readable, and includes helpful result tables.
- The work is relevant to the community and offers a practical solution for cross-lingual NER.
- Limited error analysis — lacks deeper breakdown of error types and per-entity-type failures.
- Reliance on English as the pivot may introduce bias; impact of other or multi-pivot languages is not explored.
- Use of MEMM with manual features feels dated; rationale for not using neural approaches for annotation projection is insufficient.
- Hyperparameter tuning procedures (e.g., method, validation setup) are under-specified, harming reproducibility.
- Sensitivity to dictionary size for representation projection is noted but the process to determine the ~5–6K optimal size is not detailed.
- Request for more detailed error analysis focusing on frequently misclassified entity types.
- Question about whether different pivot languages or multi-pivot strategies were explored and their results.
- Ask for justification of feature choices and the MEMM architecture versus neural models for annotation projection.
- Request for elaboration on hyperparameter tuning, especially for data selection heuristic parameters q and n.
- Request for experimental details regarding dictionary size selection for representation projection.
- Minor presentation issues: the phrase "smart way" is imprecise and should be revised.
- Figure 1 is referenced but missing; citation style is inconsistent and needs cleanup.
