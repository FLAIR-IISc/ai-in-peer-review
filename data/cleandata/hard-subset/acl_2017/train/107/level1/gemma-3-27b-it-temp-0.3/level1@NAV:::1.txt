## Review of "Weakly Supervised Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition via Effective Annotation and Representation Projection"

This paper presents two approaches for weakly supervised cross-lingual NER: annotation projection with a novel data selection scheme, and representation projection using word embeddings. The authors further propose two co-decoding schemes to combine the outputs of these approaches. The work is evaluated on both in-house and publicly available datasets, demonstrating improvements over existing cross-lingual NER methods.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the *combination of annotation projection with a targeted data selection heuristic*. While annotation projection is not novel, the authors effectively address the issue of noisy projections by focusing on data quality based on entity frequency, leading to significant performance gains, particularly in low-resource scenarios. The second notable contribution is the *demonstration of a practical co-decoding scheme* that leverages the complementary strengths of the annotation and representation projection methods. The empirical results clearly show the benefit of combining these approaches.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents comprehensive experimental results on multiple languages and datasets. The consistent improvements over baseline methods, particularly on the in-house datasets, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed techniques.
2. **Practical Data Selection:** The heuristic for selecting high-quality projection data is a valuable contribution. It addresses a key challenge in weakly supervised learning – dealing with noisy labels – in a simple yet effective manner.
3. **Effective Combination of Approaches:** The co-decoding schemes successfully integrate the strengths of both annotation and representation projection, leading to a more robust and accurate system. The rank-based co-decoding scheme consistently outperforms the confidence-based approach, suggesting a thoughtful design.
4. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the proposed methods and experimental setup. The inclusion of ablation studies (comparing NN1 and NN2) is helpful for understanding the impact of different design choices.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Techniques:** Both annotation projection and representation projection are established techniques. The novelty primarily resides in the specific implementation details and the combination of these techniques, rather than fundamentally new algorithms.
2. **Dependency on Alignment Quality:** The annotation projection approach remains heavily reliant on the quality of the comparable corpora and alignment information. While the data selection heuristic mitigates this, the performance is still limited by alignment errors. This limitation isn’t thoroughly discussed.
3. **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions tuning parameters for the data selection scheme, but lacks details on the specific tuning process and the sensitivity of the results to these parameters. 



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details on the parameter tuning process for the data selection scheme (q and n)? How sensitive are the results to these parameters?
2. How does the performance of the representation projection approach vary with the size of the training dictionary used for learning the cross-lingual embedding mappings?
3.  The paper mentions the NER systems are trained with different amounts of data. How does the amount of data used for training affect the performance of each approach?
