1. Heuristic data-selection scheme for annotation projection from comparable (non-parallel) corpora.  
2. Representation projection mapping target-language embeddings into source space for direct NER model transfer.  
3. Two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-based outputs.  
4. Novel data-selection yields large F1 improvements (up to +20) for languages with poor alignment (e.g., Japanese, Korean).  
5. Thorough evaluation across multiple languages and datasets; combined weakly supervised system approaches supervised performance.  
6. Rank-based co-decoding effectively leverages complementary strengths, improving F1 by 2.9–6.2 points.  
7. No evidence provided that the "comparable corpora" are actually comparable in topical content.  
8. Representation projection depends on a 5K–6K bilingual dictionary, limiting applicability to very low-resource languages.  
9. Lacks comparison to more recent cross-lingual NER methods (post-2015) to validate state-of-the-art claims.  
10. Question: How were comparable corpora selected and what metrics/evidence show they cover similar topics?  
11. Question: What alternatives exist when a 5K–6K bilingual lexicon is unavailable for representation projection?  
12. Question: Can the authors provide comparisons to more recent (post-2015) approaches to better contextualize their contribution?
