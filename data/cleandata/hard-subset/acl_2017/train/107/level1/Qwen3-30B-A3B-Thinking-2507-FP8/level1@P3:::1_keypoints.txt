- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: heuristic annotation-projection data selection and representation-projection via cross-lingual embedding mapping, plus two co-decoding schemes.

- Contribution: frequency-based data selection significantly improves quality of weak labels from noisy comparable corpora.

- Contribution: representation projection enables direct NER model transfer using cross-lingual word embedding mapping with English as a pivot.

- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes combine outputs from annotation and representation projections.

- Strength: novel and effective data selection scheme (frequency-based metric and selection) yields large F1 gains, especially for low-alignment languages.

- Strength: thorough experimental validation across multiple languages and datasets, including comparisons to supervised baselines.

- Strength: clear, practical implementation of cross-lingual embedding mapping and dictionary construction.

- Weakness: lacks discussion of scalability and resource requirements for truly low-resource languages (large comparable corpora and word-pair dictionaries needed).

- Weakness: insufficient comparison to more recent cross-lingual NER methods predating ACL 2017.

- Weakness: modest reported performance gains (1–3 F1) with no statistical significance testing.

- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases and factors causing gaps versus supervised learning.

- Question: clarification on computational cost and practicality of training a cross-lingual mapping per new language despite claim of "no re-training."

- Question: how were data-selection thresholds (q and n) chosen per language, and can they be set automatically without labeled data?

- Question: why does the combined system still lag supervised baselines (e.g., Portuguese) and what error types remain uncorrected?
