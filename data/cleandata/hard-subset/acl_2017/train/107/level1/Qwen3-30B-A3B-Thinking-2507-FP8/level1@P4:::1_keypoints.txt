- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods with no target annotations: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via cross-lingual embeddings, plus two co-decoding schemes; evaluated on in-house and CoNLL datasets.

- Heuristic data selection for annotation projection that substantially improves performance when alignment accuracy is low (e.g., +13.9 F1 Japanese, +20.0 F1 Korean).

- Representation projection: direct model transfer by mapping target word embeddings to source space using a linear weighted least squares mapping.

- Two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs, with a rank-based scheme prioritizing annotation projection.

- Effective data selection addresses alignment-quality issues and yields large F1 gains for poorly aligned languages.

- Well-executed application of cross-lingual embeddings to NER with a solid theoretical foundation and reasonable mapping approach.

- Co-decoding schemes leverage complementary strengths (annotation projection: high precision; representation projection: balanced precision/recall), with rank-based combining performing best.

- Thorough experimental evaluation across six languages (Japanese, Korean, German, Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch) on both in-house and CoNLL datasets, including supervised baselines.

- Clear, well-structured technical presentation with sufficient detail for reproducibility.

- Limited comparisons to more recent cross-lingual NER methods published after 2016 (e.g., multilingual BERT-based approaches).

- No analysis of how the data selection scheme performs when alignment accuracy is already high (limits understanding of applicability).

- Inconsistent neural architecture results (NN2 > NN1 on in-house but NN1 > NN2 on CoNLL) without adequate explanation.

- Absolute performance still lags supervised learning by a notable margin (e.g., 12–14 F1 for German) and improvements over prior work are modest; no statistical significance analysis provided.

- Missing discussion of computational complexity (time/memory) of the data selection scheme versus using all projection-labeled data on large corpora.

- Question: How do your results compare with more recent cross-lingual NER approaches (e.g., multilingual BERT or other transfer-learning methods)?

- Question: Can you explain why NN2 outperforms NN1 on in-house data but underperforms on CoNLL data?

- Question: What are the time and memory costs of the data selection scheme compared to using all projection-labeled data?
