- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches and a heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection, improving accuracy by filtering noisy projection-labeled data (e.g., +20 F1 for Korean with low alignment accuracy).  
- Introduces representation projection that transfers NER models by mapping target word embeddings into the source language vector space.  
- Presents co-decoding schemes to combine outputs of both approaches, with a rank-based scheme giving consistent improvements.  
- Data selection scheme is well-motivated and empirically validated, addressing noisy annotation projection.  
- Representation projection is a simple, effective direct model-transfer method that avoids language-specific features.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across multiple languages/datasets and shows combined systems outperform two prior state-of-the-art methods on CoNLL.  
- Rank-based co-decoding leverages complementary strengths (high precision/low recall vs. balanced precision/recall) effectively.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with recent state-of-the-art methods beyond 2012–2016, weakening novelty claims.  
- Reliance on comparable corpora (not parallel data) limits applicability and alignment accuracy issues are not fully addressed.  
- Representation projection’s dependence on embeddings may miss cross-lingual semantic nuances, especially for complex entity mappings.  
- Computational cost of the data selection scheme is not discussed and could be prohibitive for large-scale use.  
- Absolute performance remains well below supervised baselines, raising practical utility concerns.  
- Authors should clarify comparisons to recent cross-lingual NER work (e.g., multilingual BERT) and detail limitations of using comparable corpora.
