- Paper proposes annotation projection with sentence selection, representation projection via linear embedding mapping, and two co-decoding schemes to combine them.
- Annotation projection uses entity-frequency reliability and entity count (q, n) to select sentences from comparable corpora.
- Representation projection maps target embeddings to English space using a weighted bilingual dictionary from a phrase table and applies an English NER model directly.
- Co-decoding includes an exclude-O confidence combiner and a rank-based combiner that prioritizes annotation projection and augments with non-conflicting representation-projection entities.
- Experiments on in-house (50+ types) and CoNLL (4 types) across multiple languages show data selection greatly helps under noisy alignments.
- Representation projection is competitive with annotation projection and simpler to extend to new languages without retraining the NER model.
- Co-decoding consistently improves over individual systems and prior baselines, often by +2–6 F1.
- Data selection yields large F1 gains on Japanese/Korean (+13.9, +20.0) when alignments are noisy, supporting the selection heuristic.
- Annotation projection tends to high precision/low recall, while representation projection yields more balanced recall.
- Results are reported on diverse settings and languages (Japanese, Korean, German, Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch) and on both fine-grained and standard benchmarks.
- The pipeline is resource-light and modular, relying on monolingual embeddings and a small bilingual dictionary.
- Modeling choices (MEMMs for large label sets, two NN variants) are reasonable and accompanied by precision/recall analysis.
- Reproducibility is limited: in-house corpora, alignment models, sizes, and resources are not sufficiently specified or released.
- Potential test-set tuning: thresholds (q, n) and co-decoding choices for in-house data appear selected without clear development splits.
- Core methods show limited novelty: linear mapping and annotation projection are straightforward applications, with novelty mainly in the selection heuristic and empirical combination.
- Missing comparisons/ablations: no orthogonal/Procrustes mapping comparison, no ablation of mapping weights, dictionary size, embedding dimensionality, or confidence calibration.
- Supervision assumptions are understated: methods require comparable/aligned corpora and phrase-table-derived dictionaries, which may not exist for low-resource languages.
- Analysis gaps: possible bias toward frequent entities from sentence selection, no per-type breakdown, and no statistical significance testing.
- Unclear BIO handling in co-decoding and lack of details on conflict resolution and post-processing.
- Questions for authors include how (q, n) were selected, sources/sizes/nature of corpora and alignments, how phrase tables/dictionaries were obtained, and whether the mapping is constrained (e.g., orthogonal).
- Additional requested clarifications: BIO constraint enforcement during co-decoding, ablations on dictionary size/weights/embedding dims, per-entity-type performance, error analysis on rare vs. frequent entities, and plans to release code/models/resources.
