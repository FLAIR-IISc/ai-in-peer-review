- Annotation projection: heuristic data-selection for picking high-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora, improving target-language NER when alignments are poor.
- Representation projection: project target-language word embeddings into source-language space to apply a single source-trained NER model to target languages without re-training.
- Co-decoding schemes: two methods to combine outputs of annotation- and representation-projection systems, yielding higher accuracy than either alone.
- Strength — effective weak supervision: demonstrates weakly supervised cross-lingual NER works without target-language annotations.
- Strength — improved accuracy: outperforms state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER, especially on CoNLL test sets.
- Strength — flexibility: representation projection simplifies adding new languages without per-pair embeddings.
- Weakness — limited evaluation: experiments cover only a few languages (JP, KR, DE, PT, ES, NL) and datasets (in-house, CoNLL).
- Weakness — alignment dependence: annotation projection relies on accurate source–target alignments, which may be unavailable or noisy.
- Weakness — added complexity: co-decoding schemes increase system complexity, potentially affecting scalability and efficiency.
- Question — low-resource extension: how to apply approaches to languages with limited or no parallel data?
- Question — method generality: request more insights on the heuristic selection and representation projection across diverse language pairs and datasets.
- Question — scalability/efficiency: how will the authors address potential scalability and efficiency issues from co-decoding?
