- Proposes two cross-lingual NER approaches: Annotation Projection (AP) using comparable corpora and Representation Projection (RP) mapping target embeddings to source space, plus two co-decoding schemes.  
- Introduces a simple, effective heuristic data selection scheme for AP that filters noisy projected examples using entity-tag frequency statistics.  
- Demonstrates that combining AP and RP via co-decoding leverages their complementary strengths and yields state-of-the-art results on CoNLL benchmarks.  
- AP data selection yields large F1 gains for low-alignment languages (e.g., +13.9 for Japanese, +20.0 for Korean), showing practical impact.  
- Provides strong, comprehensive experiments across seven languages and two datasets (in-house fine-grained and CoNLL coarse-grained), outperforming recent baselines.  
- Analyzes AP as high-precision and RP as more balanced, motivating simple co-decoding (rank-based and confidence-based) that consistently improves performance.  
- RP method offers limited methodological novelty, as linear maps between monolingual embeddings follow prior work (e.g., Mikolov et al.); its contribution is mainly empirical.  
- Data selection depends on hyperparameters (q, n) but the paper lacks analysis of tuning or sensitivity to these settings.  
- Missing details for CoNLL AP experiments: comparable corpora and alignment systems are not specified, hindering reproducibility and interpretation.  
- Requests clarification on how data selection hyperparameters (q, n) were tuned and their effect on the trade-off between data quality and quantity.  
- Asks for a hypothesis explaining why rank-based co-decoding worked best on in-house data while confidence-based worked better on CoNLL data.  
- Requests specification of the comparable corpora and alignment models used for Spanish and Dutch annotation projection to improve reproducibility.
