- Tackles weakly supervised cross-lingual NER by combining Annotation Projection (AP) and Representation Projection (RP) with co-decoding, achieving state-of-the-art on CoNLL datasets.  
- Proposes a heuristic data-selection filter for AP that keeps consistently projected entity-label pairs, markedly reducing noise.  
- Uses a linear mapping of target word embeddings to the source space to directly apply a source-language neural NER model (RP).  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes (confidence-based and rank-based) to combine AP and RP outputs, exploiting their complementary strengths.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains and robustness across seven languages, providing a valuable baseline for future work.  
- Produces state-of-the-art results versus strong baselines (Täckström et al., 2012; Tsai et al., 2016) on CoNLL Spanish, Dutch, and German.  
- The AP data-selection method yields large F1 improvements for noisy-alignment languages (e.g., +13.9 Japanese, +20.0 Korean).  
- Thoughtful combination: AP tends to be high-precision/low-recall while RP is more balanced, motivating the rank-based co-decoding.  
- Clear exposition and sound experimental setup, evaluated on both public CoNLL and a private fine-grained dataset.  
- RP component largely applies known cross-lingual embedding mapping techniques and lacks comparison to more advanced embedding methods.  
- Method tuning relies on target-language dev sets (e.g., AP thresholds q, n and co-decoding choice), weakening the pure zero-shot claim.  
- Missing deeper error analysis (differences by entity type/boundary errors) and limited explanation for the NN2 smoothing layer’s mixed effects.  
- Baseline comparisons are incomplete: strong baselines aren’t reported on the in-house fine-grained dataset.  
- Questions for authors: how to set AP thresholds without target dev data; why NN2’s smoothing helps in-house but hurts CoNLL (relation to entity type count/sparsity); and whether softer combination methods (confidence calibration or learned gating) were explored.
