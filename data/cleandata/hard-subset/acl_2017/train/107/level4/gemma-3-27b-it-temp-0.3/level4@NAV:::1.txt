This paper explores weakly supervised methods for Named Entity Recognition (NER), leveraging cross-lingual projection from English. While the general approach of projection-based NER is established, the work presents incremental improvements to existing techniques. Given the standards for papers presented at ACL, I anticipated a more substantially novel contribution.

A key aspect of this work is the proposed data selection scheme. The quality score formula is appropriately simple and direct. However, the methodology for determining the thresholds used in Table 2 lacks detail. The paper states that thresholds were varied, but it’s unclear whether this optimization was performed using a development set – a detail not currently included in the manuscript. The results convincingly demonstrate the importance of data selection, but the paper doesn’t offer guidance on parameter tuning for novel datasets or language pairs.

The combination of the two developed systems represents another contribution. Despite careful review, the explanation of this combination process remains unclear. 

The paper investigates several variations for each proposed method. It would be valuable to understand if extending the combination beyond two weakly supervised systems was considered. Exploring this possibility could offer further insights.

Finally, providing additional information regarding the composition of the “in-house” dataset would be beneficial for understanding the scope and characteristics of the evaluation.
