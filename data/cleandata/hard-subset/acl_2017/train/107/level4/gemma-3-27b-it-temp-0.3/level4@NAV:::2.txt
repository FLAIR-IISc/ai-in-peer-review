This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER), leveraging conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural network-based NER techniques. The authors further introduce two methods for combining the outputs of these systems – a probability-based approach and a ranking-based approach – alongside a method for selecting optimal training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is achieved using an adaptation of Mikolov’s method. Overall, the paper is clearly written, well-organized, and demonstrates good English proficiency. The results obtained through combined annotations are promising.

However, I have several detailed comments. The motivation behind the proposed Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model variation is unclear. The paper lacks detail regarding this modification (and the parameters used). It would be beneficial to understand if the original model, or the Continuous Skip-gram model, yielded unsatisfactory results. Including the results obtained with the CBOW model would allow readers to assess the improvements offered by the proposed approach. Given the use of a decay factor for surrounding embeddings, I recommend considering the exponential decay method presented in [1]. 

Similarly, a more direct comparison between the original Mikolov’s cross-lingual projections and the authors’ frequency-weighted projections would strengthen the contribution. Demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method with empirical evidence would be valuable.

The statement that “the proposed data selection scheme is very effective…and the improvement is significant” requires substantiation. I recommend including a statistical significance test to confirm the significance of the observed differences.

Regarding the paper’s structure, integrating the content of Section 4.4 into the beginning of Section 4.2 would improve readability. Furthermore, moving the evaluation of Table 2 to the dedicated evaluation section would be a logical improvement. 

The paper currently lacks a dedicated related work section, despite containing relevant information within the introduction. Dividing the introduction into two sections – one for background and motivation, and another for related work – would be beneficial.

Finally, the evaluation section is relatively brief (1.5 pages including the conclusion). Given the state-of-the-art results achieved, a more in-depth discussion and analysis of these results would be appreciated.

Suggested reference:
[1] Iacobacci, I., Pilehvar, M. T., & Navigli, R. (2016). Embeddings for word sense disambiguation: An evaluation study. In Proceedings of the 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (Vol. 1, pp. 897-907).
