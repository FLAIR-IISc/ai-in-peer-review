**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) based on effective annotation and representation projection. The authors employ conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov, and neural network-based NER methods. They also propose two methods to combine the output of those methods (probability-based and ranking-based), and a method to select the best training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. The cross-lingual projection is done using a variant of Mikolov's proposal.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Effective Annotation Projection**: The authors develop a heuristic data selection scheme that can effectively select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.
2. **Representation Projection Approach**: The authors propose a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection, which projects word representations in vector space from a target language to a source language.
3. **Co-Decoding Schemes**: The authors design two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches in a smart way.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Accuracy**: The combined system achieves higher accuracy than both individual systems and outperforms two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.
2. **Well-Structured Paper**: The paper is easy to follow, well-structured, and the English quality is correct.
3. **Interesting Results**: The results of the combined annotations are interesting and provide a strong baseline for building cross-lingual NER systems with no human annotation in the target languages.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Details**: The paper lacks details about the variant of Mikolov's proposal and the parameters employed.
2. **Comparison with Original Models**: The paper does not provide a comparison with the original CBOW model or Mikolov's cross-lingual projections.
3. **Statistical Significance**: The paper does not provide a test of statistical significance to determine if the differences between the results are significant.
4. **Short Evaluation Section**: The evaluation section is quite short and lacks more discussion and analysis of the results.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details about the variant of Mikolov's proposal and the parameters employed?
2. Can you provide a comparison with the original CBOW model or Mikolov's cross-lingual projections?
3. Have you conducted a test of statistical significance to determine if the differences between the results are significant?
4. Can you provide more discussion and analysis of the results in the evaluation section?
