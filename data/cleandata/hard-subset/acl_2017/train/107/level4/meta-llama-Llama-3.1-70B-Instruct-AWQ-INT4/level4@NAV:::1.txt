This paper explores multiple weakly supervised approaches to developing Named Entity Recognizers (NERs), primarily leveraging projection techniques from English to other languages. While the overall strategy is not novel, the proposed methods build upon and refine existing approaches. However, considering the standards of an ACL paper, more innovative solutions would have been expected.

One of the paper's key contributions lies in its data selection scheme, which utilizes a straightforward quality score formula. Although the simplicity of this formula is not inherently problematic, the methodology behind determining the thresholds presented in Table 2 is unclear. The text mentions experimenting with different thresholds but does not specify whether this process involved a development set, leaving a gap in the explanation.

The evaluation results underscore the significance of data selection, but the lack of guidance on parameter tuning for new datasets or language pairs is a notable limitation. Furthermore, the paper's description of combining the outputs of the two developed systems is unclear, necessitating further clarification.

The authors present several variants of each proposed method, raising the question of whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would be beneficial. It would be valuable to know if the authors explored this possibility.

Additionally, providing more information about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset would be beneficial for a more comprehensive understanding of the research.
