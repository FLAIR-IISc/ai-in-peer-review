- Overall assessment: Presents weakly supervised cross-lingual NER via projection from English, but lacks strong novelty beyond improvements to existing methods.

- Summary — annotation projection: Uses heuristic data selection to pick high-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora.

- Summary — representation projection: Projects target-language word embeddings into source-language space for direct NER model transfer.

- Summary — co-decoding: Proposes two schemes to combine outputs of annotation- and representation-projection systems.

- Contribution 1: A data selection scheme that effectively extracts better-quality projection-labeled data from noisy comparable corpora.

- Contribution 2: A representation-projection approach that maps word embeddings to enable direct cross-lingual NER transfer.

- Contribution 3: Co-decoding schemes that fuse the two projection-based systems to improve NER accuracy.

- Strength 1: Thorough evaluation on multiple target languages using both in-house and public NER datasets.

- Strength 2: Combined systems outperform two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods on CoNLL test data.

- Strength 3: Detailed method and experimental setup that facilitate replication.

- Weakness 1: Overall approach is incremental and not sufficiently novel for an ACL paper.

- Weakness 2: Unclear how thresholds for the data selection scheme were computed, hindering reproducibility and tuning.

- Weakness 3: Description of the co-decoding schemes is insufficiently clear.

- Question 1: How were the thresholds for the data selection scheme calculated—on a development set or otherwise?

- Question 2: Can you provide a more detailed, example-driven explanation of the co-decoding schemes?

- Question 3: Did you try combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and what were the results?

- Question 4: What types of texts are included in the "in-house" dataset?
