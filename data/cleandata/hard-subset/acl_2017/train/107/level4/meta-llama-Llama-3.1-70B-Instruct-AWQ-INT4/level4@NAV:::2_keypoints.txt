- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model combining CRF, MEMM, and neural methods with two combination strategies (probability- and ranking-based) and a training-instance selection method from comparable corpora.  
- Cross-lingual projection is done via a variant of Mikolov’s embedding alignment; combined annotation results are particularly noteworthy.  
- Paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and written in good English.  
- Motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variation are unclear; authors should provide parameters and comparisons to original CBOW/Skip-gram.  
- The use of a decay factor for surrounding embeddings needs further investigation, potentially using exponential decay as in Iacobacci et al. (2016).  
- A direct comparison between original Mikolov cross-lingual projections and the proposed frequency-weighted projections is needed.  
- The claim that the data selection scheme significantly improves projection-labeled data should be supported by statistical significance testing.  
- Reorganize sections: integrate Section 4.4 into 4.2 and move evaluation of Table 2 into the evaluation section for clarity.  
- Separate related work from the introduction into its own section, as related work is currently lacking.  
- The evaluation section is brief; given state-of-the-art results, a more in-depth discussion and analysis of results is required.  
- Suggested reference: Iacobacci et al. (2016) for embedding decay/exponential decay considerations.
