This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for developing named entity recognition (NER) systems. The methods rely on some form of projection from English into another language. While the overall approach is not new, the individual methods proposed are improvements of existing methods.

One of the contributions of the paper is the data selection scheme, which is a crucial component in improving the accuracy of the annotation projection approach for cross-lingual NER. The formula used to calculate the quality score is straightforward and easy to implement. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of how the thresholds were calculated for Table 2. Specifically, it would be helpful to know if the thresholds were tuned on a development set or if a different method was used. The evaluation results clearly demonstrate the importance of data selection, but without further guidance, it may be challenging to determine how to tune the parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another significant contribution of the paper is the combination of the outputs of the two systems developed in the paper. However, the description provided could be clarified, particularly for readers who are not familiar with the specific co-decoding schemes used. For instance, the paper could provide more details on how the confidence scores are calculated and how the rank-based co-decoding scheme prioritizes the annotation projection system.

The paper presents several variants for each of the methods proposed, which raises the question of whether it makes sense to combine more than two weakly supervised systems. It would be interesting to know if the authors explored this direction and if so, what the results were.

Finally, it would be helpful to have more information about the types of texts that are included in the "in-house" dataset. This could provide valuable context for understanding the results and generalizing the findings to other datasets.

Strengths:
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods on multiple datasets, including in-house and CoNLL data.
- The data selection scheme is a valuable contribution to the field, and the results demonstrate its effectiveness in improving the accuracy of the annotation projection approach.
- The combination of the outputs of the two systems is a novel approach that shows promise in improving the overall performance of the NER system.

Weaknesses:
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the thresholds used in the data selection scheme and how they were calculated.
- The description of the co-decoding schemes could be clarified, particularly for readers who are not familiar with the specific methods used.
- The paper could explore the possibility of combining more than two weakly supervised systems and report the results.
- More information about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset would be helpful in understanding the results and generalizing the findings.

Contributions:
- The data selection scheme is a significant contribution to the field, as it provides a straightforward and effective way to improve the accuracy of the annotation projection approach.
- The combination of the outputs of the two systems is a novel approach that shows promise in improving the overall performance of the NER system.
- The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed methods on multiple datasets, including in-house and CoNLL data.

Questions to Authors:
- Can you provide more details on how the thresholds were calculated for the data selection scheme?
- Can you clarify the description of the co-decoding schemes, particularly for readers who are not familiar with the specific methods used?
- Did you explore the possibility of combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and if so, what were the results?
- Can you provide more information about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset?
