This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for developing Named Entity Recognizers (NERs) that rely on projecting information from English into another language. While the overall approach is not new, the individual methods proposed are improvements upon existing techniques. However, for an ACL paper, I would have expected more novel approaches to be presented.

One of the paper's contributions is its data selection scheme, which utilizes a straightforward formula to calculate a quality score. Although the simplicity of this formula is not inherently problematic, the method used to determine the thresholds presented in Table 2 is unclear. The paper mentions that different thresholds were tried, but it does not specify whether this process was performed on a development set. As a result, the reader is left without clear guidance on how to tune the parameters for a new dataset or language pair, despite the evaluation results demonstrating the importance of data selection.

Another contribution of the paper is the combination of the outputs of the two systems developed. Unfortunately, the description provided is not clear, and I found it difficult to fully understand the process. Further clarification on this aspect would be beneficial.

The paper presents multiple variants for each of the proposed methods, which raises the question of whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would be effective. It would be interesting to know if the authors explored this possibility and, if so, what the results were.

Finally, providing more information about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset would be helpful in understanding the context and potential limitations of the methods presented.
