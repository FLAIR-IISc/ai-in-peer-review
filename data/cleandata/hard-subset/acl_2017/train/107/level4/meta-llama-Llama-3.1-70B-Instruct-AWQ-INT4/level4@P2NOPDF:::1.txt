This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for developing Named Entity Recognizers (NERs) that rely on projecting information from English to another language. While the overall approach is not novel, the individual methods proposed are improvements upon existing techniques. However, for an ACL paper, I would have expected more innovative approaches.

One of the paper's contributions is its data selection scheme, which utilizes a straightforward formula to calculate a quality score. Although the simplicity of the formula is not inherently problematic, it is unclear how the thresholds presented in Table 2 were determined. The paper mentions that different thresholds were tried, but it does not specify whether this was done using a development set. This lack of clarity makes it difficult to understand how to tune the parameters for a new dataset or language pair, despite the evaluation results highlighting the importance of data selection.

Another contribution of the paper is the combination of the outputs of the two systems developed in the paper. Unfortunately, the description provided is not clear, and I found it challenging to understand how this combination works.

The paper presents multiple variants of each proposed method. It would be interesting to know whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would be beneficial. Did the authors explore this possibility, and if so, what were the results?

Additionally, it would be helpful to have more information about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset used in the study. Providing more context about the dataset could enhance the paper's clarity and usefulness.
