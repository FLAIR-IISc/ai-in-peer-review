- Paper presents weakly supervised NER methods using English-to-target projection; approaches are improvements rather than wholly novel.
- Main contributions are a data selection scheme and a method for combining outputs of two systems.
- Data selection scheme effectively filters good-quality projection-labeled data from noisy comparable corpora.
- The quality-score formula is simple and positive, but threshold determination (Table 2) is unclear.
- Tuning data-selection parameters may be challenging for new datasets or language pairs.
- Combination of two systems improves performance, but the description of the combination method lacks clarity.
- Exploring combination of more than two weakly supervised systems could be beneficial.
- Multiple method variants are evaluated on in-house and open NER datasets across languages.
- Combined systems outperform two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches on CoNLL test data.
- The "in-house" dataset is poorly described; genres, styles, and sources should be provided for generalization.
- Strengths: thorough evaluation, SOTA comparison, and detailed method descriptions (data selection and combination).
- Weaknesses: limited novelty (building on existing methods) and insufficient detail on parameter tuning, especially for data selection.
- Questions to authors: how thresholds in Table 2 were calculated and whether a dev set was used; details on in-house texts; whether combining >2 systems was tried and results; clarification on confidence-based and rank-based co-decoding.
