- Proposes a cross-lingual NER approach using weak supervision with annotation and representation projection.  
- Employs multiple NER models: CRF, MEMM, and neural networks.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine model outputs: probability-based and ranking-based.  
- Presents a heuristic data selection scheme to pick high-quality projection-labeled instances from noisy comparable corpora.  
- Uses representation projection via cross-lingual word-embedding mapping (a variant of Mikolov’s method).  
- Evaluates methods on in-house and public NER datasets and reports improved cross-lingual accuracy.  
- Methods are described in detail, facilitating replication.  
- Approach reduces need for target-language human annotation through weak supervision.  
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of results and per-method strengths/limitations.  
- Evaluation section is relatively brief and could be expanded for more insight.  
- No dedicated related work section to contextualize contributions.  
- Asks authors to detail the Mikolov-variant parameters and motivations.  
- Requests comparison to other cross-lingual NER approaches (direct transfer, annotation projection).  
- Queries whether statistical significance tests were performed for the reported improvements.
