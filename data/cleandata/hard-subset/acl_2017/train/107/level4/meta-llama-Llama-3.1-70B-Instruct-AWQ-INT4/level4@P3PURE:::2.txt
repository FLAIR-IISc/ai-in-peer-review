This paper proposes a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) by leveraging conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural network-based NER methods. The authors also introduce two methods for combining the output of these approaches (probability-based and ranking-based) and a method for selecting the best training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. The cross-lingual projection is achieved using a variant of Mikolov's proposal. Overall, the paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with good English quality. The results of the combined annotations are also noteworthy.

Detailed comments:

I appreciate the authors' efforts in proposing a variant of the Continuous Bag-of-word (CBOW) model. However, I would like to see a clearer motivation behind this proposal. The paper lacks details about the parameters employed in this variation. It would be beneficial to include the results obtained using the original CBOW model, allowing readers to analyze the improvements achieved by the proposed approach. Was the original model (or the Continuous Skip-gram model) yielding suboptimal results? Including a comparison would strengthen the paper.

The use of a decay factor for surrounding embeddings is an interesting aspect of the proposed method. I suggest exploring the exponential decay approach presented in [1] for potential improvements.

Similarly, I would like to see a more detailed comparison between the original Mikolov's cross-lingual projections and the proposed frequency-weighted projections. This would help demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method. Providing a clear analysis of the differences between these approaches would enhance the value of this contribution.

Regarding the proposed data selection scheme, the paper claims that it is "very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data" and that the improvement is "significant." However, I would like to know whether a test of statistical significance was conducted to confirm this claim.

From a structural perspective, I suggest integrating the text of Section 4.4 into the beginning of Section 4.2, as it would improve the flow of the paper. Additionally, I recommend moving the evaluation of Table 2 to the evaluation section, where it would be more relevant.

The paper lacks a dedicated related work section. While the introduction provides some background information, I suggest dividing the introduction into two sections to separate the motivation and related work.

The evaluation section is relatively short (1.5 pages, including the conclusion). Given that the paper achieves state-of-the-art results, I would appreciate a more in-depth discussion and analysis of the results.

Suggested references:
