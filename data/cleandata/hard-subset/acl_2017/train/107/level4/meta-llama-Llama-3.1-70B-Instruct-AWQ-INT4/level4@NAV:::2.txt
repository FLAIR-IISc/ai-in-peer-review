This paper presents a cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) model that leverages conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural network-based NER methods. The authors propose two combination methods (probability-based and ranking-based) for the output of these methods, as well as a method for selecting optimal training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is achieved using a variant of Mikolov's proposal. Overall, the paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and features good English quality. The combined annotation results are particularly noteworthy.

Detailed comments:

The motivation behind proposing a Continuous Bag-of-word (CBOW) model variation is unclear. The authors could provide more details about this variation (including parameters employed) and compare its performance to the original model or the Continuous Skip-gram model. Including the results of the CBOW model would allow readers to assess the improvements of the proposed approach.

The use of a decay factor for surrounding embeddings warrants further investigation, potentially incorporating the exponential decay method described in [1].

A comparison between the original Mikolov's cross-lingual projections and the proposed frequency-weighted projections would be valuable, allowing readers to evaluate the superiority of the proposed method.

The claim that "the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant" would be strengthened by a statistical significance test to confirm the differences between the results.

Reorganizing the text by integrating Section 4.4 into Section 4.2 and moving the evaluation of Table 2 to the evaluation section would enhance the paper's clarity.

The introduction could be divided into two sections, with a separate section dedicated to related work, which is currently lacking.

The evaluation section is concise (1.5 pages, including the conclusion). Given the state-of-the-art results, a more in-depth discussion and analysis of the results would be appreciated.

Suggested references:

[1] Iacobacci, I., Pilehvar, M. T., & Navigli, R. (2016). Embeddings for word sense disambiguation: An evaluation study. In Proceedings of the 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (Vol. 1, pp. 897-907).
