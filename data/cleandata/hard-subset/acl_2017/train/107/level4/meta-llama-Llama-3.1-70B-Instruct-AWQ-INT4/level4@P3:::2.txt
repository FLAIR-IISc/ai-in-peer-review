This paper proposes a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) that leverages weak supervision and effective annotation and representation projection. The authors employ a range of NER methods, including conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural networks. Two methods for combining the output of these models are proposed, namely probability-based and ranking-based co-decoding schemes. Additionally, a method for selecting the best training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora is introduced. The cross-lingual projection is performed using a variant of Mikolov's proposal.

**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

The paper presents a comprehensive approach to cross-lingual NER, addressing the challenge of annotating NER data in multiple languages. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A heuristic data selection scheme for selecting good-quality projection-labeled data from noisy comparable corpora.
2. A representation projection approach for direct NER model transfer based on cross-lingual word embedding mappings.
3. Two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems to produce more accurate results.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. The paper presents a thorough evaluation of the proposed approaches on both in-house and open NER data sets, demonstrating their effectiveness in improving the accuracy of cross-lingual NER systems.
2. The authors provide a detailed description of their methods, making it easy to follow and replicate their work.
3. The use of weak supervision and effective annotation and representation projection enables the development of cross-lingual NER systems with minimal human annotation in the target languages.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including a discussion of the strengths and limitations of each approach.
2. The evaluation section is relatively short and could be expanded to provide more insights into the performance of the proposed methods.
3. The paper lacks a dedicated related work section, which would help to situate the contributions of this work within the broader context of cross-lingual NER research.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details about the variant of Mikolov's proposal used for cross-lingual projection, including the parameters employed and the motivations behind this choice?
2. How do the proposed methods compare to other approaches to cross-lingual NER, such as those based on direct model transfer or annotation projection?
3. Have you conducted any tests of statistical significance to evaluate the improvements achieved by the proposed data selection scheme and co-decoding methods?
