This paper proposes a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) using weak supervision. The authors employ various NER methods, including conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural networks. They also propose two methods to combine the output of these methods, a probability-based and a ranking-based approach, as well as a method to select the best training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. The cross-lingual projection is done using a variant of Mikolov's proposal.

**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

The paper presents a model for cross-lingual NER using weak supervision. The main contributions of the paper are:

1. A heuristic data selection scheme that effectively selects good-quality projection-labeled data from noisy comparable corpora.
2. A representation projection approach for direct NER model transfer, which projects word representations in vector space from a target language to a source language.
3. Two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches in a smart way.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach for cross-lingual NER using weak supervision, which is a challenging task.
2. The authors evaluate their approach on various NER datasets, including in-house and open datasets, and achieve state-of-the-art results.
3. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the proposed methods with other state-of-the-art approaches for cross-lingual NER.
2. The evaluation section is relatively short and lacks a thorough analysis of the results.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential future work.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details about the variant of Mikolov's proposal used for cross-lingual projection, including the parameters employed?
2. How does the proposed data selection scheme compare to other methods for selecting training instances from comparable corpora?
3. Can you provide more analysis of the results, including a discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of each approach?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed related work section, which would help to situate the proposed approach within the broader context of cross-lingual NER.
2. The evaluation section could be expanded to include more detailed analysis of the results, including a discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of each approach.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential future work.
