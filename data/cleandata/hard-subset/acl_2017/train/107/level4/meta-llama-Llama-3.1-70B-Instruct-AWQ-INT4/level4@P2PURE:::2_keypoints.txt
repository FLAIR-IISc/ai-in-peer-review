- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model combining CRF, MEMM, and neural methods with probability- and ranking-based ensemble strategies.  
- Introduces a method to select best training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora and uses a variant of Mikolov's cross-lingual projection.  
- Paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and written in good English; combined annotation results are noteworthy.  
- Request for clearer motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variation, including parameters and comparison to original/skip-gram models.  
- Ask to include experimental results with the CBOW variant to demonstrate improvements.  
- Suggest examining exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (as used in Iacobacci et al. 2016).  
- Recommend direct comparison between original Mikolov projections and the proposed frequency-weighted projections.  
- Question whether improvements from the data selection scheme were tested for statistical significance.  
- Structural suggestion: move text of Section 4.4 to the start of Section 4.2 for better organization.  
- Structural suggestion: relocate evaluation of Table 2 into the evaluation section.  
- Recommend adding a separate related work section instead of mixing it into the introduction.  
- Evaluation section is brief; request a more in-depth discussion and analysis given state-of-the-art results.  
- Suggested reference: Iacobacci et al. (2016) on embeddings for word sense disambiguation.
