- Overall approach lacks novelty for an ACL paper: methods improve on existing techniques but are not sufficiently innovative.  
- Data selection scheme uses a simple quality-score formula, but the simplicity and utility are not problematic by themselves.  
- Thresholds in Table 2 are unexplained: unclear how they were chosen and whether a development set was used.  
- Lack of guidance for tuning parameters on new datasets/language pairs despite evaluation showing data selection is important.  
- Combination of the two systems is poorly described and hard to understand.  
- Unclear whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems was tried and what the results were.  
- The "in-house" dataset lacks description—more information about text types and domains is needed for clarity.
