This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for developing Named Entity Recognizers (NERs) that rely on projecting information from English into another language. While the overall approach is not new, the individual methods proposed do offer improvements over existing techniques. However, for a paper submitted to ACL, I would have expected more innovative approaches that push the boundaries of the field.

One of the paper's contributions is the proposed data selection scheme. The formula used to calculate the quality score is straightforward, which is not necessarily a bad thing. Nevertheless, the paper lacks clarity on how the thresholds were determined for Table 2, simply stating that different thresholds were tried. It is unclear whether this process was performed on a development set, and this omission is not addressed in the paper. The evaluation results do demonstrate the importance of data selection, but it remains unclear how to tune the parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another contribution of the paper is the combination of the outputs of the two systems developed in the paper. Unfortunately, I found the description of this process to be unclear, and I had to make a concerted effort to understand how it works.

The paper presents multiple variants for each of the proposed methods. This raises the question of whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would be beneficial. Did the authors explore this possibility, and if so, what were the results?

Additionally, it would be helpful to have more information about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset. Providing more context about the dataset could facilitate a deeper understanding of the methods and results presented in the paper.
