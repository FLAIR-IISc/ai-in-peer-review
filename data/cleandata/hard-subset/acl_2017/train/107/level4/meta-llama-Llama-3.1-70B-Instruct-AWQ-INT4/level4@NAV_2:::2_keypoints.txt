- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches using annotation projection and representation projection with CRF, MEMM, and neural models.
- Introduces probability-based and ranking-based fusion methods and a training-instance selection method for noisy comparable corpora; uses a variant of Mikolov's cross-lingual projection.
- Contribution: heuristic data selection scheme for high-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.
- Contribution: representation projection method that maps target-language word vectors into source-language space for direct model transfer.
- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes to intelligently combine outputs of the projection-based approaches.
- Strength: combined system improves accuracy over individual systems and beats two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.
- Strength: paper is well-structured and written in clear English.
- Strength: combined-annotation results are interesting and provide a strong no-human-annotation baseline.
- Weakness: lacks details on the specific variant of Mikolov’s method and the hyperparameters used.
- Weakness: no comparison with the original CBOW model or Mikolov’s published cross-lingual projections.
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing to assess whether reported differences are meaningful.
- Weakness: evaluation section is brief and lacks deeper discussion and analysis.
- Question: please provide more details on the variant of Mikolov’s proposal and parameter settings.
- Question: please compare your approach with the original CBOW/Mikolov cross-lingual projections.
- Question: have you conducted statistical significance tests on the results?
- Question: can you expand the evaluation section with more discussion and analysis of the results?
