This paper proposes several weakly supervised methods for developing Named Entity Recognizers (NERs) that rely on projecting information from English to another language. While the overall approach is not novel, the individual methods presented can be seen as improvements over existing techniques. However, for an ACL paper, I would have expected more innovative approaches that significantly advance the state-of-the-art.

One of the paper's contributions is its data selection scheme, which utilizes a straightforward formula to calculate the quality score. Although the simplicity of the formula is not inherently problematic, the method used to determine the thresholds presented in Table 2 is unclear. The paper mentions trying different thresholds but does not specify whether this process was performed on a development set, leaving the reader wondering about the robustness of the approach. The evaluation results clearly demonstrate the importance of data selection, but it is unclear how to tune the parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another contribution of the paper is the combination of outputs from the two systems developed. Unfortunately, I found the description of this process to be unclear, making it difficult to fully understand how it works.

The paper presents several variants of each proposed method, raising the question of whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would be beneficial. It would be interesting to know if the authors explored this direction.

Additionally, providing more information about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset would be helpful in understanding the paper's results and their potential applicability to other domains.
