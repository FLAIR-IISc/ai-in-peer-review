- Proposes a weakly supervised model for cross-lingual NER combining CRF, MEMM, and neural methods with projection from a variant of Mikolov's approach.  
- Introduces a heuristic data selection scheme to pick high-quality projection-labeled instances from noisy comparable corpora.  
- Presents a representation-projection method for direct NER model transfer by projecting target-language word vectors into source-language space.  
- Proposes two co-decoding schemes (probability-based and ranking-based) to combine outputs of projection-based approaches.  
- Evaluates on multiple in-house and open NER datasets and reports state-of-the-art results.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the proposed methods.  
- Tackles the challenging problem of cross-lingual NER with a novel weak-supervision approach.  
- Lacks detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods.  
- Evaluation section is brief and does not provide thorough analysis of experimental results.  
- Missing a deeper discussion of limitations and directions for future work.  
- Request for more details on the specific variant and parameters of Mikolov's projection method used.  
- Asks for comparison of the proposed data selection scheme against other instance-selection methods for comparable corpora.  
- Requests more analysis of results, including strengths and weaknesses of each approach.  
- Suggests expanding the related work section to better situate the contribution within cross-lingual NER literature.  
- Recommends expanding evaluation analysis and adding a detailed discussion of limitations and potential future work.
