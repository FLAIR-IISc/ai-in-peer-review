- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via cross-lingual word-embedding mapping.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine outputs of multiple projection-based NER systems for improved accuracy.  
- Uses a variant of Mikolov-style cross-lingual projection for mapping embeddings between languages.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and results from combined annotations are interesting.  
- Novelty: enables cross-lingual NER without target-language human annotation.  
- Evaluation covers multiple target languages on both in-house and public NER datasets.  
- Thorough comparison provided against state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.  
- Lacks clear motivation and parameter details for the proposed CBOW variant and no results reported for original CBOW.  
- Does not sufficiently compare the cross-lingual projection method to the original Mikolov proposal or analyze differences.  
- No statistical significance tests reported for improvements from the data selection scheme.  
- Evaluation section is brief and needs more discussion and analysis of results.  
- Requests more details on CBOW-variant parameters and results with original CBOW.  
- Asks for direct comparison of the proposed projection method against Mikolov’s original approach.  
- Requests statistical significance testing and deeper analysis/comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
