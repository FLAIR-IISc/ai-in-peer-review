Contributions:
- Data selection scheme using a quality score to filter low-quality projection-labeled data, improving annotation-projection NER.
- Representation projection using cross-lingual embedding mappings to apply a source-language NER model to a target language without retraining.
- Two co-decoding schemes that combine outputs of the projection-based systems to yield more accurate results than either alone.

Strengths:
- Data selection is effective, especially when alignment is noisy and projection labels are poor.
- Representation projection offers a practical direct-transfer approach for low-resource languages.
- Co-decoding successfully merges system strengths to achieve higher accuracy.

Weaknesses:
- Quality-score formula is simple and thresholds/tuning (e.g., for Table 2) are not well justified or explained.
- Co-decoding scheme descriptions are unclear, making their operation hard to follow.
- Lack of detail about the "in-house" dataset text types limits assessment of generalizability.

Questions:
- How were data-selection parameters tuned and which development set was used?
- Please provide clearer details on how each co-decoding scheme works and their pros/cons.
- Did you try combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and what were the outcomes?
- What kinds of texts are in the in-house dataset and how do you expect the methods to generalize to other text types?
