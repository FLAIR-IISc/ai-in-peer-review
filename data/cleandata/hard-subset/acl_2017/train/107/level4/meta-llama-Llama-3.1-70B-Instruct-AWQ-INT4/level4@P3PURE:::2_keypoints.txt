- Proposes a cross-lingual NER model combining CRF, MEMM, and neural NER methods.  
- Introduces two ensemble combination methods: probability-based and ranking-based.  
- Proposes a training-instance selection method from cross-lingual comparable corpora.  
- Uses a variant of Mikolov’s cross-lingual projection for projection alignment.  
- Paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and written in good English; combined annotation results are noteworthy.  
- Unclear motivation for the proposed CBOW variant and missing details on its parameters.  
- No comparison results shown for the original CBOW or Continuous Skip-gram to justify the proposed variation.  
- Suggests exploring exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (cites Iacobacci et al. 2016) as an alternative to the current decay factor.  
- Requests a detailed comparison between original Mikolov projections and the proposed frequency-weighted projections.  
- Asks whether statistical significance testing was performed to support claims about the data selection scheme’s effectiveness.  
- Structural suggestion: merge Section 4.4 into the start of Section 4.2 to improve flow.  
- Suggests moving the evaluation of Table 2 into the evaluation section for relevance.  
- Notes absence of a dedicated related work section; recommends splitting the introduction into motivation and related work.  
- Evaluation section is brief (1.5 pages); requests deeper discussion and analysis given the state-of-the-art results.  
- Provides a suggested reference: Iacobacci et al. (2016) for embeddings and decay approach.
