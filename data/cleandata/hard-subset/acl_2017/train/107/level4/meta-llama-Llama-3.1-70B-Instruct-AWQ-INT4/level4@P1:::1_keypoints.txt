- Paper proposes weakly supervised NER methods using annotation projection from English; methods are incremental improvements rather than novel paradigm shifts.  
- Data selection scheme is a key contribution that substantially improves annotation projection accuracy; quality-scoring formula is simple and implementable.  
- Paper lacks detail on how the thresholds in Table 2 were determined (e.g., tuned on a dev set or by another method).  
- Evaluation demonstrates the importance of data selection, but offers limited guidance for tuning parameters on new datasets or language pairs.  
- Combination of outputs from two systems is a significant contribution, but the co-decoding description is unclear.  
- Paper should clarify how confidence scores are computed and how the rank-based co-decoding prioritizes the annotation-projection system.  
- Multiple method variants raise the question of whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems was explored; results are not reported.  
- More information is needed about the contents and text types of the “in-house” dataset to assess generalizability.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets (in-house and CoNLL).  
- Strength: effective data selection scheme that improves projection-based NER.  
- Strength: novel combination of two system outputs that shows promise for performance gains.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of threshold calculation for data selection.  
- Weakness: co-decoding schemes are not described clearly for non-expert readers.  
- Weakness: lack of experiments or discussion on combining more than two weakly supervised systems.  
- Weakness: missing details about the in-house dataset composition.  
- Questions for authors: provide threshold calculation details, clarify co-decoding and confidence scoring, report experiments with >2 systems, and describe the in-house dataset text types.
