- Proposes a cross-lingual NER model combining CRF, MEMM, and neural approaches, with probability- and ranking-based output combination and a training-instance selection method; uses a variant of Mikolov for cross-lingual projection.  
- Paper is well-organized, easy to follow, good English; combined annotation results are noteworthy.  
- Motivation for the proposed CBOW variant is unclear and model/parameter details are insufficient; include CBOW baseline results to evaluate improvements.  
- Suggest exploring exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (as in Iacobacci et al. 2016) given the use of a decay factor.  
- Require a direct comparison between original Mikolov projections and the proposed frequency-weighted projections to demonstrate benefit.  
- The claim about the data selection scheme’s significant effectiveness needs statistical significance testing.  
- Reorganization suggested: integrate Section 4.4 into the start of 4.2 and move Table 2’s evaluation discussion into the evaluation section.  
- Lacks a dedicated related work section; recommend splitting the introduction into two sections.  
- Evaluation section is too brief (1.5 pages including conclusion); deeper discussion and analysis of state-of-the-art results are needed.  
- Recommended reference: Iacobacci, Pilehvar & Navigli (2016).
