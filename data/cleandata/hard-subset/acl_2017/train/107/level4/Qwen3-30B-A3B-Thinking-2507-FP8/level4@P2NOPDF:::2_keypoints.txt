- Paper presents a cross-lingual NER ensemble (CRF, MEMM, neural) with two fusion methods and an instance selection strategy using frequency-weighted Mikolov projection, showing promising improvements.

Major concerns:
- CBOW variant motivation is unclear; authors must justify why CBOW was modified and report comparisons with the original CBOW/skip-gram and hyperparameters used.
- No direct comparison between frequency-weighted projection and Mikolov’s original method; include ablation or side-by-side results to show concrete gains from frequency weighting.
- Claims of “significant” improvement from data selection lack statistical validation; report significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests).

Minor concerns & suggestions:
- Reorganize Section 4.4 into the methodology (beginning of 4.2) and move Table 2 evaluation to Section 4.3 to avoid repetition and improve flow.
- Reduce overlap between Introduction and Related Work; add a concise dedicated related-work subsection separating prior cross-lingual NER and projection techniques.
- Expand the evaluation (currently ~1.5 pages) with deeper analysis: error analysis, per-language/entity-type performance, and explanations for why methods outperform baselines.
- Mention exponential decay approach (Iacobacci et al., 2016) briefly when discussing decay factors for surrounding embeddings.

Suggested references:
- Consider citing more directly relevant cross-lingual embedding/projection work (e.g., Artetxe et al., 2017; Conneau et al., 2018) instead of or in addition to Iacobacci et al., 2016.

Summary:
- Technically sound with strong empirical results, but needs clearer justification for key methodological choices, direct comparisons, statistical tests, and deeper result analysis; reorganization would improve readability.
