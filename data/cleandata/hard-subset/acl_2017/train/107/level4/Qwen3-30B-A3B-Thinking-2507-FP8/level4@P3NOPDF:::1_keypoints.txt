- Methods are largely incremental refinements of established weakly supervised NER techniques, lacking substantial methodological novelty for ACL.
- Primary contribution is a simple data-selection quality score, but thresholds in Table 2 are unexplained.
- No details on how thresholds were chosen (development tuning vs. theory), harming reproducibility and practical applicability.
- Combination/fusion of two systems is described insufficiently; key mechanics (conflict resolution, voting/weighting) are missing.
- The paper does not explore combining more than two weakly supervised systems or analyze ensemble scalability/generality.
- Experiments use an "in-house" dataset with no domain, size, or characteristic details, limiting assessment of generalizability.
- Evaluation is limited to a single language pair (e.g., English→German), so cross-lingual scalability across typologies is unclear.
- Computational cost and training requirements are omitted, which are important for practical deployment.
- Overall methodological rigor and missing details (threshold tuning, fusion mechanics, dataset transparency) weaken the contribution.
- Recommendation: Weak reject; paper could be reconsidered after major revisions addressing thresholds, system combination mechanics, and dataset/expt. transparency.
