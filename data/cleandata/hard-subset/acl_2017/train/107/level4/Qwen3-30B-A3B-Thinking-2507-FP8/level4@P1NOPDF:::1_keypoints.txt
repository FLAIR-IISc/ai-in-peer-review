1. Proposes weakly supervised cross-lingual NER via projection—reasonable idea but lacks sufficient novelty for ACL.  
2. Data selection scheme is presented as the key contribution.  
3. Quality score formula is straightforward and acceptable.  
4. Paper fails to specify how thresholds in Table 2 were chosen (no dev/validation procedure described).  
5. Omission of threshold-selection details hinders reproducibility and application to new datasets/languages.  
6. Method for combining outputs from the two systems is insufficiently described, lacking implementation and technical rationale.  
7. Unclear why or how the combined system outperforms individual systems.  
8. Multiple method variants are introduced but the paper doesn’t explore combining more than two systems or discuss scalability/limitations of ensembles.  
9. “In-house” dataset is undocumented—no details on text types, domain coverage, or linguistic properties.  
10. Empirical gains shown, but incremental contribution and methodological opacity limit the paper’s suitability; authors should clarify threshold tuning, system combination, and dataset characteristics.
