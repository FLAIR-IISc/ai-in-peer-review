- Introduces a cross-lingual NER ensemble combining CRFs, MEMMs, and neural models with two ensemble strategies and a novel data selection method.  
- Modified CBOW model is introduced but lacks motivation and ablation comparing CBOW vs. skip-gram vs. the proposed variant.  
- Frequency-weighted cross-lingual projection is proposed but not directly compared to Mikolov’s original projection method.  
- Claims about the effectiveness of the data selection scheme lack statistical significance testing.  
- Section 4.4 (data selection) and Table 2 are misplaced and should be reorganized for logical flow.  
- Paper lacks a dedicated Related Work section; introduction currently conflates background and contributions.  
- Evaluation is concise but superficial; needs deeper analysis of why the method outperforms prior work and which errors are reduced.  
- Recommend including analysis of how ensemble methods mitigate cross-lingual noise and detailing failure cases or linguistic phenomena.  
- Minor technical fixes: correct terminology to "maximum entropy Markov models (MEMMs)" and compare embedding decay factor to exponential decay.  
- Suggested next steps: integrate Section 4.4 into 4.2, add a Related Work subsection, expand evaluation depth, report statistical tests, and provide CBOW and projection ablation results.  
- Overall: promising practical approach with clear writing, but currently lacks sufficient evidence and structural/technical polish to fully validate contributions.
