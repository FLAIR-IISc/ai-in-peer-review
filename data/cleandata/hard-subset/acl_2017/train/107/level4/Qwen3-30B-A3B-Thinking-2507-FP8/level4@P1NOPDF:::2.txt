

This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) that integrates conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural network-based approaches. The authors propose two ensemble methods (probability-based and ranking-based) to combine these models, alongside a novel technique for selecting high-quality training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projections leverage a frequency-weighted variant of Mikolov’s method. Overall, the paper is well-structured, clearly written, and the results demonstrate notable improvements.  

**Detailed Comments:**  
1. **CBOW Variation Motivation:** The paper introduces a modified Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model but provides insufficient detail on its rationale or parameters. Did the standard CBOW or Skip-gram models yield suboptimal results? Including comparative results with the original CBOW would help readers assess the value of your adaptation.  

2. **Frequency-Weighted Projections:** The frequency-weighted cross-lingual projection method is a key contribution, but the paper lacks a clear comparison with Mikolov’s original approach. To substantiate the superiority of your method, please include a direct comparison of performance metrics between the two projection variants.  

3. **Statistical Significance:** The claim that "the proposed data selection scheme is very effective" requires statistical validation. Please report significance tests (e.g., p-values) for the observed improvements to confirm they are not due to random fluctuations.  

4. **Section Organization:**  
   - Move the content of Section 4.4 (currently describing the data selection scheme) to the beginning of Section 4.2 to avoid redundancy and improve flow.  
   - Relocate Table 2’s evaluation results to the dedicated evaluation section (Section 4) for better coherence.  

5. **Related Work:** The introduction covers some background, but the paper lacks a dedicated related work section. Split the introduction into two parts: (1) problem context and contributions, and (2) related work. This will strengthen the positioning of your method against prior approaches.  

6. **Evaluation Depth:** While the results achieve state-of-the-art performance, the evaluation section (1.5 pages) is overly concise. Provide a more thorough analysis of the results—e.g., error cases, language-specific performance trends, and ablation studies—to help readers understand *why* the proposed methods work.
