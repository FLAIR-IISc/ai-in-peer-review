- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods—annotation projection with a novel data-selection scheme and representation projection via word-embedding mapping—and two co-decoding schemes to combine them.

- Strength: The data-selection scheme is well-motivated and yields large gains when alignment is poor (e.g., +13.9 F1 for Japanese, +20.0 F1 for Korean).

- Strength: Representation projection offers a practical alternative by mapping word embeddings so a single English NER model can transfer to multiple target languages with minimal adaptation.

- Strength: Co-decoding (especially the rank-based approach) effectively combines AP’s high precision and RP’s balanced precision/recall, improving up to +6.2 F1 over individual systems.

- Weakness: Overall novelty is limited—approaches are incremental extensions of established annotation projection and model-transfer methods.

- Weakness: The parameter-tuning procedure for the data-selection scheme is inadequately described; it's unclear whether thresholds were chosen on a development set.

- Weakness: The co-decoding schemes, particularly the rank-based method, are vaguely described and lack sufficient detail for reproducibility.

- Weakness: The paper does not investigate combining more than two weakly supervised variants (e.g., AP + NN1 + NN2) to see if further gains are possible.

- Weakness: The in-house human-annotated dataset is poorly characterized—no details on text types, domain, size, or annotation guidelines—limiting assessment of generalizability.

- Question: Clarify the parameter-tuning process for data selection—were thresholds chosen on a development set, and what were the dev set sizes per language?

- Question: Provide a detailed description of the rank-based co-decoding—how are overlapping or conflicting entity predictions resolved?

- Question: Why not experiment with combining more than two systems (e.g., AP + NN1 + NN2) to evaluate potential additional improvements?

- Question: Describe the in-house dataset’s characteristics (domain, size, annotation guidelines) and how it differs from standard NER datasets like CoNLL.
