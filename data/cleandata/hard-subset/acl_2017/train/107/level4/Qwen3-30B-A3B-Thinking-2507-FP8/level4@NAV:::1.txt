

This paper introduces several weakly supervised approaches for developing Named Entity Recognition (NER) systems, leveraging cross-lingual projection from English to other languages. The core methodology lacks novelty, as the proposed techniques represent incremental refinements of established methods rather than fundamentally new contributions. Given the high standards of ACL, the paper would benefit from more innovative approaches.  

A key contribution is the data selection strategy, where the quality scoring formula is intentionally simple and acceptable. However, the derivation of thresholds for Table 2 remains ambiguous—no details are provided on whether these were tuned via a development set, leaving readers uncertain about parameter adaptation for new datasets or language pairs. The results confirm data selection’s critical impact, but the practical tuning process is inadequately explained.  

The paper also proposes combining outputs from two independently developed systems, yet the implementation details are insufficiently clarified. The description fails to convey the mechanics of this integration, hindering reproducibility and understanding.  

Additionally, the paper explores multiple variants of each method but does not address whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems could yield further gains. The authors should clarify whether such extensions were evaluated. Finally, a brief characterization of the text types within the "in-house" dataset would strengthen the experimental context.
