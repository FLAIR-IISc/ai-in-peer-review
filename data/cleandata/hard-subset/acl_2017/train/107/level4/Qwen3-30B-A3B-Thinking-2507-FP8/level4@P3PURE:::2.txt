

This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) that integrates conditional random fields (CRFs), maximum entropy Markov models (MEMMs), and neural network-based NER approaches. The authors propose two methods for combining these models (probability-based and ranking-based) and introduce a technique for selecting high-quality training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is performed using a variant of Mikolov’s method. Overall, the paper is well-structured, clearly written, and the English is of high quality. The results of the combined approach are promising.

**Detailed Comments:**

1.  **CBOW Variant Justification:** The motivation for proposing a Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model variant is unclear. The paper lacks sufficient detail on the specific modifications or the parameters employed. It is essential to clarify whether the original CBOW or Skip-gram model yielded suboptimal results, and to include the results achieved with the CBOW variant to enable readers to assess the improvements of the proposed approach.

2.  **Comparison with Mikolov’s Method:** The contribution of the frequency-weighted cross-lingual projections over the original Mikolov’s method is not sufficiently demonstrated. A comparative analysis showing the differences and quantitative improvements would significantly strengthen the value of this contribution.

3.  **Statistical Significance:** The claim that "the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting high-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant" requires validation. Please conduct and report a statistical significance test to confirm that the observed performance differences are statistically meaningful.

4.  **Section Organization:** The content of Section 4.4 should be integrated into the beginning of Section 4.2 for improved flow and clarity. Additionally, the evaluation results presented in Table 2 should be moved to the main evaluation section.

5.  **Related Work:** The paper lacks a dedicated related work section. While the introduction contains relevant background, it would be more effective to split the introduction into two distinct sections: one for motivation and background, and a separate section for related work.

6.  **Evaluation Depth:** The evaluation section (1.5 pages, including the conclusion) is concise. Given the achievement of state-of-the-art results, a more detailed discussion and analysis of the results, including error analysis and comparative strengths/weaknesses, would substantially enhance the paper.
