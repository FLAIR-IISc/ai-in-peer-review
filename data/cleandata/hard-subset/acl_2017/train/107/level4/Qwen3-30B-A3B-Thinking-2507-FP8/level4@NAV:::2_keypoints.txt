- Paper presents a cross-lingual NER model using CRF, MEMM, and neural approaches.  
- Authors propose two fusion methods: probability-based and ranking-based.  
- They introduce a technique for selecting optimal training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora.  
- Cross-lingual projection uses a variant of Mikolov’s embedding method.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and English quality is strong.  
- Combined-annotation results are promising and achieve state-of-the-art performance.  
- Motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variation are unclear; include parameters and rationale.  
- Provide CBOW performance metrics (and compare to skip-gram) to justify the CBOW adaptation.  
- For the decay factor on surrounding embeddings, consider using/explaining exponential decay (per Iacobacci et al., 2016).  
- Explicitly compare frequency-weighted cross-lingual projections against Mikolov’s original approach with empirical results.  
- Validate the data selection scheme’s effectiveness with statistical significance testing.  
- Reorganize content: move Section 4.4 to start of 4.2 and relocate Table 2’s evaluation to the evaluation section.  
- Add a dedicated related work section instead of leaving it partially in the introduction.  
- Expand the concise evaluation with deeper discussion and analysis of results.  
- Include the suggested reference (Iacobacci et al., 2016) when discussing embedding/decay mechanisms.
