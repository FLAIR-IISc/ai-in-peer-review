- Data selection scheme for annotation projection finds high-quality weak labels from noisy comparable corpora, significantly improving performance when alignment accuracy is low (e.g., +13.9 F1 for Japanese).  
- Representation projection maps target-language embeddings into English, enabling direct application of the source-language NER system without retraining.  
- Two co-decoding schemes combine annotation and representation projection outputs and achieve state-of-the-art results on CoNLL.  
- Data selection has practical impact for low-resource languages, delivering substantial gains for languages with poor alignment (Japanese, Korean).  
- Representation projection avoids per-language NER retraining, simplifying deployment and scaling to new languages.  
- Co-decoding (especially rank-based) effectively fuses high-precision annotation projection with balanced recall of representation projection, yielding consistent +2.9–6.2 F1 gains.  
- Rigorous evaluation on in-house (50+ entity types) and CoNLL datasets demonstrates robustness and outperforms prior SOTA (Täckström et al., 2012; Tsai et al., 2016).  
- No statistical significance testing is reported for F1 improvements, weakening claims of "significant" gains.  
- Missing direct comparison to Mikolov et al. (2013b) cross-lingual embedding mapping, leaving the novelty/superiority of representation projection unverified.  
- Insufficient motivation and ablation for the weighted CBOW variant; no comparison to standard CBOW or Skip-gram to justify the choice.  
- Evaluation lacks depth: limited analysis of why rank-based co-decoding outperforms confidence-based, and little discussion of failure cases or error analysis.  
- Request statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests or confidence intervals) for reported F1 improvements across languages.  
- Ask for a comparison between the weighted cross-lingual mapping and Mikolov et al.’s unweighted approach in terms of performance and robustness.  
- Request results for a standard CBOW model (without distance-decay weights) to demonstrate the necessity of the weighted CBOW modification.  
- Ask for analysis explaining why rank-based co-decoding outperforms confidence-based, including precision-recall trade-offs and error analysis.
