

This paper presents a cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) model combining conditional random fields (CRFs), maximum entropy Markov models (MEMMs), and neural network approaches. The authors propose two ensemble methods (probability-based and ranking-based) to integrate these models' outputs, alongside a novel instance selection strategy for quality data extraction from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection leverages a frequency-weighted variant of Mikolov's method. Overall, the paper is well-structured, clearly written, and the results demonstrate promising improvements over baselines.

**Major Concerns:**
1.  **CBOW Variant Justification:** The motivation for proposing a CBOW variant (instead of skip-gram) is unclear. The paper does not detail the specific shortcomings of the original CBOW/skip-gram models or the parameters used for the variant. Including results with the original CBOW model would enable readers to assess the claimed improvements. The authors should clarify *why* this variant was necessary and provide comparative results.
2.  **Mikolov Projection Comparison:** The paper claims superiority of the frequency-weighted projection over Mikolov's original method, but lacks a direct comparison of the two approaches. A clear analysis demonstrating *how* the frequency weighting leads to tangible gains (e.g., via ablation studies showing performance differences on key metrics) is essential to validate this contribution.
3.  **Statistical Significance:** The assertion that "the proposed data selection scheme is very effective... and the improvement is significant" requires statistical validation. The authors must report significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) for the reported performance gains to substantiate this claim.

**Minor Concerns & Suggestions:**
1.  **Section Organization:** The text of Section 4.4 (on data selection) would flow better if integrated into the beginning of Section 4.2 (methodology), avoiding repetition and improving logical progression. Additionally, the evaluation of Table 2 should be moved to the dedicated evaluation section (Section 4.3) for better coherence.
2.  **Related Work:** The introduction contains overlapping content with a related work section. The paper would benefit from a dedicated, concise related work subsection (e.g., separating prior cross-lingual NER methods from projection techniques) to provide clearer context for the contributions.
3.  **Evaluation Depth:** The evaluation section (1.5 pages) is brief given the state-of-the-art results achieved. A more thorough analysis of the results is needed—e.g., error analysis, discussion of model strengths/weaknesses across languages or entity types, and a clearer interpretation of *why* the proposed methods yield improvements over baselines. This would significantly strengthen the paper's impact.
4.  **Exponential Decay Reference:** The use of a decay factor for surrounding embeddings warrants a brief mention of the exponential decay approach in [1] (Iacobacci et al., 2016) for contextualization, though this is a minor point.

**Summary:** The paper addresses an important problem with a technically sound approach and demonstrates strong empirical results. However, the current presentation lacks sufficient justification for key methodological choices (CBOW variant, frequency weighting), statistical validation for claimed improvements, and deeper analysis of results. Addressing these points—particularly by providing comparative results for the CBOW variant, directly comparing projection methods, and conducting significance tests—would substantially strengthen the paper's contribution and clarity. The proposed reorganization of sections would also improve readability. With these revisions, this work has potential to make a valuable contribution to cross-lingual NER.
