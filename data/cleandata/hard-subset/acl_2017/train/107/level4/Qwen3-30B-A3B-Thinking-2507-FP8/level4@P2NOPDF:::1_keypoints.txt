- Paper proposes weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods but is largely incremental with limited novelty relative to prior work.  
- Data selection scheme (simple quality scoring) is a notable contribution.  
- Threshold selection procedure (Table 2) is unclear—no info on whether thresholds were tuned on a dev set or how they were chosen, hurting reproducibility and adaptation.  
- Combination/merging of the two system outputs is insufficiently described; the merging mechanism lacks detail and examples, making replication and evaluation difficult.  
- The paper experiments with variants but fails to explore or justify scaling beyond two weakly supervised systems—unclear if combining more systems yields further gains.  
- An "in-house" dataset is used but its linguistic characteristics, domain, and text types are not described, limiting assessment of generalizability.  
- Overall practical utility is shown but methodological gaps, lack of theoretical insight, and limited novelty reduce suitability for a high-tier venue like ACL; clearer technical details and stronger contributions are needed.
