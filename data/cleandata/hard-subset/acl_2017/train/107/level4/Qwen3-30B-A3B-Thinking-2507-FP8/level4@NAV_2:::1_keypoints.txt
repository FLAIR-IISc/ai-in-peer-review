- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection mapping target embeddings to source space.  
- Primary contribution: a heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that boosts performance when alignment is noisy.  
- Secondary contribution: representation projection enabling direct transfer of a source NER model to target languages.  
- Tertiary contribution: co-decoding schemes to combine outputs of the two projection-based systems.  
- Strength: Data selection yields large F1 gains (up to +20.0 for Korean) under low alignment accuracy.  
- Strength: Combined systems outperform two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER baselines on CoNLL test sets.  
- Strength: Evaluation is extensive across six target languages and both in-house and public NER datasets.  
- Weakness: Overall approach is incremental, improving existing techniques rather than introducing fundamentally new methods.  
- Weakness: Threshold selection (q, n) for data selection is not clearly justified and may not generalize to new language pairs/datasets.  
- Weakness: Co-decoding schemes are insufficiently described, hindering reproducibility and understanding.  
- Question: How were the threshold parameters (q, n) chosen—tuned on a development set, and if so, how was that set constructed?  
- Question: What is the composition of the in-house dataset (genres like newswire or social media) and how does it compare to CoNLL?  
- Question: Did the authors try combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and what were the outcomes if so?
