

This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER). The authors employ conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural network-based NER methods. Additionally, they propose two methods to combine these approaches (probability-based and ranking-based) and a method to select high-quality training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is performed using a variant of Mikolov's approach. Overall, the paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and written in correct English. The results from combined annotations are interesting.

Detailed comments:
The motivation for proposing a Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model variant requires clarification. The paper provides limited detail on this modification or the parameters used. Please specify whether the original model (or Continuous Skip-gram) yielded inferior results, and include CBOW performance results to enable readers to assess the improvements of your approach.  
Regarding the decay factor for surrounding embeddings, consider examining the exponential decay mechanism described in [1].  
Similarly, to strengthen the contribution, provide a comparative analysis between your frequency-weighted cross-lingual projections and Mikolov's original method. Demonstrating clear superiority would enhance the value of this work.  
The claim that "the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant" requires statistical significance testing. Please provide such analysis to confirm the results' significance.  
I recommend integrating the content of Section 4.4 into Section 4.2 for improved flow, and moving the evaluation of Table 2 to the dedicated evaluation section.  
A related work section is missing. While the introduction contains partial coverage, it should be split into two distinct sections.  
The evaluation section (1.5 pages including the conclusion) is brief given the state-of-the-art results achieved. A more detailed discussion and analysis of these results would be valuable.

Suggested references:
