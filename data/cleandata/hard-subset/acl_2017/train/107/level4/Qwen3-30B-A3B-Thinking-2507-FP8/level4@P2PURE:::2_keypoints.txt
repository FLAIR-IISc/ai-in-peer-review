- Paper presents a cross-lingual NER model combining CRF, MEMM, and neural methods.  
- Authors propose two combination methods: probability-based and ranking-based.  
- They introduce a method to select high-quality training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora.  
- Cross-lingual projection is performed using a variant of Mikolov’s approach.  
- Paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and written in correct English.  
- Results from combined annotations are interesting and show state-of-the-art performance.  
- Motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variant are unclear; parameters and modification description are lacking.  
- Need to report whether original CBOW or Skip-gram performed worse and include CBOW baseline results.  
- Suggest examining exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (reference provided).  
- Require comparative analysis between frequency-weighted projections and Mikolov’s original method to demonstrate superiority.  
- Claim about effectiveness of data selection needs statistical significance testing.  
- Recommend reorganizing: merge Section 4.4 into 4.2 and move Table 2 evaluation to the evaluation section.  
- Related work section is missing; introduction should be split into Introduction and Related Work.  
- Evaluation section is brief given state-of-the-art results and needs more detailed discussion and analysis.
