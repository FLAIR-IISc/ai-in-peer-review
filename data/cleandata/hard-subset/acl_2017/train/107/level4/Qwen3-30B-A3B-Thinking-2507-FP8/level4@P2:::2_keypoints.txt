- Proposes annotation projection with a heuristic, frequency-based data selection scheme for filtering noisy labels from comparable (non-parallel) corpora.  
- Proposes representation projection that maps target-language word embeddings into the source-language space to apply a single source NER system without retraining.  
- Proposes co-decoding (notably a rank-based method) to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs.  

- Strength: The data selection scheme is well-motivated and yields large gains (up to +20 F1) for low-alignment-accuracy languages like Japanese and Korean.  
- Strength: The representation projection approach is simple and practical, enabling cross-lingual NER transfer without retraining per language.  
- Strength: The rank-based co-decoding effectively leverages complementary strengths of the two methods and consistently improves F1 by 2.9–6.2 points.  

- Weakness: Insufficient evidence and comparison to prior selection methods, leaving novelty of the data selection scheme unclear.  
- Weakness: Representation projection evaluation is limited to word-embedding variants with little comparison to other cross-lingual transfer techniques or analysis of why it outperforms alternatives.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing is reported for the claimed performance improvements.  

- Question: How exactly does the data selection scheme improve upon prior noisy-projection filtering methods—please provide detailed comparisons to earlier work.  
- Question: Please report results comparing your CBOW variant (distance-weighted context) to standard CBOW and skip-gram to justify the modification.  
- Question: Please include statistical significance tests for the reported gains to confirm they are not due to random variation.
