- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods requiring no target-language annotation—heuristic data selection for annotation projection, representation projection via embedding mapping—and two co-decoding schemes; combined systems achieve state-of-the-art on CoNLL.

- Strength: Heuristic data selection effectively filters noisy projection-labeled data, yielding large F1 gains when alignment is poor (e.g., +13.9 for Japanese, +20.0 for Korean).

- Strength: Representation projection enables direct model transfer without retraining by mapping word embeddings, avoiding language-specific feature engineering.

- Strength: Rank-based co-decoding leverages complementary strengths (high-precision annotation projection + balanced representation projection), improving 2.9–6.2 F1 over individual systems.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing is reported, so claimed F1 improvements may not be reliably meaningful.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison between the proposed frequency-weighted mapping and Mikolov’s original (2013b) method; no ablation to quantify the weighting contribution.

- Weakness: Evaluation lacks detailed analysis of failure modes, error types, and runtime/memory costs relative to prior methods.

- Question: Please provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) for all reported performance improvements.

- Question: Include ablation results comparing the frequency-weighted mapping to Mikolov’s unweighted approach on the same datasets.

- Question: Clarify why rank-based co-decoding outperforms confidence-based schemes given the differing precision/recall characteristics of the component systems.
