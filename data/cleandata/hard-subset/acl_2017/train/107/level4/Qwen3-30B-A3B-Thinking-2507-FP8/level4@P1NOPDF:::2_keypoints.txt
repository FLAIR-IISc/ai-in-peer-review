- Presents an ensemble model for cross-lingual NER combining CRF, MEMM, neural models, two ensemble methods, and a new training-instance selection technique with strong results.  
- CBOW variation: insufficient motivation and parameter details; provide comparisons with standard CBOW/Skip-gram to justify the modification.  
- Frequency-weighted projections: key contribution but no direct performance comparison to Mikolov’s original method; include experiment comparing the two.  
- Statistical significance: claims about the data selection scheme need significance testing (e.g., p-values) to confirm improvements.  
- Section organization: move Section 4.4 to the start of 4.2 to reduce redundancy and relocate Table 2 to the main evaluation section.  
- Related work: paper lacks a dedicated related work section; split the introduction into problem/contributions and related work.  
- Evaluation depth: evaluation is too brief; add error analysis, language-specific trends, and ablation studies to explain why the methods work.  
- Suggested reference: consider Iacobacci et al. (2016) for insights on exponential decay mechanisms relevant to the decay factor implementation.
