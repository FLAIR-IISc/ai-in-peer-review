- Proposes weakly supervised NER via projection from English to other languages, but relies on existing techniques rather than novel methods.  
- Individual methodological improvements are acceptable, yet overall novelty is weak for an ACL paper.  
- Introduces a simple data selection scheme using a quality score formula, which is acceptable in principle.  
- Fails to justify the threshold values in Table 2 or explain how they were chosen (e.g., development set tuning).  
- Lacks guidance for adapting the data selection thresholds to new datasets or language pairs despite its empirical importance.  
- Describes a combination of outputs from two systems, but the mechanism is insufficiently detailed and lacks rationale.  
- Does not explore combining more than two weakly supervised systems, missing a relevant experimental question.  
- Provides inadequate characterization of the in-house dataset (domain/genre/style), limiting result interpretability.
