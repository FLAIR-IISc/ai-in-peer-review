

This paper proposes weakly supervised methods for named entity recognition (NER) that leverage projection from English to other languages. While the overall approach builds on existing work rather than introducing novel techniques, the individual methodological improvements are acceptable. However, for an ACL publication, a stronger emphasis on novelty would be expected.  

A key contribution is the data selection scheme, which uses a straightforward quality score formula—this simplicity is acceptable. Nevertheless, the paper fails to justify the threshold values used in Table 2. It states that "different thresholds were tried" but provides no details on how these were determined (e.g., via development set tuning). Without this, readers lack guidance for adapting the approach to new datasets or language pairs, despite the clear empirical importance of data selection.  

Another contribution is the combination of outputs from the two proposed systems. The description of this mechanism is insufficiently detailed, making it difficult to understand the exact procedure or rationale. The paper also does not investigate whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would yield further gains, which is a relevant gap given the exploration of multiple variants for each method.  

Finally, the paper lacks sufficient characterization of the "in-house" dataset, particularly regarding text types (e.g., domain, genre, or style), which limits the interpretability of the results.
