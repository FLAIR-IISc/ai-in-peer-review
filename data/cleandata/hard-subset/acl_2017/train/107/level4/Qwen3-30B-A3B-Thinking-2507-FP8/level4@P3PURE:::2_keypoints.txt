- Presents a cross-lingual NER model combining CRFs, MEMMs, and neural methods, with two combination schemes (probability- and ranking-based), a high-quality instance selection technique, and a Mikolov-variant projection; paper is well-written and results are promising.
- CBOW variant rationale is unclear; paper needs details on modifications, parameters, and comparative results versus original CBOW/Skip-gram.
- The benefit of frequency-weighted cross-lingual projections over Mikolov’s original method is not demonstrated; provide comparative quantitative analysis.
- Claims of significant improvement from the data selection scheme require statistical significance testing and reporting.
- Section organization is weak: move Section 4.4 into the start of 4.2 and relocate Table 2 to the main evaluation section for better flow.
- Missing a dedicated related work section; split the introduction into motivation/background and a separate related work section.
- Evaluation is brief despite state-of-the-art claims; expand with deeper analysis, error analysis, and discussion of comparative strengths/weaknesses.
- Suggested reference to consider: Iacobacci et al. (2016) on embeddings for word sense disambiguation.
