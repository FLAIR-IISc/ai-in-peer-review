

This paper proposes several weakly supervised methods for developing NER systems, leveraging projection from English to other languages. The overall approach builds upon existing work, and the individual methods constitute incremental improvements rather than novel contributions. As an ACL submission, I would have expected more substantial methodological innovation.  

A key contribution is the data selection scheme, where the quality score formula is straightforward and acceptable. However, the paper does not specify how the thresholds for Table 2 were determined—only noting that "different thresholds were tried." Was this tuning performed on a development set? The absence of this detail impedes reproducibility. While the evaluation demonstrates data selection's critical role, the methodology for parameter tuning on new datasets or language pairs remains unclear.  

Another contribution is the combination of outputs from the two proposed systems. The description provided fails to clarify the mechanism of this integration, making it difficult to assess. Additionally, the paper introduces multiple variants per method but does not address whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would be beneficial or explore such extensions.  

Finally, a brief characterization of the text types within the "in-house" dataset would strengthen the evaluation context.
