- Proposes two projection-based cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via cross-lingual embedding mapping.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine the two approaches, with a rank-based variant that prioritizes annotation projection.  
- Main contribution is the data selection scheme that filters noisy weak labels and yields large gains (e.g., +13.9 F1 Japanese, +20.0 F1 Korean).  
- Representation projection is a novel, scalable way to transfer an English NER system to multiple target languages without retraining.  

- Data selection effectively improves annotation projection when alignment quality is low, addressing a gap in prior work.  
- Representation projection offers a clean, flexible solution for low-resource cross-lingual transfer by mapping target embeddings to English.  
- Rank-based co-decoding consistently boosts performance (≈+2.9–6.2 F1) by leveraging complementary precision/recall traits.  

- Paper does not specify how data-selection thresholds (q, n) were chosen, hindering reproducibility and applicability to new languages.  
- Co-decoding (especially rank-based) is ambiguously described; algorithmic details or pseudocode for conflict resolution are missing.  
- Evaluation is limited: comparisons omit recent baselines (e.g., multilingual BERT) and CoNLL gains are marginal in some cases.  
- The in-house dataset lacks domain/genre and linguistic detail (despite >50 entity types), limiting assessment of generalizability.  
- No exploration of combining more than two weakly supervised systems, missing potential further improvements.  

- How were data-selection thresholds (q, n) determined and were they language-specific or tuned on development sets?  
- Please provide exact algorithmic steps for the rank-based co-decoding, including conflict-resolution rules for overlapping entities.  
- How do the authors justify claiming state-of-the-art performance given modest CoNLL gains (e.g., +1.6 F1 for German) versus prior work?  
- What are the domain, genre, and linguistic characteristics (e.g., sentence length) of the in-house NER dataset, and how might they affect results?
