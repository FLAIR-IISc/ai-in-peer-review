This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for building NERs. These methods rely on some kind of projection from English to another language. The overall method is not new, and the proposed individual methods are improvements to existing methods. I expected more innovative methods for an ACL paper.

One of the contributions of this paper is the data selection method. The formula used to calculate the quality score is quite simple and not necessarily a bad thing. However, it is not clear how the thresholds for Table 2 were calculated. The paper only states that different thresholds were tried. Was this done on a development set? There is no mention of this in the paper. The evaluation results clearly show that data selection is very important, but one may not know how to tune the parameters for a new data set or a new language pair.

Another contribution of this paper is the combination of the outputs of the two systems created in the paper. I tried hard to understand how it works, but the details provided are not clear.

The paper presents several variations for each of the proposed methods. Is it meaningful to combine more than two weakly supervised systems? Have the authors tried anything in this direction?

It would be good to know a little more about what kind of text is in the "in-house" dataset.
