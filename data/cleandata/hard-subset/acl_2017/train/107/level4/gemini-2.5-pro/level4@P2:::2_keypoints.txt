- Proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER framework requiring no target-language annotations using two projection methods (AP and RP).
- Annotation Projection (AP) projects labels via comparable corpora and uses a heuristic data selection scheme to filter noisy projections.
- Representation Projection (RP) trains NER on source-language embeddings and maps target embeddings into source space with a linear mapping.
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes (confidence-based and rank-based) to combine AP and RP outputs.
- The combined system achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL 2002/2003 for Spanish, Dutch, and German.
- Main contribution: effective integration of AP and RP that leverages their complementary precision/recall trade-offs.
- Main contribution: a practical data selection heuristic for AP that substantially improves weakly labeled training data quality (e.g., +20 F1 for Korean).
- Strength: strong empirical performance consistently outperforming individual components and prior baselines.
- Strength: thoughtful combination strategy that prioritizes AP’s high precision and RP’s more balanced recall.
- Strength: practical and broadly applicable approach that adds languages with only monolingual embeddings and a bilingual mapping.
- Strength: evaluation across diverse datasets (proprietary fine-grained and CoNLL coarse-grained) showing robustness.
- Weakness: insufficient justification and lack of ablation studies for modified CBOW and weighted linear mapping choices.
- Weakness: limited qualitative/error analysis explaining why AP and RP complement each other and what each model misses.
- Weakness: absence of statistical significance testing to support claims of improvement.
- Weakness: minor structural issues—Related Work placement and experimental results are not well consolidated.
- Question: clarify motivation and provide comparisons for the CBOW modifications and weighted mapping versus standard methods.
- Question: report statistical significance tests for main comparisons (Table 5) and data selection gains (Table 2).
- Question: provide error analysis detailing the types of entities or contexts where AP or RP tend to outperform.
