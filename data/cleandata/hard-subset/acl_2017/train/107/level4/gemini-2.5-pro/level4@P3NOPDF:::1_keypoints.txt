- Main contribution: Demonstrates that careful data selection for projection-based NER, using a simple quality score, substantially improves performance by filtering noisy projected annotations.
- Novelty concern: Approach and proposed methods (projection via alignments, data selection, system combination) are incremental refinements of existing techniques rather than a fundamentally new idea.
- Hyperparameter transparency: Paper fails to explain how quality-score thresholds were chosen or whether they were tuned on a held-out dev set, raising reproducibility and potential test-set overfitting issues.
- System-combination clarity: The algorithm for combining the two weakly supervised systems is poorly described and not specified in a way that supports reimplementation.
- Experimental scope limited: Only two systems are combined; authors should analyze combining more models and alternative combination strategies to show complementary strengths.
- Dataset opacity: Use of an "in-house" dataset without domain/genre/characteristic details prevents contextualizing results and assessing generalizability.
- Overall: Important practical lesson on data selection, but paper is weakened by limited novelty and significant clarity and methodological-detail shortcomings.
