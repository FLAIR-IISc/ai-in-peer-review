- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER system combining CRF, MEMM, and neural models with two ensembling methods and a training-instance selection scheme; cross-lingual projection uses a variant of Mikolov et al.

- Overall manuscript is well-structured, easy to follow, and clearly written; combined-model results are interesting.

- Motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variant are unclear; authors should report baseline results from standard CBOW (and skip-gram) for comparison.

- The decay factor for surrounding word embeddings needs discussion/comparison with exponential decay methods (e.g., Iacobacci et al. 2016).

- The frequency-weighted cross-lingual projection should be directly compared empirically to the original Mikolov et al. projection to demonstrate its benefit.

- Claims about the effectiveness and significance of the data selection scheme require statistical significance testing to support them.

- Structural suggestion: integrate Section 4.4 into the start of Section 4.2 and move the Table 2 evaluation to the main evaluation section.

- The paper lacks a dedicated Related Work section; a separate section would give a more comprehensive overview of prior research.

- The evaluation section is brief (1.5 pages); more in-depth analysis and discussion of the reported state-of-the-art results are needed.

- Include the suggested reference (Iacobacci et al., 2016) when discussing embedding/decay-related methods.
