- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER system combining CRF, MEMM, and a neural method, with two novel ensembling techniques (probability- and ranking-based) and a method for selecting high-quality training instances from comparable corpora, using a frequency-weighted variant of Mikolov et al. for projection.

- Writing and structure are generally clear and high quality; combined-model results are interesting and promising.

- Motivation and technical details for the proposed CBOW variant are insufficient; include parameters and rationale and compare against original CBOW/Skip-gram baselines.

- Because the model uses a decay factor for surrounding embeddings, compare or discuss exponential decay methods (e.g., Iacobacci et al. 2016).

- Provide a direct empirical comparison between the original Mikolov cross-lingual projection and the proposed frequency-weighted variant to demonstrate superiority.

- Report statistical significance tests to support claims that the data selection scheme yields significant improvements.

- Reorganize the paper for clarity: integrate Section 4.4 into the start of 4.2 and move the evaluation/discussion of Table 2 into the main evaluation section.

- Add a dedicated Related Work section for a more structured and comprehensive overview of prior research.

- Expand the evaluation section (now brief) with more discussion, analyses, and an error analysis to better explain strengths and weaknesses of the combination methods.
