- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER framework combining CRF, MEMM, and neural models.
- Introduces two novel ensemble methods (probability-based and ranking-based) to fuse model outputs.
- Proposes a data selection strategy for choosing training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora.
- Uses a modified Mikolov-style cross-lingual projection (frequency-weighted variant).
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with high-quality English.
- Reported performance gains from the ensemble methods are notable.
- Motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variant are unclear; parameters and rationale are missing.
- No baseline comparison shown with standard CBOW or Skip-gram to quantify the CBOW variant’s improvement.
- Suggests considering exponential decay for contextual embeddings (reference provided).
- Lacks a direct comparison between original Mikolov projection and the proposed frequency-weighted variant to demonstrate benefit.
- Unclear whether performance improvements from the data selection scheme are statistically significant; significance tests should be reported.
- Recommends merging Section 4.4 into Section 4.2 and moving Table 2 discussion to the main evaluation for better flow.
- Paper lacks a dedicated Related Work section for a structured literature review.
- Evaluation section is brief despite state-of-the-art results; more in-depth discussion and error analysis are needed.
- Provides a suggested reference for embeddings and decay methods (Iacobacci et al., 2016).
