- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: Annotation Projection (AP) using comparable corpora and Representation Projection (RP) mapping target embeddings into source space, plus two co-decoding schemes.

- Main contribution: effective combination of label-space (AP) and feature-space (RP) projections with co-decoding to achieve state-of-the-art results; introduces a heuristic for selecting higher-quality noisy AP training instances.

- Strong empirical performance: combined system achieves SOTA on CoNLL 2002/2003 for Spanish, Dutch, and German, outperforming strong baselines.

- Well-motivated, practical methods: AP benefits from data selection, RP enables simple model transfer, and co-decoding leverages complementary precision/recall profiles.

- Comprehensive evaluation across six languages (JA, KO, DE, PT, ES, NL) and two dataset types (in-house fine-grained and CoNLL coarse-grained).

- Clear writing and good presentation, making methods and experiments easy to follow.

- Lacks ablation studies: no comparison of the proposed distance-decay CBOW variant or weighted cross-lingual mapping to standard CBOW/Skip-gram or unweighted mapping.

- Insufficient analysis: limited discussion of why models perform as they do (e.g., why different co-decoding schemes work best on different datasets) and no error analysis by entity type.

- No statistical significance testing reported for F1 improvements, making modest gains (1–3 points) potentially uncertain.

- Minor structural issues: missing a dedicated Related Work section and misplacement of Table 2 within the methods section instead of Experiments.

- Question: Provide justification or comparative results for the modified CBOW model and weighted cross-lingual mapping versus standard implementations.

- Question: Have statistical significance tests been performed to validate the reported improvements?

- Question: Explain why confidence-based vs. rank-based co-decoding differ in performance across datasets — does this stem from entity types, dataset nature, or relative system behavior?
