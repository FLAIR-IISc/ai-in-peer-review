- Paper proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with a heuristic data-selection filter, and representation projection via linear mapping of target embeddings into English, plus two co-decoding schemes to combine them; evaluated on in-house and CoNLL datasets and outperforms prior weakly supervised methods.

- Contribution: a simple yet effective heuristic for filtering noisy projected annotation data, yielding large F1 gains (up to ~20 points) especially for low-quality alignments.

- Contribution: effective combination of annotation-projection (high precision) and representation-projection (balanced precision/recall) via co-decoding, setting a new strong state-of-the-art.

- Strength: strong empirical results and new SOTA on CoNLL benchmarks, with particularly large gains for German.

- Strength: clearly demonstrates the critical importance of filtering/selection for annotation projection and provides a practical, high-impact heuristic.

- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across a fine-grained in-house dataset (50+ entity types) and standard coarse-grained CoNLL datasets, showing robustness.

- Weakness: core ideas (annotation projection and embedding-based model transfer) are incremental and build on well-established paradigms rather than offering a fundamentally novel approach.

- Weakness: insufficient detail on how key data-selection hyperparameters (q and n) were chosen, limiting reproducibility and guidance for new languages.

- Weakness: co-decoding schemes are described briefly and ambiguously (especially rank-based); clearer algorithmic descriptions are needed for reproducibility.

- Question: please clarify how the data-selection thresholds (q and n) were chosen for Table 2 — was a development set used, and what general guidance can you offer?

- Question: did you try combining more than two systems (e.g., AP + NN1 + NN2), and would a three-way combination improve results?

- Question: please provide details about the in-house dataset's domain/genre (newswire, web, etc.) to contextualize the results.
