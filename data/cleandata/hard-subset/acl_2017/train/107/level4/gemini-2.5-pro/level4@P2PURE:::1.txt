This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for training Named Entity Recognition (NER) systems by projecting annotations from English to another language. The general approach is well-established, and the individual methods proposed are best characterized as incremental improvements over existing work. The paper's primary weakness is its limited novelty, which may fall short of the standard expected for an ACL publication.

One of the paper's main contributions is its data selection scheme. While the quality score formula is commendably straightforward, the methodology for determining the selection thresholds used in Table 2 is unclear. The authors only state that "different thresholds were tried," without specifying whether this tuning was performed on a development set, which is not mentioned in the paper. Although the evaluation results clearly demonstrate the importance of data selection, this lack of detail on the parameter tuning process hinders the method's reproducibility and applicability to new datasets or language pairs.

Another contribution is the method for combining the outputs of the two systems developed in the paper. However, the description of this combination strategy is unclear, making it difficult to fully understand its mechanics.

The authors present a number of variants for each of their proposed methods. This raises the question of whether combining more than two of these weakly supervised systems could yield further gains. It would be interesting to know if the authors explored this possibility.

Finally, the paper would be strengthened by providing a more detailed description of the "in-house" dataset, particularly regarding the domain and types of texts it contains.
