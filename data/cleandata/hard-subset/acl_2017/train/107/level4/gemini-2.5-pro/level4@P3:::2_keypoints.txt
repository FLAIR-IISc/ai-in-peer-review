- Proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER system combining annotation projection and representation projection without target-language annotations.
- Main contribution: an effective ensemble/co-decoding system that leverages complementary precision/recall profiles to achieve strong performance.
- Contribution: a novel data-selection heuristic for annotation projection that filters noisy projections and improves robustness for low-quality alignments.
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL benchmarks, outperforming recent cross-lingual NER systems, especially for German.
- Strength: thoughtful combination of complementary methods (AP high-precision, RP balanced) with co-decoding yielding substantial F1 gains.
- Strength: data-selection heuristic gives dramatic improvements for languages with poor alignment (e.g., Japanese, Korean).
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and reproducible.
- Weakness: introduces weighted CBOW and weighted cross-lingual mapping without ablation studies to justify their impact.
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests reported to support claims of improvement.
- Weakness: limited error analysis and discussion (e.g., inconsistent NN1/NN2 performance across datasets and which errors co-decoding fixes).
- Weakness: minor organizational issues—related work placement and disjointed presentation of data-selection results.
- Question: please provide empirical comparisons of the proposed weighted CBOW and weighted mapping vs. the original unweighted variants.
- Question: have you conducted statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values) for main results, especially for data selection and co-decoding improvements?
