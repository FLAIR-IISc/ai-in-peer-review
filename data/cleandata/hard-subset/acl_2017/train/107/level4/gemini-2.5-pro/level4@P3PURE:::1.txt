This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for developing Named Entity Recognition (NER) systems, based on projecting information from English to a target language. While the proposed methods are improvements on existing work, the overall approach is not new. For a paper at ACL, a higher degree of novelty would be expected.

One of the main contributions is a data selection scheme. The straightforwardness of the quality score formula is a positive attribute; however, the paper's explanation of its application is lacking. Specifically, it is unclear how the thresholds reported in Table 2 were selected, as the paper only states that "different thresholds were tried." It is not mentioned whether this tuning was performed on a development set. While the evaluation results clearly demonstrate the importance of data selection, this omission makes it difficult for a reader to know how to tune these parameters for a new dataset or language pair, impacting the method's reproducibility.

Another contribution is the combination of the outputs from the two systems developed in the paper. Unfortunately, the description of this combination strategy is unclear, and I was unable to fully grasp its mechanics from the text provided.

Given that the paper presents a number of variants for each proposed method, it raises the question of whether combining more than two of these weakly supervised systems would be beneficial. It would be interesting to know if the authors explored this possibility.

Finally, for the sake of clarity and to better contextualize the results, it would be beneficial if the paper provided more details about the "in-house" dataset, such as the domain or genre of the texts it contains.
