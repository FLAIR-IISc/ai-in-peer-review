- Paper proposes cross-lingual NER via projection from a resource-rich language, using CRF, MEMM, and a neural model with two ensemble methods, data selection, and a variant of Mikolov-style embedding projection.  
- Strength: Ensemble methods (probability- and ranking-based) are well-motivated and outperform single models.  
- Strength: Data selection scheme effectively filters noisy projected labels and improves transfer quality.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written and experimental setup is clear.  
- Major: Insufficient justification and no ablation for modified CBOW and frequency-weighted projection; need direct comparisons with standard CBOW and original Mikolov projection.  
- Major: Claims of significance require statistical significance testing (e.g., paired t-test or bootstrap) to substantiate improvements.  
- Major: Results analysis is shallow; needs error analysis (which entity types fail, behavior of ensemble methods across entity types and noise levels).  
- Minor: Move background content into a dedicated Related Work section for better structure and fuller literature coverage.  
- Minor: Reorganize paper (e.g., dataset description in Section 4.2 and consolidate Table 2 discussion within main evaluation).  
- Minor: Compare the CBOW decay factor to related techniques (e.g., exponential decay) and cite relevant work.  
- Overall: Solid paper with promising results, but strengthened by ablations, significance tests, and deeper analysis.
