This paper introduces a set of weakly supervised techniques for developing Named Entity Recognition (NER) systems, which are based on projecting annotations from English to a target language. The general framework is well-established in the literature, and the specific methods presented appear to be incremental extensions of prior work. The level of novelty might be insufficient for a premier venue, where more foundational contributions are typically expected.

One of the paper's highlighted contributions is its data selection scheme. The quality scoring formula is commendably simple, which is a positive attribute. However, the methodology for selecting the thresholds presented in Table 2 remains ambiguous. The authors state only that various thresholds were explored, which lacks sufficient detail. It is crucial to clarify whether a development set was used for this hyperparameter tuning, as no such set is mentioned in the paper. While the evaluation effectively demonstrates the importance of data selection, the lack of a clear procedure for tuning these parameters could hinder the method's applicability to new datasets or language pairs.

A further contribution is the method for combining the outputs of the two developed systems. Despite careful reading, I found the description of this combination process to be unclear and difficult to follow.

The authors present several variants for each of their proposed methods. Given the demonstrated benefit of combining two systems, a natural question arises: have the authors explored ensembling more than two of these weakly supervised systems? This seems like a logical next step that was not discussed.

Finally, for reproducibility and a better understanding of the experimental context, additional details regarding the "in-house" dataset are needed, specifically concerning the domain and types of texts it contains.
