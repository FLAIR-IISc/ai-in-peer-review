- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods—annotation projection (project English labels onto comparable corpora) and representation projection (map target embeddings to English space)—plus two co-decoding schemes; achieves SOTA on CoNLL.

- Contribution 1: A heuristic data selection method for annotation projection that filters noisy projected labels and yields large F1 improvements (e.g., +20.0 Korean, +13.9 Japanese).

- Contribution 2: A comprehensive empirical comparison of annotation vs. representation projection and effective co-decoding schemes that combine them to outperform previous SOTA on CoNLL.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results, setting new SOTA on CoNLL for Spanish, Dutch, and German.

- Strength 2: Clear demonstration that the data selection heuristic substantially improves performance for languages with poor alignments.

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation across a fine-grained in-house dataset and the CoNLL benchmark, covering seven target languages.

- Weakness 1: Unclear how data selection thresholds (quality score q and entity number n) were chosen, raising overfitting/reproducibility concerns.

- Weakness 2: Limited methodological novelty since annotation projection and cross-lingual embeddings are established techniques; contributions are mainly incremental/engineering.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient detail on the co-decoding (rank-based) scheme and conflict resolution, making reimplementation difficult.

- Question 1: How were the thresholds q and n selected for each language—was a held-out development set used?

- Question 2: What are the domain and genre of the texts in the in-house dataset?

- Question 3: Did you try combining more than two systems (e.g., ensembling annotation projection with both NN1 and NN2 variants)?
