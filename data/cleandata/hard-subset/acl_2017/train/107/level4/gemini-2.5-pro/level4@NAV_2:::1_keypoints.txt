- Paper studies weakly supervised cross-lingual NER, transferring from English via annotation projection and representation projection, plus co-decoding to combine them.
- Contribution: Strong combined system achieving state-of-the-art on CoNLL cross-lingual NER, offering a new baseline.
- Contribution: Effective heuristic data-selection scheme for annotation projection that improves performance, especially with poor word alignments.
- Contribution: Thorough evaluation across multiple languages and on both a standard 4-class benchmark and a large, fine-grained in-house dataset.
- Strength: Final combined model outperforms recent strong baselines on CoNLL, demonstrating robust empirical gains.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on eight language pairs and on a >50-entity in-house dataset shows generalization beyond standard NER.
- Strength: Data selection scheme is well-motivated and yields large F1 improvements for languages with low-quality alignments (e.g., Japanese, Korean).
- Strength: Co-decoding of label-space and representation-space approaches is principled and consistently improves over individual systems.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—techniques are established paradigms; main value is in engineering, combination, and evaluation.
- Weakness: Missing details on hyperparameter tuning for data-selection thresholds (q, n), raising reproducibility and unsupervised-use concerns.
- Weakness: Insufficient description of the in-house dataset (domain, size, creation), making results hard to contextualize.
- Question: How were the q and n thresholds chosen—using labeled dev data or another procedure—and how should they be tuned for truly unsupervised target languages?
- Question: Please provide detailed descriptions or pseudo-code for the "exclude-O confidence-based" and "rank-based" co-decoding algorithms.
- Question: Please provide more information about the in-house NER dataset (text domain, size, other characteristics).
- Question: Have you tried combining more than two models (e.g., Annotation Projection + NN1 + NN2) to test further gains?
