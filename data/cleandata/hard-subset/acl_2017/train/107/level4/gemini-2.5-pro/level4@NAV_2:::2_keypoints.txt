- Proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approach requiring no human-annotated target-language data.  
- Introduces annotation projection with a novel heuristic to select high-quality training instances from noisy comparable corpora.  
- Proposes representation projection that maps target-language word embeddings into a source-language space for direct model transfer.  
- Presents two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs effectively.  
- Main contribution: a robust combined system integrating both projection methods and co-decoding, achieving state-of-the-art on benchmarks.  
- Main contribution: an effective data-selection heuristic that dramatically improves annotation projection, especially with poor alignments.  
- Main contribution: demonstration of representation projection enabling a universal NER model applicable to new languages without retraining.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance and comprehensive evaluation on CoNLL and larger in-house datasets with fine-grained entities.  
- Strength: effective combination of complementary methods—annotation projection yields high precision, representation projection balances precision/recall.  
- Strength: practical, well-motivated data-selection heuristic with large F1 gains for languages with weak alignments (e.g., Japanese, Korean).  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and reproducible method descriptions.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification and ablation for methodological novelties (CBOW variant and weighted cross-lingual mapping).  
- Weakness: evaluation lacks deeper analysis and error/qualitative analysis explaining co-decoding behavior and remaining challenges.  
- Weakness: absence of statistical significance testing to validate claimed improvements.  
- Question: please motivate the CBOW variant and weighted mapping and provide comparisons against standard CBOW/Skip-gram and unweighted Mikolov mapping.  
- Question: have statistical significance tests been conducted for the reported F1 gains (Table 2) and improvements over prior work (Table 5)?
