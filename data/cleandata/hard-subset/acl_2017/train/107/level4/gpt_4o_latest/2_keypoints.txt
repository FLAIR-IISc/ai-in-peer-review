- Paper presents a cross-lingual NER model combining CRF, MEMM, and neural approaches with two fusion methods (probability-based and ranking-based) and a training-instance selection technique using comparable corpora.
- Cross-lingual projection implemented via a modified version of Mikolov’s method (frequency-weighted projection).
- Paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and written in clear English.
- Combined annotations produce noteworthy (state-of-the-art) results.
- Reviewer requests motivation and detailed description of the proposed CBOW variation, including parameters and whether original CBOW/skip-gram underperformed.
- Recommend reporting results of the original CBOW (or skip-gram) to quantify improvements from the proposed variation.
- Suggest considering exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (reference Iacobacci et al., 2016) given the use of a decay factor.
- Ask for a direct comparison between original Mikolov cross-lingual projection and the proposed frequency-weighted projection to demonstrate superiority.
- Question whether differences reported (e.g., gains from data selection) are statistically significant and request statistical significance testing.
- Recommend reorganizing the paper: integrate Section 4.4 into early Section 4.2 and move Table 2’s evaluation to the evaluation section.
- Note absence of a dedicated related work section and suggest splitting the introduction into background and related work sections.
- Call for a more detailed evaluation section and deeper discussion/analysis of results given the claimed state-of-the-art performance.
- Suggested reference: Iacobacci, Pilehvar & Navigli (2016) on embeddings for word sense disambiguation.
