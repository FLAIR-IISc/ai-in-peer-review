This research paper describes a model for Cross-Lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER).

The authors use conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov, and neural network-based NER methods. Additionally, the authors propose two methods (probability-based and ranking-based) to combine the outputs of these methods and a method to select the best training examples from cross-lingual comparable corpora. The cross-lingual projection is done using a type of Mikulov's proposal. Generally, this paper is easy to understand, well-structured, and the English quality is also good. The results of the combined annotations are interesting.

Detailed Comments:
I was wondering what the motivation is behind proposing a Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model variation. You haven't provided much information about it (or the planned parameters). Was the original model (or Continuous Skip-Gram model) producing fewer results? I suggest including the results with the CBOW model as well, so that readers can analyze the improvements in your approach. Since you use a decay factor for the surrounding embeddings, I suggest taking a look at the exponential decay used in [1].

Similar to the previous comment, I would like to see the difference between the original Mikulov's cross-lingual projection and your frequency-weighted projection. These contributions are more valuable if readers can see that your method is actually better.

"The proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good quality projection-labeled data, and the improvement is significant" ←

Have you tested for statistical significance? I would like to know if the difference between the results in this work is significant.

I suggest integrating the text of section 4.4 at the beginning of section 4.2. This would be clearer. I also recommend moving the evaluation of Table 2 to the evaluation section.

I miss a related work section. Your introduction includes a part of that information. I suggest dividing the introduction into two parts. The evaluation is quite short (1.5 pages with the conclusion section). You achieve state-of-the-art results, and I would appreciate more discussion and analysis of the results.
