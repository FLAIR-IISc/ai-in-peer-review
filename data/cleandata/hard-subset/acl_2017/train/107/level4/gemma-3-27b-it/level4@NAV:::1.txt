This paper explores weakly supervised Named Entity Recognition (NER) techniques, leveraging cross-lingual projection from English. While the overarching strategy isn't novel, the work details incremental improvements upon existing methodologies. Given the standards for an ACL publication, I anticipated a greater degree of innovation in the core approach.

A key contribution lies in the proposed data selection scheme. The quality scoring formula is appropriately simple and direct. However, the report lacks detail regarding the threshold determination process presented in Table 2; the paper only states that various thresholds were tested. It remains unclear if a development set was used for this tuning, as this isn't explicitly stated. The results convincingly demonstrate the importance of data selection, but offer limited guidance on parameter tuning for novel datasets or language pairs.

The combination of the two developed systems constitutes another contribution. Despite careful review, the explanation of this combination process remains unclear and requires further elaboration. 

The paper investigates numerous variants for each proposed method. It would be valuable to consider the potential benefits of combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and whether the authors explored this possibility.

Finally, further characterization of the “in-house” dataset—specifically, the types of texts it contains—would be beneficial for understanding the scope of the evaluation.
