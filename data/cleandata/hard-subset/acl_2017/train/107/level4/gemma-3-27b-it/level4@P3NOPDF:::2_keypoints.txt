- Presents a cross-lingual NER approach combining CRFs, MEMMs, and neural models with two fusion methods (probability- and ranking-based) and instance selection from comparable corpora.  
- Uses a modified Mikolov projection technique and reports promising, state-of-the-art combined-annotation results.  
- Strength: tackles an important cross-lingual NLP problem and proposes valuable model combination and data-selection methods with a sound experimental setup.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and achieves strong empirical results.  
- Weakness: motivation and details for the CBOW variation are unclear; need parameter descriptions and comparisons to original CBOW/Skip-gram.  
- Weakness: frequency-weighted projection requires direct comparison to the original method and quantitative evidence that weighting improves performance.  
- Weakness: claims of significant improvement lack statistical significance testing and p-values to substantiate them.  
- Weakness: Section 4.4 is poorly placed and should be integrated into Section 4.2; Table 2’s analysis should be moved to a dedicated evaluation section.  
- Weakness: lacks a dedicated Related Work section and should more thoroughly review literature on cross-lingual NER, data selection, and embedding projection.  
- Weakness: evaluation and discussion are too brief; needs deeper analysis, error analysis, and discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Minor comment: consider citing Iacobacci et al. (2016) on decay factors in embedding weighting.  
- Overall: solid contribution deserving acceptance conditional on addressing statistical testing, comparisons to baselines, clearer method descriptions, and an expanded discussion.
