This paper describes a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER). The authors employ conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov, and neural network-based NER methods. In addition, they propose two methods to combine the output of those methods (probability-based and ranking-based), and a method to select the best training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. The cross-lingual projection is done using a variant of Mikolov's proposal. In general, the paper is easy to follow, well-structured, and the English quality is good. The results of the combined systems are promising.

Detailed comments:
I am curious about the motivation behind proposing a Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model variation. The paper doesn't provide much detail about this (or the parameters used). Was the original model (or the Continuous Skip-gram model) yielding unsatisfactory results? I suggest including the results with the standard CBOW model, allowing readers to analyze the improvements achieved by your approach. 
Since you employ a decay factor for the surrounding embeddings, I recommend considering the exponential decay used in [1].
Similarly, I would like to see a more direct comparison between the original Mikolov's cross-lingual projections and your frequency-weighted projections. These contributions are more convincing if readers can see that your method demonstrably outperforms the original.
The paper states that "the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant." Have you conducted a statistical significance test to support this claim? I would appreciate knowing if the observed differences are statistically significant.
I suggest integrating the text of Section 4.4 at the beginning of Section 4.2 for improved flow. I also recommend moving the evaluation presented in Table 2 to the experimental results section.
The paper lacks a dedicated related work section. While the introduction touches on relevant work, a separate section would provide a more comprehensive overview. I suggest dividing the introduction into two sections to accommodate this.
The evaluation section is relatively concise (1.5 pages including the conclusion). Given that you achieve state-of-the-art results, I would appreciate a more in-depth discussion and analysis of these results.

Suggested references:
