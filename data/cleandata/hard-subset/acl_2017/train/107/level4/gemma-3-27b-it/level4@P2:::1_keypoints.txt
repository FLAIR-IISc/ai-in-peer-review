- Paper studies weakly supervised cross-lingual NER using projection from English and proposes refinements (data selection, co-decoding) but novelty may be limited for ACL.
- Main contributions: a frequency-based data selection scheme for annotation projection and a co-decoding strategy to merge different projection outputs.
- Data selection scheme is simple and sensible, and demonstrably improves performance, especially with low alignment accuracy.
- The paper lacks clarity on parameter tuning for data selection (how thresholds in Table 2 were determined), hurting reproducibility and practical use.
- Co-decoding (particularly rank-based) consistently improves performance by combining complementary strengths of methods.
- Description of the co-decoding algorithm is unclear and would benefit from more detail or pseudocode.
- The paper does not explore combining more than two weakly supervised systems, which is a missed opportunity.
- Evaluation on CoNLL and in-house datasets shows competitive improvements, notably for German.
- Insufficient information about the in-house datasets (text type, domain, size) limits interpretability of results.
- Strength: data selection yields significant F1 gains by filtering noisy projections, practical for real-world scenarios.
- Strength: co-decoding effectively integrates projection methods, improving precision/recall balance.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation across standard and in-house datasets with comparisons to state-of-the-art.
- Weakness: core approach is incremental over existing projection techniques and may lack sufficient novelty for a top-tier venue.
- Weakness: missing methodological details for threshold tuning and co-decoding impede reproducibility.
- Questions for authors: provide details on development set/parameter tuning, detailed co-decoding description/pseudocode, whether more than two systems were combined, and characteristics of the in-house datasets.
