- Presents a cross-lingual NER model comparing CRF, MEMM, and neural approaches.  
- Proposes two annotation-combination methods: probability-based and ranking-based.  
- Introduces a method for selecting the most informative training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora.  
- Performs cross-lingual projection using a modified version of Mikolov’s technique.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and in good English.  
- Combining annotations yields particularly noteworthy (state-of-the-art) results.  
- Reviewer questions the motivation and parameter choices for the modified CBOW and asks for more explanation.  
- Requests results using the standard CBOW (and possibly skip-gram) to quantify improvements from the variant.  
- Suggests considering an exponential decay method for surrounding embeddings (Iacobacci et al. 2016).  
- Asks for a detailed comparison between the frequency-weighted projections and the original Mikolov projections, including a direct comparison.  
- Requests statistical significance testing to support claims about the data selection scheme’s effectiveness.  
- Recommends reorganizing Sections 4.4 and 4.2 and moving Table 2’s evaluation to the main evaluation section to improve flow.  
- Notes the absence of a dedicated related work section and suggests splitting the introduction into background and contributions.  
- Finds the evaluation section too brief and requests more in-depth discussion and analysis given the strong results.  
- Provides a suggested reference: Iacobacci et al. (2016).
