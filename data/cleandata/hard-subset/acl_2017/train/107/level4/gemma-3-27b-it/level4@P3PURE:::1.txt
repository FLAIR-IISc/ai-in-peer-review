This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for Named Entity Recognition (NER), relying on projection from English into another language. While the overall approach builds upon existing work, and the individual methods represent incremental improvements, I found myself expecting a higher degree of novelty for a paper presented at ACL.

A key contribution of this work is the data selection scheme. The quality score formula is commendably straightforward. However, the methodology for determining the thresholds used in Table 2 remains unclear. The paper states that various thresholds were tested, but it is not specified whether this optimization was performed using a development set – a detail absent from the text. The evaluation results convincingly demonstrate the importance of data selection; however, the paper lacks guidance on how to effectively tune the parameters for new datasets or language pairs. 

Another contribution lies in the combination of the outputs from the two systems developed. Despite careful review, the description of this combination process remains unclear and requires further clarification. 

The paper explores numerous variants for each proposed method. It would be valuable to know whether combining the outputs of more than two weakly supervised systems was considered. Did the authors investigate the potential benefits – or drawbacks – of expanding the ensemble beyond a pair of systems?

Finally, a more detailed description of the “in-house” dataset, including the types of texts it comprises, would be beneficial for assessing the generalizability of the results.
