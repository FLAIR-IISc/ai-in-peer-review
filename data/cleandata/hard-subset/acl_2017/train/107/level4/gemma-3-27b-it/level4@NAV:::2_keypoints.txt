- Paper presents a cross-lingual NER system combining CRF, MEMM, and neural NER techniques with two output-combination methods (probability- and ranking-based).  
- Authors introduce a method for selecting optimal training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora.  
- Cross-lingual projection uses a modified Mikolov embedding approach.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and demonstrates good English.  
- Combined annotation methods yield particularly noteworthy (state-of-the-art) results.  
- Motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variation are insufficient; parameters and rationale vs. original/skip-gram models should be provided.  
- Report CBOW performance figures and consider exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (Iacobacci et al. suggestion).  
- Provide a thorough comparison between original Mikolov cross-lingual projections and the frequency-weighted projections used here.  
- Claims about the effectiveness of the data selection scheme require statistical significance testing to substantiate improvements.  
- Reorganize sections: integrate Section 4.4 into 4.2 and move Table 2 evaluation to the evaluation section for better flow.  
- Add a dedicated related work section (split the introduction into background/related-work and contributions).  
- Expand the evaluation section with deeper discussion and analysis given the strong experimental results.
