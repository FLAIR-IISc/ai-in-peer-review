- Presents weakly supervised NER via projection from English with incremental improvements and thorough multilingual evaluation.  
- Primary contribution: interpretable data selection scheme for annotation projection; lacks detail on threshold (q, n) selection and tuning guidance.  
- Secondary contribution: co-decoding to combine systems; rank-based scheme outperforms confidence-based but is described unclearly.  
- Unclear how conflicting entities are resolved within the rank-based co-decoding scheme.  
- Many method variants are explored; raises whether combining more than two systems could yield additional gains.  
- Strong, broad evaluation across multiple languages (Japanese, Korean, German, Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch) using in-house and CoNLL datasets.  
- Data selection scheme is practically valuable, especially for low-resource or noisy scenarios.  
- Co-decoding demonstrates potential to improve performance by leveraging complementary system strengths.  
- Limited novelty: approach builds on existing English projection methods and is mostly a set of refinements.  
- Insufficient rigor and reproducibility in threshold tuning—statement that “different thresholds were tried” is inadequate.  
- Lack of clarity and detail in describing the rank-based co-decoding, particularly conflict resolution mechanics.  
- Paper focuses on combining two systems; exploration of combining more systems is missing.  
- Ask authors to explain how thresholds (q, n) were chosen and which metric/dev set was used.  
- Ask authors to provide a clearer, concrete explanation of the rank-based co-decoding and conflict handling.  
- Ask whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems was tried and what challenges/benefits were observed.  
- Recommend adding a table summarizing in-house dataset characteristics (domain, size, entity distribution).  
- Recommend discussing computational costs, especially of representation projection.  
- Note citation style inconsistency; ensure adherence to ACL formatting guidelines.
