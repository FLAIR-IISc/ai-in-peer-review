This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for Named Entity Recognition (NER). The methods leverage projection from English into another language. While the overall approach isn’t novel, and the individual methods represent improvements on existing techniques, I expected to see more groundbreaking work for an ACL publication.

One key contribution of the paper is the data selection scheme. The formula used to calculate the quality score is commendably straightforward. However, the paper lacks clarity regarding how the thresholds presented in Table 2 were determined. It states that different thresholds were tested, but it’s unclear whether this was performed on a development set—a detail not mentioned in the text. The evaluation results demonstrably highlight the importance of data selection; however, the paper doesn’t offer sufficient guidance on how to tune the parameters when applying this approach to a new dataset or language pair. 

Another contribution lies in the combination of the outputs from the two systems developed. I found the description of this process difficult to follow, and greater clarity would be beneficial.

The paper explores a number of variants for each proposed method. This raises the question of whether combining more than two weakly supervised systems would be advantageous. Did the authors investigate this possibility?

Finally, it would be helpful to have a more detailed characterization of the “in-house” dataset, including information about the types of texts it contains.
