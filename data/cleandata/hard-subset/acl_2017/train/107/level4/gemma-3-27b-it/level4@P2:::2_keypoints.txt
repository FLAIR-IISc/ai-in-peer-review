- Proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER system combining annotation projection, representation projection, and co-decoding to avoid target-language annotation.  
- Introduces a heuristic data selection scheme to improve the quality of projected annotations.  
- Uses weighted cross-lingual word embeddings with a CBOW variation for representation projection.  
- Proposes co-decoding strategies to combine outputs of annotation- and representation-based approaches.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Strong empirical improvements over prior cross-lingual NER methods on in-house and CoNLL datasets, especially for languages with low alignment accuracy.  
- Practical solution for low-resource languages using comparable corpora and pre-trained embeddings.  
- Thoughtful, comprehensive combination of complementary techniques yields robust performance.  
- Individual components (annotation projection, embedding projection) are not highly novel; contribution is mainly in their effective combination.  
- Lacks detailed comparison showing why the proposed data selection outperforms simpler baselines.  
- Insufficient explanation and experimental comparison of the specific CBOW variation versus standard CBOW and Skip-gram.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for the claimed performance gains.  
- Limited error analysis; deeper investigation into error types and failure cases is missing.  
- Ask authors to compare the data selection scheme to simpler alternatives (random sampling, highest-alignment).  
- Ask for CBOW variation hyperparameters and performance comparisons to standard embedding models.  
- Ask for statistical significance tests (p-values or confidence intervals) for reported improvements.  
- Ask for a brief error analysis identifying common system errors and mitigation strategies.  
- Suggest integrating Section 4.4 into 4.2 for better flow.  
- Recommend expanding the evaluation with more in-depth discussion and comparisons to related approaches.  
- Suggest adding a dedicated Related Work section for broader context.  
- Recommend citing Iacobacci et al. (2016) as a relevant reference.
