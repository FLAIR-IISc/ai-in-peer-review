- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER model combining CRF, MEMM, neural methods, plus two novel combination strategies (probability-based, ranking-based) and a data selection method using comparable corpora.  
- Cross-lingual projection uses a modified (frequency-weighted) version of Mikolov’s embedding alignment.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and achieves notable results with combined annotation strategies.  
- Motivation for the proposed CBOW variation is insufficiently detailed and needs clearer rationale.  
- The paper should report the CBOW model’s performance (and clarify whether standard CBOW or Skip-gram underperformed) for direct comparison.  
- If using a decay factor for surrounding embeddings, related methods (e.g., exponential decay in Iacobacci et al. 2016) should be discussed and compared.  
- A more detailed comparison (quantitative and qualitative) between original Mikolov projection and the proposed frequency-weighted projection is needed to demonstrate added value.  
- The claim that the data selection scheme is “very effective” should be supported by statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values).  
- Organization suggestion: integrate Section 4.4 into the beginning of Section 4.2 to improve flow.  
- Move the evaluation of Table 2 to a dedicated evaluation section for clearer presentation.  
- A dedicated related work section is missing and should be added to contextualize the contribution.  
- Consider restructuring the introduction into background/motivation and a separate related work section.  
- The evaluation section is too brief given the strong results; more in-depth analysis and discussion are expected.  
- Evaluation should explore strengths and limitations, potential error sources, and the impact of different components.  
- Suggested reference to consider: Iacobacci et al. (2016) on embedding decay methods.
