- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER system combining CRF, MEMM, neural NER, two combination methods (probability- and ranking-based), and a training-instance selection method from comparable corpora; cross-lingual projection uses a variant of Mikolov's approach.
- Primary contribution: comprehensive study of weakly supervised cross-lingual NER with novel co-decoding strategies.
- Secondary contributions: heuristic data selection for annotation projection that improves performance with noisy alignments, and frequency-weighted cross-lingual embedding mappings as a practical adaptation.

Strengths:
- Well-integrated system combining annotation projection, representation projection, and co-decoding to avoid expensive human annotation.
- Heuristic data selection demonstrably improves performance under noisy alignment conditions (results shown in Table 2).
- Strong empirical results, outperforming prior state-of-the-art on CoNLL datasets (Table 5).
- Clear, well-structured writing and correct English.

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient justification and experimental comparison for the proposed CBOW variant versus standard CBOW or Skip-gram.
- Lack of quantitative comparison between the frequency-weighted embedding mappings and Mikolov’s original projections.
- No statistical significance testing provided to support claims of “significant” improvements.
- Poor organization: related work (Section 4.4) is misplaced within methodology and some experimental material is in the wrong section.
- Evaluation is brief; limited error analysis and discussion of future directions.

Questions to authors:
- Please justify the choice of the CBOW variant and provide comparisons with standard CBOW and Skip-gram.
- Can you present quantitative results comparing frequency-weighted mappings to Mikolov’s original projections?
- Have you performed statistical significance tests (p-values/confidence intervals) for the reported improvements, especially for data selection?
- What errors does the combined system still make and what strategies could address them?
