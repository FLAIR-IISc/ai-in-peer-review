- Paper proposes a cross-lingual NER system combining CRF, maximum entropy Markov, and neural methods.  
- Authors introduce two ensemble combination techniques: probability-based and ranking-based.  
- A method is proposed to select high-quality training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora.  
- Cross-lingual projection uses a variant of Mikolov’s word embedding alignment (frequency-weighted projection).  
- Paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and English quality is good.  
- Combined-system results are promising and approach state-of-the-art.  
- Reviewer questions the motivation and details for the proposed CBOW variation and requests parameter/settings.  
- Reviewer suggests comparing results with the standard CBOW (and/or Skip-gram) to show improvement.  
- Suggestion to consider exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (as in Iacobacci et al.).  
- Reviewer requests a direct comparison between original Mikolov projections and the proposed frequency-weighted projections.  
- Reviewer asks whether improvements from the data selection scheme are supported by statistical significance tests.  
- Recommendation to move Section 4.4 to the start of Section 4.2 and relocate Table 2 to the experimental results for better flow.  
- Paper lacks a dedicated related work section; reviewer suggests splitting the introduction to include it.  
- Evaluation section is brief; reviewer requests more in-depth discussion and analysis of the (state-of-the-art) results.  
- Reviewer recommends citing Iacobacci et al. (2016) regarding embedding decay and evaluation.
