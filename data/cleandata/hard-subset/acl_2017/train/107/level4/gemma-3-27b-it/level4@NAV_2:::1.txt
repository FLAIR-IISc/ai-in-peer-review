This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for developing NER systems. The methods rely on some form of projection from English into another language. The overall approach is not novel, building upon existing techniques, and for a competitive conference paper, I would have expected more substantial innovation.

The primary contribution of this work is the heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection. The quality score formula is straightforward, which is positive, but the method for determining the thresholds (q and n) in Table 2 lacks detail. The paper states that different thresholds were tried, but it is unclear if this was done on a development set; this is not mentioned. While the evaluation results demonstrate the importance of data selection, the paper does not provide guidance on how to effectively tune these parameters for new datasets or language pairs.

A second contribution is the exploration of co-decoding schemes to combine the outputs of the two proposed systems. However, the description of these schemes is not sufficiently clear for full understanding. 

The paper evaluates numerous variants of each method. It would be valuable to know if combining more than two weakly supervised systems was considered. Were any investigations performed in that direction?

Finally, more information about the composition of the “in-house” dataset would be beneficial for assessing the generalizability of the results.
