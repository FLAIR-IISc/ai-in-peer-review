This paper explores weakly supervised methods for Named Entity Recognition (NER), leveraging cross-lingual projection from English. While the general approach of projection is established, the paper proposes incremental improvements to existing techniques. For a conference like ACL, I anticipated a presentation of more novel approaches.

A key contribution lies in the proposed data selection scheme. The quality score formula is commendably simple; however, the methodology for determining the thresholds used in Table 2 remains opaque. The paper states only that various thresholds were tested, without specifying whether this was performed on a development set – a detail absent from the text. The evaluation results demonstrate the significant impact of data selection, but offer limited guidance on how to optimally tune the associated parameters for new datasets or language pairs. 

The combination of the two developed systems is another noted contribution. Despite careful reading, the description of this combination method lacks clarity.

The paper investigates multiple variants for each proposed method. It remains unclear whether combining the outputs of more than two weakly supervised systems would be beneficial, and whether the authors explored this possibility.

Finally, further detail regarding the characteristics of the “in-house” dataset would be helpful for understanding the results.
