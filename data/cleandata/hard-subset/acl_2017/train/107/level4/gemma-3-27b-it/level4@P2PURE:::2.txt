This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER), exploring conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural network-based NER approaches. The authors further propose two methods for combining the outputs of these systems—probability-based and ranking-based—along with a method for selecting the most informative training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is performed using a modified version of Mikolov’s technique. Overall, the paper is clearly written, well-structured, and demonstrates good English quality. The results achieved by combining annotations are particularly noteworthy.

Detailed comments:

I am curious about the motivation for employing a variation of the Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model. The paper lacks detailed explanation regarding this choice (and the specific parameters used). Were lower results obtained with the original CBOW or Continuous Skip-gram models? I recommend including the results obtained with the standard CBOW model to allow readers to assess the improvements offered by your approach.

Given the use of a decay factor for surrounding embeddings, I suggest considering the exponential decay method presented in [1]. 

Similarly, I would appreciate a more detailed comparison between your frequency-weighted projections and the original cross-lingual projections proposed by Mikolov. Demonstrating the superiority of your method with a direct comparison would strengthen its contribution.

The statement “the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant” would be stronger with the inclusion of a statistical significance test. Please provide information regarding the statistical significance of the observed improvements.

To improve the paper’s flow, I suggest integrating the text of Section 4.4 at the beginning of Section 4.2. I also recommend moving the evaluation of Table 2 to the dedicated evaluation section.

A dedicated related work section is missing. While the introduction touches on relevant work, I suggest dividing the introduction into two sections to clearly delineate background information and the paper’s contributions.

The evaluation section is relatively brief (1.5 pages including the conclusion). Given the state-of-the-art results achieved, I would appreciate a more in-depth discussion and analysis of the findings. 

Suggested references:

[1] Iacobacci, I., Pilehvar, M. T., & Navigli, R. (2016). Embeddings for word sense disambiguation: An evaluation study. In Proceedings of the 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (Vol. 1, pp. 897-907).
