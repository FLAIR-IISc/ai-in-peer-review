This paper presents several weak supervised methods for developing NER (natural language understanding). These methods rely on some form of translation from English to another language. The overall approach is not new, and the proposed individual methods are improvements to existing methods. For an ACL paper, I expected more innovative approaches.

One of the paper's contributions is the data selection scheme. The formula used to calculate the quality score is quite straightforward, which is not a bad thing. However, it is unclear how the threshold for Table 2 was calculated. The paper only states that different thresholds were tried. Was this done on a development set? The paper makes no mention of this. The evaluation results clearly show that data selection is very important, but one would not know how to adjust the parameters for a new dataset or a new language pair.

Another contribution of the paper is the combination of the outputs of the two systems developed in the paper. I tried my best to understand how this works, but the provided description is unclear.

The paper presents several variants for each method proposed. Does it make sense to combine more than two weak supervised systems? Did the authors try to do something in this direction?

It would be good to know a little more about the types of texts present in the "in-house" dataset.
