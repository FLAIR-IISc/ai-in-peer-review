This paper describes a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER).

The authors use conditional random field, maximum entropy Markov, and neural network-based NER methods. Additionally, the authors propose two methods (probability-based and ranking-based) to combine the outputs of these methods and a method to select the best training examples from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is performed using a variant of Mikulov's proposal. Generally, this paper is easy to understand, well-structured, and the English quality is also good. The results of the combined annotations are interesting.

Detailed comments:
I was wondering what the motivation is behind proposing a Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model variation. You haven't provided much information about it (or the planned parameters). Was the original model (or Continuous Skip-Gram model) producing fewer results? I would suggest including the results with the CBOW model as well, so readers can analyze the improvement in your approach. Since you use a decay factor for the surrounding embedding, I suggest taking a look at the exponential decay used in [1].

Similar to the previous comment, I would like to see the difference between the original Mikulov's cross-lingual projection and your frequency-weighted projection. These contributions are more valuable if the reader can see that your method is actually better.

"The proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good quality projection-labeled data, and the improvement is significant" ←

Have you tested for statistical significance? I would like to know if the difference between the results in this work is significant.

I suggest integrating the text of section 4.4 at the beginning of section 4.2. This will be clearer. I also recommend moving the evaluation of Table 2 to the evaluation section.

I remember a related work section. Your introduction includes a part of that information. I suggest dividing the introduction into two parts. The evaluation is quite short (1.5 pages with the conclusion section). You achieve state-of-the-art results, and I would appreciate more discussion and analysis of the results.
