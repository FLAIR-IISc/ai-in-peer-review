This paper presents several weak supervised methods for creating NER. These methods rely on some kind of projection from English to another language. The overall method is not new, and the proposed individual methods are improvements to existing methods. I expected more innovative methods for an ACL paper.

One contribution of this paper is the data selection method. The formula used to calculate the quality score is quite simple and not bad. However, it is not clear how the threshold in Table 2 was calculated. The paper only mentions trying different thresholds. Was this done in a development set? There is no mention of this in the paper. The evaluation results clearly show that data selection is very important, but it may not be known how to tune the parameters for a new data set or to add a new language.

Another contribution of this paper is the combination of the outputs of the two systems created in the paper. I have tried hard to understand how this works, but the given details are not clear.

The paper presents several variations for each of the proposed methods. Is it meaningful to combine more than two weak supervised systems? Have the authors tried anything in this direction?

It would be good to know a little more about what kind of text is in the "in-house" dataset.
