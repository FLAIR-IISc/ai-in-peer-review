- Paper presents cross-lingual NER using CRFs, MEMMs, and neural NER, with two fusion strategies (confidence- and rank-based) and a heuristic for selecting high-quality projection-labeled training data.
- Cross-lingual transfer uses a variant of Mikolov’s embedding projection method (frequency-weighted mapping).
- Paper is readable, well organized, and yields interesting results from combined annotations.
- Reviewer asks for motivation and more details (including hyperparameters) for the modified CBOW model and requests results with the unmodified CBOW or skip-gram baseline to assess gains.
- Suggests considering exponential distance-based decay for context (references Iacobacci et al., 2016) when using distance-based decay.
- Requests a direct comparison between Mikolov’s original cross-lingual projection and the proposed frequency-weighted mapping to demonstrate improvement.
- Asks whether observed improvements (e.g., from the data selection scheme) have been tested for statistical significance and requests significance testing.
- Recommends moving Section 4.4 to the start of Section 4.2 and relocating the evaluation corresponding to Table 2 into the main evaluation section for better flow.
- Notes the absence of a dedicated related work section and suggests splitting the introduction to separate background/positioning from contributions.
- Finds the evaluation brief (~1.5 pages) and requests a deeper discussion and analysis given the state-of-the-art results.
- Provides a suggested reference (Iacobacci et al., 2016) relevant to embedding decay/context weighting.
