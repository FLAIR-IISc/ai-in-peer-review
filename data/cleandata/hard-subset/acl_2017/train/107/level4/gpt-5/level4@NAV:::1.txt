The manuscript introduces multiple weakly supervised approaches for building NER systems. These techniques hinge on projecting from English into another language. The overarching paradigm is not new, and the specific techniques are largely refinements of existing methods. For an ACL venue, I would have expected a higher degree of novelty.

A key contribution is the data selection procedure. The formula used to compute the quality score is fairly simple, which is not problematic. However, it is unclear how the thresholds in Table 2 were chosen. The paper only notes that various thresholds were tried. Was this tuning done on a development set? The manuscript does not say. The results clearly indicate that data selection matters a great deal, but it is not evident how to set the parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another contribution is the strategy for combining the outputs of the two systems developed in the paper. I made a concerted effort to understand the mechanism, but the description is not sufficiently clear.

The paper presents several variants for each proposed method. Would it make sense to combine more than two weakly supervised systems? Did the authors attempt anything along these lines?

It would be helpful to have more detail about the types of texts included in the "in-house" dataset.
