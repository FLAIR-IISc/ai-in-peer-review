- Paper studies weakly supervised cross-lingual NER without target annotations, combining annotation projection and representation projection with co-decoding.  
- Contribution: simple frequency-based selection heuristic improves annotation projection under noisy alignments.  
- Contribution: representation-projection pipeline using small bilingual dictionary enables competitive NER without target training.  
- Contribution: lightweight co-decoding that consistently improves over individual systems and prior weakly supervised methods.  

- Strength: strong empirical gains across languages, notably large F1 improvements for Japanese and Korean.  
- Strength: complementary error profiles (AP: high precision/low recall; RP: balanced), motivating the co-decoding design.  
- Strength: practical, low-resource approach requiring only parallel/comparable corpora and a modest bilingual dictionary.  
- Strength: broad evaluation across in-house fine-grained and CoNLL datasets, multiple languages and models.  

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—main ideas extend existing projection and embedding-mapping techniques.  
- Weakness: unclear how data-selection thresholds (q, n) were tuned without target labels and sensitivity/transferability across languages.  
- Weakness: co-decoding under-specified—missing details on conflict resolution, IOB consistency, and score calibration.  
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps in resource descriptions (in-house data, corpora, alignments, label inventories).  
- Weakness: comparisons and ablations incomplete (other mapping methods, varied dictionary sizes, combining more systems).  

- Question: how were thresholds q and n selected per language; was any labeled dev data used and what is sensitivity of F1 to these?  
- Question: provide pseudocode for co-decoding—how are overlapping spans, type disagreements, and IOB validity handled, and is calibration applied?  
- Question: did you try combining more than two systems or ensembling multiple dictionaries/mappings, and what were the results?  
- Question: what are domains, sizes, and label schema details for the in-house datasets, and will data/models be released?  
- Question: how sensitive is representation projection to dictionary size, embedding dimensionality, and mapping choices (orthogonality, weighting)?  
- Question: how is the “NER-trained unk vector” obtained, its impact on OOVs, and what fraction of tokens/entities use it at test time?  

- Additional: include complexity/runtime/memory discussion for models and co-decoding.  
- Additional: clarify whether sentence pairs are parallel/comparable, how alignments are obtained (sentence vs word), and whether selection needs word-level alignments.  

- Overall: solid empirical paper with practical value; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying threshold tuning and co-decoding, improving reproducibility, and adding ablations.
