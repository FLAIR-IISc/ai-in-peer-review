This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for building NER systems by projecting from English into other languages. The overall approach is not new, and the proposed methods appear to be incremental improvements on existing techniques. For an ACL paper, I would have expected more substantial novelty.

One contribution is the data selection scheme. The quality-score formula is straightforward, which is fine, but the procedure for setting the thresholds in Table 2 is unclear. The paper states only that different thresholds were tried. Were these tuned on a development set? If so, please describe the setup; if not, how were they chosen? The evaluation clearly shows that data selection is crucial, yet it remains unclear how to tune these parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another contribution is the combination of the outputs from the two systems developed in the paper. I tried hard to understand the method, but the description is not clear enough to reproduce or evaluate it. Please provide a more explicit explanation of the procedure.

The paper introduces several variants for each proposed method. Would it make sense to combine more than two weakly supervised systems? Did the authors explore this direction?

Finally, it would be helpful to know more about the “in-house” dataset, particularly the domains and text types it contains.
