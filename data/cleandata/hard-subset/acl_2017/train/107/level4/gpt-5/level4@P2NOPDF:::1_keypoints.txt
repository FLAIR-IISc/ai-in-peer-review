- Cross-lingual projection with weak supervision is well established; the paper’s contributions appear mostly incremental and lack strong conceptual novelty.
- The data selection scheme is a central contribution and the quality score is reasonable, but its novelty is limited.
- Thresholds in Table 2 are insufficiently motivated; the paper only states that “different thresholds were tried.”
- It’s unclear whether thresholds were tuned on a held-out development set, how that set was constructed per language pair, and what the tuning protocol was.
- Because data selection critically affects results, readers need principled guidance on setting thresholds for new datasets or languages.
- The method for combining outputs from the two systems is unclear: what is combined (tokens, spans, or full sequences), how conflicts are resolved, and whether confidence scores/calibration are used.
- It’s not specified whether combination occurs at training or inference time, making reproduction difficult.
- The paper introduces multiple variants; it’s unclear if combining more than two systems or using stacking/voting/ensembling was explored and what the outcomes were.
- The “in-house” dataset lacks detail about text types, domains, collection/preprocessing, and differences from standard benchmarks.
- Overall: solid empirical work on an important problem, but needs stronger novelty claims and clearer explanations of threshold tuning, combination mechanism, and dataset composition for reproducibility.
