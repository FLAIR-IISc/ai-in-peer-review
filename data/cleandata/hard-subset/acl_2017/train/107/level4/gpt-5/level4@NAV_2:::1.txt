This paper introduces several weakly supervised approaches for building NER systems. All methods depend on projecting information from English into the target language. While the overall paradigm is established, the specific techniques read as incremental enhancements. For a premier venue, I would have expected more substantial methodological novelty.

One contribution is the data selection strategy. The quality scoring formula is quite simple—which is perfectly acceptable—but the process for determining the thresholds in Table 2 is not specified. The paper only notes that different thresholds were tried. Was this tuning performed on a development set? The manuscript does not indicate this. The results make clear that data selection is crucial, yet it remains unclear how to tune these parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another contribution is the combination of outputs from the two systems. I made a serious effort to follow the procedure, but the current description is not sufficiently clear.

The paper presents multiple variants for each proposed method. Would it be sensible to combine more than two weakly supervised systems? Did the authors experiment along these lines?

It would also be helpful to have more detail about the text domains represented in the “in-house” dataset.
