Review- This manuscript presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER). The authors utilize conditional random fields, maximum entropy Markov models, and neural network–based NER methods. They further propose two strategies to combine the outputs of these methods (probability-based and ranking-based), as well as a method to select optimal training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is performed using a variant of Mikolov’s approach. Overall, the paper is easy to read, well structured, and the English is sound. The results from the combined annotations are noteworthy.

Detailed comments:
I am interested in the motivation for introducing a variant of the Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model. You provide limited detail about this choice (and the parameters used). Did the original model (or the Continuous Skip-gram model) yield weak results? I recommend also reporting results with the standard CBOW model so readers can evaluate the gains from your approach.
Since you employ a decay factor for surrounding embeddings, I suggest considering the exponential decay used in [1].
In line with the previous point, I would like to see a comparison between the original Mikolov cross-lingual projections and your frequency-weighted projections. These contributions are more compelling if readers can observe that your method is clearly superior.
"the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant" ← Have you performed a statistical significance test? I would like to know whether the differences reported in this work are significant.
I suggest integrating the text of Section 4.4 at the beginning of Section 4.2 for a cleaner presentation. I also recommend moving the evaluation associated with Table 2 to the evaluation section.
I miss a related work section. Your introduction contains part of this content; I suggest splitting the introduction into two sections.
The evaluation is rather brief (about 1.5 pages with the conclusion included). You report state-of-the-art results, and I would appreciate more discussion and analysis of the findings.

Suggested references:
[1] Iacobacci, I., Pilehvar, M. T., & Navigli, R. (2016). Embeddings for word sense disambiguation: An evaluation study. In Proceedings of the 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (Vol. 1, pp. 897-907).
