- Addresses an important setting: low/no supervision for NER in target languages.
- Empirical study shows data selection strongly affects projection-based NER performance.
- Data selection formulation is simple and potentially practical.
- Limited novelty: methods are incremental extensions of established projection paradigms.
- Data selection thresholds are unspecified — unclear whether tuned on dev sets and risk of leakage.
- Lack of guidance for setting thresholds and hyperparameters across new languages or datasets.
- Combination method for two systems is poorly described and hard to reproduce.
- No experiments or discussion on combining more than two systems or inducing ensemble diversity.
- In-house dataset is insufficiently described (domain, size, language coverage, annotation consistency).
- Reproducibility details are missing for alignment/projection pipeline, filtering, hyperparameters, seeds, and code release.
- Suggestion to provide rigorous tuning protocol and sensitivity plots for thresholds.
- Suggestion to clarify combination algorithm (decision unit, conflict resolution, confidence use) and include ablations.
- Suggestion to explore larger/diverse ensembles and quantify diversity vs. gains.
- Suggestion to expand dataset documentation or release data, and to strengthen novelty via deeper analysis or principled insights.
- Overall: promising empirical findings but contributions are incremental and clarity/reproducibility fall below ACL standards.
