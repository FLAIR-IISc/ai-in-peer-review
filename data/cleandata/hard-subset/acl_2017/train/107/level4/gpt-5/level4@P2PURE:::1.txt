This paper proposes several weakly supervised approaches to building NERs, primarily via projection from English into other languages. While the techniques are solid, the general paradigm is not new, and the proposed methods appear to be incremental improvements over prior work. For an ACL paper, I would have expected more substantive novelty.

One contribution is the data selection procedure. The quality-score formula is simple, which is fine, but the basis for the thresholds reported in Table 2 is unclear. The paper only states that different thresholds were tried. Were these tuned on a development set? The manuscript does not specify. The evaluation results make it clear that data selection is crucial, yet the paper offers no guidance on how to tune these parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another contribution is the combination of outputs from the two systems developed in the paper. I tried to understand the procedure, but the description is not sufficiently clear.

The paper also presents several variants for each method. Would it make sense to combine more than two weakly supervised systems? Did the authors explore this direction?

It would also be helpful to provide more detail about the types of texts included in the “in-house” dataset.
