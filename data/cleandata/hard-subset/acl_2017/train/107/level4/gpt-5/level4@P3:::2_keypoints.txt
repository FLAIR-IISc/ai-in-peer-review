- Paper tackles weakly supervised cross-lingual NER without target annotations using annotation projection and representation projection plus co-decoding.

- Contribution: a simple sentence-selection heuristic for annotation projection that improves noisy-alignment performance.

- Contribution: a practical frequency-weighted linear mapping of target embeddings into English to reuse an English NER model zero-shot.

- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes (confidence-based exclude-O and rank-based) that combine complementary systems for consistent gains.

- Strength: effective handling of noisy projection data with large F1 gains for low-alignment languages (e.g., Japanese, Korean).

- Strength: complementary systems exploited well; rank-based co-decoding yields 2.9–6.2 F1 gains over single systems on in-house languages.

- Strength: broad empirical evaluation across seven languages and two label settings, outperforming prior cross-lingual baselines on CoNLL.

- Strength: practical approach using monolingual embeddings and a modest bilingual dictionary to enable a universal English NER model.

- Strength: clear presentation of models, heuristics, mapping, and co-decoding; scalability rationale (MEMMs vs high-order CRFs) is sound.

- Weakness: limited ablation studies—no comparisons for CBOW variant vs standard CBOW/skip-gram or weighted vs unweighted mapping.

- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported for comparisons and gains.

- Weakness: sparse error analysis and calibration assessment, especially given confidence-based co-decoding emphasis.

- Weakness: several methodological/configuration details underspecified (CBOW params, embedding training, NN2 prototypes, tokenization).

- Weakness: paper structure and placement of some material could be improved; evaluation/discussion depth is limited.

- Question: provide motivation, exact formulation, and comparisons for the distance-weighted CBOW variant versus standard CBOW/skip-gram.

- Question: quantify the contribution of frequency weighting in the bilingual mapping and sensitivity to dictionary size/quality.

- Question: are the reported gains statistically significant across runs?

- Question: provide details on NN2 prototype vectors (count, initialization, sensitivity).

- Question: how are BIO conflicts resolved in co-decoding, and have learned meta-decoders been tried as baselines?

- Question: report robustness to alignment quality, OOV rates, tokenization errors, and whether q/n thresholds can be tuned on small dev sets.

- Comment: consider reorganizing sections (move 4.4 into 4.2, relocate Table 2) and add a separate related work section.

- Comment: include more failure-case discussion, per-entity-type results, and language-specific issues.

- Comment: releasing code, models, and mapping dictionaries would aid reproducibility.

- Suggested reference: Iacobacci et al. (2016) for distance-weighted context aggregation in embedding methods.
