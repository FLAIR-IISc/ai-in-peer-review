This paper presents several weakly supervised methods for developing NERs, relying on projection from English into another language. While the individual methods are positioned as improvements over existing techniques, the overall approach is not new. For an ACL paper, I would have expected more substantial novelty.

One contribution is the data selection scheme. The formula used to compute the quality score is quite straightforward—which is not a drawback in itself—but it is unclear how the thresholds for Table 2 were determined. The paper states only that different thresholds were tried. Was this tuning done on a development set? There is no mention of this. The results clearly show that data selection is very important, yet it remains unclear how to tune these parameters for a new dataset or language pair.

Another contribution is the combination of the outputs of the two systems developed in the paper. I tried hard to understand how this combination works, but the description is not clear.

The paper also introduces a number of variants for each proposed method. Would it make sense to combine more than two weakly supervised systems? Did the authors try anything in this direction?

Finally, it would be helpful to know more about the types of texts included in the “in-house” dataset.
