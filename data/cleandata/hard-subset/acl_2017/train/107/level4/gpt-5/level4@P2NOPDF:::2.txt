This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER). The authors employ CRF-, maximum entropy Markov-, and neural network–based NER systems, and propose two ensemble strategies (probability-based and ranking-based) along with a method to select high-quality training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is implemented via a variant of Mikolov’s approach. The paper is generally clear, well-structured, and written in good English. The combined annotations yield interesting gains.

Detailed comments:
- Motivation for CBOW variant: Please clarify why a modified Continuous Bag-of-Words model is introduced. The manuscript provides few details about the variant or its hyperparameters. Did the standard CBOW or the Continuous Skip-gram baseline perform poorly? Including results for the original CBOW (and/or Skip-gram) would help readers assess the benefits of your changes.
- Decay factor: Since you apply a decay factor to surrounding embeddings, consider discussing exponential decay as in [1]. Please justify your chosen decay function and, if possible, report a sensitivity analysis.
- Cross-lingual projection: I would like to see a direct comparison between Mikolov’s original projection method and your frequency-weighted variant. These contributions would be more compelling with side-by-side results demonstrating consistent improvements.
- Statistical significance: You state that “the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant.” Have you conducted statistical significance testing? Please report the test used (e.g., paired bootstrap, approximate randomization) and p-values or confidence intervals to confirm that the observed differences are statistically reliable.
- Organization: Consider moving the content of Section 4.4 to the beginning of Section 4.2 for a cleaner narrative. Also, relocate the evaluation associated with Table 2 to the main evaluation section.
- Related work: A dedicated related work section is missing. The introduction currently covers part of it; splitting the introduction into background/related work and contributions would improve clarity.
- Evaluation depth: The evaluation is quite brief (about 1.5 pages, including conclusions). Since you report state-of-the-art results, more analysis would be valuable—for example, per-entity-type performance, language-wise breakdowns, error analysis, ablations for each ensemble component and data selection step, and discussion of when the frequency-weighted projection helps most.

Suggested reference:
[1] Iacobacci, I., Pilehvar, M. T., & Navigli, R. (2016). Embeddings for word sense disambiguation: An evaluation study. In Proceedings of the 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (Vol. 1, pp. 897–907).
