This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER). The authors employ conditional random fields (CRF), maximum entropy Markov models (MEMM), and neural network–based NER systems. They further propose two ensemble methods to combine system outputs (probability-based and ranking-based) and introduce a method for selecting high-quality training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection is performed using a variant of Mikolov’s approach. Overall, the paper is easy to follow, well structured, and written in clear English. The combined-annotation results are interesting.

Detailed comments:
- Please clarify the motivation for proposing a variation of the Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model. The paper provides few details about this variation or the parameters used. Did the original CBOW model (or the Continuous Skip-gram model) perform poorly, prompting your modification? I suggest including results with the original CBOW model so readers can assess the improvements delivered by your approach.
- Since you use a decay factor for surrounding embeddings, please consider the exponential decay weighting employed in [1]; it may be a relevant point of comparison.
- In line with the above, it would be helpful to present a direct comparison between the original Mikolov cross-lingual projections and your frequency-weighted projections. This contribution would be more compelling if readers can clearly see that your method is superior.
- You state that “the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant.” Have you conducted statistical significance testing? Please report whether the observed differences are statistically significant.
- Presentation suggestions:
  - Consider integrating the text of Section 4.4 at the beginning of Section 4.2 for a cleaner narrative flow.
  - Move the evaluation associated with Table 2 to the evaluation section.
- A dedicated related work section is missing. While the introduction covers part of this material, I recommend splitting the introduction and adding a separate related work section.
- The evaluation is quite brief (about 1.5 pages, with the conclusion included there). Given that you obtain state-of-the-art results, more discussion and analysis would be valuable.

Suggested reference:
[1] Iacobacci, I., Pilehvar, M. T., & Navigli, R. (2016). Embeddings for word sense disambiguation: An evaluation study. In Proceedings of the 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (Vol. 1, pp. 897–907).
