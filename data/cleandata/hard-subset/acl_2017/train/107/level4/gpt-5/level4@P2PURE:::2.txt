This paper presents a model for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER). The authors use conditional random fields (CRFs), maximum entropy Markov models (MEMMs), and neural network–based NER methods. They also propose two ensembling strategies (probability-based and ranking-based) and a data selection approach to choose the best training instances from cross-lingual comparable corpora. Cross-lingual projection follows a variant of Mikolov’s method. Overall, the paper is clear, well structured, and written in solid English. The combined-annotation results are interesting.

Detailed comments:
- Please clarify the motivation for proposing a variation of the Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) model. The paper provides few details about the modification or the parameters used. Did the standard CBOW (or the Continuous Skip-gram) model perform poorly? I suggest including results with the original CBOW model so readers can assess the improvements due to your approach. Since you apply a decay factor to context embeddings, consider the exponential decay used in [1].
- Similarly, please show the differences between Mikolov’s original cross-lingual projections and your frequency-weighted projections. This contribution would be more compelling if you demonstrated that your method clearly outperforms the original.
- You write that “the proposed data selection scheme is very effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and the improvement is significant.” Have you performed statistical significance testing? Please report whether the observed differences are statistically significant.
- For organization and clarity, I recommend moving the content of Section 4.4 to the beginning of Section 4.2. I also suggest moving the evaluation currently associated with Table 2 to the main evaluation section.
- The paper lacks a dedicated related work section; parts of it appear in the introduction. Consider splitting the introduction and adding a separate related work section.
- The evaluation is quite brief (approximately 1.5 pages, including conclusions). Since you report state-of-the-art results, a more thorough discussion and analysis would be valuable.

Suggested reference:
[1] Iacobacci, I., Pilehvar, M. T., & Navigli, R. (2016). Embeddings for word sense disambiguation: An evaluation study. In Proceedings of the 54th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (Vol. 1, pp. 897–907).
