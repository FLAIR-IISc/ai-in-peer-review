Summary & contributions:
- Combines annotation projection with a sentence-selection heuristic, representation projection using a frequency-weighted embedding mapping (and CBOW variant), and two co-decoding strategies to perform cross-lingual NER without target annotations.
- Main contributions: a simple effective co-decoding framework, a practical annotation-projection selection heuristic, and a frequency-weighted cross-lingual embedding mapping (with a CBOW variant) enabling direct model transfer.

Strengths:
- Combined systems yield strong empirical gains over prior baselines on CoNLL and proprietary data, with co-decoding improving F1 by 1.3–6.2 and large gains on noisy alignments (e.g., +13.9 Japanese, +20.0 Korean).
- Clear analysis of complementary error profiles (annotation projection = high-precision/low-recall; representation projection = more balanced) and a rank-based co-decoder that leverages this.
- Practical and scalable: maps many target languages into English so a single English NER model can be reused using small bilingual dictionaries and standard embeddings.
- Well written and organized with comprehensive empirical evaluation across public and proprietary datasets.

Weaknesses:
- Limited ablations on representation learning and mapping; lacks comparisons to standard CBOW/skip-gram and unweighted Mikolov mapping.
- No statistical significance testing reported for claimed improvements.
- Evaluation and analysis are brief; missing per-entity breakdowns, error analysis (boundary vs label), sensitivity to thresholds, dictionary size, embedding dimensionality, and alignment quality.
- Presentation/organization issues: related work interleaved, section ordering could improve, and some experimental material placed awkwardly.
- Incomplete training details (CBOW variant parameters, decay function, NER hyperparameters) hindering reproducibility.

Questions to authors:
- What motivated the CBOW modification; please provide direct comparisons to standard CBOW/skip-gram and consider exponential distance decay.
- How much do frequency weights help versus unweighted mapping; report sensitivity to dictionary size, thresholds, and embedding dimensionality mismatch.
- Which reported improvements are statistically significant; include paired tests and p-values.
- Were confidence scores calibrated across systems for the confidence-based co-decoder (e.g., temperature scaling or Platt calibration)?
- How were thresholds q and n chosen without target labels; can they be tuned without target dev data?
- How is the “NER-trained unk vector” obtained and how sensitive are results to its training?

Additional comments and references:
- Suggest reorganizing (move Section 4.4 before 4.2, relocate Table 2 to evaluation, split Introduction/Related Work) and expanding discussion on when annotation vs representation projection is preferable.
- Suggested citation: Iacobacci et al. (2016) for exponential distance decay in CBOW variants.
