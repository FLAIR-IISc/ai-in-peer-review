- Summary: Evaluates weakly supervised cross-lingual NER without target annotations, comparing annotation projection and representation projection and proposing two co-decoding strategies; combined systems outperform prior methods and sometimes approach supervised baselines.

- Contribution: Proposes a simple, effective data-selection heuristic for annotation projection that improves performance with noisy alignments.

- Contribution: Introduces direct model transfer via linear mapping of target embeddings into English space, reusing a single English NER without retraining.

- Contribution: Designs two co-decoding strategies (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based) that exploit complementary precision/recall to improve final predictions.

- Strength: Strong empirical evaluation across multiple languages and datasets with comparisons to prior work, often setting new cross-lingual baselines.

- Strength: Practical and scalable approach requiring no target-language annotation and enabling easy addition of new languages.

- Strength: Insightful analysis of differing precision/recall behaviors between methods and use of that insight to design co-decoding.

- Strength: Demonstrates large gains from data selection (e.g., +13.9 and +20.0 F1 for Japanese and Korean), highlighting importance of filtering noisy projections.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—methods are incremental refinements of established techniques rather than fundamentally new approaches.

- Weakness: Insufficient guidance on tuning data-selection thresholds (q, n), unclear sensitivity and reproducibility for new languages/domains.

- Weakness: Unclear specification of co-decoding methods—confidence calibration, conflict resolution, and BIO/IOB enforcement are not well explained.

- Weakness: Limited ensembling exploration—only pairwise system combinations considered, not combinations of all variants or alternative ensemble methods.

- Weakness: In-house datasets lack transparency (domains, annotation guidelines, alignment sources), hindering assessment of generalizability and reproducibility.

- Question: How were data-selection thresholds q and n chosen, how sensitive are results, and can you provide automatic tuning guidance?

- Question: Please provide precise algorithms/pseudocode for both co-decoding schemes, including calibration, conflict handling, BIO/IOB enforcement, and tie-breaking.

- Question: Did you try ensembling AP with both NN1 and NN2 or other ensemble strategies (majority vote, stacking, CRF merging), and how did they perform?

- Question: What are the domains, language varieties, and annotation guidelines for the in-house >50-type datasets, and is there domain shift from English source data?

- Question: What dictionary sizes/qualities were used for cross-lingual mapping, and how sensitive is performance to dictionary size or mapping constraints?

- Question: What are the training/inference time and memory requirements for AP (MEMM) and representation projection (NNs), and why choose MEMM over CRF for large label sets?
