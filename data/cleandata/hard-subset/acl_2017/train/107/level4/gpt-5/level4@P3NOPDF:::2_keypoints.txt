- Summary: Proposes a cross-lingual NER system combining CRF, MEMM, and neural models via probability- and ranking-based ensembles, a projection data-selection method, and a variant of Mikolov-style mapping; clear writing and strong combined-annotation results.

- Strength: Tackles an important cross-lingual NER problem with sensible heterogeneous models and ensembling, a practical focus on projection-data quality, and generally clear exposition.

- 1) CBOW variant motivation/details: Clarify why a modified CBOW is needed, provide full implementation/hyperparameters, and compare against original CBOW and skip-gram (including decay-rate sensitivity and alternatives like exponential decay).

- 2) Projection method and ablations: Compare frequency-weighted projection directly to original Mikolov mapping (with/without weighting and orthogonality constraint), ablate dictionary size/thresholds, and report a proxy mapping-quality metric (e.g., bilingual lexicon induction).

- 3) Ensemble design and calibration: Explain how token/sequence scores from CRF, MEMM, and neural models are made comparable and whether probabilities are calibrated, describe tie-handling, compare probability- vs ranking-based performance per language/entity, and add a meta-CRF stacking baseline.

- 4) Data selection scheme: Precisely define the scoring function and hyperparameters, show learning curves vs. number of selected instances and baselines (random, heuristics), and analyze error profiles of selected vs discarded sentences.

- 5) Statistical significance and stability: Report statistical significance tests and variance across multiple runs with different random seeds.

- 6) Evaluation depth and analysis: Expand evaluation with per-language and per-entity-type results, ablations isolating each component, robustness tests to projection noise/dictionary sparsity, and qualitative error analysis with examples.

- 7) Organization and presentation: Reorganize sections for better flow (move 4.4 into 4.2, put Table 2 evaluation into Evaluation), and add a standalone Related Work section separating background from contributions.

- 8) Reproducibility: Provide all hyperparameters/training settings for CRF, MEMM, neural models, embeddings, and projection; release code, mappings, and selected subsets if possible.

- Minor comments: Define notation at first use and ensure terminology consistency, clarify domain match of comparable corpora and discuss domain effects, and state computational costs for training and ensembling.

- Reference suggestion: Compare the decay scheme to Iacobacci et al. (2016) which uses exponential context-weight decay.

- Overall assessment: Well-written paper addressing an important problem with promising techniques that would be strengthened by clearer CBOW motivation, direct projection comparisons, significance testing, deeper diagnostics, and minor reorganization.
