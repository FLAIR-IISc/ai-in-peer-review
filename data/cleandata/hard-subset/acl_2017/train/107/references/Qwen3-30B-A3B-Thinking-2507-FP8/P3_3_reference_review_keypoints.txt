- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with frequency-based data selection and representation projection that maps target embeddings to source space.  
- Introduces confidence-based and rank-based co-decoding schemes to combine the two systems.  
- Contribution: a novel frequency-based data selection scheme that greatly improves annotation projection, especially for low alignment languages (+13.9 to +20.0 F1).  
- Contribution: a representation projection approach using cross-lingual embeddings to apply a source-language NER model directly to target-language text.  
- Contribution: co-decoding strategies that consistently outperform individual projection systems (2.9–6.2 F1 gains).  
- Strength: Simple, practical, and effective data selection metric with strong empirical gains, notably for Japanese and Korean.  
- Strength: Clear analysis of complementary strengths (AP: high precision/low recall; RP: balanced), motivating the rank-based co-decoding.  
- Strength: Extensive evaluation across multiple in-house and CoNLL languages showing generalizability and improvements over older baselines.  
- Weakness: No comparison with recent transformer-based multilingual methods (e.g., XLM-R, mBERT), leaving competitiveness unclear.  
- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative error analysis and limited discussion of limitations (e.g., typologically distant languages, lack of comparable corpora).  
- Weakness: Key methodological details are missing (implementation of smoothing layer, justification of q and n thresholds, OOV handling in representation projection).  
- Weakness: Novelty may be overstated, as methods build on prior cross-lingual embedding and alignment work without sufficient differentiation.  
- Weakness: Some model choices are unjustified (using MEMMs over CRFs, choosing neural nets for projection without rationale or evidence).  
- Question: How does the method compare empirically to multilingual transformer-based NER (e.g., XLM-R)?  
- Question: How are out-of-vocabulary target words handled when no dictionary translation exists?  
- Question: Can you provide evidence that CRF training is infeasible for large label sets (e.g., training time comparisons)?  
- Question: Why prefer rank-based co-decoding over confidence-based given non-comparable confidences—what empirical support exists?  
- Overall: A well-executed, practically useful contribution with a particularly strong data-selection idea, but its impact is reduced by missing SOTA comparisons and incomplete methodological/limitation discussion.
