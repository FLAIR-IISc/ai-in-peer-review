- Paper tackles cross-lingual NER without target-language human annotation using annotation projection and representation projection, plus co-decoding to combine them.
- Evaluated on in-house (Japanese, Korean, German, Portuguese) and CoNLL (Spanish, Dutch, German) datasets.

- Main contribution: frequency-based data selection for annotation projection to pick high-quality projection-labeled data from noisy comparable corpora.
- Practical implementation of representation projection mapping target-language embeddings to English to apply a single English NER model.
- Two co-decoding schemes (including a rank-based approach) that exploit complementary precision/recall properties of the two methods.

- Strongest strength: the simple yet effective data selection scheme yields large F1 gains when alignment quality is poor.
- Thorough, multilingual evaluation with comparisons to established baselines and consistent performance improvements.
- Insightful analysis showing annotation projection is high-precision/low-recall while representation projection is more balanced, motivating co-decoding.
- Clear, well-structured presentation with sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.
- Combined systems outperform individual approaches across experiments.

- Weakness: comparisons are against older baselines (2012, 2016) and miss recent transformer-based methods (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).
- Limited analysis of why the data selection scheme works and how to set its thresholds (q, n) across conditions.
- Missing discussion of limitations: needs comparable data for frequency tables, depends on translation dictionaries, unclear behavior on diverse scripts.
- Questionable “state-of-the-art” claim given outdated baselines.
- Lack of ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution.

- Key questions: how do results compare to multilingual pretrained models in weakly supervised settings?
- Why did data selection slightly hurt German (65.7 vs 66.4 F1) and what language characteristics explain this?
- How does the number of entity types affect cross-lingual performance and label-set mismatch?
- What is the computational cost of training per-language embedding mappings vs. training target NER models?
- Minimum comparable-data requirements for the data selection scheme (e.g., 100K vs 5M tokens)?

- Additional comments: insufficient details about the alignment system and its performance; clarify the “no re-training” claim for representation projection; discuss applicability to different writing systems and embedding types.
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing comparisons to recent work and clarifying limitations.
