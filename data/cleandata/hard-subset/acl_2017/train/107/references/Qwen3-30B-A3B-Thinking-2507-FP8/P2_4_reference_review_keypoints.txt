- Proposes weakly supervised cross-lingual NER with no target-language annotation via annotation projection and representation projection plus co-decoding.
- Contribution 1: data selection scheme for annotation projection that selects high-quality projection-labeled data from noisy comparable corpora.
- Contribution 2: representation projection mapping target-language embeddings to English space to apply a single English NER model.
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs.
- Strength: data selection improves annotation projection substantially, especially when alignment accuracy is low (large F1 gains for Japanese/Korean).
- Strength: representation projection is simple, effective across multiple languages, and allows adding languages without retraining.
- Strength: rank-based co-decoder effectively combines high-precision annotation projection and high-recall representation projection, improving F1 by 2.9–6.2 points.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on six languages and comparisons showing combined approach approaches supervised performance for some languages.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent neural cross-lingual methods (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), making "state-of-the-art" claims questionable.
- Weakness: insufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of data selection, projection, and co-decoding.
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations (performance on very low-resource languages, sensitivity to alignment accuracy).
- Weakness: no explanation of how threshold parameters (q, n) were chosen or whether they generalize across languages.
- Weakness: inconsistent per-language performance between annotation and representation projection without analysis of causes.
- Questions: how do methods compare to mBERT/XLM-R and can co-decoding apply to neural approaches?
- Questions: how were threshold parameters determined and are they language-specific?
- Questions: what explains differing performance across languages (e.g., Portuguese vs. German) and how to address it?
- Questions: how does the approach scale to languages with very low alignment accuracy (e.g., <50%)?
- Overall: well-written paper with practical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add recent comparisons and detailed ablation/analysis.
