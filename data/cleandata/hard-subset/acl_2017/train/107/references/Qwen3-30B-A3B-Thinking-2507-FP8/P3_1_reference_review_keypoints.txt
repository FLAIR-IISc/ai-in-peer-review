- Proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER pipeline with no target-language annotation using (1) annotation projection with a novel data-selection scheme and (2) representation projection mapping target embeddings to English, plus co-decoding to combine them.  
- Novelty: frequency-based data selection for annotation projection that filters low-quality alignment-induced noise and substantially boosts performance.  
- Contribution: a well-executed representation-projection method that leverages cross-lingual embeddings to directly apply an English NER model.  
- Contribution: practical co-decoding schemes that combine projection outputs for improved overall performance.  
- Strength: the frequency-based selection yields large F1 improvements on low-alignment languages (e.g., +13.9 Japanese, +20.0 Korean).  
- Strength: thorough and clear empirical evaluation across multiple languages and datasets with proper baselines and metrics.  
- Strength: strong practical relevance for low-resource NER, approaching supervised performance in some cases (e.g., Portuguese).  
- Strength: well-organized presentation with supportive figures, tables, and ablation-style experiments.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent cross-lingual pretraining baselines (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), undermining state-of-the-art claims.  
- Weakness: representation-projection novelty is limited—similar embedding-mapping approaches exist and differentiation is not well explained.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of failure cases and why performance varies across languages (e.g., German vs. Korean).  
- Weakness: missing detailed ablations to attribute gains to specific components (data selection vs. co-decoding) and parameter sensitivity (q, n).  
- Weakness: the data-selection heuristic assumes majority-tag correctness for entities, which can fail for ambiguous names or systematic source biases.  
- Question: how does the selection scheme handle entities with multiple valid types (e.g., "Apple" company vs. fruit) and potential resulting mislabeling?  
- Question: why does representation projection perform better for some languages than others—any correlation with language distance or other factors?  
- Question: please compare to strong recent baselines like XLM-R/mBERT to situate results in the current landscape.  
- Question: why use different embedding dimensions for target (800d) and English (300d), and what is the impact on performance?  
- Overall recommendation: Minor Revision — solid, practical contribution (notably the data selection), but needs comparisons to recent baselines, clearer novelty claims for representation projection, and deeper analysis of limitations.
