- Contribution: Proposes a heuristic data selection scheme that filters noisy annotation-projected data using entity frequency statistics.
- Contribution: Introduces a representation projection method that maps target-language word embeddings into the source-language space for direct NER model transfer.
- Contribution: Presents two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs.

- Strength: Addresses a practical real-world problem—building NER for target languages without human annotation—with immediately useful methods.
- Strength: Novel representation projection enables using a single source-language NER model across multiple target languages with minimal adaptation.
- Strength: Strong, consistent empirical gains across languages (e.g., large F1 jumps for Japanese and Korean; co-decoding adds 2.9–6.2 F1).
- Strength: Effectively combines complementary systems (annotation projection high precision; representation projection balanced), with the rank-based co-decoder performing well.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to recent state-of-the-art multilingual transformer-based methods (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R).
- Weakness: Evaluation includes non-low-resource languages (German, Dutch, Portuguese) without analysis of truly low-resource scenarios or zero-parallel-data cases.
- Weakness: No ablation studies or qualitative failure analysis to isolate component contributions and limitations.
- Weakness: Missing reproducibility details for cross-lingual embedding mappings (training dictionary construction, word2vec parameters, thresholds).

- Question: Please compare with recent multilingual transformer-based cross-lingual NER methods (e.g., XLM-R, mBERT).
- Question: Clarify which evaluated languages are considered low-resource and how the method performs for languages with little or no parallel data.
- Question: Provide qualitative examples where rank-based co-decoding differs from confidence-based decisions and explain why rank-based is superior.

- Overall assessment: Practical, novel, and empirically strong work but needs comparisons to current SOTA and deeper analyses; recommend major revision.
