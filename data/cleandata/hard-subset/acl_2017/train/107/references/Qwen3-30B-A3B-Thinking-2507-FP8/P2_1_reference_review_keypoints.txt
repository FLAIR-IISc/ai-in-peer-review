- Tackles cross-lingual NER without target-language annotations using annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection mapping embeddings to English.

- Contribution: heuristic data selection for annotation projection that significantly improves noisy projected labels (up to +20 F1 for Korean).

- Contribution: representation projection mapping target-language embeddings into English space to apply an English NER model without retraining.

- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes (confidence-based and rank-based) that combine systems and boost performance over either alone.

- Strength: practical and well-motivated solution for low-resource NER, addressing alignment/noise issues with substantial empirical gains.

- Strength: simple, effective data selection (q(y) ≥ q; n(y) ≥ n) empirically validated, especially when alignment accuracy is low.

- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on six languages and both in-house and CoNLL datasets, outperforming prior work by 2.9–6.2 F1.

- Strength: well-rationalized co-decoding that leverages annotation projection’s high precision and representation projection’s higher recall.

- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent transformer-based cross-lingual methods (e.g., multilingual BERT), limiting relevance to current SOTA.

- Weakness: limited analysis of why methods work and why performance varies across languages (e.g., Japanese/Korean vs German/Portuguese).

- Weakness: insufficient implementation details for reproducibility (embedding training specifics, architecture, hyperparameters).

- Weakness: representation projection idea is not clearly distinguished from prior cross-lingual embedding work, weakening novelty claims.

- Weakness: claim of “no human annotation in the target languages” is overstated since English-annotated data is required.

- Question: How do the methods compare to transformer-based cross-lingual approaches like multilingual BERT?

- Question: Can the representation projection scale to languages without strong English resources or use a different pivot language?

- Question: Why does the data selection scheme yield larger gains for low-alignment languages, and can more language-specific analysis be provided?

- Question: Why does rank-based co-decoding outperform confidence-based, particularly regarding the “exclude-O” behavior?

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—strong, practical contributions but needs comparisons to recent work, deeper analysis, and fuller implementation details.
