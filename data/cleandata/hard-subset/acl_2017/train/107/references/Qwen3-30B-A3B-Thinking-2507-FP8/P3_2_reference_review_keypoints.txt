- Contribution: Proposes a heuristic data selection scheme to improve quality of annotation-projected NER labels in weakly supervised cross-lingual settings.
- Contribution: Adapts representation projection (cross-lingual word embeddings) for direct NER model transfer to target languages.
- Contribution: Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs.

- Strength: Data selection scheme is practical and effective, yielding large F1 gains (e.g., +20.0 for Korean) by filtering low-quality projections.
- Strength: Comprehensive, rigorous evaluation across multiple languages and datasets with comparisons to prior baselines.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized methodological presentation with concrete examples illustrating the data selection approach.

- Weakness: Representation projection lacks novelty beyond applying established cross-lingual embeddings to NER and is often outperformed by annotation projection.
- Weakness: Limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., multilingual BERT), so claims of outperforming SOTA may be incomplete.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why methods work, e.g., which error types are reduced by data selection or embedding quality for morphologically complex languages.
- Weakness: Co-decoding yields only modest improvements over the best single system; necessity and cases of negative impact are not well justified.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of limitations and generalizability to typologically different languages (e.g., agglutinative or non-Latin-script languages).

- Question: Why were multilingual BERT-based and other recent cross-lingual NER methods not included in comparisons, and how would the proposed methods fare against them?
- Question: How does representation projection handle morphologically rich or syntactically different languages (e.g., Turkish, Finnish), which were not evaluated?
- Question: Has the correlation between English NER errors and alignment errors been analyzed, given the data selection assumes their independence?
- Question: What training data and alignment quality metrics were used for the maximum-entropy alignment systems, and what are their accuracies on the evaluation sets?
- Question: Can the authors better justify co-decoding given its modest gains and identify scenarios where it helps or hurts?

- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable practical contribution (especially data selection) but needs stronger novelty in representation projection, comparisons to recent work, and deeper analysis.
