- Problem and approach: Builds cross-lingual NER without target-language human labels via annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection (embedding mapping), plus two co-decoding schemes.

- Key contribution 1: Heuristic data selection for annotation projection that yields large F1 gains (13–20 points) for languages with low alignment accuracy.

- Key contribution 2: Representation projection that maps target-language embeddings to English for direct NER model transfer without retraining.

- Key contribution 3: Rank-based co-decoding that effectively combines annotation- and representation-based outputs.

- Strength: Well-motivated data selection addressing projection quality dependence on alignment accuracy, with strong empirical improvements.

- Strength: Simple, clearly presented representation projection method that’s easy to implement and avoids per-language retraining.

- Strength: Comprehensive multilingual evaluation (in-house and CoNLL languages) showing consistent improvements and near-supervised performance in some cases.

- Strength: Co-decoding leverages complementary strengths (annotation projection high precision, representation projection balanced recall) in a practical way.

- Weakness: Limited novelty of the representation projection given prior cross-lingual embedding work; novelty not clearly distinguished.

- Weakness: Missing comparisons to recent state-of-the-art transformer-based methods (e.g., multilingual BERT).

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why data selection helps some languages more than others and how alignment accuracy relates to final NER performance.

- Weakness: Limited discussion/experiments on non-European or typologically distant languages (e.g., Arabic, Chinese, agglutinative languages).

- Question 1: How does the representation projection compare to multilingual transformer approaches and were such models considered?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide a deeper analysis of the relationship among alignment accuracy, data selection effectiveness, and NER outcomes?

- Question 3: Clarify the human effort required for building/using comparable corpora and alignment models versus annotating NER data in the target language.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address novelty positioning, recent baselines, and deeper analyses.
