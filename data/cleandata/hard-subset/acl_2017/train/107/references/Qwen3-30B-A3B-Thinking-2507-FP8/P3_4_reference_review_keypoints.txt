- Summary: Proposes annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping, plus co-decoding, for zero-resource cross-lingual NER; evaluated on in-house and CoNLL datasets.

- Contribution 1: Heuristic data selection for annotation projection that filters noisy alignments and improves projected label quality.

- Contribution 2: Representation projection that maps target-language embeddings to an English space to apply a single pretrained NER model without retraining.

- Contribution 3: Co-decoding schemes (rank-based) that combine annotation and representation projections to improve overall NER performance.

- Strength 1: Data selection scheme is practical and empirically effective, yielding large F1 gains for low-alignment languages (e.g., +13.9 JP, +20.0 KO).

- Strength 2: Representation projection offers an efficient, language-agnostic way to extend NER to new languages without language-specific retraining.

- Strength 3: Rank-based co-decoding leverages complementary strengths of the two methods and improves F1 by 2.9–6.2 points over the best single method.

- Weakness 1: No comparison with recent multilingual transformer baselines (e.g., mBERT, XLM-R), which limits assessment against current SOTA.

- Weakness 2: Evaluation is limited to CoNLL and proprietary datasets; lacks tests on broader benchmarks (e.g., XTREME) and uses non-public data affecting reproducibility.

- Weakness 3: Claimed novelty of representation projection is questionable given prior cross-lingual embedding work; related-work differentiation is superficial.

- Weakness 4: Missing ablation studies and insufficient implementation/training details (e.g., embedding training, dictionary/phrase-table specifics), hindering reproducibility and attribution of gains.

- Question 1: Why were multilingual transformer models (mBERT/XLM-R) not used as baselines?

- Question 2: How does the data selection scheme perform when alignment accuracy is high (e.g., Portuguese)?

- Question 3: What is the computational cost of using representation projection versus training a new NER model per language?

- Question 4: Can the authors provide ablation results isolating impacts of data selection versus representation projection?

- Overall assessment: Technically sound and practically valuable (especially the data selection), but impact is weakened by lack of modern baselines, limited evaluation, and missing experimental details — recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions.
