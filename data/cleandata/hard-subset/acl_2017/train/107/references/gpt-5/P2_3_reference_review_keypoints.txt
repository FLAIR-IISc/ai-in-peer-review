- Paper proposes three contributions: heuristic frequency-based sentence selection for annotation projection, a linear representation-projection mapping to apply an English NER model zero-shot, and two simple co-decoding fusion schemes.  
- Data-selection method builds an entity–label frequency table to estimate per-sentence quality q(y) and filters by quality and entity count, improving performance with noisy alignments.  
- Representation-projection learns a linear mapping from target embeddings to English space so an English-only NN can be applied without retraining on target data.  
- Co-decoding combines AP (annotation projection) and RP (representation projection) outputs via exclude-O confidence-based and a rank-based scheme that preserves high-precision AP entities and adds non-conflicting RP entities.  
- Strength: practical, language-agnostic data-selection metric that yields large F1 gains for noisy languages (e.g., +13.9 Japan, +20.0 Korean).  
- Strength: simple, lightweight zero-shot transfer recipe (linear map + English decoder) that is plug-and-play across languages and domains.  
- Strength: complementary subsystems with fusion improving F1 consistently (e.g., +2.9–6.2 in-house, +1.3–3.6 CoNLL dev).  
- Strength: broad evaluation across seven languages, two testbeds (in-house 50+ types, CoNLL 4 types), and clear ablation of AP and RP variants.  
- Strength: clear, coherent methodological descriptions (weighted least-squares mapping, selection metrics, conflict definition).  
- Weakness: unclear tuning protocol—Table 2 reports per-language “best” thresholds without specifying a dev set, risking test-set overfitting.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to modern multilingual pretrained transformers (mBERT, XLM-R) and stronger alignment methods (orthogonal Procrustes, VecMap), limiting claims of state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of the representation mapping (no orthogonality/normalization experiments, sparse diagnostics, limited study of dictionary size/quality).  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—core corpora, aligners, and many implementation details/resources are proprietary or under-specified.  
- Weakness: co-decoding novelty is modest and purely heuristic; a learned meta-decoder or calibration analysis is missing.  
- Questions for authors: how were thresholds (q, n) chosen—dev set or test tuning? Please clarify and rerun if needed.  
- Questions for authors: how does RP/co-decoding compare to zero-shot mBERT/XLM-R and orthogonal/VecMap mappings under identical constraints?  
- Questions for authors: what are the exact sources/sizes of comparable corpora and phrase tables, and can resources/tools be released?  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of RP to dictionary size/coverage, embedding dimensions, and whether M was constrained (orthogonal/normalized)?  
- Questions for authors: are improvements statistically significant—please report variance or confidence intervals across runs?  
- Overall: promising, practical approach with solid empirical gains, but methodological (dev tuning), baseline, and reproducibility shortcomings lower confidence; reviewer leans weak reject pending fixes (clarify tuning, add modern baselines, release resources).
