- Summary: Tackles cross-lingual NER without target-language annotation using two projection-based methods and simple co-decoding; pragmatic and empirically driven.

- Contribution 1: Proposes an entity-reliability score and entity-count threshold to select projection-labeled sentences from noisy comparable corpora, improving AP-based NER.

- Contribution 2: Introduces representation projection via a linear mapping from target to English embeddings using a small weighted bilingual dictionary to run a single English NER model on mapped target words.

- Contribution 3: Presents two lightweight co-decoding schemes (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based merging) that consistently improve over individual systems and prior baselines.

- Strength: Practical, simple methods with clear empirical gains, especially large F1 improvements for noisy alignments (e.g., Japanese/Korean).

- Strength: Broad evaluation on in-house >50-type data and CoNLL 4-type data across multiple languages, with precision/recall profiling and complementary error analysis.

- Strength: Representation projection uses only word embeddings, avoiding language-specific resources and enabling a single universal model.

- Strength: Outperforms strong prior cross-lingual NER baselines on CoNLL and approaches supervised performance for some languages.

- Strength: Resource-aware design (MEMMs and lightweight NNs) motivated for large label sets and weak supervision.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—techniques are incremental refinements of known ideas.

- Weakness: Risk of tuning on test data and missing statistical significance testing for reported improvements.

- Weakness: Dependence on non-trivial resources (comparable corpora, aligners, phrase tables, monolingual corpora) may limit applicability to very low-resource languages.

- Weakness: Insufficient ablations and sensitivity analyses (dictionary size/weighting, embedding settings, OOV impact, co-decoding alternatives).

- Weakness: Baselines could be broader and more modern (multilingual neural models, BiLSTM-CRF, orthogonal mapping methods).

- Question: How were AP selection thresholds (q, n) chosen—was a dev set used or were they tuned on test?

- Question: What are sizes/sources of monolingual corpora for embeddings, and what are test OOV rates / UNK usage?

- Question: How are phrase tables/dictionaries for RP obtained, how much parallel data is needed, and how does dictionary quality/size affect performance?

- Question: Were orthogonality constraints or different weighting schemes for the mapping tried, and any ablation of weighted vs unweighted mapping?

- Question: Can you provide statistical significance tests for the main improvements (co-decoding vs best single system) on CoNLL test?

- Question: Will the authors release code, selection scripts, and trained mappings/models for reproducibility?

- Overall assessment: Solid, practical work with consistent empirical gains; moderate novelty but likely useful—recommend acceptance pending clarifications on tuning, significance, and ablations.
