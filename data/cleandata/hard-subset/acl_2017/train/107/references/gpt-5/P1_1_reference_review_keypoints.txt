- Proposes a simple data-selection scheme for annotation projection that scores target sentences by mean projected entity-label frequency q(y) and entity count n(y), improving AP-based NER under noisy alignments (+13.9 F1 JP, +20.0 KR).  
- Introduces representation projection: learn monolingual embeddings plus a small weighted bilingual dictionary to linearly map target embeddings to English and apply an English NN NER without target retraining.  
- Presents two co-decoding schemes (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based prioritizing AP spans) to combine AP and projection outputs, achieving SOTA cross-lingual NER on CoNLL and strong in-house results.  
- Strength: Practical, low-resource effectiveness — data selection is simple, supervision-free, and yields large gains where projection is brittle.  
- Strength: Training-free transfer at test time — single English NN reused across languages via a small dictionary, aiding scalability and deployment.  
- Strength: Complementary error profiles — AP is high-precision/low-recall, projection NN is more balanced, and their combination improves F1 consistently.  
- Strength: Broad empirical evaluation across 4 in-house languages with 50+ types and 3 CoNLL languages with standard 4 types, comparing to supervised upper bounds and baselines.  
- Strength: Methodological clarity and resource frugality — weighted least squares mapping and redundancy-based AP selection are easy to reproduce.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty in embedding mapping and heuristic co-decoding; lacks exploration of more recent cross-lingual embedding methods and principled fusion.  
- Weakness: Incomplete reproducibility and data transparency — in-house datasets, comparable corpora, and alignment/phrase-table sources are insufficiently described or unavailable.  
- Weakness: Missing analytical ablations — sensitivities to dictionary size/quality, embedding dimensions, orthogonality, alignment quality, and q/n thresholds are not quantified.  
- Weakness: Fairness/comparability issues — AP trains MEMMs on large projected data while projection method uses no target training; compute/data budget comparisons and modern baselines (multilingual encoders) are missing.  
- Weakness: Lacks statistical testing — no significance tests or confidence intervals reported for modest CoNLL gains.  
- Question: What corpora/tools produced alignments and phrase tables for each language, are corpora truly comparable, and what are sizes/coverage and overlap with test vocabularies?  
- Question: Did you try orthogonality constraints, CSLS, or unsupervised/induced bilingual lexicons, and how sensitive are results to dictionary size and weighting?  
- Question: How were q and n tuned and how sensitive is performance to these thresholds; was alignment confidence considered in q(y)?  
- Question: How are per-token probabilities calibrated across MEMM and NN for confidence-based fusion, were temperature/Platt scaling tried, and what types of conflicts dominate?  
- Question: Which entity types benefit most/least from AP selection vs. representation projection, especially for fine-grained vs. head entity types?  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — useful, practical contributions with modest novelty but strong empirical utility; more analysis and transparency needed.
