- Proposes two projection-based cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with sentence-level entity-agreement data selection and representation projection via bilingual linear mappings into English embedding space.  
- Introduces two co-decoding heuristics (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based prioritizing high-precision AP) to merge model outputs.  
- Empirically outperforms prior cross-lingual NER methods on CoNLL 02/03 and yields meaningful results on in-house fine-grained NER (>50 types).  
- Clear problem framing and complementary transfer routes, exploiting complementary error profiles of AP (high-precision/low-recall) and representation projection (more balanced).  
- Simple, effective data selection based on entity-frequency quality scoring that substantially improves noisy-projection performance (e.g., +13.9 F1 JP, +20.0 KR).  
- Representation projection enables zero retraining per target language using one English NN model plus learned bilingual mappings; weighting in regression is a small sensible improvement.  
- Broad evaluation across multiple languages and settings, with supervised upper bounds and comparisons to older baselines.  
- Co-decoding consistently improves over each component alone (notable F1 gains on in-house and CoNLL dev sets).  
- Limited novelty: linear mapping and denoising-like data selection are incremental relative to contemporary cross-lingual representation literature.  
- Missing strong modern baselines (mBERT/XLM-R/LaBSE) and newer CLWE methods (orthogonal/Procrustes, self-learning), weakening claims of state-of-the-art relevance.  
- Reproducibility concerns: key resources and hyperparameters (corpora, aligners, phrase tables, NN details, prototype counts) are not fully specified or released.  
- Possible tuning/test selection bias: data selection thresholds (q,n) appear chosen using “best numbers” without clear held-out dev splits for in-house languages.  
- Scope limitations: method depends on translation lexicons/phrase tables and monolingual embeddings, and omits character/subword features important for morphologically rich or truly low-resource languages.  
- Requests clarification on threshold selection protocol (dev vs test) and re-run with proper dev splits if needed.  
- Requests full NN hyperparameters and sensitivity analyses for NN1/NN2, as well as details on mapping constraints (orthogonality/Procrustes), weighted vs unweighted regression, and dictionary size effects.  
- Asks for sources/sizes of monolingual corpora, alignment/phrase-table pipelines, and release of seed dictionaries and mappings.  
- Requests statistical significance testing (e.g., bootstrap) for co-decoding gains.  
- Suggestions: evaluate against multilingual contextual baselines (mBERT/XLM-R) and hybridize with co-decoding; provide ablations for data selection, mapping weighting, orthogonal constraint, dictionary thresholds, and co-decoding variants.  
- Suggests analyzing long-tail entity effects and consider diversity-aware sampling to avoid filtering rare but correct mentions.  
- Overall: clear, practical, and empirically solid contribution with moderate novelty; borderline accept leaning accept pending stronger baselines, clearer tuning protocol, reproducibility resources, and additional analyses.
