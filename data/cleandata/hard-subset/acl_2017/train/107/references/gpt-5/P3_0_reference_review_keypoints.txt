- Paper tackles weakly supervised cross-lingual NER without target-language annotation, combining annotation projection (AP), representation projection (RP), and co-decoding.  
- AP: filters noisy projected labels with an entity–tag frequency-based sentence quality score.  
- RP: linearly maps target-language embeddings to English using a weighted bilingual dictionary to apply an English NER model.  
- Co-decoding: proposes two simple schemes (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based) to combine AP and RP outputs.

- Contribution: a practical data-selection heuristic for annotation projection that aggregates entity–tag reliability to filter noisy sentences.  
- Contribution: a simple universal representation-projection pipeline using weighted least-squares mapping and a single English NN tagger for zero-training transfer.  
- Contribution: lightweight ensemble/co-decoding strategies that exploit complementary precision/recall profiles of AP and RP.

- Strength: clear, modular methodology with realistic resource assumptions (small dictionaries, comparable/parallel corpora) and component isolation.  
- Strength: large, robust gains in high-noise/low-alignment settings, showing the selection metric captures signal beyond projection volume.  
- Strength: complementary modeling choices (AP high-precision, RP better recall) and an effective rank-based combination yielding consistent F1 gains.  
- Strength: broad evaluation across fine-grained in-house types and CoNLL languages indicating generality.  
- Strength: engineering practicality—single English model per target, modest dictionary sizes, linear mapping is fast and deployable.

- Weakness: limited novelty—selection and linear mapping are close to prior noise-aware projection and mapping methods; missing comparisons to stronger alternatives (orthogonal Procrustes, CCA, CSLS, joint training).  
- Weakness: unclear resource assumptions and how sentence/word alignments or phrase tables were obtained; comparability vs. parallelism not well specified.  
- Weakness: possible tuning on test for in-house results—no clear dev/test split or hyperparameter selection protocol provided.  
- Weakness: under-specified reproducibility details (tokenization, entity canonicalization, dictionary extraction, monolingual data sizes, OOV handling).  
- Weakness: modeling choices may be suboptimal by current standards (no char/subword features, no CRF/BiLSTM comparisons, missing modern multilingual baselines).

- Question: How were AP thresholds q and n tuned for in-house experiments—separate dev sets or tuned on test? Provide sensitivity analyses.  
- Question: How are entity surface forms canonicalized in the frequency table (casefolding, morphology, JP/KO segmentation) and how are rare entities/smoothing handled?  
- Question: What corpora and supervision produced the sentence/word alignments and phrase tables; how were aligners trained for comparable corpora? Provide dictionary extraction thresholds and ablations.  
- Question: Why use an unconstrained linear map rather than orthogonal mapping; were hubness mitigations (CSLS) or orthogonal Procrustes tried; provide ablations on weighting/dimensionality.  
- Question: Clarify OOV handling—how can target tokens be found in English vocab (string overlap/transliteration) and what is the UNK handling strategy?  
- Question: For exclude-O co-decoding, how are confidences calibrated across models and were more principled combination methods (stacking, ILP) evaluated?  
- Question: Provide analysis of error types introduced by conflicts between AP and RP.

- Suggestion: Include per-entity-type performance and error analysis to check bias toward frequent entities and tails.  
- Suggestion: Report monolingual data amounts, domains, OOV rates, and projected embedding coverage.  
- Suggestion: Consider adding language-agnostic subword/character features to handle morphology and OOVs while preserving transferability.  
- Suggestion: Compare against stronger cross-lingual baselines (orthogonal Procrustes, joint multilingual embeddings, modern multilingual encoders) to contextualize gains.

- Overall: Coherent, practical methods with solid empirical gains, but moderate methodological novelty, several missing details, and need for stronger baselines and ablations.
