- Paper combines denoised annotation projection, representation projection, and two co-decoding schemes for zero-resource cross-lingual NER.
- Annotation projection uses a sentence-level entity–tag consistency selection heuristic to denoise projections from comparable corpora.
- Representation projection maps target embeddings into English via a weighted linear mapping and runs a single English NER model, requiring only monolingual text and a small dictionary.
- Two co-decoding strategies (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based prioritizing AP) consistently improve over individual components.
- Entity–tag consistency selection substantially improves projection under noisy alignments (e.g., +13.9 F1 Japanese, +20.0 F1 Korean).
- Representation projection offers retraining-free, per-language-free transfer that is operationally attractive and competitive when combined with projection.
- The complementary strengths of AP (high-precision/low-recall) and RP (more balanced) are leveraged effectively by the rank-based co-decoder.
- Evaluation spans multiple languages and tag sets (in-house four languages and CoNLL Spanish/Dutch/German) with precision/recall breakdowns and supervised upper bounds.
- Provides sensible engineering choices and ablations (two NN architectures, dictionary size tuning, dimensionality discussion, two co-decoders).
- Novelty is limited since core ingredients resemble prior projection/distant supervision and standard cross-lingual embedding mappings.
- Missing comparisons to stronger embedding-mapping baselines (orthogonal Procrustes, CSLS, seed-dictionary bootstrapping).
- Resource assumptions unclear: nature, size, and parallelness of “comparable” corpora and how alignments/phrase tables are obtained are underspecified.
- Potential non-apples-to-apples comparisons with prior work if resource types/sizes differ.
- Reproducibility concerns: in-house datasets, alignment systems, selection thresholds, NN hyperparameters, preprocessing, and dictionary construction details are not fully reported.
- Limited analysis depth: no entity-type/length/frequency breakdowns, script-specific performance, OOV handling, or systematic sensitivity to dictionary/alignment quality.
- Architectural omissions: no comparisons to CRF-based or multilingual subword/character models and no ablation on weighted vs. unweighted dictionary regression or exclude-O heuristic.
- Question: clarify exact nature, sizes, domains, and alignment methods for the comparable corpora and whether external parallel data were used.
- Question: detail entity normalization, multiword/transliteration handling, and sensitivity analysis for selection hyperparameters q and n.
- Question: justify not using orthogonal/CSLS mappings, and report sensitivity to dictionary size and noise (performance below 1K pairs).
- Question: how are confidence scores calibrated across systems and how are tag disagreements on identical spans resolved in rank-based co-decoding?
- Request: report entity-type-wise F1, OOV/entity-length breakdowns, and more systematic error analyses.
- Request: clarify training corpora and hyperparameters for word2vec and the NNs.
- Suggestion: release selection code and a small benchmark to improve reproducibility.
- Overall: solid, pragmatic contributions with clear empirical gains, but moderate methodological novelty and missing experimental details and stronger baselines.
