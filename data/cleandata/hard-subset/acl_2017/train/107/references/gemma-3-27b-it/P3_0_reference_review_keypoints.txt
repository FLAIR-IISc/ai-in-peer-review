- Paper tackles zero-shot cross-lingual NER from English using annotation projection with data selection and representation projection via cross-lingual embeddings, plus co-decoding to combine them.  
- Shows that a simple data selection heuristic improves annotation projection quality on noisy comparable corpora.  
- Proposes combining annotation projection (precision-focused) and representation projection (recall-focused) via co-decoding to boost overall performance.  
- Evaluates methods across multiple languages and datasets (in-house and CoNLL), demonstrating consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Clearly motivates weak supervision for multilingual NER and explains the problem and cost of annotation well.  
- Uses sound, well-established techniques (CRFs, neural nets, embeddings, alignments) and combines them effectively.  
- Empirical results, especially co-decoding gains and comparisons to prior work, are compelling.  
- Experimental setup and ablation studies are detailed and reproducible.  
- Provides practical insight that annotation projection tends to favor precision while representation projection favors recall.  
- Core techniques are not novel individually; novelty lies in their combination and the data selection refinement.  
- Method heavily depends on the quality of the English NER system, which could affect transfer performance.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of failure modes and common error patterns by entity type.  
- Co-decoding schemes (confidence- and rank-based) are relatively simple; more sophisticated ensembling is not explored.  
- Scalability is limited by the need for (comparable or parallel) corpora for annotation projection.  
- Asks about sensitivity of representation projection to dictionary size/quality and requests experiments varying these factors.  
- Inquires whether different thresholds for the data selection heuristic were explored to balance precision/recall.  
- Requests qualitative error analysis to identify frequently misclassified or missed entity types.  
- Suggests incorporating additional features (entity length, context) into co-decoding beyond model confidences.  
- Asks about exploring alternatives to reduce dependency on comparable corpora, such as MT or distant supervision.  
- Recommends adding a table comparing computational cost of each approach.  
- Notes the paper is well-written but suggests updating references to include more recent cross-lingual embeddings and NER work.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with practical gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
