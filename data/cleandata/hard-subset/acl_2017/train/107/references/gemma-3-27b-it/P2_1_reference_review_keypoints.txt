- Proposes two approaches for zero-target cross-lingual NER: improved annotation projection with data selection, and representation projection using cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-based outputs.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over prior weakly supervised methods, nearing supervised performance in some settings.  
- Effective data selection heuristic reduces noise from comparable corpora and yields measurable gains.  
- Combining distinct transfer methods via co-decoding provides additional performance improvements.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive: multiple languages, in-house and CoNLL datasets, and comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Paper is well-motivated, focusing on a practical low-resource NER problem.  
- Methodological details (CRFs, neural nets, data selection, co-decoding) are sufficiently described for reproducibility.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with a coherent presentation of problem and approaches.  
- Individual components (annotation and representation projection) lack strong novelty; main contribution is their combination and the selection heuristic.  
- Annotation projection remains heavily dependent on word alignment quality, which could limit real-world applicability.  
- Co-decoding schemes, particularly the rank-based one, are complex and would benefit from clearer intuition or examples.  
- Analysis of failure cases is limited; paper lacks detailed error patterns across entity types or linguistic phenomena.  
- Questions about alignment robustness and whether alignment quality metrics were integrated into data selection remain unanswered.  
- Unclear how data selection parameters (`q`, `n`) were tuned and whether a dev set was used.  
- Request for a concrete example illustrating the rank-based co-decoding decision process to clarify why it outperforms the confidence-based scheme.  
- Authors are asked to provide error analysis detailing residual system mistakes and challenging entity types/constructions.  
- Suggestion to report computational resources for training and inference to assess practicality.  
- Query about the rationale or tuning behind chosen embedding dimensions (300/800) and whether alternatives were tested.  
- Overall assessment: strong accept — pragmatic combination and heuristic deliver significant gains, but addressing alignment robustness and deeper error analysis would strengthen the paper.
