- Paper proposes weakly supervised cross-lingual NER transferring from English via annotation projection with a data-selection heuristic and representation projection with cross-lingual embeddings, plus co-decoding to combine them.
- Main contribution: a data selection heuristic that improves annotation projection quality, especially with poor alignment.
- Main contribution: co-decoding schemes (rank-based) to combine annotation and representation projection, yielding consistent gains.
- Main contribution: comprehensive evaluation across multiple languages and datasets (in-house and CoNLL).
- Strength: tackles an important problem of reducing annotation needs for multilingual NER.
- Strength: practical and effective data-selection method that mitigates alignment noise.
- Strength: strong empirical results outperforming baselines and prior work across languages.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with detailed parameters and results.
- Strength: reasonable potential for reproducibility given provided methodological detail.
- Weakness: individual components (annotation/representation projection) are not highly novel; novelty mainly in data selection and co-decoding.
- Weakness: annotation projection still depends heavily on parallel/comparable corpus alignment quality.
- Weakness: insufficient justification/analysis for chosen embedding mapping dictionary size (5–6k pairs).
- Weakness: use of 2nd-order MEMM feels outdated; neural models might perform better.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—doesn’t detail error types or failure modes of each approach.
- Question: how were data selection parameters q and n tuned (grid search or other)?
- Question: how did different initializations of the linear mapping M_f affect performance?
- Question: what computational resources were required for training and experiments?
- Question: what errors remain after data selection—specific entity types or linguistic phenomena challenging?
- Question: will the authors release code and data to support reproducibility?
- Additional comment: reference to “Figure 1” is incorrect or missing.
- Additional comment: suggest clarifying the term “co-decoding” as “output combination” early on.
- Overall assessment: solid, practical contribution with minor issues; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
