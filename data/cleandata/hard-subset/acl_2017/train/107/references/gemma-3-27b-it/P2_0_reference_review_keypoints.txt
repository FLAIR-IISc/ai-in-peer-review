- Paper tackles cross-lingual NER with no target annotations using annotation projection and representation projection, plus co-decoding to combine them.
- Proposes a data selection heuristic to improve annotation projection quality from comparable corpora.
- Uses cross-lingual word embeddings to transfer an English-trained NER model (representation projection).
- Combines projection methods via co-decoding schemes to leverage complementary strengths.
- Extensive evaluation across multiple languages and datasets (in-house and CoNLL) shows strong empirical validation.
- Ablation studies clarify contributions of different model variants and co-decoding strategies.
- Practical and relevant approach for low-resource cross-lingual NER scenarios.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful result tables.
- Directly addresses alignment noise in annotation projection with a practical selection strategy.
- Demonstrates competitive performance, improving over weakly supervised baselines and nearing supervised results.
- Individual components (annotation and representation projection) are not novel; novelty is mainly in their adaptation and combination.
- Reliance on English as the source language may introduce bias; sensitivity to source choice is not analyzed.
- Representation projection depends heavily on pre-trained embeddings; impact of embedding quality and training details is underexplored.
- Co-decoding schemes are somewhat simplistic; more sophisticated fusion or learned weighting could improve results.
- Questions about sensitivity to source language choice and experiments with other source languages remain unanswered.
- Unclear how embedding quality was assessed and whether different initialization strategies were tried.
- Need more detail on tuning and sensitivity of data selection parameters (q and n) across languages.
- Unclear whether more advanced co-decoding alternatives were explored and how they performed.
- Suggests adding error analysis to characterize residual system errors and their causes.
- Figures referenced in the paper (e.g., Figure 1) were missing and should be included.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed work with practical contributions and strong evaluation; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
