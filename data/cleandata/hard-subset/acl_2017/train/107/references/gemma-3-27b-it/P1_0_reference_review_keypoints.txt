- Presents a weakly supervised approach to cross-lingual NER transferring from English using annotation projection with data selection and representation projection with cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Proposes a practical data selection heuristic that improves noisy annotation projection from comparable corpora.  
- Introduces co-decoding schemes to effectively combine annotation projection and representation projection methods.  
- Demonstrates superior performance on in-house and CoNLL datasets across multiple languages (JP, KR, DE, PT, ES, NL).  
- Addresses a relevant low-resource cross-lingual NER problem with practical solutions.  
- Clearly explains methods and implementation details, aiding reproducibility.  
- Shows strong empirical gains over baselines and prior cross-lingual NER systems.  
- Evaluates comprehensively across diverse languages and datasets, improving generalizability.  
- Focuses on realistic comparable corpora scenarios where parallel data is scarce.  
- Individual components (projection, embeddings, CRFs, neural nets) are established techniques, so novelty is mainly in integration and adaptation.  
- Reliance on English as a single pivot may limit performance for linguistically distant target languages.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis to characterize system failure modes and guide future improvements.  
- Needs deeper analysis of alignment quality and its effect on projection performance.  
- Asks whether performance varies with linguistic distance to English and whether multi-pivot or direct transfer was considered.  
- Requests error analysis for annotation projection, representation projection, and the combined system.  
- Seeks clarification on how alignment tool/score differences impact projection results.  
- Requests information on computational cost and scalability, especially for cross-lingual embedding training.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and well-referenced, with minor opportunities to reduce repetition.  
- Recommends acceptance based on practical heuristic, effective combination, and comprehensive evaluation.
