- Paper proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER combining annotation projection with a data selection heuristic and representation projection via cross-lingual embeddings, plus a co-decoding scheme.
- Main contribution: a practical data selection heuristic for annotation projection that improves performance in low-resource settings.
- Main contribution: integration of annotation and representation projection with a rank-based co-decoding that effectively combines outputs.
- Main contribution: comprehensive evaluation across in-house and CoNLL datasets showing improvements over prior methods.
- Strength: addresses an important real-world problem of NER for low-resource languages.
- Strength: the data selection heuristic demonstrably improves results, especially when alignment quality is poor.
- Strength: solid experimental setup with comparisons to relevant prior work and multiple languages/datasets.
- Strength: clear presentation and logical organization make the methods and experiments easy to follow.
- Strength: practical, modular approach using readily available resources (embeddings, parallel corpora).
- Weakness: limited novelty—core techniques are existing methods combined and optimized rather than fundamentally new.
- Weakness: annotation projection still depends on alignment quality; mitigation strategies for extremely noisy alignment are underexplored.
- Weakness: co-decoding rationale (prioritizing annotation projection) lacks detailed justification and error-profile analysis.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—missing insights on error types and challenging entity categories.
- Weakness: lack of ablation studies to quantify individual component contributions (data selection, smoothing layer, etc.).
- Question: how to handle scenarios with very low alignment quality beyond data selection—e.g., use of alignment confidence in training?
- Question: request for detailed justification and error-profile evidence for prioritizing annotation projection in co-decoding.
- Question: request for a detailed error analysis identifying common error types and avenues for improvement.
- Question: request for ablation studies quantifying contributions of data selection heuristic and NN2 smoothing.
- Question: what is the computational cost of representation projection for large-vocabulary languages and its scalability?
- Additional comments: notation and terminology are consistent, references relevant and up-to-date, and English is generally good.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with valuable empirical contributions; recommend acceptance pending addressing weaknesses (justifications, error analysis, ablations).
