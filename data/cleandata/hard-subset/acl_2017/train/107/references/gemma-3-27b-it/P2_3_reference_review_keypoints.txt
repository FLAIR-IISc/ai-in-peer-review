- Tackles cross-lingual NER without target annotations using annotation projection with data selection, representation projection via cross-lingual embeddings, and co-decoding to combine them.
- Shows that a simple data selection heuristic significantly improves annotation projection quality under noisy alignments.
- Introduces co-decoding schemes to combine annotation and representation projections, leveraging their complementary strengths.
- Provides comprehensive evaluation on in-house and CoNLL datasets across multiple languages, demonstrating improvements over prior weakly supervised methods.
- Clearly defines the problem and motivates the proposed approaches.
- Proposes a practical, frequency-based data selection heuristic that is intuitive and effective, especially in low-resource scenarios.
- Reports strong, statistically significant experimental results and valuable comparisons on CoNLL data.
- Offers thorough ablation studies and analysis (e.g., NN1 vs NN2, co-decoding variants) to clarify component contributions.
- Supplies enough experimental and model detail to support reproducibility.
- Individual components (projection methods and co-decoding) are not fundamentally novel; novelty is mainly in their application and combination.
- Methods rely on external resources (parallel corpora, alignments, dictionaries); sensitivity to resource quality is not fully analyzed.
- Paper presentation is dense and could be shortened; some detailed sections should be moved to an appendix.
- Lacks a detailed error analysis to reveal which entity types or error sources remain problematic.
- Requests analysis of how performance changes with decreasing alignment accuracy or smaller dictionaries.
- Asks for a detailed error breakdown to identify common failure modes and areas for improvement.
- Suggests the authors indicate which sections can be condensed or moved to appendices to improve clarity.
- Requests clarification on computational cost and scalability of the representation projection (linear mapping) for many target languages.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — solid, well-executed work that would be stronger with clearer presentation and additional error/sensitivity analyses.
