- Summary: Proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approach using annotation projection with a novel data selection scheme, representation projection via cross-lingual embeddings, and co-decoding to combine them, showing improvements on in-house and CoNLL datasets.

- Contribution: Novel heuristic for effective data selection in annotation projection that mitigates noise from comparable corpora.

- Contribution: Combines annotation projection and representation projection through co-decoding to leverage complementary strengths.

- Contribution: Thorough experimental validation across multiple languages and datasets demonstrating improved performance.

- Strength: Addresses a relevant low-resource NER problem with a practical, weakly supervised solution.

- Strength: Methodology is sound, adapting established principles (alignment handling, embeddings) effectively.

- Strength: Strong empirical results, with notable gains on in-house data and competitive CoNLL performance.

- Strength: Clear presentation with implementation and experimental details aiding reproducibility.

- Strength: Careful exploration of multiple co-decoding strategies to maximize combined-system benefits.

- Weakness: Individual components lack fundamental novelty; main contribution is their integration and adaptation.

- Weakness: Representation projection depends heavily on phrase-table quality; alternatives are not explored.

- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of data selection and model components.

- Weakness: Limited error analysis; unclear which entity types or cases cause failures.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of parameter sensitivity (e.g., data-selection thresholds, embedding dims).

- Question: How does phrase-table size/quality affect representation projection, and were non-phrase-table methods tested?

- Question: How were data-selection parameters ‘q’ and ‘n’ tuned, and how sensitive are results across languages?

- Question: Can the authors provide an error analysis highlighting common failure modes?

- Question: What is the computational cost of training/applying these methods versus supervised training?

- Question: How well does the approach scale to languages with poor alignment resources?

- Comment: Add a table summarizing training data size per language and method.

- Comment: Discuss limitations and future directions (e.g., character-level features, more advanced co-decoding).

- Overall: Solid, practical work with strong validation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
