- Paper tackles zero-target-language cross-lingual NER using annotation projection and representation projection plus co-decoding.
- Proposes a data selection heuristic for annotation projection that prioritizes high-quality projected labels via entity frequency analysis.
- Proposes representation projection by mapping target word representations into the source embedding space for direct model transfer.
- Explores co-decoding strategies to combine annotation projection and representation projection outputs.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over prior methods on multiple languages and datasets (in-house and CoNLL).
- Effective data selection is a valuable practical contribution that mitigates noise from alignment errors.
- Systematic evaluation of combining approaches provides useful practical insights and shows complementary strengths.
- Thorough experimental setup and comparisons to established baselines (e.g., Täckström et al., Tsai et al.) strengthen validity.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and easy to reproduce.
- Strong empirical results with significant gains, especially from the data selection scheme.
- Limited novelty in individual components; main innovation is integration and optimization of existing techniques.
- Annotation projection still fundamentally depends on alignment quality; limits under very poor alignments are not explored.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (q and n) and tuning process are under-detailed; no ablation study on their effects.
- Limited error analysis—paper focuses on F1 scores without detailing common error types or per-entity weaknesses.
- Questions for authors: clarify how their representation projection differs from prior alignment methods.
- Questions for authors: explain how q and n thresholds were chosen and whether they vary by language.
- Questions for authors: is there a quantifiable alignment-quality threshold for data selection effectiveness and were alignment confidences used?
- Questions for authors: provide qualitative error analysis to identify challenging entity types for each approach.
- Additional comments: some figures referenced (e.g., Figure 1) are missing from the submission.
- Additional comments: citation list is extensive and could be slightly condensed.
- Overall recommendation: accept, given effective integration, useful heuristic, and comprehensive evaluation, despite limited novelty.
