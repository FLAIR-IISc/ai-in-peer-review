- Tackles cross-lingual NER without target-language annotations using annotation and representation projection.  
- Proposes a data selection heuristic to reduce noise from imperfect annotation projection.  
- Uses cross-lingual word embeddings for representation projection.  
- Introduces co-decoding schemes (including a rank-based scheme) to combine both systems.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over existing methods on in-house and CoNLL datasets.  
- Practical data selection heuristic is a significant, effective contribution for noisy corpora.  
- Combining annotation and representation projections via co-decoding yields clear performance gains.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across multiple languages and datasets provides a solid benchmark.  
- Work addresses an important problem by reducing reliance on labeled target-language data.  
- Methodology is logical; frequency-based scoring for projection quality is reasonable.  
- Representation projection leverages established cross-lingual embedding techniques effectively.  
- Results consistently outperform baselines, notably on CoNLL data.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with clear explanations.  
- Comparison to prior work (Tä cks tröm et al., Tsai et al.) is thorough and informative.  
- Individual components lack strong novelty; main novelty is in data selection and co-decoding.  
- Annotation projection still depends heavily on alignment quality; impact under very poor alignment is underexplored.  
- Feature description for the MEMM is sparse; more detail and justification for feature choices needed.  
- Hyperparameter tuning process lacks transparency (search space and metrics not detailed).  
- Error analysis is insufficient; unclear which entity types or phenomena remain challenging.  
- Questions about MEMM features, sensitivity to alignment, hyperparameter tuning details, and detailed error analysis remain.  
- Authors chose not to use gazetteers or character-level features; rationale and potential impact should be discussed.  
- Suggestion to mention noise-robust learning or domain adaptation related work and clarify "unk" vector handling.  
- Overall assessment: solid, practical, and effective work recommending acceptance with minor revisions.
