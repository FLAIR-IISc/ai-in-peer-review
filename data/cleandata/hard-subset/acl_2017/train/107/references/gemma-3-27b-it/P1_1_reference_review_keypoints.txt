- Paper proposes a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approach combining annotation projection with a heuristic data selection, representation projection via embedding mapping, and co-decoding to merge outputs.  
- Data selection heuristic (considering annotation quality and entity count) substantially improves annotation projection, especially in low-resource or noisy alignment settings.  
- Representation projection projects word embeddings across languages to enable direct model transfer.  
- Co-decoding schemes effectively combine annotation- and representation-based outputs, yielding further performance gains.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive, using multiple languages and both in-house and CoNLL datasets for robust comparison.  
- Methodology is clearly described and reproducible, with NER models (CRFs, MEMMs, neural) detailed.  
- Empirical results show significant improvements, favorably comparing to prior work (e.g., Täckström et al., Tsai et al.).  
- Work focuses on practical challenges (noisy data, alignment errors) and offers actionable solutions for real-world cross-lingual NER.  
- Main novelty is incremental: individual components are not fundamentally new, with the data selection heuristic being the most original contribution.  
- Annotation projection approach remains dependent on parallel alignment quality; paper lacks deep analysis for very poor alignments.  
- Representation projection lacks analysis of failure cases (semantic/structural mismatches and which words are poorly projected).  
- Data selection performance is sensitive to parameters q and n, but the paper does not systematically analyze or provide guidance for choosing them.  
- Supervised baseline details (source, size, annotation guidelines) are insufficient for fair comparison.  
- Questions for authors: provide error analysis for representation projection, sensitivity analysis for q and n, quantify correlation between alignment accuracy and selection gains, and clarify which design choices drove performance.  
- Additional notes: tables present results well; minor grammatical fixes needed; discuss computational cost of approaches.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, practically valuable work with thorough evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
