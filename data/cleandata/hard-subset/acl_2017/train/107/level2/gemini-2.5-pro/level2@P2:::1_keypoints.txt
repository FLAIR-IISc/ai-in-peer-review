- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with a novel data-selection heuristic, and representation projection via a linear embedding mapping, plus two co-decoding schemes to combine them.  
- Combined system achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL benchmarks and outperforms recent baselines.

- Contribution 1: Robust combined system that outperforms prior SOTA on CoNLL datasets.  
- Contribution 2: Simple, effective data selection heuristic that substantially reduces noise from projection (large F1 gains for low-quality alignments).  
- Contribution 3: Thorough empirical evaluation across seven target languages and two NER setups (in-house fine-grained and CoNLL).

- Strength: Strong empirical results and clear SOTA performance on Spanish, Dutch, and German.  
- Strength: Practical and effective data selection method that yields large improvements for noisy alignments (e.g., Japanese, Korean).  
- Strength: Comprehensive, rigorous evaluation including multiple languages, datasets, and supervised baselines.  
- Strength: Insightful combination of complementary transfer paradigms (data-level and model-level) with analysis of precision/recall trade-offs.

- Weakness: Representation projection lacks novelty—direct application of Mikolov et al. (2013b) linear mapping—should be framed as application/evaluation rather than a new method.  
- Weakness: No sensitivity analysis for data-selection hyperparameters (q, n) or guidance for setting them in true zero-resource settings.  
- Weakness: SOTA comparison details are incomplete—insufficient context on which prior numbers/resources were used and comparability of experimental conditions.

- Q1: How were (q, n) tuned for CoNLL—were they selected on the dev set and if so, how was overfitting avoided?  
- Q2: Why do NN architectures differ in performance across datasets (NN2 better on in-house, NN1 on CoNLL)—domain, entity-type count, or training size?  
- Q3: Why do preferred co-decoding schemes differ by dataset (rank-based vs. confidence-based), and what guidance can you offer for choosing a scheme for new tasks?
