- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with a novel data-selection heuristic, and representation projection mapping target embeddings into the source space.
- Shows that combining the two methods via co-decoding yields state-of-the-art results.
- The data-selection heuristic for annotation projection is simple but substantially boosts performance, especially in low-resource/poor-alignment settings.
- Thorough evaluation on a large fine-grained in-house dataset and the CoNLL benchmark demonstrates robustness and generality.
- Combined system outperforms two strong contemporary cross-lingual NER baselines on CoNLL.
- Effectively combines orthogonal approaches and exploits their complementary precision/recall trade-offs through co-decoding.
- Insufficient analysis of why rank-based vs. confidence-based co-decoding perform differently across datasets.
- Lacks discussion of limitations and dependencies (quality/availability of comparable corpora, alignment models, and seed dictionaries) and sensitivity to these resources.
- NN2 architecture (smoothing layer / weighted average of prototype vectors) is under-motivated and under-explained, hindering understanding and reproducibility.
- Request for authors to explain the intuition behind differing co-decoding performance on in-house vs. CoNLL datasets.
- Request for analysis of representation projection sensitivity to the size and noise level of the seed dictionary.
