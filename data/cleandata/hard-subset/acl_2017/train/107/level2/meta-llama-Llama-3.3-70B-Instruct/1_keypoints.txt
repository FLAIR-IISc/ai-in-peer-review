- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping, plus two co-decoding schemes to combine them.
- Contribution: a heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that selects higher-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.
- Contribution: a representation-projection method that maps target-language embeddings into a source space to enable a single NER model to handle multiple languages.
- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes that combine outputs from both projection approaches and improve accuracy over individual systems.
- Strength: effectively leverages comparable corpora as a resource for cross-lingual NER, beneficial for low-resource languages.
- Strength: achieves higher accuracy than prior cross-lingual NER methods, particularly for languages with limited resources.
- Strength: representation projection offers flexibility to add new languages without needing separate embeddings for every language pair.
- Weakness: annotation projection depends on alignment accuracy, which may be poor for some language pairs and limit performance.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a small set of languages and datasets, raising questions about generality across scripts and typologies.
- Weakness: co-decoding schemes increase system complexity, potentially hindering practical deployment.
- Question: How do the methods perform on languages with very different scripts or grammatical structures?
- Question: Can the representation projection approach be extended to other NLP tasks beyond NER, and if so how?
- Question: How do the co-decoding schemes resolve conflicts between the two projection approaches and what are the implications for overall performance?
