- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with entity-tag frequency-based data selection, and direct model transfer via linear projection of target embeddings into English space.
- Introduces two co-decoding strategies (exclude-O confidence and rank-based) to combine the two systems' outputs.
- Experiments on large in-house (50+ types) and CoNLL (4 types) datasets across languages show co-decoding outperforms individual methods and prior baselines.
- Contribution: A simple, effective co-decoding framework that consistently improves over projection-based systems and prior cross-lingual baselines.
- Contribution: A practical data selection criterion for annotation projection that substantially boosts performance under noisy alignments (e.g., +13.9 and +20.0 F1 for Japanese/Korean).
- Contribution: A weighted least-squares cross-lingual embedding mapping enabling zero-shot NER transfer using monolingual embeddings and small seed dictionaries.
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence across languages and settings, with consistent and often sizable improvements.
- Strength: Clear analysis of precision/recall profiles and principled co-decoding designs tailored to complementary errors.
- Strength: Practicality—requires no target-language annotations and uses accessible resources (comparable corpora, phrase-table-derived dictionaries).
- Strength: Useful ablations showing impact of data selection thresholds, alignment quality, and comparison between two NN architectures.
- Weakness: Dependence on alignment models and phrase tables limits applicability to truly low-resource languages.
- Weakness: Data selection heuristic relies on surface-form entity frequency, potentially biasing toward frequent entities and headwords.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of performance on rare/ambiguous entities and morphology-rich languages.
- Weakness: Reproducibility details (exact corpora, dictionary construction, full hyperparameters) and statistical significance testing are not provided.
- Weakness: Embedding mapping is linear and unconstrained; no comparison to orthogonal Procrustes, normalization, or modern multilingual contextual baselines.
- Question: How sensitive are results to dictionary size/quality and to enforcing orthogonality in the mapping?
- Question: Can the authors provide calibration analyses and significance tests for co-decoding gains?
- Question: How does the selection heuristic affect recall for rare entities, and can morphology/character features mitigate biases?
- Suggestion: Evaluate stronger mapping variants and add character/subword features; consider calibration or learning-to-rank for co-decoding.
- Suggestion: Release detailed reproducibility artifacts (code, data selection scripts) and include comparisons to recent multilingual pretrained baselines.
