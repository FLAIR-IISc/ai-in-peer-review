- Study of weakly supervised cross-lingual NER with no target-language human annotation using two projection approaches plus co-decoding.

- Main contribution: rank- and confidence-based co-decoding that combines high-precision annotation projection (AP) with balanced representation projection (RP), yielding consistent F1 gains and beating prior systems on CoNLL.

- Main contribution: annotation projection with a simple, effective frequency-based sentence-quality heuristic and entity-count threshold that greatly improves noisy-alignment performance (e.g., +13.9 F1 JP, +20.0 F1 KO).

- Main contribution: representation projection via weighted least-squares mapping of target embeddings into English and a smoothing NN (NN2) that improves precision/recall balance over a plain embedding model (NN1).

- Strength: clear empirical gains across many languages and settings, with consistent co-decoding improvements over constituent systems.

- Strength: practical, language-agnostic data selection for projection that substantially mitigates poor alignment quality.

- Strength: sensible design exploiting complementary error profiles of AP (high precision, low recall) and RP (more balanced), motivating the rank-based co-decoder.

- Strength: resource-efficient RP transfer that avoids per-language retraining and requires only small seed dictionaries and monolingual embeddings.

- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on CoNLL (4 entity types) and in-house (>50 types) datasets with full P/R/F1 reporting and comparisons to strong baselines.

- Weakness: limited novelty in representation projection; uses weighted linear regression similar to prior work without exploring orthogonality/Procrustes or alternatives.

- Weakness: insufficient reproducibility details (corpora sources, alignment training, NN hyperparameters, NN2 prototype mechanism, q/n threshold selection).

- Weakness: fairness/comparability issues—unequal projection-labeled training sizes across languages and different model classes used across approaches complicate attribution of gains.

- Weakness: limited baselines on CoNLL; missing comparisons to other contemporary cross-lingual embedding mappings and simple dictionary-based transfer methods.

- Weakness: shallow analysis—no breakdowns by entity type/length/OOV, no study of type-inventory mismatch, and limited robustness analysis to dictionary size or domain mismatch.

- Question: provide sources/domains/statistics for comparable corpora and alignment construction, including overlap and alignment error types.

- Question: detail NN2—number of prototype vectors, initialization, update rules, and exact computation from embeddings to prototype-weighted representation.

- Question: report mapping ablations—unweighted regression, orthogonal/Procrustes mapping, and performance vs. dictionary size (e.g., 1K/3K/10K).

- Question: explain q and n threshold tuning per language—was any target-language supervision used or were thresholds chosen unsupervised?

- Question: report OOV coverage—fractions of tokens/entities OOV for embeddings and dictionary, and effect of unk vector on entity boundary detection.

- Essential suggestion: provide full hyperparameters, training setups, corpora provenance/sizes, NN2 algorithmic description, and release configs/pseudocode to aid reproducibility.

- Essential suggestion: add ablations for representation mapping (weighted vs unweighted, orthogonality) and dictionary size, with at least dev-set results for one language.

- Essential suggestion: expand analysis with per-entity-type P/R/F1, OOV breakdowns, example successes/failures for AP/RP/co-decoding, and significance testing.

- Optional suggestion: try confidence calibration/temperature scaling to normalize scores across systems for co-decoding.

- Optional suggestion: explore agreement-based selection for projection-labeled data to complement the frequency-based heuristic.

- Optional suggestion: discuss limitations for typologically distant/very low-resource languages and plan for releasing selected resources to improve reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: practical, well-executed combination of projection paradigms with clear empirical benefits, but needs clearer details, ablations, and deeper analysis for full reproducibility and attribution.
