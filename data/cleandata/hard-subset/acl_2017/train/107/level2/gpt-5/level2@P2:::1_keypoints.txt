- Tackles zero-annotation cross-lingual NER using two projection-based approaches and their combination.
- Contribution: a heuristic data-selection method for annotation projection scoring sentences by entity-level tag frequency reliability and entity count to improve noisy alignments.
- Contribution: representation projection via a weighted linear mapping from target to English embeddings so a single English NER can run on mapped target embeddings without retraining.
- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based) to merge outputs, improving over individual systems and prior baselines on CoNLL.
- Strong empirical gains across languages and settings, with large improvements when alignment is poor and consistent gains from co-decoding.
- Practical zero-shot setup requiring only comparable corpora with alignments and a small bilingual dictionary; no target-language annotation or retraining needed for representation projection.
- Sensible, transparent engineering: intuitive annotation selection metric, weighted least-squares mapping, and simple effective co-decoding; precision/recall breakdowns show complementary model behaviors.
- Broad evaluation across multiple languages, scripts, morphologies, and two task settings (fine-grained in-house and CoNLL).
- Reproducibility concerns: sources and sizes of comparable corpora, alignment models, and phrase tables/dictionaries are not clearly specified.
- Potential tuning on test sets for in-house selection: unclear whether thresholds (q, n) were chosen on held-out dev sets or the test sets, risking optimistic results.
- Limited methodological novelty: proposed techniques are incremental over previous annotation projection and embedding-based transfer work.
- Insufficient ablations and analysis on mapping constraints, dictionary size/weighting, embedding choices, architecture effects, and sensitivity to alignment and thresholds.
- Risks and biases in selection heuristic: may favor frequent entities, propagate English NER/aligner errors, and devalue rare entities, harming recall and long-tail categories.
- Question: clarify how (q, n) were tuned for in-house languages and whether a held-out dev set was used.
- Question: provide exact sources and sizes of comparable corpora and alignment models and whether phrase tables/dictionaries can be released.
- Question: did you try orthogonally constrained mapping (e.g., Procrustes) or normalization, and how did it compare to weighted least squares?
- Question: explain why NN2 helps in-house but not CoNLL—due to entity granularity, domain, or training size?
- Question: provide sensitivity analysis for dictionary size and weighting, e.g., F1 vs dictionary size and ablation removing weights.
- Suggestion: add character/subword features to the English NN to better handle OOVs and cross-script transfer, especially for Japanese/Korean.
- Suggestion: consider learned calibration or stacking (meta-classifier on span/conflict features) for co-decoding instead of fixed heuristics.
- Suggestion: report per-entity-type results and confusion matrices to show which categories benefit from each component.
- Minor requests: clarify entity inventory differences from CoNLL, conflict resolution when tags disagree on matching spans, and OOV handling/unk vector training for representation projection.
