**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

The paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) with no human annotation in the target language. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic data selection scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word representations in vector space (word embeddings) are projected from a target language to a source language to create a universal representation of the words in different languages. Two co-decoding schemes are also designed to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches.

**Main Contributions**

1. A heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection approach to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora.
2. A representation projection approach for direct NER model transfer based on projecting word representations in vector space from a target language to a source language.
3. Two co-decoding schemes to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach for cross-lingual NER with no human annotation in the target language, which is a challenging task.
2. The heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection approach is effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and improving the accuracy of the target-language NER system.
3. The representation projection approach is able to project word representations from a target language to a source language, enabling the use of a single NER system for multiple languages.
4. The co-decoding schemes are able to combine the strengths of the two projection-based approaches and achieve higher accuracy than both individual systems.

**Weaknesses**

1. The annotation projection approach relies on the quality of the alignment information between the source and target languages, which may not always be accurate.
2. The representation projection approach assumes that the word embeddings in the source and target languages are similar, which may not always be the case.
3. The co-decoding schemes are not thoroughly evaluated, and it is not clear which scheme is more effective in different scenarios.
4. The paper does not provide a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the issue of alignment accuracy in the annotation projection approach?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the training dictionary used for cross-lingual word embedding mappings?
3. How do the authors plan to evaluate the effectiveness of the co-decoding schemes in different scenarios?
4. Can the authors provide a more thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches?

**Additional Comments**

The paper proposes a novel approach for cross-lingual NER with no human annotation in the target language. While the approach shows promising results, there are several limitations and areas for improvement that need to be addressed. The authors should provide more details on the training dictionary used for cross-lingual word embedding mappings and evaluate the effectiveness of the co-decoding schemes in different scenarios. Additionally, a more thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches would be beneficial.
