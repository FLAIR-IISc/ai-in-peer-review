- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods requiring no target-language human annotation: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping.
- Introduces a heuristic data selection scheme to pick high-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora.
- Proposes representation projection that maps target-language word embeddings into a source-language space for direct NER model transfer.
- Designs two co-decoding schemes to combine outputs of annotation- and representation-based systems.
- Novel approach addressing the challenging problem of cross-lingual NER without target annotations.
- Heuristic data selection effectively improves quality of projection-labeled training data and target NER accuracy.
- Representation projection enables reuse of a single NER system across multiple languages via embedding mapping.
- Co-decoding combines strengths of both approaches and yields higher accuracy than either alone.
- Annotation projection depends on alignment quality, which may be unreliable.
- Representation projection assumes cross-lingual embedding similarity, which may not hold for all language pairs.
- Co-decoding schemes lack thorough evaluation; relative effectiveness across scenarios is unclear.
- Paper lacks comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods.
- Requests clarification on how alignment accuracy issues will be addressed in annotation projection.
- Asks for more details about the training dictionary used for cross-lingual embedding mapping.
- Seeks plans for evaluating co-decoding effectiveness across different scenarios.
- Requests a more thorough comparison with other leading cross-lingual NER approaches.
- Recommends providing more details on training dictionaries and broader evaluation of co-decoding.
- Suggests expanding comparisons to state-of-the-art methods to better position the contribution.
