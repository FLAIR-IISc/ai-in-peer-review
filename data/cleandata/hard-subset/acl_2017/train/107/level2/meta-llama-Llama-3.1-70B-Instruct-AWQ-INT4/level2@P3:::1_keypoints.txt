- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods requiring no target-language human annotation: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs for improved accuracy.  
- Main contribution: a heuristic selection scheme that filters good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora.  
- Main contribution: a representation-projection method that maps target-language word embeddings into English space to apply an English NER model without retraining.  
- Main contribution: two co-decoding strategies that outperform individual projection systems.  
- Strength: eliminates need for target-language annotations, enabling NER in low-resource languages.  
- Strength: data selection in annotation projection yields significant accuracy gains over unfiltered projection data.  
- Strength: representation projection enables direct model transfer without retraining target-language models.  
- Strength: co-decoding effectively merges outputs from both systems to boost performance.  
- Weakness: assumes the source (English) NER system is accurate, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: annotation projection depends on the quality of comparable corpora, which can be noisy and degrade performance.  
- Weakness: representation projection needs large monolingual corpora to train embeddings, limiting applicability to very low-resource languages.  
- Weakness: co-decoding may fail when the two projection systems produce very different outputs.  
- Question: how will the method handle inaccurate source-language NER systems?  
- Question: provide details on construction of the comparable corpora used for annotation projection.  
- Question: how to adapt when the target language has limited monolingual data for embedding training?  
- Question: please detail how co-decoding parameters were tuned.  
- Additional comment: approach is promising for low-resource NER but must address noisy corpora, limited monolingual data, and unreliable source systems.
