- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via cross-lingual embedding mapping.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs for improved accuracy.  
- Main contribution: a heuristic data selection scheme that filters noisy comparable corpora to yield higher-quality projection-labeled NER training data.  
- Main contribution: a representation-projection method that maps target-language word embeddings into source space to apply a source NER model without re-training.  
- Main contribution: co-decoding strategies that outperform each individual projection approach on CoNLL test data.  
- Strength: effectively leverages noisy comparable corpora through a practical data selection heuristic.  
- Strength: achieves higher cross-lingual NER accuracy than two state-of-the-art baselines on evaluated datasets.  
- Strength: representation projection is flexible and scalable, easing addition of new languages without pairwise embeddings.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a small set of languages (Ja, Ko, De, Pt, Es, Nl) and datasets (in-house, CoNLL).  
- Weakness: annotation projection depends on alignment quality, which may hurt performance for low-resource language pairs.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis to explain mechanisms and limitations of the proposed methods.  
- Question: how will the methods generalize to more languages and diverse datasets?  
- Question: what are the specifics of the training dictionary used for cross-lingual embedding mapping?  
- Question: how do the authors plan to mitigate reliance on alignment accuracy in annotation projection?  
- Additional remark: approaches are promising but require broader evaluation and deeper analysis to fully validate strengths and limitations.
