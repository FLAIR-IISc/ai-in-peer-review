- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods requiring no target-language annotations: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine the annotation- and representation-projection outputs.  
- Combined system achieves higher accuracy than each individual method and outperforms two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.

- Contribution: effective annotation projection method with heuristic selection to extract high-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.  
- Contribution: representation projection approach that maps target-language word embeddings into English space for direct NER model transfer.  
- Contribution: two co-decoding schemes that improve performance when combining both projection-based approaches.

- Strength: introduces two novel weakly supervised approaches enabling NER for multiple target languages without human annotation.  
- Strength: experimental results show the combined system outperforms individual methods and existing cross-lingual baselines.  
- Strength: provides detailed analysis of strengths and weaknesses of the two approaches and co-decoding schemes useful for future work.

- Weakness: assumes a well-trained English (source) NER system, which may not hold for all languages or domains.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to six target languages (Japanese, Korean, German, Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch), which may not represent broader linguistic diversity.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability of the approaches and co-decoding schemes.

- Question: how can the approaches be extended to languages with limited resources or no available NER systems?  
- Question: can co-decoding be improved by incorporating language-specific features or domain knowledge?  
- Question: how can computational complexity be reduced to make the methods more scalable for large-scale NLP applications?
