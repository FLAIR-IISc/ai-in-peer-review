**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) with no human annotation in the target language. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic data selection scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word embeddings in the target language are projected into the English vector space, allowing a single English NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training. Two co-decoding schemes are also proposed to combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. A heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that effectively selects good-quality projection-labeled NER data from comparable corpora.
2. A representation projection approach for direct NER model transfer, which projects word embeddings in the target language into the English vector space.
3. Two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems to produce an output that is more accurate than the outputs of individual systems.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to cross-lingual NER that does not require human annotation in the target language.
2. The annotation projection approach with heuristic data selection scheme shows significant improvement in accuracy compared to no data selection.
3. The representation projection approach allows for direct NER model transfer without re-training, which is a significant advantage over traditional approaches.
4. The co-decoding schemes proposed in the paper effectively combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems, leading to higher accuracy.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the source language NER system is accurate, which may not always be the case.
2. The annotation projection approach relies on the quality of the comparable corpora, which can be noisy and affect the accuracy of the target-language NER system.
3. The representation projection approach requires a large amount of monolingual data in the target language to train the word embeddings, which may not always be available.
4. The co-decoding schemes proposed in the paper may not always work well, especially when the outputs of the two projection-based systems are significantly different.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to handle the case where the source language NER system is not accurate?
2. Can you provide more details on how you constructed the comparable corpora used in the annotation projection approach?
3. How do you plan to handle the case where the target language has limited monolingual data available?
4. Can you provide more details on how you tuned the parameters of the co-decoding schemes?

**Additional Comments**

The paper presents a novel approach to cross-lingual NER that has the potential to improve the accuracy of NER systems in low-resource languages. However, there are several limitations and challenges that need to be addressed, such as handling noisy comparable corpora, limited monolingual data, and inaccurate source language NER systems.
