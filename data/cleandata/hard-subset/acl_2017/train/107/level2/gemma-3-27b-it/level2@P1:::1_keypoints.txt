- Contribution: Proposes a heuristic data selection for annotation projection that uses frequency counts to improve weak labels from comparable corpora.
- Contribution: Introduces representation projection via cross-lingual embedding mapping and compares two neural architectures (NN1, NN2).
- Contribution: Designs and evaluates two co-decoding schemes, notably a rank-based method prioritizing annotation projection.

- Strength: Extensive experiments on in-house and CoNLL datasets across multiple target languages enable robust evaluation.
- Strength: Clear, well-explained methodology for annotation projection, representation projection, and data selection heuristic.
- Strength: Strong baselines and comparisons to prior work (Täckström et al., Tsai et al.), achieving improved CoNLL performance.
- Strength: Practical relevance for building NER without target-language annotations, valuable for low-resource languages.
- Strength: Detailed ablation studies (NN1 vs NN2, co-decoding variants) clarify component contributions.

- Weakness: Lacks qualitative failure analysis to explain error types and why systems (e.g., AP’s high precision/low recall) fail.
- Weakness: Annotation projection remains dependent on alignment quality; impact of very poor alignment is not thoroughly analyzed.
- Weakness: Representation projection’s reliance on a 5K–6K seed dictionary lacks deeper analysis of dictionary size effects and saturation.
- Weakness: Co-decoding schemes may add implementation and tuning complexity; computational cost and practicality are not discussed.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of how language-specific characteristics (morphology, syntax) affect method performance.

- Question: Can the authors provide concrete error examples for annotation and representation projection and indicate consistently misidentified entity types?
- Question: How sensitive is annotation projection to alignment quality and is there a threshold below which it fails?
- Question: What is the performance impact of varying dictionary sizes beyond the 5K–6K range?
- Question: What are the computational costs and implementation complexities of the co-decoding schemes versus single-method approaches?

- Additional: Add discussion of limitations and future work.
- Additional: Include a dataset summary table (size, entity types, language) for clarity.
- Additional: Consider renaming “co-decoding” to a more descriptive term.

- Overall: Strong, well-motivated paper with solid results and recommendation for acceptance.
