- Proposes annotation projection with a novel frequency-based data selection heuristic to improve projection quality.
- Proposes representation projection using cross-lingual word embeddings and a linear mapping.
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes (including a rank-based approach) to combine annotation- and representation-projection outputs.
- Shows improved cross-lingual NER performance on in-house and CoNLL datasets, especially for low-resource languages (Japanese, Korean).
- Practical relevance: addresses building NER systems without labeled target-language data.
- Well-defined heuristic: frequency-count based selection is intuitive and empirically effective.
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple languages, datasets, and comparisons to prior work.
- Effective combination: co-decoding, notably rank-based, leverages complementary strengths of both approaches.
- Clear presentation and good related-work coverage.
- Limited error analysis: lacks detailed breakdown of common mistakes by method or entity type.
- Sensitivity to alignment quality: annotation projection depends on source-target alignment, with limited analysis of its impact.
- Parameter sensitivity unclear: tuning and robustness of selection thresholds (q and n) not thoroughly analyzed.
- Representation projection details limited: linear mapping choice, potential non-linear alternatives, and embedding-dimension rationale underexplained.
- Comparison baseline gap: could include more recent and stronger cross-lingual NER baselines.
- Request for detailed error-type analysis for each approach and co-decoding scheme.
- Request quantification of the relationship between alignment quality and annotation-projection performance.
- Request clarification of tuning procedure and selection of optimal q and n per language.
- Ask whether non-linear mappings for representation projection were tried and what results they yielded.
- Suggest comparing the combined system to newer, stronger baselines.
- Additional comments: clarify “2nd-order MEMM” terminology and provide a visual illustration of co-decoding schemes.
