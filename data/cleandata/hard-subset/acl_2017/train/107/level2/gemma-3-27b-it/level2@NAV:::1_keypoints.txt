- Paper proposes two cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection from comparable corpora and representation projection of embeddings, plus co-decoding to combine them.
- Main novel contribution is a heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that boosts projected label quality via frequency analysis.
- Representation projection is applied to NER and integrated with annotation projection, providing a valuable (though not novel) transfer method.
- Co-decoding, especially the rank-based scheme, is proposed to leverage complementary strengths of both projection methods.
- Thorough experimental evaluation on in-house and CoNLL datasets across multiple languages demonstrates consistent performance gains.
- Ablation studies compare neural architectures and co-decoding methods, clarifying each component’s contribution.
- Comparisons to existing state-of-the-art methods show the approach’s practical significance.
- Limitation: reliance on comparable corpora restricts applicability where such resources are unavailable.
- Performance remains sensitive to comparable corpus quality and alignment accuracy despite the data selection scheme.
- Representation projection discussion lacks depth on limitations of linear mappings for complex cross-lingual semantics.
- Paper would benefit from more detailed error analysis to pinpoint failure cases and guide improvements.
- Related work section could be more comprehensive, particularly regarding recent cross-lingual embedding advances.
- Questions for authors: clarify linear mapping limitations and non-linear alternatives; sensitivity and automatic tuning of thresholds q and n; effect of training dictionary size and point of diminishing returns.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with significant improvements; accept with minor revisions addressing the raised points.
