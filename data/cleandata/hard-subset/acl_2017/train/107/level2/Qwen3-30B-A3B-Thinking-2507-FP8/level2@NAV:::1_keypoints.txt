- Summary: Proposes annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via cross-lingual embeddings, plus two co-decoding schemes; combined systems outperform prior state-of-the-art on multiple languages.
- Contribution 1: A heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that greatly improves performance when alignment accuracy is low (e.g., +13.9 F1 Japanese, +20.0 Korean).
- Contribution 2: A representation projection method that transfers NER models using cross-lingual word embeddings without retraining per target language.
- Contribution 3: Two co-decoding schemes to combine projection outputs, with a rank-based scheme showing particular effectiveness.
- Strength 1: Data selection effectively filters noisy projected labels, addressing limitations of prior annotation projection methods under poor alignment.
- Strength 2: Representation projection offers a simple, language-agnostic transfer approach that avoids per-language retraining.
- Strength 3: Co-decoding, especially rank-based, leverages complementary strengths of both approaches and yields improved performance.
- Weakness 1: Unclear performance for languages with very limited comparable corpora or extremely low alignment accuracy (<60%).
- Weakness 2: Evaluation on only six languages limits evidence of scalability to a broader or lower-resource language set.
- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed analysis explaining why rank-based co-decoding outperforms the confidence-based approach.
- Overall: Well-executed and practically valuable solution with clear experimental validation but somewhat limited evaluation scope.
