- Problem: Build NER systems for new languages without human-annotated data via weakly supervised cross-lingual methods.  
- Methods: Proposes Annotation Projection (with heuristic data selection) and Representation Projection (cross-lingual embedding mapping) to transfer NER.  
- Ensemble: Introduces two co-decoding schemes (confidence-based and rank-based) to combine both approaches and boost accuracy.  
- Evaluation: Tested on in-house and CoNLL datasets, outperforming prior cross-lingual NER systems, especially for low-alignment languages.  
- Contribution 1: Heuristic data selection to improve noisy annotation projection quality.  
- Contribution 2: Novel representation projection using embedding mappings for direct model transfer.  
- Contribution 3: Two effective co-decoding schemes that outperform existing methods.  
- Strength: Practical relevance for low-resource languages by removing need for target-language annotation.  
- Strength: Novelty in representation projection and co-decoding, with rank-based scheme balancing precision/recall.  
- Strength: Empirical rigor with extensive comparisons and consistent improvements.  
- Strength: Scalability of representation projection—no retraining needed for new languages.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of failure cases and why methods underperform for some languages (e.g., German).  
- Weakness: Annotation projection depends heavily on alignment quality; more alternatives for low-alignment scenarios needed.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation domain—mainly newswire, lacks diverse-domain testing (e.g., social media).  
- Weakness: Co-decoding descriptions are dense and need clearer explanations/visuals.  
- Question: How do methods perform across domains beyond newswire?  
- Question: Can representation projection benefit from additional linguistic features (e.g., POS) to improve recall?  
- Question: How sensitive are co-decoding schemes to confidence thresholds or ranking heuristics?  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify co-decoding and analyze limitations.
