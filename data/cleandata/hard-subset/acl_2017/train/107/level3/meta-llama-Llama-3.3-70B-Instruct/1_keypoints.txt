- Proposes weakly supervised NER via English-to-other-language projection but the approach lacks novelty.  
- Main contributions: a data selection scheme using a simple quality-score formula and combining outputs of two systems.  
- Primary technical claim: a fusion learning architecture that combines discriminative and generative models to learn semantic hierarchies.  
- Fusion architecture is language-independent and easily extensible to other languages.  
- Uses a simple lexical structure rule to assist the fusion model.  
- Empirical strengths: 74.20% F1 on a manually labeled test set and 91.60% precision, reportedly outperforming state-of-the-art.  
- Major weakness: overall lack of novelty is a significant concern.  
- Weakness: data selection scheme lacks clarity on how thresholds are calculated.  
- Weakness: the method for combining outputs of two systems is unclear and under-detailed.  
- Weakness: multiple method variants presented but no exploration of combining more than two weakly supervised systems.  
- Weakness: insufficient information about the types and composition of the "in-house" dataset (possible biases).  
- Suggested improvements: clarify threshold calculation and fusion details, explore fusion of more than two systems, and provide in-house dataset descriptions.  
- Questions for authors: how they will address novelty concerns, details on output combination, plans to combine >2 systems, and specifics about the in-house text types.  
- Overall assessment: has notable contributions but needs significant revisions to address the above weaknesses.
