- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping, plus two co-decoding schemes to combine them.
- Heuristic data selection for annotation projection selects high-quality projection-labeled data from comparable corpora, improving annotation projection accuracy.
- Representation projection projects target-language word embeddings into a source-language space for direct NER model transfer.
- Two co-decoding schemes are designed to combine outputs of annotation- and representation-projection systems, boosting combined accuracy.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on both in-house and public NER datasets across multiple target languages.
- The heuristic data selection scheme is effective at selecting high-quality projected training data.
- Representation projection is flexible and applicable for projecting word representations from any language to the source language.
- Paper is well-organized and provides clear explanations and evaluations.
- No clear motivation or justification is given for the proposed CBOW variant or how it benefits the representation projection.
- Evaluation is limited to a few target languages, leaving generalizability to other languages unclear.
- Lacks empirical comparison between the proposed CBOW variant and original CBOW or skip-gram models.
- No statistical significance tests are reported to support claims of improvement from the data selection scheme.
- Request authors to justify the CBOW variant and explain its impact on performance.
- Request comparisons of the CBOW variant with original CBOW and skip-gram.
- Request statistical significance testing for reported improvements.
- Request additional evaluations on more target languages to demonstrate robustness.
