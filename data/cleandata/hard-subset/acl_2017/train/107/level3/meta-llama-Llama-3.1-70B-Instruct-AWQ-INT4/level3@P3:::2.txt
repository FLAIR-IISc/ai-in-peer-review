**Summary**

This paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER): annotation projection and representation projection. The annotation projection approach uses a heuristic data selection scheme to select high-quality projection-labeled data from comparable corpora, while the representation projection approach projects word representations in vector space (word embeddings) from a target language to a source language. The authors also design two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches. The proposed approaches are evaluated on both in-house and open NER data sets for multiple target languages, and the results show that the combined systems outperform two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Effective annotation projection approach**: The authors propose a heuristic data selection scheme that can effectively select high-quality projection-labeled data from comparable corpora, which improves the accuracy of the annotation projection approach for cross-lingual NER.
2. **Representation projection approach**: The authors propose a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection, which projects word representations in vector space from a target language to a source language.
3. **Co-decoding schemes**: The authors design two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches, which can improve the accuracy of the combined system.

**Strengths**

1. **State-of-the-art results**: The proposed approaches achieve state-of-the-art results on both in-house and open NER data sets for multiple target languages.
2. **Effective data selection scheme**: The heuristic data selection scheme proposed in the annotation projection approach can effectively select high-quality projection-labeled data from comparable corpora.
3. **Flexible representation projection approach**: The representation projection approach can be used to project word representations from any language to a source language, making it a flexible approach for cross-lingual NER.
4. **Well-organized paper**: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approaches and their evaluations.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of motivation for proposing a CBOW variation**: The authors do not provide a clear motivation for proposing a variant of the CBOW word2vec model, and it is not clear how this variation improves the performance of the representation projection approach.
2. **Limited evaluation**: The evaluation of the proposed approaches is limited to a few target languages, and it is not clear how the approaches will perform on other languages.
3. **Lack of comparison with original CBOW and skip-gram**: The authors do not provide a comparison of the proposed CBOW variation with the original CBOW and skip-gram models, making it difficult to assess the improvement of the proposed variation.
4. **Lack of statistical significance tests**: The authors do not provide statistical significance tests for the claims that the data selection scheme yields significant improvements, making it difficult to assess the reliability of the results.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear motivation for proposing a variant of the CBOW word2vec model, and how this variation improves the performance of the representation projection approach?
2. Can you provide a comparison of the proposed CBOW variation with the original CBOW and skip-gram models?
3. Can you provide statistical significance tests for the claims that the data selection scheme yields significant improvements?
4. Can you provide more evaluation results for other target languages to demonstrate the robustness of the proposed approaches?
