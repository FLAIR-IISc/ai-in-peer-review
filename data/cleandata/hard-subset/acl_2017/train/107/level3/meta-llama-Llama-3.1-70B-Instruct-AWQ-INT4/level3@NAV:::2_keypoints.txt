- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection of word vectors to apply a source NER model to a target language.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine outputs from the projection approaches for improved accuracy.  
- Contribution: effective heuristic data selection that filters good projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.  
- Contribution: representation projection method that maps target-language word vectors into source-language space to enable direct model transfer without retraining.  
- Contribution: two co-decoding strategies that yield higher accuracy than either projection method alone.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art results on CoNLL test data, outperforming prior cross-lingual NER methods.  
- Strength: data selection heuristic demonstrably improves quality of projection-labeled training data.  
- Strength: representation projection is flexible and applicable to multiple languages without retraining.  
- Strength: co-decoding schemes boost accuracy beyond individual system outputs.  
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly explains methods and experiments.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a few languages and datasets, requiring broader experiments.  
- Weakness: CBOW variation lacks novelty and appears previously proposed.  
- Weakness: insufficient details on the CBOW variation’s parameters and training settings.  
- Weakness: no experimental comparison with original CBOW and skip-gram models.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests reported for the improvements.  
- Question: please provide full details on the CBOW variation, including parameters and training settings.  
- Question: how will the approaches be extended and evaluated on more languages and datasets?  
- Question: why does the rank-based co-decoding scheme outperform the confidence-based scheme—can you provide more insight?  
- Question: how will you address potential overfitting in the representation projection approach?  
- Question: please provide more details on the training dictionary used for representation projection.
