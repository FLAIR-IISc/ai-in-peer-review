- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods—annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping—plus two co-decoding schemes, evaluated on multiple datasets and languages.
- Contribution 1: Heuristic data selection scheme to pick high-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.
- Contribution 2: Representation projection enabling direct transfer of a source-language NER model to target languages by projecting target embeddings to source space without re-training.
- Contribution 3: Two co-decoding schemes to combine outputs from annotation- and representation-projection approaches.
- Strength 1: The data selection scheme effectively filters noisy projected annotations.
- Strength 2: The proposed approaches improve performance over prior cross-lingual NER methods, especially for low-resource languages.
- Strength 3: Representation projection offers flexibility to add new languages without per-language-pair embeddings.
- Weakness 1: Methods are incremental and lack substantial novelty.
- Weakness 2: Threshold determination for data selection is unclear and may be undeclared w.r.t. dev-set tuning.
- Weakness 3: Paper gives limited guidance for tuning hyperparameters on new datasets or language pairs.
- Weakness 4: The description of how the two systems’ outputs are combined is unclear and hard to follow.
- Question 1: How were threshold values for the data selection scheme determined and were they tuned on a development set?
- Question 2: Can you provide more guidance on tuning parameters for new datasets or language pairs?
- Question 3: Can you clarify the combination mechanism for outputs from the two systems?
- Question 4: Have you tried combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and if so, what were the results?
