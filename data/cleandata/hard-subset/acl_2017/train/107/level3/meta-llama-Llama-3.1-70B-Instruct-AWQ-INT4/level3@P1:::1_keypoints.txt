Summary:
- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via word-embedding mapping to English.
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine the two projection-based systems for improved accuracy.

Contributions:
- Heuristic data-selection scheme that scores and selects high-quality projection-labeled NER examples from noisy comparable corpora.
- Representation-projection method that maps target-language embeddings into English space for direct NER model transfer.
- Two co-decoding strategies that merge outputs of the projection systems to boost performance.

Strengths:
- Combined system outperforms each individual method and two prior state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.
- Data-selection scheme effectively filters noisy projected labels and improves target-language NER accuracy.
- Representation-projection is a novel and effective approach for direct transfer in cross-lingual NER.

Weaknesses:
- Overall approach is incremental with limited novelty beyond prior work.
- Threshold selection for data filtering is not clearly explained, hurting reproducibility.
- No guidance provided for tuning parameters on new datasets or language pairs.
- Description of how the two system outputs are combined is unclear and hard to replicate.

Questions to authors:
- How were the threshold parameters for data selection determined and were they tuned on a development set?
- What are the content and text types of the in-house dataset?
- Were experiments run combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and what were the results?
- How was the training dictionary for cross-lingual embedding mapping constructed and what are its details?
