**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) via effective annotation and representation projection. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic data selection scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word representations in vector space (word embeddings) from a target language are projected to a source language, allowing the source-language NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training. Two co-decoding schemes are also proposed to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches. The paper evaluates the performance of the proposed approaches on both in-house and open NER data sets for several target languages.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Heuristic data selection scheme**: The paper proposes a heuristic data selection scheme that can effectively select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora. This scheme is a significant contribution to the field of weakly supervised cross-lingual NER.
2. **Representation projection approach**: The paper proposes a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection. This approach allows the source-language NER system to be applied to the target language without re-training, making it a significant contribution to the field of cross-lingual NER.
3. **Co-decoding schemes**: The paper proposes two co-decoding schemes that can combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches. These schemes are a significant contribution to the field of weakly supervised cross-lingual NER.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective data selection scheme**: The proposed data selection scheme is effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora, which is a significant challenge in weakly supervised cross-lingual NER.
2. **Improved performance**: The proposed approaches achieve improved performance compared to state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches, especially for languages with limited resources.
3. **Flexibility**: The proposed representation projection approach allows for easy addition of new languages without requiring separate embeddings for each language pair.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of novelty**: The overall approach is not novel, and the methods are incremental improvements over existing approaches.
2. **Unclear threshold determination**: The threshold determination for the data selection scheme is unclear, and it is not specified whether the thresholds were tuned on a development set.
3. **Limited guidance**: The paper provides limited guidance for tuning parameters for new datasets or language pairs.
4. **Unclear combination of outputs**: The description of the combination of outputs from the two systems is unclear and hard to follow.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How did you determine the threshold values for the data selection scheme, and were they tuned on a development set?
2. Can you provide more guidance on how to tune the parameters for new datasets or language pairs?
3. Can you clarify the description of the combination of outputs from the two systems?
4. Have you tried combining more than two weakly supervised systems, and if so, what were the results?
