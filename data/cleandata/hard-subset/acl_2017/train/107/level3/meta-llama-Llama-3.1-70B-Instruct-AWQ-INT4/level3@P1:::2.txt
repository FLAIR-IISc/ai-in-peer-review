**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) without requiring human annotation in the target language. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic data selection scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word embeddings from a target language are projected into a source language, allowing a single NER system to be applied to multiple languages without re-training. Two co-decoding schemes are also proposed to combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches. Experimental results show that the combined system achieves state-of-the-art results on both in-house and open NER datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. A heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that effectively selects good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.
2. A representation projection approach that projects word embeddings from a target language into a source language, allowing for direct NER model transfer.
3. Two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based approaches to produce more accurate results.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to cross-lingual NER that does not require human annotation in the target language, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
2. The experimental results show that the proposed approaches achieve state-of-the-art results on both in-house and open NER datasets.
3. The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed approaches and their motivations.
4. The use of multiple evaluation metrics (precision, recall, F1 score) provides a comprehensive understanding of the performance of the proposed approaches.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the proposed CBOW variation and its parameters.
2. The paper does not provide a comparison with the original CBOW and skip-gram models, making it difficult to assess the effectiveness of the proposed variation.
3. The paper does not report statistical significance tests for the claims that the data selection scheme yields significant improvements.
4. The evaluation section is relatively short and could benefit from more discussion and analysis of the results.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the proposed CBOW variation and its parameters?
2. How does the proposed CBOW variation compare to the original CBOW and skip-gram models?
3. Can you report statistical significance tests for the claims that the data selection scheme yields significant improvements?
4. Can you provide more discussion and analysis of the results in the evaluation section?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper could benefit from a standalone Related Work section to provide a more comprehensive overview of the existing approaches to cross-lingual NER.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the co-decoding schemes and their motivations.
* The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approaches, including more datasets and evaluation metrics.
