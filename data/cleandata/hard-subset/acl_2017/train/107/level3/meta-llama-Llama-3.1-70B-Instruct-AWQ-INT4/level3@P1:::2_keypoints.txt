- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods—annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection of embeddings—plus two co-decoding schemes; combined system achieves state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution 1: Heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection that filters good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.

- Contribution 2: Representation projection that maps target-language word embeddings into a source language so a single NER model can be applied without re-training.

- Contribution 3: Two co-decoding schemes to combine outputs from annotation- and representation-projection approaches for improved accuracy.

- Strength 1: Eliminates need for human annotation in the target language, offering a practical cross-lingual NER solution.

- Strength 2: Experimental results report state-of-the-art performance on both in-house and open datasets.

- Strength 3: Paper is well-structured and clearly explains methods and motivations.

- Strength 4: Uses multiple evaluation metrics (precision, recall, F1) for comprehensive performance assessment.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient detail on the proposed CBOW variation and its hyperparameters.

- Weakness 2: No comparison with original CBOW and skip-gram models to gauge effectiveness of the CBOW variation.

- Weakness 3: Lacks statistical significance tests supporting claims of improvement from the data selection scheme.

- Weakness 4: Evaluation section is brief and could use deeper analysis and discussion of results.

- Question 1: Please provide more details on the CBOW variation and parameter settings.

- Question 2: How does the CBOW variation compare empirically to original CBOW and skip-gram?

- Question 3: Can you report statistical significance tests for improvements due to data selection?

- Question 4: Can you expand the discussion and analysis in the evaluation section?

- Additional comment 1: Add a standalone Related Work section for a more comprehensive overview of existing cross-lingual NER approaches.

- Additional comment 2: Provide a more detailed explanation and motivation for the co-decoding schemes.

- Additional comment 3: Broaden evaluation with more datasets and metrics for a more comprehensive assessment.
