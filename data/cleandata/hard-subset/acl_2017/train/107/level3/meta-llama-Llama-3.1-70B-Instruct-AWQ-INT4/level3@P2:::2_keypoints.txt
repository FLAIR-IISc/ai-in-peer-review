- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER approaches: annotation projection and representation projection, plus two co-decoding schemes.
- Main contribution: heuristic data selection for annotation projection to pick high-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora.
- Main contribution: representation projection that maps target-language word vectors into source-language space for direct NER model transfer.
- Main contribution: two co-decoding schemes to combine outputs from the projection-based systems.
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated approach addressing scarce target-language annotations.
- Strength: Experimental results outperform two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods on CoNLL test data.
- Strength: Paper is well-written with clear method and result explanations.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation including comparisons and significance testing.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on the proposed CBOW variation and its parameter settings.
- Weakness: Missing baseline results for original CBOW and skip-gram to validate the proposed CBOW variation’s superiority.
- Weakness: Limited analysis and discussion of experimental results despite strong performance.
- Weakness: Paper organization could improve—suggest integrating Section 4.4 into 4.2 and relocating Table 2’s evaluation.
- Question: Request for more details on the proposed CBOW variation and its parameters.
- Question: Ask for performance comparisons with original CBOW and skip-gram methods on the same task.
- Question: Request a more detailed analysis of experimental results, including strengths and weaknesses of each approach.
- Question: Whether exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (Iacobacci et al., 2016) was considered.
- Question: Request comparison between frequency-weighted projections and original Mikolov cross-lingual projections.
