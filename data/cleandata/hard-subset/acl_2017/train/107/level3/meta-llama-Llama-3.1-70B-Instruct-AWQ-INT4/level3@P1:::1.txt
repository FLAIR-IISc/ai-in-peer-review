**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER) with no human annotation in the target language. The first approach is based on annotation projection, where a heuristic data selection scheme is developed to select good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora. The second approach is based on representation projection, where word embeddings are projected from a target language to a source language (English) to create a universal representation of words in different languages. The paper also presents two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems to produce an output that is more accurate than the outputs of individual systems.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. **Heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection**: The paper proposes a data selection scheme that effectively selects good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora. This scheme is based on a quality-score formula that measures the reliability of labeling entity e with tag l in the target language.
2. **Representation projection approach for direct NER model transfer**: The paper proposes a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection. This approach projects word embeddings from a target language to a source language (English) to create a universal representation of words in different languages.
3. **Co-decoding schemes for combining projection-based systems**: The paper presents two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems to produce an output that is more accurate than the outputs of individual systems.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Improved performance on cross-lingual NER**: The paper shows that the combined system achieves higher accuracy than both individual systems and outperforms two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches.
2. **Effective data selection scheme**: The paper demonstrates that the proposed data selection scheme is effective in selecting good-quality projection-labeled data and improving the accuracy of the target-language NER system.
3. **Novel representation projection approach**: The paper proposes a new approach for direct NER model transfer based on representation projection, which is a novel contribution to the field of cross-lingual NER.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of novelty in overall approach**: The paper's overall approach is not novel, and the methods are incremental improvements over existing approaches.
2. **Unclear threshold determination**: The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the threshold parameters were determined for the data selection scheme, making reproducibility unclear.
3. **Lack of guidance for tuning parameters**: The paper does not provide guidance for tuning parameters for new datasets or language pairs, which may limit the applicability of the approach.
4. **Unclear description of combination of outputs**: The paper's description of the combination of outputs from the two systems is unclear and hard to follow, which may make it difficult to replicate the approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How were the threshold parameters determined for the data selection scheme, and were they tuned on a development set?
2. Can you provide more information about the content/types of texts in the "in-house" dataset?
3. Were any experiments conducted to combine more than two weakly supervised systems, and did it improve performance?
4. Can you provide more details about the training dictionary used for cross-lingual word embedding mappings, and how it was constructed?
