This paper proposes two weakly supervised approaches for cross-lingual named entity recognition (NER): annotation projection and representation projection. The authors also design two co-decoding schemes to combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems. The main contributions of this work are: (1) a heuristic data selection scheme for annotation projection, which selects good-quality projection-labeled NER data from noisy comparable corpora; (2) a representation projection approach for direct NER model transfer, which projects word representations in vector space from a target language to a source language; and (3) two co-decoding schemes that combine the outputs of the two projection-based systems.

Strengths:

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to cross-lingual NER, which is a challenging task due to the lack of annotated data in the target language. The authors' approach is well-motivated and effectively addresses the problem.
2. The experimental results show that the proposed approaches outperform two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER approaches on the CoNLL test data. This demonstrates the effectiveness of the authors' approach.
3. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed methods and experimental results.
4. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including comparisons with other methods and statistical significance tests.

Weaknesses:

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the proposed CBOW variation and its parameters. This would allow readers to better understand the method and its advantages over the original CBOW.
2. The authors should provide results for the original CBOW and skip-gram methods to demonstrate the superiority of their proposed variation.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion and analysis of the experimental results, despite achieving state-of-the-art results.
4. The organization of the paper could be improved by integrating Section 4.4 into the beginning of Section 4.2 and moving Table 2's evaluation to the evaluation section.

Questions to Authors:

1. Can you provide more details on the proposed CBOW variation and its parameters?
2. How do the original CBOW and skip-gram methods perform on the same task, and how do they compare to your proposed variation?
3. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the experimental results, including a discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of each approach?
4. Have you considered using exponential decay for surrounding embeddings, as proposed by Iacobacci et al. (2016)?
5. Can you provide a comparison of the proposed frequency-weighted projections with the original Mikolov cross-lingual projections to demonstrate their superiority?

Additional Comments:

* The paper could benefit from a standalone Related Work section to provide a clear overview of the existing approaches to cross-lingual NER.
* The authors may want to consider adding more references to related work on word embeddings and cross-lingual NER.
* The evaluation section could be expanded to include more discussion and analysis of the results, despite achieving state-of-the-art results.
