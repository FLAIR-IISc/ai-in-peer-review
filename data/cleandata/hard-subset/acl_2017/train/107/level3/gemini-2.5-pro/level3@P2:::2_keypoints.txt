- Task and approach: combine Annotation Projection (AP) with heuristic data selection, Representation Projection (RP) via linear embedding mapping, and two co-decoding schemes to build cross-lingual NER without target labels.  
- Contribution: a unified framework integrating AP and RP with co-decoding that sets new state-of-the-art on CoNLL benchmarks.  
- Contribution: a practical entity-tag co-occurrence based data selection heuristic that effectively filters noisy projected annotations (large F1 gains for Japanese/Korean).  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration that AP and RP are complementary (AP: high precision, RP: balanced P/R) and that co-decoding outperforms either alone.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, outperforming recent baselines across multiple languages.  
- Strength: methodologically sound and well-motivated combination of data-level and model-level transfer, with co-decoding justified by observed trade-offs.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation that explains components and design choices accessibly.  
- Weakness: lack of ablation and justification for proposed model variants (CBOW variant and weighted mapping) versus standard baselines.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported for reported improvements and small F1 differences.  
- Weakness: superficial evaluation/analysis—insufficient error analysis or explanation of dataset-specific behaviors (e.g., NN1 vs NN2 differences, error types fixed by co-decoding).  
- Weakness: minor organizational issues—related work scattered and premature presentation of results within methods.
