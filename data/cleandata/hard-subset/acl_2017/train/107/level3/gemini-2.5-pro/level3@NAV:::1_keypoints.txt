- Addresses weakly supervised cross-lingual NER without target-language annotations using annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping.
- Introduces co-decoding schemes to combine both approaches and reports improved performance over individual methods and prior state-of-the-art on CoNLL.

- Main contribution: shows a simple frequency-based data selection heuristic substantially improves projected annotation quality, especially with poor alignments.
- Secondary contribution: systematically compares and combines annotation- and representation-projection paradigms, demonstrating that a simple ensemble outperforms individual systems.

- Strong empirical results, with large gains (e.g., +20.0 F1 for Korean) and new baselines on CoNLL.
- Highlights importance of managing noise in annotation projection and offers an effective, easy-to-implement quality-score heuristic.
- Evaluation across multiple languages and both in-house and public benchmarks strengthens credibility.

- Lacks novelty: methods are incremental refinements of established projection techniques.
- Methodological clarity and reproducibility concerns: thresholds for quality (q) and entity (n) selection are not explained or shown to be tuned robustly.
- Key components under-described: co-decoding details and in-house dataset info are insufficient, and no general guidance is given for tuning parameters on new languages/datasets.
