- Paper summary: Proposes a framework combining annotation projection (with heuristic data selection) and representation projection (linear embedding mapping) plus two co-decoding schemes to build NER for target languages without labeled data; reports SOTA on CoNLL and strong in-house results.

- Contribution: Integrates annotation- and representation-projection methods with co-decoding to achieve new state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER performance.

- Contribution: Introduces a simple, practical heuristic for selecting high-quality projection-labeled instances, improving robustness for low-quality alignments.

- Strength: Excellent empirical results — outperforms prior work on CoNLL (Spanish, Dutch, German) and scales to an in-house dataset with >50 entity types.

- Strength: Effective combination — co-decoding (especially rank-based) leverages complementary strengths of annotation (high precision) and representation projection (balanced) to improve overall performance.

- Strength: Clear and well-structured presentation; methods and models (CRF, MEMM, NN) are explained accessibly.

- Weakness: Insufficient justification and ablation for novel components, making it hard to assess their individual contributions.

- Weakness: The distance-weighted CBOW variant lacks motivation and comparisons to standard CBOW/Skip-gram or other weighting schemes.

- Weakness: The frequency-weighted cross-lingual embedding mapping is not ablated against the original unweighted formulation.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported to support claims of "significant" improvements.

- Weakness: Organizational issues — related work is split and evaluation/results are fragmented across sections, reducing clarity.

- Weakness: Limited analysis in evaluation — lacks error analysis and discussion of why different models perform better on different datasets.

- Question: Please motivate the distance-weighted CBOW and provide comparisons to standard CBOW/Skip-gram and other decay weightings.

- Question: Please provide an ablation comparing frequency-weighted cross-lingual projection to the unweighted Mikolov et al. method.

- Question: Please perform and report statistical significance tests for main claims, especially for data selection and co-decoding improvements.
