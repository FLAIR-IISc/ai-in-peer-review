- Paper proposes weakly supervised cross-lingual NER using annotation projection (AP) and representation projection (RP), plus two co-decoding schemes, requiring no target-language annotations.
- AP projects English labels via comparable corpora with a heuristic data-selection filter; RP maps target embeddings to English space to apply one English NER model.
- Co-decoding (confidence- and rank-based) combines AP and RP outputs and the combined systems outperform prior state-of-the-art on CoNLL and an in-house fine-grained dataset.
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that combining AP and RP yields strong new baselines for cross-lingual NER.
- Main contribution: a simple, effective heuristic for filtering noisy projected training instances that significantly improves performance.
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art F1 on standard cross-lingual NER benchmarks, outperforming strong baselines.
- Strength: thorough evaluation across multiple target languages (JP, KR, DE, PT, ES, NL) and both CoNLL and a proprietary 50+ type dataset.
- Strength: clear empirical evidence that data selection greatly improves results, especially for low-quality alignments (large gains for Japanese and Korean).
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—AP and RP ideas are established and contributions are largely engineering/combination-based.
- Weakness: data-selection method is under-described; thresholds (q, n) and their tuning procedure are not explained, hurting reproducibility.
- Weakness: several method components (co-decoding rules, conflict resolution, in-house dataset details) are underspecified, limiting interpretability and reuse.
- Question: How were data-selection thresholds (q, n) chosen—were they tuned on development sets and what ranges were explored?
- Question: Did you try combining more than two systems (e.g., AP + multiple NN variants) and would a three-way combination improve results?
- Question: Please provide domain information and examples for the in-house dataset and the 50+ fine-grained entity types to contextualize results.
