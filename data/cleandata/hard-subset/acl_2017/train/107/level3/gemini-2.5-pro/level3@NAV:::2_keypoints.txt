- Proposes a hybrid weakly supervised cross-lingual NER combining annotation projection and representation projection.  
- Annotation projection: creates pseudo-labeled target data and introduces a novel heuristic for selecting high-quality projected instances.  
- Representation projection: uses a custom distance-weighted CBOW variant and a frequency-weighted cross-lingual mapping to apply a source NER model to target languages.  
- Combines both systems via probability- and ranking-based co-decoding schemes.  
- Empirically achieves state-of-the-art results on standard cross-lingual NER benchmarks.  
- The data selection heuristic substantially improves performance with noisy comparable-corpus alignments.  
- Strong empirical performance: combined system outperforms recent SOTA on CoNLL test data.  
- Demonstrates that annotation projection (high precision) and representation projection (more balanced) are complementary and benefit from co-decoding.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and reproducible.  
- Practical, intuitive data selection method validated empirically.  
- Fails to ablate or compare proposed CBOW and cross-lingual mapping variants against original methods.  
- Does not report statistical significance tests for claimed improvements.  
- Evaluation lacks deeper analysis of model differences and error patterns.  
- Organizational issues: related work is split and experimental results are misplaced within methodology.  
- Questions requested: comparisons of distance-weighted CBOW vs original CBOW/skip-gram, frequency-weighted vs original mapping, and statistical significance results.
