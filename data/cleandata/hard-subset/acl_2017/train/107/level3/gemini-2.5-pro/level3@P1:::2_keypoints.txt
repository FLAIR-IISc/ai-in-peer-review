- Paper proposes a weakly supervised framework for cross-lingual NER that needs no target-language annotations.  
- Two main approaches: annotation projection (label transfer via comparable corpora) and representation projection (map target embeddings to source space).  
- Novel heuristic data selection for annotation projection scores sentence pairs by entity-label consistency to filter noisy auto-labeled data.  
- Representation projection trains an NER model on source using only embeddings and applies it to target via a learned linear embedding mapping.  
- Two co-decoding schemes (confidence-based and rank-based) combine the complementary annotation- and representation-projection outputs.  
- Experiments on in-house and CoNLL datasets show the combined system outperforms prior weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods.  
- Main contribution: an effective combined system leveraging high-precision annotation projection and balanced representation projection for SOTA results.  
- Secondary contribution: a simple, effective data selection heuristic that yields large F1 improvements for poorly aligned languages.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art empirical performance across multiple languages versus strong baselines.  
- Strength: well-designed, comprehensive system combining label-level and feature-level transfer with thoughtful co-decoding.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-written, and experimentally reproducible.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical justification and ablation for proposed model variants (CBOW variant and frequency-weighted mapping).  
- Weakness: no comparisons showing the weighted embedding mapping improves over the original unweighted formulation.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported for claimed improvements from data selection.  
- Weakness: limited qualitative analysis of results and model behavior, e.g., reasons behind differing co-decoding performance and common error types.  
- Question: please motivate and compare the CBOW variant against standard CBOW/Skip-gram to show its impact.  
- Question: provide an ablation comparing the weighted linear mapping (Eq. 7) to the original Mikolov et al. (2013b) mapping.  
- Question: report statistical significance tests for improvements attributed to the data selection scheme.
