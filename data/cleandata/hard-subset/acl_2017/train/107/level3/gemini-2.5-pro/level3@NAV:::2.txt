### Summary and Main Contributions

This paper addresses weakly supervised cross-lingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) by proposing and combining two distinct approaches: annotation projection and representation projection. The annotation projection method creates pseudo-labeled data in a target language and introduces a novel heuristic data selection scheme to filter noisy projections. The representation projection method leverages a custom variant of CBOW embeddings and a frequency-weighted modification of Mikolov et al.'s cross-lingual mapping to apply a source-language NER model directly to the target language. Finally, the outputs of these two systems are combined using probability- and ranking-based co-decoding schemes.

The main contributions of this work are:
1.  A comprehensive empirical demonstration that a hybrid system, combining annotation projection and representation projection, can achieve state-of-the-art results on standard cross-lingual NER benchmarks.
2.  An effective and simple data selection heuristic for annotation projection that substantially improves performance, particularly when dealing with noisy alignments from comparable corpora.

### Strengths

1.  **Strong Empirical Performance:** The primary strength of this paper is its results. The proposed combined system outperforms two recent state-of-the-art methods on the CoNLL test data, establishing a strong new baseline for weakly supervised cross-lingual NER.
2.  **Effective Combination of Orthogonal Approaches:** The paper effectively demonstrates that annotation projection (high precision) and representation projection (more balanced) are complementary. The proposed co-decoding schemes successfully leverage the strengths of each, leading to significant gains over either individual system.
3.  **Clarity and Structure:** The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and logically structured. The authors clearly explain each component of their system, making the work accessible and reproducible.
4.  **Practical Data Selection Method:** The proposed heuristic for selecting high-quality training instances from noisy projected annotations is intuitive and empirically validated. It offers a practical solution to a common challenge in annotation projection.

### Weaknesses

1.  **Insufficient Justification for Novel Components:** The paper introduces variants of both the CBOW embedding model and Mikolov et al.'s cross-lingual projection method. However, it fails to provide ablations or comparisons against the original, widely-used versions of these methods. Without these crucial baselines, the benefits of the proposed modifications are unsubstantiated.
2.  **Lack of Statistical Significance Testing:** The authors claim that their data selection scheme yields "significant improvement," but they do not report any statistical significance tests to support this assertion. This makes it difficult to assess the reliability of the observed gains.
3.  **Limited Analysis in Evaluation:** While the results are strong, the evaluation section lacks deep analysis. The discussion is limited to reporting scores, with little exploration into *why* certain models (e.g., NN1 vs. NN2) perform differently across datasets or an analysis of error patterns.
4.  **Organizational Issues:** The paper would benefit from better organization. Related work is split between the introduction and a misplaced section (4.4), and experimental results (Table 2) appear in a methodology section. A dedicated Related Work section and consolidating all evaluations would improve readability.

### Questions to Authors

1.  Could you provide results comparing your proposed distance-weighted CBOW variant to the original CBOW and skip-gram models to demonstrate its advantage?
2.  Can you present a direct comparison between your frequency-weighted cross-lingual projection and the original unweighted method from Mikolov et al. (2013b) to validate the superiority of your approach?
3.  Have you performed statistical significance tests for the improvements reported in Table 2? If so, could you please include the results?
