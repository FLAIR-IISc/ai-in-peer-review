- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods (annotation projection and representation projection) and two co-decoding schemes to combine them.
- Introduces a heuristic data selection scheme that notably improves projection-labeled data quality, boosting performance for low-alignment languages.
- Evaluates methods on in-house and CoNLL datasets, reporting competitive or improved results over prior cross-lingual NER approaches.
- Data selection shows strong empirical impact (e.g., +13.9 F1 for Japanese, +20.0 F1 for Korean), effectively reducing projection noise.
- Co-decoding leverages complementary system strengths; the rank-based scheme yields consistent improvements across languages.
- Comprehensive multilingual evaluation across datasets and languages supports robustness of the approach.
- Limited methodological novelty—annotation and representation projection are established techniques and co-decoding is straightforward.
- Threshold determination for data selection (q, n) is unclear, impeding reproducibility and generalization.
- Insufficient guidance on parameter tuning (thresholds, embedding mappings) for new datasets or languages.
- Did not explore combining more than two weakly supervised systems, missing potential further gains.
- In-house datasets lack sufficient description (domains, text types), reducing clarity and applicability of results.
- Questions: how were q and n chosen in a weakly supervised setting; why only two systems for co-decoding; and request for detailed in-house dataset descriptions.
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to clarify threshold selection, parameter tuning, dataset details, and to better justify novelty.
