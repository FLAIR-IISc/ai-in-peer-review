- Presents a weakly supervised cross-lingual NER method combining annotation projection, representation projection, and novel co-decoding schemes.  
- Proposes a heuristic data selection scheme that filters noisy projection-labeled data using entity-tag frequency analysis.  
- Introduces a modified CBOW (weighted-context) embedding model and cross-lingual mapping for representation projection.  
- Develops co-decoding strategies (confidence-based and rank-based) to effectively combine outputs from both approaches.  
- Strong empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art on CoNLL and in-house datasets, with notable gains in low alignment-accuracy scenarios.  
- Comprehensive, multifaceted approach that leverages complementary strengths of projection and representation methods.  
- Practical data selection technique demonstrably improves annotation-projection quality.  
- Clear, well-written, and well-organized presentation that facilitates reproducibility.  
- Insufficient motivation and explanation for the specific weighted-CBOW variant and its hyperparameter choices.  
- Missing comparisons to baselines (original CBOW and skip-gram) to quantify the benefit of the proposed embedding model.  
- No statistical significance tests reported for the performance improvements (e.g., p-values).  
- Paper organization could be improved—related work should be separated and some sections/tables reorganized for better flow.  
- Request clarification of the rationale behind the chosen CBOW weighting function and alternatives considered.  
- Request results using original CBOW and skip-gram for representation projection as baselines.  
- Request p-values or other statistical tests to validate reported performance gains.  
- Request information on computational cost and training-time impact of the data selection scheme.  
- Suggest discussing limitations, such as reliance on comparable corpora with alignment information.  
- Suggest discussing effects of fine-grained entity-type granularity (e.g., 50+ types) on weak supervision.  
- Recommend citing Iacobacci et al. (2016) and exploring alternative contextual weighting schemes.
