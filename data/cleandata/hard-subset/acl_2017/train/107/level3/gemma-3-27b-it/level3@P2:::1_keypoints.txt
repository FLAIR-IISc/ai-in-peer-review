- Presents two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection (with data selection) and representation projection (cross-lingual embeddings), plus co-decoding to combine them.
- Main contribution: shows importance of data selection for annotation projection via a simple quality-score heuristic that reduces noisy labels.
- Empirically evaluates combining annotation- and representation-projection methods and shows combined systems outperform individual approaches using co-decoding.
- Establishes a strong weakly-supervised cross-lingual NER baseline that improves on prior CoNLL results.
- Strong experimental coverage across multiple languages (Japanese, Korean, German, Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch) and datasets.
- Clear problem motivation and generally positive results demonstrating method utility.
- Explores useful combination strategies to leverage complementary system strengths.
- Lacks novelty: methods are incremental over established annotation- and representation-projection techniques.
- Unclear how thresholds q and n for data selection were chosen, harming reproducibility.
- Limited guidance on tuning selection thresholds for new languages/datasets; generalizability is questionable.
- Co-decoding schemes (“exclude-O confidence-based” and “rank-based”) are described vaguely and need clearer implementation details.
- Proliferation of method variants (NN1 vs NN2, multiple co-decoding schemes) with unclear comparative rationale or exploration of combining more than two systems.
- Insufficient description of the in-house dataset (source, genre, entity distribution), limiting assessment of result generality.
- Requests clarification on threshold tuning procedure, detailed pseudocode/description of co-decoding, experiments combining >2 systems, and full in-house dataset details.
- Additional suggestions: expand related work discussion, add diagrams (e.g., co-decoding process), and clarify how alignment for comparable corpora was obtained.
- Overall assessment: solid empirical study but incremental and hindered by clarity/reproducibility issues; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
