- Presents a comprehensive cross-lingual NER approach combining annotation projection, a novel data-selection heuristic, representation projection via embeddings, and co-decoding of CRF/MEMM/neural models, achieving state-of-the-art on CoNLL.
- Main contribution is the systematic, effective combination of annotation and representation projection with a data-selection scheme that improves performance under poor alignments and practical co-decoding strategies.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with consistent improvements over baselines and SOTA performance on CoNLL datasets.
- Strength: Comprehensive approach addressing data scarcity and domain adaptation by integrating annotation projection, representation projection, and co-decoding.
- Strength: Well-structured, clearly written paper with useful ablation studies that clarify contributions.
- Strength: Extensive multi-language and multi-dataset experimentation that supports generalizability.
- Weakness: Lacks clear motivation and parameter details for the proposed CBOW variation.
- Weakness: Missing baseline comparisons to original CBOW and skip-gram to quantify gains from the CBOW modification.
- Weakness: Should consider and compare to exponential decay (distance-weighted) schemes for context embeddings (e.g., Iacobacci et al. 2016).
- Weakness: Improvements are not backed by statistical significance testing to confirm robustness.
- Weakness: Section 4 could be reorganized (move 4.4 into 4.2 and place Table 2 in evaluation) to improve flow.
- Question: Please justify the CBOW variation and provide its detailed parameters.
- Question: Can you provide results for original CBOW and skip-gram baselines?
- Question: Which statistical significance tests were used and what are the p-values for reported improvements?
