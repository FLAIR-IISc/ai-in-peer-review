- Paper studies weakly supervised cross-lingual NER using annotation projection and representation projection, adding data selection and co-decoding.
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that careful data selection improves annotation projection quality.
- The proposed quality-score formula is straightforward and yields measurable performance gains, especially with poor alignments.
- Representation projection contribution is incremental and not novel.
- Co-decoding schemes are incremental and lack clear explanation or justification.
- The paper explores many method variants without a clear rationale or deeper analysis of choices.
- Evaluation is broad, covering both in-house and CoNLL datasets across multiple target languages.
- Baseline approaches and prior work are presented clearly.
- Threshold selection for data selection is opaque; no description of development-set tuning, harming reproducibility.
- Paper gives limited guidance on how to tune parameters (e.g., quality thresholds) for new datasets or language pairs.
- Descriptions of the co-decoding schemes (e.g., “exclude-O” and rank-based) are vague and hard to follow.
- The “in-house” dataset lacks details about domain and characteristics, limiting result context.
- Unclear if combinations of more than two weakly supervised systems were attempted or how they perform.
- Reviewer requests: clarify threshold tuning procedure, explicate co-decoding intuition and design, describe in-house dataset, and report any multi-system combination attempts.
- Writing is generally clear but would benefit from more concise focus on key contributions and clearer table/figure captions.
