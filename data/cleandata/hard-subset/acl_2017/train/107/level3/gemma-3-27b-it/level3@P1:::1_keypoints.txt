- Paper proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection from comparable corpora and model transfer via representation projection of word embeddings.
- Main contribution is a heuristic, frequency-based data selection scheme for annotation projection to reduce noise in projected labels.
- Representation projection maps target-language embeddings into the source-language feature space as an alternative transfer method.
- Two co-decoding schemes (including a rank-based approach) are proposed to combine outputs from the two projection methods.
- Strong empirical evaluation across multiple languages (Ja, Ko, De, Pt, Es, Nl) and both in-house and CoNLL datasets demonstrates cross-lingual applicability.
- Data selection scheme is practical and improves F1 (notably for Japanese and Korean) by selecting higher-confidence projected examples.
- Co-decoding, especially the rank-based method, yields performance gains by leveraging complementary strengths of the two systems.
- Paper provides relatively detailed implementation and embedding/training specifications that aid reproducibility.
- Core techniques (annotation and representation projection) are not novel; contributions are largely incremental implementation and empirical findings.
- Parameter selection for data filtering (q, n) is insufficiently explained, harming reproducibility.
- Unclear how generalizable the data selection thresholds are to new languages or different comparable corpora.
- Rank-based co-decoding description lacks clarity and a concrete example, making the combination logic hard to follow.
- Limited details about the in-house datasets (source, domain, entity types) constrain evaluation interpretation.
- Questions for authors: how were q and n tuned (dev set size/composition)? please provide a concrete rank-based co-decoding example; describe hyperparameter tuning across languages; report sensitivity to training dictionary size for embedding mapping.
- Additional suggestions: report computational costs, include an ablation study to isolate contributions (data selection, co-decoding), and clarify tuning/reproducibility procedures.
