- Comprehensive study combining annotation projection, representation projection, and novel co-decoding schemes, achieving state-of-the-art results on CoNLL.
- Well-structured, clearly written, and easy to follow.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results: consistent improvements across languages and datasets, including in-house data.
- Comprehensive approach: systematic combination and comparison of multiple modeling and projection strategies.
- Practical data selection: effective heuristic for filtering noisy projection data that significantly improves performance.
- Clear presentation: technical details and experimental setup are well-documented and reproducible.
- Thorough evaluation: multi-language and multi-dataset experiments support generalizability.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: core components are established methods; main contribution is their combination rather than new algorithms.
- Insufficient detail on CBOW variant: lacks implementation specifics, motivations, and hyperparameters.
- No statistical significance testing reported for performance gains.
- Organization issues: suggested reordering (e.g., Section 4.4 and Table 2) would improve flow.
- Limited analysis: need deeper error analysis, ablations (e.g., co-decoding components), and study of data quality effects.

Questions for authors:
- Provide detailed description and hyperparameters for the CBOW variation and rationale for modifications.
- Report results for original CBOW and skip-gram as baselines.
- Supply statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) for reported improvements.
- Analyze sensitivity to data selection thresholds (q and n) with ablation studies.
- Elaborate on error cases and identify challenging entity types or linguistic phenomena.

Additional comments:
- Consider citing Iacobacci et al. (2016) and exploring exponential decay for surrounding embeddings.
- Compare frequency-weighted projections quantitatively with original Mikolov projections.
- Add a dedicated Related Work section to improve organization and context.
