- Combines annotation projection and representation projection with co-decoding to improve weakly supervised cross-lingual NER.
- Proposes a heuristic frequency-based data selection for annotation projection to reduce noise from poor alignments.
- Adapts cross-lingual word embedding mapping using a frequency-weighted approach to enable direct model transfer without target retraining.
- Introduces novel co-decoding schemes, notably a rank-based method, to merge outputs from different transfer approaches.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms prior state-of-the-art on CoNLL and shows competitive in-house dataset performance across multiple languages.
- Comprehensive experimental exploration: evaluates CRF, MEMM, neural models, embedding projections, and multiple co-decoding strategies.
- Practical data selection is intuitively sound and empirically effective, especially when alignment quality is low.
- Paper is clearly written with sufficient methodological detail to support reproducibility.
- Motivation for the CBOW variant’s distance-weighting is insufficiently justified.
- Lacks direct empirical comparison between the proposed frequency-weighted embedding projection and the original Mikolov projection.
- Claims about the data selection scheme’s improvements lack statistical significance testing.
- Paper organization is suboptimal: related work is interleaved in the introduction and some sections/tables should be relocated for clarity.
- Experimental discussion is brief; deeper error analysis and exploration of alignment/language-pair effects are missing.
- Question: provide a detailed rationale and intended properties for the CBOW distance-weighting scheme.
- Question: have you compared performance with the original Mikolov cross-lingual projection, and what are the results?
- Question: have you performed statistical significance tests (e.g., t-test/Wilcoxon) for the data selection improvements?
- Suggestion: consider relating the CBOW weighting to Iacobacci et al. (2016) and explore additional co-decoding weightings beyond rank- and confidence-based schemes.
