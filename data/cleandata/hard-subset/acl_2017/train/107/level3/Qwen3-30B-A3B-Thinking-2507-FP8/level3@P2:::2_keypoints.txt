- Heuristic data selection for annotation projection markedly improves NER with noisy alignments (e.g., +13.9 F1 for Japanese).  
- Frequency-weighted linear mapping projects target-language embeddings to English so the source NER can be applied directly.  
- Rank-based co-decoding combining both approaches achieves state-of-the-art on CoNLL and approaches supervised performance.  
- Data selection is empirically validated and the frequency-based entity reliability metric is intuitive and effective for low-alignment languages.  
- The combined system outperforms prior work and attains F1 within 4–6 points of supervised baselines for some languages.  
- The approach is practical and scalable: no target-language annotation and adding languages only requires mapping to English.  
- The paper lacks statistical significance testing to support claims of “significant improvements.”  
- The frequency-weighted mapping is not directly compared to the original Mikolov (2013b) mapping baseline.  
- Missing ablation studies: the proposed CBOW variant is not compared to standard CBOW or skip-gram to justify gains.  
- The motivation and necessity for the distance-weighted CBOW are unclear and unl inked to prior work.  
- Related Work is poorly organized (embedded within Section 4.2) and should be a standalone section.  
- Evaluation is superficial, lacking error analysis and explanation of why rank-based co-decoding outperforms confidence-based methods.  
- Request: provide statistical significance (p-values or bootstrap confidence intervals) for reported F1 gains.  
- Request: include baseline results comparing frequency-weighted mapping to the standard Mikolov mapping (e.g., in Table 2).  
- Request: report F1 scores for standard CBOW and skip-gram to assess the value of the proposed CBOW variant, and clarify the distance-weighting motivation.
