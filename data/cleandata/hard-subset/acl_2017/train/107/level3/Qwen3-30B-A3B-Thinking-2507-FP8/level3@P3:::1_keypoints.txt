- Proposes annotation projection with a heuristic data selection scheme to create weakly labeled target-language NER data from English via comparable corpora.  
- Proposes representation projection by mapping target-language word embeddings into English vector space so an English NER system can be applied directly.  
- Introduces two co-decoding schemes to combine outputs from annotation and representation projection approaches.  
- Data selection scheme shows clear empirical value, notably improving F1 by 13.9 and 20.0 points for Japanese and Korean.  
- Representation projection offers a viable alternative to parallel-corpus-based methods, producing reasonable results without parallel data.  
- Combined systems consistently outperform previous state-of-the-art on CoNLL test data, providing a strong empirical baseline.  
- Overall contribution lacks novelty, largely building on established annotation and representation projection techniques.  
- Threshold determination for data selection (q, n) is unclear and may impair reproducibility if tuned improperly.  
- Co-decoding schemes are poorly described with insufficient implementation detail and unclear rationale for performance differences.  
- The paper provides no clear guidance for tuning or applying the method to new languages or domains.  
- The in-house dataset is inadequately described, limiting assessment of result generalizability.  
- Questions for authors: how thresholds were chosen/tuned; detailed description of the in-house dataset; why only two systems were combined; and clearer implementation details for the co-decoding schemes.
