

This paper presents two novel approaches for weakly supervised cross-lingual NER: (1) annotation projection with a data selection scheme for filtering noisy projection-labeled data, and (2) representation projection using a modified CBOW model for cross-lingual word embeddings. The primary contributions are the effective data selection mechanism for annotation projection (which significantly improves performance when alignment is noisy) and the representation projection approach with co-decoding schemes that combine the two methods to achieve state-of-the-art results.

Strengths: The paper is exceptionally well-structured and clearly written, making complex methods accessible. The combined results are impressive, outperforming previous state-of-the-art approaches on CoNLL test data. The data selection scheme is particularly valuable for real-world applications where alignment quality varies. The evaluation is comprehensive across multiple languages and datasets.

Weaknesses: The paper lacks crucial comparisons for the proposed CBOW variant - the authors should report results for standard CBOW and skip-gram to demonstrate the value of their modification. The cross-lingual projection comparison between the proposed frequency-weighted method and standard Mikolov projections is missing, preventing readers from assessing the claimed superiority. Statistical significance tests for the data selection improvements are absent, which weakens the claims about its effectiveness. The Related Work section is embedded in the Introduction rather than presented as a standalone section, and the evaluation section would benefit from more detailed analysis despite achieving strong results.

The paper should also address the suggested reference to Iacobacci et al. (2016) regarding exponential decay for surrounding embeddings, and clarify the motivation for the CBOW variation. The organization could be improved by moving Table 2's evaluation to the appropriate section and integrating Section 4.4 earlier. These improvements would significantly strengthen the paper's technical contribution and clarity.
