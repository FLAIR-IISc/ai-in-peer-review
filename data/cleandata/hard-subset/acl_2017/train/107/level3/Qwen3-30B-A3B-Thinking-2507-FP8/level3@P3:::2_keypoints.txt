- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods—annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mapping—and co-decoding schemes, reporting state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution 1: Heuristic data selection for annotation projection that greatly improves performance with low alignment accuracy (e.g., +13.9 F1 Japanese, +20.0 F1 Korean).

- Contribution 2: Frequency-weighted representation projection that outperforms standard projection methods.

- Contribution 3: Two co-decoding schemes that effectively combine the approaches and boost accuracy over individual systems.

- Strength 1: Well-structured and clearly written paper with strong motivation and presentation.

- Strength 2: Practical and effective data selection scheme addressing alignment-quality issues in annotation projection.

- Strength 3: Combined systems achieve state-of-the-art CoNLL results (e.g., German 69.3 F1 vs. previous 65.0 F1).

- Weakness 1: CBOW variant lacks justification and has no experimental comparison to standard CBOW or skip-gram.

- Weakness 2: No comparison of frequency-weighted projection with the original (unweighted) Mikolov projection to isolate the weighting effect.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation is brief and lacks in-depth analysis, error analysis, and explanations for cross-lingual performance differences.

- Weakness 4: No statistical significance tests reported for improvements from data selection or co-decoding.

- Weakness 5: Related work is misplaced (embedded in Section 4.2) rather than presented as a standalone section.

- Question 1: Provide results comparing standard CBOW and skip-gram with the proposed CBOW variant.

- Question 2: Compare frequency-weighted projection with the original Mikolov projection (without weights).

- Question 3: Did the authors consider exponential decay for surrounding embeddings (Iacobacci et al., 2016), and if not, why?

- Question 4: Provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) for improvements from data selection and co-decoding.
