- Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER methods: annotation projection with data selection and representation projection mapping embeddings, plus two co-decoding schemes.  
- Main contributions ranked: (1) co-decoding schemes (description unclear), (2) data selection for annotation projection (strong gains in low-alignment scenarios), (3) representation projection (builds on prior work).  
- Data selection yields large F1 improvements in low-alignment languages (e.g., +13.9 for Japanese, +20.0 for Korean).  
- Combined systems outperform two state-of-the-art cross-lingual NER methods on CoNLL, offering a strong baseline.  
- Representation projection enables direct model transfer without retraining, useful for multilingual deployment.  
- Overall contribution is incremental, largely extending prior annotation- and representation-projection work.  
- Data selection quality-score is straightforward; representation projection closely follows existing embedding-mapping techniques.  
- Unclear how data selection thresholds (q and n) were chosen, raising reproducibility concerns.  
- Co-decoding schemes, especially the rank-based method, are described unclearly with missing implementation details and conflict-resolution rules.  
- Paper lacks guidance for tuning parameters on new datasets or language pairs, limiting generalizability.  
- The in-house NER dataset is not described (domain, text types, entity distribution), making evaluation relevance hard to assess.  
- Question: Were q and n tuned on a development set, and how was that dev set constructed?  
- Question: Provide detailed implementation of rank-based co-decoding and how conflicting entity detections are resolved.  
- Question: What are the characteristics of the in-house dataset and how does it differ from CoNLL?  
- Question: Have you tried combining more than two weakly supervised systems; if not, why is two sufficient?  
- Overall: Practical and promising for low-alignment cases, but impact weakened by incremental novelty and insufficient methodological clarity and reproducibility.
