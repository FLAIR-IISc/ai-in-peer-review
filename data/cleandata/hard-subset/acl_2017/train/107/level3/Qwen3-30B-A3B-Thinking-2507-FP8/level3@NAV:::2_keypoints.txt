- Presents two methods: annotation projection with a data selection scheme, and representation projection using a modified CBOW model for cross-lingual embeddings.  
- Main contributions: effective data selection that improves noisy projection and a representation-projection + co-decoding scheme achieving state-of-the-art results.  
- Strength: paper is very well-structured and clearly written, making complex methods accessible.  
- Strength: combined approach outperforms prior state-of-the-art on CoNLL test sets.  
- Strength: data selection scheme is practically valuable when alignment quality varies.  
- Strength: evaluation is comprehensive across multiple languages and datasets.  
- Weakness: missing crucial comparisons for the CBOW variant—no results for standard CBOW or skip-gram to justify the modification.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison between the proposed frequency-weighted projection and standard Mikolov projections.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests reported for data selection improvements.  
- Weakness: Related Work is embedded in the Introduction rather than as a standalone section.  
- Weakness: evaluation section needs more detailed analysis despite strong overall results.  
- Suggestion: address/explain connection to Iacobacci et al. (2016) on exponential decay for surrounding embeddings.  
- Suggestion: clarify motivation for the CBOW variation more explicitly.  
- Organization suggestion: move Table 2 evaluation to the appropriate section and integrate Section 4.4 earlier for clarity.
