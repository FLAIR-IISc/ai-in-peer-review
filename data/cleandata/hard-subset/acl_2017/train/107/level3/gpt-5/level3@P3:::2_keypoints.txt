- Summary: Proposes annotation projection and representation projection plus two co-decoding strategies for cross-lingual NER without target supervision, showing combined gains and state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution 1: Effective frequency-based instance selection for annotation projection that substantially improves noisy projections (+13.9/+20.0 F1 for JA/KO).

- Contribution 2: Scalable representation projection using a frequency-weighted linear mapping of monolingual embeddings enabling direct transfer of an English neural NER.

- Contribution 3: Two co-decoding schemes (exclude-O confidence-based and rank-based) that consistently improve performance and achieve SOTA on CoNLL.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance across benchmarks and in-house multi-entity datasets, with notable fusion gains.

- Strength: Practical and scalable approach requiring no target annotations and only lightweight resources (comparable corpora, small seed dictionaries).

- Strength: Simple yet effective instance selection that helps under low alignment quality.

- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation and convincing co-decoding intuition.

- Weakness: Missing ablation comparisons to standard CBOW and skip-gram and to unweighted/orthogonal mapping variants.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail on the CBOW variant, context weighting, and neural model hyperparameters.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported for claimed improvements.

- Weakness: Limited error analysis and sensitivity studies (entity-type errors, OOVs, alignment noise, dictionary size).

- Weakness: Organization issues—related work placement and table locations could be improved.

- Question: Please specify exact CBOW variant architecture and all training hyperparameters (window, decay form, normalization, negatives, LR, epochs, vocab cutoff).

- Question: Can you report baselines using standard CBOW and skip-gram holding other factors constant?

- Question: Please compare frequency-weighted mapping to unweighted least squares and orthogonal Procrustes; report any regularization used.

- Question: Are gains from data selection and co-decoding statistically significant (e.g., paired tests across runs)?

- Question: Under what conditions does rank-based fusion beat exclude-O fusion; please analyze precision/recall trade-offs and conflict types.

- Question: Provide sensitivity of results to dictionary size/frequency cutoff and domain mismatch.

- Question: How are OOVs handled in representation projection and what is their impact on recall by entity type?

- Additional: Compare linear vs. exponential decay for context weighting and cite relevant work on decay functions.

- Additional: Move related work to its own section earlier and relocate Table 2 to the main evaluation for better flow.

- Additional: Expand evaluation with error analysis by entity type, length, capitalization, OOV, and sensitivity to alignment/dictionary/mapping.
