- Summary: Proposes two weakly supervised cross-lingual NER strategies—annotation projection with instance selection (MEMMs) and frequency-weighted representation projection (mapped embeddings powering neural NER)—plus two co-decoding schemes, achieving strong gains on in-house and CoNLL datasets.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a simple rank-based co-decoding that consistently improves over individual systems and prior baselines.

- Contribution 2: Presents a practical training-instance selection method for annotation projection that substantially boosts performance under noisy alignments.

- Contribution 3: Proposes a frequency-weighted cross-lingual embedding mapping (CBOW variant) for direct transfer, though the variant is under-specified.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance—combined systems outperform prior weakly supervised cross-lingual NER and approach supervised baselines across multiple languages.

- Strength: Effectively exploits complementarity between AP (high precision) and representation projection (balanced) via rank-based combination, yielding sizable F1 gains.

- Strength: Pipeline is clear and reproducible with well-described modeling choices and evaluations on both in-house and public benchmarks.

- Weakness: CBOW variant lacks motivation, training details, and comparisons to standard CBOW/skip-gram, preventing validation of claimed improvements.

- Weakness: No ablation comparing the proposed frequency-weighted mapping to the original (unweighted) Mikolov mapping.

- Weakness: Missing statistical significance testing despite claims of significant improvements from data selection and co-decoding.

- Weakness: Evaluation and error analysis are limited; more analysis by entity type, boundary errors, and language-specific failures is needed.

- Weakness: Organization issues—Related Work placement and Table 2 location should be adjusted for consistency.

- Question: Provide full details and comparisons for the CBOW variant (decay form, window size, negatives, subsampling, LR, epochs, vocab thresholds, dimensions) versus standard CBOW and skip-gram.

- Question: Provide ablation results comparing weighted vs. unweighted cross-lingual mappings and report language-specific benefits.

- Question: Report statistical significance tests (e.g., bootstrap or approximate randomization) for data selection and co-decoding gains.

- Question: Have you tried or compared exponential decay over context (as in Iacobacci et al., 2016)? If not, please compare or discuss.
