- Summary: Studies weakly supervised cross-lingual NER using annotation projection with heuristic data selection and representation projection via embedding mappings, plus simple co-decoding; combined systems outperform prior cross-lingual baselines on in-house and CoNLL datasets.

Contributions:
- Shows that data selection in annotation projection substantially improves performance, especially under noisy alignments (large F1 gains on Japanese/Korean).
- Proposes simple, effective co-decoding rules (exclude-O confidence and rank-based) that consistently boost performance over individual systems.
- Presents a straightforward representation-projection baseline using monolingual embeddings and a weighted linear mapping that is competitive without target-language annotation.

Strengths:
- Broad evaluation across multiple target languages and both large in-house and CoNLL settings supports generality.
- Clear evidence that simple frequency-based quality scoring and entity-count thresholds for selection yield large gains where alignment is weak.
- Co-decoding reliably improves F1 across datasets and beats prior cross-lingual methods on CoNLL, showing practical utility.
- Methods are practical, relying on widely available resources (alignments, embeddings, small dictionaries) and no target-language supervision.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: techniques are incremental engineering/heuristics and known projection mechanisms.
- Reproducibility gaps: threshold choices (q, n) lack a described tuning procedure; co-decoding details (score calibration, conflict resolution) are unclear.
- No principled guidance for setting thresholds or co-decoding preferences for new languages/domains.
- Many variants presented without ablation: unclear when to prefer NN1 vs NN2 or how to combine more than two systems.
- In-house dataset descriptions are missing (domains, genre, entity distributions), hindering interpretation and replication.

Questions to authors:
- How were q and n chosen per language—was a target-language dev set or cross-validation used? Provide tuning protocol and sensitivity plots.
- Precisely describe the co-decoding procedure: how are O vs non-O scores calibrated and overlaps resolved deterministically?
- Did you try ensembling more than two weakly supervised systems (e.g., AP + NN1 + NN2) or score calibration for fusion?
- Please detail and (if possible) release descriptions or proxies for the in-house datasets (domains, sources, type inventories, label distributions).
