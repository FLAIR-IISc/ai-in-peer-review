- Tackles cross-lingual NER without target annotations using (a) annotation projection with sentence selection and (b) representation projection via linear mapping, plus two co-decoding strategies.

- Annotation projection uses a heuristic sentence-selection based on entity-tag frequency consistency to reduce noisy alignments.

- Representation projection linearly maps target-language embeddings into English (weighted dictionary) to run an English NN NER directly.

- Two co-decoding methods combine outputs: exclude-O confidence-based and a rank-based strategy.

- Combined systems outperform prior cross-lingual NER methods and sometimes approach supervised baselines on in-house and CoNLL languages.

- Key contribution: a simple, effective data-selection heuristic that yields large F1 gains on noisy languages (e.g., +13.9 JP, +20.0 KR).

- Key contribution: a practical, dictionary-based weighted linear mapping for representation projection enabling English-only decoding.

- Key contribution: lightweight, empirically effective co-decoding schemes that reliably improve over single-view systems.

- Strength: strong empirical results across multiple languages and settings with consistent, nontrivial combination gains.

- Strength: methods are simple, computationally light, and scale to new languages without target-side labels or retraining.

- Strength: clear identification of complementary error profiles—annotation projection is high-precision/low-recall, representation projection is more balanced—and use of rank-based decoder exploits this.

- Strength: pragmatic engineering choices (linear mapping, frequency-thresholded dictionary, tuned sentence selection) that are portable.

- Strength: paper is generally clear and well organized, with well-described methods and empirical focus on combination effects.

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty for representation projection—relies on Mikolov-style linear mappings without comparisons to unweighted or orthogonal variants.

- Weakness: insufficient analysis and ablation of the proposed CBOW variant (no motivation, hyperparameters, or comparisons to standard CBOW/skip-gram).

- Weakness: missing statistical significance tests to back claims of “significant” F1 improvements, especially for headline JP/KR gains.

- Weakness: evaluation lacks deeper analysis (per-entity/type errors, boundary vs type errors), sensitivity to dictionary size/quality, q/n thresholds, and co-decoding ablations.

- Weakness: organizational issues—related work interleaved with methods and evaluation tables placed mid-method disrupting flow.

- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—insufficient details on comparable corpora, alignment pipeline, and resource release given dependence on alignment quality and large label sets.

- Ask authors to compare unweighted vs frequency-weighted mappings and unconstrained vs orthogonal Procrustes and report NER impact and weight gains.

- Ask authors for dictionary-size sensitivity curves and threshold selection details (does performance saturate ~5–6K pairs?).

- Ask for CBOW variant details: context window, decay function, hyperparameters, comparisons to standard CBOW/skip-gram, and decay-function choices (exponential vs reciprocal).

- Request significance tests for Table 2 gains and robustness analyses for q and n thresholds, including selection–quantity trade-offs.

- Ask how frequency table T is smoothed and how rare entities are handled to avoid bias toward frequent entities.

- Ask how BIO validity is ensured after merging outputs, how conflicts are resolved, and whether sequence constraints are preserved.

- Ask why exclude-O confidence sometimes underperforms rank-based and whether calibration across models was attempted.

- Suggest moving related work to a standalone earlier section and relocating Section 4.4/Table 2 to the evaluation for better flow.

- Recommend expanding evaluations with ablations (CBOW variant, mapping choices, dictionary size/weights, q/n sensitivity), per-type analyses, and qualitative error cases.

- Suggest citing/comparing to positional/decay-weighted embedding work and newer cross-lingual mappings (CCA, BilBOWA/MultiCCA).

- Request minor clarifications: number of prototype vectors in NN2, regularization/dropout settings, OOV handling, and detailed sentence-/word-level alignment procedures and resource release status.
