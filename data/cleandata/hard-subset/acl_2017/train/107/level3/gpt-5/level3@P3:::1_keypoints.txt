- Paper studies weakly supervised cross-lingual NER with no target annotations, comparing annotation projection and representation projection and proposing co-decoding heuristics.
- Contribution: shows that sentence-level data selection (quality score + entity count) for annotation projection substantially improves F1, especially with noisy alignments.
- Contribution: provides a simple linear embedding mapping baseline for direct transfer and lightweight co-decoding heuristics that consistently boost performance.
- Strength: data selection yields large empirical gains (e.g., +13.9 F1 Japanese, +20.0 Korean) and can outweigh model capacity.
- Strength: methods are simple, broadly applicable, and suitable for low-resource settings without heavy language-specific resources.
- Strength: co-decoding consistently improves over individual systems and offers intuition about complementary error profiles.
- Strength: evaluation covers multiple languages, internal and public datasets, and beats prior cross-lingual baselines on CoNLL.
- Strength: engineering choices (weighted least-squares mapping, dictionary cap, MEMM) are sensible and practically motivated.
- Weakness: limited novelty—core components are incremental and the work is mainly an engineering/empirical study.
- Weakness: unclear how data selection thresholds (q, n) were chosen, raising reproducibility and potential test-set peeking concerns.
- Weakness: paper gives little actionable guidance for tuning selection thresholds on new languages without labeled dev sets.
- Weakness: co-decoding description lacks implementation details (score calibration, span reconciliation, tie-breaking), hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: insufficient transparency about in-house datasets (domains, annotation guidelines, relationship to CoNLL labels).
- Question: how were (q, n) thresholds chosen per language—dev set, unsupervised criteria, or otherwise?
- Question: provide sensitivity analysis of F1 vs. q and n and guidance tied to alignment quality.
- Question: are T(e,l) counts normalized or weighted by alignment/NER confidences; how are multi-token variants and transliterations handled?
- Question: how are confidences computed/calibrated across MEMM and NN; how are overlapping spans and ties resolved; any pseudo-code?
- Question: did you try fusing AP with multiple NN variants simultaneously, and what were the results?
- Question: please detail comparable corpora sources, in-house test set domains, tokenization/alignment tools and settings, and embedding dictionary sizes.
- Question: specify tuning choices for CoNLL experiments and full model/hyperparameter details (features, learning rates, regularization, early stopping).
- Additional: ablation of q(y) vs n(y) vs both would strengthen the selection claim.
- Additional: error analysis by entity type and boundary errors would clarify complementary strengths.
- Additional: report per-language OOV rates and impact of unk vector choice.
- Additional: release scripts for computing T(e,l) and q(y) and a minimal recipe for selecting (q,n) without target dev data.
