- Paper studies weakly supervised cross-lingual NER without target annotations, comparing annotation projection and embedding-based direct transfer, plus two co-decoding heuristics.
- Evaluates annotation projection (English→target via comparable corpora) with a heuristic data-selection filter.
- Evaluates representation projection (linear mapping of target embeddings into English) enabling English-only NN NER to decode target text.
- Proposes two simple co-decoding heuristics: exclude-O confidence and a rank-based priority merge.
- Experiments on four in-house languages and CoNLL Spanish/Dutch/German show consistent gains from data selection and co-decoding.
- Empirical result: alignment-aware data selection yields large improvements when alignments are noisy (e.g., +13.9 F1 Japanese, +20.0 Korean).
- Presents a practical direct-transfer baseline (linear embedding mapping + English NN NER) requiring no target retraining.
- Shows a lightweight co-decoding heuristic that reliably improves over individual components and outperforms prior baselines on CoNLL tests.
- Strong empirical results across diverse languages and setups, sometimes approaching supervised systems trained on large token counts.
- Data selection is shown to matter substantially; quality/coverage filtering improves projection-based NER under low alignment quality.
- Methods are simple and resource-light, requiring only phrase tables/alignment models and monolingual embeddings.
- Analysis of precision–recall tradeoffs justifies the ensemble: annotation projection is high-precision/low-recall, representation projection is more balanced.
- Main weakness: limited methodological novelty; approaches and heuristics are largely incremental.
- Reproducibility concern: data-selection thresholds (q, n) per language are reported but tuning protocol, dev signal, and sensitivity are not specified.
- Insufficient detail on in-house datasets: domain/genre, label inventory, labeling guidelines, and fixed splits are not described.
- Incomplete ablations and missing justification for choices (dictionary size, unconstrained linear mapping, MEMM vs CRF, weighting choices, OOV handling); no significance tests reported.
- Co-decoding underspecified: confidence calibration across models and detailed conflict-resolution behavior for overlapping spans/tags are not analyzed.
- Asks authors how (q, n) were selected per language and what development signal was used given the no-target-annotation setting.
- Requests details on in-house data domains, label inventory, and whether splits are fixed; suggests releasing descriptions or subsets.
- Requests information on alignment resources (corpora/tools) and correlation between alignment quality, q/n, and F1.
- Asks whether orthogonal Procrustes, centering, length-normalization, or regularization were tried for embedding mapping and sensitivity to dictionary size.
- Questions if confidence calibration (e.g., temperature scaling) or a learned meta-decoder were tried for co-decoding.
- Suggests including multilingual/bilingual LSTM-CRF baselines with character features for CoNLL comparisons.
- Requests clarification of exact conflict-resolution rules in rank-based decoding (BIO/BILOU constraints, nested entities, boundary disagreements).
- Asks to report numbers of selected sentences/tokens for all (q, n) tried and provide practical guidance on retained proportions relative to alignment quality.
- Recommends empirical CRF timing/accuracy comparisons to justify MEMM, trying L2 regularization/embedding centering for Eq.7, reporting statistical significance, and releasing code/data for reproducibility.
