- Introduces and releases a novel, large dataset of cybercrime forum posts annotated for products, enabling post- and token-level information extraction.
- Paper is clearly written with thorough experimentation and evaluation of proposed hypotheses.
- Main contribution is the dataset; secondary contribution is characterization of fine-grained domain shift across cybercrime forums.
- Annotation scheme is versatile and the dataset size (93,924 posts, 1,938 annotated) supports varied research applications (forum composition, trends, profiling).
- Experimental evaluation is systematic, using multiple metrics (token-, type-, post-level) and both within-forum and cross-forum settings.
- Problem setting is realistic and impactful for cybersecurity, acknowledging ungrammatical text, spelling errors, and forum-specific jargon.
- Detailed error analysis (OOV rates, failed adaptation techniques) yields useful insights, including that Darkode may be a more general domain than Hack Forums.
- Domain adaptation findings are not highly novel and largely align with known challenges for user-generated content.
- Annotation ambiguity (e.g., labeling “sombody” as product-related) raises concerns about guideline clarity and consistency.
- Baselines are relatively simple; stronger baselines like pretrained language models or richer product lexicons should be explored.
- Formatting adjustments to meet page limits (font size/line spacing) may hurt readability and could be viewed negatively.
- Request clarification on why tokens like “sombody” were annotated as product-related and the criteria for labeling people/service providers.
- Ask which specific cybercrime-domain characteristics (e.g., obfuscation, code-switching) impede existing domain adaptation methods.
- Request information on whether pretrained language models (BERT/RoBERTa) were used or considered and what the results were.
- Suggest rephrasing “frustratingly easy” to a more neutral term; type-level vs token-level evaluation distinction is well-explained.
