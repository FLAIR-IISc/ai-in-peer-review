1. Introduces a novel dataset of product mentions from cybercrime forums; paper is clearly written and experiments are sound with hypotheses well-tested and compared.  
2. Authors appear to have manipulated abstract font size/line spacing to bypass length constraints and fit the 8-page limit.  
3. Task framing is questionable — the core problem is more accurately described as information extraction rather than tagging/chunking/parsing.  
4. Some annotations’ relevance to product identification is unclear (e.g., annotation of “somebody” in Figure 1).  
5. Baselines are too basic; stronger, more sophisticated NLP baselines are feasible and expected.  
6. Domain adaptation experiments largely reconfirm known variations across user-generated communities and do not provide significant new insights.
