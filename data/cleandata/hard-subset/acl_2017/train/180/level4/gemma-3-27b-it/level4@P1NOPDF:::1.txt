This paper introduces a novel dataset comprising product mentions annotated from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clearly written and well-structured, and the experiments are sound. All hypotheses are tested and thoughtfully compared. However, I do have several concerns:

1. The authors appear to have adjusted the font size and line spacing within the abstract to exceed the typical length constraints and meet the 8-page limit.

2. I question whether this paper is best suited for the tagging, chunking, or parsing area. The core task appears to be more accurately classified as information extraction.

3. The relevance of certain annotations to product identification is unclear. For example, the annotation of “somebody” in Figure 1 doesn’t immediately demonstrate a clear connection to a specific product.

4. While the baseline results are presented, they are rather basic. Given the wealth of NLP tools currently available, I believe more sophisticated baselines could have been established with a reasonable amount of additional effort.

5. The domain adaptation experiments largely confirm existing knowledge regarding user-generated data – that variations exist across different online communities (e.g., two video game forums) due to differing user demographics (age, gender, etc.), leading to distinct textual characteristics. Therefore, these experiments do not offer significant new insights into this particular problem.
