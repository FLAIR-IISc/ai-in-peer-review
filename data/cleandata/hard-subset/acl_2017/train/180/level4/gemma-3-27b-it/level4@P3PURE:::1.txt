This paper presents a novel dataset with annotations of products discussed in online cybercrime forums. The paper is clearly written and well-organized, and the experiments are sound. The authors thoroughly test and compare each hypothesis. However, I have several concerns.

First, the authors appear to have adjusted the font size and line spacing of the abstract to exceed the page limit, allowing for a longer abstract and fitting the content within the eight-page constraint. 

Second, the categorization of this paper within the tagging, chunking, and parsing area seems inappropriate; it more closely aligns with the field of information extraction.

Third, the relevance of certain annotations, such as the example of “somebody” in Figure 1, to the product being discussed is unclear. 

Fourth, while the reported results are valid, they are rather basic. Given the wealth of tools currently available in NLP, I believe more elaborate baselines could have been established with a reasonable amount of additional effort.

Finally, the domain adaptation experiments largely confirm existing knowledge regarding user-generated data – specifically, that variations in user demographics (e.g., age, gender) between forums, such as those focused on video games, can lead to significant textual differences. Therefore, these experiments do not offer substantial new insights into this particular problem.
