- Novel dataset of products from cybercrime forums; paper is clearly written, well-organized, and experiments are sound with hypotheses thoroughly tested.  
- Authors appear to have adjusted abstract font size/line spacing to exceed the page limit and fit content within eight pages.  
- Paper is miscategorized: more appropriate for information extraction than tagging/chunking/parsing.  
- Some annotations’ relevance is unclear (e.g., the “somebody” example in Figure 1) with respect to the product.  
- Results are valid but rather basic; stronger, more elaborate baselines could be established using existing NLP tools.  
- Domain adaptation experiments largely confirm known effects of user-generated data (demographic/textual differences) and offer limited novel insight.
