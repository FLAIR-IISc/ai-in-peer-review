This paper introduces a novel dataset comprising product mentions annotated from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clearly written and well-structured, and the experimental evaluation is sound. All hypotheses are tested and results are comparatively analyzed. However, I have several concerns.

1. The authors appear to have adjusted the abstract’s formatting – specifically font size and line spacing – to exceed page limits and accommodate additional content.
2. I question the paper’s suitability for the tagging, chunking, and parsing area; the task more closely aligns with information extraction.
3. The relevance of certain annotations to product identification is unclear. For example, the annotation of “somebody” in Figure 1 is not readily apparent as being product-related.
4. While the initial results are presented, they are rather basic. Given the current state of NLP, more comprehensive baselines could likely have been established with reasonable additional effort.
5. The domain adaptation experiments confirm existing knowledge regarding user-generated data – specifically, that variations in user demographics (e.g., age, gender) across forums like those focused on video games lead to textual differences. Therefore, these results do not offer substantial new insights into this problem.
