- Paper presents a new annotated dataset of products from four cybercrime forums with clear writing and thorough experiments, but the reviewer has several concerns.
- Concern: The authors appear to have altered abstract font size/line spacing to fit the page limit.
- Concern: The paper's area is unclear — it aligns more with information extraction than tagging/chunking/parsing.
- Concern: Some annotation choices (e.g., labeling “somebody” as related to a product) are unclear and need justification.
- Concern: Baseline models are relatively simple; stronger, more modern baselines could and should have been tried.
- Concern: Domain adaptation experiments mainly reaffirm known issues with user-generated data and do not offer substantial new insights.
- Contribution: Release of a novel, hard-to-obtain annotated dataset focused on products in cybercrime forums.
- Contribution: Empirical characterization of domain differences across forums and evaluation of supervised and domain-adaptation methods.
- Contribution: Clear task formulation as a blend of slot-filling information extraction and named entity recognition.
- Strength: Novel dataset provides valuable resources for NLP and security researchers, with helpful dataset statistics.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation across multiple metrics (token-, type-, post-level) and comparison of hypotheses.
- Strength: Clear and well-reasoned problem formulation, with justification for token-level annotation.
- Strength: Detailed error analysis and OOV-rate analysis that illuminate domain-specific challenges.
- Strength: Commitment to releasing data, models, and code to support reproducibility.
- Weakness: Baselines lack complexity given current NLP capabilities (e.g., pre-trained embeddings or stronger models).
- Weakness: Domain-adaptation findings are expected and the analysis of method failures could be deeper.
- Weakness: Annotation guidelines and choices (e.g., “somebody”) need clearer justification and examples.
- Weakness: Presentation uses many tables, making it hard to quickly extract main results; more concise visualization would help.
- Weakness: Formatting manipulation for the abstract raises concerns about adherence to conference guidelines.
- Question: Please provide detailed annotation guidelines and examples to clarify ambiguous annotations like “somebody.”
- Question: Were more sophisticated baselines considered, and if so, why were they excluded?
- Question: What specific characteristics of cybercrime forums make domain adaptation especially challenging?
- Question: What future research directions could address limitations of existing domain-adaptation techniques in this context?
