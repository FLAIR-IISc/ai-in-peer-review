- Novel dataset: annotated product posts from four cybercrime forums (93,924 posts, 1,938 annotated) — main contribution.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical study showing significant domain adaptation challenges even within cybercrime forums.  
- Task formulation: frames product identification as a hybrid of slot-filling information extraction and NER.  
- Writing and clarity: paper is clear, well-written, and hypotheses are tested systematically.  
- Thorough evaluation: comprehensive experiments with multiple methods and metrics (token-, type-, post-level).  
- Domain shift evidence: clear demonstration that models trained on one forum perform poorly on others.  
- Formatting irregularity: abstract formatting changed (font/spacing) to meet page limits, raising guideline concerns.  
- Discipline alignment issue: paper placed under “tagging, chunking, parsing” rather than the more appropriate information extraction area.  
- Annotation ambiguity: unclear rationale for some annotations (e.g., “somebody”) needing clearer inclusion criteria.  
- Weak baselines: used relatively simple baselines; stronger baselines (pre-trained language models) are missing.  
- Limited novelty in domain-adaptation insight: cross-forum degradation aligns with existing knowledge and adds limited new understanding.  
- Question: please justify annotation of ambiguous terms like “somebody” and criteria for non-product relevance.  
- Question: what procedures ensured annotation consistency across the four forums?  
- Question: were pre-trained models (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa) considered as baselines — if not, why?
