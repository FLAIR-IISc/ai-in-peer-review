

Review:  
This paper introduces a novel dataset featuring product annotations derived from online cybercrime forums. The writing is clear, the experiments are well-executed, and all hypotheses are rigorously tested and compared. However, several concerns require attention:  

1.  The abstract's formatting has been altered (e.g., font size and line spacing) to extend its length, enabling the authors to accommodate additional content within the 8-page limit. This circumvents the stated page constraints.  
2.  The work does not align with ACL’s typical focus on tagging, chunking, or parsing. Instead, it addresses an information extraction problem, making its fit within the venue questionable.  
3.  It is unclear why certain annotations, such as "somebody" in Figure 1, are classified as product-related. The rationale for this specific labeling requires clarification.  
4.  The baseline results are superficial. Given current NLP capabilities, more sophisticated baselines could have been implemented with minimal additional effort, offering stronger comparative insights.  
5.  The domain adaptation experiments confirm established patterns in user-generated text (e.g., differing user demographics across video game forums leading to stylistic variations). This does not yield novel insights specific to the problem addressed.
