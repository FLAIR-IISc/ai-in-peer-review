1. Introduces a token-level annotated dataset of 93,924 posts from four cybercrime forums for a novel IE/NER-style product extraction task and shows poor cross-forum transfer.  
2. Contribution: substantial, well-annotated dataset for fine-grained domain adaptation in cybercrime forums.  
3. Contribution: identifies and characterizes domain differences between forums that impede adaptation.  
4. Contribution: empirically shows standard domain adaptation techniques perform poorly for this fine-grained problem.  
5. Strength: dataset is a valuable resource with reasonable inter-annotator agreement and diverse forum product markets.  
6. Strength: clearly demonstrates substantial performance drops when transferring models between forums (e.g., 14.4 F1 drop HF→Darkode).  
7. Strength: defines a clear task bridging IE and NER and provides thoughtful token/phrase/post-level evaluation metric analysis.  
8. Weakness: task definition and annotation guidelines need clarification for ambiguous cases (e.g., what counts as a product vs contextual text).  
9. Weakness: baselines are simplistic and do not leverage modern sequence-labeling models, making comparisons weak for an ACL paper.  
10. Weakness: domain adaptation experiments offer limited new insight beyond known stylistic differences in user-generated content.  
11. Weakness: paper may be misaligned with ACL’s NLP focus and might suit a security venue unless stronger NLP contributions are shown.  
12. Question: clarify how annotators distinguish product mentions from contextual elements with examples.  
13. Question: justify token-level annotation versus phrase-level or slot-filling for multi-word product names.  
14. Question: explain how this dataset/task differs from existing NER datasets in ways that necessitate new domain adaptation techniques.  
15. Question: justify why this work belongs in an NLP venue and specify its NLP contributions.
