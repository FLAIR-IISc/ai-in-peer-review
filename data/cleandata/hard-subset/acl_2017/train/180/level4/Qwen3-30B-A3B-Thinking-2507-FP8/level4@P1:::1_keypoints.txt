- Summary: Introduces a novel dataset/task for product identification in cybercrime forums (93,924 posts, 1,938 token-level annotated) and frames a fine-grained domain adaptation problem across four forums.

- Strength 1: Provides the first annotated resource for product identification in cybercrime marketplaces with careful annotation procedures and high-quality guidelines.

- Strength 2: Thoroughly characterizes fine-grained domain shift, showing substantial performance drops when models transfer across different forums.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive experimental evaluation and analysis (including OOV rates and seen vs. novel product performance) explaining why standard domain adaptation methods fail.

- Weakness 1: Scope misaligned with ACL — framed as information extraction but primarily addresses a domain adaptation problem rather than core ACL tasks.

- Weakness 2: Annotation guidelines lack clear justification for certain labeling decisions (e.g., annotating "sombody"), undermining dataset reliability and reproducibility.

- Weakness 3: Baseline models are overly simplistic; the paper omits stronger modern neural sequence-labeling baselines.

- Weakness 4: Domain adaptation results are unsurprising — cross-forum performance drops for user-generated content are expected and offer limited new insight.

- Weakness 5: Authors manipulated abstract formatting to fit the page limit, violating conference formatting rules.

- Question 1: Please clarify why "sombody" was annotated as part of the product reference in Figure 1.

- Question 2: Why were more sophisticated baselines (e.g., neural sequence-labeling models) not included?

- Question 3: How does this work meaningfully advance domain adaptation beyond the expected cross-forum performance drops?
