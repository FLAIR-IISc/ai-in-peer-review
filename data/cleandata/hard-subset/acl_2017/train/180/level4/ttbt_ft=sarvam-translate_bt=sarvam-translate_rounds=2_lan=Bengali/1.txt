This paper presents a new dataset with product annotations from an online cybercrime forum. The paper is clear and well-written, and the tests are also good. Each hypothesis is tested and compared to each other. However, I have some concerns about this paper:

1. The authors have taken the liberty to change the font size and summary line spacing, which has enabled them to create a longer summary and fit the content within the 8-page requirement.
2. I don't think this paper fits the tagging, chunking, and parsing fields because it is a data extraction problem.
3. I have difficulty understanding why some annotations, such as "Someone" in Figure 1, are related to a product.
4. The fundamental results are very basic, and – using all the tools available in NLP these days – I am sure it would have been possible to create a more detailed baseline without too much extra work.
5. The domain adaptation tests are consistent with what we know about user-generated data, whereas two video game forums, for example, may have different types of users (age, gender, etc.), resulting in very different texts. Therefore, it does not shed new light on this specific problem.
