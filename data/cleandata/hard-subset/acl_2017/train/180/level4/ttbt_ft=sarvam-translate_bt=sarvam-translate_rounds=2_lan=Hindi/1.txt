This research paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products coming from online cybercrime platforms. The paper is clear, well-written, and the experiments are good. Each hypothesis has been tested and compared to each other.

However, I have some concerns about this paper:

1. The authors took the liberty to change the font size and line spacing of the abstract, allowing them to keep a longer abstract and fit the content into the required 8 pages.
2. I don't think this paper fits into the tagging, chunking, and parsing field, as it is more of an information extraction problem.
3. I am having difficulty understanding why some annotations, such as a person in Figure 1, are related to a product.
4. The basic results are really very basic and – with all the tools available in NLP these days – I am sure it was possible to have a more detailed baseline without much additional work.
5. The domain adaptation experiments confirm that we already know about user-generated data, where two platforms on a video game, for example, can have different types of users (age, gender, etc.), resulting in very different texts. Therefore, it does not provide new insights into this specific problem.
