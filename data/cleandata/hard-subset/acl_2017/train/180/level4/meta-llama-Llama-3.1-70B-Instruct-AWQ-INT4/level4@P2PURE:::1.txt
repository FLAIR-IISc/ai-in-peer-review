This paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products from online cybercrime forums, and is generally well-written and clear in its presentation. The experiments conducted are thorough, with each hypothesis tested and compared to others. However, I have several concerns regarding the paper:

1. The authors have altered the font size and line spacing of the abstract, allowing for a longer abstract to be accommodated within the 8-page limit. This modification raises some concerns about adherence to formatting guidelines.

2. Upon reviewing the paper, I question its relevance to the tagging, chunking, and parsing area, as it appears to be more closely related to an information extraction problem.

3. I have difficulty understanding the rationale behind certain annotations, such as "somebody" in Fig. 1, and how they relate to a product.

4. The baseline results presented are somewhat underwhelming, and considering the numerous tools available in NLP, I believe it would have been feasible to establish more sophisticated baselines without excessive additional effort.

5. The domain adaptation experiments confirm existing knowledge about user-generated data, specifically that different online forums (e.g., those focused on video games) may attract distinct types of users (e.g., varying in age and gender), resulting in disparate text styles. As such, these findings do not provide novel insights into this particular problem.
