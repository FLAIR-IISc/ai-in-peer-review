This paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clear and well-written, and the experiments are well-designed. Every hypothesis is tested and compared to each other, which is a strength of the paper.

However, I do have some concerns about the paper. Firstly, the authors have adjusted the font size and line spacing of the abstract to fit more content within the 8-page limit. While this does not affect the content of the paper, it does raise some concerns about formatting.

Secondly, I question whether this paper fits the tagging, chunking, parsing area, as it appears to be more of an information extraction problem. The task of identifying products in online cybercrime forums seems to be a specific instance of information extraction, rather than a general parsing or chunking problem.

Thirdly, I have difficulties understanding why some annotations, such as "sombody" in Figure 1, are related to a product. It is unclear how this annotation contributes to the overall task of identifying products.

Fourthly, the basic results presented in the paper are indeed very basic, and it is surprising that more elaborate baselines were not explored. With the numerous tools available in NLP, it would have been possible to develop more sophisticated baselines without excessive additional work.

Lastly, the domain adaptation experiments, while well-executed, corroborate what is already known about user-generated data. The fact that two forums on different topics can have distinct linguistic characteristics is not a new insight, and it is unclear what new highlights this paper brings to this specific problem.

Main Contributions:
1. A new dataset with annotations of products from online cybercrime forums.
2. A thorough exploration of domain adaptation techniques for this specific task.
3. A clear and well-written presentation of the experiments and results.

Strengths:
1. The paper presents a new dataset that can be useful for future research in information extraction.
2. The experiments are well-designed and thoroughly executed.
3. The paper is clear and well-written, making it easy to follow.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper may not fit the tagging, chunking, parsing area, and may be more suited to the information extraction track.
2. Some annotations are unclear and may require further explanation.
3. The basic results are very basic, and more elaborate baselines could have been explored.
4. The domain adaptation experiments may not bring new insights to the field.

Questions to Authors:
1. Can you provide more explanation for the annotation of "sombody" in Figure 1 and how it relates to the product being sold?
2. Were more elaborate baselines explored, and if not, why not?
3. How do you respond to the criticism that the domain adaptation experiments may not bring new insights to the field?
4. Do you think the paper would be more suitable for the information extraction track, rather than the tagging, chunking, parsing area?
