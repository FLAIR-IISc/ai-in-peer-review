- Dataset of 93,924 posts (1,938 annotated) from four cybercrime forums and a hybrid slot-filling/NER product extraction task with fine-grained domain adaptation.  
- Contribution: a new dataset for product identification in online cybercrime marketplaces to model fine-grained domain differences.  
- Contribution: thorough evaluation of simple baselines and learning-based methods (binary classification, post-level classification, domain adaptation) for product extraction.  
- Contribution: exploration of fine-grained domain adaptation techniques including Brown clusters, type-level annotation, and token-level annotation.  
- Strength: clear, well-written description of the dataset and task with thorough method evaluations.  
- Strength: detailed analysis of results, highlighting strengths and weaknesses of each method.  
- Strength: identifies challenges of fine-grained domain adaptation and effective features/methods for marketplace data.  
- Weakness: domain-specific focus may limit generalizability to other domains.  
- Weakness: some annotations (e.g., "sombody") appear unclear or subjective, risking reliability issues.  
- Weakness: baseline results are basic; more elaborate baselines could have been added with little extra effort.  
- Weakness: domain adaptation experiments largely confirm existing knowledge about user-generated data and may not offer new insights.  
- Question: provide more details on the annotation process and annotator training for consistency/reliability.  
- Question: explain selection of product extraction and domain adaptation methods and whether other methods were considered.  
- Question: include more analysis (error analysis, feature importance) to clarify method strengths and weaknesses.  
- Question: how will the authors address limitations like domain focus and the basic nature of results?
