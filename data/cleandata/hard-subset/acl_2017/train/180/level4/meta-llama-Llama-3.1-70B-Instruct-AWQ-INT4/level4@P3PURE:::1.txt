This paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clear and well-written, and the experiments are well-designed. The authors have thoroughly tested and compared each hypothesis. However, I have some concerns regarding the paper:

1. The authors have altered the font size and line spacing of the abstract, effectively allowing for a longer abstract that fits within the 8-page requirement. While this may be a minor issue, it is worth noting that it deviates from standard formatting guidelines.

2. I question whether this paper is a good fit for the tagging, chunking, parsing area, as it appears to be more focused on information extraction.

3. Upon examining the annotations, I struggle to understand the relevance of certain labels, such as "somebody" in Figure 1, to the product being described. Further clarification on these annotations would be beneficial.

4. The baseline results presented are relatively basic, and given the current state of NLP tools, I believe it would have been feasible to establish more sophisticated baselines without excessive additional effort.

5. The domain adaptation experiments confirm existing knowledge about user-generated data, specifically that different online forums (e.g., video game forums with varying user demographics) can exhibit distinct linguistic characteristics. While these findings are not unexpected, they do not provide new insights into this particular problem.
