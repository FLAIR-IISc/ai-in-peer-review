1. Introduces a novel dataset of products from online cybercrime forums; paper is clear and well-structured.  
2. Experimental design is thorough with hypotheses rigorously tested and compared.  
3. Authors adjusted abstract font size and line spacing to fit a longer summary into the 8-page limit—an unconventional formatting choice.  
4. Paper’s stated focus on tagging/chunking/parsing appears misaligned; the work resembles an information extraction problem.  
5. Some annotations (e.g., Fig. 1) are not clearly linked to specific products, causing potential confusion.  
6. Baseline results are relatively basic; stronger, easily-implemented baselines are missing.  
7. Domain adaptation experiments largely reconfirm known properties of user-generated data across forums and yield limited new insights.
