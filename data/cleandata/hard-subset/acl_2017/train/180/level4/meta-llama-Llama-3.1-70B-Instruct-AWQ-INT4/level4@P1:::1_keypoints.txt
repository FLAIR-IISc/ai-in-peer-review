- Presents a new dataset of product annotations from online cybercrime forums.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, with well-designed experiments and thorough hypothesis comparisons.  
- Authors modified abstract font size/line spacing to fit 8 pages, raising formatting concerns.  
- Task appears to be information extraction rather than tagging/chunking/parsing, suggesting a misfit with the assigned area.  
- Some annotations are unclear (e.g., “sombody” in Figure 1) and their relation to products is unexplained.  
- Reported results are basic; more elaborate baselines were not explored despite available NLP tools.  
- Domain adaptation experiments are well-executed but largely corroborate known facts about user-generated data and add limited novelty.  
- Main contributions: the new dataset, a thorough exploration of domain adaptation techniques for the task, and clear presentation of experiments/results.  
- Strengths: useful dataset for future IE work, thoroughly executed experiments, and clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: possible misclassification of the paper’s area (IE vs tagging/chunking), unclear annotations, simplistic baselines, and limited new insights from domain adaptation.  
- Question: Please explain the “sombody” annotation in Figure 1 and how it relates to the product.  
- Question: Were more elaborate baselines explored, and if not, why?  
- Question: How do authors respond to the critique that the domain adaptation findings lack new insights?  
- Question: Would the paper be more suitable for the information extraction track rather than tagging/chunking/parsing?
