This paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clear and well-written, and the experiments are well-conceived. Every hypothesis is thoroughly tested and compared to one another.

However, I have several concerns about the paper:

1. The authors appear to have altered the font size and line spacing of the abstract, allowing for a longer abstract that fits within the 8-page limit. While I understand the desire to include more content, this approach may not be in line with the submission guidelines.

2. Upon reviewing the paper, I question whether it truly belongs to the tagging, chunking, and parsing area, as it seems to be more closely related to an information extraction problem.

3. I struggle to understand the relevance of certain annotations, such as the example in Fig. 1, which appears to be related to a product. Further clarification on these annotations would be beneficial.

4. The baseline results presented are somewhat basic, especially considering the numerous tools available in NLP today. I believe it would have been feasible to establish more sophisticated baselines with relatively little additional effort.

5. The domain adaptation experiments, while well-executed, largely corroborate existing knowledge about user-generated data. Specifically, they demonstrate that two forums focused on video games, for instance, may attract different types of users (e.g., varying ages and genders), resulting in distinct text styles. Unfortunately, this does not provide new insights into this particular problem.

Overall, while the paper is well-written and the experiments are sound, I believe these concerns need to be addressed to strengthen the paper's contribution to the field.
