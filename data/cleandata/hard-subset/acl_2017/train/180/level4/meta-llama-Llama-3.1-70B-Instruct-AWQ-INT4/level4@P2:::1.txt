This paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clear and well-written, and the experiments are well-designed.

**Summary of the paper:**
The paper introduces a new dataset of posts from online cybercrime marketplaces annotated with product references, which combines information extraction and named entity recognition. The authors explore various methods for fine-grained domain adaptation in this data, including the use of Brown clusters, type-level annotation, and token-level annotation.

**Main contributions:**
1. A new dataset of annotated posts from online cybercrime marketplaces.
2. Exploration of various methods for fine-grained domain adaptation in this data.
3. Evaluation of the effectiveness of these methods in improving performance on the product extraction task.

**Strengths:**
1. The paper presents a clear and well-written introduction to the problem of product extraction in online cybercrime marketplaces.
2. The experiments are well-designed and thorough, with a good range of methods explored.
3. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the results, including a breakdown of performance on seen and unseen products.

**Weaknesses:**
1. The font size and line spacing of the abstract appear to have been altered to fit the content into the 8-page requirement.
2. The paper may not fit neatly into the tagging, chunking, parsing area, as it is more focused on information extraction.
3. Some annotations, such as "sombody" in Figure 1, may not be immediately clear in their relation to a product.
4. The basic results may be considered too basic, given the range of tools available in NLP.
5. The domain adaptation experiments may not provide new insights into the problem of user-generated data, as they corroborate existing knowledge about the differences between online forums.

**Questions to authors:**
1. Can you provide more context or explanation for the annotation of "sombody" in Figure 1?
2. How did you select the specific methods for domain adaptation explored in the paper?
3. Can you discuss the potential applications of the product extraction task in online cybercrime marketplaces?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of font size and line spacing in the abstract?
5. Can you provide more information about the dataset, including the number of posts and the annotation process?
