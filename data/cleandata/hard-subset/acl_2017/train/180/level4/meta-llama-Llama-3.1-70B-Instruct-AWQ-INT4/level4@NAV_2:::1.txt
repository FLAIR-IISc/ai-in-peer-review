This paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products coming from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clear and well-written and the experiments are good. Every hypothesis is tested and compared to each other.

The main contributions of this work are: 
1. A new dataset with annotations of products coming from online cybercrime forums.
2. A task that represents a hybrid of slot-filling information extraction and named entity recognition.
3. An exploration of several methods for fine-grained domain adaptation in this data.

The strengths of this submission are:
1. The paper is clear and well-written, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed task and experiments.
2. The experiments are well-designed and thorough, testing every hypothesis and comparing them to each other.
3. The dataset and annotations provided are a valuable contribution to the field, enabling further research on this task.

The weaknesses of this submission are:
1. The authors' formatting choices, such as changing the font size and line spacing of the abstract, raise concerns about their adherence to formatting guidelines.
2. The paper's fit to the tagging, chunking, parsing area is questionable, as the task presented is more related to information extraction.
3. Some annotations, such as "sombody" in Figure 1, are not clearly related to products and may require further clarification.
4. The basic results presented are indeed basic and could be improved with more elaborate baselines, considering the current state of NLP tools.
5. The domain adaptation experiments, while thorough, corroborate existing knowledge about user-generated data and do not provide new insights into this specific problem.

Questions to Authors:
1. Can you provide further clarification on the annotation guidelines, particularly for cases like "sombody" in Figure 1?
2. How do you plan to address the formatting concerns and ensure adherence to guidelines in future submissions?
3. Can you discuss the potential applications and impact of this dataset and task on the field of NLP and information extraction?
4. Are there plans to explore more advanced baselines or methods for this task, considering the current state of NLP tools and techniques?
