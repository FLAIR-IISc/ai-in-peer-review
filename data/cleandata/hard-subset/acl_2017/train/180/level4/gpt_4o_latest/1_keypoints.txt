- Introduces a novel dataset of product annotations from online cybercrime forums; manuscript is well-structured and experiments are competently executed with thorough hypothesis testing.
- Suspected manipulation of abstract font size/line spacing to fit an 8-page limit.
- Paper is mis-tagged for the tagging/chunking/parsing domain and is better classified as an information extraction task.
- Unclear how certain annotations (e.g., "sombody" in Fig. 1) relate to products.
- Baseline models are rudimentary; stronger off-the-shelf NLP baselines could have been added with little effort.
- Domain adaptation experiments replicate known observations about user-generated content variability and offer no novel insights.
