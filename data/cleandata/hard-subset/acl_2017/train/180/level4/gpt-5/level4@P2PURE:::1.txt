This paper introduces a new dataset with product annotations from online cybercrime forums. The writing is clear, and the experiments are solid; each hypothesis is tested and compared against the others. However, I have several concerns:

1. The authors modified the abstract’s font size and line spacing to extend its length and fit the content within the eight-page limit.
2. The paper does not fit squarely within tagging/chunking/parsing; it is primarily an information extraction problem.
3. It is unclear why certain annotations are treated as products—for example, “somebody” in Fig. 1.
4. The baselines are very rudimentary; given the availability of current NLP tools, more substantial baselines should have been feasible without much additional effort.
5. The domain adaptation experiments mostly confirm what is already known about user-generated content—for instance, that two video game forums may attract different user populations (age, gender, etc.), resulting in markedly different text—so they do not provide new insights on this specific problem.
