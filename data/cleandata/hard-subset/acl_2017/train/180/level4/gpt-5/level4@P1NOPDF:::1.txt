This paper introduces a new dataset with product annotations drawn from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clearly written, and the experiments are thoughtfully designed; each hypothesis is tested and compared against the others. However, I have several concerns:

1. The abstract’s font size and line spacing appear to have been altered, resulting in a longer abstract and enabling more content to fit within the 8-page requirement. This seems to circumvent the formatting guidelines.

2. I do not think the paper fits the tagging/chunking/parsing area; it is primarily an information extraction problem.

3. I have trouble understanding why certain annotations—such as “somebody” in Fig. 1—are treated as product mentions. The criteria linking such tokens to products are unclear.

4. The results are quite basic. Given the range of NLP tools available today, it should be feasible to implement stronger, more informative baselines without substantial additional effort.

5. The domain adaptation experiments largely confirm what is already known about user-generated content: even two forums on the same topic (e.g., video games) can attract different user demographics (age, gender, etc.), leading to markedly different text. As a result, these experiments do not provide new insight into this specific problem.
