- Introduces a new dataset of product annotations from online cybercrime forums.
- Writing is clear and experiments are solid, with hypotheses tested and compared.
- Authors altered abstract font size and line spacing to extend length and fit the eight-page limit.
- Problem is mischaracterized: it is primarily an information extraction task, not tagging/chunking/parsing.
- Annotation choices are unclear (e.g., why “somebody” is labeled a product in Fig. 1).
- Baselines are rudimentary; stronger modern NLP baselines were feasible and expected.
- Domain adaptation experiments largely confirm known quirks of user-generated content and offer no new insights for this task.
