- Introduces a dataset and task for token-level product identification in cybercrime forum posts (1,938 annotated posts from four forums; evaluation within- and cross-forum).  
- Contribution 1: New dataset and task with clear evaluation protocols (token-, type-, and post-level; token-to-NP projection).  
- Contribution 2: Empirical characterization of fine-grained domain shift and systematic evaluation showing limited gains from standard semi-supervised/domain-adaptation techniques.  
- Contribution 3: Baseline models (token-level SVM, post-level latent SVM) and analyses identifying product OOV and stylistic differences as causes of cross-forum degradation.  
- Strength: Valuable dataset and task framing spanning four distinct forums with reasonable inter-annotator agreement and planned release.  
- Strength: Careful evaluation design using multiple metrics and NP projection to capture product phrases for downstream use.  
- Strength: Strong within-forum performance (e.g., Darkode ~80 F1 on product types, ~90% post-level accuracy) supporting downstream analyses.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental comparisons including OOV analysis that explain asymmetric transfer and offer actionable insights.  
- Strength: Authors commit to releasing data, models, and code for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Formatting noncompliance in the abstract (possible font/spacing manipulation) needs correction.  
- Weakness: Track fit concerns—work aligns more with IE/domain adaptation than tagging/chunking/parsing.  
- Weakness: Annotation scope questionable—labeling agent/method terms as product-related may conflate categories and complicate learning/evaluation.  
- Weakness: Baselines are limited relative to current practice; stronger neural and CRF baselines could change conclusions about absolute performance.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty in adaptation findings—results largely corroborate known domain-transfer challenges and rely on standard techniques with mostly negative results.  
- Question: Did you alter the abstract’s font size or line spacing to fit page limits?  
- Question: What fraction of annotations are agent/method tokens and what happens if they are excluded or split into separate labels?  
- Question: Why not annotate minimal NP spans directly, and how sensitive are results to the NP projection heuristic and parser errors?  
- Question: Can you add stronger baselines (BiLSTM-CRF with pretrained embeddings/char features, CRF with global features, neural post-level models leveraging titles)?  
- Question: Will the full unlabeled corpus, annotation guidelines, and code be released, and under what license/access constraints?  
- Question: Can you better isolate stylistic vs. lexical factors in domain adaptation (e.g., mask product tokens, swap titles, control thread structure)?  
- Overall: Dataset and study are valuable and careful, but concerns remain about formatting compliance, venue fit, annotation scope, baseline strength, and adaptation novelty.
