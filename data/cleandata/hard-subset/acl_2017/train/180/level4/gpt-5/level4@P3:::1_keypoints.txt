- Paper introduces a token-level product identification task and dataset from cybercrime forums, evaluating IE/NER-style annotations and cross-forum generalization.
- Releases a dataset of 93,924 posts (1,938 annotated) from four forums with token-level labels and annotation guidelines, providing a noisy, realistic testbed.
- Presents careful evaluation protocol with token, type-per-post, post-level metrics, NP projection, and a post-level latent SVM targeting one product per post.
- Strength: valuable, diverse dataset covering multiple market segments and realistic forum noise; reasonable inter-annotator agreement and commitment to release data/code.
- Strength: thoughtful evaluation views (token/type/post-level) and NP projection to match downstream needs.
- Strength: solid baselines including token/NP SVMs and a post-level latent SVM; strong within-forum performance (e.g., NP F1 ≈ 78, post accuracy ≈ 90%).
- Strength: thorough cross-domain evaluation and diagnostics showing severe cross-forum degradation and causes (OOV, Darkode coverage, adaptation directionality).
- Strength: reproducibility details provided (preprocessing, splits, statistical testing).
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty; reliance on linear SVMs and missing stronger sequence/neural baselines (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF, neural phrase models).
- Weakness: annotation scope ambiguous—agents/methods sometimes labeled as products; needs quantification and impact analysis.
- Weakness: moderate inter-annotator agreement (κ ~0.58–0.66) and potential bias from ignoring middle lines beyond first/last 10; more error analysis and sensitivity checks needed.
- Weakness: domain adaptation findings mainly confirm known patterns; further insights or successful adaptation methods would strengthen the paper.
- Weakness: abstract appears to violate formatting guidelines and needs correction.
- Question: How often are agent/method tokens annotated, what is their κ vs core product tokens, and do models over-predict them?
- Question: How do CRF and BiLSTM-CRF (with pre-trained embeddings and char features) perform, especially cross-forum?
- Question: Have the authors tried domain-adversarial training, self-/tri-training, label shift correction, or larger/in-domain embeddings versus Brown clusters?
- Question: What proportion of gold products fall outside the first/last 10 lines, and how does removing that constraint affect performance?
- Question: Will NP projections, gazetteers, and code to reproduce metrics/splits be released, and what redaction/ethical safeguards are in place?
- Comment: Task fits better under information extraction/domain adaptation than tagging/chunking tracks.
- Comment: Several references contain formatting errors and placeholders that should be fixed.
- Overall: Strong dataset and analysis paper; accept if camera-ready adds at least one contemporary sequence/neural baseline and clarifies annotation policy impacts.
