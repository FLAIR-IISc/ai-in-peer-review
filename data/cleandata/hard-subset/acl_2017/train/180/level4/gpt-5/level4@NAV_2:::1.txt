This paper introduces a new dataset with product annotations derived from online cybercrime marketplaces. The manuscript is clear, well-structured, and the experimental setup is reasonable. The hypotheses are explicitly tested and compared against one another. That said, I have several concerns:

1. It appears the authors modified the abstract’s formatting (font size and line spacing), effectively allowing a longer abstract and enabling the paper to fit within the 8-page limit.
2. The submission seems misaligned with the tagging/chunking/parsing area; the core problem is closer to information extraction.
3. Some annotation choices are hard to justify—for example, it is unclear why sombody in Fig. 1 is treated as product-related.
4. The “basic results” are indeed quite basic; with today’s NLP tooling, more robust baselines should be achievable with modest additional effort.
5. The domain adaptation experiments largely confirm established findings for user-generated content—e.g., different forums (even within the same overarching topic) can attract distinct user populations and writing styles—so the work offers limited new insights on this front.
