This paper introduces a new dataset with product annotations drawn from online cybercrime forums. The manuscript is clear and well-written, and the experiments are solid. Each hypothesis is evaluated and compared against the others. That said, I have several concerns:

1. The authors modified the abstract’s font size and line spacing, which allowed them to extend the abstract and fit the content within the 8-page requirement.
2. I do not think this work falls under tagging, chunking, or parsing; it is more appropriately framed as an information extraction problem.
3. I find it hard to see why certain annotations—such as sombody in Fig. 1—are considered product-related.
4. The baseline results are quite rudimentary, and given the current availability of NLP tools, more sophisticated baselines should have been achievable without substantial extra effort.
5. The domain adaptation experiments largely confirm what is already known about user-generated content—e.g., two video game forums can attract different demographics (age, gender, etc.), resulting in markedly different text—so they do not offer new insights into this specific issue.
