Summary
- This paper introduces a new dataset with annotations of products from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clear and well written, and the experiments are generally solid. Each hypothesis is tested and compared against others.

Concerns
1. Formatting compliance: The abstract appears to use reduced font size and altered line spacing, effectively lengthening the abstract and helping to fit the content within the 8-page limit. This does not adhere to the submission formatting requirements.
2. Topical fit: The work does not fit well within the tagging, chunking, and parsing area; it is more accurately characterized as an information extraction problem.
3. Annotation justification: It is difficult to understand why some annotations (e.g., “somebody” in Fig. 1) are related to a product.
4. Baselines and results: The reported results are quite basic. Given the range of readily available NLP tools, it should have been possible to include more elaborate baselines without substantial extra effort.
5. Domain adaptation insights: The domain adaptation experiments largely corroborate what is already known about user-generated content—for example, two video game forums may attract different user demographics (age, gender, etc.), leading to markedly different texts—so they do not provide new insights on this specific problem.
