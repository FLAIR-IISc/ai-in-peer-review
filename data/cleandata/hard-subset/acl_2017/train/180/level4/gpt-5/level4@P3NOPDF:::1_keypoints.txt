- Summary: Presents a new dataset for product mentions in cybercrime forums, plus extraction models and cross-forum domain adaptation; clearly written and experimentally sound.

- Strength: Timely, impactful problem with clear practical relevance to cybercrime forums.

- Strength: Clear hypotheses and systematic comparisons across experimental conditions.

- Strength: Dataset could be a useful benchmark if released with documentation and tooling.

- Strength: Writing is mostly clear and experiments are broadly reproducible.

- Concern 1 (Formatting): Abstract appears to violate ACL template (smaller font/spacing) and must be restored to template defaults to meet page limits.

- Concern 2 (Fit to track): Task is closer to information extraction than tagging/chunking; authors should explicitly cast it as sequence labeling or submit to a more appropriate venue/track.

- Concern 3 (Annotation clarity): Definitions and boundary guidelines for “product” are unclear (e.g., questionable labels like “somebody”); require formal definition and decision rules.

- Concern 3 (Requested details): Provide label schema, whether services are included, inter-annotator agreement (span/token and κ), adjudication protocol, representative examples/counterexamples, and dataset statistics.

- Concern 4 (Baselines): Current baselines are weak; add strong sequence labelers (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF with char encoders), pretrained embeddings/subword features, rule-based/distant supervision heuristics, ablations, and robust token/span evaluation with significance testing.

- Concern 5 (Domain adaptation): Cross-forum analysis lacks novelty; include stronger adaptation methods (feature augmentation, instance reweighting, SCL, domain-adversarial or shared–private models).

- Concern 5 (Diagnostics): Add diagnostics like topic/lexical overlap, divergence measures (JS on n-grams), label vs covariate shift, per-label transfer results, few-shot learning curves, temporal splits, and per-forum error taxonomies.

- Additional (Reproducibility): Provide preprocessing, splits, hyperparameters, seeds, code links, licensing, redactions, and access procedures.

- Additional (Ethics): Discuss data provenance, legality, privacy/PII protections, misuse risks, mitigation, and IRB/ethics review status.

- Additional (Presentation): Improve figure legibility and ensure examples match final annotation guidelines (fix/explain Fig. 1 labeling issue).

- Overall assessment: Important and potentially valuable dataset with clear writing and careful experiments, but major issues remain—template compliance, annotation validation, stronger baselines, and deeper domain-adaptation analysis—before strong publication.
