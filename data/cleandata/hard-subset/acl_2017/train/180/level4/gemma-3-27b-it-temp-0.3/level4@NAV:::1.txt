This paper introduces a novel dataset comprising annotations of products discussed within online cybercrime forums. The manuscript is well-written and clearly presented, and the experimental design is sound. The authors thoroughly investigate their hypotheses through comprehensive comparative analysis. However, I have several concerns regarding the submission:

1. The abstract appears to have been adjusted – through modifications to font size and line spacing – to exceed typical length constraints and meet the page limit.
2. The paper’s focus seems more aligned with information extraction than traditional tagging, chunking, or parsing tasks.
3. The relevance of certain annotations, such as the example provided in Fig. 1, to the identification of products is unclear.
4. While the initial results are presented, they are relatively straightforward. Given the current state of NLP tooling, I believe more sophisticated baselines could have been established with reasonable effort.
5. The domain adaptation experiments largely confirm existing knowledge regarding user-generated content; the observed differences between forums (e.g., those focused on video games) likely reflect variations in user demographics, a phenomenon already well-documented in the literature. Consequently, these results do not offer significant new insights into the problem.
