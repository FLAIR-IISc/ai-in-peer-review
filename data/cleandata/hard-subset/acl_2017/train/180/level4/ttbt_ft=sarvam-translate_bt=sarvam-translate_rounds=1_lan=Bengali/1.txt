This paper presents a new dataset with product annotations from online cybercrime forums. The paper is clear and well-written, and the experiments are also good. Each hypothesis has been tested and compared to each other. However, I have some concerns about this paper:

1. The authors have taken the liberty to change the font size and summary line spacing, which has enabled them to create a longer summary and fit the content within the 8-page requirement.
2. I don't think this paper fits the tagging, chunking, and parsing field, as it is a data extraction problem.
3. I have difficulty understanding why some annotations, such as someone in Figure 1, are related to a product.
4. The fundamental results are very basic, and - using all the tools available in NLP these days - I am sure it would have been possible to create a more detailed baseline without much additional work.
5. The domain adaptation tests are consistent with what we know about user-generated data, whereas in two video game forums, for example, there may be different types of users (age, gender, etc.), which could result in very different texts. So, it does not shed new light on this specific problem.
