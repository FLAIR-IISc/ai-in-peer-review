This research paper presents a new dataset with annotations of products coming from online cybercrime platforms. The paper is clear, well-written, and the experiments are good. Each hypothesis has been tested and compared to each other.

However, I have some concerns about this paper:

1. The authors took the liberty to change the font size and line spacing of the abstract, allowing them to keep a longer abstract and fit the content to the required 8 pages.
2. I don't think this paper fits into the tagging, chunking, and parsing field, as it is more of an information extraction problem.
3. I am having difficulty understanding why some annotations, such as a person in Figure 1, are related to a product.
4. The basic results are really very basic and – with all the tools available in NLP these days – I am sure it would have been possible to have a more detailed baseline without much extra work.
5. The domain adaptation experiments confirm what we already know about user-generated data, where two platforms on video games, for example, can have different types of users (age, gender, etc.), resulting in very different texts. Therefore, it does not offer new insights on this specific problem.
