This work introduces a novel dataset featuring product annotations sourced from online cybercrime forums. The manuscript is well-structured and clearly articulated, with a sound experimental design where each hypothesis is methodically tested and compared. Nevertheless, I have the following concerns regarding the submission:

1.  It appears the authors have deviated from the required formatting by modifying the font size and line spacing of the abstract, seemingly to accommodate a more extensive abstract while adhering to the 8-page limit.
2.  I question the paper's fit within the "tagging, chunking, parsing" area, as the core problem aligns more closely with information extraction.
3.  The justification for certain annotation choices is difficult to understand. For instance, it is not immediately obvious how the annotation for "sombody" in Figure 1 relates to a product.
4.  The baseline results are overly simplistic. Given the array of sophisticated NLP tools readily available, establishing more elaborate baselines would have been feasible with minimal additional effort.
5.  The domain adaptation experiments merely corroborate well-established findings about user-generated content—that is, different online communities (like two distinct video game forums) produce different texts due to their unique user demographics. Consequently, this analysis does not offer significant new insights into the problem.
