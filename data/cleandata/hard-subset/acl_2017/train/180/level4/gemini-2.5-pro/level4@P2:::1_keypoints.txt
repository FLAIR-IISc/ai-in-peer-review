- Summary: New dataset/task for product identification in cybercrime forums—1,938 annotated posts across 4 forums (treated as fine-grained domains); shows strong cross-forum domain-adaptation gaps and limited gains from standard adaptation methods; full 93k+ post dataset to be released.  
- Contribution: Releases a novel, high-impact annotated dataset for product identification in noisy cybercrime marketplace text.  
- Contribution: Provides a systematic empirical analysis and baselines quantifying fine-grained domain adaptation performance drops across forums.  
- Strength: Well-motivated and socially relevant task with clear utility for security researchers.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation using token-, type-, and post-level metrics plus OOV analysis that explains asymmetric transfer effects.  
- Strength: Clear, methodical exposition of task, annotation, models, and experiments.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—finding that domain mismatch hurts transfer is expected and the paper offers few new solutions.  
- Weakness: Model choices are potentially weak (SVMs); omission of stronger sequence-labeling baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF) may understate achievable performance.  
- Weakness: Annotation guidelines are ambiguous (agent vs product), possibly causing lower inter-annotator agreement and inconsistent labels.  
- Weakness: Minor formatting issue—abstract uses smaller font/line spacing than main text.  
- Question: Why were agentive terms (e.g., “coder,” “somebody”) annotated as products, how was annotator consistency ensured, and why not narrow the task to goods/services?  
- Question: Have you evaluated stronger context-aware sequence models (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF) to see if the domain adaptation gap persists?
