1. Novel dataset of product annotations from cybercrime forums; manuscript is well-structured with a sound experimental design.  
2. Authors likely modified abstract formatting (font size/line spacing) to fit the 8-page limit.  
3. Paper may be misclassified—the core problem fits information extraction more than tagging/chunking/parsing.  
4. Some annotation decisions are not well-justified (e.g., how "sombody" in Figure 1 qualifies as a product).  
5. Baseline models are overly simplistic; stronger, readily available NLP baselines should have been included.  
6. Domain adaptation results largely repeat known findings about community-specific user-generated content and offer limited new insight.
