- Novel annotated dataset for extracting products/services in online cybercrime forums; valuable for an under-resourced domain.  
- Paper is clear, well-motivated, and logically structured.  
- Experiments are methodical and provide baseline performance and domain-adaptation exploration.  
- Track misalignment: better suited to an IE/NLP Applications track than "Tagging, Chunking, and Parsing."  
- Annotation guidelines lack clarity and rationale for some labels (e.g., why "somebody" is annotated); include more examples or the annotation manual.  
- Baseline models are too basic; stronger baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF) are needed to provide a robust benchmark.  
- Domain-adaptation results reiterate known generalization issues but lack deeper qualitative/error analysis to explain linguistic causes.  
- Possible formatting violation of ACL 2017 style (abstract/font/spacing) to meet page limit; verify compliance.  
- Overall: dataset is the main strength, but evaluation depth and documentation should be improved.
