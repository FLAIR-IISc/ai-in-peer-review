- Introduces product identification in cybercrime forums as a fine-grained domain adaptation task and provides a new annotated dataset from four distinct forums.  
- Shows that supervised models trained on one forum perform poorly on others, establishing a challenging cross-domain benchmark.  
- Publicly releases a valuable dataset that enables further NLP and cybersecurity research.  
- Conducts rigorous, multi-metric evaluation (token, type, post-level) and analyzes OOV recall and asymmetric transfer difficulties between forums.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and commits to releasing data, annotations, and code for reproducibility.  
- Relies on standard SVM-based models and omits experiments with modern neural architectures, limiting technical novelty and baseline strength.  
- Demonstrates that common adaptation techniques yield limited gains but does not propose a more effective adaptation method.  
- Uses a headword-only annotation scheme (not full product spans), which discards context and depends on external parsers that may introduce errors.
