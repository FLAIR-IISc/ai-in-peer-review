- New public dataset: >93K posts from four cybercrime forums with ~2K posts manually annotated for product mentions and released with code/models.

- Characterization of fine-grained domain adaptation: Empirically shows models trained on one forum suffer large performance drops on others within cybercrime.

- Comprehensive benchmarking and analysis: Establishes baselines, uses multiple evaluation metrics (token/type/post-level), and shows standard adaptation methods are of limited effectiveness.

- Valuable and well-motivated resource: High-impact, under-resourced domain with public release of data and tooling for the community.

- Thorough task definition and annotation: Clear, context-sensitive task design with iterative guideline refinement and reported inter-annotator agreement.

- Strong empirical analysis and honest framing: Robust in-domain, cross-domain, and adaptation experiments with transparent reporting of limitations.

- Multi-faceted evaluation: Token, NP, type, and post-level metrics provide a nuanced view of system behavior for different downstream needs.

- Limited novelty of models: Experiments rely on standard techniques (linear SVMs) and omit more modern sequence-labeling baselines like BiLSTM-CRF.

- Potential information loss in annotation scheme: Annotating only headwords loses specificity and forces projection onto parsed NPs, introducing parser-error confounds.

- Lack of justification for some hyperparameters: Choices like 50 Brown clusters and 5× upweighting of target data are under-motivated and lack sensitivity analysis.

- Why single headword annotation?: Request for explanation of choosing headword-only annotation, whether span annotation was tried, and impact of parser errors on NP evaluation.

- What stylistic differences matter?: Request for qualitative examples/analysis of stylistic differences (jargon, structure, complexity) beyond product inventory affecting context features.

- Robustness to small target data?: Question whether alternative regularization or domain-incorporation methods were tried to avoid overfitting when adding limited target-domain data.
