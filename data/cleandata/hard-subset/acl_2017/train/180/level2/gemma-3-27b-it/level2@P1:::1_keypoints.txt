- Introduces a new dataset and task for identifying products in cybercrime forums, with token-level annotations across four forums.
- Dataset is the core contribution; obtaining and annotating cybercrime forum data is valuable and well-documented (size, effort, IAA).
- Shows that standard NLP/domain-adaptation methods struggle to generalize across fine-grained forum domains.
- Provides detailed analysis of cross-forum challenges, motivating further targeted research.

- Strength: Released dataset is a strong, rare resource for security and NLP research.
- Strength: Task is well-motivated and sensibly framed as a hybrid of slot-filling and NER with token-level labels.
- Strength: Evaluation is thorough, using multiple metrics and baselines, and explores OOV rates, Brown clusters, and gazetteers.
- Strength: Clearly identifies domain-adaptation difficulties and sets up future work.

- Weakness: Methodological novelty is limited; adaptation techniques are standard and not tailored to cybercrime forums.
- Weakness: Analysis of why forums differ is superficial; lacks deeper linguistic, product-type, and cultural investigation.
- Weakness: Choice and motivation for some evaluation metrics (e.g., post-level accuracy) are not fully justified.
- Weakness: Missing detailed error analysis to illuminate common model failures and guide improvements.

- Question: Please clarify forum access, data collection procedures, and any ethical considerations in obtaining the data.
- Question: Was there any correlation between annotator security expertise and annotation quality or disagreement rates?
- Question: Can you propose or evaluate alternative, more sensitive post-level metrics than the conservative one used?
- Question: Please elaborate on specific characteristics of Darkode vs. Hack Forums that explain generalization differences.

- Comment: Table 1 formatting hampers quick comparison across forums.
- Comment: Add discussion of potential dataset biases given the data source.
- Comment: Some sections would benefit from more concrete examples to illustrate points.
