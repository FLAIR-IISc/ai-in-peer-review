- Introduces a novel, token-level annotated dataset of 93,924 posts from four cybercrime forums for identifying bought/sold products.
- Claims cybercrime forums are challenging “fine-grained” NLP domains and standard domain adaptation methods are ineffective.
- Core contribution is the dataset with token-level product annotations; secondary contribution is characterizing domain differences; tertiary is baseline models and adaptation attempts.
- Strength: dataset novelty and importance for security research, with a reasonable size and detailed annotation scheme.
- Strength: task is well-motivated as a slot-filling/NER combination, enabling granular analysis and useful downstream applications.
- Strength: thorough error analysis and domain characterization, including OOV analysis and observations about product coverage and forum differences.
- Weakness: limited scope of domain adaptation experiments—few methods tested, little hyperparameter tuning, and focus on product inventories over stylistic differences.
- Weakness: annotation scheme is complex and domain-expert reliant; inter-annotator agreement is moderate (κ = 0.58–0.77) and exclusion rules (first/last 10 lines) may introduce bias.
- Weakness: evaluation metrics choice and reporting are not fully justified or clearly presented; NP-level F1 emphasis lacks clear rationale and tables are dense.
- Question: please detail the annotation adjudication process and which guidelines or examples caused disagreement.
- Question: how sensitive are results to annotation variability, and would a simpler scheme with higher agreement yield similar findings?
- Question: describe the “case studies” enabled by the dataset and how fine-grained annotations contributed.
- Question: what are the plans for maintaining/expanding the dataset, adding forums, and ensuring annotation consistency?
- Additional comment: expand related work to include IE from social media and other noisy text sources.
- Additional comment: clarify the precise meaning of “fine-grained domain adaptation” (domain granularity vs. adaptation difficulty).
- Additional comment: include a table summarizing key characteristics of each forum to clarify domain differences.
