## Review of “Identifying Products in Online Cybercrime Marketplaces: A Dataset and Fine-grained Domain Adaptation Task”

This paper introduces a new dataset of posts from four online cybercrime forums, annotated for product mentions, and investigates the challenges of applying NLP models across these forums due to significant domain differences. The authors correctly identify the difficulty of transferring knowledge even within the specific domain of cybercrime, framing this as a fine-grained domain adaptation problem. The primary contribution is the publicly released dataset, which is valuable for researchers interested in this challenging area. A secondary contribution is the empirical demonstration of the difficulty of adapting models across these forums, even with standard domain adaptation techniques. Finally, the paper provides a clear characterization of the domain differences and initial exploration of adaptation strategies.

The strengths of the paper lie in the relevance of the research area, the creation of a unique and potentially impactful dataset, and the thorough empirical evaluation. The ablation studies examining different adaptation methods are particularly strong, providing insights into why existing techniques struggle. The clear presentation of the annotation process and statistics, including inter-annotator agreement, enhances the reproducibility and credibility of the work.  The thoughtful discussion of evaluation metrics – token-level, type-level, and post-level – demonstrates a nuanced understanding of the task.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. The proposed solutions for domain adaptation (Brown clusters, gazetteers) offer limited improvement. While the authors acknowledge this, deeper analysis into *why* these techniques fail would be beneficial. The analysis could benefit from a more thorough examination of the linguistic differences between forums beyond simply differing product vocabularies.  The discussion of related work feels somewhat brief, particularly regarding recent advances in domain adaptation within NLP. While the paper correctly identifies the limitations, the concluding remarks lack concrete directions for future work, beyond simply stating the need for improved adaptation techniques. 

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more qualitative examples illustrating the specific linguistic differences between the forums that contribute to the adaptation challenges?
2.  Given the limited success of existing adaptation techniques, what specific characteristics of cybercrime forum language (e.g., code-switching, slang, obfuscation) make this task particularly difficult?
3.  The paper mentions an "anon. work in press" utilizing this dataset. Could you briefly describe the insights gained from that work, as it might provide further motivation or direction for future research in this area?
