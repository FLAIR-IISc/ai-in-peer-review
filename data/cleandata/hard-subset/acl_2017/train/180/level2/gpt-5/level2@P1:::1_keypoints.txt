- New IE task/dataset for identifying products in cybercrime forums: token-level annotations with post-level utility, four forums, cross-forum transfer framed as fine-grained domain adaptation.  
- Contribution — dataset & task: ~94k posts (1,938 annotated) with context-sensitive product annotations and multiple evaluation views (token, NP via projection, type, post-level).  
- Contribution — empirical characterization: systematic evidence of severe cross-forum degradation, analysis of OOV effects, and evaluation of adaptation strategies (Brown clusters, gazetteers, small target labeled data + EasyAdapt).  
- Contribution — baselines: clear SVM-based token and post-level models with transparent features and training protocols that perform well in-domain.  
- Strength — clear problem definition and motivation: task design aligns with downstream uses and multiple evaluation metrics are well-justified.  
- Strength — substantial, carefully curated dataset: four distinct forums, annotation choices documented, and reasonable inter-annotator kappas (~0.58–0.77).  
- Strength — solid baselines and modeling: token SVMs and post-level latent SVM show strong in-domain performance (e.g., ~80 type F1, ~90% post accuracy on Darkode).  
- Strength — thorough cross-domain analysis: quantification of in-domain vs. cross-forum gaps, OOV/recall analysis, and learning curves for small target supervision.  
- Strength — reproducibility and impact: plan to release data, models, and code to set a benchmark for fine-grained domain adaptation.  
- Weakness — span annotation/evaluation mismatch: annotating only head tokens and projecting to NPs via a parser introduces noise and potential bias (NP≤7 heuristic and parser errors).  
- Weakness — limited adaptation techniques and parameterization: Brown clusters fixed at 50 and omission of stronger alternatives (varying cluster sizes, embeddings, pivot features, instance reweighting, domain-adversarial methods).  
- Weakness — constrained deep baselines: no evaluated BiLSTM-CRF or neural models with character/subword or domain-adversarial components for OOV handling.  
- Weakness — gazetteer supervision setup underspecified: construction procedure, annotation budget, and when gazetteeers help are not fully detailed; gains are modest.  
- Weakness — ethical/release details lacking: need clearer data acquisition/usage policies, de-identification steps, and licensing/terms-of-use for this sensitive domain.  
- Question — report post-level inter-annotator agreement (Jaccard/MASI) and/or NP-span agreement on a subset to complement token-level kappa.  
- Question — sensitivity to NP length threshold and parser choice; have forum/social-media parsers or chunkers been tried to reduce parsing errors?  
- Question — effect of increasing Brown cluster granularity (100–1000) and use of embeddings trained on combined unlabeled forum data with character/subword features?  
- Question — for small target supervision, provide per-POS gains (nouns vs. verbs) and per-product-frequency-bin effects to clarify where adaptation helps.  
- Question — how was the target gazetteer constructed in practice (posts scanned, time budget, selection criteria); can a time-limited protocol be simulated?  
- Question — do titles vs. bodies contribute differently across forums; would a title-aware feature improve robustness?  
- Comment — consider a temporal split to evaluate robustness to time drift within a forum.  
- Comment — include error analysis by category (generic nouns, verbs, service vs. tool) to guide method design.  
- Comment — ablate the first/last-10-lines rule to assess its impact on training/evaluation bias.  
- Comment — releasing an auxiliary list of product paraphrases/synonyms discovered would aid type-level evaluation and downstream use.
