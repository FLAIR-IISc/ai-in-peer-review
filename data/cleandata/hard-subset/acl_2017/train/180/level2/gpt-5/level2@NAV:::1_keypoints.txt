- Introduces a new IE task: identify products bought/sold in cybercrime forums with a multi-forum annotated dataset.
- Defines token-level annotations with NP-level projection and proposes token, type-per-post, and post-level evaluation metrics.
- Releases a publicly available multi-forum dataset and clear annotation protocol for product identification.
- Provides a thorough empirical study of fine-grained cross-forum domain shift, including OOV/product-coverage analysis.
- Proposes and evaluates a simple post-level latent SVM that achieves ~90% within-forum post-level accuracy and competitive NP-level F1.
- Uses SVM token models, Brown clusters, small gazetteers, and limited token-supervised adaptation for cross-forum experiments.
- Shows substantial degradation out-of-domain with statistically grounded comparisons (e.g., Darkode F1 drop from ~77.6 to ~63.2).
- Annotation process is careful with iterative guidelines and reasonable inter-annotator agreement (κ ≈ 0.58–0.77).
- Hybrid token/NP/post evaluation design is well-motivated and aligns with practical security use cases.
- Practical insight: strong post-level model suggests feasibility for large-scale forum characterization despite noisy text.
- Limited modeling novelty: baselines and adaptation methods are standard and adaptation gains are small or insignificant.
- Dataset trimming to first/last 10 lines may bias distributions; more analysis on this choice is needed.
- Span identification depends on parser projection with no direct span-annotated evaluation, risking parser-error confounds.
- Reproducibility and ethics concerns: dataset availability unclear, and limited discussion of ethics, de-identification, and licensing.
- Lacks comparison to stronger sequence-labeling baselines (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF, domain-adversarial methods) that could change conclusions.
- Asks authors to quantify the effect of the first/last-10-lines rule on performance.
- Requests robustness checks for different NP projection thresholds and parsers.
- Requests inclusion of an ethics statement, de-identification details, and clear licensing, plus stronger baseline comparisons.
