- Introduces a new task: identifying products in cybercrime forums via a hybrid slot-filling and NER formulation.  
- Releases an annotated dataset of 93,924 posts from four forums.  
- Shows substantial domain differences and limited effectiveness of standard semi-supervised and domain-adaptation techniques.  
- Proposes fine-grained adaptation methods: Brown clusters, gazetteers, and token-level annotation.  
- Contribution: new annotated cybercrime marketplace dataset combining IE and NER.  
- Contribution: empirical characterization of fine-grained domain adaptation challenges.  
- Contribution: evaluation of several fine-grained adaptation techniques.  
- Strength: novel and interesting task bridging IE and NER.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of domain differences and adaptation challenges.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation of multiple adaptation methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on annotation process and inter-annotator agreement.  
- Weakness: limited insights into dataset/forum characteristics that affect results.  
- Weakness: unclear why some adaptation methods (Brown clusters, gazetteers) underperform.  
- Question: provide more details on annotation procedures and agreement metrics.  
- Question: how will you prevent overfitting when using domain-specific adaptation features?  
- Question: can you describe dataset/forum characteristics and their impact on results?  
- Additional comment: paper is well-written, clear, and presents results concisely.
