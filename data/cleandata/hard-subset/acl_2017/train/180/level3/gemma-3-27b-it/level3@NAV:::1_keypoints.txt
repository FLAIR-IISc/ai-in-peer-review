- Primary contribution: new annotated dataset of product mentions from four online cybercrime forums, released for research use.
- Secondary contribution: detailed characterization showing fine-grained domain differences cause performance degradation even between similar forums.
- Thorough experimental setup and evaluation with multiple metrics and baselines, providing a foundation for future work.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written; example annotations (Figure 1) help clarify the scheme.
- Authors explore various domain adaptation techniques, though results are not strongly positive.
- Formatting manipulation to meet page limits detracts from readability.
- Task framing is questionable: fundamentally an information extraction/IE problem, not tagging/chunking/parsing as presented.
- Baseline models are overly simplistic; stronger, readily available baselines (e.g., fine-tuned BERT/RoBERTa) are missing.
- Domain adaptation findings are largely expected and offer limited novel insight beyond confirming known issues with user-generated content.
- Some annotations appear unclear or irrelevant (e.g., “sombody” in Figure 1), raising concerns about annotation consistency.
- Question: justify framing as tagging/chunking/parsing rather than pure information extraction.
- Question: explain why pre-trained language model baselines were not included in experiments.
- Question: clarify annotation guidelines for unclear tokens like “sombody” and address potential inconsistencies.
- Question: given limited gains from tested adaptation techniques, what alternative approaches do authors propose and how will the dataset support future research?
