- Major contribution is a large, high-quality annotated dataset of posts from four cybercrime forums (93,924 posts, 1,938 annotated) for product identification at token level.  
- Task formulated as token-level annotation combining slot-filling IE and NER, with careful problem definition and multiple evaluation metrics.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation of several baseline models and domain adaptation strategies, revealing task difficulty.  
- Paper characterizes forums as “fine-grained domains,” showing significant cross-forum performance degradation.  
- Writing and presentation are clear, with helpful example annotations.  
- Domain adaptation experiments lack novel insights into why methods fail and offer no innovative solutions.  
- Baseline models are relatively simple and omit stronger off-the-shelf baselines like fine-tuned pre-trained LMs.  
- Some annotation choices are ambiguous (e.g., inclusion of tokens like “sombody”), and annotation consistency/inter-annotator agreement are limited.  
- Authors manipulated formatting to fit page limits, raising concerns about presentation quality.  
- Question: clarify criteria for including non-product tokens (e.g., “sombody”) in product mentions.  
- Question: specify which data features (lexical, stylistic, topical) most hinder domain generalization.  
- Question: explain implications of differing OOV rates (Darkode vs. Hack Forums) for domain representativeness/generalizability.  
- Recommend adding discussion of ethical/privacy/security risks and note that the task aligns more with Information Extraction than tagging/chunking/parsing.
