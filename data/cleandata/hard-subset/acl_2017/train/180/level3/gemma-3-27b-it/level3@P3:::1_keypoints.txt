- Introduces a token-level annotated dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled).
- Frames task as hybrid of slot-filling and NER, but reviewer argues it is fundamentally information extraction (IE).
- Dataset size is substantial (93,924 posts, 1,938 annotated) and its release is a valuable resource.
- Experiments show significant performance degradation when models are applied to unseen forums.
- Standard domain adaptation methods show limited effectiveness in this setting.
- Thorough experimental setup with clear hypotheses and multiple evaluation metrics.
- Well-written paper with clear motivation for studying cybercrime forums.
- Analysis highlights substantial domain shift across forums, supported by OOV rate differences.
- Authors plan to release models and code, aiding reproducibility.
- Baseline models are weak; missing stronger pre-trained LM baselines (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa).
- Annotation quality concerns due to inconsistencies (example: “sombody”) and unclear guidelines.
- Domain adaptation contribution is limited and reiterates known transfer-learning challenges.
- Paper formatting possibly manipulated to meet page limits, raising presentation concerns.
- Request for detailed annotation guidelines, especially for ambiguous product references.
- Query whether pre-trained language models were considered as baselines and why/why not.
- Ask if forum-specific lexical resources or pre-training strategies were explored for adaptation.
- Request comment on impact of annotation inconsistencies and any error analysis performed.
- Suggest reporting additional inter-annotator agreement metrics (e.g., Cohen’s Kappa).
- Recommend providing more examples showing when post-level accuracy is particularly useful.
