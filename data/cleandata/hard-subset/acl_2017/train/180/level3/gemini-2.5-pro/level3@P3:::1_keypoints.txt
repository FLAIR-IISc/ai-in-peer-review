- Introduces task of identifying products in cybercrime forums with a new dataset: 93,924 posts and 1,938 token-level annotations across four forums.
- Frames the task as a hybrid of information extraction and NER and evaluates SVM token-level and post-level models.
- Focuses on fine-grained domain adaptation, showing poor cross-forum generalization and limited gains from standard adaptation techniques.
- Contribution: novel annotated dataset valuable for NLP on noisy, socially relevant cybercrime text.
- Contribution: clear task formulation and thorough analysis including seen vs. unseen product breakdown and domain-shift characterization.
- Promises to release data, models, and code, aiding reproducibility.
- Paper is clear, well-written, and well-structured with detailed annotation and evaluation descriptions.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive and includes insightful analyses (OOV rates, domain "generality," adaptation asymmetries).
- Weak baseline models—heuristics are overly simplistic and omit stronger sequence-labeling baselines like CRF or BiLSTM-CRF.
- Limited novelty in domain adaptation findings; results largely reiterate known issues without new methodological solutions.
- Annotation ambiguities: annotating only NP headwords and conflating providers with products can produce inconsistency and reduce practical utility.
- Misclassification of submission track—the task aligns more with Information Extraction/Semantics than Tagging/Parsing.
- Formatting issue: abstract appears to violate conference formatting (smaller font/reduced line spacing).
- Question: clarify annotation guideline distinguishing services from service providers and how subjectivity/integrality is handled for consistency.
- Question: explain why stronger sequence-labeling baselines (e.g., feature-based CRF) were not included for comparison.
