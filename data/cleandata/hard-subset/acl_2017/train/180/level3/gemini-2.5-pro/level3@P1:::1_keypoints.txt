- Task: Introduces a token-level annotation task to identify products bought/sold in cybercrime forums (hybrid of NER and slot-filling).  
- Dataset: Releases ~93,000 posts with 1,938 posts annotated for product mentions across four forums.  
- Generalization: Supervised models trained on one forum perform poorly on others, revealing a fine-grained domain adaptation problem.  
- Adaptation methods: Evaluated standard domain adaptation techniques (e.g., Brown clusters, feature-based methods) and found only marginal improvements.  
- Benchmark value: Presents a difficult, valuable testbed for future research on domain adaptation in noisy, non-standard text.  

- Contribution 1: Novel annotated dataset for information extraction in a high-impact, under-resourced domain.  
- Contribution 2: Thorough empirical characterization of fine-grained cross-forum domain adaptation.
