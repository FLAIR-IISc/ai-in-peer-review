- Introduces a novel annotated dataset from four cybercrime forums (93,924 posts, 1,938 annotated) for identifying bought/sold products.
- Defines a hybrid task blending slot-filling IE and NER, targeting messy user-generated forum text and cross-domain adaptation.
- Evaluates baseline models and domain adaptation techniques (Brown clusters, gazetteers, token-level annotation) and reports cross-forum performance degradation.
- Contribution: release of a large, token-level annotated dataset useful for IE and domain adaptation research.
- Contribution: systematic analysis of fine-grained domain adaptation challenges with extensive experiments.
- Contribution: clear task formulation and detailed annotation guidelines enabling reproducibility.
- Strength: dataset is a significant, real-world testbed with granular annotations.
- Strength: thorough experimental setup and cross-forum analysis.
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly presented.
- Weakness: baselines are simplistic (SVMs, dictionary methods); no modern transformer-based models included.
- Weakness: some annotations are ambiguous (e.g., "sombody"), lacking discussion of edge cases and annotation quality.
- Weakness: domain adaptation experiments largely confirm known issues and offer only marginal improvements.
- Weakness: authors violated formatting rules by altering abstract font/spacing to fit page limit.
- Question: Why were transformer-based models not evaluated, and would they improve cross-domain performance?
- Question: Please clarify ambiguous annotations like "sombody" and their impact on annotations/models.
- Question: Were alternative adaptation methods (adversarial training, meta-learning) explored?
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions—address modern baselines and provide clearer discussion of annotation challenges.
