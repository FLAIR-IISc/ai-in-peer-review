- New dataset of product annotations from online cybercrime forums (93,924 posts, 1,938 annotated) and a hybrid IE/NER extraction task.  
- Contribution: creation of a new dataset for product extraction in cybercrime marketplaces.  
- Contribution: evaluation of fine-grained domain adaptation techniques (Brown clusters, type-level, token-level annotations).  
- Contribution: analysis of domain adaptation results and insights on adaptation challenges across forums.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Strength: experiments are thorough.  
- Strength: the dataset is a significant resource for future NLP/IE work.  
- Weakness: paper is miscategorized as tagging/chunking/parsing rather than information extraction.  
- Weakness: some annotations are unclear or not clearly product-related, which may hurt model performance.  
- Weakness: baseline models are overly basic and do not provide strong comparisons.  
- Weakness: domain adaptation results are expected and lack novel insights.  
- Question: please detail the annotation process and how annotation quality was ensured.  
- Question: how were baseline models chosen and why were stronger, readily available NLP baselines not included?  
- Question: please provide deeper analysis linking domain adaptation results to specific challenges of new forums.  
- Additional comment: overall clear and useful work, but miscategorization and weak baselines limit the paper’s impact; adaptation results are thorough but not novel.
