- Defines a token-level product identification task in cybercrime forums and releases dataset + code.  
- Annotated product mentions across four forums with multiple evaluation metrics (token, type, post; token vs NP projection).  
- Compares simple baselines (SVM token classifier, latent SVM post selector) and analyzes cross-forum transfer as fine-grained domain adaptation.  
- Tests semisupervised features (Brown clusters), light supervision (gazetteers), and limited token-supervised adaptation (including Daumé feature augmentation).  
- Within-forum performance is strong, but cross-forum performance drops substantially (~14 F1).  

Contributions
- Primary contribution is the new multi-forum dataset, clear annotation guidelines, and multiple evaluation views.  
- Provides empirical characterization of fine-grained domain shift and diagnostics (OOV effects, precision/recall trade-offs).  
- Offers a practical evaluation framework clarifying token-, NP-, type-, and post-level tradeoffs.  

Strengths
- Valuable, well-scoped dataset for noisy, high-stakes security data with motivating downstream use cases.  
- Transparent experimental setup with multiple metrics, inter-annotator agreement, and explicit annotation rules.  
- Thorough empirical analysis of cross-forum degradation and adaptation experiments.  
- Negative results about common adaptation techniques are informative for the community.  
- Clear writing and sensible discussion of annotation and NP projection choices.  

Weaknesses
- Limited methodological novelty and weak baseline suite; missing stronger sequence labeling and adaptation baselines (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF, domain-adversarial).  
- Adaptation findings are expected and stop short of proposing novel adaptation mechanisms tailored to the problem.  
- Annotation scheme is ambiguous (context-sensitive token/head-only labeling; agent/method boundary) and shows moderate κ on some subsets.  
- NP projection and first/last-10-lines heuristic may introduce noise and bias; span normalization and canonicalization are not addressed.  
- Formatting issues in the abstract and possible miscategorization of the paper’s area.  

Questions to Authors
- Clarify annotation boundary between “product” and “agent/method” and annotation consistency across forums.  
- Why were stronger baselines (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF, domain-adversarial) not included, and would conclusions change?  
- How sensitive are NP-level results to parsing errors or use of more robust chunkers?  
- Quantify the impact of the “first/last-10-lines” rule on recall and cross-forum generalization.  
- Will full guidelines, per-forum gazetteers, and exact train/dev/test splits be released?  

Additional comments
- Fix abstract formatting to meet template and consider reassigning to IE area.  
- Include significance tests more broadly to support small cross-domain gains.  
- Add an error analysis table (top false positives/negatives by forum) to guide future work.  

Overall
- The dataset and careful empirical characterization are the main contributions; methodological novelty is limited and stronger baselines and clearer annotation guidance are needed for camera-ready.
