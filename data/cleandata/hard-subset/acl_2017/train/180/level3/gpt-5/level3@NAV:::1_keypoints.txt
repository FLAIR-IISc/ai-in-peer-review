- Summary: Introduces token-level product identification, a new multi-forum dataset, cross-forum domain-shift study, and token/NP/post evaluation with simple supervised models and adaptation techniques.
- Contribution: Publicly released cybercrime marketplace dataset with product annotations, clear guidelines, and IAA analysis.
- Contribution: Careful empirical characterization and evaluation protocol (metrics, OOV analyses) showing cross-forum degradation.
- Contribution: Task formulation at the intersection of IE and NER with practical token/NP/post evaluation scaffolding.
- Strength: Dataset fills a high-impact, hard-to-access domain gap and is well-documented and credible.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation using multiple complementary metrics and analyses (seen vs OOV products).
- Strength: Well-written and reproducible work with explicit hypotheses and promised data/code release.
- Strength: Practical relevance for downstream security analyses (market composition, trends, profiling).
- Weakness: Baselines are too weak—omits strong/standard models (BiLSTM-CRF, CRF+contextual embeddings, span-based IE, pretrained transformers).
- Weakness: Adaptation methods are standard and offer limited novel methodological insight; results reconfirm known domain-shift behavior.
- Weakness: Annotation ambiguity—labels sometimes conflate services/agents with products, risking inconsistent semantics and poor generalizability.
- Weakness: Area-fit concern—work is primarily an information-extraction problem and may be miscategorized for the track.
- Weakness: Formatting violation—manipulating abstract font/line spacing to fit page limit is a serious compliance issue.
- Question: Please justify and quantify annotating human agents/methods as products (frequency, IAA, error patterns) and provide a cleaner product-vs-service schema.
- Question: Why were stronger neural/pretrained baselines not included, and can preliminary results be provided to contextualize findings?
- Question: Can the authors release full guidelines, all adjudicated labels, an ethics statement, and licensing terms at submission?
- Question: Did NP projection via a parser distort evaluation, and can BIO span-level evaluation be added to avoid parser dependence?
- Question: Will the authors resubmit a version compliant with formatting rules?
