- Introduces a token-level annotation task and dataset for identifying products in cybercrime forums across four forums, studying cross-forum generalization.
- Proposes simple token- and post-level SVM baselines and evaluates with token-, NP-, type-, and post-level metrics.
- Empirically shows large out-of-domain performance drops and limited gains from standard semi-supervised/domain adaptation techniques.
- Main resource contribution: a new annotated cybercrime marketplace dataset with task definition, evaluation protocols, and planned release of data/code.
- Empirical contribution: multi-metric evaluation quantifying cross-forum domain shift, OOV rates, and seen vs. unseen product performance.
- Modeling contribution: baseline SVMs and experiments with Brown clusters, gazetteers, and feature augmentation showing limited adaptation improvements.
- Strength: valuable, hard-to-obtain dataset aimed at a practical IE goal with a thoughtful evaluation suite.
- Strength: clear writing, thorough experiments, significance tests, and informative per-forum and token vs. post comparisons.
- Strength: novel framing of each forum as a fine-grained domain with useful asymmetry analyses (e.g., Darkode’s broader coverage).
- Strength: post-level accuracy is practically useful and dataset/code release will enable further work.
- Weakness: possible ACL formatting violation in the abstract (reduced font/spacing) that must be corrected.
- Weakness: task framed as NER but better fits IE/slot-filling/resource tracks, creating a mismatch with expected modeling novelty.
- Weakness: annotation scheme conflates product vs. method/agent roles, annotates only NP heads, and mixes POS types, raising face-validity and consistency concerns.
- Weakness: baselines are basic for 2017 — missing strong sequence-labeling baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF, char/subword features, pre-trained embeddings).
- Weakness: adaptation study largely reiterates known patterns (OOV-driven drops) without proposing stronger DA methods or deeper qualitative insight.
- Question: confirm/fix abstract formatting and whether corrected text fits page limit.
- Question: provide fraction and IAA for “core products” vs. “methods/agents,” and justify label choices or report sensitivity to excluding methods/agents.
- Question: did you try modern sequence-tagging baselines (BiLSTM-CRF with char/subword and pre-trained embeddings) and span-level BIO tagging instead of head projection?
- Question: how often does the single-product post-level bias fail on multi-product posts and does it suppress secondary mentions?
- Question: did you try stronger DA methods (pivot/SCL, adversarial invariance, multi-task), field-aware features (title vs. body), or subword/char features to reduce OOV?
- Request: release full annotation guidelines, per-post IDs, and scripts for computing all reported metrics.
- Minor suggestion: standardize and clarify terminology (product/method/agent) and revise ambiguous examples.
- Recommendation if accepted: add at least one modern sequence-tagging baseline and a qualitative cross-domain error analysis beyond OOV.
