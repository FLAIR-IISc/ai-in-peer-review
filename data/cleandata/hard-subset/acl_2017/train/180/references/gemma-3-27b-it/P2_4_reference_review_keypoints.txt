- Paper introduces a new manually annotated dataset of posts from four cybercrime forums for token-level product identification, framing a challenging, context-sensitive IE task.
- Authors provide a detailed dataset analysis, highlight context-dependent product identification challenges, and define a fine-grained domain adaptation task.
- Empirical baselines and initial domain adaptation experiments are presented, showing limited success of existing techniques and characterizing task difficulty.

- Contribution: release of a novel, domain-specific dataset valuable for noisy, real-world IE research in cybercrime marketplaces.
- Contribution: clear problem formulation and thoughtful analysis of annotation nuances and cross-forum variation.
- Contribution: establishment of baselines and systematic exploration of domain adaptation limitations in this setting.

- Strength: dataset appears well-constructed with reasonable inter-annotator agreement (0.58–0.77) and diverse forum selection.
- Strength: strong motivation and clear task definition tied to practical applications.
- Strength: thorough error analysis and honest reporting of negative domain adaptation results, including OOV discussion.
- Strength: commitment to reproducibility via planned public release of data, code, and models.
- Strength: well-written and well-organized manuscript.

- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited; techniques used are standard and not a major advancement.
- Weakness: limited exploration of domain-specific features and more aggressive feature engineering (e.g., domain embeddings, slang dictionaries).
- Weakness: narrow selection of domain adaptation methods; more advanced approaches (adversarial training, meta-learning) not explored.
- Weakness: multiple evaluation metrics (token/type/post-level) are confusing; clearer focus on one or two metrics recommended.

- Question: request for more detail on annotation criteria for product "methods" (e.g., when to label tokens like "coder").
- Question: ask for elaboration on the types of errors seen in cross-forum transfer—specific product misidentification vs. general failures.
- Question: whether Darkode, with lower OOV rates, would be a better source domain for transfer learning.

- Additional comment: expand related work to include more recent advances in domain adaptation and IE.
- Additional comment: add a table summarizing forum characteristics (size, product types, formality) for clearer comparison.

- Overall assessment: solid paper with a valuable dataset and insightful analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
