- Summary: New token-level annotated dataset from four cybercrime forums; frames product identification as a noisy, fine-grained domain adaptation challenge with limited success from baseline/adaptation methods.  
- Contribution — Dataset: Publicly released, high-quality annotated dataset addressing a critical data gap in cybersecurity NLP.  
- Contribution — Problem formulation & analysis: Clear articulation of task challenges and strong empirical evidence of cross-forum performance drops.  
- Contribution — Baselines & adaptation exploration: Systematic baseline experiments and analysis explaining why common adaptation methods yield limited gains.  
- Strength: High-impact, well-described dataset with token-level labels and clear statistics/guidelines (supplementary).  
- Strength: Realistic, well-motivated task tied to practical cybersecurity needs (trend tracking, profiling).  
- Strength: Rigorous evaluation using multiple metrics (token, type, post-level) and proper dev/test splits.  
- Strength: Honest reporting of negative or limited results and thoughtful explanations.  
- Strength: Detailed analysis of domain differences (e.g., OOV rates) illuminating forum gaps.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—reliance on standard baselines and omission of newer adaptation methods (adversarial, meta-learning).  
- Weakness: Insufficient qualitative error analysis—needs examples and breakdown of common failure modes.  
- Weakness: Annotation guidelines not included in submission; supplementary promise is inadequate for full assessment.  
- Weakness: Missing discussion on scalability to large volumes of forum data and practical deployment considerations.  
- Weakness: Weak baseline comparisons — dictionary baseline is simple; stronger baselines using embeddings or language models are absent.  
- Question: Provide the complete annotation guidelines (or substantial excerpt) with the submission.  
- Question: Supply detailed qualitative error analysis with examples and explanations of misidentifications.  
- Question: Discuss scalability—will active learning or more sophisticated methods be needed for large-scale application?  
- Question: Why were recent domain adaptation techniques (adversarial, meta-learning) not explored—practical limitations?  
- Question: How does forum structure (threading, roles, sections) affect performance and could forum-specific features help?  
- Additional: Paper is well-written and organized.  
- Additional: Inclusion of Fleiss’ Kappa for inter-annotator agreement is appropriate.  
- Additional: Annotating head words of noun phrases is a reasonable compromise for noisy text.  
- Overall assessment: Recommend acceptance primarily for the dataset’s value; addressing annotation guideline availability and deeper error analysis would strengthen the work.
