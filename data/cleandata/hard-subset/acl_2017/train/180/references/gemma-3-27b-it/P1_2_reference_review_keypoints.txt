- Introduces a new manually annotated dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled).  
- Frames the task as token-level information extraction akin to NER but highly context-dependent.  
- Demonstrates significant domain shift between different cybercrime forums, complicating cross-forum generalization.  
- Evaluates several baseline and semi-supervised domain adaptation methods and finds limited improvement from existing techniques.  
- Main contribution is the novel dataset release, valuable for research on criminal marketplaces and NLP in security.  
- Clearly defines the challenging extraction task and provides empirical analysis of domain shift nuances.  
- Provides a comprehensive empirical baseline and initial study of adaptation methods for future work.  
- Research topic is relevant and impactful for threat intelligence and law enforcement applications.  
- Annotation process appears rigorous with reasonable inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa 0.58–0.77) and detailed guidelines.  
- Evaluation is thorough, covering within-forum and cross-forum performance and multiple metrics.  
- Clear discussion of domain-specific challenges: noisy text, context dependence, and fine-grained domain shift.  
- Honest, realistic finding that current domain adaptation methods struggle in this specialized, evolving domain.  
- Methods explored lack novelty and mostly apply standard techniques rather than proposing new approaches.  
- Feature engineering is under-explored; more sophisticated, forum-specific or semantic features could help.  
- Comparison to stronger, modern adaptation baselines (e.g., adversarial training, meta-learning) is missing.  
- Dataset size imbalance (Hack Forums much larger) may skew results and affect generalizability.  
- Ethical considerations around collecting/analyzing cybercrime forum data are insufficiently discussed.  
- Requests clarification on annotation challenges, dataset balancing/impact of size, product generation methods, Darkode’s “generality,” and data anonymization.  
- Notes writing clarity, informative tables, and helpful example posts.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper centered on a valuable dataset; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
