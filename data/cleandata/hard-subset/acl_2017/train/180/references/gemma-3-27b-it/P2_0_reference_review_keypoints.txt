- Novel dataset: release of an annotated corpus of cybercrime forum posts for product identification, valuable and hard-to-obtain.  
- Problem formulation & baseline: clear token-level IE task framing with reasonable baselines provided.  
- Empirical domain-adaptation analysis: systematic evaluation of adaptation techniques showing limited cross-forum transfer.  
- Dataset significance: important contribution given ethical/data-collection challenges and reasonable annotation agreement.  
- Clear problem relevance: strong connection to IE tasks and cybersecurity applications.  
- Thorough experiments: variety of models, adaptation methods, and multiple evaluation metrics used.  
- Honest results discussion: authors acknowledge limited success and discuss plausible reasons for failures.  
- Limited methodological novelty: adaptation methods are standard (Brown clusters, gazetteers, simple augmentation) with no new algorithms.  
- Shallow domain-difference analysis: lacks deeper linguistic comparisons beyond OOV rates.  
- Presentation verbosity: related work and metric descriptions are long; annotation process is redundantly explained.  
- Missing error analysis: no detailed breakdown of model errors or specific linguistic failures provided.  
- Annotator background question: did NLP vs. security expertise affect annotation consistency and how was it handled?  
- Multi-product posts question: request for details on the few posts selling multiple distinct products and their implications.  
- Ethical/data-collection question: ask for more details on privacy, anonymization, and collection procedures.  
- Asymmetry in adaptation question: why does Hack Forums → Darkode transfer better than the reverse (hypotheses requested)?  
- Supplementary material note: annotation guidelines in the appendix are critical for assessing annotation quality.  
- Ethics suggestion: add a section on ethical implications and mitigation measures for working with this data.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is a valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
