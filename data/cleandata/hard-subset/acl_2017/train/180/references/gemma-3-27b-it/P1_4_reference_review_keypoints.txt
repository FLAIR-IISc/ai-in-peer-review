- Paper introduces a new manually annotated dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled).  
- Frames product identification as a token-level IE task akin to NER but with greater context dependence.  
- Provides baselines using standard supervised methods and evaluates several domain adaptation techniques.  
- Empirical results show limited cross-forum transfer, highlighting challenges of fine-grained domain adaptation.  

- Dataset is relevant for security, law enforcement, and threat intelligence research.  
- Reported inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa 0.58–0.77) indicates reasonable annotation quality.  
- Authors supply detailed annotation guidelines (in supplementary material) enabling reproducibility.  
- Experimental setup is thorough, using multiple baselines and both token- and post-level metrics.  
- Paper clearly identifies challenges like forum-specific terminology and high OOV rates.  
- The focus on adaptation between similar cybercrime forums is realistic and important.  

- Domain adaptation methods used (Brown clusters, type-level annotation) are standard and not novel.  
- Lack of deeper qualitative error analysis limits insight into model failures.  
- Annotation appears labor-intensive; discussion of scaling strategies (active learning, distant supervision) is missing.  
- Missing comparisons to stronger modern baselines (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa fine-tuning).  
- Dataset is skewed toward Hack Forums; smaller Blackhat and Nulled subsets may limit generalizability.  

- Request for more examples and clarifications from the annotation guidelines, especially challenging cases.  
- Ask for quantification of posts containing single vs. multiple products.  
- Question why adaptation Darkode→Hack Forums is easier than the reverse and what causes this asymmetry.  
- Ask what future adaptation strategies beyond those tested might be promising.  
- Inquire whether alternative tokenizers or preprocessing methods for noisy text were tried beyond CoreNLP.  

- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Public release of dataset and code is commendable.  
- Suggest adding descriptive statistics of product types per forum (e.g., top-10 products).  

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; dataset and analysis outweigh limited methodological novelty.
