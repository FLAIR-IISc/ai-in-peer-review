- Novel annotated dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled) released for research.  
- Task framed as challenging, context-dependent information extraction distinct from standard NER, with messy forum language.  
- Empirical baselines and semi-supervised methods evaluated, showing limited success on cross-forum generalization.  
- Primary contribution: valuable, hard-to-obtain dataset for product identification in cybercrime marketplaces.  
- Clear problem formulation and analysis of challenges (context dependence, noisy data, forum-specific language).  
- Provides useful empirical baselines for future work.  
- Highly relevant to ACL and security communities; enables downstream research.  
- Rigorous annotation process with iterative guidelines and reasonable inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa 0.58–0.77).  
- Comprehensive evaluation using multiple metrics (token-level, type-level, post-level; NP- and token-level).  
- Authors honestly report limited domain adaptation gains and characterize the problem transparently.  
- Resources (dataset, models, code) released publicly for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: methods explored for domain adaptation are standard and lack newer techniques (e.g., adversarial training, meta-learning).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why forums differ linguistically, in product types, or user behavior; limited OOV analysis.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns due to reliance on manual annotation for new domains.  
- Weakness: dataset limited to four forums, raising questions about broader generalizability.  
- Requested clarification: criteria for selecting the four forums and their representativeness.  
- Requested clarification: detailed error analysis of cross-forum adaptation failures (common error types).  
- Requested clarification: whether recent DA approaches (adversarial, meta-learning, PLM transfer) were considered and why not included.  
- Requested clarification: examples of annotation guideline changes and their effect on agreement.  
- Requested clarification: explain discrepancy between low OOV in Darkode and poor adaptation performance to Darkode.  
- Suggested improvements: include a summary table of forum characteristics (product types, post length, language style).  
- Suggested improvements: expand related work to cover recent IE and domain adaptation advances.  
- Suggested improvements: include full annotation guidelines in supplementary material.  
- Overall: solid, valuable contribution centered on the dataset; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses and questions.
