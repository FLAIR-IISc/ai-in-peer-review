- New publicly released, manually annotated token-level dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled).  
- Frames product identification as a token-level information extraction task with noisy, domain-specific language challenges.  
- Empirical analysis showing large performance drops when transferring models across different forums, highlighting fine-grained domain adaptation difficulty.  
- Systematic evaluation of baseline extraction and domain adaptation techniques, with limited success in improving cross-forum transfer.  
- Dataset fills a critical data scarcity gap in cybercrime NLP research and supports diverse downstream tasks.  
- Rigorous annotation process with multiple refinement rounds and inter-annotator agreement checks; token-level choice justified.  
- Evaluation uses multiple metrics (token-, type-, post-level) and analyzes OOV rates to assess generalization.  
- Authors transparently report limitations and failures of adaptation methods, improving credibility.  
- Methods explored are standard and lack methodological novelty; more advanced techniques (e.g., adversarial, meta-learning) were not tried.  
- Error analysis is shallow; more detailed breakdown of failure modes and product/language-specific errors is needed.  
- Forum selection may be biased and not fully representative of the broader cybercrime marketplace ecosystem.  
- Literature connection to cyber threat intelligence, digital forensics, and online criminal behavior is underdeveloped.  
- Questions about future dataset expansion, representativeness of forums, and annotation scalability remain unanswered.  
- Suggestion to include concrete error examples, visualization of annotation scheme, a forum-characteristics comparison table, and ethical considerations.  
- Overall: a solid, valuable contribution with thorough evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
