- Summary: New labeled dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums; framed as token-level IE task with experiments showing limited cross-forum transfer.
- Contribution: Novel dataset enabling research on product identification in cybercrime marketplaces.
- Contribution: Empirical characterization showing strong fine-grained domain adaptation challenges across forums.
- Contribution: Clear task formulation and baseline models for future work.
- Strength: Addresses a practically important problem for security researchers and law enforcement.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation using multiple metrics (token/type/post-level) and within- and cross-forum experiments.
- Strength: Detailed dataset description (sizes, lengths, annotation stats, IAA) and clear annotation guidelines.
- Strength: Realistic problem setting that acknowledges messy, context-dependent forum language.
- Weakness: Limited novelty in domain adaptation methods; explored techniques are standard and yield small gains.
- Weakness: Insufficient error analysis—lack of detailed breakdown of common model mistakes.
- Weakness: Over-reliance on supervised learning; limited exploration of semi-/unsupervised or weak supervision approaches.
- Weakness: Shallow analysis of why forum differences cause performance degradation; more linguistic/semantic investigation needed.
- Q: Could models be designed for the rare multi-product posts (3%) to improve type-level evaluation?
- Q: Please elaborate on what makes Hack Forums more “precision-oriented” than Darkode—concrete characteristics?
- Q: Share more detail on annotation disagreements during guideline refinement and how they were resolved.
- Q: Have you considered adversarial training or domain-invariant feature learning to address domain shift?
- Additional: Paper is well-written and organized; supplementary materials (guide, code, models) are crucial for reproducibility.
- Additional: Table 1 formatting needs readability improvements.
- Additional: Include discussion of ethical considerations for researching cybercrime forums.
- Overall assessment: Valuable dataset and insights into adaptation challenges; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
