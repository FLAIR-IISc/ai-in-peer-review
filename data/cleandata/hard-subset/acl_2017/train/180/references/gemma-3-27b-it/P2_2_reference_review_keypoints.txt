- Summary: New dataset of cybercrime forum posts annotated for product mentions; framed as token-level IE/fine-grained domain adaptation; adaptation methods struggle to transfer across forums.
- Contribution: Valuable new annotated dataset likely useful to security, NLP, and IE researchers.
- Contribution: Insightful formulation of the task as fine-grained domain adaptation with thorough analysis of challenges.
- Contribution: Systematic empirical exploration and baselines for adaptation techniques, providing a benchmark despite largely negative results.
- Strength: Dataset quality and relevance — reasonable inter-annotator agreement (~0.6), diverse forum selection, informative dataset statistics.
- Strength: Clear problem definition and motivation, with explanation why simple NER is insufficient and potential downstream analyses.
- Strength: Thorough experimental setup — multiple metrics (token/type/post), NP vs token evaluation, and useful ablation studies.
- Strength: Honest assessment of limitations and negative adaptation results, giving a realistic view of current methods.
- Strength: Well-written and organized paper with clear figures and tables.
- Weakness: Limited adaptation gains — methods yield little improvement and lack deeper analysis of failure causes.
- Weakness: Gazetteer construction is restrictive (requires ≥4 training occurrences), biasing against novel forum products.
- Weakness: Superficial linguistic analysis of forums — more detailed vocab/syntax/abbreviation analysis needed to explain domain gaps.
- Weakness: Limited scope of adaptation methods — missing exploration of advanced approaches like adversarial training or meta-learning.
- Question: Can the authors pinpoint specific linguistic features or data characteristics causing adaptation failure?
- Question: Have alternative gazetteer construction methods (external resources or weak supervision) been considered?
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed linguistic characterization of each forum to explain domain differences?
- Question: Were experiments run with different weighting of false positives/negatives per forum, and could this affect adaptation performance?
- Overall: Solid and valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses and questions.
