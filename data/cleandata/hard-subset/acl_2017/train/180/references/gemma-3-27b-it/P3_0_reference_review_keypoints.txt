- New token-level dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums.
- Significant fine-grained domain adaptation challenge: even similar forums exhibit distinct language that degrades transfer.
- Explored Brown clusters, type-level annotation, and simple data augmentation with limited improvement.
- Dataset fills a resources gap in NLP for security and will be publicly released.
- Clear problem formulation and empirical evidence demonstrating cross-forum transfer difficulty.
- Solid experimental setup with clear splits and relevant metrics (token F1, type-level, post-level); Fleiss’ Kappa reported.
- Honest, thorough analysis including OOV rate investigation and discussion of why methods failed.
- Well-written paper with illustrative examples that clarify annotation decisions.
- Public release of code, models, and data to facilitate future research.
- Adaptation methods lack novelty and are relatively standard.
- Error analysis is superficial; needs deeper examination of misclassification causes (slang, ambiguity).
- Token-level annotation focus may be limiting; phrase-level or hybrid schemes could be more useful.
- Missing comparison to stronger baselines such as fine-tuned pre-trained language models (e.g., BERT).
- No discussion of ethical considerations or potential harms from using cybercrime forum data.
- Reviewer requests: detailed error examples, justification or consideration of hybrid annotation, summary of related anonymized work, description of ethical safeguards, and analysis of error types in cross-forum settings.
- Suggestion to include visual data representations (e.g., word clouds or topic models) to show product distributions.
- Overall assessment: valuable dataset and clear challenge articulation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions for deeper error analysis and ethical discussion.
