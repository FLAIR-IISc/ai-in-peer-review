- Summary: New token-level annotated dataset of cybercrime forum posts and framing of product identification as a fine-grained domain adaptation problem; baseline adaptation methods show limited success.

- Contribution 1: Release of a novel, token-level annotated dataset of cybercrime forum posts useful for research and NLP on illicit marketplaces.

- Contribution 2: Clear problem formulation and empirical characterization showing significant performance drops when transferring models across forums.

- Contribution 3: Solid baseline experiments (cross-forum evaluation, multiple metrics) demonstrating task difficulty and limited gains from simple adaptation methods.

- Strength 1: High relevance and real-world importance for security and law enforcement applications.

- Strength 2: Rigorous annotation process with multiple annotators, iterative guidelines, and reasonable inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa ~0.6–0.7).

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation using token-, type-, and post-level metrics plus phrase-level investigation and comprehensive cross-forum experiments.

- Strength 4: Clear writing, organization, and helpful examples clarifying the annotation scheme.

- Weakness 1: Adaptation methods showed limited success and the paper lacks deeper analysis of why they failed.

- Weakness 2: Explored adaptation techniques are relatively standard; more advanced methods (e.g., adversarial training, meta-learning) were not investigated.

- Weakness 3: Feature engineering is limited; contextualized representations (e.g., BERT) and richer features were not explored, and ablation studies are insufficient.

- Weakness 4: Missing comparisons to stronger baselines such as modern pre-trained language models.

- Question 1: Request for deeper error analysis—what error types and product categories fail most in cross-forum transfer?

- Question 2: Can the authors quantify forum "generality" (e.g., Darkode) and relate product diversity to model transferability?

- Question 3: Request for more qualitative examples showing domain-specific language/slang that hinders product identification.

- Question 4: What additional adaptation techniques or modeling approaches do the authors consider most promising beyond those mentioned?

- Additional comment 1: Supplementary annotation guidelines are crucial for replication and dataset evaluation.

- Additional comment 2: Clarify how ambiguous or heavily obfuscated product mentions were handled during annotation.

- Overall assessment: Valuable dataset and clear baseline characterization for an important, challenging problem; recommend acceptance with minor revisions focusing on deeper error analysis and future directions.
