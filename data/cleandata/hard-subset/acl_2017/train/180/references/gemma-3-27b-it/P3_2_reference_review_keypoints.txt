- Summary: New publicly released token-level dataset of product mentions from four cybercrime forums, framing extraction as a noisy, context-heavy IE task and showing strong domain shift with limited success from standard adaptation methods.

- Contribution — Novel dataset: Release of a labeled cybercrime forum dataset that will aid security, NLP, and criminology research.

- Contribution — Domain shift demo: Clear empirical evidence that models trained on one forum perform poorly on others, highlighting fine-grained domain adaptation challenges.

- Contribution — Benchmarking: Baseline evaluations of several adaptation techniques (Brown clusters, type-level annotation, semi-supervised learning) that set a starting point despite largely negative results.

- Strength: High relevance and impact for automated analysis of cybercrime forums and threat intelligence.

- Strength: Rigorous evaluation using multiple metrics (token-, type-, and post-level) and useful analysis like OOV rates.

- Strength: Detailed dataset documentation including forum statistics, annotation process, and inter-annotator agreement.

- Strength: Realistic problem framing and careful annotation choices (e.g., treating verbs as methods) that reflect forum text nuances.

- Strength: Commitment to open science — releasing dataset, models, and code.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty — adaptation techniques are standard and largely unsuccessful for this domain.

- Weakness: Insufficient in-depth error analysis to diagnose model failures (e.g., specific confusion types).

- Weakness: Small and imbalanced dataset sizes for some forums (notably Blackhat and Nulled), limiting generalizability.

- Weakness: Token-level annotation paired with NP-level evaluation feels somewhat post hoc; span-based annotation might better match type-level goals.

- Question: How were multi-word product names handled when they spanned sentences?

- Question: Were there annotation consistency differences between annotators with NLP vs. security backgrounds?

- Question: Beyond messiness and domain shift, what specific characteristics of cybercrime forum data most impede NLP methods?

- Question: Why is Darkode considered more “general” — hypotheses about product range or language standardization?

- Additional comment: Evaluation metric discussion is strong and well-considered.

- Additional comment: Introduction would benefit from concrete real-world cybersecurity application examples.

- Additional comment: Provide annotation time per post to quantify the dataset creation effort.

- Overall assessment: Valuable dataset and analysis with rigorous evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions despite limited methodological contributions.
