- Main contribution: new token-level dataset and fine-grained domain adaptation task for identifying products across four cybercrime forums.

- Contribution detail: framed task between information extraction and NER, with empirical demonstration that models trained on one forum generalize poorly to others.

- Strength: significant, carefully annotated dataset (Fleiss' Kappa 0.58–0.77) across four forums, built with six rounds of guideline refinement.

- Strength: clear and well-justified task definition explaining preference for token-level over post- or span-level annotations.

- Strength: insightful domain-adaptation analysis (including OOV analysis) and systematic testing of standard adaptation approaches.

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—relies on basic models (binary SVMs) and standard adaptation techniques, leaving ambiguity whether poor performance is task-inherent.

- Weakness: insufficient comparison to recent/state-of-the-art neural transfer or domain-adaptation methods.

- Weakness: underdeveloped justification for token-level annotation—lack of concrete examples showing necessity over post-level metrics.

- Question: how were posts with multiple products distinguished from multiple descriptions of the same product—what criteria were used?

- Question: have the authors analyzed how web-text features (abbreviations, spelling errors, ungrammaticality) affect model performance?

- Question: why was Fleiss' Kappa used for inter-annotator agreement instead of Cohen's Kappa given the annotator assignment?

- Overall recommendation: accept for the valuable dataset and analysis of domain-adaptation challenges, but revise to include stronger comparisons to modern methods and clearer justification for token-level annotation.
