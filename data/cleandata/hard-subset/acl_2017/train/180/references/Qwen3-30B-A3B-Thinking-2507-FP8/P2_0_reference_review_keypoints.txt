- Summary: New token-level annotated dataset for identifying products in four cybercrime forums, with domain-adaptation experiments showing poor cross-forum transfer and limited success from simple adaptation methods.

- Contribution 1: Creation of a well-annotated dataset for product identification in cybercrime marketplaces.

- Contribution 2: Clear task definition blending information extraction and context-specific NER with token-level product annotation.

- Contribution 3: Thorough analysis of domain adaptation challenges across four distinct forums.

- Strength 1: Significant, well-motivated dataset contribution with detailed guidelines, iterative annotation, and reasonable inter-annotator agreement.

- Strength 2: Convincing task justification showing why token-level annotation suits noisy forum text and differs from standard NER.

- Strength 3: Insightful domain-adaptation analysis (e.g., OOV and asymmetric transfer explanations between Darkode and Hack Forums).

- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—task is essentially domain-specific NER and lacks deep comparison to prior domain-adaptation work.

- Weakness 2: Limited exploration of adaptation methods—no experiments with modern pre-trained transformers or adversarial/domain-invariant techniques.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient discussion of dataset limitations and biases, including potential misspecification of product types and dataset obsolescence.

- Question 1: How does the annotation scheme handle posts selling multiple distinct products and how would that affect model performance?

- Question 2: What strategies (e.g., periodic updates, annotation consistency methods) do the authors recommend to keep the dataset relevant as markets evolve?

- Question 3: Would state-of-the-art neural models likely improve performance, and why were they omitted from evaluation?

- Overall assessment: Valuable dataset and solid analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty framing and broader exploration/discussion of adaptation techniques and dataset limitations.
