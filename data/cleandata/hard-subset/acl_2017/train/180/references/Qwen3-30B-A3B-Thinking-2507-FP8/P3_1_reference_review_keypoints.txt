- Introduces a token-level annotated dataset for product identification in four cybercrime forums and a fine-grained domain adaptation task.  
- Evaluates multiple models for product extraction and studies cross-forum domain adaptation performance.  
- Key finding: models trained on one forum perform substantially worse on others, indicating significant domain shift within cybercrime forums.  
- Major contribution: first token-level annotated resource for cybercrime marketplaces with detailed guidelines and solid inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa 0.58–0.77).  
- Novel insight: characterization of fine-grained domain shift across forums, showing distinct vocabulary and representation differences.  
- Empirical analysis includes OOV rate comparisons and shows Darkode vs. Hack Forums differences important for adaptation.  
- Strength: rigorous, iterative annotation process producing a high-quality, well-documented dataset.  
- Strength: clear task framing distinct from NER/slot-filling with multiple sensible evaluation metrics (token-, type-, post-level).  
- Strength: important empirical evidence quantifying performance drops across forums (e.g., 14.4 F1 drop from Hack Forums to Darkode).  
- Weakness: framing as “fine-grained” domain shift may be overstated—forum content differences (e.g., Darkode vs. Hack Forums) could be substantial and not finer-grained.  
- Weakness: limited domain adaptation methods evaluated (basic techniques only), omitting modern approaches like adversarial training or transformer-based adaptation.  
- Weakness: dataset representativeness concerns due to large size disparities between forums, particularly tiny Blackhat test set (167 posts).  
- Weakness: lack of practical guidance on how many labeled examples are needed or how to select them for adaptation.  
- Questions: how do authors reconcile Darkode being called “more general” given its narrower cybercriminal-wares focus versus Hack Forums’ mixed content?  
- Questions: why weren’t advanced domain adaptation methods tested, and how might they perform on this task?  
- Questions: given Blackhat’s very small size, how reliable are results using it as a test set and should it be used in future evaluations?  
- Overall assessment: valuable dataset and characterization of domain shift; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address domain framing, additional adaptation methods, and dataset representativeness.
