- First dataset and task for identifying products in online cybercrime marketplaces with token-level annotations across four forums.  
- Paper claims three contributions: dataset creation, fine-grained domain adaptation characterization, and comprehensive evaluation of standard NLP approaches.  
- Strong dataset work: iterative annotation process and reasonable inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa 0.58–0.77).  
- Clear problem framing: distinguishes product identification from NER/slot-filling and emphasizes context-sensitive, forum-specific product references.  
- Thorough evaluation: token-, type-, and post-level metrics with within- and cross-forum experiments showing large adaptation drops.  
- Methodological novelty is limited: relies on standard techniques (SVMs, Brown clusters, gazetteers) without advancing domain adaptation methods.  
- Domain scope is limited to four forums, raising questions about representativeness and generalizability.  
- Analysis of domain differences is superficial and does not clearly disentangle vocabulary vs. stylistic causes.  
- Does not compare to modern neural transformer models (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa), a significant omission given current NLP trends.  
- Lacks deep error analysis explaining why standard adaptation techniques fail on this task.  
- Question: Is Darkode more representative of the domain and could its coverage bias findings?  
- Question: Why were transformer-based models not explored for potentially better adaptation?  
- Question: Given rare multi-product posts (~3%), why annotate at token level instead of the simpler post level?  
- Overall: Valuable dataset and evaluation, but limited methodological insight and novelty; reviewer recommendation: Weak Accept.
