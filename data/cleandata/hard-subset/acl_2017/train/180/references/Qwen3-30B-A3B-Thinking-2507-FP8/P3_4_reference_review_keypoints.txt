- Introduces a new token-level task and a dataset for identifying product references in four cybercrime forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled).
- Main contributions: a well-annotated dataset, a justified token-level task definition, and analysis of fine-grained domain adaptation challenges.
- Strength: Major dataset contribution with detailed guidelines, preprocessing, and inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa > 0.58).
- Strength: Clear, well-justified task formulation distinguishing product mentions from related concepts, with illustrative examples.
- Strength: Rigorous empirical domain-adaptation analysis showing large cross-forum performance drops and OOV rate comparisons.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—baselines and adaptation methods are standard and yield unsurprising negative results.
- Weakness: Insufficient investigation into why domains differ linguistically or structurally (e.g., naming conventions, slang, syntax).
- Weakness: Does not compare to recent domain-adaptation techniques (e.g., adversarial methods, contextualized embeddings like BERT).
- Question: Why does Darkode exhibit broader product coverage—forum maturity, community traits, or other factors?
- Question: Why does a Hack Forums-trained model perform better on Nulled—are there shared product types, structure, or user demographics?
- Question: Can the authors provide concrete examples of stylistic or linguistic differences across forums that impact model performance?
- Overall: Dataset and task are valuable and well-executed, but the paper needs stronger methodological work or deeper linguistic analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to include comparisons with modern domain-adaptation methods or a deeper study of inter-forum linguistic differences.
