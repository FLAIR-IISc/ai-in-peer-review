- Contribution: New annotated dataset from four cybercrime forums with detailed statistics, guidelines, and inter-annotator agreement.
- Contribution: Analysis showing forums are distinct “fine-grained domains,” causing domain adaptation challenges.
- Contribution: Development of multi-level evaluation metrics (token-level, type-level, post-level).

- Strength: Novel, high-quality dataset (≈51k annotated tokens) with reasonable IAA (Fleiss’ Kappa 0.58–0.77).
- Strength: Clear task definition distinct from standard NER/slot-filling and a strong, application-oriented evaluation framework.
- Strength: Insightful domain-adaptation analysis testing inventory vs. stylistic hypotheses, with OOV analysis and asymmetric forum results.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—relies on standard models (SVM, Brown clusters, gazetteers) and lacks comparison to modern sequence models.
- Weakness: Insufficient deep analysis of why adaptation methods fail (e.g., what stylistic differences block transfer).
- Weakness: Weak discussion of real-world impact and concrete uses for cybersecurity researchers or law enforcement.
- Weakness: Annotation guidelines can be vague and provide too few examples, risking inconsistent labeling.

- Question: Why does Darkode show better product coverage than Hack Forums—is it marketplace scope or forum structure?
- Question: Can the authors characterize the ~3% of posts that sell multiple product types and what makes them different?
- Question: How would results and applications change if annotations were span-level rather than token-level?

- Recommendation: Weak accept — dataset and analysis are valuable, but paper needs more methodological innovation and deeper analysis of adaptation failures.
