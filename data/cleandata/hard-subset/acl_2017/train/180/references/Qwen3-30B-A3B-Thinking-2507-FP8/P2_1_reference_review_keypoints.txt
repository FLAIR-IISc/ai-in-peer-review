- New dataset and token-level product annotation task for cybercrime marketplaces; bridges information extraction and NER and includes a fine-grained domain adaptation analysis.

- High-quality dataset: rigorous six-round annotation process with multiple annotators and reasonable inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa > 0.58).

- Clear task definition and justification: token-level approach avoids cross-mention linking issues and is supported by detailed guidelines and examples.

- Insightful domain adaptation analysis: shows substantial cross-forum performance drops (e.g., Hack Forums → Darkode −14.4 F1) and evaluates several adaptation strategies.

- Novelty not well established: paper fails to clearly differentiate the task from existing IE/NER/product extraction work.

- Limited adaptation methods tested: uses basic techniques (Brown clusters, gazetteers, simple augmentation) and omits more advanced approaches and failure analyses.

- Limited generalizability: evaluation on only four forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled) may not represent the broader cybercrime ecosystem.

- Insufficient discussion of societal impact: paper does not address ethical and dual-use implications of analyzing cybercrime marketplaces.

- Question: How does token-level product extraction differ from standard product extraction tasks and can it be adapted to other domains?

- Question: Why weren’t advanced domain adaptation techniques (e.g., adversarial or contextual methods) explored, and what data challenges might limit them?

- Question: Why does Darkode show better product coverage than Hack Forums (Table 5), and how might that affect generalizability?

- Overall: Strong dataset and task contribution with well-executed analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify novelty, expand adaptation experiments, and address ethical concerns.
