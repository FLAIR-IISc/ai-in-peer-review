- Presents a new token-level annotated dataset of product references across four cybercrime forums with detailed guidelines and IAA.  
- Demonstrates significant domain adaptation challenges: large performance drop when models trained on one forum are tested on another (e.g., within-forum F1 77.6 vs cross-forum F1 63.2).  
- Provides a nuanced evaluation framework (token-level, type-level, post-level) and analyzes product-type vs token-level needs, noting most posts contain a single core product.  
- Strength: high-quality, rigorously annotated dataset with extensive refinement and useful statistics for cybersecurity NLP.  
- Strength: clear empirical evidence of within-domain forum differences that hinder domain adaptation.  
- Strength: thoughtful discussion of evaluation metrics and task design implications.  
- Weakness: task and methods lack novelty—resembles NER/slot filling and uses standard baselines.  
- Weakness: analysis of why domain adaptation fails is superficial; unclear if issues are lexical or structural.  
- Weakness: limited scope—only four forums, limiting generalizability of conclusions.  
- Weakness: missing strong baselines such as modern transfer learning or contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT).  
- Weakness: limited discussion of implications or concrete next steps to address the identified challenges.  
- Question: How should researchers practically use this dataset given the pronounced domain adaptation issues?  
- Question: Why do Darkode-trained models generalize better—does Darkode truly have broader product coverage or another cause?  
- Question: Given only ~3% of posts sell multiple products, how does token-level annotation compare in utility to post-level extraction?  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept — dataset is a valuable resource, but methodological novelty and depth of analysis are limited.
