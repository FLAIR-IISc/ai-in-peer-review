- Introduces a new NLP task and dataset for token-level product identification in cybercrime marketplaces across four forums.  
- Shows that fine-grained domain differences between forums significantly hurt cross-forum performance.  
- Evaluates multiple baselines and domain adaptation techniques, finding little improvement when transferring between forums.  
- Main contribution: first high-quality dataset for product identification in cybercrime forums with clear annotation guidelines.  
- Main contribution: evidence that intra-domain (forum-to-forum) shifts create substantial domain adaptation challenges.  
- Main contribution: thoughtful multi-metric evaluation framework (token-, type-, post-level).  
- Strength: dataset quality and reasonable inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa > 0.58).  
- Strength: compelling analysis showing Darkode’s better product coverage and its impact on performance.  
- Strength: comprehensive use of multiple evaluation metrics suited to different applications.  
- Weakness: task novelty is limited—closely related to existing IE/NER tasks and prior forum analyses.  
- Weakness: domain adaptation analysis is superficial and doesn’t explain the linguistic/structural reasons for failure.  
- Weakness: lacks engagement with recent domain adaptation methods (e.g., adversarial approaches, transformer transfer).  
- Weakness: practical utility claims are underdeveloped; no concrete example of application of token-level annotations.  
- Question: Why does Darkode have more consistent product terminology than Hack Forums—specialization or other factors?  
- Question: How would higher rates of multi-product posts change the task, evaluation, or annotation scheme?  
- Question: What specific linguistic or naming conventions explain Darkode’s lower OOV rates and more consistent OOV performance?  
- Overall: Valuable dataset and insights on fine-grained domain shift; limited novelty and analysis depth—recommend accept with minor revisions.
