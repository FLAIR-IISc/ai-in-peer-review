- Introduces a dataset and task for identifying products in cybercrime marketplaces with token-level annotations from four forums.  
- Contributions: a novel dataset for an underexplored domain, a context-sensitive product identification task, and a study of fine-grained domain adaptation.  
- Strength: High-quality dataset created with iterative guidelines, domain experts, and reasonable inter-annotator agreement.  
- Strength: Clear task definition and comprehensive evaluation framework (token-, type-, and post-level metrics), emphasizing practical post-level accuracy.  
- Strength: Demonstrates notable fine-grained domain adaptation failures across similar forums and analyzes OOV vs. seen product performance.  
- Weakness: Limited baselines — no evaluation with modern neural architectures (e.g., BERT/transformers).  
- Weakness: Superficial analysis of why domain adaptation fails; lacks concrete examples of forum-specific differences.  
- Weakness: Overstates novelty by not adequately acknowledging prior forum-based NLP work.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of ethical issues around collecting, sharing, and preventing misuse of cybercrime data.  
- Question: Why were modern neural models (e.g., BERT) not evaluated, and would they improve performance or adaptation?  
- Question: Can the authors provide specific examples of stylistic or naming differences across forums that harm adaptation?  
- Question: What safeguards, ethical review, and responsible-use measures govern dataset collection and sharing?  
- Overall: A valuable, well-constructed dataset and task with important findings, but needs modern baselines, deeper adaptation analysis, clearer novelty positioning, and stronger ethics discussion to maximize impact.
