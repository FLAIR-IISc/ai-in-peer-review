- Summary: Introduces a new token-level dataset and task for identifying product mentions in cybercrime marketplace posts and shows domain differences across forums hinder standard adaptation.

- Contribution 1: Provides a novel annotated dataset with token-level product references, annotation guidelines, stats, and inter-annotator agreement.

- Contribution 2: Clearly defines the task (between IE and NER) and analyzes annotation nuances like verbs and multi-word phrases.

- Contribution 3: Offers a thorough evaluation framework with token-, type-, and post-level metrics and analysis across forums.

- Strength 1: Significant dataset contribution for an underexplored, high-value domain with solid agreement (Fleiss' Kappa 0.62–0.77).

- Strength 2: Insightful analysis showing fine-grained domain shifts within cybercrime forums and evidence that product coverage and style differences drive adaptation failures.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive experiments comparing baselines, classifiers, and adaptation techniques (Brown clusters, gazetteers, small target annotations).

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty—uses standard SVM-based techniques and established adaptation methods rather than newer approaches.

- Weakness 2: Relatively small dataset sizes (e.g., Darkode ~3,368 posts), potentially limiting training of complex models and generalization.

- Weakness 3: Lacks discussion of ethical considerations around collecting and releasing data from cybercrime marketplaces.

- Question 1: How was the data collected and what ethical measures were taken to ensure responsible collection and mitigate facilitation of cybercrime?

- Question 2: Were modern neural models (e.g., BERT) considered for domain adaptation, and what would be the expected trade-offs?

- Question 3: How was the 3% figure for posts selling multiple product types determined and what is its impact on model performance?

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—dataset and analysis are valuable but the paper needs stronger methodological novelty, larger-scale validation, and ethical discussion.
