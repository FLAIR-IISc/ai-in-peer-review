- Paper introduces a token-level product identification task and a new annotated dataset for cybercrime marketplaces (4 forums, ~51k words, 100+ posts/forum).  
- Authors focus on annotating product mentions that are actually being bought/sold, distinct from traditional NER or post-level labels.  
- Dataset shows reasonable inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' Kappa ~0.6) and includes guidelines and statistics.  
- Main contribution: a substantial, well-constructed dataset for an underexplored security/NLP domain.  
- Main contribution: a clearly motivated task definition emphasizing context-specific, token-level product mentions.  
- Main contribution: analysis of domain adaptation challenges showing significant cross-forum differences.  
- Strength: dataset fills an important resource gap and is relevant to both NLP and security communities.  
- Strength: task definition is well-justified with concrete examples and rationale for token-level annotation.  
- Strength: thorough domain-adaptation analysis, including OOV rates and forum comparisons (e.g., Darkode vs Hack Forums).  
- Strength: evaluation uses multiple metrics (token-level, type-level, post-level) suitable for varied applications.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—uses standard techniques (SVM, Brown clusters, gazetteers) without strong emphasis that dataset is primary contribution.  
- Weakness: annotation guidelines are vague on “integral to the method” and could use more concrete examples.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of dataset/approach limitations and impact of posts with multiple distinct annotated tokens.  
- Weakness: inadequate comparison to related work in IE and cybercrime text analysis; literature framing could be stronger.  
- Q to authors: provide details or examples from the anonymized case studies that used this dataset.  
- Q to authors: give specific examples clarifying when a token is considered “integral to the method.”  
- Q to authors: analyze whether Darkode’s better OOV rates stem from more consistent vocabulary or fewer product types.  
- Q to authors: explain choice of a simple SVM with hand-crafted features versus modern neural approaches and any experiments considered.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—dataset and domain-adaptation analysis are valuable, but paper needs clearer emphasis on dataset, better annotation guideline detail, and stronger literature context.
