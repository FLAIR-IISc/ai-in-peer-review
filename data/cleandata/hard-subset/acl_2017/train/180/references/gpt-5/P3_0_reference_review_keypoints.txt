- New annotated dataset of cybercrime forum posts (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled) for identifying products being bought/sold, annotated at token level with NP-span projection.
- Careful task and evaluation design covering token-level, type-per-post, and post-level metrics plus NP-level projection to align with downstream use.
- Empirical study of fine-grained domain adaptation across forums comparing simple baselines, token SVM, and a post-level latent SVM, with semi-/type-/token-supervised methods and OOV/asymmetry analysis.
- Valuable, hard-to-access dataset and well-motivated, context-sensitive product identification task with clear security impact.
- Pragmatic latent SVM that selects one span per post captures an important prior and achieves high within-domain post-level accuracy.
- Thorough cross-domain analysis showing forum-to-forum transfer is difficult; Brown clusters and small gazetteers help only slightly.
- Insightful OOV breakdown and coverage analysis linking transfer performance to product inventory differences.
- Strong reproducibility and transparency: clear preprocessing, released code/data, statistical significance reporting, and appropriate inter-annotator agreement treatment.
- Modeling and adaptation methods are dated; no contextual neural baselines (BiLSTM-CRF, BERT/RoBERTa) or modern adaptation techniques tested.
- Annotation design choices introduce ambiguity: head-only labels projected by a news-trained parser risk boundary errors on noisy text.
- Inclusion of “method” tokens (e.g., hack, coder) alongside core products blurs the target concept and may inflate type matches.
- Moderate inter-annotator agreement and all annotators being authors raises potential bias in guidelines and gold standards.
- Unclear or potentially leaky type-supervision setup: how target-domain gazetteers are built and whether test-set labels are used is not specified.
- Excluding mentions outside the first/last 10 lines in long posts may bias the dataset by missing buried product mentions.
- Limited adaptation scope: only two forums have sizable training data, and asymmetry findings are not validated across more domains, time splits, or categories.
- Request clarification on gazetteer construction at test time, annotation budget, and performance as a function of curated types.
- Request NP boundary error rate on forum text and a manual comparison of token labels vs projected NP spans to quantify projection errors.
- Ask for analysis of errors from selecting method tokens vs deliverables and behavior on multi-product posts (~3%).
- Request details on ethical/data governance: IRB, ToS compliance, de-identification, release license, and safeguards.
- Ask whether contextual encoders, domain-adaptive pretraining, self-training, or domain-adversarial methods were tried and expected impact.
- Suggest adding at least one contextual baseline (span classification with a pretrained encoder) and domain-adaptive pretraining on unlabeled forum data.
- Recommend structured sequence models with character/subword features to mitigate OOV/spelling noise and report OOV recall improvements.
- Suggest reporting cross-temporal transfer to disentangle inventory vs style drift.
- Recommend providing a taxonomy/coarse families of product types to analyze semantic-level transfer.
- Advise making adaptation studies budgeted (e.g., 10/20/50 curated types or labeled posts) with clear annotation-time estimates.
