- Introduces a new IE task and dataset for product mentions in online cybercrime forums, annotated token-level across four forums with multiple evaluation views and baseline models.  
- Main contribution: dataset and task design with clear guidelines, NP projection, multiple metrics, and reported inter-annotator agreement.  
- Empirical contribution: characterization of fine-grained cross-forum domain shift, OOV/coverage analysis, and negative results for common adaptation methods.  
- Methodological contribution: a simple post-level latent SVM that attains high post-level accuracy and is useful for forum-wide analysis.  
- Strength: Novel, high-impact domain and promised release of data/models/code enhancing reproducibility and downstream use.  
- Strength: Thoughtful task formulation and evaluation metrics (token/type/post-level; NP projection) aligned to different use cases.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical study with honest negative results and appropriate statistical testing.  
- Strength: Practical post-level modeling achieves ~80 F1 on product types and ~90% post-level accuracy within-forum.  
- Strength: Annotation process involved iterative guidelines, multiple annotators, adjudication, and moderate agreement (Fleiss’ κ≈0.58–0.77).  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty and missing stronger baselines (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF, contextual encoders, adversarial/self-training).  
- Weakness: Annotation scope conflates products with “integral” method/agent tokens, increasing ambiguity and potentially harming transfer.  
- Weakness: NP projection depends on a news-trained parser with no domain sanity check; heuristics and length caps may introduce errors or inflate NP-level scores.  
- Weakness: Dataset scale is modest (~700–900 train posts per forum), annotators are authors, and sampling/temporal stratification details are not provided.  
- Weakness: Adaptation space underexplored—richer unlabeled-data methods and modern domain-adaptation techniques were not tried.  
- Question: Will the datasets (raw/tokenized/anonymized), guidelines, and code be released, and under what license/access constraints?  
- Question: How were posts sampled (subforum/topic/time) and are splits temporally stratified to control for drift?  
- Question: Can authors report results for stronger baselines (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF, contextual embeddings) to isolate model capacity vs. domain shift?  
- Question: Can authors provide breakdowns separating “product” vs. “method/agent” tokens and per-category performance?  
- Question: Any manual assessment of NP projection quality or alternative span evaluations to validate NP-level metrics?  
- Question: What is the recall loss from excluding mentions outside the first/last 10 lines and could this bias models toward title-like mentions?  
- Additional comment: Fix reference formatting and include per-forum error analyses, POS/morphology breakdowns, and ablations on character features for misspellings.  
- Additional comment: Normalizing to canonical product types and demonstrating end-task utility (e.g., trends, price linkage) would strengthen impact.  
- Overall: Valuable dataset and insightful empirical study; improving baselines and clarifying annotation/evaluation choices would raise the paper to a strong accept.
