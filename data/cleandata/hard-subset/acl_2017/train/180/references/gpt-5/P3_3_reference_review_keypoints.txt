- Introduces a new token-level dataset/task for extracting products in cybercrime forums (multiple forums, detailed guidelines, moderate IAA).  
- Proposes multiple evaluation views (token-, type-, post-level; token vs NP-projected spans) with cross-forum analysis highlighting fine-grained domain shifts and OOV vocabulary issues.  
- Benchmarks simple SVM token and latent post-level models and evaluates Brown clusters, gazetteers, and small-target labeled-domain adaptation.  
- Dataset is timely, novel, and practically useful for security-focused IE on noisy forum text; releasing data/code will aid follow-on work.  
- Thoughtful task and metrics design aligning modeling choices (e.g., post-level latent SVM) with downstream needs.  
- Solid cross-domain experimental protocol with honest reporting of large within- vs cross-forum gaps and useful diagnostics (seen vs OOV, asymmetric transfer).  
- Clear annotation process with guideline refinement and moderate Fleiss’ κ (~0.58–0.77), and head-only annotation for consistency.  
- Provides useful negative results showing this is a fine-grained domain adaptation problem where lexical OOV is significant but not sole factor.  
- Modeling baselines are limited (linear SVMs); missing modern sequence-labeling and contextual encoder baselines (BiLSTM-CRF, ELMo/BERT, domain-adaptive pretraining).  
- NP projection and sentence-selection (first/last 10 lines) may bias evaluation due to parser errors and exclusion of mentions outside selected windows.  
- Annotation semantics ambiguous for product vs method/agent (e.g., “hack”, “coder”), contributing to moderate κ and modeling confusion.  
- Adaptation methods are shallow and under-explored; stronger unlabeled-target methods and systematic ablations are missing.  
- Sparse qualitative error analysis and lacking ablations on key choices (head-only vs full span, feature effects, singleton upweighting).  
- Requests neural/sequence-model baselines and clarification of constraints if infeasible; suggests domain-adaptive LM pretraining.  
- Asks for NP-projection robustness diagnostics (parser accuracy, sensitivity to projection choices) and token-level results.  
- Requests stats and performance on method/agent annotations and consideration of multi-label schema to reduce ambiguity.  
- Asks analysis of the “first/last 10 lines” rule: proportion affected and performance with/without the restriction.  
- Suggests testing constraint-based self-training aligning with post-level assumption (add top-scoring product per unlabeled post).  
- Recommends cleaning references, clarifying data licensing/ethics (de-identification, IRB), and releasing a fully span-annotated subset.  
- Overall verdict: valuable dataset and analysis; strengthening baselines and clarifying evaluation would make it a stronger contribution, reviewer leans toward acceptance.
