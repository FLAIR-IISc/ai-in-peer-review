- New task and dataset for context-sensitive token-level product extraction from four cybercrime forums, with planned data/code release.
- Multiple evaluation views proposed: token-, type-, and post-level metrics, plus NP-projection to approximate phrase-level extraction.
- Baselines compared to a token-level SVM and a post-level latent SVM that selects one product per post.
- Strong within-forum performance (e.g., ~90% post-level accuracy on Darkode) demonstrating practical utility for forum characterization.
- Cross-forum generalization degrades markedly, showing fine-grained domain shift even across similar forums.
- Brown clusters and type-level gazetteers yield small, mostly non-significant adaptation gains.
- Limited token-level target supervision helps, but improvements are asymmetric across forum pairs.
- OOV/product inventory and stylistic differences are analyzed as explanations for cross-domain degradation.
- Primary contribution: the dataset/task and careful, context-sensitive annotation/evaluation design.
- Secondary contribution: empirical characterization of fine-grained domain shift and negative results for standard adaptation methods.
- Practical modeling insight: a simple post-level latent selection model can achieve high post-level accuracy.
- Strength: valuable, timely dataset covering tens of thousands of posts with hundreds–~1K annotated posts per forum.
- Strength: clear problem formulation and complementary metrics (token F1, type F1, post accuracy) improving interpretability.
- Strength: NP-projection is a pragmatic compromise to measure phrase-level extraction without full span annotation.
- Strength: thorough cross-domain diagnostics, statistical testing, and OOV-based error analysis.
- Strength: transparent annotation process with reported Fleiss’ kappa (~0.58–0.77) and guideline/adjudication details.
- Weakness: limited modeling novelty and absence of stronger sequence-tagging and neural/adversarial adaptation baselines.
- Weakness: annotation scope (only first/last 10 lines) may bias data; annotators were the authors, raising potential bias concerns.
- Weakness: NP-projection relies on off-the-shelf parsing of noisy text, which may introduce systematic errors.
- Weakness: gazetteer supervision is simulated from labeled data rather than realistic low-effort curation, lacking effort-vs-gain analysis.
- Weakness: ethical and legal considerations of cybercrime data collection and release are under-discussed.
