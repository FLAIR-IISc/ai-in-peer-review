- New dataset: 93,924 posts (1,938 annotated) across four cybercrime forums; frames product identification as a fine-grained domain adaptation task.  
- Empirical finding: cross-forum generalization is challenging even within this domain.  
- Domain analysis: detailed characterization of product coverage and stylistic differences and their impact on transfer.  
- Task formulation: bridges information extraction and NER, motivating further domain adaptation work.  

Strengths:
- High-quality dataset: large, multi-forum resource with clear dataset statistics.  
- Relevant task: directly applicable to security use cases (threat tracking, market analysis).  
- Thorough evaluation: comprehensive baselines, adaptation methods, multiple metrics, and insightful OOV analysis.  
- Insightful analysis: distinguishes coverage vs stylistic causes of poor generalization; notes asymmetric transfer (easier from Darkode).  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty in methods: adaptation techniques are standard and not a major methodological advance.  
- Lack of statistical significance reporting: many reported differences lack comprehensive testing.  
- Overly complex evaluation: multiplicity of metrics complicates clear conclusions.  
- Limited error analysis: needs more qualitative breakdown of error types beyond OOV rates.  

Questions for authors:
- Please provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-test or bootstrap) for performance differences.  
- What advanced adaptation methods (e.g., adversarial training, meta-learning) might overcome current limitations?  
- Have you explored active learning to prioritize which posts to annotate in a new forum?  
- Can you give specific examples where stylistic differences cause system failures?  

Additional comments:
- Clarify potential biases in data and annotation across forums.  
- Include detailed annotation guidelines and sample annotated posts in the supplementary material.  
- Discuss ethical implications and potential misuse given the sensitive domain.
