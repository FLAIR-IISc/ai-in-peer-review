- New dataset for identifying products in cybercrime forums is the core contribution and fills a critical NLP gap.  
- Task framed as a challenging fine-grained domain adaptation problem across diverse forums.  
- Proposed adaptation techniques gave only marginal gains, but analysis shows significant distributional differences even within the cybercrime domain.  
- Detailed inter-annotator agreement analysis (Fleiss' Kappa 0.58–0.77) is a valuable secondary contribution.  
- High-quality dataset: 93,924 posts with 1,938 annotated and forum diversity (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled).  
- Well-defined, practically relevant task that sits between information extraction and NER and captures context-dependent, messy forum language.  
- Thorough error analysis (e.g., OOV breakdown, Darkode’s broader coverage) explains why adaptation methods struggle.  
- Rigorous evaluation metrics beyond token-level F1, including type-level extraction and post-level accuracy.  
- Adaptation methods explored (Brown clusters, gazetteers, simple augmentation) yielded limited improvements; more advanced methods could be tried.  
- Paper’s treatment of linguistic messiness is superficial; deeper analysis of slang, code-switching, and error types is needed.  
- Baselines are simplistic; comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., fine-tuned pre-trained LMs) are missing.  
- Ensure the full annotation guide in the supplement is comprehensive and accessible for reproducibility.  
- Questions for authors: examples of Darkode-overlapping products; methods for selecting a single product per post; details of the anonymized work using the dataset; impact of tokenization choices.  
- Presentation strengths: clear captions, well-organized writing, up-to-date references.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the weaknesses above.
