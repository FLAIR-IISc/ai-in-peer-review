- Primary contribution: new dataset of 93,924 posts with 1,938 annotated across four cybercrime forums.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical demonstration of substantial cross-domain adaptation challenges even within cybercrime.  
- Task framing: hybrid of slot-filling and NER with token-level annotations to support varied downstream analyses.  
- Strength — dataset value: difficult-to-access forums; dataset is a significant resource with clear annotation scheme and decent IAA (Fleiss’ Kappa 0.58–0.77).  
- Strength — clear formulation: well-articulated domain-adaptation challenge and justified token-level approach.  
- Strength — evaluation breadth: multiple baseline models, metric types (token/type/post-level), and ablation studies (Brown clusters, gazetteers).  
- Strength — domain analysis: detailed investigation of domain differences and OOV rates showing vocabulary divergence drives transfer issues.  
- Weakness — limited adaptation gains: explored techniques (Brown clusters, gazetteers, adding target data) yield modest improvements without deep analysis of failure modes.  
- Weakness — conservative metric: post-level accuracy penalizes systems that find multiple mentions and may understate real gains.  
- Weakness — weak baselines: lack of comparisons to stronger models (e.g., fine-tuned pretrained LMs like BERT).  
- Weakness — shallow error analysis: insufficient qualitative breakdown of errors (misspellings, slang, ambiguity, etc.).  
- Question: provide detailed examples of model errors and most-missed product types.  
- Question: did annotators’ domain expertise bias annotations, and how was this mitigated?  
- Question: does using training-set-derived gazetteers risk overfitting, and how was that addressed?  
- Question: were different Brown cluster sizes or alternative clustering algorithms tried?  
- Additional: expand related work discussion on domain adaptation for information extraction.  
- Additional: provide dataset and code availability link upon publication.  
- Additional: simplify or clarify complex sections (e.g., latent SVM) for broader accessibility.  
- Overall judgment: solid, valuable resource with limited adaptation results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
