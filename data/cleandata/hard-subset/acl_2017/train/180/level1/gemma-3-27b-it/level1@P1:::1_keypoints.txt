- Novel dataset: released 93,924 posts from four cybercrime forums with 1,938 annotated posts, detailed stats and annotation process.  
- Characterizes fine-grained domain shift: shows substantial performance degradation when transferring models between cybercrime forums.  
- Baseline evaluation: thorough experiments with several adaptation techniques (Brown clusters, gazetteers, token-level annotation) that yield limited improvement and insightful failure analysis.  
- Dataset quality and accessibility: well-documented annotations, reasonable inter-annotator agreement (~0.6), and commitment to releasing data, models, and code.  
- Clear problem definition: task framed as slot-filling/NER for products being bought or sold with a compelling motivation.  
- Rigorous evaluation: multiple metrics (token-, type-, post-level) and analysis of seen vs. OOV token performance.  
- Thorough experimentation: ablation studies and range of baselines provide useful empirical insights.  
- Honest limitation discussion: authors acknowledge and analyze why adaptation techniques struggle.  
- Limited impact of adaptation techniques: explored methods provide little practical improvement, leaving the main problem unsolved.  
- Annotation scope/granularity concerns: restricting annotations to tokens for items "being bought or sold" may introduce ambiguity and limit utility.  
- Forum selection/generalizability: dataset covers four forums, raising questions about representativeness across the broader cybercrime landscape.  
- Superficial feature engineering: classifier features are basic; more advanced contextual or syntactic features could improve results.  
- Lack of SOTA comparisons: no evaluation against modern models (e.g., transformers) to benchmark performance.  
- Request for forum selection rationale: clarify criteria and representativeness of chosen forums.  
- Request for annotation criteria/examples: provide edge-case guidelines and examples from supplementary material.  
- Ask for conservatism quantification: report precision/recall curves to compare token- vs. post-level systems.  
- Suggest alternative approaches: consider meta-learning or transfer from related domains (e.g., e-commerce).  
- Request computational cost details: provide training and deployment resource requirements.  
- Recommend significance testing and clearer model/feature descriptions: add statistical tests for Brown/gazetteer effects and expand Section 4 details.  
- Suggest adding a forum characteristics table: summarize key properties of each forum to clarify domain differences.
