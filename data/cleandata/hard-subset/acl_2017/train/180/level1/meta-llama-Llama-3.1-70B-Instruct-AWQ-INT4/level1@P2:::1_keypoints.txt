- Introduces a new task of identifying products in online cybercrime forums and releases a dataset of 93,924 posts.
- Proposes learning-based extraction methods (binary classification, post-level classification) and evaluates them with multiple metrics.
- Studies fine-grained domain adaptation challenges and explores methods to improve cross-forum performance.
- Contribution: provides a valuable new dataset and task for the NLP community.
- Contribution: proposes comprehensive evaluation metrics (token-level, type-level, post-level).
- Contribution: learning-based methods outperform baseline approaches.
- Strength: novel, relevant task and dataset for information extraction and domain adaptation.
- Strength: thorough evaluation across several metrics gives insight into system behavior.
- Strength: detailed analysis of domain adaptation challenges and possible mitigation strategies.
- Weakness: explores only a limited set of domain adaptation techniques.
- Weakness: does not compare methods against state-of-the-art information extraction/domain-adaptation approaches.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed error analysis to clarify method limitations.
- Question: request for more details on annotation process and labeling guidelines.
- Question: asks how authors plan to tackle fine-grained domain adaptation and what additional methods will be tried.
- Question: requests a more detailed error analysis and discussion of approach limitations.
- Additional comment: paper is well-written but would benefit from more related-work discussion and details on compute/time requirements.
