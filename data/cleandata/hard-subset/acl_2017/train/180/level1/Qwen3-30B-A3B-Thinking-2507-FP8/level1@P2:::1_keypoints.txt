- Introduces a novel dataset (93,924 posts, 1,938 token-level annotated) and a task for identifying products in cybercrime forums.  
- Frames fine-grained domain adaptation within cybercrime forums, showing different forums act as distinct subdomains.  
- Evaluates basic adaptation methods (Brown clusters, type- and token-level annotation) but finds limited gains.  
- Major dataset contribution: large, well-annotated corpus with reasonable inter-annotator agreement and careful guidelines.  
- Clear problem motivation and examples that distinguish product identification from standard NER/slot-filling.  
- Thorough empirical analysis showing large cross-forum performance drops and OOV product issues.  
- Weakness: explored adaptation techniques are basic and miss more recent sophisticated methods (e.g., adversarial, multi-task).  
- Weakness: insufficient positioning against prior work on messy forum/social media IE; novelty over existing efforts not clearly argued.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of dataset limitations and potential biases, including the exclusion rule for mentions beyond the first/last 10 lines.  
- Question: please provide additional evidence for the claim that Darkode is more "general" (e.g., product-type diversity or per-type frequency comparisons).  
- Question: clarify the rationale and impact of excluding product mentions outside the first/last 10 lines of posts.  
- Question: what more advanced domain adaptation methods might be promising for this task and why were they not evaluated?
