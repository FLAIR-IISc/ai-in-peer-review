- Introduces token-level product extraction in cybercrime forums and provides a new annotated dataset from four forums.
- Demonstrates large cross-domain performance drops and shows standard domain adaptation techniques give limited improvements.
- Contribution: public, well-annotated dataset with reported inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa 0.65–0.67).
- Contribution: detailed empirical characterization of fine-grained domain adaptation and asymmetric transfer effects between forums.
- Contribution: establishes a challenging benchmark with multiple evaluation metrics (token-, type-, post-, and phrase-level).
- Strength: high-value dataset in an important, under-explored domain, released with models and code.
- Strength: comprehensive, well-designed evaluation and informative analyses (e.g., OOV breakdown, learning curves).
- Strength: clear presentation and effective tables/figures that support the paper’s claims.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—relies on feature-based models and standard adaptation tricks rather than modern neural sequence models.
- Weakness: annotation scheme marks only headwords (not full spans), losing specificity and depending on parser projection for phrase-level evaluation.
- Weakness: small test sets for Blackhat and Nulled (80–100 posts) reduce confidence in conclusions drawn from those forums.
- Question: please justify token-only annotation and estimate the noise introduced by automatic projection to noun phrases.
- Question: did you try neural sequence labeling models (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF), and if not, can you compare to such baselines?
