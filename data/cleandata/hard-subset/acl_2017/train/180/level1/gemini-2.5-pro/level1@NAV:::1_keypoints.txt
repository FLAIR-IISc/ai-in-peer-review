- Introduces a token-level IE task to identify products for sale in cybercrime forums, framed as a fine-grained domain adaptation problem reliant on post-level context.
- Contribution: releases 94k posts from four forums with 1,938 manually annotated posts, plus documentation and inter-annotator agreement.
- Contribution: analyzes cross-forum performance degradation (e.g., high OOV product rates) and shows standard adaptation methods give limited gains.
- Strength: Well-motivated, high-impact dataset collected via careful, multi-stage annotation across multiple sources.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation using token, type, post, and NP-level metrics and a strong seen vs. OOV performance analysis.
- Strength: Clear problem formulation that distinguishes the task from standard NER and positions it as a domain-adaptation benchmark.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—models used are standard and main contribution is data/analysis rather than new techniques.
- Weakness: Baselines are outdated (SVMs); no modern neural baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF, Transformers) to contextualize difficulty.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of recent domain-adaptation techniques (e.g., adversarial methods) to support the claim that adaptation remains an open problem.
- Question: Have you evaluated modern neural baselines (e.g., a BERT-based token classifier) for in-domain and cross-domain comparisons?
