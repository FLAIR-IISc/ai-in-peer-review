- Introduces a new IE task: identify products being bought/sold in cybercrime forum posts.  
- Annotates 1,938 posts across four forums (Darkode, Hack Forums, Blackhat, Nulled) and releases 93,924 unlabeled posts.  
- Defines multi-view evaluation metrics: token-, NP/type-, and post-level accuracy, with NP projection via automatic parsing.  
- Baselines include lexical heuristics, token/NP-level linear SVMs, and a post-level latent SVM that picks one product per post.  
- Main empirical finding: substantial performance degradation under cross-forum transfer, evidencing fine-grained domain shifts.  
- Standard semi-supervised/domain-adaptation techniques (Brown clusters, small gazetteers, Daumé III) give only modest gains.  
- Contribution: a new, practically important dataset and task with token-level guidelines and reported inter-annotator agreement.  
- Contribution: systematic empirical characterization of within- vs. cross-forum domain shift with diagnostic analyses (OOV rates, learning curves).  
- Contribution: clear baseline models, NP projection pipeline, and reproducible evaluation protocol.  
- Strength: well-motivated multi-view evaluation that supports different downstream goals and pragmatic NP projection.  
- Strength: thorough cross-domain experiments showing large drops in NP F1 and post-level accuracy across forum pairs.  
- Strength: diagnostic analysis quantifying effect of product inventory mismatch and asymmetric transfer behavior.  
- Strength: intent to release data, code, and models to support reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—only linear SVMs and older adaptation methods; no neural sequence-labeling or modern adaptation baselines.  
- Weakness: annotation design choices (token/head-only, first/last 10 lines) may bias the task and agreement is only moderate (kappa ~0.58–0.77).  
- Weakness: phrase-level evaluation depends on an off-the-shelf parser applied to noisy forum text with no parser-validation or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: dataset scope/splits limited—only two forums used for training, modest annotated size (1,938 posts), and author-only annotations may introduce bias.  
- Weakness: limited ablation and qualitative error analysis—few concrete error categories or feature-level insights beyond OOV stats.  
- Question: what exactly will be released (raw text, tokenization, annotations) and how are legal/ethical issues (redaction/anonymization, access) handled?  
- Question: provide more annotation reproducibility details—disagreements on head selection vs. inclusion, adjudication stats, and whether full spans were considered.  
- Question: quantify impact of the first/last 10 lines rule on performance and annotation effort, with sensitivity analysis if relaxed.  
- Question: report parser accuracy on this domain and sensitivity of NP-level results to parsing errors and NP length threshold.  
- Question: did you try stronger baselines (CRF, BiLSTM-CRF, character/subword embeddings, adversarial/multitask adaptation), and if not, justify expected impact.  
- Question: can you report lexical/product overlap or similarity measures between forums to explain asymmetric transfer patterns?
