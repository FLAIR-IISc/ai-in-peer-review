- Introduces a new token-level product extraction task and releases annotated data and code for four cybercrime forums.  
- Proposes multiple evaluation metrics (token-, phrase-, type-, post-level) matched to application needs.  
- Finds substantial cross-forum domain shift that sharply degrades performance.  
- Shows Brown clusters, small gazetteers, and limited target labels only weakly mitigate transfer issues.  
- Presents a post-level latent “pick-one-product” model that yields high post-level accuracy and competitive type-level F1.  
- Provides careful, contextualized annotation (only transacted products) and thoughtful evaluation design.  
- Thorough empirical characterization of domain adaptation with OOV analysis and learning curves for limited target supervision.  
- Supplies practical baselines, feature engineering, semisupervised features, and reproducible experimental details.  
- Promises public release of data, code, annotation guidelines, and documentation of preprocessing/optimization.  
- Annotation scale is small (1,938 posts) relative to available data, limiting statistical power and headroom for stronger models.  
- Moderate inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa 0.58–0.77) and projection of head tokens to NP spans may introduce noise.  
- Modeling baselines lag recent advances: no neural sequence taggers or modern domain-adaptation methods are evaluated.  
- Target-domain gazetteer experiments are underspecified and may not reflect realistic annotation budgets or noise.  
- Evaluation choices (e.g., excluding mentions outside first/last 10 lines, NP projection with newswire parser) may bias results and add errors.  
- Ethical, legal, and privacy considerations around releasing cybercrime forum text are insufficiently addressed (IRB, redaction, access controls).  
- Requests experiments with neural taggers and domain-adversarial baselines to verify transfer conclusions.  
- Requests concrete protocol and budget for constructing target-domain gazetteers and performance versus annotation effort.  
- Asks for measurement of NP-projection error against gold spans and analysis of title vs. body contributions.  
- Seeks clarification of dataset release safeguards and whether IRB/ethical review was performed.  
- Overall: valuable dataset and analysis with informative findings, but needs stronger baselines, larger annotation scale, clearer adaptation protocols, and ethical documentation to fully support claims.
