- New task and dataset: token-level annotations of product mentions (including verbs) across four cybercrime forums with multiple evaluation views (token, NP/type-per-post, post-level).

- Main contributions: publicly released multi-forum dataset and metrics; empirical study of cross-forum degradation and OOV/domain differences; baseline SVM token and latent post-level models with simple adaptation techniques.

- Strength: valuable, societally relevant dataset and clear task formulation covering noisy security data with motivated evaluation metrics.

- Strength: thorough, transparent evaluation protocol showing how metric choice affects modeling and quantifying within- and cross-forum gaps.

- Strength: careful analysis of domain shift and OOV effects explaining asymmetric adaptation behavior across forums.

- Strength: reproducible, well-motivated baselines (token SVM, latent post SVM, Brown clusters, gazetteers) and solid within-domain post-level performance.

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty and outdated baselines for ACL’17—no neural sequence or modern domain-adaptive baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF, adversarial DA, SCL).

- Weakness: annotation choices may bias evaluation—head-only token labels, NP projection using a WSJ parser on noisy text, and truncating long posts could compound errors and omit mentions.

- Weakness: modest inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ 0.58–0.77) with all annotators being authors; adjudication protocol and disagreement analysis are lacking.

- Weakness: type-level/gazetteer supervision mixes regimes by extracting gazetteers from target labeled data; need clearer simulation of low-cost supervision and separation of settings with no target labels.

- Weakness: missing related work on domain adaptation (e.g., SCL, instance weighting) and no ablation on normalization/subword methods for heavy misspelling/obfuscation.

- Question: data release and ethics—what are licensing, anonymization, PII redaction, and dual-use mitigations?

- Question: parser robustness—what is parsing accuracy on forum text, sensitivity of NP-level results to parser errors and the length ≤7 cap?

- Question: gazetteer cost—how expensive/popular is constructing type-level lexicons in a new forum (time estimate) and gains vs. annotation cost?

- Question: disagreement sources—what are dominant annotation disagreements and any forum-specific trends?

- Question: why were SCL/pivot features or adversarial domain-adaptation methods not tried—any constraints?

- Additional suggestion: add character/subword neural baselines (BiLSTM-CRF), domain-adversarial training, pivot-feature methods, and normalization/spelling-correction ablations.

- Additional suggestion: report per-forum class priors/product-distribution overlap (e.g., JS divergence), cross-domain post-level accuracy vs simple heuristics (e.g., title-only), and clarify dataset splits/formatting and citation fixes.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — resource and domain-shift analysis are valuable, but paper would be stronger with at least one contemporary DA baseline and deeper annotation/normalization analysis.
