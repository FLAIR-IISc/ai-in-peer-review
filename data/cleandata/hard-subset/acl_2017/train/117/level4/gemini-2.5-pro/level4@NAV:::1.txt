- **Strengths:**
The paper addresses the timely and significant problem of mapping natural language queries to relations within a Knowledge Base (KB). This is explored in the challenging context of Question Answering (QA), where models must contend with partial information for one of the arguments and an exceptionally large space of candidate relations.

The core of the proposed method is a novel technique for integrating two distinct representations of the input: a fine-grained, word-level representation that segments both the input query and the target relation names, and a holistic representation that treats entire relations as single, atomic tokens.

A key contribution to the QA pipeline appears to be the model's capacity for effective re-ranking of candidate entities following the initial Entity Linking stage.

The empirical results demonstrate a clear improvement over existing state-of-the-art approaches.

- **Weaknesses:**
The experimental validation is conducted on a somewhat limited dataset, which may constrain the generalizability of the findings.

- **General Discussion:**
In terms of presentation, the authors might consider restructuring the paper for improved clarity. Section 3.1 seems more aligned with the Related Work section. Relocating it would allow the core proposal, currently in Section 3.2, to become the focus of a new Section 3. This new, dedicated section could then be subdivided more logically to better articulate the components of the proposed method.
