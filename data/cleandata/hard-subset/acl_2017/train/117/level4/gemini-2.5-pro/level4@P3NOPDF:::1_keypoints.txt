- Tackles mapping natural-language questions to KB relations under large label space and partial input.  
- Core contribution: hybrid model combining word-level (compositional) and single-token (holistic) relation representations.  
- Uses relation prediction to re-rank entity candidates after Entity Linking in QA pipelines.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over state-of-the-art on the chosen benchmark.  
- Strength: addresses a timely, scalable QA challenge with realistic assumptions (many relations, missing answer entity).  
- Strength: hybrid approach is well-motivated—word-level for generalization, single-token for idiomatic/frequent relations.  
- Strength: practical impact by improving entity disambiguation in multi-stage QA systems.  
- Strength: empirical results are positive and show clear gains versus baselines.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a single, relatively small dataset, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Suggestion: reorganize paper to move baseline background to Related Work and make the model section more focused.  
- Suggestion: add error analysis comparing when word-level vs. single-token components succeed or fail.  
- Suggestion: discuss scalability concerns, especially memory/compute growth for single-token representations as relation counts increase.
