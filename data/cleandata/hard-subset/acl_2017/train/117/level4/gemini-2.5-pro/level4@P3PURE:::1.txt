### Strengths

This paper addresses the relevant and challenging problem of mapping natural language questions to knowledge base (KB) relations. This is tackled in a question-answering context where only partial information is available for one of the arguments and the number of target relations is very large.

The authors propose a novel method for combining two distinct representations: one at the word level, where both the input text and target relation names are segmented, and another where relations are treated as single, unsegmented tokens.

A key contribution of this method to the QA pipeline is its ability to re-rank candidate entities following the entity linking step. The experimental results demonstrate a clear improvement over existing state-of-the-art methods.

### Weaknesses

The proposed approach is evaluated on a single, limited dataset, which may raise questions about the generalizability of the results.

### General Discussion

The structure of the paper could be improved. Section 3.1 seems more appropriate as part of the Related Work section. This would allow the current Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3, dedicated entirely to the proposed method. This new section could then be more clearly structured and subdivided.
