- Introduces HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical residual BiLSTM for KB relation detection.  
- Represents relations at both word-level (generalization) and full relation-name level (specificity).  
- Uses a two-layer BiLSTM with residual connections to create multi-level question abstractions for hierarchical matching.  
- Proposes a two-step KBQA pipeline: use HR-BiLSTM to re-rank entity candidates, then predict relations for top entities.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on relation detection and end-to-end KBQA for SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Contribution: novel hierarchical matching model that balances generalization and specificity via residual learning.  
- Contribution: practical two-step system that leverages relation detection to improve entity disambiguation and end-to-end performance.  
- Strength: well-motivated, elegant architecture aligning relation granularities with question abstractions.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and convincing ablation studies isolating key components.  
- Strength: substantial end-to-end impact through a simple, effective entity re-ranking approach.  
- Weakness: limited analysis explaining why deep/residual architecture helps WebQSP much more than SimpleQuestions.  
- Weakness: unclear whether performance gap to more complex systems (e.g., STAGG) stems from relation detector or the simpler pipeline design.  
- Question: please provide concrete analysis/examples showing what additional abstractions the second BiLSTM layer captures for WebQSP.  
- Question: did you try training separate specialized models for entity re-ranking and relation detection, and might that improve performance?
