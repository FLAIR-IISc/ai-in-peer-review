- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches question and relation representations at word and relation-token levels for relation detection.  
- Uses dual relation representation (word-level components + full relation token) to balance compositional generalization and global context.  
- Integrates the relation detector into a two-step KBQA pipeline: entity candidate re-ranking then relation identification for top entity.  
- Evaluated on SimpleQuestions (single-relation) and WebQSP (multi-relation) benchmarks, achieving state-of-the-art end-to-end results.  
- Contribution 1: novel hierarchical relation detection model with residual connections that aids deep representation matching.  
- Contribution 2: shows a strong relation detector can improve KBQA via an entity re-ranking step that reduces entity-linking ambiguity.  
- Strength: well-motivated architecture combining multi-granularity relation representations and residual learning.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains on isolated relation detection and end-to-end KBQA, demonstrating practical utility.  
- Strength: effective KBQA integration (entity re-ranking) that meaningfully boosts pipeline performance.  
- Strength: thorough ablation studies validating hierarchical representation, residuals, and BiLSTM choice.  
- Weakness: overall KBQA pipeline is simple and less novel beyond the relation detector (no joint inference or advanced re-ranking).  
- Weakness: top reported results rely on an ensemble of three models, obscuring single-model contribution.  
- Weakness: Method section structure/clarity could be improved—motivation content misplaced and sectioning unclear.  
- Question: please explain why residual learning helps much more on WebQSP than on SimpleQuestions—are there dataset-specific phenomena or examples illustrating this?  
- Question: did you try replacing entity mentions with <e> during entity re-ranking (instead of raw question text), and what was its impact on re-ranking performance?
