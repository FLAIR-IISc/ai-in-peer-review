**Strengths:**

The paper addresses the important and relevant problem of mapping natural language to knowledge base relations. This is particularly challenging in the context of question answering (QA), where only partial information (i.e., one entity argument) is available in the question, and the number of candidate relations can be very large.

The core proposal is a novel method for combining two different representations: a word-level representation, where both the input text and target relation names are segmented into tokens, and a relation-level representation, where each relation is treated as a single, atomic unit.

A key contribution to the QA task appears to be the model's ability to effectively re-rank candidate entities following an initial entity linking step. The experimental results demonstrate a clear improvement over state-of-the-art methods on the chosen dataset.

**Weaknesses:**

The evaluation is conducted on a single dataset, which may limit the generalizability of the findings. Testing the approach on additional, diverse datasets would strengthen the paper's conclusions.

**General Discussion:**

For improved clarity and structure, I suggest a minor reorganization of the paper. Section 3.1 seems better suited for the Related Work section, as it primarily covers background material. This would allow Section 3.2, which details the core proposal, to become the new Section 3. This reframed section could then be split into more logical subsections to better delineate the different components of the proposed model.
