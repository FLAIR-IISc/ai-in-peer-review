- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM that matches hierarchical question representations to multi-granular relation representations (word- and relation-level) and integrates it into a KBQA pipeline with entity re-ranking, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Strength: Novel HR-BiLSTM using hierarchical representations and residual connections effectively improves relation detection (+2–3% over strong baselines).

- Strength: Demonstrates state-of-the-art KBQA performance on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) benchmarks, showing robustness.

- Strength: Introduces an entity re-ranking step driven by relation detection to reduce entity-linking ambiguity; ablation shows this component is crucial.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—only tested on two benchmarks; additional datasets with different KB schemas/linguistic properties would strengthen generalizability claims.

- Weakness: Section 3’s organization is unclear; motivational material on granularity should be relocated and the model description streamlined into clearer subsections.
