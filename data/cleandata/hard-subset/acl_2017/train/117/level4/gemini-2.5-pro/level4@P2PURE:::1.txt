- **Strengths**: The paper addresses the relevant and challenging problem of mapping natural language to knowledge base relations. It focuses specifically on a Question Answering context, where only partial information about one argument is available and the number of potential target relations is very large.

The proposed method combines two different representations of the input: a word-level model that segments the input text and target relation names, and a relation-level model that treats entire relations as single atomic tokens.

A key contribution to the QA task appears to be the model's ability to re-rank candidate entities after the initial entity linking step. The experimental results demonstrate an improvement over state-of-the-art methods.

- **Weaknesses**: The approach has been evaluated on a single, somewhat limited dataset, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate its generalizability.

- **General Discussion**: For improved clarity, I would suggest a minor restructuring of the paper. The content of Section 3.1 seems more appropriate for the Related Work section. This change would allow Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3, dedicated entirely to the proposed method. This reframed Section 3 could then be structured with clearer subsections to better present the core contribution.
