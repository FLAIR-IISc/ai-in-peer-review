## Review

This paper addresses a relevant topic: learning the mapping between natural language and KB relations, in the context of question answering (QA) – a scenario where information for one argument is often incomplete, and the number of potential target relations is very large. The proposal introduces a novel method to combine different representations of the input text: a word-level representation (segmenting both target relation names and the input text) and a relation-level representation (treating relations as single tokens without segmentation). The key contribution to QA appears to be the ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step. Results demonstrate improvements over the state of the art.

**Contributions:**

The primary contribution of this work is a hierarchical residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model for KB relation detection, effectively combining word-level and relation-level representations with residual learning to improve performance, particularly in scenarios with a large number of relations and potential for unseen relations. 

A significant secondary contribution is the demonstration that incorporating relation detection as a re-ranking step *after* entity linking enhances the performance of a KBQA system. This is a practical and effective way to leverage relation information to mitigate ambiguity in the initial entity linking phase.

Finally, the paper provides a strong empirical evaluation on standard KBQA benchmarks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), showcasing state-of-the-art results and ablation studies that validate the design choices of the proposed model.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The proposed HR-BiLSTM model consistently outperforms existing methods on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, establishing a new state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions. The reported improvements are statistically significant, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
2. **Novel Combination of Representations:** The hierarchical matching of question representations with both word-level and relation-level representations is a novel and well-motivated technique. This allows the model to capture both fine-grained and coarse-grained information from the relation names, improving generalization.
3. **Effective Integration into KBQA Pipeline:** Integrating the relation detection model into a two-step KBQA pipeline – with entity re-ranking followed by relation detection – proves to be a practical and beneficial approach. This highlights the importance of considering the end-task when designing relation detection models.
4. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies provide valuable insights into the contribution of each component of the proposed model (residual learning, hierarchical matching, relation-level representations), justifying the design choices and highlighting the importance of the proposed architecture.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Diversity:** The evaluation is primarily conducted on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP. While these are standard benchmarks, the generalizability of the approach to other KBQA datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different knowledge base schemas, question types, or entity linking challenges) remains unclear.
2. **Complexity of the System:** While the two-step pipeline is effective, the system incorporates multiple components (entity linking, relation detection, constraint detection), making it potentially more complex to implement and tune compared to end-to-end approaches. 
3. **Dependence on Entity Linking Quality:** The performance of the relation detection re-ranking step is inherently dependent on the quality of the initial entity linking results. Errors in entity linking can propagate to the relation detection stage, potentially limiting the overall system performance.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the error cases where the proposed model fails? Specifically, what types of questions or relations are most challenging for the HR-BiLSTM?
2.  How sensitive is the performance of the system to the quality of the entity linker? Have you experimented with different entity linkers to assess the robustness of the approach?
3.  While the ablation studies are comprehensive, could you explore the impact of different pooling strategies (e.g., average pooling vs. max pooling) in the HR-BiLSTM architecture?

**Additional Comments:**
