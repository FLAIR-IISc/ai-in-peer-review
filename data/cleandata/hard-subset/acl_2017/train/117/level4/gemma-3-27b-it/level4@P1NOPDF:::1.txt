This paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem: learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations within the context of question answering (QA), specifically when only partial information is available for one of the arguments, and when dealing with a very large number of possible target relations.

The core of the proposal is a novel method for combining two distinct representations of the input text. One representation operates at the word level, incorporating segmentation of both the target relation names and the input text itself. The other treats relations as single tokens, foregoing segmentation of either relation names or the input text. A key contribution, particularly within the context of QA, appears to be the ability to re-rank entities *after* the entity linking step. The experimental results demonstrate an improvement over state-of-the-art methods.

However, the approach has been evaluated on a relatively limited dataset, which is a potential concern.

Overall, I suggest a restructuring of the paper. Currently, section 3.1 feels more appropriately placed within the related work section. Moving it there would allow section 3.2 to become the primary section 3, presenting the proposed method. This reorganization would then facilitate a more logical and coherent breakdown of the work.
