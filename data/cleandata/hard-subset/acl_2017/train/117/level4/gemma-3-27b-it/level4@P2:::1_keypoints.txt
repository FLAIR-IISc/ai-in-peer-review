- Addresses relation detection for KBQA with large relation vocabularies and zero-shot needs using a hierarchical recurrent model with residual learning.  
- Combines word-level and relation-level representations to improve question-relation matching.  
- Integrates relation detection into a KBQA pipeline to re-rank entity candidates after entity linking.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP benchmarks.  
- Hierarchical relation representation plus residual learning is a strong technical contribution for large-scale relation detection.  
- Entity re-ranking is a practical contribution that addresses a common KBQA bottleneck.  
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate effectiveness on both single- and multi-relation QA tasks.  
- Tackles critical KBQA challenges of scale and sparsity, including unseen relations at test time.  
- Novel combination of fine-grained (word) and coarse-grained (relation) representations captures relation semantics better.  
- Residual learning aids sequence matching training and improves representation power (supported by ablations).  
- Demonstrates practical system integration, showing end-to-end KBQA relevance.  
- Evaluation on two standard datasets provides a robust assessment, but is limited in diversity.  
- Generalizability to other KBs, question types, and relation distributions is unclear.  
- Pipeline complexity could be reduced or better analyzed to isolate component contributions (e.g., constraint detection).  
- Ablation studies could be more fine-grained to quantify word-level vs relation-level contributions.  
- Lacks detailed reporting of hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analyses.  
- Requests evaluation on more diverse KBQA datasets to test generalization.  
- Asks for a detailed breakdown of each component’s contribution (entity re-ranking, relation detection, constraint detection).  
- Seeks analysis of model sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, embedding size, residual weights).  
- Wants comparison against more recent/advanced entity linking systems.  
- Suggests reorganizing Section 3.1 into related work/introduction to streamline method presentation.  
- Recommends more standard in-text citation style for improved readability.
