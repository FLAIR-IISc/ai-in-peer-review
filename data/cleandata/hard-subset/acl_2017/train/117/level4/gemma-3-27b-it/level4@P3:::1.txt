## Review for ACL 2017 - Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering

This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), particularly focusing on scenarios with a large number of potential relations and limited information about entities. The authors propose a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning to effectively compare questions and relation names at different levels of abstraction. They further integrate this relation detector into a KBQA pipeline, demonstrating its benefits through entity re-ranking. The work presents solid experimental results on both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA tasks.

**Summary of Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Relation Representation and Residual Learning:** The core contribution lies in the novel hierarchical BiLSTM architecture with residual connections, designed to effectively capture both word-level and relation-level information for improved relation detection. This allows the model to leverage both fine-grained semantic details and broader contextual cues.
2. **Entity Re-ranking for Improved KBQA:** The integration of the relation detector into a KBQA pipeline, specifically the entity re-ranking step, demonstrates a practical application of the proposed method. This effectively addresses ambiguities in the initial entity linking stage and contributes to improved QA accuracy.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art or competitive results on established KBQA benchmarks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), validating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addressing a Key Challenge in KBQA:** The paper tackles a crucial bottleneck in KBQA systems – accurate relation detection in the face of a large knowledge base and potentially unseen relations. The focus on this specific challenge is highly relevant.
2. **Well-Motivated Architecture:** The rationale behind combining word-level and relation-level representations is clearly explained and justified. The use of residual learning to facilitate training of the deep hierarchical model is also well-motivated and demonstrably effective.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation, including ablation studies to dissect the contribution of different components of the proposed model. The comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods on standard benchmarks is convincing.
4. **Practical System Integration:** The incorporation of the relation detector into a complete KBQA pipeline and the demonstration of its positive impact on end-to-end performance strengthen the practical significance of the work. The entity re-ranking mechanism is a valuable addition.
5. **Clear and Detailed Explanations:** The paper is generally well-written and provides clear explanations of the proposed methods and experimental setup, aiding reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Diversity:** While the results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP are strong, the evaluation is limited to these two datasets. Expanding the evaluation to other KBQA datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different KB schemas, question types) would strengthen the generalizability claims.
2. **Complexity of the System:** While the individual components are well-explained, the overall KBQA pipeline, including the interaction between the entity linker and the relation detector, could benefit from a more detailed diagram or flowchart. This would improve clarity for readers.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but doesn't provide a detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the model to different hyperparameter settings. A more in-depth analysis of this aspect would be beneficial.
4. **Ablation Study Depth:** While ablation studies are present, the impact of different variations on the entity re-ranking step isn't fully explored. Analyzing the contribution of the relation detector's confidence scores to the re-ranking performance would be valuable.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. How does the performance of the proposed model degrade as the size of the knowledge base increases significantly beyond Freebase?
2. Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the range of values explored and the criteria used for selection?
3. How sensitive is the entity re-ranking step to the initial accuracy of the entity linker? Is there a threshold below which the benefits of re-ranking diminish?
4. In the experiments, the entity linking results were taken from existing resources. How would the overall performance be affected if a different or less accurate entity linker were used?

**Additional Comments:**

* Section 3.1, which discusses different granularity in KB relations, feels more appropriately placed in the related work section. Restructuring the paper to move this section would improve the flow.
* While the ablation studies are comprehensive, adding error analysis (e.g., examining specific cases where the model fails) could provide further insights into its strengths and weaknesses.
* Consider briefly discussing the computational cost of the proposed method compared to existing approaches.
