- Tackles mapping natural language to KB relations in QA with partial argument info and many candidate relations — a relevant, challenging problem.
- Combines word-level (segmented) and relation-level (single-token) representations to capture different relation/question aspects.
- Proposes entity re-ranking after entity linking, using relation detection to resolve entity linking ambiguities.
- Uses a hierarchical residual BiLSTM for hierarchical matching, aiding generalization to unseen relations.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP; ablations show the proposed components help.
- Evaluation is limited to two standard datasets, which may not reflect real-world KBQA diversity.
- Ablation studies lack deeper analysis separating effects of specific components (e.g., residual connections vs. weighted sum).
- The full KBQA pipeline (entity linking, constraint detection, etc.) adds complexity and may hinder reproducibility; contribution breakdown is unclear.
- Asks for detailed error analysis: which relations or question structures are most challenging.
- Asks how sensitive the entity re-ranking step is to the quality of initial entity linking.
- Asks whether alternative combination methods (e.g., attention) were explored and, if so, what the results were.
