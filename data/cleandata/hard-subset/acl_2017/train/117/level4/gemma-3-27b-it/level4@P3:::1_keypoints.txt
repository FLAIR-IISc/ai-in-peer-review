- Proposes a hierarchical BiLSTM with residual connections to combine word-level and relation-level information for improved relation detection.
- Integrates the relation detector into a KBQA pipeline via entity re-ranking to resolve entity-linking ambiguities.
- Shows strong empirical results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, achieving state-of-the-art or competitive performance.
- Tackles the core KBQA challenge of relation detection with many candidate relations and unseen relations.
- Well-motivated architecture that leverages multi-granularity representations and residual learning for effective training.
- Comprehensive experiments and ablation studies that convincingly compare to prior work.
- Practical system integration demonstrating end-to-end QA improvements through entity re-ranking.
- Clear writing and detailed method/experimental descriptions that aid reproducibility.
- Evaluation limited to two datasets (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP), reducing claims of broad generalizability.
- Overall system complexity and interactions (entity linker ↔ relation detector) need clearer visualization or explanation.
- Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning choices.
- Ablation studies do not fully explore the relation detector’s impact on entity re-ranking or use of confidence scores.
- Asks how performance scales as KB size grows beyond Freebase.
- Requests details on hyperparameter ranges and selection criteria used during tuning.
- Inquires about sensitivity of re-ranking to initial entity linker accuracy and potential thresholds for benefit.
- Wonders how using a different or less accurate entity linker would affect end-to-end results.
- Suggests moving Section 3.1 (relation granularity) to related work to improve paper flow.
- Recommends adding error analysis to better understand model failure cases.
- Advises discussing computational cost of the proposed method compared to alternatives.
