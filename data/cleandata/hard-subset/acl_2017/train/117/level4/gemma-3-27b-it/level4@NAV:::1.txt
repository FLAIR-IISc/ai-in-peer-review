## Review:

This paper tackles an important problem: learning the correspondence between natural language and knowledge base relations, specifically within a question answering framework where argument information is incomplete and the space of potential relations is vast. 

The core of the work is a novel method that effectively integrates two distinct textual representations – one operating at the word level with segmentation of both relation names and input text, and another treating relations as atomic tokens without such segmentation. A key contribution within the QA context appears to be the ability to refine entity rankings following the entity linking stage. Experimental results demonstrate performance gains over existing state-of-the-art methods.

A limitation of the study is its evaluation on a relatively constrained dataset.

Regarding the paper’s structure, I suggest relocating section 3.1 to the related work section. This would allow section 3.2 to be streamlined and presented as the primary description of the proposed method (now section 3), enabling a more logical and detailed presentation of the approach.
