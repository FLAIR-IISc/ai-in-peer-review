This paper tackles the important and challenging problem of mapping natural language to knowledge base (KB) relations within a question answering (QA) framework. The scenario addressed – QA with partial information for one argument and a large number of potential target relations – is highly relevant to real-world applications. 

The core contribution of this work is a novel method for integrating two distinct textual representations: one leveraging word-level segmentation of both the input text and relation names, and another treating relations as single tokens without segmentation. This approach appears particularly valuable for re-ranking entities following an entity linking stage, a crucial step in QA systems. The experimental results demonstrate improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods.

However, the evaluation is currently limited to a single dataset. While the results are promising, broader evaluation across diverse datasets would strengthen the claims regarding the generalizability of the proposed approach.

Regarding the paper’s structure, I suggest reorganizing sections 3.1 and 3.2. Specifically, the content of section 3.1 feels more aligned with related work and would benefit from being integrated there. Re-labeling the current section 3.2 as section 3 would allow for a more logical presentation of the proposed method and facilitate a clearer, more detailed breakdown of its components. This reorganization would improve the overall flow and readability of the paper.
