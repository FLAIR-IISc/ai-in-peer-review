## Review for ACL 2017

This paper addresses the important and challenging problem of learning the mapping between natural language questions and knowledge base (KB) relations, specifically within a question answering (QA) framework. The problem is well-motivated: QA systems often operate with incomplete information regarding one of the arguments, and identifying the correct relation from a large number of possibilities is crucial for success.

The core contribution of this work lies in a novel method for combining two distinct representations of the input text. The approach leverages both a word-level representation – incorporating segmentation of both the target relation names and the input question – and a representation treating relations as single, atomic tokens, without segmentation. This dual representation allows the model to capture both fine-grained semantic information and broader relational context. The demonstrated ability to re-rank entities *after* the entity linking step appears to be a significant contribution to the QA pipeline. Experimental results demonstrate improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods.

However, the evaluation of this approach is currently limited to a single dataset. While the reported improvements are encouraging, the generalizability of the method remains unclear without testing on more diverse datasets or knowledge bases. Further evaluation would strengthen the claims made and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the approach’s capabilities.

Regarding the paper's structure, I believe a reorganization would improve its clarity and flow. Specifically, Section 3.1, which provides background on existing approaches for relation ranking, feels more appropriately placed within the Related Work section. Moving Section 3.1 to the Related Work and re-numbering Section 3.2 as Section 3 would allow for a more direct presentation of the proposed method. This restructuring would then enable a more natural decomposition of the proposed approach into logically distinct subsections within the new Section 3. 

In summary, this paper presents a promising new method for KB relation linking in QA. The combination of word-level and relation-as-token representations is a novel and potentially effective approach. While the evaluation is currently limited, the reported results and the potential for re-ranking entities post-linking merit attention. Addressing the suggested structural changes would further enhance the paper's clarity and impact.
