- Addresses mapping natural language to KB relations for QA with incomplete arguments and many possible relations.  
- Proposes HR-BiLSTM combining word-level and relation-level representations with residual learning for relation detection.  
- Introduces relation detection as a re-ranking step after entity linking to improve KBQA performance.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and improvements on WebQSP with statistically significant gains.  
- Novel hierarchical matching of question with both fine-grained (word-level) and coarse-grained (relation-level) relation representations.  
- Effective integration into a two-step KBQA pipeline (entity re-ranking then relation detection) that leverages relation information to reduce ambiguity.  
- Thorough ablation studies validating the contributions of residual learning, hierarchical matching, and relation-level representations.  
- Limited dataset diversity: evaluation only on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, leaving generalizability to other KBQA settings unclear.  
- System complexity: multiple components (entity linking, relation detection, constraint detection) may be harder to implement and tune than end-to-end methods.  
- Dependence on entity linking quality: errors in initial linking can propagate and limit overall performance.  
- Request for detailed error analysis to identify challenging question/relation types for HR-BiLSTM.  
- Ask about sensitivity to different entity linkers and robustness of the re-ranking approach.  
- Suggest exploring different pooling strategies (e.g., average vs. max) in HR-BiLSTM.  
- Recommend reorganizing Section 3.1 into Related Work and renaming sections for better flow and clarity.
