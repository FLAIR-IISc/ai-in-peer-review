This paper tackles the important problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically addressing challenges arising from large relation vocabularies and the need for zero-shot learning. The core idea is a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced with residual learning, which combines word-level and relation-level representations for improved question-relation matching. A significant practical contribution appears to be the incorporation of relation detection into a KBQA pipeline, using it to re-rank entity candidates after the initial entity linking step. The results demonstrate improvements over state-of-the-art performance on both the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets.

**Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Relation Representation & Residual Learning:** The proposed method effectively combines fine-grained (word-level) and coarse-grained (relation-level) representations of relations, coupled with residual learning, to enhance relation detection accuracy. This is a strong technical contribution, particularly for handling the complexities of large-scale KB relation detection.
2. **Entity Re-ranking within a KBQA Pipeline:** Integrating relation detection to re-rank entity candidates after entity linking is a valuable practical contribution. This addresses a common bottleneck in KBQA systems, improving the overall system performance.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The reported state-of-the-art results on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) QA benchmarks provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addressing a Critical KBQA Challenge:** The paper directly addresses the challenges of scale and sparsity in KB relation detection, which are key limitations of existing methods. The large number of relations in KBs and the potential for unseen relations during testing make this a significant problem.
2. **Novel Combination of Representations:** The hierarchical approach to combining word-level and relation-level representations is a clever design choice. By leveraging both fine-grained and coarse-grained information, the model can better capture the nuances of relation semantics.
3. **Effective Use of Residual Learning:** The application of residual learning to sequence matching appears to be a crucial component, facilitating training and improving the representation power of the model, as demonstrated by the ablation studies.
4. **Practical System Integration:**  The integration of the relation detector into a full KBQA pipeline demonstrates the practical relevance of the work. The entity re-ranking step is a simple yet effective way to improve end-to-end performance.
5. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The evaluation on two standard KBQA datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) with comparisons to state-of-the-art methods provides a robust assessment of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Diversity:** While the paper achieves strong results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, the evaluation is limited to these two datasets.  The generalizability of the approach to other KBs or question types remains unclear. Further evaluation on more diverse datasets would strengthen the claims.
2. **Complexity of the System:** The description of the KBQA pipeline, while clear, is somewhat complex. A more detailed analysis of the contributions of each component to the overall performance would be beneficial. In particular, the exact benefit of the constraint detection step could be further highlighted.
3. **Ablation Study Depth:** While ablation studies are included, a more fine-grained analysis of the impact of different components (e.g., the relative contribution of word-level vs. relation-level representations) could provide deeper insights into the model's behavior.
4. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but does not provide detailed information about the tuning process or the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How would the proposed method perform on other KBQA datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different KB size, question complexity, or relation types)?
2.  Could you provide a more detailed breakdown of the contribution of each component (entity re-ranking, relation detection, constraint detection) to the overall KBQA performance?
3.  What is the sensitivity of the model's performance to different hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, embedding dimension, residual connection weight)?
4.  How does the performance of the model compare to more recent and advanced entity linking systems?
