This paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem: learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations within a question answering (QA) framework. The difficulty is compounded by the partial information available for one of the arguments and the presence of a very large number of possible target relations. The authors propose a novel method that combines two distinct representations of the input text: a word-level representation, incorporating segmentation of both target relation names and the input text, and a representation treating relations as single tokens, without such segmentation. The primary contribution in the context of QA appears to be the ability to re-rank entities following the entity linking step. Experimental results demonstrate improvements over state-of-the-art methods.

However, the approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset, which restricts the generalizability of the findings.

Regarding the paper’s structure, I believe Section 3.1 would be more appropriately positioned within the related work section. This would allow Section 3.2 to become the primary presentation of the proposed method (Section 3), enabling a more logical and detailed exposition of the technical details and a better-structured presentation of the overall approach.
