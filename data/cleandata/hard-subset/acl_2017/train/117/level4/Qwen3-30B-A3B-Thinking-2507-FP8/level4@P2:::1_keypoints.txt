- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical matching (word-level + relation-level) for KBQA relation detection to handle large-scale, zero-shot, and multi-relation challenges.  
- Uses residual learning to enable deeper hierarchical matching and better representation learning.  
- Adds an entity re-ranking step that leverages relation detection to disambiguate entities and boost KBQA accuracy.  
- Hierarchical matching achieves SOTA: 93.3% on SimpleQuestions and 82.53% on WebQSP; single-level representations perform markedly worse.  
- Word-level captures local phrase matches while relation-level captures global semantics, aiding zero-shot/generalization.  
- Residual connections significantly outperform weighted-sum and attention baselines (notably improving WebQSP performance).  
- Entity re-ranking yields a 2–3% KBQA accuracy improvement by using high-scoring relations to resolve ambiguous entities.  
- Evaluation is limited to two Freebase datasets, lacking tests on other KBs, diverse question types, and different relation distributions.  
- No thorough analysis of computational complexity, inference time, or memory requirements for HR-BiLSTM.  
- Ablation of entity re-ranking is limited; missing analysis of when it helps most (e.g., varying entity ambiguity).  
- Questions for authors: provide computational cost details; report entity re-ranking performance across ambiguity levels; consider extending hierarchical matching to nested/complex KB relation patterns.
