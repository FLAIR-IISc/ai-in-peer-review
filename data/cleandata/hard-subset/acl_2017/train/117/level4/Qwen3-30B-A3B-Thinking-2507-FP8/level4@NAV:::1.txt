

Strengths: The paper tackles a pertinent problem: learning the mapping from natural language to knowledge base relations within question answering, specifically addressing scenarios with partial information for one argument and an extremely large set of potential target relations. The core contribution introduces a novel method that integrates two distinct text representations: a word-level representation (incorporating segmentation of target relation names and input text) and a single-token representation (without segmentation of relation names or input text). The primary advancement for question answering lies in the capability to re-rank entities following the Entity Linking phase. Experimental results demonstrate consistent improvement over state-of-the-art approaches.

Weaknesses: The evaluation is conducted on a limited dataset.

General Discussion: Section 3.1 would be more appropriately placed within the Related Work section, allowing Section 3.2 to serve as the new Section 3 presenting the proposed method with a more logically structured organization.
