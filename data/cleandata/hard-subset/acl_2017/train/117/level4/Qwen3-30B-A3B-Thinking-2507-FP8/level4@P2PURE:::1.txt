

- Strengths: The paper addresses a relevant topic: learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations within question answering (QA), where partial information is available for one argument and a very large number of target relations must be considered. The authors propose a novel method that combines two distinct input text representations: a word-level representation (segmenting both target relation names and input text) and a representation treating relations as single tokens (without segmenting relation names or input text). The main contribution for QA appears to be the ability to re-rank entities following the Entity Linking step. Results demonstrate improvements over the state of the art.  
- Weaknesses: The evaluation is limited to a single dataset.  
- General Discussion: Section 3.1 would be more appropriately placed in Related Work, allowing Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3. The proposed Section 3 could then be better structured by splitting into subsections.
