- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical recurrent model with residual learning for KB relation detection and a simple KBQA system showing improved end-task performance.

- Contribution 1: improved relation detection via hierarchical matching with residual learning.

- Contribution 2: a simple KBQA pipeline with entity re-ranking that yields state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Strength: Clearly addresses KBQA-specific relation detection challenges (large relation sets, zero-shot needs, relation chains).

- Strength: HR-BiLSTM effectively combines word-level and relation-level representations and uses residual learning to enable deep hierarchical matching.

- Strength: Demonstrates practical impact—relation-based entity re-ranking resolves entity ambiguity and boosts end-task performance without complex engineering.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to two Freebase-based datasets, limiting claims of generalizability.

- Weakness: Lacks thorough failure-case analysis, especially for the entity re-ranking component.

- Weakness: Ablation studies do not clearly isolate the relative contributions of hierarchical matching versus residual learning.

- Suggestion: Reorganize Section 3.1 into Related Work so Section 3.2 can more clearly present the main contribution.

- Question: Provide more details on the entity re-ranking procedure and how it selects among multiple candidate topic entities.

- Question: Elaborate on the specific entity linking challenges in SimpleQuestions that motivated the approach.

- Question: How does the model handle ambiguous or polysemous relation names, given it relies on relation names as input?
