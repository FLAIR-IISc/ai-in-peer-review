

Strengths: The paper addresses a timely and relevant problem: learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations within question answering (QA), where partial information about one argument is typical and the target relation space is large. The core contribution is a novel approach for combining two complementary text representations: (1) a word-level representation with segmentation of both target relation names and input text, and (2) a single-token representation treating relations as atomic units without segmentation. Crucially, the method demonstrates significant practical value in QA by enabling effective re-ranking of entities following the Entity Linking step. The results show consistent improvements over the state-of-the-art, validating the approach’s efficacy.

Weaknesses: The evaluation is conducted on a single, relatively limited dataset. This restricts the assessment of the model’s generalizability and robustness across diverse QA scenarios, making it difficult to gauge the method’s broader applicability.

General Discussion: The organization of Section 3 warrants adjustment. Section 3.1, which describes the baseline method, would fit more naturally within Related Work. This reorganization would allow Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3, presenting the proposed method. The new Section 3 could then be better structured by splitting its content into clearer subsections (e.g., detailing the dual-representation framework and the re-ranking mechanism separately). This would improve the paper’s logical flow and enhance readability.
