

Strengths: The paper tackles a timely and significant challenge: learning mappings between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations for question answering (QA), particularly under conditions of partial argument information and in large-scale relation spaces. The core contribution lies in a novel method that integrates two complementary text representations: a word-level approach (with segmentation of target relation names and input text) and a token-level approach (treating relations as single tokens without segmentation). Crucially, the work demonstrates a practical advancement in QA by enabling entity re-ranking during the disambiguation phase, following the initial Entity Linking step. Empirical results show consistent improvements over state-of-the-art baselines on the evaluated task, validating the method's effectiveness.

Weaknesses: The primary limitation is the restricted scope of empirical evaluation. The approach is assessed solely on one dataset, which significantly limits the generalizability of the findings and the robustness claims. A broader evaluation across multiple datasets or domains would be necessary to fully substantiate the proposed method's advantages.

General Discussion: The paper's structure could be improved for better clarity and flow. Section 3.1, which describes the dual representation strategy, would be more appropriately placed within the Related Work section. This reorganization would allow Section 3.2 to be repositioned as the main methodology section (Section 3), with its content more logically divided into subsections covering the core technical proposal and its application to QA. Such a restructuring would enhance the paper's logical progression and make the novel contribution more prominent.
