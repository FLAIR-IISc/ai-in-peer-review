- Tackles mapping natural-language queries to KB relations under partial input and high relation cardinality.  
- Proposes a dual text representation: segmented word-level plus unsegmented relation-as-single-token.  
- Dual representation captures both lexical and structural relation patterns.  
- Enables effective post-Entity Linking re-ranking of candidate entities without major pipeline changes.  
- Shows consistent empirical improvements on WebQuestions over strong baselines/SOTA.  
- Method is simple, practical, and a useful addition to QA toolkits.  
- Evaluation is limited to WebQuestions, weakening claims of generalizability.  
- Unclear how the approach performs on datasets with more complex relations or diverse question phrasing.  
- Paper structure conflates background and contribution; Section 3.1 should be moved to Related Work.  
- Section 3 should clearly separate model architecture, re-ranking mechanism, and training protocol.  
- Need explicit clarification of how segmented and unsegmented relation representations interact during inference.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on expanded evaluation (e.g., SimpleQuestions), restructuring Sections 3.1/3, and clarifying representation interaction.
