

Strengths: The paper addresses a relevant topic: learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations in question answering (QA), where partial information is available for one argument and the target relation space is very large. The core contribution is a novel method that combines two distinct input text representations: (1) a word-level representation (with segmentation of both target relation names and input text) and (2) a single-token representation (with unsegmented relation names and input text). The primary QA contribution lies in the ability to re-rank entities following the Entity Linking step. Experimental results demonstrate statistically significant improvements over the state of the art.

Weaknesses: The approach is evaluated solely on a single dataset, limiting the generalizability of the findings.

General Discussion: Section 3.1 would be better positioned within Related Work, enabling Section 3.2 to be renumbered as Section 3 with a more logically structured presentation of the proposed method.
