

Strengths:
- The paper addresses a crucial challenge in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA): effectively mapping natural language questions to KB relations while dealing with the constraints of having partial argument information and a very large number of potential relation types.
- The authors introduce a novel method that combines two complementary representations for relation detection: a word-level representation (by segmenting relation names and question text) and a relation-level representation (treating relation names as single tokens without segmentation).
- The primary contribution to KBQA is the entity re-ranking mechanism that utilizes relation detection to resolve ambiguities in initial entity linking results, significantly improving the KBQA pipeline.
- The experimental results demonstrate consistent improvements over existing state-of-the-art approaches on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) benchmarks, with the proposed hierarchical residual BiLSTM model achieving the highest accuracy.

Weaknesses:
- The evaluation is restricted to two benchmark datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), which may not adequately demonstrate the approach's generalizability across diverse KBQA scenarios and question types.

General Discussion:
- Section 3.1, which discusses different granularities of relation representations, would be better positioned within the Related Work section to provide context for the proposed method. This would allow Section 3.2 to directly present the core contribution without the preliminary explanation.
