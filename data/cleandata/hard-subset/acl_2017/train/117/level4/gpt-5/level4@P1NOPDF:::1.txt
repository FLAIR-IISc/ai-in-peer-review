- Strengths:
The paper addresses an important problem: mapping natural language to KB relations for question answering, where only partial information is available for one argument and the set of possible target relations is very large. The proposed method combines two complementary representations of the input: (i) a word-level representation that segments both relation names and the input text, and (ii) a representation that treats relations as single tokens, without segmenting relation names or the input text. A key contribution to QA appears to be the ability to re-rank candidate entities after the entity linking step. The results show improvements over the state of the art.

- Weaknesses:
The approach is evaluated on a limited dataset.
