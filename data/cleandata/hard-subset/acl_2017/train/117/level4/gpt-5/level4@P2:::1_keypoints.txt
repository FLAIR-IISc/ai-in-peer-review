- Tackles relation detection for KBQA by matching questions to many relations with partial arguments using a hierarchical recurrent model with residual connections over relation-level and word-level representations.
- Integrates the relation detector into a KBQA pipeline by using relation scores to re-rank entity linker outputs and select relations/constraints.
- Contribution: Practical entity re-ranking using relation scores to disambiguate entity linker candidates.
- Contribution: Hierarchical matching model combining relation-level and word-level representations with residual BiLSTMs for robustness to unseen relations and varying context lengths.
- Contribution: Empirical gains over strong baselines on single- and multi-relation benchmarks (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP).
- Strength: Problem setup is relevant and evaluation aligns with realistic KBQA conditions.
- Strength: Two-granularity modeling is well-motivated—word-level tokens generalize to unseen relations; relation-level tokens capture broader context.
- Strength: Relation-based entity disambiguation is simple, effective, and reduces error propagation in the pipeline.
- Strength: Strong empirical results and ablation studies support the value of hierarchical matching and residual connections.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets; broader evaluation across domains/KBs would strengthen generality claims.
