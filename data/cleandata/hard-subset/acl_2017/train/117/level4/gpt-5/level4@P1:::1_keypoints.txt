- Problem: relation detection for KBQA under realistic constraints—only the topic entity is observed and the candidate relation space is very large/open.
- Proposed model: hierarchical BiLSTM with residual connections that matches questions to relations at two granularities (relation-as-token and relation word sequences).
- KBQA pipeline: relation detection is applied twice—re-ranking entity candidates after initial entity linking and selecting final relation(s).
- Contribution: a joint model that leverages both word-level and relation-as-token representations with residual BiLSTM layers.
- Contribution: practical KBQA technique of using relation detection to re-rank entities, which mainly drives end-to-end QA improvements.
- Empirical result: outperforms prior work on relation detection and end-to-end QA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.
- Strength: tackles an important, realistic KBQA problem including large/open relation vocabularies and partial argument observation.
- Strength: clear motivation for combining global relation tokens with word-level decomposition for better generalization.
- Strength: hierarchical residual BiLSTM stabilizes training and aligns question representations to different granularities.
- Strength: entity re-ranking addresses entity linking ambiguity and meaningfully improves QA performance.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets, so generalizability to other domains/KBs is unclear.
