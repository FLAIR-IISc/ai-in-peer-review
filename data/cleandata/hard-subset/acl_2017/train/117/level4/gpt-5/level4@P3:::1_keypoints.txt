- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches questions to relations at relation-level (whole tokens) and word-level (tokenized words).  
- Builds multi-level question representations with stacked BiLSTMs and residual connections to stabilize training and enable complementary matching.  
- Integrates relation detection into KBQA via a two-step pipeline: re-rank entity linker candidates using relation scores, then detect relations conditioned on the top entity.  
- Empirically improves relation detection and yields state-of-the-art or competitive end-to-end results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Strong empirical gains with clear ablations: 2–3 point improvement over strong baselines; both relation- and word-level views matter.  
- Residual connections enable better hierarchical matching than weighted sums or decomposable attention, especially on WebQSP.  
- Dual-view design (relation tokens + word tokens) helps handle large relation spaces and unseen relations.  
- Simple two-step KBQA integration effectively mitigates entity linker ambiguity without complex joint models or hand-crafted features.  
- Thorough comparisons across relation inputs, encoders (LSTM vs CNN), hierarchical matching methods, and depths; results are informative and generalizable.  
- Clear connection between dataset properties and model behavior (e.g., SimpleQuestions benefits from word-level; WebQSP benefits from deeper/residual architectures).  
- Novelty is incremental: uses known techniques (stacked BiLSTMs, residuals) in a strong instantiation rather than introducing fundamentally new methods.  
- Evaluation breadth is limited to SimpleQuestions and WebQSP; additional datasets or detailed zero-shot analyses would strengthen claims.  
- Potential pipeline bias: entity re-ranking may exploit entity–relation co-occurrence from the KB rather than pure question–relation matching.  
- Constraint detection is heuristic (character-overlap, hand-written date/type rules), brittle, underexplained, and beam size=1 may amplify early errors.  
- Reproducibility issues: hyperparameters, candidate sizes, and WebQSP relation detection construction details are incomplete and scattered.  
- Requests clarification on hyperparameters (K, K’, l, α), sensitivity, and how often true entity is lost after re-ranking.  
- Asks for details on WebQSP relation task construction: negative sampling, candidate set sizes, and zero-shot performance.  
- Seeks clarification on residual variants tested, whether layer normalization or highway connections were tried, and why one variant performs best.  
- Wants breakdown of constraint detection contributions (dates, answer-type, mentions) and whether neural mention–entity linking could help.  
- Requests runtime/memory comparisons of HR-BiLSTM vs baselines as relation vocabulary scales.  
- Suggestions to improve organization and clarity (restructure related work/model sections; clarify LSTM initialization for relation sequences).  
- Urges confirmation that baselines used identical candidate sets and entity linking outputs and to report candidate set recall for gold entities/relations.  
- Recommends releasing code and the WebQSP relation detection dataset with scripts to improve impact and reproducibility.
