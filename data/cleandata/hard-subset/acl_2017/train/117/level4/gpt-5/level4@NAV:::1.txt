Review
- Strengths:
The paper tackles a pertinent problem: learning the mapping from natural language to KB relations for QA, under conditions where only partial information is available for one argument and where the space of candidate relations is very large.
The proposed approach introduces a new way to fuse two input text representations: a word-level view (segmenting both relation names and the input text), and a single-token relation view (without segmenting either relation names or the input text).
In the QA pipeline, the primary contribution appears to be the ability to re-rank entities after the Entity Linking stage.
Empirical results show improvements over the state of the art.

- Weaknesses:
The evaluation is conducted on a limited dataset.

- General Discussion:
Section 3.1 would fit better in Related Work, allowing Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3 presenting the method. This reorganization would enable a clearer subdivision of the new Section 3.
