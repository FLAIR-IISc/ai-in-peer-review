- Strengths: The paper addresses a relevant problem: learning the mapping between natural language and KB relations in the QA setting, where only partial information is available for one of the arguments and where there is a very large number of possible target relations. The proposal introduces a new method that combines two representations of the input text: (i) a word-level representation that segments both the target relation names and the input text, and (ii) a representation that treats each relation as a single token, without segmenting either the relation names or the input text. In the QA pipeline, the main contribution appears to be the ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step. The results show improvements over the state of the art.

- Weaknesses: The approach is evaluated on a limited dataset.
