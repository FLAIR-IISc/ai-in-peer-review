Summary
The paper tackles relation detection for knowledge base question answering (KBQA), where a question must be matched to one or more KB relations despite having only partial argument information and a very large relation set. The authors propose a hierarchical recurrent model with residual connections that compares questions to relations at two granularities: (a) relation names treated as single tokens (relation-level) and (b) tokenized relation names (word-level). They integrate this detector into a simple KBQA pipeline that uses relation scores to re-rank entity linker outputs and then select relations (and optional constraints). Experiments on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP show improvements over prior work in both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA accuracy.

Contributions (as I see them)
1) Practical integration of relation detection into KBQA via entity re-ranking: Using relation scores to disambiguate entity linker candidates is, in my view, the most impactful contribution to the QA pipeline.
2) A hierarchical matching model that combines relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning, improving robustness to unseen relations and varying context lengths.
3) Empirical gains over strong baselines on single- and multi-relation benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach.

Strengths
- Relevance and problem setup: The work addresses a central KBQA challenge—mapping natural language to KB relations at scale, often with only one argument present and with many target relations. Framing and evaluation align well with realistic KBQA conditions.
- Modeling at two granularities: Combining relation-level and word-level representations is well-motivated. Word-level tokens help generalize to unseen relations; relation-level tokens capture broader contexts that word overlaps might miss. The hierarchical residual BiLSTM provides a coherent mechanism to exploit both.
- Impact on entity disambiguation: Using relation detection to re-rank entity linker outputs is simple yet effective. This step directly mitigates entity name ambiguities by exploiting entity–relation co-occurrence, reducing error propagation and improving downstream relation selection.
- Strong empirical results: The method outperforms prior systems on relation detection and yields state-of-the-art or competitive end-to-end KBQA accuracy on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP. The ablations support the value of hierarchical matching and residual connections.

Weaknesses
- Evaluation scope: The approach is evaluated on a limited set of datasets. While SimpleQuestions and WebQSP are standard, broader evaluation (e.g., across more domains or KBs) would strengthen claims of generality.

Additional comments
- Organization: Section 3.1 (on relation granularity) would read more naturally in Related Work, since it largely motivates and contrasts representation choices seen in prior literature. Consolidating the proposed model into a single Section 3 (currently 3.2) would improve focus and flow.
