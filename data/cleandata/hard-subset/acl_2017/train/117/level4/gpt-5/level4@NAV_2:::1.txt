- Strengths:
  The paper tackles an important problem: aligning natural-language questions to KB relations for QA, under the constraints that only partial information about one argument is available and the target relation space is very large. The method combines two complementary representations of the inputs: a word-level view (segmenting both relation names and the question) and a relation-as-atom view (treating each relation identifier as a single token without segmentation). In the overall QA pipeline, the most impactful addition appears to be the entity re-ranking step following initial entity linking. The reported results show improvements over prior state-of-the-art baselines.

- Weaknesses:
  The empirical assessment is conducted on a limited set of datasets.

- General Discussion:
  Section 3.1 would fit more naturally within Related Work, allowing Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3 focused on the core proposal. This restructuring would enable a clearer subdivision of the proposed approach within the new Section 3.
