- Strengths: The paper tackles a pertinent problem: learning the mapping from natural language to KB relations for QA, where only partial information is available for one argument and the set of possible target relations is very large. The proposal introduces a new method that combines two representations of the input text: (i) a word-level representation that segments both relation names and the input text, and (ii) a representation that treats each relation as a single token, without segmenting relation names or the input text. In the QA pipeline, the main contribution appears to be the ability to re-rank entities after the Entity Linking step. The reported results show improvements over the state of the art.

- Weaknesses: The evaluation is limited to a small dataset.

- General Discussion: Section 3.1 would fit better under Related Work, allowing Section 3.2 to become Section 3 presenting the proposed method. This restructuring would enable a clearer and more coherent organization of the new Section 3.
