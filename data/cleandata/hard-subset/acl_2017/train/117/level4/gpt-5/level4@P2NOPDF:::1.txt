- Strengths:
The paper tackles a relevant problem: mapping natural language to knowledge base relations for QA when one argument is only partially specified and the set of possible relations is very large. The proposed approach combines two text representations: (1) a word-level representation that segments both relation names and the input text, and (2) a representation that treats each relation as a single token and leaves the text unsegmented. The primary contribution to QA appears to be improved entity reranking after the entity linking step. The results show gains over the state of the art.

- Weaknesses:
The evaluation is conducted on a limited dataset, which restricts the strength and generality of the conclusions.

- General Discussion:
Section 3.1 would fit better within Related Work, allowing the current Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3 devoted to the proposed method. This would enable a clearer and more coherent structure for the methodology section.
