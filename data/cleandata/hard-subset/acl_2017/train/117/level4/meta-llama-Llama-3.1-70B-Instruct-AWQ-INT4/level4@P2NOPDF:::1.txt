**Strengths:**

This paper addresses a relevant and timely topic in the field of question answering (QA): learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations, particularly when only partial information is available for one of the arguments, and when dealing with a large number of possible target relations. The authors propose a novel method that combines two distinct representations of the input text: a word-level representation, which involves segmenting both the target relation names and the input text, and a representation that treats relations as single tokens, without segmentation.

A key contribution of this work lies in its ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step, which is a crucial component of QA systems. The experimental results demonstrate an improvement over the state-of-the-art, highlighting the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

One limitation of this study is that the approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset. This raises concerns about the generalizability of the results and the potential for overfitting to the specific characteristics of the dataset.

**General Discussion:**

The organization of the paper could be improved. Specifically, Section 3.1 appears to be more relevant to the related work section, and its inclusion there would allow for a clearer and more focused presentation of the proposed method in Section 3.2, which could then be renumbered as Section 3. This reorganization would enable a more logical and coherent structure, facilitating the reader's understanding of the paper's contributions.
