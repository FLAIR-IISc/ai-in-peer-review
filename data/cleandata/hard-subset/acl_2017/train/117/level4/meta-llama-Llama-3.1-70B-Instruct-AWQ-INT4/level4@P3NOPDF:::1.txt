This paper addresses a relevant topic in natural language processing: learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations, particularly in the context of question answering (QA) where only partial information is available for one of the arguments, and when dealing with a large number of possible target relations.

The proposed method combines two different representations of the input text: a word-level representation that involves segmenting the target relation names and the input text, and a representation that treats relations as a single token without segmenting relation names or input text. The main contribution of this approach in the QA setting appears to be its ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step.

The results presented in the paper demonstrate an improvement over the state-of-the-art methods, which is a significant achievement.

However, one major weakness of the paper is that the approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset. It would be beneficial to test the proposed method on more diverse and larger datasets to further establish its effectiveness.

In terms of organization, it seems that Section 3.1 would be better suited to the related work section, allowing Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3, which could then be split more effectively to improve the overall flow of the paper.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach to addressing the challenge of learning the mapping between natural language and KB relations, and the results show promise. However, further evaluation on more extensive datasets is necessary to fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
