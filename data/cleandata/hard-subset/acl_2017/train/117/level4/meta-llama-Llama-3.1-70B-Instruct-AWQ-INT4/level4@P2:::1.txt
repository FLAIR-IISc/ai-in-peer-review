**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) tasks. The authors introduce a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning that detects KB relations given an input question. The method uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction. Additionally, the authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance the other.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Improved Relation Detection Model**: The authors propose a novel relation detection model that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations, outperforming previous methods on KB relation detection tasks.
2. **Entity Re-ranking**: The proposed method allows for entity re-ranking after the entity linking step, which is a key contribution to the KBQA end task.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The authors demonstrate that their approach achieves state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

**Strengths**

1. **Relevant Topic**: The paper addresses a relevant topic in the field of NLP, namely learning the mapping between natural language and KB relations in the context of QA.
2. **Novel Method**: The proposal consists of a new method to combine two different representations of the input text, which is a significant contribution to the field.
3. **Improved Results**: The results show an improvement compared to the state of the art, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Evaluation**: The approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of the entire domain.
2. **Section Organization**: Section 3.1 seems to fit better inside the related work section, and section 3.2 could be reorganized to improve the flow of the paper.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend the evaluation to more datasets and domains?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the entity re-ranking step and how it contributes to the KBQA end task?
3. How do the authors plan to address the issue of section organization and improve the flow of the paper?
