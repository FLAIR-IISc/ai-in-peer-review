**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The model uses a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning to detect KB relations given an input question. The approach combines two different representations of the input text: a word-level representation and a relation-level representation. The model is evaluated on the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

**Main Contributions**

1. An improved relation detection model that combines word-level and relation-level representations of the input text.
2. A novel approach to re-rank entities after the Entity Linking step, which contributes to the good performance in multiple ways.
3. State-of-the-art results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets.

**Strengths**

1. The paper addresses a relevant topic in the context of QA, where the number of possible target relations is very large.
2. The proposal combines two different representations of the input text, which leads to improved performance.
3. The approach to re-rank entities after the Entity Linking step is a significant contribution to the field.
4. The results show an improvement compared to the state of the art.

**Weaknesses**

1. The approach has been evaluated on a limited number of datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP).
2. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including error analysis and ablation studies.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend the approach to more datasets and domains?
2. Can you provide more details on the error analysis and ablation studies to better understand the strengths and weaknesses of the approach?
3. How do you think the approach can be improved or modified to handle more complex questions or relations?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written, and the proposal is clearly explained. However, some sections, such as Section 3.1, could be improved by providing more context and explanations. Additionally, the related work section could be more comprehensive, including more recent papers on the topic.
