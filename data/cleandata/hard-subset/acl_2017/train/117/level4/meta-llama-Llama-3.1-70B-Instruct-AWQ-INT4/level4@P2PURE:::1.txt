- Strengths: This paper tackles a relevant problem in the context of Question Answering (QA): learning the mapping between natural language and Knowledge Base (KB) relations, where only partial information is available for one of the arguments, and the number of possible target relations is very large. 
The proposed method combines two different representations of the input text: a word-level representation (which involves segmenting the target relation names and the input text) and a single-token representation of relations (without segmenting relation names or input text). 
A key contribution of this work to QA is the ability to re-rank entities after the Entity Linking step. 
The results demonstrate an improvement over the state-of-the-art.

- Weaknesses:
The approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of more general scenarios.

- General Discussion:
I suggest reorganizing the structure of the paper. Specifically, the content of section 3.1 seems more suitable for the related work section, which would allow section 3.2 to become the new section 3, presenting the proposal. This reorganization would enable a more proper division of the new section 3.
