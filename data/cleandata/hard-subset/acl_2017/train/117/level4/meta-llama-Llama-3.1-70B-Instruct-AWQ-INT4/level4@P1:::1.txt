**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

The paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for knowledge base question answering (KBQA). The main contributions of this work are: (1) a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning that detects KB relations given an input question, and (2) a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy on both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Strengths**

1. The paper addresses a relevant topic in the field of natural language processing, namely learning the mapping between natural language and KB relations in the context of QA.
2. The proposal combines two different representations of the input text, a word-level representation and a relation-level representation, to improve relation detection.
3. The ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step is a significant contribution to the field of KBQA.
4. The results show an improvement compared to the state of the art on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. The approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of the entire KBQA domain.
2. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the proposed model's performance on different types of questions and relations.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the proposed model handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words and relations?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the entity linking step and how it is integrated with the relation detection model?
3. How does the proposed model compare to other state-of-the-art KBQA systems on different datasets?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written, and the proposed model is clearly explained. However, some sections, such as Section 3.1, could be improved by providing more context and background information. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more detailed analysis and discussion of the results.
