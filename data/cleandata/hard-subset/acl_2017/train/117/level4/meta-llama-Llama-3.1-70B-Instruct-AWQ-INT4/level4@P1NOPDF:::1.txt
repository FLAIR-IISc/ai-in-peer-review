- Strengths: This paper tackles a relevant topic in the context of question answering (QA): learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations, particularly when only partial information is available for one of the arguments, and when dealing with a large number of possible target relations.

The proposed method combines two distinct representations of the input text: a word-level representation that segments both the target relation names and the input text, and a representation that treats relations as a single token, without segmentation. Notably, the main contribution of this approach in QA lies in its ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step.

The results demonstrate an improvement over the state of the art, which is a significant achievement.

- Weaknesses:
One major limitation of this study is that it has only been evaluated on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of the complexity and diversity of real-world QA tasks.

- General Discussion:
