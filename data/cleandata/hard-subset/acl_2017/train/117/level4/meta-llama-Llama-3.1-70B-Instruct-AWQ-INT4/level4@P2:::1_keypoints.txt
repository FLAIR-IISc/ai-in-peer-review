- Proposes a hierarchical residual BiLSTM model for relation detection that compares questions and relation names across abstraction hierarchies.
- Integrates entity linking with the relation detector, allowing entity re-ranking to mutually enhance both components.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.
- Contribution: novel hierarchical matching model that outperforms prior relation detection methods.
- Contribution: entity re-ranking mechanism after linking that benefits the KBQA end task.
- Contribution: empirical demonstration of improved performance (SOTA) on evaluated benchmarks.
- Strength: addresses a relevant NLP problem—mapping natural language to KB relations for QA.
- Strength: introduces a novel method combining two representations of input text via residual learning.
- Strength: shows measurable improvement over previous approaches.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets, raising generalization concerns.
- Weakness: section organization (notably Sect. 3.1 and 3.2) hampers paper flow and should be revised.
- Question: How will evaluation be extended to additional datasets and domains?
- Question: Please provide more details on the entity re-ranking step and its contribution to KBQA.
- Question: How do the authors plan to reorganize sections to improve clarity and flow?
