Strengths: This paper tackles a pertinent topic in the realm of Question Answering (QA), specifically focusing on learning the mapping between natural language and Knowledge Base (KB) relations, where only partial information is available for one of the arguments, and a vast number of potential target relations exist.

The proposed method introduces a novel approach to combining two distinct representations of the input text: a word-level representation that incorporates segmentation of both target relation names and input text, and a tokenized representation of relations as a single entity, devoid of segmentation.

Notably, the primary contribution of this work in the QA domain lies in its ability to re-rank entities following the Entity Linking step.

The results demonstrate a notable improvement over the current state of the art.

Weaknesses: A significant limitation of this approach is its evaluation on a restricted dataset.

General Discussion: I suggest reorganizing the structure of the paper by relocating Section 3.1 to the related work section, thereby allowing Section 3.2 to become the new Section 3, which can then be divided more effectively.
