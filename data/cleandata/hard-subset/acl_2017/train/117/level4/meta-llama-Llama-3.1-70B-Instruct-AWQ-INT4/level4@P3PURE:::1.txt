This paper addresses a relevant topic in the realm of natural language processing, specifically learning the mapping between natural language and knowledge base (KB) relations in the context of question answering (QA). This task is particularly challenging when only partial information is available for one of the arguments, and when dealing with a very large number of possible target relations.

The proposed method combines two distinct representations of the input text: a word-level representation, which involves segmenting both the target relation names and the input text, and a representation that treats relations as a single token, without segmenting relation names or input text. A key contribution of this approach in the QA setting is its ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step.

The experimental results demonstrate an improvement over the state of the art, which is a significant achievement.

However, one major weakness of this work is that the approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset. This raises concerns about the generalizability of the proposed method to more diverse and larger-scale datasets.

In terms of organization, it would be beneficial to restructure the paper. Specifically, the content in Section 3.1 seems more suitable for the related work section. By moving this content, Section 3.2 could be repositioned as the new Section 3, which would allow for a clearer presentation of the proposed method. This reorganization would enable a more logical flow of ideas and improve the overall clarity of the paper.
