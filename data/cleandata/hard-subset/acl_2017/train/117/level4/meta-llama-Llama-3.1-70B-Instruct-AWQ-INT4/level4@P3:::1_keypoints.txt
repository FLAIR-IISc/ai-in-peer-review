- Proposes a hierarchical RNN with residual learning for neural relation detection in KBQA, combining word-level and relation-level input representations and reporting SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Contribution: combines word-level and relation-level text representations for improved relation detection.  
- Contribution: introduces a novel entity re-ranking step after Entity Linking that boosts performance.  
- Contribution: achieves state-of-the-art results on single- and multi-relation KBQA for SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Strength: tackles a relevant KBQA challenge where the target relation space is very large.  
- Strength: dual representations (word- and relation-level) demonstrably improve performance.  
- Strength: the entity re-ranking strategy is a meaningful methodological contribution.  
- Strength: empirical results improve over prior work.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to only two datasets (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP).  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis—insufficient error analysis and ablation studies.  
- Question: How will the approach be extended to more datasets and domains?  
- Question: Please provide detailed error analysis and ablation studies to clarify strengths and failure modes.  
- Question: How can the model be improved or adapted to handle more complex questions/relations?  
- Additional comment: The paper is well-written and clear overall.  
- Additional comment: Section 3.1 needs more context/explanation and the related work section should cover more recent literature.
