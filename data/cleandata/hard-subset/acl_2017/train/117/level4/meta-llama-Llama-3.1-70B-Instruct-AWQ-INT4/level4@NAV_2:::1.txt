**Summary of the Paper**
The paper proposes a novel approach for relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) tasks. The authors introduce a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning, which detects KB relations given an input question. The method uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction.

**Main Contributions**
The authors claim the following main contributions:
1. An improved relation detection model by hierarchical matching between questions and relations with residual learning.
2. A simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Contributions as Seen by the Reviewer**
The contributions of this work can be seen as:
1. A novel approach to combine word-level and relation-level representations for relation detection in KBQA tasks.
2. An effective method for re-ranking entities after the entity linking step, which improves the performance of KBQA systems.
3. State-of-the-art results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP benchmarks.

**Strengths**
1. The paper addresses a relevant topic in KBQA, which is learning the mapping between natural language and KB relations.
2. The proposal consists of a new method to combine two different representations of the input text, which shows an improvement compared to the state of the art.
3. Results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach in KBQA tasks.
4. The ability to re-rank entities after the entity linking step is a significant contribution to KBQA.

**Weaknesses**
1. The approach has been evaluated on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of all KBQA tasks.
2. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the proposed method and its components.

**Questions to Authors**
1. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to more complex KBQA tasks, such as those involving multiple relations or entities?
2. Can the authors provide more insights into the effectiveness of the residual learning component in their proposed method?
3. How do the authors plan to address the issue of limited training data for KBQA tasks, which may impact the performance of their approach?
