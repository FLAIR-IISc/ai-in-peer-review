## Review

**Strengths:** This paper tackles an important problem: learning the correspondence between natural language and knowledge base relations, specifically within a question answering framework where argument information is incomplete, and the space of potential relations is vast. The proposed method introduces a novel approach to integrate two distinct textual representations – one at the word level, incorporating segmentation of both relation names and the input text, and another treating relations as single tokens without such segmentation. A key contribution within question answering appears to be the ability to effectively re-rank entities following the entity linking stage. The experimental results demonstrate performance gains over existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses:** The evaluation of this approach is currently limited to a single dataset.

**General Discussion:** I recommend restructuring the paper. Section 3.1 would be more appropriately placed within the related work discussion, allowing Section 3.2 to become the primary presentation of the proposed method (Section 3). This restructuring would facilitate a more logical and detailed breakdown of the core contribution.
