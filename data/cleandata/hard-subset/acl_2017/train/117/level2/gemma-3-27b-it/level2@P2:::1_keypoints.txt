- Addresses relation detection for KBQA, focusing on unseen relations and large relation sets in KBs.  
- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical BiLSTM with residual learning to match questions and relation names at multiple abstraction levels.  
- Integrates the relation detector into a simple KBQA pipeline and reports state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Main contribution: HR-BiLSTM combining word-level and relation-level representations with residual connections to handle large vocabularies and unseen relations.  
- Ablation studies show hierarchical matching and residual learning each substantially improve performance.  
- Demonstrates that improved relation detection meaningfully boosts end-to-end KBQA performance even with a simple pipeline.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results and thorough baseline comparisons on two standard KBQA datasets.  
- Strength: Well-motivated model design addressing KB relation detection challenges.  
- Strength: Comprehensive ablation studies that isolate component contributions.  
- Strength: Practical integration into a KBQA system showing real-world impact.  
- Weakness: Individual components (BiLSTMs, residuals) are not novel; novelty mainly in their combination and application.  
- Weakness: Limited detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity to settings.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis identifying common failure modes or relation/question types that fail.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited by use of a relatively simple KBQA pipeline; unclear performance in more sophisticated systems.  
- Question: Provide details on hyperparameter search space and selection process.  
- Question: Share examples of failure cases and relation/question types the model struggles with.  
- Question: Discuss potential challenges when applying the model to KBs with different size, schema, or data quality.  
- Question: How does performance change when using pre-trained relation embeddings alongside or instead of HR-BiLSTM?  
- Additional: Writing is generally good but could be more concise in places.  
- Additional: Suggest adding limitations and future research directions.  
- Additional: References are comprehensive.
