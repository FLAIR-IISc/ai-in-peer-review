This paper proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning for relation detection in knowledge base question answering (KBQA). The core idea is to leverage both relation-level and word-level representations of relations and to use deep bidirectional LSTMs with residual connections to learn different levels of question representations for matching. A KBQA system integrating entity linking and the proposed relation detector is also presented.

The primary contribution is an improved relation detection model that addresses the challenges of large relation vocabularies and zero-shot learning in KBQA. The proposed hierarchical residual BiLSTM demonstrates performance improvements over baseline methods on both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA tasks. A second contribution is the demonstration of the benefits of integrating relation detection within a larger KBQA pipeline, showing that improved relation detection leads to improved overall QA accuracy. Finally, the comprehensive experimental evaluation on standard datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) provides a strong empirical validation of the proposed approach.

Strengths:
1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results on both the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness. The improvements over baselines are statistically significant and highlight the value of the hierarchical residual BiLSTM architecture.
2. **Addressing Key Challenges in KB Relation Detection:** The paper explicitly acknowledges and addresses the unique challenges of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction. The handling of large relation vocabularies and zero-shot learning scenarios is a significant strength. The use of both relation-level and word-level representations is a practical solution to this.
3. **Well-Designed Architecture:** The hierarchical residual BiLSTM architecture is a thoughtful approach to capturing different levels of abstraction in both questions and relations. The inclusion of residual connections is a valuable addition, making training easier and improving performance. The clear explanation of the rationale behind architectural choices is also commendable.
4. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper presents a thorough experimental evaluation, including ablation studies that clearly demonstrate the contributions of different components of the proposed model. The comparison to multiple baselines and the analysis of results on different datasets provide a strong validation of the approach.

Weaknesses:
1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching, residual learning, and BiLSTMs is effective, the individual components themselves are not entirely novel. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how the proposed approach builds upon existing work and what specifically differentiates it.
2. **Complexity of the System:** The KBQA system, while demonstrating good performance, appears relatively complex. While this is acknowledged in the paper, a simpler end-to-end approach might be more practical and easier to deploy. A more in-depth discussion of the trade-offs between complexity and performance would be helpful.
3. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linking:** The performance of the KBQA system is heavily reliant on the accuracy of the initial entity linking step. The paper acknowledges this, but further analysis of how the system handles errors in entity linking would be beneficial. Investigating the potential for end-to-end training of the entity linking and relation detection components could also be considered.

Questions to Authors:
1. The ablation study reveals that removing residual learning impacts WebQSP more than SimpleQuestions. Could you elaborate on why you believe this is the case, and what properties of WebQSP make it more sensitive to the benefits of residual connections?
2. The paper mentions that the constraint detection step is crucial for performance. Could you provide more details on the implementation of this step and the tuning of the threshold parameter (✓)? Are there any potential issues with this approach, such as overfitting to the training data?
3. Regarding the computational cost of the proposed model, can you provide some information on the training and inference time compared to the baseline methods? This would help to assess the practical feasibility of deploying the model in a real-world KBQA system.

Additional Comments:
- It would be beneficial to include a discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential areas for future research.
- The paper could benefit from more visual illustrations of the hierarchical matching process.
- Consider exploring alternative attention mechanisms to further improve the matching between questions and relations.
