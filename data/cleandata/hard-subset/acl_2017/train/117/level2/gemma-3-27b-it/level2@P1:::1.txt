This paper proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural network with residual learning for improved relation detection in the context of Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors argue that KB relation detection is more challenging than general relation detection due to the large number of relations, zero-shot learning requirements, and the need to predict relation chains. They present a system that utilizes this improved relation detector within a larger KBQA pipeline and demonstrate state-of-the-art results on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) benchmarks.

**Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM for Relation Detection:** The proposed HR-BiLSTM model, leveraging both relation-level and word-level representations combined with residual connections, is the primary contribution. This approach aims to capture both global and local information within relations and improve training stability in deep networks.
2. **KBQA System Integrating Relation Detection:** The paper presents a complete KBQA system that effectively integrates the proposed relation detector with an entity linker, demonstrating its practical application and benefits within a real-world task. The entity re-ranking step is a valuable addition.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The authors achieve state-of-the-art or competitive results on established KBQA benchmarks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The ablation studies are well-conducted and help to understand the contribution of each component.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation & Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, justifying the need for specialized methods.
2. **Novelty of the HR-BiLSTM Architecture:** The combination of hierarchical matching, residual learning, and the consideration of both relation-level and word-level representations is a novel contribution to the field.  The residual connections, in particular, are a strong design choice given the depth of the model.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors evaluate their approach on two standard benchmarks with thorough ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of each component.  The inclusion of both relation detection accuracy and end-to-end KBQA performance is a strong point.
4. **Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to understand the proposed method and its evaluation. The inclusion of Figure 2 is especially helpful for visualizing the model architecture.
5. **Practical System Design:** The KBQA system presented is relatively simple and practical, and the authors clearly explain how the relation detector contributes to the overall pipeline.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** While the ablation studies are helpful, a deeper analysis of the types of errors made by the system would be valuable.  Understanding *why* the system fails on certain questions could lead to further improvements. Specifically, are there certain relation types that are consistently misclassified?
2. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:** The performance of the KBQA system is dependent on the quality of the entity linker. While the authors acknowledge this, a more detailed discussion of the impact of linker errors would be beneficial.  Could the system be made more robust to errors in entity linking?
3. **Hyperparameter Tuning Details:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but provides limited detail about the specific ranges and search strategies used. More information in this area would enhance reproducibility.
4. **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** While the comparison to existing baselines is fair given the published literature, it would be beneficial to compare against more recently published and potentially stronger baselines, if available.  The field moves quickly, and a more up-to-date comparison would strengthen the claims.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the HR-BiLSTM model? Specifically, are there certain relations or question structures that consistently lead to misclassifications?
2. How sensitive is the performance of the KBQA system to the quality of the entity linker? Have you experimented with different entity linkers or considered methods to mitigate the impact of linker errors?
3. Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and the optimization strategy used?
4. Have you considered incorporating attention mechanisms, perhaps in place of the residual connections, to further improve the model's ability to focus on relevant parts of the question and relation representations?



**Additional Comments:**

* The reference list is comprehensive.
* The notation is generally clear and consistent.
* Minor: In the introduction, the phrase "original relation names can sometimes help" is a bit vague. Could this be rephrased for clarity?
* Minor: Table 2 could benefit from indicating statistical significance (e.g., with p-values) for the performance improvements.
