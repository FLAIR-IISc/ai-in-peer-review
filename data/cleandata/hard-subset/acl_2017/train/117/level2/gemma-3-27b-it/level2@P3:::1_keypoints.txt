1. Proposes a hierarchical residual BiLSTM that uses relation-level and word-level relation representations and deep bidirectional LSTMs with residual connections for KBQA relation detection, integrated into a KBQA pipeline.  
2. Primary contribution: an improved relation detector addressing large relation vocabularies and zero-shot scenarios, with demonstrated performance gains.  
3. Secondary contribution: shows that better relation detection improves end-to-end KBQA accuracy when integrated with entity linking.  
4. Strong empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art performance on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with statistically significant improvements.  
5. Addresses key KB relation detection challenges (large vocabularies, zero-shot) via combined relation- and word-level representations.  
6. Well-designed architecture: hierarchical matching and residual connections effectively capture multiple abstraction levels and ease training.  
7. Comprehensive evaluation: thorough experiments and ablation studies validate component contributions and compare to multiple baselines.  
8. Limited novelty: core components (hierarchical matching, residual learning, BiLSTMs) are largely incremental over existing work.  
9. System complexity: the integrated KBQA system is relatively complex, raising deployment and practicality concerns.  
10. Dependence on entity linking: performance relies heavily on initial entity linking accuracy, with limited analysis of error handling or end-to-end training.  
11. Question: Why does removing residual learning impact WebQSP more than SimpleQuestions—what dataset properties cause this sensitivity?

13. Question: Provide training and inference time comparisons versus baselines to assess practical deployment feasibility.
