This paper proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural network with residual learning for improved relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The core idea is to leverage both relation-level and word-level representations of relations and use deep bidirectional LSTMs with residual connections to capture different levels of abstraction for better matching with the question. The authors also present a simple KBQA system integrating entity linking and the proposed relation detector, demonstrating state-of-the-art results on both single and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

The primary contribution lies in the improved relation detection model itself. The hierarchical approach, combined with residual learning, demonstrably outperforms baseline models, particularly on the more challenging WebQSP dataset. The ablation studies convincingly show the importance of both the hierarchical structure and the residual connections. A secondary contribution is the end-to-end KBQA system, which validates the effectiveness of the relation detector within a practical application, achieving competitive performance.

Strengths include the clear motivation for addressing the challenges specific to KB relation detection (large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning), a well-designed model incorporating established techniques (BiLSTMs, residual learning) in a novel way, and a thorough experimental evaluation with ablation studies. The results on both relation detection and the KBQA task are compelling and demonstrate significant improvements. The inclusion of both single and multi-relation benchmarks adds to the robustness of the evaluation.

However, several weaknesses exist. The paper lacks a detailed error analysis. Understanding *where* the model still struggles would be valuable. The discussion of the constraint detection step is relatively brief and doesn't fully explain its contribution. While the authors mention comparisons to existing entity linkers, a more direct comparison of the end-to-end system against state-of-the-art KBQA systems (beyond just reporting accuracy) would be stronger. The impact of the pre-trained word embeddings isn’t explored beyond stating their use.

Questions to Authors:

1. Could you provide a more detailed error analysis of the relation detection model? What types of relations or questions are most often misclassified?
2.  What is the relative contribution of the entity re-ranking step vs. the relation detection step to the overall KBQA performance?
3. How sensitive is the performance of the model to different choices of pre-trained word embeddings?
