- Proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural network with residual learning for improved relation detection in KBQA.  
- Leverages both relation-level and word-level relation representations and deep BiLSTMs with residual connections to capture multiple abstraction levels.  
- Integrates the relation detector into a simple KBQA pipeline with entity linking, reporting state-of-the-art results on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.  
- Primary contribution: the improved hierarchical relation detection model that outperforms baselines, especially on WebQSP.  
- Ablation studies show the hierarchical structure and residual connections are important to performance.  
- Secondary contribution: an end-to-end KBQA system that validates the detector in practice and yields competitive QA performance.  
- Strength: clear motivation addressing large relation vocabularies and zero-shot issues, and a novel combination of BiLSTMs and residual learning.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across relation detection and KBQA tasks, including single- and multi-relation benchmarks.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed error analysis to identify remaining failure modes.  
- Weakness: brief discussion of the constraint detection step and unclear its contribution.  
- Weakness: end-to-end comparisons to state-of-the-art KBQA systems are limited (beyond reporting accuracy).  
- Weakness: sensitivity to choice of pre-trained word embeddings is not explored.  
- Question: provide a detailed error analysis—what relations/questions are most misclassified?  
- Question: what is the relative contribution of entity re-ranking vs. relation detection to overall KBQA performance?  
- Question: how sensitive is performance to different pre-trained word embeddings?
