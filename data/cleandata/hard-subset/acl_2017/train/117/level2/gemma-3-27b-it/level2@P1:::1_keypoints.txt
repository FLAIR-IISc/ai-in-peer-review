- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM combining relation-level and word-level representations for relation detection.
- Integrates the relation detector into a complete KBQA pipeline with entity linking and re-ranking.
- Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with thorough ablations.
- Clearly motivates why KB relation detection is harder than general relation extraction.
- Novel architecture combining hierarchical matching and residual connections to capture global/local relation information.
- Comprehensive evaluation on two benchmarks, reporting both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA performance.
- Well-structured and clearly written paper with helpful model visualization (Figure 2).
- Presents a simple, practical KBQA system design where the relation detector meaningfully improves pipeline performance.
- Lacks deep analysis of error cases and which relation types or question structures fail.
- System performance depends on external entity linkers; impact of linker errors is underexplored.
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning ranges and search/optimization strategy for reproducibility.
- Would benefit from comparison to more recent or stronger baselines given rapid field progress.
- Requests detailed error analysis to identify consistently misclassified relations or question patterns.
- Asks about sensitivity to entity linker quality and experiments or mitigations for linker errors.
- Seeks more details on hyperparameter search space and optimization methodology.
- Suggests exploring attention mechanisms as an alternative or complement to residual connections.
- Notes the reference list is comprehensive and notation is clear.
- Minor suggestions: clarify the phrase “original relation names can sometimes help” and add statistical significance indicators (e.g., p-values) in Table 2.
