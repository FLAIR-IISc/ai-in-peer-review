- Summary: Proposes a hierarchical residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) for relation detection in KBQA, comparing questions and relation names across abstraction levels and integrating with entity linking in a simple KBQA system that achieves strong results.

- Contribution 1: Introduces HR-BiLSTM for relation detection, claiming improved performance over prior methods.

- Contribution 2: Presents a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking with the relation detector to achieve state-of-the-art single- and multi-relation QA accuracy.

- Contribution 3: Proposes hierarchical matching that combines word-level and relation-level representations for final relation ranking.

- Strength 1: HR-BiLSTM yields outstanding relation detection performance, outperforming previous methods.

- Strength 2: The integrated KBQA system achieves state-of-the-art results on both single- and multi-relation benchmarks.

- Strength 3: The KBQA system is simple and efficient, facilitating easier deployment.

- Weakness 1: Limited analysis of model components, including the specific impact of residual learning and hierarchical matching.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient comparison with other state-of-the-art KBQA and relation detection models.

- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed error analysis to reveal model and system failure modes.

- Question 1: Request for details on how residual learning affects HR-BiLSTM training and relation detection performance.

- Question 2: How will the system be extended to handle complex tasks like multi-hop reasoning and entity disambiguation?

- Question 3: Ask for more implementation and performance details on the entity linking and constraint detection components.
