- Proposes HR-BiLSTM matching questions to relations at word- and relation-levels with residual connections to stabilize training and encourage complementary abstractions.  
- Integrates the model into a simple KBQA pipeline with entity re-ranking via relation evidence and optional constraint detection, yielding strong relation detection and end-to-end QA results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Main contribution: practical relation detection combining word-level and relation-level representations with hierarchical residual matching, showing consistent gains and informative ablations.  
- Main contribution: a simple KBQA pipeline that uses relation detection to re-rank entity candidates, improving robustness to entity ambiguity and boosting end-to-end performance.

- Strength: Convincing motivation and empirical support that combining relation words and relation tokens captures complementary local and global cues, helping robustness and unseen relations.  
- Strength: Residual connections across BiLSTM layers effectively address optimization difficulties in hierarchical matching and outperform weighted-sum/attention baselines.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results with thorough ablations, significant improvements over strong baselines (AMPCNN, BiCNN), and statistical significance reported.  
- Strength: Practical impact from entity re-ranking using relation evidence; ablation shows this step substantially contributes to end-to-end gains.  
- Strength: Clear, intentionally simple pipeline that isolates contributions and aids reproducibility and extension.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — core ideas (stacked BiLSTMs, residuals, multi-granularity inputs) are incremental; novelty is mainly their combination and application.  
- Weakness: Insufficient zero-shot/generalization analysis; lacks breakdown of seen vs unseen relation performance and relation-name vocabulary coverage effects.  
- Weakness: Missing/unclear reproducibility details: ranking loss formulation (margin), negative sampling, batch size, regularization, optimizer, early stopping.  
- Weakness: Missing KBQA hyperparameter reporting and sensitivity analyses for K, K’, l, α, λ, θ.  
- Weakness: WebQSP relation detection dataset slated for release but currently unavailable, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Baseline coverage incomplete — lacks comparisons to CFO and pre-trained relation-embedding methods (e.g., TransE), which are relevant for large relation sets and zero-shot behavior.  
- Weakness: Constraint detection uses heuristics and special rules; contribution and fairness vs competitors unclear without detailed ablations of constraint components.
