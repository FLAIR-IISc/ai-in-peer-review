- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches questions to relations at word- and relation-level tokens with residual connections to ease training.
- Integrates HR-BiLSTM into a simple KBQA pipeline with (1) entity re-ranking over candidate-entity relations and (2) relation/core-chain detection; optional constraint detection for multi-entity constraints.
- Reports improved relation detection (SQ 93.3%, WQSP 82.53%) and competitive/state-of-the-art end-to-end QA; ablations show benefits of hierarchy and residuals.

- Strength: Well-motivated combination of relation word-level and ID-level representations with ablations showing substantial gains over using either alone.
- Strength: Hierarchical residual matching outperforms weighted-sum and attention variants, suggesting residuals help learn complementary abstractions.
- Strength: Plugging HR-BiLSTM into a simple pipeline yields 2–3% absolute KBQA improvements without heavy joint inference.
- Strength: Comprehensive ablations and comparisons to several strong baselines; deeper LSTMs shown to matter for chain questions.
- Strength: Entity re-ranking step effectively mitigates entity linker ambiguity by using question-relation signals.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—residuals in sequence models and multi-granularity relation signals are incremental relative to prior work.
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps and missing experimental details (margin, α, λ, l, K, K’, appendix constraint details, WebQSP detection dataset not included).
- Weakness: Evaluation scope/fairness concerns—WebQSP relation detection restricts candidates to gold topic-entity relations and end-to-end uses single top-1 chain, which may inflate scores or propagate errors.
- Weakness: Missing analyses such as seen vs unseen relations split, performance by chain length, detailed error types, and effect of initializing relation-level LSTMs from word-level states.
- Weakness: Baseline coverage limited — lacks comparisons to CFO, neural symbolic machines, and stronger attention-based sequence-matching baselines.

- Q: Please report SQ performance split by seen vs unseen relations and ablate relation-level tokens on the zero-shot subset.
- Q: How does HR-BiLSTM performance vary with WebQSP chain length; does residual gain correlate with longer chains?
- Q: What are the exact hyperparameter values (margin, α, λ, l, K, K’) and sensitivity analyses?
- Q: Which residual variant worked best (hidden-state vs max-pooling shortcut); were highway connections or layer norm tried to separate training vs representational benefits?
- Q: What are training/inference times, memory footprints, and per-question relation candidate set sizes compared to AMPCNN/BiCNN?
- Q: Quantify the standalone benefit of the constraint detection step on WQSP (precision/recall) and report common failure modes (e.g., date normalization).

- Suggestion: Release the WebQSP relation detection dataset and all code/scripts to ensure reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Compare to CFO or use TransE-initialized relation embeddings to test complementarity with KB-structural priors.
- Suggestion: Ablate initialization of relation-level encoders from word-level states and include a variant without that back-off.
- Suggestion: Report calibration/score distributions for the two-step detection and sensitivity to entity linker quality.
- Suggestion: Extend attention baselines to stronger architectures (multi-head/cross-attention) to strengthen the residual-vs-attention claim.
- Suggestion: Add qualitative examples showing when relation-level tokens disambiguate word-level matches and vice versa, including multi-constraint cases.
