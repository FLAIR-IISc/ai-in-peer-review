Summary & contributions:
- Proposes HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches questions to relations at relation- and word-level tokens with residual connections for effective hierarchical matching.
- Integrates the detector into a simple two-step KBQA pipeline (relation-based entity re-ranking + constraint detection) and achieves SOTA on SimpleQuestions and competitive results on WebQSP.
- Presents comprehensive ablations showing multi-granularity representations and residual learning outperform weighted-sum and attention baselines.

Strengths:
- Significant empirical gains: 2–3% improvement on relation detection and KBQA accuracy over strong baselines (statistically significant).
- Careful ablations isolate the contributions of relation names/words, residuals, depth, and architecture choices.
- Practical KBQA impact: entity re-ranking with relation scores effectively reduces entity ambiguity without heavy engineering.
- Attention to zero-shot/open-set issues by factorizing relation names and initializing relation encoders with word-level states.

Weaknesses:
- Incremental novelty: residual connections and multi-granularity matching are adaptations of existing ideas rather than a major conceptual leap.
- Incomplete analysis: key hyperparameters, candidate sizes, runtime/memory, and per-seen/unseen zero-shot breakdowns are not fully reported; error analysis is limited.
- Reliance on external components: results depend on entity linker quality and the constraint detector uses heuristic string overlap, limiting generalization.
- Limited direct comparisons to recent end-to-end or neural programmer models under identical setups.

Questions to authors:
- Please provide exact values and sensitivity for K, K′, l, α, λ, candidate pool sizes, runtime, and memory footprint.
- Can you report separate accuracies for seen vs unseen relations and sensitivity to entity linker recall?
- How are relation names tokenized (camelCase/underscores handling) and are relation chains limited in length?
- Will you release code and the WebQSP relation detection set to ensure reproducibility?
