1. Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM combining relation- and word-level representations for KB relation detection, integrated into a simple KBQA system with state-of-the-art results.  
2. Clearly identifies KBQA-specific challenges (large relation vocabulary, zero-shot learning, multi-relation chains) and motivates a specialized model.  
3. Hierarchical residual learning is validated via ablations showing both hierarchical matching and residual connections are essential, yielding sizable gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
4. Integration into KBQA with entity re-ranking is well-executed and shows practical utility, improving KBQA accuracy by ~2–3%.  
5. Thorough experimental validation across relation detection and KBQA end tasks, with dataset-specific analysis (SimpleQuestions favors relation-level; WebQSP favors hierarchical matching).  
6. Comparison to recent relation-detection methods is limited, weakening novelty claims relative to approaches using pre-trained relation embeddings or advanced attention.  
7. Lacks deeper analysis of why residual connections outperform other hierarchical methods or insights into the learned representations.  
8. Evaluation uses a relatively simple KBQA system, so it's unclear if gains hold in more complex, state-of-the-art KBQA architectures.  
9. Missing analysis of failure cases and error patterns to illuminate limitations and guide future work.  
10. Question: How does the approach compare to methods using pre-trained relation embeddings (e.g., TransE) and could they be combined?  
11. Question: In which scenarios (e.g., ambiguous mentions) is the entity re-ranking step most beneficial?  
12. Question: What is the computational cost of HR-BiLSTM versus simpler baselines and how does it scale with larger relation vocabularies?
