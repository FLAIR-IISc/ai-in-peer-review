Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM for KBQA relation detection using relation- and word-level representations with hierarchical residual matching, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP and improving KBQA.

Main contributions:
- Novel hierarchical residual BiLSTM combining relation-level and word-level representations for KB relation detection.
- Empirical evidence that improved relation detection directly enhances KBQA on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.
- Clear articulation of KB-specific relation detection challenges that motivate the technical approach.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated focus on KBQA-specific challenges (large relation vocabulary, zero-shot, relation chains) missing in prior work.
- Hierarchical matching with residual learning is justified and empirically validated; ablations show each component adds 2–3% gains.
- Demonstrates end-to-end value by integrating relation detection into KBQA and using entity re-ranking to mitigate entity linking ambiguity.
- Thorough experimental evaluation with careful baselines, ablations, and tests on two diverse benchmarks.
- Clear, well-organized writing that makes technical contributions and results easy to follow.

Weaknesses:
- Unclear how much KBQA improvement is due to relation detection versus the entity re-ranking mechanism—needs targeted ablation.
- Computational complexity (training/inference cost) of the hierarchical residual BiLSTM is not discussed.
- "State-of-the-art" claim lacks comparison to some relevant prior work (e.g., neural symbolic machines); comparisons are incomplete.
- Explanation for why residual learning benefits hierarchical matching is superficial; deeper analysis is needed.
- No discussion of failure cases or limitations (e.g., handling negation, sarcasm, ambiguous language).

Questions to authors:
- Provide more details on the entity re-ranking step and quantify how much improvement is attributable to relation detection versus re-ranking.
- Explain how hierarchical matching addresses unseen relations (zero-shot), especially in datasets like SimpleQuestions.
- Clarify comparisons on WebQSP: how does your method compare to a fair baseline using a single relation detector versus multi-detector systems like STAGG?
