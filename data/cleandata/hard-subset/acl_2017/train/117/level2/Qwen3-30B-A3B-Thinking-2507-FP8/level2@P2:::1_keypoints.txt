- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical word- and relation-level matching for KBQA relation detection to handle large vocabularies, zero-shot cases, and relation chains.  
- Introduces residual learning to stabilize training and enable deeper hierarchical matching.  
- Presents a simple two-step KBQA system (entity re-ranking + relation detection) that reportedly achieves state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Well-motivated distinction between general relation extraction and KBQA-specific challenges, justifying the hierarchical approach.  
- Strong empirical gains (≈2–3% improvement) over baselines with ablation studies showing hierarchical matching and residuals are essential.  
- Simple yet effective KBQA pipeline where entity re-ranking via relation detection reduces entity ambiguity without complex joint inference.  
- Claims zero-shot capability but provides no isolated evaluation on unseen relations, weakening the empirical claim.  
- Comparisons omit some more recent or relevant baselines for WebQSP, undermining the state-of-the-art claim.  
- No analysis of failure cases or scalability (e.g., complex syntax, very large KBs), limiting assessment of real-world applicability.  
- Request: provide zero-shot-specific metrics (accuracy on unseen relations).  
- Request: clarify the design and appropriateness of the WebQSP relation-detection setup and how it differs from prior work.  
- Request: explain why constraint detection is crucial for their system while prior work (e.g., STAGG) treated it as optional.
