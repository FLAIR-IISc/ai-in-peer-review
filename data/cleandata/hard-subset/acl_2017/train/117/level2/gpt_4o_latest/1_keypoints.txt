- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical matching model with residual learning for relation detection in KBQA, integrated into a simple KBQA pipeline.  
- Contribution: combines relation-level and word-level representations for improved relation detection.  
- Contribution: introduces residual learning to enable deeper hierarchical sequence matching and abstract question representations.  
- Contribution: integrates relation detection into a KBQA pipeline with entity re-ranking and constraint detection, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Strength: novel hierarchical matching and well-motivated use of residual learning.  
- Strength: strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements and validating ablations.  
- Strength: practical utility shown via KBQA pipeline and impactful entity re-ranking for entity linking ambiguities.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation including baselines, ablations, and end-to-end KBQA tests.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations such as scalability to much larger KBs and reliance on high-quality entity linking.  
- Weakness: unclear generality beyond KBQA (e.g., open-domain relation extraction).  
- Weakness: added model complexity and missing analysis of computational cost vs. performance trade-offs.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns due to lack of publicly released code or pre-trained models.  
- Questions: how does HR-BiLSTM scale to KBs with millions of relations and what are computational bottlenecks?  
- Questions: can the hierarchical matching generalize to other NLP tasks like open-domain relation extraction or semantic similarity?  
- Questions: how sensitive is performance to the initial entity linking quality?  
- Conclusion: valuable and recommend acceptance, subject to addressing scalability, generality, and reproducibility concerns.
