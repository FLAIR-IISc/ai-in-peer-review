This paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) tasks. The main contributions of this work include a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning that detects KB relations given an input question, and a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to achieve state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

Strengths:
1. The proposed Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model for relation detection is well-motivated and effectively addresses the challenges of KB relation detection, such as dealing with unseen relations and large numbers of relation types.
2. The use of residual learning and hierarchical matching between questions and relations is a novel and effective approach that improves the performance of the relation detection model.
3. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model on two KBQA tasks, SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, and demonstrate that their approach achieves state-of-the-art results.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future work.
2. Some of the related work is not thoroughly discussed, and the authors could provide more context and comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.
3. The implementation details of the HR-BiLSTM model, such as the hyperparameter tuning process, could be more clearly explained.

Questions to Authors:
1. Can you provide more details on how the HR-BiLSTM model handles out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words and entities in the input questions and relations?
2. How does the proposed approach perform on more complex KBQA tasks, such as those involving multiple hops or more abstract relations?
3. Can you provide more insights into the importance of the residual learning component in the HR-BiLSTM model, and how it contributes to the overall performance of the relation detection model?

Overall, this paper presents a well-motivated and effective approach to KB relation detection, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model on two KBQA tasks. With some minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of KBQA.
