Strengths:
- HR-BiLSTM is well-motivated and effectively handles unseen relations and large relation sets.
- Combining residual learning with hierarchical question–relation matching is novel and improves detection performance.
- Thorough evaluation on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP shows state-of-the-art results.

Weaknesses:
- Lacks detailed analysis of the model’s limitations and possible future work.
- Related work coverage is incomplete and needs stronger comparison to other SOTA methods.
- Implementation details (e.g., hyperparameter tuning) are insufficiently explained.

Questions:
- How does HR-BiLSTM handle out-of-vocabulary words and entities in questions and relations?
- How does the approach perform on more complex KBQA tasks (multi-hop or abstract relations)?
- What is the specific contribution of the residual learning component to overall performance?

Overall:
- A well-motivated, effective approach with strong empirical results; minor revisions could strengthen its contribution.
