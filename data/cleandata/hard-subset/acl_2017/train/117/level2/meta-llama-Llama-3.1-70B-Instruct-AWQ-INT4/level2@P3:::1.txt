**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) tasks. The model, called Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM), uses a hierarchical matching approach to compare questions and relation names at different levels of abstraction. The model is enhanced by residual learning, which helps to improve the training process and results in more abstract question representations. The paper also proposes a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and relation detection to enable one to enhance the other. Experimental results show that the proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

**Main Contributions**

1. An improved relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations.
2. A simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and relation detection to enable one to enhance the other.
3. Experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

**Strengths**

1. The proposed HR-BiLSTM model is able to effectively capture the hierarchical structure of relation names and questions, leading to improved relation detection performance.
2. The use of residual learning helps to improve the training process and results in more abstract question representations.
3. The proposed KBQA system is able to effectively integrate entity linking and relation detection, leading to improved overall performance.
4. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the hierarchical matching approach on the performance of the model.
2. The paper does not provide a comparison of the proposed HR-BiLSTM model with other state-of-the-art relation detection models.
3. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the residual learning on the performance of the model.
4. The paper does not provide a detailed description of the KBQA system and how it integrates entity linking and relation detection.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of the hierarchical matching approach on the performance of the model?
2. How does the proposed HR-BiLSTM model compare to other state-of-the-art relation detection models?
3. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of the residual learning on the performance of the model?
4. Can you provide a more detailed description of the KBQA system and how it integrates entity linking and relation detection?

**Additional Comments**

The paper proposes a novel approach to relation detection for KBQA tasks and demonstrates its effectiveness on both single-relation and multi-relation tasks. However, there are some areas that could be improved, such as providing a more detailed analysis of the impact of the hierarchical matching approach and the residual learning on the performance of the model. Additionally, a more detailed description of the KBQA system and how it integrates entity linking and relation detection would be helpful.
