- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical residual BiLSTM for relation detection in KBQA, plus a simple KBQA system integrating entity linking and relation detection; reports state-of-the-art results on single- and multi-relation tasks.

- Contribution: HR-BiLSTM that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations.

- Contribution: A simple KBQA pipeline that jointly leverages entity linking and relation detection to improve each other.

- Contribution: Empirical results showing effectiveness on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA benchmarks.

- Strength: HR-BiLSTM captures hierarchical structure of relation names and questions, improving relation detection.

- Strength: Residual learning aids training and produces more abstract question representations.

- Strength: The integrated KBQA system effectively combines entity linking and relation detection, boosting overall performance.

- Strength: Experimental results support the proposed approach across single- and multi-relation tasks.

- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of how hierarchical matching affects model performance.

- Weakness: No direct comparison with other state-of-the-art relation detection models.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of the specific impact of residual learning on results.

- Weakness: Missing detailed description of the KBQA system architecture and the entity-linking/relations integration.

- Question: Please provide a more detailed analysis of the hierarchical matching impact on performance.

- Question: How does HR-BiLSTM compare quantitatively and qualitatively to other state-of-the-art relation detectors?

- Question: Can you analyze the contribution of residual learning to training stability and final performance?

- Question: Please give a more detailed description of the KBQA system and how entity linking and relation detection are integrated.

- Additional comment: The approach is novel and promising but would benefit from deeper ablation studies and clearer system documentation.
