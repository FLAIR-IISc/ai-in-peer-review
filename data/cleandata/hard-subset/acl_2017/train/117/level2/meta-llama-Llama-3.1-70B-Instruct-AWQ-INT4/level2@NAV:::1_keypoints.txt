- Proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural relation detector with residual learning for KBQA.
- Presents a simple KBQA pipeline that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to mutually enhance each other.
- Claims state-of-the-art relation detection and KBQA accuracy on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.
- Main contribution 1: hierarchical question-relation matching augmented by residual connections.
- Main contribution 2: an easy-to-build KBQA system that avoids joint inference or feature-based re-ranking.
- Strength: achieves strong empirical results on both single- and multi-relation tasks.
- Strength: hierarchical matching plus residual learning demonstrably improves relation detection performance.
- Strength: the KBQA system is simple to implement and does not require complex inference steps.
- Strength: thorough analysis and ablation studies support the effectiveness of the approach.
- Weakness: relies on an external entity linker without detailed analysis of its role or reliability.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other KBQA systems using different relation detection models.
- Weakness: does not discuss limitations of the proposed method or outline future work.
- Question: how sensitive is overall KBQA performance to choice or quality of the entity linker?
- Question: can the authors provide more detailed comparisons with other KBQA systems and relation detectors?
- Question: what are the method’s limitations and promising directions for future improvement?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written but would benefit from more detail on entity linking and comparative evaluations.
