**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The model uses a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning to detect KB relations given an input question. The authors also propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance another. Experimental results show that the approach achieves outstanding relation detection performance and helps the KBQA system to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Main Contributions**

1. An improved relation detection model by hierarchical matching between questions and relations with residual learning.
2. A simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance another.

**Strengths**

1. The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.
2. The use of hierarchical matching and residual learning improves the performance of the relation detection model.
3. The simple KBQA system is easy to build and does not require joint-inference or feature-based re-ranking steps.
4. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the results and ablation tests to demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the entity linker is already available and does not provide a detailed analysis of the entity linking step.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other KBQA systems that use different relation detection models.
3. The paper does not discuss the limitations of the proposed approach and potential future work.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the entity linker affect the performance of the KBQA system, and what are the implications of using a different entity linker?
2. Can the authors provide a more detailed comparison with other KBQA systems that use different relation detection models?
3. What are the potential limitations of the proposed approach, and what are the potential future directions for improving the KBQA system?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the results. However, the authors could provide more details on the entity linking step and its impact on the KBQA system. Additionally, a more detailed comparison with other KBQA systems would be helpful in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.
