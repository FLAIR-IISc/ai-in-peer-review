**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel KB relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations. The model uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction. The authors also propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance another. Experimental results show that the approach achieves outstanding relation detection performance and helps the KBQA system to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Main Contributions**

1. An improved relation detection model by hierarchical matching between questions and relations with residual learning.
2. A simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance another.
3. Experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on both relation detection and KBQA tasks.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel and effective approach to KB relation detection, which is a key component of KBQA systems.
2. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing relation detection methods and demonstrate how their approach addresses these limitations.
3. The experimental results are strong, showing significant improvements over state-of-the-art baselines on both relation detection and KBQA tasks.
4. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and its components.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the entity linker is accurate, which may not always be the case in practice. It would be beneficial to evaluate the robustness of the proposed approach to entity linking errors.
2. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach, which is important for large-scale KBQA applications.
3. The authors do not discuss the potential limitations of their approach, such as its ability to handle complex questions or its robustness to out-of-vocabulary words.
4. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work, including a comparison of the proposed approach to other state-of-the-art methods.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the proposed approach handle complex questions that require multiple relations to be detected?
2. Can you provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed approach and how it compares to other state-of-the-art methods?
3. How does the proposed approach handle out-of-vocabulary words or entities that are not present in the training data?
4. Can you provide more details on the entity linker used in the experiments and how its accuracy affects the performance of the proposed approach?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed approach. The experimental results are strong, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of existing relation detection methods. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work and a comparison of the proposed approach to other state-of-the-art methods. Additionally, the authors should consider evaluating the robustness of the proposed approach to entity linking errors and providing more details on the computational complexity of the approach.
