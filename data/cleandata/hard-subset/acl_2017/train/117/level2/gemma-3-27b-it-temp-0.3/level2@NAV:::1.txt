This paper proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural network with residual learning to improve relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The core idea is to leverage both relation-level and word-level representations of relations, and to use deep bidirectional LSTMs with residual connections to better capture different levels of abstraction in the question and relation representations. The authors also present a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector, demonstrating state-of-the-art results on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) benchmarks.

The primary contribution of this work is the HR-BiLSTM model for relation detection. The hierarchical matching approach, combined with residual learning, demonstrably improves performance on both relation detection tasks and the overall KBQA system. A secondary contribution is the demonstration of the effectiveness of jointly optimizing relation detection and entity linking within a KBQA pipeline, showing that improved relation detection directly translates to better QA accuracy. Finally, the comprehensive experimental evaluation, including ablation studies, provides valuable insights into the importance of different components of the proposed model.

Strengths include the clear motivation for addressing the challenges of KB relation detection (large relation sets, zero-shot learning), the well-designed HR-BiLSTM architecture, and the strong empirical results. The ablation studies are particularly helpful in understanding the contribution of each component. The inclusion of both single and multi-relation QA benchmarks strengthens the validity of the findings. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and experimental setup.

Weaknesses include a limited discussion of the computational cost of the proposed model. While the performance improvements are significant, the added complexity of the HR-BiLSTM compared to simpler baselines is not fully addressed. Additionally, the paper relies heavily on existing entity linking results, and the performance of the overall system is thus somewhat dependent on the quality of the linker. The analysis of constraint detection could be more in-depth, explaining why it is crucial for their system.

Questions to Authors:

1. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost (training time, inference time, memory usage) of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to the baseline models?
2. How sensitive is the performance of your KBQA system to the quality of the entity linker? Have you experimented with different entity linkers?
3. Could you elaborate on the specific challenges and potential improvements for the constraint detection module?
