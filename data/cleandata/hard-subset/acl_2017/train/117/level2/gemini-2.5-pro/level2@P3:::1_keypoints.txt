- Novel HR-BiLSTM model that hierarchically matches questions to relations using word-level and relation-name-level representations with residual BiLSTM layers.  
- KBQA pipeline that uses the relation detector for entity re-ranking and final relation selection, improving end-to-end performance.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, with comprehensive ablation studies validating design choices.  
- Well-motivated architecture addressing the trade-off between generalization (word-level) and specificity (relation-level), with effective residual matching.  
- Consistent performance gains across single-relation and multi-relation benchmarks, demonstrating robustness and generality.  
- Thorough experimental analysis showing benefits of hierarchical views, residual learning, and BiLSTMs over alternatives.  
- Practical two-step system design where relation-based entity re-ranking notably reduces entity linking errors.  
- Unclear which residual-connection implementation (sum before pool vs. sum after pool) was used for final reported results, hurting reproducibility.  
- Constraint detection module is under-described despite its importance for WebQSP performance; formal matching-score details are missing.  
- Potential scalability concerns: entity re-ranking may be expensive when entities have hundreds of relations; runtime/complexity discussion is absent.  
- Question: which residual matching variant was used for the reported experiments and which worked best?  
- Question: what was the average number of relations scored per question in WebQSP and the runtime impact of the re-ranking step?
