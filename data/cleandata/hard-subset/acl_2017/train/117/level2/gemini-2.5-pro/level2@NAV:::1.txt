**Summary and Contributions**
This paper presents a novel neural model, Hierarchical Residual Bidirectional LSTM (HR-BiLSTM), for relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The model's key idea is to perform hierarchical matching, where a multi-layer question encoder with residual connections learns representations at different levels of abstraction, which are then matched against relation representations of varying granularity (word-level and relation-level). The authors integrate this improved detector into a simple yet effective KBQA pipeline that introduces a novel entity re-ranking step, using the relation detector to disambiguate entity candidates.

The main contributions of this work are:
1.  A novel and well-motivated HR-BiLSTM architecture that effectively combines hierarchical matching with residual learning for the task of relation detection.
2.  A practical KBQA system design featuring an entity re-ranking step that demonstrates how an improved relation detector can mitigate errors from an upstream entity linker.
3.  Strong empirical validation, achieving state-of-the-art results on both the relation detection subtask and the end-to-end KBQA task for both the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP benchmarks.

**Strengths**
1.  **Novel and Sound Architecture:** The HR-BiLSTM model is technically sound and well-motivated. The approach of matching multi-granularity relation representations with multi-level question abstractions directly addresses the challenges of generalization and context in KB relation detection. The application of residual connections to facilitate this hierarchical matching is a clever and effective design choice.
2.  **Excellent Experimental Results and Analysis:** The paper presents compelling empirical evidence of the model's effectiveness, outperforming strong baselines and achieving new state-of-the-art results on two standard datasets. The ablation studies are thorough and clearly demonstrate the individual contributions of the hierarchical representation and the residual learning component.
3.  **Effective System-Level Insight:** The proposed KBQA pipeline, particularly the entity re-ranking step, is a significant strength. It showcases a practical method for improving overall system performance by allowing components to mutually enhance one another, which is a valuable contribution to the engineering of multi-component NLP systems.

**Weaknesses**
1.  **Limited Qualitative Analysis:** While the ablation study quantitatively demonstrates the benefit of residual learning, the paper lacks a qualitative analysis of what the different layers of the model are learning. Providing examples of how the first and second layers of the BiLSTM capture different semantic information would offer deeper insight and strengthen the paper's claims about learning abstract representations.
2.  **Dependence on External Components:** The KBQA system's performance is heavily dependent on the quality of the initial entity linker. The paper does not analyze how the system's performance, especially the entity re-ranking step, degrades as the quality of the initial entity linker decreases (e.g., if the correct entity is not in the top-K candidates).
3.  **Lack of Hyperparameter Sensitivity Analysis:** The KBQA pipeline introduces several key hyperparameters for combining scores and re-ranking (K, K0, α, β). The paper provides little discussion on the sensitivity of the final results to these parameters, which would be important for reproducibility and understanding the system's robustness.
