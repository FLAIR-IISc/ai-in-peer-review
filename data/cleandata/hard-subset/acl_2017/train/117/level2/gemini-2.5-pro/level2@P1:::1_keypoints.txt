- Paper tackles relation detection for KBQA, proposing HR-BiLSTM that uses word- and relation-level representations with residual deep BiLSTMs; integrates into a two-step KBQA pipeline and reports SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Contribution: novel HR-BiLSTM architecture combining multi-granularity relation views with hierarchical residual question encoding, yielding strong relation detection performance.  
- Contribution: a simple KBQA pipeline that uses relation detection to re-rank entity candidates and identify relations, addressing entity ambiguity.  
- Contribution: state-of-the-art empirical results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, supported by thorough ablation studies.  
- Strength: well-motivated model design — multi-granularity relation views and residual connections are justified and effective, especially on WebQSP.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and systematic ablation analyses that convincingly show component contributions.  
- Strength: effective system design — entity re-ranking via relation scores is an elegant solution to entity linking ambiguity.  
- Strength: clear writing and reproducibility focus, including intent to release a WebQSP relation detection dataset.  
- Weakness: unclear computational feasibility — paper lacks detail on how candidate relations are generated/pruned during entity re-ranking for large KBs.  
- Weakness: overly simple KBQA pipeline may make some comparisons to prior work (e.g., STAGG) not directly comparable; constraint detection is simple text matching and may lack robustness.  
- Weakness: missing details about the ensemble model (which three detectors and how outputs were combined), hurting reproducibility.  
- Question: what is the typical number of relations scored per question in entity re-ranking, and are there filtering/pruning mechanisms used?  
- Question: does the strong result with a simpler pipeline imply relation detection was the primary bottleneck in prior systems, allowing simpler downstream components?  
- Question: which three relation detector models comprised the ensemble in Table 3 and how were their scores combined?
