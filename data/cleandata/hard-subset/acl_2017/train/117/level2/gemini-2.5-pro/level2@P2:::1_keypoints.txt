- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical matching of word-level and relation-name representations with residual BiLSTM for relation detection in KBQA.  
- Presents a KBQA pipeline using the relation detector to re-rank entity candidates then predict final relations.  
- Strong empirical results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with thorough ablation studies showing component contributions.  
- Well-motivated model addressing relation-name sparsity by combining global relation names and constituent words; residual connections ease deep multi-level matching.  
- Rigorous evaluation: state-of-the-art performance, comparisons to strong baselines, and ablations confirming hierarchical + residual benefits and LSTMs’ advantage over CNNs.  
- Effective system integration: relation-based entity re-ranking substantially improves entity linking and overall KBQA performance.  
- Underspecified implementation details: unclear which residual connection variant was used and insufficient description of the constraint detection module.  
- Potential scalability concerns: entity re-ranking scores all relations for top-K entities, which may be costly for high-degree entities or large K.  
- Lacks qualitative error analysis: no discussion of common failure modes or when re-ranking misfires.  
- Question: which residual method (sum of hidden states vs. sum of pooled outputs) was used and how did they compare?  
- Question: what is the runtime cost of entity re-ranking, and how does accuracy trade off with different values of K?
