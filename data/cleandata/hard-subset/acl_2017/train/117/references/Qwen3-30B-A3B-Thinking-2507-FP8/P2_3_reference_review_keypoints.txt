- Problem: KBQA relation detection differs from general relation extraction due to thousands of relations, frequent zero-shot (unseen) relations, and the need to predict relation chains.  
- Contribution: Proposes Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) for hierarchical matching between questions and relations at word and relation levels.  
- Contribution: Presents a KBQA pipeline that integrates the improved relation detector with an entity re-ranking step to handle entity linking ambiguities.  
- Contribution: Reports state-of-the-art results on relation detection and end-to-end KBQA tasks.  
- Strength: Well-motivated problem with clear justification and empirical evidence (e.g., 14% unseen relations in SimpleQuestions).  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with significant improvements and ablation studies highlighting hierarchical matching and residual learning.  
- Strength: Practical integration into a KBQA system; entity re-ranking is an effective, real-world contribution.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized writing and illustrative figures that explain the approach and experiments.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison to recent KBQA and transformer-based systems (e.g., Wang et al., 2020), leaving the state-of-the-art context incomplete.  
- Weakness: No failure analysis or detailed discussion of limitations, especially for complex or zero-shot cases.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity and inference time are not reported, which matters for real-world deployment.  
- Question: How does the model handle relation names that don't split into meaningful words (non-English names, special chars, acronyms like "P1234" or "has_2015_sales")?  
- Question: Why does residual learning help more on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions, and will this generalize to other datasets?  
- Question: What is the inference time of HR-BiLSTM versus baselines, and how does it affect overall KBQA latency?  
- Overall: Strong, well-executed paper that addresses a meaningful gap and merits acceptance with minor revisions to cover computational complexity and broader comparisons.
