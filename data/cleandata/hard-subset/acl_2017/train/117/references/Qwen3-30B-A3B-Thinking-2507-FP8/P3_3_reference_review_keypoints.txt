- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM for hierarchical relation detection in KBQA and shows SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP using a simple KBQA pipeline.

- Contribution 1: Hierarchical matching approach that handles large relation vocabularies, zero-shot relations, and relation chains in KBQA.

- Contribution 2: Analysis showing complementary value of different relation representation granularities for matching questions.

- Contribution 3: Simple two-step KBQA pipeline using relation detection for entity re-ranking to improve end-to-end QA.

- Strength 1: Strong problem formulation with clear evidence of KBQA-specific challenges (e.g., 14% unseen relations).

- Strength 2: Well-justified HR-BiLSTM design with ablation studies demonstrating benefits of hierarchical matching, residual learning, and dual representations.

- Strength 3: Strong empirical results — 2–3% improvement on relation detection and SOTA end-to-end KBQA performance (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP).

- Strength 4: Useful and novel entity re-ranking component that reduces entity ambiguity via relation detection.

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty — adapts existing hierarchical matching and residual learning techniques rather than introducing fundamentally new methods.

- Weakness 2: Lacks analysis of computational cost, inference time, and scalability for the two-step detection pipeline.

- Weakness 3: WebQSP relation-task setup is artificial (using all relations connected to the topic entity) and may not reflect full semantic parsing complexity.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient failure analysis — no detailed examination of errors on ambiguous questions or similar relations.

- Weakness 5: KBQA pipeline contribution may be overstated since improvements largely stem from the relation detector, not novel pipeline components.

- Question 1: How does HR-BiLSTM's computational complexity and inference time compare to simpler baselines in real-world KBQA settings?

- Question 2: Why were all relations connected to the topic entity chosen as negatives for WebQSP, and how does this differ from the full task's challenges?

- Question 3: Why does residual learning help more on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions, and how do dataset characteristics explain this?

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed paper with valuable KBQA-focused relation detection contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational analysis and clarify pipeline vs. model gains.
