- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM that combines word- and relation-level representations with deep BiLSTMs and residual learning for KBQA relation detection, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Contribution 1: Introduces HR-BiLSTM with hierarchical residual matching to combine word-level and relation-level relation representations.

- Contribution 2: Empirically demonstrates that improved relation detection yields significant KBQA gains, outperforming prior work by ~2–3%.

- Contribution 3: Presents a simple KBQA pipeline with an entity re-ranking step that leverages the improved relation detector.

- Strength 1: Properly frames KBQA relation detection as distinct from general relation extraction (many relations, zero-shot, relation chains).

- Strength 2: Model is well-motivated and thoroughly evaluated with convincing ablation studies and statistically significant improvements.

- Strength 3: Shows end-to-end KBQA improvements without requiring complex joint inference or heavy re-ranking features.

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty—hierarchical matching and residual learning are extensions of existing ideas.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient analysis of why residual learning yields larger gains on multi-relation WebQSP than on single-relation SimpleQuestions.

- Weakness 3: Does not address computational complexity or training/inference cost and scalability concerns.

- Question 1: Please analyze why residual learning helps more on WebQSP — is it due to handling longer relation chains?

- Question 2: Provide quantitative impact of the entity re-ranking step on top-1 entity linking accuracy.

- Question 3: Report training and inference time comparisons to simpler baselines (e.g., single-layer BiLSTM, BiCNN) to assess practical deployment.

- Recommendation: Accept — strong empirical contribution and clear end-to-end impact on KBQA despite limited architectural novelty.
