- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical matching (word- and relation-level) plus residual learning for KBQA relation detection to handle many relations, zero-shot cases, and relation chains.  
- Demonstrates improved end-to-end KBQA performance using a two-step entity re-ranking then relation detection pipeline, achieving claimed state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Strength: Clearly identifies KBQA-specific challenges (large relation vocab, zero-shot, chain relations) motivating the approach.  
- Strength: Provides comprehensive evaluation and ablation studies showing each component’s contribution with statistically significant gains.  
- Strength: Shows practical KBQA benefits—relation detection improvements translate to 2–3% end-task gains without complex KBQA system design.  
- Weakness: Core techniques (hierarchical matching, residual learning) lack novelty and are similar to existing multi-level attention and residual methods.  
- Weakness: No clear explanation for dataset-specific effects—residual learning helps WebQSP more than SimpleQuestions without analysis.  
- Weakness: Missing failure/error analysis—no discussion of where or why the model fails (ambiguous entities, multi-hop reasoning, etc.).  
- Weakness: Limited comparisons to recent state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., transformer-based or advanced attention models), weakening the SOTA claim.  
- Weakness: KBQA system novelty overstated—improvements seem driven by relation detector rather than system architecture.  
- Question: Why does residual learning benefit WebQSP more—what dataset characteristics cause this and how can it be leveraged?  
- Question: How does the model compare to more recent relation detection methods (e.g., transformer-based approaches)?  
- Question: How does the hierarchical matching handle zero-shot/unseen relations and what evidence supports its effectiveness for those cases?  
- Question: What are the computational costs and scalability of HR-BiLSTM with large relation vocabularies for training and inference?  
- Comment: Paper is well-written and experimentally solid but should better situate techniques relative to prior NLP methods and temper novelty claims.  
- Comment: Recommend revising the title to reflect specific contribution (e.g., “Hierarchical Residual Learning for KBQA Relation Detection”) and adding deeper error and comparative analyses.
