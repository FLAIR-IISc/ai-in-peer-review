- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM for KBQA relation detection using hierarchical word- and relation-level matching with residual learning to handle large relation sets, zero-shot relations, and relation chains.  
- Contribution 1: Novel HR-BiLSTM combining hierarchical matching and residual learning tailored for KBQA relation detection.  
- Contribution 2: Simple KBQA pipeline integrating the model achieves state-of-the-art results without joint inference or feature-based re-ranking.  
- Contribution 3: Comprehensive empirical validation across datasets and ablation studies demonstrating component contributions.  
- Strength 1: Clear problem motivation differentiating KBQA relation detection from general relation extraction.  
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results with 2–3% absolute gains on relation detection and KBQA end tasks.  
- Strength 3: Practical simplicity—achieves high performance without complex pipeline components.  
- Strength 4: Well-presented method with clear figures and algorithmic description.  
- Strength 5: Convincing ablation studies showing necessity of hierarchical matching, residual learning, and relation-level representations.  
- Weakness 1: Core techniques (BiLSTMs, residuals, hierarchical matching) are well-known, limiting novelty of individual components.  
- Weakness 2: Missing comparisons to recent transformer-based models (e.g., BERT), weakening state-of-the-art claims.  
- Weakness 3: Insufficient discussion of limitations: scalability to millions of relations, computational costs, failure cases, and handling very long/complex chains.  
- Weakness 4: Unclear which model aspects are specific to KBQA vs. generally applicable to relation extraction.  
- Weakness 5: No error analysis provided to explain model failures.  
- Question 1: Why does residual learning have less impact on SimpleQuestions than WebQSP—what dataset differences explain this?  
- Question 2: How does the relation detection model enable state-of-the-art performance without joint inference or re-ranking used in prior work?  
- Question 3: Can you provide concrete examples where relation-level representation succeeds but word-level matching fails?  
- Question 4: How does entity re-ranking performance scale when initial entity linking accuracy is much lower (e.g., <60%)?  
- Question 5: What are the training and inference time costs and scalability of HR-BiLSTM compared to baselines?  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—solid, well-motivated empirical contribution but should add comparisons to recent models and discuss limitations.
