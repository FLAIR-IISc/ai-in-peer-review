1. Paper addresses KBQA-specific relation detection challenges: large relation vocabularies, zero-shot relations, and relation chains.  
2. Proposes Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) for hierarchical question-relation matching with residual learning.  
3. Shows improved KBQA performance by integrating better relation detection into a simple KBQA system.  
4. Clearly articulates the distinctions between general relation detection and KBQA relation detection.  
5. Main contribution: HR-BiLSTM model for hierarchical relation matching.  
6. Contribution: Demonstration that relation-detection improvements yield better KBQA end-task performance.  
7. Contribution: Clear identification and explanation of KBQA-specific challenges.  
8. Strength: Strong motivation and problem articulation with practical examples.  
9. Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with ablation studies showing 2–3% absolute gains.  
10. Strength: Practical significance—simple KBQA system achieves state-of-the-art without complex components.  
11. Strength: Clear technical explanation and rationale for residual learning in hierarchical matching.  
12. Strength: Strong baseline comparisons showing consistent improvements over prior methods.  
13. Weakness: Limited comparison to the most recent (2018–2019) state-of-the-art methods.  
14. Weakness: No failure case analysis to illustrate model limitations.  
15. Weakness: Ambiguity in entity re-ranking hyperparameter α and lack of sensitivity analysis.  
16. Weakness: Limited discussion of computational cost and deployment implications.  
17. Weakness: Insufficient theoretical justification for why residual learning helps this hierarchical matching.  
18. Question: How does HR-BiLSTM compare to very recent KBQA methods (e.g., Chen et al., 2019)?  
19. Question: Can you provide concrete failure examples and explanations?  
20. Question: How was the entity re-ranking weight α chosen and how sensitive are results to it?  
21. Question: Please clarify implementation details of constraint detection and the "maximum overlapping sequence of characters" matching.  
22. Question: How does the model's computational cost compare to baselines and is the gain worth added resources?  
23. Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance—strong contribution, convincing results, with minor revisions to address comparisons and analyses.
