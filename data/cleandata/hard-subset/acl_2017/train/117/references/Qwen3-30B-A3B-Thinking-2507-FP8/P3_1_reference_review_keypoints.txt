- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM for KB relation detection with hierarchical matching and residual learning, plus a two-step KBQA pipeline with entity re-ranking, achieving state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Contribution 1: Novel hierarchical residual matching combining word- and relation-level representations to improve KB relation detection training and accuracy.  
- Contribution 2: Thorough ablation studies demonstrating the impact of hierarchical matching and residual learning across datasets.  
- Contribution 3: Practical KBQA pipeline integration with an entity re-ranking step that reduces entity linking ambiguities.  
- Strength 1: Clear problem framing that distinguishes KB relation detection from general relation extraction with concrete data and examples.  
- Strength 2: Thoughtful model design with strong empirical validation and justification for architectural choices.  
- Strength 3: Significant empirical improvements (2–3% absolute) over prior methods on relation detection and KBQA end tasks.  
- Strength 4: Demonstrates practical integration into KBQA and shows entity re-ranking materially improves end-task performance.  
- Strength 5: Insightful dataset-specific analysis explaining differing effects of residual learning on SimpleQuestions vs. WebQSP.  
- Weakness 1: Core methodology relies on standard components (BiLSTMs, residuals, hierarchical matching) with limited argued novelty.  
- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison to recent pretrained language model baselines (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa), weakening the state-of-the-art claim.  
- Weakness 3: No error analysis or discussion of limitations and failure modes for real-world applicability.  
- Weakness 4: Insufficient justification that two-step pipeline/entity re-ranking is better than using a stronger entity linker.  
- Weakness 5: No discussion of computational complexity, training/inference cost relative to baselines.  
- Question 1: Can you provide concrete evidence (e.g., attention/hidden-state differences) that residual learning helps more on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions?  
- Question 2: How does HR-BiLSTM compare with BERT-based relation extractors, and would hierarchical residual matching help on pretrained encoders?  
- Question 3: Have you compared your pipeline to using a state-of-the-art entity linker (e.g., BERT-based) followed by your relation detector?  
- Question 4: Can you give concrete examples where entity re-ranking resolves ambiguity and improves KBQA output?  
- Question 5: Provide statistics on truly unseen relations in test sets and how well the model handles zero-shot cases.  
- Additional comment 1: Related work should better cover recent pretrained-language-model-based relation extraction.  
- Additional comment 2: Clarify why the KBQA implementation is considered “simple” versus prior systems.  
- Additional comment 3: Explain why avoiding joint inference or feature-based re-ranking is an advantage and how accuracy is maintained.  
- Additional comment 4: Quantify relation-set scale comparisons (e.g., Freebase vs. ACE2005) to strengthen motivation.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept — valuable empirical contribution with thorough analysis but needs comparisons to pretrained-LM baselines and more discussion of limitations.
