

# Peer Review: Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering

## Summary
This paper addresses a critical gap in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) systems: the specific challenges of relation detection in KBQA compared to general relation extraction tasks. The authors propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that performs hierarchical matching between questions and relations at different granularities (word-level and relation-level) using residual learning. They demonstrate that this improved relation detection enables a simple KBQA pipeline to achieve state-of-the-art results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets.

## Main Contributions
1. A well-motivated hierarchical matching approach for KBQA relation detection that effectively handles the challenges of large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and relation chains.
2. An insightful analysis of different relation representation granularities and how they complement each other in matching to question contexts.
3. A simple yet effective two-step KBQA pipeline that leverages relation detection for entity re-ranking, addressing a key bottleneck in KBQA systems.

## Strengths

1. **Strong problem formulation**: The paper makes an excellent case for the specific challenges of KBQA relation detection compared to general relation extraction. The authors clearly articulate the three key differences (large relation vocabulary, zero-shot requirements, and relation chains) with concrete evidence (e.g., 14% of test relations in SimpleQuestions are unseen in training). This is a significant contribution to understanding the problem space.

2. **Well-justified technical approach**: The HR-BiLSTM model is well-motivated by the analysis of different relation representation granularities. The ablation studies (Table 2) provide compelling evidence for the value of each component (hierarchical matching, residual learning, and both relation representations). The analysis of why naive hierarchical matching fails (Section 3.2) is particularly strong.

3. **Strong empirical results**: The model achieves state-of-the-art results on both relation detection tasks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) and enables a simple KBQA pipeline to achieve SOTA performance without complex re-ranking modules. The 2-3% improvement over the best baselines on relation detection is significant (p<0.001 for SimpleQuestions).

4. **Insightful entity re-ranking component**: The entity re-ranking step (Section 4.1) is a novel and valuable contribution that directly addresses a key bottleneck in KBQA systems. By leveraging relation detection to resolve entity ambiguities, the authors provide a simple yet effective solution that improves overall system performance.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited novelty**: While the application is well-executed, the core components (hierarchical matching and residual learning) are adaptations of existing techniques (Parikh et al., 2016; He et al., 2016) rather than fundamentally new methods. The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of how their hierarchical matching approach differs from existing hierarchical methods in NLP. The novelty is primarily in the application to KBQA rather than the method itself.

2. **Lack of computational analysis**: The paper doesn't address the computational cost of the HR-BiLSTM model. Given that the two-step relation detection process (entity re-ranking followed by relation detection) could be computationally expensive, the authors should provide analysis of inference time and scalability, especially for large knowledge bases.

3. **Artificial relation detection task setup for WebQSP**: The authors create a relation detection task from WebQSP by selecting all relations connecting to the topic entity, but this doesn't fully capture the complexity of the original WebQSP semantic parsing task. The paper doesn't adequately justify this simplification or discuss how it might affect the generalizability of their results.

4. **Insufficient failure analysis**: While the paper shows strong results on standard benchmarks, it lacks analysis of failure cases. How does the model perform on highly ambiguous questions or relations with similar word sequences? This would provide valuable insights into the limitations of the approach.

5. **Overstated contribution of the KBQA pipeline**: The paper claims that the improved relation detector "enables our simple KBQA system to achieve state-of-the-art results," but the KBQA pipeline largely uses the same entity linking results as previous work (Yin et al., 2016 for SimpleQuestions, Yih et al., 2016 for WebQSP). The improvement is primarily due to the relation detection model rather than the pipeline design.

## Questions to Authors

1. How does the computational complexity of your HR-BiLSTM model compare to previous relation detection models (e.g., BiLSTM with words)? What are the practical implications for real-world KBQA systems?

2. In creating the WebQSP relation detection task, why did you select all relations connecting to the topic entity as negative examples? How does this differ from the actual challenge of relation detection in the full WebQSP semantic parsing task?

3. The paper shows that residual learning helps more on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions. Could you provide more analysis on why this difference occurs, and how it relates to the characteristics of the two datasets?

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the KBQA field by addressing a specific problem (KB-specific relation detection) with a well-executed solution. The authors provide a clear analysis of the problem space, a well-motivated technical approach, and strong empirical results. While the novelty of the core method is somewhat limited (as it combines existing techniques), the application to KBQA is insightful and effective.

The main weaknesses are the limited novelty of the core method, lack of computational analysis, and the artificial relation detection task setup for WebQSP. However, these do not detract significantly from the paper's overall contribution, which is to demonstrate a practical and effective solution to an important problem in KBQA.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the computational cost analysis and clarify the relationship between the relation detection improvement and the KBQA pipeline performance.
