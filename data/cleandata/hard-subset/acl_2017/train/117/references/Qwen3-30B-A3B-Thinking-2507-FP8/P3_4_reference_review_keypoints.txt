- Addresses gap in KBQA relation detection and proposes HR-BiLSTM for hierarchical word- and relation-level matching.  
- Contributions: hierarchical residual matching, two-step KBQA pipeline (entity re-ranking + relation detection), SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Clearly motivates why KBQA relation detection differs from general relation extraction (large vocab, zero-shot, relation chains).  
- Hierarchical matching combines complementary local (word) and global (relation) representations, with ablations showing both are necessary.  
- Simple two-step pipeline with entity re-ranking effectively reduces entity ambiguity and boosts end-task performance.  
- Thorough experiments and analyses, including ablation studies and dataset-specific behavior of residual learning.  
- Limited comparison with recent relation detection methods (e.g., attention-based approaches like Zhang et al. 2021, Wang et al. 2022).  
- Insufficient empirical evaluation of zero-shot learning; no held-out-relation experiments to prove unseen-relation claims.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for using residual connections in hierarchical matching beyond heuristic claims.  
- Missing qualitative failure analysis to illustrate where the model still struggles (complex chains, ambiguous entities).  
- Question: How does the model handle relations with very few training examples (<5 instances)?  
- Question: Is there risk of error propagation between entity linking, re-ranking, and relation detection, and how robust is the pipeline?  
- Question: Why does residual learning help more on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions—does it relate to relation-chain distribution?  
- Overall: Strong, well-motivated contribution to KBQA relation detection with solid empirical gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (better comparisons and zero-shot evaluation).
