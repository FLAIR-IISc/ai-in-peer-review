- Proposal: HR-BiLSTM model combining relation-level and word-level relation representations with deep BiLSTMs and residual connections for hierarchical matching in KBQA.  
- Problem focus: Targets KBQA-specific challenges—thousands of relations, zero-shot requirements, and relation chains.  
- Pipeline: Integrates relation detection into a KBQA pipeline with an entity re-ranking step, yielding notable end-to-end gains.  
- Empirics: Strong results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with 2–3% absolute relation detection improvements and comparable/better KBQA performance.  
- Ablations: Comprehensive studies isolating effects of hierarchical matching, residual learning, and representation types.  
- Strength: Clearly motivates why general relation extraction methods fail for KBQA and frames the research gap.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation and practical, easily implementable approach.  
- Weakness: Core technical ideas (hierarchical matching, residual connections) are incremental and not highly novel.  
- Weakness: No rigorous/strict zero-shot evaluation to substantiate zero-shot claims (no test on exclusively unseen relations).  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis explaining why the model works—lacks example-level diagnostics and interpretable insights.  
- Question: How does the model perform under a strict zero-shot split compared to baselines?  
- Question: Can authors provide concrete examples where relation-level representation outperforms word-level representation?  
- Question: Why is constraint detection more important for this system than reported in prior work (e.g., Yih et al., 2015)?  
- Question: Would domain-specific embeddings (trained on KBQA data) further improve performance?  
- Overall verdict: Solid, practical contribution with strong empirical gains but limited novelty and insufficient zero-shot validation—Recommendation: Weak Accept.
