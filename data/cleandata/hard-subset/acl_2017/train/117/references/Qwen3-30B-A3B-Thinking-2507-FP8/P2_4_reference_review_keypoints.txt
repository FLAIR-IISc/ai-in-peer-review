- Paper targets KBQA relation detection and identifies three challenges: very large relation sets, zero-shot learning, and relation chains.  
- Proposes a hierarchical residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) that combines relation-level and word-level representations for hierarchical question–relation matching.  
- Builds a simple KBQA system using improved relation detection plus entity re-ranking, achieving SOTA on single- and multi-relation tasks (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP).  
- Strength: Strong problem justification and clear gap identification specific to KBQA relation detection.  
- Strength: Well-motivated technical approach with thorough ablation studies showing each component’s contribution.  
- Strength: Significant practical impact with 2–3% absolute gains and an effective entity re-ranking step.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—uses established components (BiLSTMs, residual learning) applied to KBQA.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to some recent SOTA works (e.g., Liang et al., 2016; Bao et al., 2016).  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of failure cases and discussion of computational scalability as relation vocabulary grows.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on clear contribution, strong evaluation, and practical value.  
- Question: How does the approach scale computationally as relation vocabulary grows beyond ~6,000; any time-complexity measurements?  
- Question: Please provide details on failure cases—what question types are most challenging and why?  
- Question: Explain why entity re-ranking is particularly important compared to approaches without it.  
- Final assessment: Well-executed, clearly written paper with meaningful KBQA contributions that merit acceptance.
