- Summary: Paper targets KBQA relation detection's unique challenges (large relation sets, zero-shot relations, relation chains) and proposes a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) plus a two-step KBQA system with entity re-ranking.

- Contribution 1: Proposes a hierarchical residual matching approach that matches questions and relations at word and relation levels to handle large-scale and unseen relations.

- Contribution 2: Shows improved relation detection yields state-of-the-art KBQA end-task performance using a simple two-step pipeline.

- Contribution 3: Provides thorough ablation studies validating the importance of hierarchical matching and residual learning.

- Strength 1: Clearly identifies the gap between general relation extraction and KBQA-specific relation detection and motivates the problem well.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical gains (2–3% absolute) and SOTA results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with compelling ablation evidence.

- Strength 3: Principled justification for the model design, connecting to residual networks and training insights for deep architectures.

- Strength 4: Practical impact via a simple, effective two-step KBQA system with entity re-ranking that reduces entity linking ambiguity.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient evidence for zero-shot performance — no metrics reported specifically on the 14% unseen relations in SimpleQuestions.

- Weakness 2: Limited comparison with the most recent related work; some cited baselines (e.g., Liang et al., 2016) are not included in results.

- Weakness 3: Superficial treatment of relation chains — unclear how the model handles multiple or competing relation-chain paths.

- Weakness 4: No analysis of computational cost or training/inference time compared to simpler baselines.

- Question 1: Please provide performance metrics on the 14% unseen SimpleQuestions relations compared to baselines.

- Question 2: Why does WebQSP benefit more from residual learning than SimpleQuestions, given WebQSP’s smaller relation set?

- Question 3: How does the model choose between multiple valid relation chains in the KB for a single question?

- Question 4: Provide training and inference time comparisons between HR-BiLSTM and a simpler BiLSTM baseline.

- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address zero-shot evaluation, chain handling, recent baselines, and computational-cost analysis.
