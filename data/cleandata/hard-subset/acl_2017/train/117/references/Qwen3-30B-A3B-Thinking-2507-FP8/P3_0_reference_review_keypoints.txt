- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM that combines relation-level and word-level representations with hierarchical residual matching to improve relation detection and KBQA performance.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a hierarchical residual matching approach combining relation-level and word-level representations for KB relation detection.

- Contribution 2: Presents a two-step KBQA pipeline that uses improved relation detection for entity re-ranking.

- Contribution 3: Provides comprehensive empirical validation with ablations showing 2–3% improvements on relation detection and KBQA across two datasets.

- Strength 1: Strong motivation and clear problem formulation highlighting differences between KB relation detection and general relation extraction.

- Strength 2: Novel, well-justified technical approach with insightful analysis of relation- vs. word-level representations and use of residual connections.

- Strength 3: Convincing empirical results and thorough ablation studies demonstrating consistent gains and statistical significance.

- Strength 4: Practical impact demonstrated by improving end-to-end KBQA via entity re-ranking, bridging detection and application.

- Weakness 1: Core components are established (BiLSTMs, residuals); novelty is primarily in their combination rather than a fundamentally new architecture.

- Weakness 2: Lacks qualitative failure-case analysis to illuminate model and system limitations.

- Weakness 3: Missing comparisons with some recent advances (e.g., GNN-based KBQA methods), limiting contextual positioning.

- Weakness 4: No analysis of computational complexity, model size, or inference speed vs. baselines.

- Weakness 5: Insufficient explanation for why residual learning helps more on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions.

- Question 1: Please provide quantitative evidence comparing the percentage of unseen relations in SimpleQuestions vs. WebQSP test sets.

- Question 2: Clarify how your constraint detection differs from prior joint-inference or feature-based re-ranking methods.

- Question 3: Provide concrete numbers showing how much smaller the entity and relation candidate sets are after constraint detection and the efficiency impact.

- Question 4: Explain why residual learning yields dataset-specific effects—e.g., relation chain complexity in WebQSP.

- Question 5: How sensitive is the system to entity linker quality; can you show results using different entity linkers?

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated paper with effective methods and solid empirical gains; recommend accept with minor revisions.
