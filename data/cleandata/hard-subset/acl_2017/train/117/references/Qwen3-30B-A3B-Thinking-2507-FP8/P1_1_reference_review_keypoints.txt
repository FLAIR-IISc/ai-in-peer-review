1. Paper proposes HR-BiLSTM for KBQA relation detection addressing large relation vocabularies, zero-shot relations, and relation chains.  
2. HR-BiLSTM combines relation-level and word-level representations with hierarchical matching and residual learning.  
3. Authors present a simple two-step KBQA system with entity re-ranking and improved relation detection, achieving SOTA results.  

4. Significant practical contribution: consistent 2–3% absolute improvements on relation detection and KBQA end tasks over prior SOTA.  
5. Strong empirical validation: comprehensive experiments and ablation studies showing importance of hierarchical matching and residual learning.  
6. Clear problem formulation: well-justified differences between KBQA relation detection and general relation extraction, with dataset examples.  
7. Novelty: applying hierarchical matching + residual learning to combine relation- and word-level representations in KBQA is innovative.  
8. Clarity and organization: paper is well-written, figures clarify architecture, and the two-step approach is easy to implement.  

9. Weakness: lacks theoretical justification for why hierarchical matching with residuals is particularly effective.  
10. Weakness: limited comparison to recent related work (e.g., GNN-based relation extraction like Zhang et al., 2020).  
11. Weakness: computational complexity and runtime/scalability (training/inference cost, scaling with K) are not analyzed.  
12. Weakness: unclear generalizability to other KBQA datasets and longer relation chains (length >2 or 3).  
13. Weakness: potential data leakage in the constructed WebQSP relation detection task is not clearly addressed.  

14. Question: How does the model handle zero-shot (unseen) relations during testing?  
15. Question: How was the entity re-ranking parameter l chosen—tuned on dev set or fixed?  
16. Question: Why do LSTMs outperform CNNs in hierarchical matching for WebQSP, contrary to some relation extraction results?  
17. Question: Please detail the "special rules" for date/answer type constraints used in constraint detection.  
18. Question: Can you provide error analysis/examples showing where the model succeeds vs. prior methods (esp. zero-shot and complex chains)?  

19. Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical justification, comparisons to recent work, and computational analysis.
