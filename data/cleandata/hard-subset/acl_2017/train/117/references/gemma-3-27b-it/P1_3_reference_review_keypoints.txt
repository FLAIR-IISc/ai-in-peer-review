1. Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM combining word- and relation-level representations with residual learning to improve relation detection for KBQA, showing gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

2. Contribution: Introduces an effective hierarchical relation representation that leverages multiple granularities of relation information.

3. Contribution: Demonstrates that improved relation detection yields better end-to-end KBQA performance.

4. Contribution: Provides comprehensive experiments, including ablation studies and comparisons to strong baselines.

5. Strength: Strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements on both relation detection and KBQA.

6. Strength: Well-motivated approach addressing KB-specific challenges like scale and zero-shot relations.

7. Strength: Thorough ablation studies that isolate the impact of residual connections, hierarchical matching, and granularity choices.

8. Strength: Presents a simple, practical KBQA system that clearly showcases the model’s applicability.

9. Strength: The paper is clear and well-written with informative figures and tables.

10. Weakness: Core components (BiLSTMs, residuals, embeddings) lack individual novelty; novelty is mainly in their combination.

11. Weakness: Heavy dependence on a pre-existing entity linker, with limited analysis of linker–detector interactions.

12. Weakness: Evaluation limited to SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, which may not reflect broader real-world KBQA diversity.

13. Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, limiting assessment of robustness.

14. Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis identifying which relations are difficult and why.

15. Question: Please provide details on hyperparameter search strategies and sensitivity analyses.

16. Question: How do entity linking errors affect relation detection, and what mitigation strategies exist?

17. Question: What is the computational cost of adding HR-BiLSTM compared to simpler models?

18. Question: Which relation types does the model struggle with the most, and can you give examples?

19. Question: How might this approach be extended to handle complex KB structures like multi-hop reasoning?

20. Additional comment: Citation formatting is inconsistent and should be standardized.

21. Additional comment: Add an explicit discussion of limitations and future research directions.

22. Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the points above.
