This paper proposes a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) for improved relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors argue that existing relation detection methods struggle with the large number of relations, zero-shot learning scenarios, and the need to predict relation chains common in KBQA. The primary contribution is the HR-BiLSTM model, designed to capture different levels of abstraction in questions and relations, and facilitate training through residual connections. A secondary contribution lies in demonstrating the improved relation detector’s positive impact on a simple two-step KBQA system, achieving state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and competitive results on WebQSP.

**Strengths:**

1. **Well-Motivated Problem:** The paper correctly identifies the challenges of relation detection specifically within the KBQA context.  The difficulties stemming from the scale of relation vocabularies and the potential for unseen relations are genuine and relevant. The authors articulate these issues clearly.
2. **Sound Methodology (at a High Level):** The use of BiLSTMs to capture different levels of representation and the inclusion of residual connections are reasonable approaches. The motivation for combining word-level and relation-level representations is logical, acknowledging the trade-offs between specificity and generalization.
3. **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, coupled with ablation studies, provide evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed HR-BiLSTM model. The results show consistent improvements over baseline models. The ablation study, though not exhaustive, offers some insight into the contribution of different components.
4. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed model and experimental setup. Figures and tables are helpful in understanding the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty:** While the combination of BiLSTMs and residual connections is sensible, neither component is particularly novel in itself. The paper lacks a truly innovative element. The hierarchical matching aspect is also not entirely new; attention mechanisms have been used for similar purposes in sequence matching tasks.  The combination, while potentially effective, doesn’t represent a breakthrough.
2. **Limited Comparison to Strong Baselines:** The baseline models used, while representing prior work, aren't consistently the *strongest* available. For example, more recent and sophisticated attention-based models for relation extraction weren’t thoroughly considered or compared. Claiming state-of-the-art results requires a demonstration of superiority over the absolute best available methods.
3. **Overstated Impact on KBQA System:** The KBQA system used for evaluation is described as "simple." While simplicity is sometimes a virtue, it also limits the generalizability of the findings. A more complex and realistic KBQA system, incorporating more sophisticated components (e.g., entity linking, query generation), would provide a more compelling demonstration of the practical benefits of the improved relation detection. The impact on a basic pipeline doesn't automatically translate to broader KBQA performance gains.
4. **Insufficient Analysis of Error Cases:** The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the model. Understanding *where* the model struggles (e.g., specific relation types, ambiguous questions) would provide valuable insights for future research.  Simply reporting accuracy numbers doesn't reveal the underlying limitations.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed comparison to more recent and state-of-the-art relation extraction models, including those based on transformers (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa)? How would the proposed HR-BiLSTM perform against these models?
2.  The paper mentions the challenges of constraint detection.  What specific types of constraints prove most difficult for the model to handle, and why?
3.  Could you elaborate on the design choices for the KBQA pipeline?  Specifically, why was a two-step approach chosen over an end-to-end model? What are the trade-offs of this approach?
4.  Please provide examples of questions where the HR-BiLSTM significantly improves performance compared to the baseline, and explain *why* the model succeeds in these cases.



**Additional Comments:**

The ablation studies are limited. Exploring the impact of different LSTM sizes or different pooling strategies would be beneficial. The details regarding the hyperparameter tuning process are somewhat sparse.  Providing more specifics would increase confidence in the reproducibility of the results.
