- Addresses relation detection in KBQA; proposes HR-BiLSTM combining word- and relation-level representations with residual connections and shows improved results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Empirically demonstrates KBQA-specific challenges: large relation space, zero-shot relations, and the need to detect relation chains.

- Proposes hierarchical relation representation and matching that leverages both fine-grained (word) and coarse-grained (relation) information.

- Integrates the relation detector into a full KBQA pipeline and achieves competitive/state-of-the-art performance.

- Strong empirical gains with convincing ablation studies, notably on the more complex WebQSP dataset.

- Well-motivated approach that explains why standard relation extraction methods fall short for KBQA.

- Clear and thorough presentation, with helpful figures and comprehensive related work.

- Practical system integration including an entity re-ranking step.

- Limited novelty: core components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are standard; novelty lies mainly in their combination for KBQA.

- Sensitivity to hyperparameters noted; robustness to different settings not analyzed in depth.

- Constraint detection component is under-described and treated somewhat as a black box.

- Limited error analysis; paper lacks detailed breakdown of failure cases and error patterns.

- Request to authors: explain why the specific combination of hierarchical matching, residuals, and BiLSTMs is especially suited to KBQA.

- Request to authors: provide insights on hyperparameter robustness (e.g., learning rate, embedding dimensions).

- Request to authors: give implementation details for constraint detection, including n-gram matching, matching score computation, and threshold selection for v.

- Request to authors: share any error analysis and examples of common failure cases with discussion of improvements.

- Using existing entity linking results is reasonable given the focus on relation detection.

- Inclusion of baselines from both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP is appreciated.

- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contribution; recommend acceptance.
