This paper addresses the problem of relation detection for knowledge base question answering (KBQA), focusing on the challenges posed by large relation vocabularies and zero-shot learning. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual connections (HR-BiLSTM) to improve relation detection performance and demonstrate its effectiveness within a simple KBQA pipeline.

The primary contribution is the HR-BiLSTM architecture for relation detection, aiming to better leverage both word-level and relation-level information. The ablation studies convincingly demonstrate the benefit of the residual connections and hierarchical matching. A secondary contribution is the demonstration of a functional, albeit simple, KBQA system built around the improved relation detector, achieving competitive results on established benchmarks.

**Strengths:**

1. **Sound Methodology:** The proposed HR-BiLSTM model is well-motivated and builds upon established techniques (BiLSTMs, residual connections). The use of both relation-level and word-level representations is a logical approach to address the challenges of large relation vocabularies and unseen relations.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors provide thorough experimental results on two established KBQA datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), including ablation studies to analyze the contribution of each component of the proposed model. This demonstrates a rigorous evaluation of the proposed approach.
3. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and experimental setup. The inclusion of Figure 1 and Figure 2 effectively illustrates the KBQA process and the HR-BiLSTM architecture, respectively.
4. **Competitive Performance:** The results demonstrate that the proposed model achieves competitive or state-of-the-art performance on the relation detection task and the overall KBQA task, considering the simplicity of the end-to-end system.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incremental Novelty:** While the HR-BiLSTM is a reasonable extension of existing techniques, the novelty is incremental. Residual connections and hierarchical architectures are well-established in deep learning, and their application to relation detection, while potentially beneficial, does not represent a breakthrough. The paper doesn't strongly articulate *why* this combination is particularly well-suited to the KBQA task beyond intuition.
2. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the system. Understanding *where* the model struggles would provide valuable insights for future work. For example, are there specific relation types that are consistently misclassified? Are there patterns in the questions that lead to incorrect predictions?
3. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:** The system's performance heavily relies on the quality of the initial entity linking step. The paper acknowledges this but does not explore the interplay between entity linking accuracy and relation detection performance in detail. A more robust evaluation would involve testing the system with different entity linkers or analyzing the impact of entity linking errors on the final results.
4. **Lack of Comparison to Stronger KBQA Baselines:** While the paper compares to existing relation detection baselines, the KBQA comparisons are somewhat limited.  A comparison to more sophisticated KBQA systems (e.g., those employing more complex semantic parsing techniques or graph neural networks) would provide a better benchmark for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the error cases made by the system? Specifically, what types of relations are most often misclassified, and what characteristics of the questions contribute to these errors?
2. How sensitive is the system’s performance to the accuracy of the initial entity linking step? Can you provide results using different entity linkers, or analyze the impact of entity linking errors on the final results?
3.  The paper mentions constraint detection is crucial. Can you quantify the improvement achieved by adding the constraint detection step (e.g., percentage point increase in accuracy) and provide some examples of questions where constraint detection is particularly important?
4.  What are the computational costs associated with the HR-BiLSTM model compared to the baseline models? Are there any considerations for scalability when applying this model to larger knowledge bases?



**Additional Comments:**

The references are complete. The authors should consider including a discussion of related work on knowledge graph embedding techniques, as these can provide valuable insights for relation representation learning.
