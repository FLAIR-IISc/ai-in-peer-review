## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:** This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the difficulties arising from large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning scenarios, and the need to predict relation chains. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual connections (HR-BiLSTM) to better match questions to relation representations at different levels of granularity (word-level and relation-level). They demonstrate improved performance on relation detection tasks and show that incorporating this improved detector into a simple KBQA pipeline achieves state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation datasets.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Hierarchical Relation Representation:** The core contribution is the successful integration of both word-level and relation-level representations for relations, combined with a residual learning mechanism to facilitate deeper, more abstract question representations.  This addresses a key limitation in prior work that struggled with unseen relations and capturing sufficient contextual information.
2. **Demonstrated Impact on End-to-End KBQA:** The paper convincingly demonstrates that improvements in relation detection directly translate to improved performance on the overall KBQA task, even within a relatively simple pipeline. This provides strong evidence for the importance of focusing on this specific subtask.
3. **Thorough Empirical Evaluation:** The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation, including ablation studies and comparisons to strong baselines, on two standard KBQA datasets, validating the effectiveness of their proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel and Well-Motivated Approach:** The problem of KB relation detection is genuinely challenging, and the authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, highlighting the limitations of existing methods in the context of KBQA. The hierarchical matching with residual learning is a logically sound and well-executed idea.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The reported improvements on both the relation detection tasks and the KBQA end task are significant and statistically compelling. The ablation studies effectively isolate the contributions of different components of the HR-BiLSTM model.
3. **Clear and Comprehensive Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details of the proposed approach. The inclusion of Figure 1 and Figure 2 is particularly helpful in understanding the system architecture.
4. **Practical KBQA Pipeline:** The two-step KBQA pipeline, leveraging the improved relation detection, is simple yet effective and provides a solid foundation for future work.  The entity re-ranking step adds a valuable layer of robustness.
5. **Reproducibility:** The authors mention making the datasets publicly available, which is commendable and will facilitate further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Building Blocks:** While the *combination* of hierarchical matching and residual learning is novel in this context, the individual components (BiLSTMs, max pooling, residual connections) are all well-established techniques in deep learning. The paper doesn't offer any breakthroughs in these core building blocks themselves. The novelty lies in their application to this specific problem.
2. **Sensitivity to Hyperparameters:** The paper acknowledges the need for hyperparameter tuning, but doesn't provide sufficient detail on the tuning process or the sensitivity of the results to different parameter settings.  A more detailed analysis of hyperparameter influence would strengthen the findings.
3. **Reliance on Pre-trained Embeddings & Existing Linkers:** The performance of the system heavily relies on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings and the initial entity linking results. While this is common in KBQA research, it's important to acknowledge the potential impact of these external components on the overall performance. A discussion on potential improvements to these aspects could be beneficial.
4. **Constraint Detection Seems Underdeveloped:** While constraint detection is presented as important, the details provided are relatively limited. It's unclear how robust this component is and whether it could be a bottleneck in more complex scenarios.  More detailed evaluation of constraint detection performance would be useful.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, specifically the search space and the criteria used for selecting the best hyperparameters?
2.  How sensitive are the results to different pre-trained word embeddings? Have you experimented with different embeddings or fine-tuning the embeddings during training?
3.  Could you elaborate on the implementation details of the constraint detection module and provide more detailed results on its performance (e.g., precision, recall)?
4. The paper mentions that the "entity re-ranking" step helps to disambiguate ambiguous entities. Can you provide specific examples of how the re-ranking step improved the accuracy in such cases?
5.  Given the reliance on existing entity linkers, have you considered incorporating the entity linking component into the training process in an end-to-end manner?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The formatting is generally good, though some references are missing full details (e.g., "Bao et al.,? 2016").
*   The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of the relevant literature.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to relation detection for KBQA. The authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the challenges in this area and provide a well-motivated and thoroughly evaluated solution. While the individual components of the proposed method are not entirely novel, the combination and application to this specific problem are significant contributions. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
