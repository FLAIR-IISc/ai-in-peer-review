## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging task of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), addressing issues like a large number of relations, zero-shot learning, and the need for chain prediction. The authors propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that combines word-level and relation-level representations with residual connections to improve matching between questions and relations. They demonstrate improvements on both relation detection and the overall KBQA task on the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Demonstration of Hierarchical Matching for KB Relation Detection:** The paper convincingly shows the benefit of considering different granularities of relation representation (word-level vs. relation-level) for this task, and importantly, demonstrates that a hierarchical approach significantly outperforms simpler baselines.  This is a valuable practical finding.
2. **Effective Integration of Residual Learning:** The application of residual learning to the hierarchical matching framework is a key innovation. It addresses the training difficulties inherent in deep architectures and leads to more robust and abstract question representations.
3. **Strong Experimental Validation and System Integration:**  The authors demonstrate improvements in both relation detection *and* a complete (though simplified) KBQA pipeline, showing the practical utility of their method. The thorough ablation studies provide insights into the contribution of each component.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the specific challenges of relation detection in KBQA, differentiating it from standard relation extraction. The motivation for addressing these challenges (large relation spaces, zero-shot learning) is well-explained.
2. **Solid Experimental Setup:** The use of established datasets (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP) and comparison with strong baselines allows for a fair evaluation of the proposed method. The ablation studies are particularly valuable in isolating the impact of each component.
3. **Detailed Model Description:** The HR-BiLSTM model is described with sufficient detail, allowing for replication. The explanations of the hierarchical matching and residual learning mechanisms are clear and easy to follow. The inclusion of Figure 2 is helpful.
4. **Strong Empirical Results:** The reported improvements on both relation detection and KBQA tasks are significant and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. Achieving state-of-the-art performance on SimpleQuestions is a noteworthy accomplishment.
5. **Practical System Integration:**  The authors demonstrate the end-to-end effectiveness of the approach by incorporating it into a complete KBQA system, showing that the improved relation detection translates to better overall performance.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components – BiLSTMs and residual connections – are well-established in the NLP literature. The core innovation lies in the specific application to this KBQA problem, rather than the introduction of fundamentally new architectural elements.
2. **Dependence on Pre-trained Embeddings and Existing Entity Linkers:** The system heavily relies on pre-trained word embeddings and an existing entity linker.  While reasonable, it would be valuable to see some analysis of how sensitive the system is to the quality of these components.  A more end-to-end trainable system (even if more complex) would be stronger.
3. **Complexity of Hyperparameter Tuning:**  The paper mentions tuning several hyperparameters. More details on the tuning process (e.g., grid search range, optimization method) would be helpful for reproducibility.
4. **Limited Analysis of Failure Cases:**  While the ablation studies are strong, a more in-depth analysis of *why* the model fails in certain cases would be valuable.  For example, are there particular types of questions or relations that are consistently misclassified? This could guide future research.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the performance of your system to the quality of the entity linker used?  Have you experimented with different entity linkers, or conducted an analysis of the impact of linker errors?
2.  Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process (e.g., search space, optimization algorithm)?
3.  Can you provide some examples of failure cases – questions where the model performs poorly – and discuss potential reasons for the errors?
4. You mention that constraint detection is crucial for performance. Could you elaborate on the interplay between the relation detection and constraint detection modules?  Does improving relation detection *always* lead to improvements in constraint detection, or are there cases where a more accurate relation detection might actually *hurt* the constraint detection stage?



**Additional Comments:**

* The paper is well-written and generally easy to understand.
* The reference list is comprehensive.
* The inclusion of Table 1 is helpful for illustrating the different levels of relation granularity.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a well-motivated, clearly explained, and empirically validated approach to a challenging problem. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, and the experimental results are compelling.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
