This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on issues arising from the large number of relations and the presence of unseen relations during testing. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning to better match questions to relations at different granularities (word-level and relation-level). They also demonstrate the effectiveness of this improved relation detector within a simple KBQA pipeline, achieving state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

The main contributions of this work are: (1) A novel hierarchical residual BiLSTM architecture for KB relation detection, effectively combining word-level and relation-level representations; (2) Demonstration of the significant impact of improved relation detection on end-to-end KBQA performance, showcasing state-of-the-art results on benchmark datasets; and (3) A comprehensive empirical evaluation, including ablation studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of each component of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty and Technical Soundness of the HR-BiLSTM Model:** The proposed hierarchical residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) is a well-motivated and technically sound approach. Combining word-level and relation-level information is a logical step towards addressing the challenges of KB relation detection. The use of residual connections is a thoughtful addition, likely aiding in training deeper networks and improving gradient flow.  The ablation studies convincingly demonstrate the benefit of each component of the model.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP are compelling. Achieving state-of-the-art performance on these benchmarks provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  The results are statistically significant and outperform strong baselines.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation & Analysis:**  The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including ablation studies, which are crucial for understanding the contribution of each component.  The analysis of the results is insightful, explaining why the proposed model performs well and highlighting the importance of different design choices. The authors clearly articulate the limitations of previous approaches and how their work addresses them.
4. **Clear Connection to KBQA:** The paper explicitly frames the relation detection task within the context of KBQA. Evaluating the relation detector within an end-to-end KBQA pipeline demonstrates the practical relevance of the work. The system design is relatively simple, allowing for a focused evaluation of the relation detection component.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:** While the results are strong, the paper lacks a detailed error analysis. Identifying specific types of errors made by the system (e.g., confusing similar relations, difficulty with complex questions) could provide valuable insights for future work and potentially reveal areas where the model could be further improved.
2. **Dependency on Existing Entity Linkers:** The KBQA system relies on pre-existing entity linkers. While this simplifies the evaluation, it obscures the potential impact of errors in entity linking on the overall performance.  A more complete system would ideally integrate entity linking and relation detection into a single, end-to-end trainable model.
3. **Hyperparameter Tuning Details:**  The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but does not provide sufficient detail on the specific tuning procedure or the range of values explored.  More information on this aspect would increase the reproducibility of the results.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, illustrating the types of errors the model commonly makes in relation detection?  Specific examples would be helpful.
2. How sensitive is the performance of the KBQA system to the accuracy of the entity linker? Have you explored the impact of using different entity linkers, or intentionally introducing noise into the entity linking results?
3. Can you elaborate on the hyperparameter tuning process? What specific ranges of values were explored for the key hyperparameters (e.g., hidden state size, learning rate)?

**Additional Comments:**

* The formatting of the references is inconsistent.
* The explanation of the constraint detection step could be clearer.
* While the paper references previous work, the connections to related research in areas like meta-learning or few-shot learning for relation extraction could be explored.
