## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging task of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors identify key difficulties specific to KBQA relation detection – a large number of relations, zero-shot learning scenarios, and the need to detect relation chains – and propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to address them.  The model leverages both relation-level and word-level representations and demonstrates strong performance on both relation detection and the overall KBQA task on the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, achieving state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Analysis of KBQA Relation Detection Challenges:** The paper clearly articulates the specific challenges of relation detection *within the context of KBQA*, distinguishing it from general relation extraction. This framing is valuable and motivates the proposed approach.
2. **Effective Hierarchical Relation Representation:** The combination of relation-level and word-level representations, coupled with the residual learning mechanism, appears to be a strong technical contribution. The results demonstrate its efficacy in handling both the large vocabulary and zero-shot aspects of KBQA.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on two benchmark KBQA datasets is a significant outcome, validating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Well-Motivated Problem Definition:** The paper does an excellent job of explaining *why* KBQA relation detection is harder than standard relation extraction. The issues of scale, zero-shot learning, and relation chains are clearly presented and serve as a strong justification for the proposed work.
2. **Sound Technical Approach:** The HR-BiLSTM architecture is well-reasoned and builds upon existing techniques (BiLSTMs, residual connections) in a novel and effective manner.  The intuition behind combining different granularity representations (word vs. relation-level) is strong, and the residual learning effectively addresses training difficulties.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough experimental evaluation, including ablation studies that demonstrate the contribution of each component of the proposed model.  Comparison with strong baselines further validates the effectiveness of HR-BiLSTM.
4. **Practical System Integration:** Integrating the improved relation detection into a complete (albeit simple) KBQA system and demonstrating end-to-end performance is a positive aspect.  This shows the practical applicability of the research.
5. **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methods and experiments.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the *combination* of techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are well-established in the literature. The novelty primarily lies in applying these techniques to the specific problem of KBQA relation detection in a thoughtful way.  The paper could benefit from more explicitly acknowledging this and emphasizing the synergistic effect of the components.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but doesn’t provide sufficient detail about the search process.  It’s unclear how sensitive the results are to specific hyperparameter settings.  A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the findings.
3. **Simplified KBQA System:**  The described KBQA system is intentionally simple, which is understandable for focusing on relation detection. However, this limits the scope of the evaluation. It’s possible that the benefits of the improved relation detection are more pronounced in more complex KBQA systems.
4. **Reliance on Existing Entity Linkers:** The performance is heavily dependent on the quality of the initial entity linking step. While they incorporate a re-ranking step based on relation detection, the initial linking accuracy remains a potential bottleneck.  More analysis of the impact of the entity linker's performance on the final results would be valuable.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the hyperparameter tuning process? Specifically, what search space was used for the learning rate, hidden layer size, and other key parameters?
2.  How sensitive are the results to the initial entity linking accuracy? Have you experimented with different entity linkers or investigated the impact of linker errors on the final KBQA performance?
3.  While the paper focuses on relation detection, do you anticipate that the HR-BiLSTM model would be equally effective in more complex KBQA systems with additional components (e.g., more sophisticated constraint detection or answer ranking)?
4.  Could you provide more detail on the implementation details, especially regarding the implementation of the residual connections (e.g., the specific type of addition used)?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The discussion of constraint detection could be expanded. While the authors acknowledge its importance, the description of the implementation is brief.
*   It might be helpful to include a small example illustrating the hierarchical matching process with specific question and relation examples.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an important problem in KBQA with a well-motivated and effective approach. The combination of techniques is novel in its application to KBQA, and the experimental results are compelling. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the paper demonstrates a clear contribution through its careful design and thorough evaluation. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
