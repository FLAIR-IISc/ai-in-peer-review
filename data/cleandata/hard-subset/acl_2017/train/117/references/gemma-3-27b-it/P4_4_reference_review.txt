This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), particularly focusing on the issues of large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation reasoning. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to better capture different levels of abstraction in both questions and relations. They also incorporate this improved relation detector into a simple KBQA pipeline and demonstrate state-of-the-art results on both single- and multi-relation tasks.

The primary contribution of this work is the HR-BiLSTM model for relation detection. The idea of combining word-level and relation-level representations with residual connections to facilitate hierarchical matching is a valuable addition to the existing techniques. The ablation studies convincingly demonstrate the benefits of each component of the proposed model. The experiments and results clearly show the efficacy of the approach.

A secondary contribution is the end-to-end KBQA system demonstrating how the improved relation detection module benefits the overall performance. The system is relatively simple yet achieves competitive results, highlighting the importance of accurate relation detection. The authors’ careful consideration of entity re-ranking and constraint detection further strengthens this contribution.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The HR-BiLSTM model consistently outperforms existing methods on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets. The improvements are statistically significant, confirming the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
2. **Well-Motivated Design:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges specific to KB relation detection (large vocabulary, zero-shot learning, multi-relation reasoning) and provides a well-reasoned design that addresses these challenges. The hierarchical matching and residual learning mechanisms are effectively motivated by these considerations.
3. **Comprehensive Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies are thorough and insightful. They clearly demonstrate the individual contributions of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model, providing a strong understanding of its inner workings.
4. **Practical KBQA System:** The inclusion of a complete KBQA system, even a simple one, adds practical value to the work. It demonstrates how the improved relation detection module can be integrated into a larger system and provides a realistic evaluation of its impact.
5. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and logically organized. The figures and tables are informative and contribute to a clear understanding of the proposed approach and experimental results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the combination of existing techniques (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) is effective, the individual components themselves are not novel. The primary novelty lies in the specific application and integration of these techniques for the task of KB relation detection.
2. **Dependence on Pre-trained Embeddings:** The performance of the model relies heavily on the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings. The paper does not explore the impact of different embedding strategies or the potential for learning embeddings specifically tailored to the KBQA task.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions tuning hyperparameters on development sets, but does not provide extensive details on the sensitivity of the model to different hyperparameter settings. This could be a potential limitation, as the optimal hyperparameters may vary for different KBs or datasets.
4. **KBQA system simplicity:** While simplicity is a design choice, the KBQA system is very basic and does not explore more advanced techniques like joint inference or end-to-end training. This limits the potential for further performance gains.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of different pre-trained word embeddings on the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model?
2.  What is the computational cost of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler relation detection models?
3.  How does the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model degrade when faced with significantly more unseen relations than observed in the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets?
4.  Would a joint training approach (relation detection and KBQA) yield further improvements in performance?

**Additional Comments:**

*   It would be beneficial to include a qualitative analysis of the types of errors made by the HR-BiLSTM model and identify potential areas for improvement.
*   Consider providing more details on the entity re-ranking step, such as the specific algorithm used and the impact of different weighting parameters.
* The paper refers to Algorithm 1 but the algorithm itself is not present in the appendix.
