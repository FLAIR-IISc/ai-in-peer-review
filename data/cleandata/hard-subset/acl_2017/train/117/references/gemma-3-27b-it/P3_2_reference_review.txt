## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:** This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the issues of unseen relations, large relation vocabularies, and the need for chain prediction. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to better match questions to relations at different levels of granularity (word-level and relation-level). They demonstrate improvements over baselines on both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA tasks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP).  They also highlight the importance of an entity re-ranking step leveraging relation detection to improve KBQA performance.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Demonstration of Hierarchical Matching for KB Relation Detection:** While the idea of combining different granularities isn't entirely novel, the authors convincingly demonstrate its effectiveness within the specific context of KBQA, where unseen relations are common. The ablation studies clearly show the benefit of combining word and relation-level representations.
2. **A Strong Baseline and Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper provides a well-implemented and thoroughly evaluated baseline system for KBQA, particularly on the relation detection component. The inclusion of ablation studies and comparisons to recent work is commendable.
3. **Highlighting the Benefit of Relation-Aware Entity Re-ranking:**  The authors correctly identify and demonstrate the value of using relation detection to disambiguate entities *before* relation prediction, improving overall KBQA accuracy.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges specific to KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, justifying the need for specialized techniques.  The distinction regarding the scale of relation vocabularies and the zero-shot learning aspect is well-explained.
2. **Well-Designed Experiments:** The experimental setup is robust. Using established datasets (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP) and existing entity linkers allows for fair comparisons to prior work.  The ablation studies are crucial for understanding the individual contributions of the proposed components.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:**  The HR-BiLSTM consistently outperforms baseline models on both relation detection and the end-to-end KBQA task, establishing the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  Achieving state-of-the-art results is a significant accomplishment.
4. **Practical System Design:** The proposed KBQA pipeline is relatively simple and efficient, focusing on improving relation detection rather than introducing complex end-to-end architectures. This makes the approach more easily adaptable and deployable.
5. **Thorough Analysis:**  The ablation studies and discussion of the results demonstrate a good understanding of the strengths and limitations of the proposed model.  The discussion regarding why residual learning is more effective on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions is insightful.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in the Core Architecture:** The core building blocks (BiLSTMs, residual connections, max-pooling) are well-established in the NLP literature. The novelty lies primarily in the *application* and combination of these techniques within the specific context of KBQA and the hierarchical matching approach.  While effective, the architectural innovation is incremental.
2. **Lack of Exploration of Alternative Matching Strategies:** While the authors compare residual learning to weighted summation and attention, a broader exploration of alternative hierarchical matching mechanisms (e.g., more sophisticated attention models, tree-based matching) could have further strengthened the work.
3. **Dependency on Existing Entity Linkers:** The performance of the KBQA system is inherently limited by the accuracy of the initial entity linker. The authors acknowledge this, but a more detailed analysis of the impact of linker errors on downstream performance would be valuable.  Exploring how the proposed system could potentially *improve* the entity linking process (e.g., through joint training) could be a future direction.
4. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** The paper focuses on overall performance metrics, but a more detailed analysis of the types of errors the model makes (e.g., confusing similar relations, failing to handle complex questions) would provide valuable insights for future improvements.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  You mention that the entity re-ranking step significantly improves performance. Could you provide a more detailed breakdown of *how* the re-ranking step helps? For example, what percentage of questions benefit from re-ranking, and in what ways does it change the top-K entity candidates?
2.  The paper states that constraint detection is crucial for performance, yet it is presented as an optional step.  Is there a significant performance drop when constraint detection is disabled, even in cases where complex questions are involved?  Could you provide more detail on the threshold (✓) used for constraint detection and how it was determined?
3.  Given the limitations of the initial entity linker, have you considered exploring techniques to jointly train the entity linker and relation detector, potentially leveraging the relation detection model to improve entity disambiguation?
4.  Could you elaborate on the choice of 300-d pre-trained word embeddings? Were other embedding methods considered, and if so, why were these selected?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is generally well-written and easy to understand.
*   The references are comprehensive and relevant.
*   The code and datasets should be made publicly available to facilitate reproducibility and further research.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. While the core architectural innovation is incremental, the empirical results, comprehensive evaluation, and insightful analysis demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
