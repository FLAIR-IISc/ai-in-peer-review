This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically focusing on the issues of large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation question answering. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to improve relation detection performance and demonstrate its effectiveness within a simple KBQA pipeline.

**Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Relation Matching with Residual Learning:** The core contribution lies in the HR-BiLSTM model. The use of both relation-level and word-level representations, combined with residual connections, allows the model to effectively leverage both global and local information within relations and improves training stability, particularly for deeper architectures. This is a well-motivated and thoughtfully designed approach to address the challenges of KB relation detection.
2. **Demonstrated Improvement in KBQA Performance:** The paper clearly demonstrates that the improved relation detector enhances the performance of a simple KBQA system on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) tasks, achieving competitive results. This practical validation strengthens the significance of the proposed method.
3. **Comprehensive Ablation Studies:** The thorough ablation studies, systematically evaluating the impact of different components (residual learning, different granularity levels) provide valuable insights into the effectiveness of the HR-BiLSTM architecture and contribute to a better understanding of the underlying mechanisms.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper effectively articulates the specific challenges of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, highlighting the nuances of handling large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation questions.
2. **Sound Technical Approach:** The HR-BiLSTM model is a well-reasoned approach to address the identified challenges.  The combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is a novel and potentially effective technique.
3. **Strong Experimental Validation:** The experiments are well-designed and the results are convincing.  The comparison with existing methods and the ablation studies provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The inclusion of results on two different datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) further strengthens the findings.
4. **Good Presentation and Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The figures and tables are informative and contribute to a clear understanding of the proposed method and experimental results.
5. **Practical KBQA Pipeline:** The focus on a complete KBQA pipeline, even a simple one, provides a more realistic assessment of the impact of the improved relation detector compared to evaluating it in isolation.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are well-established techniques in the broader NLP field. The novelty lies primarily in the application of these techniques to the specific problem of KB relation detection, but a stronger claim regarding the innovative aspects of the overall architecture would be beneficial.
2. **Dependency on Pre-existing Entity Linkers:** The performance of the KBQA system is heavily reliant on the accuracy of the pre-existing entity linkers (S-MART and the one used for SimpleQuestions).  The paper acknowledges this, but a more detailed analysis of how errors in entity linking propagate through the system would be valuable.
3. **Simplified KBQA Pipeline:** The simplicity of the KBQA pipeline, while commendable for clarity, might limit the ability to fully assess the potential benefits of the improved relation detector. More complex KBQA architectures could potentially reveal further gains or limitations.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:**  A deeper error analysis of the relation detection model would be helpful in identifying specific types of errors and guiding future research. For example, are there particular types of relations or questions that the model struggles with?



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of entity linking errors on the performance of your KBQA system?  Specifically, how does the performance degrade as the accuracy of the entity linker decreases?
2.  The ablation studies are thorough, but could you investigate the computational cost (e.g., training time, inference time) of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler baselines?
3.  What types of errors does the HR-BiLSTM model still make in relation detection? Are there specific relation types or question structures that consistently pose challenges?
4. You mention using the same question/relation pairs as Yin et al. (2016) for SimpleQuestions. Were any data cleaning or preprocessing steps applied to ensure consistency with their data?

**Additional Comments:**

*  The discussion of related work is comprehensive and well-organized.
*  The formatting of the table comparing against previous models is good, but including the p-values for statistical significance testing would strengthen the results.
* The paper would benefit from a brief discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential directions for future research.



Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to KB relation detection. The technical contributions are solid, the experimental results are convincing, and the paper is well-written and clearly presented. While the individual components of the HR-BiLSTM model are not entirely novel, the combination and application to the KBQA task are well-motivated and demonstrate significant improvements in performance. I recommend acceptance.
