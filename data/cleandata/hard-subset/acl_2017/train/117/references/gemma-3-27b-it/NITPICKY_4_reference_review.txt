This paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors address the challenges of large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation question answering by introducing a hierarchical matching approach using BiLSTMs and residual learning. They demonstrate improvements on both relation detection and KBQA tasks.

The primary contribution of this work is the exploration of hierarchical matching with residual connections for relation detection in KBQA. While the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) aren't novel, their combination and application to this specific KBQA context, particularly the justification for needing both relation-level and word-level representations, are potentially insightful. A secondary contribution is the comprehensive experimental evaluation on standard KBQA datasets, providing a solid empirical basis for the proposed method. The thorough ablation studies, though detailed, do contribute to understanding the impact of each component.

**Strengths:**

1. **Detailed Experimental Evaluation:** The paper presents a comprehensive set of experiments, including ablation studies, on standard KBQA datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP). This allows for a clear understanding of the impact of each proposed component. The inclusion of comparisons against established baselines strengthens the validity of the results.
2. **Justification for Hierarchical Matching:** The authors provide a strong rationale for combining relation-level and word-level representations. The argument that relation names provide global information while words capture local details resonates with the complexities of relation detection in KBQA.
3. **Residual Learning for Deeper Representations:** The application of residual learning to address training difficulties in deeper BiLSTM architectures appears effective, as evidenced by the performance gains in the ablation study, specifically for the WebQSP dataset. This suggests the method is indeed enabling the model to learn more abstract representations.
4. **Clear Presentation of the KBQA Pipeline:** The paper clearly outlines the proposed KBQA pipeline, including the entity re-ranking step. This aids in understanding how the improved relation detection model is integrated into the larger system.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incremental Novelty:** While the combination of existing techniques (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) is reasonable, the overall novelty is incremental. The core idea of leveraging both relation-level and word-level representations is not entirely new in NLP, and its application to KBQA, while valuable, doesn’t represent a paradigm shift.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity & Reporting:** The paper mentions extensive hyperparameter tuning but provides limited details about the search space and specific values chosen. A more detailed description of the hyperparameter tuning process is needed for reproducibility. The sensitivity to hyperparameters could also be a concern.
3. **Overly Complex System Description:** The description of the KBQA pipeline, especially the constraint detection step, is quite verbose and difficult to follow. While detail is appreciated, a more concise and focused presentation would improve readability. The benefits of the constraint detection step are asserted but not fully demonstrated in terms of quantifiable improvements.
4. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a thorough error analysis. Understanding *where* the model fails (e.g., specific relation types, question structures) would provide valuable insights and guide future research. Are there systematic errors related to unseen relations, despite the proposed methods?
5. **Formatting and Typos:** The paper contains numerous minor formatting inconsistencies (e.g., inconsistent spacing after periods, referencing styles) and typos ("hrs" instead of "embeddings"). While not critical, these details detract from the professionalism of the submission. The reference list also has errors in formatting.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details about the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and the criteria used for selecting the optimal values?
2. Can you provide an error analysis, focusing on the types of questions or relations where the model performs poorly? Specifically, how does the model handle truly unseen relations, those that are not present in any form (words or name) during training?
3.  The description of the constraint detection step is complex. Could you simplify the explanation and provide more quantitative results demonstrating its impact on overall KBQA performance?
4. Please address the formatting inconsistencies and typos throughout the paper. A careful proofread is highly recommended.



**Additional Comments:**

* The paper refers to "Appendix A" and "Appendix C" without providing them in the submitted version. These appendices should be included.
* The phrasing "we leave in future work beam-search and feature extraction on beam for final answer re-ranking like in previous research" sounds dismissive of established techniques. A more nuanced discussion of why these techniques were not adopted in this work would be beneficial.
* While the claim of achieving state-of-the-art results is made, it's important to note that the improvements are relatively modest (2-3%).




Despite the improvements achieved, the incremental novelty and numerous minor flaws make me hesitant to strongly recommend acceptance. I lean towards **rejection**, with the caveat that the authors could address the weaknesses through a thorough revision and resubmission. The core idea is promising, but the presentation and execution need significant refinement.
