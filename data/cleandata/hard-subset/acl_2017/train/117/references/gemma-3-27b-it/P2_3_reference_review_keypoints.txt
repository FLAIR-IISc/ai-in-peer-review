- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM (hierarchical BiLSTM with residuals) to improve relation detection for KBQA, achieving state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Main contribution 1: Clearly frames KBQA relation detection challenges—large relation sets, zero-shot cases, and relation chains.  
- Main contribution 2: Introduces HR-BiLSTM combining relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning.  
- Main contribution 3: Demonstrates strong empirical gains and integrates improvements into a full KBQA pipeline.  
- Strength 1: Precise problem formulation that distinguishes KBQA relation detection from general relation extraction.  
- Strength 2: Technically sound model design; residual connections facilitate deeper hierarchical matching.  
- Strength 3: Strong experimental validation with ablation studies showing component contributions.  
- Strength 4: Evaluates method within a complete (though simplified) KBQA system, showing end-task benefits.  
- Strength 5: Clear writing and informative figures/tables.  
- Weakness 1: Individual components (BiLSTMs, residual learning, hierarchical reps) are not novel; novelty is in their integration.  
- Weakness 2: Heavy reliance on external entity linker; impact of linker quality is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness 3: KBQA pipeline is simplified, which may limit conclusions about real-world applicability.  
- Weakness 4: Lacks qualitative error analysis to show remaining failure modes.  
- Question 1: How sensitive is overall performance to the accuracy of the initial entity linker?  
- Question 2: Can the authors provide examples of failure cases and common error patterns?  
- Question 3: Have the authors tried jointly learning re-ranking and relation detection rather than a two-step process?  
- Question 4: How much does constraint detection quality affect end-to-end performance and is there diminishing return?  
- Additional comment 1: Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.  
- Additional comment 2: Notation is clear and consistent.  
- Additional comment 3: Code and data should be released for reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated paper with convincing results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address analyses and ablations.
