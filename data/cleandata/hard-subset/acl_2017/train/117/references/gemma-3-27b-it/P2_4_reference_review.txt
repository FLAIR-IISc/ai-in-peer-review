## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically focusing on the issues of unseen relations, large relation vocabularies, and the need to predict relation chains. The authors propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that leverages both relation-level and word-level representations, combined with residual learning, to improve matching between questions and relations. They demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach through experiments on the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, showcasing improvements in both relation detection accuracy and end-to-end KBQA performance.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical demonstration of the importance of hierarchical relation representation for KBQA:** The paper convincingly shows that combining relation-level and word-level representations significantly boosts performance compared to using either alone, particularly in scenarios with a large number of relations and potential for unseen relations.
2. **Effective application of residual learning to sequence matching in KBQA:** The use of residual connections helps address training difficulties associated with deep BiLSTM networks and improves the model’s ability to learn abstract question representations, enhancing hierarchical matching.
3. **A solid and well-evaluated KBQA system utilizing improved relation detection:** The authors built a practical KBQA system around their improved relation detector, demonstrating the benefits for the end task. The ablation studies provide useful insights into the contribution of each component.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges specific to relation detection within the context of KBQA, distinguishing it from general relation extraction tasks. The rationale for addressing these challenges (large vocabularies, zero-shot learning, relation chains) is well-presented.
2. **Strong Experimental Results:** The HR-BiLSTM consistently outperforms strong baselines on both relation detection and KBQA tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The ablation studies effectively isolate the contributions of different components.
3. **Practical System & Implementation Details:**  The authors provide enough detail to understand the system’s architecture and implementation, and the focus on building a relatively simple KBQA pipeline enhances the practicality of their work.  The reuse of existing entity linking results allows for a focused evaluation of the relation detection component.
4. **Thorough Analysis & Ablation:** The detailed ablation studies effectively demonstrate the importance of each design choice – hierarchical matching and residual connections. This helps understand *why* the proposed model works well.
5. **Well-written and organized:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methods and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the *combination* of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, neither component is entirely novel.  Hierarchical attention has been explored in NLP, and residual connections are a standard technique in deep learning. The paper doesn’t strongly emphasize what is specifically *new* about their application in this context, beyond demonstrating its effectiveness.
2. **Sensitivity to Hyperparameter Tuning:** The authors acknowledge the importance of hyperparameter tuning, but the specific details of the tuning process aren't fully transparent. A more detailed discussion of the tuning process and its impact on results would be beneficial.  Specifically, the impact of the alpha parameter in the entity re-ranking step isn't fully explored.
3. **Reliance on Existing Entity Linking:** The reliance on pre-existing entity linking results, while practical, limits the scope of the contribution.  A more comprehensive evaluation might involve incorporating an end-to-end system where both entity linking and relation detection are jointly optimized.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** While the quantitative results are strong, a more in-depth error analysis would provide valuable insights into the limitations of the model and potential areas for future improvement.  Understanding the types of errors made could inform future research directions.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more detail on the hyperparameter tuning process, particularly for the alpha parameter in the entity re-ranking step? How sensitive are the results to variations in this parameter?
2.  What types of errors does the HR-BiLSTM model still make? Can you provide examples of challenging cases where the model fails and discuss potential reasons for these failures?
3.  While you mention the benefits of the entity re-ranking step, how does its performance vary depending on the quality of the initial entity linking results? 
4.  Given the potential benefits of joint entity linking and relation detection, do you have any plans to explore this direction in future work?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The references are comprehensive and well-formatted.
*   The figures and tables are clear and informative.
*   The paper would benefit from a brief discussion of the computational cost of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler baselines.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. The proposed HR-BiLSTM model is effective and well-supported by experimental results. While the individual components may not be entirely novel, their combination and application to the specific challenges of KBQA demonstrate a significant advance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
