This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the issues of large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation reasoning. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to better capture relation information at different granularities and improve matching with questions. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on both relation detection and KBQA tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and competitive results on WebQSP.

**Contributions:**

1. **Novel Hierarchical Relation Representation:** The core contribution lies in the approach to representing relations at both word-level and relation-level, combined with a hierarchical matching scheme. This addresses the limitations of previous methods that struggle with unseen relations and rely heavily on complete relation name matches.
2. **Residual Learning for Enhanced Training:** The application of residual learning to the hierarchical matching process is a significant improvement. It tackles the training difficulties associated with deep architectures and allows for more effective learning of abstract question representations, which ultimately improves performance.
3. **Strong Empirical Validation:** The extensive experimental results on two benchmark datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The ablation studies clearly highlight the contributions of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model. The improvements achieved in both relation detection and the end-to-end KBQA tasks provide strong evidence of the method’s practical value.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of relation detection in KBQA, differentiating it from general relation extraction and providing strong justification for the proposed approach. The discussion of the issues related to large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation reasoning is well-presented.
2. **Technically Sound Model:** The HR-BiLSTM model is well-designed and technically sound. The use of residual connections, hierarchical matching, and different granularity relation representations are all logical and well-motivated choices. The explanation of the model is clear and easy to follow.
3. **Comprehensive Experiments and Ablation Studies:** The experimental setup is thorough, including comparisons with several baseline methods and a detailed ablation study. This allows for a clear understanding of the contributions of each component of the proposed model.
4. **State-of-the-Art Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions is a strong indicator of the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The competitive results on WebQSP further demonstrate the generalizability of the method.
5. **System-Level Evaluation:** Evaluating the impact of the improved relation detection model within a complete KBQA pipeline is a valuable contribution. This demonstrates the practical relevance of the research and provides insights into the potential benefits of the proposed method in a real-world system.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are well-established techniques in the field. The novelty primarily lies in the specific application and combination of these techniques for KB relation detection.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of deep learning models is often sensitive to hyperparameter tuning. The paper does not provide a detailed discussion of the hyperparameter tuning process or the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings.
3. **Entity Linking Dependence:** The performance of the KBQA system is heavily dependent on the accuracy of the initial entity linking step. While the authors address this by incorporating entity re-ranking, the overall performance is still limited by the quality of the entity linker. It is not clear if the improvements reported are solely due to the relation detection model or if they are also influenced by the entity re-ranking step.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:**  The paper lacks a detailed error analysis to identify the types of questions or relations that the model struggles with. Such analysis could provide valuable insights for future research and model improvement.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details about the hyperparameter tuning process and the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings?
2. How much of the performance improvement in the KBQA task is attributable to the entity re-ranking step, and how much is attributable to the improved relation detection model?
3. Could you provide a detailed error analysis to identify the types of questions or relations that the model struggles with? Specifically, are there any systematic patterns in the errors?
4. In the ablation study, why does removing residual learning only have a marginal impact on SimpleQuestions but a more significant impact on WebQSP?

**Additional Comments:**

* The paper is well-written and easy to understand.
* The figures and tables are clear and informative.
* The related work section is comprehensive and provides a good overview of the existing literature.
* The code and datasets should be made publicly available to facilitate reproducibility.
