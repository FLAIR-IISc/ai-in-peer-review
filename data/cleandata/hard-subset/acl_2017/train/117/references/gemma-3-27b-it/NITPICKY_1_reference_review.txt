This paper addresses the problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the challenges of large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation question answering. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to match questions and relations at different granularities. They demonstrate improvements on both relation detection and KBQA tasks.

The primary contribution of this work is the HR-BiLSTM architecture for relation detection. While hierarchical matching and residual connections have been used in NLP, their application to the specific problem of KB relation detection – particularly with the explicit goal of handling varying granularity and data sparsity – is a reasonable addition. The second contribution lies in the thorough experimentation and ablation studies, which demonstrate the effectiveness of each component of the HR-BiLSTM. Finally, the paper provides a relatively straightforward KBQA system built around the improved relation detector, showing its benefit in a realistic setting.

**Strengths:**

1. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The paper presents a strong set of experiments on both relation detection and KBQA tasks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP). The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the individual contributions of each component of the HR-BiLSTM. This provides strong evidence supporting the claims of the paper.
2. **Clear Problem Formulation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges specific to KB relation detection – the large vocabulary, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation scenarios – and positions their work within the existing literature. This clarity makes the contribution of the paper easily understandable.
3. **Detailed Model Description:** The HR-BiLSTM architecture is described in sufficient detail, including clear explanations of the hierarchical matching and residual learning mechanisms. The inclusion of Figure 2 is helpful in visualizing the model structure.
4. **State-of-the-Art Results:** The authors achieve state-of-the-art or competitive results on the chosen benchmarks, demonstrating the practical effectiveness of their approach. The improvement over the baselines, though not overwhelmingly large, is significant and consistent.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incremental Novelty:** While the HR-BiLSTM architecture is a sensible extension of existing techniques (BiLSTMs, hierarchical matching, residual learning), the novelty is incremental. The core ideas are not groundbreaking, and the combination of existing components, while effective, doesn't represent a substantial leap forward.
2. **Overly Detailed & Redundant Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally verbose, repeating explanations and justifications unnecessarily. For example, the repeated explanation of why KB relation detection is difficult feels repetitive. The length obscures the core contributions.
3. **Minor Implementation Details & Formatting Issues:** The paper contains numerous minor formatting inconsistencies (e.g., inconsistent use of italics, spacing around equations) and references that are not properly formatted. This suggests a lack of attention to detail. Moreover, the frequent citation of the same paper (e.g., Bordes et al. 2015) multiple times within a single paragraph feels clunky.
4. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** While the ablation studies are thorough, the paper lacks a detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the model. Understanding *why* the model fails in certain cases would provide valuable insights and directions for future research. A qualitative analysis of failure modes is missing.
5. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:** The KBQA system relies heavily on the accuracy of existing entity linkers. The performance of the entire system is therefore limited by the quality of this upstream component. The paper acknowledges this but doesn't fully explore the impact of varying linker performance.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the error cases made by your model? Specifically, what types of questions or relations does it struggle with most?
2. How sensitive is your KBQA system to the performance of the entity linker? Have you experimented with different entity linkers, and how does their performance affect the overall results?
3. Given that the novelty of the HR-BiLSTM is incremental, what is the key insight that makes this combination of techniques particularly effective for KB relation detection?
4. Please address the formatting inconsistencies and redundancies in the paper to improve its clarity and readability.



**Additional Comments:**

The reference list is excessively long and could be pruned. Consider removing citations that are not directly relevant to the core contributions of the paper. The discussion of constraint detection feels somewhat tacked on and could be integrated more seamlessly into the overall system description.



Overall, this is a competent paper that presents a reasonable and well-evaluated approach to KB relation detection. However, the incremental novelty, excessive verbosity, and minor flaws in presentation detract from its overall impact. I recommend rejection, but encourage the authors to revise and resubmit after addressing the weaknesses outlined above. A more concise and focused presentation, along with a deeper analysis of the model's limitations, would significantly strengthen the paper.
