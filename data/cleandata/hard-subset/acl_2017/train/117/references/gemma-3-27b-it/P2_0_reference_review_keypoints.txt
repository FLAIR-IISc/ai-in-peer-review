- Tackles relation detection in KBQA, addressing large relation sets, zero-shot cases, and relation chains with a hierarchical BiLSTM plus residual learning (HR-BiLSTM).
- Proposes combining relation-level and word-level representations with residual connections to improve relation detection.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP for relation detection and end-to-end KBQA.
- Clearly frames KBQA relation detection challenges distinct from standard relation extraction, motivating the approach.
- HR-BiLSTM is a sound technical approach that leverages known techniques (BiLSTMs, residuals) in a novel combination for this task.
- Residual learning helps training and the hierarchical matching addresses vocabulary scale and zero-shot issues.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation with ablation studies and comparisons to strong baselines.
- Integration into a simple end-to-end KBQA system shows practical applicability of the relation detector.
- Paper is well-written and explanations are clear and easy to follow.
- Individual components are not highly novel; novelty lies in their effective combination and application to KBQA.
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter search and sensitivity; a sensitivity analysis is missing.
- Evaluation limited by a simplified KBQA system; impact in more complex systems is unclear.
- Heavy reliance on external entity linkers; final performance may be bottlenecked by initial linking accuracy.
- Requests clarification on hyperparameter ranges, sensitivity to entity linker accuracy, effectiveness in more complex KBQA pipelines, and details of the residual connection implementation.
- Suggests expanding discussion and implementation details for constraint detection and adding a concrete example of hierarchical matching.
- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper with effective results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
