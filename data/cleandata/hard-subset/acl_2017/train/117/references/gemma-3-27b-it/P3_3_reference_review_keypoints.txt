- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM combining word- and relation-level representations with residual connections to improve relation detection for KBQA, showing state-of-the-art results in end-to-end pipelines.

- Contribution 1: Integrates hierarchical (word- and relation-level) relation representations with residual learning to handle large/zero-shot relation vocabularies.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that improved relation detection yields better end-to-end KBQA performance within a simple pipeline.

- Contribution 3: Provides thorough empirical evaluation including ablations and comparisons on standard KBQA datasets.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated, logically sound hierarchical matching approach addressing real KBQA challenges.

- Strength 2: Strong, statistically significant empirical improvements on relation detection and KBQA tasks.

- Strength 3: Clear, comprehensive presentation with helpful figures explaining the architecture.

- Strength 4: Practical two-step KBQA pipeline with an effective entity re-ranking step.

- Strength 5: Commendable emphasis on reproducibility (datasets to be made public).

- Weakness 1: Core components (BiLSTMs, pooling, residuals) are standard; novelty is in their application rather than new methods.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analyses.

- Weakness 3: Heavy reliance on pre-trained embeddings and external entity linkers without exploring their impact or alternatives.

- Weakness 4: Constraint detection component is under-specified and lacks detailed evaluation.

- Question 1: Request for details on hyperparameter search space and selection criteria.

- Question 2: Ask about sensitivity to different pre-trained embeddings and whether embeddings were fine-tuned.

- Question 3: Request implementation details and precision/recall results for the constraint detection module.

- Question 4: Ask for concrete examples showing how entity re-ranking disambiguated entities and improved accuracy.

- Question 5: Query whether entity linking could be integrated into an end-to-end training regime.

- Additional comment 1: Some references are incomplete (e.g., "Bao et al.,? 2016").

- Additional comment 2: Related work section is comprehensive.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with effective, well-evaluated contributions to KBQA relation detection; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
