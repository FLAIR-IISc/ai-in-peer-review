This paper tackles the problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the challenges posed by large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and the need to predict relation chains. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to better match questions to relations at different levels of granularity, and demonstrate improvements on both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA tasks.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the thorough exploration of different representations for KB relations and their integration into a hierarchical matching framework. While the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are not novel, the combination and application to the specific challenges of KBQA relation detection is a valuable contribution. A secondary contribution is the demonstration of the effectiveness of entity re-ranking as a crucial step in a KBQA pipeline, and the accompanying analysis highlighting the importance of constraint detection.

**Strengths:**

1. **Comprehensive Problem Formulation:** The paper clearly articulates the specific difficulties of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, particularly the scale of the relation vocabulary and the zero-shot learning aspect. This framing is essential for justifying the proposed approach.
2. **Detailed Model Design:** The HR-BiLSTM model is described in significant detail, and the rationale behind each design choice (hierarchical matching, residual connections, different granularity representations) is clearly explained. The ablation studies are particularly valuable in demonstrating the contribution of each component.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper consistently reports improvements on both the relation detection task and the end-to-end KBQA tasks, establishing the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The comparison to relevant baselines is appropriate.
4. **Systematic Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies are well-designed and provide insights into the importance of different components of the proposed model. The results clearly demonstrate the benefits of the hierarchical matching and residual learning mechanisms.
5. **Practical KBQA Pipeline:** The described KBQA pipeline, while simple, is effective and highlights the importance of relation detection as a core component. The inclusion of entity re-ranking and constraint detection further enhances the system's performance.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the combination of existing techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are well-established in the literature. The paper doesn't introduce any fundamentally new neural network architectures or algorithms.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but lacks details on the specific tuning process and sensitivity analysis. This makes it difficult to assess the robustness of the results and the potential for overfitting. While not a fatal flaw, more detail would be appreciated.
3. **Data Dependency and Generalizability:** The performance gains are demonstrated on specific datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP). It is unclear how well the proposed approach would generalize to other KBs or question types. Further evaluation on a wider range of datasets would be beneficial.
4. **Formatting and Presentation Issues:** The paper suffers from several minor formatting inconsistencies, including inconsistent use of bolding, some awkwardly worded sentences, and a slightly cluttered figure layout. While not major, these issues detract from the overall readability of the paper. The long list of references at the end is also not formatted consistently with the body of the paper.
5. **Overly Detailed Description of Existing Work:** Some sections describing related work feel unnecessarily lengthy and could be condensed to focus more on the specific contributions of the paper.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space, optimization algorithm, and validation strategy?
2. Have you explored the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model on other KBQA datasets beyond SimpleQuestions and WebQSP? If so, please share the results.
3. How does the performance of the model degrade with increasing length of the question or the number of relations in the chain?
4. The paper states the entity re-ranking step is crucial, yet the constraint detection step is often overlooked in previous research. Could you elaborate on why these steps are particularly important for your proposed approach?
5. The reference list is exceptionally long. Could you prioritize the most relevant papers for discussion in the related work section and condense the reference list accordingly?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a more thorough proofreading to address minor grammatical errors and formatting inconsistencies.
*   Consider adding a visualization of the attention weights learned by the BiLSTM layers to provide further insights into the model's behavior.
*   The use of abbreviations (e.g., HR-BiLSTM) is generally appropriate, but ensure they are consistently defined and used throughout the paper.
