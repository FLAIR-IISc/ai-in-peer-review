- Contribution: Hierarchical word- and relation-level representation effectively handles unseen relations and large relation vocabularies.  
- Contribution: Residual learning applied to sequence matching improves training stability and performance for relation detection.  
- Contribution: Thorough empirical evaluation with ablation studies and state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Strength: Clearly motivated problem framing targeting KBQA-specific challenges (unseen relations, large vocabularies, relation chains).  
- Strength: Novel, well-justified HR-BiLSTM architecture combining hierarchical representations and residuals.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains, especially on WebQSP, supported by ablations and comparisons to baselines.  
- Strength: Well-written, organized paper with helpful figures.  
- Strength: Demonstrates practical benefit via integration into a functional KBQA system.

- Weakness: Core components (BiLSTMs, residuals) are established techniques; novelty is mainly in their combination.  
- Weakness: Entity re-ranking (α weight) is described superficially and not thoroughly analyzed or justified.  
- Weakness: Constraint detection is simplistic (maximal overlapping character sequences) and appears limited.  
- Weakness: Strong SimpleQuestions performance may be dataset-specific; broader evaluation would strengthen claims.

- Question: How was the entity re-ranking hyperparameter α chosen, and how does it affect overall KBQA performance?  
- Question: Why was a simple string-matching approach used for constraint detection, what are its limitations, and are more advanced methods planned?  
- Question: What is the computational complexity and scalability of HR-BiLSTM with respect to KB size and question length?  
- Question: Was the CNN baseline thoroughly tuned before concluding BiLSTM is superior for long-range dependencies?

- Additional comment: Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.  
- Additional comment: Releasing code and data would improve reproducibility and impact.

- Overall: Strong, well-supported paper presenting a valuable KBQA relation-detection contribution; recommend acceptance.
