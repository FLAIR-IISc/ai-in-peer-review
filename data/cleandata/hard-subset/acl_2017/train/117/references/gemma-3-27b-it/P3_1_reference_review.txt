## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:** This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), particularly focusing on the issues of unseen relations, large relation vocabularies, and the need to predict relation chains. The authors propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that leverages both word-level and relation-level representations, combined with residual learning to improve matching between questions and relations. They demonstrate the effectiveness of this model in both relation detection and a simple end-to-end KBQA system, achieving state-of-the-art results on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) datasets.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Hierarchical Relation Representation:** The combination of word-level and relation-level representations is a sensible and effective approach to address the challenges of unseen relations and the large vocabulary size inherent in KBQA. This is more than simply concatenating embeddings; the hierarchical structure allows the model to capture different levels of abstraction.
2. **Empirical Demonstration of Residual Learning in this Context:** The application of residual learning to sequence matching, specifically for this KBQA relation detection task, is a noteworthy contribution. It demonstrably improves training stability and performance, and the justification for its benefit in this context (allowing for deeper, more abstract representations) is convincing.
3. **Solid Experimental Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough empirical evaluation, including ablation studies and comparisons to strong baselines on established KBQA datasets. This solidifies the claims regarding the effectiveness of their approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges specific to relation detection in KBQA compared to general relation extraction, setting a strong foundation for their work. The motivation for addressing these challenges – handling unseen relations, large vocabularies, and relation chains – is well-explained.
2. **Novel and Well-Justified Model Architecture:** The HR-BiLSTM model is novel and logically designed. The use of both word-level and relation-level representations addresses a core problem, and the residual connections are well-motivated for improving training and representation quality.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:**  The results consistently outperform existing baselines on both datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. The ablation studies provide valuable insights into the contribution of each component. The reported gains on WebQSP, in particular, are significant.
4. **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the proposed approach and understand the experimental results. The inclusion of Figure 1 is helpful for visualizing the KBQA process.
5. **Practical System Integration:** Demonstrating the impact of improved relation detection within a functional KBQA system adds practical value to the research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* of techniques (hierarchical representation + residual learning) is novel in this specific context, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are well-established in the deep learning literature. The paper could benefit from a more nuanced discussion of how their work builds upon and extends existing techniques.
2. **Entity Re-Ranking Details:** The description of the entity re-ranking step (Section 4.1) feels somewhat superficial.  The hyperparameter *α* controlling the weighting between the linker score and relation score is mentioned but not thoroughly analyzed or justified.  A more detailed analysis of the impact of this re-ranking step would be beneficial.
3. **Constrained Constraint Detection:**  The performance gains from constraint detection are mentioned as significant, but the approach itself appears somewhat limited ("maximum overlapping sequence of characters"). This feels like a potentially valuable area for future exploration, but the current implementation doesn't seem to fully leverage the potential.  The claim that the approach currently only benefits from 'entity linking on a KB sub-graph' feels vague.
4. **Dataset Specificity:** The strong performance on SimpleQuestions might be partially attributed to the dataset's characteristics.  Exploring the model’s performance on more diverse and challenging KBQA datasets would further strengthen the findings.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the entity re-ranking step? Specifically, how was the hyperparameter *α* chosen, and how does its value affect the overall performance of the KBQA system?  Were any alternative weighting schemes explored?
2.  The constraint detection step relies on a relatively simple string matching approach.  Could you elaborate on why this method was chosen, and what are the limitations of this approach?  Are there plans to explore more sophisticated constraint detection techniques in future work?
3.  Could you provide a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity of the HR-BiLSTM model, especially in relation to the size of the KB and the length of the questions?  How does the model scale to larger knowledge bases?
4.  You mention the performance drop when replacing BiLSTM with CNN.  Was any effort made to tune the CNN architecture specifically to match the performance of the BiLSTM?  Could you elaborate on why you believe the LSTM is better suited for capturing long-range dependencies in this task?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The reference list is extensive and well-formatted.
*   The inclusion of code and data would further enhance the reproducibility and impact of this work.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. The proposed HR-BiLSTM model is well-designed, empirically supported, and addresses important challenges in relation detection.  While some aspects of the work could be further elaborated, the overall quality of the paper is high, and I recommend acceptance.
