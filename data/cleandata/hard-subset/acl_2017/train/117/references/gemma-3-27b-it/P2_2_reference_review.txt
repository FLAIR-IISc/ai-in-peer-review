## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), particularly focusing on the issues of handling a large number of relations, zero-shot learning, and complex multi-relation queries. The authors propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that combines relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning for improved matching between questions and relations.  They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on both relation detection and KBQA tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Demonstration of Hierarchical Matching Benefit:** The paper convincingly demonstrates that combining relation-level and word-level representations *does* improve performance in KB relation detection, especially in the context of open-domain KBs. This is a practical and useful finding.
2. **Effective Application of Residual Learning:** The authors successfully adapt residual learning techniques (from computer vision) to the sequence matching task, showing it improves training stability and allows the model to learn deeper representations, crucial for complex relation chains.
3. **Solid Experimental Evaluation:** The paper provides comprehensive experiments on standard KBQA datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) and includes thorough ablation studies, validating the effectiveness of each proposed component.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the unique challenges of relation detection in KBQA compared to traditional relation extraction, particularly highlighting the scale and zero-shot aspects.
2. **Well-Designed Model:** The HR-BiLSTM architecture is logically sound and appears well-suited to the task. The combination of different granularity relation representations and residual connections addresses key issues.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The results consistently outperform strong baselines on both relation detection and KBQA tasks, providing strong evidence of the method's effectiveness.  The ablation studies further solidify this conclusion.
4. **Comprehensive Experiments:** The use of two distinct datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) and careful ablation analysis enhances the credibility of the findings. The experiments provide a granular understanding of the impact of each component.
5. **Practical KBQA Pipeline:** The authors don't just focus on relation detection in isolation, but integrate it into a simple yet effective KBQA pipeline, showcasing the real-world applicability of their work.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* of techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, word embeddings) are well-established in the literature. The paper doesn't introduce fundamentally new building blocks. The novelty lies primarily in the application and integration.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but doesn't provide extensive details about the search process or the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter choices.  A more detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning would strengthen the analysis.
3. **Dependency on Existing Entity Linkers:** The performance of the overall KBQA system is heavily reliant on the quality of the initial entity linker.  While the authors address this with re-ranking, further exploration of the interaction between entity linking and relation detection would be beneficial.  How much improvement is *specifically* attributable to the relation detection improvement, versus improvements from the re-ranking step?
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** While quantitative results are strong, a qualitative error analysis would provide valuable insights into the types of questions where the model still struggles and potential avenues for future improvement. What kinds of relations are still hard to detect?



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and the methods used?  Specifically, how sensitive are the results to the weighting factor 'α' used in the entity re-ranking step?
2.  Can you quantify the improvement gained from the entity re-ranking step independently of the relation detection improvement?  For example, could you run the KBQA pipeline with a baseline relation detector and compare the results with and without the entity re-ranking?
3.  Could you provide some examples of questions where the model fails, along with an analysis of the reasons for the failure?  Are there specific types of relations or question structures that are particularly challenging?
4.  The paper mentions the possibility of using beam search for query generation. Have you explored this, and if so, what were the results?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*   The references are comprehensive and relevant.
*   Consider adding a section in the related work discussing recent advances in contrastive learning, as that approach could potentially be applied to relation detection.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the combination and application to the specific problem of relation detection are effective and well-supported by empirical evidence. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides a solid foundation for future research.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
