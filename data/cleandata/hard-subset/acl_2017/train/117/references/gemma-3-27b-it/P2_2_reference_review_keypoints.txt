- Paper tackles relation detection in KBQA, addressing many relations, zero-shot cases, and multi-relation queries using a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM).  
- HR-BiLSTM combines relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning for improved question–relation matching.  
- Empirically shows hierarchical matching helps relation detection in open-domain KBs.  
- Adapts residual learning to sequence matching, improving training stability and deeper representation learning.  
- Strong experimental results and ablations on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, achieving state-of-the-art performance.  
- Clear problem motivation highlighting scale and zero-shot challenges unique to KBQA.  
- Well-designed model architecture that logically integrates multi-granularity relation representations and residual connections.  
- Comprehensive experiments across two datasets and thorough ablation studies increase credibility.  
- Integrates relation detection into a practical KBQA pipeline, demonstrating real-world applicability.  
- Core components (BiLSTMs, residuals, embeddings) are standard; novelty is mainly in their integration and application.  
- Limited detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, weakening reproducibility and analysis.  
- KBQA performance depends on external entity linker quality; interaction between linking and relation detection needs deeper exploration.  
- Lacks qualitative error analysis to identify stubborn failure cases and challenging relation types.  
- Requests more detail on hyperparameter search and sensitivity, especially the re-ranking weight α.  
- Asks for ablation isolating the impact of entity re-ranking versus relation detection improvements.  
- Requests example failure cases with analysis of causes and difficult relation/question types.  
- Inquires whether beam search for query generation was explored and what the outcomes were.  
- Suggests adding related work on contrastive learning as a potential alternative approach.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and references are comprehensive.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
