- Summary: Tackles relation detection for KBQA and proposes HR-BiLSTM combining relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning, showing improved relation-detection and end-to-end KBQA results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Contribution 1: Empirically shows hierarchical relation representation (relation-level + word-level) improves performance, especially with large/unseen relation sets.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates residual learning effectively aids deep BiLSTM sequence matching and hierarchical question–relation matching.

- Contribution 3: Builds a practical KBQA pipeline using the improved relation detector and provides ablation studies to isolate component effects.

- Strength 1: Clear problem definition and motivation distinguishing KBQA relation detection from general relation extraction.

- Strength 2: Strong experimental results and ablation studies showing consistent improvement over baselines.

- Strength 3: Practical system with sufficient implementation details and focus on relation detection by reusing entity linking.

- Strength 4: Thorough analysis and ablation that clarify why the model works.

- Strength 5: Well-written and organized presentation.

- Weakness 1: Individual components (hierarchical matching, residuals) are not novel; novelty mainly in their combination/application.

- Weakness 2: Results sensitive to hyperparameter tuning; tuning process and impact (especially alpha in re-ranking) are under-detailed.

- Weakness 3: Reliance on existing entity linking limits scope; no joint end-to-end optimization of entity linking and relation detection.

- Weakness 4: Lacks in-depth error analysis to illuminate model limitations and failure cases.

- Question 1: Please detail the hyperparameter tuning process and sensitivity of results to the alpha re-ranking parameter.

- Question 2: What error types and example failure cases does HR-BiLSTM produce, and why?

- Question 3: How does the entity re-ranking step’s benefit vary with the quality of initial entity linking?

- Question 4: Do you plan to explore joint entity linking and relation detection in future work?

- Additional comments: References are comprehensive; figures/tables are clear; include discussion of HR-BiLSTM computational cost versus simpler baselines.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-supported paper that merits acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
