## Peer Review: Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection within the context of Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors argue that existing relation detection methods, often borrowed from general Information Extraction, struggle with the scale and zero-shot nature of relations in KBs. They propose a novel approach, Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM), that combines word-level and relation-level representations with residual learning to improve relation detection accuracy. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, showing improvements in both relation detection and overall KBQA performance.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Hierarchical Relation Representation:** The combination of word-level and relation-level representations, while not entirely novel, is well-motivated and effectively implemented.  The HR-BiLSTM demonstrates a clear ability to leverage different granularities of relation information.
2. **Demonstrated Impact on KBQA:** The paper convincingly shows that improved relation detection directly translates into better performance on the end KBQA task. This is a crucial contribution, as many KBQA advancements focus on components *other* than relation detection.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The thorough experimental setup, including ablation studies and comparisons to strong baselines, provides a solid validation of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The HR-BiLSTM consistently outperforms baselines on both relation detection and KBQA tasks. The improvements, while not massive, are statistically significant and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
2. **Well-Motivated Approach:**  The paper clearly articulates the challenges specific to KB relation detection (scale, zero-shot learning) and provides a logical justification for each component of the proposed HR-BiLSTM.
3. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies effectively isolate the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM (residual connections, hierarchical matching, different granularity representations). This helps to understand *why* the method works.
4. **Practical KBQA System:** The described KBQA system is relatively simple and highlights the direct application of the improved relation detection model. This showcases the real-world potential of the research.
5. **Clear and Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The diagrams and tables are clear and informative.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:**  While the *combination* of techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, word embeddings) are not particularly novel. The paper would benefit from a more nuanced discussion of the novelty relative to existing work.
2. **Dependence on Pre-existing Entity Linker:**  The KBQA performance is heavily reliant on the quality of the initial entity linking. The paper acknowledges this but doesn't deeply investigate the interaction between entity linking errors and relation detection. A more robust evaluation would consider varying entity linker performance.
3. **Focus on Limited Datasets:** While SimpleQuestions and WebQSP are standard benchmarks, they may not fully represent the diversity of real-world KBQA scenarios. Evaluating on more complex and diverse datasets would strengthen the generalizability of the findings.
4. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but does not provide sufficient detail about the search process or the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings.  This lack of detail makes it difficult to assess the robustness of the approach.
5. **Lack of Error Analysis:** A more detailed error analysis of the relation detection model would be valuable. Understanding *which* relations are difficult to detect and *why* could inform future research directions.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more detail about the hyperparameter tuning process? Specifically, what search strategies were used, and how sensitive were the results to different hyperparameter values?
2.  The paper mentions the entity linking step as a potential bottleneck. Could you elaborate on how the performance of the relation detection model is affected by errors in entity linking? Are there any strategies to mitigate the impact of these errors?
3.  The ablation studies are thorough, but could you comment on the computational cost of adding the HR-BiLSTM layer? Is it significantly more expensive than using a simpler relation detection model?
4.  What types of relations does your model struggle with the most?  Could you provide specific examples of relations where the HR-BiLSTM performs poorly?
5.  Could you discuss how this approach might be extended to handle more complex KB structures (e.g., multi-hop reasoning, complex entity types)?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The citation formatting is inconsistent (e.g., some citations include "pages" while others do not).
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the study and potential future research directions.




Overall, this is a solid paper that presents a well-motivated and effective approach to relation detection for KBQA. The strengths of the paper (strong empirical results, thorough evaluation) outweigh the weaknesses, and I believe it represents a valuable contribution to the field.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
