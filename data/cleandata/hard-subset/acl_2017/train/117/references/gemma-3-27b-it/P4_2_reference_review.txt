This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection for knowledge base question answering (KBQA). The authors identify key difficulties in applying general relation detection methods to KBQA, namely the large number of relations, the prevalence of zero-shot learning, and the need to handle chains of relations. They propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to address these issues, and demonstrate improvements on both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA tasks.

**Contributions:**

1. **Novel Hierarchical Relation Representation:** The core contribution is the HR-BiLSTM model that leverages both relation-level and word-level representations and combines them with residual connections. This addresses the challenges of data sparsity and capturing varying levels of abstraction in relation names.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on both relation detection (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP) and end-to-end KBQA tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach. The ablation studies effectively isolate the contributions of the different components.
3. **Practical KBQA Pipeline:**  The authors demonstrate a complete KBQA system leveraging their improved relation detector, including entity re-ranking and constraint detection, and highlighting its integration into a practical pipeline.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the specific challenges of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, motivating the need for specialized methods.
2. **Technically Sound Approach:** The HR-BiLSTM model is well-motivated and technically sound. The use of residual connections to facilitate training of deeper networks is a valuable addition. The hierarchical matching approach is intuitive and addresses the different granularity levels in relation names and questions.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors provide a thorough experimental evaluation, including ablation studies to analyze the impact of each component of their model. The evaluation on both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA tasks strengthens the validity of the findings.
4. **Reproducibility:** The authors provide details about datasets and implementation making the work more easily reproducible.
5. **Impactful Results:** The reported performance improvements on benchmark datasets demonstrate the potential of the proposed approach to advance the field of KBQA.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are not novel in themselves. The novelty lies primarily in their application and combination within the specific context of KB relation detection.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions tuning several hyperparameters. More details about the sensitivity of the results to these hyperparameters would be helpful. It is unclear how robust the performance gains are to different hyperparameter settings.
3. **Limited Analysis of Failure Cases:** While the ablation studies provide insight into the contribution of different components, a more detailed analysis of failure cases would be beneficial. Understanding the types of questions or relations where the model still struggles could guide future research.
4. **Reliance on Existing Entity Linkers:** The KBQA system relies on existing entity linkers. The performance of the system is therefore tied to the quality of the entity linking results. A more comprehensive evaluation that considers the impact of different entity linkers would be valuable.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details about the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameters, particularly the learning rate and the weight assigned to the relation detection score in the entity re-ranking step?
2. Can you elaborate on the types of questions or relations where the model still struggles? Are there specific patterns or characteristics of these failure cases?
3.  What is the computational cost of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler models like a single-layer BiLSTM? Does the performance gain justify the increased computational complexity?
4. How does the performance change with different pre-trained word embeddings?

**Additional Comments:**

* The formatting of the references is inconsistent.
* Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the study and potential directions for future work.
* The notation used in the equations could be clarified. For example, it's unclear what the dimensions of the vectors being concatenated are.
