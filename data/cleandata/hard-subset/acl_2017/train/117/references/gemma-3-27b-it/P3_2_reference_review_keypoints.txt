- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM for hierarchical word- and relation-level matching, improving relation detection and end-to-end KBQA (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP); entity re-ranking further boosts performance.

- Contribution 1: Empirically demonstrates hierarchical matching effectively handles unseen relations and benefits from combining word- and relation-level representations.

- Contribution 2: Provides a strong, well-evaluated baseline with comprehensive ablation studies and comparisons to recent work.

- Contribution 3: Shows relation-aware entity re-ranking meaningfully improves KBQA entity disambiguation and overall accuracy.

- Strength 1: Clearly defines and motivates KB relation detection challenges (large vocabularies, zero-shot relations).

- Strength 2: Robust experimental design using established datasets and existing entity linkers for fair comparison.

- Strength 3: Strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art relation detection and KBQA performance.

- Strength 4: Practical, simple KBQA pipeline focused on improving relation detection rather than complex end-to-end models.

- Strength 5: Thorough analysis and insightful ablations (e.g., why residual learning helps more on WebQSP).

- Weakness 1: Core architecture (BiLSTMs, residuals, pooling) is incremental rather than novel.

- Weakness 2: Limited exploration of alternative hierarchical matching strategies (more advanced attention or structure-aware methods).

- Weakness 3: System performance depends on external entity linker; limited analysis of linker error impact or joint training approaches.

- Weakness 4: Limited qualitative/error-case analysis of model failures (e.g., similar relations, complex questions).

- Question 1: Please provide a breakdown of how entity re-ranking helps (percentage of questions improved and changes in top-K candidates).

- Question 2: What is the performance drop when constraint detection is disabled, and how was the constraint threshold chosen?

- Question 3: Have you considered joint training of the entity linker and relation detector to improve disambiguation?

- Question 4: Why use 300-d pre-trained word embeddings and were alternative embeddings or methods evaluated?

- Additional comment 1: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive and relevant.

- Additional comment 3: Code and datasets should be released to aid reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with incremental architectural novelty but strong empirical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
