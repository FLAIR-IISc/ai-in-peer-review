This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically focusing on the difficulties introduced by large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and the need to identify relation chains. The authors propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that combines relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning to improve matching between questions and relations. The model is integrated into a simple KBQA pipeline and demonstrates strong performance on both single-relation and multi-relation datasets.

**Contributions:**

1. **Novel Relation Representation & Matching:** The core contribution lies in the HR-BiLSTM model that effectively leverages both relation-level and word-level representations of relations, combined with a residual learning mechanism for improved hierarchical matching with questions. This addresses the challenges of unseen relations and complex relation chains.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on both the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness for KB relation detection and its positive impact on overall KBQA performance.
3. **Systematic Analysis & Ablation Studies:** The paper includes thorough ablation studies that clearly demonstrate the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model (residual learning, hierarchical matching, different relation representations), providing valuable insights into the model’s behavior.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of relation detection in the context of KBQA, effectively differentiating it from general relation extraction tasks. The motivation for addressing these challenges is well-presented.
2. **Sound Technical Approach:** The HR-BiLSTM model is a well-motivated and technically sound approach. The combination of hierarchical matching, residual learning, and the use of both relation-level and word-level representations is a logical and promising strategy.
3. **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experimental setup is comprehensive, including comparisons to strong baselines and detailed ablation studies. The inclusion of both relation detection and end-to-end KBQA evaluation provides a complete picture of the model’s performance.
4. **Thorough Analysis:** The analysis of the results is insightful, providing explanations for the observed performance differences and highlighting the importance of different model components. The discussion of the impact of residual learning and the benefits of deeper architectures is particularly valuable.
5. **Reproducibility:** The authors mention making the datasets publicly available, which greatly enhances the reproducibility of the results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the combination of techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) are not entirely novel in themselves. The paper would benefit from a more explicit discussion of how the specific combination of these components addresses the unique challenges of KB relation detection.
2. **Dependency on Existing Entity Linkers:** The KBQA pipeline relies on an existing entity linker, which could be a potential bottleneck. The performance of the system is therefore dependent on the accuracy of the entity linker. While the entity re-ranking step attempts to mitigate this, a more thorough analysis of the impact of different entity linkers would be beneficial.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but lacks details on the specific tuning process. A more detailed description of the hyperparameter search strategy and the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings would be helpful.
4. **Constraint Detection Results:** The paper mentions the constraint detection step is crucial, but the performance of this step itself is not fully detailed. Providing more specific results on constraint detection accuracy would provide further insights.
5. **Presentation Clarity:** Some sections (e.g., Figure 2) could benefit from improved clarity and conciseness. The explanations of the shortcut connections could be more intuitive.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and the criteria used for selecting the best hyperparameters?
2. How sensitive are the results to the performance of the initial entity linker? Have you experimented with different entity linkers, and how does the performance vary?
3. Could you provide a more detailed breakdown of the performance of the constraint detection step, including precision, recall, and F1-score?
4.  The paper states that deeper architectures are beneficial for WebQSP. Is there a point where adding more layers leads to diminishing returns or overfitting? What strategies were used to prevent overfitting when using deeper models?
5. How does the performance of the re-ranking step vary depending on the ambiguity of the entity mentions in the questions?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The related work section is comprehensive but could be slightly more focused on the most relevant prior work.
*   The notation could be made more consistent throughout the paper.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential directions for future research.



Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. The proposed HR-BiLSTM model is a promising approach for addressing the challenges of relation detection, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. Despite some minor weaknesses, the paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and provides valuable insights into the problem of KB relation detection. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
