## Peer Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection within the context of Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors identify key difficulties of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, namely the large number of relations, the potential for zero-shot learning, and the need to handle relation chains. They propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to improve relation detection by matching questions to both word-level and relation-level representations.  They demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach by integrating it into a simple KBQA pipeline and achieving state-of-the-art results on both single and multi-relation KBQA tasks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP).

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical demonstration of the importance of hierarchical relation representation for KBQA:** The paper convincingly shows that considering both word-level and relation-level representations significantly improves performance on KB relation detection and, subsequently, the KBQA task. This is a valuable insight for the KBQA community.
2. **Effective integration of residual learning for improved training stability and performance:** The application of residual connections to the hierarchical matching process appears to be a key factor in the model’s success, particularly on the more complex WebQSP dataset.
3. **A strong baseline and thorough evaluation:** The authors provide a well-implemented baseline and comprehensive experimental results, including ablation studies, which clearly demonstrate the contribution of each component.



**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Performance:** The HR-BiLSTM model consistently outperforms existing baselines on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, establishing its effectiveness for KB relation detection. The results on WebQSP are particularly notable given the dataset’s complexity.
2. **Well-Motivated Approach:** The authors clearly articulate the challenges specific to KB relation detection and justify their design choices accordingly.  The rationale for using both word-level and relation-level representations is compelling and supported by the experimental results.
3. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies provide valuable insights into the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model, demonstrating the importance of hierarchical matching and residual learning.
4. **Clear and Reproducible Methodology:** The paper is well-written and clearly describes the model architecture, training procedure, and experimental setup.  The use of publicly available datasets and baselines facilitates reproducibility.
5. **Practical KBQA Integration:**  The authors don't just focus on relation detection in isolation but demonstrate its value by integrating it into a complete KBQA pipeline, showcasing its practical impact.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Individual Components:** While the *combination* of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, max-pooling, residual connections) are well-established techniques in NLP. The paper’s novelty lies primarily in their application to the specific problem of KB relation detection. While a strong result is achieved, a more groundbreaking architectural innovation would be desirable.
2. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:** The performance of the KBQA system is heavily reliant on the quality of the initial entity linking step.  The paper acknowledges this, but the improvement gained from the relation detector could be overshadowed by limitations in the entity linker. Further investigation into how the relation detector could *improve* entity linking would be valuable.
3. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper primarily focuses on quantitative results. A more detailed error analysis, identifying common failure cases and potential areas for improvement, would provide valuable insights into the limitations of the approach. For example, what types of relations are still difficult to detect? Are there specific question structures that consistently lead to errors?
4. **Hyperparameter Tuning Details:** While the paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, the specific ranges and search strategies used are not detailed.  Providing more information about the tuning process would increase the reproducibility and transparency of the results.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Entity Linking Improvement:** Have you explored ways to leverage the information from the relation detection model to improve the performance of the entity linker? For example, could relation confidence scores be used to re-rank entity candidates proposed by the linker?
2. **Error Analysis Detail:** Can you provide a more detailed error analysis, categorizing the types of errors made by the relation detection model (e.g., incorrect relation type, failure to detect a relation chain, ambiguous questions)?
3. **Computational Cost:** What is the computational cost of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to the baseline models, both during training and inference?  Is the increased accuracy worth the added computational complexity?
4. **Data Sensitivity:** How sensitive are the results to the size and quality of the training data?  Would the performance gains observed in the experiments still hold true with smaller or more noisy datasets?
5. **Generalizability:** How well would the proposed approach generalize to other KBs with different characteristics (e.g., different schemas, different relation distributions)?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a discussion of related work on transfer learning or few-shot learning, as these techniques could be relevant to addressing the zero-shot relation detection problem.
*   Consider including a qualitative example of a question where the HR-BiLSTM model correctly identifies the relation(s) while a baseline model fails.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a well-motivated and effective approach to KB relation detection. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning, coupled with the thorough evaluation, makes a significant contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
