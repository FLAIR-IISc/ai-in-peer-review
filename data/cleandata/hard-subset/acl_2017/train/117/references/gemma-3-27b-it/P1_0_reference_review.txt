## Peer Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically focusing on issues arising from large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and the need to predict relation chains. The authors propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that leverages both relation-level and word-level representations, combined with a residual learning approach to facilitate training. They demonstrate improvements in relation detection accuracy and, subsequently, in the performance of a simple KBQA system on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) tasks.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Investigation of Relation Representation Granularity:** The work provides a solid empirical analysis of the trade-offs between using relation names as single tokens versus word sequences for relation detection in a KBQA setting. Demonstrating the benefits of combining both approaches is a valuable insight.
2. **Effective Application of Residual Learning to Hierarchical Matching:** The authors successfully adapt residual learning from computer vision to the context of hierarchical sequence matching for KBQA, showing its effectiveness in improving training stability and representational capacity.
3. **Demonstration of End-to-End Improvement:** The authors not only improve relation detection in isolation but also successfully demonstrate that this improvement translates to gains in KBQA performance, validating the importance of this subtask.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The HR-BiLSTM model consistently outperforms strong baselines on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, establishing a clear performance gain. The ablation studies provide convincing evidence for the effectiveness of each component (hierarchical matching, residual learning, different relation granularities).
2. **Clear Problem Formulation and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the specific challenges of relation detection in KBQA – the scale of the relation vocabulary, the potential for unseen relations, and the need for chain prediction – providing a strong justification for the proposed approach.
3. **Well-Designed Experiments and Analysis:** The experimental setup is thorough, using established datasets and evaluation metrics. The ablation studies are particularly insightful, isolating the impact of different design choices. The inclusion of error analysis (though limited) adds to the paper's strength.
4. **Practical KBQA System Integration:** The integration of the improved relation detector into a simple KBQA pipeline demonstrates the real-world applicability of the research.  The description of the system is clear and well-structured.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the combination of techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, attention, residual learning) are well-established in the NLP literature. The core novelty lies in the application and adaptation of these techniques to the specific challenges of KB relation detection, but the conceptual breakthrough isn't substantial.
2. **Ablation Study Depth:** While present, the ablation study could be more comprehensive. For example, exploring different ways of combining the relation-level and word-level representations (beyond the weighted sum and residual connection) or examining the impact of different pre-trained word embeddings would strengthen the analysis.
3. **Analysis of Failure Cases:** The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the types of questions or relations where the model still struggles. Understanding these failure cases could provide valuable insights for future research. A qualitative analysis of incorrect predictions would be helpful.
4. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:** The KBQA system relies heavily on the performance of existing entity linkers. While acknowledging this limitation, the paper doesn’t explore the potential for joint learning or feedback between the relation detection and entity linking components.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Failure Analysis:** Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the types of questions or relations where your model performs poorly?  Please provide examples of specific failure cases.
2. **Joint Learning:** Have you considered exploring joint learning of the relation detector and the entity linker, or a feedback mechanism where the relation detection results inform the entity linking process?  If so, what were the challenges or limitations?
3. **Computational Cost:**  What is the computational overhead associated with the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler baselines? (e.g., training time, inference time, model size).
4. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** How sensitive are the performance gains to the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the weighting factor (α) in the KBQA system?  Are there specific ranges where the model is most robust?

**Additional Comments:**

* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
* The reference list is comprehensive.
* It would be helpful to include the code and trained models for reproducibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. While the individual components may not be entirely novel, the effective combination of techniques and the thorough empirical evaluation demonstrate a significant improvement in relation detection performance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
