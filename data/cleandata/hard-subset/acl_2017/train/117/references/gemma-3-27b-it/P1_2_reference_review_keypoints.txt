- Proposes HR-BiLSTM for KBQA relation detection using hierarchical (relation- and word-level) representations plus residual learning, achieving state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Empirically shows hierarchical matching and residual connections significantly improve relation detection via ablation studies.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation within a full KBQA pipeline on two standard datasets, validating practical effectiveness.  
- Clearly frames KBQA-specific challenges: many relation types, zero-shot scenarios, and relation chains.  
- Strong empirical results with statistically significant gains, especially on the harder WebQSP dataset.  
- Well-motivated model design and insightful analysis explaining why hierarchical matching and residual learning suit KBQA relation detection.  
- Thorough ablation studies that clarify each component’s contribution.  
- Practical integration into a complete KBQA system, not just isolated relation detection.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful figures (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Limited novelty: core components (BiLSTMs, residuals) are established; contribution is mainly their combination/application.  
- Heavy reliance on external entity linker; entity re-ranking yields only modest improvement.  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analysis.  
- Lacks a detailed error analysis to identify failure modes and guide future work.  
- Requests from reviewer: more hyperparameter tuning details; isolated performance and impact of constraint detection; potential gains from joint training of entity linker and relation detector; explanation for why residual learning helps with many/rare relations.  
- Additional notes: comparison to S-MART is constrained by use of its entity linking; recommend discussing computational complexity versus simpler methods; suggest adding limitations and future work section.
