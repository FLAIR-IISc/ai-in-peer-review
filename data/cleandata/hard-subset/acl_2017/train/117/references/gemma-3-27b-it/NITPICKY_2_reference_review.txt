This paper addresses the problem of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the challenges posed by large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation questions. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to better match questions and relations at different levels of granularity. They demonstrate improvements on both relation detection and KBQA tasks.

The primary contribution of this work is the empirical demonstration that a hierarchical representation, combined with residual connections, can improve relation detection performance in a KBQA setting. While the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) are not novel in themselves, their combination and application to this specific KBQA task appears effective. A secondary contribution is the comprehensive experimental evaluation on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, including ablation studies, which help to understand the importance of different components of the proposed model.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The HR-BiLSTM consistently outperforms several strong baselines on both relation detection and the end-to-end KBQA tasks. The improvements, while not massive, are statistically significant and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
2. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The paper includes detailed ablation studies that systematically evaluate the impact of different components of the HR-BiLSTM. This provides valuable insights into the importance of each component and helps to justify the design choices made by the authors. The results clearly highlight the benefits of both hierarchical matching and residual learning.
3. **Relevant Task & Problem Setting:** The focus on KB relation detection within the context of KBQA is highly relevant. The authors correctly identify the specific challenges of this task – large vocabulary, zero-shot learning, multi-relation questions – and their approach directly addresses these challenges.
4. **Clear Presentation & Reproducibility:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The methodology is clearly described, and the experimental setup is well-defined. Providing details about the datasets and baseline implementations aids reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty in Core Components:** The core components of the proposed model – BiLSTMs, residual connections, and hierarchical matching – are well-established techniques in deep learning. The paper does not introduce any fundamentally new algorithmic innovations. The novelty lies primarily in the specific combination and application of these techniques to the KBQA problem.
2. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** While the ablation studies provide quantitative results, the paper lacks a qualitative analysis of error cases. Understanding *why* the model fails on certain examples could provide valuable insights for future improvements. Are there specific types of relations or questions that consistently pose a challenge?
3. **Overly Complex System Setup:** The final KBQA system described in Section 4 relies on several components (entity linking, relation re-ranking, relation detection, constraint detection) and hyperparameters. While this isn't inherently a weakness, it makes it difficult to isolate the contribution of the HR-BiLSTM to the overall performance. A simpler, more focused evaluation would be more convincing.
4. **Minor Formatting Issues & Redundancy:** The paper contains minor formatting inconsistencies (e.g., inconsistent spacing around equations) and some redundant phrasing. For example, the introduction repeats the same points several times. A more careful editing pass is needed. The references section is also poorly formatted and requires cleanup.
5. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The impact of hyperparameter tuning isn’t discussed sufficiently. Were different hyperparameters used for different datasets? How sensitive is the performance to the choice of hyperparameters?

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the error cases made by the HR-BiLSTM? Specifically, are there any patterns or common themes in the examples where the model fails?
2.  The paper mentions the S-MART entity linker results were downloaded. Were any modifications made to the entity linking results or the linker itself? If so, please detail them.
3.  How sensitive is the performance of the HR-BiLSTM to the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the learning rate and the size of the hidden states?
4.  Given that the core components of the HR-BiLSTM are well-established techniques, can you elaborate on the rationale for choosing these specific components and how they interact to address the challenges of KB relation detection?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The references are poorly formatted.
*   Consider adding a table summarizing the hyperparameters used for each experiment.
*   The description of constraint detection in Section 4.4 is somewhat vague. More details would be helpful.
*  The repeated mentioning of the "topic entity" feels excessive.



Overall, this is a solid paper with a clear contribution and strong experimental results. However, the lack of novelty in the core components and the absence of a more detailed error analysis limit its impact. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend rejection, but encourage the authors to revise and resubmit, paying particular attention to error analysis and refining the presentation.
