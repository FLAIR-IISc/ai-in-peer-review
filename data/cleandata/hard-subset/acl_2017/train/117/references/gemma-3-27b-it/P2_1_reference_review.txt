## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors identify key difficulties distinguishing KB relation detection from standard relation extraction (large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, chain prediction) and propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to address these issues.  The model leverages both relation-level and word-level representations and achieves state-of-the-art results on both relation detection and KBQA tasks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP).

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Demonstration of the Difficulty of KB Relation Detection:** The paper clearly articulates why KB relation detection is significantly harder than typical relation extraction, specifically highlighting the scale and zero-shot nature of the problem. This framing is a valuable contribution.
2. **HR-BiLSTM Architecture:**  The proposed hierarchical BiLSTM with residual connections provides a reasonable approach to modeling relations at different levels of granularity, and the ablation studies demonstrate the effectiveness of both the hierarchical structure and residual learning.
3. **Strong Experimental Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on two benchmark datasets, combined with detailed ablation studies, provides strong evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper does an excellent job of establishing the specific challenges of relation detection *within the context of KBQA*. It moves beyond simply applying standard relation extraction techniques and explains why modifications are needed.
2. **Sound Technical Approach:** The HR-BiLSTM architecture is well-motivated.  Combining word-level and relation-level representations and utilizing residual connections feels like a natural and effective way to tackle the challenges outlined in the introduction. The justification for each design choice is well-explained.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors provide thorough experimental results, including ablation studies that clearly demonstrate the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model. Evaluation on both relation detection *and* the end-to-end KBQA task strengthens the findings.
4. **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient details regarding the experimental setup and datasets used.  (Though further details on hyperparameter tuning could be helpful – see weaknesses).
5. **Practical System Improvement:** The inclusion of entity re-ranking based on relation detection scores demonstrates the practical applicability of the method to improve a complete KBQA pipeline.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Components:** While the *combination* of techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) are all well-established in the NLP literature. The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of how this work builds upon and differentiates itself from existing techniques.
2. **Hyperparameter Tuning Details:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but lacks specific details on the search space and methodology.  Providing more information would increase confidence in the reported results and facilitate reproducibility.  Were grid search or more advanced optimization techniques used?
3. **Lack of Error Analysis:**  While the ablation studies are helpful, the paper would be strengthened by a qualitative error analysis. Examining *where* the model fails (e.g., specific types of relations, ambiguous questions) would provide valuable insights for future work. Are there systematic errors the model makes?
4. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:**  The KBQA pipeline relies heavily on the performance of an external entity linker. While acknowledging this, the paper doesn’t sufficiently explore the potential impact of linker errors on the overall system performance.  Could the relation detection component partially compensate for poor entity linking?



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more detail on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and optimization method used?
2.  Can you share examples of failure cases – specific questions and relations where the HR-BiLSTM model performs poorly? What are the common characteristics of these failures?
3.  How sensitive are the results to the performance of the external entity linker?  Have you explored methods to integrate relation detection more tightly with entity linking to potentially mitigate linker errors?
4.  You mention that constraint detection is crucial for your system.  Could you elaborate on why your approach is more effective at utilizing constraint detection compared to previous work (Yih et al., 2015)?

**Additional Comments:**

* The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.
* The extensive list of references is appreciated.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept.**  Despite the incremental nature of some of the individual components, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field of KBQA. The clear articulation of the challenges, the well-motivated architecture, and the strong experimental results make this a worthwhile addition to the literature.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper.
