- Addresses KBQA relation detection challenges (many relations, zero-shot, relation chains) and proposes HR-BiLSTM for hierarchical word- and relation-level matching.  
- Empirically demonstrates hierarchical relation representations improve relation detection and KBQA performance.  
- Applies residual learning to hierarchical matching, improving training stability and performance, especially on complex datasets.  
- Provides a strong baseline and thorough evaluation, including ablations that isolate component contributions.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP when integrated into a KBQA pipeline.  
- Model consistently outperforms baselines, with notable gains on the challenging WebQSP dataset.  
- Approach is well-motivated and clearly justifies using both word-level and relation-level representations.  
- Ablation studies give valuable insight into the importance of hierarchical matching and residual connections.  
- Paper is clearly written, reproducible, and uses public datasets and baselines.  
- Demonstrates practical value by integrating relation detection into a complete KBQA system.  
- Individual components (BiLSTMs, max-pooling, residuals) are standard, so novelty is mainly in their application and combination.  
- System performance heavily depends on external entity linking; relation detection gains may be limited by linker quality.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis to characterize common failure modes (hard relation types, chain detection, ambiguous questions).  
- Hyperparameter tuning details (ranges and search strategies) are insufficiently reported for full reproducibility.  
- Suggests exploring using relation detector outputs to re-rank or improve entity linking.  
- Requests more detailed error categorization (incorrect relation type, chain failures, ambiguity).  
- Asks for computational cost comparison (training/inference) to justify added complexity.  
- Questions sensitivity to training data size/quality and robustness on smaller/noisier datasets.  
- Asks about generalizability to other KBs with different schemas and relation distributions.  
- Recommends discussing transfer/few-shot learning literature for zero-shot relation detection and adding qualitative examples where HR-BiLSTM outperforms baselines.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper with significant empirical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
