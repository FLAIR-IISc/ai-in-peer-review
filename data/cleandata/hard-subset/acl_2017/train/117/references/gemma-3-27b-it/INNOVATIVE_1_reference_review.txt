This paper tackles the challenging problem of relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically addressing the issues of large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation questions. The core idea – hierarchical matching between questions and relations using a residual BiLSTM – is a solid and potentially impactful contribution. While the methodology isn't groundbreaking in its individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections), the *combination* and application to this specific KBQA problem feels novel and well-motivated. The empirical results demonstrate a clear improvement over existing baselines on both relation detection and the overall KBQA task.

**Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Novel Hierarchical Matching Approach:** The HR-BiLSTM architecture, combining word-level and relation-level representations with residual connections, offers a compelling way to handle the complexities of KB relation detection, particularly the need to generalize to unseen relations and handle varying phrase lengths. This is the strongest contribution.
2. **Empirical Validation on KBQA:** The authors convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach within a full KBQA pipeline, achieving state-of-the-art results on established datasets. This lends credence to the practical utility of the proposed method.
3. **Clear Problem Formulation & Motivation:** The paper does a good job of clearly articulating the unique challenges of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, providing a strong rationale for the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The consistent improvements across datasets and ablation studies provide compelling evidence for the effectiveness of the HR-BiLSTM model. The gains, while not massive, are statistically significant and demonstrate a clear benefit.
2. **Well-Motivated Approach:** The problem is well-defined, and the proposed solution directly addresses the core challenges of KB relation detection: dealing with a vast relation vocabulary, unseen relations, and complex queries.
3. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies effectively isolate the contributions of different components of the HR-BiLSTM, providing insights into the model's behavior and the importance of each element.  The finding that residual learning is particularly impactful on WebQSP is insightful.
4. **Practical KBQA Integration:** The paper doesn’t just focus on relation detection in isolation. Integrating it into a complete, albeit simplified, KBQA system demonstrates the real-world applicability of the research.
5. **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is well-written, logically organized, and easy to follow. The figures and tables are informative and well-presented.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Methodological Incrementalism:** While the combination of existing techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are not novel in themselves. The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of *why* this specific combination works so well for KB relation detection.
2. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks detailed error analysis. Understanding *where* the model still struggles could provide valuable insights for future research.  What types of relations are particularly difficult to detect? What are common error patterns?
3. **Simplistic KBQA System:**  The KBQA system is intentionally simple, but this limits the scope of the evaluation.  It’s unclear how well the relation detection component would perform in a more complex and realistic KBQA environment.
4. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:** Performance relies heavily on the accuracy of the initial entity linking step. While the entity reranking helps, the system remains vulnerable to errors introduced by the linker.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more detailed explanation of *why* the hierarchical matching approach, specifically with residual connections, is particularly effective for KB relation detection? What are the underlying mechanisms that enable it to overcome the challenges of large vocabularies and zero-shot learning?
2.  Could you share some examples of questions where the model fails and analyze the reasons for the failure? This would provide valuable insights into the model's limitations and potential areas for improvement.
3.  How sensitive is the performance of the system to the accuracy of the initial entity linker? Have you considered incorporating a joint learning approach where relation detection and entity linking are trained simultaneously?
4.  The paper mentions the potential for improvement with a more sophisticated constraint detection step. Can you elaborate on possible directions for improving this component?
