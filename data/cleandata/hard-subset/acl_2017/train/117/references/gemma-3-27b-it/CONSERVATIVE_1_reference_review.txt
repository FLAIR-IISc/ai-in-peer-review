This paper addresses the problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on challenges arising from large relation vocabularies and zero-shot learning scenarios. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) with residual learning to better match questions to relations at different levels of granularity. They demonstrate improvements on both relation detection and the overall KBQA task on the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets.

The primary contribution of this work is a reasonably well-engineered model incorporating established techniques (BiLSTMs, residual connections) to address a specific, practical challenge within KBQA. While the individual components aren’t novel, their combination and application to this problem space are a worthwhile effort. A secondary contribution is the comprehensive experimental evaluation against competitive baselines, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also provides a clear analysis of the challenges in KB relation detection, highlighting the differences between it and general relation extraction.

**Strengths:**

1. **Solid Experimental Validation:** The paper presents thorough experiments on two standard KBQA datasets, comparing against established baselines. The consistent improvements reported on both relation detection and the end-to-end KBQA task provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed HR-BiLSTM model. The ablation studies help to dissect the contribution of each component.
2. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The authors clearly articulate the challenges specific to KB relation detection, such as the large number of relations and the prevalence of unseen relations. This contextualization justifies the need for specialized approaches beyond general relation extraction techniques.
3. **Reasonable Engineering:** The proposed HR-BiLSTM leverages established deep learning architectures in a sensible way. The integration of residual connections to facilitate training of deeper networks and the hierarchical matching approach targeting different levels of granularity are logical and well-motivated.
4. **Practical System Integration:** The authors demonstrate how the improved relation detection model can be integrated into a complete KBQA pipeline, contributing to a practical and usable system. This contrasts with some research that focuses solely on individual components.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty:** The core components of the proposed method – BiLSTMs and residual connections – are well-established in the literature. While the combination is effective, the paper doesn't introduce any fundamentally new algorithmic ideas.  The hierarchical matching, while a sensible approach, isn’t particularly groundbreaking.
2. **Limited Analysis of Error Cases:** While the ablation studies are helpful, the paper lacks a detailed error analysis. Understanding *why* the model fails on specific examples could offer valuable insights for future improvements. Are there particular types of relations or questions where the model struggles?
3. **Reliance on Existing Entity Linkers:** The KBQA system heavily relies on the performance of an existing entity linker.  The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t sufficiently address the potential limitations of this dependency. Improving the entity linker could significantly impact overall performance, and this is outside the scope of the work.
4. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but lacks detail regarding the search process. It is unclear how sensitive the results are to specific hyperparameter choices, and a more systematic investigation could be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, identifying common failure cases for the HR-BiLSTM model?
2. How sensitive are the reported results to different hyperparameter settings?  Did you perform a grid search or use a more sophisticated optimization method?
3. Given the reliance on the entity linker, how might the proposed relation detection model be adapted to be more robust to errors in entity linking?
4. Could you discuss the computational cost of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to the baseline models?



**Additional Comments:**
The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The use of figures and tables is effective in presenting the key concepts and results. The references are comprehensive and relevant.  While the work does not present a radical departure from existing techniques, it represents a solid contribution to the field of KBQA through careful engineering and thorough evaluation.
