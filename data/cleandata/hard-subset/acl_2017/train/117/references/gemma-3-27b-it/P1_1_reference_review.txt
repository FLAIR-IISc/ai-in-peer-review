## Peer Review: Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection within the context of Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors identify key difficulties stemming from the scale of relations in KBs, the prevalence of zero-shot learning scenarios, and the need to handle chains of relations. They propose a hierarchical BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model with residual learning to better capture different granularities of relation information and improve matching with question representations. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on both relation detection and KBQA tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and competitive results on WebQSP.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Demonstration of the Importance of Hierarchical Relation Representation:** The authors convincingly show the benefits of considering both word-level and relation-level representations for KB relation detection, particularly in the setting of large-scale KBs and limited training data.  The ablation studies clearly demonstrate the importance of combining these granularities.
2. **Effective Application of Residual Learning to Sequence Matching:** Adapting residual connections to the context of hierarchical sequence matching proves beneficial, particularly for deeper models handling more complex relation scenarios. This provides a practical improvement to model training and representation learning.
3. **A Strong Baseline KBQA System:** The paper presents a clear and well-implemented KBQA pipeline that effectively integrates the improved relation detection component, establishing a new strong baseline for future work.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the specific challenges of relation detection in KBQA compared to generic relation extraction. The rationale behind the proposed approach is well-motivated and grounded in these challenges.
2. **Thorough Experimental Evaluation:** The authors conduct a comprehensive set of experiments, including ablation studies, on two benchmark datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP). This provides strong evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
3. **Reproducibility:** The authors mention making datasets publicly available, which greatly improves the reproducibility of their results.
4. **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is well-structured, easy to follow, and clearly presents the technical details of the proposed model and experiments. The inclusion of Figure 1 effectively illustrates the KBQA process and motivates the need for improved relation detection.
5. **Practical KBQA Pipeline:** The described KBQA pipeline is relatively simple and demonstrates a clear path for integrating improved relation detection into a functioning system.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are not novel. The paper's contribution lies primarily in the effective *application* of these established techniques to the specific problem of KB relation detection.  This is a valuable contribution, but it's important to acknowledge the incremental nature of the novelty.
2. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linking:** The performance of the KBQA system is heavily reliant on the accuracy of the initial entity linker. While the authors address this with entity reranking, the initial linker's errors still propagate through the pipeline. A more robust entity linking component would likely yield even greater improvements.
3. **Ablation Study Granularity:** While the ablation studies are comprehensive, further analysis could be beneficial. For example, exploring different configurations of the residual connections (e.g., applying them at different layers) or investigating the effect of different embedding sizes could provide further insights.
4. **Discussion of Error Analysis:** While the results are impressive, a more in-depth discussion of the types of errors the model still makes would be helpful. What kinds of relations or questions are particularly challenging? This would inform future research directions.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Novelty Discussion:**  You position your work as an improvement over existing relation detection methods. Could you explicitly compare your approach with recent state-of-the-art methods beyond the listed baselines (e.g., methods leveraging pre-trained language models like BERT)?  How does your approach differ, and what specific advantages does it offer?
2. **Entity Linking Sensitivity:** How sensitive is the performance of your KBQA system to variations in the performance of the initial entity linker? Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of different linker error rates on the overall system accuracy?
3. **Error Analysis Details:** Could you provide examples of questions where the system fails and discuss the likely reasons for these failures? What types of relations are particularly challenging for the model to detect?
4. **Computational Cost:** What is the computational overhead introduced by the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler relation detection approaches? Are there any scalability concerns for very large KBs?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The use of Table 1 to illustrate the different granularities of relation representation is particularly effective.
*   The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work in the area of pre-trained language models for relation extraction and KBQA.
*   Consider including a brief discussion of the limitations of the current approach and potential future research directions.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. While the individual components of the proposed approach are not entirely novel, the effective combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning, coupled with a thorough experimental evaluation, demonstrates a significant improvement in relation detection and KBQA performance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses and questions outlined above.
