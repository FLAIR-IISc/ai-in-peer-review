## Peer Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection within the context of Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors identify key challenges specific to KBQA relation detection – a large number of relation types, the prevalence of zero-shot learning scenarios, and the need to handle chains of relations – and propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model to address these issues. The HR-BiLSTM leverages both relation-level and word-level representations, combined with a residual learning approach, to improve matching between questions and relations. The paper also presents a simple KBQA system built around the improved relation detector and demonstrates state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Demonstration of the Importance of Hierarchical Matching & Residual Learning for KB Relation Detection:** The paper convincingly demonstrates through ablation studies that considering both word-level and relation-level representations, coupled with residual connections, significantly improves performance in this specific task. This provides practical insights for designing effective relation detectors for KBQA.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation on KBQA Tasks:** The authors provide a thorough evaluation on two standard KBQA datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), showcasing the effectiveness of their approach within a complete KBQA pipeline. The competitive results on these benchmarks support the validity of their method.
3. **Clear Identification and Framing of KBQA-Specific Challenges:** The paper accurately identifies the unique challenges of relation detection within the KBQA setting, differentiating it from general relation extraction. This contextualization strengthens the motivation for their proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The HR-BiLSTM consistently outperforms baselines on both relation detection and KBQA tasks. The gains are statistically significant, particularly on the more challenging WebQSP dataset. This demonstrates the practical value of the proposed approach.
2. **Well-Motivated Design Choices:** The rationale behind using hierarchical matching and residual learning is clearly articulated and supported by insightful analysis. The authors effectively explain why these techniques are particularly well-suited to address the challenges of KBQA relation detection.
3. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies in Section 5.2 provide valuable insights into the contribution of different components of the HR-BiLSTM model. This helps to understand the relative importance of each design choice.
4. **Practical System Integration:**  The paper doesn’t just focus on relation detection in isolation, but integrates it into a complete KBQA pipeline, demonstrating its usability and effectiveness in a real-world scenario.
5. **Clear and Well-Organized Presentation:** The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.  The inclusion of Figure 1 is particularly helpful in illustrating the KBQA process and the role of relation detection.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components themselves (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are well-established techniques in deep learning. The novelty lies in their application and adaptation to the specific problem of KBQA relation detection, but this might be viewed as incremental.
2. **Reliance on Existing Entity Linkers:** The performance of the KBQA system is heavily dependent on the accuracy of the entity linker. While the authors acknowledge this and incorporate an entity re-ranking step, the system's overall performance could be further improved by integrating a more robust entity linker or jointly training the entity linker and relation detector.  The performance gain from the re-ranking is somewhat modest.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but provides limited details on the specific tuning process. It would be helpful to have a more in-depth discussion of the sensitivity of the model to different hyperparameter settings and the methods used to optimize them.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** A more detailed error analysis would provide valuable insights into the types of questions and relations that the model struggles with. This could inform future research directions and lead to further improvements.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and optimization strategy used?
2.  The paper mentions that constraint detection is crucial for the system. Could you elaborate on the performance of the constraint detection module in isolation, and what types of constraints are most effective at improving KBQA accuracy?
3.  Given the reliance on existing entity linkers, do you foresee significant performance gains from jointly training the entity linker and the relation detector?  Have you explored this approach?
4.   The ablation study shows the impact of residual learning.  Is there an explanation as to *why* residual learning is particularly helpful in this context? Does it relate to the large number of relation types and the difficulty of learning effective representations for rare relations?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The comparison to S-MART on WebQSP is somewhat limited as the current implementation relies on their pre-existing entity linking results.
*   It would be helpful to include a discussion of the computational complexity of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler approaches.
*  Consider adding a section discussing the limitations of the current approach and future research directions.
