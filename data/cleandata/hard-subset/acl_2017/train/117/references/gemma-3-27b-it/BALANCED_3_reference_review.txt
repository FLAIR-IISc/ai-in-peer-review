This paper addresses the challenging task of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on issues like a large number of relations and zero-shot learning. The authors propose a hierarchical BiLSTM model with residual learning to better match questions to relations, considering both word-level and relation-level representations. They also present a simple yet effective KBQA system incorporating this improved relation detector, achieving strong performance on both single- and multi-relation tasks.

**Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM for Relation Detection:** The core contribution is the proposed HR-BiLSTM model. The hierarchical matching approach, considering both word-level and relation-level representations, coupled with residual learning, is a sound and well-motivated approach for tackling the complexities of KB relation detection.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:**  The paper demonstrates convincingly that the proposed model outperforms existing baselines on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets. This provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the approach. The ablation studies are also well-conducted and insightful.
3. **Effective KBQA System:** The authors demonstrate that improved relation detection directly translates into better performance in a complete KBQA system.  The system, despite its simplicity, achieves state-of-the-art results, showcasing the importance of the relation detection component.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges specific to KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction. The rationale for the proposed approach is well-explained and convincing.
* **Technical Soundness:** The HR-BiLSTM model is a well-designed architecture that leverages the strengths of BiLSTMs and residual learning. The use of both word-level and relation-level representations is a thoughtful approach.
* **Thorough Evaluation:** The experimental setup is comprehensive, with clear comparisons to strong baselines and insightful ablation studies. The use of two distinct datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) strengthens the validity of the results.
* **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The explanations are clear and concise, and the figures are helpful.
* **Reproducibility:** The authors mention making the datasets publicly available, which is a positive step towards reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty of Components:** While the combination of techniques is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) are not entirely novel. The primary contribution lies in the specific application and integration of these techniques to the challenging KB relation detection problem. This isn't necessarily a fatal flaw, but it reduces the overall impact of the work.
* **Hyperparameter Tuning Details:** The description of hyperparameter tuning is somewhat limited. Providing more details about the search space and optimization process would strengthen the results.
* **Error Analysis:** The paper would benefit from a more detailed error analysis. Understanding the types of errors the model makes could provide valuable insights for future work. For example, are there specific types of relations that are consistently misidentified?
* **Dependence on Pre-trained Embeddings:** The model relies heavily on pre-trained word embeddings.  The impact of different embedding choices isn't thoroughly explored.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process? Specifically, what search space was used for each hyperparameter, and what optimization algorithm was employed?
2.  Can you provide a more detailed error analysis, identifying the types of errors the model makes? Are there specific relation types that are consistently misclassified?
3.  Did you experiment with different pre-trained word embeddings, and if so, what was the impact on performance?
4.  The paper mentions an entity re-ranking step in the KBQA system. How sensitive is the performance of the KBQA system to the accuracy of the entity linker?  Would using a more accurate, but computationally expensive, entity linker further improve the results?

**Additional Comments:**

*   It would be helpful to include a table comparing the computational cost of the proposed model to the baseline models.
*   The mention of using S-MART entity linking results should include a proper citation.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential directions for future research.



Overall, this is a strong paper that addresses an important problem in KBQA. The proposed HR-BiLSTM model is effective and well-motivated, and the experimental results are convincing. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the combination and application to this specific task are valuable contributions. I recommend acceptance, with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
