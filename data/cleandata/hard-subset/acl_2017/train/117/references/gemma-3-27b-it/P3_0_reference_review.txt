## Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors identify key difficulties in adapting general relation extraction techniques to the KBQA setting – a large number of relations, the prevalence of zero-shot learning, and the need to detect relation chains. They propose a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that combines word-level and relation-level representations, utilizing residual connections to improve training and representation quality.  The paper demonstrates improved performance on both relation detection and the end-to-end KBQA task on benchmark datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP).

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Empirical Demonstration of KBQA-Specific Challenges:** The paper clearly articulates and empirically demonstrates the specific challenges of relation detection *within the context of KBQA* – the scale of relation spaces and the zero-shot learning problem – highlighting why directly applying general RE techniques is insufficient. This framing is valuable.
2. **Effective Hierarchical Relation Representation:**  The combination of word-level and relation-level representations, along with the hierarchical matching approach, appears to be a significant factor in performance improvement. This allows the model to leverage both fine-grained and coarse-grained information about relations.
3. **Practical KBQA System with State-of-the-Art Results:** While the core contribution is the relation detection model, the paper goes further to demonstrate its effectiveness within a full KBQA pipeline, achieving competitive results on standard benchmarks.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The results consistently outperform strong baselines on both relation detection and the KBQA task. The ablation studies convincingly demonstrate the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model, particularly the residual connections. The performance gains on WebQSP, a more complex multi-relation task, are particularly noteworthy.
2. **Well-Motivated Approach:** The motivation for addressing the specific challenges of KBQA relation detection is clear and compelling. The authors effectively argue why standard approaches fall short and justify their design choices.
3. **Clear and Thorough Presentation:** The paper is well-written, clearly structured, and provides sufficient detail to understand the proposed method. The inclusion of Figure 2 is helpful in visualizing the HR-BiLSTM architecture.  The related work section is also comprehensive.
4. **Practical System Integration:**  The authors demonstrate a full KBQA system built upon their relation detection module, showing the practical applicability of their approach.  The entity re-ranking step is a sensible addition.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* of techniques (hierarchical matching, residual connections, BiLSTMs) is effective, the individual components themselves are not entirely novel. BiLSTMs and residual connections are well-established techniques in NLP.  The main novelty lies in their specific application and combination for this KBQA problem.  The paper could benefit from more discussion about *why* this specific combination works well for KBQA.
2. **Sensitivity to Hyperparameters:** The experimental section mentions extensive hyperparameter tuning.  While necessary, it would be helpful to understand the sensitivity of the model to these hyperparameters. A brief discussion about the robustness of the results to different settings would be valuable.
3. **Constraint Detection as a Black Box:**  The description of the constraint detection step (Section 4.4) feels somewhat underdeveloped. While it's acknowledged as important, the details of the implementation and the specifics of the matching score calculation are light.  More information about this component would be helpful.
4. **Limited Error Analysis:**  The paper would be strengthened by a more detailed error analysis.  What types of questions/relations does the model still struggle with?  Are there specific patterns in the errors that could inform future research?

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Novelty Elaboration:** Could you elaborate on why the specific combination of hierarchical matching, residual connections, and BiLSTMs is particularly well-suited for addressing the challenges of KBQA relation detection?  What specific aspects of the KBQA problem necessitate this architecture?
2. **Hyperparameter Robustness:** Can you provide some insights into the sensitivity of the model's performance to different hyperparameter settings?  How robust are the results to variations in learning rate, embedding dimension, etc.?
3. **Constraint Detection Details:**  Could you provide more detail about the implementation of the constraint detection step? Specifically, how is the matching score between question n-grams and entity names calculated? What is the threshold *v* set to, and how is it determined?
4. **Error Analysis:** Have you conducted any error analysis to identify common failure cases for the model? If so, could you share some examples and discuss potential avenues for improvement?



**Additional Comments:**

* The use of existing entity linking results is a reasonable choice, given the focus on relation detection.
* The inclusion of baselines from both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP is appreciated.
* The paper is generally well-organized and easy to follow.



Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of KBQA. The authors have identified a key challenge, proposed a well-motivated and effective solution, and demonstrated strong empirical results. While the individual components may not be entirely novel, the combination and application to the KBQA problem are significant. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance.
