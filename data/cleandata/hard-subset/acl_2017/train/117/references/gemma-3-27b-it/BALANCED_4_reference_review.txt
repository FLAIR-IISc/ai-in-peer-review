This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for knowledge base question answering (KBQA), specifically targeting the issues of handling large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning, and multi-relation questions. The authors propose an improved relation detection model based on hierarchical matching between questions and relations, incorporating residual learning to facilitate training. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by integrating it into a simple KBQA system, achieving state-of-the-art results on both single- and multi-relation QA tasks.

**Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) for Relation Detection:** This is the most significant contribution. The model effectively addresses the challenges posed by large knowledge bases and unseen relations by leveraging both relation-level and word-level representations, combined with residual learning for improved training and representation learning.
2. **End-to-End KBQA System with Improved Relation Detection:**  The integration of the proposed relation detection model into a complete KBQA pipeline demonstrates its practical utility and impact on overall system performance. The inclusion of entity re-ranking adds a valuable step to improve robustness.
3. **Thorough Experimental Evaluation & Ablation Studies:** The comprehensive evaluation on established benchmarks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) alongside insightful ablation studies provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of each component of the proposed approach.  The ablation studies clearly demonstrate the benefit of the hierarchical matching and residual learning mechanisms.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Performance:** The proposed HR-BiLSTM model consistently outperforms baseline methods and achieves state-of-the-art results on both relation detection and KBQA tasks. This demonstrates the model's effectiveness and practical significance.
2. **Addresses Key Challenges:** The paper directly tackles critical issues in KBQA relation detection - the large number of relations, the zero-shot learning problem, and the need to handle multi-relation questions – providing a tailored solution.
3. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method. The inclusion of illustrative figures and tables enhances understanding.
4. **Rigorous Experimental Design:** The experimental setup is well-defined, with clear baselines and appropriate evaluation metrics. The ablation studies are particularly strong, providing valuable insights into the contribution of each component of the proposed model.
5. **Insightful Analysis:** The discussion of the results and the comparison with previous work are insightful, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the advantages of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components themselves (BiLSTMs, max-pooling, residual connections) are well-established techniques in deep learning. The novelty primarily lies in their specific application and combination within the context of KBQA relation detection.
2. **Dependency on Pre-trained Embeddings:** The model relies on pre-trained word embeddings. The performance might be affected by the quality and domain relevance of these embeddings. An investigation of the impact of different embedding sources could be beneficial.
3. **Complexity of Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but doesn’t elaborate on the resources or methods used. A detailed discussion of the hyperparameter tuning process and its impact on performance would strengthen the paper.
4. **Constraint Detection as an Optional Step:** The constraint detection step is presented as optional, and its impact isn't consistently demonstrated across all experiments.  Further investigation into the optimal integration of constraint detection with the relation detection component would be valuable.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model to the quality and source of the pre-trained word embeddings? Did you experiment with different embedding sources, and if so, what were the results?
2. Could you elaborate on the hyperparameter tuning process? What strategies were used to optimize the hyperparameters, and how much computational resources were required?
3. The paper states constraint detection is optional. Could you explore architectures or training strategies where constraint detection is more tightly integrated into the relation detection process, rather than as a separate post-processing step?
4. While you have strong performance on both datasets, are there specific types of questions or relations where the model still struggles?  Providing examples of failure cases would be useful.

**Additional Comments:**

* The references are comprehensive and well-formatted.
* The discussion of related work is thorough and provides a good overview of the current state-of-the-art.
* Consider including a discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed model.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to KBQA relation detection. The proposed HR-BiLSTM model achieves state-of-the-art results and addresses key challenges in this area. While the individual components of the model are not entirely novel, their combination and application within the KBQA context are well-motivated and demonstrate significant improvements. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.
