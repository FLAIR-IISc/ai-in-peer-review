- Addresses relation detection in KBQA, highlighting differences from standard relation extraction (large vocabularies, zero-shot, chain prediction).  
- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical BiLSTM with residual connections combining relation-level and word-level representations.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on relation detection and end-to-end KBQA (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP).  
- Contribution: clearly empirically demonstrates the unique difficulty of KB relation detection.  
- Contribution: introduces HR-BiLSTM architecture that models multi-granularity relation representations.  
- Contribution: provides strong experimental evidence and ablation studies supporting the approach.  
- Strength: clear problem definition and motivation tailored to KBQA needs.  
- Strength: technically sound and well-motivated model design (hierarchy + residuals).  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including ablations and end-to-end KBQA results.  
- Strength: reasonably reproducible with dataset and setup details provided.  
- Strength: demonstrates practical pipeline improvement via entity re-ranking based on relation scores.  
- Weakness: individual components (BiLSTMs, residuals, hierarchical matching) are not novel—novelty is mainly in their combination.  
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter tuning details (search space and optimization methodology unspecified).  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative error analysis to show where the model fails and systematic error types.  
- Weakness: heavy dependence on external entity linker without analysis of linker-error impact or integration strategies.  
- Questions: provide detailed hyperparameter tuning process and search methodology.  
- Questions: share concrete failure cases and common characteristics of model errors.  
- Questions: quantify sensitivity to entity linker performance and explore tighter integration with relation detection.  
- Questions: clarify why their constraint detection approach outperforms prior work (e.g., Yih et al., 2015).  
- Additional: paper is well-written and includes extensive references.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — solid contribution despite incremental components, would be stronger with more analysis and hyperparameter details.
