- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM combining relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning to improve relation detection and KBQA performance on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Contribution 1: Empirically analyzes relation representation granularity and shows benefits of combining relation-name and word-sequence representations.

- Contribution 2: Adapts residual learning to hierarchical sequence matching (HR-BiLSTM) to improve training stability and representation.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates that improved relation detection yields end-to-end gains in a simple KBQA pipeline.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical results with HR-BiLSTM outperforming baselines and informative ablation studies.

- Strength 2: Clear problem formulation and motivation addressing large relation vocabularies, zero-shot cases, and relation chains.

- Strength 3: Well-designed experiments using standard datasets, metrics, and useful (if limited) error analysis.

- Strength 4: Practical integration into a KBQA system showing real-world applicability.

- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty—uses established components (BiLSTM, attention, residuals) with incremental adaptation.

- Weakness 2: Ablation study could be deeper (e.g., more combination strategies, different embeddings).

- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis of failure cases and qualitative error examples.

- Weakness 4: Heavy reliance on existing entity linkers without exploring joint learning or feedback mechanisms.

- Question 1: Please provide detailed failure-case analysis and examples of problematic questions/relations.

- Question 2: Have you considered joint learning or feedback between relation detection and entity linking, and what challenges arise?

- Question 3: What are the computational costs vs. simpler baselines (training time, inference time, model size)?

- Question 4: How sensitive are results to hyperparameters, especially the KBQA weighting factor α, and what robust ranges exist?

- Additional: Paper is well-written, references are comprehensive, and code/models for reproducibility would be helpful.

- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper with meaningful empirical gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
