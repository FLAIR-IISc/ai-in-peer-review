- Addresses relation detection in KBQA, proposing a hierarchical BiLSTM with residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) to handle large KB relation spaces, zero-shot cases, and relation chains, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and competitive WebQSP results.
- Contribution: Empirically shows benefits of combining word-level and relation-level representations for KB relation detection, supported by ablations.
- Contribution: Adapts residual learning to hierarchical sequence matching, improving training and deeper model performance.
- Contribution: Presents a strong, well-integrated KBQA pipeline that serves as a new baseline.
- Strength: Clear problem motivation distinguishing KBQA relation detection from generic relation extraction.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across datasets with comprehensive ablation studies.
- Strength: Improves reproducibility by making datasets publicly available.
- Strength: Well-written, organized paper with effective figures illustrating the approach.
- Strength: Practical and simple KBQA pipeline demonstrating integration of the relation detector.
- Weakness: Core components (BiLSTMs, residuals) are not novel; novelty is mainly in their effective application, making the contribution incremental.
- Weakness: System performance heavily depends on initial entity linking; linker errors still propagate despite reranking.
- Weakness: Ablation studies could be more granular (e.g., different residual connection placements, embedding sizes).
- Weakness: Lacks in-depth error analysis describing remaining failure modes and challenging relation types.
- Question: How does the approach compare to recent SOTA methods using pre-trained LMs (e.g., BERT), and what advantages does it offer?
- Question: How sensitive is overall KBQA accuracy to different entity linker error rates?
- Question: Can authors provide concrete error examples and discuss which relations/questions the model struggles with?
- Question: What is the computational overhead and scalability of HR-BiLSTM compared to simpler detectors for very large KBs?
- Comment: Table 1 effectively illustrates relation representation granularities.
- Comment: Paper should discuss pre-trained LM related work for relation extraction and KBQA.
- Comment: Include limitations and potential future research directions.
- Overall: Strong paper with valuable empirical contributions and thorough evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses and questions.
