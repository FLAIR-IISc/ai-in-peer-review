- Core contribution: HR-BiLSTM that hierarchically matches questions to relations at relation-level and word-level with residual connections.  
- Pipeline: simple two-step KBQA pipeline using relation-aware entity re-ranking, per-entity relation detection with mention replacement, and a lightweight constraint detector.  
- Empirical results: improves relation detection by ~2–3 points and achieves new SOTA on SimpleQuestions and competitive WebQSP performance.  
- Contribution claim 1: Practical hierarchical matching architecture combining relation- and word-level signals with residuals and ablations.  
- Contribution claim 2: Effective, lightweight two-step pipeline where relation detection aids entity disambiguation without heavy joint inference.  
- Contribution claim 3: Empirical analysis across single- and multi-hop datasets with ablations showing robustness.  
- Strength: Clear modeling of relation granularity showing complementary value of relation names and words, validated by ablations.  
- Strength: Solid accuracy gains and consistent benefits from residual connections, especially on multi-hop WebQSP.  
- Strength: Simple pipeline yields tangible end-to-end QA improvements; relation-aware re-ranking materially reduces entity ambiguity.  
- Strength: Attention to multi-hop relation chains; LSTM encoders outperform CNNs for longer dependencies and scale to chains without graph modules.  
- Strength: Reasonable reproducibility effort—reimplemented baselines, varied ablations, and use of released resources/off-the-shelf linkers.  
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—components (relation-level/word-level mix, residuals) are established; novelty mainly in their combination.  
- Weakness: No targeted zero-shot evaluation (seen vs unseen relations) to support zero-shot generalization claims.  
- Weakness: Incomplete baseline comparisons—missing Golub & He (2016) on SQ, CFO (Dai et al., 2016) on WQ, and stronger cross-attention baselines.  
- Weakness: Ambiguities in architecture and loss details (which residual variant used, missing margin, relation-LSTM initialization) need clarification.  
- Weakness: No discussion of efficiency/scalability—candidate pool sizes, runtime, and pruning strategies are unreported.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps—WebQSP relation detection dataset “to be released” and several hyperparameters/details omitted.  
- Question: Provide seen vs unseen relation accuracy and the relative contribution of word-level vs relation-level signals.  
- Question: Which residual variant and loss margin were used; clarify negative sampling and batch construction.  
- Question: Add comparisons to Golub & He (2016), CFO (2016), and cross-attention baselines; explain the “top-3” ensemble differences and score combination.  
- Question: Report average candidate relation counts, inference time, sensitivity to K/K’, and any pruning heuristics.  
- Question: Provide precision/recall of the constraint detector, sensitivity to overlap threshold, and comparison to a neural mention linker.  
- Question: When will WQSP relation detection data, code, and models be released for reproducibility?
