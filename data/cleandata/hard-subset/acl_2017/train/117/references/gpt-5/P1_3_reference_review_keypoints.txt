- Targets relation detection in KBQA, arguing relations need multi-granularity (relation-name and word-level) and questions need multi-abstraction representations.  
- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM that encodes relations at word and relation-name token levels, encodes questions with a 2-layer BiLSTM, and uses residual shortcuts.  
- Integrates relation detection into a simple KBQA pipeline with relation-aware entity re-ranking and optional constraint detection.  
- Reports 2–3% absolute gains in relation detection (SimpleQuestions 93.3%, WebQSP 82.53%) and state-of-the-art or competitive end-to-end QA.  
- Primary contribution: hierarchical residual matching architecture fusing relation-name and word-level signals with multi-layer question representations.  
- Secondary contribution: practical KBQA pipeline that uses relation detection twice (entity re-ranking then per-entity relation scoring).  
- Tertiary contribution: empirical ablations showing both granularities are necessary, residuals help, and constraint detection helps when upstream selection is strong.  
- Strength: Strong, statistically significant empirical improvements and convincing ablation results.  
- Strength: Relation-aware entity re-ranking effectively reduces entity ambiguity and boosts end-task performance.  
- Strength: Clear motivation for modelling granularity and abstraction; design choices validated by experiments.  
- Strength: Simple, low-engineering pipeline achieving competitive SOTA, aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength: Comprehensive comparisons and ablations against competitive baselines and architectural variants.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—mainly a judicious combination of known ideas, with limited comparison to modern cross-attention or pre-trained contextual encoders.  
- Weakness: Weak validation of zero-shot/unseen relation claims—no explicit seen/unseen split or frequency-based performance analysis.  
- Weakness: Incomplete algorithmic details and clarity (missing margin, weights alpha/lambda, candidate sizes K/K'/l, unclear initialization, typos).  
- Weakness: End-task comparisons uneven due to differing KB resources, ensembling, and simplistic constraint detection not compared to stronger text-evidence models.  
- Weakness: Efficiency and scalability not addressed—no runtime, memory, or candidate-pool size reporting for large KBs.  
- Question: Please report performance by seen vs unseen relations, relation frequency buckets, and single vs multi-relation chains.  
- Question: Provide exact values and tuning ranges for ranking loss margin, alpha, lambda, and K/K'/l, plus sensitivity analyses.  
- Question: Explain relation-name tokenization (underscores, camelCase, aliases) and handling of relation chains (separate tokens vs concatenation).  
- Question: Clarify the LSTM initialization ("initialize relation sequence LSTMs with final state of word sequence") and ablate its effect.  
- Question: Report typical candidate pool sizes, end-to-end inference time, and scalability to high-degree nodes.  
- Question: Could re-ranking be biased for entities with similar relation neighborhoods; evaluate on ambiguous-name subsets?  
- Question: Will you release the constructed WQ relation detection dataset and code for reproducibility?
