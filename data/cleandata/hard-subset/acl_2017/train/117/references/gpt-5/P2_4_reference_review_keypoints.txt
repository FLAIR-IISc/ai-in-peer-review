- Paper targets KB relation detection for KBQA, addressing large relation vocabularies, zero-shot relations, and relation chains.
- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM matching questions to relation-level and word-level tokens with residual connections across question layers.
- Integrates model into a simple KBQA pipeline that re-ranks entity candidates by relation scores, runs relation detection conditioned on candidate entities, and applies a lightweight constraint heuristic.
- Main contribution 1: multi-granularity relation detection combining relation- and word-level representations with residual learning, giving consistent 2–3% gains over baselines.
- Main contribution 2: a simple two-step KBQA pipeline that substantially mitigates entity linking errors via relation-based re-ranking without heavy engineering.
- Main contribution 3: thorough empirical evaluation with ablations (granularity, residual vs alternatives, encoder types) on SQ and WQ, plus a new WebQSP relation-detection formulation.
- Strength: Clear motivation for multi-granularity matching; empirics show combining both levels outperforms either alone.
- Strength: Residual connections improve optimization and accuracy, especially on longer/more complex WebQSP instances.
- Strength: Strong empirical results — SOTA or competitive relation detection and end-to-end QA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.
- Strength: Practical, low-engineering KBQA pipeline; entity re-ranking and simple constraint detection deliver meaningful gains for practitioners.
- Strength: Solid ablations and baseline re-implementations bolster credibility of claimed improvements.
- Weakness: Incremental novelty — combination of known ideas rather than a fundamentally new paradigm.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on high-quality, human-readable relation names; unclear robustness to opaque/noisy labels or low lexical overlap domains.
- Weakness: Missing reporting details and hyperparameters (α, λ, l, K, K′), initialization specifics, and runtime/memory/scalability analysis.
- Weakness: Comparisons on WebQSP lack some recent neural baselines and embedding-based zero-shot methods; constraint heuristic may confound end-to-end gains.
- Weakness: Zero-shot/generalization claims not directly quantified (no seen vs unseen relation performance reported).
- Question: Please report seen vs unseen relation performance on SimpleQuestions and frequency of unseen core-chain components on WebQSP.
- Question: Clarify how relation sequence LSTMs are initialized from word sequence final states and which sequence initializes which.
- Question: Provide chosen values and sensitivity for α, λ, l, K, K′, and average candidate set sizes before/after re-ranking.
- Question: How does the method scale to larger KBs and chains longer than 2 hops; any experiments beyond length-2?
- Question: Will you release the WebQSP relation-detection dataset, code, training/inference time, and parameter counts for reproducibility?
- Question: How robust is HR-BiLSTM to noisy/opaque relation labels and have you tried character-level or subword representations to mitigate this?
