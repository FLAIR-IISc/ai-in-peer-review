- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical sequence-matching comparing questions to relations at word-level and relation-id level with residual BiLSTM connections.
- Integrates detector into a two-step KBQA pipeline: relation-aware entity re-ranking followed by relation detection with entity masking and optional constraint detection.
- Introduces a WebQSP relation-chain evaluation and reports strong gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP for relation detection and end-to-end QA.
- Empirical improvements: +2–3% absolute relation detection accuracy on SQ and WebQSP and competitive or state-of-the-art end-to-end results without heavy global reranking.
- Hierarchical matching is well-motivated: dual word/id views help generalization to unseen relations and capture global semantics; ablations show both views matter.
- Residual connections aid deeper sequence matching and optimization, especially on WebQSP with longer contexts and relation chains.
- Practical pipeline insight: relation-informed entity re-ranking effectively reduces entity ambiguity and gives measurable downstream gains.
- Strong ablation and architectural comparisons (CNNs, decomposable matching, single-layer, no-residual) isolate sources of improvement.
- Limited methodological novelty: multi-granularity views and residual RNNs are established techniques, so contribution is mainly careful application/engineering.
- Zero-shot claims are under-evaluated: no explicit seen vs. unseen relation/chain breakdown or lexical-overlap analysis to attribute gains to zero-shot handling.
- Constraint detection is heuristic and potentially brittle: character-overlap thresholds and special date/type handling lack learned alternatives and sensitivity analysis.
- Experimental comparisons are incomplete: omits contemporaneous baselines like CFO, character-level seq2seq relation generation, and neural symbolic machines for end-to-end QA.
- Missing or unclear training/inference details: ranking loss margin/negatives, candidate set sizes, chain formation/tokenization, chosen residual variant, and computational costs are not fully specified.
- Presentation issues: inconsistent formulas, malformed references, and notation problems reduce clarity and raise reporting concerns.
- Request zero-shot breakdown: report percent of test relations/chains unseen in training and accuracy stratified by seen/unseen and lexical overlap.
- Request loss/negative details: specify ranking loss (margin), number/type of negatives, and negative sampling strategy for WebQSP chains.
- Ask which residual variant (hidden vs pooled) worked best per dataset and for training dynamics—provide loss curves or convergence stats.
- Ask for efficiency numbers: average candidate relations per entity/question after re-ranking, training/inference times, and scalability to larger KBs.
- Ask for constraint detection sensitivity: impact of overlap threshold, effect of starting from top-1 vs beam, and whether a learned matching model was tried.
- Ask to include broader baselines: add comparisons to CFO and neural symbolic machines under matched entity linking, and compare to char-level seq2seq plugged into the same pipeline.
- Reproducibility request: release code and constructed WebQSP relation dataset, and clarify all hyperparameters, random seeds, and variance across runs.
