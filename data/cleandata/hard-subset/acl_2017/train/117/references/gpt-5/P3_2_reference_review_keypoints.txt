- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches questions to relations using word-level and relation-level tokens with residual connections.  
- Uses a 2-layer BiLSTM to produce multi-level question representations and hierarchically match them to relation representations.  
- Applies the model in a two-step KBQA pipeline: re-rank entities by relation scores, then predict relations (or short chains) for the chosen entity, plus optional constraint detection.  
- Reports consistent 2–3% relation detection gains over strong baselines and SOTA or comparable end-to-end KBQA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Main contributions: hierarchical residual matching combining two relation granularities and multi-level question encodings.  
- Main contributions: simple entity re-ranking via relation scoring to improve disambiguation without heavy joint inference.  
- Main contributions: ablation studies diagnosing gains from granularity combination, residual connections, LSTM vs CNN, and attention vs residual.  
- Strength: Addresses KBQA challenges with principled dual-granularity design and thoughtful initialization for unseen relations.  
- Strength: Solid empirical gains with careful ablations and reported statistical significance.  
- Strength: Simple, modular pipeline that is effective and reaches SOTA on SQ while being comparable on WQ.  
- Strength: Clear analysis of dataset differences explaining why SQ and WQ benefit from different components.  
- Strength: Modular detector is reusable as a plug-in for other systems.  
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—components like tokenized relations and residuals are known; novelty lies mainly in their specific combination.  
- Weakness: Limited zero-shot analysis—no explicit seen vs unseen relation performance breakdown or attribution of gains.  
- Weakness: Potential entity degree bias in re-ranking—high-degree entities may be favored and no normalization/analysis is provided.  
- Weakness: Incomplete experimental details and hyperparameters (K, K′, l, α, λ, margin, dropout, negative sampling, tokenization) hinder reproducibility; dataset/code release unclear.  
- Weakness: Constraint detection is heuristic (character-overlap) and under-analyzed despite large impact; lacks comparisons or error analysis.  
- Question: Please report relation detection performance split by seen vs unseen relations and quantify gains from word-level vs residual matching on SQ.  
- Question: Did you observe correlation between entity degree and re-ranking success, and have you tried degree-normalized or soft aggregation for overlap scores?  
- Question: What maximum chain length is considered on WebQSP, how are chains constructed/pruned, and do gains persist for longer chains?  
- Question: Provide exact hyperparameter and training details (K, K′, l, α, λ, margin, optimizer, batch size, dropout, epochs) and Freebase predicate tokenization rules.  
- Question: Report runtime/scalability: average relations scored per question in steps 1/2, training/inference times, and memory use.  
- Additional: A qualitative error analysis contrasting attention vs residual successes and typical relation confusions would strengthen the paper.  
- Additional: Consider evaluating character-level inputs for robustness to OOV tokens.  
- Additional: Fix minor notation issues in the ranking loss (missing margin) and ensure consistent citation formatting.
