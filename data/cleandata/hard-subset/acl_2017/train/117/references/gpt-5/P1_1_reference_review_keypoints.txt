- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical residual BiLSTM matching questions to word-level and relation-level representations for KBQA relation detection.  
- Integrates HR-BiLSTM into a two-step KBQA pipeline: relation-based re-ranking of entity candidates, then relation scoring conditioned on the top entity with <e>-replacement, plus a lightweight constraint detector.  
- Main contribution: hierarchical residual sequence matching fusing word- and relation-level tokens with residual BiLSTM layers, supported by ablations showing their importance.  
- Contribution: a simple, effective two-step pipeline that leverages relation detection to improve end-to-end KBQA with minimal feature engineering.  
- Contribution: comprehensive empirical evaluation and ablations on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP comparing against CNN, AMPCNN, BiCNN, and attention-based baselines.  
- Strength: strong, consistent empirical gains — ~2–3% improvements on relation detection and end-to-end metrics, SOTA on SimpleQuestions.  
- Strength: sensible modeling of relation granularity — word-tokenization aids unseen relations, relation-level tokens capture global signals; residuals make abstraction levels complementary.  
- Strength: practical innovation — relation-driven entity re-ranking effectively resolves entity ambiguity and contributes materially to end-to-end gains.  
- Strength: good comparisons to alternatives and minimal KB-specific engineering, indicating portability.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental — components (token decomposition, deep BiLSTMs, residuals) are known and contribution is mainly their combination/engineering.  
- Weakness: clarity and missing training details — ranking loss written incorrectly/missing margin, and many hyperparameters (K, K’, l, alpha, lambda, theta) are unreported.  
- Weakness: unclear BiLSTM initialization description — how relation sequence LSTMs are initialized from word-sequence final state and whether parameters are shared is unspecified.  
- Weakness: zero-shot/generalization claims not backed by seen vs unseen relation performance breakdown (e.g., ~14% unseen in SQ).  
- Weakness: fairness/scope of comparisons — some strong baselines and differing pipelines (e.g., STAGG) not controlled for; ensemble vs single-model SOTA not clearly distinguished.  
- Weakness: constraint detection is heuristic and under-specified (character-overlap, date/type rules) with no precision/recall or analysis of when it helps or hurts.  
- Question: provide the exact ranking loss formula, margin value, negative sampling strategy, and negatives-per-positive.  
- Question: report values and dataset-specific settings for K, K’, l, alpha, lambda, and threshold theta, plus sensitivity analyses.  
- Question: clarify how relation chains are enumerated (max length, candidates per entity, pruning) and average candidate set sizes (esp. for WQ).  
- Question: describe relation-name tokenization (schema labels vs IDs, normalization), handling of multi-word names and aliases.  
- Question: report performance split on seen vs unseen relations to substantiate zero-shot/generalization claims.  
- Question: provide runtime/throughput for relation scoring and the full pipeline on SQ and WQ.  
- Overall: solid empirical work with careful ablations and practical gains; recommend acceptance after clarifying training details, adding seen/unseen analysis, and tightening methodological descriptions.
