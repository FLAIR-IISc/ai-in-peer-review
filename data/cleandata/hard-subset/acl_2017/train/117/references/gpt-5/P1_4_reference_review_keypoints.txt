- Paper proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM matching questions to relations at relation-name and tokenized-word granularities.  
- Proposes a KBQA pipeline that uses relation detection twice: to re-rank entity-linker candidates and to select the core relation/chain (with optional rule-based constraint detection).  
- Empirical results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with ablations show consistent improvements in relation detection and end-to-end KBQA.

Strengths:
- Intuitive multi-granularity modeling: relation names give global cues while tokenized words help generalize to unseen relations; ablations confirm each view helps.  
- Residual connections across question representation layers improve optimization and outperform weighted-sum and attention alternatives.  
- Practical entity re-ranking via relation detection reduces entity-linking ambiguity and substantially improves end-task performance.  
- Solid empirical evaluation: two datasets, public EL outputs, multiple informative ablations, and statistically significant deltas.  
- Competitive performance with a relatively lightweight system, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and competitive WebQSP results (further improved by ensembling).

Weaknesses:
- Novelty is incremental: combines known ideas (hierarchical matching, multi-view, residuals) rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.  
- Lacks zero-shot and scalability analysis: no breakdown by seen/unseen relations, relation frequency, or runtime/degree analysis of scoring many relations.  
- Comparisons/fairness incomplete: custom WebQSP relation dataset complicates reproducibility; some strong baselines and ensemble protocol details are missing.  
- Constraint detection is heuristic and brittle (string-overlap and date rules), limited to top-1 chain and prone to error propagation.  
- Several implementation details underspecified: residual variant used, negative sampling, chain representation/composition, and hyperparameter values/choices.

Questions asked:
- Zero-shot: Report accuracy on unseen vs. seen relations and per-frequency buckets; quantify each view’s contribution by regime.  
- Residual/hyperparams: Specify which residual variant produced main results and provide α, λ, l, K, K′, and ranking loss margin.  
- Relation chains: Explain how chains are encoded/composed, handling of variable length, and performance difference for length-1 vs. length-2+ chains.  
- Efficiency: Provide average number of relations scored per question, per-question runtime on SQ/WQ, and sensitivity to l and K′.  
- Ensemble: Identify the “top-3 relation detectors”, describe score-combination method, and report single-model vs. ensemble performance.  
- Dataset release: Will the WebQSP relation detection dataset and code be publicly released for comparability?

Additional minor comments:
- Clarify relation-name tokenization (underscores/separators), initialization for unseen relation-name embeddings, and provide qualitative examples of correct/incorrect matches per view.
