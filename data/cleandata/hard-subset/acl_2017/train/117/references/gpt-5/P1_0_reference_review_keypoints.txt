- Paper proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM matching questions to relations at word- and relation-levels, integrated into a KBQA pipeline using relation detection for entity re-ranking and relation selection.

- Contribution 1: Combines word- and relation-level representations with hierarchical residual matching, improving relation detection on SQ and WQ.

- Contribution 2: Uses relation detection to re-rank entity candidates, reducing entity ambiguity and improving end-to-end KBQA without heavy joint modeling.

- Contribution 3: Provides extensive ablations showing the benefit of multi-granularity representations and residual learning, especially for multi-relation matching on WebQSP.

- Strength: Strong, consistent empirical gains on relation detection across datasets and model families.

- Strength: Clear motivation and evidence that combining word-level generalization and relation-level global cues improves performance.

- Strength: Residual connections enable deeper matching and yield notable improvements over weighted-sum and attention baselines.

- Strength: Simple, practical KBQA pipeline yields state-of-the-art on SQ and competitive results on WQ with minimal engineering.

- Strength: Comprehensive ablation studies and comparisons to relevant baselines and variants.

- Weakness: Technical novelty is moderate; ideas (multi-granularity matching, residuals) are incremental combinations of known techniques.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of zero-shot/generalization — lacks seen vs unseen relation breakdowns and long-chain performance.

- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns due to missing hyperparameter and methodological details (K, K′, l, α, λ, margin, residual variant, chain limits, optimization, cost).

- Weakness: Constraint detection is heuristic and dataset-specific, raising portability concerns and reliance on brittle rules.

- Weakness: Comparisons to stronger joint/global inference systems are limited; contribution of entity re-ranking vs detector improvement is not fully isolated.

- Q: Please report relation detection accuracy split by seen vs unseen relations and by chain length on SQ and WQ.

- Q: Provide exact values and sensitivity analyses for K, K′, l, α, λ, margin, and which residual variant was used.

- Q: What maximum core-chain length is supported/trained on WQ, and how does performance vary with chain length?

- Q: What is the computational cost per question for the two-step detection and constraint detection, and are there pruning strategies beyond top-l?

- Q: How does the approach handle opaque relation IDs (no tokenization)? Would char n-grams or descriptions be used instead?

- Q: Will you release code and the WQ relation detection dataset, or provide full reproduction details for candidate pools and training pairs?

- Additional: Provide details on statistical significance testing (test used and sample sizes).

- Additional: Clarify whether residuals are over hidden states or pooled representations and include training curves/loss comparisons to support training-ease claims.
