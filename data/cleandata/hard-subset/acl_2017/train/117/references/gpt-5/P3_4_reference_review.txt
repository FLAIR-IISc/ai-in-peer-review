Summary and main contributions
- The paper targets relation detection within KBQA, a setting with thousands of relations, zero-shot labels, and multi-hop relation chains. It proposes HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical sequence-matching model that jointly compares questions to relations at two granularities: word-level (tokenized relation names) and relation-level (relation ids or chain ids). Residual connections across BiLSTM layers are used to stabilize training and enable complementary abstractions.
- The paper integrates this detector into a simple but effective KBQA pipeline with a two-step relation detection: (i) relation-aware entity re-ranking over initial linker candidates and (ii) relation detection conditioned on the re-ranked topic entity with entity masking, followed by an optional constraint detection module.
- New relation detection evaluation is created for WebQSP with relation-chain candidates. The approach achieves strong gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP both for relation detection and end-to-end QA.

Strengths
- Empirical gains on two benchmarks: The model improves relation detection accuracy over strong baselines by 2–3% absolute on SimpleQuestions (SQ) and WebQSP, and yields state-of-the-art or competitive end-to-end KBQA results without heavy joint inference or global rerankers. The improvements are consistent across single- and multi-relation settings, suggesting robustness.
- Well-motivated hierarchical matching: The dual representation of relations (word-level for generalization to unseen relations; relation-level for capturing global semantics/phrase-level alignment) is compelling for the zero-shot, large-label setting. Ablations confirm both views are important (notably on SQ where unseen relations are prevalent).
- Residual connections improve deeper sequence matching: Although the effect size varies by dataset, residual connections meaningfully help on WebQSP (where longer contexts and relation chains matter), and the analysis argues they ease optimization and encourage complementary representations across layers.
- Pipeline design insight: The entity re-ranking step using relation signals is simple and effective, addressing a known bottleneck in KBQA (entity ambiguity) and providing measurable downstream gains. This is a practical contribution that others can adopt irrespective of the specific detector.
- Solid ablation and architectural comparisons: The paper compares against CNNs, attention-based decomposable matching, single-layer encoders, and variants without residual connections. These help isolate where the gains come from and support the architectural choices.

Weaknesses
- Limited novelty relative to prior multi-granularity and residual RNN ideas: Using word- and id-level views of relation labels and residual connections in sequence models are established techniques. The contribution is more in careful application and engineering to KBQA than in fundamentally new methodology.
- Claims about zero-shot generalization are under-evaluated: While the motivation is strong, there is no explicit analysis breaking down performance by seen vs. unseen relations/chains or by degree of lexical overlap. Without a controlled zero-shot evaluation, it is hard to attribute gains to better handling of unseen labels rather than to other factors (e.g., better modeling overall).
- Constraint detection is ad-hoc and potentially brittle: The character-overlap matching with thresholds and special handling for dates/types is heuristic and may be dataset-specific. No learned alternative is explored, and sensitivity analyses to thresholds or error propagation (especially starting from top-1 queries) are limited.
- Incomplete experimental context and baselines for end-to-end QA: The KBQA comparisons focus on STAGG and AMPCNN, omitting other competitive contemporaneous systems (e.g., CFO, character-level seq2seq relation generation, neural symbolic machines). Given the strong emphasis on end-to-end gains, broader comparisons would strengthen the claims.
- Missing or unclear training/inference details: Key specifics are not fully documented, e.g., the margin and negatives for the ranking loss, size and construction of candidate relation sets per entity and per question, chain formation and tokenization details, residual connection variant chosen (hidden vs. pooled) per dataset, and computational costs. The loss equation also appears to omit a margin term or sign.
- Presentation issues: Some formulas and notations are inconsistent; several references lack years or are malformed. This detracts from clarity and raises minor concerns about rigor in reporting.

Questions to authors
- Zero-shot analysis: What percentage of test relations/chains are unseen in training for each dataset? Can you report accuracy stratified by seen vs. unseen, and by lexical overlap between question and relation name? How does HR-BiLSTM compare to baselines in these subsets?
- Loss and negatives: What is the exact ranking loss (margin value, number/type of negative relations per question, in-batch vs. global negatives)? For WebQSP, are negative chains sampled or exhaustive within the topic-entity neighborhood?
- Residual variant and training dynamics: Which residual variant performed best (hidden vs. pooled) on each dataset, and by how much? Can you provide training loss curves or convergence statistics to substantiate the “easier optimization” claim?
- Efficiency: What is the average number of candidate relations per entity and per question after the entity re-ranking filter? What are training and inference times, and how does the two-step detection scale to larger KBs?
- Constraint detection: How sensitive are results to the overlap threshold and to starting from top-1 vs. a beam of core-chain hypotheses? Have you tried a simple learned text–entity matching model (e.g., BiLSTM+attention) in place of character overlap?
- Comparative baselines: Can you include end-to-end comparisons to CFO and neural symbolic machines under matched entity linking settings? On SimpleQuestions, how does your relation detector compare to character-level seq2seq predictors when plugged into the same pipeline?
- Reproducibility: Will you release code and the constructed WebQSP relation detection dataset? Please clarify all hyperparameters selected for each dataset and random seed stability (variance across runs).

Overall, this is a solid engineering paper with a well-motivated and practical approach that delivers measurable improvements. The methodological novelty is moderate, but the insights on hierarchical matching and the effective pipeline integration are valuable to the KBQA community. Addressing the zero-shot evaluation gap and clarifying training details would substantially strengthen the work.
