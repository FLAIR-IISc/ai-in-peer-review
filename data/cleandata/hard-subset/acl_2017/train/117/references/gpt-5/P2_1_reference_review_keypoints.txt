- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM matching questions to relations at relation-level and word-level.  
- Uses residual connections to enable multi-layer question encoders and hierarchical matching.  
- Integrates the model into a KBQA pipeline with relation-driven entity reranking, relation/relation-chain selection, and optional constraint detection.  
- Contribution: HR-BiLSTM yields consistent 2–3% absolute gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP relation detection.  
- Contribution: The KBQA pipeline demonstrates that improved relation detection yields 2–3% end-to-end gains and achieves SOTA/comparable results.  
- Contribution: Ablations show value of combining relation- and word-level representations and of residual connections over alternatives.  
- Strength: Clear, statistically significant empirical gains on two benchmarks (Tables 2–3).  
- Strength: Addresses scalability and zero-shot generalization by combining relation IDs and tokenized names.  
- Strength: Sound architecture and informative ablations isolating sources of improvement.  
- Strength: Practical entity reranking step effectively improves end-to-end KBQA performance.  
- Strength: Uses fair and relevant baselines and standard entity linkers, strengthening credibility.  
- Weakness: Core ideas are incremental—combining tokenized names and residual BiLSTMs is not novel individually.  
- Weakness: Lacks breakdowns for zero-shot and relation-chain-specific performance (seen vs unseen, chain length, candidate size).  
- Weakness: Ranking loss and training details (margin, negative sampling, regularization, early stopping) are underspecified hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Constraint detection is heuristic, underspecified, and lacks robustness analysis.  
- Weakness: WebQSP relation dataset and code are not yet released, raising reproducibility concerns.  
- Question: Please clarify the ranking loss formulation, negative sampling strategy, and number of negatives per positive.  
- Question: How many relations per candidate entity are scored and what are average candidate pool sizes before/after reranking?  
- Question: Provide breakdowns for seen vs unseen relations (SQ), single- vs multi-step chains (WebQSP), and effect sizes vs candidate pool size.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to residual placement and hyperparameters (margins/alpha/beta) in scoring equations?  
- Question: Will the authors release the WebQSP relation detection dataset and code for reproducibility?  
- Overall: Solid engineering contribution with meaningful empirical gains and moderate novelty; acceptance recommended pending clarifications and resource release (weak accept).
