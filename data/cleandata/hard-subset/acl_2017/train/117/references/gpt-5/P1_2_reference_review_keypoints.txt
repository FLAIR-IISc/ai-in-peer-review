- Proposes HR-BiLSTM that matches questions to relations at relation-token and word-token granularities with residual connections for hierarchical matching.  
- Integrates relation detection into a simple KBQA pipeline for entity re-ranking and relation selection, plus an optional heuristic constraint detector.  

Main contributions:
- Hierarchical matching combining relation-level and word-level representations with residuals, improving relation detection.  
- A simple KBQA pipeline using relation signals for entity reranking and relation selection that yields competitive end-to-end results.  
- Empirical ablations showing multi-granularity representations and residual matching outperform weighted-sum/attention alternatives.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical gains on SimpleQuestions (93.3%) and WebQSP (82.53%) and 2–3% end-to-end KBQA improvements, with an ensemble setting new SOTA on SQ.  
- Clear evidence that multi-granularity relation representations address large-vocabulary/zero-shot issues and both levels contribute.  
- Residual hierarchical matching stabilizes training and outperforms naive hierarchical combinations, especially on WebQSP.  
- Practical reranking approach reduces entity ambiguity without complex joint inference and materially boosts QA performance.  
- Thorough comparisons, ablations, and significance testing that attribute gains to specific design choices.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: components (word decomposition, relation-token use, residuals) are incremental; novelty is in their combination.  
- No explicit zero-shot analysis or breakdown on seen vs. unseen relations/chains to support unseen-relation claims.  
- Heuristic constraint detection is ad hoc (character overlap, date rules) and may not generalize or be learned.  
- Reproducibility concerns: many hyperparameters and candidate-generation details (margins, α, λ, K, thresholds, branching) and computational costs are missing; WebQSP split release timing unclear.  
- Comparisons lack analysis of robustness to different entity linkers, runtime/efficiency, and complexity vs. other multi-model systems.  

Questions for authors:
- Please report relation detection accuracy split by seen vs. unseen relations and by chain length; quantify word-level help on unseen cases.  
- Which residual variant performs best, by how much, and were deeper (>2-layer) BiLSTMs explored?  
- How are candidate core-chains generated (max length, branching/pruning) and what is the average candidate count and runtime impact of reranking on WebQSP?  
- Specify training hyperparameters (ranking margin, α, λ, l, K, K0, constraint threshold) and sensitivity to these choices.  
- How often does the constraint module change the top answer, what constraints does it capture/miss, and could a learned module help?
