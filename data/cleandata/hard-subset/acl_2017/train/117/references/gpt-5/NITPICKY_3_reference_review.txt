Summary and contributions
- This paper tackles relation detection for KBQA by proposing HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical sequence matching model that combines word-level and relation-name-level representations and introduces residual connections between stacked BiLSTMs on the question side. It further integrates the detector into a simple KBQA pipeline with an entity re-ranking step based on relation likelihoods and a lightweight constraint detection heuristic.
- Main contributions as I see them:
  1) A practical, multi-granularity relation detector (word-level + relation-name-level) with residual connections for hierarchical matching, showing consistent gains on SimpleQuestions and a new WebQSP relation detection benchmark.
  2) A simple but effective KBQA pipeline that leverages relation detection for entity re-ranking, improving end-to-end accuracy with minimal engineering and ablations indicating the importance of each step.

Strengths
- Clear empirical improvements with ablations: The model outperforms strong baselines (AMPCNN, BiCNN) on relation detection with 2–3% absolute gains and demonstrates that both multi-granularity (word+relation) and residual connections matter. Replacing BiLSTM with CNN degrades on WebQSP, supporting the modeling choice. The two-step use of the detector (entity re-ranking + per-entity relation scoring) improves KBQA by 2–3%.
- Addressing zero-shot and large-vocabulary issues: Splitting relation names into word tokens helps generalization to unseen relations, which is a genuine pain point in KBQA; the approach is simple and broadly applicable.
- Sensible pipeline design: Using relation signals to disambiguate entities is intuitive and effective; the reported drop when removing entity re-ranking indicates genuine contribution beyond a single module.
- Some architectural novelty in the context: While residual connections for deep nets are known, applying residual addition for hierarchical question representations to stabilize multi-view matching is a useful and well-motivated idea here, supported by ablations (weighted sum and attention worse).
- Effort on ablations and design choices: Multiple ablations (w/o rel-name, w/o rel-words, residual vs. attention vs. weighted sum, depth, CNN) and hyperparameter ranges are reported, which strengthens the empirical case.

Weaknesses
- Mathematical and notation issues that hamper clarity and may indicate errors:
  - Ranking loss is written as lrel = max{0, srel(r+;q) + srel(r;q)} “where is a constant parameter.” This seems incorrect/missing the margin and sign; a standard max(0, γ − s(r+,q) + s(r−,q)) is expected. The margin symbol γ never appears, and “ is a constant” is undefined. Please fix and specify negative sampling protocol.
  - In srerank(e; q), you write r ∈ Rlq \Re. This appears to be set difference, which contradicts the text (“the score of the most confident relation … associated to the entity e”). It should be intersection Rlq ∩ Re. As written, it would exclude the useful relations.
  - Symbols α and (later) are referenced but not defined properly; likewise for . Several equations use missing Greek letters due to formatting errors. This is consequential, not cosmetic.
- Reproducibility and fairness concerns:
  - The new WebQSP relation detection benchmark is “to be released.” Without a released split and code, claims are hard to verify. The KBQA comparison relies on different KBs across datasets (FB2M vs full Freebase), and entity linkers differ; more details on candidate set sizes, K, K′, and l (top-l relations) are needed. Table 3 with end-to-end numbers is referenced but not included here.
  - Statistical significance (p-values) is claimed without specifying the test (randomization, bootstrap, paired t-test), pairing protocol, or number of runs/seeds. Report mean±std over multiple seeds or use standard tests with details.
  - Attention baseline uses a decomposable attention model for NLI, not a standard cross-attention for sequence matching (e.g., match-LSTM/biDAF-style). The choice may disadvantage attention.
- Methodological details are underspecified:
  - Residual variants: Two options are proposed (residual at hidden states vs. at pooled vectors), but final configuration used in main results is not stated. Please report which is used and per-variant performance.
  - Optimization and regularization: Missing batch size, optimizer (SGD/Adam), gradient clipping, dropout/L2, number of negatives per positive, sampling strategy, training epochs, early stopping, and whether word embeddings are fine-tuned.
  - Tokenization of relation names: How are Freebase names normalized (underscores, CamelCase, stopwords, lowercasing), OOV handling, and whether relation-name tokens share the same embedding space as question words. For chains, do you add both individual relation IDs and a chain token? Ambiguity remains in rword vs rrel processing and parameter sharing (“two BiLSTMs with shared parameters” and “initialize the relation sequence LSTMs with the final state representations of the word sequence” need precise definitions).
- Constraint detection is ad hoc: The character-overlap heuristic with hand-crafted thresholds and special rules (Appendix A/B) is brittle, language-dependent, and may not generalize. It appears to provide a sizable portion of the end-to-end gain (given high entity+core-chain accuracy), yet is not compared to stronger textual constraint baselines or trained matchers.
- Limited analysis of core claims:
  - No breakdown on seen vs unseen relations in SimpleQuestions to substantiate the zero-shot motivation.
  - No error analysis by relation length, chain length, or proximity of evidence spans. Also no efficiency analysis (inference time vs candidate pool size).
  - No comparison to approaches leveraging KB embedding pretraining (e.g., TransE/RotatE), which is a standard alternative for unseen relations (mentioned in related work but not evaluated).

Questions to authors
- Please correct and fully specify the loss function, the margin, and negative sampling procedure. How many negatives per positive and how are they chosen?
- In srerank, is it Rlq ∩ Re? If so, what is l and how sensitive is performance to l, K, and K′?
- Which residual variant is used for the main numbers (hidden-state residual vs. pooled residual)? Provide an ablation between the two.
- What attention baseline did you implement exactly? Did you try standard cross-attention between question tokens and relation tokens (bi-attention) rather than decomposable attention?
- Provide seen vs unseen relation accuracy on SimpleQuestions; likewise, analysis by chain length on WebQSP.
- Clarify the “two BiLSTMs (with shared parameters)” for relation encoding: Do you encode rword and rrel with the same BiLSTM applied twice, initializing the second with the final state of the first? Do you backprop through both passes? How do you prevent leakage/overfitting to relation-name tokens?
- Training details: optimizer, batch size, dropout, gradient clipping, embedding fine-tuning, epochs, early stopping, number of seeds and variance. Also report inference latency per question.
- Release the WebQSP relation detection dataset/splits and code to support reproducibility, and include the missing end-to-end Table 3 with exact numbers.
