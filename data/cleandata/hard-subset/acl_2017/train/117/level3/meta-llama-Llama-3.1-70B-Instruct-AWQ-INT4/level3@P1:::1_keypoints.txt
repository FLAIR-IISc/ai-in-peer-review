- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical residual BiLSTM for relation detection in KBQA, integrates entity linking and relation detector, and reports state-of-the-art accuracy on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.

- Contribution 1: HR-BiLSTM model using deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names across abstraction hierarchies.

- Contribution 2: A simple KBQA system that jointly integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to mutually enhance performance.

- Contribution 3: A novel entity re-ranking approach after entity linking that improves KBQA performance.

- Strength 1: Tackles a relevant KBQA problem of mapping language to relations with partial arguments and large relation sets.

- Strength 2: Novel modeling choice combining segmented word-level and unsegmented relation-as-single-token representations.

- Strength 3: Experimental results show improvements over state-of-the-art in relation detection and KBQA.

- Strength 4: Paper is well-written and well-organized, easy to follow.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to a restricted dataset which may not generalize to all KBQA scenarios.

- Weakness 2: Paper organization could be improved (move section 3.1 to related work and make 3.2 the main section 3).

- Question 1: How does HR-BiLSTM handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words and relations?

- Question 2: How much does the entity re-ranking step contribute to overall KBQA performance?

- Question 3: Why does HR-BiLSTM outperform baselines on the WebQSP dataset—what drives its advantage?
