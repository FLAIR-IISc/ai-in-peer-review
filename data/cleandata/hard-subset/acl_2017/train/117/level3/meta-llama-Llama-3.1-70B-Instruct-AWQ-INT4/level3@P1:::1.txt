**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to improve neural relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The authors introduce a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning that detects KB relations given an input question. The model uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction. Additionally, the authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance the other. Experimental results show that the approach achieves outstanding relation detection performance and helps the KBQA system to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Contributions of the Work**

Based on the paper, I identify the following contributions:

1. **Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model**: The authors propose a novel neural relation detection model that uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction.
2. **Improved KBQA system**: The authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance the other.
3. **Entity re-ranking after Entity Linking**: The authors propose a novel approach to entity re-ranking after the Entity Linking step, which enables the KBQA system to achieve better performance.

**Strengths of the Paper**

Here are my strongest arguments supporting the acceptance of this submission:

1. **Addressing a relevant problem**: The paper addresses a relevant problem in KBQA, which is mapping natural language to KB relations with partial argument information and very large relation sets.
2. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to neural relation detection, which combines two input representations: segmented word-level and unsegmented relation-as-single-token.
3. **Experimental results**: The paper presents experimental results that show improvements over the state of the art in relation detection and KBQA.
4. **Well-written and organized**: The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow and understand the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

Here are my strongest arguments against the acceptance of this submission:

1. **Limited dataset**: The paper evaluates the proposed approach on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of all KBQA scenarios.
2. **Section organization**: The paper could be improved by moving section 3.1 to the related work section and making section 3.2 the main section 3.
