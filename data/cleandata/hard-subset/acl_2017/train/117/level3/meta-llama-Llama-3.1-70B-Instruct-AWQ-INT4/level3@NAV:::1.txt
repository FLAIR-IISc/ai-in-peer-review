**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for knowledge base question answering (KBQA). The model uses a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning to detect KB relations given an input question. The proposed approach combines two input representations: segmented word-level and unsegmented relation-as-single-token. The main contribution of the paper is enabling entity re-ranking after the entity linking step, which improves the performance of the KBQA system. Experimental results show that the proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) QA benchmarks.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Improved Relation Detection Model**: The paper proposes a novel KB relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations.
2. **Entity Re-ranking**: The paper introduces an entity re-ranking step after the entity linking step, which improves the performance of the KBQA system.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) QA benchmarks.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Relation Detection**: The proposed HR-BiLSTM model outperforms previous methods on KB relation detection tasks.
2. **Entity Re-ranking**: The entity re-ranking step improves the performance of the KBQA system by reducing the ambiguity of entity names.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) QA benchmarks.
4. **Simple KBQA System**: The paper proposes a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and relation detection, making it easy to build and evaluate.
5. **Experimental Evaluation**: The paper provides a thorough experimental evaluation of the proposed approach on two QA benchmarks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Dataset**: The paper evaluates the proposed approach on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of all possible KBQA scenarios.
2. **Overfitting**: The paper mentions that the deep BiLSTM question encoder may suffer from overfitting, which could be addressed by using regularization techniques.
3. **Limited Analysis**: The paper could provide more analysis on the performance of the proposed approach on different types of questions and relations.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the proposed approach handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words and entities?
2. Can the entity re-ranking step be applied to other KBQA systems?
3. How does the proposed approach perform on more complex KBQA tasks, such as multi-hop question answering?
4. Can the HR-BiLSTM model be used for other NLP tasks, such as relation extraction or text classification?
5. How does the proposed approach handle cases where the entity linking step fails to identify the correct entity?
