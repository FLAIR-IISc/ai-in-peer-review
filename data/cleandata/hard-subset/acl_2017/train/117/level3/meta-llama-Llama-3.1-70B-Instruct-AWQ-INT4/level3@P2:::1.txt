**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to relation detection in Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) systems. The authors introduce a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning, which detects KB relations given an input question. The model uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction. Additionally, the authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance another. Experimental results demonstrate that the approach achieves outstanding relation detection performance and helps the KBQA system achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Improved Relation Detection Model**: The authors propose a hierarchical matching approach for KB relation detection, which matches the input question to both word-level and relation-level representations to get the final ranking score. This approach addresses the challenges of KB relation detection, including dealing with unseen relations, large relation vocabulary, and open relation sets.
2. **Entity Re-Ranking**: The authors propose an entity re-ranking step after the initial entity linking, which uses the relation detection model to score all relations in the KB with connections to at least one of the entity candidates. This step helps to disambiguate entities and improve the accuracy of the KBQA system.
3. **State-of-the-Art KBQA System**: The authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector, which achieves state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Relation Detection Performance**: The proposed approach achieves outstanding relation detection performance, outperforming the previous state-of-the-art methods on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets.
2. **Entity Re-Ranking**: The entity re-ranking step is a novel contribution, which helps to disambiguate entities and improve the accuracy of the KBQA system.
3. **State-of-the-Art KBQA System**: The proposed KBQA system achieves state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
4. **Well-Motivated and Well-Executed Experiments**: The authors conduct thorough experiments to evaluate the proposed approach, including ablation tests and comparisons with previous state-of-the-art methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Dataset**: The authors evaluate their approach on a limited dataset, which may not be representative of all KBQA tasks.
2. **Section Organization**: The authors could improve the organization of the paper by moving Section 3.1 to the related work section and splitting the new Section 3 more cleanly.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend their approach to more complex KBQA tasks, such as multi-hop reasoning?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the entity re-ranking step, including the scoring function and the threshold used to select the top-K entity candidates?
3. How do the authors plan to address the issue of limited dataset, and what other datasets do they plan to evaluate their approach on?

**Additional Comments**

The authors provide a well-motivated and well-executed paper, which proposes a novel approach to relation detection in KBQA systems. The entity re-ranking step is a valuable contribution, which can be used to improve the accuracy of KBQA systems. However, the authors could improve the organization of the paper and provide more details on the entity re-ranking step.
