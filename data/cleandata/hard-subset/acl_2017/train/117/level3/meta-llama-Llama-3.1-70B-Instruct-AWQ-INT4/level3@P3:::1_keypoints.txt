- Proposes a novel HR-BiLSTM relation detection model performing hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations.  
- Integrates entity linking with the relation detector in a simple KBQA system to mutually enhance performance.  
- Motivated by the need for robust models that handle large and unseen relation sets in KB relation detection.  
- Uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names across abstraction hierarchies.  
- Thorough analysis distinguishing general relation extraction from KB relation detection.  
- Achieves significant improvements over state-of-the-art on single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) benchmarks.  
- Ablation study demonstrates effectiveness of hierarchical matching and residual learning.  
- Major weakness: evaluated on limited datasets that may not represent broader KBQA challenges.  
- Lacks sufficient analysis of model interpretability and which relation types are detected well.  
- Unclear whether the model itself is the core contribution versus the data/evaluation setup.
