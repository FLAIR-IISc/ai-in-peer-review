- Proposes a hierarchical residual BiLSTM model that detects KB relations by matching questions to word- and relation-level representations.  
- Introduces an entity re-ranking step that uses the relation detector to disambiguate entity candidates after initial entity linking.  
- Presents a simple KBQA pipeline integrating entity linking and the relation detector, achieving state-of-the-art accuracy on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.  
- Hierarchical matching specifically targets challenges: unseen relations, large relation vocabularies, and open relation sets.  
- Entity re-ranking is a novel contribution that improves KBQA accuracy.  
- Model outperforms prior state-of-the-art on relation detection for SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Experiments are thorough and include ablation tests and comparisons with prior work.  
- Evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Paper organization needs improvement (e.g., move Section 3.1 to related work and better split Section 3).  
- Request clarification on extending the approach to more complex KBQA tasks like multi-hop reasoning.  
- Request details on the entity re-ranking scoring function and the threshold/top-K selection.  
- Ask which additional datasets the authors plan to evaluate to address limited-data concerns.  
- Overall: well-motivated and well-executed work, but needs clearer organization and more detail on re-ranking.
