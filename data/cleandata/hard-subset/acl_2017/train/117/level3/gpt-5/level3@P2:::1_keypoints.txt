- Paper proposes HR-BiLSTM matching questions to relations at relation-token and tokenized-word granularities for KBQA with large vocabularies and zero-shot relations.
- Integrates relation detector into a simple KBQA pipeline with novel entity re-ranking using relation scores and optional constraint detection.
- Empirical improvements reported on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP for relation detection and end-to-end QA.
- Contribution: practical pipeline leveraging relation-based entity re-ranking to yield end-to-end gains without complex joint inference.
- Contribution: hierarchical residual matching architecture combining relation-level and word-level representations for robustness to unseen relations.
- Contribution: thorough empirical study with ablations and a constructed relation detection benchmark for WebQSP.
- Strength: strong empirical results and convincing ablations showing both granularities and residuals help; LSTM outperforms CNNs on multi-relation chains.
- Strength: simple entity re-ranking using relation scores yields 2–3% QA accuracy gains and competitive SOTA without heavy re-ranking.
- Strength: splitting relation names into words improves zero-shot generalization while relation-level tokens preserve global semantics.
- Strength: hierarchical residual design is well-motivated and training choices (ranking loss, entity replacement token) are reasonable.
- Strength: planned release of WebQSP relation detection setup is a useful community resource.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—combining granularities and residual BiLSTMs are natural extensions rather than a conceptual leap.
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope (only two Freebase-centric datasets) and insufficient analysis of generalization, paraphrase robustness, and breakdowns (zero-shot, chain length, error types).
- Weakness: pipeline depends on an external entity linker and does not explore joint learning to reduce error propagation.
- Weakness: baseline coverage for matching architectures is limited; stronger cross-attentive or cross-encoder models were not compared.
- Weakness: clarity and organization issues (Section 3.1 placement) and scattered/omitted training details affecting reproducibility.
- Question: report fraction of test relations unseen in training and HR-BiLSTM accuracy on unseen vs seen relations.
- Question: provide entity linking top-1/top-K accuracy before vs after re-ranking and downstream QA effects.
- Question: sensitivity analysis requested for K, K′, l, α, and λ with tuning ranges/curves.
- Question: which residual variant (hidden-state skip vs pooled skip) worked best and were gating/layer-norm tried?
- Question: report training/inference costs, parameter counts, throughput, and any training stability issues.
- Question: will code, trained models, and the WQ relation detection dataset be released and on what timeline?
- Suggestion: restructure sections (move granularity motivation to Related Work) and clarify modeling section layout.
- Suggestion: provide explicit training details (margin, optimizer, batch sizes, early stopping, embedding fine-tuning, OOV handling).
- Suggestion: include a brief qualitative error analysis of failure modes (compositional questions, type constraints, rare relations).
- Minor: fix reference formatting and figure/table caption issues for readability.
