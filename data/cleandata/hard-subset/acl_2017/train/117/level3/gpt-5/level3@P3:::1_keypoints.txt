- Paper addresses KBQA relation mapping under partial argument info and large/unseen relation inventories, proposing HR-BiLSTM with dual relation views and entity re-ranking, showing gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.
- Contribution: Dual-view relation representation (word-level + relation-as-token) with hierarchical residual matching (HR-BiLSTM) for zero-shot and global matching.
- Contribution: Practical KBQA pipeline improvement—use relation detection to re-rank entity linker candidates, reducing entity ambiguity.
- Contribution: Strong empirical evidence and ablations demonstrating the value of dual-view design and residual connections, outperforming strong baselines.
- Strength: Solid problem framing addressing large relation spaces, unseen relations, and missing arguments; motivation and analysis for mixing views are convincing.
- Strength: Methodological insight—residual hierarchical design is justified by ablations showing it outperforms single views and naive combinations.
- Strength: Practical impact—relation-based entity re-ranking is simple, effective, and alleviates ambiguous surface forms.
- Strength: Empirical rigor—consistent gains in relation detection and end-to-end QA with thoughtful ablation studies and comparisons.
- Strength: Clear description and reproducibility with detailed architecture, hyperparameters, and promised dataset release.
- Weakness: Limited dataset scope—evaluation only on two Freebase-centric datasets; generality (e.g., to Wikidata) is untested.
- Weakness: Dependence on a fixed off-the-shelf entity linker; lacks systematic sensitivity analysis to EL quality, K/K′, and α.
- Weakness: Limited targeted analysis of unseen relations and coverage—needs stratified performance by seen vs unseen relations and chain length.
- Weakness: Computational concerns—scoring many relations per entity may be heavy; runtime/pruning strategies and latency are not quantified.
- Weakness: Pipeline nature—no joint inference or beam-based query construction; unclear how much gains derive from relation modeling vs pipeline design.
- Question: Report relation detection accuracy split by whether the gold relation appears in training and contributions of each view on the unseen subset?
- Question: What fraction of questions have the gold entity outside top-K, and how do performance, K, K′, and α affect results?
- Question: What are the average numbers of relation candidates scored in re-ranking and final selection, and end-to-end latency per question?
- Question: Which residual variant worked best, sensitivity to hidden size/depth, and were layer norm or highway connections tried?
- Question: On WebQSP, can you break down performance by chain length and show examples where relation-id vs word-level views dominated?
- Question: Have you attempted transfer to another KB (e.g., Wikidata) or paraphrased relation labels to test word-level generalization?
- Additional comment: Suggest reorganizing Section 3 (move relation granularity discussion to related work/background and restructure 3.2 into clearer subsections).
