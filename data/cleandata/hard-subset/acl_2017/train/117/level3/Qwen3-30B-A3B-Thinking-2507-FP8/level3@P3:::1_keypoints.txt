- Proposes HR-BiLSTM (hierarchical residual BiLSTM) that combines word-segmented and relation-as-single-token inputs to improve KB relation detection and enable entity re-ranking, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.
- Contribution: novel hierarchical residual BiLSTM for multi-granularity question–relation matching that improves relation detection.
- Contribution: demonstrates that the improved relation detector yields state-of-the-art performance for a simple KBQA pipeline on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.
- Contribution: introduces entity re-ranking after entity linking to resolve ambiguities and boost overall KBQA accuracy.
- Strength: tackles a relevant KBQA-specific relation detection problem (large relation sets, partial arguments, zero-shot) and motivates why prior approaches fall short.
- Strength: strong empirical gains (≈2–3% absolute) with ablation studies showing the impact of hierarchical matching, residual learning, and dual input representations.
- Strength: offers a practical pipeline-level insight by using relation detection to improve entity linking via re-ranking.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to only SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, limiting claims of broad generalizability.
- Weakness: section organization is poor (suggested moving "Different Granularity in KB Relations" to Related Work and restructuring Section 3).
- Weakness: insufficient evaluation of zero-shot performance—no specific results reported for unseen relations (14% in SimpleQuestions).
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational complexity and practical runtime/deployability compared to baselines.
- Question: can you provide results on an additional KBQA dataset (e.g., ComplexWebQuestions) to show broader applicability?
- Question: how was the hyperparameter α for entity re-ranking chosen—development tuning or prior knowledge?
- Question: can you report specific performance on the 14% unseen relations in SimpleQuestions to support the zero-shot claim?
- Question: please analyze why entity re-ranking is so effective—how specifically does relation detection correct entity linking errors?
- Question: how do training and inference times compare to the best baseline (BiLSTM with words)?
