- Proposes hierarchical matching combining word-level and relation-level representations for KBQA relation detection.
- Introduces residual learning to stabilize and deepen hierarchical matching, improving relation detection performance.
- Presents a simple KBQA pipeline using entity re-ranking that leverages the improved relation detector.

- Identifies a practical gap between general relation extraction and KBQA-specific relation detection challenges (large relation sets, zero-shot, multi-hop).
- Empirically validates HR-BiLSTM with ablation studies showing both hierarchical matching and residuals are crucial.
- Entity re-ranking is a simple yet effective mechanism that substantially improves KBQA performance.

- Evaluation is limited to two datasets (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP), reducing claims of generalizability.
- Insufficient contrast with prior work (e.g., Yin et al., 2016) and unclear novelty relative to existing word-level approaches.
- Lacks theoretical explanation for why residual learning specifically benefits hierarchical matching.

- Request an example where entity re-ranking corrects an initial entity-linking error from the test set.
- Ask why residual learning benefits WebQSP more than SimpleQuestions despite both requiring relation detection.
- Ask for clarification why hierarchical residual matching outperforms common attention-based methods.

- Overall recommendation: accept after minor revisions to address limited evaluation and clearer contrast with prior work.
