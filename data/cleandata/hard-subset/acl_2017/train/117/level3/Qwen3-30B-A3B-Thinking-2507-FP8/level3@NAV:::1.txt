

This paper addresses a relevant problem in KBQA: mapping natural language to KB relations with partial argument information and large relation sets. The authors propose a hierarchical residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model that combines two input representations: segmented word-level and unsegmented relation-as-single-token. The main contribution is enabling entity re-ranking after entity linking, which resolves ambiguity in entity linking results and improves overall QA accuracy.

The primary contribution is the HR-BiLSTM model with hierarchical matching and residual learning for relation detection, which outperforms state-of-the-art methods on both SimpleQuestions (93.3% vs. 91.2%) and WebQSP (82.53% vs. 79.32%). The secondary contribution is the entity re-ranking technique, which significantly boosts KBQA performance by leveraging relation detection results to refine entity candidates. The third contribution is the demonstration that relation detection can be effectively integrated into KBQA pipelines to achieve state-of-the-art results without complex joint inference.

Strengths include the relevance of the problem addressed, the practical significance of the entity re-ranking technique (which directly addresses a key bottleneck in KBQA), and the clear experimental results showing consistent improvements over baselines. The model's ability to handle large relation sets and unseen relations is particularly valuable for real-world applications.

Weaknesses include the limited evaluation on only two datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), which may not fully demonstrate generalizability. The architectural approach combines existing techniques (residual networks, hierarchical matching) rather than introducing a fundamentally novel method. The paper structure could be improved by moving the discussion of relation representation types to related work and better organizing the technical sections.

The authors should clarify how their approach generalizes to other KBQA settings beyond the two datasets used, and provide more detailed analysis of why residual learning benefits WebQSP more than SimpleQuestions. The recommendation to restructure Section 3 is valid, as the current organization obscures the main technical contribution.
