

# Review for "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

## Summary
This paper addresses the challenging problem of KB relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), where questions must be mapped to KB relations with partial argument information and in the context of very large relation sets. The authors propose a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning (HR-BiLSTM) that detects KB relations by comparing questions and relation names through different hierarchies of abstraction. The key insight is combining two input representations: segmented word-level and unsegmented relation-as-single-token. The main contribution is enabling entity re-ranking after entity linking, which addresses a key bottleneck in KBQA systems. Experimental results show improvements over state-of-the-art on both SimpleQuestions (93.3% vs 91.2%) and WebQSP (82.53% vs 79.32%) relation detection tasks, and the improved relation detector enables their KBQA system to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy on both benchmarks.

## Main Contributions
1. A novel hierarchical residual BiLSTM model that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations with residual learning, which significantly improves relation detection performance for KBQA.
2. A practical demonstration that the improved relation detector enables a simple KBQA system to achieve state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.
3. The insight and implementation of entity re-ranking after the entity linking step, which effectively addresses ambiguities in entity linking results and improves overall KBQA performance.

## Strengths
1. **Addresses a relevant and important problem**: The paper clearly articulates the gap between general relation detection (which assumes small relation sets) and KB-specific relation detection (which must handle thousands of relations and zero-shot learning). The authors convincingly argue that this gap is significant for KBQA and justify why existing approaches fall short.

2. **Strong empirical results**: The model achieves significant improvements (2-3% absolute) over state-of-the-art on both relation detection and KBQA tasks. The ablation studies thoroughly demonstrate the contribution of each component (hierarchical matching, residual learning, and the two input representations), providing convincing evidence for the effectiveness of their approach.

3. **Practical insight on KBQA pipeline**: The paper makes an important contribution by showing how relation detection can be leveraged to improve the entity linking step (via entity re-ranking), which is a key bottleneck in KBQA systems. This demonstrates a more holistic understanding of the KBQA pipeline than previous work that treats relation detection as a standalone task.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited evaluation scope**: The paper is evaluated on only two datasets (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), which may not be representative of the broader KBQA landscape. The authors do not demonstrate performance on additional KBs or in more diverse domains, limiting the generalizability of their claims.

2. **Poor section organization**: Section 3.1 ("Different Granularity in KB Relations") should be moved to the Related Work section, as it primarily reviews existing approaches rather than presenting a novel contribution. The main contribution (Section 3.2) should be the primary section 3, and the new section 3 should be split more cleanly to improve readability.

3. **Insufficient zero-shot learning evaluation**: The paper claims to handle zero-shot learning for unseen relations (noting that SimpleQuestions has 14% of test relations not seen in training), but does not provide specific results on these unseen relations. This weakens the claim about zero-shot capability.

4. **Lack of computational complexity analysis**: The paper doesn't discuss the computational requirements of the proposed model compared to baselines, which is important for understanding practical deployability.

## Questions to Authors
1. Could you provide results on a third KBQA dataset (e.g., ComplexWebQuestions) to demonstrate broader applicability beyond SimpleQuestions and WebQSP?

2. How was the hyperparameter α (in equation 1) selected for entity re-ranking? Was it tuned on a development set or based on prior knowledge?

3. Could you provide specific results on the 14% of unseen relations in SimpleQuestions to better demonstrate the zero-shot learning capability?

4. The paper claims entity re-ranking is crucial (Table 3), but doesn't analyze why it's so effective. Could you elaborate on how relation detection specifically corrects entity linking errors?

5. How does the training and inference time of your model compare to the best baseline (BiLSTM with words)?
