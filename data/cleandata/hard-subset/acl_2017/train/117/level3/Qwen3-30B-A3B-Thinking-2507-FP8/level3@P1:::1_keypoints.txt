- Problem: Relation detection in KBQA is distinct from general relation extraction due to very large relation sets, zero-shot relations, and multi-relation chains.  
- Main contribution: Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) that combines word-level and relation-level representations for better question-to-relation matching.  
- Secondary contribution: A simple KBQA system using relation-detection scores for entity re-ranking, significantly improving end-to-end performance.  

- Strength: Clear problem motivation highlighting three KBQA-specific challenges (6k+ relations, 14% unseen in SimpleQuestions, multi-relation chains).  
- Strength: HR-BiLSTM is well-designed; hierarchical matching plus residual learning substantially improves accuracy (93.3% vs. 91.2%).  
- Strength: Entity re-ranking is a practical, low-complexity step that reduces entity ambiguity and yields notable gains.  
- Strength: Convincing experiments and ablation studies showing consistent improvements on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to two datasets (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP); broader dataset coverage is needed.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis explaining why performance is better on WebQSP than on SimpleQuestions.  
- Weakness: Ablation studies omit hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and justification for chosen settings (e.g., LSTM size, learning rate).  
- Weakness: Section 3 organization could be improved; background material should precede the model description differently.  

- Question: Why does WebQSP benefit more from residual learning than SimpleQuestions despite SimpleQuestions having more unseen relations?  
- Question: Which specific question types benefit most from the entity re-ranking step?  
- Question: How does the model handle relations that lack explicit names and must be inferred from question structure?
