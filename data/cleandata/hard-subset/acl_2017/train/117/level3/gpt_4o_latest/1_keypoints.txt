- Paper tackles KBQA relation detection with a hierarchical HR-BiLSTM (word- and token-level) plus residual learning and an entity re-ranking QA pipeline, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Contribution: novel hierarchical relation detection combining word-level and single-token representations with residual learning.  
- Contribution: entity re-ranking mechanism that uses relation detection to improve entity linking and disambiguation.  
- Contribution: empirical state-of-the-art results on single- and multi-relation QA benchmarks.  
- Strength: addresses a relevant, underexplored problem (many/unseen KB relations) with a well-motivated hierarchical matching approach.  
- Strength: methodological innovation—HR-BiLSTM effectively balances generalization and specificity; residual learning aids training and performance.  
- Strength: practical and impactful entity re-ranking addition to the KBQA pipeline.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation with thorough ablation studies.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured writing and comprehensive experimental evaluation.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to only two datasets (SimpleQuestions, WebQSP), reducing claims of generalizability.  
- Weakness: structural organization issue—Section 3.1 should be relocated to related work to tighten methodology presentation.  
- Questions: how does HR-BiLSTM generalize to other domains (e.g., medical/legal KBs)?  
- Questions: can the residual learning mechanism be extended to other sequence-matching tasks, and what changes are needed?  
- Questions: were there scaling challenges when applying the method to full Freebase for WebQSP, and how were they handled?  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve paper organization.
