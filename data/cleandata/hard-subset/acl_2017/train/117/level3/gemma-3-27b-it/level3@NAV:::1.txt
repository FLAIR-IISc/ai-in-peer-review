This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically focusing on large relation sets and the potential for unseen relations during testing. The authors propose a hierarchical recurrent neural network (HR-BiLSTM) that leverages both word-level and relation-level representations of relations, enhanced with residual learning. A key contribution lies in integrating this improved relation detection into a two-step KBQA system, enabling entity re-ranking *after* the entity linking stage, which improves overall QA accuracy. The experimental results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets demonstrate state-of-the-art performance.

The strongest contribution is the integration of improved relation detection with entity re-ranking. Prior work often treats entity linking and relation detection as independent steps. By using relation detection to refine the entity candidates, the authors demonstrably improve KBQA performance, highlighting a valuable interplay between these components. The HR-BiLSTM itself is a solid contribution, effectively combining different levels of relation abstraction to address the challenges of large and open relation sets. The use of residual learning is a sensible addition, facilitating training and improving the model's ability to learn deeper representations.

However, the evaluation is limited to the SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets. While these are standard benchmarks, the generalizability of the approach to more diverse and complex KBs remains unclear. A more extensive evaluation would strengthen the claims of the paper. Furthermore, the description of the KBQA system itself feels somewhat cursory. While the focus is on relation detection, providing more details on the entity linking component and the overall system architecture would be beneficial. Finally, the organization of Section 3 could be improved as suggested by the reviewers, clarifying the flow of ideas.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the entity linking component used in your KBQA system? What linker was used, and were any modifications made to it?
2.  How sensitive is the performance of the re-ranking step (Section 4.1) to the hyperparameter α? Did you observe significant variations in performance across different values of α?
3.  Are there any plans to evaluate the proposed method on other KBQA datasets or KBs beyond SimpleQuestions and WebQSP?
