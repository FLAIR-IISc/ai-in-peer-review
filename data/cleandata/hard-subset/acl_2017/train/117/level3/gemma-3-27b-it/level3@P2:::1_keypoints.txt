- Problem: Tackles relation detection for KBQA under large relation vocabularies and zero-shot settings.  
- Method: Proposes a hierarchical residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) combining word-level and relation-level representations with residual connections.  
- System integration: Integrates the relation detector into a KBQA pipeline with entity re-ranking and shows improved end-to-end QA performance.  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with statistically significant gains.  
- Strength — empirical: Strong experimental results with ablation studies confirming component contributions.  
- Strength — representation: Novel hierarchical matching of word-level and relation-level representations improves handling of unseen/large relation sets.  
- Strength — practicality: Demonstrates practical gains by integrating relation detection and entity re-ranking into a KBQA system.  
- Strength — analysis: Thorough ablation and analysis provide insight into component importance.  
- Weakness — datasets: Evaluation limited to SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, lacking broader or more complex real-world datasets.  
- Weakness — entity linker dependence: System relies on external entity linkers, limiting end-to-end claims and performance dependence on linker quality.  
- Weakness — model complexity: HR-BiLSTM is relatively complex; clearer intuition, visualizations, and explanation of residuals would help.  
- Question: How sensitive is overall performance to the accuracy of the initial entity linker and can linker errors be further mitigated?  
- Question: Why does residual learning help here — mainly for training depth or for learning better representations?  
- Question: When and how is constraint detection applied, and how is the filtering threshold determined?  
- Additional comment: Paper organization could be improved (e.g., move related work earlier and expand technical core).  
- Additional comment: Include discussion of computational cost compared to prior methods.  
- Additional comment: Add qualitative examples of question→relation predictions to illustrate strengths and failure modes.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper with novel, effective approach; recommend acceptance.
