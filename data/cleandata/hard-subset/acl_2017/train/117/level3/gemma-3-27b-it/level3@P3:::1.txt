This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), specifically focusing on scenarios with large relation sets and potential for unseen relations. The authors propose a hierarchical recurrent neural network (HR-BiLSTM) enhanced with residual learning to compare questions and relation names at different levels of abstraction. A key contribution lies in integrating this improved relation detector into a KBQA pipeline to enable entity re-ranking, ultimately leading to improved QA performance. The experimental results demonstrate state-of-the-art accuracy on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) benchmarks.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Novel Hierarchical Relation Representation & Matching:** The combination of word-level and relation-level representations, coupled with the residual learning mechanism within the BiLSTM architecture, is a significant contribution. This allows the model to effectively handle both seen and unseen relations, addressing a core challenge in KBQA. The residual connections demonstrably improve training stability and the learning of abstract representations.
2. **Effective Entity Re-ranking for KBQA:** The integration of the relation detector into a two-step KBQA process, specifically using it for entity re-ranking *before* relation detection, is a crucial improvement. This addresses the ambiguity inherent in entity linking and demonstrably improves the overall QA accuracy. This is a practical and impactful contribution.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP demonstrates the efficacy of the proposed approach. The ablation studies provide insights into the importance of each component of the HR-BiLSTM.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addresses a Relevant and Challenging Problem:** KBQA with large knowledge bases is a difficult problem, and the challenges of unseen relations and ambiguous entity linking are well-motivated. The paper directly tackles these issues.
2. **Well-Designed Model Architecture:** The HR-BiLSTM with residual learning is a thoughtful architecture that effectively addresses the core problem. The hierarchical matching allows the model to capture different levels of semantic similarity between questions and relations.
3. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors evaluate their approach on two standard KBQA datasets and provide thorough ablation studies to analyze the contribution of each component. The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is compelling.
4. **Practical Improvement to KBQA Pipeline:** Integrating relation detection for entity re-ranking is a practical and well-justified improvement to the standard KBQA pipeline. It showcases how advancements in relation detection can directly benefit end-to-end performance.
5. **Clear Explanation and Motivation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation behind the proposed approach, the details of the model architecture, and the experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Scope:** The evaluation is limited to SimpleQuestions and WebQSP. While these are standard benchmarks, they might not fully represent the diversity of real-world KBQA scenarios. Extending the evaluation to other datasets with different characteristics (e.g., more complex questions, different knowledge graphs) would strengthen the findings.
2. **Dependence on Existing Entity Linkers:** The performance of the system is dependent on the accuracy of the initial entity linking step. While the re-ranking helps, errors in the initial linking can still propagate through the pipeline. The paper doesn’t deeply investigate the impact of different entity linkers on the final performance.
3. **Complexity of Hyperparameter Tuning:** The number of hyperparameters involved (LSTM size, learning rate, alpha values, thresholds) likely requires significant tuning effort. The paper doesn't detail the tuning process comprehensively.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** A more detailed error analysis would provide valuable insights into the types of questions and relations that the model struggles with. This could inform future research directions.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. How sensitive are the results to the performance of the initial entity linker? Did you experiment with different entity linkers, and how did that affect the performance of the re-ranking step?
2. Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and the methods used (e.g., grid search, random search)?
3. What types of errors does the model still make? Providing examples of failed queries and analyzing the underlying reasons would be very helpful.
4. Do you anticipate the proposed HR-BiLSTM model benefiting from pre-training on a larger corpus of text and relation data?
