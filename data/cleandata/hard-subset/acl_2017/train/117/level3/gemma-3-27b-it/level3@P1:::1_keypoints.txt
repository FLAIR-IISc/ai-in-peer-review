- Tackles relation detection for KBQA under large relation sets and unseen (zero-shot) relations at test time.
- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM combining word-level and relation-level relation representations.
- Uses deep BiLSTMs with residual connections to learn multiple abstraction levels for question-to-relation matching.
- Integrates the improved relation detector into a full KBQA pipeline, including an entity re-ranking step.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP with strong ablation studies.
- Directly addresses scale and zero-shot learning challenges in KBQA.
- Hierarchical matching and residual learning are well-motivated and effectively implemented.
- Experimental validation is thorough, with comparisons to strong baselines and component ablations.
- Entity re-ranking demonstrates practical end-to-end gains for KBQA accuracy.
- Paper is clearly written with helpful diagrams and tables.
- Evaluation is limited to SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, lacking broader dataset diversity.
- System appears complex with multiple components; computational cost and deployment trade-offs are not analyzed.
- Hyperparameter tuning details and sensitivity analysis are insufficient.
- Lacks qualitative error analysis and discussion of failure cases.
- Question: How sensitive is HR-BiLSTM to the quality of initial entity linking and would a stronger linker improve results?
- Question: Please provide more details on hyperparameter selection (learning rate, hidden size, etc.).
- Question: What is the relative contribution of relation detection score versus original entity linker score in re-ranking?
- Question: What percentage of questions benefit from constraint detection and by how much?
- Suggestion: Move Section 3.1 to related work and make the hierarchical matching presentation more concise.
- Suggestion: Clarify relation-level vs word-level representations with a simpler diagram and discuss future work (e.g., transformers, noisy/incomplete KBs).
