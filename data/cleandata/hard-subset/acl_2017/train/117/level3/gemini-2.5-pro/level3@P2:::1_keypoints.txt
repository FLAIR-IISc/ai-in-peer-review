- Paper proposes HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches questions to KB relations using both word-level and relation-level relation representations.
- Contribution: novel relation detector that jointly matches word-level and holistic relation representations using deep BiLSTMs with residual connections.
- Contribution: demonstrates a simple two-step KBQA pipeline where relation scores are used to re-rank entities, improving end-to-end QA.
- Contribution: achieves state-of-the-art on relation detection and end-to-end QA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.
- Strength: well-motivated architecture addressing generalization (word-level) vs. global semantics (relation-level) trade-off, supported by ablation studies.
- Strength: strong empirical gains over prior work on both relation detection and end-to-end tasks, achieved with a simple pipeline.
- Strength: effective and impactful integration of relation detection into KBQA via entity re-ranking, shown crucial by ablations.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to Freebase-based datasets; unclear generalization to KBs with less transparent relation names (e.g., DBpedia, Wikidata).
- Weakness: unclear how HR-BiLSTM gains interact or combine with other advanced KBQA components like beam search or feature-based re-rankers.
- Weakness: model presentation (Section 3) is dense and could be reorganized into clearer subsections for readability.
- Question: please report scalability and the exact values of K (initial entity candidates) and K' (re-ranked candidates) used, since scoring all relations for many entities may be costly.
- Question: clarify the role of residual connections—are they primarily for gradient flow or is there a concrete residual-fitting mechanism given the additive final representation and single cosine similarity?
