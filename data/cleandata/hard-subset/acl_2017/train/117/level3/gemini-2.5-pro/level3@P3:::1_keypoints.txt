1. Presents HR-BiLSTM, a novel relation detection model for KBQA that matches questions to relations at two granularities (word-level and relation-level).  
2. Uses deep BiLSTMs with residual connections to build hierarchical question representations for more effective matching.  
3. Applies relation detection to re-rank entity linking candidates, reducing entity ambiguity before final KBQA inference.  
4. Achieves state-of-the-art performance on relation detection and end-to-end KBQA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
5. Demonstrates strong experimental validation with comprehensive evaluations and detailed ablation studies.  
6. The dual relation views (word-level for generalization, relation-level for global context) are well-motivated and effective.  
7. Builds a simpler KBQA system that outperforms prior complex methods without heavy feature engineering or joint inference.  
8. Evaluation is limited to Freebase-based datasets; generalizability to other KBs (e.g., Wikidata, DBpedia) is not shown.  
9. Paper organization could be clearer—Section 3’s motivation and model description should be better structured for readability.  
10. Potential scalability/runtime concerns: re-ranking requires scoring all relations for top-K entities, which may be expensive for high-degree entities.  
11. Question: How does the re-ranking scale in practice for entities with thousands of relations, and have pruning strategies been explored?  
12. Question: Can the authors analyze why the ensemble (char-3-gram BiCNN, HR-CNN, HR-BiLSTM) is complementary and which question types each model handles better?
