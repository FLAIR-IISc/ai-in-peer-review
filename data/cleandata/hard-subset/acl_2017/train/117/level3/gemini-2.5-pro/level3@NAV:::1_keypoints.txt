- Proposes HR-BiLSTM for relation detection in KBQA, representing relations both as word sequences and single tokens to improve matching and handle ambiguity.
- Uses the relation detector to re-rank entity candidates in the KBQA pipeline, disambiguating entities before finalizing the topic entity.
- HR-BiLSTM combines word-level and relation-level representations with residual connections to leverage generalizability for unseen relations and global context for seen ones.
- Addresses an important KBQA challenge: mapping questions to relations under large relation sets and ambiguous/partial mentions.
- Entity re-ranking is a simple but effective pipeline-level contribution; ablation shows a significant performance drop without it.
- Strong empirical validation: state-of-the-art relation detection and competitive/exceeding KBQA results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, with ablations supporting core design choices.
- Evaluation is limited to two datasets; claims about zero-shot and large-scale, long-tail relations need testing on datasets with greater vocabulary mismatch or long-tail distributions.
- Paper organization could be improved (e.g., relocate/meld Section 3.1 and better subdivide the model description for clarity).
