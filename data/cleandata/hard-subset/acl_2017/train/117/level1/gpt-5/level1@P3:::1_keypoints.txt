- Paper proposes HR-BiLSTM: multi-granularity (word-level + relation-level) relation detection with hierarchical residual matching and stacked BiLSTMs for KBQA.
- Presents a simple KBQA pipeline using relation detection for entity reranking and relation-chain selection, achieving strong results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Effectively models multi-granularity relation representations, improving generalization to unseen relations.
- Hierarchical residual matching yields consistent gains, especially for multi-hop relations, and stabilizes training.
- Using relation detection for entity reranking is simple and materially improves end-to-end QA accuracy.
- Strong empirical evaluation with reimplemented baselines, ablations, and reported p-values supporting significance.
- Method is simple and portable, relying on common components (pretrained embeddings, BiLSTMs, cosine loss).

- Novelty is incremental; combines existing ideas (tokenized relation matching, residuals) rather than introducing a fundamental modeling advance.
- Limited analysis of zero-shot/unseen relation performance and breakdown by chain length.
- Attention baseline may be under-specified and possibly suboptimal, weakening comparative claims.
- Reproducibility concerns: WebQSP relation dataset release, candidate generation details, and hyperparameter settings are under-specified.
- Constraint detection relies on brittle heuristics (character-overlap rules) that may hurt robustness and portability.

- Can you report relation detection accuracy separately for seen vs unseen relations and by relation-chain length?
- Which residual variant worked best and were alternatives (layer norm, highway) tried?
- What maximum chain length and average candidate pool sizes were used on WebQSP; what are practical values of l, K, K0?
- How were mixing weights (alpha, lambda) tuned and how stable are results to their values?
- What exact attention architecture was implemented for the baseline and were attention variants over both layers tried?
- What statistical test produced the p-values in Table 2?
- What is the runtime/scalability cost of scoring relations during entity reranking on larger KBs?
- Will you release the WebQSP relation detection dataset, code, and full hyperparameters?

- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper with practical impact and convincing ablations; recommend acceptance pending improved analysis and reproducibility details.
