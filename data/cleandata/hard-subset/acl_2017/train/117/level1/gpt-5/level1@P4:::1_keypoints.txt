- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM matching questions to word-level and relation-name representations for KBQA relation detection.
- Uses two BiLSTM layers for questions with residual connections to stabilize training and encourage complementary matching.
- Integrates relation detector into a simple KBQA pipeline with two-step relation detection: entity re-ranking from raw question, then relation detection conditioned on the chosen entity, plus optional constraint detection.
- Contribution: hierarchical residual matching that combines relation-word and relation-name representations and outperforms naïve combination/attention baselines.
- Contribution: pragmatic two-step KBQA integration where relation scores improve entity disambiguation and relation search, yielding state-of-the-art or competitive results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains (≈2–3% absolute) on relation detection across SimpleQuestions (zero-shot relations) and WebQSP (relation chains).
- Strength: Two-step pipeline shows end-task impact with 2–3% end-to-end gains over baselines using the same pipeline but different relation detectors.
- Strength: Clear motivation for multi-granularity relation modeling to address data sparsity and generalization, and for deeper question encoders to match varied phrase lengths.
- Strength: Thorough ablations contrasting residual vs weighted sum vs attention, BiLSTM vs CNN, and impact of removing relation words/names.
- Strength: Practical implementation using standard entity linkers, pre-trained embeddings, cosine similarity and max-pooling, making it efficient and easy to implement.
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—residual RNNs and hierarchical matching are established; primary novelty is their combination for this task.
- Weakness: Some technical details are underspecified or inconsistent (ranking loss form, relation-LSTM initialization and parameter sharing).
- Weakness: Limited analysis of zero-shot and OOV generalization—no breakdown of performance by seen vs unseen relations or by chain length.
- Weakness: Reproducibility and evaluation comparability concerns—WebQSP relation detection dataset is not yet public and different works use different entity linkers.
- Weakness: Constraint detection is heuristic and brittle; pipeline uses top-1 beam for query generation, increasing error propagation and unclear headroom from learned constraint models or wider beam search.
- Question: Please clarify the exact ranking loss (margin γ and sign) and negative sampling strategy per question.
- Question: Do word-level and relation-level encoders share parameters/embeddings, and how exactly are relation LSTMs initialized and handle unseen tokens?
- Question: Please report relation detection accuracy split by seen vs unseen relations (SimpleQuestions) and by chain length (WebQSP), plus corresponding end-to-end QA accuracies.
- Question: What are average number of relations scored in re-ranking, computational cost, sensitivity to hyperparameters K/K′/l/α, and improvement in top-1 entity linking due to re-ranking?
- Question: How many constraints are typically added, sensitivity to threshold θ, and have you tried beam search over (entity, relation) pairs before constraint detection?
- Question: Will you release the WebQSP relation detection dataset, code, and full hyperparameters to enable reproduction?
