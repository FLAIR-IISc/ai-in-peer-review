- Proposes HR-BiLSTM for relation detection that matches questions to relations at relation-name (global) and word (local) granularities with residual connections across BiLSTM layers.  
- Embeds relation detection in a KBQA pipeline using relation-aware entity re-ranking, final relation/core-chain selection, and optional lightweight constraint detection.  
- Reports state-of-the-art or competitive gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, with ~2–3% absolute improvements in relation detection and end-to-end accuracy.  

- Main contribution: a practical HR-BiLSTM architecture combining multi-granularity relation representations and residual hierarchical matching that improves relation detection accuracy.  
- Main contribution: a simple, effective KBQA pipeline using relation detection to re-rank entities and improve end-to-end results without complex joint inference.  
- Main contribution: clear ablations demonstrating the value of multi-granularity signals and residual connections, notably on multi-relation WebQSP.  

- Strength: Strong empirical results with informative ablations showing component importance and consistent end-to-end gains.  
- Strength: Tackles KBQA-specific challenges (large label space, zero-shot relations, chains) via combined relation-name and word-level representations.  
- Strength: Simple, general pipeline that effectively mitigates entity linking errors through relation-aware re-ranking.  
- Strength: Method is conceptually simple, well-explained, and easy to implement with clear figures and algorithms.  
- Strength: Model is modular and likely reusable across KBQA settings requiring robust relation matching.  

- Weakness: Incremental novelty—combines existing ideas (word/relation-level models, residuals) into an effective but not conceptually groundbreaking instantiation.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of key claims (no seen vs unseen relation breakdown, chain-length analysis, or detailed evidence that residuals ease training).  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—WebQSP relation dataset not yet released, several implementation details and hyperparameter settings under-specified, and no code mention.  
- Weakness: Comparisons could be stronger/fairer—missing comparisons to recent attention-based methods, uniform entity linking across baselines, and more thorough evaluation of the constraint module.  
- Weakness: No reported efficiency/scale metrics; re-ranking may be expensive for large K and candidate enumeration/practical limits are unclear.  

- Question: Provide zero-shot analysis—accuracy on unseen vs seen relations and contribution of relation-name vs word tokens for unseen relations.  
- Question: Clarify candidate generation for WQ—max chain length, chain enumeration strategy, CVT handling, and average candidate count per entity.  
- Question: Give training details—use of dropout/layer norm, whether embeddings are fine-tuned, and negative sampling strategy for ranking loss.  
- Question: Report sensitivity to hyperparameters K, K′, l, α, and λ and robustness to their choices.  
- Question: Ablate the relation-LSTM initialization from the question final state—what is the effect of removing it?  
- Question: Describe constraint detection alternatives/effectiveness—token/embedding-based matching, how many WQ questions benefit, and precision/recall of detected constraints.  
- Question: Provide efficiency metrics—training/inference time, memory footprint, and average per-question inference time for the end-to-end system.  

- Overall: Solid, well-written empirical paper with practical impact and moderate methodological novelty; publication justified pending additional zero-shot/chain analyses and reproducibility details.
