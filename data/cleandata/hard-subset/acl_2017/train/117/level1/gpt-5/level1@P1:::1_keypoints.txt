- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM matching questions to relations at word and relation token granularities for KBQA relation detection.
- Uses residual connections over deep BiLSTMs to make hierarchical matching trainable and complementary across layers.
- Embeds the relation detector in a two-step KBQA pipeline to re-rank entity candidates and select relation/chains, plus a lightweight constraint-detection heuristic.
- Contribution: simple, effective hierarchical matching architecture combining word-level and relation-level views with residual BiLSTMs, improving relation detection on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.
- Contribution: two-step KBQA pipeline where relation detection aids entity disambiguation and core-relation selection, yielding SOTA on SimpleQuestions and competitive WebQSP results without joint inference.
- Contribution: evaluation setup for WebQSP relation detection (candidate relations/chains per topic entity) with ablations on granularity, residual vs attention, and CNN vs LSTM.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains with clear ablations showing both granularities and residuals matter (e.g., +2–3% on relation detection benchmarks).
- Strength: Addresses KBQA-specific challenges—word-level relations help zero-shot generalization; relation-level captures global cues to avoid spurious matches.
- Strength: Practical end-to-end impact—relation detector improves entity linking bottleneck and boosts overall QA accuracy.
- Strength: Method is simple and portable, avoiding heavy parsing or feature engineering, with an easy-to-reproduce lightweight constraint module.
- Strength: Insightful comparisons (CNN vs LSTM, hierarchical residual vs decomposable attention) that justify design choices.
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—combines existing ideas (word/relation-level reps, residual connections) with modest technical novelty.
- Weakness: Zero-shot claim not directly evaluated—no seen vs unseen split or performance breakdown on unseen relations.
- Weakness: Limited comparative breadth and detail—missing direct comparisons to some contemporaneous baselines and unclear fairness of WebQSP setup.
- Weakness: Heuristic constraint detection and lack of joint modeling may be brittle and cause error propagation without beam/global re-ranking.
- Weakness: Missing training and significance details—some formulas underspecified, many hyperparameters and variance/runtime/model size not reported.
- Question: What is accuracy on test relations unseen during training, and how do granularities contribute on seen vs unseen splits?
- Question: Which residual variant (hidden-state vs pooled) produced the main results, and are gains consistent across random seeds?
- Question: For WebQSP, what are average candidate pool sizes, max chain length, and how are negatives sampled?
- Question: What values were used for pipeline hyperparameters (K, K′, l, α, λ, θ) and how sensitive are results to them?
- Question: Can you add direct comparisons to character-level seq2seq relation models on SimpleQuestions and recent WebQSP systems with joint inference?
- Question: Will you release code and the WebQSP relation detection dataset for reproducibility?
- Additional: Clarify the loss function (margin term appears omitted) and report tuned hyperparameter values.
- Additional: Provide per-question-type breakdowns and error analysis (single vs multi-hop, dates/numbers) to show where HR-BiLSTM helps most.
- Additional: Report computational cost (training time, inference latency) to aid adoption.
