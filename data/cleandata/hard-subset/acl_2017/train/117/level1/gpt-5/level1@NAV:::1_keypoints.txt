- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM that hierarchically matches questions to relations at word and relation-ID granularities, integrates into a KBQA pipeline for entity re-ranking, and yields ~2–3% gains on relation detection and end-to-end QA with extensive ablations.

- Contribution 1: Hierarchical residual BiLSTM combining relation word-level and relation-ID representations with residual connections for stable multi-layer training.

- Contribution 2: Practical two-step KBQA pipeline where relation detection re-ranks entity linker outputs, improving downstream QA on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.

- Contribution 3: Evaluation setup for relation detection on WebQSP (including relation-chain candidates) with thorough ablation studies isolating design choices.

- Strength 1: Clear empirical gains over strong baselines—2–3% improvement in relation detection and comparable end-task gains without complex joint inference.

- Strength 2: Well-motivated multi-granularity design that balances zero-shot generalization (words) and global semantics (IDs), validated by ablations.

- Strength 3: Demonstrated benefits of residual connections for optimization and accuracy, especially for longer dependencies on WebQSP.

- Strength 4: Relation-aware entity re-ranking effectively reduces entity ambiguity and materially contributes to performance.

- Strength 5: Comprehensive ablations covering residuals, attention, encoder variants, and component removals provide convincing evidence for choices.

- Weakness 1: Incremental novelty—combines existing ideas (residuals, multi-granularity) and may be seen as engineering rather than conceptual advance.

- Weakness 2: Limited zero-shot analysis—no dedicated held-out relation split or metric isolating unseen-relation performance.

- Weakness 3: Dependence on external entity linker—robustness to weaker linkers or benefits of joint training are not explored.

- Weakness 4: Reproducibility concerns—WebQSP relation detection construction and candidate generation details are not yet released.

- Weakness 5: Efficiency not reported—no runtime or memory analysis despite deeper models and two-step scoring over large candidate sets.

- Question 1: Can you provide a dedicated zero-shot evaluation (e.g., held-out relation IDs) to quantify generalization?

- Question 2: What are sensitivities for K, K′, and l, and what is the computational cost per question?

- Question 3: How are relation chains tokenized and limited in length during training and testing?

- Question 4: Will you release code, data splits, and candidate generation scripts to ensure reproducibility?
