## Review of "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

This paper presents a hierarchical recurrent neural network with residual learning for improved relation detection in the context of Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The core idea is to leverage both relation-level and word-level representations of relations, combined with deep bidirectional LSTMs and residual connections, to better handle the challenges of large relation vocabularies and zero-shot learning scenarios common in KBQA. The authors also propose a simple KBQA system integrating entity linking and their relation detector.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the **demonstrated effectiveness of the hierarchical residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) model for relation detection in KBQA**. While the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) are not novel in themselves, their combination and application to this specific KBQA task, particularly with the focus on both relation-level and word-level representations, yield significant performance gains on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets. The ablation studies convincingly show the contribution of each component. A secondary contribution is the **integrated KBQA system**, which, while simple, achieves state-of-the-art results, demonstrating the practical impact of the improved relation detection module.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The HR-BiLSTM model consistently outperforms baselines on both datasets, establishing a clear performance improvement.
*   **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies effectively isolate the contribution of each component of the proposed model, providing valuable insights into its design.
*   **Practical System Integration:** The KBQA system demonstrates the real-world applicability of the proposed relation detection method.
*   **Clear Problem Formulation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of KB relation detection compared to general relation extraction, justifying the proposed approach.
*   **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with a logical flow of ideas.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** The individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections, hierarchical matching) are well-established techniques. The novelty primarily resides in their specific combination and application to KBQA.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but doesn't provide sufficient detail on the search space or the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings.
*   **Lack of Error Analysis:** A more detailed error analysis would provide valuable insights into the limitations of the model and potential areas for future improvement. What types of relations are still difficult to detect?

**Questions to Authors:**

*   Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the search space and the impact of different hyperparameters on performance?
*   Can you provide some examples of questions where the model fails, and discuss the potential reasons for these failures?
*   How does the performance of the model degrade when the training data is limited or noisy?
