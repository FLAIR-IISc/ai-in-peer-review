**Summary**

This paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) tasks. The model, called Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM), uses a hierarchical matching approach to compare questions and relation names via different levels of abstraction. The model also employs residual learning to facilitate training and improve performance. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on two KBQA benchmarks, SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, achieving state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation tasks.

**Contributions**

1. **Improved Relation Detection Model**: The HR-BiLSTM model is a novel contribution to the field of KBQA, offering a hierarchical matching approach that effectively captures different levels of abstraction in relation names and question texts.
2. **Residual Learning for Sequence Matching**: The authors' use of residual learning to facilitate training and improve performance is a significant contribution, as it addresses the challenges of training deep neural networks for sequence matching tasks.
3. **Simple KBQA System**: The authors' proposal of a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and relation detection is a valuable contribution, as it provides a straightforward and effective approach to KBQA tasks.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The HR-BiLSTM model achieves state-of-the-art results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP benchmarks, demonstrating its effectiveness in KBQA tasks.
2. **Robustness to Unseen Relations**: The model's ability to handle unseen relations is a significant strength, as it addresses a common challenge in KBQA tasks where relations may not be present in the training data.
3. **Simple and Effective KBQA System**: The authors' proposed KBQA system is easy to implement and provides a straightforward approach to KBQA tasks, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Analysis of Hyperparameters**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameters used in their model, which may limit the reproducibility of their results.
2. **Lack of Comparison to Other KBQA Systems**: The authors primarily compare their model to other relation detection models, but do not provide a comprehensive comparison to other KBQA systems, which may limit the understanding of their model's strengths and weaknesses.
3. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis**: The authors do not provide a detailed error analysis of their model, which may limit the understanding of its limitations and areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the hyperparameters used in your model, including the learning rate, hidden state size, and number of training epochs?
2. How does your model compare to other KBQA systems, such as those using joint inference or feature-based re-ranking?
3. Can you provide a more detailed error analysis of your model, including an examination of the types of errors it makes and the factors that contribute to these errors?
