- Proposes HR-BiLSTM, a KB relation detector performing hierarchical matching between questions and relation names using deep residual BiLSTMs.  
- Integrates relation detection with a simple KBQA pipeline combining entity linking and relation scoring to mutually improve performance.  
- Introduces hierarchical residual learning to capture multiple abstraction levels and improve matching accuracy.  
- Reports outstanding relation detection results and state-of-the-art KBQA accuracy on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets.  
- Novel hierarchical residual architecture distinguishing the work from standard BiLSTM-based matchers.  
- Simple, practical KBQA system design that leverages improved relation detection for end-to-end gains.  
- Evaluation limited to only two datasets, reducing evidence of generalizability.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to other recent state-of-the-art approaches (e.g., attention-based or graph-based models).  
- Insufficient in-depth analysis of HR-BiLSTM’s internal behavior, failure modes, and comparative strengths/weaknesses.  
- Asks for broader evaluation on additional datasets such as WikiQA or TREC.  
- Requests comparisons with attention- and graph-based state-of-the-art models.  
- Requests more detailed analysis of HR-BiLSTM’s strengths, weaknesses, and comparative behavior.
