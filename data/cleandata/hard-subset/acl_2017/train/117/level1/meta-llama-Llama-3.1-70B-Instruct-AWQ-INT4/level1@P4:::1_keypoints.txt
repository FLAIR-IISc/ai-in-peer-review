- Summary: Proposes HR-BiLSTM, a hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches questions to KB relations at relation- and word-levels using deep BiLSTMs and residual learning.  
- Contribution: Proposes HR-BiLSTM, a novel relation detection model that outperforms prior methods.  
- Contribution: Introduces hierarchical matching to align questions with relations at multiple granularities.  
- Contribution: Applies residual learning to ease training and produce more abstract question representations.  
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art results on single-relation and multi-relation KBQA relation detection.  
- Strength: Empirically demonstrates that residual learning improves model performance.  
- Strength: Presents a simple KBQA pipeline combining entity linking and relation detection that attains SOTA results.  
- Weakness: Provides limited analysis of how residual learning yields performance gains.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to other relation-detection approaches, such as attention-based methods.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is confined to only SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, limiting evidence of generalizability.  
- Question: Please provide more details on how residual learning improves HR-BiLSTM performance.  
- Question: How does HR-BiLSTM compare empirically with attention-based and other relation-detection methods?  
- Question: Can you evaluate HR-BiLSTM on additional KBQA datasets to demonstrate broader generalizability?
