**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA). The model, called Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM), uses a hierarchical matching approach to match questions to KB relations. The model represents relations at different levels of granularity (relation-level and word-level) and uses deep bidirectional LSTMs to learn different levels of question representations. The model is enhanced by residual learning, which makes the model training easier and results in more abstract question representations.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Improved Relation Detection Model**: The paper proposes a novel KB relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, which outperforms previous methods on KB relation detection tasks.
2. **Hierarchical Matching Approach**: The paper introduces a hierarchical matching approach that matches questions to KB relations at different levels of granularity.
3. **Residual Learning**: The paper applies residual learning to the HR-BiLSTM model, which improves the model's performance on KB relation detection tasks.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The HR-BiLSTM model outperforms previous methods on KB relation detection tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.
2. **Effective Use of Residual Learning**: The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of residual learning in improving the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model.
3. **Simple KBQA System**: The paper proposes a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and relation detection, which achieves state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Analysis of Residual Learning**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of how residual learning improves the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model.
2. **Lack of Comparison with Other Methods**: The paper does not compare the HR-BiLSTM model with other relation detection methods, such as attention-based methods.
3. **Limited Evaluation on KBQA Tasks**: The paper only evaluates the HR-BiLSTM model on two KBQA tasks, SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how residual learning improves the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model?
2. How does the HR-BiLSTM model compare with other relation detection methods, such as attention-based methods?
3. Can you evaluate the HR-BiLSTM model on more KBQA tasks to demonstrate its generalizability?
