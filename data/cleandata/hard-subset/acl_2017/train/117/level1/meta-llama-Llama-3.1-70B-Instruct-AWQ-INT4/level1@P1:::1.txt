**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA) by improving the relation detection component. The authors introduce a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning, which detects KB relations given an input question. The model uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction. The authors also propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector. Experimental results show that the approach achieves state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Improved Relation Detection Model**: The authors propose a novel relation detection model that uses hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations with residual learning.
2. **Simple KBQA System**: The authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector, achieving state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks.
3. **Experimental Results**: The authors provide extensive experimental results on two KBQA benchmarks, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel Approach**: The authors propose a novel approach for relation detection in KBQA, which is different from traditional methods. This approach uses hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations with residual learning.
2. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The authors achieve state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation KBQA tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.
3. **Extensive Experiments**: The authors provide extensive experimental results on two KBQA benchmarks, including ablation studies and comparisons with baseline models.
4. **Well-Motivated**: The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, highlighting the limitations of traditional methods and the benefits of their proposed approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complex Model**: The authors' proposed model is complex, which may make it difficult to interpret and understand. The use of residual learning and hierarchical matching may also increase the risk of overfitting.
2. **Limited Analysis**: While the authors provide extensive experimental results, they do not provide a detailed analysis of the results. For example, they do not discuss the types of questions that their model struggles with or the types of relations that are difficult to detect.
3. **Lack of Comparison**: The authors do not compare their approach with other state-of-the-art KBQA systems, which makes it difficult to evaluate the effectiveness of their approach in a broader context.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the implementation of the residual learning component? How did you tune the hyperparameters for this component?
2. Can you provide more analysis on the types of questions that your model struggles with? Are there any specific types of relations that are difficult to detect?
3. Can you compare your approach with other state-of-the-art KBQA systems? How does your approach differ from these systems, and what are the benefits of your approach?
