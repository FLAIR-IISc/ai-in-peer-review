**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel KB relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations. The model uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction. The authors also propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance the other. Experimental results show that the approach achieves outstanding relation detection performance and helps the KBQA system achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Improved KB Relation Detection Model**: The authors propose a novel KB relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations.
2. **Hierarchical Residual Learning**: The authors introduce a hierarchical residual learning approach that enables the model to learn different levels of abstraction and improves the performance of the relation detection model.
3. **Simple KBQA System**: The authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance the other.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The proposed HR-BiLSTM model achieves outstanding relation detection performance and helps the KBQA system achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.
2. **Novel Hierarchical Residual Learning Approach**: The authors introduce a novel hierarchical residual learning approach that enables the model to learn different levels of abstraction and improves the performance of the relation detection model.
3. **Simple KBQA System**: The authors propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance the other.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their approach on two datasets, SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, and do not provide a comprehensive evaluation on other datasets.
2. **Lack of Comparison with Other State-of-the-Art Models**: The authors do not provide a comparison with other state-of-the-art models, such as those using attention mechanisms or graph-based approaches.
3. **Limited Analysis of the Proposed Model**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the proposed HR-BiLSTM model, including its strengths and weaknesses, and how it compares to other models.
