**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel KB relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations. The model uses deep residual bidirectional LSTMs to compare questions and relation names via different hierarchies of abstraction. The authors also propose a simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance another. Experimental results show that the approach achieves outstanding relation detection performance and helps the KBQA system achieve state-of-the-art accuracy for both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel KB relation detection model, HR-BiLSTM, that performs hierarchical matching between questions and KB relations.
2. A simple KBQA system that integrates entity linking and the proposed relation detector to enable one to enhance another.
3. Experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach on both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel and effective approach to KB relation detection, which is a key component of KBQA systems.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on both single-relation and multi-relation QA benchmarks.
3. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the results, including ablation tests and comparisons with baseline models.
4. The proposed KBQA system is simple and easy to implement, making it a practical solution for KBQA tasks.
5. The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the proposed approach and its components.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach, which could be a concern for large-scale KBQA tasks.
2. The paper does not provide a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art KBQA systems, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach's strengths and weaknesses.
3. The paper assumes that the entity linker is accurate, which may not always be the case in practice.
4. The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the impact of the proposed approach on different types of questions (e.g., simple vs. complex questions).
5. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the proposed approach can be extended to handle more complex KBQA tasks, such as multi-hop question answering.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach and its implications for large-scale KBQA tasks?
2. How does the proposed approach handle cases where the entity linker is inaccurate or incomplete?
3. Can you provide a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art KBQA systems, including their strengths and weaknesses?
4. How does the proposed approach handle different types of questions, such as simple vs. complex questions?
5. Can you provide a clear explanation of how the proposed approach can be extended to handle more complex KBQA tasks, such as multi-hop question answering?
