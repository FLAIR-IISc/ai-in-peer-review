- Summary: Proposes a hierarchical residual BiLSTM relation detector for KBQA, integrated into a simple entity-linking KBQA system that attains SOTA on single- and multi-relation benchmarks.

- Contribution — Improved Relation Detection Model: Introduces hierarchical matching between questions and relation names with residual learning.

- Contribution — Simple KBQA System: Builds a straightforward KBQA pipeline combining entity linking and the proposed relation detector, yielding strong performance.

- Contribution — Experimental Results: Provides extensive experiments on two KBQA benchmarks demonstrating effectiveness.

- Strength — Novel Approach: Uses a different, well-motivated hierarchical/residual method for relation detection compared to prior work.

- Strength — State-of-the-Art Results: Achieves top accuracy on both single-relation and multi-relation tasks.

- Strength — Extensive Experiments: Includes ablation studies and baseline comparisons.

- Strength — Well-Motivated: Clearly motivates approach by highlighting limitations of traditional methods.

- Weakness — Complex Model: Model complexity may reduce interpretability and increase overfitting risk.

- Weakness — Limited Analysis: Lacks detailed error analysis on question types and relation-specific failures.

- Weakness — Lack of Comparison: Does not sufficiently compare against other state-of-the-art KBQA systems.

- Question — Residual Implementation: Ask for more implementation details and hyperparameter tuning of the residual learning component.

- Question — Error Analysis: Request analysis of question types and specific relations the model struggles with.

- Question — Broader Comparisons: Request comparisons with other SOTA KBQA systems and clarification of differences/benefits.
