- Task: relation detection for KBQA, aiming to generalize to many and unseen relations.  
- Model: propose HR-BiLSTM, a two-layer hierarchical BiLSTM with residual connections for question–relation matching.  
- Relation representation: use both word-level (for generalization) and full-relation-name level (for global context).  
- Hierarchical matching: match lower/upper LSTM layers to word-level and name-level relation views respectively.  
- Residual connection: add first- and second-layer BiLSTM outputs to ease training and improve matching.  
- KBQA pipeline: integrate relation detector into a simple pipeline with an entity re-ranking step that uses raw-question relation scores to disambiguate entities.  
- Results: state-of-the-art on relation detection and end-to-end KBQA—SimpleQuestions (single-relation) and WebQSP (multi-relation).  
- Contribution 1: novel hierarchical matching architecture with residual learning for sequence matching.  
- Contribution 2: simple yet effective KBQA system demonstrating practical utility, notably via entity re-ranking.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains (+2.0% on SimpleQuestions, +3.2% on WebQSP for relation detection) and competitive end-to-end results.  
- Strength: well-motivated, elegant design adapting residual ideas to hierarchical BiLSTMs.  
- Strength: thorough ablation studies validating hierarchical views, depth, and residual mechanism.  
- Strength: practical insight—entity re-ranking provides significant improvement and addresses entity linking bottleneck.  
- Weakness: constraint detection (critical for WebQSP gains) is vaguely described and relegated to the appendix.  
- Weakness: terminology “residual learning” may be imprecise given the additive shortcut differs from original ResNet residual formulation.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—reliance on external entity linking outputs and a not-yet-public WebQSP relation dataset.  
- Question 1: request exact constraint detection method (overlap function) and how threshold θ is tuned.  
- Question 2: ask whether other parameter-free combinations were tried and why simple addition outperforms attention, especially on WebQSP.  
- Question 3: request clarification of which three models formed the ensemble in Table 3 and how their scores were combined.
