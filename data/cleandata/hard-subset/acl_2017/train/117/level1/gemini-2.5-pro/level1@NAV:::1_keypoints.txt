- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical relation detector that matches questions to relations using both word-level and full-relation representations with residual BiLSTM layers.  
- Integrates the relation detector into a KBQA pipeline that re-ranks entity candidates before final relation detection.  
- Novelty: combines hierarchical relation representations and residual learning to handle large, open relation sets.  
- Empirical claim: achieves state-of-the-art on isolated relation detection and end-to-end QA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Strength: model is well-motivated and technically sound; hierarchical inputs and residual connections are justified and effective per ablation studies.  
- Strength: experiments are comprehensive, show significant gains, and ablations clearly isolate component contributions.  
- Strength: two-step relation detector (entity re-ranking) is simple yet effective at disambiguating entities and boosts end-to-end performance.  
- Weakness: KBQA pipeline is not end-to-end trainable and depends heavily on an external entity linker.  
- Weakness: paper omits sensitivity analysis for key hyperparameters (e.g., α and β) critical to score combination and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: constraint detection is simplistic and applied greedily to top-1 path, risking error propagation on complex questions.
