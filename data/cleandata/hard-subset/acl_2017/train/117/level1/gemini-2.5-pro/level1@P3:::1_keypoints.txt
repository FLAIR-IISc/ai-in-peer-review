- Introduces HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical, residual two-layer BiLSTM that matches questions to relations at word and full-relation granularity and is applied twice in a KBQA pipeline, achieving SOTA on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.

- Contribution: novel HR-BiLSTM architecture that uses hierarchical matching and residual connections to train a deep BiLSTM for relation detection.

- Contribution: KBQA pipeline that leverages relation detection for entity re-ranking to improve entity disambiguation before final relation prediction.

- Contribution: Strong empirical results and thorough ablations showing state-of-the-art relation detection and end-to-end KBQA performance on two benchmarks.

- Strength: Clear motivation and effective model design—hierarchical inputs plus residuals yield measurable gains, especially on WebQSP.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation isolating relation detection and end-to-end performance, with convincing ablation studies.

- Strength: Insightful integration of relation detection into entity linking via a re-ranking step that recovers linker errors.

- Weakness: Sparse technical detail and simplistic description of the constraint detection module, making its contribution to final WebQSP results unclear.

- Weakness: No analysis of inference time or computational cost for running HR-BiLSTM twice and scoring many relations per entity.

- Weakness: The system’s claimed simplicity is overstated given dependence on multiple external components and several tuned hyperparameters.

- Question: Can you provide analysis or visualizations showing that the two BiLSTM layers learn qualitatively different, complementary question representations—especially for WebQSP?

- Question: How sensitive is the entity re-ranking step to the initial entity linker quality and beam size K; can it recover when the true topic entity is missing from top-K?
