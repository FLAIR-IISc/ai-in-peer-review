- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: a hierarchical residual BiLSTM that matches questions to relations using both word-level and relation-name views with residual connections.  
- Embeds this relation detector in a two-stage KBQA pipeline that re-ranks entity candidates then predicts relations, achieving state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Contribution 1: Novel HR-BiLSTM architecture combining word/name relation views and residually-combined deep BiLSTMs, validated by ablations.  
- Contribution 2: Practical two-step use of relation detection to improve entity linking via re-ranking.  
- Contribution 3: Strong empirical results across single- and multi-relation KBQA benchmarks demonstrating robustness.  
- Strength: Well-motivated, novel architecture—combining word/name views and residual learning yields clear empirical gains, especially on complex data.  
- Strength: Rigorous experimental validation, diverse benchmarks, and informative ablation studies that justify design choices.  
- Strength: High-impact, simple KBQA pipeline showing a strong downstream model can resolve upstream ambiguity without complex engineering.  
- Weakness: Constraint detection module is under-described despite large impact on WebQSP results and lacks analysis of its sensitivity and limitations.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why residual learning works here—no representation or training-dynamics analysis to support the ResNet-inspired intuition.  
- Weakness: Overall KBQA pipeline structure is standard; the novelty mainly lies in the relation detector and its dual application.  
- Question: Which residual implementation was used (summing hidden states before pooling vs. summing pooled representations), and was there a performance difference?  
- Question: Why not replace entity mentions with <e> during entity re-ranking as well, given mention spans are available, and was this variant tested?
