1) Proposal: HR-BiLSTM model for KBQA relation detection addressing large relation vocabularies, zero-shot needs, and relation chains.  
2) Contribution: Hierarchical matching between question and both word-level and relation-level representations with residual learning.  
3) Contribution: Improved relation detector enables a simple KBQA pipeline to reach claimed state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
4) Strength: Clear motivation distinguishing KB relation detection from general relation extraction.  
5) Strength: Well-motivated HR-BiLSTM design with thorough ablation studies.  
6) Strength: Empirical gains shown (e.g., 93.3% vs. 91.2% on SimpleQuestions) demonstrating benefit of hierarchical matching and residuals.  
7) Strength: Demonstrates that better relation detection directly improves end KBQA performance; entity re-ranking insight is useful.  
8) Weakness: "State-of-the-art" claim is questionable due to comparisons to older baselines and missing recent work.  
9) Weakness: System relies on an external entity linker (S-MART), limiting end-to-end novelty.  
10) Weakness: Insufficient explanation why residual learning outperforms attention beyond marginal numerical gains.  
11) Weakness: Zero-shot learning claim not properly validated—no per-performance metrics on unseen relations.  
12) Question: What are performance metrics specifically on unseen relations in SimpleQuestions?  
13) Question: How does the model scale to KBs with 10,000+ relation types?  
14) Question: What are limitations of treating relation names as word sequences, e.g., non-sequential or non-English relation names?
