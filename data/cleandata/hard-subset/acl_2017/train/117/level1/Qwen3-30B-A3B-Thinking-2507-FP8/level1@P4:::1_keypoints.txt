- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical matching of relation-level and word-level representations with residual learning for KB relation detection, and shows SOTA gains on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP when used in a simple KBQA pipeline with entity re-ranking.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical validation: significant, statistically meaningful improvements on relation detection and KBQA benchmarks with thorough ablations.
- Clear technical motivation: well-articulated challenges of KB relation detection and compelling analysis of representation granularity.
- Effective KBQA integration: demonstrates practical benefit to end-task performance via a simple two-step pipeline and novel entity re-ranking.
- Comprehensive ablation analysis: isolates contributions of hierarchical matching and residual learning with clear performance differences.
- Practical implementation: achieves competitive results without complex joint inference or re-ranking schemes.

Weaknesses:
- Limited core novelty: architecture combines existing techniques rather than introducing fundamentally new methods.
- Incomplete baseline comparisons: lacks comparison to more recent (2016–2017) relation detection methods available at submission time.
- Limited failure-case analysis: little examination of types of questions or conditions where the model fails.
- Incomplete justification of entity re-ranking: insufficient explanation of why re-ranking is necessary and how it interacts with relation detection.
- Underexplored residual-learning rationale: lacks deeper analysis of why residual connections specifically help hierarchical matching here.

Questions for authors:
- Can you quantify the claim that SimpleQuestions has more unseen relations than WebQSP (e.g., proportion of unseen relations per test set)?
- Why does residual learning help more on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions — can you provide analysis tied to single- vs. multi-relation structure?
- What exactly makes constraint detection "crucial" for your system compared to prior KBQA work, and how does it interact with your relation detection model?
