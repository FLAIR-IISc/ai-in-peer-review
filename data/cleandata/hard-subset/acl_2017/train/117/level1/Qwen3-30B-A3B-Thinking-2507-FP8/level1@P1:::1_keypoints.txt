- Summary: Addresses KBQA relation detection challenges and proposes HR-BiLSTM (hierarchical residual BiLSTM) matching questions to word- and relation-level representations, integrated into a KBQA system with entity re-ranking.

Main contributions:
- HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical relation matching with residual learning to combine word- and relation-level signals and ease deep training.
- Practical KBQA integration: uses relation detection to re-rank entities, improving end-to-end KBQA performance.
- Empirical validation: state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, including multi-relation cases.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated model that addresses limitations of single-level representations; residual learning adaptation is effective and supported by ablations.
- Insightful and practical entity re-ranking integration that tackles entity ambiguity in KBQA.
- Strong empirical gains (e.g., 93.3% on SimpleQuestions, 82.53% on WebQSP) that translate to end-task improvement without complex joint inference.
- Clear analysis distinguishing KBQA relation detection from general relation extraction and justification for hierarchical matching.

Weaknesses:
- Comparisons omit some recent state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., Bao et al. 2016 for SimpleQuestions; recent WebQSP results), making significance assessment incomplete.
- Claim of avoiding joint-inference/re-ranking is muddled because the constraint/constraint-detection step is similar to feature-based re-ranking and needs clearer differentiation.
- Unclear how much performance gain is due to the relation detector itself versus the entity re-ranking; controlled isolation experiments are missing.
- Brief and insufficient analysis of why attention-based approaches underperform compared to the proposed method.

Questions to authors:
- How does HR-BiLSTM compare to the more recent SimpleQuestions state-of-the-art (e.g., Bao et al., 2016) not included in comparisons?
- Can you quantify the separate contributions of the relation detection model and the entity re-ranking step to KBQA improvements?
- How exactly does your constraint detection/entity matching differ from prior feature-based re-ranking methods and why is it simpler?
- Why does residual learning help more on WebQSP than on SimpleQuestions—can you provide insight relating to dataset characteristics?
