This paper presents an improved neural relation detection model for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on handling the challenges of large relation vocabularies and zero-shot learning. The core idea is to leverage hierarchical recurrent neural networks with residual learning to compare questions and relation names at different levels of abstraction. The authors also integrate the relation detector into a complete KBQA system, demonstrating state-of-the-art results on both single-relation and multi-relation benchmarks.

**Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM for Relation Detection:** The primary contribution is the proposed HR-BiLSTM model. The hierarchical matching between questions and relations, combined with residual learning, appears to effectively address the challenges of a large relation space and unseen relations in KBQA. The use of both relation-level and word-level representations is a sensible approach.
2. **Integrated KBQA System:** The paper goes beyond just relation detection and presents a functional KBQA system incorporating the proposed model. This demonstrates the practical utility of the relation detection improvements in a real-world application, which is a strong point.
3. **Empirical Validation & Ablation Studies:** The authors provide thorough experimental results on established benchmarks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP), and the ablation studies effectively demonstrate the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Performance:** The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, demonstrating a clear improvement over existing approaches. This is a significant achievement.
2. **Addressing KBQA-Specific Challenges:** The paper explicitly addresses the unique challenges of relation detection in the context of KBQA, such as the large number of relations and the potential for zero-shot learning. The proposed method is tailored to these challenges, unlike many general relation extraction techniques.
3. **Well-Designed Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies convincingly demonstrate the importance of each component of the proposed model (hierarchical matching, residual learning, and different relation representations). These studies provide valuable insights into the model's behavior.
4. **Clear and Comprehensive Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, method, and results. The diagrams and tables are helpful in understanding the proposed approach. The explanation of the different levels of granularity and abstraction is particularly well done.
5. **Complete System:** The end-to-end system, with entity re-ranking and optional constraint detection, provides a more complete and practical solution to the KBQA problem.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are not novel in themselves. The novelty lies in their application to the specific problem of KB relation detection and the integration of these techniques in a hierarchical manner. A more detailed comparison against related work that also uses these components in similar contexts would be helpful.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but it lacks detail on the sensitivity of the model to different hyperparameter settings. A more in-depth analysis of hyperparameter choices and their impact on performance would strengthen the paper.
3. **Complexity of the System:** While the complete system is a strength, the multiple components (entity linking, relation detection, constraint detection) introduce complexity. A more detailed error analysis showing where the system fails and the contributions of each component to those failures would be valuable.
4. **Entity Linking Dependency:** The KBQA system relies on the accuracy of the initial entity linking step. While the authors address this with entity re-ranking, the overall performance is still tied to the quality of the entity linker. Investigating the robustness of the system to errors in entity linking would be beneficial.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed comparison of your HR-BiLSTM model to other recent methods that also utilize hierarchical or residual connections for relation extraction or semantic parsing?
2.  What is the sensitivity of your model to different hyperparameters, such as the learning rate, hidden layer size, and the weighting factor in the entity re-ranking step?
3.  Can you provide a more detailed error analysis of the KBQA system, highlighting the types of errors that occur and the contribution of each component (entity linking, relation detection, constraint detection) to those errors?
4. How does the performance of your entity re-ranking step vary across different entity linking tools?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The notation could be made slightly more consistent throughout the paper.
*   Consider providing more implementation details, such as the specific software libraries used.
*   A discussion of the computational cost of the proposed model would be helpful.



Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of KBQA. The proposed HR-BiLSTM model is effective, well-motivated, and thoroughly evaluated. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them, and I recommend acceptance.
