This paper proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural network with residual learning for improved relation detection in knowledge base question answering (KBQA). The authors also present a simple KBQA system integrating entity linking and their relation detector. The core idea is to leverage both relation-level and word-level representations of relations and utilize deep BiLSTMs with residual connections to capture different levels of abstraction for more effective question-relation matching.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM for Relation Detection:** The proposed HR-BiLSTM model, combining hierarchical matching with residual learning, is the strongest contribution. This architecture effectively addresses the challenges of KB relation detection – handling unseen relations and large relation vocabularies – by capturing both global and local information from relations and learning different levels of question representations.
2. **Integrated KBQA System:** The development of a simple yet effective KBQA system that demonstrates the benefits of improved relation detection in a practical setting is a significant contribution. By integrating entity linking and relation detection, the authors showcase the downstream impact of their work.
3. **Empirical Validation & Analysis:** Thorough experimentation on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, coupled with detailed ablation studies, convincingly demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach and provides insights into the importance of each component.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The proposed HR-BiLSTM consistently outperforms existing baselines on both relation detection and the KBQA end-task, achieving state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions. The improvements are statistically significant, strengthening the validity of the findings.
2. **Addressing KBQA-Specific Challenges:** The work directly addresses the unique challenges of relation detection in the KBQA setting, such as handling a large number of relations and dealing with zero-shot learning scenarios. The proposed approach is specifically tailored to these difficulties.
3. **Well-Motivated Design Choices:** The rationale behind using hierarchical matching and residual learning is well-explained and clearly linked to the characteristics of the problem. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of the limitations of existing approaches and propose a solution that addresses those limitations.
4. **Comprehensive Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies are well-designed and provide valuable insights into the contribution of each component of the proposed model. This helps to understand the importance of hierarchical matching, residual connections, and different relation representations.
5. **Clear and Thorough Presentation:** The paper is well-written, logically organized, and easy to follow. The explanations are clear and concise, and the experimental setup is described in detail.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the combination of hierarchical matching and residual learning is effective, both techniques are well-established in the deep learning literature. The novelty primarily lies in the application of these techniques to the specific problem of KB relation detection in the context of KBQA.
2. **Dependency on Existing Entity Linker:** The performance of the KBQA system is dependent on the accuracy of the pre-existing entity linker. While the re-ranking step helps to mitigate this dependency, it would be beneficial to explore end-to-end training of the entity linker and relation detector.
3. **Complexity of Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions the need for extensive hyperparameter tuning.  A discussion on the sensitivity of the model to different hyperparameters and guidelines for efficient tuning would be helpful.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** While the quantitative results are strong, a qualitative error analysis would provide a deeper understanding of the types of errors the model makes and potential directions for future improvement.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Regarding the choice of hyperparameters, could you provide a more detailed discussion on the sensitivity of the model to key hyperparameters like learning rate, LSTM hidden size, and the weighting factor α in the re-ranking step?
2.  The paper mentions that constraint detection significantly improves the performance of the KBQA system. Could you elaborate on the types of questions where constraint detection is most beneficial and provide examples of cases where it makes a crucial difference?
3.  While you ablate the residual connections, you don't explore other well-known regularization techniques (dropout, weight decay) in relation to the residual connections. Could you comment on the potential interplay and benefits of combining these methods?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a brief discussion of potential limitations and future directions, such as exploring more sophisticated attention mechanisms or incorporating external knowledge sources.
*   A visualization of the hierarchical representations learned by the BiLSTMs could provide further insight into the model's behavior.
*   Consider providing code and data availability statements.
