- Proposes HR-BiLSTM: hierarchical residual BiLSTM for relation detection using relation-level and word-level representations.  
- Leverages hierarchical matching and residual learning to handle large relation vocabularies and zero-shot relations.  
- Integrates the relation detector into a complete KBQA pipeline (entity linking, re-ranking, constraint detection).  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP.  
- Provides thorough empirical validation and ablation studies showing component contributions.  
- Strong empirical performance across single- and multi-relation benchmarks.  
- Method is explicitly tailored to KBQA-specific relation detection challenges.  
- Ablation studies are well-designed and informative.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful diagrams and explanations of abstraction levels.  
- Presents a practical end-to-end system beyond standalone relation detection.  
- Core components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are not novel individually; novelty is in their hierarchical application.  
- Lacks detailed comparison to recent related work using hierarchical or residual techniques.  
- Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and its impact on performance.  
- System complexity is high due to multiple components; more error analysis is needed to attribute failures.  
- Performance depends on quality of initial entity linking despite re-ranking step; robustness to linker errors is unclear.  
- Requests for authors: compare to similar methods, report hyperparameter sensitivity, provide detailed KBQA error analysis, and evaluate entity re-ranking across linkers.  
- Additional minor issues: inconsistent notation, missing implementation/library details, and no discussion of computational cost.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant contribution; recommend acceptance.
