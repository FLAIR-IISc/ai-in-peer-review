This paper addresses the challenging problem of relation detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the difficulties arising from large relation vocabularies and zero-shot learning scenarios. The authors propose a hierarchical recurrent neural network (HR-BiLSTM) with residual learning to better capture different levels of abstraction in questions and relations. They also present a simple KBQA system that leverages the improved relation detector.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM for Relation Detection:** The core contribution is the HR-BiLSTM architecture.  The idea of combining word-level and relation-level representations, coupled with the residual learning mechanism to facilitate training deeper models and capture hierarchical features, is a solid approach to tackle the challenges of KB relation detection. This addresses a crucial bottleneck in KBQA systems.
2. **Demonstrated Improvement in KBQA Performance:**  The paper convincingly demonstrates that the proposed relation detector not only achieves state-of-the-art results on relation detection tasks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) but also significantly improves the performance of a complete KBQA system.  This is key – improving a component *and* showing its positive impact on the end task is a significant accomplishment.
3. **Integrated KBQA System & Analysis of Components:** The authors don’t just present a relation detector; they build a complete, albeit simple, KBQA system around it.  The ablation studies, particularly highlighting the importance of the entity re-ranking step and constraint detection, provide valuable insights into the interplay between different components.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addresses a Key Bottleneck:** Relation detection is often a limiting factor in KBQA performance, and the authors directly tackle this challenge. The proposed approach is well-motivated by the specific characteristics of KBQA (large vocabulary, zero-shot learning).
2. **Strong Experimental Results:** The results are consistently strong across both relation detection and KBQA tasks.  The gains over existing baselines are statistically significant and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The careful comparison with previous state-of-the-art methods (Yin et al., Yih et al.) provides a clear benchmark.
3. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies are particularly strong.  They effectively isolate the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM and the KBQA system, providing a clear understanding of *why* the proposed approach works.  The analysis showing the benefits of residual learning and hierarchical matching is particularly insightful.
4. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the authors’ ideas.  The figures are helpful in illustrating the system architecture and the hierarchical matching process. The inclusion of examples (Fig 1) is helpful.
5. **Practical System & Implementation Details:** Presenting a full KBQA system, even a simple one, adds value. Describing the entity re-ranking step is a practical contribution, as it addresses a common issue in KBQA – noisy entity linking results.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* of techniques (hierarchical matching, residual learning, word/relation-level representations) is effective, each individual component is not entirely novel. Residual connections and BiLSTMs are well-established in NLP.  The paper would benefit from a more nuanced discussion of how the specific instantiation of these components is tailored to the KBQA task and provides unique advantages.
2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper acknowledges hyperparameter tuning. However, it doesn't provide a comprehensive analysis of the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings.  More details on the tuning process and the robustness of the results would strengthen the paper. Specifically, what was the search space for hyperparameters?
3. **Dependency on Existing Entity Linkers:** The KBQA system relies on pre-existing entity linkers. While this is a common practice, the performance is limited by the accuracy of the linker. A more robust evaluation would involve experimenting with different entity linkers and analyzing the impact of linker errors on the overall system performance.
4. **Modest Gains in Some Cases:** While the results are statistically significant, the performance gains on some tasks (e.g., WebQSP with constraint detection) are relatively modest. Further analysis could investigate the reasons for these limitations.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the hyperparameter search space and optimization strategy used for tuning the model? Specifically, what techniques were employed to prevent overfitting during hyperparameter tuning?
2.  The paper mentions the entity re-ranking step. How sensitive is the overall KBQA performance to the performance of the initial entity linker? Have you explored using different entity linkers, and what impact did this have on the results?
3.  You mention replacing BiLSTM with CNN.  Could you elaborate on the architectural details of the HR-CNN? Why did this result in a lower performance compared to HR-BiLSTM?
4.  In the ablation study, why is the performance drop larger on WebQSP than SimpleQuestions when removing the entity re-ranking step?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The notation in some places (e.g., the equations in Section 3.2) could be clearer.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the approach and potential directions for future research.
*   The related work section is comprehensive but could be more critical; briefly discussing the shortcomings of existing approaches would further strengthen the motivation for the proposed work.



Overall, this is a strong paper that addresses an important problem in KBQA. The proposed HR-BiLSTM is a well-motivated and effective approach, and the experimental results are compelling. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the combination and the thorough evaluation make this a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
