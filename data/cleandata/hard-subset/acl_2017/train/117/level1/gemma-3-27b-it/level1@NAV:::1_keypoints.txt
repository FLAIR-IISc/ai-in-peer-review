- Proposes a hierarchical BiLSTM with residual learning for improved relation detection in KBQA, targeting large relation vocabularies and zero-shot cases.  
- Successfully integrates hierarchical matching and residual connections tailored to relation detection, yielding empirical gains.  
- Demonstrates clear performance improvements on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP compared to strong baselines.  
- Provides thorough ablation studies that quantify each component’s contribution.  
- Paper is well-written with clear technical explanations, diagrams, and tables.  
- Individual model components (BiLSTMs, residuals) are not novel; novelty is in their combination and application.  
- The implemented KBQA system is relatively simple, so broader integration effects (e.g., with entity linking) are not explored.  
- Unclear how sensitive results are to hyperparameter choices; tuning details and robustness analysis are limited.  
- Request for detailed error analysis: which relation types or question structures cause failures?  
- Question about scalability: how does performance change as the knowledge base grows?  
- Inquiry about future work: plans to extend the simple KBQA system with components like constraint detection or query optimization?
