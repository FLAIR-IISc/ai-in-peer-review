- Proposes HR-BiLSTM with residual learning that combines word-level and relation-level representations to improve relation detection in KBQA.  
- Shows state-of-the-art relation detection on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP and demonstrates improved end-to-end KBQA performance.  
- Builds a simple KBQA system around the relation detector and performs component-level analysis.  
- Ablation studies show residual learning, hierarchical matching, entity re-ranking, and constraint detection are key contributors.  
- Effectively targets KBQA challenges: large relation vocabularies and zero-shot relation detection.  
- Strong, statistically significant experimental gains with careful comparison to prior work.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures and examples.  
- Practical implementation details (e.g., entity re-ranking) add real-world value.  
- Individual components (BiLSTM, residuals, hierarchical matching) are effective but not novel by themselves.  
- Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and limited description of the hyperparameter search space.  
- System performance depends on external entity linkers; impact of linker choice is not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Some gains are modest (e.g., WebQSP with constraint detection), warranting deeper analysis.  
- Requests clarification on hyperparameter tuning and methods used to avoid overfitting.  
- Asks about sensitivity of KBQA performance to the initial entity linker and experiments with alternative linkers.  
- Requests architectural details of the HR-CNN alternative and explanation for its weaker performance.  
- Questions why removing entity re-ranking causes a larger performance drop on WebQSP than on SimpleQuestions.  
- Notes some notation (e.g., equations in Section 3.2) is unclear and could be improved.  
- Suggests adding a discussion of limitations and directions for future work.  
- Recommends making the related work discussion more critical by highlighting shortcomings of prior approaches.  
- Overall verdict: strong paper, recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
