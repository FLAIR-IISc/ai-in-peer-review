This paper proposes a hierarchical recurrent neural network with residual learning for improved relation detection in knowledge base question answering (KBQA). The core idea is to leverage both relation-level and word-level representations of relations, combined with deep bidirectional LSTMs and residual connections, to better match questions to relevant relations, particularly in scenarios with unseen relations or multiple relations. The authors also integrate the relation detector into a KBQA system and demonstrate state-of-the-art results on both single-relation (SimpleQuestions) and multi-relation (WebQSP) benchmarks.

**Contributions:**

1. **Hierarchical Relation Representation and Matching:** The paper’s most significant contribution is the proposed method for representing and matching relations at different levels of granularity (relation-level and word-level), combined with a hierarchical BiLSTM architecture enhanced with residual connections. This approach effectively addresses the challenges of KB relation detection, specifically dealing with unseen relations and capturing different levels of abstraction.
2. **Integrated KBQA System:** The authors demonstrate the practical utility of their improved relation detector by integrating it into a complete KBQA system. This shows that the improvements in relation detection translate to gains in end-to-end question answering performance, confirming its relevance and impact.
3. **Empirical Validation:** Thorough experiments on two established KBQA benchmarks (SimpleQuestions and WebQSP) demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, achieving state-of-the-art results. The ablation studies provide valuable insights into the contribution of each component of the proposed model.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper consistently achieves state-of-the-art results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP, providing strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The improvements over existing methods are statistically significant.
2. **Well-Motivated Approach:** The authors clearly articulate the challenges of KB relation detection (large relation vocabulary, zero-shot learning, multi-relation scenarios) and provide a compelling justification for their proposed solution.
3. **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies effectively isolate the contribution of each component of the HR-BiLSTM model, demonstrating the importance of hierarchical matching and residual learning. This allows for a clear understanding of the model's inner workings.
4. **Clear and Comprehensive Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The methodology is explained in detail, and the experimental setup is clearly described, facilitating reproducibility.
5. **Practical Integration:** Building a complete KBQA system around the relation detector demonstrates the real-world applicability and value of the research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the combination of techniques (hierarchical matching, residual learning, BiLSTMs) is effective, none of these components are fundamentally novel in isolation. The primary innovation lies in their specific application and combination within the context of KB relation detection.
2. **Dependency on Pre-trained Embeddings and Existing Systems:** The performance of the system relies heavily on pre-trained word embeddings and the performance of the initial entity linker. The impact of these dependencies on the overall results is not fully explored.  A more in-depth analysis of the sensitivity to these components would be beneficial.
3. **Complexity:** The HR-BiLSTM model is relatively complex, which could make it challenging to deploy and maintain in a real-world application. A more streamlined or efficient version of the model might be desirable.
4. **Lack of Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a detailed error analysis of the relation detection model. Understanding the types of errors made by the model could provide valuable insights for future improvements.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. How sensitive is the performance of the HR-BiLSTM model to the quality of the pre-trained word embeddings? Did you experiment with different embedding sources?
2. The paper mentions that the entity re-ranking step is crucial. Could you provide more details on the performance of this step in isolation, and how it impacts the overall KBQA accuracy?
3. Could you elaborate on the computational cost of the HR-BiLSTM model compared to simpler baseline models? Are there any techniques that could be used to reduce the computational complexity without sacrificing performance?
4. What types of errors does the relation detection model make most frequently? Are there any specific types of relations or questions that the model struggles with?

**Additional Comments:**

- The notation used throughout the paper is generally clear and consistent.
- The related work section provides a comprehensive overview of relevant research.
- The code and datasets used in the experiments should be made publicly available to facilitate reproducibility.
