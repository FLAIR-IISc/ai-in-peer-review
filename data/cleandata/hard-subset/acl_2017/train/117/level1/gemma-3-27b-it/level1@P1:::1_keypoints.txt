- Proposes a Hierarchical Residual BiLSTM (HR-BiLSTM) that combines relation-level and word-level relation representations with residual learning for improved KB relation detection.  
- Presents a simple integrated KBQA system that combines entity linking and the proposed relation detector to demonstrate downstream benefits.  
- Provides thorough experiments and ablation studies on SimpleQuestions and WebQSP to validate model effectiveness and component contributions.  
- HR-BiLSTM achieves strong empirical gains, outperforming baselines and reaching state-of-the-art on SimpleQuestions with statistically significant improvements.  
- The approach targets KBQA-specific challenges like large relation vocabularies and zero-shot relations by capturing global and local relation information.  
- Design choices (hierarchical matching and residual learning) are well-motivated and aligned with the problem’s characteristics.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies clarify the importance of hierarchical matching, residuals, and multiple relation representations.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides detailed experimental setup.  
- Core components (hierarchical matching, residual learning) are established techniques, so novelty is mainly in their application to KB relation detection.  
- KBQA system performance depends on an external entity linker; re-ranking helps but end-to-end training could be explored.  
- Model requires extensive hyperparameter tuning; sensitivity analysis and tuning guidelines are lacking.  
- Lacks qualitative error analysis to illuminate common failure modes and guide future improvements.  
- Request for more detailed discussion on sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, LSTM size, re-ranking weight α).  
- Ask for elaboration on which question types benefit most from constraint detection and illustrative examples.  
- Request comment on combining residual connections with other regularization methods (dropout, weight decay).  
- Suggest discussing limitations and future directions (e.g., advanced attention mechanisms, external knowledge).  
- Recommend visualizing hierarchical BiLSTM representations to better understand model behavior.  
- Encourage providing code and data availability statements.
