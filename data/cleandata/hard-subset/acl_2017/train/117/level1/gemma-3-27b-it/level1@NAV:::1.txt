## Review of "Improved Neural Relation Detection for Knowledge Base Question Answering"

This paper addresses the critical task of relation detection in the context of Knowledge Base Question Answering (KBQA), focusing on the challenges posed by large knowledge bases and zero-shot learning. The authors propose a hierarchical recurrent neural network enhanced by residual learning to improve relation detection performance, and integrate it into a simple KBQA system.

The primary contribution of this work, in my view, lies in the successful integration of hierarchical matching with residual learning for relation detection. While the individual components (BiLSTMs, residual connections) are not novel, their combination tailored to the specific challenges of KBQA – particularly handling the varying granularity of relations and the large number of relation types – is effective. The experimental results on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP demonstrate a clear improvement over strong baselines, establishing the efficacy of the proposed approach. A second valuable contribution is the comprehensive empirical evaluation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method with thorough ablation studies, showing the importance of each component of their model.

Strengths:

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The proposed method achieves state-of-the-art or comparable performance on both SimpleQuestions and WebQSP datasets, providing strong evidence of its effectiveness.
*   **Well-Motivated Approach:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of KB relation detection – large relation vocabularies, zero-shot learning – and directly addresses these with the proposed hierarchical residual BiLSTM architecture.
*   **Thorough Ablation Studies:** The ablation experiments provide valuable insights into the contribution of each component of the proposed model, helping to understand *why* the model performs well.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the technical details are clearly explained. The diagrams and tables effectively convey the core ideas.

Weaknesses:

*   **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** The individual building blocks (BiLSTMs, residual learning) are established techniques. The novelty is in the specific application and combination for KB relation detection, but this could be highlighted more explicitly.
*   **KBQA System Simplicity:** The KBQA system described is relatively simple. While the authors focus on relation detection, a more sophisticated integration with other KBQA components (e.g., entity linking) might reveal further improvements or limitations.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** It's unclear how sensitive the performance is to hyperparameter tuning. More details on the tuning process and the effect of different parameter settings would be helpful.

Questions to Authors:

*   Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the types of errors made by your model? Are there specific relation types or question structures where the model struggles?
*   How does the performance of your model scale with the size of the knowledge base?
*   Given the simplicity of the overall KBQA system, what are the plans for extending it with more sophisticated components like constraint detection or query optimization?
