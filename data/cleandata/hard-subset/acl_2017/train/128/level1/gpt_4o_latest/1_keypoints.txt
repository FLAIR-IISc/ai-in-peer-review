- Introduces K-SAN: an RNN extension that uses external knowledge (e.g., dependency trees, AMR) and attention over substructures for slot-filling.  
- Knowledge-guided attention mechanism enables end-to-end learning of linguistically/semantically salient substructures, unlike prior feature-enrichment methods.  
- Generalizes across different knowledge sources without requiring a fixed schema (supports dependency trees, AMR graphs).  
- Shows robustness and significant performance gains in low-resource training scenarios.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across small/medium/large dataset sizes and multiple knowledge sources; attention visualizations improve interpretability.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art F1 on ATIS, outperforming baseline and structural models, especially on small datasets.  
- Flexible incorporation of syntactic and semantic knowledge demonstrates real-world adaptability.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of failure cases and scenarios where the model underperforms.  
- Relies on external knowledge quality; impact of noisy or low-quality parses/graphs is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Scalability concerns: computational overhead of encoding and attending to many substructures is not quantified.  
- Question: How does the model handle noisy or incomplete external knowledge and its performance impact?  
- Question: Request for detailed comparison of computational efficiency (training/inference) vs baselines.  
- Question: Potential for extending K-SAN to other NLU tasks (intent detection, dialogue state tracking)?  
- Conclusion: Well-executed, innovative, and effective work with minor concerns about scalability and robustness; recommends acceptance.
