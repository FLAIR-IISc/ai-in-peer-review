- Proposes K-SAN, a Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Network that uses prior knowledge to learn attention over non-flat substructures for slot filling.
- Claims K-SAN captures structured information from small data, improving generalization and robustness.

- End-to-end approach uses general knowledge to guide an attention mechanism that learns important substructures.
- Model accepts varied knowledge sources (dependency parses, knowledge-graph relations, handcrafted grammars).
- Architecture models substructures separately, enabling efficiency and parallelizability that doesn't scale linearly with sentence length.

- Effectively leverages prior knowledge to guide attention, improving NLU performance.
- Demonstrates robustness to limited training data, useful for low-data real-world settings.
- Flexible in accommodating different knowledge representations for diverse NLU tasks.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the ATIS slot-filling benchmark.

- Performance may depend heavily on the quality of the parser generating substructures.
- Analysis of the attention mechanism is limited; deeper explanation of how attention affects performance is needed.
- Comparisons to additional state-of-the-art models are lacking for fuller contextual evaluation.

- Requests more details on the attention mechanism and its effect on performance.
- Asks how the model handles incorrect or incomplete parser-generated substructures.
- Requests analysis of robustness to different types of noise or input errors.

- Paper is well-written with clear model description and convincing experiments, but needs deeper attention analysis and broader model comparisons.
