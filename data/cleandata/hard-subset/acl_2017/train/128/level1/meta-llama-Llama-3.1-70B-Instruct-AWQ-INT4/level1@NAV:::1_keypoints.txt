Summary:
- Proposes K-SAN, which uses prior knowledge to guide attention over non-flat substructures with modules for knowledge encoding, attention distribution, and RNN sequence tagging; evaluated on ATIS with SOTA on the large set.

Main contributions:
- Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention: incorporates prior knowledge to learn attention over salient substructures.
- End-to-end learning: entire architecture trained jointly via backpropagation.
- Generalization to knowledge resources: applicable to dependency trees and AMR graphs.

Strengths:
- Improved performance: outperforms state-of-the-art on ATIS, best on the large dataset.
- Robustness to data scarcity: significant gains on small and medium datasets.
- Flexibility: generalizes across different knowledge resources.
- Attention mechanism: selectively focuses on salient substructures to improve accuracy.
- End-to-end training: enables efficient joint optimization.

Weaknesses:
- Computational complexity: high resource requirements may limit scalability.
- Overfitting risk: may overfit when training data is limited.
- Knowledge encoding limits: may not capture complex substructure relationships fully.
- Hyperparameter tuning: requires careful and potentially time-consuming tuning.

Questions to authors:
- How are out-of-vocabulary words or unseen substructures handled at test time?
- Can the model be applied to other NLU tasks (e.g., intent detection, dialogue management)?
- How does performance vary with different knowledge resources or encoding schemes?
- Can K-SAN be integrated with other architectures such as transformers?
