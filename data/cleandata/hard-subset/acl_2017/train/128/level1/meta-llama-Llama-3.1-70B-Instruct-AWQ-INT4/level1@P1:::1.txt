**Summary and Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel model, Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), that leverages prior knowledge as guidance to incorporate non-flat topologies and learn suitable attention for different substructures that are salient for specific tasks. The main contributions of this work are:

1. **End-to-end learning**: The model automatically learns important substructures with an attention mechanism, utilizing general knowledge as guidance in an end-to-end fashion.
2. **Generalization for different knowledge**: The model can incorporate different types of parsing results, such as dependency relations, knowledge graph-specific relations, and parsing output of handcrafted grammars, as knowledge guidance.
3. **Efficiency and parallelizability**: The model's architecture allows for efficient and parallelizable processing of input utterances.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved performance**: The proposed model outperforms state-of-the-art neural network-based frameworks on the ATIS benchmark dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in leveraging knowledge-guided structures for learning embeddings.
2. **Robustness to data scarcity**: The model shows significant improvement on small datasets, indicating its ability to generalize well even with limited training data.
3. **Flexibility in knowledge representation**: The model can incorporate different types of knowledge resources, such as dependency trees and Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR) graphs, demonstrating its flexibility and adaptability.
4. **Interpretability**: The model's attention mechanism provides insights into the importance of different substructures in predicting semantic tags, allowing for better understanding of the model's decision-making process.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on parsing quality**: The model's performance relies heavily on the quality of the parsing output, which can be a limitation if the parsing model is not accurate.
2. **Increased computational complexity**: The model's architecture introduces additional computational complexity due to the incorporation of knowledge-guided substructures, which can be a limitation for large-scale datasets.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The model requires careful tuning of hyperparameters, such as the dimensionality of input word embeddings and the hidden layer sizes, which can be time-consuming and may not be optimal for all datasets.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words or unseen substructures in the test data?
2. Can the model be extended to incorporate other types of knowledge resources, such as knowledge graphs or ontologies?
3. How does the model's performance compare to other state-of-the-art models on different datasets, such as SNIPS or Facebook's Multitask Dialogue Dataset?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a comprehensive overview of the proposed model and its evaluation on the ATIS benchmark dataset. However, some minor suggestions for improvement include:

* Providing more details on the implementation of the knowledge encoding module and the attention mechanism.
* Including more visualizations or examples to illustrate the model's decision-making process and attention weights.
* Discussing potential applications of the model beyond natural language understanding, such as text classification or sentiment analysis.
