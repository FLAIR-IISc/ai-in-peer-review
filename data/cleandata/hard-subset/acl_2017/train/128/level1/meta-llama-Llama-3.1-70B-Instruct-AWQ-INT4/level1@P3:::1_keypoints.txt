- Summary: K-SAN leverages prior knowledge to guide structural attention over salient substructures for slot filling, evaluated on ATIS.
- Contribution: Proposes Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention to focus model attention on important input substructures.
- Contribution: Trains the model end-to-end so attention and slot-filling are learned jointly.
- Contribution: Demonstrates generalization across knowledge resources (dependency trees and AMR graphs).
- Strength: Outperforms state-of-the-art on ATIS, especially with small training sets.
- Strength: Robust to data scarcity by leveraging prior knowledge.
- Strength: Flexible to different knowledge representations (dependency, AMR).
- Strength: Provides interpretability via attention over substructures.
- Weakness: Relies on availability and accuracy of prior knowledge, which may be lacking.
- Weakness: Requires additional preprocessing (parsing and substructure generation).
- Weakness: Designed for slot filling and may not generalize to other NLU tasks.
- Question: How does the model handle incomplete or inaccurate prior knowledge?
- Question: Can the model be applied to other NLU tasks like intent detection or dialogue management?
- Question: How does performance compare to other SOTA models on larger datasets?
- Question: Can the attention mechanism be used to explain the model’s decisions?
- Question: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary words or unseen substructures?
