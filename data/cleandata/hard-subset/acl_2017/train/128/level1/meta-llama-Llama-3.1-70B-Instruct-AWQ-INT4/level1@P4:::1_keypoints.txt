- Summary: Proposes K-SAN, a knowledge-guided generalization of RNNs that uses encoded linguistic structure to guide attention and claims better substructure capture and generalization from small data.
- Contribution — end-to-end learning: Introduces an end-to-end RNN architecture that incorporates general knowledge as guidance.
- Contribution — knowledge generality: Supports multiple knowledge types (dependency relations, knowledge-graph relations, handcrafted grammar parses).
- Contribution — efficiency: Separately models substructures and is parallelizable, aiming to avoid linear time growth with sentence length.
- Strength: Outperforms state-of-the-art neural frameworks on the ATIS benchmark.
- Strength: More robust to data scarcity and better generalization to unseen examples.
- Strength: Flexible knowledge representation supports varied NLU settings.
- Weakness: Performance may hinge on the quality of the provided knowledge.
- Weakness: Adds complexity over standard RNNs, potentially making training and optimization harder.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to ATIS, raising questions about broader applicability.
- Question: How will knowledge quality issues and their impact on performance be addressed?
- Question: Can the authors provide more details on training procedures and optimization techniques for K-SAN?
- Question: What are the plans and expected challenges for extending K-SAN to other NLU tasks and datasets?
- Additional comment: Paper is well written with clear explanations and promising ATIS results, but requires broader evaluation and deeper analysis.
