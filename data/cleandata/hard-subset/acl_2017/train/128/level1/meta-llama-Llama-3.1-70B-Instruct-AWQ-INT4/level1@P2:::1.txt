**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel model, Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), that leverages prior knowledge to incorporate non-flat topologies and learn suitable attention for different substructures that are salient for specific tasks. The model is designed for natural language understanding (NLU) tasks, particularly slot filling. The authors claim that the proposed model can capture structured information from small training data, leading to better generalization and robustness.

**Main Contributions**

1. **End-to-end learning**: The paper proposes an end-to-end learning approach that utilizes general knowledge as guidance, allowing the model to automatically learn important substructures with an attention mechanism.
2. **Generalization for different knowledge**: The model can accommodate different types of parsing results, such as dependency relations, knowledge graph-specific relations, and parsing output of handcrafted grammars, as knowledge guidance.
3. **Efficiency and parallelizability**: The model's architecture allows for separate modeling of substructures from the input utterance, which may not increase linearly with respect to the number of words in the input sentence.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of prior knowledge**: The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of leveraging prior knowledge to guide the model's attention mechanism, leading to improved performance on NLU tasks.
2. **Robustness to data scarcity**: The experiments show that the proposed model can perform well even with small training datasets, which is a significant advantage in real-world applications where data may be limited.
3. **Flexibility in knowledge representation**: The model can accommodate different types of knowledge representations, making it a versatile approach for various NLU tasks.
4. **State-of-the-art performance**: The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ATIS benchmark dataset, demonstrating its effectiveness in slot filling tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Dependence on parser quality**: The model's performance may be affected by the quality of the parser used to generate the knowledge-guided substructures. If the parser is not accurate, the model may not perform well.
2. **Limited analysis of attention mechanism**: While the paper provides some analysis of the attention mechanism, it would be beneficial to have a more in-depth analysis of how the attention mechanism works and how it affects the model's performance.
3. **Comparison to other models**: While the paper compares the proposed model to several baselines, it would be beneficial to compare it to other state-of-the-art models in the field to better understand its strengths and weaknesses.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the attention mechanism works and how it affects the model's performance?
2. How does the model handle cases where the parser generates incorrect or incomplete knowledge-guided substructures?
3. Can you provide more analysis on the robustness of the model to different types of noise or errors in the input data?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed model and its components. The experiments are well-designed, and the results are convincing. However, as mentioned earlier, it would be beneficial to have a more in-depth analysis of the attention mechanism and its effects on the model's performance. Additionally, comparing the proposed model to other state-of-the-art models in the field would provide a more comprehensive understanding of its strengths and weaknesses.
