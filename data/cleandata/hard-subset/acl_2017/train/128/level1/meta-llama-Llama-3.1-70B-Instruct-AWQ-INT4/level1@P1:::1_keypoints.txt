- Proposes K-SAN to leverage prior knowledge for attention over non-flat topologies to learn task-salient substructures.  
- Contribution: end-to-end learning of important substructures via an attention mechanism guided by general knowledge.  
- Contribution: generalizes to incorporate different knowledge types (dependency relations, KG relations, handcrafted parses).  
- Contribution: architecture enables efficient and parallelizable processing of input utterances.  
- Strength: Outperforms state-of-the-art neural frameworks on the ATIS benchmark.  
- Strength: Shows robustness and significant improvement on small datasets.  
- Strength: Flexible in representing knowledge (e.g., dependency trees, AMR graphs).  
- Strength: Provides interpretability through attention weights indicating important substructures.  
- Weakness: Performance depends heavily on the quality of parsing output.  
- Weakness: Introduces additional computational complexity, a concern for large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: Requires careful and potentially time-consuming hyperparameter tuning.  
- Question: How are OOV words or unseen substructures handled at test time?  
- Question: Can the model be extended to incorporate other knowledge resources (knowledge graphs, ontologies)?  
- Question: How does performance compare on other datasets (SNIPS, Facebook Multitask Dialogue)?  
- Comment: Provide more implementation details for the knowledge encoding module and attention mechanism.  
- Comment: Include more visualizations or examples to illustrate attention and decision-making.  
- Comment: Discuss potential applications beyond NLU, such as text classification or sentiment analysis.
