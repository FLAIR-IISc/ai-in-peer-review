**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel model, Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), for natural language understanding (NLU) tasks. The model leverages prior knowledge to incorporate non-flat topologies and learn suitable attention for different substructures that are salient for specific tasks. The proposed model consists of a knowledge encoding module, a knowledge attention distribution module, and a sequence tagging module. The knowledge encoding module generates a set of vector representations for knowledge-guided substructures, which are then used to compute the attention distribution. The sequence tagging module uses a recurrent neural network (RNN) to predict the semantic tags for the input utterance. The experiments on the ATIS benchmark dataset show that the proposed model outperforms state-of-the-art models and achieves the best performance on the large dataset.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention**: The proposed model leverages prior knowledge to incorporate non-flat topologies and learn suitable attention for different substructures that are salient for specific tasks.
2. **End-to-End Learning**: The model is trained in an end-to-end manner, where the error is backpropagated through the whole architecture.
3. **Generalization to Different Knowledge Resources**: The proposed model can generalize to different knowledge resources, including dependency trees and Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR) graphs.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The proposed model outperforms state-of-the-art models on the ATIS benchmark dataset, achieving the best performance on the large dataset.
2. **Robustness to Data Scarcity**: The proposed model is robust to data scarcity, achieving significant improvements on small and medium-sized datasets.
3. **Generalization to Different Knowledge Resources**: The proposed model can generalize to different knowledge resources, demonstrating its flexibility and adaptability.
4. **Attention Mechanism**: The proposed model uses an attention mechanism to selectively focus on salient substructures, which improves the performance of the model.
5. **End-to-End Learning**: The model is trained in an end-to-end manner, which allows for efficient training and optimization.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational Complexity**: The proposed model requires significant computational resources, which may limit its applicability to large-scale datasets.
2. **Overfitting**: The model may suffer from overfitting, especially when the training data is limited.
3. **Knowledge Encoding**: The knowledge encoding module may not be able to capture complex relationships between substructures, which may limit the performance of the model.
4. **Hyperparameter Tuning**: The model requires careful tuning of hyperparameters, which may be time-consuming and challenging.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the proposed model handle out-of-vocabulary words or unseen substructures in the test data?
2. Can the proposed model be applied to other NLU tasks, such as intent detection or dialogue management?
3. How does the model's performance change when using different knowledge resources or encoding schemes?
4. Can the proposed model be integrated with other deep learning architectures, such as transformer-based models?
