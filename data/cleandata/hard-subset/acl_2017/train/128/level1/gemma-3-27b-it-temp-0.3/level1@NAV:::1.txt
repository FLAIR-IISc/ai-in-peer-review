## Review of "Knowledge as a Teacher: Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks"

This paper introduces Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), a novel approach to natural language understanding (NLU) for dialogue systems. The core idea is to augment recurrent neural networks with structural information derived from prior knowledge, guiding attention mechanisms to focus on salient substructures within sentences. The authors claim contributions in end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization across different knowledge types, and efficiency through parallelizable substructure modeling.

I believe the most significant contribution of this work lies in the *exploration of integrating external knowledge directly into the attention mechanism of an RNN*, rather than simply using it as additional features. While knowledge integration into NLU is not new, the approach of using knowledge to *guide* attention offers a potentially more effective way to leverage structural information. The empirical results on the ATIS dataset, demonstrating performance improvements over strong baselines, support this claim. A second contribution is the demonstration of the model's robustness to different knowledge sources (dependency trees and AMR graphs) and parsers, highlighting its generalizability.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Attention Guidance:** The concept of using external knowledge to directly guide the attention mechanism within an RNN is a valuable contribution. This moves beyond feature engineering and allows the model to learn which substructures are most important for understanding.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates consistent performance improvements over several strong baselines on the ATIS dataset, across different data sizes. This provides evidence that K-SAN effectively leverages knowledge for NLU.
3. **Generalizability to Knowledge Sources:** The ability to utilize both dependency trees and AMR graphs as knowledge sources demonstrates the flexibility and potential applicability of the model to various scenarios.
4. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the K-SAN architecture and experimental setup. The figures are helpful in understanding the model's components.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** The evaluation is limited to the ATIS dataset, which, while a standard benchmark, may not fully represent the complexities of real-world dialogue systems.  Performance on more diverse and challenging datasets would strengthen the claims.
2. **Dependency on Parser Quality:** While the authors acknowledge this, the performance of K-SAN is inherently tied to the accuracy of the underlying parser. Errors in parsing can propagate and negatively impact the model’s performance. The results don’t clearly demonstrate robustness to parsing errors.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning, but doesn’t provide sufficient detail on the process. It's unclear how sensitive the results are to different hyperparameter settings.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of parser accuracy on the performance of K-SAN? Specifically, how does the model perform with noisy or imperfect parsing results?
2. What is the computational overhead introduced by the knowledge-guided attention mechanism compared to a standard RNN?
3.  Could you elaborate on the hyperparameter tuning process? What strategies were used to select the optimal hyperparameters, and how sensitive are the results to these choices?
