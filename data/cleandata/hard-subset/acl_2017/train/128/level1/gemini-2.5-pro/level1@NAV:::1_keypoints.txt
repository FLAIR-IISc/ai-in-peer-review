- Proposes K-SAN: uses external linguistic knowledge to decompose sentences into substructures and an attention mechanism to build knowledge-aware representations for slot-filling.
- Contribution: novel end-to-end architecture that integrates external structural knowledge by attending over decomposed substructures rather than treating it as simple features.
- Contribution: strong empirical validation on ATIS with state-of-the-art F1, especially effective in low-data settings and robust to different structural knowledge sources.
- Strength: well-motivated and novel way to explicitly model linguistic relationships via knowledge-guided attention; end-to-end training is an advantage.
- Strength: convincing experimental results that outperform strong baselines and improve generalization/data efficiency in small/medium data regimes.
- Strength: thorough robustness analysis showing consistent gains across multiple parsers (dependency and AMR), indicating tolerance to imperfect structure.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to a single, domain-specific dataset (ATIS); lacks experiments on more diverse, complex NLU datasets to show generality.
- Weakness: introduces methodological complexity and reliance on external parsers; paper does not analyze the trade-off between performance gains and added computational/dependency costs.
- Weakness: substructure generation procedure is underspecified and examples are unclear, which hinders reproducibility.
