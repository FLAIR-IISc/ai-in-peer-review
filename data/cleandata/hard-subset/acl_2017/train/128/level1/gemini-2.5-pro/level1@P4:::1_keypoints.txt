- Proposes K-SAN: an attention-based model that distills structural substructures from external parses into a sentence-level vector to guide an RNN slot tagger.
- Contribution: a flexible architecture that treats structural components as memory and learns a global attention distribution rather than hard-wiring tree topology.
- Contribution: strong empirical gains on ATIS, especially in low-data settings, showing the structural guidance improves generalization.
- Contribution: demonstrates versatility by achieving consistent performance with different dependency and AMR parsers/sources.
- Strength: architectural novelty — decouples sequential and structural modeling via attention, potentially more robust to parser errors than Tree-RNNs.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and baselines, with focused evaluation showing largest gains on Small/Medium data and tests across four knowledge sources.
- Strength: clear presentation and analysis, including useful diagrams and attention visualizations that support model behavior claims.
- Weakness: unclear, informal description of how "substructures" are extracted from dependency/AMR graphs, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to the single-domain ATIS dataset, leaving generality to more complex or open-domain tagging tasks untested.
- Weakness: claim of "end-to-end learning" is misleading because the parser is fixed and not fine-tuned, so the system is a pipeline subject to parser errors.
- Weakness: claim of efficiency/parallelizability is unsubstantiated — no complexity analysis or timing experiments, and attention scales with number of substructures.
- Question: please provide a precise algorithm for generating ordered word sequences for each substructure from dependency trees/AMR graphs.
- Question: was the fixed fusion weight α=0.5 ever learned or replaced with a dynamic gating mechanism to control structural influence?
- Question: were alternative integrations of the knowledge vector considered (e.g., initializing RNN hidden state or recalculating attention per decoding step)?
