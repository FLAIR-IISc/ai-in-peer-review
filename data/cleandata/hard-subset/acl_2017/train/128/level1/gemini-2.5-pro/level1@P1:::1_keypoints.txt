- Proposes K-SAN: augment an RNN tagger with attention over parser-derived linguistic substructures to produce a knowledge-guided sentence representation for slot filling.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on ATIS, with particularly strong gains in low-data settings.
- Shows robustness across four knowledge sources (two dependency parsers, two AMR parsers), arguing generality of the framework.
- Well-motivated solution for injecting non-sequential linguistic structure into neural sequence tagging via attention over components.
- Thorough evaluation with varying training sizes and comparisons to strong baselines, highlighting gains in small-data regimes.
- Provides analysis and attention visualizations indicating the model focuses on semantically salient words/relations and tolerates noisy parses.
- Key design choices lack justification (e.g., why choose root-to-leaf paths as substructures versus arcs or subtrees).
- Final model is an ad-hoc interpolation (α=0.5) of K-SAN and a standard RNN without reported K-SAN-only results, obscuring the source of gains.
- Claims of efficiency and parallelizability are unsupported; no complexity analysis or runtime comparisons provided.
- Tying weights of the knowledge encoder (Mkg) and sentence encoder (Min) is unexplained and needs ablation to justify.
- Request: report performance of the non-joint Knowledge-Guided RNN Tagger (no interpolation).
- Request: justify the root-to-leaf path decomposition and report comparisons with other substructure decompositions.
- Request: explain rationale for tying Mkg and Min weights and whether untied weights change performance.
