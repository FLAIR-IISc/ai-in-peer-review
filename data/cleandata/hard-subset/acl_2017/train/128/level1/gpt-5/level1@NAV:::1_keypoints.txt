- Proposes K-SAN: augment an RNN tagger with attention-weighted summaries over knowledge-guided substructures (dependency/AMR).
- Shows consistent F1 improvements on ATIS, especially in low-resource settings, and robustness to noisy parses.

- Key contribution: attending over knowledge-guided substructures yields statistically significant gains for slot filling.
- Key contribution: end-to-end architecture integrating external structures via a memory/attention mechanism supporting multiple encoders (NN/RNN/CNN).
- Key contribution: knowledge-source agnosticism demonstrated with similar gains across different parsers and syntactic/semantic structures.

- Strength: strong empirical improvements with significance testing and competitive numbers vs non-knowledge baselines.
- Strength: robustness/generalization across two dependency parsers and two AMR parsers; attention focuses on salient spans even with parse errors.
- Strength: interpretable attention mechanism linking salient spans to slot decisions.
- Strength: explores multiple encoder variants, a joint tagger, and reports statistical tests.

- Weakness: evaluation limited to ATIS; no cross-domain, intent classification, or ASR transcript experiments.
- Weakness: missing stronger sequence-tagging baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF, char embeddings, CRF decoding) to isolate knowledge effects.
- Weakness: insufficient ablations (no-attention/uniform, no-knowledge) and limited analysis of substructure design or relation-label use.
- Weakness: unsubstantiated efficiency/parallelizability claims and fixed α (0.5) without tuning or runtime/memory measurements.

- Questions for authors: provide BiLSTM-CRF/CRF baselines and K-SAN+CRF results.
- Questions for authors: report effects of removing attention (uniform weights) and removing knowledge entirely.
- Questions for authors: do relation labels improve performance when available?
- Questions for authors: provide runtime/memory comparisons and sensitivity analysis to parser quality (correlation of F1 with parse accuracy).
