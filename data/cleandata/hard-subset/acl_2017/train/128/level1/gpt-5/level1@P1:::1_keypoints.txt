- Summary: Proposes K-SAN which embeds external-knowledge substructures, attends over them conditioned on the utterance, and injects the resulting representation into an RNN tagger for slot filling, showing gains on ATIS.  
- Summary: Experiments show consistent improvements over several baselines, robustness across parser/knowledge types, and interpretable attention visualizations.

- Contribution: Introduces substructure-level attention to incorporate external linguistic structures for task-specific sentence representations.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates that dependency and AMR knowledge sources can be plugged into the same architecture with similar gains, suggesting schema- and parser-robustness.  
- Contribution: Proposes a joint tagger combining chain-based and knowledge-guided RNNs to balance signals and mitigate parser noise.

- Strength: Clean, general, end-to-end differentiable mechanism for integrating knowledge via soft selection of salient substructures.  
- Strength: Robust improvements in low-data regimes on ATIS Small and Medium, with statistically significant F1 gains.  
- Strength: Knowledge generalizes across AMR and dependency inputs and across different parsers.  
- Strength: Attention visualizations yield interpretable focus on relevant substructures (e.g., origin/destination/time).  
- Strength: Joint modeling reduces brittleness to noisy structures and helps generalization.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to ATIS, a small/saturated dataset, and lacks comparisons to stronger contemporaneous baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF, char embeddings, CRF decoding).  
- Weakness: Missing ablations to separate the benefits of attention itself from using external knowledge (no parse-agnostic self-attention or n-gram memory baselines).  
- Weakness: Key methodological choices and sensitivities (α balance, label usage, encoder capacity, tied matrices) are underexplored and not ablated.  
- Weakness: Efficiency and parallelizability claims are unsubstantiated—no runtime, memory, or complexity comparisons given the many substructure embeddings.  
- Weakness: Error analysis is narrow, lacking slot-type breakdowns, failure modes, and effects of severe parse errors.

- Question: How does K-SAN compare to a parse-agnostic self-attention baseline over words/segments with the same joint tagger?  
- Question: Can you report results with stronger baselines (BiLSTM-CRF, char embeddings, CRF decoding) and do gains persist?  
- Question: How is α chosen; have you tried learning α per sentence or per time-step and does that improve robustness?  
- Question: Does using labeled dependency/AMR relations versus unlabeled paths change performance?  
- Question: How many runs were averaged, what are standard deviations, and are statistical tests over random seeds?  
- Question: Can you provide runtime and memory comparisons to CNN and tree-based encoders, and how does substructure count affect cost?  
- Question: For AMR, how are substructures extracted (paths/edges/subgraphs), and does varying the extraction policy affect results?
