- Contribution: Proposes K-SAN, an RNN augmented with knowledge-guided structural attention to incorporate sentence structure for slot filling, adaptable to different knowledge sources and strong on ATIS, especially with limited data.

- Strength — Novelty: End-to-end attention mechanism that explicitly uses external structural knowledge is a meaningful and well-founded advance over feature-based methods.

- Strength — Empirical results: Consistent, state-of-the-art improvements on ATIS versus strong baselines, with notable gains in small/medium data regimes and supportive ablation studies.

- Strength — Generalizability: Method works with multiple knowledge representations (dependency parses, AMR), showing flexibility across knowledge sources.

- Strength — Evaluation & analysis: Experiments are comprehensive, include ablations, and attention visualizations that provide insight into model behavior.

- Weakness — Limited integration depth: Knowledge is used to identify substructures but relationships between those substructures are not explicitly modeled.

- Weakness — Parser dependency: Model performance depends on external parser accuracy; impact of parsing errors is acknowledged but not quantified.

- Weakness — Complexity & scalability: Additional knowledge encoder and attention increase model complexity; computational cost and scalability to larger datasets/graphs are not fully analyzed.

- Weakness — Baselines: Comparisons omit recent Transformer-based NLU models (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa), limiting assessment against current state-of-the-art.

- Question: How sensitive is K-SAN to external parser accuracy, and how does performance degrade with weaker parsers?

- Question: Have you tried explicitly modeling relationships between identified substructures rather than attending to them individually?

- Question: What is the computational overhead of the knowledge encoder and attention versus a standard RNN slot filler, and how does the model scale?

- Question: How does K-SAN compare to fine-tuned Transformer-based models for slot filling on ATIS?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and organized.

- Additional comment: Figures and tables are clear and informative.

- Additional comment: Ablation studies enhance the credibility of the findings.

- Additional comment: Releasing code would improve reproducibility.
