- Paper introduces K-SAN: an RNN augmented with structural knowledge to guide attention for slot filling, claiming end-to-end knowledge guidance, generalization across knowledge types, and efficient/parallelizable substructure modeling.

- Strength: Tackles an important NLU problem—leveraging external knowledge to improve slot filling, especially in low-data settings, avoiding simple feature-based error propagation.

- Strength: Novel knowledge integration—uses structural knowledge to guide attention rather than merely adding features, and handles diverse knowledge sources (dependency trees, AMR).

- Strength: Empirical results and ablation on ATIS show consistent improvements over strong baselines and robustness across different parsers; gains are notable in small-data regimes.

- Strength: Clear explanation and architecture with a helpful diagram and well-defined knowledge encoding and attention modules.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to ATIS, leaving generalization to other domains and more complex NLU tasks untested.

- Weakness: Performance depends on parser quality; parsing errors can propagate and the sensitivity to noisy parses is not thoroughly investigated.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning (e.g., α in joint RNN tagger) and unclear sensitivity of performance to these choices.

- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis identifying specific slot-filling errors that K-SAN fails to address and why.

- Question: How was α in the joint RNN tagger tuned, what procedure found the optimal value, and how sensitive is performance to it?

- Question: Have you explored techniques to mitigate parser error impact or design the attention mechanism to be robust to noisy knowledge signals?

- Question: What is the computational overhead (training time, memory) of K-SAN versus baseline RNN tagger, and can you provide quantitative evidence for efficiency/parallelizability claims?

- Question: Beyond dependency trees and AMR, what other linguistic structures could be used as knowledge guidance and are there theoretical limits to what K-SAN can incorporate?

- Additional: Paper is well-written with comprehensive, up-to-date references and useful attention visualizations.

- Overall: Strong, novel, and promising work with encouraging results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing evaluation scope, parser sensitivity, hyperparameter details, and error analysis.
