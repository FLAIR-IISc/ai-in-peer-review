Strengths:
- Addresses RNNs’ limitation by incorporating prior knowledge through structural attention to capture linguistic substructures.
- Thorough experimental evaluation on ATIS with strong baselines and dataset-size ablation showing robustness and generalization.
- Demonstrates generalization across knowledge types (dependency trees and AMR graphs), indicating flexibility of K-SAN.
- Clear, well-structured presentation with informative figures and tables.

Weaknesses:
- Architectural novelty is incremental; attention over substructures is a familiar mechanism.
- Performance depends on dependency/AMR parser accuracy; parser errors may propagate and hurt results.
- Claim of efficiency/parallelizability lacks detailed computational complexity, runtime, and memory analysis.
- Lacks qualitative error analysis to show what mistakes remain and why.

Questions to authors:
- How sensitive is K-SAN’s performance to parser accuracy; can you quantify the impact of parsing errors?
- What is the computational complexity, runtime, and memory usage of K-SAN versus standard RNNs?
- Can you provide example utterances where K-SAN outperforms baselines and explain why?
- Do different knowledge encoding networks (NN, RNN, CNN) yield significantly different performance, and under what conditions?

Additional comments:
- Related work discussion is reasonably comprehensive.
- ATIS is an appropriate evaluation dataset.
- Hyperparameter tuning process needs more detailed description.
