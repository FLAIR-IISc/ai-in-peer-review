- Proposes K-SAN: a method to incorporate structured knowledge (e.g., dependency trees, AMR) into attention-guided RNNs for sequence tagging.
- Core contribution: systematic integration of structured substructures to directly guide attention in an RNN.
- Shows empirical gains on the ATIS dataset, especially in low-data settings, outperforming strong baselines.
- Demonstrates framework flexibility by evaluating different knowledge sources (dependency parses, AMR).
- Strength: clear problem formulation and motivation for moving beyond flat RNNs to capture linguistic relations.
- Strength: novel architecture that uses substructure-guided attention rather than just adding knowledge-derived features.
- Strength: experimental validation supports effectiveness and adaptability of the approach.
- Weakness: components (RNNs, attention, parsers) are standard; primary novelty is their combination, needing stronger justification.
- Weakness: lacks thorough ablation studies to isolate the specific effect of knowledge-guided attention versus overall model complexity.
- Weakness: performance depends on external parser quality, with limited analysis of the impact of parsing errors.
- Question: provide ablation results showing model performance without knowledge guidance.
- Question: how sensitive is the model to noisy or imperfect dependency parses; were noisy experiments run?
- Question: discuss challenges and limitations when applying K-SAN to knowledge bases beyond dependency trees and AMR.
