- Strength: Addresses RNN limitation by incorporating structural information instead of treating utterances as flat sequences.  
- Strength: Novel use of prior knowledge to guide attention, allowing dynamic weighting of meaningful substructures.  
- Strength: Empirical improvements on ATIS, especially in low-data ("Small") settings, showing better generalization.  
- Strength: Demonstrated robustness to different knowledge sources (Stanford, SyntaxNet, AMR), indicating method flexibility.

- Weakness: Core architecture components (RNNs, attention, dependency parsing) are well-known, making the novelty incremental.  
- Weakness: Claims of efficiency and parallelizability are overstated without runtime or complexity evidence.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of attention weights; lacks deeper investigation into why and when particular substructures are attended.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to baselines using parser features are insufficient; need a direct RNN-without-attention using parses to isolate benefits.

- Question: Provide runtime (training and inference) comparisons to substantiate efficiency/parallelizability claims.  
- Question: Show concrete examples where K-SAN attends to substructures missed by simpler models and explain performance gains.  
- Question: How robust is K-SAN to noisy or incorrect parses, and have you explored mitigation strategies for parsing errors?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to the choice of knowledge representation (dependency vs. AMR, rule-based vs. learned parsers), and how should one choose?

- Additional: Writing is generally clear but the introduction could focus more on the specific problem.  
- Additional: Related work should discuss recent graph neural network advances relevant to structural modeling.  
- Additional: Experimental details are clear but hyperparameter tuning procedures need more elaboration.
