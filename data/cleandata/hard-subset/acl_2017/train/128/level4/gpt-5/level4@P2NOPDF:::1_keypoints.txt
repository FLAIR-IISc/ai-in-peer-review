- Main contribution: a simple, practical mechanism that flattens structured linguistic information into a memory of substructure vectors and feeds a weighted sum to an RNN for sequence tagging.

- Strength: idea relates to existing structured/syntax-based attention and substructure encoders (e.g., treeLSTM attention, DCNN), making it straightforward and practical though not wholly novel.

- Empirical evaluation unclear: baselines (TreeRNN, DCNN) can yield node embeddings, but the paper does not explain how node-level representations were extracted or used for both sentence and substructure embeddings.

- Architecture fairness concern: two separate RNNs (chain and “knowledge-guided”) may simply add capacity; hyperparameters and parameter counts for all baselines are not reported or matched.

- Missing baseline: lacking a single-RNN baseline that incorporates linguistic signals as features (e.g., syntactic heads, NER) to isolate the value of the memory mechanism vs. simple feature augmentation.

- Robustness question: no discussion or experiments on sensitivity to parser errors or noisy parses.

- Attention design critique: attention over substructures is static (shared across words) rather than token-specific, which may limit token-level relevance and make the knowledge vector act as a global salience filter.

- Potential bias in attention: attention computed as inner product between sentence and substructure embeddings could favor substructures resembling the global sentence, risking degenerate emphasis on frequent or broadly similar nodes.

- Generalization claim qualified: method seems to require substructures linearizable as word sequences, so incorporating non-linear structures (e.g., certain constituency trees) is unclear and needs elaboration.

- Terminology issue: calling syntactic/AMR structures “knowledge” is misleading given common NLP use of “knowledge” for external/world knowledge; a more precise term is recommended.

- Overall assessment: practical and performs well on one dataset, but limited novelty and insufficient experimental clarity reduce the paper’s impact for a top venue.

- Post-rebuttal unresolved: authors did not clarify whether baselines produced independent substructure embeddings or address parameter-count fairness, so main concerns remain unanswered.
