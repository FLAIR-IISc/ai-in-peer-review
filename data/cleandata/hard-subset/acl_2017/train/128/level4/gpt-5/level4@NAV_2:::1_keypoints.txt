- Proposes a simple mechanism to encode parse-derived substructures as a memory bank and inject an attention-weighted summary into an RNN tagger for slot filling.
- Strength: practical, simple way to linearize structured signals into plug-in vector memories, resembling prior structured-attention approaches.
- Strength: substructure encoder is similar to DCNN-style ancestor-word representations, making the approach practical though not fundamentally novel.
- Major concern: unclear how baselines (TreeRNN, DCNN) were adapted to produce comparable node/substructure embeddings alongside sentence encoders.
- Major concern: model uses two separate RNNs (plain tagger and knowledge-guided tagger), which increases parameter count; unclear if baselines were matched for model size.
- Request: report hyperparameters and parameter counts to ensure fair comparisons.
- Suggested baseline: feed the same “knowledge” as simple explicit features into a single RNN to test if a memory/attention module is necessary.
- Request: analyze robustness to parsing errors and quantify sensitivity.
- Comment: attention should arguably be token-wise rather than a single sentence-level attention, since global attention acts like a static filter and favors feature baselines.
- Comment: attention scoring uses inner products between sentence and substructure embeddings, which may bias attention toward substructures most similar to whole-sentence representations (e.g., leaf nodes).
- Limitation: method requires substructures encoded as word sequences, making use of constituent parses or non-sequential structures non-trivial.
- Terminology: calling the component “knowledge” is misleading since it primarily encodes syntactic/semantic structure, not external/world knowledge like KBs.
- Overall: practical model with good single-benchmark performance but limited novelty and insufficiently rigorous experimental comparisons for a top-tier venue.
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve whether baselines produced independent substructure embeddings, and concerns about increased parameter count remain, so scores were not changed.
