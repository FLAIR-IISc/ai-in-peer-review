- Paper proposes a slot-filling model that embeds substructures as memory vectors and injects a weighted sum via attention into an RNN tagger; also uses a joint tagger combining chain and knowledge-guided RNNs.
- Claimed contributions: flattening structured linguistic info into a memory+attention abstraction, a joint chain/knowledge tagging architecture, and empirical evaluation on ATIS with different structure sources (dependency, AMR).
- Strength: simple and practical—memory-slot + attention design is easy to implement and integrate with standard taggers.
- Strength: structured attention mechanism attends over linguistically motivated substructures rather than tokens alone.
- Strength: modular encoders and support for multiple knowledge sources (NN/CNN/RNN encoders; dependency and AMR inputs).
- Strength: empirical gains on ATIS and interpretable attention visualizations that align with slot predictions (e.g., origin, destination, time).
- Weakness: unclear baseline construction—TreeRNN and DCNN may produce node/substructure embeddings but paper doesn’t clarify whether baselines used them fairly.
- Weakness: joint tagger uses two separate RNNs, likely increasing model capacity; parameter counts are not reported to confirm fair comparisons.
- Weakness: missing a strong feature-augmentation baseline (e.g., adding dependency head indices, NER, parser-derived features to a single RNN).
- Weakness: attention is static at sentence level and applied to all time steps; no ablation versus per-token (time-varying) attention to justify design.
- Weakness: no analysis of sensitivity to parser errors or applicability to structures that are hard to linearize (constituents, graph fragments).
- Question: how were TreeRNN and DCNN instantiated—did they produce node/substructure embeddings used like the proposed memory slots?
- Question: what are exact parameter counts (or ranges) for all models, and are baselines capacity-matched?
- Question: what are results for a single RNN that simply concatenates the knowledge vector (no separate knowledge-guided RNN)?
- Question: can you provide results for a feature-augmentation baseline (dependency head embeddings, NER, parser features)?
- Question: did you try per-token (ht- or wt-conditioned) attention over substructures, and how does it compare to sentence-level attention?
- Question: how sensitive is performance to parsing errors—any perturbation or multi-parser analyses?
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not address main concerns about structural baselines and the two-RNN parameter confound, so reviewer’s score remains unchanged.
