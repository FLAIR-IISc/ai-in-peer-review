- Main contribution: a simple method to flatten structured linguistic information into a memory of substructure embeddings that augments a sequence tagger; related to prior structured-attention and DCNN-style encoders and practical though not novel.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing due to insufficient details about how baselines were implemented and compared.  
- Unclear how TreeRNN and DCNN baselines were used to produce both sentence and substructure embeddings in the experimental setup.  
- Using two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) seems unnecessary, inflates model capacity, and baseline hyperparameters/parameter counts are not provided for fair comparison.  
- The paper should include a simple baseline that supplies additional knowledge as explicit features to the RNN (e.g., head word, NER).  
- Provide analysis of the model’s sensitivity to parser errors.  
- Question design choice of static attention over substructures rather than computing per-word attention at each time step.  
- Weighting substructures by inner product with the sentence embedding may bias toward nodes similar to the whole sentence (e.g., leaves).  
- Claim of generality to different knowledge types is questionable because substructures must be represented as word sequences; incorporating constituent parses is not straightforward.  
- Calling the input “knowledge” is misleading—this is primarily syntax or semantics, not external/world knowledge.  
- Overall: practical and effective on one dataset but lacks novelty and clearer takeaways; experimental evidence needs clarification.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not address whether baselines computed substructure embeddings independently and did not justify the extra RNN/parameter advantage, so reviewer kept original scores.
