- Proposed model: encode substructures (e.g., parse nodes) as memory-slot vectors and feed their weighted sum as contextual input to an RNN for sequence tagging (slot-filling).

- Contribution: a practical, simple method to "flatten" structural linguistic information into vectors for integration with RNN taggers.

- Contribution: demonstrates generalization across knowledge sources (dependency parses and AMR) and different parsers.

- Contribution: shows empirical performance gains on ATIS, notably in limited-data settings.

- Strength: approach is simple and easy to implement compared with more complex structured models.

- Strength: flexible applicability to multiple forms of linguistic structure (dependency, AMR).

- Strength: improves performance when labeled training data is scarce.

- Strength: clear presentation of the K-SAN architecture and helpful figure.

- Weakness: baseline comparisons are insufficiently detailed—missing architecture/hyperparameter/training details for TreeRNN and DCNN.

- Weakness: using two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) is not well justified and may add unnecessary complexity.

- Weakness: lacks ablation studies to measure the contribution of individual components (e.g., attention, combination method).

- Weakness: insufficient empirical analysis of model robustness to parser errors.

- Weakness: labeling the structural inputs as “knowledge” is misleading since they are structural representations rather than external world knowledge.

- Question: provide detailed descriptions and settings for baseline models and how substructure embeddings were computed for TreeRNN/DCNN.

- Question: justify the two-RNN architecture with evidence comparing it to a single-RNN alternative.

- Question: present analysis of the impact of parser errors on performance.

- Question: explain how the model would handle other substructure types (e.g., constituent parses) and what modifications are needed.

- Additional comment: explore per-word attention over substructures as a potential improvement.

- Additional comment: note that similarity between substructure and sentence embeddings might bias attention weights.

- Additional comment: include a discussion of the model’s computational complexity.
