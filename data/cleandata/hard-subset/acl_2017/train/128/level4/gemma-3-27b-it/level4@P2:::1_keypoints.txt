- Main strength: proposes a simple, practical method to integrate structured linguistic information into sequence tagging via a memory of substructure embeddings, showing modest empirical gains.
- Contribution novelty: approach is pragmatic and related to prior structured attention/DCNN work, but not fundamentally novel.
- Weakness 1: Baseline comparison unclear—uncertain whether TreeRNN/DCNN baselines use comparable substructure embeddings or only sentence-level representations.
- Weakness 2: Use of two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) seems unnecessary and inflates parameters without justification; a single RNN might suffice.
- Weakness 3: Missing a simple feature-based baseline that injects knowledge features (e.g., head words, NER) into the RNN input to isolate the memory network’s benefit.
- Weakness 4: No analysis of robustness to parser errors; model may be sensitive to noisy substructure embeddings from imperfect parses.
- Additional concern: Attention is static across words; a dynamic, per-word attention over substructures could be more effective.
- Additional concern: Inner-product scoring may bias attention toward nodes similar to the sentence (e.g., leaf nodes); this potential bias is unaddressed.
- Additional concern: Labeling the inputs as “knowledge” is misleading—paper mainly uses syntactic/semantic structure, not external/world knowledge.
- General critique: Empirical evaluation needs strengthening and clearer experimental setup to make the contribution more compelling.
- Post-rebuttal: Authors did not resolve core issues about baseline embedding consistency or justify the dual-RNN design; reviewer’s assessment remains unchanged.
