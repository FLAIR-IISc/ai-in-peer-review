- Main contribution: simple method to “flatten” structured information into a memory of substructure vectors used as extra context for sequence tagging.  
- Idea is similar to structured/syntax-based attention and related to prior work (Zhao et al., Liu et al., Eriguchi et al.).  
- Substructure encoder resembles DCNN (Ma et al.); the architecture is practical and simple though not highly novel.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing due to insufficient detail about how baselines were implemented.  
- Unclear whether baselines (TreeRNN, DCNN) were used to compute node/substructure embeddings independently of sentence embeddings and the joint tagger.  
- Model uses two RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) where the only difference is an added memory vector, raising concerns that separate weights just increase parameter count.  
- Baselines’ hyperparameters and parameter counts are not reported; they should be made comparable to the proposed model.  
- A simple baseline that adds knowledge as features to the RNN (e.g., head word, NER) is missing and should be included.  
- No analysis of sensitivity to parser errors is provided.  
- Why use static attention weights for all words instead of computing attention over substructures per word?  
- Weighting via inner product of sentence and substructure embeddings may bias toward nodes similar to the overall sentence (e.g., leaf nodes).  
- Claim of generalizing to different knowledge sources is limited because substructures must be sequences of words, making some knowledge forms (e.g., constituent trees) hard to use directly.  
- Calling the housed information “knowledge” is misleading since it is syntax/structure rather than external/world knowledge.  
- Overall: practical model with good single-dataset performance but limited novelty and unconvincing experiments; reviewer maintained original scores after rebuttal.
