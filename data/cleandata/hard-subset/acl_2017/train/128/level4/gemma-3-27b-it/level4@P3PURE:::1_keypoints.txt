- Proposes a simple method to flatten structured linguistic information into a memory array and integrate it into a tagger for slot-filling.  
- Idea is related to structured/syntax-based attention (e.g., TreeLSTM attention) and prior work on entailment and NLI.  
- Substructure encoder resembles DCNN-style embeddings over ancestor word sequences; simplicity and practicality are appealing.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing due to insufficient detail about how baselines were adapted and configured.  
- Unclear how TreeRNN and DCNN baselines were modified to compute substructure embeddings for fair comparison.  
- Using two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) may simply increase parameter count rather than provide a principled benefit.  
- Hyperparameters and model sizes for baselines should be reported to ensure comparable parameter budgets.  
- A baseline that injects the same additional knowledge as input features (e.g., head words or NER tags) is missing and should be included.  
- The paper lacks analysis of sensitivity to parser errors, which is important for syntax-guided approaches.  
- Questioning use of a static attention weight for all words rather than computing attention per word.  
- The inner-product scoring between sentence and substructure embeddings may bias weights toward nodes similar to the whole sentence, potentially favoring leaves.  
- It’s unclear how the substructure representation (word sequences) generalizes to other knowledge formats like constituent parses or non-sequential knowledge.  
- Calling the representation “knowledge” is misleading since it mainly encodes syntax (not external/world knowledge).  
- Overall, the model is practical but not sufficiently novel for ACL, and experimental evidence is currently inconclusive.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve concerns about baseline setup or the parameter advantage from two RNNs, so the reviewer maintained their original scores.
