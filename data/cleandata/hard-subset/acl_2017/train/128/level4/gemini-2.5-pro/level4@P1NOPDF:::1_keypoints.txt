- Proposes a simple method to flatten structured linguistic information into a memory of substructure embeddings used by a tagger.
- Idea is similar to prior work (structured/syntax-based attention, TreeLSTM, DCNN) and not entirely novel but practical and simple.
- Substructure encoder resembles DCNN-style embeddings computed from ancestor word sequences.

- Empirical results are unconvincing due to insufficient detail about how baselines were adapted and configured.
- Unclear how sentence-embedding models (TreeRNN, DCNN) were modified to produce both sentence and substructure embeddings for baselines.
- Using two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) seems unnecessary and likely inflates parameters, raising fairness concerns.
- Baseline hyperparameters and model sizes are not reported; baselines should match parameter counts for fair comparison.
- Missing a simple baseline that inputs the additional "knowledge" as features to a standard RNN tagger.
- No analysis of model sensitivity to parser errors.

- Why use a static global weighted sum over substructures instead of a per-step attention mechanism?
- Weighting substructures by inner product with the sentence embedding may bias toward substructures similar to the sentence (e.g., leaf nodes).
- The substructure encoder appears limited to knowledge represented as word sequences and may not generalize to other structural forms (e.g., constituent parses).
- The term "knowledge" is misleading; the model uses linguistic structure, not external/world knowledge.

- Overall: model is practical and performs on the dataset, but lacks novelty and convincing experiments due to unclear baselines and unresolved parameter-fairness issues.
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve main concerns about baseline adaptation and parameter advantage, so reviewer maintains original score.
