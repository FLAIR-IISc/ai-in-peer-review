- Main contribution: a simple method to "flatten" structured linguistic information into a memory of substructure embeddings used to augment a sequence tagger.

- Strength: practical, conceptually simple approach similar to structured/syntax-based attention and related prior work (Tree-LSTM, DCNN), and resembles ancestor-word embedding techniques.

- Weakness: unclear how baseline models (e.g., TreeRNN, DCNN) were adapted to produce sentence vs. substructure embeddings for this joint tagging task.

- Weakness: using two separate RNNs (chain RNN + knowledge-guided RNN) seems unnecessary and may give the model a parameter-count advantage over baselines; hyperparameters and parameter counts are not reported.

- Weakness: a crucial baseline is missing — a standard RNN that incorporates linguistic knowledge as per-token input features (e.g., heads, POS, NER).

- Weakness: no analysis of sensitivity to parser errors, a key concern for syntax-dependent models.

- Comment: the model uses a static, global attention over substructures rather than a dynamic per-word attention, which seems less natural and limits per-token contextualization.

- Comment: computing attention via inner product between sentence and substructure embeddings (from the same encoder) may bias weights toward substructures merely similar to the overall sentence, favoring trivial/broad substructures.

- Comment: claim of generalization to different knowledge types is unclear because the substructure encoder requires knowledge to be represented as word sequences (e.g., dependency paths), so applicability to constituents or other structures is uncertain.

- Comment: the term "knowledge" is misleading here since the model uses linguistic/syntactic/AMR information rather than external/world knowledge.

- General: model performs well on one dataset but core ideas lack novelty and the experimental evaluation is insufficiently rigorous for an ACL paper.

- Required: clarification of experimental setup and baseline configurations is needed to judge reported results.

- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve main concerns (baseline configuration and two-RNN parameter advantage), so reviewer maintains original scores.
