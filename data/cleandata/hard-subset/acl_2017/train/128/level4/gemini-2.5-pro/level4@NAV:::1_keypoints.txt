- Paper proposes encoding linguistic substructures as vectors in a memory network and supplying a weighted sum as extra context to an RNN for slot-filling.

- Strength: simple, practical method for flattening structured information into a memory, analogous to structured/syntax-based attention and DCNN-style node embeddings.

- Strength: simplicity and practical application are commendable compared to more complex prior approaches.

- Weakness: unclear how baseline models (TreeRNN, DCNN) were adapted to produce both sentence and substructure embeddings for the joint tagging task.

- Weakness: using two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) is insufficiently motivated and likely inflates parameter count; authors must report baselines' hyperparameters and total parameter counts.

- Weakness: missing a simple feature-based baseline that injects structural knowledge as direct token-level inputs to an RNN.

- Weakness: no analysis of robustness or sensitivity to parsing errors, which is critical for real-world use.

- Comment: static global "knowledge" vector is questionable — a dynamic attention over substructure embeddings per token would be more natural and should be justified.

- Comment: the weighting scheme (inner product between sentence and substructure embeddings) may bias selection toward substructures most similar to the sentence (e.g., leaf nodes).

- Comment: claim of generality to various knowledge sources is weak, since the method requires knowledge represented as word sequences and may not extend to other structures like constituent parses.

- Comment: calling the syntactic/compositional input "knowledge" is potentially misleading given the usual NLP sense of external/world knowledge.

- General: paper shows promising performance on one dataset but lacks significant novelty and an unconvincing experimental evaluation for a top-tier venue.

- Post-rebuttal: author response did not resolve primary concerns — still unclear whether baselines compute independent substructure embeddings and whether added RNN increases parameter count unjustifiably.
