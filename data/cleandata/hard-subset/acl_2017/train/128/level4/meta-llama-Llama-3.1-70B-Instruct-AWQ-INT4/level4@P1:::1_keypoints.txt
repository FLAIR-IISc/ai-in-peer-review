- Proposes K-SAN: memory network that encodes substructures as vectors and feeds a weighted sum into an RNN for sequence tagging (slot-filling).
- Claims three main contributions: end-to-end learning, generalization to different knowledge types, and efficiency/parallelizability.
- Strength: simple, practical way to flatten structured information into a memory array for the tagger.
- Strength: approach is related to prior structured/syntax-based attention and uses a DCNN-like substructure encoder.
- Weakness: empirical results unconvincing due to insufficient baseline details.
- Weakness: unclear how baseline models (TreeRNN, DCNN) were used to compute both sentence and substructure embeddings.
- Weakness: model uses two RNNs differing only by an added knowledge vector, which may be unnecessary and just increase parameter count.
- Weakness: no comparison ensuring baselines have comparable numbers of parameters/hyperparameters.
- Weakness: missing a simple baseline that supplies additional knowledge as input features to the RNN (e.g., head words, NER).
- Weakness: no analysis of sensitivity to parser errors.
- Model question: uses static attention over substructures rather than per-word attention—reason unclear.
- Model question: inner-product attention between sentence and substructure embeddings may bias toward nodes similar to the whole sentence (e.g., leaf nodes).
- Model question: representing substructures as sequences may not straightforwardly support other knowledge forms (e.g., constituent parses).
- Terminology concern: calling syntax “knowledge” is misleading compared to external/world knowledge (KBs).
- General: paper is practical and works on one dataset but lacks novelty and broader takeaways; experiments need clarification.
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve main concerns about baseline embedding use and extra RNN parameters; reviewer unchanged.
- Question to authors: how exactly are substructure embeddings computed for baseline models (TreeRNN, DCNN)?
- Question to authors: why use two separate RNNs instead of a single RNN with knowledge features?
- Question to authors: provide hyperparameter and model size details for baseline networks.
- Question to authors: how does the model perform with different knowledge types (constituent parse, AMR)?
- Question to authors: provide analysis of model sensitivity to parser errors.
