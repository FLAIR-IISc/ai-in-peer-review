This paper proposes a neural network architecture that represents structural linguistic knowledge in a memory network for sequence tagging tasks, specifically slot-filling in natural language understanding (NLU) units in conversation systems. The main idea is to encode substructures (e.g., nodes in the parse tree) as vectors (memory slots) and feed a weighted sum of these substructure embeddings into a recurrent neural network (RNN) at each time step as additional context for labeling.

**Main Contributions:**

1. A simple way to "flatten" structured information into an array of vectors (the memory), which is then connected to the tagger as additional knowledge.
2. A practical approach to incorporating structural linguistic knowledge into a sequence tagging model.

**Strengths:**

* The proposed substructure encoder is similar to DCNN (Ma et al.), where each node is embedded from a sequence of ancestor words.
* The architecture is simple and practical compared to prior work.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of details in baselines:** The proposed model has two main parts: sentence embedding and substructure embedding. However, it is unclear how the baseline models (TreeRNN and DCNN) are used to compute these two parts.
2. **Unnecessary use of two RNNs:** The model uses two RNNs: a chain-based one and a knowledge-guided one. The only difference in the knowledge-guided RNN is the addition of a "knowledge" vector from the memory in the RNN input. It seems unnecessary to have separate weights for the two RNNs, as this only increases model capacity.
3. **Missing baseline:** A baseline that inputs additional knowledge as features to the RNN (e.g., the head of each word, NER results) is missing.
4. **Model sensitivity to parser errors:** There is no discussion on the model's sensitivity to parser errors.
5. **Static attention:** After computing substructure embeddings, it seems natural to compute attention over them at each word. However, the model uses a static attention for all words.
6. **Weight computation:** The weight on a word is computed by the inner product of the sentence embedding and the substructure embedding, which may result in nodes/phrases similar to the whole sentence getting higher weights.
7. **Knowledge representation:** The substructure has to be represented as a sequence of words, which may not be straightforward for using constituent parse as knowledge.
8. **Misleading terminology:** The term "knowledge" is misleading, as it usually refers to world/external knowledge, whereas in this paper, it refers to syntax or semantics.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on how the baseline models are used to compute substructure embeddings independent of the sentence embedding and the joint tagger?
2. Why do you use two separate RNNs, and how do you ensure that the proposed model has comparable numbers of parameters to the baselines?
3. Can you include a baseline that inputs additional knowledge as features to the RNN?
4. How does the model handle parser errors, and what is the impact on performance?
5. Can you discuss the choice of static attention over substructure embeddings at each word?
6. How do you address the potential issue of nodes/phrases similar to the whole sentence getting higher weights?
7. Can you clarify how the substructure is represented as a sequence of words, and how this affects the use of constituent parse as knowledge?
8. Can you justify the use of the term "knowledge" in this context?
