- Paper proposes flattening structured linguistic substructures into a memory of vector embeddings fed as weighted context to an RNN for sequence tagging (slot-filling).
- Main idea similar to structured/syntax-based attention and related work (treeLSTM attention, Zhao et al., Liu et al., Eriguchi et al.); substructure encoder resembles DCNN.
- Contribution is practical and simple, but not highly novel compared to prior work.
- Empirical results are unconvincing due to insufficient baseline details and comparisons.
- Unclear how baseline models (TreeRNN, DCNN) were used for sentence vs. substructure embeddings; need clarification whether baselines compute substructure embeddings independently.
- Using two separate RNNs (chain and knowledge-guided) may be unnecessary and simply increases parameter count; baselines should match model capacity.
- Recommend including a baseline that supplies additional knowledge as simple features (e.g., heads, NER) to the RNN.
- Paper lacks analysis of sensitivity to parser errors (robustness concerns).
- Question why attention over substructures is static per word rather than computed dynamically at each time step.
- Inner-product weighting between sentence and substructure embeddings may bias toward nodes similar to the whole sentence (e.g., leaf nodes); needs examination.
- Claim of generalizing to different knowledge is weak since substructures must be represented as word sequences, complicating use of constituency parses.
- Referring to syntax-derived embeddings as “knowledge” is misleading; not equivalent to external/world knowledge.
- Experiments are limited (one dataset) and provide few takeaways for an ACL paper; presentation requires clarification.
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve main concerns about baseline usage and the extra RNN parameters, so reviewer’s scores remain unchanged.
