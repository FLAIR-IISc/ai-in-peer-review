- Proposes a simple memory-network architecture that encodes parse substructures as vectors and feeds a weighted sum of them as extra context to an RNN for sequence tagging (slot-filling).  
- The flattening-of-structure idea is practical and resembles structured/syntax-based attention and prior work (treeLSTM, Zhao et al., Liu et al., Eriguchi et al.).  
- The substructure encoder is similar to DCNN-style embeddings where nodes are embedded from ancestor word sequences.  
- Novelty is limited; the approach’s main appeal is simplicity and practicality rather than fundamentally new methods.  
- Experimental results are unconvincing due to insufficient baseline details and unclear how baselines compute sentence vs. substructure embeddings.  
- It is unclear whether TreeRNN/DCNN baselines were used for both sentence and node embeddings independently, which affects fair comparison.  
- Using two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) seems unnecessary and likely inflates parameters; baselines should be matched in capacity.  
- A reasonable baseline is missing where additional knowledge is provided as standard features (e.g., head word, NER) to the RNN.  
- The paper omits analysis of sensitivity to parser errors, which is important since substructures depend on parsing.  
- The model uses a static attention/filter over substructures for all words—no justification for not using per-word attention.  
- Weighting via inner product between sentence and substructure embeddings may bias toward nodes similar to the whole sentence, possibly equalizing leaf node weights.  
- Claim of generalizing to different knowledge types is questionable because substructures must be represented as word sequences, limiting use of constituency parses or other structures.  
- The term “knowledge” is misleading here, as it refers to syntax/AMR-like structure rather than external world knowledge or KBs.  
- Overall, practical and performs well on one dataset but lacks novel insights and convincing experimental validation.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not address main concerns about baselines and the extra RNN parameters, so reviewer’s evaluation remains unchanged.
