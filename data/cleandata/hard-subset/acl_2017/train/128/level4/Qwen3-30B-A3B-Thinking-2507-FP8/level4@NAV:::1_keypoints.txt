- Proposes a neural architecture that encodes structural linguistic substructures as memory vectors and uses their weighted sum as extra context for RNN-based sequence tagging (slot-filling).  
- Substructures (e.g., parse tree nodes) are embedded and aggregated into a memory, then integrated into the tagger at each time step.  
- Strength: simple, practical method to "flatten" structured information into a vector array for integration with taggers.  
- Method is conceptually related to prior structured/syntax-based attention and node-embedding approaches, so novelty is limited.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing due to insufficient baseline details and unclear experimental comparisons.  
- Unclear how baseline models (TreeRNN, DCNN) were adapted to provide both sentence and substructure embeddings for fair comparison.  
- The model uses two RNNs that differ only by an added "knowledge" vector, which may simply increase parameters rather than demonstrate a principled benefit.  
- Baselines should be matched in parameter count to rule out gains from increased capacity.  
- A baseline that incorporates comparable knowledge features (e.g., word heads, NER) into the RNN is missing.  
- Sensitivity to parser errors (which generate the substructures) is not analyzed.  
- Using a static attention (same weights for all words) over substructure embeddings is questionable; dynamic per-word attention seems more natural.  
- The weight computation may bias towards nodes similar to the full sentence, undermining intended substructure relevance.  
- Claim of generalization to diverse knowledge sources is weak because the substructure representation requires word sequences and parsing.  
- Calling the mechanism "knowledge" is misleading, as it primarily encodes syntax/limited semantics rather than external world knowledge.  
- Overall: practical and effective on one dataset but lacks novelty and robust experimental validation expected for an ACL paper.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve key concerns about baseline setup and parameter increases, so the review score remains unchanged.
