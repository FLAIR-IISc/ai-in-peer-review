- Paper proposes a neural architecture that encodes substructures as memory vectors and feeds their weighted sum into an RNN for sequence tagging/slot-filling.
- Strength: Simple, practical method to flatten structured information into a memory array integrated with a tagger; resembles prior structured/syntax-based attention work.
- Strength: Substructure encoder analogous to prior DCNN-style approaches, offering a straightforward alternative though not highly novel.
- Weakness: Empirical results are unconvincing due to insufficient baseline details and clarification.
- Weakness: Unclear how baselines (TreeRNN, DCNN) were used to compute sentence vs. substructure embeddings and whether substructure embeddings were extracted independently.
- Weakness: Use of two separate RNNs (chain-based and knowledge-guided) seems unnecessary and inflates parameter count; baselines must match parameter counts for fair comparison.
- Weakness: Missing a baseline that incorporates additional knowledge as input features (e.g., word heads, NER) for direct comparison.
- Weakness: No analysis of the model’s sensitivity to parser errors.
- Comment: Static attention over substructures for all words is likely suboptimal; word-specific attention would be more appropriate and the current mechanism behaves like a filter for important words.
- Comment: Computing weights via inner product of sentence and substructure embeddings (from same encoder) may bias toward nodes similar to the whole sentence, over-prioritizing leaf nodes.
- Comment: Claim of generalization to diverse knowledge sources is questionable because substructures must be representable as word sequences, making some knowledge types non-trivial to include.
- Comment: The term “knowledge” is misleading here since it refers to syntax (or potential semantics) rather than external world knowledge.
- General: Model is practical and performs well on one dataset but lacks strong novelty; experimental reporting is insufficient for ACL standards.
- Post-rebuttal: Authors did not resolve primary concerns about baseline usage and the justification for two RNNs increasing parameter count; reviewer retains original score.
