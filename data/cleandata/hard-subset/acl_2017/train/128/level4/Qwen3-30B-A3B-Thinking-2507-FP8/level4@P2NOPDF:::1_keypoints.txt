- Proposes a practical method to flatten structured linguistic substructures into memory-slot embeddings for sequence tagging (slot-filling).
- Core idea is similar to prior work (syntax-attention, treeLSTM, DCNN) and thus lacks strong novelty.
- The approach is well-motivated and easier to implement than more complex alternatives.
- Baseline implementation is ambiguous: unclear how TreeRNN/DCNN baselines were adapted to produce substructure embeddings, undermining fairness.
- Model uses two RNNs with separate weights differing only by a "knowledge" vector, which appears to increase parameters without justification.
- Baselines were not matched for parameter count, making comparisons potentially unfair.
- Missing simple baseline that feeds external features (e.g., heads, NER tags) into the RNN to isolate the memory mechanism's effect.
- No analysis of robustness to parser errors or sensitivity to noisy structural inputs.
- Static attention over substructures (same weighting per word) seems suboptimal compared to dynamic per-word attention.
- Weighting by inner product of sentence and substructure embeddings may bias toward leaf nodes that resemble full sentences.
- Claim of generalizability to other "knowledge" is questionable since method requires substructures as word sequences, excluding formats like AMR.
- Terminology misuse: calling syntactic/semantic structure "knowledge" is misleading; "structural context" is more accurate.
- Experimental evaluation lacks sufficient detail and comparisons to convincingly demonstrate superiority for an ACL paper.
- Post-rebuttal, authors failed to clarify baseline embedding procedure or justify the two-RNN design, leaving empirical claims unconvincing.
