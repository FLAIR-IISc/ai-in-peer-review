**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel model, knowledge-guided structural attention networks (K-SAN), that leverages prior knowledge to incorporate non-flat topologies and learn suitable attention for different substructures that are salient for specific tasks. The model is designed for natural language understanding (NLU) tasks, specifically slot filling, and is evaluated on the ATIS benchmark dataset.

**Main Contributions**

1. **End-to-end learning**: The paper proposes an end-to-end learning approach that utilizes general knowledge as guidance, where the model automatically learns important substructures with an attention mechanism.
2. **Generalization for different knowledge**: The model can generalize to different types of knowledge, including dependency relations, knowledge graph-specific relations, and parsing output of handcrafted grammars.
3. **Efficiency and parallelizability**: The model's substructure-based approach allows for efficient and parallelizable computation, making it suitable for large-scale NLU tasks.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved performance**: The proposed K-SAN model outperforms state-of-the-art baseline models, including CRF, RNN, and CNN-based approaches, on the ATIS benchmark dataset.
2. **Robustness to data scarcity**: The model demonstrates robustness to data scarcity, achieving state-of-the-art performance even with small training datasets.
3. **Flexibility and generalization**: The model can generalize to different types of knowledge and can be applied to various NLU tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The proposed model has a complex architecture, which may make it challenging to train and optimize.
2. **Dependence on external knowledge**: The model relies on external knowledge sources, which may not always be available or accurate.
3. **Limited evaluation**: The paper only evaluates the model on a single dataset (ATIS) and task (slot filling), which may not be representative of all NLU tasks and datasets.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the model handle cases where the external knowledge source is incomplete or inaccurate?
2. Can the model be applied to other NLU tasks, such as intent detection or dialogue state tracking?
3. How does the model's performance change when using different types of knowledge or knowledge sources?

**Additional Comments**

The paper presents a novel approach to NLU tasks by leveraging prior knowledge to guide the attention mechanism. The proposed K-SAN model demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the ATIS benchmark dataset and shows promise for other NLU tasks. However, the model's complexity and dependence on external knowledge sources may be limitations that need to be addressed in future work.
