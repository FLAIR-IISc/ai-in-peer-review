- Paper proposes K-SAN, a model that uses knowledge-guided structural attention to select salient substructures for NLU and is evaluated on ATIS.  
- Contribution: End-to-end model that leverages general knowledge and learns important substructures via attention.  
- Contribution: Model generalizes across different knowledge types (dependency relations, knowledge-graph relations, handcrafted grammar parses).  
- Contribution: Model is parallelizable, improving efficiency and scalability.  
- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ATIS benchmark.  
- Strength: Robust to data scarcity, performing well with limited training data.  
- Strength: Demonstrates versatility by generalizing to different forms of prior knowledge.  
- Strength: Efficient and parallelizable implementation.  
- Weakness: Model complexity and large parameter count make training and optimization challenging.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on prior knowledge, which may be unavailable or inaccurate in some settings.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to a single dataset (ATIS), raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Question: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary words or unseen substructures at test time?  
- Question: Can the model be applied to other NLU tasks like intent detection or dialogue management?  
- Question: How does K-SAN compare to other attention-based models, particularly transformer-based approaches?
