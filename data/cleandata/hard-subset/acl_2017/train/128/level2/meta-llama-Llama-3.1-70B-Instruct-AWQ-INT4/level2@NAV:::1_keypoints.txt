- Summary: Proposes K-SAN, a knowledge-guided attention model for NLU with modules for knowledge encoding, attention distribution, and sequence tagging to weight substructure embeddings.

- Contribution 1: First end-to-end neural approach that uses general knowledge to learn important substructures via attention.

- Contribution 2: Able to incorporate various knowledge types (dependency parses, knowledge-graph relations, handcrafted grammar parses).

- Contribution 3: Parallelizable design that models substructures separately, improving efficiency and limiting linear time growth with sentence length.

- Strength 1: Outperforms state-of-the-art on ATIS, notably on small and medium training sets.

- Strength 2: Robust to data scarcity and generalizes better to unseen data.

- Strength 3: Flexible in knowledge representation, supporting formats like dependency relations and AMR.

- Weakness 1: Model complexity (multiple components) may make training and optimization difficult.

- Weakness 2: Performance depends on the quality of parsing outputs.

- Weakness 3: Attention mechanism provides limited interpretability of decisions.

- Question 1: How does the model handle incorrect or incomplete parsing outputs?

- Question 2: Can the model be extended to other NLU tasks (e.g., intent detection, dialogue management)?

- Question 3: How does K-SAN compare to other attention-based models in performance and interpretability?
