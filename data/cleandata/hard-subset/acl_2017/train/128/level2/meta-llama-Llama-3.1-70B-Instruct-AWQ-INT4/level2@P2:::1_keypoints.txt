- Proposes K-SAN, a Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Network that uses prior knowledge to learn attention over non-flat substructures for NLU.
- K-SAN is trained end-to-end to automatically learn important substructures via an attention mechanism.
- The model generalizes to different knowledge types (dependency relations, knowledge-graph relations, handcrafted-parsing outputs).
- K-SAN models substructures separately, enabling efficient computation and parallelization.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the ATIS benchmark.
- Demonstrates robustness to data scarcity with improvements on small and medium training sets.
- Shows generalization across knowledge formats including dependency trees and AMR graphs.
- Efficiency through separate substructure modeling allows parallel processing.
- Evaluation is limited to ATIS, raising concerns about broader applicability.
- No ablation study is provided to quantify each component’s contribution.
- Relies on a pre-trained parser to produce dependency relations, which may be unavailable or error-prone.
- Questions: how K-SAN handles out-of-vocabulary words or unseen substructures at test time.
- Questions: whether K-SAN applies to other NLU tasks like intent detection or dialogue state tracking.
- Questions: how the choice of knowledge-encoding model (NN, RNN, CNN) affects performance.
