- Introduces K-SAN: extends RNNs to incorporate non-flat network topologies guided by external knowledge.  
- Contribution 1: end-to-end learning of salient substructures via attention instead of fixed linguistic features.  
- Contribution 2: flexible to work with various knowledge sources without requiring specific schemas.  
- Contribution 3: improved generalization, notably on small datasets.  
- Strength: Addresses limitation of flat-sequence NLU by modeling structured information.  
- Strength: Significant performance gain on very small training set (74.60 F1 vs. 73.57 CNN).  
- Strength: Attention visualizations suggest the model learns meaningful substructure patterns with limited data.  
- Strength: Robust performance across different knowledge sources (dependency trees, AMR).  
- Weakness: "End-to-end learning" claim not clearly differentiated from prior structural end-to-end models (e.g., Tree-RNN).  
- Weakness: Comparisons limited to pre-2015 baselines, omitting recent transformer-based and advanced dependency methods.  
- Weakness: No analysis of robustness to errors in external knowledge sources (e.g., parser mistakes).  
- Weakness: Lacks ablation studies isolating the effect of attention versus structural knowledge.  
- Question: How does the model perform when the external knowledge source is noisy, and how are such errors mitigated?  
- Question: What is the computational complexity relative to standard RNNs and how does it scale with input length?  
- Question: How does the model compare to recent transformer-based architectures that incorporate structural information?
