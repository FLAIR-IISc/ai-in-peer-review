This paper introduces Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), a novel architecture for natural language understanding that aims to improve slot filling by incorporating linguistic structure. The model leverages external knowledge, such as dependency parses, to define sentence substructures. It then employs an attention mechanism to learn a dynamic, task-specific sentence representation by weighing the importance of these substructures. This knowledge-guided representation is subsequently used to augment a recurrent neural network (RNN) tagger. The primary contributions of this work are:

1.  A novel architecture that effectively integrates prior linguistic knowledge into an end-to-end neural network. The attention mechanism over discrete substructures provides a principled way to focus on syntactically or semantically salient parts of an utterance.
2.  A thorough empirical evaluation demonstrating state-of-the-art performance on the ATIS benchmark. The analysis convincingly shows the model’s robustness across different knowledge sources (e.g., different parsers, AMR graphs) and its superior generalization ability, particularly in low-data scenarios.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Novelty and Soundness:** The proposed attention mechanism over knowledge-guided substructures is an elegant and intuitive method for injecting structural bias into sequence models. It directly addresses the limitation of standard RNNs that treat sentences as flat sequences, allowing the model to capture long-distance dependencies that are structurally close.
2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The model achieves significant improvements over a comprehensive set of baselines, including other structure-aware architectures. The large gains on smaller training sets are particularly compelling, highlighting the model's data efficiency and ability to generalize well by leveraging linguistic priors.
3.  **Flexibility and Generalization:** A major strength is the framework's demonstrated agnosticism to the specific source of knowledge. The experiments successfully utilize syntactic information from different dependency parsers and semantic information from AMR graphs, achieving consistently strong performance and showcasing the model's versatility.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Dependence on External Parsers:** The model relies on an upstream parser, making its performance potentially vulnerable to parsing errors. While one example suggests robustness, a systematic analysis of the model's sensitivity to parser quality is needed to understand the potential impact of error propagation.
2.  **Missing Ablation Study:** The final reported results are from a "joint RNN tagger" that interpolates between a standard chain-based RNN and the knowledge-guided one. An ablation study comparing the performance of the knowledge-guided tagger alone versus the joint model would be valuable to isolate the direct contribution of the structural attention mechanism.
3.  **Scalability of Substructure Extraction:** The process of extracting all root-to-leaf paths from a parse tree could be computationally intensive for very long or complex sentences. A discussion of the model's computational complexity and potential limitations would strengthen the paper.
