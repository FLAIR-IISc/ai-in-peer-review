Introduces K-SAN, an architecture that uses external linguistic structures and attention over sentence substructures to improve slot filling by augmenting an RNN tagger.

Contribution: Novel end-to-end architecture that injects prior linguistic knowledge via attention over discrete syntactic/semantic substructures.

Contribution: Thorough empirical evaluation showing state-of-the-art results on ATIS and strong generalization, especially in low-data settings and across different knowledge sources.

Strength: Elegant, sound mechanism that lets the model focus on structurally salient parts and capture long-distance dependencies missed by flat RNNs.

Strength: Strong empirical gains over diverse baselines, with particularly large improvements on smaller training sets indicating data efficiency.

Strength: Flexible framework agnostic to the knowledge source, performing well with different dependency parsers and AMR graphs.

Weakness: Relies on upstream parsers, so performance may degrade with parsing errors; lacks systematic analysis of sensitivity to parser quality.

Weakness: Missing ablation comparing the knowledge-guided tagger alone versus the joint RNN tagger, making it hard to isolate the structural attention’s direct contribution.

Weakness: Potential scalability concerns from extracting all root-to-leaf parse paths for long/complex sentences and lack of discussion on computational complexity.
