- Introduces K-SAN, a structural-attention model that attends over parse-derived substructures to guide RNN sequence tagging.  
- Claims state-of-the-art slot-filling results on ATIS, especially strong in low-data settings and robust across different knowledge sources (dependency, AMR).  
- Novel, well-motivated architecture that softly integrates noisy linguistic structure via attention instead of hard tree-based models.  
- Strong and comprehensive empirical results outperforming RNN/CNN and structure-aware baselines, with notable gains on smaller datasets.  
- Demonstrates robustness by showing consistent performance when guided by different parsers and formalisms, supporting real-world applicability.  
- Substructure extraction procedure (dependency paths, AMR) is under-specified and needs a formal definition or algorithm for reproducibility.  
- Lacks quantitative analysis of robustness to parsing errors; the robustness claim is supported only by a single example.  
- Claims about efficiency and parallelizability are unsupported; no timing or computational-cost experiments are provided.  
- Why was the joint tagger interpolation weight α fixed to 0.5, and how sensitive is performance to this hyperparameter?  
- Why do CNN encoders perform best on small data while RNN encoders excel on large data — what's the intuition/trade-off?
