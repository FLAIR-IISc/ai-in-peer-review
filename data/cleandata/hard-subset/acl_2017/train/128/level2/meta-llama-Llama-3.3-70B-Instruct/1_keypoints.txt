- Proposes K-SAN, a novel model that uses prior knowledge to guide structural attention for NLU sequence tagging.
- K-SAN comprises a knowledge encoding module, a sentence encoding module with attention, and an RNN-based sequence tagging module.
- Uses external linguistic structures (e.g., dependency parses, AMR graphs) to generate substructures for attention.
- End-to-end learning: first neural approach claimed to learn important substructures via attention in a unified pipeline.
- Generalizable to different knowledge sources (dependency relations, knowledge-graph relations, handcrafted grammars).
- Models substructures separately, enabling more efficient and parallelizable computation.
- Outperforms state-of-the-art neural baselines on the ATIS benchmark.
- Shows improved robustness and generalization under limited training data.
- Flexible in incorporating various knowledge resources as guidance.
- Performance may depend on the quality of external knowledge used.
- Computational complexity can grow with utterance length and number of substructures.
- Attention mechanism offers limited interpretability of model decisions.
- Open questions: impact of knowledge quality and ways to improve it.
- Open questions: applicability to other NLP tasks (e.g., MT, QA) and required modifications.
- Open questions: how to improve and analyze interpretability via attention weights.
