## Review of "Knowledge as a Teacher: Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks"

This paper introduces Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), a novel RNN-based model designed to improve Natural Language Understanding (NLU) by incorporating prior knowledge via non-flat network topologies. The core idea is to leverage external knowledge (dependency parsing in this case) to guide attention mechanisms, allowing the model to focus on salient substructures within sentences for more accurate semantic tag prediction. The authors claim contributions in end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization across different knowledge types, and efficiency through parallelizable substructure modeling.

The most significant contribution lies in the empirical demonstration of improved slot filling performance, particularly in low-data scenarios. The experimental results on the ATIS dataset consistently outperform strong baselines, including state-of-the-art structural models. The ablation studies (presented in Table 2) further demonstrate the robustness of K-SAN to different knowledge sources (dependency trees vs. AMR graphs) and parsers, highlighting its versatility. The attention visualization (Figure 5) provides compelling qualitative evidence supporting the model's ability to focus on relevant sentence parts guided by the external knowledge.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. While the authors claim efficiency, they don’t provide concrete evidence or a complexity analysis to support this claim. A discussion of the computational cost of incorporating knowledge parsing would strengthen this argument. Additionally, the dependence on parser accuracy is a potential limitation not fully addressed. The experiments show performance even with imperfect parsing, but a more thorough analysis of the impact of parsing errors on the model's performance is warranted. Furthermore, the improvements observed are relatively modest and may not warrant a complete paradigm shift in NLU approaches.

Despite these weaknesses, the paper presents a solid contribution to the field. The idea of explicitly guiding attention with external knowledge is promising and well-executed. 

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of K-SAN compared to the baseline RNN models, specifically considering the knowledge parsing step?
2.  How sensitive is the performance of K-SAN to the accuracy of the dependency parser? Did you investigate methods to mitigate the impact of parsing errors?
3.  The attention visualization is helpful. Can you provide more examples demonstrating cases where the knowledge-guided attention significantly differs from a standard attention mechanism and results in a more accurate prediction?

**Additional Comments:**

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed model. The related work section is comprehensive. Consider including a discussion of the potential application of K-SAN to other NLU tasks beyond slot filling.
