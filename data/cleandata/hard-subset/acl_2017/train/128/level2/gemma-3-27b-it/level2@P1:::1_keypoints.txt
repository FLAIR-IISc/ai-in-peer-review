Strengths:
- Novel approach: integrates external knowledge as an internal guiding signal for attention rather than as additional features, reducing mismatch/error-propagation.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms baselines (including Tree-RNN and DCNN) on ATIS, with notable gains on smaller data.
- Knowledge-source generalizability: works with multiple knowledge types (dependency trees and AMR graphs), showing representation flexibility.
- Attention visualization: attention maps demonstrate the model focuses on relevant substructures, improving interpretability.

Weaknesses:
- Limited evaluation: experiments only on ATIS, so generalization to more complex/diverse NLU tasks is uncertain.
- Parser-quality dependency: model performance depends on the accuracy of dependency/AMR parsing; parsing errors may propagate.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: tuning procedure is under-specified and optimal settings may be dataset-specific.
- Missing ablation study: unclear how much each component (attention vs structural encoding, etc.) contributes to performance.

Questions to authors:
- How sensitive is model performance to parser/AMR accuracy; can you quantify parsing-error impact on slot-filling?
- What hyperparameter tuning strategy was used and how sensitive are results to different settings?
- What is the computational cost of K-SAN versus baselines, and evidence for claimed efficiency/parallelizability?
- How are the knowledge-encoding networks (NN, RNN, CNN) initialized and trained in practice?

Additional comments:
- Writing is clear and well-organized.
- Related work coverage is good.
- Recommend adding discussion of limitations and future directions, e.g., other knowledge sources.
