- Proposes Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), an RNN-based model that incorporates prior knowledge via non-flat network topologies.  
- Core idea: use external knowledge (e.g., dependency parses) to guide attention toward salient sentence substructures for better semantic tagging.  
- Claims contributions in end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization across knowledge types, and efficiency via parallelizable substructure modeling.  
- Main empirical contribution: improved slot-filling performance, especially in low-data settings.  
- Experiments on ATIS show consistent outperforming of strong baselines and state-of-the-art structural models.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate robustness to different knowledge sources (dependency trees vs. AMR) and different parsers.  
- Attention visualizations provide qualitative evidence that K-SAN focuses on relevant sentence parts guided by external knowledge.  
- Weakness: efficiency claim lacks concrete evidence or formal complexity analysis.  
- Weakness: computational cost and effect of the knowledge parsing step are not discussed.  
- Weakness: dependence on parser accuracy is noted but not thoroughly analyzed; impact of parsing errors needs deeper study.  
- Weakness: overall improvements are modest and may not justify a paradigm shift in NLU.  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed computational complexity vs. baselines (including parsing cost); analyze sensitivity to parser accuracy and mitigation strategies; supply more examples comparing knowledge-guided vs. standard attention.  
- Additional comments: paper is well-written, related work is comprehensive, and authors should discuss applying K-SAN to other NLU tasks beyond slot filling.
