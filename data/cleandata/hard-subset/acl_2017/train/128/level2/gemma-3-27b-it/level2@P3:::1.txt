This paper introduces Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), a novel neural network architecture designed to improve natural language understanding (NLU) by incorporating prior knowledge through non-flat network topologies. The core idea is to guide attention mechanisms using external knowledge, enabling the model to capture salient substructures within sentences and improve semantic tagging. The primary contributions are end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization to different knowledge sources, and efficient parallelizable modeling.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Knowledge Integration:** The approach of using knowledge to *guide* attention within a neural network, rather than simply adding it as features, is a significant contribution. This allows the model to learn which substructures are important, which is a more flexible and potentially powerful approach than relying on handcrafted features or pre-defined knowledge representations. The end-to-end learning aspect further strengthens this point.
2. **Generalizability to Different Knowledge Sources:** The demonstrated ability to utilize different types of knowledge (dependency relations, AMR graphs) without requiring schema changes is a strong point. This broad applicability makes the model more practical and adaptable to various NLU tasks and domains where different knowledge resources are available. The experimental results support this claim.
3. **Improved Performance on ATIS:** The reported performance gains on the ATIS dataset, particularly for smaller training data sizes, demonstrate the effectiveness of the K-SAN architecture. This suggests that the model is less reliant on large amounts of training data, which is a common limitation in NLU tasks. The significant improvements with smaller datasets are particularly compelling.
4. **Clear Presentation and Motivation:** The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the motivation behind the work. The problem statement is well-defined, and the proposed solution is presented in a logical and understandable manner. The visualizations, such as Figure 2 and 5, effectively illustrate the model architecture and attention mechanism.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Scope of Evaluation:** While the results on ATIS are encouraging, the evaluation is limited to a single dataset and task. It would be beneficial to demonstrate the effectiveness of K-SAN on other NLU benchmarks or tasks with different characteristics (e.g., more complex sentence structures, different domains).
2. **Dependency on Parser Quality:** The performance of K-SAN is inherently tied to the quality of the dependency parser (or other knowledge source) used. While the paper acknowledges this, it would be valuable to investigate the model's robustness to parsing errors or noisy knowledge. A controlled experiment varying parser accuracy would strengthen the analysis. The performance difference between Stanford and SyntaxNet parsers is minor, but more thorough investigation is needed.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The optimal hyperparameters (e.g., the type of encoding network – NN, RNN, CNN) appear to vary depending on the dataset size. A more in-depth analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and a discussion of best practices for tuning these parameters would be useful.
4. **Attention Visualization Interpretation:** While Figure 5 provides a visualization of the attention mechanism, the interpretation of these visualizations is somewhat subjective. It would be helpful to provide more quantitative metrics or analysis to support the claim that the model is attending to the "correct" substructures.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the performance of K-SAN to errors in the dependency parsing? Have you explored strategies for mitigating the impact of parsing errors, such as using ensemble parsing or incorporating uncertainty estimates?
2.  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process? What strategies did you use to select the optimal hyperparameters for each dataset size?
3.  Could you elaborate on the computational cost of K-SAN compared to baseline models, particularly with respect to the complexity of parsing and knowledge encoding?
4.  Are there any insights gained from the attention visualizations that explain why certain substructures are more salient than others for specific semantic tags?



**Additional Comments:**

*   It would be beneficial to include a discussion of related work on graph neural networks and their application to NLU.
*   Consider adding an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of different components of K-SAN (e.g., the attention mechanism, the knowledge encoding module).
*   The use of '†' to denote statistically significant improvements is good practice.
