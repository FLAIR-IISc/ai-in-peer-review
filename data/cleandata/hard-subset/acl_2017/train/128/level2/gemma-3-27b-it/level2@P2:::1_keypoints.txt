- Proposes K-SAN: augment RNNs with knowledge-guided non-flat topologies to capture richer linguistic structure and improve NLU, especially with limited data.
- Claims three contributions: end-to-end knowledge-guided learning, generalization to multiple knowledge types, and efficiency/parallelizability.
- Addresses a relevant NLU problem by incorporating external linguistic structure rather than treating utterances as flat sequences.
- Novel approach: knowledge acts as a “teacher” guiding attention, learning where and how much to attend instead of relying on feature engineering.
- Empirical validation on ATIS shows consistent gains over baselines, especially with smaller training sets.
- Demonstrates flexibility by leveraging different knowledge sources (dependency trees and AMR graphs).
- Ablation study is limited; unclear how much improvement comes from knowledge-guided attention versus structural information alone.
- Performance depends on parser accuracy; impact of parsing errors on final results is not thoroughly analyzed.
- Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and its effect on performance.
- Missing comparisons to strong pre-trained LM baselines (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa) to contextualize improvements.
- Requests a more detailed ablation analyzing contributions of knowledge encoding methods and attention components.
- Asks for analysis quantifying sensitivity to parser errors and their effect on model performance.
- Wants clarification on computational overhead introduced by the knowledge-guided attention versus standard RNN/CNN models.
- Inquires whether pre-trained LM embeddings (BERT/RoBERTa) were tried within K-SAN and what the outcomes were.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the approach.
- Figure 5’s visualization effectively illustrates the attention mechanism.
- Exploration of multiple knowledge sources is a notable strength and increases applicability.
- Conclusion succinctly summarizes contributions and potential future directions.
