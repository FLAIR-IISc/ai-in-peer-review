- Proposes K-SAN: attention over parse-derived substructures as memory, embedded and attended to build a knowledge-guided sentence representation conditioning an RNN tagger, trained end-to-end and jointly with a chain tagger.
- Empirical claim: knowledge-guided attention improves slot filling, especially in low-data regimes, and is robust to different/noisy knowledge sources (dependency/AMR).
- Provides attention visualizations suggesting the model focuses on semantically salient parts (origin/destination/time) relevant to tagging.

- Strength: Principled, simple integration of external structure via shared encoders, dot-product attention, and residual combination that is easy to train end-to-end without hand-crafted features.
- Strength: Consistent empirical gains over CRF, RNN, CNN, and structural encoders, with largest improvements at 1/40 and 1/10 data; statistical tests reported.
- Strength: Robustness shown across multiple parsers/formats (two dependency, two AMR), indicating tolerance to parser noise; reports substructure counts.
- Strength: Qualitative interpretability—attention visualizations align with relevant semantic tokens/relations even with imperfect parses.
- Strength: Joint tagger design plausibly mitigates noisy knowledge by combining chain and knowledge-guided hidden states.

- Weakness: Baselines are limited/dated; missing strong competitors (BiLSTM-CRF, char-level features, joint intent-slot) that often reach ≥95 F1 on ATIS, so SOTA claim is not fully substantiated.
- Weakness: Missing ablations to isolate sources of gains (attention vs uniform, knowledge-guided vs random spans/n-grams, joint vs knowledge-only).
- Weakness: Efficiency/parallelizability claims lack empirical support—no runtime, memory, throughput, or complexity analysis.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to ATIS; additional domains needed to support generalization beyond parser robustness.
- Weakness: Several modeling/training details unclear (whether embeddings are fine-tuned, number of runs/seeds for t-tests, projection/dimensionality for injecting o, selection/sensitivity of α, effect of ignoring dependency/AMR labels).

- Question: How does K-SAN compare to a strong BiLSTM-CRF baseline with the same lexical-only feature set and comparable parameter count, and does it improve BiLSTM-CRF when added?
- Question: Can you provide ablations for (a) attention vs uniform weighting, (b) knowledge-guided substructures vs random spans/surface n-grams, and (c) joint combination α=1 vs α=0?
- Question: Did you experiment with including dependency/AMR edge labels in substructure encoding, and what was the impact?
- Question: Are improvements stable across random seeds; how many runs underpin the t-tests; please report mean±std?
- Question: Please quantify efficiency: average number of substructures per sentence per knowledge source, training/inference time per epoch, and sensitivity to sentence length.

- Suggestion: Evaluate on additional slot-filling domains (e.g., MIT Movies/Restaurant) to strengthen generalization claims.
- Suggestion: Clarify the exact mechanism for integrating o into the RNN (projection vs concatenation, dimensionalities).
- Suggestion: Release code and parsing/AMR extraction recipes to enable reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Clean up reference formatting and improve figure legibility (labels/punctuation).
