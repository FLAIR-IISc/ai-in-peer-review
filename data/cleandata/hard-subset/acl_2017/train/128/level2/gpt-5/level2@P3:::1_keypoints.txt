- Proposes K-SAN: attention over substructures from external linguistic/semantic resources injected into a recurrent tagger for slot filling.
- Core idea: encode substructures (dependency/AMR), attend conditioned on utterance, and inject a knowledge-guided vector into a joint chain+knowledge tagger.
- Contribution: general end-to-end attention-over-substructures framework treating knowledge as guidance rather than fixed features.
- Contribution: empirical gains on ATIS, especially in low-resource regimes, and robustness across heterogeneous knowledge sources.
- Contribution: practical joint architecture combining chain-based and knowledge-guided paths achieving strong ATIS performance.
- Strength: clean, differentiable mechanism to prioritize salient parts of external structures that accommodates syntax and semantics.
- Strength: significant empirical improvements over non-knowledge neural baselines, largest in Small and Medium data regimes.
- Strength: consistent performance across four distinct parsers/sources, suggesting robustness to parser choice and representation schema.
- Strength: interpretable attention patterns that highlight relevant tokens/relations, even with parser errors and limited supervision.
- Strength: reasonable joint design and reported training/hyperparameter details aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: missing stronger contemporaneous baselines (e.g., BiLSTM or BiLSTM-CRF) to assess absolute competitiveness.
- Weakness: insufficient ablations (untied weights, no-attention baseline, learned/varied α, injecting o once vs every timestep) to isolate sources of gains.
- Weakness: experiments confined to a single, small/dataset (ATIS), limiting claims of generality.
- Weakness: efficiency and parallelizability claims are unsupported by runtime or memory measurements or complexity analysis.
- Weakness: statistical testing unclear—significance reported only vs non-knowledge baselines and not vs structural baselines; multiple comparisons not addressed.
- Question: How are AMR substructures constructed given DAG/reentrancies; are root-to-leaf paths used and how are duplicates handled; are relation labels used?
- Question: Why are Mkg and Min weights tied; was an untied variant evaluated and with what effect?
- Question: Is α fixed at 0.5 or tuned/learned; would learning α or using a gating mechanism help?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to number and length of substructures; is there pruning or caps and how does that affect attention entropy?
- Question: Can K-SAN be combined with a BiLSTM-CRF tagger and how does it compare to strong BiLSTM-CRF baselines?
- Suggestion: Include BiLSTM(-CRF) baseline and K-SAN+BiLSTM(-CRF) experiments.
- Suggestion: Add ablations for attention vs uniform averaging, untied encoders, and alternative injection points for o.
- Suggestion: Provide runtime/complexity analysis and report average number of substructures and attention sparsity/entropy.
- Suggestion: Clarify use of dependency/AMR labels and experiment with labeled vs unlabeled structures.
- Suggestion: Expand qualitative analysis with error categories and examples where knowledge corrects chain-based errors.
