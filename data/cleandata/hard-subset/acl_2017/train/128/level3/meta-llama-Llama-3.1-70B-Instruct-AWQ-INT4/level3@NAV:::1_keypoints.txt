- Proposes K-SAN: a neural model that uses linguistic structures to guide attention and feed a knowledge-guided sentence representation into an RNN tagger for sequence tagging.

- Introduces a knowledge-guided attention mechanism that focuses on salient substructures via encoded linguistic knowledge.

- Presents an end-to-end framework integrating knowledge encoding, attention, and RNN tagger for joint learning.

- Claims generalization to multiple knowledge resources, including dependency trees and AMR graphs.

- Shows improved performance over state-of-the-art baselines on the ATIS benchmark.

- Demonstrates robustness to data scarcity, performing well with limited training data.

- Reiterates generalization across different knowledge resources as an empirical strength.

- Weak novelty: approach builds on existing attention and knowledge-guided neural methods.

- Lacks sufficient details on how baselines were implemented and compared.

- Unclear evaluation: F1 score usage and calculation are not fully explained or justified.

- Requests more details on baseline implementation and comparison protocol.

- Requests clarification on F1 computation and whether it is the most suitable metric.

- Requests analysis of attention weights and their relation to linguistic structures.

- Asks how the model handles errors in linguistic structures (e.g., parsing errors).

- Requests more details on the knowledge encoding module and how structures are converted to vectors.
