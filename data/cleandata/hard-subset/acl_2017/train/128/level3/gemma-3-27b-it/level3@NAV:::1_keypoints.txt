- Proposes K-SAN: attend over dependency-derived substructure embeddings to incorporate structural linguistic knowledge into slot filling.
- Core idea flattens structured information into memory vectors with attention for end-to-end learning.
- Approach is practically useful for integrating dependency parse information into an RNN-based tagger.
- Exploration of different knowledge sources (dependency trees vs. AMR graphs) is a modest positive.
- Paper is clearly written and architecture explained well.
- Main novelty is limited—similar to existing structured/syntax-based attention mechanisms.
- Empirical validation is unconvincing due to insufficient baseline details and controls.
- Unclear whether TreeRNN and DCNN baselines were trained jointly with the tagger or independently.
- Two-RNN (chain + knowledge-guided) design increases parameters without justification; parameter counts not compared.
- No baseline that adds simple knowledge features (e.g., head words, POS, NER) to the standard RNN to control for “giving” knowledge.
- Static attention over substructures is questionable; per-word dynamic attention might be more appropriate.
- No analysis of sensitivity to parser errors or other error analysis to explain model behavior.
- Claim of generalizing to different knowledge kinds is weak because method depends on representing structures as word sequences.
- Questions for authors: clarify TreeRNN/DCNN training setup, provide parameter count comparisons, and report results when simple knowledge features are added to the baseline.
