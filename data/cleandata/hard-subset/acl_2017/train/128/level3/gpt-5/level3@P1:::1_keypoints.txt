- Paper proposes Knowledge-guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN) that encode substructures from external analyzers, attend over them using a sentence embedding, and inject the result into an RNN tagger.
- Design flattens linguistic structure into substructure embeddings and unifies multiple encoders (NN/RNN/CNN) and structure sources (dependency/AMR).
- Empirical results on ATIS show modest but consistent improvements over neural baselines, with larger gains in low-resource settings (+1.1 F1 full data, +2.5 F1 with 1/10 data).
- Gains are relatively robust across different parsers/formalisms (Stanford/SyntaxNet dependencies and AMR).
- Pipeline is conceptually clear and modular: extract substructures, encode, attend with sentence embedding, feed into tagger.
- Attention mechanism and tying of Min/Mkg are straightforward and likely stable in training.
- Visualized attention focuses on salient spans (origin/destination/time) and remains stable across data sizes, offering interpretability.
- Novelty is limited: idea is incremental relative to existing syntax/structure-based attention and tree encoders.
- Experimental baselines may be unfair or under-specified: parameter counts, integration details for DCNN/Tree-RNN, and parameter-matched controls are missing.
- No simple feature-control baselines (e.g., concatenating head embeddings or NER tags) to isolate benefit of substructure attention versus feature augmentation.
- Model uses a single static sentence-level attention per token, which may be suboptimal since relevant substructures vary by token; token-level attention was not compared.
- No quantitative analysis of robustness to parser errors or noisy parses despite robustness claims.
- Inner-product attention with a global sentence embedding may bias toward globally similar substructures rather than token-relevant ones.
- Framing syntactic/AMR structures as “knowledge” is overstated: method requires linearizable structures and does not handle external KB facts or non-linguistic knowledge.
- Questions requested from authors: report parameter counts and parameter-matched baselines, compare to simple structural feature baselines, try per-token attention variants, test parser noise robustness, ablate substructure definitions, and report chain-only / knowledge-only / joint performances.
- Post-rebuttal: authors did not resolve concerns about baseline fairness and necessity of two RNNs; reviewer’s assessment and score remain unchanged.
