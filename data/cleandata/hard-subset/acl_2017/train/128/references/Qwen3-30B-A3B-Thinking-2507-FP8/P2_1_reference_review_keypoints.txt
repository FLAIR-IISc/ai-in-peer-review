- Summary: Proposes K-SAN, which uses external knowledge (dependency/AMR) to guide attention to salient sentence substructures for NLU, claiming end-to-end learning and better generalization, especially with limited data.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a knowledge-guided structural attention mechanism that focuses on substructures rather than flat sequences.

- Contribution 2: Presents an end-to-end framework that integrates knowledge guidance without relying on pre-extracted features.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates the approach generalizes across different knowledge sources (dependency trees, AMR) without a fixed schema.

- Strength 1: Novel, linguistically motivated attention mechanism with clear mathematical formulation and compelling attention visualizations.

- Strength 2: Significant and statistically supported performance gains on limited-data settings (Small ATIS), showing improved generalization.

- Strength 3: Robust performance across multiple knowledge sources and parsers, indicating practical generality.

- Strength 4: Thorough evaluations including attention visualizations, knowledge generalization tests, and encoder ablations.

- Weakness 1: Incremental novelty; architecture heavily builds on existing memory/dynamic memory network ideas and insufficiently distinguishes from prior work.

- Weakness 2: Limited comparisons to recent SOTA models (e.g., transformers), weakening claims about overall improvement.

- Weakness 3: Missing ablation studies isolating contributions of knowledge guidance, attention mechanism, and encoder choice.

- Weakness 4: Efficiency and parallelizability claims are unsubstantiated by runtime or empirical evidence.

- Weakness 5: Experiments are limited to the small, dated ATIS dataset and lack evaluation on larger, modern benchmarks.

- Question 1: Clarify how K-SAN fundamentally differs from prior work that uses external knowledge for parsing/semantic tasks.

- Question 2: Explain model behavior and minimum substructure requirements for very short sentences or cases with few/no substructures.

- Question 3: Describe how the model handles errors from upstream dependency parsing and how this compares to prior parsing-reliant approaches.

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and convincing for ATIS, but needs stronger novelty differentiation, SOTA comparisons, efficiency evidence, and acknowledgement of dataset limits.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address novelty comparison and efficiency claims.
