- Main contribution: K-SAN uses external structured knowledge to guide attention for slot filling rather than treating knowledge as static features.  
- Main contribution: Shows robustness to limited training data with notable F1 gains on small datasets.  
- Main contribution: Flexible across different knowledge sources (dependency trees, semantic graphs) and provides a concrete attention mechanism to incorporate structure.

- Strength: Addresses error-propagation problem by using knowledge to guide attention instead of as brittle features.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation — consistent improvements across different parsers/knowledge representations.  
- Strength: Attention visualizations suggest the model learns meaningful focus on substructures even when parsers err.

- Weakness: Overstates novelty — overlooks related prior work (memory networks, dynamic memory networks, tree-based RNNs).  
- Weakness: Claims about efficiency and parallelizability are unsupported by empirical timing data.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to ATIS, so generalizability to diverse, multi-domain datasets is unclear.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of what attention patterns represent and how they differ from standard attention.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of dependence on parser quality and how much attention compensates for parser errors.

- Question: How does K-SAN fundamentally differ from memory networks and dynamic memory networks that use external knowledge to guide attention?  
- Question: Can you provide training and inference time comparisons to support the efficiency/parallelizability claim?  
- Question: How does the model perform on more diverse datasets (e.g., MultiWOZ, SNIPS) with multi-domain complexity?  
- Question: Can you analyze attention patterns for correct vs. incorrect predictions to show what the model learns?  
- Question: How does performance change with a more accurate dependency parser — does robustness to parser errors hold?

- Recommendation: Weak acceptance contingent on addressing overstated novelty, providing efficiency evidence, and expanding evaluation to broader datasets.

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written with useful figures; authors should acknowledge single-turn limitation and discuss extensions to multi-turn dialogue contexts.
