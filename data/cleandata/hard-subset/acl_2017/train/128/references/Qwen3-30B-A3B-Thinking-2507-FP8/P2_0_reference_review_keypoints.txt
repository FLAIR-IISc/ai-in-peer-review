- Proposes K-SAN, which uses external knowledge (e.g., dependency relations, semantic graphs) to guide attention to salient substructures for NLU in spoken dialogue systems.  
- Contribution: a knowledge-guided attention mechanism that treats knowledge as a "teacher" to direct model focus rather than as simple features.  
- Contribution: end-to-end learning of attention that requires no manual feature engineering or external supervision for attention weights.  
- Contribution: demonstrates robustness and better generalization on small training datasets.  
- Strength: theoretically distinguishes knowledge-guided attention from feature enrichment and shows empirical gains (notably on Small dataset).  
- Strength: shows K-SAN generalizes across different knowledge sources (Stanford/SyntaxNet dependency trees, AMR graphs).  
- Strength: attention visualizations indicate the model focuses on relevant substructures (origin, destination, time) aligning with semantic tags.  
- Strength: notable robustness to data scarcity, outperforming baselines on very small training sets.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with modern transformer-based/ BERT-style sequence tagging baselines.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to dependency parser quality and parser error impact.  
- Weakness: missing ablation study isolating the attention mechanism’s contribution versus using substructures as inputs.  
- Weakness: insufficient theoretical justification for why attention-guided approach should outperform feature-based methods.  
- Weakness: claims of "end-to-end" learning are overstated or not clearly distinguished from prior feature-based extensions.  
- Question: provide evidence that attention is actually using external knowledge as a teacher rather than just treating substructures as features.  
- Question: how does automatic substructure learning via attention differ in practice from using knowledge-guided substructures as input features?  
- Question: how would K-SAN compare or interact with transformer-based models for slot filling?  
- Question: how sensitive is K-SAN to parser quality and at what point do parser errors cause significant degradation?  
- Question: why does K-SAN perform similarly with AMR graphs despite far fewer substructures, and what does that imply about necessary knowledge granularity?  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing comparisons with modern approaches, parser-dependency analysis, and ablation studies in the author response.
