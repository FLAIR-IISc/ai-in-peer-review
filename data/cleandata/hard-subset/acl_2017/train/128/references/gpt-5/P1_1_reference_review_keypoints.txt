- Proposes K-SAN: attention over parser/AMR-derived substructures combined with an RNN tagger for slot filling, showing consistent ATIS gains and interpretable attention.
- Contribution: end-to-end attention-over-substructures mechanism that leverages linguistic structure to improve data efficiency for sequence tagging.
- Contribution: empirical robustness to different knowledge sources/parsers (Stanford/SyntaxNet dependencies, AMR) and to limited training data.
- Contribution: interpretable framework with qualitative attention visualizations highlighting salient substructures.
- Strength: modular, differentiable integration of external knowledge as soft attention that mitigates parser noise and avoids hard feature error propagation.
- Strength: consistent improvements over baselines (CRF, vanilla RNN, CNN, tree-aware encoders), especially in low-resource settings, with statistical significance in many cases.
- Strength: attention visualizations show meaningful focus on relevant substructures (e.g., origin/destination/time), aiding interpretability.
- Strength: simple, implementable pipeline potentially adaptable to other structured knowledge sources.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to ATIS only; no multi-domain or other slot-filling benchmarks to assess generality.
- Weakness: missing stronger baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF with character features), making absolute competitiveness unclear.
- Weakness: incomplete ablation studies (no-attention baselines, joint vs only-guided/chain architectures, tied vs untied weights, use of edge/role labels, sensitivity to corrupted parses).
- Weakness: unclear architectural details (bidirectionality, absence of CRF decoding, how o is injected and its dimensionality) and some unsupported modeling/time claims.
- Weakness: efficiency and scalability claims lack runtime/memory analysis and statistics on substructure counts across knowledge sources.
- Weakness: generalization claim overstated because method requires structured analyses at inference and experiments use in-domain data/embeddings.
- Question: compare against stronger taggers (BiLSTM-CRF, char features) and report additive gains when stacked on them.
- Question: provide ablations for attention vs uniform/max pooling, joint vs guided-only vs chain-only, tied vs untied Min/Mkg, and inclusion of dependency/AMR labels.
- Question: clarify whether the RNN tagger is bidirectional, the effect of BiRNN+CRF, and exact mechanism/dimensionality of o injection.
- Question: quantify sensitivity to parser noise (perturbed/no parses) and report average/max substructures per sentence.
- Additional: report standard deviations/confidence intervals over multiple runs to support significance.
- Additional: provide brief error analysis showing where K-SAN helps or hurts relative to baselines.
- Additional: clarify data splits, dev set construction, and whether hyperparameters were tuned per dataset size.
