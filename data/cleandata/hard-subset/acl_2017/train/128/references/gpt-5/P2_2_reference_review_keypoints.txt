- Proposes K-SAN: attention-over-substructures using dependency/AMR root-to-leaf paths to guide sentence representations for slot filling, fused with an RNN tagger and a joint chain/knowledge tagger.
- Shows empirical F1 improvements on ATIS over CRF/RNN/CNN baselines and tree encoders, especially in low-data settings, robust across multiple knowledge sources.
- Trains end-to-end with attention over knowledge and claims efficiency by operating on substructures.

- Contribution: general end-to-end attention mechanism over external knowledge-derived substructures for sequence tagging.
- Contribution: empirical evidence of robustness across knowledge sources and data scarcity with competitive ATIS performance.
- Contribution: simple joint RNN architecture that combines chain-based and knowledge-guided representations.

- Strength: principled motivation—attention over substructures mitigates noisy parse errors and captures non-local relations relevant for slots.
- Strength: consistent empirical gains on ATIS, with larger improvements in low-resource regimes and across four knowledge sources.
- Strength: sensible encoder choices and ablations (NN/RNN/CNN) and use of a joint tagger to balance signals.
- Strength: qualitative attention analysis shows the model attends to slot-relevant substructures even with incorrect parses.
- Strength: end-to-end training with tied encoders reduces parameters and enforces consistent encoding.

- Weakness: evaluation limited to ATIS (small, saturated); absolute gains modest (~1 F1) and not tested on broader datasets.
- Weakness: missing stronger baselines (BiLSTM/BiGRU, BiLSTM-CRF, char/subword features, contextualized encoders) and comparisons to memory-network tagging variants.
- Weakness: insufficient ablations to isolate attention vs. knowledge contributions (e.g., uniform averaging, self-attention without knowledge, full-tree encodings).
- Weakness: efficiency/parallelizability claims unsubstantiated—no runtime/memory/throughput or scaling analysis.
- Weakness: under-specified methodological details (path linearization, duplicate tokens, path length, AMR token alignment, relation labels), affecting reproducibility.

- Question: clarify substructure formation—how root-to-leaf paths are linearized, duplicate handling, max path length, and AMR concept-to-surface-token mapping.
- Question: provide ablations for uniform averaging over substructures, self-attention over words without external knowledge, and inclusion of relation labels.
- Question: compare to stronger baselines (BiLSTM-CRF, memory-network over words/spans) and report chain-only and knowledge-only tagger performance and α sensitivity.
- Question: provide runtime/inference time and memory comparisons versus chain RNN and tree encoders and scaling with number of substructures.
- Question: report use of BiGRU in the tagger and effect of CRF/character features.
- Question: test generalization on additional SLU datasets (e.g., SNIPS, MIT) or provide cross-validation robustness within ATIS.

- Originality: moderate—adapts memory/attention ideas to knowledge-guided substructures for tagging.
- Soundness: generally plausible architecture and training, but missing ablations and stronger baselines reduce confidence.
- Clarity: mostly clear, but substructure extraction/encoding details need clarification.
- Significance: limited by dataset scope and modest gains; promising if validated more broadly.
- Reproducibility: partially reproducible; additional implementation details and code would help.
