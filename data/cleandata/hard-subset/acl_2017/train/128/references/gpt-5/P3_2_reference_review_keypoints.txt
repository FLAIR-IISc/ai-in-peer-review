- Summary: Proposes K-SAN, which attends over parse-derived substructures (dependency/AMR) to produce knowledge-guided representations combined with a GRU tagger for slot filling on ATIS.

- Contribution 1: Memory/attention architecture using linguistically motivated substructures as memory items, learned end-to-end with a token-level tagger.

- Contribution 2: Empirical evidence of robustness and data efficiency on ATIS, with consistent gains across parsers/formalisms and larger improvements in low-resource settings.

- Contribution 3: Joint architecture blending chain-based and knowledge-guided streams to increase robustness to noisy parses.

- Strength: Clear, modular idea extending attention/memory networks to sequence tagging by treating parse substructures as attendable memory.

- Strength: Consistent improvements over baselines and structural models, and similar performance across multiple parsers and formalisms.

- Strength: Data-efficiency and parser-noise resilience, with attention visualizations showing focus on semantically salient substructures.

- Strength: Explores multiple encoders (NN/RNN/CNN) with practical guidance on which works best at different data sizes.

- Strength: Clear presentation and appropriate connections to prior work (memory networks, dependency-CNN, Tree-RNN).

- Weakness: Limited novelty—method largely follows existing memory/attention mechanics; novelty mainly in choice of parse-derived items and joint integration.

- Weakness: Narrow evaluation (ATIS only) and modest gains in saturated regime; lacks comparisons to stronger contemporary baselines (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF).

- Weakness: Baselines are incomplete/unfair—no BiLSTM-CRF, no char features, unidirectional RNN, and no parameter-matched baseline to rule out capacity effects.

- Weakness: “End-to-end” and efficiency claims are overstated since approach depends on external parsers and provides no runtime/latency analysis.

- Weakness: Missing key ablations and design justifications (choice of substructures, ignoring dependency labels, tying Min and Mkg, impact of α, how o feeds into GRU, alternatives like uniform/random substructures).

- Question: Why no BiLSTM(-CRF) or char-level baselines, and can K-SAN be evaluated on top of BiLSTM-CRF to show orthogonality?

- Question: How many random restarts were used; report mean±std and describe t-test procedure for statistical significance.

- Question: Can you add a parameter-matched RNN/GRU baseline or increase hidden size to rule out gains from extra capacity?

- Question: Please provide ablations for dependency labels, tying Min/Mkg, sensitivity to α, attention mass distribution, multi-hop benefit, and details of o’s integration into GRU.

- Question: Can you evaluate on an additional dataset (e.g., SNIPS/MIT) and/or with stronger embeddings (GloVe) to show generalization?

- Question: Provide efficiency metrics: average #substructures per sentence, time per epoch, and inference latency vs. baselines.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—sound idea with consistent low-data gains but limited novelty and incomplete evaluation; addressing baselines and ablations would strengthen the paper.
