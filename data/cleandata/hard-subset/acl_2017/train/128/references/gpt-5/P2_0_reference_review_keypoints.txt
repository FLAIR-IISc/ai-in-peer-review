- Proposes K-SAN: attention over knowledge-guided substructures (dependency/AMR) to form a sentence vector injected into an RNN slot tagger, exploring NN/RNN/CNN encoders.  
- Empirically shows consistent F1 gains on ATIS across data regimes, robust to parser/structure choice, with best model ≈95 F1.  
- Contributions claimed: general attention-over-substructures mechanism, empirical gains (especially low-data), and analysis of attention focusing on salient substructures.

- Strength: Conceptually simple, general, differentiable design that can ingest heterogeneous structural knowledge.  
- Strength: Consistent empirical improvements over sentence-encoder and structural baselines, largest in low-resource settings.  
- Strength: Clear ablations across encoders and multiple knowledge providers strengthen attribution to the method.  
- Strength: Interpretable attention visualizations align with semantically important regions and tolerate parser errors.  
- Strength: Joint tagger architecture sensibly combines sequential and global structural cues.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to ATIS and missing strong contemporary baselines (BiLSTM, BiLSTM-CRF, joint intent+slot, char/subword features) and cross-dataset tests.  
- Weakness: Novelty is moderate; mechanism resembles memory/attention models—the novelty is mainly the choice of knowledge-derived substructures.  
- Weakness: Method details and ablations are incomplete (AMR substructure construction, relation labels, tying Min/Mkg, alpha sensitivity, attention hops, dropout).  
- Weakness: Statistical testing/protocol unclear—no run counts, mean±std, or confidence intervals for small gains.  
- Weakness: Efficiency/parallelizability claims unsupported—no runtime/memory comparisons or scaling analysis.

- Question: Can you compare to BiLSTM, BiLSTM-CRF, and joint intent+slot models with same lexical features?  
- Question: How many random restarts; please report mean±std and clarify significance testing.  
- Question: Ablations: (a) uniform attention over substructures? (b) untied Min and Mkg? (c) include dependency/AMR edge labels? (d) sensitivity to α and its optimality?  
- Question: How are AMR substructures constructed and how are re-entrancies handled?  
- Question: What is training/inference overhead vs CNN/RNN baselines as a function of sentence length and substructure count; any batching/pruning strategies?

- Originality: Moderate—useful instantiation but builds closely on existing attention/memory and syntax-aware encoders.  
- Quality: Solid method and consistent evidence, but weakened by missing baselines, limited datasets, and incomplete ablations.  
- Clarity: Generally clear with helpful figures; some methodological/statistical details need clarification.  
- Significance: Promising for low-resource SLU and generalizable, but impact limited until validated on broader benchmarks and stronger baselines.
