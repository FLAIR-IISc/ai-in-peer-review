- Proposes K-SAN: attention over knowledge-derived substructures (e.g., dependency root-to-leaf paths/AMR edges) aggregated into an RNN tagger with a joint chain+knowledge tagger.  
- Contribution: task-specific attention over substructures trained end-to-end, unlike fixed-feature or whole-structure encoders.  
- Contribution: empirical gains on ATIS, robust in low-resource settings and generalizes across multiple parsers/AMR sources.  
- Contribution: modular architecture supporting NN/CNN/RNN substructure encoders and qualitative attention analyses.  
- Strength: principled mitigation of whole-structure brittleness by selectively attending to substructures, supported by visualizations.  
- Strength: consistent F1 improvements over non-structural baselines, especially with limited training data (+1–2 F1 with CNN encoder).  
- Strength: knowledge-source generality—similar improvements using different dependency and AMR parsers.  
- Strength: architectural flexibility (multiple encoders, weight tying, joint tagger) that improves robustness to noisy knowledge.  
- Strength: clear presentation with pipeline, equations, attention heatmaps, and significance tests.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation scope (single, small/saturated ATIS benchmark) and missing comparisons to stronger modern baselines (BiLSTM-CRF, char/subword, contextual embeddings).  
- Weakness: “end-to-end” claim overstated since external parsers are required at train and test time.  
- Weakness: missing critical ablations (chain-only, knowledge-only, joint, no-attention/uniform averaging, effect of tying Mkg/Min).  
- Weakness: efficiency/parallelizability claims lack complexity analysis or runtime/latency measurements.  
- Weakness: underanalysis of knowledge choices—ignoring dependency labels and choosing root-to-leaf paths not justified or compared to alternatives; parser-noise sensitivity not quantified.  
- Question: report ablations for chain-only, knowledge-only, joint, and no-attention—how much does each component contribute?  
- Question: sensitivity to number/type of substructures and rationale for root-to-leaf paths?  
- Question: have you tried including dependency/AMR labels or alternative substructure definitions (e.g., shortest paths between entities)?  
- Question: what is test-time overhead—parsing time, model inference per utterance, and end-to-end latency vs Tree-RNN/DCNN/BiLSTM-CRF?  
- Question: quantify robustness to parser noise (e.g., bucket test utterances by parser confidence and report F1).  
- Question: is α=0.5 tuned on dev, and how does performance vary with α across data sizes/knowledge sources?  
- Question: can you add a stronger tagging baseline and/or a second dataset to establish generality and significance?  
- Overall: novelty moderate; technical quality sound but missing ablations and efficiency evidence; clarity good; significance limited by scope—lean borderline/weak accept if additional analyses and stronger baselines are added, otherwise weak reject.
