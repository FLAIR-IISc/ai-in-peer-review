- Proposes K-SAN: attend over embeddings of decomposed external structural substructures (dependency/AMR paths) to produce knowledge-guided representations injected into an RNN tagger, with a joint chain+knowledge RNN tagger.
- Evaluated on ATIS across training sizes; K-SAN (NN/RNN/CNN encoders) outperforms CRF, RNN, CNN sentence-encoder baselines and structural baselines, with larger gains in low-resource settings.
- Shows qualitative attention visualizations and compares dependency vs AMR knowledge for generalization.

- Contribution: practical architecture combining memory/attention over external linguistic substructures with end-to-end trainable tagging.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that knowledge-guided attention improves slot filling (notably in low-resource regimes) and is robust across parsers/formalisms.
- Contribution: simple method to convert heterogeneous structures (dependencies, AMR) into comparable substructures for attention.

- Strength: clear, modular, and reproducible design bridging external structures and sequence tagging without hand-crafted features.
- Strength: consistent F1 improvements across data scales, strongest in Small/Medium training sizes.
- Strength: similar results across different parsers and AMR tools, indicating robustness to knowledge source.
- Strength: attention visualizations are interpretable and show resilience to some parser errors.
- Strength: conceptually novel relative to prior SLU work by selectively attending to substructures rather than encoding full trees.

- Weakness: limited empirical scope—only ATIS used, which is small, idiosyncratic, and nearly solved.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to strong modern taggers (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF with char embeddings and CRF decoding).
- Weakness: “end-to-end” claim is overstated because external parsers/AMR tools are fixed and not jointly trained.
- Weakness: incomplete ablations and unclear architectural details (attention vs averaging, role of joint vs standalone components, tied encoders, α sensitivity, GRU integration equations).
- Weakness: efficiency/parallelizability claims unsubstantiated—no runtime, memory, or scaling analysis.
- Weakness: ignores structural relation labels (dep/AMR), possibly discarding useful information.

- Question: please report BiLSTM-CRF baselines (with/without char embeddings) and how K-SAN compares or combines with CRF decoding.
- Question: provide ablations for chain RNN vs knowledge RNN vs joint; attention vs uniform averaging; tied vs untied encoders; and α variations/learning.
- Question: give exact GRU injection equations showing how o is integrated into gates and state updates.
- Question: report distribution of substructure counts/lengths per knowledge source and whether longer paths bias attention.
- Question: did you try using relation/edge-role labels or embeddings, and what was the effect?
- Question: clarify dev set construction on ATIS and whether hyperparameters were selected per training-size setting.

- Additional: recommend a quantitative robustness test (e.g., randomly perturb edges) to support parser-error resilience claims.
- Additional: clarify whether word embeddings are trained from scratch or pre-trained/fine-tuned, and how OOV/character info is handled.
- Additional: fix incomplete/inconsistently formatted references.
