- Summary: Proposes K-SAN, which uses knowledge-guided attention (e.g., dependency trees, AMR) to focus on input substructures for SLU, improving ATIS performance especially with limited data.

- Contribution 1: Introduces an attention mechanism that integrates structured external knowledge to dynamically weight relevant substructures.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates robustness and clear performance gains in low-data regimes for slot filling.

- Contribution 3: Shows generalizability by applying the approach to different knowledge sources (dependency trees and AMR).

- Strength 1: Novel idea of knowledge acting as a “teacher” to guide attention, advancing beyond static feature usage.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements over baselines.

- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation and ablation studies across encoders (NN, RNN, CNN) and knowledge types.

- Strength 4: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures.

- Strength 5: Demonstrated applicability across multiple structured knowledge representations.

- Weakness 1: Knowledge structures explored are mainly parse-tree-like; other knowledge forms (ontologies, frame nets) are not evaluated.

- Weakness 2: Model performance depends on parser quality; paper lacks quantitative analysis of parser error impact.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient in-depth analysis of attention weights to explain what the model consistently attends to and why.

- Weakness 4: Computational complexity and resource impact (runtime, memory vs. standard RNNs) are not thoroughly discussed.

- Question 1: How sensitive is K-SAN to parser error rates—can you quantify parser accuracy’s effect on slot filling?

- Question 2: Can you provide detailed analyses of learned attention weights and their relation to dependency relations or AMR concepts per slot type?

- Question 3: What is K-SAN’s computational complexity compared to standard RNN-based SLU, and how do substructure counts affect runtime/memory?

- Question 4: Have you tried integrating other knowledge forms (ontologies, frame nets); if not, what are the integration challenges?

- Additional comment 1: Related work section is comprehensive and well-organized.

- Additional comment 2: Code release is recommended to facilitate reproducibility.

- Additional comment 3: Consider adding discussion of real-world applications and limitations in the conclusion.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, and effective approach with solid empirical support—recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
