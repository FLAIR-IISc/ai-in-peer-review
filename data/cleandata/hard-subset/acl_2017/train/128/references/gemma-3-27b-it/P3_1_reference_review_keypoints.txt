- Summary: Proposes K-SAN, which uses external structural knowledge (dependency/AMR) to guide attention in RNNs for SLU.

- Contribution: Presents a principled framework to integrate structural knowledge into sequence tagging via knowledge-guided attention.

- Contribution: Demonstrates consistent improvements on ATIS and robustness across different training data sizes.

- Contribution: Shows flexibility by applying K-SAN to both dependency parses and AMR graphs.

- Strength: Clear motivation and problem definition highlighting NLU pipeline limitations and need for knowledge integration.

- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation with baselines, ablations, varied data sizes, and significance testing.

- Strength: Well-designed architecture that combines chain-based and knowledge-guided information effectively.

- Strength: Demonstrated generalizability across different knowledge representations.

- Strength: Attention weight visualizations provide insight into how the model uses knowledge structures.

- Weakness: Individual components (RNNs, attention, parsing) are established; novelty is mainly in their integration.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on external parsers; parser errors may propagate and affect slot-filling without detailed error analysis.

- Weakness: Lacks quantitative analysis of computational complexity, including parsing cost and model runtime.

- Weakness: No comparison to recent state-of-the-art end-to-end models (e.g., Transformer-based SLU).

- Question: Please provide parser error analysis correlating parsing accuracy with slot-filling errors and example failure cases.

- Question: Provide quantitative comparisons of training and inference time (computational cost) versus baselines.

- Question: Have you compared to or considered adapting K-SAN to transformer-based SLU models; what are the challenges/benefits?

- Question: Beyond dependency and AMR, what other knowledge types can K-SAN integrate and what are the limitations?

- Additional: Paper is well-written with helpful figures.

- Additional: Provides a good set of references.

- Overall: Solid, well-executed work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address parser errors, computational cost, and SOTA comparisons.
