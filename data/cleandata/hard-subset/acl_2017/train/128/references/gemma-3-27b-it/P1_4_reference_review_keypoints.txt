- Summary: Proposes K-SAN to guide attention with external knowledge (dependency trees) for spoken dialogue state tracking, claiming end-to-end learning, knowledge-agnosticism, and efficient/parallelizable structure.
- Contribution 1: Practical framework to integrate structured knowledge (dependency parses) into sequence tagging models.
- Contribution 2: Empirical validation on ATIS showing improvements and useful ablations over strong baselines.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates generalization across parsers (Stanford, SyntaxNet) and a different representation (AMR).
- Strength 1: Clear motivation and problem definition addressing data sparsity and limits of feature-based approaches.
- Strength 2: Sound, well-explained methodology using knowledge-guided attention over substructures.
- Strength 3: Strong empirical gains, especially with limited training data.
- Strength 4: Comprehensive experiments and helpful ablation studies across encoders and knowledge sources.
- Strength 5: Well-written and organized presentation with informative figures.
- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—similar ideas exist (dependency-CNNs, tree RNNs); contribution is mainly implementation and evaluation.
- Weakness 2: Dependence on parser accuracy; parsing errors may propagate and impact performance without thorough analysis.
- Weakness 3: Lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., attention visualizations) to show how K-SAN makes decisions.
- Weakness 4: Limited scope of knowledge sources tested—mostly syntactic (dependency, AMR); other knowledge graphs not explored.
- Question 1: Request for detailed parser error analysis and patterns of error impact.
- Question 2: Request for attention visualizations on example utterances to illustrate model behavior.
- Question 3: Request for concrete computational complexity analysis and comparison to standard RNNs as sentence length grows.
- Question 4: Have alternative knowledge representations (e.g., Wikidata, ConceptNet) been tried, and what were the results?
- Additional comment 1: Suggests minor notation improvements for clarity (consistent subscripts).
- Additional comment 2: References need formatting consistency.
- Additional comment 3: Broken link to SyntaxNet in Table 2 should be fixed.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with valuable empirical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
