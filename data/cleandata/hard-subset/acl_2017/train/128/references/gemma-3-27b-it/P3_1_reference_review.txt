## Review of "Knowledge as a Teacher: Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN), a novel neural network architecture designed to incorporate external knowledge (specifically, dependency parse trees in the presented experiments) into spoken language understanding (SLU) systems. The core idea is to use this knowledge to guide attention mechanisms within an RNN, allowing the model to focus on semantically relevant substructures of the input utterance. The authors claim contributions in end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization across knowledge types, and efficiency/parallelizability.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A Principled Approach to Knowledge Integration:** The paper presents a well-defined framework for integrating structural knowledge into a neural network architecture for sequence tagging.  The attention mechanism, guided by the knowledge structure, is a clear and logical way to leverage prior information.
2. **Demonstrated Effectiveness on a Benchmark Dataset:** The authors show consistent improvements over strong baselines on the ATIS dataset, demonstrating the practical benefit of their approach.  The experiments across different dataset sizes also highlight the model's robustness.
3. **Flexibility of Knowledge Source:**  The demonstration of applicability to both dependency trees *and* AMR graphs showcases the broader potential of K-SAN to incorporate different forms of linguistic knowledge.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper effectively articulates the limitations of traditional NLU pipelines and the challenges of leveraging knowledge in deep learning models. The issues of poor generalization and error propagation are well-explained.
2. **Solid Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental setup is thorough, including a range of baseline models and ablation studies (different knowledge encoding methods).  The use of varying training data sizes is a strong point, highlighting the model's robustness to data scarcity. The statistical significance testing provides further confidence in the results.
3. **Well-Designed Architecture:** The K-SAN architecture is conceptually sound. The use of attention, guided by knowledge structures, is a natural fit for the task and avoids the issues associated with simply concatenating knowledge features.  The joint RNN tagger appears to effectively combine chain-based and knowledge-guided information.
4. **Demonstrated Generalizability of Knowledge Source:** Showing the model works with both dependency parses and AMR graphs is a significant strength. It suggests the method is not overly sensitive to the specific knowledge representation used.
5. **Analysis of Attention Weights:** The visualization of attention weights (Figure 5) provides valuable insight into how the model is utilizing the knowledge structures and reinforces the plausibility of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the *combination* of techniques is novel, the individual components – RNNs, attention mechanisms, dependency parsing – are all well-established. The core innovation lies in the specific way these components are integrated, but the novelty may be incremental rather than groundbreaking.
2. **Reliance on Pre-existing Parsers:**  The performance of K-SAN is heavily reliant on the quality of the dependency parser (and AMR parser). Errors in parsing will propagate into the model, potentially diminishing its effectiveness.  The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t fully address the impact of parser errors on the results. A more detailed error analysis, including examples of misparsed sentences and their impact on slot filling, would be beneficial.
3. **Limited Discussion of Computational Complexity:**  While the paper claims efficiency and parallelizability, there is no quantitative analysis of the computational cost of K-SAN compared to the baselines.  The parsing step itself can be computationally expensive, and the impact of this on overall performance is not discussed.
4. **Lack of Comparison to State-of-the-Art End-to-End Models:** The baselines are somewhat dated. A comparison to more recent, state-of-the-art end-to-end SLU models (e.g., those based on Transformers) would provide a more rigorous assessment of the K-SAN's performance.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Parser Error Analysis:**  Could you provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of parser errors on the performance of K-SAN? Specifically, how does parsing accuracy correlate with slot filling accuracy?  Can you provide examples of sentences where misparsing led to incorrect slot filling?
2. **Computational Cost:** Could you provide a quantitative comparison of the computational cost (training time, inference time) of K-SAN with the baselines?
3. **Transformer-Based Comparison:** Have you considered comparing K-SAN to state-of-the-art transformer-based SLU models? If not, could you discuss the potential challenges and benefits of adapting K-SAN to a transformer architecture?
4. **Knowledge Representation:** Beyond dependency trees and AMR graphs, what other forms of knowledge could be effectively integrated into K-SAN?  Are there any limitations on the types of knowledge that can be used?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.
*   The figures are helpful and informative.
*   The authors have provided a good set of references.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a promising approach to incorporating knowledge into neural network-based SLU systems. While the individual components are not novel, the integration of these components is well-motivated and effectively demonstrated. The experimental results are convincing, and the analysis of attention weights provides valuable insights.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.  Specifically, a more detailed discussion of parser errors and computational cost, and a comparison to more recent state-of-the-art models, would significantly strengthen the paper.
