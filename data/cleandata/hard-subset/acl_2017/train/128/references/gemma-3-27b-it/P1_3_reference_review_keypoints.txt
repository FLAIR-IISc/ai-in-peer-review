- Proposes K-SAN, a neural architecture that uses external structural knowledge to guide attention for improved NLU in goal-oriented dialogue.
- Contribution: End-to-end learning of attention guided by external knowledge structures (e.g., dependency parses).
- Contribution: Generalizes to different knowledge sources beyond specific schemas (demonstrated with AMR graphs).
- Contribution: Claims efficiency via parallelizable substructure modeling.
- Strength: Strong empirical results on ATIS, especially in low-data settings, outperforming strong baselines.
- Strength: Well-motivated approach addressing reliance on large datasets and ignorance of linguistic structure.
- Strength: Clear and thorough presentation with helpful architecture diagram and experimental details.
- Strength: Demonstrated flexibility by applying K-SAN to dependency parses and AMR graphs.
- Strength: Effective attention visualizations that provide qualitative insight into model reasoning.
- Weakness: Individual components (RNNs, attention, parsing) are standard; novelty is primarily in their integration.
- Weakness: Performance depends on external parser quality; lacks systematic analysis of parser error impact.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness.
- Weakness: Missing detailed ablation study on attention components and scoring/encoding choices.
- Question: Please provide a detailed analysis of the impact and types of parser errors on K-SAN performance.
- Question: How sensitive is K-SAN to key hyperparameters (learning rate, embedding size); please show performance variation.
- Question: Can you ablate the attention mechanism to quantify contributions of different knowledge encoders and scoring functions?
- Question: Please provide a detailed computational complexity comparison between K-SAN and standard RNN-based approaches for long utterances.
- Additional comment: The term "teacher" in the title is misleading; consider a clearer title like "Knowledge-Guided Attention Networks for NLU."
- Additional comment: Related work discussion could better contrast K-SAN with recent neural methods that integrate knowledge graphs beyond feature enrichment.
