- Proposes K-SAN: an RNN with knowledge-guided attention using dependency parses/structured knowledge for slot filling.
- Claims: end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization across knowledge types, and efficiency/parallelizability.
- Practical method to integrate structured knowledge into sequence tagging via attention over parse-guided substructures.
- Demonstrates robustness across different knowledge sources (different parsers, AMR graphs).
- Empirical improvements on ATIS, especially in low-data settings, with statistically significant gains.
- Clear motivation: addresses error propagation and poor generalization in traditional NLU.
- Attention mechanism effectively and intuitively integrates knowledge; visualization supports this.
- Thorough evaluation including ablations and different knowledge representations.
- Well-written, organized paper with clear figures and tables.
- Novelty is limited: uses established components (RNN, attention, parsing) combined incrementally.
- Performance depends on parsing quality; parsing errors can hurt and need deeper analysis.
- Sensitivity to encoder type and dataset size suggests hyperparameter tuning is important.
- Lacks comparison to recent NLU models (e.g., BERT/Transformer-based approaches).
- Requests more analysis: impact of parsing errors and mitigation strategies.
- Requests details on hyperparameter tuning procedures and sensitivity.
- Requests computational complexity comparison (training/inference time) versus other models.
- Asks whether K-SAN was tested with transformer/BERT architectures and results if so.
- Suggests adding limitations discussion (dependence on high-quality parsers/KBs) and clearer examples of KB-input mismatch.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
