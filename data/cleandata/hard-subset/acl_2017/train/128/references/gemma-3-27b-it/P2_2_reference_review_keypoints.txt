- Proposes K-SAN, a neural architecture that uses external structural knowledge (e.g., dependency trees) to guide attention for spoken dialogue state tracking (domain, intent, slot filling).
- Claims end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization to different knowledge sources, and efficiency/parallelizability via structural decomposition.
- Principled integration of structural knowledge: knowledge actively shapes attention rather than serving as extra features.
- Demonstrates robustness and improved performance, especially in low-data settings.
- Empirically validated across knowledge sources (dependency trees and AMR), showing flexibility beyond a single parser type.
- Novel idea in combining knowledge-guided attention, though individual components (RNNs, attention, parsers) are standard.
- Performance depends on external parser accuracy; parsing errors can propagate and need systematic analysis.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity and limited reporting on tuning reduce reproducibility; key hyperparameters and sensitivity are unclear.
- Lacks detailed error analysis to reveal remaining failure modes or challenging slot types/linguistic phenomena.
- Requests from authors: analyze impact of parser accuracy/noisy parses, report key hyperparameters and sensitivity, provide detailed error analysis, and offer empirical evidence for efficiency/parallelizability claims.
- Related work is comprehensive; ATIS is an appropriate dataset; minor typo noted in JAMR reference.
- Overall: strong, well-motivated paper with compelling results; recommend accept with minor revisions addressing the listed weaknesses.
