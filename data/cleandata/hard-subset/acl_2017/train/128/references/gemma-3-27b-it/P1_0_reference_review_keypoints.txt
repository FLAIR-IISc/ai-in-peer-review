- Summary: Proposes K-SAN to guide attention with external knowledge (dependency parses) for improved slot filling in goal-oriented dialogue systems.  
- Contribution 1: End-to-end integration of structural knowledge into attention for NLU.  
- Contribution 2: Shows generalization to different knowledge sources (dependency trees, AMR).  
- Contribution 3: Claims efficiency and parallelizability via substructure-based modeling.  
- Strength 1: Strong empirical gains on ATIS, especially with limited training data.  
- Strength 2: Clear motivation and well-articulated "knowledge as a teacher" approach.  
- Strength 3: Thorough experiments and ablation studies across knowledge encoders and parsers.  
- Strength 4: Attention visualizations that illustrate model focus on relevant substructures.  
- Strength 5: Well-written, organized paper with good related-work coverage.  
- Weakness 1: Limited novelty in core components; novelty is mainly in their integration and application.  
- Weakness 2: Performance depends on dependency parsing accuracy; impact of parsing errors not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness 3: Lack of quantitative analysis of attention behavior and substructure attention patterns.  
- Weakness 4: Missing comparisons to recent SOTA NLU models (e.g., transformer/BERT-based slot fillers).  
- Question 1: How sensitive is K-SAN to dependency parsing errors, and can parser confidence be used to mitigate issues?  
- Question 2: Can the authors provide quantitative analyses of attention distributions (e.g., root vs. leaf attention)?  
- Question 3: What is the computational cost of K-SAN versus standard RNN slot-filling models as sentence length grows?  
- Question 4: How does K-SAN compare experimentally to a BERT-based slot filling baseline on ATIS?  
- Additional comment 1: Provide more detail on hyperparameter choices (e.g., hidden layer sizes).  
- Additional comment 2: Discuss potential applications beyond dialogue systems to broaden impact.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with valuable results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address analyses and comparisons.
