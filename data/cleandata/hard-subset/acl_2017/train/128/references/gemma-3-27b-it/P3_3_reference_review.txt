## Review for "Knowledge as a Teacher: Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN) to improve natural language understanding (NLU) for goal-oriented spoken dialogue systems. The authors argue that incorporating external knowledge – specifically, linguistic structures derived from dependency parsing – can improve sequence tagging performance (domain classification, intent determination, and slot filling) by guiding attention mechanisms. The core idea is to represent knowledge as “substructures” and learn to attend to these substructures during the encoding process. They demonstrate improvements on the ATIS dataset using various knowledge encoding methods (NN, RNN, CNN) and show generalization across different knowledge resources (dependency trees and AMR graphs).

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A practical framework for integrating structural knowledge into a deep learning NLU pipeline:** While the idea of combining knowledge and deep learning isn't new, this paper presents a relatively straightforward and generalizable approach for doing so through attention mechanisms. The flexibility to accept different knowledge representations is a strength.
2. **Empirical demonstration of the benefits of knowledge guidance, particularly in low-resource settings:** The experiments show consistent gains, particularly with smaller training datasets, suggesting the model effectively leverages external knowledge to overcome data limitations.
3. **Validation of the approach's robustness to different knowledge sources:** The comparison using dependency trees and AMR graphs shows the method isn't overly reliant on a specific knowledge representation.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of NLU in dialogue systems and the potential benefits of incorporating external knowledge. The limitations of prior work – particularly the feature engineering approach and error propagation – are well explained.
2. **Well-Designed Experiments:** The experiments are thorough, including ablation studies (different knowledge encoders), varying dataset sizes, and comparisons with strong baselines. The use of both dependency trees and AMR graphs demonstrates the versatility of the approach.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** K-SAN consistently outperforms baseline models across all experimental settings, and the gains are statistically significant. The particularly strong performance with limited data is compelling.
4. **Good Presentation & Reproducibility Potential:** The paper is well-written and the architecture is clearly explained with a helpful diagram. The authors mention implementation details (optimizers, learning rates) which improves reproducibility.
5. **Effective Attention Visualization:** The visualization of attention weights (Figure 5) provides valuable insight into how the model is leveraging knowledge and reinforces the claim that it focuses on relevant substructures.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:**  The core components of K-SAN – attention mechanisms, RNNs, and dependency parsing – are well-established techniques. The primary novelty lies in *how* these are combined, rather than in fundamentally new algorithmic contributions.  The claim of being the "first neural network approach" utilizing knowledge guidance "in an end-to-end fashion" feels overstated; previous work (e.g., incorporating knowledge embeddings as features) can also be considered end-to-end.
2. **Reliance on High-Quality Parsers:** The performance of K-SAN is heavily reliant on the quality of the dependency parser and/or AMR parser. While the authors demonstrate robustness across *different* parsers, the experiments do not explicitly address the impact of *parser errors*. The example in Figure 5 highlights a parsing mistake, and it's unclear how robust the model is to such errors in practice.  This is a significant practical limitation.
3. **Limited Error Analysis:** While the results are strong, the paper lacks a detailed error analysis.  Understanding *where* the model still fails and *why* would provide valuable insights for future research.  Are there specific types of slots or utterances where the model struggles?  What kind of knowledge would be most beneficial to address these weaknesses?
4. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The impact of different hyperparameters (e.g., the weight α in the joint RNN tagger) isn't fully explored. A more detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity would strengthen the paper.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the performance of K-SAN to errors in the dependency parsing or AMR graph? Have you explored strategies to mitigate the impact of noisy or inaccurate knowledge?  Could techniques like self-training or data augmentation with intentionally corrupted structures be beneficial?
2.  Can you provide a more detailed error analysis, focusing on the types of errors K-SAN still makes and identifying potential sources of these errors?
3.  What is the computational overhead of incorporating the knowledge guidance mechanism? How does the inference time of K-SAN compare to that of the baseline models?
4.  The visualization in Figure 5 is very helpful. Can you provide more examples of attention weights for different types of utterances and demonstrate how the model learns to attend to different substructures?
5.  Regarding the claim of being the first end-to-end approach, can you elaborate on the specific differences between your approach and previous work that utilizes knowledge embeddings or other knowledge-based features in conjunction with neural networks?

**Additional Comments:**

*  Consider mentioning relevant work on robust parsing or methods for handling noisy knowledge graphs.
*  The paper would benefit from a discussion of the limitations of the current approach and directions for future research.  For example, exploring more sophisticated ways to integrate knowledge, such as knowledge graph embeddings or reasoning mechanisms, could be promising.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of NLU for dialogue systems. While the core components are not entirely novel, the effective integration of knowledge guidance through attention mechanisms and the strong empirical results make this work worthy of acceptance. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.
