- Introduces K-SAN, an architecture that uses external structural knowledge to guide attention in RNNs for SLU tasks.  
- Contribution: effectively integrates dependency-parse-based structural knowledge into neural SLU models, improving focus on relevant utterance substructures.  
- Contribution: demonstrates generalization across different knowledge sources (different parsers, AMR), showing flexibility and robustness.  
- Contribution: provides practical SLU improvements on ATIS, especially in low-resource settings, with claims of end-to-end learning and efficiency.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains over strong baselines, notably in small-data regimes.  
- Strength: well-motivated approach that addresses limitations of treating knowledge as mere features by using knowledge to guide attention.  
- Strength: clear, detailed methodology with useful diagrams and ablation studies over different knowledge encoders (NN, RNN, CNN).  
- Strength: robustness to different knowledge sources suggests reduced reliance on any single parser’s quality.  
- Strength: attention visualizations give insight into how knowledge guides the model.  
- Weakness: core components (RNNs, attention, dependency parsing) are not novel; novelty is in their integration.  
- Weakness: performance depends on parsing accuracy and the paper lacks detailed analysis of error propagation from parses.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to the relatively simple ATIS dataset; generalizability to complex domains is untested.  
- Weakness: limited detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity.  
- Question: request for parsing error analysis and examples showing degraded performance due to incorrect parses.  
- Question: request for quantitative comparisons of training and inference time versus baselines to support efficiency claims.  
- Question: ask for elaboration on integrating other knowledge types (e.g., knowledge graphs) and required model modifications.  
- Question: request for detailed hyperparameter tuning process, search space, and prioritized metrics.  
- Comment: writing is generally clear though occasionally dense.  
- Comment: related work coverage is comprehensive and provides good context.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed paper with valuable K-SAN contributions and recommended acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
