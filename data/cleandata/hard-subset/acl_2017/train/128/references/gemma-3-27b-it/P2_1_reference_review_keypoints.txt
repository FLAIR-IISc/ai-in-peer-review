- Proposes Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN) to incorporate external structural knowledge into semantic slot filling via attention over dependency substructures.  
- Integrates structural knowledge directly into learning, using attention to weight relevant substructures instead of treating knowledge as features.  
- Framework generalizes across knowledge sources (demonstrated with dependency parses) and is designed to adapt to other representations.  
- Achieves competitive/state-of-the-art results on ATIS, especially in low-data regimes, with solid empirical validation.  
- Novel idea of using knowledge as a "teacher" to guide attention, addressing feature-engineering and error-propagation issues.  
- Strong empirical improvements over baselines and robust ablation studies showing sensitivity to knowledge sources.  
- Clear motivation, presentation, and helpful diagrams aiding model understanding.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across dataset sizes and multiple knowledge sources.  
- Performance depends on dependency parsing quality; lacks thorough sensitivity analysis to parsing errors.  
- Limited exploration of other knowledge types (e.g., knowledge graphs, ontologies) and unexamined effects of graph density/quality.  
- Increased architectural complexity without clear analysis of computational cost, scalability, or training/inference time trade-offs.  
- Missing qualitative error analysis to illuminate remaining failure modes and attribution to knowledge or attention issues.  
- Requests analysis of robustness to noisy parses, computational cost comparisons, illustrative examples where K-SAN outperforms baselines, and challenges for adapting to knowledge graphs.  
- References are appropriate; code release would be beneficial.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, well-motivated paper with convincing results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
