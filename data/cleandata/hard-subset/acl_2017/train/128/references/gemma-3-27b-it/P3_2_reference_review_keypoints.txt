- Proposal: Introduces K-SAN, using external knowledge (dependency parses) to guide attention for improved slot filling in goal-oriented NLU, claiming end-to-end learning, knowledge generality, and efficiency.
- Contribution 1: Effectively integrates structural knowledge into neural attention, improving interpretability and robustness, especially in low-resource settings.
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates consistent, statistically significant performance gains on ATIS, notably with limited training data.
- Contribution 3: Shows flexibility to swap knowledge sources (different parsers/representations) with empirical validation.
- Strength: Clear motivation and problem statement addressing data reliance and ignored structure in NLU.
- Strength: Sound, well-structured methodology with a separate knowledge encoding module and guided attention.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments varying data size and knowledge sources, including significance testing.
- Strength: Positive results and helpful ablation-style comparisons; paper is well written and organized.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—uses established components (RNNs, attention, parsing) with a fairly standard attention description.
- Weakness: Performance depends on parser quality, with insufficient analysis of how parsing errors affect outputs.
- Weakness: Sparse reporting on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity across knowledge sources.
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to recent transformer-based NLU models (e.g., BERT) or discussion of why omitted.
- Questions: Request analyses on parser-error sensitivity, detailed hyperparameter tables, runtime comparisons to RNN baselines, attempts/constraints for transformer integration, and which knowledge sources underperformed and why.
- Additional comments: Reference list has missing details; Figure 5 visualization should show predicted slots alongside attention weights.
- Overall: Solid, practical contribution with valuable gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address analyses and comparisons.
