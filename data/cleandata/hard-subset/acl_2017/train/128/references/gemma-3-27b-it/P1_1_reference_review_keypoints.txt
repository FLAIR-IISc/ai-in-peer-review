- Proposes K-SAN, a neural architecture that uses external structural knowledge (e.g., dependency parses) to guide attention for slot filling and intent detection.
- Claims: end-to-end learning with knowledge guidance, generalization to different knowledge types, and efficiency/parallelizability.
- Demonstrates effective integration of structural knowledge improving performance, especially with limited training data.
- Provides comprehensive experiments on ATIS, including comparisons to strong baselines and ablation studies across varying data sizes.
- Shows generalizability across different parsers and semantic graph representations.
- Strong empirical gains in low-resource settings with statistically significant improvements.
- Clear motivation contrasting with error-prone feature-enrichment approaches; “knowledge as a teacher” analogy is effective.
- Flexibility to incorporate multiple knowledge sources (dependency trees, AMR graphs, different parsers).
- Thorough baseline comparisons strengthen validation of the approach.
- Novelty is incremental—the components (attention, RNNs, parsing) are established; contribution is in their combination.
- Performance depends on parser accuracy; impact of parsing errors is not fully quantified.
- Limited details and analysis on hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly attention-related settings and α in the joint tagger.
- Lacks detailed error analysis describing remaining failure modes and challenging slot types/utterances.
- Requests analysis correlating parsing accuracy with slot-filling performance.
- Requests detailed hyperparameter tuning ranges and selection methodology.
- Requests ablation quantifying the impact of removing the knowledge-guided attention versus using only sentence embeddings.
- Requests concrete error examples and discussion of persistent error types.
- Minor presentation notes: figures could use larger fonts; references and writing are clear and well-formatted.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses and questions.
