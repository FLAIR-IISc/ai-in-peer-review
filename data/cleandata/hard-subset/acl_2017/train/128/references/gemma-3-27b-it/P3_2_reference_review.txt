## Review for "Knowledge as a Teacher: Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN) for improving natural language understanding (NLU) in goal-oriented spoken dialogue systems. The core idea is to leverage external knowledge (dependency parsing in this work) to guide an attention mechanism, allowing the model to focus on salient substructures within an utterance. The authors claim three main contributions: 1) end-to-end learning of attention guided by knowledge, 2) generalization to different knowledge types, and 3) efficiency due to separate modeling of substructures.

**Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Integration of Structural Knowledge:** The paper presents a reasonably well-designed approach to incorporating structural knowledge into a neural network for slot filling. While attention mechanisms are common, the explicit guidance via dependency parsing (or other knowledge sources) offers a potentially valuable way to improve interpretability and robustness, particularly in low-resource settings.
2. **Demonstrated Performance Gains:**  The experimental results on the ATIS dataset show consistent and statistically significant improvements over strong baselines, particularly with limited training data. This suggests the method is genuinely benefiting from the knowledge integration.
3. **Flexibility in Knowledge Source:** The authors rightly point out the ability to swap in different knowledge representations (dependency parses, AMR graphs, etc.) is a strength.  The empirical validation of this with different parsers is a positive aspect.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation & Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing NLU approaches (reliance on large datasets, ignoring structural information) and positions K-SAN as a solution to these issues.
2. **Sound Methodology:** The K-SAN architecture is well-defined and logically structured.  The use of attention, guided by knowledge-derived substructures, is a sensible approach.  The separate knowledge encoding module is a good design choice.
3. **Comprehensive Experiments:** The authors perform a thorough evaluation on the ATIS dataset, varying the amount of training data and experimenting with different knowledge sources (parsers). The inclusion of statistical significance tests is commendable.
4. **Positive Results:** The consistent performance gains, especially in the low-data regimes, are a strong indication of the method's effectiveness. The ablation studies (implicit through the different knowledge sources) are helpful in understanding the impact of different knowledge representations.
5. **Well-written and Organized:** The paper is clearly written and logically organized, making it easy to follow the authors’ ideas and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Core Components:** While the *combination* of knowledge guidance and attention is valuable, the individual components (RNNs, attention mechanisms, dependency parsing) are all well-established. The paper lacks a truly novel architectural element or theoretical contribution.  The description of the attention mechanism feels relatively standard.
2. **Dependence on Parser Accuracy:** The performance is fundamentally tied to the quality of the external knowledge source (e.g., dependency parser). The paper acknowledges this, but doesn’t deeply investigate the impact of parsing errors. While showing robustness in some regards, a detailed analysis of how specific parsing errors affect performance would be beneficial. The visualization in Figure 5 highlights an instance of incorrect parsing - a deeper discussion of how the model handles such cases would be valuable.
3. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The report of hyperparameters (hidden layer sizes, dropout rates) is limited. A more detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance would strengthen the results.  Were different hyperparameters used for each knowledge source, and if so, why?
4. **Lack of Comparison to More Recent NLU Models:** The baseline comparisons include relatively older models.  A comparison to more recent state-of-the-art NLU architectures (e.g., transformer-based models like BERT or similar) would better position the contribution of K-SAN.  Even a brief discussion of why those models weren't used (e.g., computational cost, difficulty of integrating knowledge) would be helpful.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  How sensitive is the performance of K-SAN to errors in the dependency parsing?  Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, examining cases where incorrect parses lead to incorrect slot filling predictions?
2.  What strategies were used for hyperparameter tuning, and how did these vary depending on the knowledge source (e.g., different parsers)?  Could you provide a table of the hyperparameters used for each experiment?
3.  How computationally expensive is K-SAN compared to a standard RNN-based slot filler?  While you mention efficiency, a quantitative comparison (e.g., training time, inference time) would be helpful.
4.  Have you explored integrating K-SAN with more recent NLU models, such as those based on transformers? If so, what were the results? If not, what are the challenges in doing so?
5.  The paper mentions the flexibility to use different knowledge representations. Were there any knowledge sources that proved *less* effective than others, and if so, why do you hypothesize this is the case?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The reference list seems incomplete (e.g., missing details for some citations).
*   The visualization in Figure 5 is helpful, but could be improved by highlighting the *predicted* slots alongside the attention weights. This would make it easier to see how the attention mechanism is influencing the predictions.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a practical and effective approach to incorporating structural knowledge into NLU. While the individual components aren’t novel, the combination and demonstrated performance gains are valuable contributions. The weaknesses are relatively minor and could be addressed with further experimentation and analysis.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses and answering the questions above.
