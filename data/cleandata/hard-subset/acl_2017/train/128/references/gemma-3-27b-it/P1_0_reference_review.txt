## Review for "Knowledge as a Teacher: Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes Knowledge-Guided Structural Attention Networks (K-SAN) for improving natural language understanding (NLU) in goal-oriented dialogue systems, specifically focusing on the slot filling task. The core idea is to incorporate external knowledge (dependency parsing in the presented experiments) as a guiding mechanism for an attention network, allowing the model to focus on relevant substructures within a sentence. The authors claim three main contributions: 1) end-to-end learning of attention guided by knowledge; 2) generalization to different knowledge sources; and 3) efficiency and parallelizability due to the substructure-based modeling.

**Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Integration of Structural Knowledge:** The primary contribution lies in demonstrating a practical approach to integrate structural knowledge, represented through dependency parsing, directly into an attention mechanism within a neural network for slot filling. This is a compelling way to leverage linguistic structure beyond simply using it as features.
2. **Demonstrated Generalization Across Knowledge Sources:** The experiments showcasing the model's performance with both dependency trees and AMR graphs suggest a degree of flexibility and robustness in handling different knowledge representations.
3. **Empirical Results on a Standard Benchmark:** The authors provide solid experimental results on the ATIS dataset, consistently outperforming strong baselines, including those that also incorporate structural information.


**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Performance:** The consistent improvement over strong baselines on the ATIS dataset, particularly with smaller training datasets, demonstrates the effectiveness of the K-SAN approach. This suggests the method genuinely benefits from knowledge guidance, especially when data is limited.
2. **Clear and Well-Motivated Approach:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of previous approaches that treated knowledge as mere features and convincingly argues for a more integrated approach where knowledge *guides* the attention mechanism. The "knowledge as a teacher" analogy is apt.
3. **Thorough Experimentation & Ablation:** The experiments explore different knowledge encoding methods (NN, RNN, CNN) and present a joint RNN tagger for combining different information sources. The ablation studies in Table 2 regarding different knowledge sources and parsers are valuable.
4. **Attention Visualization:** The visualization of attention weights (Figure 5) provides helpful insight into how the model is leveraging the knowledge-guided structures and adds credibility to the claim that it’s focusing on relevant parts of the input.
5. **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is generally well-written, clearly structured, and easy to follow. The related work section provides a good overview of relevant research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Components:** While the *combination* of knowledge guidance and attention is well-executed, the individual components (RNNs, attention mechanisms, dependency parsing) are not novel. The novelty resides in the specific *application* and integration of these components, which, while effective, doesn't represent a breakthrough in the individual techniques themselves.
2. **Dependency on Parsing Accuracy:** The performance of K-SAN is inherently tied to the quality of the external knowledge source, in this case, the dependency parser. The paper briefly acknowledges this, but doesn’t thoroughly investigate the impact of parsing errors. The example in Figure 5 highlighting a parsing error suggests this could be a significant issue. A more detailed error analysis concerning the impact of incorrect parses would be beneficial.
3. **Limited Analysis of Attention Mechanism:** While the attention visualization is helpful, a more quantitative analysis of the attention weights would be valuable. For example, analyzing which types of substructures receive the most attention under different conditions could provide further insight into the model’s behavior.
4. **Lack of Comparison to More Recent NLU Models:** The baselines used are somewhat dated. A comparison to more recent, state-of-the-art NLU models, perhaps those utilizing transformers (e.g., BERT-based approaches for slot filling), would better contextualize the performance of K-SAN.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Parsing Error Robustness:** How sensitive is the performance of K-SAN to errors in the dependency parsing? Have you considered any methods to mitigate the impact of parsing inaccuracies (e.g., incorporating confidence scores from the parser)?
2. **Attention Weight Analysis:** Can you provide a more quantitative analysis of the attention weights? For example, what percentage of attention is typically allocated to the root node versus leaf nodes? Are there specific substructure patterns that consistently receive high attention?
3. **Computational Cost:** While you mention parallelizability, can you provide a more detailed analysis of the computational cost of K-SAN compared to standard RNN-based slot filling models, especially as the sentence length increases?
4. **Transformer-Based Comparison:**  How does K-SAN perform relative to a BERT-based slot filling model trained on the ATIS dataset?




**Additional Comments:**

*   The hyperparameter settings could be elaborated on; for instance, the motivation behind choosing a specific hidden layer size.
*   A brief discussion of potential applications beyond dialogue systems (e.g., information extraction) would broaden the paper’s impact.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a practical and effective approach to integrating knowledge into NLU models. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the combination and the demonstrated results are valuable. The weaknesses are relatively minor and could be addressed through further experimentation and analysis. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on the authors addressing the questions and weaknesses raised above.
