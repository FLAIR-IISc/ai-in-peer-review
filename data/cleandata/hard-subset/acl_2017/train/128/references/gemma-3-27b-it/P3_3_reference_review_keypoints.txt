- Proposes K-SAN: a framework that represents external linguistic knowledge as “substructures” and uses attention over them to improve NLU (domain/intents/slots).
- Shows empirical gains on ATIS using different encoders (NN/RNN/CNN) and knowledge sources (dependency trees, AMR).
- Claims the method is a general, practical way to integrate structural knowledge into deep NLU pipelines.
- Demonstrates particularly strong improvements in low-resource settings, suggesting effective knowledge leverage.
- Validates robustness across different knowledge representations (dependency vs AMR).
- Clear motivation and problem framing with critique of prior feature-engineering approaches and error propagation.
- Well-designed experiments including ablations, dataset-size variations, and strong baselines.
- Strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements and attention visualizations supporting the approach.
- Paper is well-written with sufficient implementation details to aid reproducibility.
- Attention visualizations (Figure 5) effectively illustrate model focus on relevant substructures.
- Core components (attention, RNNs, dependency parsing) are established methods; main novelty is in their combination.
- Claims of being the “first end-to-end” knowledge-guided neural approach are overstated given prior work using knowledge embeddings.
- Heavy reliance on parser quality; experiments don’t fully address robustness to parser errors.
- Lacks detailed error analysis to identify failure modes or slot/utterance types where the model struggles.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., joint RNN weight α) and tuning details are underexplored.
- Asks authors about sensitivity to parser/AMR errors and potential mitigation strategies like self-training or corrupted-structure augmentation.
- Requests a more detailed error analysis to understand remaining failure cases and useful knowledge types.
- Asks for computational overhead and inference-time comparison to baselines.
- Requests more attention-weight visualizations across varied utterance types to illustrate learned focus.
- Suggests clarifying the novelty relative to prior neural approaches using knowledge embeddings and discussing robust parsing/noisy-knowledge methods.
- Recommends discussing limitations and future directions, including richer knowledge integration like graph embeddings or reasoning.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed work with strong results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
