- Proposes DCNMT: a character-level NMT with a two-RNN word encoder (prefix RNN r_t + biRNN energies w_t) and a hierarchical resettable character decoder (HGRU) with an unfolding matrix for efficient batching.  
- Claims: removes large softmax/OOV issues, trains faster than prior char-only models, achieves competitive BLEU on WMT14 En–Fr and WMT15 En–Cs/Cs–En, and exhibits morphology-aware behavior.  
- Contribution: morphology-aware character-to-word encoder using attention-like weighting over character prefix states.  
- Contribution: resettable hierarchical character decoder with an unfolding/batching trick for efficient end-to-end training.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence of competitiveness with smaller models and qualitative morphology analyses.  
- Strength: encoder design plausibly induces morpheme-like units without explicit segmentation; PCA and energy peaks support this.  
- Strength: simple, practical hierarchical decoding and unfolding trick that appears to reduce wall-clock training time versus prior hybrids.  
- Strength: competitive BLEU results with fewer parameters and faster training (e.g., En–Fr BLEU 30.56 vs 29.70 BPE).  
- Strength: robustness to OOVs, misspellings, and nonce forms demonstrated qualitatively.  
- Weakness: word encoder weighting is under-specified and unusual (unnormalized exp weights, tanh after sum), making representations length-dependent and reproducibility unclear.  
- Weakness: insufficient baselines and ablations—no isolated tests of encoder vs decoder vs unfolding, no CNN char-encoder or single-RNN attention comparisons, and uneven coverage across language pairs.  
- Weakness: evaluation details and result presentation are messy/underspecified (beam sizes, length penalties, regularization, max lengths) and fairness is unclear.  
- Weakness: generality concerns—hierarchical decoder depends on whitespace delimiters, limiting applicability to non-whitespace languages and not discussed.  
- Weakness: analysis is mostly anecdotal; lacks quantitative morphology evaluation (morpheme boundary F1 or targeted morphologically-rich tests).  
- Questions for authors: why not normalize w_t with softmax and how does unnormalized summation affect length/gradient stability?  
- Questions for authors: what counts as delimiter, how handle non-whitespace languages, and how sensitive is the model to delimiter prediction errors?  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed efficiency metrics (tokens/sec, GPU utilization), beam/decoding settings, and regularization details to substantiate speed claims.  
- Questions for authors: supply ablations for (i) encoder with normalized attention, (ii) hierarchical decoder with simpler encoders, and (iii) decoder without resets, plus CNMT baselines for En–Cs/Cs–En.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially useful combination but current paper lacks clarity, rigorous formulation, controlled comparisons, and quantitative analysis—leaning toward rejection pending stronger experiments and clearer presentation.
