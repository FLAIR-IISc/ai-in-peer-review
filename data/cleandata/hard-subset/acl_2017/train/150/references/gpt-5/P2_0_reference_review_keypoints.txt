- Proposes DCNMT: a hierarchical char-level NMT with an intra-word weighted char-to-word encoder, a two-level decoder (word RNN + HGRU char generator), and a batching “unfolding matrix” trick.
- Shows competitive BLEU on WMT14 En–Fr and WMT15 En–Cs/Cs–En, smaller model (~54M), faster training than some prior char systems, plus qualitative analyses (misspellings, morphology).
- Key methodological contributions are the decoupled intra-word weighting mechanism and a practical batching trick for hierarchical character decoding.

Strengths:
- Addresses OOV/rare-form problems while avoiding long encoder character sequences via word-granularity encoding and character decoding.
- Competitive empirical results, including on morphologically rich Czech, with a modest model size.
- Efficiency-oriented engineering (HGRU state-setting and unfolding/broadcasting) enables practical batching and speedups.
- Convincing qualitative evidence for morphological generalization and robustness to misspellings/nonce words.
- End-to-end character decoding with attention and no external segmentation is deployment- and multilingual-friendly.

Weaknesses:
- Incremental novelty: encoder is attention-like pooling over char RNNs and decoder resembles prior hierarchical RNNs; batching trick is mainly an implementation detail.
- Evaluation fairness concerns: comparisons use numbers from heterogeneous setups, no significance tests, and stronger baselines not reimplemented under the same conditions.
- “Open-vocabulary” claim contradicted by character shortlist mapping many chars to <unk>, with no OOV character stats or impact analysis.
- Missing ablations for core design choices (unnormalized weighting vs softmax, alternative pooling, HGRU vs reset GRU, etc.).
- Decoding/search details underspecified (beam management across two levels, beam sizes, handling delimiter errors, latency/complexity).
- Clarity and reproducibility issues: typos, confusing equations, missing hyperparameters, no released code, and hard-to-parse figures/tables.

Questions for authors:
- Why use unnormalized exp(aff(h_t)) with tanh instead of standard softmax attention? Please ablate.
- What are the exact character sets per language, test-set OOV character rates, and frequency/impact of <unk> at test time?
- How is two-level beam search organized (character-level beam sizes, delimiter timing across beams, heuristics for word length)?
- Please provide ablations: (i) BiLSTM/CNN word encoder without weighting RNN, (ii) standard GRU with manual resets, (iii) DCNMT without target-side word encoder feedback.
- For efficiency claims, report tokens/sec or chars/sec, GPU model, and sensitivity to maximum character length cap.

Overall assessment:
- Sensible and practical architecture with solid qualitative evidence and competitive BLEU, but limited novelty and insufficiently rigorous empirical evaluation; currently borderline reject.
