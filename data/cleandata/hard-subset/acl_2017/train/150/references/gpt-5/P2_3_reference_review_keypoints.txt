- Proposal: DCNMT — morphology-aware char-level encoder (prefix RNNs + position-wise weights) to compose word vectors.
- Proposal: Hierarchical decoder — word-level states + HGRU character generator that resets at delimiters; unfolding matrix for efficient batching.
- Results: Competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En; generally better than a cited BPE baseline and robust to misspellings/nonce forms; qualitative morphology analyses provided.

Strengths:
- Practical engineering: HGRU and batching/unfolding substantially speed up char-level training and keep model size compact (~54M).
- Strong empirical performance: matches or approaches strong char-level baselines across language pairs without word vocabularies.
- Qualitative morphology/OOV evidence: PCA and heatmaps suggest morpheme-aware representations; examples show robustness to misspellings and coinages.
- End-to-end training: no pretraining or auxiliary supervision required, simplifying deployment.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: approach is incremental over prior hierarchical/char NMT; clearer positioning of new contributions is needed.
- Incomplete/incomparable baselines: baselines drawn from varied setups, missing a controlled BPE baseline and comparable char-CNN/BiLSTM encoder, and decoding/beam details unspecified.
- Missing ablations: no isolation of contributions from morphology-weighting, HGRU vs standard char decoder, unfolding trick, or weight-normalization choices.
- Anecdotal morphology claims: no quantitative segmentation or systematic generalization experiments to substantiate morphology learning.
- Reproducibility/clarity issues: confusing notation/equations, under-specified delimiter construction, missing decoding throughput and code.

Questions for authors:
- Clarify HGRU novelty vs prior hierarchical decoders and provide ablations comparing it to a standard char decoder.
- Precisely define delimiter/auxiliary sequence a_t during training and testing and discuss sensitivity to delimiter errors.
- Provide controlled experiments: (a) strong BPE baseline trained identically, (b) char CNN/BiLSTM encoder of similar size, (c) significance tests and decoding/beam settings.
- Add quantitative morphology evaluation (boundary F1 or derivation/inflection generalization tests).
- Report runtime and memory overhead of unfolding matrix R (chars/sec, words/sec, peak GPU memory) vs a standard char decoder.

Overall assessment:
- Originality: moderate (incremental).
- Quality: promising but needs stronger, controlled baselines and ablations; morphology evidence is qualitative.
- Clarity: mixed; main ideas clear but technical details under-specified.
- Significance: moderate — practical efficiency and competitive performance are of interest.
- Recommendation: lean borderline accept/weak accept conditional on addressing comparability, ablations, and clarity; otherwise borderline reject.
