- Proposes DCNMT: a character-level NMT with a two‑RNN word encoder (prefix RNN + position-wise “energy” weights) and a hierarchical word→character decoder using a settable‑state HGRU plus an unfolding matrix for batched training.
- Evaluated on En–Fr (WMT14/newstest2015) and En–Cs/Cs–En (WMT15); claims competitive BLEU, smaller model (~54M), faster training, and qualitative robustness to misspellings and morpheme-like segmentations.

Strengths
- Novel two‑RNN encoder with position-wise weighting that appears to induce morpheme-like boundaries and cluster related derivational forms.
- Practical hierarchical decoder (settable‑state HGRU) and unfolding matrix batching trick that enable efficient batched training of hierarchical decoders.
- Reported competitive BLEU with fewer parameters and faster convergence; early-epoch performance surpasses a BPE baseline.
- Qualitative examples showing robustness to misspellings and handling of nonce words.
- Achieves end‑to‑end character-level translation without explicit subword segmentation.

Weaknesses
- Method description is underspecified/inconsistent (unclear w_t normalization, nonstandard HGRU/decoder notation and updates), harming reproducibility.
- Evaluation lacks controlled comparisons (mixed test sets/codebases/hardware), no significance testing, and potentially weak or untuned baselines.
- Limited ablation studies — no isolation of encoder vs CNN/char‑RNN, HGRU vs standard char decoder, or the unfolding trick’s impact.
- Morphology learning claims are anecdotal—no quantitative segmentation evaluation or targeted robustness tests on morphology.
- “Open vocabulary” claim undermined by restricting characters to top 120 and mapping others to <unk>, with no quantification of impact.
- Reporting/writing issues: typos, broken tables, incomplete references, and missing hyperparameters (beam size, dropout, clipping, attention details).

Questions for authors
- Exact formula for w_t: is it softmax-normalized or tanh(aff(h_t)) in the final model, and are there constraints (e.g., non‑negativity)?
- How is the first‑level decoder updated: GRU/LSTM with attention like Bahdanau et al., or the linear Eq.(8); how is u_t computed and how is attention implemented?
- What tokens count as delimiters for the second‑level decoder; how is a_t derived during training and handled at inference/beam search?
- How is the unfolding matrix R computed with differing target lengths in batched teacher forcing; what are complexity and memory overheads?
- Please provide controlled baselines (BPE and hybrid) trained in your codebase with identical settings, plus beam sizes and decode times.
- Can you quantitatively evaluate induced segmentations against morphological analyzers and run robustness tests on morphologically rich languages?

Additional suggestions
- Add statistical significance tests, length-based BLEU breakdowns, OOV character rate reporting, release code and pretrained models, and consider extra evaluations on morphologically rich languages (e.g., Turkish, Finnish).
