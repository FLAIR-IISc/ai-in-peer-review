- Proposes DCNMT: a hierarchical character-level NMT with a word encoder (prefix RNN + scalar “energies” over characters) and a hierarchical decoder (word-level RNN + HGRU with reset and unfolding for batched training).  
- Claims to learn morpheme-like units without explicit segmentation while retaining training efficiency via settable-state GRU and unfolding.  
- Shows competitive BLEU on WMT En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En with fewer parameters and faster training than some prior character models, plus qualitative morphology and robustness examples.

- Clear architectural motivation and practical engineering (HGRU resets and unfolding matrix) that address hierarchical char-decoder training bottlenecks.  
- Competitive translation quality across morphologically poor and rich language pairs, outperforming BPE on En–Fr and matching strong character baselines.  
- Intuitive qualitative evidence of learned morpheme-like structure from energy plots and PCA projections, and illustrative robustness to misspellings/nonce words.  
- Trains end-to-end without auxiliary supervision (no layer-wise or attention supervision for characters).

- Novelty is incremental relative to prior char-aware encoders/decoders and not rigorously positioned against similar prior work (C2W, char CNN/RNN, hierarchical decoders).  
- Mathematical and algorithmic descriptions contain ambiguities/typos (weight normalization, HGRU notation, role of a_t, unfolding details), raising clarity and training/gradient concerns.  
- Experimental comparability and reporting are weak: inconsistent baselines/preprocessing, nonstandard test set choice, missing BLEU/tokenization/beam/statistical details, and incomplete character-vocabulary coverage stats.  
- Morphology and robustness claims rely on qualitative examples only; no intrinsic segmentation metrics or controlled OOV/misspelling experiments, and no comparison to BPE segmentation.  
- Missing ablations to quantify contributions of components (word encoder vs. CNN/RNN, normalized weighting, HGRU resets, target-side feedback, flat char decoder baseline).

- Why not normalize character weights w_t (softmax) and were alternatives (softmax/sigmoid/tanh) compared; sensitivity to this choice?  
- What exact character inventories were used per language, what fraction mapped to <unk>, and how does <unk> affect performance?  
- Decoding specifics: beam size at character level, handling/synchronization of word boundaries in beam search, and heuristics to control word length.  
- Training details: are gold delimiters used during training versus predicted at test time; exposure bias and scheduled sampling; batching strategy for variable word/char lengths?  
- Request ablation results for replacing encoder with CNN+highway, using normalized attention, removing target-side feedback, and a flat char decoder with similar budget.

- Overall: well-motivated and promising practical approach to character-level NMT with efficient engineering and qualitative evidence, but moderate novelty, unclear technical details, and insufficiently rigorous/comparable experiments and ablations — currently borderline accept pending clarifications and stronger experimental validation.
