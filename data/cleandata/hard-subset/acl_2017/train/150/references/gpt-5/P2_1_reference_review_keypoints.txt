- Summary: Proposes DCNMT combining a character-to-word encoder (weighted sum over char-RNN states) and a hierarchical decoder where a word-level RNN initializes a character-level GRU to generate words until a delimiter.

- Contribution 1: Practical hierarchical character decoder with a settable-state GRU and an unfolding/broadcasting batching trick to train in one pass.

- Contribution 2: Character-to-word encoder that learns weighted combinations of character states intended to capture morpheme-like structure, supported by qualitative energy plots and PCA.

- Contribution 3: Empirical results showing competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En with fewer parameters (~54M) and faster training than some prior char-only models.

- Strength: Explicitly handles open-vocabulary issues (OOVs, misspellings, nonce words) with convincing qualitative examples.

- Strength: Useful engineering trick for efficient training of hierarchical decoders that could be adopted by others.

- Strength: Competitive performance with smaller model size and moderate training time compared to some prior character models.

- Strength: Intuitive and interpretable morphology analyses (PCA and energy profiles) that align with model design.

- Weakness: Incremental novelty relative to prior character/subword encoders and hierarchical decoders; lacks rigorous comparison to strong alternatives and ablations.

- Weakness: Unclear/ambiguous word-encoder formulation (unnormalized exp then tanh) making interpretation of weights difficult and lacking sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness: Experimental comparability issues and reporting gaps (different datasets/preprocessing/baselines, missing throughput/decoding-speed metrics).

- Weakness: Limited quantitative evaluation of morphology learning (no segmentation metrics or systematic generalization tests).

- Weakness: Presentation and implementation details missing or unclear (typos, notation, figures/tables, beam settings, character set handling).

- Question: Provide ablations for (i) removing encoder weighting, (ii) CNN encoder vs RNN, (iii) normalized softmax vs current scheme, and (iv) HGRU vs standard GRU with reset.

- Question: Report tokens/sec or chars/sec, decoding speed, and the batching/unfolding speedup on the same hardware as reproduced baselines.

- Question: Clarify exact training data and preprocessing for En–Fr and whether baselines were reproduced under identical settings.

- Question: Specify character-level beam size, how word boundaries interact with beam pruning, and any beam-search instability.

- Question: Provide quantitative morphology evaluations or OOV/generalization statistics (e.g., Morfessor/gold boundaries, unseen forms).

- Overall assessment: Promising and practical hierarchical design with useful engineering contributions, but methodological novelty is modest and experimental validation lacks the controlled ablations and clarity needed for a stronger acceptance.
