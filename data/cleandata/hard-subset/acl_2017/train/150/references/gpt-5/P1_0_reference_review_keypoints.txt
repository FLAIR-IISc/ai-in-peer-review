- Proposes DCNMT: a character-centric NMT with a word encoder that composes characters via prefix RNNs and weighted-position biRNN energies.  
- Introduces a hierarchical decoder: a word-level decoder that initializes a settable-state character HGRU and uses an unfolding matrix to align word outputs with character steps for batching.  
- Empirical claim: competitive BLEU on WMT14 En–Fr and WMT15 En↔Cs, fewer parameters than some prior char models, and qualitative robustness to misspellings/nonce words.  
- Contribution (architectural): weighted-prefix char-to-word encoder and settable-state HGRU for hierarchical character decoding.  
- Contribution (empirical): competitive translation quality and qualitative evidence of morphological structure learning.  
- Contribution (engineering): practical batching strategy (unfolding matrix) enabling efficient joint training of hierarchical decoding.  
- Strength: addresses open-vocabulary/morphology issues; character decoder handles OOVs and misspellings with illustrative examples.  
- Strength: thoughtful hierarchical design and novel engineering (settable-state HGRU, unfolding) that enable batched training without two passes.  
- Strength: competitive results across both morphologically poor (En–Fr) and rich (En↔Cs) language pairs.  
- Strength: interpretability evidence (energy plots, PCA) suggesting morpheme-like structure capture.  
- Strength: clear articulation of end-to-end training and inference without special supervision or pretraining.  
- Weakness: limited novelty/positioning — encoder is similar in spirit to existing char encoders and lacks controlled comparisons to strong baselines (BiLSTM/CNN-char).  
- Weakness: morphology claims are qualitative only; no quantitative segmentation or morphology evaluation to substantiate claims.  
- Weakness: clarity/correctness issues in equations and batching description (unnormalized weights, unclear HGRU equations, confusing unfolding matrix shapes).  
- Weakness: cross-paper result comparisons may be inconsistent due to differing preprocessing, budgets, and decoding settings; efficiency claims lack thorough throughput/memory/time baselines.  
- Weakness: missing experimental details (dropout, gradient clipping, beam/length normalization), failure modes (delimiter errors), and handling of non-whitespace scripts.  
- Questions: provide controlled ablation vs last-state RNN, BiLSTM-char, and CNN-char under identical settings.  
- Questions: why use unnormalized/tanh weighting instead of softmax over character positions; include stability/ablation results.  
- Questions: clarify unfolding matrix R dimensions with worked example and fully specify HGRU equations and tensor shapes.  
- Questions: how are word-level steps determined during training/inference, how are delimiter errors prevented, and are any constraints used at decoding?  
- Questions: report speed (chars/sec), memory, beam/length-norm details, and rerun strong char-baseline under the same setup.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject — interesting and potentially useful but needs controlled comparisons, clearer formalism, and stronger quantitative evidence to be acceptable.
