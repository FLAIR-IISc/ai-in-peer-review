- Proposes DCNMT: a hierarchical character-level NMT with a novel char-to-word encoder (prefix RNN + position-wise weight RNN) and a hierarchical decoder (word-level states initialize a character HGRU) with an unfolding batching trick.
- Claimed contributions: (1) compositional character-to-word encoder using position-wise weights, (2) hierarchical settable-state HGRU decoder with efficient unfolding for batching, (3) competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En and qualitative morphology/robustness analyses.
- Strength: Matches or slightly improves strong baselines with fewer parameters and faster early-epoch learning.
- Strength: Practical engineering—settable-state HGRU + unfolding is a useful, pragmatic solution for batched character-level decoding.
- Strength: Qualitative analyses (PCA, energy peaks, misspelling/nonce examples) suggest the encoder captures morphemic patterns and robustness to noisy inputs.
- Strength: Fully character-level output avoids closed-vocabulary issues of word-softmax hybrids.
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental and overlaps substantially with prior C2W, character decoders, and hierarchical-decoder work; clearer positioning and isolating ablations are needed.
- Weakness: Technical clarity problems—key equations and definitions (weight normalization, HGRU indices/shapes, delimiter/a_t) are underspecified or inconsistent, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited and uneven—missing ablations (e.g., HGRU vs standard GRU reset), incomplete baselines across language pairs, incomparable reported numbers, and no statistical significance.
- Weakness: Morphology claims are anecdotal—PCA and a few segmentations are illustrative but lack rigorous intrinsic/extrinsic morphological evaluation.
- Weakness: “Open vocabulary” claim weakened by restricting to top 120 characters and mapping others to <unk>, limiting multilingual/noisy input coverage.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—typos, inconsistent notation, incomplete references, unclear dataset/test choices that affect comparability.
- Question: Precisely define encoder weighting—are weights normalized/softmaxed, is wt = tanh(exp(aff(ht))), and how is scaling controlled?
- Question: Clarify HGRU delimiter and a_t construction during training and decoding; can a standard GRU/LSTM with explicit state reset replicate HGRU?
- Question: Provide details on unfolding matrix R’s computational/memory overhead for long sentences and highly agglutinative languages.
- Question: Report decoding details (beam size, length/word penalties) and confirm whether baselines use identical data/preprocessing; re-run stronger BPE/char baselines under the same setup.
- Question: Please report CNMT vs DCNMT on En–Cs and Cs–En to isolate the encoder’s contribution.
- Question: Provide quantitative morphology evaluations (e.g., segmentation F1 on MorphoChallenge/SIGMORPHON) or downstream gains on rare/morphologically complex words.
