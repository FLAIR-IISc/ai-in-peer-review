- Proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT with a morpheme-aware word encoder and hierarchical decoder to address OOVs.  
- Word encoder learns word representations from character sequences using two RNNs, removing fixed vocabulary reliance.  
- Hierarchical decoder generates characters conditioned on word-level representations for efficient character-level decoding.  
- Provides empirical evidence (PCA, energy weights, examples) that the model learns meaningful morphological patterns.  
- Effectively solves OOV issues and can translate misspelled and nonce words, useful for morphologically rich languages.  
- Strong visual and quantitative demonstrations showing morphological clustering and morpheme boundary detection.  
- Demonstrates notable training efficiency versus other character-level models (1–2 epochs vs. months).  
- Limited novelty relative to subword/BPE approaches and unclear fundamental advantages over BPE.  
- Insufficient comparisons with state-of-the-art subword (BPE) models to establish superiority.  
- Lack of systematic, quantitative evaluation of morphological generalization to unseen patterns.  
- Requests a clearer metric/ systematic evaluation for morphological learning versus BPE and others.  
- Asks for examples where the model outperforms BPE, especially on agglutinative/complex morphology.  
- Seeks clarification on advantages of the RNN-based encoder over CNN-based word encoders for morphology.  
- Overall: well-motivated with strong empirical signals and efficiency gains, but needs stronger SOTA comparisons and systematic generalization analyses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
