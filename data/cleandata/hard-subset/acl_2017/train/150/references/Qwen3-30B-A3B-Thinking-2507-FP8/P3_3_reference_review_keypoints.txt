- Proposes a character-level NMT model that incorporates morphology via a novel word encoder and hierarchical decoder.  
- Main contributions: (1) word encoder learning morphemes with two RNNs, (2) hierarchical decoder generating characters from word representations, (3) empirical evidence for OOV handling and learned morphological representations.  

Strengths:
- Demonstrates the model learns meaningful morphological structure (PCA shows morphologically similar words cluster).  
- Effectively handles OOVs, misspellings, and nonce words better than word-level models.  
- Trains significantly faster than prior character-level approaches while achieving comparable BLEU scores.

Weaknesses:
- Core architecture lacks clear novelty and closely resembles prior work (e.g., Ling et al. 2015b, Lee et al. 2016).  
- Performance claims are overstated; BLEU scores underperform the best baselines and competitiveness is marginal.  
- Analysis of morphological learning is superficial and lacks deeper insights or comparison to explicit morphological methods.  
- Limited language evaluation (only English and Czech); unclear performance on morphologically rich languages.  
- Fails to compare against more recent subword/hybrid approaches, reducing relevance to current SOTA.  
- Implementation details (six RNNs, hierarchical decoder interconnections/training) are ambiguous and impede reproducibility.

Questions to authors:
- How does your word encoder specifically differ from Ling et al. (2015b)?  
- What factors explain the BLEU gap to top models and how can it be closed?  
- How would the model perform on highly agglutinative or morphologically complex languages (e.g., Turkish, Finnish)?

Overall assessment:
- Valuable demonstration that character-level NMT can learn morphology and handle real-world input errors, with notable training efficiency.  
- Paper overstates novelty and competitiveness; needs clearer differentiation from prior work, stronger comparisons, deeper morphological analysis, and clearer implementation details before publication.
