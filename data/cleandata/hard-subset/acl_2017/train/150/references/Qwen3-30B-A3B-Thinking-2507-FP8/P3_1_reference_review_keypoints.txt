- Proposes DCNMT: a character-level NMT with a two-RNN word encoder and hierarchical decoder to learn morphology and handle OOVs, claiming competitive performance.
- Strength: correctly targets the OOV limitation of word-level NMT and offers character-level modeling as a promising solution.
- Strength: introduces a practical HGRU-based hierarchical decoder and an unfolding technique for efficient batch training.
- Strength: reports BLEU scores comparable to BPE-based baselines on En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En, showing character-level competitiveness.
- Weakness: novelty is questionable—word encoder resembles Ling et al. (2015b) C2W and the decoder is similar to Luong & Manning (2016).
- Weakness: weak evidence for “learning morphology”; only a PCA visualization is provided, which is insufficient.
- Weakness: efficiency claims are misleading—model size (54M) is comparable to BPE models and training-time comparisons lack context.
- Weakness: claimed robustness to misspellings is overstated and based on cherry-picked examples without systematic evaluation.
- Weakness: fails to compare with more recent character-level approaches (e.g., Lee et al. 2016, Gu et al. 2017), weakening the “state-of-the-art” claim.
- Weakness: omits analysis of inference-time and memory costs, leaving practical deployment implications unclear.
- Question: how does the word encoder fundamentally differ from Lee et al. (2016) and Luong & Manning (2016)?
- Question: provide a systematic evaluation of handling misspellings versus word-level and other character-level models.
- Question: why does the model use six RNNs yet claim to be “the simplest and smallest”? clarify this contradiction.
- Question: provide inference-time comparisons with standard word-level models for practical assessment.
- Overall: reasonable attempt and a practical hierarchical decoder, but novelty and claimed benefits are not sufficiently evidenced.
- Recommendation: reject as submitted; would reconsider if authors provide stronger evidence of morphology learning, more rigorous comparisons, and clearer methodological distinctions.
