- Contribution: Proposes DCNMT with a word encoder that learns morphemes via two RNNs.  
- Contribution: Introduces a hierarchical decoder that generates characters from word-level representations.  
- Contribution: Claims a character-level model that addresses OOVs while achieving competitive performance with word-level models.  

- Strength: Provides PCA visualizations showing the encoder clusters morphologically related words better than a standard RNN.  
- Strength: Empirically handles OOVs and nonce/misspelled words (e.g., "convenienter") where word-level models produce <unk>.  
- Strength: Achieves competitive BLEU with much faster training than prior character-level work, making it more practical.  

- Weakness: Novelty is limited—approach resembles BPE/subword methods and BiRNN weighting is not clearly novel.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to BPE-based (state-of-the-art) models; results focus on character-level baselines.  
- Weakness: Architecture complexity (six RNNs) contradicts "simplest and smallest" claim and lacks ablation studies to justify components.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of how specific morphological rules are learned, especially for agglutinative languages.  
- Weakness: Overstates solving the OOV problem; does not address extremely rare words or proper nouns.  

- Question: Please provide a detailed comparison with BPE-based models to demonstrate superiority.  
- Question: How does your subword segmentation compare to BPE, and what concrete morphological advantages does it offer?  
- Question: Provide computational cost analysis (FLOPs, memory) versus standard word-level and BPE-based models.  
- Question: Explain and provide evidence why your method is better suited for morphologically rich languages like Czech.  

- Overall: Promising work with useful OOV/morphology results but incremental versus subword methods, lacking crucial BPE comparisons, architecture justification, and deeper morphological analysis; recommendation: reject but encourage revision with more comprehensive comparisons and analyses.
