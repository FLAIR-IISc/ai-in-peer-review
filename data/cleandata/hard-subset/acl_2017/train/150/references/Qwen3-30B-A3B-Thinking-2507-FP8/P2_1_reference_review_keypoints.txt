1. Paper proposes DCNMT, a hybrid deep character-level NMT that learns morphology via a novel word encoder and hierarchical decoder to address OOVs.  
2. Contribution: a two-RNN word encoder that learns morphemes and composes meaningful word representations.  
3. Contribution: a hierarchical decoder that efficiently generates character sequences while retaining word-level benefits.  
4. Contribution: demonstrates handling of morphological variations, misspellings, and nonce words.  
5. Strength: encoder is a novel approach to implicit morphology learning, with visualizations showing morphologically similar words cluster together.  
6. Strength: practical utility in translating misspelled and unseen words where word-level models output <unk>.  
7. Strength: competitive BLEU scores and substantially improved training efficiency versus prior pure character models.  
8. Weakness: title/claims overstate "deep character-level" since the model is fundamentally a hybrid, making comparisons to pure character models misleading.  
9. Weakness: inadequate and outdated comparisons with state-of-the-art subword/BPE approaches, making results not directly comparable to current practice.  
10. Weakness: insufficient quantitative analysis of morphological learning—limited to qualitative visuals and English examples, no cross-language or generalization tests, and no comparisons to other morphology-aware methods.  
11. Question: reconcile claim that character-level models outperform subword models with dominant subword-based SOTA (e.g., Sennrich et al., 2016).  
12. Question: provide quantitative evidence and details on how the model generalizes to unseen morphological forms.  
13. Question: clarify how the hierarchical decoder's unfolding mechanism compares in efficiency and design to Luong and Manning (2016)'s hybrid approach.  
14. Overall: interesting and practical contribution with a novel encoder and OOV handling, but misrepresented as character-level, lacks rigorous SOTA comparisons and deeper morphological analysis—borderline accept unless major clarifications and additional experiments are provided.
