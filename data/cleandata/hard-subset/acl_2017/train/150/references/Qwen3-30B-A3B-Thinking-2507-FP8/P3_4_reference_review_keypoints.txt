- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT with a novel word encoder and hierarchical decoder that learns morphology to address OOVs while maintaining competitive performance and better training efficiency.

- Contribution 1: Novel word encoder that learns morphemes by attending over character representations.

- Contribution 2: Hierarchical decoder that efficiently generates characters using word-level representations.

- Contribution 3: Empirical evidence that the model learns meaningful word representations capturing morphological relationships.

- Strength 1: Word encoder's attention-based morpheme learning is a significant technical contribution, with PCA visualizations showing morpheme-based clustering.

- Strength 2: Demonstrated substantial training efficiency gains (≈7 days vs. 90 days) while achieving comparable BLEU to prior character models.

- Strength 3: Strong empirical handling of OOVs, including misspellings and nonce words.

- Weakness 1: Lacks comparison to BPE-based subword models, making claims of superiority over subword methods misleading.

- Weakness 2: Morphological analysis is superficial and limited to a few examples, lacking systematic multi-language evaluation.

- Weakness 3: Questionable novelty given prior character-level work (e.g., Kim et al., Lee et al.); insufficient differentiation from prior methods.

- Weakness 4: Overstated claims about embedding quality versus word-level models; PCA clustering is not sufficient evidence.

- Question 1: How does DCNMT compare to BPE-based models in BLEU, efficiency, and OOV handling?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide a systematic analysis of learned morphological patterns (prefixes, suffixes, compounding) across languages?

- Question 3: How does DCNMT fare against recent SOTA NMT models (e.g., Transformer, M2M) that supersede RNNs?

- Overall assessment: Promising technical contribution with strong OOV handling and efficiency, but claims are overstated due to inadequate comparisons and limited analysis.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on major revisions addressing comparisons to BPE, deeper morphological analysis, and clearer novelty.
