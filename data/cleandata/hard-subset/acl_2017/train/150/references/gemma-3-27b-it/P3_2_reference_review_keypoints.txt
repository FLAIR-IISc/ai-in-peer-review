- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a hierarchical deep character-level NMT with two RNNs in the encoder and a hierarchical decoder to handle OOVs and morphology, showing competitive results on En-Fr, En-Cs, Cs-En.

- Contribution 1: Novel character-level word encoder using dual RNNs to explicitly model morphology and learn morpheme-like representations.

- Contribution 2: Practical, efficient hierarchical HGRU decoder with an unfolding strategy enabling batch training for character-level translation.

- Contribution 3: Empirical results across En-Fr, En-Cs, Cs-En showing performance comparable to state-of-the-art on morphologically rich languages.

- Strength 1: Clear motivation and formulation addressing word-level NMT issues (OOVs, large vocab) and character-level challenges (cost, structure).

- Strength 2: Innovative word encoder design that weights characters and shows initial validation (PCA) of morphological structure.

- Strength 3: Decoder design and matrix unfolding tackle a key efficiency bottleneck in character-level training.

- Strength 4: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple language pairs with comparisons to BPE and C2W baselines.

- Strength 5: Qualitative examples indicate improved handling of OOVs, misspellings, and nonce words.

- Weakness 1: Limited quantitative analysis of whether the model truly learns meaningful morphemes or which morphemes are identified.

- Weakness 2: Missing ablation studies and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to assess component contributions and robustness.

- Weakness 3: Comparisons lack stronger, more recent baselines (e.g., SentencePiece-based models) to contextualize the "comparable" claim.

- Weakness 4: Hierarchical decoder may introduce complexity and possible information loss at the word-level decoding step that is not analyzed.

- Weakness 5: No detailed error analysis showing where the model fails with morphological variations.

- Question 1: Provide quantitative metrics to evaluate learned morphological representations and clustering by morphological features.

- Question 2: Share ablation study results for components like the BiRNN, activation functions, and hidden unit sizes.

- Question 3: Compare performance against recent stronger NMT models using advanced subword segmentation such as SentencePiece.

- Question 4: Analyze potential information loss during word-level decoding and its impact on overall performance.

- Question 5: Provide a detailed error analysis focused on failures with morphologically complex words.

- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written and clearly organized.

- Additional comment 2: Figures and tables effectively illustrate architecture and results.

- Additional comment 3: References are current and relevant; request code for reproducibility.

- Overall: Strong, promising approach with a lean toward acceptance pending additional analyses on morphological learning, ablations, stronger baselines, and error analysis.
