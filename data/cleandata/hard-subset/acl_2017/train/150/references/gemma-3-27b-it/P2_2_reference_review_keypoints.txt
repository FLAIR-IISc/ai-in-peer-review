- Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a hierarchical architecture: a novel word encoder (two RNNs learning morphemes/combinations) and a hierarchical character-level decoder with HGRU.
- Claims competitive BLEU on En-Fr, En-Cs, and Cs-En and improved training efficiency over pure character-level models.
- Main contribution: the morphology-focused word encoder bridging character- and word-level modeling.
- Main contribution: hierarchical decoder + HGRU enabling practical, efficient character-level decoding.
- Main contribution: empirical validation on morphologically rich Czech in addition to French.
- Strength: directly addresses OOV and morphological complexity in NMT with a well-motivated approach.
- Strength: strong empirical results—competitive BLEU and favorable model size/training time compared to char baselines.
- Strength: clear, detailed architectural and implementation descriptions that aid reproducibility.
- Strength: qualitative analyses (PCA, energy weights, example translations) suggest the model learns morphological structure.
- Strength: decoding efficiency improvements and batch-handling strategy are practically valuable.
- Weakness: limited novelty—uses established components (RNNs, attention, hierarchical decoders); novelty is mostly in their arrangement and the encoder.
- Weakness: morphology learning analysis is suggestive but lacks rigorous, quantitative probing of representations.
- Weakness: comparison to subword methods (e.g., BPE) is superficial and lacks controlled, hyperparameter-matched experiments.
- Weakness: insufficient investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity (hidden sizes, learning rates, etc.).
- Question: can authors provide quantitative probes showing encoder representations encode prefixes/suffixes or other morphological features?
- Question: how does performance and compute (training/inference) compare to a state-of-the-art BPE-based NMT with comparable hyperparameters?
- Question: can authors provide more examples of successful morphological generalization and a systematic analysis of remaining morphological errors?
- Question: what is the memory footprint and scalability of the decoder unfolding (matrix R) for long target sequences?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written with comprehensive references; an ablation study would strengthen it.
- Overall assessment: solid, promising contribution focused on morphology and OOV handling; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
