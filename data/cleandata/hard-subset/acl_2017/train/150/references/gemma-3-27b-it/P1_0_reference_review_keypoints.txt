- Paper proposes DCNMT: a hierarchical character-level NMT with a morphology-aware word encoder and hierarchical character decoder, evaluated on En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En with competitive BLEU.
- Main contribution: hierarchical decoder (HGRU) that bridges word-level encoding and character-level decoding for efficient batch training.
- Main contribution: morphology-aware word encoder using coupled RNNs to model morphemes and their combinations.
- Main contribution: empirical validation includes morphologically rich Czech to test morphological modeling.
- Strength: strong experimental results showing competitive BLEU across tasks.
- Strength: directly addresses OOV and handles misspelled/nonce words effectively.
- Strength: clear, well-explained hierarchical architecture and rationale for design choices.
- Strength: qualitative visualizations (PCA, energy analysis) suggest the encoder captures morphological information.
- Strength: authors consider efficiency and demonstrate reasonable parameter counts and training time.
- Weakness: individual components (RNNs, attention, hierarchical decoders) lack novelty; novelty is mainly in their combination.
- Weakness: evidence for morphology learning is primarily qualitative; lacks quantitative probing of representations.
- Weakness: limited ablation studies—unclear contribution of each architectural component (e.g., encoder, HGRU).
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to stronger/subword baselines (e.g., BPE, WordPiece) to contextualize benefits.
- Questions: provide detailed computational cost comparison (memory, training time per epoch) with other models.
- Questions: report sensitivity to RNN hidden sizes and whether hyperparameter search was performed.
- Questions: justify the choice of tanh for normalizing encoder weights and whether alternatives were tried.
- Questions: specify metrics used to evaluate learned morpheme representations beyond visual inspection.
- Questions: provide a detailed comparison and trade-off analysis with BPE/WordPiece-based models.
- Additional comments: improve writing to reduce redundancy, clarify technical details, and discuss limitations/future work.
- Additional comment: reference list contains errors/incomplete entries.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution addressing character-level NMT and morphology; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
