- Summary: Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a morphology-aware two-RNN word encoder and hierarchical character-level decoder; shows competitive BLEU on En-Fr, En-Cs, Cs-En.
- Contribution 1: Novel hierarchical architecture combining morphology-aware word encoder with character-level decoder.
- Contribution 2: Empirical evidence (visualizations, examples) that the model learns meaningful morphological representations.
- Contribution 3: Competitive performance on multiple language pairs, including morphologically rich languages.
- Strength: Addresses OOV problem beyond simple copy/subword methods.
- Strength: Intuitive, well-motivated architecture; two-RNN word encoder is a clever way to model morphology.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments across three language pairs and comparisons with other character/subword models.
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures explaining the model.
- Strength: Demonstrated ability to handle OOV and nonce words in translation examples.
- Weakness: Individual components (RNNs, attention, hierarchical decoders) are not novel; novelty is in their arrangement.
- Weakness: Missing thorough ablation study isolating the contribution of the two-RNN word encoder.
- Weakness: Model complexity (six RNNs) raises concerns about computational cost and training efficiency.
- Weakness: Reliance on a fixed 120-character set may limit applicability to languages with larger character spaces.
- Question 1: Provide ablation comparing the two-RNN encoder to simpler encoders (e.g., embedding lookup, CNN).
- Question 2: Clarify differences between HGRU and standard GRU and rationale for HGRU design choices.
- Question 3: Report sensitivity to character set size and handling of OOV characters at the character level.
- Question 4: Quantify the morphological separability shown in Figure 3 (e.g., embedding distance metrics).
- Question 5: Provide detailed training time and memory usage comparisons across configurations and datasets.
- Additional comment: Reference list has incomplete citations that should be fixed.
- Additional comment: Add discussion of limitations and future work (alternative encoders, other seq2seq tasks).
- Additional comment: Clarify the matrix unfolding explanation in Section 3.2 with a concrete example.
- Recommendation: Solid contribution with minor revisions recommended for acceptance.
