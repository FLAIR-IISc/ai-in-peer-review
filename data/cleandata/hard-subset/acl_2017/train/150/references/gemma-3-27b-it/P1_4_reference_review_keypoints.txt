- Paper proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT with a morphology-aware word encoder and hierarchical character decoder (HGRU).
- Claims: learns morphology, handles OOVs, and achieves competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En.
- Contribution: novel combination of morphology-aware encoder and character-level decoder, introducing the HGRU for efficient decoding.
- Contribution: empirical evidence (PCA, examples) that the model captures meaningful subword/morpheme structure.
- Contribution: competitive performance while removing the need for a large vocabulary.
- Strength: directly addresses OOV problem and reduces reliance on large vocabularies.
- Strength: architecture is well-motivated, explicitly modeling subword structure rather than relying on BPE or implicit learning.
- Strength: clear exposition and thorough experiments across three language pairs including a morphologically rich one.
- Strength: claimed training efficiency despite multiple recurrent networks.
- Strength: analyses (visualizations, misspelling/nonce examples) support morphology learning and robustness.
- Weakness: individual components lack novelty; novelty mainly in their combination and need clearer emphasis on synergy.
- Weakness: HGRU benefits over standard GRU are not thoroughly quantified.
- Weakness: evidence for morphology learning is suggestive but not conclusive; needs systematic evaluation on unseen morphological variants.
- Weakness: missing ablation studies to isolate the contributions of components (morphology encoder, HGRU, etc.).
- Weakness: limited comparison to stronger baselines, especially robust BPE/word-level NMT implementations.
- Weakness: result tables and presentation need clearer formatting for easier comparison.
- Question: please provide quantitative comparison of HGRU vs standard GRU (training time, BLEU).
- Question: will you run ablation studies to isolate morphology-aware encoder and HGRU contributions?
- Question: can you show examples and quantitative rates of correct translations of unseen words composed of known morphemes?
- Question: how sensitive is performance to character vocabulary size?
- Question: are there challenges in scaling to larger datasets or more complex language pairs?
- Additional comment: discuss computational complexity versus word- and character-level baselines more clearly.
- Additional comment: fix inconsistent reference formatting.
- Recommendation: solid paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions focusing on ablations and stronger morphology evaluation.
