- Paper proposes a deep character-level NMT model that learns morphological structure via a novel two-RNN word encoder and a hierarchical character-level decoder.
- Main contribution: a novel word encoder that models character sequences and learns weights to build morphological-aware word representations.
- Main contribution: an efficient hierarchical decoder (HGRU) for character-by-character generation with an unfolding technique for batch training.
- Main contribution: empirical results on En-Fr, En-Cs, Cs-En showing competitive BLEU and some analysis suggesting morphological learning.
- Strength: addresses OOV and morphology by learning subword units rather than relying on fixed segmentation like BPE.
- Strength: architectural innovation balancing representation power and computational cost through encoder + hierarchical decoder.
- Strength: strong empirical BLEU scores with a relatively small parameter count.
- Strength: clear presentation and helpful diagram explaining the architecture.
- Strength: demonstrates practical trainability and efficiency compared to prior character-level models.
- Weakness: individual components are standard; novelty is mainly in their combination and needs clearer positioning relative to prior work.
- Weakness: morphological analysis (PCA plots) is superficial and not a rigorous demonstration of learned morphology.
- Weakness: comparisons miss stronger baselines (e.g., Unigram LM, larger character models, Transformer-based character models).
- Weakness: lacks detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and ablation studies.
- Weakness: limited error analysis — unclear failure modes and types of errors the model makes.
- Question: provide an ablation study showing impact of components (e.g., remove word encoder, replace HGRU).
- Question: how sensitive are results to character vocabulary size; were different sizes tried?
- Question: provide quantitative evidence for learned morphological information (e.g., representation similarity for shared morphemes).
- Question: how do training time and resource usage scale with dataset and model size?
- Question: what is the effect of using different RNN variants (LSTM vs GRU) in encoder/decoder?
- Additional comment: some acronyms (e.g., C2W) are not clearly defined.
- Additional comment: the unfolding explanation in the hierarchical decoder section is hard to follow and needs clearer illustration.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-written paper with innovative architecture and encouraging results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
