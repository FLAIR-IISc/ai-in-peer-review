- Paper proposes DCNMT: a hierarchical deep character-level NMT with two RNNs in the encoder and a hierarchical character-level decoder to handle OOVs and morphology while being trainable and efficient.
- Main contribution: hierarchical architecture combining word- and character-level decoding to reduce computational cost while retaining character-level benefits.
- Main contribution: morphology-aware dual-RNN word encoder that learns morphemes and weights character significance.
- Main contribution: demonstration of end-to-end training of a relatively deep character-level model (six RNNs) without complex pre-training.
- Strength: Addresses the OOV problem and motivates moving beyond word/subword approaches.
- Strength: Novel hierarchical decoder and HGRU unit design, intuitively initializing character decoder from word-level outputs.
- Strength: Empirical validation across En-Fr, En-Cs, Cs-En shows competitive BLEU scores versus other approaches.
- Strength: Evidence (visualizations) that the model learns morphological representations supporting the encoder design.
- Strength: Efficient training achieved without layer-wise pre-training or supervised attention.
- Weakness: Individual components are not novel; novelty mainly in their combination, which should be better emphasized.
- Weakness: Comparisons to baselines are insufficiently direct or up-to-date; stronger BPE/modern baselines and similarly-sized character models are needed.
- Weakness: Limited error analysis—insufficient insight into failure cases for OOVs and morphological variants.
- Weakness: Lack of detailed hyperparameter/ablation study (e.g., activation choice impact) reducing confidence in robustness.
- Weakness: HGRU and decoder "unfolding" explanations are hard to follow and need clearer, possibly visual, exposition.
- Question: Provide direct comparison to a state-of-the-art subword (BPE) NMT with comparable parameter count.
- Question: Provide detailed error analysis with failure examples for OOV and morphologically complex words and reasons.
- Question: Rationale for using tanh for weight normalization in word encoder and results with other activations (sigmoid, ReLU)?
- Question: Elaborate on computational cost of the decoder "unfolding" and its impact on training/inference time.
- Question: Explain effects of different RNN layer counts and why two layers were chosen for sentence encoder and hierarchical decoder.
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and overall clear despite model complexity.
- Additional comment: References are comprehensive and current.
- Additional comment: Figures help understand architecture and results.
- Overall judgment: Solid contribution with encouraging results; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
