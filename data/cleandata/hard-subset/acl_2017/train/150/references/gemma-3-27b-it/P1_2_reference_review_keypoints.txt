- Proposal: Deep character-level NMT with a morphology-aware word encoder (two RNNs: preceding characters + weighting) and a hierarchical character-level decoder.
- Claim: Model is competitive with state-of-the-art on En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En while being efficient and learning morphological structure.
- Contribution 1: Hierarchical character-level decoding (HGRU) improves decoding efficiency versus prior character-level methods.
- Contribution 2: Morphology-aware word encoder uses a weighted character-RNN to explicitly model morphology and improve generalization to unseen words.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates feasibility of training a relatively deep (6 RNNs) end-to-end character-level NMT model.
- Strength: Addresses OOV problem via principled character-level modeling targeted at morphological richness.
- Strength: Strong empirical results and improvements on morphologically rich language pairs with relatively modest parameter count.
- Strength: Efficient decoder design with clear explanation of unfolding and batch training advantages.
- Strength: Qualitative analyses (figures) suggest the model captures morphological patterns; character-weight visualizations are insightful.
- Weakness: Core components (RNNs, attention, hierarchical decoding) are not fundamentally novel; novelty is mainly in their combination.
- Weakness: Paper lacks ablation studies to isolate contributions of the weighted character encoder and HGRU versus simpler alternatives.
- Weakness: Comparisons to baselines are limited; missing evaluations against more recent subword methods (e.g., WordPiece) and simple character-only baselines.
- Weakness: Qualitative morphology analysis is subjective; more rigorous quantitative metrics for morphological awareness are needed.
- Question: Provide ablation studies to quantify the individual impact of the weighted character RNN and HGRU.
- Question: Compare the model to recent subword segmentation techniques like WordPiece.
- Question: Compare against a standard character-level NMT model without the morphology-aware encoder.
- Question: Propose and implement quantitative metrics to evaluate morphological awareness.
- Question: Report sensitivity of results to hyperparameters, especially RNN hidden state dimensionality.
- Additional: Writing needs minor grammatical/clarity edits; figures could use clearer labeling and more examples.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-motivated contribution with promising results—recommend accept with minor revisions contingent on ablations and stronger baseline comparisons.
