- Proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT with a morphology-aware word encoder (two RNNs) and a hierarchical character-level decoder.  
- Claims main contributions: automatic subword/unit learning, efficient hierarchical character decoder, comparable BLEU with fewer epochs.  
- Strength: Strong experimental results — higher BLEU than BPE models after one epoch.  
- Strength: Qualitative analysis shows learned word representations cluster morphologically related words.  
- Strength: Model handles misspellings and nonce words, addressing a limitation of word-based models.  
- Strength: Thorough baseline comparisons; hierarchical decoder improves training efficiency over prior character-level methods.  
- Weakness: Claim of "radically" avoiding large vocabulary is misleading — still uses a 120-character vocabulary.  
- Weakness: Morphology clustering evidence is shown but not directly tied to improved translation quality beyond BLEU.  
- Weakness: Computational cost of using six RNNs is not adequately analyzed or compared to simpler alternatives.  
- Weakness: Comparison with Luong & Manning (2016) is problematic due to missing training-condition details that could explain training-time gaps.  
- Weakness: Novelty is limited—similar ideas exist in Ling et al. (2015) and Lee et al. (2016); differences from other character-level methods need clearer definition.  
- Recommendation: Clarify distinctions from prior work, better justify efficiency/novelty claims, and provide more detailed complexity and ablation analyses.
