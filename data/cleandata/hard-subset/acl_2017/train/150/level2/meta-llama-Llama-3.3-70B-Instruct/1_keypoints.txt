- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a hierarchical deep character-level NMT with two recurrent networks (six RNNs total) that learns morpheme representations and achieves competitive English↔French/Czech translation performance.
- Contribution: Novel architecture that learns morphology via two recurrent networks and a hierarchical character-level decoder.
- Contribution: Efficiently trains a deep character-level NMT model, outperforming prior character-level approaches.
- Contribution: Learns morpheme representations and combination rules, enabling translation of misspelled or nonce words.
- Strength: Achieves high translation performance comparable to state-of-the-art NMT models.
- Strength: Demonstrates efficient training for deep character-level models, improving over previous character-level work.
- Strength: Can translate misspelled or nonce words, an advantage over word-level models.
- Weakness: Model complexity—uses six RNNs, which may complicate training and hyperparameter tuning.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation on only three language pairs, raising questions about generality.
- Weakness: Comparisons are limited to a small set of baselines, leaving superiority over other state-of-the-art models unclear.
- Question: How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary words not seen during training?
- Question: Can the model be applied to other NLP tasks (e.g., speech recognition, text summarization)?
- Question: How does the model compare to other state-of-the-art NMT models in translation quality and computational efficiency?
