- Morphology-aware encoder: two RNNs compute prefix-based character representations and position-wise weights aggregated into word vectors to learn morpheme-like units.
- Hierarchical decoder: a word-level decoder produces states and an HGRU generates characters per word with resets; an “unfolding” batching trick speeds training.
- Empirical claims: competitive BLEU on WMT En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En; stronger early-epoch BLEU vs BPE; qualitative morphology learning and robustness to misspellings/nonce forms.

- Strength: Well-motivated architecture balancing word-level encoder steps with character-level decoding to address large-vocab softmax, OOVs, and preserve attention quality.
- Strength: Competitive BLEU and appealing training dynamics, notably faster early-epoch gains useful for resource/time-constrained training.
- Strength: Decoder efficiency contributions (HGRU with settable state and unfolding/batching) reduce passes and parameter count compared to some prior systems.
- Strength: Qualitative analyses (energy profiles, PCA separation) suggest the encoder captures morpheme structure and exhibits robustness to misspellings.
- Strength: End-to-end single-stage training without explicit segmentation supervision increases accessibility.

- Weakness: Novelty unclear—hierarchical word→character decoding and per-word resets exist in prior work; HGRU/unfolding impact not isolated against strong baselines.
- Weakness: Insufficient encoder ablations—no comparisons to CNN encoders or biRNN+attention and no tests of normalization vs. unnormalized weighting.
- Weakness: Morphology evidence is mostly qualitative—lacks quantitative segmentation/probing or controlled robustness evaluations.
- Weakness: Efficiency and BLEU comparability issues—numbers drawn from heterogeneous sources without controlled throughput, preprocessing, or decoding settings.
- Weakness: Reproducibility gaps—missing training hyperparameters, attention/normalization details, handling of rare characters, and no code availability indicated.

- Question: Precisely specify how HGRU differs from prior hierarchical decoders and provide controlled ablations for settable state vs simple reset and unfolding vs naive broadcasting.
- Question: Justify unnormalized exp(aff(h_t)) weights followed by tanh; report if softmax-normalized weights or attention variants were tried and their effects on BLEU/speed.
- Question: Provide BLEU evaluation settings (beam size, length penalty, tokenization/detokenization, case sensitivity, and scoring tool).
- Question: Report controlled throughput (chars/sec, updates/sec) and wall-clock training time for DCNMT and a reimplemented prior hierarchical baseline on the same hardware.
- Question: State what fraction of characters are mapped to <unk> for Czech/French and how sensitive results are to this mapping.

- Essential suggestion: Add targeted ablations for the encoder (normalization, remove weighting network, CNN/biRNN+attention baselines) and decoder (HGRU vs standard GRU/LSTM), reporting BLEU and training speed.
- Essential suggestion: Provide full evaluation details and standardized BLEU computation plus throughput and memory usage to substantiate efficiency claims.
