- Proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT with a learned character-compositional word encoder and a hierarchical decoder (HGRU) plus an efficient batching trick, claiming competitive BLEU and faster training without subword segmentation.
- Contribution: morphology-aware word encoder using two RNNs to compute prefix representations and per-position energies combined into a word vector.
- Contribution: hierarchical character decoder with settable-state GRU and an unfolding matrix to efficiently condition character generation on word-level states in batch training.
- Strength: architectural novelty and practicality—cleanly decouples prefix representation and positional weighting; HGRU and unfolding enable end-to-end char generation with word-level conditioning.
- Strength: competitive results and training efficiency—comparable BLEU on En–Fr/En–Cs/Cs–En with fewer parameters and faster convergence (1-epoch and final scores reported).
- Strength: qualitative evidence of morphology learning and robustness—PCA clustering, energy visualizations, automatic segmentation, and misspelling/nonce-word examples.
- Strength: reduces dependency on large target vocabularies by using a small character set, attractive for open-vocabulary MT and morphologically rich languages.
- Weakness: limited ablations and under-justified design choices—no isolation of each component’s contribution or comparisons to attention/normalization choices.
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons—baselines differ in settings/training budgets; lack of normalized throughput metrics, stronger BPE variants, and statistical significance.
- Weakness: open-vocabulary claim weakened by pruning to 120 most frequent characters and mapping others to <unk>, with no analysis of impact.
- Weakness: incomplete inference/search and reproducibility details—beam sizes, delimiter handling, memory/speed metrics, and code/pseudocode for HGRU/unfolding missing.
- Weakness: linguistic analysis is qualitative and English-focused; no evaluation against morphological analyzers or on Czech where gains matter most.
- Question: Why not normalize positional weights (softmax) instead of using unnormalized exp-based weights and an outer tanh—any comparisons?
- Question: Please provide ablations: (a) replace morphology-aware encoder with conventional char-to-word CNN/RNN, (b) replace HGRU with standard char decoder conditioned on word state—BLEU and speed?
- Question: What are the exact 120 characters for En/Cs, how often does <unk> occur, and what is the BLEU impact of including all observed Unicode letters?
- Question: Decoding/search details—what character-level beam sizes, word-level beams, and how prevent overly long word generation before delimiters?
- Question: Efficiency metrics—report tokens/sec (source/target chars), wall-clock per epoch, memory usage, and comparisons to matched char2char baselines on the same hardware.
- Question: Can robustness to misspellings/nonce words be quantified (e.g., perturbation study) rather than anecdotal examples?
- Comment: Table 1 formatting is dense; separate 1-epoch vs final rows per model for readability.
- Comment: Provide more details on regularization (dropout, gradient clipping, layer norm), optimization schedules, and beam/length-penalty settings.
- Comment: Add a small error analysis on En–Cs to quantify morphology-related improvements and errors.
