Summary:
- Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a two-RNN morphology-aware word encoder and a hierarchical word→character HGRU decoder to learn morphology explicitly.
- Aims to remove large target vocabularies, improve training efficiency over word/BPE and fully character models, and handle misspellings/nonce words; reports competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En.

Main contributions:
- Hierarchical character decoder (word→character) using an HGRU that conditions character generation on word-level states to improve trainability/efficiency.
- Morphology-aware word encoder that aggregates character RNN states with learned position-dependent weights to induce subword/morpheme-like composition without BPE/CNN.
- Empirical claim: faster training to competitive BLEU and better handling of misspellings/nonce words than word-level/BPE systems.

Strengths:
- Addresses large softmax/vocabulary problem with a principled hierarchical character design that keeps encoder lengths manageable.
- Clear architectural description with equations and end-to-end training without layer-wise hacks.
- Quantitative results showing competitive BLEU with smaller models and fewer epochs; qualitative robustness to misspellings and morphological generalization.
- Insightful morphology analyses (PCA clusters, energy patterns, subword boundary cues) supporting model design goals.

Weaknesses:
- Baselines (especially BPE) are inconsistently sourced and may not be fairly matched; no in-house strong BPE baseline or significance testing.
- Efficiency claims use wall-clock comparisons across different hardware/papers; tokens/sec and per-epoch timing on the same GPU are missing.
- “Open vocabulary” is limited by clipping to 120 most frequent characters and mapping others to <unk>; no exploration of byte-level or larger char sets.
- Evaluation is narrow (only BLEU), lacks human evaluation and quantitative morphology/segmentation metrics.
- Missing ablation studies isolating the weighted encoder and HGRU versus standard alternatives (GRU/LSTM).
- Some implementation details (beam size, dropout, delimiter handling) are under-specified and code is not provided, hindering reproducibility.

Questions for authors:
- Can you provide a reimplemented, strong BPE baseline under identical tokenization, training budget, and beam, with significance tests?
- What is the character-level beam size and how does search depth affect translation quality and latency?
- How sensitive are results to the 120-character limit; have you tried byte-level decoding or larger character sets?
- Can you supply ablations isolating the weighted word encoder and HGRU versus standard GRU/LSTM and a plain char encoder?
- Do you have quantitative morphology metrics (e.g., morpheme boundary F1) on a labeled corpus to back the qualitative analyses?
