- Summary: Proposes a deep, fully character-level NMT with a novel encoder that weights character-prefix representations to model morphology and a hierarchical two-level decoder that generates words as sequences of characters; trained end-to-end with an efficient batching trick and evaluated on En–Fr and En–Cs/Cs–En.  
- Summary: Claims competitive BLEU, better parameter efficiency, and much faster convergence than prior character/subword models, with qualitative analyses showing learned morphological structure.

- Contribution: Novel character-level word encoder that dynamically weights character-prefix representations to capture morphemes.  
- Contribution: Efficient hierarchical decoder combining word-level hidden-state guidance with character-level generation and an unfolding matrix for batch training.  
- Contribution: Strong empirical results on morphologically poor and rich pairs, fast convergence (notably strong after one epoch), and competitive final BLEU.

- Strength: Well-motivated, novel architecture—weighted prefix encoder and hierarchical decoder are intuitive and supported by qualitative visualizations.  
- Strength: Impressive training efficiency and competitive performance, outperforming a strong BPE baseline after one epoch.  
- Strength: Strong qualitative analyses (PCA, weight heatmaps, nonce/misspelling handling) that support claims about learning morphology.

- Weakness: Key formulations are ambiguous or contain apparent typos, hindering reproducibility (unclear use of exp vs. tanh in weight computation; typo in HGRU Equation (2)).  
- Weakness: Unsubstantiated dismissal of CNN-based character encoders—no empirical comparison or sufficient justification that CNNs cannot learn meaningful subword features.  
- Weakness: Description of the unfolding matrix R for batching is purely textual; a small numeric example is needed for clarity.

- Question: Please provide the exact mathematical formula for the final word representation (how w_t and r_t are combined and where tanh is applied).  
- Question: Please clarify or correct the HGRU update (Equation (2)) that appears to contain the same term on both sides.  
- Question: Please report approximate training speed (e.g., source words/sec) for your model and the bpe2bpe baseline on the same hardware to contextualize computational cost.
