- Proposes DCNMT: a novel deep character-level NMT using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to learn morphology.
- Contribution: a word encoder that learns morpheme representations and composition rules, yielding explicit distributed word representations.
- Contribution: a hierarchical decoder that generates character sequences efficiently until a delimiter.
- Contribution: a deep character-level NMT model achieving competitive translation performance with state-of-the-art models.
- Strength: novel architecture potentially addresses OOV issues by learning morphology via two RNNs and a hierarchical decoder.
- Strength: model can be trained end-to-end efficiently, avoiding layer-wise training and extra attention supervision.
- Strength: experimental results show competitive performance against word-level and character-level baselines.
- Weakness: model complexity—six recurrent networks—may be computationally expensive and resource-intensive to train.
- Weakness: limited analysis of how well the model actually learns morphology and how that affects translation quality.
- Weakness: insufficient comparisons with other state-of-the-art character-level models.
- Question: please provide more details on training process and hyperparameters used for DCNMT.
- Question: how will the model handle rare words and out-of-vocabulary tokens in practice?
- Question: can you provide deeper analysis of the word encoder’s effectiveness in learning morphology and its impact on translation?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written and clear but has minor formatting and referencing issues to fix.
