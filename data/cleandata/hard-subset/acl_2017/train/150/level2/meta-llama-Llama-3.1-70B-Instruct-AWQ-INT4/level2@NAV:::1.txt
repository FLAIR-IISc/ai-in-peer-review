**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model consists of six recurrent neural networks and is able to learn the representation of morphemes and the rules of how they are combined. The authors evaluate their model on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks and show that it achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for one epoch.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The authors propose a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.
2. **Learning Morphology**: The model is able to learn the representation of morphemes and the rules of how they are combined, which is a significant improvement over existing word-level and character-level models.
3. **Efficient Training**: The authors show that their model is efficient to train and achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for one epoch.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The authors demonstrate that their model achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for one epoch, which is a significant improvement.
2. **Learning Morphology**: The model is able to learn the representation of morphemes and the rules of how they are combined, which is a significant improvement over existing word-level and character-level models.
3. **Efficient Training**: The authors show that their model is efficient to train, which is an important consideration for large-scale machine translation tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The authors' model consists of six recurrent neural networks, which may be more complex and difficult to train than existing models.
2. **Limited Evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their model on three language pairs, which may not be representative of all language pairs.
3. **Comparison to State-of-the-Art**: The authors do not compare their model to the state-of-the-art models in the field, which makes it difficult to evaluate the significance of their contributions.
