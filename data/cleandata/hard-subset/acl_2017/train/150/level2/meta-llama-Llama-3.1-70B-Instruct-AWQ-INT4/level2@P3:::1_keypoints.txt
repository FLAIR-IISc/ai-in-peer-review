- Proposes Deep Character-Level NMT (DCNMT): a novel architecture with two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder that translates at the character level.  
- DCNMT comprises six recurrent neural networks designed to learn word morphology and avoid large-vocabulary issues.  
- Claims more efficient training and higher performance than word-level models, and comparable performance to state-of-the-art character-based models.  
- Demonstrates that learning morphology speeds up learning and improves translation quality.  
- Strength: improved translation performance over word-level baselines.  
- Strength: faster or more efficient training relative to some baselines.  
- Strength: effective morphological learning at character-level.  
- Weakness: model complexity—six RNNs increase training difficulty and implementation complexity.  
- Weakness: high computational resource requirements that may limit usability.  
- Weakness: limited analysis and ablation studies, making it hard to fully assess strengths and failure modes.  
- Questions: how OOV words are handled and what trade-offs the character-level approach entails.  
- Questions: whether the architecture generalizes to other NLP tasks (e.g., summarization, sentiment).  
- Questions: how DCNMT compares in detail to other state-of-the-art NMT models and the merits of the hierarchical decoder.  
- Overall: promising architecture that improves character-level NMT but requires further analysis and evaluation to validate practicality and advantages.
