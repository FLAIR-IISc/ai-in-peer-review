- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a six-RNN architecture with a hierarchical decoder that learns character-level morphology to avoid large vocabularies and improve training efficiency.
- Contribution: Introduces a novel character-level NMT architecture that explicitly learns morphology.
- Contribution: Proposes a hierarchical decoder to enable more efficient training of character-level models.
- Contribution: Demonstrates the model’s ability to learn morphological patterns.
- Strength: Shows higher BLEU than a BPE baseline after one epoch on En→Fr and En→Cs.
- Strength: Claims more efficient training than word-based and conventional character-based models.
- Strength: Can translate out-of-vocabulary, misspelled, and nonce words.
- Weakness: Model complexity—six RNNs may be too complex and hard to train for some users.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation—only tested on three language pairs, raising generalizability concerns.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to the latest state-of-the-art NMT models.
- Question: How does the model handle languages with complex morphology (e.g., Arabic, Russian)?
- Question: Please provide more training details, including hyperparameters and optimization algorithm.
- Question: How does the model perform on additional language pairs such as En→De or En→Es?
- Additional comment: Promising contribution but needs more training details and broader evaluation to demonstrate generalizability.
