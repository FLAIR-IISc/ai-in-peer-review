- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT with two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder (six RNNs) that learns morphemes; evaluated on En→Fr, En→Cs, Cs→En and outperforms BPE after one epoch.

- Contribution 1: Novel architecture for deep character-level NMT using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.

- Contribution 2: Model learns morpheme representations and composition rules, improving over prior word/character-level models.

- Contribution 3: Claims efficient training, achieving higher BLEU than BPE after one epoch.

- Strength 1: Demonstrated improved BLEU score versus a BPE baseline after short training.

- Strength 2: Empirical evidence that the model captures morphological structure.

- Strength 3: Training efficiency highlighted as suitable for large-scale MT.

- Weakness 1: Model complexity—composed of six RNNs—may be harder to train and more resource-intensive.

- Weakness 2: Limited evaluation—only three language pairs, possibly not representative.

- Weakness 3: No comparison to state-of-the-art models, making impact unclear.
