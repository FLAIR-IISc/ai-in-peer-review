**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for neural machine translation (NMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder, which translates at the character level. The proposed model, called Deep Character-Level Neural Machine Translation (DCNMT), consists of six recurrent neural networks and is able to learn the morphology of words, avoiding the large vocabulary issue and improving training efficiency.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The paper proposes a novel architecture for NMT that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder, which translates at the character level.
2. **Efficient Training**: The proposed model is able to train efficiently, achieving higher performance than word-level models and comparable performance to state-of-the-art character-based models.
3. **Learning Morphology**: The paper demonstrates that the proposed model is able to learn morphology, which can speed up learning and improve translation performance.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The proposed model achieves higher performance than word-level models and comparable performance to state-of-the-art character-based models.
2. **Efficient Training**: The proposed model is able to train efficiently, which can reduce the training time and improve the overall performance.
3. **Learning Morphology**: The paper demonstrates that the proposed model is able to learn morphology, which can speed up learning and improve translation performance.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The proposed model consists of six recurrent neural networks, which can make it more complex and difficult to train.
2. **Computational Resources**: The proposed model requires significant computational resources, which can be a limitation for some users.
3. **Limited Analysis**: The paper provides limited analysis of the proposed model, which can make it difficult to understand the strengths and weaknesses of the model.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the proposed model handle out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words, and what are the advantages and disadvantages of using a character-level model for NMT?
2. Can the proposed model be applied to other natural language processing tasks, such as text summarization or sentiment analysis?
3. How does the proposed model compare to other state-of-the-art NMT models, and what are the advantages and disadvantages of using a hierarchical decoder?
