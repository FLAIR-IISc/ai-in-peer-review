- Addresses large-vocabulary and OOV problems by using a character-level approach that avoids explicit vocabulary construction.
- Proposes a novel architecture combining a morphology-learning word encoder (two RNNs) with a hierarchical decoder.
- Demonstrates competitive BLEU scores, especially on morphologically rich languages, with a smaller model size.
- Provides evidence of morphology learning via PCA visualization and character-energy analysis.
- Shows efficient training of a deep model (six RNNs) with favorable training-time comparisons.

- Comparisons are limited; lacks thorough evaluation against recent subword methods (e.g., BPE with larger vocabularies) and similar-sized character models.
- Morphological analysis is shallow: PCA is illustrative but lacks quantitative metrics and systematic tests of morphological variation handling.
- Description of the hierarchical decoder (HGRU and matrix unfolding) is dense and hard to follow; needs clearer, more intuitive exposition.
- No discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (hidden size, embedding dim, learning rate) to assess robustness.
- Missing ablation studies to quantify the individual contributions of the morphology encoder and hierarchical decoder.

- Request detailed comparisons with subword models on BLEU, model size, and training time.
- Ask for quantitative metrics beyond PCA to demonstrate morpheme separation in learned representations.
- Request explanation for choosing tanh for normalization in the word encoder and why it outperforms alternatives.
- Ask for analysis of the model’s sensitivity to key hyperparameters.
- Request information on the computational cost of matrix unfolding versus other batching approaches for character-level models.

- Reference list is incomplete; some citations are missing.
- Paper would benefit from a clear diagram showing the full architecture and data flow.
- Releasing code would improve reproducibility.
