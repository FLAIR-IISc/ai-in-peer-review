- Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a morphology-aware word encoder (two RNNs) and a hierarchical character decoder, claiming faster convergence and comparable SOTA BLEU on En-Fr and En-Cs.

Contributions:
- Novel hierarchical character-level architecture combining a word encoder that learns morphology with a hierarchical decoder to bridge word- and character-level models.
- Word encoder uses two recurrent networks (preceding-character encoder and weighting RNN) to explicitly model morphological structure.
- Demonstrates feasible training of a relatively deep character model (six recurrent networks), addressing prior training difficulties.

Strengths:
- Architecture is novel and directly addresses vocabulary and morphology limitations of existing character-level approaches.
- Empirical BLEU results are encouraging, including outperforming a BPE baseline after one epoch.
- PCA visualization and qualitative examples provide evidence the model learns morphological patterns.
- Intrinsically handles OOV words by operating at the character level, removing explicit OOV handling.
- Authors claim reasonable training efficiency despite model depth.

Weaknesses:
- Comparisons are limited; lacks thorough evaluation against strong character/subword baselines and the Luong & Manning hybrid under identical settings.
- Missing discussion and analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rate, hidden sizes, etc.).
- No ablation studies to identify which components drive performance gains.
- PCA evidence is suggestive but not quantitative; needs stronger morphological evaluation metrics.
- Hierarchical decoder and HGRU explanation is convoluted and needs clearer, simpler description/diagram.

Questions to authors:
- Please provide detailed comparisons with strong character-level and subword baselines (including Luong & Manning) using identical datasets and settings.
- Which hyperparameters were critical, and how sensitive is performance to their values?
- Have you run ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution?
- Can you provide quantitative evidence of morphological learning beyond PCA (e.g., morpheme weight analysis or segmentation tasks)?
- Please clarify and simplify the HGRU/hierarchical decoder description and include a simpler diagram.

Additional comments:
- Paper organization is generally good but some sections (e.g., 3.2) need clearer, more concise prose.
- Related work discussion should be expanded to cover recent advances in character-level NMT and subword modeling.
- References are complete.
