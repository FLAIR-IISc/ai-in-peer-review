- Paper proposes a novel deep character-level NMT architecture that leverages morphology via two encoder RNNs and a hierarchical decoder for character generation.  
- Primary contribution: the architecture aims to avoid large vocabulary/OOV issues while preserving character-level benefits and improving training efficiency.  
- Secondary contribution: analysis showing the model learns morphological features (PCA visualization).  
- Strength: well-motivated approach addressing vocabulary size and OOV problems in NMT.  
- Strength: promising experimental results on English–French and English–Czech, including outperforming a BPE model after one epoch.  
- Strength: PCA-based analysis convincingly demonstrates learned morphology; model has relatively small parameter count and efficiency.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning, search space, and justification for RNN dimensions and layer counts.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough ablation studies to isolate component contributions.  
- Weakness: comparisons are limited—needs direct quantitative comparisons to strong/state-of-the-art baselines beyond the BPE model.  
- Weakness: explanation of the HGRU and claimed efficiency gains is underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of how language morphological richness (beyond Czech) affects performance.  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed hyperparameter tuning breakdown and rationale.  
- Questions for authors: compare performance to recent strong character-level baselines (e.g., subword regularization, attention variants).  
- Questions for authors: clarify computational cost of the hierarchical decoder’s unfolding step and its impact on training speed versus other character-level methods.
