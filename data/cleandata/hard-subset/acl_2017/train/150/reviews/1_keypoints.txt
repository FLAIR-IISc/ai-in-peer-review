- Novel encoder-decoder MT that starts/ends with characters but uses morpheme/character representations in between.  
- Authors release code and final models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, aiding reproducibility.  
- System produces reasonable translation quality even after the first training epoch and improves with more epochs.  
- Learns plausible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words by backing off to morphemes.  
- Paper fails to state which WMT test/dev sets are used (README indicates newstest2015/test and newstest2013/dev); this should be explicit.  
- Software README lacks clear runnable examples and the tool does not support a --help flag.  
- Figure 2/caption is unclear: paper describes a 6-level architecture but the figure appears to show fewer layers; a full architecture figure (even in appendix) is needed.  
- Comparisons omit state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and do not acknowledge higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org results; this should be discussed.  
- Minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reversed quotation marks) need correction.  
- Overall, a valuable contribution to the character-based neural MT literature.
