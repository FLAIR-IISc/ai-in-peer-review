- Strength: Paper is well structured, clear, presents original ideas and interesting results.
- Weakness: Doubts about result interpretation and unsupported claims about learning morphology.
- Contribution: Proposes a deep word-encoding/decoding layer to combine character-level NMT benefits with sub-word representations.
- Claim: Authors assert DCNMT learns morphology better than state-of-the-art approaches.
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences with bpe2char are very small; no statistical significance testing reported.
- Training-time comparison incomplete: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs→En; En→Cs training time not reported and En→Fr bpe2char not evaluated.
- Recommendation: More complete comparison with bpe2char needed to demonstrate advantages of DCNMT.
- Morphology analysis (Section 5.2) is anecdotal with examples only—no formal experiments to support claims; suggest moving to appendix or softening claims.
- Suggestion: Extend the morphology investigation into formal experiments (could be a separate paper).
- Writing: Many acronyms are undefined or defined after use (e.g., LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE).
- Clarification needed: Reintroduce and explain the concept of “energy” in Section 5.2 and describe what peaks mean in Figure 5.
- Style: Inconsistent capitalization of BPE.
- Minor content: Unclear if noting “no monolingual corpus is used” is necessary in Section 4.1.
- Figure issue: Figure 4a’s energy color mapping appears incorrect/missing for some characters.
- Table 1 issue: Cs→En results for model (3) (Chung et al. 2016) were not reported in the cited paper—authors should state if they computed them.
- Terminology: Avoid calling French “morphologically poor”; rephrase more carefully.
- Documentation: Provide a link for WMT’14 training corpora and fix several incomplete references.
- Typos: Multiple grammatical/typo errors need correction (examples given by reviewer).
