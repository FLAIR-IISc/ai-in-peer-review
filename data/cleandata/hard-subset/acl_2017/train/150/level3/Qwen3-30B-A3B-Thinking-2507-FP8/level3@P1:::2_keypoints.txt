- Contributions: proposes DCNMT with (1) a two-RNN word encoder learning morphemes and combos, (2) a hierarchical character-level decoder (HGRU) for efficient generation, (3) empirical claims that the model learns meaningful morphological representations.

- Strength 1: Well-structured character-level NMT that avoids large vocabulary and shows competitive BLEU with faster training (e.g., En-Fr 28.13 vs. 29.31 after one epoch).

- Strength 2: Novel architecture combining character processing with automatic morphological learning, avoiding external segmentation like BPE and enabling efficient character generation.

- Strength 3: Qualitative morphology analysis (PCA visualizations) suggesting clustering of morphologically similar words in representation space.

- Weakness 1: Claim of "learning morphology" is insufficiently supported; evidence is anecdotal and lacks statistical/generalization tests for unseen forms.

- Weakness 2: Comparison with BPE-based models is incomplete/misleading (missing En-Fr BPE2char results; Cs-En training-time comparison contradicts efficiency claims).

- Weakness 3: BLEU improvements over BPE are marginal (0.5–1.0) with no statistical significance testing reported.

- Weakness 4: Several technical details are unclear/inconsistent (undefined "energy", incorrect figure color mapping, inconsistent BPE capitalization, inaccurate characterization of French morphology).

- Weakness 5: Multiple grammatical errors, typos, and incomplete/improperly formatted references; unnecessary claims (e.g., "no monolingual corpus").

- Question 1: Provide statistical significance testing for BLEU differences between DCNMT and BPE baselines.

- Question 2: Define and quantitatively measure "morphological learning" beyond qualitative examples; provide rigorous evidence.

- Question 3: Why are Cs-En BPE2char results not reported in Chung et al. 2016—did authors compute them themselves?

- Question 4: Rephrase/clarify the claim that French is "morphologically poor" with a more precise characterization.

- Overall: Interesting and promising approach to character-level NMT, but morphology claims are overstated and evaluation (comparisons, statistical tests, technical clarity) is incomplete, weakening the contribution.
