- Proposal: DCNMT — a character-level NMT with a two-RNN word encoder and hierarchical decoder to learn morphology and address OOVs.  
- Contribution: Character-level architecture that learns morphemes without subword segmentation like BPE.  
- Contribution: Achieves competitive BLEU after only one epoch, outperforming BPE-based models early in training.  
- Contribution: Handles unseen words via morphological reasoning, enabling correct translation of nonce words.  
- Strength: Demonstrates that character-level models can learn meaningful morphology and reach strong performance with minimal training.  
- Strength: Training efficiency — despite six RNNs, DCNMT trains much faster than prior character models (e.g., 7 days vs. months).  
- Strength: Empirical evidence (PCA) shows word encoder clusters morphologically related words, benefiting morphologically rich languages.  
- Weakness: Paper omits explicit statement of which WMT test/dev sets were used, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Architecture diagram is misleading and doesn’t match the claimed six-RNN design.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to state-of-the-art are incomplete; recent WMT/matrix.statmt.org results are not included.  
- Questions: Clarify which WMT test/dev sets were used (e.g., newstest2015/newstest2013).  
- Questions: Provide a clear, complete architecture diagram matching the six-layer model.  
- Questions: Expand comparisons to recent WMT 2016 or matrix.statmt.org results to contextualize claims.  
- Minor issues: English disfluencies, LaTeX errors, and README lacks runnable examples and a --help flag.  
- Overall: Strong technical contribution showing morphology-aware character-level NMT with good results and handling of unseen words, but needs presentation and comparison clarifications.
