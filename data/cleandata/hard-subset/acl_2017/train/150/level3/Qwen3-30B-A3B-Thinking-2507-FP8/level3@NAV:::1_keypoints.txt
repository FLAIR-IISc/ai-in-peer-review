- Novel character-level NMT with two-RNN word encoder and hierarchical decoder to learn morphology and avoid large vocabulary bottleneck.
- Contribution: morphological learning that handles unseen words by backing off to morphemes.
- Contribution: character-level architecture that achieves competitive BLEU scores after just one training epoch.
- Contribution: release of code and models for three language pairs to improve reproducibility.
- Strength: effective OOV handling demonstrated with nonce-word examples (e.g., “convenienter”).
- Strength: impressive training efficiency — reasonable translation quality after a single epoch.
- Strength: model learns plausible morphological tokenizations and yields competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En.
- Weakness: paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (only in README).
- Weakness: Figure 2 caption claims a 6-level architecture but the figure appears to show fewer layers, causing confusion.
- Weakness: comparisons omit important non-character-based NMT baselines that have higher WMT scores, weakening competitiveness claims.
- Weakness: software README lacks runnable examples and a --help flag, hindering reproducibility despite code release.
- Minor issues: English and LaTeX errors (e.g., reversed quotation marks) need correction.
- Recommendation: paper needs more complete experimental comparisons, clearer architecture visualization, and explicit reporting of evaluation settings.
