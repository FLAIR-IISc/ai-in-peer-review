- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT combining character processing and subword representations via a novel word encoder and hierarchical decoder; claims comparable BLEU, faster training, and OOV handling.

- Contribution 1: Novel dual-recurrent word encoder that learns morphology implicitly without explicit segmentation.

- Contribution 2: Hierarchical character-level decoder that preserves similar encoding length to word-based models.

- Contribution 3: Empirical claim that character-level modeling achieves competitive BLEU and resolves out-of-vocabulary issues.

- Strength 1: Clear, well-structured presentation with effective figures explaining the architecture.

- Strength 2: Original approach to morphology learning using energy-weighted dual-recurrent encoder with compelling qualitative visualizations.

- Strength 3: Competitive BLEU results with substantially faster training than prior character-level baselines and rapid gains over bpe2char.

- Weakness 1: Main claim that DCNMT "learns morphology better" is unsupported by formal experiments or statistical evidence.

- Weakness 2: Inadequate comparison with bpe2char—very small BLEU differences, no significance testing, and incomplete training-time reporting.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient evaluation of core morphology claims—relies on anecdotal PCA visualizations; analysis should be moved or claims softened.

- Question 1: How would the authors formally evaluate and compare morphology learning against other approaches?

- Question 2: What statistical significance testing was performed to confirm the small BLEU improvements over bpe2char?

- Question 3: Did the authors compute the Cs→En results for Chung et al. (2016) themselves, or should the paper clarify this?

- Additional comments: Introduce undefined acronyms, standardize "BPE" capitalization, avoid labeling French as "morphologically poor," fix figure 4a color mapping, complete missing references, remove unnecessary monolingual-corpus remark, and correct grammatical/typo errors.

- Recommendation: Paper has merit but requires stronger, formal evidence for the morphology claim and a complete bpe2char comparison (including training-time) before acceptance.
