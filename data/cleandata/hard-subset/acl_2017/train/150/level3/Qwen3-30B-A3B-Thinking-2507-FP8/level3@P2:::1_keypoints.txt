- Summary: Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a novel word encoder and hierarchical decoder (six-RNN architecture) that learns morphological representations, handles OOVs, avoids large vocab bottlenecks, trains efficiently, and includes released code/models.

- Contribution 1: Novel word encoder using two recurrent networks to represent morphemes and their combinations, enabling handling of unseen words.

- Contribution 2: Hierarchical decoder that translates at character level with improved training efficiency and competitive BLEU after one epoch.

- Contribution 3: Empirical evidence that learned word representations capture morphology (PCA visualizations, nonce-word translation).

- Strength 1: Achieves competitive translation quality quickly (outperforms bpe-baseline after one epoch), addressing practical training-time concerns.

- Strength 2: Convincing analyses showing morphological learning (clustering in PCA, successful nonce-word translations).

- Strength 3: Advances under-explored character-level NMT by addressing vocabulary and efficiency challenges.

- Weakness 1: Unclear which WMT test/dev sets were used; dataset choices should be explicitly stated in the paper.

- Weakness 2: Figure 2 and its caption are confusing and do not clearly show the claimed six recurrent networks.

- Weakness 3: Incomplete comparisons with recent non-character-based SOTA systems and omission of higher BLEU baselines weakens claims of competitiveness.

- Weakness 4: Software release lacks proper documentation and runnable examples; no --help flag, hindering reproducibility.

- Weakness 5: Minor English, LaTeX formatting, and presentation issues need fixing.

- Question 1: Please clarify the exact WMT test and development sets used (e.g., newstest2013/dev, newstest2015/test).

- Question 2: Provide a clearer, more detailed architecture diagram showing all six recurrent networks and connections.

- Question 3: Explain omission of comparisons with recent non-character-based NMT systems and how the approach compares to those higher-BLEU baselines.

- Recommendation: Valuable contribution but requires revisions on architecture clarity, experimental setup, comparisons, and documentation before acceptance.
