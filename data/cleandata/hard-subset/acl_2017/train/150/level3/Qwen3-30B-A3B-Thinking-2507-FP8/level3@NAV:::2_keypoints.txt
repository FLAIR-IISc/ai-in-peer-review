- Proposal: DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT combining character-level benefits with subword representations via a novel word encoder and hierarchical decoder.  
- Contribution: Morphology-aware word encoder using two RNNs to learn morphemes and their combination rules.  
- Contribution: Hierarchical decoder enabling efficient character-level translation.  
- Contribution: Demonstrated competitive BLEU scores on multiple language pairs.  
- Strength: Well-structured and clearly presented; two-RNN encoder is a novel idea with potential to address OOV issues.  
- Strength: Achieves competitive BLEU with a character-level approach, which is notable.  
- Weakness: Lacks formal evidence for claimed morphological learning—Section 5.2 uses only anecdotes and PCA without statistical validation or controlled experiments.  
- Weakness: Incomplete comparison with bpe2char—BLEU gains are small (0.5–1.0), no significance testing reported, and training times for En→Cs and En→Fr are missing.  
- Weakness: Multiple technical issues (undefined acronyms like LSTM/HGRU/BPE, inconsistent capitalization, grammatical errors) that undermine credibility.  
- Weakness: Inappropriate characterization of French as "morphologically poor."  
- Additional concerns: Figure 4a has inconsistent color mapping; Table 1 reports unverified results for Chung et al. (2016).  
- Additional concern: Unnecessary emphasis on "no monolingual corpus used."  
- Recommendation: Authors should perform formal morphology experiments, complete comparative evaluations (including significance and training times), and improve methodological rigor and language.
