- Proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT with a novel word encoder and hierarchical decoder to address the vocabulary bottleneck.  
- Authors claim three contributions: a two-RNN word encoder that learns morphemes, a hierarchical character-level decoder with word-level encoding length, and competitive BLEU without large vocabularies.  
- Reviewer sees the hierarchical architecture as the main solid contribution; morphology-learning claims are inadequately supported.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem statement and thoughtful analysis of character-level training challenges, especially attention.  
- Strength: Novel, well-justified architecture (dual-RNN encoder + hierarchical decoder) with reproducible implementation details.  
- Strength: Promising En–Cs / Cs–En results and practical robustness to misspellings and nonce words.  
- Weakness: Morphology-learning claim lacks formal experiments, metrics, or statistical validation and is mainly anecdotal.  
- Weakness: BLEU gains over BPE models are marginal (<1 BLEU) and no statistical significance testing is reported.  
- Weakness: Training-time comparisons are incomplete/misleading (missing baseline timings and En–Fr results).  
- Weakness: Presentation problems—undefined acronyms, inconsistent terminology/capitalization, grammatical errors, and debatable/unclear claims (e.g., “French is morphologically poor,” unexplained figure peaks).  
- Question: Provide statistical significance testing for BLEU differences vs BPE baselines.  
- Question: How would the authors formally evaluate morphology learning in NMT and which metrics would they use?  
- Question: Rephrase the claim that French is “morphologically poor” to acknowledge its morphological complexity.  
- Question: Clarify how energy values in Figure 4a were computed and explain the apparent inconsistent color mapping.  
- Question: Is the statement “no monolingual corpus is used” necessary or potentially misleading—please clarify.  
- Additional: Paper needs stronger evidence for morphology claims, more complete/statistical evaluation, and careful presentation fixes.  
- Additional: Authors should provide a link to the WMT’14 training corpora, fix incomplete references, and soften the “learns morphology” wording.
