This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The authors claim that their model achieves a high translation performance comparable to the state-of-the-art neural machine translation models on the tasks of En-Fr, En-Cs, and Cs-En translation.

The main contributions of this paper are:

1. The authors propose a novel word encoder that learns the morphemes and the rules of how they are combined. This is achieved by using two recurrent neural networks to compute the representation of the word.
2. The authors introduce a hierarchical decoder that decodes at the character level. This is achieved by using a second-level decoder that generates a character sequence based on the output of the first-level decoder.
3. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of their model by training it on a single GTX Titan X with 12GB RAM and achieving a high translation performance comparable to the state-of-the-art models.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The authors propose a novel architecture that learns morphology, which is a key aspect of language translation.
2. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of their model by training it on a single GPU and achieving a high translation performance.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their model's performance, including a comparison with other models and an analysis of the model's ability to learn morphology.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

1. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the architecture of their model, particularly the hierarchical decoder.
2. The authors do not provide a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art models, particularly non-character-based models.
3. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how their model handles out-of-vocabulary words.

Questions to authors:

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the architecture of your hierarchical decoder?
2. How does your model handle out-of-vocabulary words?
3. Can you provide a more thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art models, particularly non-character-based models?

Additional comments:

* The authors should provide more details about the hyperparameters used in their experiments.
* The authors should provide more details about the training procedure, including the batch size and the number of epochs.
* The authors should provide more details about the evaluation metrics used, including the BLEU score and the ROUGE score.

Overall, this paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation that learns morphology. While the authors demonstrate the efficiency of their model and provide a thorough analysis of its performance, there are some weaknesses in the paper that need to be addressed.
