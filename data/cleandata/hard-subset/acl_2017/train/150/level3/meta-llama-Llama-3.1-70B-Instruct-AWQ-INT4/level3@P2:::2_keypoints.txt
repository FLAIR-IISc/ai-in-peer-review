- Proposes a novel deep character-level NMT (DCNMT) with two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to learn morphology and reduce vocabulary issues.
- Claims higher BLEU than bpe-based model after one epoch on En→Fr and En→Cs and comparable to state-of-the-art with efficient training.

- Main contribution: new architecture (two RNNs + hierarchical decoder) for character-level translation that purportedly captures morphology.
- Demonstrates training efficiency and compares DCNMT with word-level and other character-level models.
- Reports BLEU improvements over bpe-based baselines after limited training.

- Strength: paper is well-structured and presents original, interesting ideas.
- Strength: provides detailed model architecture and training procedure.
- Strength: evaluates performance across multiple language pairs and datasets.

- Weakness: claims of learning morphology are not well supported and evidence is anecdotal.
- Weakness: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are very small and lack statistical significance testing.
- Weakness: training-time comparisons are incomplete; bpe2char training time for En-Cs and En-Fr is not reported.
- Weakness: several undefined/poorly defined acronyms and occasional unclear writing.

- Question: please clarify the concept of "energy" in Section 5.2 and explain the meaning of peaks in Figure 5.
- Question: provide a more complete empirical comparison with bpe2char to substantiate DCNMT advantages.
- Question: conduct formal experiments to support claims about learning morphology.
- Question: clarify and standardize capitalization of BPE throughout the paper.
- Question: provide a link to the WMT'14 training corpora and fix incomplete references.

- Additional: fix grammatical and typographical errors throughout the paper.
- Additional: avoid characterizing French as "morphologically poor" and rephrase more carefully.
- Additional: clearly explain the HGRU unit and its advantages over alternatives.
- Additional: fully explain and properly reference figures and tables that are currently under-described.
