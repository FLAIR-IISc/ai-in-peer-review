- Proposes DCNMT: a novel character-level MT architecture with two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder, evaluated on En→Fr, En→Cs, Cs→En with comparable SOTA results.
- Novel architecture: hierarchical (claimed 6-level) design using two RNNs to learn morphology for efficient character-level translation.
- Learning morphology: represents words as combinations of morphemes, allowing backing off to morphemes for unseen or nonce words.
- Efficient training: yields reasonable translation quality after the first epoch and improves with further training.
- Strength: enables efficient character-level translation, avoiding large vocabulary issues and improving training efficiency.
- Strength: learns morphology that improves robustness and generalization to unseen words.
- Strength: achieves comparable performance to state-of-the-art systems on the reported language pairs.
- Weakness: Figure 2 is unclear and appears to show fewer layers than the described 6-level architecture; needs a full architecture figure.
- Weakness: Omits comparisons to state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and does not acknowledge higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org results.
- Weakness: Contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues that require correction.
- Question: Please provide a full architecture figure clarifying the 6-level design.
- Question: How will the authors address the omitted comparisons to non-character MT systems and higher reported WMT results?
- Question: Please provide more details on the training process and hyperparameter tuning.
- Additional: Valuable contribution to character-based neural MT that could be a strong publication after revisions.
