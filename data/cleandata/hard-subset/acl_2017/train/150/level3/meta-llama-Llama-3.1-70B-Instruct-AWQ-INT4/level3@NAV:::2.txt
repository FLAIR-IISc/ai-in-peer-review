**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model is designed to address the issues of large vocabulary and out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words in word-level neural machine translation models. The authors claim that their model achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model and is comparable to the state-of-the-art systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Deep Character-Level Neural Machine Translation Model**: The authors propose a novel architecture that combines the benefits of character-level and sub-word representations using a deep word-encoding/decoding layer.
2. **Learning Morphology**: The model is designed to learn morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder, which allows it to capture the relationships between characters and words.
3. **Efficient Training**: The authors demonstrate that their model can be trained efficiently using a variant of the gate recurrent unit (GRU) and a matrix-based unfolding technique.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-Structured Paper**: The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed model and its components.
2. **Original Ideas**: The authors propose a novel architecture that combines character-level and sub-word representations, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural machine translation.
3. **Interesting Results**: The authors present interesting results that demonstrate the effectiveness of their model in learning morphology and achieving high BLEU scores.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Doubts about Result Interpretation**: The authors claim that their model learns morphology better than state-of-the-art approaches, but the results are not conclusive, and more experiments are needed to support this claim.
2. **Unsupported Claims about Learning Morphology**: The authors provide anecdotal evidence of their model's ability to learn morphology, but more formal experiments are needed to support this claim.
3. **Incomplete Comparison with bpe2char**: The authors compare their model with bpe2char, but the comparison is incomplete, and more experiments are needed to demonstrate the advantages of DCNMT.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more experiments to support your claim that DCNMT learns morphology better than state-of-the-art approaches?
2. Can you provide more details about the variant of the GRU used in the second-level decoder and how it differs from the standard GRU?
3. Can you provide more information about the matrix-based unfolding technique used to improve training efficiency?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper would benefit from more formal experiments to support the claim that DCNMT learns morphology better than state-of-the-art approaches.
2. The authors should provide more details about the hyperparameters used in the experiments and how they were tuned.
3. The paper would benefit from more comparisons with other state-of-the-art models to demonstrate the advantages of DCNMT.
