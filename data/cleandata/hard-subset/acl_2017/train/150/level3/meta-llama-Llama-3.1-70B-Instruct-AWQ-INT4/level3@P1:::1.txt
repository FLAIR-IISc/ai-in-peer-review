**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for neural machine translation (NMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to translate at the character level. The proposed model, called Deep Character-Level Neural Machine Translation (DCNMT), consists of six recurrent neural networks and is able to learn the representation of morphemes and the rules of how they are combined. The model is evaluated on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks and achieves comparable results to state-of-the-art systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The proposed DCNMT architecture is a significant contribution to the field of NMT. By learning morphology and using a hierarchical decoder, the model is able to effectively translate at the character level and avoid the issues associated with large vocabularies.
2. **Learning Morphology**: The model's ability to learn morphology is a key contribution. By representing words as a combination of morphemes, the model is able to capture the nuances of language and improve translation quality.
3. **Efficient Training**: The model's efficient training process is also a significant contribution. Despite consisting of six recurrent neural networks, the model is able to train quickly and achieve good results.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Translation Quality**: The model achieves comparable results to state-of-the-art systems on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks.
2. **Efficient Training**: The model's efficient training process is a significant advantage. The model is able to train quickly and achieve good results, making it a practical solution for NMT.
3. **Ability to Learn Morphology**: The model's ability to learn morphology is a key strength. By representing words as a combination of morphemes, the model is able to capture the nuances of language and improve translation quality.
4. **Handling Unseen or Nonce Words**: The model's ability to handle unseen or nonce words by backing off to morphemes is a significant advantage. This allows the model to translate words that are not in its vocabulary.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Clear Evaluation Metrics**: The paper does not clearly state which WMT test and development sets are used, which makes it difficult to evaluate the model's performance.
2. **Limited Comparisons**: The paper only compares the model's performance to other character-based NMT systems and does not acknowledge higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org results.
3. **Minor English Disfluencies and LaTeX Issues**: The paper contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues that need correction.
4. **Unclear Figure and Caption**: The figure and caption in the paper are unclear and do not accurately represent the model's architecture.
