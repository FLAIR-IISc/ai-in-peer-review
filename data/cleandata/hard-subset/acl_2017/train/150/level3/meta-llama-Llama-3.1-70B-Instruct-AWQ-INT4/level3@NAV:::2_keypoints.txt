Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT with two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to handle large vocab/OOVs, reporting higher BLEU than bpe-based and comparable to SOTA.

Main contribution 1: Novel architecture combining character-level and sub-word representations via a deep word-encoding/decoding layer.

Main contribution 2: Model designed to learn morphology using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to capture character–word relationships.

Main contribution 3: Claims efficient training using a GRU variant and a matrix-based unfolding technique.

Strength 1: Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the model and components.

Strength 2: Introduces original ideas by combining character-level and sub-word approaches.

Strength 3: Presents interesting results suggesting effective morphology learning and strong BLEU scores.

Weakness 1: Result interpretation is inconclusive; more experiments needed to substantiate claims.

Weakness 2: Claims about learning morphology are supported mainly by anecdotal evidence rather than formal evaluation.

Weakness 3: Comparison with bpe2char is incomplete and requires additional experiments to demonstrate DCNMT’s advantages.

Question 1: Please provide more experiments to support the claim that DCNMT learns morphology better than SOTA.

Question 2: Please detail the GRU variant used in the second-level decoder and how it differs from the standard GRU.

Question 3: Please explain the matrix-based unfolding technique used to improve training efficiency.

Additional comment 1: The paper needs more formal experiments to validate morphological learning claims.

Additional comment 2: Provide hyperparameter settings and tuning procedures used in experiments.

Additional comment 3: Include more comparisons against other state-of-the-art models to demonstrate DCNMT’s advantages.
