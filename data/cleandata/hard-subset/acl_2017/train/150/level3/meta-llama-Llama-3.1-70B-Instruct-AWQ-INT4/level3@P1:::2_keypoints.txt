- Paper proposes DCNMT: a novel NMT architecture combining character-level and sub-word representations with a deep word-encoding/decoding layer and hierarchical character-level decoder; evaluated on En→Fr, En→Cs, Cs→En with BLEU slightly higher than BPE after one epoch and comparable to SOTA.

- Contribution: deep word-encoding/decoding layer that learns morphology while enabling character-level decoding.

- Contribution: hierarchical decoder where a second-level decoder generates character sequences and either continues or resets states at word boundaries.

- Contribution/claim: DCNMT learns morphology better than prior approaches, supported by anecdotal examples.

- Strength: paper is well-structured and easy to follow.

- Strength: proposes original ideas with interesting empirical results.

- Strength: clear and concise presentation.

- Weakness: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are very small and no statistical significance testing is reported.

- Weakness: morphology-learning claims are unsupported by formal experiments—only anecdotal evidence provided.

- Weakness: incomplete training-time comparison—bpe2char training time not given for En→Cs and En→Fr bpe2char not evaluated.

- Weakness: multiple grammatical and typographical errors throughout the paper.

- Request: provide more complete comparisons with bpe2char to demonstrate DCNMT’s advantages.

- Request: provide formal, quantitative experiments to support claims about learning morphology.

- Request: clarify the concept of “energy” in Section 5.2 and explain what peaks represent in Figure 5.

- Request: fix energy color mapping in Figure 4a.

- Request: provide a link to the WMT’14 training corpora and correct incomplete references.

- Request: state whether Cs→En results for model (3) (Chung et al. 2016) were computed by the authors or taken from the cited paper.

- Additional comment: avoid labeling French as “morphologically poor” and rephrase more carefully.
