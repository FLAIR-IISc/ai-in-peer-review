**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model aims to address the issue of large vocabulary in word-level neural machine translation models and improve the performance of character-level models. The authors claim that their model achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for one epoch on En-Fr and En-Cs translation tasks and is comparable to the state-of-the-art systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.
2. The authors demonstrate the efficiency of the training process and the effectiveness of the model in comparison with word-level and other character-level models.
3. The model achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for one epoch on En-Fr and En-Cs translation tasks and is comparable to the state-of-the-art systems.

**Strengths**

1. The paper is well-structured and clearly presents original ideas and interesting results.
2. The authors provide a detailed description of the model architecture and the training process.
3. The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the model's performance on different language pairs and datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. The authors' claims about learning morphology are not fully supported by the results, and the evaluation of the model's ability to learn morphology is anecdotal.
2. The BLEU differences between DCNMT and bpe2char are very small, and no statistical significance testing is reported.
3. The training-time comparison is incomplete, and the authors do not report the training time for En-Cs and En-Fr bpe2char.
4. The paper includes several undefined or poorly defined acronyms, and the writing style is sometimes unclear.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the concept of "energy" in Section 5.2 and describe what peaks mean in Figure 5?
2. Can you provide a more complete comparison with bpe2char to demonstrate the advantages of DCNMT?
3. Can you provide more formal experiments to support the claims about learning morphology?
4. Can you clarify the capitalization of BPE throughout the paper?
5. Can you provide a link for the WMT'14 training corpora and fix several incomplete references?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper includes several grammatical and typographical errors that need to be corrected.
2. The authors should avoid calling French "morphologically poor" and rephrase more carefully.
3. The authors should provide a clear explanation of the HGRU unit and its advantages over other units.
4. The paper includes several figures and tables that are not fully explained or referenced in the text.
