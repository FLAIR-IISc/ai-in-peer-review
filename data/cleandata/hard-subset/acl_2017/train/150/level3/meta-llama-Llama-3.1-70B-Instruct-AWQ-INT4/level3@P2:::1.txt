**Paper Summary**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model translates at the character level, avoiding the large vocabulary issue and improving training efficiency. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks, achieving comparable results to state-of-the-art systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The authors propose a hierarchical architecture that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder, allowing for efficient character-level translation.
2. **Learning Morphology**: The model learns to represent words as a combination of morphemes, enabling it to handle unseen or nonce words by backing off to morphemes.
3. **Efficient Training**: The model achieves reasonable translation quality even after the first training epoch and improves with more epochs, demonstrating efficient training.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficient Character-Level Translation**: The model's hierarchical architecture enables efficient character-level translation, avoiding the large vocabulary issue and improving training efficiency.
2. **Learning Morphology**: The model's ability to learn morphology enables it to handle unseen or nonce words, improving its robustness and generalization capabilities.
3. **Comparable Results**: The model achieves comparable results to state-of-the-art systems on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Clarity in Figure 2**: The figure appears to show fewer layers than the described 6-level architecture, making it unclear and in need of a full architecture figure.
2. **Omitted Comparisons**: The paper omits comparisons to state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and does not acknowledge higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org results.
3. **Minor English Disfluencies and LaTeX Issues**: The paper contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues that need correction.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a full architecture figure to clarify the 6-level architecture described in the paper?
2. How do you plan to address the omitted comparisons to state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org results?
3. Can you provide more details on the training process and hyperparameter tuning for the model?

**Additional Comments**

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the character-based neural MT literature, and with some revisions to address the mentioned weaknesses, it has the potential to be a strong publication.
