- Proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT using a hierarchical decoder and multiple recurrent networks to learn morphemes and translate at the character level; evaluated on En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En with comparable results.

- Novel architecture: introduces a multi-RNN hierarchical decoder to learn morphology and avoid large-vocabulary issues in NMT.

- Learning morphology: models words as combinations of morphemes, aiming to capture morphological rules and nuances for better translation.

- Efficient training: despite using six RNNs, the model reportedly trains quickly and achieves strong results.

- Improved translation quality: achieves results comparable to state-of-the-art systems on the reported language pairs.

- Efficient training (strength): practical training speed and resource behavior is highlighted as an advantage.

- Ability to learn morphology (strength): morpheme-based representations help capture linguistic nuances and improve outputs.

- Handling unseen/nonce words (strength): can back off to morphemes to translate words not seen in training.

- Lack of clear evaluation metrics (weakness): paper does not specify which WMT test/dev sets were used, hindering reproducibility and comparison.

- Limited comparisons (weakness): comparisons are restricted to other character-based NMT systems and ignore higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org baselines.

- Minor English disfluencies and LaTeX issues (weakness): the manuscript contains typos and formatting problems that need fixing.

- Unclear figure and caption (weakness): the provided figure/caption do not clearly or accurately represent the model architecture.

- Question on evaluation details: request for explicit information on which WMT test and development sets and metrics were used.

- Question on broader comparisons: ask how the model compares to state-of-the-art non-character-level NMT systems.

- Question on unseen-word handling: request more details on the mechanism for backing off to morphemes and empirical behavior on nonce words.

- Question on code/models release: inquire whether additional code or trained models for other language pairs will be released.
