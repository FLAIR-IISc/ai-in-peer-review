**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) by learning morphology. The model consists of a word encoder and a hierarchical decoder, which translates at the character level. The authors claim that their model achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for one epoch on En-Fr and En-Cs translation tasks and is comparable to state-of-the-art systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Deep Character-Level Neural Machine Translation Model**: The authors propose a novel architecture for DCNMT, which combines the benefits of character-level and sub-word representations.
2. **Learning Morphology**: The model learns morphology by using two recurrent neural networks and a hierarchical decoder, which translates at the character level.
3. **Efficient Training**: The authors demonstrate that their model is efficient to train, achieving higher performance than other character-level models.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-structured and Clear Presentation**: The paper is well-organized, and the authors present their ideas clearly.
2. **Original Ideas and Interesting Results**: The authors propose a novel architecture and demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving high translation performance.
3. **Efficient Training**: The authors show that their model is efficient to train, achieving higher performance than other character-level models.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Doubts about Result Interpretation**: The authors' claims about learning morphology are not fully supported by their experiments, and the results are not conclusive.
2. **Unsupported Claims about Learning Morphology**: The authors' analysis of learning morphology is anecdotal and lacks formal experiments to support their claims.
3. **Incomplete Comparison with bpe2char**: The authors do not provide a complete comparison with bpe2char, including training time and BLEU scores for all language pairs.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more formal experiments to support your claims about learning morphology?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of incomplete comparison with bpe2char?
3. Can you provide more details about the training process and hyperparameters used in your experiments?

**Additional Comments**

* The paper would benefit from more formal experiments to support the claims about learning morphology.
* The authors should provide a complete comparison with bpe2char, including training time and BLEU scores for all language pairs.
* The paper could be improved by providing more details about the training process and hyperparameters used in the experiments.
* The authors should consider providing more examples of translations to demonstrate the effectiveness of their model.
* The paper would benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results, including error analysis and discussion of limitations.
