- Proposes a novel Deep Character-Level NMT (DCNMT) architecture combining word encoder and hierarchical character-level decoder.  
- Claims the model learns morphology using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.  
- Reports higher BLEU than a bpe-based model after one epoch on En→Fr and En→Cs, and comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Contribution: combines character-level and subword representations in a new architecture.  
- Contribution: explicit mechanism intended to learn morphology.  
- Contribution: claims efficient training and improved performance over other character-level models.  
- Strength: paper is well-structured and clearly presented.  
- Strength: presents original ideas with interesting empirical results.  
- Strength: demonstrates reportedly efficient training.  
- Weakness: claims about learning morphology are not fully supported or conclusive.  
- Weakness: morphological analysis is anecdotal and lacks formal experiments.  
- Weakness: comparison with bpe2char is incomplete (missing training time and BLEU for all language pairs).  
- Question: provide formal experiments that validate morphological learning.  
- Question: how will the incomplete bpe2char comparison be addressed?  
- Question: provide full training details and hyperparameters.  
- Additional: include more formal experiments, complete bpe2char comparisons (including training time/BLEU), and detailed training/hyperparameter descriptions.
