**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) model that learns morphology by using two recurrent neural networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model starts and ends with characters but uses morpheme/character representations in between. The authors evaluate their model on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks and show that it achieves a high translation performance comparable to the state-of-the-art neural machine translation models.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel encoder-decoder architecture**: The authors propose a new architecture that learns morphology by using two recurrent neural networks and a hierarchical decoder.
2. **Efficient training**: The model is able to train efficiently despite consisting of six recurrent neural networks.
3. **Ability to learn morphology**: The model is able to learn morphology and translate misspelled or nonce words.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved translation performance**: The model achieves a high translation performance comparable to the state-of-the-art neural machine translation models.
2. **Efficient training**: The model is able to train efficiently despite its complexity.
3. **Ability to learn morphology**: The model is able to learn morphology and translate misspelled or nonce words.
4. **Open-source implementation**: The authors release their code and final models for reproducibility.
5. **Reasonable translation quality**: The model produces reasonable translation quality even after the first training epoch and improves with more epochs.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of explicit statement of WMT test/dev sets**: The paper fails to explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets are used.
2. **Unclear figure caption**: The figure caption is unclear, and a full architecture figure is needed.
3. **Omitted comparisons**: The paper omits comparisons with state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and does not acknowledge higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org results.
4. **Minor English disfluencies and LaTeX issues**: The paper has minor English disfluencies and LaTeX issues that need correction.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how the model learns morphology and how it handles unseen or nonce words?
2. How does the model compare to other state-of-the-art neural machine translation models in terms of translation performance and efficiency?
3. Can you provide more information on the training process and how the model is able to train efficiently despite its complexity?
