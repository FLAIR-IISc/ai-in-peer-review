- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a six-RNN fully character-level hierarchical NMT with a source-side weighted-character word encoder and a target hierarchical decoder; reports BLEU comparable to SOTA and claims robustness to OOVs/misspellings.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel hierarchical char-level architecture that learns sub-word (morpheme-like) representations end-to-end without explicit segmentation (e.g., BPE).

- Contribution 2: Empirically shows fast early learning and qualitative robustness (correctly handling misspellings and nonce words) compared to word-level baselines.

- Strength 1: Original, well-motivated design—source encoder uses an RNN+BiRNN weighting scheme and the hierarchical decoder elegantly guides character generation with word-level context.

- Strength 2: Strong qualitative evidence and early-training performance (competitive BLEU after one epoch) suggesting useful inductive bias for morphology.

- Strength 3: Paper is clear and well-structured, with effective figures that help explain the complex architecture.

- Weakness 1: Quantitative improvements over bpe2char/char2char are marginal and no statistical significance tests are reported, so superiority claims are unsupported.

- Weakness 2: Efficiency comparison is incomplete/misleading (missing baselines for some tasks; DCNMT is slower on Cs→En where bpe2char is reported 8 days faster).

- Weakness 3: Claims that the model "learns morphology" rest on selective visualizations and anecdotes (PCA/energy plots) rather than systematic, quantitative morphological evaluations or probing tasks.

- Weakness 4 (presentation): Several presentation issues—acronyms used before definition, inconsistent BPE capitalization, unclear definition of "energy"/"peak energy", oversimplified comment on French morphology, a rendering issue in Figure 4a, missing WMT'14 link and incomplete bibliography entries.

- Question 1: Please provide statistical significance tests for BLEU comparisons, especially versus bpe2char and char2char.

- Question 2: Please clarify and complete the training-time comparisons (report bpe2char time for En-Cs, include a character-baseline on En-Fr) and explain why DCNMT was significantly slower on Cs→En.

- Question 3: Did the authors run the bpe2char Cs→En experiment themselves? If so, state that explicitly to ensure proper attribution and reproducibility.
