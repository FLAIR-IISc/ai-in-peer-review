- Proposal: DCNMT — a character-only NMT with a deep word encoder (two RNNs producing weighted character-based word reps) and a hierarchical character-level decoder to learn morphology without BPE.
- Claimed benefits: eliminates OOVs and aims to capture sub-word structure automatically.
- Strength: paper is well-structured and presents an original architecture.
- Strength: novel mechanism — using a weighted sum of character RNN states to form word representations.
- Strength: qualitative examples show the model handling misspellings and nonce words better than word/subword systems.
- Major weakness: strong claims about learning morphology are not rigorously supported and rely on anecdotal analysis.
- Needed: formal, quantitative experiments to measure morphological awareness; anecdotal/PCA evidence should be softened or moved to an appendix.
- Empirical results are unconvincing: BLEU gains over bpe2char are marginal and lack statistical significance testing.
- Incomplete runtime comparison: crucial training-time data for the bpe2char baseline is missing, weakening efficiency claims.
- Presentation problems: undefined/poorly introduced acronyms, inconsistent capitalization (e.g., BPE), unclear figure/table explanations, typos, and incomplete references.
