Contributions:
- Novel deep encoder-decoder NMT that processes characters but learns intermediate morpheme-like representations to address OOV issues.
- Empirical evidence that the model attains competitive translations, learns plausible morphological segmentations, and generalizes to nonce words.
- Public release of source code and pre-trained models to support reproducibility.

Strengths:
- Architectural innovation: end-to-end learning of subword units without relying on preprocessing like BPE.
- Strong empirical performance, achieving reasonable quality after one epoch and handling morphological variation and unseen words.
- Commitment to open science via code and model release.

Weaknesses:
- Experimental comparison is insufficient: missing benchmarks against state-of-the-art non-character NMTs and no discussion of higher public leaderboard scores.
- Lack of clarity in presentation: confusing Figure 2, mismatch with textual description, and unnamed test/development sets.
- Subpar code usability and paper polish: README lacks runnable examples and standard --help support; frequent English disfluencies and formatting errors.
