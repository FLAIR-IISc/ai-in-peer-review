## Review of Deep Character-Level Neural Machine Translation By Learning Morphology

This paper proposes a novel deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) architecture designed to combine the benefits of character-level modeling with sub-word representations, aiming to address the large vocabulary problem in NMT. The core contribution lies in the introduction of a word encoder utilizing two RNNs to learn morphology and a hierarchical decoder for character-level translation. The authors demonstrate competitive BLEU scores on En-Fr, En-Cs, and Cs-En tasks, suggesting the model effectively learns morphological information.

**Strengths:**

1. **Well-structured and Clear Presentation:** The paper is logically organized, with a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and experimental setup. The writing is generally understandable, making the core ideas accessible.
2. **Original Ideas:** The combination of a morphology-aware word encoder with a hierarchical character-level decoder is a novel approach to NMT. The attempt to explicitly model morphology within the encoder is a particularly interesting aspect.
3. **Promising Results:** The reported BLEU scores are competitive with existing character-level models, indicating the potential of the proposed architecture. The initial results on En-Fr and En-Cs are encouraging.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Unsupported Claims about Morphology Learning:** While the authors claim their model learns morphology, the evidence presented in Section 5.2 is largely anecdotal, relying on example-based analysis. There are no formal experiments or quantitative metrics to support this claim. This significantly weakens the central assertion of the paper.
2. **Incomplete Evaluation & Statistical Significance:** The BLEU score differences with the bpe2char baseline are very small, and crucially, no statistical significance testing is reported. This makes it difficult to determine if the observed improvements are meaningful or due to random chance.
3. **Insufficient Comparison with bpe2char:** The training time comparison is incomplete. While DCNMT is faster on Cs→En, the En→Cs training time is missing, and the En→Fr bpe2char evaluation is absent. A more thorough comparison is needed to demonstrate the advantages of DCNMT.
4. **Numerous Minor Issues:** The paper suffers from a large number of minor issues, including undefined acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE), inconsistent capitalization of "BPE," typos, and grammatical errors. Figure 4a appears to have a mapping issue, and Table 1 contains a discrepancy regarding the reported results for Chung et al. (2016).

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Can you provide a more rigorous, quantitative evaluation of the model's ability to learn morphology? Consider experiments that specifically target morphological awareness, such as analyzing performance on morphologically complex words or performing ablation studies.
2.  Please provide statistical significance tests for all reported BLEU score differences.
3.  Can you complete the training time comparison with bpe2char for all language pairs?
4.  Please clarify the energy concept in Section 5.2 and explain the meaning of the peaks in Figure 5.



In conclusion, while the paper presents an interesting and potentially valuable contribution to the field of NMT, the lack of rigorous evaluation and supporting evidence for the key claim regarding morphology learning prevents a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly providing a more robust analysis of morphological learning and completing the comparative evaluation, would significantly strengthen the paper.
