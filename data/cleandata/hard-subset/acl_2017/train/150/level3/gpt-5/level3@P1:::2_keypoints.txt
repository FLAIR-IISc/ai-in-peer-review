- Proposes DCNMT: hierarchical char-level NMT with two-RNN word encoder (weighted char-prefix states) and hierarchical word→char decoder (settable-state HGRU).

- Aims to capture morphology while avoiding large softmaxes and OOVs; reports competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En and faster early learning.

- Contribution: hierarchical character→word→sentence→word→character architecture with settable-state HGRU and unfolding trick for efficient batched char decoding.

- Contribution: two-RNN word encoder that learns weights over character-prefix hidden states to compose morpheme-aware word representations.

- Contribution: empirical evidence of competitiveness with subword/char baselines and robustness to misspellings/nonce words.

- Strength: novel hierarchical decoding (settable-state HGRU + unfolding) enabling end-to-end character decoding efficiently without extra supervision.

- Strength: relatively compact parameter count and competitive BLEU, with promising early-epoch learning speed.

- Strength: open-vocabulary generation and demonstrated robustness to misspellings/nonce forms.

- Strength: architecture, HGRU equations, and training/generation described clearly enough to implement.

- Weakness: morphology-learning claims are anecdotal (PCA plots and hand-picked examples) without formal evaluation or probing.

- Weakness: baselines (esp. bpe2char) comparisons are incomplete and possibly non-comparable across language pairs and timings.

- Weakness: BLEU differences are small and no statistical significance testing is reported.

- Weakness: ambiguity and inconsistent definition/visualization of “energy”; figures lack clear legend/color mapping.

- Weakness: presentation issues—undefined acronyms, inconsistent capitalization, incomplete references, missing dataset links, and some unreported results in Table 1.

- Question: Please report statistical significance for BLEU differences (e.g., bootstrap resampling).

- Question: Provide full efficiency/fairness details—wall-clock training time, tokens/sec, length limits, beam/search settings, and hardware for all systems.

- Question: Why is En→Fr bpe2char omitted? Add it and report En→Cs DCNMT training time; address speed tuning vs. bpe2char.

- Question: Provide formal morphology evaluations (boundary detection, probing, targeted agreement/inflection tests) instead of PCA anecdotes.

- Question: Ablate word encoder: compare learned-weight composition vs. last-state, max/avg pooling, CNN, and removing BiRNN for weights.

- Question: Ablate HGRU: compare to standard GRU/LSTM char decoder reinitialized at boundaries for speed/quality trade-offs.

- Question: Report character set coverage—% dev/test chars mapped to <unk> when limiting to 120 chars and impact on “pure character-level” claim.

- Additional: Use more accurate terminology for language morphology (e.g., “less morphologically inflectional”), define “energy” consistently, fix figure legends and explain peak detection.

- Additional: Define all acronyms at first mention, fix typos/grammar, complete references and dataset links, and state when results are author re-runs.

- Overall recommendation: Borderline accept pending stronger morphology evidence and complete, comparable baseline/efficiency evaluations.
