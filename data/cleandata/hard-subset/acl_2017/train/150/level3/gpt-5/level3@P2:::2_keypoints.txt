- Proposal: DCNMT — a character-level NMT with a morphology-aware word encoder (two RNNs) and a hierarchical two-level decoder (word RNN conditioning a settable-state HGRU).  
- Encoder novelty: decouples character composition and scalar weighting to bias toward morpheme-like units.  
- Decoder novelty: hierarchical word→char decoder using an HGRU to enable end-to-end character decoding without explicit segmentation.  
- Empirical claim: competitive BLEU with relatively small models and faster early-epoch gains than a BPE baseline (e.g., “1-epoch beats BPE”).  
- Open-vocabulary behavior: demonstrates handling of misspellings and nonce words (e.g., “convenienter”).  
- Qualitative morphology evidence: PCA plots, “energy” peaks, and segment-like boundary observations suggest learned morphological regularities.  
- Strength — motivation/architecture: the decoupling and hierarchical design are interesting, well-motivated, and practically useful.  
- Strength — efficiency: achieves strong BLEU with modest parameter count and promising optimization profile.  
- Strength — presentation: paper is generally readable with helpful figures and a useful batching trick.  
- Weakness — morphology evidence: claims of learning morphology are anecdotal and lack quantitative probing or segmentation evaluation.  
- Weakness — comparisons: empirical comparisons are incomplete/uneven (missing baselines, no significance testing, incomplete training-time and hardware details).  
- Weakness — overstated conclusions: some claims (e.g., character > subword) are not consistently supported by reported results.  
- Weakness — reproducibility: some reported numbers lack provenance, references and data links are incomplete, and setup details are missing.  
- Weakness — clarity and presentation: acronyms often undefined, “energy” not clearly defined in results, figure/color issues, typos, and insensitive phrasing about language morphology.  
- Questions for authors: provide quantitative morphology evaluations and clarify how “energies” are computed/normalized.  
- Questions for authors: explain omission of En→Fr bpe2char, reconcile Cs→En timing discrepancies, and report En→Cs training time, beam sizes, and hardware for all systems.  
- Questions for authors: clarify Table 1 Cs→En provenance and ensure consistent preprocessing/max-length/training settings across models.  
- Questions for authors: ablate HGRU vs. standard GRU/LSTM and report <unk> character rates and impact on diacritics/cross-lingual transfer.  
- Suggested fixes: move/soften qualitative morphology claims, define acronyms at first use, standardize capitalization, provide missing links/references, soften language about “morphologically poor” languages, and fix typos and figure legibility.  
- Overall: promising architectural contribution with competitive results, but needs stronger, more complete comparisons and quantitative morphology evidence to support key claims.
