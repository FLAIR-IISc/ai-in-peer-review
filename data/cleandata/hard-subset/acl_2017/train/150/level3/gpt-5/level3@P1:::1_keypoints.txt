- Proposes a hierarchical deep character-level NMT that learns word representations from characters and decodes characters conditioned on word-level states (morphology-aware encoder + HGRU decoder).
- Main architectural novelty: morphology-sensitive word encoder (two RNNs with learned positional “energy” weights) combined with a hierarchical character decoder using settable-state HGRU and a batching-friendly unfolding mechanism.
- Empirical claim: competitive BLEU on WMT En–Fr and En–Cs/Cs–En with strong early-data efficiency (matches/surpasses bpe baseline after one epoch).
- Shows qualitative evidence of morphemic generalization (PCA of embeddings, energy weights) and robustness to nonce/misspellings.
- Releases code and trained models for several language pairs to aid reproducibility.
- Avoids large target-word softmax and rare-word hacks by modeling at the character level while preserving word-level latent structure.
- HGRU + unfolding makes batched character-level decoding practical, addressing earlier char-model training inefficiencies.
- Moderate parameter count and faster training compared to some prior character-level systems; practical deployment benefits from hierarchical decoder.
- Lacks systematic comparison to strongest contemporary WMT systems; SOTA comparability claim is not well supported.
- Paper omits clear experimental detail in the text (exact dev/test sets, beam size, length penalties, early stopping, preprocessing).
- Architecture diagram (Fig. 2) and caption are unclear about the six-RNN design; mapping of layers is hard to follow.
- Missing quantitative morphology evaluations and key ablations isolating effects of energy-weighted encoder, HGRU, and target-word feedback.
- Released software has usability gaps (incomplete README, no --help) that hinder reproducibility despite code release.
- Requests for authors: confirm exact dev/test sets and preprocessing, and report beam sizes and decoding hyperparameters.
- Requests for ablations: replace energy-weighted encoder, replace HGRU with standard decoder, remove target-word feedback, and report effects on BLEU, speed, and robustness.
- Requests for runtime metrics: training/decoding speeds, GPU memory, epoch times, beam-search latency, and batching specifics vs. baselines and prior char models.
- Requests implementation details: how unfolding matrix is implemented, differentiability w.r.t. word boundaries during teacher forcing, and exposure-bias handling at word boundaries.
- Ask authors to contextualize BLEU versus contemporaneous WMT best systems and non-character NMT/PB-SMT SOTA.
- Request full runnable examples, end-to-end scripts, pretrained checkpoints, license, dependency stack, and a --help CLI.
- Overall recommendation: valuable and practical contribution to character-based NMT that would be stronger with clearer SOTA positioning, more experimental detail, ablations, and quantitative morphology evaluation.
