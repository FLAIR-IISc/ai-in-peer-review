- Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a novel word encoder (prefix RNN + energy weights) and a hierarchical decoder (HGRU + unfolding) for efficient batched character decoding.  
- Main contribution 1: a hierarchical char-level architecture that addresses large-vocabulary/OOV issues while remaining efficient to train.  
- Main contribution 2: empirical evidence that the model learns morphology and is robust to misspellings/nonce words, achieving competitive BLEU and surpassing a BPE baseline after one epoch.  
- Main contribution 3: release of code and pretrained models for multiple language pairs to support reproducibility.  
- Strength: strong design that removes large-vocab softmax bottleneck while preserving word-like conditioning and modest parameter counts.  
- Strength: clear, practical decoder innovation (HGRU + unfolding) enabling efficient single-pass batched char-level decoding.  
- Strength: compelling qualitative analyses (PCA, subword boundary detection, misspelling examples) showing learned morphological regularities.  
- Strength: reproducibility boosted by releasing code and models for En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En.  
- Weakness: empirical comparisons are incomplete and potentially unfair—missing strong non-char baselines and higher BLEU results on WMT leaderboards; some baselines not reimplemented under common settings.  
- Weakness: dataset/split specification is unclear (which WMT dev/test sets and filters were used).  
- Weakness: architecture presentation lacks clarity—Figure 2 and notation do not clearly show the stated six RNNs and some notation choices may confuse readers.  
- Weakness: reproducibility/usability gaps in software—no clear end-to-end example, missing --help, and scattered practical details (preprocessing, beam settings, hardware/time).  
- Weakness: minor writing and formatting issues (English disfluencies, typos, LaTeX problems) reduce polish.  
- Question: specify exact training/dev/test splits, sentence-length filtering, and training updates/epochs used for reported results.  
- Question: provide beam search settings (beam size, length normalization, early stopping) for all systems and confirm if identical across models.  
- Question: provide controlled efficiency metrics (wall-clock to fixed BLEU, chars/sec, GPU type) comparing the model to a strong BPE baseline trained by the authors.  
- Question: sensitivity analyses—how performance varies with character vocabulary size and with tanh weighting vs normalized weighting in the word encoder.  
- Question: provide ablations isolating contributions of the word encoder vs RNN/CNN and HGRU/unfolding vs simpler decoders.  
- Question: clarify how target-side “words” are segmented during training to define delimiters for the hierarchical decoder (e.g., whitespace tokenization?).  
- Additional comment: add a full architecture diagram labeling all six RNNs and data flows (character→subword→word→sentence→word-state→character).  
- Additional comment: discuss and cite stronger non-character baselines and contextualize final BLEU relative to contemporaneous WMT systems.  
- Additional comment: improve software packaging—add --help, a minimal working example from raw data to BLEU, default configs, and beam parameters; ensure versioned dependencies.  
- Additional comment: proofread for English and LaTeX typography and consider releasing scripts for morphology visualizations and subword boundary detections.
