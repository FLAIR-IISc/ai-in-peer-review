- Proposes a deep character-level NMT that induces morpheme- and word-level representations internally and begins/ends at characters.  
- Uses a two-RNN word encoder assigning “energy” weights to learn morpheme boundaries and composition.  
- Employs a hierarchical decoder with a word-level RNN conditioning a character-level HGRU for efficient character decoding.  
- Claims fast learning: competitive BLEU after one epoch and competitive final BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En.  
- Reports improved robustness to OOVs, misspellings, and nonce forms versus subword/word systems.  
- Releases code and final trained models for fr↔en and cs↔en to aid reproducibility.  
- Contribution: couples morphology-aware two-RNN encoder with hierarchical decoder enabling practical fully character-level MT.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence of plausible morphological tokenizations and strong early-epoch BLEU with a small model.  
- Contribution: provides reproducibility assets (code and models) for three translation directions.  
- Strength: architectural novelty—separating representation and energy to induce morphology without BPE.  
- Strength: early-epoch effectiveness and training efficiency valuable for low-compute settings.  
- Strength: demonstrated robustness to misspellings and nonce morphology via morpheme backoff.  
- Strength: analyses (PCA, energy profiles, segmentations) support that the encoder learns morphology.  
- Strength: public code/models lower replication barrier.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to strong non-character SOTA (competitive BPE-based systems and top WMT results).  
- Weakness: experimental/setup details (WMT year, tokenization, BLEU script, beam/length penalty) are not clearly stated.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 and architectural exposition are incomplete; HGRU and unfolding matrix need clearer, self-contained notation.  
- Weakness: README and software lack end-to-end runnable examples, scripts, and standard --help usage for reproducing results.  
- Weakness: minor writing, formatting, and LaTeX issues reduce readability.  
- Questions: request explicit dev/test set and evaluation details (WMT year, tokenization, case, BLEU script, beam settings).  
- Questions: ask for comparisons to competitive BPE-based NMT and discussion of gap to top matrix.statmt.org results.  
- Questions: request a complete architecture diagram showing all six RNNs and data flow, including unfolding mechanism.  
- Questions: ask for ablations testing the energy-weighted encoder vs. simple char-RNN, and the importance of word-level feedback in decoding.  
- Questions: request decoding speed benchmarks (chars/sec, sentences/sec) vs. BPE under reported beam settings and beam-width trade-offs.  
- Questions: ask about sensitivity to delimiter handling, HGRU reset mechanism, and whether LSTM variants were tried.  
- Additional comments: add runnable examples, --help flag, exact scripts, and random seeds to improve reproducibility.  
- Additional comments: report statistical significance for BLEU claims and temper SOTA-comparability claims while discussing when character-level modeling is most advantageous.  
- Overall: valuable contribution to character-based NMT with interesting architecture, practical early gains, and convincing morphological analyses, but needs clearer SOTA comparisons, architecture presentation, and documentation to be stronger.
