- Proposal: DCNMT — character-level NMT with a word encoder that composes characters into word representations and a hierarchical decoder that generates characters conditioned on word-level states.  
- Claims: combines character-level modeling with subword-like structure, improves training efficiency, and better captures morphology.  
- Results: competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En with small gains in some settings.  

- Primary contribution: deep word encoding/decoding layer integrating char composition with subword-like structure.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical comparison showing competitiveness with strong character/subword baselines and some efficiency advantages.  
- Tertiary contribution: qualitative analyses suggesting induction of morpheme-like segmentations.  

- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation and intuitive rationale for the architecture.  
- Strength: competitive translation quality across multiple language pairs with compact models and demonstrated handling of OOVs/misspellings.  
- Strength: some evidence of training efficiency (faster than some fully-character baselines; smaller model size).  
- Strength: interesting attempt to link character composition to morphology that could inspire follow-up work.  

- Weakness: morphology learning claims are largely anecdotal; “energy” is undefined and not formally validated.  
- Weakness: BLEU gains over bpe2char are very small and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: incomplete baseline/time reporting (bpe2char faster on Cs→En; missing En→Cs training time; En→Fr bpe2char not evaluated).  
- Weakness: presentation problems—many acronyms undefined at first use, inconsistent BPE capitalization, unclear “energy” description and Figure 5, problematic Figure 4a color mapping.  
- Weakness: Table 1 cites results not present in source paper for Cs→En without clarification; references incomplete; avoid labeling French “morphologically poor”; remove unnecessary notes.  

- Request: report statistical significance for BLEU and add En→Fr bpe2char and En→Cs training-time comparisons.  
- Request: precisely define “energy,” explain computation/normalization, interpret peaks, and fix Figures 4a and 5.  
- Request: clarify whether Table 1 Cs→En results for model (3) were recomputed and provide settings/code if so.  
- Request: provide formal morphology evaluations (e.g., morpheme boundary F1, paradigm clustering, or probing tasks).  
- Request: define all acronyms at first use, provide WMT’14/’15 corpus links, fix incomplete references, and rephrase “morphologically poor.”
