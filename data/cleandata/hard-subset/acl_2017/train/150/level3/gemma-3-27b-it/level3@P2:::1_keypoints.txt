- Proposes a novel deep character-level NMT with a dual-RNN word encoder and hierarchical character-level decoder to learn morphology and address large vocab/OOV issues.
- Releases code and trained models for En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En, improving reproducibility.
- Demonstrates ability to translate misspelled and nonce words via character-level representations.

- Strength: Dual-RNN encoder effectively captures morphological structure and yields organized embeddings for morphologically related words.
- Strength: Open-source code and models enhance impact and enable verification and follow-up work.
- Strength: Competitive BLEU scores and fast initial training (good performance after one epoch) compared to other character-level approaches.

- Weakness: Architecture description and Figure 2 are unclear and inconsistent with the claimed six-layer design, hindering understanding.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to strong non-character-level state-of-the-art NMT systems (e.g., WMT matrix), limiting evaluation context.
- Weakness: Released software has usability/documentation problems (no runnable examples, missing --help, README ambiguity about test/dev sets).

- Q: Provide a more detailed diagram (e.g., in appendix) clarifying the full six-layer architecture and component interactions.
- Q: Explain why comparisons with state-of-the-art non-character-level WMT systems were omitted.
- Q: What steps will you take to improve code usability and documentation (runnable examples, --help)?
- Q: Elaborate on challenges and benefits of adapting the approach to other tasks (speech recognition, summarization).

- Minor issues: English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX formatting problems should be corrected.
- Overall: Valuable contribution with promising morphology learning and OOV handling; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
