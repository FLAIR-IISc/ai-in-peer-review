- Proposal: Introduces DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT with a hierarchical two-RNN architecture (six-layer) to handle large vocabularies and OOVs.  
- Primary contribution: Combines character-level modeling with learned subword representations to avoid BPE and the large-vocabulary bottleneck.  
- Secondary contribution: Hierarchical decoder uses word-level guidance to make character-level generation feasible.  

- Strength: Novel architecture that integrates morphology learning via a dual RNN word encoder.  
- Strength: Competitive BLEU scores on En–Fr and En–Cs, comparable to state-of-the-art with relatively fewer parameters.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and helpful diagrams that illustrate the model.  
- Strength: Directly addresses vocabulary size and OOV issues in NMT without pre-defined subword units.  

- Weakness: Comparative analysis is limited; BLEU differences are small and statistical significance testing is missing, and training-time comparisons are incomplete.  
- Weakness: Claims about the model learning morphology are anecdotal and lack rigorous validation; PCA and energy analyses are insufficient.  
- Weakness: Paper has inconsistencies and missing details (undefined acronyms, inconsistent capitalization, unclear energy explanation).  
- Weakness: Figure 4a’s energy color mapping appears incorrect or incomplete, hindering interpretation.  

- Q: Provide statistical significance tests (e.g., bootstrap) for BLEU differences between DCNMT and baselines.  
- Q: What formal experiments validate the claim that DCNMT learns morphology, or will the authors tone/move the analysis?  
- Q: Clarify the definition of “energy” in Section 5.2 and explain the peaks in Figure 5.  
- Q: Provide the En–Cs training time for the bpe2char model to complete the Table 1 comparison.  
- Q: Share a link to the WMT’14 training corpora used in experiments.  

- Additional: Rephrase “morphologically poor” for French to a more neutral term.  
- Additional: Complete several incomplete references with full citation details.  
- Additional: Fix grammatical and typographical errors (e.g., “peak energy,” capitalization).  
- Additional: Clarify Table 1 Cs→En results for Chung et al. (2016) and confirm whether those results were reproduced by the authors.  

- Overall: Interesting and potentially valuable contribution but needs stronger comparative analysis, rigorous morphology validation, and fixes to inconsistencies before acceptance.
