- Proposes a hierarchical deep character-level NMT with a novel word encoder (two RNNs) and a character-level decoder to learn morphology and avoid large vocabularies.  
- Demonstrates competitive BLEU on En→Fr and En→Cs, with notable improvements even after a single training epoch.  
- Shows the model can learn morphological structure, handle unseen words, and cope with misspellings.  
- Releases code and trained models publicly, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Clear motivation highlighting limitations of word-level and purely character-level NMT approaches.  
- Experimental setup uses multiple language pairs and appropriate baselines for comparison.  
- Analysis includes visualizations (character energy, subword segmentation) supporting morphological learning claims.  
- Paper describes a six-layer architecture but the architecture figure (Figure 2) is insufficiently detailed.  
- Lacks explicit statement in the main text about which WMT test/dev sets were used (README mentions newstest2015/test and newstest2013/dev).  
- Software documentation is incomplete: no `--help` flag and README lacks runnable examples, hindering reproducibility.  
- Comparison to existing work is incomplete, omitting discussion of state-of-the-art non-character-based NMT and higher WMT leaderboard results.  
- Contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX errors that reduce readability.  
- Question: Please state explicitly which WMT test and development sets were used in the main paper.  
- Question: Provide a detailed comparison vs. state-of-the-art word- and character-based NMTs, including performance and computational trade-offs.  
- Question: Supply a high-resolution, detailed diagram showing all six recurrent layers and their interconnections.  
- Question: Will you release improved software documentation including a `--help` flag and runnable examples?  
- Suggest proofreading to fix language and LaTeX issues.  
- Suggest exploring deeper recurrent networks or longer sequence lengths to assess performance impact.  
- Recommend adding qualitative translation examples to illustrate strengths and weaknesses.
