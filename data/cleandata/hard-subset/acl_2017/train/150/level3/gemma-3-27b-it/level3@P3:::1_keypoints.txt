- Novel hierarchical character-level NMT architecture with two-RNN word encoder that can learn subword units automatically, mitigating large-vocabulary issues.
- Release of code and pre-trained models (fr-en, cs-en, en-cs), improving reproducibility.
- Empirical evidence that the model learns plausible morphological tokenizations (PCA analyses and examples).
- Good early training performance: reasonable BLEU after one epoch.
- Character-level design naturally handles OOV and nonce words, demonstrated with examples.
- Paper fails to explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (must be included in the paper).
- Released software lacks clear runnable examples and a helpful CLI (`--help`), hindering usability.
- Figure 2 and architecture visualization are insufficient and do not clearly reflect the described 6-level structure.
- Comparative evaluation is incomplete: missing state-of-the-art non-character-based MT baselines and no discussion of higher results on WMT/matrix.statmt.org.
- Minor presentation issues: English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX errors that reduce polish.
- Questions for authors: explicitly state datasets used, provide a detailed architecture diagram, justify omission of non-character baselines, address higher WMT benchmark results, and supply example commands to run the code.
