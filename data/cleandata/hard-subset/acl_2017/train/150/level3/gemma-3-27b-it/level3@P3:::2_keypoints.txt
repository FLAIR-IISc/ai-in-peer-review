- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures and a well-articulated motivation.  
- Strength: Novel DCNMT architecture that learns morphology via dedicated recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.  
- Strength: Promising initial BLEU improvements over bpe-based models on En–Fr and En–Cs after one epoch, claiming comparable performance to state of the art.  
- Strength: Valuable focus on modeling morphology within NMT and attempt to analyze learned morphological features.  

- Weakness: Reported BLEU improvements are very small and no statistical significance testing is provided.  
- Weakness: Claims about the model learning morphology are anecdotal and lack formal quantitative evaluation or rigorous experiments.  
- Weakness: Training-time comparisons are incomplete and potentially misleading (missing En→Cs for both models and En→Fr for bpe2char).  
- Weakness: Excessive or late-defined acronyms hinder readability (e.g., LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE).  
- Weakness: The “energy” concept in Section 5.2 is unclearly defined and its interpretation (peaks) is not well explained.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent capitalization of “BPE” throughout the paper.  

- Question: Provide statistical significance tests (e.g., bootstrap resampling) to validate BLEU differences.  
- Question: Report complete training times for En→Cs and En→Fr for both DCNMT and bpe2char.  
- Question: Describe formal experiments or probing tasks (e.g., morphological segmentation accuracy) to objectively evaluate morphology learning.  
- Question: Clearly define “energy” in Section 5.2 and explain how Figure 5’s peaks relate to morphological features.  
- Question: Clarify whether Cs→En results for Chung et al. (2016) were reproduced by the authors or taken from the original paper.  

- Additional: The note about not using a monolingual corpus in Section 4.1 is unnecessary.  
- Additional: Fix Figure 4a’s energy color mapping which appears incorrect or missing for some characters.  
- Additional: Rephrase the claim that French is “morphologically poor” to a less subjective phrasing.  
- Additional: Provide a direct link to the WMT’14 training corpora.  
- Additional: Correct incomplete references (e.g., “Bahdanau et al.,? 2015”).  
- Additional: Proofread and fix grammatical errors and typos (e.g., "convenienter," "UNk").  

- Overall: Promising and novel approach to character-level NMT with morphology focus, but weakened by lack of statistical validation, insufficient morphology evidence, and incomplete experimental comparisons.
