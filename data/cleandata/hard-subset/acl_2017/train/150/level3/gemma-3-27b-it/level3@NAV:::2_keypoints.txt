- Proposes a novel DCNMT architecture combining a character-level encoder with a hierarchical decoder to learn morphology.  
- Claims DCNMT captures subword information and learns morphology better than state-of-the-art, with competitive BLEU on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En.  
- Paper presentation is clear, well-structured, and motivations are well explained.  
- Explicitly modeling morphology within a character-level framework is an intriguing and valuable idea.  
- Initial results are promising and comparable to other character-level models, though not overwhelmingly superior.  
- Lacks rigorous statistical significance testing for reported BLEU improvements, especially versus bpe2char.  
- Training-time comparisons are incomplete: En→Cs missing, and bpe2char evaluation on En→Fr not conducted.  
- Morphology claims (Section 5.2) are anecdotal (visual examples/PCA) and lack formal, quantifiable experiments.  
- Inconsistent capitalization of “BPE” throughout the paper.  
- Numerous acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE) are undefined or defined after first use, hurting readability.  
- The note about not using a monolingual corpus (Section 4.1) is unclear and possibly unnecessary.  
- Describing French as “morphologically poor” is insensitive and should be rephrased.  
- Figure 4a’s energy color mapping appears incomplete or incorrect and needs correction.  
- Table 1 cites results for model (3) in Chung et al. (2016) that do not appear in the original paper; clarification required on how those results were obtained.  
- Requests for authors: provide statistical significance testing (e.g., bootstrap resampling) for BLEU.  
- Requests: complete training-time comparisons including En→Cs and bpe2char on En→Fr.  
- Requests: explain how model (3) results from Chung et al. (2016) were obtained.  
- Requests: consider moving or tempering Section 5.2 morphology analysis given lack of formal validation.  
- Requests: supply a link to the WMT’14 training corpora for reproducibility.
