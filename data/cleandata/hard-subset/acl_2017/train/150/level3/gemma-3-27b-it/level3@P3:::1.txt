This paper presents a novel character-level neural machine translation (NMT) model that incorporates morphological information via a two-RNN word encoder and a hierarchical decoder. The primary contribution lies in the architecture's ability to learn subword units automatically, avoiding large vocabulary issues while maintaining reasonable translation performance, even with limited training. A secondary contribution is the release of code and models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, facilitating reproducibility. Finally, the empirical demonstration that the model learns plausible morphological tokenizations is noteworthy.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel Architecture:** The proposed hierarchical architecture, combining character and subword representations, is a strong point. The use of two RNNs in the word encoder – one for preceding characters and another for their weights – offers a potentially effective way to learn morphological structure. This addresses a key limitation of purely character-level models: their difficulty in capturing meaningful subword units.
2. **Reproducibility:** The authors’ release of code and pre-trained models is a significant strength, particularly in the NMT field where reproducibility can be challenging. This allows other researchers to build upon their work and verify their results.
3. **Early Performance & Morphology Learning:** The observation that the model achieves reasonable BLEU scores even after a single training epoch is encouraging. More importantly, the evidence suggesting the model learns plausible morphological tokenizations (demonstrated with PCA and examples in Section 5.2) supports the core hypothesis of the paper. The examples involving “convenienter” and analyses of character weights are convincing.
4. **Handles OOV & Nonce Words:** The inherent advantage of character-level models in handling out-of-vocabulary (OOV) and nonce words is highlighted, and the authors provide examples demonstrating the model’s capability in this regard. This is a practical benefit of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Missing Dataset Details:** A critical omission is the lack of explicit specification of the WMT test/dev sets used. While the README file indicates newstest2015/test and newstest2013/dev, this information *must* be stated directly within the paper for clarity and reproducibility.
2. **Insufficient Documentation & Tooling:** The software documentation (README) lacks clear, runnable examples, hindering usability. Furthermore, the absence of a `--help` flag for the tool makes it more difficult for users to understand and utilize the code.
3. **Unclear Architecture Visualization:** Figure 2, intended to illustrate the model architecture, is insufficient. The paper describes a 6-level architecture, but the figure appears to show fewer layers and lacks the detail necessary for a clear understanding. A more comprehensive architecture diagram, even in the appendix, is essential.
4. **Incomplete Comparison:** The comparisons with other models are incomplete. The omission of state-of-the-art *non*-character-based MT systems makes it difficult to assess the relative merits of the proposed approach. More crucially, the paper does not acknowledge or discuss higher results reported on WMT/matrix.statmt.org, which limits the context of the presented BLEU scores.
5. **Minor Presentation Issues:** The paper suffers from minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX errors (e.g., reversed quotation marks) that detract from its overall polish.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Please explicitly state the WMT test and development sets used in the experiments within the main body of the paper.
2. Can you provide a more detailed architecture diagram (e.g., in the appendix) that accurately reflects the 6-level structure described in the paper?
3. Why were state-of-the-art non-character-based NMT systems not included in the comparative analysis? How do you position your work relative to these systems?
4. Please address the higher results reported on WMT/matrix.statmt.org. How do your findings relate to these existing benchmarks?
5. Could you provide example commands to run the released code and demonstrate its functionality?
