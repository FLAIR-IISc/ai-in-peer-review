- Proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT with a two-RNN word encoder and hierarchical decoder to handle morphology and large-vocabulary issues.  
- Demonstrates competitive BLEU scores on En→Fr and En→Cs, suggesting model effectiveness.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured paper with an original architecture addressing morphology and OOV words.  
- Strength: experimental results are promising and the authors attempt analysis of morphology learning.  
- Strength: includes (incomplete) training time comparisons and a well-articulated motivation.  
- Weakness: morphology-learning claims are anecdotal and lack rigorous quantitative validation.  
- Weakness: the "energy" concept and peaks in Figure 3 are not clearly explained or interpreted.  
- Weakness: claim that DCNMT learns morphology better than alternatives is insufficiently substantiated.  
- Weakness: comparison with bpe2char is incomplete—small BLEU differences, no statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: training-time comparison incomplete/missing (En→Cs DCNMT time, En→Fr bpe2char), and bpe2char’s 8-day faster Cs→En result is not addressed.  
- Minor issues: many acronyms undefined or defined late, inconsistent “BPE” capitalization, and unnecessary statements (e.g., about monolingual corpora).  
- Presentation errors: Figure 4a color mapping issues and Table 1’s Cs→En Chung et al. values require clarification.  
- Tone issue: phrasing like “French is morphologically poor” is misleading and should be reworded.  
- Main contributions: novel deep character-level NMT architecture enabling character-level translation without high computational cost; exploration of morphology learning in NMT.  
- Questions for authors: provide BLEU significance tests, full training-time comparisons, clarify “energy” and Figure 3 peaks, verify Chung et al. Cs→En numbers, and discuss benefits of deeper recurrent networks.  
- Additional requests: fix grammatical/typographical errors, provide WMT’14 data link, correct incomplete references, and move/soften or expand the anecdotal morphology analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions focused on validating morphology claims and completing the comparative evaluation.
