- Proposes a deep character-level NMT model that uses two recurrent networks to learn morphology (preceding characters + weighting) combined with a hierarchical decoder.  
- Aims to learn subword units automatically without BPE, using six recurrent networks in total.

- Main contribution is the novel architecture coupling a morphology-aware word encoder with a hierarchical decoder.  
- Shows comparable BLEU performance to state-of-the-art on En–Fr and En–Cs with smaller model size and faster training.

- Provides visualizations and examples indicating the model learns morphological patterns and handles OOV words inherently.

- Strength: Novel architecture that explicitly models morphology rather than only applying character RNNs.  
- Strength: Competitive BLEU scores with a simpler, faster-to-train model.  
- Strength: Empirical visualizations supporting morphology learning.  
- Strength: Naturally addresses OOV word handling.

- Weakness: Individual components (hierarchical decoder, RNNs) are not novel; novelty is mainly in their combination.  
- Weakness: Lacks thorough ablation studies to quantify each component’s contribution.  
- Weakness: No clear analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity or tuning details.

- Questions: Provide detailed computational cost comparison (training time, memory) versus other character/BPE models.  
- Questions: How does performance vary across languages with different morphological richness and were other language pairs tested?  
- Questions: Why choose tanh for normalization in the word encoder and how does it compare to other normalization methods?
