- Proposes a deep character-level NMT with two RNNs for character representation/weighting and a hierarchical character-level decoder.  
- Main contribution: novel encoder that learns morphology via weighted character representations combined with a hierarchical decoder.  
- Empirical validation on En–Fr and En–Cs showing competitive BLEU and gains over BPE after one epoch.  
- Claims the model learns morphology, supported by inconclusive PCA and translation examples.  
- Strength: addresses large-vocabulary/OOV issues and is suitable for morphologically rich languages.  
- Strength: architectural novelty in combining weighted character representations with a hierarchical decoder.  
- Strength: competitive performance relative to state-of-the-art, especially early in training.  
- Strength: training efficiency and smaller model size compared to other character-level models; encoding length comparable to word models.  
- Weakness: individual components (RNNs, hierarchical decoder, attention) are standard; novelty is largely combinatorial.  
- Weakness: morphology-learning analysis is superficial and not rigorously demonstrated.  
- Weakness: lacks ablation studies to justify design choices (e.g., impact of weighted char representations).  
- Weakness: comparisons limited to BPE and older models; missing stronger recent baselines (e.g., transformer-based character models).  
- Question: which characters consistently receive higher encoder weights and do they map to morphemes?  
- Question: sensitivity of results to character vocabulary size?  
- Question: computational cost vs. similarly performing word-level NMT (training time, memory)?  
- Question: why use tanh for weight normalization instead of softmax?  
- Additional: writing could be more concise and include generated character-sequence examples to illustrate the hierarchical decoder.  
- Additional: release of code and data would improve reproducibility.
