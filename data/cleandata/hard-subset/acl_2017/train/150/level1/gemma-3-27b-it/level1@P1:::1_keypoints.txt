- Proposed a hierarchical character-level NMT combining two RNNs for morphological encoding with a hierarchical decoder to avoid large-vocabulary issues.
- Emphasized explicit morphology learning within NMT rather than relying on subword units like BPE, and provided analysis to support this.
- Claimed efficient training of deep character-level models with competitive BLEU scores using a relatively small model.

- Clearly motivates the large-vocabulary and OOV problems of word-level NMT and justifies a character-level approach.
- Introduces novel architectural components (two-RNN word encoder, hierarchical decoder, HGRU) tailored for character-level generation.
- Provides empirical results on multiple language pairs showing competitive performance and reports efficiency gains (Table 1).
- Offers analysis (PCA, character energies, example translations) suggesting the model captures morphological information.
- Addresses common training challenges for deep character-level models and reports successful mitigation.

- Individual components are not fundamentally novel; the main novelty is their specific integration.
- PCA visualization is subjective and lacks quantitative/statistical validation of learned morphological representations.
- Comparison to stronger, more recent subword baselines (e.g., Unigram LM) is missing.
- Lacks ablation studies to isolate contributions of each architectural component.
- Error analysis is limited; more comprehensive evaluation of failures and weaknesses is needed.

- Request quantitative evaluation of learned morphological representations (e.g., predicting morphological features).
- Ask for comparison against recent subword NMT models such as Unigram Language Model (ULM).
- Request detailed computational resource and training cost breakdown (GPU memory, training time) per language pair.
- Ask for justification and ablation of the tanh activation in the weight normalization step and whether alternatives were tried.

- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and references are comprehensive.
- Strong motivation and idea, but empirical evaluation needs to be more rigorous.
