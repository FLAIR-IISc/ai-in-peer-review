- Contribution: Word encoder using two RNNs learns morphemes and their combinations, producing meaningful word representations without a fixed vocabulary.  
- Contribution: Hierarchical decoder efficiently handles character-level translation without the two-pass training of prior models.  
- Contribution: Empirical evidence shows competitive BLEU scores while avoiding out-of-vocabulary issues.

- Strength: DCNMT achieves higher BLEU than BPE-based models after one epoch, indicating a practical efficiency advantage.  
- Strength: Morphological analysis (Fig. 3) shows encoder clusters semantically similar words more clearly than standard RNN word encoders.  
- Strength: Hierarchical decoder design avoids prior character-level inefficiencies, making character-level translation more practical.

- Weakness: The one-epoch BLEU claim lacks details about the specific BPE baseline and hyperparameters used for comparison.  
- Weakness: Despite having six RNNs, the paper does not explain why DCNMT trains more efficiently, undermining the efficiency claim.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to English–Czech, leaving generalization to more morphologically complex languages unclear.

- Question: Which specific BPE baseline and hyperparameters were used for the one-epoch comparison?  
- Question: How would the model handle agglutinative languages like Turkish, and what modifications would be required?
