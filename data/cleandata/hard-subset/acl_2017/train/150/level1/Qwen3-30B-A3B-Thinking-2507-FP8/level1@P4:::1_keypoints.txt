- Summary: Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a morpheme-aware word encoder, hierarchical decoder, and evidence of learned morphology with competitive BLEU.

- Contribution: Morpheme-aware word encoder that learns subword units and combinations via two RNNs, removing need for external segmentation.

- Contribution: Hierarchical decoder (HGRU) that efficiently generates character sequences from word-level representations.

- Contribution: Empirical evidence that the model learns meaningful morphological representations (PCA, nonce-word translation) while achieving competitive BLEU.

- Strength: Addresses OOV problem via morphological learning; PCA shows similar words cluster in embedding space.

- Strength: Competitive BLEU and faster initial training—outperforms BPE models after one epoch on En-Fr and En-Cs dev sets.

- Strength: HGRU and unfolding technique make character-level decoding more efficient and practical for batch training.

- Weakness: Lacks rigorous definition/demonstration of “learning morphology” and whether grammatical morphological rules are captured.

- Weakness: Training-efficiency claim is misleading—no comparison to most efficient BPE models and model size (54M) is comparable or larger.

- Weakness: No ablation studies isolating the contribution of morphological learning versus the hierarchical decoder for improved performance.

- Question: How do you define “learning morphology” and which specific morphological features (prefixes, suffixes, etc.) does the encoder capture quantitatively?

- Question: Can you provide ablations to determine whether morphological learning or the hierarchical decoder drives the one-epoch advantage?

- Question: How does the model perform on languages with very rich morphology (e.g., Finnish, Turkish)?
