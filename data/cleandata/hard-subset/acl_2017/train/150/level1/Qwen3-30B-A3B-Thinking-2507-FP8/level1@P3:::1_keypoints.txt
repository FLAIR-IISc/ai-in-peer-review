- Morphology-aware word encoding using two RNNs to learn morphemes and compose word representations.
- Hierarchical decoder enabling character-level translation with training efficiency comparable to word-level models.
- Demonstrated practical OOV handling by translating misspelled and nonce words (e.g., "convenienter").

- Novel morphology learning evidenced by PCA visualizations showing morphologically similar words cluster together.
- Significantly faster training than previous character-level models while achieving comparable BLEU scores.
- Practical advantage of translating OOVs/misspellings instead of producing <unk>.

- Novelty overstated; approach is similar in spirit to BPE and not clearly differentiated from subword methods.
- Misleading claim about avoiding large vocabularies — the model still uses a character vocabulary; main gain is automatic segmentation.
- Limited experimental scope (only En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En) and missing evaluation on highly agglutinative languages and recent baselines.
- Questionable significance of faster one-epoch BLEU gains since final scores are comparable to BPE; unclear scaling behavior.
- No ablation studies to isolate contributions of the morphology encoder versus the hierarchical decoder.

- How does the model compare to more recent state-of-the-art NMT systems (ACL/WMT 2016+)?
- How would you quantitatively measure the quality of learned morphology?
- Can you provide comprehensive statistics on how often and how well the model translates misspellings/nonce words versus other models?

- Overall: promising, practical approach with convincing OOV handling and speed benefits, but novelty, experimental breadth, and analysis need clarification—recommend accept with minor revisions.
