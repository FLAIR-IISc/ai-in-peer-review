- Proposal: DCNMT — character-composed word encoder + hierarchical word->character decoder with an unfolding batching trick; claims comparable BLEU on WMT En–Fr/En–Cs/Cs–En.  
- Contribution: Practical hierarchical character decoder with a “settable-state” HGRU and efficient batching/unfolding enabling end-to-end character decoding.  
- Contribution: Character-to-word encoder computing a weighted sum over prefix RNN states, with weights predicted by a BiRNN to capture morpheme-like composition.  
- Contribution: Empirical claims of competitive BLEU, smaller model size (~54M), faster training, and robustness to misspellings/nonce words.  
- Strength: Addresses character-level training efficiency via a clear, implementable unfolding matrix R and hierarchical decoder.  
- Strength: Shows favorable parameter-efficiency — smaller model yet BLEU close to char-level baselines.  
- Strength: Demonstrates qualitative handling of OOVs and nonce words (recovering misspellings, compositional generation).  
- Strength: Provides suggestive visualizations that the encoder separates stems and affixes better than a plain char-RNN.  
- Weakness: Misaligned claims and results — En–Fr “one epoch beats BPE” claim contradicted by Table 1 and inconsistent baselines.  
- Weakness: Comparisons use numbers from different papers/hardware/preprocessing, making training-time and BLEU claims non-standardized and hard to trust.  
- Weakness: Word-encoder weighting is confusing and possibly incorrect — wt use, non-normalized tanh on weighted sum, and length-dependent scaling raise stability/interpretability concerns.  
- Weakness: HGRU equations and notation are unclear/possibly inconsistent (gating indices, delimiter signal roles) and need clearer derivation and ablations.  
- Weakness: Morphology evidence is anecdotal (PCA plots, a few examples) with no quantitative segmentation/probing evaluation.  
- Weakness: Missing experimental details harming reproducibility (beam size, length normalization, gradient clipping, dropout, early stopping, code release, inconsistent max lengths).  
- Weakness: Efficiency claims are not controlled; need same-hardware, same-data throughput and convergence comparisons.  
- Question: Reconcile En–Fr one-epoch claim vs Table 1 — which BPE baseline was trained or reproduced on your setup?  
- Question: How are weights wt actually used/normalized; why use exp() then tanh; did you try attention/softmax or alternatives like ReLU?  
- Question: Provide ablations for (a) your weighted encoder vs plain char-RNN, (b) hierarchical decoder with/without unfolding, and (c) HGRU vs standard GRU/LSTM with delimiter resets.  
- Question: Decoding specifics — character-level beam size, length normalization/penalties, and mechanisms to avoid over/under-generation.  
- Question: Can you provide quantitative morphology evaluations (e.g., MorphoChallenge, CELEX, inflection probing) comparing to CNN/RNN char encoders and BPE?  
- Question: Report controlled efficiency metrics: chars/sec, steps/wall-clock to reach BLEU milestones on a fixed GPU for your model and baselines.  
- Additional comment: Paper needs proofreading — several typos and notation inconsistencies impede understanding.  
- Overall: Moderate novelty — hierarchical decoder and batching trick are useful engineering contributions, but clarity, controlled baselines, and stronger quantitative morphology/efficiency evaluations are needed to strengthen the case.
