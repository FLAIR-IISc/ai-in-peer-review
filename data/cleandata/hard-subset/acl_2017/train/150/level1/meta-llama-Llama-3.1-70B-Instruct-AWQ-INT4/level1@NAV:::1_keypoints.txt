- Summary: Proposes DCNMT — a character-level NMT with two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to learn morphology, addressing large-vocab/OOV issues; tested on En→Fr, En→Cs, Cs→En with BLEU > bpe and comparable to SOTA.
- Contribution: Novel architecture combining two RNNs and a hierarchical decoder to learn morphology at the character level.
- Contribution: Efficient training — achieves higher BLEU than bpe after a single epoch.
- Contribution: Demonstrates morphology learning via analysis of the word encoder and hierarchical decoder.
- Strength: Improved performance over bpe baseline and comparable to state-of-the-art systems.
- Strength: Fast/efficient training with strong early results.
- Strength: Empirical evidence the model captures morphological structure.
- Strength: Better handling of OOV words than word-level models.
- Strength: Simple and small model size, making it efficient to train.
- Weakness: Model complexity — six RNNs may complicate training and optimization.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation — only three language pairs, raising questions about generality.
- Weakness: No comparisons to other character-level models, hindering relative evaluation.
- Question: How does the model handle words with multiple morphemes?
- Question: Can the model be applied to other NLP tasks (e.g., text summarization, speech recognition)?
- Question: How does the model perform on languages with more complex morphology (e.g., Arabic, Russian)?
