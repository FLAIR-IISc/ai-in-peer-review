**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model is designed to address the issues of large vocabulary and out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words in traditional word-level neural machine translation models. The DCNMT model consists of six recurrent neural networks, divided into four layers, and uses a hierarchical decoder to generate character sequences. The model is trained on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks, and the results show that it achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model and is comparable to state-of-the-art systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The paper proposes a new architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.
2. **Efficient Training**: The model is able to train efficiently, achieving a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for only one epoch.
3. **Learning Morphology**: The model is able to learn morphology, which is demonstrated through the analysis of the word encoder and the hierarchical decoder.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The DCNMT model achieves a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model and is comparable to state-of-the-art systems.
2. **Efficient Training**: The model is able to train efficiently, achieving a higher BLEU score than the bpe-based model after training for only one epoch.
3. **Ability to Learn Morphology**: The model is able to learn morphology, which is demonstrated through the analysis of the word encoder and the hierarchical decoder.
4. **Handling OOV Words**: The model is able to handle OOV words, which is a significant advantage over traditional word-level neural machine translation models.
5. **Simple and Small Model Size**: The model is the simplest and smallest one in terms of model size, making it efficient to train.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity of the Model**: The model consists of six recurrent neural networks, which may make it difficult to train and optimize.
2. **Limited Analysis**: The paper only analyzes the performance of the model on three language pairs, which may not be representative of its performance on other language pairs.
3. **No Comparison with Other Character-Level Models**: The paper does not compare the performance of the DCNMT model with other character-level models, which may make it difficult to evaluate its performance.
