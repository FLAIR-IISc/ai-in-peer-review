**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) by learning morphology. The model consists of six recurrent neural networks, divided into four layers: a source word encoder, a bidirectional RNN sentence encoder, a first-level decoder, and a second-level decoder. The word encoder utilizes two RNNs to learn the representation of morphemes and their combinations. The hierarchical decoder decodes at the character level, using a variant of the gate recurrent unit (GRU) to generate character sequences. The model is trained end-to-end and achieves state-of-the-art performance on English-French and English-Czech translation tasks.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The proposed DCNMT architecture is a significant contribution, as it learns morphology and decodes at the character level, avoiding the large vocabulary issue and improving training efficiency.
2. **Word Encoder**: The word encoder, which utilizes two RNNs to learn morphemes and their combinations, is a key innovation, enabling the model to generate meaningful representations of words.
3. **Hierarchical Decoder**: The hierarchical decoder, which uses a variant of GRU to generate character sequences, is an important contribution, as it allows for efficient decoding at the character level.

**Strengths**

1. **State-of-the-Art Performance**: The DCNMT model achieves state-of-the-art performance on English-French and English-Czech translation tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
2. **Efficient Training**: The model is efficient to train, consisting of only 54 million parameters, which is smaller than other character-level models.
3. **Learning Morphology**: The model is able to learn morphology, which is demonstrated through the analysis of word representations and the translation of misspelled and nonce words.
4. **Flexibility**: The model can be applied to other tasks, such as speech recognition and text summarization, due to its character-level architecture.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational Complexity**: Although the model is efficient to train, it still requires significant computational resources, which may be a limitation for some users.
2. **Limited Analysis**: The paper could benefit from more in-depth analysis of the model's performance, such as error analysis and comparison with other models.
3. **Lack of Theoretical Insights**: The paper does not provide theoretical insights into why the model is effective, which could be an area for future research.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the implementation of the word encoder and the hierarchical decoder?
2. How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary words, and what are the implications for the translation quality?
3. Have you considered applying the model to other languages or tasks, and what are the potential challenges and opportunities?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its components. The experiments and analysis demonstrate the effectiveness of the model, and the discussion provides insights into the implications of the results. However, as mentioned earlier, the paper could benefit from more in-depth analysis and theoretical insights to further understand the model's behavior.
