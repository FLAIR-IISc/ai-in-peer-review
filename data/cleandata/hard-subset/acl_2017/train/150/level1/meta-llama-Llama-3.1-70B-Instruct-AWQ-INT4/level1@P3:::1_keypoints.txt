- Proposes DCNMT: a character-level NMT that learns morphology using six RNNs organized into four layers (word encoder, biRNN sentence encoder, first- and second-level decoders).  
- Word encoder uses two RNNs to learn morphemes and their combinations for word representations.  
- Hierarchical decoder employs a GRU variant to generate character sequences efficiently.  
- Trained end-to-end and reports state-of-the-art results on English–French and English–Czech.  
- Novel architecture avoids large vocabulary issues and improves training efficiency.  
- Word encoder is a key innovation enabling meaningful morphological word representations.  
- Hierarchical decoder is important for efficient character-level decoding.  
- Strength: Achieves SOTA performance, showing model effectiveness.  
- Strength: Relatively efficient to train with only 54 million parameters.  
- Strength: Demonstrates morphology learning via representation analysis and handling misspellings/nonce words.  
- Strength: Flexible architecture potentially applicable to tasks like speech recognition and summarization.  
- Weakness: Still computationally intensive despite parameter efficiency.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks in-depth error analysis and broader comparisons.  
- Weakness: Missing theoretical insights explaining why the model works.  
- Question: Provide more implementation details for the word encoder and hierarchical decoder.  
- Question: How are out-of-vocabulary words handled and how does that affect translation quality?  
- Question: Have you applied the model to other languages or tasks; what challenges and opportunities arise?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but would benefit from deeper analysis and theoretical discussion.
