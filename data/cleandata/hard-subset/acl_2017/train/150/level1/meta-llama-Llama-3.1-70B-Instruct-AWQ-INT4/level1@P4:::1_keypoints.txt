- Summary: Proposes DCNMT, a six-recurrent-network hierarchical character-level NMT trained end-to-end, claiming to learn morphology and outperform BPE-based models.  
- Contribution 1 (Novel Architecture): Introduces a novel two-recurrent-network morphology-learning architecture with a hierarchical character-level decoder.  
- Contribution 2 (Efficient Training): Claims more efficient training than word-based and conventional character-based models.  
- Contribution 3 (Improved Performance): Reports higher BLEU than BPE-based models and comparable results to state-of-the-art systems.  
- Strength 1: Addresses the large vocabulary problem inherent in word-based NMT.  
- Strength 2: Claims significant training efficiency advantages.  
- Strength 3: Demonstrates improved BLEU scores versus BPE, suggesting effectiveness.  
- Weakness 1: Model complexity — consists of six recurrent networks, potentially hard to train and optimize.  
- Weakness 2: Limited evaluation — experiments only on En–Fr and En–Cs, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness 3: Missing comparisons to other character-level models, relying mainly on BPE baseline.  
- Question 1: How will the approach be extended to other language pairs and tasks?  
- Question 2: Please provide more details on training procedure and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Question 3: How do the authors plan to reduce model complexity and improve training/optimization efficiency?
