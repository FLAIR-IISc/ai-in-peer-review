**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology using two recurrent neural networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model consists of six recurrent neural networks and is able to efficiently train and achieve high translation performance comparable to state-of-the-art systems. The authors evaluate their model on English-to-French, English-to-Czech, and Czech-to-English translation tasks and show that it outperforms other character-level models and is competitive with word-level models.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation**: The authors propose a hierarchical architecture that learns morphology using two recurrent neural networks and a hierarchical decoder, which is able to efficiently train and achieve high translation performance.
2. **Efficient training of deep character-level models**: The authors show that their model can be trained efficiently using a variant of the gate recurrent unit (GRU) and a matrix to unfold the outputs of the first-level decoder.
3. **Learning morphology**: The authors demonstrate that their model is able to learn morphology and generate meaningful representations of words, which is useful for translating misspelled or nonce words.

**Strengths**

3. **Competitive performance**: The authors' model achieves competitive performance with state-of-the-art systems, which demonstrates its effectiveness.
4. **Ability to translate misspelled or nonce words**: The authors' model is able to translate misspelled or nonce words, which is a significant advantage over word-level models.
