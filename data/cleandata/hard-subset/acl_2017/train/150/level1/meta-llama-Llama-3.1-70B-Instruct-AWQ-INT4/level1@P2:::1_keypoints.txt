- Summary: Proposes DCNMT — a six-RNN, hierarchical character-level NMT to learn morphology, claiming higher BLEU than BPE and comparable to state-of-the-art.

- Contribution: Novel architecture using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to learn morphology.

- Contribution: Efficient training strategy using a GRU variant and a matrix to unfold first-level decoder outputs.

- Contribution: Demonstrates morphology learning via character energy analysis and morpheme-combination rules.

- Strength: Empirical improvement — higher BLEU than BPE and comparable to SOTA systems.

- Strength: Training shown to be efficient with the proposed GRU variant and unfolding matrix.

- Strength: Model appears able to learn morphological structure from characters.

- Strength: Can translate misspelled words correctly, unlike word-based systems.

- Strength: Can translate nonce words by concatenating morphemes and applying learned rules.

- Weakness: High model complexity (six RNNs) may make training and optimization difficult.

- Weakness: Morphology analysis is based on limited examples and may not generalize.

- Weakness: Comparisons limited to a few baselines, not a broad range of competing models.

- Weakness: No theoretical analysis explaining why the model works or its underlying mechanisms.

- Question: How will the model be extended to handle complex/non-linear morphology or multi-word morphological phenomena?

- Question: How will training efficiency be further improved (better optimizers, parallelization, etc.)?

- Question: Can you provide broader analysis of morphology learning (e.g., character energy and morpheme rules) on larger datasets?

- Question: Will you compare against more models and use more robust evaluation metrics?

- Question: Can you provide a theoretical analysis of the model’s performance and underlying mechanisms?
