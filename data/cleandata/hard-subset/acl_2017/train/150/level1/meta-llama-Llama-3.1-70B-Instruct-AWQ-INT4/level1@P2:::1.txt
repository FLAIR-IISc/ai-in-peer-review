**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (DCNMT) that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model consists of six recurrent neural networks and is designed to address the issues of large vocabulary and out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words in traditional word-level neural machine translation models. The authors claim that their model achieves a higher BLEU score than the byte pair encoding (BPE) based model and is comparable to the state-of-the-art systems.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Architecture**: The authors propose a new architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation that learns morphology by using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.
2. **Efficient Training**: The authors demonstrate that their model can be efficiently trained using a variant of the gate recurrent unit (GRU) and a matrix to unfold the outputs of the first-level decoder.
3. **Learning Morphology**: The authors show that their model can learn morphology by analyzing the energy of each character and the rules of how morphemes are combined.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The authors demonstrate that their model achieves a higher BLEU score than the BPE-based model and is comparable to the state-of-the-art systems.
2. **Efficient Training**: The authors show that their model can be efficiently trained using a variant of the GRU and a matrix to unfold the outputs of the first-level decoder.
3. **Ability to Learn Morphology**: The authors demonstrate that their model can learn morphology by analyzing the energy of each character and the rules of how morphemes are combined.
4. **Ability to Translate Misspelled Words**: The authors show that their model can translate misspelled words correctly, which is not possible for word-based translators.
5. **Ability to Translate Nonce Words**: The authors demonstrate that their model can translate nonce words correctly by concatenating morphemes and applying the rules of how they are combined.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity of the Model**: The authors' model consists of six recurrent neural networks, which may be difficult to train and optimize.
2. **Limited Analysis**: The authors' analysis of the model's ability to learn morphology is limited to a few examples and may not be representative of the model's performance on all types of text.
3. **Comparison to Other Models**: The authors' comparison of their model to other models is limited to a few baseline models and may not be representative of the model's performance compared to all other models.
4. **Lack of Theoretical Analysis**: The authors do not provide a theoretical analysis of their model's performance and may not fully understand the underlying mechanisms of their model.
