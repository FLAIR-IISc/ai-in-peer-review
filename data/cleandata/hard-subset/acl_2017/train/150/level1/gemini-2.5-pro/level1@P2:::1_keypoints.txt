- Proposes a deep purely character-level NMT with a six-RNN hierarchical architecture to address OOVs and large-vocabulary training inefficiencies.  
- Introduces a source-side word encoder that composes word vectors from characters using two RNNs and learned weights to capture morphology.  
- Presents a two-level hierarchical decoder: a word-level RNN predicts word representations, each initializing a character-level HGRU to generate word spellings.  
- Evaluated on En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En, achieving BLEU competitive with state-of-the-art character/subword models and claiming faster training.  
- Contribution 1: a novel hierarchical decoder that lets attention operate at word-level length while generating characters, with an HGRU and unfolding for efficient batching.  
- Contribution 2: a morphology-aware dual-RNN word encoder that reportedly yields more structured, morpheme-sensitive representations than simple RNN encoders.  
- Strength: elegant, well-motivated architecture that separates “which word” (word-level) from “how to spell it” (character-level), addressing long-sequence training issues.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and notable training efficiency, e.g., outperforming BPE baseline after one epoch on some tasks.  
- Strength: convincing qualitative analyses (PCA, internal-weight visualizations, examples translating misspellings/nonce words) supporting learned morphology claims.  
- Weakness: high model complexity (six RNNs) and insufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of encoder vs. hierarchical decoder.  
- Weakness: key design choices lack justification, e.g., using tanh (not softmax) for encoder weights and the custom HGRU/state-resetting without exploring alternatives.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation on morphologically diverse languages; agglutinative languages like Turkish or Finnish would be stronger tests.  
- Questions for authors: provide ablation studies isolating the word encoder and decoder contributions (e.g., pair with CNN encoder or flat decoder).  
- Question for authors: justify the use of tanh instead of softmax for the encoder’s weighting mechanism and explain its benefits.  
- Question for authors: report experiments comparing HGRU state-resetting to alternatives (e.g., concatenating word state to character inputs) and their impacts.
