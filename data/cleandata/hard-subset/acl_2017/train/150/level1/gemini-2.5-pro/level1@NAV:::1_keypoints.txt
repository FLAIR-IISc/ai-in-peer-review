- Novel hierarchical decoder: a word-level RNN guides a character-level HGRU, with a batching method making the six-RNN deep architecture practical to train.  
- Character-based word encoder: two-RNN encoder that weights character prefix representations to explicitly learn morphology, supported by qualitative evidence.  
- Training efficiency: despite depth, the model trains much faster than other character-level models and achieves strong results after a single epoch.  
- Convincing qualitative analysis: PCA of embeddings and visualizations of encoder weights show the model captures meaningful subword/morphological structure.  
- Robustness to misspellings and nonce words: the fully character-level approach handles misspellings and novel morphological forms better than subword (BPE) models.  
- Missing ablation studies: no experiments isolating the word encoder and hierarchical decoder to quantify individual contributions.  
- Questionable weighting design: use of an unnormalized exponential for character weighting is not well justified and may cause unstable training; no comparison to softmax-based attention.  
- Incomplete evaluation: decoding/inference speed is omitted despite character-by-character auto-regressive generation likely being much slower than subword methods.
