Paper proposes a hierarchical, fully character-level NMT with word-level scaffolding and a two-level decoder (word-state then char HGRU) to handle morphology and OOVs.  
Source-side word encoder uses two RNNs to compute weighted sums of character-prefix representations to learn morphological structure.  
Hierarchical decoder: attention-based word-level decoder produces states that initialize a character-level HGRU decoder; includes an efficient batching mechanism (unfolding matrix).  
Model achieves competitive BLEU on En→Fr and En→Cs, trains rapidly (strong results after one epoch), and handles misspellings/nonce words.  
Main contribution: novel hierarchical char-level NMT architecture that avoids long-sequence problems and includes an efficient hierarchical-decoder batching approach.  
Main contribution: strong empirical validation showing character-level model competitive with BPE and other systems, with rapid convergence.  
Main contribution: novel source-side word encoder with qualitative evidence (PCA, weight visualizations, nonce-word translations) of learned morphology.  
Strength: elegant, principled hierarchical design that maintains manageable sequence lengths and models word/char structure symmetrically.  
Strength: strong and efficient empirical performance—BLEU parity/exceeding baselines and very fast learning.  
Strength: compelling qualitative analyses (PCA plots, encoder weight inspection, successful translations of misspellings/nonce words).  
Weakness: lacks ablation studies to isolate contributions of the morphological word encoder versus the hierarchical decoder.  
Weakness: source word encoder design appears ad-hoc (unnormalized exp weights + tanh) with insufficient theoretical justification or comparison to alternatives (e.g., softmax).  
Weakness: model and paper are complex and sometimes hard to follow (HGRU, unfolding matrix); claim of simplicity is questionable despite fewer parameters.  
Question: why use unnormalized exponential weights and tanh for the word encoder instead of standard normalized attention or other aggregation functions?  
Question: does the HGRU perform a hard reset of the character state at word boundaries; were softer update mechanisms (gating/addition) tried?  
Question: how many epochs were final models trained for, how did final convergence behave, and was there overfitting or instability after early epochs?
