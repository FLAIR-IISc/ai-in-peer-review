**Strength:** Generally, the paper is well-structured and clear. Most of its explanations are possible to follow, the concepts presented are fundamental, and the results are quite interesting.

**Weaknesses:** I have some doubts about the explanations of the results. Furthermore, I believe some claims about the ability of the proposed method to learn morphology are not adequately supported by scientific evidence.

**General Discussion:**

This paper explores a complex architecture for character-level neural machine translation (NMT). The proposed architecture extends a classical encoder-decoder architecture by adding a new deep word-encoding layer that is capable of encoding character-level input into character-level representations of the source-language sentence. Similarly, a deep word-decoding layer is added to the output to transform the target-language sub-word representations into character sequences as the final output of the NMT system. The purpose of such an architecture is to take advantage of the benefits of character-level NMT (reduced vocabulary size and flexibility to deal with invisible words) and, at the same time, improve the performance of the entire system by using a intermediate representation of sub-words to reduce the size of the input sequence of characters. In addition, the authors claim that their deep word-encoding model is able to learn morphology better than other advanced methods.

I have some concerns about the evaluation. The authors compare their method with other advanced systems by considering two parameters: training time and BLEU score. However, I do not clearly see the advantage of the proposed model (DCNMT) over other methods (such as bpe2char). According to the BLEU score, the difference between the two methods is very small (0.04 in Cs-En and 0.1 in En-Cs), and it is difficult to say which one surpasses the other without statistically significant information: has statistical significance been evaluated? In terms of training time, it is worth mentioning that bpe2char takes 8 days less than DCNMT for Cs-En. Training time is not given for En-Cs (why not?) and bpe2char is not evaluated for En-Fr. I think a more complete comparison with this system should be done to prove the advantages of the proposed model.

My second concern is in section 5.2, where the authors begin to claim that their system is able to learn morphology. However, the section only contains examples and some comments on them. Although these examples are very well-chosen and explained in a very educational way, it is worth noting that no tests or formal evaluations have been done to support the authors' claims. I would certainly encourage the authors to expand on this interesting part, which could even be a separate paper. On the other hand, this section does not seem to be an important topic of the paper, so for the current work, I can only suggest moving this section to an appendix and softening some of the claims made about the system's ability to learn morphology.
