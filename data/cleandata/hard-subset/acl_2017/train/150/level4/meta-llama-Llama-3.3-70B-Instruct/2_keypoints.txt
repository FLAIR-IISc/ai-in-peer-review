- Paper is well-organized, clear, and presents original ideas with interesting results.
- Authors claim DCNMT improves character-level NMT by adding deep word-encoding and decoding layers to leverage sub-word representations.
- Claim that the model learns morphology better than state-of-the-art lacks sufficient scientific evidence.
- Evaluation methodology is weak: BLEU differences versus bpe2char are minimal and no statistical significance is reported.
- Training-time comparison is incomplete and unfavorable (bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs→En; En→Cs time not reported).
- A more comprehensive comparison with bpe2char is needed to demonstrate DCNMT’s advantages.
- Section 5.2’s morphology analysis is mainly commentary and requires formal evaluation or should be moved/softened.
- Many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA) are undefined or defined late, reducing clarity.
- The concept of “energy” (introduced in §3.1) should be refreshed and its interpretation explained in §5.2.
- Inconsistent casing of the acronym BPE should be fixed.
- Mention of not using a monolingual corpus in §4.1 may be unnecessary and could be removed.
- Figure 4a has a visualization issue: energy color values are not shown for every character.
- Table 1 reports results for Chung et al. (2016) on Cs→En that are not in the original paper; if computed, this should be stated.
- Describing French as “morphologically poor” is misleading—phrase as “less rich than Slavic languages (e.g., Czech).”
- Providing a WMT’15 link but not a WMT’14 link is inconsistent and puzzling.
- Several references are incomplete and need fixing.
- Multiple typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript should be corrected (list provided by reviewer).
