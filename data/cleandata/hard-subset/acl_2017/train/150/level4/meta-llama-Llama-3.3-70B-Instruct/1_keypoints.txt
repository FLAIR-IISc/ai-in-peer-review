- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder MT that uses characters at word boundaries and morpheme/character representations internally.
- Releases code and final trained models for French-English, Czech-English, and English-Czech, aiding replication.
- Achieves reasonable translation quality from the first epoch with clear improvements in later epochs.
- Learns useful morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words by reverting to morphemes.
- Paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (info only in README: newstest2015 test, newstest2013 dev).
- Software README lacks explicit training/testing command examples and the program does not respond to a --help flag.
- Describes a 6-level architecture but Figure 2 appears to show fewer layers; caption is insufficient and a complete architecture figure is needed.
- Results are compared to other character-based systems but omit state-of-the-art comparisons from WMT and matrix.statmt.org that outperform these results.
- Contains minor issues in English fluency, misspellings, and LaTeX formatting problems (reverse quotation marks).
- Overall, a valuable contribution to character-based neural MT with a novel approach and useful released resources.
