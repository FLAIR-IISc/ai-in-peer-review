- Strength: Paper is well-structured, clearly written, with original core ideas and interesting results.  
- Weakness: Interpretation of results is questionable; claims about the method's ability to learn morphology lack sufficient scientific evidence.  
- Contribution: Proposes DCNMT — a character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding layers to produce intermediate sub-word representations.  
- Claimed advantage: Aims to combine character-level benefits (small vocab, OOV handling) with shortened input sequences via sub-word representations.  
- Morphology claim: Authors assert superior morphological learning vs. SOTA, but provide only illustrative examples, not formal evaluation.  
- BLEU evaluation concern: BLEU differences vs. bpe2char are minimal (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs); statistical significance testing is not reported.  
- Training-time comparison issues: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs→En; En→Cs training time omitted and bpe2char not evaluated for En→Fr — comparisons are incomplete.  
- Recommendation on morphology section: Move illustrative analysis to an appendix, soften claims, or expand into a dedicated empirical study with metrics.  
- Missing acronym definitions: Several acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE) are not defined on first use.  
- "Energy" concept unclear: Introduced in Sec.3.1 but not redefined in Sec.5.2 where heavily used; interpretation (e.g., morpheme-boundary meaning) and term "peak" need clarification.  
- Inconsistent capitalization: BPE is capitalized at first mention but later used lowercase — maintain consistency.  
- Unnecessary statement: The note about no monolingual corpus in Sec.4.1 appears superfluous.  
- Figure issue: Figure 4a lacks energy color mapping for some characters.  
- Table clarification: Table 1 reports results for model (3) (Chung et al. 2016) in Cs→En not found in original paper—state whether authors computed these.  
- Language characterization: Describing French as "morphologically poor" is inaccurate; it is less rich than Slavic languages but not poor.  
- Data links: WMT'15 training-data link provided but WMT'14 link is missing — provide consistent links.  
- References: Several references are incomplete and need fixing.  
- Typos and grammar errors throughout; example corrections include:  
  - "..is the bilingual, parallel corpora provided..." → "..are the bilingual, parallel corpora provided..."  
  - "Luong and Manning (2016) uses" → "Luong and Manning (2016) use"  
  - "HGRU (It is" → "HGRU (it is)"  
  - "coveres" → "covers"  
  - "both consists of two-layer RNN, each has 1024" → "both consist of two-layer RNNs, each with 1024"  
  - "the only difference between CNMT and DCNMT is CNMT" → "the only difference between CNMT and DCNMT is that CNMT"
