

- Strengths:
The authors propose a novel encoder-decoder neural MT adaptation that processes input and output at the character level, while operating on morpheme and character representations internally. They release both code and final trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, enabling reproducibility and facilitating future extensions. The system achieves reasonable translation quality even after the first training epoch, with consistent improvement across subsequent epochs. Additionally, the system learns effective morphological tokenizations and handles unseen words—including nonce words—by implicitly reverting to morpheme-level processing.

- Weaknesses:
The paper omits explicit specification of the WMT test and development sets used for evaluation, a critical omission for readers seeking to compare results with existing work (e.g., on matrix.statmt.org). The only way to identify these sets (newstest2015 for test, newstest2013 for dev) was via the code supplement’s README, which should have been stated directly in the paper.  
The software README provides acceptable but insufficient instructions. Training and testing sections would benefit from explicit command examples, and the software should support a `--help` flag for immediate guidance.  
The paper describes a 6-level architecture, yet Figure 2 depicts fewer than six layers. The caption must clarify this discrepancy, and if the figure omits layers, a complete diagram (e.g., in an appendix) is required.  
Results compare only to character-based neural systems, omitting state-of-the-art results from other MT approaches on the same datasets (e.g., WMT/matrix.statmt.org results). The significantly higher SOTA performance should be acknowledged and discussed.  
Minor grammatical errors, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., incorrect quotation marks) require correction.

- General Discussion:
This paper makes a valuable contribution to character-based neural machine translation research.
