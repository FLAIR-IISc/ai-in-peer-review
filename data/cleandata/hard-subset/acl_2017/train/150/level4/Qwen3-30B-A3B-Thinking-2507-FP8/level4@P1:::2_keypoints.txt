- Contribution: Word encoder that learns morphemes and their combinations without explicit segmentation, producing meaningful word representations.  
- Contribution: Hierarchical decoder that maps word-level representations to character sequences, avoiding vocabulary bottlenecks while remaining efficient.  
- Contribution: Empirical validation claiming better morphology learning, faster training, and improved handling of unseen words versus subword baselines.  

- Strength: Novel DCNMT architecture addresses vocabulary size limits and OOVs without external segmentation, matching SOTA quality.  
- Strength: Hierarchical decoder enables efficient end-to-end training and avoids inefficiencies of prior character-level approaches.  
- Strength: Strong training efficiency (competitive BLEU after one epoch; compact model of 54M params) and faster training than comparable character models.  
- Strength: Evidence (PCA, energy analyses, nonce-word translations) suggests the encoder captures morphemic structure.  

- Weakness: BLEU improvements are marginal in places and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: Morphology claims are overstated—analyses are illustrative but lack quantitative or comparative evaluation against baselines.  
- Weakness: Incomplete reporting of training times and missing comparisons (e.g., bpe2char on En-Fr, En-Cs times omitted) weakens efficiency claims.
