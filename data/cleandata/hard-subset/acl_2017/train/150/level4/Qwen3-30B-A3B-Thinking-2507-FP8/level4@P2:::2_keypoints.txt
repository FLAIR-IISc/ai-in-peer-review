- Summary: Proposes a hierarchical deep character-level NMT that learns subword representations via a specialized word encoder to avoid vocabulary bottlenecks while keeping competitive performance.  
- Summary: Shows the model can learn morphological patterns enabling translation of misspelled or nonce words.  
- Summary: Claims efficient training, achieving comparable BLEU scores after only one epoch.

- Strength: Tackles vocabulary-size limitations by learning morphology instead of relying on subword tokenization, with a well-motivated hierarchical architecture.  
- Strength: Empirical results show competitive BLEU with far fewer epochs and successful handling of misspellings/nonce words.  
- Strength: Morphology analysis (e.g., PCA visualizations) indicates the model captures morphological structure, suggesting broader applicability.

- Weakness: Claims of superiority over state-of-the-art are weakly supported—BLEU differences are minimal and no statistical significance tests are provided; training-time comparisons are incomplete.  
- Weakness: The claim that the model “learns morphology” lacks rigorous, quantitative evaluation or comparison to alternative encoders.  
- Weakness: Clarity issues—many acronyms undefined, the concept of “energy” is introduced but not properly explained, figures lack necessary color-coding/labels, and an inaccurate statement about French’s morphology is made.

- Question: Please provide statistical significance testing for BLEU comparisons, especially for the small differences reported.  
- Question: Can you quantitatively compare morphological learning (e.g., metrics/benchmarks, comparisons to CNN word encoders)?  
- Question: Why is training time for En-Cs missing and why are training-time comparisons incomplete for En-Fr?  
- Question: Clarify the role of “energy” in the word encoder and how it relates to morpheme boundaries.

- Additional: Define all acronyms at first use and make BPE capitalization consistent.  
- Additional: Correct the claim that French is “morphologically poor” to a more accurate phrasing.  
- Additional: Fix figure issues (missing energy color in Fig.4a, unclear “peak” description in Fig.5).  
- Additional: Explain why no monolingual corpus was used or omit the unnecessary mention.  
- Additional: Fix phrasing/grammar inconsistencies and typos (e.g., “coveres”→“covers,” “both consists”→“both consist,” Luong and Manning citation agreement).
