- Proposes DCNMT: a hierarchical deep character-level NMT that addresses large-vocabulary bottlenecks by learning morphology and decoding at character level.

- Main contribution 1: a novel word encoder that learns morphemes and their combinations via two recurrent networks.

- Main contribution 2: a hierarchical character-level decoder that maintains word-level encoding length while translating at character granularity.

- Main contribution 3: empirical results showing competitive BLEU with much reduced training time compared to other character-level approaches.

- Strength: effectively handles out-of-vocabulary and nonce words by morphological decomposition, preserving translation quality.

- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art-like performance quickly (notably strong after one epoch) on En–Fr and En–Cs, outperforming BPE baselines early in training.

- Strength: authors released code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, improving reproducibility.

- Strength: despite using six recurrent networks, the model trains faster than prior character-level models; PCA analysis supports meaningful morphological representations.

- Weakness: paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (only discoverable in the repo README).

- Weakness: description of a “six-level architecture” conflicts with Figure 2, which appears to show fewer than six layers, causing confusion.

- Weakness: comparisons omit context versus true WMT state-of-the-art (reported BLEU scores on matrix.statmt.org are substantially higher than the paper’s numbers).

- Weakness: software documentation could be improved with explicit train/test examples and a --help flag for usability.

- Question: please explicitly state the WMT test/dev sets used (repo indicates newstest2015/test and newstest2013/dev).

- Question: clarify how the six layers map to components in Figure 2 or provide a supplementary figure detailing all layers.

- Question: explain under what experimental conditions the model is competitive with the stronger WMT baselines and why it lags behind reported SOTA BLEU.
