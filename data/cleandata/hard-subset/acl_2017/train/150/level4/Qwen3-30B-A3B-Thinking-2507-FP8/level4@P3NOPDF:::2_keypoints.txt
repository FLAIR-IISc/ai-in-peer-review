- Paper proposes a novel character-level NMT with deep word-encoding/decoding to capture morphology via sub-word representations.  
- Strength: well-structured, clearly written, technically original, with compelling illustrative morphology examples.  
- Major weakness: evaluation vs baselines is insufficient—BLEU gains are marginal (0.04, 0.1) and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Major weakness: training-time comparison is incomplete/misleading—bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs→En, DCNMT time for En→Cs omitted, and bpe2char not evaluated for En→Fr.  
- Major weakness: claim that DCNMT learns morphology better is unsupported—Section 5.2 uses only illustrative examples with no formal metrics, ablations, or comparative experiments.  
- Recommendation: provide statistical significance tests, full training-time comparisons, and empirical validation (or tone down claims/move section to appendix).  
- Minor: define all acronyms on first use and fix inconsistent capitalization (e.g., BPE).  
- Minor: rephrase inaccurate linguistic claims (e.g., “French is less morphologically rich than Slavic languages like Czech”).  
- Minor: remove or clarify unnecessary statements (e.g., “no monolingual corpus” in Sec. 4.1).  
- Figure/table issues: complete color mapping in Fig.4a, explicitly state when Table 1 results were recomputed by authors, and clarify corpus links (WMT’14 vs WMT’15).  
- Conceptual clarity: briefly reintroduce/define “energy” and explicitly define “peak” in figures.  
- Typos/grammar: fix multiple language errors (examples provided by reviewer).  
- References: ensure all references are complete.  
- Overall: architecture is promising but current evidence does not convincingly support claimed advantages; rigorous evaluation needed before publication.
