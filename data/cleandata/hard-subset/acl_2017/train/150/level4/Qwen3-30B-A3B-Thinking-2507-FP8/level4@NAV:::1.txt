

Strengths:
The authors introduce a novel adaptation of encoder-decoder neural machine translation that commences and terminates at the character level, operating internally with morpheme and character representations.
The authors publicly release both their codebase and final trained models for the fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs language pairs, facilitating validation of their methodology and enabling replication and extension by other researchers.
The reported system achieves translation quality that is reasonable even after the initial training epoch, with consistent improvement observed in subsequent epochs.
The system demonstrates effective learning of morphological tokenization and handles unseen words—including nonce words—by implicitly reverting to morpheme-level processing.

Weaknesses:
The paper fails to explicitly specify the WMT test and development sets used for reporting results. This omission hinders readers' ability to compare outcomes with existing work (e.g., via matrix.statmt.org). The reviewer identified the test set (newstest2015) and development set (newstest2013) solely by consulting the code supplement's README, which should instead be clearly stated within the manuscript.
The software README provides acceptable but suboptimal instructions. Both training and testing sections would benefit from explicit command examples. The software should also support a --help flag, which is currently absent.
The paper describes a 6-level architecture, yet Figure 2 appears to depict fewer than six layers. The caption requires greater clarity, and if Figure 2 does not illustrate all layers, a supplementary figure (e.g., in the appendix) must be provided.
The results compare against character-based neural systems but omit state-of-the-art benchmarks for other MT system types. WMT and matrix.statmt.org report higher performance for alternative systems on these datasets; this gap should be acknowledged and addressed.
Minor issues include English disfluencies, spelling errors, and minor LaTeX problems (e.g., reverse quotation marks), all of which require correction.

General Discussion:
This paper constitutes a valuable contribution to the literature on character-based neural machine translation.
