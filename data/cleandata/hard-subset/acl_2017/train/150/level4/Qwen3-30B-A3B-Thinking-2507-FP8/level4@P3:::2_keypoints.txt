- Proposes DCNMT: character-level NMT with a novel two-RNN word encoder and a hierarchical decoder claiming to learn morphology.  
- Contribution claims: (1) word encoder that learns morphemes via weighted character RNNs, (2) hierarchical decoder generating characters conditioned on word representations, (3) empirical evidence of morphological structure.  
- Strength: novel architecture for morphology learning that implicitly learns subword units, avoids large vocabulary, and yields competitive BLEU after one epoch.  
- Strength: demonstrated efficient training — comparable results achieved much faster and with a smaller model than some prior character-level approaches.  
- Strength: qualitative analyses (PCA, energy plots) show intuitive clustering and apparent morpheme boundary detection.  
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative evidence for the central claim that the model learns morphology (relies on qualitative examples only).  
- Weakness: BLEU comparisons are ambiguous/misleading (tiny gains presented as meaningful, no statistical tests, missing training-time data and baselines).  
- Weakness: novelty is overstated — components resemble prior work (hierarchical decoder and RNN word representations).  
- Weakness: inconsistent terminology and undefined acronyms reduce clarity.  
- Question: provide statistical validation that reported BLEU improvements are significant.  
- Question: why is En-Cs training time omitted and why isn’t bpe2char evaluated on En-Fr?  
- Question: provide quantitative morphological evaluations (e.g., morpheme boundary accuracy or comparison to linguistic tools).  
- Additional: correct the claim that French is "morphologically poor" (it is moderately inflected); fix noted typos.  
- Additional: define "energy" and "peak" earlier and present qualitative examples as observations rather than definitive proof.
