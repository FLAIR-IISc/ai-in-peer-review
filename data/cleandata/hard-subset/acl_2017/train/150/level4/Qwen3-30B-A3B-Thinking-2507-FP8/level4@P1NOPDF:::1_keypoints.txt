- Novel encoder-decoder MT processes characters but uses morpheme-based internal representations, bridging character- and morpheme-level modeling.  
- Code and final models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs are released, supporting reproducibility.  
- System yields reasonable translation quality after the first epoch and improves steadily across epochs.  
- Robust morphological tokenization and effective handling of unseen/nonce words via implicit morpheme decomposition.  
- Paper omits explicit specification of WMT test/dev sets in main text (found only in README; newstest2015=test, newstest2013=dev) — must be stated in the paper.  
- README lacks concrete training/inference command examples and the tool lacks a `--help` flag.  
- Figure 2 claims a 6-level architecture but appears to show four layers; caption needs clarification and a full architectural diagram should be provided.  
- Results compare to character-based systems but omit state-of-the-art non-character MT baselines on the same datasets and should acknowledge higher WMT benchmark scores.  
- Minor language issues (disfluencies, spelling, LaTeX formatting like reverse quotation marks) need correction.  
- Overall: valuable contribution to character-based NMT with solid empirical evidence and useful code/model release.
