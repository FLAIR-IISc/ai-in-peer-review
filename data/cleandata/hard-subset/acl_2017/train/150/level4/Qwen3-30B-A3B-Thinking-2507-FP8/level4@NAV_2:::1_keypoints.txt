- Proposes a novel character-level NMT architecture with a dual-recurrent word encoder and hierarchical decoder to address vocabulary bottlenecks.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: competitive BLEU after one epoch on En–Fr and En–Cs, outperforming BPE baselines and matching SOTA character models.  
- Model implicitly learns morphemes, enabling robust handling of unseen words, nonce words (e.g., "convenienter"), and misspellings, supported by analyses and samples.  
- Does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (e.g., newstest2015/newstest2013), harming reproducibility and fair comparison.  
- Software documentation is functional but needs clearer training/testing examples and a --help flag for guidance.  
- Claims a "six-layer architecture" but Figure 2 depicts fewer layers; figure caption or a complete diagram must clarify this discrepancy.  
- Omits comparisons to leading word-based neural MT systems (e.g., >30 BLEU on En–Fr), limiting assessment relative to current SOTA.  
- Contains minor grammatical and LaTeX errors (e.g., reverse quotation marks) that should be corrected.  
- Overall, contributes meaningfully by showing morphology-aware encoding yields high performance, efficient training, and resolves OOV issues of word-level models.
