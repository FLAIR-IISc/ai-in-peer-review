- Proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT with a novel word encoder for morphology and a hierarchical decoder.  
- Claims DCNMT avoids vocabulary bottlenecks while maintaining state-of-the-art translation performance.  
- Claims the word encoder learns morphological structure, improving handling of unseen words and variants.  
- Claims training efficiency compared to other character-level approaches despite model complexity.  
- Model architecture is well-explained with sufficient technical detail.  
- Experimental results show competitive BLEU scores, especially on morphologically rich languages (e.g., Czech).  
- Outperforms baselines after just one epoch, suggesting potential training efficiency.  
- Morphology analysis provides visual evidence that the model learns meaningful word representations.  
- No statistical significance tests reported for BLEU differences, making small gains (e.g., 0.1 BLEU) hard to interpret.  
- Comparison with bpe2char shows minimal performance gains but longer training times, questioning claimed advantages.  
- Morphology claims are supported mainly by illustrative examples without formal quantitative evaluation.  
- Comparison with other state-of-the-art systems is incomplete; some training times (e.g., En–Cs) are not provided.  
- Questions: Were statistical significance tests performed? Please provide formal metrics for morphology learning.  
- Questions: Why are training times missing for En–Cs and how do they compare across language pairs?  
- Questions: Please clarify the characterization of French as morphologically poor.
