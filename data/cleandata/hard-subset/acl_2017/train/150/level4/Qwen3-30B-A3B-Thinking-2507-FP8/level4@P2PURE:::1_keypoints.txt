- Novel encoder-decoder char-level adaptation that internally uses morpheme and character representations.
- Code and final trained models released for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, enabling reproducibility.
- System yields reasonable translation quality after the first epoch and improves consistently across epochs.
- Learns effective morphological tokenizations and handles unseen/nonce words by reverting to morpheme-level processing.

- Paper fails to specify the WMT test/dev sets used (only discoverable in README), hindering direct comparison with prior work.
- Software README is insufficiently detailed; needs explicit training/testing command examples and a `--help` flag.
- Described 6-level architecture conflicts with Figure 2 (fewer layers); caption must clarify and a complete diagram should be provided.
- Evaluation compares only to character-based systems and omits state-of-the-art MT results on the same datasets, which should be acknowledged and discussed.
- Contains minor grammatical, spelling, and LaTeX issues that require correction.

- Overall: paper makes a valuable contribution to character-based neural machine translation research.
