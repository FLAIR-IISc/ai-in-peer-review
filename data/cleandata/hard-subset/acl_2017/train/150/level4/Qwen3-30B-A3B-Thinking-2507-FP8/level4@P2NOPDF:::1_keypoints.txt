- Introduces a novel char-level encoder-decoder NMT architecture that internally uses morpheme-level representations, bridging character- and morpheme-based modeling.
- Authors release code and trained models for Fr-En, Cs-En, and En-Cs, supporting reproducibility and future work.
- Model shows strong early training performance (reasonable quality after one epoch) and consistent improvement across epochs.
- Learns meaningful morphological tokenizations and effectively handles unseen or nonce words by implicit morpheme decomposition.
- Paper fails to explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used, forcing inference from the README and reducing result clarity.
- Software README lacks concrete training/testing command examples and should provide a `--help` flag for better usability.
- Figure 2 claims a 6-level architecture but visually shows fewer layers; caption or an appendix diagram must clarify or correct this.
- Evaluation compares only to character-based NMT and omits state-of-the-art word/subword baselines on the same benchmarks, which should be acknowledged and discussed.
- Minor issues: typographical errors, inconsistent LaTeX formatting, and occasional English disfluencies that need correction.
- Overall, a promising and publishable contribution to character-based NMT that requires clarifications, additional comparisons, and presentation fixes to meet ACL standards.
