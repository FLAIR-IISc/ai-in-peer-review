- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation of an original character-level NMT architecture (DCNMT) with compelling core ideas and promising results.
- Strength: Results indicate interesting potential for the proposed architecture combining character- and sub-word-level processing.
- Weakness: Interpretation of results lacks statistical rigor; claims of superiority are not justified by provided evidence.
- Weakness: Assertions that DCNMT better learns morphology are unsupported by formal empirical evaluation.
- Model summary: DCNMT extends encoder-decoder NMT with deep word-encoding and word-decoding layers converting between characters and sub-word representations.
- Evaluation concern: Comparisons with bpe2char rely only on BLEU and training time; BLEU differences are minimal (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and untreated for significance.
- Evaluation concern: Training-time comparison is incomplete—bpe2char is reported as taking 8 fewer days for Cs→En, En→Cs training time omitted, and En→Fr results for bpe2char absent.
- Recommendation: Provide comprehensive comparisons including statistical significance tests and full training-time data to substantiate superiority claims.
- Morphology issue: Section 5.2 uses only illustrative examples and descriptive commentary without formal metrics (e.g., morpheme segmentation accuracy or ablation studies).
- Recommendation: Either conduct rigorous morphology experiments to support claims or move the section to an appendix and soften the assertions.
- Acronyms: Define all acronyms on first use (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE).
- Energy concept: Clarify and reintroduce the "energy" concept in Section 5.2 and define terms like "peak" used in Figure 5.
- BPE capitalization: Standardize capitalization of "BPE" vs. "bpe" throughout the paper.
- Monolingual corpus statement: Remove the unnecessary note "no monolingual corpus is used" as it is standard practice.
- Figure 4a: Fix incomplete energy color mapping so all characters have corresponding color values.
- Table 1: If Chung et al. (2016) Cs→En results were recomputed, explicitly state this rather than implying they come from the original paper.
- Morphological phrasing: Avoid saying "French is morphologically poor"; instead note it has less morphological complexity than languages like Czech.
- WMT references: Provide a link for WMT'14 training corpora to match WMT'15 citation.
- References and citations: Correct incomplete citations and other reference errors.
- Typos: Fix listed typographical errors (e.g., subject–verb agreements and missing words throughout the manuscript).
