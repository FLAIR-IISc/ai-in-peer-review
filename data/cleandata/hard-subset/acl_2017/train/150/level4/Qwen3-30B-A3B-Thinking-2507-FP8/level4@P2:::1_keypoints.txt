- Proposes DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT that learns morphology to overcome word-level vocabulary limits.  
- Contribution: morphology-aware word encoder using two recurrent networks to learn morpheme representations and combinations.  
- Contribution: hierarchical decoder enabling efficient character-level translation without large vocabularies.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that the model handles OOV and nonce words via morphological reasoning.  
- Strength: encoder learns meaningful morpheme boundaries and clusters semantically related words better than standard RNN encoders.  
- Strength: training efficiency—competitive BLEU after one epoch and faster training (~7 days) with a compact 54M-parameter model.  
- Strength: authors release code and trained models for En-Fr, En-Cs, Cs-En, improving reproducibility.  
- Weakness: paper omits explicit specification of WMT test/dev sets in the main text (reviewer inferred from README).  
- Weakness: discrepancy between claimed six recurrent networks and Figure 2’s “four layers” caption; figure needs clarification.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to current word-level SOTA (e.g., Transformer’s ~39.8 BLEU on En-Fr), so results are not fully contextualized.  
- Weakness: software documentation is insufficient (no concrete train/test examples, no --help support), hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: minor grammatical and LaTeX errors detract from presentation and should be fixed.  
- Question: clarify what is meant by “comparable to state-of-the-art systems” and provide exact BLEU scores for the strongest baselines.  
- Question: explain the Figure 2 vs. text discrepancy regarding layer/network count or revise the figure.  
- Question: provide additional examples showing performance on truly novel morphological constructions (e.g., “unconvenienter”).
