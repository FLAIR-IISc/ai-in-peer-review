- Introduces a novel encoder-decoder NMT that uses characters at input/output and morpheme+character internal representations.
- Publicly releases source code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, aiding reproducibility and future work.
- Demonstrates strong training efficiency, achieving respectable translation quality after one epoch and improving thereafter.
- Learns plausible morphological segmentations, enabling effective translation of OOV and nonce words via morpheme-level representations.
- Fails to state which WMT dev/test sets were used in the paper (newstest2013/newstest2015 only in supplementary code); this should be explicit in the main text.
- Software documentation is weak: README lacks concrete train/test command examples and the tool lacks a standard --help flag.
- Text claims a 6-level architecture but Figure 2 appears to show fewer layers; figure caption is unclear and a full architecture diagram is needed.
- Evaluation compares only to other character-based models and omits comparisons/discussion against broader MT state-of-the-art and higher-performing WMT systems.
- Manuscript needs thorough proofreading to fix minor grammatical errors, typos, and LaTeX formatting issues.
- Overall, a valuable contribution to character-level neural machine translation.
