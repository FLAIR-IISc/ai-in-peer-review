### Summary

This paper presents a novel encoder-decoder architecture for Neural Machine Translation (NMT) that operates on characters at its input and output layers but utilizes intermediate representations of morphemes. The approach is interesting and demonstrates a promising ability to handle morphology and out-of-vocabulary words. The authors are commended for releasing their code and models, which supports reproducibility. However, the paper's evaluation is weakened by a lack of clarity regarding the specific datasets used and insufficient comparison to the broader state-of-the-art in machine translation. Overall, this is a solid contribution to character-level NMT that would be significantly improved by addressing the issues raised below.

### Strengths

*   **Novel Architecture:** The paper introduces an innovative NMT model that begins and ends with character-level processing while internally operating on learned representations of morphemes. This hybrid approach is a compelling method for integrating sub-word information into the translation process.
*   **Open Science and Reproducibility:** The authors have released their source code and final pre-trained models for French-English, Czech-English, and English-Czech. This commitment to open science is commendable and greatly facilitates the validation, replication, and extension of their research by the wider community.
*   **Efficient Learning:** The model demonstrates efficient learning, achieving promising translation quality after just a single training epoch, with performance continuing to improve in subsequent epochs.
*   **Morphological Handling:** A key advantage of this approach is its ability to learn plausible morphological segmentations. It effectively handles out-of-vocabulary items, including nonce words, by leveraging its morpheme-level representations as a natural backoff mechanism.

### Weaknesses

*   **Lack of Experimental Detail:** The paper omits crucial details about the specific WMT development and test sets used for evaluation. This information was only found by consulting the README file in the supplementary code (newstest2013 for development, newstest2015 for testing). This omission significantly hinders direct comparison with established benchmarks, such as those on matrix.statmt.org, and forces readers to seek external resources to understand the experimental setup. This essential information should be explicitly stated in the main body of the paper.
*   **Insufficient Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The experimental results are compared against other character-level NMT systems, but they are not contextualized within the broader landscape of machine translation. State-of-the-art systems reported on the WMT leaderboards for these language pairs achieve significantly higher scores. The authors should acknowledge this performance gap and discuss how their work fits into the wider context of state-of-the-art MT research.
*   **Clarity of Model Diagram:** There is a discrepancy between the text, which describes a 6-level architecture, and Figure 2, which appears to depict fewer layers. The figure's caption should be clarified to explain this difference. If the figure is a simplified representation, a complete diagram of the full architecture should be provided, perhaps in an appendix, to ensure clarity.
*   **Software Usability:** While the provision of code is a strength, its usability could be improved. The README file lacks concrete command-line examples for training and testing, which would lower the barrier to entry for other researchers. Additionally, the software should implement a standard `--help` flag to provide users with command-line options.
*   **Minor Presentation Issues:** The manuscript would benefit from a thorough proofreading to correct minor grammatical errors, typos, and formatting issues (e.g., inconsistent use of quotation marks).

### Detailed Comments

This paper presents a valuable and interesting contribution to the field of character-level Neural Machine Translation. The core ideas are sound and well-motivated. The paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the issues of experimental clarity and more thorough contextualization of the results against the state-of-the-art. Addressing these points will make the paper's contributions clearer and more impactful for the community.
