- Proposes an end-to-end hierarchical character-level NMT that internally uses word-like structures to bridge character inputs/outputs and word-level processing.  
- Introduces a character-based word encoder using two RNNs that computes weighted sums of character prefix representations to learn sub-word units without BPE.  
- Shows the deep character-level model achieves BLEU competitive with strong subword baselines and qualitatively handles misspellings and nonce words.  
- Novel and ambitious hierarchical architecture combining character-, sub-word-, and word-level processing with end-to-end differentiability.  
- Clear presentation and effective figures that make the complex architecture easy to follow.  
- Demonstrates effective handling of OOV, misspelled, and nonce words through qualitative examples.  
- Empirical comparisons to baselines are unconvincing: marginal BLEU gains may be insignificant, training is slower on Cs-En, and some baseline evaluations are missing.  
- Claims about the model learning morphology are inadequately supported—analysis is anecdotal and needs quantitative, systematic evaluation or should be toned down.  
- Paper lacks clarity on key details affecting reproducibility: undefined acronyms, ill-defined "energy"/"peak energy" terms, and unclear provenance of some baseline results.  
- Question: Were statistical significance tests performed for BLEU comparisons, especially versus bpe2char?  
- Question: Please clarify the trade-off between marginal BLEU improvements and increased training time given the claim of greater training efficiency.  
- Question: Did the authors run the bpe2char Cs-En baseline themselves; if so, provide implementation details and missing baseline training times.
