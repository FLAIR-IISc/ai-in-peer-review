- Novel encoder-decoder NMT architecture that uses character input/output with intermediate morpheme and character representations, a well-motivated approach.
- Public release of source code and trained models for French–English, Czech–English, and English–Czech, aiding reproducibility.
- Model shows strong early learning, achieving reasonable translation quality after a single epoch and improving thereafter.
- Learns plausible morphological segmentations and effectively handles out-of-vocabulary and nonce words via subword representations.
- Paper omits explicit specification of which WMT dev/test sets were used (found only in README: newstest2013 dev, newstest2015 test); this should be stated in the paper.
- Software README and instructions need clearer command-line examples and a standard --help flag to improve usability.
- Diagram/f Figure 2 conflicts with the text’s described 6-level architecture; caption should clarify or a full architecture diagram should be provided.
- Experimental comparisons are limited to other character-based systems and do not situate performance relative to broader MT state-of-the-art; the gap should be acknowledged and discussed.
- Manuscript contains minor English, spelling, and LaTeX formatting errors that should be corrected.
- Overall, a solid contribution to character- and subword-level NMT with a novel architecture and promising capabilities.
