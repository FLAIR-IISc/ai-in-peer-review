- Proposes DCNMT, a deep character-level NMT with a dual-RNN word encoder and hierarchical word→character decoder (HGRU).  
- Claims DCNMT combines character-level OOV handling with subword-like efficiency and learns morphology end-to-end.  
- Reports competitive BLEU on English–French and English–Czech, and faster learning (outperforms BPE baseline after one epoch on two tasks).  
- Main contributions: novel hierarchical architecture, dual-RNN encoder for subword representations, and hierarchical decoder for efficient character generation.  
- Strength: architecture is novel, well-motivated, and addresses long-sequence/training issues of pure character models.  
- Strength: empirical results are promising, showing competitive BLEU and rapid early training gains.  
- Strength: qualitative evidence that the model handles misspellings and nonce/morphological variants effectively.  
- Strength: paper is generally clearly written with helpful diagrams.  
- Weakness: quantitative comparison to bpe2char is weak—BLEU differences are tiny and no significance tests are reported.  
- Weakness: training-efficiency claims are undermined by Cs→En where DCNMT took 8 days longer than bpe2char and missing training-time data for bpe2char on En–Cs.  
- Weakness: morphology claims lack rigorous evaluation—analysis is based on a few curated examples and visualizations only.  
- Weakness: several technical details are unclear or undefined (acronyms, the concept of “energy,” interpretation of “peak energy”).  
- Weakness: Figure 4a appears corrupted/misrendered, hindering interpretation of character-energy visualizations.  
- Question: Were statistical significance tests performed to validate BLEU differences against bpe2char?  
- Question: Why is bpe2char training time missing for En–Cs, and how do you reconcile DCNMT’s longer Cs–En training time with efficiency claims?  
- Question: Please define “energy” precisely and clarify whether “peak energy” consistently maps to morpheme boundaries across larger datasets.  
- Question: Did you reproduce the bpe2char Cs–En results yourselves, and if so, please explicitly state that in the paper.
