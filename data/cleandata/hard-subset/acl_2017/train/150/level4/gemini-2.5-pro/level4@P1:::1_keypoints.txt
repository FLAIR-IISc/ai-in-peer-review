- Novel deep character-level NMT with a source-side word encoder (two RNNs) and hierarchical character decoder to avoid large vocabularies and learn morphology implicitly.
- Model effectively learns sub-word/morphological representations, handling OOVs, rare/misspelled, and nonce words.
- Achieves strong translation performance very quickly (competitive BLEU after a single epoch).
- Authors released code and pretrained models, aiding reproducibility.
- Qualitative analyses (PCA plots, segmentation examples) provide convincing evidence of learned morphology.
- Qualitative examples show advantages over `<unk>`-based systems and embedding comparisons visualize improved representations.
- Comparison to state-of-the-art is incomplete; not benchmarked against top WMT leaderboard systems, risking overstatement of performance.
- Experimental reporting and figure clarity are lacking: development/test set details are omitted from the paper and the architecture diagram is confusing.
- Manuscript and software need polish: English/LaTeX errors, sparse README, and missing standard `--help` for usability.
