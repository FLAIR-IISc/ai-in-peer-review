- Proposes DCNMT, a deep character-to-subword encoder-decoder that combines character-level inputs with sub-word efficiency and claims improved morphological learning.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the architecture easy to follow.  
- The deep character-to-subword architecture is novel and interesting.  
- Experimental results are promising but gains are marginal.  
- Empirical evaluation is inconclusive: performance differences vs strong baselines (e.g., bpe2char) are very small.  
- No statistical significance testing is reported for BLEU differences.  
- Training-time comparisons are incomplete and inconclusive (bpe2char much faster on Cs-En; other tasks missing).  
- Morphology claims are supported only by qualitative examples, lacking rigorous quantitative evaluation or probes.  
- Recommend adding systematic morphological analyses (e.g., morphological tools or probes) or tempering the claims.  
- Several acronyms are undefined or inconsistently used; ensure all are defined at first use and capitalization is consistent (e.g., BPE vs bpe).  
- The “energy” concept is insufficiently reiterated/clarified in analysis (interpretation and “peak” definition needed).  
- Figure 4a has a rendering issue hiding some color-coded energy values.  
- Table 1 includes reproduced results (Chung et al., 2016) without stating they were reproduced by the authors.  
- Characterization of French as “morphologically poor” is an oversimplification and should be rephrased.  
- Data links are inconsistent (WMT'15 provided, WMT'14 missing); provide links for all corpora.  
- Several bibliography entries are incomplete and need to be fixed.  
- Multiple typographical and grammatical errors should be corrected (several examples cited).
