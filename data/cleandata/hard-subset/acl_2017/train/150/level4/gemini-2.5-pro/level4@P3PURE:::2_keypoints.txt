- Strength: Paper is well-structured, clear, and presents original, easy-to-follow ideas with interesting results.  
- Strength: Core architecture (deep char→sub-word encoder and sub-word→char decoder) is a promising combination of character-level robustness and sub-word efficiency.  
- Weakness: Experimental interpretation is weak; reported BLEU gains over bpe2char are marginal and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Weakness: Training-time comparisons are incomplete and inconsistent (bpe2char much faster for Cs→En; missing for En→Cs and omitted for En→Fr).  
- Weakness: Claims that the model learns morphology are not supported by rigorous, systematic evaluation—current evidence is anecdotal.  
- Suggestion: Either soften morphology-learning claims, move qualitative analysis to an appendix, or perform a rigorous morphological evaluation (could be a separate study).  
- Concern: Advantage of DCNMT over bpe2char is not convincingly demonstrated due to marginal BLEU differences and incomplete baseline reporting.  
- Comment: The concept of "energy" (Section 3.1) is used heavily later but should be briefly revisited in Section 5.2 and have interpretation guidance (e.g., does high energy indicate morpheme boundary?).  
- Comment: Define the term "peak" used in Figure 5 explicitly.  
- Comment: Several acronyms are undefined at first use (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA) or defined late (RNN, BPE); define all acronyms on first use.  
- Comment: Inconsistent capitalization of BPE vs bpe; make it consistent.  
- Comment: The statement that no monolingual corpus was used (Section 4.1) is unnecessary.  
- Issue: Figure 4a appears to miss energy-value colors for some characters—figure needs fixing.  
- Table issue: Results for Chung et al. (2016) on Cs→En in Table 1 are not reported in the cited paper; if reproduced by authors, state that explicitly.  
- Wording: Describing French as "morphologically poor" is oversimplified; say "less morphologically rich than Czech" instead.  
- Consistency: Provide a link for WMT'14 training corpora as done for WMT'15.  
- Bibliography: Several references are incomplete and need completion.  
- Typos: Fix grammatical errors (e.g., "are the bilingual", "Luong and Manning (2016) use", "HGRU (it is", "covers", "both consist of a two-layer RNN, each having 1024", "the only difference ... is that CNMT").
