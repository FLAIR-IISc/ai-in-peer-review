- Hierarchical character-level NMT that composes characters → morpheme-like reps → words → sentences and decodes with a word-level decoder guiding a character generator.  
- Novel word encoder using two recurrent networks that learn weighted character sequences to capture morphology and form subword/word representations.  
- Hierarchical decoder with a specialized HGRU generating characters conditioned on higher-level word representations, enabling end-to-end training and removing fixed-vocabulary OOVs.  
- Strong evidence the model learns meaningful morphological representations and handles misspellings and nonce words effectively (Section 5.2, Tables/Figures).  
- Remarkable training efficiency: outperforms a BPE baseline after a single epoch on En–Fr and En–Cs, despite being a deep six-RNN model.  
- Fully addresses the OOV problem by operating at the character level, handling rare words and neologisms without <unk> tokens.  
- Reproducibility: code and final trained models have been released.  
- Limited comparison to overall state-of-the-art: lacks benchmarks against top WMT/Transformer systems, so “comparable to SOTA” is not fully substantiated.
