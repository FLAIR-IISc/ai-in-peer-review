- Strength: Manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and easy to follow.
- Strength: Proposed ideas are novel and the results are compelling.
- Weakness: Interpretation of results is questionable.
- Weakness: Claims about the method’s ability to learn morphology lack rigorous evidence.
- Contribution: Introduces a character-level NMT architecture with deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding layers producing sub-word representations.
- Motivation: Aims to combine character-level advantages (small vocab, OOV handling) with shorter effective input via intermediate sub-word representations.
- Claim: Authors assert superior morphological learning compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- Concern (evaluation): BLEU differences against the bpe2char baseline are marginal (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and lack reported statistical significance testing.
- Concern (evaluation): bpe2char is much faster in training (eight days faster for Cs→En); En→Cs training time is missing and bpe2char is not evaluated on En→Fr.
- Recommendation: Provide a more comprehensive and statistically sound comparison with the bpe2char baseline.
- Concern (morphology analysis): Section 5.2 relies on qualitative examples rather than formal quantitative experiments, insufficient to support morphological claims.
- Recommendation: Move the morphology analysis to an appendix or tone down claims about morphological learning unless supported by formal experiments.
- Clarity: Define all acronyms at first use (e.g., LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE).
- Clarify: Reintroduce and interpret the concept of “energy” in Section 5.2 (e.g., does high energy indicate morpheme boundary?) and define “peak” used in Figure 5.
- Consistency: Fix inconsistent capitalization of “BPE” across the paper.
- Conciseness: Remove the possibly superfluous statement in Section 4.1 about not using a monolingual corpus.
- Figure issue: Figure 4a appears to have missing color-coding for some energy values.
- Reproducibility: If Table 1 reports reproduced results for Chung et al. (2016) Cs→En, explicitly state that these were reproduced by the authors.
- Accuracy: Avoid labeling French as “morphologically poor”; say “less morphologically rich than Czech” instead.
- Consistency: Provide hyperlinks for both WMT’14 and WMT’15 training corpora.
- Bibliography: Several bibliographic entries are incomplete and need fixing.
- Typos: Multiple grammatical and typographical errors listed that should be corrected (several example corrections provided).
