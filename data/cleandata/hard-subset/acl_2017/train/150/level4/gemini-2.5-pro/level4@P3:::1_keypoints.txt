- Summary: Proposes a six-RNN deep hierarchical character-level NMT that composes word representations from characters and uses a hierarchical decoder to generate characters, claiming efficiency, open vocabulary, and competitive En-Fr/En-Cs performance while learning morphology.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel six-RNN hierarchical architecture combining a weighted character-RNN word encoder and a multi-level decoder where a word-level state guides character generation.

- Contribution 2: Empirically demonstrates the model learns meaningful subword/morphological representations and generalizes to OOVs, misspellings, and nonce words.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated and novel design that balances character-level open-vocabulary benefits with manageable sentence lengths via hierarchical encoding and decoding.

- Strength 2: Strong qualitative and quantitative evidence (visualizations and examples) that the model captures linguistically plausible morphology and generalizes robustly.

- Strength 3: Reported training efficiency and competitive BLEU results, with fast convergence (notably good performance after one epoch) and superiority to a BPE baseline in reported experiments.

- Strength 4: Authors released code and trained models, supporting reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Missing explicit reporting of which dev/test splits were used in the paper (e.g., newstest2013/newstest2015), hindering result comparison.

- Weakness 2: Limited empirical comparison to the overall state-of-the-art (e.g., top WMT systems or phrase-based systems), reducing contextualization of results.

- Weakness 3: Slight disconnect between textual description and Figure 2’s diagram; mapping of the six RNNs/four layers is unclear and needs clearer illustration/caption.

- Weakness 4: Minor presentation issues (grammatical, phrasing, LaTeX inconsistencies) that should be fixed for readability.

- Question 1: Please clarify the mapping between the six RNNs/four layers in Section 3.3 and the components shown in Figure 2.

- Question 2: How do your newstest2015 En-Fr and En-Cs results compare to the overall top-performing WMT 2015 systems, including non-character-based models?
