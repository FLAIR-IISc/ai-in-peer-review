- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder NMT that is character-level at input/output and uses learned morpheme-level intermediate representations.  
- Demonstrates promising handling of morphology and out-of-vocabulary and nonce words via morpheme-level backoff.  
- Authors released source code and pretrained models (FR-EN, CS-EN, EN-CS), supporting reproducibility.  
- Model learns efficiently, showing strong performance after a single training epoch and improving thereafter.  
- Paper omits explicit statement of which WMT dev/test sets were used (found only in README: newstest2013 dev, newstest2015 test), hindering benchmark comparisons.  
- Lacks contextual comparison to broader state-of-the-art MT systems; leaderboard systems outperform the proposed model and this gap is not discussed.  
- Figure 2 and text disagree on the number of architecture levels (text: 6-level, figure: fewer); figure caption or an unabridged diagram is needed.  
- README lacks concrete command-line examples for training/testing and the software should provide a standard --help flag to improve usability.  
- Manuscript contains minor grammatical and formatting errors and would benefit from careful proofreading.  
- Overall a solid contribution to character-level NMT that would be stronger with clearer experimental details and better contextualization against SOTA.
