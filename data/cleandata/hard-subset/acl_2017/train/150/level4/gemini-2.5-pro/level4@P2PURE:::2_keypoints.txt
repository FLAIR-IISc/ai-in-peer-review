- Strength: Paper is well-structured and clearly written; technical explanations are easy to follow.
- Strength: Proposed ideas are novel and the results are interesting.
- Weakness: Interpretation of results is not entirely convincing.
- Weakness: Claims that the model learns morphology are insufficiently supported by empirical evidence.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel character-level NMT architecture (deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding) combining character-level benefits with sub-word efficiency.
- Claim: Authors assert their deep word-encoding model learns morphology better than state-of-the-art.
- Empirical concern: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are marginal (0.04 Cs-En, 0.1 En-Cs) and lack statistical significance testing.
- Empirical concern: Training-time comparisons are inconclusive (bpe2char trains 8 days faster for Cs-En; En-Cs training time missing; bpe2char not evaluated on En-Fr).
- Recommendation: More thorough and fair comparisons with bpe2char are needed to demonstrate advantages.
- Morphology analysis concern: Section 5.2 relies on a few illustrative examples rather than systematic quantitative evaluation.
- Recommendation: Soften morphological claims or move the analysis to an appendix; expand into a separate study in future.
- Suggestion: Define all acronyms at first use and make acronym casing consistent (e.g., BPE vs bpe).
- Suggestion: Briefly recap and clarify the “energy” concept in Section 5.2 and define the term “peak.”
- Question: Is it necessary to state that no monolingual corpus was used in Section 4.1 (usually assumed)?
- Issue: Figure 4a appears incorrectly rendered—energy color values missing for some characters.
- Table note: If authors computed missing Chung et al. (2016) Cs-En results, state this explicitly.
- Terminology: Calling French “morphologically poor” is an overstatement; phrase as “less morphologically rich than Czech.”
- Dataset link: Provide a link for WMT'14 as done for WMT'15.
- Bibliography: Several references are incomplete and need fixing.
- Typos: Multiple text errors to correct (examples provided by reviewer, e.g., "are the bilingual, parallel corpora provided" → "is/are..." and other specific corrections).
