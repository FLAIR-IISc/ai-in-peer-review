- Paper proposes DCNMT: a deep character-level NMT architecture with source word-encoding and target word-decoding layers that create intermediate sub-word representations.  
- Strength: Well-structured, clearly written, and presents an original idea bridging character-level input/output with internal sub-word representations; preliminary results are promising.  
- Weakness: Empirical comparison to strong baselines (e.g., bpe2char) is unconvincing given marginal BLEU gains and no reported statistical significance tests.  
- Weakness: Training-time and computational-cost comparisons are incomplete and unclear (DCNMT much slower on Cs–En; bpe2char time missing for En–Cs; En–Fr not evaluated).  
- Weakness: Claims about superior morphological learning are anecdotal and lack formal quantitative evaluation; recommend rigorous analysis or moving qualitative results to an appendix and softening claims.  
- Minor: Define all acronyms at first use (e.g., LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA).  
- Minor: Clarify the "energy" concept and its interpretation in Section 5.2, and explicitly define "peak" used in Figure 5.  
- Minor: Figure 4a rendering issue—energy colors not shown for every character.  
- Minor: Table 1: clarify whether the Chung et al. (2016) Cs–En results were re-implemented or sourced from the original paper.  
- Minor: Rephrase characterization of French as “morphologically poor” (use more nuanced wording) and provide a link to the WMT’14 training corpora for reproducibility.  
- Minor: Consistency/formatting issues (e.g., inconsistent BPE capitalization) and several incomplete bibliography entries.  
- Minor: Numerous typos and grammatical errors; paper needs careful proofreading (several example corrections provided).
