- Introduces a novel encoder–decoder NMT that uses character-level input/output with intermediate morpheme and character representations.
- Public release of source code and pretrained models for FR-EN, CS-EN, and EN-CS enables validation, replication, and extension.
- Model learns rapidly, producing reasonable translations after a single epoch and improving in subsequent epochs.
- System learns effective morphological segmentation and handles out-of-vocabulary and nonce words via morpheme-level representations.
- Paper fails to specify which WMT test/dev sets were used for evaluation, making score comparison difficult (only documented in the README).
- Software documentation is weak: missing explicit training/testing command examples and does not support a standard --help flag.
- Discrepancy between the text’s described six-level architecture and Figure 2’s depiction; figure/caption needs clarification or a complete diagram.
- Experimental comparisons are limited to other character-based systems and do not contextualize results against WMT state-of-the-art, which is much higher.
- Manuscript contains minor English grammar, spelling, and LaTeX formatting errors that should be corrected.
- Overall, the paper is a valuable contribution to character-based neural machine translation.
