- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, original, and presents interesting results.
- Proposed novel architecture (DCNMT) adds deep word-encoding/decoding layers to combine character-level NMT with sub-word efficiency.
- Authors claim DCNMT better learns morphology, but empirical evidence is insufficient.
- BLEU improvements over baselines are very small (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs); statistical significance not reported.
- Training-time comparison is incomplete: Cs→En bpe2char is faster by 8 days, En→Cs training time not reported, and bpe2char not evaluated for En→Fr.
- Overall experimental comparisons to state-of-the-art (e.g., bpe2char) are incomplete and don’t clearly substantiate claimed advantages.
- Morphology analysis (Section 5.2) is qualitative with only illustrative examples and lacks formal quantitative evaluation.
- Recommendation to move morphology analysis to an appendix and soften claims about learned morphology unless further evidence is provided.
- Several acronyms are undefined or defined after first use; define all acronyms at first occurrence for clarity.
- The concept of “energy” needs a brief recap and intuitive interpretation in Section 5.2; define “peak” used in Figure 5.
- Inconsistent BPE casing (defined uppercase, later written lowercase “bpe”); correct for consistency.
- Statement about not using monolingual corpora seems unnecessary and could be removed.
- Figure 4a appears to have missing color-coding for some energy values.
- Table 1 contains Cs→En results for Chung et al. (2016) that don’t appear in the original paper; clarify if these were recomputed by the authors.
- Description of French as “morphologically poor” is misleading; better to say less morphologically rich than Slavic languages like Czech.
- Provide consistent URLs for WMT’15 and WMT’14 training corpora and complete bibliographic references.
- Multiple typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript need correction (several specific examples noted).
