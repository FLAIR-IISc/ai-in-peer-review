- Proposes a deep hierarchical character-level NMT with a novel word encoder creating intermediate word representations to reduce character-sequence length.  
- Main contributions: six-RNN architecture, a weighted-sum word encoder aimed at learning morphology, and empirical results on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En.  

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated architecture combining character-level I/O with word-level intermediate processing.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation that makes the complex model understandable and reproducible.  
- Strength: Qualitative evidence that the model handles OOVs, misspellings, and unseen morphological variants effectively.  

- Weakness: Empirical comparisons to strong baselines are unconvincing; BLEU improvements over bpe2char are marginal and lack significance testing.  
- Weakness: Training-efficiency comparisons are incomplete and sometimes unfavorable; baseline training times are missing or not reported for some language pairs.  
- Weakness: Claims that the model learns morphology are inadequately supported—analysis is qualitative, anecdotal, and lacks quantitative validation.  

- Question: Please clarify the source of the bpe2char Cs–En results and state whether you ran those experiments and performed statistical significance tests.  
- Question: Provide an intuitive interpretation of the word-encoder "energy" metric and explain the "peak" markers used in visualizations.  

- Suggestion: Define all acronyms on first use for clarity.  
- Suggestion: Replace inaccurate phrasing calling French "morphologically poor" with a more precise comparison to Czech.  
- Suggestion: Fix Figure 4a rendering error and report missing bpe2char training time for En–Cs in Table 1.  
- Suggestion: Correct incomplete bibliography entries and provide consistent corpus links for WMT'14/WMT'15.
