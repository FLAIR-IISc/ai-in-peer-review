- Proposes DCNMT: a character-level NMT with two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to address large vocabularies and OOVs.  
- Claims the model learns morphology better than state-of-the-art and evaluates on English→French and English→Czech.  
- Contribution: novel character-level architecture using two RNNs and a hierarchical decoder.  
- Contribution: demonstrates the model generates meaningful word representations and captures morphology.  
- Contribution: reports outperforming other approaches on BLEU score and training time.  
- Strength: paper is well-structured and clearly explains the architecture and components.  
- Strength: provides a detailed analysis of the model’s morphology learning.  
- Strength: experimental results show effectiveness on EN→FR and EN→CS tasks.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to BLEU and training time and lacks statistical significance testing and broader metrics.  
- Weakness: morphology-learning claims are not fully supported by rigorous, formal evaluation beyond illustrative examples.  
- Weakness: minor presentation issues — undefined acronyms, inconsistent formatting, and incomplete references.  
- Question: please provide details on the statistical significance testing used for performance comparisons.  
- Question: how will you extend formal evaluation of the model’s morphology learning in future work?  
- Question: clarify the meaning of “energy” in Section 3.1 and its relation to morphology learning.  
- Additional: paper needs more detailed explanation of model components, hierarchical decoder, and PCA usage.  
- Additional: provide fuller training details (optimizer, number of epochs, etc.).
