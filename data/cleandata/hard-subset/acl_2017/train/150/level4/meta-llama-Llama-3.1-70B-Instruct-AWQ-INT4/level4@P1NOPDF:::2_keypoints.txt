- Strength: Paper is well-structured, clear, and presents original, interesting results.
- Weakness: Doubts about interpretation of results and some claims lacking solid evidence.
- Contribution: Proposes a character-level NMT with deep word-encoding/decoding layers to map characters↔sub-word representations.
- Motivation: Aims to combine character-level flexibility with reduced sequence length via intermediate sub-word representations.
- Claim: Authors assert the deep encoder learns morphology better than state-of-the-art, but evidence is weak.
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences against bpe2char are very small (0.04–0.1) and no statistical significance testing is reported.
- Training-time concern: bpe2char is faster (Cs-En: 8 days less); En-Cs training time is not reported and En-Fr lacks bpe2char comparison.
- Comparison recommendation: More complete and fair comparisons with bpe2char and other baselines are needed.
- Morphology analysis concern: Section 5.2 provides only illustrative examples without formal experiments or quantitative evaluation to support morphology claims.
- Suggestion for morphology section: Expand into a separate paper or move to appendix and soften claims for current submission.
- Clarity: Many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA) are undefined or defined after use; recommend defining all acronyms up front.
- Energy concept: Introduced in Section 3.1 but should be refreshed and better interpreted in Section 5.2 (e.g., what high energy implies, define peaks).
- Presentation quibbles: Inconsistent casing for BPE and unnecessary mention of no monolingual corpus in Section 4.1.
- Figure issue: Figure 4a appears to miss color-coded energy values for some characters.
- Table concern: Table 1 includes Chung et al. (2016) Cs-En results not reported in the original paper—authors should state if they recomputed them.
- Linguistic wording: Avoid calling French "morphologically poor"; say "less rich than Slavic languages" instead.
- Reproducibility: Provide WMT'14 link as done for WMT'15 and fix several incomplete references.
- Typos and grammar: Multiple small errors throughout manuscript (list provided) that should be corrected.
