- Proposes a novel NMT architecture that learns morphology using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical character-level decoder.
- Model comprises six recurrent networks trained end-to-end to avoid large vocabulary issues and improve efficiency.
- Reports higher BLEU than a bpe-based model after one epoch on En→Fr and En→Cs.
- Starts and ends with characters while using morpheme and character representations internally.
- Authors release code and final learned models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs for reproducibility.
- Produces reasonable-quality translations after the first epoch with further improvements over training.
- Learns plausible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words via morpheme-level backing off.
- Paper does not specify which WMT test/dev sets were used, hindering exact comparisons.
- README instructions are lacking clear examples and could be improved.
- Figure 2’s diagram appears inconsistent with the described 6-level architecture, causing confusion.
- Comparisons omit state-of-the-art MT systems and should acknowledge/discuss this limitation.
- Contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX formatting issues to correct.
- Request for more details on the hierarchical decoder’s operation and training procedure.
- Asks how out-of-vocabulary words are handled in the model.
- Requests full hyperparameter details and tuning methodology for the experiments.
- Wants comparisons against other state-of-the-art NMT systems on the same datasets.
- Seeks deeper analysis of the learned morphological tokenizations and their impact on translation quality.
