- Proposes a novel character-based encoder-decoder NMT that leverages both morphemes and characters for improved translation.
- Releases code and trained models (fr-en, cs-en, en-cs) as open-source for validation and extension.
- System shows reasonable translation quality from the first epoch and improves noticeably in later epochs.
- Model implicitly learns morphological tokenizations and handles unseen or nonce words via morpheme reliance.
- Paper fails to explicitly state the WMT test/dev sets used (info only in README: newstest2015/newstest2013), impeding direct comparisons.
- Software README lacks explicit run examples and a --help flag; usability could be improved.
- Figure 2 and caption do not clearly represent the described 6-layer architecture; an additional figure or clearer caption is needed.
- Results compare only to other character-based systems and omit comparisons to state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and published WMT/matrix.statmt.org results.
- Paper contains minor English disfluencies, spelling errors, and LaTeX issues that should be corrected.
- Overall, the paper is a valuable contribution to character-based NMT and provides useful open-source resources.
