Strengths:

The authors propose a novel character-based encoder-decoder neural machine translation (NMT) approach, leveraging both morphemes and characters to facilitate effective translation. 
The open-source release of the authors' code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs language pairs enables validation and extension of their work by the broader research community.
The reported system demonstrates reasonable translation quality from the initial training epoch, with noticeable improvements in subsequent epochs.
Moreover, the system appears to learn suitable morphological tokenizations and handle unseen words, including nonce words, by implicitly relying on morphemes.

Weaknesses:

A critical omission in the paper is the explicit specification of the WMT test and development sets used to report the results, hindering direct comparisons with existing work. Although this information is available in the code supplement's README file (newstest2015 and newstest2013), it should have been clearly stated in the paper.
The instructions provided in the software README are adequate but can be improved with explicit examples for running training and testing commands. Furthermore, the software should support a --help flag, which is currently not available.
The paper describes a 6-layer architecture, but Figure 2 appears to depict fewer layers. The caption should provide a clearer explanation, and if the figure does not represent all layers, an additional figure (possibly in an appendix) should be included to illustrate the complete architecture.
The results only compare the proposed system to other character-based neural systems, neglecting state-of-the-art results for other types of machine translation systems. The paper should acknowledge and discuss these omissions, as superior results are reported on WMT and matrix.statmt.org.
Minor English disfluencies, spelling errors, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reverse quotation marks) should be corrected to enhance the paper's overall quality.

General Discussion:

This paper constitutes a valuable contribution to the existing literature on character-based neural machine translation, offering a novel approach and open-source resources for further research and development.
