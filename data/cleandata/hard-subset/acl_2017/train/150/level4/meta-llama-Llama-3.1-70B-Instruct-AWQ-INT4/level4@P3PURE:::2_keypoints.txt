- Strength: paper is well-structured, clear, with original ideas and interesting results.  
- Weakness: doubts about interpretation of results.  
- Weakness: claims that the method learns morphology are not properly backed by evidence.  
- Contribution: proposes DCNMT — character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding layers to produce sub-word intermediate representations.  
- Goal: combine character-level advantages (smaller vocab, handling unseen words) with sub-word representations to shorten input sequences and improve performance.  
- Claim: DCNMT allegedly learns morphology better than state-of-the-art approaches.  
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences versus bpe2char are very small (0.04 Cs-En, 0.1 En-Cs) and no statistical significance testing is reported.  
- Training-time comparison incomplete/unfavorable: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs-En; En-Cs training time missing; En-Fr bpe2char not evaluated.  
- Recommendation: perform more complete and statistically rigorous comparisons with bpe2char.  
- Section 5.2 concern: morphology analysis consists only of illustrative examples without formal experiments or quantitative evaluation.  
- Recommendation: expand morphology experiments, soften claims, or move section to appendix.  
- Writing clarity: many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE) are undefined or defined after use; define them earlier.  
- Concept clarity: "energy" is introduced in 3.1 but needs re-explanation and interpretation in 5.2; clarify what a peak signifies (e.g., morpheme boundary).  
- Presentation: Figure 5’s concept of "peak" is not described; Figure 4a has color/energy display issues.  
- Consistency: inconsistent casing of the BPE acronym.  
- Minor: unnecessary statement about not using monolingual corpus in Section 4.1.  
- Table/claims: Table 1’s Cs-En results for Chung et al. (2016) were not in the original paper and should be explicitly stated if computed by the authors.  
- Terminology: avoid calling French "morphologically poor"; rephrase to “less rich than Slavic languages like Czech.”  
- Reproducibility: provide link for WMT'14 as done for WMT'15 and fix several incomplete references.  
- Typos: multiple grammatical/typo errors throughout (examples provided), requiring correction.
