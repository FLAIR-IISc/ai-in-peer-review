**Strengths:**

The authors present a novel adaptation of encoder-decoder neural machine translation (MT) that operates on characters, morphemes, and characters. This approach offers a unique perspective on character-based neural MT. 

The authors have made their code and final learned models for French-English, Czech-English, and English-Czech publicly available, which facilitates validation of their work and enables others to replicate and extend their research.

The system demonstrates reasonable translation quality even after the first training epoch, with continued improvements in subsequent epochs.

The system appears to learn reasonable morphological tokenizations and can handle previously unseen words, including nonce words, by implicitly backing off to morphemes.

**Weaknesses:**

The paper does not explicitly specify which WMT test and development sets were used to report the results. This omission hinders readers from comparing the reported results to existing work, such as the results on matrix.statmt.org. The reviewer had to consult the code supplement's README to determine that the test set was newstest2015 and the development set was newstest2013. This information should have been clearly stated in the paper.

The instructions in the software README are adequate but could be improved. Adding explicit examples for running training and testing commands would enhance the usability of the software. Furthermore, the software should respond to a --help flag, which is currently not implemented.

The paper describes a 6-level architecture, but Figure 2 appears to depict fewer than 6 layers. The caption should provide a clearer explanation, and if the figure does not show all the layers, an additional figure (possibly in an appendix) should be included to illustrate the complete architecture.

The results are compared to other character-based neural systems, but do not include state-of-the-art results for other types of MT systems. The authors should acknowledge that the state-of-the-art results reported on WMT and matrix.statmt.org are higher than those presented in the paper and discuss the implications.

Minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues, such as reverse quotation marks, should be corrected.

**General Discussion:**

This paper is a valuable contribution to the existing literature on character-based neural MT, offering a unique approach to encoder-decoder neural MT.
