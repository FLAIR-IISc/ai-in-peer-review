- Proposes a novel deep character-level NMT architecture using two RNNs to learn preceding-character representations and word-level weighting, with a hierarchical decoder.
- Aims to address large vocabulary and OOV issues of word-level NMT by explicitly learning morphology.
- Claims the model learns morpheme representations and composition rules, producing distributed word representations.
- Reports efficient training and higher BLEU than a BPE-based baseline after one epoch on En→Fr and En→Cs.
- Novel and original architecture is a clear strength.
- Demonstrated ability to learn morpheme-like representations is highlighted as a strength.
- Empirical training efficiency and early BLEU gains are reported as strengths.
- Evaluation is not comprehensive and lacks comparison with other state-of-the-art systems.
- Claim that the model learns morphology better than prior work is not convincingly supported by evidence.
- Paper lacks formal, quantitative evaluation of morphological learning; provided examples are insufficient.
- Requests a more comprehensive evaluation including comparisons to SOTA systems.
- Requests formal tests of morphological learning with experiments and statistical significance.
- Asks for clarification of the “energy” concept in Section 3.1 and interpretation guidance in Section 5.2.
- Many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA) are undefined and should be clarified.
- Minor presentation issues: BPE capitalization inconsistency, unnecessary note about monolingual corpus, missing color labels in Figure 4a.
- Unclear provenance of Chung et al. (2016) Cs→En results; not clear if authors recomputed them.
- Incorrect claim about French being morphologically poor; French is less rich than Slavic languages but not poor.
- Missing link for WMT'14, several incomplete references, and multiple typos throughout the paper.
