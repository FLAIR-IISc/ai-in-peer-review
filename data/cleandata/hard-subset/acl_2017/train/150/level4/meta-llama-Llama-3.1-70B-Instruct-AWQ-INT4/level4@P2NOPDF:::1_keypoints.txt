- Novel encoder-decoder MT adaptation operating on characters, morphemes, and characters, offering a unique perspective on character-based neural MT.
- Code and final learned models (Fr-En, Cs-En, En-Cs) are publicly released for validation and replication.
- System shows reasonable translation quality after the first training epoch with further improvements in later epochs.
- Model learns plausible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words by implicitly backing off to morphemes.
- Paper omits explicit specification of which WMT test/dev sets were used (reviewer had to find newstest2015/newstest2013 in the README).
- Software README lacks explicit run examples and the program does not implement a --help flag; usability could be improved.
- Figure 2 and caption are unclear about the claimed 6-level architecture; a complete depiction or additional figure is needed.
- Results are compared only to other character-based systems and do not include state-of-the-art non-character MT baselines; authors should acknowledge higher WMT/matrix.statmt.org scores and discuss implications.
- Minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reverse quotation marks) need correction.
- Overall, the paper is a valuable contribution to character-based neural MT.
