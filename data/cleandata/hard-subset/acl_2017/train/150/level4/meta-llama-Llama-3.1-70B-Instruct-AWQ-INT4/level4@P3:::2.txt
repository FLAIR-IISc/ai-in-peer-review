**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (NMT) by introducing a hierarchical decoder and a word encoder that learns morphology. The model consists of six recurrent neural networks and is trained end-to-end. The authors claim that their model can learn morphology better than other state-of-the-art approaches and achieves competitive results with word-level and character-level models.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel architecture for deep character-level NMT that learns morphology using a hierarchical decoder and a word encoder.
2. A deep word-encoding model that can learn sub-word representations of the source-language sentence.
3. Experimental results showing that the proposed model achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art word-level and character-level models.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel and original architecture for deep character-level NMT that learns morphology.
2. The authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of their model and its components.
3. The experimental results are interesting and show the potential of the proposed model.

**Weaknesses**

1. The evaluation of the model is limited, and the authors do not provide statistical significance information for the comparison with other state-of-the-art systems.
2. The section on learning morphology is not well-supported by scientific evidence and seems to be based on examples and comments rather than formal experiments.
3. There are several typos and formatting issues throughout the paper.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more information on the evaluation of the model, including statistical significance information for the comparison with other state-of-the-art systems?
2. Can you provide more evidence to support the claim that the proposed model can learn morphology better than other state-of-the-art approaches?
3. Can you clarify the concept of energy and its interpretation in the context of the word encoder?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper would benefit from a more detailed explanation of the acronyms used, such as LSTM, HGRU, CNN, and PCA.
2. The concept of energy should be refreshed in Section 5.2, and hints should be provided on how to interpret it.
3. The acronym BPE should be consistently written in capital letters or lower case throughout the paper.
4. The results for model (3) (Chung et al. 2016) for Cs-En should be clearly indicated as being computed by the authors themselves.
5. The paper should be proofread to correct typos and formatting issues.
