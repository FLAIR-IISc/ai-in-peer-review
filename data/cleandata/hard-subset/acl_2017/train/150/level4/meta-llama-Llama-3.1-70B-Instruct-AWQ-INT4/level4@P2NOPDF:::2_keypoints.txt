- Paper is well-structured, clear, presents original ideas, and reports interesting results.
- Reviewer doubts the interpretation of results and finds morphology-learning claims insufficiently supported.
- Proposed model: deep word-encoding and decoding layers on top of character-level NMT to produce sub-word intermediate representations.
- Claimed benefit: combine character-level flexibility with shorter input sequences via sub-word encoding.
- Comparison to bpe2char shows tiny BLEU differences (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) with no statistical significance reported.
- Training-time comparison incomplete/unfavorable: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs→En; En→Cs training time not reported; En→Fr bpe2char not evaluated.
- Morphology analysis (Section 5.2) is anecdotal (examples only), lacking formal experiments or quantitative evaluation.
- Suggestion: expand morphology experiments into a full study or move current section to appendix and soften claims.
- Many acronyms are undefined or introduced after use (e.g., LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE); define them consistently.
- "Energy" concept needs a refresher and interpretation in Section 5.2 (e.g., meaning of high energy or peaks); Figure 5 peaks not explained.
- Inconsistent BPE capitalization across the paper.
- Questionable necessity of stating that no monolingual corpus is used in Section 4.1.
- Figure 4a has visualization issues (energy colors missing for some characters).
- Table 1 likely contains authors-computed results for Chung et al. (2016) Cs→En and should explicitly state this.
- Characterization of French as "morphologically poor" is imprecise; suggest rewording.
- WMT corpus references inconsistent: WMT'15 linked but WMT'14 link missing.
- Several references are incomplete and need fixing.
- Multiple typos and grammatical errors throughout (specific corrections listed by reviewer).
