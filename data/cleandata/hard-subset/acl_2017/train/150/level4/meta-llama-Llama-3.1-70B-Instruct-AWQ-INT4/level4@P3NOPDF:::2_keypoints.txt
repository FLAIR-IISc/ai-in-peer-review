- Strength: Paper is well-structured, clear, and easy to follow.
- Strength: Ideas are original and results are interesting.
- Weakness: Doubts about interpretation of the results.
- Weakness: Claims that the method learns morphology are not properly supported by evidence.
- Contribution: Proposes a character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and word-decoding layers to produce sub-word intermediate representations.
- Motivation: Aims to combine character-level benefits (smaller vocabulary, handling unseen words) with shorter input sequences via sub-word representations.
- Claim: Authors assert the model (DCNMT) learns morphology better than prior approaches.
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences vs. bpe2char are very small (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and no statistical significance testing is reported.
- Evaluation concern: Training-time comparison unfavorable—bpe2char for Cs→En trains 8 days faster; En→Cs training time is not reported and bpe2char is not evaluated for En→Fr.
- Recommendation: A more complete and statistically grounded comparison with bpe2char is needed to demonstrate DCNMT's advantages.
- Morphology analysis concern: Section 5.2 presents only qualitative examples without formal experiments or evaluations supporting morphology-learning claims.
- Suggestion: Extend the morphology analysis (possibly as a separate paper) or move it to an appendix and soften the claims.
- Clarity issue: Many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE) are undefined or defined after use; define consistently.
- Concept issue: The notion of "energy" is introduced in Section 3.1 but should be refreshed and better interpreted in Section 5.2; "peak" in Figure 5 is not explained.
- Presentation issue: Inconsistent capitalization of BPE throughout the paper.
- Minor comment: Saying no monolingual corpus is used in Section 4.1 may be unnecessary.
- Figure issue: Figure 4a seems to miss color-coded energy values for some characters.
- Table/reporting issue: Table 1 includes Chung et al. (2016) Cs→En results not reported in originals—if computed by authors, this should be stated.
- Language note: Do not call French "morphologically poor"; phrasing should be that it's less rich than Slavic languages like Czech.
- Resource/documentation issue: Link provided for WMT'15 but not for WMT'14; several references are incomplete.
- Typos: Multiple grammatical and spelling errors (examples provided by reviewer to fix).
