- Novel encoder-decoder MT that operates on characters and morphemes; an innovative contribution to character-based neural MT.  
- Authors released code and final models for French-English, Czech-English, and English-Czech, aiding replication.  
- System yields reasonable translation quality after the first training epoch and improves with further epochs.  
- Learns plausible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen/nonce words by implicitly backing off to morphemes.  
- Paper fails to explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (README shows dev=newstest2013, test=newstest2015), complicating comparisons.  
- README instructions are adequate but should include explicit command examples and a functional --help flag.  
- Figure 2 does not clearly depict the described 6-level architecture; a complete architecture figure or clarification is needed.  
- Results are only compared to other character-based systems and do not acknowledge higher state-of-the-art WMT results.  
- Several minor issues (English disfluencies, misspellings, LaTeX formatting errors) should be corrected.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with useful public resources, but needs clearer experimental details, broader comparisons, and presentation fixes.
