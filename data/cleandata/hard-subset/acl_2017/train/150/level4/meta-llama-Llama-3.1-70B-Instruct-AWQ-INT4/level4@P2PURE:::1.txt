- Strengths:
The authors propose a novel adaptation of encoder-decoder neural machine translation (MT) that operates at the character level, while incorporating morpheme representations in the intermediate stages. 
The release of both the code and the learned models for French-English, Czech-English, and English-Czech is a valuable contribution, facilitating the validation and extension of this work by others.
The system demonstrates reasonable translation quality from the initial training epoch, with continued improvement in subsequent epochs.
Moreover, the system appears to learn plausible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen words, including nonce words, by implicitly relying on morphemes.

- Weaknesses:
The paper lacks explicit specification of the WMT test and development sets used to report the results, hindering comparison with existing work (e.g., results on matrix.statmt.org). This information is only available in the code supplement's README, indicating newstest2015 as the test set and newstest2013 as the development set. This crucial detail should have been clearly stated in the paper.
The instructions in the software README are satisfactory but could be improved with explicit examples for training and testing commands. Additionally, the software should respond to a --help flag, which is currently not supported.
The paper describes a 6-level architecture, but Figure 2 appears to depict fewer layers. The caption should provide a clearer explanation, and if the figure does not show all layers, a supplementary figure (possibly in an appendix) should be included to illustrate the complete architecture.
The results only compare the proposed system to other character-based neural MT systems, without considering state-of-the-art results for other types of MT systems. The reported results are lower than the state-of-the-art results available on WMT and matrix.statmt.org, which should be acknowledged and discussed.
Finally, the paper contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues, such as incorrect quotation marks, which should be corrected.

- General Discussion:
This paper is a welcome contribution to the existing literature on character-based neural MT, offering a novel approach and valuable resources for the research community.
