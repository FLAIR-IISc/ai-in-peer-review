- Novel model: character-level encoder-decoder MT that internally uses both morpheme and character representations.  
- Resources released: code and final models for French-English, Czech-English, and English-Czech are provided.  
- Fast learning: system yields reasonable translation quality after the first epoch and improves in later epochs.  
- Robust morphology: model learns sensible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words via implicit morpheme backing-off.  
- Missing dataset details: paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (README reveals newstest2015/test and newstest2013/dev).  
- Documentation improvements: README lacks explicit run examples for training/testing and the software should support a --help flag.  
- Figure/architecture mismatch: paper claims a 6-level architecture but Figure 2 depicts fewer layers; caption and/or an expanded figure (appendix) are needed.  
- Incomplete comparisons: results are compared to other character-based systems but do not acknowledge or compare to state-of-the-art non-character MT results (SOTA gap unaddressed).  
- Minor errors: several English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reverse quotation marks) should be corrected.  
- Overall value: paper is a valuable contribution to character-based neural MT with potential to advance the field.
