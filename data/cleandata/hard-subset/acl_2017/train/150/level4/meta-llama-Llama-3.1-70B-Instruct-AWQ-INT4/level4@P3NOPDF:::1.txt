**Strengths:**

The authors present a novel adaptation of encoder-decoder neural machine translation (MT) that operates on characters, morphemes, and characters. This approach is innovative and contributes to the existing literature on character-based neural MT.

The authors have made their code and learned models for French-English (fr-en), Czech-English (cs-en), and English-Czech (en-cs) publicly available. This is a significant contribution, as it allows others to validate and build upon their work.

The system appears to produce reasonable translation results even after the first training epoch, with continued improvement in subsequent epochs. Furthermore, the system demonstrates the ability to learn reasonable morphological tokenizations and handle previously unseen words, including nonce words, by implicitly backing off to morphemes.

**Weaknesses:**

The authors do not explicitly state which WMT test and development sets their results are reported on. This omission makes it challenging for readers to compare the reported results to existing work, such as the results available on matrix.statmt.org. Although this information is provided in the README file of the code supplement, it should have been clearly stated in the paper. Specifically, the test set used was newstest2015, and the development set used was newstest2013.

The instructions provided in the software README are adequate but could be improved. The training and testing sections would benefit from explicit examples of how to run the respective commands. Additionally, the software should respond to a --help flag, which is currently not implemented.

The paper describes a 6-level architecture, but the diagram in Figure 2 appears to show fewer than 6 layers. The caption should be more explicit, and if this figure does not depict all the layers, an additional figure (even in an appendix) should be included to illustrate the complete architecture.

The results presented in the paper only compare the proposed system to other character-based neural systems. However, the state-of-the-art results for other types of MT systems, as reported by WMT and matrix.statmt.org, are significantly higher than the results presented in this paper. This discrepancy should be acknowledged and discussed.

Finally, there are several minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues, such as reverse quotation marks, that need to be corrected.

**General Discussion:**

This paper is a nice contribution to the existing literature on character-based neural MT. The authors' novel approach and publicly available code and models make this work valuable for the research community. However, addressing the mentioned weaknesses will enhance the overall quality and impact of the paper.
