Paper proposes a novel deep character-level NMT with a hierarchical decoder and a morphology-learning word encoder (six RNNs trained end-to-end).  
Authors claim the model learns morphology better and achieves competitive results versus word- and character-level baselines.  
Main contribution: novel architecture combining hierarchical decoder and word encoder for morphology learning.  
Main contribution: deep word-encoding model that learns sub-word representations of source sentences.  
Main contribution: experimental results showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art models.  
Strength: original architecture for morphology-aware deep character-level NMT.  
Strength: clear, detailed explanation of model and components.  
Strength: experimental results are interesting and show potential.  
Weakness: evaluation is limited and lacks statistical significance testing versus baselines.  
Weakness: claims about learning morphology are not supported by rigorous scientific evidence, relying on examples/comments.  
Weakness: several typos and formatting issues throughout the paper.

Request: provide stronger, systematic evidence that the model learns morphology better than alternatives.
