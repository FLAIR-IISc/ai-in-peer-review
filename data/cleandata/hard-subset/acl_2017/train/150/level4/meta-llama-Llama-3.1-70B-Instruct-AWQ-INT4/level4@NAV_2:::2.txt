**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

The paper proposes a novel architecture for deep character-level neural machine translation (NMT) by learning morphology. The model consists of two recurrent neural networks and a hierarchical decoder, which translates at the character level. The main contributions of this work are:

1. The proposed architecture learns morphology and achieves a high translation performance comparable to state-of-the-art NMT models.
2. The model avoids the large vocabulary issue and is more efficient in training than word-based models and conventional character-based models.
3. The hierarchical decoder is able to generate a character sequence based on the output of the first-level decoder, making the model efficient to train.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a well-structured and clear explanation of the proposed architecture and its components.
2. The results obtained are quite interesting and demonstrate the effectiveness of the model in achieving high translation performance.
3. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the model's ability to learn morphology, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. The interpretation of the results is not entirely clear, and some claims regarding the capability of the method proposed to learn morphology are not properly backed by scientific evidence.
2. The comparison with other state-of-the-art systems is not comprehensive, and the advantages of the proposed model are not clearly demonstrated.
3. The evaluation of the model's ability to learn morphology is limited to a few examples and lacks formal evaluation.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Could you provide more information on the statistical significance of the results, particularly in the comparison with other state-of-the-art systems?
2. Could you elaborate on the concept of energy and how it is used in the model, particularly in Section 5.2?
3. Could you provide more details on the evaluation of the model's ability to learn morphology, including any experiments or formal evaluation that may have been conducted?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper could benefit from defining acronyms and technical terms more clearly, particularly for readers who may not be familiar with the field of deep learning.
2. The concept of energy could be refreshed in Section 5.2, and some hints provided on how to interpret it.
3. The acronym BPE should be consistently capitalized or lowercased throughout the paper.
4. The mention of no monolingual corpus being used in Section 4.1 could be clarified or removed if not necessary.
5. Figure 4a appears to be missing color labels for energy values.
6. The results for model (3) (Chung et al. 2016) for Cs-En should be clearly attributed to the authors' own computation or cited from the original paper.
7. The characterization of French as morphologically poor could be revised to reflect its actual linguistic properties.
8. A link to WMT'14 training corpora could be provided for consistency.
9. Several references appear to be incomplete and should be verified.
