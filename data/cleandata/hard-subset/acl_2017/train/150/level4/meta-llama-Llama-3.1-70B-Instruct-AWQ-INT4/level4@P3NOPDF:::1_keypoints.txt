- Novel encoder-decoder MT that operates on characters and morphemes is an innovative contribution to character-based neural MT.  
- Authors released code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs publicly.  
- System produces reasonable translations after one epoch, improves with training, and learns morphological tokenizations and handles unseen/nonce words via morpheme backing-off.  
- Paper omits explicit statement of which WMT test/dev sets were used (they used newstest2015 and newstest2013; this was only in the README).  
- README instructions are adequate but need explicit command examples and a working --help flag.  
- Figure 2 and its caption are unclear about the claimed 6-level architecture; the figure should depict all layers or an additional figure should be added.  
- Results are only compared to other character-based systems and do not acknowledge higher state-of-the-art scores reported by WMT/matrix.statmt.org.  
- Several minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reverse quotation marks) need correction.  
- Overall: valuable contribution with public resources, but addressing the above weaknesses will improve clarity and impact.
