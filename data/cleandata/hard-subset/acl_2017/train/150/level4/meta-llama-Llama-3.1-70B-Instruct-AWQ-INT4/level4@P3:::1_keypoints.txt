- Proposes DCNMT, a novel hierarchical character-level NMT architecture using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder to learn morphology and avoid large vocabularies.
- Claims higher BLEU than a BPE-based model after one epoch on En–Fr and En–Cs, with final BLEU comparable to state-of-the-art.
- Contribution: a hierarchical architecture that starts/ends with characters and uses intermediate morpheme/character representations.
- Contribution: efficient training via a GRU variant in the second-level decoder.
- Contribution: demonstrates the model’s ability to learn morphology through experiments and analysis.
- Strength: novel character-to-morpheme-to-character adaptation of encoder–decoder NMT.
- Strength: authors release code and trained models (fr–en, cs–en, en–cs) for reproducibility.
- Strength: produces reasonable translation quality even after the first epoch, improving with more training.
- Strength: learns plausible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words by backing off to morphemes.
- Weakness: paper does not specify which WMT test/dev sets were used, hindering direct comparisons.
- Weakness: README and software usability are poor; software lacks a --help flag and clearer instructions.
- Weakness: Figure 2 is unclear about the number of layers; full-layer diagrams should be provided (e.g., in an appendix).
- Weakness: comparisons omit state-of-the-art non-character MT systems; paper should acknowledge and discuss higher SOTA results.
- Weakness: contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reverse quotation marks) that need fixing.
- Question: please detail how the model handles OOV words not seen during training.
- Question: how will the model’s performance be improved on longer sentences, given current results focus on shorter sentences?
- Question: please provide more analysis of the learned morphology and its impact on translation quality.
