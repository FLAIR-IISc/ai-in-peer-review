- Novel encoder-decoder NMT that starts and ends with characters but uses morpheme/character representations internally.  
- Avoids large-vocabulary issues of word-based models and is more training-efficient than word/char baselines.

- Contribution: proposes an architecture that learns morphology via two recurrent networks and a hierarchical character-level decoder.  
- Contribution: achieves higher BLEU than BPE after one epoch on English–French and English–Czech.  
- Contribution: final BLEU is comparable to state-of-the-art systems.

- Strength: presents a novel char→morph→char adaptation of encoder-decoder NMT.  
- Strength: releases code and final models for French–English, Czech–English, and English–Czech for reproducibility.  
- Strength: produces reasonable translation quality after the first epoch and improves with more training.  
- Strength: learns reasonable morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen/nonce words via morpheme backing-off.

- Weakness: paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (only found in README: newstest2015/test, newstest2013/dev).  
- Weakness: software README lacks clear run examples and the tool does not respond to a --help flag.  
- Weakness: Figure 2/caption is unclear about the claimed 6-level architecture and may not show all layers.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and do not acknowledge higher WMT SOTA scores.
