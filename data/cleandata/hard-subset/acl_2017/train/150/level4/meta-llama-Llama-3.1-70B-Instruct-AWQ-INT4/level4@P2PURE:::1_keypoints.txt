- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder character-level NMT that incorporates morpheme representations in intermediate stages.  
- Releases code and learned models for French-English, Czech-English, and English-Czech, aiding reproducibility and extension.  
- System shows reasonable translation quality from the first epoch and continues improving in later epochs.  
- Learns plausible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words via morpheme-level reasoning.  
- Paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/development sets were used (only found in README), hindering comparisons.  
- Software README lacks explicit training/testing command examples and the program does not support a --help flag.  
- Described 6-level architecture is unclear because Figure 2 appears to show fewer layers; caption or a full supplementary figure is needed.  
- Results are only compared to other character-based MT systems; the paper omits comparisons to broader state-of-the-art MT and should acknowledge lower scores on WMT/matrix.statmt.org.  
- Contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., incorrect quotation marks) that should be fixed.  
- Overall, a welcome contribution to character-based neural MT with a novel approach and valuable released resources.
