- Proposes DCNMT: a novel hierarchical character-level NMT with two recurrent networks that learns morphology, avoids large vocabularies, and yields comparable results on en-fr, en-cs, cs-en.
- Novel hierarchical architecture that learns morphology and translates at the character level, addressing word-level NMT limitations.
- Efficient training: model produces reasonable translations even after the first epoch and scales to large datasets.
- Learns morphological tokenizations and handles unseen words by implicitly backing off to morphemes.
- Novel encoder-decoder adaptation that begins and ends with characters while using morpheme/character representations internally.
- Authors release code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, enabling validation and extension.
- Produces usable translation quality early in training with continued improvement across epochs.
- Demonstrates sensible learned morphological segmentations and robust handling of unknown words.
- Unclear which WMT test/dev sets were used, making comparisons to prior work difficult.
- Software README lacks explicit runnable examples and clearer instructions.
- Paper claims a 6-level architecture but Figure 2 appears to show fewer layers, causing confusion.
- Results are not state-of-the-art against other MT systems and this limitation should be acknowledged and discussed.
- Contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues that need fixing.
- Request for more details on how the hierarchical decoder functions and why it trains efficiently on large datasets.
- How will the approach be extended to additional languages and datasets?
- Request for deeper insight into how the model learns morphological tokenizations and handles unseen words.
- How will the authors address limitations such as the lack of comparison to other state-of-the-art MT systems?
