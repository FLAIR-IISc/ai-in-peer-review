- Strength: Paper is well-structured and clear, with original ideas and interesting results.
- Weakness: Doubts about interpretation of results and unsupported claims about learning morphology.
- Contribution: Proposes DCNMT with deep word-encoding/decoding layers to map characters ↔ sub-word representations within an encoder-decoder NMT.
- Claim: Authors assert DCNMT combines benefits of character-level NMT with intermediate sub-word representations and better morphology learning.
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences versus bpe2char are very small (0.04 for Cs→En, 0.1 for En→Cs) and no statistical significance testing is reported.
- Training-time concern: bpe2char trains 8 days less than DCNMT for Cs→En; En→Cs training time for DCNMT is missing and bpe2char is not evaluated for En→Fr.
- Recommendation: Conduct a more comprehensive and fair comparison with bpe2char (including significance tests and full training-time reporting).
- Morphology analysis issue: Section 5.2 presents only qualitative examples without formal experiments or quantitative evaluation to support morphology-learning claims.
- Recommendation for morphology section: Either move it to an appendix and soften claims or expand into a separate paper with formal evaluation.
- Writing clarity: Many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE) are undefined or defined after first use and should be clarified.
- Energy concept: Reintroduce and clarify “energy” in Section 5.2, explain interpretation of high energy and define what a “peak” means in Figure 5.
- Consistency: Inconsistent capitalization of BPE (sometimes upper, sometimes lower) should be fixed.
- Unnecessary detail: Not clear if mentioning that no monolingual corpus is used in Section 4.1 is necessary.
- Figure issue: Figure 4a appears to have missing color values for some characters (visualization bug).
- Table issue: Table 1 reports Chung et al. (2016) results for Cs→En that are not in the original paper—if computed by authors, this should be explicitly stated.
- Language/content nuance: Do not label French as “morphologically poor”; instead state it is less morphologically rich than Slavic languages like Czech.
- Missing links and references: Provide a link for WMT’14 as done for WMT’15 and complete several incomplete references.
- Typos and grammar errors: Multiple typos and grammatical mistakes throughout (examples provided by reviewer) that should be corrected.
