**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel architecture for character-level neural machine translation (NMT) that learns morphology using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder. The model, called Deep Character-Level Neural Machine Translation (DCNMT), aims to address the issues of large vocabulary and out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words in traditional word-level NMT models. The authors claim that their model can learn morphology better than other state-of-the-art approaches and demonstrate its effectiveness on English-to-French and English-to-Czech translation tasks.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. The authors propose a novel architecture for character-level NMT that learns morphology using two recurrent networks and a hierarchical decoder.
2. The model is able to learn morphology and generate meaningful representations of words, which is demonstrated through experiments and analysis.
3. The authors show that their model can outperform other state-of-the-art approaches in terms of BLEU score and training time.

**Strengths of the Submission**

1. The paper is well-structured and clear, making it easy to follow the explanation of the proposed architecture and its components.
2. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the model's ability to learn morphology, which is a key contribution of the paper.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model on English-to-French and English-to-Czech translation tasks.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

1. The evaluation of the model's performance is limited to two parameters: training time and BLEU score. A more comprehensive evaluation, including statistical significance testing, would strengthen the paper's claims.
2. The authors' claims about the model's ability to learn morphology are not fully supported by scientific evidence. While the examples provided in Section 5.2 are well-chosen and explained, a more formal evaluation of the model's morphology learning capabilities would be beneficial.
3. There are several minor issues with the paper, including undefined acronyms, inconsistent formatting, and incomplete references.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the statistical significance testing used to evaluate the model's performance?
2. How do you plan to extend the evaluation of the model's morphology learning capabilities in future work?
3. Can you clarify the concept of "energy" used in Section 3.1 and how it relates to the model's ability to learn morphology?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper would benefit from a more detailed explanation of the model's components, including the hierarchical decoder and the use of PCA for dimensionality reduction.
2. The authors should consider providing more information on the training process, including the optimization algorithm used and the number of epochs trained.
3. The paper's references are incomplete and should be updated to include all necessary information.
