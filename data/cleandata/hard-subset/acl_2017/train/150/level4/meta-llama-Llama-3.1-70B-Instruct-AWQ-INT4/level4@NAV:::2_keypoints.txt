- Strengths: Paper is well-structured, clear, and presents original ideas with interesting results.  
- Weaknesses: Concerns about result interpretation and insufficient scientific evidence for claims about learning morphology.  
- General: Proposes a complex character-level NMT with deep word-encoding/decoding aiming to leverage sub-word representations and claim superior morphology learning.  
- Evaluation concern: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are minimal, no statistical significance reported, and training-time comparisons are incomplete—need a more comprehensive comparison.  
- Learning morphology concern: Section 5.2 lacks formal experiments to support morphology claims; suggest moving to appendix and softening claims.  
- Clarity/consistency: Several acronyms are undefined or defined late (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA); the energy concept is introduced but not explained or revisited where used.  
- Minor issues: Inconsistent BPE capitalization, incorrect Figure 4a energy display, unclear source/computation of model (3) results, misleading statement about French morphology, inconsistent corpus links, incomplete references.  
- Typos: Multiple grammatical and typographical errors throughout (several specific examples provided by reviewer).
