**Strength:** Generally, the paper is well-structured and clear. Most of its explanations are possible to follow, the concepts presented are fundamental, and the results are quite interesting.

**Weaknesses:** I have some doubts about the explanations of the results. Furthermore, I believe that some claims about the proposed method being able to learn morphology are not sufficiently supported by scientific evidence.

**General Discussion:**

This paper explores a complex architecture for character-level neural machine translation (NMT). The proposed architecture extends a classical encoder-decoder architecture by adding a new deep word-encoding layer that enables character-level input to be encoded into character-level representations of source-language sentences. Similarly, a deep word-decoding layer is added to the output to transform character sequences into target-language sub-word representations as the final output of the NMT system. The purpose of such an architecture is to take advantage of the benefits of character-level NMT (reduced vocabulary size and flexibility to deal with invisible words) and at the same time improve the performance of the entire system by using a intermediate representation of sub-words to reduce the size of the input sequence of characters. In addition, the authors claim that their deep word-encoding model is able to learn morphology better than other advanced methods.

I have some concerns about the evaluation. The authors compare their method with other advanced systems by considering two parameters: training time and BLEU score. However, I do not clearly see the advantage of the proposed model (DCNMT) over other methods (such as bpe2char). According to the BLEU score, the difference between the two methods is very small (0.04 in Cs-En and 0.1 in En-Cs), and it is difficult to say which one surpasses the other without statistically significant information: has the statistical significance been evaluated? In terms of training time, it is worth mentioning that bpe2char takes 8 days less than DCNMT for Cs-En. Training time for En-Cs is not given (why not?) and bpe2char for En-Fr is not evaluated. I think a more complete comparison with this system should be done to prove the advantages of the proposed model.

My second concern is in section 5.2, where the authors begin to claim that their system is able to learn morphology. However, the section only contains examples and some comments on them. Although these examples are very well-chosen and explained in a very educational way, it is noticeable that no tests or formal evaluations have been done to support the authors' claim. I would certainly encourage the authors to expand this interesting part, which could even be a separate paper. On the other hand, this section does not seem to be an important topic of the paper, so for the current work, I can only suggest moving this section to an appendix and softening some of the claims made about the system's ability to learn morphology.

**Other Comments, Doubts, and Suggestions:**

* Many abbreviations are used here that are not defined (such as LSTM, HGRU, CNN, or PCA) or which were only defined after they started being used (such as RNN or BPE). Although some of these abbreviations are well-known in the field of deep learning, I would encourage the authors to define them to improve clarity.
* The concept of strength is first mentioned in section 3.1. Although the given explanation is sufficient at that moment, it would be good to refresh the concept of strength in section 5.2 (where it is used several times) and give some indication of how it should be explained: will a higher strength on a character indicate that the current form should be split at that moment? In addition, the concept of top (in figure 5) is not described.
* When the BPE abbreviation is defined, capital letters are used, but for the rest of the reference, it is small; is there any reason for this?
* I am not sure whether a monolingual corpus is used in section 4.1.
* It seems there is something wrong in figure 4a because the strength value is not shown for each character.
* In table 1, the results of model (3) (Chung et al. 2016) for Cs-En are not taken from the paper, as they are not reported. If the authors themselves calculate these results (as it seems), they should mention it.
* I would not say that it is linguistically poor, but rather that it is not as rich as Slavic languages like Czech.
* Why is a link given for the WMT'15 training corpus but not for WMT'14?
* Several references are incomplete
