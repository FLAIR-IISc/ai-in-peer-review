- Strengths: paper is well-organized, clearly written, presents novel ideas, and reports compelling results.
- Weakness: uncertainties in result interpretation and claims about the model learning morphology are not adequately supported.
- Model: proposes deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding layers to convert character sequences to sub-word representations and back.
- Goal: combine character-level NMT benefits (small vocab, OOV handling) with intermediate sub-word representations to shorten input sequences and improve performance.
- Claim: DCNMT can better learn morphology than prior state-of-the-art methods.
- Evaluation — BLEU: reported BLEU gains over bpe2char are minimal (0.04 Cs–En, 0.1 En–Cs) and no statistical significance testing is reported.
- Evaluation — training time: bpe2char trains 8 days faster for Cs–En; En–Cs training time is omitted and bpe2char is not evaluated for En–Fr, making comparisons incomplete.
- Recommendation: perform more comprehensive and statistically rigorous comparisons with competing systems (e.g., bpe2char) across languages and runtimes.
- Morphology analysis: Section 5.2 is largely illustrative examples and commentary without formal experiments; recommend tempering claims or moving to an appendix and expanding with rigorous evaluation.
- Acronyms: several acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, etc.) are undefined or defined after first use—define them consistently.
- Energy concept: clarify the definition and interpretation of "energy" and the meaning of "peaks," especially in Section 5.2 (e.g., relation to morpheme boundaries).
- BPE inconsistency: capitalization of BPE is inconsistent and should be standardized.
- Unnecessary statement: noting "no monolingual corpus is used" in Section 4.1 may be unnecessary.
- Figure issue: Figure 4a appears to omit energy values for some characters and needs correction.
- Table 1: if the authors computed missing prior results (Chung et al., 2016) themselves, this should be explicitly stated.
- French description: avoid calling French "morphologically poor"; say "less morphologically rich compared to Slavic languages like Czech."
- Dataset links: provide consistent links for WMT corpora (WMT'14 link missing while WMT'15 is linked).
- References: several references are incomplete and must be fixed.
- Typos and grammar: multiple typographical and grammatical errors throughout (examples provided) that should be corrected.
