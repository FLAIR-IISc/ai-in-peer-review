- Proposes DCNMT: deep word-encoding and -decoding layers to map character-level input to sub-word representations and back, aiming to combine char-level benefits with shorter input sequences and improved morphology learning.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, original, and reports interesting results.  
- Primary evaluation concern: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are marginal (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Training-time comparison incomplete/unfavorable: bpe2char trains 8 days faster for Cs→En; En→Cs training time not reported; bpe2char not evaluated on En→Fr, preventing full comparison.  
- Recommendation: perform a more thorough, statistically tested evaluation against bpe2char to substantiate claimed advantages.  
- Morphology claims (Section 5.2) are based on illustrative examples without formal quantitative experiments; either expand into a separate study or move to appendix and tone claims.  
- Acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE) are often undefined or defined after first use; define them upfront.  
- “Energy” concept needs a refresher in Section 5.2 and clearer interpretation guidance (e.g., does high energy indicate morpheme split?); clarify what “peak” means in Figure 5.  
- Inconsistent capitalization of BPE; maintain consistency.  
- The statement about not using monolingual corpora in Section 4.1 is unnecessary.  
- Figure 4a’s color mapping for energy values appears incomplete and should be fixed.  
- Table 1: results for model (3) (Chung et al. 2016) for Cs→En appear recomputed and this should be explicitly noted.  
- Calling French “morphologically poor” is inaccurate; phrase as “less morphologically rich than Slavic languages (e.g., Czech).”  
- Provide a link to WMT’14 training corpora in addition to WMT’15 for reproducibility.  
- Several references are incomplete and should be fixed.  
- Multiple typos throughout (examples provided); run thorough proofreading and correct errors such as subject-verb agreement and missing words.
