- Paper proposes DCNMT: a hierarchical character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and word-decoding layers that map characters → sub-word representations → characters.
- Main contribution: a novel recurrent word encoder and hierarchical decoder that aim to learn morphological word representations and enable efficient character-level decoding.
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates competitive BLEU on En–Fr and En–Cs while avoiding large vocabularies.
- Tertiary contribution: qualitative analysis suggesting the model can learn morphological structure.
- Strength: novel architecture combining RNN-based morphological analysis with character-level decoding to reduce vocabulary size and capture subword info.
- Strength: achieves BLEU scores comparable to state-of-the-art, with improvements over baseline character-level models.
- Strength: character-level design inherently addresses OOVs and can translate misspelled or nonce words.
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing for BLEU differences (e.g., Cs–En 0.04, En–Cs 0.1), so claims of superiority are uncertain.
- Weakness: comparison to bpe2char is incomplete—missing training time for En–Cs, En–Fr not evaluated, and bpe2char trains faster on Cs–En.
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative evidence for morphological learning; PCA and energy visualizations are qualitative and lack formal metrics or baseline comparisons.
- Question: Have statistical significance tests been run for BLEU comparisons? If so, provide p-values.
- Question: Please provide bpe2char training time on En–Cs.
