The authors present a novel encoder-decoder neural machine translation (MT) approach that uniquely combines character-level processing at the input and output with morpheme- and character-level representations internally. The public release of both code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs translation is a significant contribution, facilitating both validation of the presented work and further research by the community. The system demonstrates promising translation quality even after initial training, with continued improvement observed across subsequent epochs. Notably, the system learns plausible morphological tokenizations and exhibits an ability to handle out-of-vocabulary words – including novel words – by effectively leveraging morphemic representations. 

However, the paper lacks explicit specification of the WMT test and development sets used for evaluation, hindering direct comparison with existing results reported on resources like matrix.statmt.org. This information was only discoverable within the code’s README file, which indicates newstest2015 for the test set and newstest2013 for development. This detail should be clearly stated in the paper itself. 

The documentation provided in the software README is adequate but could be improved. Providing concrete examples for running training and testing commands would enhance usability. Furthermore, the software lacks a --help flag to display command-line options.

The paper refers to a 6-layer architecture, but the diagram in Figure 2 depicts fewer than six layers, creating confusion. The figure caption requires clarification. If the diagram does not represent the complete architecture, an additional figure (potentially in an appendix) illustrating all layers should be included. 

While the results provide comparisons to other character-based neural MT systems, they do not include comparisons to state-of-the-art results from other MT approaches. WMT and matrix.statmt.org report higher performance levels on these datasets, and this discrepancy should be acknowledged and discussed. 

Finally, the paper contains several minor errors in English, including disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., incorrect quotation marks) that should be addressed.

Overall, this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of character-based neural MT.
