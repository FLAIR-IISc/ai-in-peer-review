- Novel deep character-level NMT architecture with a recurrent word encoder and hierarchical decoder.  
- Demonstrated competitive BLEU scores on En–Fr and En–Cs comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Preliminary analysis suggests the model can learn morphological information.  

- Hierarchical, morphology-aware architecture is a creative solution to vocabulary size and OOV issues.  
- Achieves competitive BLEU performance, indicating effectiveness.  
- Authors claim efficient training despite model depth; supported partially by reported training times.  
- Character-level operation inherently addresses the out-of-vocabulary problem.  

- No statistical significance testing; reported BLEU gains over baselines are very small.  
- Incomplete baseline comparison (missing bpe2char training time for En–Cs and no En–Fr bpe2char evaluation).  
- Insufficient quantitative evidence for morphology learning; current analysis is largely qualitative.  
- Presentation issues: undefined acronyms, inconsistent capitalization, typographical errors, and unclear explanation of “energy.”
