- Proposes a novel hybrid NMT architecture combining character-level input/output with morpheme-level intermediate representations.  
- Method improves handling of morphological complexity and robustness to rare/unseen (including nonce) words via implicit morpheme fallback.  
- Public release of code and pre-trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs enhances reproducibility.  
- Continued training yields observable quality improvements across multiple epochs, with reasonable results after the first epoch.  
- Fails to explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used in the paper (only in README), hindering direct comparison to established benchmarks.  
- README lacks concrete training/testing command examples and a standard --help flag, reducing usability.  
- Paper/figure discrepancy: describes a 6-level architecture but Figure 2 shows fewer layers; figure caption or a full diagram is needed.  
- Results compare mainly to other character-based NMT but omit comparisons to non-character-based state-of-the-art on the same datasets.  
- Contains minor errors (grammar, spelling, LaTeX/quotation issues) that require proofreading.
