- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and presents original ideas with interesting results.  
- Proposed DCNMT architecture: character-level encoder/decoder with deep word-encoding/decoding layers mapping between characters and sub-word representations.  
- Motivation: combine character-level advantages (small vocab, OOV robustness) with sub-word representations to shorten sequences and boost performance.  
- Authors claim DCNMT learns morphology better than prior methods, but evidence for this is insufficient.  
- Main evaluation concern: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are marginal (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and need statistical significance testing.  
- Training-time comparison problematic: bpe2char shows an 8-day advantage for Cs→En; En→Cs training time is missing and bpe2char not evaluated for En→Fr.  
- Need a more comprehensive comparison with bpe2char (include En→Cs and En→Fr training times and significance tests).  
- Section 5.2 on morphological learning presents illustrative examples but lacks formal experimental evaluation or quantitative evidence.  
- Suggest moving the morphology examples to an appendix and tempering claims until stronger evidence is provided.  
- Many acronyms are undefined or defined after first use (e.g., LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE); definitions and consistency are needed.  
- The concept of “energy” should be reintroduced in Section 5.2 with clearer interpretation (e.g., whether high energy marks morpheme boundaries); define “peak” in Figure 5.  
- Inconsistent casing of BPE (capital vs lowercase) should be fixed.  
- Statement about not using a monolingual corpus in Section 4.1 is unnecessary and can be removed.  
- Figure 4a color mapping appears faulty — not all energy values are represented.  
- Table 1: Cs→En results for Chung et al. (2016) are not sourced; if recomputed by authors, they must state this explicitly.  
- Describing French as “morphologically poor” is imprecise; better to say it’s less morphologically rich than Slavic languages like Czech.  
- WMT’15 training corpora link provided, but WMT’14 link is missing — add it.  
- Several references are incomplete and need fixing.  
- Multiple typos and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript that should be corrected (list provided by reviewer).
