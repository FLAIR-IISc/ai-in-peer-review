- Paper proposes DCNMT: character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and word-decoding layers to map characters to sub-word representations and back, aiming to shorten input sequences and capture morphology.
- Strength: Paper is well-structured, clearly written, presents original ideas, and reports interesting results.
- Weakness: Reviewer questions the interpretation of results and finds the morphological-learning claims insufficiently supported by empirical evidence.
- Evaluation: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are minimal (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and no statistical significance testing is reported.
- Training-time comparison is incomplete: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs→En, En→Cs training time is not reported, and bpe2char is not evaluated for En→Fr.
- Recommendation: Provide a more comprehensive comparison with bpe2char (including training times and significance tests) to substantiate claimed advantages.
- Morphology analysis (Section 5.2) relies on illustrative examples without formal experiments; suggest expanding into a proper evaluation or moving to an appendix and softening claims.
- Acronyms: Several acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA) are undefined or defined after first use; definitions should be added or moved earlier.
- Energy concept: Section 5.2 needs a refresher on the “energy” concept and guidance on interpretation (e.g., whether high energy indicates morpheme boundaries); Figure 5’s peak is not explained.
- BPE casing inconsistency: BPE is presented in uppercase then lowercase; clarify the reason or standardize usage.
- Unnecessary statement: The claim about lack of monolingual corpora in Section 4.1 appears unnecessary and should be removed.
- Figure issues: Figure 4a appears incomplete—energy values are not consistently colored for all characters.
- Table 1: Cs→En results attributed to Chung et al. (2016) were not sourced from their paper; authors should state if they recomputed those results.
- Language characterization: Describing French as “morphologically poor” is imprecise; it should be phrased as less morphologically rich than languages like Czech.
- Data links and references: Link to WMT’15 corpus provided but not WMT’14; several references are incomplete and should be fixed.
- Typos and grammatical errors: Multiple typographical and grammatical mistakes are listed (e.g., subject-verb agreement, misspellings, missing words) and should be corrected.
