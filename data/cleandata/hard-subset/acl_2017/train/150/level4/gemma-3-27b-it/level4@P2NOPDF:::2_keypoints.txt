- Paper proposes a complex character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and decoding layers to map between characters and sub-word units.  
- Motivation: combine character-level benefits (smaller vocab, OOV handling) with efficiency of shorter sub-word sequences and improved morphological learning.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, original, and presents interesting results.  
- Major evaluation concern: marginal BLEU gains over bpe2char (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) without statistical significance testing.  
- Training-time comparison weak: bpe2char is much faster for Cs→En (8 days less) and training times are missing for En→Cs and En→Fr.  
- Request for more comprehensive comparison with bpe2char including significance tests and complete training-time data to substantiate superiority claims.  
- Morphology claims are insufficiently supported: Section 5.2 uses illustrative examples without formal quantitative experiments.  
- Recommendation to either perform rigorous morphological evaluation (or separate publication) or move the qualitative analysis to an appendix and tone down claims.  
- Many acronyms are undefined or defined after first use (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE); define them up front for clarity.  
- The “energy” concept (Section 3.1) and interpretation of peaks in Figure 5 are not clearly explained; clarify what high energy or peaks indicate.  
- Inconsistent capitalization of “BPE” and unnecessary statement about not using monolingual corpora in Section 4.1.  
- Figure 4a appears to have incomplete color mapping for energy values.  
- Table 1 reports Chung et al. (2016) Cs→En results not in the original paper—state if results were recomputed.  
- Characterizing French as “morphologically poor” is inaccurate; say “less morphologically rich than Czech” instead.  
- Provide a link to WMT’14 corpora (currently only WMT’15 linked) and fix several incomplete references.  
- Multiple minor typographical and grammatical errors throughout the text (list of specific corrections provided by reviewer).
