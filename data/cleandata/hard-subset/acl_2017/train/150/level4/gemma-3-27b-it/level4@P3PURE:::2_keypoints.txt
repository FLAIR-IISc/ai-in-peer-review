- Strength: Paper is well structured, clear, presents original ideas, and reports interesting results.  
- Main claim: DCNMT adds deep word-encoding/decoding layers to map between character input/output and sub-word representations.  
- Evaluation concern: BLEU improvements over bpe2char are very small (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and no statistical significance testing is reported.  
- Training-time comparison incomplete: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs→En, En→Cs training time is missing, and bpe2char is not evaluated for En→Fr.  
- Morphology claim weak: Section 5.2 uses illustrative examples but lacks formal experiments or quantitative evidence to support claims about learning morphology.  
- Recommendation for morphology section: Expand into a proper evaluation (or move to appendix) and tone down morphological-learning claims.  
- Acronym issues: Many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, etc.) are undefined or defined after first use, harming clarity.  
- "Energy" concept unclear: Introduced in Sec 3.1 but needs revisiting in Sec 5.2 with interpretation guidance (e.g., relation to morpheme boundaries and what a “peak” means).  
- BPE inconsistency: BPE is inconsistently capitalized (upper- vs lowercase) without explanation.  
- Unnecessary statement: The comment about not using monolingual corpora in Sec 4.1 seems superfluous.  
- Figure issues: Fig. 4a appears to have incomplete color mapping for some energy values; Fig. 5 lacks explanation of “peaks.”  
- Table/reporting issue: Cs→En results for Chung et al. (2016) are not in the original paper—authors should state if they computed them.  
- Language characterization: Claim that French is morphologically poor is misleading; better to say it is less morphologically rich than Slavic languages.  
- Dataset/linking omission: WMT’15 link provided but WMT’14 link is missing.  
- References incomplete: Several citations are incomplete and need fixing.  
- Typos and grammar: Multiple grammatical and typographical errors throughout (list provided by reviewer).
