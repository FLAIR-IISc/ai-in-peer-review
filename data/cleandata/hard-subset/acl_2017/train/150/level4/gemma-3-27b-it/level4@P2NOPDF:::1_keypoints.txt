- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder NMT that uses characters, morphemes, then characters—a promising approach for morphology and rare words.  
- Releases code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, aiding reproducibility and further research.  
- Shows promising translation quality early in training with continued improvement over epochs.  
- Model appears to learn plausible morphological tokenizations and effectively handle out-of-vocabulary words via implicit morpheme decomposition.  
- Paper fails to clearly state the specific WMT test and development sets used, hindering direct comparison to prior work.  
- Reviewer had to consult the code README to find that newstest2015 and newstest2013 were used; this information should appear in the paper.  
- Released code documentation is functional but needs concrete examples for running training and testing commands.  
- Software lacks a `--help` flag, reducing immediate usability.  
- Figure 2 is confusing: the text describes a 6-level architecture but the figure shows fewer layers; caption and a more complete diagram are needed.  
- Results section compares only to other character-based systems and omits broader state-of-the-art comparisons from WMT/matrix.statmt.org, which outperform this work.  
- Manuscript contains minor issues (grammar, misspellings, LaTeX inconsistencies) that reduce polish and readability.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution to character-based NMT and useful released resources, but addressing the above weaknesses would significantly strengthen the paper.
