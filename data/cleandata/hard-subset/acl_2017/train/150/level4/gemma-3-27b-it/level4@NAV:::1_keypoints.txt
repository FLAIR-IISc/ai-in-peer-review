- Introduces a novel encoder-decoder NMT using character-level input/output with morphemic and character-level internal representations.  
- Public release of code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, aiding reproducibility and future work.  
- Reports promising translation quality with noticeable improvements from the first epoch and continued refinement over training.  
- Demonstrates effective morphological tokenization and implicit fallback handling for out-of-vocabulary and novel words.  
- Paper fails to state which WMT test/dev sets were used, impeding direct comparison with prior work (README shows newstest2015 test and newstest2013 dev).  
- Software documentation is adequate but should include concrete training/testing examples and a --help flag to improve usability.  
- Figure 2 and its caption do not clearly depict the described 6-level architecture; a complete architecture figure (e.g., in an appendix) is needed.  
- Results compare to other character-based systems but do not benchmark against state-of-the-art MT approaches; higher scores on WMT/matrix.statmt.org should be acknowledged and discussed.  
- Contains minor English grammar, spelling, and LaTeX formatting issues (e.g., incorrect quotation marks) that should be fixed.  
- Overall, the paper is a valuable contribution to character-based neural machine translation.
