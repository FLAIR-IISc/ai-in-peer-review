- Novel hierarchical character-level NMT that learns morphology via a word encoder (two RNNs) and a hierarchical decoder, forming a deep model of six recurrent networks.  
- Avoids large vocabulary bottleneck and claims more efficient training than word-based or conventional character-based models.  
- Proposes HGRU (a gated unit) as a second-level decoder that initializes character-level state from word-level outputs and uses an unfolding matrix for efficient batch training.  
- Reports higher BLEU than a BPE-based baseline after one epoch on En–Fr and En–Cs, with final BLEU comparable to state-of-the-art character-level systems.  
- Demonstrates the model learns meaningful morpheme-like subword units and word representations that generalize to unseen words (e.g., translates nonce/misspelled words).  
- Claims the deep model is relatively small/simple and efficient to train despite having six RNNs; trained on a single GTX Titan X.  
- Authors publicly release code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, aiding reproducibility.  
- Experiments use WMT En–Cs and En–Fr corpora with only tokenization and 120 most frequent characters; reviewer notes dev=test splits are newstest2013/dev and newstest2015/test but this is not clearly stated in paper.  
- Weakness: paper does not explicitly state which WMT development/test sets were used, hindering direct comparison.  
- Weakness: software README and usage instructions are adequate but need clearer command examples and a --help flag.  
- Weakness: discrepancy between the claimed six-level architecture and Figure 2’s depiction; figure and caption need clarification or a full diagram.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit state-of-the-art non-character approaches and should acknowledge/have discussion of higher-performing results available online.  
- Minor issues: contains English disfluencies, typos, and LaTeX formatting errors that should be fixed.  
- Questions for authors: explicitly state the WMT dev/test sets used, clarify the total number of layers shown in Figure 2, and discuss performance relative to other state-of-the-art approaches on the same datasets.
