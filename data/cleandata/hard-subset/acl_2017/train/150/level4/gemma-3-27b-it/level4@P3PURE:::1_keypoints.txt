- Novel encoder-decoder adaptation combining character-level input/output with morpheme and character representations in intermediate layers.  
- Release of code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, facilitating validation and further research.  
- System achieves encouraging translation quality early (reasonable after one epoch) and improves with continued training.  
- Model appears to learn effective morphological tokenizations and can handle out-of-vocabulary and nonce words via morphemic cues.  
- Paper omits explicit statement of the WMT test/dev sets used (README reveals newstest2015 test and newstest2013 dev).  
- README/training instructions are adequate but need command-line examples and the software should provide a --help flag.  
- Paper/figure mismatch: describes a 6-level architecture but Figure 2 shows fewer layers; figure caption unclear and full architecture should be shown (e.g., in appendix).  
- Experimental comparisons omit state-of-the-art non-character-based systems on WMT; the performance gap should be acknowledged and discussed.  
- Minor issues in English grammar, spelling, and LaTeX formatting require careful proofreading.  
- Overall assessment: a valuable contribution to character-based neural machine translation.
