- Proposes a novel deep character-level NMT with word-encoding and word-decoding layers to map between characters and sub-word representations.
- Architecture is clearly explained and combines recurrent networks with a hierarchical decoder in an original way.
- Experimental results are promising and BLEU scores are competitive with existing methods across En-Fr, En-Cs, and Cs-En.
- Claims that the word encoder learns morphology better than prior approaches and that sub-word intermediate representations reduce input sequence length.
- BLEU improvements over baselines (e.g., bpe2char) are marginal, often only 0.04–0.1 points.
- No statistical significance testing is reported, so superiority of the model is uncertain.
- Training time comparisons are incomplete or missing for some baselines and language pairs (e.g., bpe2char En-Cs and En-Fr).
- The morphological learning section (5.2) uses illustrative examples but lacks formal, quantitative evaluation.
- Suggests moving the morphology section to an appendix and softening claims until rigorous proof is provided.
- Raises practical concerns since bpe2char trains faster for Cs-En, questioning real-world advantages of DCNMT.
- Requests authors to report statistical significance tests for BLEU differences.
- Asks authors to provide bpe2char training time on En-Cs.
- Asks what formal experiments (e.g., probing tasks) could validate learned morphology.
- Requests clarification on incomplete energy values shown in Figure 4a.
- Notes many acronyms are undefined or defined after first use; recommends consistent early definitions.
- Asks for clearer explanation of the “energy” metric in Section 5.2 and how to interpret high values.
- Points out inconsistent capitalization of "BPE" and recommends fixes.
- Flags unnecessary statement about not using monolingual data in Section 4.1.
- Notes that some results (Chung et al. 2016) appear author-computed and should be explicitly labeled as such.
- Challenges the characterization of French as “morphologically poor”; suggests more precise wording relative to Czech.
- Requests a link to WMT'14 training corpora to match WMT'15 link.
- Notes several incomplete references that should be fixed.
- Lists multiple typographical and grammar errors throughout the paper that need correction.
