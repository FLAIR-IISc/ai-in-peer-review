- Proposes a novel encoder-decoder MT model combining character-level input/output with internal morpheme- and character-level representations.  
- Public release of code and trained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs is a significant, reproducibility-friendly contribution.  
- System achieves promising translation quality early in training and continues improving across epochs.  
- Model learns plausible morphological tokenizations and effectively handles out-of-vocabulary and novel words via morphemic representations.  
- Paper fails to explicitly state WMT test/dev sets used (only in README: newstest2015 test, newstest2013 dev); this should be included in the paper.  
- Software README is adequate but would benefit from concrete training/testing command examples and a --help flag for CLI options.  
- Paper describes a 6-layer architecture but Figure 2 shows fewer layers; figure caption and/or an additional full-architecture figure are needed for clarity.  
- Results compare to other character-based systems but omit comparisons to state-of-the-art MT results (e.g., WMT/matrix.statmt.org), which should be acknowledged and discussed.  
- Paper contains minor English and LaTeX errors (disfluencies, misspellings, incorrect quotation marks) that should be corrected.  
- Overall, the work is a valuable contribution to character-based neural machine translation.
