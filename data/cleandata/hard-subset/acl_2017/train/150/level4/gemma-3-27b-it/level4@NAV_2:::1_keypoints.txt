- Proposes a novel deep character-level NMT architecture that emphasizes learning morphology with a six-layer recurrent hierarchical decoder.  
- Addresses the large-vocabulary problem in NMT via morphological learning and hierarchical decoding as an alternative to subword methods.  
- Demonstrates competitive BLEU scores on En–Fr and En–Cs and shows strong results even after a single training epoch.  
- Provides empirical evidence the model learns morphological information, generalizes to unseen words, and can translate nonce words reasonably.  
- Architectural innovations include a hierarchical decoder and learned morphological representations.  
- Authors released code and trained models for En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En, aiding reproducibility.  
- Model shows promising initial performance, achieving competitive BLEU quickly compared to baselines.  
- Analyses support the claim that the model captures subword units and morphological patterns.  
- Paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets were used (README notes newstest2015/newstest2013), which should be in the paper.  
- Software documentation is minimal; more detailed training/testing instructions and a --help flag would improve usability.  
- Figure 2 is incomplete or unclear and does not visibly represent all six layers; a more comprehensive diagram is needed.  
- Comparisons focus on other character-based systems; lacks direct comparison/discussion relative to state-of-the-art word- or subword-based models.  
- Contains minor English and LaTeX errors (typos, grammar, incorrect quotes) that require proofreading.  
- Questions for authors: clarify computational cost of morphological component vs standard word embedding lookup.  
- Questions for authors: explain which hierarchical decoder design choices were critical for efficient training and how they were selected.  
- Questions for authors: provide concrete examples of specific morphological patterns the model learns beyond current illustrative cases.
