- Strength: Paper is well-structured, clear, and explanations are easy to follow.  
- Strength: Presents original ideas with interesting results.  
- Weakness: Reservations about interpretation of results.  
- Weakness: Claims that the method learns morphology are not sufficiently supported by evidence.  
- Contribution: Proposes a character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding layers to produce sub-word intermediate representations.  
- Motivation: Aims to combine character-level benefits (small vocab, OOV handling) with shorter input sequences via sub-word representations.  
- Claim: Authors assert the deep word-encoding model learns morphology better than prior methods.  
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences vs. baselines are very small (0.04 Cs–En, 0.1 En–Cs) and statistical significance is not reported.  
- Evaluation concern: Training-time comparison is incomplete and unfavorable—bpe2char takes 8 days less for Cs–En, En–Cs training time is missing, and bpe2char is not evaluated for En–Fr.  
- Request: More comprehensive comparison with bpe2char (including statistical tests and full training-time reporting) is needed.  
- Morphology analysis concern: Section 5.2 provides illustrative examples but lacks formal experiments or quantitative evaluation to support morphology-learning claims.  
- Suggestion: Move the morphology examples to an appendix and tone down claims unless supported by additional experiments.  
- Comment: Many acronyms are used without initial definition or are defined after first use; improve acronym definitions.  
- Comment: The concept of “energy” needs to be reintroduced in Section 5.2 with guidance on interpretation and definition of “peak” in Figure 5.  
- Comment: Inconsistent capitalization of BPE (capitalized then lowercase) should be fixed.  
- Comment: Unclear necessity of mentioning lack of a monolingual corpus in Section 4.1.  
- Issue: Figure 4a appears to have missing color mapping for some characters’ energy values.  
- Issue: Table 1 includes results for Chung et al. (2016) on Cs–En that were not reported in the original paper; the authors should state if they computed these.  
- Comment: Describe French as less morphologically rich than Slavic languages rather than “morphologically poor.”  
- Comment: Provide a link for WMT’14 training corpora as done for WMT’15.  
- Issue: Several references are incomplete and need correction.  
- Typos: Multiple grammatical and typographical errors are listed that require fixing (examples provided).
