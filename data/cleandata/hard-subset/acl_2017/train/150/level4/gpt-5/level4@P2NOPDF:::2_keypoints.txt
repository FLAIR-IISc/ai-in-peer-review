- Strengths: paper is well organized, clear, with original ideas and intriguing results.
- Weakness: some result interpretations are uncertain and not fully supported by evidence.
- Contribution: proposes a character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding layers mapping between characters and subword representations.
- Goal: retain character-level benefits (small vocab, robustness) while improving efficiency by using subword intermediates to shorten sequences.
- Claim: authors assert the model learns morphology better than existing approaches.
- Evaluation concern: comparisons focus only on training time and BLEU, which is insufficient.
- BLEU differences are minimal (0.04 for Cs–En, 0.1 for En–Cs) and significance testing is not reported.
- Training-time reporting is incomplete and unbalanced: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs–En; En–Cs training time is missing; bpe2char not evaluated for En–Fr.
- Recommendation: provide a more complete and balanced comparison with strong baselines such as bpe2char.
- Morphology analysis concern: Section 5.2 presents only a few illustrative examples without formal/quantitative experiments to support morphology claims.
- Recommendation: either expand the morphological analysis into a rigorous study or move the section to an appendix and soften claims.
- Writing/clarity issues: many acronyms are undefined or defined after first use (define LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE at first mention).
- Clarify and re-explain the notion of “energy” in Section 5.2 and define what “peaks” mean in Figure 5.
- Consistency and minor fixes: ensure consistent capitalization of BPE; remove unnecessary note about not using monolingual data.
- Figure/table issues: Figure 4a energy color scale rendering problem; Table 1 reports results for Chung et al. (2016) not present in original paper—state if computed by authors.
- Dataset and references: add WMT’14 link (WMT’15 is linked); check and fix incomplete bibliographic entries.
- Style suggestion: avoid calling French “morphologically poor”; prefer “less morphologically rich than Slavic languages such as Czech.”
- Typos to correct (examples provided): several grammatical and spelling errors throughout (list of specific corrections supplied).
