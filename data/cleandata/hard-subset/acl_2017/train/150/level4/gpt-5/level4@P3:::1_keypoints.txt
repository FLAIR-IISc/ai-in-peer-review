- Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a two-RNN morphology-aware word encoder and a hierarchical HGRU decoder with unfolding for efficient batched character generation; code and pretrained models released.

- Contribution: a practical end-to-end character-level architecture that removes large target softmaxes while keeping encoder sequence lengths near word-level.

- Contribution: empirical evidence of faster early training and competitive BLEU with a relatively small (~54M) model.

- Contribution: analyses showing emergent morphological segmentation and robustness to OOVs, misspellings, and nonce words.

- Strength: addresses OOV and large-vocabulary bottlenecks via a two-RNN encoder that plausibly models morpheme boundaries while using character decoding.

- Strength: training-efficient and compact compared to prior char-based systems, achieving strong BLEU quickly and requiring fewer training days.

- Strength: decoder engineering (HGRU + unfolding) enables efficient batched hierarchical character decoding and is a practical contribution beyond this paper.

- Strength: qualitative analyses (PCA, per-character energies, segmentations) convincingly indicate morphological learning and robustness to misspellings/nonces.

- Strength: release of code and pretrained models facilitates verification and reuse.

- Weakness: dataset splits and evaluation details (specific WMT dev/test, preprocessing, filtering, tokenization) are not clearly stated in the paper and are only in the README.

- Weakness: limited comparison to strong non-character baselines and top WMT systems; claim of being “comparable to state-of-the-art” is overstated without broader baselines.

- Weakness: clarity and consistency issues in model description—Figure 2, caption, and some equations are ambiguous or contain typos, making reproduction harder.

- Weakness: insufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of the word encoder’s weighting mechanism and target-side feedback.

- Weakness: reproducibility gaps—test-time search settings, character vocabulary coverage, and differing training hyperparameters across tasks are under-specified.

- Question: please specify exact train/dev/test splits, preprocessing pipeline, and any filtering for each language pair.

- Question: what beam size, length normalization, stopping criteria were used at test time and were they tuned on dev?

- Question: provide a schematic that clearly enumerates all six RNNs and their connections, and clarify when the HGRU resets vs. continues across characters.

- Question: what is the exact novelty of HGRU versus a standard GRU with an external reset signal; could a conventional GRU replicate it?

- Question: add ablations comparing the word encoder to last-state RNN and CNN, tanh weighting vs. softmax-normalized weights, and with/without target-side word encoder feedback.

- Question: how many dev/test characters map to <unk> under the 120-character vocabulary and how sensitive are results to this choice?

- Question: can induced segmentations be evaluated against morphological analyzers for English/Czech to quantify morphology learning?

- Question: please contextualize BLEU relative to the strongest WMT systems on the same data and temper “state-of-the-art” claims.

- Additional comment: proofread for English and LaTeX typos, equation notation, and inconsistent terms.

- Additional comment: Table 1 should report consistent training settings (epochs, updates, hardware), sentence-length cutoffs, and explicit beam sizes for all compared systems.

- Additional comment: consider releasing end-to-end scripts and adding a --help with example commands to improve reproducibility.
