- Strengths:
  - Proposes a character-in/character-out encoder–decoder NMT that explicitly models morphology in between via a morphology-aware word encoder and a hierarchical character-level decoder. The design is a principled way to sidestep large-vocabulary issues while leveraging subword structure.
  - Provides released code and pretrained models for fr–en, cs–en, and en–cs, which facilitates verification, reproduction, and extension by others.
  - Demonstrates competitive translation quality after just a single training epoch, with continued gains over subsequent epochs.
  - Empirically shows that the model induces sensible morphological segmentations and can translate previously unseen or nonce forms by effectively backing off to morphemic structure.

- Weaknesses:
  - The paper does not clearly specify the exact WMT development and test sets used for reporting results. This complicates comparisons with prior work (e.g., matrix.statmt.org). The only place this reviewer found the specifics (newstest2013 as dev, newstest2015 as test) was in the code README; this should be stated explicitly in the paper.
  - The software README is serviceable but could be improved with concrete command-line examples for training and evaluation. The binaries/scripts should also support a --help option, which is currently missing.
  - Although the text describes a six-level architecture, Figure 2 appears to depict fewer layers. The caption should clarify what is shown, and a complete schematic (even relegated to an appendix) should illustrate all layers.
  - Results are compared mainly against other character-based neural models, but not against state-of-the-art non-character MT systems. WMT benchmarks (including matrix.statmt.org) indicate stronger absolute performance from other system types on these datasets; this gap should be acknowledged and discussed.
  - The manuscript contains minor English issues, typos, and LaTeX formatting problems (e.g., reversed quotation marks) that should be corrected.

- General Discussion:
A solid and timely contribution to the literature on character-based neural MT.
