- Novel encoder–decoder NMT that inputs/outputs characters while using internal morpheme- and character-level representations.  
- Code and final trained models (fr-en, cs-en, en-cs) are released to support replication and extension.  
- System attains reasonable translation quality after the first epoch and improves over subsequent epochs.  
- Learns sensible morphological segmentations and can handle unseen/nonce words by implicitly backing off to morphemes.  
- Paper fails to explicitly state which WMT dev/test sets were used (only in README: test=newstest2015, dev=newstest2013); this should be stated in the paper.  
- Software README is adequate but needs concrete command-line examples and a --help flag for usability.  
- Figure 2 does not clearly depict the described six-layer architecture; caption/appendix should clarify or show the complete architecture.  
- Results omit comparisons to state-of-the-art non-character MT systems (WMT/matrix.statmt.org report higher scores) and should acknowledge/discuss this.  
- Minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reversed quotation marks) need correction.  
- Overall assessment: a solid contribution to character-based neural MT.
