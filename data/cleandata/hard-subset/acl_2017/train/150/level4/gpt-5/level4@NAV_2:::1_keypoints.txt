- Proposes a character-in/character-out encoder–decoder NMT with a morphology-aware word encoder and hierarchical character-level decoder to handle large vocabularies and exploit subword structure.
- Provides released code and pretrained models for fr–en, cs–en, and en–cs, facilitating verification, reproduction, and extension.
- Demonstrates competitive translation quality after a single training epoch with continued improvements in later epochs.
- Empirically induces sensible morphological segmentations and can translate unseen or nonce forms by backing off to morphemic structure.

- Paper does not clearly state the exact WMT development and test sets used (found only in README); this should be explicit in the manuscript.
- Software README needs concrete command-line examples and binaries/scripts should support a --help option.
- Text describes a six-level architecture but Figure 2 seems to show fewer layers; caption should clarify and a complete schematic (e.g., in an appendix) should be provided.
- Results are compared mainly to other character-based models; the paper lacks comparisons/discussion versus state-of-the-art non-character MT systems and WMT benchmark results.
- Manuscript contains minor English issues, typos, and LaTeX formatting problems (e.g., reversed quotation marks) that should be corrected.

- Overall: a solid and timely contribution to the literature on character-based neural MT.
