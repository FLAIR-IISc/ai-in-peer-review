- Proposes DCNMT: a character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and deep word-decoding layers that convert between characters and subword representations.  
- Goal: retain character-level benefits (small vocab, OOV robustness) while improving efficiency by using intermediate subword representations.  
- Authors claim DCNMT learns morphology better than prior methods.  
- Strength: paper is well structured and clearly written.  
- Strength: ideas are original and empirical results are interesting.  
- Weakness: interpretation of some results is questionable.  
- Weakness: claims about morphological learning lack sufficient scientific evidence.  
- Comparative evaluation: no clear advantage over bpe2char—BLEU differences are minimal (0.04 Cs–En, 0.1 En–Cs) and statistical significance is not reported.  
- Training time reporting: bpe2char trains 8 days faster on Cs–En; En–Cs training time omitted; bpe2char not evaluated on En–Fr—comparison is incomplete and unbalanced.  
- Morphology analysis (Section 5.2): only qualitative examples provided; needs formal quantitative experiments or should be moved to appendix and claims softened.  
- Acronyms: several acronyms are undefined or introduced after use (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE); define on first mention.  
- Energy concept: should be briefly revisited in Section 5.2 with guidance on interpretation (e.g., link between high energy and morpheme boundaries) and define “peak” in Figure 5.  
- BPE capitalization inconsistent—make consistent.  
- Section 4.1: stating no monolingual corpus is used may be unnecessary.  
- Figure 4a: color map for energy values appears missing or inconsistent for some characters.  
- Table 1: results for Chung et al. (2016) in Cs–En are not reported in original papers—if authors computed them, this should be stated.  
- Morphology phrasing: say French is “less morphologically rich than Slavic languages such as Czech” instead of “morphologically poor.”  
- Data links: WMT’15 link provided but WMT’14 link is missing—add it.  
- References: several references are incomplete and should be corrected.  
- Typos and phrasing errors listed (several examples provided) and should be fixed for clarity.
