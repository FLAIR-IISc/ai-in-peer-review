- Contribution: Hierarchical character-level NMT with a morpheme-aware two-RNN word encoder and an efficient hierarchical decoder that avoids large-vocabulary softmax.
- Contribution: Empirical results showing competitive BLEU with a relatively small model and promising early-epoch performance versus BPE baselines.
- Contribution: Qualitative evidence that the model is morphologically sensitive and robust to misspellings/nonce words.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated architecture combining position-dependent two-RNN word encoder and hierarchical character decoder.
- Strength: Practical batching/unfolding trick enabling efficient conditioning of the character decoder on word-level states.
- Strength: Compact model (~54M parameters) achieving BLEU comparable to strong character-level baselines and favorable one-epoch learning dynamics.
- Strength: Compresses source characters into word representations to limit encoder length while preserving open-vocabulary benefits.
- Strength: Compelling qualitative examples of robustness to OOVs/misspellings and generally clear exposition and diagrams.

- Weakness: Empirical comparisons are incomplete and inconclusive (very small BLEU gaps, no significance testing, inconsistent training-time baselines).
- Weakness: Claims about learning morphology lack quantitative evaluation (no segmentation F1, correlation analyses, or probing experiments).
- Weakness: Clarity gaps and missing definitions (acronyms undefined; "energy" and "peaks" not operationalized in Section 5.2).
- Weakness: Reproducibility and reporting issues (suspect table entries, inconsistent figures/dataset links, incomplete references).
- Weakness: Some overstatements and imprecise claims (e.g., calling French “morphologically poor”; efficiency/morphology claims stronger than evidence).

- Question: Provide statistical significance (bootstrap/paired tests) and mean/variance across runs for BLEU differences.

- Suggestion: Move or temper Section 5.2 claims unless quantitative morphology experiments are added.
- Suggestion: Fix minor wording/style issues, correct dataset links (WMT’14/’15), and complete bibliographic entries.

- Overall: Well-motivated and promising architecture with competitive results, but requires more rigorous empirical comparisons and quantitative morphology evidence before strong acceptance.
