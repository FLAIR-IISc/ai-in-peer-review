- Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a two-RNN word encoder and a hierarchical settable-state character decoder (HGRU) to reduce vocab/sequence length and handle misspellings.

- Contribution: hierarchical character→subword→word architecture with novel RNN-based word encoder and HGRU enabling end-to-end character-level NMT with shorter sequences.

- Contribution: an efficient training strategy (unfolding decoder outputs over character positions) yielding competitive BLEU with a relatively small model and fewer epochs.

- Contribution: qualitative analyses suggesting the model captures morphological regularities and handles OOV/misspelled words.

- Strength: paper is clear, well organized, and provides detailed, followable model descriptions and intuitive qualitative demos.

- Strength: architectural originality in combining a morphology-aware word encoder with a hierarchical character decoder; practical HGRU and batching/unfolding trick.

- Strength: competitive performance and efficiency (modest ~54M params; one-epoch wins vs. BPE baseline in some settings; qualitative robustness to nonce/misspellings).

- Weakness: reported BLEU improvements over bpe2char are very small and no statistical significance testing is reported.

- Weakness: training-time comparisons are incomplete/inconsistent across language pairs; missing apples-to-apples comparisons with bpe2char (and En–Fr bpe2char absent).

- Weakness: morphology claims are based on illustrative PCA and examples but lack formal quantitative validation (e.g., morphological segmentation accuracy, probing, controlled generalization tests).

- Weakness: presentation problems—many acronyms undefined at first use; "energy" and "peak" are not explained; inconsistent BPE capitalization; overstated phrasing about French morphology.

- Specific presentation/data issues: Figure 4a seems to have missing/incorrect color values; Table 1 cites Cs–En results for Chung et al. (2016b) that are not in that paper without noting reproduction; missing WMT’14 link and incomplete references.

- Request: report statistical significance tests (e.g., bootstrap resampling) for BLEU differences, especially vs. bpe2char.

- Request: provide complete and consistent training-time numbers for all settings (including En–Cs) and include bpe2char for En–Fr.

- Request: if Cs–En results for model (3) were reproduced by the authors, explicitly document the reproduction setup.

- Request: clarify how "energy" and "peak" are computed/normalized and how peaks should be interpreted relative to morpheme boundaries.

- Request: define all acronyms at first use and standardize BPE capitalization.

- Request: fix/explain the color mapping issue in Figure 4a and add the WMT’14 link plus complete missing reference details.

- Additional comments/typos: prefer wording "French is less morphologically rich than Czech"; several minor grammar and typo fixes suggested throughout the manuscript.
