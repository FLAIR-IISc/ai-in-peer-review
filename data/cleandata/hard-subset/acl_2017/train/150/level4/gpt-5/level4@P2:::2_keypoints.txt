- Proposal: a deep character-level NMT with a character-to-word encoder and hierarchical HGRU decoder to avoid large target vocabularies and enable efficient character-level decoding.  
- Empirical claim: achieves BLEU comparable to strong character/subword baselines on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En, often reaching strong performance in fewer epochs and with a smaller model.  
- Analysis claim: the encoder captures subword/morphological structure, supported by case studies and visualizations.  

Strengths
- Architectural motivation: hierarchical design and two-RNN encoder address character-level drawbacks (length, attention) and are linguistically motivated.  
- Training efficiency: model reaches strong BLEU in fewer epochs and can outperform BPE baselines after one epoch in some settings.  
- Robustness: qualitative examples show better handling of misspellings and nonce words than word-level systems.  
- Compactness: despite depth, total parameters (~54M) are smaller than several compared character models.  
- Subword evidence: visualizations suggest the encoder distinguishes morphological forms and plausible subword boundaries without supervision.  

Weaknesses
- Inconclusive comparisons: BLEU gaps are small, no statistical significance or variance reported, and baseline coverage is uneven.  
- Weak morphology evidence: claims of learning morphology rest on selected examples and PCA plots without quantitative evaluation.  
- Reporting gaps: missing training-time numbers, mixed provenance of baselines, and unclear implementation details (e.g., unfold matrix R and decoding latency).  
- Presentation problems: acronyms used before definition, informal terminology (“energy”, “peak”), and figure rendering issues reduce clarity.  
- Overstatements and reference issues: some imprecise claims (e.g., “French is morphologically poor”), incomplete references, and inconsistent dataset links.  

Questions for authors
- Did you run statistical significance tests (e.g., bootstrap) for BLEU differences; please provide p-values or CIs?  
- For Table 1 model (3) (Chung et al., 2016) in Cs–En: did you reproduce these results and are settings comparable?  
- Please provide En–Cs training time for bpe2char and an En–Fr bpe2char baseline to complete comparisons.  
- Can you quantify morphology learning (e.g., segmentation metrics or probing tasks) to support Section 5.2?  
- Please clarify the “energy” measure and define “peak” in figures, and fix Figure 4a color rendering.
