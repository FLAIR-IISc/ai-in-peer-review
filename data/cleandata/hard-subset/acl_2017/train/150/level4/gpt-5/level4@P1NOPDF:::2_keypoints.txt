- Strength: Paper is well structured, clear, with original ideas and interesting results.  
- Weakness: Doubts about result interpretation and unsupported claims that the method learns morphology.  
- Contribution: Proposes DCNMT, a character-level NMT with deep word-encoding/decoding layers that produce intermediate subword representations.  
- Motivation: Aims to combine character-level robustness and smaller vocabularies with shorter subword sequences for better performance.  
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences vs. bpe2char are minimal (0.04 Cs–En, 0.1 En–Cs) and lack statistical significance testing.  
- Training-time comparison: bpe2char is 8 days faster for Cs–En; En–Cs training time is missing and En–Fr lacks bpe2char comparison—needs balanced evaluation.  
- Morphology claim: Section 5.2 shows only a few illustrative examples without formal experiments or quantitative evidence to support claims about learning morphology.  
- Recommendation on morphology: Move the qualitative analysis to an appendix and soften claims, or expand into a standalone, formal study.  
- Acronyms: Several acronyms are undefined or defined after first use; define all acronyms on first use for clarity.  
- "Energy" concept: Re-explain energy in Section 5.2 and clarify interpretation (e.g., relation to morpheme boundaries) and define “peak.”  
- BPE notation: Inconsistent capitalization of BPE—make it consistent throughout.  
- Unnecessary statement: Stating that no monolingual corpus is used may be redundant in Section 4.1.  
- Figure issue: Figure 4a’s energy color values are missing for some characters and should be fixed.  
- Reproduced results: If Cs–En results for Chung et al. (2016) were reproduced by the authors, state this clearly in Table 1.  
- Morphology remark: Avoid calling French “morphologically poor”; it is less morphologically rich than Slavic languages.  
- Dataset links: Provide a link for WMT’14 as done for WMT’15 or explain the omission.  
- References: Several references are incomplete and need completion.  
- Typos: Multiple grammatical and spelling errors throughout (list provided) should be corrected.
