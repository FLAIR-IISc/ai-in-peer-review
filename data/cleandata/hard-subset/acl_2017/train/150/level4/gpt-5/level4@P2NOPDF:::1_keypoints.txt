- Introduces a novel encoder-decoder NMT that inputs/outputs characters while internally representing morphemes and characters.  
- Authors release code and pretrained models for fr-en, cs-en, and en-cs, enabling replication and extension.  
- System achieves reasonable translation quality after the first epoch and improves in later epochs.  
- Learns plausible morphological segmentations and can handle unseen or nonce words by backing off to morphemic representations.  
- Paper does not clearly state which WMT dev/test sets are used, hindering comparisons; README implies test=newstest2015 and dev=newstest2013 but paper should state this.  
- README instructions are adequate but need clearer command-line examples for training/inference and a --help flag should be provided.  
- Text claims a six-layer architecture but Figure 2 appears to show fewer layers; caption should clarify and a complete diagram should be included (e.g., appendix).  
- Results compare only to other character-based NMT systems and omit state-of-the-art baselines from other MT paradigms (WMT/matrix.statmt.org); this gap should be acknowledged and discussed.  
- Several minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reversed quotation marks) need correction.  
- Overall assessment: a solid contribution to character-based neural MT.
