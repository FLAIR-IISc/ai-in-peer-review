- Strength: paper is well structured, clear, with original ideas and interesting results.
- Weakness: reservations about result interpretation and unsupported claims about learning morphology.
- Contribution: proposes a complex character-level NMT with deep word-encoding and word-decoding layers that convert between characters and subword representations.
- Motivation: aims to combine character-level robustness with shorter sequences via an intermediate subword representation.
- Claim: authors assert the model (DCNMT) learns morphology better than state-of-the-art approaches.
- Evaluation concern: BLEU differences vs. bpe2char are very small (0.04 Cs→En, 0.1 En→Cs) and lack statistical significance testing.
- Training-time concern: bpe2char trains 8 days faster for Cs→En; training time for En→Cs is missing and bpe2char not evaluated for En→Fr, making comparisons incomplete.
- Suggestion: provide full comparisons (including missing training times and bpe2char on all language pairs) and report statistical significance.
- Morphology claim issue: Section 5.2 provides only illustrative examples without formal experiments or quantitative evaluation to support morphology-learning claims.
- Recommendation: expand the morphology analysis into rigorous experiments (or move it to an appendix and soften the claims).
- Acronym clarity: many acronyms (LSTM, HGRU, CNN, PCA, RNN, BPE) are undefined or defined after use; define upon first use.
- Energy explanation: reintroduce and clarify the concept of energy in Section 5.2, explain interpretation (e.g., relation to morpheme boundaries) and define “peak.”
- BPE consistency: inconsistent capitalization of BPE (upper/lowercase) should be fixed.
- Unnecessary statement: mentioning that no monolingual corpus is used in Section 4.1 may be unnecessary.
- Figure issue: Figure 4a’s energy color values are not shown for every character.
- Table 1: Cs→En results for Chung et al. (2016) were not reported in the original paper—authors should state if they computed them.
- Language description: French should be described as less morphologically rich than Slavic languages, not “morphologically poor.”
- Data links: WMT’15 training corpora link provided but WMT’14 link is missing.
- References: several references are incomplete and need fixing.
- Typos: multiple grammatical and typographical errors listed (provide corrected forms as noted).
