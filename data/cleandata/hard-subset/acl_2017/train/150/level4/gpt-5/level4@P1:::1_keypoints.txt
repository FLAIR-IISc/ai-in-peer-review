- Proposes a hierarchical character-level NMT with a dual-RNN encoder (prefix-weighted character states via an “energy” BiRNN) and a word-to-character decoder (HGRU) that emits target words char-by-char.  
- Eliminates word softmax and large-vocabulary bottlenecks, keeps encoder lengths near word-level models, and introduces an efficient unfolding/batching scheme for the character decoder.  
- Experiments on En–Fr, En–Cs, Cs–En show rapid learning (surpasses BPE baseline after one epoch) and final BLEU comparable to recent character-based systems.  
- Analyses (PCA, energy plots) indicate the encoder learns morpheme-like structure and the system handles misspellings and nonce words.  
- Contribution: an efficient hierarchical character-level architecture that trains faster than prior character-only systems while remaining competitive in BLEU.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence of fast convergence and compact model size (~54M parameters) with practical single-GPU training.  
- Contribution: qualitative demonstrations that the encoder captures morphology and yields robustness to OOVs and misspellings.  
- Strength: open-vocabulary design and practical unfolding/batching enable end-to-end character-level training without complex multi-pass tricks.  
- Strength: strong early learning and compactness—exceeds BPE early and trains on a single GTX Titan X with lower time-to-quality.  
- Strength: morphology-aware word representations (energy weighting) produce coherent clusters and plausible morpheme boundaries.  
- Strength: robust handling of misspellings and unseen morphological variants, avoiding <unk> outputs.  
- Strength: released code and trained models for fr–en, cs–en, en–cs improve reproducibility.  
- Weakness: paper omits explicit dev/test set specifications (only in README), hindering direct comparison to WMT leaderboards.  
- Weakness: README/documentation lacks concrete command-line examples and a --help interface, reducing replication ease.  
- Weakness: Figure/text mismatch about number of RNNs (text says six RNNs but figure shows fewer); needs a complete architecture diagram.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit broader WMT SOTA baselines; paper should discuss absolute BLEU gap vs. wider SOTA and trade-offs.  
- Weakness: minor language/formatting issues and typos that warrant a careful editing pass.  
- Question: please state dev/test sets and tokenization/preprocessing choices in the paper.  
- Question: please provide a complete architectural diagram showing all six RNNs, HGRU set/reset behavior, and unfolding matrix/batching connections.  
- Question: how does final BLEU compare to reported WMT SOTA on the same test sets, and can you discuss the efficiency/robustness trade-off?  
- Question: please add concrete training/decoding commands in the README and a --help option for scripts/binaries.  
- Question: is HGRU novel relative to prior hierarchical decoders or a GRU variant—please provide a comparison/ablation.  
- Overall: a solid, practical contribution to character-level NMT with compelling efficiency and robustness benefits, but would be strengthened by clearer dataset specification, a full architecture figure, better documentation, and discussion against broader WMT SOTA.
