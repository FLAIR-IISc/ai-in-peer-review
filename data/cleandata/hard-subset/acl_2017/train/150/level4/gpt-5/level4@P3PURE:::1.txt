Strengths:
- The paper presents a novel adaptation of encoder–decoder neural MT that begins and ends with characters while using intermediate representations over morphemes and characters.
- The authors release both their code and their trained models for fr–en, cs–en, and en–cs. This facilitates validation of the results and supports replication and extension by others.
- The system produces reasonable translation quality after just the first training epoch, with continued improvements in subsequent epochs.
- The approach appears to learn sensible morphological tokenizations and can handle previously unseen words (including nonce words) by implicitly backing off to morphemes.

Weaknesses:
- The paper does not explicitly state which WMT test and dev sets are used for evaluation. This hinders comparison to prior work (e.g., results at matrix.statmt.org). This reviewer could only find the information in the code supplement’s README, which indicates newstest2015 as the test set and newstest2013 as the dev set. This should be clearly stated in the paper.
- The software README is adequate but could be improved. The training and testing sections should include explicit, runnable examples. The software should also support a --help flag, which it currently does not.
- The paper describes a 6-level architecture, but Figure 2 appears to depict fewer than six layers. The caption should clarify this, and if the figure is not intended to show all layers, a complete diagram (possibly in an appendix) should be provided.
- The results compare against other character-based neural systems but do not include state-of-the-art results from other MT paradigms. WMT (and matrix.statmt.org) reports substantially higher scores on these datasets than those shown here. This should be acknowledged and, ideally, discussed.
- There are several minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reversed quotation marks) that should be corrected.

General Discussion:
This is a solid contribution to the literature on character-based neural MT.
