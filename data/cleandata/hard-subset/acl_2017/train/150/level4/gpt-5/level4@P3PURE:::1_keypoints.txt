- Presents a novel encoder–decoder MT model that begins and ends with characters while using intermediate morpheme/character representations.  
- Authors release code and trained models for fr–en, cs–en, and en–cs, facilitating validation and replication.  
- System achieves reasonable translation quality after the first training epoch and improves further with more epochs.  
- Approach learns sensible morphological tokenizations and can handle unseen or nonce words by implicitly backing off to morphemes.  
- Paper does not explicitly state which WMT test/dev sets are used (README shows newstest2015 as test and newstest2013 as dev), hindering comparison to prior work.  
- Software README should include explicit runnable training/testing examples and add a --help flag.  
- Paper describes a 6-level architecture but Figure 2 appears to show fewer layers; caption should clarify or include a complete diagram.  
- Results compare to other character-based systems but omit state-of-the-art results from other MT paradigms (WMT/matrix.statmt.org report substantially higher scores) and should acknowledge/discuss this.  
- Contains minor English disfluencies, misspellings, and LaTeX issues (e.g., reversed quotation marks) that need correction.  
- Overall, a solid contribution to the literature on character-based neural MT.
