- Primary contribution: Proposes a deep character-level NMT with a two-RNN word encoder and hierarchical character decoder (HGRU) to remove large-vocabulary bottlenecks while keeping efficient training and stable attention.
- Secondary contribution: Demonstrates competitive BLEU after one epoch and robustness to OOVs, misspellings, and nonce words via learned morphemes.
- Tertiary contribution: Releases code and trained models for En–Fr, En–Cs, and Cs–En to support replication.

- Strength: Elegant character-in/character-out design that leverages word-level structure via a two-RNN encoder and hierarchical decoder, supported by BLEU and morpheme analyses.
- Strength: Fast learning and training efficiency—reasonable BLEU after one epoch and much shorter training time than prior character-only models.
- Strength: Shows morphological generalization and robustness to unseen/misspelled forms by backing off to morphemes.
- Strength: Reproducibility enhanced by releasing code and trained models.

- Weakness: Dataset splits and preprocessing choices (dev/test, filtering) are not clearly specified in the paper, only in the README, hindering fair comparison.
- Weakness: Software usability needs improvement—README lacks end-to-end command examples, flags, and binaries/scripts lack a --help option.
- Weakness: Architectural clarity is lacking—paper claims “six recurrent networks” but Figure 2 and caption do not clearly enumerate all components.
- Weakness: Limited baseline coverage and positioning—insufficient comparison to strong BPE-based/word-level or WMT SOTA systems and missing discussion of training budget/architectural/search differences.
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues—English disfluencies, typos, and LaTeX problems reduce readability.

- Question: Confirm exact dev/test sets and any filtering or length constraints used per language pair, and whether max word/char lengths were applied.
- Question: Provide precise training and decoding commands (all flags), optimizer settings, beam size, length normalization/penalty, batch size, and early stopping criteria for reproducibility.
- Question: Enumerate the six RNNs and map them to Figure 2, or provide a complete architecture diagram showing each component.
- Question: Report training and inference speed (tokens/sec or chars/sec) and memory footprints for training and decoding compared to strong baselines.
- Question: Add comparisons to stronger BPE-based or word-level attentional models trained under similar budgets and discuss the gap to best WMT systems.

- Additional editorial comment: Fix punctuation, spelling, LaTeX quote marks, ensure consistent notation, and standardize references.
- Additional software suggestion: Add a --help option and example scripts for training/validation/decoding, and specify required preprocessing/tokenization and character set handling.
