## Review of "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts"

This paper investigates the application of complex network analysis, enriched with word embeddings, to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. The core idea—modeling linguistic data as networks to capture relationships beyond traditional feature engineering—is promising, particularly given the challenges of parsing disfluent speech common in MCI patients. While the reported accuracy isn't groundbreaking, the approach demonstrates a viable alternative to relying heavily on traditional NLP pipelines susceptible to errors from disfluencies. The exploration of this method on Portuguese datasets is a notable contribution, addressing a relative scarcity of resources for this language in cognitive assessment research.

**Contributions:**

1. **Novel Application of Complex Networks to MCI Detection:** The primary contribution is the exploration of complex network features, augmented with word embeddings, as a method for identifying MCI in speech transcripts. This is a departure from conventional approaches that primarily rely on parsed linguistic features.
2. **Portuguese Language Focus:** The study’s evaluation on three Portuguese datasets (Cinderella and Arizona-Battery) is significant. This provides valuable insights into the applicability of the method to a language with fewer available NLP resources and demonstrates a commitment to broadening the scope of MCI detection research beyond English.
3. **Demonstrated Performance & Ensemble Benefit:** The authors demonstrate that complex network features can achieve competitive, and in some cases superior, performance compared to traditional linguistic features. The finding that ensembles and multi-view learning improve results, particularly on the Cinderella and Arizona datasets, suggests a fruitful direction for further development.

**Strengths:**

1. **Addressing a Core Challenge:** The paper directly addresses a key challenge in automated MCI detection: the presence of disfluencies in patient speech that hinder the performance of parsing-based methods. By utilizing network-based representations, the approach shows potential for robustness against such irregularities.
2. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The use of three datasets – DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery – provides a reasonably comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method across different narrative tasks and data characteristics. The inclusion of both English and Portuguese datasets adds breadth to the study.
3. **Clear Presentation of Methodology:** The description of the complex network modeling process, including the incorporation of word embeddings, is generally clear and well-structured. The authors provide sufficient detail to allow for replication of the experiments.
4. **Positive Results with Ensemble Methods:** The improvement in accuracy achieved through ensemble and multi-view learning demonstrates the potential for synergistic benefits from combining different feature representations and classification algorithms. This is a valuable insight for future research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Dataset Size:** The sample sizes for the Portuguese datasets (20 MCI and 20 control participants per dataset) are relatively small. This raises concerns about the generalizability of the findings and the statistical power of the experiments.
2. **Lack of Detailed Embedding Source Description:** The origin of the word embeddings used in the network enrichment process isn't sufficiently detailed. While the paper mentions using sub-word information (n-grams), it’s unclear whether the embeddings were pre-trained on large external corpora or learned specifically from the available datasets. Clarification is needed on this point, as it significantly impacts the interpretability of the results.
3. **Insufficient Justification for Feature Choices:** The selection of specific topological network measures (e.g., PageRank, Betweenness) isn't adequately justified. A more systematic exploration of different network metrics, and a rationale for focusing on the chosen set, would strengthen the paper.
4. **Overly Broad Introduction & Related Work:** The introduction and related work sections contain a considerable amount of background information that is not directly relevant to the core contributions of the paper. This dilutes the focus and could be streamlined to emphasize the novelty of the proposed approach. Specifically, references to areas like sentiment classification and pedestrian detection feel tangential.
5. **Presentation of Results:** While statistically significant improvements are mentioned, the differences in accuracy between methods (especially the variations within ensemble learning) are often small. A more nuanced discussion of the practical implications of these differences, and a consideration of effect sizes, would be beneficial.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Embedding Source:** What specific pre-trained word embeddings (if any) were used, and if embeddings were trained specifically on the datasets, please provide details on the training process (corpus size, parameters, etc.).
2. **Network Metric Selection:** What criteria were used to select the specific topological network measures included in the feature set? Was any feature selection performed, and if so, what method was used?
3. **Disfluency Handling:** Beyond simply not removing disfluencies, did the authors consider any methods for explicitly modeling or incorporating information about disfluencies into the network representation?
4. **Statistical Power Analysis:** Was a power analysis conducted to determine the necessary sample size for detecting statistically significant differences between the groups, given the observed effect sizes?

Overall, this paper presents a promising approach to MCI detection using complex networks and word embeddings. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly regarding dataset size, embedding source clarification, and justification of feature selection, would significantly strengthen the work and enhance its impact.
