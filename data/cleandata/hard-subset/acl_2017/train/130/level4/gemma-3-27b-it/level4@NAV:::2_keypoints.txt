- Paper proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence and lexical/syntactic/graph features to detect MCI from speech transcripts across three datasets.
- Evaluation uses 5-fold cross-validation with multiple classifiers and shows variable performance across datasets.
- Manuscript readability is poor due to grammatical and spelling errors; needs thorough editing.
- ML framing is unclear: task appears transcript-level classification but dataset descriptions and tables focus on study-level details and contain inconsistencies (e.g., participant counts).
- Rationale for the graph enrichment strategy is insufficient; simpler alternatives (e.g., vector-similarity graphs) are not considered or justified.
- No use of domain-specific biomedical NLP tools or resources despite the clinical datasets.
- Reporting accuracy without clear class-distribution and not reporting F1-score is inappropriate for a binary task.
- Results are reported with excessive precision on small datasets and lack statistical significance testing, making practical gains unclear.
