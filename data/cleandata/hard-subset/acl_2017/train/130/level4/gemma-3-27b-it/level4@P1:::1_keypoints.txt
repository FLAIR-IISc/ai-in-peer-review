- Paper proposes using NLP on speech transcripts to identify MCI (ICD-10 F06.7) and shows ability to distinguish healthy controls from MCI patients.  
- Reported accuracy ranges from ~60% to 85%, indicating the task remains difficult despite promising results.  
- Datasets: transcriptions from 20 healthy controls and 20 MCI patients, primarily in Portuguese, contributing to resource-scarce language research.  
- Reviewer requests at least one short transcript translated to English and a sample network visualization with embeddings to aid understanding.  

Strengths:
- Key contribution: application of complex network analysis enriched with word embeddings for MCI detection from transcripts.  
- Representation is lightweight and largely language-independent, avoiding parsing disfluent speech.  
- Evaluation across three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery) shows robustness to different data characteristics.  
- Complex network features outperform traditional linguistic features on these datasets.  
- Ensemble and multi-view classifier combination yields up to +10% (Cinderella) and +4.71% (Arizona) gains over best single classifier.  

Weaknesses:
- Introductory sections (1–3) and related work are overly long and include some tangential references that should be omitted.  
- Sections 4.1 and 4.2.3 need condensation for clarity.  
- Short narratives (especially ABCD) raise concerns about the effectiveness of embedding generation from limited text.  
- Methodology for creating word-level networks from continuous representations (lines 272–289) is unclear.  
- Unclear whether word embeddings were trained on the ABCD/Cinderella datasets alone or on external corpora.  
- Use of sub-word (n-gram) networks and the combination with full-word networks needs justification and empirical support.  
- “Out-of-vocabulary” words are mentioned but not defined or explained in handling.  

Questions for authors:
- What external corpora, if any, were used to train the word embeddings?  
- Why combine full-word and sub-word level networks for embeddings, and is there empirical evidence this helps?  
- What are the characteristics of the out-of-vocabulary words encountered and how were they handled?  

General recommendations:
- Paper addresses an important problem and the Portuguese focus is valuable; methodology needs more detail and refinement.  
- Revise for clarity and conciseness (streamline sections 1–3, compress 4.1) and add more experimental details.  
- Provide a brief explanation of the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms.
