This paper presents a novel application of existing natural language processing techniques to the problem of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are well-defined, and while some details are lacking, the proposed solution and experimental setup appear reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and potentially informative.

However, the paper requires significant editing to improve readability and clarity. Several key concepts are not adequately explained, hindering full comprehension. For example, the specifics of the multi-view learning approach are omitted, the set of “linguistic features” needs further clarification, and it remains unclear what datasets were used to generate the word embeddings – the provided datasets appear potentially insufficient for this purpose. The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) is also not provided; these could be important indicators of MCI. Similarly, the decision not to utilize the widely-adopted tf-idf weighting scheme for the Bag-of-Words (BoW) classification is unexplained. Crucially, the paper lacks statistical significance tests to support the reported experimental conclusions. Finally, the related work section feels somewhat superficial.

*   **Lines 29-30:** Rephrase: “as it a lightweight and language independent representation” to “as it is a lightweight and language-independent representation.”
*   **Lines 34-38:** Clarify which datasets are being referenced. Consider phrasing like: “using the DementiaBank dataset and two additional narrative datasets: Cinderella and…”

*   **Line 73:** Requires rephrasing for clarity.
*   **Lines 115-117:** Explain why disfluencies would pose a problem for BoW representations.
*   **Line 147-149:** Elaborate on what constitutes “the best scenario.”
*   **Line 157:** Provide a citation or link to the DementiaBank resource.
*   **Line 162:** Include a citation or link for the "Picnic picture of the Western Aphasia Battery."
*   **Line 170:** Explain the WML subtest.
