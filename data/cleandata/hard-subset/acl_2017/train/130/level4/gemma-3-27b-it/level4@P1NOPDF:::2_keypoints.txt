1. Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts using a graph-based co-occurrence feature approach across three datasets; problem and datasets are appropriate.

2. Paper is hard to read due to poor English and needs thorough grammar/spelling revision.

3. Machine-learning setup is poorly described—unclear if each transcript is a single labeled instance; dataset tables describe studies not transcript-level data; age is irrelevant and dataset descriptions are overly detailed and inconsistent (e.g., 326 vs <100).

4. Motivation for graph enrichment is unclear—no justification for using co-occurrence rather than simpler word-node + vector-similarity graphs.

5. No use of biomedical or domain-specific tools/knowledge despite biomedical datasets.

6. Evaluation metrics are questionable—class distribution unclear so accuracy may be inappropriate; F1-score would be more informative for binary classification.

7. Results lack statistical rigor—metrics reported to four decimals on very small datasets (e.g., 43 transcripts) with no significance testing, making reported gains unreliable.
