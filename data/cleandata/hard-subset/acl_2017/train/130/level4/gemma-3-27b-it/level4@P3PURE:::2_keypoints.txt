- Paper proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence features from speech transcripts to identify MCI, evaluated on three datasets with 5-fold CV and multiple classifiers.  
- Addresses a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets.  
- Paper readability is poor due to numerous English grammar and spelling errors.  
- The machine learning formulation and instance definition are unclear (are transcripts single instances?), and dataset descriptions/tables are misleading or inconsistent.  
- Excessive, irrelevant dataset detail (e.g., patient age) and contradictory data counts undermine clarity.  
- Motivation for the graph enrichment is not explained—alternative designs (e.g., nodes as words connected by embedding similarity) are not discussed.  
- Authors do not leverage biomedical/domain-specific tools or knowledge despite domain data.  
- Evaluation choices are questionable: class distribution is unclear, accuracy may be inappropriate for this binary task, and F1 would be more informative.  
- Results are reported to unrealistic precision on very small datasets without statistical significance testing, so reported gains may not be meaningful.
