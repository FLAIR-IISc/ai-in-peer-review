This paper presents a novel application of existing natural language processing techniques to the problem of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are well-defined and thoroughly described. While the proposed solution and experiments appear sound, the paper would significantly benefit from substantial editing to improve clarity and readability. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

The primary weakness of the paper lies in its lack of detail in several key areas. The description of the multi-view learning approach is insufficient; the specific “linguistic features” used require more precise definition; and the datasets used for generating word embeddings are not clearly specified – the paper’s three datasets appear potentially too small for this purpose. The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unclear, as these may be relevant indicators of MCI. Similarly, the choice not to use the widely adopted tf-idf weighting scheme for BoW classification is not justified. Crucially, the paper lacks statistical significance testing to support the reported experimental conclusions. Finally, the related work section feels somewhat superficial and could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of existing approaches.
