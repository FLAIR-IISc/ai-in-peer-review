1. Paper addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts using a graph-based co-occurrence method with lexical and syntactic graph features.  
2. Features encoded include complex network measures and combinations with linguistic features; models evaluated with 5-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers.  
3. Experiments run on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, ABCD) in English and Portuguese, showing variable performance across datasets.  
4. Strength: Novel application of complex network analysis to patient speech, offering a potentially robust, language-independent approach.  
5. Strength: Multi-dataset evaluation (including cross-language) improves generalizability and impact.  
6. Strength: Combining network features with linguistic features and using ensemble/multi-view methods is thoroughly explored and shows improvements on some datasets.  
7. Weakness: Paper clarity and writing quality need improvement; dataset presentation is overly detailed and sometimes inconsistent (demographics distract from key counts).  
8. Weakness: Evaluation lacks rigor—reporting accuracy to four decimals on small samples without significance testing is misleading.  
9. Weakness: Accuracy may be inappropriate for imbalanced data; metrics like F1, precision, and recall should be reported.  
10. Weakness: Insufficient justification for enriching co-occurrence networks with word embeddings and unclear advantages over vector-similarity networks.  
11. Weakness: Limited use of domain-specific (linguistic/neuropsychological) knowledge or tools despite biomedical datasets.  
12. Weakness: Unclear definition of an instance for classification—need explicit statement that a transcript is the data point and description at transcript level.  
13. Question: Why was accuracy chosen as the primary metric and how does class imbalance affect results; would F1 or other metrics be better?  
14. Question: What is the motivation and expected benefit of enriching co-occurrence networks with embeddings versus building networks from vector similarity?  
15. Question: How were classifier hyperparameters chosen and was any hyperparameter optimization performed (method/details)?
