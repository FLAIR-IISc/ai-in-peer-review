The paper describes a novel application of largely existing representations, feature sets, and methods: namely, detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The nature of the problem, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. While lacking some detail, the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

In terms of drawbacks, the paper requires considerable editing to improve readability. Details on some key concepts are missing. For example, details on the multi-view learning approach are omitted; the set of "linguistic features" needs clarification; it is unclear what datasets were used to generate the word embeddings (presumably the three datasets described, which appear relatively small for that purpose). It is also unclear why disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) were removed from the dataset. These might be important features in the context of MCI. It is also unclear why the more common tf-idf weighting scheme was not used for the BoW classifications. Furthermore, tests for statistical significance are not provided to substantiate the conclusions drawn from the experiments. Finally, the related work is described somewhat superficially.
