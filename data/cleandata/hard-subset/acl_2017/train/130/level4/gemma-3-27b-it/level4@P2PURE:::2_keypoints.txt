- Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts using three distinct datasets.  
- Proposes a graph-based method leveraging word co-occurrence and encoding lexical, syntactic, and other graph features.  
- Evaluates performance with 5-fold cross-validation across several classifiers and reports dataset-varying results.  
- Manuscript has many English grammar and spelling errors, reducing readability.  
- Core ML problem is unclear: unclear what constitutes an instance, transcript-level framing and dataset descriptions are inconsistent and include irrelevant details (e.g., patient age).  
- Reported dataset numbers are inconsistent (e.g., 326 participants vs. <100 in Table 1), raising data reliability concerns.  
- Motivation for the specific graph enrichment is unclear; simpler alternatives (e.g., word nodes with vector-similarity edges) are not considered.  
- Despite using biomedical datasets, no domain-specific tools or resources are leveraged.  
- Class distribution is unclear, making accuracy an inappropriate primary metric; F1-score would be more informative for this binary task.  
- Results are reported with excessive precision on very small datasets and lack statistical significance testing, so practical significance is uncertain.
