- Novelty: paper applies existing representations and methods to MCI detection in speech; problem and domain are well described and experiments seem reasonable.
- Readability: manuscript needs substantial editing for clarity and phrasing throughout.
- Missing methodological detail: multi-view learning approach is not fully explained.
- Feature description: the set of “linguistic features” is underspecified and needs expansion.
- Word embeddings: unclear which datasets were used to train embeddings and whether they are large enough.
- Disfluency handling: rationale for removing filled pauses/false starts/repetitions is not justified and may remove diagnostic signals.
- BoW preprocessing: paper does not explain why tf-idf weighting was not used for Bag-of-Words classifiers.
- Statistical analysis: no statistical significance tests are reported to support experimental differences.
- Related work: section is superficial and lacks detail on prior methods and direct comparisons.
- Abstract: should be shortened and made clearer.
- Manual corrections: unclear whether grammatical errors in transcripts were manually corrected and why.
- Examples: paper should include examples of disfluencies to illustrate preprocessing choices.
- Dataset references: ambiguous mentions of datasets (e.g., “the other two: Cinderella and…”) need explicit naming and clarification.
- DementiaBank and stimuli citations: missing citations/links for DementiaBank and the Picnic picture/Western Aphasia Battery materials.
- WML: the WML subtest is not explained and lacks citation.
- Preprocessing order: lemmatization and other text normalization steps are not clearly described or justified.
- Segmentation and datasets: unclear what was manually segmented vs. automatically segmented, and “ABCD” is undefined.
- Table 1: demographic table lacks additional dataset statistics promised in the text.
- Transcription norms: no citation or link describing the transcription conventions used.
- Feature clarifications: ambiguous references like “shortest path” and “threshold parameter” need explicit definitions and which features they refer to.
- Section 4.2.2: linguistic features section needs significant expansion for clarity and reproducibility.
- Classification details: details of the multi-view learning and classification algorithms are omitted, including how views are combined.
- Minor wording/phrasing fixes: numerous line-level rephrasings and grammar fixes requested (e.g., “15% a year,” punctuation, and sentence rewrites).
