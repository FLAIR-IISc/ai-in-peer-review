The paper presents a novel application of largely existing representations, feature sets, and methods – detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. While some details are missing, the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires considerable editing to improve readability. Several key concepts lack sufficient detail. For example, the specifics of the multi-view learning approach are omitted; the set of “linguistic features” needs clarification; and it’s unclear which datasets were used to generate the word embeddings (presumably the three datasets described, which seem relatively small for this purpose). The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) is also unclear. These could potentially be important features in the context of MCI. Furthermore, the paper doesn’t explain why the more common tf-idf weighting scheme wasn't used for the BoW classifications. Crucially, the conclusions drawn from the experiments aren’t supported by tests for statistical significance. Finally, the related work section feels somewhat superficial.
