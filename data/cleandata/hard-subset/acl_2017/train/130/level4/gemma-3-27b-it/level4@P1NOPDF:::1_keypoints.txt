- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify patients with Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI).
- Authors report ability to distinguish healthy vs MCI participants, but final accuracy is mixed (60–85%), leading to a more cautious conclusion.
- Dataset is small (40 participants: 20 healthy, 20 MCI) and limited to Portuguese.
- Recommend adding technical detail, an English-translated short transcript example, and a partial network visualization with embeddings.
- Intro and related-work are comprehensive but include some broad/irrelevant citations (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) that could be removed.
- Suggest compressing the first five pages and tightening section 4.2.3 to focus on the paper’s unique contributions.
- Unclear how continuous word representations were obtained—whether trained on the small ABCD+Cinderella corpora or external data.
- Need clarification on sub-word (n-gram) networks: training data, how they produce word embeddings, and evidence that combining word- and sub-word networks improves accuracy.
- Clarify what constitutes “out-of-vocabulary” words (source and handling).
- Recommend expanding experimental setup details and explaining transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).
- Notes that focusing on Portuguese is valuable due to resource scarcity compared to English.
- Several minor technical/typo errors to fix (Line 29, Line 188, Line 264, Table 3 decimal, duplicate DOI, incorrect bibliographic link).
