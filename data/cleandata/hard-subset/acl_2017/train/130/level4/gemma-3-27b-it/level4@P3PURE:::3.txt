The paper describes a novel application of largely existing representations, feature sets, and methods: detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The nature of the problem, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. While some details are missing, the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires considerable editing to improve readability. Details on several key concepts are omitted. For example, the specifics of the multi-view learning approach are not described; the set of “linguistic features” requires clarification; and it’s unclear what datasets were used to generate the word embeddings (presumably the three datasets described in the paper, which may be too small for this purpose). The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) from the dataset is also unclear; these might be important features in the context of MCI. Furthermore, the paper does not justify why the popular tf-idf weighting scheme was not used for the BoW classifications. Significance tests are absent, leaving the conclusions from the experiments unsubstantiated. Finally, the related work is described somewhat superficially. Detailed comments are provided below:
