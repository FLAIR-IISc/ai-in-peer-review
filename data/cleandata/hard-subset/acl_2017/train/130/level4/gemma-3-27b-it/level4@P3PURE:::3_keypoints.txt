- Paper proposes an interesting application (MCI detection from speech) using existing representations, but requires substantial editing for readability.
- Key methodological details are omitted: the specific multi-view learning scheme is not described.
- The set and definition of “linguistic features” are unclear and the section needs significant expansion.
- It’s unclear which datasets were used to train the word embeddings (possibly too small if only the paper’s datasets were used).
- The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is not justified and may remove diagnostic signals for MCI.
- No justification is given for not using tf-idf for BoW classifications; term-weighting choice needs explanation.
- Statistical significance testing is absent, leaving experimental conclusions unsubstantiated.
- Related work coverage is superficial and lacks detailed comparisons to prior methods.
- Many dataset description issues: unclear dataset names, segmentation process, undefined acronyms (e.g., ABCD), and missing dataset statistics (Table 1).
- Numerous missing citations/links requested (DementiaBank, Picnic picture, WML subtest, transcription norms) and missing references for claims.
- Preprocessing steps are incompletely described (e.g., lemmatization placement and other normalization steps).
- Several unclear implementation details: which dataset produced embeddings, what the “threshold parameter” refers to, and what “best scenario” means.
- Numerous editorial and wording problems throughout the text (abstract too long, many suggested rephrases and grammar fixes).
- Recommend consulting conference guidelines for supplementary material and addressing page/format issues.
