- Contribution: Applies complex network analysis enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, aiming for robustness to disfluencies.
- Contribution: Provides comparative evaluation of complex-network features vs. traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words across three datasets.
- Contribution: Integrates ensemble and multi-view learning to combine feature sets, reporting notable improvements (especially on Cinderella and ABCD).
- Strength: Novel, potentially impactful representation less reliant on parsing and syntax-sensitive NLP pipelines.
- Strength: Thorough experimental setup using three datasets, multiple classifiers (SVM, KNN, RF, NB) and 5-fold cross-validation.
- Strength: Clear, detailed presentation of datasets (demographics and elicitation methods) aiding reproducibility.
- Strength: Demonstrated performance gains (e.g., +10% on Cinderella, +4.71% on ABCD) when using complex networks with multi-view learning.
- Strength: Clinically relevant application with potential for automated early MCI detection.
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported, undermining claims about performance differences.
- Weakness: Insufficient description of the multi-view learning approach, weighting scheme, and majority-vote implementation.
- Weakness: Unclear source/training procedure for word embeddings; training on small datasets may limit generalization.
- Weakness: Removing disfluencies is not well justified; disfluencies themselves could be informative for MCI detection.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of feature selection or dimensionality reduction to identify the most informative features.
- Weakness: Related work section is superficial and lacks critical comparison with prior methods.
- Question: Please provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests or ANOVA) for reported performance differences.
- Question: Detail the multi-view learning method, including how feature spaces were combined and weighted.
- Question: Specify where the word embeddings were trained and discuss limitations if trained on the study datasets.
- Question: Explain the rationale for removing disfluencies and whether they were considered as model features.
- Question: Report whether feature selection or dimensionality reduction was explored and the outcomes.

- Comment: Expand Section 4.2.2 to detail specific linguistic features and their relevance to MCI.
