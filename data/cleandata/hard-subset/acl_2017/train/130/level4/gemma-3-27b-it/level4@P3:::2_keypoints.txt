- Proposes identifying MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks of word co-occurrence enriched with word embeddings, evaluated on three datasets with competitive results.
- Demonstrates feasibility of representing transcripts as complex networks for language-independent MCI detection that reduces reliance on parsing.
- Integrates word embeddings into the network to capture semantic information beyond co-occurrence.
- Evaluates method across three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, ABCD Battery) including English and Portuguese for stronger generalizability.
- Novel approach in the relatively unexplored area of network-based MCI detection from transcripts.
- Method is more robust to disfluencies and noisy speech than parsing-heavy NLP techniques.
- Empirical results show complex network features match or outperform traditional linguistic features; ensemble/multi-view learning boost performance.
- Clearly motivates the clinical importance of early MCI detection and potential for automated screening.
- Paper writing needs substantial proofreading due to grammatical errors and awkward phrasing that hinder understanding.
- Dataset descriptions contain irrelevant details, inconsistencies (e.g., participant counts vs. gender breakdown), and should be streamlined to task-relevant stats.
- Rationale for adding embeddings is not fully convincing; alternatives (e.g., direct word-similarity networks) should be discussed.
- Evaluation relies on accuracy alone; missing precision/recall/F1/AUC and, crucially, statistical significance tests given small datasets.
- Lacks integration of domain-specific knowledge or tools relevant to linguistic impairments in the biomedical context.
- Request justification for choosing word co-occurrence topology and its suitability for disfluent speech.
- Ask for class distribution details for each dataset to contextualize reported accuracies.
- Ask for statistical significance testing (e.g., t-tests, ANOVA) to validate performance differences.
- Ask for sensitivity analysis of network-construction parameters (window size, embedding similarity thresholds).
- Clarify what constitutes an "instance" (e.g., is each transcript a single instance?).
- Reference list has minor formatting inconsistencies that should be corrected.
- Supplementary material is helpful but should be better organized and more integrated into the main text.
