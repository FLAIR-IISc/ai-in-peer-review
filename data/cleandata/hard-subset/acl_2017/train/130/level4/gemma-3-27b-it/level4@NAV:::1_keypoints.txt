- Study applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to detect MCI, reporting classification ability but with accuracies (60–85%) that limit reliability.
- Dataset is small (20 healthy, 20 MCI) and Portuguese-only, reducing accessibility and raising concerns about statistical power and generalizability.
- Paper needs more technical detail and examples: provide an English-translated short transcript and a partial network with corresponding embeddings.
- Introduction and literature review are comprehensive but contain general/background citations that could be removed or condensed; initial sections and section 4.2.3 should be shortened and refocused on technical contributions.
- Include English translations of the ABCD and Cinderella narratives to aid reader understanding.
- Short narratives (especially ABCD) cast doubt on the ability to learn meaningful word similarities and embeddings from the data alone.
- Clarify source of continuous word representations: trained only on ABCD+Cinderella or augmented with external corpora.
- Clarify training source for sub-word (n-gram) networks, specify origin of "out-of-vocabulary" words, and demonstrate whether combining word- and sub-word networks improves accuracy.
- Contribution noted: worthwhile exploration of Portuguese-language NLP for cognitive impairment and discussion needed on embedding construction in low-resource languages.
- Manuscript revisions required: shorten sections 1–3, condense section 4.1, expand experimental details, and clarify NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms.
- Minor technical corrections needed: typos (line 29, 188, 264), numeric format in Table 3, duplicated DOI, and an incorrect reference link.
