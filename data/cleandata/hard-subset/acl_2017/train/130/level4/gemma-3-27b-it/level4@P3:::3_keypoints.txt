- Demonstrates that complex network representations of speech transcripts with ML can distinguish MCI from controls.  
- Enriches complex networks with word embeddings to capture semantic relations beyond co-occurrence.  
- Uses multi-view and ensemble learning to combine network, linguistic, and BoW features, improving accuracy.  
- Novel use of complex network analysis for MCI detection, offering a fresh alternative to hand-crafted linguistic features.  
- Approach is more robust to disfluencies than parsing-based methods, beneficial for clinical speech data.  
- Evaluation across three datasets (one English, two Portuguese) and multiple classifiers supports generalizability.  
- Combining diverse feature types yields measurable performance gains over single feature sets.  
- Paper clearly frames the MCI diagnosis problem and the role of automated speech analysis.  
- Results lack statistical significance testing to confirm reported improvements.  
- Description of the multi-view learning approach is too brief and needs more detail.  
- Limited justification for the choice and retention of specific linguistic features given disfluency issues.  
- Unclear whether word embeddings were pre-trained on internal or external corpora.  
- Decision to remove disfluencies is not fully justified; their potential diagnostic value is not discussed.  
- Request for statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests, ANOVA) to support performance claims.  
- Ask for detailed explanation of the multi-view learning method and classifier combination/weighting.  
- Request clarification on the corpus/source used to train the word embeddings.  
- Ask for rationale and consideration of disfluency-based features as MCI indicators.
