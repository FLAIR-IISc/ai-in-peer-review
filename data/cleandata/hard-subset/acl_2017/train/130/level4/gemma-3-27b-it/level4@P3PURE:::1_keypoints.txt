- Study applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) vs healthy controls.  
- Reported ability to distinguish groups conflicts with a cautious conclusion noting 60–85% accuracy, creating an optimistic-to-temperate discrepancy.  
- Very small corpus (20 controls + 20 MCI) limits statistical power and generalizability.  
- Corpus is exclusively Portuguese, limiting accessibility; include at least one short transcript translated into English.  
- Paper needs more technical detail and illustrative materials (e.g., partial network visualization of word embeddings).  
- Some cited references are tangential/general NLP background and could be removed to tighten focus.  
- Section 4.2.3 and the first five pages are overly long; recommend condensing and emphasizing specific contributions.  
- Shortness of ABCD narratives raises doubts about sufficient data for reliable word similarity and embeddings.  
- Unclear how continuous word representations were obtained—clarify if trained only on ABCD+Cinderella or with external corpora.  
- Clarify training data and methods for sub-word (n-gram) networks, define the “out-of-vocabulary” words and their origin.  
- Provide evidence whether combining word-level and sub-word networks improves classification accuracy.  
- Valuable contribution for NLP in low-resource languages; explain embedding strategies for limited-resource Portuguese versus English.  
- Revise structure: shorten sections 1–3, compress 4.1, expand experimental explanations, and clarify transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).  
- Technical corrections needed: grammar/typos (line 29, 188), repeated words (line 264), numeric formatting in Table 3, and duplicated DOI/link errors.
