The paper presents a novel application of established representations, feature sets, and methods to the task of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are well-defined. While some details are lacking, the proposed approach and experimental setup appear sound. Overall, I found this study to be interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires substantial editing to improve readability. Several key concepts lack sufficient explanation. For instance, the details of the multi-view learning strategy are omitted, the composition of the "linguistic features" needs clarification, and the datasets used for generating word embeddings are not explicitly stated (though presumed to be the three datasets described, which may be insufficient in size for this purpose). The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) is unclear, as these might represent important indicators of MCI. Furthermore, the justification for not utilizing the widely-used tf-idf weighting scheme for the BoW classifications is missing. Notably, statistical significance tests are absent, hindering the support of the conclusions drawn from the experiments. Finally, the related work section feels somewhat superficial in its coverage.
