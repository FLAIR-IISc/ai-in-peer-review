- Complex networks applied to MCI detection from speech transcripts to handle disfluencies that hinder traditional NLP.  
- Enriching co-occurrence networks with word embeddings to better capture semantic relations and improve classification.  
- Comparative evaluation across three datasets (one English, two Portuguese) to assess generalizability.  

- Novel approach that departs from conventional NLP and may be more robust to noisy patient speech.  
- Strong empirical results with enriched networks showing competitive performance, notably on the Arizona dataset.  
- Rigorous use of 5-fold cross-validation to reduce overfitting risk.  
- Clear problem definition and motivation for automated MCI detection tools.  

- Manuscript readability suffers from grammar and clarity issues needing thorough revision.  
- Dataset descriptions are overly detailed and inconsistent; tables should focus on transcript-level composition and class counts.  
- Weak motivation for embedding enrichment; lack of comparison to a vector-similarity-only network.  
- No incorporation of domain-specific biomedical or neuropsychological feature engineering.  
- Evaluation relies on accuracy only; precision, recall, and F1 should be reported for possible class imbalance.  
- Results reported to high precision without statistical significance testing, making practical significance unclear.  

- Please clarify class distributions (number of MCI vs control transcripts) for each dataset.  
- What benefits were observed from embedding enrichment compared to a vector-similarity-only network?  
- Were domain-specific linguistic features or tools for cognitive assessment considered?  
- Can you provide statistical analyses to determine significance of the reported improvements?
