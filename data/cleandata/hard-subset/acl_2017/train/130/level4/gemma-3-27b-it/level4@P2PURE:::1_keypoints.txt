- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and reports differentiation between healthy controls and MCI patients.  
- Reported classification accuracy spans 60%–85%, indicating promising but unreliable distinction and motivating further work.  
- Dataset is small (20 healthy, 20 MCI) and limited to Portuguese; paper would benefit from more technical detail, an English-translated example transcript, and a visual (e.g., embedding/network) illustration.  
- Introduction and literature review are overly broad; some general NLP citations (e.g., Salton 1989) and unrelated references (sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) should be removed to tighten focus.  
- Sections 1–3 and 4.1 are too long and should be compressed to emphasize the core contribution and technical innovations.  
- Narratives (especially ABCD) are very short, raising concerns about the reliability of learned word similarities and embeddings from such limited data.  
- It is unclear whether continuous word representations were learned solely from the ABCD/Cinderella datasets or from external corpora; the training source must be specified.  
- The origin/training data for sub-word (n-gram) networks used to generate embeddings is not described and needs clarification.  
- The paper does not demonstrate whether combining word-level and sub-word networks improves accuracy, nor does it explain the source of reported out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words.  
- Emphasize and document how embeddings are computed for lower-resource languages like Portuguese.  
- Experimental setup needs more comprehensive explanation, and transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2) should be clarified.  
- Technical/typo fixes: line 29 "as it a lightweight" → "as in a lightweight"; line 188 PLN → NLP; line 264 remove duplicated "out of cookie"; Table 3 value "72,0" → "72.0"; lines 995–996 DOI duplicated and lines 992–993 link misdirected.
