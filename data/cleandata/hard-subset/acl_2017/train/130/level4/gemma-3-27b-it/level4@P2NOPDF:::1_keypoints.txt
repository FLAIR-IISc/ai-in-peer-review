- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI).
- Reported classification accuracy varies widely (60%–85%), indicating inconsistent discrimination between healthy and MCI subjects.
- Creation of a new corpus (20 healthy, 20 MCI) is a key contribution but dataset is very small.
- Corpus being entirely Portuguese limits accessibility and reproducibility for non-Portuguese readers.
- Authors should provide at least one transcript translated to English and more technical details to aid reproducibility.
- The source of word embeddings is unclear—need to state whether embeddings were learned from ABCD/Cinderella only or from external corpora.
- Training data and methodology for sub-word (n-gram) networks are not specified and require clarification.
- Origins and handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words must be defined.
- Justify and demonstrate whether combining word-level and sub-word networks improves accuracy.
- The brevity of narratives (especially ABCD dataset) may undermine the effectiveness of learning word similarities and embeddings.
- Recommend including a sample network diagram (e.g., word embedding visualization) to illustrate the method.
- Intro and related work are comprehensive but contain tangential or overly broad citations; sections 1–3 should be focused and shortened.
- Condense section 4.1 and expand the experimental description and technical innovations.
- Provide additional context on NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms used.
- Positive note: research direction is promising and Portuguese focus addresses low-resource language challenges.
- List of minor technical corrections (specific line/table fixes and duplicate DOI/link verification) must be applied.
