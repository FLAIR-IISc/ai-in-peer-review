The paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods to the task of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are described thoroughly. While some details are missing, the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires considerable editing to improve readability. Several key concepts lack sufficient detail. For example, the multi-view learning approach is not fully explained, the set of “linguistic features” requires clarification, and the datasets used to generate the word embeddings are unclear (the three datasets described may be too small for this purpose). The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) is also unclear; these might be important features for MCI detection. Furthermore, the paper does not explain why the common tf-idf weighting scheme was not used for the BoW classifications, and it lacks statistical significance tests to support the experimental conclusions. The related work section is also somewhat superficial. Detailed comments are provided below:
