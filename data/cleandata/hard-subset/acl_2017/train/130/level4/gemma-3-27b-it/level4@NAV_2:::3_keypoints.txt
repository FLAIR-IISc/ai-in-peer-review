- Overall: Novel, interesting study with reasonable experiments and thorough domain/dataset description, but lacking some methodological detail.

- Contribution 1: Shows complex network features enriched with word embeddings can discriminate MCI vs. control.

- Contribution 2: Applies ensemble and multi-view learning to improve classification, with notable gains (≈10% Cinderella, 4.71% Arizona).

- Contribution 3: Provides comparative analysis where complex network features outperform traditional linguistic features across datasets.

- Strength 1: Language-independent complex network representation is valuable for noisy, disfluent speech transcripts.

- Strength 2: Effective integration of ensemble and multi-view techniques yielding measurable accuracy improvements.

- Strength 3: Consistent outperforming of linguistic features suggests network-based features capture relevant cognitive signals.

- Strength 4: Evaluation across DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona datasets strengthens generalizability.

- Weakness 1: Multi-view learning description lacks critical details (combination methods, weighting), hurting reproducibility.

- Weakness 2: Feature engineering choices are under-justified (e.g., removal of disfluencies, use of term frequency vs. tf-idf).

- Weakness 3: Insufficient detail on how word embeddings were generated or which corpora/pre-trained models were used.

- Weakness 4: No statistical significance testing reported to support reported accuracy improvements.

- Weakness 5: Related work section is superficial and needs deeper discussion of existing methods and limitations.

- Question 1: Please detail the multi-view learning scheme, including combination methods and weighting.

- Question 2: Why were disfluencies removed, and were they considered as potential informative features?

- Question 3: Which dataset(s) or pre-trained models were used to generate the word embeddings?

- Question 4: Can you provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-test, Wilcoxon) for the reported improvements?

- Question 5: Please elaborate on the challenges and limitations of using linguistic features with disfluent transcriptions.
