- Paper investigates complex network analysis enriched with word embeddings for language-independent MCI detection from speech transcripts, addressing disfluencies.  
- Primary contribution: demonstrates feasibility of using complex network features augmented with embeddings to distinguish MCI across multiple datasets.  
- Secondary contribution: applies the approach to Portuguese, providing insights for under-resourced languages.  
- Explores ensemble and multi-view learning to combine feature sets and improve classification accuracy.  
- Strength: Novel combination of complex networks and embeddings offers a robust, language-independent method that copes with speech disfluencies and achieves competitive performance.  
- Strength: Evaluation on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona Battery) including English and Portuguese enhances generalizability; multi-view reached ~80% on Arizona.  
- Strength: Systematic exploration and effective combination of topological metrics, linguistic features, and BoW representations; multi-view learning yields notable performance gains.  
- Weakness: Small dataset sizes (e.g., Cinderella, ABCD ~20 per class) limit statistical power and generalizability and may introduce demographic biases.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on word embedding training—unspecified corpus, size, and whether embeddings were pre-trained externally; unclear rationale for combining sub-word networks with co-occurrence networks.  
- Weakness: Introduction and related work are overly broad and include tangential references; paper needs a more focused literature review on MCI detection and network-based NLP.  
- Question: What corpus (size, source, characteristics) was used to train the word embeddings?  
- Question: What is the rationale and expected benefit of combining co-occurrence networks with character n-gram–based embedding networks?  
- Question: Given small sample sizes, was any power analysis or statistical significance testing performed for the reported improvements?  
- Question: Please clarify the key principles of the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms, especially regarding disfluency handling.  
- Additional: Provide example translations of short Portuguese transcripts to improve accessibility.  
- Additional: Include a visual example of a sample complex network with highlighted embeddings to aid understanding.  
- Additional: Fix minor technical errors (typos, PLN→NLP, repeated words).  
- Additional: Correct Table 3 (row 2, column 3 should be 72.0) and remove duplicated DOIs at lines 995–996.
