- Paper applies complex network analysis enriched with word embeddings to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts, achieving 60–85% accuracy across three datasets.  
- Main contribution: adapting complex network methods to analyze disfluent speech transcripts for cognitive decline detection.  
- Secondary contribution: evaluation on Portuguese datasets, addressing a low-resource language gap.  
- Combining network features with traditional linguistic features via ensemble/multi-view learning yields some performance improvements.  

Strengths:
- Novel application of complex networks offers a potentially robust, language-independent alternative to parsing-based methods.  
- Inclusion of Portuguese data broadens applicability beyond English and addresses resource-limited NLP settings.  
- Integration of multiple approaches (network, linguistic, BoW) demonstrates complementary information and notable improvement on one dataset (Cinderella).  

Weaknesses:
- Introduction and related work are overly long and include tangential references, obscuring the core contribution.  
- Description of word embedding training is unclear (sources, hyperparameters, whether external corpora were used).  
- Limited theoretical justification linking chosen network metrics to cognitive impairment.  
- Small dataset sizes (e.g., 20 MCI and 20 controls per Portuguese dataset) limit generalizability.  

Questions for authors:
- What corpora and hyperparameters were used to train the word embeddings, and were external datasets incorporated?  
- Why were the specific network metrics chosen, and how do they theoretically relate to MCI-related cognitive processes?  
- Have you considered data augmentation or transfer learning to mitigate small sample sizes and improve generalization?
