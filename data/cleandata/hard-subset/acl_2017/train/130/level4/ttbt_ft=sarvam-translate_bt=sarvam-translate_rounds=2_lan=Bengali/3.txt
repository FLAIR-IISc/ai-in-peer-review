This research paper primarily describes a new application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods, particularly in identifying mild cognitive impairment (MCI). The problem, dataset, and domain are described in detail. Although some information is missing, the proposed solutions and tests seem reasonable. Overall, I found the research to be interesting and informative.

Among the disadvantages, the paper requires some editing to improve readability. Detailed information on some key concepts is missing. For example, the details of multi-view learning are not provided; the set of "linguistic features" needs to be clarified; the dataset used to create word embeddings is not entirely clear (perhaps the 3 datasets described in the paper are too small for that purpose...). Also, it is not clear why the disfluences (full stops, false starts, repetitions, etc.) were removed from the dataset. One might argue that these are important features in the context of MCI. It is also unclear why the most popular tf-idf weight scheme for BoW classification was not used. Furthermore, no significance tests were provided to prove the test results. Finally, the related work is described somewhat superficially. Detailed comments are given below:
