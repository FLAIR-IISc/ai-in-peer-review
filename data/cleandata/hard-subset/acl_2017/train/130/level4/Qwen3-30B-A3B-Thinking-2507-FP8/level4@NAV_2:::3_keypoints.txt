- Contribution: Model speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI without manual transcript correction.  
- Contribution: Show complex network features outperform linguistic features across DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD datasets.  
- Contribution: Demonstrate ensemble and multi-view learning improve accuracy; multi-view yields ~10% gain on Cinderella.  
- Strength: Thorough description of problem domain, datasets, and methodology.  
- Strength: Semantic enrichment addresses need to avoid manual correction of disfluencies.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation on three distinct datasets with clear quantitative improvements.  
- Weakness: Paper clarity and completeness need substantial revision.  
- Weakness: Multi-view learning description is insufficiently detailed.  
- Weakness: Linguistic feature set is under-specified and unclear which exact features were used.  
- Weakness: Unclear which corpora were used to train word embeddings and why not larger external datasets.  
- Weakness: Rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is not justified despite their diagnostic relevance.  
- Weakness: Failure to explain omission of tf-idf weighting for Bag-of-Words features.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance tests to validate reported performance improvements.  
- Weakness: Related work section is superficial and lacks detailed comparisons to prior approaches.  
- Question: Which datasets were used to train the word embeddings and why were larger external corpora not used?  
- Question: What is the rationale for removing disfluencies given their potential diagnostic value?  
- Question: Why was tf-idf not used for Bag-of-Words and how would that affect results?  
- Question: Please provide statistical significance tests for reported accuracy improvements.  
- Question: Elaborate on multi-view learning implementation, how feature spaces were combined, and how weights were assigned.
