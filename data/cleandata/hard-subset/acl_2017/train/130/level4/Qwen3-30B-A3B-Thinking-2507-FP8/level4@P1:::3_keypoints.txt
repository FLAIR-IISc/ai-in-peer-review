- Proposes complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, avoiding manual disfluency correction.  
- Contributions: (1) network+embedding framework; (2) outperforms linguistic features on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD; (3) multi-view learning combining features yields up to 10% accuracy gain.  
- Addresses a meaningful problem by avoiding manual transcript correction; method is lightweight and intended for automated clinical screening.  
- Comprehensive evaluation on three datasets (English and Portuguese) with consistent gains (e.g., 75% vs 65% on Cinderella).  
- Clear preprocessing justification (no lemmatization) and ablation studies show value of network enrichment.  
- Disfluency handling lacks justification; removing disfluencies may discard diagnostic signals known to indicate MCI.  
- Insufficient detail on word embeddings: unclear whether embeddings were trained on the small MCI datasets or external corpora, raising generalization concerns.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for reported accuracy improvements.  
- Inadequate explanation of multi-view learning implementation and feature combination, harming reproducibility.  
- Missing comparison to tf-idf for BoW features; term frequency used without justification.  
- Question: Why were disfluencies removed and can authors show they are not useful for classification?  
- Question: Which datasets/corpora were used to train the word embeddings and how was overfitting mitigated?  
- Question: Why was tf-idf not used for BoW features?  
- Question: How was the cosine-similarity threshold for edge creation in enriched networks determined or optimized?  
- Question: Were statistical tests (e.g., paired t-tests) performed to confirm multi-view learning improvements?  
- Abstract and early sections need tightening and grammar corrections.  
- Related work should better compare prior disfluency-handling studies and clarify differences from them.  
- The claim that complex networks are "language independent" is overstated; word embeddings are language-specific and this should be qualified.  
- Paper should cite DementiaBank and the Western Aphasia Battery's Picnic picture when first mentioned.
