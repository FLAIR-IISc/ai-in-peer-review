- Strength: Addresses a relevant problem—identifying MCI from speech transcripts across three datasets.  
- Strength: Proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence and extracting lexical, syntactic, and graph features.  
- Strength: Presents results with 5-fold cross-validation across multiple classifiers, showing dataset-dependent performance.  
- Strength: Choice of task and datasets is appropriate and relevant.

- Weakness: Manuscript has poor English with frequent grammatical errors; needs professional language editing.  
- Weakness: Problem formulation and dataset description are unclear—transcript-level classification and transcript counts per class must be reported; current tables mislead by describing studies not transcripts, and irrelevant patient ages should be omitted.  
- Weakness: Critical dataset inconsistencies (e.g., Section 4.1.1 reports 326 participants vs. Table 1 summing to under 100) must be resolved.  
- Weakness: No justification for the specific graph construction or why enriched co-occurrence graphs are preferred over alternatives like semantic-similarity graphs.  
- Weakness: Fails to use domain-specific linguistic or clinical resources (e.g., medical ontologies, specialized NLP), which are expected for biomedical datasets.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics are inappropriate or under-specified given unknown class distributions; F1 (or similar) is preferable to accuracy for potential class imbalance.  
- Weakness: Results are overstated (too many decimal places for tiny datasets) and lack statistical significance testing, so reported gains may be due to chance.
