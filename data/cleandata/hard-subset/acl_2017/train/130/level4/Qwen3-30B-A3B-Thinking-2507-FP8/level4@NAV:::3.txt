

The paper presents a novel application leveraging primarily existing representations, feature sets, and methods for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) through speech narratives. The problem context, datasets, and domain are comprehensively described. While some details are lacking, the proposed solution and experimental approach appear reasonable. Overall, the study is interesting and informative.

Regarding drawbacks, the paper requires substantial editing to enhance readability. Key concept explanations are missing: the multi-view learning approach is not detailed; the specific "linguistic features" set needs clarification; it is unclear which datasets were used for word embedding generation (presumably the three described, which may be too small for this purpose); the rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is absent—these could be relevant MCI indicators; the absence of the commonly used tf-idf weighting for BoW classification is unexplained; and statistical significance tests are missing to support experimental conclusions. Additionally, the related work section is superficial. Detailed comments follow:
