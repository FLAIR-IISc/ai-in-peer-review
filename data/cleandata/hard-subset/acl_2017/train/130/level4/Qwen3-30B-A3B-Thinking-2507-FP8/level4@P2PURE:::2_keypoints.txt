- Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets.  
- Proposes a graph-based method leveraging word co-occurrence to extract lexical and syntactic features.  
- Evaluates models using 5-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers, reporting varying performance across datasets.  
- Targets a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets.  
- Poor English and numerous grammar/spelling errors hinder readability.  
- Core ML setup is unclear: unclear whether classification is at transcript level, transcript-level statistics are missing, and excessive/irrelevant dataset detail (e.g., patient age) is given.  
- Inconsistencies in dataset reporting (e.g., reported participant counts do not match table sums).  
- Motivation for the proposed enriched graph structure is weak; no justification against simpler alternatives like word-node graphs with vector-similarity edges.  
- No use of domain-specific biomedical or linguistic tools/resources for these datasets.  
- Class distributions are unspecified, making accuracy an inappropriate metric for binary classification; F1 would be preferable.  
- Results reported to excessive precision on very small datasets without statistical significance testing, so claimed gains are of unclear significance.
