- Models speech transcripts as enriched co-occurrence networks to capture linguistic features without linguistic preprocessing.  
- Network-derived features outperform traditional linguistic features across three datasets.  
- Ensemble and multi-view learning yield significant accuracy improvements.  
- Addresses clinically important MCI detection with a graph-based approach robust to disfluent speech and no manual preprocessing.  
- Validated on English and Portuguese datasets, showing robustness and language independence; CNE features achieved highest accuracy.  
- Ensemble/multi-view methods produced notable gains (e.g., +10% on Cinderella, +4.71% on ABCD), relevant for clinical applications.  
- Manuscript suffers from significant language and grammar errors that hinder readability.  
- Dataset descriptions include irrelevant details and omit critical classification information; Table 1 contains inconsistent participant counts.  
- The rationale for adding semantic edges to co-occurrence networks versus using similarity-based networks directly is not well justified.  
- Evaluation relies solely on accuracy for imbalanced binary tasks without statistical significance testing; F1-score and p-values are needed.  
- The method lacks integration of domain-specific biomedical tools or clinical markers, limiting real-world screening applicability.
