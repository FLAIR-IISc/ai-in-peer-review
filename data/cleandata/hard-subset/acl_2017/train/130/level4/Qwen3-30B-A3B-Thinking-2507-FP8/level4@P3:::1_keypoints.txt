- Proposes MCI detection via word co-occurrence networks enriched with word embeddings applied to speech transcripts, avoiding manual correction of disfluencies.  
- Contributions: (1) framework that bypasses manual transcript correction, (2) network-based features outperform linguistic features across three datasets, (3) multi-view learning yields up to 10% accuracy gains.  

- Practical impact: enables scalable, language-independent automated MCI screening without manual transcript cleanup.  
- Strong empirical validation: CNE and multi-view methods outperform linguistic baselines on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD, matching state-of-the-art accuracy ranges.  
- Innovative method: enriches co-occurrence networks with semantic embeddings to capture short-text semantics and handle very short narratives.  

- Small dataset sizes (Cinderella, ABCD) limit statistical power and generalizability; risk of overfitting not fully addressed.  
- Unclear how word embeddings were trained (internal vs. external corpora) and how out-of-vocabulary words were handled.  
- Paper contains tangential background references and redundant ensemble descriptions that distract from core contribution.  

- How were word embeddings trained and were external Portuguese corpora used; how were OOV words handled?  
- Why does manual transcript revision lower LM accuracy but increase network (CNE) accuracy—are networks more robust to disfluencies?  
- How does the enrichment method avoid overfitting on very short ABCD narratives (~5 sentences)?  

- Title/abstract overstate novelty; clarify that enrichment technique is applied to MCI rather than being a novel network method.  
- Add an English translation of a Portuguese transcript and a visual example of an enriched network to improve accessibility.  
- Fix minor typos and naming errors (e.g., "as it a lightweight", "PLN"→"NLP", "72,0"→"72.0").  

- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to clarify dataset size/overfitting, embedding training, and emphasize MCI-specific contribution.
