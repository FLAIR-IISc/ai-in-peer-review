- Proposed method: language-agnostic MCI detection using complex word co-occurrence networks enriched with word embeddings, avoiding manual transcript correction.  
- Contribution 1: a language-independent approach that handles disfluencies without manual transcript cleaning.  
- Contribution 2: network-based features outperform linguistic features across three datasets, with multi-view learning giving up to ~10% accuracy gain.  
- Contribution 3: demonstrated robustness across text lengths and contexts, applied to English and Portuguese datasets.  
- Strength: novel methodology addressing disfluencies and language dependence with an automated pipeline.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation across three diverse datasets; CNE > LM and BoW, multi-view achieves highest accuracies (80% ABCD, 75% Cinderella).  
- Strength: practical clinical relevance—comparable to SOTA (60–85%) and scalable for large-scale screening.  
- Weakness: poor presentation and many grammatical errors; inconsistent/contradictory dataset reporting (e.g., 326 vs. 64) and extraneous demographic tables.  
- Weakness: unclear problem formulation—uncertainty whether instances are transcripts or patients and lack of transcript-level statistics.  
- Weakness: lack of statistical validation—no p-values or confidence intervals despite small sample sizes and overly precise accuracy reporting.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for using cosine-thresholded semantic enrichment instead of alternative embedding-based connections.  
- Weakness: inappropriate reliance on accuracy alone given possible class imbalance; F1-score (and other metrics) should be reported.  
- Question: clarify whether instances are individual transcripts or patients and provide transcript counts per class in Tables 1–3.  
- Question: how were cosine similarity thresholds for network enrichment chosen—fixed or optimized on validation?  
- Question: how were statistical significance tests performed (or why omitted) given small datasets (e.g., 43 MCI transcripts)?  
- Question: why were linguistic features discarded for Portuguese and how does that affect cross-language result comparability?  
- Additional comment: the core idea has strong potential but requires clearer presentation, problem definition, and rigorous statistical validation.
