

The paper presents a novel application of established representations, feature sets, and methods for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem context, datasets, and domain are well-described, though some technical details are missing. While the proposed approach and experiments appear reasonable, the study is interesting and informative overall.

However, significant revisions are needed to improve clarity and completeness. Key omissions include: (1) insufficient detail on multi-view learning methodology; (2) lack of clarification regarding the "linguistic features" set; (3) unclear source of word embeddings (the three datasets described appear too small for this purpose); (4) no justification for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions), which are likely relevant MCI indicators; (5) absence of significance testing for experimental results; and (6) superficial related work coverage.
