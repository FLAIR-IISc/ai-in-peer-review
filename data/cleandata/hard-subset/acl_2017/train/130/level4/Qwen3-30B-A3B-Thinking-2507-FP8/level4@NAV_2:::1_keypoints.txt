- Summary: Proposes modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, claiming improved performance over traditional linguistic features across three datasets.

- Contribution 1: Novel use of enriched complex networks to model transcripts for MCI detection, avoiding manual transcript correction.

- Contribution 2: Shows network-based features outperform linguistic features, with multi-view learning giving up to ~10% accuracy improvement.

- Contribution 3: Validated on multiple datasets and languages (English and Portuguese), suggesting language-independent applicability.

- Strength 1: Addresses clinical need for scalable, less labor-intensive MCI screening by removing manual transcript correction.

- Strength 2: Demonstrates robust performance across datasets, with multi-view ensemble reaching 80% on the ABCD dataset and improving best individual classifiers.

- Strength 3: Highlights NLP potential for under-resourced languages like Portuguese where traditional linguistic tools are limited.

- Weakness 1: Datasets are small (e.g., 20 MCI/20 controls in Cinderella), limiting statistical power and generalizability.

- Weakness 2: Paper contains excessive background and irrelevant references that distract from the core technical contribution.

- Weakness 3: Missing critical details on word embedding training and OOV handling, harming reproducibility.

- Weakness 4: Manuscript has several technical/typo errors that require correction for academic rigor.

- Question 1: Which corpora were used to train the word embeddings and how do they compare to training on ABCD/Cinderella alone?

- Question 2: How were out-of-vocabulary words handled during embedding and model construction?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide an English-translated sample transcript from ABCD or Cinderella to aid non-Portuguese readers?
