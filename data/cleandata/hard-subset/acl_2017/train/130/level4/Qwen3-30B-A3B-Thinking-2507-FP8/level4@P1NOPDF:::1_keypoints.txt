- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to detect mild cognitive impairment (MCI); reported accuracy varies between ~60%–85%, yielding an overall encouraging but mixed conclusion.  
- Dataset is very small (20 healthy + 20 MCI) and not accessible to non‑Portuguese readers; include more technical details, an English-translated transcript example, and a sample network/embedding visualization.  
- Introduction contains tangential references (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) that should be removed to tighten focus.  
- Sections 4.2.3 and the first five pages are overly long and should be condensed to emphasize technical innovation and English examples from ABCD/Cinderella.  
- It is unclear how word embeddings/continuous word representations were obtained—trained only on ABCD+Cinderella or supplemented with external corpora—this must be specified.  
- The method for computing embeddings from very short ABCD narratives is not explained; questions remain about word similarity estimation with limited text.  
- Description of sub-word (n‑gram) networks and the source of out‑of‑vocabulary words is unclear and needs clarification.  
- The paper does not justify whether combining different network types (word-level, sub-word) actually improves classification accuracy.  
- The manuscript should better explain how embeddings are computed for a low-resource language (Portuguese) and detail preprocessing/transcription standards.  
- Revise structure: shorten sections 1–3, compress section 4.1, and expand experimental methodology and results explanations.  
- Clarify NURC/SP Norm 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription standards for readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Fix typos and minor errors: line 29 "as it a lightweight"→"as a lightweight"; line 188 "PLN"→"NLP"; line 264 typo; Table 3 "72,0"→"72.0"; remove duplicate DOI and correct bibliography link.
