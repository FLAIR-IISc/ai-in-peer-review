- Paper explores NLP on Portuguese speech transcripts to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI).  
- Authors claim successful distinction between healthy and MCI subjects, but reported accuracy (60–85%) contradicts early optimistic statements.  
- Work is significant for advancing healthcare NLP and for Portuguese, a lower-resource language.  
- Dataset is very small (20 MCI + 20 controls), limiting statistical power and generalizability.  
- Dataset is exclusively Portuguese and lacks English examples or translations, reducing accessibility and reproducibility.  
- Paper should include translated transcript snippets, network embedding visualizations, and detailed pipeline technicals to strengthen reproducibility.  
- Introduction overemphasizes general NLP background and includes tangential citations that should be pruned.  
- Section 4.2.3 should be condensed to focus strictly on MCI-related NLP literature.  
- Sections 1–3 (five pages) should be compressed to prioritize technical innovation and examples.  
- Unclear word-embedding sources: authors do not specify whether embeddings were trained on the ABCD/Cinderella datasets or external corpora.  
- Embedding methodology is insufficiently described—how similarities were computed for short Portuguese narratives is unclear.  
- Claim that combining two network types improves accuracy lacks evidence and comparison to single-network baselines.  
- Origin and handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words are unexplained and likely stem from limited dataset vocabulary.  
- Paper should clarify whether multilingual models, transfer learning, or external corpora were used and specify transcription norms (NURC/SP codes) for reproducibility.  
- Several technical corrections needed (typos, duplicate DOI, formatting in Table 3, wrong acronym "PLN" → "NLP").  
- Overall: promising interdisciplinary idea but requires major revision for methodological transparency, dataset limitations, and reproducibility before claims can be validated.
