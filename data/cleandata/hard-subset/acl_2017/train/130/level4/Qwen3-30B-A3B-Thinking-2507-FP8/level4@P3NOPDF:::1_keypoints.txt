- Topic relevance: applying NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts for early MCI detection is timely and clinically important.  
- Contradictory accuracy claims: paper alternately claims successful distinction and then states 60–85% makes it “not easy to distinguish,” needing contextualization.  
- Tiny dataset: only 20 MCI + 20 controls, severely limiting statistical validity.  
- Language/accessibility issue: Portuguese-only corpus reduces reproducibility and international accessibility; include English-translated examples.  
- Overlong, tangential literature review: introduction and sections include irrelevant citations and should be condensed and focused on clinical NLP.  
- Unclear embedding methodology: missing details on continuous representation sources, training data, OOV handling, and justification for combining n-gram and word-level networks.  
- Short narratives validity: no explanation for how very short transcripts yield meaningful similarity measures.  
- Unsupported claims about network fusion: assertion that combining two network types improves accuracy is not demonstrated.  
- Minor editorial issues: “PLN” should be “NLP,” multiple typos, table decimal formatting, duplicate DOI/broken link, and unexplained transcription norms.  
- Required revisions: expand dataset (suggest ≥100 per group), provide English examples, tighten literature review, clarify embedding and fusion methods, and reconcile accuracy with baselines/confidence intervals.  
- Overall verdict: important topic but currently not rigorous or replicable; recommend reject with major revision.
