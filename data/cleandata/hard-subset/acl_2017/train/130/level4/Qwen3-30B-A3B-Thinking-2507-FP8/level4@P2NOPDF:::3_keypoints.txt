- Problem, datasets, and domain context are well-described, but the approach lacks sufficient justification and methodological rigor.  
- The paper overstates novelty by presenting largely existing techniques as novel.  
- Disfluency removal is unjustified and may discard diagnostically relevant signals for MCI.  
- It is unclear whether word embeddings were trained on the small MCI datasets or external corpora; dataset names are not consistently specified.  
- The set of "linguistic features" is undefined and exclusion of tf-idf (standard BoW weighting) is not justified.  
- The multi-view learning framework is not described, preventing assessment of that core component.  
- No statistical significance tests are reported to support claimed performance differences.  
- Related work is superficial and lacks detailed comparisons of prior methods, algorithms, and results.  
- Numerous editorial and clarity issues (typos, phrasing) and missing citations/links (e.g., DementiaBank, Picnic picture) require correction.  
- Many specific clarifications requested: explicit dataset names, definitions for acronyms (WML, PLN, ABCD), transcription norms, and preprocessing steps.  
- Table 1 should include key dataset statistics (e.g., total utterances, mean age).  
- Section 4.2.2 must be expanded to define all linguistic features and explain their relevance to MCI; address tf-idf omission.  
- The paper must explicitly state embedding training sources and clarify segmentation/manual corrections of transcripts.  
- Overall recommendation: promising clinical problem but insufficient novelty, justification, and validation; requires major revision before acceptance.
