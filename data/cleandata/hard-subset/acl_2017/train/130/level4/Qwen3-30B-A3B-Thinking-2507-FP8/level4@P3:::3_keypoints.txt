- Contributions: language-independent complex-network framework enriched with word embeddings for MCI detection; empirical validation across three datasets showing networks outperform linguistic features; multi-view learning combining networks with linguistic/BoW yields up to ~10% accuracy gain.

- Strength — Relevance & novelty: addresses disfluent transcripts without manual correction or parsing, offering a different paradigm with strong reported gains (e.g., 75% vs 50–65% on Cinderella).

- Strength — Evaluation: tested on three datasets (including two Portuguese ones) with multi-view learning showing consistent, sometimes statistically claimed, improvements (e.g., 80% on ABCD).

- Strength — Method clarity: network + embedding enrichment and topological feature extraction are well motivated; preprocessing choices and standard metrics are clearly described.

- Weakness — Missing implementation details: unclear whether embeddings were trained on small MCI datasets or external corpora, and the cosine-similarity threshold for edge creation is unspecified, hindering reproducibility.

- Weakness — Inconsistent disfluency handling: filled pauses removed but other disfluencies kept without justification, despite disfluencies being potentially diagnostic for MCI.

- Weakness — No statistical testing reported: claimed accuracy improvements lack significance tests, which is critical given small sample sizes.

- Weakness — Superficial positioning vs prior work: related work doesn’t clearly contrast parsing-dependent methods or explicitly show how the proposed approach advances beyond them.

- Question: Were word embeddings trained on the MCI datasets or external corpora, and how was overfitting mitigated for small datasets?

- Question: Why remove filled pauses but retain other disfluencies, and were disfluency-based features evaluated as predictors?

- Question: What statistical tests were used to confirm the reported accuracy improvements (e.g., 10% on Cinderella, 4.71% on ABCD)?

- Question: How do the tables showing linguistic features outperforming networks after manual revision reconcile with the claim that networks outperform linguistic features?

- Additional: Shorten abstract to 150–200 words and emphasize core contributions and key results.

- Additional: Clarify the meaning of "multi-view learning" (e.g., majority voting vs. weighted fusion) in the methods.
