

The paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem statement, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. While some details are missing, the proposed solution and experimental design appear reasonable. Overall, the study is interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires substantial editing to improve clarity and completeness. Key issues include:  
1. **Missing technical details**: The multi-view learning approach is not explained. The set of "linguistic features" lacks clarification. The datasets used for word embedding generation (presumably the three described in Section 3.1) appear insufficient for this purpose.  
2. **Questionable preprocessing choices**: Disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) are removed without justification, despite their potential relevance to MCI detection.  
3. **Methodological gaps**: The absence of tf-idf weighting for BoW classification is unexplained, and statistical significance tests for experimental results are omitted.  
4. **Superficial related work**: Section 4.2.1 lacks specific details on prior methods and their comparison to this work.
