- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, avoiding manual transcript correction.  
- Language-independent approach representing short texts via network topology + semantic embeddings.  
- Evaluated on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, ABCD) showing network features outperform linguistic features; ensembles/multi-view methods boost accuracy up to ~10%.

- Practical innovation: removes manual disfluency correction bottleneck, enabling scalable clinical applications.  
- Robust multi-dataset evaluation (English and Portuguese) with consistent improvements; ABCD reaches ~80% accuracy.  
- Clear technical explanation and illustrative example of embedding-enriched networks vs. co-occurrence networks.

- Very small Portuguese datasets (Cinderella, ABCD; ~20–23 MCI cases) limit statistical reliability and reproducibility.  
- Embedding methodology is underspecified: source corpus, training details, and OOV handling are unclear.  
- Inconsistent claims about performance: “high accuracy” vs. concluding accuracy 60–85%—needs contextualization.  
- Excessive general NLP background distracts from MCI-specific contributions; suggest trimming Sections 1–3.
