Strengths:
- Tackles MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets, addressing a clear clinical problem.
- Proposes a graph-based method leveraging word co-occurrence and encoding lexical, syntactic, and graph features.
- Evaluates models via 5-fold cross-validation across multiple classifiers and reports dataset-specific performance.
- Uses appropriate, domain-relevant datasets.

Weaknesses:
- Manuscript has many grammatical and spelling errors, severely affecting readability.
- Core classification task and dataset reporting are unclear; transcript- vs. patient-level labeling and table statistics are inconsistent and sometimes irrelevant.
- Inconsistencies in dataset descriptions (e.g., participant counts mismatch between text and tables).
- Insufficient justification for the specific graph enrichment choices; alternatives like vector-similarity edges are not considered.
- No use of domain-specific biomedical or linguistic resources despite domain-specific datasets.
- Evaluation uses accuracy despite unclear class imbalance; F1-score or other imbalance-aware metrics would be more appropriate.
- Results lack statistical significance testing and report over-precise metrics on very small datasets, undermining claims of improvement.
