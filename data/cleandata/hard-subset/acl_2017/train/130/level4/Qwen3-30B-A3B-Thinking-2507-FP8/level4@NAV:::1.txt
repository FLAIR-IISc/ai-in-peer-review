

Strengths:  
This paper proposes applying NLP to speech transcripts (narratives and descriptions) to identify patients with MCI (ICD-10 code F06.7). The authors assert they distinguish healthy controls from MCI patients (lines 141–144), yet the conclusion (lines 781–785) states accuracy ranges from 60% to 85%, implying difficulty in differentiation. This discrepancy between the optimistic introduction and cautious conclusion creates an intriguing narrative, prompting interest in the actual methodology. The dataset comprises only 20 healthy participants and 20 controls (20+20), is inaccessible to non-Portuguese speakers, and lacks technical depth. Including more methodological details, an English-translated transcript example, and a sample network with embeddings would significantly strengthen the work.  

Weaknesses:  
The introduction and literature review (Section 4.2.3) contain unnecessary citations (e.g., Salton 1989, lines 608–621) and off-topic references (e.g., "sentiment classification," "pedestrian detection," lines 652–654). Streamlining Sections 1–3 and compressing Section 4.2.3 to focus strictly on the topic is recommended. The paper should prioritize detailing technological innovation, including English translations of ABCD/Cindarela narratives. The short Portuguese narratives (ABCD dataset) raise questions about word similarity computation for embeddings. Lines 272–289 describe generating word-level networks from continuous representations, but the source for these representations (ABCD+Cinderella only, or external corpora?) remains unclear. Lines 513–525 mention sub-word (n-gram) networks for embeddings, yet the training source and validation of combined network efficacy are unaddressed. The term "out-of-vocabulary words" (line 516) requires clarification regarding their origin.  

General Discussion:  
The study’s focus on NLP for cognitive impairment detection is timely and valuable. Its exploration of Portuguese NLP is particularly significant, warranting explanation of embedding computation for low-resource languages (compared to English). The text requires revision: shorten Sections 1–3, compress Section 4.1, and expand experimental details. Clarification on NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms is also needed.
