- Applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify patients with MCI (ICD-10 F06.7).  
- Claims clear distinction between healthy and MCI in intro but conclusion reports 60–85% accuracy, creating a contradictory narrative.  
- Dataset is very small (20 healthy + 20 controls), Portuguese-only, and not accessible to non-Portuguese speakers.  
- Manuscript lacks methodological depth; adding more methods, an English-translated transcript example, and a sample network with embeddings is recommended.  
- Introduction and literature review contain unnecessary and off-topic citations; Sections 1–3 and 4.2.3 should be streamlined.  
- Paper should emphasize technological innovation and include English translations of ABCD/Cinderella narratives.  
- Short Portuguese narratives raise concerns about how word similarity and embeddings are computed from limited text.  
- It is unclear whether continuous representations (for word-level networks) are trained only on ABCD+Cinderella or use external corpora.  
- Sub-word (n-gram) network training source and the validation of combined network efficacy are not addressed.  
- The term "out-of-vocabulary words" is used ambiguously and needs clarification about their origin.  
- Work is timely and valuable for cognitive impairment detection and Portuguese NLP but must explain embedding computation for low-resource languages.  
- Recommend revising text: shorten Sections 1–3, compress Section 4.1, and expand experimental details.  
- Clarify transcription norms NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2.  
- Technical corrections: fix typos and phrasing (e.g., line 029), PLN→NLP (line 188), remove duplicate phrase (line 264), correct Table 3 formatting (72.0), and fix DOI/link errors (lines ~992–1002).
