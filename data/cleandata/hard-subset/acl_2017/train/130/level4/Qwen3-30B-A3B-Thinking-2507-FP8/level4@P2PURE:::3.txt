

The paper presents a novel application of established representations, feature sets, and methods for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem definition, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. While some details are missing, the proposed solution and experimental design are reasonable. Overall, the study is interesting and informative.

The paper requires significant editing to improve readability and clarity. Key concepts are inadequately explained: the multi-view learning approach is not described; the composition of the "linguistic features" set is unclear; the source of word embeddings (presumably the three datasets mentioned, which appear insufficiently sized) is unspecified; the rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is missing despite their potential relevance to MCI detection; the omission of the standard tf-idf weighting scheme for BoW classification is unexplained; and statistical significance tests are absent to support experimental conclusions. The related work section is superficial, lacking specific methodological comparisons. Detailed comments follow:
