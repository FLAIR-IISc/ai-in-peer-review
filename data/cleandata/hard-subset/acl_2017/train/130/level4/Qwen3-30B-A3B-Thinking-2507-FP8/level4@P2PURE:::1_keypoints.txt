- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to detect mild cognitive impairment (MCI) and reports accuracy between ~60%–85%, but conclusions are less optimistic than the introduction.  
- Dataset is very small (20 healthy + 20 MCI) and not accessible to non-Portuguese speakers.  
- Recommend adding technical details, an English-translated example transcript, and a sample network with embeddings to strengthen the paper.  
- Introduction and related work include superficial or unrelated references (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) and should be condensed.  
- Sections 1–3 and 4.2.3 are overly long and should focus strictly on technical innovation and relevant literature.  
- Short Portuguese narratives raise questions about how reliable word similarities/embeddings were computed from limited text.  
- Unclear whether word- and sub-word embeddings/networks are trained on the ABCD+Cinderella transcripts alone or on external corpora; training sources must be specified.  
- Claim that combining word- and sub-word networks improves accuracy needs empirical justification and explanation.  
- Origin and handling of out-of-vocabulary words are unspecified and should be clarified.  
- Contribution to Portuguese/low-resource NLP (embedding computation) is valuable but not explicitly explained.  
- Paper needs revision for structure: shorten early sections, compress Section 4.1, and elaborate experimental details.  
- Clarify transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2) used in the study.  
- Several technical/typo issues to fix (e.g., “as it a lightweight” → “as in a lightweight”; PLN → NLP; redundant phrase; numeric/DOI/reference errors).
