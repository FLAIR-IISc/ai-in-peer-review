- Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets.  
- Proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence with lexical, syntactic, and graph-derived features.  
- Evaluates models with 5-fold cross-validation across multiple classifiers, showing dataset-specific model behavior.  
- Targets a well-defined clinical problem and uses appropriate datasets.  

- Manuscript readability is poor due to grammatical and spelling errors; needs thorough language editing.  
- Core classification setup is unclear (per-transcript labeling vs. other); dataset statistics reported at study level rather than transcript level; age is overemphasized; participant count discrepancy between text and tables.  
- No justification for enriched graph structure; alternative of word-node graphs using vector similarity is not discussed.  
- No use of domain-specific linguistic or clinical tools despite biomedical context.  
- Class distribution is unclear, so using accuracy alone is unjustified; F1-score would be more appropriate.  
- Results are reported to high precision on small datasets (e.g., 43 transcripts) without statistical significance testing, making performance claims unverifiable.
