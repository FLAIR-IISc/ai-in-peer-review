- Paper addresses MCI detection from speech and is relevant to the ACL community.  
- Reviewer recommends major revisions due to gaps undermining novelty and reproducibility.  
- Methodological ambiguity: multi-view learning lacks architecture, fusion strategy, and rationale.  
- Linguistic features are poorly defined; metrics like "syntactic complexity" and "lexical diversity" need concrete operationalization.  
- Removal of disfluencies is unjustified and problematic because disfluencies can be clinical markers of MCI.  
- Word embedding training on the three small datasets (~1,000–2,000 narratives) is likely insufficient; clarify whether external corpora were used.  
- Experimental rigor is weak: no statistical significance tests or confidence intervals for performance comparisons.  
- TF-IDF choice is unexplained; reliance on raw term frequency is questionable and needs justification.  
- Inconsistent preprocessing: rationale for removing disfluencies in BoW but not other features must be addressed.  
- Related work section is superficial and fails to contrast prior methods, datasets, and results with this work.  
- Key terms and datasets (PLN, WML subtest, ABCD, DementiaBank, Picnic picture) lack definitions and citations.  
- Manuscript has grammatical errors, repetitive phrasing, and an overly verbose abstract (should be ~150 words).  
- Requested revision: expand Section 4.2.2 to define all linguistic features and justify their relevance to MCI.  
- Requested revision: detail embedding training corpus and parameters in Section 4.2.1 and acknowledge dataset size limitations.  
- Requested revision: fully describe the multi-view classification scheme in Section 4.3 and add appropriate statistical tests.  
- Tables/Figures need demographic statistics (mean age, gender distribution, dataset sizes) and clearer captions.  
- Ensure all citations follow ACL style and add references for transcription norms and clinical assessment tools.  
- Final recommendation: major revision required; work could be valuable if the above issues are addressed, but current claims are unsubstantiated.
