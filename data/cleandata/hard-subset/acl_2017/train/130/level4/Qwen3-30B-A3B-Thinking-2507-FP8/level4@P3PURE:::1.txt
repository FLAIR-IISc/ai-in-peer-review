

Strengths:  
This paper proposes applying NLP to speech transcripts (narratives and descriptions) to identify patients with mild cognitive impairment (MCI, ICD-10 code F06.7). The authors initially claim successful distinction between healthy controls and MCI patients (lines 141–144), but the conclusion (lines 781–785) states that "accuracy ranging from 60% to 85%... means that it is not easy to distinguish between healthy subjects and those with cognitive impairments." While this creates a discrepancy between the introduction and conclusion, the overall message remains encouraging, prompting interest in the technical details. The dataset comprises only 20 healthy and 20 control participants (20+20), and its Portuguese-only nature limits accessibility for non-Portuguese speakers. Including more technical details, a translated transcript example (e.g., English), and a sample network with embeddings would significantly enhance clarity.

Weaknesses:  
The introduction and related work (section 4.2.3) contain irrelevant or overly basic references (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection in images; lines 652–654, 608–621). These should be omitted to compress the first five pages and refocus the review strictly on the paper’s core topic. The manuscript should instead emphasize technological innovation, including English translations of ABCD/Cindarela narratives. Additionally, the short Portuguese narratives (especially in ABCD) raise questions about word similarity computation for embeddings. The paper states that word-level networks derive from continuous word representations (lines 272–289), but does not clarify: (1) the source of these representations (ABCD+Cinderella only, or external corpora?), (2) whether sub-word networks (lines 513–525) genuinely improve accuracy, or (3) the origin of "out-of-vocabulary words" (line 516). These gaps require explicit resolution.

General Discussion:  
The study’s focus on NLP for cognitive impairment detection is timely and valuable. Its exploration of Portuguese NLP—particularly for low-resource languages—is also significant, though the methodology for generating embeddings must be clarified. The text requires revision: shorten sections 1–3, condense section 4.1, and expand experimental details. Further, the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms warrant explanation.
