**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex network features enriched with word embeddings. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and apply machine learning algorithms to classify them into MCI and control groups. The study uses three datasets, including the DementiaBank and two Portuguese datasets, Cinderella and Arizona-Battery. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Application of complex network features to MCI detection**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of complex network features in detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which is a novel application of this approach.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors propose a method to enrich complex networks with word embeddings, which improves the representation of short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments.
3. **Comparison with traditional linguistic features**: The authors compare the performance of complex network features with traditional linguistic features and show that the former outperforms the latter in all datasets.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel application of complex network features**: The authors propose a new approach to MCI detection using complex network features, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Effective use of word embeddings**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of enriching complex networks with word embeddings, which improves the representation of short texts.
3. **Comparison with traditional linguistic features**: The authors provide a thorough comparison of complex network features with traditional linguistic features, which is essential for understanding the strengths and limitations of each approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of detail on multi-view learning**: The authors do not provide sufficient detail on the multi-view learning scheme used in the study, which makes it difficult to understand the approach.
2. **Insufficient explanation of linguistic features**: The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the linguistic features used in the study, which makes it challenging to understand the comparison with complex network features.
3. **No statistical significance testing**: The authors do not provide statistical significance testing to substantiate the conclusions from the experiments, which is essential for validating the results.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more detail on the multi-view learning scheme used in the study?
2. How did you select the linguistic features used in the study, and can you provide more information on each feature?
3. Why did you choose not to use statistical significance testing to validate the results?

**Additional Comments**

The paper needs considerable editing to improve readability. The authors should provide more detail on key concepts, such as multi-view learning and linguistic features. Additionally, the authors should consider providing statistical significance testing to validate the results.
