- Tackles MCI detection from speech transcripts across three distinct datasets using an appropriate problem and datasets.
- Proposes a graph-based method leveraging word co-occurrence and encodes lexical and syntactic graph features.
- Evaluates models with 5-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers, reporting performance variations across datasets.

- Manuscript quality: writing contains many grammar and spelling errors, hindering readability.
- Problem formulation is unclear: indistinct definition of a classification instance and insufficient transcript-level dataset descriptions; tables focus on study details rather than data used for classification.
- Dataset reporting issues and irrelevant details (e.g., patient age) with inconsistencies in reported counts (e.g., gender totals vs. stated sample size).
- Method motivation unclear: unclear why graph enrichment is necessary versus simpler word-vector similarity graphs.
- No use of domain-specific biomedical tools despite biomedical datasets.
- Evaluation concerns: class distribution is unclear so accuracy may be inappropriate; F1 score should be reported for this binary task.
- Statistical validity issues: results reported to four decimals on very small datasets (e.g., 43 transcripts) without statistical tests to assess significance.
