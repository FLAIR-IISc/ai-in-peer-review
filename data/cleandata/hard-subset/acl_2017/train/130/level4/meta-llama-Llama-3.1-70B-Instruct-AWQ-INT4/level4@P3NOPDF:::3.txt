This paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The authors thoroughly describe the problem, datasets, and domain, providing a solid foundation for the study. While the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable, some key details are missing, and the paper requires considerable editing to improve readability.

One of the primary drawbacks of the paper is the lack of clarity on several key concepts. For instance, the authors do not provide sufficient details on the multi-view learning approach used, and the set of "linguistic features" needs to be clarified. Additionally, it is unclear what datasets were used to generate the word embeddings, which is a crucial aspect of the study. The authors mention three datasets, but these appear to be too small for this purpose.

Furthermore, the decision to remove disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) from the dataset is not adequately justified. These features might be important indicators of MCI, and their removal could impact the results. The authors also fail to explain why they did not use the popular tf-idf weighting scheme for the Bag-of-Words (BoW) classifications. Moreover, the paper lacks tests for significance to substantiate the conclusions drawn from the experiments.

The related work section is also superficial and lacks depth. The authors could provide more detailed information on the methods used in previous studies and how they compare to the current study. Additionally, some citations are missing, and the definition of "PLN" is not provided.

The paper requires significant revisions to address these issues. The authors should provide more detailed explanations of their methods, clarify the datasets used, and justify their decisions regarding feature removal and weighting schemes. Furthermore, they should expand the related work section and provide additional citations to support their claims.
