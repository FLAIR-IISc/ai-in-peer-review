- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts (narratives/descriptions) to identify mild cognitive impairment (MCI, ICD-10 F06.7).
- Authors claim successful distinction between healthy and MCI participants, but conclude 60–85% accuracy shows the task remains challenging (tone discrepancy).
- Dataset is small (20 healthy, 20 control) and limited to Portuguese speakers, raising generalizability concerns.
- Reviewer requests more technical detail, an English-translated example transcript, and a sample network with embeddings.
- Introduction and literature review are overly long; some cited works (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) are irrelevant and should be removed.
- First five pages should be condensed and focus tightened to topic-specific literature and innovations.
- Unclear how continuous word representations/embeddings were learned—whether from ABCD+Cinderella alone or external corpora.
- Use of sub-word (n-gram) networks to generate embeddings is not fully explained, nor is whether combining network types improves accuracy.
- Origin and handling of out-of-vocabulary words are not specified.
- Important to clarify how embeddings are computed for Portuguese, a lower-resource language.
- Manuscript needs revision: shorten sections 1–3, compress section 4.1, and provide more experimental explanations.
- Clarify transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).
- Minor technical/typo corrections: line 29 ("as it a" → "as in a"), line 188 ("PLN" → "NLP"), line 264 repeated words, Table 3 decimal format ("72,0" → "72.0").
- Bibliography issues: duplicate DOI entries and a mislinked title in the reference list.
