The paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem statement, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. Although some details are missing, the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable. Overall, the study is interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires significant editing to enhance readability. Certain key concepts lack clarity, such as the specifics of the multi-view learning approach employed. The definition of the "linguistic features" set is also unclear. Furthermore, it is not apparent which datasets were used to generate the word embeddings, which seem to be based on the three described datasets that appear insufficient for this purpose. The removal of disfluencies, such as filled pauses and false starts, from the dataset is also not justified, as these may be crucial features in the context of MCI. Additionally, the popular tf-idf weighting scheme was not used for the BoW classifications, and significance tests are not provided to substantiate the conclusions drawn from the experiments. The related work section is also somewhat superficial.
