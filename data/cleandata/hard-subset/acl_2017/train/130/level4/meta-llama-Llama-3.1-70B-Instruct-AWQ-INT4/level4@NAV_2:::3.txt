**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts by enriching complex networks with word embeddings. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and use topological features to classify MCI patients from healthy controls. They evaluate their approach on three datasets, including the DementiaBank and two Portuguese datasets, and compare their results with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representations.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a new approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings, which outperforms traditional linguistic features in some cases.
2. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on three datasets, including a Portuguese dataset, which is a valuable contribution to the field of MCI detection.
3. The authors provide a thorough comparison of their approach with traditional methods, including linguistic features and bag-of-words representations.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which is a valuable contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach on three datasets, including a Portuguese dataset.
3. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed approach and its evaluation.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper needs considerable editing to improve readability, with some details on key concepts missing or unclear.
2. The authors do not provide sufficient details on the multi-view learning approach used, and the set of linguistic features needs to be clarified.
3. The paper lacks tests for significance to substantiate the conclusions from the experiments.
4. The related work is described superficially, with some citations missing or unclear.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the multi-view learning approach used and how it compares to other approaches in the field?
2. Can you clarify the set of linguistic features used and how they were selected?
3. Can you provide tests for significance to substantiate the conclusions from the experiments?
4. Can you provide more details on the datasets used, including the size of the datasets and the preprocessing steps applied?
