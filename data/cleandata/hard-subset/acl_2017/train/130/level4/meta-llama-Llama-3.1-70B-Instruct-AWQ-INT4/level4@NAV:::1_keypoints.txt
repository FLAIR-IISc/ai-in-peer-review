- Proposes an innovative NLP application to speech transcripts for identifying patients with mild cognitive impairment (MCI, ICD-10 F06.7).
- Claims ability to distinguish healthy controls from MCI patients, though reported accuracy (60–85%) indicates the task remains difficult.
- Corpus of 20 healthy patients and 20 control participants is a valuable resource but limited in size and Portuguese language restricts accessibility.
- Paper would benefit from more technical details such as English translations of short transcripts and example networks with embeddings.
- Some cited references (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) are irrelevant and should be removed.
- First five pages are overly long; sections 1–3 and part of 4.1 should be condensed to focus the paper.
- Description of how word similarities were computed and how word-level networks were generated from continuous representations is unclear.
- Use of sub-word (n-gram) networks and how they were trained, combined with word-level networks, and their advantages are insufficiently explained.
- The source and handling of "out-of-vocabulary" words are not clearly described.
- The focus on Portuguese and techniques for computing embeddings in a lower-resource language is a valuable contribution.
- Revision should include shortening early sections, adding clearer experimental explanations, and clarifying transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).
- Specific technical copyedits needed: line 29 wording, line 188 replace "PLN" with "NLP", line 264 remove repeated phrase, Table 3 fix "72,0" to "72.0", and fix duplicate DOI/link at lines 995–996.
