- Innovative application of NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify patients with mild cognitive impairment (MCI).
- Authors claim successful distinction between healthy controls and MCI, but conclusion notes accuracy only 60–85%, tempering optimism.
- Dataset contains 20 healthy and 20 control participants; non-English (Portuguese) content may limit accessibility.
- Paper needs more methodological/technical detail, including an English-translated short transcript and sample network with embeddings.
- Introduction and background are overly long; some cited references (e.g., Salton 1989, unrelated examples) are extraneous and should be removed.
- Sections 1–3 and particularly section 4.2.3 should be condensed to focus on core contributions and innovations.
- Short Portuguese narratives (ABCD dataset) raise concerns about how word similarities for embeddings were learned given limited data.
- Unclear whether continuous word representations and sub-word (n-gram) embeddings were trained solely on the datasets or used external corpora; clarify sources.
- Unclear whether combining word-level and sub-word networks improves accuracy and what constitutes "out-of-vocabulary" words and their origin.
- Emphasize explanation of embedding computation for Portuguese, a relatively low-resource language.
- Revise text for clarity and structure, compress section 4.1, and add more experimental explanations.
- Clarify transcription norms NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 for reproducibility.
- Technical corrections: Line 029 ('as it a' → 'as in a'), Line 188 ('PLN' → 'NLP'), Line 264 repeated words, Table 3 formatting ('72,0' → '72.0'), and DOI/link duplication issues (lines 992–993, 995–996, 1001–1002).
