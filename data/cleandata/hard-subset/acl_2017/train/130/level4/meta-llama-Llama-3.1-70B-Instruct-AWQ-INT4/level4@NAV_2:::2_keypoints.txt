- Summary: Proposes a graph-based method using complex networks and word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, encoding lexical and syntactic graph features.

- Contribution: Applies complex networks to MCI detection, reportedly outperforming some traditional linguistic features.

- Contribution: Enriches complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts and improve detection.

- Contribution: Explores multiview learning and ensemble methods to combine feature spaces and classifiers, yielding improved results in some cases.

- Strength: Targets a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets for evaluation.

- Strength: Novel application of complex networks to speech-based MCI detection.

- Strength: Combination of multiview learning and ensembles shows performance gains.

- Weakness: Manuscript quality is poor with errors in English, grammar, and spelling that impair readability.

- Weakness: Machine learning problem is unclearly described, lacking a definition of a single classification instance.

- Weakness: Dataset descriptions are insufficient, missing key information like number of transcripts and class distributions.

- Weakness: Does not leverage domain-specific biomedical tools or expertise.

- Weakness: Evaluation is inadequate—relies on accuracy and omits metrics like F1, confidence intervals, and statistical tests.

- Weakness: Motivation for enriching graphs with word embeddings is unclear and alternative approaches are not considered.

- Question: Clarify the ML problem and explicitly define what constitutes one classification instance.

- Question: Justify using accuracy as the primary metric and provide additional metrics (e.g., F1) and confidence intervals.

- Question: Explain the motivation for graph enrichment with embeddings and discuss alternative approaches.

- Question: How will the authors address manuscript quality issues and provide more complete dataset descriptions?
