The paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described, providing a solid foundation for the study. Although some details are missing, the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable. Overall, the study is interesting and informative, offering valuable insights into the application of natural language processing techniques to a critical healthcare issue.

However, there are several areas that require improvement. The paper needs considerable editing to enhance readability. Key concepts, such as multi-view learning, are not adequately explained. The set of "linguistic features" requires clarification, and it is unclear which datasets were used to generate the word embeddings. The decision to remove disfluencies, such as filled pauses, false starts, and repetitions, from the dataset is also unclear, as these features may be relevant in the context of MCI. Additionally, the paper does not provide tests for significance to substantiate the conclusions drawn from the experiments. The related work section could be more comprehensive, with a deeper analysis of existing methods and their comparison to the proposed approach.
