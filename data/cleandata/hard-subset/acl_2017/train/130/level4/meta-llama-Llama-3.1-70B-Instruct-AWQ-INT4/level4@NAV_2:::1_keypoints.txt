- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks enriched with word embeddings, evaluated on three datasets and claiming better performance than traditional linguistic features.
- Contribution: Introduces complex networks as a lightweight, language-independent representation for MCI detection.
- Contribution: Enriches complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short neuropsychological transcripts.
- Contribution: Evaluates the approach on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery).
- Strength: Demonstrates encouraging accuracy (≈60–85%) distinguishing healthy controls from MCI patients.
- Strength: Presents a novel methodological departure from traditional linguistic features.
- Strength: Approach is language-independent, potentially applicable across languages.
- Weakness: Paper’s introduction and related work are overly detailed and could be shortened.
- Weakness: Lacks technical examples (e.g., translated short transcripts, sample networks with embeddings).
- Weakness: Unclear source and training procedure for the word embeddings (dataset vs external corpora).
- Weakness: Unclear explanation of the benefit of combining word-level and sub-word-level networks for embeddings.
- Question: Please provide technological details such as example transcripts translated to English and sample networks with embeddings.
- Question: Clarify the source and training method for the word embeddings (learned from datasets or external corpora).
- Question: Explain the benefit of combining word-level and sub-word-level networks to generate embeddings.
- Question: Provide more details on experiments and the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms.
