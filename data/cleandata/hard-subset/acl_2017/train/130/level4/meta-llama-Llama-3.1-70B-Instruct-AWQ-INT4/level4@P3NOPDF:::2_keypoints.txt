- Paper addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts using a graph-based co-occurrence method and graph-derived features.
- Experiments use three datasets with 5-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers; problem is well-defined and datasets are broadly suitable.
- Manuscript readability is poor due to incorrect English and numerous grammatical/spelling errors.
- The paper fails to clearly define the machine learning problem, particularly what constitutes a single classification instance.
- Dataset descriptions focus on study-level details rather than transcript-level information relevant to the task.
- Tables and reported demographics include irrelevant details (e.g., patient age) and contain numerical inconsistencies.
- The motivation for enriching the graph is weak and alternatives (e.g., word-node graphs based on vector similarity) are not discussed.
- The authors do not leverage biomedical/domain-specific tools despite using biomedical datasets.
- Class distributions are not reported, making accuracy a potentially inappropriate metric for this binary task.
- The paper omits F1 (and other appropriate) metrics that better reflect binary classification performance.
- Results are reported to excessive precision on very small datasets and no statistical significance tests are provided.
- Recommendations: improve writing, explicitly define the instance/ML setup, refine dataset reporting, justify/compare graph choices, use domain tools, report F1 and run statistical tests.
