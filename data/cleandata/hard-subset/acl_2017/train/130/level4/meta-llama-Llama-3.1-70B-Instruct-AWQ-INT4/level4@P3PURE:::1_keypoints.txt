- Proposes an innovative NLP application on speech transcripts to identify MCI, claiming classification accuracy between ~60%–85%, though authors temper this optimism.  
- Provides a valuable Portuguese dataset (20 healthy, 20 MCI), but dataset size and language limit generalizability; include an English-translated transcript and sample network/embeddings.  
- Literature review is overly broad and includes unrelated citations (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection); condense and focus on directly relevant work.  
- Short Portuguese narratives (especially ABCD) make it unclear how word similarities/embeddings were learned—source corpora and training procedure are not specified.  
- Use of sub-word (n-gram) networks for embeddings is ambiguous: unclear training source, how OOV (out-of-vocabulary) words are handled, and whether combining word- and sub-word networks improves accuracy.  
- Paper’s focus on NLP for Portuguese is valuable; authors should explain embedding computation and strategies for low-resource languages.  
- Recommend more technical detail and clarity in methods (e.g., example transcripts, sample networks, experimental explanations).  
- Specific technical errors to fix: phrasing typo (line 29), PLN→NLP (line 188), repeated words (line 264), numeric formatting in Table 3 (72,0 → 72.0), duplicate DOI and mislinked reference (lines ~992–1002).  
- Revision suggestions: shorten sections 1–3, compress section 4.1, add experimental details, and clarify NURC/SP N.338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms.
