- Tackles MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets using a graph-based method.  
- Leverages word co-occurrence graphs and encodes features from graph characteristics (lexical, syntactic, etc.).  
- Evaluation uses 5-fold cross-validation with multiple classifiers and varying performance across datasets.  
- Paper addresses a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets.  
- Manuscript readability is poor due to incorrect English, grammatical errors, and spelling mistakes.  
- Problem formulation is unclear—what constitutes a single classification instance is not well defined (transcript vs. study level).  
- Dataset descriptions are overly long, contain irrelevant details (e.g., patient age), and show inconsistencies in reported counts.  
- Motivation for graph enrichment is unclear; alternative designs (word-as-node with vector-similarity edges) are not justified.  
- No use of domain-specific biomedical tools despite biomedical datasets.  
- Evaluation metric choice is questionable given unclear class distribution; F1 would be more appropriate than accuracy.  
- Results are reported to excessive precision on very small datasets (e.g., 43 transcripts) without statistical significance testing.
