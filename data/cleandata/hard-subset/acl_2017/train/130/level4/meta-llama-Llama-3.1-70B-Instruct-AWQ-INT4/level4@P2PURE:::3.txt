The paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. Although the proposed solution and experiments appear reasonable, some key details are missing. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires considerable editing to improve readability. For instance, the description of multi-view learning is omitted, and the set of "linguistic features" needs clarification. It is unclear what datasets were used to generate the word embeddings, which appear to be too small for this purpose. Additionally, the removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) from the dataset is not justified, as these may be important features in the context of MCI. The use of the most popular tf-idf weighting scheme for BoW classification is also not explained. Furthermore, tests for significance are not provided to substantiate the conclusions from the experiments. The related work section is described superficially and lacks detailed explanations of the methods used in previous studies.
