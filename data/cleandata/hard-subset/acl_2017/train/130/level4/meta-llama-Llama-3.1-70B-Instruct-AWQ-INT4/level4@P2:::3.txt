The paper proposes a novel application of complex network features to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

**Main Contributions:**

1. The authors propose a new approach to detect MCI in speech narratives using complex network features.
2. They model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts.
3. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to detect MCI in speech narratives using complex network features.
2. The authors provide a thorough description of the problem, datasets, and domain.
3. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.
4. The paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The paper needs considerable editing to improve readability.
2. Details on some key concepts are missing, such as the multi-view learning used and the set of "linguistic features".
3. It is not clear why disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) were removed from the dataset.
4. The related work is described superficially, and more details on the methods used and how they compare to this study would be helpful.
5. Statistical significance of result differences is not provided.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on the multi-view learning used and how it was implemented?
2. Can you clarify why disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) were removed from the dataset?
3. Can you provide more details on the related work and how the methods used in this study compare to previous studies?
4. Can you provide statistical significance of result differences to substantiate the conclusions from the experiments?

**Additional Comments:**

1. The abstract needs to be shortened and rephrased for clarity.
2. The introduction and related work sections need to be expanded for clarity.
3. The section on linguistic features needs to be significantly expanded for clarity.
4. The conference guidelines regarding additional pages ("Supplementary Material") need to be checked.
