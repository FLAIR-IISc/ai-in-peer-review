- Study applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI); reported classification accuracy ranges from 60% to 85%, which is encouraging but inconsistent.  
- Dataset is small: 20 healthy patients and 20 control participants with Portuguese transcripts, raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Paper lacks technical detail and should include at least one short transcript translated into English and a sample network with embeddings.  
- Introduction and literature review are thorough but include irrelevant or outdated citations (e.g., Salton 1989) and unrelated topics (sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) that should be removed.  
- Recommend condensing the first five pages and compressing section 4.1 to focus on the core contributions and technological innovations.  
- Unclear how continuous word representations (embeddings) were learned — whether from the ABCD/Cinderella datasets alone or from external corpora.  
- Use of sub-word (n-gram) networks alongside word-level networks requires clarification on training data sources and the benefit of combining them.  
- Important contribution: applying NLP to detect cognitive impairments and focusing on Portuguese (a lower-resource language) is valuable.  
- Paper should provide a more detailed explanation of how embeddings are computed for Portuguese.  
- Clarify transcription norms NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 used in the study.  
- List of technical corrections: fix typos ("as it a lightweight" → "as in a lightweight"; "PLN" → "NLP"; remove repeated words), numeric formatting ("72,0" → "72.0"), and duplicate/wrong DOI/link entries in references.  
- Revision suggestions: shorten sections 1–3, compress 4.1, expand experiment explanations and data sources for continuous representations, clarify transcription norms, and include translated transcript examples and sample embeddings/networks.
