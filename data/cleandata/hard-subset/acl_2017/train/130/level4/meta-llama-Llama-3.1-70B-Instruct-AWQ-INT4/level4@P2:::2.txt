**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex network features and word embeddings. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts. They evaluate their model on three datasets and report competitive results with traditional methods.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a graph-based method to detect MCI from speech transcripts, which is a novel application of complex network analysis in the biomedical domain.
2. They introduce a new way to enrich complex networks with word embeddings, which improves the representation of short texts.
3. The authors evaluate their model on three datasets and report competitive results with traditional methods.

**Strengths**

* The paper targets a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets.
* The authors propose a novel approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts.
* The results are competitive with traditional methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Poor writing quality**: The paper suffers from incorrect usage of English, which makes it hard to read. A thorough review of grammar and spellings is necessary.
2. **Unclear problem description**: The main machine learning problem being addressed is poorly described. It is unclear what constitutes a single instance of classification, and the dataset descriptions should be revised to reflect this.
3. **Lack of domain-specific tools**: The datasets are from a biomedical domain, but no domain-specific tools have been leveraged.
4. **Insufficient evaluation metrics**: The paper only reports accuracy, which may not be a good measure for evaluation, especially since the dataset class distribution is unclear. F1 score would be a desirable metric to include.
5. **Unclear motivation for enriching the graph**: The authors should provide a clear motivation for enriching the graph with word embeddings, and explain why they chose to represent words as nodes in the graph and connect them by co-occurrence.
6. **Lack of statistical tests**: The results are reported without statistical tests over increments, which makes it unclear if the gains are significant.

**Questions to Authors**

* Can you provide a clear description of the machine learning problem being addressed, including what constitutes a single instance of classification?
* How did you decide to enrich the graph with word embeddings, and why did you choose to represent words as nodes in the graph and connect them by co-occurrence?
* Can you include F1 score as an evaluation metric, and provide a clear justification for using accuracy as the primary metric?
* Can you provide a thorough review of grammar and spellings to improve the writing quality of the paper?
* Can you include statistical tests over increments to determine the significance of the results?
