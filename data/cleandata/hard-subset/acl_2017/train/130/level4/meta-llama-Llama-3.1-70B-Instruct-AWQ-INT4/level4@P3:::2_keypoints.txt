- Proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence and complex network features, enriched with word embeddings, to detect MCI from speech transcripts across three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery).
- Uses complex network features as a lightweight, language-independent representation and word embeddings to capture semantic information in short texts.
- Evaluation performed with 5-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers; model performance varies across datasets.
- Strength: addresses a well-defined problem using appropriate datasets.
- Strength: method is novel and offers a new perspective for MCI detection.
- Strength: combines syntactic (network) and semantic (embedding) information.
- Weakness: poor writing and incorrect English make the paper difficult to read.
- Weakness: unclear description of the machine learning problem and transcript-level dataset details.
- Weakness: motivation for enriching graphs with word embeddings is not clearly explained.
- Weakness: does not leverage domain-specific biomedical tools or expertise.
- Weakness: evaluation relies on accuracy only and lacks statistical tests or significance analysis.
- Question: clarify the ML problem formulation and provide clearer transcript-level dataset descriptions.
- Question: justify the choice to enrich graphs with embeddings versus alternatives (e.g., nodes per word with similarity edges).
- Question: detail any biomedical domain tools or expertise used to support the approach.
- Question: report additional metrics (e.g., F1) and include statistical significance testing.
