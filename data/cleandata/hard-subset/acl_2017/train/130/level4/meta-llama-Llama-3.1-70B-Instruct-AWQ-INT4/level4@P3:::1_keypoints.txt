- Paper proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex network features enriched with word embeddings.
- Authors model transcripts as complex networks and augment them with embeddings to better represent short neuropsychological texts.
- Evaluated approach on three datasets: DementiaBank, Cinderella, and a Portuguese Arizona-Battery (ABCD+Cinderella).
- Reported results show complex networks outperform traditional linguistic features across all datasets.
- Overall message is encouraging, though the paper’s opening is more optimistic than its conclusion.
- Main contributions claimed: novel complex-network approach, embedding-enriched networks, and multi-dataset evaluation.
- Strength: promising empirical results indicating suitability of complex networks for MCI detection.
- Strength: novel methodology combining network topology and embeddings.
- Strength: evaluation across multiple datasets, including Portuguese data.
- Weakness: very small corpus/sample size (20 MCI + 20 controls) limits generalizability.
- Weakness: Portuguese-language corpus may limit accessibility for non-Portuguese readers.
- Weakness: insufficient technical detail—needs example transcripts (translated), sample networks with embeddings, and clearer experimental explanations.
- Weakness: some references appear irrelevant (e.g., sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) and could be removed.
- Weakness: unclear source and training data for the continuous word embeddings used.
- Question: What corpus/source was used to learn the continuous word representations?
- Question: Were embeddings trained only on the provided datasets (ABCD+Cinderella) or on external corpora?
- Question: How is it demonstrated that word-level and sub-word-level networks improve accuracy?
- Question: What are the “out-of-vocabulary” words referenced (line 516) and where do they originate?
- Additional comment: Manuscript needs editing—shorten sections 1–3, compress 4.1, and add experiment clarifications.
- Additional comment: Provide clarification on transcription norms NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2.
