The paper describes a novel application of mostly existing representations, features sets, and methods: namely, detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The nature of the problem, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. While missing some detail, the proposed solution and experiments sound reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

**Summary of the paper and main contributions**

The authors propose a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives using complex network features and word embeddings. The main contributions of this work are:

1. The application of complex network features to detect MCI in speech narratives, which is a novel approach in this domain.
2. The use of word embeddings to enrich the complex network features and improve their performance.
3. The evaluation of the proposed approach on three datasets, including the DementiaBank and two Portuguese datasets.

**Strengths of the paper**

1. The paper provides a thorough description of the problem, datasets, and domain, making it easy to understand the context and significance of the work.
2. The proposed approach is novel and has the potential to improve the detection of MCI in speech narratives.
3. The evaluation of the approach on three datasets provides a comprehensive assessment of its performance.

**Weaknesses of the paper**

1. The paper needs considerable editing to improve readability. Some details on key concepts are missing, and some sentences are unclear or awkwardly phrased.
2. The related work section is described superficially, and more details on the methods used in previous studies and how they compare to this study would be helpful.
3. The paper lacks statistical significance tests to substantiate the conclusions from the experiments.
4. The use of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) as features in the context of MCI is not clear, and it is not obvious why they were removed from the dataset.

**Questions to authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the multi-view learning scheme used in the experiments?
2. How did you generate the word embeddings, and what dataset was used for this purpose?
3. Can you provide more information on the linguistic features used in the experiments and how they were extracted?
4. Why did you choose to use term frequency instead of tf-idf weighting scheme for the BoW classifications?
5. Can you provide statistical significance tests to substantiate the conclusions from the experiments?
