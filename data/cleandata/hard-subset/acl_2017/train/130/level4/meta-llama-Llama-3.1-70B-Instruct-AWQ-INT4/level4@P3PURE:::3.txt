This paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The authors thoroughly describe the problem, datasets, and domain, making the study interesting and informative. However, the proposed solution and experiments, while reasonable, lack some essential details.

One of the major drawbacks of the paper is that it requires considerable editing to improve readability. Key concepts, such as the multi-view learning approach, are not adequately explained. Furthermore, the set of "linguistic features" needs clarification, and it is unclear which datasets were used to generate the word embeddings. The authors' decision to remove disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) from the dataset is also not justified, as these features might be crucial in the context of MCI. Additionally, the authors do not provide a clear explanation for not using the popular tf-idf weighting scheme for the BoW classifications. Statistical significance tests are also missing to substantiate the conclusions drawn from the experiments. Lastly, the related work section is described superficially and lacks specific details about the methods used in previous studies.
