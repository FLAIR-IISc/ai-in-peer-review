- Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts using three distinct datasets.
- Proposes a graph-based method leveraging word co-occurrence and extracts lexical and syntactic graph features for encoding.
- Evaluates performance via 5-fold cross-validation across multiple classifiers, reporting variable results across datasets.
- Focuses on a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets.

- Manuscript readability is poor due to grammatical and spelling errors requiring thorough English revision.
- The classification problem and instance definition are unclear (transcript-level labeling), dataset descriptions are overly long and sometimes inconsistent (e.g., participant counts vs. table totals).
- Motivation for the graph enrichment is not explained; alternative node/edge constructions (e.g., vector-similarity edges) are not considered.
- No domain-specific biomedical tools or methods are applied despite the medical nature of the datasets.
- Class distributions are unclear, making accuracy potentially inappropriate; F1 score would be more suitable for binary classification.
- Results are reported with excessive precision for small datasets and lack statistical significance testing, so performance gains may not be meaningful.
