- Novel application of existing features/methods to detect MCI in speech narratives, with reasonable experiments.  
- Paper provides thorough problem, dataset, and domain descriptions but needs better readability and editing.  
- Multi-view learning approach is insufficiently described and needs clarification.  
- The set of "linguistic features" is unclear and requires detailed definition.  
- It is unclear which datasets were used to train word embeddings; the cited datasets may be too small.  
- Decision to remove disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unjustified and may remove important MCI indicators.  
- Authors do not justify omission of tf-idf weighting for BoW; term-frequency choice needs explanation.  
- No statistical significance tests are reported to support experimental conclusions.  
- Related work section is superficial, lacks depth, and misses comparisons to prior methods.  
- Missing citations and undefined acronym "PLN."  
- Abstract is too long and should be shortened for conciseness.  
- Multiple sentences/phrases require rephrasing for clarity (specific line references provided).  
- Manual correction of grammatical errors in transcripts is unexplained and potentially problematic.  
- Ambiguity about which three datasets are referenced; dataset names should be explicit.  
- Unclear phrasing about prevalence ("15% a year") and other statistical statements needs clarification.  
- BoW limitations with disfluencies should be explained (lines 115–117).  
- Many sections (e.g., lines 323–332, 641–645) need rewriting for clarity and explicit dataset naming.  
- Table 1 lacks additional dataset statistics beyond basic demographics.  
- Transcription norms, DementiaBank, and task materials (Picnic picture, WML subtest) need citations/links.  
- Section 4.2.1 and 4.2.2 need expansion: clarify which dataset produced embeddings and elaborate methods; check supplementary material rules.  
- Feature definitions need clarification (e.g., "shortest path" feature 6 and "threshold parameter" for embeddings).  
- Editorial issues: grammar, missing periods, phrasing like "consisted of" vs "consist of," and conference citation guideline adherence.
