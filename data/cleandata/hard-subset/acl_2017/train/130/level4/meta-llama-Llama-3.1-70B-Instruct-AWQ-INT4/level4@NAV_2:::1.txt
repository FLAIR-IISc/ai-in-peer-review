**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their model on three datasets and demonstrate that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Application of complex networks to detect MCI**: The authors propose a new approach to detecting MCI using complex networks, which is a lightweight and language-independent representation.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors enrich complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments.
3. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their model on three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets.

**Strengths**

1. **Encouraging results**: The authors demonstrate that their approach can distinguish between healthy control participants and patients with MCI, with accuracy ranging from 60% to 85%.
2. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a new approach to detecting MCI using complex networks and word embeddings, which is a departure from traditional linguistic features.
3. **Language independence**: The authors' approach is language-independent, making it applicable to multiple languages.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Detailed introduction and review**: The paper starts with a detailed introduction and review of relevant work, which can be shortened to focus on the paper's topic.
2. **Lack of technological details**: The paper lacks technological details, such as examples of short transcripts translated to English and sample networks with embeddings.
3. **Unclear source of word embeddings**: The authors do not clearly explain the source of word embeddings, whether they are learned from the datasets or external corpora.
4. **Unclear benefit of combining networks**: The authors do not clearly explain the benefit of combining two kinds of networks (word-level and sub-word level) to generate word embeddings.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more technological details, such as examples of short transcripts translated to English and sample networks with embeddings?
2. Can you clarify the source of word embeddings and how they are learned from the datasets or external corpora?
3. Can you explain the benefit of combining two kinds of networks (word-level and sub-word level) to generate word embeddings?
4. Can you provide more explanations about the experiments and the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms?
