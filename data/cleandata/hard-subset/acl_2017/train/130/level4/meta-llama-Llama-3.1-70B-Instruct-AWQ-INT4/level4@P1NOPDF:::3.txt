The paper presents a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The authors thoroughly describe the problem, datasets, and domain, making the study interesting and informative. However, the paper requires considerable editing to improve readability and address several missing details.

One major drawback is the lack of clarity on key concepts, such as the multi-view learning approach used, the set of "linguistic features," and the datasets employed to generate word embeddings. The removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) from the dataset is also not justified, as these might be important features in the context of MCI. Furthermore, the authors do not provide tests for significance to substantiate their conclusions from the experiments. The related work section is also described superficially, lacking detailed explanations of the methods used in previous studies and how they compare to the current study.
