- Proposes using complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts.
- Claims improved representation of short texts by enriching complex networks with word embeddings.
- Evaluates the method on three datasets: two Portuguese (Cinderella, Arizona-Battery) and one English (DementiaBank).

- Innovative NLP-based approach for MCI detection and promising discrimination between controls and MCI patients.
- Valuable focus on Portuguese datasets, contributing to low-resource language NLP.

- Introduction and literature review are overly long and include some unrelated references; should be compressed and focused.
- Lacks technological details and illustrative examples (e.g., translated short transcripts, sample networks with embeddings).
- Unclear source of continuous word representations used to generate word embeddings (ABCD/Cinderella vs. external corpora).
- Unclear training source and comparative benefit of sub-word (n-gram) networks versus word-level networks.
- Contains minor errors and formatting issues (e.g., repeated words, table formatting).

- Request clarification on the source of continuous word representations used for embeddings.
- Ask for details on training data and validation that sub-word (n-gram) networks improve accuracy.
- Request inclusion of translated transcript examples and sample networks with embeddings for clarity.
- Ask how out-of-vocabulary words are handled and what their sources are.

- Paper needs revision: compress intro/lit review, add technical details and examples, clarify embedding and n-gram sources, and proofread to fix minor errors.
