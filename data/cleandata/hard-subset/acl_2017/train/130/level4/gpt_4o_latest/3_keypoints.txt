- Paper presents an interesting application of existing representations and methods for detecting MCI in speech narratives.  
- Problem, datasets, and domain are well described, and the approach is reasonable and engaging.  
- Significant editing required to improve overall readability and clarity.  
- Key concepts are insufficiently detailed (e.g., multi-view learning not explained).  
- The set of “linguistic features” is unclear and needs expansion and clarification.  
- Datasets used to generate word embeddings are not explicitly identified and seem too small for that purpose.  
- Decision to exclude disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unexplained and likely unjustified for MCI detection.  
- Omission of tf-idf weighting for BoW classifiers is unexplained and should be justified or evaluated.  
- No statistical significance tests reported to support experimental conclusions.  
- Related work section is superficial and needs expansion with comparisons to prior methods.  
- Abstract should be more concise and several sentences require rephrasing for clarity.  
- Clarify whether grammatical errors in transcripts were manually corrected and give disfluency examples.  
- Specify explicitly the three datasets used (e.g., DementiaBank, Cinderella, etc.).  
- Clarify ambiguous phrases (e.g., “15% a year,” “the best scenario,” and several line-specific rephrasing requests).  
- Provide citations/links for datasets and stimuli (DementiaBank, Picnic picture, transcription norms).  
- Explain what the WML subtest entails and add missing citations throughout.  
- Define acronyms such as “PLN” and “ABCD,” and avoid referring to datasets as “first/second/third.”  
- Clarify segmentation procedure and why some datasets were manually segmented while others were not.  
- Table 1 caption should include additional dataset statistics as suggested.  
- Justify removal of filled pauses, false starts, and repetitions—these may be critical features.  
- State which dataset(s) were used to create word embeddings (MCI datasets vs. external corpora).  
- Fix numerous wording/grammar issues (e.g., tense, punctuation, and unclear sentences listed by line).  
- Clarify technical details: shortest path feature reference, threshold parameter meaning (e.g., cosine distance), and preprocessing steps such as lemmatization.  
- Expand Section 4.2.2 on Linguistic Features and consider supplementary material per conference guidelines.  
- Describe the multi-view learning scheme used in Section 4.3 in detail and include statistical tests for classifier comparisons.
