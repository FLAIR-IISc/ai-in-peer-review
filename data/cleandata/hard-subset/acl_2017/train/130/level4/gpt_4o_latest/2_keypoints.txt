- Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets using a graph-based co-occurrence approach.  
- Extracts features from various graph properties (lexical, syntactic, etc.) and evaluates with 5-fold cross-validation across multiple classifiers.  
- Tackles a well-defined problem and employs appropriate datasets.

- Manuscript readability is poor due to weak English, requiring thorough grammar and spelling revision.  
- The machine learning problem is poorly specified (unclear what constitutes an instance); dataset tables focus on study descriptions and contain inconsistent statistics (e.g., participant counts).  
- Motivation for graph enrichment is unclear—no explanation why words aren’t represented as nodes connected by vector similarity instead of co-occurrence.  
- No use of domain-specific biomedical tools or techniques despite biomedical datasets.  
- Class distribution is unclear, making accuracy a potentially inappropriate metric; F1-score would be more suitable for binary classification.  
- Results report excessive precision for small datasets and lack statistical significance tests, calling the reliability of gains into question.
