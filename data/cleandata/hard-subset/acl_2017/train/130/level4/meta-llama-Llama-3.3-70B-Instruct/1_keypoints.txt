- Applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (ICD-10 F06.7).  
- Authors claim they can differentiate healthy vs MCI participants, but reported accuracy (60–85%) makes that conclusion uncertain.  
- Dataset is small (40 subjects: 20 healthy, 20 control) and limited to Portuguese, reducing accessibility and generalizability.  
- Recommend adding an English-translated short transcript example and a sample network/embeddings to improve clarity and accessibility.  
- Introduction and literature review are overly long and include some irrelevant references; recommend condensing the first five pages and focusing on technical contributions.  
- Unclear how word embeddings were learned from short Portuguese narratives—source/training data for continuous word representations is not specified.  
- Unclear how sub-word (n-gram) networks were trained, what “out-of-vocabulary” words refer to, and whether combining word- and sub-word networks improves accuracy.  
- Study’s focus on NLP for Portuguese is valuable given limited resources, but embedding computation for low-resource languages needs more explanation.  
- Manuscript needs revision: shorten sections 1–3, compress section 4.1, add more experimental details, and clarify transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).  
- Several technical/typo issues to fix: line 029 phrasing, line 188 “PLN” → “NLP”, line 264 repeated words, Table 3 number formatting, and duplicated/incorrect DOIs/links in the references.
