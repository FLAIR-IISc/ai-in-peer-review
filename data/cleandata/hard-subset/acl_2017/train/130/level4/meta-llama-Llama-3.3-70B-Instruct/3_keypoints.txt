- Paper presents a novel application of existing representations/methods for MCI detection and the problem, datasets, and domain are well described.  
- Study is interesting and informative but requires significant editing to improve readability and clarity.  
- Multi-view learning approach is not explained; details of the scheme are entirely omitted (Section 4.3).  
- The set of "linguistic features" (Section 4.2.2) is insufficiently described and needs substantial expansion.  
- It is unclear whether word embeddings were trained on the MCI datasets or external corpora; dataset for embeddings is not specified.  
- The removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is not justified and may remove diagnostic signals relevant to MCI.  
- The omission of tf-idf weighting for BoW is not justified; choice of term-frequency only needs explanation.  
- No statistical significance tests are reported to support claims about experimental differences.  
- The related work section is superficial and should include more detailed method descriptions and comparisons to prior studies.  
- Numerous clarity, phrasing, and grammar issues throughout the manuscript (many specific line suggestions listed).  
- Several missing or unclear definitions/abbreviations (PLN, ABCD) and missing citations/links (DementiaBank, Picnic picture, WML subtest, transcription norms).  
- Table 1 lacks the additional dataset statistics promised in the caption.  
- Preprocessing steps need clarification (e.g., lemmatization not necessarily final, rationale for handling disfluencies, BoW problems with disfluencies).  
- Specific feature descriptions are unclear (definition of “shortest path” in feature 6; meaning of “threshold parameter” for embedding cosine distance).  
- Manual correction of grammatical errors in transcripts is unexplained despite possible diagnostic relevance.  
- Follow conference citation and formatting guidelines and consider using Supplementary Material for expanded method details.
