The paper presents a novel application of established representations, feature sets, and methods to the task of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are described comprehensively. While some details are lacking, the proposed approach and experimental setup appear sound. Overall, I found the study to be interesting and informative.

However, the paper requires substantial editing to enhance readability. Several key concepts lack sufficient detail. For instance, the specifics of the multi-view learning technique employed are not explained; the set of “linguistic features” requires further clarification; and the datasets used to generate the word embeddings are not clearly identified (though the paper’s three datasets appear potentially limited in size for this purpose). The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) from the dataset is also unclear, as these could potentially serve as important indicators of MCI. Furthermore, the justification for not utilizing the commonly employed tf-idf weighting scheme for the BoW classifications is missing. Importantly, the conclusions drawn from the experiments are not supported by tests for statistical significance. Finally, the related work section feels somewhat superficial in its coverage.
