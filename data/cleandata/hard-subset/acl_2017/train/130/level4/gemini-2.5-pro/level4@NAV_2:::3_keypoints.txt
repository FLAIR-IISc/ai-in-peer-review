- Method: Represents transcripts as word co-occurrence networks augmented with semantic edges from word embeddings, then extracts topological features to classify MCI across three datasets (English and two Brazilian Portuguese).

- Contribution 1: Provides a comprehensive empirical comparison of complex network features against linguistic and BoW baselines on varied datasets, including a less-resourced language.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that enriching word adjacency networks with embedding-based semantic edges is a viable strategy for modeling short, disfluent clinical transcripts.

- Strength: Tackles an important clinical problem—automated, non-invasive early detection of MCI from speech—with potential societal impact.

- Strength: Offers thorough dataset and domain descriptions (participant demographics and elicitation methods), aiding experimental transparency.

- Strength: Systematically compares feature sets and explores ensemble/multi-view learning, showing performance improvements when combining features.

- Weakness: Methodological details are insufficiently described (e.g., corpus for training embeddings, unclear multi-view implementation, linguistic feature list in supplementary material only).

- Weakness: Data preprocessing choices are poorly justified—removal of disfluencies (potentially informative for MCI) and use of TF instead of TF-IDF are not explained.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing is reported, so reported performance gains may not be meaningful.

- Weakness: Related work discussion is superficial and lacks deep comparative analysis with prior methods.

- Question: What corpus and size were used to train the word embeddings, given the small experimental datasets?

- Question: Why were speech disfluencies removed, and were experiments including them conducted?

- Question: How exactly was multi-view learning implemented and how were classifier outputs combined?

- Question: Why was TF chosen for BoW instead of the more common TF-IDF weighting?

- Question: Have statistical significance tests been performed to validate the reported performance gains?
