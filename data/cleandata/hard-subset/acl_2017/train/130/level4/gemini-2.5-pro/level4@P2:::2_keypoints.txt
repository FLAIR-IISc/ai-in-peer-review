- Proposes representing transcripts as co-occurrence graphs enriched with word-embedding similarity edges to detect MCI from speech.
- Extracts topological graph features (PageRank, betweenness, clustering, etc.) and uses them with ML classifiers for MCI vs. control classification.
- Evaluates method on three datasets (one English, two Portuguese) and compares against linguistic features and Bag-of-Words baselines.
- Demonstrates gains from combining feature sets via multi-view learning and classifier ensembles.
- Strength: Tackles an important clinical problem and motivates graph-based features as robust to disfluency and agrammatism.
- Strength: Thorough comparative experimental design across multiple classifiers, baselines, and languages.
- Strength: Explores advanced techniques (ensembles, multi-view) and reports substantive improvements (e.g., ~10% on Cinderella).
- Weakness: Experimental setup is unclear—unit of classification and exact number of transcripts per class are not specified.
- Weakness: Choice of accuracy as the main metric may be inappropriate if class distributions are imbalanced; other metrics (F1, precision, recall) are needed.
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing is reported to support claimed performance differences, especially on small datasets.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for hybrid network construction (co-occurrence + embedding edges); lacks ablation studies and discussion of threshold selection.
- Weakness: Paper readability suffers from grammatical errors and awkward phrasing, requiring proofreading and editing.
- Q: Please clarify exact transcript counts per class for each dataset and provide F1, precision, and recall for main results.
- Q: Have statistical tests (e.g., paired tests or bootstrapping) been performed to assess significance of reported gains?
- Q: Provide intuition or empirical ablation comparing co-occurrence-only, embedding-only, and enriched graphs, and analyze sensitivity to the cosine similarity threshold.
