- Novelty is limited: applies largely existing representations and methods to MCI-from-speech, though the study is interesting.

- Problem domain and datasets are described thoroughly overall, but several key details are missing.

- Manuscript requires significant editing to improve clarity, readability, grammar, and phrasing.

- The specific multi-view learning approach is not described, preventing replication and understanding.

- The set of "linguistic features" is not clearly defined or detailed for reproducibility.

- It is unclear which corpora were used to train the word embeddings; the paper’s own datasets seem too small for that.

- The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is not provided despite their potential diagnostic importance.

- No justification is given for using term frequency instead of the more standard tf-idf weighting for BoW models.

- Experimental conclusions are reported without statistical significance tests to validate differences between models.

- The related work section is superficial, missing detailed descriptions of prior methods and explicit comparisons.

- The abstract is overly long and should be condensed.

- Multiple grammatical errors and unclear sentences (with examples) need revision.

- The meaning and motivation for "manually correcting grammatical errors in transcripts" are unclear and should be explained with examples.

- Several phrases and acronyms are ambiguous or undefined (e.g., "PLN", "ABCD") and should be clarified.

- The three datasets referenced are not explicitly identified by name; dataset references should be made explicit.

- Ambiguous statements (e.g., "15% a year", "the best scenario") and unclear sentence constructions need clarification.

- Missing citations/links for DementiaBank, the Picnic picture/WAB, the WML subtest, and transcription norms should be provided.

- The dataset description is confusing about segmentation (what "manually segmented" means) and inconsistently counts datasets.

- Table 1 caption promises dataset statistics but only provides demographic information — update caption or table content.

- Pre-processing description is incomplete/inaccurate (e.g., lemmatization not necessarily final step; other normalization steps omitted).

- The decision to remove disfluencies (lines 375–388) lacks justification given their potential as MCI indicators.

- The phrase "multidisciplinary team with psychiatrists" should be reworded for clarity.

- The claim that Bag-of-Words models have problems with disfluencies requires explanation.

- Section 4.2.1 should state whether embeddings were trained on the MCI datasets or larger external corpora.

- Section 4.2.2 must be significantly expanded to fully describe the linguistic features; consider using supplementary material.

- Clarify what "the shortest path" refers to (feature 6?) and what "the threshold parameter" denotes (e.g., cosine distance threshold).

- Section 4.3 omits critical details about the multi-view learning scheme used, preventing replication.

- Overall, the paper lacks statistical significance testing to substantiate experimental claims.
