This paper describes a novel application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The problem domain and datasets are thoroughly described, and the proposed solution and experiments are generally reasonable, making the study interesting and informative.

However, the paper suffers from several significant issues that detract from its contribution and would require major revisions to address. The primary weaknesses are a lack of clarity and missing methodological details.

Specifically:
*   **Clarity and Completeness:** The manuscript requires significant editing for clarity and readability. Key methodological details are either missing or insufficiently explained. For instance, the multi-view learning approach is not detailed, the set of "linguistic features" is not clearly defined, and the source of the word embeddings is ambiguous. If the embeddings were trained only on the three small datasets described, this would be a significant limitation that should be acknowledged.
*   **Methodological Justification:** The paper contains several questionable methodological decisions that are not justified. The removal of disfluencies (e.g., filled pauses, false starts, repetitions), which are potentially crucial indicators of MCI, is not explained. Similarly, the choice of term frequency (TF) over the more standard TF-IDF for the Bag-of-Words model is not defended.
*   **Experimental Rigor:** The experimental conclusions are not substantiated with statistical significance tests, making it difficult to assess the validity of the claims regarding model performance.
*   **Related Work:** The related work section is presented too superficially and lacks the necessary detail to properly contextualize the paper's contributions.

Detailed comments are provided below.

**Abstract and Introduction**
*   The abstract is overly long and should be condensed.
*   Lines 22-23: The phrase "agrammatical speech impacting in parsing results" should be rephrased for grammatical correctness (e.g., "impacting the parsing results").
*   Lines 24-25: The motivation for correcting grammatical errors in transcripts is unclear initially. A reader might assume that such errors are valuable indicators of MCI. The rationale only becomes clear later in the paper; this point should be clarified much earlier, perhaps with examples of the disfluencies being addressed.
*   Lines 29-30: Rephrase for grammar: "as it is a lightweight and language-independent representation."
*   Lines 34-38: The description of the three datasets is confusing. Consider rephrasing for clarity (e.g., "...and two others: the Cinderella story and...").
*   Line 70: The phrase "15% a year" is ambiguous. Please clarify what this rate refers to.
*   Line 73: This sentence needs rephrasing for clarity.
*   Lines 115-117: The claim that Bag-of-Words models have problems with disfluencies is not obvious and requires an explanation.
*   Lines 147-149: The meaning of "the best scenario" is unclear.

**Related Work**
*   Lines 166-182: This section, which should form the core of the related work, is too superficial. It would be more informative to detail the specific methods used in prior work and to explicitly compare them to the approach proposed in this study.
*   Line 157: A citation or link for DementiaBank is needed.
*   Line 162: A citation or link for the "Picnic picture of the Western Aphasia Battery" should be provided.
*   Line 170: The "WML subtest" should be defined.
*   Line 172: This line is missing citations.
*   Line 185: Please ensure citation formatting follows conference guidelines (e.g., "Aluisio et al. (2016) used...").
*   Line 188: The acronym "PLN" is used without prior definition.

**Data and Preprocessing**
*   Lines 233-235: The statement that lemmatization is a final step in preprocessing is inaccurate. This section should also acknowledge other common normalization and preprocessing steps that were omitted.
*   Lines 323-332 & Table 1: The description of the datasets is confusing. The term "segmented" is not defined (e.g., segmented into sentences?). It is also not explained why some datasets were manually segmented while others were not. The acronym "ABCD" is undefined. The text refers to three datasets but itemizes them in a way that is hard to follow. It would be clearer to name the datasets explicitly rather than using "first," "second," etc. Furthermore, the caption for Table 1 promises "additional statistics" that are not present.
*   Lines 375-388: A key concern is the removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions). No justification is provided for this step. These features are widely considered to be important indicators of cognitive impairment, and their removal may have negatively impacted the model's performance.
*   Lines 340-440: The transcription norms should be described or cited.
*   Line 399: "multidisciplinary team with psychiatrists" should be "multidisciplinary team consisting of psychiatrists."

**Methods, Features, and Experiments**
*   Section 4.2.1: The source of the word embeddings is a critical missing detail. It is unclear whether they were trained on the small MCI datasets (which would be inappropriate due to data sparsity) or on a larger, external corpus.
*   Section 4.2.2: The "Linguistic Features" section requires significant expansion. The current description is too brief to be understood or replicated. The authors should check conference guidelines regarding the use of supplementary material for providing these details if space is a concern.
*   Section 4.3: The paper completely omits details about the specific multi-view learning scheme used. This is a major omission that prevents reproducibility.
*   A general and critical weakness across the results section is the lack of statistical significance tests. Without these, the paper's conclusions about the relative performance of different models are unsubstantiated.
*   Line 560: Please clarify if "the shortest path" refers to feature 6 as defined earlier.
*   Line 620: The choice of term frequency (TF) over the more common and often more effective TF-IDF weighting scheme for the Bag-of-Words models is not justified. Also, a comma is needed after "In this work".
*   Lines 641-645: This sentence is convoluted and needs to be rewritten for clarity.
*   Line 662: Please define what "the threshold parameter" refers to (e.g., the cosine distance threshold for word embeddings?).
*   Line 735: This sentence is missing a period.
