- Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets using a graph-based approach.  
- Proposes building graphs from word co-occurrence and deriving lexical/syntactic graph features for classification.  
- Evaluates models with 5-fold cross-validation across several classifiers and reports variable performance across datasets.  
- Tackles an important, well-defined clinical problem and uses appropriate datasets.

- Writing and clarity are poor with many grammatical errors; needs thorough proofreading/editing.  
- Problem formulation is unclear (what constitutes an instance), transcript-level dataset statistics are missing, and dataset description contains inconsistent participant counts.  
- Motivation for the specific graph construction/enrichment is weak and not compared to alternatives (e.g., embedding-based edges).  
- No use of domain-specific biomedical resources (ontologies, specialized parsers, biomedical embeddings) to improve features.  
- Class distributions are not reported; accuracy alone is insufficient—precision, recall, and F1 should be provided for potential class imbalance.  
- Results lack statistical significance testing and report implausible precision (too many decimal places) given very small datasets.
