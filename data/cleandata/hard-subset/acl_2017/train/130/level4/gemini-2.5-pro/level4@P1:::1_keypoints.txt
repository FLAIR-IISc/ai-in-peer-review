- Paper proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using word co-occurrence graphs enriched with word-embedding similarity.  
- Evaluated on three datasets (one English, two Portuguese) and compared to linguistic features and BoW baselines; enriched complex-network features are competitive and improved by multi-view learning.  
- Contribution: novel use of complex networks enriched with embeddings for MCI detection, avoiding brittle syntactic parsers on disfluent speech.  
- Contribution: empirical evaluation including two Brazilian Portuguese datasets, demonstrating applicability to lower-resource languages.  
- Strength: tackles an important clinical problem and motivates a robust representation for atypical, disfluent speech.  
- Strength: method is novel for this task and enrichment with embeddings addresses short-text limitations.  
- Strength: results are encouraging, with network features sometimes outperforming established linguistic baselines and benefiting from ensembles.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail about how Portuguese word embeddings were trained and what corpus was used, raising reproducibility concerns.  
- Weakness: paper organization spends too much space on generic background and irrelevant citations rather than technical details.  
- Weakness: lack of accessible Portuguese data presentation—no translated transcript sample or network visualization for non-Portuguese readers.  
- Question: clarify the corpus and training details used for Portuguese word embeddings (in-domain only or larger external corpus?).  
- Question: define what counts as an OOV word in the Bojanowski et al. (2016) setup (pretrained-vocab OOV vs. task-corpus OOV).  
- Question: provide a brief explanation of the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms used for Portuguese datasets.  
- Additional comment: manuscript needs proofreading and fixes (typos, incorrect acronyms, table formatting, bibliography errors).
