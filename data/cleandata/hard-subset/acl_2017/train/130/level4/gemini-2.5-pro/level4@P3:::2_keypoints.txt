- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts by modeling words as nodes in complex networks and enriching co-occurrence graphs with cosine-similarity edges from pre-trained embeddings.  
- Extracts topological features from enriched networks to train classifiers (SVM, k-NN, etc.).  
- Evaluated on three datasets (English Cookie Theft; Portuguese Cinderella and ABCD) and compared to Coh-Metrix linguistic features and bag-of-words.  
- Reports that enriched complex network features are competitive and often outperform baselines; multi-view feature combination further improves accuracy.

- Contribution: novel application of enriched complex networks (with semantic edges) for MCI detection as a lightweight, language-independent representation.  
- Contribution: comparative evaluation across three datasets and two languages, benchmarking against established feature sets.

- Strength: well-motivated, innovative graph-based approach that avoids brittle deep linguistic processing on disfluent patient speech.  
- Strength: addresses an important clinical problem—automated, scalable early MCI detection from language.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments (multiple datasets, baselines, classifiers, and multi-view/ensemble methods).

- Weakness: experimental setup lacks clarity—unclear what constitutes an instance and the per-class instance counts are not reported, causing confusion with reported dataset statistics.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported despite small datasets and small reported improvements.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for the specific enrichment strategy; no comparison to alternative graph constructions (e.g., pure similarity graphs) or ablation study.  
- Weakness: paper readability hampered by grammatical errors, typos, and awkward phrasing.

- Question: report exact number of transcripts per class for each dataset and provide imbalance-robust metrics (precision, recall, F1).  
- Question: provide statistical significance tests (e.g., t-test, bootstrap) to validate performance gains.  
- Question: justify the enrichment method and report experiments comparing alternative network constructions (e.g., similarity-only graphs) or ablations.
