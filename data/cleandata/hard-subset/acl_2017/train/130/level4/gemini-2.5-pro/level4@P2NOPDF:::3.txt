This paper presents an interesting application of established representations and methods to the important problem of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The authors provide a thorough description of the problem domain, the datasets, and the clinical context. While the proposed solution and experimental design are generally reasonable, the manuscript in its current form has several significant drawbacks that limit its contribution.

The primary weakness is a lack of clarity and missing details regarding key methodological choices. The paper would benefit from considerable editing to improve overall readability. Crucial information is either omitted or insufficiently explained, which hinders reproducibility and a full assessment of the work. Specifically, the multi-view learning implementation is not described, the composition of the "linguistic features" is vague, and the source data for training the word embeddings is not clearly identified. Furthermore, several design choices are not justified. The rationale for removing disfluencies—potentially powerful indicators of MCI—is absent, as is the reason for preferring term frequency over the more standard TF-IDF weighting for the Bag-of-Words models. The related work section is also somewhat superficial, and the experimental results are presented without tests for statistical significance, making it difficult to verify the authors' conclusions.

Despite these issues, the study is informative and addresses a valuable research question. If the authors can thoroughly address the points below, the paper could be a solid contribution to the field.

**Detailed Comments**

*   **Abstract:** The abstract is currently too long and contains details better suited for the main body of the paper. It should be condensed to focus on the core problem, method, and key findings.
    *   L22-23: The phrase "impacting in parsing results" is grammatically incorrect. Please revise to "impacting the parsing results."
    *   L24-25: The motivation for manually correcting grammatical errors in transcripts is unclear. Since such errors can be indicative of MCI, removing them seems counterintuitive. The rationale for this preprocessing step should be explicitly stated and justified, perhaps with examples.
    *   L29-30: Please rephrase for grammatical correctness (e.g., "as it is a lightweight...").
    *   L34-38: The description of the datasets is confusing. It would be clearer to explicitly name them, for instance: "...evaluated on one dataset of speech transcripts from MCI patients and two other datasets: Cinderella and..."

*   **Introduction & Related Work:**
    *   L70: "15% a year" is ambiguous. Please clarify if this means 15% of the remaining MCI patients convert each year, or if it is a flat rate based on the initial cohort.
    *   L73: This sentence requires rephrasing for clarity.
    *   L115-117: The claim that Bag-of-Words (BoW) representations struggle with disfluencies is not self-evident. Please provide a brief explanation to support this assertion.
    *   L147-149: The meaning of "the best scenario" is unclear. Please define this more precisely.
    *   L157, L162, L170: Please provide citations or links for "DementiaBank," the "Picnic picture of the Western Aphasia Battery," and the "WML subtest" to aid readers unfamiliar with these resources.
    *   L172: This line is missing citations.
    *   L166-182: This section on related work would be strengthened by providing more specific details. For instance, what precise methods did prior studies use, and how do their results and approaches compare to the current work? The current description is too general.
    *   L185: Please adhere to the conference's citation style guidelines (e.g., "Aluisio et al. (2016) used...").
    *   L188: The acronym "PLN" is used without being defined.

*   **Methodology & Datasets:**
    *   L233-235: The statement about lemmatization as a final step could be rephrased. It is not always the last step, and other common preprocessing techniques have been omitted from the description.
    *   L322: "consisted of" should be "consist of."
    *   L323-332: This paragraph needs to be rewritten for clarity. What does "manually segmented" mean in this context? Why was this done for only some datasets? The acronym "ABCD" is not defined. The use of an itemized list (i, ii) followed by references to three datasets is confusing. Please name each dataset explicitly.
    *   Table 1 Caption: The caption claims the table shows "additional statistics of the dataset," but only demographic information is provided. Please revise the caption or add the relevant statistics.
    *   L375-388: A key methodological choice—the removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions)—is not justified. These are widely considered to be important predictive features for cognitive impairment. The authors should provide a strong rationale for their exclusion or, preferably, include them in the feature set.
    *   L399: Please rephrase "...multidisciplinary team with psychiatrists..." to "...multidisciplinary team consisting of psychiatrists...".
    *   L340-440: A citation or link to the transcription norms used would be helpful.

*   **Features & Experiments:**
    *   Section 4.2.1 / L290-299: It is crucial to clarify whether the word embeddings were trained on the (small) MCI-related datasets or on a larger external corpus. If trained on the experimental data, their quality is likely to be poor.
    *   Section 4.2.2 "Linguistic Features": This section is underdeveloped and lacks the necessary detail for another researcher to replicate the work. The features need to be clearly defined and motivated. Please check if supplementary material can be used to provide a complete description.
    *   L560: Does "the shortest path" refer to the definition in feature 6? Please clarify.
    *   L620: Please add a comma: "In this work, term frequency was...". More importantly, please justify the choice of term frequency over the more common and often more effective TF-IDF weighting scheme.
    *   L641-645: This sentence is convoluted and should be rewritten for clarity.
    *   L662: Please specify what "the threshold parameter" refers to (e.g., the cosine distance threshold for word embeddings?).
    *   L735: The sentence is missing a period.
    *   Section 4.3 "Classification Algorithms": This section is missing crucial information. The specific multi-view learning approach used is not described, making the method irreproducible. Furthermore, the experimental results lack tests for statistical significance, which are necessary to validate the conclusions drawn from performance comparisons.
