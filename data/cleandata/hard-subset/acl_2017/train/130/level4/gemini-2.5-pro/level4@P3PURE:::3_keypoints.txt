- Paper applies known representations and methods to MCI detection from speech; problem and datasets are well described and study is interesting.
- Manuscript lacks clarity and requires substantial editing for readability.
- Key methodological details are missing or insufficiently explained (multi-view learning, linguistic feature set, source of word embeddings).
- If embeddings were trained only on the three small datasets, this is a major limitation that must be acknowledged.
- Removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unjustified despite their potential diagnostic value for MCI.
- Choice of term frequency (TF) over TF-IDF for Bag-of-Words is not defended.
- No statistical significance tests are reported, undermining the validity of experimental claims.
- Related work section is too superficial and needs more detailed comparisons to prior methods.
- Abstract is overly long and should be condensed.
- Several sentences and phrases require grammatical fixes and rephrasing for clarity (multiple line-specific examples given).
- Motivation for correcting grammatical errors in transcripts is unclear and should be clarified earlier with examples.
- Description of the three datasets is confusing; dataset names and segmentation procedures should be made explicit.
- Ambiguous phrasing about rates and statements (e.g., "15% a year", "the best scenario") need clarification.
- Statements about Bag-of-Words models having problems with disfluencies require explanation.
- Related-work citations and definitions are missing or incorrectly formatted (DementiaBank, Picnic picture, WML subtest, PLN, etc.).
- Preprocessing description is incomplete and misleading (lemmatization not a final step; omitted normalization steps).
- Dataset segmentation and acronyms (e.g., ABCD) are undefined; Table 1 caption promises missing statistics.
- Transcription norms are not described or cited.
- Multidisciplinary team phrasing needs correction ("consisting of psychiatrists").
- Source and training data for word embeddings must be specified (critical reproducibility detail).
- "Linguistic Features" section is too brief to reproduce; consider supplementary material.
- Multi-view learning scheme is omitted entirely, preventing reproducibility.
- Several specific methodological parameters are unclear (e.g., "shortest path" reference, "threshold parameter" definition).
- Numerous line-specific typos, missing punctuation, and convoluted sentences throughout need fixing.
