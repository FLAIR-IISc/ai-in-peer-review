- Strength: Proposes a graph-based, word co-occurrence method to identify MCI from speech transcripts across three relevant datasets, extracting lexical/syntactic graph features and evaluating with standard classifiers using 5-fold CV.

- Clarity & presentation: Manuscript is hard to read with numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing; needs thorough proofreading.

- Problem & data description: ML task and instance definition are unclear; dataset statistics at the transcript level are missing, tables show patient cohorts not task-specific data, include irrelevant details, and contain inconsistent numbers.

- Methodological motivation: The choice of co-occurrence-based graph enrichment is not justified and alternatives (e.g., embedding-similarity graphs) are not considered.

- Use of domain knowledge: No biomedical/domain-specific tools or resources (ontologies, clinical NLP) are leveraged despite available domain relevance.

- Evaluation metrics: Relies on accuracy without reporting class distributions; F1-score or other metrics would be more appropriate for potentially imbalanced binary classification.

- Statistical significance: Reports overly precise results for very small datasets and provides no statistical significance testing to assess whether performance differences are meaningful.
