1. Paper tackles MCI detection from speech with an interesting application of NLP, but has major flaws.  
2. Methodological details are insufficiently described, hindering reproducibility.  
3. Multi-view learning approach is unspecified (algorithm, view definitions, integration).  
4. "Linguistic features" are poorly defined and need explicit descriptions or supplementary material.  
5. Word embedding training corpus is not stated; using small MCI datasets would be inadequate.  
6. Removing disfluencies is unjustified and likely discards important MCI indicators.  
7. Choice of TF weighting for BoW is unexplained versus the more common TF-IDF.  
8. Manual transcript correction is unclear and may remove diagnostic signals; needs clarification.  
9. No statistical significance testing is reported, so performance differences are not validated.  
10. Dataset descriptions are confusing (mentions two sources but three datasets; undefined acronyms like ABCD).  
11. Related work section is superficial and should better contrast prior methods with this work.  
12. Manuscript has readability issues: overly long abstract, awkward phrasing, and grammatical errors.  
13. Many methodological preprocessing steps (stopword removal, lemmatization details) are omitted or misstated.  
14. Table 1 caption and dataset statistics are incomplete (missing doc lengths, vocab size).  
15. Missing citations for key resources (DementiaBank, Picnic picture, WML subtest) and undefined acronyms (PLN).  
16. Reviewer recommends major revision: clarify methods, justify choices, add statistical rigor, and improve writing.
