- Summary: Proposes network-based NLP on Portuguese speech transcripts to detect MCI but lacks critical methodological details, especially about word embedding generation, and spends too much space on background.

- Strength: Addresses an important, socially relevant task of identifying MCI from patient narratives.

- Strength: Focuses on a less-resourced language (Portuguese), which is a valuable contribution.

- Strength: Engaging premise with mixed but encouraging results (accuracy reported 60–85%), though initial optimism is inconsistent with conclusions.

- Weakness: Methodological clarity missing—crucial details about how word and sub-word embeddings were generated and what corpus was used are not provided.

- Weakness: Unclear handling of out-of-vocabulary words due to unspecified embedding training data.

- Weakness: No ablation or evidence showing combined word-/sub-word network approach outperforms individual approaches.

- Weakness: Background and related work are overly verbose and unfocused; many general or irrelevant citations should be removed or condensed.

- Weakness: Recommend condensing Sections 1–4.1 to make room for deeper technical and experimental details.

- Weakness: Dataset accessibility—Portuguese transcripts are hard for non-speakers; include an English translation and a visualized sample network with embeddings.

- Minor: Briefly explain the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms.

- Minor: Fix typos and formatting—examples: line 29 ("as it a" → "as in a"), line 188 (PLN → NLP), line 264 repetition, Table 3 decimal separator, duplicate DOIs, and incorrect reference hyperlink.
