- Method: Represent each transcript as a word co-occurrence network enriched by adding edges between semantically similar non-adjacent words (via word embeddings) and use network topological features to train classifiers for MCI detection.

- Contribution: Novel application of complex networks enriched with word embeddings to MCI detection, aiming to be robust to agrammatical/disfluent clinical speech.

- Contribution: Empirical comparison across three datasets (English and Portuguese) against Coh-Metrix linguistic features and a Bag-of-Words baseline.

- Strength: Tackles a clinically relevant problem with multi-lingual evaluation across multiple datasets, supporting potential generalizability.

- Strength: Presents a novel, parser-free approach that may better handle disfluent clinical speech than syntax-based pipelines.

- Strength: Provides a thorough empirical comparison using multiple classifiers, ensemble/multi-view experiments, and strong baselines.

- Weakness: Experimental setup and data description lack clarity—unclear what constitutes a classification instance and dataset statistics (e.g., transcripts per class, class distribution) are missing or inconsistent.

- Weakness: Insufficient motivation and justification for the hybrid co-occurrence + embedding enrichment; no comparison to alternative graph constructions (e.g., purely semantic graphs) or ablation study.

- Weakness: Inappropriate evaluation metric—only accuracy reported with no class distribution; precision/recall/F1 per class are needed for binary classification.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing provided despite small datasets and reported small performance differences, making improvements uncertain.

- Weakness: Limited incorporation of domain-specific clinical/biomedical NLP knowledge or features that could improve MCI detection.
