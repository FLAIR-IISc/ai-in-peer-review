- Strength: Tackles a clinically relevant problem (identifying MCI from speech transcripts).  
- Strength: Evaluated across multiple datasets, improving assessment of generalizability.  
- Strength: Proposes an interesting graph-based word co-occurrence representation to capture linguistic features.

- Weakness: Experimental setup and problem formulation are unclear—instance definition, dataset statistics, and reported participant counts are confusing and inconsistent.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is inadequate—reliance on accuracy alone is misleading without class distribution and additional metrics (precision, recall, F1).  
- Weakness: Lacks statistical rigor—overly precise reporting and no significance testing given small datasets.  
- Weakness: Methodological choices are insufficiently justified—no rationale or comparison for using co-occurrence graphs versus alternatives (e.g., embedding-based similarity).  
- Weakness: Misses domain-specific opportunities—does not leverage medical ontologies or clinical NLP tools that could enrich features.  
- Weakness: Readability and presentation are poor—numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing requiring thorough proofreading.
