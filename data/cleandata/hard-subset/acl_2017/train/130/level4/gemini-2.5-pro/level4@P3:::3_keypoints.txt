- Summary: Models transcripts as word-cooccurrence networks enriched with word-embedding similarity to detect MCI across three datasets (English Cookie Theft, Portuguese Cinderella and ABCD), comparing network, linguistic, and BoW features and exploring ensemble/multi-view fusion.

- Contribution: Novel application of word-embedding–enriched complex networks for MCI detection, aiming to be robust to agrammatical/disfluent patient speech.

- Contribution: Cross-lingual empirical evaluation on English and Portuguese datasets, supporting language-independence claims and addressing less-resourced language data.

- Contribution: Shows that combining network, linguistic, and BoW features via multi-view/ensemble methods yields significant gains over single feature sets.

- Strength: Well-motivated, novel methodology that avoids heavy parsing and is resilient to short, disfluent transcripts.

- Strength: Tackles a practical problem by proposing a lightweight, potentially scalable representation suitable for clinical data.

- Strength: Extensive experimental evaluation across datasets, baselines, classifiers, and advanced fusion methods, with notable improvements (e.g., up to ~10% on Cinderella).

- Weakness: Lacks critical experimental details—unclear embedding training corpus, ambiguous multi-view implementation (claim vs. actual majority-vote use), and no justification for using TF instead of TF-IDF for BoW.

- Weakness: Removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is not justified and may discard important clinical markers; no ablation or analysis of this choice.

- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported, so reported accuracy improvements may not be meaningful.

- Weakness: Superficial analysis of results with limited explanation of why methods perform differently per dataset (e.g., SVM+BoW strong on ABCD).

- Question: Which corpus/model was used to obtain the word embeddings—pretrained vectors or trained on the small study datasets?

- Question: Why were disfluencies removed; were experiments run with them retained and what was the impact on performance?

- Question: Please clarify the multi-view learning implementation—did the reported multi-view results use only majority voting, or were learned-weight approaches used?
