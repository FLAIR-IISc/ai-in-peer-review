- Applies NLP to speech transcripts to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI).
- Claims notable discrimination between healthy and MCI subjects but conclusion downplays this (60–85% accuracy), creating a discrepancy.
- Primary dataset is very small (20 healthy, 20 control) and only in Portuguese, limiting generalizability and reproducibility.
- Paper should include an English transcript excerpt and sample visualizations (network and embeddings) to improve clarity.
- Introduction and related work are overly long; condense to focus on relevant background and free space for technical details.
- Remove irrelevant citations and examples (e.g., Salton (1989) citation, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) and shorten section 4.2.3.
- Embedding-generation methodology lacks crucial details given small data; source corpora for word embeddings and sub-word n-gram networks are unspecified.
- Unclear origin of "out-of-vocabulary" words and whether external Portuguese corpora were used.
- No empirical evidence presented that combining word-level and sub-word networks outperforms using either alone.
- Recommend shortening Sections 1–3 and 4.1 while expanding the experimental section with detailed embedding-generation procedures.
- Clarify transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2) for non-expert readers.
- Minor editorial issues: typo at line 29, PLN→NLP at line 188, duplicate/repeated transcription text at line 264.
- Formatting/reference errors: inconsistent decimal format in Table 3, duplicate DOI, and a mislinked reference.
