- Novel approach: uses network representations of word embeddings on Portuguese speech transcripts to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI), but methodology is unclear and structure imbalanced.  
- Important application: relevant real-world task of early cognitive decline detection via clinical NLP.  
- Encouraging results: reported accuracies (60–85%) suggest potential to distinguish MCI from controls, though task remains difficult.  
- New dataset: contribution of a Portuguese corpus for MCI detection, valuable for lower-resourced languages.  
- Unclear embedding training: paper fails to specify which corpus was used to train word/sub-word embeddings, raising reproducibility concerns given the small task dataset.  
- Confusing OOV handling: mention of out-of-vocabulary words is unclear without stating the vocabulary source.  
- No ablation analysis: authors need experiments showing the contribution of word-level vs. sub-word-level networks.  
- Imbalanced structure: excessive background and overly long intro/related work sections crowd out technical details.  
- Tangential citations: several references (e.g., general NLP or unrelated applications) are unnecessary and should be trimmed.  
- Limited accessibility of corpus: dataset only in Portuguese—include an English-translated transcript sample and visualization of network embeddings to improve comprehension.  
- Revision priorities: condense background, expand methodology (training corpus, architecture, technical justification), and clarify dataset/transcription norms (NURC/SP).  
- Minor technical errors: grammatical mistake ("as it a"), "PLN" should be "NLP", a copy-paste phrase ("out of cookie..."), decimal separator formatting in Table 3, duplicate DOI, and an incorrect reference hyperlink.
