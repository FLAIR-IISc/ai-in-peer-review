- Strength: Tackles MCI detection using linguistic transcripts from three established datasets.  
- Strength: Proposes a graph-based method that builds networks from word co-occurrence statistics.  
- Strength: Extracts a diverse feature set (lexical, syntactic, graph-based) for classification.  
- Strength: Evaluated with 5-fold cross-validation across standard classifiers; addresses an important clinical problem.  

- Weakness: Manuscript has many grammatical errors and needs thorough proofreading and copy-editing.  
- Weakness: Machine learning task formulation is unclear (what constitutes an instance), dataset statistics at the transcript/class level are missing, and there are inconsistencies in reported participant counts.  
- Weakness: No clear justification for the chosen co-occurrence graph construction or comparison to alternatives (e.g., semantic-similarity graphs).  
- Weakness: Fails to leverage domain-specific biomedical knowledge or tools that could improve feature representation.  
- Weakness: Class distribution (MCI vs control) is not reported; accuracy may be misleading on imbalanced data and F1 or other robust metrics should be reported.  
- Weakness: Results are reported with unjustified high precision and no statistical significance testing to assess whether differences are meaningful.
