- Proposes using word embedding–enriched complex networks to detect MCI from speech transcripts, aiming for robustness to disfluencies and agrammatical input; evaluated on three datasets (English + two Brazilian Portuguese) against linguistic and BoW baselines with ensemble/multi-view learning.  
- Contribution: applies embedding-enriched complex networks as a lightweight, parser-independent alternative for modeling patient speech.  
- Contribution: provides empirical evaluation on multiple datasets including Brazilian Portuguese, showing competitiveness and advantages when transcripts are not manually corrected.  
- Strength: novel, clinically relevant approach that creatively avoids brittle syntactic parsing for disfluent patient speech.  
- Strength: inclusion and evaluation on less-resourced Brazilian Portuguese datasets demonstrates language-agnostic potential.  
- Strength: results support claimed robustness of network features to agrammatical/disfluent transcripts, especially on automatically processed data.  
- Weakness: critical methodological details about how word embeddings were generated (training data, pre-trained vs. corpus size) are missing, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: some claims appear overstated relative to modest absolute accuracies, and BoW baseline performance (e.g., ABCD dataset) questions the added value of complex network features in some cases.  
- Weakness: paper organization and presentation are poor—intro/related work too long and sometimes irrelevant, methodology lacks depth (esp. embeddings), examples/translation missing, and references contain errors.  
- Question: Were Portuguese embeddings trained only on the dataset transcripts or on a larger/pre-trained corpus—if external, which one?  
- Question: What specific linguistic/MCI-related patterns do the CNE features capture beyond what BoW finds, given BoW's strong performance on ABCD?  
- Question: Please briefly explain the "NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms" referenced for the transcripts.
