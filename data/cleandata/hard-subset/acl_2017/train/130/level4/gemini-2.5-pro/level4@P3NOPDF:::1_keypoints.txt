- Proposes a graph-based NLP method using word and sub-word embeddings from Portuguese speech transcripts to classify MCI vs. healthy controls.  
- Tackles an important clinical problem; applying graph-based text representations to cognitive assessment is promising.  
- Reported classification accuracies are encouraging but not yet sufficient for clinical use.  
- Critical ambiguity about how word embeddings were created (trained on the 40-participant corpus or pre-trained on a larger Portuguese model).  
- If embeddings were trained solely on the 40 transcripts, severe data sparsity likely undermines embedding quality and results.  
- Unclear motivation and technical rationale for combining word-level and sub-word networks; no ablation study demonstrating benefit.  
- Source/training data for sub-word models and origin of out-of-vocabulary words are not specified.  
- Paper devotes excessive space to general NLP background and irrelevant examples, reducing focus on the core contribution.  
- Data accessibility is limited by Portuguese-only transcripts; inclusion of a translated excerpt would improve clarity for international readers.  
- Suggest clarifying embedding training corpus (name, size, domain) or discussing mitigation of small-corpus limitations.  
- Recommend condensing general background, refocusing literature review on cognitive/neurological assessment, and expanding methodological and dataset details.  
- Advise adding an English-translated sample narrative, a visualization of the generated graph, and explanations of NURC/SP transcription norms.  
- Lists specific technical comments/typos to fix (line 29 grammar, "PLN"→"NLP", transcription error, decimal separator, duplicate DOI, incorrect hyperlink).
