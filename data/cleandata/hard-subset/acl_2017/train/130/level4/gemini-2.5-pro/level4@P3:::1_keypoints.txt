- Proposes detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from speech transcripts by modeling transcripts as complex networks of word co-occurrence enriched with embedding-based semantic edges.  
- Extracts topological features from enriched networks and uses multiple classifiers and multi-view learning to distinguish MCI from controls.  
- Evaluated on three datasets (1 English, 2 Portuguese), showing enriched complex network features can outperform linguistic and bag-of-words baselines.  

- Main contribution: novel application of embedding-enriched complex networks to MCI detection, motivated by robustness to disfluent clinical speech.  
- Main contribution: cross-lingual evaluation including two new Brazilian Portuguese corpora, demonstrating applicability to low-resource languages.  

- Strength: well-motivated, novel approach addressing parser fragility on agrammatical/disfluent speech.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across datasets, tasks, baselines, classifiers, and ensemble/multi-view setups.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, notably state-of-the-art on Cinderella and ABCD Portuguese datasets (ABCD up to 80% accuracy).  

- Weakness: crucial details missing about word embedding training corpus and procedure, harming reproducibility and interpretation.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of which topological features or semantic links drive performance; lack of feature/error analysis.  
- Weakness: clarity and organization issues; background too long and space should be reallocated to methodological details and result analysis.  

- Question: clarify exact embedding training procedure and corpora for Portuguese and English.  
- Question: provide analysis/examples of semantic links added by embeddings and how they help distinguish MCI.  
- Question: explain why linguistic features outperform CNE on manually revised Cinderella but not on automatically processed text—does CNE mainly provide robustness to noisy text?  

- Additional: include an English translation of a Portuguese transcript sample in appendix for accessibility.  
- Additional: clarify NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms.
