### Strengths:

This paper presents a novel application of NLP to speech transcripts for the purpose of identifying patients with Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI, ICD-10 code F06.7). The authors initially present an optimistic claim of being able to distinguish between healthy controls and patients with MCI (lines 141-144). However, the conclusion offers a more tempered perspective, noting that accuracy rates between 60% and 85% indicate that the distinction is challenging (lines 781-785). This apparent contradiction between the introduction and conclusion, while notable, effectively frames the problem's difficulty and makes the reader curious about the methodology.

A potential limitation is the small, monolingual dataset, which consists of speech transcripts from 20 healthy and 20 control participants and is inaccessible to non-Portuguese speakers. To enhance the paper's technical depth and accessibility, the authors should consider incorporating more methodological details. Including an English translation of a sample transcript, and perhaps a visualization of a sample network with its corresponding embeddings, would be highly beneficial.

### Weaknesses:

The paper's introductory sections and literature review are overly detailed and could be significantly condensed. Several citations are either too general for a specialized audience (e.g., Salton 1989 in section 4.2.3) or not directly relevant to the paper's topic, such as those concerning "sentiment classification" and "pedestrian detection in images" (lines 652-654). The discussion in section 4.2.3 (lines 608-621) could also be shortened. The recommendation is to compress the first five pages, focusing the review strictly on the paper's core contributions and using the reclaimed space to elaborate on the technological innovations, including translated samples from the ABCD and/or Cindarela narratives.

Several key aspects of the methodology require further explanation:

*   The source of the word embeddings is unclear, particularly given the short length of the narratives in the Portuguese ABCD dataset. How were meaningful word similarities established from this limited data?
*   The paper should clarify whether the continuous word representations (lines 272-289) were trained solely on the small ABCD+Cinderella datasets or if an external, larger corpus was leveraged.
*   This question also applies to the sub-word level (n-gram) networks used to generate embeddings (lines 513-525); the training source needs to be specified.
*   The authors should provide evidence that combining word-level and sub-word level networks yields superior performance.
*   The origin and nature of the "out-of-vocabulary words" mentioned on line 516 need to be defined.

### General Discussion:

The paper addresses a significant and compelling research area: using NLP for the early detection of cognitive impairments. Furthermore, its focus on Portuguese is a valuable contribution, and it is important that the authors clearly explain their process for computing embeddings for a language with fewer resources compared to English.

The manuscript would be greatly improved by significant revision. Specifically, sections 1-3 should be shortened, section 4.1 condensed, and the experimental section expanded with greater detail. A brief explanation of the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription standards would also be beneficial for the reader.
