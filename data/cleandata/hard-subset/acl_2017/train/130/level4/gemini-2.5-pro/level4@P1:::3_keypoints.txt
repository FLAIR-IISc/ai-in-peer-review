- Proposes graph-based representation of speech transcripts enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI; extracts topological features and evaluates on three datasets (English + Portuguese).

- Novel application of complex networks to MCI detection, offering robustness to agrammatical/disfluent speech vs. parser-based NLP.

- Clear motivation and detailed dataset descriptions (sources, demographics, elicitation tasks).

- Evaluation across two languages and multiple narrative tasks supports robustness and language-independence of features.

- Word embeddings source is not specified; clinical datasets appear too small to train embeddings—need corpus details.

- Multi-view learning approach is insufficiently described; unclear how classifiers from different feature spaces were combined.

- Linguistic feature set is not clearly defined, preventing direct comparison with network features.

- Removing disfluencies from transcripts is unjustified and may discard important clinical markers; rationale or analysis is needed.

- Results lack statistical significance testing, so reported improvements may not be meaningful.

- Paper needs editing for clarity: overly long abstract, awkward sentences, and a superficial related-work discussion.

- Which corpus was used to train the word embeddings? Please cite if external.

- Please detail the multi-view learning implementation and how predictions were combined.

- Why were disfluencies removed from transcripts? Were they considered as predictive features?

- Why was TF chosen for Bag-of-Words instead of TF-IDF?

- Do you plan to run statistical significance tests to validate reported performance gains?
