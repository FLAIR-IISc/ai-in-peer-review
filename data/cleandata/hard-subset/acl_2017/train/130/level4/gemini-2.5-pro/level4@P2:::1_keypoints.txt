- Novel method: represent transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with semantic edges from word embeddings to detect MCI robustly despite agrammatical/disfluent speech.  
- Cross-lingual evaluation: validated on English and Brazilian Portuguese datasets, showing language-independent potential and contributions for Portuguese.  
- Feature combination: multi-view learning combining network, linguistic, and BoW features yields substantial performance gains (up to 80% accuracy on ABCD).  
- Well-motivated methodology: avoids fragile syntactic parsers and logically enriches sparse short texts with embedding-based semantics.  
- Thorough experimental setup: evaluated on three datasets with different tasks and compared against Coh-Metrix linguistic features and BoW baselines.  
- Promising results: CNE features often outperform linguistic features; multi-view fusion gives notable absolute accuracy improvements (e.g., +10% Cinderella, +4.71% ABCD).  
- Weakness — embedding details missing: paper omits what corpus was used to train the Bojanowski et al. (2016) embeddings, hurting reproducibility and interpretation.  
- Weakness — presentation balance: too much background material; novel methods (network enrichment, which metrics matter) need deeper explanation and analysis.  
- Weakness — no significance testing: small dataset sizes (e.g., 20 vs. 20) require statistical tests or confidence intervals to support claimed improvements.  
- Question — embedding corpora: please clarify which corpora were used to train embeddings for English and Portuguese (external large-scale, in-domain, or both).  
- Question — out-of-vocabulary words: specify whether OOVs come from domain-specific transcript terms absent from pre-trained embeddings.  
- Question — statistical validation: have you run significance tests to confirm CNE-based improvements over baselines?  
- Additional comments: paper needs final proofreading and fixes for minor typos, formatting (e.g., "PLN"→"NLP", numeric formatting), and possible duplicate DOI in references.
