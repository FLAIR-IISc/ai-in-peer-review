- Proposes an NLP-based method to identify Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts of narratives and descriptions.  
- Introduction claims clear distinction between MCI and controls, while conclusion concedes 60–85% accuracy makes discrimination difficult — contradictory messaging.  
- Small, Portuguese-only dataset (20 patients, 20 controls) limits generalizability and evaluation by non-speakers.  
- Paper needs more technical detail: at least one English-translated transcript example and a visualization of a sample network with embeddings.  
- Structure and focus are weak: introduction/related work overly detailed and should be condensed (compress first five pages).  
- Several citations are unnecessary or irrelevant (e.g., broad NLP refs, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) and could be removed.  
- Section 4.2.3 and parts of 4.1 should be shortened to make room for technical contributions.  
- Unclear how word embeddings were generated — source corpus not specified (small datasets vs. larger external Portuguese corpora).  
- Sub-word (n-gram) network generation is also unclear and needs description and justification.  
- No evidence shown that combining word- and sub-word-level networks improves accuracy.  
- “Out-of-vocabulary” term is ambiguous without stating the vocabulary source.  
- Commendable focus on Portuguese and low-resource language challenges, but requires a detailed account of how embeddings were computed for Portuguese.  
- Recommend expanding experimental section with technical explanations and clarifying NURC/SP transcription norms for reproducibility.  
- Multiple technical/typo issues and reference errors: grammar (Line 29), PLN→NLP (Line 188), repeated phrase (Line 264), decimal format in Table 3, duplicate DOI, and incorrect hyperlink in references.
