- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using word-cooccurrence complex networks enriched with word embeddings to handle short/agrammatical texts.
- Evaluates the approach across three datasets (English + two Portuguese) and compares network-derived features to linguistic features and BoW, plus ensemble/multi-view combinations.
- Addresses a clinically important problem and motivates the approach well as a non-invasive diagnostic avenue.
- Methodologically well-motivated: complex networks avoid brittle parsers on disfluent speech, and embeddings add semantic density for short transcripts.
- Thorough evaluation across multiple datasets, languages, elicitation tasks, and text lengths, strengthening claims of robustness.
- Methodological details are underspecified, harming reproducibility—key missing: how word embeddings were generated.
- Multi-view learning implementation is unspecified (e.g., majority vote vs. integrated technique), limiting interpretability of results.
- The set and selection of linguistic features are not clearly summarized in the main text, obscuring which features drove performance.
- Removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unjustified and may discard clinically relevant signals.
- BoW baseline uses TF only; no justification for omitting TF-IDF, which is a common and often stronger alternative.
- Experimental results lack statistical significance testing, so reported improvements may not be meaningful.
- Related work discussion is superficial and could better contrast prior features/methods with the complex network approach.
- Question: please clarify the corpus and training details used to obtain the word embeddings.
- Question: please justify removing disfluencies and report experiments or ablations retaining them as features.
- Question: please elaborate on the exact multi-view learning algorithm or ensemble strategy used.
- Question: please provide statistical significance tests to validate reported performance gains.
