- Addresses the important clinical task of identifying Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts across three datasets.  
- Proposes a graph-based method that leverages word co-occurrence information within transcripts.  
- Extracts a variety of graph-derived features, including lexical and syntactic properties.  
- Evaluates models with 5-fold cross-validation using several standard classifiers.  
- Uses appropriate, relevant datasets for the clinical task.  

- Manuscript has numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing, reducing clarity and readability.  
- Task and data description are unclear (uncertain what constitutes an instance, transcript-level stats missing, and conflicting dataset counts).  
- Insufficient motivation for the chosen graph construction and lack of comparison to alternative representations (e.g., semantic similarity-weighted word graphs).  
- Does not leverage domain-specific biomedical resources or clinical NLP tools that could capture relevant nuances.  
- Evaluation metrics are incomplete/possibly misleading (class distributions not reported; accuracy alone is insufficient—need precision, recall, F1).  
- Results lack statistical rigor (small datasets, overly precise reporting, no significance testing such as t-tests or bootstrap resampling).
