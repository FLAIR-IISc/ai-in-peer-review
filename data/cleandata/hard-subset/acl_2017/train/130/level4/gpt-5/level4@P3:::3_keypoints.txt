- Contributions: Demonstrates that embedding-enriched co-occurrence networks (CNE) can compete with or outperform linguistic and BoW features for MCI detection across noisy, short, cross-lingual transcripts.
- Contributions: Performs cross-dataset, cross-language evaluation on three clinically relevant corpora (including Portuguese datasets) and analyzes ensemble/multi-view combinations.
- Contributions: Proposes a practical, parser-light pipeline intended to be robust to disfluencies and resource-poor settings.

- Strength: Clear clinical motivation for early MCI detection and rationale for language-based, parser-light methods.
- Strength: Cross-language, multi-dataset evaluation (English + Brazilian Portuguese) strengthens claims of generality and fills a gap with Portuguese clinical data.
- Strength: Lightweight CNE representation offers an alternative to parser-dependent features and appears robust for short texts when using embeddings.
- Strength: Consistent comparison across classifiers and feature sets, plus exploration of ensembles/multi-view strategies with reported gains on some datasets.
- Strength: Practical insight that simple linear SVM with BoW can be a strong baseline in some settings.

- Weakness: Key methodological details are under-specified (multi-view scheme, how combinations were operationalized, threshold selection procedure).
- Weakness: Unclear embedding training/setup (which corpora, models, tokenization, OOV handling), risking reproducibility and domain/leakage concerns.
- Weakness: Preprocessing removes disfluencies (filled pauses, repetitions, short false starts) without justification or ablation, potentially discarding diagnostic signals.
- Weakness: Evaluation reports only accuracy; lacks additional metrics (precision/recall/F1/AUC), confidence intervals, significance tests, and confusion analyses.
- Weakness: Clarity and organization issues; some claims overgeneralize and contradict tables, and dataset descriptions/terminology are inconsistent.

- Q: Which corpora, sizes, domains, and models were used to train English and Portuguese embeddings, and how were tokenization and OOV/subword handling performed?
- Q: Precisely how was multi-view learning implemented — probability averaging, voting, learned weights or stacking — and how was cross-validation arranged to avoid leakage?
- Q: How were cosine similarity thresholds chosen (e.g., 0.7 vs 0.4) and were they tuned nested within cross-validation folds?
- Q: Why were disfluencies removed, and can you provide ablation results comparing with vs. without disfluencies?
- Q: Why use term frequency weighting for BoW instead of tf-idf, and can tf-idf results be reported given BoW’s strong performance?
- Q: Please report additional metrics (F1, sensitivity, specificity, AUC) and perform statistical tests (e.g., McNemar, paired bootstrap) to assess significance.
- Q: Provide exact dataset details: class counts, mean/median transcript lengths, segmentation method, demographics, and whether covariates were controlled.
- Q: Will code, feature extraction scripts, and trained models be released to ensure reproducibility of network construction and topological features?

- Minor/editorial: Improve grammar and clarity throughout and avoid ambiguous “first/second/third” references.
- Minor/editorial: Name and define the three datasets (include ABCD) early and provide standard dataset citations/links.
- Minor/editorial: Clarify network construction details (directed vs. undirected, weighted, co-occurrence window) and precise feature definitions.
- Minor/editorial: Align claims with reported tables and avoid overgeneralizations where results disagree with text.
- Minor/editorial: Consider adding an error analysis and ablation studies (CNE vs. CN; with vs. without embeddings; disfluency inclusion).

- Overall: Promising, practical application with encouraging results, but needs clearer methods, stronger evaluation (metrics and significance), and reproducibility details to be a solid contribution.
