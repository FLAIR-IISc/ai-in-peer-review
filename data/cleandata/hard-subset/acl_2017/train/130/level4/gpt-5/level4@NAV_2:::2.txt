- Strengths:
This paper investigates the detection of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts drawn from three datasets. It introduces a graph-based approach that exploits word co-occurrence and encodes a range of graph-derived characteristics alongside lexical and syntactic properties. Results are presented using 5-fold cross-validation across several classifiers, with performance varying by dataset. The work addresses a clear problem and employs appropriate datasets.

- Weaknesses:
1. The manuscript’s readability is hindered by extensive issues with English usage; a thorough edit for grammar and spelling would substantially improve clarity.
2. The core machine learning task is insufficiently specified. What constitutes a single classification instance? It appears each transcript is labeled as MCI vs. control; if so, dataset descriptions should provide counts at the transcript level. Tables 1–3 should summarize the data used for classification rather than the studies that produced them. Patient age is not directly pertinent to the classification setup. The dataset section is overly long (about two pages) and contains information that does not affect the experimental task. Additionally, some counts are inconsistent—for example, Section 4.1.1 mentions 326 participants, yet the male/female totals in Table 1 are under 100.
3. The motivation for enriching the co-occurrence graph is unclear. Why not construct a graph where each word is a node and edges are defined solely by embedding similarity, independent of co-occurrence?
4. Although the datasets are biomedical, no domain-specific resources or tools are utilized.
5. Without a clear statement of class distribution, it is hard to judge whether accuracy is an appropriate metric. For a binary task, F1 (e.g., macro- or per-class F1) would be a more informative measure.
6. Results are reported to four decimal places on very small datasets (e.g., 43 transcripts) without statistical significance testing, making it unclear whether observed gains are meaningful.
