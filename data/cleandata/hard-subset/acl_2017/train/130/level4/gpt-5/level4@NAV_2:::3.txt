This paper applies largely established representations, feature sets, and methods to the task of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The problem framing, datasets, and clinical context are described in depth. Although some details are missing, the proposed approach and experimental setup appear sound. Overall, I found the work interesting and informative.

On the negative side, the manuscript would benefit from substantial editing to improve clarity. Important methodological specifics are missing. For example, the multi-view learning setup is not described; the exact composition of the “linguistic features” is unclear; and the source and size of the corpora used to train word embeddings are not specified (if trained only on the three datasets in the paper, those seem too small for this purpose). It is also unclear why disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) were removed; these may be highly informative for MCI. Likewise, the decision not to use tf-idf for BoW features is not justified. No statistical significance testing is reported to support the experimental conclusions. Finally, the related work section is somewhat superficial. Detailed comments follow:
