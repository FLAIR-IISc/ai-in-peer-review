- Study: Uses Portuguese speech transcripts and word/subword network embeddings to classify mild cognitive impairment vs. controls, reporting 60–85% accuracy.  
- Relevance: Important, clinically motivated problem with valuable focus on a low-resource language.  
- Results: Encouraging preliminary accuracies suggesting detectable signal in the data.  
- Methodological interest: Raises useful questions about embeddings trained on small, domain-specific corpora.  
- Inconsistent messaging: Abstract claims successful discrimination but conclusion downplays separability—needs reconciliation.  
- Small dataset/accessibility: Likely only 20+20 participants; small size limits generalizability and Portuguese-only transcripts reduce accessibility.  
- Embedding details lacking: Unclear what corpora were used to train word embeddings and subword networks.  
- Subword modeling unclear: Construction, training source, hyperparameters, and tokenization for subword (n-gram) networks are unspecified.  
- OOV handling unclear: Definition of “out-of-vocabulary” and how such words were embedded are not described.  
- Overlong background: Sections contain broad or off-topic citations (e.g., sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) and should be focused.  
- Section 4.2.3 verbose: Specific subsection flagged for shortening and tighter focus.  
- Experimental clarity missing: Need explicit data splits, evaluation protocol, metrics beyond accuracy, and regularization/calibration steps.  
- Ablation and comparison needed: Provide results showing whether combined word+subword outperforms individual models and report statistical significance.  
- Participant/demographic details requested: Confirm counts, label distribution, and control for variables like age and education.  
- Suggestion—streamline related work: Compress general NLP background and focus on cognitive impairment detection and low-resource embeddings.  
- Suggestion—add examples: Include short transcript excerpts with English translations and a schematic/visualization of the networks/embeddings.
