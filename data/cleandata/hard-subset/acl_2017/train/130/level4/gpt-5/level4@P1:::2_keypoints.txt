- Paper models transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-based semantic links to detect MCI from speech across English and Portuguese datasets.  
- Main contributions: shows enriched network features can be competitive, demonstrates benefits of multi-view/ensemble learning, and proposes a language- and parser-lightweight representation.  

- Strength: Targets an important clinical task and proposes a representation intended to be robust to disfluencies and parser errors.  
- Strength: Comparative evaluation across feature types and two languages increases external validity.  
- Strength: Multi-view and ensemble methods yield practical performance gains on some datasets.  
- Strength: Enriched network features often compare favorably to linguistic baselines and prior work for short, noisy transcripts.  

- Weakness: Manuscript suffers from poor writing, grammatical errors, inconsistent terminology, and needs thorough editing.  
- Weakness: Unit of analysis and task formulation are unclear; transcript-level class counts and dataset splits are not clearly reported.  
- Weakness: Motivation for enriching co-occurrence networks with embedding edges is weak; alternative pure similarity graphs are not justified or compared.  
- Weakness: No use of domain-specific clinical features or lexicons, potentially limiting performance and practical impact.  
- Weakness: Evaluation relies on accuracy alone without reporting class-wise metrics (precision, recall, F1, AUC) given possible class imbalance.  
- Weakness: Statistical reporting is inadequate (overprecision, no significance tests or confidence intervals) despite small sample sizes.  

- Question: Clarify whether each transcript is a single labeled instance and provide exact transcript counts per class for each dataset and fold.  
- Question: Describe hyperparameter selection and confirm tuning was performed within training folds to avoid leakage.  
- Question: Justify the design choice of enriching co-occurrence networks instead of using pure similarity graphs and report any comparisons.  
- Question: Provide class distributions and report precision, recall, F1 (per class and macro) and ROC-AUC.  
- Question: Report statistical significance or confidence intervals for reported improvements (e.g., paired tests across CV folds).  
- Question: For Portuguese datasets, did you consider lightweight domain-informed features (discourse markers, error types, pause proxies) to complement network features?  

- Overall: Important and promising approach but requires clearer task/data description, stronger motivation for network design, improved evaluation rigor, and substantial language editing.
