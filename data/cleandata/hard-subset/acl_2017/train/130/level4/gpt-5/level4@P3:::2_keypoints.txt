- Paper studies MCI detection from speech transcripts using co-occurrence graphs enriched with word-embedding similarity edges, evaluated on three datasets (English, Portuguese).
- Main contribution: shows embedding-enriched adjacency networks can distinguish MCI from controls and sometimes beat linguistic metrics and BoW.
- Main contribution: proposes a parser-light hybrid graph (co-occurrence + similarity edges) to mitigate sparsity in short, noisy transcripts.
- Main contribution: compares single-view, multi-view, and ensemble strategies across feature spaces, reporting gains on two datasets.
- Strength: clinically relevant problem, multi-dataset and multilingual evaluation increases scope.
- Strength: language-independent, lightweight representation suitable for noisy/low-resource transcripts; uses subword embeddings to reduce OOV.
- Strength: broad empirical comparison of classifiers and feature sets, with notable gains (e.g., up to 10% on Cinderella, 80% on ABCD).
- Strength: movement toward automation via automatic sentence segmentation and filled-pause removal.
- Weakness: paper clarity and presentation are poor (grammatical errors, inconsistent terminology, missing key ML setup details).
- Weakness: task framing and dataset statistics unclear — unit of classification, per-dataset counts, class balance, and fold stratification are missing or inconsistent.
- Weakness: methodological motivation and ablations lacking — no comparison of co-occurrence-only, similarity-only, hybrid variants, weighted edges, or threshold sensitivity.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to accuracy, missing precision/recall/F1/ROC-AUC/confusion matrices, significance testing, and proper nested CV for hyperparameter tuning.
- Weakness: potential data leakage and reproducibility issues — embedding training corpora unspecified, possible inclusion of evaluation data, underspecified multi-view/ensemble setup, high feature dimensionality vs small sample sizes without feature selection.
- Requested clarification: specify exact unit of classification, per-dataset transcript counts per class, tokens per transcript after preprocessing, and fold stratification.
- Requested clarification: state embedding training corpora per language and OOV rates; confirm embeddings did not include evaluation transcripts.
- Requested clarification: justify hybrid graph choice vs pure similarity, provide ablation (co-occurrence/similarity/hybrid) and threshold sensitivity analysis.
- Requested clarification: report full evaluation metrics (precision/recall/F1/ROC-AUC/confusion matrices), use nested CV, provide confidence intervals and statistical tests.
- Requested clarification: detail preprocessing (stopword lists, per-transcript vs aggregated graphs), feature counts per view, scaling/selection, and ensemble voting/calibration and hyperparameter tuning.
- Requested clarification: provide error analysis, feature importance of CN metrics, and qualitative visualizations of MCI vs control graphs.
- Overall assessment: promising, language-agnostic approach with encouraging results but requires major revisions for clarity, methodological justification, stronger evaluations, and reproducibility.
