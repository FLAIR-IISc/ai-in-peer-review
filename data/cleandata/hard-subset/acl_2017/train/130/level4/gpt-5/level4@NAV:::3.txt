The paper presents a new application of largely existing representations, feature sets, and techniques: detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives. The problem setting, datasets, and domain context are well motivated and described. Although some specifics are missing, the proposed approach and experiments appear sound. Overall, I found the work interesting and informative.

Regarding limitations, the manuscript would benefit from substantial editing to improve clarity and readability. Some essential details are absent. For instance, the multi-view learning setup is not described; the precise set of “linguistic features” needs to be specified; it is unclear which corpora were used to train the word embeddings (presumably the three datasets described, which seem too small for that purpose). It is also unclear why disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) were removed from the data; one could argue these are salient indicators for MCI. It is likewise not clear why the standard tf-idf weighting was not used for the BoW experiments. In addition, no significance testing is reported to support the experimental conclusions.
