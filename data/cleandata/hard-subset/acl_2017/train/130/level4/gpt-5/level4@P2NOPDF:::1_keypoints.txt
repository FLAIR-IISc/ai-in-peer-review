- Studies NLP on Portuguese speech transcripts to detect mild cognitive impairment; reports group separation but acknowledges modest accuracies (60–85%) while calling results promising.
- Dataset is small (40 participants, 20+20) and Portuguese-only; paper should include more methodological detail and at least one short transcript translated into English with a sample derived network/embedding.
- Related work is too broad and contains tangential citations; condense the first five pages and tighten Section 4.2.3.
- Technological contribution needs expansion, with English examples from ABCD/Cinderella narratives to clarify methods.
- Unclear how word similarities and embeddings were trained given short narratives; specify whether embeddings used only ABCD+Cinderella or external corpora.
- Clarify use of subword (n-gram) networks, the training source for those embeddings, and provide empirical evidence that combining network types improves accuracy.
- Define “out-of-vocabulary words” clearly and state where they occur in the data.
- Small sample size and language restriction limit generalizability; provide stronger justification of modeling choices and validation procedures.
- Explain how embeddings were computed under resource limitations and discuss transferability to other languages and larger datasets.
- Revise manuscript structure: shorten Sections 1–3, compress 4.1, and add more detail on experimental setup, training data, and evaluation.
- Add a brief explanation of the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms for unfamiliar readers.
- Minor technical corrections: fix “as it a lightweight” (Line 029), PLN→NLP (Line 188), duplicate phrase at Line 264, Table 3 decimal format (72.0), and duplicated/incorrect DOI/link in the references.
