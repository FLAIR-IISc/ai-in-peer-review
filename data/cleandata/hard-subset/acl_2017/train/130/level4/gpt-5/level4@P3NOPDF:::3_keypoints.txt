- Strength: Clear clinical motivation, well-described datasets/problem context, and timely application to MCI detection from speech.
- Strength: Sensible use of multiple feature families and representations within a generally sound experimental framework.
- Strength: Study likely valuable to both NLP and clinical audiences once clarified.

- Major issue: Paper needs substantial editing for clarity; many passages are hard to follow and key terms undefined.
- Major issue: Missing methodological details—multi-view learning scheme is not described.
- Major issue: Linguistic features are under-specified and need full enumeration/definitions.
- Major issue: Source, size, and training details of word embeddings are unclear (dataset, algorithm, hyperparameters).
- Major issue: Preprocessing choice to remove disfluencies (filled pauses, repetitions, false starts) is unjustified and potentially discards diagnostic signals.
- Major issue: Bag-of-words baseline uses term frequency only (no tf-idf) without rationale; stronger baselines needed.
- Major issue: No statistical significance testing reported to support performance claims.
- Major issue: Related work is summarized superficially and does not clearly position this work relative to prior methods.

- Abstract: Needs to be shortened and sharpened to state problem, approach (feature groups/classifiers), datasets, and main results.
- Transcripts: Clarify whether transcripts were manually corrected and justify corrections vs. diagnostic value of errors; add disfluency examples.
- Dataset mentions: Enumerate the three datasets by name at first mention and provide references/links (e.g., DementiaBank, WAB picnic).
- Epidemiology line: Clarify “15% per year” (incidence vs progression) and provide citation.

- Related work section: Expand to list datasets, features, representations, models, metrics for each cited study and explicitly state how this work differs.
- Define acronyms (e.g., PLN) at first occurrence and fix citation formatting.

- Data/Preprocessing: Provide dataset statistics (speakers, samples, token counts, length mean/SD, class balance) and fix Table 1 caption mismatch.
- Segmentation: Define “manually segmented” (sentences/clauses/narrative units), explain inconsistent segmentation methods, and discuss effects on features.
- Text normalization: Describe full preprocessing pipeline (lowercasing, punctuation, numbers, stopwords, lemmatization) and acknowledge lemmatization is not final normalization.
- Transcription conventions: Cite or link transcription/annotation guidelines.

- Disfluencies: Provide strong justification for removing disfluencies or retain them and run ablation showing their impact.

- Embeddings: State exact corpora used, algorithm, dimensionality, window, min-count, epochs, preprocessing, whether embeddings are pre-trained or fine-tuned, and ensure no leakage across splits.
- Clarify whether embeddings were trained on in-domain vs external data and detail tuning procedure.

- Feature definitions: Expand linguistic feature list by category (lexical richness, POS/morphosyntax, dependency features, discourse, fluency markers, readability, narrative structure) with precise definitions/formulas; move full list to appendix if needed.
- Define “shortest path” feature and give example if it refers to dependency/syntactic paths.

- BoW justification: Explain why TF-only was used and consider adding tf-idf as baseline (tf-idf is standard).
- Thresholds: Specify which threshold parameters were used (e.g., similarity thresholds), their tuned values, and tuning methods.

- Multi-view learning: Precisely describe the approach (feature concat vs co-training vs MKL vs late fusion/stacking), base classifiers, meta-learner, kernels/weights, hyperparameters, and validation protocol.
- Clarify terms like “best scenario” and the claim that BoW suffers from disfluencies (disfluency tokens can also be informative).

- Experiments: State datasets, splits, evaluation metrics for each experiment; ensure no speaker overlap and report cross-validation mean±SD.
- Significance testing: Apply appropriate tests (paired bootstrap, McNemar’s, approximate randomization) for key comparisons and report confidence levels.
- Results analysis: Include error analysis or feature importance discussion to support claims about influential features.

- Minor fixes: Correct wording/typos (e.g., “a multidisciplinary team consisting of psychiatrists…”, punctuation, sentence clarity) and ensure consistent terminology/dataset naming throughout.

- Overall recommendation: Work is promising but requires clearer exposition, full methodological details, justified preprocessing, stronger baselines, and statistical validation to be substantially strengthened.
