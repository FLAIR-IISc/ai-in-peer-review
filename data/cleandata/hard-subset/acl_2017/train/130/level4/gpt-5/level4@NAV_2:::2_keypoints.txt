- Investigates MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets.
- Proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence plus graph-derived, lexical, and syntactic features.
- Evaluates models with 5-fold cross-validation across multiple classifiers, reporting dataset-dependent performance.
- Addresses a clear problem and uses appropriate datasets.

- Manuscript readability is poor due to extensive English grammar and spelling issues.
- The classification setup is underspecified (what is a single instance, inconsistent counts, dataset tables and descriptions confusing/too long).
- Unclear motivation for enriching co-occurrence graphs versus using embedding-similarity-based word graphs.
- No use of domain-specific biomedical resources or tools despite biomedical datasets.
- Lack of class-distribution reporting makes accuracy potentially inappropriate; recommend F1 (macro or per-class).
- Results are reported to high precision on very small datasets without statistical significance testing, so gains may be unreliable.
