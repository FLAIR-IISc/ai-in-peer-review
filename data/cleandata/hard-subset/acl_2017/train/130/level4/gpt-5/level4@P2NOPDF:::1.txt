Strengths:
- The paper investigates applying NLP to speech transcripts (narratives/descriptions) to identify mild cognitive impairment (ICD-10 F06.7) in Portuguese. The authors report separating MCI patients from healthy controls (lines 141–144). However, the conclusion (lines 781–785) notes accuracies of 60–85% and acknowledges that distinguishing groups is not easy. Despite this tension, the results are promising and justify a closer look at the methodology.
- The dataset includes 40 participants (20+20), but the transcripts are only in Portuguese. The paper would benefit from more methodological detail and at least one short transcript translated into English, ideally accompanied by a partial example of the derived network/embeddings for that transcript.

Weaknesses:
- The related work is overly broad. Several citations are general NLP background (e.g., Salton 1989 in section 4.2.3) or tangential (e.g., sentiment classification and pedestrian detection, lines 652–654) and could be omitted. Section 4.2.3 (lines 608–621) can be tightened. Overall, condense the first five pages to focus on the core topic, and expand the discussion of the technological contribution, including English examples from ABCD and/or Cinderella narratives.
- Given the relatively short Portuguese narratives, it is unclear how reliable word similarities and embeddings were obtained. Lines 272–289 state that word-level networks are built from continuous word representations. What data were used to train these representations—only ABCD+Cinderella, or external corpora as well? Lines 513–525 indicate that subword (n-gram) networks were used to generate word embeddings. Again, what was the training source? Is there empirical evidence that combining the two network types yields better accuracy? Please also clarify what counts as “out-of-vocabulary words” (line 516) and where they arise.
- The small sample size (20+20) and language restriction limit generalizability. More justification of modeling choices and validation procedures would help readers assess the robustness of the findings.

General discussion:
- The topic is important: using NLP to detect cognitive impairments, especially in a lower-resource language like Portuguese. Please explain how embeddings were computed given resource limitations and how the approach might transfer to other languages or larger datasets.
- The manuscript would benefit from revision: shorten sections 1–3, compress 4.1, and add more detail on the experimental setup, training data, and evaluation. A brief explanation of the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms would also help readers unfamiliar with them.
