- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts (ABCD and Cinderella) to identify mild cognitive impairment (MCI) and reports classification accuracy between ~60%–85%, though the conclusion downplays this optimism.  
- Overall message is encouraging and motivates readers to seek more methodological detail.  
- Dataset is very small (20 patients + 20 controls) and being Portuguese limits accessibility for non‑Portuguese readers.  
- Recommend adding technical details, at least one short transcript with English translation, and a (partial) sample network with embeddings.  
- Introduction and literature review are too long; many citations are either general NLP background or not directly relevant and should be removed.  
- Section 4.2.3 (lines 608–621) and the first five pages should be compressed to focus on the paper’s core topic.  
- Need clarification on how word similarities/embeddings were learned given short Portuguese narratives: were embeddings trained only on ABCD+Cinderella or on external corpora?  
- Clarify the role and training source of subword (n‑gram) networks and whether combining word‑level and subword networks improves accuracy.  
- Define “out‑of‑vocabulary words” in context and specify their source.  
- Explain how embeddings are computed for Portuguese (a lower‑resource language) and provide more experimental detail.  
- Clarify transcription norms NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 for reproducibility.  
- Text requires revision and tightening (shorten Sections 1–3, compress 4.1, add experimental details).  
- Specific technical corrections: “as it a lightweight” → “as in a lightweight”; PLN → NLP; remove duplicated words “out of cookie out of the cookie.”  
- Table/refs fixes: Table 3 decimal separator 72,0 → 72.0; DOI/link duplication and incorrect link pointing at lines ~992–1002.
