- Addresses MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets using a graph-based method leveraging word co-occurrence.
- Extracts features from various graph characteristics including lexical and syntactic properties.
- Reports results using 5-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers; different models perform variably across datasets.
- Targets a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets.

- Manuscript is hard to read due to poor English, needing thorough grammar and spelling revision.
- Core ML setup is poorly described: unclear what a classification instance is and transcript-level dataset counts are missing.
- Tables describe the original studies rather than the data used for classification; patient age is irrelevant to the presented task.
- Dataset description is overly long and contains inconsistent numbers (e.g., mismatch in reported participant counts).
- Motivation for graph enrichment is unclear; alternative word-node similarity approaches are not discussed.
- No use of domain-specific biomedical tools despite biomedical datasets.
- Class distribution is not reported, so accuracy may be inappropriate; F1 (and other metrics) should be reported.
- Results are reported to unrealistic precision on very small datasets without statistical tests, so significance of improvements is unclear.
