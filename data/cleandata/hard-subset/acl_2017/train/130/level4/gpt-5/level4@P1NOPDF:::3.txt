The paper presents a novel application of largely existing representations, feature sets, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem, datasets, and domain are thoroughly introduced. While some details are missing, the proposed approach and experiments seem reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

That said, the paper requires substantial editing to improve readability. Key methodological details are missing or unclear: the multi-view learning setup is not described; the “linguistic features” need precise definition; and the source data used to train word embeddings is not specified (if the three datasets in the paper were used, they appear too small). The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) is not justified, even though these may be crucial for MCI detection. It is also unclear why tf-idf was not used for BoW representations. Statistical significance testing is absent. Finally, the related work is treated somewhat superficially. Detailed comments follow.

- Abstract: Please shorten the abstract. See specific notes below.
- Lines 22–23: Rephrase for clarity. For example: “However, MCI-related disfluencies produce agrammatical speech, impacting parsing results.”
- Lines 24–25: Do you mean that grammatical errors in transcripts were manually corrected? Why should this be done—don’t such errors indicate MCI? Only after reading the Introduction and Related Work is your intention clear. Consider adding concrete examples of disfluencies here.
- Lines 29–30: Rephrase as: “as it is a lightweight and language-independent representation.”
- Lines 34–38: It is not immediately clear which three datasets you refer to. Consider wording such as “the other two: Cinderella and …”.
- Line 70: “15% a year” is ambiguous. Do you mean annual incidence, growth rate, or something else?
- Line 73: Needs rephrasing for clarity.
- Lines 115–117: It is not obvious why BoW would struggle with disfluencies; please explain the expected failure modes.
- Lines 147–149: Clarify what you mean by “the best scenario.”
- Line 157: “in public corpora of DementiaBank” — please provide a link or citation to DementiaBank.
- Line 162: Provide a link or citation for the “Picnic picture of the Western Aphasia Battery.”
- Line 170: Briefly explain what the WML subtest is.
- Line 172: Citations are missing.
- Lines 166–182: This seems to be the core of the related work but is treated superficially. Please specify the methods used in prior work and how they compare to your approach.
- Line 185: Follow the conference citation style, e.g., “Aluisio et al. (2016) used…”
- Line 188: Define “PLN.”
- Lines 233–235: This passage could use rephrasing. Lemmatization is not necessarily the last step in preprocessing; also, several common normalization steps are omitted.
- Lines 290–299: Were the word embeddings trained on your MCI datasets or on external corpora? Please specify.
- Line 322: “consisted of” should be “consist of.”
- Lines 323–332: This section needs rewriting. What does “manually segmented of the DementiaBank and Cinderella” mean—segmented into sentences? Why weren’t all datasets segmented automatically? Define “ABCD.” You list datasets as (i) and (ii), but later refer to three datasets, which is confusing. Explicitly name the datasets instead of using “first,” “second,” “third.”
- Table 1 caption: You mention “additional statistics,” but only demographic information is shown. Please provide the promised statistics or adjust the caption.
- Lines 375–388: Please justify the removal of filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc. These may be important features in MCI detection.
- Line 399: “multidisciplinary team with psychiatrists …” → “multidisciplinary team consisting of psychiatrists …”
- Lines 340–440: Provide a link or citation for the transcription norms used.
- Section 4.2.1: Clarify which dataset(s) were used to train the word embeddings.
- Line 560: Does “shortest path” refer to feature 6? Please make the reference explicit.
- Section 4.2.2 (Linguistic Features): This section needs significant expansion to clearly define each feature, its motivation, and how it is computed. Also verify compliance with conference guidelines for supplementary material.
- Line 620: “In this work, term frequency was …” Also, why not use tf-idf, the most common weighting scheme for BoW?
- Lines 641–645: This sentence needs to be rewritten for clarity.
- Line 662: What is “the threshold parameter”? Is this a cosine distance threshold for word embeddings? Please specify its value, tuning procedure, and rationale.
- Line 735: Missing a period.
- Section 4.3 (Classification Algorithms): The exact multi-view learning scheme is not described; please provide details (e.g., view definitions, fusion/ensemble strategy, training procedure). Also report statistical significance to support comparisons among systems.

In summary, the paper addresses an important problem with a sensible approach and informative datasets. However, to be publishable, it needs clearer methodological descriptions (especially multi-view learning, linguistic features, and embedding training data), stronger justification for preprocessing choices (notably the removal of disfluencies), explicit use or justification of feature weighting (tf-idf), proper citation and related work depth, and statistical significance testing of results.
