- Paper models speech transcripts as embedding-enriched word co-occurrence networks to detect MCI and evaluates classifiers across three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD).
- Main contribution 1: Embedding-enriched complex-network features can match or outperform parser-dependent linguistic features for noisy, short transcripts.
- Main contribution 2: Multi-view and ensemble combinations improve accuracy over single-feature baselines on two datasets.
- Main contribution 3: Proposes a lightweight, largely language-independent pipeline that avoids heavy preprocessing and parsing.
- Strength: Cross-lingual evaluation (English, Portuguese) and multiple task types (picture description, narrative recall) increase external validity.
- Strength: Approach addresses practical clinical constraints (short texts, disfluencies, limited resources) and mitigates sparsity with embeddings.
- Strength: Systematic comparison across feature families and classifiers, including ensemble and multi-view experiments.
- Strength: Reported gains from embedding enrichment and multi-view methods are consistent across datasets.
- Weakness: Multi-view learning methodology is underspecified (combination rule, weighting, training protocol).
- Weakness: Exact list of linguistic features (especially Portuguese) is unclear.
- Weakness: Embedding training data and handling of data sparsity are unspecified.
- Weakness: Preprocessing choices (removal of disfluencies) lack justification and may discard diagnostic signals.
- Weakness: BoW uses raw term frequency without rationale or tf–idf comparison.
- Weakness: No statistical significance tests reported to assess robustness of improvements.
- Weakness: Related work coverage is superficial with missing citations/definitions (e.g., DementiaBank, WAB Picnic, “PLN”) and limited positioning relative to prior methods.
- Weakness: Writing and organization need substantial editing for clarity and consistent dataset/metric descriptions.
- Question: Exactly how was multi-view learning implemented (per-view classifiers, combination rule, learned weights, tuning within CV)?
- Question: Which corpora and preprocessing were used to train English and Portuguese embeddings, and how was OOV/sparsity handled?
- Question: Why were disfluencies removed; were experiments run with them retained or encoded as features?
- Question: Was tf–idf tried for BoW, and how did it compare to raw term frequency?
- Question: Can the authors report statistical significance tests (e.g., McNemar, bootstrap) for key comparisons?
- Question: What is the “threshold parameter” (cosine similarity for edges), how was it selected, and was selection nested within cross-validation?
- Detailed: Abstract and early sections need rewriting for clarity and explicit dataset naming (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD).
- Detailed: Clarify dataset descriptions (define ABCD, explain “segmented,” sentence segmentation choices).
- Detailed: Add citations/links for DementiaBank, WAB Picnic, and explain Wechsler Logical Memory (WLM) subtest.
- Detailed: Expand related work with concrete feature sets, modeling choices, and comparisons to prior MCI/AD speech studies.
- Detailed: Follow citation/terminology style (ACL), define “PLN,” and justify preprocessing/lemmatization choices.
- Detailed: Specify embedding details (training data, tokenization, OOV handling, use/reuse of fastText, threshold tuning).
- Detailed: Make network feature definitions precise (largest connected component handling for shortest path/diameter).
- Detailed: List exact Coh-Metrix/Coh-Metrix-Dementia metrics used and justify exclusions.
- Detailed: Provide classifier hyperparameters (SVM-RBF kernel params, k for k-NN, RF config) and hyperparameter selection protocol (nested CV).
- Detailed: Ensure table captions match content and include basic dataset statistics (tokens per narrative, sample sizes).
- Detailed: Provide links/citations for transcription norms (NURC/SP) and sentence segmentation tools (e.g., DeepBond).
- Overall: Interesting and promising approach; revise to add methodological transparency, preprocessing justification, and statistical testing before acceptance.
