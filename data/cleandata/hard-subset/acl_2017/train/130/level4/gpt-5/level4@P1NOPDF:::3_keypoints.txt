- Novel application of existing representations/methods to detect MCI in speech; problem and datasets well introduced.
- Study is interesting and informative but requires substantial editing for readability.
- Multi-view learning setup is not described; details on views, fusion, and training procedure are missing.
- “Linguistic features” are not precisely defined; need clear definitions, motivations, and computation methods.
- Source corpora used to train word embeddings are unspecified; using the paper’s small datasets seems unlikely.
- Rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is not justified and may remove diagnostic signals.
- No explanation provided for not using tf-idf instead of raw term frequency for BoW.
- Statistical significance testing of results is absent.
- Related work section is superficial and needs deeper comparison of prior methods.
- Abstract is too long and should be shortened.
- Several sentences/phrases need rephrasing for clarity (lines noted throughout review).
- It’s unclear whether grammatical errors in transcripts were manually corrected and why; examples of disfluencies should be added.
- Ambiguity about which three datasets are referenced; explicitly name datasets rather than ordinal references.
- “15% a year” is ambiguous—clarify the intended statistic.
- Explain why BoW would struggle with disfluencies and expected failure modes.
- Clarify what “the best scenario” refers to.
- Provide citation/link to DementiaBank.
- Provide citation/link for the Picnic picture from the Western Aphasia Battery.
- Briefly explain the WML subtest and add missing citations.

- Specify whether word embeddings were trained on MCI datasets or external corpora and give details.

- Justify removal of filled pauses and other disfluencies given their potential importance for MCI detection.
