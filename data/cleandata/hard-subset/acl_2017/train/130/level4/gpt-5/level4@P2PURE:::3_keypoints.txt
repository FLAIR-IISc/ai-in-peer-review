- Novel application of existing features/methods to MCI detection; problem setting and datasets described, but readability needs substantial editing.
- Core methodological details are missing, notably the multi-view learning setup.
- The set of “linguistic features” is insufficiently defined and requires expansion.
- It’s unclear which corpora were used to train the word embeddings; the three in-paper datasets seem too small for this.
- Removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unexplained and may discard important MCI signals.
- The decision not to use tf-idf for BoW classification is not justified.
- Statistical significance testing is missing to support experimental conclusions.
- The related work section is superficial and needs more detailed comparisons to prior methods and results.
- Abstract should be shortened and many sentences across the paper need rephrasing for clarity.
- Clarify whether grammatical/transcription errors were manually corrected and justify given their diagnostic value; add examples of disfluencies.
- Provide citations/links for DementiaBank, the Picnic picture/WAB, transcription norms, and other referenced resources.
- Define acronyms and terms (e.g., ABCD, PLN, WML subtest) and add missing citations.
- Preprocessing description is incomplete—lemmatization is not necessarily the final step and other normalization steps should be listed.
- Clarify dataset segmentation: what “segmented” means, why some datasets were manual vs. automatic, and explicitly name the datasets.
- Table 1 caption references additional statistics that are missing; update to match provided data.
- Specify which dataset(s) generated the word embeddings and clarify terms like “shortest path” and the “threshold parameter” (e.g., cosine distance).
- Expand Section 4.2.2 (Linguistic Features) and check conference guidelines for supplementary material if needed.
- Fix grammatical/formatting issues (typos, missing periods) and follow proper citation/conference formatting.
- Provide full details of the multi-view learning scheme and include statistical tests comparing classifier results.
