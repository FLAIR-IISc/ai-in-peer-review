- Investigates MCI identification from speech transcripts across three datasets.  
- Proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence to model transcripts.  
- Constructs features from diverse graph characteristics covering lexical and syntactic properties.  
- Evaluates models with 5-fold cross-validation across multiple classifiers, showing dataset-dependent performance.  
- Addresses a well-defined problem and uses appropriate datasets.  

- Manuscript readability suffers from incorrect English and needs thorough editing.  
- The ML task and classification instance are not clearly specified; dataset reporting (transcript-level counts) and tables are inconsistent.  
- Motivation for the chosen graph enrichment is unclear—alternative node/embedding-based representations are not discussed.  
- Despite biomedical datasets, no domain-specific tools are used.  
- Class distribution is unspecified, making accuracy potentially inappropriate; F1 (or other class-sensitive metrics) would be preferable.  
- Reporting overly precise results on very small datasets without statistical significance testing leaves the meaningfulness of gains uncertain.
