- Applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to distinguish healthy controls from patients with mild cognitive impairment, reporting accuracy between ~60% and 85%.
- Overall result is encouraging but conclusions temper initial optimism; motivates more methodological detail.
- Released corpus is small (20 healthy + 20 MCI) and inaccessible to non‑Portuguese readers; paper should include at least one English-translated transcript and example network/embeddings.
- Introduction and literature review are overly long and contain tangential citations; first five pages should be compressed to focus on technical contributions.
- Provide English translations of the ABCD and/or Cinderella narratives to aid reader understanding.
- Unclear how continuous word representations/embeddings were learned from short Portuguese narratives—clarify whether training used only ABCD+Cinderella or external corpora.
- Clarify the use of subword (n‑gram) networks for embeddings, evidence that combining network types improves accuracy, and the origin/definition of “out‑of‑vocabulary” words.
- Emphasize how embeddings are obtained for a lower‑resource language (Portuguese) and document any external resources used.
- Revise and shorten Sections 1–3 and condense 4.1; expand experimental setup and results detail.
- Explain the transcription guidelines referenced (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).
- Minor technical/typo fixes: “as it a lightweight” → “as in a lightweight”; PLN → NLP; remove duplicated “out of cookie”; fix decimal in Table 3 (72.0); correct duplicate DOI and hyperlink in references.
