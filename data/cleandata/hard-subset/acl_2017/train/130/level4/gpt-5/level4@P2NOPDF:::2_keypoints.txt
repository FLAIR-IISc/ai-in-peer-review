Strengths:
- Tackles MCI detection from speech transcripts across three datasets.
- Proposes a graph-based word co-occurrence approach with lexical, syntactic, and graph-derived features.
- Evaluates multiple classifiers using 5-fold cross-validation and reports variability across datasets.
- Problem is well-defined and datasets are appropriate.

Weaknesses:
- Writing quality is poor, with frequent grammatical errors and awkward phrasing needing thorough editing.
- Task formulation and dataset description are unclear: unclear instance definition, missing transcript-level counts/properties, inconsistent numbers, and excessive irrelevant details.
- Graph design rationale is weak: no justification for the chosen "enrichment" versus alternatives (e.g., embedding-similarity graphs).
- Lacks domain adaptation: no use of biomedical-specific tools or resources.
- Evaluation metrics are inadequate: class distribution not reported and accuracy alone is insufficient—F1 and related measures needed.
- Statistical validity concerns: tiny datasets, over-precise reporting, and no statistical tests to assess significance.
