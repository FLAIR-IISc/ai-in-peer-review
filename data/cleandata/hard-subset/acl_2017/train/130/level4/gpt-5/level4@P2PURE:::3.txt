The paper presents a novel application of largely existing representations, feature sets, and methods to the task of detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The problem setting, datasets, and domain are thoroughly described. Although some details are missing, the proposed approach and experiments appear reasonable. Overall, I found the study interesting and informative.

However, the paper needs substantial editing to improve readability. Several key concepts lack detail. For example, the multi-view learning setup is not described; the set of “linguistic features” needs clarification; and it is unclear which datasets were used to train the word embeddings (presumably the three datasets described in the paper, which seem too small for that purpose). It is also unclear why disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.) were removed, as they may be important signals for MCI. Likewise, it is not clear why the most common tf-idf weighting scheme was not used for the BoW classifications. In addition, statistical significance testing is missing to support the experimental conclusions. Lastly, the related work section is somewhat superficial. Detailed comments follow.
