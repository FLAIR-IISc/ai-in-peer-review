- Paper applies existing representations and methods to MCI detection from speech; problem, datasets, and domain are well described and study is interesting.
- Manuscript needs substantial editing to improve readability and flow.
- Multi-view learning approach is not described and must be specified in detail.
- The set of “linguistic features” is inadequately defined and requires expansion.
- It is unclear which corpora were used to train the word embeddings; the described datasets seem too small for that purpose.
- Removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unjustified and may eliminate important MCI signals.
- The decision to omit tf-idf weighting for BoW features is not motivated and should be justified.
- No statistical significance testing is reported to support the experimental conclusions.
- Related work is treated superficially and needs deeper discussion and direct comparisons to prior methods.
- Abstract is too long, contains unclear phrasing, and should include brief examples of disfluencies and clearer dataset identification.
- Several sentences throughout (Introduction/Background) are unclear or need rephrasing and clarification of claims (e.g., BoW vs disfluencies, “best scenario,” “15% a year”).
- Provide citations/links for key resources (DementiaBank, Picnic picture/WAB) and define acronyms like WML and PLN.
- The description of prior studies (lines ~166–182) lacks precise methods and comparisons to the present work.
- Clarify whether word embeddings were trained on MCI datasets or external corpora and specify which datasets were used for embeddings.
- Explain feature definitions (e.g., “shortest path”), expand Section 4.2.2 on linguistic features, and consider moving lengthy details to supplementary material per guidelines.
- Clarify what the “threshold parameter” refers to (e.g., cosine distance threshold) and rewrite unclear sentences (lines ~641–645).
- Data processing/annotation descriptions are confusing: explicitly name datasets, explain manual segmentation, define “ABCD,” and provide transcription norms citation.
- Table 1 caption claims additional statistics that are not present; ensure table and caption match.
- Justify removal of disfluencies and describe the annotation/transcription norms and the multidisciplinary team composition clearly.
- Classification/evaluation section omits details of the multi-view learning scheme and must report statistical significance for comparisons.
- Overall, address missing methodological details, justify design choices, add significance testing, and strengthen the related work to substantially improve the paper.
