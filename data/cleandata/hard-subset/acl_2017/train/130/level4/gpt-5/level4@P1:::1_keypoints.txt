- Strength: Applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify MCI patients and reports ability to distinguish healthy vs. MCI subjects, though accuracies vary (60–85%).
- Strength: Overall results are encouraging and motivate further investigation of the approach.
- Strength/Request: Paper would be stronger with more implementation details, an English-translated short transcript, and a partial example network enriched with embeddings.

- Weakness: Introduction and related work are overly long and include some irrelevant citations; literature review should be compressed.
- Weakness: Certain cited works (e.g., sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) are not directly relevant and should be removed.
- Weakness: Section 4.2.3 and other early sections are too detailed and should be shortened to focus on technical innovation and examples.

- Weakness: Small, Portuguese-only datasets (20 MCI + 20 controls) limit accessibility and raise questions about result reliability.
- Weakness: Short narrative length (especially ABCD) questions the reliability of embedding-derived word similarities.
- Weakness: Unclear what corpus(s) were used to train embeddings (in-domain vs. external) and whether subword (n-gram) info was trained on the same data.
- Weakness: Need empirical evidence/ablation showing benefit of combining co-occurrence networks with embedding-based links vs. each alone.
- Weakness: Origin and proportion of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words are unclear; need clarification on how subword models mitigate OOV.

- General: Topic is important and Portuguese focus is valuable, but requires clearer explanation of embedding training, preprocessing, and validation for low-resource language.
- General: Recommend revising Sections 1–3 for concision, compressing 4.1, and adding more experimental setup and design details.
- General: Add brief explanation of NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms for non-expert readers.

- Question 1: Which corpora were used to train word embeddings for English and Portuguese—domain-specific, external, or both?
- Question 2: How were embedding hyperparameters chosen (vector size, window, epochs); were they tuned per language/dataset and was embedding quality evaluated?
- Question 3: What is the empirical contribution of embedding-enriched links beyond co-occurrence—please provide CN vs. CNE ablations with significance testing.
- Question 4: What proportion and types of OOV words were observed, where do they arise from, and how does the subword model mitigate them?
- Question 5: Can the authors provide a small anonymized transcript (with English translation) and a partial network visualization for ABCD/Cinderella?
- Question 6: Please clarify the tone discrepancy between optimistic early claims and the cautious conclusion, contextualizing accuracies with dataset size, class balance, and task difficulty.

- Technical comments: Fix typos and errors — Line 29 (“as it a” → “as in a”), Line 188 (PLN → NLP), duplicated words at Line 264, Table 3 formatting (72,0 → 72.0), and duplicate/mislinked DOIs/links at Lines 992–1002.
