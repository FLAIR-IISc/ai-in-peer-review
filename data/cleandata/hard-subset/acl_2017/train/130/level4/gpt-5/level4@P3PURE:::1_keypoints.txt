- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify MCI and reports separability between healthy and MCI participants, but conclusion tempers initial optimism (accuracy 60–85% vs “not easy to distinguish”).
- Dataset is very small (40 subjects: 20 MCI, 20 controls) and not accessible to non‑Portuguese readers; include more technical detail, an English transcript example, and a sample network with embeddings.
- Introduction and related work are overly long with some irrelevant citations; compress the first five pages and shorten Section 4.2.3.
- Need to provide English translations of ABCD/Cinderella narratives to free space for methodological detail and examples.
- Unclear how reliable word similarities/embeddings are given short Portuguese narratives; clarify training source(s) for continuous representations (only datasets or external corpora).
- Explain the subword (n‑gram) network approach: what data trained it on, how it was combined with word‑level networks, and whether combining improves accuracy.
- Define “out‑of‑vocabulary words” precisely and state their origin.
- Emphasize why NLP detection of cognitive impairment in Portuguese is important and explain how embeddings were computed for a lower‑resource language.
- Revise manuscript structure: shorten Sections 1–3, compress Section 4.1, and add detailed experiment explanations.
