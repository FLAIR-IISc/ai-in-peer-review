- Paper applies established representations and methods to MCI detection from speech; problem framing, datasets, and clinical context are well described and the approach/experiments appear sound and interesting.
- Manuscript requires substantial editing for clarity and readability (many phrasing, grammar, and formatting issues throughout).
- Key methodological specifics are missing: the exact multi-view learning setup is not described.
- The composition and definition of the “linguistic features” set are unclear and need expansion.
- The source, size, and training corpus for the word embeddings are not specified; training on the three datasets alone would likely be insufficient.
- The rationale for removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is not justified and these may be informative for MCI detection.
- The decision not to use tf-idf for Bag-of-Words features is unexplained.
- No statistical significance testing is reported to support experimental differences or conclusions.
- The related work section is superficial: methods used in prior studies and comparisons to the current approach should be specified, and some citations are missing.
- Numerous missing or unclear citations/links are noted (e.g., DementiaBank, Western Aphasia Battery “Picnic” picture, transcription norms, WLM subtest).
- Dataset descriptions are confusing (manual vs automatic segmentation, definition of “ABCD,” inconsistent references to datasets) and Table 1 lacks referenced dataset statistics.
- Several specific terms/phrases need clarification (e.g., “15% a year,” “the best scenario,” definition of “PLN,” what “shortest path” and the “threshold parameter” mean).
- Preprocessing steps are ambiguously described (lemmatization presented as a final step; other normalization steps omitted); clarify preprocessing pipeline and motivations.
- Section-level issues: expand Section 4.2.2 (linguistic features), specify corpus for embedding training in 4.2.1, and follow conference guidelines for supplementary material.
- Numerous minor editorial fixes are required (typos, missing periods, tense/word-choice corrections, citation style consistency).
