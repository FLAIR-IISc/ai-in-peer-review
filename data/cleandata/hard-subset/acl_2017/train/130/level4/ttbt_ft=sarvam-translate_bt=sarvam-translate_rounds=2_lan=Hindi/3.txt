This paper primarily describes a new application of existing representations, feature sets, and methods: namely, detecting mild cognitive impairment (MCI) in speech narratives. The nature of the problem, dataset, and domain are fully described. Despite the missing details, the proposed solution and experiments seem reasonable. Overall, I found this study interesting and informative.

In terms of shortcomings, the paper requires some significant editing to improve readability. Details on some key concepts seem missing. For example, details on the multi-view learning used are removed; the set of "linguistic features" needs to be clarified; it is not entirely clear which datasets were used to generate word embeddings (perhaps the 3 datasets described in the paper seem too small for that purpose...). It is also unclear why the datasets were purged of fillers (filler pauses, false starts, repetitions, etc.). One might suggest that they are important features in the context of MCI. It is also unclear why the most popular tf-idf weighting scheme was not used for BoW classification. Furthermore, significance tests were not provided to validate the conclusions from the experiments. Finally, the related work is described a bit superficially. Detailed notes are provided below:
