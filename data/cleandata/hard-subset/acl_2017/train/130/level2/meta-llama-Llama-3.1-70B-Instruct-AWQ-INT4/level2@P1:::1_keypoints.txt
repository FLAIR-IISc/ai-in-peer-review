- Proposes a novel MCI detection method using complex networks enriched with word embeddings applied to speech transcripts.  
- Models short neuropsychological speech transcripts as complex networks augmented by embeddings to better represent sparse text.  
- Evaluates the approach on three datasets (DementiaBank + two Portuguese corpora).  
- Reports that complex network features outperform traditional linguistic features across all datasets.  
- Explores ensemble and multi-view learning to combine classifiers and feature spaces, improving accuracy.  
- Contribution: introduces a departure from traditional linguistic-feature MCI detection approaches.  
- Contribution: demonstrates improved accuracy over baseline linguistic features.  
- Contribution: applies multi-view learning in the MCI detection context.  
- Strength: method is innovative and conceptually significant for the field.  
- Strength: evaluation includes multiple datasets including an English benchmark and Portuguese data.  
- Strength: empirical gains reported over traditional features.  
- Weakness: limited number of datasets—broader evaluation needed for generalization.  
- Weakness: strong dependence on word embeddings, which may miss MCI-relevant language nuances.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art MCI detection methods.  
- Question: how are out-of-vocabulary words handled in the embedding-based approach?  
- Question: please detail optimization of the threshold parameter in ensemble/multi-view methods.  
- Question: how will the approach be extended to other languages given limited non-English embeddings?  
- Additional note: promising novel contribution but requires more extensive evaluation and mitigation of listed limitations.
