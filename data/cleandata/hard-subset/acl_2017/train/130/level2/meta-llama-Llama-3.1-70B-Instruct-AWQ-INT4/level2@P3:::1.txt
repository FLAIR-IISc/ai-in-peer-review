**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their model on three datasets: the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets. The authors also explore the use of ensemble and multi-view learning to combine classifiers and achieve improved accuracy.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to MCI detection**: The paper proposes a new approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of enriching complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments.
3. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The paper evaluates the proposed approach on three datasets, demonstrating its generalizability and robustness.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The paper proposes a novel approach to MCI detection, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Robust evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalizability.
3. **Improved accuracy**: The paper shows that the proposed approach can achieve improved accuracy compared to traditional linguistic features.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison to state-of-the-art methods in MCI detection.
2. **Lack of detailed analysis of results**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the results, including the performance of individual features and the impact of parameter tuning.
3. **Limited discussion of limitations and future work**: The paper does not provide a detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed approach and potential avenues for future work.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the limitations of the proposed approach, including the lack of comparison to state-of-the-art methods and the limited analysis of results?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the parameter tuning process and the impact of different parameters on the performance of the proposed approach?
3. How do the authors plan to extend the proposed approach to other neuropsychological assessments and datasets?
