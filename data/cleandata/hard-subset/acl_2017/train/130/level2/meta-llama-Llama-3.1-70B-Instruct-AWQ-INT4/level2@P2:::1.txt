**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their model on three datasets and compare the performance of complex network features with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to MCI detection**: The paper proposes a new approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors introduce a method to enrich complex networks with word embeddings, which improves the representation of short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments.
3. **Comparison with traditional features**: The paper compares the performance of complex network features with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, showing that complex networks are a competitive approach for MCI detection.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The paper proposes a novel approach to MCI detection, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Effective use of word embeddings**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of enriching complex networks with word embeddings, which improves the representation of short texts.
3. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The paper evaluates the proposed approach on three datasets and compares the performance with traditional features, providing a thorough understanding of the method's strengths and limitations.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited datasets**: The paper uses only three datasets, which may not be representative of the entire population of individuals with MCI.
2. **Lack of detailed analysis**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by the proposed approach, which could help identify areas for improvement.
3. **Comparison with state-of-the-art methods**: The paper does not compare the proposed approach with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's performance.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to address the issue of limited datasets in future work?
2. Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the errors made by the proposed approach?
3. How does the proposed approach compare with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach and its evaluation. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the errors made by the proposed approach and a comparison with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection.
