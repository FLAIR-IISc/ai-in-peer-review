- Summary: Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts by modeling transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings, evaluated on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery, showing improved accuracy over linguistic features and benefits from ensemble/multi-view learning.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a novel MCI detection method using complex networks built from speech transcripts.

- Contribution 2: Enriches complex network representations with word embeddings to better capture short neuropsychological texts.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates the approach on three datasets, suggesting generalizability and robustness.

- Strength 1: Innovative methodological approach to MCI detection.

- Strength 2: Robust evaluation across multiple datasets.

- Strength 3: Achieves improved accuracy compared to traditional linguistic features.

- Weakness 1: Limited comparison to state-of-the-art MCI detection methods.

- Weakness 2: Insufficient detailed analysis of results, feature contributions, and parameter effects.

- Weakness 3: Limited discussion of limitations and directions for future work.

- Question 1: How will the authors address limited comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and expand result analysis?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide details on parameter tuning and the impact of different parameters?
