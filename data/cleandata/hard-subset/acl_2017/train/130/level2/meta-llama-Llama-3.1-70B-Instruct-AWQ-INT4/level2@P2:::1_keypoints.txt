- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks enriched with word embeddings.  
- Novel approach: complex networks + word embeddings for MCI detection.  
- Enriches complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short neuropsychological texts.  
- Empirically compares complex network features with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words on three datasets.  
- Strength: innovative method contributing to MCI detection research.  
- Strength: effective use of word embeddings to improve short-text representation.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across three datasets.  
- Weakness: only three datasets used, potentially not representative.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis to identify failure modes.  
- Weakness: no comparison with state-of-the-art MCI detection methods.  
- Question: How will limited dataset coverage be addressed in future work?  
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed error analysis?  
- Question: How does the proposed method compare with state-of-the-art approaches?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but would benefit from deeper error analysis and SOTA comparison.
