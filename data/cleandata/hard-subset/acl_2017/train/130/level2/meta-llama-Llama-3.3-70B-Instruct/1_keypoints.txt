- Summary: Models speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, evaluated on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery, claiming outperforming traditional linguistic features and competitive with SOTA.
- Contribution 1: Proposes a novel MCI detection method using complex network features.
- Contribution 2: Enriches complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts.
- Contribution 3: Compares the proposed approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words.
- Strength 1: Innovative and significant methodological contribution to MCI detection.
- Strength 2: Achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.
- Strength 3: Robust evaluation across three diverse datasets.
- Weakness 1: Lacks detailed interpretation of the complex network features and their relation to MCI.
- Weakness 2: Reliance on word embeddings may limit applicability across languages or domains.
- Weakness 3: Comparisons are limited to traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words, not a broader set of SOTA methods.
