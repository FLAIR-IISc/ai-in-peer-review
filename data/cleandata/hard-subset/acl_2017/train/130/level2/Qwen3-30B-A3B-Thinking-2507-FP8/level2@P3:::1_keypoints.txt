- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI.  
- Claims language-independent complex network features that avoid manual transcript correction.  
- Claims network features outperform linguistic features across three datasets.  
- Shows ensemble and multi-view learning improve classification accuracy.  
- Addresses an important clinical problem with practical implications for early MCI detection.  
- Modeling transcripts as complex networks is well-motivated to handle disfluencies that undermine linguistic features.  
- Evaluation across Cookie Theft, Cinderella, and ABCD datasets (English, Portuguese) indicates cross-dataset/language robustness.  
- Multi-view learning combining networks, linguistic features, and BoW yields consistent improvements (up to 10% on Cinderella).  
- Highlights the practical limitation of linguistic features on disfluent speech.  
- Claim that networks outperform linguistic features is not fully supported—linguistic features did better on the manually revised Cinderella dataset.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of why complex networks work, especially given small dataset sizes (e.g., 43 MCI transcripts).  
- Fails to address computational complexity and scalability of network construction and embedding enrichment.  
- Limited comparison with recent NLP/MCI detection methods, reducing clarity on novelty and significance.  
- Question: How sensitive are results to choice of embedding model and similarity threshold?  
- Question: What evidence supports the claim of language independence given reliance on language-specific embeddings?  
- Question: How does the approach scale to larger datasets in terms of time and resource requirements?  
- Request to clarify the apparent contradiction about linguistic features requiring grammatical texts vs. Table 6 results.  
- Suggests a more detailed discussion of practical implementation limitations in clinical settings.  
- Recommends including comparisons with recent deep-learning approaches (attention/transformers).  
- Requests better justification for the disfluency handling strategy (removing filled pauses but keeping other disfluencies).
