- Proposes using complex network representations enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, avoiding manual transcript correction.  
- Contributions: applies complex network modeling to MCI detection, shows network+embeddings outperform traditional linguistic features, and demonstrates multi-view learning improves classification.  
- Develops an automated pipeline that handles disfluencies, addressing a key limitation of prior work.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements across three datasets; multi-view learning yields +10% on Cinderella and +4.71% on ABCD vs best individual classifiers.  
- Technical execution is sound: appropriate network metrics, evaluation across multiple classifiers, methodological detail sufficient for reproducibility, and statistically significant results.  
- Correctly notes that linguistic features struggle with disfluencies, justifying the network-based approach.  
- Lacks deeper analysis explaining why the network-based method outperforms linguistic features.  
- Does not identify which specific network metrics are most informative for MCI detection.  
- Insufficient discussion of limitations for very short narratives, a common clinical challenge.  
- Overall discussion of limitations is minimal, mainly noting the need for publicly available datasets.  
- Method shows clinical promise as a lightweight, language-independent approach suitable for large-scale assessments.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental (applying existing techniques to a new domain), though practical benefits are significant.  
- Recommendation: strengthen feature-importance analysis and expand the limitations discussion to better position the work.
