- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as co-occurrence networks enriched with word embeddings and using network topological features to detect MCI.  
- Evaluated the approach on three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) and compared to linguistic features and Bag-of-Words.  
- Claims: (1) language-independent method that avoids manual disfluency correction, (2) enriched complex networks outperform linguistic features across datasets, (3) multi-view learning with network+linguistic features further improves accuracy.  
- Strength: Addresses a clinically important problem enabling potential large-scale screening without specialist intervention.  
- Strength: Novel use of complex networks enriched with embeddings, with a clear method for adding semantic links via cosine similarity.  
- Strength: Robust evaluation across multiple datasets (including two Portuguese sets) suggesting cross-lingual potential.  
- Strength: Multi-view fusion yields meaningful gains (up to ~10% on Cinderella), showing complementarity of feature sets.  
- Weakness: Lacks theoretical justification linking specific topological measures (e.g., PageRank, Betweenness) to cognitive impairment markers.  
- Weakness: Limited benchmarking—no comparisons to recent state-of-the-art NLP methods (deep learning/transformers).  
- Weakness: Language-independence claim is premature given evaluation only in English and Portuguese.  
- Weakness: Disfluency preprocessing is rule-based following TalkBank guidelines and not fully automated or trivially portable across languages/domains.  
- Question: Which specific network features drive the performance gains and how do they relate to cognitive impairment?  
- Question: How would the method adapt to languages with very different syntax (e.g., agglutinative languages)?  
- Question: How was the cosine similarity threshold for network enrichment chosen and would it need tuning per language/domain?  
- Overall: Promising and empirically compelling approach that needs stronger theoretical grounding and broader baselines; with minor revisions it could make a strong conference contribution.
