- Summary: Proposes using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, claiming to avoid manual disfluency correction.  
- Contribution: Novel method modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings for MCI detection.  
- Contribution: Claims complex network features outperform linguistic features across multiple datasets.  
- Contribution: Applies ensemble and multi-view learning to improve performance.  
- Strength: Addresses an important healthcare problem and offers a promising alternative to disfluency-sensitive linguistic approaches.  
- Strength: Innovative use of complex networks with consistent improvements across three datasets (English and Portuguese).  
- Strength: Clear motivation and explanation of embedding-based enrichment to address short-text issues.  
- Weakness: Claim that complex networks outperform linguistic features in all datasets is contradicted by Cookie Theft results (LM > CN with GaussianNB).  
- Weakness: "Avoiding manual preprocessing" is misleading because the pipeline still uses rule-based disfluency removal (e.g., filled pause removal).  
- Weakness: Incorrect reporting of ensemble improvement in Cinderella dataset (claimed 10% vs actual 15.38%).  
- Weakness: "Language independence" is questionable given reliance on language-specific word embeddings and evaluation limited to English and Portuguese.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing provided to support reported performance improvements.  
- Question: How do you explain cases where linguistic features outperform complex networks and how does this affect your main claim?  
- Question: On what basis do you claim "language independence" given the reliance on language-specific embeddings and limited language evaluation?
