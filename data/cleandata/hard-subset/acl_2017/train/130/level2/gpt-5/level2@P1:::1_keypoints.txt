- Models speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-based semantic edges to detect MCI.
- Contributions: parser-free complex-network features augmented with embeddings; cross-language evaluation on three datasets; shows enriched networks often outperform linguistic metrics and BoW.
- Language- and tool-agnostic approach suitable for noisy clinical transcripts and low-resource languages.
- Embedding enrichment reduces sparsity in short-text networks; CNE generally outperforms plain CN.
- Evaluation across diverse clinical tasks (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) and on raw vs. manually revised transcripts.
- Competitive performance: multi-view fusion reaches 80% on ABCD; linear SVM with BoW reaches 75%.
- Clear, standard topological feature set (centralities, clustering, assortativity, path lengths) aiding reproducibility.
- Risk of data leakage and evaluation bias: possible narrative-level CV with same-subject samples in train/test, threshold tuning without nested validation, and no significance tests.
- Serious confound: ABCD groups differ in age/education, risking classifiers learning demographics instead of cognitive status.
- Limited baselines and metrics: only term-frequency BoW and accuracy reported; missing tf-idf, n-grams, averaged embeddings, and class-sensitive metrics (F1, AUC, sensitivity/specificity).
- Ambiguity about embeddings: unclear training corpora, sizes, use of pre-trained vectors, and whether semantic edges are weighted or thresholded.
- Lack of sensitivity analyses for cosine threshold, number of added edges, and graph construction choices (weighted vs. unweighted, k-NN).
- Unclear dataset composition and session handling for Cookie Theft and DementiaBank; comparability to prior work not established.
- Question on data splitting: did authors enforce subject-level separation across folds, especially for ABCD with two narratives per subject?
- Question on threshold selection: was cosine threshold tuned via nested CV or held-out dev set, and are sensitivity curves available?
- Question on embeddings: which corpora and sizes were used for fastText, and were pre-trained embeddings tested?
- Question on graph definition: are semantic edges unweighted; were weighted edges or k-NN graphs tried?
- Question on baselines/metrics: request to add tf-idf, n-grams, averaged embeddings, and report F1, precision/recall, AUC, confusion matrices, plus significance tests.
- Question on confound control: did authors match groups or include demographics as covariates; request matched or controlled analyses.
- Question on reproducibility: will code, preprocessing, feature extraction parameters, and subject-level splits be released?
- Suggestion: use nested, subject-level cross-validation and report multiple metrics with confidence intervals and significance tests.
- Suggestion: include stronger text baselines and ablation studies (CN vs CNE, stopwords, weighted vs unweighted edges).
- Suggestion: control for demographics or incorporate them as a separate view in multi-view learning.
- Suggestion: provide threshold and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses; explore weighted or k-NN semantic graphs.
- Suggestion: clarify dataset composition/session handling and align with standard DementiaBank splits where possible.
