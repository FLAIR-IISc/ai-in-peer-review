- Paper proposes representing transcripts as word co-occurrence complex networks enriched with embedding-similarity edges to detect MCI, evaluated on three datasets (DementiaBank Cookie Theft EN; Cinderella and ABCD PT).
- Authors extract topological network features, compare to linguistic metrics (Coh-Metrix variants) and BoW, and explore ensembles/multi-view learning; enriched networks often outperform linguistic features and multi-view models reach up to ~80% (ABCD).
- Contribution: shows embedding-enriched complex network (CNE) features are competitive or superior to standard linguistic metrics across English and Portuguese datasets.
- Contribution: provides empirical evidence that multi-view/ensemble combinations of network, linguistic, and BoW features yield further gains over single-view models.
- Contribution: presents a lightweight, parser-independent pipeline that tolerates disfluencies and minimizes manual preprocessing for scalable screening.
- Strength: cross-lingual validation on three datasets (two languages, varied tasks and lengths) supports generality and practical relevance.
- Strength: clear, replicable complex-network feature design (named topological measures; mean/SD/skewness aggregation) and sensible embedding enrichment addressing short-text sparsity.
- Strength: consistent empirical advantage of CNE over CN and linguistic metrics in most settings, with notable multi-view improvements (e.g., +10% on Cinderella).
- Strength: minimal supervised preprocessing (no lemmatization/parsing; disfluency handling) addresses a real deployment bottleneck in clinical NLP.
- Weakness: potential subject leakage in ABCD (two narratives per participant) — paper does not state if CV folds were split by subject, possibly inflating accuracies.
- Weakness: likely demographic confounds (e.g., ABCD controls avg age 61 vs MCI 72); no covariate control, matched sampling, or stratified analysis reported.
- Weakness: limited evaluation reporting — only accuracy shown; no per-class precision/recall/F1, ROC-AUC, calibration, significance testing, class-imbalance handling, or variance estimates.
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps — unspecified embedding corpus, threshold selection not nested in CV, no code or Portuguese feature lists beyond category names.
- Weakness: “language-independent” claim overstated since results rely on language-specific embeddings and Coh-Metrix variants; BoW SVM remains competitive in some cases.
- Question: Was cross-validation stratified by subject, especially for ABCD’s two retells; if not, can authors rerun with subject-wise splits?
- Question: How were embeddings trained (corpus, size, languages); were they trained on task data, potentially causing leakage?
- Question: Were similarity thresholds and classifier hyperparameters tuned within nested CV, and were significance tests performed for improvements?
- Question: Can authors report per-class metrics and control for age/education (e.g., matched subsets or regression-adjusted evaluation)?
- Question: How robust is the approach to ASR noise compared to using gold transcripts?
