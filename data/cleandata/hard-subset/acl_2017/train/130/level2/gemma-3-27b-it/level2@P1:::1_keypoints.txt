- Novel application: Uses complex network analysis with word embeddings on speech transcripts to detect MCI, robust to disfluencies without heavy parsing.
- Multi-dataset evaluation: Validated across three datasets (one English, two Portuguese), improving generalizability and addressing low-resource language applicability.
- Outperforms linguistic features: Complex network features consistently beat traditional linguistic features, showing resilience to noisy, agrammatical patient speech.
- Effective combination strategies: Ensemble and multi-view learning yield further gains (e.g., ~10% on Cinderella, ~4.71% on Arizona).
- Limited rationale for topology metrics: Paper lists ten network measures but lacks explanation of why they relate to MCI or which are most discriminative.
- Embedding detail and sensitivity lacking: Insufficient information about the pre-trained embedding model, training data, and effect of different embeddings or fine-tuning.
- Threshold sensitivity not analyzed: No systematic analysis of how the cosine similarity threshold for adding semantic links affects performance.
- Dataset size and imbalance concerns: Small, sometimes imbalanced datasets and demographic differences (e.g., age) risk overfitting and limit statistical power.
- Missing comparison to recent SOTA: Paper does not compare directly to recent advanced methods (e.g., deep learning/transformers) on similar tasks.
- Rationale for topology choices: Request clarification on which topology metrics are expected to indicate cognitive impairment and why.
- Embedding specifics requested: Ask for details on embedding model (corpus, dimensionality) and sensitivity/fine-tuning experiments.
- Threshold selection clarification: Ask how the cosine similarity threshold was chosen and whether sensitivity analysis was performed.
- Demographic impact discussion: Request discussion of how demographic differences across datasets might affect results.
- Writing and presentation: Suggest proofreading for clarity and tightening convoluted sentences.
- Additional materials and analysis: Recommend including a main-paper table of dataset demographics and performing error analysis to identify consistent model mistakes.
