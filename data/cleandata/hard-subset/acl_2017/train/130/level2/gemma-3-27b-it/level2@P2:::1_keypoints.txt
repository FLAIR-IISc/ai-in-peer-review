- Contribution: Applies complex networks enriched with word embeddings to MCI detection from noisy speech transcripts, adapting an existing text-network approach to a new clinical domain.
- Contribution: Evaluates the method on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, ABCD) in English and Portuguese, improving generalizability, especially for Portuguese.
- Contribution: Demonstrates that ensemble and multi-view combinations of complex networks, linguistic features, and BoW significantly improve classification accuracy.

- Strength: Addresses a practical clinical need for automated, noise-robust MCI detection from spontaneous speech.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains reported (up to ~10% on Cinderella, ~4.7% on Arizona), indicating practical benefit.
- Strength: Clear methodology and thorough experimentation across feature combinations and datasets.
- Strength: Explicitly considers language-specific challenges and motivates a language-independent approach for Portuguese.
- Strength: Situates the work appropriately within relevant literature on MCI detection and text networks.

- Weakness: The use of complex networks for text is not novel per se; novelty is mainly the application to MCI detection.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of how word embeddings contribute to performance and which semantic relations matter.
- Weakness: Dataset sizes are relatively small and potential class imbalance/biases are not fully addressed.
- Weakness: Ensemble/multi-view gains are shown but the paper offers limited insight into why specific feature combinations work.

- Question: What choices were made when enriching networks with embeddings (e.g., cosine similarity threshold) and were alternatives tested?
- Question: Which linguistic features correlated most with MCI, and how did they interact with network features in multi-view models?
- Question: What measures were taken to prevent overfitting given small datasets (regularization, augmentation, etc.)?
- Question: Can you provide qualitative examples illustrating how the complex network representation differentiates MCI from controls?

- Comment: Figures are small and should be enlarged for readability.
- Comment: Add a dataset summary table (number of participants, average transcript length, narrative type).
- Comment: Clarify notation in Tables 4–7 (e.g., meaning of "CNE-LM-BoW").

- Overall: Well-written, thorough paper with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
