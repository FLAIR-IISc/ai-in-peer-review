- Proposes representing speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, aiming to be robust to disfluencies.
- Shows network-based topological features outperform traditional linguistic features across multiple datasets.
- Explores ensemble and multi-view learning to further improve classification accuracy.
- Provides evaluations on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD datasets (English and Portuguese).
- Novel application of complex network analysis with embeddings to MCI detection, offering a robust alternative to parsing-based features.
- Strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements and ~80% accuracy on the Arizona dataset.
- Dataset diversity (three corpora, two languages) strengthens generalizability of findings.
- Combining complex networks, linguistic features, and BoW via ensemble/multi-view learning yields notable gains (e.g., +10% on Cinderella).
- Lacks detailed justification for selecting the specific ten topological network metrics used.
- Word embedding implementation is under-described (training data, dimensionality, hyperparameter tuning).
- Possible dataset biases (age/education differences between groups) not fully addressed or mitigated.
- No comparison to recent state-of-the-art ASR-based MCI detection methods is provided.
- Asks authors to justify choice of topological features and cite guiding theory/prior work.
- Requests full details on word embedding training data, dimensionality, and tuning.
- Asks how demographic imbalances might affect results and whether mitigation strategies were considered.
- Queries whether comparisons to recent ASR-based approaches were made or planned.
- Notes supplementary material is helpful for dataset/features details.
- Suggests clearer explanations in some sections and proposes exploring attention mechanisms and cross-dataset/language transferability.
