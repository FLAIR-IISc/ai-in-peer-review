- Proposes detecting MCI by modeling transcripts as word co-occurrence graphs enriched with semantic edges from word embeddings, extracting topological features, and training classifiers — evaluated on three datasets (English, Portuguese).

- Contribution: Introduces enriched complex network features to handle short, disfluent clinical transcripts as an alternative to standard linguistic features.

- Contribution: Provides thorough empirical evaluation across three diverse datasets and shows gains from multi-view learning combining heterogeneous feature sets.

- Strength: Novel, well-motivated method that is robust to parsing failures and language-agnostic, using embeddings to mitigate sparsity in short texts.

- Strength: Comprehensive experimental setup covering languages, tasks, and both automated and manually corrected transcripts to demonstrate robustness.

- Strength: Strong empirical results with CNE features often outperforming linguistic baselines and notable improvements from multi-view ensembles (e.g., +10% on Cinderella, +4.7% on ABCD).

- Weakness: No analysis of which network topological features are most discriminative, limiting interpretability and clinical/linguistic insights.

- Weakness: Bag-of-Words baseline performs surprisingly well (best single-feature-set on ABCD), but the paper does not analyze why, weakening the case for the complex method.

- Weakness: Missing methodological details that hurt reproducibility, especially regarding word embedding training (pre-trained vs in-domain).

- Weakness: Missing explanation of hyperparameter selection (cosine similarity thresholds for adding semantic edges) and whether they were tuned on development sets.

- Weakness: Insufficient positioning against prior state-of-the-art on well-known datasets (e.g., lower accuracy on Cookie Theft vs reported past results) and lack of direct comparison/contextualization.

- Question: Were the word embeddings pre-trained on large external corpora or trained only on the small in-domain transcripts?

- Question: How were the cosine similarity thresholds for adding semantic edges chosen — tuned on a dev set or set heuristically?

- Question: Any hypotheses why BoW is especially predictive for the short-retelling ABCD task (e.g., lexical poverty, repetitions)?

- Question: Have you performed feature importance or ablation analyses to identify which network metrics (e.g., centrality, clustering, path length) best predict MCI?
