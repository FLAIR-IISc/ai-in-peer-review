- Summary: Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts by extracting topological features from complex networks enriched with semantic edges from word embeddings, evaluated on three datasets with CNE and multi-view models outperforming traditional features.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a robust feature-extraction method using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to avoid reliance on fragile syntactic parsers for noisy clinical text.

- Contribution 2: Provides a comprehensive empirical study across three datasets (English and Portuguese) demonstrating effectiveness and benefits of multi-view learning.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated solution that directly addresses the limitations of standard NLP tools on disfluent clinical transcripts.

- Strength 2: Strong experimental validation across datasets and baselines, with multi-view models achieving up to ~80% accuracy.

- Strength 3: High practical potential as a scalable, automated, language-agnostic tool for MCI screening.

- Weakness 1: No analysis of which network topological features are most discriminative; lacks feature-importance or explanatory insight.

- Weakness 2: The strong Bag-of-Words baseline on ABCD (75.5%) is not sufficiently analyzed, and it sometimes outperforms standalone CNE.

- Weakness 3: Reproducibility concerns about word embeddings—the paper omits the corpus/training details and should prefer or report public pre-trained models.
