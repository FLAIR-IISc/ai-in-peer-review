- Novel method: represent speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with semantic edges from word embeddings to detect MCI.  
- Pipeline: extract topological features from enriched networks and train classifiers; evaluated on English Cookie Theft and two Portuguese datasets (Cinderella, ABCD).  
- Contribution: introduces enriched complex networks for clinical NLP, addressing short/disfluent texts.  
- Contribution: provides a graph-based feature set robust to agrammatical speech that avoids reliance on syntactic parsing.  
- Contribution: validates approach across multiple languages and narrative tasks, strengthening generalizability claims.  
- Strength: clear motivation and sound methodology; enrichment with embeddings is well-justified for short texts.  
- Strength: thorough experiments comparing CNE to linguistic features and BoW, testing multiple classifiers and ensemble/multi-view approaches.  
- Strength: strong empirical results—CNE often competitive or better than baselines; multi-view yields substantial gains (e.g., +10% on Cinderella; 80% on ABCD).  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on word embeddings—training corpus and per-language models not specified.  
- Weakness: unclear how cosine similarity threshold for adding semantic edges was optimized and whether tuning leaked into final evaluation.  
- Weakness: limited analysis/interpretation—no feature importance, network-level comparisons, or illustrative network visualizations to explain why the method works.  
- Weakness: clarity of baseline comparisons—LMs beat CNE on cleaned Cinderella and BoW outperforms CNE on ABCD, needing discussion of these trade-offs.
