- Represents transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with semantic edges from word embeddings to detect MCI.
- Uses topological features of these enriched networks to train classifiers, evaluated on three datasets (English + Portuguese).
- Shows enriched complex-network features often outperform linguistic features and Bag-of-Words baselines.
- Demonstrates that multi-view feature combination yields further performance improvements.
- Contribution: novel method that densifies sparse co-occurrence graphs with embedding-based semantic edges, combining syntagmatic and paradigmatic info.
- Contribution: comprehensive empirical validation across three datasets, two languages, and multiple baselines/classifiers.
- Strength: clever, well-motivated solution to short-text sparsity that unifies structural and semantic relations.
- Strength: robust to agrammatical, disfluent clinical speech since it avoids heavy reliance on parsing.
- Strength: thorough experimental setup with diverse datasets, baselines, and combination techniques.
- Strength: practical clinical relevance for scalable, low-cost MCI screening without extensive manual correction.
- Weakness: crucial cosine-similarity threshold hyperparameter lacks description of selection procedure and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: missing details on the training corpora/source/domain for the English and Portuguese word embeddings.
- Weakness: no feature-importance analysis among the many network measures to identify the most discriminative features.
- Weakness: improvements reported without statistical significance testing, problematic given small dataset sizes.
- Question: clarify how the cosine similarity threshold was selected and whether tuning avoided data leakage (e.g., nested validation).
- Question: provide full details on corpora used to train the English and Portuguese embeddings (source, domain, size).
- Question: explain trade-offs between the proposed network approach and the strong linear SVM Bag-of-Words baseline (when extra complexity is justified).
