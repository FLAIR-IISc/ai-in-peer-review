- Paper proposes using complex networks enriched with word embeddings as features to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts.
- Experiments compare network-based features to linguistic features and bag-of-words on English and Portuguese datasets.
- Combining network features with other feature types yields improved classification accuracy.
- Main contribution: applying complex network representations to MCI detection, a novel application in this domain.
- Main contribution: cross-lingual evaluation on English and Portuguese datasets to test generalizability.
- Main contribution: thorough comparative analysis against baseline feature sets.
- Strength: novel approach by coupling complex networks with word embeddings for this clinical task.
- Strength: rigorous experimental setup with multiple datasets, algorithms, cross-validation, and both ASR and manual transcripts.
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with supplementary material aiding reproducibility.
- Strength: practical relevance for automated early detection of MCI from speech.
- Strength: competitive performance, with meaningful accuracy gains when features are combined.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty in network construction (co-occurrence + embeddings) — novelty mainly in application.
- Weakness: insufficient justification for choice of specific topological network metrics and unclear feature selection strategy.
- Weakness: small dataset sizes, especially Portuguese, limit generalizability and statistical power.
- Weakness: lack of detail on how hyperparameters and threshold parameters were optimized.
- Weakness: limited interpretability—paper does not explain why network features distinguish MCI from controls.
- Question: provide details on hyperparameter optimization and threshold selection per dataset.
- Question: explain rationale for selecting the ten topological metrics and whether feature selection was used.
- Question: comment on robustness given small datasets and ways to increase statistical power.
- Question: describe any attempts to interpret network structures and identify distinguishing patterns.
- Question: disclose potential dataset biases (demographics, narrative types) affecting generalizability.
- Additional: discuss limitations related to data quality (e.g., ASR errors) and complexity of impaired language.
- Additional: suggest exploring alternative embeddings and network construction methods in future work.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
