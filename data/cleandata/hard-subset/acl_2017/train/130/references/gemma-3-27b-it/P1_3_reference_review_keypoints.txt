- Overall: Interesting application of complex networks + embeddings to MCI detection with broad experiments, but limited novelty and only marginal gains over baselines.

- Contribution: Demonstrates feasibility of using complex network analysis (enriched with word embeddings) for MCI detection from speech transcripts.

- Contribution: Evaluates the method across three datasets (English and Portuguese), showing adaptability to different languages and tasks.

- Contribution: Systematically explores combinations of network-based, linguistic, and bag-of-words features to identify effective feature sets.

- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple datasets and elicitation methods improves generalizability.

- Strength: Methodological rigor via 5-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers (SVM, KNN, RF, GaussianNB).

- Strength: Clear problem motivation highlighting importance of early MCI detection through language analysis.

- Strength: Valuable exploration of enriching complex networks with word embeddings versus networks alone.

- Strength: Detailed feature descriptions (including Coh-Metrix) support reproducibility.

- Weakness: Lack of novelty—complex networks for text and embedding enrichment are incremental rather than a major advance.

- Weakness: Performance improvements are often marginal compared to baselines, questioning practical impact.

- Weakness: Verbose and repetitive presentation; some background/tool descriptions (e.g., Coh-Metrix) could be shortened.

- Weakness: Limited error analysis—paper does not sufficiently examine why models fail or which linguistic patterns are missed.

- Weakness: Small and potentially imbalanced datasets are not adequately addressed, raising concerns about generalizability.

- Question: Please clarify what specific aspects of the approach are novel relative to prior complex-network and NLP work in clinical text.

- Question: Were statistical significance tests performed (p-values or confidence intervals) to validate observed improvements?

- Question: Can you provide a detailed error analysis identifying common misclassification patterns?

- Question: How were dataset imbalance issues handled, and how might imbalance have affected results?

- Question: How sensitive are results to embedding and network-construction parameters (embedding dim, window size, similarity threshold)?

- Additional: Improve table formatting for readability.

- Additional: Include visualizations of complex networks to illustrate the effect of embedding enrichment.

- Additional: Reorganize supplemental material for better clarity and accessibility.
