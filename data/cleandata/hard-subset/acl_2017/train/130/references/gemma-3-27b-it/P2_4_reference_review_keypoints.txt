- Paper applies complex network models enriched with word embeddings to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts.  
- Evaluated method on three datasets (English + two Portuguese) showing network features are competitive and complementary to traditional features.  
- Main contribution: novel application of embedding-enriched complex networks to MCI detection.  
- Main contribution: cross-lingual evaluation demonstrating potential generalizability and language-specific challenges.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive comparison of network-based, linguistic, and bag-of-words features.  
- Strength: approach is a valuable exploration beyond standard NLP features for this clinical task.  
- Strength: rigorous experimental setup using three diverse datasets and 5-fold cross-validation.  
- Strength: thorough feature analysis showing combined features yield best performance.  
- Strength: clear presentation of results and well-structured discussion across datasets.  
- Strength: practical relevance for early, automated MCI detection from speech.  
- Weakness: network modeling (co-occurrence + embeddings) is not fundamentally novel.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed ablation to isolate contributions of individual network metrics.  
- Weakness: dataset sizes are small and class-imbalanced, limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: inconsistent preprocessing (manual vs automatic) across datasets may confound results.  
- Weakness: limited error analysis—paper focuses mainly on overall accuracy without detailed misclassification insights.  
- Question: what specific challenges arose applying the method to Portuguese vs English, and were feature/classifier effects language-dependent?  
- Question: have data augmentation or cost-sensitive methods been considered to address small, imbalanced datasets?  
- Question: how was the cosine similarity threshold for embedding-based edges chosen and how sensitive are results to it?  
- Question: what is the computational complexity and scalability of generating enriched network representations for larger corpora?  
- Question: which linguistic errors or disfluencies were most predictive of MCI according to their feature analysis?  
- Additional: reference list is extensive and supplementary material is helpful.  
- Additional: writing quality is generally good but some sections could be more concise.  
- Overall assessment: valuable exploratory work with solid empirical evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing analysis, dataset, and methodological concerns.
