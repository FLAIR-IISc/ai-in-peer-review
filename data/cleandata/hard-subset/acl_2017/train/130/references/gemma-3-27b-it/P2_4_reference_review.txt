## Review for ACL Submission: "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the use of complex network models, enriched with word embeddings, for the detection of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. The authors explore various features extracted from these networks, alongside traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representations, employing several machine learning classifiers.  They demonstrate that network-based features can achieve competitive performance, and that combining these features with traditional methods yields the best results. The work is evaluated on three datasets – English and two Portuguese datasets – with varying characteristics.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Application of Complex Networks to MCI Detection:** The primary contribution is the application of complex network modeling, enhanced by word embeddings, to the specific task of detecting MCI from speech.  This represents an exploration of a relatively novel approach within this domain.
2. **Cross-Lingual Evaluation:** The authors evaluate their method on both English and Portuguese datasets, showcasing the potential generalizability (and highlighting challenges) of the approach across different languages.
3. **Comprehensive Feature Comparison:**  The systematic comparison of network-based features against established linguistic features and BoW representations provides valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each approach for this task.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Approach:** While complex networks have been used in NLP, their application to MCI detection from speech transcripts, particularly with the enrichment of word embeddings, is a valuable exploration. The authors appropriately motivate this direction, citing limitations of traditional methods and the potential of network-based representations.
2. **Rigorous Experimental Setup:** The use of three datasets – differing in size, language, and acquisition method – strengthens the generalizability of the findings. The use of 5-fold cross-validation is also commendable.
3. **Comprehensive Feature Analysis:** The inclusion of a wide range of features (network topology, linguistic features, and BoW) allows for a nuanced comparison and identifies the complementary strengths of different approaches. The finding that combining features yields the best performance is particularly valuable.
4. **Clear Presentation of Results:** The tables presenting the classification accuracy are clear and easy to understand. The discussion of results across datasets is also well-structured.
5. **Practical Relevance:**  Early detection of MCI is crucial for intervention, and automated methods based on readily available speech transcripts hold significant practical promise.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Network Modeling:** The core network modeling technique (word co-occurrence) is well-established.  The enrichment with word embeddings, while beneficial, is also a commonly used technique.  The paper doesn't present any fundamentally new advancements in network construction or analysis.
2. **Lack of Detailed Ablation Study:** While the paper shows the performance of different feature combinations, it lacks a detailed ablation study to specifically isolate the contribution of each network-based metric.  It would be helpful to know which topological properties are most indicative of MCI.
3. **Dataset Imbalance & Size:**  The datasets, particularly the English and Portuguese datasets, are relatively small and exhibit imbalances between the control and MCI groups. This could influence the results and limit the generalizability of the findings.  A more thorough discussion of this limitation is needed.
4. **Manual Preprocessing in Some Datasets:** The differing levels of preprocessing across datasets (manual revision in some, automatic segmentation in others) introduces a potential confounding factor. The authors acknowledge this but a more detailed discussion of its impact would be beneficial.
5. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper primarily focuses on overall classification accuracy. A more detailed error analysis, examining specific types of misclassifications, would provide valuable insights into the limitations of the approach and potential areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the specific challenges encountered when applying the method to the Portuguese datasets, compared to the English dataset?  Were there any noticeable differences in the effectiveness of different features or classifiers across languages?
2.  Given the small dataset sizes and potential imbalances, have you considered using techniques such as data augmentation or cost-sensitive learning to mitigate these issues?
3.  Could you provide a more detailed explanation of the choice of cosine similarity threshold for adding edges based on word embeddings?  Was this threshold optimized, and how sensitive are the results to this parameter?
4.  Could you discuss the computational complexity of generating the enriched network representations, particularly for larger datasets?  Is this approach scalable to real-world applications with more extensive speech corpora?
5.  Can you elaborate on the specific linguistic errors or disfluencies that are most predictive of MCI, based on your analysis of the linguistic features?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The reference list is extensive.
*   The supplementary material is helpful.
*   The writing quality is generally good, although some sections could benefit from more concise phrasing.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable exploration of complex network modeling for MCI detection. While the core methodology isn't radically new, the application to this specific domain, the cross-lingual evaluation, and the comprehensive feature comparison are significant contributions. The weaknesses primarily relate to the lack of in-depth analysis and the limitations of the datasets. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, addressing the questions and weaknesses outlined above. Addressing these points will significantly strengthen the paper and enhance its impact.
