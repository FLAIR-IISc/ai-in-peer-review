## Review for ACL Submission: "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts"

**Summary:** This paper investigates the use of complex networks, enriched with word embeddings, as a feature representation for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. The authors compare these network-based features with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representations, evaluating performance on three datasets: a manually transcribed English dataset, and two Portuguese datasets (one manually revised and one automatically segmented).  They find that enriched complex networks perform competitively with other approaches, and that combining feature sets yields the best results.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Application of Complex Networks to MCI Detection:** This paper provides a novel application of complex network analysis to the problem of MCI detection from speech, demonstrating the potential of this approach for capturing subtle linguistic patterns.
2. **Empirical Evaluation on Multiple Datasets:** The evaluation across three datasets (English and Portuguese, manually and automatically transcribed) offers a reasonably comprehensive assessment of the method's generalizability and robustness to data quality.
3. **Demonstration of Feature Combination Benefits:** The study confirms that combining network-based features with traditional linguistic features improves classification accuracy, providing practical guidance for future research.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty of Approach:** While complex networks have been used in NLP, their application to the specific task of MCI detection from *speech transcripts*, especially with the enrichment of word embeddings, is a valuable contribution. The motivation for circumventing the challenges of manual feature engineering and handling disfluencies is well-articulated.
2. **Comprehensive Feature Comparison:** The paper provides a fair comparison against established baselines (linguistic features, BoW), allowing a clear understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed method.  The exploration of different feature combinations is particularly insightful.
3. **Multi-Dataset Evaluation:**  Evaluating the methodology across different languages and transcription qualities strengthens the findings. The inclusion of a dataset with automatic segmentation is particularly forward-looking, aiming toward a fully automated system.
4. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly structured. The methodology is explained in detail, and the experimental setup is well-defined. Figures illustrating the network enrichment are helpful.
5. **Good Baseline Performance:** The achieved accuracies are comparable to, and in some cases exceed, those reported in other studies in the field, demonstrating the potential of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Network Methodology:** The core network construction method (word co-occurrence) is well-established. The primary novelty lies in its application to this specific problem and the use of word embeddings for enrichment.  However, the embedding enrichment itself isn't a particularly novel technique – it's a standard practice.  More detailed justification for the specific network topology choices would be helpful.
2. **Superficial Analysis of Network Features:** The paper uses a large number of network metrics, but the analysis of *why* certain metrics are more predictive than others is lacking.  A deeper exploration of which network properties are most strongly correlated with MCI could provide valuable insights into the underlying cognitive mechanisms.
3. **Dataset Imbalance & Size:** The datasets are relatively small, and, in some cases, exhibit imbalance between control and MCI groups. This could limit the generalizability of the findings.  The impact of dataset size and imbalance on the results should be discussed more thoroughly.
4. **Lack of Statistical Significance Testing:** While cross-validation is used, the paper does not report statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) to compare the performance of different methods. This makes it difficult to confidently assess whether the observed differences are statistically meaningful.
5. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:**  The paper does not present any error analysis. Understanding *which* types of errors the model makes (e.g., misclassifying specific linguistic patterns) could guide future improvements.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Network Topology Justification:**  Why was the word co-occurrence network chosen specifically? Were alternative network topologies (e.g., based on dependency parsing) considered and, if so, why were they not adopted?
2. **Feature Importance Analysis:**  Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the most informative network metrics?  Are there specific network properties that consistently differentiate between MCI and control subjects?
3. **Impact of Disfluencies:**  You mention that the method is designed to be robust to disfluencies. Can you provide any quantitative evidence to support this claim?  Did you compare performance on transcripts with and without disfluencies removed?
4. **Statistical Significance:** Can you provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) to support your claims about the superiority of different methods?
5. **Error Analysis:** Can you present a brief error analysis, highlighting examples of misclassified narratives and the potential reasons for these errors?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work in the application of network science to clinical language analysis.
*   Consider providing code availability to facilitate reproducibility.
*   Clarify which version of the Cinderella dataset was used for the manually processed results (Table 6) to differentiate it from the automatically processed version.



**Overall:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a promising approach to MCI detection using complex networks. While the core methodology isn’t groundbreaking, the application to this specific problem, the comprehensive evaluation, and the demonstration of feature combination benefits are valuable contributions. Addressing the identified weaknesses and responding to the questions raised would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.
