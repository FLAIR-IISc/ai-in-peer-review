- Summary: Investigates complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, modeling transcripts as co-occurrence + semantic networks and testing multiple datasets, classifiers, and feature combinations.

- Contribution 1: Applies complex network analysis to MCI detection, offering a complementary approach to traditional linguistic methods.

- Contribution 2: Shows that enriching co-occurrence networks with word embeddings improves classification performance.

- Contribution 3: Evaluates the method across English and Portuguese datasets, including public and custom corpora, to assess generalizability.

- Strength 1: Novel application of network-based methods combined with embeddings to a clinical NLP task.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive experimental setup with multiple datasets, algorithms, and feature combinations.

- Strength 3: Clear, well-organized presentation of results facilitating comparisons across configurations.

- Strength 4: Practical focus on a fully automated system that avoids manual preprocessing.

- Weakness 1: Individual method components (networks, embeddings, classifiers) are established techniques, so novelty is mainly in the combination/application.

- Weakness 2: Performance gains are moderate and comparable to existing literature, raising questions about added complexity.

- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed error analysis identifying which transcripts or linguistic patterns are misclassified.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient justification for choice and dimensionality of the specific word embedding model used.

- Question 1: Request for detailed error analysis identifying transcript types and linguistic features linked to misclassification.

- Question 2: Ask for information on computational cost and practicality of building/analyzing networks for larger transcripts or real-time use.

- Question 3: Want quantification of benefits from avoiding dependency parsing for Portuguese and comparison to simpler parsing methods.

- Question 4: Ask about the impact of the cosine similarity threshold for embedding-based edges and whether different thresholds were explored.

- Additional comment 1: Supplementary material is helpful for understanding datasets and features.

- Additional comment 2: Writing is generally clear and concise.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed study with promising results but limited novelty and moderate gains; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses and questions.
