This paper explores the use of complex networks, enriched with word embeddings, for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. The authors motivate their work by highlighting the challenges of traditional NLP approaches to this task, particularly dealing with disfluencies and the need for manually created linguistic resources. The core idea – representing transcripts as networks and augmenting them with semantic information from word embeddings – is interesting and potentially valuable for automated MCI detection. The evaluation on multiple datasets, including English and Portuguese, is also a positive aspect.

**Main Contributions:**

1. **Novel Application of Network Enrichment:** The primary contribution is the application of word embedding enrichment to complex networks for the specific task of MCI detection from speech transcripts. While complex networks have been used in NLP, integrating word embeddings to address the limitations of short texts and noisy data is a worthwhile exploration.
2. **Multi-Dataset Evaluation:** The evaluation across three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, and ABCD) in different languages (English and Portuguese) provides a more robust assessment of the proposed approach than evaluation on a single dataset. This demonstrates the potential generalizability of the method.
3. **Comparative Analysis of Feature Types:** The comparison of complex network features with traditional linguistic features and Bag-of-Words representations provides insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each approach in the context of MCI detection. The exploration of ensemble and multi-view learning further adds to this comparative analysis.

**Strengths:**

1. **Relevant Problem & Motivation:** The detection of MCI is a clinically important problem, and the authors effectively motivate the need for automated approaches that can overcome the limitations of existing methods.
2. **Sound Methodology:** The methodology of constructing word co-occurrence networks and enriching them with word embeddings is clearly explained and follows established techniques. The use of topological network measures is well-justified.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The use of multiple datasets, several classification algorithms (SVM, KNN, RF, GNB), and ensemble/multi-view learning provides a thorough evaluation of the proposed method and its comparison to baselines.
4. **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner, with tables summarizing the performance on different datasets and feature combinations.
5. **Consideration of Real-World Data:** The inclusion of both manually transcribed and automatically segmented data (ABCD dataset) reflects a realistic approach to building a practical system.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Network Approach:** While applying complex networks to MCI detection is reasonable, the core idea of using network representations in NLP isn't entirely novel. The real novelty lies in the *enrichment* with word embeddings, but this aspect could be more prominently highlighted and contrasted with previous work using similar network-based approaches.
2. **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The paper mentions optimization of the threshold parameter but lacks detail regarding the tuning of other hyperparameters for the machine learning algorithms and the word embedding model. More information about the hyperparameter search process would strengthen the reproducibility and reliability of the results.
3. **Lack of Error Analysis:** The results are presented in terms of overall accuracy, but a more detailed error analysis would be valuable. Investigating specific types of errors (e.g., false positives vs. false negatives) and identifying the types of transcripts that are most challenging for the model could provide insights for future improvement.
4. **Word Embedding Details:** The description of the word embedding model (Bojanowski et al., 2016) is brief. Providing more details about the training data and specific parameters used for generating the embeddings would be helpful.  How well does this model handle out-of-vocabulary words, a common occurrence in spontaneous speech?
5. **Statistical Significance:** While tables report accuracy scores, there is no explicit mention of statistical significance testing to determine whether the observed differences between models are statistically significant.  Including p-values or confidence intervals would be beneficial.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the rationale for choosing the specific topological network measures used in your analysis? Did you explore other measures, and if so, why were these selected?
2.  Please provide more details about the hyperparameter tuning process. What ranges of hyperparameters were explored, and what search strategy was used?
3.  Can you provide an example of a transcript that was misclassified by your best-performing model, along with a discussion of why it might have been misclassified?
4.  How sensitive are your results to the choice of word embedding model? Have you experimented with other pre-trained embeddings?
5.  What steps are being taken to address the issue of imbalanced datasets, which might exist within the datasets used (e.g. different sizes of MCI vs Control groups)?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The supplementary material is helpful in providing details about the datasets and Coh-Metrix features.
*   The reference list is comprehensive.
*   Consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the study and future research directions beyond those already mentioned.



Overall, this is a solid paper with a well-motivated research question, a sound methodology, and a comprehensive evaluation. While the novelty of the network approach itself is limited, the application to MCI detection and the enrichment with word embeddings are valuable contributions. Addressing the weaknesses identified above, particularly providing more details about hyperparameter tuning, conducting error analysis, and including statistical significance testing, would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
