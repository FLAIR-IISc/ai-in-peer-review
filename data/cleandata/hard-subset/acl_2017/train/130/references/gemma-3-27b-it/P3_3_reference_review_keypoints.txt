- Paper applies complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, showing competitive performance and gains from combining feature types across three datasets.
- Main contribution: novel use of complex network analysis for MCI detection from speech.
- Main contribution: cross-lingual evaluation on English and two Portuguese datasets to assess generalizability.
- Main contribution: comprehensive comparison of network-based features against linguistic features and bag-of-words.
- Strength: approach is novel and addresses limitations of hand-crafted rules and disfluencies.
- Strength: strong experimental setup using three distinct datasets and 5-fold cross-validation.
- Strength: promising results where enriched networks are competitive or superior and fuse well with other features.
- Strength: clear presentation and useful supplementary material aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited interpretability—paper lacks analysis of which network properties drive MCI detection.
- Weakness: reliance on a single embedding (FastText) with default parameters and no sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: small datasets and class imbalance raise concerns about bias and robustness.
- Weakness: inconsistent preprocessing across datasets (manual vs. automatic) complicates comparisons.
- Question: can authors show specific network features or patterns that differentiate MCI from controls?
- Question: were other embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe) or hyperparameter settings tested and how did they affect results?
- Question: have data augmentation techniques been considered to mitigate small sample size and imbalance?
- Question: how did differing preprocessing steps impact results and what was done to mitigate this?
- Additional: expand related work on network-based clinical language analysis beyond general NLP.
- Additional: improve notation consistency and better organize supplementary material.
- Overall: novel and promising work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address interpretability, embedding sensitivity, and dataset/preprocessing limitations.
