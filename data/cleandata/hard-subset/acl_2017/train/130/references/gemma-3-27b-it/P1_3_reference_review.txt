## Peer Review for ACL

**Paper Title:** Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts

**Overall Assessment:** This paper explores the application of complex networks, enhanced with word embeddings, for the detection of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. While the idea is interesting and the experimental setup covers multiple datasets and algorithms, the paper suffers from a lack of significant novelty and a somewhat verbose presentation. The results, while promising, are not groundbreaking and the improvements over baseline methods are often marginal.

**1. Summary & Contributions:**

The paper investigates whether modeling speech transcripts as complex networks, and enriching those networks with word embedding information, can improve the accuracy of MCI detection. The authors leverage existing datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella story, ABCD battery) and combine network-based features with traditional linguistic and statistical features. 

The primary contributions, as *I* see them, are:

1. **Application of Complex Networks to MCI Detection:** The paper demonstrates a feasible application of complex network analysis to a challenging clinical problem. While not entirely novel (complex networks are used in NLP), applying this specifically to MCI detection from spoken language is a worthwhile exploration.
2. **Dataset Exploration:** The paper presents results across three datasets – English and Portuguese – offering a comparative analysis and showcasing the method's adaptability.
3. **Feature Combination:** The systematic exploration of combining network-based features with traditional linguistic and bag-of-words features provides insights into the most effective feature sets for MCI detection.



**2. Strengths:**

1. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors conducted experiments on three different datasets, varying in language and elicitation method. This breadth of evaluation strengthens the generalizability of the findings.
2. **Methodological Rigor:** The use of 5-fold cross-validation and multiple machine learning algorithms (SVM, KNN, RF, GaussianNB) is commendable and provides a robust evaluation framework.
3. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The introduction clearly articulates the importance of early MCI detection and the potential of language analysis for this purpose.
4. **Exploration of Feature Engineering:** The investigation into enriching complex networks with word embeddings, and the subsequent comparison to using networks alone, is a valuable contribution.
5. **Detailed Feature Description:** The paper provides a thorough list of the features used, including the Coh-Metrix features, which allows for reproducibility and understanding of the analysis.

**3. Weaknesses:**

1. **Lack of Novelty:** The core idea of using complex networks for text analysis is not new. The contribution of enriching them with word embeddings, while logical, is also relatively straightforward and doesn't represent a significant breakthrough.
2. **Marginal Improvements:** While the results are promising, the performance gains achieved by using complex networks (even enriched ones) are often small compared to the baseline methods. This raises questions about the actual practical benefit of the proposed approach.
3. **Verbose Presentation:** The paper is quite lengthy and repetitive in certain sections. The description of datasets and methods could be more concise without sacrificing clarity.  For instance, detailed descriptions of existing tools like Coh-Metrix could be shortened, referencing the original publications.
4. **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the types of errors made by the classifiers. Understanding *why* the models fail in certain cases would provide valuable insights for future improvement.  Are certain linguistic patterns missed? Are certain types of disfluencies problematic?
5. **Data Imbalance:** The datasets are relatively small, and potentially imbalanced. This could affect the performance and generalizability of the models.  The authors do not discuss this issue sufficiently.



**4. Questions to Authors:**

1. **Novelty Justification:** Can you elaborate on what specific aspects of your approach are novel compared to existing work on complex networks and NLP for clinical text analysis?
2. **Statistical Significance:** Were statistical significance tests performed on the results? Please provide p-values or confidence intervals to demonstrate the reliability of the observed improvements.
3. **Error Analysis:** Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, identifying common patterns in misclassified samples?
4. **Dataset Considerations:**  Did you address potential issues related to data imbalance in the datasets? If so, how? If not, how might data imbalance have impacted the results?
5. **Parameter Sensitivity:** How sensitive are the results to the choice of parameters used for building the word embeddings (e.g., embedding dimension, context window size) and for constructing the complex networks (e.g., cosine similarity threshold)?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The formatting of the tables could be improved for better readability.
*   Consider providing a more visual representation of the complex networks themselves to illustrate the impact of word embedding enrichment.
*   The supplementary material is useful but could be better organized.
