- Paper proposes using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, comparing against traditional linguistic and BoW features.  
- Evaluation across English and Portuguese datasets shows enriched networks are competitive and sometimes superior, especially in multi-view combinations.  
- Main contribution: applying enriched complex networks to MCI detection, a novel application in this domain.  
- Main contribution: cross-lingual evaluation increases potential generalizability beyond single-language studies.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates complementary benefits when combining network features with traditional linguistic features.  
- Strength: novel combination of complex networks and embeddings opens a new research avenue for MCI detection.  
- Strength: thorough experiments (multiple datasets, algorithms, 5-fold CV, manual vs automatic segmentation) improve result reliability.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly described, and results are presented concisely.  
- Strength: clinically relevant task—automated methods reducing manual feature engineering are valuable.  
- Weakness: individual components are established techniques; novelty is mainly their combination and deeper justification is missing.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis to explain misclassifications and feature shortcomings.  
- Weakness: small datasets and potential demographic imbalances (e.g., more males in MCI group) may limit generalizability; augmentation or imbalance handling not explored.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter choice (cosine similarity threshold for enrichment) lacks justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Question: please elaborate which linguistic features/patterns the complex network representation captures relevant to MCI detection.  
- Question: provide a detailed error analysis outlining misclassification types and causes.  
- Question: how confident are you in generalizability given small/imbalanced datasets; have you considered data augmentation or imbalance strategies?  
- Question: justify the chosen cosine similarity threshold and report sensitivity to different values.  
- Additional: references are extensive and well-formatted.  
- Additional: supplementary material (example transcript, Coh-Metrix details) is useful.  
- Additional: consider adding a discussion explicitly comparing the approach to other state-of-the-art MCI detection methods.  
- Overall: recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on addressing justification, error analysis, and dataset/parameter limitations.
