- Applies complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts, a clinically relevant task.  
- Systematically explores combinations of network-based features, linguistic features, and bag-of-words to identify effective feature sets.  
- Evaluates on multiple datasets (English & Portuguese; Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) to test generalizability.  
- Well-motivated problem with clear importance for early MCI detection.  
- Methodologically reasonable: enriching complex networks with word embeddings builds on existing NLP and network-science ideas.  
- Uses a comprehensive feature set including topological network measures, linguistic features, and BoW.  
- Employs standard, rigorous evaluation via 5-fold cross-validation.  
- Results are clearly presented in organized tables for easy comparison.  
- Limited novelty: approach is incremental and mainly an application rather than a substantive methodological advance.  
- Performance varies significantly across datasets, raising generalizability concerns linked to dataset heterogeneity.  
- Manual revisions to the Cinderella dataset may introduce bias and affect validity.  
- Lacks comparison to strong, established state-of-the-art baselines for MCI detection from speech.  
- Insufficient ablation study — unclear how much performance gains derive from embeddings vs. network structure or threshold choices.  
- Paper is densely written and sometimes obscures core contributions; presentation could be streamlined.  
- Requests clarification on causes of dataset-specific performance differences and ways to harmonize datasets.  
- Asks for ablation results (remove embeddings, vary cosine threshold) to quantify contributions.  
- Requests direct comparison to established MCI detection methods and a feature-importance analysis for network measures.  
- Wants clarification on the effect of manual dataset revisions on results and generalizability.  
- Suggests reorganizing supplementary material, adding limitations and future work, and specifying embedding implementation details (training data, dimensionality).  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after stronger baselines, deeper analyses, and clearer articulation of contributions.
