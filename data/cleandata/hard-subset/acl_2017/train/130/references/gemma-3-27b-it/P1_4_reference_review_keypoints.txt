- Models speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, evaluated on three datasets (English Cookie Theft, Portuguese Cinderella and ABCD).  
- Applies complex network analysis to MCI detection, offering a novel representation compared to traditional linguistic features.  
- Evaluates the approach across English and two Portuguese datasets, including manual and automatic transcriptions.  
- Shows that combining network-derived features with traditional linguistic/statistical features improves classification accuracy.  
- Novelty: using complex networks for transcript representation and MCI detection is a genuinely new application in this domain.  
- Uses a comprehensive feature set combining topological network metrics, linguistic features, and bag-of-words representations.  
- Employs a solid experimental setup with 5-fold cross-validation and baseline comparisons.  
- Conducts multilingual evaluation, strengthening claims about generalizability across languages.  
- Practical focus on creating a fully automated system with reduced manual preprocessing.  
- Network enrichment via cosine similarity of word embeddings is standard and not novel in how embeddings are integrated.  
- Relies heavily on existing tools (e.g., Coh-Metrix) and lacks deeper linguistic analysis linking network properties to MCI-related impairments.  
- Small and imbalanced datasets (fewer MCI participants) may limit reliability and generalizability of results.  
- Hyperparameter tuning (similarity thresholds 0.7 and 0.4) appears ad-hoc and lacks systematic justification.  
- Limited interpretability: paper doesn’t identify which specific network features most strongly indicate MCI or their cognitive relevance.  
- Requests clarification on how threshold values were chosen (validation, grid search, etc.).  
- Asks whether alternative embedding integration methods or similarity metrics were tested and how they compared.  
- Requests feature importance or ablation analysis to identify the most predictive network metrics.  
- Asks if statistical significance tests were performed to validate reported performance improvements.  
- Queries sensitivity of results to handling of disfluencies and whether removing them was evaluated.  
- Notes the supplementary material is helpful and writing is clear, but some sections need more detailed explanations.  
- Suggests the literature review could better emphasize recent advances in complex network analysis for biomedical applications.
