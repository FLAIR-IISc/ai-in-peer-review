- Paper investigates complex network models enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, comparing to linguistic features and BoW across three datasets.

- Contribution: Introduces complex network (word adjacency) models with embeddings for MCI detection, a novel approach in the field.

- Contribution: Evaluates method cross-lingually on English and Portuguese datasets, suggesting potential generalizability.

- Contribution: Provides empirical comparison against baselines and explores feature combinations showing complementary gains.

- Strength: Novel methodology combining network structure and embeddings addresses semantic limitations of standard network analysis.

- Strength: Rigorous experiments using multiple datasets, classifiers, and cross-validation, including automatic vs manual transcription scenarios.

- Strength: Clear presentation with comparative tables and ablation (CN vs CNE) analyses.

- Strength: Clinically relevant goal toward automated MCI detection and emphasis on automated pipelines.

- Strength: Detailed dataset descriptions improve reproducibility and contextual understanding.

- Weakness: Lacks deeper analysis of which specific network properties drive predictive performance.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion on dependence and potential biases of pre-trained embeddings; domain-specific embeddings not thoroughly explored.

- Weakness: Simple disfluency preprocessing; more sophisticated detection/handling and discussion of dataset differences are missing.

- Weakness: Results reported mostly as accuracy without statistical significance testing.

- Question: Which network features (e.g., centrality, clustering) are most predictive of MCI status?

- Question: How sensitive are results to choice of embeddings, and were domain-trained embeddings tried?

- Question: What is the rationale for the chosen disfluency removal strategy, and were more advanced methods considered?

- Question: Can the authors provide statistical significance testing for reported accuracy differences?

- Question: How will performance scale with larger datasets and how will overfitting be mitigated?

- Additional comment: Some references are formatted incorrectly and need fixing.

- Additional comment: Recommend adding discussion of ethical implications and responsible deployment of automated MCI diagnosis.

- Overall: Solid and promising work with recommendation for acceptance contingent on addressing the reviewer’s concerns.
