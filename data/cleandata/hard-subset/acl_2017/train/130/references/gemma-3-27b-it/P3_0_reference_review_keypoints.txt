- Summary: Proposes using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, evaluated on three English/Portuguese datasets, finding competitive performance and benefit from feature combinations.
- Contribution 1: Novel application of complex network analysis to MCI detection from speech transcripts.
- Contribution 2: Empirical evaluation across three datasets (English + two Portuguese; manual and automatic transcriptions).
- Contribution 3: Shows combining network-based features with traditional linguistic features improves classification.
- Strength 1: Novelty in applying enriched complex networks to a clinical speech task and mitigating manual feature engineering/disfluencies.
- Strength 2: Fair, comprehensive comparison against baselines (linguistic features, BoW) with various feature combinations.
- Strength 3: Multi-dataset evaluation demonstrates robustness to language and transcription quality, including automatic segmentation.
- Strength 4: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures and detailed methodology.
- Strength 5: Achieved accuracies comparable to or exceeding prior studies, indicating practical potential.
- Weakness 1: Limited methodological novelty—standard word co-occurrence networks and common embedding enrichment.
- Weakness 2: Superficial analysis of why specific network metrics are predictive; lacks deeper interpretation.
- Weakness 3: Small and sometimes imbalanced datasets may limit generalizability; impact not fully discussed.
- Weakness 4: No statistical significance testing reported to validate performance differences.
- Weakness 5: No error analysis provided to understand model misclassifications and failure modes.
- Question 1: Why choose word co-occurrence topology; were alternatives (e.g., dependency-based networks) considered?
- Question 2: Can the authors provide detailed feature importance or which network metrics consistently separate MCI vs. controls?
- Question 3: Any quantitative evidence that the method is robust to disfluencies or comparisons with cleaned transcripts?
- Question 4: Please provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) for method comparisons.
- Question 5: Can the authors present an error analysis with misclassified examples and potential causes?
- Additional comment 1: Expand related work on network science in clinical language analysis.
- Additional comment 2: Provide code availability to aid reproducibility.
- Additional comment 3: Clarify which Cinderella dataset version corresponds to manually vs. automatically processed results.
- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper with useful contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
