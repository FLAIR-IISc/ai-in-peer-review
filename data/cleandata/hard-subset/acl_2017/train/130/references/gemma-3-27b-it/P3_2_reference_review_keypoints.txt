- Paper applies complex network representations enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, evaluated on English and Portuguese datasets.
- Main contribution: novel application of enriched complex networks to a clinically relevant NLP task rather than a new network or embedding method.
- Cross-lingual evaluation on English and Portuguese datasets demonstrates some generalizability.
- Comprehensive experimental setup comparing multiple feature types, classifiers, and combination strategies.
- Strong clinical motivation and clear articulation of the importance of early MCI detection via language analysis.
- Methodology is well-defined with clear network construction, embedding enrichment, feature extraction, and classifier procedures.
- Proposed method achieves competitive performance and often outperforms traditional approaches, especially when combined with other features.
- Datasets are described in detail with demographic and collection information supporting reproducibility.
- Systematic exploration of feature combinations and multi-view learning highlights integration benefits.
- Limited methodological novelty: network construction and embedding enrichment use established techniques rather than new methods.
- Preprocessing choices are questionable—selective removal of disfluencies (keeping revisions, elaborations, paraphasias, comments) may introduce noise and need stronger justification.
- Hyperparameter reporting is insufficient; fixed cosine thresholds (0.7/0.4) feel arbitrary and sensitivity analysis is lacking.
- Limited error analysis focusing on overall accuracy; paper lacks detailed investigation of misclassification types and causes.
- Dataset size and class imbalance concerns could bias results; robustness across datasets and mitigation strategies need discussion.
- Questions for authors on rationale and impact of keeping certain disfluencies, threshold selection and sensitivity, detailed error analysis, robustness to dataset size/imbalance, and computational scalability.
- Additional comments: formatting mostly good but some incomplete references; supplementary material is helpful; related work could include more recent network-based NLP advances.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with competitive results and cross-lingual evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions, mainly addressing preprocessing and hyperparameter tuning.
