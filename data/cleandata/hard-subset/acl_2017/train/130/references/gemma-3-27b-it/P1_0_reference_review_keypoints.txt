- Paper investigates using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts across three datasets (two English, one Portuguese).
- Contribution: Applies complex network analysis to MCI detection, offering a novel perspective beyond traditional NLP features.
- Contribution: Evaluates the approach across diverse datasets (short descriptions vs. narratives, manual vs. automatic transcripts).
- Contribution: Shows that combining network-based features with traditional linguistic features improves classification performance.
- Strength: Novel use of network analysis with embeddings in the MCI-from-speech task.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation on multiple datasets, including English and Portuguese, enhancing generalizability.
- Strength: Strong baseline comparisons (linguistic features, BoW) and systematic exploration of feature combinations.
- Strength: Practical focus on a fully automated system and attention to transcript/disfluency challenges.
- Strength: Clear presentation and helpful supplementary materials (example transcriptions).
- Weakness: Network construction method (word co-occurrence) is standard; more sophisticated constructions and edge weighting not explored.
- Weakness: Insufficient details on word embedding implementation (pre-trained model, parameters) and sensitivity to embedding choice.
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported for classification results.
- Weakness: Small dataset sizes (e.g., Cookie Theft n small), limiting statistical power and increasing overfitting risk.
- Weakness: Lack of a focused ablation study to isolate contributions of individual network features and embedding enhancements.
- Question: Were alternative network constructions (e.g., syntactic dependencies, semantic relations) considered and why not used?
- Question: Which specific pre-trained embeddings (Word2Vec/GloVe/FastText), dimensions, and training choices were used, and how sensitive are results to these choices?
- Question: Can statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) be provided for reported improvements?
- Question: Can an error analysis be provided to identify common misclassification patterns and limitations?
- Question: How do different disfluency-handling strategies impact the performance of network-based features?
- Additional comment: Provide dataset access details or links to improve reproducibility.
- Additional comment: Present key findings more concisely given the large number of results.
- Recommendation: Overall promising work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (address significance testing and network construction analysis).
