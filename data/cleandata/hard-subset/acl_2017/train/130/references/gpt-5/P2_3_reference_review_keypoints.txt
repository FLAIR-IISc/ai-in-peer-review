- Proposes representing speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-similarity edges and using topological features to classify MCI vs. controls.
- Evaluated on three datasets (English Cookie Theft; Brazilian Cinderella; Portuguese ABCD immediate/delayed recalls) against Coh-Metrix and BoW, and combined via multi-view/ensemble learning.

Contributions:
- Empirical finding: CNE (complex networks + embeddings) is competitive with or outperforms linguistic metrics and BoW on noisy, short neuropsychological transcripts.
- Methodological: Provides a protocol to enrich word-adjacency networks with embedding-based edges (cosine threshold) and extract standardized topological statistics.
- Multilingual/automation focus: Demonstrates a language- and tool-agnostic approach that works with minimal preprocessing and automatic segmentation.

Strengths:
- Tackles a clinically relevant problem using only transcript-based signals.
- Language- and tool-agnostic modeling requiring only tokenization and stopword removal.
- Empirically competitive results across datasets (e.g., Cinderella 0.75, ABCD multi-view 0.80).
- Clear, reproducible specification of the CN features and summary statistics.
- Sensible comparisons to reasonable baselines and exploration of multiple classifiers and ensemble strategies.

Weaknesses:
- Critical risk of data leakage: unclear whether cross-validation folds are stratified by subject, especially for ABCD with two narratives per subject.
- Incomplete experimental rigor: only accuracy reported, no confidence intervals, no statistical tests, no per-fold variance, and no class-distribution/chance baselines.
- Ambiguity in embedding training and threshold tuning: missing details on corpora, vocabularies, whether embeddings were trained on test data, and how thresholds were selected.
- Limited novelty: method is incremental over existing word-adjacency and embedding-similarity ideas; novelty is mainly application to MCI.
- Missing ablations and controls: no length controls, no ablation on which topology measures matter, no comparison to avg-word-embedding or tf-idf baselines.
- Clarity and reporting gaps: incomplete dataset details (Ns, class balance), preprocessing steps underspecified, and some malformed references.

Questions for authors:
- Were CV folds stratified by subject across datasets, particularly ABCD with paired narratives?
- What corpora, vocabularies, and training procedures were used for FastText embeddings; were test transcripts included in training?
- How were cosine thresholds chosen—via nested CV or tuned on full data?
- Can you add baselines: tf-idf + linear SVM, average word embeddings + linear classifier, and a length-only baseline, all with subject-level CV?
- Please provide class distributions, majority baselines, mean±std over repeated CV, and statistical significance tests versus best baselines.
- Are ABCD accuracies reported per narrative or aggregated per subject; can you provide subject-level aggregation (e.g., majority vote)?

Overall assessment:
- Promising, practical, and reproducible idea but weakened by potential leakage, underspecified embedding/tuning protocols, lack of statistical rigor, and limited novelty; currently leaning to reject but fixable with stronger evaluation and clarifications.
