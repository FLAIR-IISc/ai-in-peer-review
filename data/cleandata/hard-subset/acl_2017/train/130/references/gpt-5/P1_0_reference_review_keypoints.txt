- Models transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with semantic edges based on embedding cosine similarity.
- Represents each transcript via topological graph metrics (means/stds/skewness of 10 measures).
- Evaluates these features for MCI detection on three datasets (English Cookie Theft, Portuguese Cinderella, Portuguese ABCD).
- Compares CNE features to linguistic metrics (Coh-Metrix variants) and bag-of-words, and explores ensemble/multi-view voting.

- Empirical finding: embedding-based enrichment of co-occurrence networks yields competitive or superior performance for MCI detection, especially on short/noisy transcripts.
- Cross-lingual, multi-dataset evaluation shows robustness across languages and elicitation protocols.
- Low-resource pipeline: method avoids heavy preprocessing and uses modest feature dimensionality, suitable when high-accuracy NLP tools are unavailable.

- Strength: cross-dataset, cross-lingual testing strengthens claims of generality.
- Strength: practical for noisy/short texts by avoiding lemmatization/parsing and addressing linear-network problems via semantic edges.
- Strength: competitive performance with simple, interpretable graph features; CNE often the strongest single view.
- Strength: clear ablation between plain co-occurrence (CN) and enriched (CNE) networks.
- Strength: multi-view and ensemble voting show complementary signals and improved accuracy.

- Weakness: potential subject and demographic confounds (ABCD controls younger/more educated), risking classifiers learning age/education rather than MCI.
- Weakness: unclear whether cross-validation is subject-independent; possible leakage if subjects appear in both train/test (ABCD has two narratives per subject).
- Weakness: incomplete baselines (BoW only TF; no tf-idf, regularized linear models, averaged embeddings, or sentence encoders) and limited evaluation metrics (only accuracy reported).
- Weakness: missing details on embedding training corpus, size, and whether embeddings were trained on tiny clinical transcripts.
- Weakness: unclear hyperparameter tuning/model selection procedures (similarity threshold optimization, nested CV for classifier tuning).
- Weakness: dataset description and preprocessing clarity issues (ambiguous sample selection, inconsistent disfluency handling across datasets).
- Weakness: inconsistent terminology/description for “multi-view learning” vs “ensemble.”

- Question: were splits subject-wise to prevent the same subject appearing in both train and test (especially ABCD and DementiaBank)?
- Question: were age and education controlled (stratified/matched splits or inclusion as features), and how do results change on matched subsets?
- Question: on what corpora were the fastText embeddings trained, and how do results compare to large pre-trained vectors?
- Question: how was the similarity threshold selected (grid search, inner CV), and were classifier hyperparameters tuned with nested CV?
- Question: can stronger baselines be added (tf-idf + linear SVM/logistic, averaged embeddings, lightweight sentence encoders) and additional metrics reported (F1, ROC-AUC, confidence intervals, significance tests)?
- Question: were multiple sessions per participant handled to prevent leakage during preprocessing (Cookie Theft, Cinderella)?

- Additional comment: an error analysis (false positives/negatives, dependence on transcript length) and sensitivity study of the similarity threshold would aid interpretability.
- Additional comment: reporting feature importance (which network metrics drive decisions) would strengthen clinical interpretability.
