- Paper models speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with semantic edges from embeddings and uses graph topology to classify MCI vs controls across English and Portuguese datasets.
- Reports 5-fold CV accuracies up to 0.65 (Cookie Theft), 0.75 (Cinderella variants), and 0.80 (ABCD) and explores multi-view/ensemble combinations of CNE, linguistic metrics, and BoW.

Contributions:
- Applies embedding-enriched co-occurrence networks to short, noisy clinical transcripts for MCI detection, showing competitive performance with linguistic features.
- Provides cross-lingual empirical evidence that fusing CNE, linguistic metrics, and BoW can improve classification.
- Shows that embedding enrichment mitigates sparsity/chain-like topology in very short texts.

Strengths:
- Cross-lingual, low-resource friendly approach requiring minimal preprocessing and no syntactic parsing.
- Embedding-based edges effectively address short-text sparsity, with CNE outperforming CN consistently.
- CNE yields competitive performance and multi-view fusion gives the best scores on several tasks.
- Topological measures offer methodological simplicity and interpretability useful for clinical contexts.

Weaknesses:
- Possible subject-level data leakage in ABCD (two narratives per subject) if folds are not subject-disjoint, threatening validity.
- Incomplete dataset reporting: unclear class balances, sample counts per subject, and session multiplicity across datasets.
- Weak/incomplete baselines: BoW uses term frequency only; missing tf-idf, n-grams, averaged embeddings, doc2vec, or transformer baselines.
- Limited evaluation metrics: only accuracy reported; lacking precision/recall/F1, ROC-AUC, confidence intervals, and significance testing.
- Potential overfitting in hyperparameter/threshold selection with no nested CV or sensitivity analyses reported.
- Reproducibility issues: missing details on embedding training corpora, preprocessing rules, feature lists, and absent code/data splits.
- Novelty is limited: methodological approach follows prior network/embedding work, with novelty mainly in clinical application.

Questions for authors:
- Were cross-validation folds created subject-wise (especially for ABCD) to prevent leakage?
- What corpora (size, domain, language) were used to train the fastText-style embeddings and were they language-specific?
- How were similarity thresholds (0.7/0.4) chosen and were they selected within nested CV?
- What are exact class distributions per dataset (n subjects, n narratives per class) and per-subject sample counts?
- Please report per-class precision/recall/F1, ROC-AUC, confidence intervals, and statistical significance tests.
- Can you add stronger baselines (tf-idf with n-grams, averaged embeddings, doc2vec) and compare under the same CV protocol?
- Provide ablation studies isolating CN vs CNE, effects of skew/variance features, and graph density vs threshold analyses.

Additional comments:
- Clarify preprocessing consistency across datasets (which disfluencies removed/kept; segmentation).
- Report feature counts, normalization, and any feature selection or regularization used given small datasets.
- Release code and subject-wise data splits to improve reproducibility and impact.

Overall:
- Approach is simple, promising, and timely for clinical settings but needs stronger methodological rigor (subject-wise CV, better baselines, fuller reporting) before acceptance.
