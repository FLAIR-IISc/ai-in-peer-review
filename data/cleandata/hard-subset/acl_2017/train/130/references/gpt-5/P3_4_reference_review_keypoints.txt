- Contribution: Proposes language-agnostic word adjacency networks enriched with embedding-based semantic edges for MCI detection from noisy, short transcripts.
- Contribution: Empirically shows CNE features are competitive with and complementary to Coh-Metrix linguistic metrics and BoW, with multi-view voting sometimes yielding best accuracy.
- Contribution: Emphasizes a low-dependency pipeline avoiding heavy preprocessing (parsing/lemmatization), useful for low-resource languages and clinical transcripts.
- Strength: Evaluated cross-lingually (English, Brazilian Portuguese) across multiple elicitation paradigms, demonstrating broader applicability.
- Strength: Low-dependency pipeline is practical for noisy clinical data and settings lacking robust NLP tools.
- Strength: CNE features outperform Coh-Metrix on some datasets and complement BoW/LM in ensembles, achieving top accuracies on two datasets.
- Strength: Graph-theoretic features are simple and interpretable, offering potential clinical insight into discourse structure.
- Weakness: Possible data leakage/overestimation—ABCD has two samples per subject and CV may not be speaker-independent, inflating results.
- Weakness: Evaluation reporting is limited—accuracy alone is insufficient; need macro-F1, AUC, confidence intervals, significance tests, and fold variance.
- Weakness: Threshold tuning for embedding edges is unclear; unclear if nested CV or held-out dev was used, risking optimistic bias.
- Weakness: Baselines and ablations are incomplete—missing TF–IDF + linear SVM, averaged embeddings/doc2vec/sentence embeddings, and ablations on enrichment threshold, stopwords, and document length effects.
- Weakness: Document length is a major confound for graph topology and needs to be controlled or included as a covariate.
- Weakness: Dataset descriptions are inconsistent/confusing (Cookie Theft cohort counts, MCI vs AD labels) and demographic tables are incomplete.
- Weakness: Demographic imbalances (age/sex) in datasets like ABCD could confound linguistic differences and need matching or covariate analysis.
- Weakness: Graph construction details are ambiguous—number/types of features, local vs global aggregation, edge weighting, density effects from cosine thresholds, and rationale for removing stopwords.
- Weakness: Claims that CN outperforms linguistic metrics are not consistently supported across datasets; multi-view gains may be due to ensembling rather than unique CNE strength.
- Question: Were CV folds constructed at the subject level for datasets with multiple samples per subject (e.g., ABCD)? If not, redo with speaker-independent CV.
- Question: How were similarity thresholds (e.g., 0.7, 0.4) chosen—held-out dev set or nested CV? Provide sensitivity analyses and compare to k-NN graph construction.
- Question: Which corpora were used to train the fastText embeddings for English and Portuguese, and what are OOV/vocabulary coverage rates? Were public pretrained embeddings tried?
- Question: Are graph edges weighted by frequency or similarity? Which features are local vs global, total feature dimensions, and are features normalized by graph size? Was stopword retention tested?
- Question: Please add baselines: TF–IDF + linear SVM, averaged fastText/doc2vec, pretrained sentence embeddings, and report macro-F1/AUC plus significance tests.
- Question: Clarify Cookie Theft cohort composition (counts of MCI vs controls) and reconcile mentions of probable/possible AD with MCI labels.
- Question: Did you control for demographics (age/education/sex) or assess their predictive power; report results with demographics included/excluded?
- Additional comment: A post-hoc analysis of which graph metrics drive classification decisions and how enrichment affects them would strengthen clinical relevance.
- Additional comment: Release code and graph feature extraction scripts to support reproducibility.
