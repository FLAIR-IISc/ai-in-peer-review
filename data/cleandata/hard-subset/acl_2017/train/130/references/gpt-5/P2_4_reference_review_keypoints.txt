- Paper represents transcripts as word co-occurrence networks, enriches them with embedding-similarity edges, and extracts topological features for MCI detection.
- Evaluated on three datasets (English Cookie Theft; Portuguese Cinderella; Portuguese ABCD), compared to Coh-Metrix, BoW, and simple multi-view ensembling.
- Claimed contributions: language-agnostic, low-preprocessing featureization; embedding-enrichment improves network features for short/disfluent transcripts; simple ensembling yields further gains.

- Cross-lingual, multi-dataset evaluation increases external validity and reveals dataset-dependent behaviors.
- Network-based, embedding-enriched features are robust and require minimal annotation or parsing.
- Consistent empirical trend: enriched networks (CNE) outperform plain co-occurrence networks (CN) across tables.
- Majority-vote ensembles across CNE, linguistic metrics, and BoW often give the best accuracies.
- Practical relevance: approach is appealing for low-resource clinical settings and early MCI detection.

- Potential subject leakage: unclear whether cross-validation was grouped by subject, risking inflated ABCD results where subjects have two retellings.
- Confounding demographics: ABCD controls are much younger than MCI group; analyses do not control for age/education, risking demographic confounds.
- Limited methodological novelty: co-occurrence networks and embedding-based edges are established; contribution is mainly application-focused and incremental.
- Non-nested or unclear hyperparameter tuning (similarity thresholds, classifier params) may produce optimistic performance estimates.
- Reporting gaps: only accuracy reported, no class counts per fold, CIs, F1/AUC, or significance testing.
- Baselines incomplete: missing comparisons to pretrained embeddings with linear models, simple neural models, and common acoustic features.
- Reproducibility/data clarity issues: unclear control counts, unspecified embedding training corpora, possible embedding leakage, and no code or fixed splits.

- Did you ensure subject-level separation in CV for all datasets, especially ABCD?
- Were similarity thresholds and classifier hyperparameters tuned with nested CV or a held-out validation set?
- What corpora produced the embeddings, were they trained on full data or only training folds, and what are OOV rates?
- How do results change when controlling for age/education or including demographics as covariates?
- Please report class counts, precision/recall/F1, confidence intervals, and statistical significance of CNE improvements.
- Are there repeated narrations in Cookie Theft/Cinderella and how was overlap across folds avoided; clarify control sample size.
- How sensitive are results to similarity threshold, embedding choice, and how many edges does enrichment add?
- Can you ablate stopword handling and report average network density before/after enrichment?

- Overall: promising, low-dependency empirical work with competitive results, but methodological issues (possible leakage, confounds, non-nested tuning), limited baselines, and reporting gaps currently undermine the strength of the claims.
