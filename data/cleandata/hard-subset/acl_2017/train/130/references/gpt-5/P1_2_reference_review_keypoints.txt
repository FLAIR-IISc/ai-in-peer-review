- Paper models speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-based semantic edges and extracts topological metrics to classify MCI vs controls.

- Main contribution 1: Demonstrates enriched complex network features for MCI detection across English and Portuguese with less brittle NLP preprocessing.

- Main contribution 2: Proposes densifying short, noisy transcript graphs using embedding cosine-similarity edges to improve topological feature informativeness.

- Main contribution 3: Empirically compares CN, CNE, linguistic features, and BoW across three datasets and shows multi-view combinations can improve accuracy.

- Strength: Cross-lingual, low-dependency pipeline avoiding heavy syntactic parsing, suitable for spontaneous/ASR-like clinical speech.

- Strength: Embedding enrichment reliably improves short-text graph modeling (CNE > CN in most settings).

- Strength: Multi-dataset evaluation (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) increases external validity; reported accuracies comparable to prior work.

- Strength: Uses interpretable, standard graph metrics summarized by distributional statistics, aiding clinical interpretability.

- Strength: Sensible baselines (Coh-Metrix, BoW SVM) included; BoW performs strongly on some datasets, contextualizing proposed features.

- Weakness: Potential data leakage—CV folds may not be subject-disjoint in ABCD where subjects have multiple narratives, risking inflated accuracy.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail about embedding training corpus, languages, sizes, and OOV rates; unclear if embeddings were trained appropriately per language.

- Weakness: Unclear and potentially improper threshold tuning for cosine similarity (thresholds reported but tuning protocol not specified); no nested CV reported.

- Weakness: Lack of statistical rigor—no confidence intervals, significance tests, or class-balanced metrics (only accuracy reported).

- Weakness: Dataset/preprocessing ambiguities (Cookie Theft counts inconsistent) and possible problematic stopword removal that could discard clinically relevant signals.

- Weakness: Limited analysis/ablation—no report of how many edges added by enrichment, sensitivity to threshold, feature importance, or more advanced multi-view fusion beyond majority voting.

- Question: Were CV folds constructed subject-disjoint for ABCD and any dataset with multiple samples per subject?

- Question: What corpora and procedures were used to train English and Portuguese embeddings, and what were the OOV rates?

- Question: How was the cosine similarity threshold tuned (nested CV vs. using test data), and what is sensitivity to threshold and percent of added edges?

- Question: What are class counts per dataset and additional performance metrics (macro-F1, AUC) with confidence intervals?

- Question: Did you try including function words in CN/CNE, and how does that affect performance and graph topology?

- Question: For ABCD, are results reported separately for immediate vs. delayed recalls and do they differ?
