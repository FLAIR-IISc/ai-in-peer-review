- Paper models speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks, enriches them with embedding-based edges, extracts topological features, and classifies MCI vs control across three datasets (English Cookie Theft, Portuguese Cinderella and ABCD).  
- Main claim: embedding-enrichment (CNE) improves graph-based features for short clinical texts and can compete with BoW and linguistic metrics.  
- Main claim: multi-view combinations of complex-network, linguistic, and BoW features yield best performance (notably on ABCD).  
- Main claim: graph features are practical, interpretable, and avoid heavy dependency on syntactic parsing, making them robust to disfluencies and low-resource settings.  
- Strength: cross-lingual, multi-dataset evaluation increases generalizability beyond single-corpus studies.  
- Strength: methodological simplicity and interpretability of standard topological metrics suited for noisy transcripts.  
- Strength: solid baseline coverage, comparing to BoW and Coh-Metrix(-Dementia) and exploring multiple classifiers/ensembles.  
- Strength: empirical finding that embedding-enriched networks alleviate short-text sparsity and consistently improve over plain co-occurrence graphs.  
- Strength: practical clinical relevance as a low-preprocessing approach for MCI detection from speech.  
- Weakness: potential data leakage and non-independent evaluation—threshold tuning and hyperparameter selection appear untuned or done outside nested CV.  
- Critical weakness: ABCD likely has subject-level leakage (immediate and delayed narratives per subject) if narrative-level CV was used; subject-wise CV is required.  
- Weakness: limited metrics and statistical rigor—accuracy only, no precision/recall/F1/AUC, CIs, or significance tests to assess small gains.  
- Weakness: ambiguous dataset descriptions and preprocessing (unclear class counts, inclusion criteria, disfluency removal rules, and embedding training corpora).  
- Weakness: modest and inconsistent gains across datasets; multi-view improvements may be within variance and need ablations and sensitivity analyses (thresholds, measures).  
- Weakness: missing controls and analyses—no subject-wise CV, no demographic confound control, no feature importance, no modern representation baselines, and no error/task breakdown.  
- Questions: did you use subject-wise CV for ABCD, and if not please rerun with subject-wise folds?  
- Questions: were thresholds and classifier hyperparameters selected with nested CV? Please clarify selection procedure.  
- Questions: what corpora were fastText embeddings trained on, and were training transcripts included?  
- Requests: report per-class precision/recall/F1, AUC, variances/CIs, and statistical tests for key comparisons (CN vs CNE, vs BoW/LM, multi-view vs best single).  
- Requests: clarify exact class distributions (subjects and narratives) for each dataset, especially Cookie Theft.  
- Requests: provide sensitivity analyses for similarity threshold and ablations on topological measures to show which metrics matter.  
- Request for ABCD: clarify whether immediate and delayed recalls were pooled and report separate or controlled results.  
- Additional recommendation: include stronger BoW baselines (tf-idf + linear models) and simple modern sentence-level baselines (averaged embeddings or sentence transformers).  
- Reproducibility recommendation: release code and preprocessing scripts.  
- Methodological note: justify removal of filled pauses since disfluencies can be diagnostic and removing them may discard useful signal.
