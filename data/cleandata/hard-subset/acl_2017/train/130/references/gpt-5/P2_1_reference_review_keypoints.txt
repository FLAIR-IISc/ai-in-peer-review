- Proposes representing transcripts as word co-occurrence complex networks enriched with embedding-based semantic edges (CNE) and using topological features to classify MCI vs control.
- Claims CNE is competitive or superior to traditional linguistic features and BoW across three datasets (English and Portuguese), including noisy/low-resource settings.
- Claims a multi-view ensemble (CNE + LM + BoW) yields best accuracies on two datasets, indicating complementary signals.
- Argues the pipeline is practical and portable across languages by avoiding heavy annotation and parsing.

- Strength: Cross-lingual, multi-dataset evaluation (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) supporting robustness to tasks, lengths, languages, and segmentation methods.
- Strength: Addresses short-text network limitations by adding embedding-based edges, with consistent gains of CNE over plain CN.
- Strength: Low-dependency pipeline suitable for disfluent clinical transcripts and limited NLP tooling, especially in Portuguese.
- Strength: Evidence of complementarity—multi-view/ensemble often outperforms single-view methods.
- Strength: Transparent, interpretable network features (centralities, clustering, assortativity) facilitating analysis.

- Weakness: Insufficient methodological detail and potential overfitting—unclear whether embedding similarity threshold and other tuning were nested in cross-validation; embedding training underspecified.
- Weakness: Baselines appear under-optimized (raw TF BoW, default classifiers); stronger baselines (tf–idf, n-grams, tuned models, acoustic features) could close the gap.
- Weakness: Dataset composition and evaluation unclear—control selection, class balance, fold stratification, possible subject leakage, and reliance on accuracy without CIs or significance tests.
- Weakness: Limited analysis and ablations—no per-feature importance, sparse exploration of threshold/density effects, no error analysis, and no control for confounders like age/education.
- Weakness: Reproducibility and clarity issues—missing implementation specifics (scaling, weighting, directedness, tokenization), minor writing errors, and no code/embeddings released.

- Question: Was the embedding similarity threshold tuned nested within cross-validation or on full data; please report performance vs threshold and fold variance?
- Question: On what corpus and settings were embeddings trained (size, domain, languages); were embeddings trained per language and did training include test folds?
- Question: For ABCD, were immediate/delayed retells from the same subject kept in the same CV fold and were splits class-stratified to avoid leakage?
- Question: Please report class distributions per dataset/fold and additional metrics (precision/recall/F1), confidence intervals, and statistical significance of gains.
- Question: Did you try stronger text baselines (tf–idf, word/char n-grams) and properly tuned classifiers; how do results change?
- Question: How are edges constructed/weighted (binary vs weighted), are networks directed, and are topological features normalized for graph size/length effects?
- Question: Which topological features contribute most—please provide feature importance or ablation studies?
- Question: How were demographic confounders (age, education) handled, especially in ABCD; any covariate adjustment or matching?

- Overall: Important and practical approach with promising cross-lingual results, but methodological/reporting gaps (tuning, baselines, dataset clarity, significance) limit confidence; novelty moderate and evidence not yet conclusive.
