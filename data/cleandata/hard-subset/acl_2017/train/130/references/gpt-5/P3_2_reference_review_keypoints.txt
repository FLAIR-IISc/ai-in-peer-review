- Summary: Models speech transcripts as word-adjacency networks enriched with embedding-derived semantic edges, extracts topological features, and classifies MCI vs controls on English and Portuguese datasets; enriched networks and simple multi-view fusion improve accuracy.

- Contribution: Proposes embedding-enriched co-occurrence network topology for MCI detection across English and Portuguese with minimal linguistic preprocessing.

- Contribution: Shows empirical gains from combining enriched network features with BoW and linguistic features via simple multi-view voting on small clinical datasets.

- Contribution: Provides a practical, potentially disfluency-robust feature-extraction pipeline suitable for lower-resourced languages.

- Strength: Clear, practically relevant motivation to avoid fragile NLP tools for disfluent clinical speech.

- Strength: Methodologically plausible enrichment of short-text adjacency graphs with embedding similarity and use of standard, interpretable topological measures.

- Strength: Cross-lingual evaluation across different elicitation tasks suggests generalizability.

- Strength: Competitive comparison to BoW and Coh-Metrix(-Dementia), multiple classifiers, and ensemble/multi-view experiments showing consistent improvements.

- Strength: Insight that network features remain competitive without manual transcript curation, supporting robustness claims.

- Weakness: Potential subject leakage from narrative-level CV (ABCD and possibly other datasets) that could inflate performance.

- Weakness: Demographic confounds (e.g., age differences in ABCD) not controlled, risking models learning demographics instead of impairment.

- Weakness: Insufficient details about embeddings, tokenization, stopword handling, OOVs, and whether semantic-threshold tuning used nested CV—risking overfitting.

- Weakness: Limited baselines and evaluation metrics—accuracy only, no F1/AUC/confidence intervals/statistical tests, and absence of stronger/simple ML baselines or learned fusion methods.

- Weakness: Dataset description and reproducibility issues (confusing Cookie Theft cohort description, missing Table 1, unclear preprocessing and code/data availability).

- Weakness: Potentially discarding informative signals by removing stopwords before network construction; no ablation reported.

- Question: Did you ensure subject-wise CV splits so all narratives from one subject are in a single fold; if not, please rerun with subject-level CV?

- Question: How did you control for demographics (age, education, sex); can you report results with demographics as features or on matched subsamples?

- Question: Which corpora/embeddings were used for English and Portuguese; were pre-trained fastText vectors used or were embeddings trained on small datasets; provide tokenization/OOV/preprocessing details and sensitivity analysis for thresholds.

- Question: Please clarify Cookie Theft cohort composition, diagnostic criteria, control matching, and provide the missing table and class-balance/balanced metrics.

- Question: Can you add stronger baselines (e.g., logistic regression with TF-IDF, char n-gram SVM, gradient-boosted trees), report F1/AUC with confidence intervals, and try learned multi-view fusion (stacking) or normalized concatenation?
