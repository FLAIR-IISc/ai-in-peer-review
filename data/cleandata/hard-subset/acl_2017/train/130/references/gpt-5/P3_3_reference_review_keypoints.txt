Summary/contributions:
- Proposes modeling transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-similarity edges and characterizing them via topological metrics for MCI detection.
- Empirically shows embedding-enriched networks (CNE) often outperform plain CN and sometimes Coh-Metrix LMs across three datasets in English and Portuguese.
- Demonstrates simple multi-view ensembling of CNE with LM/BoW yields additional gains on two datasets.

Strengths:
- Relevant, practical approach for language-agnostic, low-preprocessing MCI screening on noisy clinical transcripts.
- Simple, computationally light method (fastText-based enrichment) with consistent empirical improvement over plain CN.
- Multi-dataset, bilingual evaluation (different tasks, lengths, and automatic segmentation) increases external validity.
- Broad comparisons across feature families and classifiers help situate the method against common baselines.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: approach is largely an application of established graph and embedding ideas with incremental contribution.
- Potential methodological leakage: possible train/test contamination in ABCD (two narratives per subject) and unclear whether similarity-threshold tuning was nested.
- Demographic confounds: ABCD has age/education mismatches; classifiers may exploit demographics rather than MCI signals.
- Incomplete evaluation: only accuracy reported; no sensitivity/specificity, ROC-AUC, macro-F1, CIs, or significance testing; class imbalance not addressed.
- Weak baselines/ablations: BoW uses tf only; missing tf-idf, averaged-embedding, doc2vec, and coherence baselines; simple majority-vote ensemble only.
- Graph metric confounds: topological measures correlate with length/density but no controls, normalization, or partial-correlation analyses reported.
- Reproducibility gaps: fastText training corpora, preprocessing/disfluency rules, OOV rates, and code/data release are unspecified.
- Interpretation lacking: no analysis of which network metrics drive discrimination or feature importance/error analysis.

Questions for authors:
- Did you perform subject-grouped CV for ABCD to prevent narratives from the same subject appearing in both train and test, and was threshold tuning nested within CV?
- What corpora/preprocessing were used to train fastText for each language, what were OOV rates, and how sensitive are results to different embeddings?
- How do you control for network size/density effects; can performance be reported after adding length/lexical diversity controls or normalizing graph metrics?
- Please report sensitivity, specificity, ROC-AUC, macro-F1 with confidence intervals and statistical tests comparing feature sets.
- Given demographic mismatches in ABCD, can you run matched analyses or include demographics as covariates and report those results?
- Please add stronger baselines/ablations (tf-idf, averaged embeddings + classifier, coherence measures) and compare majority voting to stacked calibrated ensembles.
