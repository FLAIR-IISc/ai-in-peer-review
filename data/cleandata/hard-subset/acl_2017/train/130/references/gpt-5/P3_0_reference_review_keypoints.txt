- Method: models speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-based edges and extracts topological features per transcript for MCI vs. control classification.
- Comparisons: evaluates plain CN, enriched CNE, linguistic features (Coh-Metrix), BoW, and multi-view majority-vote ensembles.
- Datasets & results: tested on DementiaBank Cookie Theft (English), Cinderella (Portuguese raw and cleaned), and ABCD (Portuguese immediate/delayed), reporting best accuracies of 65%, 75%, 75%, and 80% respectively.
- Contribution 1: applies embedding-enriched co-occurrence networks to short, noisy clinical narratives across two languages, showing competitiveness with BoW and hand-crafted features.
- Contribution 2: proposes a multi-view ensemble combining CNE, linguistic, and BoW views that yields modest gains over single-view models.
- Contribution 3: claims reduced reliance on resource-intensive NLP preprocessing for Portuguese clinical transcripts.
- Strength: cross-lingual, multi-dataset evaluation across elicitation paradigms and preprocessing regimes increases robustness relevance.
- Strength: simple, resource-light pipeline avoids dependency parsing and heavy normalization, practical for low-resource clinical settings.
- Strength: enrichment addresses short-text sparsity by densifying otherwise linear co-occurrence graphs.
- Strength: shows complementarity with BoW and hand-crafted features on some datasets (e.g., Cinderella cleaned, ABCD).
- Strength: situates work within prior complex-network and clinical NLP literature with clear motivation for topological descriptors.
- Critical weakness: potential subject-level leakage in CV (ABCD has two narratives per subject) — folds may not be grouped by subject, inflating results.
- Weakness: ambiguous/inconsistent dataset descriptions (Cookie Theft MCI vs AD, unclear control counts and sampling), hindering interpretation and class-balance assessment.
- Weakness: missing strong baselines — no averaged embeddings, doc2vec, or transformer-based (BERT/XLM-R) classifiers to contextualize performance.
- Weakness: preprocessing removes stopwords/disfluencies that are known clinical signals; lack of ablation to justify these choices and no lemmatization justification for Portuguese.
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning and reporting are unclear (cosine threshold selection, lack of nested CV or validation, default SVM params), with no confidence intervals or statistical tests.
- Weakness: limited analysis and interpretability — no feature importance, length/control analyses, error analysis, or per-class metrics reported.
- Question: did you perform subject-wise grouped CV for datasets with multiple samples per subject (e.g., ABCD)? If not, rerun with grouped CV.
- Question: what corpora were fastText embeddings trained on, sizes/domains, and how was train-test leakage prevented if trained on task data?
- Question: how was the cosine similarity threshold tuned (grid, range, nested CV) and what are average graph densities before/after enrichment?
- Question: which disfluencies were removed and how; can you provide an ablation keeping stopwords/disfluencies?
- Question: were topological features normalized or length effects controlled; please report correlations with text length and results after controlling for length.
- Question: provide exact class counts per dataset and report macro-F1, ROC-AUC, per-class recall with confidence intervals over multiple CV splits.
- Request: add baselines using averaged embeddings, doc2vec, and transformer fine-tuning (BERT/ BERTimbau/XLM-R).
- Additional note: majority-vote ensemble is generic — stacking with calibrated probabilities would be a simple stronger alternative.
- Reproducibility/clarity: fix figure/cross-reference typos, provide code and fold definitions to aid reproducibility.
- Overall: promising, practical idea but requires methodological fixes (grouped CV), stronger baselines, clearer reporting, and deeper analysis to support claims.
