- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI without manual disfluency preprocessing.  
- Demonstrates network-based features outperform traditional linguistic features and uses multi-view learning (network + linguistic + BoW) to improve accuracy.  
- Evaluates method across three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) in English and Portuguese for generalizability.  

- Addresses an important healthcare problem with potential for more accessible diagnostic tools.  
- Provides a solid theoretical motivation for enriching co-occurrence networks with embeddings to handle short, disfluent texts.  
- Offers comprehensive multi-dataset and multi-language empirical validation.  
- Claims a practical benefit by avoiding expensive manual preprocessing of disfluencies.  

- Core methodology (complex networks + embeddings) has limited novelty and insufficient differentiation from prior work.  
- Reported performance gains are modest (max ~80% accuracy; ~4.7% improvement over best individual classifier).  
- Fails to compare against recent deep learning baselines (LSTM/Transformer), weakening competitiveness claims.  
- Evaluation reports only accuracy; lacks precision, recall, F1, ROC which are critical for clinical use.  
- Details on automatic handling of disfluencies are unclear, and the impact on network construction is not well explained.  

- How does the approach compare to recent deep learning methods (LSTM/Transformer) for MCI detection?  
- Please clarify the automatic disfluency removal strategy and how it compares to manual correction regarding clinical information retention.  
- Provide additional clinical evaluation metrics (precision, recall, F1, ROC), especially for the best model (CNE-LM-BoW on ABCD).  
- Explain why linguistic features perform much better on the manually processed Cinderella dataset versus the automatically processed version.  

- Overall: promising and practically relevant but limited novelty, modest gains, and insufficient evaluation; borderline acceptance pending major revisions.
