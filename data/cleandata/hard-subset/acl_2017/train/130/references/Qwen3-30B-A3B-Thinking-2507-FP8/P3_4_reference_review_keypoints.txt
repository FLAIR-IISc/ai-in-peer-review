- Proposes enriching word-adjacency complex networks with word-embedding similarities to detect MCI from speech transcripts, avoiding manual disfluency preprocessing.

- Contributions: novel application of enriched complex networks to MCI detection; fully automated pipeline without manual disfluency handling; multi-view learning combining network, linguistic, and BoW features.

- Strength: tackles a clinically important problem and justifies language analysis for early MCI detection.

- Strength: methodological choices well-motivated for disfluent, small-text speech; adapts embedding-based enrichment sensibly.

- Strength: comprehensive experiments across three datasets (English and Portuguese) and multi-view fusion achieves best results (up to 80% accuracy on ABCD).

- Weakness: limited novelty—method builds on established techniques with insufficient argument for domain-specific innovation.

- Weakness: inadequate baselines—no comparison to state-of-the-art deep models (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa) or statistical significance testing of improvements.

- Weakness: small dataset sizes and unaddressed potential biases (age, education, language) limit generalizability.

- Weakness: lacks analysis of why enrichment improves detection and which network/semantic features drive performance.

- Weakness: insufficient discussion of clinical implementation, particularly ASR performance on disfluent speech from cognitively impaired elderly.

- Questions: how does the method compare to transformers/contextual embeddings on the same data?

- Questions: why do linguistic features perform worse on manually processed Cinderella vs. automatically processed data—does CNE specifically suit disfluent speech?

- Questions: what diagnostic criteria were used for MCI across datasets and how do they map to standard criteria (e.g., Petersen)?

- Overall: promising and methodologically sound but needs stronger novelty, modern baselines, larger/diverse datasets, deeper analysis, and clinical deployment discussion; recommendation: Weak Accept (major revisions).
