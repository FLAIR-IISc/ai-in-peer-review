- Proposes detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from speech transcripts by modeling them as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to better represent short, disfluent texts.
- Builds word adjacency networks from transcripts and enriches edges with semantic similarity from embeddings.
- Evaluates the method on three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) across English and Portuguese, and different task types and lengths.
- Main contribution: novel adaptation of complex network analysis with semantic enrichment for short, disfluent transcripts.
- Main contribution: comprehensive cross-dataset evaluation demonstrating generalizability.
- Main contribution: shows multi-view learning (network + linguistic + BoW) can improve classification accuracy substantially.
- Strength: methodological novelty addressing preprocessing burden by using semantic enrichment instead of manual disfluency correction.
- Strength: robust evaluation including comparison of automatic vs manually revised transcripts.
- Strength: practical impact of multi-view learning outperforming simple feature concatenation.
- Weakness: lacks explanation of which specific network topological features drive performance and why they relate to MCI.
- Weakness: comparisons to prior work are questionable; no fair baseline on the same datasets and protocols.
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported for reported accuracy gains, making improvements uncertain.
- Weakness: claim of a “fully automated” system is overstated given remaining preprocessing steps (disfluency removal, sentence segmentation).
- Questions for authors: identify most predictive network features and their clinical interpretation.
- Questions for authors: provide comparisons to best prior results on the same datasets and statistical significance testing for reported improvements.
- Overall: promising and novel work with meaningful contributions but requires deeper analysis, fairer baselines, and statistical validation; recommend acceptance with major revisions.
