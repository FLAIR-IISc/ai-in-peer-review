- Contribution: Applies enriched complex networks with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates cross-lingual applicability using English and Portuguese datasets.  
- Contribution: Shows that topological features can represent short, disfluent texts effectively.  

- Strength: Addresses an important clinical problem—early MCI detection via language analysis.  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation across three diverse datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD).  
- Strength: Thoughtful preprocessing choice to omit lemmatization to avoid errors from disfluencies.  
- Strength: Practical advantage of working without extensive manual preprocessing for clinical deployment.  

- Weakness: Novelty is not well justified since word adjacency networks are established in NLP; specific contribution beyond application to MCI is unclear.  
- Weakness: Accuracy results are unimpressive for clinical use (max 80%), and clinical relevance is not convincingly argued.  
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation of how embedding-based enrichment captures semantics relevant to MCI.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison with more recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., deep learning on raw audio, contextual embeddings).  
- Weakness: Some claims are misleading—results (e.g., Cookie Theft) show linguistic metrics sometimes outperform complex networks.  

- Question: How does the enriched network specifically capture linguistic features correlated with MCI versus traditional features?  
- Question: What clinical accuracy threshold would justify adoption, and how will limitations be addressed for clinical use?  
- Question: Why choose Bojanowski et al. (2016) embeddings over newer methods like FastText or BERT, and how would alternatives affect results?  
- Question: What is the “best scenario for application of network features” and why is it optimal?  
- Question: How and why were the ten topological measurements selected and how do they relate to cognitive impairment markers?  

- Overall: Technically sound with useful multi-dataset evaluation and reduced-preprocessing advantage, but lacks clear novelty, stronger state-of-the-art comparison, and convincing clinical relevance.
