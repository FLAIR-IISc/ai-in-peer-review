- Proposes enriched complex networks (word-adjacency networks plus word-embedding enrichment) to detect MCI from speech transcripts, avoiding manual preprocessing.  
- Evaluates this approach across three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) in English and Portuguese and extracts topological features for classification.  
- Shows multi-view learning (combining network, linguistic, and BoW features) improves classification performance over individual feature sets.

- Practical clinical value: method reduces manual preprocessing and could be applied in real-world settings for early MCI detection.  
- Strong experimental validation: consistent improvements across diverse datasets, with network features outperforming linguistic features and multi-view learning giving substantial gains.  
- Clear technical innovation: novel application of embedding-enriched complex networks to handle disfluencies and short texts in neuropsychological assessments.  
- Well-structured comparisons and ablation studies clarify contributions of different feature types and classifiers.

- Limited novelty: core methodology builds on prior work (e.g., Amancio et al., Perozzi et al.) and the paper does not clearly differentiate or advance those methods.  
- Small dataset sizes (e.g., 43 MCI, 21 controls) limit statistical power and generalizability of reported accuracy gains.  
- Lack of comparison to contemporary state-of-the-art deep learning methods (e.g., transformers) for text classification.  
- Superficial description of the enrichment process; thresholding for cosine-similarity edge addition and its impact on network structure are insufficiently detailed.

- Request p-values or confidence intervals to assess statistical significance of topological-feature improvements over linguistic metrics.  
- Provide analyses showing how embedding enrichment changes network properties (e.g., clustering coefficient, diameter).  
- Justify choice of Bojanowski et al. (2016) embeddings and compare with alternatives (e.g., GloVe, FastText).  
- Discuss the clinical significance and practical utility of reported performance (e.g., 80% accuracy on ABCD dataset).

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, pending clarifications on novelty, statistical significance, enrichment details, and comparisons to stronger baselines.
