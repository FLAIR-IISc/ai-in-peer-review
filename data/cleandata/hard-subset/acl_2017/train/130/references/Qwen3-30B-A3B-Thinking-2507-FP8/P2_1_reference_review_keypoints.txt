- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as word-adjacency complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI).  
- Evaluates the approach on three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) using multiple classifiers and feature combinations.  
- Reports improved performance of enriched complex networks (CNE) over traditional linguistic features, with accuracies around 65–80%.  

- Novel application of enriched complex networks to MCI detection combining graph theory and semantic embeddings.  
- Presents a fully automated pipeline that avoids manual transcript preprocessing.  
- Empirically shows that topological network features can match or outperform traditional linguistic metrics for MCI detection.  

- Addresses a clinically relevant problem and offers a practical, annotation-free pipeline suitable for real-world settings.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains for CNE across multiple datasets, including a notable ~10% gain on Cinderella with multi-view learning.  
- Provides theoretical grounding linking complex network properties to linguistic analysis and justifies preprocessing choices (e.g., skipping lemmatization).  

- Methodological novelty is limited since enriching adjacency networks with embeddings is similar to prior NLP work (e.g., Perozzi et al., 2014).  
- Lacks comparisons to recent state-of-the-art deep learning/transformer-based MCI detection methods.  
- Does not convincingly explain which specific network properties drive improved performance or why they correlate with MCI.  
- Statistical significance and robustness of reported improvements (e.g., 10% on small Cinderella dataset) are not demonstrated given small sample sizes.  

- Request examples of network properties that correlate with cognitive impairment and an explanation of why they matter.  
- Ask for quantified comparison of processing time or human effort saved by the automated pipeline versus manual preprocessing.  
- Request clarification of the clinical significance of 65–80% accuracies for real-world diagnostic utility.  

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better contextualize novelty and provide deeper analysis/validation of why network features work.
