- Proposed method: model speech transcripts as complex word-adjacency networks enriched with word-embedding semantic edges for MCI detection.  
- Implementation: create adjacency networks from transcriptions, add edges by cosine similarity of embeddings, extract topological features for classification.  
- Datasets: evaluated on three datasets — Cookie Theft (English), Cinderella (Portuguese), and ABCD (Portuguese).  
- Results: enriched networks outperform traditional linguistic features in some cases and combining features improves accuracy.  
- Novelty claim: first use of enriched complex networks for MCI detection from speech transcripts.  
- Automated preprocessing: fully automated pipeline to handle disfluencies (repetitions, revisions) without manual revision.  
- Cross-lingual validation: multi-dataset, multi-language evaluation supports generalizability across tasks and languages.  
- Technical strength: well-motivated enrichment addresses short-text/network sparsity and captures semantic relations beyond co-occurrence.  
- Practical strength: automated approach is clinically relevant by removing expensive manual transcript correction.  
- Evaluation strength: comprehensive multi-dataset experiments stronger than prior single-dataset studies.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent deep learning/transformer-based models for MCI detection.  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis or visualizations to explain why and how semantic enrichment changes network topology.  
- Weakness: novelty claims may be overstated; literature review insufficiently supports that metrics are novel for MCI.  
- Question: how does the method compare to transformer-based/contextual embedding approaches (e.g., BERT)?  
- Question: please provide qualitative examples or visualizations of networks with and without semantic enrichment.  
- Question: clarify how enrichment mitigates "almost linear" short-text networks and the minimum text length needed, especially for ABCD (avg. 5.23 sentences).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on adding SOTA comparisons and qualitative insights.
