- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as word-adjacency complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI.  
- Enrichment captures semantic relationships without manual preprocessing of disfluencies or lemmatization.  
- Evaluated on three datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) in English and Portuguese, reporting up to ~80% accuracy.  
- Main contribution: novel application of enriched complex networks for clinical NLP/MCI detection.  
- Main contribution: fully automated pipeline that avoids manual transcript correction and lemmatization.  
- Main contribution: comprehensive evaluation across datasets and classifiers, including multi-view learning.  
- Strength: innovative methodological approach that handles disfluent speech and improves performance over traditional linguistic features.  
- Strength: high clinical relevance due to scalability and reduced need for costly manual annotation.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation (cross-validation, multiple classifiers, multi-dataset).  
- Weakness: no comparison with recent deep learning/transformer-based or sequence models to establish SOTA performance.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why enriched networks work and which specific network features drive discrimination.  
- Weakness: small dataset sizes raise concerns about generalizability and potential overfitting.  
- Questions: How does the method compare to BERT/LSTM or other recent deep models on the same data?  
- Questions: Which network features are most discriminative across datasets? Have you analyzed semantic vs. structural contributions?  
- Questions: How were overfitting and generalizability addressed, and were data augmentation or transfer learning considered?  
- Overall: promising and clinically relevant contribution, but acceptance recommended only after major revisions addressing deep-learning comparisons, explanatory analysis, and generalizability concerns.
