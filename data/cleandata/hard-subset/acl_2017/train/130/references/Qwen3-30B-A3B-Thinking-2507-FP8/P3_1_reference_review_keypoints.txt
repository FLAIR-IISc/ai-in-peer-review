- Summary: Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts by modeling texts as word-adjacency complex networks enriched with word-embedding semantic edges, evaluated on three datasets (English/Portuguese, varying lengths) without manual preprocessing.

- Contribution 1: Introduces network enrichment with word embeddings to make short-text networks more informative.

- Contribution 2: Shows network-based features often outperform traditional linguistic features for MCI detection.

- Contribution 3: Provides a fully automated pipeline that avoids manual transcription preprocessing.

- Strength 1: Novel methodological idea that addresses the limited topology of short-text networks by adding semantic edges via cosine similarity.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results overall, with the best reported accuracy of 80% on the ABCD dataset and competitive performance relative to prior ranges.

- Strength 3: Practical automation advantage—avoids manual sentence segmentation, disfluency removal, and grammatical correction.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient comparison to specific state-of-the-art methods on the same datasets (e.g., omission of direct comparison to Vincze et al.’s 78.7% on ABCD).

- Weakness 2: Small dataset sizes and limited statistical analysis—no reported standard deviations, p-values, or tests to support significance/generalizability.

- Weakness 3: Superficial treatment of network enrichment—no explanation of how the cosine similarity threshold was chosen or sensitivity/ablation studies.

- Weakness 4: No analysis of which network features drive performance, leaving unclear the source of improvements.

- Question 1: How was the cosine similarity threshold determined and what ablation/sensitivity studies were performed?

- Question 2: Can you provide detailed, direct comparisons to SOTA methods on the same datasets (e.g., Vincze et al.)?

- Question 3: What statistical tests were run to ensure reported accuracy differences are significant given small sample sizes?

- Question 4: When and why is the network approach particularly beneficial versus linguistic features—please provide a nuanced analysis.

- Overall assessment: Promising and novel approach but weakened by missing SOTA comparisons, small-sample statistical validation, and methodological detail; recommendation: Weak Accept pending major revisions.
