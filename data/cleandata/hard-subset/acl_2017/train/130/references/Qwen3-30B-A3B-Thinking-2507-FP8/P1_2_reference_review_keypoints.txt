- Contribution: Model speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to better represent short neuropsychological texts.
- Contribution: Show that topological properties of enriched networks can distinguish MCI from control participants.
- Contribution: Demonstrate improved classification by combining complex network features with linguistic and bag-of-words features across datasets.

- Strength: Novel application of complex network modeling to MCI detection from speech transcripts with clinical relevance.
- Strength: Enrichment with word embeddings mitigates the limited topology of short-text co-occurrence networks, improving informativeness.
- Strength: Evaluated across three datasets in English and Portuguese, showing multilingual robustness.
- Strength: Emphasizes a fully automated pipeline that does not require human annotators.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—enrichment approach appears based on Perozzi et al. (2014) without clear differentiation or improvement.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to modern transformer-based NLP models (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa), limiting relevance to current state of the art.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of which topological features are most informative or clinically interpretable.
- Weakness: Small dataset sizes (e.g., 43 MCI / 21 control) raise concerns about generalizability and robustness.
- Weakness: No error analysis provided to understand failure modes or misclassification reasons, problematic for clinical application.

- Question: How does your enrichment implementation differ from Perozzi et al. (2014) and why is it better suited for MCI detection?
- Question: Given linguistic features improve with manual revision, does the enriched network approach also depend on manual preprocessing, and how does this affect the automation claim?
- Question: Why were transformer-based models not included in comparisons, and how do you expect your method to perform against them?
- Question: How should the reported 80% accuracy on the ABCD dataset be interpreted relative to other recent clinical MCI detection studies?

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — interesting application with potential, but requires stronger novelty claims, comparisons to modern NLP methods, and deeper feature/clinical analysis.
