- Paper applies mostly existing representations/methods to MCI detection in speech; problem, datasets and domain are well described and the study is interesting and informative.
- Proposed solution and experiments are reasonable but some important methodological details are missing.
- Manuscript needs considerable editing to improve readability and clarity.
- Multi-view learning approach is not described in sufficient detail; exact scheme must be specified.
- The set of "linguistic features" is insufficiently described and Section 4.2.2 must be expanded.
- It is unclear which dataset(s) were used to train the word embeddings (the paper implies the three small datasets), please clarify.
- Removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is unjustified — they may be important MCI indicators and removal should be explained or reconsidered.
- Bag-of-Words classification did not use tf-idf weighting without justification; explain why term-frequency rather than tf-idf was chosen.
- No statistical significance tests are reported to support experimental conclusions.
- Related work coverage is superficial; core prior methods and direct comparisons need more depth and precise methodological descriptions.
- Abstract is too long and requires shortening.
- Numerous sentences/phrasing need rewording for clarity (lines 22–25, 29–30, 34–38, 70, 73, 115–117, 147–149, etc.).
- Provide citations/links for datasets and tools referenced (DementiaBank, Picnic picture/Western Aphasia Battery, transcription norms).
- Explain/define acronyms and terms (e.g., WML subtest, ABCD, PLN).
- Clarify text preprocessing steps and order (lemmatization and other normalization steps; lines 233–235).
- Clarify which corpus produced word embeddings (lines 290–299, Section 4.2.1).
- Dataset description and segmentation need clarification: name the three datasets explicitly, explain what “segmented” means and why some were manually segmented (lines 323–332).
- Table 1 should include additional dataset statistics beyond demographics.
- Provide justification for preprocessing choices (why disfluencies were removed; lines 375–388) and cite transcription norms (lines 340–440).
- Clarify specific feature definitions (e.g., “shortest path” in feature 6, threshold parameter for embedding cosine distance).
- Fix minor errors and typos (e.g., grammar on lines 322, 620, 735) and rewrite unclear sentences (lines 323–332, 641–645).
- Indicate conference citation style compliance (line 185) and correct missing citations (line 172).
- Check conference guidelines for supplementary material if expanding linguistic features beyond page limits.
