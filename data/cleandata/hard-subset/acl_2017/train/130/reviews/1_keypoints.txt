- Paper applies NLP to Portuguese speech transcripts to identify MCI patients and reports classification accuracy between ~60% and 85%.  
- Introduction/early text is optimistic about results, but conclusion downplays ease of distinguishing healthy vs impaired subjects (contradictory tone).  
- Dataset is very small (20 patients + 20 controls) and transcripts are in Portuguese, limiting reproducibility and broader understanding.  
- Recommend adding more technical details, an English-translated transcript example, and sample network/embedding outputs.  
- Literature review contains some irrelevant or overly general citations (e.g., Salton 1989, sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) and should be shortened/focused.  
- Sections 1–3 and parts of 4.1/4.2.3 are overly long and need compression to focus on core contributions.  
- Unclear how continuous word representations/embeddings were trained — whether only ABCD+Cinderella or external corpora were used.  
- Ambiguity about the use and interaction of word-level and subword (n-gram) networks and whether combining them improves accuracy.  
- Unclear definition and source of "out-of-vocabulary" words mentioned in the methods.  
- Important contribution: studying NLP for detecting cognitive impairment and handling Portuguese (a lower-resource language).  
- Manuscript needs clearer explanation of how embeddings are computed for Portuguese and transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).  
- Multiple technical/typographical errors to fix: "as it a lightweight" (Line 29), PLN→NLP (Line 188), duplicated words (Line 264), decimal formatting in Table 3, duplicate DOI and incorrect reference link (Lines ~992–1002).
