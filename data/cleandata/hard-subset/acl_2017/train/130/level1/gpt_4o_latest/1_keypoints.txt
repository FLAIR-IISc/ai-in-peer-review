- Proposes complex network (CN) features enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, targeting disfluencies and short texts.
- Evaluated on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets; CN features outperform linguistic metrics and BoW, with ensembles/multi-view improving accuracy.
- Main contribution: introduction of CN representation with embeddings as a lightweight, language-independent approach for MCI detection.
- Main contribution: empirical evaluation across multiple datasets (English and Portuguese) demonstrating generalizability.
- Main contribution: use of ensemble and multi-view learning yielding notable accuracy gains.
- Strength: innovative CN+embedding approach handles noisy, short transcripts without extensive preprocessing.
- Strength: comprehensive cross-dataset evaluation supporting robustness.
- Strength: CN features and combined classifiers improve classification accuracy over baselines.
- Strength: method is lightweight and language-independent, suitable for large-scale automated assessments.
- Weakness: no direct comparison with other state-of-the-art MCI detection methods (e.g., deep learning, acoustic features).
- Weakness: limited discussion on interpretability of CN features and their clinical relevance.
- Weakness: datasets are relatively small (especially Cinderella and Arizona), limiting claims about scalability and generalizability.
- Weakness: cosine similarity threshold for embedding-based enrichment is not fully justified; lacks sensitivity analysis.
- Question: how does the method compare to state-of-the-art approaches using deep learning or acoustic features?
- Question: which specific CN topological properties are most indicative of MCI?
- Question: how does the method scale to larger, more diverse linguistic and demographic datasets?
- Additional comment: addressing comparisons with state-of-the-art and improving feature interpretability would strengthen the paper.
