- Contribution: Represent transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings.
- Contribution: Empirically show this approach outperforms linguistic features across three datasets.
- Contribution: Demonstrate ensemble and multi-view learning improve classification accuracy.

- Strength: Addresses an important healthcare problem (early MCI detection) and aims to avoid manual transcript correction.
- Strength: Evaluated on three diverse datasets (Cookie Theft, Cinderella, ABCD) in English and Portuguese, supporting generalizability.
- Strength: Methodology and implementation details (preprocessing, network metrics) are clearly described for reproducibility.
- Strength: Shows consistent performance gains; multi-view approach yields up to ~10% improvement on Cinderella.
- Strength: Mitigates issues from disfluencies that hinder traditional linguistic feature extraction.

- Weakness: Novelty is unclear given prior work on complex networks for text; paper doesn’t clearly distinguish its advances.
- Weakness: Does not compare to recent deep learning baselines (CNNs, RNNs, Transformers), limiting assessment of competitiveness.
- Weakness: Key design choices (e.g., cosine-similarity threshold for edges) lack justification and no ablation studies are provided.
- Weakness: Small dataset sizes raise concerns about statistical significance and overfitting; cross-validation details are insufficient.
- Weakness: No error analysis to show when the approach succeeds or fails.

- Question: How does your network enrichment specifically differ from Perozzi et al. (2014) and why is it better for MCI detection?
- Question: Provide details on hyperparameter/threshold selection and ablation studies showing each component’s contribution.
- Question: How do you reconcile the claim that linguistic features require grammatical text with Table 6 showing revised transcripts favor linguistic features?
- Question: Have you compared to modern deep learning models (e.g., BERT, LSTM)? If not, what are the challenges in applying them here?

- Additional comment: Paper should discuss limitations more thoroughly (small datasets, specific disfluencies handled) and provide stronger evidence for language independence.
