Contributions:
- Applied complex networks enriched with word embeddings to MCI detection from speech — a novel application to a challenging domain.
- Evaluated method across three datasets including Portuguese corpora, improving generalizability beyond English.
- Demonstrated that ensemble/multi-view learning combining network, linguistic, and BoW features can improve performance on some datasets.

Strengths:
- Complex-network representation is robust to disfluencies and parsing errors common in MCI speech.
- Comprehensive evaluation using 5-fold CV, multiple datasets, and several classifiers (SVM, KNN, RF, NB).
- Clear methodology with well-described network construction, embedding enrichment, and feature extraction.
- High practical relevance for automated MCI detection and reduced reliance on manual transcription.
- Authors acknowledge and attempt to mitigate dataset size and noise limitations.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty in the core complex network methodology; main novelty is the application domain and embedding integration.
- Insufficient detail and justification for the cosine similarity threshold used to add embedding-based edges.
- No statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values or confidence intervals) reported for reported accuracy differences.
- Ensemble/multi-view improvement claims may be overly optimistic and not consistent across datasets.
- Lack of detailed error analysis to explain model failure modes.

Questions to authors:
- What rationale and experiments support the chosen cosine similarity threshold for adding edges?
- What is the computational complexity and scalability of network construction and feature extraction for larger datasets?
- Why was DeepBond chosen for sentence segmentation on the Arizona dataset, and was its performance evaluated on these transcripts?
- How sensitive are results to the choice of embedding model (e.g., did you test GloVe, FastText)?
- Can you provide qualitative examples of correctly and incorrectly classified transcripts and the features driving those outcomes?

Additional comments:
- Related work should more directly target studies on disfluent speech in cognitive impairment.
- Improve table formatting (consistent decimal places, clearer headings) for readability.
- Fix inconsistent bibliography formatting (e.g., multiple variants of Aluísio et al., 2016).
