- Paper investigates using complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts.
- Evaluated method on three datasets (one English, two Portuguese) using multiple classifiers including ensemble and multi-view learning.
- Main contribution: demonstrating complex network analysis as a viable approach for MCI detection from transcripts, especially with disfluent/short texts.
- Secondary contribution: empirical evidence that complex network features can outperform traditional linguistic features in noisy transcripts.
- Strength: Novel application of complex networks combined with embeddings to address short, disfluent narratives.
- Strength: Multi-dataset, multilingual evaluation (English and Portuguese) enhances robustness and generalizability.
- Strength: Consistent outperforming of traditional linguistic features suggests robustness to transcript noise.
- Strength: Effective use of ensemble and multi-view learning to leverage diverse feature sets and classifiers.
- Weakness: Limited explanation of how and why network construction yields discriminative power; lacking analysis of specific network properties.
- Weakness: Superficial discussion of word embedding choice and its impact; rationale for selecting Bojanowski et al. (2016) is not detailed.
- Weakness: Potential class imbalance across datasets is not fully addressed and may affect generalizability.
- Weakness: No discussion of clinical significance for reported accuracy gains (e.g., the 4.71% improvement).
- Question: Which specific network properties (e.g., centrality, clustering) correlate most with MCI status?
- Question: Why was the Bojanowski et al. embedding chosen and were other embeddings tested or compared?
- Question: How was class imbalance handled in the datasets?
- Question: What is the practical/clinical significance of the reported accuracy improvements (e.g., 4.71%)?
