- Applied complex network analysis to detect MCI from speech transcripts, offering an alternative to parsing-heavy methods.  
- Enriched co-occurrence networks with word embeddings, improving performance by incorporating semantic information.  
- Evaluated the approach across three datasets (English + two Portuguese), including a manually revised dataset to assess data quality impact.  
- Novel application of complex networks to patient speech transcripts, filling a gap in the domain.  
- Method tolerates disfluencies and requires less manual pre-processing, increasing scalability and practicality.  
- Empirical results competitive with traditional linguistic features; ensembles and multi-view learning further improve performance (notably +4.71% on Arizona).  
- Thorough evaluation across different datasets and languages strengthens generalizability.  
- Clear writing and methods description with supplementary material, aiding reproducibility.  
- Limited justification for selecting specific network topological features and their relation to cognitive impairment.  
- Error analysis is superficial; lacks analysis of failure cases and specific linguistic patterns missed.  
- Dataset imbalance (e.g., Cookie Theft) could bias results; unclear if stratified CV was used and limited metrics reported beyond accuracy.  
- Comparisons to state-of-the-art, especially deep learning methods, are limited.  
- Insufficient details about the pre-trained word embeddings (model type, training data, dimensionality).  
- Request for theoretical rationale linking chosen network features to MCI-related linguistic changes.  
- Ask whether stratified cross-validation was used and how class imbalance may have affected results.  
- Request more detailed error analysis with examples of model failures and explanations.  
- Ask for specifics on the pre-trained embedding model (e.g., Word2Vec/GloVe, training corpus, dimensions).  
- Request direct comparison of network-based approach to Aluísio et al. (2016) using their manually revised Cinderella dataset.  
- Note inconsistent citation style in the paper.  
- Suggest adding a table summarizing dataset characteristics (sample size, avg transcript length, demographics).  
- Ask to clarify meaning of "-" in results tables (not calculated vs. not reported).
