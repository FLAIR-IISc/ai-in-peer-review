- Core idea: represent speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings (co-occurrence + semantic edges) and use network features for MCI classification.
- Main contribution: network features enriched with embeddings effectively discriminate MCI and often outperform traditional linguistic features.
- Additional contributions: evaluation across three datasets (English and Portuguese) and exploration of ensemble/multi-view learning to combine network, linguistic, and BoW features.

- Strength 1: Novel feature representation less sensitive to speech disfluencies than parsing-based NLP methods.
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results — consistent competitive or superior performance (e.g., 10% improvement on Cinderella, 80% on Arizona).
- Strength 3: Multi-dataset evaluation across languages and elicitation methods increases generalizability and robustness.
- Strength 4: Thorough comparisons and ablation studies across features, classifiers, and ensemble strategies.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient detail and justification for network construction parameters (e.g., cosine similarity threshold for edges).
- Weakness 2: Class imbalance issues not explicitly addressed and unclear if stratified cross-validation was used.
- Weakness 3: Missing comparisons to recent state-of-the-art MCI-from-speech methods (e.g., deep learning models).
- Weakness 4: Lack of detailed error analysis identifying types and patterns of misclassifications.

- Question 1: Please justify parameter choices in network construction, especially the cosine similarity threshold for edges.
- Question 2: Was stratified cross-validation used, and how might class imbalance have affected results?
- Question 3: Can you compare your method to recent deep learning/state-of-the-art approaches for MCI detection from speech?
- Question 4: Can you provide an error analysis detailing common patterns among misclassified transcripts?
