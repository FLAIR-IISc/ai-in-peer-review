- Novel application: models transcripts as complex networks augmented with word embeddings for MCI detection — novel for this task.
- Performance advantage: network-based features consistently outperform traditional linguistic features across datasets.
- Effective combination: ensemble and multi-view learning combining network, linguistic, and BoW features yields further improvements, notably on Cinderella and ABCD.

- Strong empirical results: clear, compelling improvements on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD.
- Real-world relevance: tackles automatic analysis of disfluent speech for scalable MCI diagnosis.
- Comprehensive experimentation: evaluates multiple classifiers (GaussianNB, k-NN, SVM, RF) and feature combinations.
- Multi-dataset evaluation: includes English and Portuguese datasets, supporting generalizability.

- Limited methodological novelty: network construction and feature extraction are relatively standard; embedding augmentation is incremental.
- Lack of deep linguistic analysis: insufficient exploration of which linguistic features are affected by disfluencies and how networks capture them.
- Dataset bias concerns: limited demographic details and potential population biases not fully addressed.
- Hyperparameter tuning underreported: insufficient detail on tuning procedures for classifiers and embedding models.

- Please clarify types of disfluencies in the Portuguese datasets and how the network approach handles them versus linguistic feature extraction.
- Were methods applied to mitigate dataset biases (e.g., age, education)? If so, describe them.
- Provide detailed hyperparameter tuning information: search spaces and methodologies for each classifier and embedding model.
- Aside from lemmatization, were other preprocessing steps (stopword removal, stemming) tried and excluded?

- Supplementary material is helpful.
- Paper formatting and clarity are good.
- Related work section is reasonably comprehensive.

- Overall: solid paper with strong empirical results and practical relevance; recommend acceptance despite limited methodological novelty.
