- Paper models speech transcripts as co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-based semantic edges (CNE) to detect MCI, evaluated on three datasets with multiple classifiers and ensembles.
- Contribution: Shows embedding-enriched network features can match/surpass traditional linguistic features for MCI detection in noisy, short transcripts across English and Portuguese.
- Contribution: Proposes a practical adaptation to mitigate sparsity in short, disfluent narratives and provides limited analysis showing CNE > CN and benefits from multi-view/ensembles.
- Contribution: Cross-dataset evaluation highlights when parser-dependent linguistic features degrade (raw transcripts) versus improve (manually revised narratives), arguing for parser-agnostic features.

- Strength: Methodologically frugal and potentially robust by avoiding dependency on high-quality parsing and manual cleanup.
- Strength: Sensible enrichment for short texts—embedding edges meaningfully increase semantic connectivity and consistently improve CN.
- Strength: Cross-linguistic, multi-dataset evaluation (English and Brazilian Portuguese; varied elicitation protocols) supports generality.
- Strength: Includes competitive baselines (Coh-Metrix/Coh-Metrix-Dementia, BoW SVM) and honestly reports where BoW is strong.
- Strength: Emphasis on fully automatic pipelines addresses realistic deployment constraints and demonstrates where linguistic tools fail.

- Weakness: Possible data leakage in ABCD due to 5-fold CV over narratives instead of grouping by speaker, likely inflating accuracy.
- Weakness: Unclear hyperparameter tuning and model selection (no nested CV or held-out dev set reported), risking optimistic bias.
- Weakness: Missing embedding training details (source corpora, tokenization, vocab, pre-trained vs. in-domain) and limited sensitivity analysis for similarity thresholds.
- Weakness: Demographic confounds (age/education differences) not controlled for, so classifiers may exploit demographics rather than cognitive signals.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation metrics and statistical rigor—only accuracy reported, no precision/recall/F1, ROC/AUC, CIs, or significance testing.
- Weakness: Dataset descriptions and counts are unclear (Cookie Theft details, transcripts per subject/sessions), reducing reproducibility.
- Weakness: Baseline breadth and ablations limited—missing tf-idf, averaged embeddings, doc2vec baselines and ablation on which topological metrics matter.

- Question: How were CV folds constructed—were ABCD narratives grouped by speaker to prevent leakage; if not, please recompute with subject-level splits?
- Question: How were embeddings trained—specify corpora, preprocessing, hyperparameters, language-specific details, and whether pre-trained vectors were used?
- Question: How were classifier hyperparameters selected—was nested CV used, and what final parameter values were chosen?
- Question: What is the exact feature dimensionality for CN/CNE, which metrics are global vs. local, and was any feature importance analysis performed?
- Question: Can you control for or match demographics (age/education) in ABCD or include them as covariates; do results hold after regressing out demographics or stratifying by age?
- Question: What is class balance per dataset and per fold; please report per-class precision/recall/F1 and statistical significance of improvements.
- Question: How sensitive are results to similarity thresholds and embedding dimensionality?

- Additional: Clarify claim of language independence since pipeline still uses stopword lists and language-specific embeddings.
- Additional: Release code/scripts, preprocessing details, and CV splits to improve reproducibility.
- Additional: Compare against simple document-level embedding baselines (e.g., average word vectors) to isolate the value of network topology.
