- Paper investigates MCI detection from speech transcripts using embedding-enriched word co-occurrence networks, comparing CNE vs CN, Coh-Metrix/Coh-Metrix-Dementia, and BoW across three datasets with ensemble/multi-view fusion.

- Reported improvements include up to ~10% gain on Cinderella via ensemble multi-view and 80% accuracy on ABCD via multi-view CNE–LM–BoW.

- Contribution: Empirical evidence that embedding-enriched co-occurrence network features compete with or outperform parser-dependent linguistic metrics for MCI detection across English and Portuguese without manual cleanup.

- Contribution: Simple multi-view combinations of heterogeneous feature spaces (CNE, LM, BoW) consistently improve accuracy over single-view models, achieving 0.8018 on ABCD.

- Contribution: A practical, language-agnostic pipeline for clinical transcripts that is robust to disfluencies and avoids expensive manual normalization.

- Strength: Cross-lingual, clinically relevant evaluation using three datasets (English + Brazilian Portuguese), including institutional clinical data, boosting external validity.

- Strength: Robustness to noisy, short texts — embedding enrichment mitigates sparsity and improves complex-network features (CNE > CN in most cases).

- Strength: Sensible baselines and ablations (Coh-Metrix, BoW, multiple classifiers) clarify where proposed features help; e.g., CNE SVM-RBF achieves 0.65 on Cinderella.

- Strength: Practical fusion (multi-view majority voting) yields non-trivial gains (+10% on Cinderella, +4.7% on ABCD), indicating complementary information across features.

- Weakness: Possible data leakage and overoptimistic cross-validation because ABCD contains multiple narratives per subject and subject-disjoint splits are not stated.

- Weakness: Limited statistical rigor — results reported as accuracy only, lacking confidence intervals, significance tests, and class-wise metrics (sensitivity/specificity).

- Weakness: Unclear details on embedding training and thresholding — corpus for FastText, use of pre-trained vectors, and threshold sensitivity/ablation are not specified.

- Weakness: Modest absolute performance on some sets (Cookie Theft near chance; RF/Naive Bayes underperform), suggesting limited robustness and need for stronger baselines (e.g., transformer embeddings).

- Weakness: Fairness of comparisons — linguistic feature quality varies across languages/tools; manuscript-processed Cinderella benefited LM, and fusion used only simple majority voting without learned weighting.

- Question: Were cross-validation folds constructed to be subject-disjoint, especially for ABCD with repeated narratives per subject?

- Question: What corpora were used to train embeddings for English and Portuguese, were pre-trained vectors considered, and how do they affect results?

- Question: Can you report sensitivity/specificity, confidence intervals, and perform statistical significance testing across folds?

- Question: How sensitive are results to the similarity threshold and embedding dimensionality — any ablation beyond the chosen thresholds (0.4/0.7)?

- Question: How does the approach perform with actual ASR outputs (with recognition errors) rather than simulated segmentation, and are code and features available for replication?
