- Novel method: represent speech transcripts as word co-occurrence graphs enriched with word-embedding similarity to detect MCI.  
- Contribution: applies embedding-enriched complex networks to a clinical NLP task, addressing short/sparse texts.  
- Contribution: comprehensive multilingual evaluation across three datasets (English + two Portuguese) and comparisons with strong baselines.  

- Strength: well-motivated and novel methodology avoiding reliance on brittle syntactic parsing.  
- Strength: method is potentially robust to transcription noise, disfluencies, and agrammaticality.  
- Strength: thorough experimental design testing multiple classifiers, ensembles, and multi-view learning.  

- Weakness: missing crucial experimental details (source/training data for word embeddings and potential data leakage).  
- Weakness: unclear hyperparameter optimization procedure for the cosine similarity threshold.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why the method works, e.g., no feature-importance analysis of topological metrics.  
- Weakness: some claims overstated—network features do not consistently outperform linguistic or BoW baselines.  

- Weakness: small dataset sizes and no variance measures or statistical significance tests reported.  
- Weakness: strong BoW baseline (best on ABCD) suggests simple lexical cues may explain much of the performance.  

- Question: how was the cosine similarity threshold optimized—was tuning confined to validation within each CV fold?  
- Question: what corpora were used to train the English and Portuguese word embeddings?  
- Question: were statistical significance tests or variance estimates performed given small sample sizes?  
- Question: can the authors provide a feature-importance analysis for the ten topological network metrics?
