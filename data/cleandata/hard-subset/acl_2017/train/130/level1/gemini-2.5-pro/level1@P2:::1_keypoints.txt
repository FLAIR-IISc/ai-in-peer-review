- Proposes detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from speech transcripts by modeling texts as complex networks enriched with semantic links from word embeddings to handle short, disfluent texts.
- Extracts topological features from these enriched networks and uses them in various classifiers, evaluated on three datasets (English, two Portuguese) with different tasks.
- Compares network-based features against linguistic metrics and Bag-of-Words, and combines views via multi-view and ensemble learning.

- Main contribution: novel adaptation of complex networks for MCI detection by enriching co-occurrence graphs with embedding-based semantic edges.
- Main contribution: comprehensive multilingual evaluation across three datasets and multiple elicitation tasks, supporting generalizability.
- Main contribution: shows network-based features are a robust alternative to parser-dependent linguistic features for disfluent clinical speech.

- Strength: well-motivated and elegant methodology addressing parser brittleness in disfluent speech via semantic enrichment of sparse graphs.
- Strength: thorough and rigorous empirical evaluation with multiple datasets, baselines, classifiers, and multi-view experiments.
- Strength: strong and promising results—CNE often outperforms linguistic baselines and boosts accuracy when combined with other views.
- Strength: clear writing and sufficient methodological detail, using a public dataset that aids reproducibility.

- Weakness: lacks analysis of which specific topological features drive discrimination between MCI and controls.
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported to confirm whether observed accuracy gains are reliable given small dataset sizes.
- Weakness: preprocessing removed disfluencies from DementiaBank transcripts, potentially contradicting the claimed robustness to agrammatical speech and limiting applicability to raw transcripts.
- Weakness: Bag-of-Words baseline sometimes outperforms CNE (e.g., ABCD), weakening claims about the standalone superiority of the network representation.

- Question: why did BoW outperform CNE on ABCD for the best individual classifier, and what does that imply for short-term recall tasks and your method's general claims?
- Question: why were disfluencies removed from Cookie Theft data, and did you test model performance with and without that preprocessing?
- Question: were any statistical significance tests performed to validate CNE and multi-view improvements over baselines?
- Question: can you provide feature analysis showing which topological properties (clustering, path length, centrality, etc.) differ between MCI and control networks?
