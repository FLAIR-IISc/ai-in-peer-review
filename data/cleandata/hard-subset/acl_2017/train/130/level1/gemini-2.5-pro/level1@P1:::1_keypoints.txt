- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts by representing each transcript as a word co-occurrence network enriched with semantic edges from word embeddings to address sparsity in short, disfluent texts.
- Enrichment adds edges between non-adjacent words based on embedding cosine similarity, then extracts network topology metrics as classification features.
- Evaluated on three datasets (Cookie Theft-English, Cinderella & ABCD-Portuguese), comparing enriched network features (CNE) to Coh-Metrix linguistic features and Bag-of-Words (BoW).
- Reports that CNE generally outperforms linguistic features on unprocessed transcripts and that multi-view combinations yield the best results.
- Contribution: introduces a novel representation that combines graph structure with semantic embeddings to avoid brittle parsing on noisy clinical speech.
- Contribution: provides a multilingual, multi-dataset empirical study, including comparisons between automatically processed and manually cleaned transcripts.
- Strength: method is well-motivated and elegantly tackles agrammatical, disfluent clinical speech without relying on syntactic parsing.
- Strength: thorough experiments with meaningful baselines and classifiers, showing consistent advantages and a notable ~10% accuracy gain on Cinderella with multi-view learning.
- Strength: approach has high clinical relevance due to being language-agnostic and requiring minimal manual transcript correction.
- Weakness: paper omits crucial details about how word embeddings were trained or which corpora were used, harming reproducibility and interpretation of gains.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other robust representation baselines (e.g., averaged word/document embeddings or other sentence embedding methods) to isolate the value of network topology.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning—particularly the cosine threshold for semantic edges and classifier parameters—raising concerns about possible information leakage and reproducibility.
- Question: clarify which corpora were used to train English and Portuguese embeddings—pretrained large corpora or the small in-domain datasets?
- Question: describe how the similarity threshold(s) (0.7 and 0.4) were selected and whether tuning was done without using test data.
- Question: explain why BoW with linear SVM performs strongly (75.5%) on ABCD and what complementary information CNE adds to push multi-view accuracy above 80%.
