- Core idea: Model speech transcripts as word co-occurrence graphs and enrich them with semantic edges from word embeddings to better represent short, disfluent patient text.

- Contribution: Proposes word-embedding-enriched complex networks (CNE) as a novel feature-engineering approach for MCI detection, robust to noisy speech and outperforming traditional linguistic features.

- Contribution: Provides a comprehensive empirical study on three datasets (English and Portuguese), showing multi-view combinations of CNE with other features yield the best classification results.

- Strength: Well-motivated and novel method that addresses short, agrammatical patient speech by densifying sparse co-occurrence networks with semantic information.

- Strength: Rigorous and generalizable evaluation using diverse datasets, strong baselines, multiple classifiers, and combination techniques.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance—CNE is competitive alone and achieves highest accuracy in multi-view setups (e.g., 80% on ABCD).

- Weakness: Lacks justification and sensitivity analysis for the cosine similarity threshold used to add semantic edges.

- Weakness: No feature importance or ablation analysis to identify which network topological metrics are most discriminative for MCI.

- Weakness: Limited contextualization relative to recent deep learning or large language model approaches on clinical text.
