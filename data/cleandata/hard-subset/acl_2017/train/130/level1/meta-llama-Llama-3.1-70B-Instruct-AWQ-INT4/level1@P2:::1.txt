**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their model on three datasets and compare the results with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A new approach to model speech transcripts as complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings for MCI detection.
2. A comparison of the proposed approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation on three datasets.
3. An evaluation of the effectiveness of combining multiple classifiers and feature spaces using ensemble and multi-view learning.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to model speech transcripts as complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings, which is a significant contribution to the field of MCI detection.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on three datasets and compare the results with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, providing a comprehensive understanding of the effectiveness of their approach.
3. **Use of ensemble and multi-view learning**: The authors use ensemble and multi-view learning to combine multiple classifiers and feature spaces, which is a strength of the paper as it allows for a more robust evaluation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited datasets**: The authors evaluate their approach on only three datasets, which may not be representative of the entire population of individuals with MCI.
2. **Lack of detailed analysis**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the results, including the performance of individual features and classifiers, which would be helpful in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
3. **No comparison with state-of-the-art methods**: The authors do not compare their approach with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection, which makes it difficult to evaluate the significance of their contribution.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the results, including the performance of individual features and classifiers?
2. How do you plan to extend your approach to larger datasets and more diverse populations?
3. Can you compare your approach with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection and provide a discussion on the significance of your contribution?

**Additional Comments**

The authors should consider providing more details on the preprocessing steps, including the removal of stop words and punctuation marks, and the use of lemmatization. Additionally, the authors should provide more information on the word embedding model used and the hyperparameters tuned for the experiments.
