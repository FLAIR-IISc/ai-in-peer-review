- Proposes a novel method for MCI detection from speech transcripts using complex networks enriched with word embeddings, evaluated on three datasets where it outperforms linguistic features.
- Contribution: introduces a different, network-based approach to MCI detection compared to traditional linguistic-feature methods.
- Contribution: enriches complex networks with word embeddings to add semantic information and improve representation of short texts.
- Contribution: evaluates the approach on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery) to demonstrate robustness.
- Strength: complex network-based features achieve competitive performance versus traditional linguistic features.
- Strength: approach is robust to disfluencies common in spontaneous speech transcripts.
- Strength: method is language-independent, potentially applicable across languages.
- Weakness: limited interpretability due to reliance on complex networks and word embeddings.
- Weakness: heavy dependence on word embeddings, which may not capture all language nuances or suit all text types.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to three datasets, which may not cover all MCI detection scenarios.
- Question: How will the authors address the limited interpretability of their approach?
- Question: Can the authors provide details on hyperparameter selection, e.g., the cosine similarity threshold?
- Question: How do the authors plan to extend the approach to other text types (written texts) or different languages?
