- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex network features enriched with word embeddings.  
- Models transcripts as complex networks and uses topological properties for characterization.  
- Evaluated on three datasets (DementiaBank and two Portuguese datasets) to demonstrate robustness.  
- Complex network features outperform traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words across all datasets.  
- Ensemble and multi-view learning combinations further improve classification accuracy.  
- Contribution: novel complex-network + embedding approach for MCI detection.  
- Contribution: uses topological properties as lightweight, language-independent representation.  
- Contribution: multi-dataset evaluation showing method generality.  
- Strength: innovative methodology distinct from standard linguistic/BOW approaches.  
- Strength: robust evaluation across multiple datasets.  
- Strength: empirical accuracy gains and benefits from ensemble/multi-view fusion.  
- Strength: representation is lightweight and language-independent.  
- Weakness: limited number of datasets; further validation needed.  
- Weakness: no comparison with state-of-the-art MCI detection methods.  
- Weakness: lack of interpretation/explanation of why the features work.  
- Question: How will the approach extend to other languages and additional datasets?  
- Question: Can you provide interpretation of results and underlying mechanisms?  
- Question: How do you plan to compare with state-of-the-art MCI detection methods?
