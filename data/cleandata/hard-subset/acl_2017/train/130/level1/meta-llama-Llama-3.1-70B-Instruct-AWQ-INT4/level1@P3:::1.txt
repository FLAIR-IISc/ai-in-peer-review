**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their approach on three datasets and compare the results with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representations. The authors also investigate the use of ensemble and multi-view learning to combine the features and improve the classification accuracy.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel application of complex networks and word embeddings to MCI detection**: The authors propose a new approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their approach on three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets, which provides a comprehensive assessment of the method's performance.
3. **Comparison with traditional features**: The authors compare their approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representations, which provides a baseline for evaluating the effectiveness of the proposed method.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Comprehensive evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on multiple datasets and compare the results with traditional features, which provides a comprehensive assessment of the method's performance.
3. **Improved accuracy**: The authors report improved accuracy using ensemble and multi-view learning, which is a significant improvement over individual classifiers.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited explanation of the methodology**: The authors could provide a more detailed explanation of the methodology, including the preprocessing steps and the parameters used for the complex network construction.
2. **Lack of comparison with state-of-the-art methods**: The authors could compare their approach with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection, which would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance.
3. **Limited discussion of the results**: The authors could provide a more detailed discussion of the results, including the implications of the findings and the potential limitations of the approach.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the preprocessing steps used for the speech transcripts, including the removal of disfluencies and the handling of out-of-vocabulary words?
2. How did you select the parameters for the complex network construction, and what is the rationale behind the choice of these parameters?
3. Can you provide a more detailed comparison of the results with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection, and discuss the implications of the findings in the context of the existing literature?
