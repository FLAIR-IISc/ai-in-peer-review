**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts. They evaluate their approach on three datasets and compare the results with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel application of complex networks and word embeddings**: The authors propose a new approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing and cognitive impairment detection.
2. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their approach on three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets, which demonstrates the robustness of their approach.
3. **Comparison with traditional features**: The authors compare their approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, which provides a comprehensive evaluation of their approach.

**Strengths**

2. **Robust evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on multiple datasets, which demonstrates the robustness of their approach.
3. **Comprehensive comparison**: The authors compare their approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, which provides a comprehensive evaluation of their approach.
