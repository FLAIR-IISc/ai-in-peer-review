**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex network features enriched with word embeddings. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and use topological properties to characterize the networks. They evaluate their approach on three datasets, including one from the DementiaBank and two in Portuguese, and compare the results with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets. The authors also explore the use of ensemble and multi-view learning to combine the classifiers, which leads to improved accuracy.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts**: The authors propose a new approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts using complex network features enriched with word embeddings.
2. **Use of topological properties to characterize networks**: The authors use topological properties to characterize the complex networks, which provides a lightweight and language-independent representation.
3. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their approach on three datasets, including one from the DementiaBank and two in Portuguese, which demonstrates the robustness of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts, which is different from traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation.
2. **Robust evaluation**: The authors evaluate their approach on multiple datasets, which demonstrates the robustness of their approach.
3. **Improved accuracy**: The authors show that their approach outperforms traditional linguistic features in all datasets, and that the use of ensemble and multi-view learning leads to improved accuracy.
4. **Lightweight and language-independent representation**: The authors use topological properties to characterize the complex networks, which provides a lightweight and language-independent representation.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited datasets**: Although the authors evaluate their approach on multiple datasets, the number of datasets is still limited, and more datasets are needed to further validate their approach.
2. **Comparison with state-of-the-art methods**: The authors do not compare their approach with state-of-the-art methods in the field of MCI detection, which makes it difficult to evaluate the performance of their approach.
3. **Lack of interpretation**: The authors do not provide an interpretation of the results, which makes it difficult to understand the underlying mechanisms of their approach.
