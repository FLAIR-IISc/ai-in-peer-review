- Proposes a novel method to detect MCI from speech transcripts by modeling transcripts as complex networks and enriching them with word embeddings.  
- Evaluates the approach on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery).  
- Shows complex networks enriched with embeddings outperform traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words.  
- Main contribution: first application of complex networks + embeddings for MCI detection from speech.  
- Main contribution: robust evaluation across multiple, diverse datasets.  
- Main contribution: direct comparison against traditional linguistic and bag-of-words features.  
- Strength: innovative methodological approach with potential impact on NLP and cognitive impairment detection.  
- Strength: empirical robustness demonstrated by multi-dataset evaluation.  
- Strength: comprehensive baseline comparisons included.  
- Weakness: results are not deeply interpreted, limiting insight into implications.  
- Weakness: no comparison with state-of-the-art MCI detection methods.  
- Weakness: paper lacks discussion of methodological limitations and potential pitfalls.  
- Question: provide a more detailed interpretation of results and their clinical/research implications.  
- Question: compare performance explicitly with state-of-the-art methods for MCI detection.  
- Question: clarify limitations of the approach and propose directions to address them.  
- Additional note: promising and innovative work but needs deeper analysis, SOTA comparisons, and limitation discussion.
