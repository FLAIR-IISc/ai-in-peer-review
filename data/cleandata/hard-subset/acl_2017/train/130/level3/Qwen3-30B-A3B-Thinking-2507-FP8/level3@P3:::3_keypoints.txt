- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, evaluated on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD datasets.  
- Claims network features outperform linguistic features and multi-view learning improves performance (10% Cinderella, 4.71% ABCD).  

Main contributions:
- Applies complex network features to MCI detection as a language-independent, disfluency-robust representation.  
- Shows network features can outperform linguistic features in disfluent speech.  
- Introduces a multi-view learning approach combining network, linguistic, and BoW features.  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important, clinically relevant problem using low-cost speech transcripts.  
- Uses three diverse datasets (English and Portuguese) with clear demographic descriptions.  
- Network approach is well-motivated for short, unprocessed transcripts with disfluencies.  

Weaknesses:
- Multi-view learning method lacks sufficient methodological detail (classifier combination and weighting).  
- Linguistic feature description is inadequate, especially for Portuguese and excluded metrics.  
- Removal of disfluencies is unjustified despite their potential relevance to MCI.  
- No statistical significance testing reported to validate claimed improvements.  
- Unclear which corpora/datasets were used to train word embeddings, harming reproducibility.  

Questions for authors:
- Exactly how are classifiers combined in the multi-view learning (weights, optimization, implementation)?  
- Why were disfluencies removed—evidence they are unimportant for MCI detection?  
- Why use term frequency instead of tf-idf for BoW without justification?  
- Specify which dataset(s)/corpus produced the word embeddings and provide details.  

Additional comments:
- Manuscript needs substantial editing for clarity, shorter abstract, and rewording of many sentences.  
- Define acronyms (WML, ABCD, PLN) and clarify preprocessing steps.  
- Related work section is superficial and lacks precise methodological comparisons.  
- Cite transcription norms, provide dataset/tool links (DementiaBank, Picnic picture, WAB), and fix minor errors.  
- Table 1 should include more dataset statistics beyond demographics for reproducibility.
