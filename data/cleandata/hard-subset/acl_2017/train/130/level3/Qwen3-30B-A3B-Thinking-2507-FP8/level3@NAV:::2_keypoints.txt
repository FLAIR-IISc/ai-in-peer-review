- Proposes a graph-based method using word co-occurrence networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, evaluated on three datasets.  
- Contribution: Applies complex network features to MCI detection from transcripts (novel application).  
- Contribution: Uses a multi-view learning approach combining network, linguistic, and BoW features.  
- Contribution: Evaluates method across three diverse datasets (English/Portuguese, varied text lengths).  
- Strength: Addresses a clinically relevant problem—early MCI detection from speech.  
- Strength: Multi-view learning shows practical improvement (up to 10% on one dataset).  
- Strength: Uses multiple datasets, supporting some cross-dataset applicability.  
- Weakness: Poor writing quality with grammatical errors and inconsistent terminology.  
- Weakness: Unclear problem definition and inconsistent reporting of dataset/transcript-level statistics and class distributions.  
- Weakness: Lack of justification for graph enrichment versus simpler word-embedding similarity methods and no direct comparison.  
- Weakness: Inappropriate metrics—accuracy reported without addressing class imbalance and omission of precision/recall/F1 or balanced metrics.  
- Weakness: Overstated results—over-precise accuracy reporting on small datasets and no statistical significance testing.  
- Question: Why use graph enrichment when standard word-embedding similarity could be applied directly?  
- Question: How was class imbalance handled and why were F1-score or balanced accuracy not reported?  
- Question: What statistical tests were performed to confirm the reported 10% improvement?
