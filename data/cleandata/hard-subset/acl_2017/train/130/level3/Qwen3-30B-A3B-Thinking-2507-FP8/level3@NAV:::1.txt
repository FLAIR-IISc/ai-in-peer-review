

**Review**

This paper investigates the application of complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts, primarily in Portuguese. The main contributions are: (1) pioneering the use of complex networks with embeddings for MCI detection in Portuguese (a lower-resource language), which addresses a significant gap in the literature; (2) demonstrating that network-based features outperform linguistic metrics in distinguishing MCI from controls in their Portuguese datasets; and (3) providing a novel approach to handle disfluencies without manual transcript correction.

Strengths include the meaningful focus on Portuguese, a language with limited resources for NLP in healthcare applications, and the promising results showing complex networks as competitive with traditional linguistic features (achieving up to 80% accuracy). The authors make a valuable contribution by avoiding manual transcript correction, which is crucial for large-scale assessment.

However, several critical weaknesses undermine the paper's impact. First, the dataset is extremely small (20 patients + 20 controls), limiting statistical power and reproducibility. Second, the paper's tone is contradictory—introducing results optimistically while concluding that distinguishing healthy vs. impaired subjects is not easy. Third, the technical details are insufficient: it's unclear how embeddings were trained (only ABCD+Cinderella or external corpora?), how word-level and subword networks interact, and the definition of "out-of-vocabulary" words. Fourth, the literature review contains irrelevant citations (e.g., Salton 1989, pedestrian detection), and the manuscript is unnecessarily verbose in Sections 1-3 and 4.1/4.2.3. Finally, multiple technical errors (e.g., "as it a lightweight," PLN→NLP) and formatting issues (decimal errors in Table 3) need correction.

The authors should clarify embedding computation for Portuguese, provide an English-translated transcript example, and include sample network outputs. The manuscript would benefit from a more focused literature review, compressed sections, and clearer explanations of the methodology. While the core idea is promising, the current presentation and limited dataset significantly diminish the paper's contribution and reproducibility.
