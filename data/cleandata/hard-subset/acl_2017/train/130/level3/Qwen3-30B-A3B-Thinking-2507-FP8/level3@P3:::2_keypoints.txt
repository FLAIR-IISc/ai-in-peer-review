- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI.  
- Evaluated the method on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery) including English and Portuguese.  
- Uses graph-derived lexical and syntactic features as an alternative to traditional linguistic features.  

- Addresses an important clinical problem (early MCI/dementia detection).  
- Multi-dataset, multilingual evaluation supports potential generalizability.  
- Novel feature representation (complex network features) that may be robust to disfluencies.  

- Motivation for graph enrichment over simpler word-embedding similarity is unclear.  
- Lacks comparison with simpler baselines (e.g., direct use of word embeddings).  
- Evaluation reports only accuracy and does not address class imbalance or report F1/other balanced metrics.  
- Dataset descriptions are inconsistent and methodology is ambiguously reported.  
- Manuscript has poor writing quality and lacks statistical significance testing for results.  
- Does not leverage domain-specific biomedical NLP tools or resources, particularly for Portuguese.  

- Clarify class distributions (control vs. MCI) for each dataset, especially Cookie Theft.  
- Explain why accuracy was used as the primary metric and provide results with F1/other balanced metrics.  
- Provide comparisons with simpler approaches (word embeddings without graph enrichment).  
- Report how statistical significance of performance improvements was assessed.  

- Overall: novel and clinically relevant but requires substantial revision on motivation, evaluation, baselines, statistical testing, and writing before being suitable for publication.
