- Proposes a novel MCI detection method using complex network features enriched with word embeddings on speech transcripts.  
- Shows enriched networks are language-independent and handle disfluencies better than traditional linguistic features.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Portuguese transcripts where robust NLP tools are scarce.  
- Combining network, linguistic, and bag-of-words features via multi-view learning yields substantial accuracy gains (up to 10% on Cinderella).  
- Addresses an important healthcare diagnostic problem where automated handling of disfluent patient speech is practically useful.  
- Network-based approach outperforms traditional linguistic features across three datasets, reaching up to 80% accuracy on ABCD.  
- Makes a contribution to low-resource language processing by avoiding manual transcription correction for Portuguese.  
- Extremely small datasets (e.g., 20 MCI + 20 controls) limit statistical power and generalizability for a medical application.  
- Insufficient details on word embedding training (external corpora vs. study data) and unclear handling of out-of-vocabulary words hinder reproducibility.  
- Manuscript contains numerous technical and typographical errors that undermine credibility.  
- Literature review is overly long and includes irrelevant citations, distracting from core contributions.  
- Tone is inconsistent—introduction overstates results while the conclusion downplays them despite high reported accuracies.  
- Question: Clarify whether embeddings were trained on external corpora or only the study datasets, and how OOV words were handled.  
- Question: Define "out-of-vocabulary" in this work and explain its impact on network enrichment; and explain the NURC/SP transcription norms used for Portuguese transcripts.
