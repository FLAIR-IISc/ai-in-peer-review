- Contribution: Proposes a novel framework using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to represent short speech transcripts for MCI detection.
- Contribution: Empirically demonstrates the approach outperforms traditional linguistic features across three datasets.
- Contribution: Introduces a multi-view learning strategy that improves performance (up to ~10%) over individual classifiers.

- Strength: Addresses a clinically important problem with clear motivation.
- Strength: Language-independent network+embedding representation avoids parsing errors from disfluent speech.
- Strength: Consistent performance gains across three diverse datasets; enriched network features (CNE) often achieve highest accuracy.
- Strength: Multi-view combination (CNE + linguistic + BoW) yields substantial gains (10% on Cinderella, 4.71% on ABCD).
- Strength: Provides a plausible rationale that traditional linguistic features require grammatical input and fail on disfluent transcripts.

- Weakness: Linguistic feature set (Section 4.2.2) is inadequately described, preventing assessment or fair comparison.
- Weakness: Unclear which corpus was used to train word embeddings (combined small datasets vs larger external corpus).
- Weakness: Removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, repetitions) is unjustified despite their potential diagnostic value.
- Weakness: Bag-of-Words uses term frequency without justification for not using tf-idf.
- Weakness: No statistical significance tests reported to validate claimed improvements.
- Weakness: Multi-view learning method is not described in sufficient detail (how features are combined and weighted).
- Weakness: Manuscript needs substantial editing—overlong abstract, undefined acronyms (WML, ABCD, PLN), and awkward sentences.
- Weakness: Dataset descriptions and preprocessing (manual vs automatic segmentation, lemmatization order) are confusing and under-explained.
- Weakness: Table 1 is sparse (demographics only) and key resources/citations (e.g., DementiaBank, transcription norms) are missing.

- Question: Precisely which corpus(s) were used to train the word embeddings?
- Question: Why were disfluencies removed—how do you justify excluding potential MCI indicators?
- Question: Why use term frequency for BoW instead of the standard tf-idf?
- Question: Provide full details of the multi-view learning scheme, including combination method and weight assignment.
- Question: Can you include statistical significance tests to support the reported performance differences?
