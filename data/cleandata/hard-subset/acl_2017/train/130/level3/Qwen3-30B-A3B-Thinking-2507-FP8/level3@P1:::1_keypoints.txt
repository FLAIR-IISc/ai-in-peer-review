- Proposes detecting MCI using complex network features enriched with word embeddings from speech transcripts to handle disfluencies without manual correction.
- Primary contribution: applying complex network modeling to represent speech transcripts without requiring manual disfluency correction.
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates language-independence by evaluating on English and Portuguese datasets, highlighting Portuguese as a lower-resource language.
- Tertiary contribution: multi-view learning that combines complex network, linguistic, and bag-of-words features, improving classification accuracy.
- Practical strength: provides a workable solution to disfluencies, removing need for time-consuming manual transcript correction.
- Empirical strength: complex network with embeddings outperforms traditional linguistic features across datasets (65–80% accuracy).
- Multi-view strength: fusion approach yields a notable 10% improvement on the Cinderella dataset versus the best individual classifier.
- Cross-lingual strength: evaluation on three datasets, including two Portuguese, supports method robustness beyond English.
- Contribution to low-resource NLP: meaningful work on MCI detection in Portuguese where parsing tools are limited.
- Major weakness: extremely small dataset sizes (especially Portuguese 20 MCI + 20 controls) limit statistical significance and generalizability.
- Tone inconsistency: optimistic introduction vs cautious conclusion undermines credibility.
- Missing technical detail: unclear how embeddings were trained for Portuguese and whether external corpora were used.
- OOV handling unclear: paper does not explain how out-of-vocabulary words were managed.
- Literature review issues: contains irrelevant citations (e.g., sentiment classification, pedestrian detection) and should be condensed.
- Manuscript quality problems: multiple technical errors, duplicated words, and incorrect reference formatting need correction.
- Question 1: clarify whether Portuguese embeddings were trained only on study datasets or on external corpora.
- Question 2: specify which Portuguese transcription norms (NURC/SP N.338 EF and 331 D2) were followed and their impact on embeddings.
- Question 3: provide a sample English-translated Portuguese transcript to illustrate handled disfluencies.
- Question 4: justify the claim of "language independent representation" given likely language-specific embedding training.
- Recommendation: focus and shorten literature review and compress sections 1–3 and parts of 4.1/4.2.3 to highlight core contributions.
- Recommendation: clarify technical details of how embeddings enrich the network, including word-level vs subword (n-gram) relationships.
- Recommendation: explicitly acknowledge small dataset sizes as a major limitation in the conclusion.
- Overall: potential significant clinical impact if methodological and dataset limitations are addressed.
