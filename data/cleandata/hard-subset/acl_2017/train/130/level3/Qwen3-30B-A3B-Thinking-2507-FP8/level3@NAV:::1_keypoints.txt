- Contributions: first use of complex networks with word embeddings for MCI detection in Portuguese; shows network features outperform linguistic metrics; proposes handling disfluencies without manual transcript correction.
- Strength: meaningful focus on Portuguese, a low-resource language for medical NLP.
- Strength: promising results (up to ~80% accuracy) showing competitiveness of complex-network features.
- Strength: avoids manual transcript correction, useful for scalable assessment.
- Weakness: extremely small dataset (20 MCI + 20 controls) limits statistical power and reproducibility.
- Weakness: paper tone is contradictory—optimistic introduction but cautious/negative conclusion.
- Weakness: insufficient technical details about embedding training (which corpora were used) and model setup.
- Weakness: unclear interaction between word-level and subword networks and vague definition of "out-of-vocabulary" words.
- Weakness: literature review contains irrelevant citations and is overly verbose in multiple sections.
- Weakness: multiple technical errors and typos (e.g., "as it a lightweight," PLN→NLP) and formatting/decimal errors in tables.
- Requested clarifications: specify how embeddings were computed for Portuguese and whether external corpora were used.
- Requested additions: provide an English-translated transcript example and sample network outputs to improve reproducibility.
- Suggested revisions: compress and focus the literature review and Sections 1–3 and 4.1/4.2.3, and improve methodological explanations.
- Overall judgment: promising idea but limited impact due to small dataset, presentation issues, and insufficient methodological detail.
