- Proposes using complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts.  
- Claims three main contributions: language-independent complex network representation, enrichment of co-occurrence networks with embeddings for short clinical transcripts, and ensemble/multi-view learning to boost classification.  
- Addresses an important clinical problem with potential real-world impact for early dementia intervention.  
- Demonstrates generalizability by testing on multiple datasets in English and Portuguese across clinical contexts.  
- Novel technical idea of enriching networks with word embeddings to handle disfluent, short clinical speech.  
- Manuscript has poor writing quality with many grammar and spelling errors that harm readability.  
- Problem definition is unclear—uncertain whether classification is at transcript or participant level and how aggregation is done.  
- Reported dataset counts contradict stated sample sizes (e.g., Cookie Theft labels vs. table counts).  
- Insufficient justification for choosing network enrichment over simpler approaches like using word embeddings directly.  
- Missing comparisons to straightforward semantic similarity methods and lack of benchmarks against simpler baselines.  
- Evaluation is inadequate: class imbalance not addressed, only accuracy reported, and no F1/balanced metrics provided.  
- No statistical significance testing reported and results are reported with unjustified precision on very small datasets.  
- Fails to leverage domain-specific biomedical NLP tools that might improve performance.  
- Claims of outperforming linguistic features are inconsistent with reported results and lack statistical support.  
- Results are not properly contextualized against prior work; comparisons to reported accuracy ranges are not meaningful due to different datasets.  
- Question: Clarify whether classification is at the transcript or participant level and how participant-level aggregation is handled.  
- Question: Explain why complex network enrichment was chosen instead of using word embeddings directly or other simpler semantic methods.  
- Question: How is class imbalance addressed and why is accuracy used as the primary metric despite its limitations in imbalanced settings?
