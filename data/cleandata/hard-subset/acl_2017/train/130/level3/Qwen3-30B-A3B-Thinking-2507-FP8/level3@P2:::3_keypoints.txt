- Application of complex network features enriched with word embeddings for MCI detection from speech transcripts.
- Multi-view learning combining network, linguistic, and bag-of-words features improves classification (≈10% gain on Cinderella).
- Comparative evaluation across DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD highlights network-based approaches' potential.
- Addresses early MCI detection without requiring manual transcript correction; practically relevant and well-motivated.
- Evaluation on three datasets demonstrates generalizability and that network features can outperform linguistic features on disfluent speech.
- Combining different feature types via multi-view learning yields meaningful accuracy improvements over single-feature classifiers.
- Insufficient methodological detail on the multi-view learning implementation and lack of clarity about which data trained the word embeddings.
- Unjustified removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions), which may eliminate important MCI indicators.
- No statistical significance testing reported for the observed performance improvements.
- Clarity and presentation problems: inadequate description of linguistic features, undefined terms (e.g., WML subtest, ABCD), and poor writing.
- Which dataset(s) were used to train the word embeddings?
- Please justify removing disfluencies or provide results with them retained.
- Why was term frequency used for bag-of-words instead of tf-idf?
- Clarify the exact multi-view learning implementation: how feature spaces are combined and how majority voting is performed.
