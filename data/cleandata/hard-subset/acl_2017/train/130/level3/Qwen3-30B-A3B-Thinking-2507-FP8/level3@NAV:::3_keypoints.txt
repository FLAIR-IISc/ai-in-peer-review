- Contributions: proposes complex networks with word embeddings to detect MCI from transcripts as a lightweight, language-independent representation.
- Contributions: shows network features outperform linguistic features across three datasets.
- Contributions: explores ensemble and multi-view learning to improve classification accuracy.
- Strength: datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, ABCD) and problem statement are well described.
- Strength: approach avoids manual transcription correction, useful for large-scale assessments.
- Strength: results are promising, with multi-view learning improving accuracy by up to ~10%.
- Weakness: methodological details are insufficient, especially the multi-view learning implementation.
- Weakness: linguistic feature set is poorly explained (Section 4.2.2 needs expansion).
- Weakness: removal of disfluencies (filled pauses, repetitions) is unjustified despite their potential as MCI indicators.
- Weakness: Bag-of-Words uses term frequency without justifying omission of tf-idf.
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests reported to validate results.
- Weakness: related work section is superficial and lacks detailed comparisons to prior methods.
- Weakness: manuscript requires substantial editing for clarity; many sentences need rewording.
- Missing details: unclear whether word embeddings were trained on the small target datasets or externally, and dataset segmentation is not explained.
- Missing details: Table 1 lacks essential statistics; acronyms (WML, ABCD, PLN) are undefined; transcription norms are cited without references.
- Overall: core idea is promising but undermined by methodological gaps, insufficient justification, and lack of statistical rigor; major revisions required before publication.
