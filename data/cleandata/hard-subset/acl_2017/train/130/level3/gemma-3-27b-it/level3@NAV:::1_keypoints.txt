- Paper proposes using complex network analysis enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from English and Portuguese speech transcripts — a promising idea, especially for Portuguese.
- Main contribution is exploration of network-based MCI detection in Portuguese, but severely limited by a very small dataset (20 patients + 20 controls), hurting generalizability and statistical power.
- Manuscript tone is inconsistent: introduction overstates results while conclusion downplays them, creating confusion.
- Literature review contains irrelevant and overly broad citations (e.g., Salton 1989, pedestrian detection, sentiment classification) and should be focused.
- Sections 1–3 and parts of 4.1/4.2.3 are verbose and need condensation to emphasize core contributions.
- Training details for word embeddings are unclear — unclear whether only provided datasets or external corpora were used and why.
- Interaction between word-level and subword (n-gram) networks is not clearly explained, nor is it shown whether their combination improves accuracy.
- Definition and criteria for “out-of-vocabulary” words are imprecise and need clarification.
- Manuscript contains numerous technical and typographical errors that must be corrected.
- Novelty: applying network analysis to Portuguese speech transcripts is a valuable and notable aspect.
- Recommendations: provide an English-translated example transcript to aid reader understanding.
- Recommendations: illustrate the network/embedding process with sample outputs (e.g., example networks or embeddings).
- Recommendations: expand and clarify embedding training details and rationale for corpus choices.
- Recommendations: clarify how different network components (word-level vs subword) interact and contribute to results.
- Recommendations: streamline the introduction and literature review to focus on key contributions.
- Questions to authors: specify the corpora used to train embeddings and rationale for choosing them.
- Questions to authors: quantify how combining word-level and subword networks affects performance (provide comparisons).
- Questions to authors: define the criteria used to label words as out-of-vocabulary in this study.
- Additional requests: provide a table of classifier parameters (e.g., SVM regularization).
- Additional requests: perform statistical significance tests to support reported accuracy improvements.
- Additional requests: briefly explain the transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2) for readers unfamiliar with Brazilian Portuguese transcription practices.
