- Paper proposes using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts.  
- Evaluated method on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella-PT, ABCD-PT) and explored ensemble/multi-view combinations with linguistic features.  
- Contribution: applies graph-based representations as an alternative to parsing-dependent linguistic features for MCI detection.  
- Contribution: compiles and evaluates across three (including two Portuguese) datasets to assess generalizability.  
- Contribution: investigates combining network features with traditional linguistic features and multiple classifiers.  
- Strength: addresses an important clinical problem (early MCI detection) with relevant datasets.  
- Strength: inclusion of narrative speech and multilingual datasets is appropriate.  
- Strength: explores multiple classifiers and feature combinations to test robustness.  
- Strength: graph-based approach may be more robust to disfluencies and parsing errors.  
- Weakness: manuscript has numerous grammatical and stylistic errors that impede readability.  
- Weakness: unclear definition of classification instances and inconsistent dataset counts (unclear whether instances are patients, transcripts, or sentences).  
- Weakness: poor motivation for embedding-based graph enrichment versus simpler word-node similarity methods.  
- Weakness: fails to leverage biomedical/NLP domain tools that could improve feature extraction.  
- Weakness: uses accuracy as primary metric without reporting class balance; accuracy may be misleading for imbalanced data.  
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing or confidence intervals; results reported with unjustified precision on small datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on network construction, embedding parameters, and similarity thresholds for reproducibility.  
- Requested: clearly define what constitutes an experimental "instance" (transcript/paragraph/sentence).  
- Requested: report precise class distributions (controls vs. MCI) for each dataset.  
- Requested: provide statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values, confidence intervals) to validate improvements.  
- Requested: justify and detail parameter choices for embeddings, similarity thresholds, and network construction.  
- Requested: supply full preprocessing, feature extraction, and experimental setup details for reproducibility.  
- Requested: compare graph-based method against simple baselines (e.g., TF-IDF + standard classifier).  
- Additional: references are inconsistently formatted and supplementary material needs better organization.  
- Additional: report results separately for manually revised Cinderella and automatically segmented ABCD to evaluate data quality effects.  
- Overall verdict: promising direction but significant methodological, presentation, and reproducibility issues make the manuscript unsuitable for acceptance without major revisions.
