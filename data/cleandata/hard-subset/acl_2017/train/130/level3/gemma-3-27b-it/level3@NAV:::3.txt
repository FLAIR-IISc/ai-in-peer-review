## Review of "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts"

This paper explores the use of complex network features, enriched with word embeddings, for the automatic detection of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. The problem is well-defined and the datasets – including the DementiaBank and novel Portuguese datasets – are clearly described. The overall study is interesting and potentially informative, addressing a significant challenge in early dementia diagnosis.

The primary contribution lies in the application of complex network analysis, augmented with word embeddings, to this specific task. While the core methods aren’t entirely novel, adapting them to MCI detection within the context of potentially noisy speech transcripts is valuable. A secondary contribution is the creation and use of the Portuguese datasets, providing a valuable resource for research in this under-represented language.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. The proposed solution, while reasonable, lacks crucial methodological detail. The exact scheme for enriching the networks with word embeddings is insufficiently described – how are edges created based on cosine similarity? What threshold was used? Furthermore, the description of the “linguistic features” (Section 4.2.2) is inadequate and needs substantial expansion. The datasets used for training the word embeddings are unclear and need to be explicitly stated. The decision to remove disfluencies is questionable and requires justification or reconsideration, as they may hold diagnostic value. The choice of term frequency over tf-idf weighting for the Bag-of-Words approach also lacks rationale. 

Critically, the paper reports no statistical significance tests to support its claims of improved performance. The related work section is superficial, failing to provide in-depth comparisons with established methods. The abstract is overly lengthy and should be condensed. Numerous sentences require rewording for clarity (e.g., lines 22-25, 34-38, 70, 115-117).  Datasets and tools (DementiaBank, Picnic, transcription norms) need proper citations. Acronyms (WML, ABCD, PLN) must be defined.  Preprocessing steps and the order in which they are applied require clarification.  Table 1 lacks sufficient dataset statistics.

**Questions for Authors:**

1.  Please provide a detailed explanation of how word embeddings are integrated into the complex network structure – specifically, the cosine similarity threshold and edge creation process.
2.  What was the rationale for using term frequency instead of tf-idf weighting in the Bag-of-Words classification?
3.  Can you provide a justification for removing disfluencies, or explain how you considered their potential diagnostic value?
4.  Which datasets were used to train the word embeddings?
5.  Please provide statistical significance tests (e.g., t-tests, ANOVA) to support the reported performance differences.



**Additional Comments:**

*   Consider supplementary material to present the full list of linguistic features due to page limits.
*   Ensure compliance with the conference citation style.
*   Address minor grammatical errors and unclear phrasing throughout the manuscript.
