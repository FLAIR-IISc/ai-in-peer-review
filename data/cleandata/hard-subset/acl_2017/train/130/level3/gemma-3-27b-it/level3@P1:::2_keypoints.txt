- Paper proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts by modeling transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with word embeddings.  
- Evaluated across three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona Battery) in English and Portuguese.  
- Key contribution: complex network representations as a potentially robust, language-independent approach resilient to speech disfluencies.  
- Secondary contribution: ensemble and multi-view learning combining network and linguistic features improves performance.  
- Multi-dataset evaluation (including Portuguese) increases generalizability and provides valuable resources.  
- Novel use of complex network analysis addresses limitations of parsing-based methods in disfluent speech.  
- Demonstrated performance gains (e.g., ~10% on Cinderella, ~4.71% on Arizona) through multi-view learning.  
- Tackles clinically relevant problem—early MCI detection with real-world impact.  
- Unclear description of datasets and ML setup: ambiguous instance granularity (sentences vs narratives) and inconsistent counts.  
- Rationale for enriching networks with embeddings is not clearly justified versus simpler similarity methods.  
- Limited use of existing biomedical/NLP resources beyond Coh-Metrix; missed opportunity to leverage specialized tools.  
- Inappropriate evaluation metrics: accuracy only, no F1/precision/recall or class balance discussion.  
- Lack of statistical significance testing for reported improvements; precision of reported numbers questionable given small data.  
- Manuscript suffers from grammatical errors and awkward phrasing, reducing readability.  
- Requests clarification on data instance definition and preprocessing steps.  
- Requests class distribution (MCI vs control) for each dataset.  
- Asks why a particular cosine similarity threshold was chosen for network enrichment and whether alternatives were tested.  
- Requests statistical significance tests (e.g., t-tests, ANOVA) for reported accuracy improvements.  
- Asks for elaboration on parsing limitations and how complex networks specifically address them.  
- Additional comments: references need consistent formatting; supplementary material should be better integrated; consider feature selection for informative network features.
