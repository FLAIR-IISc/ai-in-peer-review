- Novel application: Applies complex network analysis to detect MCI from speech transcripts, offering a potentially robust, language-independent approach.
- Disfluency handling: Proposes a method less reliant on perfect transcripts, aiming to mitigate issues caused by disfluencies in parsing-based features.
- Dataset evaluation: Evaluated across three varied datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona Battery) in different languages and narrative types, supporting generalizability.
- Feature enrichment: Uses word embeddings to enrich network representations for short texts.
- Method combination: Employs ensemble and multi-view learning to combine network, linguistic, and BoW features, yielding performance gains.
- Reported gains: Achieves notable improvements (e.g., ~10% on Cinderella, ~4.71% on Arizona) over baselines.
- Multi-view detail missing: Insufficient description of the multi-view learning scheme, making replication and robustness assessment difficult.
- Linguistic features underdescribed: Limited explanation of which Coh-Metrix features were used and their relevance to MCI; a feature list/table is needed.
- Disfluency removal questionable: Removing disfluencies may discard clinically relevant signals; authors must justify or evaluate the impact.
- No significance testing: Results lack statistical significance measures (p-values/confidence intervals) to confirm observed differences are not by chance.
- Related work limited: Related work section lacks depth and comparison with state-of-the-art ML approaches for MCI detection.
- Embedding corpus unclear: Unclear whether embeddings were trained on the small target datasets or a larger external corpus; this affects embedding quality.
- Request 1 (multi-view): Provide detailed description of the multi-view scheme, classifiers per view, and combination method.
- Request 2 (linguistic features): List the specific Coh-Metrix features used and justify their selection.
- Request 3 (disfluencies): Justify removal of disfluencies and report experiments with/without them.
- Request 4 (stats): Provide statistical significance tests for the reported performance improvements.
- Request 5 (embeddings): Specify the corpus used for training embeddings and discuss dataset-size effects on embedding quality.
- Writing and formatting: Condense the abstract, fix grammar/clarity issues, provide citations/links for datasets/tools, define acronyms (e.g., WML, ABCD), and correct typos throughout.
- Overall verdict: Promising and practically relevant approach with incremental novelty; addressing methodological and reporting weaknesses would strengthen the manuscript.
