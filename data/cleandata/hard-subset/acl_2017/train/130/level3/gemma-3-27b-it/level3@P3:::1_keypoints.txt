- Investigates complex network analysis augmented with word embeddings to detect MCI from Portuguese and English speech transcripts.  
- Proposes a lightweight, language-independent approach aimed at handling disfluencies without manual transcript correction.  
- Evaluated on three datasets: Cookie Theft (English), Cinderella (Portuguese), and ABCD (Portuguese).  
- Reports competitive classification accuracies (~60%–85%) and improvements using ensemble/multi-view feature combinations.  

Main contributions:
- Applies complex network features to MCI detection from speech as an alternative to traditional linguistic parsing.  
- Adapts and evaluates methods on Portuguese, addressing lower-resource language needs.  
- Explores ensemble and multi-view learning to combine network, linguistic, and BoW features.  

Strengths:
- Novel perspective using complex networks that may be robust to speech disfluencies.  
- Multilingual evaluation increases assessment of method generalizability.  
- Demonstrated competitive accuracy compared to prior work.  
- Practical focus on avoiding manual transcript correction.  

Weaknesses:
- Extremely small datasets (e.g., 20 patients + 20 controls; 43 MCI + 21 controls) limit statistical power and generalizability.  
- Contradictory tone between optimistic introduction/results and a downplaying conclusion.  
- Methodological clarity lacking: unclear embedding training, use of external corpora, and interaction between word-level and subword networks.  
- Ambiguous definition and handling of out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words.  
- Literature review contains irrelevant/overly general citations and lacks focus on directly related MCI/speech/network studies.  
- Overly long background/method sections that should be condensed.  
- Multiple technical and typographical errors throughout the manuscript.  

Questions for authors:
- Were external corpora used to train word embeddings or only the provided datasets?  
- How do word-level and subword (n-gram) networks interact, and does their combination improve accuracy?  
- What exactly qualifies as an “out-of-vocabulary” word and how were OOVs handled?  
- Can you provide an example transcript translated to English and its complex network representation with visualization and feature examples?  
- Please clarify the NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2 transcription norms and preprocessing challenges they introduced.  

Additional comments:
- Include example network visualizations and embedding outputs to improve clarity.  
- Conduct more rigorous statistical analysis given small sample sizes.  
- Add a clearer discussion of limitations and future research directions.
