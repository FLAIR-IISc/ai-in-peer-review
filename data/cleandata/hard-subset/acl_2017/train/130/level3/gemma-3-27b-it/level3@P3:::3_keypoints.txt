- Core contribution: Applies complex networks enriched with word embeddings to MCI detection from speech transcripts, showing competitiveness with traditional linguistic-feature methods.  
- Ensemble/multi-view finding: Combining network, embedding, and linguistic features via ensemble and multi-view learning yields improved performance.  
- Language/datasets: Extends prior work by evaluating on Portuguese data in addition to English (DementiaBank) and includes newly collected Portuguese datasets.

Strengths:
- Relevant problem: Addresses an important, timely healthcare task using scalable automated speech analysis.  
- Motivation: Avoids reliance on manual transcript correction, reducing computational cost and error-proneness.  
- Experimental setup: Uses multiple datasets (English and Portuguese) and 5-fold cross-validation for robust evaluation.  
- Competitive results: Complex networks with embeddings perform well vs. linguistic features and bag-of-words; ensembles show promising gains.

Weaknesses:
- Multi-view details lacking: The multi-view learning/combination scheme (aggregation and weighting) is insufficiently described.  
- Linguistic features underspecified: The specific Coh-Metrix metrics used and rationale (and why some were discarded for Portuguese) are not detailed.  
- Embedding training ambiguous: Unclear whether embeddings were trained jointly or separately and what training parameters were used.  
- Disfluency removal questionable: Removing disfluencies is not justified and may remove clinically relevant signals.  
- No statistical tests: Results reported without significance testing, so improvements may be due to chance.  
- Superficial related work: Related-work section lacks depth and precise comparisons to prior methods and features.  
- Presentation issues: Overlong abstract, awkward phrasing, and clarity problems throughout the manuscript.

Questions to authors:
- Provide a detailed description of the multi-view learning scheme, including aggregation method and weights.  
- List all linguistic features used from Coh-Metrix and justify their selection (and omission for Portuguese).  
- Specify on which dataset(s) embeddings were trained and give training parameters (dimension, window, epochs, etc.).  
- Justify removal of disfluencies and provide results with and without disfluency removal.  
- Provide statistical significance tests to support performance claims.

Additional comments:
- Add citations/links for DementiaBank, Picnic picture/Western Aphasia Battery, transcription norms, and Coh-Metrix.  
- Define acronyms (WML, ABCD, PLN) at first use.  
- Clarify preprocessing order (tokenization, lemmatization, etc.).  
- Correct minor errors and typos and ensure conference citation style.  
- Consider supplementary material for detailed linguistic-feature descriptions if page limits prevent inclusion.
