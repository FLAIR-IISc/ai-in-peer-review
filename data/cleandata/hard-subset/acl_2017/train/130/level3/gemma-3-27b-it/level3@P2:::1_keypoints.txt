- Applies complex network (word co-occurrence) features enriched with word embeddings to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from Portuguese speech transcripts.  
- Novelty: uses complex network analysis for cognitively impaired speech in a lower-resource language (Portuguese).  
- Secondary contributions: comparative evaluation against traditional linguistic features and exploration of ensemble and multi-view learning.  
- Evaluated on three datasets: DementiaBank, Cinderella, and ABCD.  
- Strength: method bypasses reliance on robust parsing, useful for agrammatical patient speech.  
- Strength: addresses a gap by focusing on Portuguese, increasing generalizability beyond English.  
- Strength: network features (especially with embeddings) achieve competitive or superior performance; ensembles/multi-view improve results.  
- Strength: evaluation across varied elicitation paradigms (description vs. narrative) increases robustness.  
- Weakness: small dataset sizes (e.g., Cinderella and ABCD ≈20 controls/20–23 MCI) limit statistical power and generalizability.  
- Weakness: paper contains overly detailed and redundant background sections and could be more focused.  
- Weakness: unclear description of how word embeddings were generated (pre-trained vs. dataset-trained) and unclear interaction with subword/n-gram modeling.  
- Weakness: literature review includes tangential or weakly justified citations that should be pruned.  
- Weakness: manuscript has technical and typographical errors needing careful proofreading.  
- Question: were embeddings pre-trained on larger corpora or trained on the provided datasets—specify the corpus if pre-trained.  
- Question: how exactly were co-occurrence networks enriched with semantic info—what cosine-similarity threshold was used and how determined?  
- Question: given small datasets, was any power analysis performed to assess statistical significance of improvements?  
- Question: can the authors provide a transcript example with network visualizations (before/after enrichment) and explanation of how features capture linguistic information?  
- Additional: add an English translation of a representative transcript to improve accessibility.  
- Additional: include ablation studies to quantify the contribution of individual network features.  
- Additional: clarify the definition of “out-of-vocabulary” words (Line 264).
