## Review of "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts"

This paper explores the application of complex network analysis, enriched with word embeddings, to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts in both English and Portuguese. The authors aim to overcome challenges posed by disfluencies in patient speech by utilizing a method less reliant on traditional parsing. While the idea of applying network analysis to this problem is promising, and studying a lower-resource language like Portuguese is valuable, the current submission has several weaknesses.

The primary contribution of this work, as I see it, lies in the initial exploration of complex networks for MCI detection within the specific context of Portuguese speech data. However, this contribution is significantly limited by the very small dataset size (20 patients + 20 controls) which severely restricts the generalizability and statistical power of the findings. 

Several issues detract from the paper’s strength. Firstly, the tone is inconsistent; the introduction presents an overly optimistic view of the results, which is then downplayed in the conclusion, creating a confusing impression. Secondly, the literature review includes irrelevant citations (Salton 1989, pedestrian detection) and overly broad references (sentiment classification), diluting its focus. Sections 1-3, and parts of 4.1/4.2.3 are unnecessarily verbose and should be condensed to emphasize the core contributions. The technical details regarding the training of word embeddings are unclear – it’s not obvious whether only the provided datasets or external corpora were used. The interaction between word-level and subword networks, and whether their combination improves accuracy, requires clarification. The definition of “out-of-vocabulary” words also lacks precision. Finally, the manuscript suffers from numerous technical and typographical errors.

Despite these weaknesses, the study’s novelty in applying network analysis to Portuguese speech transcripts is notable. To improve the manuscript, I recommend: 1) Providing a translated example transcript (English) to aid understanding, 2) Illustrating the network/embedding process with sample outputs, 3) Expanding on the embedding training details, 4) Clarifying the interplay between different network components, 5) Streamlining the introduction and literature review, 6) Correcting all identified errors. 

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details regarding the corpora used to train the word embeddings, and the rationale behind your choice?
2. How does the combination of word-level and subword (n-gram) networks affect performance, and can you provide a quantitative comparison?
3. Could you elaborate on the criteria used to define “out-of-vocabulary” words within the context of this study?



**Additional Comments:**

*  Please provide a table summarizing the parameters used for each classifier (e.g., regularization strength for SVM).
*  Consider performing statistical significance tests to support the reported accuracy improvements.
*  The transcription norms mentioned (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2) could benefit from a brief explanation for readers unfamiliar with Brazilian Portuguese transcription practices.
