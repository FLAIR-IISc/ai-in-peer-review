- Paper investigates using complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts.  
- Datasets (DementiaBank and new Portuguese datasets) are clearly described and valuable for the task.  
- Main contribution: applying complex network analysis with word embeddings to MCI detection on noisy transcripts.  
- Secondary contribution: creation and use of novel Portuguese datasets for under-represented language research.  
- Methodological details are insufficient, especially how embeddings enrich network edges.  
- Unclear how edges are created from cosine similarity and what similarity threshold was used.  
- Description of “linguistic features” (Section 4.2.2) is inadequate and needs expansion.  
- Datasets used to train the word embeddings are not specified.  
- Removal of disfluencies is questionable and requires justification, as disfluencies may be diagnostically relevant.  
- Choice of term frequency over tf-idf for BoW lacks rationale.  
- No statistical significance tests reported to support performance claims.  
- Related work section is superficial and lacks in-depth comparisons to established methods.  
- Abstract is overly long and should be condensed.  
- Numerous sentences need rewording for clarity and grammar throughout the manuscript.  
- Missing citations for datasets and tools (e.g., DementiaBank, Picnic, transcription norms).  
- Acronyms (WML, ABCD, PLN) are undefined and must be expanded.  
- Preprocessing steps and their application order are unclear and should be detailed.  
- Table 1 lacks sufficient dataset statistics.  
- Recommendations: provide full linguistic feature list in supplementary material and ensure conference citation/style compliance.  
- Authors asked to: (1) detail embedding-network integration and cosine threshold, (2) justify TF vs tf-idf choice, (3) justify removal of disfluencies, (4) specify embedding training datasets, (5) provide statistical significance tests.
