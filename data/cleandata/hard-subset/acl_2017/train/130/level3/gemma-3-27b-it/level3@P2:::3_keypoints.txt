- Proposes using complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, aiming for a lightweight, language-independent approach.  
- Uses DementiaBank and two University of São Paulo datasets (including Brazilian Portuguese), expanding multilingual resources.  
- Main contributions: novel application of enriched complex networks for MCI detection and creation/use of Portuguese datasets.  
- Explores ensemble and multi-view learning to combine network, linguistic, and BoW features, reporting performance gains.  
- Novel network-based representation is promising and reportedly handles disfluent/agrammatical speech better than parsing-dependent methods.  
- Inclusion of diverse tasks (narratives, descriptions, immediate/delayed recall) strengthens generalizability.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across classifiers (GaussianNB, KNN, SVM, RF) and a BoW baseline.  
- Reported results show complex networks outperform traditional linguistic features across datasets.  
- Multi-view learning methodology lacks sufficient detail; the combination/weighting scheme is unclear.  
- Description of linguistic features (Coh-Metrix and Coh-Metrix-Dementia) is too brief and needs specifics.  
- Unclear which corpus was used to train the word embeddings; this must be specified.  
- Removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is questionable since disfluencies may be informative of cognitive impairment.  
- No statistical significance testing reported; accuracy differences lack p-values or confidence intervals.  
- Request: provide detailed explanation of the multi-view learning scheme and weight determination.  
- Request: list specific linguistic metrics included from Coh-Metrix and Coh-Metrix-Dementia.  
- Request: specify the corpus used to train the word embeddings.  
- Request: justify disfluency removal or provide an ablation study assessing its impact.  
- Request: include statistical significance tests (e.g., p-values) for reported results.  
- Manuscript needs editing: shorten the abstract and improve clarity/readability.  
- Provide citations/links for datasets/tools, explain acronyms (e.g., WML, ABCD, PLN), and follow ACL citation style.
