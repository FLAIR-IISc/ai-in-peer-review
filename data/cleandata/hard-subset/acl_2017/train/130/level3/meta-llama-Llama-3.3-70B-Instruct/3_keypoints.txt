- Proposes applying existing representations, features, and methods to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech narratives.
- Proposes a self-learning framework to learn bilingual word embedding mappings compatible with any embedding mapping and dictionary induction technique.
- Demonstrates the method can learn high-quality bilingual embeddings from minimal bilingual evidence (e.g., a 25-word dictionary or auto-generated numerals).
- Achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art systems while using much smaller bilingual resources than larger dictionaries or parallel corpora.
- Framework is simple, efficient, and easily integrated with existing embedding mapping and dictionary induction approaches.
- Method is robust, learning good bilingual mappings from very limited bilingual evidence, including distant language pairs.
- Method has potential to operate in a fully unsupervised manner without any bilingual evidence.
