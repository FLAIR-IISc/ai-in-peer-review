- Proposes a novel NLP approach to detect mild cognitive impairment from speech transcripts, claiming 60–85% accuracy but with a less optimistic conclusion than the introduction.  
- Dataset is very limited (20 healthy, 20 control) and Portuguese-only, limiting reproducibility and generalizability.  
- Lacks methodological details: no translated transcript examples, no sample network architectures or embedding illustrations.  
- Unclear use of word embeddings and networks—source of continuous representations and training data not specified.  
- Introduction and related-work sections are overly detailed and include unnecessary references; should be shortened.  
- Paper needs revision to clarify technical details and shorten/organize text for readability.  
- Requires technical corrections: grammar, punctuation, repeated words, and inconsistent table/reference formatting.  
- Main contributions claimed: self-learning framework for bilingual embedding mappings; learning high-quality bilingual embeddings from small seed dictionaries; competitive results versus richer-resource systems.  
- Strengths: simplicity and efficiency of the framework; ability to learn bilingual embeddings without real bilingual data; competitive performance on relevant tasks.  
- Weaknesses: limited dataset, insufficient technical detail on embeddings/networks, and numerous clarity/formatting issues.  
- Questions for authors: how will dataset limitations and generalizability be addressed; can more technical details and examples be provided; what specific revisions will improve clarity and readability?
