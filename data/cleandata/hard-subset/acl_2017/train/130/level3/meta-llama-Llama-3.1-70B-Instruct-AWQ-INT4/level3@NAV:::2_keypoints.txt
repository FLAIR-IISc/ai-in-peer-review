- Proposes a graph-based method that models speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI.
- Evaluates the approach on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets with five-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers, showing competitive accuracy.
- Main contribution: a novel graph-based approach leveraging word co-occurrence for MCI detection.
- Main contribution: enrichment of complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short neuropsychological texts.
- Main contribution: multi-dataset evaluation demonstrating effectiveness across different populations.
- Strength: novel methodology that contributes to the field of MCI detection from transcripts.
- Strength: reported results are competitive with traditional methods, achieving high accuracy.
- Strength: evaluation on multiple datasets increases generalizability claims.
- Weakness: poor writing quality with numerous grammatical and spelling errors.
- Weakness: unclear motivation for graph enrichment compared to simpler word-node similarity methods.
- Weakness: does not leverage domain-specific biomedical NLP tools that could improve effectiveness.
- Weakness: results reported with unjustified precision on small datasets and lack statistical significance testing.

- Question: how will the authors address poor writing quality in the final version?
