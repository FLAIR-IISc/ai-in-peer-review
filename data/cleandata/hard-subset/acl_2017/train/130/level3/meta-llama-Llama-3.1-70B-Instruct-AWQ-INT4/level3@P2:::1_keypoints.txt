- Proposes detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts by modeling transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings.  
- Shows complex network features outperform traditional linguistic features across datasets.  
- Introduces a method to enrich complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short neuropsychological texts.  
- Evaluates the approach on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery in Portuguese) to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Novel combination of complex network features and embeddings for MCI detection.  
- Effective use of word embeddings to improve short-text representations in assessments.  
- Multi-dataset evaluation supports the approach’s applicability.  
- Very small dataset size (20 patients + 20 controls) limits representativeness.  
- Transcripts in Portuguese may restrict reproducibility and broader understanding.  
- Lacks technical details on training of continuous word representations/embeddings (corpus, hyperparameters).  
- Unclear handling and source of out-of-vocabulary words.  
- Insufficient information on transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).  
- Needs a plan to address small dataset size and limited reproducibility in future work.  
- Contains multiple technical and typographical errors (e.g., wording mistakes, PLN→NLP, duplicated words, decimal formatting, duplicate DOI/incorrect reference link) that must be fixed.
