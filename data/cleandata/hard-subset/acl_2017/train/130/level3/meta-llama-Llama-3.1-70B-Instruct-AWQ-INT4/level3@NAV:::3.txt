**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts. They evaluate their approach on three datasets and compare the performance of complex network features with traditional linguistic metrics and bag-of-words representation. The results show that complex network features are competitive with traditional methods and can be improved by combining them with linguistic metrics and bag-of-words representation using ensemble and multi-view learning.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a novel approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings.
2. They evaluate their approach on three datasets and compare the performance of complex network features with traditional linguistic metrics and bag-of-words representation.
3. They demonstrate that complex network features can be improved by combining them with linguistic metrics and bag-of-words representation using ensemble and multi-view learning.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors evaluate their approach on three datasets, which provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.
3. The results show that complex network features are competitive with traditional methods, which demonstrates the potential of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper lacks methodological details, such as the exact scheme used for multi-view learning and the threshold parameter used for word embeddings.
2. The linguistic features used in the paper are not well described, and the authors do not provide a clear explanation of how they were extracted.
3. The paper does not report statistical significance tests to support the experimental conclusions.
4. The related work coverage is superficial, and the authors do not provide a detailed comparison with prior methods.
5. The paper requires significant editing to improve readability and clarity.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the multi-view learning scheme used in the paper?
2. How did you select the threshold parameter for word embeddings, and what is the justification for this choice?
3. Can you provide a more detailed explanation of the linguistic features used in the paper and how they were extracted?
4. Why did you not report statistical significance tests to support the experimental conclusions?
