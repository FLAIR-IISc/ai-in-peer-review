**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a method for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts. They evaluate their model on three datasets, including one from the DementiaBank and two in Portuguese. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets. The authors also combine the classifiers through ensemble and multi-view learning, achieving improvements in accuracy.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Applying complex networks to detect MCI**: The authors propose a novel approach to detecting MCI using complex networks, which is a departure from traditional linguistic feature-based methods.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors show that enriching complex networks with word embeddings improves the representation of short texts and leads to better classification results.
3. **Evaluating the method on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their method on three datasets, including one in English and two in Portuguese, demonstrating the generalizability of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to detecting MCI using complex networks, which is a departure from traditional linguistic feature-based methods.
2. **Improved representation of short texts**: The authors show that enriching complex networks with word embeddings improves the representation of short texts, leading to better classification results.
3. **Good performance on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their method on three datasets, including one in English and two in Portuguese, demonstrating the generalizability of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Small dataset size**: The authors use a small dataset size, which may not be representative of the larger population.
2. **Limited reproducibility**: The authors do not provide enough technical details, such as an English-translated transcript example, and sample network/embedding outputs, which may limit the reproducibility of their results.
3. **Unclear methodology**: The authors do not clearly explain how they trained the word embeddings and how they combined the word-level and subword networks.
4. **Technical errors**: The authors have multiple technical and typographical errors in their paper, such as incorrect citations and formatting issues.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more technical details on how you trained the word embeddings and how you combined the word-level and subword networks?
2. How did you select the threshold parameter for the cosine similarity between words?
3. Can you provide an English-translated transcript example and sample network/embedding outputs to facilitate reproducibility?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of small dataset size in future work?
