**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a graph-based method for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. The authors model transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their model on three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets, using five-fold cross-validation and multiple classifiers. The results show that the proposed method is competitive with traditional methods and can achieve high accuracy in detecting MCI.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Graph-based approach for MCI detection**: The authors propose a novel graph-based approach for detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which leverages word co-occurrence to encode features.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors enrich complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments.
3. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their model on three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting MCI.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The authors propose a novel graph-based approach for detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Effective results**: The results show that the proposed method is competitive with traditional methods and can achieve high accuracy in detecting MCI.
3. **Multi-dataset evaluation**: The authors evaluate their model on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in detecting MCI across different populations.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Poor writing quality**: The writing quality of the paper is poor, with numerous grammatical and spelling errors.
2. **Unclear motivation**: The motivation for the proposed graph enrichment versus simpler word-node similarity approaches is unclear.
3. **Lack of domain-specific tools**: The authors do not leverage domain-specific biomedical NLP tools, which may limit the effectiveness of their approach.
4. **Inconsistent results**: The results are reported with unjustified precision on very small datasets, and lack statistical significance testing.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify the motivation for the proposed graph enrichment versus simpler word-node similarity approaches?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of poor writing quality in the final version of the paper?
3. Can you provide more details on the domain-specific biomedical NLP tools that could be leveraged to improve the effectiveness of your approach?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of inconsistent results and lack of statistical significance testing in the final version of the paper?
