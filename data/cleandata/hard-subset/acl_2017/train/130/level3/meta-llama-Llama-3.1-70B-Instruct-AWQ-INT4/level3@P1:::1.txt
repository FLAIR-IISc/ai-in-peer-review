This paper presents a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex network features and word embeddings. The authors propose a method to model transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets. The authors also combine classifiers through ensemble and multi-view learning, achieving improvements in accuracy.

**Main Contributions:**

1. The authors propose a novel approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex network features and word embeddings.
2. They demonstrate that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.
3. The authors combine classifiers through ensemble and multi-view learning, achieving improvements in accuracy.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing (NLP) and cognitive impairment detection.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method using three datasets, including a Portuguese dataset, which is a lower-resource language.
3. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets, which is a significant finding.
4. The authors combine classifiers through ensemble and multi-view learning, achieving improvements in accuracy, which demonstrates the effectiveness of their approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The dataset used in the study is very small (20 patients + 20 controls), which may not be representative of the larger population.
2. The transcripts are in Portuguese, which may limit the reproducibility and broader understanding of the results.
3. The literature review contains some irrelevant or overly general citations, which should be shortened and focused.
4. Sections 1–3 and parts of 4.1/4.2.3 are overly long and need compression to focus on core contributions.
5. There are some technical/typographical errors that need to be fixed.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more technical details about how the continuous word representations/embeddings were trained, including whether only ABCD+Cinderella or external corpora were used?
2. How do you define and handle "out-of-vocabulary" words mentioned in the methods?
3. Can you provide a clearer explanation of how embeddings are computed for Portuguese and transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2)?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of small dataset size and limited reproducibility in future studies?
