**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach for detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex network features enriched with word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and apply machine learning algorithms to classify MCI participants from healthy controls. The study uses three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery, and reports competitive results with traditional methods.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Application of complex network features to MCI detection**: The authors successfully apply complex network features to detect MCI from speech transcripts, demonstrating the potential of this approach in neuropsychological assessments.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors propose a novel method to enrich complex networks with word embeddings, which improves the representation of short texts and enhances classification performance.
3. **Comparative evaluation with traditional methods**: The study provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach against traditional methods, including linguistic features and bag-of-words representation.

**Strengths**

1. **Interesting and informative study**: The paper presents a well-motivated study that addresses a significant problem in neuropsychological assessments.
2. **Reasonable experiments and results**: The authors design reasonable experiments and report competitive results with traditional methods.
3. **Good coverage of related work**: The paper provides a good overview of related work in the field, although some prior methods and direct comparisons could be explored in more depth.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Methodological details missing**: Some important methodological details, such as the exact scheme for multi-view learning, are not provided.
2. **Insufficient description of linguistic features**: The set of linguistic features used in the study is not sufficiently described, and Section 4.2.2 requires expansion.
3. **Lack of statistical significance tests**: The paper does not report statistical significance tests to support experimental conclusions.
4. **Numerous errors and typos**: The manuscript requires considerable editing to improve readability and clarity, including fixing minor errors and typos.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Please clarify the exact scheme for multi-view learning and provide more details on the feature extraction process.
2. Can you provide more information on the linguistic features used in the study, including their definitions and justifications?
3. How did you select the threshold parameter for embedding cosine distance, and what is the rationale behind this choice?
4. Can you provide more details on the preprocessing steps, including lemmatization and other normalization steps?
5. How did you decide to remove disfluencies, and what is the justification for this choice?

**Additional Comments**

The paper has potential, but it requires significant revisions to address the mentioned weaknesses and improve clarity. The authors should provide more methodological details, expand the description of linguistic features, and report statistical significance tests to support experimental conclusions. Additionally, the manuscript needs considerable editing to improve readability and clarity.
