- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks enriched with word embeddings, evaluated on three datasets.  
- Novel approach: uses complex networks and word embeddings instead of traditional linguistic features.  
- Shows complex networks can effectively represent speech transcripts and capture semantic information.  
- Compares the method against traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words, reporting competitive performance.  
- Innovative methodology distinct from prior linguistic feature–based MCI detection.  
- Effective transcript representation that incorporates semantic information via embeddings.  
- Empirical comparison with baselines demonstrates competitive accuracy.  
- Manuscript suffers from poor grammar and spelling, needing revision.  
- Motivation for graph enrichment vs. simpler word-node similarity methods is unclear.  
- Does not utilize domain-specific biomedical NLP tools that could improve results.  
- Class distribution is not reported clearly, making accuracy a potentially misleading metric.  
- F1 scores and class balance should be reported for proper evaluation.  
- Results lack statistical significance testing and report unjustified precision on small datasets.  
- Request for clearer motivation behind using complex networks and embeddings for MCI detection.  
- Ask how authors will address the manuscript’s writing quality issues.  
- Request clarification of dataset class distributions and plans to handle imbalance.  
- Ask how domain-specific biomedical NLP tools might be incorporated and for details on statistical significance testing.
