**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to capture semantic information. They evaluate their approach on three datasets and compare the performance of their method with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation. The results show that their approach is competitive with traditional methods and can achieve high accuracy in detecting MCI.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel approach to MCI detection**: The authors propose a new approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings, which is different from traditional linguistic feature-based approaches.
2. **Effective representation of speech transcripts**: The authors demonstrate that complex networks can effectively represent speech transcripts and capture semantic information, which is useful for MCI detection.
3. **Comparison with traditional methods**: The authors compare their approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, showing that their approach is competitive with traditional methods.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to MCI detection, which is different from traditional linguistic feature-based approaches.
2. **Effective representation of speech transcripts**: The authors demonstrate that complex networks can effectively represent speech transcripts and capture semantic information.
3. **Comparison with traditional methods**: The authors compare their approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, showing that their approach is competitive with traditional methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Poor writing quality**: The manuscript needs grammar and spelling revision.
2. **Unclear motivation**: The motivation for the proposed graph enrichment versus simpler word-node similarity approaches is unclear.
3. **Lack of domain-specific tools**: The authors do not leverage domain-specific biomedical NLP tools.
4. **Unclear class distribution**: The class distribution is unclear, making accuracy a questionable metric; F1 (and class balance) should be reported.
5. **Lack of statistical significance testing**: The results are reported with unjustified precision on very small datasets and lack statistical significance testing.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the motivation for using complex networks and word embeddings for MCI detection?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of poor writing quality in the manuscript?
3. Can you provide more information on the class distribution of the datasets and how you plan to address the issue of unclear class distribution?
4. How do you plan to leverage domain-specific biomedical NLP tools in your approach?
5. Can you provide more details on the statistical significance testing of the results?
