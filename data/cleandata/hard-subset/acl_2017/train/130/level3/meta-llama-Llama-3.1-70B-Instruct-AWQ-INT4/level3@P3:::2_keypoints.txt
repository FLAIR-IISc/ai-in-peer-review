- Proposes a graph-based method that models speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, evaluated on three datasets with competitive results.

- Contribution: First application of complex network features for MCI detection from transcripts.

- Contribution: Integration of word embeddings into the complex networks to capture semantic information in short clinical texts.

- Contribution: Empirical evaluation on three datasets, including DementiaBank and two Portuguese corpora.

- Strength: Addresses a clear clinical problem with well-motivated methodology.

- Strength: Uses appropriate datasets and presents a thorough experimental evaluation.

- Strength: Novel combination of complex network features and embeddings with a clear methodological explanation.

- Weakness: Paper writing is poor with numerous grammatical and spelling errors.

- Weakness: Machine learning problem formulation and instance definition are unclear; dataset/tables describe studies rather than transcript-level data.

- Weakness: Inconsistent reported counts and unclear class distribution make accuracy an unreliable metric.

- Weakness: Results show unjustified precision on small datasets and lack statistical significance testing.

- Weakness: Does not leverage domain-specific biomedical NLP tools that could improve performance.

- Question: Provide more details on preprocessing and how disfluencies in transcripts were handled.

- Question: Explain how the cosine similarity threshold was selected and justify the chosen value.

- Question: Clarify class distributions for each dataset and how class imbalance was handled.

- Question: How will the authors address dataset size limits and the lack of statistical significance testing?

- Comment: Recommend using more robust evaluation metrics (e.g., F1-score) to account for class imbalance.

- Comment: Suggest applying domain-specific biomedical NLP tools to enhance feature extraction and insights.

- Comment: Ask for details on computational resources and runtime for training/evaluation.

- Comment: Paper would benefit from a more detailed related-work discussion and clearer differentiation from existing methods.
