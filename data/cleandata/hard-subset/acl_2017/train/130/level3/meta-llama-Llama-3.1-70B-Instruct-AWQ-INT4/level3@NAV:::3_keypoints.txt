- Proposes a novel MCI detection method modeling speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings.
- Evaluates the approach on three datasets and compares complex-network features to linguistic metrics and bag-of-words.
- Shows complex-network features are competitive and improve when combined with linguistic features and bag-of-words via ensemble/multi-view learning.

- Contribution: novel complex-network + word-embedding approach for MCI detection from transcripts.
- Contribution: empirical evaluation across three datasets comparing to traditional representations.
- Contribution: demonstration that ensemble and multi-view combinations boost complex-network feature performance.

- Strength: introduces a significant, novel modeling approach for MCI detection from speech.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on multiple datasets.
- Strength: results indicate competitive performance of the proposed features versus traditional methods.

- Weakness: lacks methodological details on the multi-view learning scheme and embedding threshold.
- Weakness: linguistic features are poorly described and extraction methods are unclear.
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests reported for experimental conclusions.
- Weakness: related work coverage is superficial with insufficient comparison to prior methods.
- Weakness: paper requires substantial editing to improve readability and clarity.

- Question: please provide detailed description of the multi-view learning scheme used.
- Question: how was the word-embedding threshold selected and what justifies it?
- Question: please detail the linguistic features used and their extraction procedures.
- Question: why were statistical significance tests omitted and can they be provided?
- Question: please supply a more detailed comparison with prior methods and clarify differences.
