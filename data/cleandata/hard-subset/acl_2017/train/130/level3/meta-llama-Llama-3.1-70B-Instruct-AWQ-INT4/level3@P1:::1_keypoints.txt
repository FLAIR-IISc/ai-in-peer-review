- Proposes a novel method to detect MCI from speech transcripts using complex network features enriched with word embeddings.  
- Models transcripts as complex networks and augments them with embeddings to better represent short neuropsychological texts.  
- Combines classifiers via ensemble and multi-view learning, achieving improved accuracy.

- Novel contribution to NLP-based cognitive impairment detection using network+embedding representations.  
- Thorough evaluation across three datasets, including a low-resource Portuguese dataset.  
- Shows complex networks outperform traditional linguistic features across datasets.  
- Ensemble and multi-view classifier fusion yields accuracy improvements.

- Very small primary dataset (20 patients + 20 controls), limiting representativeness and statistical power.  
- Use of Portuguese transcripts may restrict reproducibility and broader applicability.  
- Literature review contains irrelevant/overly general citations and should be tightened.  
- Several sections (1–3, parts of 4.1/4.2.3) are overly long and need compression.  
- Some technical and typographical errors require correction.

- How were the word embeddings trained: only ABCD+Cinderella or were external corpora used?  
- How are out-of-vocabulary words defined and handled in the method?  
- Please clarify how embeddings are computed for Portuguese and the role of transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2).  
- How will the authors address small dataset size and limited reproducibility in future work?
