This paper proposes a novel approach to detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex networks and word embeddings. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on three datasets, including the DementiaBank and two Portuguese datasets. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. Studying NLP for detecting cognitive impairment and handling Portuguese, a lower-resource language.
2. Proposing a novel approach to modeling speech transcripts as complex networks and enriching them with word embeddings.
3. Evaluating the proposed method on three datasets and achieving competitive results with traditional methods.

The strengths of this submission are:

1. The authors tackle a challenging problem in the field of NLP and cognitive impairment, and their approach shows promise.
2. The use of complex networks and word embeddings is a novel and interesting approach to modeling speech transcripts.
3. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method on three datasets, including a comparison with traditional methods.

However, there are also some weaknesses:

1. The dataset used in this study is very small, with only 20 patients and 20 controls in the Cinderella dataset, which may not be representative of the larger population.
2. The authors do not provide enough technical details about their method, such as how the word embeddings were trained and how the complex networks were constructed.
3. The literature review is not focused and includes some irrelevant or overly general citations.
4. The text is sometimes repetitive and could be compressed to focus on the core contributions.
5. There are some technical and typographical errors that need to be fixed.

To improve this submission, the authors could:

1. Provide more technical details about their method, such as the training process for the word embeddings and the construction of the complex networks.
2. Add more datasets to the evaluation to increase the robustness of the results.
3. Focus the literature review on the most relevant and recent studies in the field.
4. Compress the text to focus on the core contributions and avoid repetition.
5. Fix the technical and typographical errors.

Questions to the authors:

1. How did you train the word embeddings, and what corpus did you use?
2. Can you provide more details about the construction of the complex networks and how you chose the parameters?
3. How did you select the features for the linguistic metrics, and are there any other features that could be used?
4. Can you provide more information about the demographics of the participants in the datasets?
5. How do you plan to extend this work to other languages and datasets?

Additional comments:

* The authors should consider adding more visualizations to help illustrate the complex networks and word embeddings.
* The use of ensemble and multi-view learning is an interesting approach, but the authors could provide more details about how they implemented these methods.
* The authors should consider adding more discussion about the limitations of their approach and potential avenues for future work.
