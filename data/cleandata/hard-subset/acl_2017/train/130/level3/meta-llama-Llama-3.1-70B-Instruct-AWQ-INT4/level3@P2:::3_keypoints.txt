- Proposes using complex network features enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts; evaluated on DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery with competitive results.
- Contribution: applies complex network features to MCI detection, demonstrating potential for neuropsychological assessment.
- Contribution: introduces enrichment of complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts and improve classification.
- Contribution: provides comparative evaluation against traditional methods (linguistic features, bag-of-words).
- Strength: well-motivated and informative study addressing an important clinical problem.
- Strength: experiments are reasonable and report competitive results with baseline methods.
- Strength: good coverage of related work, though some prior methods and direct comparisons could be expanded.
- Weakness: missing methodological details, notably the exact multi-view learning scheme.
- Weakness: insufficient description of the linguistic feature set; Section 4.2.2 needs expansion.
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests reported to support experimental claims.
- Weakness: manuscript contains numerous errors and typos and requires substantial editing for clarity.
- Question: clarify the exact multi-view learning scheme and provide detailed feature extraction steps.
- Question: provide definitions, selection criteria, and justification for the linguistic features used.
- Question: explain how the threshold for embedding cosine distance was selected and rationale for that choice.
- Question: provide detailed preprocessing steps, including lemmatization and other normalization procedures.
- Question: justify the decision to remove disfluencies and describe how that choice was made.
- Additional comment: paper has potential but needs significant revisions to add methodological detail, statistical testing, and improve readability.
