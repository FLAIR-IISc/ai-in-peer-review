- Summary: Models speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI, evaluated on three datasets, and reports complex networks outperform linguistic features.

- Contribution 1: Novel approach using complex networks + word embeddings for MCI detection from speech transcripts.

- Contribution 2: Empirical evaluation on three datasets comparing complex network features with linguistic and statistical features.

- Contribution 3: Results indicate complex network features are suitable and outperform linguistic features across datasets.

- Strength 1: Novel and significant methodological contribution combining complex networks and embeddings.

- Strength 2: Evaluation across three datasets provides comprehensive assessment.

- Strength 3: Promising empirical finding that complex networks outperform linguistic features.

- Weakness 1: Lacks methodological details (exact multi-view learning scheme and embedding cosine-distance threshold).

- Weakness 2: "Linguistic features" set is insufficiently described; Section 4.2.2 needs expansion.

- Weakness 3: No statistical significance tests reported to support conclusions.

- Weakness 4: Related work coverage is superficial; missing detailed comparisons with core prior methods.

- Weakness 5: Abstract is too long and should be shortened.

- Weakness 6: Paper requires substantial editing to improve readability and clarity.

- Weakness 7: Removal of disfluencies is unjustified and needs explanation or reconsideration.

- Weakness 8: Use of term-frequency instead of tf-idf in Bag-of-Words classification is not justified.

- Question 1: Provide exact multi-view learning scheme and the threshold used for embedding cosine distance.

- Question 2: Expand and detail the set of linguistic features and extraction methods.

- Question 3: Provide statistical significance tests for experimental results.

- Question 4: Give more precise methodological descriptions of core prior methods and direct comparisons.

- Question 5: Justify removal of disfluencies and choice of term-frequency over tf-idf.

- Question 6: Provide more details on datasets, including which datasets were used to train the word embeddings.

- Question 7: Clarify text preprocessing steps and their order (e.g., lemmatization and normalization).
