**Paper Summary and Main Contributions**

The paper "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts" proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex network features and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their model on three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery in Portuguese. The main contributions of this work are:

1. **Application of complex network features to detect MCI**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of complex network features in detecting MCI from speech transcripts, outperforming traditional linguistic features in all datasets.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors propose a method to enrich complex networks with word embeddings, which improves the representation of short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments.
3. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their model on three datasets, including the DementiaBank, Cinderella, and Arizona-Battery in Portuguese, demonstrating the generalizability of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach to detect MCI**: The authors propose a novel approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts using complex network features and word embeddings, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Effective use of word embeddings**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of word embeddings in improving the representation of short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments.
3. **Evaluation on multiple datasets**: The authors evaluate their model on multiple datasets, demonstrating the generalizability of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Small dataset size**: The dataset size is very small (20 patients + 20 controls), which may not be representative of the larger population.
2. **Limited reproducibility**: The transcripts are in Portuguese, which may limit the reproducibility and broader understanding of the results.
3. **Technical details**: The paper lacks technical details, such as the training of continuous word representations/embeddings, which may make it difficult for readers to replicate the results.
4. **Literature review**: The literature review contains some irrelevant or overly general citations, which should be shortened and focused.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more technical details on the training of continuous word representations/embeddings, including the corpus used and the hyperparameters?
2. How do you handle out-of-vocabulary words, and what is the source of these words?
3. Can you provide more information on the transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2) used in the study?
4. How do you plan to address the issue of small dataset size and limited reproducibility in future work?
