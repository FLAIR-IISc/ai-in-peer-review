- Summary: Models speech transcripts as complex networks enriched with word embeddings to detect MCI; evaluated on three datasets (DementiaBank + two Portuguese) and found complex-network methods outperform linguistic features, with ensembles improving accuracy.

- Contribution 1: Introduces the use of complex networks for MCI detection as an alternative to traditional linguistic-feature approaches.

- Contribution 2: Proposes enriching complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts and boost classification.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates the approach on three datasets (English and Portuguese), suggesting cross-lingual applicability.

- Strength 1: Innovative methodology that departs from standard linguistic-feature pipelines.

- Strength 2: Improved representation of short transcripts via embedding-enriched networks.

- Strength 3: Consistent performance gains across multiple datasets.

- Weakness 1: Dataset sizes are small, limiting generalizability to larger populations.

- Weakness 2: Reproducibility is limited by missing technical details and lack of example transcripts/network outputs.

- Weakness 3: Methodological details are unclear, specifically embedding training and combining word/subword networks.

- Weakness 4: Paper contains technical and typographical errors (incorrect citations, formatting issues).

- Question 1: Please provide detailed methods for training word embeddings and combining word-level and subword networks.

- Question 2: How was the cosine-similarity threshold between words selected?

- Question 3: Can you supply an English-translated transcript example and sample network/embedding outputs for reproducibility?

- Question 4: How will you address small dataset size in future work?
