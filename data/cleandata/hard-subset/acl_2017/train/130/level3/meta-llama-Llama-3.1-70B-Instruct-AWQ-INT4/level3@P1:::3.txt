**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel approach to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts using complex network features and word embeddings. The authors model speech transcripts into complex networks and enrich them with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments. They evaluate their approach on three datasets and compare the results with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation. The results show that complex networks are suitable for detecting MCI and outperform linguistic features in all datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Application of complex network features to MCI detection**: The authors propose a novel approach to detect MCI from speech transcripts using complex network features, which is a new contribution to the field.
2. **Enriching complex networks with word embeddings**: The authors enrich complex networks with word embeddings to better represent short texts produced in neuropsychological assessments, which is a new application of word embeddings in the field.
3. **Comparison with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation**: The authors compare their approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, which provides a comprehensive evaluation of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. **Interesting and informative study**: The paper presents an interesting and informative study on detecting MCI from speech transcripts, which is a challenging task.
2. **Well-described problem and domain**: The authors provide a clear description of the problem and domain, which helps to understand the context of the study.
3. **Reasonable proposed solution and experiments**: The authors propose a reasonable solution and experiments to evaluate their approach, which provides a solid foundation for the study.
4. **Comparison with traditional approaches**: The authors compare their approach with traditional linguistic features and bag-of-words representation, which provides a comprehensive evaluation of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Missing methodological details**: Some important methodological details are missing, such as the exact scheme of the multi-view learning approach.
