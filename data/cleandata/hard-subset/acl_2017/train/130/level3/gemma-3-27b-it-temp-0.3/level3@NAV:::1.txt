## Review of "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts"

This paper investigates the application of complex network analysis, enriched with word embeddings, to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from Portuguese speech transcripts. The authors explore a combination of network-based features, linguistic features, and traditional bag-of-words representations, utilizing several classification algorithms. While the study addresses an important problem – leveraging NLP for cognitive impairment detection in a lower-resource language – and demonstrates promising results (accuracy ranging from ~60% to 85%), several issues hinder its overall impact and reproducibility.

The primary contribution of this work, as I see it, lies in the exploration of complex network features for MCI detection *specifically within the context of the Portuguese language*. This is a valuable step, as most research in this area focuses on English. The combination of network analysis with word embeddings is a reasonable approach to capture semantic information, and the reported accuracy levels are encouraging. 

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. The most significant is the extremely small dataset (20 patients + 20 controls), which severely limits the generalizability of the findings. The reliance on Portuguese transcripts further restricts broader applicability and makes independent verification difficult. The paper’s tone is also inconsistent; the introduction is overly optimistic about the results, while the conclusion acknowledges the difficulty of distinguishing between healthy and impaired subjects.

Furthermore, the technical details are insufficient. The method for training word embeddings is unclear – was it solely on the provided datasets (ABCD+Cinderella) or were external corpora utilized? The interaction between word-level and subword (n-gram) networks is ambiguous, and it’s unclear if combining them improves performance. The definition of “out-of-vocabulary” words needs clarification. The literature review includes irrelevant citations (Salton 1989, pedestrian detection) and should be more focused. Sections 1-3 and parts of 4.1/4.2.3 are overly verbose and could be significantly condensed. Numerous technical errors (e.g., “as it a lightweight”, PLN→NLP) detract from the paper’s professionalism.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Application:** Applying complex network analysis to MCI detection in Portuguese is a valuable contribution.
*   **Promising Results:** The reported accuracy levels, while limited by dataset size, suggest the potential of this approach.
*   **Multi-faceted Feature Engineering:** Combining network features with linguistic and bag-of-words representations is a reasonable strategy.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Small Dataset:** The extremely limited sample size severely restricts generalizability.
*   **Language Specificity:** The focus on Portuguese limits broader applicability and reproducibility.
*   **Insufficient Technical Detail:** Lack of clarity regarding embedding training, network interaction, and OOV word definition.
*   **Inconsistent Tone:** Discrepancy between optimistic introduction and cautious conclusion.
*   **Literature Review Quality:** Inclusion of irrelevant citations and lack of focus.
*   **Verbosity:** Sections 1-3 and parts of 4.1/4.2.3 are overly long.
*   **Technical Errors:** Numerous typos and formatting issues.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Please provide a detailed explanation of how the word embeddings were trained. Were external corpora used, and if so, which ones?
2.  Can you clarify the interaction between the word-level and subword (n-gram) networks? Does combining them improve classification accuracy? If so, by how much?
3.  Please define what constitutes an “out-of-vocabulary” word in your context.
4.  Could you provide an example of an English-translated transcript (even a short excerpt) to illustrate the nature of the data?
5.  Could you provide a visualization of a sample network/embedding output to help understand the representation?



**Additional Comments:**

*   Please address all identified technical and typographical errors.
*   Consider providing the code and data (or a representative subset) to facilitate reproducibility.
*   A more focused discussion of related work, specifically in the area of MCI detection using NLP, would be beneficial.
