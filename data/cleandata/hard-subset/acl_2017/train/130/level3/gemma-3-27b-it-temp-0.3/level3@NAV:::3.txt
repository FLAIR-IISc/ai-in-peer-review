## Review of "Enriching Complex Networks with Word Embeddings for Detecting Mild Cognitive Impairment from Speech Transcripts"

This paper investigates the use of complex network features, enriched with word embeddings, for the automatic detection of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts. The problem is well-motivated, and the datasets – including the valuable DementiaBank and newly incorporated Brazilian Portuguese datasets – are clearly described. The overall study is interesting and informative, offering a potentially valuable approach to MCI detection, particularly given the challenges of parsing disfluent speech.

The core contribution of this work lies in the application of complex network analysis, augmented with word embeddings, to the task of MCI detection from speech. While the individual components (complex networks, word embeddings, MCI detection) are not novel, their combination and evaluation on multiple datasets, including a novel Portuguese dataset, represents a worthwhile contribution. A secondary contribution is the exploration of multi-view learning to combine different feature sets (network features, linguistic features, bag-of-words) for improved performance.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. Firstly, the methodological details are insufficient. The multi-view learning scheme is not clearly explained – how are the individual classifiers combined, and what weighting is applied? The description of the “linguistic features” (Section 4.2.2) is inadequate; a more detailed breakdown of these features is necessary. The datasets used to train the word embeddings are vaguely described; clarification is crucial. The decision to remove disfluencies is questionable and requires stronger justification or reconsideration, as they may contain valuable diagnostic information. The choice of term frequency over tf-idf weighting for the bag-of-words model also lacks explanation.

Furthermore, the paper lacks statistical significance testing to support its experimental conclusions. The reported accuracy improvements should be validated with appropriate statistical tests. The related work section is superficial and needs to be expanded with more in-depth discussion of core prior methods and direct comparisons. The abstract is overly lengthy and should be condensed. Numerous sentences require rewording for clarity and precision (as noted by the supervisor). Missing citations (e.g., line 172) and dataset/tool links (DementiaBank, Picnic, transcription norms) need to be addressed.

**Strengths:**

*   **Relevant Problem:** Addresses an important clinical challenge – early MCI detection.
*   **Dataset Diversity:** Utilizes both English and Portuguese datasets, enhancing generalizability.
*   **Novel Combination:** Explores a promising combination of complex network analysis and word embeddings.
*   **Multi-View Learning:** Investigates the benefits of combining different feature types.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Insufficient Methodological Detail:** Lack of clarity regarding multi-view learning, linguistic features, and word embedding training.
*   **Questionable Preprocessing:** Removal of disfluencies without adequate justification.
*   **Lack of Statistical Significance:** Absence of statistical tests to validate results.
*   **Superficial Related Work:** Limited discussion of relevant prior research.
*   **Clarity and Readability:** Numerous sentences require rewording for improved clarity.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Please provide a detailed description of the multi-view learning scheme used, including the method for combining individual classifiers and any weighting strategies employed.
2.  Can you justify the removal of disfluencies, or explain why their inclusion was not considered?
3.  Why was term frequency chosen over tf-idf weighting for the bag-of-words model?
4.  Which specific datasets were used to train the word embeddings?
5.  Please provide a more detailed breakdown of the linguistic features used in your analysis.



**Additional Comments:**

*   Consider providing supplementary material for the full list of linguistic features if page limits are a concern.
*   Ensure adherence to the conference citation style.
*   Address all minor grammatical errors and typos throughout the manuscript.
