- Proposes using complex networks enriched with word embeddings to classify MCI vs. controls from speech transcripts, evaluated on English DementiaBank and two Portuguese datasets.  
- Reports classification accuracies of roughly 60–85% across datasets, features, and classifiers, with multi-view/ensemble methods often performing best.  
- Primary contribution: applies NLP methods for MCI detection to Brazilian Portuguese, extending work to a lower-resource language.  
- Secondary contribution: shows complex network features + embeddings can be a parser-free alternative for noisy, disfluent clinical speech.  
- Strength: tackles an important clinical problem (early MCI detection) for an underrepresented language.  
- Strength: CN approach is well-motivated for robustness to agrammatical/disfluent patient speech where parsers fail.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation across three datasets, multiple feature sets (CN, CNE, LM, BoW), classifiers, and ensemble/multi-view techniques.  
- Weakness: paper lacks technical detail and transparency, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness (data): Portuguese datasets are extremely small (e.g., 20 MCI vs. 20 controls) and not publicly available, limiting generalizability and reproducibility.  
- Weakness (embeddings/method): unclear which corpus was used to train Portuguese embeddings and whether external corpora were used.  
- Weakness (OOV/integration): ambiguous description of how word-level co-occurrence and subword/OOV representations are integrated and how OOV is defined.  
- Weakness (writing): manuscript is unfocused and overly long in background sections, diluting core contributions.  
- Weakness (references): literature review contains irrelevant or overly general citations that should be replaced with domain-specific work.  
- Weakness (tone): inconsistent narrative—intro presents optimistic results while conclusion downplays ability to distinguish cases.  
- Weakness (errors): multiple technical and typographical errors (wrong acronyms, duplicated words, formatting issues, broken references) undermine credibility.  
- Question to authors: specify exactly what corpus(s) were used to train the Portuguese word embeddings.  
- Question to authors: detail how the word-level network and subword-based/OOV representations are formally combined and whether the combination improves accuracy.  
- Question to authors: clarify how OOV words were defined—per cross-validation fold or relative to an external vocabulary.  
- Question to authors: consider adding an English-translated example transcript and a figure showing a sample network or embedding space for clarity.
