- Paper proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts by modeling word co-occurrence graphs enriched with embedding-based semantic edges, evaluated on three datasets against linguistic and BoW baselines.

- Contribution: Empirical evaluation of graph-based feature representations for MCI detection across multiple, linguistically diverse datasets.

- Contribution: Shows that embedding-enriched co-occurrence graphs can achieve competitive performance compared to standard NLP feature sets.

- Strength: Addresses a clinically relevant problem using established (DementiaBank) and new Portuguese datasets.

- Strength: Thorough experimental design comparing graph features to linguistic and BoW baselines across datasets and classifiers.

- Strength: Offers an alternative feature representation that avoids reliance on syntactic parsers, useful for disfluent clinical speech.

- Weakness: Experimental reporting is unclear and inconsistent (e.g., subjects vs. narratives), leaving the unit of classification and reproducibility ambiguous.

- Weakness: Insufficient justification for adding similarity edges over simpler methods (e.g., averaged embeddings) and results reported with unrealistic precision without statistical testing.

- Weakness: Missing class distributions and reliance on accuracy make evaluation potentially misleading; manuscript readability is reduced by numerous grammatical errors.

- Question: Please clarify the exact number of instances and class distribution per dataset and state whether the classification unit is a subject or a transcript.

- Question: Please provide F1-scores for experiments to account for possible class imbalance.

- Question: Have you performed statistical significance tests (e.g., t-test or Wilcoxon) to confirm that reported improvements are meaningful?
