- Investigates using complex network (word co-occurrence) features enriched with word-embedding-based semantic edges to detect Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts.

- Primary contribution: empirical study applying this network+embedding representation to MCI detection on three datasets and comparing against linguistic and Bag-of-Words baselines.

- Secondary contribution: shows that adding semantic information from embeddings can improve network features for short clinical texts.

- Strength: Addresses a clinically relevant problem with clear motivation and novel application of complex network analysis to MCI detection.

- Strength: Sound experimental setup across three datasets (English and Portuguese) and comparisons to established baselines, supporting generalizability.

- Strength: Promising preliminary results showing network+embedding features are competitive with linguistic features and multi-view feature fusion can boost accuracy.

- Weakness: Methodological details missing for reproducibility (multi-view scheme, full linguistic feature set, and details about embedding training corpus).

- Weakness: Questionable preprocessing and baseline choices (removal of disfluencies without justification; use of term-frequency instead of tf-idf unexplained).

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis and presentation issues (no statistical significance testing, weak related work comparison, lengthy/confusing abstract, missing terms/acronyms/dataset citations).
