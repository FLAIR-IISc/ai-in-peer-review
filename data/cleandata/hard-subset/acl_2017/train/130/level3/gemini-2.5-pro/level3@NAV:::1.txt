**Summary and Contributions**

This paper proposes a method for identifying Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from Portuguese and English speech transcripts using complex networks enriched with word embeddings. The authors model transcripts as graphs and use topological features for classification, reporting accuracies between approximately 60% and 85%.

As I see them, the main contributions are:
1.  The application of NLP methods to the important clinical task of MCI detection, with a specific focus on Brazilian Portuguese, a language for which fewer resources exist in this domain.
2.  An empirical comparison of complex network-based features against traditional linguistic and bag-of-words features for classifying MCI, demonstrating the potential of graph-based representations.

**Strengths**

1.  The paper tackles a significant real-world problem—early detection of cognitive decline—which has high societal impact. The focus on Portuguese is a valuable contribution to a linguistic community often underrepresented in computational linguistics research.
2.  The study performs a systematic evaluation comparing multiple feature sets (network, linguistic, BoW) and classification approaches, including multi-view learning. This provides a useful comparative analysis for this specific task.

**Weaknesses**

1.  The study’s primary weakness is its extremely small dataset size (e.g., 20 patients and 20 controls), which severely limits the validity and generalizability of the findings. Results derived from such a small sample are susceptible to noise and may not be statistically significant or reproducible.
2.  The manuscript lacks critical technical details that are essential for reproducibility and understanding the methodology. It is unclear how the word embeddings were trained—whether on the small internal datasets alone or with an external corpus. The interaction between word-level and subword networks is ambiguous, and the definition of "out-of-vocabulary" words is not provided.
3.  The overall quality of the manuscript needs substantial improvement. The introduction and literature review are unfocused and overly long, containing several irrelevant citations (e.g., pedestrian detection, general sentiment classification). There is a contradictory tone between the optimistic introduction and the more subdued conclusion. The paper would benefit from significant compression of Sections 1-3 to focus on the core technical contribution.
4.  Clarity could be improved by including an English-translated example of a Portuguese transcript and sample outputs of the network or embeddings to help readers better understand the data and methodology. The manuscript also contains numerous typographical and formatting errors that need correction.
