- Proposes modeling speech transcripts as word co-occurrence graphs enriched with embedding-similarity edges to detect MCI from short, disfluent texts.
- Extracts graph-based features to train classifiers and evaluates them on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, ABCD) across English and Portuguese.
- Main contributions: novel application of complex network features for MCI detection, multilingual empirical evaluation, and demonstration that multi-view learning improves performance.
- Strength: Creative, well-motivated approach that avoids brittle deep linguistic parsing and is scalable to atypical clinical language.
- Strength: Evaluation across diverse datasets and tasks (picture description, story retelling) supports potential generalizability.
- Strength: Multi-view learning and ensembles yield substantial gains (e.g., ~10% on Cinderella, ~4.7% on ABCD), showing feature complementarity.
- Weakness: Critical methodological details missing—multi-view combination scheme, embedding training corpus, and insufficient description of linguistic features hinder reproducibility.
- Weakness: Removing disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions) is questionable since these are diagnostically relevant for cognitive impairment.
- Weakness: No statistical significance tests reported, so performance claims may not be reliable given small datasets and modest differences.
- Weakness: Manuscript clarity and presentation are poor—overlong abstract, convoluted sentences, undefined acronyms, missing citations/links.
- Question: Provide a detailed description of the multi-view learning implementation and how classifier outputs are combined.
- Question: Specify the corpus used to train word embeddings and how you avoided domain mismatch or overfitting.
- Question: Justify removal of disfluencies and report experiments retaining them to assess impact on performance.
- Question: Explain why TF was used for the BoW baseline instead of TF-IDF and analyze the high TF-SVM performance on ABCD.
- Question: Perform statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-test, bootstrap, permutation) to validate claims of outperformance.
