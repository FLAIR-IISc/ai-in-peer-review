- Proposes detecting MCI from speech transcripts using word co-occurrence graphs enriched with embedding-based semantic edges.
- Models are evaluated on DementiaBank Cookie Theft (English) and two Brazilian Portuguese datasets (Cinderella, Arizona Battery).
- Reports that complex network features, especially with embeddings, often outperform Coh-Metrix linguistic features and Bag-of-Words.
- Shows multi-view learning and classifier ensembles improve performance, reaching up to 80% accuracy on the Arizona dataset.
- Primary contribution: applies and evaluates complex network approach for MCI detection on Brazilian Portuguese and provides two Portuguese datasets/baselines.
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates that enriching co-occurrence networks with word embeddings helps handle short, noisy clinical texts.
- Strength: addresses a clinically important task for a lower-resource language, expanding research beyond English.
- Strength: method is robust to disfluent, agrammatical speech and reduces reliance on brittle syntactic parsers and manual transcript correction.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental comparison against strong baselines, multiple classifiers, and ensemble/multi-view approaches.
- Weakness: Portuguese datasets are very small (Cinderella N=40, Arizona N=43), limiting generalizability and risking overfitting.
- Weakness: key methodological details are missing, impeding reproducibility.
- Weakness (embedding training): unclear what corpus was used to train the Portuguese word embeddings (in-domain vs. large external corpus).
- Weakness (OOV handling): ambiguous definition of “out-of-vocabulary” and how character n-grams are used relative to the embedding vocabulary.
- Weakness: writing and literature review are unfocused, contain tangential citations, and the narrative tone is inconsistent.
- Question: please clarify the corpus and training details for the Portuguese word embeddings.
- Question: please define OOV words in the pipeline and explain how character-level information integrates with the co-occurrence network.
- Question: please explain the cited transcription norms (NURC/SP N. 338 EF and 331 D2) and provide an English-glossed Portuguese transcript example.
- Minor corrections: typographical fixes—Line 29 ("as it is a lightweight"), Line 188 ("NLP tools"), Line 264 ("vertex" not "vertice").
- Minor corrections: Table 3 formatting ("72.0 (7.4)"), complete Aluísio et al. (2016) reference, and remove duplicated DOI in Amancio (2015a).
