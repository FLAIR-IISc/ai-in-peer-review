1. Proposes identifying MCI from Portuguese and English speech transcripts using complex networks enriched with word embeddings, reporting ~60–85% accuracy.  
2. Contribution: applies NLP to MCI detection with focus on Brazilian Portuguese and compares complex-network features to traditional linguistic and BoW features.  
3. Strength: addresses a high-impact clinical problem and fills a gap for underrepresented Portuguese-language resources.  
4. Strength: provides a systematic comparative evaluation of network, linguistic, and BoW features and classification approaches, including multi-view learning.  
5. Weakness: very small dataset (e.g., 20 patients, 20 controls) undermines validity, generalizability, and statistical significance.  
6. Weakness: missing critical methodological details for reproducibility (embedding training data, word/subword network interaction, OOV definition).  
7. Weakness: manuscript is unfocused and overly long with irrelevant citations and contradictory tone; Sections 1–3 need substantial compression.  
8. Weakness: clarity and presentation issues — needs translated example transcripts, sample network/embedding outputs, and numerous typographical/formatting corrections.
