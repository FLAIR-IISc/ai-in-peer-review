- Method: Identify MCI from speech transcripts using word co-occurrence networks enriched with word-embedding similarities to address sparse graphs from short, disfluent texts.

- Evaluation: Tested on three datasets (DementiaBank English, two Portuguese) comparing CNE features to linguistic features and BoW across multiple classifiers; multi-view combinations improved accuracy up to ~10%.

- Contribution 1: Novel empirical application of complex network representations (with embeddings) as a parser-independent approach for MCI detection.

- Contribution 2: Comparative analysis across languages and feature types providing a useful benchmark and showing complementary strengths of different representations.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated application addressing parser brittleness on disfluent/agrammatical speech common in MCI.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive experimental setup spanning languages, tasks, baselines, classifiers, and ensemble/multi-view experiments.

- Strength 3: Results indicate CNE is competitive or superior to linguistic features in many settings and that combining feature sets yields substantial gains.

- Weakness 1: Missing methodological details hindering reproducibility—multi-view learning mechanism, embedding training corpus, and unclear description of Portuguese linguistic features.

- Weakness 2: Questionable preprocessing/experimental choices—automatic removal of disfluencies (potentially informative) and use of raw term-frequency for BoW without justification.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis and presentation—no statistical significance testing, superficial related work discussion, and numerous writing/editing issues reducing clarity.

- Question 1: Precisely describe the multi-view learning implementation (voting, probability averaging, meta-classifier, etc.).

- Question 2: What corpus was used to train the word embeddings—only the small in-domain datasets or a larger external corpus?

- Question 3: Why were disfluencies removed; were experiments conducted keeping them and what were the results?

- Question 4: Why use term frequency for BoW instead of standard tf-idf?

- Question 5: Were statistical significance tests performed to confirm reported performance improvements?

- Question 6: Provide the detailed list or description of the 58 Coh-Metrix-Dementia features used for the Portuguese datasets.
