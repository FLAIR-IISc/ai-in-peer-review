- Paper proposes enriching complex networks with word embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts, targeting short, disfluent texts.  
- Method evaluated on three datasets (English and Portuguese), reporting classification accuracies of ~60–85%.  
- Contribution: integration of CNs and embeddings that outperforms traditional linguistic metrics in single classifiers.  
- Contribution: use of ensemble and multi-view learning to boost accuracy, especially for under-resourced languages like Portuguese.  
- Contribution: lightweight, language-independent representation that reduces manual preprocessing needs.  
- Strength: novel CN + embedding approach effectively handles short, disfluent neuropsychological texts.  
- Strength: inclusion of Portuguese datasets addresses research gaps for under-resourced languages.  
- Strength: multi-view/ensemble methods show measurable improvement on Cinderella and ABCD datasets.  
- Weakness: introduction and related work are overly detailed and include tangential references; need compression.  
- Weakness: unclear methodology — sources and training of embeddings, OOV handling, and CN + linguistic metric integration are not specified.  
- Weakness: small datasets (e.g., 20 MCI / 20 control in Portuguese Cinderella) limit generalizability; lacks translated examples.  
- Tech: correct typographical errors (e.g., "PLN -> NLP") and repeated words.  
- Tech: fix formatting issues in Table 3 and DOI references.  
- Tech: clarify transcription norms and preprocessing steps, especially for Portuguese data.  
- Question: provide details on the source and training data for the word embeddings used.  
- Question: explain how out-of-vocabulary words are handled and their impact on enriched networks.  
- Question: justify the chosen cosine similarity thresholds for enriching complex networks.  
- Conclusion: promising contribution to automated MCI detection for under-resourced languages but requires significant methodological clarifications and revisions to increase impact.
