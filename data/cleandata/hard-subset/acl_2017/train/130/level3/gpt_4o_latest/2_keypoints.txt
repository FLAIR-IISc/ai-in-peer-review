- Contribution: Introduces complex network features enriched with word embeddings for MCI detection, showing improvements over traditional linguistic features in some cases.  
- Contribution: Evaluates the proposed method across three datasets in English and Portuguese, demonstrating adaptability to different languages and narratives.  
- Contribution: Explores ensemble and multi-view learning, reporting notable classification gains (up to ~10%) on specific datasets.

- Strength: Addresses an important, practical problem—early MCI detection from speech transcripts, relevant to Alzheimer’s diagnosis.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel, lightweight, language-independent CN+embedding representation that handles transcript disfluencies.  
- Strength: Uses multiple datasets from different languages/contexts, improving robustness and generalizability.  
- Strength: Ensemble and multi-view approaches yield measurable accuracy improvements, especially on Cinderella and ABCD datasets.  
- Strength: Method avoids manual preprocessing, enabling scalable automated assessment for clinical use.

- Weakness: Paper has poor grammar and spelling, requiring substantial language editing for clarity.  
- Weakness: Problem formulation and dataset details are unclear (e.g., class distributions); motivation for embedding enrichment is not well explained.  
- Weakness: Fails to incorporate domain-specific biomedical or MCI-related linguistic features/tools, reducing clinical relevance and interpretability.  
- Weakness: Overreliance on accuracy without F1 or class-aware metrics, and no statistical significance testing to validate improvements.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of alternative graph representations and embedding techniques, weakening claims about robustness.

- Question: Please clarify class distributions per dataset and justify using accuracy as the primary evaluation metric.  
- Question: Why was cosine similarity chosen to enrich networks, and were other similarity measures tested?  
- Question: Why were domain-specific MCI linguistic markers or tools not included in the model?  
- Question: Can you provide statistical significance testing for the reported performance gains?  
- Question: How does the method compare to state-of-the-art MCI detection approaches in computational efficiency and interpretability?

- Recommendation: Major revisions required—improve language/presentation, clarify problem and evaluation (use appropriate metrics and statistical tests), and explore domain-specific features and alternative representations.
