- Novel method: applies complex network features enriched with word embeddings for MCI detection, claiming a lightweight, language-independent approach.  
- Evaluation: tested on three datasets (DementiaBank, Cinderella, Arizona-Battery) with CN features outperforming linguistic features.  
- Learning strategies: integrates ensemble and multi-view learning, improving accuracy notably on Cinderella and Arizona datasets.  
- Contribution claims: (1) novel CN+embeddings for MCI, (2) comparative evaluation vs. linguistic and BoW features, (3) use of ensemble/multi-view to boost performance.  
- Strength — feature novelty: CN+embeddings circumvents manual preprocessing and grammar dependence of traditional linguistic features.  
- Strength — dataset diversity: experiments include multiple datasets and languages, suggesting cross-linguistic applicability.  
- Strength — multi-view gains: ensemble/multi-view learning yields measurable accuracy improvements.  
- Weakness — unclear explanations: key concepts (multi-view learning, linguistic features, dataset specifics) are insufficiently explained.  
- Weakness — disfluency handling: removal of disfluencies is unjustified despite their potential diagnostic importance.  
- Weakness — BoW omission: tf-idf weighting for BoW was excluded without explanation.  
- Weakness — statistical rigor: no statistical significance testing reported for claimed improvements.  
- Weakness — writing quality: paper requires substantial editing for clarity; abstract and many sentences are convoluted.  
- Questions for authors: justify disfluency removal; detail the multi-view scheme and feature-weighting; explain exclusion of tf-idf.  
- Additional comments: related work is shallow and key dataset/tool citations are missing; linguistic features and transcription norms need expansion or supplementary material.  
- Overall verdict: promising and novel results but significant methodological, presentation, and documentation revisions are needed.
