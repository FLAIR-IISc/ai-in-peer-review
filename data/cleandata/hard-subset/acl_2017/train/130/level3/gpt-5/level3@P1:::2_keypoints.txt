- Paper proposes MCI detection from speech transcripts using embedding-enriched word co-occurrence networks, topological features, linguistic metrics, BoW, and ensemble/multi-view classifiers across three datasets (English, Portuguese).
- Main contribution: application and evaluation of embedding-enriched co-occurrence network features for cross-lingual MCI detection on noisy, short transcripts.
- Empirical claim: enriched complex-network (CNE) features are competitive with or outperform parser-dependent linguistic metrics, especially on disfluent/unedited transcripts.
- Demonstrated that multi-view and ensemble combinations of CN, linguistic, and BoW features can yield additional accuracy gains on some datasets.
- Strength: addresses a clinically meaningful, underexplored cross-lingual task and avoids reliance on high-quality parsers.
- Strength: lightweight, language-independent representation that is robust to ASR/transcription noise and limited annotated resources.
- Strength: enrichment with embeddings densifies small/near-linear graphs and improves discriminative topological signals.
- Strength: multi-view/ensemble results indicate complementary information across feature sets (e.g., reported ~10% gain on Cinderella).
- Strength: broad evaluation using multiple classifiers (SVM, kNN, RF, GNB) and a consistent 5-fold protocol.
- Weakness: unclear dataset details — per-dataset class counts, subject counts, and possible label leakage not provided.
- Weakness: potential subject-level leakage in ABCD (immediate/delayed recalls) if folds are not split by subject.
- Weakness: demographic confounds (age, education) not controlled; models may learn proxies rather than MCI signal.
- Weakness: evaluation relies on accuracy only; lacks per-class precision/recall/F1, balanced metrics, AUC, confusion matrices, and statistical significance testing.
- Weakness: insufficient ablations and baselines (mean embeddings, doc2vec, tf-idf, char n-grams, graph-only similarity networks) to justify the graph approach.
- Weakness: embedding training data unspecified — unclear whether pre-trained or in-domain embeddings were used and risk of leakage.
- Weakness: inconsistent preprocessing across datasets (disfluency/stopword removal) and mixing of revised vs. unedited transcripts without clear protocol.
- Weakness: writing/presentation issues (grammar, inconsistent terminology, excessive numerical precision) hinder readability and confidence.
- Question: provide exact counts per dataset (subjects, narratives per class, tokens, vocabulary) and clarify Cookie Theft class composition.
- Question: confirm fold construction — was cross-validation stratified by subject to prevent transcript-level leakage?
- Question: specify corpora used for English and Portuguese embeddings and whether pre-trained fastText was used.
- Question: report class distributions and metrics (macro/micro F1, balanced accuracy, AUC), include confidence intervals and statistical tests for reported gains.
- Question: provide ablations comparing CNE vs CN vs mean embeddings/doc2vec, stopword inclusion, enrichment threshold sensitivity, edge weighting schemes, and topological feature importance.
- Question: control for demographics (match or include age/education as covariates) and re-evaluate model robustness.
- Question: clarify whether hyperparameters and similarity thresholds were tuned with nested cross-validation to avoid tuning bias.
- Additional comment: use tf-idf with linear SVM/logistic regression and character n-grams as stronger BoW baselines.
- Additional comment: release code, feature extraction scripts, and fold assignments for reproducibility.
- Additional comment: standardize disfluency preprocessing across datasets and improve manuscript language, formatting, and numerical reporting.
