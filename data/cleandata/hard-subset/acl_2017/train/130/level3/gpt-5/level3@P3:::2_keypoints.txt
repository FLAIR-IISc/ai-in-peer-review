- Task: automatic detection of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) from speech transcripts across three datasets (English DementiaBank Cookie Theft; Portuguese Cinderella and ABCD).
- Method: propose embedding-enriched word co-occurrence networks and extract diverse graph-theoretic features to classify transcripts.
- Evaluation: 5-fold CV with several classifiers and ensemble/multi-view combinations; performance varies by dataset.

- Contribution 1: Cross-lingual evaluation of embedding-enriched co-occurrence network features for MCI detection on English and Brazilian Portuguese clinical datasets.
- Contribution 2: Empirical finding that graph-derived features can be competitive with or outperform standard linguistic metrics on noisy transcripts, and ensembles/multi-view can boost accuracy.
- Contribution 3: A lightweight, parser-agnostic pipeline intended to be robust to disfluencies and limited NLP tooling in clinical settings.

- Strength: Clinically relevant task using realistic elicitation paradigms (picture description, story narration, recall).
- Strength: Multilingual, multi-dataset evaluation increases evidence of generality and highlights dataset-specific behavior.
- Strength: Parser-independent, lightweight features suitable for noisy transcripts and resource-poor languages.
- Strength: Comparative evaluation includes multiple baselines (CN, CNE, BoW, Coh-Metrix) and ensemble/multi-view experiments.
- Strength: Inclusion of sensible baselines common in dementia detection literature.

- Weakness: Data clarity and potential leakage/confounds—unclear instance counts, subject-level splits (ABCD has two narratives per subject), and demographic imbalance (e.g., age differences).
- Weakness: Inadequate reporting of class distributions and metrics—accuracy-only reporting, no per-class precision/recall/F1, macro-F1, ROC-AUC, or confusion matrices.
- Weakness: Lack of statistical testing and confidence intervals; reported accuracies have excessive precision for small samples.
- Weakness: Weak methodological motivation and missing ablations—no comparison to embedding-only networks, document embeddings, lexical cohesion measures, or graph-kernel alternatives.
- Weakness: ML setup under-specified—unclear hyperparameter tuning (thresholds), feature scaling/normalization, feature selection/regularization, and absence of fold variance reporting.
- Weakness: Writing and presentation issues—grammatical errors, inconsistent/mislabeled tables and figures, and confusing dataset/transcript-level descriptions.
- Weakness: Missed domain-specific baselines and tools (clinical concept mapping, disfluency detection) and limited discussion of trade-offs for a tool-light approach.

- Question: Are folds strictly subject-independent across datasets, especially ABCD with two narratives per subject?
- Question: What are exact MCI vs. control counts per dataset and per fold; please report class priors and per-class metrics.
- Question: Were demographic confounds controlled (matching, regression, stratification, or inclusion as features)?
- Question: How was the embedding-similarity threshold selected; was tuning nested within CV and how sensitive are results to it?
- Question: What corpora were used to train English and Portuguese embeddings and are they comparable/in-domain?
- Question: Can you provide ablations: co-occurrence-only vs. similarity-only networks; pooled document embeddings; CNE with/without specific measures; CNE vs. simple lexical cohesion metrics?
- Question: Please provide confidence intervals across folds and statistical significance testing for key comparisons.
- Question: For ABCD, are immediate vs. delayed recall evaluated separately and can performance be reported per condition?
- Question: Will you release code, feature extraction scripts, and exact train/test splits to support reproducibility?

- Overall verdict: Promising parser-light, multilingual approach with meaningful clinical focus, but requires major clarifications and methodological fixes (data splits, confounds, evaluation, ablations) and language editing before claims can be trusted.
