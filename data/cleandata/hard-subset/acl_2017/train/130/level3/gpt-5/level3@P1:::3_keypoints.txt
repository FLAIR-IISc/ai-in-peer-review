- Summary: The paper detects MCI from speech transcripts using word-adjacency networks enriched with word-embedding similarity, comparing network features to linguistic features and BoW across three datasets, finding enriched networks competitive and multi-view combinations beneficial.

- Primary contribution: Shows embedding-enriched complex network features are lightweight, language-independent, and strong predictors for MCI detection on short, noisy narratives in English and Portuguese.

- Secondary contribution: Empirically compares network, linguistic (Coh-Metrix/Coh-Metrix-Dementia), and BoW feature families and reports improvements from ensemble/multi-view combinations across three datasets.

- Tertiary contribution: Claims reduced dependence on high-precision parsing and manual transcript correction by using language-independent network representations.

- Strength: Clinically relevant and multilingual evaluation across varied elicitation paradigms, increasing ecological validity.

- Strength: Lightweight, parser-free representation suitable for disfluent clinical transcripts and low-resource settings.

- Strength: Embedding-based enrichment addresses sparsity in short texts and yields consistent gains over plain co-occurrence networks.

- Strength: Multi-view/ensemble combinations provide notable performance improvements, indicating complementary information across feature families.

- Strength: Inclusion of transparent baselines (e.g., linear SVM on BoW) offers realistic comparisons for future work.

- Weakness: Multi-view learning methodology is underspecified (selection of base learners, combination rule, weight learning, calibration).

- Weakness: Threshold tuning for embedding edges (e.g., 0.7/0.4) lacks a clear, leakage-free protocol (nested CV or held-out dev set).

- Weakness: Linguistic features are insufficiently described—exact metrics per dataset and justification for exclusions are missing.

- Weakness: Word embedding training data and procedures (corpora, languages, pretrained vs in-domain, fixed vs updated embeddings) are unclear.

- Weakness: Preprocessing choices (removal of filled pauses/false starts/repetitions, sentence segmentation) are under-justified and may remove diagnostic signals.

- Weakness: Evaluation lacks statistical validation (no significance tests or CIs), and cross-validation may risk subject-level leakage, especially in ABCD with two narratives per subject.

- Weakness: Related work review is superficial and needs deeper coverage and precise contrasts with recent embedding/ASR-inclusive approaches.

- Weakness: Clarity and presentation issues—acronyms undefined, missing citations/links, unclear sentences, and an overlong abstract.

- Question: What exact multi-view learning scheme was used (feature spaces per view, base learners, combination rule, learned weights, per-fold training)?

- Question: What corpora and sizes were used to train English and Portuguese embeddings; were they pretrained fastText models or trained on task data; were embeddings updated?

- Question: Were cross-validation folds split by subject to prevent leakage, and how was the embedding-edge threshold selected without test leakage?

- Question: Why were filled pauses/false starts/repetitions removed; is there an ablation showing effect of retaining them?

- Question: Why use term-frequency BoW instead of tf-idf; does tf-idf change performance across datasets?

- Question: How are shortest-path metrics computed on disconnected graphs, and what are average nodes/edges per transcript before/after enrichment per dataset?

- Suggestion: Expand Section 4.2.2 to list exact Coh-Metrix(-Dementia) variables used, with definitions and selection rationale (move full lists to supplementary if needed).

- Suggestion: Clarify preprocessing order and steps (tokenization, stopword handling, lemmatization, sentence segmentation, disfluency handling) and provide dataset links/citations.

- Suggestion: Explicitly name datasets, define “segmented”, justify manual vs automatic segmentation, and report per-dataset stats (subjects/narratives, sentences/tokens, vocabulary).

- Suggestion: Report statistical significance or CIs, use nested CV, and control for multiple comparisons.

- Suggestion: Define all acronyms on first use, fix typos/grammar, shorten the abstract, and ensure ACL citation style compliance.

- Suggestion: Provide cosine-threshold search range/step, sensitivity analysis, average added edges per document, and effect on graph density.

- Suggestion: Provide reproducibility appendix or supplementary material with code, full feature lists, and precise hyperparameters if page limits constrain the manuscript.
