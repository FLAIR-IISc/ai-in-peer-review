- Summary: Detects MCI from speech transcripts (three datasets) using word co-occurrence graphs enriched with embedding-similarity edges, extracting graph, linguistic, and BoW features and evaluating classifiers/ensembles; enriched graphs are often competitive.

- Contribution 1: Cross-dataset empirical study comparing graph-based, linguistic, and BoW features with multi-view/ensemble schemes across English and Portuguese.

- Contribution 2: Adaptation of graph-of-words enriched with embeddings for short, disfluent clinical transcripts, using a broad set of graph metrics.

- Contribution 3: Evidence that enriched graph features can match or exceed traditional linguistic metrics without heavy parsing.

- Strength: Clinically relevant problem with diverse datasets and attention to robustness to disfluencies.

- Strength: Wide feature-space (graph topology, Coh-Metrix, BoW) and multi-view/ensemble exploration provide useful comparative insights; enriched graphs often yield strongest accuracies.

- Strength: Lightweight, language-independent approach that reduces reliance on error-prone parsing.

- Weakness: Poor writing (grammar, spelling, organization) impedes clarity and reproducibility.

- Weakness: Unclear problem setup—instance definition, repeated measures, and 5-fold protocol raise subject-level leakage concerns; dataset counts inconsistent and transcript/class statistics missing.

- Weakness: Evaluation over-relies on accuracy; lacks macro-F1/weighted-F1, AUROC/PR-AUC, confidence intervals, significance tests, and may report unjustified precision on small samples.

- Weakness: Methodological motivation underdeveloped—benefit of enrichment over simpler similarity graphs or content baselines not convincingly shown; limited ablations/sensitivity analyses.

- Weakness: Baselines omit strong modern alternatives (pretrained transformers, clinical NLP resources); no error analysis or interpretability.

- Question: What is the exact instance definition and cross-validation protocol—were folds stratified by subject to prevent leakage (e.g., ABCD two narratives)?

- Question: Provide per-dataset class distributions and transcripts per class, and report macro-F1/AUROC/PR-AUC with confidence intervals and statistical tests.

- Question: How were thresholds and hyperparameters selected—was nested cross-validation used?

- Question: Add ablations comparing co-occurrence vs enrichment-only graphs, individual graph metrics, and threshold sensitivity.

- Question: Which embedding models and training corpora were used for English and Portuguese, and was any in-domain clinical data used?
