- Paper models speech transcripts as word-adjacency complex networks enriched with embeddings to detect MCI, evaluated on English and Brazilian Portuguese with accuracies ~60–80% and gains from multi-view/ensembles.
- Contribution: Empirical evidence that embedding-enriched complex-network features (CNE) are competitive for MCI detection, including Portuguese and noisy transcripts.
- Contribution: Demonstration that multi-view and ensemble learning yields non-trivial gains (e.g., +10% on Cinderella; ~80% on ABCD with CNE-LM-BoW).
- Contribution: Practical, parser-light pipeline more tolerant to disfluencies than parser-dependent features.
- Strength: Addresses low-resource language—applying NLP to Brazilian Portuguese MCI assessment is valuable and underexplored.
- Strength: CNE features consistently match or exceed traditional linguistic metrics; multi-view combinations achieve the best results (e.g., 0.80 ABCD; 0.75 Cinderella).
- Strength: Evaluation on raw vs. manually revised transcripts supports that CN features are less sensitive to parsing quality.
- Strength: Clear framing of a lightweight, language-independent approach that avoids heavy manual normalization.
- Weakness: Very limited Portuguese data and potential data leakage (small Cinderella and repeated narratives in ABCD); need clarification on subject-wise cross-validation.
- Weakness: Insufficient technical detail on Portuguese embeddings—training corpora, OOV definition/handling, and interaction of subword n-grams with word-level networks.
- Weakness: Ambiguity about network variants and whether combining word- and subword-augmented networks improves accuracy.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—Portuguese transcripts not public; request for English-translated example, code, and trained embeddings/models.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—overlong sections, tangential citations, and multiple technical/typographical errors.
- Question: Were cross-validation folds stratified by subject (to avoid leakage for immediate/delayed retells)?
- Question: Which corpora, preprocessing, and vocabulary/statistics were used to train Portuguese embeddings?
- Question: How are OOV tokens defined with subword embeddings, and how are their vectors used to add similarity edges?
- Question: Is there evidence that combining word- and subword-based networks outperforms either alone?
- Request: Provide a Portuguese transcript snippet with English translation, a sample network visualization, and embedding-similarity thresholds.
