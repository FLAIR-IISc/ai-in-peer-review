- Paper models transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-derived semantic edges, extracts topological features, and compares them to parser-based metrics and BoW for MCI detection on three datasets (English, Portuguese).

Contributions
- Embedding-enriched co-occurrence network (CNE) features often match or outperform parser-based linguistic features for MCI detection across languages/tasks.
- Demonstrates that multi-view combinations (CNE, linguistic metrics, BoW) and ensembling can improve performance on Cinderella and ABCD datasets.
- Proposes augmenting sparse short-text co-occurrence graphs with embedding-similarity edges to densify networks and improve topological signal for short/noisy clinical transcripts.

Strengths
- Addresses a clinically important problem with cross-language, cross-task experiments increasing potential impact.
- Uses lightweight, parser-independent, language-agnostic graph features suitable for disfluent transcripts.
- Modeling intuition (adding embedding edges) is sensible and empirically supported by gains of CNE over CN and LM in several settings.
- Multi-view majority-vote fusion shows practical gains (e.g., +10% on Cinderella; up to 80% on ABCD).
- Uses transparent baseline classifiers and open-source tools, reporting per-feature-space results.

Weaknesses / Methodological issues
- Multi-view fusion details are missing: exact fusion scheme, per-view classifier choices, calibration/stacking not specified.
- Embedding training details are unspecified: corpora, sizes, preprocessing, shared vs per-dataset vectors unclear.
- Graph construction and thresholding protocol are under-described: cosine threshold selection, weighting vs binarization, directed/undirected treatment, handling disconnected components not given.
- Cross-validation protocol unclear: potential subject-level leakage in ABCD (immediate vs delayed recalls) and unclear if folds were stratified/subject-disjoint.
- Preprocessing removes filled pauses/false starts/repetitions without justification; this may discard MCI-related signals and contradict robustness claims.
- BoW baseline is weak (raw TF only, no tf-idf or n-grams) and unmotivated.
- No statistical significance tests or confidence intervals reported, making small accuracy differences hard to trust.
- Related work coverage is superficial, misses direct dataset comparisons (e.g., DementiaBank) and precise methodological contrasts.
- Presentation problems: long/unclear abstract, undefined acronyms, underspecified dataset descriptions, and many typos/format issues.

Questions for the authors
- What exact multi-view fusion scheme was used (per-view classifiers, hyperparameters, calibration, stacking)?
- Which corpora and preprocessing were used to train English and Portuguese embeddings, and will vectors be released?
- Are similarity edges weighted by cosine or binarized? Are networks directed or undirected? How are disconnected components handled for path metrics?
- How was the cosine-similarity threshold selected (nested CV vs global tuning)? Provide sensitivity analysis.
- Were CV folds in ABCD split by subject so multiple narratives from one subject do not leak across folds?
- Why remove disfluencies (filled pauses, false starts, repetitions)? Please provide an ablation with/without removal.
- Did you try stronger BoW baselines (tf-idf, char/word n-grams, regularized logistic regression)? If so, report comparisons.

Additional requested clarifications / experiments
- Expand on exact Coh-Metrix/Coh-Metrix-Dementia feature list, tool versions, and language-specific preprocessing.
- Clarify preprocessing order (tokenization, case-folding, stopword removal, lemmatization, diacritics) and dataset statistics (n narratives, tokens/narrative, class balance).
- Provide dataset/tool citations and links (DementiaBank, WAB “Cookie Theft”/Picnic, NURC/SP), fix references and typos.
- Report per-fold results, statistical tests or confidence intervals, and include sensitivity analyses for thresholds.
- Shorten abstract and improve manuscript clarity; include error analysis and feature importance/ablations (CN vs CNE, per-topological measure).

Overall verdict
- Interesting and promising cross-language empirical study, but acceptance depends on addressing missing methodological details, strengthening baselines/statistics, and improving clarity and reproducibility.
