- Paper studies MCI detection from speech transcripts using co-occurrence networks enriched with word embeddings plus linguistic and BoW features across three datasets/languages; ensembles improve performance.

- Contribution: cross-dataset, cross-language empirical evaluation showing embedding-enriched network features are viable and often outperform traditional linguistic metrics.

- Contribution: evidence that multi-view/ensemble combinations can boost accuracy (e.g., +10% on Cinderella; up to 80% on ABCD).

- Strength: addresses an important clinical problem with a lightweight, language-agnostic representation less dependent on fragile parsing.

- Strength: solid empirical coverage across three datasets and two languages, testing multiple classifiers and combinations with reported improvements.

- Strength: clear motivation to enrich co-occurrence networks to mitigate short-text topology issues; approach is reasonable and informative.

- Weakness: multi-view learning scheme is underspecified — per-view classifiers, fusion method, voting weights, and validation protocol are unclear.

- Weakness: embedding-enrichment thresholding and tuning protocol lack detail.

- Weakness: linguistic feature set is insufficiently specified — selection criteria, final feature lists, and undefined acronyms/terms (e.g., WLM, ABCD, PLN).

- Weakness: unclear what corpus was used to train word embeddings; training on task data risks leakage and poor coverage.

- Weakness: preprocessing choices are under-justified — removal of disfluencies is questionable given their diagnostic value; BoW uses tf not tf-idf without justification.

- Weakness: no statistical significance testing or confidence intervals; conclusions lack inferential support.

- Weakness: writing and presentation need substantial editing (overlong abstract, many sentences to reword, typos, superficial related work).

- Weakness: dataset descriptions/segmentations are unclear; Table 1 lacks narrative/token/sentence stats; missing citations/links and citation-style issues.

- Question: precisely specify the multi-view learning setup — per-view classifiers, decision fusion (majority vs. weighted), weight setting, and validation to avoid leakage.

- Question: what corpus (size, language, domain) was used to train embeddings; if task data was used, please reconsider or justify.

- Question: justify removal of disfluencies and report results with and without them; provide full preprocessing pipeline and order.

- Question: why use tf instead of tf-idf for BoW — provide ablation results.

- Question: report statistical significance (e.g., permutation tests or bootstrap CIs) for key comparisons.

- Question: define feature computation details (e.g., “shortest path”), exact cosine threshold values and tuning; provide explicit dataset names, segmentation protocol, and expand Table 1 with narrative lengths, token counts, and sentence stats.

- Request: add missing citations/links (DementiaBank, WAB/Picnic, NURC norms) and ensure conference citation style compliance.
