- Paper models speech transcripts as word co-occurrence networks enriched with embedding-similarity edges to detect MCI, compared against linguistic metrics and BoW across three datasets.
- Uses SVM (linear/RBF), k-NN, Random Forest, GaussianNB, simple ensembles and multi-view combinations for classification.
- Main empirical claim: enriched complex-network topology (CNE) generally outperforms plain co-occurrence networks (CN) and often outperforms linguistic metrics, with multi-view combos giving notable accuracy gains.
- Primary contribution: shows embedding-enriched network topology is a robust, parser-free alternative for MCI detection on noisy, disfluent transcripts, including Portuguese.
- Secondary contribution: demonstrates consistent gains from simple multi-view/ensemble combinations of network features with BoW and linguistic metrics.
- Tertiary contribution: presents a lightweight, language-agnostic pipeline avoiding lemmatization/dependency parsing, suited to imperfect ASR outputs.
- Strength: practical, language-agnostic modeling that handles disfluencies by avoiding brittle parsing steps and improves over CN and linguistic metrics.
- Strength: cross-lingual evaluation includes lower-resource Portuguese datasets (Cinderella, ABCD) and shows robustness on automatically segmented transcripts.
- Strength: sensible embedding-based densification of short/fragmented narratives, supported by consistent CNE > CN improvements.
- Strength: clear quantification of multi-view/ensemble improvements across datasets.
- Strength: transparent network feature set (centralities, clustering, assortativity, path-length stats) enabling interpretability.
- Weakness: small datasets and limited evaluation rigor (small sample sizes, only accuracy reported, no per-class metrics, CIs, or significance tests).
- Weakness: unclear whether cross-validation is subject-disjoint (risk of speaker leakage in ABCD where subjects have two narratives).
- Weakness: insufficient detail on embeddings and OOV handling (training corpora, models used, vocabulary sizes, OOV rates not specified).
- Weakness: similarity-threshold choice (e.g., 0.7/0.4) lacks principled, data-driven justification and tuning detail.
- Weakness: ambiguities in graph construction and feature computation (sentence-boundary edges, per-narrative graphs, tokenization, interplay of subword embeddings and word nodes).
- Weakness: overlong/unfocused exposition with extraneous citations; related work could be tighter and more targeted.
- Weakness: writing and presentation issues (typos, inconsistent formatting, weak baselines like tf-only BoW, missing stronger text or neural baselines).
- Question: which corpora/trained models were used for English and Portuguese embeddings, and what are vocabulary sizes and OOV rates per dataset?
- Question: are embedding models pre-trained (e.g., fastText) or trained on the small datasets only, and was external data used?
- Question: are edges allowed across sentence boundaries and are networks constructed per narrative or aggregated across samples?
- Question: how sensitive are results to similarity-threshold selection and was threshold tuning done via nested cross-validation?
- Question: are ABCD immediate/delayed recalls from the same subject placed in the same fold to prevent leakage, and are folds subject-disjoint across datasets?
- Question: were features standardized per fold, and was any dimensionality reduction or feature selection applied?
- Question: how do stronger text baselines perform (tf-idf word/char n-grams with linear SVM, doc2vec, regularized logistic regression, simple neural baselines)?
- Recommendation: tighten literature review, compress background, and clarify embedding training, OOV definition, tokenization, sentence handling, and threshold tuning.
- Recommendation: report precision/recall/F1 per class, confidence intervals, statistical tests, ensure subject-disjoint CV, add stronger baselines, and release code/models plus example transcripts and network visualizations.
- Recommendation: fix typographical/formatting issues, standardize references, provide NURC/SP transcription conventions and English translations of Portuguese examples.
