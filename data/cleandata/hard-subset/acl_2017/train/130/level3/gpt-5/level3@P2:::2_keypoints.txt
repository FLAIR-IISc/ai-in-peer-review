- Method summary: transcripts represented as word co-occurrence graphs enriched with embedding-similarity edges; topological metrics (aggregated) used as features and compared to linguistic metrics and BoW across SVM/KNN/RF/GNB and multi-view ensembles on three datasets, reporting ~65%/75%/80% best accuracies.

- Contribution — empirical: embedding-enriched co-occurrence network features are competitive across English and Portuguese and often beat parser-dependent linguistic metrics on disfluent transcripts.

- Contribution — practical: multi-view/ensemble combinations of network, linguistic, and BoW features improve accuracy on small datasets.

- Contribution — robustness: network features offer a lightweight, parser-free representation that tolerates disfluencies better than traditional linguistic features on noisy transcripts.

- Strength — relevance and scope: tackles an important clinical problem and evaluates across English and two under-resourced Portuguese datasets, boosting generalizability.

- Strength — language-agnostic/lightweight: avoids brittle parsing and hand-crafted resources, addressing realistic ASR/transcript noise.

- Strength — systematic comparisons: contrasts network, linguistic, and BoW features and shows enriched networks outperform linguistic metrics on noisy data; cleaned vs uncleaned comparisons are informative.

- Strength — multi-view learning: ensemble/multi-view methods consistently improve results over single-view models on two datasets.

- Strength — reproducibility potential: uses standard toolkits (fastText-like embeddings, scikit-learn).

- Weakness — clarity and presentation: manuscript has numerous grammatical errors, typos, inconsistent references, and imprecise statements that hinder comprehension.

- Weakness — potential data leakage: unclear whether cross-validation was done at the subject level (ABCD has two narratives per subject), risking train/test leakage; class counts are inconsistently reported.

- Weakness — incomplete evaluation metrics: relies on accuracy despite class imbalance; lacks macro F1/precision/recall/ROC-AUC/confusion matrices, confidence intervals, and significance tests.

- Weakness — missing ablations: no analysis to justify graph design or compare to pure embedding-similarity graphs, document embeddings, or principled threshold selection/sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness — weak baselines and missing modalities: omits stronger baselines (TF-IDF+linear, averaged embeddings, doc2vec) and acoustic/prosodic features known to help MCI/AD detection.

- Weakness — dataset/preprocessing reporting: control counts, demographics, and disfluency-removal procedures are incompletely and inconsistently documented.

- Question — CV protocol: were folds constructed at the subject level for all datasets (especially ABCD) to prevent speaker leakage?

- Question — class balance and metrics: what are exact class counts per dataset/fold and can you provide macro/micro F1, precision/recall, ROC-AUC, and confusion matrices?

- Question — hyperparameter selection: how were embedding thresholds (0.7/0.4) chosen and was tuning nested within CV; please provide sensitivity analyses for thresholds, embedding size, window, and neighbor counts.

- Question — ablations/baselines: how does a pure embedding-similarity graph perform and how do averaged embeddings/SIF/doc2vec and TF-IDF+SVM compare; any per-feature network metric ablations?

- Question — multi-view details: are ensemble votes equally weighted or learned; did you try stacking or calibrated averaging; what are per-view contributions and error overlaps?

- Question — confounds/demographics: were demographic confounds (e.g., age in ABCD) controlled or included; could results be driven by such confounds?

- Question — reproducibility: will code, preprocessing scripts, stopword/tokenizer lists, disfluency rules, and trained embeddings be released?

- Additional comment — editing: the paper needs thorough language editing and correction of tables/figures/references.

- Additional comment — statistical rigor: add statistical testing (McNemar/bootstrap), confidence intervals from repeated CV, and avoid over-precise decimals on small samples.

- Additional comment — temper claims and limitations: clarify that enriched networks don’t uniformly beat all baselines (linguistic features win on cleaned Cinderella), discuss small sample sizes, overfitting risk, and plans for external validation.

- Additional comment — ethics: clarify ethical approvals and consent for the Brazilian datasets.
