- Summary: Paper uses word co-occurrence networks enriched with embeddings to detect MCI from speech transcripts across three datasets and compares CN features to linguistic metrics and BoW, exploring ensembles and multi-view combos, achieving ~65–80% accuracy.

- Contribution 1: Empirically shows embedding-enriched complex-network features are competitive and sometimes outperform parser-dependent linguistic features for MCI detection.

- Contribution 2: Proposes a practical, low-preprocessing evaluation framework that avoids heavy parsing and tests ensemble/multi-view approaches.

- Contribution 3: Provides cross-dataset analysis across English and Brazilian Portuguese with both manual and automatic segmentations.

- Strength: Motivation and relevance: Problem framing, clinical importance, and challenges (short texts, disfluencies, parser fragility) are clear and relevant.

- Strength: Lightweight/lang-agnostic: Enriched CN is a reasonable, tool-lean alternative to parser-heavy pipelines, useful for languages with weak parsers.

- Strength: Multi-dataset evaluation: Using three datasets (including two in Portuguese) and two segmentation regimes increases external validity.

- Strength: Sensible baselines: Inclusion of linguistic metrics and BoW baselines gives useful comparison; some combos show improvements.

- Strength: Transparent about parser limitations: Contrasting with manually revised data highlights brittleness of parsing-dependent features on noisy transcripts.

- Weakness: Multi-view method under-specified: Majority-vote approach lacks details on views, base learners, calibration, weighting/stacking, tie-breaking, and training protocol.

- Weakness: Missing hyperparameter reporting: No SVM C/γ, K in KNN, RF trees, feature scaling, or selection details.

- Weakness: Embeddings training unclear/insufficient: Unclear corpora used; likely trained on small task data — report corpora, vocab size, OOV rates, and ablations or use larger corpora.

- Weakness: Preprocessing choices unjustified: Removing disfluencies and stopwords may discard diagnostic signals; need justification and ablation (with/without).

- Weakness: Preprocessing order unclear: Tokenization, casing, stopword removal, disfluency handling, segmentation, normalization/lemmatization order not specified.

- Weakness: Evaluation lacks rigor: No statistical significance, confidence intervals, or detailed metrics (F1, ROC-AUC); potential subject-level data leakage (ABCD retells) not addressed.

- Weakness: Incomplete feature descriptions: Linguistic features and CN feature conventions (shortest path, disconnected graphs, edge construction) are insufficiently defined.

- Weakness: BoW weighting unjustified: Using tf instead of tf-idf not motivated; tf-idf results should be reported.

- Weakness: Related work superficial: Needs expanded coverage and precise comparisons to prior MCI/AD detection studies.

- Weakness: Readability/clarity issues: Abstract too long, unclear sentences, undefined acronyms, and confusing dataset descriptions.

- Question: Multi-view details requested — per-view classifiers, voting vs. stacking, tie resolution, and whether base learners are identical.

- Question: Embeddings details requested — training corpora per language, vocab size, OOV rates, subword use, hyperparameter tuning, and domain adaptation.

- Question: Preprocessing requested — exact rules/order for disfluency/stopword removal, sentence segmentation methods, and ablation results for keeping disfluencies/function words.

- Question: CV protocol requested — whether folds are stratified by subject (especially ABCD), and whether hyperparameter tuning used inner CV to avoid leakage.

- Question: CN construction requested — whether similarity edges are weighted or binarized, directed/undirected network, threshold τ selection, and handling of disconnected components for global metrics.

- Question: BoW rationale requested — why tf was used, normalization/feature selection, and tf-idf results.

- Request: Add statistical tests and confidence intervals and report precision/recall/F1 and ROC-AUC.

- Additional: Expand Section 4.2.2 with complete linguistic feature lists and tool versions; clarify embedding source lines and threshold tuning protocol.

- Additional: Improve dataset clarity — name datasets early, define “segmented,” explain manual segmentation, class composition, and provide citations/links.

- Additional: Table/data: Include class counts, narratives per class, tokens per narrative, vocab size, disfluency rates, durations, and segmentation method.

- Additional: Justify removing disfluencies with references and cite transcription norms.

- Additional: Specify CN feature definitions (e.g., shortest path, τ) and whether global metrics are component-based.

- Additional: Fix ACL style/citations, shorten abstract, correct grammar/typos, and consider supplementary material for extended features.

- Additional: Suggest ablations on weighted networks (co-occurrence frequency and cosine weight) and stopword retention due to their potential diagnostic value.
