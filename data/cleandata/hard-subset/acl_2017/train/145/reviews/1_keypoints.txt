- Strength: overall a very strong, well-structured, and clear paper.  
- Weakness: comparison against similar approaches could be extended.  
- Contribution: introduces multimodal (Gaussian-mixture) word distributions to represent multiple word meanings.  
- Relation to prior work: extends Vilnis & McCallum (2014) from unimodal Gaussians to multimodal ones to address polysemy.  
- Experiments: experimentation is correct and qualitative analysis (Table 1) matches expectations.  
- Suggestion: include a brief explanation of differences from Tian et al. (2014), which also uses mixture models to split word representations.  
- Missing citations to add: Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015).  
- Suggested evaluation: include results from those prior approaches in Tables 3 and 4 for comparison.  
- Question for authors: why does w2gm lose performance compared to w2g on the SWCS analysis?
