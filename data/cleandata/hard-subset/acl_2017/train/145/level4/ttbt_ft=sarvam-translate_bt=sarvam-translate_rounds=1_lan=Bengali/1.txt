Review: Multimodal Word Distribution

- Strengths: Overall, a very strong paper.
- Weaknesses: Comparison with similar methods could be more extensive.
- General Discussion:
The main goal of this paper is to introduce a new model of multimodal word distribution, formed from Gaussian mixtures for multiple meanings. In other words, a word is represented by a set of multiple Gaussian distributions. This method extends the model introduced by Vilnis and McCullum (2014), which represents a word as a unimodal Gaussian distribution. Using multimodal, the current method solves the problem of polysemy.
Overall, a very strong paper, well-structured and clear. The experiments are accurate, and the qualitative analysis in Table 1 shows results expected from the method. There are not many errors here, and all my comments below are to help the paper achieve additional clarity.
Some comments:
_ It might be interesting to include a brief explanation of the differences between Tian et al.'s 2014 method and the current method. Both methods use a mixture model, dividing the representation of a single word into multiple prototypes.
_ Some references are missing that could be mentioned in related work:
