- Overall assessment: very strong paper — well-structured, clear, sound experiments, and qualitative analysis aligns with expectations.  
- Main weakness: comparison with related approaches could be expanded.  
- Contribution: proposes multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to capture polysemy, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).  
- Request to clarify differences between this method and Tian et al. (2014), which also uses mixture models to split word representations.  
- Missing related-work citations to include: Neelakantan et al. (2014) “Efficient Non‑parametric Estimation of Multiple Embeddings per Word,” Li & Jurafsky (2015) “Do Multi‑Sense Embeddings Improve NLU?,” and Liu et al. (2015) “Topical Word Embeddings.”  
- Suggestion: include results from those cited approaches in Tables 3 and 4 for direct comparison.  
- Question for authors: what explains the drop in performance of w2gm relative to w2g in the SWCS analysis?
