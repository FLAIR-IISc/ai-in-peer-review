- Introduces probabilistic multimodal word embeddings using mixtures of Gaussians to model polysemy, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).
- Proposes a max-margin energy-based training objective with an expected likelihood kernel for analytic, tractable similarity between mixtures that incorporates uncertainty.
- Shows unsupervised sense discovery with strong quantitative results on word similarity and entailment benchmarks and supportive qualitative analyses.

- Natural and principled extension over unimodal Gaussians that captures multiple senses while retaining uncertainty information.
- Argues and demonstrates that unimodal Gaussians become overly diffuse for polysemous words and mixtures mitigate this issue.
- Training objective and energy function are technically sound and analytically tractable; paper offers insight into partial energy evolution during training.
- Qualitative nearest-neighbor results convincingly show sense separation for words like “rock” and “bank.”
- Empirically outperforms strong baselines (word2vec skip-gram, unimodal Gaussians) across multiple datasets; KL-based measures improve entailment.
- Provides practical details on numerical stability, covariance parameterization, initialization, and optimization, and demonstrates scalability to multi-billion-token corpora.

- Lacks direct comparisons to closely related multi-prototype baselines (e.g., Tian et al. 2014), weakening the empirical case.
- Missing important related-work citations and baseline comparisons: Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), Liu et al. (2015) are not included in tables or discussion.
- Needs a clearer, concise differentiation from Tian et al. (2014) regarding modeling and training differences (EM-based sense assignment vs. probabilistic mixtures).
- Underperformance of the multimodal model (w2gm) versus the unimodal variant (w2g) on SCWS is surprising and unexplained.

- What explains w2gm’s loss of performance relative to w2g on SCWS — component count K, similarity metric, or training hyperparameters?
- Please provide an explicit comparison with Tian et al. (2014) and ideally include their method as a baseline.
- Can the authors add results or discussion for Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015), or comment on expected performance differences and integration feasibility?
