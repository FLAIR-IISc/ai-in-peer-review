- Primary contribution: propose multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to capture polysemy, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).  
- Overall assessment: very strong, well-structured, and clear paper with solid experiments and consistent qualitative results (Table 1).  
- Weakness: comparisons with related multi-prototype/multi-sense approaches are limited and should be expanded.  
- Request clarification on how this approach differs from Tian et al. (2014), which also uses mixture models to split word representations.  
- Recommend adding citations: Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015).  
- Suggest including results from these cited approaches in Tables 3 and 4 for more informative comparisons.  
- Ask authors to explain the performance drop of w2gm relative to w2g in the SWCS analysis.
