- Proposal: Model words as Gaussian mixtures to capture multiple senses, asymmetric entailment, and calibrated uncertainty using an expected likelihood kernel with a max-margin objective.
- Contribution 1: Introduces multimodal probabilistic word representations (Gaussian mixtures) that jointly model polysemy, uncertainty, and entailment.
- Contribution 2: Provides an efficient closed-form energy (expected likelihood kernel) with a scalable max-margin training procedure.
- Contribution 3: Offers empirical and qualitative evidence (nearest neighbors, visualizations, similarity and entailment tasks) that the method outperforms skip-gram and unimodal Gaussian baselines.
- Strength: Effectively represents polysemy and uncertainty via distinct mixture modes and covariances, avoiding variance inflation seen in unimodal Gaussians.
- Strength: Learning setup is principled and scalable—closed-form kernel, practical stability techniques, and training on billion-token corpora.
- Strength: Quantitative improvements on word similarity benchmarks (e.g., WS-353, MEN) and better lexical entailment performance, especially using asymmetric metrics.
- Strength: Clear, interpretable presentation with useful visualizations and analyses explaining reduced variances for polysemous words.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of number of mixture components (K); experiments largely fix K=2 with little sensitivity analysis or selection criteria.
- Weakness: Restrictive use of spherical covariances limits modeling of directional uncertainty; no reported results for diagonal covariances or trade-offs.
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues—Table 4 not cited and a missing publication year for Luong et al.
- Question: How is K chosen in practice—please provide quantitative results varying K (e.g., 1,2,3) and discuss overfitting/instability as K grows?
- Question: For monosemous words, do extra components collapse or split senses—please provide examples or statistics.
- Question: Is spherical covariance chosen solely for efficiency—report results with diagonal covariances or negative findings if tried.
- Question: What practical default settings (K, covariance type, dimension, window) and failure modes do the authors recommend for new users?
- Minor comments: Reference Table 4 in the text and add the publication year for Luong et al. in the references.
