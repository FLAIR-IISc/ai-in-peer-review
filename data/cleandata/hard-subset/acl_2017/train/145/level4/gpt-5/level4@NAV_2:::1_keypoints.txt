- Overall assessment: very strong submission.  
- Proposal: represents words as mixtures of Gaussians to model multimodal senses, entailment, and richer uncertainty.  
- Novelty: extends Vilnis & McCallum (2014) by moving from single Gaussians to mixtures to directly handle polysemy.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and clear.  
- Experiments: empirical evaluations are sound and qualitative results align with multi-prototype expectations.  
- Weakness: empirical comparison should be broadened to include more closely related methods.  
- Missing citations: Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), Liu et al. (AAAI 2015) should be discussed.  
- Comparison request: briefly articulate differences versus Tian et al. (2014), since both produce multiple prototypes per word.  
- Evaluation request: include results from the mentioned related approaches in Tables 3 and 4 to strengthen comparisons.
