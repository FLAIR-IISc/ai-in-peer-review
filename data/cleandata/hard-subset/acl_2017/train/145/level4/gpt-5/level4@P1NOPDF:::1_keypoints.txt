- Overall assessment: very strong, well-structured, and clear paper with sound experiments.
- Contribution: proposes multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to model multiple word senses, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).
- Qualitative results: Table 1’s analysis matches expectations for the proposed approach.
- Weakness: empirical comparison against similar multisense methods is limited and should be expanded.
- Missing citations: Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015) should be cited.
- Empirical suggestion: include results from the above multisense embedding methods in Tables 3 and 4 for a more comprehensive comparison.
- Clarification request: explain how this approach differs from Tian et al. (2014) in modeling, training, or objective.
- Question: account for the drop in w2gm performance relative to w2g in the SWCS analysis.
- Administrative: reviewer has read the authors’ response.
