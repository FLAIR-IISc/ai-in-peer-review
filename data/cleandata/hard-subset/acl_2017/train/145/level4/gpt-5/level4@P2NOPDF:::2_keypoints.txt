- Paper proposes Mixture-of-Gaussians word embeddings with max-margin training and expected likelihood kernel similarity, showing strong results on similarity and entailment tasks.
- Problem is well motivated and clearly defined.
- Mixture-of-Gaussians embeddings are more expressive than point vectors, capturing multiple senses and encoding uncertainty.
- Max-margin objective plus closed-form similarity enables efficient training.
- Work is a meaningful contribution to word representation and the paper is generally well written.
- No major flaws; main concerns are requests for clarifications and additional analysis.
- Choice of number of components (k): experiments fix k=2 — reviewer asks how k was chosen, sensitivity to k, risk of overfitting or mode redundancy, and behavior for monosemous words.
- Covariance structure: experiments use spherical covariances — reviewer asks if this was for efficiency/stability, whether diagonal/anisotropic covariances were evaluated, and how that trade-off affects representations.
- Minor comments: Table 4 is not referenced in the text; Luong et al. reference is missing the publication year.
- Reviewer notes they have read the authors’ response.
