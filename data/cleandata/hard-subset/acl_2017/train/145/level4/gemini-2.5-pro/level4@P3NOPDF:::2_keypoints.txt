- Clear motivation/problem definition: paper is well-written and addresses the inability of standard embeddings to model multiple word senses.  
- Expressive representation: using Gaussian mixtures allows modeling multiple senses and uncertainty per word.  
- Efficient training framework: max-margin objective plus expected likelihood kernel gives an elegant, computationally efficient training approach.  
- Choice of Gaussian components (k): experiments fix k=2—need sensitivity analysis to different k and potential overfitting with larger k.  
- Analysis of learned distributions: missing inspection of whether monosemous words collapse to a single component or acquire spurious modes.  
- Covariance matrix structure: authors use spherical covariances; should evaluate diagonal (or fuller) covariances and discuss computational vs representational trade-offs.  
- Presentation issue: Table 4 is not referenced in the main text.  
- Bibliography issue: Luong et al. reference is missing the publication year.
