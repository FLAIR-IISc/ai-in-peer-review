- Proposes representing words as Gaussian mixture models to capture multiple senses, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and well-structured.
- Valuable contribution: logical extension to multimodal embeddings that targets polysemy.
- Solid experimental evaluation with compelling qualitative evidence (Table 1) showing distinct senses.
- Main weakness: limited comparison to contemporary multi-sense embedding approaches, making state-of-the-art claims unclear.
- Request to clarify differences between the proposed model and Tian et al. (2014), since both use mixture-based prototypes.
- Suggestion to include/compare against Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015) in related work and Tables 3–4.
- Question about SWCS results: multimodal w2gm underperforms unimodal w2g—ask for hypothesis whether SWCS favors single/averaged representations.
