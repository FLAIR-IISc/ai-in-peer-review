- Proposes representing words as Gaussian mixtures to capture polysemy, showing effectiveness on word similarity and entailment tasks.
- Modeling choices (Gaussian mixtures) are well-motivated and more expressive than deterministic vectors, potentially capturing multiple senses, mass, and uncertainty.
- Training uses an efficient max-margin objective with a closed-form expected likelihood kernel as the similarity measure.
- Paper is generally well-written and represents an important contribution to word embeddings.
- Main weakness: insufficient justification for key hyperparameter and modeling choices.
- Critical hyperparameter k (number of Gaussian components) is fixed at k=2 without explanation; sensitivity to k should be evaluated.
- Recommend qualitative analysis of learned distributions, e.g., behavior for monosemous words.
- All experiments use spherical covariances; need clarity if this was for efficiency and an ablation with diagonal covariances to assess modeling benefits.
- Minor: Table 4 is not referenced in the main text.
- Minor: Luong et al. citation is missing the publication year.
