- Clear motivation and well-defined problem statement.
- Gaussian mixture embeddings are more expressive than deterministic vectors, capturing multiple senses, sense probabilities, and uncertainty.
- The method is a valuable contribution to word embeddings research.
- Efficient training via a max-margin objective combined with a closed-form similarity metric.
- Manuscript is generally well-written and easy to follow.
- Choice of mixture components k is critical; experiments use k=2 and justification/analysis of larger k is requested.
- Need clarification on how monosemous words are represented by the learned distribution.
- Experiments use spherical covariance; question whether this was for efficiency and whether diagonal covariance (capturing axis-wise uncertainty) was tested.
- Table 4 is not referenced in the main text.
- The Luong et al. citation in the bibliography is missing a publication year.
- Reviewer has read the authors' rebuttal.
