- Clearly motivated problem and definition.
- Gaussian mixtures provide a more expressive representation than deterministic vectors, capturing multiple meanings, probability mass, and uncertainty.
- Max-margin learning objective with a closed-form expected likelihood kernel enables efficient training.
- Paper is generally well-written.
- Choice of number of Gaussian components (k) is unjustified (k=2 used everywhere); need analysis of varying k and effect on performance, plus qualitative analysis for unimodal words.
- Use of spherical covariances is limiting; clarify if chosen for efficiency and include ablation with diagonal covariances to capture anisotropic uncertainty.
- Table 4 is not referenced in the main text.
- Reference for Luong et al. is missing the publication year.
