Review: Multimodal Word Distributions

**Strengths:** This is a very strong paper that presents a novel approach to learning multimodal word distributions.

**Weaknesses:** One area for improvement is the comparison against similar approaches, which could be extended to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed model.

**General Discussion:**
This paper introduces a new model for learning multimodal word distributions, which represents words as a set of Gaussian distributions to capture multiple word meanings. This approach extends the unimodal Gaussian distribution model introduced by Vilnis and McCallum (2014) and addresses the problem of polysemy. The paper is well-structured, clear, and presents correct experimentation. The qualitative analysis in Table 1 shows expected results from the approach. Overall, the paper is strong, and the comments below are intended to provide suggestions for additional clarity.

**Some Comments:**

* It would be interesting to include a brief explanation of the differences between the approach proposed by Tian et al. (2014) and the current one. Both approaches use a mixture model to split single word representations into multiple prototypes.
* There are some missing citations that could be mentioned in the related work section, including:
	+ Efficient Non-parametric Estimation of Multiple Embeddings per Word in Vector Space by Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014)
	+ Do Multi-Sense Embeddings Improve Natural Language Understanding? by Li and Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015)
	+ Topical Word Embeddings by Liu et al. (AAAI 2015)
* Including the results from these approaches in Tables 3 and 4 could provide a more comprehensive comparison.
* A question to the authors: What do you attribute the loss of performance of w2gm against w2g in the analysis of SWCS?
