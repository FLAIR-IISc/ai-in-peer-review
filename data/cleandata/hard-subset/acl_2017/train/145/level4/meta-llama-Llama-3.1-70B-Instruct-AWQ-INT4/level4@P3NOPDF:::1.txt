**Review: Multimodal Word Distributions**

**Strengths:** This is a very strong paper, with a clear and well-structured presentation of a novel approach to learning multimodal word distributions.

**Weaknesses:** The comparison against similar approaches could be extended to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**General Discussion:**
The primary focus of this paper is the introduction of a new model for learning multimodal word distributions formed from Gaussian mixtures for multiple word meanings. This approach represents a word by a set of multiple Gaussian distributions, extending the model introduced by Vilnis and McCallum (2014), which represented words as unimodal Gaussian distributions. By using a multimodal approach, the current method effectively addresses the problem of polysemy.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the experimentation is sound. The qualitative analysis presented in Table 1 shows results that are consistent with the expected outcomes of the approach. The authors have done an excellent job in presenting their work, and the comments below are intended to help refine the paper further.

**Some Comments:**

* It would be interesting to include a brief explanation of the differences between the approach proposed by Tian et al. (2014) and the current one. Both methods split single word representations into multiple prototypes using a mixture model. A clear comparison of these two approaches would provide valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of each method.
* There are some missing citations that could be mentioned in the related work section, including:
	+ Efficient Non-parametric Estimation of Multiple Embeddings per Word in Vector Space by Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014)
	+ Do Multi-Sense Embeddings Improve Natural Language Understanding? by Li and Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015)
	+ Topical Word Embeddings by Liu et al. (AAAI 2015)
* The inclusion of results from these approaches in Tables 3 and 4 could provide a more comprehensive comparison of the proposed method with existing approaches.
* A question to the authors: What do you attribute the loss of performance of w2gm against w2g in the analysis of SWCS?
