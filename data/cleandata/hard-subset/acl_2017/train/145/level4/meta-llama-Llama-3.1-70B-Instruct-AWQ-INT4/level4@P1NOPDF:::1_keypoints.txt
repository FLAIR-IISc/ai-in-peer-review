- Overall assessment: paper is very strong.
- Main contribution: introduces multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to model multiple word senses.
- Novelty: extends Vilnis & McCallum (2014)’s unimodal Gaussian word representations to multimodal ones, effectively addressing polysemy.
- Presentation and experiments: paper is well-structured, clear, and experimentally sound; qualitative results align with expectations.
- Primary weakness: comparison to similar prior approaches is limited and could be expanded.
- Suggestion: briefly clarify differences between this method and Tian et al. (2014), which also uses mixture models for multiple prototypes.
- Missing citations to add: Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), and Liu et al. (AAAI 2015).
- Suggestion: include results from the missing prior approaches (above) in Tables 3 and 4 for more informative comparisons.
- Open question: explain why w2gm loses performance compared to w2g on the SWCS analysis.
