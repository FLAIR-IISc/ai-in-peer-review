Review: Multimodal Word Distributions

- Strengths: This paper presents a robust contribution to the field, showcasing a well-structured and clearly articulated argument.
- Weaknesses: A more comprehensive comparison with existing approaches would further enhance the paper's impact.

- General Discussion:
This paper primarily focuses on introducing a novel model for multimodal word distributions, leveraging Gaussian mixtures to represent multiple word meanings. By expanding on the unimodal Gaussian distribution model proposed by Vilnis and McCallum (2014), this approach effectively addresses the challenge of polysemy by representing words as a set of Gaussian distributions. Overall, the paper is exceptionally strong, with a coherent structure and lucid presentation. The experimental design is sound, and the qualitative analysis presented in Table 1 yields results consistent with expectations. The following comments aim to provide constructive feedback to further refine the paper.

Some comments:

* A brief discussion on the differences between the approach presented by Tian et al. (2014) and the current model would be beneficial. Both methods employ a mixture model to split single-word representations into multiple prototypes.
* The related work section could be strengthened by incorporating citations from relevant studies, such as:
  + Efficient Non-parametric Estimation of Multiple Embeddings per Word in Vector Space by Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014)
  + Do Multi-Sense Embeddings Improve Natural Language Understanding? by Li and Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015)
  + Topical Word Embeddings by Liu et al. (AAAI 2015)
* Including results from these approaches in Tables 3 and 4 would provide a more comprehensive comparison.
* A question for the authors: What factors contribute to the performance degradation of w2gm compared to w2g in the SWCS analysis?
