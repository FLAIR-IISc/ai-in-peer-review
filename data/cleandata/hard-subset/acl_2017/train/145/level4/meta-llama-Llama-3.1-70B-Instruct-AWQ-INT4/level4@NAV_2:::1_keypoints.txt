- Proposes representing words as multimodal Gaussian-mixture distributions to capture multiple senses and uncertainty.
- Extends Vilnis and McCallum (2014) by moving from unimodal to multimodal word distributions.
- Introduces an energy-based max-margin objective to learn Gaussian mixture parameters.
- Evaluates the method on multiple word similarity and entailment tasks with qualitative examples.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.
- Experiments are thorough and Table 1’s qualitative analysis aligns with expectations.
- Comprehensive quantitative evaluation across several tasks.
- Comparison to related methods is limited and should be expanded.
- Performance drop on the SCWS dataset is unexplained and needs analysis.
- Asks authors to explain the SCWS performance loss.
- Requests plans for extending the model to capture more complex meanings and relationships.
- Suggests clarifying differences from Tian et al. (2014) which also uses mixture prototypes.
- Recommends including comparisons to Neelakantan et al. (2014) and Li & Jurafsky (2015).
- Advises providing more detail on hyperparameter choices and their effects.
