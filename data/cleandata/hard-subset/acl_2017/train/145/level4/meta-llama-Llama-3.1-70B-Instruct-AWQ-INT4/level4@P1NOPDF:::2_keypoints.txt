- Proposes Gaussian mixture word embeddings to capture multiple word meanings (polysemy) using modes, probability mass, and uncertainty.
- Trains embeddings with a max-margin objective and uses the expected likelihood kernel as a closed-form similarity measure.
- Reports strong experimental results on word similarity and entailment tasks demonstrating effectiveness.
- Clear motivation and problem definition.
- Gaussian mixtures are more expressive than deterministic vectors and can model multi-modal meanings and uncertainty.
- Represents a significant contribution to word embedding research.
- Max-margin learning with closed-form similarity enables efficient training.
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Experiments fix the number of mixture components to k=2 without justification or analysis of how varying k affects performance.
- Questions about model behavior for words with a single dominant meaning (what learned distribution looks like).
- All experiments use spherical covariances—unclear if chosen for efficiency and how performance would change with diagonal covariances to model directional uncertainty.
- Minor issues: Table 4 is not referenced, and the Luong et al. citation is missing the publication year.
