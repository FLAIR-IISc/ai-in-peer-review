- Proposes using Gaussian mixtures to represent words, capturing multiple meanings; trained with a max-margin objective and expected likelihood kernel; shows effectiveness on similarity and entailment tasks.  
- Strength: problem is clearly motivated and well-defined.  
- Strength: Gaussian mixtures improve over deterministic vectors by modeling multiple modes, probability mass, and uncertainty.  
- Strength: Max-margin learning with closed-form similarity enables efficient training.  
- Strength: Paper is mostly well-written.  
- Weakness: Number of components k is fixed at 2 with no justification; unclear selection criteria and effect of larger k, and how single-meaning words are modeled.  
- Weakness: All experiments use spherical covariances; unclear if this choice is solely for efficiency and how diagonal/full covariances would affect performance.  
- Minor: Table 4 is not referenced in the text and should be removed or cited.  
- Minor: Reference to Luong et al. is missing the publication year.
