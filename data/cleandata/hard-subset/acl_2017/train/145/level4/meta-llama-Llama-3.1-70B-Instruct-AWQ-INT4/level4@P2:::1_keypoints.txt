- Proposes a new model learning multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to represent multiple word meanings.  
- Extends Vilnis and McCallum (2014) by moving from unimodal Gaussian to multimodal Gaussian-mixture word representations.  
- Addresses polysemy by representing each word as a set of Gaussian components.  
- Paper is well-structured, clear, and presents correct experimentation and qualitative analysis.  
- Method effectively handles polysemy in learned representations.  
- Qualitative results (Table 1) align with expected behavior of the approach.  
- Comparison to similar approaches is limited and could be expanded.  
- Missing citations (e.g., Neelakantan et al. 2014; Li and Jurafsky 2015) should be included in related work.  
- Including results from those prior multi-embedding methods in Tables 3 and 4 would strengthen empirical comparisons.  
- Question for authors: why does w2gm lose performance relative to w2g in the SWCS analysis?
