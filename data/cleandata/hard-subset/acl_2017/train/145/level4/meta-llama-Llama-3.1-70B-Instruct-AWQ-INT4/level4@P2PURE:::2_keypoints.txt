- Proposes Gaussian mixture word embeddings to capture polysemy and uncertainty, trained with a max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel as similarity.
- Experiments on word similarity and entailment show the approach is effective.
- Strength: Clear motivation and well-defined problem framing.
- Strength: Gaussian mixtures provide more expressive representations than deterministic vectors, modeling multiple meanings via modes and uncertainty.
- Strength: Important contribution to word embedding literature.
- Strength: Max-margin learning objective with closed-form similarity enables efficient training.
- Strength: Paper is mostly well-written.
- Question: Choice of number of Gaussian components (k=2) is not justified — need criteria for selecting k and effects of larger k.
- Suggestion: Visualize learned distributions for words with a single common meaning.
- Question: Use of spherical covariances throughout — is this for efficiency, and how would diagonal (non-spherical) covariances affect performance?
- Minor: Table 4 is not referenced in the text.
- Minor: Reference to Luong et al. is missing the publication year.
