- Proposes a Gaussian-mixture model to learn multimodal word distributions that capture multiple meanings (polysemy).  
- Extends Vilnis and McCallum (2014) by moving from unimodal Gaussian word representations to mixtures.  
- Presents a scalable, robust training procedure suitable for large corpora.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with thorough experimentation and qualitative results (Table 1) supporting the approach.  
- Comparison to similar mixture-based methods (e.g., Tian et al., 2014) is insufficiently detailed—differences should be explained.  
- Missing related-work citations that should be discussed: Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), Liu et al. (2015).  
- Results from the cited multi-sense and topical embedding methods should be included in Tables 3 and 4 for a more comprehensive comparison.  
- Question for authors: why does w2gm lose performance compared to w2g on the SCWS dataset?  
- Overall well-written and thoroughly evaluated, but would be strengthened by additional comparisons and explanations.
