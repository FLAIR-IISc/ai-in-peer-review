- Introduces multimodal Gaussian mixture word distributions to model polysemy, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).
- Proposes an energy-based max-margin objective for learning mixture parameters, optimized with mini-batch SGD.
- Shows effectiveness on benchmarks including word similarity and entailment tasks.
- Clear, well-structured presentation of the problem and proposed solution.
- Thorough experimentation with qualitative analysis (Table 1) matching expected behavior.
- Provides comprehensive comparisons with word2vec and Gaussian embeddings.
- Lacks comparison with more recent multi-embedding methods (Neelakantan et al., 2014; Li & Jurafsky, 2015).
- Missing inclusion of those methods’ results in Tables 3 and 4 limits comparative evaluation.
- Needs more detailed analysis of why w2gm underperforms w2g in the SWCS analysis.
- Asks authors to explain the performance loss of w2gm versus w2g on SWCS.
- Requests more details on optimization, hyperparameter choices, and convergence behavior.
- Asks how the model could be extended to capture complex semantics (word sense induction, lexical relations).
- Paper is well-written with effective figures/tables and is a potentially significant contribution to NLP applications.
