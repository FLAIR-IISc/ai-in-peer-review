- Strength: Overall very strong paper introducing a novel multimodal word-distribution model using Gaussian mixtures to capture polysemy.  
- Contribution: Extends Vilnis & McCallum (2014) by representing words with multiple Gaussian components rather than a single unimodal Gaussian.  
- Clarity/experiments: Paper is well-structured, clear, with sound experiments and qualitative results (Table 1) consistent with the approach.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to similar prior multimodal/multi-prototype approaches should be extended.  
- Suggested comparison: Briefly explain differences between this method and Tian et al. (2014), which also uses mixture-based multiple prototypes.  
- Missing citations to add: Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), and Liu et al. (AAAI 2015).
