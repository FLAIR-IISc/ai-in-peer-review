- Strength: Strong, clearly written, well-structured paper presenting a novel approach to learning multimodal word distributions.  
- Method: Introduces multimodal word representations using Gaussian mixtures to model multiple word meanings, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).  
- Benefit: Multimodal approach effectively addresses polysemy.  
- Experiments: Experimentation is sound and qualitative results (Table 1) match expectations.  
- Weakness: Evaluation lacks broader comparison to similar existing approaches.  
- Suggestion: Provide a clear comparison to Tian et al. (2014), which also uses mixture models to split word representations.  
- Missing citations: Include Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), and Liu et al. (AAAI 2015) in related work.  
- Suggestion: Add results from the cited multi-embedding/multi-sense methods to Tables 3 and 4 for a more comprehensive comparison.  
- Question: Explain why w2gm loses performance compared to w2g in the SWCS analysis.
