- Strength: The paper is solid, well-executed, and clearly written.
- Weakness: Comparisons with related multimodal/multi-prototype approaches are limited and should be expanded.

- Experiments: Experiments are appropriate and qualitative results (Table 1) align with the proposed approach.
- Clarification request: Explain how this approach differs from Tian et al. (2014), which also decomposes word representations into multiple prototypes.
- Missing citations: Add comparisons/citations to Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), and Liu et al. (AAAI 2015).
- Evaluation suggestion: Include results from the above approaches in Tables 3 and 4 for more complete comparisons.
