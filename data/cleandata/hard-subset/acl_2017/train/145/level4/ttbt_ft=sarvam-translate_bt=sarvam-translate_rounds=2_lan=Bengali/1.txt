Review: Multimodal Word Distribution

- Strengths: Overall, a very strong paper.
- Weaknesses: Could have been more comprehensive compared to similar methods.
- General Discussion:
The main goal of this paper is to introduce a new model of multimodal word distribution, which is formed from Gaussian mixtures for multiple meanings. In other words, a word is represented by a set of multiple Gaussian distributions. This method extends the model introduced by Villanis and McCullum (2014), which represents a word as a unimodal Gaussian distribution. Using multimodal, the current method solves the problem of polysemi.
Overall, a very strong paper, well-structured and clear. The experiments are accurate, and Table 1 shows results expected from the qualitative analysis method. There are not too many errors here, and below are all my comments to help the paper achieve additional clarity.
Some comments:
_ It might be interesting to include a brief explanation of the differences between Tian et al.'s 2014 method and the current method. Both methods use a mixture model, which divides the representation of a single word into multiple prototypes.
_ Some references are missing that could be mentioned in related work:
