- Clear motivation: addresses limitation of single-vector embeddings by using Gaussian mixtures to model polysemy.  
- Multiple Gaussian modes can capture distinct word senses, probability mass, and uncertainty.  
- Uses a max-margin objective and an expected likelihood kernel for efficient training and closed-form similarity measurement.  
- Experiments on word similarity and textual entailment demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Crucial hyperparameter k is fixed at 2 with insufficient justification; need experiments showing impact of varying k.  
- No analysis of model behavior for monosemous words to show whether mixtures are appropriate in those cases.  
- Spherical covariance assumption may limit expressiveness; require comparison or discussion of diagonal/general covariance trade-offs.  
- Request examples/visualizations of learned Gaussian mixtures for representative polysemous and monosemous words.  
- Minor issues: Table 4 is not referenced in the text; Luong et al. citation missing publication year.  
- Reviewer appreciated the authors’ clarifications in their response.
