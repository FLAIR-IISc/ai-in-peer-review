- Paper introduces multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to model word polysemy, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).
- Novelty and approach are clear, well-structured, and overall the paper is very strong.
- Experiments are sound and qualitative results (Table 1) align with expectations.
- Main weakness: comparisons to similar/multi-prototype approaches are limited and should be expanded.
- Request to clarify key differences between Tian et al. (2014) and the present mixture-based method.
- Suggest adding related citations: Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), and Liu et al. (AAAI 2015).
- Recommend including results from those related approaches in Tables 3 and 4 for more comprehensive comparison.
- Ask authors to explain why w2gm performs worse than w2g in the SWCS analysis.
