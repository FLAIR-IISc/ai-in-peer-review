- Strength: The paper is notably strong overall.
- Weakness: Evaluation should include more comprehensive comparisons to related work.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel multimodal word distribution model using Gaussian mixtures to represent multiple word meanings.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Vilnis & McCallum (2014)’s unimodal Gaussian embeddings to address polysemy.
- Writing and methodology: Well-written, clearly structured, with a strong methodological foundation.
- Results: Experimental results are sound and qualitative analysis (Table 1) matches expectations.
- Clarification requested: Explain distinctions between the current approach and Tian et al. (2014) which also uses mixture models.
- Related work additions: Suggest citing Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015).
- Comparative results: Recommend incorporating results from those related approaches into Tables 3 and 4 for context.
- Analysis question: Ask authors to explain why w2gm underperforms w2g in the SWCS analysis and what factors cause this.
