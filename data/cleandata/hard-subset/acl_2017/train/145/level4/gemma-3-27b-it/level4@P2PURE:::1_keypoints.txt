- Introduces multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to model multiple word meanings (polysemy).
- Extends the unimodal Gaussian word representation of Vilnis and McCallum (2014).
- Paper is strong overall: well-structured, clearly written, with sound experiments and supportive qualitative analysis.
- Main weakness: comparisons to related work are too narrow and should be broadened.
- Request to clarify key differences between this approach and Tian et al. (2014), since both use mixture models.
- Recommend citing Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015) in related work.
- Suggest including results from those related approaches in Tables 3 and 4 for comparison.
- Seeks an explanation for why w2gm performs worse than w2g in the SWCS analysis.
