- Introduces Gaussian mixture model for word embeddings to represent multiple word meanings and capture semantic nuance.
- Proposes an energy-based max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel for analytically tractable learning of mixture parameters.
- Provides comprehensive empirical evaluation and qualitative analysis showing gains on word similarity and entailment tasks.

- Novel use of Gaussian mixtures addresses limitations of unimodal distributions and offers analytic tractability.
- Consistent strong empirical results outperform unimodal Gaussian and skip-gram baselines across many datasets.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with thorough methodological and experimental descriptions.
- Practical implementation details and planned code release enhance reproducibility and real-world applicability.

- Comparison to other multi-prototype embedding methods (e.g., Neelakantan et al., Li & Jurafsky, Liu et al.) is limited and could be expanded.
- Model underperforms w2g on the SCWS dataset in some cases, requiring deeper analysis of failure modes.
- Lacks discussion of sensitivity to key hyperparameters like number of components K and margin m.

- Request for clarification on computational cost scaling of the Gaussian mixture model versus skip-gram or single-Gaussian embeddings as K increases.
- Asks whether alternative kernel functions were explored and what tradeoffs were observed.
- Requests examples of word pairs where w2gm performs worse than w2g to diagnose limitations.

- Interactive visualization website is a valuable resource for understanding embeddings.
- Supplementary material is well-organized and provides useful implementation and experimental details.
