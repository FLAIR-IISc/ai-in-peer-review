

Review: Multimodal Word Distributions  
- Strengths: Overall an exceptionally strong paper.  
- Weaknesses: The comparison against related approaches could be broadened.  
- General Discussion:  
The paper introduces a novel model for learning multimodal word distributions via Gaussian mixtures to capture multiple word meanings—representing each word as a set of Gaussian distributions. This extends Vilnis and McCallum’s (2014) unimodal Gaussian approach to address polysemy. The work is well-structured, clearly presented, and experimentally sound. Table 1’s qualitative analysis aligns with expectations. Minimal reservations exist, and all subsequent comments aim to enhance clarity.  
Some comments:  
- A brief clarification contrasting the current method with Tian et al. (2014) would be valuable, as both employ mixture models to split word representations into multiple prototypes.  
- Relevant citations are missing from Related Work:  
  • *Efficient Non-parametric Estimation of Multiple Embeddings per Word in Vector Space* (Neelakantan et al., EMNLP 2014)  
  • *Do Multi-Sense Embeddings Improve Natural Language Understanding?* (Li & Jurafsky, EMNLP 2015)  
  • *Topical Word Embeddings* (Liu et al., AAAI 2015)  
- Including results from these approaches in Tables 3 and 4 would strengthen comparative analysis.  
- Clarification request: What explains the performance decline of w2gm relative to w2g in the SWCS analysis?
