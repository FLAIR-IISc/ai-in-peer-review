- Introduces a novel multimodal word distribution model using Gaussian mixtures to represent multiple senses.  
- Extends Vilnis & McCallum (2014) from unimodal to multimodal Gaussian word representations.  
- Multimodal formulation effectively addresses word polysemy.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Experimental design and execution are sound.  
- Qualitative analysis (Table 1) matches expectations for the approach.  
- Weakness: comparisons with related work are insufficient and need expansion.  
- Request to clarify differences between this approach and Tian et al. (2014), since both use mixture-based multiple prototypes.  
- Related work should cite Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), and Liu et al. (AAAI 2015).  
- Include results from the cited approaches in Tables 3 and 4 to strengthen comparisons.  
- Authors should explain the observed performance drop of w2gm relative to w2g in the SWCS analysis and identify contributing factors.
