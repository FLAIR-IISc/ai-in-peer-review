

Review: Multimodal Word Distributions  

**Strengths:**  
This is a strong paper overall. The approach introduces a novel model for learning multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to represent multiple word senses, extending Vilnis and McCallum (2014), who modeled words as unimodal Gaussians. The multimodal formulation effectively addresses polysemy. The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and the experimental design and execution are sound. The qualitative analysis in Table 1 aligns with expectations for the approach.  

**Weaknesses:**  
The comparison with related work could be expanded to provide deeper contextualization of the contributions.  

**Comments:**  
1.  A brief explanation of the differences between the current approach and Tian et al. (2014) would be valuable. Both methods split word representations into multiple prototypes using mixture models, but the distinctions warrant clarification.  
2.  The related work section should cite the following relevant studies:  
    - *Efficient Non-Parametric Estimation of Multiple Embeddings per Word in Vector Space* (Neelakantan et al., EMNLP 2014)  
    - *Do Multi-Sense Embeddings Improve Natural Language Understanding?* (Li and Jurafsky, EMNLP 2015)  
    - *Topical Word Embeddings* (Liu et al., AAAI 2015)  
3.  Including results from these approaches in Tables 3 and 4 would strengthen the comparative analysis.  
4.  The authors should elaborate on the performance drop of *w2gm* relative to *w2g* in the SWCS analysis. What factors contribute to this observed loss?
