- Overall assessment: very strong paper—well-structured, clear, sound experimentation, and expected qualitative results.  
- Contribution: proposes multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to model polysemy, extending Vilnis & McCallum (2014).  
- Main weakness: comparison to similar prior approaches is limited and should be extended.  
- Clarification requested: explicitly state differences between this work and Tian et al. (2014), both using mixture models/multi-prototype representations.  
- Missing related-work citations: Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), Liu et al. (AAAI 2015).  
- Suggestion: include results from the above approaches in Tables 3 and 4 to strengthen empirical comparisons.  
- Question for authors: account for the performance drop of w2gm relative to w2g in the SWCS analysis.
