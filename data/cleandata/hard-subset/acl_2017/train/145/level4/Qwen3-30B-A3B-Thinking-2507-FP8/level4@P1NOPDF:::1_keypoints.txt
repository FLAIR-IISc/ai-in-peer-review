- Overall assessment: strong paper with clear, compelling contributions.  
- Main weakness: comparisons with related work are insufficient and should be strengthened.  
- Contribution: introduces multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to model polysemy (multiple Gaussian components per word).  
- Builds on prior work: extends Vilnis & McCallum (2014) unimodal Gaussian approach to capture multiple senses.  
- Presentation: well-structured, clearly written, and experiments are sound.  
- Qualitative results: Table 1 shows behavior consistent with the model’s design and effectiveness.  
- Clarify relation to Tian et al. (2014): briefly contrast mechanisms and objectives despite both using mixture-based component splits.  
- Expand related work citations: include Neelakantan et al. (2014), Li & Jurafsky (2015), and Liu et al. (2015) on multi-sense/topical embeddings.  
- Add comparative results: include performance from the cited multi-sense methods in Tables 3–4 for better contextualization.  
- Explain SWCS discrepancy: authors should elaborate on why W2GM underperforms W2G on the SWCS task.
