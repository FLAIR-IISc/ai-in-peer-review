- Overall assessment: Exceptionally strong paper.  
- Main contribution: Introduces multimodal word distributions using Gaussian mixtures to model polysemy, extending unimodal Gaussian embeddings.  
- Presentation: Well-structured, clearly presented, and experimentally sound; Table 1 qualitative analysis is convincing.  
- Main weakness: Comparisons to related approaches are too narrow and should be broadened.  
- Requested clarification: Contrast current method with Tian et al. (2014), which also uses mixture models for multiple prototypes.  
- Missing citations: Neelakantan et al. (EMNLP 2014), Li & Jurafsky (EMNLP 2015), and Liu et al. (AAAI 2015) should be cited.  
- Comparative experiments: Include results from the cited multi-embedding/topical-embedding approaches in Tables 3 and 4.  
- Clarification request: Explain why w2gm performs worse than w2g in the SWCS analysis.
