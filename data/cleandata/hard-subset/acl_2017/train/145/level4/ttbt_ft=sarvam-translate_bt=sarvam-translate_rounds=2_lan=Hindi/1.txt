Review: Multimodal Word Distribution

- Strengths: Overall, a very strong paper.
- Weaknesses: Comparison with similar approaches could have been improved.
- General Discussion:
The main focus of this paper is the introduction of a new model for learning multimodal word distribution, which is composed of Gaussian mixtures for multiple word meanings. That is, representing a word by a set of multiple Gaussian distributions. This approach is an extension of the model presented by Vilnis and McCullum (2014), which represented a word as a unimodal Gaussian distribution. Using multimodal, the current approach overcomes the problem of polysemy.
Overall, a very strong paper, well-structured and clear. The experiment is correct, and the qualitative analysis in Table 1 shows the expected results from the approach. There is not much to fault, and all my comments below are to help the paper gain additional clarity.
Some comments:
_ It might be interesting to include a brief overview of the differences between the approach of Tian et al. 2014 and the current approach. Both divide the single word representation into multiple prototypes using a mixture model.
_ Some references are missing that could be mentioned in related works:
