- Proposes representing words as Gaussian Mixture Models (w2gm) to capture multiple senses per word.  
- Learns GMM parameters using an energy-based ranking objective and the expected likelihood kernel for efficiency.  
- Shows qualitative and quantitative gains over word2vec and single-Gaussian embeddings on similarity and entailment tasks.  

- Contribution: Multimodal GMM representations better capture polysemy and nuanced meanings than single Gaussians.  
- Contribution: Practical, scalable training with attention to numerical stability and initialization, enabling training on billions of words.  
- Contribution: Strong empirical validation across tasks, with particularly notable improvements on entailment.  

- Strength: Novel and logical extension of probabilistic embeddings to mixtures, addressing polysemy.  
- Strength: Consistently strong quantitative results against competitive baselines.  
- Strength: Clear, thorough presentation with useful figures and supplementary implementation details.  
- Strength: Demonstrated practicality and scalability with stability considerations.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and metrics.  

- Weakness: Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., number of components K, learning rates) and weak justification for K=2.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art contextual embeddings (BERT/ELMo/RoBERTa) to contextualize results.  
- Weakness: Insufficient theoretical justification for choosing the expected likelihood kernel over alternatives like KL divergence.  
- Weakness: Qualitative analysis could be deeper with more case studies of polysemous words and learned distributions.  

- Question: Provide detailed analysis of hyperparameter impacts and trade-offs when increasing K.  
- Question: Any experiments or discussion comparing w2gm to contextual embedding models, or integration possibilities?  
- Question: Elaborate theoretical motivations and specific properties favoring the expected likelihood kernel.  
- Question: Provide a detailed computational cost breakdown of training w2gm versus w2g, accounting for mixture complexity.  

- Additional: Interactive visualization link is valuable.  
- Additional: Supplementary material is essential for reproducibility.  
- Additional: Overall assessment finds the paper very strong and a significant contribution.  

- Overall score: Strong Accept (8.5/10).
