- Proposes Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) word embeddings to capture polysemy, uncertainty, and entailment better than point or single-Gaussian embeddings.  
- Introduces a maximum-margin energy-based ranking objective using the expected likelihood kernel for training.  
- Presents a scalable, analytically tractable training procedure suitable for large corpora.  
- Shows strong empirical improvements on word similarity and entailment benchmarks, especially for polysemous words.  
- Effectively addresses the limitation of traditional embeddings in representing multiple senses.  
- Demonstrates technically sound derivations and computational efficiency via the expected likelihood kernel.  
- Provides practical implementation details (stability, initialization, optimization) that aid reproducibility.  
- Offers clear presentation and useful visualizations, including an interactive demo.  
- Main novelty is the integration of existing components (GMMs, kernel, energy-based training) rather than any single novel element.  
- Lacks sufficient analysis of sensitivity to the number of Gaussian components (K) and trade-offs with computational cost.  
- Comparisons to stronger, modern baselines (e.g., contextualized embeddings like BERT/ELMo) are missing.  
- Interpretation of individual Gaussian components remains challenging and underexplored.  
- Requests analysis of performance sensitivity to K and computational trade-offs.  
- Asks whether comparisons to contextualized embeddings were considered or possible.  
- Seeks elaboration on methods to improve interpretability of mixture components.  
- Requests confirmation of public availability and longevity of the interactive visualization.  
- Supplementary material and references are comprehensive and helpful.  
- Minor grammatical edits could improve readability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, worthy of acceptance with minor revisions.
