- Summary: Proposes representing words as Gaussian Mixture Models trained with a max-margin energy-based ranking objective using the expected likelihood kernel, showing improvements on word similarity and entailment tasks.

- Contribution 1: Introduces GMMs to model word polysemy and uncertainty, addressing limitations of unimodal embeddings.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates a scalable, tractable implementation (expected likelihood kernel) capable of training on very large corpora.

- Contribution 3: Provides empirical evidence and ablation studies showing gains over strong baselines (word2vec, unimodal Gaussian embeddings).

- Strength 1: Clear motivation and problem definition emphasizing polysemy and multimodal meanings.

- Strength 2: Solid theoretical foundation using the expected likelihood kernel and a max-margin ranking objective.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across tasks and datasets with appropriate baselines.

- Strength 4: Practical implementation details (optimization, numerical stability) and scalability considerations are well addressed.

- Strength 5: Convincing qualitative analysis (visualizations, nearest neighbors) showing multi-sense capture.

- Weakness 1: Individual model components are well-known; novelty is mainly in their combination.

- Weakness 2: Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially choice of number of components K.

- Weakness 3: Computational overhead of GMM training/evaluation is higher and needs clearer quantitative comparison to baselines.

- Weakness 4: Lacks detailed error analysis to show where the model still fails and why.

- Question 1: How were hyperparameters (K, learning rate, margin) chosen; were automated tuning methods used?

- Question 2: Can you provide exact training time and memory usage compared to baselines on the reported datasets?

- Question 3: Have you performed error analysis to identify failure modes or challenging word types?

- Question 4: Why was K=2 chosen as primary; what are results and challenges with other K values?

- Question 5: How do these embeddings perform on complex downstream tasks (e.g., text classification, machine translation)?

- Additional comment 1: Interactive visualization link and supplementary material are useful.

- Additional comment 2: Writing is clear and the paper is recommended for acceptance subject to addressing concerns.
