- Proposes representing words as Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) to capture polysemy and richer uncertainty than point or single-Gaussian embeddings.  
- Trains GMM embeddings with a max-margin energy-based ranking objective using the expected likelihood kernel for tractability.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains on qualitative analyses, word similarity benchmarks, and entailment tasks versus baseline methods.  

- Contribution: introduces a principled multi-modal word representation improving modeling of multiple meanings and uncertainty.  
- Contribution: presents a scalable training procedure combining GMMs, an energy objective, and the expected likelihood kernel.  
- Contribution: provides thorough empirical validation across tasks and useful visualizations.  

- Strength: theoretical motivation and soundness for using GMMs and the expected likelihood kernel.  
- Strength: strong empirical results, especially on tasks sensitive to polysemy (e.g., entailment).  
- Strength: demonstrated scalability to large corpora with implementation and optimization details.  
- Strength: clear presentation and helpful visualizations (figure and interactive site).  
- Strength: comprehensive comparisons to word2vec and prior probabilistic embeddings.  

- Weakness: core components (GMMs, expected likelihood kernel) are not individually novel; novelty is in their integration for embeddings.  
- Weakness: performance appears sensitive to hyperparameters (notably number of components K), with limited exploration beyond K=2.  
- Weakness: increased model complexity reduces interpretability of individual Gaussian components.  
- Weakness: results vary with context window size and this dependence is not deeply analyzed.  

- Question: How sensitive is performance to different values of K and were automated hyperparameter searches used?  
- Question: How are individual Gaussian components interpreted or visualized, and are there automated methods to do so?  
- Question: What is the detailed computational cost breakdown, especially as K and corpus size scale?  
- Question: How were negative sampling parameters (e.g., unigram exponent 3/4) chosen and were other strategies tested?  

- Additional: interactive visualization link was inaccessible during review.  
- Additional: paper should discuss limitations and future directions (alternative kernels, external knowledge).  
- Additional: exploring different initialization strategies could be valuable.  

- Overall recommendation: strong submission with solid integration and empirical validation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
