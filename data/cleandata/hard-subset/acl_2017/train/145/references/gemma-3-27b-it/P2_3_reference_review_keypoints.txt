- Summary: Proposes word embeddings as Gaussian Mixtures to model polysemy and uncertainty, trained with a max-margin energy-based ranking objective using the expected likelihood kernel, showing improved word similarity and entailment performance.
- Contribution 1: Principled modeling of polysemy via Gaussian mixtures offering greater flexibility than single Gaussians or point embeddings.
- Contribution 2: Empirical improvements across standard word similarity and entailment benchmarks compared to baselines.
- Contribution 3: Practical training details addressing numerical instability, initialization, and optimization for applicability.
- Strength 1: Clear motivation and problem definition explaining limits of existing embeddings and rationale for mixtures.
- Strength 2: Solid experimental evaluation with intrinsic and extrinsic tasks and comparisons to several baselines.
- Strength 3: Technical soundness using the expected likelihood kernel and practical consideration for training stability.
- Strength 4: Qualitative analyses (visualizations, nearest neighbors like “rock” and “bank”) effectively demonstrate sense capture.
- Strength 5: Commitment to reproducibility with promised code release and detailed hyperparameter/training information.
- Weakness 1: Limited novelty—Gaussian mixtures are not fundamentally new; novelty is mainly their combination with a specific energy and objective.
- Weakness 2: Increased model complexity and hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., number of components K) without thorough computational cost or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness 3: Entailment improvements are smaller than similarity gains and lack deeper analysis explaining why.
- Weakness 4: Missing ablation study to isolate the impact of the expected likelihood kernel, objective choice, and other components.
- Question 1: Provide detailed computational cost analysis as vocabulary size and K increase.
- Question 2: Report sensitivity and results from more extensive searches over the number of Gaussian components K.
- Question 3: Elaborate on training challenges (numerical instability) and how solutions resolved them.
- Question 4: Present specific entailment examples where the model outperforms baselines and failure cases.
- Question 5: Share more on the initialization scheme and its effect on convergence and final performance.
- Additional comments: Interactive visualization is useful; related work coverage is comprehensive; paper is well-written and organized.
- Overall assessment: Solid, practically and empirically valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
