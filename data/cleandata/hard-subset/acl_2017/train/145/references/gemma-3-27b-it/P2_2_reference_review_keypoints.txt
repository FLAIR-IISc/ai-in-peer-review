- Summary: Proposes word embeddings as Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) with an Expected Likelihood Kernel objective, showing improved word similarity and entailment performance.

- Main contribution 1: Applies GMMs to word embeddings to model multiple senses, uncertainty, and entailment within a single word representation.

- Main contribution 2: Uses the Expected Likelihood Kernel in a max-margin objective to enable scalable training on large corpora.

- Main contribution 3: Empirically validates that GMM embeddings capture polysemy via nearest-neighbor analyses and improved task performance.

- Strength 1: Directly addresses polysemy by separating meanings, supported by qualitative examples and visualizations.

- Strength 2: Strong experimental results with statistically meaningful improvements over several baselines.

- Strength 3: Technically sound and scalable implementation, with attention to numerical stability and training speed.

- Strength 4: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures and tables.

- Weakness 1: Core components (GMMs, Expected Likelihood Kernel) are not entirely novel; novelty is mostly in their combination and application.

- Weakness 2: Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., choice of K=2) and trade-offs for larger K.

- Weakness 3: Lacks ablation studies to isolate contributions of components like the kernel vs. simpler alternatives.

- Weakness 4: Entailment results lack deeper error analysis and illustrative failure/success examples.

- Question 1: How sensitive is performance to the number of Gaussian components (K), and were larger K values explored?

- Question 2: Can you detail computational complexity and scaling with vocabulary and corpus size?

- Question 3: How does this model compare to modern transformer-based embeddings (BERT, RoBERTa) on the same tasks or in limitations?

- Question 4: Can you provide examples where the model fails to capture distinct meanings and explain why?

- Additional comment 1: Interactive visualization link is a useful resource.

- Additional comment 2: Supplementary material is comprehensive and well-organized.

- Overall assessment: Strong submission addressing an important problem with solid empirical results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
