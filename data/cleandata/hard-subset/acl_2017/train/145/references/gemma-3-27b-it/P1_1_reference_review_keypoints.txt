- Proposes using Gaussian Mixture Models to represent words as distributions over multiple meanings to better capture polysemy.
- Trains GMM embeddings with a maximum-margin energy-based ranking objective and uses the expected likelihood kernel for tractability.
- Demonstrates improved performance on word similarity and entailment tasks versus point and single-Gaussian baselines.
- Contribution: effective probabilistic modeling of polysemy with a scalable GMM implementation.
- Contribution: practical training procedure addressing computational complexity and numerical stability.
- Contribution: strong empirical results across multiple datasets, showing practical benefits.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem definition highlighting limitations of existing embeddings for polysemy.
- Strength: technically sound choice of expected likelihood kernel and attention to optimization/numerical issues.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation against relevant baselines (word2vec, prior Gaussian embeddings).
- Strength: qualitative analysis and visualizations that illustrate distinct senses for polysemous words.
- Strength: scalability demonstrated on corpora with billions of words.
- Weakness: limited novelty of core components—the novelty is mainly in their integration and application.
- Weakness: insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., number of components K, margin m).
- Weakness: lacks exploration of pre-training effects (e.g., initializing from word2vec).
- Weakness: limited error analysis—no detailed cases showing where the model still fails.
- Weakness: dense result tables hinder quick interpretation; presentation could be streamlined.
- Question: clarify which aspects are most novel relative to existing probabilistic embeddings.
- Question: provide hyperparameter ablation results for K and margin m.
- Question: did you try pre-training GMMs from simpler embeddings (e.g., word2vec)?
- Question: provide examples and insights for cases where the model performs poorly.
- Question: confirm availability of the interactive visualization and provide a stable link.
- Additional: ensure supplementary material is comprehensive and well-organized.
- Additional: discuss relation to contextualized embeddings (BERT, ELMo) and contrast approaches.
- Recommendation: overall strong submission; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
