- Proposal: Represent words as Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) instead of single vectors to capture polysemy and uncertainty.
- Method: Train GMM embeddings with a max-margin energy-based ranking objective using the expected likelihood kernel for tractable computation.
- Empirical result: Shows improved performance over existing methods on word similarity and entailment tasks, especially for polysemous words.

- Contribution: Frames word embeddings probabilistically with GMMs to model multiple senses and uncertainty.
- Contribution: Achieves analytical tractability by combining GMMs with the expected likelihood kernel and scalable optimization.
- Contribution: Empirical demonstrations and ablations indicate advantages in representing polysemy and choice of similarity measures.

- Strength: Clear motivation and well-defined problem addressing limitations of point embeddings.
- Strength: Technically sound and novel application of GMMs with an efficient energy function.
- Strength: Compelling experimental results, qualitative nearest-neighbor analyses, and informative ablation studies.
- Strength: Thorough implementation details and claims of scalability to large corpora.
- Strength: Well-written paper with helpful figures and clarity.

- Weakness: Lacks deeper analysis of how meanings map to mixture components and quantitative measures of component semantic coherence.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, especially number of components K (mostly K=2).
- Weakness: Comparisons do not include stronger recent baselines like contextualized embeddings (BERT/ELMo).
- Weakness: Potential redundancy among mixture components is not addressed or mitigated.

- Question: Can the authors provide quantitative measures (e.g., WordNet-based) for semantic coherence within components?
- Question: What were results and trade-offs when experimenting with larger K values?
- Question: How does the model compare to contextualized embeddings like BERT/ELMo on the evaluated tasks?
- Question: Are there mechanisms to prevent redundant or highly similar mixture components?
- Question: Would using full covariance matrices improve modeling at acceptable computational cost?

- Additional: Interactive visualization and detailed supplementary material are helpful.
- Additional: Recommend discussing limitations of the expected likelihood kernel, including sensitivity to outliers/noise.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel submission with solid results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address analysis, baselines, and hyperparameter/component concerns.
