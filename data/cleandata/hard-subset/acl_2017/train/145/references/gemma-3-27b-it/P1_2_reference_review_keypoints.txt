- Proposes representing words as Gaussian Mixtures (GMs) to capture multiple meanings and nuances beyond point or single-Gaussian embeddings.
- Introduces a training method using an energy-based maximum margin objective with an expected likelihood kernel for analytic tractability.
- Presents w2gm, demonstrates improved performance on word similarity and entailment tasks over prior methods.
- Contribution: principled multimodal word embeddings that better model polysemy.
- Contribution: practical training and scaling strategies using the expected likelihood kernel and sensible initialization/stability techniques.
- Contribution: empirical validation showing consistent improvements over baselines including Gaussian embeddings.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem definition addressing limitations of unimodal embeddings.
- Strength: technically sound and novel use of the expected likelihood kernel for training GMs.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across established datasets and comparisons to multiple baselines.
- Strength: detailed implementation and supplementary material that aid reproducibility; code is publicly available.
- Strength: compelling qualitative results and visualizations showing meaningful sense separation.
- Weakness: limited analysis of how learned GM components map to linguistic categories or resources like WordNet.
- Weakness: insufficient ablation study on hyperparameter sensitivity, notably the number of components K.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to recent contextualized models (e.g., BERT, RoBERTa) for broader contextual benchmarking.
- Weakness: unclear practical advantage over simpler multi-sense approaches given modest performance gains versus added complexity.
- Question: Can the authors elaborate on interpretability and mapping of components to known semantic categories?
- Question: What is the impact of varying the number of Gaussian components K across tasks and datasets?
- Question: How does w2gm compare to contextualized models like BERT/RoBERTa, and are there scenarios where w2gm is preferable?
- Question: What are the computational costs of training and inference for w2gm versus simpler embedding models?
- Additional: paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Additional: interactive visualization is a valuable aid.
- Additional: supplementary material is thorough and strengthens the work.
- Recommendation: strong submission, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
