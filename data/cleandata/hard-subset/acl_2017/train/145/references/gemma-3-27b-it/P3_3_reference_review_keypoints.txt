- Proposes Gaussian mixture word embeddings to model polysemy, replacing single-point or unimodal Gaussian representations.
- Trains the model with a maximum-margin energy-based ranking objective using the expected likelihood kernel, with attention to numerical stability and initialization.
- Demonstrates improved performance on word similarity and entailment tasks, especially for polysemous words; includes useful visualizations and supplementary material.
- Main contributions: principled polysemy modeling via Gaussian mixtures, an effective tractable training objective/kernel choice, and thorough empirical validation.
- Strengths: strong motivation and theoretical foundation, technical soundness, comprehensive evaluation, statistically significant performance gains, and clear writing.
- Weaknesses: limited novelty of individual components (novelty mainly in their integration), insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, superficial scalability discussion, and lack of comparison with contextualized embeddings (e.g., BERT/ELMo).
- Questions for authors: provide detailed hyperparameter analysis/guidelines, specify computational resources/training time and bottlenecks for large corpora, compare to contextualized models, and include ablation studies (e.g., expected likelihood vs KL).
- Additional notes: interactive visualization and organized supplementary material are valuable; recommend discussing model limitations like increased computational complexity.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant merit; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses.
