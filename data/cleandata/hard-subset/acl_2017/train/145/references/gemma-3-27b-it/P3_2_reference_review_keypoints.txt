- Proposes Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs) for word embeddings to capture polysemy better than single Gaussians or point embeddings.  
- Introduces an energy-based objective using the expected likelihood kernel and addresses stability and scalability for training.  
- Empirically shows improved performance on word similarity and entailment tasks versus baselines.

- Demonstrates that GMMs disentangle multiple meanings of polysemous words through qualitative and quantitative analysis.  
- Provides a scalable, numerically stable implementation capable of training on very large corpora.  
- Offers thorough evaluation against strong baselines (word2vec, Vilnis & McCallum, etc.) and multiple benchmarks.

- Clearly motivates the problem and the choice of GMMs for polysemy.  
- Uses a well-justified expected likelihood kernel with clear mathematical derivation.  
- Presents convincing experimental results and useful visualizations and ablations.  
- Focuses on practical implementation details (optimization, stability, initialization) for reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written and the related work is comprehensive.

- Core components (GMMs, expected likelihood kernel, energy-based learning) are not individually novel; novelty is in their application/integration.  
- Limited exploration of the number of mixture components K; experiments primarily use K=2.  
- Entailment task description is brief and lacks analysis of why GMMs improve entailment.  
- Risk of overfitting due to increased parameters is not fully addressed beyond covariance regularization.

- What is the computational cost of training versus simpler methods as K increases?  
- Were different covariance initialization schemes tried and what were the outcomes?  
- What specific error types does GMM reduce on entailment, and where does it still fail?  
- How sensitive is performance to the margin ‘m’ in the max-margin objective; was it tuned?  
- Do learned mixture weights tend to be uniform, or are some components consistently dominant?

- Interactive visualization link is a valuable addition.  
- Supplementary material is detailed and helpful.  
- Suggest adding a limitations discussion and future work directions.  
- Recommend condensing the related work to the most relevant papers.

- Overall: strong submission with convincing results and practical implementation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing listed weaknesses.
