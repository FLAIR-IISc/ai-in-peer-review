- Proposal: Introduces w2gm, representing words as mixtures of Gaussians to capture polysemy and uncertainty, trained with an expected likelihood kernel objective and showing improved task performance.
- Contribution: Principled multi-sense embedding formulation using Gaussian mixtures.
- Contribution: Use of the expected likelihood kernel for analytical tractability and scalable training.
- Contribution: Empirical validation demonstrating improvements over baseline methods on similarity and entailment tasks.
- Strength: Clear motivation and problem definition highlighting limits of single-point embeddings.
- Strength: Technically sound and novel combination of Gaussian mixtures with the expected likelihood kernel.
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments and useful ablation studies comparing to established methods.
- Strength: Qualitative evidence (nearest-neighbor analyses) showing effective sense separation.
- Strength: Demonstrated scalability to large corpora.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., number of components, margin).
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to recent contextualized embeddings (BERT/ELMo) and limited discussion of this gap.
- Weakness: Implementation complexity and potential reproducibility concerns without detailed release/documentation.
- Weakness: Over-reliance on Spearman correlation; broader metrics or qualitative error analysis would strengthen evaluation.
- Questions: Sensitivity to number of Gaussian components K and factors affecting its optimal choice.
- Questions: Computational cost, memory requirements, and training time compared to simpler methods like word2vec.
- Questions: Impact of negative sampling strategy and whether alternatives were tested.
- Questions: How w2gm could complement or integrate with contextualized models (BERT/ELMo).
- Additional: Supplementary material is helpful; paper is well-written with comprehensive references.
- Overall: Strong, well-motivated paper with solid results; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions addressing the questions.
