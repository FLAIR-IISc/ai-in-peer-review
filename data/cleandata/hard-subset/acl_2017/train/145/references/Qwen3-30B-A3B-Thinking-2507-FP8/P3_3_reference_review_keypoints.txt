- Paper proposes representing words as Gaussian mixture models (GMMs) to better capture polysemy, trained with a max-margin objective using an expected likelihood kernel, and evaluated on similarity and entailment tasks.

- Main contributions: demonstration that multimodal embeddings capture polysemy, an analytically tractable GMM training procedure using expected likelihood kernels, and empirical improvements on standard semantic benchmarks.

- Strength: Strong qualitative evidence that GMM components capture distinct word senses (e.g., "rock" as stone vs. music).

- Strength: Practical implementation details (diagonal covariances, log-parameters, Adagrad, subsampling) support scalability claims.

- Strength: Consistent performance gains over baselines (word2vec, single-Gaussian) across multiple word similarity and entailment datasets.

- Weakness: Weak theoretical justification for using the expected likelihood kernel—no argument that it best models semantic similarity versus alternatives.

- Weakness: Limited exploration of number of mixture components K (mainly K=2), lacking systematic ablation to justify K choices.

- Weakness: Novelty claim overprior multi-prototype methods is overstated—prior deterministic multi-prototype work exists and needs clearer distinction.

- Weakness: Insufficient computational analysis—no concrete training time, memory, or scalability metrics to back large-corpus claims.

- Weakness: Incomplete comparison with more recent multi-prototype methods (e.g., Chen et al., Nalisnick & Ravi) to fully establish superiority.

- Question: Can you provide quantitative evidence that single-Gaussian means are pulled in opposing directions for polysemous words?

- Question: How many words in the corpus actually require more than two mixture components, and how does K affect generalization?

- Question: How much expressiveness is lost by using diagonal covariances instead of full covariance matrices?

- Overall assessment: Well-executed paper with convincing empirical and qualitative results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing theoretical rationale for the energy function and exploration of K.
