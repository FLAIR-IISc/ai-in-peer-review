- Proposes word representations as Gaussian mixtures (w2gm) trained with a max-margin, expected-likelihood energy that is closed-form for mixtures.  
- Trains at scale on UKWAC+Wikipedia and evaluates on intrinsic benchmarks (similarity, SCWS-like polysemy, lexical entailment).  
- Main contribution: a scalable probabilistic multi-prototype embedding model combining mixture Gaussians with an analytic expected-likelihood energy.  
- Main contribution: a practical, stable training recipe (diagonal/spherical covariances, log-params, log-sum-exp stabilization, subsampling/negative sampling, separate input/output).  
- Main contribution: intrinsic evaluations showing competitive or improved performance vs. skip-gram and unimodal Gaussian baselines, plus a heuristic entailment improvement.  
- Strength: Conceptual advance—natural combination of multi-sense and probabilistic embeddings with a tractable energy avoiding intractable mixture KL.  
- Strength: Solid engineering choices make the approach reproducible and plausibly scalable (K=2 feasible, K^2 pairwise terms manageable).  
- Strength: Qualitative nearest-neighbor results convincingly show sense separation and reduced per-sense variance.  
- Strength: Competitive intrinsic results across multiple similarity datasets and improved asymmetry-aware entailment via componentwise KL heuristic.  
- Strength: Many hyperparameters and implementation details disclosed and code release planned.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—combines existing multi-prototype and probabilistic ideas; clearer positioning against EM-style multi-sense and other probabilistic variants needed.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is mostly intrinsic; lacks downstream/extrinsic tasks and controlled comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., GloVe, multi-sense skip-gram) at comparable dimensionality.  
- Weakness: Metric selection appears inconsistent—multiple similarity metrics reported and best-per-dataset highlighted, risking unfair comparison and metric overfitting.  
- Weakness: Entailment solution (min pairwise Gaussian KL) is an ad hoc heuristic; alternative asymmetric measures or bounds are not explored.  
- Weakness: Expressivity limited in practice—experiments use spherical/diagonal covariances and no context-conditioned sense disambiguation at test time.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues (messy references, inconsistent table numbering) and anonymity risk via public visualization link.  
- Question: Commit to a single, pre-specified similarity metric or provide a development protocol and report statistical significance?  
- Question: How does performance scale with dimensionality (D=100/300) and number of components K (ablation K=1,2,3); do mixture weights indicate broad component usage?  
- Question: Which covariance structure (spherical vs. diagonal) was used in main results and how sensitive are results to this choice?  
- Question: Provide exact entailment KL computation details (directionality, min over i,j, weighting) and compare to reverse KL or other asymmetric alternatives.  
- Question: Provide controlled comparisons to strong baselines trained on the same data/dimensions and any extrinsic evaluations showing utility of uncertainty.  
- Question: Remove or anonymize the interactive visualization link to preserve review anonymity.
