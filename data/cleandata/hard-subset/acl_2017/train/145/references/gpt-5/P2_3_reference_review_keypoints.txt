- Proposal: Represent words as mixtures of Gaussians and train with a margin-based objective using an analytic expected likelihood kernel (ELK) as the energy.
- Goal: Capture polysemy, model uncertainty, and enable asymmetric measures (e.g., KL) for lexical entailment.
- Practical contribution: Scalable, closed-form mixture formulation that generalizes unimodal Gaussian embeddings with tractable ELK computations.
- Training recipe: Empirically validated tricks (diagonal/spherical covariances, log-parameterization, separate input/output embeddings, Adagrad, numerical stabilization) for corpus-scale training.
- Interpretability: Per-component nearest neighbors demonstrate separated senses (e.g., rock, bank, cell), showing the model uses mixture capacity.
- Entailment: Using minimal KL for asymmetric scoring yields consistent AP/F1 gains over unimodal Gaussians on lexical entailment.
- Evaluation: Quantitative improvements over unimodal Gaussians (and often skip-gram) across several similarity datasets and entailment, with analyses of scoring metrics (ELK, cosine, Euclidean).
- Methodological care: Paper addresses known pathologies (variance inflation, numerical issues) and provides detailed implementation and stability guidance.
- Scoring analysis: Reports multiple similarity measures for mixtures and notes which correlate best with each dataset, acknowledging optimization-target mismatch.
- Novelty concern: Approach is an incremental extension of prior multi-prototype and probabilistic embeddings rather than a radical new method.
- Baseline fairness: Comparisons use reported numbers and low dimensionality (D=50); lacks matched 300d baselines (word2vec/GloVe/fastText) and statistical significance tests.
- Context handling: Contextual disambiguation at test time is not explicitly modeled; SCWS/context-sensitive metrics and comparisons to context-aware baselines are missing or unclear.
- Mixture analysis gap: Limited study of K, per-word adaptivity, component collapse/duplication, mixture weight distributions, and effect of diagonal/spherical covariance constraint.
- Reproducibility/clarity issues: Some tables/references messy, missing SCWS results, demo link may break anonymity, and code is promised only upon publication.
- Efficiency questions: Claims of training on billions of words lack detailed wall-clock, memory, and throughput comparisons to skip-gram and scalability analysis to higher dimensions/GPU.
- Requested clarifications: Report full SCWS results with MaxSimC/AveSimC and context-selection method; provide matched 300d baselines and multi-sense comparisons; analyze component collapse, weight distributions, and sensitivity to K; supply detailed efficiency metrics.
- Overall: Technically sound, well-written, and practically useful for multi-sense probabilistic embeddings, but moderately novel and needs stronger, fairer baselines and clearer context-sensitive evaluation to fully substantiate claims.
