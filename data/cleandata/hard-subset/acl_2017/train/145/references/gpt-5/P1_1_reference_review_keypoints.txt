- Proposes probabilistic multi-sense word embeddings (w2gm) representing each word as a Gaussian mixture trained with a skip-gram-style max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel.
- Contribution: tractable probabilistic multi-sense modeling with interpretable sense-specific components and uncertainty.
- Contribution: practical training recipe (max-margin ranking, analytic GMM energy, diagonal/spherical covariances, numerical stabilizations) enabling large-scale training.
- Contribution: empirical gains on multiple word similarity benchmarks and lexical entailment, plus qualitative polysemy demonstrations.
- Strength: conceptually clean extension from single Gaussian to Gaussian mixtures while retaining closed-form similarity via the expected likelihood kernel.
- Strength: well-documented, reproducible training details and engineering choices that improve robustness.
- Strength: convincing qualitative nearest-neighbor analyses showing distinct sense components for polysemous words.
- Strength: broad evaluation with consistent improvements and useful observations about metric choice per dataset.
- Strength: computationally manageable with K=2 and simple covariance parameterizations; trained on large corpora.
- Weakness: limited novelty relative to prior multi-prototype/multi-sense work and missing direct comparisons to strong baselines.
- Weakness: fairness concerns in evaluation—smaller embedding dimensionality, differing hyperparameters, borrowed baseline numbers, and no significance testing.
- Weakness: fixed global K (mostly 2) with no mechanism to adapt senses per word or analyze mixture weight usage and collapse frequency.
- Weakness: unclear contribution of covariances versus multiple means; no ablation against deterministic multi-vector baselines.
- Weakness: train/test objective mismatch—trained with expected likelihood kernel but evaluated with KL for entailment without exploring aligned objectives.
- Requests: include strong deterministic multi-sense baselines trained on same corpora and with 200–300 dims and standard negatives.
- Requests: report mixture weight entropy distribution, frequency of component collapse, and SCWS component selection statistics.
- Requests: run ablations fixing covariances, replacing mixtures with deterministic vectors (MaxSim), and comparing energy choices (expected likelihood vs dot product).
- Requests: evaluate K>2 systematically, propose adaptive/pruning criteria for K, and report training time, memory, and throughput scaling with K.
- Requests: clarify explicit regularization for variances, report any pathologies, ensure anonymity of visualization links, and commit to releasing code/models for reproducibility.
