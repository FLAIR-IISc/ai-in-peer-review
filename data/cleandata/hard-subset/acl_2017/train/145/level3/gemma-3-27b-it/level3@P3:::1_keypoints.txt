- Introduces multimodal Gaussian-mixture word distributions to model polysemy and uncertainty.
- Proposes an energy-based max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel for analytic learning of mixtures.
- Empirically demonstrates w2gm outperforms baselines on word similarity and entailment tasks.

- Paper is clear, well-structured, and uses figures/tables effectively.
- Novel advancement over unimodal Gaussian and point embeddings for handling multiple senses.
- Strong empirical and qualitative results showing capture of different word senses.
- Technically sound derivations and implementation details, with justified optimization choices.
- Thorough evaluation including multiple datasets and ablation studies on mixture components.

- Limited comparison to related mixture-model approaches, especially Tian et al. (2014).
- Missing citations to relevant multi-sense/contextual embedding work (Neelakantan et al. 2014; Li & Jurafsky 2015; Liu et al. 2015).
- Unexplained performance drop of w2gm vs w2g on the SWCS analysis.

- Why does w2gm lose performance compared to w2g on SWCS; what factors explain this?
- What are the key methodological differences from Tian et al. (2014) in objectives and mixture assumptions?

- Expand related work and explicitly compare to Tian et al.; add missing citations.
- Include comparative results from Tian et al., Neelakantan et al., and Liu et al. in main result tables.
- Investigate and discuss causes and remedies for the SWCS performance decline.
