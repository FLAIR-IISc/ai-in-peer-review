- Contribution: Proposes Gaussian mixture word embeddings to model multiple meanings per word.  
- Contribution: Uses an energy-based max-margin objective with the expected likelihood kernel, yielding a closed-form, efficient training signal.  
- Contribution: Empirical results show improved word similarity/entailment performance and qualitative evidence that components capture distinct senses.

- Strength: Clear motivation that unimodal embeddings fail to capture polysemy without undue uncertainty.  
- Strength: Sound theoretical basis; expected likelihood kernel gives a mathematically elegant and computationally efficient similarity measure.  
- Strength: Strong qualitative results and visualization showing distinct semantic components for polysemous words.  
- Strength: Well-written with sufficient implementation and hyperparameter details for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Choice of K=2 components is arbitrary and unjustified; no criteria or analysis for optimal K or effects of larger K.  
- Weakness: Use of spherical covariances is a pragmatic simplification but its impact on capturing semantics is not analyzed.  
- Weakness: No analysis of behavior on largely monosemous words (whether components collapse or remain redundant).  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to a few similarity/entailment datasets; broader tasks or deeper analysis of error sources are missing.

- Question: Why was K fixed to 2, and how does performance change with larger K?  
- Question: How does the model represent clearly monosemous words—collapse to one component or retain unnecessary components?  
- Question: Were spherical covariances chosen purely for efficiency, and would diagonal/anisotropic covariances improve expressiveness without large cost?
