- Introduces w2gm: a Gaussian mixture model for word embeddings that captures multiple meanings and uncertainty.  
- Extends unimodal Gaussian embeddings (Vilnis & McCallum 2014) to address polysemy.  
- Contribution: proposes multimodal word distributions via Gaussian mixtures.  
- Contribution: introduces an energy-based max-margin objective to learn mixture parameters.  
- Contribution: uses the expected likelihood kernel to measure similarity between Gaussian mixtures (closed-form).  
- Strength: effectively handles polysemy, improving over unimodal Gaussian models.  
- Strength: outperforms baselines (word2vec, Gaussian embeddings) on several word similarity datasets.  
- Strength: more interpretable representations enabling identification of distinct meanings and uncertainty.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparison to other multimodal embedding approaches (e.g., Tian et al. 2014).  
- Weakness: missing citations to related work (Neelakantan et al. 2014; Li & Jurafsky 2015; Liu et al. 2015).  
- Weakness: shows a loss of performance on the SWCS dataset compared to w2g that needs investigation.  
- Reviewer questions: how authors will compare to similar approaches (e.g., Tian et al. 2014), explain the SWCS performance loss, and incorporate missing citations.
