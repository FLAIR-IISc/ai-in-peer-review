- Proposes a self-learning framework to learn bilingual embedding mappings from very limited bilingual evidence (as few as 25 word pairs or numerals).
- Method extends existing mapping techniques and can be combined with any dictionary-based mapping approach.
- Evaluated on bilingual lexicon induction and cross-lingual word similarity, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art systems using richer resources.
- Contribution: simple self-learning framework that produces high-quality bilingual embeddings from limited seeds.
- Contribution: works with very small or automatically generated seed dictionaries, enabling learning without real bilingual data.
- Contribution: competitive empirical performance on key cross-lingual tasks.
- Strength: efficient and modular—compatible with various embedding mapping and dictionary induction techniques.
- Strength: detailed analysis showing the method implicitly optimizes a meaningful objective.
- Weakness: no discussion of how the number of Gaussian components (k) was selected or its impact on performance.
- Weakness: use of spherical covariance matrices may be suboptimal; other covariance choices not explored.
- Weakness: minor issues in the manuscript (missing reference to Table 4, incomplete Luong et al. citation).
- Question: how was k selected and how does it affect performance?
- Question: have you considered using general diagonal covariance matrices instead of spherical ones?
- Question: please provide more details on the optimization objective and its relation to the self-learning framework.
