- Paper proposes w2gm: word embeddings as Gaussian mixtures to model polysemy and uncertainty, trained with a max-margin objective and expected likelihood kernel.
- Key contribution: multimodal Gaussian mixtures enabling expressive representations of multiple word senses.
- Key contribution: efficient, scalable max-margin learning objective using the expected likelihood kernel for analytic tractability.
- Key contribution: empirical validation showing superior performance on word similarity and entailment benchmarks versus baselines.
- Strength: Novel and expressive approach that captures polysemy and reduces variance for polysemous words, supported qualitatively and quantitatively.
- Strength: Scalable training suitable for large corpora, aided by diagonal or spherical covariance choices.
- Strength: Strong empirical results on datasets like WordSim-353 and SCWS, and compelling entailment performance.
- Strength: Clear writing, solid methodological derivations, and useful interactive visualization tool.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of how the number of mixture components K affects performance; K=2 is under-justified.
- Weakness: Use of spherical covariances may limit modeling of complex uncertainty; trade-offs with diagonal covariances are unexplored.
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues (unreferenced table, missing citation year).
- Question: How does varying K impact performance, and would larger K better capture words with >2 senses?
- Question: Have diagonal covariances been considered, and what are their effects on performance and efficiency?
- Question: Can external lexical resources (e.g., WordNet) be integrated to further improve entailment and related tasks?
- Recommendation: Overall a significant contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
