- Paper proposes representing words as Gaussian mixture distributions to capture multiple senses, entailment, and uncertainty, trained with an energy-based max-margin objective that outperforms alternatives on benchmark datasets.
- Contribution: Introduces multimodal (Gaussian mixture) word distributions to model multiple meanings and uncertainty.
- Contribution: Proposes an energy-based max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel to learn mixture parameters.
- Contribution: Reports improved performance over baseline methods on word similarity and entailment benchmarks.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well-structured with a concise explanation of the approach.
- Strength: Strong experimental results demonstrating effectiveness on several benchmarks.
- Strength: Novel approach addressing limitations of single-vector and unimodal embeddings.
- Weakness: Limited empirical comparison to closely related prior work (e.g., Tian et al., 2014).
- Weakness: No clear explanation for the performance drop versus Gaussian embeddings on the SWCS analysis.
- Question: Why does the Gaussian mixture model underperform the Gaussian embedding model on the SWCS analysis?
- Question: How exactly does the proposed approach address limitations of prior mixture-based methods like Tian et al. (2014)?
- Additional comment: Recommend adding a brief comparison to Tian et al. (2014) and including prior approach results in Tables 3 and 4 for clearer evaluation.
