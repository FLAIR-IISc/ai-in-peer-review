- Summary: Proposes Gaussian mixture word embeddings (multimodal distributions) with an energy-based max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel to model polysemy and outperform baselines on similarity and entailment tasks.
- Contribution 1: Introduces multimodal word distributions via Gaussian mixtures to represent multiple word meanings.
- Contribution 2: Proposes an energy-based max-margin learning objective for the mixture parameters.
- Contribution 3: Uses the expected likelihood kernel as the energy function to measure similarity between word distributions.
- Strength: Novel approach that can capture multiple word senses.
- Strength: Empirically outperforms word2vec skip-grams and unimodal Gaussian embeddings on benchmarks.
- Strength: Clear and well-structured presentation.
- Weakness: Comparisons to related approaches are limited and could be extended.
- Weakness: Insufficient implementation and training details provided.
