- Proposes w2gm: represent words as Gaussian mixture distributions to capture multiple senses, entailment, and uncertainty.
- Introduces an energy-based max-margin objective with an expected likelihood kernel to learn mixture parameters.
- Empirically outperforms word2vec and single-Gaussian embeddings on word similarity and entailment benchmarks.
- Key contributions: multimodal word distributions, novel energy-based training objective, and extensive evaluation.
- Strengths: clear writing and structure, thorough related work review, well-motivated novel model, convincing experiments.
- Weaknesses: lacks deeper comparison to related mixture-based work (e.g., Tian et al. 2014) and missing prior-results in Tables 3–4.
- Open question: why does w2gm lose performance versus w2g on the SCWS analysis and how can this be remedied?
- Additional note: authors’ response satisfied the reviewer; paper is recommended for publication.
