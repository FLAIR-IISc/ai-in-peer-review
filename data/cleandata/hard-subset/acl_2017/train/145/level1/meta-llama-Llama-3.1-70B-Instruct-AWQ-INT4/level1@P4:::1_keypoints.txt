- Proposes w2gm: representing words as Gaussian mixture distributions to capture multiple meanings and uncertainty.
- Trains w2gm with an energy-based max-margin objective using an expected likelihood kernel as the energy function.
- Claims w2gm outperforms word2vec and single-Gaussian embeddings on word similarity and entailment tasks.
- Contribution: introduces multimodal word embeddings via Gaussian mixtures for richer, uncertainty-aware representations.
- Contribution: develops an energy-based max-margin learning objective with an expected likelihood kernel.
- Contribution: demonstrates improved empirical performance on several similarity and entailment benchmarks.
- Strength: provides more expressive, flexible word representations that model polysemy and uncertainty.
- Strength: shows superior handling of polysemous words compared to existing methods.
- Strength: reports scalability to large datasets, indicating practical trainability.
- Weakness: expected likelihood kernel can be computationally expensive to evaluate.
- Weakness: lacks thorough analysis and ablation of hyperparameters affecting performance.
- Weakness: missing comprehensive comparisons to other multimodal embedding methods.
- Questions: request detailed hyperparameter analysis (number of components, embedding dimensionality, learning rate).
- Questions: ask for comparisons to other multimodal methods (e.g., Huang et al., 2012).
- Questions: seek more insight into interpretability of mixture components and how they map to distinct word meanings.
