**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes a multimodal word distribution model formed from Gaussian mixtures to capture multiple word meanings, entailment, and uncertainty information. The authors introduce an energy-based max-margin objective to learn these distributions. The main contributions of this work are:

1. A probabilistic word embedding model that can capture multiple meanings using Gaussian mixtures.
2. An energy-based max-margin objective for learning the parameters of the Gaussian mixture model.
3. An expected likelihood kernel energy function that can be evaluated in closed form for Gaussian mixtures.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Expressive word representations**: The proposed Gaussian mixture model can capture multiple word meanings and uncertainty information, which is not possible with traditional point-based word embeddings.
2. **Improved performance on word similarity tasks**: The authors demonstrate that their model outperforms alternative methods, such as word2vec and Gaussian embeddings, on several word similarity datasets.
3. **Effective energy function**: The proposed expected likelihood kernel energy function is a natural choice for similarity measure between distributions and can be evaluated in closed form for Gaussian mixtures.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Computational complexity**: The proposed model requires computing the expected likelihood kernel energy function, which can be computationally expensive for large vocabularies.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process, which can be crucial for the performance of the model.
3. **Limited evaluation on entailment tasks**: The authors only evaluate their model on a single entailment dataset, which may not be representative of the model's performance on other entailment tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the computational complexity of the proposed model for large-scale datasets?
2. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and how it affects the performance of the model?
3. How do you plan to extend the evaluation of the model to other entailment tasks and datasets?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed model and its components. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of the model on several word similarity datasets. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity and hyperparameter tuning process. Additionally, the authors may want to consider extending the evaluation of the model to other entailment tasks and datasets to further demonstrate its effectiveness.
