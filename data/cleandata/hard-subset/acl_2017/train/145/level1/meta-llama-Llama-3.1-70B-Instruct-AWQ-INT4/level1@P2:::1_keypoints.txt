- Probabilistic multimodal word embeddings using Gaussian mixtures to capture multiple meanings.
- Energy-based max-margin objective for learning Gaussian mixture parameters.
- Expected likelihood kernel energy function that has a closed-form for Gaussian mixtures.
- Model captures multiple word meanings and uncertainty beyond point embeddings.
- Outperforms word2vec and Gaussian embeddings on multiple word similarity datasets.
- Uses a natural, closed-form energy function for measuring distributional similarity.
- Computing the expected likelihood kernel can be computationally expensive for large vocabularies.
- Lacks thorough analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance.
- Evaluation on entailment is limited to a single dataset and may not generalize.
- How will the authors address computational complexity for large-scale datasets?
- Request for more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and its effects.
- Ask for plans to extend evaluation to additional entailment tasks and datasets.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the model and experiments.
- Would benefit from deeper computational-complexity analysis and broader entailment evaluation.
