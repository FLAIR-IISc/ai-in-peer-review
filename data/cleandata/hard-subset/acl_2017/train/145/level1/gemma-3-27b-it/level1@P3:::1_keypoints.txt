- Proposes Gaussian mixture word embeddings to model polysemy and uncertainty beyond single Gaussian or point embeddings.
- Uses an expected likelihood kernel within an energy-based max-margin objective to enable analytic, scalable training.
- Shows empirical improvements on word similarity and entailment tasks compared to baselines (word2vec, single Gaussian).

- Clear motivation: addresses limitations of existing embeddings in capturing multiple senses and uncertainty.
- Kernel choice is technically novel and allows closed-form computations for efficiency.
- Evaluation is comprehensive across multiple datasets and includes helpful qualitative nearest-neighbor examples.
- Authors address practical issues (numerical stability, initialization) and provide training/implementation details for reproducibility.
- Reported performance gains are statistically significant, especially versus single-Gaussian baselines.

- The core idea of multi-prototype embeddings is not entirely novel; novelty mainly in energy function and learning framework.
- Performance is sensitive to the number of mixture components K, with experiments focused mainly on K=2 and limited analysis for K=3.
- No ablation study to isolate contributions of the expected likelihood kernel, max-margin objective, or other components.
- Entailment evaluation relies on a relatively small dataset (Baroni et al., 2012), limiting generality of claims.
- Lacks detailed error analysis to identify failure modes and guide future improvements.

- How sensitive are results to initialization of covariance matrices (Σw,i)?
- How was the margin m chosen in the max-margin objective and was hyperparameter search performed?
- Why does the expected likelihood kernel outperform cosine on some datasets but not others?
- Were different subsampling and negative sampling parameters tested, and how do they affect performance?
- Can the paper include screenshots or examples from the visualization website to illustrate learned representations?

- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Related work coverage is comprehensive.
- Supplementary material would be useful for more implementation/experimental details.
- Further work needed on hyperparameter sensitivity and detailed error analysis to strengthen the paper.
