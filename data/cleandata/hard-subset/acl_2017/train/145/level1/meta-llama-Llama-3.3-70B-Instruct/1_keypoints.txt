- Introduces Gaussian mixture models to represent multimodal word distributions capturing multiple senses.
- Proposes an energy-based max-margin objective to learn parameters of the Gaussian mixture word embeddings.
- Derives a closed-form expected likelihood kernel used as the energy function in the objective.
- Improves representation of polysemous words by modeling multiple meanings.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on several word similarity benchmarks.
- Leverages uncertainty information from GMMs to improve word entailment performance.
- Training may be computationally expensive, especially on large datasets.
- Method relies on several hyperparameters (num components, margin, learning rate) that need careful tuning.
- Interpretability is limited, particularly for words with multiple related meanings.
