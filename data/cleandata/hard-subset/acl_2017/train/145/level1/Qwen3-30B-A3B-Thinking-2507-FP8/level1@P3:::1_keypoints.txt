- Proposes multimodal word embeddings using Gaussian mixture models (GMMs) to represent multiple word senses versus unimodal Gaussians or point embeddings.  
- Claims three contributions: multimodal representation, an energy-based max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel, and improved performance on similarity and entailment tasks.  
- Reviewer views the main contribution as empirical demonstration that multimodal representations capture polysemy; energy function is more of an implementation detail; variance reduction is a useful technical point.  
- Strength: Qualitative results show GMMs clearly separate different word senses where unimodal models fail.  
- Strength: Strong quantitative gains on multiple word similarity datasets and entailment, notably top results on 7/11 datasets and large improvement on SCWS.  
- Strength: Effectively addresses the variance/diffuseness problem of prior Gaussian embeddings, improving downstream performance.  
- Weakness: Novelty claim overstated — prior multi-prototype and probabilistic embedding work exists, so contribution is incremental.  
- Weakness: Incomplete empirical comparison — omission of GloVe (a dominant baseline) weakens claims of superiority.  
- Weakness: Scalability claims are vague — no concrete training-time numbers and use of diagonal covariances trades representational power for efficiency without sufficient analysis.  
- Question: How does w2gm training time compare to skip-gram word2vec on the same 3.5B-token corpus — what are actual training times?  
- Question: How much information/quality is lost using diagonal versus full covariance matrices, and how does that impact downstream tasks?  
- Question: Why choose the expected likelihood kernel as the primary energy when maximum cosine between component means outperforms it in evaluations?  
- Overall: Valuable, well-supported demonstration that multimodal embeddings better capture polysemy; however novelty is incremental and empirical positioning needs improvement — recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
