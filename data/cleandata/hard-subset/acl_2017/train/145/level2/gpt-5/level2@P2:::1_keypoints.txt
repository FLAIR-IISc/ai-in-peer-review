- Proposes word2gm: representing words as Gaussian mixtures to model polysemy, uncertainty, and entailment with an expected-likelihood energy and max-margin objective.
- Trains a scalable, analytically tractable multimodal probabilistic embedding with practical stability and scalability heuristics.
- Shows qualitative sense separation and improved nearest-neighbor sense distinctions (e.g., bank: river vs financial).
- Reports quantitative improvements over unimodal Gaussian and skip-gram baselines on several word similarity benchmarks and lexical entailment.
- Uses asymmetric KL for directional entailment and argues mixtures reduce variance for polysemous words aiding inclusion behavior.
- Provides many implementation details (covariance choices, log-sum-exp stabilization, initialization, subsampling, negative sampling) to aid reproducibility.
- Missing comparisons to important multi-sense baselines (e.g., Neelakantan et al. 2014) and stronger entailment baselines (order embeddings).
- Evaluation may be unfair: low embedding dimensionality (50) compared to common 200–300 for word2vec and uncontrolled differences from prior unimodal Gaussian baselines.
- Mixed and dataset-specific results; no analysis of when mixtures help (by frequency, polysemy degree, POS), and no analogy task results.
- Polysemy evaluation is largely qualitative; lacks context-aware disambiguation metrics (e.g., SCWS with posterior over components).
- Similarity scoring via max over component pairs can cherry-pick senses; lacks principled posterior-weighted or context-conditioned evaluations.
- Entailment scoring using min KL can falsely signal entailment via a single narrow sense; mixture-weighted or coverage-based criteria might be more appropriate.
- Experiments use spherical covariances despite claiming rich anisotropic uncertainty; no analysis of covariance spectra or tail behavior.
- Scalability claims lack concrete throughput, memory, vocabulary, and wall-clock metrics to substantiate “billions of words in days”.
- Contains numerical errors (misstated exponentials) that undermine quantitative care and need correction.
- Requests comparisons to non-parametric multi-sense and order/partial-order embeddings and higher-dimension word2vec baselines.
- Asks for context-aware SCWS results (MaxSim, AvgSimC) by inferring posterior over mixture components given context.
- Wants ablations for K, dimensionality, covariance type (spherical vs diagonal), and components K=1/2/3/4 to assess sensitivity and anisotropy claims.
- Suggests ablation separating effects of mixtures, energy function, and training objective (e.g., multi-prototype point embeddings with same loss).
- Requests experiments with mixture-weighted or worst-case KL criteria and sensitivity analysis of AP/F1 to component selection rules.
- Questions component collapse/identifiability, correlation of mixture weights with sense frequency, and whether regularizers encourage diversity.
- Asks for concrete scalability metrics: tokens/sec, memory usage, vocab size vs K and D, and scaling with number of negatives.
- Overall: well-motivated, tractable extension with promising results and solid engineering, but needs broader baselines, deeper analyses (especially context-aware polysemy), clearer entailment semantics, and numerical corrections.
