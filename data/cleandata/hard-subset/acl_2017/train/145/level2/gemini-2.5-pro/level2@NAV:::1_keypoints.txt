- Proposes a novel probabilistic word embedding where each word is a Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) to model polysemy.  
- Introduces a scalable training procedure using a max-margin objective with an expected likelihood kernel as the energy function.  
- Demonstrates strong qualitative and quantitative results, outperforming word2vec and single-Gaussian embeddings on similarity and entailment tasks.  

- High novelty and strong motivation: addresses unimodal embeddings' inability to capture multiple senses with an intuitive multi-prototype solution.  
- Technically sound and elegant: the expected likelihood kernel yields a tractable, closed-form energy that enables practical GMM optimization.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: qualitative examples show distinct senses per mixture component and quantitative benchmarks show consistent improvements.  

- Hyperparameter K (number of mixtures) is underexplored; using a fixed K for all words is likely suboptimal and lacks ablation or adaptive selection.  
- Missing analysis of computational cost and training time comparison versus baselines, despite claims of scalability.  
- Potential mismatch between training objective (expected likelihood) and evaluation metric (cosine similarity often best) is not analyzed or explained.
