- Paper proposes multimodal word embeddings using Gaussian mixture distributions to capture multiple senses, entailment, and uncertainty.  
- Learning uses an energy-based max-margin objective with an expected likelihood kernel as the energy function.  
- Model evaluated on word similarity and entailment benchmarks and reported to outperform existing methods.  
- Contribution: multimodal Gaussian-mixture embeddings enabling multiple distinct meanings and uncertainty modeling.  
- Contribution: energy-based max-margin training objective leveraging an expected likelihood kernel.  
- Contribution: achieves state-of-the-art results on several benchmark tasks.  
- Strength: more expressive representations that can model multiple senses and uncertainty.  
- Strength: improved empirical performance over prior methods on similarity and entailment.  
- Strength: proposed training procedure is scalable to very large corpora.  
- Weakness: computing the expected likelihood kernel can be computationally expensive.  
- Weakness: model requires tuning several hyperparameters, increasing development cost.  
- Weakness: multimodal embeddings can be hard to interpret semantically.  
- Question: how will computational complexity be addressed for large-scale datasets?  
- Question: can the authors detail the hyperparameter tuning strategy and selection criteria?  
- Question: what steps can improve the interpretability of the multimodal embeddings?
