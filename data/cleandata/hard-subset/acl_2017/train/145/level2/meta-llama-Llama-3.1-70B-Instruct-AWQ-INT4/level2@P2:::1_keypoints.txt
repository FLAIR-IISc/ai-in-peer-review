- Proposes Gaussian mixture models for word representations to form expressive multimodal distributions.
- Introduces an energy-based max-margin objective to learn GMM parameters and capture affinity between word distributions.
- Presents an expected likelihood kernel energy function that can be evaluated in closed form for the chosen probabilistic embedding.

- Provides a clear, well-motivated introduction and problem framing.
- Gaussian mixture model can capture multiple word senses and entailment, improving over prior embeddings.
- Thorough evaluation on benchmark tasks (word similarity and entailment).
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.

- Lacks thorough comparison with other multimodal embedding methods (e.g., Huang et al., 2012).
- Unclear how the number of Gaussian components (K) is chosen and how it affects performance.
- No detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale applications.
- Insufficient explanation of how the expected likelihood kernel relates to other energy functions (e.g., KL divergence).

- How is the number of Gaussian components (K) chosen per word and how does it impact results?
- Please provide a detailed computational complexity analysis and comparisons to other embedding methods.
- How does the model handle out-of-vocabulary words and can performance on them be improved?
- Clarify the relationship between the expected likelihood kernel and other energy functions such as KL divergence.

- Overall: strong, well-written contribution that needs more comparative analysis and clearer rationale for choosing K.
