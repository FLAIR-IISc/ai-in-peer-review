- Introduces modeling words as Gaussian mixtures to capture polysemy and uncertainty beyond point embeddings.  
- Proposes an energy-based max-margin objective using an expected likelihood kernel for learning multimodal word distributions.  
- Demonstrates improved performance on word similarity and entailment benchmarks compared to word2vec and single-Gaussian embeddings.  
- Primary contribution: Gaussian mixture word embeddings and the associated learning framework.  
- Supporting contribution: empirical gains on standard datasets plus qualitative examples and an interactive visualization.  
- Strength: clear motivation for multimodal distributions and analytic tractability via the expected likelihood kernel.  
- Strength: strong empirical results and helpful implementation details on optimization and numerical stability.  
- Strength: useful comparisons against word2vec and prior Gaussian embedding work.  
- Weakness: performance improvements are statistically significant but relatively modest.  
- Weakness: lacks extensive ablation studies (e.g., number of components K, kernel choices) to isolate design impacts.  
- Weakness: related work discussion should better address recent contextualized embeddings (BERT, ELMo).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational cost versus simpler embeddings and limited exploration of other applications of learned distributions.  
- Questions for authors: how performance scales with vocabulary and corpus size; provide detailed ablations varying K and computational impact; compare to contextualized embedding methods.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel contribution with promising results that warrant acceptance pending more thorough ablations and clarifications.
