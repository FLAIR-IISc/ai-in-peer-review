- Proposes multimodal Gaussian mixture word embeddings to capture multiple senses and uncertainty beyond unimodal representations.
- Introduces an energy-based max-margin objective with the expected likelihood kernel for learning Gaussian mixtures, including practical stability and initialization tips.
- Shows improved performance over word2vec and Gaussian embeddings on word similarity and entailment tasks, with qualitative analyses and a visualization site.

- Novel, theoretically sound extension of probabilistic embeddings using the expected likelihood kernel for analytic tractability.
- Consistently outperforms baselines on evaluated tasks with statistically significant improvements.
- Provides detailed implementation and optimization choices (e.g., Adagrad) that aid reproducibility.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across datasets and similarity metrics, plus insightful qualitative examples.
- Interactive visualization tool helps interpret learned representations.

- Training Gaussian mixtures is computationally expensive and may hinder adoption; more cost analysis versus simpler methods is needed.
- Model performance may be sensitive to the number of components K; paper focuses mainly on K=2 with limited K exploration.
- Limited comparisons to recent contextualized embeddings (e.g., BERT, ELMo) reduce benchmarking completeness.
- KL divergence use for entailment is highlighted but lacks full mathematical detail and stronger justification versus other divergences.

- How does computational cost scale with K and vocabulary size; can you provide detailed complexity analysis?
- What methods were tried for automatically selecting the optimal number of Gaussian components (K)?
- Have you tested different covariance (Σ) initialization strategies and their impact on performance?
- What optimizations are needed to compute the expected likelihood kernel efficiently at large scale?
- Could alternative kernel functions for the energy calculation improve the model, and why?

- Paper is well-written and explains technical details clearly.
- Supplementary material should include more details on the negative sampling procedure.
- Interactive visualization is a valuable addition.
- Consider discussing model limitations and future directions (other mixture models, external knowledge).
