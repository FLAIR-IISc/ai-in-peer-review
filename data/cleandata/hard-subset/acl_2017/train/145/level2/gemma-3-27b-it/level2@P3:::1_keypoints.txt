- Proposes Gaussian mixture word embeddings to capture polysemy and reduce biased distributions from single Gaussian/point embeddings.  
- Introduces a novel energy-based max-margin objective using the expected likelihood kernel for principled, analytically tractable learning.  
- Shows empirical improvements over word2vec and single Gaussian baselines on word similarity and entailment, with qualitative visualizations demonstrating sense separation.  
- Addresses practical training issues (numerical stability, initialization) to enable robust, scalable learning.  
- Lacks comparison to modern contextualized embeddings (e.g., BERT, ELMo) in performance and computational cost.  
- Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly the number of Gaussian components K (mostly uses K=2).  
- The expected likelihood kernel derivation is dense and would benefit from clearer, more intuitive explanation.  
- Missing ablation study to isolate contributions of components (kernel choice, regularization, etc.).  
- Question: How do Gaussian mixture embeddings compare to contextualized embeddings in performance and runtime?  
- Question: What are the training and inference computational costs as K increases?  
- Question: Please provide an intuitive explanation of the expected likelihood kernel and its benefits for semantics and uncertainty.  
- Question: Why does Euclidean distance outperform cosine similarity on the MC and RW datasets?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to the margin parameter m in the max-margin objective?  
- Interactive visualization is a valuable and helpful addition.  
- Supplementary material provides detailed, commendable implementation and optimization details.  
- Writing is generally clear but complex concepts should be broken into more digestible pieces.
