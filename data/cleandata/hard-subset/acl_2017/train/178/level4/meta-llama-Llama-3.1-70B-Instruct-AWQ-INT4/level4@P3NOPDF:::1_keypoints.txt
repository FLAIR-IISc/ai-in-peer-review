- Proposes extending word embeddings to phrases/concepts by assigning fixed identifiers to equivalent phrases/words from an ontology and replacing occurrences in the training corpus (tagged corpus appended to original).

- Application focused on the biomedical domain, which limits the scope and generality of the contribution.

- Technique is natural and well-presented but may not be a substantial methodological contribution.

- Authors created a new test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities, which is a novel and valuable contribution.

- Recommend more discussion and analysis of the generated test resource, as it may be the paper’s most important outcome.

- Technical issue: Equation 8’s MAP formulation is problematic — ranking the entire vocabulary is impractical; a cutoff would be more natural, and the equation does not define a probability and should remove probabilistic language.

- Minor correction: Line 556 should read "most concepts have" instead of "most concepts has."

- Overall assessment: clear and well-structured paper; true value likely lies in the test resource rather than the embedding method.
