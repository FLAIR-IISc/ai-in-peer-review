- Proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manual ontology (tested on UMLS and YAGO).  
- Evaluated against baselines on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks; results comparable to prior work.

Strengths:
- Model is simple and configurable between atomic-phrase and compositional representations, and handles concepts with multiple representative phrases.  
- Substantial effort: model development, creation of a new general-domain similarity/relatedness dataset, and multiple evaluations/analyses.

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation is limited to intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness) and lacks extrinsic downstream evaluations.  
- The datasets appear to record phrase judgments (annotators saw phrases), so they should be treated as phrase-similarity datasets.  
- Medical evaluation datasets are weak: mini MayoSRS is tiny (29 pairs); MayoSRS is small (101 pairs) with low annotator agreement; UMNSRS is limited to single-word concepts—these limitations are not properly acknowledged.  
- Hyperparameters are extensively tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results, calling the reported comparisons into question.  
- Claim of superiority due to less manual annotation is weak because the method still relies on large manual ontologies and prior work used existing clinical records.  
- Lacks insightful analysis separating different compositional relations (phrase↔word vs. concept↔alternative phrases); needs dedicated NLP analysis of these relations.

Recommendation:
- Reviewer recommends rejection and urges improving evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission.
