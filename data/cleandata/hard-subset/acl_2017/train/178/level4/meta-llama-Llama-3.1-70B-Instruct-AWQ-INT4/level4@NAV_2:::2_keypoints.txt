- Joint model that embeds concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space using ontology-linked phrases as distant supervision.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness (UMLS, Yago) without human annotation.  
- Claims scalable vocabulary expansion without additional expense.  
- Tackles non-compositional and lexically dissimilar phrases with a plausible phrase-embedding approach.  
- Trained embeddings are competitive or superior at identifying concept similarity.  
- Released software could be useful for biomedical NLP researchers.  
- Limited novelty: method is essentially a tweak to skip-gram.  
- Full model sometimes underperforms simpler baselines (Choi/Chiu) on multiple datasets.  
- Clarification needed on the meaning of “distant supervision” used in the paper.  
- Questionable choice of β values for compositionality (some cross-validated values are zero).  
- Request for details on how phrase/word vectors are used to compute similarities given many-to-many concept-phrase mappings (Table 4).  
- Why is the concept ontology necessary if approximating concepts by averaging phrases performs best—could a seed set of phrases achieve the same?
