- Proposes extending word embeddings to represent phrases and concepts by assigning fixed identifiers to equivalent phrases/words/concepts and training on a corpus where these are replaced by the identifiers.
- Method: derive concept/phrase/word sets from an ontology, create a "tagged" corpus via replacement, and combine it with the original corpus for embedding training.
- Focuses on biomedical applications, using domain-specific corpora and ontologies.
- Introduces a novel test dataset for word similarity and relatedness targeting real-world entities.
- Paper is well-written and the technique is straightforward, but may not be a substantial methodological contribution.
- Contribution scope is limited by evaluation confined to the biomedical domain.
- Recommends a more in-depth discussion of the generated test resource, which could be the paper’s most interesting asset.
- Technical issue: Equation 8’s MAP formulation should use a natural cut-off instead of ranking the entire vocabulary.
- Technical issue: Equation 8 incorrectly references a probability—its form does not define a probability and the explanation should be revised.
- Minor correction: Line 556 should read "most concepts have" instead of "most concepts has".
