- Paper proposes joint embeddings of words, phrases, and concepts using text plus a manual ontology (UMLS/YAGO) and evaluates on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks.  
- Strength: Model is simple, reasonable, and configurable between atomic and compositional phrase representations.  
- Strength: Substantial work including model development, a new general-domain evaluation dataset, and multiple analyses.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic downstream task evaluation.  
- Weakness: Human judgments appear to have been collected on phrases, not abstract concepts, so datasets should be treated/analysed as phrase-similarity data.  
- Weakness: Medical evaluation sets are very small or limited (mini MayoSRS 29 pairs, MayoSRS 101 pairs with low agreement; UMNSRS limited to single-word concepts), reducing relevance for phrase/concept representations.  
- Weakness: Extensive hyperparameter tuning on the same datasets used for reporting results undermines the reliability of comparisons to prior work.  
- Weakness: Claim of requiring less manual annotation is weak because large hand-built ontologies are used and prior work’s annotations came from existing clinical records.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper, targeted analyses differentiating phrase-word compositionality and concept-alternative-phrase relations and lacking study of hyperparameter tradeoffs.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends rejection and suggests improving evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission.
