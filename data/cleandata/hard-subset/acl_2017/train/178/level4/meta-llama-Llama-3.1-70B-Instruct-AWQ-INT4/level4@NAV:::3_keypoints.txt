- Proposed a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and multi-phrase concepts using text corpora plus a manually constructed ontology (UMLS, YAGO).  
- Created a new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset via Mechanical Turk and plan to release it; applied method to medical (UMLS) and general domains (YAGO).  
- Reported results comparable to prior work when evaluated on intrinsic similarity and relatedness benchmarks.  

- Strength: The joint embedding model is intuitive and provides a configurable middle ground between atomic phrase treatment and full compositionality, extendable to multi-phrase concepts.  
- Strength: The paper contains substantial work: model development, a new evaluation dataset, and multiple analyses.  

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic downstream evaluations to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Weakness: Human judgments were collected on phrases, so the evaluation datasets effectively measure phrase similarity, not concept-level similarity.  
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are very small or have low annotator agreement (mini MayoSRS: 29 pairs; MayoSRS: 101 pairs with low agreement).  
- Weakness: UMNSRS uses single-word concepts, limiting its relevance for phrase and multi-phrase concept representation claims.  
- Weakness: Extensive hyperparameter tuning was done on the same datasets used for final evaluation, undermining the validity of comparisons to prior work.  
- Weakness: The claim of needing less manual annotation is weakened by reliance on large manually constructed ontologies and datasets derived from clinical records.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper analysis differentiating phrase–word compositionality from concept–alternative-phrase relations; these relations deserve separate, targeted analysis.  

- Recommendation: Reject in current form; improve evaluation datasets, include extrinsic tasks, and strengthen analyses before resubmission.
