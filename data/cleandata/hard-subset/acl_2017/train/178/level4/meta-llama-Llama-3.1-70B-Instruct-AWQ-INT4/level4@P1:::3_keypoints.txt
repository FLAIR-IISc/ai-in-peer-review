- Proposed a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using an ontology (concepts as representative phrases) plus plain text; applied to UMLS (medical) and YAGO (general).  
- Evaluation compared to baselines and prior work on intrinsic similarity/relatedness; used existing medical benchmarks and a new Mechanical-Turk general-domain dataset (to be released); results are comparable to prior work.  
- Contribution: a method to embed concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space using structured ontology knowledge and an unannotated corpus.  
- Contribution: evaluation on biomedical and general-domain similarity/relatedness tasks, including a newly created dataset.  
- Contribution: analysis of compositionality of learned phrase and concept embeddings.  
- Strength: model is simple and reasonable, balancing atomic phrase treatment and compositionality, and extends the idea to concepts composed of multiple phrases.  
- Strength: substantial work including model development, a new evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations/analyses.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, which weakly predict extrinsic usefulness.  
- Weakness: human judgments were elicited on phrases, so datasets should be treated/analyzed as phrase-similarity rather than concept-similarity datasets.  
- Weakness: medical evaluation datasets are very small (mini MayoSRS = 29 pairs; MayoSRS = 101 pairs) with low annotator agreement.  
- Weakness: extensive hyperparameter tuning on the same datasets used for evaluation undermines the credibility of reported results.  
- Weakness: claim of lower annotation cost is weakened because the method still relies on large manually-constructed ontologies.  
- Weakness: insufficient deeper analysis—treating phrase-word and concept-phrase compositional relations as the same is questionable and each needs dedicated analysis.  
- Question: provide more insights into the compositionality of phrases and concepts in the learned embeddings.  
- Question: how will you address intrinsic evaluation limitations and demonstrate utility in extrinsic tasks?  
- Question: provide details on how phrase overlaps were handled during training.  
- Question: clarify how words are trained in the joint model and whether/how last words of phrases are treated as targets.
