- Summary: Proposes a method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using representative ontology phrases as distant supervision and a skip-gram/negative-sampling adaptation, evaluated on UMLS and YAGO corpora.

- Contribution 1: Novel approach for joint learning of concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using representative phrases as distant supervision.

- Contribution 2: Adapts skip-gram with negative sampling to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words and introduces two hyperparameters controlling compositionality.

- Contribution 3: Empirical evaluation on biomedical similarity/relatedness tasks and comparisons with several baseline embedding approximations.

- Strength 1: Captures semantic characteristics of ontology concepts nearly as well as more annotation-heavy prior methods.

- Strength 2: Scales to much larger vocabularies without substantial extra cost.

- Strength 3: Provides a novel dataset of real-world entity similarity and relatedness.

- Weakness 1: Training uses simple replacement of phrases/words with identifiers, which may miss complex interrelations among concepts, phrases, and words.

- Weakness 2: Evaluation is limited to biomedical similarity/relatedness, leaving cross-domain generality uncertain.

- Weakness 3: Lacks detailed analysis of the introduced hyperparameters and their effects on performance.

- Question 1: How will the method be extended to other domains or tasks, and what challenges are expected?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide more details and analysis of the hyperparameters and their performance impact?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to handle polysemy and ambiguous words or phrases in the approach?

- Additional comment 1: Paper would benefit from deeper analysis of how relationships between concepts, phrases, and words are captured.

- Additional comment 2: More details are needed on the corpora used and their preprocessing for training.

- Additional comment 3: A fuller discussion of the method’s implications for NLP and downstream applications would strengthen the paper.
