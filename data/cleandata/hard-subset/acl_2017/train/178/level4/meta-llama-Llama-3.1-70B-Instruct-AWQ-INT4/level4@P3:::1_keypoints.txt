- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-linked representative phrases as distant supervision.  
- Introduces a novel dataset of similarity and relatedness for real-world entities identified by Wikipedia page and text phrase.  
- Evaluates the method on biomedical and general-domain datasets, showing competitive performance with existing approaches.  
- Method captures semantic characteristics of ontology concepts nearly as well as prior, more heavily annotated methods.  
- Scales to a much larger vocabulary without additional expense.  
- Provides thorough evaluation including comparisons and analysis of semantic properties of embeddings.  
- Evaluation scope is limited, primarily focused on the biomedical domain.  
- Method performance depends on the quality of the ontology and chosen representative phrases.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance.  
- Requests detailed hyperparameter tuning information: ranges considered and effects on performance.  
- Asks how the authors plan to extend evaluation to other domains and datasets.  
- Requests more information on dataset construction: selection criteria for entity pairs and human judgment collection.

- Flags a minor typographical error: change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have".
