The paper proposes an extension of word embedding methods to provide representations for phrases and concepts corresponding to words. This approach works by assigning a fixed identifier to groups of phrases, words, and concepts that denote the same concept, replacing the occurrences of these phrases and words with the identifier in the training corpus, and then appending the modified corpus to the original corpus for training. The concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology, with the biomedical domain being the focus of application.

The paper is well-written, and the technique, although natural, is not a substantial contribution. The scope of the contribution is limited due to the focused evaluation within the biomedical domain. A more in-depth discussion of the generated test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities would be beneficial, as this resource could be the true interesting contribution of the paper.

There is a minor technical issue with Equation 8, which attempts to define the MAP calculation. Instead of ranking the entire vocabulary, a natural cut-off could be defined. Furthermore, Equation 8 does not define a probability, which can be easily demonstrated even with an infinite vocabulary size. Therefore, the explanation should be revised to remove the reference to probability.

Additionally, there is a small correction needed on line 556: "most concepts has" should be changed to "most concepts have".
