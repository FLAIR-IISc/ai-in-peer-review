- Proposes extending word embeddings to phrases/concepts by assigning a fixed identifier to synonymous phrase/word/concept groups, replacing occurrences to create a tagged corpus, and appending it to the original corpus for training.  
- Concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology and applied to biomedical corpora/ontologies.  
- Introduces a new test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities, a novel contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, but the proposed technique is natural and not a substantial methodological contribution.  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on the biomedical domain, limiting the scope of the contribution.  
- Recommends a more in-depth discussion of the generated test resource, which may be the most interesting part of the paper.  
- Technical issue: Eq. 8’s MAP calculation should use a natural cut-off rather than ranking the entire vocabulary.  
- Eq. 8 does not define a probability (even with infinite vocabulary); remove or revise references to probability.  
- Minor correction: change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have" (line 556).
