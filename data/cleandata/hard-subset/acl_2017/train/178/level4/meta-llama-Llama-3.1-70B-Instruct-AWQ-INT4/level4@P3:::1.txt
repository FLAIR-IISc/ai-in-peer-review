The paper proposes a novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus, using representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision. The authors evaluate their approach by learning embeddings for medical concepts and real-world entities, and demonstrate competitive performance with existing methods on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.

**Main Contributions:**

1. A weakly-supervised method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space, using distant supervision from phrases linked to concepts in an ontology.
2. A novel dataset of similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, identified both by Wikipedia page and text phrase.
3. Experimental evaluation of the proposed method on biomedical and general-domain datasets, demonstrating competitive performance with existing methods.

**Strengths:**

1. The proposed method is able to capture semantic characteristics of ontology concepts nearly as well as prior methods that require significantly more annotation.
2. The method can scale to a much larger vocabulary without additional expense.
3. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons with existing methods and an analysis of the semantic properties of the learned embeddings.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The scope of the contribution is limited, as the evaluation is focused within the biomedical domain.
2. The method relies on the quality of the ontology and the representative phrases used for distant supervision.
3. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process, which could be useful for understanding the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, including the range of values considered for each hyperparameter and the impact of different hyperparameters on the performance of the method?
2. How do you plan to extend the evaluation of your method to other domains and datasets?
3. Can you provide more information on the construction of the novel dataset of similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, including the criteria used for selecting entity pairs and the process for collecting human judgments?

**Additional Comments:**

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method and its evaluation. However, there are a few minor errors and areas for improvement, including:

* Equation 8 does not define a probability, and the explanation should be revised to reflect this.
* Line 556: "most concepts has" should be corrected to "most concepts have".
