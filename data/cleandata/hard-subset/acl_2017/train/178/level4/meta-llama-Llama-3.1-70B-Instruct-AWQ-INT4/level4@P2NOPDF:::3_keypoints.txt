- Paper proposes joint embeddings of words, phrases, and concepts using text + a manual ontology (UMLS, YAGO); evaluated mainly with intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks and a new Mechanical Turk dataset; results comparable to prior work.

- Strength: Model is simple and offers a configurable middle ground between treating phrases as atomic units and full compositionality, applied similarly to multi-phrase concepts.

- Strength: Substantial work including model development, a new evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations/analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic downstream task evaluations.

- Weakness: Datasets are framed as concept-similarity but annotators judged phrases, so they are effectively phrase-similarity datasets and should be analyzed as such.

- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are small or limited (mini MayoSRS = 29 pairs; MayoSRS = 101 pairs with low agreement; UMNSRS limited to single-word concepts), reducing relevance for phrase/concept representation claims.

- Weakness: Heavy hyperparameter tuning on the same test datasets undermines the credibility of reported results and comparisons.

- Weakness: Claim of superiority due to less manual annotation is weak because the authors rely on large manual ontologies and prior work used preexisting clinical records without extra annotation.

- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper analysis of underlying mechanisms; phrase-word and concept-phrase relations differ and need dedicated NLP analyses (e.g., headword effects) and hyperparameter tradeoff studies.

- Recommendation: Reject; authors should improve evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission.
