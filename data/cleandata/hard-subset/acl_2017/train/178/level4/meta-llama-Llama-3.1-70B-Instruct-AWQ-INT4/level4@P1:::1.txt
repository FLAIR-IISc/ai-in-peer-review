The paper describes an extension of word embedding methods to also provide representations for phrases and concepts that correspond to words. The method works by fixing an identifier for groups of phrases, words, and the concept that all denote this concept, replacing the occurrences of the phrases and words by this identifier in the training corpus, creating a "tagged" corpus, and then appending the tagged corpus to the original corpus for training. The concept/phrase/word sets are taken from an ontology. Since the domain of application is biomedical, the related corpora and ontologies are used. The researchers also report on the generation of a new test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities, which is novel.

In general, the paper is nicely written. The technique is pretty natural, though not a very substantial contribution. The scope of the contribution is limited, because of focused evaluation within the biomedical domain.

More discussion of the generated test resource could be useful. The resource could be the true interesting contribution of the paper.

There is one small technical problem, but that is probably just a matter of mathematical expression than implementation.

Technical problem:
Eq. 8: The authors want to define the MAP calculation. This is a good idea, though I think that a natural cut-off could be defined, rather than ranking the entire vocabulary. Equation 8 does not define a probability; it is quite easy to show this, even if the size of the vocabulary is infinite. So you need to change the explanation (take out talk of a probability).

Small corrections:
line 556: most concepts has --> most concepts have

**Summary of the paper**

The paper proposes a novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus. The method uses representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision and learns embeddings for medical concepts in the Unified Medical Language System and general-domain concepts in YAGO.

**Main contributions claimed by the authors**

1. A novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus.
2. The method uses representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision.
3. The authors learn embeddings for medical concepts in the Unified Medical Language System and general-domain concepts in YAGO.

**Contributions as seen by the reviewer**

1. The method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus.
2. The generation of a new test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities.

**Strengths**

1. The paper is nicely written and easy to follow.
2. The technique is pretty natural and has the potential to be useful in the biomedical domain.
3. The generation of a new test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities is a valuable contribution.

**Weaknesses**

1. The scope of the contribution is limited, as the evaluation is focused within the biomedical domain.
2. The technique is not a very substantial contribution, as it is an extension of existing word embedding methods.
3. There is a small technical problem with the MAP calculation in Eq. 8.

**Questions to authors**

1. Can you provide more discussion of the generated test resource and its potential applications?
2. How do you plan to address the limited scope of the contribution and evaluate the method in other domains?
3. Can you clarify the MAP calculation in Eq. 8 and provide a revised explanation that takes into account the issue with the probability definition?
