- Proposal: joint embedding method for words, phrases, and ontology concepts using representative phrases as distant supervision.  
- Domains: evaluated on medical (UMLS) and general (YAGO) ontologies; comparable results to prior work.  
- Contribution: weakly-supervised joint embeddings, a new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset to be released, and experiments showing semantic capture.

- Strength: model is simple, balances treating phrases as atomic vs. compositional.  
- Strength: substantial engineering and dataset/analysis work.

- Weakness: evaluation limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, lacking extrinsic task validation.  
- Weakness: datasets treated as concept-similarity but effectively measure phrase-similarity and should be analyzed accordingly.  
- Weakness: medical benchmarks are small and have low annotator agreement.  
- Weakness: extensive hyperparameter tuning on the same test datasets undermines result validity.  
- Weakness: claim of needing no manual annotation is weakened by reliance on large manually-built ontologies.

- Q: How will dataset and methodology limitations be addressed?  
- Q: Can you provide deeper analysis of phrase vs. component-word behavior behind the numbers?  
- Q: How will you study hyperparameters controlling the atomic vs. compositional tradeoff?
