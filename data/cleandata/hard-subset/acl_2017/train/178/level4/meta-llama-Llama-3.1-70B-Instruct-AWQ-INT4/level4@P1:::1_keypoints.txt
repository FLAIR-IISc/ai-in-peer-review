- Proposes extending word embeddings to jointly learn representations for phrases and ontology concepts by replacing phrase/word occurrences with a fixed concept identifier and appending this “tagged” corpus to the original for training.  
- Uses ontologies (biomedical UMLS and general-domain YAGO) to define concept/phrase/word sets for distant supervision.  
- Reports a novel test dataset for word similarity and relatedness focused on real-world entities.  
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Technique is natural and potentially useful in the biomedical domain but is an incremental extension of existing embedding methods.  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on the biomedical domain, limiting the scope and generality of the contribution.  
- The generated test resource may be the most interesting contribution and deserves more discussion.  
- Small technical issue: Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) is presented as a probability but does not define a probability; revise explanation and consider using a natural cutoff instead of ranking the entire vocabulary.  
- Minor copy-edit: line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”  
- Authors’ claimed contributions: (1) novel joint learning method for concepts/phrases/words, (2) use of representative phrases as distant supervision, (3) embeddings for UMLS and YAGO concepts.  
- Reviewer-seen contributions: the joint learning method and the new real-world entities similarity/relatedness test dataset.  
- Strength: potential usefulness in biomedical applications.  
- Strength: the new test dataset is a valuable resource.  
- Weakness: contribution is not very substantial (incremental).  
- Weakness: limited evaluation domain (biomedical only).  
- Weakness: small technical/formulation problem with MAP definition.  
- Questions to authors: provide more discussion and applications for the test resource; evaluate or plan evaluation in other domains; clarify and correct the MAP calculation/description.
