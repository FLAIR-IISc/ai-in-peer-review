- Paper proposes joint embeddings of words, phrases, and ontology concepts from raw text using representative phrases, evaluated on UMLS (medical) and YAGO (general) corpora.
- Main contributions: a joint embedding model linking concepts/phrases/words, a method to learn concept embeddings without manual annotation, and a new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset.
- Strength: the joint model is simple, sensible, and the work includes model development, a new dataset, and multiple analyses.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks rather than downstream extrinsic tasks.
- Weakness: datasets used claim to measure concept similarity but effectively measure phrase similarity.
- Weakness: the medical evaluation dataset is small and shows low annotator agreement.
- Weakness: hyperparameters are tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results, risking overfitting and unfair comparisons to prior work.
- Weakness: claim of superiority over prior work is weak because reported results are only comparable and prior work used more manual annotation.
- Question: explain the relationship between phrases and their component words in the reported results.
- Question: how does the model handle overlapping phrases?
- Question: clarify how words are trained within the joint model.
- Question: provide details of hyperparameter tuning: which task was used, development trends, and tuning procedure.
- Minor issues/edits: numerous line-specific corrections (contexts->concepts, phrase overlap handling, matrix dim should be |W|x d, confusing wording about training words, notation c vs o, missing Pedersen ref, odd 1–100 annotation scale, replace "strings" with "phrases", clarify which task used for tuning, Table 3 hard to interpret/PM+CL behavior, missing reference to Table 5).
