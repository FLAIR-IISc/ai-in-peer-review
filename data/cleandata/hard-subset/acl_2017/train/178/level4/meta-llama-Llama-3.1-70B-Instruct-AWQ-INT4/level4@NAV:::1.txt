The authors present an extension of word embedding techniques to generate representations for phrases and concepts equivalent to words. This approach involves assigning a fixed identifier to groups of phrases, words, and concepts that denote the same concept, replacing occurrences of these phrases and words with the identifier in the training corpus to create a "tagged" corpus, and then combining the tagged corpus with the original corpus for training. The concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology, specifically utilizing biomedical corpora and ontologies due to the application domain. Notably, the researchers also introduce a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed technique is intuitive, although it may not constitute a substantial contribution. The scope of the contribution is somewhat limited, primarily due to the focused evaluation within the biomedical domain. Further discussion on the generated test resource would be beneficial, as it could potentially be the most significant contribution of the paper.

One minor technical issue was identified. Specifically, in Eq. 8, the authors attempt to define the MAP calculation. While the intention is commendable, a natural cut-off could be defined instead of ranking the entire vocabulary. Moreover, Eq. 8 does not define a probability, which can be easily demonstrated, even with an infinite vocabulary size. Therefore, revising the explanation to omit references to probability is necessary.

Additionally, a minor correction is required: on line 556, "most concepts has" should be replaced with "most concepts have."
