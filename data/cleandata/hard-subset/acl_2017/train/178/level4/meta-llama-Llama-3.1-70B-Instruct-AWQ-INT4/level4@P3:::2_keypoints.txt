- Novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using ontology-derived representative phrases as distant supervision.

- Method shows competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks without requiring human corpus annotation.

- Release of software (cui2vec) and a new dataset of similarity/relatedness for real-world entities.

- Addresses phrases that are semantically close but lexically different by proposing a plausible phrase embedding training approach.

- Trained embeddings are competitive or better at identifying concept similarity.

- Released software could be useful for biomedical NLP researchers.

- Model lacks strong novelty, being essentially a tweak to skip-gram.

- Full model underperforms some baselines: Choi baseline is substantially better on two Mayo datasets and the model is only competitive on UMNSRS.

- Terminology is sometimes confusing, e.g., use of "distant supervision" that doesn't match traditional definitions.

- Clarify what is meant by "distant supervision" and how it differs from traditional supervision.

- How do authors respond to the critique that the model is not novel and the full model is not the best in results?

- Provide more explanation of the experimental setup in Table 4, specifically how phrase/word vectors are used to compute similarities.
