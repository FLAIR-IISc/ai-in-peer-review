This paper proposes an extension of word embedding methods to provide representations for phrases and concepts corresponding to words. The method involves assigning a fixed identifier to groups of phrases, words, and concepts that denote the same concept. The occurrences of these phrases and words are then replaced with this identifier in the training corpus, creating a "tagged" corpus, which is appended to the original corpus for training. The concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology, with the biomedical domain being the focus of application.

The paper is well-written, and the technique is natural, although it may not be a substantial contribution. The scope of the contribution is limited due to the focused evaluation within the biomedical domain. However, the researchers also report on the generation of a new test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities, which is a novel contribution.

More discussion on the generated test resource would be beneficial, as it could be the most interesting contribution of the paper. One small technical issue was noted, which is likely a matter of mathematical expression rather than implementation.

Specifically, Equation 8 attempts to define the MAP calculation. While this is a good idea, a natural cut-off could be defined instead of ranking the entire vocabulary. Moreover, Equation 8 does not define a probability, which can be easily demonstrated even with an infinite vocabulary size. Therefore, the explanation should be revised to remove the reference to probability.

Minor corrections include:

* Line 556: "most concepts has" should be corrected to "most concepts have".

Overall, the paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation of the proposed method and its application in the biomedical domain. However, the contribution may be considered limited, and the true value of the paper may lie in the generated test resource.
