- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases from an ontology as distant supervision.
- Introduces a new general-domain concept similarity and relatedness dataset collected via Mechanical Turk and plans to release it.
- Reports competitive results with prior work on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks without requiring human corpus annotation.
- Strength: the joint embedding model is straightforward and intuitively reasonable.
- Strength: substantial work including model development, a new evaluation dataset, and multiple analyses.
- Strength: thorough analysis of results, including hyperparameter sensitivity investigations.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, which poorly predict downstream utility.
- Weakness: human judgments were elicited on phrases, so the paper’s claim of concept-level similarity evaluation is misleading.
- Weakness: medical evaluation datasets are very small (mini MayoSRS 29 pairs; MayoSRS 101 pairs) and have low annotator agreement.
- Weakness: extensive hyperparameter tuning appears done on the same datasets used for final evaluation, undermining reported comparisons.
- Weakness: the claim of superiority because of no corpus annotation is weakened by reliance on large manually-constructed ontologies.
- Question: please clarify the relationship learned between phrases and their component words, and between concepts and their alternative phrases.
- Question: how does the model handle overlapping phrases in text?
- Question: clarify how words are trained in the joint model, particularly the meaning of “the observed phrase just completed.”
- Question: specify which task/data was used for hyperparameter tuning.
- Minor: multiple confusing notations and terminology (e.g., “contexts” vs “concepts”, “strings” vs “phrases”, Equation 1 using c instead of o).
- Minor: dimension notation likely incorrect (should be |W| x d) and “negative sampling matrix” is misleading since embeddings are used for positive contexts too.
- Minor: request to present development-set trends for hyperparameters (e.g., PM, CL, PM+CL behavior).
- Minor: missing reference (Pedersen et al. 2007) and missing table reference; odd choice of a 1–100 similarity scale for human annotations.
