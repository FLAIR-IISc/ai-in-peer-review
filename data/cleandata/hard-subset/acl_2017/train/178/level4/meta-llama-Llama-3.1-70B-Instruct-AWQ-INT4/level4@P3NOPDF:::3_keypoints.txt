- Paper proposes a joint embedding model for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manual ontology (UMLS, YAGO).
- Evaluation includes intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks and a new Mechanical Turk general-domain dataset (to be released); results comparable to prior work.
- Strength: model is simple, configurable between atomic and compositional phrase representations, and extends to concepts with multiple phrase forms.
- Strength: substantial scope—model development, new evaluation dataset, multiple analyses.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness) and lacks extrinsic downstream task evaluation.
- Weakness: datasets claimed as concept-similarity are actually phrase-based because annotators judged phrases, so they should be treated/analyzed as phrase similarity.
- Weakness: medical datasets are small or problematic—mini MayoSRS (29 pairs) and MayoSRS (101 pairs, low agreement); UMNSRS uses single-word concepts presented as words, limiting relevance to phrases.
- Weakness: extensive hyperparameter tuning on the same datasets used for evaluation makes reported comparisons and results questionable.
- Weakness: claim of superior efficiency over prior work is weak because the method still relies on large manually constructed ontologies and prior datasets came from existing clinical records.
- Weakness: paper lacks analysis into mechanisms—treats phrase-word and concept-phrase relations as similar without dedicated analyses; needs NLP-specific analysis and hyperparameter tradeoff study.
- Recommendation: reject unless evaluation datasets and methodology are improved and deeper analyses added.
