The paper presents an extension of word embedding methods to provide representations for phrases and concepts that correspond to words. The method involves assigning a fixed identifier to groups of phrases, words, and concepts that denote the same concept, replacing the occurrences of these phrases and words with this identifier in the training corpus, and then appending the modified corpus to the original corpus for training. The concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology, specifically tailored to the biomedical domain.

The researchers also report on the creation of a new test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities, which is a novel contribution. Overall, the paper is well-written, and the technique, although natural, is not a substantial contribution. The scope of the contribution is limited due to the focused evaluation within the biomedical domain.

A more in-depth discussion of the generated test resource would be beneficial, as it could be the most interesting contribution of the paper. One minor technical issue was identified. Specifically, Equation 8, which aims to define the MAP calculation, does not actually define a probability. This can be easily demonstrated, even with an infinite vocabulary size. Therefore, the explanation should be revised to remove the reference to probability.

Minor corrections are also needed. For instance, on line 556, "most concepts has" should be corrected to "most concepts have".
