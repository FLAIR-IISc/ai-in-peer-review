This paper describes an extension of the word embedding method that also provides representations of phrases and concepts that are consistent with words. This method works by assigning an identifier to a group of phrases, words, and concepts that represent this concept, replacing the presence of phrases and words by this identifier in the training corpus, creating a "tagged" corpus, and then adding the tagged corpus to the main corpus for training. The concepts/phrases/word sets are taken from an ontology. Since the application field is biomedical, related corpus and ontologies are used. The researchers also reported creating a new test dataset for word similarity and relationality for real-world entities, which is novel.

In general, the paper is well-written. The technique is quite natural, although it is not a very significant contribution. The scope of contribution is limited due to the evaluation being restricted to the biomedical domain. More discussion is needed on the test resources generated. This resource may be the paper's real attractive contribution.
