This paper presents a method for extending word embedding techniques to encompass representations for phrases and concepts alongside their corresponding words. The approach involves assigning a unique identifier to sets of synonymous phrases, words, and concepts sourced from an ontology. These identifiers then replace the original terms within the training corpus, generating a “tagged” corpus which is subsequently combined with the original corpus for model training. The authors leverage biomedical corpora and ontologies given the domain of application. A key contribution is the creation of a novel test dataset specifically designed for evaluating word similarity and relatedness concerning real-world biomedical entities.

Overall, the paper is well-written and the proposed technique is intuitive, although the contribution feels incremental. The evaluation is currently limited to the biomedical domain, restricting the broader applicability of the findings. Expanding the discussion surrounding the newly generated test resource would be beneficial, as it potentially represents the most significant aspect of this work. 

There is a minor technical issue regarding the formulation of the MAP calculation in Equation 8. While the intention is sound, defining a natural cut-off point would be more appropriate than ranking the entire vocabulary. Furthermore, the equation as presented does not represent a probability distribution, even with an infinite vocabulary size; the accompanying explanation should be revised accordingly to remove probabilistic interpretations. 

Finally, a minor correction is needed on line 556: “most concepts has” should read “most concepts have.”
