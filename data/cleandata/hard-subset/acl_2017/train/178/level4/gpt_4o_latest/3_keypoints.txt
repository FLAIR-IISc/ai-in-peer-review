- Proposes joint embeddings of words, phrases, and concepts using plain text plus a manual ontology, representing concepts by multiple phrases.
- Applied method to medical (UMLS) and general (YAGO) domains.
- Evaluated against baselines using intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks and created a new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset via Mechanical Turk (to be released).
- Reported results are comparable to prior work.
- Strength: Model is intuitive and configurable between treating phrases as atomic vs compositional.
- Strength: Paper covers substantial work—model development, new dataset creation, and multiple analyses.
- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to intrinsic tasks; lacks extrinsic task evaluations to demonstrate practical utility.
- Weakness: Annotator judgments appear to be on phrases, so datasets measure phrase similarity rather than true concept similarity—this distinction must be addressed.
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are problematic—mini MayoSRS is very small (29 pairs), MayoSRS is small with low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS is limited to single-word concepts.
- Weakness: Hyperparameters are extensively fine-tuned on the same datasets used for reporting, undermining result credibility.
- Weakness: Claim of matching prior work with less manual annotation is unconvincing given reliance on large ontologies and differing annotation sources.
- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper analysis of mechanisms; treating phrase-word and concept-phrase compositionality as analogous is questionable and needs separate investigation.
- Suggestion: Conduct more detailed NLP analysis of phrase vs component-word relations (e.g., headword effects) and study hyperparameters that balance atomic vs compositional representations.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends rejection and urges improvement of evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission.
