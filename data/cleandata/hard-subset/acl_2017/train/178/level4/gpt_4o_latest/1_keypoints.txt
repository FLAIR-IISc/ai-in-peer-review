- Paper extends word embeddings to produce representations for phrases and concepts tied to words.
- Method assigns unique identifiers to phrase/word/concept groups and replaces their occurrences to create a "tagged" corpus.
- Tagged corpus is combined with the original corpus for training embeddings.
- Concepts, phrases, and words are derived from an ontology and biomedical corpora are used for the domain focus.
- Authors introduce a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness on real-world entities.
- Paper is well-written and the technique is intuitive but not highly novel.
- Evaluation scope is narrow, being confined to the biomedical domain.
- The new test resource warrants a more detailed discussion and may be the paper’s most significant contribution.
- Technical issue: Equation 8’s MAP formulation is problematic—should use a natural cutoff and not present the expression as a probability.
- Minor correction: Line 556 — change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have."
