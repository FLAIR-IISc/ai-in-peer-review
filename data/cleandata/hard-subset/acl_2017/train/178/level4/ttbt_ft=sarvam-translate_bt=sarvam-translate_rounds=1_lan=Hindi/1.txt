This paper describes the expansion of embedding methods to provide representations for phrases and concepts that correspond to words. This method works by fixing an identifier for phrases, words, and groups of concepts that all represent this concept, replacing the occurrences of phrases and words in the training data with this identifier, creating a "tag" database, and then adding the tagged database to the original database for training. The concept/phrase/word set is taken from an ontology. Since the field of application is biomedical, the corresponding database and ontology are used. The researchers also report on the creation of a new test dataset for word similarity and relevance for real-world entities, which is novel.

In general, the paper is well-written. The technique is quite intuitive, although it is not a very significant contribution. The scope of the contribution is limited, as the evaluation is focused within the biomedical domain. More discussion on the generated test resource would be useful. This resource could be the paper's real interesting contribution.

There is a small technical problem, but it is probably a matter of mathematical expression rather than implementation.
