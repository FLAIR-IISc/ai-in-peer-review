- Proposal: weakly supervised method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings using ontology phrase mappings and raw text, treating phrases as atomic tokens and training concept vectors from representative phrase contexts.  
- Contribution: scalable annotation-free training that yields broad-coverage concept/phrase/word embeddings competitive on large similarity/relatedness benchmarks.  
- Contribution: release of open-source implementation (cui2vec) and a new crowdsourced general-domain similarity/relatedness dataset.  
- Contribution: empirical comparison of direct concept embeddings versus approximations (phrase/word averages) across multiple corpora and ontologies.  
- Strength: scalable, annotation-free setup using ontology phrase lists gives much larger coverage than prior concept-embedding methods, valuable for domains like biomedicine.  
- Strength: competitive performance on UMNSRS and other large datasets, matching or outperforming baselines on similarity with less supervision than some prior methods.  
- Strength: unified embedding space for words, phrases, and concepts enables cross-level comparisons and potential downstream uses (e.g., entity linking).  
- Strength: useful community resources (code and datasets) especially for biomedical NLP.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—straightforward extension of skip-gram with simple compositionality regularizers.  
- Weakness: mixed empirical gains—strong baselines (e.g., CHOI, CHIU-30) sometimes outperform or match the proposed method, weakening claimed advantages.  
- Weakness: direct concept vectors sometimes underperform phrase-average approximations, questioning the necessity of concept-level parameters.  
- Weakness: ambiguous use of the term “distant supervision” and unclear handling of polysemous phrase-to-concept mappings; supervision signal needs clearer justification.  
- Weakness: modeling/notation clarity issues (global β/η assumption, unused matrix notation, surprising cross-validation results setting β=0).  
- Question: clarify what “distant supervision” entails beyond phrase lexicons and how phrase-to-multiple-concept ambiguity is handled and measured.  
- Question: specify exact protocol for computing similarity/relatedness when mappings are many-to-many (averaging, canonical phrase selection, or concept vectors) for each reported result.  
- Question: justify the necessity of separate concept embeddings given that phrase-averaging often performs better—could phrase-only training plus aggregation suffice?  
- Question: rationale for global compositionality hyperparameters (β, η) versus per-phrase/type adaptation and whether learned/conditioned β/η improve performance.  
- Question: provide details on evaluation splits/tuning (per-fold variance, prevention of test leakage) and any extrinsic evaluations to support intrinsic results.  
- Overall: practical, well-executed large-coverage embedding method with valuable resources, but limited novelty and inconsistent empirical advantages; clearer framing and evaluations would strengthen the work.
