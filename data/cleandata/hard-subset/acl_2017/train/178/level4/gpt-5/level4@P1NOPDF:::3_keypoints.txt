- Strength: Joint embedding model is straightforward and offers a configurable middle ground between atomic phrase treatment and compositional modeling, applied analogously to concepts composed of multiple phrases.  
- Strength: Substantial amount of work — model development, creation of a new evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations/analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic task evaluation that better predicts downstream performance.  
- Weakness: Annotators were shown phrases rather than abstract concepts, so datasets should be treated and analyzed as phrase-similarity datasets, not concept judgments.  
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are very small or limited: mini MayoSRS (29 pairs), MayoSRS (101 pairs, low IAA), and UMNSRS only contains single-word concepts — raising relevance concerns for phrase/concept evaluation.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameters were extensively tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results and comparisons, undermining trust in reported numbers.  
- Weakness: Claim of superiority over prior work is unconvincing because the proposed method still relies on large manually constructed ontologies while prior manual annotations had different provenance.  
- Weakness: Lack of deeper, targeted analyses separating phrase–word compositional relations from concept–phrase relations; missing NLP analyses (e.g., headword effects) and study of hyperparameters that trade off atomic vs compositional views.

- General: Recommend rejection pending improved evaluation methodology — add extrinsic tasks, clarify whether judgments are phrase vs concept, avoid tuning on test sets, and provide more targeted analyses.
