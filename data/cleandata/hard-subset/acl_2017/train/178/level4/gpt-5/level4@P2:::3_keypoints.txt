- Proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and ontology concepts using unlabeled text and a curated ontology mapping concepts to phrases.  
- Evaluated on biomedical (UMLS) and general-domain (YAGO) corpora, compared to baselines on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks.  
- Introduces a new crowdsourced general-domain dataset for entity similarity/relatedness.

- Joint embedding framework with tunable parameters controlling atomic vs. compositional representations.  
- New crowdsourced evaluation set for general-domain entity similarity/relatedness (to be released).  
- Empirical cross-domain study showing competitive results with prior work.

- Conceptually straightforward model with a practical configurable trade-off between atomic and compositional representations.  
- Substantial execution: multi-corpus training, new dataset creation, and analyses of compositionality and semantic clustering.  
- Scales to large vocabularies using distant supervision from ontologies and achieves competitive intrinsic performance.  
- Cross-domain experiments (UMLS and YAGO) suggest broader applicability beyond a single ontology.

- Evaluation limited to intrinsic tasks, lacking extrinsic downstream evaluations to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Datasets are effectively phrase-level judgments despite being framed as concept-level; several biomedical sets are very small or limited in scope.  
- Extensive hyperparameter tuning was done on test sets, undermining validity of reported improvements.  
- Claim of reduced reliance on human annotation is overstated given dependence on large, manually constructed ontologies.  
- Conflates two distinct composition types (phrase-by-word vs. concept-by-phrase) without sufficient separate analyses.

- How were overlapping phrase matches resolved during preprocessing (tie-breaking rules)?  
- In the joint model, are all words in multiword phrases used for word updates or only the final token, and how are updates interleaved?  
- Which datasets and metrics were used for hyperparameter tuning, and were held-out development sets used that were not part of reported tests?  
- For the crowdsourced general-domain data, were annotators shown canonical concepts or surface phrases, and how was ambiguity/synonymy handled?  
- Are there any preliminary extrinsic results (e.g., entity linking, retrieval, clinical coding) demonstrating practical benefits?
