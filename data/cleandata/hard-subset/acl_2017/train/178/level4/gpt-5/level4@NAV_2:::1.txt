The paper presents an extension of word embedding approaches to also yield representations for phrases and ontology-linked concepts. The approach assigns a fixed identifier to groups of phrases, words, and the concept they denote, replaces occurrences of those phrases/words with this identifier to produce a “tagged” corpus, and then trains on the combination of the tagged and original corpora. The phrase/word/concept sets come from an ontology. Given the biomedical focus, the authors use domain-appropriate corpora and ontologies. The work also introduces a new evaluation dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, which is a novel resource.

Overall, the paper is clearly written. The method is intuitive, but it does not feel like a substantial technical advance. The contribution’s scope is constrained by the primarily biomedical evaluation. A more extensive discussion of the newly created test resource would strengthen the paper; this resource may be the most interesting contribution.

There is one minor technical issue, which likely reflects notation/exposition rather than an implementation error.

Technical issue:
Eq. 8: The authors aim to define the MAP computation. This is a useful idea, though a natural cut-off could be adopted instead of ranking the entire vocabulary. As written, Equation 8 does not define a probability; one can show this readily, even with an infinite vocabulary. Please adjust the explanation accordingly (remove references to a probability interpretation).
