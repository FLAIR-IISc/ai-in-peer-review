- Extends word embeddings to produce representations for phrases and underlying concepts by assigning a shared identifier to phrase/word/concept sets and replacing their occurrences in a tagged corpus appended to the original corpus.  
- Concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology and applied in the biomedical domain using biomedical corpora and ontologies.  
- Introduces a novel test dataset for word similarity and relatedness among real-world entities.  
- Paper is clearly written.  
- Technique is natural but represents a modest (not major) technical contribution.  
- Evaluation is limited in scope because it is confined to the biomedical domain.  
- The new test resource warrants more discussion and may be the paper’s most interesting contribution.  
- Technical issue: Equation 8 (MAP calculation) is incorrectly presented as a probability; the explanation should remove probability references or be revised.  
- Small copyedit: Line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
