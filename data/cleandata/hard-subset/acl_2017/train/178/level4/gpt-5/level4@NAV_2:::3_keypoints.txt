- Proposal: Jointly embed words, phrases, and concepts using unlabeled text plus a curated ontology mapping concepts to surface forms, evaluated with UMLS (biomed) and YAGO (general).
- Evaluation: Compared to simpler approximations and prior methods, mainly on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks; new MTurk similarity/relatedness dataset collected for general domain and slated for release.
- Reported results: Performance is broadly on par with prior work.
- Strength: Simple, reasonable joint embedding framework that tunably balances atomic phrase treatment and compositionality, and analogously relates concepts to multiple phrases.
- Strength: Substantial scope—model design, new evaluation dataset, multiple empirical evaluations and analyses.
- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to intrinsic similarity/relatedness, lacking extrinsic downstream tasks that better gauge utility.
- Weakness: Human judgments were elicited over displayed phrases, so evaluations are effectively phrase-based rather than true concept judgments and should be treated as such.
- Weakness: Biomedical evaluation datasets are small (MayoSRS 101 pairs, Mini MayoSRS 29 pairs) with modest annotator agreement; UMNSRS focuses on single-word concepts and may not reflect phrase/general concept representation needs.
- Weakness: Hyperparameters were extensively tuned on the same test sets used for reporting, undermining reliability of comparisons and results.
- Weakness: Claim of requiring less manual annotation is unconvincing given reliance on large, manually built ontologies and the fact prior resources were derived from clinical records.
- Weakness: Paper needs deeper qualitative analysis beyond aggregate scores; treating phrase–word composition and concept–phrase aggregation as analogous may hide important differences and each requires targeted analysis.
- Weakness: Impact of hyperparameters controlling atomic vs compositional trade-offs (β, η) is insufficiently investigated.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends rejection pending stronger evaluation design, better datasets, and more thorough analyses.
