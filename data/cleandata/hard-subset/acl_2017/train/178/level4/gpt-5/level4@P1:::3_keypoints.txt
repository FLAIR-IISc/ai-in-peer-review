- Proposes a weakly supervised joint embedding model for words, phrases, and ontology concepts using phrase mappings from ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) as distant supervision.
- Links concepts to one or more representative phrases from a manually constructed ontology and treats phrase occurrences in raw text as distant supervision for concept contexts.
- Introduces hyperparameters to balance atomic vs. compositional representations for phrases and concepts.
- Evaluates primarily on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks, including standard biomedical datasets and a new crowdsourced dataset for people/places/organizations.
- Reports performance broadly comparable to prior work with substantially larger vocabulary coverage and no corpus-level manual annotation.
- Main contributions: the joint embedding model, a new crowdsourced similarity/relatedness dataset, and multi-domain empirical analyses including compositionality and clustering.
- Strength: model is simple, sensible, and configurable with practical interpolation between atomic and compositional treatments.
- Strength: substantial effort across model development, dataset construction, and multiple evaluations/analyses.
- Strength: avoids corpus-level manual annotation while achieving competitive performance and high vocabulary coverage, aiding scalability.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to intrinsic tasks; lack of extrinsic downstream evaluations reduces the paper’s impact.
- Weakness: concept similarity datasets used are effectively phrase-based and should be treated and analyzed as phrase similarity datasets.
- Weakness: MayoSRS datasets are very small with low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS is restricted to single-word concepts—these limitations weaken relevance to phrase/concept representations.
- Weakness: hyperparameters appear to be extensively tuned on the same datasets used for evaluation, undermining the reported results.
- Weakness: claim of reduced manual annotation is misleading because the method depends on large manually constructed ontologies and prior work used existing clinical records.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of differences between phrase–word and concept–phrase relations; treating them analogously oversimplifies and needs targeted analyses (e.g., headword dominance).
- Weakness: deeper investigation of η and β hyperparameters controlling atomic/compositional trade-offs is needed to strengthen the contribution.
- Reviewer had no major additional questions beyond clarifications but urges addressing evaluation design and the role of η/β in the response.
- Recommendation: reject due to reliance on intrinsic evaluation, tuning on test sets, and limited compositionality insight; encourage improved evaluation and analyses before resubmission.
