- Paper proposes a joint embedding model for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manual ontology, evaluated on UMLS and YAGO.  
- Concepts are modeled as one or more representative phrases; authors collect a new general-domain similarity dataset via Mechanical Turk.

- Strength: The model is straightforward, offering a configurable middle ground between atomic phrase treatment and compositional modeling.  
- Strength: Substantial amount of work including model development, a new dataset, and multiple analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; no extrinsic downstream tasks to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Weakness: Annotators were shown phrases, so datasets labeled as concept similarity are effectively phrase similarity and should be treated/analysed as such.  
- Weakness: Medical datasets are very small or limited (mini MayoSRS 29 pairs, MayoSRS 101 pairs with low agreement; UMNSRS uses single-word concepts), raising relevance concerns for phrase/concept representations.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameters were extensively tuned on the same datasets used for final evaluation, undermining the reliability of reported comparisons.  
- Weakness: The claim of less manual annotation is unconvincing because the method depends on large manually constructed ontologies and prior annotated clinical records were not costly to obtain.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper analysis differentiating phrase–word compositionality from concept–phrase relations; each deserves separate NLP analyses (e.g., headword roles).  
- Weakness: Missing investigation on how hyperparameters control the atomic vs compositional trade-off.

- Recommendation: Given these methodological and evaluation weaknesses, reviewer recommends rejection and advises improving datasets and evaluation before resubmission.
