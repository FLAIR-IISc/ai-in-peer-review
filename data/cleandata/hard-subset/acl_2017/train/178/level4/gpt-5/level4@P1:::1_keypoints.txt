- Proposes a weakly supervised method to jointly learn embeddings for concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-provided phrase-to-concept mappings.  
- Creates a tagged corpus by greedily replacing mapped phrases with unique identifiers and trains on both original and tagged corpora with skip-gram and negative sampling.  
- Updates concept embeddings from the contexts of their phrase realizations; evaluated mainly on biomedical (UMLS) data and additionally on YAGO.  
- Main contributions: a new crowdsourced entity similarity/relatedness dataset, a simple ontology-driven weakly supervised embedding method, and competitive biomedical results without manual annotations.  
- Strength: clear, straightforward idea and well-written presentation making the approach easy to follow.  
- Strength: avoids manual annotation by leveraging ontology phrase mappings as distant supervision, practical for costly domains like biomedicine.  
- Strength: introduces a potentially valuable entity-level evaluation resource beyond word-level benchmarks.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—approach is a natural extension of existing embedding techniques rather than a major algorithmic advance.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedicine, limiting claims of generality across domains.  
- Weakness: paper provides insufficient discussion of the new dataset’s construction, selection criteria, quality control, and intended use.  
- Weakness: technical/formulation issue with Eq. 8—expression labeled as a probability is not normalizable; probabilistic language should be removed and evaluation consider top-k MAP.  
- Weakness: minor writing error noted (“most concepts has” → “most concepts have”).  
- Request: expand main-paper details on dataset construction—pair selection across types/relatedness, QC beyond validation pairs, annotator filtering, aggregation, and recommended splits.  
- Request: clarify intended use cases (intrinsic vs extrinsic) and interpretation of relatively low Fleiss’ κ for the dataset.  
- Request: provide more discussion or preliminary non-biomedical results to support generalization claims and report sensitivity of hyperparameters (β, η) to domain.  
- Request: clarify Eq. 8’s intent and remove probability terminology; report MAP under a top-k neighborhood if adopted.  
- Request: explain phrase identification specifics—are single words replaced, how overlapping/nested matches and concept ambiguity are handled.  
- Request: describe negative sampling choices—did you try separate distributions for words vs phrases or frequency-adjusted sampling for tagged tokens?  
- Overall: pragmatic, clearly presented weakly supervised approach yielding reasonable biomedical concept embeddings; dataset is valuable, but novelty is modest, scope is narrow, and Eq. 8 plus dataset description need revision/expansion.
