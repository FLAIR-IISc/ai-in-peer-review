- Extends word embeddings to jointly represent words, phrases, and ontology concepts by assigning a single identifier to each concept plus its synonymous surface forms.  
- Training corpus is "tagged" by replacing all occurrences of these forms with the identifier and concatenated with the original corpus for model training.  
- Uses biomedical ontology and domain-specific corpora; evaluation and scope are limited to the biomedical domain.  
- Introduces a novel test dataset for word/entity similarity and relatedness for real-world entities—likely the paper’s most interesting contribution.  
- Paper is clearly written and the technique is natural and practical, but the methodological novelty is limited.  
- More discussion and analysis of the new test resource would strengthen the paper.  
- Technical issue: Equation 8’s MAP formulation does not define a probability; a natural cutoff is more appropriate than ranking the entire vocabulary.  
- Revise the exposition of Equation 8 to avoid referring to it as a probability.  
- Minor correction: Line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
