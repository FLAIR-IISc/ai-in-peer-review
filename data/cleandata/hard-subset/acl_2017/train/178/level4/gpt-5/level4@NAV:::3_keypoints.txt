- Proposed method: joint embedding of words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manually curated ontology (demonstrated with UMLS and YAGO); evaluated mainly on intrinsic similarity/relatedness; performance comparable to prior work.
- Strength: simple, intuitive joint embedding framework that provides a tunable middle ground between atomic phrase representations and compositional models, extended to concepts via multiple representative phrases.
- Strength: substantial amount of work including model development, a new evaluation resource, and multiple evaluations and analyses.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks; lacks extrinsic downstream tasks to show utility.
- Weakness: annotators were shown phrases, so datasets are effectively phrase-similarity datasets and should be framed and analyzed as such.
- Weakness: mini MayoSRS is extremely small (29 pairs) and MayoSRS is small (101 pairs) with low inter-annotator agreement, raising concerns about reliability.
- Weakness: UMNSRS contains only single-word concepts as presented to annotators; this limitation should be clearly stated and questions relevance to phrase/general concept representation.
- Weakness: authors appear to tune hyperparameters extensively on the same datasets used for final evaluation, undermining validity of reported comparisons.
- Weakness: claim of outperforming prior work due to less manual annotation is unconvincing because the method depends on large curated ontologies and prior baselines used existing clinical-note-derived annotations.
- Weakness: analysis lacks explanatory insight beyond scores; conflates phrase–word composition with concept–phrase composition which are fundamentally different and need separate analysis.
- Weakness: missing NLP-focused analysis on phrase–word composition (e.g., headword effects) and on how hyperparameters mediate atomic vs. compositional trade-offs.
- General recommendation: reviewer recommends rejection and urges strengthening of evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission.
