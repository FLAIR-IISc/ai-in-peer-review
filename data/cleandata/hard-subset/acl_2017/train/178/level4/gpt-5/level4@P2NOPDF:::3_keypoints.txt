- Paper proposes joint embeddings of words, phrases, and concepts using plain-text corpora plus a curated ontology; evaluated on UMLS (medical) and YAGO (general); results broadly comparable to prior work.

- Strength: Model is straightforward and offers a configurable middle ground between atomic phrase representations and compositional models, extended to concepts represented by multiple phrases.

- Strength: Substantial scope—model development, creation of a new general-domain evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations/analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; lacks extrinsic downstream evaluations that better predict real-world performance.

- Weakness: Annotators saw phrases rather than abstract concepts, so datasets should be treated and analyzed as phrase-level similarity datasets.

- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are weak—mini MayoSRS is tiny (29 pairs), MayoSRS is small (101 pairs) with low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS contains only single-word concepts—limiting relevance to phrase/general concept representations.

- Weakness: Hyperparameters appear to be tuned on the same datasets used for reporting, with no clear train/dev/test separation or cross-validation, undermining credibility of results.

- Weakness: Claim of reduced manual annotation is unconvincing since the method depends on large manually constructed ontologies, while prior work used existing clinical records.

- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper analyses distinguishing phrase–word composition from concept–phrase variation; missing NLP analyses (e.g., headword dominance) and ablations on compositional vs atomic trade-offs.

- Recommendation: Reject in current form; authors should add extrinsic tasks, clarify concept vs phrase annotation/analysis, use proper dev/test splits, and provide targeted compositional analyses before resubmission.
