- Proposes extending word embeddings to learn joint representations for phrases and their underlying concepts by assigning a single identifier to each concept group and tagging the corpus.  
- Trains on a concatenation of the original and the concept-tagged corpora derived from an ontology.  
- Uses biomedical corpora and ontologies appropriate to the domain.  
- Introduces a novel evaluation set for word similarity and relatedness involving real-world entities.  
- Paper is well written and the technique is natural but not a substantial methodological advance.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to the biomedical domain, constraining the contribution’s generality.  
- The newly created test resource may be the paper’s most compelling contribution and deserves more discussion.  
- Technical issue: Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) is presented as a probability but does not define one; consider using a natural cutoff instead of ranking the entire vocabulary and revise the explanation.  
- Small correction: line 556 should read “most concepts have” (not “has”).
