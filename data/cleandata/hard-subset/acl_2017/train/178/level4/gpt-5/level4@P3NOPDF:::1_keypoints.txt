- Paper maps synonymous words/phrases to ontology-derived concept identifiers, appends a tagged corpus to original text, and trains joint word/phrase/concept embeddings (biomedical focus).
- Authors introduce a new entity-level similarity/relatedness evaluation dataset that fills a benchmark gap.
- Clear, well-written presentation with a simple, practical method leveraging ontologies and low engineering cost.
- Concept-level embeddings are practically useful for tasks needing entity normalization and synonym handling.
- New evaluation resource is potentially valuable for real-world entity evaluation.
- Core technique is incremental and not a major methodological advance.
- Evaluation is restricted to biomedicine, leaving generality and portability unclear.
- The new dataset is under-described; its construction quality, reliability, and scope need more detail.
- Equation 8 should use MAP@k (cutoff) for realism and must not be presented as a probability distribution unless reformulated.
- Provide full dataset documentation: construction protocol, annotation guidelines, quality control (IAA), coverage stats, and licensing.
- Add ablation studies: original vs. tagged vs. combined corpora, sensitivity to tagged/original data proportion, and effects of ontology coverage/noise.
- Discuss failure cases where collapsing surface forms harms meaning (near-synonyms, context-dependent senses, ambiguous mappings).
- Strengthen baselines and comparisons to alternative semantic-injection methods (retrofitting, text-graph embeddings, phrase/subword models, normalization pipelines).
- If possible, include a cross-domain pilot or analysis to indicate portability beyond biomedical data.
- Define key terms (concept, entity, CUI) early and consistently; provide implementation details for reproducibility (mapping dictionary, preprocessing, training settings, corpus combination).
- Minor correction: Line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have”.
- Overall: practical, well-executed idea with useful contributions, but needs expanded dataset documentation, clearer evaluation metric, stronger analyses, and broader comparisons to improve impact.
