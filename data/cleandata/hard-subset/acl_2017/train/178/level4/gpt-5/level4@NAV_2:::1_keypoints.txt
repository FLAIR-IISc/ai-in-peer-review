- Paper extends word embedding methods to produce representations for phrases and ontology-linked concepts.
- Method: assign fixed identifiers to phrase/word/concept groups, replace occurrences in corpus with identifiers to create a “tagged” corpus, then train on tagged + original corpora.
- Phrase/word/concept sets are drawn from an ontology; biomedical corpora and ontologies are used for evaluation.
- Introduces a new evaluation dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, a novel resource.
- Paper is clearly written and the method is intuitive.
- Method is not a substantial technical advance.
- Contribution’s scope is limited by primarily biomedical evaluation.
- Recommend a more extensive discussion of the newly created test resource, which may be the most interesting contribution.
- Technical issue: Eq. 8 (MAP computation) as written does not define a probability; remove probability interpretation or adopt a natural cutoff instead of ranking the entire vocabulary.
- Small correction: line 556 “most concepts has” -> “most concepts have.”
