- Proposes joint embeddings for words, phrases, and concepts using ontology-informed phrase tokens and a skip-gram objective, evaluated on UMLS and YAGO benchmarks.
- Strength: Clear motivation to treat known phrases as atomic units to capture non-compositional semantics.
- Strength: Empirical results are competitive and sometimes outperform prior methods on concept similarity/relatedness.
- Strength: Released software is practically useful for biomedical NLP where ontologies are rich.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—method is mainly a skip-gram variant with ontology-informed tokens.
- Weakness: Results are inconsistent; strong baselines (e.g., Choi) outperform the full model on several datasets.
- Weakness: Averaging phrase embeddings often outperforms explicit concept embeddings, questioning the added value of concepts.
- Weakness: Using a single global β to trade compositional vs atomic representations is overly coarse and may be inadequate.
- Clarity gap: The term “distant supervision” is used loosely and may be misleading without clearer explanation of supervision granularity.
- Clarity gap: Notation (e.g., E_W) introduced in Section 3.2 is not consistently referenced or tied to objectives/experiments.
- Clarity gap: Evaluation protocol, especially many-to-many concept–phrase mapping handling in Table 4, is under-specified.
- Question: What exactly is the supervision signal—are phrase occurrences labeled with unique concept IDs or are concepts attached only to phrase types without context disambiguation?
- Question: For concept similarity, are similarities computed using concept embeddings only, and how are multiple phrases per concept aggregated at test time?
- Question: How are polysemous phrases linked to multiple concepts handled during training and evaluation?
- Request: Provide analysis showing when explicit concept embeddings outperform phrase-averaging (e.g., rare concepts, long-tail phrases, cross-ontology alignment).
- Request: Justify global β choice, and provide ablations or alternatives (per-phrase β, learned gates) and sensitivity analyses.
- Suggestion: Add ablations contrasting word-only, phrase-only, compositional phrase, concept-only, and full models to isolate concept benefits.
- Suggestion: Replace global β with learned per-phrase gates or attention, or regularize/tie β to phrase statistics.
- Suggestion: Reframe “distant supervision” as ontology-guided tokenization unless instance-level disambiguation is performed, and tidy notation usage.
- Suggestion: Provide statistical significance, confidence intervals, detailed similarity computation rules, and more baselines (e.g., retrofitting).
- Suggestion: Extend evaluation to downstream tasks (entity linking, relation extraction) where concept-level semantics may be more relevant.
- Reproducibility request: Document corpus/preprocessing/phrase identification, hyperparameters, vocabularies, mappings, and report run variance.
- Overall: Well-motivated and solid but incremental; needs clearer exposition, stronger ablations, and improved compositionality modeling to demonstrate when concepts add value.
