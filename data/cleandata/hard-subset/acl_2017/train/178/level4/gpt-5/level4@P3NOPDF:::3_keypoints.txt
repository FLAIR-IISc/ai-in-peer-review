- Summary: Proposes a joint embedding model for words, phrases, and concepts using corpora + ontology, evaluated on UMLS and YAGO with a new crowdsourced general-domain dataset; results roughly match prior work.

- Strength: The joint embedding framework is conceptually clear and configurable between atomic and compositional phrase modeling, extended to multi-phrase concepts.

- Strength: Paper includes substantial work—model design, creation of a new evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations with analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation is intrinsic-only (similarity/relatedness), which poorly predicts downstream utility and omits extrinsic tasks.

- Weakness: Human judgments appear elicited over surface phrases, so datasets function as phrase-level rather than true concept-level evaluations.

- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are limited—miniMayoSRS and MayoSRS are very small/low-agreement; UMNSRS is single-word only and not ideal for phrase/multi-phrase evaluation.

- Weakness: Hyperparameters were tuned on the same datasets used for final reporting and comparisons, undermining validity of reported gains.

- Weakness: Claim of reduced manual annotation is unconvincing because approach depends on large manually constructed ontologies and prior datasets derived from clinical records.

- Weakness: Limited insight into differences between phrase compositionality and concept aggregation; analyses (e.g., headword vs modifier effects) and hyperparameter impacts are insufficient.

- Suggestion: Add at least one or two downstream extrinsic tasks (e.g., IE, entity linking, relation extraction, clinical coding) even as small controlled experiments.

- Suggestion: Separate and analyze phrase-level vs concept-level evaluations; ensure annotators assess concept meaning or provide clear mappings/instructions.

- Suggestion: Augment medical evaluation with larger, higher-quality datasets or analyses explaining performance variability due to dataset size and annotator agreement.

- Suggestion: Use a clean evaluation protocol—reserve dev sets for tuning and report test results on held-out data; avoid tuning on final test sets.

- Suggestion: Provide targeted analyses distinguishing phrase compositionality (component word contributions) from concept aggregation (mapping multiple phrases to one concept; robustness to paraphrase).

- Suggestion: Report ablations and sensitivity analyses for hyperparameters controlling atomic vs compositional balances (PM, CL, PM+CL), with development-set trends.

- Suggestion: Clarify training dynamics—how target words in phrases are updated, handling of overlapping phrases, and interaction of ontology signals with distributional context.

- Recommendation: Reject due to intrinsic-only evaluation, limited/mismatched datasets, and tuning on test sets; idea is interesting but needs stronger extrinsic validation and principled analysis.

- Minor: Correct typographical/terminology issues (e.g., “contexts”→“concepts”, “strings”→“phrases”) and standardize notation (Equation 1).

- Minor: Clarify overlapping-phrase handling and how words are trained/updated in the joint model (line 202, line 250).

- Minor: Clarify matrix dimensions and the term “negative sampling matrix” (line 220).

- Minor: Include missing reference (Pedersen et al., 2007) and justify the unusually fine-grained 1–100 annotation scale with IAA and calibration details.

- Minor: Specify exactly which task/dataset was used for hyperparameter tuning and reflect this in the appendix (line 496).

- Minor: Make Table 3 trends interpretable by providing development-set curves for relevant hyperparameters and fix missing table references (line 535).
