- Summary: Proposes a weakly supervised scheme that replaces ontology-linked phrases with identifiers and trains skip-gram on original+tagged corpora to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words, evaluated mainly in biomedical and secondarily in general domains.

- Contribution 1: Scalable weakly supervised method that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using ontology phrase forms as distant supervision without manual annotation.

- Contribution 2: Empirical study showing competitive intrinsic concept similarity/relatedness performance and substantially higher vocabulary coverage in biomedical corpora, plus analyses of compositionality and semantic clustering.

- Contribution 3: New crowdsourced general-domain dataset of human judgments for entity similarity and relatedness.

- Strength: Practical and scalable with no manual annotation, achieving >3x vocabulary coverage over prior biomedical concept embeddings.

- Strength: Competitive intrinsic results on biomedical concept similarity/relatedness and strong phrase-based approximations for general-domain entities.

- Strength: Joint embedding analyses demonstrate concept–phrase and phrase–word relationships and some semantic type clustering.

- Strength: Clear experimental setup with comparisons to multiple published biomedical embeddings and reported hyperparameter sensitivity.

- Strength: Releases a new resource for entity-level similarity/relatedness evaluation beyond words and phrases.

- Weakness: Methodological novelty is limited—approach is a natural extension of skip-gram with distant supervision and compositional regularizers are simple and often unhelpful.

- Weakness: Evaluation is largely intrinsic and biomedical; lacks downstream task evaluation to show practical utility and generalization.

- Weakness: Distant supervision assumes uniform mapping of phrases to concepts, introducing ambiguous/noisy supervision not quantified or mitigated.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning appears to use the evaluation datasets (adapted 5-fold), risking optimistic bias without a strict dev/test split.

- Weakness: New general-domain dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ = 0.24), limiting reliability; phrase approximations outperform concept embeddings, questioning concept-level signal.

- Weakness: Analyses indicate heavy reliance on surface forms over non-lexical concept information; ontology structure is not integrated to address this.

- Question: How frequent are ambiguous phrase-to-concept mappings in training, and what is their effect on learned vectors and correlations?

- Question: Can results be provided with a strict development/test split or with fixed default hyperparameters to avoid tuning bias?

- Question: In which settings do the β and η compositionality regularizers help, and can you provide targeted ablations and intuition?

- Question: Have you considered adding ontology edges (hierarchy/relations) as an auxiliary objective to capture non-lexical concept relations?

- Question: Will the new dataset, annotation instructions, quality-control details, and code be publicly released?

- Minor: Equation 8’s Pr(c|Cp, V) is misnamed as a probability—relabel as precision-at-rank and define MAP conventionally.

- Minor: Suggest reporting precision@k or cut-off analyses instead of ranking the entire vocabulary for MAP-style evaluations.

- Minor: Typo: “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
