This paper presents an extension to word embedding methods to generate unified representations for phrases, words, and corresponding concepts. The approach involves identifying a single representative identifier for groups of lexically distinct but semantically equivalent terms (phrases, words, and concepts) sourced from an ontology. Occurrences of these terms in the training corpus are then replaced with this identifier, creating a “tagged” corpus which is concatenated with the original corpus for embedding training. The study leverages biomedical corpora and ontologies appropriate for this domain. A notable contribution is the creation of a new test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness specifically designed for real-world biomedical entities.

Overall, the paper is well-written and the proposed technique is intuitive, though the contribution feels incremental. The evaluation is currently limited in scope, focusing solely on the biomedical domain. Expanding the discussion of the generated test resource would be beneficial, as it potentially represents the most significant contribution of this work. 

A minor technical issue exists in Eq. 8, where the authors attempt to define a MAP calculation. While the intention is sound, defining a cutoff point would be more appropriate than ranking the entire vocabulary. Furthermore, the equation does not represent a probability distribution, even with an infinite vocabulary size; the accompanying explanation should be revised accordingly to remove probabilistic interpretations.

Finally, a small correction is needed on line 556: “most concepts has” should be “most concepts have”.
