- Paper proposes embedding words, phrases, and concepts by treating ontology-linked phrases as atomic symbols and training with a skip-gram style method, evaluated on UMLS and Yago.
- Strength: Addresses that lexically dissimilar phrases can be semantically close and non-compositional phrases need special treatment.
- Strength: Proposed phrase embedding model is plausible and yields competitive or better performance on concept similarity tasks.
- Strength: Released software could be useful for biomedical NLP researchers.
- Weakness: Limited novelty — largely a refinement of the standard skip-gram approach.
- Weakness: Model does not consistently achieve state-of-the-art; baselines like Choi outperform it on Mayo datasets and it is only competitive on larger UMNSRS.
- Concern: The term “distant supervision” is unclear since every occurrence of a concept-associated phrase appears used as training context, differing from typical supervised paradigms.
- Issue: Notation introduced in Section 3.2 (e.g., E_W) is not used later in the paper.
- Concern: Global β to control phrase compositionality is a strong simplification; cross-validation often sets β to zero, negating compositionality.
- Ambiguity: Experimental setup for Table 4 is unclear given many-to-many concept–phrase mappings and apparent use of only concept vectors for similarity.
- Observation: The approximate phrase method (averaging constituent phrase vectors) performs best in Table 5, calling into question the necessity of the explicit concept ontology.
- Question: Please clarify the nature of the “supervision” from phrase-concept associations and how it differs from traditional supervised learning.
- Question: Provide intuition for why a single global β is sufficient and whether more nuanced compositionality strategies were considered.
- Question: Explain how phrase/word vectors are used in evaluation given many-to-many mappings between concepts and phrases.
- Question: Given the strong performance of the approximate phrase method, justify the need to incorporate an explicit concept ontology.
- Suggestion: Strengthen evaluation with additional downstream tasks beyond concept similarity/relatedness.
- Suggestion: Include a more thorough ablation study to isolate contributions of individual model components.
