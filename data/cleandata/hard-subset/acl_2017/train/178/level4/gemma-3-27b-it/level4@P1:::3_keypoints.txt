- Proposed joint embedding of words, phrases, and concepts using text plus a manual ontology (UMLS, YAGO).  
- Evaluated mainly on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks; created a new general-domain MTurk dataset intended for release.  
- Strength: model is straightforward and balances atomic vs compositional views of phrases and concepts.  
- Strength: substantial amount of work including model development, new dataset, and multiple analyses.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies solely on intrinsic tasks, limiting claims about extrinsic utility.  
- Weakness: human judgments were collected on phrases but treated as concept similarity, so datasets should be seen as phrase-level.  
- Weakness: medical datasets are small or problematic (mini MayoSRS 29 pairs, MayoSRS 101 pairs with low agreement).  
- Weakness: UMNSRS covers only single-word concepts presented as such, limiting relevance to phrase/concept representation.  
- Weakness: extensive hyperparameter tuning was done on the same datasets used for reporting results, undermining validity.  
- Weakness: claim of less manual annotation is weakened by using large manually-constructed ontologies and prior clinical records.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis distinguishing phrase↔word compositionality from concept↔phrase compositionality; missing investigation of hyperparameter trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends rejection pending improved evaluation datasets and methodology.  
- Question: Why evaluate concept similarity using phrase-level human judgments, and how does that affect result interpretation?  
- Question: Provide full details of hyperparameter tuning — metrics used, selection criteria, and whether development sets were used.  
- Question: How would results change if extrinsic evaluation tasks were included?
