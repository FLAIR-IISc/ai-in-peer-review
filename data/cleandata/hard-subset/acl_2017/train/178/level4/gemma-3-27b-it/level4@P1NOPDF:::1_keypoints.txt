- Paper extends word embeddings to jointly represent phrases, concepts and words by assigning a shared identifier and replacing occurrences to create a “tagged” corpus appended to the original text.

- Concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an existing ontology and the work is evaluated on biomedical corpora and ontologies.

- Authors created a novel test dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world biomedical entities.

- Paper is generally well-written and the method is intuitive.

- Contribution feels incremental rather than substantial.

- Evaluation is narrowly focused on the biomedical domain, limiting generality.

- The newly created test resource deserves more discussion and may be the paper’s most interesting contribution.

- Technical issue: MAP calculation in Eq. 8 is problematic — ranking the entire vocabulary is unnecessary and a natural cut-off should be considered.

- Eq. 8 does not define a probability as claimed and the explanation should be revised to remove probability references.

- Minor copyedit: Line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
