- Paper proposes a joint embedding model for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manual ontology (UMLS, YAGO) and represents concepts by multiple phrases.
- Authors evaluate primarily on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks and create a new general-domain concept similarity dataset via Mechanical Turk, reporting results comparable to prior work.

Strengths:
- Model is straightforward and offers a configurable balance between treating phrases as atomic units and modeling their compositionality.
- Same compositional approach is applied to concepts represented as combinations of representative phrases.
- Work includes model development, creation of a new evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations and analyses.

Major weaknesses:
- Evaluation is limited to intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness), lacking extrinsic task evaluations to demonstrate downstream utility.
- Human judgments were elicited on presented phrases, so the datasets should be treated and analyzed as phrase-similarity datasets, not pure concept-similarity datasets.
- Medical evaluation datasets are problematic: mini MayoSRS is extremely small (29 pairs), MayoSRS is small with low annotator agreement, and UMNSRS only covers single-word concepts—limiting relevance to phrase and general concept representations.
- Hyperparameters were heavily tuned on the very datasets used for evaluation, undermining the validity of reported comparisons and results.
- Claim of requiring less manual annotation is weakened because the method still depends on large manually-constructed ontologies and prior annotated clinical data often came from existing records.
- Paper lacks deeper analysis explaining numerical results; it conflates phrase-word relationships with concept-phrase relationships without dedicated investigation.
- Missing analysis of how hyperparameters control the atomic vs compositional tradeoff and of NLP-specific phenomena (e.g., phrase headword influence).

Recommendation:
- Reviewer recommends rejection and advises improving evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission.
