- Strength: Proposes a conceptually sound joint embedding model bridging atomic phrase representations and compositionality, including multi-phrase concepts.
- Strength: Substantial effort demonstrated through model development, a new evaluation dataset, and extensive analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation uses only intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, which poorly predict real-world performance; extrinsic tasks are needed.
- Weakness: Similarity datasets are labeled via human judgments on phrases, so they are phrase similarity datasets, not true concept-similarity benchmarks.
- Weakness: Medical evaluation sets are small with low inter-annotator agreement (mini MayoSRS, MayoSRS), and UMNSRS covers only single-word concepts, limiting relevance to phrase/concept models.
- Weakness: Extensive hyperparameter tuning on evaluation datasets raises serious overfitting concerns and undermines result validity.
- Weakness: Claim of reduced manual annotation is unconvincing because the method still depends on large manually-constructed ontologies and prior work used existing clinical records.
- Weakness: Lacks in-depth analysis of underlying mechanisms (e.g., headword influence, distinct phrase-word vs. concept-phrase behavior) and systematic study of hyperparameters controlling atomic vs. compositional tradeoffs.

- General: Reviewer recommends rejection due to methodological weaknesses and overfitting risk; authors should add extrinsic evaluations, clarify dataset nature, justify tuning strategy, and provide deeper analyses before resubmission.
