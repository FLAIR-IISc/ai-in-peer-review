- Proposes extending word embeddings to phrases and concepts by assigning unique identifiers to equivalent word/phrase/concept sets and replacing them in the training corpus.  
- Trains embeddings on a combined corpus of the original text and the identifier-tagged corpus.  
- Derives concept/phrase/word sets from existing ontologies, focusing on biomedical ontologies and corpora.  
- Introduces a new test dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world biomedical entities—a valuable, novel contribution.  
- Paper is well-written and the technique is intuitively sound but the contribution is somewhat incremental.  
- Scope is limited by exclusive focus on the biomedical domain.  
- Recommends providing more discussion of the characteristics and potential uses of the new test resource, as it may be the most significant outcome.  
- Technical issue: Equation 8 (MAP calculation) is presented with a probabilistic interpretation though it does not define a probability distribution; remove probabilistic language.  
- Minor typo: change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have" on line 556.
