- Proposes extending word embeddings by collapsing lexically distinct but semantically equivalent phrases/words/concepts into a single ontology-derived identifier, replacing occurrences in the corpus, and training on the tagged + original corpora (biomedical domain).  
- Introduces a new test dataset for word similarity/relatedness targeted at real-world biomedical entities; this dataset may be the paper’s most significant contribution and needs more discussion.  
- Paper is well-written and the technique is intuitive but the overall contribution feels incremental.  
- Evaluation is limited to the biomedical domain; broader-domain evaluation or discussion is needed to show generality.  
- Technical issue in Eq. 8: MAP should use a cutoff rather than ranking the entire vocabulary, and the equation as written does not define a probability distribution—remove probabilistic language.  
- Minor typo: change “most concepts has” to “most concepts have” (line 556).
