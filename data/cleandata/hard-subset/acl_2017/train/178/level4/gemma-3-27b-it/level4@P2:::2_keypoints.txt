- Joint embedding framework: adapts skip-gram to jointly learn embeddings for concepts, phrases (as atomic symbols), and words to better represent multi-word and non-compositional semantic units.
- Distant supervision via phrase mapping: uses ontology-derived mappings from concepts to representative phrases in unlabeled corpora as a weak supervision signal to train concept embeddings.
- Empirical validation & dataset release: evaluates on concept similarity/relatedness using UMLS and YAGO and releases a novel dataset plus code for reproducibility.
- Addresses a relevant problem: tackles representing concepts beyond lexical forms and handling non-compositional phrases.
- Competitive performance: shows comparable or better results than existing methods on larger datasets, supporting the joint embedding approach.
- Resource availability: provides software and a new evaluation dataset, aiding reproducibility and use by others.
- Limited novelty: core technique is mainly an adaptation of skip-gram, with limited algorithmic innovation beyond the application and supervision strategy.
- Full model underperforms simpler baselines: the full model with β and η does not consistently beat simpler methods on several datasets, questioning added complexity.
- Imprecise “supervision” terminology: calling phrase occurrences supervision is unclear and doesn’t match standard definitions of supervision.
- Unused notation: introduced symbols (EW, EP, EC) are not used later, adding unnecessary complexity.
- Counterintuitive hyperparameter values: cross-validation yields very low or zero β values in some corpora, suggesting issues with parameterization or modeling of compositionality.
- Ambiguous evaluation methodology: unclear how similarity/relatedness is computed when concepts map to multiple phrases—whether concept vectors alone or phrase embeddings are used.
- Questionable utility of ontology: averaging phrase embeddings (APPROXPHRco) performing best raises doubt about the need for explicit concept embeddings and the ontology’s added value.
- Q1 to authors: justify using global β and η for compositionality—why assume a uniform factor across all phrases/concepts?
- Q2 to authors: clarify how similarity scores are computed when concepts have multiple associated phrases (use of concept vs. phrase vectors).
- Q3 to authors: given APPROXPHRco’s superiority, justify the need for explicit concept embeddings and what they add beyond phrase-based approximations.
- Q4 to authors: clarify the exact nature of the “supervision” from phrase mappings—simple co-occurrence or a more structured learning objective?
