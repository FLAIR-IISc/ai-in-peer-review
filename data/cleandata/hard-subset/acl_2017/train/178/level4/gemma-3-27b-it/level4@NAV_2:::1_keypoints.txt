- Proposes extending word embeddings to jointly represent phrases and concepts by replacing phrase/word occurrences with a shared identifier and training on original + tagged corpora.  
- Concept/phrase/word sets are extracted from existing ontologies and applied to biomedical corpora.  
- Authors create a novel test dataset for similarity/relatedness of real-world entities (primary contribution).  
- Paper is well-written and the technique is intuitive but offers limited methodological novelty.  
- Focus on biomedical domain limits generalizability and broader impact.  
- Empirical results are competitive but do not show clear, substantial improvement over existing methods.  
- Equation 8 is technically incorrect: it does not define a probability distribution as stated.  
- Minor grammatical error: line 556 should read “most concepts have” not “most concepts has.”  
- Method builds on established techniques (word2vec, distant supervision) and lacks a significant algorithmic advance.  
- The evaluation’s domain specificity raises questions about transferability to other domains.  
- The new dataset may be the most significant contribution and requires more detailed discussion.  
- Questions for authors: applicability beyond biomed, Mechanical Turk setup and filtering for the dataset, and computational cost (training time/memory) compared to baselines.
