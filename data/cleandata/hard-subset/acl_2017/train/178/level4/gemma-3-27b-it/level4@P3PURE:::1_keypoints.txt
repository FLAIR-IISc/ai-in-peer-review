- Method: extends word embeddings to phrases/concepts by assigning unique identifiers to equivalent phrase/word/concept groups, replacing occurrences to create a tagged corpus, and training on combined original+tagged data.
- Data source/application: concept/phrase/word sets come from an existing ontology; experiments focus on biomedical corpora and ontologies.
- Evaluation resource: authors introduce a novel test dataset for biomedical entity similarity/relatedness, which may be the paper's most significant contribution and warrants more discussion.
- Writing and novelty: paper is well-written and technique is intuitive, but the contribution is incremental.
- Evaluation scope: experiments are limited to the biomedical domain, restricting generality.
- Technical concern: Eq. 8 (MAP) should use a natural cutoff instead of ranking the entire vocabulary and must not be presented as a probability distribution; remove probabilistic interpretation and revise explanation.
- Minor correction: change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have" (line 556).
