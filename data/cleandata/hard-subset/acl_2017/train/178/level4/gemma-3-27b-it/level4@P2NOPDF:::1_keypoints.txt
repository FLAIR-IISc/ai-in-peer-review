- Proposes creating unified embeddings by replacing synonymous/related words, phrases, and concepts (from an ontology) with a single identifier in a “tagged” corpus, then training on tagged + original corpora.  
- Method is demonstrated in the biomedical domain using domain-specific corpora and ontologies.  
- Introduces a new biomedical-focused test dataset for word similarity and relatedness.  
- Paper is well-written and the technique is intuitively sound.  
- Contribution is incremental rather than highly novel.  
- Evaluation is limited to biomedical data, limiting generalizability.  
- Recommends expanding the discussion and analysis of the new test dataset (may be the paper’s main contribution).  
- Technical issue: Equation 8 (MAP-like calculation) is not a true probability—remove probabilistic interpretation and clarify the metric.  
- Suggestion: use a natural cutoff for ranking instead of ranking the entire vocabulary.  
- Minor editorial correction: change “most concepts has” to “most concepts have” (line 556).
