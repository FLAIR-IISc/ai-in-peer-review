- Paper proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manually constructed ontology (UMLS, YAGO).
- Applied to medical (UMLS) and general (YAGO) domains and evaluated primarily on intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks.
- Authors introduce a new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset created via Mechanical Turk and plan to release it.
- Reported results are comparable to prior work rather than clearly superior.

- Strength: The joint embedding model is simple, well-motivated, and balances treating phrases/concepts as atomic versus compositional.
- Strength: The paper presents substantial work covering model development, a new dataset, and multiple evaluations and analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic downstream evaluations.
- Weakness: The similarity/relatedness evaluations are based on phrase judgments, so they evaluate phrase similarity rather than true concept similarity.
- Weakness: The medical evaluation datasets are problematic—mini MayoSRS is tiny (29 pairs), MayoSRS has low annotator agreement, and UMNSRS is restricted to single-word concepts—these limitations need explicit discussion.
- Weakness: Heavy hyperparameter tuning on the same datasets used for evaluation undermines result validity.
- Weakness: Claim of reduced manual annotation is weak because the method depends on large, manually curated ontologies, while prior work used clinical records without extra annotation.
- Weakness: Lack of in-depth analyses distinguishing phrase-word compositionality from concept-phrase relationships and absence of analysis of hyperparameter trade-offs.
- Recommendation: Given these weaknesses, the reviewer recommends rejection and urges addressing datasets and evaluation methodology before resubmission.
