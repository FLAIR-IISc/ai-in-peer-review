This paper presents a method for extending word embedding techniques to encompass representations for phrases and concepts alongside their corresponding words. The approach involves assigning a unique identifier to sets of words, phrases, and concepts representing the same underlying entity, replacing these instances with the identifier within the training corpus to create a "tagged" corpus. This tagged corpus is then combined with the original corpus for training the embedding model. The concept/phrase/word sets are derived from existing ontologies, with the study focusing on biomedical applications and utilizing relevant corpora and ontologies within that domain. A notable aspect of this work is the creation of a new test dataset specifically designed for evaluating word similarity and relatedness for real-world biomedical entities – a valuable and novel contribution.

Overall, the paper is well-written and the proposed technique is intuitively sound, although its contribution feels incremental. The scope of the work is somewhat constrained by its exclusive focus on the biomedical domain. Further discussion regarding the characteristics and potential uses of the newly generated test resource would be beneficial, as this resource may ultimately represent the most significant aspect of the research.

There is a minor technical issue in the presentation. Specifically, Equation 8, intended to define the Mean Average Precision (MAP) calculation, does not represent a probability distribution, even with an infinite vocabulary size. The accompanying explanation should be revised to remove probabilistic interpretations. 

Additionally, a small correction is needed: on line 556, "most concepts has" should be changed to "most concepts have".
