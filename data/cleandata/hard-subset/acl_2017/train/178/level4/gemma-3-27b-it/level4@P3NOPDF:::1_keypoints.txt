- Extends word embeddings to include phrases and ontology-linked concepts by consolidating synonyms into a single identifier appended to the corpus.  
- Uses biomedical corpora/ontologies and introduces a new biomedical word similarity/relatedness test dataset.  
- Paper is well-written and intuitively sound but the contribution is incremental rather than transformative.  
- Domain focus on biomedical data limits generalizability and perceived impact.  
- Equation 8 is flawed: it’s not a probability (it's a count/ratio); the probabilistic framing is misleading.  
- Recommend removing probabilistic interpretation of Eq.8 and using rank-based, thresholded precision, or a finite cutoff.  
- Expand discussion of the new dataset: creation process, inter-annotator agreement, dataset characteristics, use cases, and public release.  
- Discuss how the method could be adapted to other domains and challenges with different ontologies/corpora.  
- Strengthen motivation by better arguing why existing embedding methods are insufficient for biomedical concepts and phrases.  
- Minor typo: line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
