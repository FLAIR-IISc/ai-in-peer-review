- Paper proposes a joint embedding model for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manual ontology (UMLS/YAGO), evaluated in medical and general domains.  
- A new general-domain concept (phrase) similarity and relatedness dataset was created via Mechanical Turk and will be publicly released.  
- Results are comparable to existing methods on intrinsic similarity and relatedness benchmarks.  
- Strength: Model is well-motivated and balances treating phrases as atomic units versus compositional structure, extending this to concepts with multiple representative phrases.  
- Strength: Substantial contribution covering model development, dataset creation, and thorough evaluations and analyses.  
- Weakness: Evaluation relies exclusively on intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness), limiting insight into downstream application utility.  
- Weakness: Annotations were made at the phrase level, so the datasets are more accurately phrase-similarity datasets rather than true concept-similarity datasets.  
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are small (mini MayoSRS: 29 pairs; MayoSRS: 101 pairs with low inter-annotator agreement) and UMNSRS focuses only on single-word concepts, limiting relevance for phrase/concept representation evaluation.  
- Weakness: Extensive hyperparameter tuning on evaluation datasets raises concerns about the validity of reported results and comparisons.  
- Weakness: Claim of reduced manual annotation effort is unconvincing given reliance on large, manually-constructed ontologies and prior work’s use of clinical records with less dedicated annotation.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper analysis of what drives results; treating phrase-word and concept-phrase relationships equivalently is potentially oversimplified and needs separate investigation.  
- Suggested analyses: NLP study of phrase-word relations (e.g., headword influence) and exploration of hyperparameters controlling the atomic vs compositional tradeoff.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends rejection until evaluation concerns and methodological analyses are addressed.
