- Paper proposes a weakly-supervised framework to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using text corpora plus a manually-constructed ontology.
- Method applied to medical (UMLS) and general (YAGO) domains, with evaluation against baselines on intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks.
- Authors introduce a new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset collected via Mechanical Turk and plan to release it.
- Contribution: novel joint embedding framework allowing configurable balance between atomic and compositional representations (β, η).
- Contribution: release of a new general-domain evaluation dataset with described creation process.
- Contribution: extensive experiments across corpora, hyperparameters, and baselines, including analysis of embedding structure relative to ontologies.
- Strength: sound and well-motivated conceptual framework leveraging structured knowledge without heavy manual annotation.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental setup covering medical and general domains and multiple baselines.
- Strength: valuable dataset contribution that fills a gap for general-domain concept similarity/relatedness evaluation.
- Strength: insightful analysis showing how embeddings preserve ontological links and relationships among concepts, phrases, and words.
- Weakness: evaluation is mostly intrinsic (similarity/relatedness); lacks extrinsic downstream task evaluation to show practical utility.
- Weakness: general-domain human judgments were collected at the phrase level, so reported results reflect phrase rather than concept similarity.
- Weakness: medical datasets are problematic—mini MayoSRS is too small, MayoSRS has low inter-rater agreement, and UMNSRS uses single-word concept representations.
- Weakness: extensive hyperparameter tuning on test sets risks overfitting and inflating reported performance.
- Weakness: analysis lacks deeper exploration of how β and η affect compositionality and specific error cases.
- Weakness: claim of reduced manual annotation is weakened because large pre-existing ontologies required still embody substantial manual effort.
- Question: include at least one extrinsic evaluation (e.g., entity linking or relation extraction) to demonstrate practical applicability?
- Question: report phrase-level results separately to reflect how the general-domain evaluation was constructed and facilitate comparison with phrase embedding methods?
- Question: clarify and justify hyperparameter tuning procedure and report results on a held-out development set to avoid tuning-on-test concerns?
- Question: provide detailed compositionality analysis of β and η with illustrative examples showing impact on embedding quality?
