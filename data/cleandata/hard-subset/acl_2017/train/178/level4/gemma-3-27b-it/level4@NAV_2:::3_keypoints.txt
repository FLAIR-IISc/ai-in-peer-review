- Paper proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and ontology-linked concepts, evaluated on UMLS and YAGO.
- Strength: Simple, configurable model balancing atomic phrase treatment and compositionality, extended to multi-phrase concepts.
- Strength: Substantial experimental work including a new general-domain concept similarity dataset to be released.
- Weakness: Evaluation uses only intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, limiting evidence of downstream utility.
- Weakness: Similarity annotations were collected at the phrase level, so datasets represent phrase similarity rather than true concept similarity.
- Weakness: Medical evaluation sets are small with low inter-annotator agreement; UMNSRS is limited to single-word concepts.
- Weakness: Extensive hyperparameter tuning on evaluation datasets raises validity concerns about reported results.
- Weakness: Claim of reduced manual annotation advantage is weak given reliance on large, manual ontologies and prior access to clinical records.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of internal model behavior and of differences between phrase–component and concept–phrase relationships.
- Weakness: Need analysis of hyperparameters controlling atomic vs. compositional trade-offs and NLP-specific study of phrase-component relations.
- Question: Why was a 0–100 similarity scale chosen for the human annotation task?
- Question: Which task/dataset was used for hyperparameter tuning and can development trends be shown?
- Question: How are overlapping phrases handled during preprocessing?
- Question: In Equation 1, why use symbol 'c' instead of 'o', which creates confusion?
- Question: Clarify the negative sampling matrix dimensions (|W| x k vs. |W| x d) and the term “negative sampling matrix.”
- Question: Clarify training of words in the joint model—does the model only train the last word of a phrase?
- Additional comment: Replace “contexts” with “concepts” (line 069) and consistently use “phrases” instead of “strings” (line 430).
- Additional comment: Missing Pedersen et al. (2007) reference (line 361) and add reference to Table 5 (line 535).
- Additional comment: Table 3’s unexpected PM+CL behavior needs further investigation and discussion.
- Recommendation: Reject; authors should improve evaluation methodology, add extrinsic tasks, address dataset and analysis issues, and clarify technical details before resubmission.
