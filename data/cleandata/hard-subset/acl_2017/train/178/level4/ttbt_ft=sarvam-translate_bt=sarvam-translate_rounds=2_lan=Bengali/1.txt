This paper describes an extension of the word embedding method that also provides representation of phrases and concepts that are consistent with words. This method works by assigning an identifier to a group of phrases, words, and concepts that represent this concept, replacing the presence of phrases and words by this identifier in the training corpus, creating a "tagged" corpus, and then adding the tagged corpus to the main corpus for training. The concept/phrase/word sets are taken from an ontology. Since the application field is biomedical, related corpus and ontology are used. Researchers have also reported creating a new test dataset for word similarity and relationality for real-world entities, which is novel.

In general, the paper is well-written. The technique is quite natural, although it is not a very significant contribution. The scope of contribution is limited due to the evaluation being restricted to the biomedical domain. Further discussion is needed regarding the test resource generated. This resource could be the paper's real attractive contribution.

There is a small technical problem here, but it is probably more of a matter of mathematical expression than implementation.
