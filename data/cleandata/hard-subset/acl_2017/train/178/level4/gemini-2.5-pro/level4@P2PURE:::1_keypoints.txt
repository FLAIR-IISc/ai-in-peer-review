- Method: extend word embeddings to represent phrases/concepts by using an ontology to group synonymous words/phrases, replace them with a unique identifier, and train on the original plus tagged corpus.  
- Domain/application: method evaluated in the biomedical domain using relevant corpora and ontologies.  
- Resource: authors created a novel test dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and the technique is intuitive.  
- Weakness: technical contribution is somewhat incremental.  
- Limitation: evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data, constraining the scope of the contribution.  
- Recommendation: the new test resource is likely the paper’s most significant contribution and needs more discussion on its generation and potential uses.  
- Technical issue: Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) as written does not define a valid probability distribution; claims that it represents a probability should be removed and a cutoff over the vocabulary could be considered.  
- Minor correction: line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
