- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly learn embeddings for words, multi-word phrases, and concepts using ontologies as distant supervision.  
- Extends skip-gram with negative sampling to learn three embedding sets (words, phrases, concepts) where phrase context updates all three.  
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain corpora, showing competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.  
- Releases a new human-annotated dataset for general-domain entity similarity and relatedness.  
- Dataset collected via crowdsourcing with validation and inter-annotator agreement analysis.  
- Method is scalable and avoids costly manual annotations, enabling much larger concept vocabulary coverage than prior supervised approaches.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation across domains, multiple corpora, strong baselines, and analyses (compositionality, semantic-type clustering).  
- Limited technical novelty: primarily an adaptation of skip-gram and established ontology-based distant supervision.  
- Concept embeddings (CONCEPTco) often underperform the simpler phrase-average baseline (APPROXPHRco), questioning the utility of a separate concept layer.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; no downstream/extrinsic task experiments to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Question: why do CONCEPTco embeddings not clearly outperform averaged phrase embeddings—does the EC layer add limited value?  
- Question: consider using MAP@k instead of ranking against the entire vocabulary to reduce noise and computational cost.  
- Question: clarify the probability notation Pr(c|Cp, V) used in Equation 8.  
- Minor correction: change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have".
