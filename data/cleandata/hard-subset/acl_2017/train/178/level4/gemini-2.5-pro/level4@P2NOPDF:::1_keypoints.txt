- Proposes extending word embeddings to multi-word phrases/concepts by using an ontology to group lexical variants under unique concept identifiers.
- Creates a "tagged" training corpus by replacing occurrences of phrases/words with concept IDs, appends it to the original corpus, and trains embeddings on the combined text.
- Applies the method to the biomedical domain using domain-specific corpora and ontologies.
- Releases a new biomedical-focused test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness.
- Paper is well-written; the technique is intuitive and clearly explained.
- Method is a straightforward, incremental extension of existing embedding techniques.
- Evaluation is limited to the biomedical domain, constraining the generality of findings.
- Recommends a more detailed discussion and analysis of the new test resource to highlight its value.
- Technical issue: Equation 8 is incorrectly described as a probability (it does not sum to one); also suggest using MAP@k instead of ranking the entire vocabulary.
- Minor correction: change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have".
