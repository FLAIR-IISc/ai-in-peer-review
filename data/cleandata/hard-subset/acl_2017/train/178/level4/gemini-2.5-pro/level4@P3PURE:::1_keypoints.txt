- Proposes extending word embeddings to multi-word phrases/concepts by mapping synonymous terms to unique ontology-derived concept identifiers.  
- Creates a "tagged" corpus by replacing all occurrences of synonymous terms with their concept IDs and appends it to the original corpus for embedding training.  
- Demonstrates and evaluates the approach in the biomedical domain using domain-specific corpora and ontologies.  
- Introduces a novel test dataset for word similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.  
- Paper is well-written and the technique is intuitive.  
- Core methodological contribution is incremental rather than substantial.  
- Evaluation restricted to the biomedical domain, limiting generalizability and perceived scope.  
- Requests a more detailed discussion of the newly generated test resource, which may be the paper’s most significant contribution.  
- Technical concern: Equation 8 is incorrectly presented as a probability distribution — it does not sum to one and should not be described as a probability.  
- Suggests defining a practical ranking cut-off rather than scoring the entire vocabulary.  
- Minor correction: line 556 should read "most concepts have" instead of "most concepts has."
