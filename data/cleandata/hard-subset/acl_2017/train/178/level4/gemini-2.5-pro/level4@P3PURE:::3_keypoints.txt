- Presents a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using text plus ontologies (UMLS, YAGO); new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset via Mechanical Turk; results comparable to prior work.

- Strength: Model is simple and conceptually sound, offering a tunable middle ground between atomic and compositional phrase representations and applying the same idea to concepts.

- Strength: Substantial effort—model development, creation of a new evaluation dataset, and a range of experiments and analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation is restricted to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic downstream evaluations to demonstrate practical utility.

- Weakness: Datasets are presented as measuring concept similarity but annotators likely judged phrases, so the data should be treated as phrase similarity instead.

- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are limited—mini MayoSRS is tiny, MayoSRS is small with low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS covers only single-word concepts, limiting relevance for multi-word/complex concepts.

- Weakness: Hyperparameters were tuned on the same datasets used for final evaluation, invalidating direct comparisons to prior work and undermining result reliability.

- Weakness: Claim of requiring less manual annotation is unconvincing because the method depends on large manually constructed ontologies and prior work used existing clinical records rather than new annotations.

- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper qualitative/linguistic analysis of phrase-word vs concept-phrase composition and of hyperparameters controlling the atomic–compositional trade-off.

- Recommendation: Due to significant evaluation and methodology issues, the reviewer recommends rejection and advises strengthening datasets and evaluation before resubmission.
