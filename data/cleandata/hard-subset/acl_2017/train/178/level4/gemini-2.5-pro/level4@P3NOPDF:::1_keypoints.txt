- Method: replace words/phrases with ontology-derived concept IDs, append tagged corpus to original, and train embeddings to produce concept vectors.  
- Evaluation and contribution: demonstrated in the biomedical domain and released a new test dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clear, and well-motivated.  
- Strength: the approach is intuitive, straightforward, and effective for learning concept embeddings.  
- Strength: the manually curated test set is a valuable community resource and may be more impactful than the method.  
- Weakness: novelty is limited—method is an incremental extension of existing embedding techniques via corpus-level entity normalization.  
- Weakness: evaluation is confined to biomedicine and lacks discussion of generalizability to other domains or noisy knowledge sources (e.g., DBpedia, Wikidata).  
- Weakness: the new test resource is under-described; the paper should provide more detail on its creation, characteristics, and applications.  
- Technical: Equation 8 is incorrectly described as a probability; it should be framed as a scoring function for ranking, and the authors should justify ranking the full vocabulary versus using an MAP cutoff.  
- Minor correction: Line 556 — change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have."
