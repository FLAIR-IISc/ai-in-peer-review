This paper presents an approach for extending word embedding methods to generate vector representations for phrases and concepts in addition to individual words. The technique involves using an ontology to identify sets of synonymous words and phrases that denote a single concept. A unique identifier is assigned to each set, and these identifiers are used to replace the corresponding terms in the training corpus, creating a "tagged" corpus. This tagged corpus is then concatenated with the original corpus to train the embedding model. The application is demonstrated within the biomedical field, employing domain-specific corpora and ontologies. Furthermore, the authors report on the development of a novel test dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness between real-world entities.

Overall, the paper is well-presented, and the proposed technique is intuitive. However, the core methodological contribution is incremental rather than substantial. The scope of this contribution is also constrained by the evaluation being focused exclusively on the biomedical domain.

The paper would benefit from a more extensive discussion of the new test dataset, as this resource may ultimately be the most significant contribution of the work.

One minor technical issue was noted, which appears to be a matter of mathematical formalization rather than an implementation flaw.

**Technical problem:**
Regarding Equation 8, which defines the Mean Average Precision (MAP) calculation: The formula as presented does not define a probability distribution. This can be easily shown. The accompanying text should therefore be revised to remove the description of it as a probability. As a side note, considering a natural cut-off for the ranking would be more conventional than ranking against the entire vocabulary.
