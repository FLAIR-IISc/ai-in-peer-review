- Paper proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using plain text plus a hand-crafted ontology (UMLS, YAGO).  
- Authors introduce a new crowdsourced general-domain concept similarity dataset and plan to release it.  
- Reported performance is comparable to prior methods on intrinsic similarity and relatedness tasks.  

Strengths:
- Model is intuitive and well-motivated, allowing a configurable balance between atomic and compositional phrase representations.  
- The composition principle is extended to concepts as compositions of representative phrases.  
- Substantial work: model development, new evaluation resource, and comprehensive experiments/analyses.  

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation is limited to intrinsic tasks; no extrinsic downstream evaluation is provided.  
- Datasets may measure phrase similarity rather than true concept similarity because annotators saw phrases, not concept definitions.  
- Medical benchmarks are limited: mini MayoSRS is very small (29 pairs), MayoSRS is small (101 pairs) with low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS covers only single-word concepts—this undermines relevance for multi-word/concept evaluation.  
- Major methodological issue: hyperparameters were tuned on the same datasets used for final results and comparisons, invalidating reported claims.  
- Claim of requiring less manual annotation is weak because the method depends on large, manually constructed ontologies; prior work used existing clinical records rather than dedicated annotation.  
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of model behavior, notably distinct treatment and linguistic analysis of phrase-word vs. concept-phrase composition and the effect of hyperparameters controlling atomic vs. compositional tradeoffs.  

Recommendation:
- Due to significant evaluation and methodological weaknesses, reviewer recommends rejection and thorough revision of the evaluation framework before resubmission.
