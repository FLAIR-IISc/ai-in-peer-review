- Proposes a weakly-supervised joint embedding model for words, multi-word phrases, and concepts by linking corpus phrases to an ontology and extending skip-gram with negative sampling.  
- Introduces hyperparameters (β, η) to trade off between treating phrases/concepts as atomic vs. compositional.  
- Releases a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset (people, places, organizations).  

- Elegant, intuitive adaptation of skip-gram that learns multi-word expressions as single tokens and injects structured knowledge via distant supervision.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation across biomedical and general domains using varied corpora and multiple baselines.  
- The new similarity/relatedness dataset fills a gap for entity-level/concept-level evaluation resources.  

- Evaluation limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, which weakly predict downstream utility, and hyperparameters were tuned on the same datasets used for evaluation (risk of over-optimistic results).  
- Biomedical evaluation datasets are problematic: very small size, poor inter-annotator agreement, and some datasets use single-word concepts that misalign with the model’s focus on multi-word phrases.  
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of results (e.g., causes of bi-modal phrase–word relationships) and limited investigation of how β and η affect the embedding space beyond final scores.  
- Claim of reduced supervision is overstated because the method relies on large, manually-built ontologies, and some prior work used existing labels without dedicated annotation efforts.  

- Clarify handling of overlapping phrase matches during preprocessing (which phrase is chosen).  
- Rename “negative sampling matrix” for ENS to a clearer term like “context embedding matrix.”  
- Clarify training procedure for words inside/outside identified phrases and how phrase embeddings interact with word embeddings in the sliding window.  
- Fix notation inconsistency in Equation 1 where c denotes both context and concept.  
- Add missing bibliography entry for “Pedersen et al., 2007.”  
- Clarify whether final reported results are averages over 5-fold CV runs or from models retrained on full data with selected hyperparameters.  
- Discuss why PM+CL corpus optimal hyperparameters (β=0.0, η=0.0) disable compositionality, unlike other corpora.  
- Include an explicit reference to Table 5 in the main text when discussing real-world entity results.
