- Presents a weakly-supervised method that tags phrases with ontology IDs in a corpus and trains a modified skip-gram to jointly embed words, phrases, and ontology concepts (evaluated on UMLS and YAGO).
- Contributions: releases a new real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset and proposes an intuitive distant-supervision joint embedding method for concepts and phrases.
- Strength: provides a valuable new dataset addressing the gap beyond single-word benchmarks.
- Strength: paper is clearly written, well-structured, and details the method and experiments thoroughly.
- Strength: method is simple, natural, and achieves competitive similarity/relatedness results without heavy supervision.
- Weakness: limited technical novelty—approach is an incremental adaptation of skip-gram rather than a new learning algorithm.
- Weakness: evaluation is skewed toward the biomedical domain (UMLS); more analysis of general-domain (WIKINYT/YAGO) embeddings is needed.
- Weakness: technical inaccuracy in describing MAP/Equation (8)—precision-at-k is mislabeled as a probability and should be clarified.
- Question: please clarify Equation (8)/MAP terminology—was the probability wording intentional or should it be described as precision-at-k?
- Question: please elaborate on the new dataset’s properties and annotator difficulties, given the Fleiss’ κ=0.24 indicating only fair agreement.
