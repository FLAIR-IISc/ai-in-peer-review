- Proposes extending word embeddings to represent phrases/concepts by using an ontology to group synonyms and replacing them with unique concept IDs in the corpus.  
- Creates a "tagged" corpus of concept IDs and concatenates it with the original corpus to train embeddings.  
- Demonstrates the approach in the biomedical domain using domain-specific corpora and ontologies.  
- Introduces a novel test dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness between real-world entities.  
- Paper is well-presented and the technique is intuitive.  
- Core methodological contribution is incremental rather than substantial.  
- Evaluation is limited to the biomedical domain, constraining the scope and generality.  
- The manuscript should provide a more extensive discussion of the new test dataset, which may be its most significant contribution.  
- Technical issue: Equation 8 (MAP) is presented as a probability distribution but does not define one; revise wording and consider using a natural ranking cut-off instead of the full vocabulary.  
- Minor correction: line 556 "most concepts has" → "most concepts have".
