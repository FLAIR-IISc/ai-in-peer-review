- Model is straightforward and intuitive, finding a configurable middle ground between atomic phrase modeling and compositionality, and applying the same idea to concepts as compositions of representative phrases.
- Paper represents substantial work: model development, creation of a new general-domain evaluation dataset, and extensive evaluations and analyses.

- Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks any extrinsic downstream evaluation.
- Annotators were shown phrases, so the datasets measure phrase similarity, not true concept similarity as claimed.
- Medical evaluation datasets are problematic: mini MayoSRS is extremely small (29 pairs), MayoSRS is small (101 pairs) with low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS is limited to single-word terms—limiting relevance for multi-word concepts.
- Hyperparameters were tuned on the same test sets used for final results and comparisons, a flawed methodology that undermines result validity.
- Claim of superiority due to less manual annotation is unconvincing: the method depends on large manual ontologies, and cited prior work used existing clinical records rather than new dedicated annotation.
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of model behavior; it conflates phrase→word and concept→phrase composition without separate linguistic analysis and omits study of hyperparameters controlling atomic vs compositional trade-offs.

- Reviewer recommends rejection unless the authors address the significant evaluation and methodological weaknesses before resubmission.
