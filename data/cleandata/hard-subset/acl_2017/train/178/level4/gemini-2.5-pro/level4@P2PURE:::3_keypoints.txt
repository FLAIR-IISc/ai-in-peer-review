- Proposed joint embedding model is simple, configurable, and conceptually sound, interpolating between atomic and compositional phrase representations (and similarly for concepts).
- Paper reports substantial work: model development, a new Mechanical Turk concept/phrase dataset, and extensive experiments and analysis.
- Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks any extrinsic downstream evaluation.
- Annotators were shown phrases, not abstract concepts, so the datasets measure phrase similarity rather than concept similarity.
- Medical evaluation datasets are problematic: mini MayoSRS is tiny (29 pairs), MayoSRS has low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS is restricted to single-word concepts.
- Hyperparameters were tuned on the same datasets used for final evaluation, conflating development and test data and invalidating reported comparisons.
- Claim of requiring less manual annotation is unconvincing because the method depends on large, manually constructed ontologies and prior work used existing clinical records.
- Paper lacks deeper analysis of model behavior; word→phrase and phrase→concept relationships are treated similarly despite being fundamentally different and needing separate analyses (e.g., headword effects, hyperparameter tradeoffs).
- Reported results are only comparable to prior work, not demonstrably superior.
- Recommendation: reject unless evaluation methodology and datasets are substantially strengthened.
