- Paper proposes joint embeddings for words, phrases, and concepts using corpora plus ontologies (UMLS, YAGO), modeling concepts as compositions of representative phrases and evaluating on intrinsic similarity tasks; authors also release a new general-domain dataset.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple and elegant model that balances atomic vs. compositional representations and extends this idea to concepts.  
- Strength: Substantial engineering/experimental effort and release of a new crowdsourced evaluation dataset.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses only intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, offering poor evidence of downstream utility.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameters were tuned on the same datasets used for final evaluation, invalidating comparisons and likely inflating results.  
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are limited—mini MayoSRS is tiny (29 pairs), MayoSRS (101 pairs) has low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS is restricted to single-word concepts.  
- Weakness: New dataset is mischaracterized as measuring concept similarity when annotators judged phrases; it more accurately measures phrase similarity.  
- Weakness: Claim of superiority is unsupported—method still depends on large manually-curated ontologies and prior work’s annotations came from clinical records, not bespoke annotation.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper qualitative analysis (e.g., distinct treatment of phrase-word vs. concept-phrase relations, role of phrase headwords, and effects of hyperparameter trade-offs).  
- Recommendation: Reject; authors should add extrinsic evaluations and use proper hyperparameter tuning on held-out development sets before resubmission.
