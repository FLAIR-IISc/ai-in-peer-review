- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that jointly learns embeddings for words, phrases, and concepts by tagging corpus phrases with ontology identifiers and training an extended skip-gram model on both original and tagged text.  
- Releases a new human-annotated general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset addressing a gap in multi-word/entity benchmarks.  
- Method is practical and scalable, using distant supervision from ontologies to learn concept embeddings without manual annotation and achieving competitive results with greater vocabulary coverage.  
- Experiments are comprehensive across biomedical and general domains and include analyses of compositionality and semantic type clustering.  
- Core technical novelty is limited: the approach is a straightforward extension of skip-gram combined with distant supervision.  
- Evaluation is heavily weighted toward biomedical datasets, limiting assessment of general-domain generalizability.  
- A notation error: Equation 8 labels precision as a probability `Pr(...)`, which is mathematically incorrect and should be fixed.  
- Suggestion/question: consider using MAP@k rather than ranking against the entire vocabulary to reduce computational cost and sensitivity to irrelevant low-ranked items.  
- Minor editorial issue: grammatical error “most concepts has” should be “most concepts have.”
