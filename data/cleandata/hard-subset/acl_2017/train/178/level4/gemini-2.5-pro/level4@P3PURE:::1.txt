This paper presents a method for extending word embedding models to create representations for multi-word phrases and concepts. The approach leverages an external ontology to identify groups of words and phrases that denote the same concept. Each concept group is assigned a unique identifier, and a "tagged" corpus is created by replacing all occurrences of these synonymous terms with their corresponding identifier. This tagged corpus is then appended to the original corpus, and the combined text is used for training the embeddings. The method is demonstrated and evaluated within the biomedical domain, using domain-specific corpora and ontologies. The authors also report the creation of a novel test dataset for word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities.

Overall, the paper is well-written and the proposed technique is intuitive. However, the core methodological contribution appears incremental rather than substantial. The evaluation is focused exclusively on the biomedical domain, which limits the perceived scope of the contribution. A more detailed discussion of the newly generated test resource would be beneficial, as this resource may be the paper's most significant and lasting contribution.

There is one technical issue concerning the mathematical formalism, though it likely does not affect the implementation.

**Technical Concern:**

*   **Equation 8:** The authors use this equation to define the MAP calculation. However, the equation as written does not define a probability distribution. It is straightforward to show that it does not sum to one, and this holds even for an infinite vocabulary. The authors should revise the accompanying explanation to remove any claims that this equation represents a probability. Additionally, while not an error, it might be more practical to define a natural cut-off for the ranking rather than scoring the entire vocabulary.
