- Proposes joint embeddings for words, phrases, and ontology concepts by mapping ontology concepts to phrases, treating phrases as atomic, and adapting skip-gram; evaluated on UMLS and Yago.

- Addresses non-lexical semantic relatedness and non-compositional phrases by learning phrase embeddings as atomic units.

- Resulting embeddings are competitive with, and sometimes better than, prior methods on concept similarity tasks.

- Software release could be a useful resource for biomedical NLP researchers.

- Limited novelty: approach is largely an incremental change to the skip-gram model.

- Full model does not consistently beat baselines; Choi et al. outperforms on Mayo datasets and performance is only competitive on UMNSRS.

- The term "distant supervision" is potentially misleading and should be clarified.

- Some introduced notation (e.g., E_W) is never used and appears superfluous.

- Using a single global compositionality hyperparameter β is simplistic; optimal β=0 on some datasets undermines the compositionality motivation.

- Experimental setup for Table 4 is unclear—how phrase/word vectors are combined to yield a concept similarity score is not specified.

- Strong results from simple averaged-phrase vectors ("approximate phr") call into question the necessity of the joint concept-ontology training framework.
