- Paper presents a weakly-supervised method that jointly learns embeddings for words, multi-word phrases, and ontology concepts using distant supervision from ontologies and an adapted skip-gram with negative sampling; evaluated on biomedical and general corpora with a newly released dataset.

- Contribution: released a new crowdsourced general-domain dataset for entity similarity and relatedness benchmarking.

- Contribution: proposed a scalable weakly-supervised method that jointly learns word, phrase, and concept embeddings without manual annotation by leveraging ontology-based distant supervision.

- Strength: new dataset is a valuable community resource with a credible data-collection methodology.

- Strength: strong empirical performance on similarity/relatedness tasks, often matching or exceeding more supervised methods, with notably higher vocabulary coverage.

- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across domains, corpora, intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness, semantic clustering), and compositionality analysis.

- Strength: clear writing and planned release of code and data supporting reproducibility.

- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—essentially an incremental extension of skip-gram by maintaining three embedding matrices.

- Weakness: imprecise MAP formulation in Section 5.4—notation Pr(c|Cp,V) is misleading since it denotes precision, not a probability.

- Weakness: unclear rationale for training on two parallel corpora (untagged TU and tagged TT) instead of using only the tagged corpus.

- Question: please clarify the motivation and advantage of the parallel corpus training design and why word embeddings are trained on TU rather than solely on TT.

- Question: please consider rephrasing the MAP/precision notation (e.g., use “precision at item c” instead of Pr(c|Cp,V)) to improve clarity.
