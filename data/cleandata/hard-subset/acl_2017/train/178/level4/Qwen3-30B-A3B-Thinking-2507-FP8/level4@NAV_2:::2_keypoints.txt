- Paper proposes joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words by mapping concepts to representative phrases from an ontology and adapting skip-gram with negative sampling; evaluated on UMLS and YAGO.

- Strength: Addresses gap where semantically related concepts lack lexical overlap and non-compositional phrases exist.

- Strength: Provides a practical, annotation-free method using phrases as distant supervision signals.

- Strength: Demonstrates competitive performance on similarity and relatedness tasks and releases cui2vec implementation for biomedical NLP.

- Weakness: Method is largely an incremental adaptation of skip-gram rather than a fundamentally novel model.

- Weakness: Full model with concept embeddings does not consistently outperform simpler baselines (e.g., Choi baseline on MayoSRS/Mini Mayo SRS).

- Weakness: On larger datasets (UMNSRS) performance is only competitive with simpler methods like CHIU-2, questioning added complexity.

- Weakness: APPROXPHRco (approximating concepts via phrase embeddings) often outperforms the full concept-embedding model, weakening claims about the necessity of concept embeddings.

- Question: Clarify what the “distant supervision” signal actually is if phrases are merely treated as tokens in the corpus.

- Question: Explain why notation introduced in Section 3.2 (EW, EP, EC) is not used elsewhere in the paper.

- Question: Why include the β compositionality hyperparameter when Table 3 shows it is set to 0 for some models, suggesting compositionality is not beneficial?

- Question: Given APPROXPHRco’s strong results, justify the need for explicit concept embeddings versus using phrase embeddings directly.
