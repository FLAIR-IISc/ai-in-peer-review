- Paper proposes jointly embedding words, phrases, and concepts using ontology-derived representative phrases as distant supervision to train from unlabeled corpora.
- Strength: Addresses semantic gaps where lexical overlap is absent by jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words to capture relationships beyond word embeddings.
- Strength: Requires no manual concept annotation, enabling much larger vocabulary coverage (3x) compared to prior methods.
- Strength: Releases code (cui2vec) and a new dataset of entity similarity/relatedness judgments for the biomedical community.
- Weakness: Method is a minor adaptation of skip-gram/phrase-as-atomic-unit ideas and is not a fundamentally novel modeling contribution.
- Weakness: Does not consistently outperform simpler baselines; often only marginal gains or worse performance on smaller datasets.
- Weakness: Experimental methodology is unclear about handling many-to-many concept–phrase mappings, weakening the evaluation claims.
- Critique: Calling the approach “distant supervision” is misleading since no traditional supervision signal is provided; concept info only groups phrase embeddings.
- Critique: Notation introduced (EW, EP, EC) is unused later, adding unnecessary reader confusion.
- Critique: Hyperparameter β’s usage contradicts its stated role in controlling compositionality, with optimal β often zero, undermining the core motivation.
- Critique: A simple approximation (averaging phrase embeddings, APPROXPHRco) outperforms the proposed concept embeddings, questioning the need for ontology-based concept vectors.
