- Paper proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus a manually-constructed ontology (UMLS, YAGO) and reports intrinsic similarity/relatedness results comparable to prior work.  
- Strength: Model is simple and provides a configurable middle ground between treating phrases as atomic units and fully compositional representations; extends to concepts represented by multiple phrases.  
- Strength: Substantive contribution including model development, a new human-annotated general-domain dataset, and comprehensive evaluations/analyses.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks any extrinsic downstream evaluation to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Weakness: The annotation process yields phrase-level similarity judgments, so benchmarks measure phrase similarity rather than true concept similarity.  
- Weakness: Medical evaluation data are weak—mini MayoSRS is extremely small (29 pairs), MayoSRS is small (101 pairs) with low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS assesses single-word concepts not phrases.  
- Weakness: Extensive hyperparameter tuning on the evaluation datasets undermines the validity of reported results and comparisons.  
- Weakness: The claim of avoiding manual annotation is misleading because the method depends on large manually-curated ontologies; prior work used existing clinical records without dedicated annotation.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper behavioral analysis—no focused study on how phrase compositionality differs from concept–phrase relationships or the role of phrase headwords.  
- Weakness: Missing investigation of hyperparameters that control the atomic vs. compositional tradeoff and their effect on performance.  
- Recommendation: Due to these critical issues, the reviewer recommends rejection and asks for substantial improvements to evaluation methodology and datasets before resubmission.  
- Minor issues: Typos and clarifications needed (e.g., "contexts"→"concepts"; clarify phrase overlap handling; replace "strings" with "phrases"; Equation 1 ontology symbol; missing Pedersen et al. 2007 reference).  
- Minor issues: Clarify model details (matrix dimensions, "negative sampling matrix" wording, how words in phrases are trained) and specify exact hyperparameter tuning task (missing in appendix).  
- Minor issues: Annotation scale is overly fine-grained (1–100); report development-set trends for hyperparameter analyses (Table 3) and add a reference to Table 5.
