- Proposes extending word embeddings with phrase and concept representations via ontology-based identifier mapping, tagging the corpus, and concatenating tagged and original corpora for training in the biomedical domain.  
- Applies the approach to biomedical corpora and ontologies.  
- Introduces a new biomedical test dataset for word similarity and relatedness among real-world biomedical entities (presented as novel).  
- Paper is well-written and methodologically sound, but the technical contribution is modest and incremental.  
- The approach follows expected knowledge-enhanced embedding patterns and lacks fundamental algorithmic or theoretical novelty.  
- Narrow biomedical focus and lack of cross-domain validation limit broader applicability and relevance to ACL.  
- The new dataset is the paper’s strongest contribution but is under-detailed regarding construction, validation, coverage, and community utility.  
- Equation 8 incorrectly defines MAP as a probability (sums over the whole vocabulary without normalization); needs correction or clarification.  
- Minor language correction: Line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”  
- Recommended revisions: situate the method within prior work on knowledge-enhanced embeddings, expand dataset discussion, correct Equation 8, and more critically assess domain generalization.  
- Overall verdict: technically valid and acceptable but incremental and not compelling for ACL without the suggested revisions.
