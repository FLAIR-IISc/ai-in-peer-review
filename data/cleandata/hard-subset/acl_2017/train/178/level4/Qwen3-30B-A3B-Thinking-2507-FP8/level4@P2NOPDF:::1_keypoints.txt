- Proposes extending word embeddings by tagging phrases/words/concepts that share an ontology-based identifier and appending the tagged corpus to the original for training.  
- Applied in the biomedical domain using domain-specific corpora and ontologies.  
- Paper is well-written and conceptually straightforward but the methodological contribution is modest and incremental.  
- Evaluation is narrowly confined to the biomedical domain, limiting generalizability and broader applicability.  
- The newly created biomedical entity similarity/relatedness test dataset is a notable and potentially valuable contribution.  
- The dataset is underemphasized in the paper; it needs more detailed description of construction, coverage, utility, and comparisons to existing benchmarks.  
- Equation 8 (MAP) is unclear and mathematically problematic—should be clarified as a ranking-based score and constrained to a practical cutoff (e.g., top-k).  
- Recommend stronger discussion on adapting the method to other domains or justification for biomedical specificity.  
- Minor correction: "most concepts has" → "most concepts have."
