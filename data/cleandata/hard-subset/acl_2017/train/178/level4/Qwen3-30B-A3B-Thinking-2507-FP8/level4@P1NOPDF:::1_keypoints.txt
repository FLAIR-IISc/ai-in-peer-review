- Proposes extending word embeddings to phrases/concepts by assigning unique IDs to synonymous words/phrases/concepts and replacing them in the training corpus to create a tagged corpus appended to the original.
- Concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology; experiments and resources focus on the biomedical domain.
- Introduces a novel biomedical test dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness among real-world entities, a significant contribution.
- Paper is well-written and method is conceptually straightforward.
- Contribution is modest because the approach is narrowly focused on the biomedical domain and lacks broader validation.
- The test resource is the paper’s most valuable asset and warrants more discussion on construction, applications, and comparative utility.
- Technical issue: Equation 8 (MAP) is incorrectly described as a probability; the formulation should remove "probability" language and clarify the mathematical intent.
- Minor editorial correction: Line 556 "most concepts has" → "most concepts have."
