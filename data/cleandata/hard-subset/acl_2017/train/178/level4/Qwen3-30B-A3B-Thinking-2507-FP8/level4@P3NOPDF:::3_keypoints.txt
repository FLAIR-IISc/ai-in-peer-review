- Proposal: joint embedding model for words, phrases, and multi-phrase concepts using text corpora plus ontologies (UMLS/YAGO).  
- Strength: provides a configurable middle ground between atomic phrase representation and full compositional modeling; releases a new general-domain evaluation dataset.  
- Weakness: evaluates only on intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, missing extrinsic downstream evaluations that demonstrate utility.  
- Weakness: mischaracterizes medical evaluation sets as concept judgments when annotators rated phrases, invalidating concept-level comparisons to prior work.  
- Weakness: evaluation datasets are insufficient—mini MayoSRS (29 pairs) and MayoSRS (101 pairs) are underpowered; UMNSRS covers only single-word concepts and doesn’t test multi-phrase claims.  
- Critical methodological flaw: hyperparameters were tuned on the same evaluation datasets, creating optimistic bias and invalidating reported comparisons.  
- Weakness: claim of requiring less manual annotation is unsubstantiated—method depends on large ontologies, while prior work used existing clinical records.  
- Weakness: treats phrase and concept compositionality as analogous without analysis; missing investigations into headword effects and how atomic/compositional hyperparameters affect performance.  
- Recommendation: reject unless authors add extrinsic evaluations, reframe datasets correctly, use independent development sets for tuning, and provide much larger, robust evaluation data (e.g., ≥500 pairs).  
- Minor issues: numerous fixes needed (typos/wording, clarify phrase overlap handling and training targets, notation/equation fixes, add missing citation, use appropriate human annotation scales, report which task used for tuning, and include development-set hyperparameter ablation trends).
