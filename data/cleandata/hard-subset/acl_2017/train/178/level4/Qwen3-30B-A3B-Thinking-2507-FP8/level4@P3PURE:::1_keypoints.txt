- Proposes an extension to word embeddings that produces phrase/concept representations by grouping synonymous phrases/words/concepts from an ontology, assigning a unique identifier, replacing occurrences with that ID in the corpus, and appending the tagged corpus to the original for training.

- Uses biomedical corpora and ontologies for the domain-specific application.

- Introduces a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness on real-world biomedical entities — a significant contribution.

- Paper is well-written and conceptually straightforward.

- Contribution is modest because evaluation is restricted to the biomedical domain, limiting generalizability.

- The new test resource deserves deeper discussion and should be emphasized more prominently in the paper.

- Conceptual error in Equation 8: MAP is defined but incorrectly described as a probability; the expression does not form a probability distribution and the explanation must be revised.

- Minor grammatical correction: Line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
