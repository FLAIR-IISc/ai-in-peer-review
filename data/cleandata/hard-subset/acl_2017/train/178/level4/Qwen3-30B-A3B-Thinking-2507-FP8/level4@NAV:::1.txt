

The paper presents an extension of word embedding techniques to generate representations for phrases and concepts corresponding to words. The approach involves assigning a unique identifier to groups of phrases, words, and concepts denoting the same entity, replacing all occurrences of these elements with the identifier in the training corpus to form a "tagged" corpus, and appending this tagged corpus to the original for training. The concept/phrase/word groupings are derived from an ontology, with biomedical corpora and ontologies specifically utilized due to the domain focus. The authors additionally introduce a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness among real-world biomedical entities.  

Overall, the paper is well-written and the methodology is conceptually straightforward. However, the contribution is not highly substantial, as the scope is narrowly confined to the biomedical domain. The generated test resource represents a more significant potential contribution than the core method, and greater discussion of its utility would strengthen the paper. A minor technical issue exists: Equation 8 defines the MAP calculation. While the intent is sound, the formulation does not represent a probability (as easily demonstrated even with an infinite vocabulary), necessitating revision of the explanation to remove probabilistic terminology.
