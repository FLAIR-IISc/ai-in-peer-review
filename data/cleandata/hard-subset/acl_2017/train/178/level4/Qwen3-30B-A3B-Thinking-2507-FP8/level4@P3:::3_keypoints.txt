- Contributions: proposes a weakly-supervised joint embedding model balancing phrase compositionality and atomicity via β and η, introduces a new real-world entity similarity dataset, and shows competitive results on UMLS and YAGO without training-corpus annotation.  
- Strength: conceptually sound model offering a principled tradeoff between phrase atomicity and compositionality.  
- Strength: new Mechanical-Turk–collected dataset fills a gap for real-world entity similarity/relatedness with quality controls.  
- Strength: competitive intrinsic performance on benchmarks while avoiding manual corpus annotation for training.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks any extrinsic downstream evaluation to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Weakness: datasets are mischaracterized — human judgments target phrases, so results reflect phrase similarity, not necessarily concept-level embeddings.  
- Weakness: hyperparameters were tuned on the evaluation datasets (e.g., MayoSRS), violating standard evaluation practice and casting reported results into doubt.  
- Weakness: medical evaluation data have issues (Mini MayoSRS very small and low agreement; UMNSRS uses single-word concept representations inconsistent with multi-word focus).  
- Weakness: claim of “no human corpus annotation” is misleading because the method depends on manually constructed ontologies (UMLS/YAGO).  
- Question: how does the model handle overlapping phrases (e.g., "New York" vs. "New York City") given greedy phrase replacement?  
- Question: which specific evaluation tasks/datasets were used for hyperparameter tuning — please clarify for reproducibility.  
- Question: did the authors analyze whether phrase-level compositionality improves concept-level embeddings (e.g., for multi-word medical terms)?  
- Comment: Line 220: negative sampling matrix dimension should be |W| × d, not |W| × k.  
- Comment: Line 250: clarify the phrase "the observed phrase just completed" and how words are trained in the joint model.  
- Comment: Line 388: 0–100 similarity scale is overly fine-grained; a 0–10 scale would be more standard.  
- Comment: Table 3: explain why PM+CL hyperparameters (β=0.0, η=0.0) differ markedly from PM or CL alone.  
- Comment: Line 535: missing reference to Table 5.  
- Overall: promising approach but critical evaluation flaws (no extrinsic tasks, dataset mischaracterization, tuning on test sets) must be addressed before claims of utility are substantiated.
