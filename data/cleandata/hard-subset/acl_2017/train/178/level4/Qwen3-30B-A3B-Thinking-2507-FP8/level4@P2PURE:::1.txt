

The paper introduces an extension to word embedding methods that generates representations for phrases and concepts corresponding to words. The approach involves assigning a unique identifier to groups of phrases, words, and concepts denoting the same entity, replacing all occurrences of these elements with the identifier in the training corpus to create a "tagged" corpus, and appending this tagged corpus to the original for training. The concept/phrase/word groupings are derived from an ontology, with biomedical corpora and ontologies specifically employed given the domain. The authors also report the creation of a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness on real-world biomedical entities.

The paper is well-written and presents a conceptually straightforward technique. However, the contribution is modest in scope due to its narrowly scoped evaluation within the biomedical domain. The generated test resource represents the most significant potential contribution of the paper, and further discussion of its creation and utility would strengthen the work.

A minor technical issue concerns Equation 8, where the authors define the MAP calculation. While the idea is sound, the formulation does not constitute a probability, as it ranks the entire vocabulary rather than establishing a natural cut-off. The explanation should be revised to remove references to probability, as the expression is inherently non-probabilistic (this holds even for an infinite vocabulary).
