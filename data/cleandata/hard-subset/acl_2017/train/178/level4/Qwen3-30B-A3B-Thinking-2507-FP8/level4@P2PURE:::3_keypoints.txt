- Paper proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using text plus a manually constructed ontology (UMLS/YAGO), evaluated mainly on intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks.  
- Strength: Model is simple and conceptually sound, offering a configurable tradeoff between treating phrases as atomic units and modeling compositionality, and extending to concepts represented by multiple phrases.  
- Strength: Substantial work including model development, a new general-domain concept similarity dataset (via Mechanical Turk), and multiple evaluations/analyses.  
- Weakness: Evaluation uses only intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness), which have limited predictive power for downstream/extrinsic utility.  
- Weakness: Human annotations were collected on phrases, not concepts, so datasets are phrase-similarity datasets and should be analyzed accordingly.  
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are problematic—mini MayoSRS is tiny (29 pairs), MayoSRS (101 pairs) has low annotator agreement, and UMNSRS contains only single-word concepts, limiting relevance for multi-phrase concept representations.  
- Weakness: Extensive hyperparameter fine-tuning on the same datasets used for evaluation undermines the validity of reported results and comparisons to prior work.  
- Weakness: Claim of outperforming prior work by avoiding manual annotation is weak because the method relies on large manually constructed ontologies, whereas some prior work used clinical records without dedicated annotation.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks deeper analysis of underlying mechanisms and conflates two distinct compositional relations (phrase composition vs. multi-phrase concept representation) that require separate study.  
- Weakness: Missing linguistic/NLP analyses (e.g., headword effects within phrases) and unexplored impact of hyperparameters controlling the atomic/compositional tradeoff.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; authors should substantially improve evaluation methodology, dataset quality, and analyses before resubmission.  
- Minor: Typo/word choice corrections (e.g., Line 069 "contexts"→"concepts"; Line 430 use "phrases" not "strings").  
- Minor: Clarify phrase overlap handling (Line 202) and how words are trained in the joint model (Line 250).

- Minor: Missing/misc references and details—Pedersen et al. 2007 missing (Line 361), specify hyperparameter tuning task (Line 496), unclear Table 3 trends and include development set trends, and add missing reference to Table 5 (Line 535).  
- Minor: Annotation scale too fine-grained (1–100; Line 388).
