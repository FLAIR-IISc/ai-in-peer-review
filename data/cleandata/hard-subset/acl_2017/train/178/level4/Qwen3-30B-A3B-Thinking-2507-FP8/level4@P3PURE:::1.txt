

The paper presents an extension to word embedding methods that generates representations for phrases and concepts corresponding to words. The approach involves: (1) assigning a unique identifier to groups comprising phrases, words, and concepts denoting the same entity, (2) replacing all occurrences of these phrases and words with the identifier in the training corpus to create a "tagged" corpus, and (3) appending the tagged corpus to the original corpus for training. The grouping is derived from an ontology, with biomedical corpora and ontologies used for the domain-specific application. The authors also introduce a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness on real-world biomedical entities, which represents a significant contribution.

The paper is well-written and the proposed technique is conceptually straightforward. However, the contribution is modest, as the evaluation is confined to the biomedical domain, limiting broader applicability. The novel test resource merits deeper discussion; it constitutes the paper's most substantial contribution and should be emphasized more prominently.

A conceptual error exists in Equation 8: the authors define a MAP calculation but describe it as a probability, which is incorrect. The expression does not define a probability distribution, as it is trivial to demonstrate this even with an infinite vocabulary. The explanation must be revised to remove references to probability.
