

The paper presents a method for extending word embedding techniques to generate representations for phrases and concepts corresponding to words. The approach assigns a unique identifier to groups of phrases, words, and concepts denoting the same entity, replaces all occurrences of these items with the identifier in the training corpus to create a "tagged" corpus, and appends this tagged corpus to the original for training. Concept/phrase/word sets are derived from an ontology, with the biomedical domain guiding the choice of corpora and ontologies. The authors also introduce a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness among real-world biomedical entities, which represents a significant contribution.

Overall, the paper is well-written and the technique is conceptually straightforward. However, the contribution is modest, primarily due to its narrow focus on the biomedical domain. While the method itself is reasonable, the scope of its applicability is limited without broader validation. The generated test resource is particularly noteworthy and arguably represents the paper's most valuable contribution; additional discussion of its construction, potential applications, and comparative utility would strengthen the work.

A minor technical issue requires attention: Equation 8 defines the Mean Average Precision (MAP) calculation. While the intent is clear, the formulation is problematic. The equation as written does not represent a probability—it is straightforward to demonstrate this even with an infinite vocabulary. The explanation should therefore remove references to "probability" and clarify the mathematical intent. This appears to stem from a formulation issue rather than an implementation error.
