- Introduces a model that embeds words, atomic phrases, and ontology-linked concepts by mapping concepts to phrases and adapting skip-gram for joint training.  
- Evaluated on concept similarity/relatedness using UMLS and Yago resources.

- Strength: Addresses non-lexical phrase semantic proximity and non-compositional phrases.  
- Strength: Proposes a plausible phrase-embedding framework with competitive or superior concept similarity performance.  
- Strength: Released software could be useful for biomedical NLP researchers.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—mainly a minor adaptation of skip-gram.  
- Weakness: Full model does not consistently beat simpler baselines (Choi) on Mayo and shows only competitiveness on UMNSRS.  
- Weakness: Results show simpler methods can outperform the proposed model in key datasets.

- Concern: Calling known phrases "distant supervision" is misleading—phrases are used as context, not as supervision for concept labels.  
- Concern: Notation (e.g., E_W) introduced in Section 3.2 is unused later.  
- Concern: Compositionality control via a single global β is problematic and cross-validation yields β=0 for some settings, negating compositionality.  
- Concern: Experimental setup for Table 4 is unclear—how similarities are computed from phrase/word vectors when mappings between concepts and phrases are many-to-many.  
- Concern: Phrase-approximation (averaging phrases) outperforms the ontology-based concept vectors in Table 5, calling into question the necessity of the ontology versus using seed phrase sets.
