- Proposes jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words via distant supervision from ontology mappings.  
- Method replaces corpus phrases corresponding to concepts with unique identifiers to create a "tagged" corpus used alongside the original for training.  
- Evaluated on biomedical concepts from UMLS and general-domain entities from YAGO using biomedical and general-domain corpora.  
- Technical approach is sound and well-executed but offers limited novelty—essentially an adaptation of word2vec to incorporate ontology structure.  
- Evaluation is overly focused on the biomedical domain, limiting claims of broad applicability.  
- Main contribution is a new Mechanical Turk–created dataset for entity similarity and relatedness.  
- Paper does not sufficiently discuss the dataset’s potential value or how it could be used in other contexts; should emphasize this resource more.  
- Minor technical error: Equation 8 is mischaracterized as a probability but actually computes precision at a rank; explanation should be corrected.  
- Minor grammatical error: line 556 should read "most concepts have."  
- Overall: well-written with competitive results, but contribution is modest and not clearly superior to prior human-annotated methods.
