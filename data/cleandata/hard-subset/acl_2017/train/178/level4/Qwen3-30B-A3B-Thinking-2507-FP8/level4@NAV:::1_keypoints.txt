- Proposes extending word embeddings to phrases/concepts by assigning unique IDs to groups, replacing occurrences to create a "tagged" corpus, and appending it to the original for training.
- Groupings of words/phrases/concepts are derived from an ontology and applied to biomedical corpora/ontologies.
- Introduces a novel biomedical test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness among real-world entities.
- Paper is well-written and the methodology is conceptually straightforward.
- Contribution is limited in impact because the scope is narrowly confined to the biomedical domain.
- The new test resource is a more substantial contribution than the core method and needs greater discussion of its utility.
- Technical issue: Equation 8’s MAP formulation is incorrectly described as a probability and needs revision to remove probabilistic terminology.
- Minor correction: Line 556 should read "most concepts have" instead of "most concepts has."
