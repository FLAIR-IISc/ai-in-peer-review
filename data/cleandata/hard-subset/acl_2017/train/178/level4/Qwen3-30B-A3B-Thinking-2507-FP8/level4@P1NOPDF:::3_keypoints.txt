- Paper proposes a joint embedding method for words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora + a manually constructed ontology, applied to medical (UMLS) and general (YAGO) domains, and introduces a new MTurk-annotated general-domain dataset; results are comparable to prior work.

- Strength: The model is conceptually simple and provides a configurable middle ground between atomic-phrase and compositional approaches, extending naturally to concepts represented by multiple phrases.

- Strength: The work shows substantial effort, including model development, a new evaluation dataset, and comprehensive analyses.

- Weakness: Evaluation uses only intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, which poorly predict performance on extrinsic tasks and is insufficient by current community standards.

- Weakness: Datasets are mischaracterized as concept similarity benchmarks even though human judgments were collected on phrases, so they are phrase similarity datasets, not concept similarity datasets.

- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets are problematic—mini MayoSRS is tiny (29 pairs), MayoSRS (101 pairs) has low inter-annotator agreement, and UMNSRS covers only single-word concepts, making them ill-suited for phrase/concept embedding evaluation.

- Weakness: Hyperparameters were extensively tuned on the same datasets used for evaluation, invalidating the reported results and comparisons.

- Weakness: The claim of outperforming prior work "with less manual annotation" is weak because the method relies on large manually constructed ontologies (UMLS/YAGO) and prior work used existing clinical records rather than dedicated annotation efforts.

- Weakness: The paper conflates two distinct compositional relationships—(1) phrase vs. component words and (2) concept vs. representative phrases—without separate analyses; hyperparameter effects on the atomic/compositional tradeoff are unexplored.

- Recommendation: Due to these methodological flaws (invalid tuning, dataset mischaracterization, lack of extrinsic evaluation), the paper should be rejected until evaluations, analyses, and claims are corrected and expanded.
