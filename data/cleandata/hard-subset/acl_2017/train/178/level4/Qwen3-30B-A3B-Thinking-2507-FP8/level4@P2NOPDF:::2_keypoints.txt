- Paper proposes embedding words, phrases, and concepts by mapping ontology concepts to phrases and adapting skip-gram to jointly train their embeddings.  
- Strength: Addresses non-compositional phrase semantics and shows competitive/superior performance on concept similarity; released software is useful for biomedical NLP.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—model is a minor adaptation of skip-gram with little conceptual innovation.  
- Weakness: Full model underperforms baselines on key datasets (Mayo, UMNSRS) and is only competitive with simpler methods.  
- Weakness: Terminology confusion—calling phrase mappings “distant supervision” is misleading because phrases are used as context, not as true labeled supervision.  
- Weakness: Global β parameter for phrase compositionality is unjustified and cross-validation often picks β=0, negating compositionality.  
- Weakness: Ontology may be unnecessary—averaging phrase embeddings (approximate phr) outperforms ontology-based methods, undermining the core contribution.  
- Weakness: Methodological gaps and unclear experimental details—how similarity is computed with many-to-many concept-phrase mappings is not explained, and introduced notation is unused.  
- General concern: Theoretical framing and experiments do not convincingly validate the utility of the ontology; paper needs clearer terminology, stronger theory, and experiments directly testing ontology benefit.
