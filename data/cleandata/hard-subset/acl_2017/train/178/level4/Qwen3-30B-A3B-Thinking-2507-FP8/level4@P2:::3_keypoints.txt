- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised framework that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-mapped phrases as distant supervision, avoiding manual concept annotation.
- Summary: Releases a new human-judgment dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities (people, places, organizations).
- Summary: Shows competitive performance on concept similarity/relatedness tasks compared to prior methods that required human annotation.
- Strength: Offers a configurable middle ground between treating phrases as atomic units and fully decomposing them, addressing phrase compositionality.
- Strength: Presents substantial engineering and analysis, including a new dataset and study of compositionality and semantic clustering.
- Strength: Achieves competitive performance with much larger vocabulary coverage (3x+) without additional manual annotation.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic downstream validation, which weakens claims about practical utility.
- Weakness: Human judgments are based on textual phrases, so datasets measure phrase similarity rather than abstract concept similarity, misaligning the claimed evaluation.
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets (MayoSRS, Mini MayoSRS) are too small and have low inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.24), making them unreliable; UMNSRS tests only single-word representations.
- Weakness: Hyperparameters were tuned on the same evaluation datasets used for reporting results, causing test-set leakage and invalidating evaluation.
- Weakness: Claim of "no human corpus annotation" is misleading because the method depends on manually-constructed ontologies (UMLS, YAGO); prior work used existing clinical records without extra annotation.
- Question: Please justify the "no human corpus annotation" claim given reliance on manually-built ontologies.
- Question: Which specific extrinsic tasks would you propose to validate embedding utility in downstream applications?
- Question: How will you address the small size and low agreement of MayoSRS in future evaluations?
- Additional comment: Clarify how phrase overlaps are handled during preprocessing (Line 202).
- Additional comment: The term "negative sampling matrix" is confusing since embeddings are used for both positive and negative instances (Line 220).
- Additional comment: Clarify the phrase "the observed phrase just completed" and how words are trained in the joint model (Line 250).
- Additional comment: PM+CL hyperparameters differ from PM and CL individually; analyze why the combined corpus needs different configuration (Table 3).
- Additional comment: Specify which task was used for hyperparameter tuning to ensure reproducibility (Line 496).
- Overall assessment: The approach is modestly valuable, but flawed evaluation methodology and unsupported claims about annotation effort undermine the significance; add extrinsic tasks and fix evaluation issues before reconsideration.
