- Proposes a vector-space model mapping concepts to phrases in text, using an ontology and a skip-gram-like training method.
- Generates embeddings for words, phrases, and concepts and evaluates them on concept similarity and relatedness tasks using UMLS and Yago.
- Strength: addresses phrases that are lexically dissimilar but semantically related.
- Strength: offers a plausible method for training phrase embeddings.
- Strength: resulting embeddings are competitive or superior on some concept similarity benchmarks.
- Strength: released software could be useful to biomedical NLP researchers.
- Weakness: model is not very novel—essentially a variation of skip-gram.
- Weakness: full model does not consistently outperform baselines (e.g., Choi on Mayo datasets) and is only competitive with simpler models on UMNSRS.
- Concern: calling the training procedure “distant supervision” is misleading relative to usual predictive supervision definitions.
- Concern: notation introduced in Section 3.2 is not used elsewhere and may confuse readers.
- Concern: hyperparameter β for phrase compositionality is odd as a global constant, with cross-validated values producing unexpected results.
- Concern: experimental details for Table 4 are unclear, particularly how similarities between concepts/entities are computed.
- Concern: Table 5 shows an approximate phr method outperforming the ontology-based approach, questioning whether a seed set of phrases could achieve similar results without the ontology.
