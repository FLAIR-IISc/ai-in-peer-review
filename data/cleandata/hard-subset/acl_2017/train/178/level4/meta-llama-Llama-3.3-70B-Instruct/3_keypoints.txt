- Proposes joint embeddings of words, phrases, and concepts using text corpora plus ontologies (UMLS, YAGO).
- Applies method in medical and general domains and creates a new general-domain concept similarity/relatedness dataset via Mechanical Turk.
- Evaluation is primarily intrinsic (similarity/relatedness) and obtains results comparable to prior work.
- Strength: Model is simple and configurable, balancing atomic phrase treatment and compositionality, and extends to concepts with multiple representative phrases.
- Strength: Paper includes substantial work—model development, new dataset creation, and multiple evaluations/analyses.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic tasks; no extrinsic downstream tasks to demonstrate practical utility.
- Weakness: Datasets are phrase-presented to annotators, so they measure phrase similarity, not pure concept similarity, and should be treated accordingly.
- Weakness: Medical evaluation sets are small, of limited agreement, or focus on single-word concepts, limiting relevance for phrase/concept representation evaluation.
- Weakness: Heavy hyperparameter tuning on the same datasets used for reporting undermines the validity of comparisons with prior work.
- Weakness: Claim of needing less manual annotation is weak because large ontologies used are manually constructed and prior datasets stem from clinical records.
- Weakness: Paper lacks insightful analysis of relations between phrases and component words and between concepts and their alternative phrases, including effects of tradeoff hyperparameters.
- Recommendation: Reject; encourage improving evaluation datasets, adding extrinsic tasks, and deeper analyses before resubmission.
- Minor issues: wording/notation and clarity errors (e.g., 'contexts'→'concepts' at L69; unclear phrase-overlap handling at L202; matrix dims and 'negative sampling matrix' at L220; unclear word training in joint model at L250; Equation 1 uses 'c' vs 'o'; missing Pedersen et al. ref at L361).
- Minor issues: odd 1–100 annotation scale for similarity (L388) and confusing use of 'strings' vs 'phrases' (L430).
- Minor issues: unclear which task was used for hyperparameter tuning (L496); Table 3 trends hard to read—report dev-set hyperparameter trends; missing reference to Table 5 (L535).
