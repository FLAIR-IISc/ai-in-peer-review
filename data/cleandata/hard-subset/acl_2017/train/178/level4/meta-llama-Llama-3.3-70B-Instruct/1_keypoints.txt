- Proposes embedding method that assigns unique identifiers to groups of phrases/words/concepts, replaces occurrences to create a "tagged" corpus, and trains on both tagged and original corpora.  
- Derives concept/phrase/word sets from an ontology, with experiments focused on biomedical corpora and ontologies.  
- Introduces a novel test dataset for evaluating word similarity and relatedness for real-world entities.  
- Paper is well-written; the technique is straightforward but not particularly groundbreaking.  
- Contribution is somewhat limited by evaluation being confined to the biomedical domain.  
- The new test resource warrants a more in-depth discussion, as it may be the paper’s most significant contribution.  
- Technical issue: Equation 8’s MAP definition improperly ranks the entire vocabulary and incorrectly references probability; suggest using a natural cutoff and removing probability claims.  
- Minor typo: change "most concepts has" to "most concepts have" (line 556).
