- Presents a weakly-supervised method that jointly embeds concepts, multi-word phrases, and words by using ontologies as distant supervision and adapting skip-gram with negative sampling.

- Contribution: a simple, scalable extension of word2vec that incorporates structured ontology knowledge without manual annotations.

- Contribution: introduces a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness benchmark dataset for the community.

- Contribution: thorough empirical evaluation across biomedical and general domains with extensive baseline comparisons and analyses.

- Strength: elegant, practical approach that treats ontology-linked phrases as single tokens and leverages distant supervision for scalability.

- Strength: comprehensive experiments demonstrating general applicability and solid comparisons to varied baselines.

- Strength: provides a valuable dataset and insightful analyses of compositionality and semantic type clustering in the embedding space.

- Weakness: direct concept embeddings (EC) often don't outperform simple averaging of phrase embeddings (APPROXPHRco), questioning EC's added value.

- Weakness: preprocessing uses a greedy longest-match phrase identification that may introduce noisy or ambiguous phrase annotations (nested/overlapping phrases) and is not evaluated.

- Weakness: limited interpretation of why hyperparameter choices (e.g., compositionality weights) differ across corpora and how corpus characteristics affect them.

- Questions: please clarify EC's role during training and whether it mainly acts as a regularizer making averaged phrase vectors more robust; and whether you analyzed the impact of greedy phrase matching or considered better disambiguation methods.
