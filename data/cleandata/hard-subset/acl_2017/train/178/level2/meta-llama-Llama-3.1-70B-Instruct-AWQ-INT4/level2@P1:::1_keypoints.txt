- Summary: Proposes joint learning of concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using representative phrases as distant supervision; adapts skip-gram with negative sampling and shows competitive results on similarity/relatedness tasks.

- Contribution 1: Novel method for embedding concepts, phrases, and words in a shared vector space to capture semantic relationships.

- Contribution 2: Uses representative phrases from ontologies as distant supervision, eliminating the need for human annotations.

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates competitive performance with existing methods on concept similarity and relatedness benchmarks.

- Strength 1: Introduces a novel joint-embedding approach that advances NLP representation learning.

- Strength 2: Effectively leverages distant supervision, reducing reliance on costly labeled data.

- Strength 3: Empirically competitive with prior methods on evaluated tasks.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets and may not generalize across applications.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison to other joint embedding methods, hindering assessment of relative merits.

- Weakness 3: Does not provide detailed analysis of hyperparameters and their impact on performance.

- Question 1: How will the method be extended to other domains and applications?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide a more detailed hyperparameter analysis and its effect on results?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to handle polysemy where words/phrases have multiple meanings?

- Additional comment: Paper is clear and well-written; distant supervision is a valuable contribution but more comparisons and hyperparameter analysis are needed.
