- Proposes a method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using phrases linked to an ontology as distant supervision.
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain concepts, showing competitive performance with methods that use human annotations.
- Joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words using ontology structure and unannotated corpora.
- Uses representative phrases from an ontology as distant supervision to avoid human-annotated training data.
- Achieves competitive results on concept similarity/relatedness tasks while covering >3x larger vocabulary.
- Novel approach to joint embedding is a meaningful contribution to NLP.
- Demonstrates competitive performance despite not using human-annotated corpora.
- Scales to much larger vocabularies without additional computational expense.
- Lack of human-annotated data could limit applicability in some tasks.
- Method’s effectiveness depends on the quality of the ontology used for distant supervision.
- Requires potentially time-consuming and expert hyperparameter tuning.
- Asks how authors will mitigate reliance on ontology quality.
- Asks how the method could be extended to applications like entity linking or question answering.
- Requests more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and selection for each corpus.
- Recommends authors address ontology dependency and tuning needs despite the method’s strong contributions.
