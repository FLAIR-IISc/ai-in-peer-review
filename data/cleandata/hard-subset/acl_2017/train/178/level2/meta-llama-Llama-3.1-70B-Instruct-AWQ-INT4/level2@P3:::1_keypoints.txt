- Summary: Proposes cui2vec, a method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using distant supervision from ontology-linked phrases, evaluated on biomedical and general datasets with competitive results.

- Main contribution 1: Introduces a novel joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space.

- Main contribution 2: Uses distant supervision from ontology-linked phrases to avoid human-annotated training data.

- Main contribution 3: Demonstrates competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods across biomedical and general-domain datasets.

- Strength 1: Novel methodological approach for unified embeddings of multiple linguistic units.

- Strength 2: Effective use of distant supervision reduces reliance on costly annotations.

- Strength 3: Empirical results are competitive with methods that use human annotations.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets and may not generalize to all use cases.

- Weakness 2: Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why the method works or how to improve it.

- Weakness 3: Relies on hyperparameter tuning, which may limit practical applicability.

- Question 1: How does the method handle polysemy and homophony in concepts and phrases?

- Question 2: Can the method be extended to capture complex or hierarchical relationships between concepts and phrases?

- Question 3: How does the method compare to other state-of-the-art approaches in computational efficiency and scalability?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written with clear figures/tables but would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis and broader evaluation.
