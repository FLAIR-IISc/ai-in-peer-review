- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled corpus using representative phrases as distant supervision.
- Uses ontology-structured knowledge and phrase contexts to train concept embeddings without human annotation.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks in biomedical and general domains.
- Achieves >3x larger vocabulary coverage than prior methods while remaining scalable.
- Provides thorough evaluation and analysis of the embedding space with comparisons to existing approaches.
- Method’s effectiveness depends on the quality and coverage of the ontology and representative phrases.
- Fails to capture some non-lexical concept information; could benefit from techniques like Wang et al. (2014a).
- Evaluation is limited to similarity/relatedness tasks, so generalization to other downstream applications is unclear.
- Requests clarification on how representative phrases were selected and handled when multiple phrases exist per concept.
- Asks how hyperparameters were chosen and whether more advanced tuning will be explored.
- Inquires about applying the method to other domains/ontologies and anticipated challenges.
- Paper is well-written, acknowledges limitations, and contributes a novel similarity/relatedness dataset.
