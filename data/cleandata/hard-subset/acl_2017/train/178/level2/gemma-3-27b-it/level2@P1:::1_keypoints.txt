- Proposes a weakly-supervised framework that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases from ontologies as distant supervision.  
- Trains embeddings from phrase contexts to represent concepts independent of their textual form, avoiding manual annotation.  
- Evaluates on biomedical and general-domain datasets, showing competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.  
- Introduces a joint embedding framework that addresses limitations of word-only semantic models.  
- Presents a scalable, weakly-supervised approach that removes the need for expensive human-annotated concept data.  
- Provides comprehensive analyses including ontological structure preservation and phrase/word compositionality.  
- Releases a new dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness evaluation, filling a resource gap.  
- Delivers clear presentation and an available software implementation to aid reproducibility.  
- Performance depends heavily on the quality and coverage of ontology concept-to-phrase mappings, risking noise from poor mappings.  
- Compositionality hyperparameters (β, η) yield limited performance gains, suggesting the compositional model may be insufficient.  
- Does not explore alternative or more informed negative sampling strategies tailored to joint concept/phrase/word embeddings.  
- Hyperparameter tuning appears limited; a more exhaustive or automated search may find better settings.  
- The Mechanical Turk–sourced evaluation dataset may suffer from crowdsourcing biases that need addressing.  
- Asks how sensitive results are to concept-phrase mapping quality and requests quantitative sensitivity analyses.  
- Asks for explanations for limited compositionality impact and suggestions for improving compositional modeling.  
- Asks whether more sophisticated negative sampling strategies were considered or could be explored.  
- Asks what measures were taken to detect or mitigate bias in the Mechanical Turk dataset.  
- Recommends adding a detailed discussion of computational complexity compared to existing methods.  
- Suggests deeper comparisons to related work, especially knowledge graph embedding techniques, to highlight unique advantages.  
- Suggests improving software documentation and providing usage examples to increase accessibility.
