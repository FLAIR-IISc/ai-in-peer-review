- Proposes a weakly-supervised method adapting word2vec to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-derived representative phrases as distant supervision.
- Uses ontology knowledge (UMLS, YAGO) to link concepts to textual representations, enabling concept embedding from unlabeled text.
- Releases novel evaluation datasets for real-world entity similarity and relatedness.

- Achieves competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods on concept similarity/relatedness without human annotation.
- Scales well due to distant supervision and shows much larger vocabulary coverage than existing methods.
- Paper is clearly written with informative figures/tables and situates the work relative to prior research.
- Authors commit to releasing code and datasets, aiding reproducibility.
- Analysis of concept/phrase/word relationships and semantic-type clustering validates the embeddings’ capture of ontological structure.

- Core technique (adapting word2vec with distant supervision) lacks strong novelty relative to prior work (e.g., Wang et al., 2014a).
- Method performance depends heavily on the quality of concept–phrase mappings; impact of imperfect mappings is insufficiently analyzed.
- New datasets show only fair inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss' kappa = 0.24), needing discussion of disagreement sources and task ambiguity.
- Hyperparameter tuning appears corpus-specific with limited discussion of robustness or transfer across corpora.
- Improvements over strong baselines are sometimes modest and lack deeper error analysis identifying where gains occur.

- How sensitive is performance to the quality and completeness of ontology concept–phrase mappings; can you provide an ablation or analysis?
- Can embeddings transfer across corpora (e.g., PubMed to clinical domain) and what is the method’s cross-domain generalization?
- What quality-control measures were used for Mechanical Turk annotations beyond validation questions to mitigate bias?
- Why do β and η parameters often perform best at zero; are there datasets or concept types where nonzero settings help?

- Suggest discussing broader applications of the learned embeddings (e.g., concept prediction, relation extraction).
- Recommend explicitly defining “concept” in the paper to avoid ambiguity (e.g., ontology concepts).
- Bibliography is missing the Chiu et al., 2016b reference.
