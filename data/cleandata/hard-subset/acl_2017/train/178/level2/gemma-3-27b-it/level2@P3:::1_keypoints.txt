Strengths:
- Novel method: Uses representative phrases as distant supervision to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings by adapting skip-gram with negative sampling.
- Competitive performance: Achieves strong results on concept similarity/relatedness tasks without human annotation across multiple corpora and ontologies.
- Large vocabulary coverage: Reports a ~3x increase in vocabulary size versus existing methods, improving practical applicability.
- Comprehensive evaluation: Includes multiple baselines, hyperparameter tuning, metrics, and a new dataset for general-domain entity similarity/relatedness.
- Resources released: Implementation and the new dataset are publicly available, aiding reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Limited noise analysis: Lacks thorough investigation of how noisy distant supervision and incorrect phrase-concept mappings affect embeddings.
- Modest gains vs. strong baselines: Improvements over existing biomedical embedding methods are small on some datasets; scenarios of clear advantage are not well highlighted.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient discussion of model stability and sensitivity to hyperparameters despite reliance on careful tuning.
- Concept-phrase ambiguity: Many-to-many concept-phrase mappings and representative phrase choice may introduce noise; impact of different selection strategies is not explored.
- Annotation reliability: New dataset’s Fleiss’ kappa of 0.24 indicates only fair annotator agreement, raising concerns about label reliability and annotation process analysis.

Questions to authors:
- How does noisy supervision quantitatively affect embedding quality, and have mitigation methods for incorrect mappings been explored?
- On which datasets or scenarios does the method notably outperform existing biomedical embeddings?
- How sensitive are results to hyperparameters (e.g., β and η), and is there an ablation study showing their contributions?
- What criteria were used to select representative phrases per concept, and how would alternative strategies affect performance?
- What procedures ensured annotation reliability for the new dataset given the low Fleiss’ kappa?

Additional comments:
- Writing and organization are clear.
- Related work coverage is thorough.
- Public release of code and data is a significant contribution.
- Recommend adding embedding visualizations (e.g., t-SNE/PCA) to illustrate model behavior.
