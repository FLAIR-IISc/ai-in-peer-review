- Proposes a weakly supervised, scalable method to jointly embed ontology concepts, representative phrases, and words from unannotated text using phrase-to-concept mappings and skip-gram training.  
- Releases embeddings, code (cui2vec), and a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset.

- Practical, annotation-light approach that achieves competitive intrinsic performance and vastly increased coverage versus manually annotated/code-based embeddings.  
- Joint embedding space for concepts, phrases, and words helps link surface text to canonical concepts and reveals compositionality patterns.  
- Thorough empirical comparisons across corpora, tuned hyperparameters, and sensitivity analyses increase credibility.  
- Demonstrated cross-domain applicability on biomedical (UMLS) and general-domain (YAGO) concepts.  
- Valuable resource contribution: code, embeddings, and a new dataset.

- Core method offers limited modeling novelty and treats ambiguous phrases by uniformly assigning all candidate concepts, introducing noise.  
- Evaluation is primarily intrinsic and hyperparameters are tuned on test-like datasets; no downstream task validation is provided.  
- New Mechanical Turk dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.24) and potential selection bias.  
- Evidence that explicit concept embeddings add value is mixed; phrase-approximation often matches or outperforms concept vectors and optimal regularization is near zero.  
- Some baseline comparisons and implementation choices (e.g., negative sampling only over words) raise fairness and methodological questions.

- How sensitive are results to uniform concept assignment for ambiguous phrases; what happens if restricted to unambiguous mentions or weighted by priors?  
- What negative sampling distribution was used and why is the negative matrix indexed only by words; were negatives drawn from words+phrases+concepts?  
- Can results be reported on the intersection of concepts covered by this work and each baseline to clarify performance vs. coverage?  
- Are there consistent gains on downstream tasks (e.g., concept normalization, entity linking) using these embeddings versus word-only?

- Incorporate ontology structure (hierarchy/relations) or retrofitting to capture non-lexical semantics and improve type clustering.  
- Add lightweight disambiguation (attention, EM, context-aware weighting) to reduce distant-supervision noise.  
- Fix notation/typos, specify phrase-matching/tokenization/case-normalization, and report training time/memory and frequency thresholds.  
- Improve dataset reliability: provide guidelines, examples, higher-agreement subset, expert adjudication, and release raw judgments with recommended splits.  
- Include qualitative case studies, nearest-neighbor/error analyses, and ablations isolating β/η and TT vs. TU context effects.

- Overall: solid, pragmatic contribution with strong coverage and competitive intrinsic results, but modest methodological novelty and reliance on intrinsic metrics and a low-IAA dataset; disambiguation, ontology signals, and downstream evaluations would strengthen the work.
