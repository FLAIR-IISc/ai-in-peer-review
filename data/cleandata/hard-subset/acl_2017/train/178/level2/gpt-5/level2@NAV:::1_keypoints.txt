- Summary: Proposes a weakly supervised pipeline that replaces concept-mention phrases in raw text to jointly embed ontology concepts, phrases, and words via skip-gram with compositionality regularizers, evaluated on UMLS and YAGO with released code and a small crowdsourced dataset.

- Contribution 1: Scalable, annotation-free distant-supervision pipeline yielding joint concept–phrase–word embeddings with >3x vocabulary coverage vs prior resources.

- Contribution 2: Thorough intrinsic evaluations across corpora/ontologies and analyses of compositionality and semantic-type clustering, plus a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset.

- Contribution 3: Practical release of code, resources, and hyperparameter details to support reproducibility.

- Strength 1: Scalability/practicality—avoids manual annotation and covers 188k–748k concepts versus prior 15k–52k resources.

- Strength 2: Competitive intrinsic performance—matches or exceeds strong baselines on UMNSRS similarity and is competitive on relatedness.

- Strength 3: Joint embedding space enables compositionality and type-clustering analyses, suggesting useful learned structure.

- Strength 4: Reproducibility—code, datasets, and hyperparameter sweeps are publicly released.

- Weakness 1: Supervision ambiguity—treating every phrase occurrence as all possible concepts introduces unquantified noise and likely harms performance on smaller datasets.

- Weakness 2: Limited evaluation—relies on intrinsic correlations without statistical significance testing or extrinsic task evaluations to demonstrate practical impact.

- Weakness 3: Incremental novelty—similar prior work exists and compositionality regularizers often contribute little (optimal values near zero).

- Weakness 4: Crowdsourced dataset quality—low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ≈0.24) and small size limit its reliability.

- Question 1: Can you quantify the impact of phrase-to-multiple-concept ambiguity (e.g., via ablations with disambiguation heuristics or priors)?

- Question 2: Were statistical significance tests performed between top systems on UMNSRS and MayoSRS; if not, please provide them?

- Question 3: Can you report extrinsic task results (e.g., concept normalization, entity linking) to support real-world usefulness?

- Question 4: How does varying the ≥25-frequency threshold affect coverage and quality; does lowering it meaningfully improve coverage without degrading performance?
