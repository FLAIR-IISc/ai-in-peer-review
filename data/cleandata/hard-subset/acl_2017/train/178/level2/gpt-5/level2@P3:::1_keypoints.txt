- Proposes a weakly supervised method that jointly learns embeddings for ontology concepts, their phrases, and words using ontology phrase lists as distant supervision and a modified skip-gram objective.  
- Greedy phrase detection replaces phrases with unique IDs and trains three embedding matrices (words, phrases, concepts) with compositionality regularizers β (words→phrases) and η (phrases→concepts).  
- Evaluated on biomedical (UMLS) and general-domain (YAGO) concepts across PubMed abstracts, clinical notes, and Wikipedia+NYT.  
- Contribution: scalable, annotation-free framework yielding substantially higher concept coverage than prior work (e.g., ~193k vs 52k/15k concepts).  
- Contribution: release of a new human-annotated general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset (688 pairs per task) and code (cui2vec).  
- Contribution: analyses of compositionality and semantic-type clustering that probe structure of learned embeddings.  
- Strength: practical, scalable alternative to annotation-heavy embeddings, training only on raw text plus ontology string inventories.  
- Strength: competitive intrinsic performance on biomedical benchmarks (e.g., UMNSRS Similarity CONCEPTco 0.653 matches best word baseline).  
- Strength: cross-domain generalization to YAGO entities with strong similarity/relatedness scores (e.g., CONCEPTco ~0.750–0.789).  
- Strength: careful baselines and ablations isolate effects of joint training and distant supervision.  
- Strength: useful analyses (compositionality, neighborhood MAP, semantic-type clustering) and resource release aid reproducibility.  
- Weakness: ignores ontology structure (relations/types), potentially missing non-lexical concept signals captured by graph-based methods.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on intrinsic evaluations and potential hyperparameter tuning on test sets; no downstream task evaluations shown.  
- Weakness: distant supervision assumption that a phrase signals all candidate concepts can dilute concept-specific signals; multi-mapping impact not quantified.  
- Weakness: compositionality regularizers often tuned to zero and sometimes harm performance, weakening claim that joint constraints drive gains.  
- Weakness: new general-domain dataset has low annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ ≈ 0.24) and moderate size, limiting confidence in those results.  
- Question: how frequent are multi-mapping phrases per corpus, and how sensitive are results to excluding or differently weighting them?  
- Question: how does greedy phrase matching handle overlapping/nested phrases, and what is its precision/recall versus a standard tagger on annotated data?  
- Question: can ontology hierarchical/type information be incorporated (e.g., auxiliary loss) and would that improve concept–phrase MAPs?  
- Question: can inter-annotator reliability on the general-domain dataset be improved (stricter qualification, more annotators) and do results hold under MACE or alternative aggregation?  
- Suggestion: add at least one downstream evaluation (e.g., concept normalization, entity linking, code prediction) to show practical utility.  
- Suggestion: incorporate lightweight ontology-structure signals (parent-child proximity, relation-based negative sampling) as an auxiliary objective.  
- Suggestion: quantify distant supervision noise (fraction of ambiguous phrases, update contributions) and analyze cases where approximations outperform direct concept embeddings.  
- Suggestion: report dev/test splits or leave-dataset-out protocol to avoid tuning on intrinsic-test items.  
- Suggestion: explore multi-sense or context-dependent phrase embeddings to reduce polysemy-related signal dilution.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed, and scalable contribution with competitive quality and much higher coverage; expanding evaluation breadth and modestly integrating ontology structure would strengthen the work.
