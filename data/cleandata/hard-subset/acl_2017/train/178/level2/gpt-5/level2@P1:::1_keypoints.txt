- Proposes weakly supervised joint embeddings of ontology concepts, phrases, and words using phrase-to-concept mappings as distant supervision.  
- Trains phrase and concept embeddings with a skip-gram negative-sampling objective and two compositionality hyperparameters (β, η); requires no manual annotation.  
- Primary contribution: scalable, annotation-free method for joint word/phrase/concept embeddings from raw text using ontology phrase lists.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical results showing competitive biomedical similarity/relatedness performance with much larger concept coverage and analyses of concept–phrase/phrase–word structure.  
- Tertiary contribution: release of software/embeddings (cui2vec) and a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset.  
- Strength: scalable weak supervision yields very large vocabularies (e.g., ~193k UMLS concepts) without expensive annotations.  
- Strength: competitive intrinsic performance on biomedical benchmarks (UMNSRS Similarity tied state-of-the-art; good Relatedness/Mayo performance).  
- Strength: joint embedding space enables analyses showing concepts cluster near phrase averages and phrases exhibit bimodal relations to constituent words.  
- Strength: method applied across domains (UMLS, YAGO) and corpora (PubMed, clinical notes, Wikipedia+NYT); code and data release improves reproducibility.  
- Strength: simple, clear training pipeline based on skip-gram with tunable compositionality parameters.  
- Weakness: strong assumption that any mapped phrase occurrence signals all candidate concepts; no explicit disambiguation beyond uniform normalization.  
- Weakness: limited use of ontology graph structure (relations, hierarchy, semantic types) beyond phrase mappings.  
- Weakness: evaluation is mainly intrinsic (similarity/relatedness), with possible tuning overfitting, no statistical significance tests, and no extrinsic downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: new general-domain dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (κ≈0.24), is small (688 pairs), and may have sampling bias; baseline comparisons are limited.  
- Weakness: methodological clarity and ablation gaps — shared negative-sample matrix rationale, greedy phrase matching effects, handling overlapping/ambiguous phrases, training efficiency, and more controlled β/η and phrase-collapsing ablations.  
- Question: did you try context-based disambiguation during training (EM, sampling, posterior regularization), and how did it affect performance/stability?  
- Question: can adding simple ontology-structure regularizers (parents/children, same-type) improve similarity/type clustering — any experiments?  
- Question: provide ablations showing incremental impact of β>0 and η>0 (and why η=0 for WIKINYT) on embeddings and compositionality diagnostics.  
- Question: why use a single shared negative-sample/output matrix across words/phrases/concepts instead of separate output matrices, and what differences were observed?  
- Question: will the general-domain dataset and annotation guidelines be released, and can you report per-category agreement or effects of removing low-agreement pairs?  
- Question: what are training time/memory footprints for PM+CL and WIKINYT, and sensitivity to the 25-frequency threshold (important for rare clinical concepts)?  
- Suggestion: evaluate on extrinsic tasks where concept representations matter (entity linking, concept normalization, medical code prediction) and report statistical significance/confidence intervals.  
- Suggestion: include error analysis for cases where concept embeddings underperform phrase approximations to identify failure modes (ambiguity, rarity, domain mismatch).
