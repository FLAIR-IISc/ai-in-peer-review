- Proposes a joint embedding model that learns concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using ontology-based distant supervision.
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain concepts, showing competitive performance compared to methods needing human annotations.
- Introduces a novel dataset of similarity and relatedness judgments for real-world entities for evaluating concept embeddings.
- Strength: effectively uses distant supervision to learn concept embeddings without human-labeled data.
- Strength: achieves competitive results on concept similarity and relatedness benchmarks.
- Strength: provides a valuable new dataset for concept embedding evaluation.
- Weakness: limited analysis of hyperparameters and their effect on model performance.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other joint embedding models that also learn concepts, phrases, and words.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited; does not test embeddings on downstream tasks (e.g., entity linking, QA).
- Question: request detailed hyperparameter tuning process and impact of different hyperparameters.
- Question: ask for comparisons to other joint embedding models.
- Question: request evaluation of concept embeddings on downstream tasks such as entity linking or question answering.
