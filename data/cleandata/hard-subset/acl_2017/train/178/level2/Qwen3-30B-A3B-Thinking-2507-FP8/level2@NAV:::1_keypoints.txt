- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontology phrases as distant supervision.  
- Contribution: Requires no human annotation for concept embeddings.  
- Contribution: Achieves competitive performance on biomedical concept similarity/relatedness tasks (UMNSRS, MayoSRS).  
- Contribution: Provides substantially larger vocabulary coverage (>3x) than prior approaches.  

- Strength: Eliminates expensive manual annotation; ontology-phrase distant supervision is well-motivated.  
- Strength: Compelling experimental results and a new dataset for real-world entity similarity.  
- Strength: Analysis of embedding compositionality shows preservation of ontological relationships (Figures 3–4).  

- Weakness: Insufficient detail on preventing test-set leakage during hyperparameter tuning; “adapted 5-fold cross-validation” is unclear.  
- Weakness: Superficial analysis of β and η hyperparameters; lacks guidance on when/why they matter or how to select them.  
- Weakness: Does not address critiques of similarity/relatedness evaluation tasks (Chiu et al., 2016b), weakening validity of claims.  

- Question: Clarify exact implementation of 5-fold cross-validation to prevent leakage during hyperparameter tuning.  
- Question: Provide a more detailed analysis of β and η, including scenarios where they offer significant benefits.  
- Question: How will you address limitations of similarity/relatedness tasks for embedding evaluation in future work?
