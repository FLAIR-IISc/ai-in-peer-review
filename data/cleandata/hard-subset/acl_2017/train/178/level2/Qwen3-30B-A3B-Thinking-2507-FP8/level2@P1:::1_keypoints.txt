- Summary: Proposes weakly-supervised joint embeddings of concepts, phrases, and words using ontology representative phrases as distant supervision; trained on UMLS and YAGO, shows competitive similarity/relatedness performance and higher vocabulary coverage, and introduces a new real-world entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution 1: Novel weakly-supervised method using ontology phrases for distant supervision to avoid human annotation.

- Contribution 2: Empirical results showing competitive performance on concept similarity/relatedness with substantially larger vocabulary coverage.

- Contribution 3: New human-judged dataset for similarity/relatedness of people, places, and organizations.

- Strength 1: Addresses the practical gap of avoiding human annotation for concept embeddings with convincing multi-dataset results.

- Strength 2: Scales to much larger vocabulary sizes (over 3x coverage) without extra annotation cost.

- Strength 3: Provides a valuable dataset filling an evaluation gap for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient treatment of noise from distant supervision and how varied phrase-concept mappings (e.g., different surface forms) affect embeddings.

- Weakness 2: Comparisons to supervised prior work are misleading without acknowledging annotation cost or providing cost-benefit analysis.

- Weakness 3: Lacks analysis explaining why phrase-based approximations outperform direct concept embeddings on the general-domain dataset.

- Question 1: How are multiple representative phrases for the same concept handled—separate embeddings, averaged, or otherwise reconciled?

- Question 2: Why does CHOI outperform on small MayoSRS sets despite manual annotation cost, and what advantages does their annotation provide?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the coverage versus precision trade-off across corpora and ontologies?

- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written with sufficient supplemental detail on hyperparameters and dataset construction.

- Additional comment 2: Authors should strengthen discussion of limitations around phrase-concept mapping quality and present a more nuanced comparison acknowledging trade-offs of the weakly-supervised approach.
