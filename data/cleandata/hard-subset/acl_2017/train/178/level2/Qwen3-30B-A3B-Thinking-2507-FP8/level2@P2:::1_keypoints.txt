- Paper proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using distant supervision from ontology mappings (phrases linked to concepts).
- Main claimed contributions: learn concept embeddings without human corpus annotation, achieve competitive similarity/relatedness performance vs. annotated methods, and release a novel real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset.
- Strength: Distant supervision from ontology mappings enables training concept embeddings without manual corpus annotation and yields ~3x vocabulary coverage vs. prior work.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across biomedical and general-domain datasets and a well-documented, crowd-sourced dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.
- Strength: Analysis shows semantic type clustering and some preservation of phrase-word compositionality in the learned embeddings.
- Weakness: Claim of "competitive performance" is weakened by large underperformance on the small Mayo SRS dataset (proposed: 0.593 vs. CHOI: 0.817) with no adequate explanation.
- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning used test set via 5-fold cross-validation, violating standard evaluation practice and threatening result validity.
- Weakness: Comparison to Wang et al. (2014a) is inadequate; the paper fails to show clearly why its approach is better or why it doesn't outperform Wang et al. on larger datasets.
- Weakness: Claim that "no human corpus annotation" is required is misleading because ontology mappings and YAGO/Wikipedia links are human-constructed and not clearly acknowledged as such.
- Weakness: Evidence is limited to cosine similarity on specific datasets; no strong intrinsic or extrinsic evaluations (beyond limited concept-level analogy) demonstrate broader semantic properties of the embeddings.
- Question: How does the performance difference between small and large datasets relate to the training corpora used (PUBMED, CLINICAL, PM+CL)?
- Question: Does the performance gap on smaller datasets correlate with the number of concept occurrences in the training corpus?
