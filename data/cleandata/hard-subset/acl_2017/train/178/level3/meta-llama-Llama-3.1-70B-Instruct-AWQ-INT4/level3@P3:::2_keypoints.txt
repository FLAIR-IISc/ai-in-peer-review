- Proposes a method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using ontology structure.
- Adapts skip-gram with negative sampling to train embeddings for UMLS medical concepts and YAGO general-domain concepts.
- Uses representative phrases linked to ontology concepts as distant supervision, requiring no human corpus annotation.

Contributions:
- Novel approach to embed concepts, phrases, and words in a shared vector space leveraging ontologies.
- Training method for concept embeddings via distant supervision from ontology-linked phrases.
- Release of cui2vec software and a new dataset for entity similarity and relatedness.

Strengths:
- Targets non-compositional phrases and provides a plausible phrase embedding approach.
- Trained embeddings are competitive or superior for concept similarity in some settings.
- Released software is useful for biomedical NLP researchers.
- Demonstrates ability to model both abstract biomedical concepts and concrete real-world entities.

Weaknesses:
- Methodology lacks strong novelty, essentially a tweak to skip-gram.
- Full model does not consistently outperform baselines; Choi baseline outperforms on Mayo datasets and trends similarly elsewhere.
- On larger UMNSRS dataset, performance is at best competitive with simpler baselines (Chiu).
- Calling phrase occurrences “distant supervision” is misleading since occurrences provide context, not traditional supervision.
- Introduces notation (e.g., E_W) that is never used later.
- Using a single global β to control compositionality is surprising; cross-validated zero β in some corpora argues against compositionality.

Questions for authors:
- Clarify experimental setup for Table 4: how are similarities computed given many-to-many concept↔phrase mappings?
- How do you respond to criticisms about limited novelty and inconsistent superiority of the full model?
- Explain hyperparameter choices, particularly the single global β for compositionality.
- How will you address polysemy in concept embeddings as noted in the discussion?

Additional comments:
- Paper needs a more detailed related-work discussion, especially in biomedical NLP.
- Provide more details on Mechanical Turk quality control for annotation reliability.
- Include deeper error analysis for cases where the model performs poorly.
