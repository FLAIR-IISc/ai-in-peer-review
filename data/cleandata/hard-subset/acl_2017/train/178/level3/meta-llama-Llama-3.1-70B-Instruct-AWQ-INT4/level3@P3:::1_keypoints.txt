- Proposes a novel method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus using representative phrases as distant supervision.  
- Training procedure: replace phrase/word occurrences with fixed concept identifiers in a tagged corpus and append to the original corpus.  
- Evaluated on biomedical concepts and real-world entities, showing competitive performance with models that used human annotations.  
- Introduces a new dataset of similarity and relatedness for real-world entities.  
- Main contributions: the joint embedding method, the new evaluation dataset, and competitive results without human annotations.  
- Strength: well-written, intuitive method for joint embeddings.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation on biomedical and some general-domain datasets.  
- Strength: the new real-world entity similarity dataset is a valuable resource.  
- Weakness: contribution is limited and not a major technical advance.  
- Weakness: evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: the generated test resource needs more discussion and may be the paper’s primary contribution.  
- Weakness: technical issue with Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) — it is incorrectly described as a probability and should not rank the entire vocabulary.  
- Weakness: minor editorial error (“most concepts has” → “most concepts have”).  
- Question: provide more discussion on the generated test resource and potential applications.  
- Question: how will you address the limited biomedical-focused evaluation?  
- Question: clarify and correct the technical issue with Eq. 8 (MAP calculation).  
- Question: plans for extending the approach to other domains and datasets.  
- Additional comment: overall clear and easy to follow, but needs broader evaluation, deeper discussion of the test resource, and the Eq. 8 fix.
