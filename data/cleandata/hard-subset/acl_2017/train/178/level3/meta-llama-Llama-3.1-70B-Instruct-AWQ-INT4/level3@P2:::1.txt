**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus. The method uses representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision and adapts the skip-gram with negative sampling variant of word2vec for joint embedding. The authors evaluate their approach on biomedical and general-domain concepts, using a variety of corpora and ontologies. They also introduce a novel dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.

**Contributions of the Work**

The main contributions of this work are:

1. A novel method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space, using distant supervision from phrases linked to concepts in an ontology.
2. The introduction of a novel dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, which can be used to evaluate the performance of embedding models on this task.
3. An evaluation of the proposed method on biomedical and general-domain concepts, using a variety of corpora and ontologies.

**Strengths of the Submission**

The strengths of this submission are:

1. The proposed method is natural and well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach.
2. The evaluation of the method on biomedical and general-domain concepts provides a comprehensive assessment of its performance.
3. The introduction of a novel dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities is a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

The weaknesses of this submission are:

1. The contribution of the paper is limited and not a major technical advance. The method is an extension of existing work on word embeddings, and the authors do not provide a significant improvement over previous approaches.
2. The evaluation of the method is narrowly focused on biomedical data, which limits the generality of the results.
3. The technical issue with Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) is incorrectly described as a probability and should not rank the entire vocabulary. The explanation needs correction or a natural cutoff should be defined.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more discussion on the generated test resource, including its limitations and potential applications?
2. How do you plan to address the technical issue with Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) and provide a corrected explanation or a natural cutoff?
3. Can you provide more details on the evaluation of the method on general-domain concepts, including the corpora and ontologies used?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written, and the method is natural. However, the contribution is limited, and the evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data. The generated test resource needs more discussion and may be the paper's primary contribution. Minor editorial corrections are also needed, such as correcting the sentence "most concepts has" to "most concepts have."
