- Proposal: joint learning of concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision; claims better performance and >3x vocabulary coverage.

- Contribution 1: novel method for jointly learning concept/phrase/word embeddings with distant supervision from representative phrases.

- Contribution 2: new evaluation dataset of similarity and relatedness for real-world entities identified by Wikipedia page and text phrase.

- Contribution 3: experimental evaluation on biomedical and general-domain concepts showing competitive performance.

- Strength: method is straightforward and sensibly balances atomic vs compositional treatment of phrases/concepts.

- Strength: substantial work including model development, a new dataset, and multiple analyses.

- Strength: detailed method and experiment description that aids reproducibility.

- Weakness: evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic task validation.

- Weakness: human judgments were elicited on phrases, so results reflect phrase-similarity rather than true concept-similarity.

- Weakness: medical evaluation datasets are limited or inappropriate for comprehensive assessment.

- Weakness: hyperparameters appear heavily tuned on the same datasets used for reporting, undermining result validity.

- Weakness: claim of outperforming prior work is weakened by reliance on large manual ontologies and prior clinical annotations.

- Suggestion: run extrinsic task evaluations to demonstrate real-world effectiveness.

- Suggestion: clarify and analyze handling of phrase overlaps and the atomic vs compositional hyperparameter tradeoff.

- Suggestion: develop more comprehensive, diverse, and larger-scale evaluation datasets.

- Suggestion: use robust hyperparameter tuning procedures (e.g., cross-validation) and report development-set performance.

- Minor: typo "contexts -> concepts"; clarify phrase overlap handling; matrix dimension and “negative sampling matrix” wording confusing.

- Minor: unclear how words are trained in the joint model and confusing phrase-completion wording; Equation 1 notation (use of c instead of o) is confusing.

- Minor: Pedersen et al. 2007 missing from references; 1–100 similarity scale may be overly fine-grained; replace "strings" with "phrases".

- Minor: unspecified which task was used for hyperparameter tuning; Table 3 trends hard to see—request development-set trends especially for PM+CL; missing reference to Table 5.

- Recommendation: Reject, but encourage revision and resubmission after improving evaluation datasets and methodology.
