**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus using representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision. The authors adapt the skip-gram with negative sampling variant of word2vec for joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words. The method requires no human annotations and demonstrates competitive performance with existing methods on concept similarity and relatedness tasks, while achieving more than 3x coverage in vocabulary size.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Joint Embedding of Concepts, Phrases, and Words**: The paper proposes a novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus using representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision.
2. **Competitive Performance on Biomedical Tasks**: The authors demonstrate that their method achieves competitive performance with existing methods on concept similarity and relatedness tasks in the biomedical domain.
3. **Novel Test Dataset for Real-World Entities**: The paper introduces a novel test dataset for word similarity/relatedness for real-world entities, which can be used to evaluate the performance of various methods.

**Strengths**

1. **Natural Extension of Word Embeddings**: The paper extends word embeddings to produce representations for phrases and concepts, which is a natural extension of the original idea.
2. **Well-Written and Easy to Follow**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.
3. **Competitive Performance**: The authors demonstrate that their method achieves competitive performance with existing methods on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Technical Advance**: The contribution of the paper is limited and not a major technical advance, as it builds upon existing ideas and methods.
2. **Narrow Focus on Biomedical Data**: The evaluation of the method is narrowly focused on biomedical data, which limits the generality of the results.
3. **Technical Issue with MAP Calculation**: The paper contains a technical issue with the MAP calculation, which is incorrectly described as a probability and should not rank the entire vocabulary.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more discussion on the generated test resource and its potential applications?
2. How do you plan to address the technical issue with the MAP calculation and provide a natural cutoff for the ranking?
3. Can you provide more insights on why the method achieves competitive performance on biomedical tasks and how it can be extended to other domains?
