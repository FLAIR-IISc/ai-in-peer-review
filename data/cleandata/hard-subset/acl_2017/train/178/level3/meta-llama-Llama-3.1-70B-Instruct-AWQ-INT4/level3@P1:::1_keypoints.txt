- Proposes joint learning of concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using representative phrases as distant supervision, adapting skip-gram with negative sampling.  
- Requires no human annotations and reports competitive concept similarity/relatedness results with >3x vocabulary coverage.

- Contribution: joint embedding method for concepts, phrases, and words using representative ontology phrases.  
- Contribution: competitive performance on biomedical concept similarity/relatedness tasks.  
- Contribution: introduces a new test dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.

- Strength: natural extension of word embeddings to produce phrase and concept representations.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Strength: demonstrates competitive empirical performance.

- Weakness: limited technical novelty, mainly builds on existing methods.  
- Weakness: evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data, limiting generality.  
- Weakness: MAP calculation is technically flawed (treated as a probability and ranks the entire vocabulary without a proper cutoff).

- Question: please discuss the generated test resource and its potential applications.  
- Question: how will you address the MAP calculation issue and define a natural ranking cutoff?  
- Question: why does the method perform well on biomedical tasks and how can it be extended to other domains?
