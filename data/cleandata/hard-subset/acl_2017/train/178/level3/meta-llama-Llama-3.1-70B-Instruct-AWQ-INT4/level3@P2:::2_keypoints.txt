- Joint method to embed concepts, phrases, and words in a shared vector space to capture semantic relationships.  
- Evaluation on biomedical and general-domain concept similarity/relatedness tasks showing competitiveness with annotation-heavy methods.  
- Release of cui2vec software implementation for biomedical NLP researchers.

- Targets non-compositional phrases with a plausible training approach for phrase embeddings.  
- Trained embeddings are competitive or superior for identifying concept similarity in some settings.  
- cui2vec release can help researchers train their own concept embeddings.

- Limited methodological novelty—essentially a tweak to the skip-gram model.  
- Full model not consistently best; Choi baseline substantially outperforms on Mayo datasets and shows similar trends elsewhere.  
- On the larger UMNSRS dataset the model is at best competitive with simpler baselines (Chiu).  
- Describing phrase occurrences as “distant supervision” is misleading because occurrences provide context, not traditional supervised signals.  
- Introduces notation (e.g., E_W) in Section 3.2 that is never used later.  
- Using a single global β to control compositionality is surprising; cross-validated β values (often zero) argue against compositionality.

- Clarify Table 4 experimental setup: how are similarities computed given many-to-many concept↔phrase mappings?
