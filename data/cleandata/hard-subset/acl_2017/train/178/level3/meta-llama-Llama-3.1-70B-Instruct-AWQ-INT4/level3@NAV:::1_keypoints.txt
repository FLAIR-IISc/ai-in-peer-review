- Proposes a method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings by using representative phrases as distant supervision, replacing phrase occurrences with a fixed concept ID and appending to the corpus for training.
- Evaluated approach on biomedical and general-domain concepts and reports competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.
- Contribution: a novel joint-embedding method leveraging ontology structure and unannotated corpora to produce concept, phrase, and word representations.
- Contribution: demonstrates applicability to both biomedical and general-domain concepts using appropriate corpora and ontologies.
- Contribution: introduces a new test dataset of similarity/relatedness for real-world entities identified by Wikipedia page and text phrase.
- Strength: achieves competitive performance with existing methods on similarity and relatedness benchmarks.
- Strength: method can scale to a much larger vocabulary without additional expense.
- Strength: provides a novel dataset useful for evaluating embeddings of real-world entities.
- Weakness: overall contribution is limited/subtle and not a major technical advance.
- Weakness: evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data, limiting claims of generality.
- Weakness: technical issue with Eq. 8 — it is incorrectly described as a probability and should not rank the entire vocabulary.
- Weakness: minor editorial error (e.g., "most concepts has" → "most concepts have").
- Question: request details on hyperparameter tuning and how optimal settings were determined.
- Question: ask how the authors will address the technical issue with Eq. 8 and the noted editorial correction.
- Question: request more insight into performance on general-domain concepts and comparisons to existing methods.
- Question: ask how the method could be extended to other domains and applications.
