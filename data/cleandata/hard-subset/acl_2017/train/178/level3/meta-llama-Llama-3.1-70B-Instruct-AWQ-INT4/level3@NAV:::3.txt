**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus, using representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision. The model is designed to capture semantic relationships between concepts, phrases, and words, and is evaluated on various similarity and relatedness tasks.

**Main Contributions**

1. A novel method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space, using distant supervision from phrases linked to concepts in an ontology.
2. A new evaluation dataset of similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, identified both by Wikipedia page and text phrase.
3. Experimental results showing that the proposed method captures certain semantic characteristics of ontology concepts nearly as well as prior methods that require significantly more annotation.

**Strengths**

1. The model is straightforward and sensibly balances treating phrases/concepts as atomic vs compositional.
2. The paper presents substantial work, including model development, a new evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations/analyses.
3. The method is able to scale to a much larger vocabulary without additional expense.

**Weaknesses**

1. The evaluation uses only intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic task evaluation.
2. Human judgments were elicited on phrases, so datasets should be treated/analyzed as phrase-similarity rather than concept-similarity.
3. Medical evaluation datasets are very limited or inappropriate.
4. Hyperparameters are heavily tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results, undermining result validity.
5. The claim that the method outperforms prior work is weak because authors still rely on large manual ontologies and prior annotations from clinical records.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limitations of the evaluation datasets and the lack of extrinsic task evaluation?
2. Can you provide more details on how the hyperparameters were tuned and how the results would change with different hyperparameter settings?
3. How do you plan to extend the method to more abstract data in the general domain, similar to previous work with BabelNet?
