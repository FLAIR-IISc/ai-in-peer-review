- Proposes a method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases as distant supervision and an adapted skip-gram with negative sampling.  
- Evaluates the approach on biomedical and general-domain concepts across multiple corpora and ontologies.  
- Introduces a novel dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.  
- Main contribution: a joint embedding method that integrates ontology-linked phrases as supervision.  
- Main contribution: the new dataset for evaluating entity similarity/relatedness.  
- Main contribution: empirical evaluation across biomedical and general domains.  
- Strength: method is natural, well-motivated, and clearly explained.  
- Strength: evaluation across biomedical and general domains gives a broad performance assessment.  
- Strength: the new dataset is a valuable resource for the community.  
- Weakness: technical novelty is limited—method is an extension of existing embedding approaches without major advance.  
- Weakness: evaluation is overly focused on biomedical data, limiting claims of generality.  
- Weakness: Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) is incorrectly described as a probability and should not rank the entire vocabulary; needs correction or a defined cutoff.  
- Question: provide more discussion of the generated test resource, including limitations and applications.  
- Question: clarify how the Eq. 8 technical issue will be addressed or provide a natural cutoff.  
- Question: give more details on general-domain evaluation, including which corpora and ontologies were used.  
- Additional: paper is well-written but contributions are limited and the dataset may be its primary contribution; minor editorial fixes (e.g., "most concepts have") are needed.
