- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases as distant supervision trained on unannotated text.
- Contribution: Introduces a novel method combining structured knowledge with word embeddings to produce context-based representations for concepts, phrases, and words.
- Contribution: Presents a new evaluation dataset of real-world entity similarity and relatedness identified by Wikipedia page and text phrase.
- Contribution: Performs substantial model development, evaluation, and analysis across multiple corpora and datasets.
- Strength: Method is straightforward, sensible, and balances atomic vs compositional treatment of phrases/concepts.
- Strength: Significant effort in model development and creation of a new evaluation dataset.
- Strength: Achieves competitive performance on biomedical and general-domain concept similarity and relatedness tasks.
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic task evaluation demonstrating practical utility.
- Weakness: Medical evaluation datasets used are limited or inappropriate, potentially misrepresenting real-world performance.
- Weakness: Hyperparameters appear tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results, undermining result validity.
- Weakness: Lacks deeper analyses separating phrase-word compositionality from concept-phrase relations and exploration of atomic vs compositional hyperparameter tradeoffs.
- Question: How will the authors address limitations of the medical evaluation datasets?
- Question: How will the method be extended to handle more complex concepts and relationships?
- Question: Can the authors provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and selection of optimal hyperparameters?
