**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a weakly-supervised method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space. The method uses representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision and trains embeddings on an unannotated text corpus. The authors evaluate their approach on biomedical and general-domain concepts, achieving competitive performance with existing methods on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. **Novel method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words**: The paper proposes a method that combines structured knowledge with word embeddings to train context-based representations for concepts, phrases, and words.
2. **New evaluation dataset for real-world entities**: The authors present a novel dataset of similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, identified both by Wikipedia page and text phrase.
3. **Substantial work on model development, evaluation, and analysis**: The paper presents a thorough evaluation of the proposed method, including multiple evaluations and analyses on various corpora and datasets.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Straightforward and sensible approach**: The method is easy to understand and implement, and it sensibly balances treating phrases/concepts as atomic vs compositional.
2. **Substantial work on model development and evaluation**: The authors have put in significant effort to develop and evaluate their method, including creating a new evaluation dataset.
3. **Competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks**: The proposed method achieves competitive performance with existing methods on biomedical and general-domain concepts.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of extrinsic task evaluation**: The evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic task evaluation, which is essential to demonstrate the practical applicability of the method.
2. **Limited medical evaluation datasets**: The medical evaluation datasets used are very limited or inappropriate, which may not accurately reflect the performance of the method in real-world applications.
3. **Hyperparameter tuning on the same datasets used for reporting results**: The hyperparameters are heavily tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results, which undermines the validity of the results.
4. **Lack of deeper analyses**: The paper lacks deeper analyses separating phrase-word compositionality from concept-phrase relations and exploration of the atomic vs compositional hyperparameter tradeoff.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how you plan to address the limitations of the medical evaluation datasets?
2. How do you plan to extend the method to handle more complex concepts and relationships?
3. Can you provide more insights into the hyperparameter tuning process and how you selected the optimal hyperparameters?

**Recommendation**

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I recommend rejecting the paper in its current form. The authors should improve the evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission. Specifically, they should address the limitations of the medical evaluation datasets, provide more insights into the hyperparameter tuning process, and extend the method to handle more complex concepts and relationships.
