- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using ontology representative phrases as distant supervision.  
- Adapts skip-gram with negative sampling (word2vec variant) to embed concepts, phrases, and words jointly.  
- Experiments on UMLS (medical) and YAGO (general) concepts with multiple corpora.  
- Claims superior performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks and more than 3× vocabulary coverage without human corpus annotation.  

Contributions:
- Novel joint embedding method for concepts, phrases, and words from unlabeled corpora.  
- Uses representative ontology phrases as distant supervision, removing need for manual annotation.  
- Provides a new evaluation dataset and comprehensive evaluations/analyses.  

Strengths:
- Method is simple and balances atomic vs compositional treatment of phrases/concepts.  
- Substantial work: model development, new dataset, multiple evaluations.  
- Competitive biomedical similarity/relatedness performance without human annotation.  

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; no extrinsic task evaluation.  
- Human judgments collected on phrases, so datasets reflect phrase-similarity not pure concept-similarity.  
- Medical evaluation datasets are small or unsuitable (mini MayoSRS 29 pairs, MayoSRS 101 pairs with low agreement, UMNSRS single-word only).  
- Hyperparameters heavily tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results, risking overfitting/validity concerns.  
- Claim of outperforming prior work is weakened by reliance on large manual ontologies and prior clinical annotations.  

Questions for authors:
- How will you address limitations of current evaluation datasets and methodology?  
- How are phrase overlaps handled in the method (details requested)?  
- How will you explore the atomic vs compositional hyperparameter tradeoff in future work?  
- Why does the method perform well on biomedical tasks despite evaluation dataset limitations?
