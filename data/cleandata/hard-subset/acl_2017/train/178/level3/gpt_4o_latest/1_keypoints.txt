- Paper proposes appending an ontology-tagged corpus to the original text and training modified skip-gram embeddings to jointly represent words, phrases, and ontology concepts.  
- Introduces a new dataset for real-world entity similarity and relatedness, filling a gap in existing resources.  
- Presents a scalable, annotation-free method for embedding concepts, phrases, and words jointly, evaluated mainly in biomedicine.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance and preservation of semantic relationships and clustering of similar concepts.  
- Well-written and clearly structured paper that makes the methodology and experiments easy to follow.  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on the biomedical domain, limiting demonstrated generalizability despite inclusion of YAGO.  
- The methodological novelty is incremental, building on existing techniques rather than introducing a large technical advance.  
- Technical concern: Equation 8’s MAP calculation incorrectly references a probability and needs clarification.  
- Compositionality tradeoff: high similarity between concepts and representative phrases suggests reduced capture of non-lexical/abstract concept information.  
- Minor grammatical error: “most concepts has” should be “most concepts have.”  
- Request clarification on how the Equation 8 issue affects results reported in Section 5.5.  
- Suggestion to evaluate embeddings on downstream tasks (e.g., entity linking, analogy completion) to strengthen utility claims.  
- Question about performance and generalizability on general-domain corpora or domains with less-structured ontologies.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on clarifying the MAP calculation and discussing/generalizing the method’s applicability.
