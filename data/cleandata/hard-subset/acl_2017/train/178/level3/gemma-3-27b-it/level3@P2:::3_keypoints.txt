- Paper proposes a weakly-supervised method that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases as distant supervision.  
- Learns embeddings from unlabeled corpora, requires no human annotation, and reports substantial vocabulary coverage and competitive similarity/relatedness performance.

Strengths:
- Practical and scalable: distant supervision removes need for manual annotation and enables larger vocabulary coverage (~3x).  
- Joint embedding framework: addresses multi-word expressions and concept variation by integrating concepts, phrases, and words.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: tested on multiple corpora (PubMed, Clinical, WikiNYT) and ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) and introduces a new general-domain entity similarity dataset.

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; no extrinsic downstream evaluations to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Human judgments were elicited on phrases, not concepts, so results may reflect phrase-similarity rather than true concept similarity.  
- Biomedical datasets are small or unsuitable (e.g., Mini MayoSRS size 29, low inter-rater agreement, UMNSRS single-word focus).  
- Hyperparameters tuned on the evaluation datasets, risking overfitting and inflating reported performance.  
- Improvements over strong baselines are modest and method still depends on large curated ontologies and existing annotations.  
- Limited analysis of compositionality and impact of hyperparameters controlling phrase vs. atomic representations.

Questions for authors:
- How are overlapping phrases handled during preprocessing when multiple phrases overlap in a sentence?  
- Please clarify the confusing notation in Equation 1 where 'c' and context word 'o' are used ambiguously.  
- Can you provide hyperparameter tuning performance on a separate development set (especially PM+CL) to assess generalization?  
- Given annotations were collected for phrases, how do you justify interpreting results as concept similarity rather than phrase similarity?  
- What is the computational cost of jointly trained embeddings versus independently trained embeddings?
