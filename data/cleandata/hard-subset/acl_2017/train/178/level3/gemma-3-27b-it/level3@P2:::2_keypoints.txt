- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using representative ontology-linked phrases as supervision and a skip-gram adaptation, evaluated on UMLS and YAGO.  
- Core contribution: learn concept embeddings from unlabeled text to avoid expensive manual annotation; secondary: release of software ("cui2vec") and a new evaluation dataset.  

Strengths:
- Addresses representation of non-compositional multi-word expressions, especially relevant in biomedicine.  
- Achieves competitive performance on several concept similarity and relatedness tasks, often matching or exceeding baselines on larger datasets.  
- Public release of code and dataset is a useful resource for the community.  

Weaknesses:
- Methodological novelty is limited—mainly an incremental adaptation of skip-gram with negative sampling.  
- Inconsistent performance versus strong baselines (Choi et al. 2016b), notably worse on smaller MayoSRS datasets, raising robustness concerns.  
- Use of a single global β to control compositionality is questionable; cross-validation often favors β=0, undermining the compositionality mechanism.  

Questions to authors:
- How is cosine similarity computed for concept pairs given many-to-many concept↔phrase mappings (max, average, or other)?  
- Why introduce notation like E_W in Section 3.2 if it is never used later?  
- Why characterize the phrase-as-supervision approach as “distant supervision” when it relies on contextual co-occurrence rather than KB-triple labeling—please clarify the terminology.  
- Table 5 shows simple averaged phrase vectors often perform best—what is the added value of explicit concept embeddings versus phrase-based approximations?  

Additional comments:
- Fleiss’ kappa may be inappropriate for the created dataset’s annotator setup; consider Krippendorff’s alpha.  
- Ablation study should include more thorough hyperparameter analysis, particularly for β and η.  
- Discussion should more explicitly acknowledge limitations and outline future research directions.
