- Proposes joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases from ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) as distant supervision.  
- Aims to overcome costly annotation and lack of textual context in prior methods.  
- Presents a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness evaluation dataset.  
- Provides a relatively simple framework for joint embeddings and explores atomic vs. compositional tradeoffs.  
- Main weakness: no extrinsic task evaluation—only intrinsic similarity/relatedness used.  
- Evaluation interprets results as phrase-similarity rather than concept-similarity because human judgments were collected on phrases.  
- MayoSRS is very small and shows low annotator agreement, raising reliability concerns.  
- UMNSRS contains only single-word concepts, conflicting with the paper’s focus on multi-word concepts.  
- Hyperparameter tuning was performed on the same datasets used for evaluation, compromising result validity.  
- Claim of outperforming prior work is weakened by dependence on large, manually curated ontologies and existing clinical annotations.  
- Analysis is shallow; fails to disentangle phrase-word compositionality from concept-phrase relationships (e.g., headword effects).  
- Insufficient investigation of the impact of atomic vs. compositional hyperparameter settings.  
- Recommendation: Reject; require improved evaluation (add at least one extrinsic task) and address dataset and tuning concerns before resubmission.  
- Question: What is the rationale for choosing hyperparameters (β, η) and how sensitive are results to them?  
- Question: Please discuss potential biases from using human judgments on phrases when evaluating concept similarity.  
- Question: What experiments have been or will be run to assess embeddings on downstream tasks?
