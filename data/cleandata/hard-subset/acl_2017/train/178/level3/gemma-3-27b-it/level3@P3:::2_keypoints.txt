- Contribution: Provides a novel dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness and uses established biomedical datasets for a solid evaluation framework.  
- Contribution: Presents a scalable weakly-supervised method that generates embeddings for a much larger concept vocabulary than manually annotated approaches.  
- Contribution: Explores joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words in a single vector space, offering insights into their relationships.

- Strength: Tackles non-compositional concept representation (e.g., named diseases) by anchoring ontology-defined phrases as training signals.  
- Strength: Achieves competitive and sometimes superior performance on concept similarity tasks, showing the approach’s potential.  
- Strength: Releases the cui2vec software and the novel dataset, providing valuable resources to the community.  
- Strength: Conducts a comprehensive evaluation with strong baselines and hyperparameter tuning.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—mainly a tweak to skip-gram with added β and η parameters.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent performance, notably substantial underperformance on the Mayo datasets compared to Choi et al.  
- Weakness: Competitive but not state-of-the-art on larger benchmarks (e.g., UMNSRS), suggesting limited generalization of gains.  
- Weakness: Calling phrase occurrences “distant supervision” is misleading since they are not traditional labeled signals.

- Question: Why does the model underperform so significantly on the Mayo datasets versus Choi et al. (2016b); what architectural or training differences explain this?  
- Question: Cross-validation often selects β and η = 0 for some corpora—why does compositionality appear unhelpful in these settings?  
- Question: In Table 4’s many-to-many concept↔phrase mappings, how are cosine similarities aggregated (averaged or otherwise)?  
- Question: Table 5 shows averaged phrase vectors outperform learned concept embeddings—does adding the ontology help, or would seed-phrase training suffice?

- Comment: Notation introduced in Section 3.2 (e.g., E_W) is unused later and unnecessary.  
- Comment: Supplemental material usefully details the novel dataset collection process.  
- Comment: The discussion candidly notes limitations and future work, showing self-awareness.
