- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that replaces phrases with unique concept IDs to train joint embeddings of concepts, phrases, and words using a modified skip-gram model.
- Applies the method to biomedical (UMLS) and general-domain (YAGO) ontologies and trains on large corpora (PubMed, clinical notes, Wikipedia).
- Achieves competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks without human annotation and with wider vocabulary coverage.
- Introduces a new dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.
- Main contribution: a practical, scalable technique that bridges lexical and conceptual representations via distant supervision.
- Secondary contribution: the new similarity/relatedness dataset filling a resource gap.
- Extensive experimental evaluation across corpora and ontologies supports the approach.
- Strength: method is conceptually simple, computationally efficient, and scales to large ontologies and corpora.
- Strength: competitive results compared to state-of-the-art despite no manual annotation.
- Strength: the new dataset is a valuable benchmark with a clearly described collection and validation process.
- Strength: thorough evaluations, comparisons, ablations, and hyperparameter descriptions.
- Weakness: limited novelty since distant supervision and word embeddings are established techniques.
- Weakness: evaluation focused mainly on biomedical data, leaving generalizability to other domains less certain.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the new dataset’s characteristics and potential biases; Fleiss’ kappa of 0.24 indicates only fair annotator agreement.
- Weakness: technical imprecision in Equation 8 (MAP) which is incorrectly framed and needs clarification.
- Weakness: minor editorial errors (e.g., grammatical mistakes).
- Question: rationale and sensitivity of hyperparameters, particularly β and η?
- Question: how does performance compare to contextual embeddings (BERT, ELMo) fine-tuned on the same biomedical data?
- Question: what are the limitations and failure modes of the distant supervision labeling approach?
- Question: what potential biases exist in the new dataset and how might they affect evaluations?
- Additional comment: supplementary material provides useful hyperparameter and dataset details.
- Additional comment: the released software implementation (cui2vec) supports reproducibility.
- Additional comment: consider exploring alternative knowledge integration methods, e.g., graph neural networks.
