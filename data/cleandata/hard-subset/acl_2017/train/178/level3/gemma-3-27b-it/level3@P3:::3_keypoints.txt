- Novel dataset: Introduces a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset addressing limited existing resources.  
- Methodological framework: Proposes a weakly-supervised joint embedding method for concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases as distant supervision.  
- Comprehensive experimentation: Evaluates the model across multiple corpora and ontologies with several experiments.

- Sensible approach: Balances atomic and compositional treatment of semantic units and pragmatically uses representative phrases for supervision.  
- Substantial work: Presents model development, a new dataset, and multiple evaluations.  
- Improved vocabulary coverage: Achieves broader vocabulary size compared to annotation-dependent methods.  
- Clear presentation: Paper is generally well written and explanations are clear.

- Flawed evaluation: Relies only on intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic downstream evaluations, limiting real-world claims.  
- Misinterpretation of data: Evaluations treat phrase-level judgments as concept similarity, conflating phrases with abstract concepts.  
- Weak medical evaluations: Medical datasets are too small or have low agreement, and one dataset contains only single-word concepts, undermining phrase evaluation.  
- Potential overfitting: Extensive hyperparameter tuning on test datasets risks inflated, non-generalizable results.  
- Reliance on external resources: Performance gains may stem from large ontologies and prior annotations rather than the proposed model itself.  
- Missing deeper analysis: Lacks separation of phrase-word compositionality from concept-phrase relations and studies of headword effects.  
- Lack of trend reporting: Table 3 omits development-set trends (notably PM+CL), obscuring tuning stability.
