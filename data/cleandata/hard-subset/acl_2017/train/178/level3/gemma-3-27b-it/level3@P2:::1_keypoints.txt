- Primary contribution: Release of a novel dataset for evaluating semantic similarity and relatedness of real-world entities, with baseline evaluation using the proposed method.  
- Primary contribution: Weakly-supervised method showing concept embeddings can be learned from ontology-provided representative phrases without human annotation.  
- Primary contribution: Empirical validation on biomedical and general-domain ontologies showing competitive performance given the weak supervision.

- Strength: Novel dataset fills a gap by focusing on entities (not words) and includes documented quality-control procedures.  
- Strength: Weakly-supervised distant supervision approach reduces dependence on costly manual annotation, useful for domains like biomedicine.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple baselines and datasets (biomedical and general-domain).  
- Strength: Clear presentation, helpful diagram, and acknowledgement of limitations.

- Weakness: Method offers limited novelty—incremental extension of skip-gram and distant supervision rather than a major conceptual advance.  
- Weakness: Experimental focus is skewed toward the biomedical domain, limiting demonstrated generalizability.  
- Weakness: Distant supervision can introduce noisy/incorrect labels because phrase occurrences do not always map uniquely to concepts; impact not fully explored.  
- Weakness: Error in MAP calculation (Equation 8) — ranking the entire vocabulary for precision is impractical and needs correction/clarification.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of the new dataset’s quality (more than Fleiss’ kappa), score distribution, and entity-type breakdown needed.
