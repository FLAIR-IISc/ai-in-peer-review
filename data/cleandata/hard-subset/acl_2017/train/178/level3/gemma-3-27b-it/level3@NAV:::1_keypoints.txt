- Proposes extending word embeddings to concepts by substituting phrase/word occurrences with fixed concept identifiers and jointly training embeddings.
- Application focused on biomedical domain using corpora and ontologies like UMLS and YAGO.
- Introduces a new dataset for evaluating word/concept similarity and relatedness, a potentially valuable community resource.
- Paper is well-written and clearly communicates the proposed method.
- Methodology is intuitive and a natural extension of existing embedding techniques.
- Core technical contribution is incremental; idea is a straightforward extension of prior work.
- Demonstrated performance gains are modest, especially compared to strongly supervised methods.
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data, limiting claims of generalizability.
- Dataset creation lacks sufficient detail on crowd-sourcing quality control, selection criteria, and potential biases.
- Technical error in Equation 8: MAP is incorrectly described as a probability and seems to rank the entire vocabulary without a natural cutoff.
- Minor editorial correction needed ("most concepts has" → "most concepts have").
- Questions for authors: provide detailed dataset creation criteria and quality controls.
- Questions for authors: justify ranking the entire vocabulary for MAP or consider applying a similarity-score cutoff; plan to evaluate outside biomedical domain; clarify downstream scenarios where the method excels.
- Suggestion: move or summarize hyperparameter tuning details from supplementary material into the main paper.
