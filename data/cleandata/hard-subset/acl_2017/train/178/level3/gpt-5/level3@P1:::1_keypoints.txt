- Proposes a weakly supervised pipeline that jointly embeds words, surface phrases, and ontology concepts by replacing phrases with concept identifiers and training with skip-gram on tagged and untagged corpora.
- Uses ontology-provided representative phrases as distant supervision and alternates training on tagged/untagged data, with optional compositionality regularizers β (phrase↔words) and η (concept↔phrases).
- Instantiated on UMLS and YAGO with PubMed, clinical notes, Wikipedia, and Gigaword; evaluated on intrinsic similarity/relatedness, alignment analyses, and semantic-type clustering.
- Introduces a new human-annotated dataset for real-world entity similarity and relatedness.
- Contribution: a practical, scalable weakly supervised method requiring no manual corpus annotation and achieving high concept coverage.
- Contribution: a new entity-level similarity/relatedness evaluation resource with baseline results.
- Contribution: extensive empirical study showing competitive intrinsic performance and ~3x vocabulary coverage over prior concept-embedding methods.
- Strength: method is practical and portable to domains with ontologies but scarce annotations, with compelling coverage gains.
- Strength: achieves top/near-top Spearman correlations on larger biomedical similarity datasets with minimal annotation.
- Strength: unified embedding space enables cross-level coherence checks, compositionality analyses, and semantic-type clustering.
- Strength: resource contribution and transparency, with implementation (cui2vec) and documented hyperparameters and corpora.
- Weakness: limited technical novelty—mainly SGNS on augmented text with simple alignment terms; ontology structure largely unused.
- Weakness: evaluation is narrow and intrinsic; lacks downstream task experiments (e.g., entity linking, concept normalization, clinical prediction).
- Weakness: new dataset is small, has only fair inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ=0.24), and needs stronger validation and analysis.
- Weakness: methodological ambiguity in MAP/AP formulation—ranking over full vocabulary and “probability” wording are nonstandard and potentially misleading.
- Weakness: distant-supervision injects noise and ambiguity (multi-concept phrases, greedy matching) with limited analysis of its impact or disambiguation strategies.
- Question: clarify Eq.8/MAP candidate set, remove “probability” wording, and report AP@k or AP restricted to a candidate pool.
- Question: how frequent are multi-concept phrases, what is their effect, and did you try weighting by priors or context-based disambiguation?
- Question: can you report downstream task results (entity linking, concept normalization) to demonstrate practical utility?
- Question: did you try incorporating ontology graph relations or retrofitting/posthoc alignment, and what were the effects?
- Question: will the dataset be publicly released with full guidelines, annotator demographics, item-level agreement, and expansion plans?
- Additional: fix minor editorial error (“most concepts has” → “most concepts have”).
- Additional: report effect sizes/confidence intervals or significance tests alongside Spearman ρ for model comparisons.
