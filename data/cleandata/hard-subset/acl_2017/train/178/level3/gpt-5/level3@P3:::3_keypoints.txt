- Proposes a weakly supervised joint embedding of ontology concepts, representative phrases, and words by adapting skip-gram with negative sampling and using ontology phrase–concept mappings as distant supervision with two balancing hyperparameters (β, η).  
- Main contribution: a simple, sensible joint embedding framework that balances atomic and compositional modeling via β (words→phrases) and η (phrases→concepts).  
- Additional contributions: a set of analyses/ablations showing coverage and learned-space properties (concept–phrase closeness, semantic type clustering).  
- Additional contribution: a small crowdsourced general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset.  
- Strength: explicit, easy-to-implement modeling tradeoff targeting atomic vs compositional representations.  
- Strength: substantial scope and effort—two ontologies (UMLS, YAGO), multiple corpora, new evaluation set, and multiple analyses.  
- Strength: approach scales without manual corpus annotation and achieves broader concept coverage than some biomedical baselines.  
- Strength: joint embeddings of words/phrases/concepts enable direct cross-unit comparisons useful for tasks like concept normalization or entity linking.  
- Strength: competitive intrinsic similarity/relatedness results on several biomedical sets and on the new dataset.  
- Weakness: evaluation is exclusively intrinsic (similarity/relatedness); no extrinsic downstream tasks are provided, undermining claims of utility.  
- Weakness: biomedical evaluation datasets are small or mismatched (Mini MayoSRS tiny, MayoSRS low agreement, UMNSRS single-word only), so conclusions are fragile.  
- Weakness: human judgments were elicited on phrase surface forms; analyses should distinguish phrase-level vs concept-level evaluation to avoid conflating signals.  
- Weakness: heavy hyperparameter tuning leaked on reported test datasets (no clean dev/test separation), undermining validity of reported correlations.  
- Weakness: claims of outperforming prior work are overstated because the method relies on large curated ontologies and existing manual annotations.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the atomic vs compositional tradeoff—β and η are not systematically probed (no sweeps by compositionality, polysemy, headword effects).  
- Weakness: the new entity dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.24) and uses a too-fine-grained 0–100 scale, making small correlation differences hard to interpret.  
- Question: clarify greedy phrase matching and overlap handling, especially when overlapping phrases map to different concepts (provide examples and tie-breaking rules).  
- Question: specify which datasets/tasks were used to tune β, η, window size, negatives, and dimension, and provide a clean dev/test split with test-only results.  
- Question: precisely explain how word embeddings are updated in joint training when phrases are collapsed—does word training use TU only, TT only, or both, and what is the update schedule?  
- Question: define the “negative sampling matrix” (is it the SGNS output/context embedding table?), why it’s named separately, and how it’s shared across words/phrases/concepts.  
- Question: for datasets providing both CUIs and surface forms, report correlations separately for concept–concept (CUIs) and phrase–phrase (strings) to quantify surface-form impact.  
- Question: provide performance curves across β and η and stratified analyses by phrase compositionality and ambiguity (#concepts per phrase).  
- Question: demonstrate generalization with at least one extrinsic downstream task (e.g., concept normalization, entity linking, coding prediction).  
- Minor points: multiple requested clarifications/fixes (typos, rename “negative sampling matrix,” clarify equation notation, add missing citations, report rater calibration/reliability, clarify UMNSRS single-word limitation, improve dataset framing and release higher-IAA subset).  
- Recommendation: Reject; encourage resubmission after adding extrinsic evaluations, creating clean dev/test splits or larger medical evaluation sets, and expanding analyses of β/η, compositionality, and phrase/word/concept interactions.
