- Proposes a scalable joint embedding of words, phrases, and ontology concepts using unlabeled corpora plus phrase→concept mappings, adapting skip-gram with phrase collapsing and equal-sharing updates to candidate concepts.
- Contribution: practical method achieving competitive similarity/relatedness performance with >3× concept coverage vs. supervised baselines.
- Contribution: releases an open-source implementation (cui2vec) and a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset (people/places/organizations).
- Contribution: provides empirical analyses of compositionality and semantic-type structure showing phrase–word and concept–phrase interactions and partial type clustering.
- Strength: handles non-compositionality and synonymy by collapsing multiword mentions to atomic tokens and separating phrase/concept vectors.
- Strength: competitive intrinsic results without manual annotations (top correlation on UMNSRS Similarity) and robust cross-corpus training.
- Strength: broad coverage that scales with corpus diversity and resources released for reproducibility.
- Strength: thoughtful analyses (compositionality, type clustering) that go beyond single-number benchmarks.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—mostly a straightforward SGNS adaptation with simple regularizers β/η.
- Weakness: inconsistent empirical wins—strong baselines (e.g., CHOI) outperform on some tasks and gains on others are modest.
- Weakness: calling the method “distant supervision” is misleading because ambiguous phrase updates uniformly credit all candidate concepts, providing no disambiguation signal.
- Weakness: concept embeddings sometimes add little value—averaged phrase embeddings can outperform direct concept vectors in general-domain experiments.
- Weakness: global compositionality regularizers (single β, η) are coarse; tuned values often zero, suggesting mis-specification and need for per-phrase/type weighting.
- Weakness: clarity/notation issues around embedding matrices, negative-sampling interactions, and details of similarity computation for many-to-many mappings.
- Weakness: evaluation limits—new dataset has low inter-annotator agreement, hyperparameters tuned on test sets, and no downstream task evaluations.
- Question: do you perform any sense disambiguation for ambiguous phrases, and how sensitive are results to uniform split among candidate concepts?
- Question: how are concept–phrase similarities computed at test time when datasets include both CUIs and surface forms (CUI-only, phrase averages, or best-of)?
- Question: did you try learning β and η per phrase or per semantic type (adaptive weights) to handle idiomatic vs. compositional phrases?
- Question: have you incorporated ontology graph structure (is-a, synonyms) as additional training signals to improve type clustering and concept gains?
- Question: will you release train/test splits for the entity dataset and evaluate on extrinsic tasks (e.g., concept normalization, entity linking) to demonstrate practical benefits?
