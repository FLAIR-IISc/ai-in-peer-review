- Proposes a weakly supervised pipeline that replaces ontology-mapped phrases with concept IDs and trains skip-gram to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words.  
- Introduces a new crowdsourced entity similarity/relatedness dataset for people, places, and organizations.

Contributions:
- New general-domain entity similarity/relatedness evaluation resource with crowdsourcing details.  
- Simple, scalable distant-supervision pipeline (concept-tagging + skip-gram) requiring no manual corpus annotation.  
- Empirical analysis showing preserved concept-phrase links, partial semantic-type clustering, competitive intrinsic results, and ~3x vocabulary coverage versus some baselines.

Strengths:
- Practical, scalable method with minimal supervision and high coverage (e.g., many UMLS/YAGO entities).  
- Competitive intrinsic performance on UMNSRS and strong approximations from phrase-based vectors.  
- Single joint embedding space for concepts, phrases, and words enabling flexible mappings and compositional approximations.  
- Valuable new entity-level evaluation resource filling a gap beyond word similarity.  
- Clear writing and reproducible framing (cui2vec).

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty/technical depth; core idea is a straightforward extension and compositional regularizers add little.  
- Narrow evaluation: heavy biomedical focus, no downstream tasks to demonstrate practical utility, YAGO results rely mainly on the new dataset.  
- Crowdsourced dataset needs deeper validation (low κ≈0.24, small HIT sizes, unclear reliability).  
- Technical flaw in MAP/precision analysis (Pr mischaracterized; ranking over full vocabulary inappropriate); metric should be renamed and recomputed over sensible candidate sets (MAP@k/R-precision).  
- Risk of tuning on test data and insufficient dev/test controls; potential overfitting from cross-validation protocol.  
- Simplistic ambiguity handling (greedy longest match, uniform sense credit) with no sensitivity analysis.

Questions for authors:
- Please correct Eq. 8/9, clarify candidate sets, and report MAP@k / R-precision (sensitivity to k) to validate §5.4 conclusions.  
- How are polysemous phrases handled in tagging; were disambiguation heuristics tried and how sensitive are results?  
- Will you release the datasets, rating guidelines, per-item agreement, and recommended splits/protocols (e.g., bootstrapped CIs)?  
- Any preliminary downstream results (entity linking, concept normalization, clinical prediction) to demonstrate practical benefits?

Additional comments:
- Add more general-domain evaluations (e.g., Wikipedia/YAGO entity linking) and at least one downstream biomedical task.  
- Clarify symbol overloading in Eq. 9 (what |P| denotes).  
- Minor editorial fix: line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
