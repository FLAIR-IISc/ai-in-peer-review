- Proposes weakly supervised method that replaces phrase mentions with fixed concept IDs and trains a skip-gram model on tagged and original text to jointly learn word, phrase, and concept embeddings.
- Experiments conducted on biomedical (UMLS) and general-domain (YAGO) concepts.
- Introduces a new entity-level similarity/relatedness dataset for real-world entities.
- Contribution: new evaluation resource that could be broadly useful beyond this method.
- Contribution: simple, scalable pipeline for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words using distant supervision from ontologies.
- Contribution: empirical study showing competitive intrinsic performance and high coverage on biomedical corpora.
- Strength: practical and reproducible approach that scales and achieves >3x coverage over some prior concept-embedding resources.
- Strength: joint embedding space aligns concepts with their surface phrases, benefiting downstream tasks like entity linking and normalization.
- Strength: strong correlations on larger biomedical similarity datasets and reasonable relatedness performance compared to annotation-heavy methods.
- Strength: the released similarity/relatedness dataset fills an evaluation gap for real-world entities.
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited—largely an incremental extension of word2vec with distant supervision.
- Weakness: evaluation is narrow and mostly intrinsic and biomedical; lacks downstream task evaluations to demonstrate generality.
- Weakness: technical flaw in MAP formulation (Eq. 8)—described incorrectly as a probability and ranks full vocabulary; needs correction or principled cutoff/candidate set.
- Weakness: dataset description is thin—insufficient detail on annotator instructions, quality control, low inter-annotator agreement (κ≈0.24), demographics, licensing, and recommended splits.
- Minor editorial issue: grammatical error “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
- Questions: how are phrase-to-multiple-concept mappings handled beyond uniform normalization; any ambiguity-aware weighting tested?
- Questions: can you provide downstream evaluations where concept embeddings impact performance (e.g., entity linking, clinical coding)?
- Questions: will the new dataset be released with clear license and standard train/dev/test splits?
- Questions: please clarify and correct the MAP formulation and whether ranking should be over full vocabulary or candidate sets.
