- Method: weakly supervised joint embeddings for ontology concepts, phrases, and words using phrases from unlabeled corpora as distant supervision and a modified skip-gram; β and η control atomic vs compositional behavior.

- Evaluation: tested on biomedical (PubMed, clinical notes) and general (Wikipedia+NYT) corpora with MayoSRS, UMNSRS and a new Mechanical Turk entity dataset; shows competitive correlations and much broader coverage.

- Contribution: practical, scalable joint embedding framework with a tunable atomic/compositional trade-off (β, η) built on skip-gram.

- Contribution: new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset and public release of implementation and resources.

- Contribution: empirical analyses indicating partial preservation of ontology structure (semantic-type clustering, concept–phrase proximity).

- Strength: clean, interpretable β/η mechanisms for atomic vs compositional modeling; simple implementation and low complexity.

- Strength: large scale and coverage using UMLS/YAGO distant supervision, reporting >3x vocabulary coverage gains.

- Strength: competitive intrinsic performance without manual corpus annotation, useful in low-annotation settings.

- Strength: release of new evaluation resource and code/data enhances reproducibility.

- Strength: diagnostic analyses (compositionality, proximity, clustering) provide insight beyond headline scores.

- Weakness: evaluation limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; no extrinsic task validation to demonstrate practical gains.

- Weakness: heavy hyperparameter tuning on the same evaluation datasets risks overfitting; needs clean dev/test separation.

- Weakness: general-domain dataset conflates phrases and concepts—human judgments on surface strings may not match model’s concept embeddings.

- Weakness: biomedical datasets are small/noisy (e.g., Mini MayoSRS) and often single-word, failing to stress multiword compositionality or normalization.

- Weakness: claims of annotation savings/novelty are overstated given dependence on large, manually curated ontologies (UMLS/YAGO).

- Weakness: limited analysis of β/η trade-off—lacks per-corpus/phrase-type sensitivity, headword effects, and finer-grained ablations.

- Question: How are overlapping/embedded phrases resolved during greedy matching—specify deterministic tie-breaking and overlap frequency/effects?

- Question: Which exact development sets were used for hyperparameter selection per corpus; can results be provided with fixed dev and held-out test splits?

- Question: For the new entity dataset, were annotators shown disambiguating links or only surface strings; report ambiguous-phrase proportion and separate analyses.

- Question: Can the authors provide at least one extrinsic evaluation (e.g., entity linking, concept-based retrieval, ICD grouping)?

- Question: β and η are often tuned to 0 in results—does this imply limited benefit from compositionality; provide performance vs β/η curves and qualitative examples.

- Minor issues: fix typos/notation (e.g., “contexts”→“concepts”, Equation 1 c→o), clarify phrase overlap handling, matrix shapes, training update details, and correct citations.

- Reporting suggestions: justify 1–100 similarity scale, report inter-annotator variance by bin, include confidence intervals for Spearman’s ρ and per-category breakdowns on the new dataset.

- Overall recommendation: Reject as submitted; work is promising but needs clean dev/test splits, extrinsic tasks, and deeper β/η analysis for a strong resubmission.
