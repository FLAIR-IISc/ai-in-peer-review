Summary and contributions
- Primary: A joint embedding model that sensibly balances atomic (concept/phrase) and compositional (phrase/word) representations via explicit β (phrase-by-words) and η (concept-by-phrases) tradeoffs; technically straightforward but cleanly formulated.
- Secondary: A new crowd-sourced entity similarity/relatedness dataset (people/places/organizations) and broad empirical study across biomedical and general domains.
- Tertiary: Multiple analyses (compositionality, semantic-type clustering) and a practical pipeline for large-scale coverage.

Strengths
- Clear modeling choice: Treating phrases/concepts as potentially atomic while allowing compositional pressure is well-motivated and easy to implement/adopt.
- Substantial engineering and scope: Jointly embedding words, phrases, and concepts across PubMed/clinical/Wiki+NYT with >3x concept coverage is valuable.
- Analyses go beyond topline numbers (e.g., semantic-type clustering, MAP diagnostics), providing insight into how representations organize phrases vs words.
- Distant supervision avoids costly manual corpus annotation and unifies representations across abstraction levels.

Weaknesses
- Evaluation is almost entirely intrinsic (similarity/relatedness). No extrinsic tasks (entity linking/normalization, clinical concept extraction, code prediction, retrieval) are reported, limiting claims of utility.
- Biomedical test sets are very limited or mismatched: Mini MayoSRS (n=29), MayoSRS (n=101; low IAA), and UMNSRS uses single-word concepts; these choices weaken conclusions. Moreover, human judgments in the new dataset are on phrases, so analyses should be framed as phrase similarity, not concept similarity.
- Heavy hyperparameter tuning on the same test sets used for reporting undermines validity; trends in Table 3 are not connected to a held-out development protocol, and the chosen task for tuning is unspecified.
- Claims of outperforming or replacing prior work are weak: the method relies on large, manually curated ontologies (UMLS/YAGO) and prior clinical annotations embedded in those resources.
- Missing deeper analyses: no systematic study of headword effects, and limited exploration of the β/η tradeoff beyond point estimates.

- How are word embeddings updated in the joint model; what does “phrase completion” mean operationally (Line 250)?
- Eq. 1 uses c where o is expected; please fix notation.
- Which dataset/task was used for hyperparameter tuning (Line 496)? Provide dev-set curves; show trends for PM+CL (Table 3).
- Why a 1–100 human rating scale (Line 388)? Justify granularity.
- Minor edits: Line 069 “contexts→concepts”; replace “strings” with “phrases” (Line 430); Pedersen et al. 2007 missing (Line 361); add missing reference to Table 5 (Line 535).

Recommendation
Reject. The idea and engineering are promising, but stronger, properly controlled evaluation (better datasets, extrinsic tasks, held-out tuning) and deeper analyses are needed before acceptance.
