- Proposal: Weakly supervised skip-gram method that replaces ontology phrases with IDs to jointly learn embeddings for concepts, phrases, and words, with compositionality controlled by β and η.  
- Contribution 1: Simple, scalable joint embedding framework balancing atomic and compositional representations via interpretable hyperparameters.  
- Contribution 2: New human-annotated general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset with documented collection protocol and IAA.  
- Contribution 3: Empirical analyses across domains/corpora of concept–phrase–word relationships and semantic-type clustering.  
- Strength: Modeling is sensible, easy to implement, scales to large corpora, and uses distant supervision to avoid manual corpus annotation.  
- Strength: Thorough engineering — trained on multiple realistic corpora (PubMed, clinical notes, Wikipedia+NYT) and ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) with comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Strength: New evaluation resource fills a gap for entity-level evaluation beyond word-only datasets.  
- Strength: Analyses beyond headline metrics reveal concept–phrase compositionality and semantic-type structure in embeddings.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is intrinsic-only with no extrinsic tasks (e.g., entity linking or ICD prediction), leaving practical impact unclear.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameters were tuned on the same test sets used for reporting, risking optimistic bias, especially on small benchmarks.  
- Weakness: Biomedical benchmarks used (MayoSRS, Mini MayoSRS, UMNSRS) are limited or inappropriate for multiword concept evaluation.  
- Weakness: “No-annotation” claim is overstated because the method depends on large curated ontologies and phrase inventories; comparisons to differently supervised methods are not fully fair.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of the atomic vs compositional tradeoff (β, η) and of ambiguity handling; no ablations on unambiguous phrases or alternative weighting.  
- Recommendation: Reject — promising modeling and dataset but evaluation methodology and missing analyses prevent strong claims; improve dev/test splits, add extrinsic tasks, and deepen analysis for resubmission.  
- Key questions for authors: how overlapping/nested phrases are handled; proportion and handling of ambiguous phrase-to-concept mappings and any weighting experiments; precise training details (which contexts used, phrase completion, shared negative-sampling matrix).  
- Key requests for rebuttal/revision: performance curves for β and η stratified by phrase length/ambiguity/headword presence; clarifications about whether general-domain annotations target surface forms or concepts and phrase-only baselines; per-category IAA and pair-type distributions.  
- Minor issues to fix: several typos and notation inconsistencies, clarify greedy phrase matching, matrix dimensions/negative-sampling terminology, report coverage/ambiguity statistics, justify rating scale granularity, and add extrinsic evaluation.
