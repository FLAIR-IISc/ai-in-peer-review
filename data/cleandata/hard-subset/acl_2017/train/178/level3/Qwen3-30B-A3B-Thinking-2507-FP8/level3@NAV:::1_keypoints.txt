- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that replaces phrase occurrences with ontology concept IDs and trains embeddings with a modified skip-gram model.  
- Evaluates on UMLS biomedical concepts and YAGO real-world entities, and introduces a new human-rated dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.  
- Primary contribution is the novel dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness, filling an important gap.  
- Method is simple, well-executed, and practical by avoiding expensive manual annotation.  
- Achieves competitive performance on biomedical similarity tasks versus manually annotated methods.  
- Technical novelty is limited—approach is a subtle extension of word2vec rather than a major advance.  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Equation 8 misdescribes MAP as a probability and should not rank the entire vocabulary—this needs correction.  
- Relationship to prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2014a) should be clarified.  
- Paper under-discusses the significance and potential impact of the new dataset and should expand on this.  
- Minor editorial error at line 556 should be fixed.  
- Overall, method and dataset are sound and valuable, but the contribution is not a major technical advance for this conference.
