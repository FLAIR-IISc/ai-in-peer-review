- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that replaces phrases with ontology concept IDs (UMLS/YAGO) and trains embeddings on the modified corpus.

- Claims competitive performance on concept similarity/relatedness without human annotation and ~3x larger vocabulary coverage than prior methods.

- Contribution: new dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world entities (people, places, organizations).

- Contribution: simple, annotation-free method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words using distant supervision.

- Contribution: comprehensive evaluation of concept embeddings on multiple biomedical and general-domain datasets.

- Strength: the novel entity similarity/relatedness dataset fills an important evaluation gap and is well-documented (crowdsourcing, validation, agreement).

- Strength: method is well-motivated, conceptually simple, and easy to implement using established techniques.

- Strength: thorough biomedical evaluation with multiple datasets showing competitive results versus annotation-dependent baselines.

- Weakness: technical novelty is limited; approach is a straightforward extension of existing distant supervision/embedding techniques.

- Weakness: evaluation is heavily biomedical-focused, limiting evidence of general-domain applicability despite YAGO inclusion.

- Weakness: MAP calculation (Eq. 8) is incorrectly described as a probability, lacks explanation of ranking procedure and cutoff—needs clarification/correction.

- Weakness: insufficient discussion of dataset limitations, differences from existing word similarity datasets, and broader impact.

- Question: clarify which prior methods are used for the "3x vocabulary coverage" comparison and how vocabulary size was measured.

- Question: explain how ranking for MAP is determined and whether a natural cutoff should be applied.

- Question: provide examples of downstream NLP tasks that would benefit from increased vocabulary coverage.

- Additional: paper is well-written but contributions are incremental; emphasize dataset more and fix minor editorial errors (e.g., "most concepts has" → "most concepts have").
