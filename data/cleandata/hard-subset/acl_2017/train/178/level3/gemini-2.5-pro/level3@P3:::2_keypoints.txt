- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that uses an ontology to identify canonical multiword phrases in text and trains skip-gram style embeddings for words, phrases, and concepts jointly.  
- Learns separate embedding matrices for words, phrases, and concepts with shared context and adds hyperparameters to model compositionality between words↔phrases and phrases↔concepts.  
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain concept similarity/relatedness tasks and shows competitive or state-of-the-art results on several datasets (notably larger UMNSRS sets).  
- Contribution: a practical, scalable approach for embedding non-compositional multiword expressions and their ontology concepts without manual annotation.  
- Contribution: strong empirical evidence in the biomedical domain and release of usable resources (cui2vec software and a crowdsourced dataset).  
- Strength: effectively addresses representation of multiword expressions by treating ontology-linked phrases as atomic tokens learned from usage context.  
- Strength: competitive performance on large-scale biomedical evaluations, outperforming many baselines on similarity tasks.  
- Strength: provides valuable community resources (code and datasets) for biomedical NLP practitioners.  
- Weakness: performance is inconsistent—does not uniformly beat baselines and sometimes yields only marginal gains over simpler methods.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty; core approach is applying skip-gram to a preprocessed corpus and compositionality terms (β, η) often have little effect.  
- Weakness: framing as “distant supervision” is misleading because the ontology functions as a lexicon rather than providing true supervised labels.  
- Weakness: unclear benefit of the concept embedding layer since averaged phrase vectors sometimes outperform learned concept vectors.  
- Questions for authors: how exactly are concept–concept similarity scores computed given many-to-many concept↔phrase mappings?  
- Questions for authors: why was β (compositionality) tuned to zero for large corpora—does this imply the compositionality mechanism or global β is ineffective?  
- Questions for authors: what is the added value of learning the concept matrix EC versus stopping at phrase embeddings, given APPROXPHRco sometimes performs better?
