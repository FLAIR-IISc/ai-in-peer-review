- Summary: Jointly learns embeddings for words, multi-word phrases, and ontology concepts by treating ontology-identified phrases as atomic units in a skip-gram-style model, using phrase→concept occurrences as distant supervision and adding compositionality constraints; evaluated on biomedical and general-domain similarity/relatedness tasks.

- Contribution 1: Proposes a scalable, weakly supervised method to learn concept and phrase embeddings without manual annotation, capturing non-compositional phrases and synonymy.

- Contribution 2: Releases two new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness datasets derived from YAGO.

- Contribution 3: Provides a publicly available implementation (`cui2vec`) for reproducibility and application.

- Strength 1: Effectively handles non-compositional and synonymous phrases by modeling them as atomic units rather than via compositional averaging.

- Strength 2: Shows strong, competitive performance on larger-scale biomedical similarity tasks (e.g., UMNSRS), achieving top Spearman ρ in some settings.

- Strength 3: Presents a comprehensive empirical evaluation across domains, corpora, and multiple baselines.

- Weakness 1: Performance is inconsistent across datasets and sometimes lags strong baselines (e.g., Choi et al. on MayoSRS), raising robustness concerns.

- Weakness 2: Learned concept embeddings may add little value—simple phrase-averaging (APPROXPHRco) outperforms CONCEPTco on new general-domain datasets.

- Weakness 3: Compositionality constraints (β, η) appear ineffective; optimal tuning often sets them to zero, effectively disabling the proposed mechanism.

- Weakness 4: Some terminology and clarity issues—calling phrase occurrences “distant supervision” is misleading, and introduced notation (EW, EP, EC) is underused in equations.

- Question 1: For PHRASEco results, which phrase embedding is used when a concept has multiple phrasal forms: the dataset-provided phrase or an average?

- Question 2: Can authors explain what additional information learned concept vectors capture beyond aggregated phrase vectors, given APPROXPHRco’s superior performance?

- Question 3: Do zero-valued optimal β and η imply most concepts are non-compositional, or that the compositionality constraint formulation (e.g., a single global β) is suboptimal?
