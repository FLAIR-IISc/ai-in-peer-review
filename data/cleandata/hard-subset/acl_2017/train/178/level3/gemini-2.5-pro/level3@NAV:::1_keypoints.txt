- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that tags ontology-identified phrases with concept IDs, appends the tagged text to the corpus, and trains a skip-gram model to learn joint embeddings for concepts, phrases, and words.
- Introduces and releases a new crowdsourced dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of general-domain, real-world entities.
- Uses distant supervision from an ontology to create a simple, scalable approach for learning concept embeddings without costly manual annotations.
- The new evaluation dataset addresses a gap in resources for entity-level representation evaluation and is well-documented.
- The method is well-motivated, straightforward, and easily reproducible, effectively extending word embedding techniques to concepts.
- The paper is clearly written and the experiments are thorough within the chosen domain, including multiple baselines and qualitative analyses (compositionality, clustering).
- Methodological novelty is limited, as the approach mainly treats concept identifiers as special tokens—an elegant but incremental extension.
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on the biomedical domain and does not demonstrate performance on the newly introduced general-domain dataset, limiting generality claims.
- The MAP calculation in the evaluation (Section 5.4, Eq. 8) is described and applied unconventionally and needs clarification/correction; a minor grammatical error was also noted.
