- Focuses on representing non-compositional multi-word expressions and abstract concepts using ontologies for distant supervision, a plausible and scalable approach.  
- Produces empirically strong embeddings, competitive or superior to strong baselines on large-scale concept similarity tasks (e.g., UMNSRS).  
- Public release of the cui2vec software is a practical, valuable contribution for biomedical NLP researchers.  
- Inconsistent performance: the full model does not consistently outperform baselines (Choi et al. beats it on Mayo; averaging phrase vectors wins on general-domain datasets).  
- Limited methodological novelty: mainly a straightforward adaptation of skip-gram with negative sampling rather than a fundamentally new model.  
- Questionable modeling/experimental choices and clarity: single global compositionality hyperparameter β seems inappropriate (optimal β=0 on some corpora), and similarity computation in Table 4 is unclear given many-to-many concept–phrase mappings.
