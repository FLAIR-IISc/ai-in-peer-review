- Introduces a weakly-supervised method to jointly learn embeddings for concepts, phrases, and words using ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) and an adapted skip-gram with negative sampling.  
- Adds hyperparameters (β, η) to control compositionality of phrase and concept embeddings.  
- Evaluates embeddings on biomedical and general-domain similarity/relatedness tasks and releases a new human-annotated general-domain entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution: Releases a large-scale general-domain entity similarity dataset (688 pairs) collected via crowdsourcing.  
- Contribution: Proposes a scalable, ontology-driven weakly-supervised method that covers a much larger concept vocabulary than prior work.  
- Contribution: Provides extensive empirical evaluation across multiple corpora and domains.

- Strength: New evaluation resource fills an important gap and will be valuable to the community.  
- Strength: Practical and scalable approach that avoids expensive manual concept annotation by using distant supervision from ontologies.  
- Strength: Model is an interpretable extension of skip-gram with clear control over compositionality via β and η.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning appears to be done on evaluation/test datasets (adapted 5-fold CV), risking overfitting and inflated results.  
- Weakness: Biomedical evaluation datasets are small or ill-suited (MayoSRS, Mini Mayo SRS, UMNSRS), limiting confidence in medical-domain claims.  
- Weakness: No extrinsic/downstream task evaluation (e.g., entity linking, relation extraction) to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Weakness: New dataset annotates phrases but is used to evaluate concept embeddings, conflating phrase-level and concept-level similarity.
