- Paper proposes joint embeddings for concepts, their multi-word phrases, and words, extending skip-gram with negative sampling and using ontologies (UMLS/YAGO) for distant supervision.
- Introduces hyperparameters β and η to balance compositional (word→phrase→concept) vs. atomic representations.
- Releases a novel human-annotated general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset (688 entity pairs).
- Demonstrates training on four large corpora (biomedical, clinical, general) with extensive baselines and analyses.
- Strength: the new general-domain evaluation resource fills an important gap for entity/concept similarity research.
- Strength: the model is a sensible, intuitive extension of word2vec with a clear mechanism for compositionality control.
- Strength: substantial experimental effort and multiple analyses across domains and corpora.
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning appears to be done on test partitions ("adapted 5-fold CV"), risking tuning on the test set and inflated results.
- Weakness: evaluation relies exclusively on intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks, which may not predict downstream utility.
- Weakness: biomedical benchmarks used are small or single-word focused (MayoSRS, Mini MayoSRS, UMNSRS), making them suboptimal for multi-word concept evaluation.
- Weakness: framing as "no human corpus annotation" understates reliance on large human-curated ontologies, weakening claims about supervision requirements versus prior work.
- Weakness: analyses of compositionality hyperparameters (β, η) are shallow; lacks sweeps and deeper investigation into surprising zero-valued optima.
- Question: clarify the "adapted 5-fold cross-validation" and justify tuning on test data instead of using separate development/test sets.
- Question: why is the new dataset framed as concept similarity rather than phrase similarity, given annotators saw textual phrases?
- Question: how many UMNSRS pairs involve multi-word phrases, and how suitable is UMNSRS for evaluating multi-word concept models?

- Additional: clarify overlap handling when replacing phrases with IDs (is greedy longest-match used?).
- Additional: embedding matrix dimensions and naming conventions (d x |V| vs |V| x d; ENS naming) are non-standard and should be fixed.
