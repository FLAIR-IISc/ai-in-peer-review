- Summary: Shows that writing-task constraints produce measurable stylistic differences; a simple linear classifier on stylistic features distinguishes three cases without story context and achieves new SOTA on the story cloze task.

- Contribution 1: Proposes a novel focus on task-induced writing style differences rather than author-specific styles.

- Contribution 2: Reports state-of-the-art performance on the story cloze task, outperforming prior deep-learning models.

- Contribution 3: Offers insights that the design of NLP tasks and data-generation prompts affects writing style and should be considered.

- Strength 1: Uses a simple yet effective method—stylistic features with a linear classifier—to detect differences.

- Strength 2: Empirically strong SOTA results validate the approach.

- Strength 3: Raises cognitive implications and motivates research on how prompts influence authors' mental states and responses.

- Weakness 1: Scope is limited to the story cloze task, so generalizability to other NLP tasks is unclear.

- Weakness 2: Reliance on a linear classifier may be technically limiting; more advanced models might better capture style complexity.

- Weakness 3: Analysis of the most salient features is limited and needs deeper exploration.

- Question 1: How will the authors extend and validate their approach on other NLP tasks, and what challenges do they foresee?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide more detailed analysis linking the most salient features to the specific writing tasks?

- Question 3: How do the authors plan to address the technical limitations and which alternative models or methods will they consider?
