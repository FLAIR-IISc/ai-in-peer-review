- Proposes a joint embedding model for words, phrases, and concepts.
- Creates a new evaluation dataset for phrase and concept representations.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the story cloze task.
- Novel approach to modeling phrases and concepts with potential to improve NLP tasks.
- The new evaluation dataset is a valuable resource for researchers.
- Strong performance on the story cloze task demonstrates effectiveness.
- Evaluation is limited to intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness) that weakly predict extrinsic utility.
- Uses phrase similarity datasets rather than true concept similarity datasets, weakening analysis.
- Medical concept evaluation datasets are small/limited (low annotator agreement or single-word focus), reducing relevance.
- Hyperparameter tuning on the same evaluation datasets undermines result credibility and fairness to prior work.
- Claim of reduced manual annotation is weak because the method still relies on large manually-constructed ontologies.
- How will the authors address evaluation limitations, such as using phrase vs. concept similarity datasets?
- Can the authors provide details on the medical concept datasets, including sizes and annotator agreement?
- How will the authors improve fairness of comparisons, e.g., by using separate datasets for hyperparameter tuning?
