- Study: examines how different writing tasks affect writing style using the story cloze task; a linear classifier can distinguish ending types by stylistic features.
- Finding: authors use different styles for coherent vs. incoherent endings and for writing entire stories vs. only the final sentence.
- Contribution: shows writing tasks produce measurable stylistic differences detectable with simple features.
- Contribution: demonstrates a linear classifier can reach state-of-the-art on the story cloze task by exploiting style differences.
- Contribution: highlights NLP task-design implications—author instructions can introduce biases.
- Strength: simple, interpretable method that makes stylistic differences easy to analyze.
- Strength: achieves practical impact via state-of-the-art performance on the target task.
- Strength: offers cognitive insights linking task framing to authors’ mental states and style.
- Weakness: limited novelty—stylistic features and linear classifiers are established techniques.
- Weakness: narrow focus on story cloze, so generalizability to other tasks is uncertain.
- Weakness: experimental setup and results (notably Tables 4 and 5) need clearer explanation.
- Question: provide more details on feature extraction and the specific features used.
- Question: how will the study be extended to other NLP tasks/datasets and what challenges are expected?
- Question: elaborate on the cognitive implications and how findings relate to authors’ mental states and writing style.
