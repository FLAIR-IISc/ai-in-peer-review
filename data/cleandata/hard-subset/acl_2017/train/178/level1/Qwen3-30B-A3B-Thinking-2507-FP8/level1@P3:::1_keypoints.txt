- Novel weakly-supervised method joint-embedding concepts, phrases, and words using ontology phrase-concept mappings without human corpus annotation.  
- New dataset for evaluating similarity/relatedness of real-world entities (people, places, organizations).  
- Show embeddings trained without human annotation can capture semantic relationships comparable to supervised methods.  
- Addresses concept embedding without expensive human annotation by leveraging abundant ontology mappings.  
- New evaluation dataset fills a gap by focusing on entities rather than word-level similarity.  
- Practical scalability claim: achieves >3x vocabulary coverage versus supervised approaches.  
- Method is straightforward, building on word2vec, and is easy to implement and understand.  
- Major methodological flaw: hyperparameters tuned with 5-fold CV on evaluation sets, causing data leakage and undermining validity.  
- Overstated performance: outperformed by supervised CHOI on smaller MayoSRS/Mini Mayo SRS; only competitive on larger UMNSRS.  
- Weak theoretical justification for compositionality hyperparameters (β, η); analyses contradict claimed ontology-structure capture.  
- Vague “>3x coverage” claim lacking a clear baseline or definition (concepts vs. overall vocabulary).  
- How was data leakage from tuning on evaluation datasets avoided in the final evaluation?  
- Why does the method underperform CHOI on smaller MayoSRS/Mini Mayo SRS — small data or fundamental limitation?  
- How do you reconcile high concept–average-phrase similarity with low MAP scores for concept–phrase relationships?  
- What is the specific baseline for the “more than 3x vocabulary coverage” claim?
