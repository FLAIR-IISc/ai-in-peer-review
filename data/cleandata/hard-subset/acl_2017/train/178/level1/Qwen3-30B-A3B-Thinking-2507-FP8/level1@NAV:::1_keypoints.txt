- Proposes a novel dataset for similarity/relatedness evaluation of real-world entities (people, places, organizations).  
- Demonstrates concept embeddings can be learned without human annotation while remaining competitive on similarity tasks.  
- Shows the model preserves ontological relationships between concepts and representative phrases.

- Dataset creation is a substantial contribution with no comparable resource for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.  
- Method is practically valuable for resource-constrained domains (e.g., biomedicine) because it avoids manual annotation.  
- Analysis indicates concepts cluster with their representative phrases, providing insight into embedding space organization.

- Method lacks novelty, being a straightforward adaptation of word2vec with distant supervision similar to prior work (Wang et al. 2014a; Mintz et al. 2009) and only minor parameter changes (β, η).  
- Performance gains over strong baselines are modest (0.05–0.07 Spearman’s ρ on larger datasets) and the method is outperformed on smaller biomedical datasets.  
- Evaluation relies on similarity/relatedness tasks that have been criticized for poor correlation with downstream performance.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., η=0.0 optimal for most corpora) suggests limited robustness across different settings.

- How are β and η justified given their high sensitivity and lack of clear performance trends?  
- Can you provide additional evaluation of embeddings on downstream tasks beyond similarity/relatedness?
