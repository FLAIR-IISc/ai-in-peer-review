- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using representative ontology phrases as distant supervision.  
- Claims three main contributions: annotation-free competitive performance, a new real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset, and >3x vocabulary coverage versus prior methods.  
- Strength: addresses practical need for embeddings without costly human annotation and shows competitive results on large biomedical datasets (UMNSRS).  
- Strength: introduces a carefully collected Mechanical Turk dataset for similarity/relatedness of people, places, and organizations, filling a gap in resources.  
- Strength: demonstrates substantially larger vocabulary coverage (over threefold) compared to annotation-dependent methods.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies mainly on similarity/relatedness tasks, lacking other intrinsic (e.g., analogies) or extrinsic (e.g., entity linking) evaluations to fully validate semantic properties.  
- Weakness: method is outperformed by CHOI on two smaller biomedical datasets (Mayo SRS, Mini Mayo SRS), raising questions about consistency of "competitive performance."  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of dependence on ontology quality and phrase-to-concept mappings; no sensitivity analysis for ontologies with sparse representative phrases.  
- Question: clarify the baseline and exact numbers behind the "more than 3x coverage" vocabulary claim.  
- Question: have the embeddings been evaluated on other intrinsic tasks (analogy) or downstream tasks (entity linking), and if not, how do you justify claims about preserved semantic properties?  
- Question: beyond MAP in Figure 4, how is the degree of ontology structure preservation in the embedding space measured and demonstrated?  
- Overall: valuable, practical contribution with a useful dataset and scale advantage, but needs broader evaluation and deeper analysis of ontology dependency to fully support claims.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add additional evaluations and more detailed analysis of limitations.
