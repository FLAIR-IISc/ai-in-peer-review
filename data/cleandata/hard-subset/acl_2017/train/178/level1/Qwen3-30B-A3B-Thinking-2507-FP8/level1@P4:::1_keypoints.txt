- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using representative ontology phrases as distant supervision.  
- Trains embeddings for medical (UMLS) and general-domain (YAGO) concepts from unlabeled corpora and evaluates on similarity and relatedness tasks.  
- Contributions: novel weakly-supervised joint embedding approach, empirical validation across domains, and a new dataset for entity similarity/relatedness.

- Novel use of representative ontology phrases as distant supervision to scale embeddings without human annotation.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across biomedical and general domains, multiple corpora, and several baselines/metrics.  
- Created two new validated datasets (688 pairs each) for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.

- Novelty may be limited relative to prior work; main difference is phrase-based supervision which may not be fundamentally new.  
- Performance gains are modest; results are competitive but not clearly better than simpler baselines on key benchmarks.  
- Limited analysis of the impact of noisy distant supervision and of failure cases, ambiguous phrases, or concepts lacking representative phrases.  
- Evaluation methodology details are insufficient (possible data leakage from hyperparameter tuning; unclear handling of concept polysemy).

- Request for exact vocabulary-size numbers to substantiate the "more than 3x coverage" claim.  
- Ask for explanation why concept embeddings outperform phrase approximations in biomedical data but underperform in general-domain data.  
- Request concrete evidence that ontology structure is reflected in embedding space (examples or quantitative measures).
