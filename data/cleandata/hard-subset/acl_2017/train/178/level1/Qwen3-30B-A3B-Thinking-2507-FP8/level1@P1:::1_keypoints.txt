- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that uses ontology phrase mappings as distant supervision to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words.  
- Introduces a new human-judgment dataset for similarity and relatedness of real-world entities (people, places, organizations).  
- Shows competitive concept similarity/relatedness performance with substantially larger vocabulary coverage (3x+) without human annotation.

- Novel approach leveraging ontology mappings to train embeddings from unannotated text, avoiding costly manual annotation.  
- New evaluation dataset (688 pairs per task) fills a gap for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.  
- Demonstrates scalability and much larger vocabulary coverage while maintaining competitive task performance.  
- Provides analyses of concept-phrase proximity, phrase-word compositionality, and semantic-type clustering showing meaningful semantic structure.

- Major methodological flaw: hyperparameters tuned on test sets, violating standard evaluation and undermining result validity.  
- Outperformed by human-annotated CHOI on two smaller biomedical datasets (Mayo SRS, Mini Mayo SRS), calling into question "competitive" claims.  
- Insufficient comparison/explanation relative to closest prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2014a) and lacking argument for superiority.  
- Limited analysis of method limitations, e.g., weak concept-phrase compositionality (mean similarity ~0.5) not adequately explored.  
- Ambiguous "3x coverage" claim without clear definition or comparison to other methods' vocabulary sizes.

- Clarify which CHIU word embedding variant (CHIU-2 or CHIU-30) achieved the reported highest correlation on the similarity task.  
- Confirm whether hyperparameter tuning used test data and explain its impact on result validity.  
- Explain how the method is "competitive" given CHOI's superior performance on smaller, reliable biomedical datasets.  
- Explain the low mean cosine similarity (~0.5) between concepts and averaged phrase embeddings and its implications for model effectiveness.
