- Proposes a weakly‑supervised skip‑gram adaptation using ontologies to jointly learn embeddings for concepts, phrases, and words from unannotated corpora.
- Scalable framework that avoids manual annotation and can learn embeddings for many more concepts than prior methods.
- Creates and releases a new crowdsourced general‑domain entity similarity and relatedness dataset.
- Empirical evaluation across biomedical and general domains shows embeddings competitive with strong, more supervised baselines.
- Weak supervision via ontologies and large corpora offers a practical, scalable solution to the annotation bottleneck.
- The new dataset is a valuable, well‑documented resource for future research on entity/concept representations.
- Thorough experimental setup with multiple domains, strong baselines, and analyses of compositionality and semantic type clustering.
- Direct concept embeddings (CONCEPTco) do not consistently outperform simpler baselines; phrase‑averaging (APPROXPHRco) often performs best.
- The distant‑supervision assumption that every phrase occurrence denotes all its concepts overlooks ambiguity and potential noise, but its impact is not analyzed.
- Evaluation is limited to intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; lack of extrinsic downstream task results weakens claims of practical utility.
