- Novel weakly-supervised method jointly learns embeddings for words, phrases, and concepts by extending SGNS and using ontology-based distant supervision.

- Separate embedding matrices for words, phrases, and concepts; phrase contexts update concept embeddings; hyperparameters control phrase/concept compositionality.

- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain similarity/relatedness tasks and releases a new general-domain entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution: scalable, simple weakly-supervised joint concept embedding approach needing only an ontology and raw text.

- Contribution: new YAGO-derived dataset of human judgments for general-domain entity similarity/relatedness.

- Strength: highly practical and scalable—works on massive corpora and large ontologies (e.g., >700k YAGO concepts).

- Strength: strong empirical performance on biomedical benchmarks (CONCEPTco best on UMNSRS Similarity; competitive on Relatedness).

- Strength: thoughtful design and analysis—compositionality hyperparameters and experiments on semantic type clustering and compositional structure.

- Strength: generality demonstrated across domains (UMLS/biomedical and YAGO/general).

- Weakness: CONCEPTco embeddings are not consistently superior—APPROXPHRco (phrase-average) outperforms on the new general-domain dataset, questioning the need for a separate concept matrix.

- Weakness: limited modeling novelty—mainly an SGNS extension and standard weighted-loss compositionality enforcement.

- Weakness: preprocessing uses greedy longest-match phrase replacement, risking missed/ambiguous mentions and lacking disambiguation; impact not analyzed.

- Question: why does APPROXPHRco outperform CONCEPTco on YAGO data—are concrete entities better captured by aggregated phrase mentions?

- Question: have you measured error frequency/impact from greedy phrase matching or considered alternative mention-identification/disambiguation methods?
