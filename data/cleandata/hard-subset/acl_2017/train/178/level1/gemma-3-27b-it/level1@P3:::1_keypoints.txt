- Proposes a weakly-supervised joint embedding method using ontology phrases and unannotated text.
- Uses distant supervision from UMLS and YAGO to avoid costly human annotation.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on concept similarity/relatedness and introduces a new evaluation dataset.
- Main contribution: framework bridging structured ontologies and distributional semantics, useful for domains like biomedicine.
- Main contribution: creation of a novel dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness with a reasonable collection/filtering process.
- Main contribution: extensive empirical validation, embedding-organization analyses, and ablation studies.
- Strength: addresses representation of concepts beyond word-level embeddings without manual labels.
- Strength: strong comparisons against many baselines, including human-annotated methods.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across biomedical and general domains, plus a new dataset.
- Strength: insightful analyses of embedding space and hyperparameter effects.
- Strength: release of cui2vec code and the new dataset improves reproducibility.
- Weakness: performance gains over strong baselines are often marginal and lack deeper explanation.
- Weakness: greedy phrase matching in preprocessing is potentially suboptimal and may cause errors.
- Weakness: model appears sensitive to hyperparameters; tuning was extensive and selection not fully principled.
- Weakness: low Fleiss’ kappa (0.24) for the new dataset raises reliability and bias concerns.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to intrinsic tasks; no downstream task evaluation to show practical utility.
- Question: please elaborate on limitations of greedy phrase matching and possible alternatives.
- Question: provide more detail on hyperparameter sensitivity and which parameters most affect performance.
- Question: explain sources of annotator disagreement for the new dataset and implications for validity.
- Question: are there plans to evaluate embeddings on downstream tasks like relation extraction or entity linking?
- Question: was any attempt made to find hyperparameters that generalize across multiple corpora?
- Additional: notation is mostly clear but some equations need more explanation.
- Additional: related work is comprehensive but could focus more on directly comparable approaches.
- Additional: discussion of joint embedding benefits would be stronger with concrete example scenarios.
