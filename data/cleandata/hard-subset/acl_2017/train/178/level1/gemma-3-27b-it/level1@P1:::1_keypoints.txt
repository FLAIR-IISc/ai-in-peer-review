- Proposes weakly-supervised joint embeddings for concepts, phrases, and words using representative phrases as distant supervision.  
- Trains embeddings from contexts of concept phrases in unlabeled corpora to bridge lexical forms and abstract concepts.  
- Scalable method enabling embeddings for ~3× larger vocabularies versus annotation-dependent approaches.  
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain datasets, with a novel general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset.  
- Novel use of representative phrases as distant supervision addresses costly annotation and adds contextual grounding.  
- Strong empirical performance on biomedical benchmarks (MayoSRS, UMNSRS) without manual labels.  
- Practical significance for large knowledge bases due to scalability and broad vocabulary coverage.  
- Thorough evaluation: multiple baselines, ablations, semantic-type analyses, and detailed hyperparameter tuning.  
- Method’s effectiveness heavily depends on the quality and completeness of the underlying ontology mappings.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of failure cases and explanations for when the method performs poorly.  
- Results are sensitive to hyperparameter settings, which vary across corpora and may hinder generalization.  
- Evaluation focuses on intrinsic tasks (similarity/relatedness) and omits downstream task demonstrations (e.g., entity linking).  
- Question: How robust is the method to noisy or incomplete ontology mappings; request sensitivity analysis.  
- Question: Provide examples of poor concept–phrase pairs and analyses explaining the failures.  
- Question: Clarify intuition and selection guidelines for crucial β and η hyperparameters across datasets.  
- Question: Have alternative strategies (e.g., attention) been explored for many-to-many concept–phrase relationships beyond averaging?  
- Notation and mathematical formulation are clear and well-presented.  
- Related work section gives a good overview of existing literature.  
- Release of code and the novel dataset is a valuable contribution.  
- Recommend briefly defining “distant supervision” for readers less familiar with the term.
