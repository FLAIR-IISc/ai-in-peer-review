- Paper proposes weakly supervised joint embeddings of concepts, phrases, and words using ontology lexicons and unlabeled text via greedy phrase matching and SGNS with compositionality regularizers.
- Contributions: a general weakly supervised framework requiring only ontology phrase lists, empirical cross-domain evaluation showing competitive intrinsic performance with larger coverage, and release of a new human-judged entity similarity dataset plus code (cui2vec).
- Strength: Scalable, low-cost supervision with broad coverage (>3x vocabulary) by leveraging ontology phrase inventories and greedy matching.
- Strength: Competitive intrinsic performance on biomedical similarity/relatedness benchmarks (top or near-top Spearman ρ on UMNSRS).
- Strength: Demonstrated cross-domain applicability (UMLS and YAGO) with strong results on new general-domain datasets.
- Strength: Joint embedding space enables analyses of compositionality, alignment between levels, and semantic type clustering, adding interpretability.
- Strength: Clear training objective and transparent hyperparameter study for compositionality penalties (β and η).
- Weakness: Limited novelty—approach is incremental over prior auto-tagging/joint embedding methods, mainly SGNS plus averaging-based penalties.
- Weakness: Evaluation is largely intrinsic; no extrinsic downstream tasks and hyperparameter tuning risks overfitting to same datasets.
- Weakness: Distant supervision assumes un-disambiguated matches for ambiguous phrases, potentially injecting noise without sense weighting or disambiguation.
- Weakness: Compositionality regularizers often give limited gains (many optimal β, η = 0), questioning their practical utility.
- Weakness: Minor clarity and technical detail issues (notation errors, negative sampling specifics, preprocessing/matching details not quantified).
- Question: Should Cp replace Cs in Eq. (4)/(7), and are negative samples drawn only from words W?
- Question: Why not sample phrases/concepts as negatives for phrase/concept updates, and what is the motivation for current negative sampling?
- Question: How are word, phrase, and concept updates interleaved and weighted per token—any coefficients controlling their relative contributions?
- Question: What fraction of phrases map to multiple concepts and how often do ambiguous phrases occur; can performance be reported by phrase ambiguity or after filtering?
- Question: How are overlapping/multi-span matches handled beyond greedy longest-match; is matching case-sensitive and what token normalization is applied?
- Question: Can you provide extrinsic task evaluations (e.g., concept normalization, entity linking) or small experiments showing downstream gains?
- Question: Please release train/dev/test splits for similarity/relatedness datasets and re-run with dev-only tuning to address overfitting.
- Question: Clarify the “>3x coverage” claim—relative to which baseline, under what frequency thresholds, and quantify coverage/overlap with evaluation sets.
