- Proposes a weakly supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using phrase-ID replacement, a modified SGNS over tagged/untagged corpora, shared negative-sample embeddings, and optional β/η compositionality regularizers.

- Main contribution: a practical, scalable framework that avoids manual concept annotations and achieves competitive intrinsic performance with >3× vocabulary coverage over prior biomedical embeddings.

- Main contribution: releases a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset (688 pairs each) and software (cui2vec), expanding entity-level evaluation resources.

- Main contribution: empirical analyses showing joint training preserves concept–phrase proximity, induces semantic-type clustering, and provides interpretable compositionality trade-offs.

- Strength: leverages ontology phrase inventories to learn very large vocabularies (>190k UMLS concepts, ~750k YAGO entities) without manual tagging, greatly increasing coverage.

- Strength: competitive intrinsic performance on benchmarks (e.g., UMNSRS similarity ρ=0.653 matching CHIU-30) despite noisy distant supervision.

- Strength: cross-domain generality with strong correlations on new YAGO/WIKINYT-based datasets (e.g., APPROXPHRco relatedness ρ≈0.810).

- Strength: transparent modeling choices (many-to-many mappings, shared negative matrix) and informative compositionality and clustering analyses.

- Strength: thorough hyperparameter exploration with cross-validated tuning and reproducible training details.

- Weakness: limited novelty — approach is largely engineering/extension of SGNS with distant supervision and doesn’t leverage ontology graph structure or relations.

- Weakness: distant supervision is noisy and ambiguous (uniform mapping of phrases to candidate concepts) with no disambiguation or context-aware priors.

- Weakness: evaluations are intrinsic only (similarity/relatedness); no downstream task experiments to demonstrate practical utility.

- Weakness: new entity datasets have low inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.24), weakening evaluation conclusions.

- Weakness: mixed empirical gains — some baselines (e.g., CHOI) outperform concept embeddings on clinical sets, and phrase-aggregated approximations often beat direct concept vectors.

- Weakness: modeling choices under-explored — limited ablations on shared negative-sample matrix, β vs η effects, alternative couplings, and no error analysis for non-compositional MWEs.

- Question: what is the distribution of many-to-many ambiguity (|Cp| per phrase) and how does performance vary with phrase ambiguity or with context-aware disambiguation?

- Question: what negative sampling distribution is used (unigram^0.75 over words?) and why restrict negatives to words instead of including phrases/concepts?

- Question: can you provide ablations quantifying the effect of shared negative-sample matrix vs separate spaces and targeted β/η ablations for compositional vs non-compositional phrases?

- Question: how does coverage and performance change with lower frequency thresholds (e.g., min count <25), especially on clinical corpora?

- Question: for the new datasets, can you report per-pair variance, provide higher-agreement subsets, or compare to existing entity-relatedness resources?

- Question: do concept embeddings help downstream tasks (entity linking, concept normalization, clinical phenotyping), even in small-scale experiments?

- Question: would incorporating ontology edges/relations (UMLS, YAGO graph) during training reduce concept collapse to phrase/word averages and improve non-lexical semantics?
