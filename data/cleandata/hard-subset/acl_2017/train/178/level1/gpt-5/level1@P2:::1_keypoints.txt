- Proposes a weakly supervised method that replaces ontology phrases in text with concept IDs and jointly trains word, phrase, and concept embeddings using skip-gram with negative sampling and two compositionality regularizers (β, η).
- Uses ontology phrase-to-concept mappings as distant supervision and evaluates on UMLS and YAGO across several corpora with intrinsic similarity/relatedness benchmarks.
- Contribution: scalable weakly supervised framework enabling large coverage of concepts (e.g., 193k UMLS concepts) without manual annotation.
- Contribution: empirical evidence that these embeddings achieve competitive intrinsic performance and preserve some ontological/semantic structure (concept–phrase linkage, semantic type clustering).
- Contribution: provides a small new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset and releases code/embeddings (cui2vec).
- Strength: method is practical and scalable, eliminating need for expensive manual labeling or KB triples and substantially increasing coverage.
- Strength: competitive intrinsic performance on larger biomedical similarity datasets, matching or outperforming several baselines.
- Strength: thorough experiments and ablations across configurations, corpora, and analyses of compositionality and clustering for interpretability.
- Strength: cross-domain validation on YAGO shows the approach generalizes beyond biomedical text.
- Strength: resource contribution (public code/embeddings and dataset) beneficial to the community.
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited — an incremental adaptation of skip-gram with phrase replacement and simple regularization.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on intrinsic evaluations and some tuning on test sets, with no extrinsic task validation to demonstrate real-world impact.
- Weakness: ambiguity and noise handling is rudimentary (uniform updates for ambiguous phrases) with no disambiguation or quantification of ambiguity effects.
- Weakness: compositionality regularizers often tune to zero, undermining their practical utility and leaving joint coupling weakly enforced.
- Weakness: some comparisons are not strictly apples-to-apples due to differences in ontologies, supervision, and corpora among baselines.
- Weakness: the new entity dataset is small with low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ=0.24), limiting its benchmarking value.
- Question: how sensitive are results to phrase ambiguity, and can performance be reported separately for ambiguous vs. unambiguous phrase mappings?
- Question: can the authors include at least one downstream task (e.g., entity linking or concept normalization) to demonstrate extrinsic utility?
- Question: why do β and η usually tune to zero, and were alternative coupling methods (contrastive losses, tied parameters, retrofitting) tried?
- Question: how are negative samples drawn for phrases and concepts given ENS is word-only, and were negative samplings in corresponding spaces explored?
- Question: what proportion of training phrase mentions are ambiguous, distribution of concepts per phrase, and does longest-match replacement introduce boundary errors or biases?
- Overall assessment: moderate originality and generally sound, clear work with good scalability and practical significance but limited by ambiguity handling and lack of extrinsic validation; recommend weak accept if ambiguity and downstream utility are addressed.
