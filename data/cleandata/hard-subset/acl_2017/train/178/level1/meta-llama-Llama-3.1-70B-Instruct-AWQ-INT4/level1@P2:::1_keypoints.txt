- Proposes a novel method to jointly learn concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using representative phrases as supervision and an adapted skip-gram model, validated on biomedical and real-world entities for similarity/relatedness.
- Novel joint embedding method for concepts, phrases, and words from unlabeled corpora.
- Uses distant supervision via representative phrases linked to ontology concepts.
- Experimental validation on biomedical concepts and real-world entities, evaluated on concept similarity and relatedness.
- Achieves competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness, comparable to methods requiring human annotations.
- Scales to much larger vocabularies without additional expense.
- Preserves semantic properties such as similarity, relatedness, type-consistency, and concept–phrase links.
- Lacks direct comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
- Evaluation is limited to similarity and relatedness and omits downstream tasks (e.g., entity linking, QA).
- No analysis of the impact of hyperparameter tuning on performance.
- How does the method handle polysemy and homophony in the corpus?
- Can the method be extended to multiple languages and ontologies?
- How does the method compare to state-of-the-art in performance and computational efficiency?
- What is the impact of hyperparameter tuning on the method’s performance?
- Can the method be applied to downstream tasks such as entity linking or question answering?
