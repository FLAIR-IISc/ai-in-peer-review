- Proposes cui2vec: joint learning of concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using ontology-based distant supervision.  
- Uses representative phrases for ontology concepts and adapts skip-gram with negative sampling for joint embedding.  
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain concept similarity and relatedness tasks.  
- Achieves competitive performance with methods that require human-annotated data.  
- Introduces a new dataset of human judgments for similarity and relatedness between people, places, and organizations.  
- Strength: effective use of ontology-based distant supervision to learn shared embeddings.  
- Strength: competitive empirical results across biomedical and general-domain benchmarks.  
- Strength: demonstrates scalability to very large vocabularies without extra cost.  
- Weakness: evaluation is mostly intrinsic (similarity/relatedness) with limited downstream task assessment.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning shows no clear trends; optimal settings vary by corpus.  
- Weakness: possible performance impact from mismatches between ontologies used.  
- Questions: how to extend method to more abstract general-domain data (e.g., BabelNet)?  
- Questions: request for more detail on hyperparameter tuning and corpus-specific optimal settings.  
- Questions: how to address ontology mismatches and their effect on performance?
