**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a novel method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus. The method uses representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision and adapts the skip-gram with negative sampling variant of word2vec for joint embedding. The authors experimentally validate their approach by learning embeddings for biomedical concepts and real-world entities, and evaluate their embeddings on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. **Novel method for joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words**: The paper proposes a new method for jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from an unlabeled text corpus, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **Use of distant supervision from phrases linked to concepts in an ontology**: The paper uses representative phrases for ontology concepts as distant supervision, which is a novel approach to leveraging structured knowledge in training word embeddings.
3. **Experimental validation on biomedical and real-world entities**: The authors experimentally validate their approach by learning embeddings for biomedical concepts and real-world entities, and evaluate their embeddings on concept similarity and relatedness tasks.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks**: The paper demonstrates that the proposed method achieves competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks, comparable to prior methods that require human-annotated data.
2. **Scalability to large vocabulary**: The paper shows that the proposed method can scale to a much larger vocabulary without additional expense, which is a significant advantage over prior methods.
3. **Preservation of semantic properties**: The paper demonstrates that the proposed method preserves several semantic properties, including similarity and relatedness, and the links between concepts of the same type and between concepts and their representative phrases.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of direct comparison to state-of-the-art methods**: The paper does not provide a direct comparison to state-of-the-art methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate the performance of the proposed method in relation to existing methods.
2. **Limited evaluation on downstream tasks**: The paper only evaluates the proposed method on concept similarity and relatedness tasks, and does not provide an evaluation on downstream tasks, such as entity linking or question answering.
3. **No analysis of the impact of hyperparameter tuning**: The paper does not provide an analysis of the impact of hyperparameter tuning on the performance of the proposed method, which is an important aspect of evaluating the robustness of the method.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the proposed method handle polysemy and homophony in the text corpus?
2. Can the proposed method be extended to handle multiple languages and ontologies?
3. How does the proposed method compare to state-of-the-art methods in terms of performance and computational efficiency?
4. What is the impact of hyperparameter tuning on the performance of the proposed method?
5. Can the proposed method be applied to downstream tasks, such as entity linking or question answering?
