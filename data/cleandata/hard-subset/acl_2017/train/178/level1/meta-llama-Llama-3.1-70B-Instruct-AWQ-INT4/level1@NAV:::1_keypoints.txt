- Proposes jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using representative phrases as distant supervision.
- Adapts skip-gram with negative sampling to embed concepts, phrases, and words in a shared vector space and evaluates on biomedical and general datasets.
- Claims competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks without human corpus annotation.
- Contribution: joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words to capture cross-level semantic relationships.
- Contribution: uses ontology representative phrases as distant supervision, removing need for manual annotation.
- Contribution: demonstrates competitive results on similarity/relatedness benchmarks.
- Strength: novel joint-embedding approach that captures semantic relationships across concepts, phrases, and words.
- Strength: effective application of distant supervision from ontology phrases to train embeddings.
- Strength: achieves competitive performance on similarity and relatedness tasks without annotated corpora.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to similarity/relatedness; lacks downstream task experiments (e.g., entity linking, QA).
- Weakness: no comparison with other joint embedding methods, hindering assessment of relative novelty/effectiveness.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its effects on performance.
- Question: How will the method be extended to downstream tasks like entity linking or question answering?
- Question: Can the authors provide detailed analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its impact?
- Question: How does the method handle polysemy where words or phrases have multiple meanings?
