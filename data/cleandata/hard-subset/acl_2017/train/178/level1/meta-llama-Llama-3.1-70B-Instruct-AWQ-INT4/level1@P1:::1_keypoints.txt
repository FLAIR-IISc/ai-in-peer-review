- Proposes jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings from unlabeled text using representative ontology phrases as distant supervision, adapting skip-gram with negative sampling for UMLS and YAGO and achieving competitive similarity/relatedness results without annotations.

- Joint embedding of concepts, phrases, and words into a shared vector space to capture semantic relationships.

- Uses representative ontology phrases as distant supervision, eliminating the need for human annotations.

- Demonstrates competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks despite no human-labeled data.

- Novel method leveraging ontology-based distant supervision for concept embedding.

- Strong empirical performance comparable to existing methods without annotated resources.

- Scales to large vocabularies without additional expense.

- Evaluation is limited to a small set of datasets, raising questions about generalizability.

- Lacks comparison to state-of-the-art concept embedding methods.

- Provides limited analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its effects.

- How will the method handle polysemy and homophony in concept embeddings?

- Please provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and its impact.

- How will the authors evaluate the method on a broader range of datasets and applications to demonstrate generalizability?
