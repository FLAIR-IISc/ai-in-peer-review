- Joint method: Proposes jointly learning concept, phrase, and word embeddings in a shared space using representative phrases from an ontology as distant supervision.
- Implementation: Adapts skip-gram with negative sampling (word2vec variant) to train embeddings for UMLS (medical) and YAGO (general-domain) concepts.
- Annotation-free: Method requires no human annotations and covers a larger vocabulary than prior approaches.
- Performance: Demonstrates competitive results on concept similarity and relatedness tasks compared to existing methods.
- New datasets: Introduces two novel datasets of similarity and relatedness for real-world entities identified by Wikipedia page and text phrase.
- Strength — distant supervision: Effectively leverages ontology-linked phrases as distant supervision to eliminate manual labeling.
- Strength — empirical effectiveness: Empirical results indicate the approach is effective on evaluated tasks.
- Strength — scalability: Scales to much larger vocabularies without additional expense.
- Weakness — domain evaluation: Evaluation is primarily biomedical with limited testing on general-domain datasets.
- Weakness — hyperparameters: Lacks detailed analysis of how hyperparameters affect performance despite tuning.
- Weakness — baselines: Limited comparisons to other concept-based embedding methods; mostly compared to word-based baselines.
- Question — abstract concepts: How will the method be extended to handle more abstract general-domain concepts?
- Question — hyperparam analysis: Can the authors provide a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter impacts?
- Question — polysemy: How will the approach address polysemy in embeddings as noted in the discussion?
- Additional comment: Distant supervision is promising, but broader evaluation, deeper hyperparameter analysis, and exploration of applications (e.g., entity linking) are needed.
