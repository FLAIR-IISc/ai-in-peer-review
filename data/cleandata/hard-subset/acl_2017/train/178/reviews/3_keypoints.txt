- Model is straightforward and sensibly balances treating phrases/concepts as atomic vs compositional.  
- Paper presents substantial work: model development, a new evaluation dataset, and multiple evaluations/analyses.  
- Evaluation uses only intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks and lacks extrinsic task evaluation.  
- Human judgments were elicited on phrases, so datasets should be treated/analyzed as phrase-similarity rather than concept-similarity.  
- Medical evaluation datasets are very limited or inappropriate: mini MayoSRS (29 pairs), MayoSRS (101 pairs, low agreement), UMNSRS (single-word concepts only).  
- Hyperparameters are heavily tuned on the same datasets used for reporting results, undermining result validity.  
- Claim that method outperforming prior work is weak because authors still rely on large manual ontologies and prior annotations from clinical records.  
- Paper lacks deeper analyses separating phrase-word compositionality from concept-phrase relations (e.g., headword effects) and exploration of the atomic vs compositional hyperparameter tradeoff.  
- Review recommendation: reject; improve evaluation datasets and methodology before resubmission.
