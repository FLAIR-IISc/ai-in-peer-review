- Paper extends word embeddings to produce representations for phrases and concepts by replacing phrase/word occurrences with a fixed concept identifier (from an ontology) in a tagged corpus and appending it to the original corpus for training.  
- Application is in the biomedical domain using related corpora and ontologies.  
- Authors introduce a novel test dataset for word similarity/relatedness for real-world entities.  
- Paper is well written and method is natural.  
- Contribution is limited/subtle and not a major technical advance.  
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on biomedical data, limiting generality.  
- The generated test resource needs more discussion and may be the paper's primary contribution.  
- Technical issue: Eq. 8 (MAP calculation) is incorrectly described as a probability and should not rank the entire vocabulary; explanation needs correction (or define a natural cutoff).  
- Minor editorial correction: line 556 “most concepts has” → “most concepts have.”
