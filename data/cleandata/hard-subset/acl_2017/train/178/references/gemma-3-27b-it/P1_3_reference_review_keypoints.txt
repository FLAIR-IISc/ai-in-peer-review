Main contributions:
- New dataset released for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.
- Practical weakly-supervised method using ontology-driven phrase replacement and skip-gram training to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words.
- Empirical evidence that jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words improves capture of relationships between textual representations of the same concept.

Strengths:
- Strong empirical evaluation across biomedical and general-domain datasets with comparisons to diverse, strong baselines.
- Detailed methodological description and released implementation (cui2vec) that aid reproducibility.
- Insightful analysis showing concepts and their representing phrases are embedded closely.
- Addresses the important problem of representing concepts not directly encoded as words.

Weaknesses:
- Limited methodological novelty—primarily an adaptation of word2vec with distant supervision.
- Weaker performance on the new general-domain dataset where phrase-based approximations outperform direct concept embeddings.
- Low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ kappa = 0.24) raises concerns about dataset reliability and possible evaluation bias.
- Model shows sensitivity to hyperparameters, suggesting potential difficulty generalizing without extensive tuning.

Questions for authors:
- How well does the model generalize to domains very different from biomedicine and web data (e.g., legal, financial)?
- What types of annotator disagreements occurred and how might the low IAA bias the results?
- Have you tried alternative phrase replacement methods (e.g., dependency-based) to reduce noise from the greedy strategy?
- What are the computational costs (training time, memory) compared to baselines on large-scale data?

Additional comments:
- Improve table formatting for readability.
- Organize and make the supplemental hyperparameter tuning material easily accessible.
- Discuss limitations of distant supervision more fully, including sources of noise and bias.
