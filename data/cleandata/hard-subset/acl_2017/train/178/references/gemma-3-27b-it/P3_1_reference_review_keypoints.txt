- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-based distant supervision and unannotated text; phrases act as a bridge between concepts and words, evaluated on biomedical and general-domain datasets with a new entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution: Introduces a practical weakly-supervised approach to learn concept embeddings without costly human annotation, leveraging existing ontologies and corpora.

- Contribution: Releases a new dataset for real-world entity similarity (people, places, organizations), addressing a gap in evaluation resources.

- Contribution: Provides extensive empirical validation and analysis of embedding space organization across multiple corpora and ontologies.

- Strength: Method is practical and scalable, applicable to specialized domains (e.g., biomedicine) and large knowledge bases.

- Strength: Achieves competitive performance on concept similarity and relatedness tasks, rivaling some supervised methods.

- Strength: Offers insightful analysis of embedding structure (phrase-concept proximity, semantic clustering), with strong MAP analysis.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation and good coverage of related work, comparing to both word-embedding approximations and dedicated concept methods.

- Strength: Code and dataset release improve reproducibility and potential impact.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—primarily adapts existing word2vec-style techniques with distant supervision rather than introducing a new embedding paradigm.

- Weakness: Performance depends heavily on ontology quality and coverage; ontology errors or gaps can degrade embeddings.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning appears corpus-specific, raising concerns about generalizability and transferability to new domains.

- Weakness: New dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa = 0.24), questioning label reliability and evaluation robustness.

- Question: How does ontology quality and coverage quantitatively impact model performance, and can ontology errors be mitigated (e.g., confidence scoring)?

- Question: Have you explored transferring hyperparameters across corpora or techniques to improve hyperparameter generalization?

- Question: What quality-control steps were used for the new dataset annotations, and will raw annotation data be released for further analysis?

- Question: Can you provide ablation studies isolating the effects of β and η hyperparameters and performance when they are fixed to 0 or 1?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clearly presented.

- Additional comment: Supplemental material is helpful.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with practical contributions and thorough evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
