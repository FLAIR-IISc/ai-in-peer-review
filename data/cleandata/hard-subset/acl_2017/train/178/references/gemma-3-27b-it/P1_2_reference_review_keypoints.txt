- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method adapting skip-gram to jointly embed words, phrases, and concepts using ontologies (UMLS/YAGO) and unannotated corpora; validated on biomedical and general-domain similarity/relatedness tasks with a new dataset.
- Contribution — Novel dataset: Introduces a human-annotated dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness (people, places, organizations).
- Contribution — Weak supervision: Presents a practical, scalable approach to learn concept embeddings without costly human annotation.
- Contribution — Empirical analysis: Provides comprehensive experiments and analysis showing ontology and compositional structure in embeddings.
- Strength: Practicality & scalability: Method scales to large corpora/ontologies and is applicable in domains like biomedicine.
- Strength: Competitive performance: Achieves solid results compared to prior methods, especially those needing manual supervision.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation & ablation: Extensive baselines, hyperparameter tuning, and ablation studies (e.g., β and η).
- Strength: Insightful embedding analysis: Shows concepts and phrases cluster semantically and reflect ontology structure.
- Strength: Clear presentation & reproducibility: Well-written paper with released code and dataset.
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty: Core idea is an application of word2vec with distant supervision rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.
- Weakness: Ontology dependency: Performance depends on ontology quality; errors/ambiguities may propagate to embeddings.
- Weakness: Low inter-annotator agreement: Fleiss’ Kappa of 0.24 on the new dataset raises concerns about dataset reliability.
- Weakness: Limited task evaluation: Evaluation restricted to similarity/relatedness; lacks downstream NLP task demonstrations.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to ontology quality and were mitigation strategies explored?
- Question: Given low inter-annotator agreement, how reliable are results from the new dataset and were alternative QC measures considered?
- Question: What is the computational cost and scaling behavior with corpus size and ontology complexity?
- Question: Practical impact of β and η hyperparameters — any guidelines for choosing them?
- Question: Can the method be applied in lower-resource languages lacking high-quality ontologies and large corpora?
- Additional: Supplemental material is helpful for hyperparameter tuning and data collection details.
- Additional: Paper should discuss related work on knowledge graph embeddings that combine relational structure and text.
- Additional: Recommend adding an error analysis to identify common model mistakes and improvement directions.
- Recommendation: Solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing questions and concerns.
