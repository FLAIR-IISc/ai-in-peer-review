- Overall: Reasonable, well-motivated weakly-supervised joint embedding method with competitive results; accept with minor revisions due to limited novelty and analysis.

- Contribution: Proposes a practical weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontologies without manual annotation.

- Contribution: Introduces a new dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world entities.

- Contribution: Provides extensive empirical validation across biomedical and general-domain tasks with comparisons to baselines.

- Strength: Method is practical and scalable by leveraging ontologies and unannotated text, avoiding manual annotation bottlenecks.

- Strength: Demonstrates competitive or superior performance on several biomedical benchmarks.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation using established datasets plus the new dataset strengthens empirical claims.

- Strength: Analysis of embedding space (MAP scores, cosine similarity) gives insight into how ontological relationships are captured.

- Strength: Code and dataset release improves reproducibility and supports future work.

- Weakness: Novelty is incremental; similar ideas (e.g., Wang et al. 2014a) exist and clearer positioning is needed.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and lack of explanation for corpus-dependent optimal settings.

- Weakness: Incomplete error analysis, especially lacking investigation into why certain semantic types (e.g., “sign or symptom”) perform worse.

- Weakness: Uneven domain performance; method works better on biomedical (structured) data than on the general-domain dataset.

- Question: Explicitly clarify differences from Wang et al. (2014a), emphasizing joint embedding and ontology-based weak supervision.

- Question: Explain why optimal hyperparameters vary across corpora and identify corpus-specific factors influencing them.

- Question: Provide hypotheses and examples explaining poorer semantic type clustering for types like “sign or symptom.”

- Question: Explain causes of weaker performance on the general-domain dataset and suggest possible modifications to improve it.

- Additional: Define “distant supervision” more precisely in the paper’s context.

- Additional: Add a brief discussion of computational complexity and scalability to large ontologies/corpora.

- Additional: Improve organization and readability of the supplemental material.
