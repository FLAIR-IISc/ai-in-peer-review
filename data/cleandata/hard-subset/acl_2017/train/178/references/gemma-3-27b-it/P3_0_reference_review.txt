## Peer Review: A Weakly-Supervised Method for Jointly Embedding Concepts, Phrases, and Words

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a weakly-supervised method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words. The core idea is to leverage existing ontology mappings (concept-phrase pairs) as distant supervision for training embeddings using a modified skip-gram model. The method trains embeddings by treating phrase occurrences as concept occurrences within a corpus, effectively bridging the gap between lexical forms and abstract concepts. The authors evaluate their approach on biomedical and general-domain datasets, demonstrating competitive performance compared to existing methods, and provide a novel dataset for real-world entity similarity and relatedness.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **A practical approach to weakly-supervised concept embedding:** The paper presents a feasible and relatively straightforward method for embedding concepts without relying on expensive human annotation. The use of existing ontology mappings and a modified word2vec framework makes this approach scalable and applicable to various domains.
2. **Novel Dataset for Real-World Entity Similarity/Relatedness:** The authors contribute a valuable dataset for evaluating concept embeddings in the general domain, which is a relatively unexplored area. This dataset addresses a gap in existing evaluation resources.
3. **Demonstration of ontology structure reflection in embeddings:** The analysis showing that concept and phrase embeddings are clustered in a way that reflects the ontology structure provides insight into the model’s learning process and validates the approach's ability to capture semantic relationships.

**Strengths:**

1. **Sound Methodology & Clear Presentation:** The proposed method is well-motivated and clearly explained. The adaptation of word2vec to handle concepts alongside words and phrases is logical and easy to follow. The inclusion of the β and η parameters to control compositionality is a thoughtful addition.
2. **Strong Empirical Evaluation:** The authors provide a comprehensive empirical evaluation using a variety of datasets, including both biomedical and general-domain data. Comparison with several baselines, including supervised methods, demonstrates the competitiveness of the proposed approach.  The thorough hyperparameter tuning adds credibility to the results.
3. **Practical Value & Scalability:** The weakly-supervised nature of the approach makes it highly practical for domains where annotated data is scarce. The method’s scalability is also a significant advantage, as it can leverage large unannotated corpora.
4. **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of embedding organization (concept-phrase proximity, semantic type clustering) provides valuable insights into the model's behavior and confirms that it’s capturing meaningful semantic relationships.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Methodology:** While the combination of techniques is well-executed, the core methodology (adapting word2vec with distant supervision) isn't groundbreaking.  The novelty lies more in the specific application to concept embedding and the thorough evaluation rather than a fundamentally new algorithmic approach.
2. **Performance Gains are Modest:** The performance improvements over strong baselines (like Chiu-30) are often marginal. While competitive, the method doesn't demonstrably *outperform* existing approaches in a substantial way across all tasks. More significant gains would strengthen the contribution.
3. **Reliance on Ontology Quality:** The performance heavily depends on the quality and completeness of the underlying ontology.  Errors or inconsistencies in the ontology will propagate to the embeddings, potentially limiting their accuracy. This limitation is acknowledged but could be addressed more explicitly with sensitivity analyses.
4. **Fleiss’ Kappa for the New Dataset is Low:**  A Fleiss’ Kappa of 0.24 suggests only “fair agreement” between annotators for the new entity similarity/relatedness dataset. This raises concerns about the reliability and validity of the dataset, potentially impacting the conclusions drawn from experiments using it. Further investigation into the sources of disagreement and potential refinement of annotation guidelines would be beneficial.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Ontology Sensitivity:** How sensitive are the results to variations in the ontology? Did you experiment with different ontologies or subsets of the ontology to assess the impact of ontology quality on the embeddings?
2. **Kappa Improvement:** What steps could be taken to improve the inter-annotator agreement for the new dataset? Were there specific entity pairs or types of entities that exhibited particularly low agreement?
3. **Downstream Task Performance:**  Have you evaluated the embeddings on any downstream NLP tasks (e.g., entity linking, relation extraction)?  This would provide a stronger indication of their practical utility.
4. **Impact of β and η:**  Could you provide more intuition about *why* the β and η parameters tend to perform best at low values? Is there a trade-off between capturing compositional semantics and avoiding overfitting?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The supplementary material should include a more detailed discussion of the annotation process for the new dataset, including examples of annotation guidelines and inter-annotator disagreement.
*   Consider adding a section discussing the limitations of the method and potential future research directions.
*   The writing quality is generally good, but some sentences are lengthy and could be simplified for clarity.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of concept embedding. While the core methodology isn’t revolutionary, the practical approach, thorough evaluation, and novel dataset make it a worthwhile addition to the literature. Addressing the weaknesses regarding performance gains and the reliability of the new dataset would further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
