- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-derived phrase supervision and a modified skip-gram model; evaluated on biomedical tasks and a new real-world entity dataset.

- Contribution — Weakly-Supervised Approach: Introduces a scalable, practical method that uses phrase occurrences as distant supervision to learn concept embeddings without expensive annotations.

- Contribution — New Dataset: Provides a novel dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness that fills a gap in publicly available evaluation resources.

- Contribution — Empirical Validation: Offers comprehensive evaluation across biomedical and general-domain datasets with analyses showing embeddings reflect ontological structure.

- Strength — Practicality & Scalability: Method leverages distant supervision and scales to large corpora, making it useful where annotations are scarce.

- Strength — Competitive Performance: Achieves competitive or superior results on similarity/relatedness tasks, including strong performance on the new real-world dataset.

- Strength — Thorough Evaluation: Includes multiple baselines, embedding-space analyses, and hyperparameter tuning, providing clear insights into method behavior.

- Strength — Reproducibility: Paper is well-written and authors provide implementation ("cui2vec"), aiding reproducibility and impact.

- Weakness — Limited Novelty: Core technique (adapting word2vec with distant supervision) is not highly novel; novelty is mainly in application and evaluation.

- Weakness — Ontology Sensitivity: Performance depends on quality and coverage of underlying ontologies (UMLS, YAGO), a limitation insufficiently discussed.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Optimal hyperparameters vary across corpora, raising concerns about generalizability and need for adaptive tuning.

- Question — Ontology Dependency: How robust are results to noisy or incomplete ontology mappings; any experiments on this sensitivity?

- Question — Hyperparameter Transfer: Can optimal hyperparameters transfer between corpora, or can they be predicted/automatically adapted from corpus characteristics?

- Question — Downstream Tasks: Do embeddings improve performance on downstream tasks (e.g., relation extraction, QA) beyond intrinsic similarity/relatedness evaluations?

- Question — Negative Sampling: How important is negative sampling; have alternative strategies or varying negative sample sizes been explored?

- Additional Comment — Notation: Notation is generally clear but could be made more consistent (e.g., subscripts).

- Additional Comment — Supplemental Material: Supplemental material is useful and provides more experimental details and analyses.

- Additional Comment — Broader Applicability: Consider discussing application potential to other languages or domains beyond biomedicine and general knowledge.

- Overall Assessment: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions; method is practical and impactful, and the new dataset is a valuable contribution.
