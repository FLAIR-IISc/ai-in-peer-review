- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method using ontology-derived concept-phrase pairs to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words, validated on biomedical and general datasets and a new entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution — Novel Dataset: Introduces a new dataset for real-world entity similarity and relatedness evaluation, addressing gaps in existing resources.

- Contribution — Practical Weak Supervision: Uses distant supervision from existing ontologies to learn concept embeddings without manual annotation, offering a scalable, practical approach.

- Contribution — Joint Embedding Framework: Presents a clear framework to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words, capturing multi-granularity semantic relationships.

- Strength — Strong Experimental Validation: Comprehensive experiments show competitive or superior performance versus baselines, especially on larger datasets.

- Strength — Clear Methodology & Code: Provides clear training objectives, hyperparameters, and released code ("cui2vec"), aiding reproducibility.

- Strength — Insightful Analysis: Analysis (MAP, compositional evaluations) reveals how embeddings capture semantics, including that phrases are closer to component words than concepts to phrases.

- Strength — Biomedical Relevance: Demonstrates utility for biomedical NLP by applying to UMLS and PubMed-derived data.

- Weakness — Limited Novelty: Core method builds on existing techniques (skip-gram with negative sampling); main novelty is the application of distant supervision rather than a new algorithm.

- Weakness — Ontology Sensitivity: Performance depends on ontology quality and completeness; more discussion on impacts of ontology errors is needed.

- Weakness — Performance on Small Datasets: Underperforms compared to some methods (e.g., CHOI) on smaller biomedical datasets like MayoSRS and Mini MayoSRS, suggesting data requirements.

- Weakness — Moderate IAA on New Dataset: Fleiss’ Kappa of 0.24 indicates only moderate inter-annotator agreement, weakening dataset validity without more detail on discrepancies.

- Question — Ontology Robustness: How sensitive are embeddings to noisy or incomplete ontology mappings, and how can impacts be mitigated?

- Question — Scalability: How well does the method scale to very large ontologies (millions of concepts) and what are computational bottlenecks?

- Question — Downstream Evaluation: Have embeddings been evaluated on downstream tasks (e.g., concept normalization, relation extraction) beyond similarity/relatedness?

- Question — Annotation Discrepancies: What were the specific annotation disagreements in the new dataset and which entity pairs were hardest to annotate?

- Question — Hyperparameter Tuning: Can you detail the hyperparameter selection process beyond reporting which settings performed best?

- Additional Comment — Writing Clarity: Writing is generally good but some sentences are dense and could be simplified for clarity.

- Additional Comment — Limitations & Future Work: Recommend adding a brief discussion of method limitations and potential future research directions.
