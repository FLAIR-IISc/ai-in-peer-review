- Paper proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-derived distant supervision within a modified word2vec framework.  
- Contribution: releases a novel dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness (people, places, organizations).  
- Contribution: proposes a practical weakly-supervised approach that avoids costly human annotations and leverages large ontologies.  
- Contribution: provides thorough empirical evaluation across biomedical and general domains with multiple baselines and metrics.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and motivation for concept-level representations beyond word-level methods.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across datasets, metrics, and competitive baselines strengthens credibility.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of embedding space organization and compositionality (e.g., MAP scores).  
- Strength: practical release of code and data (cui2vec) improves reproducibility.  
- Weakness: core method is incremental, building on word2vec and distant supervision with limited algorithmic novelty.  
- Weakness: performance is often inferior to stronger baselines (e.g., Choi et al., 2016b), especially in biomedical tasks.  
- Weakness: newly created dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ kappa = 0.24), raising reliability concerns.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning is under-described and may indicate sensitivity and limited generalizability across corpora.  
- Question: request for deeper analysis of dataset disagreement sources, mitigation steps, and effect of using high-agreement subset.  
- Question: ask for an ablation study on β and η hyperparameters and scenarios where non-zero values help.  
- Question: request comparison to state-of-the-art knowledge graph embedding methods (e.g., TransE, ComplEx).  
- Question: ask whether embeddings were evaluated on downstream tasks (entity linking, relation extraction) and results if available.  
- Additional comment: writing is clear and supplemental material is appreciated; clarify “concept” vs. “entity” in the introduction.  
- Overall: solid, practical contribution with a valuable dataset and reproducible resources, but limited novelty and dataset reliability warrant minor revisions.
