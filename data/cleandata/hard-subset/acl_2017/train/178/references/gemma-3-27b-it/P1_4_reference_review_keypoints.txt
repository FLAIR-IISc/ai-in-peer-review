- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that links ontology concepts to phrases and trains joint embeddings for concepts, phrases, and words via distributional similarity.
- Trains concept embeddings by using contexts of linked phrases from an unannotated corpus.
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain similarity/relatedness tasks and shown competitive with supervised models.
- Introduces and releases a novel dataset for real-world entity (people, places, organizations) similarity and relatedness.
- Contribution: provides a new benchmark dataset addressing limits of existing resources.
- Contribution: offers a practical weakly-supervised approach that avoids costly manual annotations, useful for domains like biomedicine.
- Contribution: demonstrates benefits of jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words for capturing semantic relationships.
- Strength: clear motivation highlighting limitations of word-only embeddings for multi-word expressions and concepts.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple datasets with ablations and comparisons to strong baselines.
- Strength: insightful analysis of embedding space organization and MAP evaluations showing semantic clustering.
- Strength: code and dataset release enhance reproducibility.
- Strength: paper is well-written and well-organized.
- Weakness: core method (adapting word2vec with distant supervision) is not fundamentally novel.
- Weakness: performance gains over strong baselines are modest, limiting practical impact.
- Weakness: method shows sensitivity to hyperparameter tuning, posing usability challenges for new datasets.
- Weakness: new dataset’s Fleiss’ kappa of 0.24 indicates only fair annotator agreement and raises reliability concerns.
- Question: what is the computational cost of hyperparameter tuning on large-scale datasets and can transfer learning reduce it?
- Question: how exactly would the learned embeddings be applied to entity linking and what advantages would they offer?
- Question: were steps taken to improve inter-annotator agreement for the new dataset, and how does low kappa affect evaluation reliability?
- Question: how should practitioners choose between phrase-based approximations (APPROXPHRco) and word-based (APPROXWORD) depending on domain or application?
- Additional: recommend discussing limitations of relying on pre-defined ontologies and their effect on performance.
- Additional: suggest providing a detailed error analysis to identify and fix model weaknesses.
- Additional: propose including a short practical use-case (e.g., biomedical) to illustrate real-world benefits.
- Overall recommendation: solid, well-executed paper with minor revisions suggested; recommend acceptance with addressed weaknesses.
