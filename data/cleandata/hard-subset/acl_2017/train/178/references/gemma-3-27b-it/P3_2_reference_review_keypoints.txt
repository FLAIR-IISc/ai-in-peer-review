- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) and unannotated corpora; evaluated on biomedical tasks and a new real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset.

- Contribution — Novel Dataset: Releases a new dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.

- Contribution — Practical Method: Presents a scalable weakly-supervised approach leveraging distant supervision from ontologies to avoid costly annotations.

- Contribution — Joint Embedding Benefits: Demonstrates that jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words yields competitive performance versus word/phrase-only methods.

- Strength — Clear Motivation: Clearly defines the problem and need for representing concepts beyond simple word compositions.

- Strength — Thorough Evaluation: Comprehensive experiments across biomedical and general-domain datasets with strong baselines and ablation studies.

- Strength — Insightful Analysis: Provides useful analyses (MAP scores, concept-phrase/phrase-word relationships) and observations (e.g., difficulty with “sign or symptom” clustering).

- Strength — Clarity and Reproducibility: Well-written paper with figures/tables and plans to release code/data.

- Weakness — Limited Method Novelty: Core technique is an adaptation of existing word2vec/distant supervision rather than a novel algorithmic advance.

- Weakness — Weaker General-Domain Performance: Underperforms on the new real-world dataset, with phrase approximations beating direct concept embeddings.

- Weakness — Low IAA on New Dataset: Fleiss’ Kappa of 0.24 indicates only fair annotator agreement; participant filtering details are insufficient.

- Weakness — Insufficient Negative Sampling Analysis: Lacks investigation of negative sampling strategies and their impact (e.g., hierarchical softmax).

- Question — Novelty Clarification: Which aspects of the approach are most novel compared to prior embedding and distant supervision work?

- Question — General-Domain Failure Modes: What factors explain lower performance on the real-world entity dataset and its challenges?

- Question — Improving IAA: Were alternative annotation strategies considered to raise inter-annotator agreement?

- Question — Negative Sampling Experiments: Did the authors try different negative sampling methods and why choose the current approach?

- Question — Hyperparameter Sensitivity: How should hyperparameters be adapted for downstream tasks and what guidance can the authors provide?

- Additional — Related Work: Related work discussion is comprehensive and well-organized.

- Additional — Supplemental Detail: Supplemental material should include more detail on hyperparameter tuning.

- Additional — Error Analysis: Consider adding an analysis of common model errors to better understand limitations.

- Overall Assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with useful dataset and analyses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
