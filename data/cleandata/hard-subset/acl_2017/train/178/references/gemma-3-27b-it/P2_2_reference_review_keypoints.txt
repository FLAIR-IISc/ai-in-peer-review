- Presents a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using distributional similarity and ontologies (UMLS, YAGO).
- Adapts skip-gram/word2vec with hyperparameters controlling compositionality and phrase-to-concept preprocessing for scalable training.
- Introduces a novel real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset for evaluation.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on biomedical and general-domain concept similarity/relatedness tasks.
- Shows empirical benefits of joint embeddings in capturing ontology relationships.
- Clear motivation: addresses multi-word concepts and synonymy limitations of standard word embeddings.
- Methodology is sound and scalable, with clever phrase-to-identifier preprocessing and tunable β and η parameters.
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and comparisons to strong baselines.
- Provides analysis of embedding space and MAP-based insights into concept organization.
- Core method lacks fundamental novelty—primarily an application of existing techniques with distant supervision.
- Performance is not consistently superior; some baselines (e.g., Choi et al., 2016b) outperform on smaller datasets.
- Method appears to benefit from larger datasets, raising data-dependence concerns.
- Evaluation relies heavily on Spearman correlation; lacks downstream task evaluations for practical validation.
- New dataset has moderate inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa = 0.24), raising reliability concerns.
- Requests analysis on why the method underperforms Choi et al. on small biomedical datasets.
- Asks whether downstream/extrinsic task evaluations have been or will be explored.
- Requests clarification on data-quality controls for the new dataset and impact of removing low-quality annotators.
- Asks about hyperparameter sensitivity and trade-offs between intrinsic and extrinsic performance.
- Positive notes: code and dataset release, clear and well-organized writing.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution and recommend accept, contingent on addressing weaknesses and questions.
