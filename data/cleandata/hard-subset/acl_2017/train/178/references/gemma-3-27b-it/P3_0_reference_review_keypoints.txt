- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that uses ontology concept-phrase mappings and a modified skip-gram model to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words.
- Trains embeddings by treating phrase occurrences as concept occurrences to bridge lexical forms and abstract concepts.
- Evaluated on biomedical and general-domain datasets and introduces a novel dataset for entity similarity/relatedness.
- Contribution: practical, scalable weakly-supervised approach for concept embedding using existing ontologies and word2vec adaptation.
- Contribution: releases a new general-domain dataset for real-world entity similarity and relatedness evaluation.
- Contribution: shows embeddings reflect ontology structure via clustering of concepts and phrases.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated methodology with sensible extensions (β and η) to control compositionality.
- Strength: strong empirical evaluation across multiple datasets with comparisons to several baselines and careful hyperparameter tuning.
- Strength: method is practical and scalable for domains lacking annotated data.
- Strength: insightful analyses demonstrating semantic organization and type clustering in embeddings.
- Weakness: core methodology’s novelty is limited—mainly an application/adaptation rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.
- Weakness: performance gains over strong baselines are modest and not consistently substantial across tasks.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on ontology quality; errors or incompleteness can degrade embedding quality.
- Weakness: new dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa = 0.24), raising reliability concerns.
- Question: How sensitive are results to variations, subsets, or quality of the ontology?
- Question: How can inter-annotator agreement for the new dataset be improved, and which pairs caused disagreement?
- Question: Have the embeddings been evaluated on downstream tasks (e.g., entity linking, relation extraction)?
- Question: Why do β and η perform best at low values; what’s the trade-off between compositionality and overfitting?
- Additional: Supplementary material should detail the annotation process, guidelines, and disagreement examples.
- Additional: Add a limitations section and future work directions.
- Overall: Solid, well-executed paper with practical value and thorough evaluation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
