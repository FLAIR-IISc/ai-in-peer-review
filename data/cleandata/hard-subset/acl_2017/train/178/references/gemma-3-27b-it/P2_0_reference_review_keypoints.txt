- Proposes a weakly-supervised method that uses ontology phrase-to-concept linkages as distant supervision to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words.
- Uses a modified skip-gram model with hyperparameters controlling compositionality to train concept/phrase/word embeddings.
- Evaluates on biomedical and general-domain datasets and releases a new dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.
- Main contribution: novel dataset addressing a gap in real-world entity similarity benchmarks.
- Main contribution: practical weakly-supervised approach reducing need for costly human annotation, useful in domains like biomedicine.
- Main contribution: thorough empirical validation showing competitive performance against strong baselines.
- Strength: clear motivation—word-only embeddings struggle with non-compositional and multi-lexical concepts.
- Strength: sound, logical methodology and well-explained model modifications and hyperparameters.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets and baselines, providing strong empirical grounding.
- Strength: insightful analysis of embedding space organization showing partial preservation of ontological relationships.
- Strength: code and dataset release supporting reproducibility and future work.
- Weakness: core method (distant supervision + modified skip-gram) is not fundamentally novel relative to prior entity/sense embedding work.
- Weakness: method depends on ontology quality; paper lacks deep analysis of ontology biases/errors and their impact.
- Weakness: performance gains over strong baselines are often modest; statistical significance and downstream impact are unclear.
- Weakness: new dataset’s Fleiss’ Kappa = 0.24 (“fair”) raises concerns about annotation reliability and quality-control details.
- Questions for authors: how do ontology errors/biases affect embeddings and can noisy mappings be filtered or mitigated?
- Questions for authors: more detail on how β and η affect embeddings and which concepts/phrases benefit from tuning them?
- Questions for authors: what are computational costs for training at larger scales and higher dimensionalities?
- Questions for authors: have embeddings been tested on downstream tasks (e.g., entity linking, relation extraction), and if not, why?
- Additional comments: some figures are small and hard to read; consider expanding them.
- Additional comments: include discussion of related work on knowledge-graph embedding techniques beyond current citations.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with useful dataset and evaluations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses.
