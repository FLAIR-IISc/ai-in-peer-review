- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using distant supervision from ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) and distributional context training, validated on biomedical and general-domain tasks.

- Contribution—Novel Dataset: Releases a new dataset for entity similarity/relatedness of people, places, and organizations, filling a gap in existing resources.

- Contribution—Practical Method: Presents a viable weakly-supervised approach that leverages knowledge bases and unannotated text to learn concept embeddings without heavy annotation.

- Contribution—Empirical Validation: Provides comprehensive experiments, baselines, and ablations showing competitive performance.

- Strength—Strong Empirical Results: Method performs competitively and often outperforms baselines, especially on larger and biomedical datasets.

- Strength—Well-Motivated: Clearly motivates need for concept-level embeddings beyond word-centric models to handle multi-word concepts.

- Strength—Comprehensive Evaluation: Uses multiple datasets, similarity and relatedness tasks, and ablation studies (β and η) to analyze effects.

- Strength—Clear & Reproducible: Methodology, hyperparameters, corpora, and ontologies are detailed; code and dataset are released.

- Strength—Embedding Analysis: Provides insightful analysis of how concepts, phrases, and words relate in the embedding space (Figures 3 & 4).

- Weakness—Limited Novelty: Core method builds on established techniques (skip-gram with negative sampling); main novelty is distant supervision/application.

- Weakness—Ontology Sensitivity: Performance depends on ontology quality; paper lacks detailed discussion of effects of ontology errors.

- Weakness—Low Dataset Agreement: New dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ Kappa = 0.24), raising concerns about reliability.

- Weakness—Hyperparameter Complexity: Extensive tuning increases complexity and computational demands, possibly hindering reproducibility for limited-resource researchers.

- Weakness—Limited Failure Analysis: Paper focuses on positive results and lacks in-depth discussion of failure cases or suboptimal embeddings.

- Question 1: How sensitive are results to ontology quality and examples of ontology-induced errors affecting embeddings?

- Question 2: Can you detail annotator disagreements in the new dataset and any steps taken to mitigate low agreement (e.g., filtering annotators)?

- Question 3: Why did some semantic types (e.g., “sign or symptom”) show poor MAP clustering, and what explains these failures?

- Question 4: Any plans or preliminary results for applying embeddings to downstream tasks like entity linking or relation extraction?

- Question 5: What computational resources were required for hyperparameter tuning and would the process be feasible for researchers with limited compute?

- Additional Comment: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and supplemental material is helpful.

- Additional Comment: Code release is appreciated and will aid further research.

- Overall Assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with practical value; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing ontology quality, dataset reliability, and hyperparameter tuning complexity.
