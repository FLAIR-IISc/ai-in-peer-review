## Review for ACL Submission: "A Weakly-Supervised Method for Jointly Embedding Concepts, Phrases, and Words"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a weakly-supervised method for jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words, leveraging a distant supervision approach using existing ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) and unannotated corpora. The core idea is to train embeddings by utilizing the contexts of phrases that represent concepts, effectively bridging the gap between lexical forms and abstract semantic entities. The authors evaluate their approach on biomedical concept similarity/relatedness tasks and introduce a novel dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Novel Dataset:** The creation and release of a dataset for evaluating similarity and relatedness of real-world entities is a valuable contribution. Existing datasets in this area are limited, and this provides a useful resource for the community.
2. **Practical Weakly-Supervised Approach:** The method offers a practical approach to concept embedding that avoids the need for costly human annotations, relying on readily available ontologies and corpora. This makes it potentially scalable to a wider range of domains.
3. **Demonstration of Joint Embedding Benefits:** The paper shows that jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words can achieve competitive performance compared to methods relying solely on word or phrase embeddings, highlighting the advantages of leveraging structured knowledge.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of representing concepts beyond simple word compositions and the need for models that can handle multiple textual forms for a single concept. The motivation is well-grounded in existing challenges in semantic representation.
2. **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors conduct a thorough evaluation using multiple datasets (both biomedical and general-domain) and compare their method against a range of baselines, including strong pre-trained embeddings.  The ablation studies with different hyperparameters and approximations (APPROXPHR, APPROXWORD) are particularly valuable.
3. **Insightful Analysis:** The analysis of embedding relationships (concept-phrase, phrase-word) through MAP scores provides valuable insights into how well the model captures semantic relationships and the tradeoffs involved in the joint embedding process. The observation about the difficulty of clustering “sign or symptom” concepts is also a useful finding.
4. **Well-Written and Accessible:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and experimental setup, making it easy to understand for researchers in the field. The inclusion of figures and tables enhances readability.
5. **Code and Data Release:** The authors’ commitment to releasing the code and dataset promotes reproducibility and encourages further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of the Core Method:** While the application to joint concept/phrase/word embedding is well-executed, the underlying approach (adapting word2vec with distant supervision) is not fundamentally novel.  The core technical contribution lies in the specific adaptation and application rather than a breakthrough algorithmic innovation.
2. **Suboptimal Performance on General Domain Dataset:** While competitive on biomedical datasets, the performance on the newly created real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset is less convincing. The phrase-based approximation consistently outperforms the direct concept embeddings, suggesting limitations in the model's ability to effectively learn representations for more abstract entities.
3. **IAA on Novel Dataset is Relatively Low:** A Fleiss' Kappa of 0.24 for the newly created dataset indicates fair agreement among annotators. While acceptable, a higher level of agreement would strengthen the reliability and value of the dataset.  More details regarding the participant filtering criteria would be helpful.
4. **Lack of Analysis on Negative Sampling:** The influence of the negative sampling strategy on the performance of the model is not thoroughly investigated.  Different negative sampling techniques (e.g., hierarchical softmax) might yield improved results.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Novelty Discussion:**  Given that the core technique builds upon existing word embedding methods and distant supervision, could you elaborate on what aspects of your approach are most novel and distinct from prior work?
2. **General Domain Performance:** What factors might explain the relatively lower performance of your method on the real-world entity dataset compared to the biomedical datasets?  Are there specific characteristics of the general domain data that pose challenges for the model?
3. **IAA Improvement:** Were any alternative strategies considered to improve inter-annotator agreement on the new dataset (e.g., more detailed annotation guidelines, larger number of annotators)?
4. **Negative Sampling:** Did you experiment with different negative sampling strategies, and if so, what were the results?  Could you provide some justification for choosing the current negative sampling approach?
5. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** You mention that optimized hyperparameters are likely to change for downstream tasks. Can you elaborate on this and perhaps provide guidance on how to best adapt the hyperparameters to different applications?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The discussion of related work is comprehensive and well-organized.
*   The supplemental material would benefit from more detailed explanations of the hyperparameter tuning process.
*  Consider adding an analysis of the most common errors made by the model to gain further insights into its limitations.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a practical approach to jointly embedding concepts, phrases, and words. While the core method isn't groundbreaking, the combination of the novel dataset, comprehensive evaluation, and insightful analysis makes it a valuable contribution to the field.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above.
