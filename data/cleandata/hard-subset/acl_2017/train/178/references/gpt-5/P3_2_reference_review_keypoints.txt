- Method: Jointly embed words, phrases, and ontology concepts via SGNS using phrase→concept mappings as distant supervision, with compositionality regularizers β (phrase→word average) and η (concept→phrase average).

- Contribution 1: Practical, scalable framework that trains concept embeddings from unannotated corpora using only phrase-to-concept dictionaries (e.g., UMLS, YAGO).

- Contribution 2: Empirical results showing competitive biomedical performance on intrinsic similarity/relatedness and analyses of compositionality and semantic type clustering.

- Contribution 3: Release of a new crowdsourced entity similarity/relatedness dataset (688 pairs each) and code (cui2vec).

- Strength: Clear, pragmatic weak-supervision setup that leverages canonical mappings and scales to large ontologies without manual annotation.

- Strength: Competitive biomedical results matching or outperforming annotation-heavy baselines on UMNSRS similarity and competitive on relatedness.

- Strength: Careful comparisons, compositionality analysis, and semantic-type clustering provide nuanced diagnostics beyond lexical baselines.

- Strength: Experiments conducted across multiple domains (PubMed, clinical notes, Wikipedia/NYT) and large concept/entity counts demonstrate practicality and breadth.

- Strength: Resource contribution addresses scarcity of concept/entity-level intrinsic evaluations.

- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty—approach echoes prior work and main additions (joint learning, simple regularizers) are incremental; regularizers often tune to 0.

- Weakness: Crude ambiguity handling—treats all phrase occurrences as mentions of all candidate concepts (uniformly), introducing label noise and no context-based disambiguation or downweighting.

- Weakness: Clarity and notation issues (overloaded k, typos, confusing ENS/parameterization) impede reproducibility; asymmetric SGNS/output vocabularies not clearly specified.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to intrinsic tasks with tuning on test data and no extrinsic benchmarks (e.g., entity linking, clinical coding) to demonstrate downstream utility.

- Weakness: Crowdsourced entity datasets have low inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.24) and lack external entity-embedding baselines; phrase-averaging often outperforms direct concept embeddings.

- Q1 (ambiguity): What fraction of phrases are ambiguous and how does performance vary with ambiguity; have priors or latent-assignment approaches been tried?

- Q2 (parameterization): Clarify whether output/context embeddings are words only, and why ENS is separate—provide schematic of input/output vocabularies and losses.

- Q3 (regularizers): Provide performance curves/ablations for β and η, and identify settings (e.g., rare concepts) where they help.

- Q4 (phrase replacement): Explain resolving overlapping matches, case/morphology normalization, fraction of tokens replaced per corpus, and effects on word embeddings.

- Q5 (ontology structure): Report correlations between embedding distances and ontology graph distances beyond semantic-type clustering.

- Q6 (clinical data): Clarify IRB/consent/de-identification status for clinical notes and shareability of derived resources for reproducibility.

- Q7 (datasets): Will worker-level scores be released; report per-pair variance and analyze whether low-κ pairs drive weak conclusions.
