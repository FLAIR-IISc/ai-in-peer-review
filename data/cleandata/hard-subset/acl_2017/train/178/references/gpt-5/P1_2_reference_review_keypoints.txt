- Proposes a weakly supervised method to jointly embed ontology concepts, phrases, and words by replacing ontology-linked phrases with identifiers and adapting skip-gram with negative sampling.
- Introduces two regularizers: β to align a phrase with the average of its words, and η to align a concept with the average of its phrases.
- Requires only an ontology concept–phrase lexicon and raw text; no manual annotation or KG relations.
- Evaluates on biomedical UMLS similarity/relatedness datasets and releases a new crowdsourced YAGO entity similarity/relatedness dataset.
- Provides analyses of concept–phrase/phrase–word compositionality and semantic type clustering, plus hyperparameter studies.
- Practical and scalable: can learn large-scale concept embeddings (e.g., ~750k YAGO entities) from raw corpora without KB relations.
- Competitive intrinsic biomedical performance: matches or ties top baselines on UMNSRS Similarity while offering greater coverage and weaker supervision.
- Cross-domain evaluation and dataset release: tests on biomedical and general domains and provides a reproducible YAGO dataset with QC measures.
- Transparent analyses and ablations: investigates compositionality, clustering, and regularizer effects, aiding interpretability.
- Reproducible implementation: straightforward skip-gram adaptation and promised release of code/embeddings (cui2vec).
- Limited novelty and weak jointness: largely a direct skip-gram adaptation with minimal cross-level information sharing.
- Concept vectors often approximate phrase averages; simple approximations (APPROXPHRco) can match or outperform trained concept vectors.
- No disambiguation: model treats ambiguous phrases as denoting all candidate concepts equally, likely injecting noise.
- Unquantified ambiguity impact: paper lacks statistics on phrase-to-concept ambiguity and its effect on training/performance.
- Evaluation is intrinsic-only and may not reflect extrinsic utility; hyperparameter tuning on small evaluation sets risks overfitting.
- New YAGO dataset issues: modest size and low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ=0.24), and lacks comparisons to strong entity embedding baselines.
- Underuse of ontology structure: model ignores relations/hierarchies and regularizers (β/η) often provide little benefit.
- Questions on ambiguity handling: how many phrases map to multiple concepts and did authors try context-based weighting or disambiguation?
- Questions on negative sampling: are negatives drawn only from words, and are phrases/concepts ever used as context targets?
- Questions on phrase matching: how are overlapping/nested phrases, casing, morphology, and tokenization mismatches handled; what coverage is achieved?
- Questions on evaluation validity: can results be provided with strict held-out splits or alternative intrinsic/extrinsic tasks to avoid tuning bias?
- Request to compare against general-domain entity embedding baselines (Wikipedia links, KB-relation methods) on the new dataset.
- Ask whether extrinsic evaluations (entity linking, concept normalization, clinical coding) were attempted or planned.
- Suggestions: incorporate ontology graph structure and context-sensitive disambiguation to reduce noise and capture non-lexical semantics.
- Suggest an ablation suite: disable concept training, vary β/η, and train only on unambiguous phrases to isolate benefits.
- Recommend releasing code, full embeddings, and detailed preprocessing scripts to enable large-scale replication.
