- Proposes a weakly supervised method adapting SGNS to jointly embed words, surface phrases, and ontology concepts by replacing phrases with IDs and updating concept vectors from any surface form contexts.  
- Adds compositionality regularizers encouraging phrase ≈ mean(words) and concept ≈ mean(phrases).  
- Instantiates at scale for UMLS and YAGO and evaluates on biomedical and a new general-domain entity similarity/relatedness dataset; releases code and data.

- Practical, scalable weak supervision: DS-style mapping enables training without manual annotations or KB triples.  
- Joint word/phrase/concept space: Enables using any level as features and computing cross-level similarities for downstream tasks.  
- Competitive performance with minimal supervision: Matches strong baselines on UMNSRS Similarity; phrase approximations perform strongly.  
- Clear ablations: Comparisons to word-only, phrase-only, and averaging baselines show approximating concepts via phrases is often as effective.  
- Useful analyses and resources: Compositionality and clustering analyses plus released embeddings/dataset help the community.

- Limited novelty relative to prior joint text–KB embedding work; main methodological changes are modest tweaks to SGNS and DS mapping.  
- No explicit disambiguation: Equally updating all candidate concepts for ambiguous mentions injects noise and lacks analysis of ambiguity effects.  
- Ad hoc regularizers: β/η logσ(dot(mean)) terms are one-sided and lack principled alternatives or systematic ablation showing why they harm performance at scale.  
- Evaluation concerns: Hyperparameter tuning risks overfitting to intrinsic datasets, no downstream task evaluations, and the new dataset has low IAA (κ≈0.24).  
- Clarity/notation and implementation gaps: Conflated symbols, unclear ENS/output matrices, and missing details on sampling, overlaps, and phrase handling.

- How frequent/large is phrase ambiguity (distribution of |C_p|) and can performance be stratified by ambiguity or concept lexicalization breadth?  
- Have you tried weighting or context-conditioned selection (priors, soft attention, latent selection) for candidate concept updates?  
- Do phrases/concepts appear as context outputs, or is output space strictly words; did you try shared output matrices so phrases/concepts predict neighbors?  
- Please clarify ENS dimensions/notation and provide β/η ablation table including an L2 penalty alternative.  
- Can you compare coverage/data match to biomedical baselines and include a “phrase ID only” baseline to isolate concept-update gains?

- Suggest a latent-variable DS setup (EM or attention) to sample or weight candidate concepts by p(c|context).  
- Explore lightweight ontology signals (semantic-type or parent-child centroids) as regularizers without full KB relations.  
- Provide error analysis contrasting when direct concept embeddings outperform phrase averages to clarify added value.  
- Release detailed preprocessing and mapping statistics (longest-match, tokenization, frequency thresholds) for reproducibility.

- Overall: Practical, scalable approach with useful resources and analyses, moderate methodological novelty, and evaluation/clarity issues that should be addressed for stronger impact.
