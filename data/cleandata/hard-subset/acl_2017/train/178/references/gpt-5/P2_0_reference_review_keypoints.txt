- Proposal: Weakly supervised method to jointly embed ontology concepts, surface phrases, and words by replacing multiword phrases with single tokens and adapting skip-gram with negative sampling, with compositionality hyperparameters β and η.  
- Contribution 1: Scalable, annotation-free pipeline to learn concept embeddings from phrase lists, jointly embedding words/phrases/concepts.  
- Contribution 2: Empirical evidence that embeddings are competitive on biomedical similarity tasks and reasonable on a new YAGO entity similarity dataset, with analyses of compositionality and clustering.  
- Contribution 3: Release of code (cui2vec) and a new real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset.  
- Strength: Practicality and scalability—requires only an ontology-derived phrase list and raw text, scales to hundreds of thousands of concepts.  
- Strength: Competitive performance without supervision—matches or approaches strong word baselines on UMNSRS and relatedness benchmarks.  
- Strength: Cross-domain applicability and resource release—applies to YAGO entities and provides an openly released dataset and code.  
- Strength: Careful analyses—compositionality, semantic-type clustering, hyperparameter exploration, and cross-validation considered.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty and weak “jointness”—approach is incremental and lacks principled modeling of ontology structure or disambiguation.  
- Weakness: Concept matrix adds little—averaged phrase embeddings often outperform direct concept embeddings; high concept-phrase cosine suggests redundancy.  
- Weakness: Compositionality regularization (β/η) often hurts performance, questioning its practical benefit.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limitations—heavy reliance on intrinsic similarity/relatedness, hyperparameter tuning on test sets, and low IAA (κ=0.24) for the new dataset.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns—key training details (negative sampling distribution, negative vocabulary, update scheduling, phrase matching, normalization, frequency thresholds) are underspecified.  
- Weakness: Ontology structure underused and clustering performance modest (MAP 0.12–0.22), lower than some prior work.  
- Question: Clarify negative sampling—are negatives drawn only from words, and was a unified output space or sampling from phrases/concepts tried?  
- Question: Ambiguity handling—did authors try heuristics (priors, context similarity) instead of uniformly splitting updates across candidate concepts, and how sensitive are results to ambiguity?  
- Question: Training regimen—please detail how TT and TU are traversed, context window behavior, and sampling of (w,p,o) triples per epoch.  
- Question: External validity—can the authors provide at least one extrinsic task (e.g., concept normalization, entity linking, IR) to validate embeddings?  
- Question: Ethical/reproducibility—were clinical notes de-identified under IRB and are trained embeddings shareable given privacy constraints?  
- Overall assessment: Solid, practical, and scalable work with useful resources but limited methodological novelty and mixed empirical gains; extrinsic evaluation, clearer methodology, or integration of ontology graph structure would increase impact.
