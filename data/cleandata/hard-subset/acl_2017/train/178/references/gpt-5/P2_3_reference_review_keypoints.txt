- Proposes a weakly supervised method to jointly embed words, surface phrases, and ontology concepts by tagging raw text with a phrase lexicon (UMLS/YAGO) and training skip-gram with negative sampling over tagged/untagged corpora.  
- Uses uniform credit assignment for ambiguous phrases and two compositionality hyperparameters (β, η) to encourage phrase≈word-average and concept≈phrase-average; requires no annotated mentions or KB relations.  

Main contributions:
- Simple, scalable approach to jointly learn word/phrase/concept embeddings using only a phrase lexicon and raw text.  
- Broad empirical study across domains with competitive biomedical performance and release of a new entity similarity/relatedness dataset.  
- Analyses of compositionality and semantic type clustering illustrating what the learned space captures.

Strengths:
- Practical, easy-to-implement method that scales and relies on widely available resources.  
- Competitive results on several UMLS similarity/relatedness benchmarks despite weaker supervision.  
- Careful comparisons and ablations versus word/phrase approximations and prior biomedical resources.  
- Additional analyses (compositionality diagnostics, semantic type MAP) provide insight into model behavior and limitations.  
- Resource contribution: new entity similarity/relatedness dataset with transparent crowdsourcing and validation.

Weaknesses:
- Limited methodological novelty relative to prior dictionary-tagging and joint word-entity embedding work.  
- Evaluation is mainly intrinsic (Spearman’s ρ) with no downstream tasks to demonstrate practical impact or generalization.  
- Simplistic ambiguity handling (uniform assignment over candidate concepts) likely injects noise; ambiguity rates and impacts not reported.  
- Hyperparameter selection appears to use test sets (no held-out dev), risking inflated or confounded results.  
- New entity dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.24) and modest size (688 pairs), limiting reliability.  
- Some clarity and notation issues; unclear role of shared negative-sampling/output matrix and why phrase/concept contexts aren’t used.

Key questions for authors:
- What fraction of phrases are polysemous and how often do ambiguous mentions occur in corpora; can performance be reported by phrase ambiguity and with alternative weighting schemes?  
- How sensitive are results to the minimum frequency threshold (25) and do low-frequency concepts harm performance or clustering?  
- Ablations: do β and η provide benefits beyond intrinsic ρ, and what happens when only one is active?  
- Have you tried retrofitting or relation-regularized variants to incorporate ontology structure (parent-child/type adjacency)?  
- Will you release code, trained embeddings, and phrase lexicons; if not, can training scripts and PubMed/Wiki models be provided?

Overall assessment:
- A solid, pragmatic contribution with careful experiments and analyses; moderate novelty and mainly intrinsic evaluation, would be strengthened by addressing ambiguity, adding downstream tasks, or leveraging ontology structure.
