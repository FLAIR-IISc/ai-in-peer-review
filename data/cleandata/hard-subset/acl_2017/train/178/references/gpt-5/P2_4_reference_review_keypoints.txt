- Proposes a weakly supervised SGNS-based method to jointly embed words, phrases, and ontology concepts using only a phrase-to-concept lexicon and raw text.
- Replaces phrase mentions with unique tokens, trains word/phrase embeddings from surrounding words, and trains concept embeddings to predict word contexts of all surface forms with uniform distant supervision.
- Introduces optional compositionality regularizers (β for phrase→word, η for concept→phrase) and releases a new human-annotated entity similarity/relatedness dataset plus code/embeddings (cui2vec).

- Strength: Simple, scalable, and annotation-light—requires only lexicons and unannotated corpora and scales to hundreds of thousands of concepts.
- Strength: Competitive intrinsic performance against stronger-supervision baselines on biomedical and general-domain benchmarks despite noisy distant supervision.
- Strength: Multi-level analyses (cosine/MAP distributions, semantic-type clustering) provide insights into phrase–word–concept relationships and model behavior.
- Strength: Broad experimental setup across multiple corpora with hyperparameter sweeps and diverse baselines; resources released improve reproducibility and impact.
- Strength: New entity similarity/relatedness dataset fills an evaluation gap for real-world entities.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—approach is a straightforward SGNS variant and regularizers are simple; similar ideas exist in prior auto-tagging/Wikipedia-based embedding work.
- Weakness: Empirical gains are small or tied to strong baselines and evaluations are intrinsic-only with no statistical significance reported.
- Weakness: Distant supervision is noisy and under-modeled—uniform averaging across candidate concepts ignores context and likely harms disambiguation.
- Weakness: Analysis indicates concept embeddings often collapse to averages of phrase embeddings, suggesting limited added value beyond phrase/word compositions.
- Weakness: Clarity and technical presentation issues (notation typos, under-specified output/negative sampling, sparse phrase-matching details); the released dataset has low inter-annotator agreement (κ=0.24) and potential selection bias.

- Question: Did you try context-aware soft assignment (EM/attention/type constraints) for ambiguous phrases instead of uniform averaging, and how did it affect η and performance?
- Question: Are output embeddings shared across words/phrases/concepts, is ENS |W|×d, and what unigram smoothing was used for negative sampling?
- Question: Are reported differences statistically significant (e.g., bootstrap CIs) and how sensitive are results to phrase-frequency thresholds and greedy matching?
- Question: Can you provide extrinsic evaluations (entity linking, concept normalization, retrieval) to demonstrate downstream utility?
- Question: Will you release full trained embeddings for all corpora, preprocessing/training code, and per-item annotations/scripts for the entity dataset?

- Overall: Practical and reproducible with useful resources and careful analysis, but modest conceptual novelty, small intrinsic gains, noisy supervision, and limited evidence of downstream benefit, making the submission borderline.
