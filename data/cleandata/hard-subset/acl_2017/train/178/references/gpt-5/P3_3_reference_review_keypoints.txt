- Proposed weakly supervised method jointly embedding words, surface phrases, and ontology concepts via greedy phrase tagging, skip-gram on parallel tagged/untagged streams, and two compositionality regularizers (β, η).

- Training data: PubMed abstracts, clinical notes, and Wikipedia+NYT; evaluations on biomedical similarity/relatedness datasets (MayoSRS, UMNSRS) and a new crowdsourced entity similarity/relatedness dataset.

- Main claimed contributions: scalable ontology-driven embeddings without manual concept annotations; intrinsic evaluation competitive with supervised systems; new crowdsourced dataset and released implementation (cui2vec).

- Strength: tackles an important practical gap—aligning multiword surface forms and canonical concepts at large scale (UMLS, YAGO).

- Strength: simple, practical method—domain-agnostic, easy to implement, with interpretable compositionality hyperparameters.

- Strength: competitive intrinsic results without manual labels—matches/surpasses strong baselines on UMNSRS Similarity; phrase-based approximations perform well.

- Strength: thorough analyses (concept–phrase and phrase–word distributions, MAP, semantic type clustering) that illuminate model behavior.

- Strength: resource release (dataset and code) improves reproducibility and impact.

- Weakness: limited novelty and somewhat ad hoc objective—approach is a straightforward distant supervision variant and regularizers are under-motivated and risk representation collapse.

- Weakness: naive ambiguity handling—uniform updates to all candidate concepts for polysemous phrases introduce noise and likely hurt concept embeddings.

- Weakness: objective and notation clarity issues (ENS dimensionality, mixed symbols, unclear whether outputs/negatives exist for phrases/concepts), which impede reproducibility.

- Weakness: evaluation scope and fairness—only intrinsic tasks, no downstream application results, possible hyperparameter overfitting, and coverage differences not quantified; lags behind supervised methods on semantic type clustering.

- Weakness: new dataset concerns—low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ≈0.24), partly manual pair selection, limited types, and insufficient characterization.

- Question: clarify ENS dimensionality and whether output/context embeddings and negatives exist for phrases and concepts or only words.

- Question: specify how (w,p,o) observations are constructed from the parallel corpora, including handling of overlapping phrases and window boundaries.

- Question: report coverage statistics for evaluation pairs and sensitivity to the minimum frequency threshold (25).

- Question: did you try simple disambiguation heuristics or soft attention/EM variants, and can an ablation quantify ambiguity’s impact?

- Question: can you provide downstream results (e.g., entity linking or concept normalization) to demonstrate practical utility?

- Comment: fix notation/typos (Cs vs Cp; c vs o; ENS shape likely |W|×d).

- Comment: temper the claim of “highest correlation” on UMNSRS and consider statistical testing for comparisons.

- Comment: compare to retrofitting approaches and explore variants that directly regularize with ontology relations/types as suggested in the Discussion.
