- Proposes a weakly supervised method that jointly embeds ontology concepts, their surface phrases, and words by substituting phrases with unique IDs and training a skip-gram/negative-sampling model with optional β/η regularizers.
- Concept updates are uniformly assigned to all candidate concepts a phrase can denote, without explicit disambiguation.
- Contribution: a simple, scalable distant-supervision recipe for joint concept/phrase/word embeddings requiring no sense-annotated text, demonstrated on UMLS and YAGO.
- Contribution: empirical study showing competitive intrinsic performance vs. annotation-heavy baselines, analyses of compositionality and semantic-type clustering, and release of cui2vec plus a new entity similarity/relatedness dataset.
- Contribution: practical formulation integrating concepts, phrases, and words with tunable compositionality (β, η) and evidence that ontology structure is partially captured.
- Strength: practical, annotation-light approach that scales and works across domains/ontologies, addressing multiword expressions and synonymy limitations of word-only models.
- Strength: competitive results—top correlation on UMNSRS similarity and strong general-domain performance—despite baselines using curated annotations.
- Strength: careful experimental setup with ablations, multiple corpora, hyperparameter tuning, and geometry analyses (concept–phrase proximity, compositionality heterogeneity).
- Strength: resource release (cui2vec and MTurk dataset) fills an entity-focused resource gap.
- Weakness: limited modeling novelty—straightforward adaptation of skip-gram with phrase-ID substitution and simple β/η regularizers.
- Weakness: no disambiguation—uniformly crediting all candidate concepts likely injects noise and may explain weaker performance on small datasets and limited benefit over phrase averages.
- Weakness: evaluation is almost entirely intrinsic, with no extrinsic downstream tasks to validate practical utility; MTurk dataset has low annotator agreement.
- Weakness: technical clarity issues and missing training/preprocessing details (notation inconsistencies, negative sampling/output embedding description, subsampling, overlapping phrase matching).
- Weakness: comparisons and positioning incomplete—missing benchmarks like hyperlink-based relatedness and other KB/text joint embedding baselines.
- Question: What fraction of phrase mentions map to multiple concepts, and how do excluding ambiguous phrases or weighting by priors affect results?
- Question: Did you try context-sensitive disambiguation (e.g., soft attention or EM) over candidate concepts?
- Question: Clarify ENS dimensionality and whether there are separate output embeddings for words/phrases/concepts and the negative sampling distribution used.
- Question: How are overlapping multiword phrase matches resolved, is word subsampling applied, and what is the exact context window behavior when collapsing phrases?
- Question: Can you provide performance curves across β and η and qualitative analysis of when compositionality helps or hurts?
- Question: Can you report at least one extrinsic downstream task (e.g., concept normalization or entity linking) to demonstrate practical gains?
- Question: Were clinical notes de-identified and IRB-approved, and can you provide MTurk annotation guidelines plus per-category agreement and performance?
