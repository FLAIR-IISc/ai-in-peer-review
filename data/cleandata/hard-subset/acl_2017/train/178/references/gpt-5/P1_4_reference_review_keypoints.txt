- Proposes a weakly supervised method to jointly embed ontology concepts, their surface phrases, and words into a shared vector space using only an ontology lexicon and raw text.  
- Adapts skip-gram with negative sampling on parallel untagged and phrase-tagged corpora, collapsing matched phrases to single tokens and updating concept vectors from phrase contexts.  
- Introduces two compositionality control terms (β for words→phrases, η for phrases→concepts) and shares the output/negative-sampling matrix across types.  
- Scales to large corpora and ontologies (e.g., 3.5B tokens, >700k YAGO entities) with simple preprocessing.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on large biomedical similarity/relatedness benchmarks (e.g., UMNSRS Similarity).  
- Provides cross-domain empirical study with systematic hyperparameter tuning and analyses (compositionality, semantic type clustering).  
- Releases a new crowd-sourced entity similarity/relatedness dataset (people/places/organizations) and code/data (cui2vec).  
- Method requires minimal supervision—only ontology surface forms, no manual annotations or KG edges.  
- Thorough comparative evaluation contrasting word/phrase averages vs direct concept vectors and tuning of β/η.  
- Analyses reveal nuanced properties: concepts often near average phrase embeddings, phrase–word proximity is bimodal, and semantic types show clustering differences.  
- Core methodological novelty is limited; approach is close to prior distant supervision and phrase-tagging methods.  
- β/η regularizers and shared output matrix are incremental additions and sometimes tuned to zero, suggesting limited practical impact.  
- Ambiguity handling is naive—updates split uniformly across multiple concept mappings without P(c|p) priors or disambiguation.  
- Does not quantify ambiguity prevalence or analyze its effect, nor compare against simple disambiguation heuristics.  
- Evaluation relies mainly on intrinsic similarity/relatedness tasks; performance lags on smaller high-agreement sets and no downstream tasks are evaluated.  
- Hyperparameter tuning reuses test folds, lacks significance testing/confidence intervals, and some performance differences are small.  
- New Mechanical Turk datasets have low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ=0.24), raising reliability concerns.  
- Some clarity and technical details are underspecified (notation/typos, phrase matching/tokenization, normalization, nested mentions, frequency cutoffs).  
- The decision to share only the output/context matrix across types is unconventional and not deeply analyzed.  
- Requests clarification on ambiguity statistics, effects of dropping/weighting ambiguous phrases, and context-based disambiguation experiments.  
- Asks for controlled β/η ablation studies showing when nonzero values help or hurt alignment.  
- Seeks clarification on ENS dimensions, role, and choices about output vocabulary sharing and parameter tying.  
- Requests detailed preprocessing specifics (normalization, overlap resolution, redirects, frequency cutoff impact).  
- Recommends significance testing, robustness checks across seeds/splits, integration of lightweight ontology structure, downstream evaluations, and thorough release documentation.
