- Proposes a weakly supervised skip-gram adaptation to jointly embed words, surface phrases, and ontology concepts by collapsing phrase mentions and updating concept vectors when representative phrases occur.  
- Adds optional compositionality regularizers (phrase-by-words and concept-by-phrases) and shares a negative-sampling matrix.  
- Evaluated on biomedical (UMLS) and general-domain (YAGO) ontologies across similarity/relatedness, semantic-type clustering, and structural analyses.  
- Releases a new crowd-annotated real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset and a public implementation (cui2vec).  
- Contribution: simple, scalable framework needing only phrase-to-concept dictionaries and raw text, achieving competitive intrinsic performance without manual annotations.  
- Contribution: systematic geometric analyses of the joint space (compositionality, concept–phrase/phrase–word proximity, type clustering).  
- Contribution: a small new similarity/relatedness dataset and publicly released code/resources.  
- Strength: Practical and scalable weak supervision that matches strong baselines on some biomedical intrinsic benchmarks while using far less supervision.  
- Strength: Interpretable joint space analyses that clarify what the model captures about concepts and phrases.  
- Strength: Broad cross-domain training (PubMed, clinical notes, Wikipedia+NYT) showing robustness across UMLS and YAGO.  
- Strength: Transparent hyperparameter studies and ablations showing trade-offs (β, η) and effects of compositional constraints.  
- Strength: Useful community resources (cui2vec and dataset) accompanying the work.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—approach is close to standard skip-gram/distant supervision and prior concept/entity embedding work, with only modest extensions.  
- Weakness: Evaluation is intrinsic-only and hyperparameters are tuned on test sets, lacking extrinsic downstream validation.  
- Weakness: Concept supervision is noisy due to treating ambiguous phrases as uniformly signaling all candidate concepts, with no disambiguation.  
- Weakness: Gains are inconsistent and often underwhelming versus strong baselines (e.g., CHOI, CHIU-30, APPROXPHRco), and semantic-type clustering lags in places.  
- Weakness: Technical and reproducibility gaps (negative sampling details, vocabulary/tokenization/phrase-matching policy, notation issues, regularizer behavior).  
- Question: How sensitive are results to uniformly updating all candidate concepts for ambiguous phrases, and were disambiguation heuristics tested?  
- Question: Are negatives sampled only from words, and was unigram^0.75 or union-vocabulary sampling evaluated to better align modalities?  
- Question: Why use logσ-based regularizers without negatives—did you try contrastive or margin losses, and is there evidence of norm collapse or growth?  
- Question: Why do phrase-based approximations outperform concept embeddings on YAGO—ambiguity, frequency, or coverage issues?  
- Question: Can you provide any extrinsic evaluation (entity linking, concept normalization, retrieval) to demonstrate downstream utility?  
- Request: Clarify phrase matching/tokenization procedure, frequency thresholds, overlapping phrase handling, and whether phrases appear in word contexts.  
- Additional comment: Release annotator-level data and gold splits for the small dataset and report inter-annotator baselines given low IAA.  
- Additional suggestion: Incorporating ontology structure (types/relations/hierarchy) as training signals may improve clustering and downstream performance.  
- Overall: Carefully executed and broadly applicable work with useful resources but modest methodological novelty and some evaluation/reproducibility limitations.
