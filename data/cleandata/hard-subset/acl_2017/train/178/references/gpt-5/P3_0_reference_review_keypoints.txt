- Proposes a weakly supervised skip-gram-style method to jointly embed ontology concepts, surface phrases, and words using phrase occurrences in raw text as distant supervision and two compositionality regularizers (β, η).  
- Instantiated on UMLS and YAGO, trained on large corpora (PubMed, clinical notes, Wikipedia+NYT), with evaluations on biomedical similarity/relatedness sets and a new crowd-sourced entity similarity dataset.  
- Releases code (cui2vec) and a novel entity similarity/relatedness dataset.

- Strength: Scalable, practical weak supervision needing only a lexicon mapping phrases to concept IDs, enabling training on massive corpora without document-level annotations.  
- Strength: Competitive intrinsic biomedical results (top Spearman ρ on UMNSRS Similarity), indicating better capture of taxonomic/definitional similarity than some baselines.  
- Strength: Cross-domain applicability — same training recipe works for UMLS and YAGO, reflecting semantic type structure.  
- Strength: Careful, multifaceted analyses (compositionality, retrieval, clustering) that diagnose where supervision helps.  
- Strength: Resource contribution via code and new datasets.

- Weakness: Limited novelty relative to prior joint text–KB embedding work and does not exploit KB graph/triple structure; compositionality regularizers are simple and often tuned to zero.  
- Weakness: Concept embeddings often add little beyond phrase averages; APPROXPHRco can outperform direct concept embeddings, suggesting limited extra non-lexical knowledge.  
- Weakness: No mechanism to disambiguate polysemous phrases — distant supervision treats all senses equally, introducing label noise with no context-based sense resolution.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning uses adapted splits that risk overfitting intrinsic tests; new dataset has low annotator agreement and lacks comparisons to strong Wikipedia/YAGO entity baselines.  
- Weakness: Ambiguities in objective and training description (parameterization of input/output embeddings, negative sampling, interaction of regularizers with SGNS) and notation issues reduce clarity.

- Question: Clarify parameterization — separate input/output embeddings? Is the output matrix for words only and are negatives sampled from the word vocabulary?  
- Question: How frequent is phrase–concept ambiguity in UMLS/YAGO after filtering, and what is performance on unambiguous forms or when weighting by concept priors?  
- Question: Why does APPROXPHRco outperform CONCEPTco on general entities — are concepts with many aliases degraded by noisy averaging?  
- Question: What coverage/OOV rates occur for each evaluation set and how are OOV cases handled; are reported correlations computed on intersections or per-system coverage?  
- Question: Can you compare to common entity embedding baselines (RDF2Vec, TransE/ComplEx, hyperlink-based vectors) on the new datasets?  
- Question: Since β, η often best at zero, provide ablations showing when regularizers help and whether alternative formulations stabilize training.  
- Question: Provide runtime/memory footprints for largest settings and sensitivity to min-frequency threshold (e.g., 25).

- Suggestion: Incorporate ontology structure (hierarchies/relations) as additional signals (retrofitting, graph contrastive losses) to inject non-lexical knowledge.  
- Suggestion: Explore latent sense assignment or multi-instance learning to mitigate distant supervision noise for ambiguous phrases.  
- Suggestion: Add extrinsic evaluations (entity linking, concept normalization, coding similarity) and avoid tuning on test-only intrinsic benchmarks.
