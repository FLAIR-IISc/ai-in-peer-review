Contributions:
- Scalable weakly supervised method to jointly embed ontology concepts, surface phrases, and words without labeled mentions or KB triples.
- Empirical study across multiple corpora and ontologies with analyses of compositionality and semantic type clustering.
- Release of a new crowdsourced similarity/relatedness dataset for real-world entities.

Strengths:
- Practical, annotation-light approach that scales to large ontologies and corpora, useful where labeled mentions are scarce.
- Joint embedding space with interpretable compositionality controls (β, η) and analyses linking geometry to compositionality.
- Competitive intrinsic performance on biomedical similarity benchmarks and strong results on the new general-domain entity datasets.
- Valuable resource contributions (code, cui2vec implementation, dataset) and systematic hyperparameter analysis.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty relative to prior joint embedding work and only modest empirical gains over strong baselines.
- Naive handling of ambiguous phrases via uniform updates across candidate concepts likely introduces noise and limits performance.
- Heavy reliance on intrinsic similarity/relatedness evaluations with tuning on test pairs and no downstream task validation.
- New entity dataset shows low inter-annotator agreement (Fleiss’ κ≈0.24) and limited validation of quality.
- Some clarity and technical detail gaps (notation typos, under-explained negative sampling, incomplete phrase-matching specifics).

Questions for authors:
- Did you try context-sensitive weighting (e.g., attention or softmax) over candidate concepts instead of uniform updates, and what were the results or trade-offs?
- Why use a negative sampling matrix restricted to words (|W|×k) rather than a shared output space over words/phrases/concepts, and did you try sampling negatives from the union?
- How are overlapping/nested phrase matches resolved, is matching case-normalized or lemmatized, and what fraction of mentions are ambiguous (|Cp|>1)?
- Can you provide results with a strictly held-out dev/test split or report performance variability across folds to avoid tuning on test pairs?
- Will you release the learned embeddings (UMLS/YAGO), code, and dataset, and what replication constraints exist for the clinical corpus (privacy/IRB)?
- Do you have downstream task evaluations (e.g., entity linking, clinical concept normalization) to demonstrate practical utility over word-only models?

Overall assessment:
- Originality: moderate; soundness: generally sound but limited by ambiguity handling; significance: moderate with resource value; clarity: good but some technical rough edges.
- Recommendation: lean toward weak reject unless ambiguity resolution is improved and stronger downstream/cleaner evaluation evidence is provided.
