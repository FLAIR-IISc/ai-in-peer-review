- Proposal: Weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using distant supervision from ontologies and a new dataset for real-world entity similarity.

- Strength: Addresses a meaningful gap by arguing for concept-level embeddings when words/phrases are not atomic semantic units.

- Strength: Introduces a novel dataset with human judgments for similarity/relatedness of real-world entities (people, places, organizations).

- Strength: Strong empirical results on biomedical concept similarity tasks (UMNSRS), outperforming prior concept embedding baselines there.

- Weakness: Novelty is limited—approach is a modest modification of prior work and the “novel” claim is overstated.

- Weakness: Major methodological flaw—test set was used for hyperparameter tuning (adapted 5-fold CV), risking overfitting and invalidating reported test metrics.

- Weakness: Core claim weakened because phrase-based approximation (PHRASEco) outperforms direct concept embeddings (CONCEPTco) on real-world entity datasets.

- Weakness: Insufficient evidence that embeddings capture semantic structure—semantic type clustering MAP scores are very low (~0.1–0.2).

- Weakness: Limited analysis of important limitations, e.g., performance on concepts with few phrases, polysemy, and ontology mismatches (UMLS vs YAGO).

- Question: Please explain why direct concept embeddings underperform compared to phrase-based approximations (discrepancy with the paper’s claims).

- Question: Provide results using a proper validation set for hyperparameter selection instead of using the test set.

- Question: Reconcile the claim that ontology structure is reflected in embeddings with the low MAP clustering scores reported.

- Overall: Paper tackles a relevant problem and contributes a useful dataset, but its overstated novelty, flawed evaluation, and weak core results require substantial revision before acceptance.
