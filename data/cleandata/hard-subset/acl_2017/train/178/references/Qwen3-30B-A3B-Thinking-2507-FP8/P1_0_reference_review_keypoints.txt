- Weakly-supervised method that uses ontology phrase-concept mappings to train joint embeddings without human concept annotations.
- New dataset of 688 real-world entity pairs for similarity and relatedness evaluation.
- Jointly trains concept, phrase, and word embeddings using a modified skip-gram with negative sampling and reports competitive results.

- Truly weakly-supervised and scalable across domains with existing ontologies.
- Novel real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset fills an important gap.
- Detailed analysis showing embeddings preserve ontological relationships (concepts ↔ representative phrases, phrases ↔ words).
- Implementation and dataset (cui2vec) released, improving reproducibility.
- Validated on both biomedical (UMLS) and real-world (YAGO) domains.

- Underperforms on biomedical benchmarks compared to methods using human annotations (e.g., CHOI).
- Evaluation flaw: hyperparameters tuned on the test set despite using cross-validation.
- Insufficient empirical comparison and analysis relative to Wang et al. (2014a).
- Limited analysis of the quality and potential errors in ontology phrase-concept mappings.
- Sparse analysis of compositionality hyperparameters (β and η) and their domain-dependent effects.

- Please provide statistics and quality assessment of the phrase-concept mappings (mappings per concept, accuracy).
- Explain why CHOI outperforms on biomedical tasks and how weak supervision could be improved to close the gap.
- Release results using proper train/test splits without test-set hyperparameter tuning.
- Clarify how ambiguous phrases mapping to multiple concepts are handled and how this compares to explicit WSD approaches.
- Give concrete examples showing how the embedding space reflects ontology structure beyond the general observations.

- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to address evaluation methodology, performance gaps, and additional analyses.
