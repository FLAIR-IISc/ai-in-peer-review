- Novel dataset for real-world entity similarity/relatedness created and publicly released, filling a gap in resources for people/places/organizations.  
- Weakly-supervised joint embedding method leverages ontology mappings (UMLS, YAGO) as distant supervision to train concept/phrase/word embeddings without human annotation.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across biomedical and general-domain tasks, including compositionality analysis and semantic type clustering.  
- Major strength: the novel large-scale dataset with detailed collection/annotation methodology is a valuable community resource.  
- Major strength: the weakly-supervised method achieves competitive performance to human-annotated baselines on large biomedical datasets (UMNSRS).  
- Major strength: multi-faceted evaluation (similarity/relatedness, compositionality, clustering) offers deeper analysis than prior work.  
- Weakness: core method novelty is limited; similar ontology-based distant supervision approaches exist and modifications are incremental.  
- Weakness: inconsistent performance claims—method is outperformed by CHOI on smaller Mayo SRS datasets without adequate explanation.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning appears to use evaluation datasets (5-fold CV), risking data leakage and inflated results.  
- Weakness: joint embedding claim is questionable since concept embeddings seem largely derived from phrase/word embeddings and add little over simple averages.  
- Question: why does the method underperform CHOI on smaller datasets despite strong results on larger datasets?  
- Question: how did you prevent data leakage during hyperparameter tuning on evaluation datasets, and can you show results unaffected by this?  
- Question: why is joint embedding necessary when APPROXPHRco (phrase averaging) often outperforms CONCEPTco?  
- Overall recommendation: borderline acceptance with major revisions—novel dataset is valuable, but method novelty, evaluation practices, and performance claims need clarification.
