- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method using ontology mappings (UMLS, YAGO) to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words by replacing phrases with unique IDs and training a modified skip-gram; evaluated on biomedical tasks and a new entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution 1: Novel weakly-supervised approach for concept embedding that requires no human annotations.

- Contribution 2: New dataset for evaluating similarity/relatedness of real-world entities (people, places, organizations).

- Contribution 3: Demonstrates competitive performance with methods that require human annotations.

- Strength 1: Clever, scalable use of ontology mappings as distant supervision to avoid costly human concept annotation.

- Strength 2: Well-constructed, novel evaluation dataset with solid crowdsourcing methodology and analysis.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple corpora and ontologies with useful ablation studies and baseline comparisons.

- Weakness 1: The claimed benefit of joint embedding is questionable because simple phrase averaging (APPROXPHRco) performs nearly as well as the full model (CONCEPTco).

- Weakness 2: Comparisons to human-annotated baselines (e.g., CHOI) are unfair and potentially misleading given richer supervision available to those baselines.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient analysis of ontology mismatch (UMLS abstract concepts vs YAGO concrete entities) and its impact on performance.

- Question 1: Why does joint embedding meaningfully outperform phrase averaging given marginal score differences?

- Question 2: Can you compare against baselines that use only ontology mappings (e.g., Wang et al., 2014a) rather than human-annotated methods?

- Question 3: Please provide a performance breakdown for abstract concepts versus concrete entities to assess the ontology mismatch effect.

- Overall assessment: Practical and valuable contributions (especially the dataset), but the novelty of joint embedding is overstated and comparisons need clarification; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
