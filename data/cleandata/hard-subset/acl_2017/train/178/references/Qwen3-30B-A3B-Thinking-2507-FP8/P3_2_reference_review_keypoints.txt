- Summary: Proposes weakly-supervised joint embeddings of concepts, phrases, and words using ontology mappings (UMLS, YAGO) and a modified skip-gram with two compositionality hyperparameters (β, η); evaluated on biomedical similarity/relatedness and a novel real-world entity dataset.

- Contribution: A weakly-supervised method for joint embedding that avoids human concept annotations.

- Contribution: A new human-annotated dataset for real-world entity similarity and relatedness (688 pairs each).

- Contribution: Empirical demonstration that weakly-supervised concept embeddings can be competitive with supervised methods on intrinsic similarity tasks.

- Strength: Novel dataset creation addresses a gap by providing entity-level similarity/relatedness judgments with validated collection procedures.

- Strength: Shows feasibility of ontology-based weak supervision achieving strong results on larger biomedical similarity tasks without human-labeled concepts.

- Strength: Clearly articulates the many-to-many mapping problem between concepts and textual forms and uses ontology mappings to address it.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—using ontology mappings as distant supervision is similar to prior work (e.g., Mintz et al., Wang et al.), with β and η being relatively minor extensions.

- Weakness: Questionable evaluation—hyperparameters tuned on the test set via cross-validation and no extrinsic downstream task evaluations to support practical utility.

- Weakness: Lack of theoretical justification for the β and η hyperparameters and ad-hoc tuning without guidance for general settings.

- Weakness: Confusing terminology—"concept" is used inconsistently to mean both canonical concepts and specific textual forms, contradicting domain conventions.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—focuses on biomedical and one general-domain dataset where phrase approximations sometimes outperform concept embeddings.

- Question: How specifically does this method differ from Wang et al. (2014a) which also jointly embeds entities and words using KB structure?

- Question: Why do phrase embeddings outperform concept embeddings on the real-world entity dataset, and how does that align with claims about concepts and phrases being close in embedding space?

- Question: Given intrinsic tasks’ weak correlation with downstream performance, which downstream NLP tasks do you expect gains on and have you evaluated any?

- Overall assessment: Valuable dataset and a promising weakly-supervised direction, but limited methodological novelty, problematic evaluation practices, and unclear theoretical motivation weaken the paper’s impact.
