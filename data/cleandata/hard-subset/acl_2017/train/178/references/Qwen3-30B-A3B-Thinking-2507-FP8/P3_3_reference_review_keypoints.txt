- Summary: Proposes a weakly-supervised method that replaces phrases with ontology-backed concept IDs and trains joint concept/phrase/word embeddings via a modified skip-gram with negative sampling, introducing β and η to control compositionality; evaluated on biomedical tasks and a new real-world entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution: Introduces a weakly-supervised concept embedding method needing no extra human annotations beyond ontology mappings.

- Contribution: Provides a new crowdsourced dataset of real-world entity similarity/relatedness (688 pairs each for similarity and relatedness).

- Contribution: Shows competitive performance on biomedical concept similarity/relatedness versus methods that use human annotations.

- Strength: Novel and scalable use of ontology mappings as distant supervision to handle lexical variability of concepts.

- Strength: The new real-world entity similarity/relatedness dataset fills an important gap and benefits future research.

- Strength: Achieves top correlation on UMNSRS similarity and competitive relatedness results, demonstrating weak supervision can approach supervised methods.

- Weakness: Major methodological flaw—hyperparameter tuning was performed on evaluation datasets (adapted 5-fold CV), violating standard evaluation and potentially invalidating comparisons.

- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to Wang et al. (2014a); lacks clear analysis of practical differences and advantages over that KB-entity joint embedding work.

- Weakness: No analysis of the impact of noisy or incorrect ontology mappings on embedding quality and robustness.

- Weakness: Misleading comparison with CHOI—paper downplays CHOI's substantial human annotation cost and doesn't quantify annotation effort.

- Weakness: Compositionality analysis (Section 5.4) is confusing and poorly explained, with unclear interpretation of figures and MAP results.

- Question: How were incorrect or incomplete ontology mappings handled, and what is their empirical impact on embeddings?

- Question: Clarify the true annotation effort required given that ontologies like UMLS/YAGO involve human curation, and compare this to prior methods like CHOI or DEVINE.

- Question: Given tuning on test data, did you re-evaluate with proper train/validation/test splits to confirm results aren't overfit?

- Overall assessment: Valuable contribution with a promising weakly-supervised method and a useful dataset, but the hyperparameter-tuning-on-test flaw and insufficient comparative/robustness analyses must be addressed before acceptance.
