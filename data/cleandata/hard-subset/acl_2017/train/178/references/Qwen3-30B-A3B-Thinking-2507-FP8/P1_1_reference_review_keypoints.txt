- Proposal: weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontology mappings as distant supervision; evaluated on UMLS (biomedical) and YAGO (real-world entities) and a new entity similarity dataset.

- Contribution 1: novel weakly-supervised concept embedding approach avoiding human concept annotations.

- Contribution 2: new dataset of similarity and relatedness judgments for real-world entities (people, places, organizations).

- Contribution 3: empirical evidence that concept embeddings can be learned without direct human annotation.

- Strength: tackles non-compositional concept forms (e.g., "the Big Apple") with a practical ontology-based supervision signal.

- Strength: the new dataset fills a gap for evaluating embeddings of general real-world entities; annotated with inter-annotator agreement reported.

- Strength: strong empirical results on biomedical benchmarks (UMNSRS) and insightful compositionality analyses.

- Weakness: CONCEPT embeddings underperform simple phrase embeddings (PHRASEco > CONCEPTco) on the real-world dataset, undermining the main claimed benefit.

- Weakness: comparisons to prior work are inconsistent or unfair because baselines use different concept identifier sets (ICD9, MeSH vs UMLS CUIs).

- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning via 5-fold cross-validation on small evaluation datasets risks overfitting and potential data leakage; methodology unclear.

- Weakness: insufficient analysis of limitations (handling polysemy, concepts without clear textual forms, poor performance on certain semantic types like "sign or symptom").

- Question 1: why do phrase embeddings outperform jointly trained concept embeddings and what additional value do concept embeddings provide?

- Question 2: how does the model handle ambiguous phrases that map to multiple concepts (e.g., "diabetes") and its impact on embedding quality?

- Question 3: what non-lexical concept information is missing from the model and how might it be captured?

- Recommendation: reject as-is; would reconsider after major revisions addressing comparative validity, explanation for PHRASEco vs CONCEPTco, evaluation methodology, and limitations.
