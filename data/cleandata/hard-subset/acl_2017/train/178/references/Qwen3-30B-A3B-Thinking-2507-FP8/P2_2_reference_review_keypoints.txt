- Proposes a weakly‑supervised method that jointly embeds concepts, phrases, and words using distant supervision from ontologies (UMLS, YAGO) by replacing phrases with IDs and training a modified skip‑gram model.
- Main contributions: (1) weakly‑supervised joint embeddings without human annotations, (2) a new dataset for similarity/relatedness of real‑world entities, (3) demonstration that concept embeddings can match results of annotation‑dependent methods.
- Strength: introduces a novel, carefully collected dataset for evaluating similarity/relatedness of people, places, and organizations, useful for future work.
- Strength: competitive/best correlation on larger biomedical UMNSRS dataset, showing effectiveness where human annotation is costly.
- Strength: clear theoretical grounding, practical implementation, and public release of code/dataset (cui2vec) aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—largely an adaptation of prior distant‑supervision and skip‑gram approaches with parameter tweaks.
- Weakness: serious evaluation flaw—hyperparameters were tuned on the test/evaluation data, risking over-optimistic and non‑generalizable results.
- Weakness: inconsistent empirical performance—strong on larger UMNSRS but outperformed on smaller biomedical sets (MayoSRS, Mini Mayo SRS) without adequate explanation.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of what semantic properties are learned (e.g., hierarchy, polysemy) and weak evidence that ontology structure is reflected in embeddings.
- Question: how do you justify comparisons to baselines that use additional human‑annotated data (e.g., ICD9) when your method does not?
- Question: how will you correct the hyperparameter tuning on test data issue (proper train/validation/test split) in a revision?
- Question: can you provide stronger, specific evidence that embeddings capture ontology structure beyond the provided visualizations?
- Overall recommendation: useful dataset but core method lacks novelty and has flawed evaluation—recommend rejection pending major revisions, willing to reconsider after fixes.
