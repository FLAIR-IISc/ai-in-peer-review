- Proposes a weakly-supervised method to jointly embed concepts, phrases, and words using ontology-structured knowledge (UMLS, YAGO) as distant supervision.  
- Trains concept embeddings from phrases linked to concepts without requiring human annotations for concepts.  
- Introduces a new dataset of similarity/relatedness judgments for real-world entities (people, places, organizations).  
- Main claimed contributions: (1) weakly-supervised concept embedding, (2) novel real-world entity similarity dataset, (3) competitive performance on large biomedical similarity tasks.  

- Strength: Clever distant-supervision approach addresses the need to avoid costly manual concept annotation, useful for biomedical and large-domain settings.  
- Strength: The new dataset fills a gap for real-world entity similarity, with careful collection, validation questions, and inter-annotator agreement.  
- Strength: Achieves highest correlation on larger biomedical datasets (UMNSRS) for similarity tasks, showing scalability potential.  

- Weakness: Insufficiently differentiated from prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2014a); paper does not clearly argue how phrase-based supervision is meaningfully novel or superior.  
- Weakness: Performance gains are marginal overall, is outperformed by CHOI on smaller MayoSRS datasets, and phrase-based approximation outperforming direct concept embeddings undermines core claims.  
- Weakness: Serious methodological flaw—appears to use the test set for hyperparameter tuning despite claiming 5-fold CV, risking optimistic/invalid evaluation.  
- Weakness: Overstates that ontology structure is preserved in embeddings; presented figures provide weak or contradictory evidence.  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of when the method is most effective; hyperparameter differences across corpora are unexplained and provide no usage guidance.  

- Question: Clarify how this method specifically differs from Wang et al. (2014a) and demonstrate why it is better.  
- Question: Explain why the method is outperformed on smaller datasets but better on larger ones and when it should be preferred.  
- Question: Clarify hyperparameter tuning procedure and how test-set leakage was prevented.  
- Question: Provide additional analysis or examples to substantiate the claim that ontology structure is reflected in the embeddings.  

- Overall: Modest methodological contribution with a valuable dataset, but limited novelty, marginal empirical gains, and a serious evaluation flaw; needs stronger comparisons, clearer differentiation from prior work, and proper evaluation.
