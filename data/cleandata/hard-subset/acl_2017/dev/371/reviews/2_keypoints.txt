- Strength: Proposes a novel phrasal RNN (pRNN) architecture that generates phrase-level subnetworks.
- Unclear whether a maximum phrase length is imposed; this must be explicitly stated.
- If phrase length equals sentence length, pRNN may reduce to a convolutional-RNN-like model.
- If phrase length is limited, phrases act like token n-grams/sliding windows and parameters may be effectively disabled — this should be analyzed as an intrinsic evaluation.
- Using attention at the phrasal level without pruning may be problematic; greedy pruning described is not fully convincing.
- Precomputing phrasal attention for a fixed train set may not scale or generalize at inference on new data.
- Language modeling experiments use suboptimal datasets; larger corpora (e.g., CommonCrawl or enwiki8) would be more appropriate.
- PTB evaluation is unfair because the model was trained on a larger corpus (FBIS), biasing perplexity comparisons.
- Prior work cited in tables needs proper references and clarification whether they used the same training data.
- The GRU variant of pRNNv is missing from the FBIS results and should be reported.
- Results are presented as tables without sufficient explanation of discrepancies (e.g., LSTM perplexity differences).
- Table 4's claims that pRNN outperforms PBSMT and Enc-Dec are overstated; performance varies across test sets and reporting is unclear.
- Clarify whether "Test Avg." in Table 4 is micro-average or macro-average.
- Report statistical significance (e.g., using multeval) for BLEU improvements over baselines.
- Need intrinsic evaluation showing that pRNN phrases are more coherent than vanilla LSTM/RNN beyond BLEU/perplexity.
- Unclear whether the baseline (groundhog) includes attention; this must be stated and figure 5 adjusted accordingly.
- Comparing pRNN to an enc-dec baseline without attention is unfair; clarify additive effects with attention-enabled enc-dec.
- Suggest simpler alternative: average-pooling over pyramid RNNs as a baseline or ablation.
- Figure 2 is redundant given Figure 1; Figures 3 and 4 could be combined to save space.
- Avoid lengthy figure/table captions; move verbose descriptions into the main text for Figures 3–5 and Table 4.
- Minor formatting: reduce spacing around some equations.
