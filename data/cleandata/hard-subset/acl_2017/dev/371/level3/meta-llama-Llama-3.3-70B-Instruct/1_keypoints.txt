- Summary: Proposes a pyramid encoder to learn hierarchical phrasal representations for RNN-based language models and neural machine translation.

- Contribution — phrasal representation learning: Introduces a pyramid encoder as a new method for learning phrase embeddings.

- Contribution — improved language modeling: Claims improved LM performance, better capturing long-range dependencies and contextual information.

- Contribution — neural machine translation: Applies the approach to NMT, reporting improved translation quality and better handling of OOV words.

- Strength: Novel approach that advances phrasal representation learning.

- Strength: Demonstrated empirical improvements for both language modeling and NMT.

- Strength: Well-motivated with clear discussion of limitations of existing methods and claimed benefits.

- Weakness: Paper is difficult to follow; the methodology and descriptions are unclear.

- Weakness: Equations 1 and 2 are not clearly explained or connected to the model’s use.

- Weakness: Unclear rationale for using GRU in the pyramid encoder and LSTM in the sequential component.

- Weakness: The “simplified” GRU is insufficiently described, with no clarity on its use or scalability to large datasets.

- Weakness: Table 4 is confusing because the distinction between RNNsearch (groundhog) and RNNsearch (baseline) is not stated.

- Question: Request clearer explanation of the proposed approach and overall methodology.

- Question: Ask what changes the authors will make to improve clarity and readability.

- Question: Request justification and details on the choice and roles of GRU vs. LSTM.

- Question: Ask for clarification of the purpose and usage of Equations 1 and 2.

- Question: Request details on the simplified GRU and its performance on large datasets.
