- Introduces pRNN: parallel RNN pyramid that represents sentential history as variable-length phrase candidates and uses phrasal-level soft attention to predict next words.
- Claims substantial perplexity improvements on PTB and FBIS and competitive Chinese-English MT BLEU vs PBSMT and RNNsearch.

- Strength: Novel architecture using parallel RNNs to model phrases of varying lengths as an alternative to deeper RNN stacks.
- Strength: Learns phrase structure without external labels or alignment models, improving scalability and applicability.
- Strength: Reports empirical gains (over ~10 perplexity points and BLEU improvements) over strong baselines.

- Weakness: Unclear whether there is a phrase-length limit in pRNN and how that affects complexity and tractability.
- Weakness: Scalability and computational cost of the phrasal-level attention during inference on large datasets is not addressed.
- Weakness: Dataset choices are problematic—using FBIS may bias perplexity evaluation on PTB; more appropriate corpora should be considered.
- Weakness: GRU variant of pRNN was not evaluated on FBIS, leaving incomplete empirical comparisons.
- Weakness: Results section lacks clarity—perplexity/BLEU explanations, inconsistencies between Table 4 and Section 4.3, and unclear test-score averaging.
- Weakness: No significance testing reported for BLEU improvements, so robustness of gains is uncertain.
- Weakness: Baseline comparisons are unclear and potentially unfair because pRNN uses multi-layer phrasal attention while some baselines lack attention.
- Weakness: BLEU alone is insufficient to assess phrase coherence; additional intrinsic evaluations (phrase-level coherence/structural accuracy) are needed.

- Question: Is there a limit on phrase length in pRNN, and how does it affect complexity and performance?
- Question: How does the phrasal-level attention scale during inference on larger datasets and what are the bottlenecks?
- Question: Why was the GRU pRNN not evaluated on FBIS—can those results be provided?
- Question: How were test scores in Table 4 averaged and why do they conflict with Section 4.3?
- Question: Can the authors provide statistical significance testing for BLEU improvements?

- Comment: Figures 2 and 3 are redundant and could be combined to free space for clearer results explanation.
- Comment: Figure and table captions are overly verbose and should be integrated into the main text for readability.
- Comment: Equation spacing can be tightened to improve formatting and save space.

- Recommendation: Weak reject due to technical and empirical clarity issues; acceptance contingent on authors addressing evaluation, baseline comparisons, and clarity concerns.
