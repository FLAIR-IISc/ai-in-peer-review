- Contribution: Introduces a novel phrasal RNN (pRNN) architecture for sequence-to-sequence generation.
- Unclear phrase length: The paper does not specify a limit on phrase length, which affects complexity and model interpretation.
- Relation to conv-RNNs: If phrase length equals sentence length, pRNN could be simplified to a convolutional RNN variant.
- Tractability with limits: Setting phrase-length limits would allow n-gram/token evaluations and pyramid-encoder sliding windows.
- Attention/pruning concerns: Using attention without effective pruning at the phrasal level may not scale; greedy pruning’s efficacy is unclear.
- Training vs inference mismatch: Pre-computing phrasal attention during training may be infeasible at inference on new data.
- Dataset choice for LM: PTB/FBIS setup is suboptimal; larger corpora like Common Crawl or enwiki8 would be more appropriate for language modeling.
- Unfair PTB evaluation: Training on FBIS then testing on PTB biases perplexity comparison and makes Table 2 unfair.
- Missing citations/clarity: Authors must cite prior work properly and state whether baselines used the same training data.
- Results explanation lacking: Result tables lack discussion explaining discrepancies (e.g., LSTM perplexity differences) and interpretations.
- Table 4 / Section 4.3 mismatch: Claims about pRNN outperforming PBSMT/Enc-Dec need clarification; specify whether averages are micro or macro.
- Need qualitative phrase analysis: BLEU alone is insufficient—must show pRNN produces more coherent phrases than vanilla LSTM/RNN with example analyses.
- Baseline attention ambiguity: It’s unclear whether the Groundhog baseline includes attention; this must be clarified or figure updated.
- Simpler alternatives: Suggests average pooling over pyramid RNNs as a simpler way to obtain phrasal behavior.
- Presentation/minor issues: Figures are redundant or could be combined, captions are verbose, and spacing/formatting of equations could be tightened.
