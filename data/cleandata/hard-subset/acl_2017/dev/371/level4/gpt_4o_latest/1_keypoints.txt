- Strength: The idea of incorporating phrasal information into RNN-based language models and NMT is intriguing.
- Weakness: The paper is hard to follow and needs proofreading by a native English speaker for clarity.
- Weakness: The evaluation of the proposed approach has notable shortcomings.
- Question: Equations 1–2 define a fixed-length phrase embedding that is not used in the model—what is the purpose of that description?
- Question: Why use a GRU for the Pyramid structure and an LSTM for the sequential component, and is their combination driving the improvements?
- Question: What is the "simplified" GRU, why does it perform better, and how does it scale to larger datasets?
- Question: In Table 4, what is the difference between RNNsearch (groundhog) and RNNsearch (baseline)?
- Question: Why focus only on ending phrases rather than also modeling starting phrases?
- Question: Was the pyramid encoder used exclusively, and how does it perform in isolation for a fairer comparison?
- Question: Why was RNNsearch run multiple times while PBNMT was run only once?
- Question: What is the purpose and role of Section 5.2?
