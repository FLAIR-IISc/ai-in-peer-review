- Strength: Introduces a novel phrasal RNN (pRNN) architecture that generates subnetworks for phrases.

Technical weaknesses
- Unclear whether a maximum phrase length is imposed; this limit must be stated because it substantially affects the model design.
- If a phrase-length limit exists, phrases reduce to token n-grams and a sliding-window "pyramid encoder" view, which should be discussed and could simplify the model.
- Attention at the phrasal level may not scale without pruning; training-time fixed phrase pairs could allow precomputation but inference on new data may be problematic.

Empirical weaknesses
- Language modeling datasets are suboptimal; larger or more standard corpora (e.g., Common Crawl, enwiki8) would be more appropriate.
- PTB evaluation is unfair because the model was trained on a larger FBIS corpus; reported LSTM perplexity advantages may stem from training data differences.
- Prior work citations and training-set comparisons are missing or unclear; the GRU variant is not reported for FBIS in Table 3 and should be included.
- Result tables lack interpretation: explain why your LSTM outperforms prior numbers, reconcile Table 4 with Section 4.3 claims, and clarify that pRNN only outperforms baselines on averaged scores.
- Clarify whether "Test Avg." is micro- or macro-average and report statistical significance of BLEU improvements (e.g., using multeval).

General discussion
- Need qualitative analysis showing pRNN-generated phrases are more coherent than vanilla LSTM/RNN outputs; BLEU alone is insufficient.
- Clarify whether the baseline (GroundHog) includes attention; if not, remove attention from the enc-dec baseline or state it explicitly to ensure fair comparison.
- Suggest a simpler phrasal-RNN implementation alternative: average pooling over pyramid RNN phrase representations.

Minor issues
- Figure 2 seems redundant and could be removed.
- Combine Figures 3 and 4 to better illustrate the pyramid structure and free space for more result explanation.
- Move verbose figure/table captions into the main text; keep captions concise.
- Reduce excessive spacing between equations for tighter presentation.
