- Paper introduces Phrasal RNNs (pRNNs): a pyramid of parallel RNNs representing all possible phrases, combined via soft attention for language modeling and MT.
- Claims pRNNs capture richer structural information than sequential RNNs and improve performance on LM and MT tasks.
- Empirical gains reported: significant perplexity reductions on Penn Treebank and FBIS and higher BLEU in Chinese-English MT vs LSTMs and RNNsearch.
- Contribution: explicit modeling of phrases of varying lengths for more structured, interpretable representations.
- Contribution: novel RNN pyramid emphasizing parallel RNN sequences rather than deeper stacked layers.
- Contribution: demonstrated performance improvements in perplexity and BLEU.
- Strength: innovative framework addressing limitations of sequential RNNs and latent hierarchical structure.
- Strength: strong empirical results (e.g., >10-point perplexity reduction on datasets).
- Strength: unsupervised structure learning without reliance on labeled data or external models.
- Strength: integration of a soft attention mechanism to select and combine phrase representations.
- Weakness: high computational complexity and unclear scalability to long sentences and large datasets.
- Weakness: limited evaluation of the quality/interpretability of the generated "phrase forest" versus supervised parsers.
- Weakness: simple attention mechanism may struggle with the large number of complex phrase candidates.
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns due to insufficient details on hyperparameters and computational requirements.
- Questions: how does pRNN computational cost compare to traditional RNNs/LSTMs for long sequences and large datasets?
- Questions: has the phrase forest quality been evaluated against supervised parsing models?
- Questions: could more advanced attention mechanisms (e.g., multi-head attention) improve handling of complex candidates?
- Conclusion: promising and novel approach with strong results, but acceptance contingent on clarifying computational trade-offs, scalability, and interpretability.
