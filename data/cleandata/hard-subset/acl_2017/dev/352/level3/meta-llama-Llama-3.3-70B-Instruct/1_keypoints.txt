- Compares three models linking visual object representations to lexical distributional vectors and finds that combining them (SIM-WAP) improves naming, with cross-modal similarity mapping strong in zero-shot settings.
- Introduces SIM-WAP, a new model that combines cross-modal mapping and individual word classifiers.
- Evaluates models on a challenging zero-shot naming task requiring generalization to unseen object names.
- Provides detailed analysis of each model’s strengths and weaknesses, including taxonomic capture and singular/plural performance.
- SIM-WAP shows improved performance on the zero-shot naming task, especially on the full vocabulary.
- Combining models yields complementary benefits and better overall performance.
- Paper offers a thorough analysis of model behaviors to inform future research.
- Vocabulary size is relatively small, which may limit the generality of conclusions.
- The models do not incorporate contextual information during object naming.
- Further evaluation is needed to establish model effectiveness across different settings and limitations.
- How will the authors address the small vocabulary limitation in future work?
- Can the authors provide more details on how SIM-WAP captures taxonomic aspects of object names?
- How could the models be improved to capture contextual information during object naming?
