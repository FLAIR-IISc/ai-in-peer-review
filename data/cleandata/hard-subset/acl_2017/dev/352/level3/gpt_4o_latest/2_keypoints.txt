- Introduces ASP-MTL: an adversarial multi-task model extending binary adversarial training to multi-class to enforce task-invariant shared representations.  
- Adds orthogonality constraints to reduce redundancy and separate shared vs. task-specific feature spaces.  
- Shows shared representations transfer to new tasks, enabling reuse as off-the-shelf knowledge.  
- Clear and effective methodology addressing contamination of shared spaces via adversarial training and orthogonality.  
- Demonstrates consistent performance gains over single-task and prior multi-task baselines across 16 text classification tasks.  
- Validates transferability through task-level cross-validation experiments.  
- Provides qualitative analysis (neuron activation visualizations) that supports quantitative findings.  
- Supplies implementation details and hyperparameters to aid reproducibility.  
- Lacks statistical significance testing for reported performance improvements, weakening robustness of comparisons.  
- Does not include experimental results for the claimed semi-supervised learning potential.  
- Contains minor typographic and presentation issues, including inconsistent figure colors.  
- Limited discussion connecting adversarial training to broader regularization literature and theory.  
- Requests statistical significance testing between SP-MTL and ASP-MTL.  
- Asks for experimental validation of semi-supervised learning capabilities.  
- Inquires about sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., λ and γ).  
- Concludes the paper is a novel, effective contribution with strong empirical results but would be strengthened by statistical tests and semi-supervised experiments.
