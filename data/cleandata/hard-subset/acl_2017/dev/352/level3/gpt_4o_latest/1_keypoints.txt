- Proposes adversarial training to make shared representations task-invariant and remove task-specific features.  
- Introduces orthogonality constraints to reduce redundancy between shared and private feature spaces.  
- Shows the shared feature extractor can be used as an off-the-shelf module for transfer to unseen tasks.  
- Novel combination of adversarial loss and orthogonality provides a compelling solution to feature contamination in MTL.  
- Extensive experiments on 16 diverse text-classification tasks show consistent improvements over SP-MTL and FS-MTL.  
- Demonstrates practical transfer learning potential of the shared layer to new tasks.  
- Includes visualizations and qualitative analyses (neuron behavior, feature patterns) that help explain quantitative gains.  
- Description of the adversarial training setup is unclear; effectiveness in removing task-specific info needs more detail or an ablation study.  
- Paper lacks clear motivation and logical flow on why shared-private models fail and how the proposed methods address those failures.  
- Limited comparison scope: no evaluation against hierarchical MTL or other recent state-of-the-art approaches.  
- Evaluation omits best-known results for tasks, making absolute performance hard to judge.  
- Related work discussion is sparse, particularly missing hierarchical MTL and task-similarity literature.  
- Request for ablation demonstrating the isolated effect of adversarial loss on shared representations.  
- Ask for comparisons to hierarchical MTL methods that explicitly model task similarity.  
- Request clarification of hyperparameter choices for adversarial loss and orthogonality constraints and their sensitivity.  
- Overall: promising and empirically strong approach that needs clearer exposition, broader comparisons, and fuller related-work coverage to solidify its contribution.
