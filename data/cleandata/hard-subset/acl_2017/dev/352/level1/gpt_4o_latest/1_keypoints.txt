- Proposes adversarial multi-task learning to separate shared and task-specific feature spaces for text classification.  
- Introduces adversarial training to purify shared features and prevent task-specific contamination.  
- Employs orthogonality constraints to reduce redundancy between shared and private feature spaces.  
- Evaluates on 16 diverse text classification tasks and shows reduced error rates versus SP-MTL, FS-MTL, MT-CNN, and MT-DNN.  
- Demonstrates transferability of the learned shared layer to unseen tasks as off-the-shelf knowledge.  
- Novel methodological combination (adversarial training + orthogonality) is well-motivated and empirically validated.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across many datasets strengthens generalizability claims.  
- Qualitative analyses (neuron visualizations, shared vs private patterns) provide intuitive model insights.  
- Lacks comparison with more recent or advanced MTL methods, weakening state-of-the-art claims.  
- Adds computational overhead from adversarial training and orthogonality constraints without detailed scalability or cost analysis.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., λ and γ) is not thoroughly analyzed, raising reproducibility concerns.  
- Asks authors to report scaling behavior and computational efficiency on larger/more-class tasks.  
- Requests more details on hyperparameter robustness and sensitivity across settings.  
- Queries whether certain task types or datasets cause underperformance and potential reasons.  
- Recommends acceptance subject to addressing comparison, efficiency, and hyperparameter analysis concerns.
