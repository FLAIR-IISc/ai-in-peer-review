- Summary: Proposes an adversarial multi-task learning framework that separates task-invariant and task-specific features via adversarial training and orthogonality, evaluated on 16 text classification tasks.

- Contribution 1: Introduces an adversarial multi-task framework to learn transferable task-invariant features.

- Contribution 2: Adds orthogonality constraints to reduce redundancy between shared and private feature spaces.

- Contribution 3: Provides extensive empirical evaluation across 16 text classification tasks.

- Strength 1: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the evaluated 16 tasks.

- Strength 2: Demonstrates transferability of the shared feature extractor to new tasks.

- Strength 3: Supplies qualitative analyses that help explain model behavior and improvements.

- Weakness 1: Model is more complex than existing multi-task approaches, increasing training and tuning difficulty.

- Weakness 2: Hyperparameter search is limited (small grid), raising concerns about optimality and sensitivity.

- Weakness 3: Comparison is limited to only two existing multi-task models, lacking broader baselines.

- Question 1: How were hyperparameters selected and how sensitive is the model to them?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide more details or visualizations of the shared vs. private feature spaces?

- Question 3: How does the model compare to other state-of-the-art multi-task approaches (e.g., attention-based or graph-based methods)?
