- Introduces an adversarial MTL framework to improve separation between shared and task-specific feature spaces.
- Uses orthogonality constraints plus adversarial training to ensure disjoint shared/private feature representations and reduce contamination.
- Extends binary adversarial training to a multi-class setting for joint training and semi-supervised use of unlabeled data.
- Designs the shared feature extractor as a transferable neural layer for knowledge transfer to new tasks.
- Novel combination of adversarial training and orthogonality constraints addressing a key limitation in existing MTL methods.
- Evaluated on 16 diverse text classification tasks, showing consistent improvements and significant error reduction over baselines.
- Empirical results show the shared layer generalizes well and enables effective knowledge transfer to unseen tasks.
- Provides qualitative analyses (neuron behavior visualization, pattern extraction) that offer insight into model improvements.
- Gives sufficient experimental details (datasets, hyperparameters) to support reproducibility.
- Lacks comparison with non-neural MTL methods, limiting broader benchmarking.
- Scalability concerns: adversarial training and orthogonality constraints add computational overhead and scalability is not addressed.
- Missing detailed ablation studies to isolate the individual impact of adversarial training versus orthogonality constraints.
- Evaluation focused mainly on text classification; generalization to domains like vision or speech is unclear.
- Question: How does ASP-MTL's computational cost compare to SP-MTL and FS-MTL in training time and resources?
- Question: Have the authors considered applying the framework to non-text domains, and what challenges are expected?
- Question: How were hyperparameters λ and γ tuned, and how sensitive is performance to their values?
- Recommendation: Accept — novel, well-supported approach likely to stimulate further research despite minor weaknesses.
