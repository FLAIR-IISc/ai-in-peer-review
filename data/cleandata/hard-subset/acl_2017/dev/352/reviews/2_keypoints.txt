- Paper proposes an Adversarial Shared-Private (ASP) LSTM text-classification model that learns well-partitioned shared and task-specific feature spaces.
- Multiclass adversarial training forces the shared space to be task-agnostic; orthogonality constraints encourage distinct shared/private representations.
- ASP achieves lower error rates than single-task and other multi-task neural models, with task-level cross-validation indicating favorable transfer of shared representations.
- Analysis of shared-layer activations suggests ASP is not dominated by inappropriate task-specific weights.
- Clear presentation of Sections 2–4 and a useful task-level cross-validation evaluation (Section 5.5).
- Implementation and data are provided.
- Several minor typographic and phrasing errors throughout the paper that need fixing (multiple line-specific examples noted).
- Strange example phrasing ("infantile cart") called into question as possibly erroneous.
- Mention of unlabelled data and semi-supervised learning experiments appears in Table 1 and Section 4.2, but corresponding results are missing or unclear.
- Error-rate improvements are promising but require statistical significance testing (e.g., non-parametric approximate randomisation) to confirm differences, especially between SP-MLT and ASP-MTL.
- Visual issues: inconsistent colours in Figure 5 captions and odd shading on the final rows of Tables 2 and 3.
- Suggestion to relate the adversarial + orthogonality approach to regularisation literature, as it may function as a form of regularisation.
