- Paper proposes new configurations and training objectives for multi-task neural sequence models to separate shared vs. task-specific features.
- Methods are relatively clear and logical but could be better organized up front.
- The reviewer suggests framing methods as solving two problems: (1) task-specific features leaking into the shared space, and (2) shared features leaking into task-specific spaces.
- Authors introduce an adversarial loss to keep task-specific features out of the shared representation.
- Authors introduce orthogonality constraints to keep shared features out of task-specific representations.
- Reviewer is confused about the adversarial component: the classifier layer (U and b) might simply mask task information rather than guarantee removal of task-specific signals.
- Experiments cover the basic necessary evaluations and validate the methods, though more experiments are possible.
- It would be helpful to report best-known results or stronger baselines on the evaluated tasks.
- Primary concern: paper lacks deeper motivation and a clear argument flow explaining why shared-private models still fail and why the proposed methods fix those failures.
- Table 4 is useful in illustrating what goes wrong with shared-private models, and a simple explanatory example would improve the introduction.
- The paper misses related work citation (Søgaard & Goldberg 2016) and should discuss connections to hierarchical MTL and the notion of tasks being “sufficiently similar.”
