- Paper proposes an adversarial shared-private (ASP) LSTM model that learns well-partitioned shared and task-specific feature spaces for text classification.
- Uses multiclass adversarial training to force the shared space to be task-invariant so a classifier cannot identify task source.
- Applies orthogonality constraints to keep shared and private feature spaces distinct.
- Compares fully-shared, shared-private, and ASP architectures, with ASP showing the best error rates versus single-task and other multi-task models.
- Performs task-level cross-validation to evaluate transferability of shared representations, yielding favorable results.
- Activation analysis suggests the ASP model avoids being misled by strong task-specific weights.
- Strength: effective integration of ideas focused on explicitly constructing useful shared representations for NLP.
- Strength: Sections 2–4 are particularly clear and well-written.
- Strength: Task-level cross-validation is a robust evaluation approach.
- Strength: Authors provide implementation and data, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: Multiple typographic and phrasing errors throughout the paper (several line-specific examples provided).
- Weakness: Shading on final rows of Tables 2 and 3 appears distorted when printed.
- Weakness: Table 1 and Section 4.2 mention unlabelled/semi-supervised experiments but no results are shown—clarification needed.
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported; recommend non-parametric approximate randomization tests to compare SP-MTL and ASP-MTL.
- Weakness: Color inconsistency between Figure 5(a) and 5(b) (legend/interpretation unclear).
- General suggestion: Discuss connection between adversarial+orthogonal constraints and regularization literature to situate results in broader context.
