- Proposes novel configurations and training objectives for neural sequence models in a multi-task learning (MTL) framework.  
- Motivates MTL since tasks can share information and joint learning can improve overall performance.  
- Methods section is reasonably clear but could be better organized; presenting the two core challenges upfront would improve cohesiveness.  
- Identifies two key challenges: task-specific features leaking into the shared space, and shared features contaminating private task spaces.  
- Introduces adversarial loss to prevent task-specific features from contaminating the shared representation.  
- Introduces orthogonality constraints to prevent shared features from infiltrating task-specific representations.  
- Finds the adversarial component confusing: the added layer D(s^kT, θD) with parameters U and b might simply learn to mask task information rather than truly remove it.  
- Experiments largely meet expectations and provide foundational validation, but including best-known results would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Primary critique: paper lacks deeper motivation and a clearer argument progression showing why shared-private models still fail and why the proposed fixes are needed.  
- Table 4 partially demonstrates shortcomings of shared-private models and the improvements from the proposed methods; adding simple examples and structured explanation would help.  
- Missing citation/connection to prior hierarchical MTL work (Søgaard & Goldberg 2016) and discussion of when tasks are “sufficiently similar” for MTL to be effective.
