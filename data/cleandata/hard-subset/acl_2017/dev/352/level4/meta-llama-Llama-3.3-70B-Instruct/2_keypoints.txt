- Proposes an adversarial shared-private (ASP) LSTM text-classification model using multiclass adversarial training to learn generic shared representations.  
- Uses orthogonality constraints to encourage distinct shared and task-specific feature spaces.  
- Compares fully-shared, shared-private (SP), and ASP models and shows ASP achieves lower error rates than single-task and other multi-task models.  
- Task-level cross-validation indicates the shared representation transfers effectively across tasks.  
- Activation analysis suggests the ASP shared layer is not dominated by strong weights from a single task.  
- Overall manuscript is well-structured, well-tested, and of high quality despite minor issues.  
- Strong contribution adapting shared-representation ideas from computer vision to NLP.  
- Sections 2–4 are exceptionally clear and well-expressed.  
- Task-level cross-validation (Section 5.5) provides robust transferability evaluation.  
- Availability of implementation and data is a major strength.  
- Multiple typographical and phrasing errors throughout (including an unclear example on line 84 and wording/number-agreement mistakes in several lines).  
- Formatting issues: unusual shading in final rows of Tables 2 and 3 and inconsistent colours between Figure 5(a) and 5(b).  
- Mention of unlabelled data in Table 1 and semi-supervised learning in Section 4.2 lacks supporting results and may be an omission or misunderstanding.  
- Error-rate differences need statistical significance testing (recommend non-parametric approximate randomisation test) to validate ASP advantages over SP-MLT.  
- Suggest exploring links between the proposed adversarial/orthogonality approach and existing regularisation techniques for additional context.
