- Presents novel configurations and training objectives for neural sequence models in multi-task settings and highlights the importance of task sharing.  
- Methods section is generally clear and logical but would benefit from improved organization.  
- Addresses two primary issues: shared features leaking into private feature spaces, and private features contaminating shared spaces.  
- Proposes two solutions: an adversarial loss to block task-specific features from shared representations and orthogonality constraints to block shared features from task-specific representations.  
- Raises concern about the adversarial component (D(s^kT, θD) with parameters U and b); its formulation is confusing and may only mask rather than remove task-specific information.  
- Evaluation is broadly sound and aligns with expected experiments but should include best-known results on these tasks for context.  
- Lacks deeper motivation and a clearer argument flow explaining why partially shared features are necessary versus fully shared models.  
- Needs a more logical explanation of shared-private model failures, using simple examples and trends showing why certain terms become shared or private.  
- Table 4 provides useful insight into what goes wrong with shared-private models and how the proposed methods help.  
- Misses citation and discussion of related MTL work (e.g., deep hierarchical MTL) and could explore how the model might learn when tasks are “sufficiently similar” to share information.
