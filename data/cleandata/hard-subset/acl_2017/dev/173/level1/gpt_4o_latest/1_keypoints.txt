- Proposes a nonparametric document clustering framework using Wasserstein distance on empirical word-embedding measures.  
- Claims the approach avoids fixed-length document vectors and strong assumptions, offering robust, intuitive clustering.  
- Leverages advances in Wasserstein barycenter computation for computational feasibility and reports state-of-the-art results.  
- Provides a quantitative evaluation showing benefits of word embeddings over bag-of-words for clustering.  
- Main contribution: a scalable, robust application of optimal transport theory to practical document clustering.  
- Main contribution: empirical analysis that quantifies the utility of word embeddings in clustering tasks.  
- Main contribution: computational efficiency gains via a modified Bregman ADMM algorithm and parallelization.  
- Strength: consistently outperforms traditional and embedding-based baselines across diverse datasets and metrics.  
- Strength: bridges theoretical optimal transport concepts with a practically usable clustering method.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on six datasets with multiple evaluation metrics strengthen empirical claims.  
- Strength: offers insights into limitations of pre-trained embeddings, emphasizing task-relevant embeddings for domain data.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is incremental—primarily an application of existing Wasserstein barycenter techniques.  
- Weakness: remains computationally expensive relative to simpler clustering methods, limiting large-scale adoption.  
- Weakness: does not explore or propose solutions for domain-specific embedding tuning, e.g., for Ohsumed.  
- Weakness: dense technical exposition, especially of the modified Bregman ADMM, may reduce accessibility for non-experts.  
- Question: how does the method scale to millions of documents and when does computational cost become prohibitive?  
- Question: have you considered or tried domain-specific embeddings or fine-tuning to improve domain dataset performance?  
- Question: how sensitive are results to the choice of Wasserstein barycenter algorithm and are alternative algorithms viable?  
- Overall: strong empirical framework with practical impact, but limited novelty and computational overhead warrant further work.
