- Novel nonparametric document clustering using the Wasserstein (Earth Mover’s) distance over word-embedding empirical measures.  
- Two claimed contributions: a scalable, state-of-the-art D2-clustering tool; and a method to quantify gains of word embeddings over bag-of-words.  
- Validated on six datasets, outperforming nine baseline methods.  
- Innovative integration of Wasserstein distance provides a principled, robust metric that avoids fixed-length vector limitations.  
- Scalability achieved via a modified Bregman ADMM algorithm, runtime analysis, and pruning strategies (e.g., Elkan’s algorithm).  
- Comprehensive experiments across short/long texts and domain-specific data, showing strong gains especially on short texts.  
- Practical utility: simple to use, few hyperparameters, offers actionable insights on embedding effectiveness.  
- Limited algorithmic novelty—mainly an application of existing methods (e.g., Bregman ADMM).  
- Domain-specific limitations: weaker improvements on datasets like Ohsumed, indicating need for task-specific embeddings.  
- Computational overhead remains high compared to simpler clustering techniques, potentially limiting adoption.  
- Lack of theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence guarantees, sensitivity to K and support size m).  
- Question: How does D2-clustering perform with different pre-trained embeddings (domain-specific vs. general)?  
- Question: Can the framework be extended to hierarchical or multi-level clustering?  
- Question: What are the practical implications of computational overhead and possible further optimizations?  
- Overall: robust and effective framework with empirical strengths but more application-focused novelty; recommend acceptance with minor revisions and addressing computational and domain-embedding limitations.
