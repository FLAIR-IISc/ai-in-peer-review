- Summary: Proposes D2-clustering, a nonparametric document clustering framework using the Wasserstein distance over word-embedding empirical measures, claiming improved clustering and a tool to compare embeddings vs. bag-of-words.
- Contribution: D2-clustering framework leveraging Wasserstein distance to cluster documents by their word-embedding distributions.
- Contribution: Efficient Wasserstein barycenter computation via recent algorithmic advances.
- Contribution: Provides a quantitative tool to evaluate gains/losses of word-embedding models compared to bag-of-words.
- Strength: Demonstrated robust performance, reportedly outperforming existing methods on both short- and long-text clustering tasks.
- Strength: Computation is practical for large-scale tasks using a modified Bregman ADMM algorithm.
- Strength: The quantitative evaluation tool is valuable for document analysis beyond clustering.
- Weakness: May underperform on domain-specific datasets (e.g., Ohsumed) where embeddings lack fine-grained semantic discrimination.
- Weakness: Method is sensitive to hyper-parameters—number of clusters K and centroid support size m—requiring careful tuning.
- Weakness: Empirical comparisons are not exhaustive; other methods may outperform on specific datasets or tasks.
- Question: How will the authors address and improve performance on domain-specific text datasets?
- Question: Can the authors provide deeper insights and practical guidance on hyper-parameter sensitivity and tuning?
- Question: How do the authors plan to extend D2-clustering to other NLP tasks (e.g., classification, sentiment) and what challenges are anticipated?
