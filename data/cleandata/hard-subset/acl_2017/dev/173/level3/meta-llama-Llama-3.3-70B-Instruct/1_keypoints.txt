- Proposes SIM-WAP: a novel model combining cross-modal mapping and per-word classifiers using lexical similarity in a distributional space.  
- Evaluates three models (TRANSFER, WAC, SIM-WAP) on standard and zero-shot object naming; SIM-WAP outperforms others in zero-shot.  
- Shows that combining model predictions improves performance, suggesting complementary representations of referential word meaning.  
- Clear, detailed explanations of models and motivations that make the work easy to follow.  
- Comprehensive evaluation including standard and zero-shot tasks, with analysis of each approach’s strengths and weaknesses.  
- Discusses implications for referring expression generation (REG) systems and proposes future research directions.  
- Paper contains many grammar errors (notably in the abstract) that reduce clarity.  
- Lacks sufficient context and background on related work to clarify significance and novelty.  
- Some tables and figures need improved readability and clarity.  
- Requests more details on SIM-WAP training and how it leverages lexical similarity in the distributional space.  
- Asks how the approach can be scaled to larger test sets and more complex object naming tasks.  
- Seeks discussion of real-world applications and integration into REG systems (e.g., attribute selection, contextual information).
