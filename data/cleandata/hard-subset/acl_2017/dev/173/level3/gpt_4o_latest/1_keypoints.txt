- Proposes a nonparametric document clustering framework using Wasserstein distance and adapted D2-clustering with efficient Wasserstein barycenter computation.
- Claims two contributions: a scalable, computationally feasible clustering tool that outperforms baselines and a framework to quantify gains from word embeddings vs. bag-of-words.
- Empirical validation on six datasets showing robustness and effectiveness.
- Reviewer finds no clear strengths worth highlighting due to poor presentation and coherence.
- Grammar and writing quality are poor, with numerous errors that harm readability and professionalism.
- Paper is dense and overly technical; lacks accessible intuition and clear explanations of key concepts like Wasserstein distance.
- Novelty and positioning are unclear; method appears to heavily build on prior work (e.g., D2-clustering, Kusner et al. 2015) without a convincing distinction.
- Experimental analysis lacks depth; fails to explain poor performance on domain-specific data (e.g., Ohsumed) and provides superficial discussion of embedding sensitivity.
- Reported runtimes (e.g., 121s/iteration for Newsgroups) raise concerns about scalability and practicality, which are not adequately addressed.
- Questions: comparison to other recent Wasserstein/embedding clustering advances and specific gaps addressed.
- Questions: request for more intuitive explanations or visualizations of Wasserstein distance’s role in clustering.
- Questions: how performance scales with larger datasets or higher-dimensional embeddings and plans to optimize computational efficiency.
- Recommendation: reject; major revision needed to improve clarity, address computational limitations, and provide deeper insights into contributions and limitations.
