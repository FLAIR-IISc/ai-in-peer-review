**Strengths:**

*   A relatively clear description of the proposed approach's context and structure.
*   A relatively complete description of the mathematics. Comparison with a broad set of alternative systems.

**Weaknesses:**

*   In Section 5, the weak results/summary of the "Side-by-Side Human" comparison. Some ambiguity/grammatical errors.

**General Discussion:**

The article proposes a theoretical tool for modeling pronunciation context, which includes a sequence of previous pronunciations. Some minor issues:

1.  In Table 1, previous changes can be numbered, which would make it less difficult to understand the text associated with this table (lines 095-103). Currently, when identifying references in the authors' description, readers need to count the changes from top to bottom, and they might think that "second", "third", and "last" mean a side-specific or global count.
2.  The confusion of some readers can be resolved by clearly defining the meaning of "खंड" in the "खंड स्तर" as in line 269. Previously, in line 129, this similar-looking thing was referred to as "a sequence-sequence [similarity matrix]". Both words seem to be mutually exchanged, but it is not clear what they actually mean, even if the text is the same in Section 3.3. It seems that the authors mean "word sub-sequence" and "word sub-sequence to word sub-sequence", where "sub-" means "not the whole pronunciation", but not certain.
3.  Currently, the variable symbol "n" seems to be used to count words in a pronunciation (line 306), as well as in a dialogue (line 389). The authors may have chosen two different characters for these two different purposes, so as not to confuse readers in going through their equations.
4.  The statement given at the end of Section 5, "This indicates that a retrieval-based chatbot exercise with SMN may provide a better experience than a state-of-the-art generation model," seems unsupported. The two approaches referred to are considered comparable in 555 out of 1000 cases, with the proposed method being a better baseline in 238 out of the remaining 445 cases. The authors are encouraged to assess and present the statistical significance of this comparison. If it is weak, their comparison allows the best claim to be made that their proposed method is not worse than the VHRED baseline (but rather "better").
5.  The authors may choose to place the clear "first layer", "second layer", and "third layer" labels in Figure 1, which they use in the accompanying text.
6.  In line 280, "a response candidate can complete each pronunciation" has extensive use of "to meet" which is difficult to understand.
7.  Spelling: "gated recurrent units"; "respectively" should be removed in line 133; punctuation has been exchanged in lines 186 and 188; "on the baseline model" -> "by the baseline model"; "no one can neglect".
