- Introduces Sequential Matching Network (SMN) for multi-turn response selection, matching responses with each utterance at multiple granularities and aggregating via GRU.  
- Evaluated on Ubuntu Dialogue Corpus and new human-labeled Douban Conversation Corpus, showing significant improvements over state-of-the-art.

- Novel "matching first" strategy prevents information loss from early concatenation or abstract context representations.  
- Strong empirical gains: >6% R10@1 on Ubuntu and 3–4% on R10@1 and P@1 for Douban, with statistical significance.  
- Comprehensive analysis including ablation studies, visualizations, and context-length comparisons; visualizations show how model captures key information.  
- Practical contribution: release of the Douban Conversation Corpus for open-domain multi-turn response selection.

- Limited discussion of model limitations (e.g., scalability, reliance on pre-trained embeddings) beyond candidate retrieval.  
- Inadequate comparison with generation models; only a small-scale human eval against VHRED.  
- Potential dataset bias: Douban is Chinese social-media specific and may not generalize across languages/domains.

- How does SMN handle more diverse/noisy conversational contexts (slang, incomplete sentences)?  
- Can the "matching first" strategy be adapted to generation-based models, and how?  
- What are SMN’s computational costs versus baselines, especially for longer contexts?

- Recommendation: Strong contribution with novel, effective model and valuable dataset release; recommend acceptance.
