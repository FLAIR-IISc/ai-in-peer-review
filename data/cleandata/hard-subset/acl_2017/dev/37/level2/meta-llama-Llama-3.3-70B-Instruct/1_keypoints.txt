- Proposes a Sequential Matching Network (SMN) that matches a response with each context utterance at multiple granularities and accumulates matches chronologically via an RNN.  
- Evaluated on Ubuntu and Douban datasets, showing significant improvements over prior methods.  
- Contribution: a new context-based matching model for multi-turn response selection in retrieval-based chatbots.  
- Contribution: release of the large, human-labeled Douban Conversation Corpus for multi-turn response selection.  
- Contribution: empirical validation of SMN’s effectiveness on public benchmarks.  
- Strength: effectively captures inter-utterance relationships and identifies important context information for response selection.  
- Strength: achieves significant performance gains over state-of-the-art on two datasets.  
- Strength: Douban Corpus publication provides a valuable resource for the community.  
- Weakness: relies on a heuristic candidate retrieval method that may be suboptimal.  
- Weakness: does not explicitly model logical consistency of responses.  
- Weakness: performance may degrade with longer contexts or more complex conversations.  
- Question: How will the authors improve the candidate retrieval method to be more effective?  
- Question: Can the authors clarify how SMN captures or could capture logical consistency of responses?  
- Question: How do the authors plan to extend SMN to handle longer contexts or more complex dialogues?
