- Proposes SMN: matches a response with each context utterance at multiple granularities and accumulates matching via an RNN; evaluated on Ubuntu and Douban with significant improvements.
- Contribution: new context-based matching model for multi-turn response selection in retrieval-based chatbots.
- Contribution: published a large human-labeled dataset for multi-turn response selection.
- Contribution: empirically verifies model effectiveness on public datasets.
- Strength: SMN captures important contextual information and leverages it for matching.
- Strength: Evaluation on two public datasets demonstrates effectiveness and generalizability.
- Strength: The released human-labeled dataset can facilitate future research.
- Weakness: "Side-by-side human" comparison is weak, unclear, and contains disfluency/agrammaticality issues.
- Weakness: Claim of better user experience than state-of-the-art generation model is unsupported and lacks statistical significance.
- Weakness: Several minor spelling, punctuation, and grammar errors throughout the paper.
- Question: How were optimal hyperparameters chosen and how sensitive is the model to them?
- Question: Request details on the "side-by-side human" comparison—evaluation metrics and number of human evaluators.
- Question: How will the authors address logical consistency of responses in future work?
