- Proposes Sequential Matching Network (SMN) that matches responses with individual utterances at multiple granularities, distills matches via CNN/pooling, and accumulates them chronologically with a GRU; releases the Douban Conversation Corpus.
- Main contributions: novel context-based SMN model, Douban human-labeled dataset, and empirical gains over state-of-the-art on multiple datasets.
- Strength: Novelty and technical rigor—extends 2D matching to multi-turn conversations and uses GRU to preserve utterance relationships.
- Strength: Strong empirical validation—significant improvements on Ubuntu and Douban (e.g., R10@1, MAP).
- Strength: Dataset contribution—the Douban Conversation Corpus is a valuable open-domain, human-labeled resource.
- Strength: Visualization and analysis—similarity matrices and GRU gate visualizations improve interpretability.
- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology (e.g., "segment" vs "sequence") that needs clearer definitions.
- Weakness: Variable overloading (using "n" for both word count and utterance count) causes confusion.
- Weakness: Weak human comparison results—SMN only marginally beats VHRED (238 vs 207), questioning practical impact.
- Weakness: Unsupported claims—no statistical significance tests reported for human evaluation.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—table/figure labeling, unclear phrasing, grammatical errors, and typos reduce readability.
- Question: Please clarify the definition of "segment" and how it differs from "sequence" in similarity matrices.
- Question: How does SMN performance change with different attention mechanisms in the matching accumulation layer?
- Question: Provide statistical significance tests for the human comparison with VHRED to substantiate chatbot superiority.
- Additional comment: Improving terminology, variable naming, and statistical validation would substantially strengthen the paper.
