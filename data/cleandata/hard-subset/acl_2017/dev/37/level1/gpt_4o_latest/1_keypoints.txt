- Problem: Multi-turn response selection in retrieval-based chatbots; proposes Sequential Matching Network (SMN).  
- SMN: Matches responses with each utterance at multiple granularities, distills info via CNN/pooling, and accumulates chronologically with an RNN.  
- Evaluation: Tested on Ubuntu Dialogue Corpus and a new Douban Conversation Corpus with significant improvements over baselines.  
- Dataset: Introduces Douban Conversation Corpus — human-labeled, open-domain, multi-turn response selection dataset.  
- Contribution: Novel "matching-first" architecture integrating standard components to capture utterance-response and inter-utterance relations.  
- Empirical strengths: Strong results, ablation studies, visualizations, and diverse metrics (R@k, MAP, MRR).  
- Practical relevance: Douban corpus offers a realistic benchmark addressing limitations of existing datasets.  
- Weakness: Individual model components are standard; novelty is primarily in integration.  
- Weakness: Candidate retrieval relies on heuristic TF-IDF keyword expansion, raising scalability concerns.  
- Weakness: Does not explicitly address logical consistency in multi-turn conversations.  
- Question: How does SMN perform with advanced dense retrieval methods?  
- Question: How robust is SMN to noisy or incomplete contexts compared to baselines?  
- Question: What is SMN's computational complexity relative to other state-of-the-art models for long contexts?  
- Overall: Strong, well-motivated, empirically validated contribution that would benefit from addressing scalability and logical consistency.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
