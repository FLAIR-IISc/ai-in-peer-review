- Proposes SMN: a sequential matching network matching a response with each context utterance at multiple granularities and aggregating via an RNN.  
- Publishes Douban Conversation Corpus: a large human-labeled dataset for multi-turn response selection.  
- Empirically validates SMN on Ubuntu and Douban corpora, showing improved performance.  
- SMN achieves significant gains over prior state-of-the-art methods on both datasets.  
- Provides detailed model analysis, including visualizations of similarity matrices and RNN gates.  
- Douban dataset offers a more realistic benchmark for chatbot response selection research.  
- SMN needs large amounts of labeled data to train, which is time-consuming and expensive.  
- Performance may degrade with increasing context length due to RNN difficulties with long-range dependencies.  
- Paper lacks thorough comparisons with other state-of-the-art approaches like attention-based or graph-based models.  
- Question: How will the authors address the requirement for large labeled training data?  
- Question: How does SMN capture long-range dependencies among utterances and how can it be improved for longer contexts?  
- Question: How will SMN be extended to handle more complex conversations involving multiple topics or speakers?
