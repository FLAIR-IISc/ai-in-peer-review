- Summary: Proposes a neural network for causality classification that disambiguates causal lexical markers using sentence-level encoding and outperforms prior work on the AltLex corpus.
- Contribution: Novel two-input neural architecture encoding the two events with LSTMs and dense tanh layers.
- Contribution: Effective use of pretrained word embeddings (GloVe) to represent events and lexical markers.
- Contribution: Empirical improvement—higher F1 and accuracy—over state-of-the-art on the AltLex corpus.
- Strength: Innovative and effective neural approach demonstrating neural networks' potential for causality classification.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation including comparison to two baselines and detailed result analysis.
- Strength: Word embeddings successfully capture semantic meaning of events and markers.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to the AltLex corpus, which may not generalize to other datasets or domains.
- Weakness: Dataset exhibits class imbalance between Causal and Non-Causal classes.
- Weakness: Model lacks interpretability, making its predictions hard to explain.
- Question: How will the authors address the class imbalance in future work?
- Question: Please provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process.
- Question: How would the system perform on other datasets/domains and what adaptations are needed?
- Additional comment: The work is a significant contribution with potential cross-task applicability but needs broader evaluation and limitations addressed.
