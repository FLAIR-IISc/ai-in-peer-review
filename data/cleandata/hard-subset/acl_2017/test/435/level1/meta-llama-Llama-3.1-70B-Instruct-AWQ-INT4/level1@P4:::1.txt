**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically for disambiguating the causal meaning of lexical markers in context. The authors argue that the encoding of the meaning of a sentence is required for the disambiguation of its causal meaning. They evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which is a corpus of alternative lexicalizations of causal relations. The system outperforms the state-of-the-art method on this corpus.

**Main Contributions**

1. The proposal of a neural network architecture for causality classification that encodes the meaning of a sentence to disambiguate its causal meaning.
2. The evaluation of the system on the AltLex corpus, which is a challenging corpus for causality classification due to its diverse range of causal expressions.
3. The demonstration that the proposed system outperforms the state-of-the-art method on this corpus.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to causality classification that encodes the meaning of a sentence to disambiguate its causal meaning.
2. The system is evaluated on a challenging corpus, which demonstrates its robustness and ability to generalize.
3. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the results, including an examination of the system's performance on specific types of causal expressions.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on the AltLex corpus, which may not be representative of all types of causal expressions.
2. The system's performance may be affected by the quality of the word embeddings used, which are not thoroughly evaluated in the paper.
3. The authors do not provide a detailed comparison of their system with other state-of-the-art methods for causality classification.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the issue of corpus bias, given that the AltLex corpus may not be representative of all types of causal expressions?
2. Can you provide more details on the word embeddings used in the system, including their quality and how they were evaluated?
3. How do you plan to extend the system to handle more complex types of causal expressions, such as those involving multiple events or entities?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a clear and well-organized presentation of the proposed system and its evaluation. However, some minor suggestions for improvement include:

* Providing more details on the implementation of the system, including the specific hyperparameters used and the optimization algorithm employed.
* Including more examples of the system's output, including both correct and incorrect classifications, to provide a better understanding of its strengths and weaknesses.
* Discussing the potential applications of the system, including its potential use in natural language processing tasks such as text summarization and question answering.
