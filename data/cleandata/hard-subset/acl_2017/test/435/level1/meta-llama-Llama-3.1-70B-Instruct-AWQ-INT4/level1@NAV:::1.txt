**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically for disambiguating the causal meaning of a sentence. The authors argue that the encoding of the meaning of a sentence is required for the disambiguation of its causal meaning. They evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus and compare it to the state-of-the-art method. The results show that their system outperforms the state-of-the-art method in terms of precision, recall, and F1 score.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neural Network Architecture**: The authors propose a neural network architecture with two inputs, one for the first event and the other for the lexical marker and the second event. The architecture is based on the encoding of the two inputs with an LSTM and the use of several dense layers with a tanh activation function.
2. **Evaluation on AltLex Corpus**: The authors evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which is a freely available corpus for causality classification. They compare their system to the state-of-the-art method and show that their system outperforms it in terms of precision, recall, and F1 score.
3. **Analysis of Results**: The authors provide an analysis of the results, including examples of correctly classified instances and misclassified instances. They also discuss the limitations of their system and potential future work.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty of the Approach**: The authors propose a novel approach to causality classification, which is based on the encoding of the meaning of a sentence. This approach is different from traditional approaches, which rely on syntactic and semantic features.
2. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The authors achieve state-of-the-art results on the AltLex corpus, which is a challenging task.
3. **Thorough Analysis**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of the results, including examples of correctly classified instances and misclassified instances.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which may not be representative of other datasets.
2. **Lack of Comparison to Other Methods**: The authors only compare their system to the state-of-the-art method, but do not compare it to other methods that may be relevant to the task.
3. **Limited Discussion of Limitations**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of the limitations of their system, including potential biases and errors.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limitation of only evaluating your system on the AltLex corpus?
2. Can you provide a comparison of your system to other methods that may be relevant to the task?
3. How do you plan to address the potential biases and errors of your system?
