- Proposes a neural network architecture that encodes sentence meaning to disambiguate causal lexical markers for causality classification.  
- Evaluates on the AltLex corpus and outperforms the state-of-the-art method.  
- Analyzes system performance across different types of causal lexical markers, showing strengths on ambiguous and unambiguous markers.  
- Well-motivated argument for the necessity of encoding sentence meaning in causality classification.  
- Carefully designed neural architecture with thorough evaluation and per-marker analysis.  
- Comprehensive comparison with and empirical superiority over prior state-of-the-art.  
- Detailed result analysis highlighting system strengths and weaknesses.  
- Heavy reliance on the AltLex corpus, raising concerns about generalizability to other datasets.  
- Lacks a detailed error analysis to reveal specific failure modes and improvement areas.  
- Insufficient discussion of the broader implications and potential applications of the results.  
- No clear explanation of how out-of-vocabulary words are handled, posing a potential limitation.  
- Asks authors to clarify strategies for handling out-of-vocabulary words.  
- Requests a detailed error analysis with examples of misclassified instances.  
- Asks how the system could be extended to handle more complex causal relations (multiple events/entities).  
- Requests a more detailed discussion of potential applications and impact on NLP/AI.  
- Paper is well-written and clear but would benefit from deeper error/OOV handling analysis.  
- Overall, presents a significant contribution to NLP with potential broad applicability.
