- Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture to disambiguate causal lexical markers using context; evaluated on the AltLex corpus and claims state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution: Thorough evaluation of neural approaches for causal disambiguation on the AltLex corpus.

- Contribution: Demonstrates contextual encoding (LSTM) outperforms lexical-resource and syntactic-feature methods.

- Contribution: Analysis showing the model handles ambiguous causal markers that prior approaches miss.

- Strength: Addresses a significant problem with clear theoretical motivation against syntax-grounded causality.

- Strength: Uses the AltLex corpus and provides careful corpus analysis, particularly class distributions of ambiguous markers.

- Strength: Strong empirical results on ambiguous cases (e.g., correctly classifying “which then” as causal).

- Strength: Clearly defines the task and distinguishes causal meaning classification from argument identification.

- Strength: Comparative analysis of neural architectures and padding strategies, finding independent event encoding effective.

- Weakness: Neural architecture is not novel; standard two-input LSTM lacks justification over other models.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of limitations and generalization to other domains or datasets.

- Weakness: Class imbalance in AltLex is not adequately addressed or mitigated.

- Weakness: Baselines (B1, B2) are weak; comparison to stronger/neural baselines is missing.

- Weakness: Missing detailed implementation/hyperparameter information, hindering reproducibility.

- Q: How did differing train/test class distributions in the bootstrapped AltLex affect performance and what strategies handled this shift?

- Q: What exact hyperparameters (LSTM units, dropout, batch size) produced the reported 81.96% F1?

- Q: How would the model perform compared to more recent neural approaches (transformers, attention)?

- Q: How would the model generalize to causal expressions outside AltLex and what additional data is needed?
