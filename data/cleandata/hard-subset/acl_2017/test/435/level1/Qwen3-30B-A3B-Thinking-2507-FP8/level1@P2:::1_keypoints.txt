1. Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture to encode context for causal meaning disambiguation, arguing context is essential beyond lexical/syntactic cues.  
2. Claims the model outperforms prior state-of-the-art on the AltLex corpus and handles ambiguous lexical markers better than previous approaches.  
3. Empirically demonstrates improved performance (best config: "Pair LSTM Mean Adam") with 81.94% F1 on the bootstrapping AltLex variant.  
4. Strength: Clearly identifies the limitation of prior work relying on lexical resources/syntactic features, reducing coverage for causal constructions.  
5. Strength: Strong empirical results showing consistent improvements over Hidey and McKeown (2016) on both AltLex versions.  
6. Strength: Provides evidence that context encoding is necessary, successfully classifying ambiguous cases (e.g., "since", "after", "which then", polysemous verbs).  
7. Strength: Evaluation design is appropriate—compares against baselines, analyzes padding/optimizer impacts, and inspects frequent connectives.  
8. Weakness: Insufficient justification for choosing LSTM; no empirical comparison with CNNs, attention mechanisms, or transformers.  
9. Weakness: Inadequate handling and analysis of dataset imbalance; balancing experiments lack thorough impact analysis and significance testing.  
10. Weakness: Limited comparison to contemporary methods beyond B2 (Hidey and McKeown, 2016), leaving broader context unclear.  
11. Weakness: Superficial analysis of failure cases; lacks systematic error analysis by lexical markers, syntactic structures, or contextual features.  
12. Question: Please provide details/comparisons showing why LSTM was chosen over CNNs or attention-based models for this task.  
13. Question: Explain why the bootstrapping AltLex variant's 1.25% F1 improvement did not reach statistical significance while the non-bootstrapping version did.  
14. Question: Clarify the bootstrapping process for creating the AltLex corpus—how were causal relations identified during alignment of Wikipedia sentences?
