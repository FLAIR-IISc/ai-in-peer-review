- Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture for causal relation classification, arguing sentence-level encoding is crucial for disambiguating causality.
- Claims to outperform prior state-of-the-art on the AltLex corpus, showing competitive F1 for the causal class.
- Primary contribution: demonstrates a simple LSTM model is effective for AltLex causal disambiguation.
- Secondary contribution: thorough related work that highlights limitations of prior feature-engineered approaches and datasets.
- Strength: Strong empirical results, outperforming Hidey & McKeown (2016) in most configurations, especially causal F1.
- Strength: Clear problem definition and motivation emphasizing linguistic ambiguity of causality and limits of syntactic heuristics.
- Strength: Comprehensive related work situating the approach within existing causality research.
- Strength: Ablation studies and hyperparameter tuning (padding, optimizers, dropout) show careful model optimization.
- Strength: Qualitative analysis of examples helps explain model successes and failures tied to ambiguous connectives.
- Weakness: Architecture novelty is limited—LSTM usage is standard and needs stronger justification versus other encoders.
- Weakness: Results depend heavily on the AltLex corpus; generalizability to other corpora/genres/languages is not shown.
- Weakness: Lacks a systematic error analysis categorizing common failure modes and marker types the model struggles with.
- Weakness: Limited treatment of counterfactual reasoning and its potential integration into the model.
- Weakness: Over-reliance on numerical metrics; more qualitative analysis of model behavior is needed.
- Question: Why choose LSTMs over other sequence models (e.g., Transformers), and what makes LSTMs especially suitable here?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to training data size/composition; did you try augmentation or transfer learning?
- Question: What are the most frequent error types; can you provide a systematic, categorized error analysis?
- Question: Did you consider modeling temporal information or counterfactual constraints, and why/why not?
- Question: What other tasks could this model apply to, and what model modifications would those require?
- Additional comment: Improve table formatting for readability.
- Additional comment: Add a visualization of the network architecture.
- Additional comment: Define abbreviations (e.g., LSTM, PDTB) on first use.
