- Tackles causal relation classification by disambiguating causal meaning of discourse markers using contextual encoding.  
- Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture to encode sentence context for causal disambiguation.  
- Evaluates on the AltLex corpus and reports improvements over a strong baseline (Hidey & McKeown, 2016).  
- Main contribution: effective application of a simple LSTM model to AltLex, providing a solid empirical baseline.  
- Secondary contribution: highlights limitations of lexical/syntactic methods and motivates context-aware approaches.  
- Strength: Empirical validation on a public dataset showing model outperforms prior baseline.  
- Strength: Thorough experimentation across LSTM variants, optimizers, and padding strategies.  
- Strength: Clear problem definition and motivated discussion of prior limitations.  
- Strength: Result analysis focuses on challenging cases and suggests future improvements.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty since LSTMs are standard and innovation is primarily dataset application.  
- Weakness: Lacks a systematic ablation study to quantify each component’s contribution.  
- Weakness: AltLex dataset is small and possibly biased, limiting generalizability to other domains.  
- Questions: Request details on hyperparameter tuning, especially dropout and regularization selection.  
- Questions: Ask for examples of ambiguous connectives and analysis of model failures on them.  
- Questions: Suggest considering attention mechanisms to focus on relevant input parts.
