- LSTM-based model for causal meaning disambiguation outperforms previous feature-engineered systems on the AltLex corpus.
- Paper provides a thorough experimental evaluation and analysis establishing a strong neural baseline for this task.

- Achieves new state-of-the-art results on AltLex with consistent F1 and accuracy gains over prior work.
- Model is simple, clearly explained, and well-motivated (LSTMs preferred over CNNs for variable-length causal context).
- Experiments are comprehensive, comparing strong baselines, dataset variants, and reporting full metrics.
- Qualitative analysis shows the model disambiguates challenging cases by using broader context rather than relying on lexical cues.

- Limited methodological novelty: the Pair LSTM is a standard architecture and the contribution is mainly empirical.
- Input representation is ambiguous—unclear how e1, e2, and lexical marker l are extracted and concatenated for the model.
- Comparison conflates model architecture and information sources (embeddings vs. lexical resources), making it hard to attribute gains.
- Claims that the model “encodes meaning” are strong but inadequately substantiated by systematic analysis or probing.

- Clarify precisely how events and the lexical marker are extracted and partitioned into the two LSTM inputs.
- Explain why the state-passing Pair LSTM consistently underperforms the independent Pair LSTM.
- Address whether differing optimal padding/optimizer choices indicate hyperparameter sensitivity or have linguistic justification.
