- Task: causal meaning classification — determine if relation between two text spans linked by a lexical marker is causal.  
- Problem: prior methods relying on fixed syntactic patterns or lexical resources are brittle.  
- Hypothesis: encoding semantic context of events with a neural network is essential for accurate disambiguation.  
- Method: dual-input LSTM that processes the two related events independently before classifying their relationship.  
- Results: model outperforms previous feature-based state-of-the-art on two versions of the AltLex corpus.  
- Primary contribution: shows a simple, context-focused neural architecture can surpass complex feature-engineered baselines, validating the context hypothesis.  
- Secondary contribution: thorough experimental evaluation and qualitative analysis with concrete examples of handling ambiguous cases.  
- Strength: establishes new state-of-the-art with significant F1 and accuracy gains.  
- Strength: well-motivated and clearly articulated move from feature-based to context-aware neural approach.  
- Strength: qualitative analysis provides valuable insight into model behavior on ambiguous instances.  
- Weakness: limited architectural novelty — dual-input LSTM is a standard sentence-pair setup.  
- Weakness: evaluation confined to a single dataset (AltLex), limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Weakness: missing comparison to contemporary contextual encoders (e.g., BERT/Transformers), leaving true SOTA status unclear.
