- Shows a neural, feature-free dual-input LSTM can disambiguate causal markers from context, outperforming feature-engineered systems.  
- Establishes a strong new baseline on the AltLex corpus with systematic evaluation of neural configurations.  
- Significant empirical gains over prior SOTA (≈+2.13% F1 and +7.72% accuracy on non-bootstrapping AltLex).  
- Methodologically simple and general: uses only pre-trained embeddings and standard architecture, avoiding external linguistic resources.  
- Thorough experimentation and analysis, including baselines, architectural variants, padding/optimizer sweeps, qualitative examples, and class-balanced tests.  
- Limited architectural novelty: the dual-input LSTM+concatenation is a standard approach rather than a methodological advance.  
- Weak justification/analysis for why the "Pair LSTM" outperforms the "Stated Pair LSTM"; outcome feels post-hoc without a clear hypothesis.  
- Framing overreaches: paper positions itself in broad causal reasoning but addresses a narrower supervised classification of pre-identified event pairs.  
- Question: Why does independent encoding (Pair LSTM) outperform state-transfer (Stated Pair LSTM)—could state transfer add noise or hamper optimization?  
- Question: Were attention mechanisms considered, and might attention over e1 while encoding e2 improve focus on salient causal cues?
