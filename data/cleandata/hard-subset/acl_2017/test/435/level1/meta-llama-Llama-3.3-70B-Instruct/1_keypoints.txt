- Proposes a dual-input neural architecture for causality classification and reports state-of-the-art results on the AltLex corpus.
- Argues that encoding the meanings of both events is necessary to disambiguate causal relations.
- Contribution: a two-input network (first event; lexical marker + second event) that outperforms prior work on AltLex.
- Contribution: demonstrates the importance of encoding relation context for causal disambiguation.
- Contribution: evaluates on the large, challenging AltLex corpus and achieves SOTA performance.
- Strength: shows neural networks effectively encode contextual information for causality classification.
- Strength: achieves improved performance over previous state-of-the-art on AltLex.
- Strength: model appears robust to ambiguity in causal expressions.
- Weakness: evaluation restricted to AltLex, raising generalization concerns to other causal expressions.
- Weakness: lacks interpretability and detailed analysis of model decisions.
- Weakness: depends on pre-trained word embeddings that may miss causal nuances.
- Question: how will the authors address the limited size and scope of the AltLex corpus and build a more comprehensive dataset?
- Question: can the authors provide a detailed analysis of the neural network’s decisions and insights from that analysis?
- Question: how will the system be extended to handle more complex causal expressions (multiple events, nested relations)?
