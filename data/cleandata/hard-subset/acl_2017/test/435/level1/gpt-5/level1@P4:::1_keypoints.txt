- Task: Causal meaning disambiguation—decide if (e1, l+e2) expresses a causal relation.  
- Proposed models: two LSTM architectures—Pair LSTM (independent encodings) and Stated Pair LSTM (second LSTM initialized with first’s final state).  
- Data and baselines: evaluated on AltLex (bootstrap & non-bootstrap) against per-marker majority (B1) and feature-based SVM (B2).  
- Main empirical finding: LSTM-based contextual encoders improve disambiguation over B1 and slightly over B2, with largest gains on the non-bootstrapping split (e.g., F1 75.40% vs 73.76%; Acc 77.90% vs 71.86%).  
- Architectural insight: independent encoding (Pair LSTM) generally outperforms state-initialized encoding (Stated Pair LSTM).  
- Training/optimizer analysis: experiments compare optimizers (Adam vs Adadelta) and pooling choices, showing precision–recall trade-offs.  
- Reduced feature reliance: method uses only pre-trained embeddings, reducing dependence on lexical resources and helping rare/ambiguous markers.  
- Qualitative analysis: case studies show contextual modeling disambiguates ambiguous markers and verbs (e.g., “break”, “which then”).  
- Novelty concern: methodological novelty is limited—standard LSTMs, embeddings, and dense layers; contribution is mainly empirical.  
- Clarity issues: confusing descriptions of “zero-padding” vs pooling, unclear computation graph (final hidden state vs pooled embeddings).  
- Missing hyperparameters: LSTM size, layers, batch size, epochs, learning rate, embedding fine-tuning, and dev/selection protocol are not specified.  
- Evaluation scope limited: experiments only on AltLex—no tests on PDTB, Causal-TimeBank, cross-domain transfer, or broader datasets.  
- Baseline/control gaps: no simple neural baselines (e.g., BiLSTM over full sentence or averaged embeddings) and no ablations isolating marker identity.  
- Confound/robustness issues: lack of per-marker performance, no analysis of generalization to unseen/rare markers, and unquantified distribution shift effects in bootstrapping split.  
- Statistical/reproducibility gaps: limited significance testing (only vs B1 on reduced set), and no code, seeds, or full training protocol released.  
- Key architecture question: clarify whether LSTM outputs use final hidden states or pooled embeddings and clarify “padding” vs “pooling.”  
- Key training question: provide LSTM hidden sizes, layers, batch size, epochs, learning rate schedule, early stopping, and whether embeddings are fine-tuned.  
- Key evaluation questions: define train/dev/test splits, hyperparameter selection procedure, number of random seeds, statistical significance vs B2, ablation results (only e1, only l+e2, marker masked), per-marker/OOD performance, and code/model release plans.
