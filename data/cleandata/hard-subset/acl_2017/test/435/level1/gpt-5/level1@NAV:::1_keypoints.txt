- Task: Disambiguating causal vs non-causal meanings between two events signaled by ambiguous lexical markers using neural models on AltLex.
- Model: Proposes Pair LSTM and Stated Pair LSTM that encode event contexts with GloVe and classify relation as causal/non-causal.
- Data/experiments: Evaluated on AltLex (bootstrapping and non-bootstrapping), comparing to majority-per-marker baseline and Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM.
- Result: Neural context-encoding models outperform feature-engineered SVM and marker-majority heuristics, improving F1 and accuracy on non-bootstrapping set.
- Empirical insight: Independent encoding (Pair LSTM) outperforms state-initialized LSTM; optimizer and padding choices affect precision–recall trade-offs.
- Analysis: Ablations on Pair vs Stated LSTM, Adam vs Adadelta, and padding strategies provide actionable architectural/optimization guidance.
- Qualitative: Examples show the model handles ambiguous markers (e.g., “which then”) and non-causative uses of causative verbs (e.g., “break”), supporting context sensitivity.
- Task formulation: Clear separation between causal meaning classification and argument identification to scope the problem.
- Novelty concern: Architecture is standard (two LSTMs + dense layers + GloVe); lacks attention, contextualized embeddings, or newer architectures.
- Evaluation scope: Narrow evaluation limited to AltLex; no experiments on PDTB, Causal-TimeBank, or out-of-domain transfer to test generality.
- Method detail gap: Padding strategy (“max/mean/mode”) and preprocessing/tokenization/event span extraction are under-specified.
- Baselines weakness: Missing stronger neural baselines (e.g., BiLSTM+attention, contextual encoders); significance testing is limited/inconsistent and tables have typos.
- Reproducibility question: Unclear exact train/dev/test splits and tuning protocol; unclear if a dev set was used.
- Input at test time: Unclear whether event/marker spans are gold or automatically detected during testing.
- Ablation question: Request to report performance without the lexical marker input and on items with unseen markers.
- Robustness question: Asks for results on other datasets or zero-shot/transfer experiments to assess generalization.
