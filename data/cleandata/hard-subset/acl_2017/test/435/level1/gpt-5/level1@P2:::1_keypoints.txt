- Paper proposes a two-input LSTM encoding e1 and marker+e2 for causal vs. non-causal disambiguation on AltLex, showing small consistent gains over baselines.
- Main contributions: (1) LSTM encoder matches/outperforms strong SVM baseline on AltLex, improving precision and accuracy.
- Main contributions: (2) Analysis shows contextual event encoding improves robustness on ambiguous markers and out-of-distribution senses with illustrative examples.
- Main contributions: (3) Modest exploration of architecture/design choices (two-branch vs. state-initialized LSTMs, optimizer, padding heuristics).
- Empirical strength: Credible improvements over Hidey & McKeown (2016) on both bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped AltLex, e.g., +2.13 F1 and +7.72 accuracy in one setting.
- Qualitative strength: LSTM corrects some ambiguous cases where SVM fails, supporting benefit of context beyond connective identity.
- Architectural strength: Simple two-branch LSTM with late interaction is sensible; state-initialized LSTM performed worse, informing design choices.
- Robustness checks: Results reported across two corpus variants and imbalance-controlled subsets; McNemar test used against per-marker baseline in reduced setting.
- Major weakness: Evaluation may not isolate reliance on the connective because input includes marker+e2 and many markers are near-unambiguous.
- Major weakness: Missing critical ablations (mask/remove connective, ambiguous-only subset) to quantify contextual contribution vs. marker priors.
- Weakness: Padding-length description is confusing/incorrect (pad(x) defined via max/mean/mode on vector sequences); preprocessing and training details are incomplete, hurting reproducibility.
- Weakness: Limited baselines and ablations — no CNN, simple bag-of-embeddings, BiLSTM sentence encoders, or embedding ablations to support LSTM claims.
- Weakness: Narrow evaluation scope — only AltLex used; no cross-corpus or even small-sample tests on Causal-TimeBank; assumes gold event spans and known marker, limiting practical applicability.
- Weakness: Writing, referencing, and organization issues; missing per-marker breakdowns for ambiguous markers and inconsistent citations.
- Key requested analyses: Report performance for (a) e1+e2 only, (b) e1+e2 with MASK for l, and (c) l only to quantify marker dependency.
- Key requested analyses: Provide performance on the subset of genuinely ambiguous connectives (per-marker or aggregated).
- Key requested clarifications: Precisely define padding-length procedure, max lengths for e1 and l+e2, truncation/padding rules, and how mean/mode yield integer lengths.
- Key requested clarifications: Report train/dev/test splits, hyperparameter tuning procedure, epochs, batch size, early stopping, and parameter counts.
- Key requested clarifications: State whether B2 was reimplemented or authors’ code used, confirm feature/resource parity and preprocessing comparability.
- Key requested clarifications: Explain OOV handling with GloVe 840B and whether embeddings are fine-tuned during training.
- Request to authors: Release code and scripts for reproducibility.
- Overall assessment: Promising but limited — small improvements over a strong baseline, but methodological ambiguities, missing ablations, and narrow evaluation weaken claims; reviewer leans toward rejection pending requested analyses.
