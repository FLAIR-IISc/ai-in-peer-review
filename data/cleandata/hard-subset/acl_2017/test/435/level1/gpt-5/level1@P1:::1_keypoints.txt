- Paper tackles causal meaning disambiguation for event pairs linked by lexical markers using two-input LSTM encoders classifying Causal vs Non-Causal.
- Experiments on AltLex show the LSTM models outperform a per-marker majority baseline and a feature-rich SVM, setting new SOTA on the non-bootstrapped split.

Main contributions:
- Simple two-input LSTM on pre-trained embeddings matches or outperforms feature-heavy SVMs, new SOTA on non-bootstrapped AltLex.
- Independent “Pair LSTM” encoders work better than “Stated Pair LSTM” that initializes the second LSTM from the first.
- Analysis shows improved handling of ambiguous/distribution-shifted markers and includes a balanced-data experiment with McNemar significance testing.

Strengths:
- Clear motivation: argues contextual semantics are needed beyond fixed lexicons/patterns.
- Competitive results with a resource-light, feature-free model using only GloVe embeddings.
- Useful ablations and negative results comparing architectures, optimizers, and padding strategies.
- Qualitative examples and robustness checks illustrating where the neural model succeeds over SVM.
- Practical portability across domains/languages due to minimal feature engineering.

Weaknesses:
- Limited modeling novelty; architectures are straightforward and lack attention or interaction mechanisms.
- Missing stronger baselines (biLSTM over full span, attention, CNNs, averaged-embedding MLP).
- Methodological details crucial for reproducibility (hyperparameters, pooling, dropout, embedding tuning, OOV handling) are unclear or missing.
- Evaluation limited to AltLex with gold argument spans assumed; no cross-corpus generalization or end-to-end argument detection analysis.
- Metric reporting and significance testing are incomplete (macrо-F1/per-class F1 absent; main splits lack significance tests).
- Claim that contextual encoding is required is not isolated by controls (masking connective, shuffling spans, per-marker baselines).

Questions for authors:
- Clarify the padding strategy and correct Eq. (1): are padded lengths max/mean/mode of inputs and how is truncation handled?
- Provide full LSTM hyperparameters: hidden size, layers, bidirectionality, pooling method, epochs, batch size, learning rates, early stopping, dropout, and embedding fine-tuning.
- How are e1 and e2 spans obtained—are they gold annotations or derived heuristically?
- Please report macro-F1 and per-class F1, run significance tests on main results, and report results for ambiguous markers only.
- How does a simple averaged-embedding concatenation + MLP or CNN baseline perform?

Additional comments:
- Fix typos, notation issues, missing Figure 1, and ambiguous “pad” equation.
- Report whether the model is symmetric or sensitive to e1/e2 order and consider swapping argument experiments.
- Provide a broader per-marker precision/recall breakdown beyond the “which then” example for deeper analysis.
