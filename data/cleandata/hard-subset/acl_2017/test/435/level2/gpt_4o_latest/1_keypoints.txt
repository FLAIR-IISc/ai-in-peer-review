- Proposal of an LSTM-based architecture (Stated Pair LSTM and Pair LSTM) to encode causal context between two events.
- Empirical validation showing improved F1 and accuracy over prior work on the AltLex corpus.
- Analysis demonstrating the model’s ability to generalize beyond lexical markers and syntactic patterns in ambiguous cases.
- Novelty: moves beyond reliance on lexical/syntactic features by using LSTMs to encode event meaning.
- Strong empirical performance with statistically significant gains over baselines, especially on the non-bootstrapped AltLex subset.
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple configurations (padding strategies, optimizers).
- Insightful error analysis highlighting cases where the model succeeds where prior methods fail.
- Limited scope: only addresses causal meaning classification, not causal argument identification.
- Reliance on the AltLex corpus with limited size/coverage, reducing generalizability.
- Insufficient discussion of model limitations (e.g., potential overfitting to frequent lexical markers, handling of rare/ambiguous expressions).
- Narrow comparison set: mainly compared to Hidey & McKeown (2016) and simple baselines, lacking comparisons to other neural architectures (e.g., transformers) or transfer learning approaches.
- Question: How does the system perform on other datasets, domains, or multilingual corpora?
- Question: Can the architecture be extended to causal argument identification, and what modifications would that require?
- Question: How does the model handle implicit causal relations without lexical markers, and where does it fail?
- Conclusion: A novel and effective approach with compelling results, but limited generalizability; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address limitations and clarify future work.
