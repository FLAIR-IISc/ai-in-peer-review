- Neural Architecture for Causal Disambiguation: Applies LSTMs to encode sentence meaning for causal relation classification, framing the task as meaning encoding.  
- Empirical Evaluation on AltLex Corpus: Evaluates the approach on the AltLex corpus, demonstrating performance on non-standard causal expressions.  
- Analysis of Limitations of Lexical Feature-Based Approaches: Critiques lexical/syntactic resource-based methods and motivates a context-aware neural approach.  

- Clear Motivation and Problem Definition: Well-motivated problem statement and justification for neural methods.  
- Solid Experimental Setup: Reasonable baseline comparisons, exploration of LSTM configurations/optimizers, and use of two AltLex versions.  
- Insightful Analysis: Discusses baseline limitations, ambiguous connectives, and corpus class-distribution effects.  
- Well-Written and Organized: Paper is generally clear and easy to follow.  

- Limited Novelty of the Core Method: LSTM architecture is standard with no major novel modifications.  
- Insufficient Ablation Study: Lacks thorough ablations (e.g., embedding sizes, marker-event concatenation effects).  
- Limited Discussion of Error Analysis: Needs deeper analysis of failure cases to guide improvements.  
- Overstated Claims of Outperforming SOTA: Reported improvements are modest and SOTA comparison is not fully substantiated.  
- Lack of Comparison to Simpler Baselines: Missing simple baselines (e.g., bag-of-words or TF-IDF) for context.
