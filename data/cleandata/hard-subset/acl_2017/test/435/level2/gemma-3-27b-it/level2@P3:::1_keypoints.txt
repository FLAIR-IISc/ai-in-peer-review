- Task & contribution: Proposes an LSTM-based model for contextual causal relation classification, achieves state-of-the-art on the AltLex corpus and analyzes AltLex’s challenges.
- Strength — Strong empirical results: Model outperforms baselines and prior SOTA on AltLex with clear Precision/Recall/F1/Accuracy metrics.
- Strength — Well-motivated approach: Argues against reliance on syntactic rules/lexical resources and justifies a context-aware neural method with linguistic references.
- Strength — Comprehensive related work: Thorough literature review positioning the work within prior rule-based and ML approaches.
- Strength — Thoughtful dataset analysis: Examines class distribution, ambiguous markers, and experiments with padding/optimizer choices.
- Weakness — Limited evaluation scope: Results are reported only on AltLex, raising questions about generalizability to other datasets or splits.
- Weakness — Missing architecture details: Key hyperparameters (LSTM size, dense layers, dropout, optimizer) are not fully specified, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness — Insufficient error analysis: Lacks in-depth examination of error types and the linguistic phenomena causing failures.
- Weakness — Brief counterfactual discussion: Limited treatment of counterfactual reasoning and its role in defining causality.
- Question — Model specification: Please provide detailed hyperparameters (LSTM hidden size, dense layers, dropout, optimizer).
- Question — External evaluation: Have you tried other causality datasets or alternative AltLex splits, and what are the challenges?
- Question — Error analysis request: Can you provide a finer-grained analysis of model errors and challenging linguistic cases?
- Question — Counterfactual rationale: Why exclude or not explicitly incorporate counterfactual constraints in your causality definition?
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written with effective tables/figures; consider discussing potential biases in the AltLex corpus.
