- Primary contribution: Empirically shows a simple LSTM architecture outperforms prior state-of-the-art on the AltLex causal relation corpus.  
- Secondary contribution: Provides thorough experimental analysis and ablation studies over LSTM configs, padding strategies, and optimizers.  
- Motivation: Addresses causal relation classification and highlights AltLex as important for coverage beyond syntactic/lexical methods.  
- Strength — Strong results: Substantial F1 improvements over Hidey & McKeown (2016).  
- Strength — Well-designed experiments: Systematic exploration of model variants and reduced-corpus analysis.  
- Strength — Task relevance: Causal relation classification is important for downstream NLP applications.  
- Strength — Clear writing: Problem, method, and experiments are clearly described with helpful examples.  
- Weakness — Limited novelty: The LSTM architecture is standard and lacks methodological innovation.  
- Weakness — Insufficient error analysis: Paper lacks detailed qualitative analysis of misclassifications and failure modes.  
- Weakness — Embedding dependency: Heavy reliance on GloVe without exploring other embeddings or fine-tuning.  
- Weakness — Limited baselines: No comparison to stronger recent models like BERT/RoBERTa-based systems.
