- Proposes a two-input LSTM neural architecture (event1 and lexical-marker+event2) for disambiguating causal relations and reports SOTA on the AltLex corpus.
- Contribution: Introduces a two-input model that encodes relation context explicitly.
- Contribution: Uses LSTM RNNs to capture sequential information, argued superior to CNNs for this task.
- Contribution: Evaluates the approach on the publicly available AltLex causality corpus.
- Strength: Outperforms prior work on the challenging, low-resource AltLex dataset.
- Strength: LSTM encoding is an appropriate choice to model sequential causality signals.
- Strength: Using a public corpus improves reproducibility and comparability.
- Weakness: Evaluation is confined to AltLex and may not generalize to other causal expression types.
- Weakness: Performance reporting uses only Precision/Recall/F1, possibly missing other evaluation aspects.
- Weakness: Model may struggle with out-of-vocabulary words and unseen causal expressions.
- Question: How does the system handle OOV words and unseen causal expressions?
- Question: Can the approach generalize to other causal expression types, languages, or domains?
- Question: How does the system compare to other SOTA methods on different causality corpora?
- Additional comment: Paper is clear and well-structured but would benefit from broader evaluations and more robust OOV handling.
