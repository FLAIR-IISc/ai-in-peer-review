**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically focusing on the disambiguation of causal lexical markers based on context. The authors argue that the encoding of the meaning of a sentence is required for the disambiguation of its causal meaning. They evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus and report state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel neural network architecture**: The authors propose a neural network architecture with two inputs, one for the first event and the other for the lexical marker and the second event. This architecture is designed to encode the context of the causal relation and disambiguate its causal meaning.
2. **Evaluation on the AltLex corpus**: The authors evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which is a challenging dataset for causality classification. They report state-of-the-art results, outperforming the previous best system by 2.13% in terms of F1 score.
3. **Analysis of the results**: The authors provide an in-depth analysis of the results, including an examination of the performance of their system on different types of causal expressions and a comparison with the previous best system.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective neural network architecture**: The authors' neural network architecture is effective in encoding the context of the causal relation and disambiguating its causal meaning.
2. **State-of-the-art results**: The authors report state-of-the-art results on the AltLex corpus, demonstrating the effectiveness of their system.
3. **In-depth analysis**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of the results, which helps to understand the strengths and weaknesses of their system.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited evaluation**: The authors only evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which may not be representative of all types of causal expressions.
2. **Dependence on word embeddings**: The authors' system relies on pre-trained word embeddings, which may not always capture the nuances of causal expressions.
3. **Limited analysis of errors**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the errors made by their system, which could help to identify areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. **How does the system perform on other datasets**: Have the authors evaluated their system on other datasets, such as the Penn Discourse Tree Bank or the Causal-TimeBank?
2. **How does the system handle out-of-vocabulary words**: How does the system handle words that are not present in the pre-trained word embeddings?
3. **Can the system be applied to other tasks**: Can the authors' neural network architecture be applied to other tasks, such as temporal relation extraction or sentiment analysis?
