- Paper proposes a neural network for causality classification that disambiguates the causal meaning of two events using context encoding and is evaluated on the AltLex corpus.  
- Architecture uses two inputs (first event; lexical marker + second event) with LSTM encoders and several dense tanh layers.  
- System evaluated on the freely available AltLex corpus.  
- Reported to outperform Hidey & McKeown (2016) in precision and accuracy.  
- Strength: Effective use of LSTM to encode event context.  
- Strength: Good performance on the challenging AltLex dataset.  
- Strength: Thorough comparison with prior state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of results, reducing insight into system behavior.  
- Weakness: No error analysis provided to characterize mistakes.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of future work and extensions.  
- Question: Request for more architectural details (number of layers, units per layer).  
- Question: Ask how hyperparameters were selected and their impact on performance.  
- Question: Request a breakdown/analysis of error types in results.  
- Question: Ask how the approach could be extended to handle complex causal relationships (multiple events/entities).
