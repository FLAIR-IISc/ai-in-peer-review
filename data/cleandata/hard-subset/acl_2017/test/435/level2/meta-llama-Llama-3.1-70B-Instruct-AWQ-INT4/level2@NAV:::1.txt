**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically for disambiguating the causal meaning of a relation between two events. The authors argue that the encoding of the context is required for a suitable disambiguation of causality. They use a neural network with two inputs, one for the first event and the other for the lexical marker and the second event. The proposed system outperforms the state-of-the-art on the AltLex corpus, which is a freely available corpus for causality classification.

**Main Contributions**

1. A neural network architecture for causality classification that uses two inputs to encode the context of the relation.
2. The use of LSTM RNN to encode the sequential information of the input text, which is more suitable for the task of causality classification than CNN.
3. The evaluation of the proposed system on the AltLex corpus, which is a freely available corpus for causality classification.

**Strengths**

1. The proposed system outperforms the state-of-the-art on the AltLex corpus, which is a challenging task due to the lack of resources and the complexity of causality classification.
2. The use of LSTM RNN to encode the sequential information of the input text is a suitable choice for the task of causality classification.
3. The evaluation of the proposed system on a freely available corpus makes it easier to compare and reproduce the results.

**Weaknesses**

1. The proposed system is limited to the AltLex corpus, which may not be representative of all types of causal expressions.
2. The evaluation of the proposed system is limited to the performance metrics of Precision, Recall, and F1, which may not capture all aspects of the task.
3. The proposed system may not be able to handle out-of-vocabulary words or unseen causal expressions.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How does the proposed system handle out-of-vocabulary words or unseen causal expressions?
2. Can the proposed system be applied to other types of causal expressions, such as those found in other languages or domains?
3. How does the proposed system compare to other state-of-the-art systems on other corpora for causality classification?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a clear and well-structured presentation of the proposed system and its evaluation on the AltLex corpus. The use of LSTM RNN to encode the sequential information of the input text is a suitable choice for the task of causality classification. However, the proposed system may benefit from additional evaluation on other corpora and the inclusion of more robust handling of out-of-vocabulary words and unseen causal expressions.
