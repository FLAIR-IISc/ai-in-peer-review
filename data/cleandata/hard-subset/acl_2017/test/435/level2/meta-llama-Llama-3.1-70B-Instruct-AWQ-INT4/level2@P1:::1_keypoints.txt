- Summary: Proposes a neural network to disambiguate causal lexical markers by encoding sentence meaning; evaluated on AltLex with reported state-of-the-art performance.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a two-input neural architecture (first event; lexical marker + second event) to encode causal context and disambiguate causal meaning.

- Contribution 2: Evaluates the model on the challenging AltLex corpus and reports a 2.13% F1 improvement over prior best.

- Contribution 3: Provides in-depth analysis of performance across different types of causal expressions and compares to the previous best system.

- Strength 1: The proposed architecture effectively encodes causal context and disambiguates causal markers.

- Strength 2: Achieves state-of-the-art results on the AltLex dataset.

- Strength 3: Offers a thorough analysis that highlights system strengths and weaknesses.

- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to AltLex and may not generalize to other types of causal expressions or datasets.

- Weakness 2: The approach depends on pre-trained word embeddings, which may not capture causal nuances.

- Weakness 3: Lacks a detailed error analysis that could guide further improvements.

- Question 1: Has the system been evaluated on other datasets (e.g., PDTB, Causal-TimeBank)?

- Question 2: How are out-of-vocabulary words handled when absent from pre-trained embeddings?

- Question 3: Can the architecture generalize to other tasks like temporal relation extraction or sentiment analysis?
