**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically for disambiguating the causal meaning of two events. The authors argue that the encoding of the context is required for a suitable disambiguation of causality. They propose a system with two inputs, one for the first event and the other for the lexical marker and the second event, and evaluate its performance on the AltLex corpus.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neural Network Architecture**: The authors propose a neural network architecture with two inputs, which is mainly based on the encoding of the two inputs with an LSTM and the use of several dense layers with a tanh activation function.
2. **Evaluation on AltLex Corpus**: The authors evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which is a freely available corpus for causality classification.
3. **Comparison with State-of-the-Art**: The authors compare their system with the state-of-the-art system proposed by Hidey and McKeown (2016) and show that their system outperforms it in terms of precision and accuracy.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Use of LSTM**: The authors effectively use LSTM to encode the context of the two events, which is a key contribution of the paper.
2. **Good Performance on AltLex Corpus**: The authors show that their system performs well on the AltLex corpus, which is a challenging dataset for causality classification.
3. **Comparison with State-of-the-Art**: The authors provide a thorough comparison with the state-of-the-art system, which is a strength of the paper.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Analysis of Results**: The authors provide limited analysis of their results, which makes it difficult to understand the strengths and weaknesses of their system.
2. **No Error Analysis**: The authors do not provide an error analysis, which would have been helpful in understanding the types of errors made by their system.
3. **Limited Discussion of Future Work**: The authors provide limited discussion of future work, which is an important aspect of any research paper.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the architecture of your system, including the number of layers and the number of units in each layer?
