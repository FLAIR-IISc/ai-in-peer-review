- Proposes a neural network with two inputs (first event; lexical marker + second event) to disambiguate causal meaning of lexical markers in context.
- Evaluated on the AltLex corpus of implicit causal relations and reports outperforming the state-of-the-art.
- Provides analysis indicating that encoding sentence/context meaning is important for disambiguating markers.
- Clear and well-motivated proposal for causality classification.
- Thorough evaluation including comparison to prior work and result analysis.
- Highlights context encoding as a key contribution to the field.
- Heavy reliance on the AltLex corpus, which may not represent all types of causal relations.
- Missing a detailed error analysis that could reveal common failure modes and challenges.
- Limited discussion of broader implications and potential applications of the system.
- Question: How will the authors address overfitting given the relatively small AltLex corpus?
- Question: Can the authors provide more detailed error analysis, including common error types and causes?
- Question: How do the authors envision practical uses and applications for their proposed system?
