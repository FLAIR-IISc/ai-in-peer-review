- Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture for causality classification that encodes both events and the lexical marker; evaluated on the AltLex corpus and outperforms prior work.

- Architecture: two-input model (event1; lexical marker + event2) using LSTM encoders to capture sequential information.

- Encoding causal meaning: shows encoding both events’ meanings is necessary to disambiguate causal relations with ambiguous markers.

- Empirical result: reports state-of-the-art performance on AltLex, improving previous F1 by 2.13%.

- Strength: effectively uses LSTMs to model sequential text information for causality detection.

- Strength: demonstrates robustness to ambiguity in lexical markers.

- Strength: achieves strong empirical gains on the benchmark corpus.

- Weakness: evaluation is limited to the AltLex corpus and may not generalize to other types of causal relations.

- Weakness: lacks interpretability or analysis explaining why the model works.

- Weakness: depends on pre-trained word embeddings that may not capture causal nuances.

- Question: How will the system be extended to handle more complex causal relations involving multiple events or entities?

- Question: Can the authors provide analyses (e.g., of learned representations or attention) to explain model effectiveness?

- Question: How do the authors plan to address reliance on pre-trained embeddings (e.g., advanced embeddings or training domain-specific embeddings)?
