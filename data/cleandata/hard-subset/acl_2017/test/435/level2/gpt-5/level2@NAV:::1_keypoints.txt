- Proposes an LSTM-based end-to-end model that encodes event context to disambiguate causal relations in AltLex, claiming improvements over feature-engineered methods.
- Contribution: a two-input LSTM architecture that reduces reliance on handcrafted features for causal disambiguation.
- Contribution: an empirical study on AltLex showing consistent improvements over strong baselines and analysis under class imbalance.
- Empirical gains: reported increases in Causal F1 and Accuracy on both non-bootstrapped and bootstrapped AltLex versus prior work.
- Dataset framing: argues fixed lexical/syntactic patterns are insufficient and analyzes marker ambiguity and distribution shifts from bootstrapping.
- Modeling choices: uses a simple architecture with pretrained GloVe, compares two-input variants, optimizers, and padding strategies; finds independent encodings (Pair LSTM) outperform state-transfer.
- Significance testing: provides preliminary McNemar tests showing significant gains over the frequency baseline in a reduced balanced setting.
- Limited novelty: architecture (parallel LSTMs + dense layers) is standard with minimal architectural innovation.
- Narrow evaluation: experiments are confined to AltLex with no cross-corpus validation or transfer tests.
- Incomplete ablations: unclear how much signal comes from the lexical marker vs. event context; no marker-masking, e1/e2 swap, or context-window analyses.
- Reproducibility concerns: preprocessing, event boundary extraction, truncation/padding lengths, training schedules, hyperparameters, and significance vs. SVM are not fully specified.
- Requested: report significance versus the SVM baseline on both corpus versions.
- Requested: evaluate performance when the lexical marker is masked/removed and when e1/e2 are swapped.
- Requested: specify maximum sequence lengths and truncation/padding policies for the two inputs.
- Requested: evaluate on additional corpora (e.g., PDTB, Causal-TimeBank) to test cross-dataset robustness.
