- Task: Classify whether an expression between two events signals a causal relation (causal meaning disambiguation).
- Proposed method: LSTM-based architecture with two inputs: e1 and (marker l concatenated with e2), emphasizing context encoding.
- Evaluation: Tested on AltLex corpus vs. per-connector majority baseline and a feature-rich SVM, reporting consistent F1 and accuracy gains on original and bootstrapped splits.
- Contribution 1: Simple neural architecture reducing reliance on hand-engineered features and lexicons for causal disambiguation.
- Contribution 2: Empirical study on AltLex with analysis of ambiguous markers and error cases.
- Contribution 3: Clear problem framing separating causal meaning classification from argument identification.
- Strength: Empirical improvements over strong baselines (+1.6 F1 over SVM on both splits; larger accuracy gains on non-bootstrapped split).
- Strength: Context-focused modeling is well-motivated and successful on examples where markers are ambiguous.
- Strength: Careful comparison to a strong per-connector majority baseline, showing the model learns beyond connector identity.
- Strength: Useful analysis of optimizer and pooling choices, showing practitioner-relevant trade-offs (e.g., Adam vs. Adadelta).
- Strength: Sensible problem decomposition clarifying scope of the task.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—standard LSTM architecture and no comparison to simpler neural baselines or adequate ablations.
- Weakness: Method description and reproducibility issues—confusing sequence-length handling, unclear role of pooling vs. LSTM, and missing training details (epochs, batch size, embedding tuning, seeds).
- Weakness: Evaluation scope limited to AltLex; lacks tests on other datasets (PDTB causal, Causal-TimeBank), and missing metrics (macro F1, per-connector averages) and full significance reporting.
- Weakness: Task framing overclaims generality since inputs require an explicit marker l, excluding many implicit causal relations.
- Weakness: Dataset presentation issues—table inconsistencies, unclear train/test split protocol, and unspecified hyperparameter tuning/dev usage.
- Question: Clarify architecture pipeline—where are max/mean/mode applied, how LSTMs are used, and provide tensor-shape schematic.
- Question: Provide full hyperparameters and training details (batch size, epochs, early stopping, learning rates, weight decay, embedding fine-tuning, OOV handling, seed variance).
- Question: Specify data splits and tuning protocol, report significance for main results and averages over runs.
- Question: Provide ablations/controls: full-sentence encoder with marker indicator, inputs ablations (e1 only, l+e2 only), LSTM vs. average/CNN, and masking connector identity.
- Question: Assess generalization on other causal resources or via unseen-connector evaluation and report per-connector performance.
- Question: Will code, trained models, and preprocessing scripts (event and marker extraction) be released to enable replication?
- Recommendation: Strengthen methodological clarity, add ablations and broader evaluations, and calibrate claims to the restricted experimental scope.
