- Task & model: Paper tackles causal meaning disambiguation for (e1, e2, marker l) using a two-input LSTM that encodes e1 and (l+e2) with dense interaction layers for classification.
- Empirical result: Consistent improvements over a majority-by-marker baseline and the feature-rich SVM of Hidey & McKeown (2016) on both AltLex splits.
- Model variants & analysis: Compares Pair LSTM vs. Stated Pair LSTM, optimizer and length-handling choices, and provides qualitative analysis plus a significance test under a balanced setting.
- Strength — empirical gains: Best config exceeds prior SVM by +2.13 F1 (Causal) and +7.72% accuracy (non-bootstrapping), with modest gains on the bootstrapped split.
- Strength — resource-light: Uses only pre-trained GloVe embeddings (no WordNet/VerbNet/PropBank) yet matches or surpasses a feature-rich system.
- Strength — experimental breadth: Evaluations across two corpus versions, multiple variants, optimizers, length settings, and a downsampled test with McNemar’s test.
- Strength — ambiguity analysis: Provides useful discussion of ambiguous markers and cases where context helps (e.g., “which then”, polysemous causative verbs).
- Strength — task framing: Sensible separation of causality classification from argument identification for practical pipelines.
- Weakness — unclear model description: Padding/truncation and equations (padding as max/mean/mode, shapes, dense layer vectorization) are underspecified and harm reproducibility.
- Weakness — missing simple neural baselines: No ablations against avg-embeddings+MLP, full-sentence BiLSTM, or CNN baselines to justify architectural choices.
- Weakness — limited external validation: All experiments are on AltLex; no transfer/zero-shot tests to other corpora (e.g., Causal-TimeBank) or end-to-end evaluation with predicted spans/markers.
- Weakness — reporting under imbalance: Metrics focus on Causal class and accuracy; missing macro-F1, per-class P/R, confusion matrices, calibration, and main-test significance tests.
- Weakness — over-interpretation: The negative Stated Pair LSTM result is overgeneralized; poorer performance may stem from optimization or capacity issues rather than absence of event-event dependencies.
- Questions — padding/truncation: Clarify how max/mean/mode settings determine sequence lengths, truncation/padding strategy, and precise shapes in Equations (1)-(2).
- Questions — LSTM specifics: Report hidden sizes, layers, directionality, normalization/dropout, OOV handling, and casing treatment.
- Questions — inputs/context: Specify whether inputs include only annotated spans or sentence context, and what is provided when l is absent.
- Questions — baselines: Provide performance of bag-of-embeddings+MLP and any CNN baselines on the same inputs.
- Questions — generalization: Any preliminary results on Causal-TimeBank or transfer/few-shot evaluations to support broader applicability?
- Suggestions — reproducibility: Clarify/repair model spec, include full hyperparameters, and release code if possible.
- Suggestions — ablations & metrics: Add simple neural baselines (avg-embeddings+MLP, CNN, BiLSTM), report macro-F1/per-class metrics/confusion matrices, and run significance tests on main sets.
- Suggestions — broader evaluation: Test at least one additional corpus or a controlled synthetic/held-out subset targeting ambiguous markers to isolate contextual gains.
- Suggestions — deeper error analysis: Categorize failure modes (temporal vs. causal, scope, anaphora) and sensitivity to marker presence/absence.
- Minor fixes: Correct typos/formatting, table numbering, and standardize/complete references.
- Overall assessment: Promising, resource-light approach with solid AltLex gains and useful analysis, but needs clearer specification, stronger simple-neural ablations, and wider evaluation to be a robust contribution.
