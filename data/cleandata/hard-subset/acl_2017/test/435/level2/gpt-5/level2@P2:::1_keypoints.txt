- Task: Disambiguate whether a lexical marker between two events indicates a causal relation using contextual encoding of the events.
- Contribution 1: Two-input LSTM model encoding e1 and (l + e2), concatenated and fed to dense layers for binary classification.
- Contribution 2: Empirical evaluation on AltLex (bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped) showing improvements over a connective-majority baseline and Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM.
- Contribution 3: Preliminary analysis showing the model can use context to correctly classify ambiguous markers and causative verbs.
- Strength: Empirical gains on a relevant benchmark, with modest F1 and notable accuracy improvements over prior work on the non-bootstrapped set.
- Strength: Clear focus on contextual disambiguation with convincing example-based analysis.
- Strength: Task framing distinguishes causal meaning classification from argument identification and motivates the approach.
- Strength: Model simplicity and portability, relying only on pre-trained embeddings and standard LSTM components.
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues and underspecified architecture (unclear padding/pooling, LSTM details, hyperparameters, training protocol).
- Weakness: Evaluation methodology unclear (no explicit dev set, unclear tuning), no variance across random seeds reported.
- Weakness: Limited statistical testing—significance tested only against the baseline B1 and not against B2 where SOTA claims are made.
- Weakness: Metrics focus on Causal F1 and accuracy; macro-F1 and per-class metrics are missing given class imbalance.
- Weakness: Missing baselines and ablations (e.g., MLP on averaged embeddings, BiLSTM over full sentence, ablation removing l).
- Weakness: Unclear how e1/e2/l spans are obtained from AltLex and whether span extraction noise affects results.
- Weakness: Writing and reference issues; tables/figures have formatting problems and missing numbers.
- Question: How are e1 and e2 spans obtained—gold spans or extracted via heuristics, and what is the error rate?
- Question: Clarify the “zero-padding” vs pooling operations and exactly what representation is passed to dense layers.
- Question: Were GloVe embeddings fine-tuned; what were LSTM sizes, layers, dropout, batch size, epochs, early stopping, and was a dev set used?
- Question: What is performance when excluding lexical marker tokens from input, or when encoding only the marker/local context?
- Question: Were unseen or ambiguous markers controlled for at test time, and can per-marker breakdowns be provided?
- Question: Are improvements over B2 statistically significant; please report significance and confidence intervals across seeds.
- Suggestion: Report macro-F1, per-class F1, confusion matrices, and PR curves for the Causal class.
- Suggestion: Add stronger baselines and ablations (remove l; full sentence encoding vs segmented events).
- Suggestion: Provide results specifically on ambiguous markers to support the main claim.
- Suggestion: Clarify dataset splits and ensure model selection uses a dev set, not the test set.
- Suggestion: Clean up tables/figures, complete references, and release code/preprocessing for reproducibility.
- Overall: Promising simple neural approach on an important problem, but method description and evaluation require tightening to substantiate claims and enable replication.
