- Paper proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture to disambiguate causal meaning in sentences and claims it outperforms the state-of-the-art on the AltLex corpus.

- Main contribution 1: thorough analysis of the AltLex corpus focusing on ambiguous causal markers and their contextual dependence.

- Main contribution 2: an LSTM-based sentence-pair architecture for causal meaning disambiguation that outperforms baseline systems on AltLex.

- Strength 1: correctly identifies contextual dependence of causal markers (e.g., "since") and uses AltLex appropriately to study this issue.

- Strength 2: valid critique of lexical-resource-based approaches (WordNet/VerbNet) and a more general neural approach using word embeddings.

- Strength 3: experiments show significantly better precision for the causal class compared to majority baseline (B1) and Hidey & McKeown (B2).

- Weakness 1: central claim that encoding sentence meaning is required is not sufficiently substantiated and not clearly differentiated from prior context-based work (Do et al., Riaz & Girju).

- Weakness 2: comparison to state-of-the-art is inadequate—only compared to Hidey & McKeown (2016) and not to more recent neural methods (e.g., Kruengkrai et al. 2017).

- Weakness 3: class imbalance in AltLex (Causal much smaller than Non-Causal) is not adequately addressed or analyzed for its effect on results.

- Weakness 4: proposed "Pair LSTM" architectures are standard and not novel; paper lacks strong justification for architecture choices.

- Weakness 5: evaluation is limited to AltLex, so generalizability to other corpora or broader causality tasks is not demonstrated.

- Question 1: Have you compared your system to other recent neural approaches for causality classification?

- Question 2: How does your system handle bootstrapped AltLex instances with differing training/test class distributions, and what is the performance impact?

- Question 3: Can you clarify how your neural approach is fundamentally different from previous context-based methods that also encode surrounding context?
