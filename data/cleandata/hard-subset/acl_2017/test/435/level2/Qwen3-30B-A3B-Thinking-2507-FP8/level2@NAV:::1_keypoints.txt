- Proposes an LSTM-based architecture that encodes cause and effect events plus the lexical marker to disambiguate causal meaning using context.
- Claims three contributions: novel LSTM architecture, empirical evidence that context encoding outperforms lexical/syntactic features, and application to the Ambiguous AltLex corpus.
- Correctly identifies prior reliance on specific syntactic constructions and lexical resources as a key limitation and proposes a more generalizable approach.
- Experimental results are strong: the "Pair LSTM Mean Adam" variant improves F1 by 2.13% over prior state-of-the-art on the non-bootstrapping corpus.
- Demonstrates ability to disambiguate ambiguous markers (e.g., "which then") where previous methods fail.
- Thoughtful discussion of corpus limitations and class imbalance issues.
- Does not sufficiently address that the AltLex corpus is small (~20k examples) and may be inadequate for robust neural training.
- Lacks critical architecture/hyperparameter details (exact LSTM cell size, embedding dimensions), hindering reproducibility.
- Novelty claim is overstated; the approach is not clearly distinguished from prior LSTM-based context encoding (Melamud et al., 2016).
- Fails to analyze why the "Pair LSTM" (without event connection) outperforms the "State Pair LSTM" variant.
- Open question: how does performance scale with larger datasets?
- Open question: what specific contextual features does the model learn to separate causal from non-causal relations?
- Open question: can the model be adapted to the harder causal argument identification subtask?
