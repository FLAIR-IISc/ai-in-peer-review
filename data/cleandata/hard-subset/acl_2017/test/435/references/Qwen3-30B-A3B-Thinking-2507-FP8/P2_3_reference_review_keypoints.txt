- Task: Disambiguate whether a lexical marker (e.g., "because", "since") signals a causal relation between two events in context.  
- Proposal: Neural model using two LSTMs to encode cause and effect plus the lexical marker, evaluated on the AltLex corpus.  
- Contribution: Effective use of the AltLex corpus for context-dependent causal disambiguation.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates neural context encoding can disambiguate causal markers without syntactic rules or lexical resources.  
- Contribution: Thorough empirical evaluation across model configurations with clear gains over strong baselines.  
- Strength: Addresses a meaningful linguistic problem—context-dependent ambiguity of causal markers—with solid theoretical grounding.  
- Strength: Appropriate and comprehensive use of both versions of the AltLex corpus (non-bootstrapped and bootstrapped).  
- Strength: Detailed metrics (precision/recall/F1/accuracy) and analysis showing improved balance and handling of ambiguous markers.  
- Weakness: Model architecture (two LSTMs + dense layers) lacks novelty and isn't justified against simpler alternatives.  
- Weakness: Narrow task scope—only binary causal vs. non-causal classification; does not tackle causal argument identification.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of failure cases and limited examination of why the model misclassifies ambiguous markers.  
- Question: How does the architecture compare to a simpler single-LSTM on concatenated input?  
- Question: How would the model handle implicit causal relations with no lexical marker?  
- Question: How much performance gain is due to pre-trained GloVe embeddings versus the neural architecture itself?  
- Overall: Solid empirical contribution with well-supported claims; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty, scope, and failure analysis.
