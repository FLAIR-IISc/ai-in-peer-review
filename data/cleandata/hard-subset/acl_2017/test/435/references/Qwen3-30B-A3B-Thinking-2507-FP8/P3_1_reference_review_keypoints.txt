- Paper addresses causal meaning disambiguation of lexical markers (e.g., "because", "since") using LSTMs to encode context and evaluates on the AltLex corpus claiming improved performance.

- Contribution: demonstrates context-based causal disambiguation using neural networks.

- Contribution: clarifies distinction between causal meaning disambiguation and causal argument identification.

- Contribution: provides practical evaluation on the AltLex corpus to address limitations of prior work.

- Strength: strong theoretical motivation arguing causality is context-dependent and not reducible to syntactic patterns, with convincing ambiguous-marker examples.

- Strength: appropriate methodological choice (LSTMs over CNNs) and thorough hyperparameter/strategy comparisons (padding strategies, optimizers).

- Strength: significant empirical gains (Pair LSTM Mean Adam beats SOTA by +2.13% F1 on Causal and +7.72% accuracy on non-bootstrapping AltLex) and corrected ambiguous examples.

- Strength: clear task definition that separates meaning disambiguation from argument identification.

- Weakness: novelty is overstated—similar neural approaches (Kruengkrai et al. 2017; Melamud et al. 2016) are not adequately compared or differentiated.

- Weakness: misrepresents prior reliance on lexical resources while using GloVe embeddings, failing to explain how embeddings differ from criticized resources.

- Weakness: insufficient error analysis and lack of systematic examination of failure cases (e.g., multiple markers, complex discourse).

- Weakness: corpus limitations (bootstrapping-induced train-test class shifts) are acknowledged but not adequately addressed, raising concerns about robustness vs. dataset leakage.

- Question: how does the LSTM approach specifically differ from and improve upon Kruengkrai et al. (2017)?

- Question: how do you reconcile criticizing lexical-resource reliance while using GloVe embeddings derived from lexical data?

- Question: how was the severe class imbalance in AltLex handled and what effect did it have on results?

- Question: how does the model distinguish causal vs. temporal interpretations for ambiguous markers like "after"?

- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated contribution showing context matters for causal disambiguation with convincing results, but novelty and dataset issues need clearer treatment.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing novelty claims and corpus limitations.
