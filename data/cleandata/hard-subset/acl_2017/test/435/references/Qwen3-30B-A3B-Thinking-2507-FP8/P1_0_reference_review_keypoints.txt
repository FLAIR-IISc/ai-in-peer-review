1. Paper addresses causal meaning disambiguation for lexical markers using an LSTM-based context encoder evaluated on the AltLex corpus, claiming improved performance over prior baselines.  
2. Contribution: Shows neural context encoding can disambiguate causal meaning without relying on lexical resources.  
3. Contribution: Applies and evaluates a novel approach on the diverse AltLex corpus.  
4. Contribution: Provides detailed examples where the model disambiguates ambiguous markers (e.g., "which then").  
5. Strength: Strong theoretical grounding in linguistic theories of causation and limits of syntactic-pattern approaches.  
6. Strength: Clear empirical validation with thorough AltLex evaluation and case analyses.  
7. Strength: Practical contribution by using and promoting the AltLex resource for causal relation research.  
8. Weakness: Novelty is overstated—prior neural work (Kruengkrai et al. 2017) is not adequately distinguished.  
9. Weakness: Baseline comparisons are weak (majority baseline and older SOTA only); lacks comparison to recent neural models.  
10. Weakness: Method requires pre-identified events but paper does not discuss integration into a full extraction pipeline.  
11. Weakness: Insufficient analysis of model behavior and what contextual information drives decisions.  
12. Weakness: Evaluation limited to Wikipedia-based AltLex; unclear cross-domain or cross-lingual generalizability.  
13. Question: How does the approach compare to more recent neural architectures (e.g., transformers)?  
14. Question: How would the system handle or be extended to the event-identification step?  
15. Question: How would the model perform on other domains (news, scientific texts) with different causal expressions?  
16. Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—clarify novelty, include stronger baselines, and discuss limitations and integration into pipelines.
