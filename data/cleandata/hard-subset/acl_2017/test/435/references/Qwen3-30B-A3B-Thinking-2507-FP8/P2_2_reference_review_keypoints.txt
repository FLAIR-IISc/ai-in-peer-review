- Paper tackles causality disambiguation for AltLex markers, using an LSTM-based model that encodes context instead of relying on fixed lexical/syntactic resources.
- Main contributions: a neural context-based approach to causality disambiguation, empirical gains over prior baselines on the AltLex corpus, and analysis showing context is crucial for disambiguation.
- Strength: Addresses an important gap by moving beyond lexical/syntactic heuristics to model contextual cues for causality.
- Strength: Evaluation is well-designed, using both bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped AltLex sets and meaningful baselines, with illustrative analyses of ambiguous cases.
- Strength: The Pair LSTM architecture and ablations (padding strategies, optimizers) are well-motivated and empirically justified.
- Strength: Clear demonstrations that context resolves ambiguity for markers like “since” and “after,” with concrete example analyses.
- Weakness: Novelty is overstated because similar neural approaches exist for semantic relation classification and the paper doesn’t sufficiently distinguish its model from prior neural work.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison with contemporary state-of-the-art neural models (e.g., transformer-based approaches) to fully establish significance.
- Weakness: Task definition is ambiguous—unclear whether the task is binary causality detection or also involves identifying cause vs. effect.
- Weakness: Limited analysis of model failures; the paper should provide examples and discussion of when and why the model fails.
- Question: How does the model compare to more recent neural methods for semantic relation classification, such as transformer-based models?
- Question: Does the model implicitly learn syntactic patterns despite the claim that causality lacks fixed syntactic structure, and can the authors provide illustrative examples?
- Question: How would the model handle causal relations that span beyond the two-event input format (e.g., causes mentioned earlier in the paragraph)?
- Overall: Solid paper demonstrating the importance of context and a strong neural approach with convincing results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify task, expand comparisons, and analyze failures.
