- Task: Disambiguating whether relations between events are causal or non-causal, addressing context-dependent causal expressions.
- Contribution 1: Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture that encodes context instead of relying on predefined syntactic or lexical features.
- Contribution 2: Empirically outperforms prior baselines on the AltLex corpus, especially for ambiguous markers like "since" and "after" (≈ +2.13% F1).
- Contribution 3: Provides detailed analysis of model behavior on ambiguous expressions that resist simple lexical/syntactic rules.
- Strength 1: Clearly identifies and supports the limitation of prior methods that rely on fixed syntactic constructions or lexical resources.
- Strength 2: Strong empirical results on a challenging AltLex dataset and convincing examples of handling ambiguous markers.
- Strength 3: Well-designed experiments and ablation studies comparing architectures, padding strategies, and baselines with precision/recall analysis.
- Weakness 1: Lacks comparison to recent neural/contextual embedding approaches (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa), weakening the state-of-the-art claim.
- Weakness 2: Analysis of failure cases is superficial; paper omits concrete examples and deeper error analysis.
- Weakness 3: Limited integration with linguistic theory; does not show how model encodings align with human causal cognition.
- Weakness 4: Insufficient discussion of AltLex corpus limitations, bootstrapping biases, and class imbalance effects on results.
- Question 1: Why not use contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT) instead of GloVe given the focus on contextual encoding?
- Question 2: Please provide more details on the bootstrapping process and potential biases introduced in AltLex.
- Question 3: Provide specific examples where training/test class distributions differ in the bootstrapped data and explain model difficulties.
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept — valuable contribution with strong results but needs comparisons to modern neural approaches and deeper theoretical/error analysis.
