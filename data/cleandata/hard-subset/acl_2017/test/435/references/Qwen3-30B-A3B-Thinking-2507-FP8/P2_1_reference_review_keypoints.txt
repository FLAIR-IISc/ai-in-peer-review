- Contribution: Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture for causal meaning disambiguation that avoids lexical resources and encodes context.  
- Contribution: Provides a comprehensive analysis of the AltLex corpus, covering diverse and ambiguous lexical constructions.  
- Contribution: Empirically outperforms prior work (Hidey & McKeown, 2016), achieving ~79.82% F1 (bootstrapped) and 75.40% F1 (non-bootstrapped).  
- Strength: Clearly identifies and motivates the gap that causation depends on context rather than fixed lexical/syntactic cues.  
- Strength: Strong empirical improvement over prior state-of-the-art with illustrative examples where the model succeeds.  
- Strength: Insightful analysis of corpus properties and the impact of bootstrapping/class distribution shifts.  
- Weakness: Neural architecture has limited novelty (Pair LSTM pattern is standard) and lacks justification beyond LSTM-vs-CNN claims.  
- Weakness: No thorough ablation study or comparison to simpler baselines (e.g., averaged embeddings) to isolate architectural gains.  
- Weakness: Missing analysis of model failure cases and limitations across marker types or complex structures.  
- Weakness: Task definition and evaluation are somewhat ambiguous—unclear whether true semantic disambiguation vs. surface association is learned.  
- Question: Request details on the Hidey & McKeown (2016) baseline feature set for clearer comparison.  
- Question: Clarify whether GloVe embeddings were fine-tuned and how out-of-vocabulary words were handled, especially in bootstrapped data.  
- Question: Explain why class imbalance was reduced by a factor of ten and how that decision affected results.  
- Overall: Valuable contribution with strong results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address novelty, ablation, failure analysis, and task clarity.
