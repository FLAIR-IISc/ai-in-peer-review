- Task: Causal meaning disambiguation for event pairs given a potential causal marker in context.  
- Claim: Causality should be learned from context, not fixed syntactic/lexical patterns.  
- Models: Propose LSTM-based architectures comparing a Pair LSTM and a State-initialized Pair LSTM.  
- Data: Evaluated on AltLex corpus using non-bootstrapping and bootstrapping splits.  
- Results: Improvements over majority-per-connective baseline (B1) and competitive or slight gains over SVM+lexicon baseline (B2) in some settings.  
- Contribution 1: Empirical evaluation showing simple neural models perform competitively on AltLex.  
- Contribution 2: Framing that separates causal classification from argument identification and emphasizes contextual semantics.  
- Contribution 3: Qualitative analysis illustrating cases where context disambiguates ambiguous markers.  
- Strength — relevance: Focuses on an important, realistic setting with varied causal expressions beyond rule-based approaches.  
- Strength — empirical gains: Neural models outperform B1 across splits and match or slightly exceed B2, with notable F1/accuracy gains in some settings.  
- Strength — architecture insight: Independent encoders (Pair LSTM) outperform state-initialized LSTM, suggesting late interaction is preferable.  
- Strength — ambiguity analysis: Examples and downsampling show context can override connector priors and maintain better precision-recall than B1.  
- Strength — reduced resources: Model relies only on pre-trained embeddings and supervised data, not heavy lexical resources.  
- Weakness — clarity: Key modeling details (LSTM directionality, representation fed to MLP, padding) are unclear or inconsistent, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness — experimental rigor: No clear development split or tuning protocol; many configurations appear selected on test data; limited significance testing.  
- Weakness — missing ablations: Lacks marker-only, events-only, simple BoW/logistic baselines, and end-to-end BiLSTM over full sequence.  
- Weakness — generalization: No controlled leave-one-connector-out or similar tests to assess handling of unseen/distribution-shifted markers.  
- Weakness — presentation: Typos, corrupted tables, incomplete references, and inconsistent corpus/split reporting reduce readability and trust.  
- Question — model specifics: Are LSTMs uni- or bidirectional and what exact vector(s) are passed to the MLP (final state, pooled, flattened)?  
- Question — padding: How are “mean” and “mode” padding lengths computed and applied; is truncation used and why over standard masking?  
- Question — training/tuning: What dev split, hyperparameters, epochs, batch size, and early stopping criteria were used?  
- Question — inputs: How are e1/e2 spans extracted and is the marker always concatenated with e2; were marker-only or events-only models tried?  
- Question — baselines requested: Report marker-only (one-hot/embedding), BoW logistic regression over e1,l,e2, and single-input BiLSTM results.  
- Question — generalization test: Add leave-one-connector-out evaluation to measure performance on unseen markers.  
- Question — significance: Provide statistical tests vs B2 on full datasets and report mean/std over multiple runs.  
- Additional request: Provide per-connector breakdowns and confusion matrices to show performance on ambiguous vs unambiguous markers.  
- Additional request: Compare optimizers (Adadelta vs Adam) and report stability across runs.  
- Additional request: Clean up tables/references, ensure consistent corpus reporting, and release code/preprocessing scripts.  
- Overall: Promising and relevant work but needs clearer methodology, stronger baselines/ablations, controlled generalization tests, and cleaner presentation.
