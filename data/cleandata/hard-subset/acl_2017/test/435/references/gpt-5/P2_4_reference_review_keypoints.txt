- Proposes causal meaning disambiguation using a two-input LSTM (“Pair LSTM”) encoding e1 and l+e2, with ablations showing independent encoding outperforms state-passing.
- Evaluates on original and bootstrapped AltLex, showing consistent gains over a per-connective majority baseline and an SVM feature-based system.
- Provides limited analysis of pooling/optimizer choices and a downsampling experiment indicating robustness to class imbalance, with a McNemar significance test.

- Addresses an important, under-resourced task of disambiguating causal meaning beyond fixed connective lists and positions it as a pipeline-first step.
- Shows competitive empirical results on AltLex, improving overall accuracy and often F1 for the Causal class versus prior work.
- Relies minimally on lexical resources (only GloVe embeddings), avoiding coverage issues of lexical databases.
- Reports an architectural insight that independent encoding of arguments is preferable to state-passing for this task.
- Includes some robustness analysis (downsampling and statistical testing) to guard against class-imbalance artifacts.

- Novelty is limited and modeling exploration is narrow; missing stronger neural baselines (full-sentence BiLSTM, attention, avg-emb+classifier, context2vec).
- Model description has clarity/correctness issues: “zero-padding” vs pooling confusion, unclear tensor shapes, and whether LSTM outputs are last states or sequences.
- Assumes gold spans (e1, l, e2) without explaining how they are obtained from AltLex, limiting practical applicability and reproducibility.
- Key ablations missing: e1-only, l+e2-only, sentence-only, remove-l tests, embedding ablations, and per-connective analysis for ambiguous markers.
- Experimental reporting gaps: missing hyperparameters, training details, seed variance, dev split usage, and no significance tests for main results; figures/tables and references are sometimes unclear.
- Evaluation limited to AltLex; no cross-dataset tests (e.g., Causal-TimeBank) to assess generalization.

- Request clarification on exact architecture: are LSTM outputs last hidden states or full sequences, where pooling is applied, what “zero-padding strategies” mean, and tensor shapes.
- Ask how e1, l, and e2 spans are obtained from AltLex (provided or heuristically extracted) and request span extraction accuracy if heuristics used.
- Ask for stronger baselines and ablations: full-sentence BiLSTM, averaged-embedding + logistic regression, e1-only/l+e2-only/sentence-only, and no-marker variant.
- Request complete training details: hidden sizes, dropout, batch size, epochs, learning rates, early stopping, embedding fine-tune vs frozen, dev set usage, and variance across seeds.
- Suggest small-scale cross-dataset testing (Causal-TimeBank) or per-connective results, especially for ambiguous markers and train–test connective shifts.
- Ask rationale for concatenating l with e2 only and whether symmetric modeling or attention across encodings was tried.
