- Contribution: Two-input LSTM classifier using GloVe embeddings outperforms feature-based SVM on AltLex for causal vs non-causal marker disambiguation.
- Contribution: “Pair LSTM” (independent encodings for e1 and l+e2) outperforms state-passing LSTM, suggesting late interaction is preferable.
- Contribution: Task framing distinguishes causal meaning disambiguation from argument identification and includes qualitative examples where context-aware models correct feature-based errors.

- Strength: Sensible empirical focus on the AltLex dataset, including analysis of original and bootstrapped versions and their distributional differences.
- Strength: Simple, reproducible model yields non-trivial, consistent improvements in precision and overall accuracy over the SVM baseline.
- Strength: Architectural insight that late fusion beats state-passing is a useful negative result for pairwise encoders.
- Strength: Analysis beyond aggregate metrics (connectives with divergent distributions, ambiguous markers, non-causal uses of causative verbs) supports the context-dependence claim.
- Strength: Related work and motivation argue convincingly that surface syntax/verb-class approaches are insufficient.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — architecture is standard and paper omits engagement with prior neural discourse and event-causality work.
- Weakness: Missing contemporary baselines — no transformer (BERT/RoBERTa) or simple biLSTM/attention comparisons.
- Weakness: Methodological details underspecified (padding strategy, LSTM sizes, layers, bidirectionality, optimizer, batch size, epochs, early stopping, tokenization, OOV handling, seeds).
- Weakness: No clear dev-set/hyperparameter selection protocol or multi-seed variance reporting.
- Weakness: Evaluation may overestimate context modeling because marker identity is included and many markers are near-unambiguous; no controls (masking, ambiguous-only evaluation, per-marker breakdown).
- Weakness: Claims about LSTMs vs CNNs are speculative without direct comparison on this dataset.
- Weakness: Reporting/presentation issues — malformed tables/equations, unclear dataset size reporting, single-run results without confidence intervals, and insufficient justification for reduced-data experiment.

- Question: Clarify “padding by mean/mode”: exact sequence length computation, whether fixed across train/test, and implementation details.
- Question: Provide full hyperparameters (LSTM hidden size, layers, bidirectionality, learning rate, optimizer, batch size, epochs, dropout, seeds) and how they were selected.
- Question: Specify train/dev/test splits for both AltLex versions and whether model selection used the test set.
- Question: Quantify dependence on marker identity: results for ambiguous markers only, masked/replaced marker experiments, and per-marker performance breakdowns.
- Question: Add comparisons to at least one transformer baseline and a simple biLSTM-attention sentence-pair model.

- Overall: Important problem with promising empirical gains, but methodological clarity, missing modern baselines, and lack of controls for marker memorization warrant rejection in current form; revisions suggested above to strengthen the paper.
