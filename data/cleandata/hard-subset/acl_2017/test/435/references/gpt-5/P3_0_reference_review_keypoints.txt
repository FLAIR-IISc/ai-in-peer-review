- Task and approach: causal meaning disambiguation using two LSTM encoders (e1; l+e2) + MLP, evaluated on AltLex.
- Main contribution: simple neural architecture that slightly improves over a strong feature-based SVM baseline on AltLex (original and bootstrapped).
- Main contribution: empirical analysis of precision/recall trade-offs and behavior on ambiguous markers with qualitative examples.
- Main contribution: clear task framing that isolates causal meaning classification from argument identification.
- Strength: emphasizes context encoding over fixed syntactic patterns and shows qualitative disambiguation examples.
- Strength: competitive results — ~1.6–1.7 F1 and ~6 accuracy improvement over SVM on non-bootstrapped set; smaller but positive gains on bootstrapped set.
- Strength: informative architecture comparison (state-passing vs independent encoders) and optimizer analysis (Adam vs Adadelta) affecting precision/recall.
- Strength: attempts to address class imbalance (reduced negative class experiment) and reports significance vs majority baseline.
- Strength: clear scope by using gold e1, l, e2 and avoiding conflation with argument identification.
- Weakness: limited novelty — standard two-LSTM+MLP architecture and small gains.
- Weakness: unclear and internally inconsistent modeling details (padding vs pooling confusion, missing dimensions, hidden sizes, layers, dropout, training details).
- Weakness: narrow/unrealistic evaluation setup — assumes gold arguments/triggers and evaluates only on AltLex with possible construction biases.
- Weakness: incomplete baselines and ablations — lacks simple neural baselines, pairwise interaction models, and ablations isolating contributions of e1, l, e2.
- Weakness: modest gains with no statistical testing vs SVM and no multiple-run variance reporting.
- Weakness: inadequate related work discussion and weak argument against CNNs; lack of engagement with recent neural discourse relation literature.
- Question: clarify architecture details — LSTM over tokens vs pooling before LSTM, pooling type, hidden sizes, layers, dropout, batch size, epochs, early stopping, seeds.
- Question: justify input representation — why use l+e2 rather than full sentence or e1+l+e2, and were other context variants tried?
- Question: provide ablations for only l, only e2, only e1, e1+e2 to test reliance on marker priors vs context.
- Question: report cross-corpus generalization (PDTB explicit, Causal-TimeBank) and train/test transfer experiments.
- Question: provide statistical testing and multiple-run results (mean±std) to validate improvements over SVM.
- Question: describe handling of class imbalance beyond downsampling (class-weighted loss, focal loss) and sensitivity to class distribution.
- Question: commit to releasing code and exact preprocessing details (tokenization, OOV handling with GloVe, max sequence lengths, data splits).
