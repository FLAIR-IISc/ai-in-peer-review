- Proposes two-input LSTM architectures (Pair LSTM and Stated Pair LSTM) to disambiguate causal vs non-causal uses of lexical markers, evaluated on AltLex.  
- Claims modest improvements over a connective-majority baseline and the Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM on AltLex.

- Strength: tackles an important subproblem—disambiguating ambiguous causal markers—to improve discourse parsing coverage.  
- Strength: simple, sensible sequence-pair LSTM formulation that yields consistent improvements over a majority baseline and sometimes over the SVM.  
- Strength: qualitative examples suggest contextual modeling helps beyond lexical priors.  
- Strength: ablations on optimizers and padding choices show attention to optimization effects.  
- Strength: reframes task as “causal meaning classification” separate from argument direction detection—pragmatic decomposition.

- Weakness: limited methodological novelty; model is a standard sequence-pair LSTM without attention or interaction.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to strong modern baselines (BiLSTM+attention, CNN, and especially transformer-based encoders like BERT/roberta).  
- Weakness: empirical claim that LSTMs beat CNNs is unvalidated.  
- Weakness: evaluation confined to AltLex, no tests on PDTB explicit causals, Causal-TimeBank, or cross-dataset transfer.  
- Weakness: development set, early stopping, and random-seed variability are not reported, raising selection/overfitting concerns.  
- Weakness: incomplete hyperparameter and architecture details (hidden sizes, layers, weight sharing, bidirectionality, embedding fine-tuning).  
- Weakness: unusual padding/truncation strategy (max/mean/mode) lacks quantification of truncation rates or information loss.  
- Weakness: statistical testing is limited (only one McNemar test on a downsampled setting); other improvements lack significance testing.  
- Weakness: potential leakage—concatenating the marker with e2 may let the model rely on marker identity rather than context.  
- Weakness: no ablation removing/replacing marker token to measure reliance on arguments.  
- Weakness: no per-connector or per-ambiguity-bin evaluation, calibration, or comprehensive error typology.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—tables inconsistent, missing numbers, formatting errors, incomplete references.  
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—code, preprocessing, and span-extraction from AltLex are underspecified.

- Question: Are the two LSTMs weight-shared or independent, bidirectional, what hidden size, and are embeddings fine-tuned?  
- Question: In Stated Pair LSTM, how is state passed (final hidden only or cell state too), and does biLSTM improve it?  
- Question: How are e1/e2 spans derived from AltLex (contiguous spans, heads+arguments, multi-sentence cases)?  
- Question: Why is the marker concatenated to e2 rather than modeled symmetrically or as a separate input, and have you tried masking/replacing the marker?  
- Question: Clarify the max/mean/mode padding strategy—do mean/mode imply truncation, and what proportion of tokens are truncated?  
- Question: Provide per-marker breakdowns, performance by ambiguity bins, and cases where training majority conflicts with test labels.  
- Question: Did you use a development set for model selection and report mean/std over multiple seeds; provide significance tests for main results.  
- Question: Can you evaluate on PDTB explicit causal relations and/or Causal-TimeBank or run cross-corpus experiments to test generalization?  
- Question: Please include transformer baselines (BERT/roberta fine-tuned on Arg1 [SEP] marker [SEP] Arg2), a BoW/logistic baseline, and a BiLSTM+attention baseline.  
- Question: Instead of downsampling, did you try class-weighted loss or focal loss, and how sensitive are results to class distribution?
