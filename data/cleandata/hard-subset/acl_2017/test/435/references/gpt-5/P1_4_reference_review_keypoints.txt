- Task/addressed: causal meaning disambiguation for event spans (e1, e2) with a lexical marker l using Pair LSTM and Stated Pair LSTM with GloVe+MLP.

- Evaluation/claims: tested on AltLex (original and bootstrapped), compared to per-connective majority and Hidey & McKeown SVM, reporting consistent F1 and accuracy gains.

- Contribution 1: simple span-level LSTM outperforms a strong feature-based baseline without external lexicons across two corpus variants.

- Contribution 2: analysis suggests encoding event context helps beyond connective memorization, supported by ambiguous-marker examples and a reduced-class-imbalance test with significance testing.

- Contribution 3: task framing separates causal disambiguation from argument identification and proposes a practical span-encoding architecture.

- Strength: consistent improvements over baselines on both datasets (e.g., ~+1.6 F1 and ~+6 accuracy on non-bootstrapped).

- Strength: reasonable precision–recall trade-offs explored across optimizers and architectures, maintaining precision while preserving recall in key settings.

- Strength: resource-light, portable approach using only pre-trained embeddings and span encoders, avoiding hand-built lexicon limitations.

- Strength: qualitative examples where the model fixes SVM errors (e.g., ambiguous markers, non-causative uses) and limited statistical testing add insight.

- Weakness: limited novelty—span LSTMs + MLP are standard—and missing critical ablations to justify architectural choices.

- Weakness: unclear/incorrect technical descriptions (padding strategy unclear, Eq. (1) likely incorrect, confusing shapes/vectorization).

- Weakness: missing experimental details (uni/bi-directional LSTMs, hidden sizes, layers, dropout, optimizer settings, batch size, epochs, early stopping, seeds, validation, hyperparameter selection), harming reproducibility.

- Weakness: unclear data/task framing—how e1/e2 and l are obtained (gold or heuristics) and how the model would work in a full pipeline; evaluation limited to one corpus family.

- Weakness: evaluation gaps—only causal-class P/R/F1 and accuracy reported, no macro/micro F1, no per-marker breakdowns for ambiguous connectives, no significance tests on main results, limited comparisons in reduced-imbalance experiment.

- Weakness: presentation and referencing issues—grammatical errors, missing/inconsistent tables/figures, and malformed references reduce clarity and confidence.
