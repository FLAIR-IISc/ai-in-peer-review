- Task: Causal meaning disambiguation — decide if (e1, l + e2) expresses Causal or Non-Causal.  
- Approach: Two LSTM-based architectures — Pair LSTM (independent encodings) and Stated Pair LSTM (state-passing between encoders).  
- Inputs and classifier: Use GloVe embeddings with MLP on top.  
- Empirical claim: LSTM models outperform a feature-rich SVM baseline (Hidey & McKeown, 2016) on AltLex (bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped).  
- Contribution 1: Empirical demonstration that simple two-input LSTMs trained on embeddings beat feature-rich SVMs for this task.  
- Contribution 2: Clear problem framing separating causal meaning disambiguation from causal argument identification, with analysis of ambiguous markers and dataset effects.  
- Contribution 3: Small architecture/optimization exploration showing independent encodings work better and Adam gives more balanced precision/recall.  
- Strength: Consistent empirical gains — non-bootstrapped F1 +1.64 and accuracy +6.04; bootstrapped F1 +1.06 and accuracy +0.94.  
- Strength: Clean end-to-end setup relying only on pretrained embeddings and supervision, avoiding hand-crafted lexical features.  
- Strength: Useful architectural insight that independent encoding of inputs is preferable to state-passing in this setting.  
- Strength: Good analysis of class distribution, majority-per-connector baseline, and qualitative examples of model advantages.  
- Strength: Task framing could simplify dataset construction and system design for future work.  
- Weakness: Limited methodological novelty — standard LSTM+MLP recipe and not conceptually new.  
- Weakness: Missing experimental details hindering reproducibility (uni/bi-LSTM, hidden sizes, layers, pooling, batch size, epochs, dropout, embedding fine-tuning, padding/truncation policy).  
- Weakness: Possible test-set tuning — no development set or tuning protocol, no variance across seeds or confidence intervals reported.  
- Weakness: Baseline coverage incomplete — missing pooled-embedding/BoW/logistic, marker-only, attention or pretrained-encoder baselines.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope — only AltLex used, no tests on PDTB, Causal-TimeBank, or cross-dataset generalization.  
- Weakness: Writing and presentation problems — typos, broken table/references, inconsistent statistics, unclear math.  
- Questions: Clarify LSTM directionality, hidden sizes, layers, pooling/aggregation, dropout placement, and embedding fine-tuning.  
- Questions: Explain padding/truncation implementation for “mean”/“mode” lengths and handling of longer sequences.  
- Questions: Describe training protocol — hyperparameter selection, use of a development set, number of epochs, early stopping, and seed variance.  
- Questions: Confirm model inputs — only spans or broader sentence context included?  
- Questions: Provide results for simple baselines (averaged embeddings+MLP, marker-only, bag-of-ngrams+logistic).  
- Questions: Have you evaluated cross-dataset or transfer performance (e.g., PDTB, Causal-TimeBank)?  
- Questions: Will code, preprocessing, and hyperparameters be released for reproducibility?  
- Overall assessment: Incremental but useful empirical contribution; needs stronger baselines, clearer experimental rigor, variance estimates, and broader evaluation to be compelling.
