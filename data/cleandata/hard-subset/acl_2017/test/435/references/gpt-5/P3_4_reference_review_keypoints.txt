- Task & result: Pair LSTM encodes e1 and l+e2 to disambiguate causal vs. non-causal on AltLex, yielding modest but consistent gains over prior work.

- Contribution 1: A simple two-encoder LSTM with pre-trained embeddings improves precision and precision–recall balance vs. an SVM baseline.

- Contribution 2: Problem framing — separate causal meaning classification from argument identification and encode both argument spans instead of relying on fixed syntactic patterns.

- Contribution 3: Qualitative analysis suggests the model can disambiguate ambiguous markers (e.g., "which then") and non-causal uses of causative verbs (e.g., "break").

- Strength: Addresses an important discourse parsing gap around ambiguous causal markers and long-tail AltLex expressions.

- Strength: Competitive empirical results (+2–3 F1 on some splits) and clear improvement over a per-marker majority baseline.

- Strength: Reduced reliance on brittle lexical resources — uses only pre-trained embeddings, aiding generalization to unseen markers.

- Strength: Example-level analysis supports the claim that contextual encoding helps overcome lexical priors.

- Strength: Paper acknowledges data issues (class imbalance, split shift) and reports a reduced-imbalance experiment with McNemar’s test on that subset.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — standard BiLSTM encoders and minor architectural distinctions offer little methodological innovation.

- Weakness: Padding/length policy is confusing and potentially flawed (mean/mode/max strategy ill-defined, likely truncation) and lacks justification.

- Weakness: Key model hyperparameters and training details (hidden sizes, layers, dropout, batch size, epochs, early stopping, tuning procedure, seeds) are missing.

- Weakness: Narrow experimental scope — evaluation confined to AltLex with no transfer experiments (e.g., Causal-TimeBank) or small-scale cross-corpora tests.

- Weakness: Missing ablations and stronger baselines (remove l/e1, avg-embeddings baseline, CNN, BiLSTM+attention, pretrained contextual encoders).

- Weakness: Insufficient control analyses to substantiate "context over marker" claims; lack of systematic breakdown by connective ambiguity or marker disagreement cases.

- Weakness: Metrics and imbalance handling are insufficient — report focuses on Causal F1/accuracy; should include macro-F1/AUROC, cost-sensitive training, and significance on main splits.

- Weakness: Clarity and reproducibility problems — typos, garbled tables/equations, incomplete references, under-specified preprocessing, and no released code.

- Question: Precisely define the padding/truncation policy and report what fraction of instances are truncated under mean/mode lengths.

- Question: Provide full model/training hyperparameters, tuning procedure, seed variability, and whether results are averaged across runs.

- Question: Justify concatenating l with e2 instead of treating l separately or encoding the full sentence; report experiments excluding l to test contextual reliance.

- Question: Provide controlled results for ambiguous markers and cases where the marker’s training majority disagrees with the gold label, plus macro-F1 and significance on main test sets.

- Question: Add stronger neural baselines (avg-emb + logistic regression, BiLSTM with attention, fine-tuned transformer) to contextualize claimed gains.
