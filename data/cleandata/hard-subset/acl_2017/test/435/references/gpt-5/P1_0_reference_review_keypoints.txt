- Task: Causal meaning disambiguation—decide if two spans (e1, e2) with a candidate marker l express causality.
- Approach: Propose LSTM-based pair-encodings (with/without state transfer) using only pre-trained GloVe embeddings and an MLP classifier.
- Data: Experiments on AltLex corpus in non-bootstrapping and bootstrapping setups, compared to connective-majority baseline and Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM.
- Main empirical finding: Simple neural pair-encoding outperforms a strong feature-based SVM on AltLex, especially on the non-bootstrapping set.
- Task framing contribution: Clear decomposition into causal meaning disambiguation vs. argument identification and focused evaluation protocol.
- Design exploration: Limited comparison of pair architectures, optimizers, and padding/aggregation strategies with observations favoring independent encodings + Adam.
- Strength — relevance: Causal disambiguation is important for discourse understanding and downstream tasks.
- Strength — empirical gains: Consistent improvements over baselines, sizable on non-bootstrapping, modest but consistent on bootstrapping.
- Strength — ambiguous markers: Analysis shows the model handles ambiguous or shifted markers (e.g., “which then”).
- Strength — simplicity: Uses only embeddings (no lexical resources), aiding generalization beyond curated lexicons.
- Weakness — novelty/clarity: Modeling choices are standard and some methodological descriptions/equations are confusing or incorrect.
- Weakness — experimental rigor: No clear dev set, early stopping, seed variance, or cross-corpus evaluation; significance testing incomplete.
- Weakness — baselines: Lacks stronger/simple neural/text baselines (avg-embeddings, BiLSTM over full sequence, CNN) and ablations (l-only, without l).
- Weakness — data/preprocessing: Unclear how e1/e2/l are extracted/tokenized; some corpus stats garbled; many hyperparameter details missing.
- Weakness — evaluation with imbalance: Metrics and downsampling choices can be misleading; macro-F1/AUROC and proper significance tests recommended.
- Question — dev protocol: How were hyperparameters and architectures selected; how many runs and what variance?
- Question — padding strategies: Clarify whether pooling over LSTM outputs is meant and fix dimensionally inconsistent equations.
- Question — LSTM details: Uni- or bi-directional, hidden sizes, dropout placement, and regularization specifics?
- Question — span extraction: How are e1/e2 defined in AltLex; is surrounding context included; how are multi-word markers tokenized?
- Question — ablations: Did you try removing the marker l or using l-only to quantify marker vs. context contributions?
- Question — reproducibility: Will code and preprocessed splits be released?
- Suggestion: Include stronger/diverse baselines, thorough ablations, macro-averaged metrics, and significance tests on main results.
- Suggestion: Correct/clarify equations and tables, complete references, and provide error analyses by marker type and argument order.
- Overall: Promising empirical gains on a timely task but needs stronger methodological clarity, experimental rigor, and baseline breadth to meet ACL standards.
