- Task: Decide if two event spans (e1, e2) with a lexical marker l express a causal relation (causal meaning disambiguation).
- Method: Propose LSTM-based encoders that encode e1 and (l + e2) and classify via dense layers.
- Variants: Compare Stated Pair LSTM (LSTM2 initialized with LSTM1's final state) vs Pair LSTM (independent encoders).
- Data and baselines: Evaluate on AltLex (bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped) against majority-per-connective and a feature-rich SVM (Hidey & McKeown 2016).
- Main empirical result: LSTM encoder improves Causal F1 and accuracy over the SVM, especially on the non-bootstrapped AltLex.
- Contribution 1: Empirical evidence that a simple, resource-light LSTM outperforms a feature-based SVM on AltLex.
- Contribution 2: Architectural comparison showing independent encoders (Pair LSTM) outperform state-sharing, plus optimizer and padding strategy analyses.
- Contribution 3: Qualitative examples indicate contextual encoding helps disambiguate ambiguous markers beyond per-connective priors.
- Strength: Clear empirical gains vs a strong feature-based baseline, with sizable accuracy improvement on non-bootstrapped data and modest gains on bootstrapped data.
- Strength: Resource-light approach uses only pre-trained embeddings and avoids handcrafted features or external lexical resources.
- Strength: Modeling choices validated (Pair LSTM preferred, Adam vs Adadelta comparison, padding strategies) offer practical guidance.
- Strength: Attention to class imbalance (precision/recall reporting, downsampled experiments, McNemar tests) improves robustness assessment.
- Strength: Qualitative analysis supports contextual disambiguation claims with concrete examples.
- Weakness: Limited novelty; LSTM encoding for spans is standard and similar neural baselines from prior work are under-cited and not compared.
- Weakness: Missing neural baselines (BiLSTM with separators, avg-embeddings + MLP, CNN, Transformer, attention pooling) and unclear incremental contribution.
- Weakness: Methodological clarity issues and notation inconsistencies (unclear padding strategy, inconsistent dimensions, missing hyperparameters), hurting reproducibility.
- Weakness: Narrow evaluation confined to AltLex; no cross-corpus validation (e.g., PDTB causal relations, Causal-TimeBank) or deeper subset analyses.
- Weakness: Weak ablations on modeling choices—no tests for including/excluding the marker l, alternative encoding orders, or single-encoder baselines.
- Weakness: Reporting and presentation problems (corrupted/misaligned tables, incomplete references, grammatical errors) and limited significance testing for main results.
- Key requested clarifications: Explain the “zero-padding strategies” (max/mean/mode) and how variable-length sequences are handled in LSTMs.
- Key requested clarifications: Provide full architectural and training hyperparameters and model selection procedures (dev set, early stopping).
- Key requested experiments: Report results for a single encoder over [e1][SEP][l][e2] and ablations removing or relocating l.
- Key requested analyses: Provide per-marker results by ambiguity/skew, and assess train/test distribution differences (bootstrapped vs non-bootstrapped).
- Key requested evaluation: Attempt or report cross-corpus generalization on PDTB or Causal-TimeBank, even if limited.
