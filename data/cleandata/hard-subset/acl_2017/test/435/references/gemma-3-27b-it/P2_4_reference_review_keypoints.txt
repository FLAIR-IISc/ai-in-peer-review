- Summary: LSTM-based contextual encoding is applied to causal relation classification on the AltLex corpus, outperforming a strong baseline.

- Contribution 1: Thorough empirical evaluation of LSTM models on the AltLex corpus, providing a benchmark for non-explicit causal markers.

- Contribution 2: Demonstrates that contextual encoding via LSTMs improves disambiguation over lexical-marker-based baselines.

- Contribution 3: Clearly defines the task as causal meaning classification rather than full causal argument identification.

- Strength 1: LSTM models yield statistically significant F1 improvements over the Hidey & McKeown (2016) baseline.

- Strength 2: Well-motivated background and critique of syntax- and lexical-resource-dependent methods.

- Strength 3: Clear task definition and well-documented experimental setup, including ablation studies.

- Strength 4: Thoughtful analysis with example-based insights into ambiguous connectives and model behavior.

- Weakness 1: Methodological novelty is limited—the LSTM architecture is not new.

- Weakness 2: Findings rely solely on the AltLex corpus, raising questions about generalizability.

- Weakness 3: Lacks systematic error analysis categorizing model mistakes.

- Weakness 4: Missing discussion on the role of counterfactual reasoning in causal interpretation.

- Question 1: How would performance generalize to other corpora or domains (e.g., biomedical text)?

- Question 2: Can the authors provide a detailed error taxonomy (e.g., temporal vs. causal confusions)?

- Question 3: Were alternative architectures (e.g., Transformers) explored, and why choose LSTMs?

- Question 4: Did you try embeddings tailored to causal connectives versus general-purpose embeddings like GloVe?

- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written and accessible.

- Additional comment 2: Minor typos and awkward phrasing present.

- Additional comment 3: Suggest adding a visualization of the model architecture (Figure 1).

- Overall assessment: Solid empirical contribution with competitive results on AltLex; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
