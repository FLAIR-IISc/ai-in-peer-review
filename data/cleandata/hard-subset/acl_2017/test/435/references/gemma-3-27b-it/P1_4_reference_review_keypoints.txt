- Paper addresses identifying causal relations between events beyond explicit causal connectives using LSTM-based contextual encoding.  
- Authors argue causality is not tied to specific syntactic structures or fixed lexical resources.  
- Proposed neural (LSTM) architecture encodes event context for causal disambiguation.  
- Evaluated on the AltLex corpus and shows improvements over the Hidey & McKeown (2016) baseline.  
- Empirically validates that contextual encoding helps disambiguate ambiguous causal lexical markers.  
- Use of the AltLex corpus provides a realistic benchmark focused on implicit causality and ambiguous connectives.  
- Systematic comparison to a strong prior baseline strengthens the evaluation.  
- Problem is well-motivated, clearly articulating limitations of syntax- or lexicon-based approaches.  
- LSTMs are a reasonable methodological choice for capturing sequential and long-range dependencies.  
- Experiments include sensible model configuration explorations (padding strategies, optimizers).  
- Results are clearly presented, including analysis on ambiguous connectives like “since” and “after.”  
- Authors acknowledge dataset size and balance limitations and propose future corpus creation.  
- Architecture novelty is limited—using LSTMs is not a new contribution by itself.  
- Missing granular ablation studies (embedding size, hidden dims, dropout) to identify drivers of gains.  
- Error analysis is insufficient; more comprehensive identification of systematic failures is needed.  
- Evaluation is limited to AltLex; generalizability to other datasets or domains is not shown.  
- Questions: clarify critical LSTM design choices and how they differ from a standard LSTM application.  
- Questions: provide detailed error analysis and common failure cases/linguistic challenges.  
- Questions: report whether techniques (oversampling, cost-sensitive learning) were tried for AltLex class imbalance.  
- Questions: specify desired characteristics (size, annotation scheme, relation types) for the proposed new corpus.  
- Additional notes: reference list is comprehensive and paper is well-written and readable.  
- Suggest adding a figure of the “Pair LSTM” architecture and discussing related commonsense reasoning work.
