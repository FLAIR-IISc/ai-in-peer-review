- Paper addresses identification of causal relations signaled by non-canonical lexical markers, arguing against syntax-based approaches and proposing LSTMs to encode context; evaluated on AltLex with improvements over a strong baseline.

- Contribution: Empirical validation that neural contextual encoding (LSTMs) improves disambiguation of ambiguous causal markers.

- Contribution: Applies deep learning to the AltLex corpus and establishes a strong baseline for future work on this dataset.

- Contribution: Provides thorough comparison and analysis versus Hidey & McKeown (2016), exploring LSTM configurations and optimization strategies.

- Strength: Strong motivation and clear problem definition highlighting limits of lexicon- and syntax-based causality detection.

- Strength: Sound methodology—LSTMs justified for sequential language modeling and experiments explore hyperparameters/configurations.

- Strength: Positive empirical results consistently outperform the baseline; ablation studies yield insights into model behavior.

- Strength: Detailed qualitative error analysis with illustrative examples and discussion of ambiguous connectives.

- Strength: Clear writing and logical organization.

- Weakness: Limited novelty in the neural architecture—no new LSTM variants or training techniques introduced.

- Weakness: Evaluation restricted to the AltLex corpus, raising concerns about generalizability and class-distribution issues.

- Weakness: Only word embeddings are used; limited exploration of additional linguistic features (POS, dependency relations) that might help ambiguous cases.

- Weakness: Error analysis lacks a quantitative breakdown of error types (lexical ambiguity, syntax, world knowledge).

- Questions for authors: How does the philosophical view of causation as human-centered affect modeling and handling of subjectivity in labels?

- Questions for authors: Have pre-trained contextual embeddings (BERT/RoBERTa) been considered to reduce ambiguity compared to GloVe?

- Questions for authors: Please elaborate on the planned new corpus—its coverage of causal relations and lexical markers and how it fixes current resource limitations.

- Questions for authors: Why does the “Pair LSTM Mean Adam”/‘Mean’ padding strategy outperform other padding strategies?

- Additional comments: Related work discussion is comprehensive; tables/figures and captions need clearer formatting; consider addressing ethical implications (bias, misinterpretation).

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with valuable application and compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
