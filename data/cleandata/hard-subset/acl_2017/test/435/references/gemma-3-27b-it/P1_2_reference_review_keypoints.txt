- Tackles causal relation disambiguation by identifying causal links between events even without explicit connectives.  
- Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture to encode context around potential causal links.  
- Evaluates on the AltLex corpus and reports improved performance over a baseline.

- Contribution: Empirical application of neural methods to the challenging AltLex corpus, providing a strong baseline.  
- Contribution: Demonstrates that contextual encoding with LSTMs improves causal relation identification versus feature-based SVMs.  
- Contribution: Offers a clear critique of rule-based and feature-engineered causality detection approaches.

- Strength: LSTM model consistently outperforms baseline; ablation studies give useful insights.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and problem definition, with focus on explicit vs. implicit causality and AltLex.  
- Strength: Thorough related work situating the contribution within prior research.  
- Strength: Useful analysis of results, including examples illustrating precision–recall tradeoffs.  
- Strength: Well-written and clearly organized paper.

- Weakness: Methodological novelty is limited; architecture is relatively standard LSTM application.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper error analysis explaining why the model fails on certain instances.  
- Weakness: Limited scope—focuses only on causality classification and not on argument identification.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning effects.

- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed error analysis identifying recurring linguistic failure patterns?  
- Question: Which specific architectural or training choices were most crucial for performance gains?  
- Question: Any ideas or plans to integrate classification and argument identification into a single model or pipeline?  
- Question: What computational resources are required to train and evaluate the model for reproducibility?

- Additional: Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.  
- Additional: Tables and figures are clearly formatted and consistent.  
- Additional: Recommend discussing limitations of the AltLex corpus (biases, annotation inconsistencies).

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper; valuable contribution despite limited novelty—recommend Accept with Minor Revisions.
