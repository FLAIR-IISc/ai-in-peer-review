- Paper tackles disambiguating causal relations when lexical markers are ambiguous, using an LSTM to encode context and evaluated on the AltLex corpus.  
- Empirically evaluates neural approaches on the AltLex dataset, providing insights on ambiguous causal expression handling.  
- Shows a simple LSTM-based model outperforms a baseline SVM (Hidey & McKeown 2016) and majority classifier.  
- Emphasizes the importance of addressing implicit causality beyond explicit lexical markers.  
- Clear motivation and problem definition, critiquing syntax- or lexicon-dependent approaches.  
- Appropriate experimental setup with relevant baselines and choice of the AltLex corpus.  
- Detailed analysis of LSTM configurations, optimizers, padding strategies, and useful example-driven discussion.  
- Well-written and well-organized presentation.  
- Limited exploration of alternative architectures (e.g., Transformers, attention mechanisms).  
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of components like pre-trained embeddings or embedding sizes.  
- Model does not explicitly address AltLex corpus issues (class imbalance, bootstrapped inconsistencies) with techniques like weighted loss or augmentation.  
- Error analysis is anecdotal rather than systematic; lacks categorized error types.  
- Asks for justification of LSTM choice over contemporary architectures and whether attention was tried.  
- Queries whether class imbalance techniques (weighted loss, data augmentation) were considered.  
- Requests ablation study details and rationale for chosen hyperparameters (dropout, regularization).  
- Requests a systematic categorization of model errors and consistent failure modes.  
- Notes Equation 2 notation (W and b) needs clarification.  
- Points out an incomplete reference in Kruengkrai et al. (2017) citation.  
- Overall verdict: solid contribution with recommendation to accept pending minor revisions addressing raised weaknesses.
