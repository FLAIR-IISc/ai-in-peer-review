- Addresses causal relation classification, focusing on disambiguating causality signaled by non-canonical (AltLex) markers.
- Proposes LSTM-based contextual encoding to disambiguate causal meanings from context rather than syntax/lexicon alone.
- Evaluates the approach on the AltLex corpus and reports improvements over a strong lexical baseline.
- Contribution: Thorough empirical evaluation on the AltLex corpus, providing a useful benchmark.
- Contribution: Demonstrates that LSTM-based contextual encoding improves performance over lexical baselines.
- Contribution: Provides a concise, theory-grounded discussion of linguistic challenges in defining and identifying causality.
- Strength: Uses the AltLex corpus, which targets non-explicit causal markers and is more relevant than some alternatives.
- Strength: Clearly motivated problem definition and insightful breakdown into causal meaning classification and argument identification.
- Strength: Well-written, logically structured paper with clear methodology and relevant related work summary.
- Strength: Careful model development with ablation studies, architecture comparisons, padding strategies, and optimizer tuning.
- Strength: Error analysis showing where the model outperforms baseline on ambiguous connectives offers useful insights.
- Weakness: LSTM architecture is not novel; main contribution is application and empirical validation rather than new model design.
- Weakness: Error analysis is limited; lacks systematic exploration of failure cases and error types.
- Weakness: Class imbalance in AltLex is concerning; data reduction to address it may affect robustness and generalizability.
- Weakness: No comparison to stronger baselines like fine-tuned pre-trained language models (BERT/RoBERTa).
- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to AltLex, reducing confidence in cross-dataset generalizability.
- Question: How might incorporating counterfactual reasoning (even not strictly) improve model performance?
- Question: How did data reduction for class imbalance affect performance across different data subsets, and were other imbalance techniques considered?
- Question: What computational resources are required to train and run the model for reproducibility and practicality assessment?
- Question: Why does the “Stated Pair LSTM” underperform the “Pair LSTM”; what causes the negative effect of connecting LSTMs?
- Question: What are the limitations of classifying causal meaning without identifying causal arguments, and where is argument identification crucial?
- Additional comment: Consider adding attention visualizations to show model focus during predictions.
- Additional comment: Reference list is extensive.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with valuable empirical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
