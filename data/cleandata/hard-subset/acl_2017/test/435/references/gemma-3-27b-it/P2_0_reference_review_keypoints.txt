- Empirical evaluation on the AltLex corpus establishing a neural baseline for non-canonical causal expressions.  
- Argues for a contextual disambiguation approach supported by linguistic literature.  
- LSTM-based model achieves competitive performance and outperforms an SVM baseline.  
- Strong motivation and comprehensive, well-organized related work.  
- Sound experimental setup using a challenging dataset and meaningful baseline comparison.  
- Paper is clear and well-written with a clearly described architecture and results.  
- Ablation studies (stated vs. paired LSTM) provide insight into model components.  
- Work is relevant to ACL tasks with implications for QA, summarization, and text understanding.  
- Limited exploration of alternative architectures (e.g., attention, transformers).  
- Insufficient details on hyperparameter tuning and search strategy for reproducibility.  
- Error analysis is limited and lacks identification of systematic misclassification patterns.  
- Small size of the AltLex corpus raises concerns about overfitting and generalizability.  
- Request for detailed hyperparameter ranges and selection strategy.  
- Question whether other architectures (transformers/attention) were tried or why LSTMs were exclusively used.  
- Request for a more detailed error analysis specifying problematic relations or markers.  
- Question about expected generalization to other datasets, domains, or annotation schemes.  
- Suggest adding a clearer visual illustration of the proposed architecture.  
- Recommend including discussion of computational complexity and efficiency.  
- Overall: solid, promising paper with valuable contributions; recommends acceptance.
