- Paper tackles disambiguating whether a relation between two events is causal given ambiguous lexical markers, using LSTM-based contextual encoding evaluated on the AltLex corpus.
- Main contribution: solid empirical evaluation of neural models on the AltLex corpus, providing a useful benchmark.
- Main contribution: demonstrates that LSTM-based contextual encoding improves causal disambiguation over a strong feature-engineered baseline.
- Main contribution: analyzes challenges posed by ambiguous connectives (e.g., "since", "after") and the importance of full context.
- Strength: LSTM models consistently outperform the Hidey & McKeown (2016) baseline, especially with Adam optimizer.
- Strength: Clear motivation and problem definition highlighting limitations of syntactic/lexical rules for causality.
- Strength: Careful experimental setup covering padding strategies, optimizers, regularization, and both bootstrapping/non-bootstrapping AltLex variants.
- Strength: Insightful error analysis focusing on successes and failures with ambiguous connectives.
- Strength: Addresses a relevant, underexplored problem in causal reasoning and lexical disambiguation.
- Weakness: The LSTM architecture is not novel; architectural contribution is incremental and needs stronger justification.
- Weakness: Missing a systematic ablation study to quantify the importance of components like lexical marker concatenation and embedding choice.
- Weakness: AltLex corpus is small, raising overfitting and generalizability concerns.
- Weakness: Limited comparison to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., transformer-based models like BERT/RoBERTa).
- Question: Provide an ablation study showing the impact of model components and hyperparameters.
- Question: Have you compared to fine-tuned transformer baselines, and if not, why?
- Question: Provide more specific qualitative error examples, especially where the system diverges from the baseline.
- Question: Elaborate on plans for a new causality corpus—its features and how it addresses current dataset limitations.
- Additional: Paper is well-written, references are comprehensive, and related work discussion is thorough.
- Overall assessment: Solid, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses; authors’ responses will influence the final decision.
