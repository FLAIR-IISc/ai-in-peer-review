- Empirical evaluation showing LSTM networks effectively classify causal relations on the AltLex corpus, outperforming a strong baseline.
- Argues for encoding full sentence context (contextual encoding) rather than relying solely on syntactic/lexical features for causal disambiguation.
- Provides detailed comparison of LSTM architectures and hyperparameters, offering practical insights for model design.

- System consistently and significantly outperforms Hidey & McKeown’s SVM baseline on AltLex.
- Focuses on a well-defined, challenging task and uses the AltLex corpus targeting non-explicit causal markers.
- Conducts comprehensive experiments across architectures, padding strategies, optimizers, and regularization.
- Releases TensorFlow code to enable reproducibility.
- Presents results clearly with organized tables.

- Introduction lacks a clear, committed theoretical framework for causation (e.g., counterfactual stance).
- Introductory section is redundant with related work and obscures the paper’s core contributions.
- Figure 1 is overly simplistic and omits key architectural details, hindering clarity.
- Justification for different padding strategies and their relation to causal meaning is unclear.
- Claim that independent LSTMs are superior is not convincingly supported; improvements may have other causes.
- Example-based claims (e.g., disambiguating “which then”) are limited and not broadly validated.
- Optimal hyperparameters vary across tasks, raising concerns about model stability and generalizability.

- Please state explicitly the theoretical assumptions about causation underlying the work.
- Please clarify and detail the layers between the embedding and LSTM layers in the model diagram.
- Please provide an intuitive rationale for each padding strategy and how it affects performance.
- What alternative explanations exist for the slight performance gain with independent LSTMs?
- Please evaluate the model’s handling of ambiguous connectives (e.g., “which then”) on a broader, more diverse set of examples.

- Integrate the Copley and Wolff discussion of Lewis’s counterfactual theory more directly into the theoretical framing.
- Clarify how the AltLex corpus findings generalize to broader challenges in causal relation extraction.
