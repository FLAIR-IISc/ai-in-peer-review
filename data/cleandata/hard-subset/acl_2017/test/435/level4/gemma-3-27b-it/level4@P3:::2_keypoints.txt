- Proposes using LSTMs with GloVe embeddings and dense layers to detect implicit causal relations on the AltLex dataset.  
- Claims modest F1 improvement (0.5–1.5%) over prior state-of-the-art (Hidey & McKeown, 2016).  
- Argues LSTMs better capture contextual nuance than CNNs for causality disambiguation.  
- Empirically evaluates on AltLex and establishes a baseline for future work.  
- Provides qualitative examples showing the model generalizes beyond simple lexical cues.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem definition and motivation for AltLex.  
- Methodology is sound: justified LSTM choice, clear architecture, use of pre-trained embeddings, and exploration of optimizers/padding.  
- Code and data are provided, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Authors show thoughtful analysis, discussing precision vs. recall and limitations.  
- Demonstrated instances where model correctly avoids surface-level cue reliance, indicating learned causal semantics.  
- Improvement is modest, raising questions about practical impact versus model complexity.  
- Model selection based on test-set performance risks overfitting and inflated results.  
- Missing statistical significance testing and insufficient discussion of AltLex annotation reliability.  
- Lacks simpler neural baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM) for stronger comparative assessment.  
- Needs clearer discussion of how this task relates to broader implicit discourse connective recognition.  
- Requests for authors: provide significance tests for results.  
- Requests for authors: clarify bootstrapping criteria and error-mitigation for AltLex labeling.  
- Requests for authors: state whether a development set was used for hyperparameter tuning and provide held-out dev results.  
- Requests for authors: discuss impact of annotation noise on reported results and generalizability.  
- Additional minor requests: clarify 0-1-2 TSV coding, confirm AltLex redistribution rights, and include an error-analysis of common failure cases.
