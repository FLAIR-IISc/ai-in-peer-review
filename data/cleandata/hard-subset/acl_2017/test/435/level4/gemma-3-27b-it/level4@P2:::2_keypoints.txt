- Task & result: LSTM-based model for clause-level causal relation identification on AltLex, yielding a modest 0.5–1.5% F1 improvement over prior SVM.

Main contributions:
- Applied LSTM neural networks to causal relation disambiguation on the AltLex dataset.
- Empirical evaluation comparing the proposed model to the Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM baseline.
- Qualitative analysis showing model generalization beyond simple connective cues.

Strengths:
- Clear problem motivation and justification for neural approaches.
- Robust experimental setup with baseline comparison and released code/data (with caveats).
- Detailed quantitative and qualitative analysis of results and example cases.
- Well-structured and clearly written paper.

Weaknesses:
- Small performance gain with no reported statistical significance testing.
- Best configuration selected on test data, risking selection bias/overfitting.
- Unclear whether permission was obtained to re-distribute the AltLex dataset.
- Vague description of the “bootstrapping” process used to extend the corpus.
- No comparison to simpler neural baselines to justify model complexity.

Questions to authors:
- Please provide statistical significance tests for the reported improvement.
- Was a development set used for model selection; if so, report dev performance, otherwise re-evaluate.
- Did you obtain permission from Hidey & McKeown (2016) to redistribute AltLex?
- Please detail the bootstrapping procedure used to extend the corpus.
- What measures assess the reliability of the AltLex gold-standard annotations?

Additional comments:
- Strengthen discussion of related work on implicit connective recognition and why prior methods don’t directly apply.
- Clarify the 0-1-2 coding in the TSV files.
