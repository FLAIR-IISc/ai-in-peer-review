- Proposes an LSTM-based neural approach to detect clause-level causal relations using the AltLex dataset.  
- Architecture: GloVe embeddings → LSTM layer → three tanh dense layers → softmax classifier.  
- Authors argue LSTMs are better than CNNs for capturing clause-level features.  
- Reports a 0.5–1.5% F1 improvement over Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM baseline; best config shows 2.13% F1 gain.  
- Qualitative examples indicate the model can generalize beyond explicit indicator words.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem definition.  
- Needs a clearer distinction from implicit connective recognition and why those methods aren’t directly applicable.  
- Code and data are provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- The 0-1-2 coding in TSV files conflicts with the stated binary task and requires clarification.  
- Must address redistribution/rights issues for the Hidey & McKeown dataset.  
- Bootstrapping that expanded the corpus by ~15% is insufficiently explained.  
- Model selection was done on the test set rather than a dev set, undermining the reported improvement.  
- No statistical significance testing reported; significance should be assessed given dataset size.  
- Concerns about gold-standard annotation reliability due to inconsistencies in the English/SimpleEnglish Wikipedia source.  
- Recommend comparing to simpler baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM) and analyzing why the model yields higher precision than recall.
