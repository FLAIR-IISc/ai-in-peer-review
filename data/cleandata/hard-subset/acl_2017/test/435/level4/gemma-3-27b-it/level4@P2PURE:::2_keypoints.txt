- Introduces a neural network method to detect causal relations between clauses using the AltLex dataset.  
- Uses relatively shallow networks and argues LSTMs outperform CNNs for clause representation.  
- Proposes architecture: GloVe clause embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax.  
- Best configuration yields a modest F1 improvement (0.5–1.5% over Hidey & McKeown 2016 SVM; reported 2.13% in places).  
- Provides qualitative examples showing correct identification of non-causal relations despite cue words; analysis is thorough.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem description.  
- Needs clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods don’t apply.  
- Code and AltLex dataset release are appreciated and aid reproducibility.  
- The 0-1-2 coding in TSV files is unexplained given the paper’s binary framing.  
- Unclear whether authors have rights to redistribute Hidey & McKeown data—requires clarification.  
- The “bootstrapping” step that expands the corpus by ~15% is insufficiently described.  
- Methodological concern: best model selection appears tuned on the test set rather than a development set.  
- Reported improvement’s significance is questionable; statistical significance testing is needed given dataset size.  
- Gold-standard annotations (from English/Simple English Wikipedia) may be noisy; annotation reliability should be considered.  
- Novelty is limited—largely confirms known NN advantages over feature engineering.  
- Recommend comparing to simpler baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM) and analyzing the precision–recall trade-off.
