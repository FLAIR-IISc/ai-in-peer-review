- Proposes an LSTM-based model to classify when connective uses indicate intended causal relations, evaluated on the AltLex corpus with modest but consistent gains and released TensorFlow code.

- Strength: tackles a clear, timely problem—causal interpretation of connectives is important and challenging.

- Strength: shows empirical improvements on AltLex, suggesting the approach has promise.

- Strength: compares architectural variants, offering useful initial modeling guidance.

- Strength: reproducibility is strong due to code release.

- Introduction needs reframing: define “causal meaning,” state concrete research questions/hypotheses, and preview model, dataset, and key findings.

- Theoretical stance on causation is unclear; author should either adopt an explicit theory or provide a dataset-driven operationalization decoupled from metaphysical debate.

- Example discussion (line 256) is confusing about truth conditions and event/sub-event segmentation—clarify judgments about causation.

- Figure 1 and caption are ambiguous: unlabeled elements, unclear embedding/projection layers, and unspecified LSTM outputs; redraw and match textual specs.

- Clarify padding remarks: distinguish engineering/batching constraints from semantic claims about encoding.

- The claim that independent encoders are superior is too strong; treat as an empirical observation and explore interaction mechanisms (cross-attention, shared encoders, late interaction).

- Hyperparameter stability is questionable; report variance across runs, perform significance testing, and run ablations to isolate key effects.

- The claim of correctly disambiguating “which then” is unsupported by a single example; provide controlled example sets or targeted annotations for systematic evaluation.

- Standardize terminology and model names across text, tables, and figures and fix minor typos.

- Provide complete architectural specifications: tokenization, vocab/OOV handling, embedding dims and tuning, LSTM layers/directions, pooling, activations, and regularization.

- Document training details for reproducibility: optimizer, learning rate schedule, batch size, gradient clipping, early stopping, class balance/reweighting, and exact preprocessing.

- Overall: promising work with commendable code release, but needs clearer theoretical framing, precise model specification, stronger/stable empirical evidence, and more cautious architectural interpretations.
