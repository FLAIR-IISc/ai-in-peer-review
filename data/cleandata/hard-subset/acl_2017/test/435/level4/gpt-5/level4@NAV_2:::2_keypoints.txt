- Paper addresses clause-level causal relation detection using neural models on the AltLex dataset.  
- Authors argue LSTMs outperform CNNs and propose a classifier using GloVe embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax.  
- Reported improvement over Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM baseline is small (~0.5–1.5% F1; also cited as 2.13% in best-test-selected config).  
- Qualitative examples show correct generalization; writing and problem framing are clear.  
- Paper should more clearly distinguish this task from implicit connective recognition and explain why prior methods don’t directly apply.  
- Code and older data were shared, but meaning of 0–1–2 labels in TSVs is unclear and redistribution permission for Hidey & McKeown’s data is uncertain.  
- “Bootstrapping” for corpus enlargement (~15%) is insufficiently described.  
- Experimental methodology is weak: best configuration appears chosen on test set rather than on a development set.  
- No statistical significance testing reported; given dataset size and noisy gold annotations, reported gains need caution.  
- Results largely confirm expected advantage of neural methods over feature-engineered baselines; paper should include simpler neural baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM).  
- Error analysis is limited; should examine why the neural approach seems to favor precision over recall.
