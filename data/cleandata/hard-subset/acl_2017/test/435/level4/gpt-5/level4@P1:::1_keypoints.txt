- Paper proposes an LSTM-based model to classify whether a connective occurrence signals a causal relation, motivated by diversity of causal expressions.

- Experiments on the AltLex corpus show modest but consistent support for context-sensitive neural encodings; TensorFlow code is provided.

- Introduction reads like related work, lacks a clear positive statement of goals and contributions, causing redundancy and vagueness.

- Paper is unclear about its underlying theory of causation: seems to lean toward a Lewisian counterfactual view but contradicts this elsewhere, and no coherent operative theory is stated.

- Example discussion (line 256) is unclear about truth/falsehood and fails to address event granularity and sub-event issues relevant to causation.

- Figure 1 caption and diagram are misleading and imprecise (depicts only Pair_LSTM, unclear symbols, unclear embedding/lookup layers and connections).

- Confusing claim about padding being required by the models; likely a batching/TensorFlow detail and unrelated to encoding causal meaning.

- Claim that independent LSTMs outperform sequential encoding is interesting but the conclusion that the assumed relation between event meanings does not hold is too strong.

- Hyperparameter reporting is hard to interpret (possible optimizer–padding interactions); paper should clarify whether patterns are robust or idiosyncratic.

- Single illustrative example (Table 8) is insufficient to claim correct disambiguation of "which then"; recommend a controlled test set to validate generalization.

- Overall: worthwhile problem and reasonable neural approach, but needs clearer causation theory, sharper/consistent model description, and more cautious interpretation of comparative results.
