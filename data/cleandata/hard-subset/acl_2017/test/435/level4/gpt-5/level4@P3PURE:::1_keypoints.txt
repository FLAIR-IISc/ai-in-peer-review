- Proposes an LSTM-based model to classify whether connectives signal intended causal relations, motivated by the diversity of causal expressions.  
- Experiments use the AltLex corpus and show modest but consistent support for the approach; TensorFlow models are released.  
- Introduction reads like a literature survey and fails to clearly state the paper’s contributions or a positive stance on causation.  
- The paper is unclear about its operative theory of causation—appears to oscillate around Lewisian counterfactuals without committing or addressing known problems.  
- The discussion of the example near line 256 is ambiguous about truth and event decomposition, leaving the example’s status unclear.  
- Figure 1’s caption is misleading (shows only Pair_LSTM) and the diagram is imprecise about components (empty circles, lookup/GloVe, LSTM outputs, layer connections).  
- The sentence around line 480 about padding is unclear—padding is an implementation/training detail and its link to encoding “causal meaning” is unexplained.  
- The conclusion that independently encoded LSTMs are preferable is overstated; the observed behavior does not definitively justify that encoding assumption.  
- Hyperparameter choices producing best performance are inconsistent and hard to interpret (conflicting lines 578 vs 636), possibly reflecting idiosyncratic interactions with data.  
- The claim that the system correctly disambiguates “which then” over prior work is based on a single example and needs evaluation on a diverse set of ambiguous examples.
