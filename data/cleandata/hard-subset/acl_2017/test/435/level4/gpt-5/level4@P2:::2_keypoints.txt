- Task: Neural approach to disambiguating causal vs. non-causal relations between two textual arguments when explicit connectives are absent or ambiguous.
- Model: Uses GloVe embeddings, LSTM encoders, multiple tanh dense layers, and a softmax classifier; authors argue LSTMs capture clause-level meaning better than CNNs.
- Dataset and results: Evaluated on AltLex (Hidey & McKeown, 2016); best config shows modest improvements (~1–2 F1) over a feature-based SVM and higher accuracy.
- Contribution claim: Proposes a neural architecture for causal relation disambiguation and provides empirical evaluation plus qualitative analysis on AltLex.
- Strength: Focuses on context-driven disambiguation for ambiguous/absent causal markers, aligning with the need beyond pattern-driven systems.
- Strength: Competitive empirical gains with useful qualitative examples showing model overrides of indicator priors.
- Strength: Clear framing and writing, with well-motivated task distinctions and examples.
- Strength: Reproducibility effort — code and example data provided.
- Weakness: Model selection appears to have used test data; hyperparameters seem picked based on test performance, risking optimistic results.
- Weakness: No reported statistical significance tests and limited discussion of AltLex label reliability given bootstrapping and alignment noise.
- Weakness: Insufficient positioning against implicit discourse relation recognition and lack of comparison to that literature.
- Weakness: Reporting/data clarity issues — unclear 0–1–2 TSV codes, unclear bootstrapping details, and redistribution permissions for AltLex-derived data.
- Weakness: Narrow baseline set and limited ablation studies; missing simpler/stronger neural baselines and analysis of precision–recall trade-offs.
- Question: How does this task relate to implicit discourse relation classification and can implicit DRR methods be adapted here?
- Question: What do the 0–1–2 labels mean, and is redistribution of the AltLex-derived data authorized?
- Question: Define the bootstrapping process, quality controls, and its effect on class distributions across splits.
- Question: Were hyperparameters tuned on a separate dev set; please provide dev-selected test results and statistical significance tests.
- Question: Can the authors include baselines like single-layer LSTM or avg-embeddings+MLP and analyze why the model favors precision over recall?
- Overall: Well-written, important subproblem with reasonable neural approach and qualitative analysis, but empirical claims need stronger validation via proper model selection, significance testing, clearer positioning, and broader baselines/ablations.
