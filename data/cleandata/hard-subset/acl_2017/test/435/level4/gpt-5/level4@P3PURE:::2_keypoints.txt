- Proposes an LSTM-based neural classifier (GloVe clause embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax) for detecting causal vs. non-causal clause relations using the AltLex dataset.  
- Claims modest F1 improvements (0.5–1.5% over Hidey & McKeown 2016; reports a 2.13% gain for the selected config).  
- Presents qualitative and quantitative analyses with examples showing useful generalizations (e.g., handling indicator words).  
- Paper is well written with a clear problem description.  
- Needs clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior implicit-connective methods aren’t applicable.  
- Authors uploaded code and data, but the 0–1–2 TSV coding is unexplained and it’s unclear whether they have rights to repost the Hidey & McKeown dataset.  
- The “bootstrapping” step that extends the corpus by ~15% is not explained and should be clarified.  
- Experimental selection appears to use the test set to pick the best configuration, which is unfair; model selection should be done on a development set.  
- No statistical significance testing is reported; significance (and label reliability) should be assessed given dataset size.  
- Gold-standard annotations from English/Simple English Wikipedia may be noisy; results should be interpreted with caution and possibly validated with dataset creators.  
- The main claim (neural methods beat engineered features) is not surprising; the paper should include simpler neural baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM).  
- Analysis should investigate why the neural method favors precision over recall.
