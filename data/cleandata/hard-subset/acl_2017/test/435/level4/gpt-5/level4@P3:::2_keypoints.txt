- Task and approach: paper addresses causal meaning disambiguation using a two-input LSTM with GloVe embeddings to classify whether a contextual marker indicates causality, evaluated on AltLex with code release.  
- Main empirical contribution: contextual neural encoding (LSTM) modestly improves causal disambiguation over feature-based methods, including on ambiguous markers and distribution-shifted data.  
- Analysis contribution: examines precision/recall trade-offs across optimizers, padding strategies, and architecture variants, with qualitative examples showing generalization beyond connective priors.  
- Practical contribution: releases code and discusses dataset characteristics (ambiguity, bootstrapping, class imbalance).  
- Strength: clear problem framing tying linguistic motivation to data characteristics (ambiguous/variant markers).  
- Strength: solid empirical setup using both original and bootstrapped AltLex splits and reporting Causal F1 and accuracy across configurations.  
- Strength: consistent, modest improvements over strong baselines (B1, B2), with notable precision gains in bootstrapped settings.  
- Strength: qualitative examples where the model overrides misleading connective priors, supporting contextual learning claims.  
- Strength: reproducibility aided by code release and documentation of key implementation choices.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—architecture is standard (two-input LSTM + MLP) and CNN claims lack direct baselines.  
- Weakness: potential selection bias—best configuration appears chosen via test-set performance rather than dev-set model selection.  
- Weakness: statistical significance and robustness are insufficient; no significance tests versus B2 and no multiple-runs variability reported.  
- Weakness: insufficient positioning relative to implicit discourse relation classification (IDRC) and lack of comparison to IDRC methods.  
- Weakness: dataset/replicability details (bootstrapping procedure, TSV label coding, licensing for AltLex redistribution) are unclear.  
- Weakness: baseline coverage and ablation studies are missing (simpler neural baselines, marker inclusion ablation, encoding variants).  
- Weakness: precision–recall imbalance across configurations is unexplained and could benefit from threshold tuning or class-weighting analyses.  
- Key question: did you use a held-out dev set for hyperparameter selection and early stopping, and can results be rerun with a proper dev/test split?  
- Key question: can you report statistical significance versus B2 on original splits with multiple random seeds?  
- Key question: how does this task/setup differ from IDRC and can you compare to strong IDRC baselines?  
- Key question: please provide results for requested baselines/ablations: 1-layer LSTM, avg-embeddings+MLP, CNN, and marker-ablation.  
- Key question: clarify TSV label meanings, the bootstrapping process, and licensing/permissions for AltLex redistribution.  
- Key question: did you try class weighting, focal loss, or threshold tuning to address recall deficits, and any calibration analysis?  
- Additional comment: two-step framing (disambiguation then argument directionality) is reasonable—provide a brief roadmap for the second step and reuse of the encoder.  
- Minor: proofread references for formatting inconsistencies and missing bibliographic details.
