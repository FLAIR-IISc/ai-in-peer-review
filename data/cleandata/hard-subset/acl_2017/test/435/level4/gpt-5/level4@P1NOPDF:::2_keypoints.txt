- Proposes an LSTM-based neural model (GloVe clause embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax) to detect implicit clause-level causal relations using the AltLex dataset.  
- Reports modest gains (≈0.5–1.5 F1) over Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM and provides qualitative examples of successful generalization.  
- Paper is clearly written and well motivated.  
- Needs clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition/discourse parsing and why prior implicit-relation methods aren’t directly applicable.  
- Uploaded code and older data are helpful, but the meaning of the 0–1–2 labels in the TSV files is unclear given the binary task framing—explain these labels.  
- Must clarify whether redistribution of Hidey & McKeown’s data is permitted.  
- “Bootstrapping” reportedly increases the corpus by ~15% but is not described—provide a detailed account of the bootstrapping procedure and its impact.  
- Model/hyperparameter selection appears to be made on the test set; hyperparameters should be chosen on a development set and a single test result reported.  
- Recommend statistical significance testing and discussion of gold-standard quality (annotation noise, inter-annotator agreement) to contextualize small F1 differences.  
- Stronger baselines are needed (e.g., one-layer LSTM, lightweight architectures) to clarify the contribution beyond the general neural-vs-feature-engineered trend.  
- Analyze why the neural model favors precision over recall and whether this stems from class imbalance, thresholding, loss weighting, or architectural choices.
