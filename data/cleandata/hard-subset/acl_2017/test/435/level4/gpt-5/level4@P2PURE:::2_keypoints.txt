- Paper proposes an LSTM-based classifier (GloVe clause embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax) to detect causal vs. non-causal clause relations without explicit connectives.
- Uses the AltLex dataset (Hidey & McKeown 2016) to handle cases without explicit causal markers.
- Reports modest F1 improvements (0.5–1.5% over Hidey; one claim of 2.13% F1) and highlights qualitative generalization cases where the model corrects misleading indicator words.
- Paper is well written with a clear problem description and useful combination of quantitative and qualitative analyses.
- Needs clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior implicit-connection methods aren’t applicable.
- Code and older data were uploaded, which is helpful, but the 0–1–2 coding in the TSV files is unexplained and the authors’ right to repost Hidey & McKeown’s data is uncertain.
- The paper mentions “bootstrapping” that expands the corpus by ~15% but does not explain what bootstrapping entails.
- Methodological concern: best model configuration appears selected based on test-set results rather than development-set tuning, which biases comparisons.
- Statistical significance of the reported improvements is not provided and should be computed given dataset size.
- Reliability of gold-standard annotations is questionable (derived from English/Simple English Wikipedia) and should be examined, ideally with input from dataset creators.
- Novelty/value limited: neural methods outperforming hand-engineered features on binary classification is expected; simpler neural baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) should be included.
- Analysis should investigate why the neural approach favors precision over recall and provide deeper error analysis.
