- Proposes an LSTM-based model to disambiguate when lexical markers (e.g., since, after) signal causality, arguing context-sensitive modeling helps beyond syntactic templates.  
- Evaluated on the AltLex corpus, showing modest but consistent gains over baselines and yielding design insights (e.g., independent vs state-initialized LSTMs, optimizer/pooling choices).  
- Authors release TensorFlow models for reproducibility.

Strengths:
- Addresses an important gap in discourse parsing by targeting context-dependent causal marker disambiguation.  
- Neural encoders produce consistent empirical gains over a frequency baseline and compete with a feature-rich SVM.  
- Comparison between independent “Pair LSTM” and “Stated Pair LSTM” offers useful architectural guidance.  
- Public release of code/models improves reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Introduction is repetitive and fails to state a clear theory of causation or positive theoretical stance.  
- Theoretical grounding is inconsistent: the paper hints at counterfactuals but later disavows them, needing explicit theory-agnostic or formal alignment.  
- Example analysis (near line 256) is confusing and ignores event granularity or subevent projection issues.  
- Figure 1 and model description omit crucial implementation details (embedding setup, layer counts, symbol meanings, and which LSTM outputs feed dense layers).  
- Padding discussion conflates implementation convenience with representational choice; motivation for padding strategies should be clarified in terms of pooling effects.  
- Claim that independent encoders invalidate assumptions about inter-span relations is over-interpreted and not decisive.  
- Best hyperparameter settings vary across conditions; interactions between optimizers and pooling choices need characterization or caution.  
- Error analysis is anecdotal (single “which then” example) and needs a broader targeted evaluation for ambiguous connectives.

Questions for authors:
- What is the paper’s explicit theoretical stance on causation; is the approach deliberately theory-agnostic?  
- For the example near line 256, do you treat the sentence as false or true-but-non-causal, and how do you handle event decomposition?  
- Please clarify model architecture specifics: embedding lookup vs. pretrained GloVe, fine-tuning, what empty circles denote, and which LSTM outputs are used.  
- Is padding used solely for batching/implementation, and how do max/mean/mode padding strategies map to sequence pooling and affect disambiguation?  
- Can you temper the independent-vs-conditional LSTM interpretation or provide diagnostics (e.g., attention/similarity analyses) to clarify captured inter-span information?  
- Can you provide focused evaluation on ambiguous markers (e.g., “which then,” “since,” “after”) with more constructed and natural examples?  
- How general are the results to other corpora (e.g., PDTB) and what are the key observed failure modes?

Overall:
- Useful empirical contribution with promising results and practical modeling insights, but needs clearer theoretical framing, sharper model exposition, and stronger targeted analyses of ambiguity.
