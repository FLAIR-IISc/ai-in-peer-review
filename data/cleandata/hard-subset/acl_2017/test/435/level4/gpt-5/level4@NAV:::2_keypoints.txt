- Paper introduces neural method (GloVe → LSTM → dense layers → softmax) to detect causal vs. non-causal clause relations without explicit markers.
- Uses AltLex (Hidey & McKeown 2016) dataset to handle relations lacking explicit discourse markers.
- Authors argue LSTMs outperform CNNs for clause-level features and present experimental configurations.
- Best system shows a modest 0.5–1.5% F1 improvement over Hidey & McKeown’s SVM baseline.
- Paper includes useful qualitative analysis and examples of successful generalizations.
- Manuscript is clearly written with a lucid problem formulation.
- Needs clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods don’t apply.
- Code was uploaded, but the 0-1-2 TSV coding is unexplained and bootstrapping (15% corpus expansion) is unclear.
- Possible legal/permission concern about reposting Hidey & McKeown’s data.
- Model selection appears to be done on the test set rather than the development set, which biases reported improvements.
- Reported gains lack statistical significance testing and should consider annotation reliability given imperfect Wikipedia-derived labels.
- Contribution largely confirms known trend that neural methods beat hand-engineered features; should include simpler neural baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM).
- Error analysis is incomplete — needs investigation into why the model favors precision over recall.
