- Proposes an LSTM-based approach for identifying causal relations on the AltLex corpus with modest yet consistent results; experiments use TensorFlow models.  
- Introduction reads like a literature review, is redundant with related work, lacks a clear stance on causation, and delays stating the paper’s contribution until the final paragraph.  
- The underlying theory of causation is unclear (appears counterfactual/Lewis-like but not stated); related theoretical problems and unclear comments (line 238, temporal constraint p.3) are not addressed.  
- Discussion of the example at line 256 is confusing—authors treat the sentence as false while implying a causal link if true; event segmentation and its theoretical implications are not explored.  
- Figure 1 caption and diagram are misleading/imprecise (depicts Pair_LSTM variant, unclear empty circles, unspecified number of representation layers, and vague connections).  
- The sentence around line 480 misstates padding requirements—if padding is needed for TensorFlow or efficiency that should be clarified, and the link between padding and encoding causal meaning is unexplained.  
- Finding that two independent LSTMs (Stated_LSTM) outperform a single LSTM echoes entailment-literature debates; needs further analysis to determine optimal encoding and challenges the assumed relation between input event meanings.  
- Reported hyperparameter effects are hard to interpret and possibly inconsistent (comparison of lines 578 and 636), raising questions about model–data interaction stability.  
- Claim that the system disambiguates the causal meaning of “which then” vs. prior work is unsupported by a single example; a broader set of controlled examples is needed to validate this claim.
