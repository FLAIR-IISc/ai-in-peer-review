- Proposes an LSTM-based neural model for disambiguating causal relations without explicit lexical markers, evaluated on the AltLex corpus with a reported 2.13% F1 gain.  
- Contributions: context-encoding neural architecture, corpus analysis showing diverse causal expressions, and demonstration that neural methods can beat a feature-based baseline.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem framing with strong argumentation against syntax-based causality models.  
- Strength: Thorough qualitative analysis showing the model handles ambiguous cases (e.g., non-causal uses of "break") beyond numeric gains.  
- Strength: Well-organized paper, strong task description, and publicly released code/data for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Unclear model/configuration selection process—possible test-set selection/data leakage concerns.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported for the performance improvements.  
- Weakness: The paper does not clearly explain the "bootstrapping" procedure used to expand the AltLex corpus.  
- Question: How does this task differ from implicit connective recognition, and why not apply existing implicit-connective methods?  
- Question: What does the 0-1-2 coding in the provided TSV files represent given the paper describes a binary task?  
- Question: Precisely what is the bootstrapping step that increases the corpus by ~15%?  
- Additional: Authors should clarify whether they have permission to repost the AltLex corpus.  
- Additional: Claiming to "outperform the state-of-the-art" is misleading since comparison is only to one baseline (Hidey & McKeown); need broader baseline/context.  
- Additional: The practical significance of the modest 2.13% F1 improvement should be discussed.
