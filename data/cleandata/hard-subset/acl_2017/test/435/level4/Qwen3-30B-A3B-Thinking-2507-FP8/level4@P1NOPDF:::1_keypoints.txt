- Proposes an LSTM-based model to classify whether connectives express intended causal relations, with experiments on the AltLex corpus and TensorFlow implementations.
- Introduction reads like a literature review, duplicates related work, and fails to clearly state the paper's contributions or a positive theoretical stance on causation.
- Lacks a clear theoretical foundation for causation; references to counterfactual/Lewis frameworks are problematic and no alternative theory is offered.
- Example interpretation (Line 256) is confusing regarding event segmentation and whether the sentence implies a causal link, leaving core semantic assumptions unresolved.
- Figure 1 is misleading and imprecise (caption, unlabeled nodes, unclear layer counts and LSTM connections); diagram semantics must be clarified.
- Statement that models "require padding" misattributes a TensorFlow/efficiency implementation detail to the model and conflates convenience with encoding causal meaning.
- The claim that independent LSTMs outperform sequential encoding is surprising and under-justified given likely information overlap; more evidence and analysis are needed.
- Widely varying best hyperparameters across tasks are dismissed as "unpredictable" but require explanation about model sensitivity or data effects.
- The single example claim about correctly disambiguating "which then" over prior work is overstated; broader evaluation on ambiguous cases is needed.
- Overall: idea is promising with modest supporting results, but the paper needs substantial clarification on theory, model details, and result interpretation before publication.
