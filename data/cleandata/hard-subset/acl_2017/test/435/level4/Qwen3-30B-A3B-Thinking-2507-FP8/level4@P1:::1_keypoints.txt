- Proposes an LSTM-based model for disambiguating causal meaning in lexical markers, arguing context-aware neural representations are needed beyond syntactic cues.
- Evaluated on the AltLex corpus and shows modest but consistent improvements over prior work.
- Main contributions: neural architecture encoding contextual information, analysis of LSTM configurations (independent vs connected), and empirical improvement over state-of-the-art on AltLex.
- Strength: convincingly argues causality isn’t reducible to fixed syntactic constructions, aligning with recent linguistic work.
- Strength: demonstrates neural models capture contextual nuances in causal expressions better than rule-based or feature-heavy approaches.
- Strength: experiments show the proposed architecture outperforms the Hidey and McKeown (2016) baseline, improving precision–recall balance.
- Strength: provides detailed implementation details and code for reproducibility.
- Weakness: introduction focuses too much on critiquing prior work instead of clearly stating the paper’s specific contributions.
- Weakness: theoretical foundation for causation is unclear and inconsistent, vacillating about counterfactual views without a coherent framework.
- Weakness: the example about “The argument between John's parents broke the window” is confusing and poorly presented.
- Weakness: Figure 1 is misleading and imprecise; empty circles and architecture semantics are ambiguous.
- Weakness: padding strategy description is vague and doesn’t clarify implementation vs theoretical constraints.
- Weakness: claim that "Stated Pair LSTM" performs worse than "Pair LSTM" is counterintuitive and under-justified given expected event overlap.
- Weakness: analysis of the 'which then' connective relies on a single example and lacks broader evidence.
- Weakness: hyperparameter handling is inconsistent and poorly explained across bootstrapping vs non-bootstrapping experiments.
- Question: Clarify the theoretical framework for causation, especially the role of the counterfactual constraint.
- Question: Explain why Pair LSTM outperforms Stated Pair LSTM despite assumptions about event relationships.
- Question: Provide additional examples for the 'which then' connective to show robustness across ambiguous markers.
- Question: Report statistical significance tests for the claimed 2.13% F1 improvement given the small corpus size.
- Additional comment: paper should state the specific task definition earlier and strengthen theoretical discussion with philosophy/cognitive science references.
- Minor correction: fix the “Hidley” typo to “Hidey.”
