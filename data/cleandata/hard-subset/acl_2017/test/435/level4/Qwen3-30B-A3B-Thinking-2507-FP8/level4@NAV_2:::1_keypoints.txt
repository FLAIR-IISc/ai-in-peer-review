- Proposes an LSTM-based architecture to disambiguate causal lexical markers in context.  
- Novel model encodes context with two independent LSTMs and outperforms prior work on the AltLex corpus.  
- Shows contextual encoding is necessary for disambiguating ambiguous markers like "since" and "after."  
- Provides a TensorFlow implementation for reproducibility.  
- Argues causal relations cannot be captured by syntactic constructions alone, supported by linguistic analysis and prior work.  
- Empirical results improve over Hidey and McKeown (2016): F1 +2.13% and Accuracy +7.72% (non-bootstrapping).  
- Fully reproducible implementation (TensorFlow code) is a strong practical contribution.  
- Introduction lacks a clear theoretical foundation for causation and does not articulate an alternative to the counterfactual constraint.  
- Claim that independent LSTM encoding outperforms connected (Stated Pair LSTM) rests on a single observation without sufficient analysis.  
- Disambiguation claim for connectives like "which then" is supported by only a single example, which is insufficient.  
- Request to provide broader analysis of how the model handles "which then" across multiple examples.  
- Request to clarify the paper's theoretical stance on causation and how it differs from Lewis’s counterfactual theory.  
- Request to correct/clarify Figure 1 so both Stated Pair LSTM and Pair LSTM variants are accurately depicted and labeled.
