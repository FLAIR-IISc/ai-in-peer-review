- Summary: Proposes an LSTM-based neural model that encodes cause and effect events separately to disambiguate causal relations in AltLex where lexical markers are ambiguous, claiming +2.13% F1 over prior work.

- Contribution 1: Shows neural networks can disambiguate causal meaning when causal relations lack explicit lexical markers.

- Contribution 2: Provides a detailed analysis of the AltLex corpus and the contextual challenges of ambiguous lexical markers.

- Contribution 3: Presents qualitative evidence that the neural model outperforms simple lexical heuristics on ambiguous cases.

- Strength 1: Clear, well-motivated problem statement with compelling examples demonstrating limitations of prior syntax/lexical approaches.

- Strength 2: Strong qualitative analysis illustrating correct classifications where context, not markers, determines causality.

- Strength 3: Valuable use and analysis of the AltLex corpus, addressing limited causal expression coverage in existing resources.

- Weakness 1: Critical evaluation flaw: best model configuration appears selected on the test set rather than a dev set, invalidating the reported comparison.

- Weakness 2: No statistical significance testing reported for improvements on the main corpus (e.g., McNemar’s test), so gains may be due to chance.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient justification that complex neural models are necessary; simpler baselines (single-layer LSTM or linear) are not compared.

- Weakness 4: Bootstrapping procedure for expanding AltLex is poorly explained and its impact on train/test distributions is unclear.

- Question 1: Clarify how the bootstrapping to expand AltLex was implemented and why it was necessary.

- Question 2: Explain how the best configuration (Pair LSTM Mean Adam) was selected—on dev or test data?

- Question 3: Provide statistical significance tests (e.g., McNemar’s) to validate improvements over the baseline.

- Overall: Promising contribution on contextual causal disambiguation but experimental and methodological issues must be resolved before acceptance.
