- Paper proposes detecting causal relations between clauses using an LSTM-based neural classifier with GloVe clause representations and three dense tanh layers plus softmax.  
- Authors use the AltLex dataset (Hidey & McKeown, 2016) to distinguish causal vs. non-causal relations without explicit discourse connectives.  
- Claim that LSTMs outperform convolutional networks for capturing clause features.  
- Best system shows a small F1 improvement (reported as 0.5–1.5% over the SVM baseline; a 2.13% gain is also mentioned).  
- Qualitative analysis is appreciated, showing correct generalizations (e.g., indicator words treated correctly in test).  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem description.  
- Need clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods are unsuitable.  
- Authors uploaded code and a dataset snapshot, but the reviewer did not run the code and questions remain about the 0–1–2 TSV coding.  
- Must confirm permission to redistribute the Hidey & McKeown dataset.  
- The term “bootstrapping” and the reported 15% corpus expansion via bootstrapping need clearer explanation.  
- Methodological concern: best configuration appears to have been selected on the test set, biasing reported results; selection should be on a development set.  
- Statistical significance of the improvement is not reported and should be assessed given dataset size and noisy gold annotations.  
- Results largely confirm known trends that neural methods beat hand-crafted features; novelty/impact of the contribution is limited without comparisons to simpler neural baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM).  
- Recommend analysis explaining why the proposed model favors precision over recall.
