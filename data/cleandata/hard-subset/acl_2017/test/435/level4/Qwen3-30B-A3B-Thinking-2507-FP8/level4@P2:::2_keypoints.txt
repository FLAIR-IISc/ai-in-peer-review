- Paper addresses causal relation disambiguation for relations without explicit lexical markers using an LSTM that encodes two events plus potential markers; reports +2.13% F1 on the Causal class over Hidey & McKeown (2016).

- Contribution: shows context encoding is necessary to disambiguate ambiguous lexical markers (e.g., “break”, “since”).

- Contribution: provides a thorough analysis of the AltLex corpus, highlighting ambiguous causal markers and distributional challenges.

- Contribution: publicly releases code and the AltLex corpus to improve reproducibility.

- Strength: strong empirical support and qualitative examples demonstrating the model correctly identifies non-causal uses of commonly causal verbs.

- Strength: public release of resources enhances transparency and enables future research.

- Strength: detailed corpus analysis effectively demonstrates limitations of prior lexical-resource-based methods.

- Strength: clear, well-written paper with a well-motivated distinction between causal meaning disambiguation and causal argument identification.

- Weakness: fails to clearly differentiate the task from standard implicit connective recognition and does not explain why prior methods cannot apply.

- Weakness: methodological flaw of selecting best configuration on the test set rather than a validation set, undermining reported improvements.

- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing and does not address annotation noise; significance of the 2.13% F1 gain is unestablished.

- Weakness: insufficient justification for using a complex neural architecture; missing simpler neural baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM).

- Weakness: explanation of the corpus bootstrapping process is insufficient and raises redistribution/rights questions regarding AltLex.

- Question: how does this task specifically differ from implicit connective recognition, and why can’t prior methods be applied?

- Question: clarify the bootstrapping process used to expand AltLex—what improvements does it provide and why is it necessary?

- Question: explain differences between "non-bootstrapping" and "bootstrapping" corpus versions and their effect on annotation consistency.

- Additional comment: the claim that neural networks are needed is only partially validated; the paper should justify neural advantages beyond general classification gains.

- Additional comment: the paper underdevelops analysis of the model’s bias toward precision over recall and should investigate this limitation.
