- Proposes a neural network for classifying causal vs. non-causal relations between text spans using the AltLex dataset; model uses GloVe embeddings, LSTM, three dense layers, and reports a modest 0.5–2% F1 gain over an SVM baseline.

- Strength: clear problem formulation and well-written presentation situating the task in broader linguistic context.

- Strength: authors released code and data, aiding reproducibility.

- Strength: includes quantitative results and qualitative error analysis with examples showing generalization beyond simple lexical cues.

- Weakness: evaluation appears to select hyperparameters/configuration on the test set, which can overstate performance and invalidates fair comparison.

- Weakness: reported gains are modest; paper lacks statistical significance testing and discussion of potential label noise from AltLex alignment.

- Weakness: limited novelty—neural model outperforming feature-based SVM is unsurprising; missing simpler neural baselines to isolate architectural contributions.

- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why the model favors precision over recall and whether this stems from architecture, class imbalance, or representations.

- Question: clarify relationship to implicit discourse relation recognition and why methods from that literature weren’t used as baselines.

- Question: explain the bootstrapping process used to extend the AltLex corpus.

- Question: clarify the meaning of 0-1-2 coding in the submitted TSV files given the binary task description.

- Question: confirm whether the original AltLex licensing permits redistribution of the corpus.
