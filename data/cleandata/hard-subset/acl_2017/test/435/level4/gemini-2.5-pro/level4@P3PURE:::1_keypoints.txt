- Proposes an LSTM-based model to classify whether discourse connectives signal causality; evaluated on the AltLex corpus with modest but consistent support; TensorFlow code included.

- Introduction reads like a literature review, is redundant with Related Work, and fails to state the paper’s contributions or theoretical stance on causation early on.

- The paper lacks an explicit theory of causation, appears to implicitly use a Lewis-style counterfactual view without addressing its problems or offering a coherent alternative.

- The discussion of the example around line 256 is confusing and ignores important issues (e.g., event sub-division) needed for causal judgments.

- Figure 1’s model diagram is misleading and imprecise: caption omits that it shows only the Pair_LSTM variant, symbols and layer counts are unclear, and LSTM output usage is not explicitly shown.

- The justification for padding (line 480) conflates an implementation detail with an argument about encoding causal meaning and needs clarification.

- The claim that independent (Stated_LSTM) models outperforming sequential models implies the relation between input event meanings does not hold is too strong given a single experimental result.

- Reported hyperparameters vary across tasks without discussion; authors should clarify whether differences reflect stochastic training effects or meaningful distinctions.

- The assertion that the model “correctly disambiguates” ambiguous connectives (Section 4.3) is based on a single example and requires a systematic test suite to be substantiated.
