- Paper proposes an LSTM-based neural method to identify causal relations between clauses, focusing on cases without explicit connectives (AltLex data).
- Network is shallow/modest in depth: GloVe embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax classifier.
- Authors argue LSTMs better capture clausal features than CNNs.
- Best system yields a 0.5–1.5% F1 gain over the SVM baseline from Hidey and McKeown (2016).
- Provides solid quantitative and qualitative analysis, including examples of correct generalization (e.g., indicator words treated as non-causal in test).
- Manuscript is well written with clear problem exposition.
- Needs clearer distinction from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods from that area do not apply.
- Code and data are shared, which aids reproducibility, but the provided materials need clarification (meaning of 0-1-2 coding in TSVs).
- Must confirm rights to redistribute the Hidey & McKeown dataset.
- The bootstrapping procedure (≈15% corpus extension) is mentioned but not sufficiently explained.
- Experimental methodology is concerning: it appears model/hyperparameter selection used the test set rather than only the dev set.
- Reported gains lack statistical significance testing and should consider annotation quality issues (noisy Wikipedia-sourced gold labels).
- The superiority of a neural approach over engineered features is somewhat expected; include simpler neural baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM) to isolate contributions.
- Recommend deeper analysis of model behavior, particularly why the neural model favors precision over recall.
