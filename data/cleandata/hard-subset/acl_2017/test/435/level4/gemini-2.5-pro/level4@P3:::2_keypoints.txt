- Paper tackles causal meaning disambiguation: classifying whether an ambiguous lexical marker between two events signals causality.  
- Authors propose a dual-input LSTM that encodes the two events separately and argue LSTMs capture semantic context better than CNNs.  
- Model evaluated on the AltLex corpus and shows modest F1 improvement over the feature-based SVM baseline (Hidey & McKeown 2016).  
- Includes qualitative analysis showing the model can generalize beyond simple lexical cues.  

- Contribution: application of an LSTM architecture to causal disambiguation, yielding small but consistent gains over a strong SVM baseline.  
- Contribution: qualitative case analysis demonstrating context-sensitive non-causal predictions despite biased lexical cues.  

- Strength: clear writing and strong problem motivation linking linguistic and computational perspectives.  
- Strength: authors release code and specific data versions, supporting reproducibility.  
- Strength: solid quantitative and qualitative analysis across bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped corpus versions.  

- Weakness: model selection appears to be based on test set performance, risking optimistic results without a separate development set.  
- Weakness: reported F1 improvements are small and may not be statistically significant or may fall within dataset annotation noise.  
- Weakness: limited technical novelty—architecture is standard and simpler neural baselines were not compared.  
- Weakness: insufficient contextualization with related tasks (e.g., implicit discourse relation recognition) and lack of related-method baselines.  

- Question: clarify methodology for model selection and whether a dedicated development set was used.  
- Question: have you performed statistical significance tests comparing to the B2 baseline?  
- Question: please explain the AltLex bootstrapping process and differences between bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped dataset versions.
