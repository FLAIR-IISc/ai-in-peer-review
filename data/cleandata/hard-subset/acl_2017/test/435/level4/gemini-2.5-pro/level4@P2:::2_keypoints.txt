- Paper addresses classifying causal vs. non-causal relations signaled by ambiguous lexical markers using LSTM-encoded event and marker context, evaluated on the AltLex corpus.  
- Empirical contribution: a standard LSTM architecture achieves new state-of-the-art on AltLex, outperforming a feature-engineered SVM.  
- Qualitative contribution: analysis shows the model generalizes beyond lexical cue bias by using sentential context.  
- Strength: achieves improved (albeit modest) F1 and better precision–recall balance on a challenging task.  
- Strength: paper is clearly motivated and well-presented, with an easy-to-follow method description.  
- Strength: insightful error analysis with concrete examples demonstrating contextual learning over cue memorization.  
- Strength: authors provided code, supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: model selection appears to have been done on the test set, risking overfitting and invalidating the reported gains.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—standard LSTMs applied to the task without new architecture or techniques.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported for the small F1 improvements.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison/positioning relative to implicit discourse relation recognition literature and neural baselines from that area.  
- Question: clarify the model selection procedure—was a separate development set used or was the test set used to pick the best configuration?  
- Question: explain the relationship to implicit discourse relation recognition and why prominent neural methods from that area were not used as baselines.  
- Question: provide details on how the "bootstrapping" extension of the AltLex corpus was performed.
