- Proposes an LSTM-based model to classify whether a connective signals causality, arguing neural models capture diverse causal expression better than syntactic methods.
- Evaluated on the AltLex corpus with modest but consistent results supporting the central hypothesis; TensorFlow code is provided.
- Introduction reads like a literature review, duplicates related work, and fails to clearly state the paper's positive contributions until the end.
- The paper lacks a clear theoretical definition of causation; it ambiguously leans toward a counterfactual/Lewis view while disclaiming it, leaving assumptions unspecified.
- The discussion of the example around line 256 is unclear about truth conditions and ignores event decomposition issues central to causal theory.
- Figure 1’s caption and diagram are misleading and imprecise (unclear empty circles, layer semantics, what the “LSTM” boxes represent, and connections to later layers).
- The sentence around line 480 conflates an implementation detail (padding for batching in TensorFlow) with semantic claims about encoding causal meaning, which is confusing.
- The conclusion favoring two independent LSTMs over a sequential encoding is premature given analogous open questions in NLI and the shared context of many argument pairs.
- Differences in optimal hyperparameters across tasks are not interpreted; authors should clarify whether these reflect meaningful patterns or training stochasticity.
- Claim that the model “correctly disambiguates” connectives like “which then” is based on a single example and needs broader evaluation on ambiguous instances.
