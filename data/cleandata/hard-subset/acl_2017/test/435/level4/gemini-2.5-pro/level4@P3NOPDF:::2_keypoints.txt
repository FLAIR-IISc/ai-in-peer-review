1. Model: LSTM with GloVe embeddings + MLP applied to AltLex data to detect clause-level causal relations; reports modest F1 gains over an SVM baseline.

2. Strength: Paper is well-written with a clear motivation and problem description.

3. Strength: Provides both quantitative and qualitative analyses, including insightful error analysis showing generalization beyond training patterns.

4. Strength: Authors released code and data, enhancing reproducibility.

5. Major methodological flaw: Best-performing model appears to have been selected on the test set, risking overfitting and invalid comparisons.

6. Reported F1 improvements are marginal (~0.5–1.5%) and require statistical significance testing.

7. Dataset noise concern: AltLex labels (from Wikipedia alignments) may be imperfect, so small performance differences might be meaningless.

8. Limited novelty: Results mainly confirm the trend that neural models outperform feature-engineered systems.

9. Missing analyses: Need a more thorough ablation study and comparisons to simpler neural baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM, bag-of-words).

10. Clarify relation to implicit discourse relation classification and why related methods/baselines were not used.

11. Explain the "bootstrapping" step that purportedly expands the corpus by 15%.

12. Experimental detail needed: How was the final model configuration selected—re-run selection on the development set if it was chosen on the test set.

13. Result analysis suggestion: Investigate why the neural method favors precision over recall compared to the baseline.

14. Data clarification: Explain the meaning of the 0-1-2 coding in the TSV files for a reportedly binary task.

15. Legal/rights check: Confirm that the authors have permission to redistribute the AltLex dataset.
