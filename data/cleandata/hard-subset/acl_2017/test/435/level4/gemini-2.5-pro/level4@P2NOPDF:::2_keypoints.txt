- Proposes an LSTM-based neural model using GloVe embeddings and three dense tanh layers to detect clause-level causal relations (AltLex dataset).

- Task: binary classification of causal vs. non-causal relations, focusing on implicit causality not marked by connectives.

- Strength: paper is well-written with useful qualitative analysis showing generalization beyond lexical cues.

- Strength: authors provide code and data, aiding reproducibility.

- Major methodological flaw: model/configuration selection appears to be done using the test set rather than a development set, invalidating reported results.

- Reported improvement (2.13% F1 over Hidey & McKeown 2016) is unreliable due to improper model selection.

- Reported gains are modest and need statistical significance testing to confirm they are meaningful given dataset size.

- Task definition needs clearer distinction from implicit discourse relation recognition (e.g., PDTB) and justification why existing methods don’t apply.

- Data/reproducibility issues: unclear bootstrapping procedure for corpus extension, unexplained 0-1-2 coding in TSV files versus described binary task, and unclear redistribution rights for AltLex.

- Gold-standard quality concern: AltLex (aligned Wikipedia data) may contain annotation noise; paper should discuss dataset reliability and inter-annotator agreement.

- Baselines/analysis weakness: should include simpler neural baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM) to isolate the contribution of extra layers.

- Model behavior note: model favors precision over recall; this bias requires further analysis to explain why.
