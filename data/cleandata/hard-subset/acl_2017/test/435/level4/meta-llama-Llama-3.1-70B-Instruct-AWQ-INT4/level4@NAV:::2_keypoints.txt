- Introduces an LSTM-based neural classifier for identifying causal relations between clauses using the AltLex dataset.  
- Argues LSTMs capture clause features better than convolutional networks and details an architecture with GloVe inputs, an LSTM, three tanh dense layers, and softmax output.  
- Reports a modest 0.5–1.5% F1 improvement over Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM baseline.  
- Provides qualitative and quantitative analyses and examples of successful generalization.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement.  
- Lacks discussion distinguishing this task from implicit connective recognition and limitations of prior methods.  
- Code/data release is commendable, but 0-1-2 TSV coding and redistribution rights need clarification.  
- “Bootstrapping” methodology and its claimed ~15% corpus size effect are insufficiently explained.  
- Model selection used test data (not development set), making the comparison potentially unfair.  
- Improvement significance is unclear; statistical significance tests are missing.  
- Reliability of the gold-standard annotations (Wikipedia-derived) is questionable and should be considered.  
- Results are somewhat expected given neural methods’ typical advantage over engineered features.  
- Recommend experiments with simpler networks (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) and analysis of why the model favors precision over recall.
