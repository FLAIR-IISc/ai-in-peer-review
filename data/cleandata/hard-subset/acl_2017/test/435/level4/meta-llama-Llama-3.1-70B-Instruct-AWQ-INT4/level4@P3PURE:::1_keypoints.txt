- Proposes an LSTM-based approach to classify connective uses for causal relations, arguing neural abstractions suit diverse causal expressions better than syntactic methods.
- Experiments on the AltLex corpus show modest but consistent support and the TensorFlow models are released.
- Introduction reads like a literature review, redundant with related work, and fails to clearly state the paper’s contributions.
- The paper does not specify or justify an explicit theory of causation (e.g., counterfactual/Lewisian) nor explore implications of any assumed theory.
- Discussion of the example around line 256 is unclear about truth/cause judgments and ignores event sub-division issues that affect causal analysis.
- Figure 1’s caption and diagram are misleading and imprecise: Pair_LSTM scope, unclear empty nodes, and ambiguous relations among lookup, GloVe, LSTM, and pooling layers.
- The comment about padding (line 480) is unclear—padding is an implementation/training convenience (e.g., TensorFlow), not an intrinsic model requirement—and its link to encoding causal meaning is unexplained.
- The claim that two independent LSTMs outperform sequential LSTMs is tentative and mirrors unresolved debates in entailment; the conclusion drawn is not fully convincing given shared information between sub-sentence parts.
- Reported best hyperparameters are inconsistent across sections, making results hard to interpret and raising questions about model–data interactions.
- The claim that the system disambiguates 'which then' better than prior work is based on a single example and needs broader, systematic evaluation to rule out chance.
