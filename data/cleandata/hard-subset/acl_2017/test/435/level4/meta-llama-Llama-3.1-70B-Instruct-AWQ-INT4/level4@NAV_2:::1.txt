**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically focusing on the disambiguation of causal lexical markers based on context. The authors argue that the encoding of the meaning of a sentence is required for the disambiguation of its causal meaning. They evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus and demonstrate that it outperforms the state-of-the-art.

The main contributions of this work are:

1. A neural network architecture that encodes the context of two events to disambiguate their causal meaning.
2. An evaluation of the proposed system on the AltLex corpus, which shows that it outperforms the state-of-the-art in terms of precision, recall, and F1 score.
3. An analysis of the results, which highlights the importance of encoding the context of the events and the lexical marker to disambiguate their causal meaning.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel approach to causality classification, which focuses on the encoding of the context of two events to disambiguate their causal meaning.
2. The evaluation of the proposed system on the AltLex corpus demonstrates its effectiveness in outperforming the state-of-the-art.
3. The analysis of the results provides insights into the importance of encoding the context of the events and the lexical marker to disambiguate their causal meaning.

**Weaknesses**

1. The introduction is more like a literature review than a full overview of the paper, which leads to some redundancy with the related work section.
2. The paper does not clearly define the theory of causation being assumed, which makes it difficult to understand the underlying assumptions of the proposed system.
3. The diagram in Figure 1 is needlessly imprecise and does not clearly depict the architecture of the proposed system.
4. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the hyperparameters that led to the best performance across tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear definition of the theory of causation being assumed in the proposed system?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of the diagram in Figure 1 being needlessly imprecise?
3. Can you provide a clear explanation of the hyperparameters that led to the best performance across tasks?
4. How do you plan to evaluate the proposed system on a wider range of examples to demonstrate its effectiveness in disambiguating the causal meaning of lexical markers?
