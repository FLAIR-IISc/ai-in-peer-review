This paper proposes a method for detecting causal relations between clauses, using neural networks. Indeed, while certain discourse connectives are unambiguous regarding the relation they signal (e.g., 'because' is causal), the paper takes advantage of a recent dataset (called AltLex, by Hidey and McKeown, 2016) to solve the task of identifying causal vs. non-causal relations when the relation is not explicitly marked.

**Summary of the Paper:**
The paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification. The authors argue that the encoding of the meaning of a sentence is required for the disambiguation of its causal meaning. They claim that their proposed system outperforms the state-of-the-art.

**Main Contributions:**

1. The authors propose a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification.
2. They argue that the encoding of the meaning of a sentence is required for the disambiguation of its causal meaning.
3. The authors claim that their proposed system outperforms the state-of-the-art.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper is well written, and the description of the problem is particularly clear.
2. The authors provide a qualitative and quantitative analysis of their system.
3. The authors uploaded their code to the submission site, which is appreciable.
4. The authors provide a detailed explanation of their proposed system and its components.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The paper does not provide a clear clarification of the differences between this task and the task of implicit connective recognition.
2. The authors do not provide a discussion of why previous methods for implicit connective recognition cannot be used in this case.
3. The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the meaning of "bootstrapping" and how it extends the corpus.
4. The authors do not provide a fair comparison of their system with the state-of-the-art, as they select the best configuration on the test data.
5. The paper does not provide statistical significance indicators to evaluate the improvement of their system over the state-of-the-art.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Can you provide a clear clarification of the differences between this task and the task of implicit connective recognition?
2. Can you provide a discussion of why previous methods for implicit connective recognition cannot be used in this case?
3. Can you provide a clear explanation of the meaning of "bootstrapping" and how it extends the corpus?
4. Can you provide a fair comparison of your system with the state-of-the-art, by selecting the best configuration on the devset?
5. Can you provide statistical significance indicators to evaluate the improvement of your system over the state-of-the-art?
