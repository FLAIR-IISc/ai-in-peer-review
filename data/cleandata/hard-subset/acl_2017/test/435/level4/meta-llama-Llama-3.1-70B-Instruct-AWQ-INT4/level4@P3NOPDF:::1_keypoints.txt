- Proposes LSTM-based model to classify connective uses for causal relations, arguing neural models better capture diverse causal expressions.
- Experiments on the AltLex corpus show modest but consistent support and the TensorFlow models are provided.
- Introduction is unconventional and redundant with related work; should clearly state contributions and stance on causation.
- Fails to specify the theory of causation assumed (appears counterfactual) and needs to define this explicitly.
- Some example discussions are confusing or misleading (e.g., line 256 implies a sentence is false despite a possible causal link).
- Figure 1 caption is misleading (diagram only depicts Pair_LSTM); the diagram is imprecise and overly complex.
- Misstates model requirements by implying padding is intrinsic to the models rather than a TensorFlow/training convenience.
- Comparison of Stated_LSTM vs. Pair_LSTM is inconclusive; claim that two independent LSTMs are superior needs further investigation.
- Best-performing hyperparameters are hard to interpret; authors should explain model–data interactions more clearly.
- Claim that the system “correctly disambiguate[s]” the connective ‘which then’ is insufficiently supported; more examples are needed.
- Overall: interesting idea but requires substantial improvements in clarity, theoretical framing, and experimental validation.
