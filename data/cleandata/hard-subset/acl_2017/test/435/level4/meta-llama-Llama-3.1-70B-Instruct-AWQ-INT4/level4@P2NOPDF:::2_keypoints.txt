- Proposes an LSTM-based neural classifier (GloVe embeddings → LSTM → 3 dense tanh layers → softmax) for detecting causal relations between clauses using the AltLex dataset.
- Authors argue LSTMs model clause features better than convolutional networks for this task.
- Best configuration yields a modest 0.5–1.5% F1 improvement over Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM baseline (reported as 2.13% in one place).
- Provides useful qualitative and quantitative analyses and examples of correct generalizations.
- Paper is well-written and problem statement is clear.
- Needs clarification on differences between this task and implicit connective recognition and why prior implicit-connective methods aren’t applicable.
- Code was uploaded and briefly inspected by the reviewer, but not executed.
- Unclear meaning of 0-1-2 coding in the TSV files despite paper stating binary classification.
- Unclear whether authors have the rights to repost the Hidey & McKeown data.
- The term “bootstrapping” and how it extends the corpus by ~15% is not explained.
- Experimental procedure concern: best model selection appears to be done on the test set rather than the dev set, undermining the fairness of comparisons.
- Statistical significance of the reported improvement is not provided and should be computed given dataset size.
- Reliability/quality of the gold-standard annotations (English/SimpleEnglish Wikipedia) is questionable and may affect scores.
- Results are not surprising given prior findings that neural methods outperform engineered features; simpler neural baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) should be evaluated.
- Suggest analysis to explain why the model favors precision over recall.
