This paper proposes a neural method to detect causal vs. non-causal clause relations using the AltLex dataset (Hidey & McKeown 2016).  
Architecture: GloVe embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax classifier.  
Reported improvement over Hidey & McKeown’s SVM is small (0.5–1.5% F1), with a claimed 2.13% F1 when selecting best config on test.  
Includes useful qualitative and quantitative analysis showing generalization examples.  
Paper is well written with a clear problem description.  
Needs clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior implicit-connective methods aren’t applicable.  
Authors uploaded code and data, but the meaning of 0-1-2 coding in TSVs is unclear and reposting rights for Hidey & McKeown’s data are questionable.  
“Bootstrapping” that extends the corpus by ~15% is mentioned but not adequately explained.  
Experimental protocol concern: best configuration appears chosen on the test set rather than on a dev set.  
Statistical significance of the improvement isn’t reported, and the gold-standard annotation quality (Wikipedia-derived) may be unreliable.  
Novelty limited since neural methods already often outperform engineered features; recommend adding simpler baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) and analysis explaining why the model favors precision over recall.
