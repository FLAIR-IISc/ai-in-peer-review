- Proposes an LSTM-based model to classify whether connectives signal causal relations, arguing neural models capture diverse, non-syntactic expressions of causation.
- Experiments on the AltLex corpus show modest but consistent support for the approach; TensorFlow models are released.
- Introduction reads like a literature review, duplicates related work, and fails to present a clear positive stance or early statement of contributions.
- The paper lacks clarity on the assumed theory of causation (appears to imply a counterfactual view) and does not commit to or justify a specific theoretical framework.
- Discussion of the example around line 256 is confusing regarding truth conditions and event sub-division, leaving unresolved implications for causal theory.
- Figure 1’s caption is misleading (shows only the Pair_LSTM variant) and the diagram is imprecise/ambiguous about layer structure and connections.
- Sentence around line 480 is unclear about whether padding is a model requirement or an implementation/training convenience in TensorFlow and how this relates to encoding causal meaning.
- The claim that independent LSTMs (Stated_LSTM) outperform a sequential LSTM is not fully convincing and mirrors open questions from entailment research that need deeper investigation.
- Reported best-performing hyperparameters are hard to interpret and possibly reflect instability or unpredictable interactions with the data.
- The claim that the system disambiguates 'which then' better than prior work is based on a single example and requires broader, systematic evaluation to substantiate.
