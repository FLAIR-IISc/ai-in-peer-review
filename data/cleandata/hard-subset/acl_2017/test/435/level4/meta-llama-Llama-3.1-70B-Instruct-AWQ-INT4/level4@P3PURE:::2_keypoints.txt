- Proposes an LSTM-based classifier (GloVe embeddings → LSTM → 3 tanh dense layers → softmax) for detecting causal vs. non-causal clause relations on the AltLex dataset.  
- Authors argue LSTMs represent clause features better than convolutional networks for this task.  
- Reports a small F1 improvement (~0.5–1.5%, claimed 2.13% in one configuration) over Hidey and McKeown (2016) SVM system.  
- Provides qualitative and quantitative analyses and examples of successful generalizations.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem description.  
- Requests clarification on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why previous implicit-connective methods aren’t applicable.  
- Code was uploaded but TSV label coding (0-1-2) is unclear given the binary classification framing.  
- Unclear whether authors have the rights to repost Hidey and McKeown’s data.  
- “Bootstrapping” that extends the corpus by ~15% is mentioned but not explained in the paper.  
- Concern that the reported best configuration may have been selected on the test set rather than the development set, biasing the comparison.  
- Statistical significance of the reported improvement is not provided and should be computed given dataset size.  
- Gold-standard annotation quality (from English/Simple English Wikipedia) is imperfect, so reported scores should be interpreted cautiously.  
- The result largely confirms the known tendency of neural methods to outperform engineered features; stronger baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) are suggested.  
- Suggest analyzing why the neural method favors precision over recall.
