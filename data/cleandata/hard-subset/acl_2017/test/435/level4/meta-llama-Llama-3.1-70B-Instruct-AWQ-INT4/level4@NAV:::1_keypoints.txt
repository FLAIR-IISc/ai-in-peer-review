- Proposes an LSTM-based approach using the AltLex corpus to identify connective uses implying causation, yielding modest but consistent support and preliminary implementation insights.
- Introduction reads like a literature review and duplicates related work; lacks a clear stance on causation and the paper's non-syntactic focus.
- Underlying theory of causation is ambiguous: authors appear to default to a counterfactual view without explicitly stating or defining it, then contradict themselves.
- Discussion of the example around line 256 is confusing—authors treat the sentence as false despite an apparent causal link and omit sub-event division implications.
- Figure 1 caption is misleading (only shows the Pair_LSTM variant) and the diagram is imprecise; architecture details (lookup, GloVe, representation layers) need clarification.
- Sentence starting at line 480 is unclear about padding requirements, the role of TensorFlow, and the connection to encoding causal meaning and efficient training.
- The claim that two independent LSTMs outperform a single LSTM with feed-forward connection raises concerns about shared information and resembles natural language entailment issues; the conclusion favoring independent encoding is not fully convincing.
- Reported best hyperparameters are inconsistent between lines 578 and 636 and hard to interpret; discrepancies should be clarified or explained as model-data variability.
- Conclusion in Section 4.3 that the system disambiguates the causal meaning of "which then" is based on a single example and requires broader evaluation to rule out coincidence.
