- Paper proposes a neural network method to detect causal vs. non-causal clause relations using the AltLex dataset.
- Authors claim sentence-level meaning encoding is required to disambiguate causal meaning.
- Authors claim their system outperforms the state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well written with a clear problem description.
- Provides both qualitative and quantitative analysis of the system.
- Authors made their code available with the submission.
- Detailed explanation of the proposed system and its components is provided.
- Lacks clarification of differences between this task and implicit connective recognition.
- No discussion of why previous implicit connective recognition methods are not applicable here.
- "Bootstrapping" is not clearly defined nor is it explained how it extends the corpus.
- Comparison with state-of-the-art is unfair because best configuration was selected on the test set.
- No statistical significance tests reported to support claimed improvements.
- Reviewer asks for clarification of differences from implicit connective recognition.
- Reviewer requests discussion on applicability of prior implicit connective methods.
- Reviewer asks for a clear explanation of the bootstrapping process and corpus extension.
- Reviewer requests a fair comparison using best configuration chosen on the dev set.
- Reviewer requests statistical significance indicators for reported improvements.
