1) Proposes LSTM-based model to classify whether connectives signal causal relations, arguing neural representations handle causal diversity better than syntactic methods.

2) Experiments on the AltLex corpus show modest but consistent support; authors release TensorFlow models.

3) Introduction reads like a literature review, is redundant with Related Work, and fails to state a clear positive contribution or stance.

4) Paper lacks clarity about the underlying theory of causation—appears to lean on a Lewis-style counterfactual view at times but is inconsistent and doesn't commit to an alternative.

5) Discussion of the example around line 256 is confusing about truth/falsity and ignores issues of event segmentation that affect causal analysis.

6) Figure 1 caption is misleading and the diagram is imprecise—model components (lookup/Glove layers, LSTM semantics, pooling) and their connections are unclear.

7) Sentence around line 480 about padding is unclear—needs clarification whether padding is an implementation/training requirement and how that relates to encoding causal meaning.

8) The finding that two independent LSTMs outperform a sequential LSTM is interesting but the authors’ conclusion to encode arguments independently is premature and requires deeper investigation.

9) Hyperparameter choices and reported best settings are inconsistent and hard to interpret across the paper—unclear if results are systematic or idiosyncratic to data/model interactions.

10) Claim that the system disambiguates 'which then' better than prior work is based on a single example and needs a broader, systematic evaluation of ambiguous cases.
