**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically for disambiguating causal lexical markers based on context. The authors argue that the encoding of the meaning of a sentence is required for the disambiguation of its causal meaning. They evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus and report improvements over the state-of-the-art method by Hidey and McKeown (2016).

**Main Contributions**

1. **Proposed Neural Network Architecture**: The authors propose a neural network architecture with two inputs, one for the first event and the other for the lexical marker and the second event, which uses LSTM and dense layers to encode the context of the causal relation.
2. **Evaluation on AltLex Corpus**: The authors evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which is a challenging dataset for causality classification, and report improvements over the state-of-the-art method.
3. **Analysis of Results**: The authors provide a qualitative and quantitative analysis of their results, including examples of generalizations where the system performs well.

**Strengths**

1. **Clear Problem Statement**: The paper provides a clear description of the problem and the proposed solution.
2. **Well-Organized Experiments**: The authors conduct a thorough evaluation of their system, including comparisons with baseline methods and analysis of results.
3. **Code and Data Availability**: The authors provide their code and data, which is useful for reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Clarity on Bootstrapping**: The paper does not provide a clear explanation of the bootstrapping process used to extend the corpus.
2. **Limited Comparison with Baselines**: The authors only compare their system with the state-of-the-art method by Hidey and McKeown (2016) and do not provide a thorough comparison with other baseline methods.
3. **Unclear Significance of Improvement**: The authors do not provide a clear indication of the statistical significance of their improvement over the state-of-the-art method.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clearer explanation of the bootstrapping process used to extend the corpus?
2. How did you select the best configuration of your system, and did you use a development set for this purpose?
3. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the reliability of the gold-standard annotation and its potential impact on the results?
4. Have you considered using simpler network architectures as baselines, such as a 1-layer LSTM, to evaluate the effectiveness of your proposed architecture?
