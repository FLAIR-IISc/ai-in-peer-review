- Proposes a neural network (GloVe + LSTMs + three tanh dense layers + softmax) for causality classification between two events.  
- Uses the AltLex dataset, which contains more causal expressions than PDTB.  
- Argues LSTMs are better than CNNs for clause representation due to lack of fixed syntactic patterns in causation.  
- Compares against a most-common-connective baseline and Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM system with engineered features.  
- Reports a 2.13% absolute F1 improvement on the Causal class over the prior state-of-the-art.  
- Provides qualitative analysis showing correct disambiguation for commonly causative verbs but failure cases remain.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement and thorough related-work analysis.  
- Experimental concern: best configuration appears to be selected on the test set rather than the development set.  
- No statistical significance testing reported, weakening confidence in reported gains.  
- Dataset annotation quality is questioned and may limit score reliability.  
- Contribution may be incremental since neural methods outperforming engineered features is expected.  
- Limited novelty: using LSTMs, GloVe, and tanh dense layers is standard practice.  
- Missing simpler neural baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) for more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Lacks analysis explaining why the model favors precision over recall.  
- Questions for authors: how was the best configuration selected, details on dataset annotation quality, and plans to address experimental/evaluation concerns.
