- Paper proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture to disambiguate causal lexical markers and reports modest improvements on the AltLex corpus.
- Contribution: novel neural architecture taking (first event, marker, second event) as inputs with LSTM + dense layers to encode context.
- Contribution: achieves higher precision, recall, and F1 than a baseline on the AltLex corpus.
- Contribution: releases TensorFlow code and trained models for reproducibility.
- Strength: introduces a novel neural approach to capture contextual relationships for causality classification.
- Strength: obtains improved performance on a challenging dataset (AltLex).
- Strength: public release of code and models supports replication and extension.
- Weakness: unclear theory of causation and lack of engagement with counterfactual causation issues.
- Weakness: discussion of the example at line 256 is confusing and provides unexplained truth/event-decomposition judgments.
- Weakness: figure caption and diagram are misleading and imprecise (unclear symbols, missing layer semantics, ambiguous LSTM connections).
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices and reported best settings are inconsistent or hard to interpret across the paper.
- Weakness: claim about disambiguating "which then" is based on a single example and lacks systematic evaluation.
- Question: please clarify your theory of causation and how it informs your classification approach.
- Question: please provide detailed explanation of the example at line 256, including truth and event-decomposition judgments.
- Question: please revise the figure caption and diagram to accurately and clearly show model architecture and semantics.
- Question: please detail hyperparameter selection and how best settings were chosen and evaluated.
- Question: please provide systematic tests (many constructed ambiguous examples) to support claims about disambiguating "which then" and similar cases.
