- Proposes a neural network with two inputs (first event; lexical marker + second event) for causality classification.
- Model uses GloVe embeddings, LSTM, three tanh dense layers, and a softmax output.
- Evaluated on the AltLex dataset and reports improvements over Hidey & McKeown.
- Provides both quantitative results and qualitative examples showing model generalization.

- Paper is well-written with a clear problem description.
- Evaluation is clear and includes comparisons to the state of the art.
- Qualitative analyses highlight examples where the model generalizes correctly.

- Task definition is unclear and its relation to implicit connective recognition is not well explained.
- Bootstrapping procedure that increases the corpus by ~15% is not described.
- Best configuration appears to be selected on the test set rather than a dev set.
- No statistical significance testing is reported for the results.
- Novelty may be incremental since neural methods outperforming engineered features is expected.

- Requests clarification on how the task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods cannot be applied.
- Asks for an explanation of the bootstrapping procedure used to extend the corpus.
- Requests statistical significance testing to support reported gains.
- Suggests including simpler baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) and analysis of why the model favors precision over recall.

- Recommends addressing task clarity, bootstrapping, and experimental setup concerns and adding simpler baselines for a more comprehensive evaluation.
