**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural network architecture for the task of causality classification, specifically for disambiguating causal lexical markers in context. The authors argue that encoding the meaning of the sentence is necessary for disambiguating its causal meaning. They evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus and achieve state-of-the-art performance, outperforming the baseline system by 2.13% in terms of F1 score.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Neural Network Architecture**: The authors propose a neural network architecture that uses Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) recurrent neural networks to encode the context of the relation and disambiguate its causal meaning.
2. **Use of Word Embeddings**: The authors use pre-trained GloVe word embeddings to represent the input text, which allows the system to capture semantic relationships between words.
3. **Evaluation on AltLex Corpus**: The authors evaluate their system on the AltLex corpus, which is a challenging dataset for causality classification.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Well-written and Clear Problem Description**: The paper is well-written, and the problem description is clear and concise.
2. **Qualitative and Quantitative Analyses**: The authors provide both qualitative and quantitative analyses of their system's performance, including examples where the model generalizes correctly.
3. **State-of-the-Art Performance**: The authors achieve state-of-the-art performance on the AltLex corpus, outperforming the baseline system.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of Clarity on Task Definition**: The paper does not clearly explain how the task of causality classification differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods cannot be applied.
2. **Unclear Bootstrapping Procedure**: The paper mentions bootstrapping that extends the corpus by ~15%, but does not explain the bootstrapping procedure.
3. **Experimental Concerns**: The best configuration appears to be selected on the test set rather than the dev set, potentially inflating reported gains.
4. **No Statistical Significance Testing**: The paper does not report statistical significance testing, which may limit the reliability of the scores.
5. **Novelty Concern**: The contribution of the paper may be incremental, as neural methods outperforming engineered features is expected.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify how the task of causality classification differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods cannot be applied?
2. Can you explain the bootstrapping procedure used to extend the corpus by ~15%?
3. How did you select the best configuration of your system, and did you use a development set to tune hyperparameters?
4. Did you perform statistical significance testing to evaluate the reliability of your results?
5. Can you provide more details on the annotation quality of the AltLex corpus and how it may impact the reliability of your results?
