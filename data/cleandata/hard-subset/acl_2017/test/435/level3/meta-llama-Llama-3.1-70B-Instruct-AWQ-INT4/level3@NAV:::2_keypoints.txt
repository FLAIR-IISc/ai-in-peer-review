- Paper proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture for clause-level causality classification and reports outperforming prior work by 0.5–1.5% F1.  
- Uses GloVe embeddings, an LSTM clause encoder, three tanh dense layers, and softmax for classification.  
- Evaluates on the AltLex dataset, argued to contain more causal expressions than PDTB.  
- Contribution: proposal of a task-specific neural architecture that improves state-of-the-art performance.  
- Contribution: argues and demonstrates that LSTMs are better than CNNs for clause representation in this task.  
- Contribution: comprehensive evaluation on the AltLex dataset.  
- Strength: achieves improved F1 scores (0.5–1.5% gains) over prior methods.  
- Strength: presents a novel architecture tailored to causality classification.  
- Strength: evaluation uses a more extensive dataset (AltLex), giving a broader assessment.  
- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited since LSTMs and neural nets are standard in NLP.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported, so result reliability is unclear.  
- Weakness: potential overfitting risk because the best configuration was chosen on the test set, not the dev set.  
- Question: request for more details on the bootstrapping procedure used to expand the AltLex corpus.  
- Question: ask how the authors mitigated overfitting given test-set-based configuration selection.  
- Question: request clarification on the 0-1-2 coding in TSV files and the dataset reposting/usage rights.
