- Proposes an LSTM-based neural architecture to disambiguate causal lexical markers by encoding sentence meaning; evaluated on AltLex with a reported +2.13% F1 over baseline.  
- Contribution: neural network architecture using LSTMs to encode relation context for causal disambiguation.  
- Contribution: uses pre-trained GloVe word embeddings to capture semantic relationships.  
- Contribution: evaluates the system on the challenging AltLex corpus.  
- Strength: well-written paper with a clear and concise problem description.  
- Strength: provides both qualitative examples and quantitative analyses showing model generalization.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art performance on the AltLex corpus.  
- Weakness: unclear distinction between causality classification and implicit connective recognition and why prior methods are inapplicable.  
- Weakness: bootstrapping procedure that extends the corpus by ~15% is not explained.  
- Weakness: best configuration appears to be selected on the test set rather than a development set, risking inflated gains.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported to support reliability of results.  
- Weakness: novelty is potentially incremental since neural methods outperforming engineered features is expected.  
- Question: How does causality classification differ from implicit connective recognition and why can't prior methods be used?  
- Question: Please explain the bootstrapping procedure used to expand the corpus.  
- Question: How was the best configuration selected and were hyperparameters tuned on a development set?  
- Question: Were statistical significance tests performed to evaluate result reliability?  
- Question: What is the annotation quality of the AltLex corpus and how might it impact result reliability?
