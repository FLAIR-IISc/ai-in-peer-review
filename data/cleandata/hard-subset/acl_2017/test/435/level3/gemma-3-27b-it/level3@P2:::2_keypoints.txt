Contributions:
- Shows a simple LSTM-based model improves causal relation classification on the AltLex corpus by encoding contextual information for ambiguous causal expressions.
- Provides a thorough empirical evaluation and qualitative analysis, establishing a clear baseline for future work.

Strengths:
- Clear problem definition and motivation, with helpful linguistic/psychological background.
- Solid empirical evaluation comparing against strong baselines and both bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped AltLex versions.
- Insightful qualitative analysis with examples and discussion of ambiguous connectives like "since" and "after."
- Promotes reproducibility by releasing code and data (older versions).
- Well-written and well-organized paper.

Weaknesses:
- Incremental novelty: using LSTMs is not a novel idea and lacks justification versus other recurrent variants (e.g., GRUs) or recent discourse methods.
- Experimental setup concern: best configuration was chosen on the test set, risking inflated results due to overfitting.
- No statistical significance testing to confirm reported F1 improvements are meaningful.
- Data/corpus issues: unclear redistribution rights, inadequate documentation (0-1-2 coding), and insufficient explanation of the bootstrapping procedure.
- Precision-recall trade-off: model favors precision over recall without deeper analysis or exploration of alternatives to improve recall.

Questions to authors:
- How does this task differ fundamentally from implicit connective recognition, and why aren't existing methods directly applicable?
- Please detail the bootstrapping procedure: selection criteria and its impact on dataset characteristics.
- Why choose LSTM over other RNN architectures (e.g., GRUs)?
- What steps ensured annotation quality for AltLex given its Wikipedia-derived sources?

Additional recommendations:
- Include simpler baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM) for clearer benchmarking.
- Explore other embedding strategies or pre-trained models (e.g., BERT).
- Add statistical significance testing and a more detailed error analysis.
