- Paper tackles identifying implicit causal relations between clauses using the AltLex corpus with an LSTM-based neural approach.
- Main contribution: demonstrating a competitive neural (LSTM) model outperforming feature-engineered baselines on AltLex, with systematic evaluation.
- Uses GloVe embeddings → LSTM → three tanh dense layers → softmax architecture.
- Strength: clear problem definition and motivation for detecting implicit causality beyond lexical cues.
- Strength: strong experimental setup including comparisons to prior baselines, ablation (Stated Pair vs. Pair LSTM), and two AltLex versions (bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped).
- Strength: detailed qualitative analysis and illustrative examples showing model successes (Table 8 highlighted).
- Strength: well-written, organized paper with a comprehensive related work overview.
- Strength: code and an earlier dataset version made available, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental — applying neural nets to the task is expected and not deeply justified beyond empirical gains.
- Weakness: potential test-set optimization — best configuration chosen on the test set rather than a development set, risking overfitting.
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing reported, important given small dataset size.
- Weakness: bootstrapping procedure for corpus extension is underspecified, limiting assessment of its impact and data quality.
- Weakness: unclear dataset provenance and reposting rights; reliance on Simple Wikipedia may introduce biases.
- Question: how does this task differ from standard implicit discourse connective recognition and are existing methods applicable?
- Question: request full details of the bootstrapping procedure, selection criteria, and quality controls.
- Question: what were the results on a dedicated development set used for model selection and hyperparameter tuning?
- Question: why does the model favor precision over recall; what data/model factors cause this behavior?
- Additional comment: include simpler baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTM) to clarify baseline performance.
- Additional comment: explore impact of embedding strategies (e.g., fine-tuning GloVe).
- Additional comment: analyze error types and specific lexical markers the model struggles with.
- Overall recommendation: solid paper with minor revisions — address evaluation methodology, statistical testing, and data transparency to strengthen claims.
