- Contribution: Applies LSTM models to causal discourse relation classification on the AltLex corpus, showing competitive performance over a strong SVM baseline.  
- Contribution: Releases code and trained models to support reproducibility and further research.  
- Contribution: Argues that context-aware neural models overcome limits of lexicon/syntax-based causality detection.

- Strength: Empirical results show consistent, sometimes statistically significant, gains over prior work.  
- Strength: Evaluation on the challenging AltLex corpus that includes a wide range of implicit causal expressions.  
- Strength: Public code/model release facilitates reproducibility.  
- Strength: Clear definition of the causality classification task and literature framing.

- Weakness: Lacks a clear theoretical grounding in causation; counterfactuals and causal framework are underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: Introduction is overly descriptive and lacks a concise thesis and motivation.  
- Weakness: Design choices (e.g., Stated Pair vs. Pair LSTM) are insufficiently justified and explanations of results are weak.  
- Weakness: Presentation issues—unclear example, misleading/imprecise Figure 1, and confusing padding discussion.  
- Weakness: Analysis is limited and anecdotal, without systematic error analysis for ambiguous connectives.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent reporting of hyperparameters and best settings across the paper.

- Question: What theoretical framework for causation is adopted (e.g., counterfactual) and how are its NLP challenges addressed?  
- Question: Why are two separate LSTMs used in the Pair LSTM—what specific limitations does this address?  
- Question: Can the authors provide a systematic, larger-scale evaluation on ambiguous connectives like “which then”?  
- Question: Is the padding strategy a technical implementation detail or does it theoretically affect the model’s encoding of causal meaning?

- Additional comment: Paper would benefit from a more concise introduction, clearer causal theory, deeper analysis, improved figures, and consistent hyperparameter reporting.
