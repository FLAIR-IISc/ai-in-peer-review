- Proposes an LSTM-based neural context-encoding approach for causal meaning disambiguation of implicit causal relations (AltLex).
- Claims the neural model outperforms previous feature-based methods on the AltLex corpus.
- Argues neural methods better generalize to ambiguous causal expressions and handles ambiguous lexical markers.

- Clear, well-structured problem definition emphasizing limits of syntactic/lexical-resource approaches.
- Thorough experimental analysis with examples showing correct disambiguation of ambiguous cases (e.g., "break", "which then").
- Well-written paper with comprehensive comparison to prior work and clear methodology.

- Reported performance gains are marginal (≈2.13% F1) and lack statistical significance testing.
- Best configuration appears selected using the test set, risking overfitting and inflated results.
- Novelty is limited—result that neural methods beat feature-based ones is incremental without deeper analysis.
- Bootstrapping procedure for extending the AltLex corpus (~15% increase) is poorly explained and non-transparent.
- Fails to clearly distinguish the task from implicit connective recognition in discourse analysis.

- How does this task differ from implicit connective recognition and why can't those methods be applied?
- Please detail the bootstrapping procedure and how bootstrapped example quality was validated.
- Clarify the 0-1-2 coding in the TSV files and confirm reposting rights for the AltLex corpus.
- Confirm whether a separate development set was used for model selection, and if not, how test overfitting was addressed.
- Provide statistical significance (p-values) for the reported 2.13% F1 improvement.
