

# Review for ACL 2017

## Summary and Contributions
The paper addresses the task of causal meaning disambiguation for implicit causal relations (AltLex), where causal relations are not explicitly marked by lexical markers. The authors propose a neural network architecture using LSTMs to encode contextual information of causal relations, arguing that this approach better captures causal meaning than previous feature-based methods. 

The main contributions of this work, as assessed by this reviewer, are:
1. A neural context-encoding approach for causal meaning disambiguation that outperforms previous feature-based methods on the AltLex corpus
2. Detailed analysis showing the model's ability to handle ambiguous lexical markers (e.g., "break" as a causative verb in non-causal contexts)
3. Demonstration that neural methods can generalize better to ambiguous causal expressions than methods relying on lexical resources

## Strengths
1. The paper provides a clear and well-structured problem definition, particularly regarding the distinction between explicit and implicit causal relations and the limitations of previous approaches that rely on syntactic constructions or lexical resources. The authors effectively argue that causality lacks a fixed linguistic construction, making neural context encoding more appropriate than previous feature-based approaches.

2. The experimental analysis is thorough and provides valuable insights into the model's behavior with ambiguous cases. The examples in Table 8 clearly demonstrate the model's ability to correctly disambiguate causal meaning in cases where previous methods fail (e.g., "break" in non-causal contexts and the ambiguous "which then" construction), providing strong evidence for the authors' claim about the necessity of contextual encoding.

3. The paper is well-written with clear explanations of the task, data, and methodology. The comparison with prior work is comprehensive, and the authors effectively highlight the limitations of previous approaches (e.g., reliance on lexical resources, restricted coverage, and syntactic constraints).

## Weaknesses
1. The performance gains over the state-of-the-art are marginal (2.13% F1 improvement on the non-bootstrapping version), and the paper lacks statistical significance testing to confirm whether this improvement is meaningful. Given the small dataset sizes (especially for the bootstrapping version), these gains could be due to chance rather than a meaningful methodological advance.

2. The best configuration appears to have been selected based on test set performance rather than a validation set, which risks overfitting to the test set and inflates the reported gains. The authors should have used a proper dev set for model selection and reported results on an independent test set.

3. The novelty of the work is questionable. The paper demonstrates that neural methods outperform feature-based approaches for this task, which is expected in NLP given the success of neural networks in various language understanding tasks. The contribution appears incremental rather than transformative, especially without a thorough analysis of why the neural approach works better.

4. The bootstrapping procedure for extending the AltLex corpus is not clearly explained, and the paper mentions a 15% corpus extension without detailing the methodology. This lack of transparency makes it difficult to assess the quality of the extended corpus.

5. The paper fails to clearly distinguish this task from implicit connective recognition in discourse analysis, which is a significant concern. The authors should clarify how their task differs from existing work on identifying implicit causal relations.

## Questions to Authors
1. How does this causal meaning disambiguation task differ from implicit connective recognition in discourse analysis? Why can't existing methods for implicit connective recognition be directly applied to this task?

2. The paper mentions bootstrapping that extends the corpus by ~15%, but does not explain the bootstrapping procedure. Please provide details on how the bootstrapping was performed and how the quality of the bootstrapped examples was validated.

3. The code and data were uploaded, but the meaning of the 0-1-2 coding in the TSV files is unclear. Please clarify this coding scheme and confirm the reposting rights for the AltLex corpus.

4. The paper states the best configuration was selected based on test set performance. Please confirm whether a separate development set was used for model selection, and if not, how you addressed the risk of test set overfitting.

5. The paper claims a 2.13% F1 improvement over the state-of-the-art, but does not report statistical significance testing. Please provide p-values for the reported improvements to confirm whether they are statistically significant.
