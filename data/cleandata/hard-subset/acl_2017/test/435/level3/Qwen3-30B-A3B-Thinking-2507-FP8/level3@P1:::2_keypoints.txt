- Task: Causal relation disambiguation on AltLex—identify causal vs. non-causal clause relations when not explicitly marked.  
- Claim: Neural models (LSTMs) better encode contextual meaning for causal disambiguation than syntactic/lexical-rule approaches.  
- Method: Proposed architecture uses two LSTM inputs (first event, marker+second event), dense tanh layers, and softmax output.  
- Contribution 1: Argues need for contextual encoding rather than reliance on syntactic constructions.  
- Contribution 2: Presents a neural architecture that reportedly outperforms prior state-of-the-art on AltLex.  
- Contribution 3: Provides qualitative and quantitative analyses showing handling of ambiguous causal markers.  
- Strength: Convincing argument that causality isn’t tied to specific syntactic constructions (cites Neeleman & van de Koot).  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on AltLex (bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped) with consistent improvements; best config gains ~2.13% F1 over baseline.  
- Strength: Qualitative examples demonstrate correct disambiguation of ambiguous markers where baseline fails.  
- Major weakness: Best configuration appears selected on the test set, risking inflated performance claims.  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported for improvements.  
- Weakness: Novelty is limited—neural methods outperforming engineered features is expected in NLP.  
- Weakness: Bootstrapping procedure for corpus expansion (~15%) is not described.  
- Weakness: Task distinction from implicit connective recognition is unclear, weakening motivation.  
- Weakness: Reported improvements are modest (0.5–1.5% F1 in places) and may not be meaningful.  
- Weakness: Code/data issues—0/1/2 TSV coding is unclear and dataset reposting rights are uncertain.  
- Recommendation: Clarify difference from implicit connective recognition, provide a proper development set, and add statistical significance testing.  
- Recommendation: Describe bootstrapping procedure and dataset provenance/permissions.  
- Recommendation: Include simpler baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) to justify architectural complexity.  
- Questions to authors: How does this task differ from implicit connective recognition; explain bootstrapping; how was best config selected; why does the model favor precision over recall and which examples are hardest; what does 0-1-2 coding mean and what are reposting rights?
