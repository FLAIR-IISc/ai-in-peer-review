- Proposed LSTM-based architecture for disambiguating causal lexical markers using the AltLex dataset, claiming better performance than feature-based methods.  
- Paper offers comprehensive qualitative and quantitative analyses showing model generalization to ambiguous causal expressions.  
- Demonstrates that context encoding is essential for accurate causal disambiguation.  
- Well-written with a clear problem statement and thorough analysis across different lexical markers.  
- Provides convincing examples where the model correctly identifies causal relations (e.g., "which then").  
- Empirical improvement of ≈2.13% F1 over prior state-of-the-art on the non-bootstrapped corpus.  
- Task definition is unclear relative to implicit connective recognition and lacks justification why prior methods are inapplicable.  
- Experimental design flaw: best configuration appears selected on the test set instead of the dev set, risking inflated results.  
- Bootstrapping procedure that extends the corpus by ~15% is not explained, making corpus construction opaque.  
- No statistical significance testing reported and no analysis of dataset annotation quality for Wikipedia-based data.  
- Novelty is weak: neural superiority over engineered features is expected, and simpler baselines (e.g., single-layer LSTMs) were omitted for comparison.  
- Recommended fixes: clarify task distinction, explain bootstrapping, report statistical significance, fix test-set selection bias, and analyze precision vs. recall behavior.
