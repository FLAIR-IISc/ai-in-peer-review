- Empirical demonstration that LSTM-based models outperform a strong feature-based SVM baseline for causal connective disambiguation on the AltLex dataset.
- Public release of TensorFlow code and trained models to support reproducibility and future work.
- Tackles the important task of causal relation classification and motivates neural models to capture diverse contextual cues over brittle syntactic patterns.
- Consistent, if modest, empirical improvements over the state-of-the-art baseline on the AltLex corpus.
- Publicly available resources (code and models) are a valuable contribution to the community.
- Paper lacks clarity and rigor: introduction reads like a literature review, theoretical grounding is vague, and technical descriptions (e.g., Figure 1, padding) are unclear or misleading.
- Several claims are insufficiently supported or speculative (e.g., reasons independent LSTMs outperform sequential architecture; anecdotal evidence for "which then").
- Inconsistent and hard-to-follow reporting of experimental settings and hyperparameters, compromising reproducibility.
