- Paper summary: tackles causal meaning disambiguation by classifying whether a lexical marker between two events signals causation; proposes Pair LSTM and Stated Pair LSTM and reports modest but consistent gains on the AltLex corpus.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a dual-input LSTM architecture encoding full event contexts for causal connective disambiguation.

- Contribution 2: Empirically outperforms a strong feature-based SVM baseline on AltLex, improving F1 and balance between precision and recall.

- Contribution 3: Presents analysis suggesting neural models can disambiguate ambiguous or non-prototypical causal markers beyond lexical cues.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated use of neural models for context-dependent causality, moving beyond heavy feature engineering.

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results across two corpus versions, showing consistent improvement over prior state of the art.

- Strength 3: Thoughtful qualitative analysis with examples where the model handles non-causal uses of causative verbs and ambiguous connectives.

- Weakness 1: Introduction/theoretical framing is unclear, duplicates related work, and lacks a precise operational definition of causality; dismissal of counterfactual constraint is unsupported.

- Weakness 2: Model description and figures are imprecise—Figure 1 omits the Stated Pair LSTM, uses undefined symbols, and leaves out dense layer/LSTM output details; zero-padding discussion is confusing.

- Weakness 3: Conclusion that independent encoding is superior is unsupported by deeper analysis given small performance differences and counter-intuitive implications.

- Weakness 4: Evidence for disambiguation of ambiguous connectives is anecdotal (single example) and needs systematic evaluation.

- Question 1: Clarify the operational definition of causality used and justify dismissing the counterfactual constraint in the given example.

- Question 2: Explain why Pair LSTM outperformed Stated Pair LSTM and whether this result could be an artifact of dataset or architecture.

- Question 3: Provide a more systematic analysis of ambiguous connectives (e.g., per-connective performance or curated ambiguous-instance evaluation).
