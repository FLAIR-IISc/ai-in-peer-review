- Paper proposes LSTM-based models to classify whether a lexical marker signals causality between two spans, evaluated on the AltLex corpus with modest but consistent improvements over a feature-based SVM baseline; code and models released.

- Primary contribution: empirical demonstration that a standard LSTM architecture outperforms a complex, feature-engineered system on AltLex, providing a strong neural baseline.

- Secondary contribution: public release of TensorFlow code and trained models, improving reproducibility and enabling future comparisons.

- Strength: tackles the important, hard problem of identifying causal relations beyond fixed unambiguous connectives by using the AltLex benchmark.

- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art results on AltLex with meaningful F1 and accuracy gains (notably 2.13% F1 and 7.72% accuracy on the non-bootstrapping set).

- Strength: strong commitment to reproducibility through code and model release.

- Weakness: unclear motivation and theoretical grounding — introduction duplicates related work and the paper fails to define precisely what notion of "causality" the model targets.

- Weakness: poor presentation of technical details — Figure 1 is imprecise, symbols undefined, and dense-layer/LSTM connection semantics unspecified.

- Weakness: confusing discussion of padding framed as encoding meaning rather than a standard batching necessity; needs clarification of chosen padding strategy.

- Weakness: inconsistent and insufficient hyperparameter reporting (e.g., conflicting best-model entries across tables and vague hyperparameter ranges), hindering interpretation and reproducibility.

- Weakness: superficial analysis and unsubstantiated claims — the explanation for why independent LSTMs outperform sequential ones is circular and lacks deeper analysis.

- Weakness: anecdotal evidence used to claim better disambiguation (e.g., "which then") based on a single example instead of systematic evaluation.

- Question: please clarify the operational definition or theory of causality assumed by the work.

- Question: explain why passing LSTM state from the first to second event hurts performance — dataset artifact or implementation detail?

- Question: have you considered a systematic evaluation of ambiguous connectives (rather than single examples) to substantiate disambiguation claims?
