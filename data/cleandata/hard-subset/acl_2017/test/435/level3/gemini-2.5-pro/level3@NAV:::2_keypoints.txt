- Tackles disambiguating causal vs non-causal relations between clause pairs without explicit connectives using the AltLex dataset; proposes a Pair LSTM with GloVe embeddings and dense layers.  
- Primary contribution: empirical demonstration that this standard neural architecture yields a modest F1 improvement over a strong feature-engineered baseline.  
- Secondary contribution: qualitative analysis showing examples where the neural model generalizes better than prior work.

- Clear problem formulation and well-written background motivating the task.  
- Achieves a small but consistent F1 improvement (~0.5–1.5%) over prior state-of-the-art (Hidey & McKeown, 2016).  
- Provides useful qualitative/error analysis illustrating cases where context encoding helps.  
- Makes code and data available, supporting reproducibility.

- Model selection appears to be driven by test-set performance, risking overfitting and invalidating claimed gains.  
- Limited novelty: using a standard LSTM is incremental and lacks simpler neural baselines to justify architectural choices.  
- No statistical significance testing reported and no discussion of noise/quality issues in the bootstrapped AltLex data.  
- Insufficient detail on how this task differs from implicit discourse relation recognition and missing dataset/bootstrapping procedure details.

- Did you use a development set for model selection and hyperparameter tuning, or was selection based on the test set?  
- Please describe the bootstrapping procedure used to extend the corpus.  
- How does this task fundamentally differ from implicit discourse connective recognition, and why weren’t methods from that field used as baselines?
