- Task: Causal meaning disambiguation — classify whether two event clauses joined by a marker are "Causal" or "Non-Causal".
- Approach: Two-input LSTM encoding each event clause with dense layers for classification using pre-trained embeddings.
- Result: Modest improvement over Hidey & McKeown (2016) on the AltLex corpus, achieving state-of-the-art on two dataset versions.

- Contribution: Empirically shows a standard LSTM-based neural model can outperform a feature-engineered SVM for this task.
- Contribution: Provides qualitative analysis demonstrating the model disambiguates challenging markers by using full context.

- Strength: Clear problem formulation, related work positioning, and transparent model description.
- Strength: Solid empirical results across original, bootstrapped, and balanced AltLex subsets.
- Strength: Insightful qualitative/error analysis with concrete examples supporting the context-encoding claim.
- Strength: Authors provided code and data to aid reproducibility.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — architecture and approach are standard and incremental.
- Weakness: Model selection appears to be tuned on the test set rather than a dev set, risking overestimated performance.
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing provided to validate small reported gains (~1.5% F1).
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to related tasks (e.g., implicit discourse relation recognition) and lack of simpler neural baselines (e.g., bag-of-words or 1-layer LSTM).

- Question: Clarify hyperparameter tuning and model selection procedure — was a development set used?
- Question: How does this task differ from implicit discourse relation recognition, and why weren't those models used as baselines?
- Question: Clarify the 0-1-2 coding in supplementary data and confirm rights to redistribute the AltLex dataset.
