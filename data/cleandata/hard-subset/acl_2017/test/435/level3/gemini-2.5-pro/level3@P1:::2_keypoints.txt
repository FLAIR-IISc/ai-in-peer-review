- Task: classify whether the relation between two text spans linked by an ambiguous lexical marker is causal or not; authors argue full event context is needed.  
- Method: propose a dual-input "Pair LSTM" that encodes each event span separately and uses dense layers for classification.  
- Data/results: trained/evaluated on the AltLex corpus and report new state-of-the-art, outperforming Hidey & McKeown (2016).  
- Contribution 1: new SOTA on AltLex with consistent F1 improvements (up to 2.13% non-bootstrapped, ~1% bootstrapped).  
- Contribution 2: qualitative analysis showing the model disambiguates causality in challenging, ambiguous examples.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation, strong motivation, and well-written presentation.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on both bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped splits with consistent gains.  
- Strength: insightful qualitative examples demonstrating contextual disambiguation (e.g., non-causal "break", "which then").  
- Strength: authors included code to facilitate reproducibility.  
- Weakness: potential methodological flaw—ablation/hyperparameter choices appear reported on the test set (risk of tuning on test data).  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—Pair LSTM is an incremental application of standard LSTM sentence-pair modeling.  
- Weakness: lack of statistical significance testing for modest reported improvements.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to related tasks/approaches, notably implicit discourse relation classification.  
- Question: clarify whether a separate development set was used for model selection and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Question: provide details on the "bootstrapping" method used to create the bootstrapped AltLex corpus.  
- Question: explain how this task differs from implicit discourse relation classification and why standard baselines were not used.  
- Question: clarify data file format (meaning of 0-1-2 coding) and confirm rights to redistribute the AltLex dataset.
