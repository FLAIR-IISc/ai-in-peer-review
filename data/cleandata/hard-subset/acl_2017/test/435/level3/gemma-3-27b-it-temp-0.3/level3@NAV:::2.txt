## Review of "Neural Disambiguation of Causal Lexical Markers based on Context"

This paper tackles the challenging task of identifying causal relationships when they are not explicitly marked by connectives, utilizing the AltLex dataset. The authors propose an LSTM-based neural network architecture for this purpose, arguing that contextual encoding is crucial for accurate disambiguation. They demonstrate improvements of roughly 0.5-1.5% F1 over the existing state-of-the-art (Hidey & McKeown, 2016).

The primary contribution of this work, as I see it, lies in the empirical validation of LSTM networks for this specific task. While the general trend in NLP favors neural methods, this paper provides concrete evidence that LSTMs outperform previous feature-engineered approaches on AltLex, suggesting their superior ability to capture contextual cues for causal reasoning. A secondary contribution is the application of neural networks to the AltLex dataset itself, providing a strong baseline for future research in this area. The inclusion of qualitative analysis, with examples of correct generalizations, is also valuable.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of identifying implicit causal relations and its importance.
*   **Solid Empirical Results:** The reported F1 improvements, while incremental, are statistically meaningful and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   **Qualitative Analysis:** The inclusion of example cases where the model succeeds provides valuable insight into its behavior and strengths.
*   **Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-structured and easy to follow.
*   **Dataset Utilization:** Applying a neural approach to the AltLex dataset is a valuable contribution, establishing a new baseline.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Experimental Setup Concerns:** The selection of the best configuration appears to be based on the test set, potentially leading to inflated performance gains. A proper dev set evaluation is crucial.
*   **Lack of Statistical Significance:** The paper lacks statistical significance testing, making it difficult to assess the robustness of the reported improvements.
*   **Dataset Annotation Quality:** The reliance on English/Simple Wikipedia for data annotation raises concerns about the inherent quality and potential biases of the dataset.
*   **Incremental Novelty:** The finding that neural networks outperform engineered features is somewhat expected. The contribution feels incremental rather than groundbreaking.
*   **Bootstrapping Procedure:** The paper mentions a bootstrapping procedure to extend the corpus but provides no details on how it was implemented. This lack of transparency hinders reproducibility.
*   **Coding Clarity:** The meaning of the 0-1-2 coding in the TSV files is unclear, and questions regarding dataset reposting rights need to be addressed.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Was the final model selection performed using a separate development set, or was the test set used for hyperparameter tuning? If the latter, please re-evaluate the results using a proper dev set split.
2.  Please provide a detailed explanation of the bootstrapping procedure used to extend the AltLex corpus.
3.  Can you clarify the meaning of the 0-1-2 coding in the TSV files and confirm the rights for redistribution of the dataset?
4.  Why does the model exhibit a preference for precision over recall? Can you provide an analysis of the types of errors the model makes?
