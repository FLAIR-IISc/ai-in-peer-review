- Summary: Proposes LSTM-based models to decide if a lexical marker signals a causal relation between two event spans, showing modest consistent gains on the AltLex dataset and releasing TensorFlow code/models.  
- Contribution: Context-sensitive neural approach that outperforms a strong SVM baseline on AltLex, especially improving precision.  
- Contribution: Architectural finding that independent encoders for the two arguments (Pair LSTM) outperform an initialized/sequential LSTM variant.  
- Strength: Empirical improvement over majority-by-connective and feature-rich baselines on both bootstrapped and non-bootstrapped AltLex splits, with clear precision gains.  
- Strength: Focuses on context rather than fixed syntactic patterns or lexicons, addressing coverage limitations of prior work.  
- Strength: Sensible experimental design (evaluation on both corpus versions, P/R/F1 + Accuracy, reduced-class-imbalance analysis, McNemar test) and claimed code release for reproducibility.  
- Strength: Reports a useful negative result that state-initialized LSTM underperforms independent encoders.  
- Weakness: Unclear and sometimes contradictory conceptual framing of “causation” (counterfactual/temporal constraints and event decomposition not clearly defined).  
- Weakness: Missing or underspecified baselines/ablations (averaged embeddings + logistic regression, full-sentence BiLSTM/CNN, connective-only classifier, shallow lexicalized linear model).  
- Weakness: Ambiguous padding/pooling methodology (confuses padding length selection with pooling/truncation; unclear impact on representations and reproducibility).  
- Weakness: Figure 1 and architectural exposition are imprecise and inconsistent with hyperparameter reporting, making replication difficult.  
- Weakness: Interpretation overreach from limited evidence (single-instance claim about “then”; small gains on bootstrapped split and lack of statistical significance reporting).  
- Question: How are e1, l, and e2 segmented/concatenated for implicit/AltLex cases, and how are cases without l handled?  
- Question: What does “max/mean/mode” padding refer to — chosen padding length, truncation, batching artifact, and does it affect learned representations?  
- Question: Which LSTM outputs are used for encoding (final hidden state, mean pooling, etc.), with exact dimensions and operations?  
- Question: Can you report results for connective-only classifier, averaged embeddings + logistic regression, and single-sentence BiLSTM/CNN baselines?  
- Question: Can you provide per-connector results and evaluations where test connectors are unseen or ambiguous in training to test generalization/lexical memorization?  
- Question: What are standard deviations across random seeds and are improvements over baseline B2 statistically significant?  
- Additional comment: Redraw and relabel Figure 1 to clearly depict both architectures with unambiguous symbols and layer semantics.  
- Additional comment: Ensure consistent hyperparameter reporting (optimizer, dropout, regularization, LSTM size).  
- Additional comment: Tighten Introduction to avoid repeating Related Work and include concise contributions statement.  
- Additional comment: Report macro-F1 and include significance tests for main comparisons given class imbalance.
