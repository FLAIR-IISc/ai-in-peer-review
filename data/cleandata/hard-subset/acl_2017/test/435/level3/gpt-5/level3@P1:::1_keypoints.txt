- Proposes LSTM-based models to disambiguate whether a sentence expresses a causal relation given two events and a lexical marker.  
- Hypothesis: contextual sentence meaning via neural sequence encoders is necessary to resolve ambiguous markers/AltLex expressions.  
- Two architectures evaluated: Pair LSTM (independent encoders) and Stated Pair LSTM (second LSTM initialized with first’s final state).  
- Experiments on AltLex (non-bootstrapping and bootstrapping variants) against marker-majority and Hidey & McKeown (2016) SVM baselines.  
- Best neural models yield small but consistent F1/accuracy gains over baselines, with error analysis showing where context helps.  
- Contribution: empirical demonstration that neural sequence encoders improve marker disambiguation over a strong feature-based baseline on AltLex.  
- Contribution: practical neural setup exploring input factorization, padding strategies, and optimizers, finding independent LSTMs usually outperform state-initialized pairs.  
- Contribution: reproducibility resources — TensorFlow code/models released.  
- Strength: empirical improvements over strong baselines (+2–8% F1/accuracy) without external lexical resources and robust under rebalanced sampling.  
- Strength: clear task formulation separating causal meaning classification from causal argument identification.  
- Strength: thorough comparative modeling of architectural and training choices, revealing repeatable patterns about encoder independence.  
- Strength: focused qualitative error analysis showing contextual cues resolve ambiguous expressions.  
- Strength: reproducibility via released code/models increases utility and credibility.  
- Weakness: muddled and duplicative theoretical framing with unclear operational theory of causation and confusing counterfactual discussion.  
- Weakness: reliance on AltLex only and modest gains; no cross-dataset evaluation to assess generalization to harder cases.  
- Weakness: underdeveloped and sometimes contradictory architectural rationale; alternative explanations and ablations not explored.  
- Weakness: reporting and clarity issues (misleading Figure 1, unclear padding role, inconsistent hyperparameter descriptions, limited significance testing).  
- Weakness: over-interpretation from single example(s) supporting claims about handling highly ambiguous expressions.  
- Question: what precise operational definition of “causal” guided modeling and annotation usage, and how are events segmented and borderline cases treated?  
- Question: architecture details — are LSTMs bidirectional, are weights shared, was attention tried, and how exactly are sequences truncated/padded?  
- Question: provide consolidated hyperparameters, random seed handling, early stopping, number of runs, and confirm released code/models reproduce reported results.  
- Question: report controls/ablations: markers-only, events-only, learned vs. GloVe embeddings, shared-encoder Siamese, averaging vs. LSTM.  
- Question: provide robustness analyses on ambiguous markers/AltLexes and multiple constructed minimal-pair tests for expressions like “which then”, “since”, “after.”  
- Question: add statistical significance testing for all main comparisons on original splits.  
- Additional: improve Figure 1 with accurate captioning and depiction of both architectures and layer semantics.  
- Additional: tighten and de-duplicate Introduction and Related Work and state concrete contributions earlier.  
- Additional: clarify the role and semantic interpretation of the padding strategies and why they matter.
