- Task: Causal meaning disambiguation — decide if two event spans plus a lexical marker express a causal relation.  
- Model: LSTM-based encoder for e1 and [marker+e2] using GloVe, followed by dense layers for classification.  
- Dataset/eval: Evaluated on AltLex (original + bootstrapped extension) against connective-majority baseline and Hidey & McKeown's feature-rich SVM.  
- Main contribution 1: Simple neural pair-encoding (LSTM over e1 and [marker+e2]) improves over a strong SVM baseline, especially in precision and balanced P/R on ambiguous markers.  
- Main contribution 2: Exploration of architectural/training choices (independent vs state-initialized LSTMs; Adadelta vs Adam; length normalization strategies) and their impact on performance.  
- Main contribution 3: Qualitative evidence that the model generalizes beyond lexical memorization (handles ambiguous markers and non-causal uses of causative verbs).  
- Strength: Clear motivation and focused task framing that separates causal meaning classification from argument identification.  
- Strength: Consistent empirical gains on non-bootstrapped and some bootstrapped configurations, outperforming connective priors.  
- Strength: Useful qualitative analysis with examples showing contextual disambiguation.  
- Strength: Informative ablations (e.g., independent vs state-initialized encoders) including negative results.  
- Strength: Code and data release promotes reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty — architecture is a standard two-encoder + MLP application and gains are modest.  
- Weakness: Potential overfitting / model-selection issues — best configuration may have been chosen on test set; unclear dev/selection protocol and early stopping.  
- Weakness: Lack of statistical significance testing on main/full results.  
- Weakness: Underdeveloped relation to implicit discourse relation classification and missing comparisons to standard implicit DR neural baselines.  
- Weakness: Bootstrapping procedure and dataset expansion are insufficiently described; class imbalance and annotation quality not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: Missing simpler neural baselines (e.g., single BiLSTM over [e1,marker,e2], CNN) and limited analysis of why model favors precision over recall.  
- Question: How does this setup concretely differ from implicit discourse relation classification and how would standard implicit DR baselines perform here?  
- Question: What was the development protocol — which choices were made on dev vs test, and was early stopping used (on which metric)?  
- Question: Can you provide statistical significance for main results on the full datasets?  
- Question: Please clarify the “max/mean/mode” notation for padding/length strategy — preprocessing vs pooling?  
- Question: Why concatenate the marker only with e2 instead of encoding [e1, marker, e2] jointly; were single-sequence BiLSTMs or attention variants tried?  
- Question: Provide full details of the bootstrapping procedure (seed sets, expansion rules, noise control, manual validation rates).  
- Question: Clarify TSV label codes (0–1–2), data licensing/redistribution rights, and provide a README for replication.  
- Additional suggestion: Add simple neural and CNN baselines, report macro-F1 and per-connective breakdowns, and provide error analysis on false negatives to explain P/R skew.  
- Additional suggestion: Improve clarity with architectural diagrams and explicit descriptions of data splits and preprocessing.
