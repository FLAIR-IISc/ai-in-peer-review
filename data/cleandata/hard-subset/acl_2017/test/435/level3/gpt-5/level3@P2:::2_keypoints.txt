- Task and approach: Study of causal meaning disambiguation (given e1, marker, e2) using LSTM-based encoders (two variants) + MLP to predict causal vs non-causal on AltLex.
- Main empirical claim: Simple contextual neural encoder (LSTM+MLP) yields small but consistent gains over a feature-rich SVM baseline on both non-bootstrapped and bootstrapped AltLex.
- Architectural/experimental comparison: Paper compares two LSTM variants (independent vs state-initialized), padding strategies, optimizers, and a class-rebalanced setting.
- Task framing contribution: Decomposes problem into causal meaning classification vs argument identification and focuses on the former without hand-crafted lexica.
- Strength — empirical robustness: Models outperform lexical-prior and SVM baselines across configurations, with qualitative examples showing real disambiguation.
- Strength — clarity and motivation: Well-written motivation arguing for sentence-level context encoding and sensible two-step task formulation.
- Strength — reduced feature engineering: Uses only pre-trained embeddings (no handcrafted lexical/syntactic features), aiding portability.
- Weakness — limited novelty: Architecture is straightforward (two LSTMs + MLP) with modest empirical gains (~1–2 F1 points).
- Weakness — evaluation/protocol concerns: No clear development set or model selection protocol; possible test-set overfitting and lack of statistical significance reporting.
- Weakness — dataset and scope limits: Assumes gold spans (limits practical use), AltLex bootstrapped labels may be noisy, and no analysis isolating ambiguous-marker performance.
- Weakness — missing baselines/ablations: Lacks simple baselines (avg-embeddings+MLP, single LSTM over concatenated input, marker-only baseline) and ablations (marker vs event contributions, random vs pre-trained embeddings).
- Weakness — shallow analysis: Notes precision-recall imbalance but lacks investigation (class weighting, thresholding) or phenomenon-level error analysis.
- Request — validation protocol: Clarify hyperparameter/architecture selection, presence of a dev set, random-seed variance, and significance testing.
- Request — relation to prior work: Explain differences from implicit discourse relation/connective classification and whether those methods apply to AltLex.
- Request — bootstrapping details: Describe bootstrapping expansion of AltLex, selection criteria, noise estimates, and any annotation/IAA.
- Request — marker-level analyses: Provide per-marker results and results restricted to ambiguous markers; control for marker frequency effects.
- Request — data/code/licensing: Clarify label coding in released TSVs (0/1/2) and redistribution rights for augmented data.
- Request — precision vs recall methods: Report whether class weighting, focal loss, or threshold tuning were tried and why optimizer choice affects precision/recall.
- Minor fixes/suggestions: Clarify zero-padding strategy notation, add stronger simple baselines and ablations, report confidence intervals/averages over seeds, include per-class metrics and confusion matrices, and release preprocessing/tokenization details for reproducibility.
