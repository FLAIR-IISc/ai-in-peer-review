- Empirical finding: Contextual neural encoders modestly but consistently improve disambiguation of whether a connective signals causality on the AltLex corpus; authors release TensorFlow code/models.  
- Method: Introduces a simple two-input LSTM architecture (Pair LSTM vs Stated Pair LSTM) with comparisons across padding strategies and optimizers; novelty is incremental.  
- Qualitative analysis: Preliminary analysis of ambiguous expressions suggests potential generalization beyond hand-engineered features.

- Reproducibility: Code and models are released; experiments use both bootstrapping and non-bootstrapping AltLex splits and include strong baselines (frequency baseline and prior SVM work).  
- Empirical support: Neural models outperform baseline B1 and often B2, improving precision and overall accuracy across splits and some downsampling analyses.  
- Design insights: Reporting on independent vs sequential LSTMs, optimizers, and padding strategies offers practical guidance for future work.  
- Corpus-driven framing: Moves away from brittle syntactic/lexical heuristics toward contextualized representations aligned with current practice.

- Theoretical framing: Conceptualization of causation is unclear; paper invokes counterfactual ideas without committing to a theory or articulating a clear stance.  
- Method exposition: Ambiguities in method description—Figure 1 misleadingly shows only Pair LSTM, diagrams lack symbol definitions and precise LSTM connections.  
- Padding discussion: Confusing explanation of padding/aggregation; unclear if padding tokens are masked and why max/mean/mode over sequence length would encode causal meaning.  
- Interpretation risk: Claim that independent LSTMs outperform sequential ones is speculative, counterintuitive, and lacks controls for parameter count or shared weights.  
- Reporting inconsistencies: Hyperparameter settings are inconsistent across the paper, making attributions of gains difficult; example-based evidence (e.g., “which then”) is weak.  
- Evaluation scope: Evaluation limited to AltLex and gains over B2 are modest.

- Theory question: What explicit theory of causation underpins labels and modeling assumptions—clarify stance and implications for annotation/modeling.  
- Example clarification: Clarify the line-256 example regarding event decomposition, truth/falsity judgments, and the resulting causal claim.  
- Padding/aggregation: Precisely specify padding/aggregation behavior—are padding tokens masked and why should aggregation choice affect meaning?  
- Reproducibility details: Provide a consolidated hyperparameter table per result, parameter counts, whether encoders share weights, and a systematic test suite of constructed ambiguous cases (e.g., many “which then” instances).
