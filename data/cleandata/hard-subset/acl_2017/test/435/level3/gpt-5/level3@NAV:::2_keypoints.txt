- Paper addresses disambiguating causal vs. non-causal clause relations without explicit connectives using the AltLex corpus and a GloVe-initialized LSTM model.  
- Main contribution: simple LSTM-based clause representation that slightly outperforms a feature-based AltLex baseline on F1 and accuracy.  
- Contribution: analysis showing the model generalizes beyond explicit connectives and engineered lexicons in some cases.  
- Contribution: problem framing that treats causal-meaning classification separately from argument identification.  
- Strength: clear problem statement, thorough related work, and precise writing.  
- Strength: solid empirical evaluation on the AltLex resource with bootstrapping and non-bootstrapping setups.  
- Strength: proposed architecture is simple, reproducible, and largely resource-agnostic (GloVe + LSTM).  
- Strength: qualitative examples demonstrate correct generalization (ambiguous markers, causative verbs in non-causal usage).  
- Strength: reported improvements in accuracy and F1 over prior work, with higher precision in the best setting.  
- Weakness: risk of test-set tuning—configuration choices appear selected on test, with no clear held-out dev selection.  
- Weakness: missing statistical significance testing and limited discussion of annotation reliability for the AltLex data.  
- Weakness: incomplete methodological details—bootstrapping procedure, 0–1–2 TSV coding, and dataset redistribution rights are unclear.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental; LSTM improvements over CNNs/feature methods are expected and some design choices lack principled justification.  
- Weakness: insufficient baselines and ablations (no 1-layer LSTM, BiLSTM, pooling baselines) and limited analysis of precision vs. recall trade-offs.  
- Question: how does this task differ from implicit connective recognition and why not apply PDTB-style implicit relation models?  
- Question: which hyperparameter/configuration choices were fixed on dev vs. tuned on test—provide dev-set selection and test-only results.  
- Question: detail the bootstrapping procedure used to expand AltLex and describe quality controls.  
- Question: clarify the 0–1–2 coding in released TSVs and confirm licensing/permission for dataset reposting.  
- Question: reconcile reported F1 improvements (0.5–1.5% vs. 2.13%) and add statistical significance tests.  
- Request: add simpler baselines (1-layer LSTM, average-embedding MLP) and analyze the precision–recall trade-off and how to tune it.
