- Paper tackles detecting causal vs. non-causal clause relations when not explicitly marked, using the AltLex dataset.
- Authors argue LSTMs better than CNNs for clause representation and use GloVe embeddings + LSTM + three tanh dense layers + softmax.
- Best system improves roughly 0.5–1.5% F1 over Hidey & McKeown (reported as 2.13% F1 in one place).
- Qualitative and quantitative analyses are provided, including examples where the model generalizes correctly.
- Paper is well written and the problem description is clear.
- Clarification requested on how this task differs from implicit connective recognition and why prior methods cannot be applied.
- Code and (older) data were uploaded, but the meaning of 0-1-2 coding in TSV files is unclear and reposting rights for the dataset are questioned.
- The paper mentions bootstrapping that extends the corpus by ~15% but does not explain the bootstrapping procedure.
- Experimental concern: best configuration appears selected on the test set rather than the dev set, potentially inflating reported gains.
- No statistical significance testing reported; dataset annotation quality (English/SimpleEnglish Wikipedia) may limit score reliability.
- Novelty concern: neural methods outperforming engineered features is expected; contribution may be incremental.
- Suggestion to include simpler baselines (e.g., 1-layer LSTM) and analyses explaining why the model favors precision over recall.
