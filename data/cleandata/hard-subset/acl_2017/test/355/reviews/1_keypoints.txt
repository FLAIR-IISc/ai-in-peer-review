- Uses Grid-type RNNs for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) prediction, a sophisticated neural application.
- Model does not use explicit syntactic structure yet outperforms state-of-the-art systems that do.
- Paper gives a clear, detailed description of implementation and experimental results.
- Special focus on dropped arguments (zero pronouns) which are frequent and challenging in Japanese.
- Multi-sequence model that considers all predicates in a sentence achieves best performance on zero pronouns.
- Overall paper is detailed and clearly written.
- Main weaknesses are minor: several typos and English fluency issues needing correction.
- Suggestion to illustrate the PRED-context point using the Fig. 6 example where the accusative marker is included with the verb.
- Reviewer did not understand the use of boldface in Table 2 and asks for clarification.
- Lists specific typographical corrections across pages 1–8 (many small grammar/article fixes).
