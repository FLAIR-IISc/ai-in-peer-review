- Proposes new Japanese SRL prediction models by adapting the English state-of-the-art model (Zhou & Xu, 2015).
- Extends the single-sequential model with Grid-RNNs to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.
- Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark and achieves significantly better performance than the current state of the art.
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- Proposed model improves accuracy over the previous state-of-the-art system.
- Grid-RNN variant yields slight additional gains, mainly by improving detection of zero arguments.
- Main contribution appears to be an extension from single- to multi-sequential modeling rather than a fundamentally novel method.
- Impact of modeling predicate interactions is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which also extends Ouchi et al. with neural-network modeling.
