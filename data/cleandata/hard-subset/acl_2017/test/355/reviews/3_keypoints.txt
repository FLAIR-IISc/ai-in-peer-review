- Paper proposes joint neural modelling for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and compares to single-sequence RNNs.  
- Solidly executed work achieving state-of-the-art results on a well-established Japanese PAS task.  
- Presentation is mostly accessible to non-Japanese specialists, with helpful figures.  
- Recommend emphasizing task complexity more (e.g., ambiguity between NOM and ACC and contrast with SRL, especially zero-pronoun ambiguity).  
- Ask for statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable to clarify task difficulty.  
- Request clarification on representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns (no marking vs. explicit empty argument) and discussion of implications.  
- Note model appears not to enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per type per predicate; an argument filling only one slot) and request analysis of how well model learns these.  
- Suggest comparative output analysis between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq models to show whether outputs are similar or differ systematically and in which contexts.  
- Point out Multi-Seq mainly improves NOM in Table 3 and ask for discussion of relative differences and contexts of improvement.  
- Including SRL results would be valuable but acknowledged as additional work.  
- Presentation quality is good but contains minor language issues; provide a list of specific typographical/casing corrections.
