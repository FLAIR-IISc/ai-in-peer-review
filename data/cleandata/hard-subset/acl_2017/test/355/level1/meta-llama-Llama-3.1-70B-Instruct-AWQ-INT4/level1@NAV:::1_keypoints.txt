- Summary: Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model that induces multi-predicate interaction features from word sequences for Japanese PAS and achieves SOTA on NAIST without using syntactic parsers.

- Contribution: Introduces a novel neural architecture that captures multi-predicate interactions without relying on syntactic information.

- Contribution: Applies Grid-RNN to model long-distance dependencies and interactions among multiple predicates.

- Contribution: Reports state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming parser-based baselines.

- Strength: Effectively models multi-predicate interactions, addressing a central PAS challenge.

- Strength: Demonstrates strong empirical results (SOTA) on the NAIST dataset.

- Strength: Removes dependence on potentially error-prone parser-derived syntactic features.

- Weakness: Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (NAIST), so cross-dataset or cross-lingual generality is unclear.

- Weakness: No direct comparisons with other neural models for Japanese PAS are provided.

- Weakness: Missing analysis of model hyperparameters and their impact (layers, hidden units, learning rate).

- Question: How will the approach be extended or adapted to other languages or datasets?

- Question: Can the authors provide comparisons with alternative neural PAS models?

- Question: Will the authors analyze and report effects of model parameters and training choices to improve performance?
