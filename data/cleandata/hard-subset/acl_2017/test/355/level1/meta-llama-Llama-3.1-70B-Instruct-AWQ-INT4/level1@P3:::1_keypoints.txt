- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis that captures multi-predicate interactions from sentence word sequences without using syntactic information.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus.  
- Suggests the grid-type architecture is generalizable to other semantic tasks like semantic role labeling (SRL).  

- Novel approach that avoids reliance on syntactic information common in prior work.  
- Empirical effectiveness demonstrated by improved results on a challenging dataset.  
- Clear and well-written presentation of the model and its components.  
- Thorough evaluation including baseline comparisons and ablation studies.  

- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why the grid architecture captures multi-predicate interactions.  
- Limited comparison set—only two baselines, possibly not representative of full state-of-the-art.  
- No error-type analysis to reveal model limitations or failure modes.  
- No discussion of broader potential applications beyond Japanese PAS.  

- Request for theoretical justification of the grid architecture’s effectiveness.  
- Ask how the model handles out-of-vocabulary words and unseen predicates.  
- Request for an error analysis linking mistakes to model limitations.  
- Ask for comparisons to other state-of-the-art models, e.g., transformer-based approaches.  

- Paper would benefit from deeper analysis of limitations and more comprehensive comparisons to recent models.
