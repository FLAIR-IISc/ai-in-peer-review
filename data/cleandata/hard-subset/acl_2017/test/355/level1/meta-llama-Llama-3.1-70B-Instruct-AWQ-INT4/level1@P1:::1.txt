**Summary and Contributions**

This paper proposes a neural model for Japanese predicate argument structure (PAS) analysis, specifically focusing on multi-predicate interactions. The model uses grid-type recurrent neural networks (Grid-RNNs) to capture interactions between predicates without relying on syntactic information. The authors claim three primary contributions: (1) a neural model that effectively captures multi-predicate interactions, (2) a Grid-RNN architecture that outperforms state-of-the-art models without syntactic information, and (3) a demonstration of the model's effectiveness on the NAIST Text Corpus.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective modeling of multi-predicate interactions**: The paper presents a novel approach to modeling multi-predicate interactions using Grid-RNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field of Japanese PAS analysis.
2. **State-of-the-art performance without syntactic information**: The authors demonstrate that their model can achieve state-of-the-art performance without relying on syntactic information, which is a significant advantage over previous models.
3. **Thorough evaluation and analysis**: The paper provides a thorough evaluation and analysis of the model's performance, including comparisons with baseline models and ablation studies.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited comparison with other neural models**: The paper primarily compares the proposed model with traditional models, but does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other neural models for Japanese PAS analysis.
2. **Lack of interpretability**: The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the Grid-RNN architecture captures multi-predicate interactions, which may limit the model's interpretability and understanding.
3. **Dependence on hyperparameter tuning**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameter tuning process, which may raise concerns about the model's robustness and generalizability.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more insight into how the Grid-RNN architecture captures multi-predicate interactions, and how it differs from other neural architectures?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of interpretability in your model, and what methods do you propose for understanding the model's decisions?
3. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process, and how you ensured that the model's performance is robust across different hyperparameter settings?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed model and its evaluation. However, there are some minor issues with formatting and typography that should be addressed in the final version. Additionally, the authors may consider providing more background information on Japanese PAS analysis and its challenges to make the paper more accessible to a broader audience.
