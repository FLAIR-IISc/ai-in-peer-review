- Proposes a neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs to induce multi-predicate interactions from word sequences without syntactic parsers.  
- Evaluated on the NAIST Text Corpus and shown to outperform the state-of-the-art Japanese PAS analyzer.  
- Main contribution: novel Grid-RNN architecture that captures multi-predicate interactions.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art PAS analysis without relying on syntactic information from parsers.  
- Shows significant improvement in identifying zero arguments compared to single-sequence models.  
- Strength: Effective use of Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions.  
- Strength: Achieves strong performance without syntactic features.  
- Strength: Robust evaluation on a standard dataset with thorough baseline comparisons.  
- Strength: Paper is well-organized and clearly presented.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of how Grid-RNNs specifically drive performance gains.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison with other existing neural models for Japanese PAS.  
- Weakness: No error analysis provided to pinpoint remaining challenges.  
- Question: Please provide a detailed analysis of how Grid-RNN contributes to performance improvements.  
- Question: How will the model be extended to handle inter-sentential (Inter-Zero) arguments?  
- Question: Can you compare the model against other neural approaches for Japanese PAS?  
- Additional comment: Novel and well-written work, but would benefit from deeper analysis and broader comparisons.
