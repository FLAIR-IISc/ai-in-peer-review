- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis to capture multi-predicate interactions without syntactic input.
- Claims three contributions: effective multi-predicate interaction modeling, Grid-RNN outperforming state-of-the-art without syntax, and validation on the NAIST Text Corpus.
- Strength: novel and effective modeling of multi-predicate interactions using Grid-RNNs.
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art performance without relying on syntactic information.
- Strength: thorough evaluation including baseline comparisons and ablation studies.
- Weakness: limited comparison with other recent neural models for Japanese PAS.
- Weakness: lacks interpretability—unclear how Grid-RNN captures interactions.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and model robustness/generalizability.
- Question: please clarify how Grid-RNN captures multi-predicate interactions and differs from other neural architectures.
- Question: how will you address model interpretability and methods to explain decisions?
- Question: provide more details on hyperparameter tuning and how performance robustness was ensured.
- Additional: paper is well-written but needs minor formatting and typography fixes.
- Additional: consider adding more background on Japanese PAS and its challenges for broader accessibility.
