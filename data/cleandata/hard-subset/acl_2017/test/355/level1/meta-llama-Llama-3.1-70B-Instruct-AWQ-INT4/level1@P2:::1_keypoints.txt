- Proposes a neural Grid-RNN model for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis that captures multi-predicate interactions without syntactic parsers.
- Introduces single-sequence and multi-sequence variants, with the latter jointly predicting arguments for all predicates in a sentence.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, especially improving zero-argument identification.
- Grid-RNN effectively models long-distance dependencies and multi-predicate interactions.
- Models perform robustly without relying on parser-predicted syntactic information, reducing error propagation.
- Multi-sequence model significantly outperforms single-sequence in F-measure for zero arguments.
- Provides comprehensive comparative analysis against prior work.
- Lacks detailed analysis of model hyperparameters and their impact on performance.
- Omits a thorough error analysis to reveal model limitations.
- Relies on pre-trained word embeddings, which may not be optimal for Japanese PAS.
- Evaluation limited to a single dataset, raising concerns about generalizability across datasets or languages.
- Requests clarification on how model parameters (layers, window size) affect performance.
- Asks for a detailed error analysis to identify failure modes.
- Questions the impact of pre-trained word embeddings on results.
- Suggests evaluating the models on other datasets or languages to demonstrate generalizability.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains models and experiments.
- Recommends more detailed parameter and error analyses to better characterize limitations.
