**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural model for Japanese predicate argument structure (PAS) analysis, which automatically induces features sensitive to multi-predicate interactions from word sequence information of a sentence. The model uses grid-type recurrent neural networks (Grid-RNNs) to capture the interactions between all predicates in a sentence, without relying on syntactic information predicted by parsers. The authors evaluate their model on the NAIST Text Corpus and demonstrate that it outperforms the state-of-the-art Japanese PAS analyzer.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Grid-RNN architecture**: The authors propose a novel Grid-RNN architecture that effectively captures multi-predicate interactions in Japanese PAS analysis. This is the primary contribution of the paper.
2. **Neural PAS analysis without syntactic information**: The authors demonstrate that their neural model can achieve state-of-the-art performance in Japanese PAS analysis without relying on syntactic information predicted by parsers.
3. **Improved performance on zero argument identification**: The authors show that their multi-sequence model significantly outperforms the single-sequence model in identifying zero arguments, which is a challenging task in Japanese PAS analysis.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of Grid-RNNs**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Grid-RNNs in capturing multi-predicate interactions in Japanese PAS analysis.
2. **Improved performance without syntactic information**: The authors show that their neural model can achieve state-of-the-art performance without relying on syntactic information predicted by parsers.
3. **Robust evaluation**: The authors evaluate their model on a standard dataset (NAIST Text Corpus) and provide a thorough comparison with baseline models.
4. **Well-organized and clear presentation**: The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear presentation of their model, experiments, and results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited analysis of Grid-RNNs**: While the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Grid-RNNs, they do not provide a detailed analysis of how the Grid-RNN architecture contributes to the performance improvement.
2. **Lack of comparison with other neural models**: The authors do not compare their model with other neural models that have been proposed for Japanese PAS analysis.
3. **No discussion on error analysis**: The authors do not provide an error analysis of their model, which would help to identify areas for further improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of how the Grid-RNN architecture contributes to the performance improvement in Japanese PAS analysis?
2. How do you plan to extend your model to handle inter-sentential arguments (Inter-Zero)?
3. Can you compare your model with other neural models that have been proposed for Japanese PAS analysis?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear presentation of their model, experiments, and results. The use of Grid-RNNs is a novel contribution to Japanese PAS analysis, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in capturing multi-predicate interactions. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the Grid-RNN architecture and a comparison with other neural models.
