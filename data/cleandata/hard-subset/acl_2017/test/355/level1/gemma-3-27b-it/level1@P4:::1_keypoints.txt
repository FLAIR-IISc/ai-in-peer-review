- Core contribution: Grid-RNN model for Japanese PAS achieving state-of-the-art on NAIST Text Corpus without relying on syntactic parses.  
- Secondary contribution: Empirical demonstration that modeling multi-predicate interactions via Grid-RNNs improves performance, notably for zero anaphora.  
- Additional contribution: Exploration of network depth and residual connections to boost performance.  

- Strength: Strong empirical results beating prior SOTA while avoiding noisy syntactic input, with substantial gains on zero anaphora.  
- Strength: Well-motivated focus on zero anaphora, a key challenge in Japanese PAS, with demonstrable improvements.  
- Strength: Sound architecture choices—Grid-RNNs for multi-predicate interactions and use of residual connections/depth are well justified.  
- Strength: Thorough experimentation with strong baselines, ablations, and bootstrap significance testing.  

- Weakness: Limited novelty of individual components; claiming to "introduce" Grid-RNNs is overstated.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed error analysis to identify remaining failure modes and difficult zero-anaphora types.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to NAIST Text Corpus; generalizability to other datasets/domains is untested.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analysis.  

- Question: Please provide a detailed error analysis—what types of zero anaphora are hardest to resolve?  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to embedding dimensionality (dword) and other hyperparameters?  
- Question: What adaptations or challenges would arise applying the model to other languages (e.g., English)?  
- Question: Which methods for leveraging large-scale unlabeled data are being considered (pretraining, semi-supervised, etc.)?  

- Additional comment: References formatting could be more consistent.  
- Additional comment: Figures are clear and helpful.  
- Additional comment: Writing is generally clear and concise.
