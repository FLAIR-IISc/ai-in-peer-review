- Proposes Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, reducing reliance on syntactic parses.  
- Automatically induces features from word sequences to model predicate interactions, improving zero pronoun resolution.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus without using syntactic information.  

- Novel and effective adaptation of Grid-RNNs for multi-predicate relationships, supported by ablation studies.  
- Strong empirical improvements over prior state-of-the-art with statistical significance testing.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with helpful figures and detailed experimental setup.  
- Notable performance gains specifically on zero pronoun resolution, a key challenge in Japanese PAS.  
- Source code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.  

- Lacks detailed qualitative error analysis to reveal systematic failure modes (e.g., specific predicates/structures).  
- Limited comparisons to more recent neural end-to-end models for Japanese PAS beyond Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Brief treatment of hyperparameter sensitivity; unclear which settings critically affect performance.  
- Evaluation restricted to the relatively small NAIST corpus, limiting evidence of generalizability.  

- Request for a detailed error analysis identifying common error types and causes.  
- Ask which recent neural PAS models were considered and why they were not included in comparisons.  
- Ask how sensitive results are to hyperparameters like learning rate, embedding size, and layer count.  
- Ask whether pre-trained word embeddings were tried or could improve performance.  

- Citation style is inconsistently applied in places.  
- Recommend reporting computational cost of Grid-RNNs versus simpler RNNs.  
- Suggest exploring interpretability/visualization of learned Grid-RNN interactions.
