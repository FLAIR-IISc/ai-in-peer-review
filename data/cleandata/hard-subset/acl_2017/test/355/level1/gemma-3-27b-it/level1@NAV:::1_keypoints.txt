- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model to directly capture multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, avoiding reliance on syntactic parsing.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, especially improving zero-pronoun resolution, without using syntactic information.  
- Main contribution: successful application of Grid-RNNs to model predicate interactions in PAS.  
- Secondary contribution: empirical validation on a standard benchmark showing tangible performance gains.  
- Strength: strong empirical results outperforming Ouchi et al. (2015), notably on zero arguments.  
- Strength: novel and well-motivated use of Grid-RNNs to model predicate interactions.  
- Strength: reduces error propagation by removing dependence on parser output.  
- Strength: clear presentation, detailed experimental setup, and available source code for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of how Grid-RNNs learn interactions; lacks visualizations and deeper ablations.  
- Weakness: narrow comparative scope—mainly compared to one baseline; lacks comparisons to transformer-based methods.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to the relatively small NAIST corpus; generalizability to larger/diverse datasets is unclear.  
- Question: can the authors provide concrete examples of interactions the Grid-RNN captures that help zero-pronoun resolution?  
- Question: were different pre-trained word embeddings (e.g., Word2Vec, fastText) tried, and what was their impact?  
- Question: how does performance scale with sentence length and are there grid-architecture limitations for long sentences?  
- Additional comment: connection to English SRL literature is well-established.  
- Additional comment: residual connections seem beneficial but require a more detailed ablation study.
