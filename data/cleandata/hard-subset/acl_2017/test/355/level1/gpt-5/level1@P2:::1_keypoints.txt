- Task & approach: Syntax-free Japanese PAS with two neural sequence models — Single-Seq (predicate-wise stacked BiRNN) and Multi-Seq Grid-RNN (jointly models all predicates with cross-sequence interactions and residuals).
- Main contributions: Grid-type recurrent architecture achieving SOTA on NAIST 1.5, empirical evidence that multi-predicate interactions help (esp. zero anaphora) with significance tests, and depth/residual analysis showing benefits of deeper RNNs.

- Strength: Strong overall results without syntax, notably large gains on zero arguments (e.g., overall F1 81.42 vs 79.23 Ouchi+15; Zero +3.0 F).
- Strength: Demonstrated, statistically significant benefit of multi-predicate interaction over a strong single-predicate model (small but reliable improvements).
- Strength: Substantial improvements on sparse/difficult DAT roles, suggesting sequence context can learn robust case-role patterns.
- Strength: Depth and residual connection analysis shows measurable gains from deeper recurrent stacks, supporting model design.
- Strength: Sound evaluation practices (standard splits, no external resources, bootstrap significance, strong baselines).

- Weakness: No decoding constraints discussed to enforce at-most-one-filler-per-case per predicate; paper lacks analysis of duplicate/ill-formed outputs and violation rates.
- Weakness: Missing ablations on input features, class weighting, and simpler multi-predicate alternatives (shared BiRNN + predicate MLP or attention), leaving attribution of gains unclear.
- Weakness: Multi-Seq gains over Single-Seq are modest and mixed across roles, indicating benefits are case-specific and need deeper analysis.
- Weakness: Claims about avoiding parser error propagation are not fully substantiated — no comparisons to models using gold syntax or injecting syntactic features.
- Weakness: Limited analysis depth — no breakdown by sentence length or argument distance, no error-type analysis, and no runtime/memory scaling analysis for Grid-RNN (M×T cost).

- Questions: How is well-formedness enforced at decoding (one argument per case)? If not, how frequent are violations and how are they scored?
- Questions: Did you try a shared sentence-level BiRNN with predicate-specific classifiers or simple cross-predicate attention/message-passing? How do accuracy and efficiency compare?
- Questions: Are baselines trained/evaluated with identical predicate sets and filtering as Ouchi+15? Please clarify any differences affecting comparability.
- Questions: What are training/inference complexity and GPU memory for Multi-Seq vs Single-Seq, and how does performance scale with number of predicates M?
- Questions: Did you explore class-imbalance handling (loss weighting) for the large null class, and what was its impact?
- Questions: Can you provide breakdowns by argument distance and sentence length to support long-distance claims?
- Questions: Confirm code availability URL and reproduction steps for reported numbers.

- Overall: Novelty moderate, soundness generally solid but needs decoding and ablation improvements, significance good (esp. zero anaphora and DAT), clarity good; recommendation: lean accept contingent on clarifying decoding and strengthening analyses/ablations.
