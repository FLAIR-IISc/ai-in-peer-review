- Paper proposes a BiRNN single-sequence model achieving SOTA on NAIST 1.5 without syntax, plus a Grid-RNN multi-sequence model to jointly model predicates and improve Zero argument prediction, and shows deeper RNNs with residuals help performance.

- Strong empirical gains over syntax-free baselines (single 81.15 F1, multi 81.42 F1 vs prior 79.23/78.15), with larger improvements on Zero arguments and statistical significance for multi vs single.

- Grid-RNN’s multi-predicate interaction without syntactic input is novel in this setting and yields improvements especially for Zero arguments, matching linguistic intuition.

- Model performs well on sparse/infrequent cases (notably dative Dep), suggesting robustness and effective use of sentence-level cues.

- Method is simple and resource-light (only word indices, predicate window, predicate marker), avoiding POS/parses/external embeddings.

- Depth and residual connection study provides practical architectural guidance, enabling deeper RNN/Grid-RNN stacks and modest F1 gains.

- Limited novelty: approach composes known components (BiRNN/GRU, softmax tagging, residuals, grid connections) without deeper theoretical or algorithmic advances.

- Inference constraints are under-specified: unclear whether and how the model enforces at most one argument per case per predicate or prevents multiple-token same-case outputs.

- Incomplete comparisons and ablations: no CRF/max-margin single-predicate baseline, no simpler predicate-interaction baselines (e.g., attention), no syntax-aware variants, and missing feature ablations (MARK, window size).

- Evaluation/setup details unclear: which predicates are used at test time (gold predicates? excluding predicates without intra-sentential arguments?), and how that affects comparability to prior work.

- Statistical significance vs prior baselines (Ouchi et al., Imamura et al.) is not reported.

- Scalability and efficiency concerns: Grid-RNN complexity grows with number of predicates×tokens (M×T); no runtime, memory, batching, or convergence details provided.

- Requests clarification on inference: how PAS constraints (one argument per case) are enforced with per-token softmax outputs and evaluation of multiple-token predictions.

- Requests clarification on test-time predicate set: whether predicates are gold and whether predicates without intra-sentential arguments are excluded, plus performance if all predicates are included.

- Asks for significance testing results comparing to prior work.

- Asks for ablations: remove MARK, vary predicate window C, and replace grid connections with simpler interactions (shared BiRNN with predicate indicators or cross-predicate attention).

- Requests runtime/memory statistics and typical number of predicates per sentence, and discussion of handling long sentences and variable predicate counts.

- Suggests error analysis and qualitative examples showing where grid helps (especially Zero-NOM) to demonstrate captured long-distance and cross-predicate cues.

- Recommends reporting results with and without gold tokenization and with larger/pretrained embeddings to separate architectural gains from optimization/embedding choices.
