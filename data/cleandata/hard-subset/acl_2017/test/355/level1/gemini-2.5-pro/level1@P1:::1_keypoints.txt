- Proposes neural sequence models for Japanese PAS: a baseline stacked Bi-RNN (single-sequence) and a Grid-RNN multi-sequence model that jointly analyzes all predicates.  
- Main contribution: novel application of Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions without syntactic features.  
- Achieves new state-of-the-art on NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming syntax-based baselines, with largest gains on zero argument identification.  
- Multi-sequence Grid-RNN yields ~2 point overall F-measure improvement and notable jump on zero arguments (44.09 → 47.12).  
- Well-designed experiments and ablation: compares single- vs. multi-sequence to isolate Grid-RNN benefits; breakdowns by argument type and case role.  
- Clear writing and model diagrams; authors plan to release code, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Grid-RNN architecture choices lack justification—unidirectional predicate dimension may block later→earlier information flow.  
- Weakness: Little qualitative analysis showing what multi-predicate interactions the model actually learns or example cases where it succeeds vs. single-sequence.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison/discussion with recent neural PAS models (e.g., Shibata et al., Iida et al.); direct numeric comparison absent and conceptual trade-offs under-discussed.  
- Question: How are predicates ordered in the grid (presumably linear sentence order), and was predicate-order or bidirectional predicate modeling investigated?  
- Question: Why does the model show an unusually large (~30 F1) improvement on Dative (DAT) Dep arguments compared to NOM/ACC—any hypothesis?  
- Question: Did the authors try richer input features (e.g., learned relative-position embeddings between candidate and predicate) to potentially boost performance further?
