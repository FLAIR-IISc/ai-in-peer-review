- Novel application of a Grid-RNN to jointly model multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS without relying on syntactic parses.
- Strong empirical result: a purely sequence-based model achieves new state-of-the-art on the NAIST Text Corpus, especially improving zero-pronoun argument detection.
- Method is well-motivated and addresses parser error propagation by forgoing syntactic features.
- Convincing, statistically significant experimental gains over prior SOTA and the authors' single-sequence baseline.
- Thorough analysis breaking down performance by argument type and case, showing notable gains for zero and dative arguments.
- Lacks comparison or justification versus contemporary architectures like Transformers/self-attention.
- Input features are very simple (word indices + predicate marker) with no ablation to show each feature's contribution.
- Scalability concerns: Grid-RNN complexity scales with words × predicates and the paper does not discuss computational cost for sentences with many predicates.
