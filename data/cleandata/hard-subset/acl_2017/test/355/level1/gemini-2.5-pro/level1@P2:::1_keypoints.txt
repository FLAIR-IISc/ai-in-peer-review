- Task: Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis focusing on zero anaphora (omitted pronouns) in a pro-drop language.
- Models: baseline single-sequence Bi-RNN (per-predicate) and proposed multi-sequence Grid-RNN that jointly models all predicates in a sentence.
- Contribution 1: Novel application of Grid-RNNs to jointly model multi-predicate interactions across predicates and sentence positions.
- Contribution 2: Empirical demonstration that a purely sequence-based model (no syntactic features) outperforms prior syntax-aware joint models on NAIST.
- Strength 1: Strong empirical gains — multi-sequence achieves SOTA 81.42 F1 vs prior 79.23, with +3.03 F1 on Zero argument identification.
- Strength 2: Architecture well-motivated for the linguistic problem; inter-sequence connections plausibly capture predicate interactions and improve Zero predictions (+1.02 F1 over single-sequence).
- Strength 3: Solid experimental setup and analyses (standard dataset/splits, single-sequence ablation, case-type breakdowns, depth/residual experiments).
- Weakness 1: Lacks qualitative/interpretability analysis showing examples of what multi-sequence interactions the model captures.
- Weakness 2: Scalability and computational cost unaddressed — M x T Grid-RNN may be expensive; small minibatches suggest heavy resource use.
- Weakness 3: Minor clarity issue in feature description (PRED feature/windowing) that hinders reproducibility.
- Question 1: Request for a concrete test-case where multi-sequence correctly finds a Zero argument missed by single-sequence, with analysis of the captured interaction.
- Question 2: Request for details on computational complexity, average/max predicates per sentence, and training/inference time comparison to single-sequence.
