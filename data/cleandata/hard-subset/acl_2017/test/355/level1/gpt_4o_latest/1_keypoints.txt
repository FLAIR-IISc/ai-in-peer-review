- Proposes a Grid-RNN architecture to model multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, addressing argument omission in pro-drop languages.
- Demonstrates the model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus, especially for zero argument identification.
- Removes dependence on syntactic parsers, reducing error propagation and simplifying the pipeline.
- Novel architecture effectively captures intra- and inter-sequence dependencies crucial for PAS analysis.
- Strong empirical results with notable improvement in F-measure for zero arguments compared to baselines.
- Thorough ablation studies and depth/residual analyses that clarify architectural contributions.
- Authors claim potential applicability to other languages and tasks (e.g., SRL), indicating broader relevance.
- Missing discussion of computational efficiency and possible overhead introduced by Grid-RNNs (training/inference/scalability).
- Lacks comparison with models using external resources or pre-trained embeddings, limiting evaluation scope.
- No experiments or qualitative analysis to substantiate claims of generalization to other languages.
- Question: How does Grid-RNN computational cost compare to traditional RNN approaches and what are the trade-offs?
- Question: Could performance improve with pre-trained embeddings or external resources, and why were these not tested?
- Question: Have the authors tried applying the model to other pro-drop languages or SRL tasks, and what challenges are expected?
- Paper is well-written and clear but would benefit from a deeper discussion of limitations and potential extensions.
