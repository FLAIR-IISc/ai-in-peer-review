- Proposes a neural approach for Japanese PAS analysis that avoids parser-based syntactic input by modeling multi-predicate interactions from word sequences.
- Claims state-of-the-art PAS performance without syntactic information, challenging prior assumptions.
- Introduces a multi-sequence Grid-RNN to capture interactions between all predicates in a sentence.
- Significantly improves zero-argument identification, with a 3.0-point F-measure gain (47.12% vs. 44.09%) and ~30-point improvement on the dative case.
- Avoids pipeline errors from parsers and achieves strong results on the NAIST Text Corpus.
- Demonstrates the value of modeling multi-predicate interactions directly from text.
- Novelty is questionable due to similarity with Kalchbrenner et al. (2016) Grid LSTM and limited explanation of advances.
- Overall F-measure gain is modest (only 0.27% absolute), making added model complexity hard to justify.
- Fails to explain why prior neural Japanese PAS models (e.g., Shibata et al. 2016) did not reach similar results, weakening the novelty claim.
- Comparison with English SRL work (Zhou and Xu 2015) is superficial and doesn't establish clear generalization.
- Questions: how does your Grid-RNN differ from Kalchbrenner et al.'s Grid LSTM and why is it better for Japanese PAS?
- Questions: provide ablation studies for residual connections and multi-sequence vs. single-sequence contributions.
- Questions: why did previous neural approaches for Japanese PAS not achieve comparable results without syntactic information?
