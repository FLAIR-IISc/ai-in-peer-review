- Proposes a neural Japanese PAS analysis that removes reliance on syntactic parsers to avoid error propagation.  
- Introduces a single-sequence BiRNN model capturing full-sentence context for each predicate.  
- Introduces a multi-sequence Grid-RNN model that explicitly models interactions among all predicates from word sequences.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance without syntactic information, with notable gains on zero argument identification.  
- Strength: Addresses parser-induced error propagation, showing neural models can succeed without syntactic parses.  
- Strength: Strong experimental results—multi-sequence achieves 81.42% F with significant improvement and large gain on zero args (47.12% vs 44.09%).  
- Strength: Thorough ablation studies showing multi-sequence > single-sequence and deeper residual networks improve performance.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty in applying Grid-RNNs; paper doesn’t clearly differentiate its instantiation from Kalchbrenner et al. (2016).  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to prior neural approaches that used syntactic features—unfair baselines.  
- Weakness: Lacks qualitative examples or analysis explaining how Grid-RNN captures multi-predicate interactions and improves zero argument detection.  
- Question: How exactly does your Grid-RNN differ in connection patterns or training from Kalchbrenner et al. (2016), and what specific interactions does it capture?  
- Question: Can you re-implement prior neural models without syntactic features to provide a fair comparison?  
- Question: Can you provide concrete examples showing how the multi-sequence model identifies zero arguments compared to the single-sequence model?
