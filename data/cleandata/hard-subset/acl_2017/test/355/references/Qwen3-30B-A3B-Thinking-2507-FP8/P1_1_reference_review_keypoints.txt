- Proposes single-sequence (BiRNN) and multi-sequence (Grid-RNN) neural models for Japanese PAS, focusing on zero argument identification and showing multi-predicate interactions improve performance.  
- Main contribution: a Grid-RNN multi-sequence model that captures multi-predicate interactions and improves PAS analysis, especially for zero arguments.  
- Main contribution: shows state-of-the-art PAS performance can be achieved without relying on syntactic parser information.  
- Main contribution: provides analysis demonstrating multi-predicate interactions particularly help zero argument identification.  
- Strength: strong empirical gains for zero arguments (47.12% F1 vs 44.09% prior SOTA), with a clear explanation of why multi-predicate interactions help.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation against strong baselines, per-case analysis (NOM/ACC/DAT), and statistical significance testing.  
- Strength: clear explanation and motivation of the Grid-RNN architecture for modeling predicate interactions.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—Grid-RNNs were previously proposed (Graves & Schmidhuber 2009) and the paper doesn’t clearly define novel architectural aspects.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with prior neural approaches and unclear why previous models couldn’t achieve similar results without syntactic features.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis comparing Grid-RNNs to simpler alternatives (e.g., single RNN over the sentence) to justify necessity of the grid structure.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and generalizability (longer sentences, non-newspaper domains, computational costs, cross-linguistic applicability).  
- Question: how does your Grid-RNN implementation specifically differ from Graves & Schmidhuber (2009) and what novel aspects are leveraged?  
- Question: why not use a single RNN over the full sentence—how does Grid-RNN better capture multi-predicate interactions?  
- Question: have you tested other domains/corpora (novels, web text) and how might performance vary on longer/complex sentences?  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Grid-RNN novelty and provide more detailed comparisons with alternative architectures.
