- Paper proposes neural sequence models for Japanese PAS, including a single-sequence BiRNN and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN to handle zero pronoun identification.  
- Main contributions: parser-free neural PAS approach, novel Grid-RNN for multi-predicate interactions, and improved results on NAIST TC 1.5, especially for zero arguments.

- Strength: Removes reliance on syntactic parsers and avoids error propagation by working directly on word sequences.  
- Strength: Multi-sequence model yields a measurable improvement on zero-argument F-measure (47.12% vs. 46.09%).  
- Strength: Grid-RNN is a clear architectural innovation tailored to multi-predicate interactions.

- Weakness: "State-of-the-art" claim is overstated given modest gains and missing comparison to Iida et al. (2016).  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why the model succeeds, especially which multi-predicate interactions enable zero argument resolution.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparisons with recent neural approaches (e.g., direct adaptation of Zhou & Xu 2015 to Japanese).  
- Weakness: No scalability analysis of Grid-RNN computational cost for sentences with many predicates.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of model limitations (long sentences, complex predicate relations, RNN long-range dependency issues).

- Question: How does the model compare to Iida et al. (2016) and why does it outperform their neural approach?  
- Question: Can authors provide concrete examples showing which predicate relationships help zero argument identification?  
- Question: How does O(M^2T) Grid-RNN complexity affect performance on longer or predicate-dense sentences?

- Overall: Well-motivated, technically sound work with meaningful zero-argument gains and a clever Grid-RNN, but requires additional comparisons, deeper analysis, and discussion of limitations — recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions.
