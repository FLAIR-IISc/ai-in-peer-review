- Proposes neural models for Japanese PAS that induce multi-predicate interaction features without using syntactic parsers (single-sequence BiRNN and multi-sequence Grid-RNN).
- Main contribution: multi-sequence Grid-RNN effectively models multi-predicate interactions.
- Achieves state-of-the-art on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, notably +3 F for zero argument identification.
- Shows neural models can match or surpass parser-based pipelines, challenging reliance on syntactic parsers.
- Thorough experimental evaluation and ablation studies support the proposed architecture (Grid-RNN + residuals + depth).
- Substantial improvement on the challenging zero pronoun (omitted argument) identification task.
- Limited novelty: Grid-RNNs are from prior work and paper lacks strong justification for choosing them over transformers/attention.
- Insufficient comparison with more recent neural approaches (e.g., post-2016 models, multi-column CNNs, contextual embeddings).
- Claim of "no syntactic information" is misleading because features like the PRED window are syntactically motivated.
- Questions for authors: justify Grid-RNN vs other architectures; compare to recent/contextualized models (e.g., BERT); clarify what syntactic info is truly avoided.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Grid-RNN novelty and the "no syntactic information" claim.
