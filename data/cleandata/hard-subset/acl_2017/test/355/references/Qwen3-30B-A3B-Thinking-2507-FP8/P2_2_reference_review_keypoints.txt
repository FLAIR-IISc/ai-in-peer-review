- Proposes neural sequence models (Grid-RNNs) to capture multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS without relying on syntactic parsers.  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance without syntactic information and improved zero argument identification.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements on NAIST Text Corpus, especially for zero arguments and dative cases.  

- Addresses the challenging and practically important problem of zero argument identification in Japanese.  
- Provides strong experimental validation with comparisons to solid baselines (Imamura+09, Ouchi+15).  
- Clearly explains and visualizes the Grid-RNN technical contribution and its adaptation to PAS.  
- Achieves notable performance gains (81.42% F1 vs. prior 79.23%), with largest gains on zero arguments.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized with clear motivation and presentation.  

- Limited architectural novelty: Grid-RNN is not new and adaptation differences are not fully highlighted.  
- Insufficient comparison to neural SRL approaches for English and lack of discussion on dependency differences.  
- Missing ablation study to attribute gains to specific components (multi-sequence vs. residuals, etc.).  
- Limited discussion of limitations (performance on longer sentences, complex zero pronoun cases, generalizability beyond NAIST).  
- No direct comparison to neural models that incorporate syntactic information, leaving contribution source unclear.  

- How does the multi-sequence Grid-RNN compare to a simple concatenated-predicate single RNN baseline?  
- Why is the improvement for zero arguments small (1.02%) and seemingly inconsistent with expectations?  
- How much of the performance gain stems from using word sequence information versus from the neural architecture itself?  

- Overall: a strong, well-executed paper with meaningful contributions to Japanese PAS analysis that merits acceptance despite some comparative and analysis gaps.
