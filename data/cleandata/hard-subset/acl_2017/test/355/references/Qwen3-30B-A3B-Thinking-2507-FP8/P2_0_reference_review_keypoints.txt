- Proposes neural models for Japanese PAS focused on zero argument identification: a single-sequence BiRNN and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN for predicate interactions.
- Main contributions: parser-free neural PAS model, Grid-RNN for multi-predicate interactions, and significant gains in zero argument identification.
- Shows a 3.0-point F-measure improvement for zero arguments over prior state-of-the-art (Ouchi et al., 2015).
- Eliminates reliance on syntactic parsers, avoiding error propagation while improving performance.
- Uses a clear experimental setup on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 with proper splits, multiple baselines, and statistically significant results.
- Reports substantial gains for less frequent roles (notably the dative case) and provides per-case-role analysis.
- Architecture is well-motivated and clearly explained; Grid-RNN extension aligns with linguistic intuition about predicate semantics.
- Lacks comparison with more recent neural/transformer-based SRL/PAS models (e.g., BERT-based approaches).
- Limited qualitative analysis of why multi-predicate interactions help—no case studies demonstrating specific successes.
- Insufficient ablation studies to isolate contributions of Grid-RNN vs other components and residual connections.
- Fails to discuss failure cases and limitations of the proposed models.
- Asks how Grid-RNN compares to transformers for capturing multi-predicate interactions and zero argument ID.
- Requests specific examples where multi-predicate interactions were crucial and explanation of Grid-RNN’s role.
- Requests information on computational costs (training time, memory) of Grid-RNN vs single-sequence and parser-based approaches.
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution that meaningfully advances Japanese PAS without parsers; recommendation to accept.
