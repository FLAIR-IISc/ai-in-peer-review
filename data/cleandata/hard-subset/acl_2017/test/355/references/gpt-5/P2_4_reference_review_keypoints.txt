- Proposes two syntax-free neural sequence models for Japanese PAS: single-sequence BiRNN and multi-sequence Grid-RNN that jointly models predicates.
- Claims Grid-RNN achieves state-of-the-art on NAIST 1.5 by capturing multi-predicate interactions and improves Zero argument prediction.

- Grid-RNN obtains 81.42 overall F1 and 47.12 F1 on Zero arguments, outperforming prior SOTA (e.g., 79.23 overall).
- Empirical gains are especially strong for Zero and DAT roles, showing robustness beyond zero anaphora focus.
- Methodologically principled extension of stacked BiRNNs to a grid to allow inter-sequence information flow without syntactic inputs.
- No reliance on syntactic parsers or external resources while still surpassing syntax-dependent prior systems.
- Ablation study shows deeper networks with residual connections improve performance and stability.
- Comprehensive evaluation across case roles (NOM/ACC/DAT/Dep/Zero).

- Limited analysis of what Grid-RNN actually learns and why inter-sequence interactions yield gains; modest delta over single-sequence model.
- Unclear decoding/structural constraints: unclear if uniqueness constraints per case per predicate are enforced at inference.
- Reproducibility concerns: placeholder code URL and missing implementation/training details (exact dimensions, batching, runtime/memory).
- Scope limited to intra-sentential PAS on a single dataset; no experiments on other pro-drop languages or syntax-aware neural baselines.
- Computational complexity and scalability (O(MT) states, scan order, parallelism) not discussed.
