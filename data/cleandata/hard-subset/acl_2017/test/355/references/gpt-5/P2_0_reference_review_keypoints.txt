- Problem & approach: Tackles Japanese PAS (Dep/Zero) with two syntax-free neural models — single-sequence BiGRU and multi-sequence Grid-RNN with residual connections.  
- Claim: Both models outperform strong syntax-based baselines on NAIST TC 1.5, with Grid-RNN best overall and especially for zero arguments.  
- Main contributions: Effective syntax-free PAS approach; an end-to-end Grid-RNN for multi-predicate interactions; empirical study showing benefits of depth and residual connections.  
- Key empirical result: Overall F1 improves from 79.23 (Ouchi+15) to 81.15 (single-seq) and 81.42 (multi-seq); Zero F1 from 44.09 to 46.10/47.12.  
- Strength — multi-predicate gains: Grid-RNN yields consistent, statistically significant but modest gains, supporting cross-predicate cues helping zero anaphora.  
- Strength — evaluation rigor: Uses standard NT C 1.5 splits, strong baselines, bootstrap significance testing, and informative per-role breakdowns (large DAT gains).  
- Strength — depth/residuals: Deeper stacks with residual connections give ~+1 F1, addressing optimization issues and improving contextual encoding.  
- Strength — simplicity/practicality: Avoiding parser dependencies reduces pipeline errors and eases deployment for weak-parser domains/languages.  
- Weakness — marginal Grid-RNN gains: Improvements over a strong single-seq baseline are small, requiring stronger ablations to isolate inter-sequence contributions.  
- Weakness — lack of constraints/decoding discussion: No explicit global constraints reported (e.g., single NOM per predicate) or analysis of illegal assignments and their impact.  
- Weakness — comparative & reproducibility gaps: Limited harmonized comparisons, anonymized code link, and single-run results without seed variance reported.  
- Weakness — limited analysis of multi-predicate effect: No qualitative examples or stratified analyses (e.g., by number of predicates, antecedent distance) to demonstrate when interactions help.  
- Weakness — technical clarity gaps: Grid-RNN computation order, predicate-order invariance, PRED label role, and initialization/padding details are underspecified.  
- Requested ablations/analyses: Enforce/report constraints frequency; parameter-matched deeper single-seq; multi-seq with inter-sequence disabled; predicate-order shuffling; stratification by predicate count.  
- Requested robustness/efficiency info: Report mean/std over seeds, qualitative examples of flips from cross-predicate info, antecedent-distance breakdown, and training/inference costs vs. M and T.  
- Overall assessment: Moderate novelty and solid empirical gains; generally sound but weakened by missing constraints, ablations, and technical details; significance is practical and of interest to PAS/SRL researchers.
