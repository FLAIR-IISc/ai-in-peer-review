- Paper tackles Japanese PAS zero-argument resolution using sequence-only neural models instead of syntax-heavy joint models.
- Proposes a single-sequence BiRNN/GRU that predicts case labels per token relative to a predicate without syntactic input.
- Proposes a multi-sequence Grid-RNN that jointly processes all predicates via inter-sequence connections and uses residual links for deeper stacking.
- Empirically outperforms prior syntactic baselines on NAIST 1.5, with stronger gains on zero arguments.
- Strong empirical results without external resources: All F1 79.23 → 81.15 → 81.42 and Zero F1 44.09 → 46.10 → 47.12 (single → multi improvements reported).
- Demonstrates a neural mechanism for multi-predicate interactions, with Grid-RNN giving ~+1.0 F1 on Zero arguments over the single model.
- Shows that residual connections allow stable deeper models (6–8 layers) with performance gains.
- Practical benefit: removes dependency on parsers, reducing error propagation and improving robustness and portability.
- Novelty is moderate since multidimensional/Grid RNN ideas are existing; contribution is mainly an application to PAS.
- Decoding/structural constraints under-specified: unclear how at-most-one-argument-per-case per predicate is enforced.
- Limited baselines and ablations for multi-predicate modeling—no comparisons to simpler interaction mechanisms or graph-based encoders.
- Analysis is shallow: lacks qualitative/error analysis by distance, argument type, and predicate density; some reported per-case claims seem inconsistent.
- Reproducibility concerns: code link is placeholder and key details (decoding, batching, tokenization, handling unknowns) are missing.
- Question: How are uniqueness constraints (one argument per case per predicate) enforced during decoding or post-processing?
- Question: Why restrict inter-sequence links to neighbor predicates; were fully connected or different orders tried and how sensitive are results to ordering?
- Question: What is the role/impact of the PRED label and MARK feature; were these ablated to check for trivial cue leakage?
- Question: What is the average number of predicates per sentence, and how do training/inference times scale relative to the single model?
- Question: Please provide error breakdowns by predicate-argument distance and by sentence predicate count, and re-check Table 3 (DAT claim).
- Overall: Significance is solid, originality moderate, soundness mostly good but needs clarification on decoding and deeper analysis.
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on clarifying decoding constraints, adding ablations/analyses, and improving reproducibility.
