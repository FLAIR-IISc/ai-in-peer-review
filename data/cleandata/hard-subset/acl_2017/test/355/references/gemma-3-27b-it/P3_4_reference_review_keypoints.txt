- Introduces single-sequence and Grid-RNN multi-sequence models for Japanese PAS, tackling zero pronouns and achieving SOTA on the NAIST corpus.  
- Shows Grid-RNNs effectively model multi-predicate interactions and improve zero-pronoun resolution.  
- Establishes a strong neural baseline without relying on explicit syntactic inputs and reports significant improvements over prior work.  
- Conducts thorough experiments including ablation studies (network depth, residuals) and per-case-role analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, figures clarify the architecture, and code is publicly released.  
- Lacks a detailed error analysis identifying specific failure types and syntactic contexts where the model fails.  
- Does not compare against more recent transformer or pre-trained language model based PAS approaches.  
- Provides limited detail on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, especially regarding zero-pronoun optimization.  
- Omits discussion of Grid-RNN computational cost and scalability compared to the single-sequence model and alternatives.  
- Asks authors for: deeper error analysis, experiments with pre-trained/contextual embeddings, hyperparameter search details, computational overhead info, and linguistic justification for input features (ARG, PRED, MARK).  
- Notes that wording “without syntactic information” is misleading since word order encodes syntax; suggests clarifying phrasing.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel contribution with effective Grid-RNN modeling and recommended acceptance pending author responses.
