- Overall: Proposes Grid-RNN multi-sequence model for Japanese PAS to handle zero pronouns, achieving state-of-the-art on NAIST Text Corpus.

- Contribution 1: Empirically shows Grid-RNNs effectively model multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS.

- Contribution 2: Establishes a strong baseline and achieves competitive, state-of-the-art results on a standard benchmark.

- Contribution 3: Targets zero anaphora explicitly and reports specific gains on this challenging aspect.

- Strength 1: Solid experimental validation with comparisons to strong baselines, statistical testing, and ablation studies.

- Strength 2: Clear motivation and problem definition highlighting issues with argument omission and syntax-dependent pipelines.

- Strength 3: Well-structured, readable paper with effective figures and comprehensive related work.

- Strength 4: Source code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.

- Strength 5: Justified and effective use of Grid-RNNs to model predicate interactions without heavy syntactic reliance.

- Weakness 1: Individual components (RNNs, Grid-RNNs) lack novelty; novelty is mainly in application and empirical demonstration.

- Weakness 2: Lacks qualitative analysis of model successes and failures, especially for zero anaphora cases.

- Weakness 3: Limited comparison to more recent neural PAS approaches (e.g., Transformer or pre-trained LM based methods).

- Weakness 4: Hyperparameter tuning details and search process are insufficiently transparent for full reproducibility.

- Question 1: Request examples where multi-sequence Grid-RNN outperforms single-sequence, particularly for zero anaphora.

- Question 2: Ask about sensitivity to the PRED feature window size (C) and results from different window sizes.

- Question 3: Inquire whether pre-trained word embeddings were tried for initialization and potential impact.

- Question 4: Ask how performance scales with sentence length and whether long-range dependencies pose problems.

- Additional comment 1: Recommend clarifying notation for omitted arguments (ϕi) in the introduction.

- Additional comment 2: Suggest discussing computational complexity of Grid-RNN versus single-sequence model.

- Recommendation: Accept — solid empirical contribution despite limited architectural novelty, with suggested improvements.
