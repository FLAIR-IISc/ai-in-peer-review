- Paper proposes neural models (single-sequence Bi-RNN and multi-sequence Grid-RNN) for Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis to handle argument omission.
- Main contribution: empirical evidence that modeling multi-predicate interactions with Grid-RNNs improves PAS accuracy.
- Adapts Grid-RNNs to PAS, a sensible engineering application though not a novel component.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus.
- Strength: strong empirical results with statistical significance demonstrating multi-sequence model superiority.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem definition regarding argument omission in Japanese.
- Strength: well-explained architectures with helpful diagrams, including Grid-RNN explanation.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments, dataset/hyperparameter details, and useful ablation study (residual connections).
- Strength: code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited novelty in model components; contribution is mainly in application/combination.
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis to identify specific model failure modes.
- Weakness: limited comparison to the most recent approaches, such as models using pre-trained language models.
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is insufficiently thorough.
- Question: request for a detailed error analysis identifying common error types and case-role confusions.
- Question: how performance degrades with smaller training data—sensitivity to dataset size?
- Question: were pre-trained word embeddings (Word2Vec, FastText) tested and what was their impact?
- Question: any experiments with optimizers other than Adam and their outcomes?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written, well-formatted, and figures are clear.
- Additional comment: reference list is comprehensive.
- Overall assessment: solid, worthwhile contribution with strong results and reproducible code; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
