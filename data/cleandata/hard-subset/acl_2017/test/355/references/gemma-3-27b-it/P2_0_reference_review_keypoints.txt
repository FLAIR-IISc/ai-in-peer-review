- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model to learn multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, avoiding reliance on syntactic parsers.  
- Presents both single-sequence baseline and multi-sequence (Grid-RNN) models, achieving state-of-the-art on the NAIST Text Corpus.  
- Empirically demonstrates Grid-RNNs effectively capture multi-predicate interactions and improve zero-argument identification.  
- Focuses on the core Japanese NLP problem of argument omission, constrained to intra-sentential arguments for practical relevance.  
- Provides a clear, detailed model description with helpful diagrams and equations.  
- Conducts a thorough experimental evaluation, including ablations on network depth and residual connections and per-case role analysis.  
- Releases source code for reproducibility.  
- Strong empirical results with statistical significance testing bolster the paper’s claims.  
- Well-motivated approach that avoids noisy syntactic parsing and targets a central linguistic challenge.  
- Architectural novelty is limited—the contribution is mainly in applying and combining known RNN/Grid-RNN components.  
- Lacks deep error analysis identifying specific linguistic phenomena the model fails on.  
- Evaluation is restricted to the relatively small NAIST corpus, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Results may be sensitive to hyperparameter choices; more discussion of sensitivity and search ranges is needed.  
- Questions: request for detailed error-type analysis, hyperparameter sensitivity info, use of external resources (e.g., larger embeddings), and potential extension to inter-sentential anaphora.  
- Additional notes: paper is well-written with thorough references, though some figure captions could be more descriptive.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
