- Summary: Proposes a Grid-RNN multi-sequence model for Japanese PAS to model multi-predicate interactions and handle argument omission, achieving SOTA on NAIST.
- Contribution 1: Effectively applies Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions in Japanese PAS, avoiding pipeline parsing.
- Contribution 2: Achieves strong empirical results, outperforming prior methods, especially on zero-argument identification.
- Contribution 3: Shows that useful PAS features can be learned directly from word sequences without explicit syntax.
- Strength 1: Tackles the core, well-motivated problem of argument omission in Japanese NLP.
- Strength 2: Solid experimental evaluation with standard dataset, strong baselines, ablations, and statistical significance tests.
- Strength 3: Clear and concise presentation with helpful figures and explanations.
- Strength 4: Demonstrates that multi-predicate modeling materially improves performance over single-sequence models, especially for zero arguments.
- Strength 5: Source code is publicly released, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness 1: Individual components (RNNs, Grid-RNNs) lack novelty; novelty is mainly in their combination and application.
- Weakness 2: Lacks qualitative error analysis to show what error types remain and inform future work.
- Weakness 3: Limited discussion of the deeper linguistic motivation for multi-predicate interactions in Japanese.
- Weakness 4: Dataset limitation—NAIST is small and generalizability to larger/diverse corpora is not fully addressed.
- Question 1: Request examples where the model outperforms baselines and what structural/semantic cues it captures.
- Question 2: Ask which error types or zero-anaphora subsets remain challenging for the model.
- Question 3: Ask for elaboration on the linguistic rationale and theories motivating multi-predicate focus.
- Question 4: Inquire about plans to evaluate on larger datasets or improve generalizability.
- Additional comment 1: References are comprehensive.
- Additional comment 2: Paper could briefly discuss related work in coreference resolution due to task similarity.
- Overall assessment: Recommend acceptance based on strong results, effective Grid-RNN application, and clear presentation despite minor weaknesses.
