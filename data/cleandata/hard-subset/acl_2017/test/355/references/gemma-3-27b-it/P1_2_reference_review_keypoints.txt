- Proposes neural models (Bi-RNN single-sequence and Grid-RNN multi-sequence) for Japanese PAS, addressing argument omission.
- Grid-RNNs empirically outperform prior work on the NAIST Text Corpus, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Establishes a strong neural baseline that avoids dependency on syntactic parsing.
- Conducts comprehensive experiments including ablations (depth, residuals) and per-case-role analysis.
- Effectively models multi-predicate interactions; multi-sequence Grid-RNN improves over single-sequence.
- Strong empirical gains particularly for Zero argument identification.
- Thorough analysis provides insights into model behavior and best practices.
- Paper is clearly written with helpful figures/tables.
- Source code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.
- Core components (Bi-RNNs, Grid-RNNs) are not novel; novelty mainly in their application/combination for Japanese PAS.
- Scope limited to intra-sentential arguments; inter-sentential resolution is not addressed.
- Lacks comparison to more recent or stronger neural baselines from related work/languages.
- Hyperparameter tuning details and rationale (e.g., embedding dim, window/minibatch sizes) are brief.
- Requests clarification on which aspects of Grid-RNN application are genuinely novel.
- Asks about extending the model to inter-sentential argument resolution and potential challenges.
- Requests more detail on hyperparameter search and sensitivity, including why embedding dim=32 was chosen.
- Requests qualitative error analysis with example failure cases and common error patterns.
- Suggests adding discussion of computational complexity and resource requirements.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed paper with strong empirical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
