- Paper proposes neural models (single-sequence RNN and multi-sequence Grid-RNN) for Japanese PAS to handle argument omission, achieving state-of-the-art results on NAIST.
- Main contribution: empirical demonstration that Grid-RNNs effectively model multi-predicate interactions and improve zero-argument identification.
- Main contribution: strong baselines and thorough evaluation, including ablation studies on depth and residual connections.
- Main contribution: clear problem formulation and motivation focused on Japanese zero-anaphora.
- Strength: consistently superior performance versus prior work, notably on zero argument identification.
- Strength: Grid-RNN architecture plausibly propagates information between predicates; residuals help performance.
- Strength: well-designed experiments on a standard dataset with per-case-role analysis and clear writing/figures.
- Weakness: limited novelty of individual components (RNNs/Grid-RNNs); combination/application is the main novelty.
- Weakness: lacks detailed error analysis to characterize remaining failure modes (e.g., types of zero pronouns).
- Weakness: reliance on the relatively small NAIST corpus raises generalization concerns to other domains.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter search and sensitivity analysis.
- Questions: please provide detailed error analysis (hard zero-anaphora types), experiments on larger/diverse data or discussion of generalizability, sensitivity to PRED window size C, and computational cost comparison vs. single-sequence model.
- Additional comments: inconsistent reference formatting, unclear term “atomic features,” and suggest a brief discussion of potential ethical implications.
- Overall: strong paper with valuable results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
