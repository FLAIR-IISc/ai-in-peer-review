- Primary contribution: end-to-end, syntax-free neural PAS model with a Grid-RNN that jointly models all predicates and achieves SOTA on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5.  
- Secondary contribution: strong single-sequence BiRNN/GRU baseline using token/predicate/marker features and a systematic study of depth and residual connections.  
- Tertiary contribution: empirical evidence that multi-predicate modeling yields modest but statistically significant gains, especially for zero-argument identification and DAT role performance.  

- Strength: both single-sequence and Grid-RNN outperform prior syntax-based baselines (Ouchi et al. 2015), raising overall F1 from 79.23 to 81.15/81.42.  
- Strength: meaningful improvements for zero anaphora (Zero F1 increases ~2 points with single model, +1 more with Grid-RNN).  
- Strength: Grid-RNN delivers consistent, significant gains over the single-sequence model, showing value of explicit predicate–predicate interactions.  
- Strength: deeper RNN stacks and residual connections improve performance and stability (up to 8 layers; residuals ≈ +1 F1).  
- Strength: substantial per-role gains for DAT, indicating the model learns cues for rarer argument types.  

- Weakness: limited novelty relative to established neural SRL architectures; Grid-RNN is an incremental extension with modest overall gains.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparison to closely related neural PAS/SRL work (notably Shibata et al. 2016) making positioning unclear.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation and analysis of key design choices (predicate-window, MARK, GRU vs LSTM, attention alternatives) and limited qualitative error analysis for zero args.  
- Weakness: scope limited to intra-sentential arguments; no evaluation/extension to inter-sentential zero anaphora.  
- Weakness: no reported efficiency/scalability metrics for Grid-RNN (training/inference time, memory) as predicate count grows.  

- Question: provide direct comparison to Shibata et al. (2016) on NAIST 1.5 or explain methodological differences.  
- Question: are improvements over Ouchi et al. (2015) statistically significant under the reported bootstrap test?  
- Question: ablations—impact of predicate window size C and MARK feature; GRU vs LSTM; other skip/highway connections; attention across predicates instead of Grid-RNN?  
- Question: report training/inference time and memory for single vs Grid-RNN as a function of number of predicates; how does performance scale?  
- Question: have you tried pretrained embeddings, semi-supervised objectives, or adding syntactic signals on top of the proposed models?  

- Overall evaluation: well-executed, syntax-free baseline and Grid-RNN yield new SOTA and meaningful zero/DAT gains; main concerns are novelty and incomplete comparisons/ablations, but reviewer leans toward acceptance.
