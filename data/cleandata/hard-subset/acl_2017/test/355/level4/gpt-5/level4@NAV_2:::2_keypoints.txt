- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) for Japanese PAS and adds a Grid-RNN to model predicate interactions; evaluated on NAIST with statistically significant improvements over prior SOTA.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, with sound motivation.  
- Proposed approach yields accuracy gains over the strongest prior system.  
- Grid-RNN produces modest but consistent improvement over the single-sequence model, mainly by better detection of zero arguments.  
- Novelty is primarily the move from single-sequence to multi-sequence formulation; overall benefit from modeling predicate interactions seems smaller than reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Missing direct comparison or thorough discussion with Shibata et al. (2016), which neuralized Ouchi et al.'s model; reviewer requests such a comparison.
