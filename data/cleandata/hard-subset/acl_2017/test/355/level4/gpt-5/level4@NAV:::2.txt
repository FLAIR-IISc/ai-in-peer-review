Review- This paper presents new prediction models for the Japanese SRL task by adapting the English state-of-the-art model of (Zhou and Xu, 2015). The authors further extend the model by applying the Grid-RNNs framework to handle interactions among the arguments of multiple predicates. The evaluation is conducted on a well-known benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL and shows significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well organized and well motivated.
The proposed model achieves higher accuracy compared with the current state-of-the-art system.
Moreover, the model using Grid-RNNs attains a slightly better performance than the proposed single-sequential model, mainly due to improved detection of zero arguments, which is the focus of this paper.

Weakness:
To the best of my understanding, the primary contribution is extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model. The effect of predicate interactions appears somewhat smaller than that of (Ouchi et al., 2015). There is prior work (Shibata et al., 2016) that extends (Ouchi et al., 2015)'s model with neural network modeling. I am curious about how they compare.
