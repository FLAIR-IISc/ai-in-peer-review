This paper proposes new prediction models for the Japanese SRL task by adopting the English state-of-the-art model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend the model by applying the Grid-RNN framework to handle interactions between the arguments of multiple predicates. The evaluation is conducted on a well-known benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL and shows significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
- The paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- The proposed model improves accuracy compared with the current state-of-the-art system.
- The Grid-RNN variant achieves slightly better performance than the proposed single-sequential model, mainly due to improved detection of zero arguments, which is the focus of this paper.

Weaknesses:
- To the best of my understanding, the main contribution is extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model.
- The impact of modeling predicate interactions appears smaller than that reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
- There is prior work (Shibata et al., 2016) that extends Ouchi et al. (2015)’s model with neural network modeling. I am curious about the comparison with their approach.
