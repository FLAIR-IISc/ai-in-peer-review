- Strengths:
This paper offers a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Nets to Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis. The approach avoids any explicit syntactic structure yet outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do rely on syntax. The authors provide a clear, detailed description of the implementation and results. They pay particular attention to performance on dropped arguments (zero pronouns), which are prevalent in Japanese and especially challenging for PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which takes all predicates in a sentence into account, achieves the best performance on these cases. Overall, the paper is detailed and clearly written.

- Weaknesses:
I have only minor comments. There are several typos (listed below) whose correction would improve English fluency. It would be helpful to illustrate the point that PRED includes context around the “predicate” using the example from Fig. 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. I also did not understand the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7).
