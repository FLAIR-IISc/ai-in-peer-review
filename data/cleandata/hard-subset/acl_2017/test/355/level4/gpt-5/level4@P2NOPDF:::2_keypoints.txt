- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) English SRL model to Japanese, proposes new prediction models, and adds a Grid-RNN to capture cross-predicate argument interactions, showing significant gains on a Japanese SRL benchmark.
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- Proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the previously reported best system.
- Grid-RNN yields a small but consistent improvement over the single-sequence model, primarily by better detecting zero arguments (the paper’s main focus).
- Main contribution is extending a single-sequence model to a multi-sequence architecture; gains from modeling predicate interactions are smaller than reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks a direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which extended Ouchi et al.’s model with neural methods, leaving relative impact unclear.
