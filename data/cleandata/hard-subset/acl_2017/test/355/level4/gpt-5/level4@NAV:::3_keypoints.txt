- Introduces a joint neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and compares it to a conventional single-sequence RNN.  
- Well-executed work achieving state-of-the-art performance and generally accessible explanations.  
- Recommend emphasizing task complexity, e.g., walk through Figure 1’s ambiguity between NOM/ACC of the first predicate and the NOM of the second.  
- Clarify how this PAS task differs from SRL, primarily because of zero-pronoun ambiguity.  
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable within the sentence vs. inter-sentential (intra- vs. inter-sentential zero anaphora).  
- Clarify representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns (left unmarked vs. explicitly marked); current implicit representation may limit the model.  
- Note missing explicit constraints (as in ILP SRL): (1) typically one argument per role per predicate, (2) an argument usually fills only one role for a predicate—ask whether the model learns these implicitly.  
- Request analysis comparing Single-Seq and Multi-Seq outputs to determine if outputs are mostly the same or diverge in specific contexts (Table 3 shows mixed results, gains mainly for NOM).  
- Suggest adding SRL results/baselines (acknowledging this would require substantial extra work).  
- Paper presentation and figures are clear; list of minor language errors and reference casing issues provided for correction.
