- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) to Japanese SRL and proposes models, including a Grid-RNN to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significant improvement over the current state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.  
- Proposed model improves overall accuracy compared to the prior state-of-the-art.  
- Grid-RNN variant yields slightly better performance, mainly by improving detection of zero arguments.  
- Main contribution appears to be extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model.  
- The benefit from modeling predicate interactions is smaller than reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks comparison with prior neural extension work (Shibata et al., 2016) of Ouchi et al.
