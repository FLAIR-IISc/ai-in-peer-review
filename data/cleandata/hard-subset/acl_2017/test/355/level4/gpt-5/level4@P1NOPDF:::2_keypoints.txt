- Proposes Japanese SRL models by adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) and extends them with a Grid-RNN to model interactions among arguments for multiple predicates.
- Experiments on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark show significant gains over the current state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well structured and well motivated.
- Proposed model improves accuracy over the current state-of-the-art system.
- Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the single-sequential model, mainly by better detecting zero arguments.
- Main contribution appears to be extending single-sequential to multi-sequential modeling; gains from predicate interaction modeling seem smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks direct comparison to prior neural extension of Ouchi et al. (2015) by Shibata et al. (2016).
