- Solid, well-executed paper presenting a joint Grid-RNN approach to Japanese PAS that achieves state-of-the-art performance.
- Paper should better convey the task’s complexity; e.g., walk readers through the ambiguity in Figure 1 between NOM/ACC of one predicate and NOM of another.
- Clarify how Japanese PAS differs from SRL, especially given the prevalence and ambiguity of zero pronouns.
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable versus inter-sentential, since this affects problem setting and evaluation.
- Explain how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (explicit empty placeholder vs left unmarked) and justify the design choice.
- If zero pronouns are left unmarked, discuss implications: no explicit signal may hinder the model’s ability to detect zero pronouns.
- The model does not explicitly enforce structural constraints common in ILP-based SRL (one argument per role, arguments fill a single role); analyze whether these constraints are learned implicitly.
- Report error-type breakdowns such as frequency of multiple NOMs assigned to one predicate or the same argument assigned multiple roles.
- Deeper comparison between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq is needed: overlap vs complementary errors, and contexts where Multi-Seq helps or hurts (long-distance dependencies, multiple predicates, zero pronouns).
- Consider including direct comparisons to SRL systems, acknowledging this may require additional work.
- Overall presentation and figures are clear and supportive of the architecture explanation.
- Minor language/style corrections and reference-casing standardization are needed (list of specific typos provided).
- Main recommended improvements: clarify zero-pronoun representation (especially inter-sentential), analyze role- and argument-uniqueness learning, and add comparative error/ablation analyses.
