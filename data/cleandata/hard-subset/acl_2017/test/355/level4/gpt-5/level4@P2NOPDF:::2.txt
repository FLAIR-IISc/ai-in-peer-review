This paper adapts the English state-of-the-art SRL model by Zhou and Xu (2015) to Japanese and proposes new prediction models. The authors further extend the approach with a Grid-RNN framework to capture interactions among arguments across multiple predicates. Experiments on a well-established Japanese SRL benchmark show significant improvements over the current state of the art.

Strengths:
- The paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- The proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the best previously reported system.
- The Grid-RNN variant delivers a small but consistent gain over the single-sequence model, primarily through improved detection of zero arguments, which is the paper’s main focus.

Weaknesses:
- The main contribution appears to be extending a single-sequence model to a multi-sequence architecture; the benefits of modeling predicate interactions are smaller than those reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Prior work by Shibata et al. (2016) extends Ouchi et al.’s model with neural network methods. A direct comparison with their approach is missing and would help clarify the relative impact of the proposed method.
