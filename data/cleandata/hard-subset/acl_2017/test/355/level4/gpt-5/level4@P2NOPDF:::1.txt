- Strengths:
This paper offers a well-executed application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis. Notably, the model dispenses with explicit syntactic representations yet outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do rely on syntax. The authors provide a clear, thorough description of both the model and the experimental results. They also devote careful attention to dropped arguments and zero pronouns—phenomena that are common in Japanese and particularly difficult for PAS—showing strong performance on these cases. The multi-sequence model, which jointly considers all predicates in a sentence, achieves the best results on such examples. Overall, the paper is detailed, well-organized, and clearly written.

- Weaknesses:
My comments are minor. There are a few typos and stylistic issues listed below; fixing them would improve readability. It would also help to illustrate the point that PRED includes contextual material around the “predicate” by using the example from Figure 6 where the accusative marker appears with the verb in the PRED string. Finally, the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7) is unclear; a brief note in the caption or text explaining the emphasis would be useful.
