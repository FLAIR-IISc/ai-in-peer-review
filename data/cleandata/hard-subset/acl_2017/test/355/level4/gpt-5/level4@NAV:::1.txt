- Strengths:
This paper offers a sophisticated use of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks for identifying predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The method avoids explicit syntactic representations yet surpasses current state-of-the-art systems that do rely on syntax. The authors provide a clear, thorough account of the implementation and results. They pay particular attention to dropped arguments (zero pronouns), which are common in Japanese and especially difficult for PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which conditions on all predicates in a sentence, delivers the strongest results on these cases. The paper is detailed and well written.

- Weaknesses:
My comments are largely minor. A few typos are listed below, and fixing them would improve the English. It would also help to illustrate the point about PRED including context around the “predicate” using the example in Fig. 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. I was unsure about the use of boldface in Table 2, p. 7.
