- Strengths:
This paper offers a well-executed use of grid-style recurrent neural networks for Japanese predicate–argument structure (PAS) analysis. The method dispenses with explicit syntactic inputs yet surpasses current state-of-the-art systems that rely on syntactic analysis. The implementation details and empirical results are presented clearly and comprehensively. Notably, the authors focus carefully on dropped arguments (zero pronouns), which are common in Japanese and particularly difficult for PAS; the multi-sequence model that conditions on all predicates in a sentence delivers the strongest gains on these cases. The paper is thorough and clearly written.

- Weaknesses:
My comments are largely minor. There are several typographical and stylistic issues listed below whose correction would improve readability. It would also help to illustrate the point about the PRED feature including context around the predicate using the example in Fig. 6, where the accusative marker is bundled with the verb in the PRED string. In addition, the meaning of the boldface in Table 2 (p. 7) was not clear to me.
