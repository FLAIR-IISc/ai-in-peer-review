- Primary contribution: Proposes Grid-RNN to jointly model interactions among all predicates for Japanese PAS, achieving SOTA on NAIST 1.5 without syntactic features, with clear gains on zero pronouns.
- Secondary contribution: Introduces a strong syntax-free single-sequence baseline (stacked Bi-GRU + softmax); deeper models with residual connections improve performance.
- Tertiary contribution: Provides careful empirical breakdown by argument type and case role, with significance testing showing when multi-predicate modeling helps (notably Zero).
- Strength: Strong empirical results vs competitive baselines; multi-sequence model improves overall and Zero F1 with bootstrap significance.
- Strength: Clear benefit on zero-pronoun arguments—cross-predicate context yields measurable Zero F1 gains over single-sequence baseline.
- Strength: Thorough ablations and analyses showing depth and residuals help (~+1 F1 at 8 layers) and per-role improvements (notably DAT despite sparsity).
- Strength: Well-presented and reproducible—architecture, training, hyperparameters, and code are provided; figures clarify design.
- Strength: Conceptual novelty—joint multi-predicate modeling via grid-style recurrence is a neat, general idea avoiding explicit syntax.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—single dataset/domain (NAIST newspaper) and only intra-sentential arguments; no out-of-domain or inter-sentential tests.
- Weakness: Non-uniform gains—multi-sequence underperforms single-sequence on some fine-grained categories (e.g., Zero ACC/DAT; Dep DAT); more analysis needed on when interactions hurt.
- Weakness: Baseline coverage could be broader—lack of comparison to other strong syntax-free alternatives (e.g., BiLSTM-CRF, attention models).
- Weakness: Computational aspects underreported—no discussion of complexity, runtime, memory scaling with number of predicates.
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues—unclear PRED window illustration and unexplained boldface in Table 2.
- Question: What are training/inference runtimes and memory footprints for single- vs multi-sequence models, and how do they scale with predicate count?
- Question: Were pre-trained word embeddings or external resources tried, and what was their effect?
- Question: Can syntactic cues be added as optional features on top of Grid-RNN, and do they bring further gains?
- Question: Can the authors provide qualitative examples where multi-predicate interactions changed decisions vs single-sequence?
- Question: Please clarify boldface in Table 2 and give a concrete example showing how the PRED window captures particles attached to predicates.
- Additional comment: Fix numerous minor typos/phrasing issues noted across the paper (specific line suggestions provided).
- Additional suggestion: Briefly illustrate how the PRED feature window captures context (e.g., accusative marker paired with verb) using Fig. 6 example.
- Verdict: Reviewer read and is satisfied with the author response.
