- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) SRL model to Japanese and adds a Grid-RNN to model cross-predicate interactions, yielding SOTA gains, especially for zero anaphora.  
- Clear motivation and organization; articulates Japanese SRL challenges and zero anaphora well.  
- Strong empirical results: adapted single-sequence model already surpasses previous state of the art.  
- Grid-RNN provides additional improvements, mainly by better detecting zero arguments.  
- Contribution feels incremental compared to prior multi-predicate interaction work (e.g., Ouchi et al., 2015).  
- Missing direct comparison to related neural work (Shibata et al., 2016); such a head-to-head is crucial.  
- Interaction effect magnitude seems smaller than reported by Ouchi et al.; causes (dataset, features, supervision, optimization) need clarification.  
- Analysis is limited; deeper error breakdowns by argument type, distance, case marker presence, and discourse distance are needed.  
- Request direct comparison or partial replication of Shibata et al. (2016) under identical protocols.  
- Request ablation isolating the Grid-RNN’s contribution versus embeddings, character/subword features, dropout, CRF/decoding choices.  
- Ask whether reported gains are statistically significant across multiple seeds; report test and p-values.  
- Ask for computational overhead details of Grid-RNN (training time, memory, inference latency).  
- Ask whether Grid-RNN improves recall or precision for zero arguments and request precision/recall breakdowns by role and predicate class.  
- Suggest including direct comparison or replication of Shibata et al. (2016) for fair evaluation.  
- Suggest detailed ablations (with/without Grid-RNN, predicate-level features, cross-predicate connections) and per-category zero-argument metrics.  
- Suggest qualitative examples showing how cross-predicate signals resolve difficult zero-anaphora cases and baseline failure modes.  
- Suggest clarifying implementation details (initialization, pretrained embeddings, hyperparameters, decoding) for reproducibility.  
- Suggest discussing generalization to other pro-drop or non-configurational languages and to English SRL with long-distance dependencies.  
- Paper is well written; include a schematic comparing single-sequence and Grid-RNN and ensure dataset/split/evaluation documentation.  
- Overall: solid, timely contribution with SOTA gains; novelty somewhat incremental and empirical case weakened by lack of direct comparisons and deeper analysis—lean toward acceptance if revisions address these concerns.
