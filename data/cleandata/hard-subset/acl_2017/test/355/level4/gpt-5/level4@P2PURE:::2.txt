This paper introduces new prediction models for Japanese SRL by adapting the English state-of-the-art model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend the approach with Grid-RNNs to capture interactions among arguments across multiple predicates. Experiments on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark show significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
- The paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- The proposed model improves accuracy over the current state-of-the-art system.
- The Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the single-sequence model, primarily due to better detection of zero arguments, which is the paper’s main focus.

Weaknesses:
- To my understanding, the main contribution is extending a single-sequence model to a multi-sequence model; the benefit from modeling predicate interactions appears smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Prior work by Shibata et al. (2016) extends the Ouchi et al. (2015) model with neural networks. A direct comparison with that approach would be informative.
