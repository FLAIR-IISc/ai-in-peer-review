- Adapted Zhou & Xu (2015) single-sequence model for Japanese PAS, improving accuracy over prior systems.
- Introduced a Grid-RNN multi-sequence model to capture interactions across predicates, yielding further gains mainly via better zero-argument detection.
- Empirical evaluation on the NAIST Text Corpus shows statistically significant improvements over the previous state of the art.

- Clear motivation and organization; extension from single-predicate to multi-predicate modeling is natural and well explained.
- Strong empirical results on a standard benchmark with significance testing, facilitating comparison.
- Grid-RNN’s benefit aligns with the paper’s focus by improving zero-argument detection.

- Limited novelty: the core contribution is an incremental architectural extension rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- The impact of predicate interactions is modest and smaller than gains reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks direct comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), making it hard to position the work among contemporaneous neural methods.

- Can the authors provide a direct or discussion-based comparison to Shibata et al. (2016)?
- Is there an analysis (by case, distance, sentence length) explaining when the Grid-RNN helps versus when it does not?
- How sensitive are the reported improvements to architectural depth and hyperparameters; are multi-predicate gains robust across settings?
