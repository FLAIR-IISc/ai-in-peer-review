- Paper is solid, well executed, targets a well-established Japanese PAS task, and achieves state-of-the-art results.  
- Task complexity should be emphasized more, especially the ambiguity between NOM and ACC arguments and differences from SRL due to zero pronouns.  
- Figure 1 needs a walk-through showing ambiguity between predicates’ arguments to clarify the problem.  
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable intra-sententially to clarify intra- vs. inter-sentential zero anaphora challenges.  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (left unmarked vs. explicit empty-argument marker) and discuss implications of that choice.  
- Current model appears not to explicitly encode common constraints (e.g., one argument per type per predicate; an argument fills only one role); discuss this omission.  
- Analyze whether the model learns such constraints implicitly and report error types where constraints are violated.  
- Table 3 shows mixed results between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq (main Multi-Seq gain on NOM); provide analysis of where outputs differ and in what contexts.  
- Compare model behaviors (are predictions largely identical or complementary) and report divergences to illuminate model internals.  
- Including SRL results would be informative, though acknowledged to require substantial extra work.  
- Presentation and figures are helpful overall, but there are several minor language/typo issues (specific line errors listed) and inconsistent capitalization in references.
