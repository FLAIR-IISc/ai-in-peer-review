This paper proposes new prediction models for Japanese SRL by adapting the English state-of-the-art model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend the model with a Grid-RNN framework to capture interactions among arguments across multiple predicates. Experiments on a well-known Japanese SRL benchmark show significant gains over the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
- The paper is well structured and well motivated.
- The proposed model improves accuracy compared to the current state-of-the-art system.
- The Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the proposed single-sequential model, primarily due to improved detection of zero arguments, which is the main focus of the paper.

Weaknesses:
- To the best of my understanding, the main contribution is the extension from a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model. The benefit from modeling predicate interactions appears smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- There is prior work (Shibata et al., 2016) that extends Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural network modeling. I would like to see a direct comparison with their approach.
