- Paper adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) to Japanese SRL and proposes new prediction models.
- Extends the model with Grid-RNNs to model interactions among arguments of multiple predicates.
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significantly better performance than the current SOTA.
- Strength: paper is well organized and well motivated.
- Strength: proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the current state-of-the-art.
- Strength: Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the single-sequential model, mainly via improved zero-argument detection.
- Weakness: main contribution appears to be extending single-sequential to multi-sequential, which may be incremental.
- Weakness: predicate-interaction gains seem smaller than those reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Weakness: missing comparison to related neural extension by Shibata et al. (2016).
