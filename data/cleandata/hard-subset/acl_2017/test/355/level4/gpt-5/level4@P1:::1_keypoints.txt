- Proposes syntax-free neural models for Japanese PAS: a per-predicate single-sequence BiRNN and a joint multi-predicate Grid-RNN to capture predicate interactions.  
- Empirically outperforms strong baselines on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, including syntax-based methods, with largest gains on zero anaphora (dropped arguments).  
- Paper provides clear architectural descriptions, training details, and separates single- vs. multi-sequence settings to isolate multi-predicate effects.  
- Modeling multi-predicate interactions (Grid-RNN) yields additional improvements, especially for resolving zero arguments.  
- Deeper networks with residual connections give consistent performance gains; experiments support these design choices.  
- Strength: strong empirical results without relying on syntactic features, showing models learn contextual cues from raw sequences.  
- Strength: effective handling of zero pronouns, with multi-sequence model offering further improvements over single-sequence.  
- Strength: thorough methodology and clear presentation of input representations, architectures, hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: mostly minor presentation issues—several typos and phrasing problems reduce fluency.  
- Weakness: need clearer illustration of how PRED features include local predicate context and interact with the MARK feature (example in Figure 6 unclear).  
- Weakness: meaning of boldface in Table 2 is unclear and should be explained.
