This paper presents neural models for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis by adapting the English state-of-the-art architecture of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this model with a Grid-RNN framework to model interactions across multiple predicates within a sentence. Evaluation on a standard Japanese PAS benchmark (NAIST Text Corpus) shows statistically significant improvements over the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
- The paper is clearly written, well organized, and the motivation is sound.
- The proposed approach yields accuracy gains over the strongest prior system.
- The Grid-RNN variant achieves a modest but consistent improvement over the single-sequence model, largely due to better detection of zero arguments, which is a central challenge addressed in this work.

Weakness:
- The primary novelty appears to be the extension from a single-sequence to a multi-sequence formulation; the observed benefit from modeling predicate interactions seems smaller than that reported by Ouchi et al. (2015). Moreover, prior work (Shibata et al., 2016) neuralizes the model of Ouchi et al. (2015); a direct comparison or thorough discussion relative to that line of work is missing, and I would like to see such a comparison.
