- Strengths:
This paper presents a sophisticated application of grid-type recurrent neural networks to predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis in Japanese. The approach does not rely on explicit syntactic structure, yet it outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do. The authors provide a clear, detailed description of both the implementation and the results. In particular, they carefully analyze performance on dropped arguments (zero pronouns), which are prevalent in Japanese and especially challenging for PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which takes all predicates in a sentence into account, achieves the best performance on these cases. Overall, the paper is thorough and clearly written.

- Weaknesses:
I have only minor comments. The typos listed below, once corrected, would improve fluency. It would also be helpful to illustrate the point about PRED including context around the “predicate” with the example from Fig. 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. I also did not understand the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7).
