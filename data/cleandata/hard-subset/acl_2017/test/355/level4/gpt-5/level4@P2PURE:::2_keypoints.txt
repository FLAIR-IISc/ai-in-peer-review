- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) to Japanese SRL and introduces Grid-RNNs to capture argument interactions across predicates.  
- Experiments on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark show significant improvement over the current state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.  
- Grid-RNN slightly outperforms the single-sequence model, mainly via better detection of zero arguments (the main focus).  
- Main contribution appears to be extending a single-sequence model to multi-sequence; gains from modeling predicate interactions seem smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks a direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which extended Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural networks and would be informative.
