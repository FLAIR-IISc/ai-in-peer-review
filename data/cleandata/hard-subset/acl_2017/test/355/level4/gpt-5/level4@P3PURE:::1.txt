Strengths:
- This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type recurrent neural networks to determining predicate–argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach does not rely on any explicit syntactic structure and outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do include syntactic information.
- The authors provide a clear and detailed description of both the implementation and the results.
- They pay close attention to performance on dropped arguments (zero pronouns), which are prevalent in Japanese and particularly challenging for PAS. The multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates in a sentence, achieves the best performance on these cases.
- The paper is detailed and clearly written.

Weaknesses:
- My comments are minor. There are several typos (listed below) whose correction would improve the fluency of the English.
- It would be helpful to illustrate the point that PRED includes context around the “predicate” using the example in Fig. 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string.
- I did not understand the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7).
