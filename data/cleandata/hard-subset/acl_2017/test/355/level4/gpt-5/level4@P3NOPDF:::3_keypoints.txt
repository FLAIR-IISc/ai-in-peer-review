- Proposes a joint Grid-RNN approach for Japanese PAS and reports state-of-the-art results versus a single-sequence RNN baseline.  
- Paper is clear and accessible, with effective figures illustrating model and data layout.  
- Solid technical execution with careful comparison to a reasonable baseline.  
- Clear motivation for joint modeling across sentence positions.  
- Strong empirical gains, especially on nominative (NOM) arguments.  
- Needs a clearer, explicit walkthrough of the ambiguity in Figure 1 to motivate task complexity (zero pronouns and case ellipsis).  
- Report corpus statistics distinguishing intra-sentential vs. inter-sentential zero pronouns, since this affects learning and evaluation.  
- Clarify how extra-sentential arguments are represented (empty slot vs. explicit NULL/EXO) and discuss consequences; current approach may conflate “no argument” with “unresolvable zero pronoun.”  
- Quantify how often model outputs violate simple structural constraints (e.g., at most one argument per role, no argument filling multiple roles) and consider a light inference layer/ILP.  
- Provide a detailed analysis of where Single-Seq and Multi-Seq diverge (e.g., long-distance dependencies, across-clause arguments, presence/absence of case markers, predicate types, dependency distance).  
- Clarify relation to SRL: explicitly contrast task formulation and modeling implications (richer labels vs. zero anaphora ambiguity).  
- Clarify whether decoding is fully local per grid cell or includes any global decoding enforcing role uniqueness.  
- Clarify training setup: are predicates given or predicted, and which tokens are considered candidate arguments (all tokens vs. pre-filtered).  
- Fix minor editorial errors (list of specific line corrections provided).  
- Ensure consistent casing in references and text (e.g., “Japanese,” “LSTM,” “CoNLL,” “ILP”).  
- Overall: well-executed paper that advances Japanese PAS, but stronger analysis (zero-pronoun handling, constraint adherence, error breakdown) is needed to clarify the source of gains.
