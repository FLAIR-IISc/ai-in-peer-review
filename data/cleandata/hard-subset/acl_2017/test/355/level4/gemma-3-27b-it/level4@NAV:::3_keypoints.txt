- Introduces a joint Grid-RNN approach for Japanese PAS analysis and achieves state-of-the-art performance versus a single-sequence RNN.  
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible to readers without Japanese linguistics background.  
- The inherent complexities and ambiguities of PAS (e.g., nominals that may serve as arguments of multiple predicates, as in Fig. 1) need fuller discussion.  
- Needs clearer distinction between PAS and SRL, especially regarding the specific challenges posed by zero pronouns.  
- Report statistics on the proportion of zero pronouns resolvable within the sentence to quantify task difficulty and intra- vs inter-sentential anaphora separation.  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (absent vs explicitly marked empty arguments) since current omission may limit handling of zero anaphora.  
- The model does not explicitly encode common SRL constraints (e.g., one-to-one predicate–argument type or argument–slot constraints); examine whether it learns these implicitly.  
- Mixed results in Table 3 (improvement only for NOM) warrant deeper analysis of differences between models and contexts where they diverge.  
- Suggest comparing model outputs (identical vs complementary errors and contexts of divergence) to better understand strengths and failure modes.  
- Including SRL results would be substantial work but useful; the primary immediate improvement is more diagnostic analysis of the models.  
- Presentation and figures are helpful, but several minor linguistic/typo corrections and reference casing inconsistencies should be fixed.
