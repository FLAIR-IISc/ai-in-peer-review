- New state-of-the-art on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 for Japanese PAS analysis.
- Effective application of Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions and improve zero-argument identification.
- Thorough experimental evaluation including ablation studies on network depth and residual connections.
- Consistently outperforms prior methods, with notable gains for zero arguments.
- Well-motivated approach addressing Japanese argument omission via multi-predicate modeling.
- Rigorous experimental setup with clear dataset/metrics/implementation details and significance testing.
- Detailed analyses (ablation and per-case role) that illuminate model behavior and design choices.
- Clear, well-structured, and readable presentation.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental—an extension of Zhou & Xu (2015) with Grid-RNNs rather than a major conceptual breakthrough.
- Insufficiently detailed comparison to recent neural work (Shibata et al., 2016); lacks architectural contrast and analysis.
- Limited scope focused on intra-sentential arguments; doesn't address inter-sentential argument resolution.
- Request for a detailed comparison with Shibata et al. (2016) highlighting architectural differences and performance impact.
- Request for computational resource details (training time, memory) for the multi-sequence/deeper models.
- Request for explanation of which Grid-RNN characteristics aid zero anaphora resolution.
- Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.
- Availability of source code is a positive asset.
- Suggest clarifying the role of residual connections for readers less familiar with deep learning.
