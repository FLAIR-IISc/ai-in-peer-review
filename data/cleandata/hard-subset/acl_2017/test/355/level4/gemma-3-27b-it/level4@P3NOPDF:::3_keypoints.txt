- Paper proposes a joint Grid-RNN model for Japanese PAS and achieves state-of-the-art results compared to single-sequence RNNs.
- Writing and figures are clear and accessible even to non-specialists in Japanese linguistics.
- The paper would benefit from a deeper discussion of task complexities, especially the ambiguity in predicate-argument attachments.
- Figure 1’s example shows NOM/ACC arguments of one predicate can be NOM of another; this ambiguity needs elaboration.
- Contrast PAS analysis with SRL and highlight challenges from zero pronouns (implicit arguments).
- Provide statistics on the proportion of intra-sententially resolvable zero pronouns to clarify task difficulty.
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (omitted vs. explicit empty arguments) and discuss implications.
- Address that the model does not explicitly enforce common SRL constraints (e.g., one argument per type per predicate, single-slot filling).
- Include analysis showing whether the model implicitly learns these constraints from data.
- Table 3 improvements are mainly for NOM; perform a deeper comparison of single- vs multi-sequence outputs to identify where they diverge.
- Adding standard SRL results would further contextualize the contribution, though it is a larger extension.
- Minor language and capitalization errors noted (list of specific typos and inconsistent reference casing) that should be corrected.
