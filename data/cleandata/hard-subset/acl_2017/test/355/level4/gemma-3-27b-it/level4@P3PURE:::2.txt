This paper proposes new prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task, adapting the English state-of-the-art model from (Zhou and Xu, 2015). The authors further extend this model by applying a Grid-RNN framework to better capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluation on a well-known Japanese SRL benchmark dataset demonstrates a significant performance improvement over the current state of the art.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated. The proposed model achieves an improvement in accuracy compared to the current state-of-the-art system. Furthermore, the model incorporating Grid-RNNs achieves a slightly better performance than the proposed single-sequential model, primarily due to improved detection of zero arguments – a key focus of this work.

**Weaknesses:**

The primary contribution of this paper, as I understand it, is the extension from a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model. However, the impact of explicitly modeling predicate interactions appears somewhat smaller than that reported in (Ouchi et al., 2015). A relevant prior work, (Shibata et al., 2016), extends the (Ouchi et al., 2015) model with neural network modeling. I would be interested to see a comparison between the proposed approach and that of Shibata et al. (2016).
