This paper presents novel prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task, building upon the English state-of-the-art model from (Zhou and Xu, 2015). The authors further extend this model by incorporating a Grid-RNN framework to capture interactions between arguments associated with multiple predicates. Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark demonstrates a significant performance improvement over existing state-of-the-art systems.

Strengths:
The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and effectively motivated. The proposed model achieves improved accuracy compared to the current state of the art. Furthermore, the Grid-RNN variant exhibits a slight performance gain over the single-sequential model, primarily attributable to improved detection of zero arguments, which is a key focus of this work.

Weakness:
The primary contribution of this paper appears to be the extension from a single-sequential to a multi-sequential model. However, the impact of modeling predicate interactions seems relatively modest compared to that reported in (Ouchi et al., 2015). Notably, Shibata et al. (2016) previously extended the (Ouchi et al., 2015) model using neural network approaches, and a comparison with their work would be insightful.
