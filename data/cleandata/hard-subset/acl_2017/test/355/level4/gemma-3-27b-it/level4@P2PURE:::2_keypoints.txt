- Presents novel prediction models for Japanese SRL, extending Zhou & Xu (2015) and adding a Grid-RNN to model interactions among arguments for multiple predicates.  
- Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark and reports significant improvement over existing state-of-the-art systems.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and well-motivated.  
- The proposed model achieves improved accuracy over the current state of the art.  
- The Grid-RNN variant yields a slight additional gain over the single-sequential model, mainly via better detection of zero arguments.  
- Main contribution is the single- to multi-sequential extension; the effect of modeling predicate interactions is modest compared to Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which previously extended Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural methods and would be an insightful baseline.
