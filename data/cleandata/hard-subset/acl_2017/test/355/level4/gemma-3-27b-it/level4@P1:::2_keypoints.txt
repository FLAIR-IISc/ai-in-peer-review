- Proposes adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) ideas and using Grid-RNNs for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis.  
- Presents a novel end-to-end neural architecture that outperforms prior systems without relying on syntactic features.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 benchmark.  
- Shows Grid-RNN effectively models multi-predicate interactions, improving zero anaphora resolution.  
- Significant improvement on zero arguments indicates better contextual modeling for omitted arguments.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and well-organized.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including strong baselines and ablation studies on depth and residual connections.  
- Contribution may be incremental—extending from single- to multi-sequence feels modest despite gains.  
- Lacks direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which similarly extends prior models with neural components.  
- Error analysis is limited; more detailed breakdowns of remaining failure modes are needed.  
- Asks authors for a detailed performance comparison to Shibata et al. (2016).  
- Requests a fuller error analysis with example error types.  
- Seeks elaboration on planned use of large-scale unlabeled data and expected benefits.  
- Notes the reference list is extensive and the paper’s notation is consistent and clear.
