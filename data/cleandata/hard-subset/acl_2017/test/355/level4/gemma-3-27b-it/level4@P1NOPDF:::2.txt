This paper proposes new prediction models for Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) by adapting the English state-of-the-art model from Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this model by incorporating a Grid-RNN framework to better handle interactions between arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluation on a well-known Japanese SRL benchmark dataset demonstrates a significant performance improvement over the current state of the art.

**Strengths:**

The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated. The proposed model achieves an improvement in accuracy compared to the current state-of-the-art system. Notably, the model utilizing Grid-RNNs achieves a slightly better performance than the proposed single-sequential model, largely due to improvements in the detection of zero arguments – a key focus of this work.

**Weaknesses:**

In my assessment, the primary contribution of this paper lies in extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential one. However, the impact of modeling predicate interactions appears somewhat limited compared to that reported in Ouchi et al. (2015). Furthermore, previous work by Shibata et al. (2016) extends the model of Ouchi et al. (2015) using neural network modeling. I would be interested to see a comparison between the proposed approach and that of Shibata et al. (2016).
