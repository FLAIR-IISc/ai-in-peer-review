This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach does not use any explicit syntactic structure and outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do include it. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and results. In particular, they pay close attention to performance on dropped arguments – zero pronouns – which are prevalent in Japanese and particularly challenging for PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which takes all predicates in the sentence into account, achieves the best performance on these examples. The paper is detailed and clearly written.

**Weaknesses:**

I have only minor comments. There are some typos listed below, the correction of which would improve English fluency. It would be worth illustrating the point about the PRED feature including context around the “predicate” with the example from Figure 6 where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. I did not understand the use of boldface in Table 2 on page 7.
