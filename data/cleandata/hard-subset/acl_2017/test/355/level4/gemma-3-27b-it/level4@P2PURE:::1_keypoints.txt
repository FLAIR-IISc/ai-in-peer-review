- Proposes Grid-type RNNs for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification, achieving strong performance without explicit syntactic information and surpassing syntactic SOTA.
- Detailed and clear description of model implementation and experimental setup.
- Explicit focus on handling dropped arguments and zero pronouns common in Japanese.
- Multi-sequence model that considers all predicates in a sentence yields the best results on challenging examples.
- Paper is well-written and detailed overall.
- Minor weaknesses: several typographical and wording errors that should be corrected.
- Suggests clarifying how contextual information around the predicate is included in the PRED representation (e.g., accusative marker in Fig. 6).
- Notes the use of boldface in Table 2 is unclear and should be explained.
- Lists specific typos/edits by page (p1, p2, p3, p4, p7, p8) for correction.
- Reviewer is satisfied with the authors' responses to queries.
