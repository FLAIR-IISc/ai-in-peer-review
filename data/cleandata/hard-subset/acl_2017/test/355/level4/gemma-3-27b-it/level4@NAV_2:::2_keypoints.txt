- Clear writing, strong motivation, and well-structured paper.
- Proposed neural sequence models outperform existing Japanese PAS analyzers on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5.
- Extending single-sequence to multi-sequence with Grid-RNNs improves modeling of multi-predicate interactions.
- Grid-RNNs notably improve zero-argument identification compared to the single-sequence baseline.
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies (network depth, residual connections) provide thorough evaluation.
- Effectiveness shown for learning contextual word-sequence information without relying on syntax.
- Primary novelty is adaptation/extension of existing sequence techniques rather than a fundamentally new architecture.
- Performance gains are statistically significant but modest compared to some prior work (e.g., Ouchi et al., 2015).
- Lacks direct comparison with neural extensions of prior models (e.g., Shibata et al., 2016), limiting assessment of novelty/impact.
- Substantial, disproportionate improvement for the dative case needs further analysis to explain underlying causes.
- Authors are asked to provide a detailed methodological and performance comparison with Shibata et al. (2016).
- Authors are asked to explain why the model is particularly effective for dative case identification.
