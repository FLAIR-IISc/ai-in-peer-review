Introduces novel prediction models for Japanese SRL based on Zhou & Xu (2015).  
Integrates a Grid-RNN to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark and shows significant gains over prior state-of-the-art.  
Paper is clearly written, logically organized, and approach is well-justified.  
Proposed model achieves notable overall accuracy improvement compared to existing methods.  
Grid-RNN model yields a marginal but positive improvement over the single-sequential model.  
Improvement is largely due to better zero argument detection.  
Main contribution is an extension from single-sequential to multi-sequential modeling.  
Predicate interaction impact is less pronounced than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
Lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which previously extended Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural methods.
