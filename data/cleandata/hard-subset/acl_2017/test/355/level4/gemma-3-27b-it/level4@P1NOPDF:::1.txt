- Strengths:
This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification in Japanese. Notably, the approach achieves strong performance without relying on any explicit syntactic structure, outperforming current state-of-the-art (SOTA) systems that *do* incorporate syntactic information. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of both the implementation and the results. They pay particularly close attention to performance on dropped arguments and zero pronouns – phenomena prevalent in Japanese and especially challenging for PAS tasks. Their multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates within a sentence, achieves the best performance on these difficult examples. Overall, the paper is detailed and clearly written.

- Weaknesses:
I have only minor comments. A few typos are listed below, and correcting them would improve the overall fluency. It would be beneficial to illustrate the point about the PRED representation including context around the predicate with the example from Figure 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. I also didn’t understand the use of boldface in Table 2 on page 7.
