- Adapts the English SRL model of Zhou & Xu (2015) to Japanese and proposes new prediction models.  
- Extends the single-sequential model with a Grid-RNN framework to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significant performance gains over the current state of the art.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.  
- The Grid-RNN model slightly outperforms the single-sequential model, mainly by improving detection of zero arguments.  
- The main technical contribution is essentially extending single-sequential to multi-sequential modeling.  
- Modeling predicate interactions yields limited impact compared to Ouchi et al. (2015), and the paper lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016) which also applies neural modeling to Ouchi et al.’s approach.
