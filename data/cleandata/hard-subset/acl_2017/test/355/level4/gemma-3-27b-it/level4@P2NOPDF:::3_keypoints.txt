- Introduces a novel joint neural approach for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and reports state-of-the-art results versus a single-sequence RNN baseline.  
- Effectively presents the challenging task to readers unfamiliar with Japanese linguistics.  
- Needs deeper discussion of task complexities, e.g., the ambiguity in Figure 1 where NOM/ACC of the first predicate could be reinterpreted as the NOM of the second.  
- Should more clearly contrast this task with traditional SRL, emphasizing the specific challenges introduced by zero pronouns.  
- Provide statistics on the proportion of intra-sententially resolvable zero pronouns and differentiate intra- vs inter-sentential anaphora to clarify task difficulty.  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (explicit empty arguments vs absent) and discuss the implications of the current design choice.  
- Model does not explicitly enforce SRL constraints (e.g., one NOM/one ACC per predicate); analyze whether the model implicitly learns these constraints.  
- Table 3 shows improvements mainly for NOM identification; a more detailed comparison of single- vs multi-sequence model outputs is needed.  
- Investigate whether the two models produce similar or divergent predictions and identify contexts where their outputs differ to better understand model behavior.  
- Including results on a standard SRL dataset would provide useful context, though this is acknowledged as additional work.  
- Overall presentation and figures are clear and informative.  
- Several minor language errors and inconsistent reference casing require correction (specific line examples provided).
