This paper introduces novel prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task, building upon the strong performance of the English SRL model from Zhou and Xu (2015). Furthermore, the authors enhance this foundation by integrating a Grid-RNN framework to effectively capture interactions between arguments associated with multiple predicates. The models were evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark dataset, demonstrating a significant performance gain over existing state-of-the-art systems.

**Strengths:**

The paper is clearly written, logically organized, and well-justified in its approach. The proposed model achieves a notable improvement in overall accuracy compared to current state-of-the-art methods. Importantly, the Grid-RNN enhanced model demonstrates a marginal, but positive, performance increase over the single-sequential model, largely attributable to improved zero argument detection – a key focus of this work.

**Weakness:**

The primary contribution of this paper appears to be the extension from a single-sequential to a multi-sequential model. While predicate interactions are addressed, their impact is somewhat less pronounced than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  Notably, Shibata et al. (2016) previously extended the Ouchi et al. (2015) model utilizing neural network approaches. A comparative analysis against this prior work would be valuable.
