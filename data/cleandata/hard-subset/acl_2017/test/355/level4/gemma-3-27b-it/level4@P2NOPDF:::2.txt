This paper presents novel prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task, building upon the strong performance of the English SRL model proposed by Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further enhance this model by incorporating a Grid-RNN framework to explicitly model interactions between arguments associated with multiple predicates. Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark demonstrates significant performance gains over the current state-of-the-art.

**Strengths:**

The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and provides strong motivation for the proposed work. The presented model achieves a substantial improvement in SRL accuracy compared to existing systems. Notably, the Grid-RNN variant outperforms the single-sequential model, primarily due to improved detection of zero arguments – a key focus of this research.

**Weaknesses:**

The primary contribution of this work appears to be the extension from a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model leveraging Grid-RNNs. While effective, the impact of explicitly modeling predicate interactions seems somewhat limited compared to the results reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  Furthermore, a related work, Shibata et al. (2016), also extends the Ouchi et al. (2015) model using neural network techniques.  A direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016) would be valuable to better understand the relative strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach, and to contextualize the contribution within the existing body of work on Japanese SRL.
