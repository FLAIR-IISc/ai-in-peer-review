- Paper proposes joint neural modeling for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and reports state-of-the-art results versus single-sequence RNNs.
- Well-executed and accessible presentation, even for readers unfamiliar with Japanese linguistics.
- Recommend deeper discussion of task complexity, especially ambiguity in Figure 1 between NOM and ACC and shared arguments across predicates.
- Clarify how PAS here differs from SRL, emphasizing challenges from zero pronouns.
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable intra-sententially versus inter-sentential.
- Explain representation for inter-sentential zero anaphora (unmarked vs explicitly marked) and discuss limitations of the chosen design.
- Note the model does not explicitly enforce common constraints (one NOM/ACC per predicate; arguments fill only one slot) and ask for analysis of whether the model learns these constraints.
- Request detailed comparison of Multi-Seq vs Single-Seq outputs (are predictions similar or different, and in which contexts) given mixed aggregate improvements (Table 3).
- Suggest that analysis of internal behavior would strengthen the paper; adding SRL results would be valuable but is a large effort.
- Presentation and figures are generally good but several minor language errors should be fixed (list of specific word corrections provided).
- Ensure consistent capitalization in references (e.g., “Japanese”, “LSTM”, “CoNLL”, “ILP”).
