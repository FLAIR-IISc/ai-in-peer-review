This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Nets to the task of determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach notably does not use any explicit syntactic structure, yet outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that *do* include such structure. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of both the implementation and the results. In particular, they demonstrate careful attention to performance on dropped arguments and zero pronouns, phenomena prevalent in Japanese and particularly challenging for PAS identification. Their multi-sequence model, which takes all predicates in the sentence into account, achieves the best performance on these examples. The paper is detailed and clearly written.
