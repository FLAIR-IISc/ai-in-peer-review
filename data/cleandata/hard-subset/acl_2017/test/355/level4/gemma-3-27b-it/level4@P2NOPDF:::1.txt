This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to the challenging task of predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification in Japanese. A key strength of this approach is its ability to achieve strong performance *without* relying on explicit syntactic structure, notably surpassing current state-of-the-art (SOTA) systems that *do* incorporate such information. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of both their implementation and experimental results. 

The paper demonstrates a particularly insightful focus on handling dropped arguments and zero pronouns, phenomena that are highly prevalent and problematic for PAS analysis in Japanese. Their proposed multi-sequence model, which effectively considers all predicates within a sentence, yields the best performance specifically on these difficult cases. Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a thorough investigation of the topic.
