This paper demonstrates a compelling application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification in Japanese. Notably, the approach achieves strong performance without relying on explicit syntactic information, surpassing current state-of-the-art systems that *do* utilize syntactic structure. The implementation and results are described with commendable clarity and detail. The authors specifically address the challenging issue of dropped arguments and zero pronouns, common in Japanese, and their multi-sequence model – which considers all predicates within a sentence – yields the best results for these cases. Overall, the paper is well-written and thorough.
