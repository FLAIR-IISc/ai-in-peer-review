- Paper proposes novel Japanese SRL models that extend Zhou & Xu (2015) and integrate Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significant improvements over prior state-of-the-art systems.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and well-motivated.  
- Reported accuracy improvements are substantial and represent a valuable contribution to Japanese SRL.  
- Use of Grid-RNNs to capture predicate-argument interactions is noteworthy.  
- The model focuses on the challenging problem of zero argument detection and shows meaningful gains over a single-sequential model in this area.  
- Core contribution is primarily an adaptation/extension of existing models rather than a wholly new method.  
- The impact of explicitly modeling predicate interactions appears somewhat limited compared to Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Related work discussion is insufficiently thorough, notably failing to address Shibata et al. (2016).  
- A direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), including methodological differences and performance on the same dataset, is needed.  
- Clarify architectural choices when adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) to Japanese and justify language-specific modifications.  
- Provide a deeper error analysis identifying persistent error types and challenging predicate-argument combinations.  
- Explain in detail how Grid-RNNs improve zero-argument detection and include illustrative examples where they succeed but sequential models fail.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed work with significant gains and a reasonable approach, recommended for acceptance subject to minor revisions addressing related work and deeper analysis.
