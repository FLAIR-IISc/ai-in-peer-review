- Applies grid-type RNNs to Japanese PAS and outperforms syntactic-parser-based systems.
- Model does not rely on explicit syntax and shows strong handling of zero pronouns (dropped arguments).
- Multi-sequence model that considers all predicates yields best performance on zero anaphora cases.
- Provides clear, detailed architecture and implementation aiding reproducibility.
- Empirical validation on the NAIST Text Corpus demonstrates strong results.
- Thorough experimentation including ablation studies (network depth, residual connections) and baselines.
- State-of-the-art performance without syntactic information highlights effective multi-predicate interaction modeling.
- Effectively addresses zero anaphora, a major challenge in Japanese PAS analysis.
- Limited generalizability: results shown only on NAIST newspaper corpus, unclear cross-domain/language transfer.
- Potential hyperparameter sensitivity due to many tuning choices; more analysis of hyperparameter impact needed.
- Presentation issues: unclear boldface in Table 2 and need for clearer visual illustration of PRED feature context.
- Question about computational cost of the multi-sequence model versus simpler methods.
- Question about use and effect of pre-trained word embeddings on performance.
- Question about adapting the model to related languages (e.g., Korean, Chinese).
- Minor editorial corrections suggested (article usage, phrase fixes, Figure 4 example correction, phrasing consistency).
