- Proposes a joint neural PAS model for Japanese using Grid-RNNs, compares to single-sequence RNN, and achieves state-of-the-art results; well-executed and accessible.
- Paper needs a deeper discussion of task complexity, e.g., ambiguity in Figure 1 between NOM/ACC of the first predicate and NOM of the second predicate.
- Clarify how this PAS task differs from semantic role labeling (SRL), especially regarding ambiguity introduced by zero pronouns.
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable to highlight the need to distinguish intra- vs inter-sentential zero anaphora.
- Clarify representation for inter-sentential zero pronouns: are out-of-sentence arguments left unmarked or explicitly marked as empty, and discuss implications of the chosen design.
- The model lacks explicit constraints common in PAS/SRL (e.g., one argument per type per predicate; arguments fill only one slot), and the paper should analyze whether the model learns these constraints.
- Table 3 shows mixed results (only NOM consistently improved); include an analysis comparing the two models’ outputs—are predictions similar or do they differ systematically, and in what contexts?
- A detailed error/ disagreement analysis between models would meaningfully strengthen the paper.
- Reporting SRL results would be valuable but is acknowledged to require substantial additional work.
- Presentation and figures are good and effective.
- Numerous minor linguistic and reference-casing errors need correction (specific examples provided: multiple typos and inconsistent reference casing such as “japanese”, “lstm”, “conll”, “ilp”).
