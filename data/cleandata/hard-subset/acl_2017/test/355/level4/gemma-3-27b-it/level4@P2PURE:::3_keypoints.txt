- Introduces a joint neural approach for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and compares it to a single-sequence RNN.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results and presents the task accessibly for non-specialists.  
- Paper should discuss task complexity and inherent ambiguities (e.g., Figure 1 NOM/ACC ambiguity) in more detail.  
- Needs clearer comparison to semantic role labeling (SRL), emphasizing challenges posed by zero pronouns.  
- Request for statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable within a sentence to quantify task difficulty.  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (unmarked vs. explicit empty arguments) and discuss the design choice implications.  
- Model appears to lack common SRL constraints (one argument per type per predicate; an argument occupying only one slot), so analyze whether the model learns these.  
- Mixed results in Table 3 warrant analysis: multi-sequence model shows significant improvement mainly for NOM—explain why.  
- Compare outputs of the two models: are predictions largely identical or do they diverge in systematic contexts?  
- Deeper analysis of discrepancy contexts would illuminate model internals and strengthen the paper.  
- Suggestion that adding SRL comparisons would be valuable future work despite being substantial.  
- Presentation is generally good and figures are helpful for understanding the model.  
- List of minor language errors to correct (several line-specific typos provided).  
- Ensure consistent casing in references (e.g., “japanese,” “lstm,” “conll,” “ilp”).
