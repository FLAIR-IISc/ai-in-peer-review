- Proposes novel Japanese SRL models building on Zhou & Xu (2015) and adds a Grid-RNN to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and provides strong motivation.  
- Grid-RNN variant achieves substantial improvement over the current state-of-the-art on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark.  
- Grid-RNN outperforms the single-sequential model mainly by improving detection of zero arguments.  
- Main contribution is an extension from single-sequential to multi-sequential (Grid-RNN) modeling—effective but somewhat incremental.  
- Explicit modeling of predicate interactions shows limited impact compared to Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks a direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which would clarify relative strengths and contextualize the contribution.
