- Grid-RNN effectively models multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, achieving state-of-the-art results and improving zero-argument identification.
- Adapts RNN/grid-RNN techniques to Japanese PAS, moving toward parser-independent systems.
- Provides comprehensive experiments including ablations on network depth and residual connections.
- Achieves significant performance gains on the NAIST Text Corpus, validated via bootstrap resampling.
- Captures predicate relationships well, improving handling of omitted arguments common in Japanese.
- Shows robustness across case roles, with notable improvement on the dative case.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents models, diagrams, and results.
- Novelty is incremental—extension of sequential models to grid-RNNs with modest impact relative to prior feature/joint modeling.
- Missing direct comparison to recent neural Japanese PAS work (e.g., Shibata et al. 2016).
- Limited analysis of remaining error cases, especially which zero-anaphora resolutions fail.
- Request: provide direct performance comparison with Shibata et al. (2016) on the same dataset/settings.
- Request: give examples and types of zero anaphora errors the model still makes.
- Request: report model sensitivity to training dataset size and whether performance scales with larger corpora.
