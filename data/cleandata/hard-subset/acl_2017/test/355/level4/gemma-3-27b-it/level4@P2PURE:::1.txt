This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification in Japanese. Notably, the approach achieves strong performance without relying on explicit syntactic structure, surpassing current state-of-the-art systems that *do* incorporate syntactic information. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of both the implementation and experimental results. They demonstrate particular attention to the challenge of handling dropped arguments and zero pronouns, which are common in Japanese and pose a significant difficulty for PAS tasks. Their multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates within a sentence, yields the best performance on these challenging examples. The paper is detailed and clearly written.

Regarding weaknesses, I have only minor comments. Several typos are noted below, correcting which would enhance the readability of the text. It would be beneficial to further illustrate the inclusion of contextual information around the predicate in the PRED representation, perhaps by referencing the example in Figure 6 where the accusative marker is included with the verb. The use of boldface in Table 2 on page 7 was also unclear to me.
