- Paper applies Grid-type RNNs to Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) without explicit syntactic input and outperforms state-of-the-art systems that use syntax.  
- Implementation and experimental setup are clearly and thoroughly described.  
- The work pays close attention to dropped arguments (zero pronouns), a major challenge in Japanese PAS.  
- The multi-sequence model that conditions on all predicates in the sentence achieves the best performance on zero-pronoun examples.  
- Overall manuscript is detailed and clearly written.  
- Weaknesses are minor: several typos and English-fluency issues throughout the paper.  
- Suggestion to better illustrate the PRED feature by using the Figure 6 example where the accusative marker is included in the PRED string.  
- Reviewer did not understand the purpose of the boldface in Table 2 (page 7) and asks for clarification.  
- Specific typo/wording corrections noted across pages (examples): remove unnecessary "the" in phrases like "error propagation", fix "As an solution" → "As a solution", "She ate a bread" → "She ate bread", adjust tense/number in phrases like "assumes the independence" → "assumed independence", and add "the" in "the NAIST Text Corpus".
