- Contribution: Proposes a joint neural modelling approach for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and compares it to a conventional single-sequence RNN, achieving state-of-the-art results.
- Strength: Solidly executed paper on a well-established Japanese task, presented accessibly to non-specialists.
- Task complexity: Introduction should better highlight inherent ambiguities (e.g., Fig.1) where NOM/ACC of one predicate can be misinterpreted as NOM of another.
- SRL distinction: Paper should more clearly differentiate the task from SRL, emphasizing ambiguity from zero pronouns.
- Zero-pronoun resolvability: Request statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable within the same sentence to illustrate intra- vs inter-sentential challenges.
- Representation of inter-sentential zeros: Unclear whether inter-sentential zero arguments are left unmarked or explicitly marked as empty; this potential limitation needs discussion.
- Structural constraints: Model appears not to capture constraints like (1) predicates typically having only one argument of a given type and (2) an argument filling only one slot per predicate; analysis requested.
- Output analysis: Given mixed results (Table 3), the reviewer wants analysis comparing Multi-Seq and Single-Seq outputs—how similar/different are predictions and in what contexts do they diverge (NOM improvements noted).
- Broader analysis: A deeper exploration of model internals and error patterns would strengthen the paper and elevate it from solid to strong.
- Presentation & language: Overall good presentation and helpful figures, but several minor language errors and reference-casing issues are listed and should be fixed.
