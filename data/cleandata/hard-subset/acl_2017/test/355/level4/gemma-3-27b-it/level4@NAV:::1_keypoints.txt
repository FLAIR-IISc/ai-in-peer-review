- Applies Grid-type RNNs to Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification and achieves strong performance without explicit syntactic information, outperforming syntax-using state-of-the-art systems.  
- Implementation and experimental results are presented with commendable clarity and detail.  
- Explicitly addresses dropped arguments and zero pronouns common in Japanese; the multi-sequence model (considering all predicates) yields the best results for these cases.  
- Overall paper quality: well-written and thorough.  
- Minor issues: several typos throughout the manuscript that should be corrected (detailed page-specific corrections provided by reviewer).  
- Suggestion: better illustrate how the PRED representation incorporates predicate context (e.g., show inclusion of the accusative marker with the verb in the PRED string as in Figure 6).  
- Clarify the purpose of boldface formatting in Table 2 (currently unclear).  
- Reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ revisions after response.
