- Proposes Grid-type RNNs for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification.
- Achieves strong performance without explicit syntactic structure, outperforming syntax-based SOTA.
- Provides a clear and detailed description of implementation and experiments.
- Explicitly addresses dropped arguments and zero pronouns, common and challenging in Japanese.
- Multi-sequence model (considering all predicates in a sentence) yields best results on implicit/dropped arguments.
- Overall paper is well-written and detailed.
- Minor weaknesses: several English typos and wording issues throughout the paper.
- Recommend explicitly discussing Figure 6 to illustrate the benefit of predicate contextual information (accusative marker in verb).
- Clarify the use of boldface in Table 2.
- Lists specific suggested typo corrections on pages 1–8 (article usage, phrasing, example correction).
- Highlights significance: shows grid-RNNs can capture predicate-argument relations without parsing for morphologically rich, free-word-order languages.
- Reviewer satisfied with author response and recommends acceptance at ACL.
