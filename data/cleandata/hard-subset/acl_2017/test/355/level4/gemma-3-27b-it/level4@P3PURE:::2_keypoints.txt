- Proposes Japanese SRL models adapted from Zhou & Xu (2015) and extends them with Grid-RNNs to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.
- Evaluation on a well-known Japanese SRL benchmark shows significant improvement over the current state of the art.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.
- The proposed model improves accuracy compared to the previous SOTA.
- Grid-RNN variant yields slightly better performance, mainly via improved detection of zero arguments.
- Main technical contribution is extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential (predicate-interaction) model.
- Impact of explicitly modeling predicate interactions appears smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks comparison to related neural extension by Shibata et al. (2016), which the reviewer would like to see.
