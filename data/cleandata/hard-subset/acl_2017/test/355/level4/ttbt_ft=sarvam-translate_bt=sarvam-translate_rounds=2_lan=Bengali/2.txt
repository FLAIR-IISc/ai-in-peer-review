This paper proposes a new prediction model for Japanese SRL tasks, following the state-of-the-art model in English (Zhou and Xu, 2015).

The authors further improved the model by using the Grid-RRNAF framework to manage the interaction between multiple predictor arguments.

The evaluation was conducted on the widely known benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL, and the performance was significantly better than current state-of-the-art systems.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
The proposed model achieves improved accuracy compared to current state-of-the-art systems.
Additionally, the model built using Grid-RRNAF achieves slightly better performance than the proposed one-sequence model, mainly due to the improvement in zero-argument detection, which is the main focus of this paper.

Weaknesses:
To my knowledge, the main contribution of this paper is expanding the one-sequence model into a multi-sequence model. The effect of predictor interaction (Oucci et al., 2015) is somewhat less than that of a previous paper called (Shibata et al., 2016) which expanded the model of neural network modeling (Oucci et al., 2015). I am curious about the comparison between them.
