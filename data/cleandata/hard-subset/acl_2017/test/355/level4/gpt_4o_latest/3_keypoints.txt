- Introduces a joint neural Grid-RNN approach for Japanese PAS analysis, compared to a single-sequence RNN baseline.  
- Well-executed work that achieves state-of-the-art results and is accessible to non-Japanese readers.  
- Paper should emphasize task complexity more, especially inherent ambiguities in examples (e.g., NOM vs ACC argument ambiguities).  
- Needs clearer explanation of how PAS differs from SRL, particularly regarding ambiguity from zero pronouns.  
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable versus inter-sentential.  
- Clarify representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns: left unmarked or explicitly marked as empty, and implications for modeling.  
- Model appears to lack explicit structural constraints (e.g., one argument of a given type per predicate, arguments filling only one slot) used in ILP-based SRL.  
- Analyze model outputs to assess whether it implicitly learns those constraints or violates them.  
- Discuss mixed results in Table 3: Multi-Seq mainly improves NOM — compare outputs of Single-Seq vs Multi-Seq and identify contexts where they diverge.  
- Including SRL results would strengthen the paper, though it would require substantial additional work.  
- Presentation and figures are strong and helpful.  
- Minor language errors listed (multiple line corrections) and reference casing issues (e.g., "Japanese", "LSTM", "CoNLL", "ILP") should be fixed.
