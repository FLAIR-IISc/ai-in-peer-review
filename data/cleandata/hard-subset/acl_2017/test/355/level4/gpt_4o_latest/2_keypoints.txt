- Introduces novel Japanese SRL prediction models based on Zhou & Xu (2015).  
- Enhances the model with a Grid-RNN to capture interactions among arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significant improvement over the current state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-organized and presents a clear motivation.  
- Proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the existing state-of-the-art.  
- Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the single-sequential model, mainly by better handling zero arguments.  
- Main contribution is extending single-sequential to multi-sequential modeling; predicate interaction impact seems smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks comparison with related prior work (Shibata et al., 2016) that also extends Ouchi et al. (2015) using neural techniques.
