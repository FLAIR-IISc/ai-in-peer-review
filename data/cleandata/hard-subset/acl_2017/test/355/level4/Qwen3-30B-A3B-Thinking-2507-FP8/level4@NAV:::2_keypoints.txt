- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) English SRL approach to Japanese, proposing novel prediction models.  
- Extends the framework with Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments across multiple predicates.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significantly better performance than existing state-of-the-art systems.  
- Well-structured paper with clear motivation.  
- The proposed model achieves notable accuracy gains over current state-of-the-art.  
- Grid-RNN variant gives marginal additional gains, mainly by improving zero argument detection.  
- Main contribution is primarily extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential one.  
- Modeling of predicate interactions appears less impactful than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks clear comparison or clarification versus Shibata et al. (2016), which already extended Ouchi et al.’s model with neural methods.
