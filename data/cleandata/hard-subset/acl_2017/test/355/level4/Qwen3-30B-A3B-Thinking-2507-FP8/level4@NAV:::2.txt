

This paper introduces novel prediction models for Japanese SRL by adapting the English state-of-the-art approach of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this framework by incorporating Grid-RNNs to manage interactions between arguments across multiple predicates. Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark demonstrates significantly superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art systems.

Strengths:
The paper exhibits strong structure and clear motivation.
The proposed model achieves notable accuracy gains over current state-of-the-art systems.
The Grid-RNN-based variant yields marginally better results than the single-sequential model, primarily through enhanced zero argument detection—a core focus of the work.

Weakness:
The primary contribution appears to be extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential one. The impact of modeling predicate interactions seems less pronounced than in Ouchi et al. (2015). A prior study (Shibata et al., 2016) already extended Ouchi et al.'s model using neural network techniques; I would appreciate clarification on how this work compares to Shibata et al. (2016).
