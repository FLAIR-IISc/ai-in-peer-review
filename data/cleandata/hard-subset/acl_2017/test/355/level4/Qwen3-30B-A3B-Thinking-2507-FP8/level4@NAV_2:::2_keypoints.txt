- Paper introduces syntax-free neural PAS models for Japanese: (1) single-sequence RNN for argument identification, (2) multi-sequence Grid-RNN for multi-predicate interactions, (3) achieves SOTA on NAIST Text Corpus.
- Effectively tackles zero-argument identification, showing neural models can capture long-distance dependencies without syntax.
- Multi-sequence model improves zero-argument F1 (47.12% vs 46.10% for single-sequence).
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with comparisons to relevant baselines (including Ouchi et al., 2015) and shows clear multi-metric improvements.
- Main novelty is incremental—extension from single- to multi-sequence via Grid-RNN rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Overall gains are modest: only 0.27 F1 improvement over the single-sequence model.
- Paper lacks adequate comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), making novelty and relative contribution unclear.
- Impact of modeling multi-predicate interactions seems smaller than claimed; zero-argument improvement (~1.0 F1) is not substantially larger than prior baseline gains.
- Request detailed comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), especially on handling multi-predicate interactions.
- Ask authors to explain why Grid-RNN yields only modest gains and specify which multi-predicate interactions most help zero-argument identification.
