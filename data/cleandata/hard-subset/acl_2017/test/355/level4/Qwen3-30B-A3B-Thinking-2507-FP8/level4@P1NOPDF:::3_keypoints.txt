- Paper presents a joint Grid-RNN approach for Japanese PAS, achieves SOTA and explains the task accessibly.  
- Need stronger discussion of task complexity: ambiguous cases (e.g., overlapping arguments in Fig.1) require deeper analysis.  
- Clarify how PAS ambiguity differs from SRL, especially concerning zero pronouns, for non-Japanese readers.  
- Provide statistics on proportion of intra-sententially resolvable zero pronouns to highlight intra- vs inter-sentential distinction.  
- Address representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns: current omission of explicit zero-pronoun markers seems potentially suboptimal.  
- Discuss implications of not explicitly marking zero pronouns and whether the model can learn them implicitly.  
- Analyze whether the model enforces common SRL/ILP constraints (one argument per type; single slot per predicate) or learns violations.  
- Examine error patterns/output statistics to show if the model respects those constraints.  
- Investigate why Multi-Seq shows minimal improvements beyond NOM (Table 3) with comparative output analysis between models.  
- Compare models’ outputs to determine if gains are meaningful or superficial (identical predictions, different patterns, or context-specific differences).  
- Presentation is clear and figures are effective, but several minor language and capitalization errors should be corrected.  
- Overall contribution is valuable; addressing task complexity, zero-pronoun representation, constraint modeling, and deeper model comparison would substantially strengthen the paper.
