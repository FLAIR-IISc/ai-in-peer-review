- Proposes adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) for Japanese SRL and extending it with Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments across predicates.
- Evaluates on the standard Japanese SRL benchmark and reports a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.
- Proposed model yields meaningful accuracy gains over prior work.
- Grid-RNN variant gives a modest additional boost, mainly by improving detection of zero arguments.
- Main contribution is mainly the move from single-sequential to multi-sequential modeling.
- Modeling predicate interactions appears less impactful than in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks direct comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which already applied neural extensions to Ouchi et al.’s model.
- Needs to clarify differences/improvements relative to Shibata et al., especially for zero-argument handling, to establish novelty and significance.
