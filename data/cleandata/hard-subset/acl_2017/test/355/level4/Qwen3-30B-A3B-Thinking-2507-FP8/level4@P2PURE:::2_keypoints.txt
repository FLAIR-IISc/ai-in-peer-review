- Proposes new Japanese SRL models by adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) and adding Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.
- Evaluated on the standard Japanese SRL benchmark and achieves significantly higher performance than the current state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.
- The proposed model yields improved accuracy over existing SOTA; Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the single-sequential variant.
- Performance gains mainly come from better detection of zero arguments, the paper's core focus.
- Main contribution is extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential framework.
- The effect of modeling predicate interactions is less pronounced than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Authors should clarify how their approach differs from and improves upon Shibata et al. (2016), which also extended Ouchi et al. with neural methods.
