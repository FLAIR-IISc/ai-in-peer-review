- Presents a strong application of Grid-type RNNs for Japanese PAS that outperforms syntactically-informed state-of-the-art systems using no explicit syntax.
- Proposes a clear multi-sequence model that processes all predicates in a sentence simultaneously to capture multi-predicate interactions.
- Demonstrates notable gains on challenging phenomena like dropped arguments and zero pronouns, with rigorous evaluation showing best performance on these cases.
- The technical description is detailed and the paper is well-structured and thoroughly documented.
- Main weaknesses are minor presentation issues: unclear rationale for boldface in Table 2 and ambiguous description of the PRED tokenization strategy.
- Recommends adding a concrete illustration of PRED tokenization (e.g., Figure 6 with "tabemashita" + accusative "o") to clarify input representation.
- Lists several typos and grammatical corrections throughout the paper (articles, phrase choices, pluralization) that should be fixed for fluency.
- Authors’ responses to reviewer points are adequate and suggested corrections are straightforward.
- Core contribution is robust: a syntactically-agnostic neural model can surpass syntactically-informed baselines on Japanese PAS, especially for zero pronouns.
- The multi-sequence approach effectively models predicate interactions, a key insight for Japanese NLP.
- With minor editorial revisions addressing presentation and typos, the paper is suitable for publication at ACL 2017.
