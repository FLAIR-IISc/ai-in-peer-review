- Proposes Japanese SRL models adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) and extends to multi-predicate interactions using Grid-RNNs.  
- Evaluated on JNLP-2015 benchmark and reports statistically significant improvements over prior state of the art.  
- Paper is clearly structured, well-motivated, and situates its contribution within Japanese SRL literature.  
- The Grid-RNN variant yields a modest but notable accuracy gain over the single-sequential baseline.  
- Main performance improvement derives from better detection of zero-valued (zero) arguments.  
- Experimental design is sound and results include appropriate statistical validation.  
- Contribution appears incremental relative to prior work, lacking substantial conceptual novelty.  
- Reported gains from modeling predicate interactions are smaller than those by Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Shibata et al. (2016) already applied neural methods to Ouchi et al.’s multi-predicate framework, and the paper fails to compare explicitly to that work.  
- Authors need to clarify architectural/implementation differences from Shibata et al. (2016) and why Grid-RNNs are a meaningful advance.  
- Unclear whether zero-argument improvements are uniquely due to Grid-RNNs or could be achieved with simpler interaction mechanisms.  
- Without stronger comparative analysis and contextualization, the work risks being viewed as a minor adaptation rather than a substantive advance.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending detailed comparison with Shibata et al. (2016) and clearer justification of Grid-RNN’s unique advantages.
