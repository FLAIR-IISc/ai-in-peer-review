- Proposes neural PAS models for Japanese that avoid syntactic parsers: a predicate-wise single-sequence model and a Grid-RNN multi-sequence model for all predicates.

- Claims contributions: novel parser-free neural architecture achieving SOA performance.

- Claims contributions: multi-predicate interactions substantially improve zero argument identification.

- Claims contributions: shows word-sequence-based models can outperform syntactic-dependent approaches without external resources.

- Strength: eliminates error propagation from syntactic parsers and achieves 81.42% F1 on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, surpassing prior syntactic-dependent methods.

- Strength: multi-sequence model gives a 3.0-point F1 boost for zero arguments (47.12% vs. 44.09%), addressing a key PAS challenge.

- Strength: thorough experiments and ablations show Grid-RNN and residual connections help; results are statistically significant and strong for low-frequency dative arguments.

- Weakness: lacks comparison to other neural, syntax-free baselines (e.g., Shibata et al., 2016; Iida et al., 2016), weakening the novelty claim.

- Weakness: PRED feature is ambiguously described—unclear how surrounding words are encoded in the Grid-RNN representation.

- Weakness: Table 2 formatting (boldface) is confusing and leaves emphasis/exact comparisons unclear.

- Question: How do results compare to Shibata et al. (2016) and can their model match performance without syntactic info?

- Question: Does the PRED feature (e.g., including "を") imply implicit syntactic capture or is it an input-representation artifact?

- Question: Given prior neural work using selectional preferences, how do you justify that pure word-sequence input is more effective?

- Additional: Paper has minor grammatical errors that should be fixed.

- Additional: The claim that multi-sequence significantly outperforms single-sequence should clarify consistency across case roles.

- Additional: The "zero pronouns" discussion should more clearly distinguish Dep and Zero arguments in the example.
