

This paper proposes new prediction models for Japanese SRL by adapting the English state-of-the-art model from Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors extend this model by incorporating Grid-RNNs to handle interactions between arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluation on the standard Japanese SRL benchmark dataset demonstrates significantly improved performance over the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and well-motivated. The proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the existing state-of-the-art system. Additionally, the Grid-RNN-based model yields slightly better results than the single-sequential model, primarily due to enhanced detection of zero arguments—a key focus of this work.

Weakness:
The primary contribution appears to be the extension of the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model. However, the reported impact of predicate interactions is smaller than that in Ouchi et al. (2015). Furthermore, Shibata et al. (2016) previously extended Ouchi et al. (2015)'s model using neural network techniques. The authors should clarify how their approach differs from and improves upon Shibata et al. (2016), particularly regarding the handling of predicate interactions.
