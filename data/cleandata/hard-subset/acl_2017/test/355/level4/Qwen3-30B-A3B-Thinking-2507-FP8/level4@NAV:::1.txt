

Strengths:
This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) determination. The approach operates without explicit syntactic structure and outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that incorporate syntactic information. The authors provide a clear, detailed implementation description and results analysis. Notably, they address performance on dropped arguments and zero pronouns—common in Japanese and particularly challenging for PAS—with careful attention. Their multi-sequence model, which processes all predicates in a sentence simultaneously, achieves the highest performance for these cases. The paper is well-written and thorough.

Weaknesses:
