- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model for Japanese PAS that induces multi-predicate interaction features from word sequences without syntactic parsing, achieving state-of-the-art on NAIST.
- Claims the model avoids error propagation common in joint modeling by eliminating reliance on syntactic information.
- Demonstrates significant improvement in zero argument identification, a core challenge in Japanese PAS.
- Well-executed solution to a known problem, achieving strong empirical results without syntactic features.
- Clear, well-motivated model design with solid experimental evaluation showing Grid-RNNs capture multi-predicate interactions.
- Presentation is accessible to non-specialists and includes helpful figures illustrating architecture and concepts.
- Needs clearer elaboration on task complexity, especially ambiguity in zero pronoun resolution across predicates (e.g., NOM vs ACC distinctions).
- Lacks sufficient discussion of how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented or handled, affecting zero pronoun capture.
- Does not analyze whether the model learns linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per case role per predicate).
- Asks whether inter-sentential zero pronouns are marked as empty arguments or omitted in the model representation.
- Requests analysis on how well the model enforces linguistic constraints and on contexts where Single-Seq vs Multi-Seq predictions differ.
