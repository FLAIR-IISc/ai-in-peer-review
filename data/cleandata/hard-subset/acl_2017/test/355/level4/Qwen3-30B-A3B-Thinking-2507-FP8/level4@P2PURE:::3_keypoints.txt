- Presents a joint Grid-RNN approach for Japanese PAS, outperforming single-sequence RNNs and achieving state-of-the-art results with accessible exposition.
- The paper should better emphasize task complexity: Figure 1 shows inherent ambiguity between NOM and ACC arguments and argument-sharing across predicates.
- Provide a clearer contrast with Semantic Role Labeling (SRL), especially regarding ambiguity introduced by zero pronouns, to strengthen motivation.
- Include statistics on how many zero pronouns are resolvable intra-sententially vs. inter-sentential to clarify the task difficulty and modeling needs.
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented; omitting explicit marking appears suboptimal and needs discussion.
- The model does not explicitly enforce ILP-like constraints: (1) one argument per type per predicate (esp. NOM/ACC) and (2) an argument occupying only one slot per predicate; analyze outputs relative to these constraints.
- Investigate mixed results in Table 3 (Multi-Seq gains mainly for NOM): are outputs largely identical or do meaningful differences exist—identify contexts where models diverge.
- Pointing out specific divergence contexts would be the most valuable analysis improvement (adding SRL comparisons is larger additional work).
- Presentation and figures are strong and helpful for understanding the model.
- Correct minor language errors (list of specific line fixes provided) and ensure consistent casing in references (e.g., Japanese, LSTM, CoNLL, ILP).
