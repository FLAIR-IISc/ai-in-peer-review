- Paper proposes a joint Grid-RNN approach for Japanese PAS, achieves state-of-the-art results and is well-executed.  
- Task framing needs stronger emphasis on inherent complexity and ambiguity (e.g., NOM/ACC ambiguity and zero-pronoun resolution).  
- Clarify how PAS differs from SRL, especially regarding zero pronouns and cross-sentence anaphora.  
- Report statistics on intra-sentential vs. inter-sentential zero pronouns to highlight key challenges.  
- Explain how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (omitted vs. explicitly marked) and justify that design choice.  
- Address whether the model enforces or learns PAS constraints: one argument per type per predicate and one slot per argument.  
- Analyze model outputs for constraint violations and how well the network approximates ILP-like constraints.  
- Table 3 shows only small gains for Multi-Seq over Single-Seq (mainly for NOM); provide more detailed, significance-tested comparisons.  
- Provide qualitative/context-specific analysis of differences (e.g., particular syntactic constructions) to reveal model behavior.  
- Presentation is clear and figures helpful, but fix listed minor language errors and ensure consistent reference casing.  
- Adding SRL comparisons would be useful but is acknowledged as out-of-scope; focus revisions on task clarity, zero-pronoun representation, constraint validation, and deeper model comparison.
