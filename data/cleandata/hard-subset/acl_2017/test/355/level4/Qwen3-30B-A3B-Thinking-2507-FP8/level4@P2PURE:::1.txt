

- Strengths:  
This paper presents a novel application of Grid-based Recurrent Neural Networks to predicate-argument structure (PAS) prediction in Japanese. The approach operates without explicit syntactic structure and achieves superior performance over current state-of-the-art (SOA) systems that incorporate syntactic features. The authors provide a clear, detailed description of both the implementation and results. Notably, they thoroughly address the challenge of dropped arguments and zero pronouns—prevalent in Japanese and particularly difficult for PAS tasks. Their multi-sequence model, which jointly processes all predicates in a sentence, attains the highest performance on these examples. The paper is well-written and technically thorough.
