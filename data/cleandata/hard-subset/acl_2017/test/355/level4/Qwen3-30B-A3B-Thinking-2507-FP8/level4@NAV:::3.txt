

This paper introduces a joint neural modeling approach for Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis in Japanese using Grid-RNNs, benchmarking it against a conventional single-sequence RNN method. The work is well-executed, addresses a well-established Japanese NLP task, achieves state-of-the-art results, and presents the problem accessibly to non-Japanese specialists. However, the task complexity could be emphasized more clearly—particularly regarding the inherent ambiguity in Figure 1 (e.g., distinguishing the NOM/ACC arguments of the first predicate from the NOM argument of the second predicate) and clarifying how PAS differs from Semantic Role Labeling (SRL), especially due to zero pronoun ambiguity. Additionally, statistics on the proportion of intra-sententially resolvable zero pronouns would strengthen the analysis, as this distinction (intra- vs. inter-sentential zero anaphors) significantly impacts the task definition. A critical question remains: for inter-sentential zero pronouns, how is the argument represented? The paper implies no explicit marking (i.e., no representation of the zero pronoun), which risks a defective model representation and may hinder zero pronoun capture; this requires explicit discussion.  

The model also overlooks constraints captured by ILP-based SRL methods, such as: (1) predicates typically having only one argument per type (NOM/ACC), and (2) arguments filling only one predicate slot. An analysis of model outputs to assess constraint adherence would be valuable. Furthermore, Table 3 shows minimal improvement (only in NOM) for Multi-Seq over Single-Seq; a deeper comparison of output differences—whether models are largely identical, contextually distinct yet aggregate-equivalent, or where discrepancies arise—would provide crucial insight into model behavior and elevate the paper from solid to strong.
