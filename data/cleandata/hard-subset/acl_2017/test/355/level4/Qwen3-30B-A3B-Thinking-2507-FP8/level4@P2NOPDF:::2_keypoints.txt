- Proposes novel Japanese SRL prediction models by adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) English SRL framework.  
- Extends the approach with Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments across multiple predicates.  
- Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark.  
- Reports significant performance gains over the current state-of-the-art system.  

- Strength: Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.  
- Strength: Model shows notable overall accuracy improvement vs prior work.  
- Strength: Grid-RNN variant gives a modest but meaningful improvement over the single-sequential baseline.  
- Strength: Gains are primarily driven by better zero-argument detection.  

- Weakness: Main contribution is an extension from single- to multi-sequential modeling, which is incremental.  
- Weakness: Performance gains from modeling predicate interactions are modest compared to Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Weakness: Shibata et al. (2016) already applied neural extensions to Ouchi et al.’s model, but no comparison is made to that work.  
- Weakness: Without comparing to Shibata et al. (2016), novelty and advancement over prior neural SRL work remain unclear.  
- Weakness: Emphasis on zero-argument detection is valuable, but the methodological contribution needs stronger justification.
