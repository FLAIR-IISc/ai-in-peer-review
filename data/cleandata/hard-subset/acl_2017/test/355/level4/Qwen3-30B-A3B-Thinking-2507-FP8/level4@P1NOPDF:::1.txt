

Strengths:  
This paper presents a novel application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to predicate-argument structure (PAS) prediction in Japanese. Notably, the approach achieves state-of-the-art results without relying on explicit syntactic structure—outperforming current methods that do incorporate syntactic features. The authors provide a clear, detailed account of both implementation and results. They particularly emphasize performance on challenging cases involving dropped arguments and zero pronouns, which are prevalent in Japanese. Their multi-sequence model, which jointly processes all predicates in a sentence, delivers the strongest results for these cases. The paper is well-structured and clearly written.  

Weaknesses:  
My comments are minor. First, the description of how PRED includes contextual elements around the predicate (e.g., the accusative marker in Figure 6) would benefit from a concrete example to clarify the design. Second, the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7) is unclear and should be explained.
