

This paper proposes novel prediction models for Japanese SRL by adapting the English state-of-the-art approach of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this framework using Grid-RNNs to capture interactions between arguments across multiple predicates. Evaluation on the standard Japanese SRL benchmark demonstrates a significant improvement over the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated. The proposed model achieves a meaningful accuracy improvement over prior work. Additionally, the Grid-RNN-based variant yields a modest performance gain over the single-sequential model, primarily through enhanced detection of zero arguments—a key focus of this work.

Weaknesses:
The primary contribution appears to be the extension from a single-sequential to a multi-sequential model framework. However, the impact of modeling predicate interactions seems less pronounced than in Ouchi et al. (2015). Crucially, Shibata et al. (2016) already extended Ouchi et al.’s model using neural networks, making a direct comparison between their approach and this work highly relevant. The paper would benefit from addressing how this method differs from or improves upon Shibata et al.’s neural extension, particularly regarding zero-argument handling. Without this comparison, the novelty and relative significance of the proposed Grid-RNN extension remain somewhat unclear.
