- Proposes Japanese SRL models by adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) and incorporating Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluated on the standard Japanese SRL benchmark and shows significant improvement over the current state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.  
- Proposed model achieves higher overall accuracy than existing state-of-the-art.  
- Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the single-sequential model, mainly via better detection of zero arguments.  
- Main contribution is extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model.  
- Reported effect of modeling predicate interactions is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Need clarification on how this work differs from and improves upon Shibata et al. (2016), especially in handling predicate interactions.
