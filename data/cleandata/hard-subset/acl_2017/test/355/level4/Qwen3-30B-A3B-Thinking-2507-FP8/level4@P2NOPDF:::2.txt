

This paper proposes novel prediction models for Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) by adapting Zhou and Xu’s (2015) English SRL framework. The authors extend this approach using Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments across multiple predicates. Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark, the proposed method achieves significant performance gains over the current state-of-the-art system.  

Strengths:  
The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated. The model demonstrates a notable accuracy improvement over prior work, with the Grid-RNN variant offering a modest but meaningful enhancement over the single-sequential baseline. This improvement is primarily driven by better zero-argument detection—a key focus of the study.  

Weaknesses:  
The primary contribution appears to be the extension of the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential framework. However, the performance gains from modeling predicate interactions are more modest compared to Ouchi et al. (2015), whose work this paper directly builds upon. Critically, Shibata et al. (2016) already extended Ouchi et al.’s model using neural networks, addressing similar challenges. The authors do not compare their approach with Shibata et al. (2016), which is essential for establishing the novelty and significance of their contribution. Without this comparison, it remains unclear how their Grid-RNN extension advances beyond prior neural SRL work. The paper’s emphasis on zero-argument detection is valuable, but the incremental nature of the methodological contribution warrants stronger justification.
