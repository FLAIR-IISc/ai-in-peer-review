- Introduces a Grid-RNN neural model for Japanese PAS that removes reliance on syntactic parsers and captures multi-predicate interactions from word sequences.
- Claims state-of-the-art performance without syntactic information, especially on zero pronoun resolution.
- Reports significant gains on zero arguments (47.12% F1 vs. 44.09% prior best).
- Provides thorough experimental validation including ablation studies on depth and residual connections.
- Demonstrates the model’s linguistic relevance with clear explanations of components and zero pronoun handling.
- Evaluates on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 with standard splits for fair comparison to prior work.
- Shows that eliminating parser dependency can overcome error propagation in previous pipeline approaches.
- Manuscript has minor grammatical errors that hurt readability (e.g., "an solution", inconsistent article use).
- PRED feature explanation (Fig. 6) is unclear, particularly how the accusative marker is included with the verb.
- Use of boldface in Table 2 is unexplained and potentially confusing.
- Lacks explicit comparison to similar neural approaches in English SRL, especially constituent- vs. dependency-based argument identification.
- Asks authors to clarify how Grid-RNN differs architecturally from prior multi-predicate neural approaches and why DAT (dative) shows unusually large improvement.
