

This paper proposes new prediction models for Japanese SRL by adapting the English state-of-the-art model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors extend this model by incorporating Grid-RNNs to handle interactions between arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluation on the standard Japanese SRL benchmark dataset demonstrates significantly higher performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated. The proposed model achieves improved accuracy over the existing state-of-the-art. Additionally, the Grid-RNN-based model yields slightly higher performance than the single-sequential variant, primarily due to enhanced detection of zero arguments—the core focus of this work.

Weakness:
The primary contribution appears to be extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential framework. The impact of modeling predicate interactions is less pronounced than in Ouchi et al. (2015). Furthermore, Shibata et al. (2016) previously extended Ouchi et al.'s model using neural network techniques. The authors should clarify how their approach differs from and improves upon Shibata et al. (2016).
