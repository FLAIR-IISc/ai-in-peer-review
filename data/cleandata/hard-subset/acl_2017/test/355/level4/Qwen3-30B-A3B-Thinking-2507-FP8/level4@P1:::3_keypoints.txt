- Introduces Grid-RNN to model multi-predicate interactions from word sequences, avoiding syntactic parsers and achieving state-of-the-art on NAIST, especially for zero-argument identification.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms prior work by 1.0–3.0 F1 on zero arguments (47.12% vs. 44.09%) and achieves best results on the NAIST benchmark without syntactic features.
- Effective handling of zero pronouns: multi-sequence model yields ~1.0 F1 improvement over single-sequence, supporting the value of cross-predicate context for omitted arguments.
- Novel architecture and training: Grid-RNN with residual connections is well-motivated and deeper (8-layer) models give additional ~1.0 F1 gains.
- Insufficient discussion on zero-argument resolution scope: unclear what proportion of Zero instances are intra- vs inter-sentential and how that affects evaluation.
- Unclear representation of inter-sentential zeros: paper omits whether inter-sentential zero pronouns are marked or treated as unmarked/null in training data.
- Lack of analysis on linguistic constraints: no evaluation of whether the model respects Japanese PAS constraints (e.g., one NOM per predicate) or how often constraint violations occur.
- Limited explanation for NOM gains: marginal Multi-Seq vs Single-Seq improvements on NOM (88.17% vs 88.10%) are reported but not explained or exemplified.
- Misleading comparison to SRL work: claiming extension of Zhou & Xu (2015) is inaccurate without clarifying that Japanese PAS is dependency-based and the model jointly predicts multiple predicates.
- Key questions for authors: percentage of Zero arguments that are intra-sentential vs inter-sentential and impact on model performance.
- Key question for authors: frequency of constraint violations (e.g., multiple NOMs) in cases where Multi-Seq corrects Single-Seq.
- Presentation issues: minor language errors and inconsistent reference casing; more discussion of inter-sentential challenges would improve task framing.
