- Neural model achieves state-of-the-art Japanese PAS performance without syntactic parsers, avoiding pipeline error propagation.  
- Grid-RNN architecture effectively models multi-predicate interactions to identify zero arguments.  
- Comprehensive empirical validation shows significant gains on zero arguments and overall F-measure.  
- Significant performance improvement: multi-sequence model 81.42% F vs prior 79.23%, +3.0 points on zero arguments.  
- Multi-predicate modeling advantage: multi-sequence outperforms single-sequence, improving zero-argument detection.  
- Thorough evaluations: detailed case-role and argument-type analyses, large dative-case improvement, and ablation studies.  
- Ambiguous description of the PRED feature—unclear whether predicate window includes particles/markers.  
- Inconsistent and confusing boldface formatting in Table 2.  
- Underdeveloped comparison to English SRL—insufficient justification for replacing CRF with softmax and relation to prior multi-predicate work.  
- Minor grammatical errors throughout the paper that hurt readability.  
- Question: Does the PRED window intentionally include particles like "を" or is that an artifact?  
- Question: How does your "joint prediction for all predicates" differ from Ouchi et al. (2015)?  
- Question: Would incorporating unlabeled Japanese data (pretraining) likely further improve results?  
- Overall: strong contribution to Japanese PAS; fix minor grammar/formatting issues (authors addressed typos satisfactorily).
