- Presents a joint neural Grid-RNN approach for Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis and reports state-of-the-art results.  
- Paper is well-executed and accessible to readers without Japanese linguistics background.  
- Needs a more detailed discussion of task complexity, especially ambiguity in argument identification (e.g., NOM vs ACC overlap in Figure 1).  
- Clarify how PAS differs from SRL regarding prevalence and handling of zero pronouns.  
- Provide statistics on the proportion of intra-sententially resolvable zero pronouns to quantify task difficulty.  
- Explain representation and treatment of inter-sentential zero pronouns and whether the model can detect their presence versus treating them as absent.  
- Address lack of explicit modeling of linguistic constraints: at most one argument per role per predicate and arguments occupying only one role per predicate.  
- Analyze whether the model implicitly learns these constraints and where Single-Seq and Multi-Seq outputs diverge (error analysis, specific cases).  
- Deeper analysis of Table 3 improvements (primarily for NOM) to determine if gains reflect constraint adherence or incidental patterns.  
- Recommend discussing model limitations from representation choices to clarify realistic capabilities.  
- Minor language and formatting corrections needed (specific line fixes and consistent casing for terms like Japanese, LSTM, CoNLL, ILP).  
- Overall: strong empirical contribution, but addressing zero-anaphor representation, constraint learning, and comparative output analysis would significantly strengthen the paper.
