- Proposes Grid-RNNs for Japanese PAS to model multi-predicate interactions from word sequences without syntactic parsers.  
- Claims a novel Grid-RNN architecture that achieves state-of-the-art performance on NAIST (81.42% F1).  
- Reports up to ~3 F1 point improvement for zero argument identification and shows multi-predicate interactions help resolve zero pronouns.  
- Novel approach eliminates parser-induced error propagation and outperforms prior SOTA (79.23% → 81.42% F1).  
- Strong empirical validation and ablation studies demonstrating statistically significant gains, especially for Zero arguments (47.12% vs. 46.10%).  
- Clear task definition and evaluation protocol, distinguishing Dep/Zero/Inter-Zero and using standard NAIST splits.  
- Fails to sufficiently explain the inherent difficulty of zero pronoun resolution and lacks statistics on intra-sentential resolvability.  
- Unclear how inter-sentential zeros are represented/handled in training (marked empty vs. omitted) and possible bias implications.  
- No analysis of linguistic constraint enforcement (e.g., one argument per role) or qualitative breakdown of where Multi-Seq improvements occur.  
- How are inter-sentential zero pronouns handled in training data and does that affect intra- vs inter-sentential distinction?  
- Please provide qualitative examples where Multi-Seq yields different/better predictions that explain the F1 gains.  
- What percentage of zero pronouns in NAIST are intra-sentential (resolvable within the sentence)?  
- Minor typos and grammatical errors (e.g., "significent", "an solution") need correction.  
- Strengthen the comparison to English SRL by clarifying differences between dependency-based PAS and constituent-based SRL.  
- References have inconsistent casing (e.g., "japanese," "lstm") and should be standardized.
