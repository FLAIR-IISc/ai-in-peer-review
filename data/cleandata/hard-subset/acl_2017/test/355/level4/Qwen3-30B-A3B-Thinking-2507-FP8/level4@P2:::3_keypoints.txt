- Grid-RNN model that induces features for multi-predicate interactions without using syntactic input, avoiding parser error propagation.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, with notable improvements on zero argument identification.  
- Analysis indicates modeling multi-predicate interactions significantly benefits zero-argument resolution.

- Removes reliance on syntactic parsers by operating directly on word sequences, increasing robustness.  
- Empirical gains are strong and significant (up to +3.0 F) for the challenging zero-argument case.  
- Clear explanation of the Japanese PAS task and a justified focus on intra-sentential arguments.  
- Thorough experimental design with ablation studies on depth, residuals, and multi-predicate modeling.

- Does not adequately explain intra-sentential zero-pronoun ambiguity or how the model resolves conflicting arguments.  
- Handling of inter-sentential zero pronouns is unclear; omission may create a representation gap.  
- Lacks analysis of how linguistic constraints (e.g., one NOM/ACC per predicate, single-slot filling) are enforced or violated.  
- Insufficient analysis of differences between single-sequence and multi-sequence models, despite modest overall improvements.

- Request for statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns in NAIST are intra-sententially resolvable.  
- How are inter-sentential zero pronouns represented or handled if they cannot be resolved within the sentence?  
- Request detailed analysis comparing single-sequence and multi-sequence outputs, especially where multi-sequence improves performance.  
- How does the model enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., one NOM per predicate), and are constraint violations observed in outputs?
