

- Strengths:  
This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to predicate-argument structure (PAS) prediction in Japanese. Notably, the approach achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance without leveraging explicit syntactic structure, outperforming prior systems that incorporate such structure. The authors provide a clear, detailed description of both the implementation and results. They particularly emphasize performance on challenging cases involving dropped arguments and zero pronouns—common in Japanese—demonstrating that their multi-sequence model, which jointly processes all predicates in a sentence, delivers the best results for these cases. The paper is well-written and technically thorough.  

- Weaknesses:  
The weaknesses are minor. The following clarifications would improve the paper:  
1. The description of how the PRED token incorporates contextual elements (e.g., the accusative marker in Figure 6) should be illustrated with a concrete example.  
2. The purpose of the boldface formatting in Table 2 (p. 7) is unclear.
