This paper proposes new models for Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) by adapting the state-of-the-art English model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this model using Grid-RNNs to handle interactions between the arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluated on the standard benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL, the proposed models achieve significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
*   The proposed model achieves a significant improvement in accuracy over the current state-of-the-art system.
*   Furthermore, the Grid-RNN model achieves slightly better performance than the single-sequence model. This improvement is attributed mainly to better detection of zero arguments, which is a key focus of the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
*   The primary contribution of this paper appears to be the extension of a single-sequence model to a multi-predicate model using Grid-RNNs. However, the performance gain from modeling these predicate interactions is less substantial than that reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
*   The paper would also be significantly strengthened by a comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), who previously extended the work of Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural networks. Without this comparison, it is difficult to fully assess the contribution of the proposed model.
