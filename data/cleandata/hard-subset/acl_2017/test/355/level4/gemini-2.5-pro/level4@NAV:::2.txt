This paper presents new models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task, adapting the state-of-the-art English model developed by Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further enhance this model by incorporating a Grid-RNN framework, designed to capture the interactions among arguments from multiple predicates. The empirical evaluation, conducted on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark dataset, shows that the proposed model significantly outperforms the current state-of-the-art.

**Strengths:**
*   The manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and provides strong motivation for the research.
*   The proposed model establishes a new state-of-the-art performance, achieving a notable improvement in accuracy.
*   The Grid-RNN extension provides a slight performance boost over the single-sequential model, primarily by improving the detection of zero arguments, which is a central goal of this research.

**Weakness:**
*   The primary contribution appears to be the extension of the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential architecture. The reported impact of modeling predicate interactions is less substantial than that shown by Ouchi et al. (2015). Additionally, the paper does not discuss or compare against a relevant previous study by Shibata et al. (2016), which also extended the Ouchi et al. (2015) model with neural networks. A comparison with this work would be valuable for contextualizing the contributions of the current paper.
