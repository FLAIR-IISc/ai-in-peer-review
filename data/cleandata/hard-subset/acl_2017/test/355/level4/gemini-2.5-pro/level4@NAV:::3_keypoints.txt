- Paper introduces a joint neural approach using Grid-RNNs for Japanese PAS and compares it to single-sequence RNNs.  
- Well-executed work that advances the state of the art and presents the task accessibly for non-specialists.  
- Paper should better emphasize task complexity with a detailed walkthrough of Figure 1 to show predicate/argument ambiguities.  
- Clarify how PAS differs from standard SRL, especially the role of zero pronouns and associated ambiguities.  
- Provide statistics on what fraction of zero pronouns are resolvable intra-sententially to highlight a core challenge.  
- Clarify how the model represents inter-sentential zero pronouns (omitted vs. explicitly marked) and discuss the design tradeoffs.  
- The model does not explicitly enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., at most one argument per core role; arguments fill only one role); discuss whether these are learned implicitly.  
- Analyze model outputs to check if it respects role-uniqueness and single-role-per-argument constraints or if errors persist.  
- Deeper analysis needed for mixed results in Table 3, since gains concentrate on NOM — explain qualitative differences behind aggregate scores.  
- Provide a qualitative comparison between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq outputs to show where they agree and diverge.  
- Suggest that more behavioral analysis (contexts of divergence) would elevate the paper’s contribution.  
- Fix minor language errors listed (several line-specific typos) and standardize capitalization in references (e.g., Japanese, LSTM, CoNLL, ILP).
