- Task: Adapts English SRL model (Zhou & Xu 2015) to Japanese and proposes two new prediction models.  
- Main contribution: Extends single-predicate model to a multi-predicate framework using Grid-RNNs to capture inter-predicate argument interactions.  
- Results: Achieves new state-of-the-art on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark, significantly outperforming prior systems.  
- Incremental gain: The multi-sequential (Grid-RNN) model yields a further, but modest, improvement over a strong single-sequential baseline.  
- Targeted improvement: The Grid-RNN’s gain is concentrated on detecting zero arguments, a difficult and important aspect of Japanese SRL.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and easy to follow.  
- Novelty concern: The performance gain from modeling predicate interactions is limited and smaller than gains reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Major omission: No discussion or comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which also jointly models multiple predicates with neural networks.  
- Request to authors: Clarify the relationship and relative advantages/disadvantages between the proposed Grid-RNN model and prior multi-predicate approaches (Shibata et al. 2016 and Ouchi et al. 2015).
