- Proposes a joint neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs, compared to a single-sequence RNN baseline, achieving state-of-the-art results and presented accessibly.

- Paper should emphasize task complexity more, e.g., walk through Figure 1’s ambiguity where the NOM of the second predicate could be the NOM or ACC of the first.

- Add a clearer contrast between Japanese PAS and standard SRL, especially the challenges introduced by zero pronouns.

- Provide statistics on how many zero pronouns are resolvable within a sentence to clarify the intra- vs inter-sentential zero-anaphora challenge.

- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (explicit empty-argument markers vs. no labeled argument); current lack of explicit signal may hinder learning.

- The model does not explicitly enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., one core argument per predicate, one role per phrase) as ILP-based methods do; analyze whether it learns these constraints implicitly.

- Deepen analysis: Table 3 shows Multi-Seq gains mainly on NOM — include qualitative/error analysis comparing model outputs, types of divergent errors, and specific linguistic contexts.

- Paper is well-presented with helpful figures but contains several minor grammatical/style errors and reference-capitalization issues that should be corrected.
