### Strengths

This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of identifying predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. Notably, the proposed approach does not rely on any explicit syntactic structure, yet it outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do.

The authors provide a clear and detailed description of their implementation and results. A particular strength is the paper's focus on zero pronouns (dropped arguments), a phenomenon that is prevalent in Japanese and poses a significant challenge for PAS analysis. The proposed multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates in a sentence jointly, achieves the best performance on these difficult cases. Overall, the paper is thorough and clearly written.

### Weaknesses

My comments are minor and are intended as suggestions for improving the paper's clarity.

First, it would be helpful to further illustrate the concept of the PRED input, specifically how it can include context surrounding the predicate verb. Using the example from Figure 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string, would be an effective way to demonstrate this point.

Second, the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7) is not explained. The authors should clarify what this formatting signifies.
