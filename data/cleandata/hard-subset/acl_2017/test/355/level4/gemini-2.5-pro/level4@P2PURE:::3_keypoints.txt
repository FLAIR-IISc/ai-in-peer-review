- Proposes a joint neural Grid-RNN model for Japanese Predicate-Argument Structure (PAS) analysis, compared to a single-sequence RNN baseline.  
- Well-executed study achieving state-of-the-art results with largely accessible presentation.  
- Paper should emphasize task complexity, e.g., ambiguity where NOM/ACC of one predicate can be NOM for another (Figure 1 example).  
- Recommend an explicit comparison with standard Semantic Role Labeling (SRL), highlighting challenges from zero-pronoun ambiguity.  
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable intra-sententially to contextualize difficulty.  
- Clarify representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns for a predicate’s argument (left unmarked vs explicitly marked) and discuss implications.  
- Model does not explicitly enforce common linguistic constraints (e.g., one NOM/ACC per predicate, one role per argument); analyze whether these constraints are learned implicitly.  
- Table 3 shows mixed gains (clear improvement mainly for NOM); request qualitative analysis of where Single-Seq and Multi-Seq outputs differ and in what contexts.  
- Overall presentation is clear but contains several minor typos/formatting issues (specific line corrections and reference capitalization should be fixed).
