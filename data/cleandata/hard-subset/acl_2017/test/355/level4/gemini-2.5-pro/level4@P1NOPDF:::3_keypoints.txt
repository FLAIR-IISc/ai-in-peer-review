- Proposes a joint neural Grid-RNN model for Japanese PAS analysis and compares it to a conventional single-sequence RNN.

- Solidly executed work achieving state-of-the-art results and accessible presentation for non-Japanese specialists.

- Paper should better emphasize task complexity, e.g., use Figure 1 to illustrate ambiguity where arguments of one predicate are potential arguments of another.

- Needs a clearer contrast between Japanese PAS analysis and standard SRL, especially regarding zero pronouns.

- Request statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable within a sentence to quantify intra- vs inter-sentential anaphora difficulty.

- Unclear how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (left unmarked vs explicitly marked); discussion of this design choice is needed.

- Model does not explicitly enforce linguistic constraints (one argument per predicate type; phrase fills one slot); analyze whether the model learns these implicitly.

- Mixed results between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq (clear improvement mainly for NOM) merit qualitative analysis of output differences and contexts of divergence.

- A more direct comparison with SRL models would strengthen the paper but would require substantial additional work.

- Presentation and figures are helpful, but there are minor English errors, typos, and inconsistent capitalization in references that should be corrected.
