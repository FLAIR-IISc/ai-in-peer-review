- Proposes a joint neural model for Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis using Grid-RNNs and reports state-of-the-art results versus a single-sequence RNN baseline.  
- Well-executed paper with clear presentation accessible to non-specialists in Japanese linguistics.  
- Needs more explicit discussion of task complexities, e.g., a step-by-step walkthrough of the ambiguity in Figure 1 where arguments can serve multiple predicates.  
- Should better articulate how Japanese PAS differs from standard SRL, especially the prevalence and ambiguity of zero pronouns.  
- Recommend providing statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable intra-sententially to quantify task difficulty.  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (omitted argument slots vs. explicit empty-argument markers) and discuss implications for learning.  
- Request deeper analysis of whether the model implicitly learns linguistic constraints (e.g., one NOM/ACC per predicate, single-role-per-argument) that traditional methods enforce explicitly.  
- Mixed results in Table 3—Multi-Seq mainly improves NOM—require more detailed discussion and targeted analysis.  
- Ask for qualitative comparison of Single-Seq vs. Multi-Seq outputs to identify specific contexts where each model differs and why.  
- Paper would be substantially strengthened by analyses beyond aggregate metrics to show practical and linguistic impacts of joint modeling.  
- Minor grammatical/typographical fixes needed (list of example corrections provided).  
- Ensure consistent and correct capitalization in the bibliography (e.g., "Japanese", "LSTM", "CoNLL", "ILP").
