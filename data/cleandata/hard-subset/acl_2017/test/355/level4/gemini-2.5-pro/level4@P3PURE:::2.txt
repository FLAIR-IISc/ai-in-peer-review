This paper proposes new models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task by adapting the state-of-the-art English SRL model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this model using Grid-RNNs to handle interactions between the arguments of multiple predicates. The models are evaluated on a well-known benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL and achieve significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.
*   The proposed model achieves a significant improvement in accuracy over the current state-of-the-art system.
*   Additionally, the model incorporating Grid-RNNs achieves slightly better performance than the proposed single-sequence model. This improvement is primarily attributed to better detection of zero arguments, which is a key focus of the paper.

**Weaknesses:**
To the best of my understanding, the paper's main contribution is the extension of a single-sequence model to a multi-sequence model using Grid-RNNs. However, the performance gain from modeling predicate interactions is smaller than that reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).

Furthermore, there is a relevant prior work by Shibata et al. (2016) that extended the model of Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural network modeling. A comparison between the proposed Grid-RNN model and the work of Shibata et al. (2016) is missing but would be highly informative for contextualizing the contributions of this paper.
