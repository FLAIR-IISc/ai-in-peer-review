- Summary: Proposes syntax-free Japanese PAS analysis using a single-sequence Bi-RNN baseline and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN to model interactions among predicates, achieving better results on the NAIST Text Corpus.  
- Contribution 1: Novel application of Grid-RNNs to jointly model multi-predicate interactions directly from word sequences, avoiding dependency on syntactic parsers.  
- Contribution 2: Establishes new state-of-the-art performance on NAIST, with notable gains on identifying zero (dropped) arguments.  
- Strength 1: Demonstrates that learning from word sequences with a sophisticated neural architecture can avoid parser error propagation and outperform syntax-based systems.  
- Strength 2: Achieves significant empirical improvements, especially a >3-point F-measure gain on zero-argument identification versus prior work.  
- Strength 3: Paper is clear and thorough, with useful analyses (network depth, residual connections, per-case breakdown) that illuminate model behavior.  
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues—motivation for PRED context window and meaning of boldface in Table 2 are under-explained.  
- Question 1: Please provide an example or justification for including a window of words around the target predicate in the PRED feature (e.g., capturing adjacent case markers or auxiliaries).  
- Question 2: Clarify the meaning of the boldfaced numbers in Table 2.  
- Additional comments: Several minor typos and grammatical issues are listed for correction (e.g., "suffers from the error propagation" → "suffers from error propagation", "She ate a bread" → "She ate bread", and other small fixes).
