- Strengths:
This work details an advanced application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks for identifying predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. Notably, the proposed method achieves superior performance compared to current state-of-the-art systems without relying on the explicit syntactic structures that those systems typically require. The authors provide a thorough and lucid explanation of their implementation and experimental outcomes. A key contribution is the careful analysis of performance on zero pronouns (dropped arguments), a frequent and particularly difficult phenomenon for Japanese PAS analysis. The proposed multi-sequence model, which jointly considers all predicates within a sentence, demonstrates the highest efficacy on these challenging cases. Overall, the paper is well-written and comprehensive.

- Weaknesses:
My comments are minor and intended for clarification and improvement. There are several typographical errors listed below; addressing these would enhance the paper's readability. To better illustrate how the PRED representation incorporates contextual information, I suggest the authors explicitly reference the example in Figure 6, which shows the accusative marker being included with the verb in the PRED string. Additionally, the purpose of the boldface formatting in Table 2 (p. 7) was unclear.
