- Paper proposes neural models for Japanese PAS: a single-sequence Bi-RNN and a novel multi-sequence Grid-RNN to model predicate interactions without syntactic parsers.
- Claims state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, with notable gains on zero-argument identification.
- Contribution: novel multi-sequence Grid-RNN explicitly models interactions among multiple predicates, improving zero-argument resolution.
- Contribution: shows a purely sequence-based, syntax-free approach can surpass prior syntax-dependent joint models.
- Strength: achieves SOTA without syntactic features, outperforming Ouchi et al. (2015) by >2 F-measure and avoiding parser error propagation.
- Strength: Grid-RNN effectively captures multi-predicate interactions, with the largest improvement on zero-argument F1.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation, significance tests, breakdowns by argument/case, and analysis of depth/residual connections.
- Weakness: single-sequence model is mainly an adaptation of Zhou & Xu (2015), offering limited novelty.
- Weakness: multi-sequence adds complexity for a modest overall F1 gain (+0.27%), lacking qualitative cases showing when it’s essential.
- Weakness: omits comparison/discussion of Shibata et al. (2016), a relevant neural baseline that uses syntactic features.
- Question: please clarify differences between your syntax-free approach and Shibata et al. (2016), and why a direct comparison was not included.
- Question: please provide qualitative examples demonstrating cases where multi-predicate modeling (Grid-RNN) was crucial, especially for zero arguments.
