- Adapts the Zhou & Xu (2015) English SRL model to Japanese.
- Introduces Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.
- Evaluated on the standard Japanese SRL benchmark and achieves significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- Grid-RNN gives a slight improvement over the single-sequence model, mainly by better detecting zero arguments.
- Main contribution is the multi-predicate Grid-RNN extension, but its gains are smaller than those reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), making it hard to fully assess the model’s contribution.
