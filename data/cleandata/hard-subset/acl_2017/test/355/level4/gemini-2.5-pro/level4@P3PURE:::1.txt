### Strengths

This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis in Japanese. Notably, the proposed approach does not rely on explicit syntactic features, yet it outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do incorporate such information. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and a thorough analysis of the results.

A key strength is the model's handling of dropped arguments (zero pronouns), a prevalent and challenging aspect of Japanese PAS analysis. The authors demonstrate that their multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates in a sentence simultaneously, achieves the best performance on these specific cases. The paper is well-structured and clearly written.

### Weaknesses

The weaknesses are minor and mainly relate to presentation.

*   To improve clarity, the authors could better illustrate their point that the PRED feature includes contextual information. For example, using the case from Figure 6, where the accusative case marker is part of the PRED string, would be a helpful clarification.
*   The meaning of the boldface formatting in Table 2 (p. 7) is unclear and requires an explanation.
*   The paper contains a number of small grammatical errors and typos, which are listed below for correction.
