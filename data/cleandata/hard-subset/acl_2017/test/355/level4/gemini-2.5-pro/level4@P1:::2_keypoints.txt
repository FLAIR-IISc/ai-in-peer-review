- Paper tackles Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis without syntactic features, proposing a Single-Sequence RNN baseline and a novel Multi-Sequence Grid-RNN to jointly model multiple predicates.  
- Both models outperform prior state-of-the-art on the NAIST Text Corpus, with the Multi-Sequence model yielding the best results, especially for zero-argument identification.  
- Contribution: a new Multi-Sequence Grid-RNN architecture that captures predicate interactions without relying on syntactic dependency information.  
- Contribution: empirical evidence that sequence-only neural models can achieve state-of-the-art PAS performance and avoid parser error propagation.  
- Strength: achieves new state-of-the-art overall F-measure (from 79.23% to 81.42%) without using syntactic information, simplifying the pipeline.  
- Strength: substantially improves zero-argument identification (about a 3-point F-measure gain versus prior work), showing effectiveness on long-distance dependencies.  
- Strength: well-motivated, clearly written, and methodically presented from baseline to novel model.  
- Weakness: Multi-Sequence yields only a marginal improvement over the Single-Sequence baseline (0.27 F), raising questions about the practical benefit relative to added complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to a relevant neural baseline (Shibata et al., 2016), leaving unclear whether gains stem from Grid-RNN design or general RNN advantages and how syntax-free models compare to syntax-aware neural models.
