This paper proposes a new prediction model for Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) by adapting the state-of-the-art English model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this model using a Grid-RNN framework to capture the interactions between arguments of multiple predicates within a sentence. Evaluations conducted on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark dataset show that the proposed model significantly outperforms the current state-of-the-art system.

**Strengths:**

*   The paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
*   The primary proposed model achieves a significant improvement in accuracy compared to the previous state-of-the-art system for Japanese SRL, establishing a new benchmark.
*   The extension using Grid-RNNs to model inter-predicate dependencies achieves a further, albeit slight, performance gain over the proposed single-predicate model. Notably, the authors demonstrate that this improvement is primarily driven by better detection of zero arguments, a key focus of this work.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The primary contribution of this paper appears to be the extension of a strong single-predicate sequence labeling model to a multi-predicate model using Grid-RNNs. However, the performance gain from modeling these predicate interactions appears smaller than that reported in previous work, such as Ouchi et al. (2015).
*   The paper omits a comparison to the recent work of Shibata et al. (2016), which also extends the multi-predicate model of Ouchi et al. (2015) using neural networks. Since Shibata et al. (2016) represents a direct neural enhancement of prior art in Japanese multi-predicate SRL, a comparison with their model would be crucial for contextualizing the contributions of the Grid-RNN approach. The absence of this comparison makes it difficult to fully assess the novelty and impact of the proposed multi-sequential model.
