- Sophisticated application of Grid RNNs to Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification.  
- Approach does not rely on explicit syntactic structure yet outperforms state-of-the-art syntax-based systems.  
- Implementation and experimental results are clearly and thoroughly described.  
- Careful analysis of zero pronouns (dropped arguments), a challenging aspect of Japanese PAS.  
- Multi-sequence model that accounts for all predicates simultaneously achieves the best performance on zero pronouns.  
- Paper is detailed and clearly written.

- Minor presentation issue: meaning of boldface formatting in Table 2 is unclear and should be explained.
