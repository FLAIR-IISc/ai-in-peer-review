**Strengths:**

This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of identifying predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. Notably, the proposed approach does not rely on explicit syntactic structure, yet it outperforms state-of-the-art systems that do. The authors provide a clear and thorough description of their implementation and results.

A particular strength is the careful analysis of performance on zero pronouns (dropped arguments), a prevalent and challenging aspect of Japanese PAS analysis. The paper shows that its multi-sequence model, which accounts for all predicates in a sentence simultaneously, achieves the best performance on these cases. The paper is detailed and clearly written.

**Weaknesses:**

The weaknesses are minor and primarily relate to presentation.
*   To better illustrate the point that the PRED string includes contextual information, it would be helpful to explicitly reference the example in Figure 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb.
*   The meaning of the boldface formatting in Table 2 (p. 7) is unclear.
