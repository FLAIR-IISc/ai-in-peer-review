This paper proposes new models for Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) by adapting the state-of-the-art English model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors extend this model with a Grid-RNN framework to capture interactions between the arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluated on a standard benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL, the proposed model achieves significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.
*   The proposed model obtains a notable improvement in accuracy over the current state-of-the-art system.
*   Furthermore, the model using Grid-RNNs achieves a slightly better performance than the proposed single-sequence model. This gain is primarily attributed to the improved detection of zero arguments, which is a key focus of this work.

**Weakness:**
*   The paper's main contribution appears to be the extension of the single-sequence model to a multi-sequence model. However, the performance gain from modeling predicate interactions seems more modest than that reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
*   A previous work by Shibata et al. (2016) extended the model of Ouchi et al. (2015) using neural networks. The paper would be strengthened by a comparison to this related work.
