- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model that jointly models interactions between all predicates in a sentence for Japanese PAS analysis.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus without using explicit syntactic features, avoiding parser error propagation.
- Compares a single-sequence (per-predicate) RNN baseline with the multi-sequence Grid-RNN and shows the latter yields significant gains.
- Grid-RNN shows particular strength on the challenging zero-argument identification task (+3.0 F1 over previous SOTA).
- The application of Grid-RNNs for multi-predicate joint modeling is novel and well-motivated.
- Experiments are thorough, clearly described, and include detailed result breakdowns by argument type and case role.
- Minor clarity issue: Table 2’s use of boldface is unexplained and should be clarified in the caption.
- Minor motivation issue: the PRED feature’s linguistic motivation (e.g., capturing nearby case-marking particles) should be explained more explicitly and illustrated.
- Several minor grammatical errors and typos are listed that should be corrected for the final version.
