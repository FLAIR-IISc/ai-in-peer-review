Proposes a syntax-free neural approach for Japanese PAS using a single-sequence BiRNN and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN.  
Multi-sequence Grid-RNN jointly models interactions among all predicates, learning semantic relations from raw word sequences without syntactic input.  
Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, with notable 2–3 F-point gains on zero-argument identification.  
Outperforms prior syntax-based SOTA (Ouchi et al., 2015), reducing vulnerability to parser error propagation.  
Multi-sequence model outperforms single-sequence on zero-argument resolution, supporting the importance of multi-predicate interactions.  
Paper is clearly written with well-motivated model exposition, helpful diagrams, and a reproducible experimental setup.  
Empirical analysis is thorough, including investigations of network depth and residual connections.  
Paper lacks detailed qualitative/error analysis; needs concrete examples where multi-sequence succeeds and single-sequence fails.  
Minor presentation clarifications needed (e.g., explain bold numbers in Table 2; better illustrate the PRED feature using Figure 4).  
Contains several minor typographical and grammatical errors that should be corrected for improved fluency.
