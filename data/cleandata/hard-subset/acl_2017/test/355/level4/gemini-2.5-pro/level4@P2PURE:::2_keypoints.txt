- Proposes new Japanese SRL models by adapting Zhou & Xu (2015).
- Extends the model with a Grid-RNN to capture interactions among multiple predicates.
- Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark and achieves significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.
- The proposed model yields a notable accuracy improvement over prior state-of-the-art.
- Grid-RNN variant gives a slight additional gain, mainly from improved detection of zero arguments.
- Main contribution is extending a single-sequence model to a multi-sequence model; gains from modeling predicate interactions are more modest than reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Lacks a comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which extended Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural networks.
