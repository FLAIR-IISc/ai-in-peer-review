- Well-structured, clearly motivated paper that is easy to follow.  
- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) to Japanese SRL and sets a new state-of-the-art with significant accuracy improvement.  
- Extends the model with Grid-RNNs to capture inter-predicate interactions, yielding a further (small) performance gain.  
- Grid-RNN improvement is mainly driven by better detection of zero arguments.  
- The multi-predicate interaction gain is smaller than gains reported in prior work (e.g., Ouchi et al., 2015).  
- Lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), making it hard to assess novelty and impact of the Grid-RNN multi-predicate approach.
