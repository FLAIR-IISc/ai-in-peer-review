- Paper adapts Zhou and Xu (2015) to Japanese SRL and evaluates on a standard benchmark, achieving significantly better performance than the prior state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.  
- Grid-RNN extension yields slight additional gains over the single-sequence model, mainly by improving detection of zero arguments.  
- Main contribution is extending a single-sequence model to a multi-sequence Grid-RNN, with modest improvements from modeling predicate interactions.  
- The performance gain from predicate-interaction modeling is smaller than reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Missing comparison with relevant prior work by Shibata et al. (2016), which would help contextualize contributions.
