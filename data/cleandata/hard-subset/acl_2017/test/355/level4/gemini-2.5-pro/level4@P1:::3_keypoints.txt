- Proposes a Grid-RNN model that jointly models multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, avoiding external syntactic features and achieving SOTA on the NAIST corpus (notably for zero arguments).
- Contribution: novel application of Grid-RNNs to learn multi-predicate semantic relationships directly from word sequences without syntax.
- Contribution: establishes a new state-of-the-art on NAIST, outperforming previous syntax-based joint models.
- Contribution: empirically demonstrates the value of joint modeling—multi-sequence Grid-RNN significantly outperforms a strong single-sequence RNN baseline, especially for zero argument ID.
- Strength: strong empirical results (+2.19 F1 overall improvement; +3.03 F1 on zero arguments), setting new benchmark performance.
- Strength: effective end-to-end, syntax-free modeling that avoids parser error propagation.
- Strength: well-motivated and elegant Grid-RNN architecture that captures inter-predicate dependencies, supported by clear gains over the baseline.
- Strength: clear presentation with helpful figures explaining model architectures and information flow.
- Weakness: lacks in-depth qualitative/error analysis to explain where and why the joint model helps or fails relative to the independent model.
- Weakness: does not analyze whether common linguistic constraints (e.g., one core argument per role) are learned or violated by the model.
- Weakness: understates task complexity for a general NLP audience; could better motivate Japanese PAS challenges (e.g., zero anaphora) and compare to English SRL.
- Question: how are inter-sentential zero pronouns represented in training data and outputs—are out-of-sentence arguments simply labeled null, and what are the implications?
- Question: please provide corpus statistics on intra-sentential vs. inter-sentential zero pronoun prevalence to contextualize the task scope.
- Additional comment: minor typos and grammatical/capitalization issues noted throughout (specific line corrections and inconsistent reference capitalization).
