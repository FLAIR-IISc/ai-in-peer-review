- Task and result: Proposes syntax-free neural models (single-sequence Bi-RNN and multi-sequence Grid-RNN) for Japanese PAS, achieving state-of-the-art on the NAIST benchmark and beating prior work.

- Contribution 1: Demonstrates a purely sequence-based model can outperform syntax-dependent systems for Japanese PAS, reducing reliance on external parsers.

- Contribution 2: Introduces a Grid-RNN multi-sequence architecture to jointly model interactions among multiple predicates, improving zero-argument identification.

- Strength 1: Achieves new state-of-the-art F1 without syntactic features, with the single-sequence model giving a large gain over prior work.

- Strength 2: Effectively models multi-predicate interactions via Grid-RNNs, yielding statistically significant improvements, especially on zero arguments.

- Strength 3: Paper is clear, well-motivated, includes thorough analysis (ablation, per-role breakdown), and releases code for reproducibility.

- Weakness 1: Absolute performance gain of the Grid-RNN over the simpler single-sequence model is small (≈0.27 F1), questioning the practical impact of the complex grid architecture.

- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison/discussion with Shibata et al. (2016), a relevant neural baseline that uses syntactic features, leaving literature placement incomplete.

- Question 1: Request for detailed analysis of specific sentence types or phenomena where Grid-RNN yields substantial benefits versus where it does not.

- Question 2: Request for clarification of differences and advantages relative to Shibata et al. (2016)’s neural, syntax-dependent approach.
