This paper demonstrates a compelling application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to predicate-argument structure (PAS) identification in Japanese. Notably, the proposed approach achieves superior performance compared to existing state-of-the-art systems, despite forgoing the use of explicit syntactic information. The authors provide a thorough and well-documented description of both the implementation details and the experimental results. A particular strength lies in their careful consideration of, and strong performance on, the challenging issue of dropped arguments and zero pronouns – phenomena common in Japanese that significantly impact PAS determination. The multi-sequence model, leveraging information from all predicates within a sentence, proves most effective in handling these cases. Overall, the paper is detailed and clearly presented.

Regarding weaknesses, I have only minor suggestions for improvement. Correcting a few typographical errors (detailed below) would enhance the clarity and fluency of the text. To further illustrate the point regarding the predicate representation (PRED) incorporating contextual information, referencing the example in Figure 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string, would be beneficial. Finally, the purpose of the boldface formatting in Table 2 on page 7 was unclear to me.
