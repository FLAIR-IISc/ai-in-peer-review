This paper introduces novel prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task, building upon the strong performance of the English SRL model from Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further enhance this foundation by integrating a Grid-RNN framework to effectively model interactions between arguments associated with multiple predicates. Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark demonstrates a substantial performance gain over existing state-of-the-art systems.

**Strengths:**

The paper is clearly written, logically organized, and provides strong motivation for the work. The proposed model achieves a notable improvement in accuracy compared to current state-of-the-art results. Furthermore, the Grid-RNN enhanced model demonstrates a modest but consistent performance advantage over the single-sequential model, primarily driven by improved detection of zero arguments – a key focus of this research.

**Weakness:**

The primary contribution of this work, as I see it, lies in extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential approach. While the paper addresses predicate interactions, the impact appears somewhat limited compared to that reported in Ouchi et al. (2015). Notably, Shibata et al. (2016) previously extended the Ouchi et al. (2015) model using neural network techniques. A comparative analysis against this prior work would be of significant interest.
