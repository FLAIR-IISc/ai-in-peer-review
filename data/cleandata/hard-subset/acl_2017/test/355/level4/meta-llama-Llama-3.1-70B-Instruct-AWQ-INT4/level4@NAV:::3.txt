This paper introduces a novel joint neural modeling approach for PAS analysis in Japanese, leveraging Grid-RNNs and comparing its performance to a traditional single-sequence RNN method.

The paper is well-executed, addressing a well-established task in Japanese and achieving state-of-the-art results, while presenting the task in an accessible manner for non-experts in Japanese. However, I believe the authors could have further emphasized the complexity of the task, such as discussing the inherent ambiguity between NOM and ACC arguments in Figure 1 and better contrasting PAS analysis with Semantic Role Labeling (SRL), particularly regarding the challenges posed by zero pronouns. Additionally, statistics on the proportion of zero pronouns that are intra-sententially resolvable would have been valuable, as this further complicates the task by requiring the implicit distinction between intra- and inter-sentential zero anaphors.

I was unclear about the representation used for inter-sentential zero pronouns as arguments of a given predicate. My interpretation is that no explicit marking is used, which may result in a defective representation that affects the model's ability to capture zero pronouns. A discussion on this aspect would have been appreciated.

Certain constraints, explicitly modeled by some ILP-based SRL methods, seem to be overlooked in the proposed model, including the fact that a predicate typically has only one argument of a given type and that an argument usually fills only one argument slot for a given predicate. I would have liked to see an analysis of the model's output to assess its ability to learn these constraints. Furthermore, given the mixed results in Table 3 between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq models, a discussion on the relative differences in their outputs would have been valuable, including the contexts in which differences are observed.

The paper's presentation is clear, with helpful figures that facilitate understanding of the model. However, there are some minor language issues: l19: "the error propagation" should be "error propagation"; l190: "an solution" should be "a solution"; l264 (and Figure 2): "a bread" should be "bread"; l351: "the independence" should be "independence"; l512: "the good" should be "good"; l531: "from their model" should be "of their model"; l637: "significent" should be "significance"; l638: "both of" should be "both". Additionally, the authors should ensure consistent casing in their references (e.g., "Japanese", "LSTM", "CoNLL", "ILP").
