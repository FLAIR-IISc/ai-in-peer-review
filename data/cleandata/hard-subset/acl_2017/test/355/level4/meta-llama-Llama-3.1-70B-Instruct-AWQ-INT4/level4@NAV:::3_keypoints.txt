- Introduces a novel joint neural approach for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and compares it to a traditional single-sequence RNN baseline.
- Well-executed study that achieves state-of-the-art results and presents the task accessibly for non-experts.
- Paper should better emphasize task complexity (e.g., NOM vs ACC ambiguity in Fig.1) and contrast PAS with SRL, especially regarding zero-pronoun challenges.
- Missing statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable, which affects difficulty and required modeling distinctions.
- Unclear how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented; if unmarked, this could produce a defective representation and harm zero-pronoun modeling.
- The model appears to ignore constraints explicitly enforced by ILP-based SRL methods (one argument per type per predicate; arguments filling only one slot); no analysis provided to show whether the model learns these constraints.
- Mixed results between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq (Table 3) need a discussion of output differences and the contexts in which each model performs differently.
- Presentation is clear with helpful figures, but there are several minor language errors and inconsistent reference casing that should be corrected.
