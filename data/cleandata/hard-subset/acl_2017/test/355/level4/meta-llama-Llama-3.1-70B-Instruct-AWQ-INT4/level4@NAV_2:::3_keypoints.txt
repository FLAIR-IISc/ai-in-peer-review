- Proposes a joint neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions, compared with single-sequence RNNs.
- Model induces features from word sequences and is evaluated on the NAIST Text Corpus.
- Claims state-of-the-art performance without using syntactic information.
- Demonstrates effectiveness for zero argument identification.
- Novelty: uses Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions.
- Empirical strengths: detailed analysis, baseline comparisons, and clear writing.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of how outputs capture constraints like predicate-argument uniqueness.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of how single-sequence and multi-sequence model outputs differ.
- Weakness: does not report experiments on semantic role labeling to show broader applicability.
- Weakness: some minor language/editing issues throughout the paper.
- Question: Please provide more analysis on capturing uniqueness constraints for arguments.
- Question: How do outputs differ between single- and multi-sequence models and in what contexts?
- Question: Have you tried applying the model to SRL, and what were the results?
- Question: How are inter-sentential zero pronouns represented and how do they affect performance?
