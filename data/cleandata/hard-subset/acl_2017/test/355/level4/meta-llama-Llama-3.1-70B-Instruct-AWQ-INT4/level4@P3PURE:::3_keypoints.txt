- Proposes a joint neural approach to Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs, compared against a single-sequence RNN baseline.  
- Solidly executed work achieving state-of-the-art results on a well-established Japanese PAS task.  
- Paper is mostly accessible to non-experts, but under-emphasizes the intrinsic complexity of the task.  
- Figure 1 could be used to illustrate ambiguity between NOM and ACC arguments and NOM ambiguity across predicates.  
- Needs clearer contrast with Semantic Role Labeling (SRL), especially regarding ambiguity from zero pronouns.  
- Request for statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable (vs. inter-sentential).  
- Unclear how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented—marked as empty or ignored—which may hurt modeling of zeros.  
- Model appears not to explicitly enforce constraints ILP-based SRL methods capture (e.g., only one NOM/ACC per predicate, one argument fills one slot).  
- Would like analysis of model outputs to see whether those structural constraints are learned or violated.  
- Table 3 shows mixed results between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq (only clear gain for NOM); need analysis of differences in their predictions and contexts where they diverge.  
- Including SRL results would be useful but acknowledged as requiring substantial extra work.  
- Presentation and figures are helpful, but there are multiple minor language errors and inconsistent reference casing that should be corrected.
