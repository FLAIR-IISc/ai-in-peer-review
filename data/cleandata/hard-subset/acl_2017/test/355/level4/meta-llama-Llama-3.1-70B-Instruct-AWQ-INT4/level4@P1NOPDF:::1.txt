- Strengths:
This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Nets to the task of determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach is notable for not relying on explicit syntactic structure, yet outperforming current state-of-the-art (SOA) systems that do incorporate syntactic structure. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and results. They pay particularly close attention to performance on dropped arguments, specifically zero pronouns, which are prevalent in Japanese and pose a significant challenge for PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which takes all predicates in the sentence into account, achieves the best performance for these examples. The paper is well-written and detailed.
