- Strengths:
This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach is notable for not relying on explicit syntactic structure, yet it outperforms current state-of-the-art (SOA) systems that do incorporate syntactic structure. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and results. Notably, they pay close attention to performance on dropped arguments and zero pronouns, which are prevalent in Japanese and particularly challenging with respect to PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates in a sentence, achieves the best performance on these examples. The paper is well-written and detailed.

- Weaknesses:
I have only minor comments. There are some typos listed below, the correction of which would improve the English fluency. It would be beneficial to illustrate the point about the PRED including context around the "predicate" with the example from Figure 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. Additionally, I found the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7) unclear.
