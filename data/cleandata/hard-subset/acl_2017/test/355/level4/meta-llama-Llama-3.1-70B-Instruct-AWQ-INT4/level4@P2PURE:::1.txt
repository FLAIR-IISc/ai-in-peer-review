- Strengths:
This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Nets to the task of determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach is notable for not relying on explicit syntactic structure, yet it outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that incorporate syntactic structure. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and results, with particular attention to performance on dropped arguments and zero pronouns, which are prevalent and challenging in Japanese PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates in a sentence, achieves the best performance on these examples. The paper is well-written and detailed.

- Weaknesses:
My comments are minor. There are several typos (listed below) that, when corrected, would improve the paper's English fluency. It would be beneficial to illustrate the inclusion of context around the "predicate" in the PRED representation using the example from Fig 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. I found the use of boldface in Table 2 (p. 7) unclear.
