- Proposes Japanese SRL models adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) and incorporating Grid-RNN to capture inter-argument interactions across predicates.
- Evaluated on a well-known Japanese SRL benchmark, showing significant improvement over the current state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.
- Proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the prior state-of-the-art system.
- Grid-RNN variant slightly outperforms the single-sequential model, mainly by better detecting zero arguments.
- Main contribution framed as extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model, but predicate-interaction gains seem smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Reviewer requests comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which also extended Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural modeling, to better contextualize the work.
