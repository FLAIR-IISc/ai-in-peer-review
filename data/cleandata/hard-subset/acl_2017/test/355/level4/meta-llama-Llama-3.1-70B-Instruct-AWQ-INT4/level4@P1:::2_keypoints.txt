- Proposes two neural models for Japanese PAS: a single-sequence model and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN model adapted from Zhou & Xu (2015).
- Grid-RNN is introduced to capture interactions among multiple predicates without relying on syntactic features.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, notably improving zero argument identification.
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- Grid-RNN yields slightly better performance than the single-sequence model, mainly on zero argument detection.
- The impact of modeling predicate interactions is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Prior work (Shibata et al., 2016) also uses neural modeling to extend Ouchi et al.; comparison with it is missing.
- Reviewer requests more implementation details on how Grid-RNNs capture multi-predicate interactions.
- Reviewer asks about plans to leverage external resources (large-scale unlabeled data) to improve performance.
- Reviewer requests a detailed comparison between the proposed multi-sequence model and Shibata et al. (2016).
