- Strengths:
This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks to the task of determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach is notable for not relying on explicit syntactic structure, and it outperforms current state-of-the-art (SOA) systems that do incorporate syntactic structure. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and results, with particular attention to performance on dropped arguments and zero pronouns, which are prevalent in Japanese and especially challenging for PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which considers all predicates in a sentence, achieves the best performance on these examples. The paper is well-written and detailed.
