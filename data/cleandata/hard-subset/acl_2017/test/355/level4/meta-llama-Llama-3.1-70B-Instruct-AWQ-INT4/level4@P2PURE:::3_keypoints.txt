- Proposes a joint neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs and compares it to a single-sequence RNN baseline, achieving state-of-the-art results.  
- Paper is well-executed and accessible to non-experts in Japanese.  
- Authors should emphasize task complexity, e.g., ambiguity between NOM and ACC arguments across predicates (Figure 1).  
- Need clearer contrast with SRL, particularly regarding zero-pronoun ambiguity.  
- Provide statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are resolvable intra-sententially versus inter-sentential.  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (marked as empty vs. omitted) and discuss implications for model performance.  
- Model appears not to explicitly enforce constraints ILP-based SRL methods capture (one argument per type per predicate; an argument fills only one slot); analyze whether the model learns these constraints.  
- Mixed results in Table 3 (Multi-Seq gains mainly for NOM) warrant analysis of differences between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq outputs and contexts where they diverge.  
- Presentation and figures are effective and helpful.  
- Several minor language errors need correction (list of specific line-level fixes provided).  
- Ensure consistent casing and formatting in references (e.g., "Japanese", "LSTM", "CoNLL", "ILP").
