- Proposes two neural models for Japanese PAS: a stacked BiRNN single-sequence model and a Grid-RNN multi-sequence model for multi-predicate interaction.
- Claims Grid-RNN model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus without using syntactic information.
- Main contribution: introducing Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions in Japanese PAS.
- Main contribution: achieving improved accuracy, especially for zero argument identification, on NAIST.
- Main contribution: demonstrating Grid-RNNs’ ability to capture long-distance dependencies.
- Strength: Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and explains the approach thoroughly.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation on NAIST showing significant performance gains over prior state-of-the-art.
- Strength: Novel use of Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions and improve zero argument detection.
- Weakness: Impact of predicate interactions isn’t clearly better than Ouchi et al. (2015), which also models interactions.
- Weakness: Lacks comparison with related neural extension by Shibata et al. (2016).
- Weakness: No detailed error analysis to identify limitations or common failure modes.
- Question: Provide a detailed comparison/evaluation against Shibata et al. (2016) on the same dataset.
- Question: How will the model be extended to handle inter-sentential (cross-sentence) arguments?
- Question: Provide a detailed error analysis showing common error types and contributing factors.
