- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) English SRL model for Japanese SRL.  
- Extends the model with a Grid-RNN to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significant improvement over the current state of the art.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivated.  
- Grid-RNN achieves slightly better performance than the single-sequential model, mainly by improving detection of zero arguments.  
- Main contribution appears to be extending single-sequential to multi-sequential modeling.  
- Effect of predicate interactions is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Missing comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which extended Ouchi et al. with neural modeling, to better contextualize results.
