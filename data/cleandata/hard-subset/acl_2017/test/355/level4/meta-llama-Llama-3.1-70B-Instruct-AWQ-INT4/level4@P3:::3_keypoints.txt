- Proposes a joint neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions, achieving state-of-the-art on the NAIST corpus.  
- Well-executed and accessible presentation, but should emphasize task complexity (e.g., NOM/ACC ambiguity in Figure 1).  
- Needs clearer contrast between PAS analysis and SRL, especially regarding zero pronouns.  
- Should report statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable.  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (unmarked vs. empty argument) and its modeling impact.  
- Model appears not to enforce constraints like unique argument per type or single-slot filling; authors should analyze whether the model learns these.  
- Table 3 shows mixed Single-Seq vs Multi-Seq results with substantial gains mainly for NOM; authors should discuss relative differences and overlap of outputs.  
- Recommend analyzing contexts where the two models differ to illuminate model behavior and strengths.  
- Presentation is generally strong with helpful figures, but contains minor language errors and inconsistent reference casing.  
- Main contributions summarized: Grid-RNN joint model for PAS, SOTA results on NAIST, and improved handling of long-distance dependencies and zero pronouns.  
- Strengths: innovative approach, thorough evaluation, clear organization and figures.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient emphasis on task difficulty, lack of analysis on constraint learning and zero-pronoun representation, and need for deeper output analysis.  
- Questions for authors: provide more PAS vs SRL context; explain inter-sentential zero-pronoun representation; analyze model outputs for constraint learning (argument uniqueness/slot filling).
