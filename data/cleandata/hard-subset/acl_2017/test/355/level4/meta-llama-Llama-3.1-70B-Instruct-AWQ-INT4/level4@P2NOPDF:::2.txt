This paper proposes a new prediction model for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task by adapting the state-of-the-art English model of Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further extend this model by incorporating the Grid-RNN framework to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluation on a well-established Japanese SRL benchmark dataset yields significantly improved performance over the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and well-motivated, presenting a clear and logical progression of ideas. The proposed model demonstrates improved accuracy compared to the current state-of-the-art system. Furthermore, the Grid-RNN model achieves slightly better performance than the single-sequential model, primarily due to enhanced detection of zero arguments, which is the primary focus of this paper.

Weakness:
In my understanding, the main contribution of this paper lies in extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model. However, the impact of predicate interactions appears to be relatively smaller compared to Ouchi et al. (2015). Notably, Shibata et al. (2016) have previously extended Ouchi et al.'s model using neural network modeling, and I would appreciate a comparison between these approaches to better contextualize the current work.
