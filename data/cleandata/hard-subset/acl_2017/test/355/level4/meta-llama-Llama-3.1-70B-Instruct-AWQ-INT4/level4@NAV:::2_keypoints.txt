- Paper adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) English SRL model for Japanese and proposes novel prediction models.  
- Authors incorporate a Grid-RNN framework to model interactions between multiple predicate arguments.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows substantial improvement over the current state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is clearly structured with a well-articulated motivation.  
- Proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the prior state-of-the-art.  
- Grid-RNN variant marginally outperforms the single-sequential model, mainly via better zero-argument detection.  
- Main contribution appears to be extending single-sequential to multi-sequential modeling.  
- Impact of predicate interactions seems limited relative to Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which also extended Ouchi et al. (2015) with neural modeling.
