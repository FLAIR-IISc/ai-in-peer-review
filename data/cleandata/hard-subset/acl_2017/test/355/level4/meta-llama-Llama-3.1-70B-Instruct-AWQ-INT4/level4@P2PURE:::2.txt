This paper proposes new prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task by adapting the state-of-the-art English model of (Zhou and Xu, 2015). The authors extend this model by incorporating the Grid-RNN framework to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates. Evaluation on a well-known Japanese SRL benchmark dataset yields significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and well-motivated, clearly establishing the need for improved Japanese SRL models. The proposed model achieves an improvement in accuracy over the current state-of-the-art system. Furthermore, the Grid-RNN-based model slightly outperforms the single-sequential model, primarily due to enhanced detection of zero arguments, a key focus of this paper.

Weakness:
In my understanding, the main contribution of this paper lies in extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model. However, the impact of predicate interactions appears to be relatively smaller compared to (Ouchi et al., 2015). Notably, a previous work (Shibata et al., 2016) has also extended (Ouchi et al., 2015)'s model using neural network modeling, and I would appreciate a comparison with this study to better contextualize the current work.
