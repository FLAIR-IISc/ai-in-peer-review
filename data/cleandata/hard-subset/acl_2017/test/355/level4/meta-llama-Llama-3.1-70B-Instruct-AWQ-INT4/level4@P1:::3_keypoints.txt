- Proposes a Grid-RNN joint neural model for Japanese PAS that attains state-of-the-art results without using syntactic information.  
- Compares multi-sequence (Grid-RNN) and single-sequence RNN models, showing multi-predicate interaction modeling improves performance.  
- Evaluates on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, reporting better handling of long-distance dependencies and improved zero-argument identification.  
- Well-executed approach that demonstrates the effectiveness of neural models for Japanese PAS and achieves SOTA.  
- Provides a clear empirical comparison that highlights benefits of modeling multi-predicate interactions.  
- Delivers a thorough evaluation with detailed results and comparisons against baselines.  
- Lacks deeper discussion of task complexity, inherent ambiguity in Japanese PAS, and zero-pronoun resolution challenges.  
- Does not explicitly represent inter-sentential zero pronouns, which may limit the model’s ability to capture them.  
- Omits analysis of model outputs with respect to constraints like argument uniqueness and argument-slot learning.  
- Asks how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented in the model.  
- Asks how the authors plan to address the lack of explicit inter-sentential zero pronoun representation in future work.  
- Requests more analysis of model outputs, specifically whether it learns constraints such as uniqueness of arguments and argument slots.
