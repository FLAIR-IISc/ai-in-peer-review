- Proposes new Japanese SRL prediction models by adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) English SOTA approach.  
- Extends the model with Grid-RNNs to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark and achieves significantly better performance than the current SOTA.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and explains limitations of existing models and need for a new approach.  
- Grid-RNN variant yields slight additional gains over the single-sequential model, especially for detecting zero arguments.  
- Improvement on zero-argument detection is a notable and challenging contribution for Japanese SRL.  
- Main technical contribution is extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential/Grid-RNN model.  
- Impact of modeling predicate interactions appears smaller than reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which also extends Ouchi et al. (2015) using neural networks—needs this comparison to clarify novelty.  
- Overall a valuable contribution with promising results, but requires clearer comparison with related work to strengthen significance.
