This paper introduces novel prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task by adapting the state-of-the-art English model proposed by Zhou and Xu (2015). The authors further enhance their model by incorporating the Grid-RNN framework to capture the interactions between multiple predicate arguments. 

The evaluation, conducted on a widely recognized benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL, demonstrates a substantial performance improvement over the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
This paper exhibits a clear structure and well-articulated motivation.
The proposed model achieves enhanced accuracy compared to the current state-of-the-art system.
Furthermore, the Grid-RNN-based model marginally outperforms the proposed single-sequential model, primarily due to improved detection of zero arguments, which is a primary focus of this study.

Weakness:
From my understanding, the primary contribution of this paper lies in extending the single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model. However, the impact of predicate interactions appears to be somewhat limited compared to the work by Ouchi et al. (2015). Additionally, Shibata et al. (2016) previously extended the Ouchi et al. (2015) model using neural network modeling, and I would appreciate a comparison with their approach.
