This paper proposes new prediction models for the Japanese Semantic Role Labeling (SRL) task by adapting the English state-of-the-art model of (Zhou and Xu, 2015). The authors also extend the model by applying the Grid- Recurrent Neural Networks (Grid-RNNs) framework to handle interactions between the arguments of multiple predicates. The evaluation is performed on the well-known benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL, and the results show a significantly better performance than the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and well-motivated. The proposed model achieves an improvement in accuracy compared to the current state-of-the-art system. Furthermore, the model using Grid-RNNs performs slightly better than the proposed single-sequential model, mainly due to the improvement in detecting zero arguments, which is the primary focus of this paper.

Weakness:
To the best of my understanding, the main contribution of this paper is the extension of the single-sequential model to the multi-sequential model. However, the impact of predicate interactions seems relatively smaller compared to (Ouchi et al., 2015). Additionally, there is a previous work (Shibata et al., 2016) that extends the (Ouchi et al., 2015) model using neural network modeling. I am curious about the comparison between these models, as it would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed approach's effectiveness.
