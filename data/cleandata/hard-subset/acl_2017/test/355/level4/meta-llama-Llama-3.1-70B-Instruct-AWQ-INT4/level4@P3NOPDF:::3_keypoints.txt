- Paper proposes a joint neural approach to Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs, compares to single-sequence RNN, and achieves state-of-the-art results.  
- Presentation is solid and mostly accessible to readers unfamiliar with Japanese; figures effectively aid understanding.  
- Authors should emphasize task complexity, e.g., ambiguity between NOM and ACC arguments and ambiguous NOMs across predicates (Figure 1).  
- Better contrast PAS with Semantic Role Labeling (SRL), especially regarding ambiguity in zero pronouns.  
- Provide statistics on proportion of zero pronouns that are intra-sententially resolvable to show task difficulty (intra- vs inter-sentential zero anaphora).  
- Clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented or marked for a predicate, and discuss impact on model learning.  
- Investigate constraints not captured by the model (e.g., one NOM/ACC per predicate, an argument filling only one slot) and analyze whether the model learns them.  
- Analyze and discuss mixed results between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq (Table 3): compare outputs to see if they are largely identical or different with similar aggregate performance.  
- Identify contexts or cases where the two models differ to provide insight into model behavior.  
- Note minor language errors and suggested corrections (e.g., "the error propagation" → "error propagation", "an solution"→"a solution", "a bread"→"bread", etc.).  
- Ensure consistent casing in references (e.g., "Japanese", "LSTM", "CoNLL", "ILP").
