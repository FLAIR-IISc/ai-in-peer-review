Strengths:
This paper showcases a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Networks in determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) for Japanese, achieving state-of-the-art results without relying on explicit syntactic structure. The authors provide a thorough description of their implementation and results, paying particular attention to performance on dropped arguments and zero pronouns, which are prevalent and challenging in Japanese PAS. Notably, their multi-sequence model, considering all predicates in a sentence, yields the best performance for such examples. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, facilitating comprehension.

Weaknesses:
My comments are minor and primarily focus on refinement. Correcting the listed typos would enhance the paper's English fluency. To further clarify the inclusion of context around the "predicate" in the PRED, incorporating an example from Fig 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string, would be beneficial. Additionally, the use of boldface in Table 2 on page 7 is unclear and warrants explanation.
