- Proposes a neural model for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis that captures multi-predicate interactions from word sequences.  
- Introduces a Grid-RNN to model interactions between all predicates without relying on parser-predicted syntactic information.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming the current Japanese PAS analyzer.  
- Main contribution: effective multi-predicate interaction modeling for Japanese PAS without syntactic features.  
- Main contribution: the Grid-RNN architecture for capturing predicate–predicate interactions.  
- Main contribution: empirical improvement over prior state-of-the-art on a standard corpus.  
- Strength: well-structured, well-motivated presentation of problem and solution.  
- Strength: overall accuracy improvement compared to previous systems.  
- Strength: Grid-RNN particularly improves detection of zero arguments versus a single-sequential model.  
- Weakness: impact of predicate interactions may be smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which also applies neural modeling to Ouchi et al.’s approach.  
- Question: please provide a detailed comparison with Shibata et al. (2016).  
- Question: how will large-scale unlabeled data be exploited to train the neural models as future work?
