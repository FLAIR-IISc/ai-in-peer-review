This paper presents a sophisticated application of Grid-type Recurrent Neural Nets to the task of determining predicate-argument structures (PAS) in Japanese. The approach does not rely on explicit syntactic structure, and outperforms current state-of-the-art systems that do include syntactic structure. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and results. In particular, they pay close attention to the performance on dropped arguments, specifically zero pronouns, which are prevalent in Japanese and especially challenging with respect to PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which takes all predicates in the sentence into account, achieves the best performance for these examples. The paper is detailed and clearly written.

The paper's weaknesses are minor. A few typos were found, which are listed below. Correcting these errors would improve the English fluency of the paper. It would be beneficial to illustrate the point about the PRED including context around the "predicate" using the example from Figure 6, where the accusative marker is included with the verb in the PRED string. Additionally, the use of boldface in Table 2 on page 7 is unclear.

Several minor errors were found in the paper. These include:
