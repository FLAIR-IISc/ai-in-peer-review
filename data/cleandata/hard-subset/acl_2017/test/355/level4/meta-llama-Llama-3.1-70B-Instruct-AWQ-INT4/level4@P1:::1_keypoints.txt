- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model for Japanese PAS that induces features for multi-predicate interactions without syntactic parsers.  
- Presents a basic single-sequence model and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN that models interactions between all predicates in a sentence.  
- Evaluated on the NAIST Text Corpus and reported state-of-the-art performance.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments and clear implementation/details of results.  
- Offers in-depth analysis of performance on zero (dropped) arguments, a key challenge in Japanese.  
- Multi-sequence model yields the best performance, especially for dropped arguments.  
- Advantage: outperforms parser-based systems despite not using explicit syntactic structure.  
- Strength: sophisticated application of Grid-type RNNs to PAS analysis.  
- Weakness: minor typos and formatting issues throughout the manuscript.  
- Needs minor clarifications (e.g., illustrate PRED context around the predicate as in Fig. 6).  
- Presentation issue: unclear use of boldface in Table 2.  
- Lists specific wording corrections across multiple pages (several small grammar/wording fixes).  
- Reviewer asks for more implementation details of Grid-RNN usage, plans for other languages, and per-argument-type performance analysis (nominal, accusative, dative).
