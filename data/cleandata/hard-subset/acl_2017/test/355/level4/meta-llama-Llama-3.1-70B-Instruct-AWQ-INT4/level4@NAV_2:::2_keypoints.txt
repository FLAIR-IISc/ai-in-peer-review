- Proposes adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) for Japanese PAS and extending it with Grid-RNNs to model interactions among multiple predicates.  
- Model has two components: single-sequence stacked bidirectional RNNs and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN for predicate-sensitive representations.  
- Evaluated on a standard Japanese PAS benchmark and achieves significantly better performance than the prior state-of-the-art.  
- Main contributions: (1) neural sequence model that auto-induces features from word sequences; (2) Grid-RNN extension to handle multi-predicate argument interactions.  
- Strength: paper is well-structured and motivated, with overall accuracy gains and Grid-RNN improving zero-argument detection.  
- Weakness: the benefit from modeling predicate interactions is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Weakness: lacks comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which also extends Ouchi et al. (2015) using neural methods.  
- Reviewer questions: request detailed comparison to Shibata et al. (2016); plans for leveraging large-scale unlabeled data; more insight on predicate-interaction impact versus Ouchi et al. (2015).
