- Proposes Japanese SRL models by adapting Zhou & Xu (2015) and extending them with Grid-RNNs to model interactions among multiple predicates.
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significantly better performance than the current state of the art.
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
- The proposed model improves overall accuracy; the Grid-RNN variant yields slight additional gains, mainly by better detecting zero arguments.
- Main contribution is extending a single-sequential to a multi-sequential model; the benefit from predicate interactions is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Reviewer requests a comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), who previously extended Ouchi et al. (2015) using neural-network modeling.
