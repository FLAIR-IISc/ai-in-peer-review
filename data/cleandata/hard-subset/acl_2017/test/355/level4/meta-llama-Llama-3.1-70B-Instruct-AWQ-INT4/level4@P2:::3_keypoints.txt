- Proposes Grid-RNNs for Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis to capture multi-predicate interactions.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, with notable gains on zero argument identification.  
- Contribution: introduces a neural Grid-RNN model that improves zero argument identification by modeling interactions across predicates.  
- Contribution: empirically outperforms single-sequence RNN baselines on PAS tasks.  
- Contribution: provides detailed comparative analysis between Grid-RNN and single-sequence RNN models.  
- Strength: novel application of Grid-RNNs to PAS, addressing multi-predicate relations.  
- Strength: thorough performance analysis highlighting model strengths and weaknesses.  
- Strength: significant empirical improvements, especially for identifying zero arguments.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of task complexity, e.g., NOM vs ACC ambiguity and relation to SRL.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of model outputs regarding learned constraints like argument uniqueness and slot assignments.  
- Weakness: needs deeper comparison of when and why the two models produce different outputs and in what contexts.  
- Question: How are inter-sentential zero pronouns represented—explicitly marked or unmarked in the model?  
- Question: What are the authors' plans for addressing inter-sentential zero anaphora resolution in future work?  
- Question: Can the authors provide more analysis showing how well the model learns constraints such as uniqueness of arguments and argument slots?
