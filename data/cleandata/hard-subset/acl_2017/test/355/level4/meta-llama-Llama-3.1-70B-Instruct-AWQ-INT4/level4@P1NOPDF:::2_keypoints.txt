- Proposes adapted English SRL model (Zhou & Xu, 2015) for Japanese and extends it with Grid-RNNs to model interactions among arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluated on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark and shows significantly better performance than the prior state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.  
- Proposed model improves overall accuracy over the previous best system.  
- Grid-RNN extension yields slight additional gains, mainly by improving detection of zero (implicit) arguments.  
- Main novelty is extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential (predicate-interaction) model.  
- Impact of predicate interactions appears smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Missing or unclear comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which also extends Ouchi et al. using neural modeling.
