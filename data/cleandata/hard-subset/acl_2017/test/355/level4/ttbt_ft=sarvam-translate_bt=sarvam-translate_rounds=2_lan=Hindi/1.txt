**Strengths:**

This paper presents a refined application of a grid-type recurrent neural network to determine predicate-argument structures (PAS) in the Japanese language. This approach does not use a explicit syntactic structure and performs better than current SoA systems that incorporate syntactic structure. The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the implementation and results. In particular, they pay special attention to the performance on dropped arguments and null pronouns, which are prevalent in Japanese and are particularly challenging in relation to PAS. Their multi-sequence model, which takes into account all predicates in the sentence, achieves the best performance for these examples. The paper is written in a detailed and clear manner.

**Weaknesses:**

I only have minor comments. Some typos are listed below, which correcting would improve English fluency.
