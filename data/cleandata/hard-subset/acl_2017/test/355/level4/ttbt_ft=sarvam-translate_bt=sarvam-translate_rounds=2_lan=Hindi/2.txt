This paper proposes a new prediction model for Japanese SRL tasks, which adopts a state-of-the-art English model (Zhou and Xu, 2015).

The authors also expand the model by implementing the Grid-RRN framework to handle interaction between arguments of multiple predicates.

The evaluation was performed on a well-known benchmark dataset in Japanese SRL, and significantly better performance was achieved compared to the current state-of-the-art system.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
The proposed model achieves better accuracy compared to the current state-of-the-art system.
Furthermore, the model using Grid-RRN achieves slightly better performance compared to the proposed single-sequence model, mainly due to the improvement in detecting zero arguments, which is the central focus of this paper.

Weaknesses:
In my understanding, the main contribution of this paper is the extension of the single-sequence model to a multi-sequence model. The effect of predicate interactions is slightly less than that of (Ochi et al., 2015). There is a prior work (Shibata et al., 2016) that extends (Ochi et al., 2015)'s model with neural network modeling. I am curious about the comparison between them.
