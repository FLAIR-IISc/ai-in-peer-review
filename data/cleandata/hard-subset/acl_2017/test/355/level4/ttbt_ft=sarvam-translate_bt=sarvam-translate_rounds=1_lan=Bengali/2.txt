This paper proposes a new prediction model for Japanese SRL tasks, following the state-of-the-art model in English (Zhou and Xu, 2015).

The authors further improved the model by using the Grid-RNNs framework to handle interactions between multiple predictor arguments.

The evaluation was performed on the widely known benchmark dataset for Japanese SRL, and the performance was significantly better than that of current state-of-the-art systems.

Strengths:
The paper is well-structured and well-motivated.
The proposed model achieves improved accuracy compared to current state-of-the-art systems.
Additionally, the model created using Grid-RNNs achieves slightly better performance than the proposed one-sequence model, mainly due to the improvement in zero-argument detection, which is the main focus of this paper.

Weaknesses:
To my knowledge, the main contribution of this paper is extending the one-sequence model to a multi-sequence model. The effect of predictor interactions is somewhat less than that of (Ouchi et al., 2015). There was a previous paper called (Shibata et al., 2016) which extended (Ouchi et al., 2015)'s model through neural network modeling. I am curious about the comparison between them.
