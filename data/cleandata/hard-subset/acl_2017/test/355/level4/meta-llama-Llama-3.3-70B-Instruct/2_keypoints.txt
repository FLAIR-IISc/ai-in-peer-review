- Presents novel predictive models for Japanese SRL building on Zhou & Xu (2015).  
- Incorporates Grid-RNNs to model interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Evaluation on a standard Japanese SRL benchmark shows significantly improved performance versus the current state of the art.  
- Paper is clearly written with a well-defined motivation.  
- Proposed model achieves higher accuracy than the previous state-of-the-art system.  
- Grid-RNN variant yields marginal gains over the single-sequential model, mainly via better zero-argument detection.  
- Main contribution appears to be extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential one, with limited predicate-interaction impact compared to Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which extended Ouchi et al.’s model using neural networks.
