- Tackles Japanese PAS with frequent zero pronouns by modeling interactions between multiple predicates without using syntactic parsers.
- Proposes a single-sequence Bi-RNN baseline and a novel Grid-RNN multi-sequence model that jointly processes all predicates.
- Contribution: novel application of Grid-RNN to jointly model all predicate-argument structures, improving zero-argument identification.
- Contribution: demonstrates a purely sequence-based neural model (no syntactic features) achieves state-of-the-art on the NAIST benchmark.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with significance testing and detailed breakdowns by argument type (Dep vs Zero) and case role.
- Strength: Well-motivated, novel architecture that removes parser dependency and captures sentence-level multi-predicate context.
- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with helpful diagrams and solid literature grounding.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of computational cost—Grid-RNN processes M sequences for M predicates and may be more intensive.
- Weakness: Lacks feature ablation to assess the individual contributions of input features (ARG, PRED, MARK).
