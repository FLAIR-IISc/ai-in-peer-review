- Proposes an end-to-end Grid-RNN model for Japanese PAS that models interactions between multiple predicates without syntactic features, targeting zero-argument resolution.  
- Reports new state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, with especially large gains on zero-argument identification.

- Elegant Grid-RNN design that jointly models predicate argument sequences, addressing brittle reliance on syntactic parsers.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms prior SOTA despite simpler inputs, with detailed analysis by argument type and case role.  
- Clear writing and reproducible research: model architecture is well-explained, illustrated, and code is released.  
- Demonstrates benefits of deep architectures (up to 8 layers) and residual connections for structured NLP prediction.

- Lacks qualitative examples showing what multi-predicate interactions the Grid-RNN captures (e.g., specific cases where it fixes zeros).  
- No discussion or experiments on adding simpler linguistic features (e.g., POS tags) to delineate trade-offs without full syntax.  
- Potential sensitivity to the fixed predicate ordering in the grid is not explored or justified.

- Request for a concrete test-set example where Multi-Sequence corrects a difficult (Zero) argument that Single-Sequence misses, with intuition for the aiding interaction.  
- Asks for hypotheses explaining the unusually large performance gain on dative (DAT) arguments compared to NOM/ACC.  
- Queries whether the model’s performance depends on the order of predicate sequences (m-dimension) or is robust to predicate permutation.
