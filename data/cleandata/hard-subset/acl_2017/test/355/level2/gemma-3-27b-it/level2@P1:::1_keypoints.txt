- Novel application: Adapts Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, avoiding explicit syntactic parsing.
- State-of-the-art results: Outperforms previous NAIST Text Corpus systems, with notable gains on zero argument identification.
- Syntactic independence: Demonstrates strong performance without relying on syntactic information, learning semantic representations directly from word sequences.

- Clear motivation: Clearly articulates challenges of Japanese PAS (argument omission, noisy syntactic parses).
- Sound methodology: Grid-RNN/RNN architecture is well-motivated and clearly explained; residual ablation study is informative.
- Strong experimental evidence: Statistically significant improvements over baselines, especially for zero arguments.
- Comprehensive comparison: Thorough comparisons and case-role breakdowns increase credibility.
- Reproducibility: Source code is publicly available.

- Limited error analysis: Paper lacks detailed examination of remaining error types and challenging sentence structures.
- Hyperparameter details missing: Insufficient information on hyperparameter search ranges and sensitivity.
- Dataset scope limited: Evaluation confined to the relatively small NAIST Text Corpus, raising generalizability concerns.
- Missing comparisons to recent models: Fails to compare against newer transformer- or attention-based PAS models.
- Computational cost unclear: No discussion of Grid-RNN runtime/memory overhead or efficiency strategies.

- Q1: Provide detailed error analysis identifying common failure cases and causes.

- Q3: Report experiments with other architectures (e.g., transformers) and comparative results.

- Writing quality: Generally well-written with minor grammatical issues.
