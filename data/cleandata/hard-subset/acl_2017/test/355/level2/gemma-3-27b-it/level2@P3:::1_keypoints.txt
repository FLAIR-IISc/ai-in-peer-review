- Introduces a Grid-RNN model for Japanese PAS analysis that avoids syntactic parsing and addresses error propagation from pipeline approaches.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus using only word-sequence information, notably improving zero anaphora resolution.  
- Shows that grid-type architectures effectively model multi-predicate interactions, which contributes to performance gains.  
- Ablation studies reveal the impact of network depth and residual connections, guiding model design choices.  
- Strong empirical results on a standard benchmark, highlighting robustness without parser input.  
- Effective use of Grid-RNNs is a key technical strength, empirically validated by comparisons and ablations.  
- Experiments and ablation studies are thorough and compare to strong baselines.  
- Clear problem statement and motivation, especially regarding challenges of zero anaphora in Japanese.  
- Limited error analysis—paper lacks detailed breakdowns of remaining failure cases and specific hard predicate-argument types.  
- Dataset limitations—the NAIST Text Corpus is relatively small, raising questions about generalizability to other domains or larger datasets.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is under-discussed; more detail on tuning and robustness to choices (embedding size, learning rate, batch size) is needed.  
- No comparison to recent Transformer-based models, leaving the contribution’s position relative to current architectures unclear.  
- Requests for examples of model failures, especially for zero anaphora, to better understand limitations.  
- Asks about sensitivity to input window size (C) and whether attention mechanisms were considered or tested.  
- Paper is well-written, code is available, references are comprehensive, and related work discussion is adequate.
