- Proposes Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions from word sequences for Japanese PAS, avoiding syntactic parsing and claiming SOTA on the NAIST corpus without syntax.
- Primary contribution: demonstrates Grid-RNNs effectively capture multi-predicate interactions, improving zero-argument identification.
- Secondary contribution: publicly released source code for reproducibility.
- Tertiary contribution: comprehensive experimental evaluation on the NAIST corpus as a benchmark.

- Strong empirical results: outperforms Imamura (2009), Ouchi (2015), and a single-sequence RNN baseline with significant F-measure gains, especially for zero arguments.
- Addresses a relevant problem: directly tackles argument omission and vulnerability to parsing errors in Japanese PAS.
- Clear and comprehensive experiments: well-defined dataset, learning parameters, baselines, ablation study on depth/residuals, and case-role breakdowns.
- Well-motivated architecture: Grid-RNN choice is justified with clear explanations and helpful diagrams.

- Limited error analysis: lacks detailed breakdown of remaining error types and failure cases (e.g., specific zero anaphora challenges).
- Missing comparison with more recent approaches: no evaluation against newer neural or pretrained-language models post-2015.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity unclear: insufficient detail on tuning process and robustness to hyperparameter choices.
- Limited generalizability discussion: no exploration of adapting the approach to other pro-drop languages or semantic tasks.

- Request a detailed error analysis identifying common error types and causes.
- Ask whether newer architectures or pretrained LMs (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa) were tested and how they compare.
- Ask for clarification on the hyperparameter optimization process and any automated tuning used.
- Request discussion of challenges and adaptations needed to apply the method to other pro-drop languages.

- Reference list is extensive and relevant.
- Code availability is a positive aspect.
- Notation throughout the paper is clear and consistent.
