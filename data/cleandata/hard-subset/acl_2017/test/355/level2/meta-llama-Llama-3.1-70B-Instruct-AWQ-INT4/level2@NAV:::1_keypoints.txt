- Proposes Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS from word sequences.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus without using syntactic information.  
- Shows effectiveness at capturing long-distance dependencies and improving zero-argument identification.  
- Novel neural architecture that explicitly models multi-predicate interactions.  
- Evaluated thoroughly on a standard benchmark with baseline comparisons and ablation studies.  
- Well-motivated, clearly explained approach; paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Assumes reader familiarity with Japanese PAS background, which may hinder non-experts.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of the model’s limitations and potential future work.  
- Does not clearly explain applicability or generalizability to other languages or tasks.  
- Requests more details on Grid-RNN design and how it captures multi-predicate interactions.  
- Asks how out-of-vocabulary words and rare predicates are handled.  
- Seeks discussion of potential applications to other tasks (e.g., semantic role labeling, MT).
