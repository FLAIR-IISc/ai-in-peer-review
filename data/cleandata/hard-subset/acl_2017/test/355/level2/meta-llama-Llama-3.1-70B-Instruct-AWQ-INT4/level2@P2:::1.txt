**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural model for Japanese predicate argument structure (PAS) analysis, which automatically induces features sensitive to multi-predicate interactions from word sequence information of a sentence. The model uses grid-type recurrent neural networks (Grid-RNNs) to capture the interactions between all predicates in a sentence, without relying on syntactic information predicted by parsers. The authors evaluate their model on the NAIST Text Corpus and demonstrate that it outperforms the state-of-the-art Japanese PAS analyzer.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neural Model for Japanese PAS Analysis**: The authors propose a neural model that uses Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS analysis. This is a significant contribution, as previous approaches relied heavily on syntactic information predicted by parsers.
2. **Effectiveness of Grid-RNNs**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of Grid-RNNs in capturing multi-predicate interactions, which is a key challenge in Japanese PAS analysis.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The authors achieve state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming previous approaches that relied on syntactic information.

**Strengths**

1. **Innovative Use of Grid-RNNs**: The authors' use of Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions is a novel and effective approach to Japanese PAS analysis.
2. **Strong Experimental Results**: The authors' experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach, outperforming previous state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus.
3. **Clear and Well-Organized Presentation**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed model and experimental results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Comparison to Previous Work**: While the authors compare their results to previous state-of-the-art results, they do not provide a detailed comparison of their approach to other neural models for Japanese PAS analysis.
2. **Lack of Analysis of Error Types**: The authors do not provide an analysis of error types, which would be helpful in understanding the limitations of their approach.
3. **No Discussion of Computational Complexity**: The authors do not discuss the computational complexity of their approach, which is an important consideration for practical applications.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed comparison of your approach to other neural models for Japanese PAS analysis?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of error propagation in your approach?
3. Can you provide an analysis of error types to better understand the limitations of your approach?

**Additional Comments**

The authors' use of Grid-RNNs is a promising approach to Japanese PAS analysis, and their experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. However, further analysis and comparison to other approaches are needed to fully understand the strengths and limitations of their approach.
