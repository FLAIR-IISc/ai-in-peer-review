- Paper proposes neural models for Japanese PAS: a single-sequence (stacked BiRNNs) and a multi-sequence (Grid-RNNs) model.  
- Contribution: single-sequence model induces context-sensitive argument representations from candidate sequences without syntactic information.  
- Contribution: multi-sequence model uses Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions across a sentence.  
- Contribution: achieves state-of-the-art performance on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 without syntactic features.  
- Strength: thorough analysis and detailed descriptions of both single- and multi-sequence models.  
- Strength: experimental results outperform baselines on all metrics; multi-sequence model attains SOTA.  
- Strength: per-case-role analysis shows robust learning of the infrequent dative case.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of why Grid-RNNs were chosen and how they concretely capture multi-predicate interactions.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the proposed models’ limitations and potential failure modes.  
- Weakness: unclear how the models can be applied or adapted to related tasks like semantic role labeling (SRL).  
- Question: please provide a detailed rationale for choosing Grid-RNNs and how they model multi-predicate interactions and their advantages.  
- Question: please provide a detailed analysis of the models’ limitations when learning contextual PAS information from word sequences and ways to mitigate them.  
- Question: please explain how the models can be modified or extended to apply to SRL and what benefits or challenges would arise.
