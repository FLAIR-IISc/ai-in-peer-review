- Paper introduces Grid-RNN: a multi-sequence RNN modeling predicates×tokens to capture multi-predicate interactions without syntactic parses.
- Presents a strong syntax-free single-sequence BiRNN baseline using only word embeddings, a predicate indicator, and a local window.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on NAIST 1.5, outperforming syntax-based joint models, with notable gains on Zero arguments and analysis of depth/residual connections.

- Grid-RNN is a natural 2D extension of stacked BiRNNs to propagate information across predicates, addressing error propagation from pipeline syntactic parsers.
- Strong empirical rigor: standard splits, bootstrap significance testing, and meaningful F1 improvements (esp. +2–3 on Zero vs Ouchi et al., 2015).
- Minimal feature engineering and no external resources, suggesting architectures learn useful sequence representations.
- Experiments examine depth and residual connections, showing deeper models with residuals improve performance.
- Per-role breakdown (NOM/ACC/DAT; Dep vs Zero) highlights large gains on DAT and Zero-NOM, indicating better long-distance dependency capture.

- Incremental gains from multi-predicate modeling are modest (~0.3 F1 overall, ~1 F1 on Zero), raising questions about practical impact vs added complexity.
- No reported training/inference time, memory usage, or parameter counts; scalability to many-predicate sentences is unclear.
- Limited ablations: lack of feature removals (MARK, window), alternative inter-sequence mechanisms (pooling/attention), and update-direction studies.
- Experimental details incomplete: placeholder code link, missing regularization/training specifics, unclear grid update/boundary rules, and unclear whether predicates are gold or predicted.
- Sparse qualitative/error analysis: no example resolutions, error typology, or breakdowns by distance/type to substantiate claimed strengths.

- Requests breakdown of performance on single-predicate vs multi-predicate sentences to validate multi-predicate benefit.
- Requests computational overhead metrics (training/inference time, peak memory) as functions of M (predicates) and T (tokens).
- Asks whether predicates are gold at evaluation and sensitivity to predicate detection errors or end-to-end integration.
- Requests ablations removing MARK, predicate window, and replacing Grid-RNN with simpler inter-sequence mechanisms (e.g., attention).
- Requests clarification of grid update schedule, boundary handling, and whether bidirectional flow was tried across both axes.
- Asks for working code link and release of configs for reproducibility.

- Suggests adding qualitative zero-anaphora examples and detailed error analysis (distance bins, role ambiguities).
- Recommends reporting precision and recall per case role and Dep/Zero, not just F1.
- Suggests discussion of combining syntax-free representations with robust syntactic cues.
- Notes several reference formatting/placeholders need fixing for camera-ready.
