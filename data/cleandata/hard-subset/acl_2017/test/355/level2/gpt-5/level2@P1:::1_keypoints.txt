- Proposes syntax-free neural PAS models: a single-sequence BiRNN per predicate and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN across predicates.  
- Grid-RNN explicitly models multi-predicate interactions and uses residual connections to train deep (8-layer) networks.  
- Both models achieve state-of-the-art results on NAIST 1.5, outperforming syntax-based baselines.  
- Particularly strong improvements for Zero anaphora and DAT case identification, with large F1 gains reported.  
- Residual connections enable consistent gains from deeper architectures.  
- Experimental setup is rigorous: standard splits, bootstrap significance testing, and no external resources.  
- Multi-Seq design (odd/even inter-sequence propagation) is well-motivated and technically principled.  
- Paper lacks ablations to pinpoint which Grid-RNN design choices drive its advantage (directionality, layer count, alternatives).  
- Unclear how inference enforces PAS constraints (e.g., at most one argument per case per predicate) — possible multiple tokens per case.  
- Limited qualitative/conditional analysis (e.g., effect of number of predicates, predicate distance, sentence length) showing when multi-predicate interactions help.  
- DAT improvements are surprisingly large and warrant further discussion to rule out confounds like preprocessing or annotation differences.  
- Scalability concerns: Grid-RNN complexity scales with M×T×L but training/inference speed and memory are not reported.  
- Questions on whether any global decoding/constraints are used at test time to enforce uniqueness per case.  
- Request for breakdown of accuracy and runtime by number of predicates M and sentence length.  
- Ask whether simpler cross-predicate sharing (pooled context or attention) was tried and how it compares to Grid-RNN.  
- Request error analysis and examples where Multi-Seq corrects Single-Seq, especially for Zero NOM and DAT.  
- Ask about preprocessing choices (e.g., case-marker tokenization) and per-label frequency stats to contextualize DAT gains.  
- Clarify predicate setup: are predicates gold, are nominal predicates included, and how would predicted predicates affect performance?  
- Suggest clarifying candidate granularity (bunsetsu vs word) for comparability with other Japanese PAS work.  
- Recommend reporting variance over multiple random seeds and exploring lightweight syntactic inputs as complementary signals.
