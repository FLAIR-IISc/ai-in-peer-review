- Summary: Proposes a Grid-RNN model for Japanese PAS analysis that induces multi-predicate interaction features from word sequences and achieves strong results on the NAIST corpus.
- Contribution 1: Introduces a neural Grid-RNN architecture to capture multi-predicate interactions for PAS.
- Contribution 2: Demonstrates automatic induction of effective feature representations from raw word sequences without syntactic input.
- Contribution 3: Reports state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming syntax-based models.
- Strength 1: Novel architecture that explicitly models multi-predicate interactions.
- Strength 2: Eliminates dependence on syntactic features by learning useful representations directly.
- Strength 3: Empirical results show strong effectiveness on a standard benchmark.
- Weakness 1: Evaluation is limited to the NAIST Text Corpus and may not generalize.
- Weakness 2: Lacks comparison to other neural PAS models, hindering relative assessment.
- Weakness 3: No analysis of error types to illuminate model limitations.
- Question 1: How does the model perform on other Japanese PAS datasets (e.g., Japanese WordNet)?
- Question 2: Can the model be adapted to other languages (English, Chinese) with minimal changes?
- Question 3: How sensitive is performance to different recurrent units (LSTM, GRU, etc.)?
