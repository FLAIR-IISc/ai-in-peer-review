## Review of "Neural Modeling of Multi-Predicate Interactions for Japanese Predicate Argument Structure Analysis"

This paper addresses the challenging task of Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis, specifically tackling the problem of argument omission. The authors propose a neural model utilizing Grid-RNNs to automatically capture multi-predicate interactions from word sequences, aiming to reduce reliance on potentially error-prone syntactic parsing. The core contribution is demonstrating that this grid-RNN based model surpasses the state-of-the-art Japanese PAS analyzer *without* utilizing syntactic information, achieving competitive results on the NAIST Text Corpus. A secondary contribution lies in the empirical demonstration of the effectiveness of grid-RNNs in modeling these multi-predicate interactions, particularly for zero-argument identification.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The model demonstrably outperforms existing methods on a standard benchmark (NAIST Text Corpus) without relying on syntactic information, a significant advantage given error propagation issues in pipeline approaches. The reported improvements, particularly for zero arguments, are noteworthy.
* **Novel Application of Grid-RNNs:** Applying grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions in PAS analysis is a reasonable and potentially impactful approach. The architecture seems well-suited to the task, and the results support this intuition.
* **Clear Problem Definition & Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the challenges of Japanese PAS analysis, specifically argument omission, and provides a compelling motivation for exploring methods that reduce dependence on syntactic parsing.
* **Thorough Experimentation:** The authors conduct experiments comparing their models with strong baselines and investigate the impact of network depth and residual connections, providing a comprehensive evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty of Individual Components:** While the *combination* of grid-RNNs with PAS analysis is novel, the individual components (RNNs, Grid-RNNs) are well-established. The paper doesn't present significant architectural innovations within these components themselves.
* **Lack of Ablation Study on Grid-RNN Design:** The paper doesn’t explore different configurations or variations of the grid-RNN architecture. A more detailed ablation study examining the impact of different grid sizes or connection patterns could strengthen the findings.
* **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:** While the results are positive, the paper lacks a detailed error analysis. Understanding *where* the model still struggles could provide valuable insights for future improvements.
* **Dataset Limitations:** The NAIST Text Corpus, while standard, is relatively small. Evaluating the model on larger and more diverse datasets would increase confidence in its generalizability.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide a more detailed error analysis, highlighting common error cases and potential reasons for these errors?
2. Have you experimented with different grid sizes or connection patterns within the Grid-RNN architecture? If so, what were the results?
3.  What is the computational cost of the proposed model compared to the baseline methods?

**Overall:**

This is a solid paper presenting a promising approach to Japanese PAS analysis. The strong empirical results and clear motivation make it a valuable contribution. While the novelty of individual components is limited, the successful application of grid-RNNs to this task is significant. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly through a more detailed error analysis and exploration of the grid-RNN architecture, would further strengthen the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
