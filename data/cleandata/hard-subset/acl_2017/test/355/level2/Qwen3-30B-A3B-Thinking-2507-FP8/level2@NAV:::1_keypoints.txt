- Proposes a neural approach to Japanese PAS that models multi-predicate interactions without relying on syntactic parsers to avoid error propagation.  
- Introduces two models: a single-sequence model for individual predicates and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN model capturing interactions across all predicates.  
- Claims a neural framework that automatically induces multi-predicate interaction features from raw word sequences, eliminating the need for syntactic features.  
- Reports substantial improvement in zero argument identification: 47.12% F1 (vs. 44.09% from Ouchi et al., 2015).  
- Shows overall state-of-the-art performance using only word sequence information (81.42% vs 79.23% F1 baseline), with results statistically significant.  
- Strength: clearly identifies parser-induced error propagation as a key limitation of prior joint modeling approaches.  
- Strength: achieves notable gains on the challenging zero argument identification task.  
- Strength: provides thorough experimental validation, including ablation studies on network depth and residual connections.  
- Weakness: lacks sufficient comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), limiting attribution of gains to Grid-RNN vs. absence of syntactic features.  
- Weakness: missing analysis of computational complexity and training time, hindering assessment of practical adoption.  
- Weakness: explanation of Grid-RNN novelty is superficial despite citing prior work, making the architectural contribution unclear.  
- Recommendation: authors should clarify Grid-RNN advantages over standard RNNs, compare more comprehensively with syntactic neural approaches, and report computational efficiency; paper has good potential for multilingual semantic analysis.
