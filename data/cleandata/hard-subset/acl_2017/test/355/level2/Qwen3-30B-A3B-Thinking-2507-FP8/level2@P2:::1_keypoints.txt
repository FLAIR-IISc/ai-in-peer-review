- Paper proposes neural sequence models for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions, claiming SOTA without parsers and targeting zero-argument identification.  
- Strong empirical improvement on zero arguments: 47.12% F1 vs. 44.09% (Ouchi et al. 2015).  
- Clear problem framing and ablation: isolates zero-argument challenge and shows Multi-Seq > Single-Seq by 0.27 F1 (p<0.05).  
- Robust experimental design: uses NAIST 1.5 splits, thorough hyperparameter tuning, and residual connections that boost F1 by ~1.0 point.  
- Grid-RNN lacks novelty: architecture is borrowed from Kalchbrenner et al. (2016) and not properly discussed in related work, misrepresenting novelty (critical).  
- Missing key neural baseline: omission of Shibata et al. (2016, 81.1% F1) undermines the “SOTA without syntactic info” claim (critical).  
- Insufficient analysis of Grid-RNN’s role: deeper non-Grid models nearly match Multi-Seq, so unclear if Grid provides gains beyond increased depth (major).  
- Question: How does your Grid-RNN adaptation differ from Kalchbrenner et al. (2016) and why isn’t it contrasted in Sec 5.2?  
- Question: Why was Shibata et al. (2016) excluded from comparisons, and how does your 81.42% result compare to it?
