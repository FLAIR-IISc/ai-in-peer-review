- Introduces Grid-RNN extension of Zhou & Xu (2015) for Japanese SRL to model multi-predicate interactions and improve zero argument detection.  
- Proposes a syntactic-independent neural architecture that avoids parser error propagation.  
- Reports significant empirical gains on the NAIST Text Corpus, especially for zero argument identification.  
- Strength: Outperforms prior state-of-the-art (Ouchi et al., 2015), showing Grid-RNN effectiveness.  
- Strength: Well-motivated, clearly structured paper with detailed methodology and experiments.  
- Strength: Strong emphasis and demonstrated improvements on the challenging zero argument detection task.  
- Strength: Includes ablation studies and analyses (e.g., network depth) to validate design choices.  
- Weakness: Multi-predicate interaction gains are modest compared to single-sequence models (e.g., 81.42% vs. 81.15% F1).  
- Weakness: Missing direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which limits the strength of the state-of-the-art claim.  
- Weakness: No multilingual experiments despite claims of broader applicability, limiting generalizability.  
- Question: Please provide a direct comparison with Shibata et al. (2016).  
- Question: Why are multi-predicate interaction gains small, and could additional architectural changes improve this?  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to include the missing comparison and clarify the impact of multi-predicate interactions.
