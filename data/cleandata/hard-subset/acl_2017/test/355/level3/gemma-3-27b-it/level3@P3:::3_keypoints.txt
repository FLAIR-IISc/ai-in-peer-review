- Proposes joint neural modeling for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions without syntactic parsing.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming syntax-based methods.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation including ablation studies (network depth, residual connections) and strong baseline comparisons.  
- Strong empirical performance, especially the multi-sequence model, with statistically significant gains across metrics and case roles.  
- Clear, accessible presentation with helpful model architecture figures.  
- Thorough baseline comparison and detailed experimental setup enabling reproducibility.  
- Insightful analysis contrasting single-sequence and multi-sequence models and effects of depth/residuals.  
- Paper underplays the full complexity of Japanese PAS, especially NOM vs ACC ambiguity and zero-pronoun challenges.  
- Missing statistics on how often zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable.  
- Unclear treatment and representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns in the corpus and implications for the model.  
- No explicit enforcement or analysis of linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per predicate per type).  
- Limited qualitative output analysis comparing single- and multi-sequence models; lacks examples showing where multi-sequence helps.  
- Asks authors for proportion of intra-sententially resolvable zero pronouns in NAIST.  
- Asks how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (e.g., explicit empty arguments) and implications for modeling.  
- Asks whether the model implicitly learns argument-structure linguistic constraints and what was found.  
- Requests example sentences illustrating multi-sequence improvements and analysis of reasons.  
- Requests contexts/examples explaining NOM improvements of Multi-Seq over Single-Seq from Table 3.  
- Notes typographical and formatting errors (several page/figure/table/reference issues) needing correction.  
- Suggests including results on a standard SRL dataset would strengthen the paper.
