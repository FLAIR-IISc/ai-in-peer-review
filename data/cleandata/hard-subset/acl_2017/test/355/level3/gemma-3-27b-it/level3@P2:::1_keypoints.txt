- Novel approach: uses Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS without relying on syntactic parsing.  
- Strong empirical result: the model outperforms state-of-the-art parser-dependent Japanese PAS analyzers on the NAIST Text Corpus.  
- Focus on zero pronouns: explicitly addresses argument omission, a key challenge in Japanese PAS analysis.  
- Contribution: demonstrates effective PAS analysis from word sequences and provides a detailed model description for reproducibility.  
- Effective modeling: Grid-RNNs capture inter-predicate relationships and improve over single-sequence baselines.  
- Rigorous evaluation: includes ablation studies, per-case-role analysis, and significance testing (bootstrap resampling).  
- Clear presentation: paper is well-written with helpful diagrams and implementation details.  
- Weakness — language issues: numerous minor grammatical errors and awkward phrasing throughout the paper.  
- Weakness — limited error analysis: lacks detailed analysis of remaining errors, especially failures in zero anaphora resolution.  
- Weakness — unclear formatting: purpose of boldface in Table 2 is not explained and is confusing.  
- Question: clarify the meaning/purpose of the boldface in Table 2.  
- Question: provide example cases where the model fails on zero anaphora resolution to illustrate remaining challenges.  
- Question: explain why the accusative marker is included with the predicate in the input and how this relates to the PRED-context idea.  
- Additional comment: note minor typographical errors throughout the text.  
- Additional comment: include discussion of computational complexity of Grid-RNNs versus simpler recurrent architectures.
