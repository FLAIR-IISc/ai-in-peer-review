- Presents a Grid-RNN-based approach to Japanese PAS analysis that achieves state-of-the-art results without explicit syntactic input.  
- Addresses the key challenge of zero pronoun (dropped argument) resolution in Japanese with a well-motivated and thoroughly evaluated method.

Contributions:
- Applies Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions, avoiding reliance on syntactic parses and reducing error propagation.  
- Achieves strong improvement in zero pronoun identification, with the multi-sequence model particularly effective.  
- Provides clear, detailed implementation and experimental descriptions that support reproducibility.

Strengths:
- Surpasses existing state-of-the-art Japanese PAS analyzers, notably improving zero argument identification.  
- Novel and successful use of Grid-RNNs to capture inter-predicate relationships.  
- Direct focus on the challenging problem of zero pronoun resolution.  
- Clear writing and helpful architectural figures that aid understanding.  
- Thorough experimentation including ablation studies on depth and residual connections.

Weaknesses:
- Paper contains numerous minor typos and non-fluent English that impair readability.  
- Boldface usage in Table 2 is unclear and needs explicit explanation.  
- Lacks in-depth error analysis to identify common failure cases.  
- Comparisons to some more recent neural baselines are missing, weakening the state-of-the-art claim.

Questions to authors:
- Please clarify what the boldface denotes in Table 2.  
- Expand the “PRED-context” explanation and illustrate how the accusative marker is incorporated with the verb in Fig. 6.  
- Provide examples of common model errors and discuss likely causes.

Additional comment:
- The provided list of typographical corrections is comprehensive and should be applied to improve polish.
