- Presents a neural model using Grid-RNNs for Japanese PAS analysis to handle argument omission without relying on syntactic parsing.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming systems that use syntax, especially for zero-argument resolution.  
- Grid-RNN architecture effectively captures multi-predicate interactions; ablation vs single-sequence RNNs shows clear benefits.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation including network depth/residual connection studies and statistical significance testing.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful figures and publicly available code improving reproducibility.  

- Does not sufficiently discuss inherent task ambiguity (e.g., NOM vs ACC confusions) or compare more directly to SRL challenges.  
- Unclear handling of inter-sentential zero pronouns in the corpus and whether they are explicitly marked or absent.  
- Model lacks explicit linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per role); no analysis of whether such constraints are learned or violated.  
- Limited qualitative/error analysis comparing single-sequence vs multi-sequence outputs to explain how Grid-RNNs improve performance.  

- Request: report proportion of zero pronouns that are resolvable intra-sententially in NAIST.  
- Request: clarify representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns in the corpus and implications for future work.  
- Request: provide example cases where Multi-Sequence predictions differ substantially from Single-Sequence.  
- Request: explain why improvements concentrate on NOM F-measure and in which contexts they occur.  

- Minor language/notation issues: suggest brief notation fixes (w1…T), consistent hyphenation of "Inter-Zero", consistent citation formatting (e.g., "Taira et al. (2008)"), and clearer definition of functions like g(ℓ).
