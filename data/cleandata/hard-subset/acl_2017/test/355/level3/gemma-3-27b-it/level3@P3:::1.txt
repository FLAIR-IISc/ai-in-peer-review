This paper presents a novel approach to Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis using Grid-RNNs, achieving state-of-the-art results without relying on explicit syntactic information. The authors address a critical problem in Japanese NLP: handling dropped arguments (zero pronouns). The work is well-motivated, and the experimental setup is thorough.

**Contributions:**

1. **Effective Neural Architecture for Multi-Predicate Interaction:** The core contribution is the successful application of Grid-RNNs to model interactions between multiple predicates in a sentence. This is particularly impactful as it allows for strong performance *without* the need for traditionally relied-upon syntactic parsing, mitigating error propagation issues.
2. **Strong Performance on Zero Pronoun Resolution:** The paper demonstrates a significant improvement in identifying zero pronouns, a notoriously difficult task in Japanese PAS analysis. The multi-sequence model proves particularly effective in this regard. This is a key practical contribution given the prevalence of dropped arguments in the language.
3. **Detailed Implementation and Evaluation:** The paper provides a clear and comprehensive description of the model architecture, experimental setup, and results. This allows for reproducibility and facilitates further research in the area.

**Strengths:**

1. **State-of-the-Art Results:** The proposed model surpasses existing state-of-the-art Japanese PAS analyzers, demonstrating the effectiveness of the approach. The results in Table 1 are compelling, particularly the gains on zero argument identification.
2. **Novel Application of Grid-RNNs:** Utilizing Grid-RNNs for PAS analysis is a novel idea, and the authors successfully demonstrate its benefits for capturing multi-predicate interactions. This contributes to the broader understanding of how to effectively model relationships between linguistic elements using neural networks.
3. **Focus on a Challenging Problem:** The paper directly addresses the difficult problem of zero pronoun resolution in Japanese, a key challenge for accurate PAS analysis.  The focused experimentation and analysis on this aspect are commendable.
4. **Clear and Detailed Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology and experimental setup. The inclusion of figures illustrating the model architectures (Figs. 2, 3, 6) is helpful. The detailed implementation section supports reproducibility.
5. **Thorough Experimentation:** The ablation studies (Table 2) investigating the impact of network depth and residual connections provide valuable insights into the model's behavior.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **English Fluency and Typos:** While the paper is generally clear, it contains numerous minor typos and instances of non-fluent English, which detract from the reading experience. (The detailed list of corrections provided by the reviewer is very useful.)
2. **Clarification Needed for Table 2:** The use of boldface in Table 2 is unclear and requires explanation. It is not immediately apparent what the boldface highlights.
3. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:** While quantitative results are presented, a more in-depth error analysis would be beneficial. Identifying common failure cases could provide valuable insights for future improvements.
4. **Lack of Comparison to More Recent Approaches:** The comparison to baseline models is somewhat dated. Including a comparison to more recent neural approaches (if any exist) would strengthen the claim of state-of-the-art performance.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Table 2 Clarification:** Please clarify the meaning of the boldface formatting in Table 2. What metric or condition does it highlight?
2. **Illustrative Example:** Could you please expand on the explanation of the “PRED-context” point, specifically illustrating how the accusative marker is incorporated with the verb in Fig. 6? A clearer explanation of this aspect would be beneficial.
3. **Error Analysis:** Could you provide some examples of common errors made by the model, and discuss potential reasons for these errors?



**Additional Comments:**

The list of typographical corrections provided is comprehensive and valuable. Addressing these issues will significantly improve the readability and polish of the paper.
