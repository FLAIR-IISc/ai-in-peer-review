- Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) sequence-based model to Japanese PAS and extends it with Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions.
- Core aim is improving argument identification, especially zero arguments, without heavy reliance on syntactic parsing.
- Empirically outperforms prior state-of-the-art on the NAIST Text Corpus, with notable gains for zero-argument detection.
- Demonstrates that Grid-RNNs can effectively model multi-predicate interactions for Japanese SRL.
- Provides a clear, well-structured presentation and publicly available code for reproducibility.
- Well-motivated problem framing emphasizing zero anaphora and parser noise issues.
- Contribution is incremental: heavily builds on existing work (Zhou & Xu, Ouchi et al.) rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.
- Missing a direct comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), which limits contextual evaluation against related neural extensions.
- The measured benefit of predicate-interaction modeling is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015), needing further explanation.
- Requests clarification on why interaction impact is smaller than in prior work (data preprocessing, hyperparameters, etc.).
- Asks for a detailed ablation study to isolate contributions of Grid-RNN vs. residual connections.
- Seeks discussion on challenges when applying the model to languages with different syntax or argument realization patterns.
- Suggests adding an error analysis to identify remaining error types and directions for future improvement.
