- Proposes Grid-RNNs for Japanese Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) analysis, modeling multi-predicate interactions directly from word sequences.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results without relying on explicit syntactic information.  
- Effectively handles dropped arguments (zero pronouns), a common challenge in Japanese PAS.  
- Presents both single-sequence and multi-sequence model variants with clear implementation details.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements from grid-type RNNs over baselines.  
- Ablation studies show the impact of network depth and residual connections on performance.  
- Detailed error analysis per case role (Table 3) provides insight into model strengths and weaknesses.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough explanations and experiments, strengthening reproducibility.  
- Minor issues: numerous typographical errors and awkward phrasing that reduce polish.  
- Visualization suggestion: provide a more intuitive “PRED-context” illustration (e.g., include accusative marker with the verb as in Figure 6).  
- Presentation issue: the use/significance of boldface in Table 2 is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Questions to authors: clarify boldface purpose in Table 2 and supply a clearer PRED-context example (possibly using Figure 6).
