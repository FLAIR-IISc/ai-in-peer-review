- Novel joint neural model using Grid-RNNs to model multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS, achieving SOTA on NAIST without syntactic input.  
- Demonstrated empirical effectiveness, especially for zero-pronoun resolution, addressing error propagation from pipeline systems.  
- Thorough experimental setup with ablations (network depth, residuals) and strong baseline comparisons.  
- Clear, accessible presentation with helpful figures illustrating architecture and task.  
- Insufficient discussion of Japanese PAS task complexity, particularly nominative vs. accusative ambiguity and the severity of zero-pronoun ambiguity.  
- Missing statistic: proportion of zero pronouns that are intra-sententially resolvable in the dataset.  
- Unclear how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented (unlabelled vs. explicit empty-argument) and implications of that choice.  
- Model does not enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per predicate/type); lacking analysis of whether these constraints are learned implicitly.  
- Missing comparative output analysis between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq models to show systematic differences beyond aggregate NOM improvements.  
- Request for example cases where Multi-Seq succeeds and Single-Seq fails to illustrate benefits of inter-predicate modeling.  
- Question whether methods to incorporate linguistic constraints (regularization or output constraints) were investigated.  
- Minor language and formatting issues throughout the paper; recommends careful proofreading and specific wording fixes.
