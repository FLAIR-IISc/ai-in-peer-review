- Paper applies Grid-RNNs to Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis, outperforming current state-of-the-art, especially on dropped arguments/zero pronouns.
- Contribution: proposes a neural Grid-RNN model that captures multi-predicate interactions without relying on syntactic information.
- Contribution: achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, beating previous syntax-based models.
- Contribution: improves zero-argument identification by modeling multi-predicate interactions.
- Strength: effective modeling of multi-predicate interactions with Grid-RNNs, addressing a central challenge in Japanese PAS.
- Strength: thorough evaluation using multiple metrics (F-measures for different argument types and case roles).
- Strength: direct comparison to prior state-of-the-art demonstrates the proposed approach’s superiority.
- Weakness: minor typos and formatting issues (e.g., boldface in Table 2) reduce readability.
- Weakness: lacks explicit illustrative examples showing how Grid-RNNs deliver their benefits.
