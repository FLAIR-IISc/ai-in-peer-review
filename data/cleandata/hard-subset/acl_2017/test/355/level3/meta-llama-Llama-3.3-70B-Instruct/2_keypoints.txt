- Introduces a Grid-RNN-extended prediction model for Japanese SRL by adapting an English state-of-the-art system.  
- Demonstrates significantly better performance than the current Japanese SRL benchmark system.  
- Shows Grid-RNNs are effective at detecting zero arguments.  
- Paper is well-structured and well-motivated.  
- Reports improved accuracy and better performance with Grid-RNNs.  
- Grid-RNNs capture complex interactions between predicates and arguments, enhancing PAS analysis.  
- The impact of predicate interactions is smaller than reported in previous work.  
- Main contribution is essentially extending a single-sequential model to a multi-sequential model, with limited comparison to similar prior work.  
- Paper lacks detailed analysis of results and broader comparison with other state-of-the-art models.  
- Novel and effective approach to Japanese PAS analysis supports acceptance.  
- Experimental results demonstrate superiority over baseline models, supporting acceptance.  
- Use of Grid-RNNs offers a new perspective on modeling predicate-argument interactions.  
- Smaller predicate-interaction impact compared to past work argues against acceptance.  
- Lack of comparison with similar previous methods weakens the submission.  
- Insufficiently detailed and extensive result analysis argues against acceptance.  
- How will the authors address the smaller impact of predicate interactions relative to previous work?  
- Can the authors provide more detailed analysis and comparisons with other state-of-the-art models?  
- How do the authors plan to extend this approach to other languages and tasks?
