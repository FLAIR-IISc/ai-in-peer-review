- Proposal: extends Zhou & Xu (2015) English SRL model to Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions.  
- Contribution 1: introduces multi-sequential modeling to capture interactions between arguments of multiple predicates.  
- Contribution 2: shows neural models can reach state-of-the-art performance without syntactic information.  
- Contribution 3: demonstrates slight improvements in zero-argument detection using Grid-RNN variants.  
- Empirical strength: strong results on NAIST Text Corpus; multi-sequence model achieves 81.42% F1 vs Ouchi et al.'s 79.23%.  
- Clarity: paper is well-structured and motivated by the zero-argument challenge in Japanese PAS.  
- Ablation: Grid-RNNs improve zero-argument detection by ~1.0 point (46.10% → 47.12%).  
- Weakness: method is an extension of prior work rather than a fundamentally novel approach.  
- Limited impact: multi-predicate interaction benefit is marginal (81.15% single → 81.42% multi).  
- Missing comparison: omits Shibata et al. (2016), making the state-of-the-art claim questionable.  
- Missing analysis: lacks sufficient discussion on why results differ from Ouchi et al. regarding predicate interactions.  
- Recommendations: authors should test Grid-RNN applicability to English SRL and provide a detailed comparison with Shibata et al. (2016).
