- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural architecture for Japanese PAS that captures multi-predicate interactions from word sequences without using syntactic information.  
- Claims state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus 1.5.  
- Demonstrates that joint predicate modeling improves zero-pronoun resolution and avoids parser error propagation.  

- Empirical results are strong and well-executed, achieving SOTA without syntactic dependencies.  
- Significant improvement on NOM zero-pronoun resolution (≈+2.5 points), important for Japanese PAS.  
- Clear, accessible presentation with helpful figures; comparison of single-sequence vs. multi-sequence models is informative.  

- Underemphasizes task complexity, especially NOM/ACC ambiguity and differences from standard SRL.  
- Omits statistics on what fraction of zero pronouns are resolvable within the sentence, leaving task difficulty unclear.  
- Does not enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per case per predicate) nor analyze whether the model learns them.  
- Lacks analysis explaining why Multi-Seq improves NOM and in which contexts this improvement occurs.  

- Request stats on proportion of zero pronouns that are intra-sententially resolvable.  
- Clarify representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns (unmarked vs. explicit empty argument) and implications.  
- Analyze whether and how the model enforces linguistic constraints like one argument per case.  
- Compare Single-Seq and Multi-Seq outputs to identify systematic differences in errors.  
- Explain why Multi-Seq primarily improves NOM and specify contexts of improvement.  

- Minor: several typographical inconsistencies (e.g., "F-measure" formatting, "Zero" vs. "zero").
