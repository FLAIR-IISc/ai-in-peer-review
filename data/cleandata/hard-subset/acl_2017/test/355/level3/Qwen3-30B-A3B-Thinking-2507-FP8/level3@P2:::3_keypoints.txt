- Introduces neural sequence models with Grid-RNNs for Japanese PAS that induce multi-predicate interaction features from word sequences without syntactic parsers.  
- Claims state-of-the-art results on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, notably improving zero argument identification by up to 3.0 F-measure points.  
- Demonstrates that modeling multi-predicate interactions particularly benefits zero argument resolution.  

- Novel approach avoids error propagation from syntactic parsers and operates directly on word sequences.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation with multiple metrics and statistical significance testing, showing clear gains for the multi-sequence Grid-RNN model.  
- Results corroborate the linguistic intuition that predicate interactions help resolve zero pronouns.  
- Presentation is accessible with helpful architecture figures and clear distinction between Dep and Zero arguments.  

- Does not discuss enforcement or analysis of linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per slot per predicate), risking inconsistent outputs.  
- Lacks statistics on what fraction of zero pronouns are intrasententially resolvable in the corpus, obscuring task difficulty.  
- Fails to clarify how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented or handled, leaving model limitations unclear.  
- No detailed analysis of where multi-sequence outperforms single-sequence; improvements seem concentrated on NOM but are unexplained.  
- Insufficient contextualization relative to English SRL work and the dependency vs. constituent distinction.  

- Requests corpus statistics on proportion of intrasententially resolvable zero pronouns.  
- Asks how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented in the dataset and their impact on modeling.  
- Seeks analysis on whether and how the model learns linguistic constraints (one argument per slot).  
- Requests comparative output analysis between single-sequence and multi-sequence models to pinpoint improvements.  
- Asks why improvements are strongest for the nominative case and what that indicates about model behavior.  

- Paper is well written with minor language issues; addressing the above points would strengthen clarity and contribution.
