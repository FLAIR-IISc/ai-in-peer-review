- Summary: Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model for Japanese PAS that induces multi-predicate interactions from word sequences without syntactic input and outperforms parser-based SOTA, especially on zero pronoun resolution.

- Contribution: Introduces a Grid-RNN model that attains state-of-the-art PAS performance without relying on syntactic parsers, avoiding error propagation.

- Contribution: Achieves a 3-point F-measure improvement on zero pronoun resolution (47.12% vs 44.09% from prior SOTA).

- Contribution: Provides comprehensive experiments and ablation studies showing the importance of multi-predicate interactions, network depth, and residual connections.

- Strength: Methodologically significant advance by removing dependence on syntactic parsers and directly learning from word sequences.

- Strength: Notable gains on the challenging zero pronoun task, demonstrating effectiveness of modeling multi-predicate interactions.

- Strength: Thorough, well-structured experimental evaluation with detailed ablations and strong per-case improvements (e.g., large gain on dative case).

- Weakness: Several minor grammatical errors and typos that require correction.

- Weakness: Ambiguity about the meaning/indication of boldface in Table 2.

- Weakness: Insufficient clarity on how the PRED-context feature is extracted and applied, particularly for the Figure 6 accusative-marker example.

- Question: Please clarify what the boldface in Table 2 denotes.

- Question: Please explain how the PRED-context feature is constructed and used for the Figure 6 example where the accusative marker is bundled with the verb.

- Additional comment: Paper is overall well-written with reproducible implementation details; main issues are minor clarifications and fixes before publication.
