- Proposes neural sequence models for Japanese PAS that avoid reliance on parser-derived syntactic information.  
- Extends a single-sequence SRL-style model to a multi-sequence Grid-RNN to capture interactions among multiple predicates.  
- Evaluated on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 and reports state-of-the-art results without syntactic features.  

- Main claim: achieves SOTA Japanese PAS performance without parser features.  
- Introduces multi-sequence/Grid-RNN modeling to improve multi-predicate interaction modeling, helping zero argument identification.  
- Shows neural models can learn contextual information from raw word sequences, reducing need for engineered syntactic features.  

- Strength: removes parser-dependence, addressing error propagation from syntactic parsers.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation with baselines, ablations (network depth), and per-role analyses.  
- Strength: notable gains on zero argument identification (≈1.0 F over single-sequence, ≈3.0 F over Ouchi et al. 2015).  

- Weakness: novelty is limited—mainly an extension of Zhou & Xu (2015) rather than a fundamentally new method.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), an important recent related work.  
- Weakness: multi-predicate interaction gains are marginal (≈0.27 F) compared to single-sequence, smaller than gains reported by Ouchi et al. (2015).  

- Q: How does the model compare to Shibata et al. (2016) and why was it omitted?  
- Q: Is a 0.27 F improvement sufficient to justify the added Grid-RNN complexity and computational cost?  
- Q: Why are multi-predicate interaction gains smaller than Ouchi et al. (2015)—corpus, experimental setup, or modeling differences?
