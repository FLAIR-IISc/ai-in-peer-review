- Proposes a Grid-RNN that models interactions among all predicates for Japanese PAS using word sequences without explicit syntax.  
- Introduces single-sequence BiRNN baseline and multi-sequence Grid-RNN; multi-seq jointly predicts arguments for all predicates and performs best.  
- Achieves new state-of-the-art on NAIST 1.5, with notable gains on zero pronouns (~+3 F over Ouchi et al., ~+1 over single-seq).  
- Provides strong evidence that syntax-free sequence models can outperform syntax-dependent PAS systems, supported by thorough ablations (depth, residuals).  
- Grid-RNN effectively captures multi-predicate interactions, yielding consistent, statistically significant improvements over single-seq.  
- Experimental setup is clear and replicable: implementation details, hyperparameters, significance testing, and per-case-role analyses provided.  
- Uses sound deep sequence modeling practices (stacked BiRNNs/GRUs, residual connections); deeper models with residuals give best results.  
- Presentation issues: numerous minor typos, grammar slips, and occasional notational inconsistencies that need correction.  
- Table 2 caption unclear: boldface meaning (best-in-column, per-model, or overall) must be specified.  
- PRED-context exposition is insufficient; an illustration showing PRED window capturing accusative markers would help.
