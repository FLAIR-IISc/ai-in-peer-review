- Proposes Grid-RNN multi-sequence architecture to model interactions among multiple predicates without explicit syntactic parses.
- Presents a single-sequence BiRNN baseline that ignores cross-predicate interactions.
- Claims state-of-the-art results on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5, with notable improvement on zero-pronoun resolution.
- Contribution: multi-sequence Grid-RNN captures multi-predicate interactions directly from word sequences, outperforming parser-based systems.
- Contribution: thorough empirical results on NAIST 1.5, including gains on zero arguments and analysis of depth/residual connections.
- Contribution: clear, reproducible setup with public code and detailed hyperparameters, and careful comparisons to prior baselines.
- Strength: achieves SOTA accuracy (81.42 F1) without syntactic features, avoiding parser error propagation.
- Strength: substantial improvements on zero pronouns (47.12 F1 vs 44.09 prior work), showing sentence-level context helps zero anaphora.
- Strength: multi-sequence Grid-RNN outperforms single-sequence, demonstrating benefits of cross-predicate signals.
- Strength: ablation over depth (2–8 layers) and residual connections shows optimization benefits and ~+1 F1 at 8 layers.
- Strength: model description, training details, and code availability make the work reproducible.
- Weakness: limited novelty in the modeling primitive—Grid-/multidimensional RNNs are existing; novelty is primarily the application to PAS.
- Weakness: scope limited to intra-sentential arguments; inter-sentential zero anaphora is excluded and not deeply discussed.
- Weakness: limited interpretability and qualitative analysis—needs visualizations/error analysis showing information flow in the grid.
- Weakness: computational complexity and memory scaling with sentence length, predicate count, and depth are not reported.
- Weakness: presentation issues and typos reduce polish; some formatting and table conventions are unclear.
- Question: clarify what boldface in Table 2 indicates (per-configuration, per-depth, etc.).
- Question: how do training/inference time scale with number of predicates M and sentence length T; any batching strategies or practical limits?
- Question: are target predicates, word boundaries, and tokenization gold or predicted; any differences from Ouchi+15 that affect comparability?
- Question: did authors try alternative predicate-conditioning methods (predicate embeddings at all positions, attention from candidates to predicates)?
- Question: how do performance and runtime behave for many-predicate sentences; any heuristics for predicate-dense sentences?
- Suggestion: add an illustrative example (e.g., Fig. 6 augmentation) showing how PRED window captures case markers and how signals propagate across the grid.
- Style/typo corrections: consistent hyphenation, spelling fixes (state-of-the-art, significance test), grammar fixes, consistent naming/formatting for Grid-RNN/Bi-RNN, and reference formatting.
- After author response: reviewer satisfied; clarifications addressed presentation questions and reinforced empirical claims.
