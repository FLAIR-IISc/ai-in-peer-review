- Tackles Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) without predicted syntax, using a syntax-free BiRNN and a multi-predicate Grid-RNN extension.

- Empirical result: new SOTA on NAIST 1.5 — single model overall F 81.15 (zero F 46.10) vs prior 79.23/44.09.

- Grid-RNN further improves performance (overall F 81.42, zero F 47.12), with gains statistically significant, especially for zero arguments.

- Contribution: strong empirical demonstration that syntax-free end-to-end models can beat syntax-heavy baselines on Japanese PAS.

- Contribution: joint multi-predicate modeling via a Grid-RNN captures cross-predicate signals without explicit syntactic features.

- Contribution: reports useful architectural/training insights (residual connections, increased depth), showing their positive impact.

- Strength: clear empirical superiority without syntactic features, with meaningful gains on zero anaphora.

- Strength: Grid-RNN improvements concentrated on zero arguments, supporting the multi-predicate interaction hypothesis.

- Strength: sensible adaptation of prior neural SRL work (predicate-centric features, shared BiGRUs, softmax for dependency PAS).

- Strength: thorough experimental setup — standard NAIST splits, bootstrap significance testing, detailed breakdowns by Dep/Zero and case roles.

- Strength: paper is clear and well-organized with implementation details aiding reproducibility.

- Weakness: methodological novelty is limited; core components (BiGRU tagging, residuals) are standard and the grid is a straightforward extension.

- Weakness: overall gains from Grid-RNN are modest (+0.27 overall F; +1.02 Zero; +0.07 Dep), suggesting most improvement comes from a stronger single-sequence model.

- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to recent neural PAS/SRL baselines (e.g., Shibata et al., Iida et al.), weakening SOTA claims.

- Weakness: limited analysis of what cross-predicate cues the Grid-RNN captures; no qualitative/probing/error analysis presented.

- Weakness: scope limited to intra-sentential arguments; inter-sentential (Inter-Zero) handling is not addressed.

- Question: clarify comparability to Shibata et al. (2016) — datasets/splits differences or bridging experiments?

- Question: sensitivity analysis requested for predicate window size C, embedding dims, and GRU hidden size.

- Question: report computational overhead of Grid-RNN vs single-sequence (training time, memory) as a function of number of predicates and sentence length.

- Question: did you try adding simple syntax-aware signals (gold/predicted heads/distances) to the single-sequence model for comparison?

- Question: provide qualitative examples where Grid-RNN corrects single-sequence errors to illustrate captured cross-predicate info.

- Question: did pretrained embeddings from large Japanese corpora improve zero-argument performance; if not tried, discuss expected impact.

- Additional comment: release code and trained models (placeholder URL should be updated) to aid reproducibility.

- Additional comment: consider reporting performance by sentence length and number of predicates to substantiate long-distance/context claims.
