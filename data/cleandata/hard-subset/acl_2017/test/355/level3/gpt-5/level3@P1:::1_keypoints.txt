- Tackles Japanese PAS focusing on intra-sentential (dependency-linked and zero) arguments with two syntax-free neural sequence models.
- Proposes a single-sequence BiRNN per-predicate encoder and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN that jointly models all predicates in a sentence.
- Grid-RNN achieves new state-of-the-art on NAIST 1.5 without syntactic features, with notable gains on zero arguments.
- Paper includes ablations on network depth and residual connections, per-case-role results, and statistical significance testing.
- Contribution: a grid-style multi-sequence RNN that captures multi-predicate interactions from word sequences, avoiding predicted syntax and error propagation.
- Contribution: strong empirical evidence that syntax-free models can outperform syntax-dependent SOTA for Japanese PAS, especially for zero pronouns.
- Contribution: practical modeling insights (ARG/PRED/MARK features, residual connections, benefits of depth when training end-to-end).
- Strength: significant impact—demonstrates sequence-based, syntax-free approach surpasses strong baselines and improves zero-pronoun resolution.
- Strength: clean multi-predicate interaction modeling via Grid-RNN and effective use of residual connections to train deeper grids.
- Strength: solid experimental design and reporting (clear splits, no external resources, hyperparameter ranges, bootstrap significance, per-role breakdown).
- Strength: clear, reproducible architecture description and contextualization with related SRL work.
- Weakness: scope limited to intra-sentential arguments; unclear scalability to inter-sentential zero anaphora and document-level grids.
- Weakness: missing ablations isolating contributions of ARG vs PRED vs MARK features and the PRED window size C.
- Weakness: unclear whether decoding enforces structural constraints (e.g., unique NOM/ACC/DAT) or predicts labels independently per token.
- Weakness: minor clarity issues (unexplained boldface in Table 2, figure captions, and several typos/wording inconsistencies).
- Questions for authors: clarify Table 2 boldface; state whether case assignments are globally constrained at decoding; report computational complexity w.r.t. sentence length T and predicates M; provide feature ablation results.
- Suggestions: augment visualization of PRED-context feature, fix listed typos/style issues, state F-measure averaging method, report confidence intervals, and add a reproducibility checklist if releasing code.
- Overall assessment: strong, practically impactful paper with convincing empirical gains and minor addressable weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
