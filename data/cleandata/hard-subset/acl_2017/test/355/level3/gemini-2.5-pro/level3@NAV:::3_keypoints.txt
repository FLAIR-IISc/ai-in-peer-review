- Proposal: Joint neural Grid-RNN model for Japanese PAS that models predicate interactions from word sequences and achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus.
- Strength: Strong empirical results, notably large gains on zero-pronoun argument identification, establishing a new benchmark.
- Strength: Achieves high performance without syntactic parser input, avoiding upstream error propagation.
- Strength: Paper is well-written and accessible with clear figures illustrating architectures.
- Weakness: Lacks deeper analysis explaining why the joint model outperforms single-sequence baselines; qualitative comparisons (Single-Seq vs Multi-Seq) are missing.
- Weakness: Insufficient task context—needs discussion of NOM/ACC ambiguity, zero-pronoun resolution challenges, and stats on intra-sentential resolvability.
- Weakness: No analysis of whether the model learns or violates linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per role) implicitly.
- Weakness: Presentation issues with frequent minor typos and inconsistent capitalization.
- Question: Clarify representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns in the data (no label vs explicit empty marker) and the implications.
- Question: Provide analysis of how often the model violates linguistic constraints, such as assigning multiple fillers to the same argument slot.
