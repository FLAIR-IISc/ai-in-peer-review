- Paper proposes neural models for Japanese PAS: a single-sequence stacked BiRNN and a multi-sequence Grid-RNN that jointly models all predicates to capture predicate interactions.  
- Grid-RNN effectively models predicate-predicate dependencies and achieves state-of-the-art PAS performance without syntactic features, especially improving zero-pronoun identification.  
- Main contributions: novel application of Grid-RNNs for multi-predicate interactions, demonstrating SOTA PAS without syntactic parsers, and a clear, reproducible empirical study.  
- Strength: well-motivated, sophisticated multi-sequence/Grid-RNN architecture that allows information flow between predicate-specific sequences.  
- Strength: impressive empirical gains on NAIST Text Corpus, notably +3.03 F1 for zero arguments over prior work.  
- Strength: paper clarity—models, features, and experiments are described in detail with helpful figures and tables.  
- Weakness: minor presentation issues—typographical and grammatical errors require a final proofreading pass.  
- Question addressed: boldface in Table 2 indicates best-performing configurations for Single-Seq and Multi-Seq models; reviewer is satisfied with the clarification.
