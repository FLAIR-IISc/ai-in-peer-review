- Proposes a Grid-RNN multi-sequence neural model for Japanese PAS that learns from word sequences without explicit syntax and targets zero (dropped) arguments.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, notably improving zero-argument identification.  
- Contribution: shows a purely sequence-based, syntax-free model can match or exceed syntax-dependent PAS systems, avoiding parser error propagation.  
- Contribution: demonstrates multi-predicate interaction modeling via Grid-RNN is especially effective for zero pronoun resolution in pro-drop languages.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance that challenges the necessity of explicit syntactic features for PAS.  
- Strength: clear focus and significant gains on the difficult task of zero pronoun identification.  
- Strength: well-written, detailed presentation with reproducible model and experimental descriptions.  
- Weakness: manuscript has typographical errors and minor English fluency issues needing proofreading.  
- Weakness: PRED-context feature explanation is insufficient and could be clarified using the Figure 6 example.  
- Weakness: meaning of boldface in Table 2 is not explained and should be clarified.
