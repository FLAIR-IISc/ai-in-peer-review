- Presents single-sequence Bi-RNN and multi-sequence Grid-RNN models for Japanese PAS analysis, focusing on zero pronoun (dropped argument) resolution and evaluated on the NAIST Text Corpus without syntactic parsers.  
- Shows the multi-sequence Grid-RNN achieves new state-of-the-art performance, especially improving zero-argument resolution.  
- Main contribution: demonstrates state-of-the-art PAS performance without explicit syntactic features, avoiding parser error propagation.  
- Main contribution: effectively models multi-predicate interactions with Grid-RNN, yielding significant gains on zero pronouns.  
- Main contribution: clear, detailed model and experimental descriptions with intent to release code, making the work highly reproducible.  
- Strength: multi-sequence model outperforms prior state-of-the-art by >2 F1 (overall) and >3 F1 on zero arguments.  
- Strength: targeted, concrete improvements on the challenging zero-argument subtask, validating the multi-predicate interaction approach.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and thoroughly analyzed, with strong baselines and insightful breakdowns.  
- Weakness: minor English fluency issues and typos that detract from polish; proofreading recommended.  
- Weakness: explanation/illustration of the PRED input feature is suboptimal and could use a clearer example (e.g., Figure 6 predicate).  
- Question: Table 2 contains unexplained boldfaced numbers—clarification of their meaning is requested.  
- Additional comments: lists specific typographical corrections and wording improvements throughout the paper.
