- Paper proposes sequence-based neural models (single-sequence Bi-RNN and multi-sequence Grid-RNN) for Japanese PAS, outperforming syntax-based SOTA, especially on zero arguments.
- Contribution: Introduces a Grid-RNN architecture to jointly model all predicates in a sentence without using syntactic parsers.
- Contribution: Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance without syntactic features, with notable gains on zero pronoun identification.
- Strength: Elegant, principled Grid-RNN creating a 2D grid over words and predicates to capture multi-predicate interactions.
- Strength: Strong empirical results on the NAIST Text Corpus, simplifying the pipeline by removing parser dependencies (+3 F1 on zero arguments).
- Strength: Clear, detailed presentation, thorough experiments, ablations, and public code release supporting reproducibility.
- Weakness: Several minor typographical and awkward English phrasings throughout the paper.
- Weakness: Table 2 uses bolded F-measure scores without explanation, causing confusion about what is being highlighted.
- Weakness: Missed opportunity to better illustrate the PRED feature with a concrete example showing how local context aids argument identification.
- Question for authors: Please clarify the criterion for boldface in Table 2.
- Additional comments: Reviewer provides a list of specific typographical and phrasing corrections to fix in the final version.
