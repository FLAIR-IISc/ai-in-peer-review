- Proposes end-to-end neural models for Japanese PAS that operate on word sequences, avoiding external syntactic features.  
- Introduces Single-Sequence (per-predicate BiRNN) and Multi-Sequence (Grid-RNN joint modeling) architectures.  
- Claims Grid-RNN captures multi-predicate interactions and improves zero-pronoun argument resolution.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus, with the Multi-Sequence model performing best.  
- Novel application of Grid-RNNs for joint multi-predicate PAS analysis without a syntactic pipeline.  
- Demonstrates empirical gains over prior syntax-based joint models, especially on zero arguments.  
- Strong empirical results: >2 F1 overall improvement and ~3 F1 on Zero arguments compared to Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Elegant, principled architecture that avoids parser error propagation and provides a clear Single→Multi comparison.  
- Thorough experiments using standard splits, depth analysis, residual connections, and per-role breakdowns.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with effective illustrative figures.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis explaining why Multi-Sequence outperforms Single-Sequence; needs error comparisons and examples.  
- Model does not enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., at most one argument per case role); no analysis of invalid predictions.  
- Insufficient context on task scope: missing corpus statistics on intra- vs. inter-sentential zero arguments and handling of Inter-Zero in training.  
- Asks authors to report how often multiple arguments are predicted for the same case role and analyze those cases.  
- Requests corpus statistics on zero-pronoun distribution and clarification on labeling/handling of inter-sentential zeros.  
- Requests a concrete example showing a Zero NOM correctly recovered by Multi-Seq but missed by Single-Seq, with analysis of the interaction.  
- Minor language and formatting corrections suggested (abstract wording, typos in sections, and inconsistent reference formatting).
