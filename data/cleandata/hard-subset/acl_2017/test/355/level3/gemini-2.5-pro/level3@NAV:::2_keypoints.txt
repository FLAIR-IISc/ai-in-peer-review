- Presents two neural models for Japanese PAS: an adapted end-to-end RNN baseline and a Grid-RNN multi-sequence extension to model predicate interactions.
- Achieves new state-of-the-art on the NAIST Text Corpus without syntactic features (overall F1 from 79.23 to 81.42).
- Main contribution: shows a syntax-free end-to-end neural model can outperform prior syntax-dependent systems for Japanese PAS.
- Main technical proposal: applies Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate interactions, yielding modest gains, especially for zero-argument identification.
- Strength: substantial empirical improvement over prior SOTA (Ouchi et al., 2015) on a standard benchmark.
- Strength: well-structured, clearly motivated progression from single-sequence to multi-sequence modeling addressing zero anaphora.
- Strength: eliminates reliance on syntactic parsers, avoiding pipeline error propagation and simplifying the system.
- Weakness: omits comparison to Shibata et al. (2016), a relevant neural baseline, which is a critical oversight.
- Weakness: Grid-RNN yields only marginal improvement over the strong single-sequence baseline (81.42 vs. 81.15 F1), questioning practical impact versus complexity.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty since most gains stem from applying an existing architecture (Zhou & Xu, 2015) rather than introducing a fundamentally new technique.
