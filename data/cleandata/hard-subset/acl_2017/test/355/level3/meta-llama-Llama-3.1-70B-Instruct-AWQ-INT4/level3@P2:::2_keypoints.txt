- Extends single-sequence PAS model to capture multi-predicate interactions using Grid-RNNs.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 benchmark.  
- Demonstrates improved handling of long-distance dependencies, notably better zero-argument detection.  

- Paper is well-structured, well-motivated, and surveys related work thoroughly.  
- Shows significant accuracy gains over the previous state-of-the-art.  
- Grid-RNN variant yields additional gains, mainly by improving zero-argument detection.  
- Effective use of residual connections improves deeper model performance.  

- No comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), limiting assessment of novelty.  
- Reported impact of modeling predicate interactions is smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).  
- Method’s novelty is limited—primarily an extension from single- to multi-sequential modeling.  

- Request a detailed comparison with Shibata et al. (2016) and clarification of differences.  
- Ask how authors will address perceived limited novelty and what future directions are planned.  
- Ask for more insight/quantification of the impact of predicate-interaction modeling versus Ouchi et al. (2015).  

- Paper is well-written with clear model/component explanations.  
- Use of Grid-RNNs is interesting and effective for multi-predicate interactions.  
- Paper would benefit from more visualizations (diagrams/plots) of architecture and results.
