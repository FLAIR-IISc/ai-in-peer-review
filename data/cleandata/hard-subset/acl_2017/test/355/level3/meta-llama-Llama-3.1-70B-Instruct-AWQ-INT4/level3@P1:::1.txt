**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural model for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis, which automatically induces features sensitive to multi-predicate interactions from word sequence information of a sentence. The model uses grid-type recurrent neural networks (Grid-RNNs) to capture the interactions between multiple predicates in a sentence, without relying on explicit syntactic information. The authors evaluate their model on the NAIST Text Corpus and demonstrate that it outperforms state-of-the-art systems that use syntactic information.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Novel Application of Grid-RNNs**: The paper proposes a sophisticated neural application of Grid-RNNs for Japanese PAS prediction, which effectively captures multi-predicate interactions without explicit syntactic information.
2. **State-of-the-Art Performance**: The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus, outperforming systems that use syntactic information.
3. **Detailed Description and Experimental Results**: The paper provides a clear and detailed description of the implementation and experimental results, making it easy to follow and reproduce the work.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Modeling of Multi-Predicate Interactions**: The Grid-RNN model effectively captures multi-predicate interactions, which is crucial for Japanese PAS analysis.
2. **Robust Performance on Zero Pronouns**: The multi-sequence model achieves the best performance on zero pronouns, which are frequent and challenging in Japanese.
3. **Clear and Detailed Description**: The paper provides a well-written and detailed description of the implementation and experimental results.
