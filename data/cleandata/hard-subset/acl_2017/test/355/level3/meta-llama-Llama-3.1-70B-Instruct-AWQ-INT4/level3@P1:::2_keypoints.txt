- Summary: Proposes a neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs to capture multi-predicate argument interactions and reports SOTA gains on the NAIST corpus.
- Contribution 1: Adapts Zhou & Xu (2015) single-sequential model to Japanese and extends it to multi-sequential modeling with Grid-RNNs.
- Contribution 2: Achieves significantly better performance than prior SOTA on the NAIST Text Corpus.
- Contribution 3: Grid-RNN variant yields additional gains, particularly improving zero-argument detection.
- Strength 1: Paper is well-structured, well-motivated, and easy to follow.
- Strength 2: Demonstrates clear empirical improvement over previous SOTA.
- Strength 3: Effectively uses Grid-RNNs to model interactions among predicate arguments.
- Weakness 1: No comparison with Shibata et al. (2016), which is a relevant neural extension of prior work.
- Weakness 2: Reported impact of modeling predicate interactions is smaller than in Ouchi et al. (2015).
- Weakness 3: Methodological novelty is limited—mainly an extension from single- to multi-sequential modeling.
- Question 1: Please provide a detailed comparison with Shibata et al. (2016) and explain why this model outperforms it.
- Question 2: How will the authors address the limited novelty and what future directions will improve the model?
- Question 3: Can the authors clarify why the impact of modeling predicate interactions is smaller than reported in Ouchi et al. (2015)?
