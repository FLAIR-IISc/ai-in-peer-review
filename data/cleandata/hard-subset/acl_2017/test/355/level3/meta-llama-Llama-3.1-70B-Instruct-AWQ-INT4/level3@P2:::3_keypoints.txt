- Proposes a novel Grid-RNN architecture for Japanese PAS that models multi-predicate interactions.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on NAIST Text Corpus 1.5 without using syntactic information.  
- Provides analysis of model performance, comparing to baselines and studying network depth and residual connections.  

- Solid execution with SOTA results on a well-established Japanese PAS task.  
- Clear, accessible presentation with helpful architecture figures.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness of the architecture in capturing multi-predicate interactions.  

- Lacks detailed discussion of task complexity, including NOM vs ACC ambiguity and zero-pronoun contrast with SRL.  
- No statistics on what proportion of zero pronouns are intra-sententially resolvable.  
- Unclear representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns and implications (no marking vs explicit empty argument).  
- Model does not enforce linguistic constraints and lacks analysis of whether it learns constraints (one argument per type per predicate; argument fills only one slot).  

- Request statistics on proportion of zero pronouns that are intra-sententially resolvable.  
- Ask how inter-sentential zero pronouns are represented and implications of that choice.  
- Ask for analysis of how well the model learns linguistic constraints (argument-slot and uniqueness constraints).  
- Request comparative output analysis between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq models to identify systematic differences and contexts.  

- Presentation is generally good but contains minor language/typographical issues needing correction.  
- Including SRL results would be valuable though it requires additional work.  
- Paper needs more detailed discussion of relative differences and contexts of improvement between Single-Seq and Multi-Seq models, especially for NOM.
