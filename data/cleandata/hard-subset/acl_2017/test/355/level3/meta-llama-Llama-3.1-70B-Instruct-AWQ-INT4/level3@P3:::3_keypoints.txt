- Proposes a joint neural model for Japanese PAS using Grid-RNNs that captures multi-predicate interactions.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on a standard Japanese PAS benchmark.  
- Compares the multi-sequence Grid-RNN to a single-sequence RNN and shows the multi-sequence model outperforms it and baselines.  
- Presentation is clear and accessible to non-Japanese specialists, with helpful explanatory figures.  
- The paper should emphasize task complexity more, especially NOM vs ACC ambiguity and differences from SRL including zero-pronoun ambiguity.  
- Need to report the proportion of zero pronouns that are intra-sententially resolvable to clarify task difficulty and results interpretation.  
- Representation of inter-sentential zero pronouns is unclear—authors should state whether they use explicit empty arguments or no marking and discuss implications.  
- The model appears not to enforce linguistic constraints (e.g., one argument per type per predicate); analyze how well such constraints are learned and their effects.  
- A comparative output analysis between single-sequence and multi-sequence models is requested to reveal systematic differences and contextual strengths/weaknesses.  
- Table 3 indicates most gains are on NOM cases; the authors should discuss the relative differences and contexts driving this improvement.  
- Including SRL results would be useful for comparison, though acknowledged as extra work.  
- Paper has minor language/typographical issues (examples provided) that should be corrected.
