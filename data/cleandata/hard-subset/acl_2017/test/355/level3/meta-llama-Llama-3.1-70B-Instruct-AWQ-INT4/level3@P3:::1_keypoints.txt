- Proposes a Grid-RNN neural model that captures multi-predicate interactions for Japanese PAS prediction.  
- Model induces interaction-sensitive features from word sequences without relying on explicit syntactic structure.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the NAIST Text Corpus despite not using syntactic information.  
- Multi-sequence model significantly improves prediction of zero (dropped) arguments compared to single-sequence models.  
- Sophisticated neural architecture effectively models multi-predicate interactions.  
- Implementation and experimental setup are clearly and thoroughly described, aiding reproducibility.  
- Strong, focused improvement on the challenging problem of dropped arguments (zero pronouns).
