- Proposes a Grid-RNN model for Japanese predicate-argument structure (PAS) analysis that captures multi-predicate interactions without explicit syntax and outperforms syntactic baselines on NAIST Text Corpus.
- Introduces Grid-RNNs as a novel application to Japanese PAS analysis to model multi-predicate interactions.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the NAIST Text Corpus without using explicit syntactic structure.
- Shows significant improvement in identifying zero pronouns, a difficult aspect of Japanese PAS.
- Grid-RNN architecture effectively captures multi-predicate interactions, improving analytical performance.
- Model is robust to syntactic errors by not relying on explicit syntactic information.
- Empirical results demonstrate state-of-the-art effectiveness on the evaluated dataset.
