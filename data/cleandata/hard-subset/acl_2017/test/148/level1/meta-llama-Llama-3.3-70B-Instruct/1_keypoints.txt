- Paper analyzes RC datasets using two metric classes: prerequisite skills and readability.
- Contribution: adopts two evaluation metric classes for comprehensive dataset analysis.
- Contribution: refines prerequisite skills into 13 categories (e.g., object tracking, math reasoning, coreference).
- Contribution: annotates six RC datasets with these metrics and makes results publicly available.
- Strength: thorough dual-metric analysis revealing dataset characteristics.
- Strength: refined skill taxonomy can guide development of more effective RC systems.
- Strength: public release of annotated datasets facilitates future research.
- Weakness: only six datasets annotated, limiting representativeness.
- Weakness: potential annotation bias from relying on human selection and labeling.
- Weakness: unclear methodology for evaluating competence in selecting necessary sentences.
- Question: how will authors address annotation bias and ensure reliability of annotations?
- Question: provide more details on sentence distance calculation and its link to sentence-selection competence.
- Question: how will this analysis be used to build a system applicable across multiple datasets, and what challenges are expected?
