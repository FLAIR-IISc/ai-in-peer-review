- Proposes two complementary metric classes for RC datasets: a refined prerequisite-skill taxonomy and readability metrics computed on sentences needed to answer.  
- Annotates six diverse RC datasets and analyzes correlations between skill counts and readability.

- Contribution: Empirical cross-dataset finding of weak correlation between question difficulty (skill counts) and text readability, with dataset-specific qualitative observations.  
- Contribution: Refines a theoretically grounded skill taxonomy (bridging, elaboration, ellipsis, meta-knowledge) linking textual entailment and psycholinguistics.  
- Contribution: Commits to releasing annotations and analyses to aid dataset design and system development.

- Strength: Strong cross-disciplinary grounding that increases interpretability and reuse of the skill taxonomy.  
- Strength: Broad empirical coverage across six datasets and genres, yielding informative per-dataset observations.  
- Strength: A unified annotation protocol enabling comparison across MC, cloze, and span-extraction formats, plus analysis of sentence number/distance.  
- Strength: Reproducibility commitment through planned release of annotations and analyses.  
- Strength: Practical insight that readability metrics alone are poor proxies for question difficulty.

- Weakness: Limited reliability reporting — small sample sizes, percent agreement only, and no per-skill kappa or confidence intervals.  
- Weakness: Annotation framing (providing context and answer; pre-selecting sentences) may bias difficulty estimates via hindsight and removes retrieval/selection challenges.  
- Weakness: Unvalidated operationalization of difficulty as raw skill counts, with no weighting, interaction modeling, or validation against human/model performance.  
- Weakness: Correlation analysis is shallow — missing sample sizes, p-values, controls, and possibly omitting discourse/cohesion readability features.  
- Weakness: Heterogeneous sampling and filtering across datasets and subjective “nonsense” labels may bias cross-dataset comparisons.

- Question: Please report per-skill inter-annotator agreement (kappa), adjudication procedure, annotator training/background, time per item, and disagreement examples.  
- Question: Can skill counts be validated against human accuracy/response times or model performance stratified by skill count?  
- Question: Provide exact Pearson coefficients with sample sizes, significance tests, and (if possible) mixed-effects regressions controlling for dataset and document length.  
- Question: Share full annotation guidelines with decision rules and illustrative examples for borderline cases (e.g., idiom vs. metonymy; ellipsis vs. bridging).  
- Question: Will you release per-question skill labels, selected sentences, “nonsense” flags, and code/scripts for readability computation?  
- Question: Have you normalized sentence distance by paragraph/document length or analyzed within-dataset quartiles to address length confounds?
