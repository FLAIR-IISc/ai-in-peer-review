- Paper proposes two metric classes for RC datasets: prerequisite skills and readability features, with annotations on six datasets.
- Main contributions: cross-dataset empirical analysis with new annotations; refined 13-skill taxonomy grounded in theory; public release of annotations.
- Strength: theoretically grounded and clearer taxonomy (bridging vs. elaboration, ellipsis, meta-knowledge).
- Strength: uniform cross-dataset analysis reveals actionable, interpretable differences across datasets.
- Strength: Empirical finding that readability weakly correlates with question difficulty, with illustrative examples.
- Strength: Practical implications for dataset design, curriculum learning, and system diagnostics; annotations released.
- Strength: Reasonable annotation reliability reported (90.1% agreement).
- Weakness: Difficulty operationalization is under-validated; skill count not tested against human or model difficulty measures.
- Weakness: Limited sampling (100 items/dataset) and potential selection biases; no sensitivity analysis or confidence intervals.
- Weakness: Annotation protocol may bias results—annotators saw gold answers and selected supporting sentences before skill labels.
- Weakness: Agreement reporting is insufficient—single overall number, no per-skill kappas, confusion matrices, or adjudication detail.
- Weakness: Readability modeling is narrow (few lexical/syntactic features); limited statistical testing and lack of discourse features.
- Weakness: Dataset inclusion criteria are mixed and exclude some major corpora, weakening generality.
- Question: How were annotators trained, calibrated, and what was the annotation manual and average time per item?
- Question: Please report per-skill agreement metrics, confusion matrices, and disagreement rates on supporting sentences and “nonsense” labels.
- Question: How robust are findings if readability is computed over entire contexts or fixed windows rather than selected sentences?
- Question: Do skill counts or specific skills predict system difficulty (model error rates) or human difficulty measures?
- Question: Were correlations tested within datasets or using mixed-effects models to control for dataset/genre confounds?
- Question: How often are questions answerable without the context (world knowledge or QA overlap), and how were such items handled?
- Overall: Paper fills an important gap with useful annotations and insights but needs stronger validation, reliability reporting, and statistical analyses to increase impact.
