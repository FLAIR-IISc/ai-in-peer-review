- Proposes an RC evaluation framework using two metric classes: prerequisite skills (refined taxonomy) and readability (linguistic features).  
- Annotates six diverse RC datasets and releases the annotations and analyses.  
- Finds weak correlations between readability and question difficulty, suggesting datasets can be easy to read but hard to answer.  
- Contribution: cross-dataset human annotation study producing comparable dataset profiles.  
- Contribution: theoretically grounded refinement of prerequisite skills (e.g., bridging vs. elaboration).  
- Contribution: public resource of annotations to support dataset design and evaluation.  
- Strength: taxonomy grounded in prior theory (Kintsch, McNamara, RTE) with practical distinctions like meta-knowledge and ellipsis.  
- Strength: systematic multi-dataset analysis with high reported annotation agreement (90.1%).  
- Strength: actionable insights for dataset curation, e.g., weak readability–difficulty link and example contrasts (MCTest vs. SQuAD).  
- Strength: diagnostics for dataset quality, including detection of “nonsense” questions and sentence-distance analysis.  
- Strength: open release increases transparency and enables follow-up work.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—incremental extension of prior work (Sugawara et al., 2017) and modest methodological advances.  
- Weakness: small samples (100 questions per dataset) and limited statistical testing; potential confounds not controlled.  
- Weakness: readability metrics are narrow (surface/lexico-syntactic features, FK grade) and omit discourse/pragmatic measures.  
- Weakness: framework excludes sentence selection and answer extraction, so findings may not map directly to end-to-end RC difficulty and may be circular when using annotator-selected sentences.  
- Weakness: annotation reporting incomplete—per-skill agreement, co-occurring skills handling, and “nonsense” adjudication are under-specified.  
- Questions for authors: report per-skill IAA and sentence-selection agreement plus statistical significance/confidence intervals for correlations.  
- Questions for authors: assess sensitivity to computing readability on full passages versus selected sentences.  
- Questions for authors: provide detailed guidelines and borderline examples distinguishing bridging from elaboration.  
- Questions for authors: evaluate whether these metrics predict model performance and can support automatic filtering during dataset construction.
