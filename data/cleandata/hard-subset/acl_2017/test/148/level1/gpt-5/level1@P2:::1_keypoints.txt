- Paper proposes two evaluation metric classes for RC: a refined 13-category prerequisite-skill taxonomy and readability metrics (Vajjala & Meurers features + Flesch–Kincaid).
- Authors annotate 100 questions from six RC datasets with required sentences and skills, claiming weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.
- Main contribution: cross-dataset empirical finding that readability and skill-based difficulty are only weakly correlated.
- Main contribution: a theoretically grounded, reorganized taxonomy separating bridging, elaboration, ellipsis, and meta-knowledge.
- Main contribution: public release of annotations linking questions to required skills and sentences for further research.
- Strength: Clear empirical insight with cross-dataset plots and feature sets, challenging assumptions and giving dataset construction guidelines.
- Strength: Theory-informed skill taxonomy improves interpretability and maps to system capabilities.
- Strength: Comparative profiling highlights dataset-specific artifacts (e.g., QA4MRE bridging/elaboration, WDW ellipsis, MARCO “nonsense” items).
- Strength: Transparent methodology with annotator workflow description and partial reliability check (90.1% raw agreement on 62 questions).
- Strength: Practical discussion on evaluation, proposing dataset staging by readability/skill and analyzing sentence-distance effects.
- Weakness: Difficulty proxy (count of prerequisite skills) is narrow and unvalidated against human difficulty or model performance.
- Weakness: Limited and uneven sampling (100 items/dataset, pre-filters, sampling choices) may bias cross-dataset comparisons.
- Weakness: Insufficient reliability reporting—only raw agreement on a small subset, no per-skill metrics or multi-label reliability coefficients.
- Weakness: Statistical analysis underdeveloped—no CIs/significance tests, multiple-comparison corrections, or robustness checks.
- Weakness: Method excludes retrieval/answer-localization and distractor handling since annotators saw gold answers, limiting claims about overall RC difficulty.
- Weakness: Minor internal inconsistency in reported QA4MRE average skills (text vs. table).
- Question: Provide per-skill inter-annotator agreement (e.g., Krippendorff’s alpha), sentence-selection agreement, annotator training, and adjudication details?
- Question: Do skill counts correlate with human difficulty measures (time/accuracy) or baseline/model error rates?
- Question: What is the availability, format, and licensing of released annotations and will readability extraction code be released?
- Question: How sensitive are conclusions to sampling choices and can you provide bootstrapped CIs for correlations?
- Additional comment: Report statistical significance and CIs, and control for confounds like number/length of required sentences.
- Additional comment: Clarify definition and handling of “nonsense” items and how solvable-without-context cases were treated.
- Additional comment: Further analyze interaction of sentence distance with skill demands for finer insights.
- Overall: Useful, well-grounded analysis with relevant findings; needs stronger validation of the difficulty proxy, expanded reliability/statistical reporting, and clearer scope framing.
