- Proposes a dual-axis evaluation (prerequisite skills + readability) for RC datasets, annotating 100 questions from six datasets with a 13-skill taxonomy and finding weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.
- Contribution: provides an empirical analysis across six diverse RC datasets and publicly releases annotations to aid dataset selection and model evaluation.
- Contribution: introduces a revised 13-skill taxonomy grounded in psychology and textual entailment for fine-grained reasoning analysis.
- Strength: Central finding overturns the assumption that readability determines question difficulty, emphasizing the need to craft complex reasoning chains even with simple language.
- Strength: Framework comprehensively integrates two difficulty dimensions and yields detailed dataset characterizations (e.g., SQuAD: hard-to-read/easy-to-answer; MCTest: easy-to-read/hard-to-answer).
- Strength: Coverage of six diverse datasets increases the generality of conclusions and offers a useful comparative overview.
- Weakness: Limited annotation scale (100 questions per dataset) may not capture full diversity in large datasets, potentially weakening statistical robustness.
- Weakness: Skill labeling is inherently subjective; subtle distinctions (e.g., Bridging vs. Elaboration) and missing details on annotator training/dispute resolution may hurt replicability.
- Weakness: Methodology omits the sentence retrieval/search skill by providing annotators with relevant sentences, so the evaluation misses a key component of real-world RC difficulty.
