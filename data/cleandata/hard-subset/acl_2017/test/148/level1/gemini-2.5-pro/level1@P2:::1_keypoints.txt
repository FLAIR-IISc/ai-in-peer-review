- Paper analyzes six RC datasets along two axes: prerequisite skills (refined 13-skill taxonomy) and readability (10 metrics), annotating 100 questions per dataset.
- Main empirical finding: readability and question difficulty (measured by number of required skills) are only weakly correlated; e.g., SQuAD is "easy-to-answer but hard-to-read," MCTest the opposite.
- Introduces a principled, theory-grounded two-axis framework combining cognitive/linguistic theory with readability metrics for dataset comparison.
- Produces and will release an annotated resource spanning six major RC benchmarks for meta-analyses and curriculum learning.
- Strength: Timely and impactful—shifts focus from dataset scale to what datasets actually measure, with implications for dataset creation and model training.
- Strength: Strong methodological grounding—refines the skill taxonomy (e.g., splitting commonsense into Bridging vs. Elaboration) based on established literature.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation across six diverse datasets and clear presentation of comparative results.
- Weakness: Limited annotation scale (100 questions per dataset) raises concerns about sampling bias and statistical robustness for large corpora.
- Weakness: Subjectivity in skill annotation with insufficient IAA reporting details (unclear metric, per-skill vs. per-question) and ambiguous skill distinctions.
- Weakness: Oversimplifies "question difficulty" by equating it to an unweighted count of skills, ignoring varying intrinsic difficulty of different skills.
- Weakness: Excludes the sentence-selection/span-finding challenge, evaluating only the reasoning step after relevant sentences are identified.
- Question: How was the 90.1% inter-annotator agreement calculated (which metric and at what granularity)?
- Question: Have you considered weighting skills by difficulty (rather than simple counts), and would that change conclusions about dataset difficulty?
- Question: How was the 100-question sample drawn and validated as representative, especially for dense datasets like SQuAD?
