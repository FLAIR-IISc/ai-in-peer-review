- Paper proposes a 2D evaluation framework for RC datasets measuring question difficulty (13 refined prerequisite skills) and text readability (10 linguistic features), and releases annotations.  
- Authors annotate 100 questions from six diverse RC datasets to analyze the relation between readability and reasoning complexity.  
- Main contribution: systematic, comparative empirical analysis showing readability and reasoning complexity are largely orthogonal.  
- Main contribution: a well-motivated, theory-grounded 13-skill taxonomy for RC prerequisite skills.  
- Strength: Addresses the need to look beyond accuracy and disentangle reading difficulty from reasoning difficulty.  
- Strength: Framework is principled and grounded in established psychological and textual-entailment literature.  
- Strength: Broad empirical evaluation across six datasets yields insightful per-dataset findings and practical guidance.  
- Strength: Provides actionable implications for dataset design and model training (e.g., curriculum learning).  
- Weakness: Annotation reliability is unclear—reported 90.1% IAA lacks context and chance-corrected metrics for multi-label coding.  
- Weakness: Equating difficulty with the unweighted number of required skills oversimplifies varying intrinsic complexity of skills.  
- Weakness: Limited sample size (100 per dataset) may not represent large datasets like SQuAD or MS MARCO adequately.  
- Weakness: Omission of influential datasets (e.g., CNN/Daily Mail) weakens the completeness of the landscape analysis.  
- Question: Please clarify the inter-annotator agreement metric and how it was computed for the multi-label task.  
- Question: How reliable was the sentence-selection step (guidelines, IAA) used for skill/readability annotations?  
- Question: Did you consider weighting skills by intrinsic complexity, and could weighted difficulty alter the readability–difficulty correlation?
