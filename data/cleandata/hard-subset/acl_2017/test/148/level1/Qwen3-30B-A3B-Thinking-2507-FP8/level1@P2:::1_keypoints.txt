- Paper proposes two evaluation metric classes for RC—prerequisite skills and readability—refines skills taxonomy, and applies them to six RC datasets, finding weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty.  
- Contribution: a theoretically grounded, refined taxonomy of prerequisite skills for RC based on psychological and textual entailment research.  
- Contribution: comprehensive annotation of six RC datasets, revealing dataset-specific insights about question difficulty vs. readability.  
- Contribution: public release of annotation results to support future dataset construction and system development.  
- Strength: strong theoretical grounding that improves on prior ad-hoc skill categorizations (bridging vs. elaboration; retrieved vs. generated inferences).  
- Strength: important empirical finding showing weak correlation (Pearson r = 0.025–0.416) between readability and question difficulty, exemplified by MCTest.  
- Strength: practical community value from released annotations and dataset-characteristic analyses (e.g., QA4MRE hardest, MS MARCO easiest).  
- Weakness: methodology cannot evaluate competence in selecting necessary sentences, a core RC skill, and this limitation is not adequately explored.  
- Weakness: annotation reliability concerns—90.1% agreement reported but no details on disagreement resolution or handling of problematic "nonsense" questions.  
- Weakness: lack of concrete guidance or examples for applying metrics in practice (e.g., curriculum learning steps).  
- Question: how will future work address the inability to evaluate sentence-selection competence and its impact on interpreting question difficulty?  
- Question: how were "nonsense" questions defined and how were annotator disagreements about nonsense vs. external-knowledge solvable handled?  
- Question: please provide a concrete curriculum-learning scenario detailing dataset sequencing and metric use for stepwise RC system development.  
- Overall: valuable, theoretically grounded contribution with important empirical insights, recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions addressing limitations and application guidance.
