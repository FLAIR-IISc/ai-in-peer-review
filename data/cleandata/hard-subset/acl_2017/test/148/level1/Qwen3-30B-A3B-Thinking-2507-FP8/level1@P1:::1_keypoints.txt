- Empirical demonstration that readability weakly correlates with question difficulty in RC datasets.  
- Theoretical refinement of prerequisite-skill taxonomy based on textual entailment and human text comprehension.  
- Public release of annotations for six RC datasets.  

- Addresses a gap by evaluating what makes RC questions difficult beyond superficial labels.  
- Strong theoretical grounding integrating psychology and textual entailment research.  
- Practical implication: readability and question difficulty are separable—easy-to-read texts can yield hard questions (e.g., MCTest).  

- Insufficient justification showing the 13-skill taxonomy is superior, comprehensive, or non-redundant.  
- Methodology cannot assess systems' ability to select the sentences that must be read, a key RC competence.  
- Lacks concrete guidance on how to apply the metrics for dataset construction or system development.  
- Fails to provide clear solutions for identifying or handling “nonsense” questions in datasets.  

- Request evidence that the 13-skill taxonomy is complete and non-redundant.  
- Ask how to develop metrics more directly relevant to RC systems rather than human readability.  
- How will the authors address evaluating sentence-selection competence—should a separate metric be introduced?
