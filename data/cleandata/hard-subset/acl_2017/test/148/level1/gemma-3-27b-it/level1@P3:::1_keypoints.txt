- Paper analyzes six reading-comprehension datasets using "prerequisite skills" and "readability," arguing these facets aren’t necessarily correlated and offering finer-grained evaluation beyond accuracy.  
- Core contribution: adapts/refines established psychological comprehension models (McNamara & Magliano, Kintsch) to characterize RC datasets.  
- Secondary contribution: publicly releases dataset annotations with the defined metrics and documents the annotation procedure.  
- Well-motivated and clearly written; tackles the important issue of moving evaluation beyond simple accuracy.  

Strengths:
- Addresses a critical gap by providing a more interpretable, granular framework to explain model performance and dataset difficulty.  
- Strong theoretical grounding in established cognitive models and an effort to integrate textual entailment perspectives.  
- Public annotations of six datasets are a valuable community resource and enable follow-up work.  
- Provides detailed dataset analyses and informative tables showing prerequisite skill frequencies and dataset-specific insights.  

Weaknesses:
- Skill annotation is subjective; 90.1% inter-annotator agreement reported but nature of disagreements and potential annotation biases are not detailed.  
- Correlation analysis between readability and skills is limited (uses Pearson correlation) and may miss non-linear relationships.  
- Methodology evaluates comprehension given selected sentences but does not assess systems’ ability to select relevant sentences, a crucial limitation.  
- Discussion of implications for system development (e.g., curriculum learning) is high-level and lacks concrete, actionable examples.  

Questions for authors:
- Please provide a detailed analysis of inter-annotator disagreements, examples, resolution procedures, and steps taken to minimize bias.  
- Were alternative correlation measures or regression models explored beyond Pearson, and what were their results?  
- How might the methodology be extended to evaluate sentence selection as well as comprehension of selected text?  
- Can you give practical steps or an example curriculum-learning strategy using the annotated metrics to prioritize datasets/questions?  

Additional comments:
- Appendix A clearly explains the sampling method.  
- References are comprehensive and relevant.  
- Minor: some long sentences could be shortened for readability.  

Recommendation:
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable contribution with addressable weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
