## Review for "Evaluation Metrics for Reading Comprehension: Prerequisite Skills and Readability"

This paper presents an investigation into the relationship between question difficulty and text readability in reading comprehension (RC) datasets. The authors adopt and refine two classes of metrics – prerequisite skills and readability – and apply them to six existing datasets (QA4MRE, MCTest, SQuAD, WDW, MARCO, NewsQA). Their key finding is a weak correlation between readability and question difficulty, demonstrating that challenging questions can be posed using easily readable text. The authors also provide a publicly available annotation of these datasets using their defined metrics.

The primary contribution of this work lies in the comprehensive annotation of six popular RC datasets using a refined set of prerequisite skills and readability metrics. This annotated data is a valuable resource for the RC community, enabling more nuanced analysis of dataset characteristics and facilitating the development of targeted system improvements. The reorganization of prerequisite skills, grounding them in theories of textual entailment and human text comprehension, is a positive step towards a more theoretically sound framework for evaluating RC tasks. Finally, the empirical demonstration that readability does not directly correlate with question difficulty provides a crucial insight for dataset creation and system evaluation. 

The paper is well-motivated, clearly structured, and benefits from a thorough review of related work. The methodology appears sound, and the results are presented clearly with supporting tables. The discussion section appropriately highlights the implications of the findings and suggests avenues for future research. The choice of datasets provides good coverage of the RC landscape. 

However, the work has a few weaknesses. The annotation process relies on human judgment, and while inter-annotator agreement is reported, the potential for subjective bias remains.  The paper could benefit from a deeper analysis of *why* certain datasets exhibit a weaker correlation between readability and difficulty – is it due to question design, topic complexity, or other factors?  Additionally, while the authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach regarding sentence selection, a more in-depth discussion of this aspect would be beneficial. Finally, the impact of these findings on system development isn't fully fleshed out; concrete examples of how the metrics can be used to guide system design would strengthen the contribution.

**Questions to Authors:**

1. Could you provide more details about the annotation guidelines given to the annotators? Specifically, how were disagreements resolved?
2. You mention that your methodology doesn't evaluate sentence selection. Do you have any plans to incorporate sentence selection evaluation into a future iteration of this work?
3. Could you elaborate on how these metrics could be integrated into a curriculum learning approach for training RC systems, providing a specific example?

**Additional Comments:**

The table numbering appears inconsistent (Table 6 is mentioned but doesn’t exist in the provided text). The formatting of references is slightly inconsistent.
