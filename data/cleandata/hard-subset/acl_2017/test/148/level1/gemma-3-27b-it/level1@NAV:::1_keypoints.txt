- Paper investigates the relationship between question difficulty and text readability in RC datasets using refined prerequisite-skill and readability metrics.
- Authors apply these metrics to six datasets (QA4MRE, MCTest, SQuAD, WDW, MARCO, NewsQA).
- Main finding: readability weakly correlates with question difficulty — hard questions can come from easily readable text.
- Primary contribution: comprehensive, publicly available annotations of six RC datasets with refined prerequisite-skill and readability labels.
- Theoretical contribution: reorganization of prerequisite skills grounded in textual entailment and human text comprehension theories.
- Methodology: well-motivated, clearly structured, sound methods with clear results and supporting tables.
- Dataset choice provides broad coverage of the RC landscape.
- Weakness: annotations rely on human judgment and may suffer from subjective bias despite reported inter-annotator agreement.
- Weakness: paper lacks deeper analysis of why some datasets show weaker readability–difficulty correlation (e.g., question design, topic complexity).
- Weakness: limited discussion of sentence-selection evaluation and its implications.
- Weakness: limited concrete discussion of how findings and metrics would impact system development or guide system design.
- Question: provide more details on annotation guidelines and how annotator disagreements were resolved.
- Question: plans to incorporate sentence-selection evaluation in future work?
- Question: elaborate with a concrete example how these metrics could be used in curriculum learning for RC systems.
- Additional comments: table numbering inconsistency (Table 6 referenced but missing) and minor reference formatting issues.
