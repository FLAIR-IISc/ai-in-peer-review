- Paper studies relationship between question difficulty and text readability in RC datasets using prerequisite-skill and readability metrics.  
- Authors annotate six RC datasets and find weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.  
- Main contribution: systematic application and dataset-level analysis combining prerequisite skills and readability metrics.  
- Contribution: refinement of prerequisite-skill taxonomy grounded in textual entailment and psychology.  
- Contribution: publicly released annotated datasets for future research.  
- Strength: comprehensive comparative analysis across six diverse RC datasets (QA4MRE, MCTest, SQuAD, WDW, MARCO, NewsQA).  
- Strength: theoretically grounded prerequisite-skill classification that includes bridging and elaboration skills.  
- Strength: released annotations enable targeted model training and analysis of skill-specific performance.  
- Strength: clear presentation with tables and figures summarizing skill frequencies, counts, and readability relationships.  
- Weakness: annotation of prerequisite skills is subjective; guidelines and examples are insufficiently detailed.  
- Weakness: chosen readability metrics focus on syntactic/lexical complexity and omit semantic complexity measures.  
- Weakness: paper reports correlation (weak) but does not investigate causal reasons for lack of strong correlation.  
- Weakness: methodology does not evaluate sentence-selection competence, limiting scope of analysis.  
- Question: request for detailed annotation guidelines and handling of ambiguous cases.  
- Question: ask whether steps were taken to mitigate annotator bias in skill interpretation.  
- Question: inquire about plans to evaluate sentence selection or incorporate selection metrics in future work.  
- Question: ask why datasets like MS MARCO showed higher frequencies of “nonsense” questions and implications for dataset quality.  
- Additional note: references, writing, and related work coverage are strong and well-organized.  
- Additional observation: key takeaway that texts can be easy to read but hard to answer has important dataset-design implications.
