- Refines and reorganizes prerequisite skills for RC based on entailment phenomena and human text comprehension.
- Annotates six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics to guide dataset construction and RC system development.
- Provides a thorough analysis that highlights dataset-specific characteristics, strengths, and weaknesses.
- Clearly and concisely explains the prerequisite skills and readability metrics, making methodology and results accessible.
- Discusses implications for RC system development, emphasizing the role of corpus genre and question types.
- Relies on annotations of a limited number of questions per dataset, raising concerns about representativeness.
- Omits a detailed analysis of inter-annotator agreement, undermining confidence in annotation reliability.

- Requests clarification on how annotator consistency was ensured and how discrepancies were handled.
- Asks for more details on the annotation process, including annotation time per question and required annotator expertise.
- Seeks plans for addressing metric limitations and proposed future work to improve RC dataset evaluation.
- Recommends a more detailed related-work comparison, contrasting the proposed metrics with existing evaluation methods.
