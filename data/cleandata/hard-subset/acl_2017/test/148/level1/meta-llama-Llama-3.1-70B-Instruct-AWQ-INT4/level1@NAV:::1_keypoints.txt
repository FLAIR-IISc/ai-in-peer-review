- Paper evaluates RC datasets using two metric classes: prerequisite skills and readability, applied to six datasets (e.g., MCTest, SQuAD).
- Key finding: readability does not directly determine question difficulty — datasets can be easy-to-read but hard-to-answer.

- Contribution: adopts prerequisite-skills and readability metrics to assess RC dataset quality.
- Contribution: revises prior prerequisite-skill taxonomy, aligning knowledge reasoning with entailment phenomena and psychology-based text comprehension.
- Contribution: annotates six existing RC datasets with the revised metrics to guide dataset construction and RC system development.

- Strength: provides a comprehensive comparative analysis of six RC datasets and metric correlations.
- Strength: introduces a novel, more nuanced evaluation approach combining skills and readability.
- Strength: offers implications for RC system design to handle easy-to-read yet difficult-to-answer data.

- Weakness: analysis limited to six datasets, restricting scope.
- Weakness: annotation process is subjective and may introduce annotator bias.
- Weakness: findings may have limited generalizability across other datasets or domains.

- Question: how will the authors mitigate subjectivity in annotations in future work?
- Question: please provide detailed annotation protocols, instructions, and evaluation criteria used.
- Question: how will the study be extended to more datasets/domains and what are the broader implications for RC systems?
