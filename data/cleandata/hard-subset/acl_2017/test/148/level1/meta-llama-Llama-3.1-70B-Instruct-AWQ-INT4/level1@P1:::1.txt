**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a framework for evaluating reading comprehension (RC) datasets using two classes of metrics: prerequisite skills and readability. The authors refine the prerequisite skills for RC, which were previously proposed by Sugawara et al. (2017), and define new skills based on textual entailment and human text comprehension. They also adopt readability metrics from Vajjala and Meurers (2012) to evaluate the difficulty of reading contents in documents. The authors annotate six existing RC datasets with these metrics and demonstrate the characteristics of each dataset. The results show that there is only a weak correlation between the difficulty of questions and the readability of context texts in RC datasets.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Refined prerequisite skills for RC**: The authors refine the prerequisite skills for RC, which were previously proposed by Sugawara et al. (2017), and define new skills based on textual entailment and human text comprehension.
2. **Adoption of readability metrics**: The authors adopt readability metrics from Vajjala and Meurers (2012) to evaluate the difficulty of reading contents in documents.
3. **Annotation of six existing RC datasets**: The authors annotate six existing RC datasets with the refined prerequisite skills and readability metrics, providing a valuable guide for the construction of new datasets and the development of RC systems.

**Strengths**

1. **Comprehensive framework for evaluating RC datasets**: The paper proposes a comprehensive framework for evaluating RC datasets, which considers both the difficulty of questions and the readability of context texts.
2. **Refined prerequisite skills for RC**: The authors refine the prerequisite skills for RC, which provides a more accurate evaluation of the difficulty of questions.
3. **Adoption of readability metrics**: The authors adopt readability metrics from Vajjala and Meurers (2012), which provides a more objective evaluation of the difficulty of reading contents in documents.
4. **Annotation of six existing RC datasets**: The authors annotate six existing RC datasets with the refined prerequisite skills and readability metrics, providing a valuable guide for the construction of new datasets and the development of RC systems.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited annotation**: The authors only annotate 100 questions from each dataset, which may not be representative of the entire dataset.
2. **Subjective annotation**: The annotation process is subjective and may be influenced by the annotators' biases.
3. **Limited evaluation of the methodology**: The authors do not evaluate the effectiveness of their methodology in improving the performance of RC systems.
4. **No comparison with other evaluation metrics**: The authors do not compare their evaluation metrics with other existing metrics, which may provide a more comprehensive evaluation of RC datasets.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to address the limited annotation and subjective annotation issues in future work?

3. How do you plan to evaluate the effectiveness of your methodology in improving the performance of RC systems?
