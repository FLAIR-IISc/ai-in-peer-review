- Proposes a framework for evaluating RC datasets using prerequisite skills and readability metrics.
- Refines prior prerequisite skills and defines new skills based on textual entailment and human text comprehension.
- Adopts Vajjala & Meurers (2012) readability metrics to assess context text difficulty.
- Annotates six RC datasets with these metrics and finds weak correlation between question difficulty and context readability.
- Strength: comprehensive framework that considers both question difficulty and context readability.
- Strength: refined prerequisite skills aim to provide more accurate question difficulty evaluation.
- Strength: uses established readability metrics for more objective assessment of texts.
- Strength: annotated six datasets, offering guidance for dataset construction and RC system development.
- Weakness: only 100 questions annotated per dataset, limiting representativeness.
- Weakness: annotation process is subjective and may reflect annotator bias.
- Weakness: no experiments showing the methodology improves RC system performance.
- Weakness: lacks comparison with other existing evaluation metrics.
- Question: how will limited and subjective annotation issues be addressed in future work?
- Question: please provide more details on how prerequisite skills were refined and new skills defined.
- Question: how will you evaluate whether the methodology improves RC system performance?
- Question: can you compare your metrics with other existing metrics and discuss relative advantages and disadvantages?
