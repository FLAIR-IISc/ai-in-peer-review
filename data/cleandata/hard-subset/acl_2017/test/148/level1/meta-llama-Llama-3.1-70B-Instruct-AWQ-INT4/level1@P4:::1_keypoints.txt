- Summary: The paper proposes and applies two classes of evaluation metrics—prerequisite skills and readability—to six RC datasets and analyzes dataset characteristics and correlations between the metrics.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a comprehensive framework for evaluating RC datasets using prerequisite-skill and readability metrics.

- Contribution 2: Refines the taxonomy of prerequisite skills for RC, adding categories like object tracking, mathematical reasoning, and knowledge reasoning.

- Contribution 3: Annotates six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics and provides detailed analyses of the results.

- Strength 1: Provides thorough dataset analyses that help reveal individual dataset strengths and weaknesses.

- Strength 2: Offers a broadly applicable framework that can be used across various RC tasks.

- Strength 3: Emphasizes the joint importance of prerequisite skills and readability in dataset evaluation.

- Weakness 1: Focusing on skills and readability may ignore other important dataset aspects such as question and answer types.

- Weakness 2: Reliance on human annotators introduces potential subjectivity and variability in annotations.

- Weakness 3: Lacks a clear explanation of how the metrics can directly guide improvements in RC systems.

- Question 1: How will the analysis be used to build a system applicable across multiple datasets?

- Question 2: How will annotator subjectivity and variability be mitigated in the annotation process?

- Question 3: Can you provide concrete examples of how these metrics can identify and drive system improvements?

- Additional comment: The framework is a valuable contribution but requires further work to address limitations and demonstrate practical applications for improving RC systems.
