- Summary: Proposes two evaluation metric classes for RC—refined prerequisite skills (13 skills covering textbase and situation model) and readability—and annotates six datasets, finding readability doesn't directly determine question difficulty.

- Contribution 1: Introduces two classes of evaluation metrics for RC datasets to better characterize dataset properties.

- Contribution 2: Refines and defines 13 prerequisite skills for RC grounded in literature.

- Contribution 3: Annotates six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics, creating a community resource.

- Strength 1: Provides a thorough dataset analysis emphasizing both prerequisite skills and readability.

- Strength 2: Offers a nuanced, literature-based set of prerequisite skills for RC.

- Strength 3: The annotated datasets enable cross-dataset comparison and research use.

- Weakness 1: Annotation methodology is limited (only three annotators; competence in selecting necessary sentences not assessed).

- Weakness 2: Analysis of correlation between skills and readability relies only on Pearson correlation, which may be insufficient.

- Weakness 3: Lacks clear explanation of how proposed metrics can be used to improve RC system development.

- Question 1: How will the analysis be used to construct systems applicable across multiple datasets?

- Question 2: How will annotation limitations (few annotators, sentence-selection competence) be addressed?

- Question 3: How can the proposed metrics be concretely leveraged to improve RC system development and what specific steps are planned?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and a significant contribution but needs more detail on applying the metrics to system development and improving annotation methodology.
