- Strength: The paper attempts to bridge human text readability and machine reading comprehension (RC).
- Main goal unclear: The paper does not clearly articulate its central research question even after multiple readings.
- Scope ambiguous: It is often unclear whether the focus is machine comprehension or human readability.
- Title ambiguous: The title should clarify the paper concerns machine comprehension, not human reading comprehension.
- Human vs. machine conflation: The manuscript repeatedly mixes human reading constructs with machine RC without clear separation or justification.
- Missing literature: Important foundational and recent work in the readability literature is not cited or discussed.
- Readability vs. difficulty conflation: The paper fails to clearly define and distinguish “readability” and “question difficulty” and needs grounding in established readability literature.
- Claims need qualification: Assertions that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty likely depend on modeling choices and feature sets and require robustness analyses.
- Skills section unclear: It’s not clear whether discussed “skills” are for humans or machines, and human-focused citations are used without justification for transfer to RC models.
- Methodology opaque: The process for annotating datasets (automatic vs. human), annotator instructions, counts, and quality control are not described.
- Section 2.2 irrelevant: Theoretical topics in Section 2.2 appear unrelated and should be connected to the research questions or removed/shortened.
- Overambitious scope: The paper tries to answer too many questions simultaneously, weakening its contributions; narrowing and isolating questions is needed.
- Writing issues: The manuscript needs careful proofreading and consistent terminology distinguishing human readability from machine comprehension.
- Recommended fixes: Clarify central question, separate human/machine constructs, provide full methodological transparency, reassess claims across models/features, and expand related work.
