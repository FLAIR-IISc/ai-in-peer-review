- Strength: connects human text readability and machine reading comprehension.
- Objective unclear: core message is not sufficiently communicated even after multiple readings.
- Ambiguity on scope: manuscript is unclear whether it addresses machine comprehension or human readability.
- Missing related work: important prior literature on readability is not cited.
- Section 2.2 contains unrelated theoretical discussion.
- Overambitious scope: pursuing too many questions at once weakens the contribution; questions should be analyzed independently.
- Title ambiguous: should explicitly state focus on machine reading comprehension, not human.
- Empirical claim weak: statement that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty depends on the answer-detection approach/features (e.g., POS/dependency parsing).
- Proofreading issues: significant omissions and poor phrasing throughout the paper.
- Methodological clarity missing: unclear how datasets are “annotated” with readability metrics.
- Conceptual conflation: paper mixes up machine reading comprehension and human reading comprehension, which are distinct areas.
- Terminology caution: “readability of text” is not the same as “difficulty of understanding the content” (see DuBay 2004).
- Ambiguous statements need specification: claims should explicitly note when they refer to machine comprehension.
- Section 3.1 unclear: are the discussed skills for humans or machines, and why cite human studies if the target is machines?
- Annotation details missing: number of labeled questions and whether annotators were instructed to judge from a machine rather than human perspective.
