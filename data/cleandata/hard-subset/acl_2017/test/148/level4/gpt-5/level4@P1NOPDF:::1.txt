- Strengths:
  - The paper brings together two areas: human-oriented text readability and machine reading comprehension.

- Weaknesses:
  - The overall goal is not clear. I read the paper multiple times and still do not understand the main research question or contribution.
  - The paper is highly ambiguous about whether it focuses on machine comprehension or human text readability.
  - Important prior work in the readability field is missing.
  - Section 2.2 digresses into theoretical topics that appear unrelated to the main argument.
  - The paper attempts to answer too many questions at once, which weakens the contribution. Questions such as “Does text readability impact RC datasets?” should be analyzed separately from the discussion of prerequisite skills.

- General Discussion:
  - The title is ambiguous. Please clarify that the focus is machine comprehension, not human reading comprehension, since “reading comprehension” and “readability” are typically used in the human context.
  - You claim that your “dataset analysis suggested that the readability of RC datasets does not directly affect the question difficulty,” but this likely depends on the modeling approach and features used for answer detection (e.g., POS tags or dependency parses). Please qualify this claim.
  - The paper needs careful proofreading; there are notable errors (e.g., page 1: “the question is easy to solve simply look..”).
  - How are datasets “annotated with metrics”? Please explain the procedure in detail: what is being annotated, how, and by whom?
  - The paper repeatedly mixes machine reading comprehension and human reading comprehension. These are related but distinct areas. Please keep the distinctions explicit throughout.
  - “Readability of text” is not the same as “difficulty of reading contents.” See: DuBay, W.H. 2004. The Principles of Readability. Costa Mesa, CA: Impact Information.
  - Please add clearer pointers distinguishing your work from research on the readability of questions for humans; the current presentation is highly ambiguous. For example, on page 1, “These two examples show that the readability of the text does not necessarily correlate with the difficulty of the questions” should specify “for machine comprehension.”
  - Section 3.1: Are the listed skills intended for humans or for machines? If for machines, why are you citing studies on human readers, and how confident are you that those findings transfer to machine models?
  - Annotation details are missing. How many questions did annotators label? Were annotators instructed to judge difficulty with respect to machines rather than humans?
