- Strength: The paper attempts to bridge human text readability and machine reading comprehension.  
- Weakness: The paper’s primary objective is not clearly articulated; central premise remains unclear after multiple readings.  
- Weakness: The manuscript’s focus oscillates between machine comprehension and human readability, creating ambiguity.  
- Weakness: Important prior work on readability is missing from the references.  
- Weakness: Section 2.2 contains tangential theoretical discussion that is not directly relevant.  
- Weakness: The paper tries to address too many questions at once, weakening its overall impact.  
- Weakness/Recommendation: The question of whether text readability affects RC datasets should be examined separately from prerequisite skills.  
- Title issue: The title is unclear and should explicitly indicate the focus on machine comprehension rather than general “reading comprehension” or “readability.”  
- Method caveat: The claim that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty depends on the answer-detection methods/features used (e.g., POS or dependency parse features).  
- Language/editing: The manuscript requires thorough proofreading—there are significant language and incomplete-sentence issues.  
- Method clarity: The process for annotating datasets with “metrics” is not well explained.  
- Conceptual confusion: The paper conflates machine reading comprehension and human reading comprehension, which are related but distinct.  
- Terminology: “Readability of text” is not synonymous with “difficulty of reading contents”; canonical readability literature (e.g., DuBay 2004) should be considered.  
- Presentation: The paper should clearly distinguish findings that apply to machine comprehension from those about human question readability.  
- Scope of cited work: Section 3.1 is unclear about whether discussed skills apply to humans or machines and why human-focused studies are cited for machine tasks.  
- Annotation details missing: The paper does not report how many questions were annotated or whether annotators were instructed to assess questions with machines in mind.
