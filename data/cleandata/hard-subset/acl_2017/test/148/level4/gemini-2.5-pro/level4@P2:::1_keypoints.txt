- Paper proposes a framework evaluating RC datasets along question-answering difficulty (13 prerequisite skills) and context readability (10 standard readability metrics).  
- Method: manually annotate 100 questions from six RC datasets (600 total) with skills and compute readability scores for contexts.  
- Main finding: weak correlation between text readability and skill-based question difficulty, implying easy-to-read texts can pose hard RC questions.  
- Contribution: empirical multi-dataset analysis demonstrating the weak link between readability and question complexity.  
- Contribution: a refined taxonomy of 13 prerequisite skills for analyzing RC question difficulty.  
- Contribution: public release of 600 annotated questions with prerequisite-skill labels across six datasets.  
- Strength: novel synthesis connecting human-centered readability research with machine reading comprehension analysis.  
- Strength: broad, relevant dataset coverage (including SQuAD, MS MARCO, MCTest, QA4MRE) making results generalizable.  
- Strength: clear, actionable conclusion advocating datasets that are simple to read but require complex reasoning and suggesting curriculum-style dataset design.  
- Weakness: ambiguous framing that conflates human cognitive skills with machine comprehension without justifying the mapping to neural models.  
- Weakness: central claim not empirically validated against actual RC model performance; relies on theory-based skill counts rather than system error rates.  
- Weakness: superficial treatment of "readability" lacking deeper engagement with the readability research literature.  
- Question: were annotators instructed to judge skills from a human or machine perspective, and how was consistency ensured?  
- Question: how would the readability vs. difficulty finding change if difficulty were measured by state-of-the-art RC model error rates (e.g., BiDAF, BERT)?  
- Question: consider clarifying in title/framing that the focus is on machine reading comprehension to avoid human-cognition ambiguity.
