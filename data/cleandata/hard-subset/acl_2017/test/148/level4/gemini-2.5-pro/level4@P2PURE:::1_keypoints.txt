- Strength: The paper novelly attempts to connect human-centric text readability with machine reading comprehension.

- Weakness: The central objective and main contributions are not clearly articulated.

- Weakness: The paper ambiguously conflates machine comprehension with human text readability.

- Weakness: The literature review is incomplete, missing significant relevant works on readability.

- Weakness: Section 2.2’s theoretical discussion is disconnected from the main argument and methodology.

- Weakness: The scope is overly broad, addressing too many distinct research questions at once.

- Weakness: The question of whether text readability impacts RC dataset performance is substantial and warrants a separate, in-depth study.

- General: The title is ambiguous and should explicitly state the focus on machine comprehension.

- General: The claim that RC dataset readability does not affect question difficulty is insufficiently qualified and model-dependent.

- General: The manuscript needs careful proofreading to fix grammatical errors and incomplete sentences.

- General: The phrase “datasets were annotated with ‘metrics’” is unclear and needs clarification.

- General: The paper frequently conflates machine and human reading comprehension, undermining clarity.

- General: The paper misunderstands “readability,” equating it with semantic difficulty rather than structural complexity; consult established work (e.g., DuBay 2004).

- General: Authors should explicitly distinguish their work from human readability studies throughout the manuscript.

- General: In Section 3.1, it’s unclear whether “prerequisite skills” refer to humans or machines; if machines, justify citing human-cognition literature.

- General: The experimental annotation methodology lacks key details (e.g., number of questions per annotator and whether annotators judged difficulty from a machine perspective).
