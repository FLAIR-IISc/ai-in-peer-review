- Attempts to link human-focused text readability research with machine reading comprehension, but lacks clarity on its primary research question.
- Novel and worthwhile direction exploring how human readability features affect RC system performance.
- Central objective is unclear, making the paper's narrative and contributions hard to follow.
- Repeatedly conflates human cognition and machine processing, blurring "reading comprehension" and "readability" contexts.
- Literature review is insufficient and misses foundational work on text readability.
- Scope is overly broad—multiple complex questions are attempted at once, diluting focus and depth.
- Section 2.2 contains theoretical discussion that appears disconnected from the experiments and main research question.
- Title is ambiguous and should explicitly indicate focus on machine reading comprehension.
- Conflates readability (surface linguistic features) with content difficulty (semantic complexity); needs clearer definitions and citations.
- Claim that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty is overstated and dependent on model type and features used.
- Authors should consistently state whether claims refer to machine or human comprehension.
- "Prerequisite skills" section mixes human cognitive concepts with machine concerns without justification of transferability.
- Annotation methodology lacks key details (annotator workload and whether instructions targeted machine vs. human perspective).
- Terminology is unclear—e.g., "annotating datasets with metrics" should be clarified (compute vs. annotate).
- Paper requires thorough English proofreading to fix grammatical errors and improve readability.
