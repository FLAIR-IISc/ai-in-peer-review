- Proposes a framework separating question prerequisite skills from passage readability to analyze RC datasets, expanding a prior skill taxonomy and annotating 600 questions across six datasets; finds weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.

- Contribution: a large manual annotation of 600 questions with a 13-category skill taxonomy, to be released as a community resource.

- Contribution: empirical comparative analysis of six datasets (e.g., SQuAD vs MCTest) showing differing readability vs skill complexity profiles.

- Strength: Novel direction decoupling text complexity from question complexity, challenging assumptions linking human text difficulty to RC task difficulty.

- Strength: Significant annotation effort producing a valuable resource that enables analysis by underlying reasoning skills rather than surface question types.

- Strength: Actionable insights (e.g., dataset characterizations, curriculum-learning suggestions) for dataset design and RC system development.

- Weakness: Ambiguous theoretical grounding—unclear whether metrics and analyses target human or machine comprehension, and insufficient justification for applying human readability metrics to neural models.

- Weakness: Overly broad and disjointed scope—taxonomic revision, psychological theory, annotation, analysis, and proposals dilute depth and cohesion.

- Weakness: Readability analysis is superficial and reliant on an older feature set; conclusions about readability vs difficulty are not conditioned on model architecture and may be overstated.

- Q to authors: Clarify target audience and goal—diagnosing current ML models or evaluating difficulty for an abstract reasoning agent—and justify applicability of human readability metrics to models like LSTMs/Transformers.

- Q to authors: Detail annotator instructions—were annotators labeling skills for an ideal logical/machine process or for human cognitive steps?

- Q to authors: How would the reported weak correlation between readability and skill-based difficulty vary across different RC architectures (e.g., TF-IDF baseline vs pre-trained models like BERT)?
