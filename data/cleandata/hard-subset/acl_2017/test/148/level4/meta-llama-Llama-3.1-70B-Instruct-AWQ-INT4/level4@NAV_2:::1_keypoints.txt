- Contributions: Proposes two classes of evaluation metrics (prerequisite skills and readability) for RC, refines prerequisite skills, and annotates six RC datasets.  
- Method: Uses psychology-informed refinements of Sugawara et al.'s skills and Vajjala & Meurers' linguistic readability features.  
- Strength: Bridges human text readability and machine reading comprehension.  
- Strength: Provides a comprehensive multi-metric analysis of existing RC datasets.  
- Strength: Offers practical guidance for building new datasets and developing RC systems.  
- Weakness: Paper goal is unclear and ambiguous about focusing on machine comprehension vs. human readability.  
- Weakness: Omits important prior work in the readability literature.  
- Weakness: Section 2.2 contains unrelated theoretical discussion.  
- Weakness: Attempts to answer too many questions at once, weakening focus.  
- Weakness: Title is ambiguous and should explicitly reference machine comprehension.  
- Weakness: English needs proofreading due to omissions and errors.  
- Weakness: Conflates machine reading comprehension with human reading comprehension without clear distinctions.  
- Weakness: Lacks clear pointers distinguishing the work from human-question readability studies.  
- Question: How were datasets annotated with the proposed metrics—detailed annotation process required?  
- Question: Are prerequisite skills intended for humans or machines, and why use human-focused citations if for machines?  
- Question: How many questions were annotated and were annotators instructed to consider machines vs. humans?  
- Question: What were the inter-annotator agreement scores and how were disagreements resolved?  
- Question: How will this analysis be used to build a system that generalizes across multiple datasets?
