- Paper proposes two classes of evaluation metrics for RC: prerequisite skills and readability.  
- Authors apply these metrics to six RC datasets (including MCTest and SQuAD) and analyze dataset characteristics and correlations.  
- Contribution: formulation of two metric classes for RC datasets.  
- Contribution: refined taxonomy of thirteen prerequisite skills, with knowledge reasoning subdivided by inference types.  
- Contribution: annotated six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics and released results.  
- Strength: bridges human text readability and machine reading comprehension research.  
- Strength: provides comprehensive dataset analysis using the proposed metrics.  
- Strength: emphasizes importance of multi-metric evaluation beyond accuracy.  
- Weakness: overall goal and manuscript are unclear and often ambiguous.  
- Weakness: misses important prior work in readability and includes tangential theoretical discussions.  
- Weakness: attempts to address too many questions, weakening focus and depth.  
- Weakness: conflates human reading comprehension and machine reading comprehension without clear distinction.  
- Question: request for details on the annotation process for assigning metrics to datasets.  
- Question: ask how many items annotators labeled and whether they were instructed to consider machine rather than human readers.  
- Question: ask for clearer distinctions between machine-focused metrics and human readability measures.  
- Question: question the appropriateness of citing human-readability papers in the context of machine comprehension and request justification.  
- Additional: title is ambiguous and should specify machine comprehension of text.  
- Additional: manuscript needs proofreading to fix omissions and improve clarity.  
- Additional: terminology should be more precise (e.g., “readability” vs “difficulty of reading contents”).  
- Additional: paper would benefit from stronger analysis of correlations between proposed metrics and RC system performance.
