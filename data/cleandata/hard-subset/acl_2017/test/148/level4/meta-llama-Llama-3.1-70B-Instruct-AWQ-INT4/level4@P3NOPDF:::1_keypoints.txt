- Strength: Paper attempts an interdisciplinary bridge between human text readability and machine comprehension, which could yield valuable insights.

- Weakness: Primary goal and central research question are unclear, causing confusion about the paper’s main argument.

- Weakness: Ambiguity about whether the focus is machine comprehension or human readability undermines clarity of the contribution.

- Weakness: Important prior work in the readability field is omitted, weakening the paper’s foundation and credibility.

- Weakness: Section 2.2 contains theoretical discussion that appears unrelated to the rest of the paper, harming coherence.

- Weakness: The paper tries to address multiple questions at once, diluting focus; topics like dataset readability effects and prerequisite skills need separate, in-depth analysis.

- Title issue: The title is ambiguous and should explicitly indicate it concerns machine comprehension rather than human reading comprehension.

- Methodological caveat: The claim that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty may depend on the specific methods/features used (e.g., POS/dependency features).

- Writing quality: The manuscript needs thorough proofreading to fix English errors and incomplete sentences.

- Clarity issue: It is unclear what the authors mean by “annotating datasets with metrics,” and this should be explained.

- Conceptual conflation: The paper repeatedly conflates machine reading comprehension with human reading comprehension, which are distinct research areas.

- Conceptual conflation: The authors conflate “readability of text” with “difficulty of reading contents,” which are not interchangeable (see DuBay 2004).

- Suggestion: Authors should provide explicit comparisons to human readability research and clarify statements to specify when claims apply to machine comprehension.

- Section 3.1 issue: It’s unclear whether listed skills refer to humans or machines, and citing human-skills literature may be inappropriate if the focus is machines.

- Annotation details missing: The paper should report annotation procedures, number of questions annotated, and whether annotators were instructed to consider machine comprehension.
