- Proposes two evaluation metric classes for RC datasets: prerequisite skills and readability, applied to six datasets to study relation between answering and reading difficulty.
- Adopts combined metrics to provide a more comprehensive RC dataset analysis.
- Revises prerequisite-skill taxonomy, framing knowledge reasoning as entailment phenomena and mapping comprehension to psychological concepts.
- Annotates six RC datasets with the proposed metrics and publishes the results.
- Strength: integrates human text readability and machine comprehension perspectives.
- Strength: offers practical guidance for building datasets and developing RC systems.
- Weakness: paper goal is unclear and writing is ambiguous, hindering comprehension.
- Weakness: omits key readability literature and contains unrelated theoretical discussion (Sec. 2.2).
- Weakness: attempts to answer too many questions at once; readability impact and prerequisite skills should be analyzed separately.
- Weakness: ambiguous title—should specify machine, not human, reading comprehension.
- Weakness: requires English proofreading to fix omissions and grammatical errors.
- Question: request details on how datasets were annotated with the proposed "metrics" and the annotation protocol.
- Question: clarify whether prerequisite skills target human readers or machine models (Section 3.1).
- Question: report how many questions annotators labeled and whether they were instructed to consider machine behavior versus human behavior.
- Question: provide clearer distinctions between this work and human-question readability research to resolve ambiguity.
