- Strength: Combines text readability and machine comprehension, which could yield valuable cross-disciplinary insights.
- Primary goal is unclear and the paper is ambiguous about whether it targets machine comprehension or human readability.
- Significant prior work in the readability field has been overlooked.
- Section 2.2 discusses theoretical topics that appear unrelated to the rest of the paper.
- The paper attempts to answer multiple questions at once (e.g., readability effects vs. prerequisite skills), weakening its focus and impact.
- Title is ambiguous and should explicitly indicate the focus on machine comprehension rather than human reading comprehension.
- The claim that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty may depend on the specific answer-detection methods and features used (e.g., POS/dependency parses).
- The manuscript needs thorough proofreading and fixes for notable omissions and phrasing errors.
- The authors must clarify how datasets were annotated with “metrics” and provide details on the annotation process.
- The paper conflates machine reading comprehension with human readability; readability is not equivalent to reading difficulty (cf. DuBay, 2004).
- Section 3.1 is unclear whether listed “skills” apply to humans or machines, and the relevance of human-skills citations should be justified; provide annotator counts and instructions regarding machine-focused annotation.
