- Strength: Successfully integrates text readability and machine comprehension, offering a promising interdisciplinary approach.

- Weakness: Primary objective is unclear and requires multiple readings to grasp.

- Weakness: Scope oscillates between machine comprehension and human readability, causing confusion.

- Weakness: Neglects significant prior contributions in the readability literature.

- Weakness: Section 2.2 includes unrelated theoretical discussions that disrupt coherence.

- Weakness: Attempts to answer multiple questions at once, weakening overall impact (e.g., mixing dataset readability effects and prerequisite skills).

- Title ambiguity: Should clarify it concerns machine comprehension rather than human reading comprehension.

- Method-dependence: Claim that dataset readability doesn't affect question difficulty may depend on chosen features (e.g., POS/dependency parses).

- Language issues: Paper needs thorough proofreading to fix English omissions and errors.

- Annotation clarity: The dataset annotation process using "metrics" is unclear and needs more explanation.

- Conceptual blurring: The paper conflates machine reading comprehension with human reading comprehension despite important differences.

- Terminology: Distinguish "readability of text" from "difficulty of reading contents" (DuBay 2004).

- Recommendation: Qualify statements to explicitly refer to machine comprehension where relevant.

- Section 3.1 concern: Unclear intended audience (humans vs. machines) and relevance of cited, human-focused papers.

- Annotation details: Specify how many questions annotators labeled and whether annotators were instructed to consider machine comprehension.
