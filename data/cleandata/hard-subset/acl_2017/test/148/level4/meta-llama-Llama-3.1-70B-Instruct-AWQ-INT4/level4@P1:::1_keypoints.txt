- Proposes two classes of evaluation metrics (prerequisite skills and readability) for analyzing RC datasets.
- Revises prior classification of prerequisite skills, reorganizing knowledge-reasoning skills by entailment phenomena and human text comprehension.
- Annotates six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics to guide dataset construction and RC system development.
- Connects human text readability and machine comprehension, offering a cross-disciplinary perspective.
- Provides a comprehensive dataset analysis that highlights characteristics of each evaluated RC dataset.
- The paper’s goal is unclear and the writing is often ambiguous, obscuring the main message.
- Omits important work in the readability literature; theoretical discussion in Section 2.2 appears unrelated to the rest of the paper.
- Attempts to answer too many questions at once, leaving the paper weak and hard to follow.
- Title is ambiguous and should explicitly refer to machine (not human) reading comprehension.
- Ask authors to clarify the paper’s goal and produce a more focused message.
- Request details on how datasets are annotated with the proposed “metrics” and the annotation procedure.
- Ask authors to distinguish their work from human-question readability and provide clearer pointers to that difference.
- Ask whether prerequisite skills in Section 3.1 target humans or machines, and why human-comprehension papers are cited if aimed at machines.
- Ask how many questions annotators labeled and whether annotators were instructed to annotate with machines (not humans) in mind.
