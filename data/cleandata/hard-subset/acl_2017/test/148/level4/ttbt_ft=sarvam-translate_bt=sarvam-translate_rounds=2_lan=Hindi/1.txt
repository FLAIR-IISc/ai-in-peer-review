General Discussion:
The title is a bit vague; it would be good to clarify whether you are referring to machine understanding of text, not human reading comprehension, because "reading comprehension" and "readability" usually mean the same thing.

You say that your "dataset analysis has shown that the readability of RC datasets does not directly affect the difficulty of the questions," but this depends on the method/features used to find the answer, for example, if you use POS/dependency parsing features.

You need to proofread the English of your paper; there are some important omissions, such as on page 1, "The question is easy to solve, just look at it..".

How do you annotate datasets with "metrics"?
Here you are mixing machine reading comprehension of texts and human reading comprehension of texts, which are somewhat similar but also quite different, and also have large areas.

"Text readability" is not "difficulty of reading the content." Check this:
Dubey, W.H. 2004. Principles of Readability. Costa Mesa, CA: Impact Information.

It would be good if you indicated and pointed out your work to distinguish it from the readability of questions for humans, because this article is very vague. For example, on page 1, "These two examples show that text readability is not necessarily related to the difficulty of the questions" you should add "for machine understanding".
