- Proposes two complementary RC evaluation metrics — prerequisite skills and readability — and applies them to six existing datasets with public annotations.
- Main contributions: a refined, theory-aligned taxonomy of prerequisite skills; evidence that dataset readability weakly correlates with question difficulty; and a released annotated resource.
- Strength: Introduces a systematic framework (prerequisite skills) that offers a nuanced measure of question difficulty beyond surface question types.
- Strength: Reorganizes prerequisite skills using insights from textual entailment and psychology, producing a more theoretically grounded taxonomy.
- Strength: Public release of annotated datasets is a useful resource for future RC dataset construction and system development.
- Weakness: Paper’s goal and scope are unclear, conflating human and machine reading comprehension without clarifying intended target.
- Weakness: Fails to engage key readability literature and misrepresents DuBay (2004), undermining its readability foundation.
- Weakness: Annotation methodology is under-specified — unclear how skills were assigned, how ambiguous cases were handled, and whether annotators considered machine vs. human comprehension.
- Weakness: Tries to address too many questions at once, diluting focus and muddling the core claim about readability’s effect on difficulty.
- Weakness: Significant grammatical issues and unclear phrasing harm credibility and readability.
- Question: Clarify whether "prerequisite skills" target human or machine comprehension and how that choice affects methods and results.
- Question: Would conclusions about readability vs. difficulty hold under different feature sets (e.g., POS/dependency parse features)?
- Question: How did the annotation procedure ensure consistent evaluation from a machine comprehension perspective rather than human comprehension?
- Question: Clarify your definition of "readability" and reconcile it with standard measures (e.g., DuBay 2004).
- Question: Distinguish this work from research on question readability for humans and provide clear pointers separating the concepts.
