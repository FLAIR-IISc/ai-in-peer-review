- Novel contribution: integrates human text readability with machine comprehension of texts.  
- Main objective is unclear; paper ambiguously alternates between machine comprehension and human readability.  
- Significant prior work in the readability literature is omitted.  
- Section 2.2 includes tangential theoretical discussion not aligned with the paper’s central thesis.  
- Paper attempts to answer multiple distinct questions at once (e.g., dataset readability impact, prerequisite skills), weakening its contribution.  
- Title is ambiguous; should explicitly indicate “machine comprehension” rather than generic “reading comprehension.”  
- Claim that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty depends on answer-detection methods/features and needs qualification.  
- Manuscript contains proofreading errors and critical omissions in phrasing.  
- Annotation methodology for computing “metrics” is inadequately described.  
- The paper inconsistently mixes human and machine comprehension contexts and should specify when statements refer to machines.  
- Needs clearer distinction between “readability of text” and “difficulty of reading contents” (e.g., DuBay 2004).  
- Section 3.1 ambiguously treats human skills as applicable to machines without justification or validation.  
- Annotation process lacks key details: number of questions annotated and whether annotators were instructed to evaluate from a machine-comprehension perspective.
