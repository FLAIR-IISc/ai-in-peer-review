- Strength: Paper promisingly bridges human text readability and machine comprehension, offering interdisciplinary value.

- Weakness 1: Central goal is ambiguous, conflating human "readability" with machine comprehension—needs to specify "for whom" readability/difficulty applies.

- Weakness 2: Fails to distinguish human vs. machine tasks—relies on human-focused readability literature without justifying relevance to RC models.

- Weakness 3: Omits key foundational work in readability and machine RC (e.g., DuBay, Kintsch, SQuAD/Paper by Rajpurkar et al., Chen et al.), weakening theoretical grounding.

- Weakness 4: Methodological and presentation problems: irrelevant theoretical sections, unclear annotation protocol, proofreading errors, and an overambitious, unfocused scope.

- Specific concern: Section 4.1’s claim that readability doesn't affect question difficulty likely depends on the chosen feature set and may be a methodological artifact.

- Specific concern: Title is misleading—must specify "machine reading comprehension" rather than generic "reading comprehension."

- Specific concern: Annotation validity is unclear because annotators’ instructions (evaluate for machines vs. humans) are not described.

- Recommendation: Clearly define and consistently state the paper’s focus as machine comprehension, replacing ambiguous terms like "readability" when needed.

- Recommendation: Narrow and restructure the paper to address one core question (e.g., does text readability affect machine comprehension?) and remove tangential topics.

- Recommendation: Cite and engage with key readability and RC literature to properly contextualize claims and findings.

- Recommendation: Revise methodology details—explain metrics, annotator training, annotation procedure, and clarify how feature choices affect results.

- Recommendation: Proofread thoroughly to correct grammatical errors and improve clarity.

- Conclusion: Work addresses a worthwhile intersection but is currently undermined by conceptual confusion and methodological gaps; major revision recommended.
