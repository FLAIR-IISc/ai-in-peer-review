- Introduces a framework evaluating reading comprehension (RC) datasets via prerequisite skills and readability, annotating six datasets and releasing results.
- Main contributions: weak correlation between question difficulty and text readability; refined prerequisite skills classification; annotated six RC datasets.
- Strength: Integrates human text readability and machine RC into a useful analytical framework.
- Strength: Systematic annotation methodology for prerequisite skills to assess cognitive demands of RC questions.
- Strength: Empirical analysis of six datasets yields insights on readability vs. question difficulty with implications for dataset construction.
- Weakness: Primary objective is unclear and not effectively articulated in the introduction.
- Weakness: Ambiguous focus oscillating between human readability and machine comprehension, causing reader confusion.
- Weakness: Related work omits important literature in the readability domain, especially works on machine comprehension.
- Weakness: Section 2.2 includes theoretical discussions irrelevant to the main contribution, disrupting flow.
- Weakness: Attempts to address multiple research questions simultaneously, diluting focus and impact.
- Question: Title should clarify the work pertains to machine comprehension, not human reading comprehension.
- Question: The claim that readability doesn't affect question difficulty is method-dependent—how robust is this across different answer detection features (e.g., POS/dependency)?
- Question: Correct grammatical error in the phrase "the question is easy to solve simply look..." on page 1.
- Question: Provide more detail on the annotation methodology, guidelines, and inter-annotator agreement procedures.
- Question: Clarify whether prerequisite skills are defined for humans or machines and justify using human-focused literature for machine comprehension.
- Question: Explicitly distinguish focus from human readability by adding qualifiers like "for machine comprehension" where relevant.
- Question: Specify how many questions were annotated and whether annotators were instructed to consider machine rather than human comprehension.
