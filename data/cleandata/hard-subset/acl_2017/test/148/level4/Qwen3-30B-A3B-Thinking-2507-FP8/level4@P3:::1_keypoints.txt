- Proposes two complementary evaluation metrics for RC datasets: prerequisite skills (cognitive demands) and readability (text complexity), refines a skills taxonomy, applies metrics to six datasets, and releases annotated metrics.

- Primary contribution: a systematic methodology for evaluating RC datasets beyond superficial metrics like question type.

- Secondary contribution: a refined, theoretically grounded taxonomy of prerequisite skills informed by psychology and textual entailment.

- Tertiary contribution: public release of annotated dataset metrics for future research.

- Strength: addresses a gap by moving beyond superficial dataset characteristics to provide nuanced evaluation metrics.

- Strength: refined taxonomy is theoretically sound and potentially valuable for dataset construction and system development.

- Strength: empirical finding that readability and question difficulty are weakly correlated (r=0.025–0.416), showing texts can be easy-to-read but hard-to-answer.

- Weakness: unclear whether the work targets human or machine RC; conflates the two and lacks justification for applying human comprehension theories to machine tasks.

- Weakness: annotation/methodology is unclear—how human-comprehension concepts (e.g., bridging, elaboration) map to machine evaluation is not explained.

- Weakness: misrepresents "readability," misunderstands key literature, omits foundational work (e.g., Flesch, Gunning Fog), and conflates text readability with question readability.

- Weakness: theoretical discussion of human comprehension models is poorly integrated with the paper’s machine-RC focus, creating confusion about purpose.

- Question: does the claim that readability does not affect question difficulty assume a specific model architecture, or is it intended to be model-agnostic?

- Question: was the prerequisite skills taxonomy designed for human or machine comprehension, and how does that choice affect result interpretation?

- Question: clarify scope in title/abstract—are you addressing human reading comprehension, machine RC, or both?

- Additional comment: paper contains grammatical errors that reduce professionalism and should be corrected.

- Additional comment: authors should clarify distinction from existing literature on question/readability for human readers.

- Additional comment: the claim that MCTest is easy-to-read but difficult-to-answer needs explicit qualification between human and machine evaluation.
