- Strength: paper bridges human text readability and machine comprehension, offering an interdisciplinary contribution.  
- Central objective unclear: paper doesn't precisely state whether it targets human readability, machine comprehension, or both.  
- Core claims ambiguous: statements (e.g., readability vs question difficulty) conflate human and machine contexts without qualification.  
- Key prior work omitted (e.g., DuBay 2004), undermining readability foundations.  
- Section 2.2 is tangential and does not clearly support the paper’s focus.  
- Paper attempts to answer multiple distinct questions (impact on RC datasets, prerequisite skills, measurement) without separating analyses.  
- Title ambiguous: should explicitly specify "machine" if focusing on machine comprehension.  
- Conclusion that dataset readability doesn't affect question difficulty is oversimplified and depends on chosen methods/features.  
- Multiple grammatical errors and proofreading issues throughout the paper.  
- Annotation methodology unclear: metrics definition, annotation procedure, and application are not described.  
- Conflation of human and machine literature: citations and discussions often mix contexts without justification.  
- Readability annotation lacks crucial details (number of questions annotated, annotator instructions, whether assessment was machine-focused).  
- Recommendation: clearly distinguish human readability, human question difficulty, machine comprehension of text, and machine comprehension of questions to resolve ambiguity.
