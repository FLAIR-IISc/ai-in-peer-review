- Central goal is unclear and the core argument is hard to discern.  
- Scope is ambiguous, shifting between machine comprehension and human readability.  
- Overlooks significant prior research on readability.  
- Section 2.2 contains theoretical discussion that seems unrelated to the paper’s focus.  
- Attempts to answer too many research questions at once, causing lack of focus; analyses of readability effects and prerequisite skills should be separated.  
- Title should explicitly state a focus on machine comprehension rather than general “reading comprehension.”  
- The claim that dataset readability doesn’t affect question difficulty may depend on answer detection methods and feature choices.  
- Manuscript needs careful proofreading due to grammatical errors and typos.  
- Unclear how datasets were annotated with “metrics” and what those metrics are.  
- Frequently conflates machine and human reading comprehension and fails to distinguish them.  
- Uses “readability” and “difficulty of reading contents” interchangeably, which is inaccurate (see DuBay 2004).  
- Statements claiming lack of correlation between readability and question difficulty should specify they refer to machine comprehension.  
- Section 3.1 is unclear whether discussed skills are for human or machine understanding; citing human-focused studies requires justification for generalizing to machines.  
- Annotation details are incomplete: number of annotated questions and whether annotators considered machine comprehension are not reported.
