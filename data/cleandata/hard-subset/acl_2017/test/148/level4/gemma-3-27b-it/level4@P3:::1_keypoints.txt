- Empirical analysis of six RC datasets using prerequisite-skill and readability metrics to assess dataset difficulty beyond accuracy.  
- Refinement of prerequisite-skill categorization grounded in textual entailment and psychology, though novelty is incremental.  
- Public release of dataset annotations and metrics as a resource for future research.  

- Timely and relevant work addressing dataset quality in RC.  
- Comprehensive comparative analysis revealing trends (e.g., weak correlation between readability and question difficulty).  
- Interdisciplinary framing drawing on NLP, psychology, and linguistics to justify metrics.  
- Valuable resource creation via publicly available annotated datasets.  

- Core argument (readability vs. RC difficulty) is unclear and diluted by attempting to address too many questions.  
- Ambiguity about whether metrics target human or machine comprehension; psychological theory use is not clearly justified for machines.  
- Prerequisite-skill refinement lacks strong novelty and is largely organizational.  
- Insufficient detail on annotation procedure, annotator training, guidelines, and disagreement resolution despite reported inter-annotator agreement.  
- Missing citation and discussion of relevant readability/NLP literature (e.g., DuBay 2004).  

- Request for detailed annotation protocol, training, and guidelines used.  
- Request for explanation of how annotator disagreements were resolved.  
- Request to clarify whether evaluation metrics target human or machine comprehension and revise paper accordingly.  
- Request to describe which RC system design choices are sensitive to identified readability and skill characteristics.  

- Paper needs English proofreading to fix awkward phrasing and grammatical errors.  
- Suggest focusing the paper more tightly on readability vs. question difficulty and moving broader topics to future work.  
- Ask to clarify what "difficulty" means (accuracy, time to answer, or other metrics).
