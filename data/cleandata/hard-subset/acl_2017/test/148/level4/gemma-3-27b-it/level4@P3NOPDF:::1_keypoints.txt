- Novel idea: exploring the relationship between text readability and machine reading comprehension (MRC) is interesting and promising.
- Lack of clear research question: the paper attempts several distinct questions simultaneously, diluting its focus.
- Ambiguous terminology: “reading comprehension” and “readability” are used without consistently distinguishing human vs. machine comprehension.
- Literature gap: overlooks established work and metrics in the readability literature.
- Irrelevant content: Section 2.2 presents theoretical discussion that is not clearly connected to the core research question.
- Methodology vague: the phrase “annotate datasets with 'metrics'” is unclear and requires concrete explanation.
- Annotation details missing: number of questions, annotator instructions, and whether assessments targeted machine comprehension are unspecified.
- Annotator bias risk: potential human-centric judgments of readability are not addressed or mitigated.
- Unacknowledged dependence on methods: the claim that readability does not affect question difficulty ignores dependence on features/methods used for answer detection.
- Writing quality issues: grammatical errors and unclear phrasing undermine credibility and require proofreading.
- Conceptual conflation: mixing human and machine reading comprehension throughout weakens the manuscript’s contribution.
- Recommended fixes: refine the research question, clarify terminology, strengthen literature review, provide detailed methodology, address biases, and proofread.
- Overall recommendation: Weak Reject.
