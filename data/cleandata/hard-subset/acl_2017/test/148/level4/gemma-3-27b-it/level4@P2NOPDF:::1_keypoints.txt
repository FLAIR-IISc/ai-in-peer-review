- Paper explores linking text readability and machine reading comprehension (MRC), a potentially valuable direction.  
- Manuscript lacks clear focus; core contribution and central goal remain unclear.  
- Attempts to answer multiple related questions (readability's impact on MRC, prerequisite skills, question readability) without prioritization.  
- Conflates machine and human reading comprehension; framing and title are ambiguous.  
- Citations to human cognitive skills (Section 3.1) are not justified as relevant to machine comprehension.  
- Overlooks key readability literature and includes tangential theoretical discussion (Section 2.2).  
- Claim that readability does not affect question difficulty is presented without sufficient nuance or consideration of methods/features used.  
- Annotation methodology is unclear: what was annotated, how, by whom, and how many questions per annotator.  
- Unclear whether annotators were instructed to assess difficulty from a machine or human perspective.  
- Equates "readability" with "difficulty of reading contents," which is conceptually inaccurate; needs reference to established definitions (e.g., DuBay 2004).  
- Paper contains grammatical errors and awkward phrasing; requires substantial proofreading.  
- Recommended fixes: refocus the research question, clearly distinguish machine vs. human comprehension, strengthen literature review, detail annotation procedures, and provide a more nuanced analysis tied to specific MRC features/methods.
