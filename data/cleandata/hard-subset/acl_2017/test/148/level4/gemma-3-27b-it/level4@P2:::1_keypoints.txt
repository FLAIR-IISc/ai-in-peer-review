- Contributions: analyzes six RC datasets using prerequisite-skill and readability metrics to assess dataset quality beyond accuracy.
- Contributions: refines prerequisite-skill taxonomy drawing on textual entailment and psychology.
- Contributions: releases annotated datasets publicly for community use.

- Strength: novel pairing of educational readability metrics with prerequisite-skill analysis gives a holistic evaluation framework.
- Strength: detailed quantitative analysis of six datasets shows readability and question difficulty are not strongly correlated.
- Strength: thoughtful reorganization of prerequisite skills with cognitive grounding (bridging, elaboration).
- Strength: public release of annotated datasets is a valuable community resource.

- Weakness: paper conflates human and machine reading comprehension; scope and language should explicitly focus on machine RC.
- Weakness: concluding that readability doesn't affect difficulty based on weak correlations overstates evidence; causation not ruled out.
- Weakness: annotation methodology focuses on required skills but ignores systems’ ability to discover relevant info; limited evaluation scope.
- Weakness: broad scope (dataset evaluation, skill taxonomy, annotation methods) reduces focus and weakens central argument.

- Q: Provide detailed annotation guidelines, target audience specification (machine vs human), and examples of disagreements.
- Q: How was inter-annotator reliability computed (e.g., Cohen’s Kappa)? Provide agreement breakdown by skill.
- Q: Do you plan to evaluate sentence-selection or information-discovery competence of RC systems in future work?
- Q: How might different feature sets for answer detection influence the readability–difficulty relationship, and can this be tested empirically?

- Comment: paper needs careful English proofreading to fix awkward phrasing and grammatical errors.
- Comment: explicitly differentiate “readability of text” vs “difficulty of reading contents” (cite DuBay 2004).
- Comment: clarify relevance of human text-comprehension background (Section 2.2) to machine RC.
- Comment: annotation procedure lacks concrete detail on metric application and would benefit from illustrative examples.
