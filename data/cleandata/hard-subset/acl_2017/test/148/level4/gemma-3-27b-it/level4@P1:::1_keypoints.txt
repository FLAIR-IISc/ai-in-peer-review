- Paper analyzes relationship between question difficulty and text readability in RC datasets using prerequisite-skill and readability metrics.
- Evaluates six RC datasets (MCTest, SQuAD, QA4MRE, WDW, MS MARCO, NewsQA) and finds weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.
- Contribution: comprehensive comparative analysis of six prominent RC datasets with new evaluation metrics.
- Contribution: refinement of a prerequisite-skills taxonomy grounded in textual entailment and human comprehension theories.
- Contribution: publicly releases annotated datasets for community use.
- Strength: novel combination of readability metrics and refined prerequisite skills offers a more holistic RC evaluation than accuracy alone.
- Strength: detailed dataset-level insights (e.g., MS MARCO’s nonsensical questions, QA4MRE’s high difficulty).
- Strength: public annotations provide a practical resource for benchmarking and dataset design.
- Strength: theoretical grounding (links to Kintsch, McNamara & Magliano) strengthens the taxonomy.
- Weakness: scope ambiguity between human and machine reading comprehension needs clarification.
- Weakness: conclusion that readability does not affect difficulty overstates results — correlation vs causation not resolved.
- Weakness: annotation methodology lacks detail on annotator training, disagreement resolution, and perspective (human vs machine).
- Weakness: limited discussion of prior readability literature; more related work (e.g., Dubay 2004) needed.
- Weakness: paper’s multi-question scope risks diluting focus; separating topics might improve impact.
- Question: request for detailed annotator training procedures and instructions aligning to machine RC.
- Question: request for annotation disagreement resolution process and post-resolution inter-annotator agreement rates.
- Question: ask for discussion of Pearson correlation limitations and alternative statistical analyses for readability vs difficulty.
- Question: ask for example questions that are easy to read but hard to answer, with explanation of challenges for RC systems.
- Additional: manuscript needs proofreading to fix grammar and awkward phrasing.
- Additional: suggest making the title explicit about machine reading comprehension.
- Additional: recommend more concise presentation distinguishing “readability of text” from “difficulty of reading contents.”
- Additional: clarify in Section 3.1 whether prerequisite skills target human or machine comprehension and how cited works relate to ML.
