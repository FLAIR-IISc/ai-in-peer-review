- Strength: Attempts to bridge text readability (human-focused) and machine comprehension fields.  
- Core goal is unclear; central argument difficult to discern even after multiple readings.  
- Ambiguous focus: paper repeatedly conflates machine reading comprehension with human readability.  
- Overlooks important prior work in human readability literature.  
- Section 2.2 discusses theoretical topics that seem unrelated to the main investigation.  
- Tries to address too many questions simultaneously, resulting in lack of focus and weakened contributions.  
- Claim that dataset readability does not affect question difficulty may depend on specific methods/features used for answer detection.  
- Title is ambiguous and should explicitly state focus on machine comprehension if that is the intent.  
- Methodology issues: annotation process and the metrics used to label datasets are unclear and under-explained.  
- Paper inaccurately equates “readability of text” with “difficulty of reading contents,” contradicting established readability research.  
- Citations of human readability work need justification if the target is machine comprehension; applicability is questionable.  
- Presentation and proofreading are poor, with grammatical errors and incomplete sentences that require fixing.
