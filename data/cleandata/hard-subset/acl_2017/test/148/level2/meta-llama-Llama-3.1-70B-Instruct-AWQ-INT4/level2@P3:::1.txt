**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a new evaluation metric for reading comprehension (RC) datasets, focusing on two classes of metrics: prerequisite skills and readability. The authors refine the prerequisite skills proposed by Sugawara et al. (2017) and Sugawara and Aizawa (2016) using insights from psychology research on human text comprehension. They also adopt readability metrics proposed by Vajjala and Meurers (2012) to evaluate the difficulty of reading contents in documents. The authors annotate six existing RC datasets with these metrics and demonstrate the characteristics of the datasets according to each metric and the correlation between the two classes.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a new evaluation metric for RC datasets, which includes two classes of metrics: prerequisite skills and readability.
2. They refine the prerequisite skills proposed by Sugawara et al. (2017) and Sugawara and Aizawa (2016) using insights from psychology research on human text comprehension.
3. They annotate six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics and demonstrate the characteristics of the datasets according to each metric and the correlation between the two classes.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of RC datasets using two classes of metrics, which can help researchers understand the strengths and weaknesses of different datasets.
2. The authors refine the prerequisite skills proposed by previous studies, making them more comprehensive and applicable to various RC datasets.
3. The paper provides a valuable guide for the construction of new datasets and the development of RC systems.

**Weaknesses**

1. The annotation process is time-consuming and requires human annotators, which may limit the scalability of the approach.
2. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how to use the proposed metrics to develop RC systems, which may make it difficult for researchers to apply the metrics in practice.
3. The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the correlation between the two classes of metrics, which may be an important aspect to investigate in future studies.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on how to use the proposed metrics to develop RC systems, such as how to select the most relevant metrics for a specific task or how to combine the metrics to evaluate system performance?
2. How do you plan to address the scalability issue of the annotation process, such as using automated annotation tools or crowdsourcing?
3. Can you provide more insights on the correlation between the two classes of metrics, such as how the prerequisite skills affect the readability of the text or how the readability affects the difficulty of the questions?
