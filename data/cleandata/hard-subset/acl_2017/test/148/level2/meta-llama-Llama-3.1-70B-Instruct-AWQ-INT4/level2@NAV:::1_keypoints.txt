- Proposes two classes of RC evaluation metrics (prerequisite skills and readability), refines skills taxonomy including knowledge reasoning, annotates six datasets, and finds weak correlation between question difficulty and context readability.

- Contribution: Introduces two metric classes to provide a more comprehensive analysis of RC datasets.

- Contribution: Refines prerequisite skills for RC by adding knowledge reasoning and providing a finer-grained skill classification.

- Contribution: Annotates six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics to guide dataset construction and RC system development.

- Strength: Provides thorough analysis of RC datasets, highlighting dataset characteristics and insights for system development.

- Strength: Offers a comprehensive classification of prerequisite skills that can be used to evaluate RC system performance.

- Strength: Highlights the importance of considering both prerequisite skills and readability when evaluating RC datasets.

- Weakness: Reliance on human annotators may introduce subjectivity and variability into the annotations.

- Weakness: Analysis is limited to six RC datasets, which may not represent the full diversity of RC datasets.

- Weakness: Paper does not clearly explain how the proposed metrics can be used to improve RC system development.

- Question: How can the proposed metrics be used to improve RC systems and what concrete benefits would they provide?

- Question: How can annotation subjectivity and variability be mitigated, and what measures ensure annotation reliability?

- Question: Can the proposed metrics be applied to other NLP tasks, and what potential applications exist beyond RC?
