- Study evaluates RC datasets using two metric classes (prerequisite skills and readability), refines metrics, annotates six datasets, and analyzes outcomes.
- Contribution: Adopts two complementary metric classes for more comprehensive RC dataset analysis.
- Contribution: Refines prerequisite skills for RC, structuring reasoning by entailment phenomena and psychological comprehension.
- Contribution: Annotates six existing RC datasets with the refined metrics and publishes the results.
- Strength: Delivers a thorough analysis that highlights dataset strengths and weaknesses.
- Strength: Refinement of prerequisite skills is a notable, comprehensive contribution to RC research.
- Strength: Reveals an insightful correlation between readability and question difficulty with implications for RC systems.
- Weakness: Limiting evaluation to two metric classes may reduce the generalizability of findings.
- Weakness: Annotation process may be subjective and reliant on annotator expertise.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed discussion on implications for building RC systems and new datasets.
- Question: How will the authors address annotation subjectivity in future studies?
- Question: How can the refined prerequisite skills be used to improve RC system development?
- Question: How can the readability–question difficulty findings be applied to other NLP areas?
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written and clear but would benefit from deeper discussion of implications and limitations.
