**Summary of the Paper and Main Contributions**

This paper proposes two classes of evaluation metrics for reading comprehension (RC) datasets: prerequisite skills and readability. The authors apply these metrics to six existing RC datasets and demonstrate the characteristics of each dataset according to these metrics. The main contributions of this paper are: (1) the adoption of two classes of evaluation metrics to analyze RC datasets, (2) the revision of the previous classification of prerequisite skills for RC, and (3) the annotation of six existing RC datasets with these metrics.

**Strengths**

1. **Comprehensive analysis of RC datasets**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of six RC datasets using two classes of evaluation metrics, which helps to understand the characteristics of each dataset.
2. **Revision of prerequisite skills**: The authors revise the previous classification of prerequisite skills for RC, making it more comprehensive and applicable to various RC datasets.
3. **Annotation of RC datasets**: The authors annotate six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics, making the results publicly available for future research.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited scope of analysis**: The authors only analyze six RC datasets, which may not be representative of all RC datasets.
2. **Subjective annotation**: The annotation of RC datasets is subjective and may vary depending on the annotators.
3. **Lack of system evaluation**: The authors do not evaluate the performance of RC systems using the proposed metrics, which limits the practicality of the work.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do the authors plan to extend the analysis to other RC datasets?
2. Can the authors provide more details on the annotation process and how they ensured the consistency of the annotations?
3. How do the authors plan to use the proposed metrics to evaluate the performance of RC systems?

**Additional Comments**

The paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of RC by proposing two classes of evaluation metrics and annotating six existing RC datasets. However, the authors should consider extending the analysis to other RC datasets and evaluating the performance of RC systems using the proposed metrics to make the work more comprehensive and practical.
