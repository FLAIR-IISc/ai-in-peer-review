- Summary: Evaluates RC datasets using prerequisite-skills and readability metrics, annotates six datasets, and finds readability doesn't directly determine question difficulty—datasets can be easy-to-read but hard-to-answer.

- Contribution 1: Introduces two metric classes (prerequisite skills and readability) to characterize RC datasets.

- Contribution 2: Revises prerequisite-skill taxonomy, organizing reasoning skills by entailment phenomena and comprehension by psychological constructs.

- Contribution 3: Annotates six existing RC datasets with the metrics and releases the annotations publicly.

- Strength 1: Provides a comprehensive dual-metric analysis useful for dataset design and improving RC systems.

- Strength 2: Reveals an interesting weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.

- Strength 3: Presents a detailed, replicable methodology and annotation procedure.

- Weakness 1: Limited to six datasets, reducing the generalizability of findings.

- Weakness 2: Methodology cannot evaluate the ability to select necessary sentences to answer questions.

- Weakness 3: Lacks deeper analysis and discussion of implications for RC system development and dataset construction.

- Question 1: How will you extend the study to more RC datasets and which additional datasets will you include?

- Question 2: How will you address the limitation of evaluating sentence-selection competence?

- Question 3: How can the findings be used to improve RC system development and what are the possible NLP applications?
