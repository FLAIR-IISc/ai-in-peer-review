Paper analyzes the relationship between question difficulty and text readability in six RC datasets using prerequisite-skill and readability metrics, finds weak correlation, and releases annotated datasets.

Strength: Addresses an important gap by moving beyond accuracy to nuanced dataset characteristics and shows readability and difficulty can be decoupled.

Strength: Uses a comprehensive, well-justified metric set combining NLP (textual entailment) and psychology-based comprehension models; refines prerequisite-skill taxonomy.

Strength: Large-scale annotation across six datasets with public release of annotations enables replication and further research.

Strength: Results are clearly presented with useful tables and figures and thoughtful discussion.

Weakness: Annotation methodology cannot evaluate sentence selection competence, limiting analysis of the full RC process.

Weakness: Prerequisite-skill annotations are somewhat subjective; resolution of annotator disagreements and guideline details are insufficiently described.

Weakness: The paper reports but does not deeply explain the weak correlation between readability and difficulty or analyze why certain readable texts yield hard questions.

Weakness: Limited discussion of dataset biases (e.g., topic distributions, automated curation artifacts) that could affect findings.

Question: Please provide the annotation guidelines with example questions and their skill annotations.

Question: How were annotator disagreements resolved during annotation?

Question: Can you provide qualitative examples of questions that are difficult despite high readability and the specific skills they require?

Question: How should these findings concretely inform the design or improvement of future RC datasets beyond using broader metrics?

Additional: Reference list is extensive and well-organized.

Additional: Table 6’s sentence-selection distance detail is a useful result.

Additional: Minor grammatical edits would improve clarity.

Additional: Consider discussing potential cultural/background knowledge biases in datasets.

Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address annotation detail, deeper analysis of weak correlations, and discussion of dataset biases.
