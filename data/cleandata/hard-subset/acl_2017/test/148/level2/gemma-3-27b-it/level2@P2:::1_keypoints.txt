- Paper studies relationship between question difficulty and text readability across six RC datasets using prerequisite-skill and readability metrics, finding a weak correlation.  
- Contributions: adopts two metric classes and shows weak correlation; revises prerequisite-skill taxonomy grounded in textual entailment and psychology; annotates and releases six datasets.  
- Strength: Tackles an important problem of dataset quality beyond simple accuracy metrics.  
- Strength: Uses a comprehensive, literature-grounded set of metrics (skills + readability) for holistic evaluation.  
- Strength: Provides annotated versions of six widely used datasets with public release and clear annotation procedures (including NewsQA filtering).  
- Strength: Finds the counterintuitive result that texts can be easy to read yet questions remain difficult, highlighting dataset biases and need for targeted reasoning tests.  
- Weakness: Skill annotation is subjective; 90.1% overall agreement reported but lacks per-skill disagreement analysis and details on annotator background.  
- Weakness: Readability relies on Vajjala & Meurers (2012) features, which emphasize syntactic/lexical complexity and omit coherence, discourse, and background-knowledge aspects.  
- Weakness: Demonstrates correlation but not causation and offers limited analysis on whether weak correlation reflects metric flaws or dataset construction issues.  
- Weakness: Annotation identifies sentences needed to answer but does not evaluate systems’ ability to find those sentences (no IR precision/recall evaluation).  
- Question: Please provide a detailed breakdown of inter-annotator agreement by prerequisite skill and describe how disagreements were resolved.  
- Question: Do the authors plan to develop methods that assess the competence of locating relevant sentences, not just identifying them?  
- Question: Can the authors offer hypotheses for the weak readability–difficulty correlation and dataset characteristics that may drive it?  
- Question: Please clarify the specific criteria and process used to filter and exclude "bad" questions in NewsQA.  
- Additional comment: Table and figure numbering appears inconsistent.  
- Additional comment: Reference list formatting is inconsistent.  
- Additional comment: Recommend adding a brief limitations section and avenues for future research in the conclusion.
