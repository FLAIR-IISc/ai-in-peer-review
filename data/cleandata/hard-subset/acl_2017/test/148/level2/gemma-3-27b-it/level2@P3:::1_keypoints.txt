- Core idea is valuable: moving beyond accuracy to analyze RC datasets via prerequisite skills and readability metrics.
- Claimed contributions: two classes of metrics for dataset characterization, revised prerequisite-skill taxonomy, and public annotations for six datasets.
- Strength: addresses the need for nuanced evaluation beyond accuracy, offering preliminary insights into dataset characteristics.
- Strength: method grounded in cognitive theories (McNamara & Magliano, Kintsch) and refines prior skill categories with textual-entailment insights.
- Strength: provides a publicly available, reproducible annotation resource for six datasets with a described annotation procedure.
- Strength: uncovers interesting observations (e.g., weak readability–difficulty correlation; MCTest is easy-to-read but hard-to-answer).
- Weakness: weak correlation finding is unsurprising and underexplored—paper does not analyze why the correlation is weak or implications for dataset design.
- Weakness: inter-annotator agreement reported (90.1%) lacks essential details (metric used, how agreement was computed, how disagreements were resolved).
- Weakness: methodological limitation—paper evaluates understanding of selected content but not the ability to select the necessary sentences for answering questions.
- Weakness: lacks system-level evaluation—no analysis of how proposed metrics correlate with actual RC system performance.
- Question: clarify the origin and collection process of the NewsQA filtering annotations (is answer absent, is question bad, validated answers).
- Question: specify the inter-annotator agreement metric (e.g., Cohen’s Kappa) and the disagreement resolution procedure.
- Question: explain how the metrics could be adapted to evaluate sentence-selection capabilities of RC systems.
- Question: have the authors tried to predict system performance using the identified prerequisite-skill distributions (e.g., as features in regression)?
- Additional: related work should focus more on dataset-evaluation literature; discussion is somewhat speculative and needs more empirical grounding.
