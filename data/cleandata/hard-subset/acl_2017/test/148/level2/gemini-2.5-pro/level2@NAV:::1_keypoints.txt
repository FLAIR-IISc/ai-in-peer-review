- Proposes a two-part evaluation framework for RC datasets (prerequisite skills + readability) and applies it via manual annotation to six datasets, revealing a weak correlation between readability and question difficulty and releasing the annotations.

- Strength: Addresses a critical need for qualitative RC dataset evaluation and shows that text readability does not reliably predict question difficulty (e.g., MCTest vs SQuAD).

- Strength: Presents a well-motivated, comprehensive framework grounded in comprehension theory with a refined, more granular prerequisite-skills taxonomy.

- Strength: Provides a valuable community resource by publicly releasing annotations to enable targeted evaluation, error analysis, and curriculum learning research.

- Weakness: Small sample size (100 questions per dataset) may limit representativeness and statistical robustness, especially for large corpora like SQuAD or MS MARCO.

- Weakness: Skill annotation is inherently subjective (e.g., distinguishing Bridging vs Elaboration); more discussion of disagreement cases would strengthen reliability claims.

- Weakness: Analysis focuses mainly on inference and largely sidesteps the information-location component of RC in long documents, with only preliminary sentence-distance analysis.

- Question: Request for statistical significance analysis and validation that the 100-question samples are representative for the reported correlations.

- Question: Request for challenging annotation examples illustrating how annotators consistently differentiated Bridging from Elaboration.
