- Introduces a principled evaluation framework for RC datasets using (a) a 13-category prerequisite-skill taxonomy and (b) 10 readability metrics.  
- Applies the framework to six RC datasets and manually annotates 600 questions (100 per dataset) for required skills.  
- Central empirical finding: only a weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty.  
- Releases manual skill annotations for 600 questions as a public resource.  
- Provides a refined, literature-grounded 13-category taxonomy of prerequisite skills combining NLP and psychology insights.  
- Strength: Addresses a timely need for qualitative characterization of RC datasets to measure true NLU progress.  
- Strength: Thorough comparative analysis across diverse datasets, with effective visualization (readability vs. skill count).  
- Strength: Taxonomy is theoretically grounded, making it more credible and reusable than ad-hoc schemes.  
- Strength: Public annotation release promotes reproducibility and enables targeted model evaluation.  
- Weakness: Small sample size (100 questions per dataset) may limit generalizability, especially for large corpora like SQuAD and MS MARCO.  
- Weakness: Defines question difficulty solely by number of required skills, ignoring that some skills may be intrinsically harder for models.  
- Weakness: Reporting of 90.1% inter-annotator agreement lacks crucial details (which metric, unit of calculation, disagreement resolution).  
- Question: Please specify how the 90.1% inter-annotator agreement was computed (which metric and on what annotation unit).  
- Question: Please explain sampling methodology and discuss how the 100-question samples were drawn and how representative they are for large datasets.
