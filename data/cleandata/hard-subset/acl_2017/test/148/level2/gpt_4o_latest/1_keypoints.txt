- Proposes two evaluation metrics—prerequisite skills and readability—to assess RC dataset quality and finds weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.  
- Revises prerequisite-skill taxonomy for RC by integrating insights from psychology and textual entailment research.  
- Annotates six RC datasets with the proposed metrics and releases the annotations publicly to support future work.  

- Introduces a novel, theory-grounded framework for systematic RC dataset evaluation.  
- Conducts a comprehensive analysis across six diverse RC datasets, challenging assumptions about readability and difficulty.  
- Publishes annotations to enable reproducibility and further research.  
- Offers practical guidance for constructing better RC datasets (e.g., narrative texts, diverse question types).  

- Only analyzes six datasets and omits prominent corpora (e.g., CNN/Daily Mail, LAMBADA), limiting generalizability.  
- Provides insufficient detail on annotation reliability, inter-annotator disagreement, and resolution methods.  
- Does not evaluate systems’ ability to select relevant sentences, leaving out a key RC capability.  
- Reports weak readability–difficulty correlations without deep exploration of underlying causes or other influencing factors.  

- How were annotator disagreements resolved and could resolution methods affect annotation reliability?  
- Might excluding datasets like CNN/Daily Mail and LAMBADA bias findings, and are there plans to include them?  
- How can the proposed metrics be integrated into automated dataset evaluation pipelines?  

- Overall, the paper makes a valuable contribution but would be stronger by addressing dataset selection, annotation methodology, and system-level evaluation limitations.
