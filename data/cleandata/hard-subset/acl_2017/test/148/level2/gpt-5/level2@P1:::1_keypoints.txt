- Framework: proposes evaluating RC datasets along prerequisite skills (bridging vs. elaboration) and readability metrics.  
- Annotation: multi-label skill annotation and readability computed on necessary sentences for six RC datasets; finds weak correlation between readability and number of skills.  
- Release: authors claim public release of annotations.

- Taxonomy strength: clear, theoretically grounded reorganization of knowledge reasoning into bridging vs. elaboration.  
- Multi-dataset analysis: comparative annotation across six datasets yields useful dataset-level profiles (e.g., QA4MRE hardest, MS MARCO easiest).  
- Empirical insight: demonstrates weak coupling between text readability and QA difficulty, challenging common assumptions.  
- Methodology: transparent procedures, supporting-sentence selection, reported inter-annotator agreement (90.1%), and a defined readability feature set.  
- Practical impact: provides actionable recommendations for dataset and system design (e.g., staging curricula, genre considerations).

- Sample & stats: only 100 questions per dataset limits power; correlation analyses lack detailed significance testing and CIs.  
- Annotation bias: annotators saw answers and selected sentences, "nonsense" label subjective, and filtering differs across datasets, risking bias.  
- Difficulty proxy: equating number of skills with difficulty assumes equal additive weighting and lacks validation against human or system difficulty.  
- Readability scope: computed on selected sentences only, ignoring evidence-finding in long/noisy passages and discourse-level factors.  
- Dataset coverage: excludes influential datasets (e.g., CNN/DailyMail), limiting generality and adaptation discussion.

- Provide full correlation stats (coefficients, p-values, CIs) and whether dataset effects were modeled (e.g., mixed-effects).  
- Explain multi-label inter-annotator agreement calculation and report per-skill agreement and "nonsense" vs. solvable agreement.  
- Share annotator training/guidelines for distinguishing bridging vs. elaboration vs. coreference, with examples.  
- Plan to validate "number of skills" as difficulty via human studies or system error correlations, or to weight skills by difficulty?  
- Will you release the code for readability features and sentence-selection annotations and expand annotations for more power?

- Complement skills count with measures of reasoning depth/graph structure and whole-passage discourse/readability features.  
- Report per-dataset, per-skill confusion matrices and typical failure modes to aid future analyses.  
- Investigate automatic prediction of prerequisite skills from question–context pairs to enable scalable profiling.
