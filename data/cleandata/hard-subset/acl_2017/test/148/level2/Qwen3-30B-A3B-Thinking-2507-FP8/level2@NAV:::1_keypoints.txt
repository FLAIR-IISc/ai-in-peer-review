- Proposes a novel framework for evaluating RC datasets using prerequisite skills and readability metrics.  
- Refines Sugawara et al.'s prerequisite-skill taxonomy, aligning it with textual entailment and human text-comprehension principles.  
- Applies the metrics and provides public annotations for six RC datasets (MCTest, SQuAD, WDW, MARCO, NewsQA, QA4MRE).  
- Main contributions: a comprehensive analysis framework, a refined skill classification grounded in psychology/NLP, and released annotations.  
- Strength: fills a critical gap by offering meaningful dataset-evaluation metrics beyond superficial question types.  
- Strength: methodology is well-structured with high inter-annotator agreement (90.1%) and clear metric definitions.  
- Key finding: readability and question difficulty are only weakly correlated (Pearson r = 0.025–0.416), so easy texts can have hard questions.  
- Practical implication: suggests curriculum learning for dataset construction and system development.  
- Weakness: lacks concrete guidance on how the metrics can directly improve RC system design or training.  
- Weakness: annotation guidelines for distinguishing bridging vs. elaboration skills are vague, risking reproducibility issues.  
- Weakness: acknowledges inability to evaluate sentence-selection competence but does not fully address its implications for systems.  
- Overall: well-written and contextualized, provides valuable resources, but needs clearer operationalization for system development and more detailed annotation protocols.
