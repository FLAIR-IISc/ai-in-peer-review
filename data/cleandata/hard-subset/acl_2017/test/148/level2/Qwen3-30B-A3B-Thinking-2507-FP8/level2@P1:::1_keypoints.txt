- Proposal: two complementary evaluation metric classes for RC — prerequisite skills and readability — with a refined skills taxonomy, applied to six datasets and released annotations.

- Primary contribution: refined, theoretically grounded prerequisite-skills taxonomy informed by textual entailment and psychology.

- Secondary contribution: empirical analysis showing weak correlation between question difficulty and text readability; identifies MCTest as easy-to-read but difficult-to-answer.

- Tertiary contribution: publicly available annotations of six RC datasets (QA4MRE, MCTest, SQuAD, WDW, MARCO, NewsQA).

- Strength: Fills a gap by providing a nuanced framework for dataset evaluation beyond superficial question/answer type metrics.

- Strength: Taxonomy refinement is well-justified and theoretically sound, building on prior work.

- Strength: Thorough, systematic empirical annotation and analysis across diverse datasets with meaningful conclusions.

- Strength: Practical implications suggested (e.g., curriculum learning) to guide RC system development.

- Weakness: Does not explain how to integrate the proposed metrics into system evaluation or prioritize metrics for systems.

- Weakness: Annotation procedure lacks detail on resolving annotator disagreements (inter-annotator agreement 90.1% — unclear handling of remaining 9.9%).

- Weakness: Fails to discuss how the inability to evaluate "selection of necessary sentences" affects findings or alternatives to address it.

- Weakness: Claim that one can create easy-to-read but hard-to-answer datasets is unsupported by a clear methodology for dataset creators.

- Weakness: Limited discussion on applying the metrics to new datasets, different genres, or extending to other NLP tasks.

- Question: How can metrics be integrated into RC system evaluation and which metrics are most valuable for system developers?

- Question: What specific design choices in MCTest produce easy-to-read yet hard-to-answer properties, and how can they be systematically reproduced?

- Question: How might the metrics be extended to handle different text genres, complex discourse structures, or specialized domains?

- Overall: Valuable and impactful framework with strong analysis, but needs clearer practical implementation guidance and discussion of limitations to maximize usefulness.
