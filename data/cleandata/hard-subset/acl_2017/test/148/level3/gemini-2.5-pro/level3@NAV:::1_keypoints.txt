- Analyzes six RC datasets using prerequisite-skill annotations and readability scores, claiming text readability only weakly correlates with QA difficulty.  
- Primary contribution: novel joint analysis linking human text readability with machine RC difficulty; secondary: annotated dataset resource (methodology needs clarification).  
- Strength: creative idea exploring how human linguistic difficulty relates to machine-required skills; potential to inform dataset design and training.  
- Weakness: conflates human and machine reading comprehension, causing conceptual confusion and undermining assumptions.  
- Weakness: methodology opaque—missing annotation instructions and unclear whether skills were judged from human or model perspective, so claims are unsupported.  
- Weakness: paper lacks focus, omits important readability literature, contains disconnected theory and proofreading issues.  
- Q1: Were annotators instructed to evaluate prerequisite skills from a human or machine perspective?  
- Q2: How do you justify applying human readability metrics (e.g., for L2 learners) to computational models?
