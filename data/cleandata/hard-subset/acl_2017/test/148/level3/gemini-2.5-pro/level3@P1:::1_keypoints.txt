- Proposes a two-dimensional framework for evaluating RC datasets by question-answering "prerequisite skills" and text "readability" metrics.  
- Refines an existing set of prerequisite skills and selects established readability measures to analyze datasets.  
- Applies the framework to annotate and analyze six popular RC datasets and releases annotations publicly.  
- Primary empirical finding: weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty, allowing "easy-to-read but hard-to-answer" datasets (e.g., MCTest).  
- Contribution: introduces a novel, more nuanced analytical vocabulary for discussing dataset characteristics beyond accuracy metrics.  
- Contribution: provides cross-dataset annotations and systematic analysis useful for dataset selection and diagnosing model performance.  
- Strength: addresses an important and underexplored direction—disentangling text complexity from question complexity to inform dataset design and modeling.  
- Strength: yields valuable empirical insights explaining dataset-specific model generalization (e.g., SQuAD vs MCTest) and implications for curriculum/transfer learning.  
- Strength: refines prerequisite skills with theoretical grounding in textual entailment and human psychology, adding rigor to the skill taxonomy.  
- Weakness: unclear focus and audience—insufficient distinction between human readability and machine comprehension weakens motivation and conclusions.  
- Weakness: superficial engagement with readability literature, relying on surface-level metrics and omitting richer computational features that may better reflect machine difficulty.  
- Weakness: inconsistent theoretical grounding—unclear reconciliation of psychology- and computational-linguistics-based motivations for skills and metrics.  
- Question: clarify annotator instructions—were skills judged from a human-reader or machine-model perspective?  
- Question: how would conclusions change if more sophisticated syntactic/discourse readability features were included?  
- Question: why exclude the competence of locating answer sentences, and how would including it affect dataset characterizations like SQuAD?  
- Additional comment: manuscript needs careful proofreading to fix grammatical errors and awkward phrasing.
