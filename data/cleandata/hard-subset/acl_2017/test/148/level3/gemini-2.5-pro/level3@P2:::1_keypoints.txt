- Summary: Proposes a framework using 13 "prerequisite skills" and readability metrics to analyze RC datasets; annotates 100 questions from six datasets and finds a weak correlation between readability and question difficulty; will release annotations.

- Contribution: Provides a large-scale comparative analysis of six RC datasets using a unified annotation framework, revealing dataset-specific patterns (e.g., SQuAD hard-to-read/easy-to-answer, MCTest easy-to-read/harder-to-answer).

- Contribution: Releases a public resource of prerequisite-skill annotations for 600 questions to aid fine-grained RC analysis.

- Strength: Addresses an important, under-explored problem by connecting text complexity and question complexity, bringing a novel human-centric perspective.

- Strength: Comprehensive empirical study across diverse, widely-used datasets with useful quantitative comparisons.

- Strength: Practical relevance—analysis can inform curriculum learning and dataset selection for system development.

- Weakness: Fails to clearly distinguish human vs. machine reading comprehension, creating conceptual ambiguity about the paper’s goal.

- Weakness: Annotation methodology is under-specified—unclear whether annotators judged human or machine skills, threatening validity of conclusions.

- Weakness: Conclusion that readability does not affect difficulty is oversimplified and dependent on limited readability measures and a specific skill-based difficulty definition.

- Weakness: Insufficient engagement with computational readability literature and overreliance on human comprehension theory; writing needs proofreading.

- Question: Clarify annotator instructions—were skills judged from a human or machine perspective, and how does that affect interpretation?

- Question: How robust is the weak correlation finding to richer syntactic readability features or difficulty defined by model performance?

- Question: Justify the applicability of human comprehension theories (e.g., bridging vs. elaboration) to machine reading and compare to frameworks based on NLP system capabilities and failures.
