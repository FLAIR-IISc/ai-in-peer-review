- Empirical analysis of six RC datasets is the paper’s primary (but weak) contribution.
- The annotated dataset could be a useful MRC resource despite methodological flaws.
- Confirms prior observation that readability metrics don’t necessarily predict machine QA difficulty.
- Paper lacks clear focus: unclear whether it targets human or machine reading comprehension.
- Conflates human psychology literature with machine-focused claims, weakening arguments.
- Attempts too many contributions (skill classification, many readability metrics, annotation) without sufficient depth.
- Section 2.2 (construction-integration model) reads like an irrelevant digression with no clear link to the work.
- The claim that readability doesn’t affect QA difficulty is overstated and depends on chosen features (e.g., POS/dependency).
- Annotation methodology is under-specified (number of labels per annotator, and whether difficulty was judged for humans or machines).
- Important prior readability work beyond Vajjala & Meurers (e.g., DuBay 2004) is overlooked.
- Manuscript contains numerous grammatical errors and needs thorough proofreading.
- Reviewer questions: were annotators instructed to judge machine vs human difficulty; clarification of the “distance” metric; response to conflation critique.
