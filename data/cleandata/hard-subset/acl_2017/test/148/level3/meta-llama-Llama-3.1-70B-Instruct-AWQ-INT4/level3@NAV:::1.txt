**Paper Review**

**Summary**

The paper "Evaluation Metrics for Reading Comprehension: Prerequisite Skills and Readability" proposes two classes of evaluation metrics for reading comprehension (RC) datasets: prerequisite skills and readability. The authors apply these metrics to six existing RC datasets and analyze the characteristics of the datasets and the correlation between the two classes. The paper aims to provide a framework for evaluating the quality of RC datasets and improving RC systems.

**Contributions**

As I see them, the main contributions of this work are:

1. **Adoption of two classes of evaluation metrics**: The paper attempts to connect text readability for humans with machine reading comprehension, which is a novel approach.
2. **Annotation of six existing RC datasets**: The authors provide a valuable resource for the research community by annotating six RC datasets with the proposed metrics.
3. **Analysis of the correlation between prerequisite skills and readability**: The paper investigates the relationship between the two classes of metrics and provides insights into the characteristics of the datasets.

**Strengths**

My strongest arguments supporting the acceptance of this submission are:

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new framework for evaluating RC datasets, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Comprehensive analysis**: The authors provide a thorough analysis of the datasets and the correlation between the two classes of metrics.
3. **Valuable resource**: The annotated datasets will be a valuable resource for the research community.

**Weaknesses**

My strongest arguments against the acceptance of this submission are:

1. **Unclear goal**: The paper's goal is unclear, and the reviewer is still confused after multiple readings.
2. **Ambiguity**: The paper conflates machine reading comprehension and human reading comprehension, which are distinct areas.
3. **Lack of prior work**: Important prior work in the readability literature is missing.
4. **Unrelated theoretical discussion**: Section 2.2 contains unrelated theoretical discussion.
5. **Too many questions**: The paper tries to answer too many questions at once, weakening its contributions.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you clarify the goal of the paper and how it contributes to the field of reading comprehension?
2. How do you distinguish between machine reading comprehension and human reading comprehension?
3. Can you provide more context on the prior work in readability literature and how it relates to your paper?
4. Can you explain why Section 2.2 is included and how it contributes to the paper's main argument?
5. Can you focus on one or two main research questions and provide more in-depth analysis?
