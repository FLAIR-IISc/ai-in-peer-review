- Studies evaluation metrics for reading comprehension (RC) datasets focusing on two classes: prerequisite skills and readability.
- Main contributions: proposes two classes of RC metrics to better assess dataset/system quality.
- Main contributions: refines prerequisite skills by reorganizing knowledge reasoning around entailment phenomena and human text comprehension.
- Main contributions: annotates six existing RC datasets with the proposed metrics to guide dataset construction and system development.
- Strength: attempts to connect human text readability with machine reading comprehension and provides thorough dataset analyses.
- Strength: reports dataset characteristics and correlations between prerequisite-skill and readability metrics.
- Weakness: paper goal is unclear and still confusing after multiple readings.
- Weakness: conflates machine reading comprehension with human reading comprehension; title should target machine comprehension.
- Weakness: attempts to answer too many questions at once, diluting contributions.
- Weakness: claim that readability does not affect QA difficulty is not robust and may depend on chosen features/methods.
- Weakness: lacks clear distinctions from prior human-question readability studies and needs explicit pointers.
- Weakness: requires editorial revision and English proofreading for grammatical errors.
- Question: provide details on annotation procedure and annotator instructions.
- Question: clarify whether referenced skills/applications target humans or machines and justify use of human-focused citations for machine claims.
- Question: report how many questions annotators labeled and whether labeling was done from a machine vs. human perspective.
- Question: supply more robust evidence supporting the conclusion that readability does not affect QA difficulty.
- Question: describe plans to address the conflation of machine and human reading comprehension in future work.
