- Proposes a novel evaluation approach for RC datasets using two metric classes: prerequisite skills and readability, aiming to link human readability and machine comprehension.  
- Strength: addresses an important gap between human text readability and machine reading comprehension.  
- Paper goal and focus are unclear and oscillate between machine comprehension and human readability.  
- Significant prior work in readability literature is not acknowledged.  
- Tries to answer too many questions at once, weakening its contributions.  
- Conclusion that readability doesn’t affect question difficulty is dependent on chosen features and methods and is not sufficiently qualified.  
- Title should clarify the work targets machine comprehension rather than human reading comprehension.  
- Manuscript needs English proofreading due to grammatical errors.  
- Annotation process is under-described: unclear how datasets were annotated, how many questions were labeled, and annotator instructions.  
- Conflates machine reading comprehension with human reading comprehension; needs clearer distinctions and pointers to related human-question readability studies.  
- Inter-annotator agreement is reported only for a subset; agreement for the entire dataset is missing.  
- Main contributions (two metric classes, revised prerequisite skills, annotations of six datasets) should be prioritized and presented more clearly.  
- Results discussion is informative but lacks deeper insights and specific recommendations for future work.  
- Reviewer asks for more details on annotation procedure, ensuring machine-perspective judgments, full inter-annotator agreement, and plans to address study limitations.  
- Overall: interesting ideas but requires significant revisions to clarify goals, methods, and contributions.
