- Paper studies how human-oriented readability relates to machine RC difficulty, introducing a prerequisite-skill taxonomy and readability features annotated across six datasets.

- Contribution: empirical cross-dataset analysis showing weak correlation between readability metrics and number of prerequisite skills required.

- Contribution: refined and reorganized prerequisite-skill taxonomy informed by entailment and cognitive psychology distinctions.

- Contribution: promised public release of annotations enabling comparative diagnostic analyses.

- Strength: consistent cross-dataset methodology with unified taxonomy and reported inter-annotator agreement (~90.1% on a sample).

- Strength: diagnostic insight that easy-to-read passages can still require multiple skills and multi-sentence reasoning.

- Strength: taxonomy refinement clarifies distinctions (e.g., bridging vs. elaboration) and grounds skill labels for entailment research.

- Strength: practical implications for dataset curation, highlighting "nonsense" questions and sourcing effects.

- Weakness: unclear scope and goal—ambiguity between human readability vs. machine comprehension objectives.

- Weakness: conflation of constructs—surface readability proxies are not equivalent to reading difficulty or machine difficulty.

- Weakness: incomplete related work coverage on readability, cohesion, and discourse-based measures.

- Weakness: methodology and annotation details are vague or insufficiently reported (units, IAA metric, adjudication, instructions, sample size).

- Weakness: Section 2.2 theoretical discussion feels disconnected and inflates scope.

- Weakness: correlation analyses lack controls for confounds (passage length, number of sentences, genre), limiting causal claims.

- Question: clarify primary goal and intended audience—machine RC diagnosis vs. human readability study.

- Question: specify exact readability features used and whether discourse/cohesion or entity-based features were tested.

- Question: provide full annotation protocol details—annotators per question, instructions, IAA statistic, disagreement resolution, training.

- Question: define how “nonsense” was operationalized and whether world-knowledge-only answers were flagged separately.

- Question: report sensitivity analyses controlling for paragraph length, selected sentences, genre, and dataset filters.

- Question: confirm whether full annotation set and guidelines will be publicly released and in what format.

- Comment: consider retitling to emphasize machine RC evaluation to avoid confusion with human reading research.

- Comment: explicitly distinguish “readability for humans” vs. “question difficulty for machine comprehension” in wording.

- Comment: move or condense theoretical material (Section 2.2) to appendix and better link theory to annotation choices.

- Comment: add a subsection clarifying differences among human readability, human comprehension difficulty, and machine RC difficulty with citations.

- Comment: manuscript needs English proofreading.

- Suggestion: correlate skill counts with actual system performance to strengthen practical value of the taxonomy.
