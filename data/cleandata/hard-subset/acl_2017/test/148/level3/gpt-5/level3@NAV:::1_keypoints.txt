- Paper analyzes six RC datasets using two metric families (prerequisite skills and readability) and reports weak correlation, arguing datasets can be easy to read but hard to answer and vice versa.
- Primary contribution: cross-dataset empirical analysis linking per-question skill requirements to readability, plus released annotations.
- Secondary contribution: refinement of a prerequisite-skill taxonomy integrating entailment phenomena and cognitive psychology concepts.
- Tertiary contribution: empirical claim that readability weakly correlates with machine QA difficulty for the chosen datasets and features.
- Strength: addresses an important gap about how text difficulty relates to machine QA difficulty; two-axis (skills vs. readability) view is a useful diagnostic lens.
- Strength: cross-dataset comparison (MCTest, SQuAD, QA4MRE, WDW, MS MARCO, NewsQA) reveals nuanced, dataset-specific differences.
- Strength: grounding the skill taxonomy in textual entailment and cognitive psychology improves interpretability and could aid dataset design.
- Strength: public release of annotations is valuable for future dataset construction and error analysis.
- Weakness: unclear scope and positioning—paper conflates human and machine reading comprehension and uses human-focused theory without bridging to measurable machine outcomes.
- Weakness: central conclusion is method-dependent, relying on a limited set of readability features; stronger claims need ablations with discourse, entity-grid, coreference, and LM-derived features.
- Weakness: important readability literature is missing and the paper fails to separate "readability of text" from "difficulty of reading content"; constructs need explicit operationalization.
- Weakness: goal ambiguity and overreach—trying to address dataset evaluation, taxonomy revision, and system guidance dilutes the main contribution.
- Weakness: methodological opacity about which metrics were annotated vs. computed, number of items per annotator, annotator perspective (human vs. machine), and incomplete IAA reporting; manuscript needs proofreading and clearer title.
- Question: clarify the primary target—if about machine RC, consistently state “for machine comprehension” when asserting lack of correlation.
- Question: detail the annotation protocol—items per annotator, instructions, and full inter-annotator agreement; explain how readability metrics were computed per question and rationale for mixing FK with Vajjala-Meurers features.
- Question: provide ablations with richer readability/complexity features (discourse relations, entity grids, coreference chains) to test robustness of the main claim.
- Question: distinguish the work from human-question readability studies, add missing citations (e.g., DuBay 2004), and clarify construct boundaries between readability and reading difficulty.
