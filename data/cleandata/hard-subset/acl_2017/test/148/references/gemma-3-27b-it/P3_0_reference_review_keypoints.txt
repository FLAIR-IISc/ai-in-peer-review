- Paper investigates relationship between question difficulty and text readability in RC datasets and proposes finer-grained evaluation metrics.  
- Proposes two metric classes: prerequisite skills (cognitively grounded) and readability to better characterize RC datasets.  
- Reorganizes and refines prerequisite skill categories drawing on textual entailment and cognitive psychology.  
- Provides publicly available annotations of six popular RC datasets with the proposed metrics.  
- Finds a weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty.  
- Strongly motivated problem statement with clear examples illustrating shortcomings of current evaluation practices.  
- Theoretical grounding in cognitive psychology (McNamara & Magliano, Kintsch) gives the framework credibility.  
- Annotation methodology is detailed and limitations (e.g., not evaluating sentence selection) are acknowledged.  
- Inter-annotator agreement is considered and discussed, demonstrating care in annotation quality.  
- Initial findings highlight that readability alone is insufficient to assess dataset difficulty.  
- Annotated datasets are a practical resource for the community and for creating better analyses.  
- Analysis is largely descriptive and lacks deeper investigation into why weak correlations occur.  
- Skill annotation is inherently subjective and the paper insufficiently addresses annotator bias at the individual-skill level.  
- Readability metrics are imperfect proxies for cognitive difficulty; more relevant metrics (e.g., syntactic/semantic complexity) should be explored.  
- No system-level evaluation showing how metrics relate to existing RC system performance across datasets.  
- Related work coverage is somewhat cursory and could better situate the contribution amid literature on question difficulty and cognitive modeling.  
- Requests for more detailed inter-annotator agreement broken down by skill and steps taken for low-agreement skills.  
- Asks for deeper analysis linking prerequisite skills to dataset/system performance and whether some skills consistently drive difficulty.  
- Queries whether the authors plan system-level evaluations and if any preliminary results exist.  
- Suggests exploring alternative, more directly relevant metrics for cognitive demand and discusses using the framework to guide new dataset creation.  
- Notes the paper is well-written, figures/tables are helpful, public dataset is a significant strength, and recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
