- Proposal: Augment RC evaluation with metrics for prerequisite skills and text readability to go beyond accuracy.
- Method: Refine skill categories using psychology and textual entailment, annotate six popular RC datasets, analyze skill–readability relationship.
- Key finding: Weak correlation between text readability and question prerequisite skills, indicating mismatched dataset difficulty.
- Contribution: Provides a comprehensive, well-motivated framework for qualitative analysis of RC datasets.
- Contribution: Refined skill categorization (e.g., bridging vs. elaboration, ellipsis) improves prior taxonomies.
- Contribution: Publishes annotations for six datasets, creating a practical resource for the community.
- Strength: Strong motivation and clear problem definition with illustrative examples.
- Strength: Solid theoretical grounding in human text comprehension and textual entailment literature.
- Strength: Thorough annotation methodology with high inter-annotator agreement (90.1%) and transparent limitations.
- Strength: Actionable insight that dataset design should consider both readability and skill requirements separately.
- Strength: Publicly available annotations are a practical asset for researchers.
- Weakness: Readability measures rely on surface linguistic features and omit domain knowledge, logical complexity, and ambiguity.
- Weakness: Skill annotation is partly subjective despite high agreement; more detailed guidelines would help reduce bias.
- Weakness: Analysis reports correlation but does not investigate causal reasons for the weak relationship.
- Weakness: Lacks concrete recommendations or methodologies for designing new datasets that balance readability and skills.
- Question: Request for more detailed annotation guidelines and strategies used to handle ambiguous skill definitions.
- Question: Why were the chosen readability metrics selected, and were metrics for reasoning complexity considered?
- Question: Did the authors analyze specific question types or datasets that drive the weak correlation between readability and skills?
- Question: What specific dataset-design recommendations do the authors suggest to address identified shortcomings?
- Additional comment: Paper is well-written, with comprehensive references and effective tables/figures.
- Additional comment: Recommend adding discussion of potential biases in existing RC datasets.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
