- Paper proposes evaluating reading comprehension datasets using "prerequisite skills" and "readability" metrics to go beyond simple accuracy.
- Authors annotate six RC datasets with these metrics and find weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty.
- Contribution: a comprehensive framework combining prerequisite skills and readability for dataset analysis.
- Contribution: a refined skills taxonomy improving on Sugawara et al., distinguishing constructs like bridging vs. elaboration.
- Contribution: publicly released annotations for six widely used RC datasets.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem statement, with an illustrative example showing disconnect between readability and difficulty.
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding in psychological models (McNamara & Magliano, Kintsch) and textual entailment.
- Strength: thorough annotation process with allowance for multiple labels and standardized conditions.
- Strength: high reported inter-annotator agreement (90.1%).
- Strength: finding that readability weakly correlates with question difficulty, informing dataset design.
- Strength: annotated datasets are a practical resource for the community.
- Weakness: limited scope—only 100 questions per dataset may limit generalizability and risk sampling bias.
- Weakness: inherent subjectivity in skill annotation; disagreements could affect results despite high agreement.
- Weakness: analysis shows correlation but does not establish causation between readability and skills/difficulty.
- Weakness: readability metrics chosen for human readability may not reflect machine difficulty; alternative model-centric metrics suggested.
- Weakness: lacks system-level evaluation to show whether metrics predict actual RC model performance.
- Question: request for more detail on the sampling method and representativeness (e.g., stratified sampling).
- Question: request for description and analysis of annotation disagreements and particularly challenging skills.
- Question: ask whether authors plan to use metrics to predict or debug RC model performance.
- Question: ask if authors will explore readability or other metrics better suited to machine performance.
- Question: ask whether annotator confidence levels were considered or quantified.
- Additional: related work coverage is reasonable; writing is clear and appendices add transparency.
- Overall: solid, valuable contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to address raised concerns.
