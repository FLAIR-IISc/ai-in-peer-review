- Proposes a nuanced RC evaluation framework using “prerequisite skills” and “readability” instead of relying on accuracy alone.  
- Refines skill categorization grounded in cognitive psychology and textual entailment theory.  
- Annotates and publicly releases six RC datasets with the proposed metrics.  
- Empirically finds a weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty.  
- Strong theoretical grounding linking metrics to human text comprehension models.  
- Addresses a critical gap by offering a practical alternative to accuracy-focused evaluation.  
- Annotation methodology is thorough with high inter-annotator agreement (90.1%).  
- Paper is clearly written and presents results in an accessible, well-organized manner.  
- The released annotated datasets are a valuable community resource.  
- Skill evaluation is limited to required skills for correct answers and doesn't diagnose misidentifications or flawed categorizations.  
- Readability metrics used are human-focused and may not reflect RC systems’ specific challenges.  
- Lacks system-level analysis showing how RC models perform relative to the annotated skills.  
- Annotation task is complex and subjective, raising concerns about bias and the need for clearer training/disagreement procedures.  
- Sentence distance metric is overly simplistic and ignores syntactic relationships that may matter.  
- Asks authors how to adapt methodology to diagnose why systems fail (misidentification vs. skill weakness).  
- Questions whether NLP-focused readability measures were considered and requests justification if not.  
- Requests any preliminary system performance analyses on the annotated datasets.  
- Asks for details on annotator bias mitigation, training, and disagreement resolution.  
- Requests justification for using linear sentence distance and whether syntactic metrics would improve analysis.  
- Notes appendices are useful, suggests expanding related work on explainable RC, and cautions about applying the framework to datasets not originally designed for this purpose.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution, recommend acceptance with minor revisions emphasizing system analysis and annotation/metric refinements.
