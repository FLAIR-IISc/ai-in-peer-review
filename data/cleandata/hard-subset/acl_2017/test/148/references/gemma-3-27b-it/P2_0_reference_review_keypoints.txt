- Overall: Valuable investigation proposing prerequisite-skills and readability metrics for RC evaluation; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary: Argues accuracy is insufficient, annotates six RC datasets for prerequisite skills and readability, finds weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.  
- Contribution 1: Provides detailed annotations and analysis of six widely used RC datasets, offering a useful public resource.  
- Contribution 2: Refines prerequisite-skill taxonomy by integrating insights from textual entailment and cognitive psychology.  
- Contribution 3: Empirically demonstrates a disconnect between text readability and question difficulty in RC datasets.  
- Strength 1: Strong motivation and clear problem statement with illustrative examples.  
- Strength 2: Solid theoretical grounding in human text comprehension and textual entailment literature.  
- Strength 3: Rigorous, well-documented annotation process with high inter-annotator agreement (90.1%).  
- Strength 4: Public release of annotated datasets to support reproducibility and further research.  
- Strength 5: Authors acknowledge limitations and discuss future work avenues.  
- Weakness 1: Skill annotation is inherently subjective; uncertainty/distribution of annotations not fully reported.  
- Weakness 2: Readability metrics used may miss discourse-level factors, domain knowledge, and deeper RC-relevant difficulty.  
- Weakness 3: Analysis shows correlation, not causation, between readability and prerequisite skills; other confounders not explored.  
- Weakness 4: Focus on entailment narrows scope—does not evaluate sentence selection competence or broader RC failure modes.  
- Question 1: Please report annotation uncertainty and per-skill agreement distributions—were some skills harder to annotate?  
- Question 2: Justify chosen readability metrics and discuss consideration of discourse coherence or domain-specific measures.  
- Question 3: How might sentence-selection competence be incorporated into the framework (e.g., a separate annotation stage)?  
- Question 4: How does an entailment-focused methodology complement other RC evaluation techniques like knowledge integration and inference generation?  
- Additional comment 1: Figures (e.g., 3 & 4) effectively visualize metric relationships.  
- Additional comment 2: Related work discussion is thorough and situates contributions well.  
- Additional comment 3: Writing is clear and accessible.  
- Conclusion: Paper is a meaningful contribution with some methodological limits; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
