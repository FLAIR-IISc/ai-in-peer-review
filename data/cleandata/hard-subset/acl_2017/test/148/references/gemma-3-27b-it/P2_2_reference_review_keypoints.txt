- Proposal: Framework to evaluate RC datasets via prerequisite skills and readability; annotated six datasets and found weak readability–difficulty correlation.
- Contribution: Presents a comprehensive, theory-grounded taxonomy of prerequisite skills for RC.
- Contribution: Empirically annotates and analyzes six popular RC datasets, revealing dataset characteristics and weak readability–difficulty links.
- Contribution: Releases the annotated datasets publicly to support future research.
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation drawing on cognitive science (e.g., Kintsch’s C-I model) and textual entailment literature.
- Strength: Broad metric selection covering logical reasoning, causal relations, bridging inferences, and readability measures.
- Strength: Annotated datasets are practically useful for training difficulty predictors, evaluating dataset quality, and crafting targeted training sets.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation of methodology and results.
- Strength: Important observation that higher textual readability does not strongly predict question difficulty.
- Weakness: Annotation subjectivity and insufficient detail on annotation guidelines, disagreement resolution, and annotator backgrounds despite reported 90.1% agreement.
- Weakness: Narrow operationalization of difficulty as the number of required skills, ignoring differences in skill type and inherent complexity.
- Weakness: Methodological limitation in only evaluating comprehension given relevant sentences, not the crucial task of sentence selection.
- Weakness: Correlation analysis relies on Pearson’s correlation, which may be inappropriate; requires alternative statistical approaches and clearer interpretation of “weak.”
- Question: Please provide detailed annotation guidelines, examples of disagreement resolution, and annotator expertise.
- Question: Did you consider weighting different prerequisite skills by relative difficulty, and is that a viable extension?
- Question: Any plans to evaluate sentence selection (e.g., compare performance with pre-highlighted relevant sentences vs. not)?
- Question: Were alternative correlation measures beyond Pearson’s explored, and what were the results?
- Additional: Appendices A and B were missing from the submission—please include them.
- Additional: Discuss practical implications and concrete recommendations for creating more challenging and informative RC datasets.
- Overall: Solid and valuable contribution with strong grounding and useful resources; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing noted issues.
