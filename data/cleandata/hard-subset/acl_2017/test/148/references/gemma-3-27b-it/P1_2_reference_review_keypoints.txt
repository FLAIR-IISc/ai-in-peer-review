- Paper proposes a framework of "prerequisite skills" and "readability" metrics to evaluate RC datasets beyond simple accuracy.
- Framework grounded in cognitive psychology (McNamara & Magliano, Kintsch) and connected to textual entailment research.
- Authors annotate six popular RC datasets with the proposed metrics and release the annotations as a community resource.
- Empirical analysis shows a weak correlation between readability and question difficulty across datasets.
- Strong motivation and clear problem statement, with effective illustrative examples.
- Solid theoretical foundation lending credibility to the prerequisite-skills metric.
- Thorough annotation methodology, including inter-annotator agreement and reasonable dataset selection.
- The annotated datasets are a practical, impactful resource for researchers.
- Discussion thoughtfully addresses corpus genre effects and the need for more sophisticated question types.
- Analysis scope is limited—mostly descriptive statistics without deep correlations to system performance or model-specific failure modes.
- Skill annotation is subjective; potential bias and ambiguity remain despite reported agreement.
- Readability metrics used are limited; other linguistic features (discourse, argument complexity) are not explored.
- Annotation focuses on sentences required to answer questions, not systems’ ability to identify those sentences.
- Requests for clarification: any correlations between metrics and RC system performance?
- Requests for clarification: how was annotation consistency enforced and how were ambiguous cases resolved?
- Requests for clarification: plans to expand readability metrics to include discourse-level features?
- Requests for clarification: availability of annotation tool and plans for easier data access (e.g., website).
- Additional notes: Figure 1 is helpful, references are comprehensive, paper is well-written and organized.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing weaknesses and questions.
