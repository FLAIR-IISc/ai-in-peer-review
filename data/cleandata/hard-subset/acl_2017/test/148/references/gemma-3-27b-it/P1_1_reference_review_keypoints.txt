- Paper proposes nuanced RC evaluation using "prerequisite skills" and readability metrics, annotating six datasets and finding a weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.
- Primary contribution: publicly released annotations for six established RC datasets, adding richer metadata for targeted evaluation.
- Refined prerequisite-skill taxonomy grounded in textual entailment and cognitive models, offering a more theoretically sound framework.
- Empirical finding: weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty, challenging the assumption that complex text yields harder questions.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem definition, with an illustrative example showing the readability–difficulty disconnect.
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation linking NLP and psychological models of comprehension.
- Strength: comprehensive selection of readability metrics from prior work for assessing textual complexity.
- Strength: practical value of public release for dataset design, targeted evaluation, and potential curriculum learning.
- Strength: detailed annotation methodology with reported inter-annotator agreement, increasing reliability.
- Weakness: limited scope—annotation of six datasets may not generalize across different domains, formats, or task types.
- Weakness: annotations do not evaluate a system’s ability to find relevant information in context, only skills once sentences are identified.
- Weakness: paper reports weak correlation but does not investigate causal reasons for the disconnect between readability and difficulty.
- Weakness: readability metrics are human-focused and may not align with RC system difficulty; alternative or system-specific metrics are not explored.
- Weakness: lacks statistical significance testing to support observed trends and differences across datasets.
- Question: how could the methodology be extended to evaluate systems' ability to locate relevant information, and what challenges would that pose?
- Question: what hypotheses explain the weak readability–difficulty correlation, and are some question types (e.g., commonsense) more prone to this?
- Question: have the authors considered machine-specific readability metrics or developing new metrics tailored to RC models?
- Question: can statistical analyses (e.g., ANOVA) be provided to substantiate trends in skill distributions across datasets?
- Question: how should researchers balance corpus genres (news vs. narratives) when constructing RC datasets?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written with comprehensive references; recommend adding a table summarizing annotated datasets (source, size, characteristics).
- Overall assessment: valuable and publishable contribution (notably the refined skills and annotated resource); recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address listed weaknesses.
