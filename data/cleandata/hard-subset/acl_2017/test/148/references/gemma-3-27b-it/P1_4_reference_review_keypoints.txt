- Paper proposes evaluating RC datasets using two metric classes: prerequisite skills and readability, annotated on six datasets.  
- Finds a weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty, implying many questions need little reading or complex reasoning on simple text.  
- Contribution: a comprehensive, multi-faceted framework for RC dataset evaluation beyond accuracy.  
- Contribution: refined, theoretically grounded categorization of prerequisite skills based on psychology and textual entailment.  
- Contribution: publicly released annotated datasets to aid RC research and dataset design.  
- Strength: addresses a critical gap by analyzing why systems succeed/fail rather than just reporting accuracy.  
- Strength: strong theoretical grounding drawing from cognitive science and NLP literature.  
- Strength: thorough annotation methodology with clear guidelines, inter-annotator agreement, and nonsense question identification.  
- Strength: clear, insightful results with dataset-specific analyses highlighting important dataset characteristics.  
- Weakness: framework measures comprehension complexity but not the difficulty of locating relevant information in context.  
- Weakness: prerequisite skill annotation is subjective; reported agreement may mask nuanced disagreements.  
- Weakness: reports correlation without investigating causal reasons for the weak relationship between readability and skill requirements.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of how these metrics relate to actual system performance on RC tasks.  
- Question: how to extend the framework to capture information-retrieval difficulty (e.g., sentence position, highlighting)?  
- Question: details on annotation disagreements—were some skills harder to categorize than others?  
- Question: hypotheses or analyses explaining why readability and prerequisite skills correlate weakly, and dataset-specific contributors?  
- Question: plans to evaluate RC systems using these metrics and the anticipated experimental setup?  
- Additional: include more discussion of dataset bias and evaluation limitations, a visual skill hierarchy, and public release of annotation and readability code.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address noted weaknesses and questions.
