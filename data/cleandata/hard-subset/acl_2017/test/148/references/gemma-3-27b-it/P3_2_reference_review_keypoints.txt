- Proposes a framework for RC evaluation based on prerequisite cognitive skills and context readability, annotating six datasets and finding a weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.

- Contributions: articulates a nuanced evaluation framework beyond accuracy focused on skills and readability.

- Contributions: refines prerequisite skill categories grounded in cognitive psychology and textual entailment theory.

- Contributions: provides publicly released annotations for six widely used RC datasets.

- Strength: clear motivation and well-illustrated problem framing highlighting limits of current RC evaluation.

- Strength: strong theoretical grounding using cognitive psychology and linguistics to inform metric definitions.

- Strength: rigorous annotation methodology with high inter-annotator agreement (90.1%) and attention to dataset biases.

- Strength: insightful results showing weak readability–difficulty correlation and dataset-specific analyses.

- Strength: public release of annotated datasets benefits the research community.

- Weakness: scope limited to descriptive analysis; lacks correlation of metrics with actual system performance.

- Weakness: annotation omits sentence-selection competence and relies on subjective annotator judgments.

- Weakness: identifies correlations but does not investigate causal mechanisms behind findings.

- Weakness: limited justification for the specific readability metrics chosen; theoretical bases underexplained.

- Weakness: some skill definitions (e.g., commonsense reasoning) are overly broad, risking annotation ambiguity.

- Question: have you correlated your metrics with RC system performance on the annotated datasets?

- Question: how could sentence selection be incorporated into the evaluation framework (e.g., via attention or sentence-selection evaluation)?

- Question: how was ambiguity handled during annotation and what training ensured consistent application of broader skill labels?

- Question: could prerequisite skills be weighted by importance or difficulty, and how would such weights be determined?

- Question: can parts of the skill annotation be automated using NLP tools (coreference, causal extraction), and what challenges remain?

- Additional: writing is generally clear but descriptions of readability metrics need more detail.

- Additional: references are comprehensive and relevant.

- Additional: consider adding a table of typical RC system performance per dataset to contextualize metrics.

- Overall assessment: strong, theoretically grounded contribution with useful dataset annotations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
