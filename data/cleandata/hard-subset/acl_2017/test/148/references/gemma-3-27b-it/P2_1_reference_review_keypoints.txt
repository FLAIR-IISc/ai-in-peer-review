- Summary: Paper proposes evaluating RC datasets using two axes—prerequisite cognitive skills and readability—annotates six datasets, and shows weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.
- Contribution: Advocates a nuanced evaluation framework moving beyond accuracy to explain why RC systems succeed or fail.
- Contribution: Proposes a theoretically grounded, refined categorization of prerequisite skills drawing from cognitive psychology and textual entailment.
- Contribution: Publishes annotations for six existing RC datasets, providing a public resource for future work.
- Strength: Addresses a crucial gap by incorporating qualitative, cognitive aspects into RC evaluation.
- Strength: Strong theoretical grounding in established cognitive and NLP literature.
- Strength: Substantial, publicly available annotation effort across six datasets.
- Strength: Clear presentation with illustrative examples and well-organized exposition.
- Strength: Important finding that readability and question difficulty are only weakly correlated.
- Weakness: Analysis is mainly descriptive; lacks deeper statistical modeling to link skills/readability to system performance.
- Weakness: Annotation reliability concerns due to limited detail on annotator training and disagreement resolution despite reported inter-annotator agreement.
- Weakness: Method only assesses understanding of contextual entailments and not information retrieval/finding within text.
- Weakness: Potential overlap and redundancy among prerequisite skill categories (e.g., bridging vs. elaboration) complicates interpretation.
- Questions for authors: Provide more detailed statistical analyses linking skills/readability to model performance.
- Questions for authors: Describe annotation procedures, training materials, and disagreement resolution in detail.
- Questions for authors: Explain how annotators distinguished overlapping skills and guidelines used.
- Questions for authors: Clarify how these metrics can be used to design targeted RC datasets, with concrete examples.
- Additional comments: References are well-curated; discussion should be expanded on implications for robust, human-like RC systems.
- Additional comments: Suggest examining connections between identified difficulty levels and specific model error types.
- Overall assessment: Solid, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address analysis and annotation concerns.
