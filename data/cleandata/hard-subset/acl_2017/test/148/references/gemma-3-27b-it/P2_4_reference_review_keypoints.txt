- Summary: Proposes a framework evaluating RC datasets via "prerequisite skills" and "readability", refines skill categories, annotates six datasets, and finds weak correlation between readability and question difficulty.

- Contribution — Framework: Introduces a nuanced evaluation framework for RC beyond accuracy that incorporates cognitive skill requirements.

- Contribution — Skill categorization: Refines prerequisite-skill taxonomy grounded in textual entailment and cognitive psychology.

- Contribution — Data release: Provides publicly released annotations for six RC datasets.

- Strength: Addresses a critical need by moving evaluation beyond accuracy and exposing dataset issues.

- Strength: Strong theoretical grounding in cognitive psychology and textual entailment.

- Strength: Detailed annotation methodology with sensible multi-labeling and sentence-distance quantification.

- Strength: Insightful empirical finding that readability poorly predicts question difficulty, with useful dataset-specific observations.

- Weakness: Analysis is mainly descriptive (skill frequencies) and lacks evaluation of how metrics correlate with actual RC system performance.

- Weakness: Annotation reliability concerns — inter-annotator agreement reported but no breakdown of where disagreements occurred.

- Weakness: Skill assignment is inherently subjective and the paper lacks detail on annotator training and bias mitigation.

- Weakness: Methodological limitation — annotation focuses on required sentence identification, not on systems' ability to retrieve those sentences.

- Question: Have the authors analyzed correlations between these metrics and performance of models like BERT or RoBERTa?

- Question: Can the authors provide a detailed breakdown of inter-annotator disagreements and which skills were hardest to label?

- Question: What training, guidelines, and examples were provided to annotators to ensure consistent skill labeling?

- Question: Are there plans to extend the work to evaluate sentence retrieval in addition to sentence selection?

- Additional comment: Paper would benefit from clearer visualizations (scatter plots, heatmaps) to show metric correlations.

- Additional comment: Discuss the computational cost of applying these metrics to assess practicality and adoption barriers.

- Overall assessment: Promising and valuable contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions pending responses on evaluation scope and annotation details.
