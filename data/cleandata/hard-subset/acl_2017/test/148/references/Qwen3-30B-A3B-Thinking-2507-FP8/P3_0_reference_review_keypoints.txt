- Summary: Proposes a framework evaluating RC datasets by prerequisite skills and text readability, grounded in psychological theory; annotates six datasets and finds a weak correlation between question difficulty and readability.

- Contribution 1: Theoretically refines prerequisite-skill classification using psychological text-comprehension and textual-entailment research.

- Contribution 2: Empirically analyzes six major RC datasets across the two dimensions, revealing dataset-specific skill/readability patterns.

- Contribution 3: Publishes dataset annotations as a community resource to guide dataset construction and system development.

- Strength 1: Strong theoretical foundation linking skill taxonomy to established cognitive and entailment theories.

- Strength 2: Significant empirical finding that question difficulty and text readability are only weakly correlated, with illustrative dataset examples.

- Strength 3: Practical utility via a clear evaluation methodology and publicly available annotations, with suggested use in curriculum learning.

- Weakness 1: No connection to actual RC system performance—no experiments showing how metrics relate to model accuracy.

- Weakness 2: Annotation methodology cannot assess sentence-selection competence; sentence-distance analysis is only in the appendix and not integrated.

- Weakness 3: "Nonsense" question category is ambiguous and lacks clear annotation criteria or adjudication details.

- Weakness 4: Limited practical implementation details—insufficient concrete examples for applying metrics (e.g., curriculum design, dataset construction).

- Weakness 5: No validation against human performance to confirm that metrics reflect human-perceived cognitive difficulty.

- Question 1: How will you connect these metrics to actual system performance—will you run experiments relating model success across datasets with similar skill requirements?

- Question 2: Can you provide concrete dataset examples that clearly show high readability with many skills required and vice versa?

- Question 3: How does the inability to evaluate sentence-selection competence affect your conclusions, and how might you address this limitation?

- Question 4: What specific criteria and annotator agreement procedures were used to label questions as "nonsense"?

- Question 5: How should dataset creators practically apply your metrics—can you provide a step-by-step guide or concrete workflow?

- Overall recommendation: Accept with major revisions to address the lack of system-performance linkage and annotation-methodology shortcomings.
