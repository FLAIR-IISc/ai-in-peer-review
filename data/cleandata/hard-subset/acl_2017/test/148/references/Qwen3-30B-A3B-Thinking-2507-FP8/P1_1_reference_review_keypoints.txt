- Paper proposes two RC evaluation metric classes: prerequisite skills (refined to 13) and readability, arguing for more nuanced dataset analysis than superficial metrics.  
- Authors annotate six RC datasets and find only a weak correlation between question difficulty (prerequisite skills) and text readability.  
- Main contributions: systematic annotation of six datasets, demonstration of weak difficulty–readability correlation, and a revised prerequisite skills taxonomy grounded in psychology and entailment research.  
- Strength: Addresses a gap in RC research by providing a framework for more meaningful dataset quality and difficulty analysis.  
- Strength: Revision of prerequisite skills is theoretically grounded in established cognitive and textual-entailment literature.  
- Strength: Empirical analysis across six datasets offers concrete evidence challenging assumptions linking text difficulty to question difficulty.  
- Strength: Practical guidance for dataset construction and curriculum learning in RC system development.  
- Strength: Annotation results are publicly released, supporting community reuse.  
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for changing the skill taxonomy from 10 to 13 skills and for specific merges (e.g., statistical with mathematical reasoning).  
- Weakness: No statistical significance testing reported for the observed weak correlations (coefficients 0.025–0.416).  
- Weakness: Annotation methodology under-described—training, disagreement resolution, and rationale for using three annotators are not detailed despite reporting 90.1% agreement.  
- Weakness: Limited comparison with other RC dataset-evaluation frameworks and prior work (e.g., Chen et al., 2016).  
- Weakness: Discussion of limitations is underdeveloped, particularly regarding inability to evaluate sentence-selection competence and its impact on results.  
- Questions: Request for statistical significance testing of correlations to support the weak-correlation claim.  
- Questions: Request for detailed justification of taxonomy revisions and specific skill merges.  
- Questions: Ask how the sentence-selection evaluation limitation affects result interpretation and what further work is needed.  
- Questions: Ask for a more thorough comparison between this metric framework and existing evaluation approaches.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept conditional on addressing statistical testing, stronger justification for taxonomy changes, clearer annotation methodology, and expanded discussion of limitations and related work.
