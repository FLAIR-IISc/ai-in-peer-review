- Paper proposes a framework to evaluate reading comprehension datasets using two metric classes: prerequisite skills and readability.
- Contribution: a refined taxonomy of 13 prerequisite skills grounded in psychological theories of text comprehension.
- Contribution: a comprehensive annotation of six major RC datasets (QA4MRE, MCTest, SQuAD, Who-did-What, MS MARCO, NewsQA) with public release.
- Contribution: empirical finding that prerequisite-skill-based question difficulty is only weakly correlated with context readability.
- Strength: addresses a critical gap by offering a meaningful, qualitative evaluation framework beyond superficial dataset categories.
- Strength: taxonomy is theoretically grounded and refines prior work (e.g., distinguishing bridging vs. elaboration inferences).
- Strength: provides a valuable public resource with robust annotation (90.1% inter-annotator agreement) and dataset specifications.
- Weakness: limited practical utility—paper lacks concrete examples or evidence of how to use metrics to improve RC systems (curriculum learning analogy too vague).
- Weakness: methodological limitation—framework does not evaluate competence in locating necessary sentences or answer candidates within contexts.
- Weakness: insufficient treatment of "nonsense" questions—identifies the problem (e.g., in MS MARCO) but offers no clear detection or mitigation strategy.
- Weakness: weak correlation analysis lacks deeper exploration of why the correlation is weak and its implications for dataset design and evaluation.
- Question: How would the metrics be specifically used to guide RC system development—concrete examples requested?
- Question: How can the framework be extended to evaluate sentence selection/IR or attention mechanisms critical to RC tasks?
- Question: How should dataset creators prevent or detect "nonsense" questions without relying on dataset-specific meta-information?
- Overall recommendation: valuable and significant contribution but practical and methodological gaps warrant revision; reviewer recommends borderline acceptance pending fixes.
