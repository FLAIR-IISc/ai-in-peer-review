- Proposes a dual-metric framework evaluating RC datasets via prerequisite skills and text readability.  
- Refines a theoretically grounded taxonomy of prerequisite skills drawing on psychological and NLP literature.  
- Annotates six major RC datasets with these metrics and analyzes skill frequency across datasets.  
- Finds a weak correlation between question difficulty (prerequisite skills) and text readability.  

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation using Kintsch, McNamara & Magliano, and entailment research.  
- Strength: Valuable empirical analysis with dataset annotations and detailed statistics (e.g., QA4MRE, MCTest).  
- Strength: Practical insight challenging assumptions about readability and question difficulty, affecting curriculum learning.  
- Strength: Clear methodological transparency, including annotation process and 90.1% inter-annotator agreement.  

- Weakness: No empirical demonstration that the metrics improve or predict actual RC system performance.  
- Weakness: Arbitrary exclusion of several major datasets (e.g., CBTest, LAMBADA) without sufficient justification.  
- Weakness: Annotation method cannot evaluate sentence selection competence and provides limited analysis of sentence distance.  
- Weakness: The taxonomy has theoretical inconsistencies (e.g., bridging vs. elaboration, status of meta-knowledge).  
- Weakness: Limited validation of metric validity against human difficulty or system performance.  

- Question: Could readability correlate more strongly with question difficulty at the question level rather than the dataset level?  
- Question: How should metrics account for target audiences (e.g., MCTest designed for children vs. adult/readability measures)?  
- Question: How do nonsense questions in MS MARCO affect metric interpretation and how should annotation handle them?  
- Question: What specific theoretical or empirical justifications differentiate the revised taxonomy from prior work (e.g., Sugawara et al.)?  

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept with major revisions needed to connect metrics to system performance, justify dataset choices, and address methodological/theoretical issues.  
- Additional suggestion: Expand practical guidance for using metrics in dataset creation and curriculum design, reconsider CBTest exclusion, and relate these metrics to standard RC evaluation measures (F1, EM).
