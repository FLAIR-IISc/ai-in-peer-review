- Summary: Proposes framework evaluating RC datasets via "prerequisite skills" and "readability," finding weak correlation between question difficulty and text readability across six datasets.

- Contribution 1: Systematic, theoretically grounded analysis showing question difficulty and text readability are largely independent dimensions.

- Contribution 2: Refines Sugawara et al.'s prerequisite skills into a reorganized, entailment- and inference-based classification.

- Contribution 3: Publishes annotations for six major RC datasets as a community resource.

- Strength 1: Addresses a major gap in dataset evaluation by demonstrating that readability does not imply easier questions, with implications for dataset design.

- Strength 2: Methodologically rigorous with clear metrics, high inter-annotator agreement (90.1%), and thorough statistical analysis.

- Strength 3: Practical value for curriculum learning and system development; annotated datasets are publicly released.

- Weakness 1: Prerequisite skills reclassification is somewhat incremental with limited justification for superiority over prior work.

- Weakness 2: Analysis limited to six datasets, excluding prominent ones (e.g., CNN/Daily Mail), reducing generalizability.

- Weakness 3: Does not investigate how the proposed metrics relate to actual system performance or identify system-specific weaknesses.

- Weakness 4: Insufficient treatment and definition of "nonsense" or unsolvable questions, despite noting high rates in some datasets.

- Weakness 5: Limited discussion of the framework's inability to evaluate competence in selecting necessary sentences and the practical impact of this limitation.

- Question 1: How can the framework identify or filter "nonsense" questions without a clear solvability definition?

- Question 2: How can the methodology reconcile claims about contextual entailment understanding with its inability to assess sentence-selection competence?

- Question 3: How would the framework be concretely applied to design curriculum learning sequences and is there evidence it improves system performance?

- Recommendation: Accept (minor revisions); valuable and well-executed work but should broaden dataset scope and strengthen links to system performance — Score: 7 (Strong Accept).
