- Summary: Proposes a framework evaluating RC datasets along two orthogonal dimensions—prerequisite skills and readability—and annotates six datasets, finding weak correlation between the dimensions.  
- Contribution 1: Introduces a principled two-dimensional framework to evaluate RC datasets beyond superficial metrics.  
- Contribution 2: Refines prerequisite-skill taxonomy using insights from textual entailment and psychology.  
- Contribution 3: Provides large-scale, public annotations of six RC datasets with the proposed metrics.  
- Strength 1: Addresses a significant gap by moving beyond superficial dataset evaluation metrics.  
- Strength 2: Grounds the framework in established psychological theories of text comprehension.  
- Strength 3: Annotation process is comprehensive and well-documented with high inter-annotator agreement (90.1%).  
- Strength 4: Produces valuable empirical insights, e.g., weak link between readability and question difficulty.  
- Strength 5: Public release of annotations offers practical utility for future dataset design and curriculum learning.  
- Weakness 1: Unclear, insufficiently defined methodology for labeling “nonsense” (unsolvable) questions.  
- Weakness 2: Limited practical guidance on how to use the metrics for system development or curriculum learning.  
- Weakness 3: No analysis of how the proposed metrics correlate with actual system performance.  
- Weakness 4: Lacks comparison to other existing RC evaluation frameworks.  
- Weakness 5: Unclear handling and impact of multiple-choice formats on skill annotation validity.  
- Question 1: How were “nonsense” questions operationally defined and how were annotator disagreements resolved?  
- Question 2: How could the limitation of not evaluating sentence-selection competence be addressed in future work?  
- Question 3: Can you give concrete examples/steps for applying these metrics in curriculum learning for RC systems?  
- Question 4: How do metrics generalize across answer formats (open-ended vs. multiple-choice) and does showing choices affect skill labels?  
- Question 5: How was NewsQA’s meta information on question quality produced and how reliable is it relative to annotators?  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with solid theory and useful data; accept with minor revisions to clarify methodology and strengthen analysis.
