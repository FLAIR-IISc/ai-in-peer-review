- Proposes a framework with two complementary metrics (prerequisite skills and readability) to evaluate RC datasets and annotates six existing datasets.  
- Refines prior prerequisite-skill taxonomy with psychological and NLP grounding (13 skills, distinguishing bridging vs. elaboration).  
- Empirically shows only a weak correlation between text readability and question difficulty across six RC datasets.  

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation tying the skills taxonomy to cognitive psychology and textual-entailment work.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical finding that challenges the assumption that harder-to-read texts yield harder questions.  
- Strength: Offers a practical, curriculum-like stepwise methodology for RC system development.  

- Weakness: Metrics do not capture sentence-selection / retrieval competence, a key component of RC.  
- Weakness: Limited dataset scope—excludes major datasets (e.g., CNN/Daily Mail, CBTest, LAMBADA) without sufficient justification.  
- Weakness: No comparison with other evaluation frameworks or prior dataset-quality work to contextualize novelty.  
- Weakness: Insufficient concrete guidance or examples for implementing the proposed stepwise system-development approach.  
- Weakness: Surface-level treatment of the “nonsense question” issue in datasets like MS MARCO and Who-did-What.  

- Q: How will you extend the framework to evaluate selection/retrieval of relevant sentences?  
- Q: How do you reconcile MCTest’s easy readability with question difficulty—are genre/narrative factors at play?  
- Q: How should researchers handle datasets with many nonsense questions when using these metrics for system development?  
- Q: How can datasets standardize meta-information (e.g., question reliability) as NewsQA does?  

- Additional: Paper is well-written with useful figures/tables and publicly released annotations.  
- Additional: Would benefit from integration guidance into existing RC evaluation pipelines and a concrete case study.
