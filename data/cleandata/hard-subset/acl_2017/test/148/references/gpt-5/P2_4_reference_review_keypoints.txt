- Proposes two complementary evaluation perspectives for RC datasets: a 13-skill prerequisite taxonomy and readability metrics applied to answer-containing sentences.  
- Annotates 100 questions from each of six RC datasets and releases the annotations.  
- Core conceptual contribution: a theory-grounded, reorganized taxonomy distinguishing bridging vs elaboration and other skills.  
- Empirical contribution: cross-dataset comparative analysis showing weak correlation between readability and required skills.  
- Practical contribution: public annotations to support dataset construction and targeted evaluation.  
- Strength: taxonomy grounded in textual entailment and cognitive psychology, making labels more principled.  
- Strength: uniform cross-dataset analysis yields informative profiles and genre/sourcing insights (e.g., QA4MRE, MCTest, WDW).  
- Strength: actionable recommendations for dataset design, highlighting genre effects and issues from automatic question generation.  
- Strength: clear negative result that readability ≠ answer difficulty (weak Pearson correlations reported).  
- Strength: release of annotations improves reproducibility and enables finer-grained evaluation.  
- Weakness: assumes "number of skills" proxies difficulty without validating against human or system performance.  
- Weakness: small sample size (100 per dataset, 62 for IAA) and differing sampling protocols may bias comparisons.  
- Weakness: annotation protocol (providing gold answer/choices) may bias toward textual-entailment framing and understate reading difficulty.  
- Weakness: reliability reporting is insufficient (unclear agreement metric, no per-skill κ/α or "nonsense" reliability).  
- Weakness: limited statistical analysis and controls (no significance tests, CIs, or confound controls) and some numerical/table inconsistencies.  
- Question: How was inter-annotator agreement computed and what are per-skill reliability scores, including for "nonsense"?  
- Question: Can you validate skill-count as a difficulty proxy via correlations with human accuracy/RT or model performance?  
- Question: How do readability–skill correlations change when controlling for context length, sentence count, and sentence distance?  
- Question: Do conclusions change if readability is computed on full context rather than annotator-selected sentences?  
- Question: Will you release full annotation guidelines with positive/negative examples for each skill?  
- Overall: Valuable, theory-grounded analysis with useful annotations; acceptance recommended pending validation of difficulty claims, fuller reliability reporting, and stronger statistical analysis.
