- Proposes a two-pronged evaluation for RC: a refined prerequisite-skill taxonomy and readability metrics, applied to six datasets and showing weak correlation between readability and skill count.  
- Contribution: theory-informed reorganization of skills, notably splitting knowledge reasoning into bridging vs elaboration.  
- Contribution: systematic multi-dataset annotation revealing qualitative differences and weak coupling between readability and skill demand.  
- Contribution: release of annotated data to support dataset construction and curriculum-style system development.  

- Strength: Conceptual grounding and taxonomy refinement linking to RTE and cognitive models for a more principled diagnostic framework.  
- Strength: Cross-dataset comparative analysis (e.g., QA4MRE skill-heavy, MCTest easy-to-read but multi-sentence, SQuAD hard-to-read but local) and identification of “nonsense” questions.  
- Strength: Clear empirical separation of reading difficulty vs answering difficulty, cautioning against using readability as a proxy for question difficulty.  
- Strength: Transparent annotation protocol, sampling choices, and documentation supporting reproducibility.  
- Strength: Practical implications for dataset creators and curriculum-style system builders.  

- Weakness: Methodological confound—annotators see gold answers and preselect sentences, bypassing retrieval and likely underestimating task difficulty.  
- Weakness: Reliability reporting is incomplete—uses percent agreement only, lacks chance-corrected IAA, per-skill and sentence-selection agreement.  
- Weakness: Readability metrics are limited (L2 features), computed only on selected sentences, and correlation analysis lacks statistical rigor and controls.  
- Weakness: Taxonomy boundaries (bridging vs elaboration vs coreference vs meta-knowledge) can be fuzzy with no reported dispute rates or discriminant validation.  
- Weakness: Sampling and “nonsense” labeling are potentially unrepresentative and inconsistent across datasets (only 100 items each, varying selection rules).  
- Weakness: No validation linking metrics to actual model or human performance to demonstrate predictive utility.  

- Question: Report chance-corrected inter-annotator agreement per skill and on sentence selection—how often do annotators disagree?  
- Question: How was “nonsense” defined and adjudicated; were borderline items quantified?  
- Question: Were correlations computed within datasets with appropriate statistical controls (mixed-effects), and are p-values/confidence intervals provided?  
- Question: Why compute readability only on selected sentences; how do results change when computed on whole passages or union spans?  
- Question: Will you release full annotation guidelines, examples, and the annotation tool for replication?  

- Suggestion: Augment readability with discourse-level features (entity-grid, PDTB connectives, coreference density) and structural features (paragraph/sentence count).  
- Suggestion: Normalize distance measures by document length for fair cross-dataset comparison.  
- Suggestion: Provide error analyses showing representative bridging vs elaboration items and common confusions among skills.  
- Suggestion: Improve statistical rigor, reliability reporting, and validate metrics against human/model difficulty to increase impact.
