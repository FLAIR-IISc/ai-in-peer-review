- Summary: Proposes an evaluation framework for RC datasets using prerequisite-skill taxonomy and readability features, and annotates six datasets with sentence selections.
- Contribution: Refines a 13-skill taxonomy, reorganizing knowledge reasoning into bridging, elaboration, and meta-knowledge grounded in literature.
- Contribution: Computes readability features (largely following Vajjala and Meurers) for selected evidence sentences.
- Contribution: Publicly annotates six RC datasets with skills and necessary-sentence selections and reports frequencies, readability, and correlations.
- Main finding: Readability and question difficulty (proxied by required skills) are only weakly correlated across datasets.
- Strength: Taxonomy is literature-grounded, interpretable, and better anchored than prior ad hoc schemes.
- Strength: Cross-dataset annotation enables comparability and reveals dataset-specific skill profiles (e.g., QA4MRE, MCTest, WDW).
- Strength: The negative result (weak readability–difficulty correlation) is practically useful for dataset design.
- Strength: Discussion offers practical guidance for curriculum/transfer learning and promises public release of resources.
- Strength: Methodological transparency about limitations, sampling, annotation inputs, and feature choices.
- Weakness: Core assumption (skill count = difficulty) is not validated against model or human performance.
- Weakness: Small sample size (100 questions/dataset) and insufficient statistical reporting (no p-values, CIs, or confound controls).
- Weakness: Agreement reporting is inadequate for a multilabel task; lacking per-skill kappa and sentence-selection reliability.
- Weakness: Annotation protocol may bias skill attribution due to annotators seeing gold answers and selecting sentences.
- Weakness: Readability features are limited (ESL-focused) and omit discourse/coreference/surprisal factors, possibly biasing results.
- Question: Provide per-skill inter-annotator agreement (e.g., kappa) and sentence-selection overlap statistics.
- Question: Does skill count correlate with model error or human difficulty (time/accuracy) even in a small validation?
- Question: Share annotator training, the annotation manual, and per-question sentence selections upon release.
- Question: Report statistical significance (p-values/CIs) and partial correlations controlling for sentence count/length and alternate features.
- Overall assessment: Valuable taxonomy and cross-dataset resource with useful insights, but weakened by limited validation, agreement reporting, and statistical rigor; borderline but positive contribution.
