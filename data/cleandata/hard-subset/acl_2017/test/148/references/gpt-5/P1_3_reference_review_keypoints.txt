- Paper proposes a two-pronged RC evaluation: a refined 13-skill taxonomy and readability metrics applied to RC datasets.
- Authors annotated 100 questions each from six datasets (QA4MRE, MCTest, SQuAD, Who-did-What, MS MARCO, NewsQA) and will release annotations.
- Main empirical finding: weak correlation between reading difficulty and number of prerequisite skills required to answer questions.
- Contribution 1: Cross-dataset human analysis showing weak readability–skill-count correlation and dataset-specific skill profiles.
- Contribution 2: A more principled prerequisite skill taxonomy (bridging vs. elaboration) with an annotation protocol.
- Contribution 3: Publicly available skill annotations across six prominent RC datasets.
- Strength: Provides clear, actionable empirical insight challenging assumptions that readability tracks answering difficulty.
- Strength: Uniform annotation across diverse datasets enables direct comparisons of dataset characteristics.
- Strength: Skill taxonomy is theoretically grounded in textual entailment and cognitive psychology.
- Strength: Paper offers practical recommendations for dataset design, curriculum learning, and genre considerations.
- Strength: Authors are transparent about scope, sampling choices, and dataset-specific limitations.
- Weakness: Assumes skill count maps to difficulty without validating against human judgments or system performance.
- Weakness: Small sample size (100 per dataset) and sampling constraints may bias conclusions for large datasets.
- Weakness: Inter-annotator agreement reporting is insufficient—likely raw agreement reported without kappas or per-skill reliability.
- Weakness: Readability features used may miss discourse-level complexity important for RC (entity coherence, connectives).
- Weakness: Annotation procedure (annotators given answers and required sentences) cannot assess retrieval/span-selection components of RC.
- Questions: Request details on how agreement was computed, per-skill kappas, dispute resolution, and aggregation of multi-skill labels.
- Questions: Ask for statistical significance/confidence intervals for reported correlations and alternative correlation measures.
- Questions: Why only top-10 Vajjala & Meurers features and FK grade; were discourse/cohesion features tested?
- Questions: Any preliminary evidence linking skill counts to human response time/accuracy or model errors?
- Additional comment: Release detailed annotation guidelines and per-skill examples to improve reproducibility.
- Additional comment: Report distributions (not just means) for sentence counts, distances, and skill counts, and stratify by question type.
- Additional comment: A small-scale validation relating skill profiles to model performance would strengthen practical impact.
- Additional comment: Clarify the meta-knowledge skill boundary with elaboration and provide more examples.
