- Contribution 1: Proposes a theory-grounded taxonomy of prerequisite skills for RC, splitting “knowledge reasoning” into bridging vs. elaboration and adding ellipsis and meta-knowledge.  
- Contribution 2: Applies the taxonomy to six RC datasets (QA4MRE, MCTest, SQuAD, WDW, MS MARCO, NewsQA) with sentence selection and readability features to produce dataset profiles.  
- Contribution 3: Finds weak correlation between context readability and estimated difficulty (number of prerequisite skills), with dataset case studies (e.g., MCTest vs. SQuAD).  

- Strength: Taxonomy is conceptually grounded in RTE and cognitive psychology, improving over ad-hoc commonsense buckets.  
- Strength: Uniform, cross-dataset annotation reveals actionable comparative insights (e.g., high ellipsis in WDW; structural complexity in QA4MRE).  
- Strength: Resource value — planned release of annotated examples can seed per-skill evaluation and dataset curation.  
- Strength: Clear scope — focuses on contextual entailment rather than retrieval/span selection.  
- Strength: Additional analyses (readability on selected sentences, sentence-distance) add construct validity and insight into multi-sentence reasoning.  

- Weakness: Inter-annotator agreement is under-specified — unclear computation method, lack of chance-corrected metrics, and small sample (62 questions).  
- Weakness: Equating number of skills with difficulty is unvalidated; no correlation with human difficulty ratings or system performance.  
- Weakness: Limited sampling and experimental design (100 questions/dataset, sampling heuristics, dev-only subsets) reduce generalizability and may bias distributions.  
- Weakness: Annotation protocol biases results by exposing gold answers and removing retrieval/span selection, limiting dataset-level conclusions.  
- Weakness: Readability analysis lacks statistical rigor (feature selection, parsing quality, absence of significance tests, non-parametric checks, CIs).  
- Weakness: “Nonsense” label is underspecified and shows annotator disagreement, yet is used to assess dataset quality.  
- Weakness: Inconsistencies and clarity issues in reporting (e.g., QA4MRE average skills discrepancy, missing methodological details).  

- Question: How was multi-label inter-annotator agreement computed (overall and per-skill), and please report chance-corrected measures and “nonsense” agreement.  
- Question: Reconcile the QA4MRE average skills discrepancy (4.6 in text vs. 3.25 in Table 3).  
- Question: Which readability metrics were used in correlations (FK alone or full set), and were correlations tested for significance/robustness (Spearman, bootstrapped CIs)?  
- Question: Will you release annotation guidelines and code, and can you report sensitivity analyses (multiple sampling seeds and CIs for frequencies/correlations)?  

- Overall: Originality moderate (thoughtful taxonomy, builds on prior work); soundness mixed due to reliability/statistical gaps; clarity generally good but with some inconsistencies; significance moderate if resources released.  
- Recommendation: Borderline — lean toward acceptance as a short/resource/analysis paper if reliability/statistical reporting is strengthened and discrepancies corrected; otherwise weak reject for validation/reporting gaps.
