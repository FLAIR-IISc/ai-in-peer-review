- Summary: Proposes a neural adaptation of the entity grid with end-to-end training and distributed representations, showing improved performance on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.
- Contribution 1: Neuralization of the entity grid to learn distributed entity-transition representations.
- Contribution 2: End-to-end, task-specific feature learning instead of handcrafted features.
- Contribution 3: Strong empirical, state-of-the-art results across multiple coherence evaluation tasks.
- Strength 1: Clear motivation and connection to coherence literature and Centering Theory.
- Strength 2: Sound technical approach using CNNs with a well-explained embedding–convolution–pooling–scoring pipeline.
- Strength 3: Comprehensive evaluation on three tasks using established datasets and strong baselines.
- Strength 4: Statistically significant performance improvements with significance testing.
- Strength 5: Code release promoting reproducibility.
- Weakness 1: Limited architectural novelty—standard CNN+embeddings; novelty mainly in application.
- Weakness 2: Superficial incorporation of entity-specific features via simple concatenation.
- Weakness 3: Lack of analysis of learned embeddings and filter weights to interpret what the model learns.
- Weakness 4: Insufficient comparison to other recent neural coherence models beyond one cited example.
- Question 1: Why was a CNN chosen and what other architectures were considered or rejected?
- Question 2: Are there plans to integrate entity-specific features more deeply (e.g., attention or alternative embeddings)?
- Question 3: Can the authors provide insights or interpretable patterns from learned embeddings or filters?
- Question 4: Has an ablation study been performed to quantify contributions of embedding, convolution, and feature components?
- Additional comment 1: Paper is well-written and clearly organized.
- Additional comment 2: References are comprehensive.
- Additional comment 3: Tables and figures effectively present results.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper warranting acceptance; addressing representation analysis and architectural justification would further strengthen it.
- Recommendation: Accept.
