- Proposes a neural adaptation of the entity grid using CNNs to model local text coherence with distributed representations.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements and state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  
- Shows benefit of end-to-end learning of coherence features, reducing need for hand-engineering.  
- Strong baseline comparisons including extended entity grid and graph-based models.  
- Clear motivation and explanation connecting convolutional design to entity-grid assumptions (column independence, location-invariant transitions).  
- Consistent, statistically significant experimental gains, notably on the insertion task.  
- Code release enhances reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful figures.  
- Architecture novelty is limited: using CNNs and distributed embeddings is standard in NLP.  
- Entity-specific feature integration is basic (simple concatenation) and could be more sophisticated.  
- Missing ablation study to quantify contributions of components (convolutions vs. entity features).  
- Limited error analysis; lacks examples of model failures and challenging coherence phenomena.  
- Sparse details on pre-training for summary coherence rating (dataset and hyperparameters unclear).  
- Requests clarification on which architectural choices are critical and how they differ from standard CNN uses in NLP.  
- Asks what other entity-specific features were considered and why excluded.  
- Requests that authors perform an ablation study to isolate component effects.  
- Requests illustrative error analysis with failure cases and explanations.  
- Requests full pre-training details (dataset, hyperparameters).  
- Reference list is comprehensive and current.  
- Use of the Brown coherence toolkit aids reproducibility.  
- Suggests adding visualizations (embeddings or filters) to show what the model learns.  
- Overall: strong, effective contribution to coherence modeling; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (add ablation, error analysis, pre-training details).
