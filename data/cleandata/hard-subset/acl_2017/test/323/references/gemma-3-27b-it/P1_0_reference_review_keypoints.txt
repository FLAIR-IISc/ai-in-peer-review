- Proposes a neural network adaptation of the entity grid for local text coherence using distributed representations and end-to-end training.  
- Uses convolutional layers to model long-range entity transitions and includes entity-specific features.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  
- Neuralization of entity grid enables continuous representations and learning of complex entity-transition patterns.  
- End-to-end learning removes need for hand-engineered features and learns task-specific representations.  
- Strong empirical evidence of practical effectiveness across three standard coherence tasks.  
- Clear problem motivation and connection to Centering Theory.  
- Sound technical choice: CNNs effectively capture long-range dependencies in the entity grid.  
- Comprehensive evaluation using established datasets and strong baselines.  
- Code released, supporting reproducibility.  
- Significant performance gains (up to ~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion, >4% summary rating).  
- Architecture itself lacks deep novelty—application of standard CNNs rather than a new model.  
- Ablation studies are limited; unclear contribution of individual entity-specific features.  
- Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning methodology and search space.  
- Dataset limitations: WSJ and especially the small summary coherence set may limit generalizability.
