- Summary: Proposes a neural entity-grid model using distributed representations and end-to-end training, showing improved coherence evaluation on discrimination, insertion, and summary rating tasks.
- Contribution: Neuralized entity grid that captures longer-range entity transitions via distributed representations.
- Contribution: Strong empirical improvements and state-of-the-art results on three standard coherence benchmarks.
- Contribution: End-to-end learning removes manual feature engineering and learns task-specific features automatically.
- Strength: Strong experimental validation with standard benchmarks and statistical significance testing.
- Strength: Clear motivation and well-defined problem statement addressing limits of traditional grids.
- Strength: Architecture is clearly explained with helpful diagram and layer descriptions.
- Strength: Code release enhances reproducibility and practical impact.
- Strength: Thorough and thoughtful positioning within related work.
- Weakness: Core neural architecture (CNN + embeddings) is standard; novelty is mainly in application rather than architectural innovation.
- Weakness: Ablation study is insufficient—missing analysis of embedding sizes, filter sizes, pooling strategies, etc.
- Weakness: Focuses on local coherence and gives limited discussion of global/long-range discourse limitations.
- Weakness: Reliance on coreference resolution is not analyzed; potential error propagation is unaddressed.
- Question: Please provide a more detailed ablation study over hyperparameters and architectural choices.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to coreference resolution errors and to different coreference systems?
- Question: Any plans or directions to model longer-range/global discourse (e.g., recurrent or transformer architectures)?
- Question: Can you provide error analysis with examples of failure cases and problematic coherence phenomena?
- Additional comment: Clarify the reference to "SIGF V.2" for readers unfamiliar with the significance test.
- Additional comment: Consider streamlining related work by grouping papers thematically rather than chronologically.
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed contribution with strong results and code release; recommended for acceptance despite limited architectural novelty.
