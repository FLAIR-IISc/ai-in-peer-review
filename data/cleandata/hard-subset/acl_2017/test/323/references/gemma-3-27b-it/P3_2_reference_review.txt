## Review of "A Neural Local Coherence Model"

**Summary:** This paper presents a neural network architecture for assessing local text coherence, building upon the established entity grid framework. The authors address limitations of traditional entity grid models – namely, discrete representations and task-decoupled feature computation – by utilizing distributed representations and end-to-end training. They demonstrate improvements over existing methods across three standard coherence evaluation tasks: discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Neuralization of the Entity Grid:** The core contribution is a successful adaptation of the entity grid concept into a neural framework using convolutional neural networks. This allows for modeling longer-range dependencies and avoids the curse of dimensionality inherent in traditional, discrete representations.
2. **Demonstrated Empirical Improvements:** The paper provides solid empirical evidence showing consistent improvements across three benchmark tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results. The gains, particularly on the insertion task, are encouraging.
3. **End-to-End Learning of Coherence Features:** The authors successfully demonstrate the benefit of learning task-specific features directly from the data, circumventing the need for hand-engineered features.

**Strengths:**

1. **Solid Baseline Comparison:** The authors rigorously compare their model against a range of established coherence models, including the widely used extended entity grid model.  The inclusion of a graph-based model also provides a valuable point of comparison.
2. **Clear and Well-Motivated Approach:** The paper clearly articulates the shortcomings of existing entity grid models and logically motivates the proposed neural architecture as a solution. The explanation of the convolutional approach and its connection to the underlying assumptions (independence of columns, location-invariant transitions) is well-reasoned.
3. **Strong Experimental Results:** The consistent improvements across all three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model. The statistical significance tests add credibility to the findings.  The fact that gains are seen even without extensive feature engineering is notable.
4. **Reproducibility:** The release of the code is a significant strength, fostering reproducibility and facilitating further research.
5. **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is well-written, logically organized, and easy to follow.  The figures are helpful in illustrating the architecture and process.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of Architecture:** While the application of CNNs to the entity grid framework is effective, the overall architecture is relatively standard.  The core idea of using distributed representations and convolutional layers is not entirely novel in NLP. The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work using similar architectural components in different contexts.
2. **Feature Incorporation is Basic:** The incorporation of entity-specific features, while shown to be beneficial, is implemented in a straightforward manner (concatenation). Exploring more sophisticated ways to integrate these features into the neural network could potentially yield further improvements.  The authors acknowledge this implicitly but could expand on potential avenues for future work.
3. **Ablation Study is Missing:**  The paper would be stronger with an ablation study to understand the relative contribution of different components of the model – for example, the impact of the convolutional layers versus the entity-specific features.  This would provide valuable insights into which aspects of the architecture are most critical for performance.
4. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:** While the results are positive, the paper lacks a detailed analysis of where the model struggles.  Understanding the types of coherence issues that remain challenging for the model could inform future research directions.
5. **Pre-training details are sparse:** The summary coherence rating experiments show benefit from pre-training, but the details of *how* and *where* the pre-training was done are not fully explained.



**Questions to Authors:**

1. **Architectural Novelty:**  Could you elaborate on the specific design choices within the neural architecture that are most critical for modeling coherence, and how these differ from standard CNN applications in other NLP tasks?
2. **Feature Engineering:**  What other entity-specific features did you consider incorporating into the model, and why were they ultimately excluded?
3. **Ablation Study:**  Would you be willing to perform an ablation study to assess the individual contributions of the convolutional layers and the entity-specific features to overall performance?
4. **Error Analysis:** Can you provide some examples of cases where the model fails to accurately assess coherence, and discuss potential reasons for these failures?
5. **Pre-training Details:** Please clarify the exact details of the pre-training process used for the summary coherence rating task. What dataset was used, and what hyperparameters were employed?




**Additional Comments:**

* The reference list is comprehensive and up-to-date.
* The use of the Brown coherence toolkit is helpful for reproducibility.
* Consider adding a visualization of the learned embeddings or convolutional filters to provide further insight into what the model is learning.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of coherence modeling.  The neuralization of the entity grid framework is effective and well-supported by experimental results.  While the architecture itself isn't revolutionary, the successful application to this problem and the demonstrated improvements over existing methods are significant.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly the inclusion of an ablation study and a more in-depth error analysis, would further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
