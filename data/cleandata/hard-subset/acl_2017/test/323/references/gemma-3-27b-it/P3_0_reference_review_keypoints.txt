- Proposes a neural adaptation of the entity grid using distributed representations and CNNs with end-to-end training.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.
- Releases code to support reproducibility.
- Thorough comparisons to strong baselines, including traditional entity grids and recent deep-learning work.
- Design choices (CNNs for long-range dependencies, end-to-end feature learning) are well motivated.
- Evaluated comprehensively across three diverse coherence tasks.
- Paper is clear and well-written with helpful figures and organized experiments.
- Incorporates simple entity-specific features that yield a notable performance boost.
- Neural components (CNNs, embeddings, pooling) are standard; novelty is mainly in adaptation/application.
- Insufficient analysis of learned representations (no visualizations of embeddings or filters).
- Heavy dependence on coreference resolution without analysis of error propagation or mitigation.
- Lacks detailed hyperparameter sensitivity or robustness analysis.
- Requests to authors: analyze coreference error impact and mitigation, share representation visualizations, report ablation results on more entity features, and provide computational cost comparisons.
- Suggests providing pre-trained models and explicitly naming the coreference system used.
- Overall assessment: solid, empirically strong work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
