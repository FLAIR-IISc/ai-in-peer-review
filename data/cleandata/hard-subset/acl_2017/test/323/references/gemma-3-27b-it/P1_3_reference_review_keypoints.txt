- Presents a neural convolutional architecture that neuralizes the entity grid for local text coherence assessment, trained end-to-end.
- Uses distributed representations of entity transitions to address discrete representations and task-decoupled features in prior entity grid models.
- Evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks, achieving state-of-the-art and consistent improvements over non-neural baselines.
- Contribution: successfully applies neural (convolutional) modeling to entity grids, capturing longer-range dependencies and richer features.
- Contribution: demonstrates benefits of end-to-end training to learn task-specific coherence features without hand engineering.
- Contribution: provides strong empirical results with statistical significance across multiple tasks.
- Strength: clear motivation and precise problem definition highlighting limitations of prior entity grid approaches.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across three standard tasks with multiple baselines and significance testing.
- Strength: well-explained architecture and design choices, with helpful figures illustrating information flow.
- Strength: practical impact through state-of-the-art results and released code for reproducibility and extension.
- Strength: effective, simple incorporation of entity-specific features showing performance gains.
- Weakness: limited architectural novelty—uses standard components (embeddings, conv layers, pooling).
- Weakness: no ablation study to quantify each component’s contribution (e.g., conv layers, entity features).
- Weakness: limited qualitative/error analysis; paper focuses mainly on quantitative results.
- Weakness: dataset limitations—scarce training data for summary coherence and domain narrowness from WSJ.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to other recent neural coherence models and unclear reasons for Li & Hovy (2014) exclusion.
- Question: provide ablation results to show impact of removing convolutional layers and entity-specific features.
- Question: include error analysis with example failures and explanations for model mistakes.
- Question: report computational cost (training time, memory) versus baselines to assess trade-offs.
- Question: clarify setup and troubleshooting for Li & Hovy (2014) comparisons and poor performance on their dataset.
- Question: report hyperparameter sensitivity (embedding size, filter size, dropout) and performance variation.
- Additional comment: paper is well-written with comprehensive references.
- Additional comment: improvements’ statistical significance should be indicated directly in result tables.
- Additional comment: reference artifact (“\Q2012\E”) should be fixed.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with strong empirical results; acceptance recommended pending ablation and error-analysis additions.
