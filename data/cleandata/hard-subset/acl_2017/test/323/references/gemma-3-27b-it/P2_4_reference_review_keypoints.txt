- Proposes a neural adaptation of the entity grid using distributed representations and end-to-end training, achieving state-of-the-art on multiple coherence tasks.
- Core contribution: effective neuralization of the entity grid to capture longer-range dependencies.
- Core contribution: end-to-end learning that replaces hand-engineered features with task-specific learned representations.
- Core contribution: strong empirical results across discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.
- Strength: clearly motivated problem statement and justification for a neural approach.
- Strength: technically sound architecture (CNN over entity grid) that learns relevant features automatically.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation across three established coherence tasks.
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art performance on all evaluated tasks.
- Strength: authors release code, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited novelty—main ideas (entity grid, coherence as ranking) are established; novelty is primarily in implementation.
- Weakness: experiments mainly on WSJ news data, risking genre-specific bias and limited generalization.
- Weakness: ablation study lacks granular analysis of individual components (embedding size, filter size, pooling).
- Weakness: limited qualitative/error analysis to explain failure cases.
- Question: have you evaluated or considered performance on other genres (narrative, scientific, dialogue)?
- Question: what is the specific impact of individual entity-specific features on performance?
- Question: what are the computational costs (training/inference) compared to traditional entity grid models?
- Question: can you provide examples and analysis of cases where the model fails to assess coherence?
- Additional comment: paper is well-written and clearly organized.
- Additional comment: references are comprehensive and relevant.
- Additional comment: related work discussion is thorough.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with strong empirical evidence; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
