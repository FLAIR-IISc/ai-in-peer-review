- Paper proposes a neural network that replaces the discrete entity grid with distributed representations and end-to-end training to assess local text coherence, showing improvements on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.

- Main contribution 1: Neuralization of the entity grid—replaces handcrafted features with a learned neural architecture.

- Main contribution 2: Demonstrated consistent, statistically significant empirical improvements (state-of-the-art) across three standard coherence tasks.

- Main contribution 3: End-to-end training enabling the model to learn task-specific features directly from data.

- Strength: Clear problem formulation and a well-justified motivation for a neural approach to coherence modeling.

- Strength: Strong empirical results, including a 2.5% gain on insertion and >4% on summary coherence rating, with statistical significance testing.

- Strength: Uses well-established evaluation protocols and datasets, facilitating direct comparison and reproducibility.

- Strength: CNNs are a principled fit for the grid representation; design choices (wide convolutions, max pooling) are reasonably justified.

- Strength: Authors released code, enhancing reproducibility and follow-up work.

- Weakness: Limited architectural novelty—the model uses fairly standard CNN+embedding components, with novelty mainly in application.

- Weakness: Paper lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness to parameter settings.

- Weakness: Insufficient ablation studies to isolate the contributions of individual components (entity features, embedding sizes, filter sizes).

- Weakness: Dataset limitations—evaluation mainly on WSJ may limit claims about generalizability across genres.

- Question 1: Provide detailed ablation studies isolating entity-specific features and convolutional parameter effects.

- Question 2: Report sensitivity of performance to key hyperparameters (embedding size, learning rate, dropout) with plots/tables.

- Question 3: Provide error analysis with example failures and discussion of residual coherence challenges.

- Question 4: Report computational cost (training and inference) relative to traditional entity grid approaches and justify complexity vs. gains.

- Question 5: Discuss generalizability to other coherence-related tasks (e.g., text generation, machine translation).

- Additional comment: Writing is generally good but could be more concise in places.

- Additional comment: Related work coverage is thorough.

- Additional comment: More visualizations of the entity grid and neural architecture (beyond Figure 2) would be helpful.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper with valuable contributions and compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
