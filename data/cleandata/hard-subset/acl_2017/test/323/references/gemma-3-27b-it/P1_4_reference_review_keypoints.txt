- Paper proposes a neural network version of the entity grid for local coherence, using distributed representations and end-to-end training, evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.
- Contribution: Replaces discrete entity-grid representations with learned distributed embeddings to capture longer-range dependencies and reduce sparsity.
- Contribution: Uses end-to-end training to learn task-specific coherence features instead of manual feature engineering.
- Contribution: Reports state-of-the-art empirical results across three standard coherence evaluation tasks.
- Strength: Directly addresses discrete-representation limitations of entity-grid models; CNN over the grid is a sensible design for capturing transitions.
- Strength: Thorough experimental validation with consistent improvements over strong baselines and statistical significance testing.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation with helpful figures explaining the model.
- Strength: Commits to code release, improving reproducibility.
- Strength: Demonstrates easy incorporation of entity-specific features, bridging neural and prior approaches.
- Weakness: Neural architecture novelty is limited—application-focused rather than architecturally groundbreaking; comparisons to other sequence models are thin.
- Weakness: Ablation study is insufficient; needs experiments on embedding sizes, filter counts, pooling strategies, and removal of entity-specific features.
- Weakness: Dataset/task limitations—WSJ reliance may limit generalizability to other domains, and the summary coherence dataset is small.
- Weakness: Lacks qualitative analysis or examples showing what entity transitions the model leverages.
- Question: Why was a CNN chosen over alternatives like RNNs or transformers, and how do they compare?
- Question: How would rhetorical relations be incorporated—via entity features or architectural changes?
- Question: Have data augmentation or transfer learning been considered to address the small summary coherence dataset?
- Question: Can the authors provide a more detailed ablation breakdown (embedding size, filter number, etc.)?
- Question: Can the authors provide examples where the model succeeds or fails compared to prior models?
- Additional: Reference list is extensive and well-formatted.
- Additional: Use of standard tools (e.g., Brown coherence toolkit) is appreciated.
- Additional: Reword an awkward sentence about pairwise input in Section 3 and discuss computational complexity versus traditional entity-grid methods.
