## Review of "A Neural Local Coherence Model"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for assessing local text coherence, building upon the established entity grid framework. The authors address limitations of traditional entity grid models – namely, their discrete representations and task-decoupled feature computation – by leveraging distributed representations and end-to-end training. They demonstrate improvements over existing methods on three standard coherence evaluation tasks: discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Successful Neuralization of the Entity Grid:** The paper effectively translates the entity grid concept into a neural framework, demonstrating that learned distributed representations can improve performance compared to hand-engineered features. This is a solid engineering contribution.
2. **Empirical Improvement on Standard Tasks:** The consistent gains across three different coherence evaluation tasks, and achieving state-of-the-art results, provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
3. **End-to-End Training for Coherence:** The approach of training the model end-to-end for specific tasks is a step towards more adaptable and task-aware coherence models, albeit not a radically new concept in NLP.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing entity grid models and motivates the need for a neural approach. The connection to established theories of coherence is well-explained.
2. **Strong Empirical Results:**  The results presented consistently demonstrate improvements over strong baselines, including the established extended entity grid model.  The gains, while not enormous, are consistent and statistically significant. The ablation study (Extended vs Grid-CNN) is particularly convincing.
3. **Well-Established Evaluation Framework:** Using the standard discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks allows for a direct comparison with prior work and establishes a strong baseline for future research.
4. **Code Release:** The release of code is a significant positive, facilitating reproducibility and further research.
5. **Sound Methodology:** The use of convolutional neural networks seems well-suited to the task, allowing for the capture of local transition patterns. The incorporation of entity-specific features is also a logical extension.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty of the Architecture:** While the application to the entity grid is effective, the neural architecture itself (CNN with embeddings) is fairly standard. The core innovation lies more in *applying* this architecture to the specific problem of coherence modeling rather than a novel architectural contribution.
2. **Lack of Deep Analysis of Learned Representations:** The paper does not delve into *what* the model is learning.  Visualizing the learned embeddings, filter weights, or activation patterns could provide valuable insights into the model’s behavior and how it differs from traditional methods.  This would strengthen the understanding of *why* the model works.
3. **Dependence on Existing NLP Tools (Coreference Resolution):** The reliance on coreference resolution introduces a potential source of error and limits the end-to-end nature of the model. The quality of the coreference resolver could significantly impact performance.  This limitation isn't fully acknowledged.
4. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:**  A detailed error analysis, identifying types of coherence issues the model still struggles with, would be valuable.  Knowing where the model *fails* is as important as knowing where it succeeds.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide some examples of learned embeddings or filter weights to illustrate what features the model is capturing?
2.  How sensitive are the results to the performance of the coreference resolution system? Have you experimented with different coreference resolvers, and how do their performance differences affect your model?
3.  Could you provide examples of cases where the model performs poorly and discuss why these examples are challenging for the model?
4.  The introduction mentions the possibility of using pretrained embeddings. Have you experimented with pretrained embeddings (e.g., word2vec, GloVe) for the entity roles, and if so, what was the impact on performance?



**Additional Comments:**

*   The writing is clear and concise.
*   The paper is well-structured and easy to follow.
*   The references are comprehensive and relevant.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **Accepting** this paper. While the architectural novelty is limited, the successful application of neural networks to the established entity grid framework, combined with strong empirical results and a clear presentation, makes this a valuable contribution to the field of coherence modeling. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above (particularly the lack of analysis of learned representations and the dependence on coreference resolution) would further strengthen the work.
