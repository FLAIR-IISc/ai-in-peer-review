- Summary: Proposes a convolutional neural network that neuralizes the entity grid, uses distributed representations and end-to-end training, and reports state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.
- Contribution 1: Neuralization of the entity grid using a CNN to capture long-range entity transitions beyond discrete representations.
- Contribution 2: End-to-end, task-specific feature learning that improves coherence assessment across tasks.
- Contribution 3: Strong empirical results with consistent improvements (up to ~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion, >4% summary rating) establishing new state-of-the-art.
- Strength 1: Directly addresses entity-grid limitation of modeling long-range dependencies via distributed representations and convolutional modeling.
- Strength 2: Comprehensive evaluation across three diverse coherence tasks and reproduction on the WSJ benchmark.
- Strength 3: Achieves state-of-the-art performance with substantial reported gains over prior entity-grid baselines.
- Strength 4: Clear writing and motivation, with linkage to Centering Theory and prior work.
- Strength 5: Code release improves reproducibility and facilitates future research.
- Weakness 1: Limited architectural novelty—application of standard CNNs rather than new neural components or paradigms.
- Weakness 2: Entity-specific feature integration is superficial and limited to a subset of prior features; simple concatenation may be restrictive.
- Weakness 3: No ablation study to isolate contributions of distributed representations, convolutional layers, and entity features.
- Weakness 4: Dataset limitation—reliance on WSJ may limit generalizability to informal or conversational text.
- Question 1: Why choose CNNs for entity transitions—were RNNs or other architectures considered and rejected?
- Question 2: What are the challenges (e.g., computational cost, sparsity) in incorporating a larger set of entity-specific features and how might they be addressed?
- Question 3: Can the authors provide an ablation study showing the impact of each model component on performance?
- Question 4: How does the model perform on conversational or informal corpora beyond WSJ?
- Additional comment 1: Fix inconsistent citation formatting throughout the paper.
- Additional comment 2: Briefly discuss the computational complexity of the proposed model compared to traditional entity-grid approaches.
