## Review for "A Neural Local Coherence Model"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for assessing local text coherence, building upon the established entity grid framework. The authors address limitations of traditional entity grid models – namely, their reliance on discrete representations and task-agnostic feature computation – by using distributed representations and end-to-end training. They demonstrate improvements over existing methods on three standard coherence evaluation tasks: discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Neuralization of the Entity Grid:** The core contribution is adapting the entity grid, a well-established method for coherence modeling, into a neural framework. This allows for learning distributed representations of entity transitions, potentially capturing more nuanced relationships than discrete representations.
2. **End-to-End Training for Coherence:**  The proposed model learns task-specific features directly from data, moving away from handcrafted features. This is a valuable step towards more adaptable and potentially higher-performing coherence models.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates consistent and statistically significant improvements across multiple evaluation tasks, achieving state-of-the-art performance.

**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing entity grid models and motivates the need for a neural approach. The connection to Centering Theory and established coherence modeling literature is well-defined.
2. **Sound Technical Approach:**  The use of convolutional neural networks to process the entity grid is a logical choice, given the local nature of coherence and the ability of CNNs to capture patterns.  The modular design – embedding layer, convolution, pooling, scoring – is well-explained.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:**  Evaluating on three different tasks – discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating – provides a robust assessment of the model’s generalizability.  The use of established datasets and comparison to strong baselines is commendable.
4. **Statistically Significant Improvements:**  The reported gains over existing models are substantial and statistically significant, lending credibility to the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  The inclusion of statistical significance testing is a positive aspect.
5. **Code Release:** The authors have released their code, which promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Architecture:** While the application to the entity grid context is valuable, the neural network architecture itself (CNN with embeddings) is relatively standard. The novelty lies more in the *application* than in a novel architectural breakthrough.  The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of why this specific architecture is particularly well-suited to the coherence task.
2. **Feature Incorporation Could Be Deeper:** The incorporation of entity-specific features feels somewhat superficial. Simply concatenating features to the grid entry feels ad-hoc.  Exploring more integrated ways to incorporate these features into the neural network (e.g., through attention mechanisms or different embedding spaces) could further improve performance.
3. **Limited Analysis of Learned Representations:** The paper lacks detailed analysis of *what* the model is learning. Examining the learned embeddings or filter weights could provide valuable insights into how the model represents coherence and what features it finds most important.  
4. **Comparison to Recent Neural Coherence Models:** The paper briefly mentions a related neural model (Li and Hovy, 2014) but dismisses it due to poor results. A more detailed comparison to other recent neural approaches to coherence, beyond just this one example, would strengthen the paper.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Architectural Justification:** Can you elaborate on why you chose a CNN specifically for this task? What other architectures were considered, and why were they rejected?
2. **Feature Integration:**  Do you have plans to explore more sophisticated ways to incorporate entity-specific features into the neural network beyond simple concatenation? 
3. **Representation Analysis:**  Can you provide any insights into the learned embeddings or filter weights? Are there any discernible patterns or interpretable features that the model is capturing?
4. **Ablation Study:** Have you performed an ablation study to determine the relative contribution of the embedding layer, convolutional layers, and entity-specific features to the overall performance?



**Additional Comments:**

*  The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*  The reference list is comprehensive.
*  The use of tables and figures is effective in presenting the experimental results.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of coherence modeling. While the architectural novelty is limited, the successful application of neural networks to the entity grid framework, coupled with the strong empirical results and clear presentation, justifies acceptance.  Addressing the weaknesses identified above, particularly regarding representation analysis and a more thorough discussion of architectural choices, would further strengthen the paper.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
