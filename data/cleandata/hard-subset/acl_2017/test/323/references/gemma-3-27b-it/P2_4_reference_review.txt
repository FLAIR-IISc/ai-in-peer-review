## Review for "A Neural Local Coherence Model"

**Summary:**

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for assessing local text coherence, building upon the established entity grid framework. The authors address limitations of traditional entity grid models – namely, discrete representations and task-decoupled feature computation – by leveraging distributed representations and end-to-end training. They demonstrate improved performance on three standard coherence evaluation tasks (discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating), achieving state-of-the-art results.

**Main Contributions (as I see them):**

1. **Effective Neuralization of Entity Grid:** The paper successfully adapts the entity grid formalism to a neural network setting, overcoming the limitations of discrete representations and enabling the capture of longer-range dependencies. This is the core technical contribution.
2. **End-to-End Learning for Coherence:** The proposed end-to-end training approach allows the model to learn task-specific features automatically, a significant improvement over traditional feature engineering.
3. **Strong Empirical Results:**  The consistently reported improvements across multiple established evaluation tasks demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed approach and solidifies its contribution to the field.



**Strengths:**

1. **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The introduction clearly articulates the problem of coherence modeling, its importance, and the limitations of existing approaches. The motivation for using a neural approach is well-justified.
2. **Sound Technical Approach:** The use of convolutional neural networks to process the entity grid is a sensible and effective design choice. The architecture allows for capturing long-range dependencies and automatically learning relevant features. The explanation of the architecture is clear and well-structured.
3. **Comprehensive Evaluation:**  Evaluating on three different tasks (discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating) provides a robust assessment of the model's generalization ability and demonstrates its utility in diverse coherence-related applications.
4. **State-of-the-Art Performance:** Achieving state-of-the-art results on all three tasks is a significant accomplishment and provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed model.
5. **Code Release:** The authors’ decision to release their code is highly commendable and facilitates reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Novelty in Core Concepts:** While the application of CNNs to entity grids is a good engineering step, the fundamental concepts (entity grids, coherence as a ranking problem) are well-established. The novelty primarily lies in the *implementation* rather than the core theoretical idea.
2. **Dataset Choice and Potential Bias:** The primary experiments are conducted on the WSJ corpus. While a standard benchmark, the nature of news text might introduce biases that don’t generalize to other text genres (e.g., narrative, conversational).  Exploring performance on diverse datasets would strengthen the findings.
3. **Ablation Study Could be More Granular:** While the authors compare against baselines with and without entity-specific features, a more detailed ablation study exploring the contribution of each component (embedding size, filter size, pooling) would provide deeper insights into the model’s behavior.
4. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis:**  The paper focuses primarily on quantitative results. A qualitative error analysis – examining cases where the model fails – could reveal valuable insights into its strengths and weaknesses, and suggest directions for future improvement.




**Questions to Authors:**

1.  **Generalization to other genres:**  Have you considered evaluating your model on datasets representing different text genres (e.g., narrative, scientific writing, dialogue)? If so, what were the results? If not, can you speculate on how the model might perform on these other genres?
2. **Impact of Entity-Specific Features:** Could you elaborate on the specific impact of the entity-specific features? Did certain features contribute more significantly to the performance gains than others?
3. **Computational Cost:** What is the computational cost (training time, inference time) of your model compared to the traditional entity grid models? Is the increased performance worth the added computational burden?
4. **Error Analysis:** Can you provide examples of cases where your model fails to accurately assess coherence, and explain why the model might be making these errors?

**Additional Comments:**

*   The paper is well-written and clearly organized.
*   The references are comprehensive and relevant.
*   The discussion of related work is thorough.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper presents a solid contribution to the field of coherence modeling. While the core ideas are not groundbreaking, the effective neuralization of the entity grid framework and the strong empirical results make this a worthwhile publication. The weaknesses are relatively minor and can be addressed in future work. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the questions and concerns raised above.
