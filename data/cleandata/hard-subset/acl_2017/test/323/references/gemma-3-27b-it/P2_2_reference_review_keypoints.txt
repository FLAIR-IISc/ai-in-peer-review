- Proposes a CNN-based neural model over the entity grid to assess local text coherence, trained end-to-end and evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.
- Main contribution: neuralization of the entity grid, enabling distributed representations of entity transitions.
- Main contribution: end-to-end training to learn task-specific coherence features automatically.
- Main contribution: strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art across three coherence tasks.
- Strength: clear motivation and problem definition, articulating limitations of traditional entity grid models.
- Strength: solid experimental evaluation on WSJ and DUC’03 with multiple baselines and significance testing.
- Strength: well-explained architecture with a helpful diagram and clear rationale for using CNNs.
- Strength: reproducibility—authors have released code.
- Strength: consistent improvements over non-neural counterparts across all tasks.
- Weakness: limited architectural novelty—the use of CNNs is an incremental adaptation rather than a breakthrough.
- Weakness: some feature engineering remains (a small set of entity-specific features), so not fully purely learned.
- Weakness: limited error analysis; paper lacks detailed exploration of failure cases and coherence types the model struggles with.
- Weakness: related work discussion is shallow and doesn’t deeply engage broader discourse or neural discourse literature.
- Question: how do learned embeddings capture semantic relations between grammatical roles (S, O, X, -); any visualization or nearest-neighbor analysis?
- Question: have you tried a purely end-to-end model without pre-defined features, and how did it perform?
- Question: can you provide concrete failure examples and explanations for model errors (detailed error analysis)?
- Question: why are summary coherence rating results weaker and what are potential ways to improve performance in that task?
- Additional comment: experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning are clearly described.
- Additional comment: the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Overall assessment: a solid, well-executed contribution with strong empirical evidence; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address error analysis and deeper related-work engagement.
