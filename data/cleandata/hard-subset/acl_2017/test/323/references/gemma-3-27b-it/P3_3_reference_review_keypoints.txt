- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural architecture for local text coherence built on the entity grid, trained end-to-end and evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks with state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution: Neuralized entity grid—applies CNNs to capture long-range entity transitions within the entity grid framework.

- Contribution: Empirical improvements—consistent, substantial gains across three standard coherence tasks over strong baselines.

- Contribution: End-to-end training—learns task-specific features from data, avoiding manual feature engineering.

- Strength: Strong empirical results—reported gains (~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion, >4% summary rating) demonstrate clear benefits.

- Strength: Clear problem definition and motivation—well-articulated limitations of prior entity grid models and ties to Centering Theory.

- Strength: Well-chosen baselines—comparison includes comprehensive and strong baselines (extended entity grid).

- Strength: Solid experimental setup—proper data splits, hyperparameter tuning, significance testing, and code release for reproducibility.

- Strength: Effective integration of entity-specific features—simple method improves performance further.

- Weakness: Limited novelty in architecture—CNN + pooling are standard; novelty is mainly application rather than a new model.

- Weakness: Lack of ablation studies—missing detailed breakdown of each component’s contribution (embeddings, conv layers, pooling, filter sizes).

- Weakness: Limited analysis of learned representations—no visualization or interpretation of embeddings/filters to show what the model learns.

- Weakness: Small dataset for summary coherence rating—results on this task may be less robust and improvements smaller.

- Question: Why choose a CNN—what alternatives were considered and why were they rejected?

- Question: Can you provide detailed ablation results showing performance drops when removing components (embedding layer, conv layers, etc.)?

- Question: Have you analyzed learned embeddings or filters—can you show examples of learned coherent/incoherent patterns?

- Question: Can you provide error analysis with examples of incorrect predictions and types of coherence phenomena the model struggles with?

- Question: What is the computational cost (training/inference time) compared to existing entity grid models?

- Comment: Discuss limitations of the entity grid framework itself—are there inherent limits to modeling coherence via entity transitions?

- Comment: Clarify “pairwise input” in Figure 2—explain how input pairs are constructed and used during training.

- Comment: Expand on difficulty reproducing the distributed sentence model (Li & Hovy, 2014)—explain why it failed to reproduce.

- Overall assessment: Strong paper with effective neural enhancement of the entity grid and compelling empirical results; recommend acceptance pending responses/revisions addressing weaknesses.
