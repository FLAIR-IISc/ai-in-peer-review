- Paper proposes a neural extension of the entity grid model using distributed entity representations and CNNs to model local coherence end-to-end with pairwise ranking.

- Contribution: Neural architecture that models long-range entity transitions without the entity grid's curse of dimensionality.

- Contribution: End-to-end pairwise ranking training that learns task-specific features instead of decoupled feature extraction.

- Contribution: Strong empirical gains and state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.

- Strength: Well-motivated novel neural extension that clearly addresses identified limitations of the entity grid model.

- Strength: Statistically significant empirical improvements across multiple standard coherence tasks.

- Strength: Clear, comprehensive evaluation using standard datasets and comparisons to strong baselines.

- Strength: Practical contribution through public code release enabling reproducibility.

- Strength: Solid theoretical grounding linking to the entity grid and Centering Theory.

- Weakness: Limited comparison with other neural approaches and no clear explanation for poor replication of Li & Hovy (2014).

- Weakness: Little analysis of what the model actually learns (no examples or interpretation of salient entity transitions).

- Weakness: Weak theoretical justification for choosing a convolutional architecture over alternatives like RNNs or transformers.

- Weakness: Smaller gains on the insertion task without analysis of failure modes or adaptation strategies.

- Weakness: No ablation studies to quantify the contribution of components (CNN vs. distributed representations vs. entity features).

- Question: Why did the Li & Hovy (2014) model perform poorly in your implementation—architecture, data, or implementation issues?

- Question: How much of the performance gain comes from the neural architecture versus using a subset of Extended Grid features (request for ablation)?

- Question: Can you analyze insertion task failures and identify specific sentence/document types that cause problems?

- Question: How would the model generalize to smaller, constrained corpora (e.g., AIRPLANES, EARTHQUAKES), and can you provide results?

- Question: Have you compared to more recent neural coherence models (e.g., transformer-based) and considered such baselines?

- Overall recommendation: Strong accept for ACL — novel, well-executed work with significant empirical gains and useful code release despite some analysis and comparison gaps.
