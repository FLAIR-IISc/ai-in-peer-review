- Proposes a neural local coherence model building on the entity grid to address discrete role sparsity and decoupled feature extraction.  
- Uses distributed entity-transition representations and a CNN to model longer transitions (k=5–8) and learn task-specific features end-to-end.  

- Contribution: enables modeling of much longer entity transitions than prior entity-grid models.  
- Contribution: trains with a pairwise ranking loss to learn task-specific coherence features automatically.  
- Contribution: integrates entity-specific features (named entity type, salience, proper mention) into a neural framework.  

- Strength: yields consistent, statistically significant improvements across discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks (state-of-the-art).  
- Strength: clear technical exposition with helpful figures and equations.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on multiple tasks and datasets with comparisons to several baselines.  
- Strength: authors released code, supporting reproducibility.  
- Strength: approach is practically relevant to NLP tasks like generation and summarization.  

- Weakness: neural architecture components (embedding, convolution, pooling) are standard and not strongly justified versus other architectures (RNNs/Transformers).  
- Weakness: lacks detailed ablation studies to quantify each component's contribution to improvements.  
- Weakness: inadequate comparison with more recent neural coherence models beyond one prior attempt.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations — scalability to longer documents, computational cost, and domain variability.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis showing that longer transitions capture meaningful coherence patterns.  

- Question: Which specific entity features mattered most and why the neural model performs well with fewer features?  
- Question: Are smaller gains on insertion due to task difficulty or lack of task-specific training, and would fine-tuning help?  
- Question: How does the model perform on shorter documents where long transitions may be less useful?  
- Question: How would alternative sequence models (RNNs/Transformers) compare, and what are CNNs’ specific advantages here?  

- Overall recommendation: Strong paper, accept with minor revisions to add ablations, comparisons to recent neural work, and deeper analysis of why the model works.
