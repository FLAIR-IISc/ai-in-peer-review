- Contribution: Neural model using distributed representations to capture long-range entity transitions (k=5–8) without the curse of dimensionality.
- Contribution: End-to-end pairwise ranking training that automatically learns task-specific features.
- Contribution: Method to incorporate entity-specific features (named-entity type, salience, proper mention) without exponential parameter growth.
- Strength: Well-motivated technical extension; convolutional architecture plausibly models arbitrary-length, position-invariant transitions and addresses prior limitations.
- Strength: Strong empirical results—outperforms non-neural baselines on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence with statistically significant gains.
- Strength: Clear technical exposition, including role-to-distributed-representation mapping, convolutional transition modeling, and interpretable filter behavior.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—neuralizing the entity grid is incremental and prior neural coherence work is not thoroughly distinguished.
- Weakness: Insufficient ablation studies, so the relative contributions of the neural architecture versus entity-specific features are unclear.
- Weakness: Incomplete comparison to recent neural state-of-the-art; the reported poor performance of Li & Hovy (2014) lacks analysis.
- Question: How does this approach differ from Li and Hovy (2014), and why did their model perform poorly on your data?
- Question: Please provide detailed ablation studies isolating each component’s contribution.
- Question: Which Extended Grid features were omitted in Extended Grid-CNN, and how does that affect performance?
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—solid incremental contribution but needs clearer novelty claims, stronger comparisons to recent neural work, and more ablations.
