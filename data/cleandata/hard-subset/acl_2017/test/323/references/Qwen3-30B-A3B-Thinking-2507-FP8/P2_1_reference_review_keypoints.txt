- Summary: Proposes a neural extension of the entity grid model using distributed entity-transition embeddings and CNNs, evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks with consistent improvements.

- Contribution 1: Models longer-range entity transitions (k=5–8) via distributed representations, mitigating the curse of dimensionality.

- Contribution 2: Enables end-to-end training to learn task-specific coherence features rather than decoupled feature extraction.

- Contribution 3: Incorporates entity-specific features (named entity type, salience, proper mention) into the neural framework without exponential parameter growth.

- Strength 1: Clearly formulates the problem and provides a well-motivated neural solution that naturally extends the entity grid model.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive, rigorous evaluation with statistically significant gains (≈4% discrimination, 2.5% insertion, 4%+ summary coherence).

- Strength 3: Strong technical execution with detailed architecture, intuitive filter interpretations, and reproducible implementation details.

- Weakness 1: Limited justification for choosing CNNs over alternatives (RNNs/transformers) and no comparative ablation to support the architectural choice.

- Weakness 2: Lacks qualitative analysis and concrete examples of learned transition patterns to illustrate what the model captures.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient comparison to prior neural coherence work (e.g., Li & Hovy 2014) and unclear how differences yield improvements.

- Weakness 4: Little discussion of data efficiency—whether the neural model needs substantially more data than traditional entity grid models.

- Question 1: Provide evidence or analysis explaining why CNNs are particularly suitable versus RNNs or transformers for entity-transition modeling.

- Question 2: Give examples of coherence patterns that require transitions longer than k=3 and are captured by the model.

- Question 3: Clarify which entity-specific features mattered most and how the neural representations differ from discrete entity-grid encodings.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to strengthen architecture justification, add qualitative analysis, and compare more thoroughly to prior neural work.
