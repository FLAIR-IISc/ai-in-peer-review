- Proposed neural architecture for local coherence that addresses discrete representation limits and task-agnostic features.  
- Contributions: distributed representations to capture longer entity transitions (k≈5–8), end-to-end task-specific feature learning, and state-of-the-art results on three coherence tasks.  

- Strength: Clearly identifies prior limitations and theoretically motivates distributed representations and convolutions to avoid combinatorial explosion.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence with consistent improvements (≈4% discrimination, 2.5% insertion, >4% summary).  
- Strength: Architectural choices (convolution + max-pooling) are well-motivated and appropriate for modeling entity transitions.  

- Weakness: No ablation studies demonstrating how performance varies with transition length k, leaving the "long-range" claim unverified.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of data scarcity for the summary coherence task (only 144 training pairs) and lack of comparison to simpler models suited for small data.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to recent neural coherence models (e.g., Li & Hovy 2014) and lack of detailed differentiation from window-based approaches.  
- Weakness: Overstated claim about supporting "arbitrary" entity-specific features while only evaluating three features and not showing scalability.  

- Question: Please provide ablation results for performance versus transition length k (e.g., k=2–8).  
- Question: Justify using a neural model on the 144-pair summary dataset and compare to a linear model trained on the same data.  
- Question: Clarify the pre-training and fine-tuning procedure used for the summary coherence task and whether it follows standard practice.  

- Overall: Technically sound and well-motivated work with meaningful improvements; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address ablation and data-scarcity concerns and to qualify some claims.
