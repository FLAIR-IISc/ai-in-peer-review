- Proposes a neural local coherence model using distributed entity representations and convolutional layers to model longer-range entity transitions (5–8) trained end-to-end with pairwise ranking loss.

- Claims three main contributions: neural architecture for long-range transitions, end-to-end task-specific feature learning, and state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

- Strength: Clearly identifies and addresses the curse-of-dimensionality in discrete entity-grid models by using embeddings and convolutions to capture longer transitions.

- Strength: Provides comprehensive experiments across three standard coherence tasks and multiple datasets, justifying dataset choices for deep learning models.

- Strength: Reports strong, statistically significant improvements over baselines with results averaged over five random seeds and detailed hyperparameter tuning.

- Strength: Releases code to support reproducibility.

- Strength: Paper is well-written with clear presentation and an illustrative architecture diagram.

- Weakness: Core approach uses standard neural components (convolutions, embeddings) and lacks clear differentiation from prior neural coherence work like Li and Hovy (2014).

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis and interpretability—no inspection of convolutional filters or feature importance to explain why the model works.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to the most recent neural coherence models (e.g., transformer-based approaches) beyond entity-grid baselines.

- Weakness: Unclear rationale for selecting only 3 of 9 entity-specific features from Elsner & Charniak (2011) and no ablation study on feature contributions.

- Weakness: No discussion of computational complexity or runtime trade-offs for modeling longer transitions.

- Question: Why did Li & Hovy (2014)’s model yield poor results (19.34%)—implementation, dataset differences, or fundamental model gaps?

- Question: Why do transitions of length 5–8 work best—how do convolutional filters learn these, and is there a pattern to optimal lengths?

- Question: What motivated the selection of only three entity-specific features, and would including more (or all nine) improve performance?

- Question: How do the results compare to the latest neural coherence models (e.g., transformer-based methods) beyond entity-grid variants?

- Question: How exactly is pre-training on the discrimination task used for summary coherence rating, and is pre-training necessary for strong summary-task performance?

- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance at ACL based on a well-motivated neural solution, strong empirical gains, reproducibility, and meaningful contribution despite some novelty and analysis limitations.
