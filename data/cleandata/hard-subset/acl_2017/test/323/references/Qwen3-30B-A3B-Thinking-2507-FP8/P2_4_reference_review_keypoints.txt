- Paper proposes a neural extension of the entity grid model to address discrete representations and task-agnostic features in coherence modeling.
- Contribution: a convolutional neural architecture using distributed representations to model long-range entity transitions (k = 5–8).
- Contribution: an end-to-end pairwise ranking training method to learn task-specific features from entity grids.
- Contribution: consistent, statistically significant improvements over non-neural baselines on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.
- Strength: well-motivated neuralization that overcomes dimensionality issues and suits convolutional modeling of entity transitions.
- Strength: strong empirical gains across three evaluation tasks using a larger WSJ corpus, with statistically significant improvements.
- Strength: thorough experimental design including ablation studies and multiple strong baselines.
- Strength: clear technical presentation with sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.
- Weakness: novelty claim is weakened by overlooking Li and Hovy (2014) and insufficient comparison/analysis against that work.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of learned features and explanations for why the model works (no examples of filter-detected patterns).
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of cases where the model fails or broader limitations of the approach.
- Weakness: overstated “end-to-end” claim since entity grid construction remains a separate preprocessing step.
- Question: why does this model outperform Li and Hovy (2014) — were preprocessing, hyperparameters, or training procedures comparable?
- Question: rationale for selecting only three entity-specific features in Extended Grid-CNN and the impact of including more (e.g., all nine features)?
- Question: how could the training approach be modified to better address the insertion task, given permutation-based training may not capture single-sentence differences?
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed contribution with strong results but needs improved novelty comparison and deeper model analysis; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
