- Proposes a convolutional neural model over distributed entity transitions to address limitations of the entity grid for local coherence.
- Claims to overcome curse of dimensionality and model longer transitions (k=5–8) with end-to-end task-specific learning.
- Evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks with consistent improvements over entity grid baselines.
- Contribution: neural architecture for distributed entity transitions enabling longer-range transition modeling without exponential features.
- Contribution: end-to-end pairwise ranking training to learn task-specific coherence features automatically.
- Contribution: incorporation of entity-specific features (e.g., named entity types) without exponential parameter growth.
- Strength: significant and statistically robust performance gains (~4% discrimination and summary rating, up to 2.5% insertion).
- Strength: clear and detailed technical explanation of architecture and design choices (wide convolution, max-pooling).
- Strength: authors released code, aiding reproducibility and adoption.
- Strength: careful evaluation using standard tasks and appropriate baselines for comparability.
- Strength: practical handling of entity-specific features demonstrated without parameter explosion.
- Weakness: core approach (CNN over sequences) lacks strong novelty relative to existing neural NLP methods.
- Weakness: inadequate comparison to recent neural coherence models (e.g., Li and Hovy 2014) and no detailed explanation for excluding their results.
- Weakness: no qualitative analysis of learned patterns or convolutional filters to explain model behavior.
- Weakness: potential overfitting concerns on small datasets (e.g., 144 training pairs for summary rating) with limited evidence of generalization.
- Weakness: missing ablation studies to quantify contributions of components (entity features, filter sizes, etc.).
- Question: Why was Li and Hovy (2014) excluded—were their hyperparameters tuned or directly reused, and how does a direct comparison look?
- Question: Which Extended Grid features are included vs. omitted in the model, and how would adding omitted features affect performance?
- Question: How much does pre-training help on the small summary task, and what if larger training data were available or used?
- Question: Can you provide qualitative examples of convolutional filters/entity transition patterns that the model learns?
- Question: How does the model scale to much longer documents (e.g., 100+ sentences), and would architectural changes be required?
- Recommendation: Accept—paper makes a valuable, reproducible contribution despite some novelty and analysis shortcomings.
