- Neural architecture: Replaces discrete entity-grid representations with distributed embeddings and CNN layers to model longer entity transitions and avoid the curse of dimensionality.  
- Empirical gains: Shows consistent, significant improvements (4%+ on discrimination and summary rating; ~2.5% on insertion) over non-neural entity-grid baselines across tasks.  
- End-to-end pairwise ranking: Trains task-specific coherence features end-to-end rather than using task-agnostic handcrafted features.

- Strong experimental results: Robust and statistically significant improvements across three standard coherence evaluation tasks.  
- Clear presentation: Well-written, logically organized paper with accessible model description.  
- Reproducibility: Authors released code, aiding reproducibility and future work.  
- Appropriate evaluation: Uses three established coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary rating) to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Literature grounding: Strong connection to prior coherence work and clear motivation from entity-grid limitations.

- Inadequate comparison to recent neural models: Brief and dismissive treatment of Li and Hovy (2014) without thorough analysis.  
- Limited feature analysis: Little insight into what convolutional filters actually learn or detect.  
- Weak analysis of insertion gap: Superficial explanation for lower gains on insertion versus discrimination.  
- Missing ablations: No detailed ablation studies on filter sizes, number of filters, embedding dims, etc.  
- Limited theoretical discussion: Does not deeply explore implications for Centering Theory or coherence theory.

- Q1: Explain why Li and Hovy (2014) performed poorly on WSJ and confirm if preprocessing/evaluation matched the original.  
- Q2: Provide concrete examples of patterns detected by convolutional filters.  
- Q3: Explain why the model struggles more with insertion and suggest modifications to improve it.  
- Q4: Clarify benefits of wide convolution for coherence modeling and impact of using narrow convolution.

- Overall recommendation: Strong, well-executed paper with meaningful empirical contributions; recommend acceptance to ACL.
