- Proposes a neural local coherence model that replaces discrete entity-grid transitions with learned embeddings and a CNN trained with pairwise ranking, evaluated on WSJ sentence ordering and DUC’03 summary rating with consistent gains.

- Contribution 1: CNN over embedded entity-grid entries with pairwise ranking enabling longer transition windows and end-to-end learning.

- Contribution 2: Empirical gains—statistically significant improvements over strong grid baselines on discrimination, insertion, and summary rating, including ablation-like comparisons.

- Contribution 3: Reasonably transparent experimental description and claimed code release (no link provided).

- Strength: Architecture directly addresses entity-grid sparsity by using embeddings, convolutional filters, and pooling to detect location-invariant transition patterns.

- Strength: Strong, statistically validated empirical improvements across tasks, including outperforming extended-grid baselines with fewer explicit features.

- Strength: Solid experimental practices (large WSJ split, multiple seeds, early stopping, dropout, adaptive optimizers) and appropriate baseline choices and tests.

- Strength: Method is conceptually simple, compatible with existing pipelines using coreference and syntactic roles, and practically useful.

- Strength: Modular architecture and ranking framework are adaptable to downstream tasks and extensions (e.g., discourse relations).

- Weakness: Incremental novelty—applies standard CNN+embeddings+ranking to entity grid without a fundamentally new coherence modeling idea.

- Weakness: Incomplete baselines and replication concerns—Li & Hovy (2014) reimplementation failed and RST/discourse baselines are not run on WSJ.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—permutation discrimination is synthetic, DUC’03 is very small, and gains partly rely on cross-domain pretraining.

- Weakness: Model treats entity columns independently and pools over locations, possibly missing cross-entity interactions and positional trends important for coherence.

- Weakness: Light analysis and ablations—no tests on transition window k, pooling choices, embedding initialization, entity-feature impact, error analysis, or coreference sensitivity.

- Question: Which coreference resolver and syntactic role pipeline were used, and how sensitive are results to coreference quality?

- Question: What were the optimal transition window sizes k and pooling lengths p, and do longer transitions (k>3) drive gains?

- Question: Can you provide a head-to-head comparison with RST-based models (e.g., Feng et al., 2014) on the same WSJ split, and if not, why?

- Question: What settings/datasets were used for the Li & Hovy (2014) reimplementation, what diagnostics explain the replication failure, and can published results be reported for context?

- Question: Did you try k-max or average pooling to capture multiple occurrences of patterns, rather than max pooling?

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed, and practically useful incremental advance with consistent results; recommend acceptance provided camera-ready adds stronger baselines and deeper analysis/ablations.
