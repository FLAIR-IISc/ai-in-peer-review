- Proposes a neuralized entity-grid: embed grid cells, apply 1-D convolutions over per-entity transitions, max-pool, then a linear pairwise ranking layer for document coherence scores.
- Enables longer transition windows and end-to-end task-specific learning, reducing sparsity and allowing composite entity feature embeddings.
- Shows consistent, statistically significant gains over entity-grid baselines on WSJ (discrimination, insertion) and transfers to DUC summary coherence, achieving SOTA among compared models.

- Addresses prior limitations with a simple, effective architecture that replaces discrete transitions with distributed representations and CNNs.
- Strong empirical results (+4% discrimination, up to +2.5% insertion) with significance testing, multiple runs, and tuned hyperparameters.
- Demonstrates transfer learning: pretraining on WSJ then fine-tuning improves DUC summary coherence ratings.
- Reproducible and fair: uses public baseline toolkits, reports settings, and releases code.
- Practical: requires only common preprocessing (POS/NPs, simple entity features) and no heavy external resources.

- Incremental novelty: essentially a CNN over the entity grid without deeper modeling of cross-entity interactions or global discourse structure.
- Limited evaluation breadth and external validity: synthetic sentence-ordering proxy, small DUC set, no modern-generation or cross-domain evaluations.
- Insufficient analysis/ablations: no systematic study of transition length k, pooling length p, individual feature contributions, coref sensitivity, or alternative architectures.
- Preprocessing and baseline details unclear: ambiguous use of coreference vs noun matching, missing tool versions/settings, and limited comparison to stronger discourse baselines.
- Modeling assumptions (independent entity columns, max pooling) may discard cross-entity alignment and positional cues, possibly hurting insertion performance.
- “State-of-the-art” claims hinge on a limited baseline set.

- Which coreference system and parser (versions/settings) were used, or was noun matching used; how sensitive are results to coref quality or using all nouns?
- What is the effective vocabulary size when composing role with entity features, and did you try embedding features separately and composing them?
- How do performance and overfitting vary with transition length k and pooling length p; please report ablations removing each entity feature.
- Did you try an insertion-specific ranking loss (varying sentence position) and how did it affect insertion/discrimination performance?
- How much of the DUC improvement comes from pretraining versus regularization; was zero-shot transfer to DUC attempted?

- Provide qualitative analyses (top-activating transition patterns per filter) and a small error analysis contrasting neural vs grid failures.
- Report runtime, memory usage, and model size to aid practitioners.
- Clarify Section 3.1 ambiguity on coreference vs noun matching, whether pooling is per-entity column or across columns, and how entity features are aggregated before the final layer.

- Overall: solid, well-executed, practical neuralization of the entity grid with consistent empirical gains and good reproducibility; contribution is primarily incremental but merits acceptance if space allows.
