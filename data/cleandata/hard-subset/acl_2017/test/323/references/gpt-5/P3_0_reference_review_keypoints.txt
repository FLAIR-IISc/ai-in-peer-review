- Proposes a neuralized entity grid: embed grid symbols, apply per-entity 1D CNN over transitions, max-pool salient patterns, linear scoring, trained with pairwise ranking.
- Evaluated on WSJ sentence ordering (discrimination & insertion) and DUC’03 summary rating; neural models outperform non-neural grids and pretraining on WSJ helps DUC performance.
- Simple entity-feature fusion by concatenating feature values with roles (e.g., PERSON-S) yields additional gains.
- Contribution: a straightforward neural architecture that mitigates sparsity, enables longer transition windows, and supports end-to-end task-specific learning.
- Contribution: empirical evidence of consistent, statistically significant gains on standard coherence evaluations, including low-data transfer for summary rating.
- Contribution: a practical, low-engineering recipe for incorporating entity-specific features into the neural grid.
- Strength: clear motivation and appropriate architectural choice—moving from discrete counts to learned embeddings plus convolution.
- Strength: consistent, significant improvements over non-neural baselines, notably versus Elsner & Charniak (2011) despite fewer features.
- Strength: sensible training protocol and hyperparameter search (pairwise ranking, dev set, multiple restarts, dropout, significance testing).
- Strength: simplicity and applicability—the model is compact, requires only entity grids, easy to integrate, and code release aids adoption.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—approach is a direct adaptation of standard sequence CNNs to the entity grid rather than a conceptual advance.
- Weakness: modeling assumptions cause information loss—entities treated independently and max-pooling erases relative positional information and cross-entity interactions.
- Weakness: limited ablation and analysis—no systematic study of transition length k, pooling/filter counts, separate feature contributions, or qualitative filter/error analysis.
- Weakness: preprocessing/coreference details are underspecified (resolver, parser, mention handling, entity counts, pruning), harming reproducibility and variance assessment.
- Weakness: evaluation scope and baselines are limited—overreliance on random permutations, insertion is not trained for, DUC’03 is small, and comparisons to some prior/neural baselines are incomplete.
- Question: Which coreference resolver and syntactic analyzer were used, are non-head nouns included, and how are nominals/variants unified?
- Question: Do filters operate strictly within one entity column, and were 2D convolutions or cross-entity interaction mechanisms tried?
- Question: Were k-max pooling or positional encodings experimented with, and how sensitive are results to pooling size?
- Question: Please report performance as a function of transition length k and compare to k≤3 to substantiate long-range benefits.
- Question: Did you try factorized embeddings (separate role and feature embeddings composed later) instead of fused symbols, and how does vocabulary size affect training/performance?
- Question: If the model is trained directly for insertion (candidates differing by one sentence position), does insertion performance improve?
