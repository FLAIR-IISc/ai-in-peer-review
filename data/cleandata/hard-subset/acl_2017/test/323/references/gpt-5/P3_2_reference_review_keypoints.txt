- Proposes a neural local coherence model that embeds entity-grid symbols, applies 1D convolutions down entity columns, max-pools salient patterns, and learns a coherence scorer via pairwise ranking.  
- Evaluated on WSJ sentence ordering (discrimination and insertion) and DUC’03 summary coherence; outperforms non-neural entity-grid variants and a graph-based baseline; WSJ pretraining improves transfer to summary rating.  
- Contribution: simple end-to-end CNN over entity grids enabling longer transition modeling and task-specific representation learning with strong benchmark results.  
- Contribution: practical mechanism to add entity-specific features into the neural grid and evidence that the neural model outperforms strong baselines even with limited features.  
- Contribution: shows transfer learning from sentence-ordering pretraining to summary coherence rating, indicating reusable local coherence cues.  
- Strength: consistent empirical gains and robustness with significance testing and multiple random seeds; pretraining transfer is meaningful.  
- Strength: conceptually clear and simple design that addresses sparsity of discrete transition counts and supports longer k without parameter blow-up.  
- Strength: end-to-end pairwise ranking directly optimizes the target discrimination objective rather than relying on task-agnostic features.  
- Strength: sensible engineering and reproducibility practices (hyperparameter search, regularization details, multiple seeds, promised code release).  
- Strength: coverage of multiple benchmarks and use of a larger WSJ split appropriate for neural models.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—mainly neuralizing an existing representation with independence assumptions.  
- Weakness: treats entity columns independently and uses max pooling, losing cross-entity interactions and positional information.  
- Weakness: encodes role+features as cross-product tokens, inflating vocabulary and losing compositionality; lacks ablation with factorized embeddings.  
- Weakness: evaluation gaps—no direct training for insertion task, some relevant semantic/discourse baselines not re-run or compared in the same setting.  
- Weakness: reliance on coreference and syntactic-role preprocessing with unclear tooling/settings; potential overestimation under noisy conditions and some ambiguous notation/details.  
- Question: which coreference and syntactic-role systems were used, are pronouns included, and are WSJ parses/coref gold or automatic?  
- Question: why use cross-product role+feature tokens rather than compositional embeddings, what is the resulting vocabulary size, and were factorized representations tried?  
- Question: please clarify convolution axis, feature-map lengths, pooling window/stride, and how aggregation across entity columns is performed.  
- Question: was training directly for insertion attempted (pairwise ranking over positions), and if so what were the results?  
- Question: request ablations on k, pooling length, number of filters, document-length analysis, and qualitative inspection of learned filters.  
- Question: how sensitive is the model to coreference errors and does it generalize cross-domain beyond WSJ?  
- Overall: solid empirical modernization of the entity-grid approach with moderate novelty; results and clarity support acceptance though design and evaluation could be strengthened.
