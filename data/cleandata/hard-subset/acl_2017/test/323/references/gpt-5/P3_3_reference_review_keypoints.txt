- Proposes a CNN over entity-grid columns embedding grammatical roles and entity features, scored with pairwise ranking for local coherence.
- Replaces sparse discrete transition counts with distributed representations and longer transition windows for end-to-end training.
- Shows consistent empirical gains on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 summary coherence, with beneficial pretrain/fine-tune transfer.

- Architecture is clear and principled, addressing sparsity and k-length transition explosion while preserving column independence.
- Strong baselines and fair setup on standard datasets, with consistent absolute improvements and reported statistical tests for WSJ.
- End-to-end pairwise ranking training is appropriate and effective; transfer learning from WSJ to DUC is sensible for low-resource tasks.
- Sensible engineering choices (hyperparameter search, dropout, wide conv, multiple seeds) and discussion of window/pooling behavior.
- Model is simple, compact, uses modest entity features, and is easy to adopt or extend.

- Novelty is incremental relative to prior neural coherence work and the paper lacks detailed ablations (k, pooling, embedding size, filters, feature contributions).
- Independence-of-columns ignores cross-entity interactions like competition/coordination that can be crucial for coherence.
- Evaluation scope is limited: discrimination uses synthetic permutations, insertion is synthetic, and DUC is very small/unstable; downstream realism is limited.
- Coreference and preprocessing dependencies are under-specified; no robustness analysis to coref or parsing errors.
- Baseline coverage/fairness issues and inconsistencies (e.g., exclusion of Li & Hovy 2014, training differences for extended grid) need clarification.

- Which parser and coreference system were used, and how were non-head nouns linked into entity chains (string match vs full coref)? Any salience thresholds?
- Are embeddings shared or composed for role-feature pairs, and did you try factoring role and feature embeddings instead of concatenation?
- What are the ablation results for k, pooling length, number of filters, and how much gain comes from embeddings vs convolution vs pairwise loss?
- Did you try 2D convolutions or inter-column attention to model cross-entity interactions, and any preliminary observations?
- Which metrics are reported for DUC (accuracy/F1/Kendall’s tau), clarify table labels, and provide significance tests for DUC results.
- Did you try training on insertion-style pairs or near-miss permutations instead of only discrimination, and how did that affect insertion performance?

- Add qualitative analyses showing top-activated filters/patterns and error cases contrasting extended grid vs Grid-CNN.
- Report parameter counts and runtime for reproducibility.
- Consider normalizing for document length when comparing to random permutations to avoid length-driven discrimination effects.
