- Proposes a neuralized entity-grid: embed grammatical-role symbols, apply per-entity 1D convolutions + max-pooling, output coherence score via linear layer trained with pairwise ranking loss.
- Shows consistent improvements and new SOTA on sentence ordering (discrimination, insertion on WSJ) and summary coherence (DUC), with further gains from limited pretraining.
- Demonstrates that embeddings + convolutions over longer entity transitions (k≈5–8) and simple entity features mitigate sparsity of classic grids.

- Clear, simple, and principled mapping of entity-grid concepts to embeddings, local convs, and ranking loss.
- Empirical gains are consistent and statistically significant across tasks and datasets, strengthening general utility claims.
- Handles longer transitions and easy feature augmentation without combinatorial blow-up via shared embeddings and filters.
- Good experimental hygiene: dev tuning, multiple seeds, significance testing, transparent implementation details.
- Practical and lightweight end-to-end model producing a scalar coherence score; code reportedly released.
- Attractive for generation/ordering pipelines compared to heavy discourse parsers.

- Limited novelty: essentially CNNs applied to entity grids with incremental technical contribution.
- Independence assumptions (per-entity columns, pooling) may miss cross-entity interactions and positional info, limiting modeling power.
- Incomplete comparison to strongest alternatives, notably discourse-relation models; limited tuning/analysis of some neural baselines.
- Sparse ablation and diagnostic analysis (k, pooling, features, coref sensitivity, filter visualization, error breakdown).
- Evaluation/reporting gaps: no direct training for insertion, unclear metric labels, missing runtime/memory/coref-robustness analysis, small DUC training with no CIs.

- Which coreference resolver and syntactic pipeline were used, and how sensitive are results to coref errors or different resolvers?
- How do transition window size k and pooling length p empirically affect performance across tasks; is there a sweet spot?
- Did you try convolutions spanning columns (2D conv) to capture inter-entity interactions, and what were the results?
- Can you train the ranking loss directly on insertion candidates (or use listwise loss) and does insertion performance improve?
- Please compare against discourse-relation models (Lin et al., 2011; Feng et al., 2014) on the same WSJ split to confirm SOTA claims.
- Can you show high-activation filter patterns and an error analysis for insertion cases?

- Clarify metric/table labels (Acc vs F1), report standard deviations and confidence intervals (especially for DUC).
- Discuss computational costs and training/inference speeds versus E&C and graph-based methods.
