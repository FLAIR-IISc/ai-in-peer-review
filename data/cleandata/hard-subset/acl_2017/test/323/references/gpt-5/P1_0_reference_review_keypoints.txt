- Proposal: Neuralized entity-grid using embedded grid symbols and 1D convolutions over entity columns with max-pooling, trained with pairwise ranking.
- Empirical contribution: CNN-over-grid architecture yields consistent, significant improvements on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 summary rating; code released.
- Methodological contribution: Distributed, task-trained representations for entity transitions that support longer transition windows and incorporate entity features without combinatorial explosion.
- Transfer contribution: Pretraining on sentence-ordering (WSJ) improves summary coherence rating in low-data settings.
- Strength: Strong, carefully validated empirical gains (~4% discrimination, up to 2.5% insertion, >3–4% summary) with significance testing and multiple seeds.
- Strength: Simple, well-motivated architecture addressing sparsity in discrete transition counts and enabling longer-range patterns via convolution and max-pooling.
- Strength: Efficient incorporation of entity-specific features via symbol composition and embeddings, outperforming extended discrete grids despite using fewer features.
- Strength: Practical transferability demonstrated from WSJ pretraining to DUC summary rating.
- Strength: Reproducibility: dataset splits, protocols, hyperparameters, optimization details, and code release reported.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—primarily “neuralizing” a known model using standard CNN components without deep analysis of learned representations.
- Weakness: Incomplete/uneven baselines — omits comparisons to Li & Hovy (2014) replication analysis, Feng & Hirst (2012) learning-to-rank, and discourse-relation models (Lin et al., 2011; Feng et al., 2014).
- Weakness: Missing ablations and analyses—no systematic study of transition window k, pooling strategy, filter counts/sizes, embedding sizes, or per-feature contributions and learned filters.
- Weakness: Reliance on NP identification and coreference not discussed—tools, sensitivity, and domain robustness unspecified.
- Weakness: Modeling assumptions (independent, location-invariant entity columns) may be inappropriate for tasks needing precise positional or inter-entity interaction modeling; insertion training mismatch.
- Question: Please include or justify omission of comparisons to Feng & Hirst (2012) and discourse-relation models on the same WSJ split.
- Question: Which parser and coreference resolver were used, and how sensitive are results to these choices or noisier alternatives?
- Question: Please report ablations over transition window size k, pooling stride/overlap, per-feature contribution (NE type, salience, proper), and multi-width filters.
- Question: If trained directly on insertion-style pairs, does performance improve compared to using discrimination-trained models?
- Question: Can you provide qualitative examples of learned filters/transition n-grams and representative failure cases?
- Question: For summary rating, report exact metrics, absolute numbers, significance, and whether WSJ pretraining consistently helps across clusters.
