- Proposes a neural local coherence model that embeds entity-grid entries, applies 1D convolutions over entity transitions, max-pools salient patterns, and is trained end-to-end with pairwise ranking, claiming SOTA on WSJ and DUC tasks.

- Contribution: a simple, effective neural architecture over entity-grid embeddings that models longer transitions and enables end-to-end task-specific learning.

- Contribution: a practical method to incorporate entity-specific features by expanding the symbol space and learning embeddings, reducing manual feature engineering.

- Contribution: strong empirical results across WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03, with beneficial pretraining for low-data settings.

- Strength: conceptually clear design addressing sparsity and task-agnostic feature extraction via embeddings and convolutional transition modeling.

- Strength: strong empirical performance with reasonable comparisons, showing consistent gains and transferability from WSJ to DUC.

- Strength: sound training setup and reproducibility—details on optimization, splits, multiple runs, significance testing, and available code.

- Strength: minimal reliance on manual features—outperforms extended grid with only a few entity-level features, suggesting better generalization.

- Strength: compatibility with established coherence theory (entity-based and centering-inspired), keeping interpretability.

- Weakness: treats entity columns independently, ignoring cross-entity interactions and global discourse structure which may limit performance.

- Weakness: max pooling discards positional information, potentially harming tasks like insertion where position matters.

- Weakness: preprocessing and coreference pipeline are under-specified, leaving robustness and comparability unclear.

- Weakness: baseline coverage is incomplete—excludes some relevant neural and discourse baselines, raising fairness questions.

- Weakness: limited analysis depth—missing ablations (k, filters, pooling), qualitative analysis of learned patterns, and detailed error analysis.

- Question: which tools/settings were used for NP detection, grammatical roles, and coreference; were any gold annotations used and are baselines preprocessed identically?

- Question: sensitivity and ablation results requested for transition window k, pooling length, number of filters, DEV curves, and effect of removing entity-specific features.

- Question: whether alternatives to max pooling (k-max, avg, per-column) or positional encodings were tried and if they help insertion.

- Question: whether pairwise training tailored to insertion (documents differing only by sentence position) was attempted and its effect.

- Question: whether cross-entity convolutions or attention mechanisms were explored to capture entity interactions.

- Question: what are the model’s parameter count, typical training time, and scalability with document length and entity count.

- Overall: well-executed neural reinterpretation with clear gains; addressing preprocessing clarity, stronger baselines, and deeper analysis would strengthen the contribution.
