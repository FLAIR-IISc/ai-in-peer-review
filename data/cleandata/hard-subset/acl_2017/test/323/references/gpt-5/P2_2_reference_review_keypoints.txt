- Proposes a neural local coherence model that embeds grammatical roles and entity features, applies 1D convolutions over entity-grid columns, pools, and trains with pairwise ranking.  
- Encodes entity-specific features (NE type, salience, properness) as distributed embeddings to avoid combinatorial explosion.  
- Reports consistent gains over strong entity-grid baselines on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 summary rating, with code released.  
- Main contribution: a simple convolutional architecture over grid columns enabling longer transition modeling and end-to-end learning.  
- Main contribution: a principled way to encode and jointly learn embeddings for entity-specific features.  
- Main contribution: strong empirical results with careful training, tuning, and significance testing.  
- Strength: Conceptually clear — convs over transitions target the core entity-grid intuition and mitigate sparsity via distributed representations.  
- Strength: Empirically robust — consistent improvements (+~4% discrimination, up to +2.5% insertion, +>4% summary with pretraining) averaged over seeds with significance testing.  
- Strength: End-to-end pairwise ranking ties feature learning to the task and supports transfer via pretraining/fine-tuning.  
- Strength: Lightweight, transparent architecture and implementation details with code release aid reproducibility.  
- Strength: Fair, strong comparisons within the entity-grid family (grid-all-nouns, extended grid, graph-based method).  
- Weakness: Limited baseline scope — omits discourse-structure models (RST) and strong sentence-based neural coherence baselines.  
- Weakness: Columns treated independently — ignores cross-entity interactions like competition or co-reference chains.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis/ablations — lacks studies on window size k, pooling, filter counts, per-feature contributions, and qualitative filter inspection.  
- Weakness: Relies on coreference and parsing without specifying tools or testing robustness to their errors.  
- Weakness: Task/data limitations — training on discrimination but evaluating insertion, small summary dataset with incomplete significance reporting.  
- Q: Which coreference resolver and parser/settings were used, and how sensitive are results to coreference quality?  
- Q: Did you try modeling cross-entity interactions (2D convs, attention across columns), and what were the results?  
- Q: Can you provide ablations on transition window k, pooling size, number of filters, and each entity-specific feature’s contribution?  
- Q: Are the summary-task improvements statistically significant, and what is the impact of pretraining embeddings only vs. all parameters?  
- Q: Have you trained directly on insertion-style pairs, and how does that compare to training on discrimination?  
- Q: What are training/inference time and memory footprints, and are there issues with very long documents?  
- Overall: A solid, well-motivated advancement of entity-grid coherence modeling with clear empirical gains; main reservations are limited cross-family comparisons, deeper analysis, and coreference robustness.
