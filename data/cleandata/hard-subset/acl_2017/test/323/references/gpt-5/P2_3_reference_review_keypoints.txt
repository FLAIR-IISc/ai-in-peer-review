- Proposes a neural local coherence model: embed entity-grid cells, apply 1D convolutions along sentence order per entity column, max-pool + linear layer to score coherence, trained with pairwise ranking.
- Reports consistent improvements over non-neural entity-grid baselines on sentence ordering (discrimination, insertion) and DUC’03 summary coherence rating, with gains from pretraining on WSJ and fine-tuning.
- Shows that embedding entity-specific features avoids feature-space blow-up, reducing sparsity and enabling longer transition windows.

- Main contribution: a simple, effective CNN over entity grids enabling longer-range transition modeling and end-to-end task learning.
- Empirical contribution: statistically significant, consistent gains on standard coherence tasks, including transfer learning for low-resource summary rating.
- Practical contribution: a recipe to incorporate entity-specific features in a neural grid without exponential feature growth.

- Methodologically clear and principled: embeddings + convolutions address sparsity and share parameters to model longer transitions.
- Uses a pairwise ranking objective that directly optimizes document comparison tasks.
- Strong, consistent empirical gains with robust evaluation (averaging 5 seeds, approximate randomization tests).
- Sensible dataset and baseline choices, reproducing comparisons via public toolkits for reproducibility.
- Demonstrates end-to-end training and transferability while preserving interpretability via entity-centric filters.

- Limited novelty relative to prior neural coherence work and lacks substantive comparison to Li & Hovy (2014).
- Treats entity columns independently and aggregates via max-pooling, likely missing cross-entity interactions and global discourse structure.
- Sparse analysis and ablations: no detailed study of window size k, pooling p, filter counts, or per-feature contributions; missing error analysis.
- Pipeline dependence on coreference and NP detection is unquantified; robustness to coref errors is not evaluated.
- Evaluation gaps and clarity issues: no comparisons to discourse-relation models, insertion not trained task-specifically, and Table 3 contains an unexplained “F1” column.

- Which coreference resolver and NP pipeline were used, and how sensitive are results to different coref systems and precision/recall trade-offs?
- Can you provide ablations on transition window k, pooling size p, number of filters, and each entity-specific feature to pinpoint sources of gains?
- Did you attempt cross-entity composition (e.g., 2D convolutions or attention across columns), and what were the outcomes?
- Why not train directly on the insertion task (pairwise insertion positions), and does task-specific training improve insertion performance?
- Please clarify Table 3’s last column/F1 metric and add comparisons against discourse-relation models (Lin et al., 2011; Feng et al., 2014).

- Overall: a well-motivated, clear, and practically useful neuralization of the entity grid with solid empirical gains and moderate novelty; acceptance recommended if additional analyses and comparisons are added.
