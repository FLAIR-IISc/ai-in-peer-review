Summary and claimed contributions
- Proposes a neural “entity grid” model for local coherence that replaces discrete transition counts with distributed embeddings of grammatical-role tokens and uses a 1D convolution over per-entity sentence transitions. Training is end-to-end via pairwise ranking (original vs. perturbed documents).
- Extends the neural grid to incorporate entity-specific features (e.g., NE type, salience, properness) by concatenating them with role symbols and embedding the resulting tokens.
- Reports consistent improvements over reproduced non-neural grid baselines on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 summary coherence ranking; claims state-of-the-art results.

Strengths
- Clear, intuitive idea: neuralizing the entity grid with CNNs addresses sparsity in long transitions and enables end-to-end training for downstream tasks. This is a natural, reasonable modernization of a widely used model.
- Empirical improvements are consistent across tasks: +4% discrimination, up to +2.5% insertion, and +3–4% on summary coherence rating over strong reproduced grid baselines. Multiple runs and approximate randomization tests are used (though see concerns below).
- Sensible engineering choices: shared embedding matrix for roles/features, wide convolution to reach boundaries, dropout and early stopping, hyperparameter tuning on a dev set, and reporting of tuned hyperparameters.
- Reduced reliance on external resources: the best neural variant uses only a subset (3) of entity features, avoiding the external-corpus-derived features required by some prior grid extensions, yet still outperforms them.

Weaknesses
- Clarity and correctness issues in the model description:
  - The convolution/pooling formalization is imprecise and sometimes incorrect. Equation (2) defines h_t over “Lt:t+k−1,j”, but the feature map size is later given as m.n+k−1, which is nonsensical if columns are treated independently; it should be per-entity of length m+k−1. The text also says zero-padding for t > {m,n}, conflating sentence and entity indices. These imprecisions hinder reproducibility.
  - It claims columns (entities) are independent, yet pooling appears to aggregate across entities. How exactly are feature maps arranged (per-entity vs. concatenated across entities)? What is the pooling axis and window p relative to sentences vs. entities?
  - “Location-invariant” detection is asserted, but with wide conv plus max-pooling over full maps, the model may collapse entity identity; this trade-off and its effect on performance are not analyzed.
- Grid construction is under-specified and internally inconsistent:
  - Section 2 assumes a coreference resolver to track entities; later, “all nouns” are used as entities (E&C variant), which typically dispenses with coreference. Which is used in the neural models? Are pronouns included? Are mentions lemmatized or matched by string head? If coref is used, which system and settings?
  - How are numeric features like salience encoded when “attaching” to role tokens? Are values bucketized? How many buckets? For NE types, which tagger is used, and how are errors handled?
  - It is unclear whether multiple mentions per sentence for an entity use highest role (S≻O≻X) in the neural grids as in the original grid, and whether this holds for the “all nouns” setting.
- Evaluation concerns and incomplete baselines:
  - Discrimination is trained and tested on original vs random permutations. This setup is known to be sensitive to document length; no length controls or stratified analyses are provided. Insertion gains are relatively small; significance vs. strongest non-neural baseline isn’t reported.
  - Summary rating uses very small data (144/80 pairs). Pretraining on WSJ and fine-tuning may bias results; fairness vs. prior non-neural methods (which were not pretrained) is unclear. Statistical testing for Table 4 is missing.
  - Comparisons omit several strong and relevant baselines: discourse-relation-based models (Lin et al. 2011; Feng et al. 2014) on WSJ with published numbers, more recent neural coherence methods. The paper mentions attempting Li & Hovy (2014) but discards it due to low performance without diagnosing or collaborating with authors; this undermines the empirical case.
  - Lack of ablations: no study of transition length k, pooling p, effect of entity features, role-only vs. role+entity features, pretraining vs. none, or replacing CNN with MLP/bi-RNN over columns.
- Reproducibility and missing details:
  - RMSprop parameters are not numerically specified; “values suggested” is too vague. Random seeds used for the five runs are not reported. Early stopping criterion (“accuracy”) not defined precisely for ranking tasks.
  - Preprocessing (sentence segmentation, parsing, dependency vs. constituency, role extraction pipeline) is unspecified. No runtime or computational cost analysis.
  - Code is said to be “released with the submission,” but no link is provided in the text.
- Numerous formatting and editorial issues that indicate sloppiness:
  - Broken/inconsistent references (many with “?,” malformed years, backslashes, missing venues), typos (“loosing” vs. “losing,” “less verbose”), mismatched/missing figures and tables (Figure 2 repeatedly referenced but not present; Table 3 header mixes “Acc F1” with discrimination/insertion), inline footnotes that interrupt flow, and incorrect mathematical notation (m.n+k−1).

Questions to Authors
- Precisely specify the grid construction for each model variant: Are you using coreference? Which system? Are pronouns included? Are entities head nouns only or all nouns? How are multiple mentions per sentence resolved? Do you lemmatize? Provide a full preprocessing pipeline (tools, versions, settings).
- Clarify the convolution/pooling shapes: Is convolution applied per entity column with shared filters? What is the exact size of each feature map (per entity)? Over which axis is max-pooling applied? Are feature maps concatenated across entities before pooling? Please correct Equations (2)–(3) and the padding description.
- How do you encode salience in the neural model when “attaching” features to roles? If bucketized, what bins? Provide the vocabulary size |V| for each variant.
- Provide ablation studies: (i) effect of transition length k, (ii) without entity features, (iii) without pretraining on DUC’03, (iv) replacing CNN with an RNN over columns, (v) sensitivity to document length and coreference errors.
- Add comparisons to strong published baselines on the same WSJ setup (e.g., Lin et al. 2011; Feng et al. 2014) and report significance vs. the strongest non-neural baseline for insertion and summary rating. If Li & Hovy (2014) underperformed, can you report their numbers on your split and analyze why?
