- Proposes applying a CNN to the entity grid to learn distributed role/transition representations, trained end-to-end with a pairwise ranking objective.  
- Main contributions: a novel CNN-based entity-grid architecture and state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary rating tasks.  
- Strength: Successfully neuralizes the entity grid, addressing sparsity and capturing longer-range dependencies with end-to-end learning.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains (~4% on discrimination and summary rating) over the Elsner & Charniak (2011) extended grid baseline.  
- Strength: Clear presentation, detailed experimental setup, and code release supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Simple integration of entity-specific features via composite symbols inflates vocabulary and likely misses compositional interactions; more sophisticated fusion needed.  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of why the Li & Hovy (2014) neural baseline failed to reproduce, leaving comparative positioning incomplete.  
- Question: Have you tried alternative fusion methods (e.g., concatenating separate embeddings for grammatical role and entity features) before convolution?  
- Question: Have you trained directly on an insertion-style objective, and if so did that reduce the smaller performance gain on the insertion task?
