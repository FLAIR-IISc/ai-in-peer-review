- Proposes a CNN-based neuralization of the entity grid: continuous embeddings for grammatical roles, 1D convolutions over entity transitions, and end-to-end pairwise ranking; achieves SOTA on discrimination, insertion, and summary-rating tasks.
- Main contributions: a novel deep architecture that overcomes discrete grid limitations to model longer-range transitions, and strong, statistically significant empirical results across standard coherence tasks.
- Strength: principled and novel design—distributed role representations and CNNs enable learning from sparse data and modeling longer transition windows, with end-to-end ranking loss.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation on WSJ with substantial, statistically significant gains over strong entity-grid baselines.
- Strength: clear writing, helpful figures (e.g., Fig.2), and released code that promote reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited feature integration—model uses only 3 of 9 E&C features and the composite-symbol approach risks vocabulary explosion as features scale.
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis or visualization of learned filters/embeddings to explain what linguistic patterns the model captures.
- Weakness: incomplete comparison to contemporary neural approaches (e.g., Li & Hovy 2014) and insufficient discussion of why such models perform poorly or differ.
- Question: Did the authors consider vocabulary explosion and alternative feature-integration methods (e.g., separate embeddings for role and features concatenated before convolution)?
- Question: Can the authors provide qualitative analyses/visualizations of learned convolutional filters to show which entity-transition patterns the model learns?
