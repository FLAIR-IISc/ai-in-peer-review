- Novel neural architecture: CNN applied to entity-grid columns with learned role embeddings and convolutional filters to model long-range entity transitions, trained end-to-end with a pairwise ranking objective.
- Main contributions: neural solution addressing sparsity and task-agnostic features, strong SOTA empirical results on three coherence tasks, and a simple method to add entity-specific features.
- Model elegance and motivation: well-argued shift from discrete grids to dense embeddings and convolutional filters to capture location-invariant patterns.
- Thorough evaluation: experiments on discrimination, insertion, and summary rating tasks with appropriate baselines, multiple runs, and significance testing.
- State-of-the-art performance: consistent, significant improvements (≈4% discrimination, ≈2.5% insertion) over strong non-neural baselines.
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-written, details are sufficient for replication, and code release is provided.
- Weakness — lack of analysis: no qualitative or visualization analysis (e.g., filter activations or embedding plots) to explain what the model learns.
- Weakness — incomplete neural baseline comparison: Li & Hovy (2014) model excluded from main results with insufficient analysis of why it failed on these datasets.
- Weakness — feature-integration design: combining entity type and role into single tokens (e.g., PERSON-S) may hinder compositionality and generalization; alternatives not explored.
- Question — learned representations: request for visualization/analysis of combined role/feature embeddings and clustering patterns across roles and types.
- Question — Li & Hovy comparison: ask whether document length or domain differences explain its poor performance and sensitivity compared to entity-grid models.
- Question — alternative feature integration: suggest experimenting with separate embeddings for roles and features combined via concatenation/addition for better generalization.
