- Proposes a CNN-based neuralization of the entity grid that embeds grid cells, applies 1D convolutions over per-entity sentence transitions, max-pools salient features, and uses pairwise ranking for coherence scoring.
- Claims contributions: (1) CNN architecture for longer distributed entity transitions and end-to-end ranking, (2) simple composition mechanism for entity-specific features reducing feature engineering, (3) empirical validation across tasks with code release.

- Empirical performance: Outperforms all-nouns and extended grid baselines on discrimination and insertion with statistically significant gains and improvements on summary rating with pretraining.
- Clear architecture: Mapping grid entries to embeddings and convolving over per-entity transitions addresses sparsity and enables longer transition windows; pairwise ranking is appropriate.
- Reduced feature engineering: Matches/exceeds extended grid using only three entity-specific features (NE type, salience, proper-name flag).
- Reproducibility: Provides implementation details, uses standard corpora/toolkits, reports multiple random seeds, significance tests, and mentions code release.
- Simplicity and efficiency: Conceptually simpler and likely faster than sentence-level RNNs while leveraging entity-transition inductive bias.

- Missing comparisons: Omits Li & Hovy (2014) and other sentence-representation baselines, weakening evidence against neural sentence-ordering methods.
- Limited analysis/ablations: Lacks sensitivity studies (transition window, pooling, embedding size, dropout), ablations of entity features, and qualitative analysis of learned filters.
- Coreference dependence: Relies on automatic NP extraction/coreference without robustness tests to resolver quality or oracle vs predicted mentions.
- Independent entity columns: Treating entity columns independently prevents modeling inter-entity interactions; no experiments with 2D convolutions or cross-entity attention.
- Task mismatch and limited generalization: Trained on discrimination but evaluated on insertion with modest gains; limited datasets (WSJ, small DUC subset) and no cross-domain tests.

- Are convolutions applied strictly along sentences per-entity with filters shared across entities, and were 2D entity×sentence filters tried?
- How sensitive is performance to transition length k and pooling size p; do longer windows overfit or saturate?
- Did composing entity-specific features with role symbols cause vocabulary sparsity, and how does separate embedding+concatenation compare?
- Which coreference system was used, and how sensitive are results to different resolvers or coreference errors (discrimination vs insertion)?
- Did you try training directly on insertion or multi-tasking discrimination+insertion, and what was the effect on insertion performance?
- What are training/inference times compared to the extended grid SVM ranker, and can the model score all insert positions in real time?

- Recommend an error analysis (failure cases, document length, entity sparsity) to guide improvements.
- Suggest including qualitative examples linking high-activation filters to interpretable transition patterns to clarify why the model works.
