- Proposes a CNN-based neuralization of the entity grid using embedded grammatical-role symbols and entity features, with convolution over column-wise transitions and pairwise ranking training.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements on WSJ sentence-ordering (discrimination and insertion) and DUC’03 summary coherence, including statistically significant gains on WSJ.  
- Uses WSJ pretraining (discrimination) to improve low-resource summary coherence rating.

- Strength: Addresses sparsity and task-agnostic feature extraction by modeling longer transition windows with convolution over distributed embeddings.  
- Strength: Shows consistent, significant empirical gains over grid baselines with multiple seeds, dev tuning, and significance testing.  
- Strength: Uses a sensible pairwise ranking objective and provides training details supporting reproducibility.  
- Strength: Model has interpretable inductive bias—filters as transition-pattern detectors and max-pooling for location invariance.  
- Strength: Achieves gains with few external features, demonstrating data and feature economy.

- Weakness: Incremental novelty—straightforward application of CNNs to the grid with limited conceptual advance over prior neural coherence models.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to discourse-relation-based ordering models (e.g., Lin et al., 2011; Feng et al., 2014) on WSJ.  
- Weakness: Paper omits preprocessing details and does not analyze sensitivity to coreference and syntactic-role assignment errors.  
- Weakness: Limited ablations and analysis—no studies on transition window size k, per-feature contribution, pretraining vs. scratch, pooling choices, or qualitative filter inspection.  
- Weakness: Narrow domain evaluation (WSJ and DUC’03); insertion training uses only discrimination pairs rather than insertion-specific negatives.

- Question: Which coreference resolver and parser/role labeler were used; how are pronouns and non-head nouns handled?  
- Question: What is the extended-model vocabulary size after concatenating entity-specific features, and how are rare composite symbols handled?  
- Question: How does performance vary with transition window k, pooling size, number of filters, and individual entity features?  
- Question: For pretraining, which parameters are transferred and fine-tuned; how sensitive are results to pretraining corpus/task?  
- Question: How robust is the model to coreference quality (gold vs. automatic; weaker vs. stronger systems)?  
- Question: What are training/inference time and memory costs compared to extended grid baselines, especially for insertion?

- Suggestion: Add discourse-relation baselines or justify their omission; report significance for the summary task.  
- Suggestion: Provide ablations on k, features, and pretraining; include qualitative analyses of filters and error analysis for insertion.  
- Suggestion: Clarify preprocessing choices and assess sensitivity to coreference/role-assignment quality.  
- Suggestion: Consider training with insertion-style negative pairs to better align training and evaluation.
