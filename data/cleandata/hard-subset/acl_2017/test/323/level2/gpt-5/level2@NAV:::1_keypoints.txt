- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model over entity grids that learns role and entity embeddings end-to-end with pairwise ranking, claiming SOTA on sentence discrimination, insertion (WSJ), and summary coherence (DUC03).

- Contribution 1: Neuralized entity-grid with convolution over columns enabling longer transition patterns and end-to-end learning, yielding consistent improvements across three tasks.

- Contribution 2: Simple, effective incorporation of entity-specific features that outperforms a feature-rich extended grid while using fewer features.

- Contribution 3: Practical training recipe (pairwise ranking, dropout, pretraining/fine-tuning) that improves robustness and sample efficiency for low-data tasks.

- Strength: Strong empirical evidence with statistically significant gains on discrimination (+~4%), insertion (+up to 2.5%), and summary rating (+>4% with pretraining), using multiple runs and randomization tests.

- Strength: Sound modeling—CNN over distributed role embeddings addresses sparsity for longer k; wide convolution + max-pooling plausibly capture position-invariant transition motifs.

- Strength: Reproducibility—public code, hyperparameter search details, and reporting of seeds/runs.

- Strength: Sensible comparisons—grid-only vs grid+entity-features isolates sources of improvement; pretraining shows useful cross-task transfer.

- Weakness: Coreference dependence—the paper does not specify the coref resolver or analyze sensitivity to coref errors.

- Weakness: Limited notion of global coherence—columns modeled independently, missing cross-entity interactions and discourse relations.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—WSJ-only ordering, small DUC03 summaries, no out-of-domain or robustness tests; more ablations (k, pooling p, entity features) needed.

- Weakness: Training-task mismatch—training on random permutations may not match insertion task requirements; smaller insertion gains suggest need for harder negatives or direct training.

- Question: Which coreference system was used and how sensitive are results to coref quality?

- Question: What are the effects of varying transition length k and pooling length p on each task?

- Question: Can the architecture model cross-entity patterns (e.g., 2D conv or attention) and would that improve performance?

- Question: What are runtime and memory footprints compared to extended grids and graph-based models?
