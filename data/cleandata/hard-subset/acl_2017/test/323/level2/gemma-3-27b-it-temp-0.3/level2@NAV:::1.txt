## Review of "A Neural Local Coherence Model"

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for modeling local coherence in text, building upon the established entity grid framework. The core idea is to replace discrete representations of entity transitions with distributed representations learned via a convolutional neural network, enabling the model to capture longer-range dependencies and incorporate entity-specific features in an end-to-end fashion. The authors evaluate their model on three standard coherence assessment tasks – discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating – and report state-of-the-art results.

The primary contribution of this work is the successful application of a convolutional neural network to the entity grid representation for coherence modeling. This allows for learning richer, more generalizable features compared to traditional, hand-engineered features. A second, valuable contribution is the end-to-end training approach, which allows the model to learn task-specific features directly from the data, rather than relying on pre-defined feature engineering. Finally, the strong empirical results across three diverse tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Empirical Performance:** The model consistently outperforms existing methods on all three evaluation tasks, achieving state-of-the-art results with statistically significant improvements. This provides strong evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   **Principled Approach:** The use of convolutional neural networks is a logical extension of the entity grid framework, addressing its limitations regarding discrete representations and feature engineering.
*   **End-to-End Training:** The pairwise ranking training method allows the model to learn task-specific features, potentially leading to better performance compared to models with fixed feature representations.
*   **Code Release:** The authors have released their code, which promotes reproducibility and facilitates further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Architecture:** While the application to the entity grid is effective, the core CNN architecture itself isn't particularly novel. The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of how the chosen architecture specifically addresses the challenges of coherence modeling.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions hyperparameter tuning but lacks detail on the search process and the sensitivity of the results to different parameter settings.
*   **Limited Analysis of Learned Features:** The paper doesn't provide much insight into *what* the model is learning. Visualizing the learned filters or analyzing the activations could provide valuable understanding of the model's behavior.
*   **Comparison to Recent Neural Coherence Models:** The related work section could be more comprehensive, specifically addressing recent neural approaches to coherence modeling beyond Li & Hovy (2014).

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide more details on the hyperparameter search process and the sensitivity of the results to different parameter settings?
2.  Can you provide any insights into the learned convolutional filters? Are there any interpretable patterns that emerge?
3.  How does the performance of your model compare to more recent neural coherence models that utilize transformers or other advanced architectures?
