- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural coherence model over entity grids to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific distributed features, trained end-to-end via pairwise ranking.

- Contribution 1: Introduces CNNs over entity grids to model long-range entity transitions beyond prior k=3 limits.

- Contribution 2: Uses a pairwise ranking objective for end-to-end training that learns task-specific features automatically.

- Contribution 3: Reports state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.

- Strength 1: Strong empirical improvements with statistical significance (≈4% discrimination, 2.5% insertion, >4% summary).

- Strength 2: Model design addresses curse of dimensionality and task-agnostic feature extraction via distributed representations and end-to-end learning.

- Strength 3: Comprehensive experiments across standard datasets (WSJ, DUC'03) with careful hyperparameter tuning and statistical validation.

- Weakness 1: Lacks discussion of computational limitations—scalability to long documents and training time vs baselines not addressed.

- Weakness 2: Incomplete comparison with Li & Hovy (2014); poor reported performance (19.34%) is unexplained.

- Weakness 3: Does not adequately differentiate the proposed approach from Li & Hovy’s neural framework despite claiming fundamental differences.

- Question 1: Why did Li & Hovy (2014) perform poorly on the WSJ dataset, and how does this architecture specifically overcome that limitation?

- Question 2: How does the model's computational complexity scale with document length compared to traditional entity grid models?
