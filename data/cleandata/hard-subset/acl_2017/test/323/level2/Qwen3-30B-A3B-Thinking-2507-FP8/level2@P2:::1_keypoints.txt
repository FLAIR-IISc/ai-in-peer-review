- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model over entity grids claiming to fix discrete representation/dimensionality and task-agnostic feature issues, with end-to-end task-specific training and SOTA results on three coherence tasks.

- Strength 1: Clear, well-structured extension of the entity grid that addresses dimensionality and represents transitions with distributed embeddings.

- Strength 2: Comprehensive experimental evaluation across three tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary coherence rating) with consistent improvements over non-neural baselines.

- Strength 3: Good technical explanation of the neural architecture, convolutional layers, and training procedure, linking it to the entity grid framework.

- Weakness 1: Claims about capturing long-range entity transitions (k=5–8) lack justification for those specific lengths and the reported gains are modest (~4% absolute on discrimination).

- Weakness 2: Reporting very poor replication results for Li and Hovy (2014) (19.34% accuracy) without explaining implementation details or adherence to the original method undermines the comparison.

- Weakness 3: Missing thorough comparison with more recent neural coherence models (post-2014/through 2017), limiting contextual relevance.

- Weakness 4: “State of the art” claim is not properly contextualized or supported (e.g., comparisons on summary coherence and statistical significance beyond p<0.01).

- Question 1: How were the optimal transition lengths (k=5–8) determined and how do they compare to what traditional grid models require?

- Question 2: Why did the Li and Hovy (2014) implementation perform poorly—were original methods followed, and what implementation choices were made?

- Question 3: Can the authors provide additional comparisons with more recent neural coherence models published after 2014?
