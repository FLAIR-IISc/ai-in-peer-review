

Summary: The paper proposes a neural coherence model based on convolutional neural networks operating over entity grid representations. It aims to overcome limitations of existing entity grid models by capturing long-range entity transitions and incorporating entity-specific features through distributed representations, with end-to-end training via pairwise ranking.

Main Contributions: 
1. A neural architecture using CNNs over entity grids to model long-range entity transitions (beyond k=3 limit of prior work).
2. A pairwise ranking approach for end-to-end training that learns task-specific features automatically.
3. State-of-the-art results across three coherence assessment tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary coherence rating).

Strengths:
1. The paper provides strong empirical evidence with statistically significant improvements (4% on discrimination, 2.5% on insertion, 4%+ on summary coherence) over previous entity grid models, demonstrating clear value.
2. The model design effectively addresses key limitations of prior work (curse of dimensionality, task-agnostic feature extraction) through distributed representations and end-to-end learning.
3. Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple standard tasks and datasets (WSJ, DUC'03) with careful hyperparameter tuning and statistical validation.

Weaknesses:
1. The paper lacks sufficient discussion of computational limitations - the model's scalability to very long documents and training time compared to baseline models is not addressed.
2. The comparison with Li and Hovy (2014) is incomplete; the authors mention their model performed poorly (19.34% accuracy) but don't explain why or how their approach differs fundamentally.
3. The paper does not adequately differentiate their approach from Li and Hovy's neural framework, despite claiming it's "fundamentally different" in Section 6.

Questions to Authors:
1. Can you clarify why the Li and Hovy (2014) model performed so poorly on your WSJ dataset, and how your architecture specifically overcomes this limitation?
2. How does your model's computational complexity scale with document length compared to traditional entity grid models?
