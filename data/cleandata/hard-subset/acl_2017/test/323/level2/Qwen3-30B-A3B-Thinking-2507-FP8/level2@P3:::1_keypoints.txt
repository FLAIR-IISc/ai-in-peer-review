- Proposes a neural local coherence model using a CNN over the entity grid to capture long-range entity transitions with distributed entity representations.  
- Primary contribution: neural architecture that models longer entity transitions while incorporating entity-specific distributed features.  
- Secondary contribution: end-to-end pairwise ranking training that learns task-specific features.  
- Tertiary contribution: reports state-of-the-art performance on three coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary coherence rating).  
- Addresses limitations of prior entity-grid models (discrete representations, task-agnostic features) and enables longer transition modeling (5–8 vs. previous 3).  
- Demonstrates consistent, significant improvements over non-neural baselines with reported absolute gains (≈4% discrimination, 2.5% insertion, ≈4% summary rating).  
- Provides thorough evaluation, appropriate baselines, documentation of implementation and hyperparameter tuning, and released code for reproducibility.  
- Does not justify why a CNN is preferable to other architectures (e.g., RNNs) for this sequential task.  
- Fails to explain why the Li and Hovy (2014) neural model performed poorly on their dataset or whether this was implementation- or dataset-related.  
- Does not address how the model handles coherence dependencies longer than the convolutional window or the effect of extending that window.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the specific features learned by the neural model to explain why it outperforms prior methods.  
- The "state of the art" claim is overstated because the paper omits comparisons to the most recent neural coherence models.  
- Q1: How does the model handle dependencies that exceed the convolutional transition length, and what is the effect of extending it?  
- Q2: Which entity-specific features were most influential and how do they compare to Elsner & Charniak (2011)?  
- Q3: Please provide details on the Li & Hovy (2014) implementation and potential reasons for its poor performance on this dataset.
