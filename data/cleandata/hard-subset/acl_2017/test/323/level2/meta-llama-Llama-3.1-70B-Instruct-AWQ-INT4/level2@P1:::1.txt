**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization, thanks to the power of distributed representation. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neural Local Coherence Model**: The authors propose a neural architecture for modeling local coherence that can capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization.
2. **Pairwise Ranking Method**: The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The authors evaluate their model on three different coherence assessment tasks and achieve state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Use of Distributed Representation**: The authors effectively use distributed representation to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization.
2. **End-to-End Training**: The authors propose a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task, which allows the model to learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The authors achieve state-of-the-art results on three different coherence assessment tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Analysis of Hyperparameters**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameters used in their model, which could be useful for understanding the impact of different hyperparameters on the model's performance.
2. **Limited Comparison with Other Models**: The authors do not compare their model with other neural models that have been proposed for coherence assessment, which could provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
3. **Limited Discussion of Future Work**: The authors do not provide a detailed discussion of future work, which could be useful for understanding the potential applications and extensions of their approach.
