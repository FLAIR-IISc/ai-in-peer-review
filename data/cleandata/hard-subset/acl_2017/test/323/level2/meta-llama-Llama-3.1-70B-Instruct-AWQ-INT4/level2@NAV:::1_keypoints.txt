- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model over entity grids that captures long-range entity transitions and learns task-specific high-level features end-to-end.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a neural local coherence model that captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization.

- Contribution 2: Presents an end-to-end training method to automatically learn task-specific high-level features.

- Contribution 3: Achieves state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.

- Strength 1: Novel CNN approach effectively models local coherence and long-range entity transitions.

- Strength 2: Thorough evaluation across three coherence assessment tasks demonstrating effectiveness.

- Strength 3: Detailed model architecture and training description, facilitating replication.

- Weakness 1: Heavy reliance on the entity grid representation, which may not suit all text types.

- Weakness 2: Lacks detailed analysis of performance across different text types or genres.

- Weakness 3: Insufficient discussion of model limitations and potential improvements.

- Question 1: How will the model be extended to other discourse forms like asynchronous conversations?

- Question 2: How do you plan to incorporate rhetorical relations into the model?

- Question 3: How can the model be improved to better handle varied text types or genres?

- Additional comment: Well-written paper with thorough evaluation but would benefit from deeper discussion of limitations and future work.
