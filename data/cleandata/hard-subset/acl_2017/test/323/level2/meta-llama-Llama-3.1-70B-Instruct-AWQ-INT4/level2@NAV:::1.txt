**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization. The authors also present an end-to-end training method to learn task-specific high-level features automatically.

**Main Contributions**

1. A neural local coherence model that can capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization.
2. An end-to-end training method to learn task-specific high-level features automatically.
3. State-of-the-art results on three coherence assessment tasks: discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to modeling local coherence using a CNN, which can capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model on three coherence assessment tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
3. The paper includes a detailed description of the model architecture and training method, making it easy to follow and replicate.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper relies heavily on the entity grid representation, which may not be the most effective representation for all types of text.
2. The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the model's performance on different types of text or genres.
3. The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the model's limitations and potential areas for improvement.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend your model to other forms of discourse, such as asynchronous conversations?
2. Can you provide more details on how you plan to incorporate rhetorical relations into your model?
3. How do you think your model could be improved to better handle different types of text or genres?

**Additional Comments**

The paper presents a well-written and thorough evaluation of a novel approach to modeling local coherence. The authors provide a clear description of their model architecture and training method, making it easy to follow and replicate. However, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the model's limitations and potential areas for improvement.
