**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neural Local Coherence Model**: The paper proposes a novel neural architecture for modeling local coherence, which can capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power.
2. **Pairwise Ranking Method**: The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Use of CNN**: The paper effectively uses CNN to model entity transitions and entity-specific features, which leads to state-of-the-art results.
2. **End-to-End Training**: The pairwise ranking method allows for end-to-end training, which enables the model to learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **Generalization Power**: The model's ability to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power is a significant strength.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Evaluation**: The evaluation is limited to three coherence assessment tasks, which may not be comprehensive enough to fully demonstrate the model's capabilities.
2. **Dependence on Hyperparameters**: The model's performance may depend on the choice of hyperparameters, which can be a weakness if not properly tuned.
3. **Lack of Theoretical Analysis**: The paper lacks a theoretical analysis of the model's behavior, which can make it difficult to understand the underlying mechanisms.

**Questions to Authors**

1. How do you plan to extend the model to other forms of discourse, such as asynchronous conversations?
2. Can you provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning process and how you arrived at the optimal settings?
3. Have you considered using other neural architectures, such as recurrent neural networks (RNNs) or transformers, to model local coherence?
