- Proposes a neural entity grid using CNNs to learn distributed entity-transition representations, addressing dimensionality issues of discrete grids.
- Trains the model end-to-end with pairwise ranking so task-specific features are learned jointly rather than computed separately.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.
- Addresses limitations of traditional entity-grid models by moving from discrete, task-agnostic features to neural representations.
- Provides a convincing experimental evaluation across three diverse tasks with absolute performance gains and statistical significance testing.
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, with a helpful architecture diagram.
- Authors release code, improving reproducibility and enabling follow-up work.
- Individual components (CNNs, pairwise ranking) are not novel; the main novelty is their application to the entity grid.
- Entity-specific features are basic (only three) and not deeply analyzed; more sophisticated features or learned embeddings may help.
- Hyperparameter tuning details and justification (e.g., learning curves) are insufficiently reported.
- Comparison to more recent neural coherence/discourse models is limited and could be expanded.
- Lacks qualitative error analysis and example failures to illuminate model weaknesses.
- Question: Why choose CNNs over RNNs for entity transitions, and were RNNs tried?
- Question: Rationale for selecting the specific entity-specific features and whether others were considered.
- Question: How sensitive are results to pre-training on WSJ and would performance drop without it?
- Question: Can the authors provide examples of strong/weak model outputs and explanations for those cases?
- Minor fixes: correct "loosing" to "losing", clarify Equation 5 as maximizing the margin, and fix premature references to Figure 2.
