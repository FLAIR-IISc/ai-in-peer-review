- Proposes a neural network architecture applying CNNs to the entity grid for text coherence assessment.
- Uses distributed representations and convolution to capture long-range entity transitions; trained end-to-end.
- Evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks with reported state-of-the-art results.
- Key contribution: successful application of CNNs to entity-grid enabling modeling of longer-range dependencies.
- Key contribution: end-to-end training that learns task-specific features instead of hand-engineered ones.
- Empirical gains are substantial and statistically significant across tasks; code is made available.
- Strength: solid experimental evaluation, clear presentation, and thorough baseline comparisons.
- Weakness: lacks motivation/explanation for chosen hyperparameter values and tuning decisions.
- Weakness: insufficient investigation of the impact of different entity-specific features (only three used).
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations, failure cases, and sensitivity to coreference resolution errors.
- Question: please provide more insight into the hyperparameter tuning process and key observations.
- Question: did you test different combinations of entity-specific features and what were the results?
- Question: how robust is the model to coreference resolution errors and have you considered modeling resolver uncertainty?
