- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based local coherence model over entity grids that captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features, trained end-to-end via pairwise ranking and achieving SOTA on three coherence tasks.

- Contribution 1: Introduces a neural architecture for coherence assessment that models long-range entity transitions and arbitrary entity-specific features.

- Contribution 2: Presents a pairwise ranking training approach to learn task-specific high-level features end-to-end.

- Contribution 3: Reports state-of-the-art performance on three different coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models significantly.

- Strength 1: Effectively uses distributed representations to model long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features.

- Strength 2: End-to-end pairwise ranking training enables the model to learn task-specific features directly.

- Strength 3: Demonstrates strong empirical results, achieving SOTA across multiple coherence tasks.

- Weakness 1: Lacks detailed analysis of hyperparameters and their impact on performance.

- Weakness 2: Does not compare against other neural coherence models, limiting contextual evaluation of advantages.

- Weakness 3: Evaluation is limited to three coherence tasks; generalizability to other tasks is not tested.

- Question 1: Please provide a detailed analysis of hyperparameters and their effects on model performance.

- Question 2: How does this model compare empirically to other neural coherence models (e.g., Li and Hovy 2014)?

- Question 3: Can the model be evaluated on related tasks (e.g., summarization, QA) to demonstrate broader generalizability?
