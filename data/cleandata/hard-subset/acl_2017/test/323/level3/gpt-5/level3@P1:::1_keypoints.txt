- Proposes a neural local coherence model: CNN over entity grids that composes entity-role sequences and is trained with pairwise ranking; achieves state-of-the-art on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.  
- Contribution: CNN architecture over entity grids that models long-range entity transitions and avoids sparsity of discrete grids.  
- Contribution: End-to-end pairwise ranking training that learns task-specific features and improves over strong grid baselines.  
- Contribution: Neuralized grid with a small set of features matches or exceeds extended hand-engineered grids; WSJ pretraining benefits summary rating.  

- Strength: Strong empirical gains—~4% absolute on discrimination vs basic grid, ~3–4% vs extended grid, up to 2.5% on insertion, and 2–4% from pretraining on summary rating.  
- Strength: Sound modeling—embeddings + convolutions enable longer transition modeling (k up to 5–8) and pairwise ranking aligns with ranking tasks.  
- Strength: Practical and modular—preserves interpretable entity-grid abstraction, easy to attach entity features, and filters act as pattern detectors.  
- Strength: Careful experimental setup—hyperparameter tuning, early stopping, dropout, multiple seeds, significance testing, baseline comparisons, and released code.  
- Strength: Clear exposition and motivation with convincing intuition about filters and max pooling.  

- Weakness: Incomplete/possibly unfair comparison to Li & Hovy (2014); poor replication reported but not deeply analyzed or reconciled with original results.  
- Weakness: Limited evaluation beyond WSJ and DUC’03, raising concerns about portability to short-document or noisier-coref corpora.  
- Weakness: Analysis is shallow—lacks ablations on transition window size, number of filters, pooling width, and per-feature contribution; insertion-specific training not attempted.  
- Weakness: Dependence on coreference quality is unquantified; no sensitivity analysis across coref systems or oracle coref.  
- Weakness: Minor writing issues (missing definite articles, repetitive phrasing).  

- Question: Which exact code/version, preprocessing, embeddings, and hyperparameters were used for the Li & Hovy replication, and were their original settings tested on their corpora?  
- Question: How does the model perform on AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES and other short-document corpora with noisier coreference?  
- Question: What are the effects of transition window size k and pooling size p per task, and how many filters activate at test time?  
- Question: Does direct training on insertion pairs or a multi-task setup improve insertion performance?  
- Question: How sensitive are results to different coreference resolvers or oracle coreference?  
- Question: What are training and inference runtimes relative to extended grid models, and how do they scale with document length and entity count?  

- Additional request: Reproduce and investigate Li & Hovy’s reported results, document hurdles, and explain divergences.  
- Additional request: Add ablations on entity features and transition window length, and test an insertion-trained variant.  
- Additional request: Proofread and fix wording issues (definite articles, phrasing).  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (4)—moderate novelty but principled, simple, and empirically convincing; finalize after clarifying Li & Hovy replication and adding more analysis.
