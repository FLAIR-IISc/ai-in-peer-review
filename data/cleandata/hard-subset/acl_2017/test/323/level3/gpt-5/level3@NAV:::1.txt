Summary and Contributions
- Empirical contribution: A CNN over entity grids with pairwise ranking achieves state-of-the-art performance on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 summary coherence, with statistically significant gains over strong grid baselines while using fewer handcrafted features.
- Methodological contribution: Convolution over entity-transition sequences enables modeling longer transitions with distributed representations, retaining the interpretability of entity-centric modeling.
- Practical contribution: A simple hybrid recipe (neural layers atop the entity grid) that transfers across tasks, with code release.

Strengths
- Strong, consistent improvements: The model outperforms grid and graph baselines on two WSJ ordering tasks and on DUC’03, with significance testing and multiple random seeds. Transfer learning further boosts the low-data summary rating task, supporting the method’s robustness.
- Clear, well-motivated architecture: The locality-invariant convolution over transition columns directly targets entity-transition patterns; using longer windows (k≈5–8) addresses sparsity in discrete grids; pairwise ranking aligns with coherence as a ranking problem.
- Solid experimental hygiene: Sensible dev splits, hyperparameter search, dropout/early stopping, and reporting of average over runs; public toolkits used for baselines.
- Generally fluent and readable manuscript.

Weaknesses
- Incomplete comparison to neural coherence baselines: The Li and Hovy (2014) model was attempted but omitted after an unexpectedly low score (≈19% discrimination). This raises concerns about setup or domain mismatch and weakens claims of state-of-the-art over neural approaches.
- Potential corpus dependence: Results focus on WSJ; evaluation on domains where Li and Hovy report success (e.g., AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES) is needed to establish generality and to diagnose why sentence-based neural models underperform here.
- Limited analysis beyond accuracy: More ablations (e.g., transition length sensitivity, no-coref setting), qualitative filter visualizations, and error analyses by discourse phenomena would strengthen insights. The hybrid design, while practical, limits novelty relative to a fully neural discourse model.
- Presentation issues: Numerous missing definite articles. The recurring phrase “limits the model to do X” should be revised to “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “stops the model from doing X.”

Questions to Authors (optional)
- Please detail your Li and Hovy setup (embeddings, window size, segmentation, hyperparameters, pretraining) and obstacles faced. Can you reproduce their reported results on their original datasets as a sanity check?
- Can you report results for your model on AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES and/or other corpora where Li and Hovy succeed?
- How sensitive are results to k, pooling size, and to coreference quality (e.g., gold vs. automatic)?
