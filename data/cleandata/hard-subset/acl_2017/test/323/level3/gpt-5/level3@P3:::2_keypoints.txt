- Proposes a neural local coherence model applying 1-D CNNs over embedded entity-grid columns with max-pooling and pairwise ranking, evaluated on WSJ and DUC’03.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating with significance testing and released code.  
- Contribution: neuralized entity-grid architecture with pairwise ranking achieving strong results across three coherence tasks.  
- Contribution: motivates CNNs over grid columns to capture longer transitions while using distributed representations for generalization.  
- Contribution: consolidates coherence evaluation on larger WSJ data and DUC’03 with reproducibility-oriented release.  
- Strength: strong empirical improvements over graph-based and classic/extended entity-grid baselines across multiple tasks.  
- Strength: sensible design—1-D convolutions align with locality/position-invariance and pairwise ranking fits ordering objectives.  
- Strength: effective use of embeddings to address sparsity and enable longer transition modeling without combinatorial explosion.  
- Strength: clear “Why it works” intuition and step-by-step architectural explanation.  
- Strength: reproducibility focus through larger datasets, significance testing, and public code.  
- Weakness: limited novelty—mainly a straightforward neural reimplementation of the entity grid without major conceptual advances.  
- Weakness: questionable justification for large embedding/filter sizes given small categorical inventory; needs ablation/sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: unclear benefit of long transition windows on relatively short WSJ documents; lacks analysis by document length and k.  
- Weakness: reported inability to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) results suggests possible implementation/dataset mismatches needing investigation.  
- Weakness: verbose writing and organizational issues with duplicated content and minor language errors.  
- Question: provide sensitivity analyses for embedding dimensionality, number of filters, and pooling length on DEV/TEST.  
- Question: report accuracy variation with transition window k and stratify results by document length.  
- Question: did you train pairwise loss specifically for insertion (single-sentence moves) and how does that compare?  
- Question: clarify preprocessing/sentence segmentation/negative sampling and settings used when attempting to replicate Li & Hovy (2014).  
- Question: any comparisons to RNNs, attention, or multi-k filter architectures to justify CNN choice?  
- Editorial: condense/reorganize duplicated sections, tighten intro/related work, and proofread for language and phrasing issues.  
- Editorial: consider adding novel features (discourse relations, rhetorical structure), more analyses (ablation, error analysis, runtime), or new tasks/datasets to strengthen contribution.  
- Overall: well-executed neuralization with solid empirical gains and clear intuition but limited conceptual novelty and missing analyses; borderline accept without additional analysis/clarity.
