- Paper proposes a CNN over the entity grid to learn distributed representations of entity-transition sequences, generalizing to longer transitions than discrete features.
- Model is trained end-to-end with pairwise ranking, enabling task-specific feature learning.
- Experiments on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 show consistent gains over strong entity-grid baselines and report state-of-the-art performance.
- Main contribution: CNN-over-grid architecture that captures longer, entity-conditioned transition patterns via embeddings and convolutions.
- Main contribution: End-to-end ranking setup tying feature induction to target coherence tasks.
- Main contribution: Empirical evidence of improvements across multiple coherence evaluations.
- Strength: Methodological advance over entity grid—embeddings + convolution overcome sparsity and share parameters across documents.
- Strength: Use of wide convolution and max-pooling effectively detects salient transition patterns in the grid.
- Strength: Strong empirical results with statistically significant improvements on WSJ tasks and transfer benefits from pretraining.
- Strength: Hybrid design leverages proven entity-grid representation, focusing learning on entity continuity rather than end-to-end discourse modeling.
- Strength: Clear presentation, reported hyperparameters/training details, and code availability support reproducibility.
- Strength: Good positioning in coherence literature and clear explanation of architectural choices (e.g., location-invariant convolution).
- Weakness: Troubled comparison to Li & Hovy (2014)—their reported WSJ accuracy seems implausibly low, raising concerns about experimental mismatch.
- Weakness: Potential corpus dependence—evaluation focused on WSJ/PTB may not generalize to AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES used in prior work.
- Weakness: Under-analyzed assumptions and ablations (independence across entity columns, convolution window sizes, pooling, salience choices).
- Weakness: Task mismatch—training on discrimination pairs but evaluating on insertion may limit insertion gains; insertion-targeted training could help.
- Weakness: Writing issues—missing definite articles and awkward phrasing like “limits the model to do X.”
- Question: What preprocessing, hyperparameters, datasets, and Li & Hovy variants were used when reproducing their results?
- Question: Can you evaluate both models on AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES to assess corpus sensitivity and relative rankings?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to coreference errors; have different coreference systems or oracle mentions been tested?
- Question: What are the effects of window size k, pooling length, number of filters, and dropping salience/NER features?
- Question: Does training the pairwise ranker directly on insertion-derived pairs improve insertion accuracy?
- Additional comment: Proofread for article usage and rephrase “limits the model to do X” to clearer alternatives.
- Additional comment: Document concrete efforts and difficulties running Li & Hovy’s system (environment, versions, preprocessing).
- Recommendation: Score 4 (after author response); method and results are strong but Li & Hovy baseline issues require deeper investigation and reproduction on original datasets.
