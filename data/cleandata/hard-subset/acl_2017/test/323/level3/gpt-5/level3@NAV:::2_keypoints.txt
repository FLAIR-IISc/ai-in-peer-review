Summary: Proposes a CNN over the entity grid with embedded grid symbols, convolution over entity-transition windows, and max-pooling trained with pairwise ranking, showing consistent gains on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 summary ranking.

Contribution 1: Strong empirical evaluation showing consistent, significant improvements over entity-grid baselines across multiple coherence tasks with multiple runs.

Contribution 2: Clear architectural proposal applying CNNs to entity-transition patterns with an end-to-end pairwise ranking objective aligned to coherence scoring.

Contribution 3: Practical consolidation of entity-grid features into a learnable framework (including entity-specific features) with code released.

Strength 1: Empirical performance — outperforms extended entity-grid baselines with reported absolute gains (e.g., ~4% discrimination, up to 2.5% insertion) and significance testing.

Strength 2: Sensible training objective — pairwise ranking directly optimizes the evaluation metric and beats task-agnostic feature extraction.

Strength 3: Clear design intuition — CNN filters capture transition patterns and pooling provides location invariance, well explained in the paper.

Strength 4: Robustness/generalization — pretraining on WSJ transfers to DUC’03; multiple seeds and early stopping mitigate variance.

Strength 5: Transparency — authors report difficulty reproducing prior work rather than making questionable comparisons.

Weakness 1: Limited novelty — mainly neuralizes an existing representation without major representational or task innovations for a full paper.

Weakness 2: Hyperparameter justification lacking — 100-d embeddings for a 4-symbol grid and other choices lack explanation or sensitivity analysis.

Weakness 3: Transition length unclear — benefits of long chains (k=6) are not convincingly demonstrated given short WSJ articles; k ablations needed.

Weakness 4: Writing and structure — verbose and repetitive (e.g., Section 6 repeats Section 2); organization should be tightened.

Weakness 5: Reproducibility/context — failure to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) needs deeper analysis; excluding their result weakens comparative context.

Question 1: Provide ablations for embedding size, k, number of filters, pooling length, and dropout and report the best minimal configuration.

Question 2: How does performance vary with document length and entity density; do long k filters mainly help longer articles?

Question 3: What is the incremental gain from each entity-specific feature in the Extended Grid-CNN?

Question 4: Can you diagnose the Li & Hovy (2014) reproduction failure (preprocessing, training regime, or data mismatch)?
