- Proposes a neural local-coherence model that embeds entity-grid symbols and applies CNNs over entity-transition sequences, trained with pairwise ranking.  
- Evaluated on WSJ discrimination and insertion tasks and DUC’03 summary coherence rating, showing consistent improvements and released code.  
- Primary contribution: neuralization of the entity grid that learns distributed entity-transition representations with convolution and ranking, yielding SOTA gains.  
- Secondary contribution: simple, extensible integration of entity-specific features into the neural grid without combinatorial feature explosion.  
- Tertiary contribution: thorough empirical evaluation with significance testing and reproducible code release.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance—statistically significant improvements across tasks and consistency across five random seeds.  
- Strength: Well-motivated architecture—convolution captures location-invariant local patterns and longer-range transitions without discrete-grid sparsity.  
- Strength: Practical extensibility—composing NE type, salience, and mention features into embeddings avoids brittle feature conjunctions.  
- Strength: Good experimental hygiene—large WSJ corpus, clear training details, hyperparameter sweeps, early stopping, and significance testing.  
- Strength: Clear writing and positioning with honest discussion of prior discrete grid limitations.  
- Weakness: Incomplete/nebulous comparison to Li & Hovy (2014) due to a surprising low replication result that may reflect setup mismatches.  
- Weakness: Limited cross-domain validation—focus on WSJ and DUC’03; lacks results on common smaller corpora like AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES.  
- Weakness: Training-task mismatch for insertion—model trained on discrimination only; task-aligned training for insertion could improve results.  
- Weakness: Limited ablations and analysis—needs studies on window length, pooling, filters, feature contributions, sensitivity to coreference, and qualitative filter inspection.  
- Weakness: Clarity/style issues—missing definite articles and recurring awkward phrasing reduce readability.  
- Rebuttal request: Provide exact replication details for Li & Hovy (code version, preprocessing, embeddings, encoder, negatives, hyperparameters) and sanity checks on original datasets.  
- Rebuttal request: Add cross-domain tests on AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES or subsets of Li & Hovy’s data to assess robustness.  
- Rebuttal request: Report results when fine-tuning/training specifically for insertion (e.g., correct vs. incorrect insertion positions).  
- Rebuttal request: Provide error analysis on coreference sensitivity, entity-type error clusters, and examples of interpretable learned filters.  
- Rebuttal request: Report model size, training time, and inference speed compared to extended grid baselines.  
- Additional comments: Fix proofreading (definite articles, phrasing), clean up citations/formatting, clarify convolution/zero-padding details, and add qualitative visualizations and ablations.  
- Overall recommendation: 4 — Weak Accept.  
- Post-rebuttal note: Reviewer accepts authors’ difficulty running other code but asks for documented replication attempts or sanity-check experiments to strengthen the paper.
