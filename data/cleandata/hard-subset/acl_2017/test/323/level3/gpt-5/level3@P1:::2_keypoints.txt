- Proposed model: CNN applied to embedded entity grids scored with a pairwise ranking objective for document coherence.
- Main contributions: neuralized entity-grid (embeddings + CNNs), SOTA empirical gains on WSJ discrimination/insertion and DUC’03 summary rating, and a compact method to add entity-specific features.
- Strength: Solid empirical improvements with significance testing and effective WSJ pretraining/fine-tuning.
- Strength: Clear modeling intuition for using CNNs (location invariance, longer receptive fields, shared parameters).
- Strength: End-to-end, task-aligned ranking objective that avoids combinatorial feature growth.
- Strength: Practicality and reproducibility: reported implementation details, hyperparameter ranges, and released code using standard preprocessing.
- Weakness: Limited conceptual novelty — straightforward neuralization of existing entity-grid ideas.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification and sensitivity analysis for design choices (e.g., 100-d embeddings).
- Weakness: No rigorous ablation for longer transition windows (k) to support claimed benefits.
- Weakness: Reproducibility gap with Li & Hovy (2014) baseline not investigated, leaving comparisons uncertain.
- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope and ablations (other domains, task-aligned insertion training, per-feature and per-component ablations).
- Weakness: Writing and organization issues, including verbosity, repetition, awkward phrasing, and minor editorial errors.
- Question: How sensitive is performance to embedding dimension and can much smaller embeddings match results?
- Question: Please provide ablations of accuracy vs. transition window k and pooling size (k=2,3,4,5,6,8).
- Question: What are the incremental gains of each entity-specific feature (NE type, salience, properness) and their interactions with k?
- Question: If trained specifically for insertion with a tailored pairwise objective, does performance improve more?
- Question: How robust is the model to coreference quality (simple heuristics vs. oracle coreference)?
- Question: For DUC’03, how much improvement comes from WSJ pretraining vs. fine-tuning, and was any negative transfer observed?
- Additional comment: Fix editorial issues (figure reference, “Firstly/Secondly,” phrasing) and remove redundant paragraph in Section 1.
- Additional comment: Streamline related work to avoid duplication and include brief error analysis for failures (e.g., insertion).
- Additional comment: If space allows, report training/runtime and model size to contextualize practicality.
