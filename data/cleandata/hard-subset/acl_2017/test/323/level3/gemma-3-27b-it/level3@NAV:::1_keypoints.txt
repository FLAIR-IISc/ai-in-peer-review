- Proposes a novel local coherence model by extending the entity grid with a convolutional neural network (CNN).  
- Leverages distributed representations and CNNs to capture long-range entity transitions, addressing limits of traditional feature-engineered grids.  
- Shows empirical improvements over prior methods on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  
- Manuscript is well-written and shows clear understanding of related literature.  
- Primary contribution: successful hybrid integration of a neural network with the interpretable entity grid representation.  
- Secondary contribution: end-to-end training enabling task-specific feature learning.  
- Strongest argument: consistently strong performance across tasks, with substantial gains on summary coherence rating and benefits from entity-specific features.  
- Major concern: unexpectedly poor performance of Li and Hovy (2014) in their experiments raises serious questions about the experimental setup.  
- Authors did not sufficiently investigate the Li & Hovy discrepancy—need details on settings, preprocessing, code execution, and attempts to reproduce original results.  
- Lack of documentation about difficulties running the Li & Hovy system is problematic for reproducibility.  
- Minor issue: numerous missing definite articles reduce readability and professionalism.  
- Reviewer questions: provide detailed settings/parameters for Li & Hovy runs, results of reproducing Li & Hovy on original datasets, and specific challenges encountered plus mitigation steps.
