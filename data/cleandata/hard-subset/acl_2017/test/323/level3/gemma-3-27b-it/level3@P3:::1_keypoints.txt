- Integrates a convolutional neural network with the entity grid to capture long-range entity transitions and richer representations.
- Uses pairwise ranking for end-to-end training so the model learns task-specific coherence features instead of hand-engineered ones.
- Reports state-of-the-art performance across three coherence assessment tasks.

- Manuscript is well-written, clearly motivated, and methodologically well-explained.
- Logical and effective use of CNNs to address limitations of traditional entity grid models and model long-range dependencies.
- Pairwise ranking objective is appropriate for coherence assessment.
- Strong empirical gains (up to ~4% on discrimination and summary rating, ~2.5% on insertion) demonstrating effectiveness and generalizability.
- Code release enhances reproducibility.

- Insufficient comparison to recent neural models, especially Li and Hovy (2014), with an unexpectedly low reported baseline raising concerns about evaluation or implementation.
- Authors provide limited detail about replication difficulties with the Li and Hovy system; more thorough investigation required.
- Numerous minor grammatical issues (missing definite articles) needing proofreading.
- Recurrent awkward phrasing ("limits the model to do X") should be reworded for clarity.

- Please detail the difficulties, parameters, and settings used when attempting to reproduce Li and Hovy (2014).
- Did you try reproducing Li and Hovy’s reported results on their original datasets, and what were the outcomes?
- What hyperparameter search was performed for the CNN, and how were final settings justified?
- Have you conducted ablation studies to quantify contributions of entity-specific features and convolutional layers?

- Add discussion of the model’s computational complexity versus traditional entity grid approaches.
- Explore the impact of different pre-trained word embeddings on performance.
- Provide qualitative analysis of learned features to illustrate what the model captures.

- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (address Li & Hovy replication details and minor language fixes).
