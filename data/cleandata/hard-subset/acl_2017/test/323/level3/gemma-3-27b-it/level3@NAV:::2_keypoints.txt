- Proposes applying a CNN to the entity grid to model text coherence, aiming to capture long-range entity transitions.  
- Well-motivated framing with clear articulation of limitations in prior feature-engineered approaches.  
- Empirical evaluation across discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating shows strong performance, generally outperforming existing methods.  
- Primary contribution: successful “neuralization” of the entity grid, demonstrating CNNs can learn coherence features from the grid.  
- Secondary contribution: thorough multi-task evaluation and inclusion of entity-specific features demonstrating model flexibility.  
- Limited novelty: approach feels incremental beyond adapting the entity grid to a neural model, lacking new representational mechanisms or task applications.  
- Unclear methodological choices: no sufficient justification for using 100-dimensional vectors for four-dimensional grid entries.  
- Unconvincing benefit of longer transition chains (k=6 vs k=3), especially given short WSJ article lengths.  
- Reproducibility concern: authors could not reproduce Li/Hovy (2014) results, suggesting potential implementation or modeling issues.  
- Writing issues: overly verbose, repetitive (significant overlap between sections 2 and 6), and contains minor language/phasing problems.  
- Requests for clarification: detail experiments/selecting 100-d dimensionality, elaborate attempts to reproduce Li/Hovy (2014), and clarify tangible benefits of k=6 vs k=3.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: fix Figure numbering, prefer "First, Second" to "Firstly/Secondly", rephrase specific wording, consider removing final paragraph in section 1.  
- Suggestion to add discussion of computational complexity and scalability of the proposed approach.
