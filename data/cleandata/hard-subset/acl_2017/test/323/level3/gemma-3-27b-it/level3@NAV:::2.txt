## Review of “A Neural Local Coherence Model”

This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) applied to the entity grid representation for modeling text coherence. The core idea – leveraging the representational power of CNNs to capture long-range entity transitions – is well-motivated, and the authors clearly articulate the limitations of prior, feature-engineered approaches that this work aims to address. The extensive evaluation across three tasks (discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating) demonstrates strong performance, generally outperforming existing methods.

The primary contribution of this work is the successful “neuralization” of the entity grid model, demonstrating that a CNN can effectively learn relevant features for coherence assessment directly from the grid representation. A secondary contribution is the thorough evaluation across a range of tasks, providing a strong empirical basis for the proposed approach. The inclusion of entity-specific features further strengthens the model and showcases flexibility.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of substantial innovation beyond this neural adaptation. While the results are impressive, the method feels largely incremental and doesn’t introduce significant new representational mechanisms or task applications beyond consolidating previous work. The justification for using 100-dimensional vectors for the four-dimensional grid entries is unclear and requires further explanation. The benefit of increasing the transition chain length (k=6 vs. k=3) is also not convincingly demonstrated, particularly given the brevity of many WSJ articles. 

A significant weakness is the inability to reproduce a prior result (Li/Hovy 2014), suggesting a potential implementation error or modelling issue that needs addressing. The writing is also overly verbose and repetitive, with significant overlap between sections 2 and 6, impacting readability. Several minor language issues and phrasing problems are present throughout.

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you provide a more detailed justification for the choice of 100-dimensional vectors to represent the grid entries? What experiments were conducted to determine this dimensionality?
2.  Can you elaborate on the difficulties encountered while attempting to reproduce the Li/Hovy (2014) results? What specific steps were taken to ensure a fair comparison?
3.  Please clarify the tangible benefits of using longer transition chains (k=6) versus shorter ones (k=3) given the length distribution of articles in the WSJ dataset.



**Additional Comments:**
