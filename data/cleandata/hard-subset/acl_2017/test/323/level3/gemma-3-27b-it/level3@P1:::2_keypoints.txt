- Proposes a neural local coherence model using CNNs over entity-grid representations, trained end-to-end.  
- Adapts the entity grid framework to a neural architecture and evaluates on coherence tasks.  
- Evaluated on sentence discrimination, sentence insertion, and summary coherence rating with state-of-the-art results.  
- Authors release code to promote reproducibility.  

- Empirical results are strong: consistent SOTA improvements (~2.5–4%) across three tasks.  
- Clear and well-motivated identification of limitations in prior entity grid models (dimensionality, task-decoupled features).  
- Model design and CNN rationale (capturing long-range dependencies via distributed representations) are well explained.  
- Code release is a valuable contribution for reproducibility.  

- Limited novelty: mainly a straightforward neuralization of prior entity grid approaches without major architectural innovations.  
- Embedding dimensionality (100) is not justified; mapping 4D grid entries into 100D seems excessive and needs ablation.  
- Authors could not reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) results, raising concerns about implementation or modeling discrepancies.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive, with overlap across Introduction, Related Work, and entity grid limitations; paper needs reorganization and condensation.  
- Several minor language and proofreading errors (missing articles, awkward phrasing).  

- Question: Please justify embedding dimensionality and provide ablation/analysis over different sizes.  
- Question: Analyze the benefit and optimality of longer transition chains (k=6 or 8) given WSJ article lengths.  
- Question: Specify which parts of Li & Hovy (2014) were irreproducible and whether internal representations/gradients were compared to diagnose issues.  
- Question: Elaborate on potential benefits and concrete steps for incorporating rhetorical relations as suggested.  

- Minor edits: change 'Figure 2' to 'Figure 1' (p.1 bottom left).  
- Style suggestions: replace "Firstly/Secondly" with "First, Second" and rephrase "limits the model to" to "prevents the model from considering...".  
- Consider removing the unnecessary final paragraph in Section 1 and perform careful proofreading for grammar and consistency.
