- Proposal: apply a convolutional neural network to the entity grid to model long-range entity transitions and enable end-to-end task-specific training, achieving SOTA on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

- Primary contribution: successful neuralization of the entity grid with CNNs to learn distributed features and longer-range transitions.

- Secondary contribution: comprehensive evaluation across three coherence tasks providing a strong empirical baseline; code release for reproducibility.

- Strength: strong empirical results, outperforming prior methods by up to ~4% across tasks.

- Strength: well-motivated and effective CNN application to the entity grid, with a clear "why it works" explanation.

- Strength: end-to-end training removes need for hand-engineered features and learns task-specific representations.

- Strength: clear motivation and problem formulation highlighting limits of prior entity-grid models.

- Strength: authors released code, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.

- Weakness: innovation is incremental—mainly "neuralizing" an existing approach rather than introducing fundamentally new architectures.

- Weakness: embedding dimensionality (100d) lacks justification and no ablation over embedding size is reported.

- Weakness: claimed benefit of modeling longer transitions is not convincingly demonstrated given short WSJ articles; needs ablation on transition length k.

- Weakness: reproducibility concern—authors couldn’t reproduce Li and Hovy (2014), raising questions about implementation or assumptions.

- Weakness: writing is verbose and repetitive, with duplicated material between sections and some redundant paragraphs.

- Question: please justify chosen embedding dimensionality and report performance for other sizes.

- Question: please present experiments or ablations showing the effect of transition length k on performance.

- Question: please elaborate on the difficulties reproducing Li and Hovy (2014) and steps taken to resolve discrepancies.

- Question: please report computational cost of the proposed model versus traditional entity-grid approaches.

- Minor edits: fix figure numbering (Figure 2→Figure 1), replace "Firstly/Secondly" with "First, Second", rephrase "limits the model to" to "prevents the model from considering…", and remove redundant final paragraph of Section 1.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing embedding justification, reproducibility concerns, and clarity/conciseness.
