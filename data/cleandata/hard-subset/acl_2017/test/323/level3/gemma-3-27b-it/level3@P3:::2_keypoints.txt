- Proposes a CNN applied to the entity grid to model text coherence by learning distributed representations and long-range transitions.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance across three coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary rating).  
- Provides an empirical consolidation and reproducible baseline for entity-grid approaches in a modern neural framework.  

- Neuralizing the entity grid with CNNs yields clear improvements over traditional grid methods and enables end-to-end feature learning.  
- Evaluation is comprehensive, covering discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  
- Strong empirical results with statistically significant gains (up to ~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion, >4% summary rating).  
- Clear motivation and design rationale for using CNNs and distributed representations.  
- Authors released code, supporting reproducibility.  
- Experimental comparisons are thorough and baselines appropriate.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presented.  

- Core novelty is limited; approach is an incremental application of deep learning to an existing framework.  
- Unclear justification for using 100-dimensional embeddings for grid entries; no ablation on embedding size.  
- Benefit of longer transition chains (k=6 vs k=3) is not convincingly shown and may introduce noise.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive, with duplicated material between sections.  
- Authors could not reproduce Li and Hovy (2014) results, raising reproducibility concerns that need investigation.  

- Request justification/ablation for 100-dimensional embeddings and results for other sizes.  
- Ask for analysis determining optimal transition length k and effects of longer chains.  
- Request details on issues encountered when reproducing Li and Hovy (2014) and potential causes.  
- Ask for breakdown of entity-specific features in the Extended Grid-CNN and their individual contributions.  

- Minor editorial fixes: correct figure reference, adjust phrasing (Firstly/Secondly → First, Second), rephrase certain sentences, remove an unnecessary final paragraph.  
- Suggest adding discussion of the model's computational complexity.
