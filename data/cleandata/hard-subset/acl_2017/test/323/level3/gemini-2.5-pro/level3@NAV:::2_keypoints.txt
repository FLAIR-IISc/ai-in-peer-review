- Proposes a neural model applying a CNN to the entity grid to learn distributed entity-role and transition representations and score local text coherence via a pairwise ranking objective.
- Shows state-of-the-art empirical performance on three coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary rating), outperforming prior methods.
- Presents a clear, well-motivated neural “neuralization” of the entity grid, with CNNs capturing local, position-invariant entity transition patterns.
- Trains end-to-end using a direct pairwise ranking loss suited to coherence assessment.
- Evaluation is extensive and convincing, consistently improving over strong baselines including non-neural extended entity grid models.
- Model intuition and architectural choices are clearly explained, especially the rationale for using CNNs.
- Authors released code, supporting reproducibility and follow-up research.
- Main novelty is limited: method is a straightforward application of a standard CNN to an existing representation rather than a major representational innovation.
- Paper suffers from poor writing and organization, with repetitive sections, numerous language errors, and specific typos (e.g., incorrect figure reference).
- Several modeling choices lack justification (e.g., why 100-dimensional embeddings for four-valued entries, and why long transition window k=6 for short WSJ articles).
- Experimental comparison is incomplete/uncertain because authors could not reproduce and thus excluded results from a key prior work (Li/Hovy 2014), raising concerns about the setup or implementation.
