- Paper proposes applying a 1D CNN to an entity-grid (embedding grid entries, convolving columns, max-pooling) trained with pairwise ranking to score local text coherence; shows SOTA on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.
- Main contribution: strong empirical demonstration that a standard CNN on the entity grid outperforms prior feature-engineered models across multiple coherence benchmarks.
- Main contribution: a reproducibility analysis reporting inability to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) results, highlighting potential issues in prior work.
- Strength: clear motivation and intuition explaining why the entity grid and convolutional treatment are appropriate (independence of columns, location-invariance).
- Strength: thorough, convincing evaluation against strong baselines with statistically significant gains (e.g., ~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion).
- Strength: sensible training paradigm—pairwise ranking is an appropriate objective for relative coherence judgments and enables end-to-end learning.
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—mostly a straightforward “neuralization” of the existing entity-grid representation rather than a fundamentally new model.
- Weakness: unclear justification for key hyperparameters, notably using 100-d embeddings for a 4-symbol vocabulary and long convolution windows (k=5–6) that may be unnecessary or exploit padding.
- Weakness: poor paper organization and verbosity with repetitive sections (Related Work repeats Entity Grid background) and generally rough prose.
- Question: please justify 100-dimensional embeddings for the small grammatical-role vocabulary and report results for smaller embedding sizes (e.g., 10, 20).
- Question: provide analysis of how often long, non-padded entity transitions occur in WSJ and whether performance gains stem from long-range modeling or merely distributed representations and end-to-end training.
- Question: detail the experimental setup used to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014), and hypothesize reasons for the large performance discrepancy (preprocessing, grid generation, hyperparameters).
- Additional comment: fix minor language and proofreading issues (awkward phrasing, incorrect figure reference, prefer “First/Second,” rewording suggestions).
- Additional comment: remove or shorten the introductory roadmap to improve flow and save space.
