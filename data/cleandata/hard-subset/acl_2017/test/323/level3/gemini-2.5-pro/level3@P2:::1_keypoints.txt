- Proposes a CNN applied to the entity grid to learn distributed entity-transition representations, trained end-to-end with a pairwise ranking loss to model local text coherence.  
- Claims contributions: a novel hybrid neural coherence model (CNN + entity grid) that addresses sparsity and long-range transitions.  
- Claims strong empirical validation: state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  
- Strength: sound and elegant model design—CNN suits local, position-invariant transition patterns and end-to-end ranking learns task-specific features.  
- Strength: convincing experimental results—consistent, significant gains over strong baselines including Elsner & Charniak (2011).  
- Strength: clarity and reproducibility—well-written, detailed, and authors plan to release code.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with Li & Hovy (2014)—reported very low accuracy and exclusion from main table without thorough investigation or explanation.  
- Weakness: hybrid pipeline reliance—model depends on upstream parsing/coreference, risking error propagation; discussion of fully neural alternatives is lacking.  
- Key question: provide details on attempts to run Li & Hovy (2014)—code used, hyperparameters, reproduction on original datasets, and hypotheses for failure on WSJ.  
- Additional comments: minor copy-editing needed (missing definite articles, rephrase “limits the model to do X”).
