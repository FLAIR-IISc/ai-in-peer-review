- Proposes a CNN over the entity grid with role embeddings and pairwise ranking, evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks, achieving SOTA.  
- Novelty: "Neuralizes" the entity grid by using embeddings and convolution to capture longer-range entity transitions.  
- Empirical contribution: Consistently and significantly outperforms strong non-neural baselines (e.g., Extended Grid).  
- Strength: Elegant integration of linguistically-motivated entity grid with data-driven CNN learning.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments with substantial, statistically significant gains (≈4% discrimination, >2.5% insertion) using fewer hand-engineered features.  
- Strength: Clear writing, detailed experimental setup, helpful diagram, and released code for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to Li & Hovy (2014) neural baseline—reported 19.34% is anomalous and undermines neural-space claims.  
- Weakness: Model performance depends on upstream NLP (POS, parsing, coreference) and may be limited by their errors; needs discussion.  
- Weakness: Minor grammatical issues and awkward phrasings; requires proofreading.  
- Question: Provide details on experimental setup and hyperparameter tuning for the Li & Hovy baseline and explain the anomalous low accuracy.  
- Question: Did you attempt to reproduce Li & Hovy’s original results on their datasets (e.g., AIRPLANES, EARTHQUAKES) to sanity-check the implementation?
