- Proposes a neural local coherence model that extends the entity grid by applying a CNN to learn distributed representations of entity transition sequences.
- Demonstrates strong empirical validation with state-of-the-art performance on three standard coherence assessment tasks.

- Novel fusion of entity-grid linguistic structure with CNNs to capture long-range entity-transition dependencies and avoid feature sparsity.
- Paper is very well written, with clear motivation, intuitive model explanation, and an easy-to-follow experimental setup.
- Empirical results are strong and statistically significant, outperforming strong baselines including non-neural counterparts.

- Inadequate comparison to Li and Hovy (2014); their reported baseline performance is abnormally low, undermining the SOTA claim.
- Authors do not analyze or explain the poor performance of the Li and Hovy baseline, leaving open whether the issue is experimental, corpus-dependent, or implementation-related.
- Minor copy-editing needed to fix missing articles and awkward phrasing.

- What difficulties were encountered when running Li and Hovy (2014) publicly available code?
- Have the authors attempted to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original reported results on their datasets as a sanity check?
- Any hypotheses why Li and Hovy’s model might perform particularly poorly on the WSJ corpus compared to its original evaluation corpora?
