- Summary: Proposes a CNN applied to an embedded entity-grid, trained end-to-end with pairwise ranking, evaluated on discrimination/insertion/summary tasks, and reports state-of-the-art results.

- Contribution 1: Neural architecture that "neuralizes" the entity grid by embedding grammatical roles and applying a CNN to model longer entity transitions.

- Contribution 2: Strong empirical results, outperforming prior entity-grid and graph-based models by notable margins.

- Strength: Strong empirical performance with consistent gains, proper tuning, and averaged runs supporting effectiveness.

- Strength: Well-motivated, clearly explained method that addresses sparsity and task-agnostic feature issues of classic grids.

- Strength: Reproducibility through standard datasets, established baselines, and released code.

- Weakness: Limited technical novelty—mainly combining existing components (entity grid, embeddings, CNN, ranking loss) without fundamental innovation.

- Weakness: Poorly justified hyperparameter choices (e.g., 100-d embeddings for a 4-symbol vocabulary) with no ablation analysis.

- Weakness: Unconvincing claim that long-range transitions drive gains—no analysis disentangling window-size effects from embeddings/end-to-end training.

- Weakness: Failure to reproduce a key neural baseline (Li & Hovy 2014) yielding a suspiciously low 19.34% accuracy, undermining comparative completeness.

- Weakness: Clarity and organization issues—verbose/repetitive sections and minor grammatical errors; related work should be condensed.

- Question: Justify 100-dimensional embeddings for four symbols and report experiments with lower dimensions.

- Question: Provide analysis comparing different filter sizes k to show that long-range transitions, not other factors, cause the performance gains.

- Question: Explain the implementation/training details for reproducing Li & Hovy (2014) and investigate the anomalous 19.34% result.
