- Proposes a CNN-based neural extension of the entity grid to learn distributed entity-role representations and model long-range entity transitions.
- Claims state-of-the-art results on discrimination, sentence insertion, and DUC summary coherence tasks, outperforming strong baselines.
- Contribution: a novel hybrid architecture combining the linguistic entity grid with a CNN to capture longer transition patterns.
- Contribution: strong empirical gains over prior extended entity-grid models, with consistent improvements across tasks.
- Strength: elegant, well-motivated integration of structured linguistic input and neural learning addressing k>3 transition limitations.
- Strength: robust and consistent experimental improvements (~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion) validating the approach.
- Strength: clear, well-written manuscript with good positioning in related work.
- Weakness: inadequate comparison to a key neural baseline (Li & Hovy 2014), with an unexplained anomalous low accuracy leading to its exclusion.
- Weakness: no investigation or analysis into why the Li & Hovy model failed—no debugging, replication, or discussion of implementation/hyperparameter/domain issues.
- Weakness: recurring minor grammatical errors and awkward phrasing that need proofreading.
- Question: What difficulties were encountered running Li & Hovy (2014)? Were the authors contacted and were their original results reproduced as a sanity check?
- Question: Do the authors have hypotheses for the Li & Hovy performance discrepancy (e.g., domain sensitivity of sentence-based representations) and can they discuss this further?
