- Proposes a neural coherence model applying a CNN over entity-grid representations, with pairwise ranking training and extensive empirical validation.  
- Main contribution is largely a neuralization of existing entity-grid approaches rather than a substantial representational or task innovation.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation showing consistent improvements (≈4% discrimination, 2.5% insertion, 4% summary coherence).  
- Strength: clear motivation for using CNNs to capture long-range transitions and distributed representations.  
- Strength: sensible end-to-end pairwise ranking training aligned with coherence-as-ranking.  
- Weakness: incremental contribution—insufficient novelty for a full paper.  
- Weakness: failed reproduction of Li and Hovy (2014) raises concerns about implementation or modeling correctness.  
- Weakness: verbose and repetitive writing; key design choices (e.g., 100-dim grid vectors) are not justified.  
- Weakness: unclear benefit of long transition chains (k=6) for short documents like WSJ articles.  
- Weakness: pervasive minor language issues that reduce professionalism.  
- Questions: why 100-dimensional grid vectors instead of smaller dimensions?  
- Questions: how is the k=6 transition choice justified for short documents?  
- Questions: what caused the failure to reproduce Li and Hovy (2014) and how will it be fixed?  
- Overall: solid empirical results but insufficient novelty—recommend either more substantive innovations or a condensed, evaluation-focused paper.
