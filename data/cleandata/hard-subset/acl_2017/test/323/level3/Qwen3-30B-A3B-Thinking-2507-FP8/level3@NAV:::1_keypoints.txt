- Proposes a neural coherence model extending the entity grid with a CNN to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features.  
- Main contribution: neural extension of the entity grid enabling learning of longer transition patterns (up to length 8) with distributed representations.  
- Secondary contribution: pairwise ranking training for end-to-end learning of task-specific features.  
- Third contribution: reports state-of-the-art results across three coherence tasks.  
- Empirical gains reported: ~4% on discrimination, ~2.5% on insertion, and 4%+ on summary coherence rating versus non-neural baselines.  
- Strength: technically well-executed, addresses clear limitation of existing entity grid models.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and makes complex concepts accessible.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with Li and Hovy (2014); their model performed very poorly (19.34% accuracy) on the authors' dataset, raising experimental/corpus-dependence concerns.  
- Requested sanity checks: reproduce Li and Hovy’s reported results on their original datasets and document challenges running their system.  
- Writing issues: numerous grammatical problems (missing definite articles) and repeated awkward phrasing (“limits the model to do X”) needing rewording.  
- Questions for authors: explain why Li and Hovy performed poorly on this dataset, whether they tested it on the original corpora, and how experimental differences were handled.  
- Recommendation after response: adjusted to 4 (weak acceptance) pending revisions addressing the Li & Hovy comparison and grammar.
