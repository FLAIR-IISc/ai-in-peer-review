- Proposes a neural extension of the entity grid: a CNN over distributed entity-transition representations, trained end-to-end with pairwise ranking, achieving SOTA on sentence discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

- Contribution: CNN-based model that captures longer-range entity transitions (length 5–8) while avoiding the curse of dimensionality.

- Contribution: Hybrid approach that augments the established entity grid framework with neural network learning rather than replacing it.

- Contribution: Empirical improvements over non-neural baselines: ~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion, ~4% summary coherence rating gains.

- Strength: Strong, statistically significant empirical results across three coherence tasks, including good performance on small summary datasets.

- Strength: Well-structured, clearly written manuscript with logical flow from motivation to evaluation.

- Strength: Pragmatic hybrid design makes the model accessible to researchers familiar with entity grid methods.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to Li and Hovy (2014); paper does not adequately explain discrepancies with their reported results.

- Weakness: Authors did not investigate or reproduce Li and Hovy’s results on original datasets to diagnose poor performance.

- Weakness: Proofreading issues (missing definite articles) distract from the technical content.

- Weakness: Repeated grammatically incorrect phrasing (“limits the model to do X”) that should be fixed for clarity.

- Questions for authors: Provide details on difficulties/errors running Li and Hovy’s system and whether you reproduced their results on AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES.

- Questions for authors: Explain why Li and Hovy’s model performed poorly on your WSJ corpus—corpus differences, configuration, or implementation?

- Recommendation: Weak accept (score 4) pending author response, with fixes for Li & Hovy comparison and proofreading.
