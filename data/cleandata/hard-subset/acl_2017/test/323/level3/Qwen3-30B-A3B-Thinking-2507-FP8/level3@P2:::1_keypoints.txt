- Proposes a CNN extension of the entity grid to model long-range entity transitions (up to length 8), addressing curse-of-dimensionality issues in discrete grids.  
- Introduces an end-to-end pairwise ranking training approach so the model learns task-specific high-level features automatically.  
- Provides comprehensive evaluation on three coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary coherence) showing consistent improvements: ~+4% discrimination, +2.5% insertion, and +4%+ summary rating.  
- Strength: Technically significant extension of entity grid with CNNs that captures longer-range transitions and yields empirical gains, notably on the harder insertion task.  
- Strength: Well-justified and effectively implemented end-to-end pairwise ranking training that integrates feature learning with the target task.  
- Strength: Thorough, well-designed evaluation using three complementary tasks and the large/diverse WSJ corpus, with consistent improvements over strong baselines.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison with Li and Hovy (2014); their reimplementation performed poorly (19.34% accuracy) but authors don’t investigate or document why.  
- Weakness: Manuscript contains numerous grammatical and phrasing issues (definite article misuse) that require proofreading.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks justification for choosing a convolutional approach over other sequence models (e.g., RNNs/LSTMs); needs brief comparison.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of why the neural model improves the insertion task; authors should analyze learned features or adapt training for insertion.  
- Question: Provide implementation details and reproduction attempts for Li and Hovy (2014), including any code changes and sanity checks on original datasets.  
- Question: Elaborate how to adapt the model to better handle the insertion task directly (e.g., training or architectural changes).  
- Question: Explain selection of entity-specific features (named entity type, salience, proper mention) and why these were chosen over other features from Elsner & Charniak (2011).  
- Overall recommendation: Solid contribution and fit for ACL 2017; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the Li & Hovy comparison, proofreading, and additional analyses.
