- Contributions: proposes a neural local coherence model using a CNN-extended entity grid, pairwise ranking training, and reports state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.

- Strength 1: Well-motivated extension of entity grid using distributed representations to model longer entity transitions (k=5–8) and enable task-specific feature learning.

- Strength 2: Strong, consistent experimental gains (≈+4% discrimination, +2.5% insertion, +4%+ summary) with statistical significance and evaluation on a large WSJ corpus.

- Strength 3: Clear, well-structured presentation of the architecture (lookup, convolution, pooling) and relation to prior work.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient analysis of why Li & Hovy’s neural model performed poorly on their dataset, leaving potential implementation or dataset confounds unexplored.

- Weakness 2: Numerous grammatical errors (missing definite articles) that hurt readability and professionalism.

- Weakness 3: Inadequate discussion of why the CNN approach outperforms Li & Hovy’s recurrent/recursive architectures; needs deeper architectural comparison.

- Question 1: What specific challenges or implementation details led to poor reproduction of Li & Hovy’s model, and were their original results reproducible as a sanity check?

- Question 2: Which Extended Grid features from Elsner & Charniak (2011) were excluded, and how might including them affect performance?

- Question 3: How do pre-trained discrimination models on WSJ compare to training from scratch on the summary task, and what does that imply about transfer learning for coherence?

- Overall: Valuable neuralization of the entity grid with strong results, but requires deeper comparison to Li & Hovy and better proofreading before final acceptance.
