- Proposes a neural extension of the entity grid using a CNN to capture long-range entity transitions with distributed representations.
- Introduces end-to-end training to learn task-specific coherence features.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on three coherence assessment tasks.

- Clear motivation for the CNN: location-invariant modeling and longer-range dependencies (k=6) with convincing filter-level explanations.
- Strong empirical evaluation across discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks with statistically significant gains.
- Effectively neuralizes the entity grid, addressing dimensionality issues via distributed embeddings and convolutions.

- Limited novelty: largely a straightforward neuralization of prior entity grid work rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Unjustified design choices: use of 100-dim (and 32-dim) vectors for grid entries lacks rationale.
- Questionable use of long transition chains (k=6) given short average WSJ article lengths (~21.5 sentences).
- Poor writing and organization, with repetition (notably between sections 2 and 6) and numerous language issues.
- Could not reproduce Li and Hovy (2014) result, which raises concerns about implementation or prior work.

- Request clarification on why 100-dim embeddings were chosen and their effect on performance.
- Ask for justification of k=6 versus shorter transitions given dataset sentence lengths.
- Request details on the issues encountered reproducing Li and Hovy (2014).
- Ask for a revised manuscript addressing repetition and language problems.

- Overall: technically solid with strong empirical gains but insufficient novelty, questionable design choices, and poor writing, so not yet suitable for ACL acceptance.
