- Proposal: A CNN-based neural adaptation of the entity grid to model text coherence and capture long-range entity transitions.
- Training: End-to-end pairwise ranking framework that learns task-specific features from distributed representations.
- Evaluation: Tested on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks, reporting state-of-the-art results.
- Contribution 1: Leverages CNNs to model long-range entity transitions within the entity grid framework.
- Contribution 2: Provides an end-to-end training approach that integrates task-specific feature learning.
- Contribution 3: Demonstrates consistent improvements over prior methods across multiple coherence tasks.
- Strength: Well-motivated use of CNNs with clear explanation of benefits of convolutional layers and distributed representations.
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation with strong gains, especially on discrimination and insertion tasks.
- Strength: Simple, accessible model design and released code enhance reproducibility.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—mainly a neuralized, incremental extension of existing entity grid models.
- Weakness: Unclear design choices, notably the unexplained use of 100-dimensional embeddings and limited analysis of transition chain lengths (k).
- Weakness: Writing is repetitive and could be better organized, particularly introductions to entity grids and related work.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns due to inability to replicate Li and Hovy (2014), casting doubt on some comparisons.
- Questions: Why choose 100-dimensional embeddings and were other sizes tried?
- Questions: How sensitive is performance to transition chain length k, and would task-specific optimization help?
- Questions: What challenges were faced reproducing Li and Hovy (2014) and how do they affect comparative validity?
- Conclusion: Solid, well-motivated contribution that achieves strong results but needs clearer justification of design choices and improved writing; suitable for acceptance with minor revisions.
