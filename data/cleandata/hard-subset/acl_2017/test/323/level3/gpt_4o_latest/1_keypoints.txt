- Proposes extending the entity grid model with a CNN to capture long-range entity transitions and learn distributed entity representations.  
- Introduces a pairwise ranking end-to-end training method enabling task-specific feature learning.  
- Evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks, reporting state-of-the-art results across multiple datasets.  
- Effectively captures longer entity transitions compared to traditional grid models, addressing a key limitation.  
- Enables task-specific end-to-end learning rather than decoupled feature extraction.  
- Uses comprehensive evaluation across three tasks and multiple datasets, strengthening empirical validity.  
- Incorporates entity-specific features without exponential parameter growth, showing model flexibility.  
- Paper is well-organized with sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Insufficient comparison with Li and Hovy (2014) and lack of detailed analysis of their poor reported performance.  
- Limited investigation into why Li and Hovy’s neural coherence model underperforms and implications for coherence modeling.  
- Minor proofreading and grammatical issues that hurt readability.  
- Unclear generalizability due to corpus dependency, particularly choice of WSJ over smaller domain-specific corpora.  
- Asks authors to clarify experimental setup and reproduction challenges for Li and Hovy’s model.  
- Requests evaluation on smaller domain-specific datasets (e.g., AIRPLANES, EARTHQUAKES) to assess generalization.  
- Suggests considering incorporation of rhetorical relations or discourse structures and how they would affect the architecture.  
- Recommendation: accept (score 4) with suggestions to document reproduction challenges for Li and Hovy’s system and to proofread for clarity.
