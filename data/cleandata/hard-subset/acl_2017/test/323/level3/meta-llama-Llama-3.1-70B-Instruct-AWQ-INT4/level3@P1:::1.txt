**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization power. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a significant margin.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Neuralization of Entity Grid Model**: The paper proposes a neural extension of the basic entity grid model, which can capture long-range entity transitions and incorporate entity-specific features without losing generalization power.
2. **Convolutional Neural Network Architecture**: The authors design a convolutional neural network architecture that can model entity transitions of arbitrary lengths in a location-invariant way, allowing the model to capture higher-level features from the entity grid.
3. **Pairwise Ranking Approach**: The paper presents a pairwise ranking approach to train the model on a target task and learn task-specific features, which enables the model to learn a scoring function that can be used to compare alternative hypotheses.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved Performance**: The model achieves state-of-the-art results on three different coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models by a significant margin.
2. **Flexibility and Generalization**: The neural architecture can capture long-range entity transitions and incorporate entity-specific features, allowing the model to generalize well to different tasks and datasets.
3. **End-to-End Training**: The pairwise ranking approach enables the model to learn task-specific features in an end-to-end fashion, which simplifies the training process and improves the model's performance.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Comparison to Li and Hovy's Model**: The paper does not provide a detailed comparison to the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy, which raises concerns about the experimental setup and corpus dependence.
2. **Experimental Setup**: The paper does not provide sufficient details about the experimental setup, including the hyperparameter tuning process and the training procedure.
3. **Proofreading**: The paper contains numerous missing definite articles throughout, which affects the readability and fluency of the manuscript.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details about the experimental setup, including the hyperparameter tuning process and the training procedure?
2. Can you reproduce Li and Hovy's reported results on their original datasets as a sanity check?
3. Can you document the efforts and difficulties encountered when running the Li and Hovy system?
4. Can you provide more insights into why the Li and Hovy model performed poorly on your dataset?
5. Can you discuss the potential applications of your model in real-world scenarios, such as text generation and summarization?
