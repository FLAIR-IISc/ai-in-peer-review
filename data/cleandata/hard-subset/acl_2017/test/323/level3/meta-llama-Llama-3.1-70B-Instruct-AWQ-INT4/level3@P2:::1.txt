**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model aims to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power. The authors introduce a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a significant margin.

**Contributions of the Work**

1. **Neural Extension of Entity Grid Model**: The authors propose a neural extension of the basic entity grid model, which can capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power.
2. **Convolutional Neural Network Architecture**: The authors introduce a CNN architecture that can model entity transitions and entity-specific features in a location-invariant way, allowing for the capture of longer transitional dependencies without overfitting.
3. **Pairwise Ranking Method**: The authors propose a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.

**Strengths of the Submission**

1. **Well-Written and Fluent Manuscript**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read.
2. **Effective Use of Hybrid Approach**: The authors' decision to use a hybrid approach, combining a neural network with an existing model, is effective in achieving state-of-the-art results.
3. **Strong Experimental Results**: The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed model.

**Weaknesses of the Submission**

1. **Limited Comparison to Li and Hovy's Model**: The authors do not provide an in-depth comparison to Li and Hovy's neural coherence model, which is a significant omission.
2. **Concerns about Experimental Setup**: The poor performance of Li and Hovy's model raises concerns about the experimental setup or corpus-dependence.
3. **Minor Errors in Writing**: There are numerous missing definite articles throughout the paper, which can be distracting.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed comparison to Li and Hovy's neural coherence model, including an explanation for the significant difference in performance?
2. Can you document the efforts and difficulties encountered when running Li and Hovy's system, and provide more information about the experimental setup?
3. Have you considered reproducing Li and Hovy's reported results on their original datasets as a sanity check?

**Additional Comments**

The authors should consider rephrasing the recurring expression "limits the model to do X" to clearer alternatives like "limits the model's capacity to do X" or "stops the model from doing X." Additionally, the authors should proofread the paper for article usage to ensure that it is free of errors.
