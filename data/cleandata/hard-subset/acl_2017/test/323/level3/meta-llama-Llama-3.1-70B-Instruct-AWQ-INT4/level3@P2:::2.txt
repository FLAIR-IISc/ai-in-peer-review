**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model aims to capture long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization power. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a significant margin.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neuralization of Entity Grid Model**: The authors propose a neural extension of the basic entity grid model, which captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features using a CNN architecture.
2. **Pairwise Ranking Approach**: The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task, allowing the model to learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a significant margin.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-Motivated Approach**: The authors provide a clear discussion of design intuitions, including the choice of CNN architecture and the pairwise ranking approach.
2. **Extensive Evaluation**: The authors evaluate the proposed method on multiple tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and robustness.
3. **Improved Performance**: The model achieves state-of-the-art results on three different coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models by a significant margin.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Representational Innovations**: The method is largely a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid, lacking substantial representational or task innovations expected for a full paper.
2. **Unclear Justification for Design Choices**: The authors do not provide a clear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries.
3. **Verbose and Repetitive Writing**: The writing is verbose and repetitive, with duplicated introductory material and related work that should be reorganized or condensed.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a clear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries?
2. How do you plan to address the lack of representational innovations in the proposed method?
3. Can you reorganize or condense the writing to make it more concise and clear?

Minor language and proofreading issues, such as missing articles and phrasing problems, should be addressed. Specific editorial fixes, such as replacing 'Figure 2' with 'Figure 1' and rephrasing certain sentences, should also be considered.
