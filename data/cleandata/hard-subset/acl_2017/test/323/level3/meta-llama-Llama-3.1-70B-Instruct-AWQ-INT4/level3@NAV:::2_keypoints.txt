- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model over entity grids that captures long-range entity transitions and learns task-specific features end-to-end, outperforming prior methods on three coherence tasks.

- Contribution: Neuralization of entity grid model to capture long-range transitions and entity-specific features while retaining generalization.

- Contribution: Use of a convolutional architecture to model local patterns of entity transitions in the grid.

- Contribution: Pairwise ranking training approach to learn task-specific high-level features.

- Strength: Achieves state-of-the-art results on three coherence assessment tasks.

- Strength: Effectively leverages convolutional architecture to capture local entity transition patterns.

- Strength: End-to-end training simplifies learning of task-specific representations.

- Weakness: Lacks significant innovation beyond straightforward neuralization of the entity grid.

- Weakness: Unclear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries.

- Weakness: Contains minor language and proofreading issues throughout the paper.

- Question: Please provide more details on hyperparameter choices and their impact on performance.

- Question: How will the model be extended to other discourse forms, such as asynchronous conversations?

- Question: Can the authors explain the failure to reproduce Li/Hovy (2014) and whether it indicates implementation or modeling errors?
