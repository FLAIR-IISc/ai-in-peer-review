- Proposes a neural local coherence model extending the entity grid with a CNN to learn and generalize entity-transition sequences.
- Captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features via distributed representations.
- Trains the model end-to-end with a pairwise ranking method to learn task-specific high-level features.
- Well-written, fluent manuscript that is easy to follow.
- Hybrid approach combining neural networks with traditional entity grid strengths.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on three coherence assessment tasks, outperforming prior models.
- Provides thorough evaluation including comparisons and result analyses.
- Lacks an in-depth comparison with the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy, which performed unexpectedly poorly.
- Needs investigation into why the Li and Hovy model performed poorly and tests on corpora where it succeeds.
- Missing detailed documentation of efforts and difficulties when running the Li and Hovy system.
- Should reproduce Li and Hovy’s reported results on their original datasets as a sanity check.
- Requests explanation for the poor Li and Hovy performance in their experiments.
- Asks how hyperparameters were selected and what their impact was on results.
- Requests more information on the entity-specific features and their selection process.
- Asks about plans to extend the model to other discourse forms (e.g., asynchronous conversations).
- Recommends a more detailed description of the entity grid model and its extensions.
- Suggests adding visualizations to illustrate the model architecture and experimental results.
- Advises a deeper discussion of result implications and potential applications.
