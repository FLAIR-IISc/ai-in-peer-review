- Proposes a neural architecture that extends the entity grid with a CNN to model local coherence and long-range entity transitions.  
- Introduces end-to-end training to automatically learn task-specific high-level features.  
- Evaluated on three coherence assessment tasks and reports state-of-the-art results.  
- Strength: CNN models entity transitions to capture long-range dependencies without overfitting.  
- Strength: Pairwise ranking training enables learning of task-specific features.  
- Strength: Experimental results outperform existing models across all three tasks.  
- Weakness: Li and Hovy model performed unexpectedly poorly, raising concerns about experimental setup or corpus dependence.  
- Weakness: Authors did not investigate or document efforts/difficulties running the Li and Hovy system.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to other neural coherence models.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of learned features and hyperparameter effects.  
- Weakness: Minor presentation issues (missing definite articles; awkward recurring phrasing).  
- Questions: Explain Li and Hovy’s poor performance, document reproduction attempts, reproduce original Li and Hovy results as a sanity check, and discuss extension to other discourse forms (e.g., asynchronous conversations).  
- Overall: Novel and effective approach that, with revisions addressing weaknesses, could make a significant NLP contribution.
