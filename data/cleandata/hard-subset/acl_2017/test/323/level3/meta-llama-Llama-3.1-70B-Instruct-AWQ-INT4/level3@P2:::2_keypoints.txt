- Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model operating on the entity grid to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features.  
- Trains the model end-to-end using a pairwise ranking approach to learn task-specific high-level features.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on three coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models.  
- Well-motivated design with clear discussion of CNN architecture choice and ranking loss.  
- Extensive evaluation across multiple tasks demonstrating effectiveness and robustness.  
- Significant empirical performance improvements over prior models.  
- Largely a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid with limited representational or task innovations.  
- No clear justification provided for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive, with duplicated introductory and related-work material.  
- Asks authors to justify the 100-dimensional representation choice.  
- Requests plans to address the lack of representational innovations.  
- Requests reorganization/condensing of the manuscript for clarity and conciseness.  
- Notes minor language and proofreading issues and suggests specific editorial fixes (e.g., figure numbering and sentence rephrasing).
