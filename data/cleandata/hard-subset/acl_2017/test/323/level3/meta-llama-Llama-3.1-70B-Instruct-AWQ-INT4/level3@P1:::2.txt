**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural local coherence model that uses a convolutional neural network (CNN) to operate on the entity grid representation of a text. The model aims to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Contributions of the Paper**

1. **Neuralization of the Entity Grid Model**: The paper proposes a novel approach to model text coherence by applying a CNN on top of the entity grid representation. This approach allows for the capture of long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power.
2. **Pairwise Ranking Method**: The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. This method is sensible and allows for the direct optimization of coherence ranking.
3. **Extensive Evaluation**: The paper presents an extensive evaluation of the proposed method on multiple tasks, including sentence ordering and summary coherence rating. The results show that the proposed method outperforms previous approaches.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Well-Motivated Approach**: The paper presents a well-motivated approach to model text coherence, with clear discussion of design intuitions.
2. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The evaluation results show that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art results on multiple tasks.
3. **Extensive Evaluation**: The paper presents an extensive evaluation of the proposed method on multiple tasks, which demonstrates its effectiveness.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Lack of Representational or Task Innovations**: The method is largely a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid, lacking substantial representational or task innovations expected for a full paper.
2. **Unclear Justification for Hyperparameter Choices**: The paper does not provide a clear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries.
3. **Verbose and Repetitive Writing**: The writing is verbose and repetitive, with duplicated material in the introductory section and related work.
4. **Minor Language and Proofreading Issues**: There are minor language and proofreading issues throughout the paper.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more insight into the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the dimensionality of the vector representations?
2. How do you plan to address the issue of short articles in the WSJ dataset, which may limit the effectiveness of longer transition chains?
3. Can you provide more details on the implementation of the pairwise ranking method and how it is used to train the model?
4. How do you plan to extend the model to other forms of discourse, such as asynchronous conversations?
