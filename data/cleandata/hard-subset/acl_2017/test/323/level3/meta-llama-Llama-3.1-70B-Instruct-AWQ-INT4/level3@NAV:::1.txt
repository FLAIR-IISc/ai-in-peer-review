**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model that extends the entity grid model with a convolutional neural network (CNN) to learn entity-transition sequences and better generalize over longer sequences. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization, thanks to the power of distributed representation. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.

**Main Contributions**

1. The paper proposes a neural architecture that extends the entity grid model with a CNN to learn entity-transition sequences and better generalize over longer sequences.
2. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization, thanks to the power of distributed representation.
3. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.

**Strengths**

1. The paper presents a well-written and fluent manuscript that is easy to follow.
2. The authors propose a hybrid approach that combines the strengths of both neural networks and traditional entity grid models.
3. The model achieves state-of-the-art results on three different coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models by a good margin.
4. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model, including a comparison with other existing models and an analysis of the results.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper could benefit from a more in-depth comparison with the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy, which scored unexpectedly low in the experiments.
2. The authors should investigate why the Li and Hovy model performed poorly and test their model on corpora where it succeeds.
3. The paper could benefit from more detailed documentation of the efforts and difficulties encountered when running the Li and Hovy system.
4. The authors should consider reproducing Li and Hovy's reported results on their original datasets as a sanity check.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on why the Li and Hovy model performed poorly in your experiments?
2. How did you select the hyperparameters for your model, and what was the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the results?
3. Can you provide more information on the entity-specific features used in your model and how they were selected?
4. How do you plan to extend your model to other forms of discourse, such as asynchronous conversations?

**Additional Comments**

1. The paper could benefit from a more detailed description of the entity grid model and its extensions.
2. The authors should consider adding more visualizations to help illustrate the architecture of their model and the results of their experiments.
3. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results and the potential applications of the model.
