- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model on the entity grid, trained with pairwise ranking, and reports state-of-the-art results on three coherence tasks.

Contributions:
- Neuralization of entity grid: Applies a CNN over the entity grid to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features while retaining generalization.
- Pairwise ranking method: Introduces end-to-end pairwise ranking training to learn task-specific high-level coherence features.
- Extensive evaluation: Evaluates on sentence ordering and summary coherence rating tasks and outperforms prior models.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated approach with clear design intuitions.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple coherence assessment tasks.
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation demonstrating effectiveness.

Weaknesses:
- Largely a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid with limited novel representational or task innovations.
- Unclear justification for hyperparameter choices (e.g., 100-dimensional vectors for four-dimensional entries).
- Verbose and repetitive writing, with duplicated material in intro and related work.
- Minor language and proofreading issues.

Questions to authors:
- Please justify hyperparameter choices, particularly the vector dimensionality.
- How will you address short WSJ articles that limit the utility of long transition chains?
- Provide more implementation details on the pairwise ranking method and training procedure.
- How can the model be extended to other discourse forms, such as asynchronous conversations?
