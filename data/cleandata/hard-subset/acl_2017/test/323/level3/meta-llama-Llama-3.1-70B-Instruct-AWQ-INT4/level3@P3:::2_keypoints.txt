- Proposes a neural local coherence model using a CNN over the entity grid to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features.  
- Trains the model end-to-end with a pairwise ranking method to learn task-specific high-level features.  
- Clear design motivation and rationale for choosing CNNs, with thorough explanation of architecture (role vectors, transition modeling, coherence scoring).  
- Describes how to incorporate entity-specific features into the model.  
- Evaluation on three coherence tasks shows state-of-the-art performance, outperforming several existing models and baselines.  
- Summary coherence rating task improves further with pre-training.  
- Unable to reproduce results from Li/Hovy (2014), indicating a possible implementation or modeling error.  
- Method is largely a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid and lacks substantial representational or task innovations for a full paper.  
- Unclear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries.  
- Questionable benefit of longer transition chains (k=6 vs k=3), especially given many WSJ articles are short.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive, with duplicated sections (notably section 6 repeats section 2); needs reorganization and condensation.  
- Minor language, phrasing, and proofreading issues throughout; specific editorial fixes are needed (figure reference, wording changes).  
- Primary contribution: proposal of a neural local coherence model that attains SOTA on multiple coherence assessment tasks.  
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive evaluation across multiple tasks demonstrating the method’s effectiveness and robustness.  
- Reviewer questions: request implementation details for Li/Hovy reproduction, plans to address representational/task novelty, justification for 100-d vectors, investigation plan for longer transitions, and details on pre-training/fine-tuning.
