## Review of "A Neural Local Coherence Model"

This paper presents a neural network extension to the established entity grid model for local coherence assessment. The core idea – leveraging convolutional neural networks to learn entity-transition sequences and improve generalization – is a sensible and potentially impactful direction. The manuscript is well-written and fluent, making the technical details accessible. The hybrid approach of augmenting an existing model with neural components, rather than building a fully neural system from scratch, is a pragmatic choice.

The primary contribution of this work, as I see it, lies in demonstrating the effectiveness of applying convolutional networks to the entity grid representation. This allows for capturing longer-range dependencies than traditional methods, which are limited by the curse of dimensionality. A second contribution is the end-to-end training approach, enabling the model to learn task-specific features automatically. Finally, the thorough evaluation across three coherence assessment tasks provides a solid empirical foundation for the proposed method.

However, a significant weakness is the insufficient comparison to the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy (2014). The reported performance of the Li and Hovy model (19.34% accuracy) is unexpectedly low, raising serious concerns about the experimental setup or corpus dependence. The authors briefly mention difficulties running the Li and Hovy system, but this is insufficient. 

Specifically, the authors need to: 1) Document *exactly* what efforts were made to reproduce the Li and Hovy results, including specific parameters, hardware, and any modifications to their code. 2) Detail the difficulties encountered – were there errors, convergence issues, or other problems? 3) As a sanity check, reproduce the Li and Hovy results on their original datasets to confirm the implementation is functioning correctly. Without this, the comparison is not meaningful and casts doubt on the validity of the reported improvements.

Furthermore, the paper contains numerous missing definite articles ("limits the model to do X" should be "limits the model's capacity to do X"). A careful proofread is necessary. 

**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Please provide a detailed account of the steps taken to reproduce the Li and Hovy (2014) model, including the specific code version used and any modifications made.
2.  Can you elaborate on the difficulties encountered when running the Li and Hovy system? What specific errors or issues arose?
3.  Have you attempted to reproduce the Li and Hovy results on their original datasets? If so, what were the results?
