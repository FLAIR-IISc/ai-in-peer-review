- Proposes a CNN-based neural architecture operating on distributed entity grids for improved text coherence modeling.
- Introduces end-to-end, task-specific feature learning to overcome limitations of discrete, task-agnostic grid models.
- Captures long-range entity transitions and includes simple entity-specific features, achieving state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.
- Releases code to enhance reproducibility.
- Novel technical contribution by adapting CNNs to entity grids, addressing dimensionality and feature extraction challenges.
- Strong empirical evaluation across three tasks and datasets, with notable gains (≈4% discrimination, ≈2.5% insertion) and use of pretraining/fine-tuning.
- Good reproducibility through detailed model/hyperparameter descriptions and public code.
- Scalable ability to model longer entity transitions without overfitting, improving over traditional k ≤ 3 constraints.
- Limited richness of entity-specific features (only three simple features), suggesting room for semantic or rhetorical enhancements.
- Model trained on discrimination but evaluated on insertion without task-specific fine-tuning, possibly limiting insertion performance.
- Primary empirical evidence relies on the WSJ dataset; more diverse-domain testing (conversational/narrative) is needed for generalizability claims.
- Lacks deeper analysis comparing to recent neural models (e.g., Li and Hovy 2014) to explain their poor performance on WSJ.
- Questions for authors: have they tried richer entity features, tested on non-WSJ domains, or trained directly on the insertion task?
- Conclusion: significant and innovative contribution with strong results; recommendation to accept with minor revisions addressing noted weaknesses.
