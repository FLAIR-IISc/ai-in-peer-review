- Proposes a novel neural entity-grid model: map grammatical roles to embeddings, apply 1D CNN over each entity's transition column, max-pool, and train end-to-end with a pairwise ranking loss.  
- Claims ability to learn distributed transition representations and capture longer-range dependencies, overcoming prior sparsity limitations.  
- Strong empirical contribution: achieves new state-of-the-art on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks versus classic entity-grid baselines.  
- Elegant, well-motivated architecture: treating entity transitions as 1D signals and using embeddings addresses feature explosion and sparsity.  
- Significant performance gains: ~4% on discrimination, up to 2.5% on insertion, and >4% on summary rating compared to strong baselines.  
- Thorough and rigorous evaluation: uses standard datasets (WSJ, DUC'03), fair baselines (Elsner & Charniak 2011), significance testing, and multiple runs.  
- Clear writing and reproducibility: well-described model, helpful figures, and released code.  
- Lacks analysis of learned filters and interpretability: no qualitative inspection of what convolutional filters capture, so mechanisms remain speculative.  
- Missing ablation on filter window size k: no empirical evidence showing benefits of larger windows for long-range transitions.  
- Incomplete neural baselines comparison: excludes Li & Hovy (2014) from main results without sufficient analysis of the failure.  
- Uneven feature comparison in extended model: Extended Grid-CNN uses only 3 entity features vs. 9 in the non-neural extended grid, weakening apples-to-apples claims.  
- Requests for authors: provide qualitative filter analyses, ablation study over k (e.g., 2→5/6), and detailed explanation why Li & Hovy (2014) performed poorly on these datasets.
