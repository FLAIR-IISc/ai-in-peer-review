- Proposes a CNN-based neural model operating on the entity grid: role embeddings → convolutions over entity transition sequences → max-pooling → pairwise ranking for coherence scoring, trained end-to-end.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks, outperforming strong non-neural entity-grid baselines.  
- Contribution: novel neuralization of the entity grid that uses dense embeddings and CNNs to capture longer-range, location-invariant transition patterns.  
- Contribution: end-to-end training with a pairwise ranking loss that jointly learns role embeddings and scoring weights optimized for coherence ranking.  
- Contribution: strong empirical validation across three standard tasks and a large WSJ corpus, with significant, statistically tested gains over baselines.  
- Strength: technically sound and clearly presented; CNN choice and pairwise objective are well-motivated and the architecture is thoroughly explained.  
- Strength: robust experimental setup with multiple strong baselines, large corpus, and statistical significance testing, making results convincing.  
- Strength: directly addresses entity-grid limitations (data sparsity, limited transition length) via distributed representations and convolutional modeling.  
- Strength: authors provide detailed hyperparameters and release code, supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: entity-specific features are integrated by creating composite symbols (e.g., PERSON-S), which can explode vocabulary size and reintroduce sparsity; concatenated embeddings were not explored.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparison with alternative neural approaches (e.g., sentence-centric models like Li & Hovy 2014) due to reproduction issues, leaving a gap in evaluation.  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis of learned components (filters, embeddings), so it’s unclear what patterns the model captures beyond quantitative gains.  
- Question: did you consider separate embeddings for feature types concatenated with role embeddings as a more scalable alternative to composite symbols?  
- Question: have you tried training objectives aligned with the insertion task (e.g., predicting correct insertion point) to improve insertion performance?  
- Question: can you provide analysis/visualizations of learned components (filter effects, different window sizes, geometric relationships among {S,O,X,-} embeddings)?
