- Proposes a CNN that operates on the entity grid to assess local text coherence, using embeddings for grammatical roles (and optional entity features) and trained end-to-end with a pairwise ranking objective.
- Contribution: novel application of convolutional filters over entity transition columns to overcome sparsity and model long-range transition patterns with distributed representations.
- Contribution: strong empirical validation showing state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary-rating tasks using a large WSJ corpus and released code.
- Strength: method is well-motivated and technically sound, enabling learning of complex, location-invariant entity transition patterns that discrete counts struggled with.
- Strength: delivers substantial, statistically significant performance gains (≈4%+ on key tasks) over strong non-neural baselines.
- Strength: evaluation is thorough and reproducible — large, diverse dataset and public code increase robustness and community benefit.
- Weakness: lacks comparison to contemporary neural baselines (e.g., Li & Hovy 2014), making it hard to situate gains within current neural coherence work.
- Weakness: limited model analysis — little inspection of learned filters or ablation studies (e.g., filter width k) to explain why the model works.
- Weakness: simplistic integration of entity-specific features by fusing role and type into single symbols; more sophisticated fusion (e.g., separate embeddings/concatenation) was not explored.
