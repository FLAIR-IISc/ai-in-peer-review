- Proposes a neural entity-grid coherence model: embed grammatical-role symbols, apply 1D convolutions per entity column, max-pool salient transitions, train end-to-end with a pairwise ranking loss.  
- Replaces discrete, sparse entity-grid features with learned distributed representations to model longer-range transitions.  
- Main contributions: a novel CNN-based "neuralization" of the entity grid and state-of-the-art performance on discrimination, insertion, and summary-rating tasks.  
- Strong motivation: clearly addresses limitations of sparse entity-grid features and task-agnostic feature engineering.  
- Well-suited architecture: 1D CNN is an effective, simple, and position-invariant way to capture local entity-transition patterns.  
- Thorough evaluation: comprehensive experiments on WSJ across three tasks with direct comparisons to non-neural baselines and statistically significant gains.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-written and code is released, facilitating replication and extensions.  
- Weakness: simplistic integration of entity-specific features via atomic symbols causes combinatorial vocabulary growth and prevents parameter sharing.  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis of learned convolutional filters or explanations of what linguistic patterns the model captures.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the Li & Hovy (2014) neural coherence baseline and reasons for reported differences.  
- Q1 to authors: have you considered separate embedding channels for roles and entity-types (concatenated) to enable parameter sharing?  
- Q2 to authors: can you provide visualizations or analyses of convolutional filters to show the learned transition patterns and longer-range dependencies?  
- Q3 to authors: have you considered architectures that model inter-entity interactions (e.g., 2D convolutions over the whole grid or cross-column composition layers)?
