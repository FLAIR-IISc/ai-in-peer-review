- Proposes a neural local coherence model applying CNNs over entity grids with embedded grammatical-role symbols, trained end-to-end via pairwise ranking and evaluated on multiple tasks.  
- Contribution: Neuralized, end-to-end entity-grid capturing longer entity-transition dependencies with distributed representations and convolution to address sparsity and manual features.  
- Contribution: Strong empirical SOTA improvements across sentence discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating, with effective pretraining transfer to low-data settings.  
- Contribution: Simple mechanism to incorporate entity-specific features by composing them with role symbols, avoiding combinatorial feature explosion.  
- Strength: Embedding roles and convolving over columns enables longer transition windows (k≈5–8) without sparsity, linked to performance gains.  
- Strength: Consistent, significant absolute improvements (~4% discrimination, up to 2.5% insertion, >4% summary rating) with significance testing and multiple runs.  
- Strength: Sound training/evaluation protocol (pairwise ranking, dev tuning, early stopping, dropout, RMSprop) and use of large WSJ corpus for robustness.  
- Strength: Practical gains from only three easy-to-obtain entity features, suggesting a good accuracy–complexity trade-off.  
- Strength: Reproducibility aided by public code and clear hyperparameter/settings reporting.  
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—natural extension of entity grids with CNNs and related to prior neural sentence-level models.  
- Weakness: Limited ablation and interpretability analyses (sensitivity of k, pooling, embedding size, learned filters not systematically studied).  
- Weakness: Baseline fairness concerns—stronger non-neural grids use more features (9 vs 3); lack of controlled feature-matched comparisons and missing certain discourse-relation baselines.  
- Weakness: Task mismatch—model trained on discrimination but evaluated on insertion without insertion-trained results to assess task-specific learning.  
- Weakness: Reliance on coreference quality with no error analysis or broader domain-transfer evaluation beyond WSJ/DUC.  
- Question: How sensitive is performance to coreference errors and entity salience thresholds?  
- Question: What are the effects of transition window k, pooling length, and embedding size, and are there qualitative examples of learned filters?  
- Question: Can the model be trained directly for insertion, and does that close the insertion performance gap?  
- Question: How do results change when entity feature sets are matched between neural and non-neural grids?  
- Question: What are runtime/latency and model size implications for deployment?
