- Proposes a neural local coherence model using entity-grid embeddings, 1D convolutions over entity transitions, max-pooling, and a linear scorer trained with pairwise ranking; evaluated on WSJ (sentence ordering) and DUC’03 (summary rating) with SOTA results.
- Main contribution 1: Neuralized entity-grid with CNNs and distributed role/feature embeddings that capture longer transition patterns and reduce sparsity, yielding consistent SOTA gains.
- Main contribution 2: End-to-end pairwise ranking training that learns task-specific features and supports transfer learning (pretrain on WSJ, finetune on DUC’03).
- Main contribution 3: Empirical finding that the neural grid outperforms the extended grid while using fewer handcrafted features, suggesting better generalization.
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated architecture—embeddings + convolution address sparsity and capture longer k≈5–8 transitions; max-pooling gives location invariance.
- Strength: Strong, statistically significant empirical gains (e.g., ≈4% discrimination and ≈1% insertion over Grid-all-nouns on WSJ; similar gains for extended grid variants; pretraining helps DUC’03).
- Strength: Sensible training objective (pairwise ranking) and effective transfer learning demonstrated on small DUC’03 data.
- Strength: Good reproducibility and methodology—implementation details, dev set, early stopping, dropout, multiple runs, statistical tests, and code release.
- Strength: Fair baseline coverage within the entity-grid family, reimplementing strong non-neural references and surpassing them.
- Weakness: Limited ablation/analysis—no systematic study of transition window k, pooling length, number of filters, or per-feature contributions; lacks qualitative filter/error analysis.
- Weakness: Coreference and syntactic role dependencies underexplored—tools/quality unspecified and sensitivity to coref/parser errors not tested.
- Weakness: Independence assumption across entity columns and location-invariant pooling may ignore cross-entity interactions and positional cues; no experiments to test this.
- Weakness: Training/evaluation mismatch for insertion—trained on discrimination pairs but evaluated on insertion without direct training for insertion tasks.
- Weakness: Dataset and baseline gaps—choice of WSJ limits comparability to some prior datasets, omission of some baselines or fair adaptations (e.g., non-neural extended grid on DUC, Li & Hovy reimplementation issues).
- Questions for authors: Which coreference resolver and role extractor were used and how sensitive are results to them?
- Questions for authors: How does performance vary with transition window k and pooling length, and is longer k responsible for gains?
- Questions for authors: Have cross-entity interactions been tried (2D convs or attention) or preliminary results?
- Questions for authors: Can the model be trained directly for insertion and can sentence position features be included?
- Questions for authors: For DUC’03, can you report variance/confidence intervals, significance, and a comparable non-neural extended-grid baseline?
- Overall assessment: Well-motivated and effective neural approach with consistent significant improvements; acceptance recommended but would be stronger with more ablations and robustness analyses.
