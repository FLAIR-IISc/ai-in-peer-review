- Paper proposes a neural local coherence model: CNNs over entity grids with learned embeddings and pairwise ranking, evaluated on WSJ (sentence ordering) and DUC’03 (summary coherence), achieving state-of-the-art results.

Contributions:
- Neuralized entity-grid architecture (embeddings + convolution + pooling) that models longer entity-transition patterns and is trained with pairwise ranking.
- Practical method to incorporate entity-specific features by composing role-symbols with feature values, avoiding combinatorial explosion.
- Thorough experimental setup with significance testing and cross-domain pretraining/fine-tuning (WSJ→DUC’03), demonstrating robustness.

Strengths:
- Clear empirical gains over strong grid baselines (Grid-CNN ≈ +4% discr., +1% ins.; Extended Grid-CNN ≈ +3.7% discr., +2.5% ins.) with significance testing.
- Addresses sparsity/transition-length limits of traditional grids by learning role+feature embeddings and using convolutions (best window sizes 5–6).
- End-to-end, task-aligned pairwise ranking training with reproducibility practices (early stopping, dropout, averaging over seeds).
- Sensible feature design and ablation showing incremental gains from simple entity features while outperforming non-neural extended grids.
- Cross-task robustness: WSJ pretraining plus DUC’03 fine-tuning yields additional gains, showing reusability on low-data tasks.

Weaknesses:
- Limited comparison to other neural coherence models (e.g., Li & Hovy 2014) and insufficient hyperparameter tuning or reporting for those baselines.
- Unclear whether preprocessing (coreference, NP detection, parsing) was identical for proposed model and baselines, raising fairness concerns.
- Model assumes independence across entity columns and locality within columns, missing cross-entity interactions and some global document structure signals.
- Lack of qualitative/error analysis and finer ablations (e.g., examples of learned filters, sensitivity to document length or coref errors, isolated effect of k and pooling).
- Notational and technical clarity issues (ambiguous dimensions/padding in equations, possible Table 3 errors showing identical discrimination/insertion numbers).

Questions for authors:
- Which coreference resolver, parser, and NP extraction settings were used for your model and the baselines, and were they identical?
- How did you reproduce Li & Hovy (2014): what hyperparameters/training data; did you try larger windows, pre-trained embeddings, or curriculum strategies?
- Do convolutional filters slide only along the sentence axis within a single entity column or also across entities; please clarify dimensionality and provide a column schematic.
- Is Table 3’s identical discrimination and insertion numbers a typesetting error; please provide correct insertion accuracies and metric definitions.
- How sensitive is the model to coreference noise, number of entities, and document length (gold vs. automatic coref)?

Additional comments:
- Recommend ablations for filter width, pooling size, each entity feature, and training the pairwise loss specifically on insertion (near-miss examples).
- Minor fixes: correct typos (e.g., “losing” vs. “loosing”) and clarify the DUC baseline training setup (why extended grid wasn’t trained pairwise).
