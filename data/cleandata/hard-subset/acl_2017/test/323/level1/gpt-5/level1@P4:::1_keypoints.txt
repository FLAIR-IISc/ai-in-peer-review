- Proposes a neural local coherence model over the entity-grid that replaces discrete transition counts with learned embeddings and applies a CNN over entity-transition columns, trained with pairwise ranking and able to include tokenized role-feature entity info.  
- Evaluated on WSJ sentence ordering (discrimination and insertion) and DUC’03 summary coherence rating, with pretraining on WSJ improving summary rating.

Main contributions:
- Empirical: strong, statistically significant gains on three coherence tasks using a simple neural architecture and limited feature engineering.  
- Methodological: introduces a CNN over entity-grid columns with learned role/feature embeddings and pairwise ranking to model longer transitions end-to-end.  
- Practical: a simple tokenization trick to incorporate entity-specific features without combinatorial feature explosion.

Strengths:
- Shows consistent, significant empirical improvements over Extended Grid and a graph-based method on discrimination, insertion, and summary rating.  
- Uses a task-aligned pairwise ranking objective and end-to-end training to learn useful transition filters.  
- Mitigates sparsity and models longer-range transitions via distributed embeddings and convolutional receptive fields.  
- Achieves superior results with only three simple entity features, reducing reliance on heavy feature engineering.  
- Good reproducibility practices: hyperparameters, optimizer, multi-seed averages, significance testing, and promised code release.

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: essentially a straightforward neuralization of prior entity-grid ideas using standard components.  
- Baseline coverage/fairness: omits key baselines (e.g., Li & Hovy 2014, Feng & Hirst 2012, RST-based models), weakening SOTA claims.  
- Reporting inconsistencies: Table 3 shows identical discrimination and insertion numbers and a confusing “F1” column; metrics/evaluation details unclear.  
- Insufficient detail on entity extraction/coreference pipeline and sensitivity to extractor errors.  
- Modeling assumptions/analysis gaps: treats columns independently, lacks ablations (window size k, pooling, filters), limited qualitative analysis of learned patterns, and no task-specific training experiments for insertion.

Questions to authors:
- Clarify Table 3: exact evaluation protocols for discrimination vs. insertion, definition of “F1”, and provide corrected/clear results.  
- Specify the entity detection and coreference pipeline (what counts as an entity, tool/settings, handling of mentions) and sensitivity analysis.  
- Compare to stronger discourse/coherence baselines or report comparable published numbers and discuss differences.  
- Explain why Li & Hovy (2014) could not be reproduced and report any remediation or ablation attempts.  
- Provide ablations for transition window size k, pooling choices, number of filters, and each entity-specific feature, plus visualization/examples of learned filters.  
- Report whether training with insertion-style negatives or harder permutations improves insertion/discrimination performance.

Overall evaluation:
- A clear, technically sound, and useful neural extension of the entity-grid with strong empirical results; novelty is moderate and some experimental/reporting gaps should be addressed.
