- Neural entity-grid model: adapts the entity grid to a neural architecture using distributed embeddings and CNNs to capture longer-range entity transitions.
- End-to-end trainability: the model learns task-specific features directly, removing the need for hand-engineered features.
- State-of-the-art performance: shows consistent improvements on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.
- Clear motivation: paper well articulates limitations of prior entity-grid models and the need for a neural approach.
- Solid experimental evaluation: tested on three coherence tasks with established datasets and strong baselines.
- Well-explained architecture: clear descriptions and helpful diagram of convolutional and pooling components.
- Code release: authors provide code, aiding reproducibility and follow-up research.
- Significant performance gains: reported improvements (~4% discrimination, up to 2.5% insertion, >4% summary rating) are meaningful.
- Limited novelty of components: CNNs and embeddings are not novel individually; paper lacks deep comparison to alternatives like RNNs.
- Simplistic entity-specific features: concatenation of type and grammatical role may be insufficient; more advanced integration (e.g., attention) is suggested.
- Hyperparameter tuning details lacking: insufficient description of search space and robustness to hyperparameter changes.
- Limited error analysis: paper focuses on aggregate metrics without qualitative examination of failure cases.
- Question: Why choose CNNs over RNNs for modeling entity transitions, and were RNNs evaluated?
- Question: Provide details on hyperparameter tuning (search space and rationale).
- Question: Share example failure cases and analysis of why the model fails on them.
- Question: Which layers were fine-tuned after WSJ pretraining and how did partial vs. full fine-tuning affect performance?
- Additional comment: expand discussion of recent neural discourse models (e.g., transformers).
- Additional comment: clarify notation in Equation 5 regarding shared parameters φ(Gi) and φ(Gj) with θ.
