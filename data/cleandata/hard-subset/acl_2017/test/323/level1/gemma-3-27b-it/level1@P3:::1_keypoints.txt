- Proposes a neural local coherence model using CNNs over entity grids with distributed representations and end-to-end training.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks with statistically significant gains.  
- Main contribution: successful application of CNNs to the entity grid framework and released code for reproducibility.  

- Strength: strong empirical results across three tasks (up to ~4% gains) with statistical significance testing.  
- Strength: CNN architecture is well-motivated for capturing long-range entity transitions and is modular (lookup, conv, pooling, scoring).  
- Strength: end-to-end pairwise ranking training reduces reliance on hand-engineered features and learns task-specific representations.  
- Strength: clear presentation, helpful diagrams, and publicly released code enabling reproducibility.  

- Weakness: limited novelty in applying CNNs—architectural choice isn’t groundbreaking and needs comparison to other neural sequence models (LSTMs/Transformers).  
- Weakness: incorporation of entity-specific features is rudimentary (only a few features used); broader or more sophisticated feature integration is lacking.  
- Weakness: evaluation datasets are relatively small (particularly for summary coherence), raising generalizability concerns; more robust statistics or confidence intervals needed.  
- Weakness: missing comprehensive ablation studies to quantify the contribution of components (convolution, pooling, embedding size, etc.).  

- Question: were other architectures (LSTMs, Transformers) considered, and why choose CNNs over them?  
- Question: why only three entity-specific features in the Extended Grid-CNN and what prevented using a larger set?  
- Question: what measures ensured quality of coreference resolution for entity grids, and how do coreference errors impact performance?  
- Question: what is the computational cost of training and inference compared to baseline entity grid models?  

- Additional: references are comprehensive.  
- Additional: related work discussion is generally thorough but could focus more on recent neural coherence modeling advances.  
- Additional: clarify hyperparameter search strategy (e.g., grid vs random search) and other tuning details.
