- Proposes a neural local coherence model using CNNs over entity grids to learn distributed role representations and capture long-range entity transitions end-to-end.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on three coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary coherence rating).  
- Primary contribution: successful neural architecture for local coherence that improves upon entity grid baselines.  
- Secondary contribution: thorough evaluation across multiple datasets/tasks supporting effectiveness.  

- Strength: Strong empirical results—consistent improvements (2.5–4%+) over baselines on all tasks.  
- Strength: Effective architecture—CNNs over entity grids capture longer-range dependencies and salient features via pooling.  
- Strength: End-to-end training lets the model learn task-specific features without hand-engineering.  
- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation using standard datasets (WSJ, DUC’03) enhances comparability and reproducibility.  
- Strength: Code release facilitates replication and further research.  

- Weakness: Limited novelty—components (entity grids, CNNs) are not individually novel; novelty is mainly in their application.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning process is under-described; selection criteria beyond DEV accuracy are unclear.  
- Weakness: Limited analysis of learned features—no visualization/interpretation of filters or activations.  
- Weakness: Small dataset for summary coherence rating reduces statistical power of reported gains.  
- Weakness: Related work misses or inadequately discusses recent neural coherence models beyond Li and Hovy (2014).  

- Q: Please detail the hyperparameter tuning strategy (search method, ranges, stopping criteria).  
- Q: Have you visualized learned convolutional filters or activations; can you share insights from such analyses?  
- Q: Did you try incorporating other (non-entity-specific) features into the model?  
- Q: How does model performance scale with document length and does it struggle with very long texts?  
- Q: Why were recent neural coherence models omitted from comparisons and how do they relate to your approach?  

- Additional: Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Additional: Figures and tables effectively present results.  
- Additional: Related work should be expanded to include more recent neural advances in coherence modeling.  
- Additional: Recommend an ablation study to quantify the contribution of entity-specific features.
