- Neural Entity Grid Representation: Applies CNNs to entity grids to learn distributed representations of entity transitions and capture longer-range dependencies.  
- End-to-End Training: Trains the model end-to-end with a pairwise ranking loss, enabling automatic, task-specific feature learning.  
- Strong Empirical Results: Reports state-of-the-art improvements on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  

- Clear Problem Statement and Motivation: Effectively motivates moving beyond discrete, task-agnostic entity grid features to a neural approach.  
- Well-Designed Architecture: Uses convolution and max-pooling appropriately to capture local patterns and long-range dependencies in the grid.  
- Thorough Evaluation: Evaluates across three established coherence tasks and benchmarks for robust comparison.  
- Ablation Study & Feature Incorporation: Includes entity-specific features and ablation analysis showing their positive impact.  
- Code Release: Provides released code, aiding reproducibility and follow-up work.  

- Limited Novelty of CNN Application: Applying CNNs to entity grids is sensible but not a fundamentally novel neural technique.  
- Hyperparameter Sensitivity & Details: Hyperparameter tuning is reported cursorily; more rationale and sensitivity analysis are needed.  
- Limited Analysis of Error Cases: Lacks detailed error analysis showing where and why the model fails.  
- Dataset Limitations: Reliance on WSJ may limit generalizability; broader datasets are needed.  
- Comparison to Recent Neural Models: Related work lacks comparison to more recent neural/discourse and transformer-based coherence models.  

- Hyperparameter Rationale and Sensitivity: Please detail rationale for chosen hyperparameters and their effect on performance.  
- Error Case Examples and Analysis: Provide concrete failure cases and analyses explaining the errors.  
- Other Architectures Tried: Report experiments (if any) with RNNs, Transformers, or other architectures and their outcomes.  
- Cross-Domain Performance: Evaluate performance across genres/domains beyond the WSJ corpus.  
- Comparison to Transformer LMs: Compare against transformer-based language models fine-tuned for coherence tasks.  

- Figure Clarity: Improve the small figure for better readability.  
- Model Size: Report the number of model parameters for comparison.  

- Recommendation: Solid paper with strong results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
