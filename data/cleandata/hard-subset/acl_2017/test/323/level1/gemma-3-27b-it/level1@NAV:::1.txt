## Review of "A Neural Local Coherence Model"

This paper proposes a neural network architecture for assessing text coherence, building upon the entity grid representation. The authors address limitations of traditional entity grid models – namely, their reliance on discrete representations and task-decoupled feature computation – by employing convolutional neural networks to capture long-range entity transitions and learning task-specific features end-to-end. They evaluate the model on three coherence assessment tasks (discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating), demonstrating state-of-the-art results.

The primary contribution of this work is the successful application of a convolutional neural network to the entity grid framework for local coherence modeling. While neural networks have been applied to coherence previously (as acknowledged in the related work), this work specifically leverages CNNs to address the limitations of discrete representations in the traditional entity grid, allowing for potentially richer and more generalizable feature learning. A second, notable contribution lies in the end-to-end training methodology, allowing the model to learn task-specific features directly from the data, rather than relying on handcrafted features. Finally, the comprehensive empirical evaluation across three distinct tasks provides strong evidence of the model's effectiveness and generalizability.

**Strengths:**

1. **Strong Empirical Results:** The model consistently outperforms existing baselines across all three evaluation tasks, achieving statistically significant improvements on several metrics. This provides compelling evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
2. **Addressing Limitations of Existing Work:** The paper clearly articulates the weaknesses of traditional entity grid models and provides a plausible solution by incorporating distributed representations and end-to-end learning.
3. **Clear and Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized, making it easy to understand the proposed model and the experimental setup.
4. **Availability of Code:** Releasing the code fosters reproducibility and encourages further research in this area.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Incremental Novelty:** While the combination of CNNs and entity grids is effective, the core idea of applying neural networks to coherence modeling isn't entirely novel. The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of how this work differentiates itself from prior neural approaches.
2. **Limited Feature Exploration:** The extended grid model utilizes only a limited subset of available entity-specific features. Exploring a wider range of features could potentially yield even stronger performance.
3. **Task-Specific Tuning:** The model's performance on the summary coherence rating task is somewhat limited, suggesting the need for task-specific fine-tuning or architectural adjustments.



**Questions to Authors:**

1.  Could you elaborate on the computational cost associated with using longer transition lengths (k) in the convolutional layers?  Does this become a limiting factor for very long documents?
2.  You mention that the training set for summary coherence rating is limited. Did you explore any data augmentation techniques to address this issue?
3.  How sensitive is the model to the choice of pre-trained embeddings (if any are used) and the coreference resolution system employed for generating the entity grid?
