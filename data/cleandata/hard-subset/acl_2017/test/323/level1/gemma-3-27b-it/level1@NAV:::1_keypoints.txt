- Proposes a CNN-based neural architecture on the entity grid to model local text coherence, enabling distributed representations and end-to-end learning.
- Addresses traditional entity grid limitations (discrete representations, task-decoupled features) by learning task-specific features via CNNs.
- Evaluated on three tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary coherence rating) and reports state-of-the-art results.
- Primary contributions: applying CNNs to the entity grid, end-to-end training, and comprehensive empirical evaluation across tasks.
- Strength — Strong empirical results with consistent, often statistically significant improvements over baselines.
- Strength — Effectively addresses prior model weaknesses through distributed representations and end-to-end feature learning.
- Strength — Paper is clearly written and well-structured, facilitating understanding of model and experiments.
- Strength — Code release supports reproducibility and further research.
- Weakness — Incremental novelty: neural approaches to coherence exist; clearer differentiation from prior neural work is needed.
- Weakness — Limited exploration of entity-specific features in the extended grid; broader feature sets might improve performance.
- Weakness — Performance on summary coherence rating is limited, indicating need for task-specific tuning or architecture changes.
- Question — What is the computational cost of using longer transition lengths (k) in the convolutional layers, and does it limit applicability to long documents?
- Question — Were data augmentation techniques tried to mitigate the limited training data for summary coherence rating?
- Question — How sensitive is performance to choice of pre-trained embeddings and the coreference resolution system used to build the entity grid?
