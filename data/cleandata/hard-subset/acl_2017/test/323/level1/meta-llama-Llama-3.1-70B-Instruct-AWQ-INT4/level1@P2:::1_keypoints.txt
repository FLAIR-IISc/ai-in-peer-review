- Proposes a convolutional neural local coherence model operating on entity grids to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features.
- Introduces a pairwise ranking training method for end-to-end learning of task-specific high-level features.
- Shows state-of-the-art performance on three coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models.
- Effectively leverages distributed representations to improve generalization and robustness in modeling entity transitions.
- End-to-end pairwise ranking enables automatic learning of task-specific high-level features.
- Empirical results demonstrate strong effectiveness across multiple coherence tasks.
- Authors release code, facilitating reproducibility and follow-up work.
- Paper lacks detailed hyperparameter analysis and its impact on performance.
- Comparison set is limited; more comparisons to other neural models would strengthen claims.
- Paper provides insufficient error analysis to illuminate model failure modes.
- Requests more detailed hyperparameter studies from the authors.
- Asks for evaluation on additional coherence-related tasks (e.g., readability assessment).
- Requests a more thorough error analysis to better understand strengths and weaknesses.
- Paper is well-written and clear, but would benefit from deeper hyperparameter and error analyses.
