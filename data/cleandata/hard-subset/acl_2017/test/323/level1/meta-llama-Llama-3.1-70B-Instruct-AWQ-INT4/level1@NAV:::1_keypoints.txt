- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model over the entity grid, trained with pairwise ranking, that captures long-range entity transitions and achieves SOTA on three tasks.

- Contribution: Neural local coherence architecture that models long-range entity transitions and arbitrary entity-specific features without losing generalization.

- Contribution: Pairwise ranking training method enabling end-to-end, task-specific high-level feature learning.

- Contribution: Demonstrated state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models on three coherence assessment tasks.

- Strength: Effective use of distributed representations to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features, improving generalization.

- Strength: End-to-end pairwise ranking training allows the model to learn task-specific high-level features.

- Strength: Empirical SOTA performance across three coherence assessment tasks validates the approach.

- Weakness: Limited evaluation scope—only three coherence tasks—may not fully demonstrate general effectiveness.

- Weakness: Lack of theoretical analysis explaining why the model works.

- Weakness: Dependence on multiple hyperparameters (embedding size, filter count, window size) that likely need careful tuning.

- Question: How were hyperparameters selected and are there guidelines for choosing them?

- Question: Can the authors provide theoretical analysis of why the approach works for coherence assessment?

- Question: How does the model perform on other coherence tasks, e.g., readability assessment and essay scoring?
