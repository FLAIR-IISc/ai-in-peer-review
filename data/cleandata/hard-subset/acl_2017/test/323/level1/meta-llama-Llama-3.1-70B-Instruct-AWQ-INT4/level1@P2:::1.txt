**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model that uses a convolutional neural network to operate over the entity grid representation of a text. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization. The authors also present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neural Local Coherence Model**: The paper proposes a neural architecture that can capture long-range entity transitions along with arbitrary entity-specific features without losing generalization.
2. **Pairwise Ranking Method**: The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The model achieves state-of-the-art results on three different coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Use of Distributed Representation**: The paper effectively uses distributed representation to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features, which leads to better generalization and robustness.
2. **End-to-End Training**: The pairwise ranking method allows for end-to-end training, which enables the model to learn task-specific high-level features automatically.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The model achieves state-of-the-art results on three different coherence assessment tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
4. **Code Release**: The authors release their code with the submission, making it easier for others to reproduce and build upon their work.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Analysis of Hyperparameters**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the hyperparameters and their impact on the model's performance.
2. **Limited Comparison with Other Neural Models**: The paper only compares the proposed model with a few existing models, and it would be beneficial to compare it with other neural models as well.
3. **Limited Discussion of Error Analysis**: The paper does not provide a detailed error analysis, which would help to understand the model's strengths and weaknesses better.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide a more detailed analysis of the hyperparameters and their impact on the model's performance?
2. How does the model perform on other coherence assessment tasks, such as readability assessment?
3. Can you provide a more detailed error analysis to understand the model's strengths and weaknesses better?

**Additional Comments**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed model and its components. The experimental results are impressive, and the code release is a significant contribution to the research community. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the hyperparameters and error analysis to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the model's strengths and weaknesses.
