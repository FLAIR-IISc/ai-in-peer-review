- Proposes a neural local coherence model using a CNN over the entity grid, claiming state-of-the-art on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  
- Contribution: introduces a neural local coherence architecture that captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features while maintaining generalization.  
- Contribution: uses convolutional feature learning to compose local entity-transition patches into higher-level representations to capture longer dependencies without overfitting.  
- Contribution: employs an end-to-end pairwise ranking training approach to automatically learn task-specific features.  
- Strength: demonstrates improved performance, reportedly outperforming prior models on three coherence assessment tasks.  
- Strength: convolutional design enables capturing long-range dependencies better than traditional grid models.  
- Strength: model is flexible and can generalize across target tasks while incorporating entity-specific features.  
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to only three coherence tasks, which may not fully demonstrate the model’s capabilities.  
- Weakness: model performance on summary coherence rating depends on pre-training, potentially limiting applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other neural models with similar architectures or objectives.  
- Question: how will the model be extended to other discourse forms, such as asynchronous conversations?  
- Question: request for more details on the pre-training process and its impact on performance across tasks.  
- Question: how do the authors plan to mitigate overfitting, especially on smaller datasets?
