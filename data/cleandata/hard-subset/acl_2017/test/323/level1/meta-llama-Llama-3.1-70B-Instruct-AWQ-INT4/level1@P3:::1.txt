**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization, thanks to the power of distributed representation. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neural Local Coherence Model**: The authors propose a novel neural architecture for modeling local coherence, which captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization.
2. **Pairwise Ranking Method**: The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective Use of Distributed Representation**: The authors effectively use distributed representation to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization.
2. **End-to-End Training**: The pairwise ranking method allows for end-to-end training, which enables the model to learn task-specific high-level features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.
4. **Well-Designed Experiments**: The authors conduct well-designed experiments, including a comparison with existing models and an analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters.
5. **Clear and Concise Writing**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach and results.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited Analysis of Model Components**: While the authors provide a good explanation of their approach, they do not provide a detailed analysis of the individual components of their model, such as the convolutional layer and the pooling layer.
2. **Lack of Comparison with Other Neural Models**: The authors only compare their model with traditional coherence models and do not provide a comparison with other neural models that have been proposed for coherence assessment.
3. **Limited Discussion of Future Work**: While the authors mention some potential future work, they do not provide a detailed discussion of the potential applications and limitations of their approach.
4. **No Analysis of Computational Complexity**: The authors do not provide an analysis of the computational complexity of their model, which could be an important consideration for practical applications.
5. **No Discussion of Potential Biases**: The authors do not discuss potential biases in their model, such as biases in the training data or biases in the evaluation metrics.
