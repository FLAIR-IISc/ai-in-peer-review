- Proposes a neural local coherence model using a CNN over the entity grid to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features.  
- Trains the model end-to-end with a pairwise ranking method to learn task-specific high-level features.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on three coherence assessment tasks, outperforming prior models.  
- Novel architecture claims to capture long-range transitions without losing generalization.  
- Pairwise ranking enables end-to-end task adaptation.  
- Strong empirical results across multiple evaluation tasks/datasets.  
- Effective use of distributed representations to model entity transitions and features.  
- Experiments are well-designed, include comparisons with existing models and hyperparameter analyses.  
- Paper is clearly and concisely written.  
- Does not provide detailed analysis of individual model components (e.g., convolutional and pooling layers).  
- Lacks comparison with other neural coherence models, relying mainly on traditional baselines.  
- Limited discussion of future work, potential applications, and limitations.
