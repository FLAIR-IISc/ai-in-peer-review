- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based neural local coherence model over the entity grid claiming longer-range transitions, end-to-end pairwise ranking training, and state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.

- Contribution 1: Neural architecture using distributed representations to model long-range entity transitions (up to length 8), avoiding discrete representation dimensionality issues.

- Contribution 2: End-to-end pairwise ranking training that learns task-specific high-level features automatically.

- Contribution 3: Empirical validation showing consistent improvements over non-neural entity grid baselines across three coherence tasks.

- Strength 1: Clear problem identification and strong motivation articulating limitations of discrete entity-grid models (curse of dimensionality and task-agnostic features).

- Strength 2: Strong empirical results with well-justified evaluation, notable absolute gains on discrimination (~4%), insertion (~2.5%), and summary coherence (~4%+), plus ablation studies.

- Strength 3: Effective model design that leverages the entity grid and uses convolution and distributed role representations for location-invariant, longer transition modeling.

- Weakness 1: Insufficient theoretical justification for why the neural model outperforms non-neural approaches beyond empirical results.

- Weakness 2: Lack of detailed comparison with prior neural coherence work (Li and Hovy 2014) and no explanation for their reported poor performance on this dataset.

- Weakness 3: Underdeveloped discussion of limitations (performance on very short texts, complex discourse structures, and smaller gains on insertion).

- Question 1: Why did Li and Hovy (2014) perform poorly (19.34% discrimination) on your dataset compared to their reported results elsewhere?

- Question 2: Please clarify theoretically why the neural approach can capture long-range entity transitions while existing grid models cannot.

- Question 3: How were the three Extended Grid features chosen, and would including all nine features from the Extended Grid improve performance?

- Additional comment: Paper is well-written, code is released, and empirical claims of state-of-the-art are supported, but theoretical grounding and comparison to prior neural models need strengthening.
