- Proposal: neural local coherence model extending the entity grid with CNNs to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features via distributed representations and end-to-end training.  
- Contribution 1: CNN architecture modeling long-range entity transitions (up to length 8) while preserving generalization.  
- Contribution 2: Pairwise ranking training method enabling end-to-end learning of task-specific features.  
- Contribution 3: State-of-the-art performance on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating with ~4–5% absolute gains over non-neural baselines.  
- Strength: Compelling motivation for neuralizing the entity grid and clear, statistically significant improvements over strong baselines.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across multiple coherence tasks and well-described implementation details.  
- Strength: Pairwise ranking is well-suited to the ranking nature of coherence assessment.  
- Weakness: No detailed justification for choosing CNNs over RNNs or Transformers that may better model sequential dependencies.  
- Weakness: Modest absolute improvements (4–5%), raising questions about practical impact.  
- Weakness: Minimal analysis of what the model learns, leaving unclear whether linguistic patterns or task-specific memorization are captured.  
- Question: How does the model compare to sequence-based neural models (e.g., LSTM or Transformer) that process text directly rather than entity grids?  
- Question: Can you provide examples of entity transitions the model identifies as particularly important for coherence?
