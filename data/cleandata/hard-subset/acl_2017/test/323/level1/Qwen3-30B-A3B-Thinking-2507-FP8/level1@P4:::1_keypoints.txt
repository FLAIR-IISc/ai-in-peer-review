- Neural CNN over the entity grid to model long-range entity transitions and mitigate the curse of dimensionality.  
- End-to-end pairwise ranking training to automatically learn task-specific coherence features.  
- Mechanism to incorporate entity-specific features into the neural coherence model.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating with statistically significant gains.  
- Clearly identifies and addresses key limitations of traditional entity grid models via distributed representations and end-to-end learning.  
- Comprehensive evaluation using three coherence tasks, appropriate baselines, and a larger WSJ dataset.  
- Limited novelty: neuralizing the entity grid with CNNs is incremental and lacks justification versus RNNs/transformers.  
- Insufficient analysis of what the model actually learns and why it outperforms traditional features.  
- Poor treatment of prior neural work: no deep analysis of why Li and Hovy (2014) performed badly while this model succeeds.  
- Question: Why does this approach outperform Li and Hovy (2014); which architectural or training choices drive the improvement?  
- Question: Can you analyze the convolutional filters—what specific entity-transition patterns are most predictive?  
- Question: Would training specifically on insertion data (instead of discrimination) improve performance on the insertion task?
