- Proposes enhancing the entity grid model with a convolutional neural network to learn entity transition sequences, improving generalization over longer sequences versus original transition probabilities.
- Well-executed and clearly written; opts for a hybrid approach that augments a specific component with neural networks rather than a fully neural solution.
- Lacks sufficient comparison to the Li and Hovy neural coherence model; authors report surprisingly low performance for that model on their task.
- Raises concerns whether Li and Hovy’s low performance is due to experimental setup errors, corpus-dependence, or dataset-specific issues, and how the proposed model would perform where Li and Hovy excels.
- Recommends a more thorough exploration and discussion of these comparison issues to strengthen the paper’s claims.
- Multiple instances of missing definite articles and awkward phrasing (e.g., “limits the model to do X”); recommends careful proofreading and rewording for clarity.
- Final recommendation updated to 4; acknowledges third-party software difficulties shouldn’t penalize authors but asks that these issues be documented and that authors attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results as a sanity check.
