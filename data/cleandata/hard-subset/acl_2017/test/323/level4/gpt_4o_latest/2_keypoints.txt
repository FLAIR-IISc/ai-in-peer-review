- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence that augments the established entity grid representation.  
- Method is well-justified and clearly explained, with a strong "Why it works" rationale.  
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple tasks shows the model outperforms prior approaches.  
- Unable to replicate one prior result (Li & Hovy 2014), suggesting a possible implementation or modeling error that needs investigation.  
- The model is largely a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid; novelty beyond that is limited.  
- Unclear rationale for using 100-dimensional vectors to encode four-dimensional grid entries; benefit of high dimensionality not demonstrated.  
- Direct optimization for coherence ranking is suggested; difference between k=3 and k=6 transition chains may be negligible for short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is repetitive and could be more concise; entity grid introduction and related work are redundantly presented.  
- Recommend consolidating section 6 into section 2 (rename to "Related Work") and removing redundant material.  
- Expected more substantial contributions (e.g., novel feature representations or tasks) given emphasis on prior work.  
- Minor editorial issues: missing articles, incorrect figure reference (Figure 2 → Figure 1), phrasing suggestions, and removal of a standard concluding paragraph.
