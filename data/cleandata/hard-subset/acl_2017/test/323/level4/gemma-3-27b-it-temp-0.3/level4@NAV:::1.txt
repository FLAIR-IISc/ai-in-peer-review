The paper presents an extension to the entity grid model, leveraging a convolutional neural network to learn entity transition sequences and improve coherence modeling. This allows for better generalization to longer entity sequences compared to the original model’s direct transition probability estimates. This is a well-written and valuable contribution. The authors wisely build upon existing work, employing a neural network to specifically address limitations within one component, rather than pursuing a completely neural approach. However, a more comprehensive comparison to the neural coherence model proposed by Li and Hovy would be beneficial. The surprisingly low performance observed for the Li and Hovy model raises questions regarding potential errors in the experimental setup or corpus-dependence. Investigating whether the proposed model’s performance varies on corpora where the Li and Hovy model performs better would significantly strengthen the paper’s claims. 

The writing is generally clear and fluent, but the manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading, particularly regarding the consistent omission of definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely elsewhere). Additionally, the phrasing "...limits the model to do X..." appears repeatedly and could be improved for clarity; alternatives such as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” might be more natural.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 following review of the author response. I acknowledge that difficulties in replicating external software should not negatively impact the evaluation of this work. The authors’ attempts to evaluate the Li and Hovy system, and the challenges encountered, should be documented within the paper. Furthermore, I recommend the authors attempt to reproduce the Li and Hovy results on their original datasets as a validation step, if not already performed.
