This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) approach for modeling text coherence. This model is based on the widely known entity grid representation for coherence, but with a CNN superimposed on top. The method is provided with sufficient reasons and descriptions, especially the clear discussion of the concepts behind some design decisions (such as the "why CNN" and "why it works" sections), which I particularly appreciate.

A comprehensive evaluation has been conducted on several works, which shows that the proposed method has surpassed previous methods. However, it is strange that a previous result could not be reproduced: the results of Li/Hovy (2014) indicate an implementation or modeling error that should be resolved.

Still, the model is a relatively simple 'neuralization' of the entity grid model. I don't understand why a 100-dimensional vector is needed to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or a few more in the case of an extended grid). How does this help? I understand that learning a good model optimized for direct coherence ranking will help, but the difference between k=3 and k=6 transition chains may not make a big difference, especially since many WSJ articles can be very short.

The text seems a bit long, the paper repeats some parts in several places, for example, the introduction to the entity grid. In particular, the work related to section 2 is also presented, so the first 2/3 of section 6 should be repeated and deleted (or added to section 2 if necessary). The rest of section 6 should probably be added under a sub-paragraph under section 2 (then the name of section 2 should be changed and the related work should be done).

Overall, it seems like a difficult implementation of applying a neural network model for entity-grid-based coherence. However, considering the proposed integration of previous work, I would expect more from a complete paper, such as innovations in presentation (other features?) or work.

A few minor points:
- This paper could benefit from proof-reading by a native speaker: the article is missing in many places, such as 'the WSJ corpus' (2x), 'the Brown ... toolkit' (2x), etc.
- p.1 bottom left column: 'Figure 2' -> 'Figure 1'
- p.1 Firstly/Secondly -> First, Second
- p.1 'limits the model to' -> 'prevents the model from considering ...' ?
- Consider removing the 'standard' last paragraph of section 1, since it is not necessary to follow such a short paper.
