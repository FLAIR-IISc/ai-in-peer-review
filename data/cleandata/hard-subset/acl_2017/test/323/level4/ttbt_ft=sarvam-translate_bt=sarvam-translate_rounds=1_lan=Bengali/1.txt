This paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model. It uses a convolutional neural network to learn the sequence of entity transformations that indicate coherence. This allows for better generalization on longer sequences of entities than direct estimation of the transition probabilities of the main model.

It is a well-written and concise paper. Instead of proposing a completely neural approach, the authors solve specific problems in one step using a neural network based on existing work. This is a valid approach, but it would be more useful to compare it with the existing neural coherence models of Li and Hovy. The authors acknowledge that they were surprised by the low scores of the Li and Hovy models in their work. This makes the reader wonder if there was any error in the experimental setup, whether the low performance of other models is corpus-dependent, and if so, what results the proposed model in this paper would achieve in a corpus or task where the other model is more successful. A deeper investigation of these issues would provide sufficient justification for the argument.

In general, the paper is very fluent and readable, but the definite article is missing in many places (such as lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and possibly more). I would advise proofreading the paper with the use of articles in mind. The expression "...model-কে X করতে সীমাবদ্ধ করে..." (model-ke X karne nukarate), which is used repeatedly, sounds a bit unusual. Perhaps "model-er X karne ke kapabilityke nukarate" (model-er X karne ke kapabilityke nukarate) or "model-ke X karne se abharte রাখে" (model-ke X karne se abharte রাখে) would be more clear.

After considering the author's response, the final recommendation has been upgraded to 4. I agree that objective difficulties in running other people's software should not be used against the current authors. The effort made to test the Li and Hovy system and the problems encountered in doing so should be documented in the paper. I would also suggest that the authors try to reproduce the Li and Hovy results in their original data set as a sanity check (if they have not already done so), just to see if it works for them.
