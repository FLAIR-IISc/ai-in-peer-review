This paper introduces an expansion of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate consistency, allowing for better generalization on longer sequences of entities compared to direct estimation of transition probabilities in the original model.

This is a good and well-written paper. Instead of proposing a completely neural approach, the authors build on existing work and use only a neural network to address specific issues in a single step. This is a valid approach, but it would be useful to extend the comparison with Lee and Howe's existing neural consistency model. The authors acknowledge that they are surprised by the very low scores obtained by the Lee and Howe model on their work. This makes the reader wonder if there was an error in the experimental setup, if the lower performance of other models is corpus-dependent, and if so, what results the proposed model in this paper would achieve on the corpus or task where other models are more successful. A thorough examination of these factors would make the argument much stronger.

In general, the paper is very fluent and readable, but some articles are missing in several places (for example, lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and perhaps more). I would suggest proofreading the paper, especially considering the use of articles. "...model X is limited by...", which is used repeatedly, seems a bit unusual. Perhaps "model X is limited by the ability to do..." or "model X is prevented from doing..." would be more clear.

The final recommendation was adjusted to 4 after considering the author's feedback. I agree that objective difficulties in running other people's software should not be held against current authors. The effort made to test the Lee and Howe system, and the problems encountered in doing so, should be documented in the paper. I would also suggest that the authors try to reproduce Lee and Howe's results on their original data set as a sanity check (unless they have already done so), to see if it works for them.
