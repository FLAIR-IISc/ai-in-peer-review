- Model: applies a CNN to the entity grid with learned embeddings for grammatical roles, trained end-to-end using a pairwise ranking objective.
- Contribution: state-of-the-art performance on three coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary rating).
- Contribution: reframes the entity grid as an end-to-end neural framework allowing task-specific feature learning.
- Contribution: novel application of CNNs to capture longer-range entity transitions in the grid.
- Strength: strong empirical results with statistically significant gains (≈4% on discrimination, >2.5% on insertion).
- Strength: clear motivation and exposition, with effective intuitions and a "Why it works" explanation.
- Strength: end-to-end pairwise ranking yields better task-specific feature learning than prior task-agnostic approaches.
- Weakness: limited conceptual novelty—mainly a straightforward neuralization of existing entity grid ideas.
- Weakness: model complexity questionable—using 100-d embeddings for a tiny role vocabulary lacks justification or ablation.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of filter/window size impact and whether long transition modeling is critical.
- Weakness: reproducibility/experimental concerns—authors could not reproduce Li & Hovy (2014), raising setup questions.
- Weakness: paper organization redundancy—Sections 2 and 6 repeat background and should be consolidated.
- Question: clarify why Li & Hovy (2014) results could not be reproduced and confirm experimental setup.
- Question: justify 100-dimensional role embeddings and report ablation with lower dimensions.
- Question: provide analysis of performance vs. filter window size (e.g., k=3 vs k=5–6) to quantify benefits.
