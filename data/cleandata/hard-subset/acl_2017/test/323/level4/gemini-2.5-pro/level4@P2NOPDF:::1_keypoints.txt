- Proposes a well-motivated extension of the entity grid coherence model using a CNN to learn entity-transition sequences, improving generalization over longer sequences.  
- Methodologically sound and well-written; appropriately builds on prior work by adding a targeted neural component rather than an entirely new end-to-end architecture.  
- Major concern: unexpectedly poor performance of the Li and Hovy (2014) neural baseline—need deeper investigation into potential experimental setup errors or corpus-dependent behavior.  
- Suggests evaluating the proposed model on datasets where Li and Hovy is known to perform well to provide a more conclusive comparison.  
- Minor issue: paper needs careful proofreading for missing definite articles and stylistic fixes (e.g., replace "limits the model to do X" with "limits the model's ability to do X").  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer raises recommendation to accept (score 4) after author response, acknowledging authors shouldn't be penalized for difficulties running others' code.  
- For camera-ready: document efforts and problems encountered when testing Li and Hovy's system, and attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy's original results as a sanity check.
