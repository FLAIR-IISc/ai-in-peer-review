This paper introduces a neural extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn coherent sequences of entity transitions, which enables better generalization over longer sequences than the direct probability estimates used in the original model.

The paper is well-written and presents a sensible approach. Rather than proposing an entirely new neural architecture, the authors build upon a strong existing model, using a neural network to target and overcome its specific limitations. While this is a valid strategy, the experimental comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy (2014) could be expanded. The authors note their surprise at the very low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their evaluation tasks. This naturally leads the reader to question whether there was an error in the experimental setup, or if that model's performance is highly corpus-dependent. To strengthen the paper's claims, it would be valuable to investigate this discrepancy further. For example, what results would the proposed model achieve on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy model is known to be successful? A deeper investigation into these factors would considerably strengthen the paper.

On a minor note, while the paper is generally very fluent and readable, definite articles are missing in many places (e.g., on lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely others). I suggest proofreading the paper specifically for article usage. Additionally, the recurring phrase "...limits the model to do X..." sounds slightly unnatural. Alternatives such as "limits the model's ability to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X" would be clearer.

--------------
Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that the authors should not be penalized for objective difficulties in running external code. The paper should, however, document the efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system and the specific problems that were encountered. I would also suggest, if they have not done so already, that the authors attempt to reproduce the original results of Li and Hovy on their own datasets. This would serve as a valuable sanity check to ensure their implementation of the baseline is correct.
