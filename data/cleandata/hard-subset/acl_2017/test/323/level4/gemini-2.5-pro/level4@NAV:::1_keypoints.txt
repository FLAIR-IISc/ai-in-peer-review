- Proposes a CNN-based extension of the entity grid model to learn coherent entity transition sequences, improving generalization over longer sequences versus the original probability-based model.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Praised methodological choice: integrating a neural component into an established framework rather than pursuing an entirely neural solution.  
- Evaluation is weak due to insufficient comparison with the Li & Hovy neural coherence model.  
- The surprisingly poor performance of the Li & Hovy baseline raises concerns about implementation errors or dataset-specific effects.  
- Recommend evaluating on a dataset/task where Li & Hovy is known to perform well to clarify the discrepancy.  
- A more in-depth analysis of the baseline discrepancy is needed to strengthen the paper's claims.  
- Minor grammar issues: repeated omission of definite articles at multiple lines; a careful proofread is recommended.  
- Recurring phrasing "...limits the model to do X..." is unconventional; suggest "limits the model's ability to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X."  
- After the author response, final recommendation updated to 4; reviewer agrees failing to run third-party code shouldn't unduly penalize the paper.  
- Final paper should document attempts and specific issues running the Li & Hovy baseline, and the authors should try to reproduce Li & Hovy's original results on their dataset as a sanity check.
