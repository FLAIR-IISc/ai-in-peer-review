- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid; approach is well-motivated and clearly described.
- Clear, transparent design rationale with a strong "Why it works" discussion.
- Extensive evaluation showing the model outperforms prior methods.
- Major reproducibility concern: authors fail to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) results, suggesting possible baseline implementation/modeling errors that must be investigated and explained.
- Contribution appears incremental: largely a straightforward neural adaptation of the entity grid without substantial novelty in representation or tasks.
- Unclear justification for using 100‑dim embeddings for inherently low‑dimensional grid entries; needs analysis of embedding dimensionality impact.
- Questionable practical benefit of comparing transition chains k=3 vs k=6 given short WSJ articles; require evidence that larger k helps.
- Paper contains repetition (e.g., entity grid explained multiple times); related work (Section 6) duplicates Section 2 and should be pruned/merged.
- Structural recommendation: delete redundant parts of Section 6, merge remaining content into Section 2, rename Section 2 to "Related Work" with subsections.
- English/proofreading issues: missing articles and stylistic edits needed.
- Specific small fixes: change "Figure 2" reference to "Figure 1"; use "First"/"Second" instead of "Firstly"/"Secondly"; rephrase "limits the model to"; remove unnecessary final intro paragraph.
- Overall: solid implementation with strong results but concerns about baseline reproduction, incremental novelty, and writing/structure limit the paper's current impact.
