- Presents a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid representation.  
- Model is well-motivated, described in detail, with clear design intuitions and a "Why it works" section.  
- Evaluation is extensive across standard coherence tasks and shows improved performance over prior methods.  
- Fails to reproduce a key result from Li & Hovy (2014), suggesting a possible implementation or modeling error.  
- Contribution feels incremental—mainly a "neuralization" of the classic entity grid rather than a major innovation.  
- Design choices need stronger justification (e.g., why 100-dimensional embeddings for essentially 4-dimensional grid entries).  
- Claims about learning longer-range transitions (k=6 vs k=3) lack clarity on practical benefit, especially for short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is generally clear but overly repetitive; related-work material is duplicated and sections should be merged/reorganized.  
- Recommend removing the standard short paper outline paragraph for conciseness.  
- Minor proofreading and editorial fixes needed (missing articles, figure reference, wording suggestions, and style changes).
