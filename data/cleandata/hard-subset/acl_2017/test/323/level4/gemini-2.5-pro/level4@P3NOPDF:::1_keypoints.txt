- Paper introduces a CNN-based extension to the entity grid model to learn coherent entity-transition sequences.
- CNN approach enables better generalization over longer entity sequences than direct transition probability estimates.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Methodologically strong: builds on established work and uses a targeted neural component to fix a specific limitation.
- Approach is elegant, sound, and makes a valuable contribution to coherence modeling literature.
- Main weakness: surprisingly low performance reported for the Li and Hovy (2014) neural coherence baseline.
- Raises questions about possible implementation or experimental setup issues for the baseline.
- Unclear whether baseline failure is corpus-specific or due to other evaluation factors.
- Suggests testing proposed model where Li and Hovy is known to perform well for a fair comparison.
- Reproduction difficulties are acknowledged; authors should document the specific problems encountered.
- Recommend attempting to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results on their datasets as a sanity check.
- Minor clarity/language issues: fix missing definite articles and rephrase awkward constructions like "limits the model to do X."
