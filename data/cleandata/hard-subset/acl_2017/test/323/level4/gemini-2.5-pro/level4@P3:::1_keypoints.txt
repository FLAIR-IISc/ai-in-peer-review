- Proposes a CNN applied to the entity grid to learn distributed entity-transition features for local text coherence.
- Trains the model end-to-end with a pairwise ranking objective and evaluates on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results, significantly outperforming non-neural entity grid baselines (up to ~4% absolute gains).
- Main contribution: novel neural architecture combining entity grid representation with convolutional filters to capture longer-range transitions.
- Strong empirical contribution: thorough evaluation on three standard tasks using WSJ, with statistically significant improvements.
- Well-motivated, elegant model design that augments an interpretable NLP representation with modern neural methods.
- Convincing and comprehensive evaluation across tasks and baselines.
- Clear writing, detailed experimental setup, and released code supporting reproducibility.
- Major weakness: inadequate comparison to the neural model of Li & Hovy (2014); their poor reported baseline performance (19.34%) is unexplained.
- Open issues about Li & Hovy comparison: possible implementation/setup errors, corpus dependence of that model, and lack of reciprocal evaluation on datasets where Li & Hovy succeeded.
- Request to authors: provide detailed experiments and sanity checks for reproducing Li & Hovy (2014) and analyze why it failed on WSJ.
- Additional comments: paper needs proofreading for missing definite articles and some awkward phrasing (e.g., "limits the model to do X").
