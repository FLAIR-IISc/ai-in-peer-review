- Proposes a CNN applied to the established entity-grid representation to model textual coherence.
- Approach is well-motivated and design choices (especially using a CNN) are clearly explained, with an explicit "Why it works" section.
- Thorough evaluation across tasks showing the model outperforms prior methods.
- Reproducibility concern: a prior result (Li/Hovy, 2014) could not be reproduced, requiring clarification.
- Conceptually a straightforward neural adaptation of the entity grid rather than a major methodological advance.
- Insufficient justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries (and slightly larger for extended grid).
- Questionable practical gain from longer transition chains (k=3 vs k=6), given many short source articles.
- Writing is verbose with repetition, especially redundant content between Sections 2 and 6.
- Recommendation to remove first two-thirds of Section 6 and integrate any unique content into a retitled Section 2.
- Overall: solid implementation but limited novelty; stronger contribution would require more innovative feature representations or tasks.
- Minor edits suggested: proofreading for English/articles, fix figure reference (Figure 2→Figure 1), change "Firstly/Secondly" to "First, Second", rephrase "limits the model to", and remove nonessential final paragraph in Section 1.
