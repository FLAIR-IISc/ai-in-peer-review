This paper presents an extension to the entity grid model for coherence assessment. The authors use a convolutional neural network (CNN) to learn coherent sequences of entity transitions, which allows for better generalization over long-range entity sequences compared to the original model's reliance on direct estimates of transition probabilities.

The paper is well-written and presents a commendable research approach. Rather than proposing a fully neural architecture, the authors thoughtfully build upon existing work, integrating a neural component to address a specific limitation of the original model.

While the approach is sound, the paper would be significantly strengthened by an expanded comparison to the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors themselves note their surprise at the low performance of the Li and Hovy model on their task. This result raises several questions for the reader: Was there an error in the experimental setup? Is the baseline model's poor performance specific to the corpus used? If so, how would the proposed model perform on a task or corpus where the Li and Hovy model is known to be successful? A more in-depth investigation into these factors is needed to contextualize the paper's results and would make the authors' claims more robust.

The paper is generally fluent and easy to read. However, it would benefit from careful proofreading for grammatical articles, as definite articles are frequently missing (e.g., on lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely elsewhere). Additionally, the recurring phrase "limits the model to do X" is stylistically awkward. More standard alternatives, such as "limits the model's ability to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X," would improve clarity.
