- Paper proposes a CNN-based extension of the entity grid model to learn coherent entity-transition sequences, improving generalization over longer sequences.
- The work is well-written and clearly presented, and sensibly builds on prior work rather than proposing a wholly new architecture.
- The methodology of using a neural network to address a specific limitation of the original model is sound and valuable.
- The paper lacks an expanded comparison to the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy, which weakens the experimental claims.
- The very low score reported for the Li and Hovy model raises concerns about the experimental setup or corpus dependence.
- Authors should test the proposed model on corpora/tasks where Li and Hovy succeeds and/or reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results as a sanity check.
- Problems encountered running third-party software should be documented in the paper to provide context for the negative baseline results.
- Minor proofreading is needed: numerous missing definite articles and awkward phrasing (e.g., "limits the model to do X") should be corrected.
- Final recommendation adjusted to 4, acknowledging objective difficulties in running third-party software and advising additional validation of the baseline implementation.
