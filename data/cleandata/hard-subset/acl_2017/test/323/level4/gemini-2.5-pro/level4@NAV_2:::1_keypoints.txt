- Proposes applying a CNN to the entity grid to learn distributed grammatical-role features for local coherence assessment.  
- Replaces explicit entity-transition probabilities with end-to-end learned features to capture longer-range transitions.  
- Evaluated on sentence ordering (discrimination and insertion) and summary coherence rating tasks, reporting state-of-the-art results.  
- Main contribution: novel combination of a classic entity-grid representation with a CNN to mitigate data sparsity in long transition modeling.  
- Strong empirical results: consistent, significant gains (e.g., ~4% absolute on discrimination and >4% on summary rating) over non-neural entity-grid baselines.  
- Principled, well-motivated hybrid approach that leverages symbolic structure and neural generalization.  
- Clear presentation and reproducibility: paper is well-written and authors released code.  
- Weakness: inadequate comparison with Li and Hovy (2014) — their neural baseline reported very poor performance (19.34%) and was excluded from main results, raising concerns about experimental setup or corpus-dependence.  
- Weakness: limited architectural novelty — standard embedding→CNN→pool→scoring pipeline; novelty is mainly the grid+CNN combination.  
- Weakness: minor writing issues and grammatical slips that need proofreading.  
- Requested clarification: authors should elaborate on their Li & Hovy (2014) experiments, sanity checks on their implementation/dataset, and possible reasons for the baseline’s poor performance.
