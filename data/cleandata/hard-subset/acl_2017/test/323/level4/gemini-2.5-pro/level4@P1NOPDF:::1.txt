This paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model, using a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach allows for better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to the direct estimates of transition probabilities used in the original model.

This is a well-written paper. I appreciate that rather than proposing a fully neural approach, the authors build upon existing work, integrating a neural network to overcome a specific limitation of the original model.

While this is a sound approach, the paper would be significantly strengthened by an expanded comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors note their surprise at the very low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task. This result raises questions for the reader: could there have been an error in the experimental setup? Is the model's poor performance corpus-dependent? If so, how would the proposed model perform on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy model is known to be successful? A deeper investigation into these factors would considerably strengthen the paper's claims.

In terms of writing, the paper is generally fluent and readable. However, I noticed several instances of missing definite articles (e.g., on lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, and 764). I recommend a careful proofread focusing on article usage. Additionally, the recurring phrase "...limits the model to do X..." is slightly unidiomatic. More natural phrasing might include "limits the model's ability to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X".
--------------
Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that the authors should not be penalized for objective difficulties encountered when running another research group's software. The efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, and the problems encountered in doing so, should be documented in the paper. I would also suggest, as a sanity check, that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets, if they have not already done so.
