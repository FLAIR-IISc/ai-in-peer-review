- Proposes a CNN applied to the entity grid to learn distributed grammatical-role embeddings and score local text coherence end-to-end with a pairwise ranking objective.
- Empirically outperforms prior methods on three coherence tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary rating), claiming new state-of-the-art results.
- Neuralizes the entity grid, overcoming sparsity and enabling modeling of longer entity transitions (up to k=8) via learned convolutional filters.
- Achieves substantial gains (~4% discrimination, ~2.5% insertion) over strong baselines including Elsner & Charniak (2011).
- Model is well-motivated and clearly explained, with convincing intuitions for design choices and an accessible "Why it works" discussion.
- Elegantly addresses prior limitations by learning task-specific features and capturing long-range transitions through distributed representations and CNNs.
- Methodological novelty is incremental: combines existing entity-grid and standard CNN ideas rather than introducing a fundamental new architecture.
- Uses possibly unnecessary 100-dimensional embeddings for a tiny grid-symbol vocabulary without justification or ablation.
- Fails to reproduce results from Li & Hovy (2014) (reported 19.34% discrimination accuracy), leaving an important comparison unresolved.
- Paper structure is redundant: background and related work (Sections 2 and 6) overlap and should be consolidated.
- Requests clarification on reproduction attempts for Li & Hovy (2014) to explain the discrepancy.
- Asks for ablation/analysis on embedding dimensionality to justify 100-D embeddings.
- Requests sensitivity analysis on filter/window size (different k values) to quantify benefits of modeling longer transitions.
