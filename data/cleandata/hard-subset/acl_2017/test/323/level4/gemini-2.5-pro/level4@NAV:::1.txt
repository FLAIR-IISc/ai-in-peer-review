This paper presents a novel extension to the entity grid model for coherence. The proposed method employs a convolutional neural network (CNN) to learn coherent sequences of entity transitions. This approach allows for improved generalization across longer entity sequences compared to the original model, which relies on direct probability estimates of transitions.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. I commend the authors for their methodology; rather than pursuing a fully neural solution, they effectively integrate a neural component to address a specific limitation within an established framework. This is a sound and practical research strategy. However, the evaluation would be strengthened by a more thorough comparison with the neural coherence model proposed by Li and Hovy. The authors' expressed surprise at the poor performance of the Li and Hovy model on their dataset raises questions about the experimental conditions. It prompts consideration of potential implementation errors or dataset-specific effects. Specifically, it is unclear whether the baseline's low score is an artifact of the chosen corpus. To clarify this, it would be beneficial to evaluate the proposed model on a task or dataset where the Li and Hovy model is known to perform well. A more in-depth analysis of these discrepancies would significantly bolster the paper's claims.

Overall, the paper is fluent and easy to follow. A minor point of revision concerns grammar, specifically the consistent omission of definite articles in several places (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764). A careful proofread focusing on article usage is recommended. Additionally, the recurring phrase "...limits the model to do X..." is slightly unconventional. Alternatives such as "limits the model's ability to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X" might offer greater clarity.

After reviewing the author response, my final recommendation has been updated to 4. I concur with the authors that challenges in successfully running third-party code should not negatively impact the assessment of their work. It is important, however, that the final version of the paper documents the attempts made to run the Li and Hovy baseline and the specific issues that arose. Furthermore, I recommend that the authors, if they have not done so already, attempt to reproduce the original Li and Hovy results on their original dataset as a sanity check to confirm their implementation.
