This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) for modeling text coherence. The model is built upon the well-established entity grid representation, applying a CNN to learn coherence patterns directly from the grid structure.

The approach is well-motivated and clearly described. I particularly appreciated the authors' discussion of the intuitions behind their design choices, such as the use of a CNN and the dedicated "Why it works" section. The paper also provides an extensive evaluation across several tasks, demonstrating that the proposed approach outperforms previous methods.

However, there are a few points that weaken the paper's contribution. A primary concern is the failure to reproduce a key result from previous work (Li & Hovy, 2014). This discrepancy is significant and suggests a potential implementation or modeling error that the authors should address to strengthen their claims.

Furthermore, while effective, the model can be seen as a relatively straightforward "neuralization" of the classic entity grid. The motivation for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent a grid entry that originally contains only four features (or slightly more in the extended version) is not entirely clear. The paper would be stronger if it explained how this high-dimensional embedding helps capture information beyond the original, low-dimensional features. Similarly, while optimizing directly for coherence ranking is a clear advantage, the impact of extending transition chains from k=3 to k=6 seems questionable, especially given that many articles in the WSJ corpus are relatively short. The practical benefit of this extension could be further clarified.

The paper's structure and writing could also be improved for conciseness. There is significant repetition, particularly in the introduction to entity grids. Section 6 (Related Work) largely repeats material already presented in Section 2. I recommend merging these sections. For instance, the first two-thirds of Section 6 could be integrated into Section 2, and the remainder of Section 6 could become a subsection within a consolidated "Related Work" section (renaming Section 2 accordingly).

In summary, this is a solid paper that successfully applies a neural network to the entity-grid coherence model. However, for a full paper, the contribution feels somewhat incremental. I would have liked to see more innovation, perhaps in the form of novel features for the grid representation or the application of the model to new tasks.

Minor points:
- This paper would benefit from proofreading by a native English speaker. There are numerous instances of missing articles (e.g., "the WSJ corpus," "the Brown... toolkit").
- p.1, bottom of the left column: "Figure 2" should be "Figure 1."
- p.1: "Firstly/Secondly" should be "First/Second" for standard academic style.
- p.1: The phrase "limits the model to" could be rephrased for clarity, for example, as "prevents the model from considering..."
- The standard "roadmap" paragraph at the end of Section 1 is not essential for a paper of this length and could be removed to save space.
