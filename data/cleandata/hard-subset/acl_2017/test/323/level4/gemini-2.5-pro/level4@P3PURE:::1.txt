This paper presents an extension to the entity grid model for coherence. The authors employ a convolutional neural network to learn coherent sequences of entity transitions, which allows for better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to the direct probability estimates used in the original model.

This is a well-written and clearly presented paper. I appreciate the authors' approach of building upon existing work by using a neural network to address a specific limitation of the original model, rather than proposing an entirely new neural architecture. This is a sound and valuable research methodology.

However, the paper would be strengthened by an expanded comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors themselves express surprise at the very low score this model achieves on their task. This leads the reader to question the experimental setup. For instance, the baseline model's poor performance could be due to an error in the setup or it might be highly corpus-dependent. If the latter is true, it would be important to know how the proposed model would perform on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy model is more successful. A deeper investigation of these factors would substantially strengthen the paper's claims.

On a minor note, while the paper is generally fluent and readable, it would benefit from careful proofreading. I noticed numerous instances of missing definite articles (e.g., on lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, and 764). I would suggest a review of the paper with a specific focus on article usage. Additionally, the recurring phrase "...limits the model to do X..." is somewhat awkward. Clearer alternatives, such as "...limits the model's ability to do X..." or "...prevents the model from doing X...", would improve readability.
--------------
Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running third-party software should not be held against the present authors. The efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, and the problems encountered in doing so, should be documented in the paper to provide context for the results. I would also suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check (if they have not already done so) to confirm their implementation is working as expected.
