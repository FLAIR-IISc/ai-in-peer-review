- Proposes a neural extension of the entity grid using a CNN to learn entity-transition sequences, improving generalization over longer sequences versus the original model.
- Paper is well-written and takes a sensible approach by building on a strong existing model rather than inventing a wholly new architecture.
- Experimental comparison to Li and Hovy (2014) is insufficient and should be expanded.
- Reviewer is surprised by the very low score of the Li and Hovy baseline, raising concerns about experimental error or corpus-dependent performance.
- Recommend testing the proposed model on corpora/tasks where the Li and Hovy model is known to perform well to investigate the discrepancy.
- Paper should document attempts to run the Li and Hovy system and detail specific problems encountered.
- Authors should try to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results on their own datasets as a sanity check.
- Minor language issues: missing definite articles in many places and awkward phrasing like "limits the model to do X"; suggest clearer alternatives.
- Final recommendation adjusted to 4; authors shouldn’t be penalized for difficulties running external code but must document efforts and verification steps.
