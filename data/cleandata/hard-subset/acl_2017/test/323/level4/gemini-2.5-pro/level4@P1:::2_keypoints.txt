- Proposes a CNN applied directly to the entity grid to learn coherence features end-to-end, capturing longer-range entity transitions.  
- Demonstrates new state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.

- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides good intuition for design choices (notably "Why it works").  
- Strong, thorough empirical evaluation showing consistent improvement over prior coherence models.  
- Sound experimental methodology with comprehensive baselines and a suitable pairwise ranking training objective.

- Failed to reproduce a recent neural baseline (Li and Hovy 2014), reporting an implausibly low discrimination accuracy that undermines comparative claims.  
- Limited conceptual novelty: essentially a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid using a standard CNN rather than new representations or tasks.  
- Hyperparameter choices lack justification (e.g., 100-d embeddings for a tiny role vocabulary and long transition chains up to k=6) and their practical benefit is unclear.

- Request for ablation/analysis on the necessity of 100-dimensional embeddings for the small grammatical-role vocabulary.  
- Ask whether the authors investigated causes for the poor Li and Hovy (2014) reproduction and if it could be a configuration/implementation issue.

- Suggests consolidating redundant background/related-work sections to improve paper structure.  
- Recommends a final proofreading to fix minor grammatical errors and removing an unnecessary introductory roadmap paragraph.  
- Notes confusing figure reference for the entity grid and suggests a simpler dedicated figure illustrating the grid itself.
