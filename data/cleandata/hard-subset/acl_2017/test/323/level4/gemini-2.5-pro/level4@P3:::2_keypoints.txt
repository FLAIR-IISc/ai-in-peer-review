- Proposes a CNN applied to the entity grid that maps grammatical-role symbols to embeddings and uses wide convolutions + pooling to score local coherence, trained with pairwise ranking.
- Claims state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks, outperforming prior neural and non-neural baselines.
- Strength: strong empirical gains (≈4% improvements on discrimination and summary coherence rating) across multiple established tasks.
- Strength: model design and motivations are clearly explained, with a convincing intuition for CNNs capturing location-invariant transition patterns.
- Strength: principled end-to-end pairwise ranking framework that learns task-specific distributed features rather than hand-engineered transition probabilities.
- Weakness: limited conceptual novelty—mainly a “neuralization” of the classic entity-grid rather than a fundamentally new coherence modeling approach.
- Weakness: insufficient justification/analysis of model complexity (e.g., 100-D embeddings for a tiny role vocabulary) and lack of ablation studies isolating contributions (embedding size vs. longer transition window).
- Weakness: incomplete experimental comparison due to inability to reproduce a closely related neural baseline (Li & Hovy, 2014), leaving a gap in evaluation.
- Question: please provide intuition/analysis for what the 100-D role embeddings capture and results for lower-dimensional embeddings.
- Question: please provide an ablation comparing filter window sizes (e.g., k=3 vs. k=6) and against non-neural Grid-all nouns to isolate benefits of longer transitions.
- Minor: writing is repetitive (Sections 2 and early Section 6); these should be consolidated.
- Minor: proofreading needed for articles and phrasing (e.g., “the WSJ corpus”, “the Brown coherence toolkit”).
- Minor: typographical and style fixes suggested (Figure numbering, “Firstly/Secondly” → “First/Second”, rephrase “limits the model to consider”).
- Minor: consider removing the final introductory paragraph outlining paper structure to save space.
