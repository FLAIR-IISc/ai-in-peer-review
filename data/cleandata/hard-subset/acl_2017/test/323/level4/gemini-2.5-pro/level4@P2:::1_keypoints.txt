- Proposes a neural extension of the entity grid: CNN over distributed entity-grid representations to model longer-range transitions and reduce sparsity, trained end-to-end with pairwise ranking.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results across discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks on WSJ, outperforming prior entity-grid and other baselines.  
- Well-motivated, elegant fusion of linguistically-grounded entity grid and CNN, enabling task-specific feature learning instead of decoupled feature extraction.  
- Comprehensive, robust evaluation on three tasks with substantial and consistent gains (e.g., ~4% absolute on discrimination, >4% on summary rating).  
- Paper is clearly written, reproducible, and authors release code to facilitate follow-up work.  
- Major weakness: inadequate comparison to a key neural baseline (Li & Hovy 2014); reported poor performance (19.34%) raises concerns about experimental setup or corpus dependence.  
- Reviewer asks authors to validate their Li & Hovy implementation by reproducing original results on that model's dataset to rule out errors.  
- Reviewer requests a discussion/hypothesis explaining why sentence-representation models might fail on WSJ while entity-centric models succeed, and whether this indicates an intrinsic advantage for entity-grid approaches on such texts.
