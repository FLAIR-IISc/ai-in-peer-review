This paper proposes a convolutional neural network approach for modeling text coherence. This model is based on the well-known entity grid representation for coherence, but adds a CNN on top of it. The approach is well-motivated and described; I particularly appreciate the clear discussion of the insights behind certain design decisions (for example, the section titled "Why CNNs and 'Why It Works').

There is a broad assessment on several tasks, which shows that the proposed approach beats previous methods. However, it is odd that a previous result could not be reproduced: the results on Lee/Howe (2014) suggest an implementation or modeling error that should be addressed.

Nevertheless, the model is a relatively simple 'neuralization' of the entity grid model. I don't understand why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or something more in the case of an extended grid). How does this help? I can see that optimizing directly for coherence ranking would help in learning a better model, but the difference in transition sequences from k=3 sentences vs. k=6 cannot make such a big difference, especially since many WSJ articles can be very short.

The writing seemed a bit lengthy; the paper repeats some parts in several places, for example, the introduction to the entity grid. In particular, section 2 also presents related work, thus the first 2/3 of section 6 is a redundancy and should be removed (or worked into section 2 where necessary). The rest of section 6 should probably be added in section 2 under a subsection (then the name of section 2 should be changed to Related Work).

Overall, this seems like a solid implementation of applying a neural network model on entity-grid-based coherence. But considering the proposed consolidation of previous work, I would expect a little more from a full research paper, such as representation (other features?) or innovations in tasks.
