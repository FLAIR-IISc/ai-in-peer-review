This paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence, allowing better generalization on longer sequences of entities compared to direct estimates of transition probabilities in the original model.

This is a good and well-written paper. Instead of proposing a completely neural approach, the authors build on existing work and use only a neural network to address specific issues in a single step. This is a valid approach, but it would be useful to extend the comparison to Lee and Howe's existing neural coherence model. The authors acknowledge that they are surprised by the very low scores the Lee and Howe model achieves on their task. This makes the reader wonder if there was an error in the experimental setup, if the lower performance of the other model is corpus-dependent, and, if so, what results the proposed model in this paper would achieve on the corpus or task where the other model is more successful. A thorough examination of these factors would greatly strengthen the argument.

In general, the paper is very fluent and readable, but certain articles are missing in several places (for example, lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and perhaps more). I would suggest proofreading the paper, especially keeping in mind the use of articles. The expression "...model is limited by doing X...", which is used repeatedly, seems a bit unusual. Perhaps "model is limited by the ability to do X" or "model is prevented from doing X" would be more clear.

The final recommendation was adjusted to 4 after considering the author's response. I agree that objective difficulties in running other people's software should not be held against the current authors. The effort made to test the Lee and Howe system, and the problems encountered in doing so, should be documented in the paper. I would also suggest that the authors try to reproduce Lee and Howe's results on their original data set as a sanity check (unless they have already done so), to see if it works for them.
