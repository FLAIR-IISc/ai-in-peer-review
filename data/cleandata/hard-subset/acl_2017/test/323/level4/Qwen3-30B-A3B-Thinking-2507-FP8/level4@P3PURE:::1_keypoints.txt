- Proposes an extension of the entity grid model using a convolutional neural network to learn entity-transition sequences indicating coherence.  
- Neural approach enables better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to direct transition probability estimation.  
- Methodological choice is sound: augmenting the original model with a targeted neural component rather than replacing it entirely.  
- Comparison with Li and Hovy (2014) is insufficient and needs deeper analysis.  
- Unexpectedly low performance of Li and Hovy's model raises concerns about experimental setup errors, corpus dependency, or dataset mismatch.  
- Authors should thoroughly investigate whether results stem from implementation/corpus issues or genuine model differences.  
- Paper is generally readable but contains numerous missing definite articles; a proofreading pass is recommended.  
- The phrase "...limits the model to do X..." is awkward; suggest alternatives like "limits the model's capacity to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X."  
- Final recommendation adjusted to 4; document efforts and difficulties in running Li and Hovy's code and attempt to reproduce their original results as a sanity check.
