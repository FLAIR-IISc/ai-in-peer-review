

The paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model, using a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that capture coherence. This approach enables better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to the original model's direct transition probability estimates. The work is well-written and clearly presented. Rather than proposing a fully neural solution, the authors build incrementally on prior work, employing a neural network to address specific limitations in one step—a well-justified approach. However, the comparison with existing neural coherence models would be strengthened by including a more thorough evaluation against Li and Hovy’s model. The authors note their surprise at the model’s low performance on their task, which raises questions about potential experimental errors, corpus-specific performance issues, or whether the proposed method would generalize to settings where Li and Hovy’s model excels. A deeper analysis of these factors would significantly bolster the paper’s argument.

The paper is generally fluent and readable, though several instances of missing definite articles require attention (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764). A focused proofread for article usage is recommended. Additionally, the phrase "...limits the model to do X..." appears repeatedly and sounds unnatural; alternatives such as "limits the model’s ability to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X" would improve clarity.

After reviewing the author response, I adjust my recommendation to a 4 (Weak Accept). I acknowledge that objective difficulties in running others’ software should not be held against the authors. However, the challenges encountered while testing Li and Hovy’s system should be explicitly documented in the paper. I also recommend that the authors attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy’s results on their original datasets as a sanity check—unless this has already been done—to confirm the observed performance discrepancy. This would provide greater confidence in the comparative claims.
