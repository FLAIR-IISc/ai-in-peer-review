

The paper introduces a convolutional neural network (CNN) framework for modeling text coherence, building upon the established entity grid representation. The approach is well-motivated, with a particularly strong discussion of design intuitions (e.g., the rationale for using CNNs and the "Why it works" section). Extensive evaluation across multiple tasks demonstrates superior performance over prior methods. However, a notable discrepancy arises: the authors cannot reproduce the results reported by Li and Hovy (2014), suggesting a potential implementation or modeling error that requires clarification.

While the neural adaptation of the entity grid model is technically sound, the paper lacks sufficient innovation. The necessity of 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries (or slightly more in the extended grid) is unclear—this dimensionality choice requires justification, as its benefit over simpler representations is not explained. Additionally, the marginal difference between transition chains for k=3 and k=6 sentences appears questionable, especially given that many WSJ articles are short, potentially diminishing the practical impact of this design choice.

The writing suffers from redundancy, particularly in the explanation of entity grids. The introduction and Section 2 both cover foundational concepts, and the first two-thirds of Section 6 largely repeat material already presented. To improve conciseness, Section 6 should be consolidated into Section 2 (which should be renamed "Related Work" to reflect its expanded scope), with relevant content integrated into subsections. The remaining content of Section 6 should be omitted or moved accordingly.

The paper’s contribution feels incremental rather than transformative. Given the authors’ consolidation of prior work, a full paper should offer deeper innovations—such as novel representations, additional features, or expanded task coverage—beyond a straightforward neural adaptation of an existing framework.

**Minor issues requiring attention:**  
- Proofreading is needed; missing articles appear frequently (e.g., "the WSJ corpus" [x2], "the Brown toolkit" [x2]).  
- Page 1, bottom left: "Figure 2" should be "Figure 1".  
- Page 1: Replace "Firstly/Secondly" with "First, Second".  
- Page 1: Rephrase "limits the model to" as "prevents the model from considering...".  
- Remove the concluding paragraph in Section 1, as it is unnecessary for this concise paper.  

Overall, the work presents a solid, well-executed application of CNNs to entity-grid coherence modeling. The evaluation is thorough, and the core approach is clearly articulated. However, the lack of significant representational or task innovations, coupled with the unresolved Li/Hovy (2014) discrepancy and structural redundancies, prevents the paper from making a compelling advance. Addressing these points would substantially strengthen the submission.
