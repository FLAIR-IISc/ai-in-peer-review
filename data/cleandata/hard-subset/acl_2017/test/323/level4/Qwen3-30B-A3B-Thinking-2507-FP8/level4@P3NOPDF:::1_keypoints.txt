- Proposes extending the entity grid model with a CNN to learn longer entity transition sequences, improving coherence modeling over direct transition probabilities.  
- Approach is well-motivated and gracefully blends traditional modeling with targeted neural augmentation rather than a full neural overhaul.  
- Practical strength: addresses a concrete limitation (poor generalization beyond short transitions) with a focused neural component.  
- Writing is generally clear and fluent but contains minor grammatical issues that reduce precision.  
- Specific grammatical problem: frequent missing definite articles and awkward phrases (e.g., "limits the model to do X"); recommends proofreading and rephrasing.  
- Evaluation weakness: insufficient comparison with Li and Hovy (2016) neural coherence model, which performed unexpectedly poorly on their task.  
- Paper fails to investigate whether Li & Hovy’s low performance is due to methodological issues, corpus sensitivity, or experimental error.  
- Reviewer asks for deeper analysis: test Li & Hovy’s model on corpora where it usually succeeds or reproduce their original results as a sanity check.  
- After author response, reviewer raises to weak accept (4) but insists the paper should document reproduction challenges (code/dependency unavailability) in experiments or appendix.  
- Overall: technically sound and valuable contribution; addressing the Li & Hovy comparison, documenting experimental hurdles, and fixing grammar would strengthen the paper.
