- Paper proposes a neural extension of the entity grid using a CNN to learn long-range entity transition sequences, enabling better generalization than direct transition probability estimation.

- Main contribution: neural architecture that models long-range entity transitions while retaining entity-specific features and generalization, trained end-to-end via pairwise ranking.

- Evaluation: comprehensive across three tasks (discrimination, insertion, summary coherence rating) with consistent improvements and state-of-the-art results.

- Reported gains: ~4% absolute on discrimination, up to 2.5% on insertion, and >4% on summary coherence rating.

- Strength: clear, well-motivated approach that effectively builds on established coherence modeling work rather than replacing it entirely.

- Strength: thorough multi-task evaluation demonstrating empirical improvements.

- Weakness: insufficient comparison with Li and Hovy (2014), the only other neural coherence model in the literature.

- Weakness: no investigation into why the Li and Hovy model performed very poorly on their data; could be experimental error, corpus limitations, or dataset-specific issues.

- Suggested fix: authors should document their attempts to run Li and Hovy's system and attempt reproducing its original results as a sanity check.

- Writing issues: multiple missing articles throughout the manuscript and repetitive awkward phrasing ("limits the model to do X"); recommend proofreading and rephrasing to improve clarity.

- Final recommendation: accept with minor revisions (score 4), conditioned on documenting experiments with Li and Hovy and addressing proofreading concerns.
