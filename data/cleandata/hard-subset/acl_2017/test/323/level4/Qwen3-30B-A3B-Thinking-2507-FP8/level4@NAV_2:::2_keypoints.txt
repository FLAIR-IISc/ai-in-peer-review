- CNN framework replaces discrete entity-grid transitions with distributed representations, enabling longer entity transitions (k=6) and entity-specific features.  
- End-to-end pairwise ranking training learns task-specific features without decoupling feature extraction from downstream tasks.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks.  
- Strong, clear technical motivation: convolutional filters detect transition patterns and max-pooling captures salient features ("Why it works").  
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets (WSJ, AIRPLANES, EARTHQUAKES) with significant improvements (+4.0% discrimination, +2.5% insertion, +4.0% summary) and p<0.01.  
- Pairwise training demonstrably outperforms non-neural counterparts (Extended Grid-CNN > non-neural on insertion by 2.5%).  
- Unexplained failure to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) results (19.34% discrimination) raises concerns about experimental setup.  
- No justification for using 100-dimensional embeddings for a small discrete set of grammatical roles; potential overfitting concern.  
- Claim of modeling "long-range" transitions (k=6) is questionable for WSJ (avg 21.5 sentences/article); need evidence that k=6 is sufficient or that larger k offers no benefit.  
- Paper contains significant repetition (entity grid descriptions repeated across sections), reducing conciseness.  
- Question: Clarify discrepancy reproducing Li & Hovy (2014)—was implementation/code/dataset identical or modified?  
- Question: Justify 100D embeddings for grammatical roles and compare to lower-dimensional choices (e.g., 50D).  
- Question: Explain why k=6 is adequate for WSJ and provide evidence on whether k>8 would yield further gains.
