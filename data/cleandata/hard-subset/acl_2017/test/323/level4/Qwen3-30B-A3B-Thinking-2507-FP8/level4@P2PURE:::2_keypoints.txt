- Proposes a CNN applied to the established entity-grid representation for modeling text coherence; motivation and design choices (including the "Why it works" section) are clearly explained.  
- Evaluation is extensive and shows superior performance over prior methods.  
- Unable to reproduce Li/Hovy (2014) results — possible implementation or modeling error that must be resolved.  
- The model is a relatively simple "neuralization" of the entity grid; need justification for using 100-dimensional vectors for four-dimensional grid entries (and slightly larger for extended grid).  
- Directly optimizing for coherence ranking is reasonable, but increasing transition chain length from k=3 to k=6 may offer limited gains given short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive: entity-grid introduction is duplicated and Section 6 largely repeats content that should be integrated into Section 2; recommend renaming Section 2 to "Related Work" and consolidating sections.  
- For a full paper, deeper innovations are needed (e.g., novel representations, additional features, or broader task coverage) beyond consolidating prior work.  
- Minor issues: proofreading needed for missing articles ("the WSJ corpus", "the Brown toolkit"), fix "Figure 2"→"Figure 1", change "Firstly/Secondly" to "First, Second", clarify "limits the model to" phrasing, and consider removing the final redundant paragraph in Section 1.
