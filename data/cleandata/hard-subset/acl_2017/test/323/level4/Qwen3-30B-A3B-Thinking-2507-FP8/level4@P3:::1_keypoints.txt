- Proposes a CNN-based neural extension of the entity grid to capture long-range entity transitions via distributed representations.  
- Introduces end-to-end pairwise ranking training to learn task-specific coherence features automatically.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks with consistent absolute improvements (~4%, 2.5%, >4%).  
- Effectively overcomes the entity-grid limitation of modeling only short transitions by using embeddings and convolutional layers to handle transitions of length 5–8 without overfitting.  
- Uses practical end-to-end pairwise ranking that removes manual feature engineering and yields robust gains across tasks.  
- Provides strong empirical evidence with statistically significant improvements and analysis of task-specific performance differences.  
- Fails to thoroughly compare or reproduce Li and Hovy (2014) results, leaving unclear whether poor performance is due to implementation, dataset differences, or the prior model.  
- Does not clearly explain how the convolutional grid architecture differs technically from Li and Hovy’s window-based sentence representation, weakening the novelty claim.  
- Contains minor grammatical and phrasing errors that hurt readability and professionalism.  
- Requests details on how Li and Hovy (2014) was implemented/tuned for the authors’ experiments.  
- Asks for speculation on why Li and Hovy’s model performed poorly on the WSJ corpus versus other datasets.  
- Seeks explanation of how insertion performance could be improved, given the model was trained primarily for discrimination.
