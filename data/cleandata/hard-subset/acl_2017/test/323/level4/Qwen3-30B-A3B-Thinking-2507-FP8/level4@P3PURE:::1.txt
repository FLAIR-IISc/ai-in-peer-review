

The paper introduces an extension to the entity grid model, employing a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach enables better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to the original model's direct estimation of transition probabilities. The work is well-written and presents a valid methodological choice: rather than proposing a fully neural approach, the authors build on existing work by using a neural network to address specific limitations in one step. However, the comparison with the neural coherence model by Li and Hovy (2014) would be strengthened by deeper analysis. The authors note being surprised by the significantly lower performance of the Li and Hovy model on their task, which raises questions about potential experimental setup errors, corpus dependency, or whether the proposed model would achieve comparable results on corpora where Li and Hovy's model performs better. A thorough investigation of these factors would substantially reinforce the paper's claims.

The paper is generally fluent and readable, but it contains several instances of missing definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764). A focused proofreading pass on article usage is recommended. Additionally, the recurring phrase "...limits the model to do X..." (e.g., line 174) is awkward; alternatives such as "limits the model's capacity to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X" would improve clarity.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running other researchers' code should not be held against the authors. The efforts to test the Li and Hovy system, along with the challenges encountered, should be documented in the paper. I also recommend that the authors attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy's results on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless already performed).
