

The paper introduces a convolutional neural network (CNN) framework for modeling text coherence, building upon the established entity grid representation. The approach is well-motivated and clearly articulated, particularly appreciating the detailed explanations of design choices (e.g., the rationale for using CNNs and the section 'Why it works'). Extensive evaluation across multiple tasks demonstrates that the proposed method outperforms prior work. However, it is concerning that one previous result (Li/Hovy, 2014) could not be reproduced, indicating a potential implementation or modeling error requiring clarification.  

The model represents a relatively straightforward neural adaptation of the entity grid approach. The necessity of 100-dimensional vectors for representing four-dimensional grid entries (or slightly more in the extended grid) is unclear and warrants justification. While optimizing directly for coherence ranking is beneficial, the distinction between transition chains for up to k=3 versus k=6 sentences may not yield substantial gains, especially given the brevity of many WSJ articles.  

The writing is somewhat verbose, with redundant content repeated across sections—specifically, the entity grid introduction appears in both the introduction and Section 2. The first two-thirds of Section 6 are redundant and should be removed or integrated into Section 2 (which should then be re-titled 'Related Work'). The remaining content of Section 6 should be incorporated into Section 2 under a new subsection.  

Overall, this constitutes a solid application of neural networks to entity-grid-based coherence modeling. Given the consolidation of prior work, the paper would benefit from more significant innovations, such as novel representations or additional tasks.
