- Introduces a CNN-extended entity grid model that learns sequences of entity transitions to capture coherence.  
- Claims better generalization over longer entity sequences than original transition-probability-based model.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and justifiably takes an incremental neural approach rather than a fully neural solution.  
- Comparison to existing neural coherence models (notably Li & Hovy) is insufficient and should be strengthened.  
- The model’s unexpectedly low performance versus Li & Hovy raises concerns about experimental errors, corpus-specific issues, or limited generalizability and needs deeper analysis.  
- Several instances of missing definite articles require proofreading (examples given by line numbers).  
- Repetitive/unidiomatic phrasing “limits the model to do X” should be revised (suggested alternatives provided).  
- Reviewer updated score to 4 (Weak Accept) after the author response, acknowledging difficulties running others’ software shouldn’t be penalized.  
- Authors should explicitly document challenges running Li & Hovy’s system and attempt to reproduce its results on original datasets as a sanity check.
