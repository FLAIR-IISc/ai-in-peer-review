- Paper proposes a neural extension of the entity grid: a CNN over distributed entity representations with pairwise ranking training, evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks with SOTA results.  
- Contribution 1: CNN models long-range entity transitions overcoming the entity grid’s curse of dimensionality.  
- Contribution 2: Pairwise ranking enables end-to-end, task-specific learning.  
- Contribution 3: Comprehensive evaluation (WSJ, DUC’03) showing statistically significant gains.  
- Strength 1: Effectively integrates neural methods with the established entity grid framework, allowing longer transition modeling without overfitting.  
- Strength 2: Substantial, statistically significant performance improvements (≈4% discrimination, 2.5% insertion, 4%+ summary coherence).  
- Strength 3: Thorough evaluation on appropriate, well-justified datasets (WSJ chosen over smaller corpora).  
- Weakness 1: Insufficient investigation of Li and Hovy (2014)’s poor performance on the WSJ dataset.  
- Weakness 2: No analysis on whether Li and Hovy’s failure is dataset-dependent, implementation-related, or model-inherent.  
- Weakness 3: Multiple grammatical errors and unusual phrasing that reduce readability.  
- Question 1: Why did Li and Hovy’s model perform so poorly (19.34%); dataset, implementation, or model issues?  
- Question 2: Have you reproduced Li and Hovy’s results on their original datasets (AIRPLANES, EARTHQUAKES), and what were the outcomes?  
- Question 3: Would your model’s ability to handle longer transitions make it more effective on tasks requiring longer discourse structure understanding?  
- Additional comment: Paper is well-structured and leverages established insights; authors should document attempts to reproduce Li and Hovy and fix grammatical issues.  
- Final recommendation: Accept (3.5/5) — solid contribution with minor comparative-analysis and proofreading shortcomings.
