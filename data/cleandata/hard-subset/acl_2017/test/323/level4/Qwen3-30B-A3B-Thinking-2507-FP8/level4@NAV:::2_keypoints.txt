- Proposes a CNN-based framework for text coherence built on the entity grid representation; approach is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Detailed design choices (e.g., rationale for CNNs and the "Why it works" section) are appreciated.  
- Extensive evaluation across multiple tasks shows the method outperforms prior work.  
- Failure to reproduce a previous result (Li/Hovy, 2014) is concerning and needs clarification (possible implementation/modeling error).  
- Model is a relatively straightforward neural adaptation of the entity grid; originality beyond consolidation of prior work is limited.  
- Unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are needed to represent four-dimensional grid entries (and slightly larger in extended grid); requires justification.  
- Direct optimization for coherence ranking is beneficial, but increasing transition chain length from k=3 to k=6 may not give substantial gains, especially on short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is verbose and contains redundant content (entity grid intro repeated in Intro and Section 2).  
- First two-thirds of Section 6 are redundant and should be removed or merged into Section 2, with Section 2 retitled "Related Work."  
- Remaining content of Section 6 should be incorporated into Section 2 under a new subsection.  
- Paper would benefit from more significant innovations (e.g., novel representations or additional tasks) beyond consolidating prior methods.  
- Minor proofreading and editorial fixes needed (missing articles, figure reference error, wording suggestions, remove redundant concluding statement).
