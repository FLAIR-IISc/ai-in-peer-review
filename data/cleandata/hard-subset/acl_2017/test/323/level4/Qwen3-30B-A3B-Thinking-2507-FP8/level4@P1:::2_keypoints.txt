- Contribution: Proposes a CNN-based entity-grid model that captures long-range entity transitions with distributed entity representations and learns coherence via end-to-end pairwise ranking.

- Strength: Well-motivated solution that overcomes curse of dimensionality, enabling modeling of longer transitions (k=5–8) without overfitting.

- Strength: Comprehensive evaluation showing state-of-the-art gains (~+4% discrimination, up to +2.5% insertion, >+4% summary coherence).

- Strength: Clear explanations of design choices; "Why it works" shows how convolutional filters detect meaningful transition patterns.

- Weakness: Reported 19.34% accuracy for Li and Hovy (2014) on WSJ is unexpectedly low and contradicts original results, raising reproducibility concerns.

- Weakness: Insufficient justification for using 100-dimensional embeddings for grid entries with a tiny vocabulary (S, O, X, -).

- Weakness: Significant redundancy between Section 2 and Section 6; much of Related Work repeats earlier material and should be consolidated.

- Question: Why does the Li and Hovy (2014) implementation perform so poorly on your WSJ dataset—implementation error or dataset mismatch?

- Question: Why are 100-dimensional vectors necessary for grid entries with only 4–6 possible values, and what advantages do they provide?

- Question: Given average document length of 22.3 sentences, how does the model obtain sufficient context for long transitions (k=5–8), especially when transitions may exceed document length?

- Additional comment: Paper needs proofreading to fix missing articles (e.g., "the WSJ corpus", "the Brown coherence toolkit").

- Additional comment: Correct the erroneous "Figure 2" reference on page 1 to "Figure 1".

- Additional comment: Make Section 2.1 formatting consistent (use "First," "Second," etc.).

- Additional comment: Remove the unnecessary concluding paragraph in Section 1 to improve conciseness.

- Overall: Solid technical contribution but should address reproducibility issues, better justify key design choices, and eliminate redundant content.
