- Proposes a neural local coherence model using a CNN over the entity grid to overcome discrete-role limitations and task-agnostic features.
- Contributions: distributed long-range entity transitions (k=5–8), pairwise ranking training, and state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence tasks.
- Clearly identifies limitations of prior entity grid models (curse of dimensionality, task-agnostic features) and motivates a neural solution.
- Thorough evaluation showing consistent improvements (≈4% discrimination, 2.5% insertion, >4% summary) with convincing ablation studies.
- Design choices are well justified: CNNs for locality/position-invariance and pairwise ranking preferred over classification; "Why it works" section is effective.
- Fails to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) and excludes that baseline without explanation, undermining the comprehensiveness of comparisons.
- Unjustified use of 100-dimensional embeddings for grid entries despite few discrete categories.
- Extended transition lengths show marginal gains and may be impractical given average WSJ article length (k=6 covers ≈30% of document).
- Significant repetition between Sections 2 and 6; related work and entity grid intro should be consolidated.
- Question: Why did Li & Hovy (2014) perform poorly on the WSJ dataset—implementation differences or dataset issues?
- Question: Why are 100-dimensional grid-entry embeddings necessary; is there evidence lower dimensions would not suffice?
- Question: How meaningful are long (k=6) transitions in practice given short document lengths and modest performance gains?
- Suggestion: Tighter editing to remove redundancy would improve the paper's flow.
- Suggestion: Address grammatical issues (missing articles, etc.) before final submission.
- Note: SOTA claim is supported by results but weakened by exclusion of Li & Hovy (2014) without explanation.
