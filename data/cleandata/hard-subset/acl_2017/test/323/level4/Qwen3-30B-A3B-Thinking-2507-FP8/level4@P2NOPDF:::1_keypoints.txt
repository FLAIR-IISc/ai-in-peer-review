- Proposes a well-motivated extension to the entity grid using a CNN to learn entity-transition sequences that capture coherence.  
- Claims better generalization over longer entity sequences than the original model's direct transition probability estimates.  
- Methodological choice to integrate a neural component into an existing framework (rather than a fully neural model) is sound and practical.  
- Insufficient comparison with Li and Hovy (2014); their surprisingly low performance is not explained.  
- Unclear whether Li & Hovy’s low score stems from experimental error, corpus-specific limitations, or task mismatch.  
- Authors should test their model on datasets where Li & Hovy performs well and/or reproduce Li & Hovy on the paper’s data for a fair comparison.  
- Paper is generally readable but has grammatical issues, notably missing definite articles in multiple places.  
- Repeated awkward phrasing ("limits the model to do X") should be revised to standard forms ("limits the model's ability to..." or "prevents the model from doing...").  
- Reviewer raised recommendation to 4 (weak accept) after the author response, acknowledging reproduction difficulties but urging further validation.  
- Current comparison lacks a critical validation step against the state of the art; reproducing Li & Hovy’s results would strengthen the paper’s claims.
