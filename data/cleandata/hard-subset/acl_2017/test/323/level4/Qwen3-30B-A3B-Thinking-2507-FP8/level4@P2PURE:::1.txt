

The paper introduces an extension to the entity grid model that employs a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions, capturing coherence and enabling better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to the original model's direct transition probability estimates. This is a well-written paper that adopts a sensible approach by building on existing work rather than proposing a fully neural solution; specifically, the authors use a neural network to address a particular limitation in one step. However, expanding the comparison to include the neural coherence model by Li and Hovy would strengthen the evaluation. The authors note their surprise at the Li and Hovy model's very low performance on their task, raising questions about potential experimental errors, corpus dependency, or whether the model proposed here would perform better on corpora where Li and Hovy's approach is more successful. A deeper investigation of these factors would significantly bolster the paper's argument.

The paper is generally fluent and readable, but it contains multiple missing definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely others). I recommend a focused proofread to address article usage. Additionally, the repeated phrasing "...limits the model to do X..." is awkward; alternatives such as "limits the model's capacity to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X" would improve clarity.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running others' software should not be held against the authors. The paper should document the efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system and the challenges encountered. I also suggest the authors attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy's results on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless already performed).
