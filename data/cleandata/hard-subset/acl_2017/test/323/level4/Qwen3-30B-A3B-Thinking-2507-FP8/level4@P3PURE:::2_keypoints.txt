- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid, with clear motivation and intuitive design explanations.  
- Evaluation is extensive and shows the method outperforms prior approaches across multiple tasks.  
- A prior result (Li & Hovy, 2014) could not be reproduced, indicating a potential implementation/modeling issue to resolve.  
- The approach is essentially a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid rather than a major methodological innovation.  
- The need for 100-dimensional vectors for four-dimensional grid entries (and slightly larger for the extended grid) is unclear and not well justified.  
- Direct optimization for coherence ranking may help, but transition-chain benefits for k=3 vs k=6 are limited, especially given many short WSJ documents.  
- The writing is verbose and redundant, with the entity-grid introduction repeated across sections; sections should be consolidated (merge into Related Work/Section 2).  
- Recommend removing or integrating the redundant first two-thirds of Section 6 and placing remaining content into Section 2 under a new subsection.  
- For a full paper, more substantial innovations (e.g., novel representations, additional features, or broader task coverage) are needed to justify the contribution.  
- Minor editorial issues: missing articles (proofreading needed), incorrect figure reference (Figure 2 → Figure 1), wording suggestions ("Firstly/Secondly" → "First, Second", "limits the model to" → "prevents the model from considering ..."), and consider omitting the standard concluding paragraph in Section 1.
