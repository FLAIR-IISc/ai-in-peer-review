- Summary: Proposes a CNN-based extension of the entity grid that models long-range distributed entity transitions and learns task-specific features end-to-end, claiming SOTA on discrimination, insertion, and summary rating.

- Strength: Overcomes discrete representation limits of prior entity-grid models to model longer transitions (k up to 8), yielding notable gains (≈4% discrimination, ≈2.5% insertion).

- Strength: End-to-end pairwise training learns task-specific features, with Extended Grid-CNN outperforming non-neural baselines (≈2.5% insertion, ≈3.7% summary rating).

- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation across three tasks with appropriate baselines and statistical validation, establishing robust empirical improvements.

- Weakness: Incomplete and unexplained comparison to Li & Hovy (2014); reported poor performance for that model (19.34% on WSJ) is not investigated, weakening the SOTA claim.

- Weakness: Multiple minor language and phrasing issues throughout the paper that reduce readability and clarity of technical descriptions.

- Weakness: Insufficient rationale for excluding Li & Hovy results—authors should reproduce original results or analyze implementation/corpus issues to justify omission.

- Question: Please clarify whether Li & Hovy’s poor WSJ performance is due to corpus-specific issues, implementation errors, or inherent model limitations, and reproduce their results on original datasets as a sanity check.

- Question: Given 25.95% insertion accuracy, how would the Extended Grid-CNN be expected to perform on AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES datasets used by prior work where Li & Hovy reportedly did better?

- Question: Please elaborate which specific feature limitations arise when training to distinguish documents from random permutations that cause weaker performance on single-sentence insertion tasks.
