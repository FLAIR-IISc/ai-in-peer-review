

The paper presents an extension to the entity grid model, employing a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach enables better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to the original model's direct transition probability estimates. The work is well-written and presents a reasonable methodological choice by building on established frameworks rather than proposing a fully neural solution, instead using a neural network to address specific limitations in one component. However, expanding the comparison to include Li and Hovy's neural coherence model would significantly strengthen the contribution. The authors note their surprise at the notably low performance of the Li and Hovy model on their task, raising questions about potential experimental errors, corpus dependency, or whether their model would perform better on corpora where Li and Hovy's approach is more effective. A more thorough investigation of these factors would substantially bolster the paper's argument.

Overall, the paper is fluent and readable, but it contains multiple instances of missing definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764). A targeted proofread focusing on article usage is recommended. Additionally, the recurring phrase "...limits the model to do X..." is awkward; alternatives such as "limits the model's capacity to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X" would improve clarity.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 following the author response. I concur that objective challenges in reproducing prior work should not disadvantage the authors. The paper should document the efforts and obstacles encountered when testing the Li and Hovy system. The authors are also encouraged to attempt reproducing Li and Hovy's results on their original datasets as a sanity check, unless this has already been completed.
