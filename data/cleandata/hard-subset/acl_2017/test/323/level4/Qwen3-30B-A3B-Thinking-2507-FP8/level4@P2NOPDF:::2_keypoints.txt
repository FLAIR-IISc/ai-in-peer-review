- Introduces a CNN-based framework for text coherence built on the entity grid with strong design intuition and explanation.  
- Extensive evaluation shows superior performance over prior methods across multiple tasks.  
- Authors cannot reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) results, indicating a potential implementation/modeling error that requires clarification.  
- Neural adaptation is technically sound but the contribution is incremental, lacking significant innovation.  
- Unclear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries; needs explanation or ablation.  
- Small performance difference between transition chain lengths k=3 and k=6 seems questionable given many short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is redundant—entity-grid explanations and large portions of Section 6 repeat earlier material.  
- Recommend consolidating Section 6 into Section 2 (rename to "Related Work") and removing or relocating remaining redundant content.  
- A full paper should include deeper innovations (novel representations, extra features, broader tasks) beyond a straightforward neural adaptation.  
- Minor issues: proofreading for missing articles, fix Figure 2→Figure 1, replace "Firstly/Secondly" with "First, Second", rephrase "limits the model to", and remove Section 1’s concluding paragraph.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed application with thorough evaluation, but unresolved replication issue, lack of representational novelty, and structural redundancies weaken the paper’s contribution.
