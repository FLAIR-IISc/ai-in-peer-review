- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid; approach is well-motivated and clearly explained with strong design-intuition discussion.  
- Evaluation shows strong performance and outperforms prior methods across multiple tasks.  
- Authors could not reproduce a key result from Li and Hovy (2014), raising concerns about possible implementation/modeling errors that need clarification.  
- The model is a relatively straightforward neural adaptation of the entity grid framework rather than a major methodological innovation.  
- Unclear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries; need explanation of how such high-dimensional vectors meaningfully contribute.  
- Extending transition chains from k=3 to k=6 yields limited practical benefit, especially given short average WSJ article length.  
- Paper contains redundancy between Sections 2 and 6; recommend consolidating redundant material into a single "Related Work" section and restructuring remaining content as a subsection.  
- As currently written, the paper would need more substantial innovations (e.g., novel representations or broader task coverage) to merit a full paper.  
- Minor issues: needs proofreading for missing articles, incorrect figure reference (Figure 2 → Figure 1), wording suggestions ("Firstly/Secondly" → "First, Second"; "limits the model to" → "prevents the model from considering..."), and consider removing the unnecessary concluding paragraph in Section 1.
