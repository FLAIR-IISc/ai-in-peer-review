- Proposes a CNN applied to the entity grid to model text coherence; approach is well motivated and clearly described.  
- Clear discussion of design intuitions, including rationale for using a CNN and a “Why it works” section.  
- Extensive evaluation across tasks showing the proposed method outperforms prior methods.  
- Reproduction concern: inability to reproduce a previously reported result (Li and Hovy 2014) suggests a possible implementation or modeling error that needs investigation.  
- The model is essentially a straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid, lacking substantial novelty.  
- Questionable modeling choice: unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are needed to represent 4-dimensional (or slightly larger) grid entries and how this helps.  
- Optimizing directly for coherence ranking is sensible, but gains from increasing transition chain length from k=3 to k=6 may be limited given short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is lengthy and occasionally repetitive, particularly multiple introductions to entity grids.  
- Structural suggestion: merge/reduce section 6 content into section 2, then rename section 2 “Related Work,” and delete repetitive material.  
- As a full paper, expected more beyond consolidating prior work—e.g., novel representations, additional features, or new tasks.  
- Minor proofreading and wording issues: missing articles, incorrect figure reference (Figure 2 → Figure 1), “Firstly/Secondly” → “First, Second,” and suggested phrasing tweaks.  
- Suggest removing the standard final paragraph in section 1 because it’s unnecessary for a short paper.
