- Paper introduces a CNN-based local coherence model over the entity grid, trained end-to-end with pairwise ranking and evaluated on sentence ordering (discrimination, insertion) and summary coherence rating with consistent gains.

- Contribution: Neuralization of the entity grid yielding statistically significant improvements across multiple coherence tasks.

- Contribution: End-to-end pairwise ranking framework that learns task-specific embeddings and permits longer-range entity transitions.

- Contribution: Intuitive CNN architecture for detecting location-invariant entity transition patterns with clear motivation.

- Strength: Clear motivation and “why it works” discussion explaining CNN suitability for entity transitions.

- Strength: Strong empirical improvements (≈4% discrimination, up to 2.5% insertion, >4% summary rating) with significance testing and multiple datasets/baselines.

- Strength: Moves from hand-crafted transitions to learned embeddings/filters, addressing sparsity and enabling longer k (≈5–8) transitions.

- Strength: Broad evaluation, pretraining experiments for scarce data, and sufficient implementation/hyperparameter details with dev sets and multiple seeds.

- Weakness: Limited novelty beyond applying CNNs to the entity grid; lacks richer representations or broader analyses.

- Weakness: No justification or ablation for 100-dimensional embeddings and model capacity choices (embedding size, filter count).

- Weakness: Unclear benefit of longer transition lengths (k); needs ablation to isolate k’s effect given short WSJ articles.

- Weakness: Failure to reproduce Li and Hovy (2014) results suggests possible experimental/configuration issues that require investigation and reporting.

- Weakness: Redundant writing and structure; related work and background are repeated across sections and should be consolidated.

- Question: Why 100-dimensional embeddings for grammatical roles—do smaller embeddings perform similarly? Include an ablation.

- Question: How sensitive is performance to transition length k and do gains persist beyond k=3? Provide isolated results.

- Question: Was the model trained directly on insertion pairs and how does that compare to training on discrimination pairs?

- Question: What exact settings/preprocessing/splits were used to reproduce Li and Hovy (2014)? Were variant settings (RNN/recursive, window sizes, hyperparameters) tried?

- Question: Do larger models overfit? How do performance and variance change with filter count, pooling size, and dropout?

- Minor: Paper needs light copy-editing and fixes (figure reference, phrasing, article use, remove redundant final paragraph).
