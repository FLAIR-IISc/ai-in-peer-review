- Proposes a convolutional neural network built on the entity grid representation for modeling text coherence.
- Method is well motivated and clearly explained, with explicit discussion of design intuitions (e.g., “Why it works”).
- Evaluation is extensive across multiple tasks and shows the method outperforms prior approaches.
- Replication issue: reported numbers for Li/Hovy (2014) could not be replicated, suggesting a possible implementation or modeling mistake that should be investigated.
- The model is essentially a straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid and lacks novel representational ideas.
- Questions the necessity of 100-dimensional embeddings for grid cells that have only four (or slightly more) states.
- Doubts that increasing transition chain length from k=3 to k=6 yields substantial gains, especially given many short WSJ articles.
- Writing is verbose and repetitive, with the entity grid introduction appearing multiple times.
- Reorganization recommended: move much of Section 6 into Section 2 and retitle Section 2 as Related Work; remove redundant material.
- For a full paper, more contributions are expected (e.g., new features, representational ideas, or tasks).
- Minor proofreading and wording issues (missing definite articles, incorrect figure reference, “Firstly/Secondly” usage, awkward phrasing).
- Suggest removing the standard final paragraph in Section 1 for conciseness.
