- Proposes a CNN-based coherence model built on the entity grid; approach is well motivated with clear design rationale (including a “Why it works” section).  
- Thorough evaluation across multiple tasks showing consistent improvements over prior methods.  
- Failure to reproduce Li and Hovy (2014) is concerning and indicates a possible implementation or modeling issue that must be investigated and explained.  
- Contribution reads as a straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid, lacking substantial conceptual innovation.  
- Unclear justification for using 100-dimensional embeddings to encode grid entries that have only four (or slightly more) roles; question whether high dimensionality is needed or beneficial.  
- Skepticism that extending transition chains from k=3 to k=6 yields meaningful gains given the short length of many WSJ articles.  
- Writing is generally clear but verbose and repetitive; entity grid introduction is repeated and Section 6 duplicates earlier content.  
- Recommend merging/restructuring related content (move/merge parts of Section 6 into Section 2 and retitle as “Related Work”).  
- Overall a solid empirical application but would benefit from stronger innovation in representation or broader task coverage for a full paper.  
- Minor issues: proofreading for missing articles; correct figure reference (Figure 2 → Figure 1); replace “Firstly/Secondly” with “First, Second”; rephrase “limits the model to”; consider removing the standard concluding paragraph in Section 1.
