- Paper extends the entity grid model with a CNN to learn entity-transition patterns and capture longer-range dependencies.
- Approach replaces direct transition-probability estimation with learned convolutional features and is well motivated and clearly written.
- Sensible, focused extension of a strong baseline instead of an entirely neural pipeline.
- Clear exposition of model design and its benefits for longer transition sequences.
- Empirically convincing improvements over the original entity grid on the evaluated tasks.
- Comparison to Li and Hovy’s neural coherence model is underdeveloped and raises questions about experimental comparability.
- Authors should document exact efforts to run Li and Hovy’s system (versions, hyperparameters, preprocessing, software issues) for transparency.
- Attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy’s published results on their original datasets as a sanity check to rule out implementation issues.
- Discuss corpus and task dependence explicitly, explaining why Li and Hovy’s model may perform poorly on the authors’ data.
- If feasible, evaluate the proposed model on at least one dataset where Li and Hovy reported strong results or analyze expected behavior there.
- Include ablations or analyses to isolate what the CNN contributes beyond longer n-gram–like transition patterns (e.g., varying convolution widths, stronger baselines).
- Writing needs proofreading for definite-article omissions and non-idiomatic phrases (e.g., “limits the model to do X”).
- Overall assessment: solid, scoped contribution that improves a classic model but requires better comparison to the related neural baseline.
- Final recommendation: score 4 (accept after author response); require transparent documentation/replication of Li and Hovy comparisons to strengthen the paper.
