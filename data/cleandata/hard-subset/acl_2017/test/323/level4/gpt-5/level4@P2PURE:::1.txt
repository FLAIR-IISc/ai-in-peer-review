The paper presents an extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence, enabling better generalization over longer sequences of entities than the original model’s direct transition-probability estimates.

This is a solid, well-written paper. Rather than proposing a fully neural approach, the authors build on prior work and apply a neural network specifically to address one problematic step. This is a valid strategy, but the comparison with the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy should be expanded. The authors note that they were surprised by the very low score the Li and Hovy model achieves on their task. This raises questions about whether there was an error in the experimental setup, whether the other model’s poor performance is corpus-dependent, and, if so, how the proposed model would perform on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy model is more successful. A deeper investigation of these factors would considerably strengthen the argument.

Overall, the paper is fluent and readable, but definite articles are missing in many places (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely elsewhere). I suggest proofreading with particular attention to article usage. The repeated expression “…limits the model to do X…” sounds unusual; “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” would be clearer.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running other people’s software should not be held against the present authors. The efforts to evaluate the Li and Hovy system, and the problems encountered, should be documented in the paper. I would also suggest attempting to reproduce Li and Hovy’s results on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless this has already been done), simply to verify whether those results can be replicated.
