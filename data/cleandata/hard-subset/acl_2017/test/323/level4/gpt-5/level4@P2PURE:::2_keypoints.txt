- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid; approach is well motivated and clearly described with explicit design intuitions.
- Evaluation is extensive and shows improved performance over prior methods.
- One prior result (Li and Hovy 2014) could not be replicated, indicating a potential implementation or modeling error that needs investigation.
- The model is largely a straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid; unclear why 100-d vectors are needed for low-dimensional grid entries.
- Questionable benefit of modeling transitions up to k=6 versus k=3 given short WSJ articles; direct optimization for coherence ranking may explain gains.
- Writing is repetitive and verbose (entity grid introduction repeated; Section 6 duplicates earlier content); suggest merging/deleting and retitling Section 2 as “Related Work.”
- Paper lacks expected novelty for a full paper (e.g., new representation features or new tasks).
- Minor editorial issues: missing articles, incorrect figure reference, phrasing suggestions, and unnecessary closing paragraph.
