The paper presents an extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions that signal coherence, enabling better generalization over longer entity sequences than the direct transition-probability estimates used in the original model. This is a clear and well-executed paper. Rather than proposing a fully neural approach, the authors build on established work and introduce a neural component to address a specific limitation. This is a valid strategy, but the comparison with the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy should be expanded.

The authors note their surprise at the very low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task. This raises several questions: whether there might be an error in the experimental setup; whether the Li and Hovy model’s performance is corpus-dependent; and, if so, how the proposed model would perform on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy approach is known to be stronger. A deeper investigation of these factors would significantly strengthen the paper’s empirical claims.

The writing is generally fluent and readable, but definite articles are frequently missing (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely others). I recommend a careful proofreading pass focused on article usage. In addition, the phrase “...limits the model to do X...” appears repeatedly and sounds unnatural. Consider alternatives such as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X.”

Final recommendation: 4, after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running third-party software should not be held against the authors. However, the attempts to evaluate the Li and Hovy system, and the problems encountered, should be documented in the paper. I also recommend reproducing Li and Hovy’s results on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless this has already been done), to verify that the setup works as expected.
