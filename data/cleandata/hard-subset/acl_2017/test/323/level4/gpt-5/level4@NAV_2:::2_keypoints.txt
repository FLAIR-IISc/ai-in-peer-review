- Well-motivated, clearly presented CNN built on the entity grid with a helpful “Why it works” explanation and justified architectural choices.  
- Evaluation covers multiple coherence tasks and shows consistent improvements over prior work.  
- Failure to replicate Li & Hovy (2014) is puzzling and suggests an implementation or experimental-setup discrepancy that must be investigated.  
- Method is essentially a straightforward neural reimplementation of the entity grid rather than a substantially novel approach.  
- Unclear why 100-dimensional embeddings are needed for a four-value role inventory (or slightly larger in extended settings); dimensionality justification is weak.  
- Doubtful that extending transition windows from k ≤ 3 to k ≈ 6 yields substantial gains, especially given many WSJ articles are short.  
- Manuscript is verbose and repetitive (entity grid introduced multiple times); substantial redundancy in Sections 1–6.  
- Recommend reorganizing: fold much of Section 6 into Section 2 (retitle Section 2 to broader scope) and integrate remaining material as a subsection.  
- Paper consolidates prior ideas in full form but lacks expected novelty (e.g., richer representations or additional tasks).  
- Minor copyediting issues: missing articles (e.g., “the WSJ corpus”, “the Brown … toolkit”) and other language fixes.  
- Specific textual fixes: p.1 bottom left “Figure 2” → “Figure 1”; use “First, … Second, …” not “Firstly/Secondly”; replace “limits the model to” with “prevents the model from considering …”; remove boilerplate closing paragraph of Section 1.
