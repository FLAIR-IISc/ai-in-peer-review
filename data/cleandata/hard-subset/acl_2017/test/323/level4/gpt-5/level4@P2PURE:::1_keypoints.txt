- Extends the entity grid model with a convolutional neural network to learn entity-transition sequences, improving generalization over longer sequences compared to direct transition-probability estimates.
- Solid, well-written paper that applies a focused neural enhancement rather than proposing a fully neural approach.
- Using a neural network for a specific problematic step is a valid strategy that builds on prior work.
- Comparison with the Li and Hovy neural coherence model is insufficient and needs expansion.
- The unexpectedly low score of the Li & Hovy model on the task raises concerns about possible experimental errors or corpus-dependent performance.
- Authors should investigate whether the poor performance is due to setup issues or dataset differences and evaluate on corpora where Li & Hovy’s model is known to work.
- Proofreading is needed for missing definite articles and awkward phrasing (e.g., use “limits the model’s capacity to do X”).
- Final recommendation adjusted to 4; reviewer agrees that difficulties running others’ software shouldn’t be held against the authors but must be documented.
- Suggest reproducing Li & Hovy’s results on their original datasets as a sanity check unless already performed.
