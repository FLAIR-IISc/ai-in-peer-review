The paper extends the entity grid model by introducing a convolutional neural network (CNN) that learns sequences of entity transitions indicative of local coherence. This neural component replaces the direct estimation of transition probabilities in the original model, enabling better generalization over longer entity sequences. The approach leverages established insights from the entity grid while addressing specific limitations with a neural module rather than proposing a fully end-to-end neural system.

This is a clear and well-written paper. The choice to build on the entity grid and selectively apply a neural architecture to overcome particular bottlenecks is reasonable and well motivated. However, the comparison to the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy (2014) should be strengthened. The authors report being surprised by that model’s very low score on their task. This raises questions about whether there may have been an error in the experimental setup, whether the poor performance is corpus-dependent, and, if so, how the proposed model would fare on datasets or tasks where Li and Hovy’s approach performs better. A deeper investigation of these factors would significantly bolster the paper’s empirical claims.

Stylistically, the paper is generally fluent, but definite articles are missing in many places (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely elsewhere). I recommend a careful proofreading with article usage in mind. In addition, the recurrent expression “...limits the model to do X...” is somewhat unusual in English. Alternatives such as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” would be clearer and more idiomatic.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running third-party software should not be held against the authors. The attempts to evaluate the Li and Hovy system, and the specific problems encountered, should be documented in the paper. I also suggest reproducing Li and Hovy’s reported results on their original datasets as a sanity check (if this has not already been done), to verify that their implementation and setup can achieve the expected performance before applying it to the WSJ-based tasks.
