This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) approach to modeling text coherence. The model builds on the well-known entity grid representation and applies a CNN on top. The approach is well motivated and clearly described. I especially appreciate the discussion of the intuitions behind the design choices (e.g., the rationale for using a CNN and the section titled “Why it works”).

There is an extensive evaluation across several tasks, and the results show that the proposed approach outperforms previous methods. However, it is concerning that one previously reported result could not be reproduced: the discrepancy with Li and Hovy (2014) suggests a possible implementation or modeling error that should be investigated and addressed.

That said, the model still amounts to a relatively straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid. I do not understand why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or slightly more in the extended grid). How exactly does this help? I can see how optimizing directly for coherence ranking could yield a better model, but the difference between transition chains of up to k=3 sentences versus k=6 may not be large, especially given that many WSJ articles are quite short.

The writing feels somewhat lengthy and occasionally repetitive, particularly in its multiple introductions to entity grids. In particular, section 2 already presents related work, so the first two-thirds of section 6 repeat earlier material and should be deleted (or integrated into section 2 where necessary). The remainder of section 6 should likely be moved into section 2 under a subsection; then rename section 2 as “Related Work.”

Overall, this is a solid implementation of a neural network approach applied to an entity-grid-based coherence model. Given the consolidation of prior work, though, I would expect a bit more for a full paper—for example, innovations in the representations (additional features) or new tasks.
