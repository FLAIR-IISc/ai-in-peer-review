- Proposes a CNN-based neuralization of the entity grid that embeds grid symbols, convolves over entity transition sequences, and trains end-to-end with pairwise ranking.  
- Adds a simple mechanism to include entity-specific features in the grid alphabet, achieving gains with fewer features than prior extended grids.  
- Evaluates on sentence ordering (discrimination, insertion) and summary coherence rating, reporting consistent state-of-the-art improvements on WSJ and DUC.  
- Clear motivation: convolution over embedded transitions addresses sparsity of discrete transition counts and captures position-invariant longer-range patterns.  
- Strong empirical results: ~4% absolute gain on discrimination, up to 2.5% on insertion, and ~3–4% on DUC summary rating with significance testing and multiple runs.  
- Sound training/evaluation: pairwise ranking objective, held-out dev set tuning, early stopping, dropout, and repeated trials mitigate variance.  
- Practical and reproducible design: uses standard components (embeddings, CNN, max-pooling) and code release promised.  
- Novelty is limited: primarily a straightforward CNN applied to existing entity-grid representation with a standard ranking loss.  
- Missing ablations: no analysis of embedding dimensionality, transition window k, filter widths, pooling, or pretraining effects to isolate sources of gains.  
- Training-task mismatch for insertion: model trained only on discrimination pairs and not directly on insertion-style training, weakening insertion claims.  
- Model assumptions/scope: treats entity columns independently and assumes location-invariance, with no analysis of cross-entity interactions or robustness to coreference errors and domain/style shifts.  
- Reproducibility concern: failure to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) results is noted but not investigated, affecting comparative validity.  
- Requests ablation analyses: effects of embedding size, k, number of filters, pooling size, and learning curves for k.  
- Asks how much gain is from end-to-end pairwise training vs. switching to embeddings (e.g., linear model over learned embeddings).  
- Suggests training/evaluating directly on insertion-style pairs and error analysis for insertion failures.  
- Wants sensitivity analysis to coreference quality (ablate resolver) and variance/pretraining contribution for small summary dataset.  
- Recommends reorganizing related work to avoid redundancy and improving writing and proofreading.  
- Requests consistent figure numbering and clearer figure references.  
- Advises controlled feature-comparison experiments where neural and non-neural models use the same feature subset.  
- Reproducibility checklist: provide scripts for grid construction, coreference settings, data splits, hyperparameters, and qualitative analyses of top activated filters.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed application with clear gains but limited novelty and missing analyses; recommend borderline acceptance pending stronger analysis and organization.
