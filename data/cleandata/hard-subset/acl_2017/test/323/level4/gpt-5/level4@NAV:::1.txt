The paper presents an extension to the entity grid framework. A convolutional neural network is employed to learn patterns of entity transitions that signal coherence, enabling better generalization over longer sequences of entities than the original model’s direct transition-probability estimates. This is a clear and well-written paper. Rather than proposing a fully neural solution, the authors build on prior work and use a neural component to address a specific limitation. This is a valid strategy, but it would be helpful to broaden the comparison to the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors express surprise at the very low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task. This prompts questions about possible errors in the experimental setup, whether the other model’s poor performance is corpus-dependent and, if so, how the proposed model would perform on a corpus or task where the other model does better. A deeper investigation of these issues would substantially strengthen the case.

Overall, the manuscript reads smoothly, but definite articles are missing in many places (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely others). I recommend a proofreading pass with article usage in mind. The expression “…limits the model to do X…,” used repeatedly, sounds awkward. “Limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” would be clearer.

Final recommendation updated to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties running other people’s software should not be held against the authors. The attempts to evaluate the Li and Hovy system, and the problems encountered in doing so, should be documented in the paper. I also suggest trying to reproduce Li and Hovy’s results on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless already done), to verify that this setup works for them.
