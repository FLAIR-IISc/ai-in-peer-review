- Proposes an enhanced entity grid using a CNN to learn entity-transition patterns, improving generalization over longer transition sequences.  
- Manuscript is clear, well organized, and sensibly augments the established grid approach with a targeted neural component rather than a wholesale neural replacement.  
- Comparison against Li and Hovy’s neural coherence model is insufficient and should be expanded.  
- The surprisingly low performance of Li and Hovy’s model raises concerns about possible experimental misconfiguration, corpus sensitivity, and the proposed method’s behavior on datasets where Li and Hovy performs well.  
- A more thorough investigation of those factors (misconfiguration checks, corpus sensitivity, cross-dataset evaluation) would substantially strengthen the claims.  
- Practical difficulties running third-party code should not penalize the authors, but the attempts and issues encountered must be described in the paper.  
- Recommend reproducing Li and Hovy’s results on their original datasets as a sanity check to confirm implementation correctness.  
- Stylistic issues: frequent missing definite articles throughout the paper (multiple line examples) and awkward phrasing like “…limits the model to do X…” that should be revised.  
- Final recommendation updated to 4 after author response, with the above experimental clarifications and documentation requested.
