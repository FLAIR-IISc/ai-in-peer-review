- Reported very low score for the Li and Hovy model is surprising and raises concerns about possible experimental setup errors.  
- Performance of Li and Hovy may be corpus-dependent; authors should test on corpora/tasks where that approach is known to be stronger.  
- A deeper investigation of why Li and Hovy performed poorly (error, corpus effects, etc.) is needed to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Writing is generally fluent but frequently misses definite articles; a proofreading pass focused on article usage is recommended.  
- The phrase “limits the model to do X” is awkward and overused; use alternatives like “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X.”
