- Paper introduces a neural local coherence model applying CNNs over the entity grid, embedding grammatical-role symbols and training end-to-end with a pairwise ranking objective.
- Contribution: neuralized entity-grid using distributed embeddings and convolution to capture longer-range entity transitions without combinatorial sparsity.
- Contribution: end-to-end pairwise ranking training yielding state-of-the-art results on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.
- Contribution: simple mechanism to incorporate entity-specific features (NE type, salience, properness) without external resources, improving prior grid variants.
- Strength: strong empirical gains across tasks with statistical significance and multiple random seeds.
- Strength: clear architectural motivation and explanation for using CNNs and pooling to capture local transition patterns.
- Strength: training with a task-appropriate end-to-end ranking objective likely contributes to improved performance.
- Strength: practical, simple feature design that composes entity features with grammatical roles and avoids external corpora.
- Strength: reproducibility support via use of public toolkits, significance testing, and code release.
- Weakness: limited conceptual novelty beyond substituting discrete transition features with embeddings and CNNs.
- Weakness: insufficient justification for large embedding dimensionality and long convolution windows; lacking ablations on embedding size, window length, and filter count.
- Weakness: poor reproduction of Li & Hovy (2014) baseline (19.34% discrimination) suggests possible implementation or setup mismatch that undermines comparisons.
- Weakness: task mismatch for insertion — model trained on permutation discrimination may not suit insertion, limiting gains; needs insertion-specific training or harder negatives.
- Weakness: exposition and organization issues, including repeated related-work coverage, minor language errors, and a figure reference error.
- Question: provide ablations on embedding sizes (e.g., 20/50/100/200), number of filters, and convolution window length k to show saturation points.
- Question: do discrimination results hold when controlling for document length (e.g., binning by sentence count) or on shorter documents?
- Question: have you tried insertion-specific training (near-miss negatives, curriculum learning, triplet losses) and any preliminary results?
- Question: how sensitive is the model to coreference errors; has it been evaluated with different coreference systems or gold coreference?
- Question: for DUC transfer, what components are transferred from WSJ (embeddings, filters, full network), and which layers are fine-tuned; provide learning rates and early stopping details.
- Question: detail preprocessing, tokenization, sentence splitting, vocabulary, training data, and hyperparameters for the Li & Hovy baseline and confirm matching of original evaluation protocol.
- Minor: fix language issues and missing articles (e.g., “the WSJ corpus,” “the Brown coherence toolkit”).
- Minor: correct figure reference on p.1 bottom-left (“Figure 2” → “Figure 1”).
- Minor: stylistic suggestions (prefer “First, Second” to “Firstly, Secondly”; rephrase some sentences for clarity).
- Minor: restructure to consolidate related work and shorten redundant paper-organization paragraph.
- Minor: ensure consistent citation formatting and fix placeholders (e.g., “Barzilay and Lapata.,? 2005”).
