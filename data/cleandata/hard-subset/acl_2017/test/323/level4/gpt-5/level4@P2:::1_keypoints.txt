- Paper extends the entity-grid model with a CNN over distributed entity-grid representations to learn longer local coherence patterns.
- Trained end-to-end with a pairwise ranking objective and supports symbol augmentation for entity-specific features (e.g., PERSON-S).
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on three coherence tasks: discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated incremental improvement that retains interpretability of the entity-grid while addressing sparsity and feature limitations.
- Strength: Strong empirical gains over non-neural baselines, with additional benefits from entity features and WSJ pretraining.
- Strength: Methodological clarity—architecture, training, hyperparameter search, significance testing are well described and code is available.
- Strength: Effective use of distributed representations and convolution to model longer transitions and mitigate curse of dimensionality.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of the surprisingly low performance of Li and Hovy (2014) on the authors’ setup; raises concerns about experimental alignment and implementation differences.
- Weakness: Domain sensitivity not explored—no tests on datasets/settings where Li and Hovy’s model is known to perform well or matched-preprocessing comparisons.
- Weakness: Writing issues—frequent missing definite articles and awkward repeated phrasing (“limits the model to do X”).

- Question: Provide exact details of Li & Hovy code/version, preprocessing, embeddings, window size, negative sampling, optimizer, learning rates, early stopping, and evaluation protocol used.
- Question: Were you able to reproduce Li & Hovy’s reported results on their original datasets, and what diagnostics were run if not?
- Question: Can you provide a controlled comparison where both models use matched preprocessing and training on WSJ?
- Question: Report ablations on transition window size k, pooling size, number of filters, and effects of each entity-specific feature.
- Question: How do you handle vocabulary growth when concatenating entity features with roles (e.g., PERSON-S), what is the OOV strategy, and how does it affect sparsity and embedding size?
- Question: Have you evaluated your model on AIRPLANES/EARTHQUAKES or Li & Hovy’s datasets, and can you run Li & Hovy’s model on your WSJ setting with aligned preprocessing?

- Minor: Proofread for article usage and replace “limits the model to do X” with clearer alternatives (“limits the model’s capacity to X” or “prevents the model from doing X”).

- Final recommendation: Score adjusted to 4 after author response; document efforts and obstacles running Li & Hovy’s system and, if feasible, attempt to reproduce their original results as a sanity check.
