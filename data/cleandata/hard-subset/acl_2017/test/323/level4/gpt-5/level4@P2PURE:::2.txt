The paper presents a convolutional neural network (CNN) approach to modeling text coherence. It builds on the well-known entity grid representation and places a CNN on top of it. The approach is well motivated and clearly described; I especially appreciate the explicit discussion of the intuitions behind key design choices (e.g., why use a CNN and the section titled “Why it works”).

The evaluation is extensive across several tasks and shows that the proposed method outperforms prior approaches. However, it is odd that one prior result could not be replicated: the Li and Hovy (2014) findings. This suggests a possible implementation or modeling error that should be investigated and addressed.

That said, the model remains a relatively straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid. I do not see why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to encode a four-dimensional grid entry (or slightly more in the extended grid). How does this help? While optimizing directly for coherence ranking likely improves performance, the difference between modeling transition chains up to k=3 sentences versus k=6 may not be substantial, especially given that many WSJ articles are quite short.

The writing is somewhat lengthy and repeats material in several places—for instance, the introduction to entity grids appears multiple times. Since Section 2 already covers related work, the first two-thirds of Section 6 largely repeat earlier content and should be deleted or merged into Section 2 where appropriate. The remainder of Section 6 should also be moved under Section 2 as a subsection, and Section 2 should be retitled “Related Work.”

Overall, this is a solid application of a neural network to an entity-grid-based coherence model. However, given the paper’s aim to consolidate prior work, I would expect more from a full paper—e.g., innovations in the representation (additional features?) or new tasks.
