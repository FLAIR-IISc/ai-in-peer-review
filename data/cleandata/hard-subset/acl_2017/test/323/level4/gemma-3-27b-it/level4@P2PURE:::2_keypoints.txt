- Introduces a CNN-based model for text coherence grounded in the entity grid representation with clear motivation and strong explanations (including "Why it works").  
- Model is thoroughly evaluated across multiple tasks and shows performance gains over existing methods.  
- Failed to reproduce a prior result on Li/Hovy (2014), indicating a possible implementation or modeling error that needs investigation.  
- The approach is a straightforward application of neural networks to entity grids, lacking major methodological novelty.  
- The choice to use 100-dimensional vectors for each grid entry is not justified and its benefit is unclear.  
- Increasing transition chain length from k=3 to k=6 may have limited impact given short WSJ articles.  
- Paper contains redundant and repetitive writing; section 6 largely duplicates section 2 and should be removed or integrated into a renamed “Related Work.”  
- A full paper should offer more substantial contributions (e.g., richer representation learning or new tasks) beyond consolidating prior work.  
- Recommend proofreading for English (missing articles) and minor editorial fixes (Figure numbering, “Firstly/Secondly” phrasing, rewording “limits the model to”, remove unnecessary concluding paragraph).
