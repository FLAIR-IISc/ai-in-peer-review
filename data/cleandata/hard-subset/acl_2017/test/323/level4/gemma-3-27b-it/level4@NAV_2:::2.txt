The paper proposes a convolutional neural network approach to model the coherence of texts. The model is based on the well-known entity grid representation for coherence, but employs a CNN on top of it. 
The approach is well motivated and described, and I particularly appreciate the clear discussion of the intuitions behind certain design decisions (e.g., why a CNN is suitable, and the section titled 'Why it works').
The evaluation is extensive, across several tasks, and demonstrates that the proposed approach outperforms previous methods. However, the inability to reproduce results from Li and Hovy (2014) is concerning and should be investigated – it suggests a potential implementation or modelling error.
The model represents a relatively straightforward 'neuralization' of the entity grid model. It is unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or a few more in the case of the extended grid); the benefit of this dimensionality is not fully justified. While end-to-end optimization for coherence ranking is valuable, the impact of extending transition chains from k=3 to k=6 sentences may be limited, especially given the potential for shorter articles within the WSJ corpus.
The writing is somewhat verbose, with repeated information. For instance, the introduction to entity grids is reiterated in multiple places. Specifically, section 2 also presents related work, and the first two-thirds of section 6 is redundant and should be removed (or integrated into section 2 where appropriate). The remainder of section 6 could be added to section 2 as a subsection, prompting a renaming of section 2 to 'Related Work'.
Overall, this is a solid implementation of applying a neural network model to entity-grid-based coherence. However, given the consolidation of prior work, I would anticipate greater innovation in a full paper – potentially through novel representations (incorporating additional features) or evaluation on more challenging tasks.
Minor points:
- The paper would benefit from proofreading by a native speaker, as there are missing articles in several places (e.g., 'the WSJ corpus' (2x), 'the Brown... toolkit' (2x)).
- p.1, bottom left column: 'Figure 2' should be 'Figure 1'.
- p.1: Replace 'Firstly/Secondly' with 'First, Second'.
- p.1: Consider replacing 'limits the model to' with 'prevents the model from considering...'.
- Consider removing the standard concluding paragraph in section 1, as it is unnecessary for a paper of this length.
