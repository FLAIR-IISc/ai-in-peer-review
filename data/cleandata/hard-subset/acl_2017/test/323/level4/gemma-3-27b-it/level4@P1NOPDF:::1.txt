The paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions, capturing coherence and enabling better generalization over longer entity sequences than the direct estimation of transition probabilities in the original model. This is a well-written and clear paper. Rather than proposing a fully neural approach, the authors build upon existing work, strategically employing a neural network to address specific limitations in one component. This is a valid and sensible approach, but it would be beneficial to expand the comparison to include the neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors acknowledge being surprised by the very low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task. This raises questions for the reader about potential errors in the experimental setup, or whether the other model's poor performance is specific to this corpus. Furthermore, it would be valuable to understand how the model proposed in this paper would perform on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy model demonstrates greater success. A more in-depth investigation of these factors would significantly strengthen the paper’s argument.

Overall, the paper is fluent and readable, although it contains some instances of missing definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, and 764, and likely elsewhere). I recommend careful proofreading, specifically focusing on the correct usage of articles. The phrase “…limits the model to do X…”, which appears repeatedly, sounds somewhat awkward. Alternatives such as “limits the model’s ability to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” might be clearer.

The effort made to test the Li and Hovy system, and the challenges encountered, should be documented within the paper. I also suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless this has already been done) to confirm their implementation and results.
