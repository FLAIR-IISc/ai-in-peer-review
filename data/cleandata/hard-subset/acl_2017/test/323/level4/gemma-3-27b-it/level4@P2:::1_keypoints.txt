- Proposes a neural extension of the entity grid using CNNs to model coherence with distributed representations and end-to-end learning.  
- Claims ability to capture longer-range entity transitions and learn task-specific features, overcoming discrete representation limits.  
- Evaluated on discrimination, insertion, and summary coherence rating tasks and reports state-of-the-art performance.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical gains: up to ~4% on discrimination, ~2.5% on insertion, and >4% on summary coherence rating.  
- Effective integration of CNNs with entity grids to learn hierarchical, long-range coherence patterns.  
- End-to-end training reduces manual feature engineering and learns task-specific features automatically.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with helpful architectural diagram.  
- Code release promotes reproducibility and further research.  

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient comparison to recent neural models (Li & Hovy 2014) and lack of investigation into reproduction issues.  
- Minor linguistic/editing issues (missing articles, awkward phrasing) reduce polish.  
- No analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity or robustness to different settings.  
- Limited exploration of a broader set of entity-specific features in experiments.  

Questions for authors:
- What specific challenges and modifications occurred when reproducing Li & Hovy (2014), and why rule out implementation errors?  
- Were ablation studies performed to isolate contributions of convolutional layers versus the entity grid representation?  
- What are the training and inference computational costs relative to traditional entity grid models?  

Additional recommendations:
- Attempt reproducing Li & Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check.  
- Document challenges encountered running Li & Hovy’s system in the paper.  
- Proofread to fix minor linguistic and phrasing issues.
