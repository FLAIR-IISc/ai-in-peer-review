- Proposes a CNN extension of the entity grid to learn entity-transition sequences, improving coherence modeling and generalization over longer sequences.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and uses a targeted neural component rather than an end-to-end neural approach.
- The approach is sensible but needs a more comprehensive comparison to Li and Hovy’s neural coherence model.
- The authors report surprisingly low scores for Li and Hovy’s model, raising concerns about possible experimental setup errors or corpus-dependent performance.
- Recommend further investigation into why Li and Hovy’s model performed poorly and evaluation on corpora/tasks where it performs well.
- Document the efforts and problems encountered when running Li and Hovy’s code within the paper.
- Suggest attempting to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results on their datasets as a sanity check.
- Many instances of missing definite articles; advise a thorough proofreading pass focused on article usage.
- Repeated awkward phrasing (“limits the model to do X”); recommend clearer alternatives like “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X.”
- Final recommendation adjusted to a score of 4 after author response; do not penalize authors for difficulties running others’ software but require documentation of those difficulties.
