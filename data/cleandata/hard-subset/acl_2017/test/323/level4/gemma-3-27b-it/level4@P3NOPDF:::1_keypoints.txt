- Proposes a CNN extension of the entity grid to learn entity-transition sequences, improving generalization to longer sequences versus the original transition-probability approach.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and the focused approach (neural component for a specific step rather than a fully neural overhaul) is reasonable and effective.  
- Lacks a comprehensive comparison to existing neural coherence models, particularly Li and Hovy’s model.  
- Authors report surprisingly low performance for Li and Hovy’s model, raising concerns about possible experimental setup differences or corpus dependence.  
- Recommend investigating whether Li and Hovy’s poor results are corpus/task-specific and reporting performance on datasets where their model performs well.  
- Advise attempting to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results as a sanity check to validate generalizability.  
- The paper should explicitly document the authors’ efforts and difficulties reproducing the Li and Hovy system to improve transparency and reproducibility.  
- Minor editing needed: fix frequent definite-article omissions and rephrase awkward constructions like “limits the model to do X” (use “limits the model’s ability to…” or “prevents the model from…”).  
- Final recommendation adjusted to 4, contingent on addressing comparison, reproduction, and documentation concerns.
