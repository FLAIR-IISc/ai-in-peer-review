- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid representation.  
- Approach is well motivated with clear discussion of intuitions (e.g., why a CNN; "Why it works" section).  
- Evaluation is extensive across multiple tasks and shows improvement over prior methods.  
- Failure to reproduce results from Li and Hovy (2014) is concerning and suggests possible implementation or modeling errors that must be investigated.  
- Model is a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid and lacks strong representational novelty.  
- Unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are needed to represent 4-dimensional grid entries; dimensionality benefits are not justified.  
- Extending transition chains from k=3 to k=6 may have limited impact, especially given short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive; entity-grid introduction is reiterated in multiple places.  
- Section organization is redundant: section 2 also contains related work; first two-thirds of section 6 is redundant and should be removed or integrated into section 2, renaming it "Related Work."  
- Overall a solid implementation but would benefit from greater innovation (novel representations, additional features) and evaluation on more challenging tasks.  
- Minor proofreading issues and missing articles (e.g., "the WSJ corpus", "the Brown... toolkit").  
- Specific editorial corrections: p.1 bottom left column "Figure 2" should be "Figure 1".  
- Style suggestions: replace "Firstly/Secondly" with "First, Second" and "limits the model to" with "prevents the model from considering...".  
- Suggest removing the standard concluding paragraph in section 1 as unnecessary for a short paper.
