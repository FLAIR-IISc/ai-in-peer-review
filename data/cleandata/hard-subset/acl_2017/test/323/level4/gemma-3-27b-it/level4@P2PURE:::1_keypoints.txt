- Paper extends the entity grid with a convolutional neural network to learn entity-transition sequences, improving generalization over longer sequences versus the original transition-probability approach.  
- The manuscript is well-written and presents a pragmatic hybrid approach, using neural nets to address specific limitations rather than adopting a fully neural architecture.  
- The paper lacks a thorough comparison with the Li and Hovy neural coherence model.  
- The authors’ surprise at the Li and Hovy model’s low performance raises concerns about possible experimental setup errors or corpus-dependent results.  
- The authors should test whether their model behaves similarly on datasets/tasks where the Li and Hovy model performs well to strengthen their claims.  
- The paper contains numerous missing definite articles; careful proofreading focused on article usage is recommended.  
- The recurring phrasing “…limits the model to do X…” is awkward; alternatives like “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” are suggested.  
- Final recommendation was adjusted to 4 after the author response, acknowledging that difficulties running external software should not be held against the authors.  
- The authors should document their efforts and challenges running the Li and Hovy system and attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy’s results on the original datasets to verify replicability.
