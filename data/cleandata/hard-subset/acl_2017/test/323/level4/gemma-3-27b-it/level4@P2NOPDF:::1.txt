This paper presents an extension to the entity grid model, leveraging a convolutional neural network to learn entity transition sequences and model coherence. This allows for improved generalization to longer entity sequences compared to the original model’s direct estimation of transition probabilities. Overall, this is a well-written and clear paper. The authors demonstrate a pragmatic approach by building upon existing work and employing a neural network to address a specific limitation, rather than advocating for a fully neural solution. 

However, a more comprehensive comparison to existing neural coherence models, specifically that of Li and Hovy, would strengthen the contribution. The authors note their surprise at the comparatively low performance of the Li and Hovy model in their experiments. This observation raises questions regarding potential issues with the experimental setup or the corpus-dependence of the results. Investigating whether the proposed model’s performance differs on datasets where the Li and Hovy model performs better would provide valuable insight and bolster the paper’s argument. A deeper analysis of these factors is recommended.

The paper is generally very fluent and readable, but a careful proofread focusing on definite article usage is suggested. Definite articles are frequently omitted throughout the text (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely elsewhere). Additionally, the phrasing “…limits the model to do X…” appears repeatedly and could be improved for clarity. Alternatives such as “…limits the model’s capacity to do X…” or “…prevents the model from doing X…” might be more natural.

(Post-rebuttal) My final recommendation is adjusted to 4. I agree that difficulties encountered when attempting to run others’ software should not negatively impact the evaluation of this work. However, the challenges faced when implementing the Li and Hovy system should be explicitly documented within the paper. Furthermore, I suggest the authors attempt to reproduce the Li and Hovy results on their original datasets as a validation step, if feasible, to ensure consistency and provide a further point of comparison.
