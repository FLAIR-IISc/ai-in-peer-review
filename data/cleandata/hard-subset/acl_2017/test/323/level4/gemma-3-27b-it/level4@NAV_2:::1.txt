The paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions indicating coherence, permitting better generalization over longer sequences of entities than the direct estimates of transition probabilities in the original model. This is a well-written paper that presents a sensible and incremental advance to existing coherence modeling techniques. Rather than advocating for a fully neural approach, the authors judiciously leverage neural networks to address specific limitations within the established entity grid framework – a valid and pragmatic design choice. However, a more comprehensive comparison to existing neural coherence models, such as that of Li and Hovy, would be beneficial. The authors note unexpected low performance from the Li and Hovy model, raising questions about potential experimental setup issues or corpus sensitivity. A deeper investigation of these factors would significantly strengthen the paper’s contribution. 

The paper is generally fluent and readable, but contains numerous instances of missing definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely others). Thorough proofreading with specific attention to article usage is recommended. The phrasing “…limits the model to do X…” appears repeatedly and sounds slightly awkward; alternatives such as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” may be clearer.

**Questions to Authors:**

*   Could you elaborate on the difficulties encountered when attempting to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy (2014)?
*   Have you considered evaluating your model on the original datasets used by Li and Hovy (2014) as a sanity check?
