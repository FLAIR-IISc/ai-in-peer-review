The paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions, capturing coherence and permitting better generalization over longer sequences of entities than the direct estimates of transition probabilities in the original model. This is a well-written and clear paper. Rather than proposing a fully neural approach, the authors build upon existing work, strategically employing a neural network to address specific limitations in one step of the process. This is a valid and sensible approach, but a more comprehensive comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy would be beneficial. The authors acknowledge being surprised by the very low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task. This raises questions regarding potential errors in the experimental setup or whether the observed performance is corpus-dependent. Further investigation into these factors – and a presentation of the model proposed in this paper’s performance on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy model performs more strongly – would significantly strengthen the argument. 

In general, the paper is fluent and readable, but it contains numerous instances of missing definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, and 764, and likely others). I recommend a thorough proofreading pass specifically focused on article usage. Additionally, the phrasing “…limits the model to do X…” which appears repeatedly, sounds somewhat awkward. Alternatives such as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” would likely be clearer.

--------------
Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running other people’s software should not be held against the present authors. However, the efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, and the problems encountered during this process, should be documented within the paper. I also suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless this has already been done), to confirm the reproducibility of those results.
