The paper proposes a convolutional neural network approach to model the coherence of texts. The model is based on the well-known entity grid representation for coherence, but incorporates a CNN on top of it. 
The approach is well motivated and described; I especially appreciate the clear discussion of the intuitions behind certain design decisions (e.g., why a CNN is used, and the section titled 'Why it works').

There is an extensive evaluation on several tasks, which demonstrates that the proposed approach outperforms previous methods. However, the inability to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy (2014) is concerning and suggests a potential implementation or modeling error that should be addressed.

Still, the model is a relatively simple ‘neuralization’ of the entity grid model. I am unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or a few more in the case of the extended grid). While I understand that optimizing directly for coherence ranking can help learn a better model, the benefit of representing transitions for up to k=6 sentences versus k=3 is not entirely convincing, especially given that many WSJ articles may be short.

The writing is somewhat lengthy, with certain parts repeated in multiple places, for example, the introduction to entity grids. In particular, section 2 also presents related work, so the first two-thirds of section 6 is redundant and should be deleted (or integrated into section 2 where appropriate). The remainder of section 6 could be added to section 2 as a subsection (then rename section 2 as “Related Work”).

Overall, this is a solid implementation of applying a neural network model to entity-grid-based coherence. However, given the consolidation of previous work, I would expect more from a full paper – such as innovations in the representations (e.g., incorporating additional features) or the tasks addressed.

Minor points:

*   This paper would benefit from proofreading by a native speaker; articles are missing in many places (e.g., “the WSJ corpus” (2x), “the Brown coherence toolkit” (2x)).
*   p.1, bottom left column: ‘Figure 2’ should be ‘Figure 1’.
*   p.1: Replace “Firstly/Secondly” with “First, Second”.
*   p.1: Replace “limits the model to” with “prevents the model from considering”.
*   Consider removing the standard concluding paragraph in section 1, as it is not necessary for a concise paper.

**Questions to Authors:**

*   Could you elaborate on the reasoning behind the choice of 100-dimensional embeddings for the grid entries? What benefits are gained from using such a high-dimensional representation?
*   Please address the discrepancy in reproducing the results of Li and Hovy (2014). What steps have you taken to ensure the correctness of your implementation?
*   Could you provide some insights on the impact of increasing the transition length (k) from 3 to 6? Did you observe a significant performance gain, and if so, on which tasks?
