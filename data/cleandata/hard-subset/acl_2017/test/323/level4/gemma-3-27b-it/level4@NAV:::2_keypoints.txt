- Proposes a CNN applied to the entity grid for text coherence; approach is well-justified and clearly explained (notably the “Why it works” section).  
- Extensive evaluation shows performance gains over existing methods.  
- Unable to replicate Li and Hovy (2014) result — suggests a possible implementation or modeling issue that needs investigation.  
- Method is a straightforward application of neural nets to the entity grid; novelty is limited.  
- Justification for using 100-dimensional vectors per grid entry is unclear and benefits are not demonstrated.  
- Directly optimizing for coherence ranking is a promising direction.  
- Varying transition chain length (k=3 vs k=6) may have limited practical impact given the short length of many WSJ articles.  
- Paper is verbose and repetitive; the entity-grid introduction is duplicated and much of section 6 repeats section 2.  
- Recommend consolidating material into an expanded Related Work and moving the remainder of section 6 as a subsection (remove the redundant section-1 concluding paragraph).  
- Overall a sound implementation but a full paper would benefit from more innovation (e.g., alternative representations or additional evaluation tasks).  
- Minor edits needed: native-English proofreading for articles, fix “Figure 2” → “Figure 1,” replace “Firstly/Secondly,” rephrase “limits the model to,” and remove unnecessary concluding paragraph in section 1.
