The paper proposes a convolutional neural network approach to model text coherence, building upon the well-established entity grid representation. This approach is well motivated and clearly described; I particularly appreciate the detailed discussion of the design choices and the intuitions behind them (e.g., the rationale for using a CNN and the section titled ‘Why it works’).

The model is evaluated extensively on several tasks, demonstrating performance superior to previous methods. However, the inability to reproduce a prior result is concerning; the findings on Li & Hovy (2014) suggest a potential implementation or modeling error that requires investigation. 

Despite strong results, the model represents a relatively straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid model. I question the necessity of representing each four-dimensional (or slightly larger, in the extended grid) entry with 100-dimensional vectors. The benefit of this increased dimensionality is unclear. While optimizing directly for coherence ranking is a sensible approach for learning a robust model, the significance of expanding transition chains from *k*=3 to *k*=6 sentences seems potentially limited, particularly given the frequent brevity of articles in the WSJ corpus.

The writing is somewhat verbose, with noticeable repetition, particularly in the introduction to entity grids. Specifically, Section 6 currently duplicates material presented in Section 2; the first two-thirds of Section 6 should be deleted or integrated into Section 2 as relevant related work. The remaining portion of Section 6 would be better suited as a subsection within an expanded Section 2 (renamed “Related Work”).

Overall, this appears to be a solid implementation of a neural network model applied to entity-grid-based coherence modeling. However, given the consolidation of prior work presented, I would expect a full paper to offer greater innovation, perhaps through enhancements to the representations (e.g., incorporating additional features) or exploring new tasks.

Minor points:

- p.1, bottom left column: ‘Figure 2’ should be ‘Figure 1’.
- p.1: Replace ‘Firstly/Secondly’ with ‘First, Second’.
- p.1: Consider rephrasing ‘limits the model to’ as ‘prevents the model from considering …’.
- Consider removing the standard concluding paragraph in Section 1, as it is unnecessary for a paper of this length.
