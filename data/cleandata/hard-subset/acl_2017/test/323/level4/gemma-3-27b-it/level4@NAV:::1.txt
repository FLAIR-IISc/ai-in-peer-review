The paper presents an extension to the entity grid model, leveraging a convolutional neural network to model entity transitions and capture coherence. This approach enables improved generalization to longer entity sequences compared to the original model’s direct transition probability estimation. This is a well-written and valuable contribution. The authors wisely choose to augment existing methods with a neural network component, specifically targeting and addressing limitations within a defined step of the process, rather than pursuing a complete neural implementation. However, a more comprehensive comparison with the neural coherence model proposed by Li and Hovy would be beneficial. The surprisingly low performance observed for the Li and Hovy model on this task raises questions regarding potential experimental errors or corpus sensitivity. Investigating whether the proposed model’s performance holds on datasets where Li and Hovy’s model performs well would significantly strengthen the paper’s claims.

The writing is generally clear and accessible, but a careful review for missing definite articles is recommended (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and potentially others). Additionally, the recurring phrase “…limits the model to do X…” appears awkward; consider alternatives such as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” for enhanced clarity.

Furthermore, I suggest the authors attempt to reproduce the Li and Hovy results on their original datasets as a validation step, if not already performed.
