The paper presents an extension to the entity grid model, employing a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions and thereby model coherence. This approach allows for improved generalization over longer entity sequences compared to the original model’s direct estimation of transition probabilities. This is a well-written and compelling paper. Rather than advocating for a completely neural architecture, the authors strategically leverage a neural network to address specific limitations within an existing framework – a valid and pragmatic approach. However, a more thorough comparison with the neural coherence model proposed by Li and Hovy would be beneficial. The authors note their surprise at the low performance of the Li and Hovy model on their task, which raises questions regarding potential errors in the experimental setup or the corpus-dependence of the observed results. Investigating whether the proposed model would perform similarly on a corpus or task where the Li and Hovy model excels would substantially strengthen the paper’s arguments. 

Overall, the paper is fluent and readable, but it contains numerous instances of missing definite articles (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely elsewhere). Careful proofreading with specific attention to article usage is recommended. Furthermore, the recurring phrase “…limits the model to do X…” is somewhat awkward; alternatives such as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” may be clearer.
