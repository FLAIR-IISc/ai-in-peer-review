The paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model. A convolutional neural network is used to learn sequences of entity transitions indicating coherence, permitting better generalization over longer sequences of entities than the direct estimates of transition probabilities in the original model. This is a well-written and clear paper. Instead of proposing a fully neural approach, the authors build upon existing work and use a neural network to address specific limitations in one step. This is a valid approach, but it would be beneficial to expand the comparison to include the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors acknowledge being surprised by the low score the Li and Hovy model achieved on their task. This raises questions about potential errors in the experimental setup, whether the other model’s low performance is corpus-dependent, and, if so, what results the model proposed in this paper would achieve on a corpus or task where the other model performs better. A more in-depth investigation of these factors would considerably strengthen the argument.

In general, the paper is fluent and readable, but definite articles are frequently missing (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely elsewhere). I suggest proofreading the paper specifically with article usage in mind. The expression “…limits the model to do X…”, which is used repeatedly, sounds somewhat unnatural. Phrasing it as “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or “prevents the model from doing X” might be clearer.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties running other people's software shouldn't be held against the present authors. The efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, and the problems encountered in doing so, should be documented in the paper. I would also suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless they have already done so), simply to verify if that works for them.
