- Proposes a CNN applied to entity-grid representations for text coherence with clear motivation and strong "why it works" explanation.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art empirical results across coherence tasks, notably on insertion and summary coherence rating.  
- Provides a thorough evaluation using established datasets and comparisons to a wide range of baselines.  
- Releases code, aiding reproducibility and future work.  
- Cannot reproduce results from Li & Hovy (2014), indicating a concerning discrepancy or implementation issue.  
- Primary contribution: end-to-end CNN learning of coherence from entity grids that captures long-range entity transitions.  
- Secondary contribution: comprehensive empirical baseline for neural coherence modeling.  
- Limited architectural novelty: straightforward CNN and simple concatenation of entity features, with little exploration of richer integrations.  
- The choice of 100-dimensional embeddings for small input spaces is not well justified.  
- Paper contains redundancy and organization issues (Section 6 duplicates Section 2); structure should be streamlined.  
- Writing needs proofreading (missing articles and phrasing issues reduce polish).  
- Request for authors to justify embedding dimensionality and report experiments with lower dimensions.  
- Request for detailed explanation and implementation/settings to resolve Li & Hovy reproduction discrepancy.  
- Request for comparison of computational cost versus traditional entity-grid models.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: fix figure reference, adjust phrasing (Firstly/Secondly → First, Second), rephrase certain sentences, and remove an unnecessary closing paragraph.
