- Proposes a well-motivated CNN model for text coherence built on the entity grid; design choices and intuition are clearly explained (including “Why it works”).
- Extensive evaluation shows performance superior to prior methods on several tasks.
- Failure to reproduce Li & Hovy (2014) is concerning and suggests a potential implementation or modeling error that requires investigation.
- The model is largely a straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid and may lack sufficient novelty for a full paper.
- Unclear necessity/benefit of using 100-dimensional vectors to represent each four-dimensional (or slightly larger) grid entry.
- Expanding transition chains from k=3 to k=6 may have limited impact given the short length of many WSJ articles.
- Writing is verbose and repetitive; Section 6 duplicates Section 2 — first two-thirds of Section 6 should be removed or integrated, and the remainder moved under a Related Work section.
- Recommend greater innovation for a full paper (e.g., enhanced representations or new tasks).
- Minor issues: frequent missing articles (proofreading needed), incorrect figure reference (Figure 2→Figure 1), replace “Firstly/Secondly” with “First, Second”, rephrase “limits the model to…”, and consider removing the standard concluding paragraph in Section 1.
