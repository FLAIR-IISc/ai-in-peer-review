- Proposes a CNN-based model on the entity grid representation for text coherence.
- Approach is well motivated and clearly described, with a strong "Why it Works" section.
- Presents extensive evaluation across tasks and reports outperforming existing methods.
- Cannot reproduce results from Li and Hovy (2014), indicating a possible implementation or modeling error that must be addressed.
- Model is essentially a straightforward neuralization of the entity grid and lacks substantial novelty.
- Unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are used for each grid entry and what benefit the high dimensionality provides.
- Questionable impact of using transition chains up to k=6 versus k=3, given many WSJ articles are short.
- Directly optimizing for coherence ranking is likely to improve performance and should be considered.
- Writing is verbose and repetitive, especially with redundant introductions to entity grids.
- Section 6 largely repeats section 2 and should be deleted or merged into section 2, renaming it “Related Work.”
- Expected greater contribution for a full paper, such as novel representations (additional features) or new tasks.
- Minor issues: proofreading needed (missing articles), incorrect figure reference, phrasing suggestions, and removal of an unnecessary concluding paragraph.
