- Proposes a CNN-based model on top of the entity grid to model text coherence.  
- Approach is well motivated with clear discussion of design intuitions (e.g., why CNN, “Why it works”).  
- Extensive evaluation shows the method outperforms previous approaches.  
- Failure to reproduce Li and Hovy (2014) results raises concerns about implementation or modeling errors.  
- Model is essentially a straightforward “neuralization” of the entity grid without notable representational innovations.  
- Questionable necessity of 100-dimensional embeddings for representing a small discrete set of grid entries.  
- Benefit of modeling transitions up to k=6 versus k=3 is not convincingly justified, especially for short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is verbose and repetitive (redundant related work/introduction material); suggest consolidating sections 2 and 6.  
- Overall a solid implementation but the full paper should offer more novelty (new representations or tasks).  
- Minor editorial issues: non-native phrasing/articles missing, figure reference error, suggested wording changes, and an unnecessary concluding paragraph.  
- Question to authors: justify the choice and benefit of 100-dimensional embeddings for grid entries.  
- Question to authors: explain steps taken to resolve the discrepancy reproducing Li and Hovy (2014).  
- Question to authors: report the impact of increasing transition length k from 3 to 6 and which tasks show gains.
