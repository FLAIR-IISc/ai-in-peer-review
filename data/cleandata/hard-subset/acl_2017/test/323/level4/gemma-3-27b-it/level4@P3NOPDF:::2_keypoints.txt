- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid; motivation is clear and model description is strong.
- Appreciates thorough discussion of design choices and the “Why it Works” explanation.
- Evaluation is extensive across coherence ranking tasks and shows performance gains over prior methods.
- Unable to reproduce reported results from Li and Hovy (2014), suggesting a possible implementation or modeling error that needs investigation.
- Questions use of 100-dimensional vectors for each grid entry; paper lacks justification that high dimensionality is superior to lower-dimensional or alternative representations.
- Varying transition chain length from k=3 to k=6 is of limited practical significance given the brevity of many WSJ articles.
- Paper presentation is occasionally verbose and repeats the introduction to entity grids unnecessarily.
- Section 6 duplicates material from Section 2; recommends consolidating most of Section 6 into Section 2 and making the remainder a subsection to improve flow.
- Overall, a solid neuralization of the entity-grid framework but the full paper’s contribution feels limited given the comprehensive related work coverage.
- Suggests future work exploring richer feature representations beyond entity grids and applying the model to a broader range of coherence tasks.
- Minor proofreading issues: missing articles and English phrasing errors throughout.
- Specific editorial fixes: “Figure 2” should be “Figure 1”; replace “Firstly/Secondly” with “First, Second”; rephrase “limits the model to” for clarity.
- Recommends removing the standard concluding paragraph from Section 1 as unnecessary.
