- Proposes a convolutional neural network built on the entity grid to model text coherence.  
- Authors argue design choices clearly, including a "Why it works" section explaining intuitions.  
- Contribution: neural architecture capturing long-range entity transitions and arbitrary entity features.  
- Contribution: end-to-end training to automatically learn task-specific high-level features.  
- Contribution: state-of-the-art results on three coherence assessment tasks.  
- Strength: well-motivated hybrid of entity grid models and neural networks.  
- Strength: extensive evaluation across several tasks demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation of model and results.  
- Weakness: model is a simple "neuralization" of the entity grid, novelty may be limited.  
- Weakness: unclear necessity/effectiveness of using 100-dimensional vectors for four-dimensional grid entries.  
- Weakness: repetitive content (e.g., entity grid introductions) and writing could be more concise and polished.  
- Suggested fix: justify the 100-dimensional vector choice and explore alternative features/representations.  
- Suggested fix: revise to remove repetition and improve clarity and concision.  
- Questions: please justify 100-dimensional vectors; have other features/representations been explored; can the paper be revised for conciseness?
