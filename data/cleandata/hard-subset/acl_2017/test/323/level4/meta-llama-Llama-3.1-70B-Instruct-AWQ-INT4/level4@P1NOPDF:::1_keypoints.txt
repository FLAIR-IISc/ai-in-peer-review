- Paper extends the entity grid model using a convolutional neural network to learn entity-transition sequences indicating coherence.
- The CNN approach allows better generalization over longer entity sequences than direct transition-probability estimates.
- The paper is well-written and builds validly on prior work, addressing issues in a single, focused step.
- The comparison to Li and Hovy’s neural coherence model is insufficient and should be expanded.
- The authors report a surprisingly low score for Li and Hovy’s model, suggesting possible experimental-setup errors.
- It’s unclear whether the low performance of Li and Hovy’s model is corpus-dependent; testing on datasets where that model succeeds is needed.
- Authors should attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results as a sanity check (unless already done).
- Document the efforts and objective difficulties encountered when running Li and Hovy’s code in the paper.
- Paper contains recurring definite-article errors and awkward phrasing (e.g., “limits the model to do X”); recommend proofreading and rephrasing.
- Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after author response, acknowledging that implementation difficulties shouldn’t penalize the authors.
