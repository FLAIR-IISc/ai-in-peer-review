The paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) approach to model the coherence of texts, building upon the well-known entity grid representation. The authors provide a clear and well-motivated description of their approach, and the discussion of the intuitions behind certain design decisions (e.g., the choice of CNN and the section titled 'Why it works') is particularly appreciated.

The paper presents an extensive evaluation on several tasks, demonstrating that the proposed approach outperforms previous methods. However, the failure to reproduce one previous result (Li/Hovy, 2014) raises concerns about potential implementation or modeling errors that should be addressed.

While the model represents a solid 'neuralization' of the entity grid model, it is relatively simple and does not introduce significant innovations. Specifically, it is unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or a few more in the case of the extended grid). The benefits of this design choice are not explicitly explained, and it would be helpful to provide more insight into how this representation contributes to the model's performance.

Additionally, the difference in performance between transition chains for up to k=3 sentences versus k=6 sentences may not be substantial, especially considering that many WSJ articles are relatively short. It would be beneficial to provide more analysis on the impact of this design choice.

The writing could be improved for conciseness, as certain parts are repeated throughout the paper. For instance, the introduction to entity grids is repeated in multiple places. Section 6, in particular, repeats content from Section 2, and the first two-thirds of Section 6 could be removed or integrated into Section 2 where necessary. The remaining content of Section 6 could be added to Section 2 under a subsection, and the section title could be revised to 'Related Work'.

Overall, this paper presents a solid implementation of applying a neural network model to entity-grid-based coherence. However, considering the proposed consolidation of previous work, more innovations in representations (e.g., additional features) or tasks would be expected from a full paper.

Minor points:

- The paper would benefit from proof-reading by a native speaker, as there are several instances of missing articles (e.g., 'the WSJ corpus' and 'the Brown toolkit').
- On page 1, bottom left column, 'Figure 2' should be corrected to 'Figure 1'.
- On page 1, 'Firstly' and 'Secondly' could be rephrased as 'First' and 'Second' for improved clarity.
- On page 1, 'limits the model to' could be rephrased as 'prevents the model from considering' for better accuracy.
- Consider removing the final paragraph in Section 1, as it is not necessary for a paper of this length.
