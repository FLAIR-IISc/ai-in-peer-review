The paper presents an extension of the entity grid model, incorporating a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach allows for better generalization over longer sequences of entities compared to the direct estimation of transition probabilities in the original model.

The paper is well-written, and the authors' decision to build upon existing work by utilizing a neural network to address specific issues is a valid approach. However, it would be beneficial to expand the comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors' admission of being surprised by the low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task raises questions about potential errors in the experimental setup, corpus-dependent performance, and the proposed model's performance on a corpus or task where the other model is more successful. A deeper investigation into these factors would significantly strengthen the argument.

The paper's overall fluency and readability are notable, but there are instances where definite articles are missing (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, and 764). It is recommended that the authors proofread the paper with a focus on article usage. Additionally, the repeated expression "...limits the model to do X..." sounds somewhat unusual; rephrasing it to "...limits the model's capacity to do X..." or "...stops the model from doing X..." might improve clarity.

In light of the author response, I adjusted my final recommendation to 4. I concur that difficulties in running other people's software should not be held against the present authors. The efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, along with the encountered problems, should be documented in the paper. I suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original data sets as a sanity check, if they have not already done so, to verify the results.
