- Summary: Proposes a neural local coherence model using an entity grid and CNN to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features, achieving state-of-the-art results on three coherence tasks.

- Contribution 1: Neural architecture that captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization.

- Contribution 2: Uses a CNN to automatically learn task-specific high-level features for coherence assessment.

- Contribution 3: Empirical evaluation on three tasks showing state-of-the-art performance.

- Strength 1: Novel architecture addressing limitations of existing entity grid models.

- Strength 2: Thorough evaluation demonstrating effectiveness and generalizability across tasks.

- Strength 3: Clear, well-written presentation with understandable model and evaluation descriptions.

- Weakness 1: Lacks comprehensive comparison with existing neural coherence models, notably Li and Hovy.

- Weakness 2: No deep analysis of why the Li and Hovy model performed poorly on the authors' tasks.

- Weakness 3: Paper needs proofreading and some sentence rephrasing for clarity and article usage.

- Question 1: Provide detailed experimental setup and any issues encountered when evaluating the Li and Hovy model.

- Question 2: Did you try to reproduce Li and Hovy’s original results on their datasets as a sanity check?

- Question 3: Provide details on hyperparameter tuning and how optimal hyperparameters were selected for each task.
