This paper proposes a convolutional neural network (CNN) approach to model the coherence of texts, building upon the well-known entity grid representation. The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach and offer insightful discussions on the intuitions behind certain design decisions, such as the choice of CNN and the explanation in the section titled 'Why it works'. These discussions are particularly appreciated, as they facilitate a deeper understanding of the proposed method.

The paper presents an extensive evaluation on several tasks, demonstrating that the proposed approach outperforms previous methods. However, it is concerning that the authors were unable to reproduce one previous result, as indicated by the inconsistent results on Li/Hovy (2014). This discrepancy suggests a potential implementation or modeling error that warrants further investigation.

While the proposed model is a notable extension of the entity grid model, it can be seen as a relatively straightforward 'neuralization' of the existing approach. One aspect that requires further clarification is the use of 100-dimensional vectors to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or a few more in the case of the extended grid). It is unclear how this high-dimensional representation contributes to the model's performance. Additionally, the difference in transition chains for up to k=3 sentences versus k=6 sentences may not be substantial, especially considering that many WSJ articles are relatively short.

The writing could be improved in terms of conciseness, as certain sections repeat information presented elsewhere in the paper. For instance, the introduction to entity grids is repeated in multiple places. Specifically, section 2 presents related work, which renders the first two-thirds of section 6 redundant. It is recommended that these redundant sections be removed or integrated into section 2 where necessary. The remaining content of section 6 could be added to section 2 under a subsection, and section 2 could be renamed to 'Related Work'.

Overall, this paper presents a solid implementation of applying a neural network model to entity-grid-based coherence. However, considering the proposed consolidation of previous work, one would expect more innovative contributions, such as new representations or tasks, in a full paper.

Minor points:

* The paper would benefit from proofreading by a native speaker to address grammatical errors, such as missing articles in several places (e.g., 'the WSJ corpus' and 'the Brown toolkit').
* On page 1, the reference to 'Figure 2' should be corrected to 'Figure 1'.
* In the same section, 'Firstly/Secondly' could be rephrased as 'First, Second' for improved clarity.
* The phrase 'limits the model to' could be reworded as 'prevents the model from considering' for better accuracy.
* The 'standard' final paragraph in section 1 is not necessary and could be removed, as it does not add significant value to the paper.
