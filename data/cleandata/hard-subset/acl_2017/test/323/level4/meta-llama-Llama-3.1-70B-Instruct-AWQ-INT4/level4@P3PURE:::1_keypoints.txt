- Proposes an extension of the entity grid model using a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions for coherence.  
- Claims better generalization over longer entity sequences compared to direct transition probability estimation in the original model.  
- Paper is well-written, builds on existing work, and uses a neural approach to address specific issues in one step.  
- Approach is valid but comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy is insufficient.  
- Authors’ surprise at Li and Hovy’s low score raises concerns about possible experimental errors, corpus-dependent performance, or dataset mismatch.  
- Recommends deeper investigation into these factors and testing the proposed model on corpora/tasks where Li and Hovy’s model performs well.  
- Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after author response; acknowledges that difficulties running others’ code shouldn’t be held against the authors.  
- Authors should document the efforts and problems encountered when testing the Li and Hovy system in the paper.  
- Suggests attempting to reproduce Li and Hovy’s results on their original datasets as a sanity check (if not already done).  
- Paper needs proofreading for missing definite articles and awkward phrasing (e.g., rephrase “limits the model to do X” to “limits the model’s capacity to do X” or similar).
