The paper presents an extension of the entity grid model, incorporating a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach enables better generalization over longer sequences of entities compared to the direct estimation of transition probabilities in the original model.

This is a well-written paper that builds upon existing work, using a neural network to address specific issues in one step. This approach is valid, but it would be beneficial to expand the comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors express surprise at the low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task, which raises questions about potential errors in the experimental setup, the corpus-dependent performance of the other model, and the proposed model's performance on a corpus or task where the other model is more successful. A deeper investigation into these factors would significantly strengthen the argument.

In general, the paper is fluent and readable. However, there are several instances where definite articles are missing (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, and 764). I recommend proofreading the paper specifically to address article usage. Additionally, the repeated expression "...limits the model to do X..." sounds somewhat unusual. Consider rephrasing it to "...limits the model's capacity to do X..." or "...stops the model from doing X..." for clarity.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that difficulties in running other people's software should not be held against the present authors. The efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, along with the problems encountered, should be documented in the paper. I also suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless they have already done so), to verify if the results are replicable.
