The paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model, leveraging a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach enables better generalization over longer sequences of entities compared to the direct estimates of transition probabilities in the original model. 

This is a well-written paper that takes a valid approach by building upon existing work and utilizing a neural network to address specific issues in one step. However, it would be beneficial to expand the comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors acknowledge being surprised by the very low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task, which raises questions about potential errors in the experimental setup. It also prompts speculation about whether the other model's low performance is corpus-dependent and how the proposed model would perform on a corpus or task where the other model is more successful. A deeper investigation into these factors would significantly strengthen the argument.

In general, the paper is fluent and readable, but it lacks definite articles in many places (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and likely others). I suggest proofreading the paper specifically to address article usage. The repeated expression "...limits the model to do X..." sounds somewhat unusual; rephrasing it to "...limits the model's capacity to do X" or "...stops the model from doing X" might be clearer.

Final recommendation adjusted to 4 after considering the author response. I agree that objective difficulties in running other people's software should not be held against the present authors. The efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, along with the problems encountered, should be documented in the paper. I also suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check (unless they have already done so), to verify if they can obtain the same results.
