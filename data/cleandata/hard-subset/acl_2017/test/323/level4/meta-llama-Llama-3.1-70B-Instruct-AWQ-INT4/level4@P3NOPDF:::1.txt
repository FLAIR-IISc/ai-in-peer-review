The paper presents an extension of the entity grid model, incorporating a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach allows for better generalization over longer sequences of entities compared to the direct estimation of transition probabilities in the original model.

This is a well-written paper that effectively builds upon existing work by utilizing a neural network to address specific issues in one step, rather than proposing a fully neural approach. This method is valid, but it would be beneficial to expand the comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors express surprise at the low score achieved by the Li and Hovy model on their task, which raises questions about potential errors in the experimental setup, the corpus-dependent performance of the other model, and the potential results of the proposed model on a corpus or task where the other model is more successful. A deeper investigation of these factors would significantly strengthen the argument.

In general, the paper is fluent and readable. However, there are several instances where definite articles are missing (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, and 764). It is recommended that the authors proofread the paper specifically with article usage in mind. Additionally, the expression "...limits the model to do X...", which is used repeatedly, sounds somewhat unusual. Alternative phrases, such as "limits the model's capacity to do X" or "stops the model from doing X", may be clearer.

In response to the author feedback, I adjusted my final recommendation to 4. I agree that difficulties in running other people's software should not be held against the present authors. The efforts made to test the Li and Hovy system, as well as the problems encountered, should be documented in the paper. Furthermore, I suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original data sets as a sanity check, unless they have already done so, to verify the validity of their findings.
