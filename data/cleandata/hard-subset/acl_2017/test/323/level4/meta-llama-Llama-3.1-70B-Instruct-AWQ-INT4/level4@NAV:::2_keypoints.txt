- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid; motivation and design choices are clearly explained, including a helpful "Why it works" section.
- Extensive evaluation shows the approach outperforms existing methods across tasks.
- Failure to reproduce Li/Hovy (2014) results raises concerns about possible implementation or modeling errors that need investigation.
- The model is essentially a neuralized entity grid; the rationale for using 100-dimensional vectors for four-dimensional grid entries is unclear and their contribution to performance is not explained.
- Although optimizing for coherence ranking improves results, differences between transition chains for k=3 versus k=6 may be minimal given the short length of many WSJ articles.
- Writing is verbose and repetitive (intro to entity grids repeated); section 6 should be trimmed or integrated into section 2 and section 2 possibly retitled "Related Work."
- Overall a robust implementation but the paper lacks more novel representations or additional tasks expected for a full paper.
- Minor fixes: native-speaker proofreading; correct "Figure 2" to "Figure 1"; rephrase "Firstly/Secondly" and "limits the model to"; remove the unnecessary final paragraph in section 1.
