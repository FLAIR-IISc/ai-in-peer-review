**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model based on a convolutional neural network (CNN) that operates over the entity grid representation of a text. The model captures long-range entity transitions along with entity-specific features without losing generalization, thanks to the power of distributed representation. The authors present a pairwise ranking method to train the model in an end-to-end fashion on a task and learn task-specific high-level features. The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Main Contributions**

1. **Neuralization of the Entity Grid Model**: The authors propose a neural architecture that can capture long-range entity transitions along with arbitrary entity-specific features, obtaining generalization through distributed representations of entity transitions and entity features.
2. **End-to-End Training Method**: The authors present a pairwise ranking approach to train the model on a target task and learn task-specific features.
3. **State-of-the-Art Results**: The evaluation on three different coherence assessment tasks demonstrates that the model achieves state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a good margin.

**Strengths**

1. **Well-Motivated Approach**: The paper proposes a well-motivated approach to model the coherence of texts, based on the well-known entity grid representation.
2. **Clear Discussion of Design Decisions**: The authors provide a clear discussion of the intuitions behind certain design decisions, such as the use of CNN and the section titled 'Why it works'.
3. **Extensive Evaluation**: The paper presents an extensive evaluation on several tasks, which shows that the proposed approach beats previous methods.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Simple Neuralization**: The model is a relatively simple 'neuralization' of the entity grid model, and it is not clear why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry.
2. **Repetition**: The paper repeats certain parts in several places, such as the introduction to entity grids, and some sections could be consolidated or removed.
3. **Limited Innovation**: Considering the proposed consolidation of previous work, the paper could benefit from more innovations in representations or tasks.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more insights on why 100-dimensional vectors are necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry?
2. How does the model handle short texts, such as those in the WSJ corpus?
3. Can you provide more details on the implementation of the pairwise ranking approach and how it learns task-specific features?

**Minor Points**

1. The paper may benefit from proof-reading by a native speaker to correct missing articles and other minor errors.
2. Some minor errors, such as 'Figure 2' -> 'Figure 1', should be corrected.
3. Consider removing the 'standard' final paragraph in section 1, as it is not necessary.
