This paper presents an extension of the entity grid model, leveraging a convolutional neural network to learn sequences of entity transitions that indicate coherence. This approach enables better generalization over longer sequences of entities compared to the direct estimation of transition probabilities in the original model. 

The paper is well-written and takes a valid approach by building upon existing work, utilizing a neural network to address specific issues in one step. However, it would be beneficial to expand the comparison to the existing neural coherence model of Li and Hovy. The authors' admission of surprise at the Li and Hovy model's low score on their task raises questions about potential errors in the experimental setup, corpus-dependent performance, and the proposed model's performance on tasks where the Li and Hovy model is more successful. A more in-depth investigation into these factors would significantly strengthen the argument.

In general, the paper is fluent and readable. Nevertheless, it lacks definite articles in several places (e.g., lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and possibly more). I recommend proofreading the paper with a focus on article usage. Additionally, the repeated expression "...limits the model to do X..." sounds unusual; alternative phrases, such as "limits the model's capacity to do X" or "prevents the model from doing X," may be clearer.

After considering the author response, I adjusted my final recommendation to 4. I concur that difficulties in running other people's software should not be held against the authors. The efforts to test the Li and Hovy system, along with the encountered problems, should be documented in the paper. Furthermore, I suggest that the authors attempt to reproduce Li and Hovy's results on their original datasets as a sanity check, unless they have already done so, to verify if it works for them.
