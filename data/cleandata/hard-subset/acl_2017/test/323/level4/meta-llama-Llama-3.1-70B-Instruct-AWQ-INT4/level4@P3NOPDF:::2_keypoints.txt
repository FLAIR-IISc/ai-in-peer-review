- Proposes a CNN-based model built on the entity grid to model text coherence.
- Provides clear motivation and helpful "Why it works" explanations for design choices.
- Extensive evaluation shows the approach outperforms prior methods on several tasks.
- Unable to reproduce Li/Hovy (2014) result; inconsistent reproduction suggests possible implementation/modeling error.
- Model reads as a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid rather than a novel representation or task.
- Unclear justification for using 100-d vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries; contribution of high dimensionality is unexplained.
- Unclear benefit of increasing transition chain length (k=3 vs k=6), especially for short WSJ articles.
- Writing is repetitive and could be made more concise; entity grid introduction appears in multiple places.
- Recommend consolidating redundant sections (move or merge section 6 content into related work and rename section 2 to "Related Work").
- Minor proofreading and wording fixes needed (articles, "Figure 2" → "Figure 1", "Firstly/Secondly" → "First/Second", rephrase "limits the model to", remove unnecessary final paragraph in section 1).
- Overall a solid implementation but would benefit from more innovative contributions beyond consolidating prior work.
