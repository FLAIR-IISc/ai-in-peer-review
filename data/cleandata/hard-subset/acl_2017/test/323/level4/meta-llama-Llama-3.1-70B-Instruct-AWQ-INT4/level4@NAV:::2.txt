The authors propose a novel approach to modeling text coherence using a convolutional neural network (CNN) built upon the well-established entity grid representation. The motivation behind this approach is clearly articulated, and the discussion on design decisions, such as the choice of CNN and the explanation in the 'Why it works' section, is particularly appreciated.

The paper presents an extensive evaluation across multiple tasks, demonstrating the proposed approach's superiority over existing methods. However, the inability to reproduce one previous result, specifically the findings on Li/Hovy (2014), raises concerns about potential implementation or modeling errors that warrant attention.

While the model represents a straightforward 'neuralization' of the entity grid model, the use of 100-dimensional vectors to represent a four-dimensional grid entry (or a slightly larger extended grid) is not entirely clear. It is unclear how this contributes to the model's performance. Although optimizing directly for coherence ranking can lead to a better model, the difference in transition chains for up to k=3 sentences versus k=6 sentences may not be substantial, particularly considering the brevity of many WSJ articles.

The writing could be more concise, with certain sections, such as the introduction to entity grids, repeated in multiple places. Specifically, the first two-thirds of section 6 are redundant and could be removed or integrated into section 2, where relevant. The remaining content of section 6 could be incorporated into section 2 under a subsection, with a potential retitle of section 2 to 'Related Work'.

Overall, this paper presents a robust implementation of a neural network model applied to entity-grid-based coherence. However, considering the proposed consolidation of previous work, more innovative representations (e.g., additional features) or tasks would be expected in a full paper.

Minor suggestions:

- A proofread by a native speaker could improve the paper's clarity, addressing issues such as missing articles (e.g., 'the WSJ corpus,' 'the Brown toolkit').
- On page 1, the bottom left column, 'Figure 2' should be corrected to 'Figure 1.'
- On page 1, 'Firstly/Secondly' could be rephrased as 'First, Second.'
- On page 1, 'limits the model to' might be reworded as 'prevents the model from considering.'
- The 'standard' final paragraph in section 1 could be removed, as it is not necessary in a concise paper.
