- Proposes a CNN-based model for text coherence built on the entity grid; combination and design choices are well-motivated and clearly explained (including "Why it works").  
- Evaluation is extensive and shows superiority over prior methods, but failure to replicate Li & Hovy (2014) is puzzling and suggests a possible implementation/modeling error to investigate.  
- The model is a relatively straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid; it lacks clear innovation in representations or tasks expected for a full paper.  
- Unclear why 100-dimensional vectors are used to represent a 4-dimensional grid entry (or slightly larger for extended grids); representation dimensionality seems excessive.  
- Optimizing directly for coherence ranking would likely yield a better model; differences between transition chains for k≤3 versus k=6 may be minimal given many short WSJ articles.  
- Writing is repetitive and could be more concise; the entity-grid introduction appears in multiple places and sections should be reorganized (merge Section 6 material into Section 2 and rename as "Related Work").  
- Minor edits needed: proofread for missing articles, correct "Figure 2"→"Figure 1", replace "Firstly/Secondly" with "First/Second", rephrase "limits the model to", and consider removing the boilerplate final paragraph of Section 1.
