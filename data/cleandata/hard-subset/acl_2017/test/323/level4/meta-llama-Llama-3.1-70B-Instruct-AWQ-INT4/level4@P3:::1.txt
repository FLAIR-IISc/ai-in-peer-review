**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a neural local coherence model that operates on the entity grid representation of a text. The model uses a convolutional neural network to capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features, allowing for better generalization over longer sequences of entities. The authors evaluate their model on three coherence assessment tasks and achieve state-of-the-art results, outperforming existing models by a significant margin.

**Main Contributions**

1. The authors propose a neural architecture for coherence assessment that can capture long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features without losing generalization power.
2. The model uses a convolutional neural network to learn task-specific high-level features automatically, allowing for better performance on coherence assessment tasks.
3. The authors evaluate their model on three coherence assessment tasks and achieve state-of-the-art results, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach.

**Strengths**

1. The paper proposes a novel neural architecture for coherence assessment that addresses the limitations of existing entity grid models.
2. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their model on three coherence assessment tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness and generalizability.
3. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed model and its evaluation.

**Weaknesses**

1. The authors do not provide a comprehensive comparison with existing neural coherence models, particularly the model proposed by Li and Hovy.
2. The paper lacks a deeper investigation of the factors that may have contributed to the low performance of the Li and Hovy model on the authors' task.
3. The authors could improve the readability of the paper by proofreading it specifically for article usage and rephrasing some sentences for clarity.

**Questions to Authors**

1. Can you provide more details on the experimental setup used to evaluate the Li and Hovy model, and whether you encountered any issues that may have contributed to its low performance?
2. Have you tried to reproduce the results of Li and Hovy on their original datasets as a sanity check?
3. Can you provide more information on the hyperparameter tuning process for your neural model, and how you selected the optimal hyperparameters for each task?
