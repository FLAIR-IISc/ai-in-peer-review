- Introduces a CNN-based extension of the entity grid model to learn long-range entity transition patterns for coherence assessment.  
- Proposes a neural architecture that captures long-range entity transitions and entity-specific features using distributed representations.  
- Presents an end-to-end training method to automatically learn task-specific high-level features.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on three coherence assessment tasks, outperforming existing models.  
- Novel approach enabling better generalization over longer sequences of entities compared to classic transition probability estimates.  
- Provides a thorough evaluation including comparisons with existing models and result analysis.  
- Does not include a comprehensive comparison with prior neural coherence models, especially Li and Hovy.  
- Experimental setup for evaluating the Li and Hovy model is insufficiently explained, raising validity concerns.  
- Lacks a deeper analysis of which factors (e.g., entity features, architecture choices) drive the model's performance.  
- Requests more details on the experimental setup used to evaluate the Li and Hovy model (corpus and task).  
- Asks whether the authors reproduced Li and Hovy’s results on the original datasets as a sanity check.  
- Seeks more insight into the impact of entity-specific features and the choice of CNN architecture on performance.
