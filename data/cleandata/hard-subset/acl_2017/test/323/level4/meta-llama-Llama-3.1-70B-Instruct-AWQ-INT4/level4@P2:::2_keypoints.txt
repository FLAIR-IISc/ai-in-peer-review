- Proposes a CNN-based neural model for text coherence built on the entity grid representation; well motivated and clearly described.
- Contribution: neural architecture capturing long-range entity transitions and arbitrary entity-specific features.
- Contribution: end-to-end training method that learns task-specific high-level features automatically.
- Contribution: achieves state-of-the-art results on three coherence assessment tasks.
- Strength: thorough evaluation showing the approach outperforms previous methods.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated discussion of model design decisions.
- Strength: pairwise ranking training enables end-to-end learning of task-specific features.
- Weakness: model is a simple 'neuralization' of the entity grid and necessity of 100-d vectors for 4-d entries is unclear.
- Weakness: suspicious results on Li/Hovy (2014) suggest a possible implementation or modeling error.
- Weakness: writing is somewhat lengthy and repetitive in places.
- Question: why are 100-dimensional vectors necessary to represent a four-dimensional grid entry?
- Question: how will the authors investigate and fix the Li/Hovy (2014) result discrepancy and verify implementation correctness?
- Question: are there plans to explore additional features or tasks to improve the model further?
- Minor: paper needs proofreading to fix missing articles and grammatical errors.
- Minor: formatting issues such as incorrect figure references and inconsistent transition words.
