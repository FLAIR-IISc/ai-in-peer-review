This paper introduces an extension of the entity grid model. It uses convolutional neural networks to learn the sequence of entity transformations that indicate coherence. This allows for better generalizations on the long sequence of entities than direct estimation of the transition probabilities of the original model.

It is a well-written and concise paper. Instead of proposing a complete neural approach, the authors solve a specific problem in one step using a neural network based on existing work. This is a valid approach, but it would be better to compare it with the existing neural coherence models of Li and Hovy. The authors acknowledge that they were surprised to see the low scores of Li and Hovy's models in their work. This makes the reader wonder if there were any errors in the experimental setup, whether the low performance of other models is corpus-dependent, and if so, what results the model proposed in this paper would achieve in a corpus or task where the other model is more successful. A deeper investigation of these issues would provide sufficient justification for the argument.

In general, the paper is very fluent and readable, but the definite article is missing in many places (such as lines 92, 132, 174, 488, 490, 547, 674, 764, and possibly more). I would advise proofreading the paper with the article in mind. The expression "...model-ke X karne seedak samakshito..." (model-ke X karne seedak samakshito), which is used repeatedly, sounds a bit unusual. Perhaps "model-er X karne ke kapability ke nukarate" (model-er X karne ke kapability ke nukarate) or "model-ke X karne se abartake rakhe" (model-ke X karne se abartake rakhe) would be clearer.

After considering the author's feedback, the final recommendation has been upgraded to 4. I agree that objective difficulties in running other people's software should not be used against the current authors. The effort made to test the Li and Hovy systems and the problems encountered in doing so should be documented in the paper. I would also suggest that the authors try to reproduce the Li and Hovy results on their original data set as a sanity check (if they haven't already done this), just to see if it works for them.
