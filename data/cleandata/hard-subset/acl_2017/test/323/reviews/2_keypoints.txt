- Proposes applying a convolutional neural network on top of the entity grid representation to model text coherence.
- Approach is well motivated with clear discussion of design intuitions (e.g., why CNN, section "Why it works").
- Extensive evaluation on multiple tasks shows the proposed method outperforms previous approaches.
- Unable to reproduce one prior result (Li/Hovy 2014), suggesting a possible implementation or modelling error that needs addressing.
- Method is largely a straightforward "neuralization" of the entity grid; lacks substantial representational or task innovations expected for a full paper.
- Unclear justification for using 100-dimensional vectors to represent four-dimensional grid entries (and slightly higher for the extended grid).
- Direct optimization for coherence ranking is sensible, but the benefit of using longer transition chains (k=6 vs k=3) is questionable given many WSJ articles are short.
- Writing is verbose and repetitive; introductory material and related work are duplicated (notably section 6 repeats section 2) and should be reorganized/condensed.
- Suggests adding more innovations (new features or tasks) beyond consolidating prior work.
- Minor language and proofreading issues: missing articles and phrasing problems throughout.
- Specific editorial fixes: replace 'Figure 2' -> 'Figure 1' (p.1 bottom left), change 'Firstly/Secondly' -> 'First, Second', rephrase 'limits the model to' to 'prevents the model from considering ...', and consider removing the unnecessary final paragraph in section 1.
