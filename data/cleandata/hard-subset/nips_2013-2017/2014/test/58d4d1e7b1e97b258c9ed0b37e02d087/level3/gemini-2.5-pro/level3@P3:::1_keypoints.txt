- Presents a computational framework for a priori identifiability of Bayesian observer models via a flexible, parametric observer formulation.  
- Core contribution: an expected log-likelihood metric (proportional to KL divergence) to quantify model distinguishability and map an "identifiability landscape."  
- Demonstrated on two case studies (time-interval priors and slow-speed motion prior), yielding non-trivial insights (e.g., MAP-like loss can worsen prior recovery).  
- Significance: offers a principled method for designing experiments that better constrain priors, likelihoods, and loss functions.  
- Clarity/quality: paper is well-written, logically structured, and case studies effectively illustrate the approach.  
- Originality: novel use of expected KL divergence as an a priori experimental-design tool.  
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with substantial prior literature on identifiability, Fisher information, model manifolds, and related model-comparison methods.  
- Weakness: key modeling assumptions (e.g., two-component Gaussian-mixture measurement noise, fixed moments, chosen reference-observer parameters) are not fully justified.  
- Concern: lack of sensitivity analyses to show robustness of conclusions and experimental-design rankings to different plausible reference observers.  
- Recommendation: provisionally acceptable if major revisions address literature connections and modeling justifications.  
- Required revision: add a thorough discussion situating the method relative to existing work (Myung, Pitt, Navarro, etc.) and clarify unique advantages.  
- Required revision: provide stronger justification for likelihood/loss functional forms and include sensitivity analyses over reference-observer choices.  
- Minor comments: explain the "trivial mathematical fact" (line 53), fix the typo on line 73, and clarify Figure 2 caption (star and dashed lines).  
- Summary judgment: promising and original but currently limited by insufficient literature context and potentially brittle modeling assumptions; revise and resubmit.
