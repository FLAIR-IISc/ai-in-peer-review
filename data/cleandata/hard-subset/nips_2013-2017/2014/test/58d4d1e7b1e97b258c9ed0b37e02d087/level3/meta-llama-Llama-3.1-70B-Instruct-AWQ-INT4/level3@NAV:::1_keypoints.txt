- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perception models, addressing a long-standing cognitive-science issue.  
- Develops a new class of observer models that are flexible and computationally efficient for exploring identifiability.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception tasks.  
- Writing is clear and accessible, with a thorough introductory review for non-experts.  
- Framework is well-motivated and grounded in Bayesian decision theory.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and candid about strengths and limitations.  
- Originality is questioned because prior model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004) is largely ignored.  
- Authors need to justify chosen constraints and assumptions that render Bayesian models identifiable; some appear arbitrary.  
- Empirical validation of the results is limited and should be strengthened beyond theoretical analyses.  
- Conclusions should be more deeply situated within and compared to existing literature.  
- Contribution is novel but builds on existing work; the differences from prior approaches need clearer emphasis.  
- Significance is high: the framework could be widely applicable and advance understanding of Bayesian perception models.
