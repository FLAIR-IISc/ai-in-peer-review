- Tackles practical identifiability of prior, likelihood, and loss in Bayesian cognitive models with a framework mapping expected log-likelihood differences to a sum of KL divergences and sampling an “expected posterior.”

- Demonstrates the approach with two case studies: temporal interval reproduction (Jazayeri & Shadlen) and speed perception with a slow-speed prior (Stocker & Simoncelli).

- Situates the work well in existing Bayesian observer and identifiability literature and addresses concerns about degeneracy and over-interpretation.

- Core technical contribution: reduction of expected model evidence to a weighted sum of KL divergences (Eqs. 11–13) and practical MCMC exploration of the induced landscape.

- Case studies are informative; interval-timing design insights are especially actionable and speed-perception validation supports prior inferences.

- Major limitation: many strong modeling assumptions (parametric sensory mapping, maximum-entropy mixtures, unimodality constraints, fixed lapse/motor noise) reduce generality unless stress-tested.

- Several analytical approximations lack explicit conditions of validity and require formal justification or empirical bounds.

- Writing and organization are clear overall but some definitions, figure choices, and lack of statistical reporting hinder interpretation.

- Novelty: a priori identifiability analysis via expected log-likelihood/KL and landscape sampling is new and moves beyond ad hoc checks in prior work.

- Significance: method is likely to influence experimental design and reconstruction of internal representations in perception research.

- Strengths: principled, general framework; clear connection to experimental design; elegant KL reduction and efficient MCMC; good related-work coverage.

- Weaknesses: cumulative impact of strong assumptions is insufficiently evaluated; approximations need formal error analysis.

- Quantify interchangeability of sensory vs motor noise and report statistical reliability (credible intervals/bootstraps) and uncertainty for key parameters (σ, ρ1, σℓ).

- Bring the flat-prior sanity check from the supplement into the main text to demonstrate recovery of no slow-speed prior.

- Clarify whether stimulus discretization is experimental or computational and discuss how Nexp/binning affect identifiability (bias–variance tradeoff).

- Assess sensitivity to the log-stimulus transform (d), test mis-specification (e.g., d∈{0.7,1.3}), and compare to power-law transforms to identify degeneracies.

- State whether maximum-entropy/mixture parametrization enforces unimodality, and add checks or penalties if bimodality can arise.

- Justify the fixed Gaussian prior lattice and the choice of prior support width; consider adaptive bases or hierarchical priors and show robustness to misspecification.

- Revise Figure 1 for clearer pipeline presentation and remove confusing derived panels to improve readability.

- Justify approximations in Eqs. 14–15 (Stirling, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) with formal error bounds or empirical validation and specify required N for negligible error.

- Specify precisely which unnormalized density is sampled (Eq. 13), the role of Pi, MCMC proposal/diagnostics (ESS, R̂), and strategies for scaling with Ntr (e.g., tempering).

- Clarify or temper the claim that priors don’t matter in 2AFC by stating required symmetry conditions or analyzing 2AFC within the framework.

- Quantify “weakly identifiable” with posterior SDs or profile likelihoods, provide power analyses for required Ntr, report computational cost, and share code for reproducibility.

- Pros for acceptance: important problem, principled and general framework, solid derivation, efficient method, and useful case-study guidance.

- Cons for acceptance: heavy reliance on strong assumptions, insufficient sensitivity analyses, unclear approximation validity, and a few overstated claims.

- Overall evaluation: above acceptance threshold for methodology and clarity but needs stronger approximation justification, robustness checks, explicit uncertainty reporting, and clearer figures to be broadly useful.
