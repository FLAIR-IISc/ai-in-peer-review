- Presents a Bayesian framework to analyze identifiability of perceptual models with explicit equations for noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Core contribution: systematic method to assess whether different parameter combinations yield indistinguishable behavioral predictions.  
- Demonstrates framework with two case studies (time-interval estimation and speed perception) and discusses implications for experimental design.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance conditional on significant revision.  
- Strength: well-motivated and correctly identifies degeneracy/non-uniqueness in Bayesian perceptual models.  
- Strength: mathematically rigorous formulation building on established Bayesian methods.  
- Strength: clear presentation and relevant case studies illustrating utility.  
- Potential impact: useful tool for researchers designing experiments and interpreting perceptual models.  
- Weakness: contribution feels incremental; non-identifiability of Bayesian models has been previously discussed.  
- Major concern: insufficient contextualization and comparison to related work, especially Acerbi et al. (2012, 2014).  
- Request: add a dedicated section comparing this framework to recent Bayesian perceptual identifiability work, explicitly stating advances over prior methods.  
- Claim of “identifiability” needs qualification because results depend on model assumptions and do not resolve practical separation of sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Request: expand discussion of practical identifiability limitations and challenges in validating noise/loss assumptions, and suggest strategies or acknowledge need for complementary experimental approaches.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, addresses an important problem, clear presentation, potential for impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance (mitigable by revision): incremental contribution, insufficient related-work comparison, and limited discussion of practical identifiability.  
- Conclusion: solid paper with potential value but must address originality and practical limitations via revision to justify acceptance.
