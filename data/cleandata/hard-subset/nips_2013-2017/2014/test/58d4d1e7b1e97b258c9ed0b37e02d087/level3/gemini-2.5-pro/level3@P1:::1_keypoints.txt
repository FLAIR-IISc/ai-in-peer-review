- Paper proposes a framework to test a priori identifiability of Bayesian observer models using a flexible parametric class and an expected log-likelihood / KL-based "identifiability landscape," demonstrated on time-interval and speed-perception tasks.
- Significance/timeliness: Tackles a major, longstanding identifiability problem in Bayesian perceptual modeling that is important to the community.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, logically organized, and the examples make the framework's utility clear.
- Technical contribution: Introduces a computationally tractable parametric observer class (mixtures of Gaussians for prior/likelihood/loss) and an efficient method to compute response distributions for identifiability analysis.
- Weakness — relation to prior work: Fails to adequately position itself relative to extensive existing literature on identifiability, parameter recovery, Fisher information, and model comparison; novelty over established methods is not clearly stated.
- Weakness — modeling assumptions: Key conclusions depend on specific parametric choices (e.g., two-component Gaussian mixtures) that are justified mainly by convenience; robustness to these assumptions is not demonstrated.
- Recommendation: Authors should thoroughly relate their framework to existing identifiability and model-comparison methods and clarify the unique contribution.
- Recommendation: Authors should better justify their parametric modeling choices and provide analyses showing robustness of case-study conclusions to those assumptions.
- Minor: Line 53 should explicitly state the mathematical reason for the degeneracy (scaling by functions whose product is constant).
- Minor: Line 73 contains a grammatical error ("nor" should be "or").
- Minor: Figure 2 caption should explicitly define the star and dashed lines (ground truth and inferred posterior mean).
