- Proposes a framework to systematically test identifiability of Bayesian perception models plus a flexible, computationally efficient observer model.  
- Correctly highlights degeneracy in Bayesian inference: multiple priors/likelihoods/losses can produce identical behavior.  
- Framework enables a priori identifiability analysis and can be used to improve experimental design.  
- Presents rigorous mathematical formulation covering sensation, perception, decision-making, and response stages.  
- Validates approach with two case studies (time-interval estimation, speed perception) linked to existing findings.  
- Builds on prior Bayesian decision-theory work and balances model flexibility with computational efficiency.  
- Includes realistic features (lapse rates, response noise) and insightful analysis of loss-function effects in time estimation.  
- Contribution feels incremental relative to recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al.); insufficiently differentiated from prior analyses of identifiability.  
- Lacks a dedicated review/section clearly situating the method among recent Bayesian perceptual-modeling efforts.  
- Practical identifiability limitations under-addressed: hard to disentangle sensory, decisional, and motor noise in real data.  
- Identifiability claims depend on model assumptions (likelihood, prior, loss); sensitivity to misspecification should be discussed.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on adding literature contextualization and expanding discussion of practical limitations and sensitivity.
