- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perception models.  
- Introduces a flexible, efficient class of Bayesian observer models for various psychophysical tasks.  
- Presents a combined theoretical and simulation method to explore model identifiability.  
- Demonstrates the approach on two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Framework can rank experimental designs by ability to resolve parameter degeneracy and identify critical parameters.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Main results depend on many strong assumptions, reducing their overall impact.  
- Interchangeability of sensory and motor noise (Sec 4.1) is unsurprising; statistical reliability of condition differences should be reported.  
- In Sec 4.2, authors must show the model can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior in the reference observer.  
- Log-stimulus transform is a strong representational constraint that may heavily affect identifiability.  
- Need clarification on modeling a continuous stimulus with a discrete distribution (experimental design vs true stimulus).  
- Constraints and entropy-maximization approach do not guarantee unimodality and could permit bimodal fitted distributions.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 is confusing; suggest integrating panels and removing derived p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Authors should justify approximations in Equations 14–15 and specify conditions on N where they hold.  
- Overall assessment: above NIPS threshold due to methodology and clarity, but limitations and minor issues must be addressed.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel framework, broadly applicable methodology, clear writing.  
- Con-accept arguments: heavy assumptions, presentation/clarity issues, and unaddressed limitations (e.g., noise interchangeability).
