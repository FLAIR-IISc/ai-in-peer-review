- Introduces a novel, computationally tractable framework to assess a priori identifiability of Bayesian observer models of perception.
- Framework combines a flexible parametric class of Bayesian observer models with an identifiability metric based on expected log-likelihood (KL divergence).
- Shows the expected log-likelihood is proportional to KL divergence between response distributions, enabling efficient exploration via MCMC without heavy data simulation.
- Demonstrates utility with two case studies: time-interval estimation (comparing experimental designs) and speed perception (validating a slow-speed prior).
- Strength: addresses an important, underexamined identifiability problem with a principled, original approach.
- Strength: elegant methodology that avoids expensive simulations by leveraging KL-divergence and expected log-likelihood.
- Strength: clear writing and logical structure; case studies effectively illustrate practical application.
- Main weakness: conclusions depend on strong parametric assumptions (log-like sensory mapping, mixture-of-Gaussians), raising sensitivity concerns.
- Needs sensitivity analysis for choices like the sensory map f(s) and other assumed representational forms.
- Missing control in Section 4.2: must show recovery fails/flat prior is recovered when reference observer has a flat prior (FLA results not shown).
- Section 4.1 needs statistical rigor: quantify reliability/significance of differences in reconstruction error between experimental conditions.
- Technical clarifications required for derivation of Eq. 12 and approximations (e.g., Stirling’s, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) including conditions on N for validity.
- Justify modeling choices: discrete stimulus representation, lattice-based prior form and its range being 50% wider than stimulus range.
- Provide more detail on MCMC: specify the exact target/approximate expected posterior density used for sampling.
- Justify the assumption in the 2AFC task that subjects do not learn the experimental prior, with references or evidence.
- Clarify whether the mixture-of-Gaussians maximum-entropy approach guarantees unimodality or if unimodality is separately enforced.
- Improve Figure 1 for clarity: integrate panel (b) into the graphical model and consider removing derived p_est(s*|x).
- Overall recommendation: novel and impactful work above acceptance threshold but requires addressing strong assumptions, technical justifications, and missing controls.
