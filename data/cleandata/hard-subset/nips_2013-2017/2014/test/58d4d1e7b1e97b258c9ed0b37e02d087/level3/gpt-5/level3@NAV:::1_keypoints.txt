- Paper proposes an a priori framework to test identifiability of Bayesian observer models using a flexible parametric class and a KL-based expected-posterior sampling criterion.  
- Implements monotonic sensory map, mixture-of-Gaussians likelihood/loss, Gaussian-mixture priors, lapse and motor noise in the parametric family.  
- Two case studies: interval timing (priors hard to recover unless motor noise constrained; narrow losses can worsen recovery) and speed perception (priors and noise parameters better recovered).  
- KL derivation and use of expected log-likelihood are sensible; case studies are carefully executed.  
- Key modeling choices feel arbitrary or under-justified (e.g., p_meas two-component Gaussian fixed by a heuristic, fixed prior basis size, loss family, Eq.12 choices).  
- The identifiability maps may critically depend on those parametric choices; paper needs systematic sensitivity analyses (vary components, non-Gaussian noise, alternative f(s), asymmetric/heavy-tailed losses, different stimulus schedules and trial counts).  
- Clarity is overall good, but fix minor issues: explain the “trivial mathematical fact” (Line 53), change “nor” to “or” (Line 73), and clarify star/dashed lines in Figure 2.  
- Under-cites prior work on model distinguishability and principled model comparison (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004) and should position relative to design-optimization tools.  
- Significance is high (identifiability is important and can guide experimental design) but impact depends on demonstrating robustness beyond current parametric family and clearer guidance on necessary constraints.  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles an important question with a practical framework, clear demonstrations linking design to identifiability, and a useful pre-registration/design-evaluation tool.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient engagement with prior literature and heavy reliance on specific parametric assumptions without robustness checks.  
- Recommendation: justify constraints/assumptions that enable identifiability, add sensitivity and ablation studies, broaden related work and clarify methodological positioning.
