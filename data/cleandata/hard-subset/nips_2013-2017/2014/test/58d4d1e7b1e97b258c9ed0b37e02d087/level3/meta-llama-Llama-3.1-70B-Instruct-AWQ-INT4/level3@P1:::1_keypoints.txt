- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian models of perception, addressing a key issue in cognitive science.
- Provides a thorough review of Bayesian Decision Theory and psychophysics, highlighting non-identifiability of internal representations.
- Introduces a new class of Bayesian observer models that are presented as flexible and computationally efficient.
- Presents a method for exploring model identifiability and applies it to two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.
- Paper is well-written and shows deep theoretical understanding of Bayesian models of perception.
- Framework is a significant contribution with potential to improve experimental design and assess robustness of internal representations.
- Demonstrated case studies indicate practical utility and potential impact on perception and cognition research.
- Major concern: paper largely ignores substantial prior literature on model comparison and identifiability (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004).
- Important modeling assumptions (e.g., measurement distribution pmeas in Eq. 3 and parameter choices in Eq. 12) appear arbitrary or insufficiently justified.
- Authors need to discuss and justify chosen constraints and assumptions that render Bayesian models identifiable.
- Need clearer explanation of how this framework differs from and builds on previous work, and what new insights it provides.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated framework with systematic identifiability testing and demonstrated case-study utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of prior literature, unjustified modeling choices, and insufficient clarity about novelty and relation to existing methods.
- Recommended actions: cite and relate to prior model-comparison work, justify key modeling choices and constraints, and explicitly contrast this framework with prior approaches.
