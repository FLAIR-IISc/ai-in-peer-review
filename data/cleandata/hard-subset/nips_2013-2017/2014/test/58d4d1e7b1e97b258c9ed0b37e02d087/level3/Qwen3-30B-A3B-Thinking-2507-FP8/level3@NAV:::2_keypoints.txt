- Proposes a framework to test identifiability of Bayesian observer models using KL-divergence analysis.  
- Extends prior Bayesian perception work with a flexible class of observer models and a priori identifiability exploration.  
- Applies framework to two case studies (time interval estimation and speed perception) showing how experimental design affects parameter recovery.  
- Strengths: clear writing, interesting methodological contribution, and practical applications for experimental design.  
- Strengths: sound theoretical foundation and valuable addition to cognitive modeling tools.  
- Major concern: heavy reliance on strong assumptions that may dictate identifiability outcomes.  
- Representational constraint: the log-stimulus transform (line 134) is a significant assumption needing justification.  
- Prior assumption: preferring lattice representations over uniform priors (line 158) is unjustified.  
- Questionable claim: assertion that learning priors wouldn't matter in 2AFC tasks (line 365) lacks support.  
- Section 4.1: fails to report statistically reliable differences when claiming sensory/motor noise interchangeability.  
- Section 4.2: does not demonstrate the model can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior in the reference observer.  
- Technical issue: log-likelihood approximations in Equations 14–15 (line 252) require justification and validity conditions.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 is confusing; derived p_est(s*|x) should be integrated into panel a.  
- Overall: promising methodology but weakened by unexamined assumptions and insufficient validation; needs addressing of technical concerns and edge-case demonstrations.
