- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian observer models in perception.
- Summarizes Bayesian Decision Theory and highlights non-identifiability from Bayesian degeneracy.
- Framework targets a broad class of observer models parametrized by θ.
- Demonstrates method on two case studies: inferring priors in time-interval estimation and reconstructing prior/noise in speed perception.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and presents results clearly.
- Major concern: inadequate engagement with prior literature on model comparison/identifiability (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004).
- Major concern: key modeling choices (definition of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter choices in Eq.12) appear arbitrary and lack justification.
- Novelty of contribution is unclear without clarifying differences from or extensions to existing work.
- Authors should expand the literature review and explicitly position their framework relative to prior methods.
- Authors should justify modeling assumptions and report sensitivity of results to those assumptions.
- If revised to address literature gaps and assumption justifications, the paper could make a significant contribution to cognitive science and psychophysical experiment design.
