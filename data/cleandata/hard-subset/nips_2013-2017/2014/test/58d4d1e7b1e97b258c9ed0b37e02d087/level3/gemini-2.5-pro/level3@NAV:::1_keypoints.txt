- Proposes a framework for a priori identifiability analysis of Bayesian observer models; applied to time-interval estimation and visual speed perception.  
- Addresses an important, long-standing problem of model identifiability in cognitive modeling.  
- Strength: direct engagement with the foundational issue; technically interesting, well-written, and clearly structured.  
- Major concern 1: Largely overlooks substantial prior work on model comparison and identifiability (e.g., Pitt et al., Navarro et al.), leaving novelty and contribution unclear.  
- Major concern 2: Relies on arbitrary or insufficiently justified technical assumptions (e.g., mixture-of-Gaussians p_meas and parameter choices for model support), raising questions about robustness and generality.  
- Needs sensitivity analyses to show how conclusions depend on specific modeling choices and parameter settings.  
- Clarity issues: explain the “trivial mathematical fact” (Line 53), fix typo on Line 73, and clarify the star/dashed lines in Figure 2 caption.  
- Significance: high potential if methodological concerns are resolved; a robust a priori identifiability tool would be valuable for experiment design.  
- Recommendation: thoroughly discuss and justify the constraints that enable identifiability and explicitly connect the approach to previous literature on model comparison.
