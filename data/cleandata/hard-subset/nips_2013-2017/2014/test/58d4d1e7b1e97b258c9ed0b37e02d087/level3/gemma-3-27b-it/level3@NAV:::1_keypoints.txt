- Tackles identifiability in Bayesian perceptual models and proposes a framework for testing parameter recoverability addressing Bayesian degeneracy.
- Framework components: summarizes Bayesian Decision Theory, identifies non-identifiability sources, prescribes constraints, tests parameter differentiability, and applies to time-interval and motion-perception tasks.
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and provides clear explanations and detailed methodology.
- Demonstrates the framework's utility with concrete psychophysical examples.
- Major weakness: insufficient engagement with prior literature on model comparison and identifiability (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004), limiting assessment of novelty.
- Key modeling assumptions (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq. 3 and parameter choices in Eq. 12) are under-justified and may be arbitrary.
- Lacks a thorough sensitivity analysis to show how results depend on chosen assumptions and constraints.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical theoretical problem, offers a clear framework, and demonstrates applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor literature contextualization, weak justification of assumptions, and unclear sensitivity.
- Minor comments: clarify “trivial mathematical fact” (Line 53), change “nor” to “or” (Line 73), and clarify star/dashed lines in Figure 2.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution but should be accepted only after major revisions to address literature context and justify modeling choices.
