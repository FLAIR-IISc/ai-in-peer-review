- Tackles identifiability in Bayesian perception models and proposes a framework to test identifiability a priori.  
- Applies the framework to time interval estimation and speed perception tasks, showing utility for experimental design.  
- Emphasizes that multiple combinations of priors, likelihoods, and loss functions can produce the same behavior (degeneracy).  
- Builds on Bayesian Decision Theory and the goal of inverting models to recover internal representations.  
- Strength: addresses an important and timely problem in cognitive modeling.  
- Strength: proposes a novel, pre-data method for evaluating identifiability.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and mathematically well explained.  
- Strength: case studies provide practical, actionable experimental recommendations.  
- Strength: theoretically rigorous, leveraging KL divergence and maximum entropy.  
- Weakness: limited engagement with related literature on model comparison and identifiability (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004).  
- Weakness: some modeling assumptions (e.g., form of p_meas and parameter choices) seem arbitrary and need justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of prior distributions used within the identifiability analyses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a fundamental issue, presents a novel framework, demonstrates utility with case studies, and is well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited related-work context, potentially arbitrary assumptions, and missing discussion on priors.  
- Minor comments: clarify "trivial mathematical fact" (L53), change "nor" to "or" (L73), and explain the star and dashed lines in Figure 2.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand related-work discussion and justify key modeling choices.
