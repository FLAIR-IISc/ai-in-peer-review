- Proposes a framework for Bayesian model comparison on behavioral data addressing identifiability of internal representations/processes.  
- Reviews Bayesian Decision Theory and emphasizes non-uniqueness of the inverse mapping from behavior to decision-process elements.  
- Introduces a novel class of Bayesian observer models to study identifiability across psychophysical tasks.  
- Paper is well-written and demonstrates deep theoretical understanding.  
- Framework is efficient and flexible, enabling exploration of a large class of observer models.  
- Applies framework to two case studies (time interval estimation and speed perception), yielding insights into identifiability.  
- Major concern: substantial prior literature on model identifiability/distinguishability (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002, Navarro et al. 2004) is ignored, making novelty unclear.  
- Some modeling assumptions appear arbitrary or insufficiently justified (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter choices in Eq.12).  
- Unclear how sensitive results are to chosen assumptions and constraints; authors should discuss dependence and justification.  
- Paper needs clearer explanations of technical aspects (e.g., the “trivial mathematical fact” at Line 53).  
- Contains minor errors/typos (e.g., second “nor” should be “or” at Line 73).  
- Despite issues, the paper is a valuable contribution and its conclusion about interval timing needing strong constraints is well-supported.  
- Provides a useful tool for ranking experimental designs by quality of inferred features.  
- Reviewer scoring: high on clarity and significance, lower on originality and quality.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing literature omissions, justifying modeling assumptions, and clarifying technical details.
