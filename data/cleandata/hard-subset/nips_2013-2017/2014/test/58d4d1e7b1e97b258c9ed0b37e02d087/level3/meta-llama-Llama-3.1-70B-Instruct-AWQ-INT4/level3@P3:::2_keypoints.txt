- Presents a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perception models.  
- Proposes a general method for a priori investigation of identifiability and systematic comparison of experimental designs.  
- Develops a flexible, efficient Bayesian observer model parametrized by θ to span a large representational basis.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two case studies: time-interval prior inference and reconstruction of prior/noise in speed perception.  
- Strength: tackles the crucial problem of model non-identifiability in psychophysics.  
- Strength: offers a systematic approach that can improve experimental design.  
- Strength: introduces a novel, generalizable Bayesian observer model applicable to many tasks.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and methodologically clear and organized.  
- Weakness: main results depend on strong assumptions (e.g., scalar property of timing noise, almost-quadratic loss) that may limit generality.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on simulations, which may not reflect real-world data.  
- Presentation issue: Section 4.1 asserts interchangeability of sensory and motor noise without clear explanation.  
- Presentation issue: Section 4.2 fails to demonstrate recovery of the absence of a slow-speed prior in the reference observer.  
- Presentation issue: some figures and equations lack clarity and could be improved.  
- Evaluation: high quality, original, and significant work overall.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a crucial issue, offers a novel flexible model, and presents clear methodology and significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumptions, simulation-only validation, and clarity/presentation shortcomings.  
- Recommendations: clarify and justify assumptions (sensory noise, loss function), show recovery of no slow-speed prior, improve figures/equations, and include more real-world data.
