- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perception models, demonstrated on two case studies (time interval estimation and speed perception).  
- Manuscript is clear, well-written, and well-organized.  
- Addresses a crucial, timely issue in cognitive modeling—identifiability of Bayesian models.  
- Results depend on many strong assumptions (sensory mapping, prior, loss function), reducing their generality and impact.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of statistical reliability and significance of reported differences between conditions.  
- Several modeling choices are not well-justified (e.g., discretizing a continuous stimulus, use of a log-stimulus transform).  
- Insufficient detail on constraints and entropy maximization; e.g., using a two-Gaussian mixture without discussing potential bimodality.  
- Use of a lattice prior is unexplained; unclear how users should set priors wider than true stimulus range.  
- Figure 1 is confusing; recommend integrating panel b into a and removing derived p_est(s^*|x).  
- Equations rely on approximations (Stirling, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) without discussing validity conditions.  
- Unclear which "unnormalized density" is being sampled from in Line 262 (is it the dataset distribution?).  
- Recommendations: justify assumptions, test statistical reliability, clarify modeling and constraint choices, improve Fig. 1, justify approximations, and specify sampled density.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, clarity, and importance of the addressed problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: excessive strong assumptions, insufficient statistical analysis, and poorly justified modeling choices.
