- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perceptual models.  
- Introduces a flexible, efficient class of observer models for diverse psychophysical tasks.  
- Develops a method for exploring model identifiability within this framework.  
- Validates framework via two case studies showing improved experimental design and recovery of internal representations.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible.  
- The proposed model is mathematically elegant and offers a general formalism for perceptual estimation.  
- Authors provide a thorough discussion of limitations, including potential non-identifiability of parameters.  
- Major strength: enables systematic, a priori investigation of identifiability to evaluate experimental designs before running experiments.  
- Demonstrates practical applicability through real-world case studies.  
- Concern: contribution is incremental and closely resembles recent work in the field.  
- Suggestion: add a section reviewing recent Bayesian perceptual models and clearly state advances over them.  
- Concern: identifiability claims depend on model assumptions and do not resolve practical distinctions between sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Suggestion: discuss practical identifiability limitations and how they might be addressed in future work.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10).
