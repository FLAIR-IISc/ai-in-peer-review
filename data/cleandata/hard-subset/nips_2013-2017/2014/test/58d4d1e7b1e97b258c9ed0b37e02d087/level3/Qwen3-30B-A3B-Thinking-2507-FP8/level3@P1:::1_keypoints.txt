- Paper addresses identifiability of internal representations (priors, likelihoods, loss) from behavioral data in Bayesian perceptual models.  
- Proposes an a priori framework using KL divergence to quantify model distinguishability under experimental designs.  
- Implements framework efficiently via MCMC over the identifiability landscape.  
- Applies framework to two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Time-interval results: motor noise measurement and loss-function constraints critically affect prior recovery; narrow loss can worsen identifiability.  
- Speed-perception results: robust reconstruction of slow-speed priors, corroborating prior findings.  
- Framework is flexible, unifying sensory noise, priors, loss functions, and motor errors in one parametrization.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear, well-structured, and technically sound with directly relevant case studies for experimentalists.  
- Major weakness: fails to cite and contextualize foundational identifiability/model-comparison work (Navarro et al. 2004; Pitt et al. 2002), undermining novelty claims.  
- The KL-based approach closely parallels prior methods, making this largely a domain-specific application rather than a novel technique.  
- Key modeling choices lack justification (e.g., two-component Gaussian mixture for p_meas, motor-noise param σ²report(s) = ρ₀² + ρ₁²s²).  
- KL divergence approximation assumptions (fixed Ntr, uniform stimuli) are untested and may bias results; sensitivity analyses are missing.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept conditional on major revisions—add comparisons to prior work, run robustness/sensitivity analyses, and justify/clarify modeling assumptions.  
- Minor fixes requested: clarify/avoid phrase "trivial mathematical fact" and correct the "nor/or" typo.  
- Overall: high significance for cognitive science and useful experimental-design tool, but methodological novelty for NIPS is limited without stronger contextualization and robustness checks.
