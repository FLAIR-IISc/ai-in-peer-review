- Proposes a mathematically elegant, general Bayesian model of perception combining stimulus-dependent noise with tractable inference; model is mathematically identifiable.  
- Introduces a novel class of Bayesian observer models built in four stages: sensation, perception, decision-making, and response.  
- Presents an efficient method to probe model identifiability using model support (log likelihood) and expected support across datasets.  
- Demonstrates the approach on two case studies: inferring priors in time-interval estimation and reconstructing prior/noise in speed perception.  
- Strengths: general framework for perceptual estimation, mathematical elegance, robustness plus analytical convenience, and an identifiability exploration method.  
- Main criticism: contribution is incremental and closely resembles prior work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Key concern: identifiability claims rely on model assumptions and do not resolve practical separability of sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Reviewer asks authors to explicitly discuss practical identifiability limitations in the revision.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized but assumes high technical expertise; needs more detailed mathematical derivations and clearer discussion of implications.  
- Originality: offers a novel model class and identifiability method but does not demonstrably advance the state of the art.  
- Significance: provides a useful general framework with experimental/data-interpretation implications, but limited demonstrable advancement.  
- Recommendations: add a review of recent Bayesian perceptual models and state advances, discuss practical identifiability limits, expand derivations, and clarify result implications.  
- Overall: reviewer supports acceptance to NIPS contingent on addressing the outlined concerns in revision.
