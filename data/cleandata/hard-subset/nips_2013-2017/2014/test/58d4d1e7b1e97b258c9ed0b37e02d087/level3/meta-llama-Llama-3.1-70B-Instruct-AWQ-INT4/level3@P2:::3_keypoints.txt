- Proposes a novel framework to test identifiability of Bayesian perception models, tackling degeneracy where different element combinations yield indistinguishable results.  
- Introduces a continuous class of Bayesian observer models parameterized by θ that covers many sensorimotor estimation tasks.  
- Develops an efficient method to explore identifiability within this continuous observer-model framework.  
- Main contribution: a general, a priori identifiability analysis framework for Bayesian perceptual models.  
- Demonstrates framework on two case studies (time-interval estimation and speed perception).  
- Case study results: interval-timing reconstruction requires strong experimental constraints and validation.  
- Case study results: speed-perception inference is generally robust and not prone to major degeneracies.  
- Strengths: clear, concise presentation, thorough case-study analysis, and detailed Bayesian observer-model description.  
- Weakness: contribution is incremental and closely resembles recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Weakness: identifiability claims depend on model assumptions and do not resolve practical distinctions between sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Originality: presents a novel testing framework but builds on and is not far removed from existing literature.  
- Significance: useful for improving psychophysics experimental design and understanding robustness of internal representations.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with effective use of equations and figures.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—add a review of recent Bayesian perceptual models and more thoroughly discuss practical identifiability limitations.
