- Proposes a novel framework to test practical identifiability of Bayesian perceptual models using KL divergence and MCMC.  
- Demonstrates the framework on time interval estimation and speed perception tasks, showing potential utility.  
- Writing and organization are clear; methodology is promising and methodologically sound.  
- Main concern: many strong assumptions (detailed in Section 2) limit generalizability and may drive results.  
- Needs a more thorough sensitivity analysis to determine whether identifiability findings stem from data or model structure.  
- Section 4.1: interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is observed but lacks statistical reliability measures (confidence intervals/p-values).  
- Section 4.2: missing critical validation showing the method can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior when the true prior is flat.  
- Clarify justification for modeling a continuous stimulus distribution with a discrete one (line 106): experiment limitation or simplification?  
- Explain and justify the logarithmic stimulus transform (line 134) and consider how alternative transforms affect identifiability.  
- Clarify likelihood estimation (line 143): do constraints and entropy maximization ensure unimodality, and how would bimodality affect results?  
- Justify choice of lattice-based prior representation over uniform priors and rationale for making the prior wider than true stimulus range (line 158).  
- Improve Figure 1: integrate panel b into a and remove derived p_est(s*|x) to enhance clarity.  
- Provide justification for approximations (lines 252–253), specify conditions where they hold, and discuss impacts if violated.  
- Equation 15 needs more formal justification beyond intuitive appeal.  
- Specify what is meant by “unnormalized density” and clearly state which distribution is being sampled from (line 262).  
- Clarify and justify the claim that learning priors would not matter in 2AFC tasks (line 365), since priors can influence 2AFC performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, sound techniques, clear writing, and practical demonstrations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: excessive assumptions, insufficient statistical rigor, missing negative-control validation, and multiple methodological clarifications needed.  
- Overall recommendation: worthwhile contribution but requires major revisions to address assumptions, statistical analyses, and methodological clarity.
