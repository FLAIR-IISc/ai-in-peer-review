- Presents a novel computational framework for assessing a priori identifiability of Bayesian observer models.  
- Argues model recovery is ill-posed because different priors, likelihoods, and loss functions can yield identical behavior.  
- Proposes a flexible parametric observer class and a KL-divergence-based method to map an experiment’s "identifiability landscape."  
- Enables researchers to assess parameter recoverability before collecting data.  
- Demonstrates the method on two case studies: interval timing and speed perception.  
- Strength: addresses a fundamental problem with an original, systematic, and technically sophisticated approach suitable for the conference.  
- Major weakness: relies on many strong assumptions (e.g., specific log-stimulus transform) that limit generalizability.  
- Insufficient justification for key approximations and modeling choices (e.g., Eqs. 14–15, lattice prior).  
- Case studies need stronger analysis; finding interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is unsurprising and condition differences should be tested for statistical reliability.  
- Critical validation missing in speed-perception case: must show the method can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior to prove specificity.  
- Reproducibility/clarity issues: need rationale for discrete stimulus model, whether entropy maximization ensures unimodality, and clarification of "unnormalized density."  
- Presentation problems: Figure 1 is confusing; overall the paper requires major revisions to substantiate claims and test robustness to assumptions.
