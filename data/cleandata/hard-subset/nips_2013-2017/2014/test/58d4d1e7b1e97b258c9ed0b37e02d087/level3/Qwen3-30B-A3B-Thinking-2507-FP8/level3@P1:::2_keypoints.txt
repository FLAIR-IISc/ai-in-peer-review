- Paper tackles non-identifiability of internal representations in Bayesian cognitive models and proposes an a priori identifiability testing framework using KL divergence.  
- Framework extends prior Bayesian observer work by offering a practical experimental-design methodology rather than just theory.  
- Applies framework to two case studies (time interval estimation, speed perception) to show how design choices affect identifiability.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-structured, and presents a novel, efficient framework addressing a key methodological challenge.  
- Strength: time-estimation case shows imposing a MAP-like loss hinders prior recovery due to parameter degeneracy.  
- Strength: measuring motor noise (MTR) resolves identifiability in the time-estimation case.  
- Strength: speed-perception case validates slow-speed priors and shows robust recovery even with decoupled noise representations.  
- Strength: generative model is flexible and computationally tractable, enabling systematic exploration of identifiability.  
- Strength: practical utility for experimental design—potentially a standard tool if assumptions are justified.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on strong, untested assumptions undermines generality of conclusions.  
- Weakness: log-stimulus transform (Eq. 2) presented as “natural” but its impact on identifiability is untested and alternatives aren’t explored.  
- Weakness: entropy-maximization claim to guarantee unimodality may be false; derivation does not rule out bimodal solutions.  
- Weakness: lattice prior choice (50% wider range) is arbitrary and lacks rationale or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: interchangeability of sensory and motor noise (Fig. 2c) is unsurprising and lacks reported statistical reliability (e.g., confidence intervals).  
- Weakness: Section 4.2 should explicitly validate recovery of the absence of a slow-speed prior (flat-prior sanity check) and emphasize it in results.  
- Technical concern: approximation E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x] (Eq. 15) lacks justification for required sample size N or error bounds.  
- Technical concern: notation/interpretation of "unnormalized density" (E(θ|θ*) ∝ e^〈log Pr(D|θ)〉) is ambiguous—posterior or dataset distribution?  
- Technical concern: dismissal of learning priors in 2AFC tasks is unjustified given prior studies (e.g., Stocker & Simoncelli, 2006) that used 2AFC.  
- Recommendation: weak accept—novel and methodologically sound but needs sensitivity analyses and justification of key assumptions.  
- Requested revisions: test/justify log-transform, validate flat-prior recovery, provide sensitivity analyses or citations for assumptions, and clarify technical ambiguities for reproducibility.
