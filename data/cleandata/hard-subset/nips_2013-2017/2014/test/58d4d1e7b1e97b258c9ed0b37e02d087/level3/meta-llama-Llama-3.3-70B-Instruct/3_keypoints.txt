- Proposes a Bayesian perceptual model combining robustness and computational tractability.  
- Model is identifiable, enabling clear evaluation and distinction of parameter estimates.  
- Novelty is incremental due to related prior work in the literature.  
- Main contribution: analysis of consistency of the Nitzan–Paroush optimal decision rule (weighted majority vote).  
- Provides sharp error estimates for the optimal rule when expert competence levels are known.  
- Presents two solutions for unknown competence levels: a frequentist approach and a Bayesian approach.  
- Frequentist approach lacks an optimal empirical decision rule but analyzes empirical rules in high- and low-confidence regimes.  
- Bayesian approach yields a simple, provably optimal empirical decision rule but cannot compute/estimate its induced error probability.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, well-organized, and easy-to-follow proofs.  
- Strength: provides a unified framework for analyzing consistency of weighted majority votes.  
- Raises interesting open problems, notably computing the error probability for the Bayesian optimal rule.  
- Limitation: assumes a simplistic model of expert competence (fixed correctness probability per expert).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add more related-work context, address model limitations, and review recent Bayesian perception models.
