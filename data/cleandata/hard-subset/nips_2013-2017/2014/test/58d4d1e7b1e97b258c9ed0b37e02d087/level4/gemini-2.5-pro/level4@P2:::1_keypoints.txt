- Addresses identifiability in Bayesian observer models: multiple priors, likelihoods, and loss functions can yield identical behavior.
- Proposes a systematic a priori framework using a flexible parametric observer class and KL-divergence–based MCMC to map the identifiability landscape.
- Demonstrates the method in two case studies: time-interval estimation (compare experimental designs) and speed perception (test robustness of the slow-speed prior).
- Significance: tackles a fundamental, widely relevant problem for Bayesian modeling and inference of internal representations.
- Practical utility: framework can guide experimental design to produce more identifiable parameter estimates.
- Clarity: paper is well written; four-stage observer model and the KL/log-likelihood rationale are explained clearly.
- Case studies: well-chosen and effective at illustrating design weaknesses and validating classic results.
- Major weakness: limited engagement with extensive prior literature on model comparison, parameter recovery, and identifiability (e.g., Pitt, Myung, Navarro).
- Major weakness: key modeling assumptions (mixture-of-Gaussians for likelihood and loss) lack justification and no sensitivity analysis to alternative forms is provided.
- Minor: scope focused on one-dimensional continuous estimation; should discuss extension challenges to discrete-choice paradigms (e.g., 2AFC).
- Minor: Figure 2 caption/text ambiguous about symbols indicating “true value.”
- Recommendation: promising and potentially strong; recommend acceptance conditional on revisions to situate within prior identifiability literature and to justify or test core modeling choices.
