- Proposes a mathematically elegant Bayesian model of perception that supports tractable inference and handles stimulus-dependent noise.
- Main contribution is a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perceptual models.
- Demonstrates framework effectiveness through two case studies that improve experimental design and assess robustness of inferred internal representations.
- Model is well-motivated with clear assumptions and rigorous mathematical derivations; paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Enables systematic, a priori evaluation of identifiability across different experimental designs.
- Framework is flexible and applicable to distinct case studies, suggesting broad utility for perceptual model evaluation.
- Contribution is incremental given prior similar Bayesian models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), requiring clearer differentiation from past work.
- Paper should include a review of recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state how it advances the state-of-the-art.
- The claim of “identifiable” should be qualified: identifiability holds under the paper’s mathematical assumptions and may not address alternative identifiability notions.
- Authors should discuss identifiability with respect to distinguishing noise sources (sensory, decision, response) in perceptual science.
- With revisions to contextualize novelty and acknowledge identifiability limitations, the work could make a significant impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel identifiability framework, successful case studies, strong motivation and derivations.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, insufficient contextualization vs. recent literature, and limited discussion of practical identifiability limitations.
