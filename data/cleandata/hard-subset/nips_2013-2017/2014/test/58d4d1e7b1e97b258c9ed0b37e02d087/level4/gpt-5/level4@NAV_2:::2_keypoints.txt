- Addresses identifiability in Bayesian observer models; clearly written, novel methodology, contribution suitable for NIPS.  
- Main results rest on many strong assumptions, reducing empirical impact.  
- Section 4.1: near-exchangeability of sensory and motor noise is unsurprising; questions necessity of separate modeling.  
- Need statistical tests to show which differences across experimental conditions are reliable; box plots are hard to interpret.  
- Section 4.2: must demonstrate recovery of a flat prior when the reference observer lacks a slow-speed prior.  
- Line 106: justify using a discrete stimulus sampling scheme for an underlying continuous variable.  
- Line 134: the log-like transform (Eq. 2) is a strong representational constraint driving identifiability claims.  
- Line 143: clarify whether the constraints plus maximum-entropy ensure unimodal measurement distributions (mixtures could be bimodal).  
- Line 158: justify why an observer prior would favor a lattice over uniform representation and the 50% wider prior assumption.  
- Figure 1: current layout is confusing; consider merging panels and removing derived p_est(s^*|x).  
- Line 252: state conditions on N for Stirling’s approximation and when E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x] holds; Eq.15 appears more heuristic than rigorous.  
- Line 262: clarify which “unnormalized density” is being sampled (distribution over datasets or over parameters).  
- Line 365: provide a brief justification for why prior learning does not matter in 2AFC.  
- Overall: innovative approach on an important problem, but results are not especially striking.
