- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with explicit mathematical components (sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, response distribution).
- Able to handle diverse scenarios (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise) while remaining computationally tractable, robust, and mathematically convenient.
- Demonstrates mathematical identifiability: parameters are uniquely determined from data under the model’s assumptions.
- Reviewer finds the framework mathematically elegant and recommends acceptance.
- Concern about novelty: significant methodological overlap with recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al., 2012, 2014), making the contribution incremental.
- Recommendation to add a dedicated literature review comparing recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state how this work advances beyond prior art.
- Identifiability claim needs refinement: mathematical identifiability under assumptions does not resolve experimental difficulties in separating sensory, decision, and response noise.
- Overall: technically sound and elegant but needs stronger differentiation from prior work and clarification of identifiability limitations; accept pending these revisions.
