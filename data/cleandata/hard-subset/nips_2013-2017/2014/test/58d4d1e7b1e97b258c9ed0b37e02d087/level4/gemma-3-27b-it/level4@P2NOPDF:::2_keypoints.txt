- Methodology is interesting and innovative; reviewer recommends acceptance (above threshold).
- Section 4.1: Interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is unsurprising; question necessity of including both noise sources.
- Statistical reliability of observed differences is unclear; box plots are hard to interpret and significance should be indicated.
- Section 4.2: Need control showing model can recover stimulus distribution when reference observer lacks a slow-speed prior.
- Line 106: Using a discrete stimulus distribution for an underlying continuous variable requires explicit clarification.
- Line 134: Success may depend heavily on the log-stimulus transform, implying identifiability may rely on strong pre-defined representational constraints.
- Line 143: Unclear whether constraints and entropy maximization guarantee a unimodal fit—bimodality could occur.
- Line 158: Need justification for the observer’s prior that favors a lattice representation and is 50% wider than the actual stimulus range.
- Figure 1: Current layout adds complexity; integrate panels and remove derived p_est(s*|x) to improve clarity.
- Line 252: Request clarification on conditions where Equation 14 approximations (Stirling, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) are valid and whether Eq.15 is justified.
- Line 262: Phrase "sample from this unnormalized density" is ambiguous—specify which density is meant.
- Line 365: Learning priors appears to have negligible impact in the 2AFC task.
- Overall: Innovative approach to an important problem, but empirical results are less substantial than expected.
