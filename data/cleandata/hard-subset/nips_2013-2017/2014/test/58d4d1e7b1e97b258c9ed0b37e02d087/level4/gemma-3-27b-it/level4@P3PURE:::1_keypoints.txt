- Paper proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models of behavioral data, summarizes Bayesian Decision Theory, and addresses identifiability by imposing constraints.  
- Authors introduce assumptions, analyze parameter differentiability, and apply the method to time-interval estimation and motion-perception studies.  
- Identifiability of internal representations/processes is a longstanding problem in cognitive science (e.g., Anderson 1978) and limits Bayesian-model adoption (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Reviewer finds the topic important and the manuscript generally well written and clearly presented.  
- Major concern 1: The paper overlooks substantial existing literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004), making its novelty unclear.  
- Major concern 2: Key assumptions appear arbitrary and insufficiently justified; it's unclear how reliant identifiability is on these specific assumptions.  
- Specific methodological questions: rationale for defining p_meas in Eq. 3 and whether the parameters in Eq. 12 truly capture all relevant cognitive/perceptual process characteristics.  
- Authors’ claim of robustness and broad expressive power does not by itself justify the restrictive assumptions used to achieve identifiability.  
- Recommendation: Provide a more thorough discussion and justification of the assumptions and relate the framework explicitly to prior model-comparison methods.  
- Minor comment: Line 53 - explain why the stated “trivial mathematical fact” holds rather than asserting it.  
- Minor comment: Line 73 - likely typo, second “nor” should be “or.”  
- Minor comment: Figure 2 - meaning of the star and dashed lines is unclear despite caption saying both represent the true value.  
- Overall summary: Important, well-written contribution but insufficiently connected to prior work and in need of stronger justification for its assumptions.
