- Proposes a framework combining Bayesian Decision Theory to diagnose and constrain non-identifiability in Bayesian cognitive models, demonstrated on time-interval estimation and motion-perception case studies.  
- Tackles an important, long-standing identifiability problem in cognitive modeling with clear writing and motivation.  
- Fails to engage key prior model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004), so its novelty and contextual contribution are unclear.  
- Several modeling assumptions appear unmotivated (e.g., the constraint on p_meas in Eq.3 and the parameter definition in Eq.12) and need stronger justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Recommend explicitly justifying each constraint and discussing how results change under alternative assumptions.  
- Minor editorial fixes: rephrase Line 53, change “nor” to “or” on Line 73, and clarify starred/dashed lines in Figure 2 caption.  
- Overall: important and well-written but should better situate itself in existing frameworks and justify key assumptions to strengthen its contribution.
