- Paper presents a Bayesian perception model with explicit formulations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distribution.  
- Strength: combines robustness (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise) with mathematical convenience and tractable inference.  
- Strength: model is mathematically identifiable—competing parameter estimates can be evaluated and distinguished.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Main weakness: contribution is largely incremental relative to recent Bayesian perception work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Suggestion: add a section reviewing recent Bayesian models and clearly articulate advances over prior work.  
- Concern about “identifiability” in the broader experimental sense: model cannot decisively separate sensory, decision, and response noise sources.  
- Overall summary: mathematically elegant framework for perceptual estimation but incremental given closely related prior literature.
