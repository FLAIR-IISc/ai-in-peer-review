- Addresses model identifiability in cognitive modeling; well-written and clear.
- Heavy reliance on strong assumptions that seem disproportionate to the impact of results.
- Methodology is interesting and innovative; reviewer recommends acceptance above threshold.
- Section 4.1: finding that sensory and motor noise are interchangeable is unsurprising; unclear necessity of including both.
- Need clarification which observed condition differences are statistically reliable; box plots are hard to interpret.
- Section 4.2: must show model can recover stimulus information when the reference observer lacks a slow-speed prior.
- Line 106: clarify why a continuous stimulus is drawn from a discrete distribution (likely experimental design rationale).
- Line 134: log-stimulus transform may be a strong constraint driving identifiability—Bayesian models may be identifiable only given this transform.
- Line 143: question whether constraints and entropy maximization guarantee a unimodal fit or allow bimodality.
- Line 158: justify prior favoring a lattice representation and a prior range 50% wider than the true stimulus range.
- Figure 1: current layout is confusing; suggest integrating panels and removing derived p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.
- Line 252: request elaboration on conditions for Eq.14 approximations (Stirling, E[xlogx] ≈ E[x]logE[x]) and justification for Eq.15 after many approximations.
- Line 262: clarify what is meant by “sample from this unnormalized density” (which density is referenced).
- Line 365: note lack of prior-learning influence in the 2AFC task; overall approach is innovative but results are not highly impactful.
