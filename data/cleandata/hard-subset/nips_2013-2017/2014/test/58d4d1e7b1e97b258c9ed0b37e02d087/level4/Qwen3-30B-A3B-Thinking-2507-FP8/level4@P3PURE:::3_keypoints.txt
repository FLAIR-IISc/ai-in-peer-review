- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with detailed mathematical formulations for sensory noise, likelihoods, priors, loss functions, and response distributions.  
- Key strength: balances robustness (handles diverse scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise) with computational tractability (analytically tractable inference).  
- Highlights mathematical identifiability that allows clear distinction between parameter estimates.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance; framework is mathematically elegant and broadly applicable across experimental paradigms.  
- Main concern: novelty is limited—substantial conceptual and methodological overlap with recent Bayesian work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), making the contribution incremental.  
- Recommendation: add a dedicated review of recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state how this model advances beyond existing approaches.  
- Criticism of identifiability claims: current identifiability depends on mathematical assumptions (fixed noise structures) rather than experimental design and cannot disambiguate sensory, decision, or response noise.  
- Request that the authors explicitly discuss this identifiability limitation and how it affects interpretation of perceptual errors.
