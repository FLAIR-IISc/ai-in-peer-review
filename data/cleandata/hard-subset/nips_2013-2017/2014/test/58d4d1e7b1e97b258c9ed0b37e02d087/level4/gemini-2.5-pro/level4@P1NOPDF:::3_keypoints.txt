- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with a complete mathematical framework (sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, response distribution).  
- Strength: successfully combines robustness with mathematical convenience, accommodating stimulus-dependent noise while remaining computationally tractable.  
- Strength: emphasizes mathematical identifiability—model parameters can be uniquely recovered under its assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: supportive of the paper and recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Main concern: conceptual novelty is limited; the model is similar to recent Bayesian perceptual models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), so the contribution feels incremental.  
- Suggestion: add a new section reviewing recent Bayesian perceptual models and explicitly state how this work advances beyond them.  
- Concern about identifiability: the formal identifiability result follows from model assumptions but does not address the practical identifiability issues faced by perceptual scientists.  
- Specific limitation: the model cannot disambiguate whether behavioral variability arises from sensory noise, decision noise, or response noise.  
- Request: authors should discuss this limitation and clarify the scope of their identifiability claims in the revised manuscript.
