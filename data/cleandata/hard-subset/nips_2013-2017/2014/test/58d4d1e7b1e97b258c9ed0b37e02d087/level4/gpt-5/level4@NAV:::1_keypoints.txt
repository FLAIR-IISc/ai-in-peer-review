- Paper presents a framework for comparing Bayesian models with behavioral data, reviewing Bayesian decision theory, identifying non-identifiability sources, specifying constraints to achieve identifiability, and applying the method to time-interval estimation and motion perception.  
- Engages a long-standing identifiability issue in cognitive science, connecting to Anderson (1978) and critiques of Bayesian approaches (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Main concern 1: Omits substantial prior literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004), so incremental contribution is unclear.  
- Main concern 2: Several constraints/assumptions appear arbitrary and insufficiently defended; unclear how strongly results depend on choices like the form of p_meas (Eq. 3) or fixed parameters (Eq. 12).  
- Recommendation: The paper should explicitly discuss and justify the specific assumptions used to render Bayesian models identifiable.  
- Minor: Line 53—don’t label the fact “trivial”; briefly explain why it holds.  
- Minor: Line 73—typo: the second “nor” should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2—clarify what the star and dashed lines indicate; caption currently ambiguous about the true value.  
- Overall judgment: Clear and well written on an important topic but missing essential links to prior related work.
