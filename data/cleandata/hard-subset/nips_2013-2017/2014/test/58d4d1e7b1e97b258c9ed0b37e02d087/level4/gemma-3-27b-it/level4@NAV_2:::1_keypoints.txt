- Proposes a framework to systematically assess identifiability of Bayesian models applied to perceptual behavior, with case studies on time-interval estimation and speed perception.
- Frames the problem within Bayesian Decision Theory, identifies inherent non-identifiability, and proposes constraints to achieve identifiability of model components.
- Introduces specific modeling assumptions, explores parameter space and differentiability to assess identifiability empirically.
- Paper addresses an important theoretical issue and is generally well written and executed.
- Major concern 1: Overlooks substantial prior work on model comparison and distinguishability (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang; Navarro, Pitt & Myung), making the novel contribution unclear.
- Major concern 2: Some assumptions (e.g., the functional form of p_meas in Eq.3 and the sufficiency of constraints in Eq.12) seem arbitrary and their sensitivity/justification is inadequately addressed.
- Minor: Line 53—“It is a trivial mathematical fact…” should include a brief explanation for readers.
- Minor: Line 73—consider replacing the second “nor” with “or” for clarity.
- Minor: Figure 2—clarify the meanings of the star and dashed lines in the caption (both currently appear to represent the true value).
- Overall: A valuable, well-written contribution that would benefit from stronger engagement with prior literature and clearer justification of key assumptions.
