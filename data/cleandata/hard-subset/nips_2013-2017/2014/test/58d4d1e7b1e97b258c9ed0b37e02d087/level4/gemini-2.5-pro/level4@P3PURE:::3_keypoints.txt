- Proposes a Bayesian perceptual model specifying sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Strength: combines robustness with mathematical convenience and remains computationally tractable.  
- Flexible framework accommodates experimental conditions like stimulus-dependent noise.  
- Emphasizes mathematical identifiability allowing reliable parameter estimation.  
- Overall supportive of acceptance.  
- Main concern: novelty—model is similar to recent Bayesian work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014) and may be incremental.  
- Recommend adding a dedicated related-work section that explicitly contrasts and clarifies the advances over prior models.  
- Identifiability caveat: although mathematically identifiable given assumptions, the model cannot disentangle sensory, decision, and response noise—so it does not resolve the common identifiability problem in perceptual science.  
- Suggest the authors acknowledge and discuss this limitation in the revised manuscript.
