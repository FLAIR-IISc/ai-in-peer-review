- Presents a Bayesian model of perception that explicitly specifies sensory noise, likelihood, priors, loss, and response distributions.  
- Strength: combines robustness with mathematical convenience, keeping inference tractable across varied experimental situations (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise).  
- Strength: model is mathematically identifiable—different parameter settings can be evaluated and distinguished.  
- Reviewer recommendation: positive—would be happy to see it accepted to NIPS.  
- Major concern: degree of novelty appears incremental relative to recent Bayesian perceptual/decision-making work.  
- Specific related work cited as similar: Acerbi et al. (2012) and Acerbi et al. (2014) in PLoS Computational Biology.  
- Revision request: add a dedicated related-work section reviewing recent Bayesian models and clearly state how this model is a substantive advance (methodologically, theoretically, or empirically).  
- Identifiability caveat: while parameters are identifiable under model assumptions, the model does not practically disambiguate subject errors due to sensory vs decision vs response noise—address this nuance in revision.  
- Overall summary: mathematically elegant and tractable framework for perceptual estimation, but novelty is limited given closely related prior work.
