- Proposes a systematic framework for testing identifiability in Bayesian observer models: reviews Bayesian Decision Theory, identifies non-identifiability sources, outlines constraints, builds a flexible observer model, and applies it to time-interval and speed-perception case studies.  
- Demonstrates how experimental design can be optimized to improve recovery of internal representations.  
- Places work in a broader theoretical context: identifiability has long been a core concern (e.g., Anderson 1978) and limits adoption of Bayesian models in psychology.  
- Major concern 1: Fails to adequately engage with existing model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004) and does not clarify novelty or differences from established methods.  
- Major concern 2: Key parametric assumptions lack justification (notably the p_meas mixture-of-Gaussians in Eq.3); unclear how conclusions depend on these arbitrary choices despite claims of flexibility.  
- Minor: Line 53’s phrase “It is a trivial mathematical fact...” should be explained rather than dismissed.  
- Minor: Line 73 has a wording error—second “nor” should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 is unclear—the star and dashed lines’ meaning (true value) is not obvious from the figure itself.
