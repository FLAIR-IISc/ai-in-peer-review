- Proposes a Bayesian model of perception with explicit mathematical formulations for sensory noise, likelihoods, priors, loss functions, and response distributions.  
- Model balances robustness and mathematical convenience, handling scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise while remaining computationally tractable.  
- Authors claim and demonstrate parameter identifiability within the model’s mathematical assumptions.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance to NIPS, praising the model’s mathematical elegance for formalizing perceptual estimation.  
- Contribution is viewed as incremental and closely aligned with recent Bayesian models in the literature (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Reviewer requests a new section reviewing recent Bayesian perception models that explicitly articulates how this work advances prior work.  
- Concerns raised about the use of “identifiable”: the model does not resolve practical identifiability between sensory, decision, and response noise in experimental data.  
- Suggestion to address the above limitation and clarify the scope of identifiability in the revised manuscript.
