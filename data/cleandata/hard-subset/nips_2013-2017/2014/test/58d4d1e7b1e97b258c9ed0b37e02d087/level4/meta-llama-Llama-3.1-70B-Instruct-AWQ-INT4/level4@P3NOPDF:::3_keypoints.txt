- Presents a comprehensive Bayesian perception model with equations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Balances robustness and mathematical convenience, allowing tractable inference even with stimulus-dependent noise.  
- Model identifiability is a strength, making parameter estimates easy to evaluate and distinguish.  
- Reviewer views the work as a solid formalization of perceptual estimation and potentially NIPS-worthy.  
- Novelty is limited — model is similar to recent Bayesian perception models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), so contribution is incremental.  
- Suggest adding a section reviewing recent Bayesian models and explicitly stating how this work advances beyond prior literature.  
- Note that identifiability claims rely on mathematical assumptions and may not resolve real experimental confounds (sensory vs. decision vs. response noise).  
- Recommend addressing the above identifiability caveat in the revised manuscript.  
- Overall: mathematically elegant framework but incremental relative to existing work.
