- Introduces a comprehensive Bayesian framework for perceptual tasks with formal modeling of sensory noise, likelihoods, priors, loss functions, and response distributions.  
- Model achieves an elegant balance of flexibility and computational tractability, handling diverse experimental scenarios including stimulus-dependent noise.  
- Provides efficient inference procedures suitable for practical use.  
- Emphasizes theoretical identifiability that permits systematic evaluation and distinction between parameter estimates under the model's assumptions.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance, citing mathematical rigor and clear practical applications for experimental design.  
- Concern that the contribution is incremental and closely aligned with recent Bayesian modeling work (e.g., Acerbi et al., 2012; 2014).  
- Recommendation to add a thorough review of recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly articulate advances over existing approaches.  
- Terminological concern: the paper’s claim of “identifiability” may be misleading because it does not resolve the common empirical problem of separating sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Authors should clarify the distinction and limitations of their identifiability claims to avoid misinterpretation.  
- Strengths: mathematically elegant framework, strong computational tractability, and clear applicability to experimental design.  
- Weaknesses: perceived incremental novelty and ambiguity in the use of “identifiability.”
