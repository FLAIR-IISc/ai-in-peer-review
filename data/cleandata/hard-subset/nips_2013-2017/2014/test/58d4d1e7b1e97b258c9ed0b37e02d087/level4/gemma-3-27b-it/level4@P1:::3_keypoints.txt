- Presents a Bayesian observer model emphasizing parameter identifiability and offering a mathematically convenient framework for perceptual estimation.
- Strength: balances robustness to scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise with computational tractability enabling analytical solutions and efficient model-space exploration.
- Emphasizes assessing whether different parameter settings produce indistinguishable behavior, addressing a key interpretability concern for Bayesian perception models.
- Reviewer finds the paper promising and recommends acceptance to NIPS.
- Model is mathematically elegant and can formalize perceptual estimation across a wide range of experiments.
- Concern about novelty: contribution feels incremental given existing Bayesian perception literature (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).
- Recommends adding a dedicated literature-review section to clearly situate the work and delineate its unique advances over prior models.
- Notes that claimed identifiability is constrained by the model’s internal assumptions and may not disentangle error sources (sensory vs decision vs motor).
- Suggests authors explicitly acknowledge identifiability limitations and discuss implications for interpreting perceptual data.
- Arguments for acceptance: elegant/comprehensive framework, addresses identifiability, useful for experimental design, demonstrates application to known tasks.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental advance and identifiability limits due to model assumptions that may not resolve broader perceptual-inference challenges.
