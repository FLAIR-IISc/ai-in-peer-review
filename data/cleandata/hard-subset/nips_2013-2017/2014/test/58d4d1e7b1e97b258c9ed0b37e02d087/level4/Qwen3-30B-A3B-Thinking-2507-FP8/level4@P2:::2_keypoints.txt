- Core contribution: presents a systematic, theoretically sound framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian observer models, useful for experimental design.
- Motivation: addresses the critical problem that multiple model component combinations can produce identical behavior, complicating recovery of internal representations.
- Scope: develops a comprehensive observer model class enabling efficient identifiability exploration and applies it to time interval estimation and speed perception.
- Strength: framework provides a priori evaluability of identifiability, filling a gap beyond ad hoc solutions in the literature.
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing with thorough mathematical development and attention to detail.
- Strength: case studies demonstrate utility; speed perception results show robust recovery of internal representations with minimal bias.
- Strength: offers actionable experimental recommendations (e.g., measuring motor parameters to reduce degeneracy).
- Weakness: framework depends on strong assumptions that may limit generality, notably the sensory transformation (Equation 2).
- Weakness: time interval estimation interpretation is unconvincing; the claim that narrower loss (MAP) worsens inference needs stronger justification.
- Weakness: insufficient demonstration that the framework can recover the absence of a prior, weakening claims about reliably identifying internal representations.
- Weakness: manuscript should more explicitly discuss limitations and extensions to other perceptual domains.
- Specific: discrete stimulus distributions appear to be a modeling convenience and should be justified given experimental practice using continuous stimuli.
- Specific: the log-stimulus transform (Eq. 2) is central and imposes a strong constraint that must be explicitly acknowledged.
- Specific: unimodality constraint for the sensory model (Eq. 3) requires more justification and clarification of the entropy maximization step.
- Specific: choice of prior lattice spacing (50% wider than true range) needs explanation to justify optimality over arbitrariness.
- Specific: Figure 1 is confusing; suggested integration of panels and removal of p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.
- Specific: approximations leading to Eq. 15 need clearer justification and specification of conditions for validity.
- Specific: phrase "sample from this unnormalized density" (Line 262) is ambiguous and should be clarified.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel methodological contribution with clear presentation; recommended for acceptance despite noted issues.
