- Introduces a Bayesian observer framework specifying sensory noise, likelihoods, priors, loss functions, and response generation.  
- Model combines flexibility (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise) with computational efficiency and tractable inference.  
- Strong emphasis on identifiability—ability to compare and distinguish competing parameterizations within the model class.  
- Reviewer is favorably inclined and would support acceptance to NIPS.  
- Main reservation: novelty is limited—approach is incremental and closely aligned with recent PLoS Computational Biology work (Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommendation to add a dedicated section reviewing recent Bayesian observer models and explicitly state substantive advances over prior art.  
- Important caveat on identifiability: demonstrated identifiability depends on modeling assumptions and does not disentangle sensory, decision (policy/loss), and response (motor/report) noise.  
- Request to explicitly acknowledge and address the above identifiability nuance in a revision.  
- Overall: mathematically elegant and unified formalism for perceptual estimation, but contribution is incremental.
