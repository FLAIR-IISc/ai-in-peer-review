The manuscript tackles an important issue in cognitive modeling, namely model identifiability. The text is well-written and clear, but relies on a lengthy list of assumptions that the reader must accept and understand, which somewhat diminishes the impact of the key results presented. Nevertheless, the methodology is interesting and innovative, and I believe the work meets the threshold for presentation at NIPS.

Regarding the key results: Section 4.1's finding on the interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is not particularly surprising, and it would be helpful to indicate which condition differences were statistically reliable. Additionally, the use of box plots could be clarified for better understanding.

In Section 4.2, it is crucial to demonstrate that the model can recover the fact that the reference observer did not have a slow-speed prior, in order to strengthen the argument.

Specific comments:

Line 106: The authors should clarify why the stimulus is drawn from a discrete distribution despite being a continuous variable. This may be due to typical experimental design, but an explanation would be beneficial.

Line 134: The log-stimulus transform (Equation 2) is motivated by psychophysical laws, and its incorporation may be a primary contributor to the success of this work. This suggests that the results may be heavily dependent on the assumption of strong representational transformations in the mind, rather than a general property of Bayesian models.

Line 143: It is unclear whether the constraints in Equation 4 and the maximization of differential entropy guarantee that Equation 3 will yield a unimodal distribution. In principle, the fit could result in a bimodal distribution.

Line 158: The authors should justify why the observer's prior favors the lattice representation over a uniform representation. What information does the observer have about the true stimulus distribution that allows them to have a prior 50% wider than the true range?

Figure 1: The figure is confusing and could be improved by integrating Figure 1b into Figure 1a and removing p_est(s^*|x), as it is a derived inference.

Line 252: The authors should comment on the conditions under which Equation 14 is approximately correct, specifically when Stirling's approximation of the factorial holds and when E[xlogx] ≈ E[x]logE[x]. By the time Equation 15 is derived, having abandoned priors on θ and made several questionable approximations, it seems that the equation is justified primarily by its intuitive result.

Line 262: The authors should specify which unnormalized density is being sampled from, presumably the dataset distribution.

Line 365: The authors should explain why learning of priors does not matter in the 2AFC scenario. Overall, the manuscript presents an innovative approach to an important problem, although the results are not overwhelming.
