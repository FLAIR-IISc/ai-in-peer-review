- Presents a framework for assessing a priori identifiability of Bayesian observer models in perceptual tasks.  
- Proposes a flexible class of parametric observer models (priors, likelihoods, loss) to explore identifiability and experimental design effects.  
- Demonstrates utility with two case studies: time-interval estimation (recovering priors) and speed perception (recovering prior and noise).  
- Frames the work as timely and addressing a fundamental identifiability problem in cognitive science and Bayesian brain debates.  
- Major concern 1: Fails to engage sufficiently with existing model comparison and identifiability literature (e.g., Navarro et al., Pitt et al.), making originality unclear.  
- Major concern 2: Conclusions depend on specific parametric assumptions (e.g., two-component Gaussian mixture for measurement); sensitivity to these choices is not shown.  
- Recommends stronger justification of modeling choices and/or sensitivity analyses to support generality of conclusions.  
- Overall: thoughtful and clearly presented but would be substantially strengthened by connecting to prior work and justifying assumptions.  
- Minor: Explain why the degeneracy holds rather than calling it “trivial.”  
- Minor: Typo—replace second “nor” with “or” in the motivation sentence.  
- Minor: Clarify Figure 2 caption—the meaning of the star and dashed lines is ambiguous.
