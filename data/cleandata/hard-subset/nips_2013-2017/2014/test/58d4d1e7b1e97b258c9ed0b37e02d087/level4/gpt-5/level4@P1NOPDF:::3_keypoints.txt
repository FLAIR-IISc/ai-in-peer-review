- Presents a Bayesian model that explicitly specifies sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss function, and response distribution.  
- Major strength: combines robustness with computational convenience, accommodating scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise while keeping inference tractable.  
- Emphasizes identifiability in the sense that distinct parameter settings are distinguishable under the model.  
- Reviewer likes the paper and recommends acceptance, praising its mathematical elegance and clear formalization across experimental paradigms.  
- Main concern: novelty appears incremental relative to recent Bayesian perception models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Requests a dedicated section reviewing recent Bayesian models and explicitly articulating dimensions of improvement (noise model scope, priors/loss generality, theoretical guarantees, tractability, new empirical predictions).  
- Notes a caveat on identifiability: mathematical identifiability under model assumptions differs from the broader practical identifiability problem in perception science.  
- Points out the model cannot unambiguously dissociate sensory, decision, and response noise from behavior; asks authors to clarify what is identifiable and what experimental manipulations could separate noise sources.  
- Summary judgment: mathematically elegant and useful framework but incremental given closely related prior work.
