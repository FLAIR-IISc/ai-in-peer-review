- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with explicit equations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss function, and response distribution.  
- Model balances robustness and mathematical convenience, handling cases like stimulus-dependent noise while remaining computationally tractable.  
- Mathematical identifiability allows evaluation and distinction of two parameter estimates, supporting parameter reliability assessment.  
- Identifiability is a consequence of the model’s mathematical assumptions and may not hold beyond those assumptions.  
- Provides a mathematically elegant framework for formalizing perceptual estimation across many experiments.  
- Novelty is questionable—model appears incremental and stylistically similar to recent PLoS Computational Biology papers (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Suggest adding a review of recent Bayesian perceptual models and explicitly stating how this model advances beyond prior work.  
- Important limitation: mathematical identifiability may not resolve whether behavioral errors stem from sensory, decision, or response noise—this practical identifiability issue should be addressed.  
- Overall valuable but impact would improve by clarifying novelty and acknowledging/addressing broader identifiability limitations.
