- Presents a Bayesian perception model with formal mathematical components: sensory noise, likelihoods, priors, loss functions, and response distributions.  
- Model balances robustness and computational convenience, supporting stimulus-dependent noise while keeping inference tractable.  
- Model identifiability is emphasized, enabling straightforward evaluation and differentiation of parameter estimates.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS, citing a mathematically refined framework applicable to many experiments.  
- Concern about novelty: model closely aligns with recent Bayesian perception work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), making the contribution incremental.  
- Suggests adding a literature review of recent Bayesian perception models and clearly stating the new advances over existing work.  
- Notes identifiability claims depend on model assumptions and may not resolve practical identifiability issues faced by perceptual scientists.  
- Recommends discussing limitations in distinguishing sensory, decision, and response noise in experimental data.  
- Overall assessment: mathematically elegant framework but with only incremental novelty relative to prior work.
