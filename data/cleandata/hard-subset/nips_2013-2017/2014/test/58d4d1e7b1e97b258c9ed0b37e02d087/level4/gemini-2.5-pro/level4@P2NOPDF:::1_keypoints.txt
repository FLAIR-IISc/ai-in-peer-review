- Paper proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models of behavior, tackling long-standing non-identifiability issues in Bayesian Decision Theory and demonstrating solutions on time-interval estimation and motion perception case studies.

- Manuscript is well-written and the proposed formalization and solutions are generally reasonable and effective.

- Major concern 1: The paper overlooks substantial prior work on model comparison/identifiability (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004) and needs a discussion contrasting the novel contributions with these established methods.

- Major concern 2: The framework depends on several assumptions to enforce identifiability whose justification is unclear or arbitrary; stronger rationale and sensitivity analyses are needed, especially for the measurement probability (Eq. 3) and the parameterization (Eq. 12).

- Minor: Line 53’s claim “It is a trivial mathematical fact…” should be briefly explained rather than labeled trivial.

- Minor: Line 73 likely contains a wording error—second “nor” should be “or.”

- Minor: Figure 2 caption is ambiguous about what the star and dashed lines represent; clarify each symbol.
