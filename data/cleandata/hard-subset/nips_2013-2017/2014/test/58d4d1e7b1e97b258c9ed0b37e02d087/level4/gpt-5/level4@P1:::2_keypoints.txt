- Addresses identifiability in Bayesian perception models with a general observer class, an a priori distinguishability measure (expected log-likelihood/KL), and MCMC-based exploration; approach is interesting and clearly written.

- Framework relies on many strong assumptions and the empirical takeaways are modest given the setup, though contribution merits acceptance.

- Section 4.1: Partial interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is unsurprising; need explicit statistical tests of condition differences and clearer box-plot interpretation.

- Section 4.2: Must demonstrate recovery of absence of a slow-speed prior when the reference observer lacks it and consider moving that result from the supplement to the main text.

- Line 106: Clarify why a discrete stimulus distribution is used despite stimuli being continuous—presumably reflecting typical experimental stimulus control.

- Line 134: The log-stimulus transform is a strong constraint that may drive identifiability; discuss sensitivity of conclusions to this representational choice.

- Line 143: Clarify whether constraints plus maximum-entropy guarantee unimodality of Eq.3 and how bimodality is prevented in practice.

- Line 158: Justify the lattice prior representation choice over alternatives and the assumption of prior support being 50% wider than the true stimulus range.

- Figure 1: Figure is confusing; suggest integrating panel 1b into 1a and removing derived p_est(s*|x) to reduce clutter.

- Line 252: State conditions on N where Stirling’s approximation and E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x] hold, and provide a sensitivity analysis or error bounds for Eq.15.

- Line 262: Specify which “unnormalized density” is meant for sampling—MCMC target over θ per Eq.13 or the dataset distribution.

- Line 365: Explain why learning of priors is negligible in 2AFC and clarify assumptions that justify ignoring learning of the experimental stimulus distribution.

- Overall assessment: Innovative, important, and well-written work but weakened by many assumptions; clarifications would strengthen the paper, recommend acceptance.
