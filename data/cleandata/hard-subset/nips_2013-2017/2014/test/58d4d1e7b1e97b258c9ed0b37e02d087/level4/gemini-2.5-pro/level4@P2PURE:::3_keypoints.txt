- Introduces a Bayesian perceptual model with sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response components.  
- Strength: combines modeling flexibility (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise) with computational tractability for inference.  
- Claim: model parameters are identifiable, allowing reliable estimation and differentiation.  
- Presents a mathematically elegant framework potentially useful for analyzing diverse perceptual experiments.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance but raises concerns about novelty, calling the contribution incremental.  
- Notes close conceptual similarity to recent Bayesian perceptual models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Suggests adding a literature review section that clearly contrasts the proposed model with recent related work and highlights advances.  
- Cautions that the paper’s identifiability claim stems from specific model assumptions and does not resolve the broader problem of disentangling sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Recommends explicitly discussing this limitation and clarifying the scope of the identifiability claim in the revision.
