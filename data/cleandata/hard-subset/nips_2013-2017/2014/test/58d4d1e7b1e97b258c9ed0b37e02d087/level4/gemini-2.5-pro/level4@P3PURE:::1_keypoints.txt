- Proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models on behavioral data, summarizing Bayesian Decision Theory and sources of non-identifiability and proposing constraints to make terms identifiable.  
- Introduces specific assumptions to constrain each term, studies parameter differentiability, and demonstrates the approach on time-interval estimation and motion-perception case studies.  
- Addresses an important, longstanding identifiability problem in cognitive science and psychology.  
- Major concern 1: The paper largely omits extensive prior literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004), making it hard to assess novelty and contribution.  
- Major concern 2: Several assumptions appear arbitrary and under-justified—specifically the definition of p_meas (Eq. 3) and the sufficiency of the parameterization in Eq. 12—raising questions about sensitivity to these choices.  
- Requests clearer justification and discussion of how dependent the framework’s success is on the chosen assumptions.  
- Minor: Line 53’s “It is a trivial mathematical fact…” should be briefly explained for readers who may not find it trivial.  
- Minor: Line 73 likely has a typo—second “nor” should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 caption is ambiguous—unclear what the star and dashed lines represent (both seem to indicate “true value”).
