- Overall: Tackles an important identifiability problem, well-written, but relies on many assumptions relative to its key findings; acceptable for presentation.  
- Major concern: The manuscript depends on a lengthy list of assumptions that must be accepted to interpret results.  
- Sec 4.1: Interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is expected; authors should state which condition differences are statistically reliable.  
- Sec 4.2: Must demonstrate the model can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior in the reference observer.  
- Line 106: Clarify why a continuous stimulus is drawn from a discrete distribution (likely experimental design origin).  
- Line 134: The log-stimulus transform is critical; results suggest Bayesian model identifiability holds only under strong representational constraints.  
- Line 143: Clarify whether constraints and entropy maximization ensure unimodality of Equation 3 or allow bimodal solutions.  
- Line 158: Justify why the observer prior favors a lattice and why the prior is set 50% wider than the true stimulus range.  
- Figure 1: Current figure is confusing; integrate panels and remove derived p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Line 252: Specify conditions on N for Equation 14’s approximations (e.g., Stirling, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]); Equation 15’s derivation relies on questionable approximations.  
- Line 262: Clarify which "unnormalized density" is meant when instructing to sample from it (e.g., dataset distribution?).  
- Line 365: Explain why prior learning would be irrelevant in 2AFC tasks.  
- Summary: Innovative methodological contribution but results and assumptions limit impact.
