- Paper tackles model identifiability in Bayesian observer models, highlighting degeneracy where different priors/likelihoods/losses yield identical behavior.
- Authors propose an a priori identifiability-testing framework using (1) a flexible parametric class of Bayesian observer models and (2) an MCMC-based sampler of the identifiability landscape via expected posterior density.
- Framework demonstrated on two case studies: prior recovery in time-interval estimation and priors/noise recovery in speed perception.
- Strength: addresses a fundamentally important, timely problem in cognitive science with clear theoretical relevance.
- Strength: technical approach is well-conceived; flexible yet tractable observer model and use of expected log-likelihood/KL to map identifiability is elegant.
- Strength: paper is generally clear and case studies give concrete, non-obvious insights for experimental design (e.g., MAP-like loss harms prior recovery).
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with extensive prior literature on identifiability and model comparison in psychology—hard to assess novelty.
- Weakness: method resembles established techniques (parameter recovery, likelihood-geometry methods); authors should compare and contrast with works like Pitt/Myung/Zhang and Navarro/Pitt/Myung.
- Weakness: inadequate justification for chosen parametric forms (e.g., two-Gaussian likelihood); unclear sensitivity of results and which observer models are excluded.
- Weakness: potential bias from restrictive modeling class; need discussion of limitations or robustness checks.
- Minor: line 53 wording "trivial mathematical fact" is dismissive—explain degeneracy briefly.
- Minor: line 73 likely typo ("nor" → "or").
- Minor: Figure 2 caption confusing—star and dashed lines both appear to mark true parameter.
- Summary: technically strong and useful but undermined by lack of literature situating and weak justification of modeling choices; recommend revision before acceptance.
