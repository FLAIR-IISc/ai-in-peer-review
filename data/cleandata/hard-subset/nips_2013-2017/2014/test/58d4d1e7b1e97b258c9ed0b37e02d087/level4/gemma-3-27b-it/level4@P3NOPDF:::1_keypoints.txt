- Proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models of behavioral data, summarizing Bayesian Decision Theory and addressing identifiability with constraints.  
- Explores parameter differentiability and demonstrates the framework via re-analysis of time-interval estimation and motion-perception studies.  
- Tackles the important, longstanding identifiability problem in cognitive/psychological modeling and is generally well-written.  
- Major concern: overlooks relevant model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al., Navarro et al.); needs explicit discussion of similarities/differences and prior methods.  
- Major concern: key assumptions (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter choices in Eq.12) appear arbitrary; sensitivity to these assumptions is not evaluated and requires stronger justification.  
- Requests exploration of alternative constraining choices and discussion of how well the parameter definitions capture cognitive/perceptual processes.  
- Minor: Line 53—avoid calling a statement “trivial” and briefly explain why it’s true.  
- Minor: Line 73—second “nor” likely should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 legend unclear—clarify distinction between the star and dashed lines.  
- Overall: paper is insightful but would be strengthened by connecting to existing literature and more robust justification/evaluation of assumptions.
