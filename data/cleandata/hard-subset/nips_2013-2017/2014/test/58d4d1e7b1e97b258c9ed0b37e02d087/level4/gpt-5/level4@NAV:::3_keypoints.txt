- Presents a Bayesian perceptual model with explicit mathematical formulations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss function, and response distribution.  
- Key advantage: combines robustness with analytical convenience, handling scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise while keeping inference tractable.  
- Manuscript emphasizes model identifiability (alternative parameter settings can be assessed and distinguished).  
- Reviewer favorable—finds the framework mathematically elegant and broadly applicable to perceptual estimation.  
- Major reservation: contribution appears incremental and closely resembles recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommendation: add a review section of recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state how this work advances beyond prior approaches.  
- Caveat on identifiability claim: identifiability depends on model assumptions and the model cannot decisively separate sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Revision should clarify that the model does not resolve the broader identifiability concerns most perceptual scientists prioritize.
