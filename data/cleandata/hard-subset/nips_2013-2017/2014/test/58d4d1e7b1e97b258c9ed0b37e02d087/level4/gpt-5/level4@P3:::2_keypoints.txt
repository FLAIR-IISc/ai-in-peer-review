- Paper studies identifiability of priors, likelihoods, and loss functions from behavior in Bayesian observer models.  
- Introduces a parametric observer family and an a priori identifiability analysis producing an “expected posterior” proportional to exp(sum of KLs).  
- Framework is MCMC-samplable and used to map identifiability landscapes across parameterizations.  
- Case study (interval timing): sensory and motor noise are hard to distinguish, degrading prior recovery.  
- Case study (interval timing): assuming a narrow, MAP-like loss can worsen prior identifiability.  
- Case study (interval timing): independently measuring motor noise substantially improves inference.  
- Case study (speed perception): slow-speed prior and noise characteristics can be reliably recovered, even with mismatched internal likelihoods.  
- Situates work amid debates on Bayesian explanations and prior inverse-inference literature; contribution is systematic pre-experimental identifiability analysis.  
- Strength: addresses a timely, important problem where non-identifiability undermines inferences.  
- Strength: methodological contribution recasting expected model support as sum of KLs for efficient exploration.  
- Strength: provides concrete experimental design guidance and clear modeling pipeline.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on strong parametric assumptions (sensory transforms, mixture-of-Gaussians, lattice priors) that may drive identifiability.  
- Weakness: some findings are unsurprising (e.g., sensory/motor confound), reducing novelty impact.  
- Weakness: key approximations leading to the expected log-likelihood and unnormalized expected posterior are under-explained and lack quantitative error analysis.  
- Weakness: reporting and figures (notably Figure 1) are confusing; statistical reliability of differences is not always clear.  
- Technical request: justify approximations leading to Eq. 14–15 and characterize when they hold (e.g., Ntr, Stirling; E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) with simulations.  
- Technical request: include sensitivity analyses to show how identifiability conclusions change under parametric misspecification (e.g., sensory transform, mixture families).  
- Clarity request: report credible intervals/SDs on KL-based metrics and explicitly test reliability of design differences.  
- Clarity request: simplify/redo Figure 1 (integrate panels, remove derived quantities, annotate variable flow).  
- MCMC transparency: specify the unnormalized density sampled (Eq. 13), proposal, and convergence diagnostics.  
- Experimental control: include a flat-prior control to test recovery when the true observer lacks a slow-speed prior.  
- Visualization request: show 2D posterior slices for sensory vs. motor noise and demonstrate how independent motor measurements break degeneracy.  
- Specific manuscript queries: justify discrete stimulus modeling, log-like transform choice, unimodality constraints, lattice prior range, and assumptions behind translating 2AFC to estimation.  
- Overall assessment: innovative, useful framework with actionable recommendations, but needs approximations checks, sensitivity analyses, additional controls, and clearer reporting before acceptance.
