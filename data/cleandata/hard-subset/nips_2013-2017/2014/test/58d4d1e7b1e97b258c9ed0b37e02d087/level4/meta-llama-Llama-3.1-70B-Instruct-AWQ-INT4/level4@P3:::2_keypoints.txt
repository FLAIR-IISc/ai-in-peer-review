- Tackles model identifiability in cognitive/Bayesian perception models with a novel testing framework.  
- Well-written, clear, and well-organized manuscript.  
- Presents a continuous class of Bayesian observer models enabling efficient exploration of identifiability.  
- Demonstrates method on two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Framework can rank experimental designs by their ability to resolve parameter degeneracies.  
- Technically sound but some methodological clarifications are needed.  
- Authors assume stimuli are drawn from a discrete distribution; this assumption needs clearer justification.  
- Results may rely heavily on the log-stimulus transform, which imposes a strong inference constraint.  
- Constraints in Eq. 4 and maximizing differential entropy may not guarantee Eq. 3 is unimodal—needs discussion.  
- Observer prior favors a lattice representation over uniform, but it’s unclear what knowledge justifies a prior 50% wider than the true range.  
- Section 4.1 should report which condition differences are statistically reliable.  
- Section 4.2 must show the model can recover absence of a slow-speed prior in the reference observer.  
- Manuscript is original, significant for experiment design in perception, and suitable for NIPS presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance focus on unclear assumptions (discrete stimuli, log-transform influence, entropy/constraint issues).
