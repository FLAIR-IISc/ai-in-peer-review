- Paper tackles model identifiability in Bayesian perception models with a novel framework for systematic testing, judged above acceptance threshold.  
- Core contribution: a method to assess how well model parameters can be recovered given an experimental design.  
- Case study 1: sensory and motor noise appear interchangeable, underscoring difficulty disentangling variability sources; statistical reliability of condition differences needs clarification.  
- Case study 2: shows robust prior inference for a slow-speed prior, but should demonstrate ability to detect the absence of such a prior.  
- Overall results are not groundbreaking, but the methodology is interesting and innovative.  
- The approach relies on numerous and strong assumptions, which may limit generalizability.  
- Clarify use of a discrete stimulus distribution despite an underlying continuous variable (Line 106).  
- Emphasize that the success of the logarithmic stimulus transform depends on the strong representational assumption (Equation 2, Line 134).  
- Explain whether constraints and entropy maximization guarantee unimodality of Equation 3 or allow bimodal solutions (Line 143).  
- Justify the lattice prior choice (Equation 4) over a uniform prior and explain why the observer would have a prior 50% wider than the true range (Line 158).  
- Simplify Figure 1 by integrating panels and removing the derived p_est(s*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Discuss validity conditions for approximations used to derive Equation 14 (Stirling’s approximation, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) and the robustness of Equation 15 (Line 252).  
- Clarify which density is being sampled from when referring to sampling an “unnormalized density” (Line 262).  
- Provide further explanation for the claim that prior learning does not matter in the 2AFC task (Line 365).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, theoretically sound framework with practical utility for researchers; rejection arguments: limited impact, strong assumptions, and need for clearer technical exposition and limitations.
