- Presents a Bayesian observer model for perception that balances robustness and computational tractability.
- Model integrates sensory noise, likelihoods, priors, loss functions, and response distributions in a mathematically convenient framework.
- Central emphasis on parameter identifiability — assessing whether model parameters can be reliably estimated from data.
- Strength: elegant mathematical formulation with potential broad applicability to psychophysical tasks.
- Demonstrates utility with case studies in time-interval estimation and speed perception.
- Valuable contribution: introduces an a priori identifiability analysis to inform experimental design and assess recovery of internal representations.
- Main weakness: contribution appears incremental given extensive prior Bayesian-perception literature.
- Prior work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014) shares similar aims and methods; the manuscript needs clearer articulation of novel advances.
- Recommendation: add a dedicated section reviewing recent Bayesian models and explicitly position and compare the current work to prior approaches.
- Identifiability claims need nuance: mathematical identifiability under model assumptions does not resolve broader inferential ambiguities in perceptual science.
- Specifically, the framework may not distinguish errors arising from sensory noise, decision processes, or motor responses; authors should acknowledge and discuss these limitations.
- Overall: solid, well-executed paper; leans toward acceptance if revisions address novelty positioning and clarify identifiability limitations.
