Paper addresses identifiability in Bayesian cognitive models with clear writing and generally sound technical execution.  
Proposes constraints and differentiable parameterizations to resolve non-identifiability, demonstrated on time-interval estimation and motion perception.  
Major concern: insufficient engagement with existing model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004), leaving the paper’s novelty unclear.  
Needs explicit comparison and demonstration of how the proposed framework advances beyond established methods for model distinguishability.  
Major concern: key assumptions lack justification—specific form of p_meas (Eq.3) and the claim that Eq.12 “captures all characteristics” appear ad hoc.  
Paper should discuss scope, limitations, and sensitivity of these assumptions, and whether main results depend critically on those choices.  
Minor issues: explain the “trivial mathematical fact” (line 53), fix grammar ("nor"→"or" at line 73), and clarify Figure 2 caption (distinguish star vs dashed lines).  
Overall: technically competent and potentially valuable but novelty is overstated; recommend major revisions to address literature engagement and justify key constraints.
