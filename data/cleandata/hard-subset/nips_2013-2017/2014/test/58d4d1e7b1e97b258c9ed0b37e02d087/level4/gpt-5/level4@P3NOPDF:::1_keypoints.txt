- Summary: Proposes a general framework to compare Bayesian models of cognition by imposing identifiability constraints within Bayesian Decision Theory and applies it to time-interval estimation and motion perception.

- Strength: Addresses a central problem—making Bayesian cognitive models empirically distinguishable and testable.

- Strength: Writing and paper structure are clear and logically progress from theory to applied demonstrations.

- Strength: Explicit emphasis on identifiability is timely and valuable given concerns about Bayesian model flexibility.

- Major concern: Limited engagement with prior literature on model comparison and distinguishability, making the novelty hard to assess.

- Evidence cited: Overlooks relevant foundational work (Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004), Anderson (1978), and critiques of Bayesian flexibility (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).

- Recommendation: Provide a comparative mapping between the proposed constraints/diagnostics and established tools (Bayes factors, marginal likelihoods, MDL, posterior predictive checks, model recovery).

- Recommendation: Explicitly connect the framework to model mimicry results and cases of observational equivalence across priors/likelihoods/decision rules.

- Recommendation: Discuss how the framework addresses specific criticisms raised by Jones & Love (2011) and Bowers & Davis (2012).

- Major concern: Reliance on strong or unmotivated modeling assumptions (e.g., specific form of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameterization in Eq.12) without theoretical or empirical justification.

- Recommendation: Run sensitivity analyses varying p_meas functional form, noise distributions, priors, and decision rules and report effects on model rankings and parameters.

- Recommendation: Conduct model recovery studies with generative models including alternatives to Eq.3/Eq.12 and report confusion matrices and recovery rates.

- Recommendation: Demonstrate practical identifiability (profile likelihoods, posterior curvature, simulation-based calibration) and highlight parameter trade-offs and near-non-identifiabilities.

- Recommendation: Assess prior sensitivity and behavior under mild model misspecification.

- Recommendation: Quantify model separability using distinguishability metrics (KL divergence, Fisher information, predictive cross-validation).

- Clarity/presentation: Clarify visual annotations in Figure 2 (star vs. dashed lines), explain the “trivial mathematical fact” at Line 53 or cite/briefly justify it, fix the Line 73 typo, and consolidate/maintain consistent notation across sections.

- Assessment of applications: Add quantitative predictive performance metrics (cross-validated log likelihood, WAIC/LOO), posterior predictive checks targeting bias/variability/lapse behavior, report parameter uncertainty and identifiability, and release code and data for reproducibility.

- Overall evaluation: Well-written and important work, but its contribution is weakened by insufficient positioning relative to prior work and under-justified assumptions; with clearer framing, sensitivity analyses, and stronger empirical diagnostics it could be a valuable contribution.
