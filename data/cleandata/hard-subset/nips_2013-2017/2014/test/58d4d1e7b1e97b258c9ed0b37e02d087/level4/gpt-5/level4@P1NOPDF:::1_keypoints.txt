- Proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models using behavioral data, recapping Bayesian decision theory and identifying sources of non-identifiability.  
- Describes constraints that render framework components identifiable, imposes specific constraints, tests parameter distinguishability, and applies the approach to time-interval estimation and motion perception.  
- Notes identifiability of internal representations/processes is a longstanding, central issue in cognitive science and relevant to debates about Bayesian modeling.  
- Major concern 1: The paper largely overlooks substantial prior literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang; Navarro, Pitt & Myung), making novelty and contribution unclear.  
- Major concern 2: Several key assumptions (e.g., form of p_meas in Eq.3; fixed parameter values in Eq.12) appear arbitrary or under-justified, and robustness to alternative constraints is not assessed.  
- Recommendation: More fully discuss and justify modeling constraints and evaluate sensitivity of results to plausible alternative assumptions.  
- Minor: Line 53 should briefly justify the “trivial” mathematical fact rather than label it trivial; Line 73 likely has a typo (“nor” should be “or”).  
- Minor: Figure 2 caption unclear—specify what the star and dashed lines denote, since caption suggests both indicate the true value.  
- Overall: Well-written and important topic, but missing crucial links to prior related work and stronger justification/robustness checks for key assumptions.
