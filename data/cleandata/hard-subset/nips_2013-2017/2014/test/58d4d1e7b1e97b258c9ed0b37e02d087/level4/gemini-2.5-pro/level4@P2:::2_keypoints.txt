- Addresses identifiability in Bayesian observer models and shows recovering internal representations from behavior is ill-posed.  
- Proposes a novel framework that tests model identifiability a priori using a flexible parametric class of Bayesian observers.  
- Uses an efficient KL-divergence–based sampling method to map an "identifiability landscape" and reveal well-constrained vs degenerate parameters.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two case studies: time-interval estimation (comparing designs) and speed perception (validating slow-speed prior findings).  
- Strength: tackles a fundamental, often-overlooked problem and provides a practical tool to assess experimental designs before data collection.  
- Strength: technically solid modeling class capturing stimulus-dependent noise, non-Gaussian likelihoods, flexible priors, and non-quadratic losses; efficient expected log-likelihood method.  
- Strength: clear, well-written paper that explains the degeneracy problem and the proposed solution effectively.  
- Weakness: conclusions depend on strong parametric assumptions (sensory mapping, Gaussian-mixture representations, max-entropy fixes) limiting generality.  
- Weakness: case studies mainly reproduce expected trade-offs (sensory vs motor noise) and validate prior work, offering limited new scientific insight.  
- Weakness: use of approximations and KL-divergence proxy for expected log-likelihood feels heuristic and needs stronger theoretical justification.  
- Request: indicate statistical significance in Figure 2 comparisons (e.g., BSL vs MTR) to clarify meaningful differences.  
- Request: summarize control showing recovery of a flat prior (FLA condition) in main text to validate method robustness.  
- Clarify/modeling questions: justify discrete stimulus assumption, unimodality enforcement in max-entropy mixture, and rationale for prior as regularly spaced Gaussians with 50% wider range.  
- Clarify presentation and methods: simplify Figure 1 (remove/merge p_est node), specify which density is sampled by MCMC (is E(θ|θ*) treated as unnormalized), and justify assuming no learning of experimental prior in 2AFC.  
- Overall: interesting, innovative, and practically valuable contribution; recommended for acceptance pending responses to the above concerns.
