- Presents a Bayesian perception model specifying sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Strength: combines robustness with computational convenience, handling settings like stimulus-dependent noise while keeping inference tractable.  
- Emphasizes parameter identifiability, enabling competing parameterizations to be evaluated and distinguished.  
- Reviewer is generally positive and would support acceptance to NIPS.  
- Framework is mathematically elegant and broadly applicable across many perceptual estimation paradigms.  
- Main reservation: novelty is modest—model closely aligns with recent Bayesian work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014) and feels incremental.  
- Recommendation: add a dedicated section positioning the work within recent Bayesian models and explicitly state what is genuinely new (generality, tractability results, identifiability proofs, distinct predictions or better fits).  
- Concern: statistical identifiability under the model does not resolve practical separation of behavioral error sources (sensory vs decision vs motor noise).  
- Request to explicitly discuss this limitation, provide simulations/theoretical demonstrations of parameter trade-offs, and suggest experimental manipulations to improve separability.  
- Overall: mathematically polished and useful, but needs stronger related-work comparison and clearer scope/limits of identifiability to bolster claims of novelty.
