- Proposes a Bayesian model of perception with full mathematical formulation (sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, response).  
- Model is robust (handles scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise) and computationally tractable for efficient inference.  
- Emphasizes model identifiability, aiding clear evaluation and parameter distinction.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS and describes the framework as mathematically elegant for perceptual estimation.  
- Main concern: contribution appears incremental and closely aligned with recent literature (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Suggests adding a dedicated review of recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly articulating the paper’s advances over existing work.  
- Notes a caveat that identifiability relies on model assumptions and the model cannot definitively separate sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Overall summary: elegant and promising framework but limited novelty given closely related prior work.
