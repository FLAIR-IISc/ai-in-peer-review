- Addresses an important challenge (model identifiability); writing is clear and well‑structured.  
- Relies on a substantial, lengthy set of assumptions that must be accepted relative to the impact of the results.  
- Methodology is innovative and compelling, making the paper above the acceptance threshold.  

- Section 4.1: Interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is expected; authors should clarify inclusion of both and mark which condition differences are statistically reliable.  
- Section 4.2: Must demonstrate the model can recover the absence of a slow‑speed prior when the reference observer lacks it.  

- Line 106: Stimulus is treated as drawn from a discrete distribution despite being continuous—authors should clarify this experimental design choice.  
- Line 134: The log‑stimulus transform is central and imposes strong constraints; identifiability appears contingent on assuming specific representational transforms.  
- Line 143: Do the constraints and entropy maximization guarantee unimodality of Equation 3’s fit, or could it produce bimodality?  
- Line 158: Why should the observer’s prior favor a lattice representation over uniform, and what justifies a prior 50% wider than the true stimulus range?  
- Figure 1: Figure is confusing; recommend integrating panel b into a and removing the derived p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Line 252: Specify conditions on N for Equation 14’s approximations (e.g., Stirling’s validity and when E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x])—Equation 15 rests on questionable approximations.  
- Line 262: Clarify which "unnormalized density" is being sampled (dataset distribution or another density).  
- Line 365: The claim that prior learning does not matter in 2AFC requires justification.  

- Overall: Innovative approach tackling an important problem, but results are limited and less than overwhelming.
