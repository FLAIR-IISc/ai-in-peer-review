- Presents a framework for model comparison of Bayesian models applied to behavioral data, grounded in Bayesian Decision Theory.  
- Identifies non-identifiability issues in Bayesian models and outlines constraints to make components identifiable.  
- Proposes specific assumptions to constrain the framework and investigates parameter differentiability.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two applications: time interval estimation and motion perception.  
- Emphasizes the long-standing identifiability challenge in cognitive science (e.g., Anderson 1978).  
- Notes recent critiques that identifiability limits Bayesian models' adoption (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Major concern: paper largely overlooks existing cognitive-science model-comparison literature, making novelty hard to assess.  
- Major concern: several assumptions appear arbitrary and insufficiently justified (notably the form of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter choices in Eq.12).  
- Suggests the claimed robustness and generality do not fully justify the imposed constraints; needs deeper discussion of validity dependence on assumptions.  
- Minor: Line 53’s “trivial mathematical fact” should be explained for readers.  
- Minor: Line 73 likely misuses “nor” — should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 caption unclear about meaning of star and dashed lines (both described as true value).  
- Overall: well-written and important topic, but lacks sufficient connection to prior work.
