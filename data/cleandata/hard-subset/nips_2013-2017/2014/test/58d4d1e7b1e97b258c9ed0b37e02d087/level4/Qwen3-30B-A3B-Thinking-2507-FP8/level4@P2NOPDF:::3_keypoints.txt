- Presents a Bayesian model formalizing sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Model accommodates diverse scenarios (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise) while remaining computationally tractable for efficient inference.  
- Authors emphasize mathematical identifiability, enabling distinct parameter estimates to be evaluated and distinguished within the model.  
- Framework is mathematically elegant and technically sound; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Novelty appears incremental: core methodological overlap with recent Bayesian perceptual models (e.g., Acerbi et al., 2012, 2014).  
- Authors should add a dedicated section reviewing recent Bayesian perceptual models and clearly articulate advances beyond prior work.  
- Claim that the model “solves” identifiability is misleading—identifiability holds only under the model’s assumptions, not practically in experiments.  
- The model conflates sensory, decision, and response noise, so it cannot experimentally distinguish those noise sources.  
- Revision should clarify the limits of identifiability and address the incremental contribution; acceptance recommended after these revisions.
