- Paper addresses identifiability of Bayesian observer models in perception and proposes a systematic framework to test it.  
- Framework is practical and assesses which experimental designs resolve degeneracies in parameter recovery.  
- Key innovation: efficient computation of expected posterior densities avoiding costly dataset sampling.  
- Demonstrated utility via two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Theoretical development is sound, linking to information theory (KL divergence) and Bayesian inference.  
- Empirical results reveal useful diagnostics and unexpected interactions (e.g., narrow loss functions harming interval timing recovery).  
- Paper is clearly written with sufficient detail for experts to reproduce results.  
- Main limitation: framework relies on strong assumptions, notably the sensory transformation in Equation 2.  
- Need more explicit discussion of framework performance under alternative sensory transformations.  
- Speed perception case study assumes observers do not learn priors during the experiment, contradicting literature on rapid adaptation.  
- Lacks crucial validation showing the framework can detect the absence of a slow-speed prior when none exists.  
- Some methodological aspects need clearer justification (e.g., Equation 15).  
- Significance: provides a valuable tool that could improve experimental design and standardize identifiability testing in perception research.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add the absent-prior validation and clarify assumptions about the sensory transformation.
