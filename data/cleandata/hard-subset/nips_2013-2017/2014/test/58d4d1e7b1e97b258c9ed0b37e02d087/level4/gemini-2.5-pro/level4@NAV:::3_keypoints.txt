- Proposes a Bayesian framework for perception that specifies sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Model combines robustness with mathematical tractability, handling stimulus-dependent noise while remaining computationally efficient.  
- Authors emphasize mathematical identifiability of model parameters and the ability to evaluate/distinguish parameter estimates.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance, calling the framework theoretically principled and elegant for formalizing perceptual estimation across paradigms.  
- Primary concern: the model is conceptually and methodologically close to recent Bayesian perceptual models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), so the contribution may be incremental.  
- Suggests adding a new section reviewing recent Bayesian perceptual models and explicitly articulating how this work advances beyond prior approaches.  
- Second concern: "identifiable" here is limited to mathematical identifiability under the model’s assumptions and does not resolve practical identifiability (e.g., distinguishing sensory vs. decision vs. response noise in behavioral data).  
- Recommends including a discussion clarifying this practical identifiability limitation and its implications for interpreting behavioral errors.
