- Paper proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models using behavioral data, reviewing Bayesian Decision Theory and identifying sources of non-identifiability.  
- Authors specify constraints needed to render components of the Bayesian framework identifiable and introduce assumptions to constrain each term.  
- They assess how well parameter values can be differentiated in their model and apply the method to time-interval estimation and motion perception domains.  
- Identifiability of internal representations/processes is a longstanding issue in cognitive science, with prior analyses (e.g., Anderson 1978) showing behavioral indistinguishability without process constraints.  
- Major concern 1: The paper largely overlooks substantial prior literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt et al., Navarro et al.), making its incremental contribution unclear.  
- Major concern 2: Several assumptions used to constrain the framework appear arbitrary and are insufficiently justified (e.g., choice of p_meas in Eq. 3 and parameterization in Eq. 12).  
- Recommendation: The paper should explicitly discuss and defend the particular assumptions used to make the Bayesian models identifiable.  
- Minor: Line 53’s “It is a trivial mathematical fact…” should instead briefly explain why it is true.  
- Minor: Line 73 contains a typo—the second “nor” should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 caption is unclear about what the star and dashed lines denote; caption currently suggests both indicate the true value.  
- Overall: Well-written and tackles an important problem, but insufficiently connected to prior related work.
