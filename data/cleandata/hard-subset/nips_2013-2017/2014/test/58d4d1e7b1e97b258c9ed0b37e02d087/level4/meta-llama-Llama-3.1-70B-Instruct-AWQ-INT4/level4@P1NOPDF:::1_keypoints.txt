- Proposes a framework for Bayesian model comparison on behavioral data, summarizing Bayesian Decision Theory, identifying non-identifiability, specifying constraints, and applying the method to time interval estimation and motion perception studies.
- Tackles an important, long-standing theoretical issue in cognitive science and psychology and is generally well written.
- Fails to engage with substantial prior literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang; Navarro, Pitt & Myung), making the novelty and contribution unclear.
- Key assumptions used to make terms identifiable appear arbitrary or under-justified, raising concerns about dependence of results on those choices.
- Questions the specific modeling choices: why define p_meas as in Equation 3, and whether parameter definitions in Equation 12 suffice to capture cognitive/perceptual processes.
- The manuscript should more thoroughly discuss and justify the assumptions used to constrain Bayesian models and assess sensitivity to those assumptions.
- Minor: Line 53—avoid calling the statement a "trivial mathematical fact"; briefly explain it.
- Minor: Line 73—replace the second "nor" with "or."
- Minor: Figure 2—clarify what the star and dashed lines represent, as the caption is ambiguous.
- Overall verdict: important and well-written work but missing links to prior related work and needing stronger justification of key assumptions.
