- Positive: Clear writing and genuinely innovative methodology; contribution is promising and above NeurIPS threshold.  
- Major concern: Heavy reliance on strong, often implicit assumptions that require deeper scrutiny.  
- Critical dependence on the log-stimulus transform (Eq. 2); authors must show whether identifiability fails without it and justify it empirically/theoretically.  
- Stimulus representation mismatch: paper states a discrete stimulus distribution but treats it as continuous—clarify and justify that discrete sampling doesn't invalidate continuous inference.  
- Prior specification issues: why the observer’s prior favors a lattice and is 50% wider than the true range—this must be justified by experiment or theory.  
- Noise modeling: interchangeability of sensory and motor noise follows from model structure—authors should justify including both or explain why interchangeability is non-trivial.  
- Statistical reporting: box plots are insufficient—report which observed condition differences are statistically reliable.  
- Validation of priors: show the model can detect the absence of a slow-speed prior (not only recover it when present).  
- Technical approximations: clarify whether Eq. 4 and entropy maximization guarantee unimodality (or allow bimodality) and its impact on inference.  
- Approximation conditions: quantify when Stirling’s approximation and E[x log x]≈E[x]logE[x] hold (e.g., minimum N, SNR) and state limits of Eq. 14/15.  
- Equation 15 needs stronger theoretical derivation or tempered claims about its generality after abandoning priors on θ.  
- Figure 1 confusingly structured: integrate panels, remove redundant p_est(s*|x), and clarify what “sample from this unnormalized density” refers to (dataset vs. posterior).  
- 2AFC analysis caveat: the claim that “learning of priors does not matter” is overstated—either justify fixed-prior assumption or acknowledge limitations.  
- Overall: the core idea of using strong representational constraints for identifiability is valuable but underexplored; paper overstates generality by conflating assumptions with theoretical claims and needs deeper justification.
