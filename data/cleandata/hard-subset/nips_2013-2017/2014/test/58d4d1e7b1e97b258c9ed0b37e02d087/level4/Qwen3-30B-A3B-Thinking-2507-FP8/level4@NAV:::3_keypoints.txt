- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with detailed mathematical formulations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Model is strong in both robustness to diverse scenarios (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise) and computational tractability (analytically tractable inference).  
- Authors emphasize model identifiability, enabling straightforward evaluation and distinction of parameter estimates within the model.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance, calling the work mathematically elegant and useful for formalizing perceptual estimation in experiments.  
- Major reservation: novelty is limited—model is closely aligned with recent Bayesian literature (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), so contribution appears incremental.  
- Recommendation to add a critical review section comparing recent Bayesian perceptual models and explicitly state how this work advances beyond prior art.  
- Clarify the identifiability claim: parameters are identifiable within the mathematical model but practical identifiability (separating sensory, decision, and response noise in experiments) remains unresolved.  
- Overall: mathematically elegant framework but the contribution’s novelty is incremental.
