- Proposes a framework to assess parameter identifiability in Bayesian perception models, addressing degeneracy where different priors/likelihoods/losses can produce identical behavior.  
- Demonstrates the framework on time-interval estimation and speed perception, showing potential to improve experimental design and validate inferences.  
- Emphasizes that identifiability is a longstanding problem in cognitive science (e.g., Anderson 1978) and a current critique of Bayesian modeling (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and tackles a theoretically important issue.  
- Novel, systematic approach that could be valuable to researchers assessing model identifiability.  
- Illustrative examples effectively show the framework’s potential utility.  
- Major concern: insufficient engagement with existing model-comparison and identifiability literature within cognitive science (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004; Myung et al. 2000).  
- Major concern: some modeling assumptions appear arbitrary (e.g., choice of Eq. 3 for p_meas) and sensitivity to these choices is not fully explored.  
- Needs more discussion of implications of imposing specific functional forms on priors and likelihoods and how they affect identifiability.  
- Risk of overconfidence: framework assesses identifiability but does not guarantee unique or meaningful recovered parameters.  
- Minor comment: avoid calling facts “trivial” without explanation (Line 53).  
- Minor comment: change “nor” to “or” (Line 73).  
- Minor comment: clarify meaning of the star and dashed lines in Figure 2 caption (they are redundantly described as the true value).
