- Introduces a Bayesian observer model for perceptual estimation that is mathematically convenient and robust, handling scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise.  
- Emphasizes parameter identifiability as crucial for reliably inferring internal representations.  
- Provides a formal tool useful for analyzing and improving experimental design in perception research.  
- Demonstrates utility through two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Contribution is valuable but novelty is incremental within existing Bayesian perception literature.  
- Related prior work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014) covers similar Bayesian approaches and should be more fully acknowledged.  
- Recommend expanding the related work section to explicitly compare/contrast and highlight novel aspects of the proposed model.  
- The paper’s identifiability results concern mathematical identifiability from model assumptions, not the practical disentangling of sensory vs. decision vs. motor noise.  
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge the limitation that their framework may not resolve practical source-separation of variability in perceptual data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous, tractable Bayesian framework; useful for a priori experimental and identifiability analysis; compelling case studies; tackles potential non-identifiability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty; identifiability focus may not address broader challenges of inferring underlying perceptual mechanisms.
