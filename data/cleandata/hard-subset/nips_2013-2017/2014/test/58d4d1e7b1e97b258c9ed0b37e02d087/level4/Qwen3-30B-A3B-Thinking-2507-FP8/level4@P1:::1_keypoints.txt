- Paper targets the non-identifiability of internal representations in Bayesian perceptual models and proposes an a priori identifiability-testing framework.  
- Framework is formalized using Bayesian Decision Theory and derives constraints to resolve degeneracies in inverse mappings from behavior to cognitive components.  
- Authors validate the approach with two case studies (time interval estimation and speed perception), showing the method can rank experimental designs and flag problematic parameters.  
- The proposed observer model is flexible, computationally efficient, and applicable to a broad class of sensory estimation tasks.  
- Case studies yield concrete insights: e.g., a narrow (MAP-like) loss can worsen prior recovery in interval timing; speed perception inferences are robust to noise-representation assumptions.  
- Major limitation: insufficient engagement with prior model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al., Navarro et al.); novelty over established KL-divergence/MCMC techniques is not clearly articulated.  
- Major limitation: weak justification for key parametric choices (Eq. 2 mapping, Eq. 3 two-Gaussian noise); need sensitivity analyses and links from parameters to measurable psychophysics.  
- Minor issues: suggested wording changes (line 53, line 73) and clarification of Figure 2 caption regarding star/dashed/solid lines.  
- Overall conclusion: technically rigorous and useful methodological contribution but conditionally acceptable—requires clearer contrast with prior work and stronger justification of modeling assumptions.
