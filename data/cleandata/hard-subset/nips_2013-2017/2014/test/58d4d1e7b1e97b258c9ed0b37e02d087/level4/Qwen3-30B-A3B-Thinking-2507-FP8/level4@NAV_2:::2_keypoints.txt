- Writing is clear and well-structured and the work is methodologically novel and worthy of presentation at NIPS despite reservations about assumptions.  
- The paper relies on numerous, strong assumptions that may be disproportionate to the significance of the main findings.  
- Section 4.1: sensory and motor noise parameters appear interchangeable; authors should indicate which condition differences are statistically reliable.  
- Section 4.2: authors should show the model can correctly infer the absence of a slow-speed prior when the reference observer lacks one.  
- Clarify why a discrete stimulus distribution is used despite the stimulus being inherently continuous.  
- The log-stimulus transformation is critical and imposes strong constraints, meaning identifiability holds under specific internal-representation assumptions.  
- Do the constraints and entropy-maximization in Eq.4 guarantee a unimodal solution for Eq.3, or could the fit become bimodal?  
- Justify the choice of a lattice-based prior over a uniform prior and the rationale for using a prior range 50% wider than the true range.  
- Figure 1 is confusing; consider integrating panel b into a and omitting the derived p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Elaborate conditions under which Eq.14 is approximately valid, including limits of Stirling’s approximation and the E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x] step.  
- Clarify what is meant by “sample from this unnormalized density” and specify which density (e.g., dataset distribution) is referenced.  
- The manuscript does not consider how learning of priors affects 2AFC results, and overall results feel not particularly compelling despite the innovative approach.
