- Proposes a Bayesian model of perception that balances robustness with mathematical convenience for tractable inference.  
- Major strength: model identifiability enables evaluation and distinction of different parameter estimates.  
- Provides a comprehensive framework to formalize perceptual estimation across various experiments.  
- Handles complex scenarios such as stimulus-dependent noise and offers computational convenience.  
- Model is flexible and general, making it useful for researchers in perception and related fields.  
- Emphasis on identifiability is well-justified and a crucial contribution.  
- Concern: novelty is limited — the approach is similar in spirit to recent Bayesian perception models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Contribution is viewed as incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Recommend adding a literature review of recent Bayesian perception models to clarify advances over prior work.  
- Recommend addressing identifiability more broadly, acknowledging inability to separate sensory, decision, and response noise in data.  
- Important limitation: mathematical identifiability does not guarantee distinguishability of different noise sources in experiments.  
- Overall assessment: mathematically elegant framework with potential, but needs revisions to better contextualize novelty and limitations.
