- Presents a Bayesian perception model specifying sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Strength: combines robustness with mathematical convenience and computational tractability for efficient inference.  
- Flexible enough to handle stimulus-dependent noise and various experimental situations.  
- Emphasizes mathematical identifiability and clarity of parameter estimation.  
- Overall well-executed and recommended for acceptance.  
- Concern 1 (novelty): framework closely resembles recent Bayesian perception models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), risking an incremental contribution.  
- Recommendation: add a section reviewing recent Bayesian models and explicitly state unique contributions and advances.  
- Concern 2 (identifiability): mathematical identifiability under assumptions does not resolve practical experimental identifiability (distinguishing sensory, decision, and motor noise).  
- Recommendation: add discussion qualifying the identifiability claim and contrast it with broader empirical identifiability challenges.
