- Addresses model identifiability in cognitive modeling; well-written and clear.  
- Relies on a lengthy list of assumptions, which reduces the impact of the results.  
- Methodology is interesting and innovative and is suitable for presentation at NIPS.  
- Section 4.1: sensory and motor noise appear interchangeable; this was not surprising and raises questions about including both.  
- Recommend indicating which condition differences are statistically reliable and using clearer visualizations than box plots.  
- Section 4.2: must show the model can recover that the reference observer lacked a slow-speed prior.  
- Line 106: stimulus is drawn from a discrete distribution despite being continuous—authors should clarify this experimental design choice.  
- Line 134: the log-stimulus transform strongly constrains inference; identifiability may hinge on this representational assumption.  
- Line 143: constraints and entropy maximization may not guarantee unimodal fits; fits could be bimodal.  
- Line 158: unclear why the observer’s prior favors a lattice representation or why the prior range is 50% wider than the true stimulus range.  
- Figure 1: current presentation is confusing; suggest integrating panels and removing the derived p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Line 252: authors should state when Equation 14’s approximations hold (Stirling’s approximation, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]); Equation 15 seems justified mainly by intuition after approximations.  
- Line 262: unclear which “unnormalized density” is being sampled—clarify whether this refers to the dataset distribution.  
- Line 365: claim that learning priors does not matter in 2AFC is innovative but the evidence presented is not fully convincing.
