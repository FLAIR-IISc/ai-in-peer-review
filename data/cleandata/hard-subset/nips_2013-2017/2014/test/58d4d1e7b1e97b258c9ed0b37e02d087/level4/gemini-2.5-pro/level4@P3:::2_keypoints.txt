- Addresses model identifiability in Bayesian observer models and questions whether internal representations can be uniquely recovered.  
- Core contribution: a computational framework to test identifiability a priori across a flexible class of Bayesian observer models.  
- Demonstrates method on two case studies: time-interval estimation and visual speed perception.  

- Strength: tackles a fundamental and under-addressed problem, offering a novel, principled tool to assess identifiability before experiments.  
- Strength: technically solid parametric observer model using mixture-of-Gaussians and an intuitive KL-based identifiability metric.  
- Strength: clearly written with practical utility; case studies yield actionable experimental design insights (e.g., measuring motor noise).  

- Weakness: conclusions depend on many structured model assumptions, so results speak to this model class more than to Bayesian models in general.  
- Weakness: derivation from expected log-likelihood to KL divergence is not fully transparent; approximation conditions should be clarified.  
- Weakness: some findings (sensory vs motor noise degeneracy) are expected, while other interesting results (narrow loss harms prior recovery) deserve more emphasis; FLA control should be highlighted.  

- Specific: justify discrete stimulus distribution as reflecting common psychophysical designs.  
- Specific: acknowledge that the sensory map constraint likely contributes substantially to identifiability.  
- Specific: clarify how unimodality of the likelihood mixture is enforced.  
- Specific: simplify or integrate panels in Figure 1 to reduce density and improve clarity.  
- Specific: explicitly state that MCMC samples approximate the expected posterior density E(θ|θ*) from Eq.13.  

- Summary: important, well-executed, and useful framework that advances identifiability analysis, though conclusions are conditional on model choices; recommendation for acceptance.
