- Proposes a framework to test identifiability of Bayesian models of perception.  
- Provides a clear summary of Bayesian Decision Theory and identifies sources of non-identifiability.  
- Outlines constraints required to make model terms identifiable.  
- Introduces a novel class of Bayesian observer models and applies them to time-interval estimation and speed perception case studies.  
- Well-written and effectively communicates the importance of identifiability in Bayesian models.  
- Major concern: insufficient engagement with existing model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al., 2002; Navarro et al., 2004).  
- Major concern: some assumptions (e.g., definition of pmeas(x|s) in Eq. 3) appear arbitrary and lack empirical justification.  
- Unclear whether parameterization (e.g., Eq. 12) captures the complexity of cognitive and perceptual processes.  
- Minor clarity edits: explain the “trivial” mathematical fact on line 53, replace “nor” with “or” on line 73, and clarify Figure 2 legend (star and dashed lines).  
- Strengths: tackles a crucial problem, provides a concise BDT summary, and demonstrates utility via two case studies.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete literature review, unjustified assumptions, and insufficient discussion of assumption implications.  
- Recommendation: support publication contingent on better literature engagement and clearer justification of assumptions.
