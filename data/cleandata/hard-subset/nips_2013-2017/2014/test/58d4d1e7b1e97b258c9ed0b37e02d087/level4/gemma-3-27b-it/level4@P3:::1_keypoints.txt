- Proposes a framework to assess identifiability in Bayesian perceptual models, addressing degeneracy where different priors/likelihoods/losses yield identical behavior.  
- Introduces a flexible Bayesian observer model and demonstrates it on time-interval estimation and speed-perception tasks to evaluate experimental setups.  
- Highlights the fundamental challenge that non-identifiability poses to interpreting Bayesian models as reflections of cognitive processes.  
- Paper is well-written, conceptually sound, and clearly explains the identifiability problem and proposed approach.  
- Mathematical framework appears rigorous and derivations are logically consistent.  
- Case studies show practical potential for improving experimental design and interpreting results.  
- Limited engagement with prior work on model comparison/selection (e.g., Pitt, Navarro, Myung), weakening assessment of novelty.  
- Several modeling choices (specific likelihood and loss forms; shared parameters across conditions) seem arbitrary and under-justified.  
- Lacks sensitivity analysis examining robustness to different reference-observer parameter values (θ*).  
- Insufficient discussion of computational demands and scalability to more complex models.  
- Paper should better situate itself within the broader model-comparison literature.  
- Needs more justification and sensitivity checks for key modeling assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing literature review, modeling assumptions, and sensitivity analysis.  
- Minor editorial comments: avoid dismissive phrasing on Line 53, fix "nor"→"or" on Line 73, and clarify Figure 2 caption (star vs dashed lines).
