1. Addresses model identifiability in cognitive modeling with clear writing, but relies on many assumptions that weaken impact; methodology is innovative and NIPS-worthy.  
2. Claim of interchangeability between sensory and motor noise (Sec. 4.1) is unsurprising and needs statistical tests showing which condition differences are reliable.  
3. In Sec. 4.2, must demonstrate the model can recover that the reference observer lacked a slow-speed prior.  
4. Line 106: stimulus is drawn from a discrete distribution despite being continuous—this experimental choice needs explanation.  
5. Equation 2’s log-stimulus transform strongly constrains inference, so success may largely stem from this transform and requires discussion.  
6. Constraints in Eq. 4 and maximizing differential entropy do not guarantee Eq. 3 yields a unimodal distribution; bimodal fits are possible and should be addressed.  
7. It is unclear why the observer’s prior should favor a lattice representation over a uniform one given the prior’s width relative to the true range—need justification.  
8. Figure 1 is confusing; recommend integrating panel b into panel a and removing p_est(s^*|x) for clarity.  
9. Conditions for Eq. 14’s approximation need comment, including applicability of Stirling’s approximation and validity of replacing E[x log x] with E[x] log E[x].  
10. Derivation of Eq. 15 relies on dropping priors on θ and several questionable approximations and appears justified mainly by its intuitive outcome—require stronger justification.  
11. Clarify which density is meant on line 262, and explain the claim on line 365 that learning priors does not matter in 2AFC.  
12. Overall: approach is innovative and addresses an important problem, but the results and justifications are not fully convincing.
