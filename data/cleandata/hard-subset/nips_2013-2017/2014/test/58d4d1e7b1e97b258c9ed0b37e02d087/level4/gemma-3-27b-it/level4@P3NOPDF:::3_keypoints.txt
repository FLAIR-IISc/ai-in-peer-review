- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with full mathematical formulation (noise, likelihood, prior, loss, responses).  
- Strength: balances robustness and computational tractability, handles scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise.  
- Strength: demonstrates internal parameter identifiability and efficient inference.  
- Overall assessment: promising, mathematically elegant framework applicable to many experiments.  
- Major concern: novelty is limited—conceptually similar to recent Bayesian perception work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommendation: add a dedicated review section comparing recent Bayesian models and clearly state advances over prior work.  
- Concern about identifiability claim: identifiability holds within the model but does not resolve the broader problem of separating sources of perceptual error (sensory vs. decision vs. response).  
- Suggestion: explicitly acknowledge and discuss the scope and limitations of the model’s identifiability in the manuscript.  
- Conclusion: revise to better position the work in the literature and provide a nuanced discussion of identifiability to strengthen the paper.
