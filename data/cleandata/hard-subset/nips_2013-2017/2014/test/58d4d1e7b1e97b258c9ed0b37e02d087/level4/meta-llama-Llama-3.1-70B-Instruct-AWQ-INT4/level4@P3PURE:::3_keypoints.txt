- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with formal equations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distribution.  
- Strength: balances robustness and mathematical convenience, handling cases like stimulus-dependent noise while remaining computationally tractable.  
- Claims model identifiability, allowing evaluation and distinction of parameter estimates.  
- Provides a mathematically elegant framework for formalizing perceptual estimation and is potentially NIPS-acceptable.  
- Concern about novelty: model appears similar in spirit to recent works (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), so contribution may be incremental.  
- Recommendation to add a section reviewing recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state how this work advances beyond prior models.  
- Caveat on identifiability: mathematical identifiability may not resolve practical ambiguity between sensory, decision, and response noise—suggest discussing this limitation.  
- Overall summary: elegant and useful framework but incremental relative to existing literature.
