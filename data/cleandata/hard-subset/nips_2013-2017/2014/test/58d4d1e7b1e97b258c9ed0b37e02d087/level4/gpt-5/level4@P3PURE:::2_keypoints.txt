- Manuscript addresses model identifiability in cognitive modeling; clearly written and above threshold for NIPS despite concerns.
- Main contributions depend on a long list of strong assumptions, which weaken the impact of results.
- Methodology is interesting and innovative.
- Section 4.1: Sensory and motor noise appear nearly interchangeable; statistical reliability of condition differences should be made explicit.
- Section 4.2: Must demonstrate the method recovers absence of a slow-speed prior when the reference observer lacks it.
- Clarify why stimulus is modeled as drawn from a discrete distribution when the underlying variable is continuous.
- The log-stimulus transform (Eq. 2) imposes a strong constraint and may be a primary reason for identifiability.
- Question whether constraints in Eq. 4 plus differential-entropy maximization guarantee a unimodal fit to Eq. 3 or could allow bimodality.
- Unclear why observer’s prior should favor a lattice representation or be 50% wider than the true stimulus range.
- Figure 1 is confusing; consider integrating panels and removing derived p_est(s^*|x).
- Request clarification on conditions for Eq. 14 approximations (Stirling’s approximation and E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) and justification for abandoning priors on θ in Eq. 15.
- Clarify which "unnormalized density" is being sampled in the procedure.
- Explain why learning of priors does not matter in the 2AFC task.
- Overall: valuable and above-threshold work, but conclusions are tempered by numerous and strong assumptions.
