- Addresses identifiability in Bayesian cognitive modeling by constraining Bayesian decision-theory components to enable meaningful behavioral model comparison.  
- Develops a framework that systematically analyzes sources of non-identifiability and proposes constraints for each term.  
- Validates the approach with two behavioral studies (time interval estimation and motion perception).  
- Topic is highly relevant given long-standing identifiability problems in cognitive science.  
- Fails to engage adequately with prior foundational work (e.g., Pitt et al., 2002; Navarro et al., 2004), making novelty unclear.  
- Proposed framework resembles established approaches for model distinguishability and data informativeness without explicit comparison.  
- Lacks justification for critical assumptions (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameterization in Eq.12).  
- Does not demonstrate that the proposed constraints are necessary for identifiability or provide sensitivity analyses.  
- Paper would benefit from a dedicated rationale section for each constraint and empirical/theoretical support.  
- Minor fixes needed: clarify "trivial mathematical fact" (Line 53), correct "nor" to "or" (Line 73), and clarify Figure 2 caption (star vs. dashed lines).  
- Recommendations: explicitly contrast with prior work, justify key assumptions, and discuss limitations and generalizability beyond sensory domains.  
- Overall: technically useful and well-written but fails to establish novelty or necessity relative to existing model-comparison methods.
