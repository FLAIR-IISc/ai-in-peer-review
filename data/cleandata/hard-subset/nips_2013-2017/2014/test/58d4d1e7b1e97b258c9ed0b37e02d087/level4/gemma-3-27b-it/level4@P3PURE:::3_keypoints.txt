- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with detailed mathematical formulations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distribution.  
- Model combines robustness and computational tractability, accommodating scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise while remaining analytically convenient.  
- Emphasizes identifiability, enabling evaluation and distinction of parameter estimates under the model’s assumptions.  
- Reviewer is generally supportive and would back acceptance to NIPS; finds the framework mathematically elegant and broadly applicable to perceptual estimation.  
- Concern about novelty: contribution appears incremental and stylistically similar to recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommends adding a dedicated review section of recent Bayesian perception models that explicitly states how this work improves on prior approaches.  
- Points out that identifiability is contingent on the model’s assumptions and does not solve broader identifiability issues in perceptual science.  
- Urges clarification that the model cannot definitively separate errors from sensory, decision, and response noise, and to specify the model’s identifiability scope.  
- Overall: mathematically elegant framework but novelty is limited given closely related existing literature.
