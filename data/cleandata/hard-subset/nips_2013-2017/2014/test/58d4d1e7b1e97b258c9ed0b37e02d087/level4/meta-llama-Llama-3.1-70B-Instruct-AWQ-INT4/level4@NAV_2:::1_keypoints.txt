- Paper proposes a framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian models of perception, addressing an important problem in cognitive science and psychology.
- The manuscript is well-written and clearly summarizes Bayesian Decision Theory and points of non-identifiability.
- Authors outline types of constraints to make terms in the Bayesian framework identifiable.
- Framework is demonstrated on two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.
- Paper largely ignores prior model-comparison literature in cognitive science (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004), and its general framework is not clearly distinct from that work.
- Several assumptions appear arbitrary (notably the definition of pmeas(x|s) in Eq. 3) and lack sufficient justification or discussion of how dependent results are on them.
- The article should better justify and discuss the assumptions used to constrain Bayesian models for identifiability.
- Some mathematical statements are dismissed as "trivial" without explanation and need clearer derivation or rationale.
- Minor issues: line 73 has a grammatical error ("nor" → "or"), and Figure 2’s star and dashed-line annotations are ambiguously explained.
- Overall conclusion: important and well-executed study but needs stronger connections to prior work and more justification for modeling assumptions.
