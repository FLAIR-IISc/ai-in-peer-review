- Clear, well-organized manuscript addressing an important problem (model identifiability) with innovative methodology, meriting NIPS acceptance.  
- Results rely on substantial assumptions, which limits their impact.  
- Section 4.1: interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is unsurprising; question necessity of modeling both.  
- Box plots need clearer labeling/significance markers to show which condition differences are statistically reliable.  
- Section 4.2: must show model can recover stimulus distribution when the reference observer lacks a slow-speed prior to support identifiability claims.  
- Line 106: clarify why stimuli are drawn from a discrete distribution while the underlying variable is treated as continuous.  
- Line 134: the log-stimulus transform is a strong prior and may drive identifiability—acknowledge its central role.  
- Line 143: constraints claimed to enforce unimodality do not obviously guarantee it; address possible bimodal solutions.  
- Line 158: justify the observer’s prior favoring a lattice representation and the rationale for a prior 50% wider than the true stimulus range.  
- Figure 1: currently confusing—suggest integrating panels and removing derived quantity p_est to improve clarity.  
- Line 252–262: state validity conditions for Stirling’s approximation and E[xlogx]≈E[x]logE[x], and clarify which “unnormalized density” is being sampled.  
- Line 365: learning priors appears irrelevant for 2AFC tasks, which reduces the broader impact of the results.
