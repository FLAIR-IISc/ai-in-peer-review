- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with full mathematical specification (sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, response distribution).  
- Balances robustness and mathematical convenience, accommodating stimulus-dependent noise while remaining computationally tractable.  
- Claims mathematical identifiability, allowing straightforward evaluation and distinction between parameter estimates.  
- Reviewer finds the paper appealing and recommends it for consideration; describes the framework as mathematically elegant for perceptual estimation.  
- Novelty is limited/incremental because the model is similar in spirit/style to recent Bayesian perception work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommends adding a review of recent Bayesian models and explicitly stating how this work advances beyond prior literature.  
- Questions the practical meaning of “identifiability”: current identifiability stems from model assumptions and may not resolve experimental separation of sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Encourages authors to address these practical identifiability concerns in the revised manuscript.  
- Overall verdict: elegant formal framework but an incremental contribution relative to existing work.
