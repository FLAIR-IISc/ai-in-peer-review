- Proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models on behavioral data based on Bayesian Decision Theory and identifies non-identifiability issues.  
- Outlines types of constraints needed to make each term in the Bayesian framework identifiable and examines differentiability of parameter values.  
- Applies the technique to two case studies: time interval estimation and motion perception.  
- Notes the longstanding identifiability problem in cognitive science and that this work tackles a theoretically important issue.  
- Paper is well-written and generally effective.  
- Major reservation: overlooks extensive prior literature on model comparison in cognitive science, making its novelty hard to assess (e.g., Pitt et al., Navarro et al.).  
- Major reservation: authors make seemingly arbitrary assumptions (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3) and it’s unclear how much the framework depends on them.  
- Concern that parametrization in Eq.12 may not capture all characteristics of cognitive/perceptual processes despite covering many sensory models.  
- Recommendation to more fully discuss and justify the assumptions used to ensure identifiability.  
- Minor comments: clarify the “trivial mathematical fact” at Line 53, change second “nor” to “or” at Line 73, and clarify Figure 2 caption regarding the star and dashed lines.
