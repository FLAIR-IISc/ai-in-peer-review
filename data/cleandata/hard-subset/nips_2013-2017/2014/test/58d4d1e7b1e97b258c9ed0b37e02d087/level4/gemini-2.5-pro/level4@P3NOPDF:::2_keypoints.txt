- Addresses the important, challenging problem of model identifiability in cognitive modeling.  
- Proposed methodology is novel and clearly explained.  
- Manuscript is generally clear and well written.  
- Heavy reliance on strong, domain-specific assumptions (e.g., log-stimulus transform) raises questions about generality.  
- Overall impact feels modest because key results depend on a lengthy chain of assumptions.  
- Empirical validation is not fully convincing; sensory vs. motor noise interchangeability undermines the need for separate noise sources.  
- Statistical significance of condition differences shown in box plots is not reported and should be clarified.  
- Missing crucial control: need to show the method correctly recovers the absence of a slow-speed prior, not just its presence.  
- Clarify why a continuous stimulus is drawn from a discrete distribution (line 106).  
- Explain whether the constraints plus differential-entropy maximization guarantee a unimodal fitted distribution (line 143).  
- Justify the observer prior favoring a “lattice representation” and why it is 50% wider than the true stimulus range (line 158).  
- Clarify and justify approximations in derivations (Stirling’s approximation, when E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x] holds, and abandoning priors on θ) (line 252).  
- Specify which “unnormalized density” is being sampled (dataset distribution or other) (line 262).  
- Elaborate why learning priors is claimed to not matter in 2AFC tasks (line 365).  
- Improve Figure 1 for clarity: consider integrating panels and removing derived p_est(s^*|x) to focus the schematic.
