- Proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models on behavioral data, summarizing Bayesian Decision Theory, identifiability constraints, parameter differentiability, and re-analyzing two example tasks.  
- Highlights the longstanding identifiability problem in cognitive science, citing Anderson (1978) and later critiques of Bayesian modeling.  
- Strength: paper addresses a theoretically important issue and presents arguments clearly and logically.  
- Major concern 1: Largely overlooks relevant prior work on model comparison in cognitive science (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang; Navarro, Pitt & Myung), hindering assessment of novelty.  
- Major concern 2: Several assumptions seem arbitrary and under-justified (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq. 3 and parameter choices in Eq. 12); unclear if they sufficiently capture cognitive/perceptual complexity.  
- Recommendation: provide more detailed discussion and rigorous justification of the framework’s constraints and assumptions.  
- Minor: Line 53 — the phrase “It is a trivial mathematical fact…” should be explained rather than asserted.  
- Minor: Line 73 — likely wording error where the second “nor” should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 — legend should clarify the distinction between the star and dashed lines, which both appear to represent the true value.  
- Overall: Well-written and important, but impact is reduced by insufficient connection to prior literature.
