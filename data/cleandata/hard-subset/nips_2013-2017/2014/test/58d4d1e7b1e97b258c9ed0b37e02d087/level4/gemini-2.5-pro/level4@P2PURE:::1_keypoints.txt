- Paper proposes a framework for comparing Bayesian models of behavioral data by imposing constraints to achieve identifiability across Bayesian Decision Theory components.  
- Authors introduce specific assumptions, analyze parameter differentiability, and demonstrate the approach on time-interval estimation and motion-perception case studies.  
- Topic is timely and important because identifiability of internal representations/processes is a long-standing problem in cognitive science and Bayesian cognitive modeling.  
- Manuscript is generally well-written and addresses a significant theoretical issue.  
- Major concern 1: The paper fails to engage with extensive prior literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt et al., 2002; Navarro et al., 2004), making novel contributions hard to assess.  
- Major concern 2: The framework depends on several assumptions whose justifications are unclear or seem arbitrary, and the paper does not explore how dependent results are on these choices.  
- Specific concern: No motivation provided for defining the measurement probability p_meas as in Equation 3.  
- Specific concern: Questionable whether the parameterization in Equation 12 sufficiently captures full characteristics of cognitive/perceptual processes; claimed robustness is not a full justification.  
- Recommendation: Include a thorough discussion and justification of the assumptions used to achieve identifiability and analyze sensitivity to those choices.  
- Minor: Line 53—explain why the stated “trivial mathematical fact” is true instead of calling it trivial.  
- Minor: Line 73—typo: change the second “nor” to “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 caption ambiguous about which markers denote the true value; clarify caption.
