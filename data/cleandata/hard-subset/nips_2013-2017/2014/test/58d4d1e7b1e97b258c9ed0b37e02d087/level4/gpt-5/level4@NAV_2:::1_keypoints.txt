- Paper presents a framework for comparing Bayesian observer models on behavioral data with a focus on testing identifiability.  
- Authors recap Bayesian Decision Theory, identify sources of non-identifiability, and list constraint types that can render Bayesian model components identifiable.  
- They instantiate specific assumptions for each component, test parameter distinguishability, and apply the approach to interval timing and visual motion perception.  
- Identifiability of internal representations has a long history in cognitive science (e.g., Anderson 1978) and has been a central critique of Bayesian approaches (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Major concern: the manuscript largely overlooks extensive prior literature on model comparison in cognitive science (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004), making its contribution hard to situate.  
- Major concern: several modeling assumptions appear ad hoc and insufficiently justified (e.g., the chosen form for p_meas in Eq. 3 and fixing parameters in Eq. 12); dependence of results on these assumptions is unclear.  
- Recommendation: explicitly motivate and defend the constraints used to achieve identifiability and assess robustness to assumption choices.  
- Minor: Line 53 — avoid calling the result “trivial”; briefly explain why it holds.  
- Minor: Line 73 — likely typo: second “nor” should be “or.”  
- Minor: Figure 2 — clarify what the star and dashed lines denote, since caption suggests both correspond to the true value.  
- Overall: strong, clearly written manuscript on an important topic but needs stronger integration with prior related work.
