- Proposes a framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian models of perception.  
- Introduces a novel, flexible and efficient class of Bayesian observer models.  
- Provides an efficient method for exploring model identifiability.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Addresses the important problem of inferring internal representations/processes in Bayesian models.  
- Framework is a valuable, systematic contribution that could increase adoption of Bayesian approaches.  
- Lacks a thorough comparison to prior model-comparison/distinguishability work (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004).  
- Several modeling assumptions appear arbitrary and are not fully justified (notably the measurement distribution p_meas(x|s) as a two-Gaussian mixture).  
- Needs a clearer discussion of the rationale for assumptions and their implications for identifiability.  
- Writing and organization are strong; explanations of framework and applications are clear.  
- Clarity could be improved by more detailed mathematical explanations and justification of assumptions.  
- Minor fixes: explain the “trivial mathematical fact” at Line 53, change “nor” to “or” at Line 73, and clarify star/dashed annotations in Figure 2 caption.  
- With revisions addressing related work and assumptions, the paper has potential to make a significant contribution.
