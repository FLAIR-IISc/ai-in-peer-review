- Main contribution: proposes an a priori framework to assess identifiability of Bayesian observer models before data collection.  
- Framework components: (1) flexible, tractable class of Bayesian observer models; (2) KL-divergence-based approximation of the expected posterior to map an "identifiability landscape."  
- Practical demos: applied to time-interval estimation (to compare/improve designs) and speed perception (supports robustness of slow-speed prior results).  
- Significance: addresses the important issue of model non-identifiability and offers experimenters a tool to evaluate design power pre-data.  
- Technical quality: models are comprehensive and computationally efficient, using flexible parameterizations (e.g., mixtures of Gaussians) and principled KL-based metrics.  
- Clarity and insights: paper is well-written; case studies yield non-obvious findings (e.g., MAP-like loss can hinder prior recovery; importance of measuring motor noise).  
- Originality concern: many model components extend prior sensorimotor work (e.g., Acerbi et al.), so the contribution may be seen as incremental without clearer differentiation.  
- Recommendation: explicitly compare and contrast with prior models to clarify what is novel in the model class and in the a priori identifiability analysis.  
- Scope limitation: framework addresses parameter identifiability within a fixed model architecture, not fundamental distinctions between different cognitive architectures or sources of behavioral variability.  
- Suggested clarification: add discussion acknowledging that the method detects parameter degeneracy given assumed causal structure but cannot resolve ambiguity between sensory noise, suboptimal inference, or motor execution.
