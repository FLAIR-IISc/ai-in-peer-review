- Paper introduces a framework for comparing Bayesian models on behavioral data by outlining Bayesian Decision Theory, identifying non-identifiability, and specifying constraints for identifiability.  
- Authors constrain terms in the Bayesian framework, analyze parameter distinguishability, and apply the method to time-interval estimation and motion perception case studies.  
- The problem of identifying internal representations/processes is longstanding and limits Bayesian approaches (cites Anderson 1978; Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Main concern 1: The paper overlooks substantial prior literature on model comparison in cognitive science (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004), weakening claims of novelty.  
- Main concern 2: Several assumptions appear arbitrary and under-justified (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3; whether parameter choices in Eq.12 fully characterize cognitive/perceptual processes).  
- Recommendation: Provide more thorough discussion and justification of the assumptions used to achieve identifiability.  
- Minor: Line 53 — explain the “trivial mathematical fact” rather than labeling it trivial.  
- Minor: Line 73 — replace the second “nor” with “or” (typo).  
- Minor: Figure 2 caption — clarify meaning of the star and dashed lines since both are described as “true value.”  
- Overall: Well-written and theoretically significant, but needs better connection to prior work and stronger justification of modeling assumptions.
