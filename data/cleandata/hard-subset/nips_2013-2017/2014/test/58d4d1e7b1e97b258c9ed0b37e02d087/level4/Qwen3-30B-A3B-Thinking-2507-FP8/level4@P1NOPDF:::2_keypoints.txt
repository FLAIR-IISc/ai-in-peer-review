- Addresses identifiability in cognitive models; well-written but relies on many insufficiently justified assumptions, which reduce impact despite overall merit.  
- Section 4.1: Sensory and motor noise interchangeability is unsurprising; authors should justify including both and clarify which condition differences are statistically reliable.  
- Section 4.2: Must demonstrate the method can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior in a reference observer to validate the approach.  
- Line 106: Stimulus treated as drawn from a discrete distribution despite being continuous—clarify experimental rationale.  
- Line 134: The log-stimulus transform is critical and imposes strong constraints, implying identifiability claims depend heavily on this representational assumption.  
- Line 143: Question whether constraints (Eq.4) and entropy maximization guarantee Eq.3 yields a unimodal rather than bimodal distribution—address potential multimodality.  
- Line 158: Unclear why the observer prior should favor a lattice over uniform or be 50% wider than the true range—provide justification.  
- Figure 1: Figure 1b is unclear and could be integrated into 1a; omit p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Line 252: Specify conditions on N for Eq.14 validity; Stirling’s approximation and E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x] are questionable and need justification.  
- Line 262: “Sample from this unnormalized density” is ambiguous—specify which density (e.g., dataset distribution).  
- Line 365: The claim that prior learning does not matter in 2AFC is interesting but the presented results are not fully convincing.  
- Overall recommendation: Methodologically creative and valuable, but requires stronger justification of strong assumptions and clearer discussion of their implications for interpretability.
