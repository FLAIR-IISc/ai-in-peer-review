- Proposes a framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perception models, addressing a major barrier to Bayesian approaches in cognitive science.
- Provides a thorough summary of Bayesian Decision Theory and identifies sources of non-identifiability and constraints to achieve identifiability.
- Approach is well-motivated given longstanding concerns about indistinguishable internal representations and processes in cognitive science.
- Paper is well-written and effectively communicates the authors' ideas.
- Major concern: insufficient engagement with prior model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004) and lack of clear differentiation from existing frameworks.
- Major concern: key assumptions appear arbitrary and under-justified—specifically the choice and motivation for Equation 3 defining p_meas and its claim of robustness.
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed rationale and implications for the chosen assumptions and definitions.
- Minor: clarify the “trivial mathematical fact” at line 53 for readers who may not find it obvious.
- Minor: correct grammar at line 73 (replace second “nor” with “or”).
- Minor: clarify Figure 2 caption to explain the meaning of the star and dashed lines (true value).
- Recommendation: revise the paper to better engage existing literature and more thoroughly justify assumptions; with revisions it could make a significant contribution.
