Manuscript introduces a framework for comparing Bayesian models on behavioral data, summarizing Bayesian Decision Theory, identifying non-identifiability sources, and specifying constraints for identifiability.

Authors introduce assumptions to constrain framework terms, analyze parameter differentiability, and demonstrate the approach on two case studies (time-interval estimation and motion perception).

Identifiability of internal representations/processes is a long-standing problem in cognitive science (Anderson 1978).

The identifiability issue has been cited as a key limitation of Bayesian models (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).

Primary concern: the manuscript overlooks substantial prior literature on model comparison (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004), making its novelty hard to assess.

Primary concern: several key assumptions are inadequately justified (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter specification in Eq.12), raising doubts about the framework’s validity and sufficiency.

Claimed robustness and expressive power of the formulation do not by themselves justify the identifiability assumptions.

Recommendation: include a thorough discussion and defense of the assumptions required for identifiability.

Minor: Line 53’s “It is a trivial mathematical fact…” should include a brief explanation for clarity.

Minor: Line 73 likely contains a typo—second “nor” should probably be “or.”

Minor: Figure 2 legend is ambiguous—star and dashed line both described as the true value and need clarification.

Overall: a well-argued paper on an important topic, but difficult to evaluate due to insufficient engagement with prior related literature.
