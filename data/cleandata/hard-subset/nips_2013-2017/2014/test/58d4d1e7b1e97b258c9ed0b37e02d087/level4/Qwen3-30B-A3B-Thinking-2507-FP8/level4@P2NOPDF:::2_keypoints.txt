1. Manuscript addresses an important identifiability problem in cognitive modeling and is well-written with an innovative methodology.  
2. Results rely on extensive, strong assumptions, making the scope of conclusions disproportionate to their impact.  
3. Technical contribution is solid and merits NIPS consideration despite limitations.  
4. Section 4.1: sensory and motor noise are shown interchangeable—authors must justify why including both is unnecessary.  
5. Section 4.1: statistical reliability of condition differences is unclear from box plots and needs explicit testing.  
6. Section 4.2: recovery of a slow-speed prior depends on the reference observer having one—authors should show the method fails when the reference lacks that prior.  
7. Line 106: clarify why a continuous stimulus is modeled as discrete (experimental design vs. model assumption).  
8. Line 134: the log-stimulus transform heavily constrains inference; identifiability claims apply only given this representational choice.  
9. Line 143: constraints and entropy maximization do not guarantee unimodality—possible bimodal solutions must be addressed or excluded.  
10. Line 158: unexplained choice of an observer prior favoring a lattice and being 50% wider than the true stimulus range needs justification.  
11. Figure 1: presentation is confusing—merge panels and remove derived p_est(s*|x) to improve clarity.  
12. Line 252: Stirling’s approximation and E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x] require stated conditions/sample size N and stronger justification; Equation 15 abandons priors and relies on dubious approximations.  
13. Line 262: specify which unnormalized density is being sampled (dataset distribution vs. posterior).  
14. Line 365: the claim that prior learning “does not matter” in 2AFC tasks contradicts common assumptions and needs stronger justification.  
15. Overall: conclusions depend critically on the log transform and lattice prior; authors must clarify that identifiability holds only under their specific representational assumptions.  
16. Recommendation: acceptable for NIPS with minor revisions to clarify assumptions, justify approximations, and strengthen validation.
