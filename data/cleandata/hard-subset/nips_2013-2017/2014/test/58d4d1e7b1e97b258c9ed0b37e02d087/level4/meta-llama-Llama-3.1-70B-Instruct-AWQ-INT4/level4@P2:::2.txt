The manuscript tackles the important issue in cognitive modeling of model identifiability, presenting a novel framework for testing the identifiability of Bayesian models of perception. The work is nicely written and quite clear, but relies on a long list of assumptions that one must accept and understand, which may detract from the impact of the key results.

The methodology is interesting and innovative, making the work above threshold for presentation at NIPS. However, the key results could be strengthened by addressing some concerns. For instance, in section 4.1, the interchangeability of sensory and motor noise is not surprising, and it would be helpful to indicate which condition differences were reliable. Additionally, it would be beneficial to provide a clearer visual representation of the results, as the box plots may be difficult to interpret.

In section 4.2, it is essential to demonstrate that the model can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior if the reference observer does not have one. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the model's performance.

Some specific comments on the manuscript include:

* Line 106: The authors should clarify why the stimulus is drawn from a discrete distribution when it is, in fact, a continuous variable. This may be due to typical experimental design, but a clear explanation would be helpful.
* Line 134: The success of the work may be attributed, in part, to the incorporation of the log-stimulus transform, which imposes a strong constraint on inference. This highlights the importance of representational transformations in the mind.
* Line 143: The authors should ensure that the constraints in Eq 4 and the maximization of differential entropy guarantee that Eqn 3 is a unimodal distribution, rather than potentially bimodal.
* Line 158: The authors should explain why the observer's prior favors the lattice representation over a uniform representation. What information does the user have about the true stimulus distribution that allows them to have a prior that is 50% wider than the true range?
* Figure 1: The figure could be improved by integrating Figure 1b into Figure 1a and removing p_est(s^*|x), as it is a derived inference.
* Line 252: The authors should comment on the conditions under which Equation 14 is approximately correct, including the circumstances under which Stirling's approximation of the factorial holds and E[xlogx] ~= E[x]logE[x].
* Line 262: The authors should specify which unnormalized density is being sampled from.
* Line 365: The authors should explain why learning of priors does not matter in 2AFC.

Overall, the manuscript presents an innovative approach to an important problem, but the results could be strengthened by addressing these concerns and providing clearer explanations and visualizations.
