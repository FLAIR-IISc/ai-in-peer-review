- Tackles identifiability/non-identifiability of internal representations in Bayesian observer models and formalizes the degeneracy problem (Eq. 1).  
- Proposes a framework for testing identifiability a priori using expected log-likelihood comparisons (Eqs. 11–13).  
- Applies the framework to two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Shows practical experimental guidance (e.g., measuring motor noise parameters improves identifiability in interval timing).  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and grounded in solid theoretical development.  
- Framework is useful for experimental design and highlights that some prior findings may need reevaluation.  
- originality concern: insufficient engagement with broader model-comparison literature (e.g., Pitt, Myung & Zhang 2002; Navarro, Pitt & Myung 2004) and unclear differentiation from those approaches.  
- Quality concern: key modeling choices (e.g., two-component Gaussian mixture, claim of approximating Weber–Fechner and Stevens’ laws) lack justification and empirical/sensitivity analyses.  
- Significance concern: paper does not convincingly show why this framework advances the field beyond existing methods or addresses previously unanswerable theoretical questions.  
- Clarity issues: dismissive phrasing ("trivial mathematical fact") and unclear figure captioning (Figure 2 star/dashed lines) that could confuse readers.  
- Recommended revisions: engage the model-comparison literature, provide sensitivity analyses and justifications for model forms, clarify theoretical significance, and improve explanations/figures.  
- Overall: valuable and practically useful framework but novelty and impact are not sufficiently established; substantial revision required.
