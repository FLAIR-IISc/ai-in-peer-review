- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with formal equations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distribution.  
- Strength: balances robustness and mathematical convenience, handling stimulus-dependent noise while enabling tractable inference.  
- Strength: model is claimed to be identifiable, facilitating parameter estimation and evaluation.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS, finding the framework mathematically elegant and useful for perceptual estimation.  
- Main concern: novelty is incremental—model resembles recent PLoS Computational Biology work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommendation: add a section reviewing recent Bayesian perception models and clearly state how this work advances beyond them.  
- Limitation: practical identifiability is restricted—the model may not distinguish sensory, decision, and response noise; authors should discuss this.  
- Overall: elegant formal framework but contribution is incremental given existing related literature.
