- Addresses important problem (model identifiability); manuscript is well-written and clear.  
- Relies on an extensive set of strong assumptions that are disproportionate to the significance of the results.  
- Methodology is interesting and innovative, making the work acceptable for NIPS despite limited impact.  
- Section 4.1: Sensory/motor noise interchangeability is unsurprising; authors should report which condition differences are reliably detected.  
- Section 4.2: Must show the model can recover the absence of a slow-speed prior in the reference observer.  
- Line 106: Clarify why a discrete stimulus distribution is used when the stimulus is continuous.  
- Line 134: The log-stimulus transform is central and may drive identifiability; results may heavily depend on this choice.  
- Line 143: Clarify whether constraints and entropy maximization guarantee a unimodal fit or could produce bimodality.  
- Line 158: Justify why the observer prior favors a lattice over uniform and why it is set 50% wider than the true stimulus range.  
- Figure 1: Current layout is confusing; integrate panels (1b into 1a) and remove derived p_est(s^*|x) for clarity.  
- Line 252/around Eq.14: State conditions on N for approximations (e.g., Stirling’s validity, E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]).  
- Eq.15 and surrounding approximations: Questionable approximations and abandonment of priors make Eq.15 feel justified mainly by intuition rather than rigor.  
- Line 262: Specify which "unnormalized density" is being sampled (model density, dataset distribution, etc.).  
- Line 365: Explain why learning priors does not affect 2AFC performance.  
- Overall: Innovative approach with important goals but results are modest and heavily assumption-dependent.
