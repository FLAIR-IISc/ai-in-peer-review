- Introduces a Bayesian perceptual model detailing sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions with tractable inference.  
- Model balances robustness and mathematical convenience and can handle stimulus-dependent noise.  
- Claims model identifiability, allowing straightforward evaluation and distinction of parameter estimates.  
- Provides a mathematically elegant framework for formalizing perceptual estimation across experiments.  
- Novelty is questionable—model is similar in spirit to recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014) and may be incremental.  
- Recommends adding a review of recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly stating how this model advances beyond prior work.  
- Notes identifiability depends on model assumptions and may not distinguish sensory, decision, or response noise, limiting practical utility.  
- Overall: elegant framework but offers only an incremental contribution over existing literature.
