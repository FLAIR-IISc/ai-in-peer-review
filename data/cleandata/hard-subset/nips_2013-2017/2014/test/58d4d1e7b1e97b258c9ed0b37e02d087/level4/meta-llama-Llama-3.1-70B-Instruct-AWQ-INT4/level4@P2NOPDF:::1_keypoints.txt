- Proposes a framework to compare Bayesian models of behavioral data, targeting non-identifiability of internal representations and processes.
- Summarizes Bayesian Decision Theory, identifies non-identifiable components, and outlines constraints to make terms identifiable.
- Applies these constraints, examines parameter differentiability, and demonstrates the method on time-interval estimation and motion-perception studies.
- Strength: well-written and tackles a long-standing, important identifiability problem in cognitive science and psychology.
- Major limitation 1: Overlooks extensive prior work on model comparison (e.g., Pitt et al., Navarro et al.), and fails to acknowledge or differentiate its contribution from that literature.
- Major limitation 2: Makes seemingly arbitrary/unjustified assumptions (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter choices in Eq.12) without discussing their impact on identifiability or model expressiveness.
- Recommendation: Authors should justify and discuss assumptions and demonstrate how they affect identifiability and coverage of sensory-model classes.
- Minor: Line 53 — explain the “trivial” mathematical fact briefly; Line 73 — replace second “nor” with “or”; Figure 2 caption — clarify meanings of the star and dashed lines.
- Overall: Important topic and useful approach, but limited impact until literature engagement and assumption justification are addressed.
