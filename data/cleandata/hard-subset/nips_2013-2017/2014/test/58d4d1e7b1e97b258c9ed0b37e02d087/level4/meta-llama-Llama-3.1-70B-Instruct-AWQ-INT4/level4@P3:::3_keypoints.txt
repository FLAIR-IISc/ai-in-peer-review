- Proposes a Bayesian model of perception that combines robustness with computational tractability.  
- Model handles stimulus-dependent noise and a variety of experimental situations.  
- Authors emphasize the model's mathematical identifiability, facilitating parameter evaluation.  
- Concern: identifiability may not separate sources of experimental error (sensory vs. decision vs. response noise).  
- Contribution is incremental and closely related to recent models (e.g., Acerbi et al., 2012, 2014).  
- Recommend adding a review of recent Bayesian perception models to clarify novelty and advance over prior work.  
- Recommend clarifying identifiability assumptions and implications for perceptual scientists.  
- Strengths: mathematically elegant framework, robustness, clear writing and organization.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty, insufficient discussion of limitations and applications.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.
