- Paper presents a Bayesian model of perception with explicit mathematical formulations for sensory noise, likelihoods, priors, loss functions, and response distributions.  
- Model combines robustness and computational tractability, handling scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise while keeping inference tractable.  
- Authors emphasize the model’s identifiability, facilitating evaluation and distinction of parameter estimates.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Model offers a mathematically elegant framework for formalizing perceptual estimation across diverse experimental contexts.  
- Major reservation: novelty appears incremental and closely aligned with recent Bayesian work (e.g., Acerbi et al., 2012, 2014).  
- Suggestion: add a new section reviewing recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state how this work advances beyond prior studies.  
- Limitation: identifiability claims depend on specific model assumptions and do not solve core empirical identifiability issues (distinguishing sensory, decision, and response noise).  
- Recommendation: authors should acknowledge and address this limitation in the revised manuscript.
