- Proposes a mathematically elegant framework for Bayesian modeling of perception focused on parameter identifiability.  
- Introduces a flexible observer model that spans many psychophysical tasks to systematically study how experimental design affects parameter recovery.  
- Model is robust to different noise types and prior structures while remaining computationally tractable for efficient model-space exploration.  
- Applies the framework to time-interval estimation and speed perception, showing practical value for experimental design and validating inferences about internal representations.  
- Uses a maximum-entropy approach to increase model generality.  
- Introduces an MCMC-based method (Eqs. 12–13) for a priori evaluation of experimental designs, which is novel and useful.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a detailed and clearly presented mathematical formulation.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental and overlaps conceptually with prior Bayesian-perception work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- The manuscript needs a more comprehensive review of recent related work and a clearer statement of how it differs in generality, efficiency, or methodology.  
- The notion of “identifiability” should be clarified: identifiability is conditional on the model’s assumptions and does not necessarily disentangle sensory, decision, or motor noise in real-world data.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on strengthening related-work discussion and clarifying the scope/limits of identifiability claims.
