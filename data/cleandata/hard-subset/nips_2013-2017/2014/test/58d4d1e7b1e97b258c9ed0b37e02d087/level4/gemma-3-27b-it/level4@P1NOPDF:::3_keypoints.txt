- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with full mathematical formulations (sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, response distribution).
- Model is robust and computationally tractable, remaining analytically convenient for inference.
- Can accommodate stimulus-dependent noise and a variety of experimental scenarios.
- Authors emphasize model identifiability and ease of evaluating/distinguishing parameter estimates.
- Reviewer supports acceptance to NIPS and finds the framework mathematically elegant for perceptual estimation.
- Contribution appears incremental and closely aligned with recent PLoS Computational Biology work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).
- Recommendation to add a thorough review of recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state how this work advances prior art.
- Concern that the identifiability claim is limited: the model cannot firmly distinguish sensory, decision, and response noise sources.
- Concludes the paper is elegant but its novelty over existing literature is limited.
