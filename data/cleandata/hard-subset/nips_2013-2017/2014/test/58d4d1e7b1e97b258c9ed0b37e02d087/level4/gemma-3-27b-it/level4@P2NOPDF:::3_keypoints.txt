- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with complete mathematical formulation (sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, response distribution).
- Model combines robustness and computational tractability, allowing analytical inference across diverse scenarios (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise).
- Authors demonstrate parameter identifiability within the model framework.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS; model is mathematically elegant with broad experimental applicability.
- Major reservation: novelty is incremental due to substantial overlap with recent work (notably Acerbi et al., 2012 and 2014).
- Suggest adding a dedicated review section comparing recent Bayesian perception models and clearly articulating the paper’s advances over them.
- Caveat on identifiability: current identifiability holds because of specific model assumptions and does not resolve the broader perceptual-science identifiability problem (sensory vs. decision vs. response noise).
- Recommend clarifying the scope and limitations of the model’s identifiability in the revised manuscript.
