- Proposes a framework for Bayesian model comparison in cognitive science, summarizing Bayesian decision theory, addressing non-identifiability, imposing constraints, analyzing parameter differentiability, and applying to time-interval estimation and motion perception.  
- Notes identifiability of internal representations/processes is a longstanding challenge, citing Anderson (1978) and recent critiques (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Major concern 1: Overlooks substantial prior work on model comparison (e.g., Pitt et al., 2002; Navarro et al., 2004), making the novelty and contribution unclear.  
- Major concern 2: Assumptions used to enforce identifiability appear arbitrary and lack justification (questions about the choice of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter definitions in Eq.12).  
- Requests a thorough discussion and justification of why the chosen assumptions/constraints are necessary and adequate.  
- Minor edits: clarify line 53 wording, correct "nor" to "or" on line 73, and clarify star/dashed lines meaning in Figure 2 caption.  
- Overall: well-written and tackling an important problem, but contribution is unclear without stronger engagement with prior literature and justification of assumptions.
