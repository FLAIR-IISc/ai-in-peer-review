- Addresses the important problem of identifiability in Bayesian models of perception.  
- Proposes a novel, systematic framework for testing identifiability that can guide experimental design.  
- Uses a KL-divergence–based approach to quantify how different parameter settings produce similar predictions.  
- Develops a continuous class of Bayesian observer models with principled exploration of the identifiability landscape.  
- Demonstrates the framework on time-interval estimation and speed-perception case studies.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and technically rigorous.  
- Reliance on many strong assumptions (prior form, sensory mapping, loss function) limits generalizability.  
- Specific case-study results are of limited novelty (e.g., interchangeability of sensory and motor noise).  
- Fails to demonstrate ground-truth recovery in the speed-perception study (need to show recovery of a flat/no slow-speed prior).  
- Figure 1 is dense and hard to interpret, hindering understanding of the model architecture.  
- Question the choice of a discrete stimulus distribution when the underlying variable is continuous (Line 106).  
- Emphasize the strong constraint imposed by the log-stimulus transform and frame it as a limitation (Line 134).  
- Clarify whether constraints and entropy maximization guarantee unimodality or merely encourage it (Line 143).  
- Provide justification for assuming a prior 50% wider than the true stimulus range (Line 158).  
- Reconsider Figure 1 layout (integrate panels, remove derived inference p_est(s*|x)) to improve clarity.  
- Justify approximations used to derive Equation 15 and clarify which density is sampled (Lines 252–262).  
- Explain why prior learning might be less relevant in 2AFC tasks (Line 365).  
- Arguments for acceptance: important topic, novel and sound method, useful case studies, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy assumption dependence, limited empirical impact, and presentation issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address assumptions, add a flat-prior recovery test, and improve figure clarity.
