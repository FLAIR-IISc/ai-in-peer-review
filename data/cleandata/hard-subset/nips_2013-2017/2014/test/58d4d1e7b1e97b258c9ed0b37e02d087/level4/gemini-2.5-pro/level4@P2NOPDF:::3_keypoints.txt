- Presents a Bayesian model of perception specifying sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distributions.  
- Strength: combines robustness with mathematical convenience and is computationally tractable.  
- Flexible enough to handle experimental scenarios like stimulus-dependent noise.  
- Emphasizes model identifiability—parameters can be readily evaluated and distinguished under the model's assumptions.  
- Overall elegant framework with potential to analyze diverse experiments; reviewer generally supportive and inclined to accept.  
- Major concern: novelty is limited—substantial conceptual/methodological overlap with recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014), appearing incremental.  
- Suggestion: add a literature review section comparing recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state the paper’s novel advances.  
- Identifiability caveat: formal identifiability within assumptions does not resolve broader issue of distinguishing sensory noise, decision suboptimality, and motor noise.  
- Recommendation: temper claims about identifiability and discuss the model’s inability to dissociate distinct error sources.  
- Conclusion: with revisions clarifying novelty and scope of identifiability claims, the paper would be a solid conference contribution.
