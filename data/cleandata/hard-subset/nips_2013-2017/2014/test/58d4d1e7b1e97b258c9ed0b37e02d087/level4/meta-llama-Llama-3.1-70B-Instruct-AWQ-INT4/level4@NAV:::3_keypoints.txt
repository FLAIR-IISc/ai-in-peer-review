- Presents a Bayesian perceptual model with full mathematical specification (sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, response distribution).  
- Advantage: balances robustness and mathematical convenience, handles stimulus-dependent noise while keeping inference tractable.  
- Claims identifiability of model parameters, facilitating evaluation and distinction of estimates.  
- Praised as a mathematically elegant framework for formalizing perceptual estimation across experiments.  
- Concern: novelty appears incremental due to similarities with recent Bayesian models (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommendation: add a section reviewing recent Bayesian perception models and explicitly state the advances over existing work.  
- Caveat: identifiability depends on the model's mathematical assumptions and may not separate sensory, decision, and response noise—authors should address this limitation.  
- Overall verdict: valuable formal framework but incremental contribution relative to closely related literature.
