- Manuscript addresses important model identifiability problem in cognitive modeling; writing is clear and presentation is good.  
- Methodology is interesting and innovative, and the work is above threshold for presentation at NIPS.  
- Work relies on many strong assumptions, which feel disproportionate to the impact of the main results.  
- Section 4.1 result (sensory and motor noise interchangeability) is unsurprising and calls into question including both noise sources.  
- Need to report which condition differences are statistically reliable because box plots are hard to interpret.  
- Section 4.2 must show recovery when the reference observer does not have a slow-speed prior.  
- Clarify why the stimulus is modeled as discrete when it is fundamentally continuous (line 106).  
- The log-stimulus transform (Eq. 2) may drive much of the success; identifiability may depend on assuming specific representational transforms (line 134).  
- Question whether constraints in Eq. 4 and maximizing differential entropy guarantee a unimodal fit for Eq. 3, since bimodality seems possible (line 143).  
- Justify why the observer’s prior should favor a lattice representation over uniform and why a prior 50% wider than true range is warranted (line 158).  
- Figure 1 is confusing; suggest merging panels and removing p_est(s^*|x) to improve clarity.  
- Need conditions on N for Eq. 14 approximations (validity of Stirling’s approximation and when E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x]) and acknowledge approximations leading to Eq. 15 (line 252).  
- Clarify what “sample from this unnormalized density” refers to—specifically which density (line 262).  
- Explain why learning of priors does not matter in 2AFC and address that the results are not especially compelling (line 365).
