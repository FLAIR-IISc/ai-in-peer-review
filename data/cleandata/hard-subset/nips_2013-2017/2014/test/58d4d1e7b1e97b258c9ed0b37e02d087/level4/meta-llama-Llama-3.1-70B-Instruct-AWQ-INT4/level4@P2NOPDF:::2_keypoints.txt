- Addresses important issue of model identifiability in cognitive modeling; manuscript is well-written and organized.
- Relies on many strong assumptions that readers must accept, which may weaken the impact of results.
- Methodology is innovative and presentation meets threshold for NIPS despite reservations.
- Section 4.1: Claim that sensory and motor noise are interchangeable is unsurprising; need to report which condition differences are reliable.
- Section 4.1: Box plots may be a poor choice for displaying results; clearer visualization and clarification are needed.
- Section 4.2: Must demonstrate the model can recover that the reference observer lacks a slow‑speed prior to strengthen claims.
- Line 106: Explain why the stimulus is modeled as drawn from a discrete distribution even though it is continuous in reality.
- Line 134: The log-stimulus transform (Eq. 2) is a major modeling assumption and may drive the identifiability results — acknowledge this interpretation.
- Line 143: Constraints and entropy maximization (Eq. 4) do not guarantee a unimodal fit; address potential for bimodal solutions.
- Line 158: Justify why the observer’s prior favors a lattice representation and why the prior is 50% wider than the true stimulus range.
- Figure 1: Figure is confusing — consider integrating panels and removing derived inference p_est(s^*|x) for clarity.
- Line 252: State conditions under which Eq. 14 is approximately correct, including Stirling’s approximation and when E[x log x] ≈ E[x] log E[x].
- Line 262: Specify which density is meant by “sample from this unnormalized density.”
- Line 365: Explain why learning priors is claimed to not matter in 2AFC; this needs justification.
- Overall: Interesting and innovative contribution but results are not fully convincing given assumptions and unresolved methodological clarifications.
