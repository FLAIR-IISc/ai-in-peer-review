- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian models of perception to improve experimental design.  
- Introduces a flexible, efficient class of Bayesian observer models for exploring many psychophysical tasks.  
- Demonstrates the framework with two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Provides strong theoretical analysis and simulation-based validation of the framework.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Strength — originality: significant contribution to the field.  
- Strength — technical soundness: thorough theoretical and simulation support.  
- Strength — clarity: easy to follow presentation.  
- Limitation — technical analysis is complex and may be hard for non-experts.  
- Limitation — scope limited to a specific class of Bayesian observer models; generalizability is unclear.  
- Limitation — lacks empirical data; relies on simulations only.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, significant contribution; suitable for publication with minor revisions.
