- Addresses non-identifiability in Bayesian perception models arising from degenerate inverse mappings from behavior to priors, likelihoods, and loss functions.  
- Proposes an a priori identifiability testing framework that evaluates experimental designs before data collection.  
- Uses KL divergence between response distributions under different parameters to quantify identifiability, avoiding costly Monte Carlo integration.  
- Demonstrates framework utility with two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Time-interval case yields counterintuitive insight: imposing a MAP-like loss can worsen prior recovery.  
- Speed perception case shows robustness of prior inference and aligns with previous findings.  
- Strength: practical, theoretically grounded method that fills a timely methodological gap.  
- Strength: clearly articulated problem, distinction from prior model-fitting work, and effective case-study validation.  
- Strength: framework is flexible (multiple conditions, motor noise) and computationally efficient via analytical KL.  
- Weakness: computational scalability not benchmarked; MCMC step (Eq.13) may be prohibitive for high-dimensional θ.  
- Weakness: sensory mapping (Eq.2) used without sufficient justification or testing of alternatives (e.g., power-law).  
- Weakness: limited scope—does not test scenario with learned/context-dependent priors.  
- Weakness: omits related prior work (e.g., Myung et al. 2012) on model identifiability/model selection.  
- Suggestion: add a "Computational Considerations" subsection discussing runtime scaling and heuristics for high-dimensional models.  
- Suggestion: justify the choice of sensory mapping in Section 2.1 and note dependency of results on this parametrization.  
- Suggestion: extend speed perception case to include learned priors to test confounds between learning and prior recovery.  
- Suggestion: cite Myung et al. (2012) and related model-identifiability literature to better contextualize contribution.  
- Overall: strong, methodologically rigorous paper with minor addressable weaknesses; recommendation: Accept.
