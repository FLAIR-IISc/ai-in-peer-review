- Presents a computational framework to assess a priori identifiability of Bayesian observer models in perceptual science.
- Addresses degeneracy in Bayesian Decision Theory where multiple priors/likelihoods/losses yield indistinguishable behavior.
- Proposes a flexible, efficient parametric class of observer models that unifies many internal components.
- Introduces a method to explore the "identifiability landscape" via sampling an approximate expected posterior density linked to KL divergence between response distributions.
- Demonstrates framework utility with two case studies: time-interval estimation (experimental design comparison) and speed perception (validating slow-speed prior robustness).
- High significance: tackles a core problem that can undermine inferences from Bayesian cognitive models.
- Novel approach: tailored, systematic identifiability analysis for Bayesian observer models with a theoretical link between expected log-likelihood and KL divergence.
- Technical soundness: four-stage observer model with flexible function classes (e.g., Gaussian mixtures) enables broad model exploration.
- Practical demonstration: uses model to evaluate and rank experimental designs in the timing task.
- Insightful findings: independently measuring motor noise best breaks timing-task degeneracies.
- Insightful finding: narrow/MAP-like loss functions can worsen recovery of the prior—an unintuitive, testable prediction.
- Validates robustness of classic slow-speed prior finding in speed perception.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and supported by informative figures.
- Suggestion 1 — sensitivity to reference observer: discuss how conclusions depend on the choice of θ* and whether degeneracies are task-general or parameter-specific.
- Suggestion 2 — practicality/computational cost: provide quantitative runtime details (e.g., MCMC runtimes) to inform usability.
- Suggestion 3 — scope/generalization: justify the conversion of 2AFC to an equivalent estimation task and clarify assumptions for discrete-choice paradigms.
