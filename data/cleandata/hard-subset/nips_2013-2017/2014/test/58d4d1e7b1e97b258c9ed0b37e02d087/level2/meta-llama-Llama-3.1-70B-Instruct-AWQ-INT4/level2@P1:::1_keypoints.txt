- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perception models.
- Develops a flexible, efficient class of Bayesian observer models for many psychophysical tasks.
- Introduces an identifiability method using expected support computed as log likelihood.
- Applies framework to two case studies: time-interval prior inference and speed-perception prior/noise reconstruction.
- Framework can rank experimental designs by their ability to resolve parameter degeneracies.
- Finds that imposing a narrow loss function can hinder prior inference in the time-interval task.
- Well-motivated approach with clear, detailed explanation of methods and results.
- Case studies are well-chosen and demonstrate practical utility.
- Assumes high background knowledge in psychophysics and Bayesian modeling, limiting accessibility.
- Framework is restricted to a specific class of observer models; generalizability is unclear.
- Paper could discuss implications for experimental design and behavioral data interpretation more.
- Original contributions: novel framework, new observer-model class, and original identifiability method.
- Significant potential impact on psychophysics and experimental design practices.
- High quality: well-written, clearly organized, methods and results easy to follow.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, flexible models, clear identifiability method, strong case studies.
- Arguments for rejection: steep expertise requirement, limited model scope, insufficient discussion of broader implications.
