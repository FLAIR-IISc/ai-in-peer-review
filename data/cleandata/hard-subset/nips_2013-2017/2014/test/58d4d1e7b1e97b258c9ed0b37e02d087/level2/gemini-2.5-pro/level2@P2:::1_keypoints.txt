- Addresses model identifiability in Bayesian observer models and the risk of invalid inferences when different internal components produce identical behavior.  
- Main contribution: a principled, a priori framework to test parameter identifiability and guide experimental design.  
- Framework component 1: flexible yet tractable class of 1D Bayesian observer models parameterized by Gaussian-mixture moments for sensory mapping, noise, prior, and loss.  
- Framework component 2: efficient identifiability mapping via an analytic expected log-likelihood proportional to negative KL divergence between model response distributions.  
- Uses sampling from the expected-log-likelihood density to explore which parameters are well-constrained versus degenerate for a given design.  
- Case study 1 (time estimation): finds strong non-identifiability, notably a degeneracy between sensory and motor noise parameters.  
- Practical finding: independently measuring motor noise improves identifiability far more than increasing trials or enforcing a MAP-like loss.  
- Case study 2 (speed perception): inference of a slow-speed prior is theoretically robust and not highly degenerate.  
- Strength: original and significant shift from post-hoc model selection to principled, quantitative experimental design tools.  
- Strength: technically elegant and tractable formulation using KL-based expected evidence and moment-parameterized Gaussian mixtures.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with effective case-study demonstrations.  
- Weakness: current applicability limited to 1D estimation tasks; extensions to 2AFC or multidimensional stimuli need clarification.  
- Weakness: unimodality constraint on likelihoods and loss (via Gaussian-mixture choices) may be restrictive and requires justification or extension.  
- Weakness: some surprising results (e.g., MAP-like loss worsening prior recovery) need more intuition and deeper explanation.  
- Practical concern/suggestion: framework complexity may hinder community adoption—public software would greatly aid impact.  
- Overall conclusion: excellent, original, and technically strong paper with minor addressable weaknesses and high potential to become a standard tool.
