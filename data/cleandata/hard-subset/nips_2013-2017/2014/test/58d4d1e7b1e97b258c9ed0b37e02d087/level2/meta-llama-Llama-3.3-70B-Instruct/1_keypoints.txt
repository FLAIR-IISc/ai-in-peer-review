- Proposes a novel framework for systematically testing identifiability of Bayesian observer models in psychophysical tasks.  
- Introduces a general class of observer models for sensorimotor estimation and an efficient method to explore their identifiability landscape.  
- Applies the framework to two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Main claim: the framework can improve experimental design by identifying the most informative setups for recovering internal representations.  
- Shows experimental design affects reconstruction error of recovered priors in the time interval estimation task.  
- Finds inference of internal representations in speed perception is relatively robust and not prone to major degeneracies.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed explanations and a thorough literature review.  
- Significant contribution: provides a systematic approach to studying identifiability of Bayesian models of perception.  
- Strengths: clear writing, comprehensive literature review, detailed framework exposition, and important contribution to the field.  
- Weaknesses: complex mathematical derivations that may be difficult for non-experts to follow.  
- Accessibility limited by assumptions of high familiarity with Bayesian decision theory and psychophysics.  
- Originality: presents a novel framework and demonstrates expertise through thorough explanation and literature grounding.  
- Significance: potential to improve experimental design in psychophysics and enhance understanding of human perception.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and accessibility, adding explanations/examples for non-experts.  
- Suggests authors consider extending the framework to other psychophysics areas, e.g., cue-integration or discrete-choice paradigms.
