- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian models of perception.  
- Introduces a general class of observer models applicable to many psychophysical tasks.  
- Develops an efficient method for exploring model identifiability.  
- Applies the framework to two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with an easy-to-follow methodology.  
- Introduction provides a thorough literature review and clear motivation.  
- Observer modeling approach is flexible and computationally efficient.  
- Uses a maximum-entropy approach to shape measurement distributions and loss functions, integrating prior knowledge.  
- Framework enables exploration of a large class of models while retaining flexibility.  
- Case studies are well-executed and yield interesting, non-trivial results.  
- Key finding: reconstructing internal representations in interval timing requires strong experimental constraints and validation.  
- Main strength: clear writing, thorough literature coverage, and flexible observer modeling.  
- Main weakness: framework may not be directly applicable to all psychophysical tasks; suggested modifications and their effects are unclear.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to psychophysics with practical value for experimental design.  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8.5/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication; acknowledges need for further work to explore framework limitations.
