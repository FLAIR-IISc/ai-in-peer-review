- Addresses non-identifiability of internal representations in Bayesian perceptual models, where different parameter combinations yield identical behavior.  
- Proposes an a priori identifiability-testing framework including a novel observer model and expected log-likelihood difference metric.  
- Demonstrates framework via two case studies (time estimation and speed perception) showing counterintuitive and validating results.  
- High significance: tackles a fundamental problem that many prior works ignore, with direct implications for experimental design.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous theory using KL divergence and concrete evidence from case studies.  
- Originality: first systematic a priori identifiability framework, improving on prior ad-hoc approaches.  
- Clarity and structure: well-organized presentation with effective figures.  
- Model scope limitation: assumes unimodal sensory noise and a specific sensory map parametrization, limiting generality.  
- Computational cost concern: MCMC-based identifiability analysis may not scale well to high-dimensional models.  
- Limited case study breadth: only two tasks analyzed; additional examples would strengthen generality.  
- Recommendation: valuable, immediately applicable contribution with minor addressable weaknesses.  
- Verdict: Strong accept (significance outweighs limitations).
