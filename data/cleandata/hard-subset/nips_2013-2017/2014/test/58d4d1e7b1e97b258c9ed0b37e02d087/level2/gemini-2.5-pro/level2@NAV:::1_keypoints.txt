- Addresses model identifiability in Bayesian observer models, noting the inverse problem is ill-posed (multiple component combinations can yield identical behavior).  
- Central contribution: a computational framework to test a priori identifiability of observer models.  
- Framework components: a flexible parametric class of observers and an efficient Kullback–Leibler divergence-based method to map the identifiability landscape.  
- Builds on existing Bayesian perceptual modeling literature but fills a gap by assessing validity and uniqueness of inferences.  
- Significant and original: provides a principled tool to evaluate and improve experimental designs before data collection.  
- Potential practical impact: can save resources and prevent erroneous conclusions from poorly identifiable designs.  
- Technical approach is sound, and two case studies demonstrate the framework’s utility.  
- Case study insight: designs that encourage MAP-like loss may worsen recovery of the observer’s prior in time-interval estimation.  
- Positive control: successful robust inference of a slow-speed prior validates the method.  
- Primary weakness: proposed observer model is complex with many parameters, which may hinder adoption.  
- Releasing code is recommended to boost impact and reproducibility.  
- Limitation: framework currently restricted to one-dimensional estimation tasks; conversion of 2AFC paradigms to estimation lacks detail and may rely on questionable assumptions.  
- Suggestion: include discussion of robustness to the choice of the “reference observer.”  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, significant contribution with minor addressable weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
