- Paper addresses identifiability in Bayesian perception models where different parameter sets can produce identical behavior.
- Proposes a novel, practical framework for systematically testing identifiability to guide experimental design.
- Framework uses Kullback-Leibler divergence to explore parameter space and assess inference sensitivity to experimental setups.
- Built on a flexible observer model enabling efficient computation and model-space exploration.
- Demonstrated via two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.
- Time-interval case: narrow loss functions can hinder accurate prior reconstruction.
- Time-interval case: independent measurement of motor noise is crucial for identifiability.
- Speed-perception validation reaffirms existing findings and lends credibility to the framework.
- Strengths: clear articulation of the problem, well-motivated methodology, actionable insights, and reproducible supplementary material.
- Weakness: reliance on Gaussian priors/likelihoods limits claimed generality; alternative distributional assumptions not explored.
- Weakness: analysis focused on a specific observer model, leaving generalizability to other Bayesian perceptual models unclear.
- Weakness: discussion of the MAP condition is underdeveloped and causes of worsened reconstruction not fully investigated.
- Acceptance arguments: tackles an important methodological challenge with a solid, practical contribution and clear writing.
- Rejection arguments: limited distributional assumptions and scope of validation reduce robustness of claims.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address distributional assumptions, explore generalizability, and release implementation code.
