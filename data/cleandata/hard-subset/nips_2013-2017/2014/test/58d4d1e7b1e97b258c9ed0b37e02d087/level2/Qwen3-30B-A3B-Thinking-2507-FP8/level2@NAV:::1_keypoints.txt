- Proposes a novel framework for systematically testing identifiability in Bayesian perception models.  
- Provides a method to assess whether priors, likelihoods, and loss functions can be reliably recovered from behavioral data before experiments.  
- Tackles degeneracy in Bayesian observer models where different parameter combinations produce identical behavior.  
- Formalizes a priori identifiability using KL divergence (Equation 12).  
- Offers a practical tool for guiding experimental design to ensure parameter recoverability.  
- Well-motivated: addresses the common lack of constraints that prevents unique model component identification.  
- Demonstrates utility with two case studies: time interval estimation (Section 4.1) and speed perception (Section 4.2).  
- Reveals non-intuitive findings, e.g., narrow loss functions can degrade prior recovery in interval timing.  
- Finds speed perception inferences are relatively robust to degeneracy.  
- Rigorous mathematical derivations (Section 3) and a clear four-stage observer model (Section 2).  
- Main limitations: currently restricted to continuous one-dimensional estimation tasks and lacks empirical validation.  
- Authors show strong domain knowledge and appropriate citation of prior Bayesian perception work.  
- Minor omission: would benefit from a brief comparison to existing identifiability/distinguishability methods (e.g., Navarro et al.).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with a recommendation to accept.
