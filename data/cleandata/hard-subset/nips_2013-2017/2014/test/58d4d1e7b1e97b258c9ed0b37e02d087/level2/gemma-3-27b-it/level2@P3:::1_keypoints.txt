- Tackles identifiability in Bayesian perception models: multiple priors/likelihoods/losses can produce identical behavior.  
- Proposes a framework for a priori identifiability analysis and extends prior work to a continuous class of observer models.  
- Emphasizes the degeneracy inherent in Bayesian modeling and provides clear explanations of the problem.  
- Claims a computationally tractable framework and demonstrates application via two case studies (time interval estimation, speed perception).  
- Time interval case study highlights the importance of motor noise parameters for reconstruction.  
- Speed perception case study shows robustness but is less exploratory than the time-interval analysis.  
- Strong connections to existing literature with appropriate citations.  
- Details of the MCMC sampling procedure for approximate posterior exploration (Eq. 13) are insufficiently described and validated.  
- Choice of parametric sensory mapping (Eq. 2) and loss function (Eq. 6) is justified but sensitivity to these choices is underexplored.  
- Narrow loss function worsening reconstruction is a surprising result that needs deeper analysis and explanation.  
- Experimental scope is limited—results are tied to a specific setup; broader experimental designs and parameter sweeps could improve generality.  
- Framework is currently restricted to one-dimensional stimulus magnitudes; extension to higher dimensions is not addressed.  
- Impact of simplifying assumptions (e.g., parameter independence) is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Overall: valuable and well-motivated contribution, but needs more methodological detail, sensitivity analyses, broader experiments, and discussion of limitations before acceptance.
