- Addresses non-identifiability in Bayesian observer models where different priors, likelihoods, and losses produce identical behavior.
- Proposes a novel a priori framework to test model identifiability before running experiments.
- Core method computes the expected Kullback–Leibler divergence between a hypothetical ground-truth observer and alternative models to map an "identifiability landscape."
- Demonstrated on two case studies: interval timing (Jazayeri & Shadlen) and speed perception (Stocker & Simoncelli).
- Technically sound and high-quality work with a flexible parametric observer model.
- Elegant derivation of expected log-likelihood as a metric for model distinguishability.
- Clear, well-organized manuscript with informative figures that support conclusions.
- Significant practical impact: guides experimental design choices (stimulus distributions, feedback, complementary measurements).
- Non-obvious insight: MAP-like loss functions can worsen prior identifiability.
- Validates robustness of the slow-speed prior inference, supporting a landmark finding.
- Strong arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental methodological problem and provides a practical tool with well-chosen case studies.
- Primary limitation: framework is currently restricted to one-dimensional estimation tasks; extensions to discrete-choice or multi-cue tasks are future work.
