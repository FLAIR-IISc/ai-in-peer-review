- Proposes a novel framework to test identifiability of Bayesian observer models in perception.  
- Argues Bayesian observer models can be intrinsically degenerate, making recovery of priors, likelihoods, or loss functions challenging.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction to model identifiability.  
- Provides a comprehensive review of related literature and limitations of existing approaches.  
- Framework uses a new class of observer models claimed to be flexible and computationally efficient.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on two case studies: time interval prior inference and reconstruction of prior/noise in speed perception.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, novel flexible model class, empirical demonstrations, and strong writing.  
- Weakness: assumes observer’s internal likelihood is represented in the same measurement space as the true likelihood.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity, raising scalability concerns.  
- Weakness: does not address limitations for handling complex or non-linear mappings between internal representations and behavior.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant contribution with demonstrated effectiveness and thorough literature context.  
- Argument against acceptance: problematic assumptions and missing analyses on complexity and non-linear applicability.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution but should address stated limitations to strengthen the work.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
