- Tackles practical identifiability of priors, likelihoods, and loss functions in Bayesian observer models.
- Proposes a unified, parametric 1D estimation model class covering sensation f(s), moment-constrained unimodal Gaussian-mixture likelihood, Gaussian lattice mixture prior, inverted Gaussian-mixture loss, expected-loss decision rule with lapses, and motor-noise responses.
- Derives an a priori identifiability metric: expected log-likelihood under θ* reducing to a weighted sum of KL divergences across stimulus conditions (Eq. 12).
- Introduces sampling of an “expected posterior” E(θ|θ*) to explore parameter degeneracies and design choices before data collection.
- Case study (interval timing): priors are poorly recovered unless motor noise is known; incentivizing near-MAP loss worsens recovery.
- Case study (speed perception): slow-speed prior and noise parameters are relatively robust, consistent with prior literature.
- Theoretical derivation of expected support is sound under i.i.d. trials and known stimulus frequencies.
- Modeling framework is coherent and computationally motivated, offering practical design guidance.
- Conclusions depend on strong parametric assumptions (two-component mixtures, unimodality, 1D tasks) and assumed stimulus-to-internal mapping f(s).
- Sensitivity analyses to model misspecification are limited.
- The 2AFC-to-estimation reduction is plausible but not validated in the paper.
- Exposition is generally clear and well-structured, but key computational and construction details are deferred to the supplement and some notation could be streamlined.
- The combination of a general observer class with KL-based expected support is novel and practically useful.
- Framework has potential high significance for experimental design and addressing degeneracy critiques in Bayesian cognition.
- Pros: principled KL-based identifiability metric, unified tractable parameterization, actionable insights in two domains, strong literature connections.
- Cons: lacks empirical validation/model-recovery benchmarks on full simulated datasets, heavy parametric assumptions, reliance on accurate f(s), limited comparison to other design criteria, and assumed 2AFC casting.
- Suggested improvements: add recovery curves vs. full simulation, report computational costs, test robustness to misspecified loss/likelihood, compare with BOED objectives (e.g., expected information gain/Fisher), and clarify extension to discrete-choice tasks.
