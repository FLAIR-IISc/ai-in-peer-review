- Paper addresses model non-identifiability in Bayesian perception: multiple component combinations can yield identical behavior.
- Proposes a novel, systematic framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian observer models a priori.
- Framework builds a flexible Bayesian observer model class covering many psychophysical tasks.
- Uses expected support (via KL divergence) as a theoretically grounded identifiability metric.
- Demonstrates framework on two case studies: time perception and speed perception.
- Technically sound, mathematically rigorous, and empirically validated; supplementary material supports reproducibility.
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization; figures effectively illustrate results.
- Minor clarity suggestion: include more MCMC sampling details in the main text.
- Original and significant contribution—first general practical method for identifiability testing in this domain.
- Important experimental-design implications; measuring motor parameters and design choices affect identifiability.
- Key empirical insight: imposing a narrow loss function (near-MAP) can worsen prior recovery.
- Strengths: fills a critical gap, practical framework, concrete guidance, well-executed case studies.
- Weaknesses: limited to one-dimensional stimulus estimation models; potential high computational cost.
- Could better relate framework to existing model comparison metrics (e.g., Bayesian model selection).
- Overall recommendation: technically excellent, high impact, strongly recommended for acceptance.
