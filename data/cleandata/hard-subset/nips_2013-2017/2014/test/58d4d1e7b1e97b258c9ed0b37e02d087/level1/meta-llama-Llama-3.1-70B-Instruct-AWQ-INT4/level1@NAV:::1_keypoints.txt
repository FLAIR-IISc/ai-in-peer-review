- Proposes a novel framework to test identifiability of Bayesian perceptual models and introduces a new class of Bayesian observer models for this purpose.
- Provides a comprehensive review of Bayesian Decision Theory (BDT) and its application to perceptual tasks, highlighting degeneracy and non-identifiability issues.
- Framework demonstrated via case studies that illustrate practical application to perceptual experiments.
- Time-interval estimation case study shows how experimental designs can be ranked by their ability to resolve parameter degeneracy.
- Speed-perception case study demonstrates robustness of inferred internal representations in that task.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of theoretical background and the proposed framework.
- Strength: thorough BDT review that grounds the proposed methods.
- Strength: novel framework with potential wide applicability in perception research.
- Strength: effective use of case studies to validate and illustrate the approach.
- Strength: clear theoretical exposition overall.
- Weakness: paper is highly technical and may be difficult for some readers to follow.
- Weakness: assumes substantial prior knowledge in perception and BDT.
- Weakness: some derivations (e.g., expected support for model θ) are dense and may need multiple readings.
- Weakness: could benefit from more visual aids (diagrams/figures) to clarify concepts.
