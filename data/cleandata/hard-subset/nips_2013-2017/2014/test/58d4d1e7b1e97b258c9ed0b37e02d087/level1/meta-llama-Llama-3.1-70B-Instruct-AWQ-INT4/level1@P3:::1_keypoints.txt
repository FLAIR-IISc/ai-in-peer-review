- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian perception models, addressing a critical psychophysics issue.  
- Introduces a general class of Bayesian observer models applicable to many psychophysical tasks.  
- Develops an efficient method to explore identifiability by computing expected support for a model given data from a reference model.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with an accessible presentation of methods.  
- Strong literature review and clear motivation for systematic identifiability testing in psychophysics.  
- Provides a clear explanation of the degeneracy problem in Bayesian models that the framework targets.  
- Uses a maximum-entropy approach to define likelihood and loss functions, offering a principled specification.  
- Presents technical details and the computation of expected support clearly and concisely.  
- Case studies validate the framework and yield useful insights into model parameter identifiability.  
- Main weakness: assumes relatively simple forms for likelihood and loss functions, raising generalization concerns.  
- Additional concern: analysis uses a small number of parameters, leaving scalability to more complex models unclear.  
- Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance ratings: 8/10, 9/10, 8/10, 9/10 respectively.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, addresses degeneracy, informative case studies with experimental design implications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generality of likelihood/loss assumptions and potential scalability issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—add analyses demonstrating robustness to more complex likelihood/loss forms and scalability to larger models.
