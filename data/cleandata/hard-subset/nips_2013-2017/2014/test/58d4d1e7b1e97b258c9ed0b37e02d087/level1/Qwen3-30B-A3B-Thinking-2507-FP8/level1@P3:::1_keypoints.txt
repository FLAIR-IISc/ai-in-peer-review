- Addresses the non-identifiability problem in Bayesian perception: multiple priors/likelihoods/losses can yield identical behavior.  
- Proposes a framework to test identifiability a priori by computing expected log-likelihood support using KL divergence.  
- Develops a flexible Bayesian observer model class applicable to many sensory estimation tasks.  
- Demonstrates the framework with two case studies: time interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Strength: tackles an important, often-overlooked methodological problem with high significance for the field.  
- Strength: technically sound with clear derivations; KL divergence is an appropriate identifiability metric.  
- Strength: practical utility—can rank experimental designs by their ability to recover specific parameters.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, organized, and supported by clear math and visualizations.  
- Weakness: case studies are limited in scope and may overstate generality to more complex tasks (multi-cue, categorical, complex losses).  
- Weakness: computational complexity of posterior sampling (Eq. 13) and scalability to high-dimensional spaces are not adequately addressed.  
- Weakness: lacks concrete practical implementation guidance (tools, handling noisy/outlier real-world data).  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to alternative identifiability methods (e.g., Bayesian model averaging, regularization, informative constraints).  
- Overall: valuable, theoretically sound contribution with practical impact, but needs more on scalability and implementation.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
