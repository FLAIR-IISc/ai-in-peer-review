- Proposes a novel framework for testing identifiability of Bayesian observer models in perception.  
- Introduces a continuous class of Bayesian observer models applicable to tasks like time and speed perception.  
- Develops an efficient method to explore model identifiability within this framework.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two case studies: prior inference in time-interval estimation and reconstruction of prior/noise in speed perception.  
- Technically sound with clear, well-supported theoretical analysis and well-motivated framework.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, with useful demonstrations of the framework’s potential.  
- Minor issues: lacks detailed description of computational implementation and some figures need clearer presentation.  
- Some technical details may be hard for non-experts; more explanations or references would help accessibility.  
- Novel and significant contribution that builds on prior work but would benefit from clearer positioning relative to existing approaches.  
- Addresses an important problem and has potential impact for experiment design and data analysis in psychophysics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, useful case studies, clear writing and motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient computational details, accessibility concerns for non-experts, limited discussion of relation to prior methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address computational detail, clarity, and contextualization.
