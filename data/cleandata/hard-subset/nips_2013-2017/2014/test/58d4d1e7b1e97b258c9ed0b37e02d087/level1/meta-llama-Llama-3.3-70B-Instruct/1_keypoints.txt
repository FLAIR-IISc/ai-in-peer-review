- Proposes a novel framework to systematically test identifiability of Bayesian observer models in psychophysical tasks.  
- Introduces a general class of observer models applicable to various sensorimotor estimation tasks.  
- Provides an efficient method to explore model identifiability.  
- Demonstrates the framework on two case studies: time-interval estimation and speed perception.  
- Well-written with clear, detailed explanations of the framework and applications.  
- Builds on established Bayesian observer models and extends their analysis toward identifiability.  
- Main contribution is a systematic approach to assess identifiability, crucial for reliable model fitting and interpretation.  
- Strengths: clear organization, thorough explanation, useful case studies, and detailed discussion of results and experimental implications.  
- Weakness: framework complexity and dense notation may hinder comprehension for some readers.  
- Weakness: assumes high familiarity with Bayesian decision theory and psychophysics, limiting accessibility across fields.  
- Could improve by more explicitly discussing framework limitations and potential applicability in other domains.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant; with revisions to improve accessibility, the paper is a valuable contribution.
