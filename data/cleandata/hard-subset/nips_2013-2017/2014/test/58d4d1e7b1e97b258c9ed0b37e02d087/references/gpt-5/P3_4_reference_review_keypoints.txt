- Paper proposes an a priori framework to assess identifiability of Bayesian observer models in perceptual estimation tasks.  
- Builds a flexible parametric observer class: internal mapping f(s), sensory measurement (mixture of Gaussians), lattice-mixture prior, inverted-mixture loss, lapses, and motor/report noise.  
- Derives response distributions p_resp(r|s; θ) and shows expected log-likelihood reduces to a weighted sum of KL divergences across stimuli (Eq. 12).  
- Defines an “expected posterior” E(θ|θ*) ∝ exp(〈log L〉) and samples it via MCMC to visualize identifiability and compare designs.  
- Case study 1 (interval timing): reveals degeneracy between sensory/motor noise and loss width that impedes prior recovery unless motor noise is independently measured.  
- Case study 2 (speed perception): slow-speed prior exponent and contrast-dependent noise are recovered relatively robustly even with decoupled internal likelihood parameters.  
- Situates work within Bayesian perception/decision theory and prior critiques on non-identifiability and “Bayesian just-so stories.”  
- Novelty lies in an implementable, task-generic pipeline and an analytical reduction enabling efficient exploration of identifiability.  
- Strength: tackles a timely, important problem with potential broad utility for experimental design in psychophysics and cognitive neuroscience.  
- Strength: technically sound derivation of the expected log-likelihood as sum of KLs and practical use of E(θ|θ*) for ranking designs.  
- Strength: flexible, tractable observer class covering many tasks via analytic components.  
- Strength: insightful case studies that yield actionable experimental advice (e.g., measure motor noise).  
- Weakness: identifiability conclusions may depend on specific parametric choices (mixture components, prior lattice size, lapse model, noise assumptions).  
- Weakness: E(θ|θ*) is not a true posterior (lacks priors, scales with Ntr) and could be misinterpreted as calibrated uncertainty.  
- Weakness: limited validation — lacks simulated model-recovery comparisons to confirm E(θ|θ*) predicts actual recovery.  
- Weakness: framework ranks predefined designs but does not perform stimulus/frequency optimization (e.g., maximizing expected information).  
- Weakness: potential entanglement and unexamined identifiability of internal mapping f(s) parameters with prior/likelihood.  
- Weakness: minor clarity/consistency issues and deferred MCMC/max-entropy details to supplement reduce immediate readability.  
- Quality: derivations and reasoning are correct; additional simulated recovery would boost confidence.  
- Clarity: generally clear and organized but some notation and methodological choices need clearer main-text summaries.  
- Originality: moderately novel — combines versatile observer class, KL-based identifiability objective, and explicit design comparisons.  
- Significance: high practical relevance for researchers inferring internal representations; extensible to cue integration and choice paradigms.  
- Suggestions: run limited model-recovery experiments, test alternative lapse models, analyze identifiability of f(s), sketch design-optimization using KL/Fisher objectives, and release code.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important gap with a principled, efficient, and broadly applicable framework and concrete case studies.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on specific parameterizations, lack of empirical recovery validation, and undeveloped design optimization.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
