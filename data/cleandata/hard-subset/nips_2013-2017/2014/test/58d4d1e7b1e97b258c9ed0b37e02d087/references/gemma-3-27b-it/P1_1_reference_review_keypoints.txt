- Paper tackles non-identifiability of parameters in Bayesian perception models and proposes an a priori framework to assess identifiability, demonstrated on time and speed perception tasks.
- Builds on Bayesian Decision Theory literature and addresses prior critiques about non-uniqueness in Bayesian modeling, citing relevant prior work.
- Strength: Addresses an important, often-overlooked problem in Bayesian modeling of perception.
- Strength: Presents a flexible, general framework that accommodates different sensory mappings and loss functions.
- Strength: Emphasizes a priori identifiability analysis to guide experimental design.
- Strength: Provides a clear methodology using KL-divergence and MCMC sampling to explore parameter sensitivity.
- Strength: Illustrative case studies reveal non-intuitive effects (e.g., influence of loss assumptions) and demonstrate framework utility.
- Strength: Manuscript is well-written, organized, and supported by helpful equations and a flowchart.
- Weakness: Potentially high computational cost and unclear scalability for high-dimensional or complex models.
- Weakness: Relies on simplifying assumptions (e.g., unimodal sensory distributions, specific prior/loss forms) with limited analysis of their impact.
- Weakness: Limited scope of examples—two case studies may not fully represent broader Bayesian modeling applications.
- Weakness: Insufficient detail about numerical marginalization (Eq. 9) and possible numerical instabilities.
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of how the framework could integrate or be constrained by neural data.
- For acceptance: Novel, practical framework addressing a fundamental issue, useful for experimental design, and technically sound.
- Against acceptance: Concerns about computational cost, simplifying assumptions, and limited example scope.
- Overall: Significant contribution warranting acceptance pending minor revisions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computation/scalability, sensitivity to assumptions, neural-data integration, and numerical details for marginalization.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
