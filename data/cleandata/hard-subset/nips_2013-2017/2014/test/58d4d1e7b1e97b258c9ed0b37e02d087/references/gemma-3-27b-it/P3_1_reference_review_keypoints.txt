- Presents a novel framework for a priori assessment of identifiability in Bayesian observer models of perception.  
- Uses flexible parameterization and KL divergence with MCMC to efficiently explore parameter distinguishability.  
- Demonstrates utility via case studies in time and speed perception, showing experimental design affects parameter recovery.  
- Builds on prior Bayesian perception and model-identifiability literature, filling a gap for pre-experiment identifiability analysis.  
- Tackles an important, fundamental problem for the validity of inferences from Bayesian perceptual models.  
- Offers a general and flexible observer model that balances expressiveness and computational tractability.  
- Employs a principled methodology (KL divergence, MCMC) to quantify model distinguishability.  
- Provides practical, well-chosen case studies that reveal useful experimental design insights.  
- Paper is clearly written and supported by detailed supplementary material.  
- Framework has strong open-source/tool development potential for researchers.  
- MCMC-based identifiability analysis can be computationally expensive and may not scale well to high-dimensional spaces.  
- Framework’s usefulness depends on the correctness of the chosen observer model; sensitivity analyses are limited.  
- Response-stage modeling is simplistic (independent motor noise assumption) and may introduce identifiability issues.  
- Parameter exploration is focused and not exhaustive; broader parameter ranges may reveal more problems.  
- Lacks integration with neural data despite motivation to link behavior and neural representations.  
- For acceptance: addresses a crucial problem, presents a rigorous novel framework, and demonstrates practical impact.  
- Against acceptance: computational cost, reliance on chosen model validity, and limited scope beyond estimation tasks.  
- Overall: significant and practically relevant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational and sensitivity concerns.
