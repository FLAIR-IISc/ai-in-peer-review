- Introduces a flexible one-dimensional observer model parameterizing stimulus-to-measurement mapping, likelihoods, priors, loss, lapses, and motor noise.  
- Derives an a priori identifiability measure based on expected log-likelihood under a reference observer, yielding a sum of KL divergences between response distributions.  
- Proposes sampling an “approximate expected posterior” via MCMC to map identifiability landscapes.  
- Applies the framework to interval timing and speed perception case studies.  
- Finds timing exhibits strong degeneracy between sensory and motor noise and poor prior recovery unless motor noise is known.  
- Shows encouraging MAP-like behavior in timing worsens prior recovery due to low identifiability of loss width.  
- Finds speed perception parameters (slow-speed priors, noise) are relatively well recoverable, even when decoupling internal/external noise.  
- Situates work within prior identifiability and model-distinguishability literature, relating to expected log-score and optimal design concepts.  
- Offers a computationally efficient, analytically structured observer class using Gaussian-mixture components.  
- Provides concrete experimental-design implications (e.g., measure motor noise independently; narrow loss priors can harm identifiability).  
- Emphasizes comparing designs by expected identifiability and locating fragile parameters.  
- Analysis is entirely within-class; robustness to model misspecification is not systematically assessed.  
- The “approximate expected posterior” is not a true posterior; its dependence on parameterization, implicit volume, and MCMC diagnostics are not detailed.  
- Lacks comparisons to local identifiability metrics (Hessian/Fisher, profile likelihoods) or Bayesian optimal design baselines.  
- Some modeling choices (two-component mixtures, prior lattice size, mapping f(s)) may bias identifiability and need sensitivity checks.  
- Claims of theoretical validation for slow-speed priors are strong given simulation-only evaluation and 2AFC-to-estimation conversion.  
- Important practical details missing: computational cost, MCMC samples/chains/diagnostics, annealing/temperatures, and KL integration methods.  
- Technical derivation and observer construction are sound, but empirical work needs more rigorous diagnostics and ablations.  
- Notation can be heavy/overloaded; paper would benefit from clearer algorithmic details, pseudocode, and an MCMC workflow schematic.  
- The contribution is original in packaging identifiability mapping for Bayesian perception models, though related to established ideas.  
- The framework is significant for experimental design and preventing over-interpretation of fitted Bayesian models.  
- Suggested additions: release code, report computational/MCMC details, compare to local metrics, run sensitivity/misspecification analyses, and explore stimulus optimization.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important problem, solid theory, and useful case-study findings.  
- Cons against acceptance: simulation-only, within-model evaluation, missing diagnostics and scalability info, and some overstated claims.  
- Overall: strong and timely contribution that would be compelling with added robustness checks, implementation details, and tempered claims.
