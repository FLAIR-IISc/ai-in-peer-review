- Addresses non-identifiability in Bayesian perception models and shows multiple parameter combinations can produce identical behavior.
- Proposes a novel a priori identifiability analysis framework using expected log-likelihood to evaluate parameter recoverability before experiments.
- Implements the framework in a general class of Bayesian observer models applicable to many psychophysical tasks.
- Demonstrates utility through two case studies: temporal interval estimation and speed perception.
- Mathematically rigorous: derivation of expected log-likelihood (Eq. 12) is well-justified and computationally considered.
- Provides efficient approximations and practical implementation details, with thoughtful discussion of limitations.
- Original contribution: first systematic framework for a priori identifiability analysis in Bayesian perception.
- Novel use of expected log-likelihood as a tractable proxy for identifiability.
- Significant methodological impact: can improve experimental design and interpretation of internal representations.
- Reveals non-intuitive experimental design effects (e.g., narrow loss can worsen prior recovery).
- Well-written and organized, with clear notation, helpful figures, and reproducible supplementary material.
- Concern about computational complexity and scalability for models with many parameters.
- Framework relies on specific parametric observer model assumptions, which may limit applicability to some tasks.
- Limited empirical validation: framework tested on case studies but not on real behavioral datasets.
- Overall evaluation is very positive (Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5, Originality 9, Significance 9) and recommends acceptance at NeurIPS despite minor weaknesses.
