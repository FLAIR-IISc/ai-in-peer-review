- Addresses non-identifiability of internal components (priors, likelihoods, loss) in Bayesian perception and presents the first systematic a priori identifiability framework.  
- Extends prior Bayesian perception models by providing a method to assess parameter recoverability before running experiments.  
- Develops a flexible, computationally efficient observer model suitable for a wide range of sensory estimation tasks.  
- Proposes expected log likelihood (Eq. 12) as a theoretically sound and practical metric to quantify identifiability.  
- Case study 1 (temporal interval estimation): shows designs that force narrower loss (more MAP-like) can worsen prior recovery — a non-intuitive finding.  
- Case study 2 (speed perception): validates robust prior recovery and provides theoretical justification aligning with prior empirical findings.  
- Paper is well-written with thorough mathematical derivations and sufficiently detailed, reproducible case studies.  
- Acknowledges key assumption that the reference model is correctly specified.  
- Major weakness: no empirical validation with real behavioral data; unknown performance under noise and model misspecification.  
- Underutilizes related identifiability literature in statistics and ML; needs better contextualization and comparison.  
- Scope limited to estimation tasks; does not demonstrate handling of discrete-choice paradigms or multi-cue scenarios where identifiability may differ.  
- Significance: offers a principled tool to evaluate identifiability pre-experiment, potentially improving experimental design and preventing misinterpretation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong technical contribution, recommended for acceptance (meets quality, clarity, originality, significance).  
- Suggested improvements: discuss framework performance with real/misspecified data and better situate work within broader identifiability literature.
