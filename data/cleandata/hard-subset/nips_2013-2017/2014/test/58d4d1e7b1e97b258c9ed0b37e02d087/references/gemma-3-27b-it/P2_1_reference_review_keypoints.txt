- Paper addresses non-identifiability of Bayesian perception models where different priors/likelihoods/losses yield identical behavior.
- Proposes an a priori identifiability framework using expected support for alternative models approximated via KL divergence.
- Introduces a flexible continuous class of Bayesian observer models with tractable parameterization (Eqs. 2,4,5,6).
- Demonstrates framework on two case studies: time perception and speed perception.
- Connects to prior work on Bayesian decision theory, process models, and concerns about inferring internal representations.
- Advances beyond prior acknowledgements of identifiability by offering a systematic analytical approach.
- Clear mathematical derivation linking identifiability to KL divergence (Eq. 12).
- Illustrative examples validate practical applicability and experimental design implications; speed perception case aligns with literature.
- Important and impactful problem that could improve rigor in Bayesian modeling of perception.
- Framework is novel and has potential for broad use by researchers designing experiments.
- Computational cost: exploring identifiability landscape (sampling Eq. 13) may be expensive for complex/high-dimensional models.
- Framework relies on assumptions (e.g., unimodal likelihoods, specific loss forms) whose sensitivity needs further study.
- Results depend strongly on the choice of reference observer (θ*); more exploration of θ* effects is needed.
- Limited experimental scope; counterintuitive finding (MAP loss worsening inference) requires further investigation across scenarios.
- Paper lacks sufficient MCMC implementation details (proposal distribution, burn-in, convergence), hurting reproducibility.
- Overall technically sound, well-written, and original with consistent notation and helpful figures.
- Reviewer views it as a strong paper and recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: address computational cost, test sensitivity to assumptions and θ*, and provide detailed MCMC implementation info.
