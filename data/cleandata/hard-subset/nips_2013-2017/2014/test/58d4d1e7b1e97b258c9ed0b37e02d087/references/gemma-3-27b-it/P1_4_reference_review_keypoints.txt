- Addresses non-identifiability of parameters in Bayesian perception models and shows different priors/likelihoods/losses can yield identical behavioral predictions.  
- Proposes a framework to assess identifiability a priori using a flexible parameterized observer model and KL divergence to quantify alternative explanations.  
- Demonstrates the framework on time and speed perception paradigms, yielding actionable experimental-design insights.  
- Builds on Bayesian decision theory literature and prior discussions of ill-posed inverse problems in cognitive modeling.  
- Generalizes and systematizes earlier attempts to constrain model shape and applies KL divergence in a novel, practical way.  
- Tackles an important, often-overlooked problem in Bayesian cognitive modeling.  
- Offers a novel, systematic, and quantitative framework for testing identifiability rather than only qualitative arguments.  
- Presents a flexible observer model that can capture many perceptual tasks with a compact parameterization.  
- Provides practical applications with concrete examples and insights for experimental design.  
- Paper is mostly clear and well-organized, with well-motivated mathematical derivations and helpful figures.  
- Emphasizes computational efficiency, enabling exploration of high-dimensional parameter spaces.  
- Relies on assumptions about parametric forms of sensory mapping and loss functions that need further discussion.  
- Use of KL divergence as a proxy for posterior probability is a simplification and has limitations with finite data.  
- Results depend on the chosen reference observer (θ*); sensitivity to that choice is not fully explored.  
- Case studies are illustrative but limited in scope; broader application to other perceptual domains would strengthen the work.  
- Lacks sufficient implementation details on the MCMC sampling procedure, convergence diagnostics, and sensitivity analysis.  
- For acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem, offers a novel and potentially impactful framework, includes useful case studies, and is clearly written.  
- Against acceptance: simplifying assumptions may limit generality, KL proxy is a simplification, and case-study scope is restricted.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution that merits acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revision — conduct sensitivity analysis on the choice of reference observer (θ*).  
- Suggested revision — expand discussion of assumptions, especially sensory mapping and loss function parametric forms.  
- Suggested revision — provide more MCMC implementation details, convergence diagnostics, and sensitivity checks.  
- Suggested revision — briefly discuss potential applications to other perceptual domains (e.g., visual illusions, multisensory integration).
