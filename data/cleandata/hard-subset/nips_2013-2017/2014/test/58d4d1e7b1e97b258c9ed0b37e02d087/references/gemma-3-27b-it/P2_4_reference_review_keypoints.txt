- Paper addresses non-identifiability in Bayesian perception models and proposes a framework to test identifiability a priori.
- Strength: Tackles a fundamental and consequential problem in Bayesian modeling of perception.
- Strength: Introduces a novel, systematic framework for assessing identifiability before data collection.
- Strength: Uses a flexible, parameterized observer model and maximum-entropy principles for likelihoods and losses.
- Strength: Demonstrates practical utility with case studies in time and speed perception, yielding useful experimental insights.
- Strength: Generally well-written with helpful figures, though mathematically dense.
- Weakness: Main text is very mathematically heavy and could be made more accessible with intuitive explanations.
- Weakness: Relies on assumptions (e.g., unimodal likelihoods, specific priors/losses) without thorough robustness checks to violations.
- Weakness: Exploring the full parameter space may be computationally expensive; MCMC details are lacking.
- Weakness: Case studies are limited in scope; speed perception example mostly validates prior work rather than revealing new problems.
- Weakness: Paper stays at the behavioral level and does not connect findings to neural implementation or constraints.
- Quality: Technically sound and rigorous; analysis is complete within stated scope. (Score: 8/10)
- Clarity: Logically structured but hindered by heavy notation and dense equations. (Score: 6/10)
- Originality: Novel a priori identifiability analysis and flexible observer model. (Score: 9/10)
- Significance: Potentially high impact on experimental design and reliability of Bayesian model inferences. (Score: 8/10)
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key issue, offers a novel framework, provides practical applications, and could significantly improve model fitting and interpretation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility due to mathematical density, assumption-driven generalizability concerns, and narrow case-study scope.
- Overall: Valuable contribution that warrants acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, discuss limitations, and mention neural implications.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (clarify math intuitively, expand discussion of assumptions/limitations, add brief neural modeling implications).
