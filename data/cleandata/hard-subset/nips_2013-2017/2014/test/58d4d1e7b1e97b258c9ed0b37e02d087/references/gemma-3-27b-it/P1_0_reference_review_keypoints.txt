- Addresses non-identifiability of parameters in Bayesian perceptual models, showing multiple model combinations can yield identical behavior.
- Proposes a novel framework for a priori testing of identifiability using a parameterized class of Bayesian observer models.
- Provides an efficient method for exploring parameter space to assess robustness of parameter recovery.
- Demonstrates framework on time and speed perception, highlighting experimental design pitfalls and improvements.
- Builds on prior Bayesian decision theory and identifiability/model-selection literature, adapting methods to psychophysical modeling.
- Flexible, comprehensive observer model using maximum entropy principles to cover many tasks and assumptions.
- Clear presentation with well-explained equations and illustrative figures referenced in text.
- Illustrative examples show impact of design choices (loss shape, motor noise) and practical utility.
- Claims computational efficiency, enabling exploration of complex model spaces.
- Relies on specific functional-form assumptions (priors, likelihoods, loss functions, Gaussian mixtures); sensitivity not fully explored.
- MCMC posterior approximation may be computationally expensive and scalability is insufficiently detailed.
- KL divergence used to quantify support is asymmetric; implications need further discussion.
- MCMC may not fully explore parameter space, risking missed low-support regions.
- Lacks empirical validation on real behavioral data to strengthen practical claims.
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: discuss sensitivity to model assumptions, detail computational demands and scaling, consider alternative support metrics, and outline plans for empirical validation.
