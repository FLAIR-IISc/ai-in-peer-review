- Proposes an efficient, general framework for a priori identifiability analysis of 1D Bayesian observer models in psychophysics.  
- Defines a flexible observer class: parametric sensory mapping, max-entropy mixture-of-Gaussians likelihood, lattice-mixture prior, mixture-Gaussian loss, lapses, and motor/report noise.  
- Derives an analytic expected log-evidence that reduces to a sum of KL divergences across stimuli.  
- Constructs an “approximate expected posterior” E(θ|θ*) ∝ exp(⟨log p(D|θ)⟩) and samples it via MCMC to map identifiability.  
- Demonstrates framework on interval timing and speed perception case studies exposing practical identifiability issues and design implications.  
- Situates work within Bayesian perception, model inversion, and identifiability/distinguishability literature; claims novelty in task-specific, tractable observer class.  
- Derivation of KL-sum is sound and modeling choices are reasonable, but E(θ|θ*) is not a true Bayesian posterior and this should be clarified.  
- Case studies are insightful but rely on assumptions that need sensitivity analyses.  
- Implementation details (mixture construction, MCMC configuration, diagnostics, runtime) are deferred to supplement and should be reported.  
- Some parameter constraints and constructions (unimodality, achievable skew/kurtosis with two Gaussians, prior simplex parameterization) need clearer specification.  
- The 2AFC-to-estimation transformation is briefly justified and requires stronger explanation or a discrete-choice example.  
- Novel contribution: unified expressive observer parameterization plus analytic expected log-likelihood for systematic identifiability/design comparison.  
- Framework is significant for improving interpretation of Bayesian model fits and guiding experimental design in psychophysics.  
- Strengths: clear identifiability criterion, flexible efficient model, insightful case studies, and relevance to debates about BDT interpretability.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: clarify E(θ|θ*) terminology, provide MCMC/runtime/convergence details, validate against other identifiability methods, expose max-entropy mixture construction, include discrete-choice example, link to Bayesian OED, and discuss robustness to model mismatch.  
- References and bibliographic details need completion.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, practical/theoretically grounded tool, actionable insights, and broad relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: under-specified technical choices, limited empirical evaluation against alternatives, questionable 2AFC mapping, and missing reproducibility details.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept conditional on clarifications, added methodological details, and improved reproducibility.
