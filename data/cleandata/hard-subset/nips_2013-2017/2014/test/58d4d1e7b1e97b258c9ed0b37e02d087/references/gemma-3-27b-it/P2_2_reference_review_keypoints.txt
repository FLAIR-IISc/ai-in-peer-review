- Summary: Proposes an a priori framework using a parameterized Bayesian observer model and KL divergence to assess identifiability of perceptual model parameters, demonstrated on time and speed perception tasks.

- Strength: Addresses the fundamental and common problem of non-identifiability in Bayesian perceptual modeling.

- Strength: Paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and mathematically sound.

- Strength: The observer model is general and flexible, covering many perceptual tasks and internal representations.

- Strength: Framework is practical for evaluating experimental designs before data collection and uses KL divergence sensibly.

- Strength: Demonstrated applications yield useful insights (e.g., risks of narrow loss functions).

- Weakness: Computational complexity concerns—MCMC exploration may be intensive for complex or high-dimensional models and isn’t fully addressed.

- Weakness: Relies on model assumptions (e.g., Gaussian noise, unimodal likelihoods) without thorough sensitivity analysis.

- Weakness: Limited validation—applications are mostly theoretical and lack rigorous testing on simulated or real datasets.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on KL divergence; no exploration of alternative similarity metrics.

- Weakness: Connections to established identifiability techniques (e.g., profile likelihood, Fisher information) could be more explicitly discussed.

- Qualitative evaluation: Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10; Originality 8/10; Significance 8/10.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, general, and useful framework that addresses a crucial issue and is well-presented.

- Arguments against acceptance: Practical applicability limited by computational demands, limited validation, and dependence on specific assumptions.

- Overall assessment: Significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational complexity, model assumptions, and validation.
