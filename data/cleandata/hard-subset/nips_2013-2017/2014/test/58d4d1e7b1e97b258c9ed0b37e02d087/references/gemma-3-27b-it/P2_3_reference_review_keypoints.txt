- Addresses non-identifiability in Bayesian perception models: multiple priors/likelihoods/losses can produce identical behavior.
- Proposes an a priori framework for testing identifiability to evaluate experimental designs before data collection.
- Framework components: a novel class of observer models, an MCMC-based method to explore the identifiability landscape, and applications to time and speed perception.
- Strength: tackles an important, often-overlooked limitation of Bayesian modeling in perception.
- Strength: novel, systematic alternative to ad-hoc constraints for assessing identifiability.
- Strength: flexible Bayesian observer model using analytical expressions where possible for efficiency and expressivity.
- Strength: illustrative applications reveal concrete insights (e.g., narrow loss functions harming identifiability in time perception).
- Strength: clear motivation, logical organization, and generally clear mathematical notation.
- Weakness: computational cost—MCMC exploration can be intensive and may not scale to high-dimensional or more complex models.
- Weakness: relies on simplifications (unimodal likelihoods, parametric priors/losses, independent response noise) limiting generality and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: limited empirical validation—primarily simulation with idealized observers, lacks tests on real psychophysical datasets.
- Weakness: use of KL-divergence to quantify support is imperfect (asymmetry, sensitivity to small differences).
- Weakness: dependence on specifying a “true” observer θ* is problematic since the true mechanism is unknown.
- Overall evaluation: technically sound, novel, and potentially impactful (quality/clarity/originality/significance all rated ~4/5).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; authors should address computational complexity, sensitivity to assumptions, and add empirical validation.
