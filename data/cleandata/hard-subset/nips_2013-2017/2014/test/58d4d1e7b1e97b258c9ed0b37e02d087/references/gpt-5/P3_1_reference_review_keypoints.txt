- Paper formalizes a flexible 1D Bayesian observer class (sensation, prior, decision under parametric loss, lapses, motor noise) and provides tools to assess a priori identifiability.
- Derives that expected support for alternative parameters reduces to a weighted sum of KL divergences between response distributions.
- Uses an “expected posterior” E(θ|θ*) ∝ exp(〈log L〉) to map degeneracies and compare experimental designs via MCMC sampling.
- Two case studies (interval timing, speed perception) yield practical design insights: motor-noise calibration improves identifiability; encouraging MAP-like loss can harm prior recovery; speed prior parameters are relatively robust.
- Situates work within Bayesian observer modeling and connects to model-mimicry literature, but does not fully integrate optimal experimental design (OED) literature.
- Strength: tackles an important, underappreciated identifiability problem with a practical pre-experiment tool.
- Strength: elegant reduction of expected log evidence to KL sums and an intuitive, tunable expected-posterior framework.
- Strength: efficient mixture-of-Gaussians observer parameterization enables exploration of high-dimensional θ.
- Strength: provides actionable experimental design recommendations relevant to cognitive modeling and OED communities.
- Weakness: core identifiability criterion (expected KL / expected log likelihood) is standard; novelty lies mainly in tailoring it to this observer class.
- Weakness: maximum-entropy claims for two-Gaussian mixtures under moment constraints are questionable and need justification/references.
- Weakness: limited robustness checks for model misspecification (e.g., skewed/heavy-tailed noise, nonstationarity, priors not well captured by fixed lattice).
- Weakness: lacks formal invariance/gauge fixing analysis (e.g., local sensitivity/Hessian or profile-likelihood) to isolate near-symmetries between prior, likelihood, and loss.
- Weakness: 2AFC-to-estimation conversion used in speed example is not fully justified; a direct 2AFC variant or sensitivity analysis is needed.
- Clarity issue: missing implementation details (MCMC proposals, priors on θ, convergence diagnostics, runtime, numerical integration specifics).
- Clarity issue: notation inconsistencies and typos; figures should directly illustrate claimed degeneracies.
- Clarity issue: rescaling of internal space f(s) to [−1,1] requires discussion of comparability of σ and σℓ across designs.
- Originality: end-to-end pipeline is useful, but paper should highlight distinctive aspects beyond using expected KL (e.g., joint treatment of prior/likelihood/loss/lapses/motor noise).
- Significance: design insights are actionable and likely impactful; public code/templates would increase utility.
- Concrete suggestions: incorporate formal design optimization (maximize expected KL or mutual information), report parameter-wise identifiability metrics (local curvature/profile likelihoods), examine lapse–prior confounds, test multi-condition transfer constraints, and consider hierarchical observer extensions.
- Pros summary: fills methodological gap with a computational framework, provides experiment-relevant insights, and generally clear exposition.
- Cons summary: identifiability metric not novel, heavy reliance on specific parametric choices, incomplete justification for 2AFC mapping, and clarity/notation issues.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on stronger positioning relative to OED literature, clearer justification of distributional choices, and additional robustness analyses.
