- Presents a Bayesian model of perception with explicit equations for sensory noise, likelihood, prior, loss, and response distribution.  
- Model combines robustness (e.g., stimulus-dependent noise) with mathematical convenience (tractable inference).  
- Emphasizes that the model is mathematically identifiable.  
- Offers a mathematically elegant, general framework for formalizing perceptual estimation.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance to NIPS.  
- Main concern: contribution is incremental and closely resembles recent work (e.g., Acerbi et al. 2012, 2014).  
- Recommendation: include a new section reviewing recent Bayesian perceptual models and clearly state how this work advances them.  
- Caveat: identifiability claims hinge on model assumptions and do not resolve practical distinctions between sensory, decision, and response noise.  
- Request: discuss this practical identifiability limitation in the revised manuscript.
