- Paper proposes a framework for Bayesian model comparison on behavioral data: summarizes Bayesian Decision Theory, identifies non-identifiability, prescribes constraints, makes assumptions, tests parameter differentiability, and applies to time interval estimation and motion perception studies.  
- Identifiability of internal representations/processes is a longstanding problem in cognitive science (e.g., Anderson 1978).  
- Recent critiques have highlighted identifiability as a major drawback limiting Bayesian frameworks’ adoption (Jones & Love 2011; Bowers & Davis 2012).  
- Overall assessment: well written and tackles an important theoretical issue.  
- Major concern 1: The paper largely ignores substantial prior literature on model comparison in cognitive science, making its novel contribution unclear (e.g., Pitt et al. 2002; Navarro et al. 2004).  
- Major concern 2: Key modeling assumptions appear arbitrary or insufficiently justified (e.g., definition of p_meas in Eq.3 and parameter choices in Eq.12); unclear how dependent results are on these assumptions.  
- Recommendation: The authors should discuss and justify the chosen constraints/assumptions that make Bayesian models identifiable.  
- Minor comments: explain the “trivial mathematical fact” at Line 53; change second “nor” to “or” at Line 73; clarify what the star and dashed lines denote in Figure 2.  
- Summary judgment: A well-written, important paper but missing links to previous related work.
