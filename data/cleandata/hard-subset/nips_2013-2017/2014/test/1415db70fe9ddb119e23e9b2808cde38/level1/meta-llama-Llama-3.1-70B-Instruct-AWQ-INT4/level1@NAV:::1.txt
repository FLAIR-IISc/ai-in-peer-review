**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper introduces a novel approach to natural language processing called Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs). The authors aim to address the limitation of traditional recursive neural networks (RNNs) in capturing contextual information in sentence-level sentiment analysis. GB-RNNs incorporate a feedbackward step during inference, allowing phrase-level predictions and representations to give feedback to words. The model is evaluated on the task of contextual sentiment analysis using the SemEval 2013 dataset and achieves state-of-the-art performance.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new approach to incorporating contextual information in RNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **State-of-the-art performance**: The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 dataset, outperforming other baselines and model ablations.
3. **Well-motivated**: The authors provide a clear motivation for the need to incorporate contextual information in sentence-level sentiment analysis and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach.
4. **Well-written**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, making it easy to follow.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The model is complex, and the authors could provide more insight into the computational cost and scalability of the approach.
2. **Limited evaluation**: The evaluation is limited to a single dataset, and it would be beneficial to see the model evaluated on other datasets to demonstrate its generalizability.
3. **Comparison to other models**: While the authors compare their model to other baselines and model ablations, a more detailed comparison to other state-of-the-art models in the field would be beneficial.
