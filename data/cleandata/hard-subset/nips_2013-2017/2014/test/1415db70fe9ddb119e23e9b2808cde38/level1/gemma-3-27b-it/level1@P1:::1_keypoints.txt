- Proposes GB-RNNs with a feedbackward pass so phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations for context-dependent sentiment.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Twitter sentiment and can predict sentiment toward specific entities.  
- Investigates dropout and a hybrid unsupervised–supervised word vector approach.  
- Builds on compositional semantics and RNN literature; compares to bidirectional RNNs and Irsoy & Cardie (2014); related to attention ideas.  
- Novelty: feedbackward mechanism offers a different way to incorporate global context beyond bidirectional models.  
- Strong empirical performance demonstrated on a challenging benchmark.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies analyze contributions of components like dropout and hybrid vectors.  
- Paper has clear motivation and detailed model description with equations and illustrative figures.  
- Weak theoretical distinction from Irsoy & Cardie (2014); Eq. 4 vs. Eq. 5 justification feels contrived.  
- Added model complexity from the feedbackward pass lacks analysis of computational cost and scalability.  
- Reliance on a parser creates potential error sources and limits applicability across languages/domains.  
- Experiments are mainly on Twitter data, leaving generalizability to other domains/tasks unclear.  
- Hyperparameter tuning procedures are not described in enough detail for reproducibility.  
- For acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong results, thorough ablations, and clear writing.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient theoretical separation from prior work, missing computational cost analysis, and limited demonstrated generalizability.  
- Overall: reviewer leans “strong accept” but urges addressing comparison, complexity, and generalizability concerns.
