- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) that add a feedbackward pass so phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations.  
- Demonstrates improved performance on SemEval 2013 and claims state-of-the-art results, highlighting benefits of dropout and a hybrid word-vector approach.  
- Situates work well within compositional semantics and RNN literature, referencing bidirectional and window-based methods.  
- Motivation is clear: address context-dependence limitations of purely feedforward compositional models, relevant to aspect-specific sentiment.  
- Technical approach is sound with clear forward/backward computation and use of backpropagation-through-structure.  
- Experimental setup is appropriate (development set, baselines), but ablation studies are insufficient—impact of feedbackward dimension (nd) needs deeper analysis.  
- Claim of state-of-the-art is supported but would benefit from comparisons to more recent methods beyond SemEval winners.  
- Paper acknowledges increased parameter count of GB-RNN and justifies hybrid word vectors and dropout usage.  
- Generally well-written with helpful figures and clear equations, though some notation is cumbersome and certain comparisons (e.g., Irsoy & Cardie) are overly dense.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing details on parsing specifics and hyperparameter search may hinder exact replication.  
- Originality: feedbackward pass is a novel extension distinct from bidirectional RNNs; hybrid vectors are sensible though not novel.  
- Significance: substantial improvements for contextual sentiment and potential applicability to tasks like WSD and relation extraction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical gains, clear motivation, practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete ablation, limited comparison to recent SOTA, overly detailed sections, and some reproducibility gaps.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add recent related work and expand ablation details.
