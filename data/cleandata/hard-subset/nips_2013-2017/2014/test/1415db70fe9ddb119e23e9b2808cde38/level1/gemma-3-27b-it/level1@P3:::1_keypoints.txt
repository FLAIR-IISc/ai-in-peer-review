- Introduces GB-RNNs with a feedbackward pass allowing phrase-level predictions to influence word-level interpretations.  
- Core idea logically extends RNNs to let context refine initial predictions, addressing feedforward limitations in aspect-specific sentiment.  
- Demonstrates improvements over standard RNNs and achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.  
- Well-motivated introduction with a clear example illustrating the need for broader sentence context.  
- Related-work coverage is solid, with appropriate comparisons to recursive nets, bidirectional RNNs, and Irsoy & Cardie (2013).  
- Experimental setup is sound: standard dataset, comparisons to baselines and winners, and useful ablation studies (hybrid vectors, feedback pass).  
- Theoretical description is clear but the specific backward-pass implementation (Eq. 3) lacks strong justification and seems arbitrary.  
- Recommend deeper theoretical exploration of alternative feedback mechanisms and links to belief propagation/message passing.  
- Discussion of the W↓ parameter matrix is cursory and needs more detail.  
- Empirical results would benefit from more detailed error analysis and visualizations showing how feedback alters word representations.  
- No analysis of sensitivity to parser outputs, despite parsing errors likely affecting tree structure and performance.  
- Hyperparameter tuning details beyond mentioning AdaGrad and cross-validation are insufficient.  
- Claim that the model predicts entity-level sentiment is overstated; it predicts phrase-level sentiment and lacks entity recognition/coreference mechanisms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective extension to recursive nets with significant empirical gains and clear motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical grounding for the chosen implementation, limited error analysis, and overstated entity-sentiment claims.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen backward-pass justification, provide deeper error analysis/visualizations, and temper entity-level claims.
