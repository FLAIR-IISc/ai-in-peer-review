- Introduces GB-RNN, augmenting standard feedforward RNNs with a top-down "feedbackward" pass to propagate global sentence context down the parse tree.  
- Core idea: use a global belief from the whole sentence to refine word and phrase representations and predictions.  
- Contrasts method with prior RNN work (Socher et al.) and concurrent bidirectional recursive models (Irsoy & Cardie).  
- Also demonstrates effective use of dropout for RNNs and a hybrid word vector combining unsupervised and supervised features.  
- Significance: tackles a key compositional semantics problem—context-dependent meaning—using an elegant and intuitive solution.  
- Empirical quality: shows a 3% F1 improvement over a standard RNN and achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.  
- Thorough evaluation: includes ablation studies on word vectors and comparisons to multiple baselines.  
- Originality: feedbackward formulation is novel and differentiated theoretically and empirically from Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Clarity: paper is very well-written, with clear motivation, equations, and diagrams.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper qualitative or error analysis explaining why the feedbackward formulation (Eq. 5) outperforms Irsoy & Cardie (Eq. 4).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, SOTA results, technical soundness, clear presentation, and generalizability to other NLP tasks.  
- Argument against acceptance: contribution could be seen as an incremental extension of existing RNN models.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with clear contribution and high impact; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
