- Proposes GB-RNN: a tree-structured model with bottom-up forward pass and a single top-down “feedbackward” pass, concatenating inside/outside vectors for node-level classification.  
- Adds hybrid word embeddings (fixed unsupervised + small supervised tunable) and applies dropout to RNN training.  
- Conceptually close to inside-outside and bidirectional recursive models; backward pass conditions on both forward and backward parent vectors and uses a V transform to allow dimension mismatch.  
- Methodology: architecture is well-motivated, differentiable, and uses sensible regularization choices.  
- Theory: largely empirical; lacks formal analysis of expressivity, convergence, or justification that one backward sweep suffices; “belief” terminology is misleading (not probabilistic).  
- Experiments: GB-RNN improves ~3–5 F1 over a feedforward RNN and is competitive on SemEval 2013; ablations attribute gains to backward pass, hybrid embeddings, dropout, and external data.  
- Dataset clarity: unclear which SemEval 2013 subtask(s) were evaluated; needs official metrics per subtask and domain (Tweets vs SMS).  
- Parser constraint: forcing labeled phrases to be subtrees may bias results and leak span information; baseline without this constraint and frequency of non-constituent spans needed.  
- Parser robustness: Twitter parsing errors are a major confound; evaluate robustness to parser errors and compare to sequence models (biLSTM) that do not require trees.  
- Baseline comparisons: missing quantitative head-to-head results against bidirectional recursive models under identical settings.  
- External resources: use of NRC lexicon and large weakly labeled data blurs architecture vs. data contributions; report GB-RNN vs RNN without external resources.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear with helpful equations and example, but has notational ambiguities, typos, and underspecified supervision details (which nodes are labeled/ignored).  
- Reproducibility: should report full hyperparameters, runtime/complexity, and parameter counts.  
- Originality: incremental relative to contemporaneous inside-outside/bidirectional work; backward parameterization and dimensional decoupling are useful but not majorly novel.  
- Significance: important for target-dependent sentiment and other contextual labeling tasks, but dependence on parses for tweets may limit practical impact without strong sequence comparisons.  
- Pros: clear architecture injecting global context, solid empirical gains, effective hybrid embeddings/dropout, and thorough model-level relation to related work.  
- Cons: incremental novelty, limited theoretical insight, potential inflation from parsing constraints, reliance on external data, missing strong baselines, and limited complexity discussion.  
- Suggestion: clarify evaluated SemEval subtasks and report official metrics with confidence intervals.  
- Suggestion: report performance when target spans are not forced to be constituents and quantify how often that occurs.  
- Suggestion: add sequence baselines (biRNN/biLSTM with target attention) and structured baselines (tree-CRF/structured SVM).  
- Suggestion: provide direct quantitative comparison to Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional recursive model using identical embeddings/data.  
- Suggestion: include error analysis showing examples where global context flips local predictions to substantiate the mechanism.  
- Suggestion: report runtime, parameter counts, and test whether multiple backward passes add value over a single pass.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, well-executed empirical paper with incremental novelty and some experimental gaps; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending clarifications and stronger baselines.
