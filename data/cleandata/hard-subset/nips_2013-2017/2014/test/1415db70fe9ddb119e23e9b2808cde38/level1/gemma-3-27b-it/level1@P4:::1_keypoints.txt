- Introduces GB-RNNs that add a feedbackward pass during inference to let phrases update predictions using global sentence context.  
- Shows state-of-the-art improvements on a Twitter sentiment dataset and SemEval 2013.  
- Novel contribution: feedbackward formulation is distinct from prior bidirectional/recursive models.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation including baselines, ablation studies, dropout, and hybrid word-vector experiments.  
- Paper is generally well-structured and equations are clearly presented.  
- Clarity issue: motivation and theoretical justification for the chosen backward-pass formulation (Eq.3 vs Eq.4) is underexplained.  
- Parameter/complexity concern: feedbackward vectors increase parameters, with insufficient analysis of computational/parameter efficiency.  
- Dataset dependence: strong results shown mainly on Twitter; generalizability to other NLP tasks is not demonstrated.  
- Limited qualitative error analysis; lacks examples illustrating when GB-RNN succeeds or fails.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 2 is cluttered and could be clearer.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical support and thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical clarity on backward pass, limited generalizability evidence, and sparse error analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify the backward-pass rationale and discuss limitations.
