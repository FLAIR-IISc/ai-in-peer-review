- Main idea: Proposes Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) that add a feedbackward step to incorporate phrase-level context into word representations for contextual sentiment.  
- Hybrid word vectors: Introduces a combined unsupervised+supervised approach for word embeddings.  
- Evaluation: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 contextual sentiment challenge.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on recursive NNs and draws inspiration from bidirectional RNNs and window methods, claiming better long-range context capture.  
- Technical quality: Methodologically sound with theoretical analysis and thorough experimental support.  
- Reproducibility/clarity: Clearly written with consistent notation, well-designed figures/tables, and sufficient detail for replication.  
- Originality: Novel feedbackward mechanism for RNNs and a new hybrid embedding approach.  
- Significance: Demonstrates substantial improvement and potential applicability to other NLP tasks (e.g., word-sense disambiguation).  
- Pros: Novel model, strong empirical gains, and well-presented experiments.  
- Cons: Needs more experiments on diverse datasets/tasks and deeper interpretability analysis of the feedbackward step.  
- Ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8.5/10, Significance 9/10; overall recommendation: accept.
