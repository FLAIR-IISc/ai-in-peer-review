- Introduces GB-RNNs that add a feedbackward inference step to update phrase-level predictions using global sentence context.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on contextual sentiment analysis (SemEval 2013) with a significant F1 improvement over baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and a detailed description of model architecture and training.  
- Experiments are thorough, comparing against traditional RNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and other SemEval 2013 models.  
- Uses hybrid word vector representations (unsupervised + supervised) and shows they outperform either type alone.  
- Demonstrates that applying dropout improves model performance.  
- Strength: novel and effective architecture tailored for contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation and baseline comparisons.  
- Strength: clear and coherent presentation.  
- Weakness: model architecture is complex and lacks sufficient intuition for why the feedbackward step works.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of performance across different sentence/phrase types.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong empirical gains and novelty, but should add more intuition and targeted performance analysis.
