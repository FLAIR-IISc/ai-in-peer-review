- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) to incorporate global sentence context into phrase/word label prediction.  
- Proposes a novel feedbackward inference step that updates phrase representations using entire sentence structure.  
- Extends prior compositional and bidirectional recursive models while avoiding fixed-context window methods.  
- Combines hybrid unsupervised-supervised word vectors and dropout for improved representations.  
- Reports a 3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs on SemEval 2013, outperforming competition winners and baselines.  
- Methodology is technically sound with thorough ablation studies and rigorous experimental validation.  
- Hybrid word vector approach and dropout usage are well-justified.  
- Feedbackward mechanism is original and distinct from prior bidirectional approaches.  
- Problem of context-dependent sentiment disambiguation is well-motivated and significant.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results with clear implications for social media analysis and broader NLP tasks.  
- Comparison to Irsoy & Cardie (2014) is overly technical and needs a concise summary for accessibility.  
- Computational cost and parameter increase are not analyzed, raising deployment concerns.  
- Lacks error analysis and discussion of failure cases to strengthen evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept—high-quality, original contribution with minor clarity and evaluation gaps.
