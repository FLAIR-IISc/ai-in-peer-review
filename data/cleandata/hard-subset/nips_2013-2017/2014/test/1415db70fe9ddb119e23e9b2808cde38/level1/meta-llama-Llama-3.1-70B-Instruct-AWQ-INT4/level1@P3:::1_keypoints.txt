- Proposes GB-RNN: a novel recursive neural network with a feedbackward (top-down) step to incorporate global sentence context for contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Motivation: argues feedforward RNNs fail to capture context-dependent sentiment.  
- Model detail: uses a top-down recursive function to update node vectors based on global context.  
- Presentation: paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and well-structured (intro, related work, methodology, evaluation).  
- Evaluation: extensive experiments on SemEval 2013 Task 2 showing GB-RNN outperforms standard RNN, bidirectional RNN, and logistic regression in F1.  
- Strength: clear description of architecture and training procedure.  
- Limitation: lacks thorough analysis of performance on different sentence types (e.g., multiple entities, complex structures).  
- Limitation: insufficient analysis and reporting of model parameters and hyperparameters.  
- Limitation: does not clearly explain the intuition for why the feedbackward step improves contextual sentiment capture.  
- Limitation: unclear how GB-RNN differs from or builds on prior recursive neural network work.  
- Significance: presents a meaningful, potentially broadly applicable contribution to NLP (sentiment analysis, opinion mining, text classification).  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to add detailed performance analyses, clearer intuition for the model, hyperparameter analysis, and explicit comparison to prior work.
