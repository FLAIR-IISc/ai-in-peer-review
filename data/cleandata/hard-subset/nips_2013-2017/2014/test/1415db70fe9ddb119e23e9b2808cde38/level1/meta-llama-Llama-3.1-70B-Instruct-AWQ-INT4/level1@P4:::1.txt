This paper introduces a novel extension to recursive neural networks (RNNs), called global belief recursive neural networks (GB-RNNs), which incorporate a feedbackward step during inference to capture contextual information. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this model on the task of contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a thorough introduction to the problem of contextual sentiment analysis and the limitations of existing RNNs. The authors provide a detailed explanation of their proposed GB-RNN model, including its architecture and training procedure. The experimental results are also well-presented, with a thorough comparison to several baseline models and an analysis of the impact of different hyperparameters.

The technical soundness of the paper is strong, with a clear and well-motivated extension to existing RNNs. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the differences between their model and existing bidirectional RNNs, and demonstrate the advantages of their approach through experiments. The use of hybrid word vector representations, which combine unsupervised and supervised learning, is also a novel contribution.

The originality of the paper is high, as it introduces a new architecture for RNNs that addresses a specific limitation of existing models. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a challenging task, and provide a thorough analysis of the results.

The significance of the paper is also high, as it has the potential to impact a wide range of natural language processing tasks that require contextual understanding. The authors demonstrate the applicability of their approach to sentiment analysis, but it is likely that the GB-RNN architecture could be applied to other tasks, such as question answering or text classification.
