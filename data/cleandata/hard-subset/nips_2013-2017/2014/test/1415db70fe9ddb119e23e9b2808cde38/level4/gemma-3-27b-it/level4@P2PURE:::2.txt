In natural language processing, recursive neural networks (RNNs) generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining word or shorter snippet representations, starting from individual words and progressing up a pre-defined parse tree to obtain a representation for the entire snippet at the root. However, this unidirectional process prevents information flow from larger to smaller contexts. This paper presents a bidirectional extension to RNNs, incorporating a downward pass following the standard upward pass, thereby enriching node representations with information from broader contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.

The proposed extension to the established RNN approach is simple, elegant, and effective. While the core concept shares similarities with that of [18], the computation performed during the downward pass differs, and the presented results demonstrate superior performance. 

The method is well-motivated and clearly explained, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are strong, though I have some questions regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, was dropout and the additional training data used when reporting results for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] in Table 2? Were hybrid vectors also employed in that configuration? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, its inclusion in a table comparing individual model performance seems inappropriate.

Finally, Section 4.4 refers to a "widely used mix of both" – could this be clarified? Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
