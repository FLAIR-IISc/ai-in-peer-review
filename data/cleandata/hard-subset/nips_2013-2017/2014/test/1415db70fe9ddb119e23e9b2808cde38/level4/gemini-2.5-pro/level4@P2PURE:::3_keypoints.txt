- Proposes sentiment classifier using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder.  
- Labels approach “belief propagation,” which is misleading and likely to confuse readers.  
- Paper is generally well-written but motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.  
- Primary weakness: experimental evaluation is weak and unclear.  
- Reporting an ensemble result is unjustified because benchmark models are not ensembles, obscuring true performance.  
- Experimental setup lacks details on how proposed models were tuned and how best single model and ensemble were selected.  
- Unclear how benchmark models in Table 2 were tuned.  
- Unclear whether benchmarks were trained on the same augmented data as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear whether baseline RNN and BiRNN used the hybrid word vectors.  
- Most appropriate comparison appears to be the second entry in Table 3 against the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 given missing details.  
- Minor: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser for noisy tweets — parser accuracy on that domain is unknown.  
- Minor: several citations are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).  
- Conclusion: results seem reasonable but contribution is undermined by weak motivation and unclear experimental methodology.
