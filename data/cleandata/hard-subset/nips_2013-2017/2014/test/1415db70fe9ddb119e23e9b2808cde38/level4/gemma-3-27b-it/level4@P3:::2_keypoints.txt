- Introduces GB-RNN with a feedbackward pass that lets phrase-level predictions refine word-level representations to capture context-dependent meaning.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2 and strong entity-directed sentiment prediction.  
- Ablation studies show dropout and hybrid unsupervised/supervised word vectors materially contribute to performance.  
- Conceptually simple, well-motivated extension to standard RNNs addressing feedforward limitations.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful diagrams (e.g., Fig. 2) aiding understanding.  
- Thorough and well-positioned related work discussion linking to bidirectional and compositional semantics literature.  
- Practical contributions (dropout findings, hybrid vectors) likely useful beyond this paper.  
- Comparison to Irsoy & Cardie [12] lacks experimental detail (dropout, hybrid vectors, extra training data), weakening direct attribution of improvements.  
- Presenting ensemble results alongside single-model comparisons (Table 1) is misleading and should be separated.  
- Some phrasing is ambiguous (e.g., "widely used mix of both" in Sec. 4.4) and needs clarification.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor issues; makes a valuable NLP contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
