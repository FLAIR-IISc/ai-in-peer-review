- Proposes using encoding and decoding phrase representations from an RNN auto-encoder for sentiment classification, labeled “belief propagation,” but this terminology is misleading (not actual belief propagation).  
- Paper is clearly written but the model’s motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.  
- Primary weakness: evaluation methodology—reporting ensemble results is unwarranted and obscures the proposed approach since baselines are not ensembles.  
- Model/comparison details are insufficiently specified; unclear how individual models were tuned and how ensemble/“best” models were selected.  
- Unclear how the benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned.  
- Unclear whether baselines in Table 2 used the same data augmentation as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear whether the RNN and BiRNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.  
- Given the missing details, the second entry in Table 3 is likely the fairest comparison to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 for assessing BP-RNN.  
- Minor: an off-the-shelf parser was used on tweets—report its accuracy on this domain.  
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Overall: presents a recursive NN for phrasal sentiment in context with reasonable results, but weak motivation and unclear experimental methodology prevent firm conclusions.
