In Natural Language Processing (NLP), recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been employed to produce representations of snippets of text by recursively combining pairs of representations of words or shorter snippets. This process commences with the representation of words and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree until the representation of the entire snippet is obtained at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process does not allow information to be propagated down the tree from larger to smaller contexts. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs, wherein the upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, augmenting the representations of all nodes with information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The extension of the established RNN-based approach proposed in the paper is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea bears some resemblance to the one presented in [18], the computation performed on the downward pass in the paper differs, and the resulting performance appears to be superior.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is well-written in general. The experimental results appear to be excellent; however, I have some concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, I would like to know whether the result for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2 was obtained using dropout and the additional training data. Additionally, were the hybrid vectors also employed in that case? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not really belong in a table comparing the performance of individual models.

I would appreciate clarification on the phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4. Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
