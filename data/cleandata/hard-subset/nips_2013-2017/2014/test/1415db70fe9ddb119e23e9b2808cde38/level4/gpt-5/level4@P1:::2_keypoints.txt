- Problem: Standard recursive neural nets are purely bottom-up, preventing larger-context information from influencing lower nodes.  
- Proposal: Introduce a bidirectional recursive model with a top-down downward pass that propagates global context from root to leaves.  
- Representation: Augments each node by concatenating upward and downward vectors to enable context-dependent labeling (e.g., aspect-specific sentiment).  
- Empirical result: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval Task 2 and outperforms standard RNN baselines.  
- Additional findings: Reports gains from dropout and from hybrid word vectors (concatenating fixed unsupervised and smaller supervised embeddings).  
- Relation to prior work: Conceptually related to bidirectional recurrent/recursive models; differs from [18] in the top-down computation that combines forward and backward parent information.  
- Strength: Clear motivation, simple and elegant model extension, and strong empirical results with clear presentation.  
- Concern — baseline fairness: Unclear whether the competing bidirectional model [18] was trained with the same enhancements (dropout, extra data, hybrid vectors).  
- Concern — ensemble reporting: Ensemble result presented alongside single-model comparisons, which may be misleading for fair comparison.  
- Concern — wording clarity: Ambiguous phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 needs clarification.  
- Qualitative: Paper is technically sound, well-written, and complete; claims are supported by experiments.  
- Originality: Top-down idea not entirely new, but the specific downward computation and empirical effectiveness are novel.  
- Significance: Likely impactful for compositional semantics and aspect-level sentiment due to simplicity and strong results.  
- Overall assessment: Well-executed and compelling work; main reservation is ensuring fair comparisons to the bidirectional baseline and clarifying ensemble reporting and minor phrasing.
