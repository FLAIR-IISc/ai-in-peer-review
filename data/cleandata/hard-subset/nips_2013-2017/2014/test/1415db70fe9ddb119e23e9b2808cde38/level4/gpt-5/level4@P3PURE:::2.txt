This paper extends recursive neural networks (RNNs) for NLP by introducing a bidirectional variant that augments node representations with information from larger contexts. Standard RNNs compute representations by recursively combining pairs of word or phrase representations along a given parse tree, proceeding bottom-up to the root. Because this process is unidirectional, information cannot flow from larger contexts back down to smaller constituents. The proposed approach remedies this by performing a downward pass after the upward pass, propagating contextual information from parent nodes to children. The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The extension is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea resembles that of [18], the computation used in the downward pass here is different, and the resulting performance appears to be superior.

The method is well motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well written. The experimental results appear excellent, but I have concerns about the experimental setup:
- For the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2, were dropout and the additional training data also used?
- Were hybrid vectors used for that comparison as well?
- While the ensemble result is impressive, it does not belong in a table comparing the performance of individual models.

Additionally, in Section 4.4, what does the “widely used mix of both” refer to?

Overall, this is a well-executed paper built on a simple and elegant idea.
