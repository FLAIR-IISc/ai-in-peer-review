- Introduces GB-RNNs: adds a feedbackward inference step so phrase-level predictions influence word representations for context-dependent sentiment.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Twitter sentiment task, outperforming standard RNNs and prior winners.  
- Builds on compositional distributional semantics and recursive neural network literature, differentiating from bidirectional RNNs by claiming better long-range dependency modeling.  
- Novelty: the feedbackward/global belief propagation mechanism is a new contribution for incorporating global context.  
- Strong empirical evaluation: explores dropout and hybrid unsupervised/supervised word vectors and reports clear performance gains.  
- Paper is well-written with a detailed model description, mathematical formulation, training procedure, and helpful figures.  
- Motivation/theory: theoretical justification for the “global belief” mechanism and its link to belief propagation is weak and terminology may be misleading.  
- Experimental clarity: ensemble composition, tuning/selection process, and whether data augmentation was used are not clearly described, hindering reproducibility and comparison.  
- Baseline tuning: insufficient details on hyperparameter tuning and whether baselines used the same hybrid word vectors or optimization as GB-RNN.  
- Parser reliance: uses an off-the-shelf tweet parser without reporting its accuracy on the dataset, raising robustness concerns.  
- Minor citation issues: some references are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy & Cardie), limiting access to related work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem with a novel, technically sound approach, strong empirical gains, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear experimental methodology, weak theoretical motivation for the core mechanism, and unassessed parser dependency.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify ensemble composition, full tuning details for all models, and parser accuracy on the Twitter dataset.
