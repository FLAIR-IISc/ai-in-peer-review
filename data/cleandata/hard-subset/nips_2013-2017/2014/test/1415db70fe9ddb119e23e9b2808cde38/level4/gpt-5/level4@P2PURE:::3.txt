This paper proposes using both the encoder and decoder phrase vectors from a recursive neural network autoencoder for sentiment classification. The authors refer to this as “belief propagation,” but the method is not an instance of that inference algorithm; the term is therefore misleading and likely to confuse readers. Overall, the paper is well written, but the motivation for the proposed model is vague and lacks a clear theoretical justification.

The principal weakness is the evaluation. Reporting an ensemble result is unwarranted in this context: none of the benchmarks are ensembles, and including an ensemble obscures the true comparison. Moreover, the exact composition of the compared systems is unclear. Key questions that need clarification include:
- How were the models tuned, and how were the ensemble and best models selected?
- How were the benchmarks in Table 2 tuned?
- Did the benchmarks use the same augmented data as the BP-RNN?
- Did the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN also use hybrid word vectors?

In the absence of this information, I would assume that the second entry in Table 3 is the most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 when evaluating the BP-RNN architecture.

Minor points:
- I am surprised that an off-the-shelf parser was used to parse tweets; please report its accuracy on this data.
- The Irsoy and Cardie citation is incomplete (as are several others).

In summary, the paper presents a recursive neural network model for classifying phrasal sentiment in context. The results appear reasonable, but the model lacks strong motivation, and the experimental methodology is not entirely clear.
