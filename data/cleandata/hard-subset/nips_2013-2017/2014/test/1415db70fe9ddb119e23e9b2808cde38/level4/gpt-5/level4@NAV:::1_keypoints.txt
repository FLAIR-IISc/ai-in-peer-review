- Proposes "belief propagation" recursive neural networks that add a top-down pass so phrase-level predictions/embeddings feed back into word-level embeddings and labels.
- Paper is clearly written and the model is well motivated.
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008); citation appears incomplete.
- Main novelty seems to be the use of hybrid word vectors—clarify if this is the primary distinction from prior work.
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair: clarify whether RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors.
- Clarify model tuning and selection: how were "best" models chosen and was a single best model selection procedure used?
- Baselines do not use ensembles—state whether authors used ensembles and account for this in comparisons.
- The term "belief propagation" is potentially misleading; "forward-backward" might be more appropriate.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; provide more detail on backpropagation through structure (unrolling the recursive computation) and cite Goller (1996).
- Section 3.6 is confusing; make the distinction from Irsoy & Cardie clearer.
- Section 4.1 should be improved for non-NLP readers; explicitly state which SemEval 2013 Task 2 subtask is addressed (contextual vs message polarity) and clarify parser constraints.
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper presenting an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model.
