In natural language processing, recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been used to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining representations of words or shorter snippets. This process begins with word representations and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree, culminating in a representation of the entire snippet at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process prevents information from propagating down the tree, from larger to smaller contexts. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs, where an upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, enriching the representations of all nodes with information from broader contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The extension to the established RNN-based approach proposed in this paper is simple, elegant, and effective. While the core idea shares similarities with that of [18], the computation performed during the downward pass differs, and the resulting performance appears to be superior. 

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are strong, though I have a few concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, was the result for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2 obtained using dropout and the additional training data? Were hybrid vectors also used in that case? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, it seems inappropriate to include it in a table comparing the performance of individual models.

Finally, could the authors clarify what “widely used mix of both” refers to in Section 4.4? Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
