- Recursive neural nets build phrase representations bottom-up but cannot propagate larger-context information downward.  
- Paper proposes a bidirectional RNN: an upward pass followed by a novel downward pass to augment node representations with global context.  
- The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- The extension is simple, elegant, and effective; similar in spirit to [18] but with novel downward computation and apparently superior performance.  
- The method is well-motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is well written.  
- Concern: unclear whether the [18] baseline reported in Table 2 used dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors (potential fairness issue).  
- Presentation issue: the ensemble result is impressive but inappropriately included in a table comparing single models.  
- Clarification needed: what does the phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 refer to?  
- Overall: a well-executed paper grounded on a simple, elegant idea.
