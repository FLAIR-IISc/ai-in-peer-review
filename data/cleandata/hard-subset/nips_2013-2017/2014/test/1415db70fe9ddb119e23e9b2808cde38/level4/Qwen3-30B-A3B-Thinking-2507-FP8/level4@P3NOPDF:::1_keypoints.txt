- Proposes BPRNNs with a backward (top-down) inference step; core contribution is hybrid word vectors and phrase-level refinement, but citation to Irsoy & Cardie (2008) is incomplete.  
- Paper is well-written and motivation for bidirectional updates is clear and compelling.  
- Experimental evaluation is potentially unfair: unclear whether baselines used hybrid vectors, how hyperparameters were tuned, and how models/ensembles were selected.  
- Authors must explicitly describe experimental setup, tuning procedures, and model selection to validate reported gains.  
- Calling the method "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" is a more accurate and standard term.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) lacks crucial BPTS details: how unfolding is implemented, whether gradients are truncated or fully unrolled, and effects on training stability/convergence; cite Goller & Küchler (1996).  
- Section 3.6 does not clearly distinguish BPRNNs from Irsoy & Cardie (2008); need a concise comparison (table/paragraph) explaining novelty of hybrid vectors.  
- Section 4.1 must clarify which Semeval 2013 Task 2 subtask is addressed (contextual vs. message polarity), parser constraints, and why contextual polarity is the focus.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 (ablation and results) should be consolidated to improve flow and reduce redundancy.  
- Overall: promising extension with the hybrid vectors as the main contribution, but requires stronger experimental rigor, clearer terminology, and more methodological detail before acceptance.
