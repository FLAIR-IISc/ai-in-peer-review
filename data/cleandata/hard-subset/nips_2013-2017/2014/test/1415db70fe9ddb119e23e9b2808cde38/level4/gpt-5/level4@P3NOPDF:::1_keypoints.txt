- Proposes recursive neural nets with a top-down feedback pass to let phrase-level predictions refine word-level embeddings/labels and introduces hybrid word vectors combining bottom-up and top-down info.

- Clear motivation for adding feedback to bottom-up composition and generally well written.

- Hybrid word vectors are an interesting and potentially novel differentiator from prior bidirectional recursive models.

- Empirical results are promising.

- Terminology concern: calling the method “belief propagation” is misleading; prefer “forward–backward” or “bidirectional recursive.”

- Prior work relation unclear: correct and complete citation for Irsoy and Cardie is needed and differences to prior bidirectional recursive models should be spelled out.

- Clarify whether top-down and bottom-up passes share parameters, how feedback is injected (directly to leaves or not), and whether feedback is iterative or single-pass.

- Training details are insufficient: specify backpropagation through structure, how top-down is handled during training (single vs multiple passes), objectives, optimizer, learning rates, regularization, initialization, and embedding fine-tuning.

- Address vanishing/exploding gradients measures (clipping/normalization) and report computational complexity/runtime overhead of the top-down step.

- Experimental fairness issues: ensure RNN/B-RNN baselines use comparable hybrid vectors or provide ablations isolating hybrid vectors vs top-down pass.

- Report hyperparameter tuning procedures, search spaces, selection criteria, early stopping, and whether results are ensemble or single-model.

- Provide clear description of model selection and ensure test sets are used only once.

- Dataset/task description needs expansion: specify SemEval subtask (contextual polarity vs message-level), data splits, label space, metrics, and handling of parser errors.

- Clarify inference procedure: exact sequence and number of passes at test time, whether iteration/convergence is used, and how phrase- and word-level predictions are combined.

- Suggest merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for conciseness.

- Recommended additional analyses: ablate hybrid vectors and top-down pass, compare single-pass bidirectional recursion vs feedback mechanism, test sensitivity to parser quality/tree depth, and show qualitative examples of phrase-level effects on word-level outputs.

- Minor presentation fixes: correct Irsoy and Cardie citation, clarify and keep notation consistent, and proofread typos (e.g., “wok” → “work”).

- Overall: interesting and well-presented extension with promising gains, but overstates link to belief propagation and needs stronger experimental/training details and fair single-model baselines; with those fixes, reviewer leans toward acceptance.
