- Introduces BPRNNs with a backward pass to let phrase-level predictions and embeddings refine word embeddings and labels.
- Paper is well-written and clearly motivated but builds on BRNNs (Irsoy & Cardie 2008) with an incomplete citation.
- Hybrid word vectors are highlighted as a key contribution, but it's unclear if this is the primary innovation over prior work.
- Major concern about experimental fairness: unclear if RNN/BRNN baselines used hybrid vectors and whether ensembling differences bias results.
- Hyperparameter tuning and model selection procedures are insufficiently described; need detail on how “best” models were chosen.
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading; a term like “forward-backward” would better describe the architecture.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; likely uses BPTT—cite Goller (1996)—and discuss unfolding, depth, and vanishing-gradient challenges.
- Section 3.6 lacks clarity in distinguishing this model from Irsoy & Cardie; provide explicit architectural comparisons and claimed benefits.
- Section 4.1 needs to explain the SemEval 2013 task 2 setup for non-NLP readers and specify which subtask (likely contextual polarity) and parser constraints.
- Suggest merging sections 4.3 and 4.4 for conciseness.
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially valuable variant, but strengthen paper by addressing experimental fairness, training details, and clearer comparison to prior work.
