This paper introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks." Such networks extend "feedforward" (bottom-up) recursive networks with a backward (top-down) step. This allows for sentence-level prediction and embedding to respond to word embeddings and labels.

The paper is well-written overall. The model is well-motivated and described. It extends the concept of bidirectional recursive neural networks introduced by Ersoy and Cardy in 2008 (note that the reference to that paper is incomplete). For example, one contribution is the introduction of hybrid word vectors. Perhaps this is the most significant difference with previous work? Moreover, the experimental comparisons are not entirely fair: do both RNNs and B-NNs use hybrid vectors? How are the "best" models tuned and selected? How is a single best selected? It is noteworthy that other methods do not use an ensemble. These issues should be clarified. The term "belief propagation" is misleading, and the term "forward-backward" might be more appropriate.

Section 3.5 (Training) is a bit short and could provide more detailed information. For example, I believe the training used a back-propagation through structure algorithm (Gোলার's 1996 paper can be referenced). This algorithm implies that the recursive model is exposed. In this case, it creates a very deep network that can "reach" the word embedding part that has been updated. If I understand correctly, I believe the authors could have provided more detailed information on this important step.

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing and should clarify the difference between this model and the work of Ersoy and Cardy.

Section 4.1 should be improved for readers outside the NNLP community to understand. Task 2 of Semeval 2013 has 2 subtasks: relevance and message polarity. I believe you discussed the relevance polarity task. Through a better presentation of the task, one can even understand the parser limitations.

Perhaps, sections 4.3 and 4.4 can be combined.

This paper is well-written overall and describes an interesting type of bidirectional recursive model.
