This paper presents “belief propagation recursive neural networks,” a novel architecture that augments standard feedforward recursive networks with a backward pass during inference. This allows for phrase-level information to influence word embeddings and label predictions.

The paper is generally well-written, with a clear motivation and description of the proposed model. It builds upon the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008), though the citation is currently incomplete. A key contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors, potentially representing the most significant distinction from prior work. However, the experimental comparisons require further scrutiny. Specifically, it is unclear whether the RNN and B-RNN baselines also utilized hybrid vectors, and details regarding the tuning and selection of “best” models – including the criteria for selecting a single best model – are lacking. The fact that the presented model employs an ensemble, while others do not, should also be addressed. 

The use of the term “belief propagation” may be misleading; “forward-backward” might be a more appropriate descriptor. Section 3.5 (Training) is somewhat concise and would benefit from additional detail. It is likely that backpropagation through structure is employed, and citing the work of Goller (1996) would be relevant. This unfolding process creates a deep network, and further explanation of this crucial training step would be valuable.

Section 3.6 is currently unclear and requires a more precise delineation of the differences between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie. Section 4.1 should be revised to improve accessibility for readers outside the field of Natural Language Processing. Specifically, clarifying that the study focuses on the contextual polarity subtask of the SemEval 2013 task 2 would enhance understanding, and could also illuminate the role of parser constraints. 

Consider merging sections 4.3 and 4.4 for improved flow. Overall, this is a well-written paper that introduces an interesting and potentially valuable extension to Bidirectional recursive models.
