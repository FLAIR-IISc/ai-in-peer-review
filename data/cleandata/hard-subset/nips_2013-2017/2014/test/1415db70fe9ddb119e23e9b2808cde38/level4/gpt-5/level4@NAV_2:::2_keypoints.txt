- Proposes a bidirectional RNN that adds a top-down (downward) pass to standard bottom-up recursive composition, enabling context to refine smaller constituents; achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval Task 2 (2012).
- Extension is simple, elegant, and empirically effective.
- Similar high-level idea to [18], but uses a different top-down computation and reports superior empirical results.
- Method is well motivated, clearly described, and the paper is well written.
- Experimental concern: unclear if dropout and additional training data were used for the [18] bidirectional baseline reported in Table 2.
- Experimental concern: unclear whether hybrid word vectors were used for the baseline comparison.
- Ensemble performance is impressive but should not be presented in a table comparing individual models.
- Clarification requested on what “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 refers to.
- Overall assessment: a well-executed paper based on a simple, elegant idea.
