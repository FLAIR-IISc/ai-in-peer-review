This paper proposes a novel extension to recursive neural networks (RNNs) called global belief recursive neural networks (GB-RNNs), which incorporates contextual information by including a feedbackward step during inference. The authors apply this model to the task of contextual sentiment analysis and demonstrate its effectiveness on the SemEval 2013 challenge.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the proposed model, highlighting the limitations of standard RNNs in capturing contextual information. The related work section is comprehensive, covering various approaches to natural language processing, including neural word vectors, recursive neural networks, and sentiment analysis.

The GB-RNN model is introduced in Section 3, where the authors describe the architecture and the training process. The model is an extension of the standard RNN, where the forward node vectors are computed using a bottom-up recursive function, and the feedbackward vectors are computed using a top-down recursive function. The authors also propose a hybrid approach to word vector representations, combining unsupervised and supervised word vectors.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, with the authors comparing their model to several baselines and state-of-the-art models on the SemEval 2013 challenge. The results show that the GB-RNN model outperforms the standard RNN and other baselines, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the challenge.

However, there are some concerns regarding the evaluation methodology. The authors present an ensemble result, which may not be directly comparable to the benchmarks. Additionally, the exact composition of the models compared is not clear, and it is unclear how the models were tuned and the ensemble/best models selected. It would be helpful to clarify these points to ensure a fair comparison.

Minor points include the use of an off-the-shelf parser to parse tweets, which may not be the most accurate approach. Additionally, some citations are incomplete.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective approach to contextual sentiment analysis using GB-RNNs. While there are some concerns regarding the evaluation methodology, the results appear promising, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

* The paper proposes a novel extension to RNNs that incorporates contextual information.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
