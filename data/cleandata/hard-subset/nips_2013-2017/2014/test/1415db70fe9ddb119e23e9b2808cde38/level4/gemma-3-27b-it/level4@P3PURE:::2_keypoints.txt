- Introduces a bidirectional recursive neural network with an upward pass followed by a downward pass to augment node representations.  
- Addresses the limitation of standard RNNs that cannot propagate information top-down in the parse tree.  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- The extension is simple, elegant, and effective; computation during the downward pass differs from related work [18] and yields better performance.  
- The method is well motivated and the paper is clearly written.  
- Experimental concern: unclear whether the [18] baseline in Table 2 used dropout and the additional training data.  
- Experimental concern: unclear whether hybrid vectors were used in the alternative bidirectional RNN configuration.  
- Inclusion of the ensemble result alongside individual models in the comparison table is inappropriate.  
- Clarification needed for the phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4.
