This research paper proposes using both the encoding and decoding phrase vectors of a recursive neural network auto-encoder in a sentiment classification task. The author calls it "belief propagation," although this is not an example of a belief algorithm, and thus the name may mislead the reader. In general, it is a well-written paper, although the motivation for the proposed model is unclear and lacks clear theoretical justification.

The main weakness of this paper is the evaluation. There is no reason to present an ensemble result in the context of this evaluation; there is no benchmark ensemble, and this obscures the result. The exact structure of the models compared is also not clear. The key questions that need to be clarified are: how were the models tuned, and how was the ensemble/best model selected? How was the benchmark tuned in Table 2? Were they using the same enhanced data as the BP-RNN? Were the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN also using hybrid word vectors?

In the absence of this information, I would guess that the second entry in Table 3 is most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 when the BP-RNN architecture is assessed.

Minor points:
- I am surprised that an off-the-shelf parser was used to parse the tweets. I would be interested to know how accurate it is on this data.
- The Irsoy and Cardie citation is incomplete (as are many others).

This paper presents a recursive neural network model for classifying phrase sentiment in the context. The results seem reasonable, but the model lacks a strong motivation, and the experimental methodology is not entirely clear.
