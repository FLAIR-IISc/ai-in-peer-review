- Paper proposes GB-RNN: adds a top-down (global) pass to recursive neural nets, uses hybrid word vectors and dropout, and reports state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 contextual sentiment.

Strengths:
- Addresses limitation of standard feedforward recursive models by allowing global sentence context to influence local predictions.
- Logical, well-written extension of existing recursive architectures.
- Strong experimental results showing large improvement over standard RNN baseline and outperforming the shared-task winner.
- Hybrid (partially fixed/partially fine-tuned) word vectors are a useful practical contribution.

Weaknesses:
- Originality vs Irsoy & Cardie (2013) is unclear; Section 3.6 does not intuitively explain why the backward-pass formulation is superior.
- Experimental comparisons lack crucial details: unclear if baselines used hybrid vectors and dropout.
- Hyperparameter tuning and model selection procedures are not described, making comparisons hard to assess.
- Best GB-RNN result is an ensemble of five models but compared directly to single-model baselines; single-model comparison is missing.
- Training description is too brief; need explicit mention of backpropagation through structure and details on gradient flow through the unfolded two-pass network.
- SemEval task description is insufficient for non-NLP readers; the contextual polarity subtask and parser constraints should be explained.
- The name "Global Belief Recursive Neural Network" is misleading (invokes belief propagation); a clearer name like forward-backward recursive NN is preferable.
- Citation to Irsoy and Cardie (2013) appears incomplete and should be verified.
- Minor organization issue: Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for better flow.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Explicitly state whether hybrid vectors and dropout were used for all baselines and report single-best GB-RNN alongside ensemble results.
- Expand Section 3.5 to detail the training algorithm, cite backpropagation through structure, and discuss implications of the model's depth on gradients and embeddings.
- In Section 3.6, give a clearer, intuitive comparison to Irsoy & Cardie and explain the practical advantages of the proposed backward pass.
- Expand Section 4.1 to define the SemEval contextual polarity subtask and justify parser constraints for a general audience.
- Consider renaming the model to reflect the two-pass forward-backward mechanism.
- Verify and complete all citations (especially Irsoy & Cardie 2013).
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for improved readability.
