- Introduces a bidirectional extension to recursive neural networks for richer text representations.  
- Standard (unidirectional) RNNs combine representations up a parse tree but cannot propagate context downward.  
- Proposed method adds a downward pass after the upward traversal to enrich all node representations with broader context.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2, showing clear empirical effectiveness.  
- Core idea is simple, elegant, and well-motivated.  
- Related to prior work [18], but the downward pass uses distinct computations and yields superior performance.  
- Method and theory are clearly explained and the paper is generally well written.  
- Experimental results are compelling but require clarification on whether the baseline from [18] used dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors.  
- The ensemble result should not be included in Table 2 (which compares individual models); ensembles are a different experimental configuration and should be presented separately.  
- Section 4.4’s phrase “a widely used mix of both” lacks context and should be clarified.  
- Overall assessment: technically significant, well-implemented work; minor experimental-detail clarifications needed but do not undermine the contribution.
