This paper presents “belief propagation recursive neural networks,” an extension of feedforward recursive networks that incorporates a backward pass during inference. This allows for phrase-level information to influence word embeddings and label predictions.

The paper is generally well-written, with a clear motivation and description of the model. It builds upon the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008), though the citation is currently incomplete. A key contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors, potentially representing the most significant distinction from prior work. However, the experimental comparison requires further scrutiny. Specifically, it is unclear whether the RNN and B-RNN baselines also utilized hybrid vectors, and details regarding the tuning and selection of the “best” models – including the single best model – are missing. The lack of ensemble methods in the baselines should also be acknowledged. These points require clarification.

The use of the term “belief propagation” may be misleading; “forward-backward” might be a more accurate descriptor. Section 3.5 (Training) lacks sufficient detail. It is likely that back-propagation through structure is employed, and citing the work of Goller (1996) would be beneficial. This unfolding process creates a very deep network, and further explanation of how gradients propagate to the word embedding layer would strengthen the paper. 

Section 3.6 is somewhat unclear and requires a more precise distinction between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie. 

Section 4.1 would benefit from clarification for readers outside the field of Natural Language Processing. The SemEval 2013 task 2 consists of two subtasks—contextual and message polarity—and the authors likely addressed the contextual polarity task. A clearer presentation of the task would also illuminate the impact of the parser constraints.

Consider merging sections 4.3 and 4.4 for improved flow.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that describes an interesting and worthwhile extension to the Bidirectional recursive model.
