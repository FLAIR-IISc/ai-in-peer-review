This paper proposes leveraging both the encoder and decoder phrase representations of a recursive neural network autoencoder for sentiment classification. The authors characterize this as “belief propagation,” although it is not the standard inference algorithm by that name, which may be misleading. The paper is generally clearly written, but the motivation for the model is somewhat nebulous and lacks a strong theoretical grounding.

The principal shortcoming is the evaluation. Reporting an ensemble in this context is unnecessary—none of the baselines are ensembles—and it obscures the true contribution. The exact makeup of the compared systems is also unclear. Key clarifications needed include: how were individual models tuned and how were the best/ensemble members selected? How were the baselines in Table 2 tuned? Did those baselines use the same additional training data as the proposed BP-RNN? Did the RNN and bidirectional RNN also employ hybrid word vectors?

Without these details, my assumption is that the second row of Table 3 offers the most appropriate comparison to the baselines in Tables 1 and 2 when evaluating the BP-RNN architecture.

Minor points:
- It is surprising that an off-the-shelf parser was used for tweets; please report its accuracy on this domain.
- The Irsoy and Cardie citation is incomplete (as are several others).

This work presents a recursive neural model for context-sensitive phrasal sentiment classification. The results seem reasonable, but the paper lacks a compelling motivation and the experimental methodology is not sufficiently transparent.
