- Problem: Standard recursive neural networks merge bottom-up only, preventing information flow from larger contexts to smaller nodes.
- Contribution: Proposes a bidirectional RNN with an upward pass followed by a downward pass to enrich node representations with higher-level context.
- Empirical result: Method achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2012 Task 2.
- Strengths: Extension is simple, elegant, effective, and clearly presented.
- Relation to prior work: Concept resembles [18], but the downward-pass computation differs and appears to yield superior performance.
- Method clarity: Methodology is well-justified and lucidly articulated.
- Experimental clarification needed: Unclear if Table 2 results for the alternative bidirectional model from [18] used dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors.
- Presentation issue: Inclusion of ensemble results in a table meant for individual model comparisons is misplaced.
- Terminology issue: The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 is undefined and ambiguous.
- Overall verdict: Well-executed paper that builds on a simple idea and advances the field.
