In NLP, a Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) is used to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining pairs of word/short snippet representations. This process begins with the representation of words and moves upwards through a pre-specified parse tree until the entire snippet is represented at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process does not allow information to be propagated downwards in the tree from larger contexts to smaller ones. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of the RNN, which involves going down after going up through the tree, increasing the representation of all nodes containing information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on task 2 of Semeval 2012.

The established RNN-based approach proposed in the paper is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea is similar to [18], the computation on the path down in the paper is different, and the resulting performance seems better.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results seem excellent, but I have some concerns about the experimental setup. Was the result of the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] obtained in Table 2 using dropout and additional training data? Was a hybrid vector also used in that case? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not appear in the table comparing the performance of the individual models.

What is related to "a widely used combination of both" in Section 4.4? A well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
