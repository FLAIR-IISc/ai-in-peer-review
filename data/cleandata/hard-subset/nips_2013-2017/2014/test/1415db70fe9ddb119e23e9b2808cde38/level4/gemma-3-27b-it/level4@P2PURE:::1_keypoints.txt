- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" with a backward (top-down) pass to let phrase-level predictions and embeddings feedback to words and labels.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly described, and the model is well-motivated.  
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008); citation appears incomplete and relation to that work needs clearer delineation.  
- Hybrid word vectors are a key contribution and may be the most significant novelty over prior work.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair: unclear if RNN/B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors.  
- Missing details on hyperparameter tuning, model selection criteria for choosing the single "best" model, and the absence of ensembling in baselines.  
- The term "belief propagation" may be misleading; "forward-backward" could be more appropriate.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too concise; authors should clarify training procedure and whether backpropagation-through-structure (unfolding into a deep network) is used.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing; require clearer distinction between this model and Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Section 4.1 needs clearer exposition for readers outside NLP.  
- For SemEval 2013 Task 2, specify whether results are for contextual or message polarity subtask and provide a more detailed task description and parser-constraint discussion.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be combined for clearer presentation.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, promising variant of bidirectional recursive models.
