- Introduces GB-RNNs, an RNN extension adding a single feedbackward inference step so phrase-level predictions feed back to words.  
- Motivated by capturing contextual information for tasks like aspect-specific sentiment classification.  
- Feedbackward step yields ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNN on contextual sentiment.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge.  
- Proposes a hybrid word-vector approach combining unsupervised and supervised vectors, outperforming either alone.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents the method succinctly.  
- Strengths: simplicity, elegance, strong empirical effectiveness, and thorough baseline comparisons.  
- Potential weakness: unclear whether the bidirectional RNN baseline from [18] used dropout or extra training data.  
- Criticism: ensemble result is arguably inappropriate in a table comparing individual model performances.  
- Originality: builds on existing RNN/bidirectional work, but differs in the computation on the downward pass.  
- Significance: useful contribution to NLP with potential applicability beyond sentiment analysis.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel feedbackward idea, strong results, clear motivation, and effective hybrid embeddings.  
- Rejection arguments: experimental setup ambiguities, ensemble reporting issues, and limited emphasis on novelty.
