The paper proposes a “belief-propagation recursive neural network” that augments standard feedforward (bottom-up) recursive architectures with a single backward (top-down) pass at inference time. This top-down step lets phrase-level predictions and representations influence word-level embeddings and labels.

Overall, the manuscript is clearly written, and the model is well motivated and described. It extends the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks of Irsoy and Cardie (the citation appears incomplete). One notable contribution is the hybrid word vector scheme; perhaps this is the principal technical difference from prior work? The experimental comparisons raise fairness questions: did the RNN and bidirectional RNN baselines also use the same hybrid vectors? How were the “best” models tuned and selected, and how was the “single best” model chosen? It is also worth noting that other competing methods are not ensembles. These points should be clarified. The term “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; “forward–backward” might be more appropriate. Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and should include more detail. I assume training uses backpropagation through structure (Goller, 1996), which entails unfolding the recursive model; in that case, the effective depth to reach and update the word embeddings is large. If this interpretation is correct, more details on this critical aspect would be helpful.

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing, and the distinction between this model and that of Irsoy and Cardie needs to be clarified. Section 4.1 should be made accessible to readers outside NLP. SemEval 2013 Task 2 has two subtasks—contextual polarity and message polarity—and I infer that you address contextual polarity. A clearer presentation of the task would also make the parser constraints easier to understand.

Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could potentially be merged. In sum, the paper is well written and presents an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive approach.
