Recursive neural networks (RNNs) in NLP construct text representations by recursively combining word or sub-snippet representations, starting from the word level and progressing up a predefined parse tree to arrive at a snippet-level representation. A limitation of this approach is its unidirectional flow of information, preventing context from larger structures influencing smaller ones. This work presents a bidirectional extension to RNNs, incorporating a downward pass following the standard upward pass to enrich node representations with broader contextual information. The proposed system demonstrates state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2.

The paper’s extension of existing RNN methods is straightforward, well-conceived, and demonstrably effective. While conceptually related to the work in [18], the downward pass computation differs, leading to improved performance. 

The method is well-justified and clearly articulated, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are strong, though some aspects of the experimental setup warrant clarification. Specifically, regarding the results for the bidirectional RNN model from [18] presented in Table 2, was dropout and the additional training data utilized? Were hybrid vectors also employed? Furthermore, the inclusion of an ensemble result within a table comparing individual model performance seems inappropriate. 

Finally, the phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 could benefit from further specification. Overall, this is a strong paper built upon a simple yet elegant idea, and executed effectively.
