- Proposes a bidirectional extension of recursive neural networks where an upward tree pass is followed by a downward pass to augment node representations with larger-context information.
- Addresses limitation of traditional RNNs’ unidirectional flow by enabling context to flow from larger to smaller constituents.
- Method is simple, elegant, clearly presented, and well-motivated.
- Downward-pass computation differs from the related method in [18], and yields apparently superior performance.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2, demonstrating empirical effectiveness.
- Unclear experimental details for the [18] baseline in Table 2: whether dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors were used.
- Ensemble result is inappropriately placed with individual-model comparisons and should be presented separately.
- Minor clarification needed in Section 4.4 regarding the phrase “widely used mix of both.”
- Overall strong contribution with promising results that require minor revisions to experimental reporting and clarifications.
