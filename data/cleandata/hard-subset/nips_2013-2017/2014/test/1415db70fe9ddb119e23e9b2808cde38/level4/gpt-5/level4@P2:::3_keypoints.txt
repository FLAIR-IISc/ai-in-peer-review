- Introduces GB-RNN: a recursive NN with a single top-down “feedbackward” pass that combines forward and backward vectors for local predictions.
- Proposes hybrid embeddings (fixed unsupervised + small supervised component) and uses dropout.
- Reports state-of-the-art ensemble results on SemEval 2013 targeted sentiment; single-model gains over RNN and bidirectional recursive baselines are promising.
- Strength: simple, clear architectural extension enabling sentence-level context to inform local predictions.
- Strength: useful engineering finding that hybrid embeddings with dropout outperform either alone.
- Strength: broadly applicable to other context-dependent labeling tasks.
- Terminology concern: “Global belief” and “propagating beliefs” misleadingly evoke belief propagation; recommend “top-down contextual pass” or “bidirectional recursive RNN.”
- Evaluation transparency: ensemble emphasized over single-model comparisons; single-model GB-RNN should be primary.
- Missing detail: hyperparameter search, model selection, ensemble top-k selection criteria, development/test splitting and stopping rules not specified.
- Fairness of baselines unclear: need to state whether baselines used same embeddings, external resources (lexicons, distant supervision), dropout, and preprocessing.
- Ablations needed: effect of removing top-down pass, using V=I at root, varying backward dimensionality nd, concatenation vs. other fusions, and using only supervised/unsupervised embeddings.
- Statistical rigor: provide confidence intervals or significance tests for single models and variability across seeds for ensembles.
- Parsing robustness: Stanford news-trained parser may be ill-suited to tweets—quantify parsing quality or test robustness (tweet parser, dependency vs constituency).
- Quality: technically sound and well-implemented, but would benefit from clearer theoretical motivation for conditioning backward messages on forward/backward parents.
- Clarity: generally readable, but confusing terminology (“belief”, “feedbackward”) and incomplete citations need fixing.
- Originality: moderate — similar top-down ideas exist; novelty in specific backward parameterization, root transform, and hybrid embedding/dropout recipe.
- Significance: potentially meaningful if gains hold under matched baselines and single-model evaluation; could transfer to other token/phrase labeling tasks.
- Action items requested: report single-model results, detail hyperparameter tuning protocol, confirm baseline resource parity, add ablations, report significance/variance, assess parsing on tweets, and correct citations.
- Minor: justify or replace the off-the-shelf news parser for tweets, describe tokenization (hashtags/emoticons), and fix small typographical/equation/figure reference errors.
- Overall: promising and practical extension of recursive NNs, but the motivation and experimental methodology need tightening for the contribution to be convincing.
