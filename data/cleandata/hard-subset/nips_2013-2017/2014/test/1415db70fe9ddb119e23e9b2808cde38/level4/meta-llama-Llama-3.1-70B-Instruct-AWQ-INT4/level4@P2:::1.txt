This paper introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), which extend feedforward recursive neural networks with a backward step during inference. This allows phrase level predictions and representations to give feedback to words, enabling the model to capture context-dependent compositional vector representations of variable length phrases.

The paper is well-written, and the model is well-motivated and described. The authors extend the idea of Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie, with a notable contribution being the introduction of hybrid word vectors. However, it is unclear whether the RNN and Bidirectional RNN also use hybrid vectors, and how the "best" models are tuned and selected. Additionally, the single best model selection process should be clarified.

The term "Global Belief" might be misleading, and "forward-backward" could be a more suitable term. Section 3.5 (Training) is brief and could provide more details, such as the use of back-propagation through structure algorithm. This algorithm implies that the recursive model is unfolded, resulting in a very deep network to update the word embedding part. The authors could provide more details on this crucial step.

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing, and the difference between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie should be clarified. Section 4.1 could be improved to be understood by readers outside of the NLP community. The task 2 of Semeval 2013 should be better presented, including the contextual polarity task and parser constraints.

The paper could be improved by merging sections 4.3 and 4.4. Overall, the paper is well-written and describes an interesting variant of the Bidirectional recursive model.
