In natural language processing, recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been employed to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining representations of words or shorter snippets. This process begins with word representations and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree until a representation of the entire snippet is obtained at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this approach prevents information flow down the tree, from larger to smaller contexts. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs, wherein an upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, augmenting node representations with information from broader contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The proposed extension to the established RNN-based approach is simple, elegant, and effective. While the high-level idea shares similarities with that of [18], the computation performed during the downward pass differs, and the resulting performance appears to be superior. 

The method is well motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are excellent, although I have some concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, was the result for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2 obtained using dropout and the additional training data? Were hybrid vectors also utilized in that configuration? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, its inclusion in a table comparing the performance of individual models seems inappropriate. 

Finally, what does the phrase "widely used mix of both" refer to in Section 4.4? Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
