This paper proposes a model for sentiment classification that utilizes both the encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive neural network auto-encoder. While the paper is generally well-written, its core contributions are undermined by a weakly motivated model and significant issues with the experimental evaluation.

A primary point of confusion is the authors' reference to their method as "belief propagation." This term denotes a specific and well-known inference algorithm, of which the proposed model is not an instance. This choice of name is misleading and should be revised. More broadly, the motivation for the proposed architecture is vague and lacks a clear theoretical justification for why combining encoder and decoder vectors should be beneficial.

The key weakness of this paper lies in its evaluation. The main results are presented for an ensemble model, which obfuscates the contribution of the proposed architecture, as none of the benchmarks are ensembles. This prevents an apples-to-apples comparison and makes it difficult to assess the true performance of the model itself.

This issue is compounded by a lack of clarity regarding the experimental setup. To properly interpret the results, the authors should clarify the following:
*   How were the proposed models tuned, and what was the criterion for selecting the models used in the ensemble or as the "best" single model?
*   How were the benchmark models in Table 2 tuned?
*   Were the benchmarks trained using the same augmented data as the BP-RNN?
*   Were the baseline RNN and Bidirectional-RNN models also implemented with the hybrid word vectors?

In the absence of this information, the most appropriate comparison to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 appears to be the second entry in Table 3. This result should be highlighted as the primary point of comparison when assessing the BP-RNN architecture.

**Minor Points:**
*   The use of an off-the-shelf parser on tweet data is a potential concern, given the noisy and ungrammatical nature of the text. It would be helpful if the authors could report on the parser's accuracy on this specific dataset.
*   Several citations are incomplete, including Irsoy and Cardie (2014). The bibliography should be carefully checked and corrected.

In summary, this paper presents a recursive neural network model for classifying phrasal sentiment in context. While the results seem promising, the model lacks strong motivation, and the experimental methodology is not sufficiently clear to validate the claimed improvements over prior work.
