- Paper tackles context-dependent sentiment, arguing standard RNNs can't revise constituent meanings and proposing GB-RNN with a bottom-up then top-down "feedbackward" pass to incorporate global context.

- Final node representations are concatenations of feedforward and feedbackward vectors; paper also uses dropout and hybrid (fixed + trainable) word vectors and reports state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.

- Strength: Addresses an important limitation of compositional NLP models (contextual modulation of meaning).

- Strength: Proposes an intuitive, novel two-pass architecture that propagates global context down parse trees.

- Strength: Strong empirical results, outperforming baselines and the SemEval 2013 winning system.

- Strength: Useful secondary contributions—hybrid word vectors and empirical validation of dropout for RNNs.

- Weakness: Confusing name ("Global Belief RNN") and potential mischaracterization of belief propagation; model resembles recursive autoencoder/encoder–decoder more than BP.

- Weakness: Theoretical motivation and justification for the specific feedbackward formulation (Equation 3) are underdeveloped.

- Weakness: Experimental methodology lacks crucial details, undermining reproducibility and attribution of gains.

- Weakness: Ensemble result presented against single-model baselines obscures fair comparison; single-model GB-RNN performance should be primary.

- Weakness: Unclear whether baselines used the same hybrid vectors and dropout—need explicit statement to ensure fair comparisons.

- Weakness: Unclear whether augmented training data (NRC lexicon, noisy tweets) was used for baselines; data differences could explain gains.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter tuning procedures for baselines are not described; unclear if tuning effort was comparable.

- Minor: Using the Stanford Parser on noisy Twitter data is surprising—paper should comment on parser accuracy/impact of parsing errors.

- Minor: Several citations are incomplete and should be fixed.

- Conclusion: Novel and promising model with impressive results, but the paper needs clearer experimental details to verify that improvements stem from the architecture itself.
