- Introduces GB-RNN, adding a backward (top-down) computation so phrase-level representations can inform word-level representations for context-aware inference.  
- Motivates the need with examples where feedforward RNNs misclassify context-dependent sentiment (e.g., "Android beats iOS").  
- Proposes a hybrid word vector combining unsupervised and supervised representations; uses dropout to improve training.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with strong motivation for context-aware sentiment analysis.  
- Connects appropriately to NIPS 2013 literature (Socher et al., Irsoy & Cardie) and cites relevant prior work.  
- Technical contribution: feedbackward mechanism enabling global context propagation beyond local context windows.  
- Solid experimental results: ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs and state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013.  
- Hybrid word vectors show clear advantages over purely supervised or unsupervised representations.  
- Experimental details lacking: unclear model selection, hyperparameter tuning, and whether baselines used the same hybrid vectors.  
- Ensembled top 5 GB-RNNs but did not explain why other models were not ensembled or how ensembles were chosen.  
- Misleading terminology: "belief propagation" should be replaced with "forward-backward" or similar.  
- Training description (Section 3.5) is too brief; needs explicit description of backpropagation through structure and unfolding (cite Goller & Küchler 1996).  
- Comparison section (3.6) is confusing; requires a clearer mathematical distinction from Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional model.  
- Overall originality and significance are strong, but novelty claims would be strengthened by more explicit contrasts to related bidirectional approaches.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify training, comparisons, and terminology.
