This paper introduces the Global Belief Recursive Neural Network (GB-RNN), a novel model for contextual sentiment analysis. The GB-RNN extends traditional recursive neural networks by incorporating a feedbackward step during inference, allowing phrase-level predictions and representations to be influenced by the global context of the sentence.

The authors provide a clear overview of related work, including neural word vectors, recursive neural networks, and sentiment analysis. They also discuss the limitations of existing models, such as the inability to capture contextual information and the reliance on hand-crafted features.

The GB-RNN model is well-motivated by the need to capture contextual information in sentiment analysis. The authors provide a detailed explanation of the model's architecture, including the use of hybrid word vectors and the feedbackward step during inference. The model is evaluated on the SemEval 2013 Task 2 competition dataset, where it achieves state-of-the-art performance.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their model and its results. However, there are some minor issues with the evaluation methodology. Specifically, the authors present an ensemble result, but it is unclear how the models were tuned and selected. Additionally, the exact composition of the models compared is not clear, and it is unclear whether the benchmarks were using the same augmented data as the GB-RNN.

To strengthen the evaluation, the authors should provide more details on the tuning and selection of the models, as well as the composition of the models compared. Additionally, the authors should clarify whether the benchmarks were using the same augmented data as the GB-RNN.

Overall, the GB-RNN model appears to be a promising approach for contextual sentiment analysis. However, further clarification on the evaluation methodology is needed to fully assess the model's performance.
