- Proposes using encoder and decoder phrase representations from a recursive autoencoder for sentiment classification, labeled as “belief propagation” though not the standard algorithm (misleading).  
- Paper is generally clearly written but the model motivation is nebulous and lacks strong theoretical grounding.  
- Principal shortcoming: evaluation is problematic—reporting an ensemble is unnecessary and obscures the true contribution since baselines are not ensembles.  
- Comparison details are unclear: how were individual models tuned and how were best/ensemble members selected?  
- Unclear how baselines in Table 2 were tuned and whether they used the same additional training data as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear whether the RNN and bidirectional RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.  
- Given missing details, the reviewer assumes the second row of Table 3 is the most appropriate comparison to Tables 1 and 2.  
- Minor: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser for tweets—report its accuracy on that domain.  
- Minor: several citations are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).  
- Overall: model yields reasonable results but lacks compelling motivation and transparent experimental methodology.
