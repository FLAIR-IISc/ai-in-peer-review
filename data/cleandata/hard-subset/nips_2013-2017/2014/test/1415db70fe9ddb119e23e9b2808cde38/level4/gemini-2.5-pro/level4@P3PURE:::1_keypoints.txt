- Paper proposes Belief Propagation Recursive Neural Networks that add a top-down inference pass to recursive nets, allowing phrase-level feedback to refine word embeddings and labels.
- Manuscript is well-written, model is well-motivated and clearly described, and presents an interesting variant of Bidirectional RNNs.
- Novelty is unclear: authors should clarify how their model differs from Irsoy & Cardie (2014) and whether hybrid word vectors are the main contribution.
- Section 3.6 and distinctions from prior work need clearer articulation and complete citations.
- Experimental comparisons are potentially unfair or incomplete: clarify whether baseline RNN/B-RNN used the proposed hybrid vectors.
- Explain model selection and tuning procedures: how were models validated, tuned, and how was the single best model chosen for reporting?
- Address ensemble usage: the proposed method seems to use an ensemble while baselines do not—this should be explicitly discussed for fair comparison.
- Training details are too brief: provide a thorough description of backpropagation through structure, unfolding, and how word embeddings are updated through the deep recursive network.
- Terminology critique: “belief propagation” may be misleading; consider using “forward-backward” or bottom-up/top-down terminology.
- Task description needs improvement for non-NLP readers: explicitly state which Semeval 2013 subtask is addressed (contextual vs. message polarity) and clarify parser constraint motivations.
- Organization suggestion: consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 to improve readability.
