- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" that add a top-down (backward) inference step to feedforward recursive nets, enabling phrase-level feedback to words and labels.
- Paper is generally well written with clear model motivation and description.
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008) bidirectional recursive networks, but the citation/discussion of that prior work is incomplete.
- The introduction of hybrid word vectors is a key contribution and likely the main novelty over prior work.
- Experimental comparisons lack clarity: it is unclear whether RNN and B-RNN baselines were also trained with hybrid vectors.
- Unclear model selection/tuning procedure: how the "best" models were tuned and selected needs specification.
- Unclear whether results come from a single best model or ensembles (others used ensembles), which affects fairness of comparison.
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" is a more appropriate, standard term.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and must detail backpropagation through structure and how the embedding layer is reached via deep unfolded paths.
- Section 3.6 is confusing and fails to clearly differentiate the proposed model from Irsoy & Cardie’s work.
- Section 4.1 needs revision for accessibility: clarify SemEval 2013 Task 2 subtasks (contextual vs message polarity) and explicitly state the paper addresses contextual polarity; explain task and parser constraints for non-NLP readers.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 should be merged to improve flow.
- Overall assessment: an interesting and worthwhile variant of the bidirectional recursive model.
