- Terminology is misleading: calling the method "belief propagation" is incorrect since it is not the belief propagation algorithm.  
- Motivation and theoretical foundation are weak or unclear for the proposed model.  
- Primary weakness is evaluation methodology, especially unjustified inclusion of ensemble results that obscure fair comparison.  
- Composition of compared models is unclear—paper does not state exactly which models comprise ensembles or baselines.  
- Key experimental details are missing: how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected.  
- Unclear whether benchmark models in Table 2 used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear whether RNN and bidirectional RNN baselines were trained with hybrid word vectors.  
- Given the missing details, the second entry in Table 3 seems the fairest comparison to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.  
- Minor: using an off-the-shelf parser for tweets is surprising—its accuracy on Twitter data should be reported.  
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Overall: model yields reasonable results but lacks compelling motivation and requires clearer experimental methodology.
