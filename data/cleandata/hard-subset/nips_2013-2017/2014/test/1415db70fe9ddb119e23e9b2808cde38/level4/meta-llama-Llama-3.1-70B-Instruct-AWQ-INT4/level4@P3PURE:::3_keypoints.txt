- Proposes using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment classification, termed "belief propagation."
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading since it does not implement the belief propagation inference algorithm and may confuse readers.
- Paper is generally well-written but the model motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.
- Major weakness in evaluation: reporting an ensemble result is unnecessary (benchmarks aren’t ensembles) and obscures comparisons.
- Unclear composition of compared models; insufficient details on how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected.
- Unclear whether benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned using the same augmented data as BP-RNN.
- Unclear whether RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors, making comparisons ambiguous.
- In the absence of clarification, the second entry in Table 3 appears most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.
- Minor concern: using an off-the-shelf parser on tweets may be unreliable for this data.
- Minor issue: Irsoy and Cardie citation (and several others) are incomplete.
- Overall: model produces reasonable phrasal sentiment classification results but lacks strong motivation and has unclear experimental methodology.
