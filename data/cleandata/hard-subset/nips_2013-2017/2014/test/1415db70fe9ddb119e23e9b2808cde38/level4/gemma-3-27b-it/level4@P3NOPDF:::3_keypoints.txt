- Method name “belief propagation” is misleading and may cause confusion with the standard algorithm.  
- Motivation for the proposed model is vague and lacks strong theoretical grounding.  
- Major weakness: evaluation methodology is flawed because results are reported as an ensemble while benchmarks are single models.  
- Ensemble composition is unclear—no details on which models are included or how the ensemble was formed.  
- Missing tuning details: how individual models were tuned, criteria for selecting the final ensemble, and how the “best” model was identified.  
- Unclear whether benchmark models used the same data augmentation and hybrid word vectors, preventing fair comparison.  
- The single BP-RNN entry in Table 3 seems the most comparable to benchmarks and should be highlighted or used for core comparisons.  
- Paper needs a clear justification for presenting an ensemble or a breakdown of individual model performances.  
- Surprising choice to use an off-the-shelf tweet parser; parser accuracy on this dataset should be reported.  
- Several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete and should be corrected.  
- Overall: proposes a reasonable recursive neural model, but ambiguous motivation and evaluation details limit the paper’s impact.
