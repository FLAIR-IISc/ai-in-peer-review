- Paper proposes BP-RNNs that add a top-down (backward) pass to recursive networks to refine word-level embeddings and labels; idea is intuitive and well-motivated.
- Core novelty is unclear relative to Irsoy & Cardie (2008) Bi-RNN; Section 3.6 fails to clearly delineate differences.
- Unclear whether hybrid word vectors are the primary contribution or an auxiliary engineering choice; need explicit statement of novel aspects.
- Baselines may not use the proposed hybrid vectors—comparisons could be unfair; require ablation or consistent input features across models.
- Hyperparameter tuning and model selection procedures are not described, making robustness of results uncertain.
- BP-RNN results appear to be from an ensemble while baselines are single models—this is not an apples-to-apples comparison.
- The term “Belief Propagation” may be misleading given its established meaning; consider alternative terminology like “Forward-Backward” or “Bottom-Up/Top-Down.”
- Training description is too brief; authors should detail backpropagation through structure, gradient flow, and techniques for handling vanishing/exploding gradients.
- Experimental task description (Section 4.1) is unclear for non-NLP readers—explicitly state which SemEval 2013 subtask is used and clarify parser constraints.
- Section 3.5 (Training) and 3.6 need expansion and clearer explanations of algorithmic details and distinctions from prior work.
- Suggest comparing single best models fairly and separately comparing ensembles to ensembles; include ablation studies for hybrid vectors.
- Minor fixes: consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for conciseness and correct the incomplete Irsoy & Cardie (2008) bibliography entry.
