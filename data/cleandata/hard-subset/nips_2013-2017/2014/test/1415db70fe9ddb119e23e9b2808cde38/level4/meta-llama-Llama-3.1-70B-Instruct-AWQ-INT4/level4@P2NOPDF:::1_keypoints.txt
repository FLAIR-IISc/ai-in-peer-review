- Paper proposes "belief propagation recursive neural networks": adds a top-down inference step to feedforward recursive networks for phrase-level feedback to words/labels.
- Model is well-motivated, clearly described, and builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008).
- Main novel contribution is the introduction of hybrid word vectors, which may be the most significant difference from prior work.
- Experimental comparisons are weak: unclear if baseline RNN and B-RNN used hybrid vectors.
- Unclear how models were tuned and how the single “best” model was chosen; need transparency on model selection and hyperparameter search.
- Other methods did not use ensembles, raising fairness questions about comparisons.
- The term "belief propagation" may be misleading; "forward-backward" could be a more appropriate descriptor.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; authors should clarify use of back-propagation through structure and implications of unfolding a very deep network for updating embeddings.
- Section 3.6 is confusing and should more clearly delineate differences from Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional model.
- Section 4.1 needs to be clearer for non-NLP readers; explicitly describe Semeval 2013 Task 2 (contextual polarity) and parser constraints.
- Consider merging sections 4.3 and 4.4 as they are closely related.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper presenting an interesting variant of bidirectional recursive models.
