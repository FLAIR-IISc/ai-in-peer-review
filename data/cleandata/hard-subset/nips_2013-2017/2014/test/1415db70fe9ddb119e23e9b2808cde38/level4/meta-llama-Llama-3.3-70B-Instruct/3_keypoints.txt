- Proposes novel sentiment classification using encoding and decoding vectors from a recursive auto-encoder, misleadingly called "belief propagation."
- Paper is generally well-written but the model's motivation is unclear and lacks solid theoretical foundation.
- Evaluation methodology is significantly limited and problematic.
- Inclusion of an ensemble result is unjustified and obscures comparison with non-ensemble benchmarks.
- Composition and tuning of compared models are ambiguous and need clarification.
- Unanswered questions: how models were tuned, how ensemble and best models were selected.
- Unclear whether benchmark models in Table 2 were tuned with the same augmented data as BP-RNN.
- Unclear whether RNN and BiRNN baselines used hybrid word vectors like BP-RNN.
- Second entry in Table 3 may be the most comparable result to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.
- Use of an off-the-shelf parser for tweets is surprising; its accuracy on this dataset should be investigated.
- Some citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.
- Overall: model yields reasonable phrasal sentiment results but suffers from weak motivation and unclear experimental methodology.
