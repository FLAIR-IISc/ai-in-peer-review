- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" with a top-down feedback step allowing phrase-level predictions to inform word-level representations.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and presents a clear variant of Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks.  
- Unclear novelty: authors must clarify whether hybrid word vectors are the main contribution and better distinguish this work from Irsoy & Cardie (2008); the Irsoy & Cardie reference is incomplete.  
- Comparison to Irsoy & Cardie (Section 3.6) is confusing and needs revision to delineate specific contributions.  
- Experimental comparisons lack fairness details: did baseline RNN and B-RNN use the proposed hybrid vectors?  
- Experimental methodology unclear: describe hyperparameter tuning, model selection procedure, and how the single best model was chosen versus baselines/ensembles.  
- Training procedure (Section 3.5) is too brief; likely involves backpropagation through structure (Goller & Küchler, 1996) and unfolding—provide a more thorough explanation.  
- Section 4.1 needs clearer description for non-NLP readers (e.g., specify which SemEval 2013 subtask was used and parser constraints).  
- Minor suggestions: the term "belief propagation" may be misleading—consider "forward-backward"; consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for conciseness.
