- Proposes GB-RNNs: augment recursive neural nets with a feedbackward inference step so phrase-level predictions influence word representations.  
- Targets contextual, entity-specific sentiment analysis by capturing dependencies between phrases.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on Twitter data and SemEval 2013.  
- Introduces and evaluates dropout and hybrid (unsupervised+supervised) word vector representations.  
- Builds on recursive NN literature and differs from standard recurrent/bidirectional models by leveraging tree structure with long-range feedback.  
- Strength: addresses an important, practical problem in sentiment analysis.  
- Strength: novel architecture extending recursive nets with feedback.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance and clear, well-structured presentation.  
- Strength: thorough exploration of related techniques (dropout, hybrid vectors) improving insights.  
- Weakness: theoretical justification for the “global belief” mechanism is intuitive but not rigorous.  
- Weakness: experimental presentation lacks clarity—ensemble setup, model tuning, data augmentation, and baseline treatment are under-specified.  
- Weakness: reliance on an off-the-shelf tweet parser raises concerns about parsing errors affecting results; parser accuracy on this dataset is not discussed.  
- Weakness: several incomplete citations hinder reproducibility and literature access.  
- For acceptance: novel solution to a significant problem with compelling empirical evidence.  
- Against acceptance: weak theoretical grounding, ambiguous experimental methodology, parser concerns, and incomplete citations limit robustness/generalizability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify experiments, complete citations, and address parser-accuracy impact.
