- Proposes a bidirectional extension to recursive neural networks that adds a downward pass to infuse broader context into lower-level node representations.  
- Addresses the unidirectional limitation of traditional RNNs by augmenting node vectors with information from larger spans.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2, showing strong empirical performance.  
- Conceptually similar to prior work [18], but uses a distinct downward computation that yields improved results.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and method is easy to understand.  
- Experimental results are strong and improvements over existing methods are notable.  
- Clarification needed on whether the baseline bidirectional RNN from [18] used dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors in Table 2.  
- Ensemble result is impressive but feels out of place in a table focused on individual model performance; suggest separate discussion of ensembles.  
- The phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 is vague and should be clarified.
