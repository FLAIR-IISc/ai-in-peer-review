- RNNs build text representations by recursively combining word/substring vectors up a parse tree, but are unidirectional so larger-context info cannot flow down to smaller nodes.
- Paper proposes a bidirectional tree RNN: an upward pass followed by a downward pass to augment node representations with larger-context information.
- The proposed model achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.
- The extension is notable for its simplicity, elegance, and empirical effectiveness.
- Conceptually similar to prior work [18], but the downward computation differs and yields apparently better performance.
- Methodology is well-motivated, clearly explained, and the paper is well-written.
- Experimental results are impressive but there are concerns about the experimental setup and comparisons.
- It is unclear whether the reported result for the alternative bidirectional model from [18] used dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors.
- Inclusion of the ensemble result in a table comparing individual models may be unjustified.
- The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 needs clarification.
- Overall assessment: well-executed paper that builds on a simple, elegant idea.
