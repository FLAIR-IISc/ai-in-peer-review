- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks": adds a backward step so phrase-level predictions/embeddings inform word embeddings and labels.  
- Well-structured, clearly written, and well-motivated; builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008); hybrid word vectors are a notable contribution.  
- Experimental comparisons are unclear/unfair: unknown whether RNN/B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors, tuning/model-selection methodology is opaque, and other methods weren’t ensembled.  
- The term "belief propagation" is potentially misleading; "forward-backward" may be more appropriate.  
- Section 3.5 (training) is too brief; should detail backpropagation through structure (Goller 1996), unfolding, and how word embeddings are updated.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing; the difference from Irsoy & Cardie’s work needs clearer explanation.  
- Section 4.1 is hard for non-NLP readers; clarify SemEval-2013 Task 2 subtasks and state clearly that the paper addresses contextual polarity and parser constraints.  
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for clarity.  
- Overall: an intriguing variant of bidirectional recursive models that requires additional clarifications and refinements.
