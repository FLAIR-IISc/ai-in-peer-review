- Proposes belief propagation recursive neural networks: a feedforward recursive net with a backward pass to let phrase-level info influence word embeddings and labels.

- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and model description.

- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008); citation is incomplete and distinction from that work needs clarification.

- Hybrid word vectors appear to be a key, possibly novel contribution.

- Experimental comparison is unclear: unclear if RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors and whether baselines used ensembles.

- Missing details on model tuning and how the “best” or single best models were selected.

- The term “belief propagation” may be misleading; “forward-backward” might be more accurate.

- Section 3.5 (Training) lacks detail—likely uses back-propagation through structure; cite Goller (1996) and explain gradient flow to embeddings.

- Section 3.6 is unclear; needs a more precise distinction from Irsoy & Cardie.

- Section 4.1 needs clarification for non-NLP readers about SemEval 2013 Task 2 (contextual vs message polarity) and parser constraint impacts.

- Suggest merging sections 4.3 and 4.4 for improved flow.

- Overall assessment: interesting and worthwhile extension to bidirectional recursive models.
