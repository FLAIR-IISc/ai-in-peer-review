

In natural language processing, recursive neural networks (RNNs) generate text representations by recursively combining word or snippet representations along a pre-specified parse tree, starting from word-level representations and progressing upward to the root node. However, this unidirectional processing prevents information propagation from larger to smaller contextual units. The paper addresses this by introducing a bidirectional RNN extension, where an upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass that augments all node representations with information from larger contexts. This approach achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  

The proposed bidirectional extension is simple, elegant, and effective. While the high-level concept resembles [18], the paper's downward pass computation differs, yielding superior performance. The method is well motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is generally well written. The experimental results are strong, though I have concerns about the experimental setup. Specifically: (1) Were the results for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] in Table 2 obtained using dropout and additional training data? Were hybrid vectors also employed in that case? (2) The ensemble result, while impressive, does not belong in a table comparing individual model performances. Additionally, the phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 requires clarification. Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
