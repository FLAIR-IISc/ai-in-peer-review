This paper proposes a novel recursive neural network architecture, Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), to address contextual sentiment analysis. The core idea is to incorporate a feedbackward step during inference, allowing phrase-level predictions to influence word-level representations. This is motivated by the observation that the sentiment of a word can be context-dependent, as illustrated by examples like “Android” in the sentence “Android beats iOS.” The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their model on contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge and showing improvements over standard RNNs. They also present findings on the benefits of dropout and hybrid word vector representations.

The paper builds upon recent advances in recursive neural networks for NLP [25, 26, 28, 24, 10] and addresses a limitation of purely feedforward architectures: their inability to capture context-dependent meaning. Related work includes recurrent neural networks [8, 21] and bidirectional models [12, 14], which attempt to incorporate context, but may struggle with long-range dependencies. The authors differentiate their approach by leveraging the tree structure provided by parsing, enabling the incorporation of context from distant words. The use of hybrid word vectors, combining unsupervised and supervised representations [27, 11, 15, 5, 24], is also a noteworthy contribution, improving performance compared to using either type of vector alone.

**Strengths:**

*   **Addresses an important problem:** Contextual sentiment analysis is a challenging task with real-world applications, and the authors effectively highlight the limitations of existing approaches.
*   **Novel approach:** The introduction of the feedbackward step in the GB-RNN is a conceptually sound idea that allows for contextual information to influence phrase and word representations.
*   **Strong empirical results:** The model achieves state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 dataset and outperforms baselines, demonstrating its effectiveness.
*   **Well-written and clearly presented:** The paper is generally well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the model and experimental setup.
*   **Useful insights:** The findings on dropout and hybrid word vectors provide valuable practical guidance for training RNNs.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Motivation could be stronger:** While the authors provide intuitive examples, a more rigorous theoretical justification for the global belief propagation mechanism would strengthen the paper. The term "belief propagation" may be misleading, as it doesn't directly correspond to the standard inference algorithm.
*   **Evaluation clarity:** The presentation of ensemble results alongside single model baselines is potentially confusing. It's unclear how the ensemble was constructed and whether the baselines were tuned to the same degree. Clarifying the exact training and tuning procedures for all models is crucial. Specifically, details on data augmentation (if any), hyperparameter selection, and whether the baselines also utilized the hybrid word vectors are needed.
*   **Parser accuracy:** The reliance on an off-the-shelf parser for tweet parsing raises concerns about the accuracy of the resulting parse trees, potentially impacting performance. An analysis of parser accuracy on this specific dataset would be beneficial.
*   **Incomplete citations:** Several citations are incomplete, hindering readers’ ability to explore related work (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper tackles a significant problem with a novel and well-motivated approach.
*   The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating a clear improvement over existing methods.
*   The paper is generally well-written and provides valuable insights into training RNNs for sentiment analysis.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a strong theoretical justification and the ambiguous evaluation setup weaken the paper's overall contribution.
*   The incomplete citations detract from the paper's professionalism.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of contextual sentiment analysis. While the motivation could be strengthened and the evaluation clarified, the novelty of the approach and the strong empirical results make it a worthwhile addition to NIPS. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the concerns raised regarding the evaluation methodology and completing the missing citations. Specifically, the authors should clearly state how the ensemble was created, how the baselines were tuned (including data augmentation and word vector usage), and provide complete citations.
