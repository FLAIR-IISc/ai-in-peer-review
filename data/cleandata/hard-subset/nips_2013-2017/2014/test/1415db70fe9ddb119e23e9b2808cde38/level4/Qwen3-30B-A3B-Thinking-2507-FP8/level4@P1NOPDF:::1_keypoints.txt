- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" adding a top-down inference step to feedforward recursive nets, enabling phrase-level predictions to inform word embeddings and labels.  
- Well motivated and clearly described; natural extension of Irsoy & Cardie (2008), though that reference/discussion is incomplete.  
- Hybrid word vectors appear to be a key, possibly novel, contribution distinguishing this work from prior models.  
- Experimental comparisons are uneven: unclear whether RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors as well.  
- Model selection and tuning are insufficiently explained—how "best" models were chosen and whether ensembles were used needs clarification.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" is a more precise, standard description.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; clarify training procedure, especially whether backpropagation through structure was used and how deep unfolded networks affect embedding updates.  
- Section 3.6 lacks a clear distinction from Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional recursive networks and must explicitly compare/contrast.  
- Section 4.1 needs improvement for non-NLP readers: clarify task setup (SemEval 2013 Task 2 subtasks) and how parser constraints affect the model.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 should be consolidated for better flow.  
- Overall manuscript is well written and presents a compelling variant, but the above methodological and experimental clarifications are required for rigor and clarity.
