- Proposes using encoder and decoder phrase representations from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment classification, but calling it “belief propagation” is misleading and confusing.  
- Paper is generally well written, but the model motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.  
- Primary weakness: evaluation is poor—reporting ensemble results is unnecessary and obscures fair comparisons to non-ensemble baselines.  
- The composition and tuning of compared systems are insufficiently described; key experimental details are missing.  
- Need clarification on how hyperparameters were tuned and how ensemble and “best” models were selected.  
- Need clarification on how the baselines in Table 2 were tuned.  
- Need to state whether all systems used the same augmented training data as BP-RNN.  
- Need to state whether the RNN and bidirectional RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.  
- Given missing details, the second entry in Table 3 appears the most directly comparable to benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.  
- Minor: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser on tweets—report its accuracy on that domain or provide diagnostics.  
- Minor: several citations are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).  
- Overall: model and results are plausible but would be substantially strengthened by renaming to avoid confusion, providing a stronger rationale for combining encoder/decoder representations, and detailing training and comparison protocols.
