This paper proposes leveraging both the encoding and decoding phrase vectors of a recursive neural network auto-encoder for sentiment classification. The authors label this as belief propagation, but it is not an instance of that inference algorithm, and the terminology is therefore misleading. Overall, the paper is well written, but the motivation for the proposed model is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.

The primary weakness lies in the evaluation. There is no justification for including an ensemble result in this setting; none of the baselines are ensembles, and this choice obscures the conclusions. The exact composition of the compared models is also unclear. Key questions that should be addressed include: how were the models tuned and how were the ensemble/best models selected? How were the benchmarks in Table 2 tuned? Did they use the same augmented data as the BP-RNN? Were the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN also using hybrid word vectors?

Without this information, I would infer that the second entry in Table 3 is the most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 when evaluating the BP-RNN architecture.

Minor points:
- I am surprised that an off-the-shelf parser was used for tweets; I would like to know its accuracy on this domain.
- The Irsoy and Cardie citation is incomplete (as are several others).

This paper presents a recursive neural network model for classifying phrasal sentiment in context. The results seem reasonable, but the model lacks strong motivation and the experimental methodology is not entirely clear.
