- Introduces GB-RNNs: adds a backward (top-down) inference pass to recursive neural networks to incorporate global sentence context into phrase-level representations.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors that concatenate fixed unsupervised embeddings with task-specific learned components.  
- Applies dropout during training and reports it improves performance.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 Task 2, outperforming the winner by ~3% F1 and standard RNNs by >5%.  
- Ablation studies show the backward pass and hybrid vectors both contribute significantly to gains (see Table 3).  
- Builds on Socher et al.’s recursive NNs and Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional RNNs, but claims key architectural differences.  
- Key difference: GB-RNNs use both forward and backward parent vectors for backward computation, argued to improve disentanglement versus position-independent transforms.  
- Writing and presentation are strong, with clear motivation, diagrams, and organization.  
- Practical contributions (hybrid vectors, dropout) are immediately useful for NLP practitioners.  
- Terminology issue: calling the method “Global Belief” is misleading; “forward-backward” or “bidirectional inference” is recommended.  
- Experimental transparency concerns: unclear whether baselines used hybrid vectors, how model selection/hyperparameter tuning was done, and why an ensemble of top-5 models was used vs. single-model baselines.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; should cite Goller & Küchler and explain backpropagation through structure and how the backward pass updates word vectors.  
- Section 3.6 needs a clearer, more intuitive explanation of why GB-RNN’s dual-vector approach yields better disentanglement compared to prior work.  
- Task description should explicitly state the focus is contextual sentiment polarity (SemEval 2013 Task 2a) and justify the parser constraint about keeping labeled phrases in single subtrees.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel forward-backward inference with compelling results, but requires minor revisions on terminology and experimental/methodological clarity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address terminology, experimental details, and clearer contrasts with related work.
