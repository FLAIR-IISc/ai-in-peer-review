- Method uses encoder and decoder vectors from a recursive auto-encoder and is labeled “belief propagation,” a misleading term that may cause confusion.
- Paper is generally well-written but lacks clear motivation and a robust theoretical foundation.
- Primary weakness: results are presented as an ensemble while benchmark comparisons are single models, obscuring the BP-RNN’s specific contribution.
- Paper omits critical details on model selection and hyperparameter tuning for all models and the ensemble composition.
- Unclear whether benchmark models in Table 2 were tuned identically or used the same data augmentation as BP-RNN.
- Unclear whether RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors, affecting fairness of comparisons.
- Given current reporting, the single BP-RNN configuration in Table 3 (second entry) is the most directly comparable to other benchmarks.
- Minor concern: reliance on an off-the-shelf tweet parser for informal Twitter data—parser accuracy on this dataset should be reported.
- Several citations are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) and numerous references need correction.
- Overall: approach is promising, but lack of clear motivation and experimental/tuning/citation details limits the paper’s impact; addressing these issues would strengthen it.
