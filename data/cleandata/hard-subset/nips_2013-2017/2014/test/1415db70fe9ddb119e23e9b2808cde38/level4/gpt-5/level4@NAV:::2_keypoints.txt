- Problem addressed: standard recursive neural networks compose upward-only along parse trees, preventing context flow from larger spans to smaller ones; paper proposes a bidirectional variant (upward then downward pass) that enriches node representations and achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- Contribution assessment: the extension is simple, elegant, and effective; it resembles prior work [18] but uses a different downward computation and yields superior performance.  
- Presentation: method is well-motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is generally well written.  
- Experimental concern: empirical results look excellent but there are questions about the experimental setup and fairness of comparisons.  
- Specific reproducibility/comparison queries: for the alternative bidirectional RNN from [18] in Table 2, were dropout and additional training data used, and were hybrid vectors employed?  
- Ensemble reporting issue: the ensemble result is impressive but should not be included in a table intended to compare individual models.  
- Clarification request: unclear what “widely used mix of both” refers to in Section 4.4.
