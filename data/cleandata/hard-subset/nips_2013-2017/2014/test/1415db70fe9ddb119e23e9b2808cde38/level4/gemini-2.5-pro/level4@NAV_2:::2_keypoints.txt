- Problem & Proposal: Introduces GB-RNN, adding a top-down "feedbackward" pass to standard feedforward RNNs to inject global context into node representations.  
- Additional techniques: Uses hybrid unsupervised+supervised word vectors and applies dropout during training.  
- Task & Results: Evaluated on aspect-specific sentiment analysis and achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013.  
- Significance & Novelty: Tackles an important compositional semantics problem; the two-pass architecture and the specific feedbackward formulation (Eq. 3) are novel and effective.  
- Empirical Strength: Substantial gains over feedforward RNN baseline and surpasses previous competition winner, demonstrating strong empirical evidence.  
- Clarity & Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear explanations, equations, diagrams, and insightful related-work discussion.  
- Practical Contribution: Shows that hybrid word vectors and dropout yield practical improvements for recursive models.  
- Experimental Comparison Concern: Needs explicit confirmation that the bidirectional RNN baseline was re-implemented and tuned fairly with identical training setup and data.  
- Ensemble Reporting Issue: GB-RNN results are reported as a 5-model ensemble; single-model performance should also be reported for transparency.  
- Ablation Study Request: An empirical ablation comparing the GB-RNN update rule to the bidirectional RNN rule (Eq. 4 vs Eq. 5) within the same framework is needed to isolate the source of improvement.  
- Overall Assessment: A simple, elegant, and effective extension to recursive networks with strong results; minor clarifications on experimental comparisons would strengthen the paper.
