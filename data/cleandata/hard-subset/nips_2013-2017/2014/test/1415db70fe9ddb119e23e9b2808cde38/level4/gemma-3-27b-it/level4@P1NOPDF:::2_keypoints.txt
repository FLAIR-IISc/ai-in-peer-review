- Introduces a bidirectional recursive neural network that adds a downward pass to propagate context from larger to smaller tree nodes.  
- Method is simple, elegant, and clearly presented, improving node representations with broader-context information.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- Related to prior bidirectional RNN work [18], but differs in downward-pass computation and obtains superior performance.  
- Paper is well-motivated, well-written, and the experimental results are strong.  
- Experimental concerns: unclear whether the [18] baseline in Table 2 used dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors.  
- Criticism that including an ensemble result in a table comparing individual models is inappropriate.  
- Request for clarification of the phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4.
