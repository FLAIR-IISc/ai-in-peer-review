- Introduces GB-RNNs that add a top-down feedbackward pass to recursive neural nets for context-dependent phrase-level sentiment.  
- Addresses limitation of standard RNNs where phrase labels (e.g., "Android") need global sentence context to be correct.  
- Backward computation conditions on both forward and backward parent nodes, differing from position-independent bidirectional RNNs.  
- Reports state-of-the-art SemEval 2013 results with ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors (unsupervised + supervised) and shows dropout improves training.  
- Technically sound experiments and strong empirical results on a standard benchmark.  
- Evaluation lacks transparency: ensemble results obscure single-model GB-RNN performance.  
- Unclear whether baselines used the same hybrid vectors, augmented data, or identical hyperparameter tuning.  
- Theoretical justification for the feedbackward mechanism is weak and not formalized relative to alternatives.  
- The term "global belief" is misleading and risks confusing readers about probabilistic interpretation.  
- Section distinctions (hybrid word vectors vs. concatenated node vectors) need clarification.  
- Minor issues: incomplete citations and unreported parser accuracy on noisy tweets.  
- Novelty is moderate: top-down recursion is new relative to standard RNNs but architectural changes are incremental.  
- Hybrid word vectors are not highly novel but are well-validated empirically.  
- Significance is practical for aspect-specific sentiment and social media, but methodological advance beyond sentiment tasks is limited.
