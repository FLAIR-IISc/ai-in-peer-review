- Introduces belief propagation recursive neural networks (forward + backward inference) enabling phrase-level feedback to word embeddings and labels.
- Paper is well-written, motivated, and clearly described.
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008) Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks; hybrid word vectors are a notable, possibly major, contribution.
- Experimental comparisons are unfair/unclear: unclear whether baseline RNN and B-RNN used hybrid vectors; tuning/selection of “best” models not described.
- Other methods’ lack of ensembling should be acknowledged in comparisons.
- The term “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; “forward-backward” may be more appropriate.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief — need details on back-propagation-through-structure, unfolding, and deep updates to word embeddings.
- Section 3.6 is confusing; clarify differences from Irsoy & Cardie’s approach.
- Section 4.1 needs clearer exposition for non-NLP readers; better description of SemEval 2013 contextual polarity task and parser constraints.
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for concision.
- Overall: an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model with useful contributions but requires clarifications and stronger experimental reporting.
