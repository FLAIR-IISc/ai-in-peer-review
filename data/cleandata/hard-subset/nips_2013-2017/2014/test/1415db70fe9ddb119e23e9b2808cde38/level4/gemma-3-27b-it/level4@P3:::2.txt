This paper introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), a novel extension to standard Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) designed to address limitations in capturing context-dependent meaning in natural language. Standard RNNs, while effective for compositional semantics, operate in a feedforward manner, preventing contextual information from influencing lower-level representations. GB-RNNs address this by incorporating a feedbackward pass during inference, allowing phrase-level predictions to refine word-level representations. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach on contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art results on the SemEval 2013 challenge and showcasing an ability to accurately predict sentiment directed towards specific entities. The paper also explores the benefits of dropout regularization and hybrid unsupervised/supervised word vector representations.

This work builds upon a rich history of compositional distributional semantics [27, 11, 15, 25, 28, 24, 10] and recursive neural networks for NLP [19, 26].  The motivation for incorporating feedback mechanisms is well-rooted in the observation that word meaning is often context-dependent, as illustrated by the example provided in Fig. 1. Recent work exploring bidirectional architectures, such as bidirectional recurrent neural networks [8, 21] and bidirectional recursive neural networks [12, 14], share a similar goal of incorporating contextual information, but differ in their specific implementation.  The authors clearly position their work within this landscape, acknowledging related efforts and highlighting the unique aspects of their approach. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The introduction of a feedbackward pass within a recursive neural network framework is a conceptually simple yet effective idea. It elegantly addresses the limitations of unidirectional RNNs in capturing context-dependent meaning.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The GB-RNN achieves state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 Task 2, demonstrating its practical effectiveness.  The ablation studies (Table 2 & 3) provide valuable insights into the contribution of different components (dropout, hybrid word vectors).
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and experimental setup.  The diagrams (especially Fig. 2) are helpful in understanding the model architecture.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide a comprehensive overview of relevant research, acknowledging key contributions in areas such as word vectors, RNNs, and bidirectional architectures.
* **Practical Contributions:** The findings regarding dropout and hybrid word vector representations are valuable practical insights that could be broadly applicable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Comparison Detail:** While a comparison to Irsoy and Cardie [12] is provided, the details regarding experimental setup (dropout, hybrid vectors, additional training data) for that model are lacking in Table 2. It’s difficult to directly attribute performance differences solely to the architectural difference without controlling for these factors.
* **Ensemble Result Presentation:** Including the ensemble result in a table comparing single models (Table 1) is slightly misleading. While impressive, it's not a fair comparison. A separate mention of the ensemble performance in the text is appropriate, but its inclusion in the primary results table obscures the performance of the core GB-RNN model.
* **Ambiguous phrasing**: The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 is vague and should be clarified. What specifically does this refer to?



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach to contextual sentiment analysis.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a clear improvement over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written, clearly explained, and provides a thorough review of related work.
* The practical insights regarding dropout and hybrid word vectors are valuable to the community.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The comparison to related work [12] could be more rigorous, with a clearer specification of experimental details.
* The presentation of the ensemble result detracts from the evaluation of the core model.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing. The proposed GB-RNN is a conceptually simple yet effective extension to standard RNNs, and the empirical results demonstrate its practical effectiveness. While the comparison to related work could be strengthened and the presentation of the ensemble result clarified, these are minor issues. I believe this paper is worthy of acceptance to NIPS 2017.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
