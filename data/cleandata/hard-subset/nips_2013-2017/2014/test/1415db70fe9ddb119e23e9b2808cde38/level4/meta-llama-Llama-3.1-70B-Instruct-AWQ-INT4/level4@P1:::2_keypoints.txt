- Introduces GB-RNN, a novel RNN extension that adds a feedbackward inference step to let phrase predictions use global sentence context.  
- GB-RNN addresses a key limitation of standard feedforward RNNs by enabling context-dependent representations.  
- Paper provides clear motivation and a thorough related-work review (word vectors, recursive/bidirectional RNNs).  
- Model formulation is well-defined and easy to follow, with clear forward and backward passes through parse trees.  
- Uses a hybrid word vector approach combining unsupervised and supervised learning.  
- Experimental results on SemEval 2013 are strong, with GB-RNN outperforming baselines and achieving state-of-the-art performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and concise for readers familiar with RNNs and NLP.  
- Concern: unclear whether the bidirectional RNN (Irsoy & Cardie 2014) comparison used the same experimental setup (dropout/training data).  
- Concern: ensemble result in Table 2 seems out of place when compared with individual-model results.  
- Originality: belief propagation through neural networks is a novel idea with potential wider applications.  
- Significance: achieves SOTA on SemEval 2013 and suggests broader implications for NLP tasks.  
- Quality: strong exposition and thorough experimental presentation overall.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, impressive empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear experimental comparability and questionable presentation of ensemble results.
