- Introduces “belief propagation recursive neural networks” adding a backward pass so phrase-level predictions/embeddings feed back to word embeddings and labels.
- Paper is generally well written with clear motivation and model description.
- Builds on Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (Irsoy & Cardie 2008), but the citation/discussion of that prior work is incomplete.
- Main novel contribution appears to be hybrid word vectors, which distinguish it from prior work.
- Experimental comparisons are not fully fair or clear—uncertain whether RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors.
- Details on hyperparameter tuning and model selection (how “best” models were chosen) are missing and need clarification.
- Other methods lacked ensembling; this discrepancy should be acknowledged in comparisons.
- The term “belief propagation” may be misleading; “forward-backward” is likely a better descriptor.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too concise; authors should detail whether training uses backpropagation through structure and how the recursive model is unfolded for updating embeddings.
- Section 3.6 is unclear; authors must explicitly state precise differences from Irsoy and Cardie’s model.
- Section 4.1 needs a more accessible explanation for non-NLP readers and clearer presentation of the SemEval 2013 task subtasks and parser constraints.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be combined for conciseness.
- Overall, a well-written, interesting, and valuable variant of bidirectional recursive models.
