This paper introduces “belief propagation recursive neural networks,” extending feedforward recursive networks with a backward pass during inference. This allows phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to word embeddings and labels.

The paper is generally well written, with a clear motivation and description of the model. It builds upon the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008), though the reference to their work is incomplete. A key contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors, and it is perhaps the most significant distinction from prior work. However, the experimental comparisons lack complete fairness. It is unclear whether the RNN and B-RNN baselines also utilized hybrid vectors, and the details of hyperparameter tuning and model selection – specifically, how the “best” models were identified – require clarification. The lack of ensembling in the other methods should also be acknowledged. 

The term “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; “forward-backward” might be a more suitable descriptor. Section 3.5 (Training) is somewhat concise and could benefit from additional detail. I suspect the training process employs backpropagation through structure (e.g., Goller, 1996), which would necessitate unfolding the recursive model, resulting in a very deep network to update the word embeddings. The authors should elaborate on this critical step.

Section 3.6 is currently unclear, and the precise differences between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie need to be explicitly stated. 

Section 4.1 would be improved by providing a more accessible explanation for readers outside the NLP community. In the SemEval 2013 task 2, there are two subtasks – contextual and message polarity – and I believe the authors address the contextual polarity task. A clearer presentation of the task itself would also help readers understand the parser constraints.

Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could potentially be combined for conciseness. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper describing an interesting and valuable variant of bidirectional recursive models.
