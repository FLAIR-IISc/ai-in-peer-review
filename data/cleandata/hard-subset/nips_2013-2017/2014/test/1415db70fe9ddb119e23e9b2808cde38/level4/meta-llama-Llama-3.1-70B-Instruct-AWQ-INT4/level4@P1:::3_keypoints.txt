- Proposes GB-RNN, an extension to recursive neural networks that adds a feedbackward (top-down) step to incorporate contextual information.  
- Applies GB-RNN to contextual sentiment analysis and evaluates on the SemEval 2013 challenge.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a clear motivation about RNNs’ contextual limitations.  
- Related work section is comprehensive, covering neural word vectors, recursive networks, and sentiment analysis.  
- Model architecture: forward node vectors via bottom-up recursion and feedbackward vectors via top-down recursion.  
- Introduces a hybrid word-vector approach combining unsupervised and supervised embeddings.  
- Experimental evaluation compares GB-RNN to multiple baselines and reports state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013.  
- Concern: Use of ensemble results may not be directly comparable to benchmarks.  
- Concern: Unclear composition of compared models and unclear tuning/selection process for ensemble/best models.  
- Minor issue: Using an off-the-shelf parser for tweets may reduce parsing accuracy.  
- Minor issue: Some citations in the paper are incomplete.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel RNN extension, effective contextual modeling, strong empirical results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: evaluation methodology ambiguities, parser choice for tweets, and incomplete citations.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
