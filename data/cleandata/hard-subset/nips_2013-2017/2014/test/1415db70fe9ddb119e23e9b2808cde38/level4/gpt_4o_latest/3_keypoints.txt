- Proposes a sentiment classifier using both encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive neural network auto-encoder.  
- Uses the term "belief propagation" misleadingly; it does not match the standard inference algorithm and may confuse readers.  
- Paper is generally well-written but the model motivation is unclear and lacks theoretical justification.  
- Major shortcoming: evaluation methodology—reporting an ensemble result is unwarranted and obscures fair comparison with non-ensemble benchmarks.  
- Insufficient detail on model composition and tuning: unclear tuning procedures, and how ensemble and best-performing models were selected.  
- Unclear whether benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned equivalently or used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear whether RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors, complicating comparisons.  
- Implication that the second entry in Table 3 may be the most direct and fair comparison to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.  
- Minor: surprising choice to use an off-the-shelf parser for tweets—needs clarification on parser accuracy for this dataset.  
- Minor: several incomplete citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).  
- Overall: model shows promise, but motivation and experimental methodology lack sufficient clarity.
