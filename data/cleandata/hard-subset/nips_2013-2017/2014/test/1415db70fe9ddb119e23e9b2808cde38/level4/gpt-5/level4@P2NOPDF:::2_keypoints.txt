- Proposes a bidirectional Tree-RNN that adds a downward pass over parse trees to propagate top-level context down to subconstituents.  
- Standard Tree-RNNs build bottom-up representations but cannot feed larger-context information back to subconstituents.  
- The model performs an upward pass followed by a downward pass that injects global contextual information into every node.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval-2012 Task 2.  
- The extension is simple, elegant, and empirically effective.  
- Conceptually similar to prior work [18], but the downward-pass computation differs and yields stronger reported performance.  
- Paper is well motivated, clearly explained, and well written.  
- Experimental concerns: did the [18] baseline use dropout and the extra training data when reported in Table 2?  
- Experimental concerns: were hybrid vectors used for that baseline as well?  
- Methodological note: the ensemble result should not be presented in a table meant to compare single models.  
- Request for clarification: what does “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 refer to?  
- Overall assessment: a well-executed paper built on a simple and elegant idea.
