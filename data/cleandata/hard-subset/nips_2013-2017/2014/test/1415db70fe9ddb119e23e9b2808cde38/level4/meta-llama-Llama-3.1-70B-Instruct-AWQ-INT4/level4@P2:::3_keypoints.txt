- Proposes GB-RNN, a novel RNN extension that propagates contextual beliefs for contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013, showing promising effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear model overview.  
- Motivation for GB-RNN and its intuition are not sufficiently explained.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for the proposed approach.  
- Evaluation is unclear: ensemble results are not directly comparable to benchmarks.  
- Unclear how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected.  
- Unclear whether benchmarks used the same augmented data and whether RNN/BiRNN used hybrid word vectors.  
- Use of an off-the-shelf tweet parser is surprising; parser accuracy on tweets is not reported.  
- Some citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Strength: introduces a promising method that emphasizes contextual information.  
- Weakness: methodological and reporting gaps raise concerns about comparability and rigor.  
- Recommendation: provide deeper intuition/theoretical backing, clarify experimental setup and tuning, report parser accuracy, and fix incomplete references.
