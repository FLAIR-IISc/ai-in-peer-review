- Extends feedforward recursive nets with a top-down inference step, enabling phrase-level feedback to word embeddings and labels.  
- Core idea builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008), but that citation/reference is incomplete and must be corrected.  
- Introduces hybrid word vectors as a key contribution central to the approach.  
- Experimental comparisons are unfair/unclear—uncertain whether RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors.  
- Model selection/tuning is poorly described: how "best" models were chosen, tuned, and why a single model was reported instead of an ensemble.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" is a more accurate and standard descriptor.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) lacks detail—need explicit training algorithm, clarification that backpropagation through structure is used, and how the model is unfolded and updates embeddings.  
- Clarify training efficiency/implementation implications (deeply unfolded structure and when embeddings are updated).  
- Section 3.6 must better distinguish this work from prior bidirectional recursive models and clearly state novel contributions relative to Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Section 4.1 needs clearer task description: state explicitly that only the contextual polarity subtask was addressed and explain parser constraints for non-NLP readers.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged; overall the paper is promising but needs substantial revision on methodology, experimental rigor, and clarity.
