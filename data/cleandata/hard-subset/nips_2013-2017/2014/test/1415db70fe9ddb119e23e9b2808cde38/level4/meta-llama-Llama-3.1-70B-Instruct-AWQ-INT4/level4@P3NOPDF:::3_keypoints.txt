- Proposes sentiment classification using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive neural network auto-encoder.
- Calling the method "belief propagation" is misleading and may confuse readers, as it is not true belief propagation.
- Paper is generally well-written but the model motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.
- Evaluation is the primary weakness: presenting an ensemble is unnecessary and obscures results since benchmarks are not ensembles.
- The composition of compared models is unclear; authors must report how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected.
- Unclear whether benchmarks in Table 2 used the same augmented data as BP-RNN and whether RNN/Bi-RNN used hybrid word vectors.
- In absence of clarifying details, the second entry in Table 3 seems the most comparable to benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.
- Minor issues: surprising choice of an off-the-shelf parser for tweets—its accuracy on this data should be reported.
- Some citations are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).
- Overall: model yields reasonable results but lacks strong motivation and presents unclear experimental methodology.
