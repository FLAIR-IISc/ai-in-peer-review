- Identifies a limitation of standard recursive neural networks: bottom-up-only composition prevents global sentence context from influencing local word/phrase representations.  
- Proposes GB-RNN, a bidirectional recursive network that adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass to refine node representations with global information.  
- Evaluated on contextual sentiment analysis and achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2.  
- Method is simple, elegant, well-motivated, clearly written, and empirically effective.  
- High-level idea resembles Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional RNN, but the specific backward-pass implementation is novel and appears superior.  
- Experimental results are strong but need explicit confirmation that the Irsoy & Cardie baseline used the same hybrid word vectors and dropout settings for fair comparison.  
- Table 1 should more clearly distinguish single-model versus ensemble performance to avoid confusion.  
- Overall a valuable contribution to compositional semantics; recommend acceptance pending minor experimental-setup clarifications.
