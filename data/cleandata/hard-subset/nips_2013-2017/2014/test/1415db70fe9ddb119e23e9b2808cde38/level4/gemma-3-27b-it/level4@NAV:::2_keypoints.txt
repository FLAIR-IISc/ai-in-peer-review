- Identifies limitation of standard recursive neural networks (RNNs): upward-only parse-tree pass prevents information flow from larger to smaller contexts.  
- Proposes a bidirectional RNN that adds a downward pass after the upward pass to enrich node representations with broader context.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- Contribution is simple, elegant, and effective; conceptually similar to prior work [18] but differs in downward-pass computation.  
- Downward-pass design differences yield improved performance over [18].  
- Method is well-justified, clearly explained, and the paper is generally well-written.  
- Experimental results are strong but require clarification about training setup for the [18] bidirectional model in Table 2 (use of dropout and extra training data).  
- Request clarification whether hybrid vectors were used for the [18] model reported in Table 2.  
- Suggests the ensemble result should be presented separately from a table of individual model performances.  
- Notes ambiguous phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 needs clearer definition.  
- Overall assessment: a well-executed paper grounded in a simple and elegant idea.
