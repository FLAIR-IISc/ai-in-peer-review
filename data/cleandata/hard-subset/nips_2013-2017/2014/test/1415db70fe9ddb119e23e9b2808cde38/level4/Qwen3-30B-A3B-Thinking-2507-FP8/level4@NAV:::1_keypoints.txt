- Proposes "belief propagation recursive neural networks" that add a top-down inference step to feedforward recursive nets, enabling phrase-level feedback to word embeddings and labels.  
- Paper is well written with clear motivation and model description.  
- Builds on Irsoy and Cardie (2008) Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks, but the citation is incomplete and the relation to prior work needs clearer distinction.  
- Introduces hybrid word vectors, which may be an important novelty compared to prior models.  
- Experimental comparisons are unclear/unfair: unclear if RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors, how models were tuned/selected, and why a single best model was chosen while others didn’t use ensembles.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" would be a more appropriate description.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) lacks detail—authors should explain the training procedure and whether back-propagation through structure (Goller & Küchler, 1996) was used to update word embeddings.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and fails to clearly distinguish the proposed model from Irsoy and Cardie’s work.  
- Section 4.1 needs improvement for non-NLP readers: clarify that SemEval 2013 Task 2 has contextual and message polarity subtasks and state which subtask is addressed and parser constraints.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for conciseness.  
- Overall assessment: well written and an interesting extension of bidirectional recursive models.
