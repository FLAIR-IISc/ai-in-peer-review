This paper proposes a novel extension to recursive neural networks (RNNs), called global belief recursive neural networks (GB-RNNs), which incorporates contextual information by propagating beliefs through the network. The authors apply this model to the task of contextual sentiment analysis and demonstrate its effectiveness on the SemEval 2013 challenge.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear overview of the model and its components. However, the motivation for the proposed model is not entirely clear, and the theoretical justification for the approach is lacking. The authors could benefit from providing a more detailed explanation of the intuition behind the GB-RNN model and how it addresses the limitations of traditional RNNs.

The evaluation of the model is also a key aspect of the paper. The authors present an ensemble result, which is not directly comparable to the benchmarks. It would be helpful to clarify how the models were tuned and the ensemble/best models were selected. Additionally, the composition of the models compared is not entirely clear. Specifically, it is unclear whether the benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned using the same augmented data as the GB-RNN and whether the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN models also used hybrid word vectors.

In terms of the experimental methodology, it is surprising that an off-the-shelf parser was used to parse tweets, and it would be interesting to know how accurate it is on this data. Furthermore, some citations, including the Irsoy and Cardie citation, are incomplete.

Despite these limitations, the paper presents a promising approach to contextual sentiment analysis, and the results appear reasonable. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the GB-RNN model on a challenging task and provide insights into the importance of contextual information in sentiment analysis.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel extension to RNNs that incorporates contextual information.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the GB-RNN model on a challenging task.
* The paper provides insights into the importance of contextual information in sentiment analysis.

Weaknesses:

* The motivation for the proposed model is not entirely clear.
* The theoretical justification for the approach is lacking.
* The evaluation of the model is not entirely clear, and the composition of the models compared is not well-defined.
* Some citations are incomplete.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and promising approach to contextual sentiment analysis.
* The results appear reasonable, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the GB-RNN model on a challenging task.
* The paper provides insights into the importance of contextual information in sentiment analysis.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The motivation for the proposed model is not entirely clear, and the theoretical justification for the approach is lacking.
* The evaluation of the model is not entirely clear, and the composition of the models compared is not well-defined.
* Some citations are incomplete, which may indicate a lack of attention to detail.

Recommendations for improvement:

* Provide a more detailed explanation of the intuition behind the GB-RNN model and how it addresses the limitations of traditional RNNs.
* Clarify the experimental methodology, including how the models were tuned and the ensemble/best models were selected.
* Provide more information about the accuracy of the off-the-shelf parser used to parse tweets.
* Complete the citations and ensure that all references are accurate and up-to-date.
