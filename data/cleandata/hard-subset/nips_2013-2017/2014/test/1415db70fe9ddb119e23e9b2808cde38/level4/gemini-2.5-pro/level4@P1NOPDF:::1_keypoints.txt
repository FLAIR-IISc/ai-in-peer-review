- Introduces "Belief Propagation Recursive Neural Networks" that add a top-down feedback pass to standard bottom-up recursive networks for refining word-level embeddings and labels.  
- Paper is well-written and the top-down feedback idea is clearly motivated and compelling.  
- Novelty is unclear; need to explicitly state precise contributions and whether hybrid word vectors are the primary novelty.  
- Section 3.6’s comparison to Irsoy & Cardie (2008) is confusing and should be revised for a direct contrast; Irsoy & Cardie reference appears incomplete.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair: unclear if baselines used the same hybrid word vectors—feature differences could explain gains.  
- Experimental methodology lacks details on hyperparameter tuning and how the single “best” model was selected, harming reproducibility.  
- Reported results appear to use an ensemble for the proposed method while baselines do not; either report single-model results or compare ensembles fairly.  
- Training details in Section 3.5 are too brief; likely requires backpropagation-through-structure, discussion of unfolding into very deep networks, and handling of optimization issues (vanishing/exploding gradients).  
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading; consider using “forward-backward” or “bidirectional inference.”  
- Section 4.1 (Experimental Setup) should be clearer for non-NLP readers—specify which Semeval 2013 subtask is used and clarify parser constraint motivations.  
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for better flow, as both analyze model behavior.  
- Overall: promising and well-motivated variant of bidirectional recursive models, but needs clearer positioning vs prior work, fairer experiments, and more training detail.
