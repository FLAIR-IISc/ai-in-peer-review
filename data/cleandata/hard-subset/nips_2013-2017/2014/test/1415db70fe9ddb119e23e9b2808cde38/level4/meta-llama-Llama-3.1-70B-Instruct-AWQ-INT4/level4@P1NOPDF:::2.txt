In natural language processing (NLP), recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been employed to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining pairs of word or shorter snippet representations. This process begins with word representations and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree until the representation of the entire snippet is obtained at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process does not permit information to be propagated down the tree from larger to smaller contexts. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs, where an upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, thereby augmenting the representations of all nodes with information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The proposed extension of the established RNN-based approach is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea bears some resemblance to the one presented in [18], the computation performed during the downward pass in this paper differs, and the resulting performance appears to be superior.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results appear excellent, but I have some concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, I would like to know whether the result for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2 was obtained using dropout and the additional training data. Additionally, were the hybrid vectors also used in that case? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not really belong in a table comparing the performance of individual models.

I am also unclear about the phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4. Could the authors provide further clarification on this point? Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
