- Proposes using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from an RNN auto-encoder for sentiment classification, called "belief propagation."
- Terminology "belief propagation" is misleading since it is not true belief propagation inference.
- Paper is well-written but lacks clear theoretical justification and has vague motivation.
- Major weakness: evaluation methodology is flawed by presenting an unjustified ensemble result while benchmarks are not ensembles.
- Composition of compared models is unclear, obscuring reproducibility and comparison.
- Authors must detail model tuning, ensemble selection, "best model" criteria, and tuning for benchmarks in Table 2.
- Need to clarify whether benchmarks used the same augmented data and whether RNN/Bi-RNN used hybrid word vectors.
- Without clarifications, the second entry in Table 3 seems the most comparable to Tables 1 and 2 for BP-RNN assessment.
- Using an off-the-shelf parser for tweets may be inappropriate; parser accuracy on this data should be reported.
- Several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) and other references are incomplete.
- Overall: model shows reasonable phrasal sentiment results but lacks strong theoretical motivation and clearer experimental methodology.
