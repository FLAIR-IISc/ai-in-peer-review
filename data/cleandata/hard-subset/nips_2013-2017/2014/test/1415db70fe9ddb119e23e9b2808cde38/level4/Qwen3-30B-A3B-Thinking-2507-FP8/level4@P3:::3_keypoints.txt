- Introduces GB-RNNs with a top-down "feedbackward" inference pass to propagate global sentence context to phrase-level representations.  
- Demonstrates GB-RNNs outperform standard RNNs by ~3% F1 on SemEval 2013, surpassing the competition winner.  
- Presents a hybrid word vector approach (fixed unsupervised + task-specific fine-tuning) that consistently improves performance.  
- Uses dropout on high-dimensional vectors and achieves state-of-the-art without heavy hand-crafted features.  
- Model description is clear and uses the Stanford Parser for tree construction.  
- Main weakness: insufficient theoretical justification for why the top-down feedback is superior to alternatives (bidirectional RNNs, attention).  
- The term "global belief" is misleading and conflates the method with probabilistic belief propagation.  
- Evaluation issues: ensemble "best model" obscures single-model performance relative to other systems.  
- Baseline tuning is unclear—uncertain whether all models used identical hybrid vectors, dropout, and data augmentations, possibly inflating reported gaps.  
- If baselines used different vectors (e.g., purely supervised), the reported 5% gap may be exaggerated.  
- Parser accuracy on noisy Twitter data is not reported, raising concerns about tree reliability.  
- Irsoy & Cardie citation is incomplete.  
- Claim that feedback reaches context "many words away" is overstated—context range depends on parse tree depth, not absolute token distance.  
- Originality: novel application of recursive feedback to compositional NLP but somewhat incremental given related bidirectional RNN work and insufficiently differentiated.  
- Recommendation: weak accept — strong empirical results but needs clarifications for theoretical justification and reproducibility.  
- Requested revisions: rename "global belief" (e.g., "contextual feedback") and distinguish from belief propagation; explicitly state baseline tuning procedures; report parser accuracy on Twitter; strengthen theoretical analysis comparing information flow to bidirectional RNNs.
