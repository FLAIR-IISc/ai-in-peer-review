Recursive neural networks (RNNs) in NLP construct representations of text by recursively combining word or sub-snippet representations, starting from the word level and proceeding up a predefined parse tree to represent the entire snippet. However, this unidirectional approach limits information flow from broader to narrower contexts. This paper presents a bidirectional extension to RNNs, incorporating a downward pass following the standard upward pass, thereby enriching node representations with information from larger contexts. The proposed system achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2.

This paper’s extension of existing RNN methods is a simple, elegant, and effective contribution. While the core concept shares similarities with work in [18], the downward pass computation differs, leading to improved performance. 

The method is well-justified and clearly explained, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are strong, though some aspects of the experimental setup warrant clarification. Specifically, was dropout and the additional training data used to obtain the results for the bidirectional RNN model from [18] presented in Table 2? Were hybrid vectors also employed in that instance? Furthermore, the ensemble result, while notable, may be more appropriately presented outside of a table focused on individual model performance. 

Finally, the phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 requires further definition. Overall, this is a well-executed paper grounded in a simple and elegant idea.
