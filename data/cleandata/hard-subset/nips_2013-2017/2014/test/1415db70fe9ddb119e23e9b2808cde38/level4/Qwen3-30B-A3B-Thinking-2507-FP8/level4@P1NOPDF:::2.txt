

In natural language processing, recursive neural networks (RNNs) generate text snippet representations by recursively combining word or short snippet embeddings along a predefined parse tree, starting from word-level representations and progressing upward to the root node. However, this unidirectional approach prevents information from propagating downward from larger to smaller contextual units. This paper proposes a bidirectional extension where an upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, enriching all node representations with contextual information from larger segments. The resulting model achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2.

The paper’s extension of the established RNN framework is simple, elegant, and effective. While the high-level concept bears resemblance to [18], the downward-pass computation differs significantly, and the reported performance clearly surpasses that of the prior work.

The method is well-motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are strong, but I have concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, were the results for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] (reported in Table 2) obtained using dropout and the additional training data? Were hybrid word vectors also employed in that case? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not belong in a table comparing individual model performances.

Additionally, the phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 is unclear. What specific combination is being referenced?

Overall, this is a well-executed paper presenting a simple yet effective idea.
