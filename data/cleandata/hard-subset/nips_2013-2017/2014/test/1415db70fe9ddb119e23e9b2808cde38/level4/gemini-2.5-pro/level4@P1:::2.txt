This paper addresses a key limitation of standard Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) in natural language processing. In a typical RNN, semantic representations are composed in a feedforward, bottom-up fashion along a parse tree. This architecture prevents information from the broader sentence context from influencing the representations of smaller constituents like words or phrases. The authors propose the Global Belief Recursive Neural Network (GB-RNN), a bidirectional extension that adds a top-down, "feedbackward" pass after the initial bottom-up pass. This allows global sentence-level information to refine the local representations of every node in the tree. The proposed model is evaluated on the task of contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art results on Task 2 of the SemEval 2013 challenge.

The proposed extension is simple, elegant, and empirically effective. The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and presents a compelling case for the need for bidirectional information flow in compositional models. The high-level idea is similar to the bidirectional RNN proposed by Irsoy and Cardie [12], but the authors' specific implementation of the backward pass is novel and, based on the reported results, appears to be superior.

The experimental results are strong, but the presentation could be improved with a few clarifications. To ensure a fair comparison with the bidirectional RNN baseline from Irsoy and Cardie [12], it would be helpful for the authors to state explicitly in the main text that this baseline was also trained using the same hybrid word vectors and dropout settings as the proposed GB-RNN. While Section 4.3 seems to imply this, an explicit confirmation would strengthen the claim of superiority. Additionally, while the ensemble result reported in Table 1 is impressive, its placement in a table comparing various systems could be made clearer; distinguishing between single-model and ensemble performance would help avoid potential confusion.

Overall, this is a well-executed paper built on a simple yet powerful idea. The proposed GB-RNN is a valuable contribution to the literature on compositional semantics, and the model's effectiveness is demonstrated through strong experimental results. With minor clarifications to the experimental setup, this paper would be an excellent contribution to the conference.
