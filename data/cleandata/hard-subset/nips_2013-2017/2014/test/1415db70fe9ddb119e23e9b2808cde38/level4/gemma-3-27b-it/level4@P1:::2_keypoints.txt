- Paper introduces a bidirectional extension of recursive neural networks that adds a downward pass to propagate context from larger to smaller tree nodes.  
- Method augments node representations with larger-context information and yields state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2.  
- The extension is simple, elegant, effective, and clearly presented.  
- Conceptually similar to Irsoy & Cardie [12], but uses a different downward computation and appears to outperform that prior work.  
- Experimental results are excellent, but some details of the experimental setup are unclear.  
- Question: Was the reported Irsoy & Cardie baseline in Table 2 trained with dropout and the additional training data described in Section 4.4?  
- Question: Were the hybrid word vectors also used for the Irsoy & Cardie baseline?  
- The ensemble result is impressive but presenting it alongside individual-model results in the same table is somewhat misleading.  
- Question: The phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- Overall assessment: a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
