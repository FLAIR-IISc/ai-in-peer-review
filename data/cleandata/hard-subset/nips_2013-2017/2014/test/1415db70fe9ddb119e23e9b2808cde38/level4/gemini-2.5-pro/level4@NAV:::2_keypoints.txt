- Identifies limitation of standard RNNs: bottom-up-only flow prevents higher-level context from refining lower-level constituent representations.
- Proposes a bidirectional RNN that adds a downward pass to propagate global context down the parse tree.
- Shows state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.
- Extension praised as simple, elegant, and effective; downward-pass computation is novel despite high-level similarity to prior work [18].
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and strongly motivated.
- Experimental concern: unclear whether the baseline bidirectional model from [18] was evaluated under identical conditions (dropout, extra training data, hybrid vectors); equitable comparison required.
- Concern that including an ensemble model in a table comparing single-model architectures is unconventional and potentially misleading.
- Requests clarification in Section 4.4 on what is meant by the "widely used mix of both."
