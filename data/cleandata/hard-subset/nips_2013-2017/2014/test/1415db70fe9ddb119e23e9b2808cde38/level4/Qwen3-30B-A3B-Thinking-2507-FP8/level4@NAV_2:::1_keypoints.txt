- Introduces GB-RNNs that add a top-down feedbackward step to standard recursive neural nets for context-aware phrase-level sentiment classification.  
- Addresses limitation of prior RNNs by allowing phrase representations to incorporate global sentence context (e.g., aspect-specific sentiment).  
- Shows a 3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs on SemEval 2013 Task 2 and reports state-of-the-art results.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors (unsupervised + task-specific) that outperform purely unsupervised or purely task-specific embeddings.  
- Technical novelty: top-down feedbackward propagation with node-dependent transformations (Eq.5) distinct from Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional RNN.  
- Theoretical justification for the feedbackward step is clear and formally presented (Section 3.3).  
- Empirical validation is rigorous, with ablation studies confirming contributions of hybrid vectors and dropout.  
- Paper is well-organized and clear, with precise definitions, helpful examples, and transparent experimental setup.  
- Original contribution: global belief mechanism linking compositional semantics to context-dependent sentiment disambiguation.  
- Broader significance: solves entity-specific sentiment issues relevant to social media and can generalize to tasks like word-sense disambiguation.  
- Weakness: Section 3.5 (Training) needs expansion and should cite backpropagation through structure to clarify how embeddings are updated.  
- Weakness: Comparison to Irsoy & Cardie needs quantification of the 2n·nd parameter overhead and clearer empirical impact (e.g., contextualizing the 1% accuracy gain).  
- Weakness: Paper should more explicitly state real-world importance of context-dependent sentiment beyond SemEval 2013.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically sound and significant, with minor revisions to Sections 3.5 and 3.6 suggested.
