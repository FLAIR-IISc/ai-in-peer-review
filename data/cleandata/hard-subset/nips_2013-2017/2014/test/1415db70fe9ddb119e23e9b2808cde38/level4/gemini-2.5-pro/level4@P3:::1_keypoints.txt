- Proposes GB-RNN: a two-pass tree RNN (feedforward bottom-up then feedbackward top-down) to refine node representations using global sentence context.
- Motivated by aspect-specific sentiment where word meaning depends on full-sentence context (e.g., "Android beats iOS").
- Introduces a hybrid word vector combining pre-trained unsupervised vectors with task-specific supervised vectors.
- Shows dropout effectively regularizes recursive neural networks.
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 Task 2, with a 3% F1 gain from the feedback pass and thorough ablations versus strong baselines.
- Paper is generally well-written and well-illustrated, with clear motivation and presentation.
- The name "Global Belief Recursive Neural Network" is misleading (invokes belief propagation); a name like "Forward-Backward RNN" would be clearer.
- Needs a clearer, more insightful comparison to Bidirectional RNNs (Irsoy & Cardie 2013) and explanation why the proposed update rule is superior.
- Training details are insufficient—need explanation of unfolding, network depth, and gradient propagation to embeddings for reproducibility.
- Hyperparameter tuning is under-specified; must report search ranges, selection procedure, and show baselines were tuned comparably.
- State-of-the-art result relies on an ensemble of five models; this should be explicitly acknowledged and single-model comparisons emphasized.
- Task description assumes SemEval familiarity and should better explain the contextual phrase polarity subtask for a general audience.
- Experimental organization could be improved by merging related sections (e.g., baselines and additional data) to improve narrative flow.
- Overall assessment: interesting and effective idea with strong results, but clarity on training, comparisons, and prior work is needed to strengthen the contribution.
