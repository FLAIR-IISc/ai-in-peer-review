- Introduces a bidirectional RNN for NLP that adds a downward pass to augment node representations with larger-context information.
- Notes that standard RNNs are bottom-up and cannot propagate information from larger contexts back to smaller constituents.
- Proposed method performs an upward pass followed by a downward pass to propagate parent context to children.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.
- Extension is simple, elegant, and effective.
- Approach resembles prior work [18] but uses a different downward computation and shows superior performance.
- Paper is well motivated, clearly presented, and generally well written.
- Questions about experimental setup: whether dropout and additional training data were also used for the [18] baseline in Table 2.
- Asks whether hybrid vectors were used for that baseline comparison.
- Criticizes inclusion of the ensemble result in a table comparing individual models.
- Requests clarification in Section 4.4 about what “widely used mix of both” refers to.
- Overall assessment: a well-executed paper built on a simple, elegant idea.
