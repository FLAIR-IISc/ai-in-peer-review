- Proposes GB-RNN: standard bottom-up tree RNN plus a single top-down “feedback” pass at inference to incorporate global sentence context for node labeling.
- Evaluates on SemEval 2013 Task 2, reporting gains over standard RNN and bidirectional-recursive baseline and state-of-the-art with an ensemble.
- Adds a hybrid word representation (fixed unsupervised + small supervised embeddings) and uses dropout for high-dimensional embeddings.
- Strength: addresses feedforward RNNs’ lack of global context with a simple architectural change and minimal overhead.
- Strength: clear model description and practical focus on entity/aspect sentiment in noisy social media text.
- Strength: empirical gains and evidence that hybrid embeddings outperform purely supervised/unsupervised vectors.
- Weakness: the name “global belief” misleadingly evokes belief propagation in graphical models.
- Weakness: motivation relies on examples; lacks deeper theoretical justification for why a single top-down pass reliably improves local predictions.
- Weakness: evaluation transparency issues—headline ensemble results obscure single-model performance and comparability to baselines.
- Weakness: unclear tuning protocol—how hyperparameters, dev selection, ensembles, seeds, and variance were handled for all models.
- Weakness: resource parity unclear—did baselines use the same hybrid vectors and extra resources (NRC lexicon, distant supervision, reviews)?
- Weakness: unclear whether SemEval leaderboard systems had access to the same external data; fairness of comparisons is questionable.
- Weakness: parsing fairness—using an off-the-shelf parser on tweets may bias results; parser accuracy/error rates on this domain aren’t reported.
- Weakness: some citations (e.g., Irsoy & Cardie) are incomplete and need fixing.
- Quality: model is technically sound and training details are reasonable, but experimental methodology and ablations need more documentation.
- Suggested ablations: remove V transform, vary bottom-up vs. top-down dimensions, use only top-down vectors, test multiple top-down steps to isolate contributions.
- Clarity: writing is generally clear but recommend replacing “belief propagation” with “top-down contextual pass” and clarifying evaluation/resource usage.
- Originality: approach is incremental and closely related to concurrent bidirectional recursive models; differences are in the backward transform and parameterization.
- Significance: context-sensitive phrase labeling is important and reported improvements are practically valuable if shown robust under fair single-model comparisons.
- Pros for acceptance: simple effective fix to feedforward RNN limitations, strong benchmark results, clear exposition.
- Cons for acceptance: misleading terminology, weak theoretical motivation, incomplete citations, and unclear/possibly unfair experimental comparisons.
- Minor requests: report parser performance on tweets, detail preprocessing (e.g., negation handling), provide full citations, and add error analysis with examples showing top-down effects.
- Overall: promising method but needs clearer motivation, fairer and more transparent experiments, and better documentation of tuning, resources, and parsing reliability.
