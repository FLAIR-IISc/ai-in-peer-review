This paper introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks," which extend traditional feedforward recursive networks by incorporating a backward (top-down) step during inference. This modification enables phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to word embeddings and labels, allowing for more informed predictions.

The paper is well-written, and the model is well-motivated and clearly described. The authors build upon the idea of Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008), with a notable contribution being the introduction of hybrid word vectors. This distinction from previous work is potentially the most significant.

However, the experimental comparisons could be improved. Specifically, it is unclear whether the RNN and B-RNN models also utilized hybrid vectors, and how the "best" models were tuned and selected. Additionally, the authors should clarify how the single best model was chosen. It is worth noting that the other methods did not employ an ensemble approach.

The term "belief propagation" might be misleading, and the term "forward-backward" could be more suitable. Section 3.5 (Training) is somewhat concise and could benefit from additional details. For instance, it appears that the training process employs the back-propagation through structure algorithm (Goller, 1996), which involves unfolding the recursive model. This results in a very deep network to update the word embedding part. The authors could provide more insight into this crucial step.

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing, and the differences between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie should be clarified. Section 4.1 could be improved to make it more accessible to readers outside the NLP community. In the context of Task 2 of Semeval 2013, the authors seem to address the contextual polarity task. A clearer presentation of the task would facilitate understanding, including the parser constraints.

Consider merging sections 4.3 and 4.4, as they appear to be closely related. Overall, the paper is well-written and presents an interesting variant of the Bidirectional recursive model.
