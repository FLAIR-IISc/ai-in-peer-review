

This paper introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks," extending feedforward (bottom-up) recursive networks with a backward (top-down) inference step. This enables phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to word embeddings and labels.  

The paper is well written and the model is well motivated and described. It builds upon Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (Irsoy and Cardie, 2008), though the reference to this work is incomplete. A key contribution is the introduction of hybrid word vectors, which may represent the most significant distinction from prior work. However, experimental comparisons require clarification: were the RNN and B-RNN baselines also trained with hybrid vectors? How were the best models tuned and selected, and how was the single best model determined? Notably, other methods do not employ ensembles, and these points must be addressed.  

The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" would be more appropriate. Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and should provide more detail. Specifically, the training likely employs back-propagation through structure (Goller and Kuchler, 1996), which unfolds the recursive model. This results in a very deep network to update the word embedding layer, and the authors should elaborate on this critical step.  

Section 3.6 is confusing and fails to clarify the distinction between this model and Irsoy and Cardie’s work. Section 4.1 should be improved for broader accessibility: in Semeval 2013 Task 2, there are two subtasks (contextual and message polarity); the paper addresses contextual polarity, and a clearer description of the task and parser constraints would aid understanding. Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for conciseness.  

Overall, the paper is well written and presents an interesting extension of the bidirectional recursive model.
