- Proposes a bidirectional extension to tree-structured RNNs by adding a top-down (downward) pass to propagate higher-level context, achieving SOTA on SemEval 2012 Task 2.
- Contribution is simple, elegant, well-motivated, and differs from prior bidirectional work (notably [18]) in the downward computation design.
- Paper is clearly written and implementation/intuitions are easy to follow; experiments are thorough and report strong numbers.
- Concern about fairness of comparisons to [18]: need to confirm whether that baseline used the same training regime, dropout, additional training data, and hybrid vectors.
- Request matched comparisons: specify if hybrid vectors were used for [18], how they were constructed/integrated, or provide new matched experiments.
- Ensembles vs single models: ensemble results should be separated/clearly marked and compared to competing ensemble baselines if any.
- Clarify “widely used mix of both” (Section 4.4): precisely state what is being mixed, the mixing mechanism (concatenation/gating/interpolation), and mixture weights; include ablation vs pure variants.
- Reporting details requested: report averages and standard deviations over multiple random initializations, compute overhead of the downward pass (train/inference) vs upward-only, and sensitivity to parse quality.
- Additional ablation needed isolating the downward pass effect (same architecture/hyperparameters with vs without top-down component).
- Overall positive assessment: method is compelling and timely; addressing the above clarifications will strengthen and solidify the paper’s claims.
