- Proposes GB-RNNs: recursive neural networks augmented with a feedbackward pass so global sentence context influences word/phrase representations.
- Addresses limitation of standard (feedforward) RNNs when phrase meaning depends on broader context.
- Demonstrates improved aspect-specific sentiment classification (e.g., correctly inferring sentiment toward “Android” in context).
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the SemEval 2013 sentiment challenge.
- Reports gains from using dropout during training and combining unsupervised and supervised (hybrid) word vectors.
- Builds on prior compositional semantics and RNN work and clearly differentiates its approach from bidirectional RNNs.
- Notes a specific technical difference from [12] in how the backward pass is computed and claims superior performance.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes a helpful visual example (Fig. 2).
- Ablation studies (Table 2) effectively demonstrate the contributions of individual GB-RNN components and the hybrid vectors.
- Strengths summarized: novel idea, significant empirical gains, clarity of presentation, and robust experimental support.
- Concern: unclear whether comparisons to bidirectional RNNs (e.g., [18]) used the same experimental settings (dropout, hybrid vectors, extra data).
- Concern: including an ensemble result alongside single-model comparisons is potentially misleading.
- Minor issue: the phrase “widely used mix of both” (Section 4.4) is vague and needs clarification.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with recommendation to accept, pending clarification of experimental comparisons and table presentation.
