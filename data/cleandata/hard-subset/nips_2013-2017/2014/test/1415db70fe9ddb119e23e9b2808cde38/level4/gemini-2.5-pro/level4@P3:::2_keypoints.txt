- Addresses RNNs' limitation of purely bottom-up processing by proposing GB-RNN, which adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass and concatenates feedforward+feedback representations for nodes.  
- Also introduces dropout for regularization and a hybrid word vector (fixed pre-trained + task-tuned) representation.  
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013 aspect-specific sentiment analysis and reports state-of-the-art performance, outperforming the competition winner and strong baselines.  
- Technical quality is high: model is well-motivated, mathematically described, and supported by strong empirical results.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate contributions of the feedbackward pass, hybrid vectors, and dropout.  
- Main experimental concern: unclear if the bidirectional RNN baseline received the same extra training data and two-stage training, which could affect the fairness of the comparison.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized; the comparison to related bidirectional work is helpful.  
- Minor clarity issue: the two-stage training procedure (Section 4.4) needs more detail for reproducibility.  
- Originality: feedbackward mechanism is a novel, distinct contribution from concurrent bidirectional approaches; hybrid vectors are an incremental but practical contribution.  
- Significance: tackles an important limitation of recursive models and offers a general mechanism likely useful across NLP tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel elegant model, state-of-the-art results, high-quality presentation, and significant impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: potential unfair baseline comparison that should be clarified; authors should confirm which training resources/procedures were used for the compared baselines.
