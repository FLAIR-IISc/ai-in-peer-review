- Proposes using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive neural network auto-encoder for sentiment classification.
- Labels the approach “belief propagation,” which is misleading and does not correspond to the standard algorithm.
- Paper is generally well-written but has unclear motivation and weak theoretical grounding.
- Major evaluation weakness: presenting ensemble results is inappropriate given single-model benchmarks and obscures the proposed method’s performance.
- Composition of compared models is insufficiently specified; crucial details on model tuning and selection criteria are missing.
- Unclear how benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned and whether they used the same data augmentation as BP-RNN.
- Unknown whether the RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.
- Given the missing details, the second entry in Table 3 is likely the most directly comparable result to the benchmarks.
- Minor: uses an off-the-shelf tweet parser—its accuracy on this dataset should be reported.
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.
- Overall: presents a promising recursive neural network for contextual phrasal sentiment classification, but motivation and experimental methodology require clarification.
