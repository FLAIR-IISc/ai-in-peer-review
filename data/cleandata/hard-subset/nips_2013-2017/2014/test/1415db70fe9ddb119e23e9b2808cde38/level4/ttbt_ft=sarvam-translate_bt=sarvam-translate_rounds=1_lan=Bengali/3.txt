This paper proposes the use of both encoding and decoding phrase vectors in a recurrent neural network auto-encoder for sentiment classification. The authors refer to it as belief propagation, although it is not an example of this inference algorithm, and as such, the name may mislead the reader. In general, it is a well-written paper, but the purpose behind the proposed model is unclear, and it lacks a clear theoretical justification.

The main weakness of this paper is the evaluation. There is no reason to present an ensemble result in the context of this evaluation; no benchmark is an ensemble, and this makes the result ambiguous. The exact structure of the models being compared is also not clear. The key questions that should be clarified are: how were the models tuned, and how were the ensemble/best models selected? How were the benchmarks in Table 2 tuned? Did they use the same augmented data as the BP-RNN? Did the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN also use hybrid word vectors?

Due to this lack of information, I would speculate that the second entry in Table 3 is most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 when evaluating the BP-RNN architecture.

A few minor points:
- I was surprised that an off-the-shelf parser was used to parse tweets. I would be interested to know how accurate it is in this data.
- The Irsoy and Cardie citation is incomplete (as are several others).

This paper presents a recurrent neural network model for classifying phrasal sentiment in relevant contexts. The results seem reasonable, but the model does not have a strong purpose, and the experimental method is not entirely clear.
