- Proposes "belief propagation recursive neural networks" that add a backward inference step to feedphrase-forward recursive nets, enabling phrase-level predictions to influence word embeddings and labels.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and model description.  
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008) bidirectional recursive networks; must clarify whether hybrid word vectors are the primary novel contribution.  
- Introduces hybrid word vectors—need explicit statement if this is the main distinction from prior work.  
- Experimental comparisons are unclear about whether RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors and how they were tuned.  
- Clarify model selection/tuning: how "best" models were chosen and how a single best model was selected (ensembles vs single-model baselines).  
- The term "belief propagation" is potentially misleading; "forward-backward" may be a more accurate label.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; expand on using backpropagation through structure (Goller 1996), unfolding the recursive model, and updating embeddings.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and needs a clearer differentiation from Irsoy & Cardie’s work.  
- Section 4.1 should be clarified for non-NLP readers—explicitly state Semeval 2013 subtasks and confirm the paper addresses contextual polarity, including parser constraints.  
- Consider merging sections 4.3 and 4.4 for concision.  
- Overall, an interesting and worthwhile variant of bidirectional recursive models, but several clarifications and experimental details are required.
