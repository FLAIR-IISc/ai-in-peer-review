This paper proposes a novel extension of recursive neural networks (RNNs) for contextual sentiment analysis, introducing global belief recursive neural networks (GB-RNNs). The main idea is to include a feedbackward step during inference, allowing phrase-level predictions and representations to give feedback to words. This approach aims to address the limitation of standard RNNs in capturing contextual information.

The paper relates to previous work on RNNs and sentiment analysis, but the authors could provide more theoretical justification for their proposed model. The GB-RNN architecture is well-explained, and the use of hybrid word vector representations is an interesting contribution. However, the experimental methodology and evaluation could be improved.

The strengths of the paper include its clear explanation of the GB-RNN model, its ability to capture contextual information, and its state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge. The authors also provide a thorough comparison with other models, including the bidirectional RNN.

However, there are several weaknesses and areas for improvement. The evaluation could be more transparent, with clearer explanations of how the models were tuned and selected. The composition of the models compared is not entirely clear, and it is unclear whether the benchmarks were using the same augmented data as the GB-RNN. The use of an ensemble result in the evaluation also obfuscates the comparison with other models.

Minor points include the lack of clarity on the accuracy of the off-the-shelf parser used to parse tweets and the incomplete citation of Irsoy and Cardie's work.

In terms of the NIPS evaluation criteria, the paper scores well on originality, as it proposes a novel extension of RNNs for contextual sentiment analysis. The paper also scores well on significance, as it addresses an important problem in natural language processing and achieves state-of-the-art performance on a challenging task. However, the paper could improve on clarity, as some aspects of the experimental methodology and evaluation are unclear. The paper also scores well on quality, as the GB-RNN model is well-explained and the results are reasonable.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel extension of RNNs for contextual sentiment analysis.
* The GB-RNN model is well-explained and achieves state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge.
* The paper provides a thorough comparison with other models.
