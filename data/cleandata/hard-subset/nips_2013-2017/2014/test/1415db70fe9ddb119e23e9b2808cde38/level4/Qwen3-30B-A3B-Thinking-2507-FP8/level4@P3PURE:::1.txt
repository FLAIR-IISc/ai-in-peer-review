

This paper introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks," which extend feedforward (bottom-up) recursive networks by incorporating a backward (top-down) step during inference. This enables phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to word embeddings and labels.  

The paper is generally well written, with a clear model motivation and description. It builds upon the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks framework introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008), though the reference to this work is incomplete. A key contribution is the introduction of hybrid word vectors, which may represent the most significant distinction from prior work. However, experimental comparisons require clarification:  
- Were the RNN and B-RNN baselines also trained with hybrid vectors?  
- How were the "best" models tuned and selected?  
- How was the single best model chosen (as opposed to ensemble methods used by others)?  
These points must be addressed to ensure a fair comparison.  

The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" would be a more appropriate and standard descriptor. Section 3.5 (Training) is overly brief and requires additional detail. Specifically, the training procedure likely employs backpropagation through structure (Goller & Kümmel, 1996), which unfolds the recursive model into a deep network. This implies that the word embedding layer—updated during training—is effectively reached via a very deep path. The authors should elaborate on this critical step.  

Section 3.6 is confusing and fails to clearly differentiate this model from Irsoy and Cardie’s work. Section 4.1 must be revised for broader accessibility: Semeval 2013 Task 2 comprises two subtasks (contextual and message polarity); the paper addresses contextual polarity. A clearer explanation of the task and parser constraints would greatly aid readers outside NLP.  

Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for improved flow. Overall, the paper is well written and presents an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model.
