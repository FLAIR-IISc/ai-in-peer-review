- Method is misnamed: calling the model "belief propagation" is misleading and likely to confuse readers.  
- Paper is generally well-written but model motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.  
- Major experimental weakness: reporting an ensemble result is inappropriate and makes comparisons to non-ensemble benchmarks unfair.  
- Experimental setup lacks sufficient detail, harming interpretability and reproducibility.  
- Unclear hyperparameter tuning and model selection procedures for both single best model and ensemble.  
- Unclear tuning process for the benchmark models in Table 2.  
- Unclear whether benchmark models were trained on the same augmented data as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear if baseline RNN and Bi-RNN used the hybrid word vectors.  
- In absence of clarifications, the second entry in Table 3 is the most direct comparison to Tables 1 & 2.  
- Minor: surprising choice to use an off-the-shelf tweet parser; parser accuracy on this noisy dataset should be reported.  
- Minor: several citations are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).  
- Overall: results seem reasonable but motivation must be strengthened and experimental methodology clarified before the contribution can be fully assessed.
