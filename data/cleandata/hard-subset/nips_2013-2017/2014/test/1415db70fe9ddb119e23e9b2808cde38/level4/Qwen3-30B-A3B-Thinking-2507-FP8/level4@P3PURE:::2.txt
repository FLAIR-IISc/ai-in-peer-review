

This paper introduces a bidirectional extension to recursive neural networks (RNNs) for text representation learning. In standard RNN-based approaches, representations are built by recursively combining word or snippet representations along a pre-specified parse tree, starting from leaves and proceeding upward to the root. However, this unidirectional process cannot propagate information downward from larger contexts to smaller ones. The proposed method addresses this by performing an upward pass followed by a downward pass through the tree, augmenting each node's representation with contextual information from larger subtrees. This simple, elegant, and effective extension achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2.

The core idea, while conceptually similar to [18], differs in its downward pass computation and yields superior performance. The method is well-motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are strong, but I have concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, were the results for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2 obtained using dropout and the additional training data? Were hybrid vectors also employed in that case? Additionally, while the ensemble result is impressive, it is inappropriate for a table comparing individual models.

The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 requires clarification. Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
