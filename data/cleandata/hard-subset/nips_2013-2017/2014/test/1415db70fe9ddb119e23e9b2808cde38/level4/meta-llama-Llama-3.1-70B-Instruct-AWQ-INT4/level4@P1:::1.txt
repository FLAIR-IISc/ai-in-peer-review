This paper introduces "Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks" (GB-RNNs), which extend the traditional feedforward recursive neural networks with a backward (top-down) step during inference. This allows phrase-level predictions and representations to give feedback to word embeddings and labels. The paper is well-written, and the model is well-motivated and described.

The GB-RNN model is an extension of the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie. One of the key contributions of this paper is the introduction of hybrid word vectors, which combine unsupervised and supervised word vector representations. This approach is shown to perform better than both purely supervised and unsupervised semantic word vectors.

The experimental comparisons are thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the GB-RNN model on the task of contextual sentiment analysis. However, some clarifications are needed to ensure fairness in the comparisons. Specifically, it is unclear whether the RNN and Bidirectional RNN models also used hybrid vectors. Additionally, the process of tuning and selecting the "best" models could be more transparent. It is also worth noting that the other methods do not use an ensemble, which may affect the comparison.

The term "Global Belief" might be misleading, and the term "forward-backward" could be more suitable. Section 3.5 on training is brief and could provide more details on the back-propagation through structure algorithm used. Specifically, it would be helpful to clarify how the recursive model is unfolded and how this affects the training process.

Section 3.6 could be improved to clarify the differences between the GB-RNN model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie. Section 4.1 could be rewritten to make the task and parser constraints more accessible to readers outside the NLP community. It would be helpful to explicitly state that the paper addresses the contextual polarity task in the Semeval 2013 challenge.

Finally, sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged to improve the flow of the paper.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting variant of the Bidirectional recursive model, and the results demonstrate its effectiveness on the task of contextual sentiment analysis.

Strengths:

* The GB-RNN model is well-motivated and described.
* The introduction of hybrid word vectors is a key contribution.
* The experimental comparisons are thorough, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of the GB-RNN model.

Weaknesses:

* Some clarifications are needed to ensure fairness in the comparisons.
* The term "Global Belief" might be misleading.
* Section 3.5 on training could provide more details.
* Section 3.6 could be improved to clarify the differences between the GB-RNN model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie.
* Section 4.1 could be rewritten to make the task and parser constraints more accessible to readers outside the NLP community.

* The paper presents a novel and effective model for contextual sentiment analysis.
* The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the GB-RNN model on a challenging task.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
