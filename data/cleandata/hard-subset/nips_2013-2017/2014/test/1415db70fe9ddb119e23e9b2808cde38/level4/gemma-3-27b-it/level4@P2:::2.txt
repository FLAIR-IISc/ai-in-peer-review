This paper introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), an extension to standard Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) designed to address limitations in capturing contextual information for tasks like aspect-specific sentiment classification. Standard RNNs operate in a feedforward manner, composing meaning from smaller constituents to larger phrases. However, this unidirectional approach struggles with scenarios where phrase meaning is determined by broader context. GB-RNNs augment this feedforward pass with a feedbackward step, allowing information from the overall sentence context to influence the representations of individual words and phrases. This allows the model to, for example, correctly identify positive sentiment towards “Android” in the sentence “Android beats iOS.” The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach on contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art results on the SemEval 2013 challenge and showcasing the model’s ability to accurately predict sentiment towards specific entities. They also report improvements from utilizing dropout during training and combining unsupervised and supervised word vector representations.

The core idea of incorporating a feedback mechanism into recursive neural networks to capture global context is both intuitive and valuable. This addresses a known limitation of standard RNNs, as highlighted in the introduction. The paper builds upon a strong foundation of prior work in compositional semantics [27, 11, 15, 25, 28, 24, 10] and recursive neural networks [26], presenting a novel and effective extension. While bidirectional RNNs and recursive neural networks [8, 12, 14, 21] have been explored, the authors clearly differentiate their approach. Notably, their method differs from [12] in how the backward pass is computed, as detailed in Section 3.6, and appears to offer superior performance. The presentation is clear and well-organized, making the method accessible and easy to understand. The inclusion of a visual example (Fig. 2) aids in comprehension.

The strengths of this paper lie in its elegant simplicity, strong motivation, and impressive experimental results. Achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 task is a significant contribution. The ablation studies (Table 2) effectively demonstrate the individual contributions of the GB-RNN’s components and the benefits of the hybrid word vector approach. The addition of dropout is also a valuable insight. 

However, a few points warrant further clarification. While the comparison to [18] is discussed, it would be helpful to know if the results reported in Table 2 for the bidirectional RNN from that work were obtained using the same experimental setup (dropout, hybrid vectors, additional training data) as the GB-RNN. Ensuring a fair comparison is crucial. Additionally, the inclusion of an ensemble result in a table comparing single models is somewhat misleading. While the ensemble performance is noteworthy, it doesn't directly reflect the capabilities of the core GB-RNN architecture. The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 is vague and should be clarified.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Introduces a novel and effective extension to RNNs that addresses a key limitation in contextual sentiment analysis.
*   **Significance:** Achieves state-of-the-art results on a challenging benchmark dataset (SemEval 2013).
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, clearly explains the method, and provides helpful visualizations.
*   **Soundness:** The experimental results are robust and supported by ablation studies.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Experimental Clarity:** A more detailed comparison to existing bidirectional RNN models (like [18]) with consistent experimental settings is needed.
*   **Table Consistency:** The inclusion of ensemble results in a table comparing individual models is somewhat misleading.
*   **Minor Ambiguity:** The phrase "widely used mix of both" requires clarification.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field. The proposed GB-RNN effectively addresses a limitation of standard RNNs and demonstrates impressive performance on a challenging task. Addressing the minor concerns regarding experimental clarity and table presentation would further strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance.
