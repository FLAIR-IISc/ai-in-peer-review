- Proposes a novel sentiment classification method using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive neural network auto-encoder.  
- Uses the term "belief propagation" misleadingly, which may confuse readers due to mismatch with the traditional algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written but lacks clear theoretical justification and motivation for the proposed model.  
- Evaluation methodology is problematic: presenting ensemble results is unnecessary and obscures outcomes since benchmarks are not ensembles.  
- Composition of compared models is not explicitly stated, reducing clarity on what is being compared.  
- Authors must detail model tuning, selection, and benchmark tuning (Table 2) to ensure fair comparison.  
- Unclear whether benchmarks used the same augmented data as BP-RNN and whether RNN/Bi-RNN used hybrid word vectors.  
- Based on available info, the second entry in Table 3 is the most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 for BP-RNN evaluation.  
- Minor issues: using an off-the-shelf parser for tweets may be inappropriate, and some citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Overall: the recursive neural network yields reasonable results but requires clearer motivation and improved experimental methodology to strengthen contribution.
