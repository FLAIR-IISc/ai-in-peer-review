- Introduces GB-RNN: adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass to standard feedforward recursive neural nets to propagate global sentence context down the parse tree.  
- Node predictions use concatenated feedforward and feedbackward vectors to refine local representations with global context.  
- Proposes and evaluates dropout for RNN training and a hybrid word-vector scheme combining pre-trained unsupervised and task-specific supervised vectors.  
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013 aspect-specific sentiment task, comparing to competition winners and other recursive models (including Irsoy & Cardie 2013).  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important limitation of compositional models by targeting context-dependent phenomena in aspect-specific sentiment analysis.  
- The two-pass architecture is intuitive and a novel extension that improves contextual refinement over pure feedforward RNNs.  
- Strong empirical results: GB-RNN achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013, outperforming prior winners and strong baselines.  
- Auxiliary findings (dropout helps; hybrid vectors outperform single-source vectors) are useful contributions.  

Weaknesses:
- Experimental methodology lacks clarity, making it hard to isolate whether gains come from architecture or training/data enhancements.  
- Headline results use an ensemble, obscuring single-model GB-RNN performance and complicating fair comparisons to non-ensemble baselines.  
- Crucial training details for baseline models are omitted (use of augmented data, hybrid vectors, dropout, hyperparameter tuning), preventing attribution of the reported ~5% F1 gain.  
- Theoretical motivation for specific update rules (Eq. 3 and 5) and the design of the de-composition matrix W↓ is weak and under-justified.  
- The name "Global Belief" is misleading since the method is not belief propagation; a name emphasizing contextual feedback would be clearer.  

Minor points:
- Using an off-the-shelf parser for tweets is questionable; the parser's domain accuracy should be reported.  
- Several bibliography entries are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie, 2013).  

Summary:
- Novel, effective recursive architecture with strong empirical performance, but the paper needs more transparent and rigorous baseline comparisons to fully support claims about the architectural contribution.
