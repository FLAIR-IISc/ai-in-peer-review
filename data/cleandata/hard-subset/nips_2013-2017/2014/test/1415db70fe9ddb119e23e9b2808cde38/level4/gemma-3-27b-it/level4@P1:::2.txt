In NLP, recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been used to produce representations of text snippets by recursively combining pairs of representations of words or shorter snippets. This process starts with the representation of words and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree until the representation of the entire snippet is obtained at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process prevents information from propagating down the tree, from larger to smaller contexts. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs in which the upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, augmenting the representations of all nodes with information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 Task 2.

The extension of the established RNN-based approach proposed in this paper is simple, elegant, and effective. While the high-level idea is similar to that of Irsoy and Cardie [12], the computation performed during the downward pass differs, and the resulting performance appears superior. 

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are excellent, though I have some questions regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, was the result for the bidirectional RNN model from Irsoy and Cardie [12] reported in Table 2 obtained using dropout and the additional training data described in Section 4.4? Were the hybrid word vectors also used in that case? Additionally, while the ensemble result is impressive, presenting it in a table comparing the performance of individual models is somewhat misleading.

Finally, what does the phrase “widely used mix of both” refer to in Section 4.4? Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
