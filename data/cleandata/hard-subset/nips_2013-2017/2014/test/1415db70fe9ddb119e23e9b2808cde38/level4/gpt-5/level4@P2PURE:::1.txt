This paper introduces “belief propagation recursive neural networks,” which extend standard feedforward (bottom-up) recursive networks with a backward (top-down) inference step. This top-down pass lets phrase-level predictions and embeddings feed back into the word embeddings and labels.

Overall, the paper is well written; the model is well motivated and clearly described. It extends the idea of Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008; note that the citation appears incomplete). One contribution is the introduction of hybrid word vectors—perhaps the most important difference from previous work?

However, the experimental comparisons do not seem fully fair or transparent. Specifically:
- Did the RNN and B-RNN baselines also use hybrid word vectors?
- How were the “best” models tuned and selected?
- How was the single best model chosen?
- It is worth noting that other methods do not use an ensemble.

These issues should be clarified. In addition, the term “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; “forward–backward” might be a more appropriate term.

Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and should provide more detail. I assume training uses backpropagation through structure (Goller, 1996), which entails unfolding the recursive model; this results in a very deep network to reach and update the word-embedding component. If I understand correctly, the paper should elaborate on this crucial step and cite Goller (1996).

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing; the differences between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie need to be clarified.

Section 4.1 should be made accessible to readers outside the NLP community. SemEval 2013 Task 2 contains two subtasks: contextual polarity and message polarity. I assume you address the contextual polarity task. A clearer presentation of the task would also help readers understand the parser constraints.

Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could potentially be merged.

In sum, this is a well-written paper that presents an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model.
