- Introduces GB-RNNs that extend feedforward recursive neural nets by adding a feedbackward/top-down inference step.  
- Feedbackward step propagates root/phrase-level information back to leaf nodes, allowing phrase predictions to update word embeddings and labels.  
- Model evaluated on contextual sentiment analysis and shown to accurately predict sentiment of specific entities.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors (unsupervised + supervised) that outperform either representation alone.  
- Experimental comparisons (including bidirectional RNN) are useful but unclear whether baselines used hybrid word vectors.  
- Unclear how "best" models were tuned and selected; model selection/tuning details needed.  
- Term "global belief" may be misleading; "forward-backward" could be a clearer name.  
- Section 3.5 (training) is too brief and needs more detail on back-propagation through structure.  
- Section 3.6 should better clarify differences from Irsoy and Cardie’s work.  
- Section 4.1 should give more context about the task/dataset (clarify it’s the Semeval 2013 contextual polarity task).  
- Strengths: novel model extending state-of-the-art, well-motivated description, effective results, and valuable hybrid word-vector contribution.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient training/comparison details and some clarity/readability issues.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
