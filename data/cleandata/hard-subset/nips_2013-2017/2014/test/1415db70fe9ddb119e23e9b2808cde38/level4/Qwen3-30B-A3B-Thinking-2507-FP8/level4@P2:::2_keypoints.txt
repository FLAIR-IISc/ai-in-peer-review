- Introduces Global Belief RNNs (GB-RNNs) that add a top-down (downward) pass through parse trees to standard bottom-up RNNs.  
- GB-RNNs propagate global sentence context downward so phrase/word representations are updated by full-sentence information.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains (≈3–5% F1) and state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 contextual sentiment.  
- Clearly motivates the problem: standard RNNs fail at context-dependent sentiment (entity-specific polarity changes).  
- The feedbackward/downward step is elegant, simple, and effectively addresses context-dependence.  
- Properly situates and compares to prior work, especially Irsoy & Cardie (2013), and explains how GB-RNN differs and outperforms it.  
- Uses a hybrid word vector representation (unsupervised + supervised) that outperforms pure alternatives.  
- Experimental setup is generally sound with appropriate baselines, dropout, and careful hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strong theoretical comparison with related work and clear illustrative examples (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Writing and organization are excellent and accessible to NLP researchers.  
- Concerns: Table 2 comparison with Irsoy & Cardie lacks clarity on whether experimental settings (dropout, hybrid vectors, extra data) were matched.  
- Concerns: Ensemble result should not be mixed into the single-model comparison table.  
- Concern: The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Significance: Approach is generalizable to other context-dependent NLP tasks (e.g., word-sense disambiguation) and important for social-media entity sentiment.  
- Novelty: While bidirectional processing is not wholly new, the specific feedbackward implementation is original and well-differentiated.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper with minor clarifications needed; recommend acceptance.
