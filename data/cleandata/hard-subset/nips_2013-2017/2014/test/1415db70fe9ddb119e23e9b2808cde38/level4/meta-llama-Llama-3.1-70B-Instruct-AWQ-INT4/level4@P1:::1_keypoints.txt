- Proposes GB-RNNs: recursive neural nets with an added backward (top-down) inference step allowing phrase-level feedback to word embeddings and labels.  
- Paper is well-written and the model is clearly motivated and described.  
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie’s Bidirectional RNNs; main novelty is hybrid word vectors combining supervised and unsupervised embeddings.  
- Hybrid word vectors outperform purely supervised or unsupervised vectors.  
- Experiments are thorough and show GB-RNN effectiveness on contextual sentiment analysis (SemEval 2013 contextual polarity).  
- Unclear whether baselines (RNN and BiRNN) used hybrid vectors; fairness of comparisons and absence of ensembles need clarification.  
- Tuning and model selection procedures are not transparently reported.  
- The term “Global Belief” may be misleading; “forward-backward” is a clearer alternative.  
- Section 3.5 (training) is too brief; needs details on backpropagation through structure and how the recursive model is unfolded.  
- Section 3.6 should more clearly distinguish GB-RNN from Irsoy & Cardie’s work.  
- Section 4.1 should be rewritten to explain the task and parser constraints for non-NLP readers and explicitly reference the SemEval 2013 task.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged to improve the paper’s flow.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions addressing the above clarification and presentation issues.
