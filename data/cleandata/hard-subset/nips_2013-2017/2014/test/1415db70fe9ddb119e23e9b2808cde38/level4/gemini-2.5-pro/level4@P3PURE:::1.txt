This paper proposes "Belief Propagation Recursive Neural Networks," which extend standard feedforward (bottom-up) recursive neural networks with a backward (top-down) inference step. This mechanism allows phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to, and thereby refine, word-level embeddings and labels.

The paper is generally well-written, and the model is well-motivated and clearly described. It presents an interesting variant of the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Network model, but several aspects require significant clarification.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Novelty and Comparison to Prior Work:** The paper extends the idea of Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (Irsoy and Cardie, 2014; note: the reference in the paper is incomplete). A key contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors. The authors should clarify if this is the most significant difference from previous work. Furthermore, the distinction between the proposed model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie needs to be more clearly articulated, as Section 3.6 is currently somewhat difficult to follow.

2.  **Experimental Fairness:** The experimental comparisons lack sufficient detail to be considered fair. The authors must clarify the following points:
    *   Did the baseline RNN and B-RNN models also use the proposed hybrid vectors? If not, the comparison is not direct.
    *   What was the procedure for tuning and selecting the "best" models for all methods?
    *   How was the single best model selected for reporting?
    *   It is worth noting that the proposed method appears to use an ensemble, while the other methods do not. This should be explicitly addressed to ensure a fair comparison.

3.  **Training Details:** The training section (3.5) is too brief and should provide more detail. The training presumably uses the back-propagation through structure algorithm (Goller & Kuchler, 1996, could be cited), which involves unfolding the recursive model. In this case, updating the word embeddings requires back-propagating through a very deep network. This is a critical and non-trivial step, and the authors should provide a more thorough explanation of the process.

**Minor Comments:**

1.  **Terminology:** The term "belief propagation" may be misleading, as the mechanism described appears more analogous to a forward-backward or bottom-up/top-down pass. The authors might consider whether "forward-backward" is a more suitable term.

2.  **Task Description:** Section 4.1, which describes the experimental task, should be improved for readers outside the NLP community. For instance, Task 2 of Semeval 2013 contains two subtasks (contextual and message polarity). The review assumes the paper addresses the contextual polarity task, but this should be stated explicitly. A clearer presentation of the task would also help readers understand the motivation for the parser constraints.

3.  **Organization:** The authors could consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for improved readability.
