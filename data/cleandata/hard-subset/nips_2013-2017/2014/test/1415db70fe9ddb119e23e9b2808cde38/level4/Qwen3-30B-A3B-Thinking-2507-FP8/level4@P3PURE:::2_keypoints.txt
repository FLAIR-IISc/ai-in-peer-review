- Proposes a bidirectional recursive neural network that does an upward pass then a downward pass to augment node representations with top-down contextual information.  
- Addresses the limitation of standard (unidirectional) RNNs that cannot propagate information from larger contexts to smaller constituents.  
- Simple, elegant extension that yields state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- Conceptually similar to prior work [18] but differs in the downward-pass computation and achieves superior performance.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and generally well-written.  
- Experimental concerns: unclear if the [18] baseline results in Table 2 used dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors.  
- Methodological concern: including an ensemble result in a table comparing individual models is inappropriate.  
- Minor clarification needed: the phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 is ambiguous.
