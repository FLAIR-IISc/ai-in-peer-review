This paper extends recursive neural networks (RNNs) for NLP by adding a downward pass over parse trees. Standard Tree-RNNs build representations bottom-up by recursively combining word and phrase representations along a fixed parse tree to produce a root representation, but they cannot propagate information from larger contexts back to subconstituents. The proposed bidirectional variant performs a standard upward pass, then a downward pass that injects top-level contextual information into all nodes. On SemEval-2012 Task 2, this approach achieves state-of-the-art results.

The extension is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea resembles [18], the downward-pass computation here differs, and the reported performance is stronger.

The paper is well motivated, clearly explained, and generally well written. The empirical results are strong, but I have a few concerns about the experimental setup:
- For the alternative bidirectional RNN from [18] reported in Table 2, did you apply dropout and use the additional training data?
- Were hybrid vectors also used for that baseline?
- While the ensemble result is impressive, it should not be placed in a table intended to compare single models.

Please also clarify what the “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 refers to.

Overall, this is a well-executed paper built on a simple and elegant idea.
