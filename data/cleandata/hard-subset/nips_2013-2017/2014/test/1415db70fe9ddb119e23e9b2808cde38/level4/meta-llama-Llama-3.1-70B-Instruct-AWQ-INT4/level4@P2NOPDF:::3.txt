This paper proposes a novel approach to sentiment classification by utilizing both the encoding and decoding phrase vectors of a recursive neural network auto-encoder. The authors refer to this method as "belief propagation," which may be misleading as it does not implement the traditional inference algorithm. Despite this, the paper is generally well-written. However, the motivation behind the proposed model is unclear and lacks a solid theoretical foundation.

The primary weakness of this paper lies in its evaluation methodology. Presenting ensemble results in this context is unnecessary, as none of the benchmarks are ensembles, and this obscures the findings. Furthermore, the composition of the compared models is not explicitly stated. To improve clarity, the authors should address the following questions: How were the models tuned, and how were the ensemble/best models selected? How were the benchmarks in Table 2 optimized? Were they using the same augmented data as the BP-RNN? Did the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN also employ hybrid word vectors?

In the absence of this information, the second entry in Table 3 appears to be the most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 when evaluating the BP-RNN architecture.

Some minor points to consider:

* The use of an off-the-shelf parser to parse tweets is surprising. It would be interesting to know the parser's accuracy on this specific data.
* The citation for Irsoy and Cardie is incomplete, as are several other references.

Overall, this paper presents a recursive neural network model for classifying phrasal sentiment in context. Although the results seem reasonable, the model's motivation is not strongly justified, and the experimental methodology requires further clarification.
