- Proposes using both encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive neural network auto-encoder for sentiment classification.
- Labels the approach as "belief propagation," but this terminology is misleading because it is not actual belief propagation.
- Paper is well written but the model motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.
- Primary weakness: evaluation is unclear and unjustified, particularly the inclusion of an ensemble when baselines are not ensembles.
- Composition of compared models is unclear; key details on model tuning and selection of ensemble/best models are missing.
- Unclear how benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned and whether they used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.
- Unclear whether the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.
- Given the missing details, the second entry in Table 3 appears most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.
- Minor: surprised an off-the-shelf parser was used for tweets; its accuracy on that domain should be reported.
- Minor: the Irsoy and Cardie citation (and several others) is incomplete.
- Overall: results seem reasonable, but the model lacks strong motivation and the experimental methodology is not sufficiently clear.
