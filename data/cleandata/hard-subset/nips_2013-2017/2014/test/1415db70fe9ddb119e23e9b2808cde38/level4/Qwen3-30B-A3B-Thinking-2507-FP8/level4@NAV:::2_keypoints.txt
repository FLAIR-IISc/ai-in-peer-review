- Problem: Standard recursive neural networks process upward along parse trees and cannot propagate contextual information downward from larger to smaller segments.  
- Proposal: Introduces a bidirectional RNN that performs an upward pass followed by a downward pass to enrich node representations with larger-context information.  
- Result: Method achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- Contribution: Extension is simple, elegant, effective, and well-motivated; paper is clearly written.  
- Novelty vs prior work: High-level idea similar to [18], but the computational treatment of the downward pass differs and yields better results.  
- Experimental strength: Empirical results are strong, supporting the approach.  
- Experimental concerns: Unclear whether the baseline bidirectional model from [18] in Table 2 used dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors — affecting fairness of comparison.  
- Presentation issue: Ensemble result is reported alongside individual model performances inappropriately.  
- Clarity issue: The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 is ambiguous and needs clarification.
