

This paper introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks," extending feedforward (bottom-up) recursive networks with a backward (top-down) inference step. This enables phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to word embeddings and labels. The model is well motivated and clearly described, offering a natural extension of Irsoy and Cardie's (2008) Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (though the reference to their work is incomplete). A key contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors, which may represent the most significant distinction from prior work.  

However, several points require clarification. First, the experimental comparisons appear uneven: did the RNN and B-RNN baselines also employ hybrid vectors? How were the "best" models tuned and selected, and how was the single best model chosen? Notably, other methods do not use ensembles, which should be explicitly addressed.  

The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" would be more precise and standard. Section 3.5 (Training) is overly brief. The authors should elaborate on the training procedure, particularly whether backpropagation through structure (Goller & Kümmel, 1996) was used. Given that this algorithm unfolds the recursive model, the resulting network becomes very deep, potentially complicating updates to word embeddings. Clarifying this critical step would strengthen the paper.  

Section 3.6 lacks sufficient distinction from Irsoy and Cardie's work; this comparison must be explicitly clarified. Section 4.1 also needs improvement for broader accessibility: Semeval 2013 Task 2 includes both contextual and message polarity subtasks, and the authors address only the contextual polarity task. A clearer explanation of the task setup—particularly how parser constraints interact with the model—would aid readers outside NLP.  

Finally, Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be consolidated for better flow. Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a compelling variant of bidirectional recursive networks, but the points above must be addressed to ensure rigor and clarity.
