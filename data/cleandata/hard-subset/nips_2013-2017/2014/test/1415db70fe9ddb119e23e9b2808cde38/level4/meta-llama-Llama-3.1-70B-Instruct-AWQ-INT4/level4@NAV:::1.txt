This paper presents the concept of "belief propagation recursive neural networks", which expands upon traditional "feedforward" recursive networks by incorporating a backward step during inference. This enhancement enables phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to word embeddings and labels.

The paper is well-written, with a clear motivation and description of the model. It builds upon the idea of Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008), with notable contributions such as the introduction of hybrid word vectors. However, it is essential to clarify whether this is the primary distinction from previous work.

The experimental comparisons require further clarification, specifically regarding the use of hybrid vectors in RNN and B-RNN models, the tuning and selection process of the "best" models, and the method for selecting the single best model. It is also worth noting that other methods do not employ an ensemble approach. These points necessitate clarification.

The term "belief propagation" may be misleading, and the term "forward-backward" could be more suitable.

Section 3.5 (Training) is concise and could benefit from additional details, such as the use of the back-propagation through structure algorithm (Goller, 1996). This algorithm involves unfolding the recursive model, resulting in a deep network that updates the word embedding part. Providing more information on this crucial step would be beneficial.

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing, and the distinction between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie requires clarification.

Section 4.1 needs improvement to facilitate understanding for readers outside the NLP community. Specifically, the task 2 of Semeval 2013 consists of two subtasks: contextual and message polarity. It appears that the authors address the contextual polarity task. A clearer presentation of the task would enhance comprehension, including parser constraints.

Consider merging sections 4.3 and 4.4.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents an interesting variant of the Bidirectional recursive model.
