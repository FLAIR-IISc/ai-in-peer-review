- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), an extension of RNNs for context-dependent sentiment analysis.  
- Adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass that propagates root-level context back to leaf nodes.  
- Solves misclassification of context-sensitive phrases (e.g., interpreting "Android" as positive in "Android beats iOS").  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2 with ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs.  
- Shows hybrid word vectors (unsupervised pretraining + task-specific fine-tuning) and dropout markedly improve performance.  
- Clarifies difference from bidirectional recursive networks [12]: feedbackward uses both forward and backward parent nodes versus position-independent transforms.  
- Experimental section is thorough, using SemEval 2013 and rigorous ablation studies.  
- Strengths: simple architectural change, clear motivation, practical hybrid vectors, and strong empirical validation.  
- Demonstrates feedbackward handles context beyond fixed-window methods.  
- Concern: baseline comparison with [12] omits whether dropout, hybrid vectors, or extra training data were used, reducing transparency.  
- Concern: ensemble result (top-5 averaged) is inappropriately listed alongside single-model baselines.  
- Limitation acknowledged: reliance on parse trees.  
- Overall: technically sound, significant contribution, recommended for acceptance.
