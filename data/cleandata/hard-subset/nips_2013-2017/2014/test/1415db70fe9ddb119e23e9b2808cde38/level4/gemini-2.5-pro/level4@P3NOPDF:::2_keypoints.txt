- Paper identifies limitation of standard recursive neural networks: purely bottom-up representations cannot incorporate broader tree context into smaller constituents.  
- Proposal: a bidirectional RNN that adds a downward pass from parents to children, augmenting node representations with larger-context information.  
- Empirical claim: the model achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 (Task 2) sentiment analysis.  
- Clarity/quality: paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and easy to follow with sufficient formulation detail.  
- Originality/significance: bidirectional tree processing relates to prior work but the specific downward-pass computation is novel and empirically effective.  
- Concern 1: unclear whether comparison to baseline [18] used identical experimental enhancements (dropout, additional training data, hybrid word vectors).  
- Concern 2: ensemble result in Table 2 may mislead when placed alongside single-model comparisons and should be separated or clearly delineated.  
- Concern 3: minor wording ambiguity—clarify what “widely used mix of both” refers to in Section 4.4.  
- Conclusion: solid, simple, and powerful contribution that merits acceptance pending the above experimental clarifications.
