- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) as an extension to recursive neural networks for NLP.  
- Identifies a key limitation of traditional RNNs: feedforward architecture fails to capture necessary contextual information.  
- GB-RNN adds a feedbackward inference step so phrase-level representations can provide feedback to word-level representations.  
- Feedbackward step enables better contextual modeling and improves aspect-specific sentiment classification.  
- Proposes a hybrid word vector approach combining unsupervised and supervised representations.  
- Provides a clear background on RNNs and a thorough analysis of related work (word vectors, RNNs, bidirectional models).  
- Experimental results show GB-RNN outperforms several baselines and achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.  
- Paper includes detailed analysis of the impact of the feedbackward step and hybrid word vectors on performance.  
- Strength: novel and effective model design that addresses contextual shortcomings of traditional RNNs.  
- Strength: well-written, well-motivated experiments, and technically sound presentation.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and runtime costs of GB-RNN.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter choices and sensitivity to different settings.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed comparisons with other state-of-the-art models.  
- Originality: feedbackward inference and hybrid word vectors are novel and effective contributions.  
- Significance: approach could improve many NLP tasks requiring contextual understanding, especially sentiment analysis.
