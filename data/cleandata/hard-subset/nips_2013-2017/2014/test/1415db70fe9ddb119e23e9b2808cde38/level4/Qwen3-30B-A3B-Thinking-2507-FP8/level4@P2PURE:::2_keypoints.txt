- Problem: Recursive neural networks process from leaves to root only, preventing information flow from larger to smaller contextual units.  
- Contribution: Introduces a bidirectional RNN with an upward pass followed by a downward pass that augments node representations with larger-context information.  
- Results: Method achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- Strength: The bidirectional extension is simple, elegant, effective, well-motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is well written.  
- Relation to prior work: High-level idea resembles [18], but the paper’s downward-pass computation differs and yields superior performance.  
- Experimental concern 1: Unclear whether the alternative bidirectional model from [18] in Table 2 used dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors.  
- Experimental concern 2: Ensemble result should not be compared directly with individual models in the same table; the phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 needs clarification.  
- Overall judgment: A well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
