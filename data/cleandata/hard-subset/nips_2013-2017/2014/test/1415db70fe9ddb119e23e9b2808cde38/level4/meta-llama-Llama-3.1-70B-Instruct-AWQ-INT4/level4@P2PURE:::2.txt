In Natural Language Processing (NLP), recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been employed to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining pairs of word or shorter snippet representations. This process begins with word representations and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree, culminating in the representation of the entire snippet at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process restricts the propagation of information from larger contexts to smaller ones. This paper proposes a bidirectional extension of RNNs, wherein the upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, thereby augmenting the representations of all nodes with information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The proposed extension of the established RNN-based approach is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea bears some resemblance to the concept presented in [18], the computation performed during the downward pass in this paper differs, and the resulting performance appears superior.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results appear excellent; however, I have some concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, was the result for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2 obtained using dropout and additional training data? Were hybrid vectors also employed in that case? Furthermore, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not belong in a table comparing individual model performances.

In Section 4.4, the phrase "widely used mix of both" is unclear; please provide clarification. Overall, the paper is well-executed and based on a simple yet elegant idea.
