This paper introduces the "Trust-Propagating-Repeat Neural Networks" concept. Such networks extend "feedforward" (bottom-up) recurrent networks with a backward (top-down) step. This allows for sentence-level prediction and embedding to respond to word embeddings and labels.

Overall, the paper is well-written. The model is well-inspired and described. It expands the concept of bidirectional recurrent neural networks introduced by Ersoy and Kardi in 2008 (note that the paper's reference is incomplete). For example, one contribution is the introduction of hybrid word vectors. Perhaps this is the most significant difference with previous work? Moreover, the experimental comparisons are not entirely fair: both RNNs and BNNs use hybrid vectors? How are the "best" models tuned and selected? How is a single best selected? It is noteworthy that other methods do not use an ensemble. These issues should be clarified. The term "Trust-Propagating" is misleading, and the term "Forward-Backward" might be more appropriate.

Section 3.5 (Training) is somewhat short and could provide more detailed information. For example, I believe the training used back-propagation through the Structure algorithm (Goller's 1996 paper can be referenced). This algorithm implies that the recurrent model is open. In this case, it creates a very deep network that can "reach" the updated word embedding part. If I understand correctly, I believe the authors could have provided more detailed information on this important step.

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing and should clarify the difference between this model and the work of Ersoy and Kardi.

Section 4.1 should be improved for readers outside the NNLP community to understand. Semeval 2013's Task 2 has 2 subtasks: Relevancy and Message polarity. I believe you discussed the Relevancy polarity task. Through a better presentation of the task, even parser limitations can be understood.

Perhaps, sections 4.3 and 4.4 can be combined.

This paper is well-written overall and describes an interesting type of bidirectional recurrent model.
