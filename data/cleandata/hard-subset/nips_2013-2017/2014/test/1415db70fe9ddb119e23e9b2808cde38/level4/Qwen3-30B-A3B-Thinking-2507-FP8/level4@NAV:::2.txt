

In natural language processing, recursive neural networks (RNNs) generate representations for text snippets by recursively combining word or short snippet representations along a predefined parse tree, starting from word-level representations and progressing upward to the root node. However, the unidirectional processing inherent in this approach prevents downward propagation of information from larger to smaller contexts. This paper proposes a bidirectional RNN extension where an upward tree traversal is followed by a downward pass, enhancing all node representations with contextual information from larger segments. The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  

The paper’s extension of the established RNN framework is simple, elegant, and effective. While the high-level concept resembles that in [18], the computational approach during the downward pass differs, yielding superior performance. The method is well-motivated, clearly articulated, and the paper is generally well-written. Experimental results are strong, but I have reservations about the experimental setup. Specifically, were the results for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] in Table 2 obtained using dropout and additional training data? Were hybrid vectors also employed in that scenario? Additionally, while the ensemble result is impressive, it is misplaced in a table comparing individual model performances.  

What does "widely used mix of both" refer to in Section 4.4? Overall, this is a well-executed paper built upon a straightforward and elegant concept.
