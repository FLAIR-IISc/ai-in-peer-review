- RNNs build phrase/tree representations bottom-up but cannot propagate information down the tree, so node representations lack larger context.
- The paper proposes a bidirectional RNN that adds a downward pass after the usual upward pass to inject parent/contextual information into each node.
- The downward computation is novel compared to prior work (e.g., [18]), and the method achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2012 Task 2.
- The idea is simple, elegant, well-motivated, and the paper is clearly written.
- Experimental results look strong, but fairness of comparisons is unclear.
- It is unspecified whether the baseline bidirectional model from [18] used the same settings (dropout, additional training data, hybrid vectors) as the proposed model.
- Including the ensemble result in a table comparing individual models is unconventional and potentially misleading; ensembles should be reported separately.
- Minor clarification requested: what does “widely used mix of both” refer to in Section 4.4?
- Overall judgment: well-executed paper based on a simple, effective extension despite minor reporting concerns.
