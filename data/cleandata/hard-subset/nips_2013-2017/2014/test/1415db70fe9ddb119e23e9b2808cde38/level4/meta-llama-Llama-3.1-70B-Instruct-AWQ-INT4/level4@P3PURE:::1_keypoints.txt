- Introduces belief propagation recursive neural networks with a top-down backward step allowing phrase-level feedback to word embeddings and labels.  
- Paper is well-written, model well-motivated and clearly described.  
- Main novel contribution appears to be hybrid word vectors, distinguishing it from prior bidirectional RNN work.  
- Experimental comparisons are potentially unfair: unclear whether baseline RNN and B-RNN use hybrid vectors.  
- Lacks detail on model selection and tuning procedures, including how the single best model was chosen; other methods did not use ensembling.  
- The term "belief propagation" is potentially misleading; "forward-backward" might be more appropriate.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; likely uses back-propagation through structure and needs details on unfolding and deep updates to embeddings.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and needs clearer distinction from Irsoy and Cardie (2008).  
- Section 4.1 is unclear for non-NLP readers; must clarify that SemEval 2013 Task 2 has contextual and message polarity subtasks and specify which is addressed.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 overlap and could be merged.  
- Overall, an interesting and worthwhile variant of bidirectional recursive models.
