- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" adding a top-down inference step so phrase-level predictions influence word-level embeddings/labels.  
- Paper is clearly written, well motivated, and described at a sufficient high level.  
- The primary novel contribution appears to be the use of hybrid word vectors and this distinction should be made explicit.  
- Unclear whether RNN and BRNN baselines also used hybrid word vectors; clarify for fair comparison.  
- Provide details on hyperparameter tuning and model-variant selection for all methods, including baselines.  
- Explain how the "single best" model was chosen and whether an ensemble was used; report single-model results if ensemble was employed.  
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading since classical probabilistic message passing is not used; consider "forward–backward" or "top-down/bottom-up recursive network."  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; describe unfolding/backpropagation through structure, gradient stabilization (clipping/normalization), initialization, and time/memory costs.  
- State whether and how training differs between bottom-up and top-down passes.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing; explicitly articulate architectural, training, and inference differences from Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Section 4.1 should explicitly state the paper targets SemEval 2013 Task 2 contextual polarity and include a concise task description for non-NLP readers.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged to streamline exposition.  
- Overall: interesting variant of bidirectional recursive models, but needs clearer terminology, fuller training details, and fairer, better-documented comparisons to strengthen the contribution.
