In NLP, recurrent neural networks (RNNs) have been used to create text representation snippets by repeatedly combining pairs of word/short snippet representations. This process begins with the representation of words and progresses to a predetermined parse tree until the representation of the entire snippet is found in the root node. However, the one-way nature of this process does not allow information to spread from the larger context to the smaller context. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs where the representation of all nodes is enhanced with information coming from the larger context through the lower pass after the upper pass. As a result, the system achieves state-of-the-art performance in Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The extension of the established RNN-based method proposed in this paper is simple, elegant, and effective. Although it bears a strong resemblance to the high-level concept [18], the computation performed in the lower pass in this paper is different and seems to improve performance.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results seem great, but I have some concerns about the experimental setup. Are the results of the alternative bidirectional RNN model obtained from [18] reported in Table 2 using dropout and additional training data? If so, were hybrid vectors also used? Also, although the ensemble results are impressive, it is not consistent with a table for comparing the performance of individual models.

What is meant by "a commonly used combination of both" in section 4.4? A well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant concept.
