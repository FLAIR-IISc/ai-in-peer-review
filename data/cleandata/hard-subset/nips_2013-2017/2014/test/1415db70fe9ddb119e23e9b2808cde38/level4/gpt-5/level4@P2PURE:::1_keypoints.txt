- Paper introduces “belief propagation recursive neural networks” with a top-down inference pass that feeds phrase-level predictions/embeddings back into word embeddings and labels.
- Paper is well written, model is well motivated and clearly described.
- Model extends Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (Irsoy & Cardie 2008); citation appears incomplete.
- Introduction of hybrid word vectors is a key contribution and possibly the main difference from prior work.
- Experimental comparisons lack transparency and may be unfair: unclear if RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.
- Unclear how models were tuned and selected: how “best” models were chosen and how the single best model was determined.
- Need to clarify that other methods do not use an ensemble (comparison fairness issue).
- Term “belief propagation” may be misleading; “forward–backward” might be more appropriate.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and should detail training procedure, likely backpropagation through structure, and cite Goller (1996).
- Clarify how unfolding the recursive model produces a very deep network to reach/update word embeddings.
- Section 3.6 is confusing; differences from Irsoy & Cardie need clearer explanation.
- Section 4.1 should be made accessible to non-NLP readers by explaining SemEval 2013 Task 2’s subtasks and specifying which subtask is addressed and parser constraints.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could potentially be merged for clarity.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper presenting an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model.
