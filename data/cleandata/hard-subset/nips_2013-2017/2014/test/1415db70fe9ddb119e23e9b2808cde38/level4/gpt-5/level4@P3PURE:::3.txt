This paper proposes using both the encoder and decoder phrase vectors of a recursive neural network auto-encoder for sentiment classification. The authors refer to this as “belief propagation,” although it is not an instance of that inference algorithm; the terminology is therefore misleading and likely to confuse readers. The paper is generally well written, but the motivation for the proposed model is vague and lacks a clear theoretical justification.

The key weakness is the evaluation. Presenting an ensemble result is not warranted in this context: none of the benchmarks are ensembles, and doing so obfuscates the contribution. The exact composition of the models compared is also unclear. Important details that need clarification include:
- How were the individual models tuned, and how were the ensemble and “best” models selected?
- How were the benchmarks in Table 2 tuned?
- Did the benchmarks use the same augmented data as the BP-RNN?
- Did the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN also use hybrid word vectors?

In the absence of this information, my best guess is that the second entry in Table 3 is the most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2 when assessing the BP-RNN architecture.

Minor points:
- It is surprising that an off-the-shelf parser was used to parse tweets; please report its accuracy on this data.
- The Irsoy and Cardie citation is incomplete (as are a number of others).

Overall, the paper presents a recursive neural network model for classifying phrasal sentiment in context. The results appear reasonable, but the model lacks strong motivation and the experimental methodology is not entirely clear.
