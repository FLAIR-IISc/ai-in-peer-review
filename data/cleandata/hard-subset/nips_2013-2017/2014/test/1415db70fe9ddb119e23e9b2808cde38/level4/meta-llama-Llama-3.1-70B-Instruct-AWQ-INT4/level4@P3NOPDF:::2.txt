This paper introduces a novel extension of recursive neural networks (RNNs) for natural language processing tasks. RNNs have been successfully employed to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining pairs of word or shorter snippet representations, following a pre-specified parse tree. However, the unidirectional nature of this process restricts the flow of information from larger contexts to smaller ones. To address this limitation, the authors propose a bidirectional extension of RNNs, where an upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, augmenting the representations of all nodes with information from larger contexts.

The proposed extension of the established RNN-based approach is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea bears some resemblance to the approach presented in [18], the computation performed during the downward pass is distinct, and the resulting performance appears to be superior.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results are impressive, with the system achieving state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012. However, I have some concerns regarding the experimental setup. Specifically, it is unclear whether the result for the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2 was obtained using dropout and the additional training data. Furthermore, it is not specified whether hybrid vectors were used in this case.

Additionally, while the ensemble result is notable, it does not belong in a table comparing the performance of individual models. It would be more appropriate to present this result separately, as it does not provide a direct comparison with the other models.

There is also a minor point of clarification needed in Section 4.4, where the authors mention a "widely used mix of both." It is not entirely clear what this refers to, and a brief explanation would be helpful.

Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple yet elegant idea. The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their approach, and the results are promising. With some minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, this paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
