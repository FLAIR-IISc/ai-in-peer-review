This paper introduces a novel extension of recursive neural networks (RNNs) for natural language processing tasks, particularly contextual sentiment analysis. The proposed global belief recursive neural networks (GB-RNNs) incorporate a feedbackward step during inference, allowing phrase-level predictions and representations to be influenced by the global context of the sentence. This is a significant improvement over standard RNNs, which have a purely feedforward architecture and cannot capture context-dependent information.

The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, highlighting the limitations of existing RNN-based models in tasks such as aspect-specific sentiment classification. They also present a thorough review of related work, including neural word vectors, recursive neural networks, and bidirectional-recurrent and bidirectional-recursive neural networks.

The GB-RNN model is well-defined and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the forward and backward passes through the parse tree. The use of a hybrid approach for word vector representations, combining unsupervised and supervised learning, is also a notable contribution. The experimental results on the SemEval 2013 dataset are impressive, with the GB-RNN model outperforming several baselines and achieving state-of-the-art performance.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of presenting their ideas and results. The text is clear and concise, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with RNNs and natural language processing.

One potential concern is the experimental setup, particularly with regards to the comparison with the bidirectional RNN model from Irsoy and Cardie (2014). It is unclear whether the result for this model in Table 2 was obtained using the same experimental setup as the GB-RNN model, including the use of dropout and additional training data. Additionally, the ensemble result, while impressive, seems out of place in a table comparing the performance of individual models.

Overall, this is a well-executed paper that presents a simple yet effective extension of RNNs for natural language processing tasks. The idea of propagating beliefs through neural networks is a powerful one, and the authors demonstrate its potential in the context of contextual sentiment analysis.
