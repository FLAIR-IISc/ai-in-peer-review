- Proposes GB-RNNs: recursive neural nets with a feedbackward pass so phrase-level predictions refine word representations for contextual sentiment.
- Shows strong empirical gains, achieving state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 and ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs.
- Investigates dropout and a hybrid unsupervised/supervised word-vector method, with ablation studies illuminating component effects.
- Situates work well in prior literature, comparing to compositional models, recursive nets, and bidirectional approaches.
- Strength: addresses an important contextual sentiment problem and RNNs' inability to capture context-dependent meaning.
- Strength: strong empirical results and clear presentation.
- Strength: thorough component analysis, with the hybrid word-vector finding noteworthy.
- Strength: novel contribution in integrating a feedbackward/global-belief pass into recursive networks.
- Weakness: theoretical motivation for “global belief” propagation is underdeveloped and the term is somewhat misleading.
- Weakness: insufficient experimental detail—hyperparameter tuning, model selection, and ensemble construction are unclear.
- Weakness: benchmark comparisons may be unfair or hard to interpret because tuning/data-augmentation details across models are not specified.
- Weakness: potential impact of parser errors on Twitter data is not assessed; parser accuracy should be reported.
- Weakness: several incomplete citations hinder reproducibility and literature follow-up.
- For acceptance: novel, well-written, strong empirical evidence and useful ablations.
- Against acceptance: weak theoretical justification, incomplete experimental transparency, and unaddressed parser impact.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying ensemble/hyperparameter procedures, reporting parser accuracy on Twitter, and strengthening the theoretical justification of the feedback mechanism.
