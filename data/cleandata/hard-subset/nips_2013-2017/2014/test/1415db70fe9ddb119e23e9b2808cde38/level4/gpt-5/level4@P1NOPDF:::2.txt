In NLP, recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been used to build representations of text snippets by recursively composing pairs of word or shorter-phrase representations. The process starts with word representations and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree until the representation of the entire snippet is obtained at the root. However, its unidirectional nature prevents information from flowing down the tree from larger to smaller contexts. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs in which an upward pass through the tree is followed by a downward pass, augmenting all node representations with information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The proposed extension to the established RNN-based approach is simple, elegant, and effective. While the high-level idea resembles [18], the computation performed during the downward pass here is different, and the resulting performance appears to be superior.

The method is well motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is well written overall. The experimental results are excellent, but I have some concerns about the setup. For the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2, were dropout and the additional training data used? Were the hybrid vectors also applied in that comparison? Also, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not really belong in a table comparing the performance of individual models. Finally, in Section 4.4, what exactly does the “widely used mix of both” refer to?

Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
