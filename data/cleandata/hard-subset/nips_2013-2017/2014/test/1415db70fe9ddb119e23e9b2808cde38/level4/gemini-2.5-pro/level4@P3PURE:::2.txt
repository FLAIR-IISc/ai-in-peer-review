In natural language processing, recursive neural networks (RNNs) are used to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining representations of words or shorter phrases. This process begins with word representations and proceeds up a pre-specified parse tree, culminating in a representation for the entire snippet at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process prevents information from larger contexts from propagating down to smaller ones within the tree. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension to RNNs where the standard upward pass is followed by a downward pass. This second pass augments the representation at each node with information from these larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The proposed extension to the established RNN-based approach is simple, elegant, and effective. While the high-level idea is similar to that of [18], the computation performed during the downward pass is novel, and the resulting performance appears to be superior.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is well-written overall. The experimental results appear to be excellent; however, I have some concerns regarding the experimental setup. For the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2, was the result obtained using dropout and the additional training data? Were the hybrid vectors also used in that case? Additionally, while the ensemble result is impressive, it is misplaced in a table intended to compare the performance of single models.

In Section 4.4, what does the phrase "widely used mix of both" refer to?

Overall, this is a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
