- Addresses limitation of feedforward RNNs by allowing global sentence context to influence constituent node representations.  
- Proposes Global Belief RNN (GB-RNN) with a bottom-up pass followed by a top-down "feedbackward" pass to refine node representations.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 Task 2.  
- Feedbackward step yields a substantial improvement (≈3% absolute F1) over the standard RNN baseline.  
- Introduces a novel formulation for downward propagation using both forward and backward parent vectors.  
- Secondary contributions: hybrid word vector representation (unsupervised + supervised) and successful use of dropout in RNN training.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and compares related work (e.g., Irsoy & Cardie) insightfully.  
- Question on fairness: unclear if the Irsoy & Cardie bidirectional RNN baseline used the same hybrid vectors and dropout enhancements.  
- Question on reporting: GB-RNN results in Table 1 are for a 5-model ensemble but compared to single-model competitors; single-model GB-RNN performance should be reported.  
- Overall: technically solid, significant empirical advance; addressing the experimental-comparison clarifications would strengthen the claims.
