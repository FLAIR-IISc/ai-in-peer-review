- Proposes GB-RNN, a recursive neural network with a feedbackward step that lets phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations for contextual sentiment analysis.
- Motivated by context-dependent word sentiment (e.g., “Android” in “Android beats iOS”).
- Uses parse-tree structure to incorporate long-range context, differentiating from feedforward and standard bidirectional RNNs.
- Introduces hybrid word vectors (unsupervised + supervised) and finds benefits from dropout and hybrid embeddings.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 and outperforms standard RNN baselines.
- Builds on recent recursive NN literature and related work in RNNs and bidirectional models.
- Strength: tackles an important, real-world problem with a conceptually novel approach.
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating clear improvements.
- Strength: well-written and clearly presented with useful practical training insights.
- Weakness: theoretical motivation for the global belief propagation mechanism is weak; "belief propagation" terminology may be misleading.
- Weakness: evaluation presentation is unclear—ensemble vs. single-model comparisons, ensemble construction, and baseline tuning are ambiguous.
- Weakness: reliance on an off-the-shelf parser for tweets raises concerns about parse accuracy affecting results; no parser analysis provided.
- Weakness: several incomplete citations hinder reproducibility and related-work exploration.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying evaluation methodology (ensemble creation, baseline tuning, data augmentation, hyperparameter selection, whether baselines used hybrid vectors) and completing missing citations.
