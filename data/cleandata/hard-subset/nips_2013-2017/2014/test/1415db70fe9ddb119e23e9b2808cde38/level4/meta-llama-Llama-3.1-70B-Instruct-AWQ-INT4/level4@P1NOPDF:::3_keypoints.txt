- Proposes using both encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment classification.
- Calls the approach "belief propagation," which is misleading and may confuse readers.
- Paper is generally well-written but motivation and theoretical justification for the model are vague.
- Major weakness: evaluation methodology is unclear and possibly flawed.
- Reporting an ensemble result is unnecessary and obfuscates comparisons to non-ensemble benchmarks.
- Composition of compared models is unclear: how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected is not explained.
- Unclear whether benchmarks in Table 2 used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.
- Unclear whether RNN and BiRNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.
- Likely the second entry in Table 3 is the most comparable baseline to assess BP-RNN.
- Minor concerns: using an off-the-shelf parser for tweets is surprising—parser accuracy on tweets should be reported.
- Several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.
