- Introduces GB-RNNs: adds a backward pass so phrase-level predictions influence word representations, addressing feedforward limits in context-dependent tasks (e.g., aspect sentiment).
- Paper is well-written, model clearly described, and builds on prior recursive NN work (notably extends Irsoy & Cardie 2008).
- Hybrid word vector representations are a notable contribution.
- Experimental comparisons are unclear: unclear whether baselines used hybrid vectors and whether comparisons are fair.
- GB-RNN used an ensemble while baselines did not, which may bias results.
- More detail needed on hyperparameter tuning and how the “best” models were selected.
- Training (Section 3.5) is too brief; should clarify backpropagation through structure and how updates reach the embedding layer.
- The term “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; “forward-backward” may be more appropriate.
- Section 3.6’s comparison to Irsoy & Cardie needs clearer, more explicit distinctions.
- Section 4.1 should clarify which SemEval 2013 subtask is addressed (contextual vs message polarity) and explain parser constraints for non-experts.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be consolidated for a more streamlined presentation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension addressing context-dependent meaning, improved/state-of-the-art SemEval 2013 results, valuable hybrid vectors, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: unfair experimental setup, insufficient training detail, unclear comparison to prior work, potentially misleading terminology.
- Overall: worthwhile contribution with promising results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address experimental, training, and clarity concerns.
