- Paper proposes GB-RNNs: bidirectional recursive nets with a top-down (global belief) pass to let phrase-level information inform word embeddings and labels.
- Model is well-motivated and clearly described overall.
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008) bidirectional RNNs, but that citation/reference is incomplete and the distinction from their work is insufficiently clarified.
- Key claimed contribution is hybrid word vector representations; unclear whether baselines also used these hybrids.
- Experimental methodology lacks detail on model selection and hyperparameter tuning (how “best” models were chosen and single-model vs ensemble comparisons).
- The term “belief propagation” may be misleading; “forward-backward” could be more appropriate.
- Section 3.5 (Training) needs more detail—likely uses backpropagation through structure (cite Goller & Küchler 1996) and should explain unfolding to update word embeddings.
- Section 3.6 is unclear; equations comparing this model to Irsoy & Cardie need more thorough explanation.
- Section 4.1 should state which SemEval 2013 subtask is addressed (contextual vs message polarity) and clarify parser constraints for non-NLP readers.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for clearer presentation.
- Strengths: addresses a relevant problem (contextual sentiment), proposes a novel global-context approach, shows state-of-the-art SemEval 2013 results, and introduces useful hybrid word vectors.
- Weaknesses: unclear differentiation from prior bidirectional recursive models, limited training/backprop detail, and experimental comparisons lack rigor and transparency.
