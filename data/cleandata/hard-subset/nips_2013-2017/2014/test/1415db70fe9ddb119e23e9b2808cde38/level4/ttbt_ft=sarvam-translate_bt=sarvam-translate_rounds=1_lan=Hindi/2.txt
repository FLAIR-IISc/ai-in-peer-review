In NLP, Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) have been used to generate representations of text snippets by recursively combining pairs of word/short snippet representations. This process begins with the representation of words and progresses upwards through a pre-specified parse tree until the representation of the entire snippet is obtained at the root node. However, the unidirectional nature of this process does not allow information to be propagated downwards in the tree from larger contexts to smaller ones. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension of RNNs that involves going downwards after going upwards through the tree, which increases the representation of all nodes with information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The proposed established RNN-based approach in the paper is simple, elegant, and effective. Although quite similar to the high-level idea [18], the computation performed on the downwards path in the paper is different, and the resulting performance appears to be better.

The method is well-motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written. The experimental results appear excellent, but I have some concerns about the experimental setup. Was the result of the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] obtained using dropout and additional training data in Table 2? Was a hybrid vector also used in that case? Also, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not actually appear in the table comparing the performance of individual models.

What is related to "a widely used combination of both" in Section 4.4? A well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
