- Paper proposes using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment classification.  
- Calling the method “belief propagation” is a misnomer and may mislead readers.  
- Paper is well-written but the model motivation is vague and lacks solid theoretical justification.  
- Main weakness is evaluation: reporting an ensemble result is inappropriate given single-model benchmarks and complicates result interpretation.  
- Composition of compared models is unclear—need details on how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected.  
- Clarify whether benchmark experiments (Table 2) used the same augmented data as BP-RNN and whether RNN/BiRNN used hybrid word vectors.  
- In absence of clarification, the second entry in Table 3 is likely the most direct comparison to Tables 1–2 for assessing BP-RNN.  
- Minor: report accuracy of the off-the-shelf tweet parser on this dataset.  
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) and other references are incomplete.  
- Summary: model shows promising results but needs stronger motivation and much clearer experimental methodology.
