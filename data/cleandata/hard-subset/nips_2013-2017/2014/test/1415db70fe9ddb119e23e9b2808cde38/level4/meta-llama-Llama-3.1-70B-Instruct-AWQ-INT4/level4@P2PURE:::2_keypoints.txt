- Proposes a bidirectional extension to recursive neural networks: an upward pass followed by a downward pass to enrich node representations with larger-context information, achieving state-of-the-art on SemEval 2012 Task 2.

- The extension is simple, elegant, and effective.

- The idea is similar in spirit to [18], but the downward-pass computation differs and yields superior performance.

- Method is well-motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is generally well-written.

- Experimental results look excellent, but there are concerns about the experimental setup and comparability.

- Clarify whether the alternative bidirectional RNN result from [18] in Table 2 used dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors.

- The ensemble result should not be presented in a table comparing individual model performances.

- In Section 4.4, the phrase "widely used mix of both" is unclear and needs clarification.

- Overall, the paper is well-executed and based on a simple, elegant idea.
