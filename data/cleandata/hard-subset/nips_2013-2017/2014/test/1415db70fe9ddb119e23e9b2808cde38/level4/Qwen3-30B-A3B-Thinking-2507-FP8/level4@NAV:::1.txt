

This paper presents "belief propagation recursive neural networks," extending feedforward (bottom-up) recursive networks with a backward (top-down) inference step. This enables phrase-level predictions and embeddings to provide feedback to word embeddings and labels.  

The paper is well written, with a clear model motivation and description. It builds upon Irsoy and Cardie's (2008) Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (though the reference is incomplete), notably introducing hybrid word vectors—a potentially key distinction from prior work. However, experimental comparisons lack full fairness: it remains unclear whether RNN and B-RNN baselines also employed hybrid vectors, how "best" models were tuned/selected, or how the single best model was chosen (particularly since other methods did not use ensembles). These points require clarification.  

The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" would be more appropriate. Section 3.5 (Training) is insufficiently detailed; the authors should elaborate on the training procedure, including whether back-propagation through structure (Goller & Küchler, 1996) was used—this algorithm unfolds the recursive model, creating a deep network that updates word embeddings, a critical aspect needing further explanation.  

Section 3.6 is confusing and fails to clearly distinguish this model from Irsoy and Cardie's work. Section 4.1 requires improvement for non-NLP readers: Semeval 2013 Task 2 involves two subtasks (contextual and message polarity), and it appears the authors address contextual polarity; a clearer task description would aid understanding, including parser constraints. Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for conciseness.  

Overall, the paper is well written and describes an interesting extension of bidirectional recursive models.
