- Terminology misleading: calling the method "belief propagation" is inaccurate and confuses readers since it is not BP inference.  
- Motivation weak: model lacks clear motivation and theoretical grounding.  
- Evaluation flawed: presenting ensemble results is unjustified because benchmarks are not ensembles, obscuring comparisons.  
- Ambiguous baselines: composition and tuning of compared models are unclear.  
- Missing experimental details: need to explain how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected.  
- Benchmark comparability unclear: clarify whether Table 2 benchmarks used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.  
- Feature parity unclear: state whether RNN and BiRNN baselines used the same hybrid word vectors.  
- Table interpretation: without clarifications, the second entry in Table 3 appears the most directly comparable to Tables 1–2.  
- Minor: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser for tweets — report its accuracy on this dataset.  
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Overall: model and results are reasonable and paper is well-written, but motivation and experimental transparency need improvement.
