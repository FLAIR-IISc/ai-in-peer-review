- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" adding a top-down step to feedforward recursive nets so phrase-level predictions feed back to word embeddings and labels.
- Paper is well-written, and the proposed model is well-motivated and clearly described.
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008) Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks, but the citation/reference is incomplete.
- Introduces hybrid word vectors, likely the most significant novelty compared to prior work.
- Experimental setup is unclear: unclear if RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors and how models were tuned/selected.
- Unclear criteria for choosing the single best model and concern that other methods do not rely on ensembles.
- The term "belief propagation" may be misleading; "forward-backward" could be a more appropriate descriptor.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is brief and needs detail (e.g., back-propagation through structure, unfolding into a very deep network to update embeddings).
- Section 3.6 is unclear and should explicitly clarify differences from Irsoy & Cardie.
- Section 4.1 should be clarified for non-NLP readers (e.g., specify SemEval 2013 subtasks and indicate the paper addresses contextual polarity).
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 to improve coherence.
- Overall assessment: an intriguing, well-written variant of the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Network model.
