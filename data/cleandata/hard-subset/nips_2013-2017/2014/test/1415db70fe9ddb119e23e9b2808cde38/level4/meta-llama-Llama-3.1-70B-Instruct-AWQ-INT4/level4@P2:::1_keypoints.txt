- Introduces GB-RNNs: feedforward recursive nets with a backward inference step to let phrase-level predictions feedback to words.  
- Model motivation and description are strong and the paper is well-written.  
- Notable contribution: introduction of hybrid word vectors.  
- Unclear whether baseline RNNs and Bidirectional RNNs also use hybrid vectors; model selection/tuning and single-best model selection process are not described.  
- The term "Global Belief" may be misleading; "forward-backward" could be a clearer name.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and should detail algorithms used (e.g., backpropagation through structure) and the implications of unfolding a deep recursive network for embedding updates.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and needs clearer differentiation from Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional recursive model.  
- Section 4.1 is hard to follow for non-NLP readers and should better present Semeval 2013 Task 2, the contextual polarity task, and parser constraints.  
- Suggest merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for clarity and conciseness.  
- Empirical results convincingly show improvements over standard RNN and other baselines.  
- Originality: extension of bidirectional recursive RNNs and the hybrid word vectors are new contributions.  
- Significance: addresses context-dependent compositional vector representations for variable-length phrases.  
- Quality: paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and presents convincing results.  
- Clarity: model and results are clearly described, though several sections need more detail or explanation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated model, novel hybrid vectors, and improved empirical performance.  
- Arguments for rejection: misleading terminology, insufficient training details, confusing related-work distinction, and sections needing clearer exposition for broader readership.
