- Introduces belief propagation recursive neural networks with a top-down pass to allow phrase-level feedback to word embeddings and labels.  
- Paper is well written, well motivated, and describes the model clearly.  
- Builds on Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (Irsoy & Cardie 2008), but the citation/reference is incomplete.  
- Key contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors, which may distinguish it from prior work.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair — unclear if baseline RNN and B-RNN were trained with hybrid vectors.  
- The paper should clarify how models were tuned/selected and how the single “best” model was identified; other methods did not use ensembles.  
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading; “forward-backward” might be a more accurate descriptor.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; likely uses back-propagation through structure (cite Goller 1996) and needs more detail on unfolding and deep updates to word embeddings.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and needs clearer differentiation from Irsoy & Cardie’s work.  
- Section 4.1 should better explain SemEval 2013 Task 2 (contextual vs. message polarity) and clarify parser constraints if the focus is contextual polarity.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for conciseness.  
- Overall, the paper describes an interesting and worthwhile variant of the bidirectional recursive model.
