In NLP, recursive neural networks (RNNs) compose representations for text spans by recursively combining word and subphrase representations up a predefined parse tree, starting from word embeddings and proceeding to the root to obtain a representation for the entire span. Because this computation proceeds only bottom-up, information from larger contexts cannot be propagated back to refine representations of smaller constituents. This paper proposes a bidirectional extension in which a downward pass follows the standard upward pass, enriching node representations with information from broader context. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval Task 2 (2012).

The extension of the standard RNN framework is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea is similar to that in [18], the paper uses a different top-down computation, and the empirical results appear superior.

The method is well motivated, clearly described, and the writing is strong overall. The empirical results look excellent, but I have some concerns about the experimental setup. For the alternative bidirectional RNN from [18] reported in Table 2, were dropout and the additional training data also used? Were the hybrid word vectors employed there as well? In addition, while the ensemble performance is impressive, it does not belong in a table that compares individual models.

What does the phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 refer to? Overall, a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
