- Proposes GB-RNN: a novel RNN extension adding a feedbackward inference step so phrase-level predictions inform word representations.
- Addresses a limitation of standard recursive RNNs by enabling contextual feedback from phrases to words.
- GB-RNN architecture is clearly explained and uses hybrid word vector representations as an interesting contribution.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the SemEval 2013 sentiment challenge and compares to bidirectional RNNs.
- Lacks sufficient theoretical justification for the proposed model.
- Experimental methodology and evaluation are insufficiently transparent (model tuning, selection procedures unclear).
- Unclear whether baseline/benchmark models used the same augmented data and unclear composition of compared models.
- Use of ensemble results obscures direct comparison with other models.
- Minor issues: unclear accuracy of the off-the-shelf tweet parser and incomplete citation of Irsoy and Cardie.
- NIPS criteria: strong on originality, significance, and quality; weaker on clarity due to experimental/evaluation ambiguities.
- Pro-acceptance: novel method, well-explained model, strong empirical performance and comparisons.
- Con-acceptance: opaque evaluation/tuning details, unclear baselines/data parity, ensemble use and minor citation/parser issues.
