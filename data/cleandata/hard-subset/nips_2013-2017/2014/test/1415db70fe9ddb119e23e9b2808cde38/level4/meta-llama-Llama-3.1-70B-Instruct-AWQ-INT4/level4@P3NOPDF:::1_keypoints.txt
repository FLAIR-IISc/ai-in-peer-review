- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks," extending feedforward RNNs with a backward (top-down) inference step for phrase-level feedback to word embeddings and labels.
- Paper is well-written; model is well-motivated and clearly described.
- Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008) bidirectional RNNs; main novel contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors.
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair or unclear—uncertain whether RNN and B-RNN baselines also used hybrid vectors.
- Authors should detail how models were tuned and selected, including how the "best" and single best model were chosen.
- Other comparison methods did not use ensembles, raising fairness concerns in comparisons.
- The term "belief propagation" is potentially misleading; "forward-backward" might be a better descriptor.
- Section 3.5 (training) is brief and needs more detail, e.g., use of backpropagation through structure, unfolding, and depth implications for updating word embeddings.
- Section 3.6 is confusing and should more clearly distinguish the proposed model from Irsoy & Cardie (2008).
- Section 4.1 should be clarified for non-NLP readers with a clearer explanation of the task and parser constraints.
- Clarify which Semeval 2013 subtask is addressed (contextual vs. message polarity); presentation of Task 2 should be improved.
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 since they cover related material.
- Overall assessment: interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model with promising contributions.
