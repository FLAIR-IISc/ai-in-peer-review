- Proposes sentiment classification using phrase representations from both encoder and decoder of a recursive auto-encoder.  
- Naming the approach "belief propagation" is misleading since it is not the standard inference algorithm.  
- Paper is clearly written but lacks a strong theoretical motivation or justification for the model.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is unclear and problematic, especially inclusion of an ensemble while benchmarks are single models.  
- Comparative model specifics are ambiguous and need clarification to validate results.  
- Clarify hyperparameter tuning and model/ensemble selection procedures and whether benchmarks were tuned similarly.  
- Clarify whether benchmarks used the same data augmentation as BP-RNN and whether hybrid word vectors were used by baseline RNN/Bi-RNN.  
- In absence of clarifications, the most direct comparison is the second entry in Table 3.  
- Minor: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser for tweets — report its performance on this domain.  
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Conclusion: model performance seems plausible but the method is weakly motivated and experimental methodology lacks sufficient clarity.
