This paper presents “belief propagation recursive neural networks.” These networks augment standard “feedforward” (bottom-up) recursive architectures with a backward (top-down) inference pass. This permits phrase-level predictions and embeddings to feed back into word-level embeddings and labels.

Overall, the paper is clearly written. The model is well motivated and articulated. It builds on the Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks of Irsoy and Cardie 2008 (the citation appears incomplete). One contribution is the use of hybrid word vectors—perhaps the primary distinction from prior work? Additionally, the experimental comparisons do not seem entirely fair: did the RNN and B-RNN baselines also employ hybrid vectors? How were the “best” models tuned and selected? By what procedure was a single best model chosen? Note that the competing methods do not use an ensemble. These issues should be clarified. The term “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; “forward-backward” might be more appropriate. Section 3.5 (Training) is somewhat brief and should include more detail. I assume training relies on backpropagation through structure (Goller, 1996 could be cited). This entails unrolling the recursive computation, which results in a very deep network to reach and update the word embedding layer. If my understanding is correct, more details on this crucial step would be helpful.

Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing, and the distinction from Irsoy and Cardie’s work should be made clearer. Section 4.1 should be improved for readers outside NLP. In SemEval 2013 Task 2 there are two subtasks: contextual and message polarity. I infer that you address the contextual polarity subtask. A clearer task description would also make the parser constraints easier to understand.

Perhaps Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged. Overall, this is a well-written paper that presents an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model.
