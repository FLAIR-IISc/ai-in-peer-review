- Introduces GB-RNNs: adds a feedbackward (top-down) pass to recursive neural nets to incorporate global sentence context for contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Addresses limitation of prior RNNs where phrase-level predictions ignore sentence context (e.g., context-dependent sentiment like "Android beats iOS").  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 Task 2 by propagating root-level representations down the parse tree to update phrase predictions.  
- Uses hybrid word vectors (100-d unsupervised + 30-d task-specific) and dropout (p=0.5), which improve performance over baselines.  
- Strength — Originality: novel bidirectional/feedbackward propagation that handles context beyond fixed k-neighborhoods, distinct from prior bidirectional RNNs.  
- Strength — Quality: sound technical motivation, clear formulations, robust empirical validation, ~3% F1 gain and outperforming SemEval 2013 winners.  
- Strength — Clarity: well-written, precise equations and intuitive examples; thorough comparison to related work (Irsoy & Cardie).  
- Strength — Significance: high-impact for contextual sentiment analysis and potentially generalizable to other NLP tasks.  
- Weakness — Baseline details unclear: unclear if dropout, hybrid vectors, and extra training data were applied to the Irsoy & Cardie bidirectional RNN baseline.  
- Weakness — Ensemble reporting: including a top-5 ensemble in model comparison conflates architecture gains with ensembling and is inappropriate for single-model comparison.  
- Weakness — Ambiguity in Section 4.4 wording about "widely used mix of both," and unclear whether hybrid vectors were used in baseline comparisons.  
- Overall verdict: strong, high-quality contribution with minor, addressable experimental-clarity issues; recommend accept.
