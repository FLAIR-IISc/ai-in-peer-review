- Paper introduces a bidirectional extension to recursive neural networks that adds a downward pass to propagate larger-context information to all tree nodes.  
- Method is simple, elegant, differs from Irsoy & Cardie in downward computation, and yields superior performance.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2.  
- Presentation is clear and the paper is generally well-written and well-motivated.  
- Strong experimental results, but some experimental details are ambiguous.  
- Unclear whether the Irsoy & Cardie baseline in Table 2 used dropout and the extra training data described in Section 4.4.  
- Unclear whether hybrid word vectors were used in the baseline comparison.  
- Inclusion of the ensemble result in a table comparing individual models is misleading and should be separated.  
- The phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 lacks clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel fix to a clear limitation, strong empirical performance, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor ambiguities in experimental details, misleading presentation of ensemble results, and unclear wording in Section 4.4.
