- Method: uses encoder and decoder phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment classification, labeled as “belief propagation” — terminology is misleading.
- Writing is clear, but model motivation is vague and lacks theoretical justification.
- Major weakness: evaluation relies on an ensemble result that is unwarranted and obscures the contribution compared to single-model benchmarks.
- Unclear model composition and selection: how individual models were tuned and how the ensemble and “best” models were chosen is not reported.
- Unclear how benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned.
- Unclear whether benchmarks used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.
- Unclear whether RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.
- Tentative assessment: the second entry in Table 3 is likely the most comparable to the benchmarks in Tables 1 and 2.
- Minor: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser on tweets — parser accuracy on this data should be reported.
- Minor: several citations are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie).
- Overall: results look reasonable but model lacks strong motivation and the experimental methodology is not sufficiently clear.
