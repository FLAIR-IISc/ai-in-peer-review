- Introduces GB-RNNs that add a backward pass to recursive neural nets so phrase-level predictions inform word-level representations.
- Targets contextual sentiment analysis, showing improved disambiguation of word sentiment using broader context.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains and achieves state-of-the-art on the SemEval 2013 challenge.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly motivated.
- GB-RNN’s feedback mechanism is a logical extension of prior recursive/bidirectional work.
- Key contribution: hybrid word vectors combining unsupervised and supervised representations boost performance.
- Experimental results outperform standard RNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and the SemEval competition winner.
- Comparison clarity is insufficient—unclear if baselines used hybrid vectors and how “best” models were selected.
- Use of an ensemble for GB-RNNs creates an uneven comparison with single-model baselines.
- Terminology “belief propagation” is misleading; terms like “forward-backward” might be more accurate.
- Training details are brief; needs clearer description of backpropagation through structure and gradient challenges (vanishing/exploding).
- Requires clearer theoretical and architectural differentiation from Irsoy & Cardie (2015).
- Task description needs expansion—specify the exact SemEval subtask (likely contextual polarity) and parser constraints.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical results and valuable hybrid vectors.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate experimental detail, insufficient clarification versus prior work, and limited training procedure discussion.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address experimental details, model comparisons, and training clarifications.
