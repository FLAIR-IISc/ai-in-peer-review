This paper introduces "Belief Propagation Recurrent Neural Networks." This type of network extends "feedforward" (bottom-up) recurrent networks with a backward (top-down) step during inference. This phrase allows for level-wise predictions and embedding, allowing word embedding and feedback to the label.

The paper is well-written overall. The model is well-motivated and described. It advances the idea of bidirectional recurrent neural networks introduced by Ersoy and Kardy in 2008 (note that the reference to that paper is incomplete). For example, one contribution is the introduction of hybrid word vectors. Perhaps this is the most significant difference with previous work? Furthermore, the experimental comparisons are not entirely fair: do RNNs and B-RNNs also use hybrid vectors? How is the "best" model tuned and selected? How is the best chosen? It is worth noting that other methods do not use an ensemble. These points should be clarified. The term "belief propagation" is misleading, and the term "forward-backward" might be more appropriate.

Section 3.5 (Training) is a bit short and could use more detail. For example, I assume the training uses a back-propagation through structure algorithm (Goller 1996 paper can be cited). This algorithm states that the recurrent model has unfolded. In this case, it gives a very deep network to "reach" the word embedding part that has been updated. If I understand correctly, I think the authors could provide more detail on this very important step.

Section 3.6 is a bit confusing, and the difference between this model and the work of Ersoy and Kardy should be clarified.

Section 4.1 should be made better for readers outside the NLP community to understand. Semewal 2013's Task 2 has 2 subtasks: relevance and message polarity. I assume you are addressing the relevance polarity task. With a better presentation of the task, any parser can also understand the constraints.

Perhaps, sections 4.3 and 4.4 can be combined.

The paper is well-written overall and describes an interesting version of the bidirectional recurrent model.
