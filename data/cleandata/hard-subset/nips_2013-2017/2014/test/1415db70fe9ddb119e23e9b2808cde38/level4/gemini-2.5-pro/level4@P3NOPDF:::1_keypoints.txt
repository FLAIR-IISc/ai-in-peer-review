- Proposes BPRNNs: standard recursive nets extended with a top-down (backward) pass to let phrase-level predictions refine word-level embeddings/labels.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and the top-down feedback idea is intuitive.  
- Major novelty concern: insufficient differentiation from Irsoy & Cardie’s Bidirectional RNN (2014); Section 3.6 is confusing about what is new.  
- Unclear whether hybrid word vectors are the main novelty or a separate contribution—needs precise comparison to prior B-RNN work.  
- Fairness of experiments is questionable: did baselines (RNN, B-RNN) use the same hybrid vectors as BPRNN?  
- Model selection and hyperparameter tuning procedures are not described; how the reported “best” models were chosen is unclear.  
- Reporting appears to compare an ensemble of BPRNNs to single-model baselines, which is an unfair experimental setup.  
- Training description (Section 3.5) is too brief; authors should detail gradient computation/unfolding (e.g., backpropagation through structure) given deep top-down/bottom-up passes.  
- Terminology issue: calling the method “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; consider “forward-backward” or “bidirectional inference.”  
- Task description (Section 4.1) needs more detail for non-NLP readers and should state which SemEval 2013 subtask is addressed (likely contextual polarity).  
- Presentation fixes: consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for flow, and correct the incomplete Irsoy & Cardie reference.  
- Conclusion: paper is an interesting variant but must better distinguish novelty, ensure fair/rigorous experiments, and provide fuller training details to be convincing.
