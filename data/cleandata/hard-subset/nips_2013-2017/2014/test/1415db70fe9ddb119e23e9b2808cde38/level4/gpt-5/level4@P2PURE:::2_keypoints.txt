- Introduces a bidirectional recursive neural network: an upward pass followed by a downward pass to augment node representations with larger-context information.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.  
- The extension is simple, elegant, and effective.  
- Similar high-level idea to prior work [18], but uses a different downward computation that yields superior performance.  
- Method is well motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is generally well written.  
- Experimental results look excellent, but there are concerns about the experimental setup and comparisons.  
- Unclear whether the alternative bidirectional model from [18] in Table 2 used dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors—this could affect fairness of comparison.  
- The ensemble result, while impressive, should not be presented in a table comparing individual models.  
- The phrase “widely used mix of both” in Section 4.4 is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- Overall assessment: a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
