- Proposes a belief-propagation recursive neural network that adds a single top-down inference pass to standard bottom-up recursive architectures to let phrase-level predictions influence word-level embeddings and labels.

- Manuscript is clearly written, model well motivated and described.

- Extends Irsoy and Cardie’s bidirectional RNNs, but the citation appears incomplete and the distinction from that prior work needs clearer clarification.

- The hybrid word vector scheme is a notable technical contribution and may be the principal difference from prior work.

- Experimental comparisons raise fairness questions: did baselines (RNN and bidirectional RNN) use the same hybrid vectors?

- Unclear how models were tuned/selected and how the “single best” model was chosen; clarify model selection and tuning procedures.

- Note that competing methods are not ensembles, which should be addressed when comparing results.

- The term “belief propagation” may be misleading; consider using “forward–backward” instead.

- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; likely uses backpropagation through structure and needs details on unfolding and effective depth to update word embeddings.

- Section 3.6 is confusing and the distinction between this model and Irsoy & Cardie’s model must be made explicit.

- Section 4.1 should be made accessible to non-NLP readers and clarify that the paper addresses SemEval 2013 Task 2 contextual polarity and the related parser constraints.

- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could potentially be merged for clarity.

- Overall assessment: well written and presents an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive approach.
