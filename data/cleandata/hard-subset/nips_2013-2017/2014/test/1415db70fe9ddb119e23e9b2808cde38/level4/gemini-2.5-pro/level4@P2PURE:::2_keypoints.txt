- Addresses limitation of standard tree-structured RNNs by adding a top-down pass so higher-level context influences lower-level node representations.
- Proposes a bidirectional RNN that augments node representations with parent/context information after an initial bottom-up pass.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2.
- Extension is simple, elegant, well-motivated, clearly presented, and effectively executed.
- Downward-pass computation is novel despite conceptual similarity to prior work [18], and yields superior performance.
- Experimental results are strong but require clarification on whether the baseline bidirectional model from [18] in Table 2 used identical settings (dropout, extra training data, hybrid vectors).
- Ensemble result should be presented separately rather than alongside individual model comparisons.
- Request clarification on the meaning of "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4.
