- Proposes Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), adding a feedbackward step to feedphrase-level information back to words.  
- Claims improved contextual sentiment analysis and better performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge versus standard RNNs and baselines.  
- Paper is well-written and the model is well-motivated and clearly described.  
- Hybrid word vectors (unsupervised + supervised) are a significant contribution.  
- Unclear whether RNN and B-RNN baselines used hybrid vectors—experimental comparisons need clarification.  
- Authors should explain how models were tuned, selected, and how the single best model was chosen.  
- Note that other compared methods did not use ensembles, which may affect fairness of comparisons.  
- The name “Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks” may be misleading; consider “forward-backward recursive neural networks.”  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief; clarify use of back-propagation through structure and how the model is unfolded for training and word embedding updates.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing; clarify differences from Irsoy and Cardie (2008) and provide a detailed comparison.  
- Section 4.1 needs more context for non-NLP readers about SemEval 2013 contextual polarity task and parser constraints used.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 present overlapping experimental results and could be merged.  
- Overall, an interesting variant of bidirectional recursive models with strong results and potential for significant contribution.  
- Main weaknesses: experimental comparison details, training procedure description, and clarity in some sections.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
