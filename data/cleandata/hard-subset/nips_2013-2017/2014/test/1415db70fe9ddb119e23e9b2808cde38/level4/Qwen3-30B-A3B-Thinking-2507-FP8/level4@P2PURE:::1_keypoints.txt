- Introduces belief propagation recursive neural networks: adds a top-down inference step to feedphrase recursive nets to enable phrase-level predictions and feedback to word embeddings/labels.
- Paper is well written, model is well motivated and clearly described.
- Builds on Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks (Irsoy & Cardie, 2008), but the citation/discussion of that work is incomplete.
- Key novel component is hybrid word vectors, which may be the main distinction from prior work.
- Experimental details are unclear: did RNN and B-RNN baselines use hybrid vectors as well?
- Model selection/tuning and how the single best model was chosen (vs. ensembles) are not specified, but other methods do not use ensembles.
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward–backward" would be a more accurate description.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and should explain use of backpropagation through structure and the resulting very deep unfolding for updating word embeddings.
- Section 3.6 is confusing and fails to clarify differences from Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional model.
- Section 4.1 needs clearer explanation of SemEval 2013 Task 2 (two subtasks) and should state the paper addresses contextual polarity and parser constraints.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 are repetitive and could be merged for conciseness.
- Overall assessment: interesting extension of bidirectional recursive models with solid presentation.
