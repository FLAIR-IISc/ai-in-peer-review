- Proposes using encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment classification, labeled “belief propagation,” which is misleading because it is not the traditional algorithm.  
- Paper is generally well-written but the model’s motivation is unclear and lacks solid theoretical justification.  
- Major weakness: evaluation methodology—presenting ensemble results is unnecessary and obscures comparisons since benchmarks are not ensembles.  
- Composition and tuning of compared models are not specified, leaving unclear how ensemble/best models were selected.  
- Missing details on benchmark optimization: were Table 2 baselines tuned the same way, did they use the same augmented data as BP-RNN?  
- Unclear whether RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors, affecting comparability.  
- Given the missing information, the second entry in Table 3 seems the most comparable to the baselines in Tables 1 and 2.  
- Minor: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser for tweets—parser accuracy on this dataset should be reported.  
- Minor: several incomplete citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) need correction.  
- Overall: model yields reasonable results but requires stronger motivation and much clearer experimental methodology.
