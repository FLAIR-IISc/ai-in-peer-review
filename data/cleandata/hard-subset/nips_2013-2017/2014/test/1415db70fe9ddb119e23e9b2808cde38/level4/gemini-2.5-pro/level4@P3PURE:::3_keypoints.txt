- Proposes sentiment classifier using both encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading — the method is not the standard belief propagation algorithm.  
- Model motivation is vague and lacks theoretical justification for why combining encoder and decoder vectors helps.  
- Main results are reported for an ensemble, obscuring the contribution of the proposed architecture and preventing fair comparisons.  
- Experimental setup is unclear: how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected is not specified.  
- Unclear whether benchmark models in Table 2 were tuned equivalently and whether they used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear if baseline RNN and Bi-RNN used the same hybrid word vectors as the proposed model.  
- The second entry in Table 3 appears to be the most appropriate baseline comparison and should be emphasized.  
- Using an off-the-shelf parser on noisy tweet data is questionable; parser accuracy on this dataset should be reported.  
- Several bibliography entries are incomplete (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie 2014) and should be corrected.  
- Overall: results seem promising but the paper is weakened by poor motivation and insufficiently clear experimental methodology.
