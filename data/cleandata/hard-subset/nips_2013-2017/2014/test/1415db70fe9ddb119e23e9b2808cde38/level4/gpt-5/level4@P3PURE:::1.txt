This paper introduces “belief propagation recursive neural networks,” which extend feedforward (bottom-up) recursive networks with a backward (top-down) inference step. This enables phrase-level predictions and embeddings to feed back to word embeddings and labels. The paper is generally well written; the model is well motivated and clearly described.

Relation to prior work and contributions
- The approach extends the idea of Bidirectional Recursive Neural Networks introduced by Irsoy and Cardie (2008; note that the reference is incomplete and should be fixed). One notable contribution here is the introduction of hybrid word vectors; this may be the most important difference from previous work. Please clarify this point explicitly.
- Section 3.6 is somewhat confusing; the distinction between this model and the work of Irsoy and Cardie should be made clearer.

Terminology
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading in this context; “forward–backward” may be more appropriate.

Training details
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and should provide more detail. I assume training uses backpropagation through structure (the paper of Goller, 1996, could be cited). This implies unfolding the recursive model, yielding a very deep network to reach and update the word embedding layer. If this understanding is correct, please describe this step in detail, as it is crucial to the method.

Experimental comparisons and fairness
- The experimental comparisons are not entirely fair or sufficiently documented. Please clarify:
  - Did the RNN and B-RNN baselines also use hybrid word vectors?
  - How were the “best” models tuned and selected?
  - How was the single best model chosen?
- It is important to note that the other methods do not use an ensemble. Please discuss this to ensure a fair comparison.

Task description and clarity
- Section 4.1 should be improved for readers outside the NLP community. SemEval 2013 Task 2 has two subtasks: contextual and message polarity. I infer that you address the contextual polarity subtask; please make this explicit. With a clearer presentation of the task, the parser constraints would also be easier to understand.

Organization
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 to streamline the presentation.

Overall assessment
- Overall, the paper is well written and presents an interesting variant of the bidirectional recursive model. Addressing the concerns above—especially clarifying the relation to prior work, detailing the training procedure, and ensuring fair experimental comparisons—would significantly strengthen the submission.
