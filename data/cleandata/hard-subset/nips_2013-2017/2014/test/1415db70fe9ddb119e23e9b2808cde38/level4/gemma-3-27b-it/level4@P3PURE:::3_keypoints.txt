- Paper proposes using both encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment classification.  
- Calling the approach “belief propagation” is a misnomer and could mislead readers, as it is not the standard inference algorithm.  
- The motivation and theoretical underpinning for the proposed model are vague and weakly articulated.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is flawed because presenting an ensemble result is unjustified and obscures the BP-RNN architecture’s true performance.  
- The composition and tuning of compared models are unclear: how individual models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected is not specified.  
- Unclear whether benchmark models in Table 2 used the same data augmentation as BP-RNN or whether RNN/BiRNN used hybrid word vectors.  
- Given the uncertainties, the single-model entry in Table 3 is likely the most appropriate comparison to Tables 1 & 2.  
- Minor: using an off-the-shelf tweet parser is surprising; the parser’s accuracy on this dataset should be reported.  
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete and need correction.  
- Overall: model and results are reasonable, but motivation is weak and experimental methodology lacks necessary clarity.
