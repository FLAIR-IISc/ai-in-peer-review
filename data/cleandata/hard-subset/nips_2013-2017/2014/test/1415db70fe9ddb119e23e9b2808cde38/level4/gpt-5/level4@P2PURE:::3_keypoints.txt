- Proposes using encoder and decoder phrase vectors from a recursive autoencoder for sentiment classification.  
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading — the method is not actual belief propagation and will confuse readers.  
- Paper is well written but the motivation for the model is vague.  
- Lacks clear theoretical justification for the proposed approach.  
- Principal weakness: evaluation — reporting an ensemble result is unwarranted and obscures fair comparison to non-ensemble benchmarks.  
- Composition of compared systems is unclear, undermining reproducibility and validity of comparisons.  
- Unclear how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected.  
- Unclear how the benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned.  
- Unclear whether benchmarks used the same augmented data as BP-RNN.  
- Unclear whether the RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.  
- Minor: using an off-the-shelf parser for tweets requires reporting its accuracy on this data.  
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Overall: results look reasonable, but weak motivation and unclear experimental methodology limit the paper’s contribution.
