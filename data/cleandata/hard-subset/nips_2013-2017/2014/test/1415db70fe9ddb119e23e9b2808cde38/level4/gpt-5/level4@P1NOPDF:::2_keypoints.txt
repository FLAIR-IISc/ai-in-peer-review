- Introduces a bidirectional extension to recursive neural networks with an upward then downward pass that augments node representations and achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2012 Task 2.  
- The extension is simple, elegant, and effective.  
- Similar high-level idea to [18], but uses a different downward computation and shows superior performance.  
- Method is well motivated, clearly presented, and the paper is well written.  
- Experimental results are excellent but some setup details are unclear.  
- Question: In Table 2, did the alternative bidirectional RNN from [18] use dropout and the additional training data?  
- Question: Were hybrid vectors applied in the comparison with [18]?  
- Criticism: The ensemble result should not be presented alongside individual model performances.  
- Clarification requested: In Section 4.4, what exactly does the “widely used mix of both” refer to?  
- Overall verdict: A well-executed paper built on a simple and elegant idea.
