- Introduces GB-RNNs: recursive nets with a top-down "feedbackward" pass that lets phrase-level context influence word predictions, improving context-dependent interpretation.  
- Demonstrates SOTA on SemEval 2013 contextual polarity, outperforming prior winner by ~3% F1 and feedforward RNN baseline by 5.2% F1.  
- Key contributions: feedbackward mechanism, hybrid word vectors combining unsupervised and supervised embeddings, and empirical finding that dropout stabilizes training for high-dimensional embeddings.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly structured, and situates itself within compositional semantics and recursive networks literature.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior bidirectional RNNs (Irsoy & Cardie 2013) by using both forward and backward parent nodes in the feedbackward computation.  
- Originality & significance: principled architectural extension addressing context-dependent meaning with potential broader NLP applicability (e.g., word-sense disambiguation).  
- Clarity & quality: clear model description, equations, figures, sound experimental design, and ablation studies isolating contributions.  
- Technical soundness: appropriate use of backpropagation through structure, dropout, and thorough hyperparameter tuning; ablation convincingly isolates feedbackward and hybrid vector impacts.  
- Experimental fairness concern: unclear whether baselines used hybrid vectors and GB-RNN results use an ensemble while competitors are single models—this could confound comparisons.  
- Terminology issue: "global belief" is misleading (suggests probabilistic belief propagation); recommend clearer term like "forward-backward" or clarify meaning.  
- Insufficient technical detail: Section 3.5 needs explicit citation of Goller & Küchler (1996) and clarification of how backpropagation through structure interacts with the feedbackward pass and unfolded depth.  
- Comparison clarity needed: Section 3.6 should more clearly contrast backward computation rules with Irsoy & Cardie (2013), e.g., a concise table comparing equations.  
- Task/context explanation lacking: Section 4.1 should clarify focus on SemEval 2013 contextual polarity vs. message polarity and parser/phrase constraints for non-NLP readers.  
- Recommendation: technically sound, novel, and empirically strong work; accept with minor revisions addressing experimental details, terminology, and clarifications.
