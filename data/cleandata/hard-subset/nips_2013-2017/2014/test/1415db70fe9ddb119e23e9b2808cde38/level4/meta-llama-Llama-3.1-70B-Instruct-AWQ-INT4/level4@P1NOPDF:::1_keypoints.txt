- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks": a novel extension adding a top-down step to feed phrase-level predictions/embeddings back to word embeddings and labels.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and the model is clearly described.  
- Key contribution: introduction of hybrid word vectors, likely the main difference from prior bidirectional recursive models.  
- Experimental comparisons are potentially unfair or incomplete; important methodological details are missing.  
- Clarify whether baseline RNN and B-RNN models also used hybrid vectors.  
- Explain how models were tuned/selected and how the single best model was chosen; note other methods didn’t use ensembles.  
- The term "belief propagation" may be misleading; "forward-backward" could be more appropriate.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is brief; provide more details (e.g., use of back-propagation through structure and unfolding depth implications).  
- Unfolding the recursive model likely creates a very deep network impacting word-embedding updates—this critical step needs elaboration.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing; clarify distinctions from Irsoy and Cardie (2008).  
- Section 4.1 should better explain SemEval 2013 Task 2 subtasks (contextual vs. message polarity) and parser constraints for non-NLP readers.  
- Suggest merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 as they appear closely related.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and solid variant of bidirectional recursive models, but several clarifications and experimental details are required.
