In NLP, recursive neural networks (RNNs) have been used to build representations for snippets of text by recursively combining representations of words and shorter phrases. The process starts from word representations and proceeds up a given parse tree until the representation of the entire snippet is obtained at the root. However, this strictly bottom-up, unidirectional process does not allow information to flow back down the tree from larger to smaller contexts. This paper introduces a bidirectional extension that performs an upward pass followed by a downward pass, augmenting every node’s representation with information from larger contexts. The resulting system achieves state-of-the-art performance on Task 2 of SemEval 2012.

The proposed extension to established RNN-based methods is simple, elegant, and effective. Although the high-level idea is similar to [18], the computation used in the downward pass here differs, and the resulting performance appears superior.

The method is well motivated and clearly presented, and the paper is generally well written. The experimental results look excellent, but I have some concerns about the setup. For the alternative bidirectional RNN model from [18] reported in Table 2, were dropout and the additional training data used? Were hybrid vectors also employed in that comparison? Also, while the ensemble result is impressive, it does not really belong in a table comparing individual models.

What does the “widely used mix of both” refer to in Section 4.4? Overall, a well-executed paper based on a simple and elegant idea.
