- Proposal: Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" adding a top-down (backward) pass at inference to refine word-level embeddings and labels.  
- Writing and motivation: Paper is generally well-written with clear motivation and thorough model description.  
- Prior work citation: Builds on Irsoy & Cardie (2008) but the citation is incomplete and distinction from that work is not clearly articulated.  
- Hybrid word vectors: Use of hybrid word vectors is notable, but authors should clarify if this is the primary novelty versus prior work.  
- Experimental fairness: Unclear whether baseline RNN and B-RNN used hybrid vectors; this risks unfair comparisons.  
- Hyperparameter/model selection: Paper lacks detail on hyperparameter tuning and how the "single best" model was chosen across methods.  
- Ensemble vs single models: Proposed model is evaluated as an ensemble while baselines are single models, weakening the comparison.  
- Terminology: The term "belief propagation" may be misleading; "forward-backward" might better describe the architecture.  
- Training details: Section 3.5 is too condensed—need elaboration on training (likely back-propagation through structure; cite Goller 1996) and implications of very deep unfolded networks.  
- Clarity of discussion: Section 3.6 is unclear and should more explicitly differentiate the proposed model from Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Task description: Section 4.1 needs a clearer, more accessible description of the experimental task (specify which SemEval 2013 Task 2 subtask and justify parser constraints).  
- Structure/redundancy: Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could likely be merged for conciseness.  
- Overall assessment: Well-presented paper introducing an interesting variant of bidirectional recursive neural networks.
