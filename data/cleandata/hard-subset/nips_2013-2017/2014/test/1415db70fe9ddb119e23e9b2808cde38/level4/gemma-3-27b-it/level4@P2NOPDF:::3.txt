This paper introduces a method for sentiment classification that leverages both the encoding and decoding vectors from a recursive neural network auto-encoder. The authors term this approach “belief propagation,” a label that is misleading as it does not correspond to the standard inference algorithm and may cause confusion. While the paper is generally well-written, the motivation for the proposed model remains unclear and lacks a robust theoretical foundation.

The primary weakness lies in the evaluation methodology. Presenting results as an ensemble is problematic given that the benchmark comparisons are all single models. This obscures the performance attributable specifically to the proposed BP-RNN architecture. Crucially, the paper lacks detail regarding the model selection and tuning process. Several questions need to be addressed: How were the hyperparameters tuned for all models, and what criteria were used to select the final, reported configurations or the ensemble composition? How were the benchmark models in Table 2 tuned – specifically, did they utilize the same data augmentation techniques as the BP-RNN? Finally, did the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN benchmarks also employ hybrid word vectors? 

Without this information, assessing the fair comparison between the BP-RNN and existing approaches is difficult. Based on the current presentation, the second entry in Table 3—representing a single BP-RNN configuration—appears to be the most directly comparable to the benchmarks presented in Tables 1 and 2 when evaluating the core BP-RNN architecture.

Several minor points also warrant attention. The use of an off-the-shelf parser for tweets is surprising, considering the informal and often syntactically unconventional nature of Twitter data. Reporting the parser’s accuracy on this specific dataset would be beneficial. Furthermore, the citation for Irsoy and Cardie is incomplete, as are a number of other citations throughout the paper.

In summary, this paper presents a recursive neural network model for context-aware phrasal sentiment classification. The reported results are promising, but the lack of clear motivation and ambiguities in the experimental setup limit the impact of this work. Addressing the concerns regarding evaluation methodology, model tuning, and citation completeness would significantly strengthen the paper.
