- Paper proposes belief propagation recursive neural networks (BPRNN) adding top-down inference to standard bottom-up recursive nets to propagate phrase-level information to word-level embeddings/labels.
- Well written and clearly motivated; appears to extend bidirectional recursive networks of Irsoy & Cardie (incomplete citation).
- Hybrid word vectors are introduced and may be the main novelty — this should be emphasized and discussed explicitly.
- Experimental comparisons are under-specified and possibly unfair: unclear whether RNN and B-RNN baselines use hybrid word vectors.
- Need clarification on model selection: how “best” models are tuned/selected and how the “single best” model is chosen.
- Ensembling effects must be clarified and comparisons should be made like-for-like (other methods don’t use an ensemble).
- The term “belief propagation” is potentially misleading; “forward–backward” may be a clearer descriptor of the inference.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief: specify whether backpropagation through structure is used, how unrolling over the parse tree is performed, how gradients reach word embeddings, and techniques to manage depth/stability.
- Section 3.6 is confusing: concretely clarify differences from Irsoy & Cardie (message-passing schedule, parameterization, training objective).
- Section 4.1 should better explain SemEval 2013 Task 2 for non-NLP readers and state which subtask (contextual polarity) is addressed and how parser constraints apply.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 overlap and could be merged to streamline presentation and reduce redundancy.
- Overall recommendation: clarify terminology, training details, distinctions from prior work, and ensure fair, fully-specified experimental comparisons (especially regarding hybrid vectors and ensembling).
