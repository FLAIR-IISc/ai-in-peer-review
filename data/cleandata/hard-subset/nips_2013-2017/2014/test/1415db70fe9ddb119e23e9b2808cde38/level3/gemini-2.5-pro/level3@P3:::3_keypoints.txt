- Proposes GB-RNN: adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass to standard recursive NNs and concatenates bottom-up and top-down vectors for classification.  
- Applied to aspect-specific sentiment analysis on SemEval 2013 Twitter data; uses hybrid word vectors and dropout; reports state-of-the-art (ensemble) performance.  
- Strength: Simple, intuitive architectural solution for incorporating global sentence context into constituent predictions.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well-written and compares clearly to related bidirectional recursive models.  
- Strength: Empirical results appear strong and practical contributions (hybrid vectors, dropout) are useful.  
- Weakness: Framing as “Global Belief” and likening to belief propagation is misleading; the feedback decomposition lacks theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: Major evaluation concerns undermine the claimed contributions.  
- Weakness (evaluation): Headline result is an ensemble of five models compared against apparent single-model baselines, producing an unfair comparison.  
- Weakness (evaluation): Baseline details unclear—uncertain whether baselines received the same hyperparameter tuning, augmented training data, or hybrid vectors.  
- Weakness (evaluation): Results are reported on different data splits (dev-test vs final test) across tables, making direct comparisons and ablations difficult.  
- Minor issue: Using an off-the-shelf syntactic parser on noisy Twitter data without reporting parser performance or discussing parsing errors.  
- Minor issue: Several incomplete or sloppy citations in the bibliography.  
- Overall: Interesting and promising idea, but experimental ambiguities and methodological flaws require major revision before the contribution can be validated.
