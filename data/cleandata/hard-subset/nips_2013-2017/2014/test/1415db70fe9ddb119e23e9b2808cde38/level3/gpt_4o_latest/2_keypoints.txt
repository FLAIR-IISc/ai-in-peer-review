- Introduces GB-RNNs: recursive neural networks with upward (feedforward) and downward (feedbackward) passes on parse trees to incorporate global context.  
- Addresses limitation of standard RNNs in contextual disambiguation, particularly for sentiment analysis.  
- Novel extension distinct from prior bidirectional recursive models by leveraging both forward and backward parent information.  
- Technically well-founded with clear math, justification for design choices (e.g., transform matrix for feedback vectors), and comparisons to related work.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2, with ~5% improvement and further gains from ensembling.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation including ablation studies, hybrid word vector representations, and use of dropout.  
- Well-written paper with clear explanations, figures, and illustrative examples.  
- Concern: use of additional training resources (noisily labeled tweets, sentiment lexicons) may undermine fair comparisons with baselines.  
- Concern: ensemble results are not clearly separated from single-model performance, reducing transparency.  
- Concern: vague phrasing in Section 4.4 ("widely used mix of both") needs clarification about methods/datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on authors clarifying contribution of extra training data and resolving ambiguous descriptions.
