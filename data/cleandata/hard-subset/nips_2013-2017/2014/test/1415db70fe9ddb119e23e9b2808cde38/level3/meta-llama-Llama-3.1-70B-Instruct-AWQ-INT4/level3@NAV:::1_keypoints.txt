- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), adding a top-down inference step to standard RNNs to capture contextual information.  
- GB-RNNs allow phrase-level predictions and representations to influence word embeddings and labels, improving context-sensitive predictions.  
- Key contribution: hybrid word vectors that combine unsupervised and supervised representations to boost performance.  
- Experiments show GB-RNN outperforms standard RNN and bidirectional RNN baselines on contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and presents an interesting variant of bidirectional recursive models.  
- Relation to Irsoy & Cardie (2008) is incomplete; differences from that bidirectional recursive model need clearer clarification.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" may be a more appropriate name.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair/unclear—uncertain if baselines used hybrid vectors or consistent tuning/model selection.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too short; needs details on backpropagation-through-structure, unfolding, and how deep updates reach embeddings.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and should explicitly clarify differences from Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Section 4.1 needs clearer explanation for non-NLP readers, specifying which SemEval 2013 subtask is used and parser constraints.  
- Suggest merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for clarity.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 7/10.
