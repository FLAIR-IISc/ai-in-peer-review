- Introduces GB-RNNs, extending feedforward recursive neural networks with a backward step to let phrase-level predictions update word embeddings and labels.
- Proposes hybrid word vectors that combine unsupervised and supervised representations, a key contribution.
- Demonstrates strong experimental performance on contextual sentiment analysis, outperforming baselines and achieving state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly organized.
- Hybrid word vectors enable capturing both task-specific and general semantic information.
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" is a more appropriate description.
- Section 3.5 (training) is too brief and lacks details (e.g., back-propagation through structure, model unfolding).
- Section 3.6 is confusing and needs clarification on differences from Irsoy and Cardie’s work.
- Section 4.1 should more clearly present the task and parser constraints for non-NLP readers.
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 could be merged for improved organization.
- Originality lies in combining familiar techniques to extend recursive neural networks with a backward step.
- Significance stems from effectively using surrounding context to improve sentiment analysis, with broader NLP implications.
- Overall assessment: well-written and significant, but requires revisions on terminology, training details, and clearer comparisons to related work.
- Pro-acceptance points: novel model, impactful hybrid vectors, strong empirical results, clear presentation.
- Con-acceptance points: misleading terminology, insufficient training details, confusing related-work distinction, unclear task/parser exposition.
