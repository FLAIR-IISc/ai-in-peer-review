This paper introduces a novel extension to recursive neural networks (RNNs) called global belief recursive neural networks (GB-RNNs). The key idea is to add a downward pass to propagate larger-context information to all tree nodes, allowing phrase-level predictions and representations to give feedback to words. This approach addresses a limitation of standard RNNs, which cannot correctly predict phrase or word labels that are determined by context.

The authors provide a clear and well-motivated introduction to the problem and their proposed solution. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work, including neural word vectors, recursive neural networks, and sentiment analysis.

The GB-RNN model is described in detail, including the architecture, training procedure, and experimental setup. The authors evaluate their model on the SemEval 2013 Task 2 dataset and report state-of-the-art performance. They also provide an analysis of the model's performance, including the impact of different word vector representations and the effect of adding additional training data.

The experimental results look excellent, but some concerns are raised about the experimental setup. Specifically, it is unclear whether the baseline model in Table 2 was trained with dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors. Additionally, the ensemble result should not be presented alongside individual-model comparisons in the same table.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the benefits of their approach. The GB-RNN model is a significant extension to standard RNNs, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on a challenging task. However, some minor issues with the experimental setup and presentation of results detract from the overall quality of the paper.

Strengths:

* The paper introduces a novel and well-motivated extension to recursive neural networks.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough review of related work.
* The GB-RNN model is described in detail, including the architecture, training procedure, and experimental setup.
* The authors report state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 Task 2 dataset.

Weaknesses:

* Some concerns are raised about the experimental setup, including the unclear training procedure for the baseline model and the presentation of ensemble results alongside individual-model comparisons.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the model's performance, including the impact of different hyperparameters and the effect of adding additional training data.
