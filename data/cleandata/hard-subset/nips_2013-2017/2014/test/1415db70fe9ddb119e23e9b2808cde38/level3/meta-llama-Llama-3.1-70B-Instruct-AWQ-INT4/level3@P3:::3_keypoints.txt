- Introduces GB-RNN, an RNN extension with a feedbackward step for contextual information, evaluated on SemEval 2013 sentiment analysis.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the GB-RNN architecture and components.  
- Related work discussion is thorough and comparisons to other RNN models and sentiment techniques are provided.  
- Experimental evaluation is clearly presented with datasets, setup, and reported results.  
- Use of the term "belief propagation" for the feedbackward step is misleading and inaccurate.  
- Motivation for GB-RNN is weak; benefits of adding a feedbackward step are not well explained.  
- Experimental comparisons are not comprehensive and lack clear benchmarking against state-of-the-art.  
- Evaluation methodology is problematic: ensemble results may obscure comparisons and model tuning/selection procedures are unclear.  
- Unclear whether competing benchmarks were tuned or used the same augmented data, confounding comparisons.  
- Baseline model implementations (RNN, Bi-RNN) lack detail (e.g., use of hybrid word vectors), hurting reproducibility.  
- Minor issues: off-the-shelf tweet parser used without reported accuracy on tweets; some incomplete citations.  
- Technically sound overall, but claims are not strongly supported by theoretical analysis or experiments.  
- Originality is moderate: GB-RNN is novel but feedbackward ideas are not entirely new and comparisons are insufficient.  
- Significance is limited by weak motivation and unclear experimental methodology.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, clear presentation, reasonable experimental reporting.  
- Arguments for rejection: misleading terminology, unclear motivation, incomplete experimental evaluation, and vague baseline descriptions.
