- Introduces GB-RNN: a two-pass recursive architecture (bottom-up then top-down "feedbackward") to propagate global sentence context back to words.  
- Proposes a hybrid word vector combining fixed unsupervised embeddings with fine-tuned supervised embeddings.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and presents an intuitive extension of recursive models.  
- Shows strong empirical results, achieving state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 sentiment analysis.  
- Ablation study convincingly demonstrates the effectiveness of the hybrid word vectors.  
- Fails to clearly differentiate from prior bidirectional recursive work (Irsoy & Cardie 2012); Section 3.6 is confusing and does not establish clear novelty.  
- The term "Belief Propagation" is misleading—the method is effectively a forward-backward/two-pass inference scheme.  
- Technical clarity is lacking in training details (Section 3.5), especially backpropagation-through-structure and how second-pass gradients update embeddings.  
- Experimental setup needs clarification on tuning, baseline treatments, and whether results are single-best or ensembles to ensure fair comparison.  
- Reproducibility is hindered by missing implementation details; significant revision required.
