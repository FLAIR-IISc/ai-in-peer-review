- Proposes a novel hierarchy of VAR models to learn graphical structure of multi-scale spatio-temporal systems.
- Introduces a bidirectional RNN extension that propagates information top-down to capture complex multi-scale dynamics.
- Extension is simple, elegant, effective, and similar in spirit to prior work but uses different computations with superior performance.
- Method is well-motivated and clearly presented with a thorough analysis of hierarchical advantages.
- Uses physically derived priors (convection-diffusion) to estimate meaningful multi-scale graphical structures and replicate large-scale dynamics.
- Experimental results are excellent and demonstrate advantages over alternative approaches.
- Concern about comparability: alternative bidirectional RNN uses dropout, extra training data, and hybrid vectors—effects on performance are unclear.
- Suggest clarifying the phrase "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4.
- Recommend providing more details on the optimization procedure and hyperparameter selection.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with clear presentation and strong results, but requires clarifications and minor improvements.
- Pro-accept arguments: novel effective approach, clear presentation, strong experiments, use of physical priors.
- Con-accept arguments: experimental setup/comparability issues, unclear optimization/hyperparameter details, and minor clarity improvements needed.
