**Paper Review: Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks**

**Summary**

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture called Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) that extends traditional Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) by incorporating a feedbackward step to propagate larger-context information to all tree nodes. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of GB-RNNs on the task of contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge.

**Strengths**

* The paper introduces a simple yet elegant extension to traditional RNNs, which is well-motivated and clearly explained.
* The experimental results are excellent, with GB-RNNs outperforming several baselines and model ablations.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the model's performance, including a comparison with a bidirectional RNN and an ablation study.

**Weaknesses**

* The experimental setup raises some concerns, particularly regarding the comparison with the [18] baseline in Table 2. It is unclear whether the baseline was trained with dropout, additional training data, or hybrid vectors.
* The ensemble result should not be presented alongside individual-model comparisons in the same table, as it may be misleading.
* There is some ambiguity in Section 4.4, where the phrase "widely used mix of both" is unclear.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and thorough experimental evaluation. The authors are careful to evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results.

**Originality**

The paper introduces a novel combination of familiar techniques, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.

**Significance**

The results are important, as they demonstrate the effectiveness of GB-RNNs on a challenging task. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper introduces a novel and effective neural network architecture.
* The experimental results are excellent, with GB-RNNs outperforming several baselines and model ablations.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the model's performance.

Con:

* The experimental setup raises some concerns, particularly regarding the comparison with the [18] baseline.
* The ensemble result should not be presented alongside individual-model comparisons in the same table.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting this paper, as it makes a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing and demonstrates the effectiveness of GB-RNNs on a challenging task. However, I suggest that the authors address the concerns regarding the experimental setup and revise the paper accordingly.
