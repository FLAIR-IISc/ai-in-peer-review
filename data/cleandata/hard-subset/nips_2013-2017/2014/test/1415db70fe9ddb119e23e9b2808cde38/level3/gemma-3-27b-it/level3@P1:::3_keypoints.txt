- Introduces Global Belief RNNs (GB-RNNs) that add a feedbackward pass to let phrase-level predictions influence word representations.  
- Targets the limitation of standard feedforward RNNs in modeling context-dependent sentiment (e.g., aspect-specific sentiment).  
- Claims state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 and notable improvements over baseline RNNs.  
- Investigates dropout and hybrid unsupervised/supervised word vectors, providing useful training and representation insights.  
- Positions the work well within related literature on word vectors, recursive/recurrent NNs, and bidirectional RNNs.  
- The feedbackward pass is a novel architectural extension for incorporating global context.  
- The term “global belief” and the theoretical motivation for the feedbackward mechanism are vague and under-justified.  
- Reporting ensemble results without individual model performance obscures comparisons and harms reproducibility.  
- Unclear whether baselines (RNN/BiRNN) used the same hybrid word vectors, undermining fair comparisons.  
- Insufficient detail on model tuning and how the “best” models were selected.  
- Reliance on an off-the-shelf tweet parser is concerning; parser accuracy on this dataset is not reported.  
- Several citations are incomplete, reducing scholarly rigor.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, proposes a novel approach, shows strong empirical gains, and offers practical insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical grounding, unclear experimental methodology/ensemble dependence, and inconsistent experimental setup.  
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision to strengthen theory, clarify experimental setup and reporting (individual vs. ensemble), report parser reliability, and fix citations.
