

This paper introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), extending standard recursive neural networks with a top-down inference step to enable phrase-level predictions to influence word embeddings and labels. The authors argue this addresses context-dependent sentiment classification challenges, such as determining that "Android" is positive in "Android beats iOS" while "iOS" is neutral. The key innovation appears to be the hybrid word vector approach, combining unsupervised and supervised representations.

The paper is well-written and well-motivated, presenting an interesting variant of bidirectional recursive models that could advance contextual sentiment analysis. The experimental results on SemEval 2013 show a 3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs, with state-of-the-art performance. The hybrid word vectors are convincingly demonstrated to outperform purely supervised or unsupervised approaches.

However, several significant weaknesses prevent this from being a strong contribution. The term "belief propagation" is misleading (a "forward-backward" approach would be more appropriate), and the relationship to Irsoy and Cardie's (2008) bidirectional recursive model is inadequately clarified in Section 3.6. The experimental comparisons are questionable: it's unclear whether baselines used the same hybrid vectors, and the paper doesn't specify if models were selected using single best or ensemble approaches. Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief, lacking details on backpropagation-through-structure and how deep updates reach word embeddings. Section 4.1 requires better explanation of which SemEval 2013 subtask was addressed and parser constraints for non-NLP readers. Additionally, Sections 4.3 and 4.4 should be merged for improved clarity.

While the paper makes a technically sound contribution to contextual sentiment analysis, the incomplete comparisons with prior work, misleading terminology, and insufficient methodological details undermine its quality. The significance is limited by the narrow focus on sentiment analysis without broader applicability demonstration. The paper would benefit from addressing these concerns to make a stronger contribution to the field.
