- Paper introduces GB-RNNs: add a downward pass to propagate global context to all tree nodes.
- Motivation: feedforward architectures struggle with context-dependent phrase labels, e.g., aspect-specific sentiment.
- Model: conceptually simple top-down recursive computation combined with standard bottom-up pass.
- Empirical claim: state-of-the-art on SemEval 2012 Task 2, ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs.
- Strength: core idea is well-motivated and elegantly implemented.
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing.
- Strength: useful contributions on hybrid word vector representations and effective dropout for RNN training.
- Novelty: extends prior work by adding downward computation using both forward and backward parent nodes.
- Major concern: baseline comparison lacks crucial details (dropout, extra training data, hybrid vectors), preventing fair assessment.
- Presentation issue: ensemble results are inappropriately shown alongside single-model comparisons, leading to misleading interpretation.
- Clarity issue: ambiguous phrasing in Section 4.4 about "widely used mix of both" without clarification.
- Evaluation summary: Quality compromised by experimental ambiguities; Clarity generally good but undermined; Originality moderate; Significance limited by methodological issues.
- Recommendation: reject due to methodological concerns, but revision could address the issues.
