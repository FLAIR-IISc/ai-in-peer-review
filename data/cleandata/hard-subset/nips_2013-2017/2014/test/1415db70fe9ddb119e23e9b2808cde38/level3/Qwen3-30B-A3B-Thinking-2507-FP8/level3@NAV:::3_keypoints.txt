- Proposes GB-RNN with a top-down "feedbackward" pass to inject global sentence context for aspect-specific sentiment; reports ~3% F1 gain and claims SOTA on SemEval 2013.  
- Strength: Clear technical description and a reasonably motivated hybrid embedding approach; builds on prior recursive/bidirectional RNN work and shows empirical gains.  
- Weakness: Misleading terminology — calling the method "belief propagation" is inaccurate and undermines technical precision.  
- Weakness: Evaluation flaws — ensemble results obscure comparisons, tuning/ensemble rationale is unclear, and the most comparable baseline is not directly compared to the ensemble, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous methodology — unclear if baselines used the same hybrid embeddings, data augmentation, or tuning; model selection (e.g., "top 5 models") is not specified.  
- Weakness: Weak motivation — the paper fails to justify why the feedbackward mechanism is necessary or superior to existing bidirectional approaches.  
- Weakness: Minor issues — parser accuracy on tweets is unreported and several citations are incomplete.  
- Overall: Novel implementation but evaluation and methodological weaknesses prevent a convincing advance over bidirectional models; recommendation: reject.
