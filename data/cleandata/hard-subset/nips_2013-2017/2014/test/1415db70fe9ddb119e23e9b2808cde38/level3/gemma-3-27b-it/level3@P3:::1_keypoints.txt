- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) that add a backward pass to recursive nets to incorporate global context into phrase/word representations.  
- Motivated by limitations of feedforward RNNs for context-dependent sentiment (e.g., aspect-specific sentiment).  
- Claims ~3% F1 improvement on a contextual sentiment task and state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and presents a logical progression to the GB-RNN architecture.  
- Backward pass idea is a natural, interesting extension of bidirectional recursive models.  
- Hybrid word vectors (combining unsupervised and supervised representations) are a key contribution that may differentiate the work.  
- Insufficient comparison to Irsoy and Cardie (2012); distinctions (especially around equations 4 & 5) are unclear.  
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading; “forward-backward” or “bidirectional recursive” would be more accurate.  
- Experimental setup lacks clarity: unclear if baselines used the same hybrid vectors, affecting fairness of comparisons.  
- Model selection/tuning details are sparse—unclear whether results reflect single models or ensembles and how ensembles were averaged.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief and omits crucial reproducibility details (unfolding, gradient reach to embeddings, dropout hyperparameters/effects).  
- Section 3.6’s comparison with Irsoy and Cardie is convoluted and poorly explained.  
- Section 4.1 assumes reader familiarity; unclear which SemEval 2013 subtask is addressed and parser constraints need clearer explanation.  
- Results discussion would benefit from merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for improved flow.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension addressing a clear limitation, strong motivation, empirical improvements, and valuable hybrid word vector approach.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear distinction from prior work, opaque experimental setup, insufficient training details, misleading terminology, and confusing comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify differences with Irsoy & Cardie, expand experimental/training details, and revise terminology.
