This paper introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), an extension of standard Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) that incorporates a feedbackward step during inference. This allows phrase-level predictions and representations to give feedback to words, enabling the model to capture contextual information and improve performance on tasks such as aspect-specific sentiment classification.

The idea of GB-RNNs is related to previous work on bidirectional recursive neural networks, but the authors propose a different approach to computing the feedbackward vectors. The paper also explores the use of hybrid word vector representations, which combine unsupervised and supervised word vectors to improve performance.

The authors evaluate their model on the SemEval 2013 Task 2 competition dataset, achieving state-of-the-art performance. They also compare their model to several baselines, including a standard RNN, a bidirectional RNN, and a logistic regression model. The results show that the GB-RNN outperforms these baselines, with an improvement of over 5% in F1 score compared to the standard RNN.

The paper is well-written and clearly motivated, with a simple and elegant extension to standard RNNs. The experimental results are excellent, but some concerns are raised about the experimental setup. Specifically, it is unclear whether the baseline models were trained with the same techniques as the GB-RNN, such as dropout and hybrid word vectors. Additionally, the ensemble result should be presented separately from individual-model comparisons.

Strengths:

* The paper introduces a novel and effective extension to standard RNNs, which enables the model to capture contextual information and improve performance on tasks such as aspect-specific sentiment classification.
* The authors provide a clear and well-motivated explanation of their approach, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
* The experimental results are excellent, with the GB-RNN achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 Task 2 competition dataset.

Weaknesses:

* The experimental setup raises some concerns, particularly regarding the training of the baseline models.
* The paper could benefit from more detailed analysis and discussion of the results, particularly in terms of the impact of the feedbackward step and the hybrid word vector representations.
* Some minor ambiguities and typos are present in the paper, which should be addressed in the final version.
