- Proposes GB-RNN with a feedbackward inference step to capture contextual information and improve aspect-specific sentiment classification.
- Clearly motivates the need to capture context and explains limitations of traditional RNNs and differences from bidirectional RNNs.
- Model and components are well-explained and paper is generally well-written.
- Motivation is insufficiently strong and lacks theoretical justification for the feedbackward step.
- Evaluation is weak: results are presented as ensembles, making direct comparison difficult.
- Experimental methodology is unclear: composition of compared models, tuning procedures, and ensemble/best-model selection are not specified.
- Unclear whether baselines used the same hybrid word vectors, tuning, or augmented data as GB-RNN; Table 2 benchmarks lack comparability details.
- Results are reasonable and GB-RNN appears to outperform others on contextual sentiment, but further validation is needed.
- Originality: novel RNN extension and new feedbackward inference contribution.
- Significance: potential applicability to other context-dependent NLP tasks (e.g., machine translation, question answering).
- Recommendations: add theoretical justification, clarify experimental details and tuning, report non-ensemble results, and provide parser accuracy for tweets.
