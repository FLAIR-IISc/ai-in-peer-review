- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) with a top-down inference step allowing phrase-level predictions to influence word embeddings and labels.  
- Proposes a hybrid word vector combining unsupervised and supervised representations as a core innovation.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and presents an interesting bidirectional recursive variant for contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Reports a 3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs and state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013.  
- Hybrid word vectors are convincingly shown to outperform purely supervised or unsupervised vectors.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" would be more accurate.  
- Relationship to Irsoy and Cardie (2008) bidirectional recursive model is inadequately clarified (Section 3.6).  
- Experimental comparisons are questionable: unclear if baselines used the same hybrid vectors and whether model selection used single best or ensembles.  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too brief, lacking details on backpropagation-through-structure and depth of updates to word embeddings.  
- Section 4.1 needs clarification on which SemEval 2013 subtask was addressed and parser constraints for non-NLP readers.  
- Sections 4.3 and 4.4 should be merged for clarity.  
- Incomplete comparisons with prior work, misleading terminology, and insufficient methodological detail undermine the paper’s contribution.  
- Significance is limited by narrow focus on sentiment analysis without demonstrating broader applicability.
