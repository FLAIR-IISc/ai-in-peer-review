- Introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks: adds a feedbackward step so phrase-level predictions influence word embeddings and labels.  
- Enables context-dependent compositional vector representations for variable-length phrases.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on contextual sentiment (SemEval 2013).  
- Key novel contribution: hybrid word vectors combining unsupervised and supervised representations.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and presents an interesting bidirectional recursive variant.  
- Relation to Irsoy & Cardie (2008) is inadequately addressed; differences need clearer, explicit clarification.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" is a more appropriate descriptor.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair or unclear—uncertain whether baselines used hybrid vectors or matched tuning/selection procedures.  
- Training section is too short and lacks crucial details (backpropagation-through-structure, network unfolding, how deep updates reach embeddings).  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and should explicitly state differences from Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Recommend clarifying experimental setup (use of hybrid vectors in baselines, single best vs ensemble model selection).  
- Suggest expanding training details and describing optimization and gradient flow clearly.  
- Recommend merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for clarity.  
- Paper quality and significance are high; claims are well-supported and results are impressive.  
- Originality is strong and the approach has broader applicability (e.g., word-sense disambiguation).
