Introduces GB-RNN: extends recursive neural networks with a backward pass from parse-tree root to leaves to inject global sentence context.  
Addresses limitation of feedforward RNNs by incorporating sentence-level context into phrase- and word-level representations.  
Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 Task 2, outperforming prior baselines including the competition winner.  
Investigates dropout regularization and a hybrid word-vector representation combining unsupervised and supervised vectors.  
Positions contribution within prior work on distributional semantics, compositional models, and bidirectional RNNs, and differentiates from Irsoy & Cardie.  
Core idea is conceptually elegant and well-motivated, especially for aspect-specific sentiment tasks.  
Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and accessible.  
Experimental setup is detailed and includes ablation studies and comparisons to strong baselines.  
Empirical results are strong and compelling, showing clear improvements over existing methods.  
Weakness: Baseline comparisons lack clarity—uncertain if baselines used identical hyperparameters, dropout, additional training data, and hybrid vectors.  
Weakness: Presentation in Table 2 is misleading by directly comparing ensemble results to single-model baselines.  
Weakness: Ambiguous description of additional training data ("widely used mix of both")—datasets and proportions unspecified.  
Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify baseline training conditions and cleanly present single-model vs ensemble results.
