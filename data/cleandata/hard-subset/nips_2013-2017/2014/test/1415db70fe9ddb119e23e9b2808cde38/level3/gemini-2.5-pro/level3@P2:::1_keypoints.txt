- Proposes GB-RNN: a two-pass (bottom-up then top-down) extension to recursive neural networks that refines local word/phrase representations using global sentence context.  
- Well-motivated solution to context-dependent compositionality; natural extension of standard RNNs.  
- Strong empirical results: achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013, ~3% F1 gain over standard RNN and outperforms prior competition systems.  
- Useful practical contributions: hybrid word vectors (fixed unsupervised + trainable supervised) and effective use of dropout for RNN regularization.  
- Naming concern: "Global Belief" is misleading (evokes probabilistic belief propagation); suggest clearer names like "Forward-Backward RNN" or "Top-Down Refinement RNN."  
- Comparison to related work (Irsoy & Cardie 2013) is confusing and lacks intuitive explanation of why the proposed info flow is superior.  
- Reproducibility issues: training/backprop details for the two-pass architecture are insufficient—need clear gradient flow and how backward vectors update forward parameters and embeddings.  
- Experimental clarity issues: ensemble vs single-model reporting is inconsistent and not emphasized; the specific SemEval subtask and parser constraints should be explicitly stated.  
- Overall assessment: novel and effective contribution with strong results; recommend acceptance contingent on improved clarity and reproducibility.
