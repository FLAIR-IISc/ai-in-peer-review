- Introduces GB-RNNs with a feedbackward inference step to incorporate global context for sentiment (example: "Android beats iOS").  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors (unsupervised + supervised) and uses dropout to improve training.  
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013, reporting ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear mathematical formulation and a conceptually interesting idea.  
- Misleading terminology: calling the feedbackward step "belief propagation" is inaccurate and confusing.  
- Lacks theoretical motivation or formal justification for why the feedbackward step is necessary or effective.  
- Major evaluation flaws: ensemble results presented without justification, obscuring fair comparison to baselines.  
- Unclear model selection/tuning procedures and how the “top 5 GB-RNN models” were chosen/trained.  
- Inconsistent evaluation protocol: unclear whether baselines used same tuning, data augmentation, or hybrid vectors.  
- Minor issues: parser accuracy on tweets not reported and several incomplete citations.  
- Overall: technically interesting but weakened by methodological and theoretical shortcomings.  
- Recommendation: reject; requires substantial revision to clarify experiments, justify ensemble use, and situate relative to related work.
