- Introduces GB-RNNs, a novel RNN extension with a feedbackward step to propagate global context in trees, applied to contextual sentiment analysis.  
- GB-RNNs achieve state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 and outperform several baselines and ablations.  
- The model proposal is simple, elegant, well-motivated, and clearly explained.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including comparisons with bidirectional RNNs and ablation studies.  
- Concern: comparison with baseline [18] is unclear—uncertain if dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors were used.  
- Concern: ensemble results are presented alongside single-model results in the same table, which may mislead.  
- Concern: ambiguous wording in Section 4.4 (“widely used mix of both”) needs clarification.  
- The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims.  
- The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- The approach is an original and significant combination of familiar techniques that advances NLP state of the art.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, pending revisions to address experimental setup and presentation concerns.
