- Proposes GB-RNNs, extending recursive neural networks with a backward pass to propagate global context to parse-tree nodes.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2012 Task 2 and motivates the approach with an example showing feedforward RNN limitations.  
- Differentiates from prior work by using a specific downward computation mechanism and shows better performance than related bidirectional recursive networks.  
- Introduces hybrid word vectors (unsupervised + supervised) that improve performance, but Section 4.4 is ambiguous about the exact mix proportions.  
- Experimental setup lacks clarity about the baseline [18], specifically whether it used dropout, extra data, or the same hybrid vectors.  
- Presenting ensemble results alongside single-model results in Table 2 is misleading; ensembles should be reported separately.  
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly explains the GB-RNN architecture and experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective method, strong empirical results, clear motivation and presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental transparency, misleading ensemble presentation, and ambiguity in hybrid vector composition.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve experimental clarity and result presentation.
