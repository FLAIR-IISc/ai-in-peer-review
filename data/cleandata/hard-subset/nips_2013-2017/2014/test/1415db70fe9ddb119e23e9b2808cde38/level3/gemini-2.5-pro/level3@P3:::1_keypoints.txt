- Proposes GB-RNN: adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass to recursive neural nets so word/phrase vectors are refined by global sentence context.
- Introduces hybrid word vectors: concatenation of fixed pre-trained embeddings and trainable task-specific vectors.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 aspect-specific sentiment task.
- Well-motivated and intuitive solution for context-dependent meaning; elegant variant of bidirectional recursive architectures.
- Hybrid vector design is simple and empirically effective (ablation supports benefit).
- Generally clear and well-written explanations of standard RNN and GB-RNN architecture.
- Terminology is misleading: "Global Belief" / "belief propagation" implies iterative probabilistic message passing, but the model uses a single deterministic top-down pass.
- Insufficient conceptual and empirical comparison to prior bidirectional recursive models (Irsoy & Cardie 2008); update-rule advantages are underexplained and lack ablation.
- Training details are too sparse: need clear description of backpropagation-through-structure for the two-pass architecture and how gradients update embeddings.
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair/unclear: ensemble GB-RNN vs single-model baselines and unclear tuning parity for baselines.
- Paper lacks accessibility for a general NIPS audience—e.g., explain SemEval 2013 Task 2 and the parser constraint keeping labeled phrases in subtrees.
- Minor organization issue: merge Sections 4.3 and 4.4 into a unified "Experiments and Analysis" for better flow.
