- Introduces GB-RNNs, adding a downward pass to RNNs to propagate global context for improved contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 Task 2, showing practical effectiveness.  
- Builds on prior work in compositional semantics and bidirectional/recursive networks, clearly situating the contribution.  
- Novel and elegant idea: backward pass for contextual refinement is intuitive and well-justified.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow; figures (e.g., Fig. 2) clarify the model and computation.  
- Thorough exploration of hybrid word vectors and use of dropout indicates careful model optimization.  
- Main experimental-setup concern: fairness of comparisons to baselines is unclear and could affect conclusions.  
- Baseline ambiguity: unclear whether [18] used dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors, hindering assessment.  
- Inappropriate comparison: ensemble results are presented alongside single-model results, which is misleading.  
- Vague description in Section 4.4 about the “widely used mix of both” (datasets/lexicons) reduces reproducibility.  
- Limited error analysis: paper lacks detailed case-level analysis showing where GB-RNN succeeds or fails.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong benchmark results and clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: unresolved experimental fairness and missing implementation details undermine confidence and reproducibility.  
- Overall: strong and promising paper but acceptance should be conditional on addressing experimental and clarity issues.  
- Requested revisions: report training parameters for the [18] baseline, separate ensemble results from individual-model comparisons, and precisely describe the data/lexicon mixture used.
