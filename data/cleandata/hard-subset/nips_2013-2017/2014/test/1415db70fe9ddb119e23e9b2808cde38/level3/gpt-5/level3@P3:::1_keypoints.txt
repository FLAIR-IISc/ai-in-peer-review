- Proposes GB-RNN: a single top-down feedback pass added to tree RNNs to enable context-sensitive, aspect-specific labeling.
- Introduces hybrid word vectors that concatenate fixed unsupervised embeddings with smaller task-tuned supervised dimensions.
- Empirical claim: single GB-RNN outperforms RNN and bidirectional recursive baselines; ensemble achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval-2013 Twitter sentiment.
- Model is related to prior bidirectional recursive/inside–outside work (Irsoy & Cardie; Le & Zuidema) but differs in top-down computation and combination.
- Clear motivation for top-down pass to let phrase/sentence context influence word/target labels.
- Hybrid vectors are simple, effective, and empirically supported.
- Good experimental ablations (RNN, Bi-RNN, GB-RNN) and practical training choices (dropout, two-stage training).
- Writing is generally clear and model is easy to implement on standard RvNNs.
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too terse; request full details on loss placement, backprop-through-structure, gradient flow, and parameter updates.
- Need clarification on unfolding/update depth: do leaf errors propagate to root then back down; are forward/backward params updated jointly?
- Clarify exactly how dropout is applied (which vectors, shared vs independent across passes).
- Provide optimization specifics: AdaGrad schedule, early stopping, gradient clipping, and epoch counts.
- Section 3.6 is confusing; explicitly contrast with Irsoy & Cardie (conditioning, parameterization, capacity) with a small diagram and shape annotations.
- Terminology “Global Belief”/“belief propagation” is misleading; suggest renaming to reflect top-down/forward–backward mechanism.
- Experimental fairness concerns: explicitly state that baselines use the same hybrid vectors/dropout; report single-model scores separately from ensemble results.
- Clarify hyperparameter search spaces and model selection criteria for all systems.
- Dataset/task details are unclear: explicitly name SemEval-2013 Task/Subtask, and report parser coverage and how non-subtree targets were handled in noisy Twitter data.
- Scope is limited to one domain; recommend adding a second dataset/task to demonstrate generality.
- Organizational fixes: expand Sections 3.5/3.6, make Section 4.1 accessible to non-NLP readers, and merge ablation results into a single table.
- Originality is incremental relative to bidirectional recursive models; main novelty is hybrid vectors plus a clean single backward inference step—need to sharpen empirical distinctions.
- Significance is practical and likely adopted, but would be stronger with broader evaluation and clearer baselines.
- Specific author questions: do leaf gradients update frozen unsupervised embedding part; are backward vectors tied to forward embeddings; sensitivity to backward dimension nd and root V vs identity; robustness to parse errors.
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated, strong results, useful hybrid vectors, reproducible and practical.
- Cons for acceptance: incremental novelty, terminology overreach, insufficient training/comparison transparency, limited evaluation scope.
- Overall: positive leaning review contingent on clearer positioning vs prior work, detailed training/experimental reporting, and broader evaluation.
