## Review of "Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks"

This paper introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), an extension of standard Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) designed to improve contextual sentiment analysis. The core idea is to incorporate a backward pass during inference, propagating global context information down to individual nodes in the parse tree, allowing for more accurate sentiment prediction of phrases dependent on broader sentence context. The authors demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2012 Task 2, and effectively motivate the approach through a clear example highlighting the limitations of purely feedforward RNNs. 

The work builds upon existing research in recursive neural networks [19, 25, 26] and contextual modeling [5, 18, 21]. It differentiates itself by employing a specific downward computation mechanism for contextualization, which, as the authors demonstrate, yields superior performance to a closely related bidirectional recursive network [12, 14]. The introduction of hybrid word vectors, combining unsupervised and supervised representations, is also a valuable contribution, showing practical gains in performance. The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the GB-RNN architecture and experimental setup.

However, despite the strong results, several points raise concerns. The experimental setup lacks clarity regarding the baseline comparison with [18]. It is crucial to know if this baseline was trained with dropout, additional data, or the same hybrid vectors used in the GB-RNN experiments to fairly assess the reported improvements. Furthermore, presenting the ensemble result alongside single-model performance in Table 2 is misleading. Ensemble methods invariably outperform individual models and should be presented separately for a clear comparison. The description in Section 4.4 regarding “widely used mix of both” is ambiguous; specifying the exact proportions of unsupervised and supervised vectors would improve clarity.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and effective extension of RNNs, addressing a limitation in contextual understanding.
*   State-of-the-art performance on a benchmark dataset.
*   Clear and well-motivated approach with strong empirical results.
*   Well-written and organized paper.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of transparency in the experimental setup regarding baseline comparisons.
*   Misleading presentation of ensemble results alongside single-model results.
*   Ambiguity in the description of hybrid word vector composition.



**Overall:** This paper presents a strong contribution to the field of recursive neural networks and contextual sentiment analysis. Addressing the concerns regarding experimental clarity and presentation would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
