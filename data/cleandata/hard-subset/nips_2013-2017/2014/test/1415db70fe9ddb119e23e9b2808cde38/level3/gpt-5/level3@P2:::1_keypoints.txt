- Proposes GB-RNNs: a tree-structured RNN extended with a top-down “feedbackward” pass, concatenating bottom-up and top-down representations for final predictions.  
- Reports ~3% F1 improvement over a standard RNN and state-of-the-art on SemEval-2013 Twitter contextual sentiment.  
- Finds dropout helps training high-dimensional recursive models and hybrid embeddings (frozen unsupervised + small trainable tail) outperform single representations.  
- Training uses BPTS and mini-batch AdaGrad with two-stage incorporation of external resources (NRC lexicon, noisy tweets, sentiment treebank).  
- Model is conceptually related to prior bidirectional/inside-outside recursive models; novelty is mainly the specific parameterization (use of both parents + V transform).  
- Soundness: equations are plausible but several implementation/training details are under-specified, harming reproducibility.  
- Key missing training details: precise BPTS through both passes, gradient flow into hybrid embeddings, dropout placement, initialization, and optimization settings.  
- Empirics: core claims supported by results and ablations, but fairness requires confirming baselines used identical embeddings, dropout, parser, and tuning budgets.  
- Experimental reporting should include per-model hyperparameter ranges/selection, single-model vs ensemble results, variance across random seeds, and comparisons with/without external resources.  
- Comparison to Irsoy & Cardie is confusing/incomplete; needs a controlled empirical comparison with equalized parameter budgets and complexity/runtime reporting.  
- Terminology “global belief”/“feedbackward” risks confusion with probabilistic belief propagation and should be clarified or renamed.  
- §3.5 (Training) needs substantial expansion: loss placement, unlabeled node handling, joint vs staged updates, pretraining schedule, AdaGrad settings, early stopping, and depth handling.  
- §3.6 should present a clearer mathematical contrast to prior bidirectional recursive work with consistent notation and parameter counts.  
- §4.1 must specify which SemEval subtask is used, how labeled spans are provided, dataset splits, and discuss parser constraints and impact on noisy tweets.  
- Suggest merging §§4.3–4.4 for clearer ablation/resource narrative.  
- Originality: approach is incremental relative to prior bidirectional recursive models; hybrid embeddings are practical but not fundamentally novel.  
- Significance: useful for target-specific sentiment and potentially other tasks, but reliance on accurate constituency parses for tweets may limit robustness; single-model/no-external results should be emphasized.  
- Specific requests: clarify target conditioning and multi-entity disambiguation, report per-class F1 and SMS vs tweet breakdown, and provide statistical significance over multiple runs.  
- Ablation requests: equalize parameter counts when comparing to Irsoy & Cardie, test variants without V and varying nd.  
- Reproducibility requests: specify unsupervised vector source/dimensionality, code release, and exact preprocessing/tokenization/OOV strategy.  
- Practical analysis requested: report runtime and memory overhead of the top-down pass.  
- Pros: clear motivation, simple effective extension, solid empirical gains, and thorough ablations.  
- Cons: incremental novelty, underspecified training/comparison details, potentially misleading terminology, and unclear dataset/task description for general readers.  
- Overall: well-motivated with encouraging results; recommend acceptance conditional on improving clarity, prior-work positioning, and experimental reporting.
