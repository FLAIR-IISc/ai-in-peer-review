- Introduces GB-RNNs: adds a top-down "feedbackward" inference pass to recursive neural nets to inject global sentence context into phrase-level predictions.  
- Motivated by examples where feedforward RNNs fail on context-dependent sentiment (e.g., "Android beats iOS").  
- Shows state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2, outperforming prior RNN baselines and the competition winner by ~3% F1.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors combining unsupervised embeddings with task-specific fine-tuning.  
- Demonstrates dropout improves training and model performance.  
- Strength — Originality: novel, simple, well-motivated top-down context propagation distinct from prior bidirectional RNNs.  
- Strength — Quality & Significance: technically sound with theoretical grounding and meaningful empirical gains; generalizes beyond sentiment.  
- Strength — Clarity: well-written paper with precise equations, pseudocode, and effective visualizations.  
- Weakness — Experimental ambiguity: baseline details (use of hybrid vectors, dropout, training data) are not specified, threatening reproducibility and fair comparison.  
- Weakness — Methodological confusion: ensemble (top-5 average) reported alongside single-model results, conflating ensemble gains with model architecture gains.  
- Weakness — Ambiguous claims: unclear wording in Section 4.4 about what "both" refers to regarding the hybrid vectors.  
- Pro acceptance argument: solid idea, clean implementation, and compelling results advancing contextual compositional models.  
- Con acceptance argument: unclear experimental design and reporting obscure the true source of the 3% improvement.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept with major revision; authors should (1) specify baseline training conditions, (2) separate/remove ensemble results from main table, and (3) clarify what "both" denotes in Section 4.4.
