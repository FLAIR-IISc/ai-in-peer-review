- Introduces GB-RNNs: a bidirectional recursive architecture adding a top-down “feedbackward” pass to standard bottom-up tree composition.  
- Downward pass propagates root/global context to all nodes; final prediction concatenates upward and downward representations.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 Task 2 with notable gains over standard RNNs and prior bidirectional-recursive variants.  
- Explores hybrid word vectors (fixed unsupervised embeddings + small supervised component) and dropout, with useful analysis.  
- Related to prior bidirectional recursive work (Irsoy & Cardie; Le & Zuidema) but differs in top-down computation and separate root/parent-conditioned transforms.  
- Model is technically sound, well motivated, and architectural differences are clearly explained.  
- Experimental setup has major ambiguities: unclear if baseline in Table 2 used same ingredients (dropout, extra data, hybrid vectors).  
- Inclusion of an ensemble system alongside single-model baselines is misleading and should be separated.  
- Section 4.4 contains unclear phrasing (“widely used mix of both”); specifics of resources and combination are needed.  
- Reproducibility requires explicit training details for all systems (data per model, exact hyperparameters, seeds) and code/embeddings release.  
- Writing and illustrations are generally excellent and clear.  
- Originality is moderate: downward pass is known, but the specific computation and hybrid embedding use are novel and effective.  
- Significance is high for NLP practitioners using tree-structured models due to strong empirical gains and ease of adoption.  
- Pros: simple elegant extension with strong gains; clear writing and analysis; demonstrated SOTA.  
- Cons: unclear fairness of baseline comparisons; ensemble conflated with single-model results; incomplete/ambiguous training details.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on clarifying experimental setup and separating ensemble vs single-model comparisons.
