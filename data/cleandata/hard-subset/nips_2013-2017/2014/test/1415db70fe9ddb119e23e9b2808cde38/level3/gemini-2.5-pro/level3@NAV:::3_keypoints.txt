- Introduces GB-RNN: a standard bottom-up recursive neural network augmented with a top-down “feedbackward” pass to propagate sentence-level context back to words/phrases.  
- Aims to address context-dependent meaning and differs from prior bidirectional recursive models by using a deterministic two-pass encoding/decoding.  
- Clear writing and strong motivation for an important problem in contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Empirical results on SemEval 2013 appear to be state-of-the-art.  
- Theoretical motivation is vague and lacks clear justification for the proposed mechanism.  
- The term “belief propagation” is misleading because the method is deterministic, not probabilistic inference.  
- Major evaluation flaw: main results are reported for a 5-model ensemble, making comparisons to non-ensemble baselines unjustified.  
- Experimental details are missing (e.g., how baselines were tuned, whether they used the same augmented data and hybrid word vectors).  
- Unclear whether baseline RNNs and Bi-RNN used the novel hybrid vectors, preventing isolation of the feedbackward architectural contribution.  
- The most comparable baseline (second entry in Table 3) shows smaller, less convincing gains.  
- Minor issues: use of an off-the-shelf tweet parser without reporting its accuracy on noisy data.  
- Minor issues: several incomplete citations.  
- Overall: reasonable results but contribution undermined by weak theory, flawed/unclear experimental methodology, and unfair comparisons making claims hard to validate.
