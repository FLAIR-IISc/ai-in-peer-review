- Introduces GB-RNN: adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass to feedforward recursive neural nets to refine node predictions using full-sentence context.  
- Motivated by aspect-specific sentiment analysis where word/phrase sentiment depends on global sentence context.  
- Final node prediction concatenates feedforward and feedbackward vectors for context-aware classification.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors (pre-trained unsupervised + task-specific supervised) and uses dropout for better training.  
- Achieves reported state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 sentiment task, outperforming prior winners and baselines.  
- Conceptually related to bidirectional recurrent/recursive models and unfolding recursive auto-encoders rather than classical belief propagation.  
- Strength: addresses an important limitation of compositional models and uses an intuitive, principled top-down refinement.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance and useful ablation showing benefits of hybrid vectors and dropout.  
- Weakness: "Global Belief" / belief propagation framing is misleading and lacks clear theoretical grounding.  
- Weakness: update rule motivation (Eq. 3) is not well-justified relative to alternatives like Irsoy & Cardie (2013).  
- Major experimental concern: headline result is an ensemble of 5 models, improperly compared to likely single-model baselines.  
- Major experimental concern: unclear whether baselines received identical training conditions (hybrid vectors, dropout, data augmentation, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Need clarity on core result — single GB-RNN score (81.6 F1) should be explicitly highlighted and compared fairly.  
- Minor issues: surprising use of an off-the-shelf parser for noisy Twitter data without discussion of parser errors; some citations incomplete.  
- Overall: technically reasonable and promising, but significance is weakened by experimental clarity/fairness issues and overstated novelty.
