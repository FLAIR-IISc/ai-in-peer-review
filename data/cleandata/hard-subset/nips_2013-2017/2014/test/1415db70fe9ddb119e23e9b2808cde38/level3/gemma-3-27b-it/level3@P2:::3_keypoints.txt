- Introduces GB-RNNs: augments recursive neural networks with a feedbackward pass to let phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Twitter sentiment dataset.  
- Explores dropout and a hybrid unsupervised-supervised word vector representation.  
- Situates work within compositional semantics, recursive RNNs, distributional semantics, and sentiment analysis literature.  
- Feedbackward pass is novel relative to feedforward and bidirectional recursive models.  
- Addresses an important and challenging problem (contextual sentiment in social media).  
- Strong empirical performance supports the proposed approach.  
- Thorough experimentation across model variants and word representations.  
- The term “global belief” and theoretical motivation for backward propagation are vague and under-justified.  
- Experimental methodology is unclear, notably reporting ensembles without single-model baselines.  
- Comparisons are inconsistent; unclear whether baselines received the same tuning, data augmentation, and word vectors.  
- Reliance on an off-the-shelf tweet parser without reporting its accuracy could confound results.  
- Several citations lack full bibliographic details, hindering reproducibility.  
- Paper is technically sound and generally well-written but experimental details need more precision.  
- GB-RNN is original in its feedback mechanism; hybrid word vectors are a useful contribution.  
- Results are significant but their impact is reduced by methodological concerns.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong benchmark results, comprehensive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical justification, unclear methodology, ensemble reliance, insufficient baseline/tuning details, missing parser accuracy.  
- Recommendation: reject pending revisions to provide single-model baselines, full tuning details, stronger theoretical justification for global belief, and parser accuracy reporting.
