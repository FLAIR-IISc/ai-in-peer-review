- Proposes GB-RNN: adds a feedbackward (top-down) pass to recursive neural networks to propagate global sentence context to all parse-tree nodes.
- Targets a key failure of standard RNNs: inability to handle context-dependent sentiment (e.g., word sentiment changing by sentence).
- Combines bottom-up feedforward and top-down feedbackward passes so phrase/word representations include global context.
- Reports state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2, improving F1 by ~3% over standard RNNs and outperforming competition baselines.
- Theoretically distinguishes itself from prior bidirectional work (Irsoy & Cardie) by using both forward and backward parent vectors in feedbackward computation.
- Introduces hybrid word vectors (pre-trained unsupervised + task-specific supervised) that outperform pure unsupervised or supervised vectors.
- Strengths: elegant, well-motivated idea with minimal added complexity, thorough experiments, clear explanations, and likely reproducibility.
- Concern: Comparison with Irsoy & Cardie is unclear—uncertain whether baselines used dropout, hybrid vectors, or extra training data, which may confound the claimed advantage.
- Concern: Ensemble GB-RNN result is reported alongside single-model results, making direct comparison inappropriate; ensemble results should be separated.
- Concern: Claim that a “widely used mix” of unsupervised and supervised vectors is superior is vague and lacks explicit comparison to common baselines like GloVe or word2vec.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with strong empirical results, but experimental comparisons need clarification.
- Clarity assessment: Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear model and experiment descriptions.
- Originality assessment: Bidirectional processing is not entirely novel, but the specific feedbackward implementation is a novel, well-motivated contribution.
- Significance assessment: Addresses a meaningful limitation in compositional NLP with clear practical impact and potential to generalize to other tasks.
- Overall recommendation: Strong NIPS submission; accept with minor revisions to clarify experimental comparisons and presentation of ensemble results.
