- Introduces GB-RNN: a recursive neural network extension with a downward pass to propagate larger-context information to all tree nodes.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 for contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and presents a simple yet elegant extension to standard RNNs.  
- Provides thorough explanation of GB-RNN components and training procedure.  
- Experimental results are strong, outperforming multiple baselines and ablation models.  
- Uses a hybrid word vector representation (unsupervised + supervised), noted as a valuable contribution.  
- Concern: unclear whether the bidirectional-RNN baseline in Table 2 used dropout, extra training data, or hybrid vectors, hindering fair comparison.  
- Concern: ensemble result is presented alongside individual-model comparisons in the same table, which may unfairly advantage GB-RNN.  
- Ambiguity: unclear reference to a "widely used mix of both" in Section 4.4 that needs clarification.  
- Related work discussion is thorough but lacks clearer differentiation from prior approaches (e.g., Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional-RNN).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and potentially highly influential with minor revisions needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, clear presentation, strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental setup ambiguities, inappropriate mixing of ensemble and single-model results, and insufficient comparison against closely related models.
