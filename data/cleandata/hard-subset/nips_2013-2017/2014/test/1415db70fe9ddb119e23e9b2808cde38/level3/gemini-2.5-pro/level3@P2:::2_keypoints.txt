- Proposes GB-RNN: adds a top-down feedback pass to feedforward RNNs to propagate global sentence context to all tree nodes for context-dependent classification.
- Motivated by tasks like aspect-specific sentiment where local word labels depend on global context (e.g., "Android beats iOS").
- Introduces dropout in RNNs and a hybrid word vector (concatenate fixed unsupervised + trainable supervised vectors) as practical contributions.
- Achieves strong empirical gains: ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNN and state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.
- Provides clear, well-written exposition with helpful equations, diagrams, and positioning relative to prior work.
- Offers a detailed theoretical and empirical comparison to bidirectional RNNs [12], arguing the downward computation is different and superior for this task.
- Ablation study shows hybrid word vectors outperform purely supervised or unsupervised alternatives.
- Primary weakness: experimental setup ambiguous — unclear if baselines used the same pre-training on large noisy sentiment data and two-stage training as GB-RNN.
- If baselines did not use the same external data/training scheme, reported gains may be due to training procedure rather than architecture.
- Presentation issue: best GB-RNN result is an ensemble of 5 models shown alongside single-model results from others; single-model and ensemble should both be reported for transparency.
- Reviewer leans toward acceptance given the elegant idea, solid execution, and results, but urges clarification of experimental details in the rebuttal.
