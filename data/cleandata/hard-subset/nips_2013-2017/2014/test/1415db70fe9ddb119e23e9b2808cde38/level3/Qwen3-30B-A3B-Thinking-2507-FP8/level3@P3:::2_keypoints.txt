- Paper introduces GB-RNNs: recursive neural nets with a backward pass that propagates root/context information down the parse tree.  
- GB-RNNs allow phrase-level predictions to influence word-level labels, addressing context-dependent labeling failures in standard RNNs.  
- Key technical novelty: backward pass uses both forward and backward parent information, differing from prior bidirectional recursive models.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 Task 2 for contextual sentiment, improving F1 by ~3% over standard RNNs.  
- Introduces hybrid word vectors combining unsupervised and supervised representations, which outperform either alone.  
- Strong empirical work: clear motivation, elegant method, thorough ablation studies, and compelling experimental results.  
- Writing and presentation are excellent with clear figures and explanations, making the model easy to understand.  
- Major experimental concern: comparison with bidirectional RNN baseline [12] lacks details (dropout, extra data, hybrid vectors) and may be misleading.  
- Inappropriate mixing of ensemble results with individual model comparisons in Table 2; ensembles should be reported separately.  
- Ambiguity in Section 4.4 about what "widely used mix" of word vectors refers to—needs clarification.  
- Claims of superiority are plausible but insufficiently validated due to missing baseline implementation details.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant for context-dependent NLP tasks; recommend minor revisions to clarify experiments and baseline comparisons.
