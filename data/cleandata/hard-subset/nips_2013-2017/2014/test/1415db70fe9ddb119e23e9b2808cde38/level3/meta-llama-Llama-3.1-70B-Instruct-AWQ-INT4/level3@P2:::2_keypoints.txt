- Introduces GB-RNNs: an RNN extension with a feedbackward step allowing phrase-level predictions to inform word representations.  
- Feedbackward mechanism captures contextual information and improves aspect-specific sentiment classification.  
- Related to bidirectional recursive neural nets but proposes a different method for computing feedbackward vectors.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors that combine unsupervised and supervised embeddings to improve performance.  
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013 Task 2 and achieves state-of-the-art results with >5% F1 improvement over standard RNN.  
- Compared against baselines (standard RNN, bidirectional RNN, logistic regression); GB-RNN outperforms them.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and presents a simple, elegant extension to RNNs.  
- Experimental setup concerns: unclear whether baselines used the same training techniques (dropout, hybrid vectors), potentially confounding comparisons.  
- Ensemble results should be presented separately from individual-model comparisons.  
- Lacks detailed analysis on the specific impact of the feedbackward step and the hybrid word vector representations.  
- Contains minor ambiguities and typos that should be addressed in the final version.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective extension, clear exposition, excellent experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental setup concerns, need for deeper analysis, and presentation issues.
