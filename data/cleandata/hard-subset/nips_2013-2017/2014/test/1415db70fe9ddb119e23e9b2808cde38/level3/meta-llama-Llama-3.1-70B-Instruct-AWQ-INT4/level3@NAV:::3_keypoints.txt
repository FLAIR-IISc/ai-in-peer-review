- Proposes GB-RNN: extends Recursive Neural Networks with a feedbackward step so phrase-level predictions inform word representations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 and outperforms other baselines.  
- Paper is generally well-written with a clear explanation of the GB-RNN model and its components.  
- Motivation for the model is somewhat vague and lacks sufficient theoretical justification.  
- Evaluation is a major weakness due to reliance on ensemble results, making fair comparisons difficult.  
- Composition, tuning, and selection criteria for compared models and ensembles are unclear.  
- Benchmarks in Table 2 lack details on whether they were tuned similarly or used the same augmented data.  
- Unclear whether RNN and Bi-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors, affecting comparison validity.  
- Insufficient information about the Stanford Parser applied to tweets, including its accuracy on that data.  
- Results are reasonable and GB-RNN shows promise; highlights value of hybrid word vectors and dropout.  
- Suggest adding stronger theoretical justification for GB-RNN and its components.  
- Suggest clarifying compared-model compositions, tuning details, and reporting non-ensemble results for fair comparison.  
- Suggest reporting parser accuracy and other preprocessing details used on tweet data.
