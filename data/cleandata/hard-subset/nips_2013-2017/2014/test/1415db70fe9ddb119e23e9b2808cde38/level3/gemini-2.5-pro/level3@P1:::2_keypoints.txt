- Proposes GB-RNN: a two-pass recursive network with a top-down "feedbackward" pass to inject global sentence context into phrase/word representations.  
- Addresses contextual labeling problems (e.g., aspect-specific sentiment) where local meaning depends on full sentence.  
- Combines feedforward and feedbackward vectors for final classification during inference.  
- Uses hybrid word vectors (pre-trained unsupervised + task-specific supervised) and dropout for improved training.  
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013 Task 2 and achieves state-of-the-art performance, outperforming the original competition winner.  
- Reports a ~3% F1 improvement over a standard RNN baseline, demonstrating the feedbackward pass's value.  
- Paper is very clear, well-written, and well-motivated with intuitive examples.  
- Novelty: introduces a new formulation of the backward pass and explicitly compares to related bidirectional recursive work (Irsoy & Cardie) both theoretically and empirically.  
- Technical quality: sound model, strong experiments, and useful ablation studies isolating the feedbackward contribution.  
- Concerns about fairness of comparisons: unclear if baselines were re-implemented and hyperparameters tuned equivalently.  
- Comparison in Table 1 may be misleading by presenting an ensemble of five GB-RNNs against single-model baselines; single-model results should be shown.  
- Reproducibility issue: ambiguous description of the training protocol with additional data ("two-step" vs "jointly") needs clarification.
