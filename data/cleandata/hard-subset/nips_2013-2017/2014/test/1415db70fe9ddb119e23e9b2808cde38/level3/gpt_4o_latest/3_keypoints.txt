- Proposes GB-RNNs: RNN extension with a feedbackward inference step letting phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2, outperforming standard RNNs and the competition winner.  
- Introduces hybrid word vectors combining unsupervised and supervised embeddings.  
- Applies dropout and a two-step training process, and uses additional training data to improve performance.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the GB-RNN architecture and motivation.  
- Feedbackward mechanism is an interesting and practical extension with demonstrated empirical gains.  
- Provides detailed ablation studies validating design choices.  
- Evaluation methodology is unclear: model tuning, ensemble composition, and benchmark comparisons are insufficiently described.  
- Use of ensembles obscures how much improvement comes from GB-RNN versus ensembling.  
- Calling the method “belief propagation” is misleading given standard usage of that term.  
- Reliance on an off-the-shelf parser for noisy tweets raises concerns about parsing accuracy not addressed.  
- Feedbackward step lacks theoretical justification, and some citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.  
- Recommendation: accept only if authors clarify evaluation/ensembling, justify feedbackward theoretically, address parser concerns, and fix citations.
