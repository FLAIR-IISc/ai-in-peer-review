- Proposes GB-RNN: a two-pass recursive neural network that composes bottom-up phrase vectors then propagates global sentence context top-down to update representations for context-dependent classification.  
- Applies GB-RNN to aspect-specific sentiment analysis, showing how word sentiment can change with sentence context.  
- Introduces hybrid word vectors: concatenation of fixed unsupervised vectors with task-specific trainable vectors.  
- Applies dropout to regularize recursive neural network training.  
- Reports state-of-the-art results on SemEval 2013 Task 2, outperforming the competition winner and several baselines.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show the hybrid vectors outperform purely unsupervised or purely supervised alternatives.  
- Empirical results strongly support the value of the feedbackward (top-down) pass compared to standard feedforward RNNs.  
- Paper is technically sound overall but omits crucial training details that hurt reproducibility.  
- Section 3.5 lacks explanation of how backpropagation is adapted for the two-pass GB-RNN and how gradients from the feedbackward pass update parameters.  
- Comparison tables mix ensemble and single-model results, creating potential unfairness and requiring clearer presentation.  
- Writing and organization are generally good; introduction and motivation are clear and Figure 2 is helpful.  
- The model name "Global Belief Recursive Neural Network" is misleading because it does not perform probabilistic belief propagation.  
- The comparison to Irsoy and Cardie (2014) is technically dense and conceptually unclear; a clearer, diagrammatic comparison is needed.  
- Section 4.1 assumes domain knowledge; it should briefly explain SemEval Task 2 and parser constraints for general readers.  
- Experimental narrative could be streamlined by merging related sections describing the final setup.  
- The two-pass idea is an elegant extension of recursive models and broadly applicable to other NLP tasks (e.g., WSD, NER).  
- The hybrid vector technique is a simple, practical contribution likely to be widely adopted.  
- Novelty is somewhat tempered by closely related concurrent bidirectional recursive work; architectural differences should be better distinguished.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated problem, strong empirical gains, and a practically useful hybrid vector contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient training details for reproducibility, unclear distinction from related work, and misleading terminology.  
- Recommended revisions: detail the backpropagation for GB-RNN, clarify comparison to Irsoy & Cardie (2014), consider renaming the model, and clarify single-model vs. ensemble experimental comparisons.
