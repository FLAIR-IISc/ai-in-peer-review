- Introduces GB-RNNs: a feedbackward step added to recursive neural nets to enable context-dependent phrase and word labeling.  
- Model computes feedforward and feedbackward vectors at each parse-tree node and concatenates them for final classification.  
- Proposes a hybrid word-vector representation (unsupervised + supervised) and applies dropout; ablation studies show these help.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 sentiment, improving F1 by ~3% over standard RNNs and beating the competition winner.  
- Strength: Feedbackward idea is well-motivated with clear examples showing standard RNN failures on context-dependent sentiment.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation with ablation studies isolating contributions.  
- Strength: Compelling empirical gains over prior models.  
- Strength: Advances understanding of incorporating context into compositional models.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis and error analysis explaining why feedbackward works.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to other context-modeling approaches (e.g., window-based or other long-distance models) are limited.  
- Weakness: Computational cost of the feedbackward step is not discussed.  
- Weakness: Hybrid word-vector approach needs stronger justification beyond the reported table results.  
- Presentation: Well-written, clearly organized, precise notation and helpful figures.  
- Significance: Addresses an important limitation of compositional NLP models with potential applications beyond sentiment.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
