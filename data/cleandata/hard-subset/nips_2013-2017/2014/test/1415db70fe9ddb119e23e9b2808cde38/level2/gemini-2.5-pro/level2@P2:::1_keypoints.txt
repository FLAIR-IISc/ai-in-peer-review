- Introduces GB-RNN: adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass to standard tree-structured RNNs so node representations incorporate global sentence context.  
- Final node predictions use concatenated feedforward and feedbackward vectors, enabling early phrases to be influenced by later words.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors combining pre-trained unsupervised embeddings with task-specific trainable vectors; dropout shown to help training.  
- Clearly situates work among compositional models and distinguishes architecture from concurrent bidirectional recursive RNNs (Irsoy & Cardie).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 aspect-specific sentiment, >5% F1 gain over standard RNN baseline.  
- Technical contribution is elegant, novel for tree structures, and addresses a significant limitation of feedforward recursive models.  
- Paper is well-written with clear equations, helpful diagram, and a focused comparison to related bidirectional models.  
- Ablation study on hybrid vectors effectively supports the auxiliary word-representation contribution.  
- Lacks deeper qualitative/error analysis to reveal GB-RNN failure modes and contextual phenomena it still struggles with.  
- Training description is under-specified—unclear whether feedforward parameters are frozen or fine-tuned during the two-stage training.  
- Minor clarity issues: the term "feedbackward" is non-standard, and the method for constraining parser subtrees is not explained.  
- Overall conclusion: minor issues aside, the paper is a high-quality, significant contribution and suitable for NIPS.
