- Proposes GB-RNNs that add a feedbackward inference step so phrase-level predictions influence word-level interpretations for context-dependent sentiment.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on the SemEval 2013 sentiment task.
- Builds on extensive prior work in RNNs, compositional semantics, and unfolding autoencoders, and situates GB-RNN relative to feedforward architectures.
- Related work coverage is reasonable but should be broadened to include recent attention mechanisms addressing context dependency.
- Architecture is clearly explained with well-defined equations and effective illustrations (Fig. 2).
- Hybrid use of unsupervised and supervised word vectors is well-motivated and empirically beneficial.
- Ablation studies (Table 2) provide thorough analysis and validate the contribution of the feedbackward step.
- Training details (dropout, regularization) are carefully reported, supporting result validity.
- Theoretical comparison to Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional RNN is superficial and needs deeper analysis of differing feedback mechanisms and impacts.
- No explicit analysis of parameter count vs. performance or the computational cost of the backward pass.
- Evaluation limited to SemEval; additional datasets/tasks with varied contextual dependency are needed to support generalizability claims.
- Lacks error analysis and examples of failure cases to illuminate remaining weaknesses.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel context-incorporating method, clear presentation, strong empirical performance, and solid ablations.
- Arguments against acceptance: superficial related-work comparison, insufficient computational/complexity analysis, and missing error/generalization studies.
- Overall: valuable contribution likely warranting acceptance if authors address the comparison to Irsoy & Cardie, provide computational cost/parameter analysis, and add error/generalizability analyses.
