- Introduces GB-RNN, an RNN extension with a feedbackward inference step to capture context-dependent information.  
- Applied to contextual sentiment analysis and evaluated on the SemEval 2013 challenge.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation, motivation, and stated advantages over existing methods.  
- Provides a thorough related-work analysis, including comparisons to bidirectional RNNs and other context-aware models.  
- Uses intuitive examples and gives a detailed experimental setup (dataset, metrics, hyperparameter tuning).  
- GB-RNN outperforms several baselines and achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013.  
- Includes analysis of the feedbackward step’s impact and the use of hybrid word vector representations.  
- Novel extension addressing a limitation of existing RNNs, with potential applicability to other NLP tasks.  
- Main limitation: evaluation is tied to a single dataset/task, so generality to other tasks is uncertain.  
- Paper lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability.  
- May be less accessible to readers not familiar with RNNs and NLP.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8.5/10, Significance 9/10.
