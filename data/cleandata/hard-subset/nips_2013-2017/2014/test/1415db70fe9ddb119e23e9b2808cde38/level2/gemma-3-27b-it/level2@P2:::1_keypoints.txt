- Proposes GB-RNNs with a feedbackward pass to let phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations for contextual sentiment.  
- Novelty: feedbackward pass is an intuitive, genuinely new extension addressing RNNs' contextual limitations.  
- Empirical strength: achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 and outperforms baselines and prior winners.  
- Clear motivation and explanations for the problem, model components, and training.  
- Thorough experimentation: comparisons, ablations, and analyses of word vectors and dropout.  
- Practical insights: benefits of dropout and a hybrid unsupervised–supervised word vector approach.  
- Theoretical comparison to Irsoy & Cardie (2014) is superficial and lacks justification for claimed disentanglement.  
- Model complexity: feedbackward pass and extra parameters (e.g., V matrix) raise training difficulty and interpretability concerns.  
- Parser dependency: reliance on parsing may introduce errors and limit applicability across languages/domains; impact not evaluated.  
- Limited ablation: need clearer separation of the feedback pass’s contribution from hybrid word vectors (e.g., feedback-only results).  
- Presentation issues: some tables are dense and hard to quickly interpret.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical results and thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments for rejection: weak theoretical comparison, model complexity and parser reliance, and clarity/presentation shortcomings.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical comparison, discuss parser error impact and computational cost, and improve presentation clarity.
