- Introduces GB-RNN: adds a top-down "feedbackward" pass to standard feedforward Recursive Neural Networks to incorporate global sentence context.  
- After a bottom-up pass computes node representations, a top-down pass propagates root information to leaves and combines forward/backward vectors for final predictions.  
- Proposes hybrid word vectors that concatenate fixed unsupervised embeddings with trainable supervised vectors, and demonstrates dropout utility for RNN training.  
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013 sentiment task, outperforming standard RNNs, baselines, and achieving state-of-the-art (≈3% F1 gain over standard RNN).  
- Paper is technically sound with thorough experiments and careful baseline comparisons, supporting the claimed gains.  
- Includes an ablation study isolating the hybrid word vector contribution, strengthening empirical conclusions.  
- Training details (AdaGrad, cross-validation) are well-described.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; motivation and Figure 2 clearly illustrate the two-pass architecture.  
- Suggests clarifying the recursive definition of the backward pass (Equation 3) with a more general formulation for readability.  
- Helpful explicit comparison to Irsoy & Cardie's bidirectional RNN for situating and differentiating the contribution.  
- Core idea is novel for tree-structured data and provides a principled analogue of bidirectionality for trees.  
- Significant impact: applicable to tasks needing local predictions informed by global context (e.g., aspect-specific sentiment, WSD, coreference).  
- Questions remain about sensitivity to parse errors on noisy social media text and the additional computational cost of the feedbackward pass.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance.
