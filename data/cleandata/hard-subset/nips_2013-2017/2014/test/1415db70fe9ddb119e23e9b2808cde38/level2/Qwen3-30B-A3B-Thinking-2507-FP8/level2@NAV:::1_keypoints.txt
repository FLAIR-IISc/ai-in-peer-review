- Proposes GB-RNNs: adds a "feedbackward" inference step to recursive neural networks for context-dependent phrase/word labeling.
- Targets limitation of standard RNNs on labels requiring broader sentence context (e.g., aspect-specific sentiment like "Android" in "Android beats iOS").
- Claims: GB-RNNs yield ~3% F1 improvement over standard RNNs on contextual sentiment, hybrid unsupervised+supervised word vectors beat either alone, and feedbackward enables correct context-dependent predictions.
- Experiments on SemEval 2013 show GB-RNNs outperform standard RNNs and the competition winner.
- Provides a thorough theoretical and empirical comparison to bidirectional RNNs.
- Justifies hybrid word vectors and dropout with ablation studies.
- Includes sufficient reproducibility details (hyperparameters, training procedures).
- Model description is clear with well-explained equations and illustrative figures.
- Motivation for addressing context-dependent sentiment is well-articulated.
- Novelty is incremental and overlaps with Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional RNN work.
- Lacks clear differentiation from other context-integration methods like attention mechanisms.
- Significance is moderate; a 3% F1 gain may not justify broad adoption without broader applicability evidence.
- Needs more discussion of limitations, e.g., parser dependency and computational overhead.
- Overall: technically sound, modest contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify novelty and limitations.
