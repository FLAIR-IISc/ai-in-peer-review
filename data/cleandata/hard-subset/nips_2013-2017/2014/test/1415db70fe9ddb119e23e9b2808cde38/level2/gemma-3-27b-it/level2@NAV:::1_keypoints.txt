- Proposes Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) that add a feedbackward pass to standard RNNs to let phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations.  
- Targets contextual sentiment analysis, aiming to resolve context-dependent sentiment toward specific entities.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013 Twitter sentiment benchmark.  
- Reports additional findings on dropout regularization and benefits of combining unsupervised and supervised word vectors.  
- Motivation and relation to prior work (including bidirectional RNNs) are clearly articulated.  
- Experimental setup is thorough, with comparisons to baselines, competition systems, and ablation studies.  
- Description of the backward pass (Eq. 3) and how it differs from Irsoy & Cardie (2012) is unclear.  
- The specific advantages of the proposed feedback formulation are not fully convincing or theoretically justified.  
- Increased parameter count is acknowledged, but computational cost and scalability analysis are insufficient.  
- Inclusion of extra training data (NRC lexicon, movie reviews) appears ad-hoc and per-dataset impacts are not individually analyzed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension of RNNs, strong empirical results, clear motivation, and thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical justification of backward pass, limited discussion of complexity/scalability, and ad-hoc data usage.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting clearer explanation of the backward pass and a discussion of computational complexity.
