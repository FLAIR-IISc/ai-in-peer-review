This paper introduces a novel extension to Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs), called Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), which incorporates a feedbackward step during inference to capture context-dependent information. The authors apply this model to the task of contextual sentiment analysis and demonstrate its effectiveness on the SemEval 2013 challenge.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed model, its motivation, and its advantages over existing approaches. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of related work, including a comparison with other models that incorporate context information, such as bidirectional RNNs.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to clearly explain complex concepts and provide intuitive examples to illustrate the benefits of the proposed model. The authors also provide a detailed description of the experimental setup, including the dataset, evaluation metrics, and hyperparameter tuning.

The results presented in the paper are impressive, with the GB-RNN outperforming several baseline models and achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge. The authors also provide an analysis of the model's performance on different aspects of the task, such as the impact of the feedbackward step and the use of hybrid word vector representations.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel extension to RNNs that addresses a specific limitation of existing models. The idea of incorporating a feedbackward step during inference is innovative and has the potential to be applied to other NLP tasks.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. One of the main limitations is the reliance on a specific dataset and task, which may not be representative of other NLP tasks or datasets. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed model and its potential applications to other tasks.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed model is innovative and effective. The results are impressive, and the analysis is thorough. The paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of NLP and is a strong candidate for acceptance.
