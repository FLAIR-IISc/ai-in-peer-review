- Proposes Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) that add a feedbackward pass so phrase-level predictions influence word-level representations.  
- Demonstrates improvements on a Twitter sentiment dataset and achieves state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013.  
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear example showing limitations of feedforward RNNs.  
- Related work section is adequate and correctly positions the contribution relative to bidirectional recursive models.  
- Technical description of GB-RNN, including forward and feedbackward equations and the V matrix, is clear and concise.  
- Introduces a useful hybrid word vector representation combining unsupervised and supervised features.  
- Training details (backpropagation through structure, dropout) are standard but sufficiently described.  
- Empirical gains are modest (≈3% F1 over standard RNN), limiting impact.  
- Ablation studies are helpful but not extensive; need more granular analyses (e.g., V matrix configurations, feedback dimensionality).  
- Paper acknowledges parameter increase from feedbackward pass but lacks detailed computational cost analysis.  
- Comparison to Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional RNN is superficial and needs deeper theoretical or experimental contrast.  
- Heavy reliance on SemEval dataset; additional datasets would strengthen generalizability claims.  
- Writing is generally clear and organized, but the down-arrow (↓) notation for feedback vectors is visually distracting.  
- Originality: the feedback mechanism in recursive nets and the hybrid vectors are novel contributions.  
- Significance: tackles contextual sentiment analysis and suggests promising directions for other NLP tasks despite modest gains.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand ablations, deepen comparisons, and test additional datasets.
