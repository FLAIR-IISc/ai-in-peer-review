**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper introduces Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs), a novel extension of Recursive Neural Networks (RNNs) that incorporates a feedbackward step during inference to capture contextual information. The GB-RNN is designed to address the limitations of traditional RNNs in predicting phrase or word labels that are determined by context. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of GB-RNNs on the task of contextual sentiment analysis, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the SemEval 2013 challenge. They also explore the benefits of combining unsupervised and supervised word vector representations and applying dropout to improve RNN training.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The GB-RNN builds upon previous work on RNNs, which have been widely used in natural language processing tasks. The authors acknowledge the limitations of traditional RNNs in capturing contextual information and propose a novel solution by incorporating a feedbackward step. They also draw inspiration from bidirectional recurrent neural networks and bidirectional recursive models, but propose a distinct approach that uses both forward and backward parent nodes to compute the feedbackward vectors.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The GB-RNN introduces a new way of incorporating contextual information into RNNs, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **State-of-the-art performance**: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of GB-RNNs on the SemEval 2013 challenge, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the task of contextual sentiment analysis.
3. **Combination of unsupervised and supervised word vector representations**: The authors explore the benefits of combining unsupervised and supervised word vector representations, which is a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing.
4. **Dropout**: The authors apply dropout to improve RNN training, which is a useful technique for preventing overfitting.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The GB-RNN introduces additional computational complexity due to the feedbackward step, which may be a limitation for large-scale applications.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors perform extensive hyperparameter tuning, which may be time-consuming and require significant computational resources.
3. **Limited evaluation**: The authors evaluate the GB-RNN on a single task (contextual sentiment analysis), which may not be representative of its performance on other tasks.
