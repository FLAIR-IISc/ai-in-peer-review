- Paper introduces GB-RNN: a tree-structured recursive NN with a single learned top-down “feedbackward” pass that concatenates forward/backward node vectors; also proposes hybrid word vectors and node-dropout.  
- Empirical result: GB-RNN outperforms strong baselines on SemEval 2013 Task 2; ablations show top-down pass gives ~3–5% F1 gain; hybrid embeddings, distant supervision, and lexicon augmentation add further improvements.  
- Motivation strength: correctly argues that many token/phrase labels are context-dependent and bottom-up trees alone are insufficient.  
- Mechanism strength: simple, general top-down pass that can be plugged into existing recursive models; conceptually similar to belief propagation.  
- Empirical rigor strength: solid results with careful ablations and a fair comparison to a related bidirectional recursive model.  
- Practical contribution: sensible training recipe (AdaGrad, dropout on node reps) and a useful data pipeline (distant supervision + fine-tuning).  
- Ambiguity weakness: unclear whether the top-down pass is used during training or only at test time; need clarification on gradient flow if trained jointly.  
- Resource confound weakness: results potentially confounded by external resources; request an ablation table isolating architecture effects with identical resources (no lexicon/distant supervision).  
- Significance testing weakness: no statistical significance tests reported for improvements, particularly the 3–5% F1 differences.  
- Complexity/speed weakness: missing analysis of extra parameters (n·nd), runtime cost, training/inference time multipliers, and sensitivity to nd and root transform V.  
- Parsing robustness weakness: approach depends on binary parses of noisy tweets; ask for analysis of performance vs parse quality or alternative parsers.  
- Evaluation scope weakness: limited to SemEval tweets; suggest adding a second dataset with aspect/entity supervision and SST to show behavior when context is less critical.  
- Clarity issues: ambiguous use of “RNN,” minor typos, stray sentences, and inconsistent equation dimensions—request cleaning and a concise algorithmic description.  
- Baseline omission: recommend adding a strong chain-based BiRNN/BiLSTM baseline with wide context to better test long-range context claims.  
- Quality assessment: model is sound and well-motivated; training is standard; paper would benefit from significance testing and computational analysis.  
- Clarity assessment: generally readable but needs terminology fixes and explicit clarification of training/inference top-down usage.  
- Originality assessment: approach relates to bidirectional recursive and inside–outside ideas but is a meaningful variant; hybrid embeddings are pragmatic though less novel.  
- Significance assessment: simple, broadly applicable mechanism with benchmark gains; impact would increase with clearer evaluation and resource-role disclosure.  
- Questions for authors: Is the top-down pass learned jointly or test-only? How sensitive are results to nd and V vs root copying? Are parameters shared across child positions? Can you provide error analysis of phenomena helped?  
- Recommendation: Lean accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
