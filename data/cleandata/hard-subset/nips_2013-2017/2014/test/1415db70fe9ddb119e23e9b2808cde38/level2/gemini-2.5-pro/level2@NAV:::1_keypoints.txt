- Addresses RNNs' inability to model how global sentence context affects constituents by introducing a top-down "feedbackward" pass.  
- Proposes the Global Belief Recursive Neural Network (GB-RNN) that propagates full-sentence information down the parse tree to refine node representations.  
- Situates work within compositional models and compares to bidirectional recursive models, notably Irsoy and Cardie.  
- Significant and original: feedbackward mechanism is an intuitive, novel way to incorporate global context and improves modeling.  
- Strong empirical results: state-of-the-art on SemEval 2013 and thorough ablation studies showing >5% gain from feedbackward, benefits of hybrid word vectors, and dropout.  
- Clear presentation with helpful equations, figures, and comparison to related bidirectional work.  
- Concern: comparison to Irsoy and Cardie may be confounded by differing parameter counts; controlled parameter-matched experiments are needed.  
- Concern: paper lacks an explicit limitations section (e.g., sensitivity to parse quality and potential failure modes).  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution; strongly recommend acceptance.
