- Proposal uses encoding and decoding phrase vectors from a recursive auto-encoder for sentiment and calls it “belief propagation,” which is misleading.
- Paper is generally well written but motivation is vague and lacks clear theoretical justification.
- Major weakness: evaluation — presenting ensemble results is unjustified and obscures comparisons to non-ensemble benchmarks.
- Composition of compared models is unclear: how models were tuned and how ensemble/best models were selected is not specified.
- Unclear whether benchmarks in Table 2 were tuned the same way or used the same augmented data as the BP-RNN.
- Unclear whether the RNN and Bidirectional-RNN baselines used hybrid word vectors.
- Likely the second entry in Table 3 is the most comparable baseline to Tables 1 & 2 for assessing BP-RNN.
- Minor: surprising choice to use an off-the-shelf parser for tweets—its accuracy on this data should be reported.
- Minor: several citations (e.g., Irsoy and Cardie) are incomplete.
- Overall: results are reasonable but the model lacks strong motivation and the experimental methodology is not sufficiently clear.
