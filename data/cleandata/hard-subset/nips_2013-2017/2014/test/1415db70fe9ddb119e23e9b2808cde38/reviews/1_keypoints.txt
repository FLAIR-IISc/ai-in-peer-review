- Introduces "belief propagation recursive neural networks" adding a top-down (backward) inference step to standard bottom-up recursive networks to let phrase-level predictions/embeddings influence word embeddings and labels.  
- Well written and well motivated; presents an interesting variant of bidirectional recursive models.  
- Key contribution appears to be the introduction of hybrid word vectors—may be the main difference from prior work.  
- Relation to Irsoy and Cardie (2008) is incomplete and the distinction from that bidirectional recursive model needs clearer clarification.  
- The term "belief propagation" is misleading; "forward-backward" might be a more appropriate name.  
- Experimental comparisons may be unfair or unclear: did baselines (RNN, B-RNN) use hybrid vectors, and how were models tuned/selected (single best vs ensemble)?  
- Section 3.5 (Training) is too short—needs details on training procedure, e.g., backpropagation-through-structure, network unfolding, and how deep updates reach word embeddings.  
- Section 3.6 is confusing and should explicitly clarify differences from Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Section 4.1 requires better explanation for non-NLP readers (clarify which SemEval 2013 subtask is addressed and parser constraints).  
- Consider merging Sections 4.3 and 4.4 for clarity.
