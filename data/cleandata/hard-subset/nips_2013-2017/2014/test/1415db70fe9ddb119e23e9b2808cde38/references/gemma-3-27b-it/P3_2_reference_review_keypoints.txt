- Overall: Introduces a feedback-based extension to RNNs that captures global context; strong SemEval results; recommend accept with minor revisions due to incrementalism, limited ablations, and verbosity.

- Summary & Related Work: GB-RNN augments bottom-up recursive computation with a top-down belief propagation step to update phrase representations using global sentence context; related work is comprehensive and distinctions from prior bidirectional recursive nets are clearly made.

- Strength — Strong Empirical Results: GB-RNN outperforms strong baselines, including the SemEval 2013 winner.

- Strength — Clear Motivation: Clearly motivated by context-dependent sentiment issues with illustrative examples (e.g., Android vs. iPhone).

- Strength — Novelty: Feedbackward/top-down computation during inference for contextual disambiguation is a genuine contribution.

- Strength — Hybrid Word Vectors: Combining pre-trained and task-specific vectors balances generalization and domain adaptation.

- Weakness — Incrementalism: Contribution feels incremental; improvement over prior bidirectional RNNs is modest and theoretical justification for the backward pass is weak.

- Weakness — Limited Ablations: Ablation studies are insufficient; need exploration of feedback-vector dimensionality (nd) and the role of transformation matrix V.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Hyperparameter selection is briefly reported; sensitivity analysis is missing.

- Weakness — Verbosity: Paper is verbose and repetitive in places; presentation should be streamlined.

- Weakness — Dataset Limitations: SemEval 2013 is small and may not capture complex contextual sentiment; broader dataset evaluation is needed.

- Weakness — Theoretical Naming/Connection: The “Global Belief” name implies belief propagation, but the theoretical relationship is not explored or justified.

- For Acceptance: Clear benchmark improvement, addresses an important limitation, and presents a novel, well-motivated approach.

- Against Acceptance: Incremental contribution, insufficient ablations, limited hyperparameter discussion, and verbose presentation.

- Questions for Authors: Provide theoretical justification for Eq.3 vs alternatives; report sensitivity to nd; have you tried attention or other global-context methods; applicability to other NLP tasks (e.g., coreference, relation extraction)?

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add detailed ablation studies and streamline the presentation.
