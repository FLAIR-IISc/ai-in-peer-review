- Overall: Proposes Global Belief RNN (GB-RNN) to propagate global context down parse trees for context-dependent sentiment; recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Core idea: Add a backward pass from root to leaves so phrase representations are updated with sentence-level context.  
- Positioning: Builds on compositional semantics, recursive models, and bidirectional RNNs; differentiates from Irsoy & Cardie by backward-pass computation.  
- Empirical result: Outperforms strong baselines and SemEval 2013 winner on Twitter sentiment dataset.  
- Novelty: GB-RNN is a genuinely new method for incorporating global context into recursive models.  
- Motivation: Well-motivated for entity- and context-dependent sentiment (e.g., Android vs. iPhone example).  
- Practicality: Paper covers training details (BPTS, AdaGrad, dropout) and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Clarity issues: Explanations (notably Section 3.4) and notation are sometimes abrupt or inconsistent.  
- Ablation gap: Lacks systematic ablation studies (contribution of backward pass, effect of feedbackward dimensionality nd).  
- Error analysis: Missing detailed qualitative analysis of model errors and failure cases.  
- Generalizability: Experiments are limited to SemEval 2013; more datasets needed to assess robustness.  
- Theoretical comparison: Needs a more rigorous discussion contrasting GB-RNN with Irsoy & Cardie and when each should perform better.  
- Significance: Method has broad implications for social media sentiment and opinion mining.  
- Decision factors: Pro — novel approach, strong results, thorough related work; Con — clarity, ablations, single-dataset evaluation.  
- Requested revisions: Improve clarity (esp. Section 3.4), run ablation studies, add brief error analysis, and expand theoretical comparison with Irsoy & Cardie.
