- Proposes Global Belief Recursive Neural Networks (GB-RNNs) that add a backward pass to update phrase representations using global sentence context.  
- Demonstrates improved performance on SemEval 2013 Twitter sentiment analysis, outperforming standard RNNs and the competition winner.  
- Situates the work well within prior literature, acknowledging word vectors, compositional models, and related recurrent/bidirectional approaches.  
- Differentiates clearly from bidirectional recursive networks (Irsoy & Cardie, 2012) in how the backward pass is implemented.  
- Novelty: backward pass for context-dependent phrase updates is a conceptually sound extension to standard RNNs.  
- Empirical strength: significant improvement on a challenging benchmark supports the model’s effectiveness.  
- Clear motivation: provides compelling examples showing the necessity of context-dependent representations.  
- Thorough comparison and ablation studies clarify the contribution of each GB-RNN component.  
- Hybrid word vector initialization (pre-trained + task-specific) is sensible and empirically effective.  
- Provides detailed mathematical explanation of the GB-RNN architecture.  
- Limited experimental scope: evaluation only on SemEval 2013; more datasets/tasks needed to assess generalizability.  
- Lacks detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to show which parameters most affect performance.  
- Computational cost of the backward pass is not quantified, raising scalability concerns.  
- The rationale for concatenating forward and backward vectors needs deeper theoretical justification beyond empirical gains.  
- Notation ambiguity: forward and feedbackward vector symbols (e.g., p vs. p↓) could be clearer.  
- Overall assessment: strong, promising contribution addressing a key limitation of compositional models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong empirical results, clear exposition, tackles fundamental NLU challenge.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluations, missing computational-cost analysis, and insufficient theoretical justification for vector combination.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add more datasets, discuss computational cost, explain forward/backward combination rationale, and clarify notation.
