- Problem: Standard recursive RNNs compute phrase representations feedforward and miss global sentence context that can change phrase meaning (e.g., "Android" in different comparisons).

- Contribution: Proposes Global Belief RNN (GB-RNN) that adds a backward pass from root to leaves so phrase representations incorporate global sentence context.

- Experimental comparison: Evaluates GB-RNN against standard RNNs, bidirectional RNNs, and baselines on SemEval 2013, showing consistent improvements.

- Hybrid word vectors: Introduces a hybrid representation combining unsupervised pre-trained vectors with task-specific vectors for better performance without massive extra data.

- Strength — problem ID: Clearly identifies a genuine limitation in recursive RNNs with a compelling motivating example.

- Strength — architecture: GB-RNN’s backward pass is novel, well-motivated, and clearly explained; strong comparison to Irsoy & Cardie’s bidirectional RNN.

- Strength — results: Empirical gains are convincing (over 5% improvement, beating competition winner) with useful ablation studies.

- Strength — practical: Hybrid word vector approach is a practical, low-overhead performance improvement.

- Weakness — theory: Lacks theoretical analysis or explanation of why the backward pass outperforms other approaches or guarantees about information flow.

- Weakness — evaluation scope: Only evaluated on one dataset/task (SemEval 2013 sentiment), limiting evidence of broader applicability.

- Weakness — modern comparisons: Related work omits discussion of more recent contextual models (e.g., transformers/BERT) and should acknowledge their emergence.

- Weakness — parameter efficiency: Notes GB-RNN has n·nd more parameters than bidirectional RNN but doesn’t analyze effects on training/inference time or scalability.

- Weakness — terminology: The term "global belief" is vague and not clearly distinguished from other contextual modeling approaches.

- Quality: Technically sound, well-executed experiments, and careful baseline comparisons, but theoretical gaps remain.

- Clarity: Well-written and organized with helpful figures; main clarity issue is the vague "global belief" terminology.

- Originality: Novel for adding a backward pass to recursive NNs, though the general idea of using global context isn’t entirely new and should be better contextualized.

- Significance: Meaningful contribution with potential for other NLP tasks, but limited by narrow evaluation and later dominance of stronger contextual models.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify "global belief" and discuss relations to newer contextual modeling approaches.

- Pro/Con summary: Pros — identifies limitation, novel extension, strong results, ablations, hybrid vectors; Cons — lacks theory, limited evaluation, vague terminology, missing relation to newer models.
