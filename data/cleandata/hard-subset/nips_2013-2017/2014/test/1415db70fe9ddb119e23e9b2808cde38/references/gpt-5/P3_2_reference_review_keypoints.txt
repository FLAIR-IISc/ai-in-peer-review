- Summary: Proposes GB-RNN, a bidirectional tree RNN with a top-down “feedbackward” pass to inject global context into node predictions, plus hybrid embeddings and dropout; reports gains on SemEval-2013 Task 2.

- Relation to prior work: Model is closely related to recursive compositional models and inside-outside/top-down tree passes, and to prior bidirectional recursive/chain RNNs (e.g., Irsoy & Cardie).

- Novelty claim: Uses both forward and backward parent states and separate left/right transforms in the top-down pass; hybrid fixed+trainable word embeddings are a pragmatic variant.

- Strength: Clear motivation for context-dependent phrase labeling in noisy social media text.

- Strength: Simple, practical extension (top-down pass) that brings sentence-level context to each node beyond short windows.

- Strength: Empirical improvements reported over strong baselines and competition systems indicate potential impact.

- Strength: Hybrid embeddings and dropout are sensible engineering choices for noisy tweets.

- Strength: Paper provides useful training details (two-stage training, AdaGrad, dropout).

- Math/notation issues: Equations and tensor shapes are inconsistent/incorrect (typos like V ∈ Vnd×n; wrong W↓ dimensions); requires precise shapes and computational graph.

- Internal contradiction: Text claims backward vectors don’t use forward vectors, but Eq.3 uses forward parent — contradiction must be resolved.

- Novelty vs prior work: Architectural difference from Irsoy & Cardie appears incremental; needs clearer conceptual distinction and justification beyond added parameters.

- Evaluation reporting incomplete: Missing referenced tables, exact numbers, standard deviations, statistical tests, and detailed ablations to substantiate claimed gains.

- Baseline fairness: Unclear if bidirectional recursive baseline and other comparisons are capacity- and resource-matched (embeddings, dropout, extra data).

- External data/ensembling: Uses NRC lexicon, 176k distant tweets, and 5-model ensemble; paper should disentangle gains from model vs external data vs ensembling and provide single-model/no-external-data results.

- Parsing/span alignment: Method constrains parser to keep labeled phrases as subtrees; must detail enforcement, check for label leakage, and handle non-constituent spans.

- Generality claims: Broader applicability (e.g., WSD) is untested; an additional task would strengthen significance.

- Efficiency/scalability: Top-down pass roughly doubles computation; provide complexity/runtime analysis and discuss scalability.

- Quality: Core idea is sound but presentation has inconsistencies and experimental evidence is incomplete; fixing math and expanding experiments would increase credibility.

- Clarity: Readable overall but model section needs rigorous notation, corrected shapes, precise tensor dimensions, and pseudo-code/figures; avoid neologisms like “feedbackward.”

- Originality: Moderate — bidirectional tree passes are known; contributions are incremental but practical.

- Significance: Potentially useful for contextual phrase classification if gains hold under controlled comparisons and resource disentanglement.

- Questions for authors: Please fix equation shapes and clarify backward computation dependence; report capacity-matched comparisons to Irsoy & Cardie; provide single-model/no-external-data/ablation results; explain parser constraints for labeled spans.

- Pros for acceptance: Addresses a real limitation of recursive models with a simple top-down pass, shows promising benchmark results, and offers practical insights for noisy text.

- Cons for acceptance: Incremental novelty, inconsistent technical specification, and insufficiently controlled experimental analysis with missing resource/ensemble details.

- Overall assessment: Borderline accept conditional on resolving mathematical inconsistencies and adding thorough, controlled experiments (ablations, significance tests); confidence: medium.
