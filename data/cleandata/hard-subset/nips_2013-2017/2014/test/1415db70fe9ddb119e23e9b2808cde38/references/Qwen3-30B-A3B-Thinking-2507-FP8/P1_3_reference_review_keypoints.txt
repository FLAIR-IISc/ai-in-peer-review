- Summary: Proposes GB-RNNs that add a top-down feedback pass to recursive neural nets to incorporate global sentence context for better phrase representations; shows improvements on SemEval 2013 sentiment analysis.

- Strength: Strong motivation with clear, real-world example demonstrating context-dependent sentiment failure of standard RNNs.

- Strength: Technically sound model with clear mathematical formulation and a well-explained feedback mechanism.

- Strength: Empirical results show significant gains (≈5% absolute F1) over standard RNNs and strong baselines on SemEval 2013.

- Strength: Practical hybrid word vectors combining unsupervised and supervised representations help address antonym issues without full fine-tuning.

- Weakness: Limited theoretical justification — lacks deeper analysis of why the feedback mechanism outperforms other context-integration methods.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison with related work, especially the contemporaneous bidirectional RNN work by Irsoy and Cardie [12].

- Weakness: Narrow evaluation scope — experiments are limited to a single sentiment dataset, reducing claims of broader applicability.

- Weakness: Missing implementation details about hyperparameter tuning, computational complexity, inference time, and scalability.

- Weakness: The term "global belief" is not well-defined conceptually and could be misleading without clearer exposition.

- Overall (Quality): Technically solid and experimentally competent, but theoretical depth is lacking.

- Overall (Clarity): Generally well-written with clear equations and figures, but distinctions from related work and terminology need clarification.

- Overall (Originality): Novel extension of recursive nets with feedback, but conceptually related to existing bidirectional approaches.

- Overall (Significance): Important problem and meaningful empirical gains for sentiment analysis, but broader impact is not demonstrated.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — promising contribution that requires stronger theory, broader evaluation, clearer relation to prior work, and implementation details.

- Suggested improvements: add theoretical analysis of the feedback mechanism; evaluate on additional context-dependent tasks/datasets; more clearly distinguish from related bidirectional work; provide computational and implementation details.

- Pro arguments: strong motivation and example, significant empirical improvements, practical hybrid word-vector approach, and clear mathematical formulation.

- Con arguments: weak theoretical support, single-dataset evaluation, unclear distinction from related work, underdefined "global belief" term, and limited discussion of scalability.
