- Proposes Global Belief RNN (GB-RNN): adds a deterministic top-down “feedbackward” pass to tree-structured RNNs for target-dependent sentiment, plus hybrid word vectors (fixed unsupervised + small supervised tail).
- Reports improved performance on SemEval 2013 Twitter sentiment using GB-RNN, lexicon/distant supervision, two-stage training, and ensembling.
- Builds on prior tree-RNN and bidirectional recursive models (Irsoy & Cardie, inside-outside RNN); novelty is using both forward/backward parent vectors and allowing different backward dimensionality.
- Terminology (“global belief”, “feedbackward”) is unconventional and may mislead by evoking probabilistic inference while the model is deterministic.
- Strength: addresses lack of top-down/global context in bottom-up compositional models, relevant for target-dependent labeling.
- Strength: simple, conceptually clear extension reusing parse structure; hybrid embedding trick is practical and broadly applicable.
- Strength: sensible training choices (dropout on node vectors, AdaGrad) and reported consistent improvements over several baselines.
- Major weakness: experimental reporting is incomplete—missing tables, absolute scores, standard deviations, and significance tests, making claims unverifiable.
- Dataset description is unclear/inaccurate (repeated counts, unspecified SemEval subtask(s), label distributions, presence of SMS), needing clarification.
- Baseline comparisons are limited—no sequence BiLSTM (with/without attention) or tree-LSTM comparisons; claim about recurrent/window methods failing at long-range dependencies is overstated.
- Clarity issues: confusing notation and inconsistent equation shapes (W↓ block structure, Eq.3), unclear role of V, and several typos/incomplete references.
- Heavy reliance on external resources (lexicons, distant supervision) and ensembling obscures how much gain is due specifically to the top-down pass.
- Parsing robustness on noisy tweets is unaddressed; no analysis of parse errors or justification for using tree models vs sequence models.
- Methodologically reasonable but presentation gaps and missing empirical detail prevent thorough verification of results and robustness.
- Originality: moderate—top-down/contextual passes exist in prior work; combining forward/backward parents and hybrid embeddings is a small but reasonable variant.
- Significance: potentially useful for token/phrase labeling where global context matters, but unclear without quantified gains on strong baselines and multiple datasets.
- Key requests for authors: provide full numerical results (per-class/macro/micro F1, accuracy, splits, stdevs, significance), disentangle contributions (ablation of top-down, embeddings, extra data, ensembling), and specify all tensor shapes and W↓ block structure.
- Additional author requests: compare to sequence BiLSTMs (with target conditioning/attention), report parsing quality on tweets, provide efficiency/parameter/time analysis, and clarify supervision scheme for backward vectors and target specification.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important limitation of tree-RNNs, has promising motivation, and a practical embedding idea.
- Cons against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail, weak/limited baselines, clarity and terminology issues, and reliance on external data and ensembling.
- Recommendation: weak reject pending clearer, complete empirical evidence and a tighter, more precise presentation; revision could make the work stronger.
