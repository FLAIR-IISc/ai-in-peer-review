- Paper proposes GB-RNN: tree-structured RNN with a top-down “feedbackward” pass to condition node predictions on global sentence context.
- Evaluated on SemEval-2013 Task 2 Twitter sentiment (term-level contextual polarity), reporting gains over standard RNN and the competition winner; best result from an ensemble of 5 GB-RNNs.
- Introduces hybrid word vectors (fixed unsupervised embeddings concatenated with a small trainable segment) and applies dropout on node vectors.
- Relates to compositional distributional semantics, bidirectional recursive models, Twitter sentiment work, and use/fine-tuning of pre-trained embeddings.
- Main novelty is adding a deterministic top-down pass to tree composition to provide global context, distinct from feedforward-only tree models.
- Model definition is mostly clear, but contains notational typos/inconsistencies (e.g., "feedbackward"/"feedwordard", wrong matrix dims).
- Key contradiction: equation shows backward child depends on both forward and backward parent, yet text claims backward computation omits forward node vectors.
- Hybrid embeddings and dropout are reasonable additions, but parameter-dimension derivations need clearer specification.
- Empirical claims of substantial improvements are promising but lack complete tables, exact numbers, and significance testing in the provided text.
- Ablations are incomplete: missing tests for V vs copying root, using/omitting forward parent in backward pass, nd dimension sensitivity, and dropout effects.
- Use of ensembles and external resources (NRC lexicon, pretraining) complicates attribution of gains; single-model comparisons exist but need clearer reporting.
- Method depends on constituency parses for noisy tweets; authors constrained parser to keep labeled phrases in their own subtree, which may bias evaluation and is under-specified.
- Runtime/complexity and training stability are not discussed.
- Paper is generally readable with good motivation, but hampered by typos, notation errors, and unclear comparisons to Irsoy & Cardie.
- Originality is moderate: top-down propagation is timely and related to concurrent bidirectional recursive work; framing as “global belief” is suggestive but not probabilistic inference.
- Significance is moderate-to-strong for context-sensitive composition and aspect-level sentiment; broader impact plausible but needs more datasets.
- Pros: clear motivation, simple implementable architectural change, sensible hybrid embeddings/dropout, empirical gains on a benchmark.
- Cons: notational contradictions, limited ablations, under-specified parser constraint, limited comparisons to other strong baselines (e.g., token-level bidirectional RNNs, TreeLSTM).
- Requests: clarify backward equations and resolve contradiction about forward parent usage.
- Requests: provide full quantitative results (dev/test, single vs ensemble, with/without external resources) and statistical significance.
- Requests: add ablations for root-copy vs V, nd choices, dropout sites, and removing hybrid embeddings.
- Requests: precisely describe parser constraint and analyze sensitivity to parse errors.
- Requests: report training/inference time and parameter counts for all models.
- Suggest evaluating on additional contextual sentiment datasets to demonstrate generality and discuss relation to belief propagation/CRFs if invoking “global belief.”
- Recommendation: borderline accept (leaning accept); strengthen clarity, ablations, and fairness of comparisons to secure acceptance.
