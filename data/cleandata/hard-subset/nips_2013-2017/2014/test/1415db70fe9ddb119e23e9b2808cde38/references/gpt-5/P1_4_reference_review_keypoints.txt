- Proposes GB-RNN: tree-structured model with bottom-up forward pass and a top-down “feedbackward” pass to produce concatenated forward+backward node representations for targeted sentiment.  
- Introduces hybrid word embeddings: fixed unsupervised vectors concatenated with a small trainable segment and dropout to reduce overfitting.  
- Targets contextual/targeted sentiment on Twitter and reports improvements over standard RNNs, a bidirectional recursive baseline, and other baselines; ensemble beats competition winner.  
- Relates to prior recursive and bidirectional models (Socher et al., Irsoy & Cardie, Le & Zuidema) with novelty in conditioning child backward states on both parent forward and backward states and a different parameterization.  
- The top-down propagation idea is incremental rather than fundamentally new, but the specific parameterization and embedding use are reasonable contributions.  
- Training setup (BPTS, AdaGrad, dropout) is standard and appropriate.  
- Paper contains notational/inconsistency errors (dimensions of W↓ and V, contradictory claims about using forward states in backward computation) that hinder reproducibility.  
- Experimental reporting lacks exact numbers, variance, statistical significance, and per-class F1s; single-model vs ensemble details are missing.  
- Use of external resources (NRC lexicon, noisy tweets, movie reviews) complicates fairness of comparisons; need clear separation of resource usage across systems.  
- Forcing labeled phrases to be tree subtrees biases evaluation and avoids the non-constituent target problem; impact of this constraint is not analyzed.  
- Baselines are incomplete: missing strong sequence models (BiLSTM/Tree-LSTM/CRF, wide-window CNNs) that could challenge the “global context” claim.  
- Limited qualitative error analysis; lacking breakdown by phenomena (negation, sarcasm, coreference) to show where top-down pass helps.  
- Clarity issues: notational typos, inconsistent terminology (“feedbackward” vs top-down), and missing tensor shape schematics.  
- Missing training details: sizes of n and nd, learning rates, epochs, early stopping, dropout placement, and preprocessing of unlabeled data.  
- Significance is modest: potential practical adoption by tree-based practitioners if gains hold, but reliance on noisy parses and subtree constraint limits applicability.  
- Pros: well-motivated task, practical hybrid embeddings, empirical improvements and useful ablations, clear comparison to Irsoy & Cardie.  
- Cons: technical inconsistencies, biased evaluation setup, lack of statistical rigor, heavy use of extra data and ensembling, and weak sequence-model comparisons.  
- Suggested fixes: correct dimensionality/notation, reconcile contradictory equations, provide tensor shapes, full single-model metrics with CIs and significance tests.  
- Suggested experiments: evaluate non-constituent targets or span-based variant, add strong sequence baselines, ablate nd and V transform, provide runtime/parameter counts, and error analysis.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept pending corrections to technical inconsistencies and stronger experimental rigor and baselines.
