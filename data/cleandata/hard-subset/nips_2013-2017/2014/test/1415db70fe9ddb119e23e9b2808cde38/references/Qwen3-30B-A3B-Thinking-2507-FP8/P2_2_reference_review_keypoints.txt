- Paper introduces GB-RNN: a recursive neural network with a “feedbackward” root-to-leaf pass plus hybrid word vectors, evaluated on SemEval 2013 sentiment analysis.  
- Argument: standard RNNs miss context-dependent phrase meanings; feedbackward pass lets phrase predictions update using global sentence context.  
- Mathematical formulation of forward and feedbackward computations is clear and distinguishes GB-RNN from bidirectional RNNs.  
- Empirical result: GB-RNN outperforms standard RNNs and SemEval 2013 baselines (≈5%+ improvement); ablation studies show component contributions.  
- Hybrid word vectors (pretrained + task-specific) are well-motivated and shown to outperform purely unsupervised or supervised vectors.  
- Good coverage and positioning relative to related work in recursive and bidirectional models and sentiment analysis.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of model limitations, including scalability to longer/complex sentences and computational cost of the backward pass.  
- Weakness: insufficient experimental detail for full reproducibility (missing hyperparameters, model selection procedure, number of trained models).  
- Weakness: claims of broader applicability (e.g., word-sense disambiguation) are overstated without empirical evidence beyond sentiment analysis.  
- Weakness: comparisons do not include the most recent state-of-the-art beyond SemEval 2013 competition systems.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of why GB-RNN should outperform standard or bidirectional RNNs (representational capacity/information flow).  
- Overall: technically sound, well-written, and novel (incremental) contribution with meaningful empirical gains for contextual sentiment analysis.  
- Significance: notable improvement for sentiment tasks but broader generalization remains speculative without further evidence.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
