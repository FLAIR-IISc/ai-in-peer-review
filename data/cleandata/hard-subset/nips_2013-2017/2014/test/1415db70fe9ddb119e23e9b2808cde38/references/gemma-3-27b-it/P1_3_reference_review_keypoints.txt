- Summary: Introduces GB-RNNs that add a feedbackward pass to recursive neural networks to update phrase representations with global sentence context, improving sentiment classification on SemEval 2013 Twitter data.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on distributional semantics, RNNs, and bidirectional models while distinguishing its feedbackward mechanism from prior bidirectional recursive approaches.  
- Novelty (Strength): Feedbackward pass is a significant new mechanism for capturing context-dependent sentiment.  
- Empirical results (Strength): GB-RNN achieves state-of-the-art performance on SemEval 2013, outperforming standard RNNs and the competition winner.  
- Experimental rigor (Strength): Detailed comparisons, ablations, and analysis (including lexicon and movie review data) clarify component contributions.  
- Clarity and presentation (Strength): Well-written paper with clear explanations, figures, and theoretical comparisons to related work.  
- Practical considerations (Strength): Discusses dropout and hybrid word vectors, demonstrating attention to implementation details.  
- Limited evaluation (Weakness): Experiments are confined to a single dataset/task, limiting claims of generality.  
- Model complexity (Weakness): Feedbackward vectors and extra parameters increase complexity; impact on performance and computation needs more analysis.  
- Parse dependency (Weakness): Heavy reliance on Stanford Parser accuracy; robustness to parsing errors is not assessed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (Weakness): Lacks thorough analysis of sensitivity despite extensive cross-validation.  
- Minor clarity issues (Weakness): Some equations need clearer dimensionality explanations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound model with strong empirical gains, clear writing, and useful theoretical/practical discussion.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited dataset evaluation, added complexity and parser dependency, and insufficient hyperparameter analysis.  
- Overall assessment: Strong submission recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand evaluation and analyze hyperparameter/parser impacts.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
