- Proposes GB-RNN: adds a root-to-leaf "feedbackward" pass to recursive neural nets to incorporate global context for sentiment analysis, showing improvements on SemEval 2013.  
- Strength: tackles context-dependent sentiment with clear motivation and illustrative examples.  
- Strength: architecture is clearly presented with equations and figures.  
- Strength: experiments compare multiple baselines and report consistent ~5%+ improvements over standard RNNs.  
- Strength: hybrid word-vector approach (unsupervised pretraining + supervised fine-tuning) is sensible and effective.  
- Weakness: Insufficiently differentiated from prior bidirectional RNN work (Irsoy & Cardie); comparison is superficial and extra parameter cost is not justified.  
- Weakness: Theoretical grounding is lacking; "global belief" is undefined, no rationale for superiority or analysis of failure modes, and unclear necessity vs simpler context models.  
- Weakness: Limited scope—only evaluated on one sentiment task, modest gains not shown against more recent SOTA, so broader applicability is unproven.  
- Weakness: Methodological clarity issues around hybrid word vectors and missing hyperparameter/details about how vectors are used in feedbackward pass.  
- Weakness: Overstated claims about aspect-aware classification—model doesn’t demonstrate target aspect identification beyond provided task setup.  
- Overall: Interesting and promising but incremental; needs clearer novelty, stronger theory, and broader/stronger empirical evidence.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; requires substantial revision to establish novelty and significance.
