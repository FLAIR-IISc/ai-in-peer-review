- Proposal: GB-RNN augments a bottom-up tree RNN with a top-down “feedbackward” pass that combines forward and backward parent states to produce node predictions.  
- Additional design: hybrid word embeddings concatenate fixed unsupervised vectors with a small trainable supervised component; trained with backprop-through-structure, AdaGrad, and dropout.  
- Task and results: applied to target-level sentiment on SemEval-2013 Twitter data; single models outperform tree RNN and bidirectional-recursive baselines, ensembles yield best test performance.  
- Relation to prior work: closely related to Inside-Outside/bi-directional recursive models; claimed novelty is using both forward and backward parent states in the backward recursion.  
- Strength: tackles important context-dependent sentiment task where local/bag-of-words models fail.  
- Strength: clear architectural intuition—top-down pass injects global sentence context into local predictions.  
- Strength: empirical improvements over baselines and competitive SemEval systems reported.  
- Strength: simple, practical training/regularization and a sensible hybrid-embedding approach that helps noisy/sparse data.  
- Strength: ablations suggest hybrid embeddings and staged/distant supervision help.  
- Weakness: model description has dimensional inconsistencies (wrong matrix/vector shapes) that must be fixed.  
- Weakness: contradiction in text about whether forward node vectors are used in computing backward vectors; equations and prose conflict.  
- Weakness: manuscript contains editorial artifacts, typos, and incomplete references undermining reproducibility.  
- Weakness: novelty not convincingly established versus prior bidirectional/inside-outside models; extra capacity could explain gains.  
- Weakness: experiments lack necessary details (exact scores, significance tests, single vs ensemble, external resource usage).  
- Weakness: fairness of comparisons unclear—were baselines ensembled or given same external data?  
- Weakness: missing hyperparameters, model sizes, training schedules, and compute/memory costs.  
- Weakness: no analysis of parser quality or robustness to noisy Twitter syntax despite dependence on parses.  
- Weakness: claims about advantages over window/BiRNN methods are overstated; stronger sequence baselines (e.g., tuned BiLSTM) are needed.  
- Suggestion: correct dimensional errors, remove artifacts, and provide clear equations or pseudocode for forward/backward passes.  
- Suggestion: run targeted ablations (use/no-use forward parent, parameter-matched baselines) to isolate design vs capacity gains.  
- Suggestion: report single-model vs ensemble results, with/without external resources, and include statistical significance.  
- Suggestion: provide full experimental protocols, preprocessing, parser settings, hyperparameters, code, and runtime/memory analysis.  
- Suggestion: add qualitative error analysis showing where top-down context helps or fails and examine sensitivity to parse errors.  
- Suggestion: consider gating or attention mechanisms in the top-down pass to control global context injection.  
- Pros for acceptance: important task, principled architecture linking inside-outside inference to neural composition, empirical gains, and practical embedding/training ideas.  
- Cons for acceptance: presentation/math flaws, unclear novelty, incomplete/possibly unfair experiments, and lack of parse-robustness and cost analysis.  
- Overall: promising direction with encouraging results but current manuscript has clarity and experimental isolation issues; reviewer leans toward rejection but encourages resubmission after fixes.
