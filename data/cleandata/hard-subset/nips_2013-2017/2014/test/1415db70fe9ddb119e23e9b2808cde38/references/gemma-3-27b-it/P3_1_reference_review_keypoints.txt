- Proposes GB-RNN: a recursive neural network with a backward pass that refines phrase representations using global sentence context.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains, outperforming strong baselines including the SemEval 2013 winner.  
- Technical contribution is incremental—backward pass is a straightforward extension of existing RNN ideas.  
- Clear, intuitive motivation addressing context-dependence in compositional semantics with helpful illustrations and equations.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive, correctly positioning GB-RNN relative to bidirectional RNNs (Irsoy & Cardie) and noting links to unfolding autoencoders.  
- Practical training details are addressed (backpropagation through structure, AdaGrad, dropout) and additional data/initialization (hybrid word vectors) are evaluated.  
- Ablation studies provide useful insights into each component’s contribution (e.g., hybrid vectors, backward pass).  
- Lacks deep theoretical discussion connecting the backward pass to probabilistic inference methods (e.g., belief propagation).  
- Limited qualitative error analysis—few concrete examples showing where GB-RNN succeeds over competitors.  
- Performance depends on parser quality (Stanford Parser) with no thorough robustness analysis to parsing errors.  
- Hyperparameter search details and sensitivity analysis are insufficiently reported.  
- Computational cost of the backward pass is not discussed and should be quantified.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions contingent on addressing theoretical links, error analysis, parser robustness, hyperparameter reporting, and computational cost.
