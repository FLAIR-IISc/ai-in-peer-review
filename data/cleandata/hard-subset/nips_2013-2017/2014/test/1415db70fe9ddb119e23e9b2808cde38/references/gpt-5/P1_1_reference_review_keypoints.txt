- Proposes GB-RNN: a tree-structured model that adds a top-down “feedbackward” pass to standard bottom-up recursive nets for context-sensitive node labeling.
- Final node classification concatenates forward (bottom-up) and backward (top-down) vectors computed via root-to-leaf recursion conditioned on parent forward and backward vectors.
- Introduces hybrid word vectors (fixed unsupervised embeddings + small task-tuned component) and dropout on node vectors as practical robustness measures.
- Evaluated on SemEval 2013 Twitter sentiment, showing gains over a feedforward tree RNN, a bidirectional recursive baseline, and the SemEval winning system (including an ensemble).
- Clear motivation: many phrases require global sentence context for correct sentiment labeling.
- Simple, general mechanism that integrates naturally with existing tree RNNs to inject global context into all nodes.
- Sensible training strategy: noisy pretraining then fine-tuning with AdaGrad and mini-batching.
- Novelty is incremental: top-down propagation and inside–outside ideas have prior precedents (e.g., Irsoy & Cardie, inside–outside RNNs).
- Equational/parameter differences from prior bidirectional models are modest and the main architectural change isn’t strongly justified experimentally.
- Experimental reporting is incomplete: key metrics (F1/accuracy), variances, and single-model/no-external-data baselines are not fully reported.
- Reliance on ensembles and external resources complicates fair comparison; unclear how gains decompose by resource vs architecture.
- Notational inconsistencies and typos (e.g., “feedbackward/feedwordard”, dimension definitions, Eq. 3) hinder clarity and reproducibility.
- Model depends on accurate syntactic parses; tweet parsing is brittle and the paper does not quantify parse-quality impact or robustness to malformed trees.
- Design choice to not condition backward updates on the child’s forward vector lacks ablation or analysis of capacity trade-offs.
- Limited task scope (only Twitter sentiment); broader applicability claims (e.g., WSD) are unvalidated.
- Requests for additional experiments: detailed quantitative results (per-class F1, macro/micro), ablations for nd, V, child forward conditioning, resource-controlled comparisons, parse-error analysis, and runtime/parameter counts.
- Reproducibility needs: consistent notation, full hyperparameters/training details, pseudocode or computational graph, and preferably code release.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and practically useful but incremental and currently under-evaluated; borderline acceptance pending clearer experiments isolating the architectural contribution.
