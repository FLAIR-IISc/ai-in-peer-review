- Overall assessment: Sensible, well-motivated extension to RNNs that improves contextual sentiment analysis; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Main idea: Introduces GB-RNNs that add a backward pass from root to leaves to incorporate global sentence context into phrase representations.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions correctly within embeddings, recursive networks, and bidirectional models, distinguishing its disentangling of parent/child information and use of global context.  
- Strength — Motivation: Clear problem statement with compelling examples showing limitations of standard RNNs.  
- Strength — Methodology: GB-RNN is a principled and well-explained extension of standard recursive networks.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: Outperforms strong baselines, including SemEval 2013 winner, with proper development set tuning.  
- Strength — Related work: Thorough literature review and appropriate differentiation from existing approaches.  
- Strength — Hybrid embeddings: Combining unsupervised and supervised word vectors is a strong design choice.  
- Strength — Writing: Paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Weakness — Incremental improvement: Performance gains are statistically significant but not large, raising questions about practical impact.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: Backward pass increases complexity; paper lacks analysis of training/inference overhead.  
- Weakness — Parser dependency: Heavy reliance on Stanford Parser without analysis of parser error impact or robustness.  
- Weakness — Limited ablation: Needs more detailed ablations (e.g., feedback vector size, backward activation choices).  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Unclear how sensitive results are to hyperparameter settings.  
- For acceptance: Addresses real RNN limitation, technically sound, empirically stronger than baselines, clearly written, and useful for sentiment analysis.  
- Against acceptance: Gains are modest, computational and parser-dependency concerns, and insufficient ablation/sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggestions — Computational cost: Add analysis of added training/inference overhead from the backward pass.  
- Suggestions — Parser robustness: Discuss parser error impact, try different parsers, or explore robustness to parsing mistakes.  
- Suggestions — Ablation: Expand experiments varying feedback vector dimensionality and backward-pass activations.  
- Suggestions — Hyperparameters: Report sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters.  
- Suggestions — Practical implications: Briefly discuss applications of GB-RNN beyond sentiment analysis.  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
