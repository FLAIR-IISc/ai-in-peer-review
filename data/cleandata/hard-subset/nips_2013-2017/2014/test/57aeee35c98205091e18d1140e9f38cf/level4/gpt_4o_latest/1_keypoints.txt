- Paper tackles estimating average causal effect (ACE) for two binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially known.  
- Builds on Entner et al. (2013) rule for selecting covariates that block back-door paths, but that rule relies on the faithfulness assumption.  
- Proposes using Entner et al.'s rule to derive bounds (not point estimates) for ACE, with a method inspired by Ramsahai (2012).  
- Reviewer finds the core idea sound and potentially valuable but unclear on technical details.  
- Main conceptual concern: how Entner et al.'s conditions inform this approach without assuming faithfulness.  
- Reviewer suggests the conditions might guide selection of relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly leaves this issue aside.  
- Curious whether the reported experimental robustness is related to the limited role of Entner et al.'s conditions.  
- Minor wording issue: phrase about “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is confusing—unfaithfulness permits extra independencies; framework seems to aim at “near violations.”  
- Experimental suggestion: report Entner et al.’s method performance under strict thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds.  
- Semantic/coding concern: example on p.8—if X=1 is vaccinated and Y=1 is not hospitalized, why would a negative ACE imply the vaccine is desirable?  
- Formatting error: table at top of p.8 labeled “Table 1” is referred to as “Table 5” in text.  
- Overall assessment: engaging paper but technical details are challenging to follow and need clarification.
