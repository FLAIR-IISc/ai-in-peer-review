- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to bound ACE while allowing violations of faithfulness.
- Timely and interesting problem; principled attempt to quantify weak unfaithfulness effects.
- Paper is well-organized and algorithmic steps are clearly described.
- Some methodological details are missing, making reproduction from the main text difficult (supplementary may help).
- Technically sound and ideas are well-introduced.
- Overall context is occasionally lost and key choices (e.g., relaxation parameters) are deferred to future work.
- Choosing relaxation parameters is not explained, making practical use and issues unclear.
- Experimental evaluation is unbalanced and not robust enough.
- The 'bias' metric is inappropriate for comparing WPP to competitors because they measure different things.
- Practical performance on the influenza dataset is weak: ACE intervals are too wide to be informative.
- Significance lies in the novel combination of familiar techniques and potential to stimulate further work.
- Interpretation of the output distribution over bounds is unclear; no preferred point estimate is provided.
- Uncertainty about how likely or realistic the unfaithful instances generating wide bounds are.
- Paper needs more detailed explanation of relaxation parameters, more comprehensive experiments, and clearer interpretation to be practitioner-ready.
- Summary scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Minor positive note: the name "Witness Protection Program" is catchy and memorable.
