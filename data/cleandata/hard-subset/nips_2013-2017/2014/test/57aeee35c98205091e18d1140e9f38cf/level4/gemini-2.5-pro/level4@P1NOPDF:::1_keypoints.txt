- Addresses estimating average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is unknown.
- Builds on Entner et al. (2013) rule for selecting covariates to block back-door paths, which relies on faithfulness.
- Main contribution: adapts Entner et al.'s rule to produce bounds on ACE (not point estimates), relaxing the faithfulness assumption.
- Method developed within Ramsahai (2012) framework.
- Reviewer could not verify all technical details but finds the central idea intuitive, interesting, and potentially useful.
- Major concern: unclear role of Entner et al.'s conditions once faithfulness is dropped—what do they imply about causal structure or relaxation parameters?
- Suggestion/request: authors should clarify whether the method’s core machinery depends on Entner et al.'s conditions or if experimental robustness arises independently.
- Minor comment 1: wording “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading—framework seems to model near-faithfulness (small nonzero dependencies), not violations of independencies.
- Minor comment 2: experiments should compare to Entner et al.’s original method (treat abstentions as uninformative bounds) for a direct baseline.
- Minor comment 3: interpretation error in flu shot experiment—if X=1 is vaccinated and Y=1 is not hospitalized, a desirable vaccine yields positive ACE, not negative.
- Minor comment 4: typo: table labeled “Table 1” but cited as “Table 5.”
- Overall: interesting and promising paper but technically dense and some points need clearer exposition.
