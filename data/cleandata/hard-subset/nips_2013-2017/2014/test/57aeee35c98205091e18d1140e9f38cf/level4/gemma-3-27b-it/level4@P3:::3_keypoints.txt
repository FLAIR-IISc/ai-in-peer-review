- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute ACE bounds under relaxed faithfulness via relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Timely and interesting problem; principled, well-motivated approach combining known mathematical tools.
- Technically sound with detailed supplementary material, though overall context sometimes lost.
- Main conceptual challenge: interpreting a distribution over bounds from unfaithful relaxations lacks clear practical meaning.
- Bounds can be wide, potentially rendering results uninformative for practitioners.
- Method provides intervals but no preferred point estimate, risking misinterpretation by users.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and not convincing about practical benefits.
- The ‘bias’ metric used for comparisons is inappropriate because methods estimate fundamentally different quantities.
- Results on the influenza dataset are uninformative (e.g., ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64) offers little actionable insight).
- Crucial omission: no practical guidance on selecting relaxation parameters; left for future work.
- Algorithmic presentation is hard to follow; many parameters and steps lack detailed explanation, hindering reproducibility.
- Supplement likely contains sufficient implementation detail, but main text should be clearer.
- Method is novel as a combination of familiar techniques and distinct from recent related work.
- Significance lies in new direction and ideas; not immediately applicable to practitioners in current form.
- Back-substitution step’s practical efficacy is questioned and needs empirical support.
- Multiple bounds arise from different possible (W, Z) pairs, which should be clarified.
- Paper overlooks related prior work (e.g., Geiger et al. UAI 2014) when claiming novelty of analytical bounds.
- Some terminology, phrasing, and minor typos should be corrected or clarified (multiple specific line references).
- Table 1 and other experiments do not clearly show WPP’s advantage over faithfulness-based estimates.
- Claims like “WPP is quite stable” may reflect uninformative bounds rather than method strength.
- Framing WPP as a “compromise” is misleading; it addresses different inference trade-offs.
- Reviewer recommends rejection in current form due to weak experiments, interpretability issues, and missing parameter-selection guidance.
- Addressing experimental validation, interpretation of output, and relaxation-parameter selection would substantially strengthen the paper.
