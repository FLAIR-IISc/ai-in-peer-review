- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to estimate ACE by exploiting observational conditional independencies and "weak" causal paths.  
- Relaxes the standard faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellations that induce conditional independencies without ruling out confounding.  
- Produces a posterior over bounds on the ACE using linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Builds on Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to generate candidate instrumental variables and modify point-estimate approaches.  
- Introduces inequality constraints on causal paths and a linear-programming approach to compute ACE bounds.  
- Proposes computational relaxations: symbolic bounds and a fast message-passing-like algorithm to avoid full LP optimization.  
- Demonstrates empirically (synthetic and real data) that WPP can yield tighter ACE bounds than existing methods.  
- Technically sound and innovative in combining Entner's Rule 1, faithfulness relaxation, and inequality constraints.  
- Insufficient justification for using Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction; paper should explicitly assume and discuss the Fig.2 structure.  
- Lacks general, principled criteria for selecting relaxation parameters, though these crucially affect bound results.  
- Experimental section and implementation details are under-explained; Table 1 and some algorithm descriptions are unclear/incorrectly referenced.  
- Originality is moderate: combines known ideas in a novel unified way but does not strongly depart from prior work.  
- Significance is promising for tighter ACE bounds but limited by sparse experiments and incomplete assessment of generality.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel method, useful compromise between faithfulness-based point estimates and IV bounds, demonstrated improvements in experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: theoretical/validity gaps (Entner inverse), missing parameter selection guidance, weak experimental/implementation exposition, and minor typos.
