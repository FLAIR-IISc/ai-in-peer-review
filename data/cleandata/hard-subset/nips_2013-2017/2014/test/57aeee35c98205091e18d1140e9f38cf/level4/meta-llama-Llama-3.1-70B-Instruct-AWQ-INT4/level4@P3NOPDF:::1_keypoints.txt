1. Paper tackles ACE estimation between binary variables with partially unspecified covariate causal structure.  
2. It builds on Entner et al.'s (2013) rule to select covariates that block back-door paths, but that rule is only justified under faithfulness.  
3. Authors propose using Entner et al.'s rule to produce bounds (not point estimates) and develop a method following Ramsahai (2012).  
4. Main idea is interesting and potentially useful, though reviewer hasn't fully digested technical details.  
5. Major concern: role of Entner et al. conditions without faithfulness is unclear—those conditions lack obvious causal implications absent faithfulness.  
6. Reviewer suspects the conditions might inform choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly leaves this aside and does not clarify.  
7. It seems the proposed machinery may not crucially depend on Entner et al.'s conditions; reviewer wonders if experimental robustness stems from that.  
8. Minor: phrasing “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is odd—unfaithfulness implies extra independencies; framework seems to aim for “almost violations.”  
9. Experimental suggestion: report performance of Entner et al.'s method with stringent thresholds, treating “don't know” as uninformative bounds.  
10. Clarification needed: if X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized, why does a negative ACE indicate a desirable vaccine?  
11. Formatting error: table cited as “Table 5” but labeled “Table 1.”  
12. Overall verdict: interesting contribution but details are hard to follow and key conceptual clarifications are needed.
