This paper proposes a novel approach to derive bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. The main principles of this approach are the use of Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption to introduce the possibility of path-cancellation at some degree, and the introduction of assumptions represented by inequality constraints. A computational scheme for the ACE bound is formulated using linear programming under these principles.

Quality
The authors have made an effort to establish a novel method to derive more accurate bounds on ACE than the state of the art. They combined several feasible principles and succeeded in providing tighter bounds. However, some criteria used to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained. Specifically, the validity of using Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed. For its use in the inverse direction, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig. 2. This should be clearly explained and discussed.

Furthermore, the generic criteria for choosing appropriate parameters for relaxation are not provided. Since these parameters significantly affect the result of the bounds, the principle to determine the parameters should be more thoroughly investigated and included in the work.

Clarity
The explanation of the paper's theoretical part is basically comprehensive, except for the essential discussion mentioned above. However, the demonstration of the experimental results is not well described. The actual implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained. The definition of the parameters shown in Table 1 is not clearly given.

Additionally, there are some typos. For example, "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" on page 6 is incorrect, as there is no table of the algorithm. Similarly, "Results are summarized in Table 5" on page 8 is incorrect, as there is no Table 5 (maybe Table 1).

Originality
The problem setting of this paper has been addressed by some earlier work, as the authors pointed out. In this regard, the scope of this paper does not have very strong originality. However, the idea of combining the three ideas in a unified shape contains some originality. But, as mentioned earlier, the validity of the combination is not very clearly discussed.

Significance
In terms of deriving tighter bounds on ACE, this method shows some significance. However, its performance has been checked by quite limited experiments. Its generality in both theory and wide experiments has not been strictly assessed. The paper addresses an important problem, which is to evaluate the bound of the strength of causal relations. However, the validity of the presented idea should be more thoroughly described in both theory and experiments.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to derive bounds on ACE, which is an important problem in causal inference.
* The authors have made an effort to combine several feasible principles to provide tighter bounds on ACE.
* The paper addresses a significant problem in evaluating the bound of the strength of causal relations.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper lacks a comprehensive explanation of some criteria used to define the bounds.
* The generic criteria for choosing appropriate parameters for relaxation are not provided.
* The demonstration of the experimental results is not well described.
* The paper has some typos and incorrect references to tables.
* The originality of the paper is limited, as the problem setting has been addressed by earlier work.
* The performance of the method has been checked by limited experiments, and its generality has not been strictly assessed.
