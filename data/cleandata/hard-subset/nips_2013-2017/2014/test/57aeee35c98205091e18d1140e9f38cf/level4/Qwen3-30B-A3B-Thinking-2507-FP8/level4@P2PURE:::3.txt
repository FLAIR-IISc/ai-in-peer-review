

The paper introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm, designed to compute bounds on an inferred average causal effect (ACE) while accounting for potential violations of the standard faithfulness assumption in causal inference. It achieves this by introducing relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and employing a linear programming framework to derive upper and lower ACE bounds for a given link, subject to these constraints and appropriate covariate adjustment. This problem is timely and significant, making the paper both relevant and interesting. I commend the principled approach to explicitly quantifying the impact of (weak) unfaithful interaction patterns, combined with well-established mathematical tools for solving the associated optimization problems.

However, the practical relevance of the method remains questionable. The output—a distribution over bounds parameterized by "unfaithfulness relaxations"—lacks conceptual clarity: it is unclear how likely these unfaithful scenarios are, and thus whether the derived bounds are meaningful or realistic. Many relaxations yield excessively wide bounds, rendering them practically uninformative. Furthermore, the method does not provide a preferred point estimate within the interval, despite practitioners likely interpreting the bounds as confidence intervals around a central estimate.

The experimental evaluation is inadequate. The "bias" metric used for comparison is meaningless, as it measures fundamentally different quantities across methods, obscuring whether WPP offers tangible value to practitioners or merely adds unnecessary complexity. The practical performance on the influenza dataset is particularly concerning: the ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64) yields almost no actionable conclusion (though this may be the most accurate assessment possible).

**Quality**  
The technical approach is sound, with sufficient detail provided in the supplementary material (though I did not verify its contents). Ideas are well-introduced, though the broader context is occasionally lost. Critical aspects like relaxation parameter selection are deferred to future work, hindering assessment of potential pitfalls. The experimental evaluation lacks balance and robustness.

**Clarity**  
The paper is reasonably well-written, except for the conclusion. The algorithmic steps and numerous subtle parameter modifications are difficult to follow due to insufficient methodological explanation (the supplement may suffice, but the main text is unclear).

**Originality**  
The method constitutes a novel combination of established techniques, closely related yet sufficiently distinct from recent work on the same problem. It has potential to stimulate cross-fertilization across approaches.

**Significance**  
The paper’s primary significance lies in its conceptual contribution to incorporating additional techniques for solving this challenging problem. However, its current implementation is unlikely to be immediately useful for practitioners, though some WPP ideas may prove valuable in future work.

**Summary**  
Relevant and highly topical problem. Interesting method with clear audience potential. Current application limited but likely extendable to broader cases. Reasonably well-written, though improvements are needed. Technically interesting solutions. Poor experimental evaluation. Relevance and proper interpretation of the output distribution over bounds remain unclear.
