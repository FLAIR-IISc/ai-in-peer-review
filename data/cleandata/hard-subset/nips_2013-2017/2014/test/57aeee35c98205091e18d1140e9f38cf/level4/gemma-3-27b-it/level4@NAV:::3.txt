The paper introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm, designed to compute bounds on the inferred average causal effect (ACE) while accounting for potential violations of the faithfulness assumption in causal inference. The approach utilizes relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions, and then employs a linear programming framework to determine upper and lower bounds on the ACE for a given link, adjusting for an appropriate set of covariates under these constraints.

This is a timely and interesting contribution, as the problem of robustness to unfaithfulness currently receives considerable attention. The paper’s approach is principled, attempting to explicitly quantify the impact of (weak) unfaithful interactions using established mathematical tools for optimization.

However, the practical interpretation of the results remains challenging. The output is a distribution over bounds that are functions of the ‘unfaithful relaxations’ relative to a given model; the likelihood of these unfaithful instances, and consequently the relevance of the resulting bounds, is unclear.  Many relaxations yield wide bounds, potentially diminishing their usefulness. Furthermore, the method does not provide a preferred point estimate within the resulting interval, despite the likelihood that users will interpret it as such.

The experimental evaluation is weak. The ‘bias’ metric used for comparison with competitors is problematic, as it measures fundamentally different quantities. Consequently, it’s unclear whether the WPP output provides a valuable addition for practitioners or represents a complicated distraction. The practical performance on the influenza dataset is also concerning; an ACE in the range of (-0.23, 0.64) provides limited conclusive information, although this may be a fair assessment given the data. 

**Quality:** The approach is technically sound, with detailed explanations in the supplementary materials (which I have not fully verified). The ideas are well-introduced, though the broader context is occasionally lost.  Important aspects, such as selecting the relaxation parameters, are deferred to future work, hindering a full assessment of their potential impact. The experimental evaluation lacks balance and robustness.

**Clarity:** The paper is reasonably well-written, with the exception of the conclusion. The algorithmic steps and parameter modifications were challenging to follow due to a lack of detailed explanation. While I believe I understand the core concepts, complete implementation based solely on the provided description might be difficult (the supplement may suffice).

**Originality:** The method appears to be a novel combination of existing techniques. It is related to, but distinct from, recent work addressing the same problem, potentially fostering cross-fertilization of approaches.

**Significance:** The paper’s significance lies primarily in the ideas and direction it offers for addressing this challenging problem, demonstrating how to incorporate different techniques. In its current form, it is unlikely to be a definitive solution for practitioners, although the underlying ideas within the WPP algorithm could prove influential.

**Summary:** This work addresses a relevant and highly topical problem. It presents an interesting method that will likely attract attention. While current applications are limited, the approach has the potential to extend to more general cases. The paper is reasonably well-written, though improvements are possible. It features technically interesting solutions, but is hampered by a poor experimental evaluation, and the relevance and interpretation of the resulting distribution of bounds require further consideration.
