- Paper tackles estimating average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially unknown.  
- Uses Entner et al. (2013) selection rule to identify covariates that block back-door paths, but that rule depends on the faithfulness assumption.  
- Proposes applying Entner’s rule to derive bounds (not point estimates) on the ACE, leveraging Ramsahai (2012) methods.  
- Core idea appears sound, novel, and potentially valuable, though reviewer has not fully parsed all technical details.  
- Reviewer is puzzled about the role of Entner et al.’s conditions without faithfulness, since they do not clearly constrain the causal structure in that setting.  
- Reviewer’s tentative interpretation: verified conditions might inform relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly rejects this link and needs clarification.  
- Experiments’ robustness may not critically depend on Entner et al.’s conditions; reviewer requests clarification or evidence.  
- Minor: phrasing in Section 3 misleading—unfaithfulness is extra independence, so should describe modeling bounded deviations from independence.  
- Minor: experiments should report Entner et al.’s method performance under stringent thresholds (treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds) for comparison.  
- Minor: on p.8, wording about X=1 (flu shot) and Y=1 (no hospitalization) is confusing—negative ACE implies reduced hospitalization (vaccine efficacy).  
- Minor: Table citation error—Table 1 (top of p.8) is cited as Table 5.  
- Overall: interesting contribution but technical exposition is challenging to follow and some conceptual/experimental clarifications are needed.
