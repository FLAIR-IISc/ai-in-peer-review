- Proposes WPP algorithm that computes ACE upper/lower bounds under possible violations of faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Timely and interesting; principled attempt to quantify impact of weak unfaithful interactions with established mathematical tools.
- Conceptual interpretability problem: output is a distribution over bounds tied to relaxation choices but gives no notion of how likely those unfaithful instances are.
- Many relaxation settings produce very wide, potentially useless bounds; method does not provide a preferred point estimate for users.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced; the "bias" metric used is meaningless for comparing WPP to competitors because they measure different quantities.
- Practical performance concerns: influenza dataset yields an almost uninformative ACE interval (−0.23, 0.64).
- Technically sound with supplementary details, but key practical aspects (e.g., choosing relaxation parameters) are deferred and underexplored.
- Clarity issues: algorithmic steps and many similar parameters are hard to follow; paper may not be sufficient to implement the method without the supplement.
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques and related to recent work, but sufficiently different to be potentially influential.
- Significance: brings a valuable direction/ideas to the problem, though current implementation is unlikely ready for practitioners.
- Doubts about efficacy of the back-substitution refinement step and need for empirical evidence of its effectiveness.
- Several presentation and wording issues, typos, and places that need more intuition/explanation (e.g., interpretation of parameters, relation between search and bounds computation).
- Missing or insufficient discussion of related prior work (e.g., UAI2014 paper on bounding confounding).
- Critique of emotive/biased language in discussion and conclusion; several specific line-level corrections suggested.
