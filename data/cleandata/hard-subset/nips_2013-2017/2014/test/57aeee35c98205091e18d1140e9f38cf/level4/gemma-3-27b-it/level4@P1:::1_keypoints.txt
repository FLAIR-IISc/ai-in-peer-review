- Addresses ACE estimation between two binary variables under uncertain causal structure over covariates.  
- Builds on Entner et al.’s (2013) rule to select covariates but relaxes the faithfulness assumption.  
- Proposes deriving bounds on the ACE using a Ramsahai (2012)-inspired method.  
- Central idea is sensible and potentially valuable, though some technical details are hard to follow.  
- Unclear how Entner et al.’s rule conditions inform the method without faithfulness; they may guide relaxation parameters but this is not explained.  
- The paper seems not critically dependent on Entner et al.’s conditions; a clarification of this connection is requested.  
- Wording in Section 3 is slightly inaccurate: unfaithfulness allows additional independencies, so describe the framework as accommodating “near-violations” of faithfulness.  
- Experimental section should include Entner et al.’s original method as a baseline (reporting “don’t know” when thresholds aren’t met).  
- Data labeling/interpretation error: claim “Y = 1 indicates the patient was not hospitalized” contradicts the statement that a negative ACE implies a desirable vaccine.  
- Citation/table inconsistency: “Table 1” appears on page 8 but is referenced as “Table 5” in the text.  
- Argument for acceptance: addresses an important, fundamental problem in observational causal inference.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel approach by relaxing faithfulness and reporting bounds instead of point estimates.  
- Argument for acceptance: methodology combining causal inference, linear programming, and Bayesian inference is well-motivated.  
- Argument for acceptance: experiments (synthetic and real-world) show the method’s potential, albeit limited.  
- Arguments against acceptance: paper is technically dense and difficult to fully understand; role of Entner et al.’s rule remains unclear; experimental validation is limited.
