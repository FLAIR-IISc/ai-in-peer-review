- Addresses estimating average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially unspecified.  
- Builds on Entner et al.'s (2013) rule for selecting covariates to block back-door paths, but that rule assumes faithfulness.  
- Proposes using Entner et al.'s rule to derive bounds for the ACE (rather than point estimates) and develops a method following Ramsahai (2012).  
- Main idea is intriguing and potentially useful, but several technical details are unclear to the reviewer.  
- Key concern: the role and implications of Entner et al.'s conditions without the faithfulness assumption are unclear.  
- Reviewer suspects the conditions might inform choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly leaves this aside.  
- Questions whether the methodology actually depends on Entner et al.'s conditions and whether experimental robustness is related to that.  
- Minor comment 1: Phrase "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is odd—unfaithfulness usually means extra independences; suggest clarifying intent (e.g., "almost violations").  
- Minor comment 2: Experiments should report Entner et al.'s method performance using their stringent thresholds, treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds.  
- Minor comment 3: Text implying X=1 (flu shot) and Y=1 (not hospitalized) makes ACE sign interpretation unclear—needs clarification.  
- Minor comment 4: Citation error—"Table 1" on top of page 8 is incorrectly cited as "Table 5" in the text.
