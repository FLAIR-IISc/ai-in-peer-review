- Introduces WPP algorithm to compute ACE bounds under possible faithfulness violations using relaxation parameters and linear programming.  
- Timely and interesting problem; principled attempt to quantify weak unfaithful interactions with standard mathematical tools.  
- Novel combination of familiar techniques; sufficiently distinct from recent related work and potentially stimulative.  
- Output (a distribution over bounds) is conceptually hard to interpret and its practical relevance and realism are unclear.  
- Many relaxation choices produce wide bounds, which may make results uninformative for practitioners.  
- Method does not provide or justify a preferred point estimate within intervals, risking user misinterpretation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and unbalanced; the “bias” metric is not meaningful for comparing WPP to competitors.  
- Practical performance on the influenza dataset is weak (very wide ACE interval), limiting usefulness.  
- Technically sound with many details in the supplement, but key aspects (e.g., choosing relaxation parameters) are deferred and under-specified.  
- Clarity issues: algorithmic steps and many subtly different parameters are hard to follow and may impede reproducibility.  
- Questionable practical efficacy of the back-substitution refinement; need evidence of its effectiveness.  
- Concerns about sample-size sensitivity (e.g., behavior with small n like 500 records) and stability claims.  
- Several specific methodological clarifications requested: interpret constraint parameters, relation between search and bounds computation, how admissible sets depend on theta.  
- Minor issues: wording/typos, misleading phrases (e.g., “unstable”), missing citation to related UAI2014 work, and emotive/biased language in discussion.
