- Proposes a novel approach to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using reverse application of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness allowing path cancellation, and inequality constraints.  
- Introduces a linear-programming computational scheme to derive ACE bounds from these principles.  
- Claims resulting bounds are narrower and more accurate than state-of-the-art methods.  
- Does not adequately justify the validity of applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse; this implicitly assumes the structural configuration shown in Fig. 2 and should be explicitly stated.  
- Lacks generic guidance for selecting relaxation parameters, despite those parameters having large influence on the resulting bounds.  
- Theoretical explanations are largely comprehensive but omit crucial discussions about reverse Entner’s Rule and parameter selection.  
- Experimental presentation is unclear: algorithmic implementation is not thoroughly described.  
- Table references and parameter definitions are confusing or incorrect (e.g., incorrect references to Table 1 and Table 5), and the manuscript contains typographical errors.  
- The idea of unifying reverse Entner’s Rule, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints shows some originality but is not a substantial leap beyond prior work.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited, so the method’s generality and robustness across diverse scenarios remain unassessed.  
- Overall significance is promising—tighter ACE bounds are important—but the approach needs stronger theoretical and empirical justification.
