- Introduces a novel method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using a reversed application of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints.  
- Formulates a linear programming approach to compute the resulting ACE bounds.  
- Claims tighter and more accurate bounds than state-of-the-art methods despite no algorithmic optimization.  
- Justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is insufficient and the structural assumption (e.g., Fig. 2) must be explicitly stated and discussed.  
- The relaxation of the faithfulness assumption lacks guidance on how to select parameter values, which strongly influence the bounds.  
- Implementation and experimental details are under-specified; parameters in Table 1 (and referenced Table 5) are not clearly defined.  
- Manuscript contains minor errors and incorrect table references that hinder clarity (e.g., “full algorithm shown in Table 1” is inaccurate).  
- Originality stems from the unified combination of techniques rather than the core problem, but the validity and benefits of this combination are not fully substantiated.  
- Evaluation is limited; broader theoretical analysis and more comprehensive experiments are needed to establish generality and practical significance.
