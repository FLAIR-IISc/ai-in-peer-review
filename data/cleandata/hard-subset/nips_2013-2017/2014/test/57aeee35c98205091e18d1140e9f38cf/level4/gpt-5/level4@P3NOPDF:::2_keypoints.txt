- Proposes an LP-based method combining reverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints to compute tighter ACE bounds.  
- Empirically appears to produce narrower and more accurate bounds than prior approaches.  
- Core assumption—applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse—is not formally justified or constrained, risking unsound inferences.  
- The paper fails to specify formal conditions or provide counterexamples clarifying when reverse Entner’s Rule 1 is valid or unsafe.  
- The faithfulness relaxation is only partially specified; the choice and interpretation of relaxation parameters are underexplained.  
- No principled guidance or data-driven procedures are provided for selecting relaxation parameters.  
- Lacks sensitivity analyses showing how bounds change with different relaxation settings.  
- Theoretical presentation is mostly clear except for missing justification of reverse Entner’s Rule 1 and relaxation parameter guidance.  
- Algorithmic details are insufficient for reproducibility: missing full pseudo-code and explicit LP specification (variables, constraints, objective).  
- Parameter definitions and ranges (e.g., in tables) are unclear and not linked to constraints or relaxations.  
- Inconsistencies and misnumbering in tables/figures hinder clarity and cross-referencing.  
- Novelty lies in combining known ideas, but originality is limited without rigorous treatment of reverse Entner’s Rule 1 and relaxation selection.  
- Significance is promising but currently limited by narrow empirical evaluation and unclear generality.  
- Experiments lack breadth, ablation studies, and comparisons to strong baselines across varied graphs, sample sizes, and noise regimes.  
- Suggestions: provide formal conditions/proofs or counterexamples for reverse Entner’s Rule 1, define and justify relaxation parameters, include sensitivity analyses, add full LP pseudo-code and complexity/runtimes, expand experiments and ablations, and fix presentation issues.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially impactful approach but not yet convincing due to foundational justification gaps and weak experimental/reproducibility support.
