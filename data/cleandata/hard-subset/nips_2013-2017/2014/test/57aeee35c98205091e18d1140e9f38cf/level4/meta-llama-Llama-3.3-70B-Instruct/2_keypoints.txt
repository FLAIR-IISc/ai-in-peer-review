- Proposes a novel method for ACE bounds using the inverse of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxing faithfulness to allow path-cancellation and adding inequality constraints.  
- Formulates a linear-programming computational scheme to compute the ACE bounds.  
- Empirical results show narrow and accurate bounds compared to state-of-the-art, though algorithm is not optimized.  
- Insufficient justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction; needs explicit assumption of the Fig.2 structure and discussion.  
- Key criteria for defining bounds and the validity of combining the three principles are not thoroughly explained.  
- No generic principle or guidance for choosing relaxation parameters, which strongly affect results.  
- Theoretical exposition is mostly comprehensive but misses the essential discussion noted above.  
- Experimental description and algorithm implementation details are unclear; parameter definitions in tables are ambiguous.  
- Paper contains typos and incorrect/missing table references, hurting clarity.  
- Limited originality: the problem is studied before, though the unified combination of concepts has some novelty.  
- Limited significance due to sparse experiments and lack of rigorous assessment of generality in theory and practice.
