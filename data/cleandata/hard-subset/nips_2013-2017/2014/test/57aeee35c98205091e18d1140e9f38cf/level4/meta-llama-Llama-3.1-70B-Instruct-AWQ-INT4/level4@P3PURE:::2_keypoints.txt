- Proposes a novel method to bound average causal effect (ACE) using inverse Entner's Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing partial path-cancellation), and inequality constraints solved via linear programming.
- Reported bounds are narrow and more accurate than state-of-the-art approaches despite unoptimized algorithms.
- Combination of principles yields tighter bounds and is an interesting contribution.
- Validity of using Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly justified; authors should explicitly assume and explain the Fig. 2 structure.
- Criteria for choosing relaxation parameters are not provided; principled guidance for parameter selection is missing despite their strong influence on results.
- Theoretical exposition is mostly comprehensive but lacks the essential discussion noted above.
- Experimental implementation details are poorly described and the algorithm is not fully explained.
- Definitions of parameters in Table 1 are unclear and there are incorrect/missing table references and other typos.
- Originality is limited because the problem setting has prior work, though the unified combination of three ideas has some novelty.
- Significance is promising for tighter ACE bounds, but experiments are limited and the method’s theoretical and empirical generality is insufficiently assessed.
