- Paper tackles ACE estimation between two binary variables with partially known covariate causal structure, using Entner et al.’s selection rule to produce bounds (inspired by Ramsahai 2012).  
- Central idea appears sound, interesting, and practically useful.  
- Unclear role of Entner et al.’s conditions once faithfulness is dropped: they do not obviously constrain the causal graph and may not materially affect the core machinery.  
- Authors hint Entner conditions could guide relaxation-parameter choices but explicitly set that issue aside; need clarification on whether observed empirical robustness depends on those conditions.  
- Minor: The phrase “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading—unfaithfulness implies extra independences; suggest rephrase to “allow near-violations by bounding dependency magnitudes.”  
- Minor: Experiments should include Entner et al.’s method with stricter thresholds (treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds) for clearer comparison.  
- Minor: Clarify sign convention in the flu-shot example (if X=1 is vaccinated and Y=1 is not hospitalized, explain why a negative ACE would be desirable).  
- Minor: Fix table label mismatch (table at top of p.8 labeled “Table 1” but cited as “Table 5”).  
- Overall: Promising approach but exposition is dense; clearer discussion of how the Entner rule interacts with relaxation parameters and exactly where/when faithfulness is used or relaxed would improve readability and interpretability.
