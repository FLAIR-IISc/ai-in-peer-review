- Proposes a novel LP-based method to derive ACE bounds using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness allowing path cancellation, and added inequality constraints.  
- Empirically yields remarkably tight and accurate bounds compared to state-of-the-art, despite unoptimized algorithm implementation.  
- Theoretical contribution is interesting by unifying three principles, but the combination’s validity is not fully justified.  
- Critical gap: applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is inadequately justified—must explicitly assume and discuss the structural configuration (Figure 2).  
- Critical gap: no generic criteria or principled method for selecting relaxation parameters, though they strongly affect bound quality.  
- Clarity: theoretical exposition is mostly clear except for the unresolved justification and parameter-selection issues.  
- Clarity/experiments: algorithm implementation details are incomplete and experimental parameters (Table 1 vs. Table 5) are ambiguously defined.  
- Typos/inconsistencies: references to a missing algorithm table and contradictory table citations need correction.  
- Originality: some novelty in unifying ideas, but overall scope lacks strong originality given prior related work.  
- Significance: method is promising for tighter ACE bounds but lacks rigorous theoretical generality and extensive empirical robustness across diverse settings.
