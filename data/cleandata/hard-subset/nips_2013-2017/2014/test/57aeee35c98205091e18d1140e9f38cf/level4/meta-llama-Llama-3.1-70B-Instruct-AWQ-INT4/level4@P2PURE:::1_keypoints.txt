- Problem: estimates average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables with partially specified causal structure, using Entner et al.'s rule to select covariates and developing bounds following Ramsahai (2012).  
- Overall impression: main idea is sensible, interesting, and useful, though technical details are not fully digested.  
- Concern: Entner et al.'s rule is justified only under faithfulness, so its implications without faithfulness are unclear.  
- Hypothesis: the Entner conditions might inform the choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly leaves this connection unexplored.  
- Observation: the proposed machinery may not crucially depend on Entner’s conditions, which could explain the method’s apparent experimental robustness.  
- Request: please clarify the role of Entner et al. conditions in the approach and their relation to relaxation parameters and robustness.  
- Minor comment: wording that the framework “expresses violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is unusual—unfaithfulness implies extra independences; the framework seems to allow “almost violations” by bounding dependence magnitude.  
- Experimental suggestion: report performance of Entner et al.'s method using their stringent thresholds, treating “don't know” as uninformative bounds.  
- Clarification needed: in the flu-shot example, why a negative ACE implies the vaccine is desirable is unclear.  
- Correction: table reference mismatch—text cites “Table 5” but the table is labeled “Table 1.”
