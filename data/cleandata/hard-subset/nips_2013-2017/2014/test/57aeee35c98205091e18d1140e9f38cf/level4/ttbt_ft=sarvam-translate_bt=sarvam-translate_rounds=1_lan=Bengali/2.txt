This paper proposes a new method for estimating the average causal effect (ACE) of an X on Y. Its main principles are to use the inverse of the rule of 1, to relax the assumption of faithfulness to introduce the possibility of path cancellation to some extent, and to introduce assumptions represented by some inequality constraints. A method for estimating ACE limits using linear programming under these principles has been formulated. Although the authors have not yet optimized the algorithm for estimating limits, the obtained limits appear to be very narrow and accurate compared to other state-of-the-art methods.

Quality
The authors have attempted to establish a new method for estimating more accurate ACE limits than state-of-the-art methods. They have succeeded in providing more rigorous limits by combining several possible principles. In this regard, the content of the paper is very interesting.

However, some criteria for determining limits have not been fully explained. In particular, the validity of using the rule of 1 for the inverse has not been clearly discussed. For its use for the inverse, the structure shown in Figure 2 should be clearly assumed by the authors. It should be clearly explained and discussed.

Also, general criteria for selecting suitable parameters for relaxation have not been provided. Since these significantly affect the limit results, the principle of parameter determination should be better explored and incorporated into the work.

Clarity
The explanation of the paper on the theoretical part seems to be largely broad, except for the essential discussion mentioned above.

However, the demonstration of experimental results has not been well described. As I mentioned above, the actual implementation of the algorithm has not been fully explained. The definitions of the parameters shown in Table 1 (Table 5?) have not been clearly given.

Originality
As the authors have mentioned, the problem formulation in this paper has been discussed through some previous work. In this regard, the scope of this paper is not very original.

There is some originality in the idea of combining three concepts into an integrated form. However, as I mentioned above, the validity of the combination has not been discussed very clearly.

Importance
In the case of estimating more rigorous limits of ACE, this method shows some significance. However, its effectiveness has been verified through fairly limited experiments. Its generality in both theory and extensive experiments has not been strictly evaluated. The paper discusses an important problem such as estimating the strength of causal relationships. However, the validity of the presented concept should be better described in both theory and experiment.
