This paper proposes a novel approach to derive bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. The main principles underlying this approach are the use of Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption to allow for path-cancellation to some degree, and the introduction of assumptions represented by inequality constraints. A computational scheme for the ACE bound is formulated using linear programming under these principles. Although the authors have not yet optimized the algorithm to derive the bounds, the resulting bounds appear to be very narrow and accurate compared to other state-of-the-art approaches.

Quality:
The authors have attempted to establish a new method to derive more accurate bounds on ACE than the current state of the art. They have successfully combined several feasible principles to provide tighter bounds. In this regard, the content of the paper is very interesting. However, some criteria used to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained. In particular, the validity of using Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed. To justify its use in the inverse direction, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig. 2. This should be clearly explained and discussed.

Furthermore, the generic criteria for choosing appropriate parameters for relaxation are not provided. Since these parameters significantly affect the results of the bounds, the principle for determining the parameters should be more thoroughly investigated and included in the work.

Clarity:
The explanation of the paper's theoretical part is generally comprehensive, except for the essential discussion mentioned above. However, the demonstration of the experimental results is not well described. As mentioned earlier, the actual implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained. The definition of the parameters shown in Table 1 is not clearly given.

Additionally, there are some typos, such as "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" on page 6, where no table of the algorithm is provided, and "Results are summarized in Table 5" on page 8, where Table 5 is not found (possibly referring to Table 1).

Originality:
The problem setting of this paper has been addressed by earlier work, as the authors pointed out. In this regard, the scope of this paper does not have very strong originality. However, the idea of combining the three ideas in a unified framework contains some originality. Nevertheless, as mentioned earlier, the validity of this combination is not clearly discussed.

Significance:
In terms of deriving tighter bounds on ACE, this method shows some significance. However, its performance has been checked by quite limited experiments. Its generality, both in theory and through extensive experiments, has not been strictly assessed. The paper addresses an important problem, namely evaluating the bound of the strength of causal relations. Nevertheless, the validity of the presented idea should be more thoroughly described in both theory and experiments.
