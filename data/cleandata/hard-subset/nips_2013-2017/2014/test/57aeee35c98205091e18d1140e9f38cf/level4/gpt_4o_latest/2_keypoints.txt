- Paper proposes a novel method to derive ACE bounds using inverse application of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and added inequality constraints.  
- Method is implemented as a linear-programming framework to compute ACE bounds.  
- Resulting bounds appear substantially tighter and more accurate than current state-of-the-art.  
- The inverse application of Entner’s Rule 1 is not sufficiently justified; required structural assumptions (e.g., Fig. 2) must be explicitly stated and defended.  
- The relaxation of the faithfulness assumption lacks general criteria for choosing relaxation parameters, though these parameters strongly affect bounds.  
- The validity of combining the three principles into a single framework is not thoroughly discussed.  
- The algorithmic implementation is not optimized and implementation details are inadequately described.  
- Experimental presentation is unclear: parameter definitions in tables are ambiguous and referenced tables (Table 1, Table 5) are missing or mislabeled.  
- Typographical and referencing errors reduce clarity (e.g., incorrect table references on pages 6 and 8).  
- The evaluation is limited in scope; theoretical generality and empirical robustness across broader settings are not assessed.  
- Overall contribution is interesting and moderately novel, but theoretical justification and experimental validation need significant expansion.
