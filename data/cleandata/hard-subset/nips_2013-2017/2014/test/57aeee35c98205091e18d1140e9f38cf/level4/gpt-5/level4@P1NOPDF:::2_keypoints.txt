- Proposes a new method for bounding ACE using reverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (path cancellation), and inequality assumptions solved via linear programming.  
- Reported bounds are narrow and competitive, though the algorithm is not optimized.  
- Justification for using Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is unclear; the implied structural assumption (Fig. 2) must be stated and analyzed.  
- Paper lacks principled guidance for selecting relaxation parameters controlling faithfulness violations; these parameters strongly affect bounds.  
- Needs sensitivity analyses, domain-informed priors, or data-driven calibration to choose relaxation parameters and show how bounds vary.  
- Theoretical exposition is mostly clear except for the inverse-Entner justification and related identifiability/robustness implications.  
- Experimental details are under-specified: missing algorithm table, unclear parameter definitions (Table 1 vs Table 5), and incomplete LP constraint descriptions hindering reproducibility.  
- Editorial errors and incorrect table references must be fixed so all references resolve.  
- Originality is limited by prior work, but the specific combination of ingredients in a single LP framework has some novelty.  
- Needs clearer argument that the combined assumptions are consistent, under what structural conditions, and how each component tightens bounds.  
- Empirical validation is limited; requires broader synthetic experiments, multiple real datasets, more baselines, and an ablation study isolating each assumption’s impact.  
- Theoretical generality is not fully assessed; must formalize conditions for soundness under relaxed assumptions and characterize failure modes when assumptions are violated.  
- Overall: promising advance but requires explicit structural assumptions, principled parameter selection/sensitivity analysis, expanded/clearer experiments, and editorial fixes.
