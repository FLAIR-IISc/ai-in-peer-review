1. Paper proposes bounds for ACE with partially specified covariate causal structure, repurposing Entner et al.’s rule and using a linear-programming approach akin to Ramsahai (2012).  
2. Central idea appears sound, interesting, and potentially useful, though not all technical details were verifiable.  
3. Unclear what precise role Entner et al.’s rule plays without faithfulness — the rule’s conditions lack obvious structural implications in this framework.  
4. Authors set parameter selection aside, but the rule might instead guide choice/calibration of relaxation parameters; clarification is needed.  
5. Concern that the method’s empirical robustness may be largely independent of the rule’s conditions unless the connection to relaxed constraints is clarified.  
6. Minor comment: rephrase “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” to reflect that the framework permits near-violations (small deviations) rather than literal violations.  
7. Minor comment: experiments should report Entner et al.’s method with stringent thresholds (treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds) for direct comparison.  
8. Minor comment: reconcile outcome coding with the sign of the ACE (inconsistency about whether Y=1 means hospitalized or not).  
9. Minor comment: Table numbering error (Table 1 referenced as “Table 5” on p.8).  
10. Overall: interesting contribution but several technical sections are hard to follow and need clearer exposition.
