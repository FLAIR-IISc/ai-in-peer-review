- Proposal: new method to derive ACE bounds using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (partial path cancellation), inequality constraints, and an LP-based computation.  
- Empirical result: resulting bounds appear notably tighter and more accurate than state-of-the-art, though the algorithm is not optimized.  
- Major validity concern: applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is inadequately justified and requires explicitly assuming the causal structure shown in Figure 2.  
- Parameter selection issue: no principled framework or generic criteria are provided for choosing relaxation parameters that strongly affect bound accuracy.  
- Experimental clarity problem: the experiment/algorithm implementation is poorly described and insufficiently explained.  
- Ambiguous reporting: parameters in Table 1 (referred to as Table 5) are not clearly defined.  
- Typographical/reference errors: "full algorithm shown in Table 1" and "Results summarized in Table 5" are incorrect/missing.  
- Originality: combines three existing ideas into one framework, offering some novelty, but scope overlaps prior work and the combination’s validity isn’t rigorously established.  
- Theoretical quality: exposition is largely comprehensive aside from the unresolved issues (inverse Entner justification and parameter selection).  
- Significance limitations: method is promising for tighter ACE bounds but empirical validation is narrow and theoretical generality is not rigorously assessed; deeper justification and more experiments are needed.
