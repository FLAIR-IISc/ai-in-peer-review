- Proposes a novel method for bounding ACE by applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse, relaxing faithfulness to allow path cancellation, and encoding assumptions as inequality constraints solved via linear programming; reported bounds are tighter than prior methods.  
- The justification for using Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is insufficient and the paper should explicitly state the structural conditions (e.g., Fig. 2) that this assumes.  
- The relaxation of faithfulness to permit path cancellation is introduced but the underlying assumptions and implications are not fully discussed.  
- No general guidelines are provided for choosing relaxation parameters, despite their strong influence on resulting bounds.  
- The computational linear-programming scheme is presented but algorithmic efficiency has not been optimized.  
- Implementation details are under-specified and the experimental setup is unclear, hampering reproducibility.  
- Parameter definitions in the reported tables (Table 1 and/or Table 5) are ambiguous or missing.  
- The paper contains typos and incorrect table/algorithm references that create confusion (e.g., erroneous “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1” and “Results are summarized in Table 5”).  
- Combining the three key ideas demonstrates some originality, but the paper does not sufficiently justify the validity of their joint use.  
- The method is potentially significant due to tighter bounds, but empirical evaluation is limited and theoretical/general applicability has not been rigorously established.
