This paper proposes a novel approach to bounding the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to Y. The method leverages a reversed application of Entner's Rule 1, relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellation, and introduces inequality constraints to formalize these relaxations. The authors formulate a computation scheme for the ACE bound using linear programming, and preliminary results suggest their bounds are tighter and more accurate than those produced by existing state-of-the-art methods, despite lacking algorithmic optimization.

**Quality:**

The authors make a valuable attempt to establish a new method for deriving more accurate ACE bounds. Their combination of established principles to achieve tighter bounds is a compelling contribution. The core idea is interesting and demonstrates potential.

However, certain aspects of the bound derivation lack sufficient explanation. Specifically, the justification for applying Entner's Rule 1 in reverse requires more thorough discussion. This application implicitly assumes a structural configuration, as depicted in Fig. 2, which should be explicitly stated and its implications carefully considered. 

Furthermore, the paper does not provide clear guidance on selecting appropriate parameters for the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption. Given the significant impact of these parameters on the resulting bounds, a more rigorous investigation into their determination and sensitivity analysis is needed.

**Clarity:**

The theoretical exposition is generally comprehensive, with the exception of the critical points mentioned above regarding the reversed application of Entner's Rule 1 and parameter selection. 

The presentation of experimental results, however, needs improvement. The algorithmic implementation is not adequately detailed. The parameters presented in Table 1 (and referenced as Table 5 on page 8 – a potential typo) are not clearly defined. Providing a more complete description of the experimental setup, including parameter definitions and implementation details, would significantly enhance clarity.

I also noted several minor typos throughout the manuscript. For example, the statement “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1” on page 6 is inaccurate, as no algorithm table is present. Similarly, “Results are summarized in Table 5” on page 8 likely refers to Table 1.

**Originality:**

The problem of bounding causal effects has been previously addressed, as the authors acknowledge. Consequently, the core problem setting itself does not represent a substantial leap in originality. 

The originality of this work lies in the unification of Entner's Rule 1 (applied in reverse), relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints within a single framework. However, as previously noted, the validity of this combination is not yet fully substantiated, limiting the demonstrable originality.

**Significance:**

The method demonstrates potential significance in achieving tighter ACE bounds. However, the evaluation is currently limited in scope. While the initial experiments are promising, the generality of the approach – both in theoretical terms and across a wider range of experimental settings – remains unconfirmed. The paper tackles an important problem—evaluating the strength of causal relationships—but further work is needed to convincingly establish the validity and robustness of the proposed method. More extensive experimentation and a deeper theoretical justification are required to fully assess its impact.
