

The paper introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm for computing bounds on an inferred average causal effect (ACE) under potential violations of the standard faithfulness assumption in causal inference. It achieves this by defining relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and employing a linear programming framework to determine upper and lower ACE bounds for a given link, after adjusting for an appropriate covariate set under these constraints. The problem is timely and relevant, and the approach presents a principled effort to explicitly quantify the impact of (weak) unfaithful interaction patterns using established mathematical tools for optimization.  
However, the interpretability of the output remains conceptually challenging: the method produces a distribution of bounds dependent on 'unfaithful relaxations' relative to a model, yet the likelihood of such unfaithful instances—and thus the relevance of the bounds—is unclear. Many relaxations yield excessively wide bounds, diminishing practical utility, and the method does not specify a point estimate within the interval despite common misinterpretation as such.  
Experimental evaluation is inadequate: the reported 'bias' metric is invalid for comparison, as it measures fundamentally different quantities than competing methods, leaving uncertainty about the method's practical value. The influenza dataset results are particularly weak, with an ACE interval of (-0.23, 0.64) implying negligible actionable insight.  
Quality  
The technical approach is sound, with sufficient detail in the supplementary material (though unverified). Ideas are well-introduced, though contextual gaps exist. Critical aspects like relaxation parameter selection are deferred to future work, hindering assessment of potential pitfalls. Experimental evaluation lacks balance and robustness.  
Clarity  
The writing is generally clear (except the conclusion), but algorithmic steps and parameter nuances are difficult to follow due to insufficient methodological explanation. Implementation would likely require the supplementary material.  
Originality  
The method represents a novel combination of established techniques, closely related yet distinct from recent literature, potentially fostering cross-fertilization in the field.  
Significance  
The paper’s significance lies in its conceptual direction for incorporating diverse techniques into this challenging problem. While the current implementation is unlikely to be practitioner-ready, core ideas may prove influential.  
Summary:  
Relevant, topical problem with an interesting method likely to attract interest. Current application is limited but may generalize. Reasonably well-written but improvable. Technically sound solutions. Experimental evaluation is poor. Relevance and interpretation of the output bound distribution remain unclear.
