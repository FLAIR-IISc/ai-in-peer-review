- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to compute ACE bounds under possible faithfulness violations using relaxation parameters.
- Uses linear programming to derive upper and lower bounds on the ACE constrained by relaxed unfaithful interactions.
- Adjusts for covariates under these constraints, offering a principled way to quantify weak unfaithful interactions.
- Problem is timely and approach is technically sound and well-presented overall.
- Key methodological novelty is the use of relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions.
- Interpretation of the output distribution over bounds is unclear to users.
- Resulting bounds are often wide, reducing practical usefulness.
- Method provides no preferred point estimate within the interval, risking misinterpretation.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: the 'bias' metric is meaningless for comparisons to competitors.
- Choice of relaxation parameters is not addressed in the paper and deferred to future work, hindering assessment.
- Algorithmic steps and modifications can be hard to follow due to missing explanations of some details.
- Originality lies in combining familiar techniques; significance is mainly conceptual and directional.
- Practical performance (e.g., on the influenza dataset) is worryingly weak, questioning efficacy.
- Paper needs clearer parameter-selection guidance, better comparison to other methods, and a more balanced experimental evaluation.
