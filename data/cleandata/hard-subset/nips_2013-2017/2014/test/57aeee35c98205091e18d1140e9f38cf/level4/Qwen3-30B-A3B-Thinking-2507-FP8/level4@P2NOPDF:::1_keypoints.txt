- Paper proposes bounding the average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables under partially unknown covariate causal structure, building on Entner et al. (2013) and Ramsahai (2012).  
- Core idea is sound and potentially valuable, shifting from point estimation to bounds for robustness.  
- The role of Entner et al.'s conditions in the new framework is unclear and not explicitly justified.  
- Authors avoid assuming faithfulness, but then Entner's conditions do not inherently constrain the causal structure—this undermines theoretical grounding.  
- Unclear how Entner's conditions relate to relaxation parameters or whether observed experimental robustness is dependent on that relation.  
- If Entner's conditions are only a heuristic for parameter selection, the robustness claims lack causal justification and need clarification.  
- Minor: phrasing "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is misleading; should frame as modeling near-faithfulness/bounded dependence.  
- Minor: include baseline results using Entner et al.'s original method (stringent thresholds/"don't know" as uninformative bounds) for better comparison.  
- Minor: clarify wording in vaccine example—negative ACE indicates vaccination reduces hospitalization risk.  
- Minor: fix typographical error citing Table 1 as "Table 5".  
- Overall: technically substantial with promising experiments, but theoretical ambiguity about Entner conditions without faithfulness weakens the contribution and should be resolved.
