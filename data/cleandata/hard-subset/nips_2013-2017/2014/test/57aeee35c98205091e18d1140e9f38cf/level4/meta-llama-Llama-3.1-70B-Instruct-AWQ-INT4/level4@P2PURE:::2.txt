This paper proposes a novel approach to derive bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from variable X to variable Y. The main principles underlying this approach are the application of Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellation to some degree, and the introduction of assumptions represented by inequality constraints. A computational scheme for the ACE bound is formulated using linear programming under these principles. Although the authors have not yet optimized the algorithm for deriving bounds, the resulting bounds appear to be very narrow and accurate compared to state-of-the-art approaches.

Quality
The authors have attempted to establish a novel method for deriving more accurate bounds on ACE than existing state-of-the-art methods. By combining several feasible principles, they have successfully provided tighter bounds. In this regard, the content of the paper is very interesting.

However, some criteria used to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained. In particular, the validity of applying Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed. To use this rule in the inverse direction, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig. 2, which should be clearly explained and discussed.

Furthermore, the authors do not provide generic criteria for choosing appropriate parameters for relaxation, which significantly affect the resulting bounds. The principle for determining these parameters should be more thoroughly investigated and included in the work.

Clarity
The explanation of the theoretical part of the paper is generally comprehensive, except for the essential discussion mentioned above.

However, the demonstration of the experimental results is not well described. As mentioned earlier, the actual implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained. The definitions of the parameters shown in Table 1 (possibly Table 5) are not clearly provided.

Additionally, there are some typos, such as "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" on page 6, where no algorithm table is present, and "Results are summarized in Table 5" on page 8, where Table 5 is not found (possibly referring to Table 1).

Originality
The problem setting addressed in this paper has been explored in earlier work, as acknowledged by the authors. In this regard, the scope of this paper does not exhibit strong originality.

However, the idea of combining the three principles in a unified framework contains some originality. As mentioned earlier, the validity of this combination is not clearly discussed.

Significance
This method shows some significance in deriving tighter bounds on ACE. However, its performance has been evaluated through limited experiments, and its generality has not been strictly assessed in both theory and wide experiments. The paper addresses an important problem of evaluating the bound of the strength of causal relations. Nevertheless, the validity of the presented idea should be more thoroughly described in both theory and experiments.
