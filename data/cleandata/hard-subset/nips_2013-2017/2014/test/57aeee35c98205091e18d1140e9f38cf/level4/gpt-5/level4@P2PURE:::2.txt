This paper proposes a new approach to derive bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to a variable Y. Its main principles are: applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction, relaxing the faithfulness assumption to allow for some degree of path cancellation, and introducing assumptions expressed as inequality constraints. Under these principles, a computational scheme for ACE bounds is formulated via linear programming. Although the algorithm for deriving the bounds has not been fully optimized, the resulting bounds appear very tight and accurate compared with other state-of-the-art approaches.

Quality
The authors aim to establish a novel method for obtaining more accurate ACE bounds than existing methods. They combine several plausible principles and successfully produce tighter bounds. In this respect, the paper is quite interesting.
However, some criteria used to define the bounds are not explained in sufficient depth. In particular, the validity of using Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly addressed. To justify its inverse use, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig. 2; this should be clearly explained and discussed.
In addition, no general criteria are provided for choosing appropriate relaxation parameters. Because these parameters significantly affect the resulting bounds, the principles for selecting them should be further investigated and included in the paper.

Clarity
The theoretical exposition is largely comprehensive, aside from the essential discussion noted above.
However, the presentation of the experimental results is not well described. As mentioned, the actual implementation of the algorithm is not explained in sufficient detail. The definitions of the parameters shown in Table 1 (Table 5?) are not clearly provided.
I also noticed some typos/inconsistencies. For example:
- “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1.” on page 6 => I do not see any such algorithm table.
- “Results are summarized in Table 5.” on page 8 => I do not see Table 5 (perhaps this refers to Table 1).

Originality
As the authors note, earlier work has addressed this problem setting, so the scope of the paper is not highly original.
The idea of unifying the three principles does contain some originality. However, as noted above, the validity of this combination is not clearly discussed.

Significance
With respect to deriving tighter ACE bounds, the method has clear significance. However, its performance has been evaluated on quite limited experiments. Its generality—both in theory and across broader empirical studies—has not been rigorously assessed. The paper tackles an important problem, namely evaluating bounds on the strength of causal relations, but the validity of the proposed approach should be better substantiated in both theory and experiments.
