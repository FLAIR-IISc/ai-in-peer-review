This research paper proposes a new approach to obtain the bound of the average causal effect (ACE) from one variable X to another variable Y. Its main principles are the use of Antonar's rule in the opposite direction, relaxing the faithfulness assumption to introduce the possibility of some path cancellation, and introducing assumptions shown by some inequality constraints. A plan for calculating the ACE bound using linear programming under these principles has been prepared. Although the authors have not yet optimized the algorithm to obtain the bounds, the resulting bounds appear to be much narrower and more accurate than other state-of-the-art approaches.

Quality
The authors have attempted to establish a new method to obtain a more accurate bound of ACE than state-of-the-art methods. They combined several viable theories and succeeded in providing strict bounds. In this regard, the content of the research paper is very interesting. However, some criteria for defining the bounds have not been widely explained. In particular, the validity of using Antonar's rule in the opposite direction has not been explicitly discussed. For its use in the opposite direction, the authors should clearly consider the structure shown in Figure 2. This should be clearly explained and discussed.

Furthermore, general criteria for choosing suitable parameters for relaxation have not been provided. Since these significantly affect the result of the bounds, the principle of determining the parameters should be further investigated and incorporated into the work.

Clarity
The explanation of the research paper on the theoretical part seems to be broadly based, except for the necessary discussion that I mentioned above. However, the performance of the experimental results is not well described. As I mentioned above, the actual implementation of the algorithm is not widely explained. Originality
The problem setting of this research paper has been addressed by some earlier works, as the authors have stated. In this regard, there is not much originality within the scope of this research paper. There is some originality in the idea of combining three ideas into an integrated form. However, as I mentioned above, the validity of the combination has not been discussed very clearly.

Importance
In terms of obtaining strict bounds of ACE, this method shows some importance. However, its performance has been tested by quite limited experiments. Its generality in both theory and extensive experiments has not been strictly assessed. The research paper solves an important problem, which is to evaluate the limit of the strength of causal relationships. However, the validity of the presented idea should be better described in both theory and experiment.
