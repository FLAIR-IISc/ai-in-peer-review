- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute ACE bounds under relaxed faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.  
- Topic is timely and of considerable interest; the approach is systematic and conceptually engaging.  
- Unclear whether WPP effectively captures the influence of weak unfaithful interactions; the method’s practical fidelity is uncertain.  
- Output—distribution over bounds as a function of relaxations—is conceptually hard to interpret and its practical relevance is unclear.  
- Likelihood of unfaithful instances is not addressed, so many relaxations produce overly wide, uninformative bounds.  
- The method does not provide a preferred point estimate within derived intervals, risking misinterpretation by readers.  
- Experimental evaluation is inadequate and the chosen "bias" metric is inappropriate for comparing WPP to competing methods.  
- Comparison to other methods is questionable; WPP may add complexity without clear user benefit.  
- Case study on influenza dataset yields very wide ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64), offering little actionable insight.  
- Technically sound with many details in the supplement, but critical elements (e.g., choosing relaxation parameters) are deferred to future work.  
- Broader context and certain methodological details are insufficiently explained, making reproduction or implementation uncertain.  
- Paper is reasonably well-written but algorithmic steps and multiple similar parameters are difficult to follow; conclusion could be improved.  
- Method is novel as a combination of established techniques and related to recent work, with potential to inspire further research.  
- Significance lies mainly in ideas and direction rather than immediate practical usability; concepts may inform future advances.  
- Overall: relevant and interesting approach but limited by weak experimental validation and ambiguous practical interpretation of results.  
- Numerous editorial and clarity issues: wording fixes, clarify assumptions (causal sufficiency, W → X), and explain witness role and parameter intuitions.  
- Methodological clarifications needed: link search process to bounds computation, how admissible sets Z depend on θ, and what "max. 10 free parameters" means.  
- Cite and discuss related analytical bounds work (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI2014) and evaluate effectiveness of back-substitution in practice.  
- Clarify that bounds are not confidence intervals, avoid emotive language, address potential misinterpretations of “stable” bounds, and fix typographical errors.
