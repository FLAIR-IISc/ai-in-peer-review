- Paper estimates average causal effect (ACE) for binary variables with partially specified covariate causal structure, adapting Entner et al.'s rule to produce bounds instead of point estimates.
- Method follows Ramsahai (2012), but the role and causal interpretation of Entner et al.'s conditions within this bounding framework are unclear.
- Without the faithfulness assumption, Entner et al.'s conditions lose causal interpretability, and the paper fails to explain how they inform the bounding method.
- The reviewer suspects the conditions may correspond to relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly avoids making this connection and should clarify it.
- It appears experimental robustness may not depend critically on Entner et al.'s conditions; the authors should demonstrate or clarify this dependency.
- Technical exposition needs greater clarity overall.
- Minor: Phrase "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is misleading—unfaithfulness implies extra independencies; the framework should express "near-faithfulness" via constrained dependence magnitudes.
- Minor: Experiments should report Entner et al.'s method performance using their strict thresholds, treating "don't know" outputs as uninformative bounds.
- Minor: Clarify causal interpretation in example (X=1 flu shot, Y=1 hospitalization)—a negative ACE means the vaccine reduces hospitalization.
- Minor: Fix citation error—Table 1 (top of page 8) is referred to as "Table 5" in the text.
