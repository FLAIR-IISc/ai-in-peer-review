- Paper proposes bounding the ACE between two binary variables using Entner et al.'s covariate-selection rule combined with Ramsahai-style bounds.  
- Central conceptual gap: Entner et al.’s conditions are valid only under faithfulness, yet the paper explicitly avoids assuming faithfulness.  
- Without faithfulness, Entner conditions do not meaningfully constrain causal structure, undermining their justification.  
- The method may be robust despite not depending on Entner’s rule rather than because of it; this tension is unresolved.  
- Authors must clarify whether Entner conditions are necessary for bound derivation or merely a red herring.  
- Misleading phrasing: calling the framework “expresses violations of faithfulness” is incorrect—should describe it as bounding departures from faithfulness (dependence magnitudes).  
- Experimental comparison is incomplete: include Entner et al.’s original method with strict thresholds (treat “don’t know” as uninformative bounds) for fairness.  
- Flu vaccine example needs precise outcome sign interpretation (if Y=1 = no hospitalization, a negative ACE indicates reduced hospitalization).  
- Typo to fix: Table 1 is cited as “Table 5” in the text.  
- The shift from point estimation to bounds is valuable, but the theoretical foundation for using Entner’s rule without faithfulness is insufficient.  
- Authors should explicitly state (a) whether Entner conditions are necessary, (b) how the method avoids dependence on faithfulness, and (c) whether robustness holds when the rule is not relied upon.  
- Experiments are promising but their interpretation hinges on resolving the core theoretical issue; otherwise the approach may seem ad hoc.
