- Paper tackles estimating average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables when covariate causal structure is unknown, extending Entner et al.'s rule to derive bounds instead of point estimates.  
- Method relaxes the faithfulness assumption and builds on Ramsahai (2012) to produce bounded ACE estimates.  
- Reviewer finds the central idea intuitive, interesting, and potentially useful, though they could not verify all technical details.  
- Unclear how Entner et al.'s rule conditions function within the bounding framework once faithfulness is dropped; their implication for causal structure is not explained.  
- Question whether satisfying Entner et al.'s conditions should guide selection of relaxation parameters—paper defers this, leaving ambiguity about dependence on those conditions.  
- If the proposed machinery is largely independent of Entner et al.'s conditions, that might explain observed experimental robustness; clarification requested.  
- Minor comment: wording “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is confusing—should instead frame as modeling near-unfaithful distributions by constraining dependency magnitudes.  
- Minor comment: experiments should include comparison to original Entner et al. method (treating “don't know” as uninformative bounds) for a fairer evaluation.  
- Minor comment: clarify sign/interpretation of ACE in the flu-shot example (negative ACE currently described inconsistently).  
- Minor comment: typo—table labeled “Table 1” is referred to as “Table 5” on page 8.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and important topic but technical presentation is sometimes hard to follow and key conceptual connections need clarification.
