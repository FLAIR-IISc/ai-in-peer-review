1. Paper extends Entner et al.'s covariate selection rule to derive bounds on the ACE instead of point estimates—conceptually sound and potentially valuable.  
2. Major concern: Entner et al.'s rule is justified under faithfulness, but the paper explicitly avoids faithfulness, leaving the causal interpretation of those conditions unclear.  
3. Questioned necessity: the bound derivation appears to rely only on the covariate selection mechanism, not the specific Entner conditions, so the paper must clarify whether those conditions actually matter.  
4. Unclear whether and how Entner et al.'s conditions influence the relaxation parameters; the relationship needs detailed explanation.  
5. Minor wording issue: "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is confusing—unfaithfulness is extra independencies; the paper likely intends bounded deviations from faithfulness via constrained dependence magnitudes.  
6. Experiment suggestion: report Entner et al.'s method under stringent thresholds (treat "don't know" as uninformative bounds) to strengthen comparisons.  
7. Result interpretation error: a negative ACE (with X=1 as vaccination and Y=1 hospitalization) implies vaccination increases hospitalization risk—contradicts the claim it "suggests a desirable vaccine."  
8. Typo: Table 1 is incorrectly cited as "Table 5" in the text.  
9. Overall: approach is interesting but the technical exposition is hard to follow; clarifying the role of Entner et al.'s conditions is critical for theoretical assessment.
