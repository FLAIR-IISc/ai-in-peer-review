- Paper addresses ACE estimation with unobserved confounding by generating bounds rather than point estimates, building on Entner et al. (2013).

- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), combining linear programming and Bayesian inference to produce posterior ACE bounds.

- Key innovation: relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow bounded violations of conditional independence, producing more robust causal bounds.

- Positions WPP as a middle ground between faithfulness-based point estimation and instrumental variable bounds.

- Empirical validation: synthetic experiments and a real-world influenza vaccination application show WPP yields more informative bounds than standard IV methods.

- Theoretical strength: formalizes a relaxation of faithfulness enabling more practical causal estimation.

- Practical strength: provides a concrete implementation strategy usable by practitioners (Bayesian + LP).

- Experimental strength: demonstrates robustness in simulations and a compelling real-data example.

- Methodological clarity: presents a well-structured algorithm (Algorithm 1) and detailed technical development.

- Conceptual weakness: connection between Entner et al.'s rule and WPP is insufficiently explained; role of rule conditions vs relaxation parameters is unclear.

- Terminology issue: the phrase "bounded violations of local independence" is confusing and mischaracterizes faithfulness violations.

- Experimental presentation weakness: lacks contextualization of Entner et al.'s method under standard thresholds and unclear interpretation of negative ACE in the vaccination example.

- Technical exposition weakness: Sections 3–4 are dense and need more intuitive explanations of relaxation parameters and their relation to causal structure.

- Argument for acceptance: addresses a significant practical problem, offers a meaningful theoretical advance, and shows empirical utility.

- Argument against acceptance: requires clearer conceptual linkage to Entner's rule, clearer terminology about faithfulness, and better explanation of relaxation parameters.

- Recommendation: accept, contingent on clarifying the Entner rule connection, faithfulness terminology, and interpretation of negative ACE in the application.
