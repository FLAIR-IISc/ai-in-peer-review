- Proposes a novel method (WPP) to estimate/bound the average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially unspecified.  
- Leverages Entner et al.'s rule but relaxes the faithfulness assumption, providing bounds rather than point estimates.  
- Inverts Entner's rule by using variable pairs to create instrumental-variable–style bounds instead of back-door adjustments.  
- Introduces a new parameterization of the joint distribution to allow bounded violations of local independence.  
- Uses linear programming and Bayesian inference to compute bounds on the ACE.  
- Presents extensive experiments (synthetic and real-world influenza vaccination study) demonstrating WPP’s robustness.  
- Empirical results show WPP gives more accurate and informative bounds than naive estimators and Entner et al.’s faithfulness-based method.  
- Strengths: novel approach, provides bounds (robustness to unfaithfulness), and strong experimental support.  
- Limitations: technical details are complex and some theoretical conditions (from Entner et al.) need clearer exposition.  
- Minor suggestion: rephrase first paragraph of Section 3 to better explain bounded violations of local independence.  
- Minor suggestion: include comparisons with Entner et al.’s method using stringent thresholds and report when it yields uninformative bounds.  
- Minor suggestion: clarify why a negative ACE indicates a desirable vaccine in the influenza study.  
- Minor correction: fix citation “Table 1” on page 8 to “Table 5.”  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, potentially significant, but needs clarification/refinement for accessibility and interpretation.
