The paper presents the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm for bounding an average causal effect (ACE) while allowing for potential departures from the standard faithfulness assumption in causal inference. It does so by introducing relaxation parameters that limit unfaithful dependencies and then employing a linear programming setup to obtain upper and lower ACE bounds for a given edge, adjusting for suitable covariates under these constraints. The topic is currently of broad interest, making the work timely and relevant. I appreciate the paper’s principled approach: it explicitly quantifies the impact of (mild) unfaithful interaction patterns and leverages familiar optimization machinery to solve the associated maximization/minimization problems. Whether this is the best way to capture the impact of such deviations remains unclear. Conceptually, the output is hard to interpret: a distribution over bounds parameterized by “unfaithful relaxations” with respect to a chosen model, yet the plausibility of those unfaithful instances—and thus the practical relevance of the bounds—remains unspecified. Many relaxations yield very wide intervals that can become of limited utility. The method also avoids committing to a point estimate inside the interval, although many readers may implicitly treat it that way. Experimental assessment is weak: the “bias” metric does not offer a meaningful comparison to competing methods because it evaluates fundamentally different quantities. Consequently, it is uncertain whether WPP provides actionable insight for practitioners or mainly adds complexity. The influenza application is also concerning: an ACE interval of (-0.23, 0.64) offers virtually no actionable conclusion—though that may be the most honest takeaway.

Quality
The methodology is technically sound, with many details deferred to the supplement (which I did not verify). The ideas are well motivated, though the broader context is occasionally obscured. Key elements—such as how to choose the relaxation parameters—are postponed to future work, making it hard to anticipate practical pitfalls. The empirical evaluation lacks balance and robustness.

Clarity
Generally well written (the conclusion is an exception; see below). The algorithm and its variations across subtly different parameterizations are difficult to track, partly due to missing explanations of some method components. I think I understand the procedure, but I am not confident I could implement it solely from the main text; the supplement may suffice.

Originality
To my knowledge, the method is a novel synthesis of known techniques. It is closely related to recent efforts on the same problem yet sufficiently distinct. It could help cross-pollinate several lines of work.

Significance
The primary contribution is the conceptual direction and the demonstration of how other tools can be integrated to tackle this challenging problem. In its current form, it likely falls short of a practitioner-ready solution, but elements of WPP may become influential components of future methods.

Summary:
Addresses an important and timely problem. Proposes an interesting method that will likely attract attention. Present practical utility is limited, but the approach may extend to more general settings. Writing is generally good but could be tightened. Technically engaging ideas. Empirical evaluation is weak. The meaning and practical interpretation of the output distribution over bounds are still unclear.
