- Paper addresses estimating the average causal effect (ACE) for binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially unknown.
- Builds on Entner et al. (2013) rule to select covariate subsets that block back-door paths under faithfulness.
- Proposes using Entner et al.'s rule to derive bounds (not point estimates) for ACE, leveraging Ramsahai (2012).
- Core idea is sensible and interesting, but the technical presentation is hard to follow.
- Unclear role of Entner et al.'s conditions in the framework when faithfulness is not assumed.
- Authors do not explain whether verified Entner conditions should guide selection of relaxation parameters and explicitly set that aside.
- Method appears not to rely critically on Entner et al.'s conditions, raising questions about the source of observed robustness.
- Section 3 claim that violations of faithfulness are expressed as bounded violations of local independence is counterintuitive; unfaithfulness implies extra independences.
- Suggestion to include Entner et al.'s original method in experiments using strict thresholds and treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds.
- Page 8: interpretation of a negative ACE as a desirable vaccine outcome seems inconsistent with the definitions of X and Y.
- Page 8 citation mismatch: "Table 1" is referred to as "Table 5" in the text.
- Overall assessment: interesting approach but difficult to follow in technical detail.
