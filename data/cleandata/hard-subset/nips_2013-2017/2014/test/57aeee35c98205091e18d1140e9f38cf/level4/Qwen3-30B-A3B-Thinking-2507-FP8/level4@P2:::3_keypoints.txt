- Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP), a framework that relaxes faithfulness via parameters and uses linear programming to bound the ACE under potential unfaithful interactions.
- Builds on Entner et al.'s witness/instrumental variable ideas and extends them to account for uncertainty about faithfulness violations.
- Technical approach is sound with thorough derivations and a verifiable algorithmic framework; supplementary material is sufficient.
- Parameter selection is underdeveloped and deferred to future work, leaving practical guidance lacking.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: the bias comparison conflates point and interval estimates, making comparisons meaningless.
- Influenza case study yields very wide, practically uninformative bounds and fails to justify why narrower WPP bounds are preferable.
- Writing is generally clear but has abrupt abstraction shifts and unclear relationships between witnesses, admissible sets, and relaxation parameters.
- Key terms like “unfaithful relaxations” lack intuitive explanation; Algorithm 1 does not clearly link witness selection to bound computation.
- Presents a novel combination of existing techniques and a meaningful adaptation of Entner’s Rule 1 to explicitly model faithfulness violations.
- Differs from recent causal-bounds work by incorporating Bayesian learning and symbolic algebraic bounds.
- Conceptual significance is high: provides a principled way to quantify impacts of faithfulness violations and incorporate structure uncertainty into estimation.
- Practical significance is limited by current implementation’s inability to produce actionable bounds in many realistic scenarios.
- Strengths: elegant relaxation-parameter idea, well-motivated Bayesian treatment of uncertainty, and valuable symbolic bounds derivations.
- Weaknesses: inadequate experimental demonstration of advantages, unclear interpretation of bound distributions, and insufficient guidance on choosing relaxation parameters.
- Recommendations: use interval-appropriate metrics (width, coverage), provide parameter-selection guidance, and clarify when WPP outperforms simpler methods.
- Overall: technically strong and conceptually important with potential, recommended for acceptance subject to revisions addressing experimental robustness and practical interpretation.
