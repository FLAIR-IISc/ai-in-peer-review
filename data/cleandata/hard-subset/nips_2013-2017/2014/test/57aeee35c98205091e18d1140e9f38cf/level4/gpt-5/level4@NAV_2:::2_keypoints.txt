- Algorithm not fully optimized, but yields narrow and accurate bounds compared to existing methods.
- Positive contribution: combining the three principles produces tighter ACE bounds and is an interesting advance.
- Main theoretical gap: justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is insufficient and the required structural assumption (Fig. 2) is not explicitly stated or discussed.
- Key practical gap: no general guidance on selecting relaxation parameters, though they critically affect the bounds.
- Experimental description is weak: implementation details and parameter definitions (Table 1/Table 5) are unclear or missing.
- Presentation issues: typographical and cross-referencing errors (missing algorithm table, incorrect table references).
- Originality is limited by prior work on the problem setup, but the unified framework offers some novel value.
- Significance: method shows promise for tighter bounds, but empirical evaluation is limited and theoretical/general applicability is not rigorously assessed.
