- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the average causal effect (ACE) under possible faithfulness violations by treating a witness W as an instrument conditional on Z.  
- Introduces relaxation parameters that bound departures from conditional independences, yielding LPs that produce upper and lower ACE bounds.  
- Presents a Bayesian scheme for posterior distributions over bounds, analytic inequalities for insight and speed, and a back-substitution routine to tighten constraints.  
- Positions the work at the intersection of faithfulness-based causal discovery, Entner et al.'s Rule 1, and IV bounding literature (Balke–Pearl, Dawid, Ramsahai).  
- Conceptual contribution: bridges faithfulness-based identification and instrumental-variable bounds via controlled relaxations.  
- Formal contribution: clear LP formulation with analytic bounds and an efficient back-substitution heuristic.  
- Statistical contribution: Bayesian treatment of sampling uncertainty over bounds rather than point estimates.  
- Practical motivation: addresses near-unfaithfulness and model misspecification concerns in observational studies.  
- Implementation: provides public code and considers computational scalability.  
- Major interpretability issue: outputs are posterior distributions over intervals driven by user-chosen relaxation parameters, with little guidance on selecting them.  
- No default point estimate provided, risking practitioner confusion and misinterpretation of intervals as confidence intervals around a point.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: unclear practical utility of the “bias/error” metric, few datasets, large sample reliance, and broad intervals in the influenza example.  
- Presentation can be dense; key steps and parameter roles need clearer intuition and explicit links between the search and bounding phases.  
- Technically sound and well-motivated, but critical choices (relaxation parameters) are deferred and empirical validation (coverage, sensitivity, small-n behavior) is lacking.  
- Clarity issues: heavy notation and abrupt abstraction shifts; relaxation parameters and their role should be explained earlier.  
- Originality: novel synthesis repurposing Rule 1 for IV-style bounding under controlled faithfulness violations, with new algebraic bounds and a practical algorithm.  
- Significance: promising methodological direction but currently more a framework than a practitioner-ready tool; needs guidance on parameterization and interpretability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled relaxation framework addressing a timely problem, new analytic inequalities, and computationally efficient heuristics.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient guidance on choosing relaxations, weak empirical demonstration of utility and robustness, and clarity/presentation shortcomings.  
- Suggestions: add a concise schematic for W, Z, and relaxations; clarify W’s role; state assumptions explicitly; provide intuition for each relaxation parameter; explain search–θ interaction; stress that posterior is over bounds not ACE; compare coverage/width and sensitivity; quantify back-substitution gains; discuss influenza example specifics; neutralize emotive language.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, technically solid contribution that relaxes faithfulness to bound ACEs via LP, but limited by interpretability, parameter selection guidance, and weak empirical evidence.
