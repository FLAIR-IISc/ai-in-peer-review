This paper proposes a novel approach to derive the bound of the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. The main principles underlying this approach are the use of Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption to introduce the possibility of path-cancellation to some degree, and the introduction of assumptions represented by some inequality constraints. A computational scheme for the ACE bound is formulated using linear programming under these principles. Although the authors have not optimized the algorithm to derive the bounds yet, the resulting bounds appear to be very narrow and accurate in comparison to other approaches in the state of the art.

Quality: 7

The authors have made an effort to establish a novel method to derive more accurate bounds of ACE than the state of the art. They have combined several feasible principles and succeeded in providing tighter bounds. In this regard, the content of the paper is very interesting. However, some criteria used to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained. Particularly, the validity of using Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed. For its use in the inverse direction, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig. 2. This should be clearly explained and discussed.

Additionally, the generic criteria for choosing appropriate parameters for relaxation are not provided. Since these parameters significantly affect the result of the bounds, the principle to determine the parameters should be more thoroughly investigated and included in the work.

Clarity: 6

The explanation of the paper's theoretical part seems basically comprehensive, except for the essential discussion mentioned above. However, the demonstration of the experimental results is not well described. As mentioned earlier, the actual implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained. The definition of the parameters shown in Table 1 (possibly Table 5) is not clearly given.

Furthermore, there are some typos, such as "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" on page 6, where no table of the algorithm is present. Similarly, "Results are summarized in Table 5" on page 8, but Table 5 is not present (possibly referring to Table 1).

Originality: 5

The problem setting of this paper has been addressed by some earlier work, as the authors pointed out. In this regard, the scope of this paper does not have very strong originality. However, the idea of combining the three ideas in a unified shape contains some originality. Unfortunately, the validity of the combination is not very clearly discussed.

Significance: 7

In terms of deriving tighter bounds of ACE, this method shows some significance. However, its performance has been checked by quite limited experiments. Its generality in both theory and wide experiments has not been strictly assessed. The paper addresses an important problem, which is to evaluate the bound of the strength of causal relations. However, the validity of the presented idea should be more thoroughly described in both theory and experiments.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

The authors should address the mentioned issues, particularly providing a clear explanation of the use of Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction and the criteria for choosing relaxation parameters. Additionally, they should correct the typos and provide a more comprehensive explanation of the experimental results and algorithm implementation.
