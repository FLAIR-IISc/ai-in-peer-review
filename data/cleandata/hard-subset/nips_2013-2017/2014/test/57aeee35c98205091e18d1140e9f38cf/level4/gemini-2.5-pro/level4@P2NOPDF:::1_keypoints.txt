- Paper tackles estimating the average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when the covariate causal structure is not fully known.  
- Builds on Entner et al. (2013) rule for selecting covariates to block back-door paths, though that rule is justified under faithfulness.  
- Adapts Entner et al.’s rule to produce bounds (not point estimates) on ACE, relaxing the faithfulness assumption using Ramsahai (2012)’s bounding framework.  
- Reviewer finds the central idea intuitive, interesting, and potentially useful, though they could not verify all technical details.  
- Main concern: unclear role and necessity of Entner et al.’s conditions in the new framework since their original justification depends on faithfulness.  
- Paper appears to set aside how Entner et al. conditions inform the choice of relaxation parameters, leaving ambiguity about their practical importance.  
- Reviewer suspects the method’s core machinery may not critically depend on Entner et al. conditions and asks for clarification to strengthen the contribution.  
- Minor: wording in Section 3 is misleading—should frame as “near-unfaithfulness” or bounded dependence rather than “violations of faithfulness.”  
- Minor: experiments should include a direct comparison to Entner et al.’s method, treating their “don’t know” outputs as uninformative bounds (e.g., [0,1]).  
- Minor: contradiction on page 8 about ACE sign in the vaccine example—interpretation is reversed.  
- Minor: typographical error on page 8 referencing “Table 5” when it seems to mean “Table 1.”  
- Overall: thought-provoking and promising but technical density and the unresolved role of Entner et al. conditions limit clarity and impact.
