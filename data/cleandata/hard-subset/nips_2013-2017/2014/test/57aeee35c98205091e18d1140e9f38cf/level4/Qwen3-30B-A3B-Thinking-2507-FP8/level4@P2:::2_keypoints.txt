- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE by inverting Entner et al.'s Rule 1, using LP and Bayesian inference.  
- Provides a novel middle ground between faithfulness-based point estimates and conservative instrumental-variable bounds.  
- Theoretical foundation and parameterization are solid, with a clear linear programming formulation.  
- Key theoretical gap: insufficient justification for reversing Entner's Rule 1; needs explicit causal-structure assumptions and validity conditions (Figure 2).  
- Selection of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) is underexplained; requires a principled method rather than defaults.  
- Experimental details are insufficient for reproducibility; missing/incorrect table references and several typos.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (synthetic data + one real-world example) and uses small parameter spaces; needs broader robustness checks.  
- Originality is conceptual (combining reversed Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, LP bounds) rather than primarily algorithmic.  
- Should better distinguish from related IV and faithfulness work and clarify when it yields tighter bounds.  
- Potentially significant for epidemiology and social sciences if validated and integrated into existing pipelines.  
- Strengths: novel framework, solid theory, promising tighter bounds, well-motivated handling of faithfulness violations.  
- Weaknesses: inadequate theoretical justification, unclear parameter criteria, limited experiments, and presentation errors.  
- Recommendation: strengthen theoretical arguments, provide principled parameter selection, expand experiments, and fix clarity/typos to improve impact.
