- Introduces WPP, a framework that estimates bounds on ACE while explicitly allowing bounded violations of the faithfulness assumption.  
- Relaxes faithfulness by bounding "path cancellations" and derives posterior distributions over ACE bounds via linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Identifies candidate instrumental variables (witnesses) using a modified Entner et al. Rule 1 and incorporates relaxation parameters to quantify unfaithfulness.  
- Provides analytical bounds in the supplement and a fast back-substitution algorithm for computational efficiency.  

- Strength: Addresses fragility of faithfulness-based methods by explicitly modeling and quantifying unfaithfulness with interpretable relaxation parameters.  
- Strength: Uses Bayesian inference to propagate uncertainty over bounds instead of producing single point estimates.  
- Strength: Offers analytical insights and a computationally efficient algorithm, giving users a trade-off between bound width and assumptions.  
- Strength: Supplementary material is detailed and the influenza vaccination case study shows practical applicability.  

- Weakness: Output interpretability and practical utility are unclear — posterior distributions over bounds are not straightforward for practitioners to act on.  
- Weakness: Paper does not emphasize enough that the posterior is over bounds, not point estimates, risking misinterpretation.  
- Weakness: Evaluation metric (Euclidean distance to true ACE) improperly compares interval estimates to point estimates and can misleadingly favor wide intervals.  
- Weakness: Stability of WPP in unsolvable cases is due to overly wide, uninformative intervals (e.g., [-1,1]) rather than meaningful inference.  
- Weakness: Influenza case study’s narrower WPP bounds are not sufficiently justified as practically meaningful relative to standard IV intervals.  
- Weakness: Lacks analysis of how bound width scales with sample size, limiting understanding of performance on small datasets.  

- Quality & Clarity: Technical approach is sound and well-connected to prior work, but exposition of relaxation parameters is initially opaque and their chosen values are under-motivated.  
- Quality & Clarity: Algorithmic description works but gives little intuitive guidance on selecting relaxation parameters θ, a major practical hurdle.  
- Originality & Significance: Novel reconceptualization of witness identification as faithfulness relaxation that bridges IV bounds and faithfulness-based methods, with high methodological significance.  
- Originality & Significance: Not yet a drop-in replacement due to required user-specified θ and Bayesian computation overhead, but has clear potential to influence robust causal tools.  

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify interpretation of bounds, justify parameter choices, and improve evaluation and small-sample analysis.  

- Key action items for authors: clarify that bounds are conservative ranges (not confidence intervals), replace the Euclidean error metric with interval-focused metrics, discuss pragmatic default θ values, and report how bounds scale with dataset size.
