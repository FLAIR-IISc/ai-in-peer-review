- Addresses estimating average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially unspecified, using Entner et al.'s rule to select covariates and producing bounds instead of point estimates.  
- Proposes a bounding method following Ramsahai (2012) to relax reliance on faithfulness for ACE estimation.  
- Reviewer finds the main idea sensible and potentially useful but has not fully digested technical details.  
- Major concern: role of Entner et al.'s rule without the faithfulness assumption is unclear—its conditions have no obvious causal implications absent faithfulness.  
- Reviewer suspects the rule’s conditions might inform choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper does not address this and seems the method might not depend crucially on those conditions.  
- Requests clarification on whether the method’s apparent empirical robustness relates to the use (or non-use) of Entner et al.'s conditions.  
- Minor comments: (1) phrasing about “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading—should frame as allowing/quantifying near-unfaithfulness.  
- Minor comments: (2) experiments should report Entner et al.’s method performance with stringent thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds.  
- Minor comments: (3) clarify the example where X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized—why a negative ACE implies a desirable vaccine.  
- Minor comments: (4) citation error: “Table 1” is referred to as “Table 5” in the text.  
- Overall verdict: interesting paper but details are hard to follow and key conceptual links need clarification.
