- Proposes bounding the average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables using Entner et al.'s covariate selection rule combined with Ramsahai-style ACE bounds.  
- Major concern: Entner et al.'s rule relies on faithfulness, but the paper explicitly discards faithfulness, leaving the rule’s implications unclear.  
- Unclear whether verified Entner conditions inform the choice of relaxation parameters or are merely incidental to the method.  
- Experimental robustness may depend on the above connection; the paper lacks an explicit discussion of how Entner conditions interact with the relaxation framework.  
- Minor wording issue: describing unfaithfulness as "violations of faithfulness" or "violations of local independence" is misleading; unfaithfulness involves extra independences and should be framed as near-faithfulness (bounded dependence).  
- Experimental suggestion: include Entner et al.’s method performance under stringent thresholds, treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds (e.g., [−1, 1]).  
- Clarify causal interpretation in examples: a negative ACE (E[Y|X=1] − E[Y|X=0]) indicates reduced hospitalization after vaccination, so wording about it “suggesting a desirable vaccine” is confusing.  
- Correction: Table 1 is cited as "Table 5" on p. 8—please fix the reference.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially useful approach, but the presentation is dense and the paper must better justify and clarify the role of Entner et al.’s conditions within the relaxation framework.
