- Paper addresses estimation of the average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is only partially known.  
- Revisits Entner et al. (2013) rule for selecting covariates that block back-door paths, but uses it to produce bounds (not point estimates).  
- Builds on Ramsahai (2012) to develop a bounding method.  
- Reviewer finds the main idea sound, interesting, and useful, but has not verified all technical details.  
- Puzzlement about the role of Entner et al.’s conditions: without faithfulness their implications for causal structure are unclear.  
- Suspects Entner conditions might inform choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper sets that choice aside, making the conditions’ importance unclear.  
- Requests clarification on whether the method truly relies on Entner et al.’s conditions or is robust without them.  
- Minor wording issue: “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading; framework appears to allow “near violations” by constraining dependence strength.  
- Experimental suggestion: report Entner et al.’s original method performance using their stringent thresholds and treat “don’t know” as vacuous bounds.  
- Question on interpretation: if X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized, why would a negative ACE indicate a desirable vaccine?  
- Typo/inconsistency: table labeled “Table 1” on p.8 is cited in text as “Table 5.”  
- Overall verdict: interesting paper but technical development is hard to follow and needs clarification.
