- Introduces WPP algorithm to bound average causal effect (ACE) allowing controlled violations of faithfulness via relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Timely and interesting problem with a principled mathematical approach to quantify weak unfaithful interactions.
- Novel combination of familiar techniques, sufficiently different from recent related work.
- Technical approach is sound, with many details in the supplementary material.
- Output is a distribution over bounds as a function of relaxations that is conceptually hard to interpret.
- Likelihood and practical relevance of the considered unfaithful instances are unclear.
- Many relaxations produce very wide bounds, limiting usefulness.
- Method provides no preferred point estimate within the interval, risking misinterpretation by users.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced.
- The 'bias' metric used for comparison is meaningless because competitors measure different quantities.
- Practical performance on the influenza dataset yields overly wide bounds (e.g., -0.23 to 0.64), offering limited conclusions.
- Choosing relaxation parameters is deferred to future work, making assessment difficult.
- Some algorithmic steps and parameter variants are hard to follow; full implementation from the paper may be infeasible without supplements.
- Important implementation/context details (e.g., selection of relaxation parameters) are omitted or deferred.
- The paper’s significance lies in ideas and direction rather than immediate practical utility.
- Claim of no prior analytical bounds overlooks closely related recent work (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI2014).
- The back-substitution process’s practical effectiveness in tightening bounds is doubtful.
- Many derived bounds are so loose they don't indicate whether the causal effect is identifiable.
- Claim that "WPP is quite stable" is misleading.
- Risk that readers will misinterpret WPP bounds as confidence intervals around a best estimate.
- Conclusion language is emotive and potentially biased (e.g., "risky Bayesian approaches," "keeps inference more honest").
- WPP addresses an additional aspect of inference rather than serving as a compromise between methods.
- Several specific clarity issues: misuse of "unstable," unclear cartoon representation, and abrupt abstraction shift in section 4.
- The role of witness W in identifying set Z and dependence of admissible Z on relaxation parameters theta need clearer explanation.
- Phrases like "gives greater control over violations of faithfulness" are too vague.
- The phrase "max. 10 free parameters" is misleading—refers to entire set Z, not 10 variables.
- A minor issue: typo in reference 9 ("inuenza").
