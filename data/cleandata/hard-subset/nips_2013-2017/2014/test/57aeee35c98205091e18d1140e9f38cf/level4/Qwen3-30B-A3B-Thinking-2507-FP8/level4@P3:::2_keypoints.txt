- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE by relaxing faithfulness and treating witnesses as instrumental variables via an inverse of Entner et al.’s Rule 1.  
- Models bounded violations of local independence (path cancellations) and derives posterior ACE bounds using linear programming and Bayesian sampling.  
- Provides symbolic algebraic bounds in terms of relaxation parameters, extending point-estimation approaches to uncertainty-aware intervals.  
- Technical approach is sound and well-motivated; bounds are at least as conservative as Entner’s estimator.  
- Lacks sufficient justification for the inverse application of Rule 1 and should state explicit causal-graph assumptions underlying that inversion.  
- No principled method for choosing relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄); current ad hoc defaults (e.g., 0.2) undermine reliability.  
- Linear programming and Bayesian posterior sampling are rigorously derived and appropriate for the problem.  
- Exposition has minor inconsistencies and errors (incorrect references to tables/algorithms, typos) and relies heavily on supplementary material for key details.  
- Experimental description omits important implementation details (how brute-force search for Z scales, why GRP excluded in influenza study).  
- The combination of inverse Rule 1, bounded path cancellations, LP bounds, and Bayesian sampling is original relative to Entner et al. and Ramsahai.  
- Paper should better contrast with related bounding work (e.g., Geiger et al., Manski) to clarify novelty.  
- Empirical results show WPP yields tighter, more stable bounds than naive adjustment and Entner’s estimator, and the influenza case demonstrates practical utility.  
- Experimental scope is limited (synthetic with |W|=8 and one real dataset); broader validation on high-dimensional and varied confounding structures is needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on clarifying causal assumptions, providing guidance for parameter selection, and expanding experimental validation.
