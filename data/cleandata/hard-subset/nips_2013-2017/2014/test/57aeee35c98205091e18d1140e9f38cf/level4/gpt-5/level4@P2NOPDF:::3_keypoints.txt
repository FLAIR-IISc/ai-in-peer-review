- Introduces WPP: an LP-based algorithm to bound ACE under relaxed faithfulness; timely and principled.  
- Method leverages optimization tools and is technically sound, though many derivations are in the supplement.  
- The chosen parameterization may not capture all meaningful departures from faithfulness.  
- The output is a distribution over bounds tied to relaxation choices, but there is no way to assess plausibility of different unfaithful scenarios.  
- Many relaxation settings produce very wide intervals, which limits practical utility.  
- The procedure provides intervals but no point estimate, risking misinterpretation of bounds as a “best guess.”  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and not robust; comparisons using a “bias” metric are unfair for interval vs point-estimate methods.  
- The influenza data result (ACE ∈ (−0.23, 0.64)) is largely uninformative for actionable conclusions.  
- Selection and calibration of the relaxation parameters are not addressed and are deferred to future work.  
- Broader context and practical guidance are sometimes lost despite good core motivation.  
- The algorithm and its many parameter variants are hard to track from the main text; the supplement appears necessary for implementation.  
- The paper combines familiar ingredients in a novel way and could stimulate further research.  
- Contribution is primarily conceptual and directional; not yet a finished tool for practitioners.  
- Doubts about the practical efficacy of back-substitution for tightening bounds; needs empirical evidence on its benefit.  
- Multiple admissible witnesses can produce multiple bounds; clarify how this affects comparisons and interpretation.  
- Emphasize that bounds are not confidence intervals and clarify their implications for decision-making.  
- Numerous specific clarity, notation, and wording issues and typos throughout the manuscript that need fixing.  
- Recommend summarizing key proofs/derivations in the main text because readers may not verify the supplement.  
- Discuss how bounds behave with smaller samples (e.g., N ≈ 500) and whether claimed stability reflects overly loose bounds.  
- Table 1 does not demonstrate WPP’s benefit over standard point estimation under faithfulness.  
- Avoid emotive or biased phrasing (e.g., “risky Bayesian approaches,” “keeps inference more honest”) and misleading terms like “providing a compromise.”  
- Remove accidental repeated summary at the end and fix reference typos (e.g., “influenza”).
