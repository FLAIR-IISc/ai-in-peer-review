- Paper introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to estimate bounds on ACE by reversing Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to generate candidate instruments while allowing bounded violations of faithfulness.  
- Main contributions: (1) reinterpretation of Entner's Rule 1 for candidate IVs, (2) relaxation of faithfulness to permit partial path cancellations, (3) linear-programming + Bayesian framework for posterior bounds on ACE.  
- Theoretical approach is sound and technically correct; LP formulation and Bayesian treatment properly account for estimation uncertainty.  
- Relaxation of faithfulness via bounded violations is well-motivated and provides a middle ground between point estimates and IV bounds.  
- Analytical bounds (Section 4) give useful insight into how relaxation parameters influence ACE bounds.  
- Weak justification for applying Entner's Rule 1 in reverse without explicitly assuming the causal graph (Figure 1(c)); authors should state underlying causal-structure assumptions.  
- Selection criteria for relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) are underdeveloped and treated as arbitrary hyperparameters; need principled guidance.  
- Experimental validation is limited (one real dataset: influenza vaccination); more diverse simulations and real-data tests are needed to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Paper does not clearly distinguish its approach from prior work (Entner et al., Ramsahai); needs a more detailed comparison highlighting differences.  
- Clarity issues and typos: misreferenced tables/algorithms (Table 1 vs Algorithm 1, missing Table 5) and insufficient explanation of parameters where first introduced.  
- Claim that WPP should become “standard practice” is premature given limited empirical validation and unresolved assumptions.  
- Overall promising and original extension to causal bounding, but requires stronger theoretical justification, principled parameter selection, clearer presentation, and broader experiments.
