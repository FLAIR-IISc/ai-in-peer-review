- Proposes a new LP-based method for bounding ACE by (i) applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse, (ii) relaxing faithfulness to allow controlled path cancellation, and (iii) adding inequality constraints.  
- Reported intervals are notably tight and competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Insufficient justification for using Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse; needs explicit statement, necessity, and implications of the required structural assumptions (e.g., Fig. 2).  
- No general criteria or principled guidance for choosing relaxation parameters that control path cancellation and constraint strength.  
- Theoretical exposition is mostly clear, but experimental and implementation details are under-specified and impede replication.  
- Mapping from assumptions to linear constraints and handling of parameter relaxations are not described with sufficient precision.  
- Definitions and roles of parameters listed in the table(s) are unclear, and table references appear inconsistent or missing.  
- Manuscript contains inconsistencies/typos (missing algorithm/table references) and requires careful proofreading and consistent cross-referencing.  
- Originality: combining the three ideas has merit, but novelty is weakened by lack of rigorous justification for the combination and parameter selection.  
- Significance: promising potential for tighter ACE bounds, but limited empirical evaluation and unclear generality; stronger theoretical guarantees and broader experiments are needed.  
- Recommended fixes for publication: justify reverse Entner’s Rule 1 and structural assumptions; provide principled guidance/sensitivity analysis for relaxation parameters; clarify algorithmic/implementation details for replication; correct table references and improve experimental reporting.
