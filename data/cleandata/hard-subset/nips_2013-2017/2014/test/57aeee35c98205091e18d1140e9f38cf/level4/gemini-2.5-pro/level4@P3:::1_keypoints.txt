- Addresses estimating average causal effect (ACE) with unobserved confounders by blending faithfulness-based identification and IV-style bounding.  
- Proposes the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP): repurpose Entner et al.'s witness W and covariates Z, relax strict conditional independencies into bounded constraints, and compute ACE bounds via linear programming.  
- Introduces user-controlled relaxation parameters to quantify allowed dependence and derive posterior distributions over ACE bounds.  
- Adds a Bayesian treatment for finite-sample uncertainty.  
- Develops a fast back-substitution algebraic approximation for scalability.  
- Strength: novel and practically relevant idea to navigate the trade-off between strong assumptions and uninformative bounds.  
- Strength: technically sound, building on established LP bounding frameworks and extending them with Bayesian and computational contributions.  
- Strength: empirical results on synthetic data and an influenza vaccine dataset show tighter, more robust bounds than naive or strict-faithfulness methods.  
- Main weakness: unclear justification for using Entner et al.'s rule as the initial step once its conditional-independence assumptions are relaxed.  
- Key question: does satisfying the rule imply one can use tighter relaxation parameters (and thus tighter bounds), and if so, this link needs clearer explanation.  
- Experimental gap: missing direct comparison to the original Entner et al. (2013) method (treating "I don't know" as interval [-1,1]) as a baseline.  
- Minor: wording about "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is confusing; better to frame as handling near-independencies or small path coefficients.  
- Minor: typo/confusion in influenza study was noted but resolved by reviewer.  
- Minor: relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) need clearer intuition linking them to graph structure.  
- Minor: verify correctness of table and figure citations (possible mismatches mentioned).
