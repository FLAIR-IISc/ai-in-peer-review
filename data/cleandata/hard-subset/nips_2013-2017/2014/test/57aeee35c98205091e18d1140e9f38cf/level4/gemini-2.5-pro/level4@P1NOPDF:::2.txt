This paper proposes a new approach for deriving bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) between a variable X and a variable Y. The proposed method is based on three main principles: 1) applying Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, 2) relaxing the faithfulness assumption to allow for some degree of path cancellation, and 3) introducing new assumptions in the form of inequality constraints. A computational scheme for the ACE bounds is formulated as a linear program based on these principles. While the authors have not yet optimized the algorithm for deriving these bounds, the resulting bounds appear to be very narrow and accurate compared to other state-of-the-art approaches.

### Quality

The authors aim to establish a novel method for deriving more accurate ACE bounds than existing state-of-the-art methods. They successfully combine several plausible principles to provide tighter bounds. In this regard, the paper's contribution is very interesting.

However, some of the criteria used to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained. Specifically, the validity of applying Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed. To justify its use in the inverse direction, the authors must explicitly assume the structure depicted in Figure 2. This assumption needs to be explicitly stated and its implications thoroughly discussed.

Furthermore, the paper does not provide generic criteria for choosing appropriate relaxation parameters. Given that these parameters significantly affect the resulting bounds, the principles for their selection should be investigated more thoroughly and included in the paper.

### Clarity

The theoretical exposition of the paper is generally comprehensive, with the exception of the crucial discussion I noted above.

However, the presentation of the experimental results is not well-described. As noted previously, the implementation details of the algorithm are not sufficiently explained. The definitions of the parameters shown in Table 1 are not clearly provided.

In addition, I noticed several typos and inconsistencies. For example:
*   On page 6, the text states, "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1," but there is no such table describing an algorithm.
*   On page 8, the text refers to "Table 5," which does not exist (perhaps this should be Table 1).

### Originality

As the authors acknowledge, the problem setting has been addressed in prior work. In this regard, the problem itself is not novel.

However, the idea of combining these three principles into a unified framework does demonstrate originality. But, as mentioned previously, the validity of this combination is not clearly discussed.

### Significance

The method shows potential significance in its ability to derive tighter ACE bounds. However, its performance has only been checked in a limited set of experiments. Its generality, both theoretically and across a wider range of experiments, has not been rigorously assessed.

The paper addresses the important problem of estimating bounds on the strength of causal relationships. However, the validity of the proposed approach needs to be more thoroughly established, both theoretically and experimentally.
