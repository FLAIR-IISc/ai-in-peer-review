- Proposes a novel method for tight ACE bounds using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing path cancellation), inequality constraints, and an LP formulation.  
- Implementation is unoptimized, but reported bounds are significantly tighter and more accurate than state-of-the-art.  
- Core theoretical contribution is interesting but undermined by lack of explicit justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse.  
- The causal structure in Figure 2 must be explicitly stated and validated; without this the method lacks theoretical grounding.  
- No principled criteria are provided for choosing relaxation parameters (e.g., degree of faithfulness relaxation), leaving parameter selection ad hoc.  
- Insufficient discussion of the inverse use of Entner’s Rule and parameter selection creates ambiguity in the theory.  
- Experimental description is unclear: algorithm implementation details are missing and parameter definitions are not provided.  
- Table references and labels are inconsistent (e.g., “Table 1” vs “Table 5”), and typographical errors hurt readability and reproducibility.  
- Originality is incremental—method unifies known ideas but lacks rigorous justification for that unification, especially regarding Entner’s Rule.  
- Empirical validation is limited to a small set of experiments and does not assess theoretical generality or performance across diverse causal structures.  
- Recommendation: reject pending major revisions to strengthen Entner’s Rule justification, formalize parameter selection, and clarify experiments, algorithm, and tables.
