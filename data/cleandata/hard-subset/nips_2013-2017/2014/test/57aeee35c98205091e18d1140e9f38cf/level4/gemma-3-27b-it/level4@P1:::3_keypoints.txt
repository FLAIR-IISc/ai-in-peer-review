- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute ACE bounds allowing controlled violations of faithfulness.  
- Uses relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and linear programming to compute upper/lower ACE bounds adjusting for covariates.  
- Timely and interesting problem; a principled attempt to quantify the impact of weak unfaithful interactions.  
- Novel combination of established techniques that could stimulate further cross-fertilization.  
- Practical usefulness is uncertain: output is a distribution over bounds that is conceptually challenging to interpret.  
- Bounds can be wide, reducing actionable value, and the method does not provide a preferred point estimate.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited, unbalanced, and uses an inappropriate bias metric for comparisons.  
- Real-data performance is weak (e.g., influenza ACE interval ≈ (−0.23, 0.64)), offering limited actionable insight.  
- Method is technically sound and many details are in the supplement, but key choices (e.g., relaxation parameters) are deferred to future work.  
- Algorithmic steps and the many parameters are hard to follow; may be difficult to implement from the paper alone.  
- Explanations lack intuition in places; transitions in abstraction are sudden and some constraint meanings are unclear.  
- Need clarification on how witness selection and admissible sets Z depend on relaxation parameters.  
- Doubts about practical efficacy of the back-substitution step for tightening bounds; empirical evidence of effectiveness is missing.  
- Claim of “no previous analytical bounds” is questionable given related prior work (e.g., UAI2014).  
- Concern about sensitivity to sample size; bounds may deteriorate substantially with small datasets common in practice.  
- Table 1 and stability claims do not convincingly demonstrate WPP’s practical advantage over standard point estimates.  
- Important interpretational risk: readers may treat bounds as confidence intervals or point-estimates unless clarified.  
- Tone and wording include emotive/dismissive language and unclear sentences that should be revised.  
- Numerous minor issues flagged (typos, phrasing, unclear sentences, and specific line comments throughout the paper).  
- Overall: interesting and significant direction with promising ideas, but not yet practitioner-ready—needs better experiments, interpretation guidance, and clarity.
