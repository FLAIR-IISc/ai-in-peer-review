- Problem importance: Estimating ACE under partial knowledge of causal structure is important and bounds can be more defensible than point estimates.
- Core contribution: Proposes using Entner et al.’s selection rule to generate constraints and apply Ramsahai-style bounding to obtain partial identification instead of point estimates.
- Strength: Turns a brittle faithfulness-dependent rule into a tool for partial identification, potentially improving robustness.
- Strength: Empirical results suggest the method yields informative bounds in realistic settings where strict assumptions fail.
- Concern: The logical link between Entner et al.’s conditions and the causal structure once faithfulness is relaxed is unclear—are the conditions essential or just heuristic?
- Clarification needed: Are Entner conditions used only to propose candidate adjustment sets, with bounds computed regardless of causal validity?
- Clarification needed: Do observed independences inform selection of relaxation parameters in a principled way, or is parameter choice left ad hoc?
- Ablation request: Show how results change if Entner’s rule is replaced by other structure-lean procedures or by using all small subsets.
- Guarantee question: Are the derived bounds valid regardless of whether the Entner-selected set is truly sufficient, given the relaxation constraints, and when might bounds be vacuous?
- Robustness interpretation: The apparent robustness may be due to the bounding machinery rather than the Entner step—clarify which part drives performance.
- Experimental requests: Report results with and without the Entner selection step and demonstrate sensitivity to relaxation parameters and CI-test thresholds.
- Experimental requests: Include cases where Entner’s conditions fail or return “don’t know,” and show corresponding bounds.
- Clarity: Provide a brief, self-contained overview of how Ramsahai (2012) is instantiated here (variables, constraints, objective, guarantees).
- Clarity: Explicitly map each testable independence to its relaxation parameter and corresponding constraint in the bounding program.
- Writing/style suggestions: Rephrase “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” to correctly describe allowing bounded departures from expected conditional independences.
- Additional suggestions: Include Entner et al. as a conservative baseline treating “don’t know” as uninformative, fix the ACE sign convention in the vaccine example, correct table numbering, and provide practical guidance or sensitivity plots for choosing relaxation parameters.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and timely direction with promising empirical results, but needs clearer account of Entner’s role, formal guarantees, sensitivity analyses, baselines/ablations, and presentation improvements.
