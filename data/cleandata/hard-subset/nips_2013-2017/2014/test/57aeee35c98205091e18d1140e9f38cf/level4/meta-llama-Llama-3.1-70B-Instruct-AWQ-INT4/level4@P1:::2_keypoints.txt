- Proposes a novel method to bound average causal effect (ACE) using the inverse of Entner's Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing path-cancellation), inequality constraints, and linear programming.  
- Reported bounds are notably narrower and more accurate than existing state-of-the-art approaches.  
- The validity of applying Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not sufficiently justified and requires explicit structural assumptions (e.g., Fig.2).  
- The paper does not provide clear, generic criteria for choosing relaxation parameters, though these strongly influence the bounds.  
- Theoretical exposition is mostly comprehensive but missing the essential discussion on the inverse Entner’s Rule 1 usage.  
- Experimental description and implementation details are insufficiently explained, including unclear definitions of parameters in Table 1.  
- The manuscript contains typos and incorrect table references (e.g., algorithm/table numbering errors).  
- Originality is limited in problem setting but the unified combination of the three ideas (Entner’s Rule 1 inverse, faithfulness relaxation, inequality constraints) shows some novelty.  
- Significance is promising because of tighter ACE bounds, but empirical validation is limited and generality is not rigorously assessed.  
- Pros: tighter ACE bounds and a novel combination of principles.  
- Cons: unclear justification for key assumptions, missing parameter-selection principles, weak experimental/implementation reporting, and insufficient generality assessment.
