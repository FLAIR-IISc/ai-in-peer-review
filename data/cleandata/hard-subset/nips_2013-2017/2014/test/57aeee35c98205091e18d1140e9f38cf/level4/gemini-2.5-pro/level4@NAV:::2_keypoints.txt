- Paper proposes a novel LP-based method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using inverse Entner's Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and new inequality constraints.  
- Algorithm not optimized but yields remarkably tight and precise ACE bounds compared to state-of-the-art in initial experiments.  
- Major positive: successful synthesis of three plausible principles leads to compelling tighter bounds.  
- Major concern: justification for applying Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is insufficiently explained.  
- Related concern: inverse application appears to require assuming the specific graphical structure in Figure 2, which is not explicitly stated or defended.  
- Key missing element: no general, principled criteria for selecting relaxation parameters that heavily influence final bounds.  
- Theoretical exposition is largely clear aside from the crucial omissions noted above.  
- Experimental presentation is weak: implementation details are incomplete and parameters in result tables (e.g., Table 1) are not defined.  
- Manuscript contains typographical and reference errors (e.g., incorrect references to Table 1 on p.6 and Table 5 on p.8).  
- Originality: problem setting is established, but the combined framework is novel; however, its theoretical soundness needs stronger justification.  
- Significance: method shows promise due to tighter bounds but has limited empirical validation, so generalizability and impact are not yet established.  
- Recommendation: strengthen theoretical arguments, provide principled parameter selection, fix presentation errors, and expand experiments to substantiate claims.
