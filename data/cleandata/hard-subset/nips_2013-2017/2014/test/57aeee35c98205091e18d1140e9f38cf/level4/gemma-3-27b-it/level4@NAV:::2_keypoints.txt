- Introduces a novel method to bound ACE using reversed Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints solved by linear programming.
- Method yields demonstrably tighter and more accurate ACE bounds than state-of-the-art, though algorithmic optimization is left for future work.
- Promising and valuable contribution for more precise ACE bounds.
- Insufficient justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse; structural conditions (Figure 2) need explicit statement and discussion.
- Lack of guidance on selecting relaxation parameters despite their significant impact on bounds.
- Theoretical validity of combining reverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints requires more robust discussion.
- Experimental presentation is weak: algorithmic implementation is under-described and parameters in tables lack clear definitions.
- Table references are inconsistent/mislabeled (e.g., "Table 1" and "Table 5" errors), and there are minor typographical mistakes.
- Evaluation is limited in scope; broader empirical validation and rigorous assessment of generality are needed.
- Overall significant potential but requires stronger theoretical justification, clearer experimental details, and expanded experiments.
