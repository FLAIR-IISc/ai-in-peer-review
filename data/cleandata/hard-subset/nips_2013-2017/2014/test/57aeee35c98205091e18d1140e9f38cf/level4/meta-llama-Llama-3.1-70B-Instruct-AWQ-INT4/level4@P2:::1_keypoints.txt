- Paper addresses estimating average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially unknown.  
- Uses Entner et al.'s (2013) rule to select covariates but proposes bounding ACE instead of point estimation, following Ramsahai (2012).  
- Main idea: invert Entner's rule so pairs (W,Z) act as instruments to provide ACE bounds rather than back-door adjustments.  
- Approach is interesting and useful because it enables ACE estimation without strong assumptions about the causal graph.  
- Concern: without the faithfulness assumption, the conditions from Entner et al.'s rule lack clear causal implications and may not be crucial to the method.  
- Requests clarification on whether the method’s apparent experimental robustness is related to reliance (or non-reliance) on Entner et al.'s conditions.  
- Minor comment 1: wording in Section 3 is misleading—unfaithfulness relates to extra independences; the framework should be described as allowing "almost violations" of faithfulness.  
- Minor comment 2: experiments should report Entner et al.'s method performance using their stringent thresholds, treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds.  
- Minor comment 3: typo/logic error on p.8 regarding X/Y labeling (vaccine effect and sign of ACE) needs correction.  
- Minor comment 4: citation typo—"Table 1" cited as "Table 5" on top of p.8.  
- Overall assessment: interesting paper but hard to follow in places; asks for clarifications and minor textual corrections.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
