The paper presents the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm for bounding the average causal effect (ACE) while allowing controlled violations of the faithfulness assumption. It introduces relaxation parameters to constrain such violations and uses linear programming to compute upper and lower bounds on the ACE for a target link, after adjusting for appropriate covariates under these constraints.

This is a timely and interesting contribution to an active area. I like the principled attempt to quantify the impact of weak faithfulness violations and to solve the resulting optimization problems with standard tools. Whether this is the right way to capture that impact remains unclear. The output is hard to interpret conceptually: a distribution over bounds as a function of the “unfaithful relaxations” relative to a given model. How likely those unfaithful instances are—and thus how relevant or realistic the bounds are—remains unspecified. Many relaxations yield very wide bounds that may be of limited practical value. The method does not commit to any preferred point estimate within the interval, though users may be tempted to interpret it that way.

The experimental evaluation is weak. The “bias” metric is inappropriate for comparing WPP with competitors because they measure fundamentally different quantities; the comparison is therefore not meaningful. It is unclear whether the WPP output is practically useful or a distraction. Performance on the influenza dataset is also concerning: an ACE in (−0.23, 0.64) supports almost no actionable conclusion—although, admittedly, that may be the most honest assessment.

Quality
Technically sound, with many details in the supplement (which I did not verify in depth). The ideas are well introduced, though the broader context is sometimes lost. Crucial issues, such as how to choose relaxation parameters, are deferred to future work, making it difficult to anticipate practical pitfalls. The experimental evaluation is neither balanced nor robust.

Clarity
Generally well written (except the conclusion; see below). The algorithm and its many variants defined by subtly different parameters are hard to follow, partly due to missing explanations of some steps. I believe I understand the method, but I am not confident I could implement it from the main text alone; the supplement may be sufficient.

Originality
As far as I can tell, the method is a novel combination of familiar techniques. It is closely related to, yet distinct from, recent work on the same problem and may spur cross-fertilization among approaches.

Significance
The primary contribution is conceptual: showing how to incorporate additional techniques to address a challenging problem. In its current form, it seems unlikely to be a practitioner-ready solution, though some WPP ideas could become influential components of future methods.

Summary
A relevant, topical problem. An interesting, technically solid method likely to attract attention. Current practical utility appears limited, but the approach seems extensible. Reasonably well written, with room for improvement. Experimental evaluation is poor. The relevance and interpretation of the output distribution over bounds remain unclear.
