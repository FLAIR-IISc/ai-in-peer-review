This paper introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm, a method for computing bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) that explicitly accounts for potential violations of the faithfulness assumption in causal inference. The approach utilizes relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and employs linear programming to derive upper and lower bounds on the ACE, adjusted for appropriate covariates. 

The problem addressed is timely and important, and the core idea of quantifying the impact of unfaithfulness is appealing. The paper represents a principled attempt to combine causal inference with established mathematical optimization techniques. However, the practical interpretability of the results remains a significant concern. The output is a distribution of bounds dependent on the chosen ‘unfaithful relaxations,’ and the likelihood of these unfaithful scenarios—and therefore the relevance of the derived bounds—is not adequately addressed. Many relaxation settings yield extremely wide bounds, diminishing their practical utility. Critically, the method refrains from suggesting a specific point estimate, despite the potential for users to misinterpret the bounds as a confidence interval. 

The experimental evaluation is weak. The “bias” metric used for comparison with competing methods is fundamentally flawed, as it measures different quantities. This makes it difficult to ascertain whether WPP provides a valuable contribution or merely adds complexity. Performance on the influenza dataset is particularly concerning, yielding a wide ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64) that offers little actionable insight, although this may be a fair reflection of the data.

**Quality:** The technical foundation of the approach appears sound, with details elaborated in the supplementary material (which I have not fully vetted). The ideas are well-introduced, though the broader context is occasionally lost. Crucially, the methodology for selecting relaxation parameters – a critical aspect of the method – is deferred to future work, hindering a full assessment of potential issues. The experimental evaluation lacks robustness and balance.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, though the conclusion requires refinement (see detailed comments below). The algorithmic steps and parameter adjustments were challenging to follow due to insufficient explanation of certain methodological details. While I believe I grasp the overall process, complete implementation solely based on the presented description would be difficult; the supplementary material may provide necessary clarification.

**Originality:** The method presents a novel combination of established techniques. It builds upon recent work in the field but offers a sufficiently distinct approach that could foster cross-pollination of ideas.

**Significance:** The paper’s significance lies primarily in its conceptual contribution – demonstrating how to integrate different techniques to address this challenging problem. In its current form, it is unlikely to become a standard tool for practitioners, although the ideas underlying WPP could prove influential.

**Summary:** This paper tackles a relevant and topical problem with an interesting method that will likely attract attention. While its current practical application is limited, the approach has the potential to extend to more general cases. The writing is reasonably clear, but could be improved, and the technical solutions are noteworthy. However, the experimental evaluation is inadequate, and the relevance and interpretation of the output distribution over bounds remain unclear.
