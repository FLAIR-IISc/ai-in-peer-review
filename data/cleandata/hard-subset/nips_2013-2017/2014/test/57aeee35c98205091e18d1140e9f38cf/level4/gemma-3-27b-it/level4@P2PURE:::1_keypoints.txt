- Addresses ACE estimation for binary treatment/outcome with partially unknown covariate causal structure, building on Entner et al.’s covariate selection rule and using faithfulness to derive ACE bounds via a Ramsahai-inspired method.  
- Reviewer finds the core idea sound, interesting, and potentially valuable, though technical details were not fully verified.  
- Unclear how Entner et al.’s conditions function here without faithfulness; they don’t imply causal structure under weaker assumptions, and the paper doesn’t explain their role in choosing relaxation parameters.  
- The proposed machinery doesn’t seem critically dependent on Entner et al.’s conditions, and it’s unclear if experimental robustness is related to those conditions.  
- Minor: wording in Sec. 3 about “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is awkward; framework should be framed as bounding near-violations of faithfulness (extra independences).  
- Minor: experiments should include Entner et al.’s original method with stricter thresholds, treating “don’t know” cases as uninformative bounds for comparison.  
- Minor: correction — with X=1 = flu shot and Y=1 = not hospitalized, a negative ACE implies the vaccine is not effective, not desirable.  
- Minor: fix table reference inconsistency (Table 1 referenced as “Table 5”).  
- Overall: interesting paper but technical presentation is challenging to follow.
