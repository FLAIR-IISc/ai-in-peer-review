- Introduces WPP: an LP-based method to bound ACE while allowing controlled violations of faithfulness via relaxation parameters.
- Timely, broadly relevant idea that principledly quantifies weak unfaithfulness using optimization tools.
- Novel combination of familiar techniques; sufficiently distinct from recent related work and could spur cross-fertilization.
- Technically sound in presentation, with detailed supplement (not fully verified by reviewer).
- Major interpretability concern: outputs are a distribution over bounds given relaxations, but the plausibility/likelihood of those unfaithful instances is not addressed.
- Many relaxation choices produce very wide, practically uninformative intervals, and the method does not provide a preferred point estimate.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced; the “bias” metric is not meaningful for comparing WPP to competitors.
- Empirical result on influenza data yields a very wide ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64), offering little actionable guidance despite being honest.
- Key practical piece—how to choose relaxation parameters—is deferred and under-explained, making assessment of pitfalls difficult.
- Algorithmic details and many subtly different parameters are hard to follow; main text may be insufficient for implementation without supplement.
- Doubts about effectiveness of back-substitution step for tightening bounds; empirical evidence of its utility is lacking.
- Comparison to prior analytical bounds literature seems incomplete (missing some related work).
- Claims about stability and benefits of WPP can be misleading: very loose bounds may appear “stable” but provide limited insight.
- Some writing and clarity issues (conclusion, abrupt abstraction jumps, vague phrasing) plus numerous minor typos and wording fixes suggested.
