- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute ACE bounds under potential violations of faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.  
- Novel, principled use of relaxation constraints to quantify weak unfaithful interactions with optimization tools.  
- Timely and well-motivated problem with a technically sound approach and sufficient supplementary detail.  
- Interpretability is problematic: the distribution over bounds lacks grounding on the likelihood or realism of unfaithful scenarios.  
- Many relaxation choices produce excessively wide, practically useless bounds.  
- Method does not provide a preferred point estimate and risks practitioners misinterpreting bounds as confidence intervals.  
- Experimental evaluation is inadequate and unbalanced, weakening claims of practical utility.  
- The proposed "bias" metric is inappropriate for comparing WPP to competitors because it measures different quantities.  
- Application to the influenza dataset yields an ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64) that offers little actionable insight.  
- Critical aspects like selection of relaxation parameters are deferred to future work, hindering assessment of pitfalls.  
- Writing is generally clear, but the conclusion and explanations of key methodological details (e.g., role of θ, admissible sets Z) need more intuition.  
- The method is an original combination of established techniques and is related to recent work, potentially fostering cross-fertilization.  
- Significance is mainly conceptual; current implementation is unlikely to be immediately useful for practitioners.  
- Back-substitution efficacy and practical advantages are asserted but lack empirical support.  
- Bounds arise from multiple admissible sets Z and different witnesses, which should be clarified.  
- The authors should explicitly state assumptions (e.g., causal sufficiency or knowledge of W → X) and clarify that witness W is only for identifying Z.  
- Paper contains several clarity and wording issues (e.g., replace "unstable" with "unfaithful," fix typos, clarify "cartoon representation").  
- Need better guidance and intuition for setting constraint parameters and linking them to weak interactions.  
- Comparisons should avoid meaningless bias-based evaluations and demonstrate practical benefit over standard faithfulness-based point estimates.  
- Emphasize that WPP bounds are not confidence intervals around point estimates to prevent misinterpretation.  
- Remove emotive or dismissive language and correct factual claims that contradict prior work (e.g., Geiger et al. 2014).  
- Overall: conceptually interesting and technically sound, but requires substantial clarification, stronger experiments, and better interpretability to be practically useful.
