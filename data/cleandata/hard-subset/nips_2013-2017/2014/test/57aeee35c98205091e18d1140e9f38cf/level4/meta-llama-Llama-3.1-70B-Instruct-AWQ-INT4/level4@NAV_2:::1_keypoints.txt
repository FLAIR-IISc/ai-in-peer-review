- Problem: Estimates average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables with incomplete covariate causal structure using Entner et al.'s rule to select covariates and provides bounds (not point estimates) via a Ramsahai (2012)-based method.  
- Main idea: Interesting and potentially useful, but the role of Entner et al.'s conditions is not clearly explained without the faithfulness assumption.  
- Conceptual gap: Without faithfulness, Entner et al.'s conditions do not have obvious causal implications; unclear how they inform relaxation parameters.  
- Method dependence: The proposed machinery appears not to crucially depend on Entner et al.'s conditions, so experimental robustness may be unrelated to those conditions.  
- Request: Authors should clarify how Entner et al.'s rule conditions are used and whether they affect parameter choices or bounds.  
- Technical wording: Phrase “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading; should describe allowing “almost violations” or bounded dependence instead.  
- Experimental suggestion: Report performance of Entner et al.'s original method using stringent thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds for fair comparison.  
- Empirical error: Possible sign/mapping mistake in example—if X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized, a negative ACE interpretation is inconsistent and should be corrected.  
- Editorial error: Table citation mismatch — “Table 1” is cited as “Table 5” in the text.  
- Strengths: Novel approach (bounds for ACE), sound implementation following Ramsahai (2012), and experiments show apparent robustness.  
- Weaknesses: Unclear role of Entner et al. conditions, unclear exposition of how those conditions are incorporated, and several minor technical/wording mistakes.  
- Impact: Addresses an important causal-inference problem with potentially wide applicability if clarified.  
- Clarity & recommendation: Paper is hard to follow; promising but requires revisions to improve clarity and technical soundness before acceptance.
