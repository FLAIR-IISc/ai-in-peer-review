- Proposes a novel method to bound average causal effect (ACE) by inverting Entner’s Rule 1, relaxing faithfulness, and using linear programming constraints.  
- Technically sound combination of instrumental variables, Entner’s Rule, and LP, addressing limitations of strict faithfulness.  
- Justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is insufficient and needs clearer exposition of required causal structure (Fig. 2).  
- Selection criteria and rationale for relaxation parameters are under-specified despite their strong influence on bounds.  
- No rigorous sensitivity analysis on how parameter choices affect derived bounds.  
- Reported bounds appear tighter than prior work, but computational limitations are acknowledged.  
- Theoretical exposition is generally clear except for the inverse-Entner point; experimental presentation lacks detail.  
- Implementation details and parameter definitions (table references) are unclear or misreferenced, with minor typographical errors.  
- Originality lies in the specific combination of techniques and relaxing faithfulness, not in the problem itself.  
- Needs clearer comparison and articulation of improvements over existing methods in concrete scenarios.  
- Significance is promising (tighter ACE bounds) but limited by narrow experimental scope and uncertain generalizability.  
- Requires broader empirical validation across datasets and more theoretical robustness checks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel technique combination, potential tighter ACE bounds, addresses faithfulness limitation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak justification for inverse Entner use, unclear relaxation parameter criteria, limited experiments, and clarity/typo issues.
