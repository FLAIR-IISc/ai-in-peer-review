- Paper proposes bounding the Average Causal Effect (ACE) between two binary variables using Entner et al.'s covariate-selection rule but deriving bounds (Ramsahai-style) instead of point estimates.  
- Central idea is intuitive and potentially useful, though the reviewer could not verify all technical details.  
- Unclear what precise role Entner et al.'s conditions play without the faithfulness assumption; paper sets aside how these conditions inform relaxation parameters.  
- The method’s core machinery appears decoupled from Entner et al.'s conditions, which may explain observed robustness—authors should clarify this relationship.  
- Minor: The phrase about "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is misleading; it should refer to "near-violations" and constraining dependence magnitudes.  
- Minor: Experiments should include Entner et al.'s original method as a baseline and treat its "don't know" outputs as uninformative bounds for comparison.  
- Minor: Interpretation error on page 8—given X=1 is vaccine and Y=1 is not hospitalized, a negative ACE implies the vaccine increases hospitalization (undesirable).  
- Minor: Table numbering mismatch on page 8 (text refers to "Table 5" but labeled "Table 1").  
- Overall: Interesting contribution but clarity on the role of Entner et al.'s conditions and several fixes would significantly improve understanding.
