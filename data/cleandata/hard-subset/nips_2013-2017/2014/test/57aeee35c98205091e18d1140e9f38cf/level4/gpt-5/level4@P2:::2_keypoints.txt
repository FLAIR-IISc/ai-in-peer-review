- Paper introduces WPP: bounds average causal effect (ACE) for binary X,Y by inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1, relaxing faithfulness, and solving LPs to produce posterior bounds.  
- Core idea 1: treat a witness W and admissible set Z as the basis for IV-style bounds instead of back-door adjustment.  
- Core idea 2: relax faithfulness via bounded deviations (path cancellations) expressed as inequality constraints on local conditionals.  
- Core idea 3: compute bounds with a linear program and obtain a posterior over bounds under constrained Bayesian priors.  
- Adds algebraic/symbolic bounds and a back-substitution algorithm to speed testing and bounding relative to full LP solves.  
- Experiments (synthetic + flu vaccine data) show WPP can yield substantially tighter bounds than standard IV when conditional independencies help.  
- Strength: novel technical combination of causal discovery constraints with IV-style bounding and tunable relaxation parameters (w,x,y,β,β̄).  
- Strength: LP formulation and posterior approach are principled and empirically produce narrower intervals while remaining robust in simulations.  
- Strength: algebraic bounds and back-substitution provide practical computational improvements.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for using Rule 1 in reverse—paper must state and defend required graphical/structural assumptions.  
- Weakness: selection of relaxation parameters is under-specified; needs principled guidance, calibration, and systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: some constraints, parameters (ζ, κ, η, δ, ω) and mappings between observable and latent quantities are not fully defined in the main text.  
- Weakness: Bayesian implementation uses constrained Dirichlet with rejection sampling and an ad hoc falsification test; computational cost, diagnostics, and prior robustness are not discussed.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited—needs benchmarks against standard IV bounds, monotonicity variants, partial-identification methods with coverage and width metrics.  
- Clarity issues: must explicitly state structural assumptions for inverted Rule 1 and precise meaning of “bounded” faithfulness violations; fix inconsistent numbering and provide a symbol glossary.  
- Originality: combination of known components is novel enough (inversion of Rule 1, bounded faithfulness LP, algebraic/back-substitution), but incremental relative to individual literatures.  
- Significance: promising practical/methodological tool if validated, but stronger claims require broader empirical validation, parameter-selection principles, and sensitivity studies.  
- Pros for acceptance: attractive conceptual bridge, potential for tighter bounds in practice, and useful algebraic/algorithmic contributions.  
- Cons for acceptance: inadequate justification of inverted Rule 1, lack of principled parameter guidance, presentation/notation problems, and narrow empirical study.  
- Suggestions: prove/illustrate conditions for using (W,Z) for IV-style bounding; provide principled parameter-selection strategies and sensitivity plots.  
- Suggestions: consolidate notation, define all parameters in main text, fix numbering/cross-references, and add a schematic of parameter flow.  
- Suggestions: report standard IV bounds, coverage and average width in experiments; discuss computational complexity and LP vs. back-substitution trade-offs.  
- Suggestions: expand experiments, include ablations, and discuss/generalize beyond binary variables where possible.
