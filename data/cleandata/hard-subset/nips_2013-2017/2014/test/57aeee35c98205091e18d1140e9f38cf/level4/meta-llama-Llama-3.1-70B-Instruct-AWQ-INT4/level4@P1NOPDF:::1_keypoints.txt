- Paper proposes bounding the average causal effect (ACE) for two binary variables under partial causal-structure knowledge, using Entner et al.'s covariate-selection rule and a Ramsahai (2012)-style bounding method.  
- Reviewer finds the idea interesting but has not fully digested the technical details.  
- Main concern: the role and implications of Entner et al.'s conditions are unclear without the faithfulness assumption—those conditions do not obviously constrain the causal structure if faithfulness is dropped.  
- Reviewer conjectures the Entner conditions might guide choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly leaves this connection unexplored.  
- Observation/question: the proposed machinery appears not to crucially depend on Entner's conditions; the reviewer wonders if that explains the method's empirical robustness.  
- Minor comment 1: wording “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is odd—unfaithfulness usually allows extra independences; the framework may intend “almost violations” by bounding dependence.  
- Minor comment 2: experiments should also report Entner et al.'s method performance using stringent thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds.  
- Minor comment 3: clarification needed on example interpretation—if X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized, why would a negative ACE indicate a desirable vaccine?  
- Minor comment 4: table labeling error—Table 1 (top of page 8) is cited as “Table 5” in the text.  
- Overall: interesting contribution but the paper needs clarification on the role of Entner conditions and clearer exposition of technical details.
