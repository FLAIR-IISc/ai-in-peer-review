- Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE under controlled violations of faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Timely and relevant problem: quantifying robustness to faithfulness violations is important and active.
- Principled formulation: relaxation parameters transparently encode departures from assumptions; LP is appropriate for computing bounds.
- Cross-fertilization potential: connects causal discovery, sensitivity analysis, and partial identification.
- Output interpretability concern: bounds depend on unspecified relaxation parameters, making practical interpretation and action unclear.
- Many plausible relaxations produce very wide intervals, limiting usability in practice.
- No recommended point estimate or calibrated summary within bounds for users who want a central answer.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: comparing bias across interval and point methods is inappropriate; influenza case study yields very wide, uninformative bounds.
- Major practical component missing: no defensible procedure for selecting or eliciting relaxation parameters.
- Unclear practical effectiveness of back-substitution: frequency, magnitude, and conditions under which it tightens bounds are not quantified.
- Technical framework appears sound and supplementary derivations are provided, but not fully verified by reviewer.
- Exposition issues: abrupt abstraction jumps, many closely related parameters are hard to track, and key steps lack intuition or worked examples.
- Original and novel synthesis of existing tools; distinct from related work and potentially impactful conceptually.
- As a practical tool it is premature for routine use; promising ideas but not yet practitioner-ready.
- Suggested improvements: provide principled parameter elicitation, quantify back-substitution benefits, compare to relevant bounding methods with interval-appropriate metrics, and include decision-focused case studies.
- Numerous minor edits and clarifications requested (terminology, assumptions, linkages between witness/admissible set/bounds, wording, typos).
- Overall verdict: interesting and technically sound contribution with good writing, but needs clearer exposition, better experiments, and practical guidance before wide adoption.
