This paper presents a novel approach to estimating the bound of the average causal effect (ACE) from variable X to Y, leveraging Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, relaxing the faithfulness assumption to accommodate path cancellation, and introducing inequality constraints. The authors formulate a computational scheme for ACE bounds using linear programming, yielding remarkably narrow and accurate results compared to existing methods.

Quality
The authors have made a commendable effort to develop a new method for deriving more accurate ACE bounds, successfully combining several feasible principles to achieve tighter bounds. The paper's content is engaging and demonstrates a clear contribution to the field.

However, some aspects of the bound definition criteria require more comprehensive explanation. Specifically, the validity of applying Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not adequately discussed. To justify this application, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig. 2 and provide a clear explanation.

Furthermore, the paper lacks generic criteria for selecting appropriate parameters for relaxation, which significantly impact the bound results. The authors should investigate and include the principles for determining these parameters.

Clarity
The theoretical explanation in the paper is generally comprehensive, except for the essential discussion mentioned above.

However, the demonstration of experimental results is not well described. The actual implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained, and the definition of parameters in Table 1 (possibly Table 5) is unclear. Additionally, there are several typos, such as the reference to a non-existent table of the algorithm on page 6 and the incorrect table number on page 8.

Originality
While the problem setting has been addressed in earlier work, the authors' idea to combine three concepts in a unified framework exhibits some originality. Nevertheless, the validity of this combination is not thoroughly discussed.

Significance
The proposed method demonstrates significance in deriving tighter ACE bounds. However, its performance has only been evaluated through limited experiments, and its generalizability in both theory and extensive experiments has not been rigorously assessed. Although the paper addresses an important problem in evaluating the bound of causal relation strength, the validity of the presented idea should be more thoroughly described in both theory and experiments.
