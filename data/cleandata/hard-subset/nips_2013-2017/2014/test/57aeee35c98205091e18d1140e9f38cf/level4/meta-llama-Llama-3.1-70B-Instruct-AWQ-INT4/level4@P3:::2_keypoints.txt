- Proposes a novel method to estimate ACE by leveraging observational conditional independencies and a relaxed faithfulness allowing path cancellations, yielding a posterior over ACE bounds via linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Builds on prior work (Entner et al., Ramsahai) and combines three ideas into a unified methodology.  
- Technically sound with solid theoretical analysis and supporting experimental results.  
- Major methodological concern: validity of using Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not adequately justified.  
- The paper should explicitly assume and discuss the implications of the causal structure shown in Fig.2.  
- No generic criteria are provided for selecting relaxation parameters, which materially affect the resulting bounds.  
- Experimental results presentation and actual algorithm implementation details are inadequately described.  
- Definitions of parameters (e.g., in Table 1) are unclear and the manuscript contains typos.  
- Originality: offers a new perspective by unifying existing ideas, providing some novel contribution.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and can produce tighter ACE bounds, but empirical validation is limited and generality is not fully assessed.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel approach, thorough analysis, and relevance to causal inference.  
- Cons for acceptance: unclear justification for key rule, missing parameter-selection guidance, weak experimental description, and limited empirical evaluation.
