- Proposes a novel method to bound ACE using a reversed application of Entner's Rule 1 combined with relaxed faithfulness and inequality constraints.
- Introduces a linear programming computation scheme for ACE bounds.
- Preliminary results indicate tighter and more accurate bounds than current state-of-the-art, despite no algorithmic optimization.
- Insufficient justification for applying Entner's Rule 1 in reverse; the implicit structural assumptions (e.g., Fig. 2) need explicit statement and discussion.
- Lacks clear guidance and rigorous analysis for selecting relaxation parameters of the faithfulness assumption; sensitivity analysis is needed.
- Theoretical derivation around the reverse-rule application and parameter choices requires more thorough explanation.
- Experimental implementation details are inadequate; algorithmic steps are not fully described.
- Parameter definitions in tables are unclear and there are likely typos/misreferenced tables (e.g., Table 1 vs. Table 5).
- Originality is moderate: novelty lies in unifying existing ideas (reverse Entner's Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, inequalities) rather than introducing a wholly new problem setting.
- Significance is promising but currently limited by narrow evaluation; broader experiments and deeper theoretical validation are required to establish robustness and generality.
