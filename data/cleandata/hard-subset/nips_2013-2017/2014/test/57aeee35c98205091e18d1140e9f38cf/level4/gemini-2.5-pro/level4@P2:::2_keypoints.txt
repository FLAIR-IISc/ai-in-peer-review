- Introduces the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) to compute ACE bounds under unmeasured confounding, bridging faithfulness-based and IV approaches.  
- Reinterprets Entner et al.'s witness W as a candidate instrumental variable conditioned on Z, allowing relaxed IV assumptions via tunable violation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄).  
- Encodes parameter-induced constraints into a linear programming framework (building on Dawid and Ramsahai), with a Bayesian layer for finite-sample uncertainty and an efficient symbolic/message-passing approximation.  
- Strength: Novel, principled, and original heuristic for data-driven identification of plausible IVs in hard causal-inference settings.  
- Strength: Provides a nuanced relaxation of the faithfulness assumption, enabling a formal trade-off between assumption strength and bound tightness (near-faithfulness).  
- Strength: Empirical results are promising—WPP yields tighter, more robust bounds than standard IV in synthetic and real-data (influenza vaccine) examples.  
- Weakness: Lacks practical guidance for selecting the critical relaxation parameters; no sensitivity analysis or data-driven default is provided.  
- Weakness: Theoretical justification for turning a witness into an (imperfect) instrument is limited; needs formal characterization of when the heuristic is valid.  
- Weakness: Empirical validation is narrow—requires testing on more diverse causal graphs and discussion of sensitivity/aggregation when multiple witness/Z pairs exist.  
- Clarity/typos: Generally readable but experimental descriptions need clarity; several incorrect table/algorithm references and unclear parameter-table linkage.  
- Conclusion: Interesting and potentially significant contribution, but incomplete without principled parameter-selection, stronger theoretical support, and broader empirical validation.
