- Problem: Estimating average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables under unknown confounding and causal structure.
- Approach: Builds on Entner et al.’s rule to select covariates but relaxes faithfulness, estimating bounds on ACE instead of point estimates.
- Novelty: Uses conditional independence constraints to derive bounds (inspired by Ramsahai 2012) for robustness to faithfulness violations.
- Metaphor: Introduces the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) as a memorable framing of the method.
- Key confusion: Unclear justification for using Entner et al.’s rule without faithfulness; conditions W ⟂ Y | Z and W ⟂ Y | Z∪{X} may not constrain causal structure absent faithfulness.
- Parameter selection: Unclear how satisfying Entner-style conditions informs choice of relaxation parameters.
- Theoretical contribution: Presents algebraic bounds and a back-substitution algorithm to improve computational efficiency.
- Technical methods: Combines linear programming, Bayesian inference, and algebraic manipulation.
- Experiments: Includes synthetic and a real-world influenza vaccination study demonstrating method potential.
- Computational strengths: Addresses complexity via relaxations and an efficient back-substitution procedure.
- Weakness — clarity: Some sections are dense and hard to follow, potentially hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness — theoretical gap: Lack of clear justification for the foundational role of Entner et al.’s rule weakens theoretical grounding.
- Weakness — minor errors: Minor inconsistencies (e.g., wrong table reference) and confusing explanation of ACE sign in influenza example.
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles an important problem, offers a novel, technically solid approach, and shows empirical promise.
- Arguments for rejection: Insufficient justification for relying on Entner et al.’s rule, dense presentation, and clarity/reproducibility concerns.
- Overall assessment: Paper is meritorious and the reviewer leans toward acceptance contingent on addressing Entner-rule justification and improving clarity.
- Minor suggested fixes: Rephrase “violations of faithfulness” as “near violations” or constrained dependency, include Entner et al.’s method results with strict thresholds, clarify Y=1 interpretation in the influenza example, and correct the table citation.
