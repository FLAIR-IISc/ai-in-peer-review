The paper introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute bounds on the inferred average causal effect (ACE), accounting for potential violations of the faithfulness assumption in causal inference. It achieves this by incorporating relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and then employing a linear programming framework to determine upper and lower bounds on the ACE for a given link, adjusted for an appropriate set of covariates subject to these constraints. 

This is a timely and interesting problem, and the paper presents a principled attempt to explicitly quantify the impact of (weak) unfaithful interaction patterns, leveraging well-established mathematical tools for maximization and minimization. However, the interpretation of the results remains challenging. The output is a distribution over bounds that are functions of the ‘unfaithful relaxations’ relative to a given causal model, and the likelihood of these unfaithful instances – and therefore the relevance of the resulting bounds – is unclear. Many relaxations lead to wide bounds, diminishing their practical utility. The method does not commit to a single point estimate within the interval, despite the likelihood of such interpretation by users.

The experimental evaluation is weak. The ‘bias’ metric used to compare WPP with competitors is not meaningful, as they assess fundamentally different quantities. Consequently, it is unclear whether WPP provides a valuable addition to practitioners’ toolsets or simply introduces unnecessary complexity. Furthermore, the practical performance on the influenza dataset is concerning: an ACE in the interval (-0.23, 0.64) offers limited actionable conclusions, although this may represent a fair assessment given the data.

**Quality:** The approach is technically sound, with details elaborated in the supplementary material (which I have not thoroughly reviewed). The ideas are well-introduced, though the broader context is sometimes lost. Critically, the choice of relaxation parameters – key to the method’s function – is deferred to future work, hindering a full evaluation of potential issues. The experimental evaluation lacks robustness and balance.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, although the conclusion requires improvement. The algorithmic steps and parameter modifications are difficult to follow, partially due to a lack of detailed explanation. While I believe I understand the core mechanism, complete implementation would likely necessitate reference to the supplementary material.

**Originality:** The method appears to be a novel combination of existing techniques. It is closely related to, but distinct from, recent work in the field and may encourage cross-fertilization of approaches.

**Significance:** The significance of the paper lies primarily in the ideas and direction it offers to this challenging problem, demonstrating how to integrate other techniques. In its current form, it seems unlikely to represent a finished solution for practitioners, though the concepts within WPP could become important.

**Summary:** This paper addresses a relevant and topical problem with an interesting method likely to attract attention. Its current application is limited but has potential for extension to more general cases. The writing is reasonably good, but could be improved. The technical solutions are interesting, but the experimental evaluation is poor. The relevance and appropriate interpretation of the output distribution over bounds remains unclear.
