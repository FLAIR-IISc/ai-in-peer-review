- Introduces WPP algorithm to compute ACE bounds accounting for faithfulness violations via relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Timely, interesting, and principled approach that explicitly quantifies weak unfaithful interactions; technically sound.
- Output is conceptually hard to interpret: bounds depend on unspecified unfaithful relaxations with no guidance on their likelihood.
- Many relaxation settings produce overly wide, potentially uninformative bounds; no recommended point estimate is provided.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced; the chosen “bias” metric is meaningless for comparing WPP to competitors.
- Influenza data result (ACE in −0.23 to 0.64) is too wide to draw actionable conclusions, questioning practical usefulness.
- Choosing relaxation parameters is left to future work, making practical application and assessment difficult.
- Clarity issues: algorithmic steps and many parameter variants are hard to follow and may require the supplement to implement.
- Originality: a novel combination of familiar techniques and sufficiently distinct from recent related work.
- Significance: brings valuable ideas and direction but current implementation is unlikely ready for practitioners.
- Methodological concern: effectiveness of the back-substitution step in tightening bounds is doubtful and needs empirical assessment.
- Comparative claims and tables (e.g., Table 1, “WPP is quite stable”) are unconvincing or misleading.
- Risk of misinterpretation: readers may treat bounds as confidence intervals; paper should better clarify interpretation.
- Tone and phrasing: some emotive or dismissive language and several wording/typo issues and missing explanations throughout the manuscript.
