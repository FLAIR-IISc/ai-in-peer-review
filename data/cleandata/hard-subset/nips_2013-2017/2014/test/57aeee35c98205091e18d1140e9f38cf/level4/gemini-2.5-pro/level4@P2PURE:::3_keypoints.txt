- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute ACE bounds while allowing quantified violations of faithfulness via relaxation parameters.  
- Uses relaxation parameters as constraints in a linear programming framework to obtain upper and lower ACE bounds after adjusting for covariates.  
- Addresses a timely and important causal-inference problem and principledly quantifies the impact of unfaithful conditional independencies.  
- Linear programming is a sound, well-motivated choice for solving the resulting optimization problem.  
- Practical utility is unclear because the output is a distribution of bounds over relaxation parameters with no method to assess the likelihood of those relaxations.  
- Many relaxations produce bounds so wide they become uninformative for decision-making.  
- Risk that practitioners may misinterpret the bounds as confidence intervals or as point-estimate uncertainty.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: the bias metric is inappropriate for comparing interval outputs to point estimates.  
- Experiments fail to demonstrate whether WPP is practically useful or unnecessarily complex.  
- Influenza dataset results produce very wide ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64), preventing meaningful causal conclusions.  
- Technical approach appears sound and details are provided in the supplement, though the supplement was not fully checked.  
- Methodology for choosing relaxation parameters is deferred to future work, leaving a crucial gap in practical applicability.  
- Paper is generally well written but the many similar parameters and some algorithmic steps are hard to follow and may impede implementation without the supplement.  
- The method is an original combination of existing techniques and could stimulate future cross-fertilization.  
- Significant in ideas and direction but not yet a ready-to-use tool for practitioners; components may be important for future work.  
- Summary: relevant and interesting approach, but poor experimental validation and unclear interpretation of the bounds limit impact.  
- Numerous clarity and wording issues (imprecise terms, unclear sentences, abrupt abstraction shifts) and several typos and phrasing fixes recommended.  
- Specific technical concerns: need clearer link between witness W, admissible set Z, and relaxation parameters; clarification of constraint parameter interpretation; and justification of claims about prior analytical bounds.  
- Question practical efficacy of the back-substitution refinement step and sensitivity to sample size (e.g., small medical trials).  
- Tone of conclusion is emotive and should be more neutral; several statements overreach and should be softened.  
- Positive note: the algorithm name "Witness Protection Program" is memorable.
