- Introduces WPP algorithm to compute ACE bounds while allowing controlled violations of faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.  
- Timely and compelling approach that quantifies impact of weak unfaithful interactions with principled optimization tools.  
- Practical relevance is questionable because the output—a distribution over bounds parameterized by relaxations—lacks clear conceptual interpretation.  
- Paper does not clarify the likelihood or realism of unfaithful scenarios, and resulting bounds are often extremely wide (e.g., nearly [-1,1]).  
- Method provides no point estimate inside intervals, risking practitioner misinterpretation of bounds as confidence intervals.  
- Experimental evaluation is inadequate and does not convincingly demonstrate practical value.  
- Proposed "bias" metric is meaningless for fair comparison because it measures fundamentally different quantities than competing methods.  
- Influenza dataset results are unconvincing: ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64) offers little actionable insight and may reflect method limitations.  
- Technical approach is sound and sufficiently detailed in supplements, though not independently verified.  
- Key methodological choices—especially how to select relaxation parameters—are deferred, obscuring potential pitfalls.  
- Experimental design lacks balance and robustness.  
- Writing is generally clear but the algorithm’s intricate parameter dependencies are hard to follow without more intuition.  
- Main text should better explain the interpretation of constraint/relaxation parameters (e.g., as violation magnitudes).  
- WPP is an original combination of established techniques and is distinct from recent related work, potentially enabling cross-fertilization.  
- Primary significance is conceptual: incorporating unfaithfulness into causal bounds is valuable, but current implementation is unlikely to be directly useful for practitioners.  
- Overall: promising technical novelty but poor experiments and ambiguous output interpretation limit practical utility.
