- Proposes a novel ACE-bounding approach combining reverse application of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing path cancellation), and inequality-constraint assumptions.
- Implements a computational scheme to derive ACE bounds via linear programming, though the algorithm is not yet optimized.
- Reported bounds appear narrower and more accurate than existing state-of-the-art methods.
- The validity of applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is not clearly justified and implicitly requires structural conditions (e.g., those in Fig. 2) that should be stated.
- Criteria used to define bounds are underexplained, especially the principles for choosing relaxation parameters.
- No general guidelines are provided for selecting relaxation parameters despite their strong influence on results.
- Theoretical exposition is mostly comprehensive except for the critical discussion on reverse Entner use.
- Experimental presentation lacks detail: the algorithm implementation is not thoroughly described.
- Parameters in the tables are not clearly defined, and table references appear incorrect or missing (Table 1 vs. Table 5).
- Typographical errors and misreferenced tables reduce clarity and readability.
- Originality is moderate: the problem is studied previously, but the unification of the three concepts provides some novelty.
- Significance is promising due to tighter bounds, but the method’s generality and validity need stronger theoretical and broader empirical assessment.
