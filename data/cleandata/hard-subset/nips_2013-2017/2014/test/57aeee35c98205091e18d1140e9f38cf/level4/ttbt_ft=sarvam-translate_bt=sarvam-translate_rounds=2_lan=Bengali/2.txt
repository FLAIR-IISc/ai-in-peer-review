This paper proposes a new method for estimating the average causal effect (ACE) of Y on X. Its core principles are to use the inverse of the rule of 1, to relax the assumption of reliability to introduce some degree of path cancellation, and to introduce assumptions represented by some inequality constraints. A method for estimating ACE bounds using linear programming under these principles has been formulated. Although the authors have not yet optimized the algorithm for bound estimation, the obtained bounds appear to be very tight and accurate compared to other state-of-the-art methods.

Quality
The authors have attempted to establish a new method for estimating more accurate ACE bounds than state-of-the-art methods. They have succeeded in providing more rigorous bounds by combining several possible principles. The content of the paper on this subject is very interesting.

However, some criteria for determining the bounds have not been fully explained. In particular, the validity of using the rule of 1 for the inverse has not been clearly discussed. The structure shown in Figure 2 for its use for the inverse should be clearly assumed by the authors. It should be clearly explained and discussed.

Also, general criteria for selecting suitable parameters for relaxation have not been provided. Since these significantly affect the bound result, the principle of parameter selection should be better explored and incorporated into the work.

Clarity
The explanation of the paper on the theoretical part seems to be largely broad, except for the essential discussion mentioned above.

However, the demonstration of experimental results has not been well described. As I mentioned above, the actual implementation of the algorithm has not been fully explained. The definition of the parameters shown in Table 1 (Table 5?) is not clearly given.

Originality
As the authors have mentioned, the problem formulation in this paper has been discussed through some previous work. In this regard, the scope of this paper is not very original.

There is some originality in the idea of combining three concepts into a unified form. However, as I mentioned above, the validity of the combination has not been discussed very clearly.

Importance
In estimating more rigorous ACE bounds, this method shows some significance. However, its effectiveness has been verified through fairly limited testing. Its generality in both theory and extensive testing has not been strictly evaluated. The paper discusses an important problem such as estimating the strength of causal relationships. However, the validity of the concept presented in both theory and testing should be better described.
