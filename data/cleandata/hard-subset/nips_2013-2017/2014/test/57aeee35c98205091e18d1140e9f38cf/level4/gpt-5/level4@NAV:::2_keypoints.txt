- Introduces a new method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using reverse application of Entner’s Rule 1, controlled relaxation of faithfulness, and inequality constraints.
- Formulates ACE bounds as a linear programming problem; algorithm not yet optimized but yields tight, accurate bounds versus state-of-the-art.
- Core novelty lies in unifying three principles (reverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness/path cancellation, inequality assumptions).
- Major concern: insufficient justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse — the necessary structural assumption (Fig. 2) must be explicitly stated and examined.
- Major concern: no general guidance or procedure for selecting relaxation parameters, though these critically affect the bounds.
- Experimental description is under-specified: algorithmic implementation details and parameter definitions (e.g., Table 1/Table 5) are missing or unclear.
- Paper contains text errors/misreferences (missing algorithm/table references), reducing clarity and reproducibility.
- Originality is moderate: problem is well-studied, and the unification has some novelty but combined-use justification is weak.
- Significance is promising due to tighter bounds, but theoretical and empirical generality and validation are currently limited by sparse experiments and weaker theoretical support.
