

The paper introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm, which computes bounds on the inferred average causal effect (ACE) while accounting for potential violations of the standard faithfulness assumption in causal inference. It achieves this by introducing relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and formulating a linear programming framework to solve for upper and lower ACE bounds under these constraints, given appropriate covariate adjustment. This problem is timely and well-motivated, and the approach represents a principled effort to explicitly quantify the impact of weak unfaithful interaction patterns using established mathematical tools for optimization.  

However, the interpretability of the method’s output remains problematic. The resulting distribution over bounds—dependent on "unfaithful relaxations" relative to a given model—lacks clear conceptual grounding: it is unclear how likely these unfaithful scenarios are, or how relevant or realistic the derived bounds are. Many relaxations yield excessively wide bounds that become practically useless. Furthermore, the method does not commit to a preferred point estimate within the interval, despite the strong likelihood that practitioners would misinterpret the bounds as confidence intervals around a point estimate.  

The experimental evaluation is inadequate. The proposed "bias" metric is meaningless for comparing WPP to competitors, as it measures fundamentally different quantities. Consequently, it remains unclear whether WPP offers practical value or merely adds complexity. The application to the influenza dataset is particularly concerning: the ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64) implies near-zero actionable insight, suggesting the method may be unhelpful in practice.  

**Quality**  
The technical approach is sound, with sufficient detail in the supplementary material (though not verified in depth). Ideas are well-introduced, though the overall context is occasionally lost. Critical aspects like relaxation parameter selection are deferred to future work, hindering assessment of potential pitfalls. Experimental evaluation lacks balance and robustness.  

**Clarity**  
The writing is generally clear, except for the conclusion. Algorithmic steps and parameter modifications are difficult to follow due to insufficient explanation of key methodological details (e.g., the role of relaxation parameters θ in identifying admissible sets Z). While the supplement may suffice for implementation, the main text requires better intuition.  

**Originality**  
The method constitutes a novel combination of established techniques, closely related to but distinct from recent work on the same problem. It may foster fruitful cross-fertilization across approaches.  

**Significance**  
The significance lies primarily in the conceptual direction it offers for incorporating additional techniques into this challenging problem. However, the current implementation is unlikely to be immediately useful for practitioners, though components of the WPP algorithm may later prove influential.  

**Summary**  
The paper addresses a relevant, highly topical problem with an interesting, technically sound method likely to attract an audience. Current applications are limited but may generalize. While reasonably well-written, the experimental evaluation is poor, and the relevance and proper interpretation of the output distribution over bounds remain unclear.
