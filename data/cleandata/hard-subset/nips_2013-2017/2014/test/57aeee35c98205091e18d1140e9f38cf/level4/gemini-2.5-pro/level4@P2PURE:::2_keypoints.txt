- Proposes a new method to derive tighter ACE bounds by combining inverse Entner's Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints solved via a linear program.
- Initial (non-optimized) algorithm yields significantly narrower and more accurate ACE bounds compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- Insufficient justification for applying Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction; this requires explicitly assuming and discussing the causal structure shown in Figure 2.
- No generic criteria or guidance are provided for selecting parameters that govern the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption, despite their large impact on results.
- Theoretical exposition is mostly strong but omits critical assumptions and their implications, weakening the argument's completeness.
- Experimental section lacks clear implementation details and parameter definitions; references to tables are inconsistent (Table 1 vs Table 5) and several typographical errors exist.
- The problem setting itself is not highly original; the paper's novelty is in synthesizing three ideas, but the theoretical soundness of that synthesis is under-discussed.
- Method has potential significance but is only validated on limited experiments; broader theoretical and empirical evaluation is needed to establish generality.
