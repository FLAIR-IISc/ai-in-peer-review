- Paper tackles estimating average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables with partially specified covariate causal structure, using Entner et al.'s rule and Ramsahai-style bounding.  
- Main idea—use Entner et al.'s selection rule to derive ACE bounds rather than point estimates—is clear, interesting, and potentially useful.  
- Reviewer did not fully follow all technical details but finds the approach promising.  
- Major concern: unclear role of Entner et al.'s conditions without the faithfulness assumption—implications for causal structure are not apparent.  
- Suggestion/interpretation: those conditions might inform choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly leaves this connection unexplored.  
- Question whether the method’s experimental robustness stems from not fundamentally relying on Entner et al.'s conditions.  
- Minor comment 1: wording “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is odd—unfaithfulness implies extra independences; framework seems to allow “almost violations” by bounding dependence.  
- Minor comment 2: experiments should report Entner et al.’s method performance under their stringent thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds.  
- Minor comment 3: clarify interpretation on page 8—if X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized, why does a negative ACE indicate a desirable vaccine?  
- Minor comment 4: table numbering error—text refers to “Table 5” but it’s labeled “Table 1.”  
- Overall: interesting paper but some technical points and explanations need clarification.
