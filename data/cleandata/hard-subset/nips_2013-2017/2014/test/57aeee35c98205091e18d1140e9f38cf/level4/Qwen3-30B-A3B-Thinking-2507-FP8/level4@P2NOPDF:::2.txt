

This paper proposes a novel method for deriving tight bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from variable X to Y. The approach leverages Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, relaxes the faithfulness assumption to permit path cancellation, and incorporates inequality constraints. The authors formulate a linear programming scheme to compute these bounds. While the algorithm implementation remains unoptimized, the resulting bounds demonstrate significant tightness and accuracy compared to state-of-the-art methods.

**Quality and Theoretical Soundness**  
The paper’s core contribution—combining these principles to achieve tighter ACE bounds—is theoretically interesting. However, two critical gaps undermine its validity. First, the inverse application of Entner's Rule 1 requires explicit justification. The authors must explicitly state and validate the causal structure depicted in Figure 2, as this is foundational to their method. Without this, the approach lacks theoretical grounding. Second, the paper provides no principled criteria for selecting relaxation parameters (e.g., the degree of faithfulness relaxation). Since these parameters critically influence bound tightness, their selection must be systematically addressed, not left as an ad hoc choice.

**Clarity and Presentation**  
The theoretical exposition is generally comprehensive but falters on key points. The insufficient discussion of Entner's Rule 1’s inverse use and parameter selection (as noted above) creates ambiguity. Experimental sections suffer from poor clarity: the algorithm implementation is not thoroughly described, and Table 1 (referenced as Table 5 on page 8) lacks clear definitions for its parameters. Additionally, typographical errors impair readability: "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" (page 6) contradicts the absence of such a table, and "Results are summarized in Table 5" (page 8) appears to reference an incorrect table label. These issues hinder reproducibility and reader comprehension.

**Originality and Significance**  
The problem of bounding ACE has been previously explored (as acknowledged by the authors), so the paper’s scope lacks strong novelty. Its incremental contribution lies in unifying three ideas—inverse Entner’s Rule, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints—into a single framework. However, the lack of rigorous justification for this unification (particularly regarding Entner’s Rule) diminishes this contribution. The significance of the method is partially supported by empirical results showing tighter bounds, but the evaluation is limited to a small set of experiments. The paper fails to rigorously assess the method’s theoretical generality or its performance across diverse causal structures. Without broader validation, the practical significance remains tentative.

**Recommendation**  
The paper presents an intriguing direction for ACE bounding but requires substantial revisions. The theoretical justification for Entner’s Rule 1 must be strengthened with explicit structural assumptions and validation. The parameter selection framework needs formalization, and the experimental section must clarify algorithm implementation, table references, and parameter definitions. Addressing these issues would significantly enhance the paper’s quality and impact. As currently written, the work’s theoretical foundation is insufficiently justified, and its empirical claims lack robust support. We recommend rejection pending major revisions that resolve these fundamental concerns.
