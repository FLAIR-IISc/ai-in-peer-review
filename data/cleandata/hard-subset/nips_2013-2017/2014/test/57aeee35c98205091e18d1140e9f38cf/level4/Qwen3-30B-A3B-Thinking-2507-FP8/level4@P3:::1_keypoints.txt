- Introduces a novel method for bounding average causal effect (ACE) in observational studies instead of producing point estimates.  
- Reinterprets Entner et al. (2013)’s admissible-set rule to generate instrumental-variable-like witnesses that allow bounds under relaxed faithfulness.  
- Allows "path cancellations" (conditional independencies that do not rule out confounding) by relaxing the faithfulness assumption.  
- Uses a linear programming framework to compute ACE bounds.  
- Provides a Bayesian implementation to quantify uncertainty in constraint estimation.  
- Proposes a back-substitution algorithm to improve computational efficiency.  
- Method is technically sound with clear theoretical development and proper Bayesian uncertainty treatment.  
- Theoretical link between Entner et al.’s rule conditions and the relaxation parameters is insufficiently explained and weakens the theoretical foundation.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; Algorithm 1 (Witness Protection Program) is especially helpful.  
- Needs clearer explanation of how Entner et al.’s conditions map to the relaxation parameters central to the bounds.  
- Original and meaningful contribution: bridges point-estimation under faithfulness and IV approaches while controlling faithfulness violations.  
- Significant potential impact for applied researchers; influenza vaccination case shows practical utility and more informative bounds than traditional IV in some cases.  
- Strengths: addresses a real limitation, theoretically grounded bounds, Bayesian uncertainty quantification, validated on synthetic and real data, computationally efficient algorithm.  
- Weaknesses: unclear theoretical connections, influenza result interpretation needs clearer linking of Y=1 (hospitalized) to ACE sign, and distinction from traditional IV methods should be clarified.  
- Specific suggested fixes: clarify the phrase "bounded violations of local independence," explicitly connect Y=1 to vaccine benefit interpretation, report Entner et al.’s method under their original stringent thresholds (treating "don't know" as uninformative), and correct the table citation error.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, and practical contribution that would be excellent with clarifications and minor presentation fixes.
