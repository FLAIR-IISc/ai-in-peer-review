- Proposes a novel method combining inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (path cancellation), and inequality constraints to compute tighter ACE bounds via linear programming.  
- The inverse use of Entner’s Rule 1 is unjustified; the structural assumption in Figure 2 must be explicitly stated and validated.  
- Relaxation parameters (e.g., tolerances for path cancellation) are not systematically defined and lack principled selection criteria, threatening robustness and enabling overfitting.  
- The algorithm description is missing (Table 1 absent/mislabeled) and experimental tables are misreferenced, reducing reproducibility.  
- Key parameters (ε, δ) are undefined and must be explicitly defined in the text and tables.  
- Experimental setup omits critical details (dataset sizes, causal graph structures), impeding replication and interpretation.  
- Originality is moderate: the synthesis of techniques is interesting but insufficiently distinguished from prior work and existing relaxation methods.  
- The method could be significant for cases violating faithfulness, but current evaluation is limited and does not demonstrate generality.  
- Experiments are confined to synthetic, minimal (3-node) graphs and lack testing on diverse graph types, real-world datasets, and parameter sensitivity analyses.  
- Required revisions: justify inverse Entner’s Rule use, define/select relaxation parameters, correct presentation issues, expand empirical evaluation, and sharpen comparisons to prior work.  
- Recommendation: weak accept pending major revisions—the idea has merit but needs stronger theoretical justification, clearer presentation, and more comprehensive experiments.
