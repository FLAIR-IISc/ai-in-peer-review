- Paper tackles ACE estimation between binary variables when covariate causal relationships are incompletely specified.  
- Builds on Entner et al. (2013) covariate selection rule and reframes it to produce bounds (not point estimates) using ideas from Ramsahai (2012).  
- Core idea appears sound, promising, and practically relevant, though the reviewer didn’t fully verify all technical details.  
- Unclear how Entner et al.’s conditions function in the bounding approach without the faithfulness assumption; their link to the causal structure is not established.  
- The paper avoids detailing whether those conditions guide selection of relaxation parameters, suggesting the proposed machinery may not fundamentally depend on them.  
- Reviewer requests clarification on whether experimental robustness arises from this apparent independence.  
- Comment that the phrasing “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is counterintuitive; unfaithfulness usually implies extra independencies, and the framework seems to limit dependence rather than characterize violations.  
- Suggests adding a performance comparison with the original Entner et al. method (using conservative thresholds and treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds).  
- Notes interpretation issue: example where X=1 (flu shot) and Y=1 (non-hospitalization) makes a negative ACE appear counterintuitively desirable.  
- Minor error: table numbering mismatch—“Table 1” above page 8 is referenced as “Table 5” in the main text.  
- Overall judgment: valuable contribution despite technical complexity.
