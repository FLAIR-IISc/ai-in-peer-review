- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to derive bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) under unmeasured confounding.  
- Inverts Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to treat witnesses as instrumental variables rather than discarding them.  
- Relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow controlled path cancellations, enabling conservative bounds when faithfulness may fail.  
- Formulates bounds using constrained linear programming and Bayesian posterior inference with constrained Dirichlet priors.  
- Builds directly on Entner et al. (2013) and Ramsahai (2012), extending point estimates to rigorous bounds that tolerate faithfulness violations.  
- Validated on synthetic data and a real influenza vaccination study, showing tighter bounds than standard IV methods.  
- Demonstrates computational feasibility via symbolic algebraic relaxations and a back-substitution algorithm for efficiency.  
- Technically sound contribution: formalizes faithfulness relaxation and provides a Bayesian scheme to avoid hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Needs clearer justification linking the inverted Rule 1 to the specific causal graph (Figure 1(d)) and explicit connection to Figure 2.  
- Lacks a principled methodology for selecting relaxation parameters (e.g., default w=x=y=0.2) based on data or prior knowledge.  
- Minor clarity issues: misreferenced Algorithm/Table numbers and ambiguous parameter definitions in the tables.  
- The back-substitution algorithm is valuable, but the motivation for moving from numerical LP to symbolic bounds should be more explicit.  
- Originality: novel use of inverted conditional independencies and faithfulness relaxation; situates well among prior bounds literature.  
- Significance: provides practical, narrower bounds in real data (e.g., influenza study) and scalable, open-source implementation, though more diverse real-world validation would strengthen claims.
