- Paper tackles ACE estimation between two binary variables with partially unspecified covariate causal structure, adapting Entner et al. (2013) covariate selection to bound ACE using Ramsahai (2012) techniques.  
- Core idea—using the Entner et al. rule to produce ACE bounds rather than point estimates—is interesting and potentially useful.  
- Major confusion: the role of Entner et al. conditions without the faithfulness assumption is unclear, since those conditions no longer imply specific causal structure.  
- The reviewer speculates the conditions might guide selection of relaxation parameters, but the paper postpones this discussion.  
- It appears the method’s machinery may not critically depend on satisfying Entner conditions; clarification is needed on whether observed robustness stems from this decoupling.  
- Key question: why follow the Entner rule in a bounds-setting without faithfulness—does it still heuristically identify “good” covariate sets?  
- Minor comment 1: wording “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is counterintuitive; better to frame as bounding dependence strength/near-violations.  
- Minor comment 2: experiments should include comparison to original Entner et al. (2013) method using strict thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds baseline.  
- Minor comment 3: vaccine example wording is misleading—negative ACE would mean vaccination increases hospitalization; rephrase.  
- Minor comment 4: fix table citation inconsistency (Table 1 vs Table 5).  
- Overall: promising contribution for causal inference under limited causal knowledge; addressing the clarification about Entner conditions and the minor fixes would strengthen the paper.
