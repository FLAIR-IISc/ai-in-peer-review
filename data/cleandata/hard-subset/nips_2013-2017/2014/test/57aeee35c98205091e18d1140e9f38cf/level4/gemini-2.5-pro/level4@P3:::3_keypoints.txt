- Introduces WPP: computes ACE bounds by relaxing faithfulness using witness-based covariate selection, relaxation parameters (w,y,x,β,β̄), LP constraints, and outputs a posterior over bounds with an algebraic speedup.
- Strength: technically sound synthesis of covariate selection, IV analysis, and LP to quantify uncertainty from faithfulness violations.
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation uses an inappropriate "error" metric for comparing intervals to point estimates; trivial wide intervals would score perfectly.
- Major weakness: no clear, practical guidance for choosing relaxation parameters; selection deferred to supplement/future work limits applicability.
- Clarity: overall well-written background, but methodology section is dense and lacks sufficient intuition and step-by-step explanation for implementation from the main paper alone.
- Originality: novel idea to start from witness search and systematically relax implied independence constraints, building on Entner et al.
- Significance: addresses an important problem and could spur research on robust causal inference, but immediate practical usefulness is questionable.
- Empirical concern: produced bounds are often very wide (e.g., vaccine effect [-0.10, 0.17]), reducing usefulness for decision-making and interpretation.
- Output ambiguity: providing a distribution over bounds without guidance on interpreting points within those bounds limits practitioner adoption.
- Argument for acceptance: topical problem, novel principled synthesis, conceptual advance in parameterizing faithfulness violations, potential impact.
- Argument against acceptance: flawed evaluation, unclear practical utility due to wide/complex outputs, incomplete methodology on parameter selection.
- Suggestion: replace "unstable" with "unverifiable" or "potentially violated" when describing faithfulness assumptions.
- Clarification: explicitly state that witness W identifies the admissible set Z and is not itself used for back-door adjustment in Entner et al.'s formulation.
- Request: add intuition for parameters (η, δ) and constraints (3–6) in causal-graph terms to aid understanding.
- Request: concretely explain how background knowledge should be used (e.g., to set relaxation parameters).
- Related work: discuss and cite bounding confounding literature (e.g., Geiger, Janzing, & Schölkopf UAI 2014) when claiming novelty of algebraic bounds.
- Evaluation improvement: measure trade-off between interval width and coverage frequency rather than the current "error" metric.
- Robustness check: examine effect of smaller sample sizes (e.g., N=500) since many real studies are smaller and bounds may widen.
- Table 3 critique: low average error for WPP likely reflects wide intervals that contain the truth, not demonstrated superiority over point methods.
- Tone advice: avoid emotive language in the conclusion and present WPP as a robustness-focused alternative in neutral, scientific terms.
