This paper proposes a novel approach to bounding the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. Its core principles involve applying Entner's Rule 1 in reverse, relaxing the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellation to some degree, and introducing assumptions formalized as inequality constraints. The authors formulate a computational scheme for deriving the ACE bound using linear programming, based on these principles. While the algorithm has not yet been optimized, the resulting bounds appear narrower and more accurate than those produced by existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Quality**

The authors attempt to establish a new method for deriving more accurate ACE bounds than currently available. They successfully combine several plausible principles to achieve tighter bounds, making the content of the paper quite interesting.

However, some of the criteria used to define the bounds are not fully explained. In particular, the validity of applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is not clearly discussed. Its use in this direction implicitly assumes the structural conditions depicted in Fig. 2, and this should be explicitly stated and justified.

Furthermore, the paper does not provide general guidelines for choosing appropriate parameters for the relaxation step. As these parameters significantly influence the resulting bounds, the principles governing their determination require further investigation and inclusion in the work.

**Clarity**

The paper’s theoretical explanations are generally comprehensive, aside from the critical discussion point mentioned above. 

However, the presentation of the experimental results is lacking in detail. The implementation of the algorithm is not described thoroughly. The parameters presented in Table 1 (or potentially Table 5 – see below) are not clearly defined. 

Additionally, there are some typographical errors. For example:

*   “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1” on page 6 – I could not locate a table detailing the algorithm.
*   “Results are summarized in Table 5” on page 8 – I could not locate Table 5 (it may be a reference to Table 1).

**Originality**

The problem addressed by this paper has been investigated in previous work, as the authors acknowledge. Consequently, the scope of this work does not demonstrate exceptionally strong originality. 

The idea of unifying these three concepts—reverse application of Entner's Rule 1, relaxation of faithfulness, and inequality constraints—does represent some originality. However, as noted previously, the validity of this combination is not fully elucidated.

**Significance**

This method demonstrates potential significance in terms of deriving tighter ACE bounds. However, its performance has been evaluated using a limited set of experiments. Its generality, both theoretically and through broader experimentation, has not been rigorously assessed. The paper tackles an important problem – evaluating the bounds on the strength of causal relationships – but the validity of the presented approach requires more robust justification, both theoretically and empirically.
