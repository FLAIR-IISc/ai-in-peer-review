- Proposes a new method for ACE bounds combining inverse Entner's Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing path-cancellation), and inequality constraints, solved via linear programming.
- Preliminary results indicate substantially narrower and more accurate bounds than current state-of-the-art.
- LP formulation is computationally tractable and potentially a significant advance if assumptions are valid.
- Critical concern: inverse application of Entner's Rule 1 is non-standard and lacks rigorous justification and stated structural assumptions.
- Method is highly sensitive to relaxation parameters, yet the paper gives no principled guidance or data-driven procedure for choosing them.
- Empirical evaluation is limited; robustness across a wider range of causal graphs and data-generating processes is unestablished.
- Presentation lacks clarity on key theoretical points and omits definitions of parameters shown in Table 1, hindering reproducibility.
- Novelty stems from combining three ideas into one framework, but the combination’s validity is insufficiently justified.
- Implementation is not fully optimized and experimental implementation details are sparse.
- Minor issues: typos and incorrect/missing table references (e.g., Table 1 and Table 5).
- Conclusion: interesting and promising but not ready for publication—requires stronger theoretical proofs, parameter-selection guidance, and broader validation.
