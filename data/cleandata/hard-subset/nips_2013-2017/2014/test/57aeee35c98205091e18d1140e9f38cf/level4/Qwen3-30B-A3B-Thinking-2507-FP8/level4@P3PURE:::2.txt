

Review:  

This paper proposes a new approach to derive bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable \(X\) to another variable \(Y\). The core principles involve (1) applying Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, (2) relaxing the faithfulness assumption to permit partial path cancellation, and (3) introducing inequality constraints to represent auxiliary assumptions. The authors formulate a linear programming-based computation scheme for deriving ACE bounds under these principles. Although the algorithm has not yet been optimized, the resulting bounds appear notably tighter and more accurate than state-of-the-art approaches.  

**Quality**  
The authors present a novel method for deriving more precise ACE bounds. By integrating feasible theoretical principles, they successfully achieve tighter bounds, rendering the paper’s content highly interesting. However, key aspects require clarification. Specifically, the validity of applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is inadequately justified. To support this, the authors must explicitly assume the causal structure depicted in Figure 2 and thoroughly discuss its necessity. Additionally, the paper lacks generic criteria for selecting relaxation parameters, which significantly impact bound accuracy. A principled framework for parameter selection should be developed and incorporated.  

**Clarity**  
The theoretical exposition is largely comprehensive, except for the unresolved issues noted above. The experimental section, however, suffers from poor description. The algorithm implementation is not sufficiently explained, and the parameters in Table 1 (referenced as Table 5 on page 8) are ambiguously defined. Furthermore, typographical errors are present:  
- "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" (page 6) — no algorithm table is provided.  
- "Results are summarized in Table 5" (page 8) — Table 5 is absent (likely a reference to Table 1).  

**Originality**  
While the problem setting overlaps with prior work (as acknowledged by the authors), the paper lacks strong originality in scope. The unification of three distinct ideas into a cohesive framework holds some novelty; however, the validity of this combination is not rigorously established, as discussed under *Quality*.  

**Significance**  
The method demonstrates significance in deriving tighter ACE bounds. Yet, its empirical validation is limited to a narrow set of experiments, and its theoretical generality has not been rigorously assessed. The paper addresses an important problem—evaluating causal strength bounds—but the theoretical and experimental validity of the proposed approach requires deeper justification.
