- Proposes a novel method to estimate bounds on the Average Causal Effect by reinterpreting Entner et al.’s witness-based admissible-set search as generating candidate instrumental variables.  
- Integrates bounded violations of faithfulness as constraints into a linear programming framework to derive posterior distributions over ACE bounds.  
- Introduces an algebraic approximation to the LP-derived bounds to improve computational efficiency.  
- Methodologically strong and technically sound, combining causal discovery, IV ideas, and LP for a principled compromise between strict faithfulness and wide IV bounds.  
- Core modeling choice—treating a witness W as an instrument—lacks explicit theoretical justification and an assumed causal structure is not clearly stated.  
- Selection of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) is critical but the paper provides little principled guidance or sensitivity analysis for choosing them.  
- Experimental section is under-detailed and contains presentation errors (misreferenced tables, unclear parameter definitions), harming reproducibility.  
- Original in its synthesis of existing techniques, but the novelty would be stronger with clearer theoretical underpinnings.  
- Potentially significant for practitioners, but empirical validation is limited (few synthetic scenarios and one real dataset), so broader evaluation is needed.
