- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to bound ACE under possible violations of faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Addresses a timely and interesting problem with a principled mathematical approach to quantify weak unfaithful interactions.
- Outputs a distribution over bounds tied to relaxation parameters, making interpretation of results challenging.
- The likelihood of unfaithful instances (and thus relevance of bounds) is unclear, reducing practical interpretability.
- Many relaxation settings produce wide bounds, limiting the method’s actionable utility.
- Method does not provide a single point estimate within intervals, though users may expect one.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced, undermining empirical support for the method.
- The “bias” metric used for comparison is not meaningful because competitors estimate fundamentally different quantities.
- Practical performance on the influenza dataset yields an uninformative ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64).
- Technical approach appears sound with supplementary details, but key choices (e.g., relaxation parameters) are deferred to future work.
- Paper is generally well-written but algorithmic steps and parameter explanations are hard to follow; conclusion needs improvement.
- Method is an original combination of existing techniques and related to recent work, potentially fostering cross-fertilization.
- Significance lies in the ideas and direction offered, but the current form is unlikely to be a finished solution for practitioners.
- Overall: interesting and relevant contribution with conceptual promise but limited practical impact and poor experimental validation.
