This paper proposes a novel approach to derive the bound of the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. Its main principle is the use of Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption to introduce the possibility of path-cancellation at some degree, and the introduction of assumptions represented by some inequality constraints. A computational scheme of the ACE bound is formulated by using linear programming under these principles. Although the authors have not optimized the algorithm to derive the bounds yet, the resulting bounds seem very narrow and accurate in comparison with other approaches in the state of the art.

Quality
The authors attempted to establish a novel method to derive more accurate bounds of ACE than the state of the art. They combined several feasible principles and succeeded in providing tighter bounds. In this regard, the content of the paper is very interesting. However, some criteria to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained. Particularly, the validity of the use of Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed. For its use in the inverse direction, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig.2. This should be clearly explained and discussed.

Furthermore, the generic criteria to choose appropriate parameters for relaxation are not provided. Since these parameters significantly affect the result of the bounds, the principle to determine the parameters should be more investigated and included in the work.

Clarity
The explanation of the paper on the theoretical part seems basically comprehensive, except for the essential discussion pointed out above. However, the demonstration of the experimental results is not well described. The actual implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained. The definition of the parameters shown in Table 1 is not clearly given. Additionally, there are some typos, such as "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" on page 6, where no table of the algorithm is provided. Similarly, "Results are summarized in Table 5" on page 8 refers to a non-existent table, possibly meant to be Table 1.

Originality
The problem setting of this paper has been addressed by some earlier work, as the authors pointed out. In this regard, the scope of this paper does not have very strong originality. However, the idea to combine the three ideas in a unified shape contains some originality. But, as pointed out above, the validity of the combination is not very clearly discussed.

Significance
In terms of deriving tighter bounds of ACE, this method shows some significance. However, its performance has been checked by quite limited experiments. Its generality in both theory and wide experiments has not been strictly assessed. The paper addresses an important problem, which is to evaluate the bound of the strength of causal relations. However, the validity of the presented idea should be more well described in both theory and experiments.

Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance:

Pro:
- The proposed method provides tighter bounds of ACE compared to other approaches in the state of the art.
- The idea of combining Entner's Rule 1, relaxation of faithfulness assumption, and inequality constraints is novel and shows some originality.

Con:
- The validity of the use of Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed.
- The generic criteria to choose appropriate parameters for relaxation are not provided.
- The experimental results are not well described, and the implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained.
- The paper lacks strict assessment of its generality in both theory and wide experiments.
