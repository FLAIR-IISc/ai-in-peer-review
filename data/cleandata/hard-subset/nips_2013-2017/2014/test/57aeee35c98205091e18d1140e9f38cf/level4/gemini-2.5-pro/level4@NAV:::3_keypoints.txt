- Proposes WPP: linear-programming method to bound ACE while relaxing faithfulness via explicit relaxation parameters.
- Timely and relevant problem; principled synthesis of established optimization and causal techniques.
- Technically sound with many details in supplement, but some critical parts (e.g., parameter selection) deferred to future work.
- Novel combination of ideas distinct from but related to recent work; could spur further research.
- Output is hard to interpret: yields a distribution over bounds depending on unfaithful relaxations.
- Practical relevance unclear because the likelihood of unfaithful instances that produce those bounds is not addressed.
- Many relaxation settings produce bounds that are too wide to be useful in practice.
- Method does not provide a preferred point estimate within intervals, risking misinterpretation by practitioners.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced; key metric ("bias") is inappropriate for comparing WPP to competitors.
- Real-world influenza example yields an uninformative ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64), raising concerns about utility.
- Back-substitution refinement step's practical efficacy is questioned and not sufficiently evaluated.
- Dependence of admissible sets Z on relaxation parameters and relationship between search and bounds computations need clearer explanation.
- Paper is reasonably well-written overall but has unclear sections, abrupt abstraction shifts, and implementation details that may require the supplement.
- Selection and interpretation of relaxation/constraint parameters are insufficiently explained and could be misread.
- Some claims overlook related literature (e.g., UAI 2014 work) and should be contextualized.
- Several phrasing issues, ambiguous statements, and minor typos (including in references) need correction.
- Emotive language comparing methods (e.g., "risky Bayesian approaches", "more honest") introduces potential bias and should be toned down.
- In current form, WPP is more a promising direction than a ready-to-use tool for practitioners.
