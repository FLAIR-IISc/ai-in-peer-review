- Proposes repurposing Entner et al.'s back-door covariate selection rule as a heuristic to generate bounds on the ACE rather than a point estimate.  
- Key innovation: relaxes the faithfulness assumption and frames ACE bounding within linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Introduces the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) to trade off between precise but assumption-heavy estimates and wide but assumption-light bounds.  
- Develops a Bayesian framework for inference on bounds and derives algebraic approximations for computational efficiency.  
- Strength: pragmatic, novel approach that aims to be more robust to near-violations of faithfulness in finite data.  
- Strength: thoughtful navigation of the trade-off between informativeness and assumption strength.  
- Central weakness: unclear justification for using Entner et al.'s (W,Z) selection when faithfulness is relaxed—why is this selection better than arbitrary choices?  
- Question: Does satisfying Entner’s conditions (even approximately) inform the choice of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), and if so, how?  
- Concern: the bounding procedure seems independent of the selection rule, raising doubt about the rule’s necessary role.  
- Suggestion: clarify terminology distinguishing exact unfaithfulness from approximate/weak independencies and how the framework handles them.  
- Suggestion: include experimental comparison to the original Entner et al. method (treating “I don’t know” as bounds [−1,1]) to benchmark robustness and informativeness.  
- Minor corrections: clarify sign/interpretation of ACE in the influenza study and fix table citation errors (e.g., Table 5 vs. Table 1).
