- Introduces a novel method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints.  
- Formulates ACE bounding as a linear programming problem and reports preliminary, narrower bounds than prior methods.  
- Addresses an important problem with a potentially significant combination of feasible principles for tighter bounds.  
- Insufficient theoretical justification, especially for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction and its implicit causal-structure assumptions.  
- The requirement of a specific causal structure (e.g., Figure 2) should be stated explicitly and its implications discussed.  
- No principled guidance for selecting parameters that relax faithfulness, limiting practical applicability and reproducibility.  
- Limited empirical evaluation; generality and robustness across diverse scenarios are not adequately assessed.  
- Clarity is weakened by critical theoretical omissions and poorly described experimental implementation.  
- Reproducibility issues: algorithm implementation details and parameter definitions (e.g., Table 1) are unclear or missing.  
- Manuscript contains typographical errors and incorrect cross-references that need correction.  
- Originality stems from combining three distinct ideas into one framework, but the combination’s validity is not rigorously justified.  
- Conclusion: promising approach but requires stronger theoretical grounding, parameter-selection principles, and more thorough empirical validation.
