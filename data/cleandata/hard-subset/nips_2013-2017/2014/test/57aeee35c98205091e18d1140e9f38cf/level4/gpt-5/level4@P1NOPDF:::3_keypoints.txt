- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to bound ACE while allowing controlled faithfulness violations via relaxation parameters and linear programming.  
- Timely, principled attempt to quantify weak-faithfulness violations and solve the resulting optimization problems with standard tools.  
- Conceptual interpretability concerns: outputs are distributions over bounds and the likelihood of unfaithful instances is unspecified, making relevance unclear.  
- Many relaxations produce very wide bounds of limited practical value; the method does not provide a preferred point estimate and intervals may be misinterpreted.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced; the “bias” metric used to compare methods is inappropriate and comparisons are therefore not meaningful.  
- Influenza dataset results yield overly wide ACE bounds (e.g., (−0.23, 0.64)) that support no actionable conclusions.  
- Technically sound with extensive supplement material, but crucial practical issues—especially how to choose relaxation parameters—are deferred to future work.  
- Clarity problems: overall well written but the algorithm’s variants and some steps are hard to follow and the main text may not suffice for implementation.  
- Originality: a novel combination of familiar techniques, related to recent work and potentially a source of cross-fertilization.  
- Significance: conceptually interesting but not yet practitioner-ready; some ideas could inform future methods.  
- Summary judgment: relevant and solid work likely to attract attention, but practical utility and interpretability of the output are limited and experiments are poor.  
- Numerous specific editorial and clarity suggestions (wording, missing explanations, parameter interpretation, and a typo in ref. 9).
