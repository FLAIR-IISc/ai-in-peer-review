- Introduces WPP algorithm to compute ACE bounds under potential violations of faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Timely and interesting contribution addressing robustness to unfaithfulness in causal inference.
- Principled approach that quantifies impact of weak unfaithful interactions with established optimization tools.
- Practical interpretation is challenging: outputs are distributions over bounds tied to relaxation choices whose likelihood is unclear.
- Many relaxation choices produce wide bounds, reducing practical usefulness.
- Method does not provide a preferred point estimate within intervals, though users may expect one.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced.
- The ‘bias’ metric used for comparisons is problematic because it measures fundamentally different quantities.
- Real-data example (influenza) yields a wide ACE interval (−0.23, 0.64), offering limited practical conclusions.
- Technically sound with detailed supplementary material, but key aspects (e.g., selecting relaxation parameters) are deferred to future work.
- Algorithmic steps and parameter modifications are hard to follow; full implementation from the paper alone may be difficult.
- Method appears novel as a combination of existing techniques and related to recent work in the area.
- Significance lies in idea and direction rather than being an immediate definitive solution for practitioners.
- Overall: relevant and potentially influential, but current applicability and experimental validation need improvement.
