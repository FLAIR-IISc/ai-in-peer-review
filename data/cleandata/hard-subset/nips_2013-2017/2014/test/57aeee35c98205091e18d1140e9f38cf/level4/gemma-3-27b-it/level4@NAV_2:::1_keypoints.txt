- Tackles estimating average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables with unobserved confounding, relaxing reliance on fully specified causal structures.  
- Builds on Entner et al.’s Rule 1 and leverages Ramsahai (2012) to bound ACEs rather than obtain point estimates.  
- Core idea—using conditional independence constraints to derive ACE bounds under possible faithfulness violations—is interesting and practically valuable.  
- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) framework and linear-programming relaxations; conceptually sound.  
- Central clarification needed: the role of Entner et al.’s Rule 1 conditions is unclear when faithfulness is relaxed.  
- Unclear whether method performance depends critically on Rule 1 holding, or whether observed robustness is independent of it; linkage to relaxation parameters is postponed.  
- Minor wording issue: unfaithfulness should be described as additional independencies rather than “violations” of existing ones.  
- Experimental comparison should include Entner et al.’s original method with strict Rule 1 thresholds (reporting uninformative bounds when criteria fail) as a baseline.  
- Empirical description error: influenza experiment’s sign interpretation—if Y=1 means not hospitalized, a negative ACE implies harm, not benefit.  
- Citation/presentation error: “Table 1” is incorrectly referred to as “Table 5” on page 8.  
- Technical details are complex and sometimes hard to follow; parts need clearer exposition.  
- Overall assessment: worthwhile contribution with promising empirical results and potential for further development.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel bounding approach under relaxed assumptions, practical relevance, clear WPP framework, promising synthetic and real-data results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear connection to Entner et al.’s Rule 1, difficult-to-follow technical details, and minor presentation errors.
