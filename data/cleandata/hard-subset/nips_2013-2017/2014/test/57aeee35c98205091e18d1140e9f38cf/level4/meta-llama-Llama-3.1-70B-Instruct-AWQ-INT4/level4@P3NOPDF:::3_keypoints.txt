- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to estimate ACE bounds under faithfulness violations using relaxation parameters and linear programming.

- Problem is timely and relevant; approach is interesting and principled but it's unclear if it captures the intended impact.

- WPP output is conceptually hard to interpret: a distribution over bounds tied to unfaithful relaxations with unclear likelihood and relevance.

- Many relaxations yield wide, uninformative bounds; method offers no preferred point estimate.

- Experimental evaluation is weak and unbalanced; the chosen "bias" metric is meaningless for comparing WPP to competitors.

- Unclear whether WPP is a useful tool for practitioners or a complicated distraction.

- Influenza dataset results (ACE interval -0.23, 0.64) are uninformative and concerning for practical use.

- Method is technically sound with detailed supplement, but overall context is sometimes lost.

- Choosing relaxation parameters is a critical practical issue that is deferred to future work.

- Paper is decently written but algorithmic steps and parameter variants are hard to follow; implementation clarity is questionable.

- Method is an original combination of familiar techniques and may stimulate cross-fertilization in the field.

- Significance lies in ideas and direction rather than a ready-for-practice solution.

- Summary: relevant and interesting idea, but limited current application, poor experiments, and unclear interpretation of results.

- The name "Witness Protection Program" is catchy and memorable.

- Term "unstable" is inaccurate; interactions are stable but may not hold.

- The cartoon representation is unclear and should be improved.

- The assumption of causal sufficiency or W -> X is not explicitly stated.

- The role of witness W in identifying the adjustment set Z needs clarification.

- The phrase "gives greater control over violations of faithfulness" is too vague and should be rephrased.

- Section 4 makes a sudden abstraction leap that may confuse less familiar readers.

- Provide the original referenced introduction (1) for clarity.

- Constraint (relaxation) parameters need clearer explanation to avoid misinterpretation.

- The relation between the search phase and the bounds computation should be explicitly described.

- Clarify how admissible set Z identification depends on relaxation parameters θ.

- Several sentences/phrases are unclear (e.g., "Background ... used here", "max. 10 free parameters...") and need rephrasing.

- Claiming "no previous analytical bounds" is inaccurate; related prior work (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI2014) exists.

- Discuss the efficacy of the back-substitution process in refining bounds in more detail.

- Explain why there are multiple bounds (due to different admissible sets for different witnesses).

- The impact of dataset size on bound tightness should be discussed, especially for small medical trials.
