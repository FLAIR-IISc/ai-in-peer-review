- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), an LP-based method that computes ACE bounds under controlled violations of faithfulness via relaxation parameters.  
- Timely and relevant problem: relaxing faithfulness in causal inference is of growing interest.  
- Novelty: principled, optimization-based approach that explicitly quantifies unfaithful interactions; combines existing techniques in a new way.  
- Major concern — interpretability: bounds depend on user-chosen relaxations with little guidance on plausibility, making results hard to interpret or use.  
- Major concern — utility: many relaxation settings yield overly wide, uninformative bounds (e.g., ACE ∈ (-0.23,0.64)), limiting practical use.  
- Major concern — risk of misinterpretation: method provides intervals without a preferred point estimate, risking readers treating them like confidence intervals.  
- Experimental weakness: comparing WPP bounds to point-estimate methods using 'bias' is inappropriate and misleading.  
- Experimental weakness: results presentation (e.g., Table 1) is unclear, preventing assessment of WPP’s advantage over standard estimates.  
- Experimental weakness: claim of “stability” on unsolvable problems masks the production of very wide, uninformative bounds.  
- Experimental weakness: experiments use large samples (n=5000); need analysis of performance and bound width at smaller, realistic sample sizes.  
- Quality/clarity: technical approach seems sound but key details are deferred to supplements and the algorithm description is hard to follow.  
- Quality/clarity: selection and interpretation of relaxation parameters θ is critical but left to future work, undermining assessment of method.  
- Significance: promising research direction likely to stimulate further work, but current implementation is not yet a practical tool.  
- Recommended improvements: stronger experiments comparing to credible/confidence intervals and exploring sample-size sensitivity.  
- Recommended improvements: provide substantive guidance on interpreting bounds and choosing relaxation parameters.  
- Recommended improvements: clarify algorithmic exposition and add intuition for constraint parameters and admissible-set search.  
- Recommended improvements: tone down emotive language in conclusions and avoid biased phrasing (e.g., “risky Bayesian approaches,” “more honest”).  
- Minor: clarify that witness W is only for identifying Z and not required for final ACE computation.  
- Minor: fix typo "an useful" → "a useful" and soften the claim of “no previous analytical bounds” given related work (e.g., Geiger et al. UAI 2014).
