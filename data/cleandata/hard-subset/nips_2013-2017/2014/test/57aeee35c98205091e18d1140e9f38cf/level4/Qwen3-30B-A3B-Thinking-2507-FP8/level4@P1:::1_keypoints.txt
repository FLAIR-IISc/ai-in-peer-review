- Problem: estimating the average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables when the covariate causal structure is not fully specified, avoiding reliance on faithfulness.  
- Contribution: propose the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to derive bounds (not point estimates) on the ACE by relaxing faithfulness and using Entner et al.'s rule.  
- Methodology: generate posterior distributions over ACE bounds via linear programming combined with Bayesian inference, extending Ramsahai (2012).  
- Practical benefit: provides conservative, robust causal estimates useful in observational studies where faithfulness may fail.  
- Technical soundness: approach is generally sound, well-motivated, and demonstrates utility on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Core concern: unclear connection between Entner et al.'s rule (W ⟂ Y | Z∪{X} and W ⟂ Y | Z) and the proposed relaxation framework without faithfulness.  
- Parameter issue: authors do not explain how relaxation parameters relate to Entner's conditions or how those conditions remain necessary, weakening the argument.  
- Language/clarity problem: phrase "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is misleading and should clarify they mean "almost violations" via constrained dependence magnitudes.  
- Example inconsistency: influenza vaccine example mixes up variable interpretation (X/Y) and the sign/meaning of the ACE, causing confusion.  
- Presentation errors: typographical mistake citing "Table 1" as "Table 5" and other clarity issues.  
- Missing comparison: should include Entner et al.'s method with stringent thresholds (treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds) for a fair baseline.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on clarifying the Entner-rule relationship, relaxation parameters, example correctness, and presentation fixes.
