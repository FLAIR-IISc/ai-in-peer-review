- Paper studies bounding the average causal effect (ACE) between binary variables using Entner et al.’s covariate-selection rule and a Ramsahai-inspired bounding method rather than point estimates.
- Entner et al.’s rule fundamentally relies on the faithfulness assumption, which the paper does not assume, raising questions about the rule’s implications for causal structure here.
- Main concern: without faithfulness the conditions from Entner et al. may not constrain the causal graph meaningfully; the paper should clarify how those conditions inform (or do not inform) the relaxation parameters.
- The manuscript explicitly sets aside using the rule to choose relaxation parameters, but it’s unclear whether the empirical robustness reflects this choice—clarification is requested.
- Reviewer finds the core idea sensible and potentially useful, though some technical details were hard to follow.
- Minor comment 1: phrasing “…express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence…” is awkward—unfaithfulness allows extra independences; suggest rephrasing to “allow” or “bound near-violations” of faithfulness.
- Minor comment 2: experiments should also report Entner et al.’s method with stricter thresholds, treating “don’t know” as an uninformative bound, for comparison.
- Minor comment 3: sign error in example—if X=1 is vaccination and Y=1 is not hospitalized, a negative ACE implies the vaccine is effective, not undesirable.
- Minor comment 4: text on page 8 references “Table 5” but should reference “Table 1.”
- Overall: interesting contribution but needs clearer linkage between Entner conditions and the bounding approach and clearer presentation of technical details.
