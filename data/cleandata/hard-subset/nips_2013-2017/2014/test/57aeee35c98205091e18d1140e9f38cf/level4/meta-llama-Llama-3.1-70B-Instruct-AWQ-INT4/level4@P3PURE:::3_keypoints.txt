- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute ACE bounds under relaxed faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.
- Uses relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and solves maximization/minimization LPs to obtain ACE upper and lower bounds adjusted for covariates.
- The problem is timely and the approach is principled, leveraging familiar mathematical tools in a novel combination.
- Methodological novelty is reasonable and related to recent work, but its superiority for capturing unfaithfulness is not established.
- Major conceptual concern: the output is hard to interpret because it gives a distribution over bounds tied to unfaithful relaxations whose likelihood and realism are unclear.
- Many relaxations produce wide bounds, reducing practical usefulness and offering no preferred point estimate within intervals.
- Experimental evaluation is poor and unbalanced; the bias metric used is meaningless because it compares incompatible quantities.
- Empirical performance on the influenza dataset is weak (ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64)), yielding no actionable conclusions.
- Key methodological choices, particularly how to choose relaxation parameters, are deferred to future work, leaving practical applicability unclear.
- The approach appears technically sound with supplementary details, but the paper sometimes loses overall context and omits implementational clarity.
- Algorithmic steps and variants are hard to follow; the description may be insufficient for reimplementation without the supplement.
- Doubts about the practical efficacy of the back-substitution process; exploiting constraint violations to tighten bounds may be rare.
- The method may stimulate cross-fertilization with existing techniques but is unlikely, in current form, to be ready for practitioners.
- Concern that bounds will grow larger and less informative with smaller datasets typical of medical trials.
- Table 1 and other empirical summaries do not convincingly demonstrate benefits of WPP over standard point estimates based on faithfulness.
- Claim that "WPP is quite stable" is misleading; stability may reflect overly loose bounds rather than robustness.
- Crucial interpretational warning: WPP bounds are not confidence intervals and should not be read as a confidence band around a best guess.
- Language in parts of the paper is emotive and biased (e.g., "risky Bayesian approaches", "keeps inference more honest"), which is inappropriate.
- Several presentation issues and unclear phrases hinder readability (e.g., use of 'unstable', 'cartoon representation', vague claims about "control" over faithfulness violations).
- Transitions and explanations for key parameters (pages 4, lines 172-180) are abrupt and lack intuition for readers less familiar with the topic.
- Need clarification that the witness W is used to identify Z but not needed directly for ACE computation, and how admissible sets Z depend on relaxation parameters theta.
- Numerical/notation clarifications required (e.g., maximum of 10 free parameters refers to describing set Z, not 10 variables).
- Prior work overlap: the claim of no previous analytical bounds is questionable given related papers (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI 2014).
- Miscellaneous typos and minor fixes noted (e.g., "an useful" → "a useful", "inuenza" → "influenza") and several line-specific wording suggestions.
