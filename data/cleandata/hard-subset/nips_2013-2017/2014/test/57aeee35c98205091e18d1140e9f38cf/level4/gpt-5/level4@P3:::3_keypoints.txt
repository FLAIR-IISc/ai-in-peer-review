- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE under faithfulness violations by treating a witness W as an approximate instrument and using LP/algebraic bounds with constrained Bayesian posteriors.  
- Inverts Entner–Hoyer–Spirtes Rule 1 to generate conditional-IV-like constraints and propagate relaxations into ACE bounds.  
- Encodes relaxations with parameters controlling W→Y direct effect, dependence between W and latent confounders, and discrepancies between observed and latent conditional probabilities.  
- Offers two computational routes: exact LP (polyhedral approach) and faster, looser algebraic bounds with a back-substitution procedure.  
- Provides a Bayesian treatment using constrained Dirichlet priors to obtain a posterior distribution over ACE bounds.  
- Situates the work between faithfulness-based discovery (e.g., PC) and classical IV bounding (Balke–Pearl, Dawid, Ramsahai) and relates to recent approximate-bounding efforts (e.g., Geiger et al. 2014).  
- Strength: clear focus on causal estimation under realistic faithfulness departures.  
- Strength: principled use of inequality constraints and LP geometry to translate relaxations into ACE bounds.  
- Strength: Bayesian uncertainty treatment for observed frequencies via constrained posteriors.  
- Strength: practical computational innovations (algebraic bounds/back-substitution) and open-source code with a real influenza application.  
- Weakness: interpretability—posterior interval widths depend on user-specified relaxations with little guidance for choosing them.  
- Weakness: evaluation metric (distance from interval to true ACE) can favor trivially wide bounds; lacks coverage, width-vs-coverage, decision-oriented metrics, falsification rates, and sample-size sensitivity.  
- Weakness: scope limited to binary variables and small Z; unclear scalability, computational cost, and tightness for higher-dimensional Z.  
- Weakness: claims about “greater control” need data-driven heuristics or elicitation protocols and sensitivity summaries.  
- Weakness: clarity—abrupt parameterization transitions, unclear role of each relaxation parameter, and some overstated terminology.  
- Quality: technically sound and well grounded; geometric construction and constrained Bayesian posterior are appropriate.  
- Quality caveat: empirical validation of back-substitution vs LP (runtime, mean width inflation, infeasibility frequency) is brief and should be quantified.  
- Clarity suggestions: give early intuitive explanations of relaxation parameters, clarify that W proposes Z (not used for adjustment), and outline how search over (W,Z) depends on θ and falsification works.  
- Evaluation suggestions: report coverage and width summaries, sensitivity to n (e.g., 500 vs 1000), decision-loss metrics, and falsification rates for “no witness” cases.  
- Application suggestion: for the influenza study, present sensitivity plots over θ and avoid language implying the posterior over bounds is a posterior for ACE.  
- Presentation fixes: tone down emotive phrasing, replace casual words (e.g., “cartoon”) with neutral terms, and fix minor typos.  
- Positioning: explicitly compare and cite Geiger, Janzing, Schölkopf (UAI 2014) and related inequality-bounding work to better situate contributions.  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses an important topic, thoughtful technically grounded framework, computational innovations, and open-source code.  
- Rejection arguments: empirical evaluation and guidance for parameter choice are insufficient; presentation gaps and reliance on the supplement weaken practical utility.  
- Overall: novel and timely conceptual/methodological contribution that needs clearer parameter guidance, stronger empirical validation, and improved exposition to be practically impactful.
