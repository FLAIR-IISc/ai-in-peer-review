- Paper tackles bounding the Average Causal Effect (ACE) between binary variables when covariate causal graph is incompletely known, adapting Entner et al.'s rule and Ramsahai's bounding framework.  
- Central idea is intuitive, compelling, and practically valuable, though full technical verification was not performed by the reviewer.  
- Unclear how Entner et al. conditions function within the bounding framework absent the faithfulness assumption; their causal implications here are not immediate.  
- Reviewer suspects Entner conditions might guide selection of relaxation parameters but notes authors did not address this, raising concern about dependence on those conditions and possible robustness explanations.  
- Request for authors to clarify whether the method fundamentally relies on Entner et al. conditions and why it performs well empirically.  
- Minor wording: "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is counterintuitive; suggest phrasing like "near-violations" with bounds on dependency strength.  
- Experiments should include the original Entner et al. method as a baseline, treating its "don't know" outputs as uninformative bounds.  
- Correction: interpretation of ACE on page 8 is inverted—positive ACE should indicate a beneficial vaccine effect given X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized.  
- Typo: table labeled "Table 1" is mistakenly referred to as "Table 5" on page 8.  
- Overall assessment: stimulating and important paper despite some dense technical arguments.
