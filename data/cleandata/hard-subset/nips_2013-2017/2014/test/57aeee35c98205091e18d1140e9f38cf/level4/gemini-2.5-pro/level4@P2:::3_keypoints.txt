- Proposes WPP: a method to compute ACE bounds by relaxing faithfulness using witness-based covariate selection and LP-based causal bounds.
- Introduces relaxation parameters to model near-independencies and encodes them as LP constraints to obtain upper and lower ACE bounds.
- Outputs a posterior distribution over bounds via Bayesian inference rather than a point estimate.
- Strength: Timely and relevant topic addressing robustness to faithfulness violations in causal inference.
- Strength: Novel synthesis of constraint-based discovery, instrumental-variable logic, and linear programming.
- Strength: Conceptually appealing idea of parameterizing near-independencies instead of treating faithfulness as binary.
- Weakness: Output interpretability—posterior over bounds is hard to translate into actionable decisions and risks misinterpretation.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is inadequate; the error metric rewards wide intervals and prevents fair comparison to point estimates.
- Weakness: Empirical results are weak—example interval ([-0.10,0.17]) is too wide to be useful and small-sample performance is unexplored.
- Weakness: Method feels incomplete because selection of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β) is left unspecified and is crucial to results.
- Suggestion: Clarify earlier that witness W identifies admissible set Z and isn’t used in back-door adjustment in the non-relaxed case.
- Suggestion: Provide more intuition linking relaxation parameters to causal-graph structures (e.g., weak W→Y edge or weak confounding).
- Suggestion: Specify the search procedure used in experiments to improve reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Quantify the effectiveness of the back-substitution algorithm versus the full LP (tightening and computation trade-offs).
- Suggestion: Moderate confrontational language in conclusions to maintain an objective tone.
- Overall assessment: Technically sound and original with a valuable conceptual contribution, but practical significance is limited by interpretability, weak experiments, and incomplete methodology.
