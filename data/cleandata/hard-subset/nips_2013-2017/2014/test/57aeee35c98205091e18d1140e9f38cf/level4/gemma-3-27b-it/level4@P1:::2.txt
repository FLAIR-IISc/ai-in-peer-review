This paper proposes a new approach to derive bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. Its main principle is the inverse application of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxing the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellation to a certain degree, and introducing assumptions represented by inequality constraints. A computational scheme for deriving these ACE bounds is formulated using linear programming under these principles. While the authors haven’t yet fully optimized the algorithm, the resulting bounds appear narrower and more accurate compared to state-of-the-art approaches.

**Quality**

The authors attempt to establish a novel method for deriving more accurate ACE bounds than currently available. By combining several feasible principles, they succeed in providing tighter bounds. In this regard, the paper’s content is highly interesting. 

However, some criteria for defining these bounds are not comprehensively explained. Specifically, the validity of using Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse isn’t clearly discussed. Its inverse application implicitly assumes the structure depicted in Figure 2, which should be explicitly stated and thoroughly discussed.

Furthermore, the paper lacks generic guidelines for choosing appropriate parameters for relaxation. Since these parameters significantly impact the resulting bounds, the principles for determining them should be further investigated and included in the work.

**Clarity**

The paper’s explanation of the theoretical aspects is generally comprehensive, except for the crucial discussion point mentioned above. 

However, the presentation of experimental results is not well-detailed. While the authors describe the algorithm conceptually, a comprehensive explanation of its implementation is missing. The parameters presented in Table 1 (potentially Table 5?) are not clearly defined.

Additionally, the paper contains some minor typographical errors. For example:

*   “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1.” on page 6 – No algorithm table is present.
*   “Results are summarized in Table 5.” on page 8 – Table 5 (likely Table 1) is not found.

**Originality**

The problem addressed in this paper has been previously investigated, as the authors acknowledge. Consequently, the paper’s scope doesn’t exhibit groundbreaking originality. 

The idea of unifying these three concepts—inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints—demonstrates some originality. However, as previously mentioned, the validity of this combination isn’t clearly established.

**Significance**

In terms of deriving tighter ACE bounds, this method shows promise. However, its performance has only been evaluated through limited experiments. Its generality, both theoretically and empirically, hasn't been rigorously assessed. The paper tackles an important problem—evaluating the strength of causal relationships—but the validity of the presented approach requires further clarification in both theory and experimentation.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper proposes a novel approach for bounding the ACE.
*   The method appears to produce tighter bounds than existing techniques.
*   The combination of existing techniques presents a potentially useful framework.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The theoretical justification for inverting Entner’s Rule 1 is not adequately explained.
*   The paper lacks clear guidelines for selecting relaxation parameters.
*   The experimental evaluation is limited and lacks sufficient detail.
*   The presentation contains minor errors and ambiguities.

**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend rejection. While the paper presents an interesting idea with the potential to improve ACE bounding, the current presentation lacks the clarity, rigor, and comprehensive evaluation necessary for publication at NIPS. Addressing the concerns regarding the justification of key assumptions, parameter selection, and experimental detail would substantially strengthen the paper.
