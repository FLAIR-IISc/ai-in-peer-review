- Proposes a novel method to bound average causal effect (ACE) using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, inequality constraints, and linear programming.
- Combines three feasible principles into a unified approach to derive tighter ACE bounds than prior work.
- Authors claim tighter bounds, showing potential significance for evaluating causal strength.
- The validity of using Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly justified and needs explicit structural assumptions (e.g., Fig. 2).
- The paper does not provide generic criteria or principles for choosing relaxation parameters, though these strongly affect the bounds.
- Some criteria used to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained, weakening theoretical clarity.
- The theoretical exposition is mostly clear except for the essential discussion on the inverse rule and parameter selection.
- Experimental demonstration and algorithm implementation are poorly described and lack sufficient detail.
- Definitions of parameters in Table 1 are unclear or missing.
- The paper contains typos and incorrect table references (e.g., claims about Table 1 and Table 5 that are wrong).
- Originality is limited because the problem setting overlaps prior work, though the specific combination of ideas has some novelty.
- Empirical evaluation is limited, so the method’s generality and practical performance are not thoroughly assessed.
- Overall, the idea is promising but requires stronger justification of assumptions, clearer parameter-selection guidance, and more comprehensive experiments.
