- Problem: estimates ACE between two binary variables with unknown covariate causal structure, building on Entner et al. (2013) and Ramsahai (2012).

- Contribution: relaxes faithfulness by interpreting conditional independencies as "weak" paths and derives ACE bounds via a linear programming framework ("Witness Protection Program").

- Strength: offers a pragmatic middle ground between strong unverifiable assumptions and overly wide bounds.

- Main weakness: unclear connection between Entner et al.'s selection rule and the proposed bounding parameterization once faithfulness is relaxed.

- Key omission: no principled method for selecting relaxation parameters from data; deferred discussion leaves the mapping from observed (near) independencies to concrete bounds unspecified.

- Fundamental question: unclear whether Entner's rule is essential or merely a heuristic for candidate variable selection; empirical gains may stem from the bounding method itself.

- Requested clarification: explicitly show how the selection rule informs choice of relaxation parameters to strengthen theoretical and practical claims.

- Minor 1: rephrase Section 3 to state the method models near-unfaithful distributions (bounded weak dependencies) rather than "violations of faithfulness" as currently worded.

- Minor 2: experimental baseline suggestion—include Entner et al. (2013) using their thresholds and treat "I don't know" as the interval [-1,1].

- Minor 3: clarify the definition of the outcome Y in the influenza study (what Y=1 represents).

- Minor 4: fix typo where Table 1 is referred to as "Table 5."

- Overall: novel and promising idea, but needs clearer, principled linkage between the data-driven selection rule and the bounding parameterization to improve clarity and impact.
