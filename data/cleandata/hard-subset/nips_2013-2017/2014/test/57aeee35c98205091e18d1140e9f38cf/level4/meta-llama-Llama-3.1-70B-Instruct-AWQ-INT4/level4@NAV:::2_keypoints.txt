- Proposes a novel method combining inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (path cancellation), and inequality constraints, using linear programming to compute tight ACE bounds.  
- Demonstrates remarkably narrow and accurate bounds compared to existing methods, indicating a clear contribution.  
- Quality is strong and content engaging, but some bound-definition criteria need deeper explanation.  
- Insufficient justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction; should explicitly assume the Fig. 2 structure and explain the reasoning.  
- No generic criteria given for selecting relaxation parameters, despite their large impact on results; principles for choosing these are needed.  
- Theoretical exposition is generally comprehensive except for the key justification above.  
- Experimental and implementation details are poorly described; algorithm implementation and parameter definitions (Table 1/5) are unclear.  
- Multiple typos and incorrect/missing table references (e.g., a non-existent algorithm table on p.6 and wrong table number on p.8).  
- Combining three prior ideas has some originality, but the validity and coherence of the combination are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Significance is promising due to tighter bounds, but evaluation is limited and generalizability (theoretical and empirical) is not rigorously assessed.
