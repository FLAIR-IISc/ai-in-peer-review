- Paper proposes bounding ACE using Entner et al.’s Rule 1 (W, Z) candidates with controlled relaxations of faithfulness instead of point identification.
- Method adapts binary IV linear-program bounds to include bounded violations via parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄) and optimizes ACE over a feasible parameter set.
- Two computational routes: constrained-Dirichlet Bayesian posterior intervals via rejection sampling, and a fast algebraic “back-substitution” approximation producing box bounds.
- Experiments (synthetic + influenza reminder data) show WPP gives narrower bounds than standard IV for reasonable relaxations and more robustness than faithfulness-based point estimates.
- Novel compromise between faithfulness-based point identification and worst-case IV bounds; original and well-motivated.
- Framework generalizes standard IV and chain models; relaxation parameters are interpretable for sensitivity analysis.
- Incorporating constrained Dirichlet posteriors reduces overconfidence from plug-in inequality tests.
- Algebraic bounds and back-substitution give intuition and practical speedups.
- Empirical results indicate stability when faithfulness is approximately satisfied and improvements over naive adjustments.
- Unclear formal role of Rule 1 once faithfulness is relaxed; need clarity whether Rule 1 just heuristically selects candidates or justifies parameter ranges.
- Paper defers guidance on selecting relaxation parameters, leaving a conceptual gap between Rule 1 and non-vacuous bounds.
- Need clearer sensitivity diagnostics (e.g., coverage-width curves) showing how y, x, w, β, β̄ affect bounds.
- Bayesian rejection sampling may scale poorly as constraints tighten or |Z| grows; report acceptance rates, runtimes, and consider alternative samplers.
- Method returns multiple posterior bound distributions; paper should specify aggregation/selection rules and report how often falsification discards candidates.
- Experimental baselines are limited; recommend including Entner et al.’s estimator and reporting coverage and interval widths alongside error metrics.
- Paper is technically sound but some derivations are deferred and Bayesian inference needs more algorithmic detail and diagnostics.
- Presentation is dense with notational shifts and misnumbered references/tables; readability needs improvement.
- Originality is high: inverting Rule 1 for bounding plus relaxation framework and algebraic bounds are novel contributions.
- Potentially significant: WPP could complement back-door adjustment and IV in practical observational studies.
- Pros for acceptance: innovative bridging framework, honest uncertainty quantification, and useful computational contributions.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient clarity on Rule 1’s formal role and parameter selection, limited empirical baselines/diagnostics, and presentation issues.
- Minor: Reword confusing phrasing about faithfulness violations, report Entner et al.’s method with “don’t know” as uninformative bounds, clarify sign interpretation in influenza example.
- Minor: Fix table/figure numbering, consolidate ζ/κ/ω notation, provide defaults and sensitivity for (w,x,y,β,β̄), report constrained-Dirichlet acceptance rates, and specify independence-test priors/thresholds.
