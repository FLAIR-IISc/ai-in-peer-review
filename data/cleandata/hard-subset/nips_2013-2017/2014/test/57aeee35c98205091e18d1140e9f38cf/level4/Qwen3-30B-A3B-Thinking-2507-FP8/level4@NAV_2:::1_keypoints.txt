- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to estimate bounds on ACE between binary variables by relaxing faithfulness and using a linear-programming approach to produce posterior bounds instead of point estimates.  
- Extends Entner et al.'s (2013) rule but replaces strict faithfulness-based point identification with bounded violations of local independence inspired by Ramsahai (2012).  
- Main contribution: provides a practical middle ground between faithfulness-based point estimators and instrumental-variable bounds, useful for observational studies.  
- Potentially valuable complement to existing causal inference tools for practitioners wary of faithfulness assumptions.  
- Paper is technically dense and hard to digest; the role of Entner et al.'s conditions within the relaxed-faithfulness framework is unclear.  
- The manuscript leaves open the crucial practical question of how to choose relaxation parameters for bounded violations.  
- Phrase "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is misleading; unfaithfulness usually implies unexpected independencies, not violations—better described as "approximate" faithfulness.  
- Experimental comparison should report Entner et al.'s method performance using their thresholds, treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds rather than excluding those cases.  
- Influenza case study needs clarification: if Y=1 denotes hospitalization, explain that a negative ACE means vaccination reduces hospitalization to avoid confusion.  
- Reference inconsistency: Table 1 is cited as "Table 5" in the text.
