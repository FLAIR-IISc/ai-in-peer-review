- Paper proposes bounding the average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables under partially known covariate causal structure, using Entner et al.’s covariate-selection rule and a Ramsahai (2012)-style bounding method.

- Reviewer finds the main idea sound, interesting, and practically useful, though some technical details were not fully absorbed.

- Major concern: unclear role and implications of Entner et al.’s conditions without the faithfulness assumption — the paper does not explain how these conditions inform the bounding procedure or the choice of relaxation parameters.

- Suggestion: clarify whether Entner conditions are used to set or motivate relaxation parameters, since the current presentation seems to sidestep this and the method may not critically depend on those conditions.

- Minor comment 1: wording in Section 3 about “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is confusing — unfaithfulness usually implies extra independences; please clarify intended meaning.

- Minor comment 2: experiments should include Entner et al.’s original method (with stringent thresholds and “don’t know” treated as uninformative bounds) to provide a clearer baseline comparison.

- Minor comment 3: possible sign/interpretation error on p.8 — if X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized, a negative ACE would imply the vaccine increases hospitalization; please check sign convention.

- Minor comment 4: table numbering inconsistency (table labeled “Table 1” but cited as “Table 5”) should be corrected.

- Overall: promising paper but needs a more explicit account of how Entner et al. conditions interact with relaxation parameters and the bounding results to strengthen clarity and justification.
