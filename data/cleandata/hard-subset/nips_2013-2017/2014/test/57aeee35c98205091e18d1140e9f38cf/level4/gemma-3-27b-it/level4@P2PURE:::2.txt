This paper presents a novel approach to bounding the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to Y. The core ideas involve applying Entner's Rule 1 in reverse, relaxing the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellation, and introducing inequality constraints to formalize underlying assumptions. The authors formulate a computational scheme based on linear programming to derive these ACE bounds. While the algorithm’s efficiency has not been optimized, the resulting bounds appear tighter and more accurate than those produced by existing state-of-the-art methods.

**Quality**

The authors attempt to develop a new method for obtaining more accurate ACE bounds than currently available. By combining several plausible principles, they succeed in providing tighter bounds, making the paper’s content highly interesting. 

However, the criteria used to define these bounds are not fully explained. Specifically, the justification for using Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction lacks sufficient discussion. Its application in this manner implicitly assumes the structural conditions depicted in Fig. 2, a point that should be explicitly stated and discussed. 

Furthermore, the paper does not provide general guidelines for selecting appropriate relaxation parameters, which significantly influence the resulting bounds. A more thorough investigation into the principles governing parameter selection and inclusion of those principles in the work are needed.

**Clarity**

The theoretical explanations are generally comprehensive, with the exception of the critical discussion points noted above. 

However, the presentation of experimental results is unclear. As previously mentioned, the algorithm's implementation is not described in sufficient detail. The parameters presented in Table 1 (or potentially Table 5) are not clearly defined. 

Additionally, the paper contains several typos. For example, the statement “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1” on page 6 is inaccurate, as no algorithm table appears there. Similarly, “Results are summarized in Table 5” on page 8 is likely a reference error, potentially intending Table 1.

**Originality**

The problem addressed in this paper has been investigated in prior work, as acknowledged by the authors. Consequently, the paper’s scope does not represent a substantial departure from existing research. 

The combination of the three key ideas into a unified framework demonstrates some originality. However, the validity of this combination remains insufficiently discussed, as previously noted.

**Significance**

This method demonstrates potential significance in its ability to derive tighter ACE bounds. However, its performance has been evaluated through a limited number of experiments. Its generality, both theoretically and empirically, has not been rigorously assessed. The paper tackles an important problem – evaluating the strength of causal relationships – but the validity of the proposed approach needs to be more comprehensively supported by both theoretical analysis and broader experimental validation.
