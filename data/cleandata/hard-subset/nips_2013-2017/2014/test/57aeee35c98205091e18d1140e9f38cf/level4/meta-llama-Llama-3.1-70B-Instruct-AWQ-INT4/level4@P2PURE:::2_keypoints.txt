- Proposes a novel method to bound ACE using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing path cancellation), inequality constraints, and linear programming.
- Authors have not optimized the algorithm, but the resulting bounds are narrow and appear more accurate than state-of-the-art methods.
- The validity of applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly justified; the required Fig. 2 structural assumption must be explicitly stated and discussed.
- No generic criteria or principled method are provided for choosing relaxation parameters, which strongly affect the resulting bounds.
- Theoretical exposition is generally comprehensive except for the missing justification of the inverse Rule 1 application and parameter-choice principles.
- Experimental implementation and algorithmic details are not well described, hindering reproducibility.
- Definitions of parameters in the reported tables are unclear, and the paper contains typos/misreferences to tables (e.g., “Table 1” and “Table 5” errors).
- Originality is limited because the problem setting overlaps prior work, though the unified combination of the three principles offers some novelty.
- Significance is promising—tighter ACE bounds—but experiments are limited and the generality/theoretical validity have not been thoroughly assessed.
