- Proposes a novel method to derive ACE bounds using inverse application of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing specified-degree path-cancellation), and inequality constraints, solved via linear programming.  
- Resulting ACE bounds are notably narrower and more accurate than state-of-the-art, though the bound-derivation algorithm is not optimized.  
- The inverse use of Entner’s Rule 1 is not adequately justified; the authors must assume and clearly argue the structural configuration shown in Figure 2.  
- No generic criteria or principled guidance for selecting relaxation parameters are provided despite their strong impact on bounds.  
- Experimental methodology and implementation details are poorly described; parameters in the referenced table(s) are unclear or misreferenced.  
- Typographical and table-reference errors (e.g., incorrect mentions of Table 1 vs. Table 5) reduce clarity.  
- Theoretical exposition is largely comprehensive except for the unresolved justification and parameter-selection issues.  
- Originality is limited: the problem is previously explored, though integrating three concepts into one framework is somewhat novel but insufficiently validated.  
- Significance is promising due to tighter bounds, but theoretical generality and broader experimental validation remain unassessed.
