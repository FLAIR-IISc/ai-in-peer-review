- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a novel method for estimating causal effects from observational data.  
- WPP relaxes the standard faithfulness assumption to allow for varying degrees of path cancellations that produce conditional independencies.  
- Exploits observational conditional independencies to identify “weak” confounding paths in an unknown causal graph.  
- Uses linear programming and Bayesian inference to obtain a posterior distribution over bounds on the average causal effect (ACE).  
- Relies on choosing covariate sets that block back-door paths and identify admissible adjustment sets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear technical exposition of assumptions, constraints, and derivations.  
- Method is technically interesting and an original combination of familiar techniques, with potential for extension.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited, comparisons are not comprehensive, and results are unconvincing.  
- Comparison to point estimators is problematic and the chosen evaluation metric (Euclidean distance) is inappropriate.  
- Bounds produced can be wide and potentially uninformative; practical usefulness of the intervals is unclear.  
- Method does not provide or endorse a preferred point estimate within bounds, risking misinterpretation by practitioners.  
- Key implementation choices (e.g., relaxation parameter selection) are deferred and under-specified.  
- Overall approach is technically sound but practical efficacy and robustness need stronger empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—strengthen experiments, compare to more methods, use suitable metrics, and clarify interpretation and parameter selection.
