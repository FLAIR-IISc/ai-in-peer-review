- Core idea: Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to compute ACE bounds from observational data by allowing user-defined relaxations of the faithfulness assumption and solving linear programs.  
- Originality/significance: Novel synthesis of covariate selection, instrumental variables, and LP-based bounds to quantify near-unfaithfulness.  
- Technical approach: Uses linear programming, algebraic bounds, and a back-substitution algorithm for computationally efficient bound estimation.  
- Interpretability/practical utility: Output is hard to interpret—bounds depend on user-chosen relaxation parameters and can be very wide and uninformative in practice.  
- Risk of misinterpretation: Posterior distributions over bounds may be mistaken for confidence intervals or point estimates by practitioners.  
- Experimental evaluation: Evaluation metric is inappropriate for interval methods (trivial wide intervals score perfectly), making comparisons with point estimators misleading.  
- Stability claim questioned: Observed “stability” may reflect overly wide bounds rather than method robustness; experiments use only large (n=5000) datasets.  
- Small-sample performance unknown: No results reported on smaller datasets common in practice.  
- Clarity and completeness: Methodology is hard to follow due to many interacting parameters; guidance on choosing relaxation parameters is deferred, leaving the framework incomplete.  
- Related work oversight: Paper omits discussion of closely related bounds-based work (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI 2014) and overclaims novelty about algebraic bounds in w.  
- Overall assessment: Promising and technically interesting but reads like a proof-of-concept due to weak experiments, unclear practical utility, and presentation issues.  
- Wording suggestion: Replace imprecise term “unstable” assumptions with “assumptions that may be violated in practice.”  
- Figure wording: Replace “cartoon representation” with the standard term “schematic diagram.”  
- Claim clarification: Vague claim that method “gives greater control over violations of faithfulness” needs concrete explanation.  
- Role of witness W: Clarify that W identifies admissible set Z but is not required for back-door adjustment itself.  
- Parameterization jump: Provide more intuition before introducing the parameterization in Table 1 and justify the choices.  
- Use of background knowledge: Clarify how background knowledge should be incorporated into the method.  
- Back-substitution efficacy: Demonstrate empirically or analytically how much the back-substitution step tightens bounds in practice.  
- Tone in conclusion: Remove emotive or dismissive language (e.g., “risky Bayesian approaches,” “keeps inference more honest”) and adopt a more objective tone.
