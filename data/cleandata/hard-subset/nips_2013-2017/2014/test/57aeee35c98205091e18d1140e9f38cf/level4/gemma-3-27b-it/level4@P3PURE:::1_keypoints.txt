- Paper proposes bounding the average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables using Entner et al.’s covariate selection rule without assuming full causal structure.  
- Method leverages Entner et al.’s rule together with a Ramsahai (2012)-inspired approach to derive bounds instead of point estimates.  
- Central idea is sound and potentially useful, though some technical details were not fully absorbed by the reviewer.  
- The role and implications of Entner et al.’s conditions are unclear without the faithfulness assumption and need clarification.  
- It’s unclear how the Entner et al. conditions inform the choice of relaxation parameters; the paper defers this discussion.  
- The methodology appears not to critically depend on those conditions, and experimental robustness may stem from that—this warrants explanation.  
- Minor wording: phrase about “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading; should refer to “near violations” or bounded dependence.  
- Experimental suggestion: report Entner et al.’s original method performance with their stringent thresholds and treat “don’t know” as an uninformative baseline.  
- Correction needed: interpretation of a negative ACE in the flu-shot example (X=1, Y=1 means not hospitalized) implies the vaccine increases hospitalization risk.  
- Typo/inconsistency: “Table 1” is referenced but cited as “Table 5” in the text.  
- Overall assessment: interesting paper but technically dense and challenging for quick comprehension.
