- Proposes an approach to bound ACE using Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse, relaxed faithfulness (allowing path cancellation), inequality constraints, and a linear programming formulation.  
- Algorithm not optimized, but resulting bounds appear narrower and more accurate than current methods.  
- Strength: combining multiple feasible principles yields tighter ACE bounds, making the approach interesting.  
- Major weakness: validity of using Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly justified; the required structural assumption (Fig. 2) must be explicitly stated and discussed.  
- Major weakness: no general criteria for selecting relaxation parameters, despite their strong influence on the resulting bounds.  
- Theoretical exposition is mostly comprehensive aside from the issues above.  
- Experimental presentation is poorly described: implementation details are lacking and parameter definitions (Table 1/“Table 5”) are unclear.  
- Typos and inconsistencies in table references (e.g., missing or misnumbered Table 1 and Table 5).  
- Originality is limited—the problem is well-studied—but the unified combination of the three principles offers some novelty that needs clearer validation.  
- Significance: method can produce tighter bounds, but evaluation is limited and broader theoretical and empirical assessment is needed.
