- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the average causal effect (ACE) by allowing controlled departures from faithfulness via relaxation parameters and solving a linear program.  
- Timely, relevant, and a principled way to model “near-faithful” scenarios using optimization tools.  
- Unclear whether the specific relaxation scheme realistically captures practical departures from faithfulness.  
- The resulting posterior over bounds is hard to interpret because plausibility of the assumed departures is not obvious.  
- Many relaxation settings produce very wide intervals, which may limit practical usefulness.  
- The method does not recommend a point within the interval, risking user misinterpretation as a point estimate.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak and unbalanced; the “bias” metric is not appropriate for comparing interval outputs to point estimators.  
- The influenza application yields extremely broad, non-actionable bounds (e.g., ACE in (-0.23, 0.64)).  
- Technical development appears sound, but substantial derivations are deferred to the supplement and were not verified.  
- Key choices, especially selection of relaxation parameters, are left to future work, making robustness and sensitivity unclear.  
- Paper is generally readable but has clarity problems: many parameters/variants lack intuition and the main text may not suffice for implementation.  
- The approach is original as an integration of known components and could stimulate further research.  
- Significance is mainly methodological/framing; method seems premature for routine practice but parts may become influential.  
- Reviewer requests many clarifications and fixes (notation/typos, interpretive guidance on parameters, how witness/admissible-set search interacts with bounds, empirical evidence for back-substitution, scaling to small n, relation to prior bounding work) and urges clearer interpretation to avoid treating bounds as confidence intervals.
