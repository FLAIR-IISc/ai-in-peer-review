This research paper proposes a new approach to obtain the average causal effect (ACE) of one variable X on another variable Y. Its main principles are to use Antonar's rule in the opposite direction, to relax the notion of consistency to introduce the possibility of some path cancellation, and to introduce estimates shown by some inequality constraints. A plan is formulated to calculate the ACE limit using linear programming under these principles. Although the authors have not yet optimized the algorithm to obtain the limit, the resulting limit appears to be much narrower and more accurate than other state-of-the-art approaches.

**Quality**

The authors have attempted to establish a new method to obtain a more accurate limit of ACE compared to state-of-the-art methods. They combined several viable theories and succeeded in providing a strict limit. In this regard, the content of the research paper is very interesting. However, some criteria for defining limits have not been widely explained. In particular, the validity of using Antonar's rule in the opposite direction has not been explicitly discussed. Furthermore, general criteria for choosing suitable parameters for relaxation have not been provided. Since these significantly affect the result of the limits, the principle of determining the parameters should be further investigated and incorporated into the work.
