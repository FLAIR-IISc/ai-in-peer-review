This paper proposes a novel approach to derive the bound of the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. The main principles underlying this approach are the use of Entner's Rule 1 in its inverse direction, the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption to introduce the possibility of path-cancellation to some degree, and the introduction of assumptions represented by some inequality constraints. A computational scheme for the ACE bound is formulated using linear programming under these principles. Although the authors have not optimized the algorithm to derive the bounds yet, the resulting bounds appear to be very narrow and accurate in comparison with other state-of-the-art approaches.

Quality
The authors have attempted to establish a novel method to derive more accurate bounds of ACE than the state of the art. By combining several feasible principles, they have succeeded in providing tighter bounds. In this regard, the content of the paper is very interesting. However, some criteria used to define the bounds are not comprehensively explained. In particular, the validity of using Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly discussed. To justify its use in the inverse direction, the authors should explicitly assume the structure depicted in Fig. 2. This assumption should be clearly explained and discussed.

Additionally, the generic criteria for choosing appropriate parameters for relaxation are not provided. Since these parameters significantly affect the results of the bounds, the principle for determining the parameters should be more thoroughly investigated and included in the work.

Clarity
The explanation of the paper's theoretical part seems basically comprehensive, except for the essential discussion mentioned above. However, the demonstration of the experimental results is not well described. As mentioned earlier, the actual implementation of the algorithm is not comprehensively explained. The definitions of the parameters shown in Table 1 are not clearly provided. Furthermore, there are some typos, such as "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" on page 6, where no table of the algorithm is presented, and "Results are summarized in Table 5" on page 8, where Table 5 is not found (possibly referring to Table 1).

Originality
The problem setting addressed in this paper has been explored in earlier work, as the authors pointed out. Consequently, the scope of this paper does not exhibit very strong originality. However, the idea of combining the three ideas in a unified framework contains some originality. Unfortunately, as mentioned earlier, the validity of this combination is not clearly discussed.

Significance
In terms of deriving tighter bounds of ACE, this method shows some significance. However, its performance has been evaluated through quite limited experiments. The generality of the method, both in theory and through extensive experiments, has not been strictly assessed. The paper addresses an important problem, which is evaluating the bound of the strength of causal relations. Nevertheless, the validity of the presented idea should be more thoroughly described in both theory and experiments.
