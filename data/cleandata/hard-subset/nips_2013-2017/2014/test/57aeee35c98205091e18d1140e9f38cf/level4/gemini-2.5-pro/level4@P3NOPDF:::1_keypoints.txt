- Addresses estimating ACE between two binary variables when the covariate causal graph is unknown, building on Entner et al.'s rule.  
- Main contribution: adapting Entner et al.'s rule to relax faithfulness and compute bounds (not point estimates) on ACE using Ramsahai's sensitivity framework.  
- Core idea—bounding ACE under violations of faithfulness—is interesting and practically important for robustness.  
- Reviewer finds the approach compelling but has trouble understanding how Entner et al.'s conditions function within the relaxed-faithfulness bounding framework.  
- Key confusion: whether Entner et al.'s conditions inform the choice of relaxation parameters or whether the bounding method works independently of those conditions.  
- Requests clarification on whether the bounding machinery fundamentally depends on Entner et al.'s conditions or if their role is primarily motivational.  
- If the bounding procedure is effective independently of Entner et al.'s conditions, that robustness should be emphasized.  
- Paper is promising but technically dense; clarifying the central connection would strengthen the contribution.  
- Minor comment 1: Reword "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" to reflect that unfaithfulness involves unexpected independencies/near-unfaithful distributions.  
- Minor comment 2: Improve experiments by comparing to Entner et al. with their thresholds and treat abstentions as uninformative bounds (e.g., [-1,1]).  
- Minor comment 3: Correct interpretation error on page 8 regarding sign of ACE for vaccine effect (positive ACE implies protective effect).  
- Minor comment 4: Fix citation/table reference error on page 8 ("Table 1" referred to as "Table 5").
