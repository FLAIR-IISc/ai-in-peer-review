- Proposes a new method to derive ACE bounds using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints, implemented via a linear program.  
- Resulting bounds are narrow and more accurate than state-of-the-art, though the algorithm is not yet optimized.  
- Major methodological gap: applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not justified unless the specific structure in Figure 2 is explicitly assumed.  
- The paper fails to state and discuss the necessary structural assumption (Figure 2) and its implications.  
- No generic criteria are provided for selecting relaxation parameters, even though they critically affect the bounds.  
- Experimental implementation details are insufficiently described and reproducibility is hindered.  
- Definitions of parameters in Table 1 are unclear or missing.  
- There are typographical errors and incorrect table references (e.g., missing Table 1 algorithm, erroneous reference to Table 5).  
- Originality: combining the three principles is novel, but the overall problem setting is not new.  
- Significance: approach is promising for tighter ACE bounds but lacks theoretical and empirical validation across broader settings.
