- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP): an algorithm using relaxation parameters and linear programming to compute ACE bounds under possible violations of faithfulness.  
- Strength: technically sound framework with detailed supplementary material and a novel perspective modeling unfaithfulness via relaxations.  
- Strength: Algorithm is well-motivated and appropriately connected to existing bounding/confounding literature.  
- Concern: Outputs a distribution over bounds parameterized by relaxations without quantifying how plausible those relaxation scenarios are, making results hard to interpret.  
- Concern: Wide/vacuous intervals (e.g., ACE in (-0.23, 0.64)) provide little actionable information and risk misinterpretation as point-estimate confidence intervals.  
- Concern: Experimental evaluation is flawed—comparing WPP intervals to point estimates using a “bias” metric is incommensurate and invalidates practical comparisons.  
- Concern: The influenza case study likely reflects loose bounds rather than genuine estimator stability, contradicting claims of robustness.  
- Technical gap: Relaxation parameters are under-specified (selection, justification, and the rationale for having many parameters are unclear).  
- Technical gap: Key algorithmic steps (role of witness W, admissible sets Z, back-substitution refinement) are poorly explained and hinder reproducibility.  
- Clarity issues: High-level abstractions lack intuitive explanation; several terminology and characterization errors are present.  
- Literature gap: The paper overlooks closely related analytical bounds work (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI 2014).  
- Overall: Conceptually novel and potentially valuable, but current form lacks practical interpretability and methodological rigor.  
- Recommendations: Justify and quantify relaxation choices (e.g., priors/sensitivity), use interpretable evaluation metrics, improve exposition and fix technical errors, and reframe claims toward future potential.  
- Verdict: Reject, with strong encouragement to revise and resubmit after major improvements.
