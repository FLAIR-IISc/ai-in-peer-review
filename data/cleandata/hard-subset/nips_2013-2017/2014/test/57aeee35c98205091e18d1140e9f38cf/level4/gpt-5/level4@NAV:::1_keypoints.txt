- Addresses estimating average causal effect (ACE) for binary X,Y when covariate causal relations are partially unspecified; builds on Entner et al.’s rule to select covariates and proposes bounds (not point estimates), with a method inspired by Ramsahai (2012).
- Reviewer finds the central idea coherent, interesting, and potentially useful, though technical details were not fully digested.
- Unclear role of Entner et al.’s conditions under this framework: without faithfulness they lack straightforward causal implications and the paper does not explain how they inform relaxation parameters.
- Suggests the proposed machinery may not rely critically on Entner et al.’s conditions and questions whether experimental robustness stems from that.
- Minor point: phrasing “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is odd—unfaithfulness permits extra independences; the framework likely intends “near-violations” by bounding dependence strength.
- Experimental suggestion: report performance of Entner et al.’s method with stringent thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds.
- Clarification needed (p.8): if X=1 is flu shot and Y=1 is not hospitalized, a negative ACE would not indicate a desirable vaccine—sign interpretation seems confused.
- Minor error: Table at top of p.8 labeled “Table 1” is cited as “Table 5” in the text.
- Overall judgment: interesting paper but details are hard to follow and some conceptual/notation clarifications are needed.
