- Introduces a new method to bound the Average Causal Effect (ACE) when the causal graph is unknown, repurposing Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to identify candidate instruments rather than yielding a faithfulness-dependent point estimate.
- Models violations of faithfulness as bounded "path cancellations" and derives a posterior over ACE bounds using a linear programming approach extending Ramsahai (2012) and Dawid (2003).
- Tackles a fundamental causal inference problem and offers a novel compromise between assumption-heavy point estimates and uninformative IV bounds.
- The “Witness Protection Program” framework is theoretically grounded and practically useful, with a computationally efficient back-substitution algorithm based on symbolic bounds.
- Main weakness: insufficient justification for using Entner et al.'s Rule 1 as the search heuristic once faithfulness is explicitly relaxed.
- Under relaxed faithfulness, the conditional independences in Rule 1 (W ⟂ Y | Z and W ⟂ Y | Z ∪ {X}) no longer clearly imply causal structure; the heuristic needs stronger theoretical or intuitive motivation.
- Unclear whether the method’s strong empirical performance is due to the specific search heuristic or the general robustness of the bounding machinery; this relationship should be clarified.
- Suggest rephrasing Section 3: framing the approach as relaxing strict independence constraints rather than describing faithfulness violations as local independence violations.
- Recommend including the original Entner et al. method as a baseline in experiments, treating their "I don't know" results as the uninformative interval [-1, 1].
- Point out a likely contradiction in the influenza study regarding the coding of Y (hospitalization vs. not) and the sign interpretation of ACE; request clarification.
- Report a typo: "Table 1" is mistakenly referred to as "Table 5" on page 8.
- Overall assessment: promising and impactful work, but needs a convincing justification for the search procedure and fixes to the minor issues to be a strong conference paper.
