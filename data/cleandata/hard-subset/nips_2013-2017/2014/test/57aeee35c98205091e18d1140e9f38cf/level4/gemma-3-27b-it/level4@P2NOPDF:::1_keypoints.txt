- Paper proposes bounding the Average Causal Effect (ACE) for binary variables when covariate causal structure is partially unknown, building on Entner et al. (2013) covariate selection rule.  
- Uses Entner et al.’s faithfulness-based rule to construct bounds (not point estimates), following methodology inspired by Ramsahai (2012).  
- Core idea is sound and promising, though some technical details were not fully clear to the reviewer.  
- Main concern: unclear role of Entner et al.’s conditions in the bounding framework without faithfulness — the conditions’ implications for causal structure are not established.  
- Question whether the Entner conditions actually inform selection of relaxation parameters, since that connection is postponed and the method seems not to require the conditions.  
- Experimental robustness may be coincidental or due to other factors; clarification on dependence on Entner conditions is needed.  
- Minor point 1: wording about “violations of faithfulness” is misleading — framework better described as allowing bounded/near violations (almost independence), not extra independences.  
- Minor point 2: experiments should compare to Entner et al.’s original method with strict thresholds and report “don’t know” as a baseline.  
- Minor point 3: example interpreting negative ACE for flu shot is misleading — negative ACE would mean increased hospitalization, contradicting the claimed desirable effect.  
- Minor point 4: inconsistency in table references (Table 1 vs. Table 5).
