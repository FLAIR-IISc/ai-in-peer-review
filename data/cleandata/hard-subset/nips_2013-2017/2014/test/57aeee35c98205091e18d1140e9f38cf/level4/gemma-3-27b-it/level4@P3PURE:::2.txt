This paper proposes a novel approach to bounding the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to another Y. The core principles involve applying Entner's Rule 1 in reverse, relaxing the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellation to some degree, and introducing assumptions formalized as inequality constraints. A computational scheme for deriving the ACE bound is formulated using linear programming based on these principles. While the authors acknowledge the lack of algorithmic optimization, the resulting bounds appear narrower and more accurate than those achieved by state-of-the-art methods.

**Quality**

The authors attempt to establish a new method for deriving more accurate ACE bounds than existing approaches. By combining several plausible principles, they succeed in providing tighter bounds, making the content of the paper interesting. 

However, the criteria used to define these bounds are not fully explained. Specifically, the validity of applying Entner's Rule 1 in reverse is not clearly justified. Utilizing the rule in this direction implicitly assumes the structural configuration depicted in Fig. 2, which should be explicitly stated and discussed. 

Furthermore, the paper does not provide generic guidance on selecting appropriate parameters for the relaxation step. Given that these parameters significantly impact the resulting bounds, the principles governing their determination warrant further investigation and inclusion in the work.

**Clarity**

The theoretical explanations are largely comprehensive, with the exception of the crucial discussion points noted above. 

However, the presentation of experimental results lacks clarity. The algorithmic implementation is not thoroughly described, and the parameters presented in Table 1 (and referenced as Table 5) are not clearly defined. 

Additionally, the paper contains several typographical errors. For example, the statement "The full algorithm is shown in Table 1" on page 6 is inaccurate, as no algorithm table appears there. Similarly, “Results are summarized in Table 5” on page 8 is likely a reference to Table 1.

**Originality**

The problem addressed in this paper has been investigated in prior work, as the authors acknowledge. Consequently, the scope of the paper does not represent a substantial leap in originality. 

The idea of unifying these three concepts – reverse Entner’s Rule, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints – exhibits some originality. However, as previously noted, the validity of this combination is not sufficiently substantiated.

**Significance**

This method demonstrates potential significance in deriving tighter bounds for ACE. However, its performance has been evaluated through a limited set of experiments. Its generality, both in theory and across a wider range of experimental conditions, has not been rigorously assessed. The paper tackles an important problem – evaluating the strength of causal relationships – but the validity of the proposed approach requires more thorough justification, both theoretically and empirically.
