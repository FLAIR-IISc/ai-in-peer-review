- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm to compute ACE bounds under violations of faithfulness using relaxation parameters and linear programming.  
- Timely and relevant problem; method principled in quantifying weak unfaithful interactions with optimization tools.  
- Defines relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and uses LP to obtain upper/lower ACE bounds after adjusting for covariates.  
- Output interpretability is challenging: bounds depend on unspecified 'unfaithful relaxations' whose likelihood is unclear.  
- Many relaxation settings yield excessively wide bounds, reducing practical usefulness.  
- Method produces intervals but does not provide a point estimate, risking misinterpretation by practitioners.  
- Experimental evaluation is inadequate: the reported 'bias' metric is invalid for fair comparison to competing methods.  
- Influenza dataset results are weak (ACE interval (-0.23, 0.64)), offering limited actionable insight.  
- Technical approach is sound and detailed in supplementary material, though that material is not fully verified.  
- Critical aspects like how to choose relaxation parameters are deferred, hindering assessment of pitfalls and usability.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks balance, robustness, and evidence for claims (e.g., back-substitution effectiveness).  
- Writing is generally clear but the conclusion, algorithmic steps, and parameter explanations need clarification; implementation likely requires supplements.  
- Method is an original combination of established techniques and related to recent literature, potentially fostering further work.  
- Significance lies in conceptual direction and potential influence, but current implementation is not practitioner-ready.  
- Summary: relevant and interesting method with limited current applicability, reasonable writing, sound technical ideas, but poor experiments and unclear output interpretation.  
- Numerous specific manuscript fixes requested (clarify terminology, correct typos, explain parameter meanings, and better link sections).  
- Criticisms of comparisons and phrasing: the bias comparison is misleading, claims of stability are questionable, and emotive/dismissive language should be avoided.  
- Miscellaneous: correct reference typos (e.g., 'inuenza'→'influenza') and clarify the role of witness W and admissible set Z dependence on θ.
