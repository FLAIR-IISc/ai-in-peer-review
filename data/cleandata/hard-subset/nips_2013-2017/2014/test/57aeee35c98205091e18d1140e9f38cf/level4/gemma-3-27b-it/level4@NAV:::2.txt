This paper introduces a new methodology for bounding the average causal effect (ACE) between variables X and Y. The core of the approach leverages a reversed application of Entner's Rule 1, a relaxation of the faithfulness assumption to accommodate potential path-cancellation, and the formulation of inequality constraints defining key assumptions.  A linear programming scheme is developed to compute these ACE bounds, which appear demonstrably tighter and more accurate than existing state-of-the-art techniques, although algorithmic optimization remains an open area for future work.

**Quality:**

The authors pursue a promising novel method for achieving more precise ACE bounds. Successfully integrating these principles to generate tighter bounds represents a valuable contribution and makes the paper's content compelling. 

However, the justification for certain bounding criteria requires further elaboration. Specifically, the rationale for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is not sufficiently detailed; its use implicitly relies on the structural conditions depicted in Figure 2, which should be explicitly stated and discussed. 

Furthermore, the paper lacks guidance on selecting appropriate parameters for the relaxation process.  Given the significant impact these parameters have on the resulting bounds, a more thorough investigation into their determination and inclusion in the work is necessary.

**Clarity:**

The theoretical exposition is generally comprehensive, with the exception of the crucial points noted above. 

However, the presentation of experimental results needs improvement. The algorithmic implementation is not described with sufficient detail, and the parameters defined in Table 1 (potentially a reference error as Table 5 is also mentioned) lack clear definitions. 

Minor typographical errors also detract from clarity; for example, the statement “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1” (page 6) is inaccurate as no such table is present, and the reference to “Results are summarized in Table 5” (page 8) appears to be mislabeled (likely referring to Table 1).

**Originality:**

While the problem of bounding ACE has been previously addressed, the combination of Entner's Rule 1 (in reverse), relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints presents a novel approach.  However, as previously mentioned, the theoretical validity of this specific combination requires more robust discussion.

**Significance:**

This method demonstrates potential significance in achieving tighter ACE bounds.  However, its evaluation relies on a limited experimental scope. A more rigorous assessment of its generality—both in terms of theoretical applicability and broader empirical validation—is needed. The paper tackles an important problem regarding the evaluation of causal relationship strength, but the soundness of the presented approach should be more thoroughly established through both theoretical justification and expanded experimentation.
