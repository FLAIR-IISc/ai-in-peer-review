- Introduces a novel method to derive tight ACE bounds using reverse Entner’s Rule, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints.  
- Formulates a linear programming scheme to compute ACE bounds, yielding significantly tighter bounds than prior methods.  
- Algorithm implementation is unoptimized and lacks implementation details.  
- Justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is insufficient and requires explicit structural argument using Figure 2.  
- No principled framework for selecting relaxation parameters; current choices appear arbitrary and critically affect bound quality.  
- Experimental description is incomplete, with ambiguous parameter definitions and missing/inconsistent table references (Table 1 vs Table 5).  
- Minor typos and presentation inconsistencies harm readability.  
- Integration of the three concepts is novel, but the paper fails to rigorously justify why this combination produces improved bounds.  
- Experimental validation is limited to a few synthetic scenarios, limiting claims about generalizability.  
- Overall contribution is promising but needs stronger theoretical grounding and broader empirical evaluation to substantiate claims.
