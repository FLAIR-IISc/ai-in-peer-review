- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a method for bounding ACE under possible faithfulness violations using conditional independencies.  
- Uses a linear programming framework with relaxation parameters to quantify path cancellations and generate ACE bounds.  
- Extends instrumental variable methods and builds on Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to handle imperfect faithfulness.  
- Produces interpretable bounds instead of point estimates, acknowledging uncertainty in causal assumptions.  
- Addresses an important and timely problem in causal inference with potential practical implications.  
- Methodology is well-motivated and represents a novel combination of established techniques.  
- Technical approach is sound and supported by detailed supplementary material.  
- Selection and interpretation of relaxation parameters are not fully resolved and deferred to future work.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and lacks robustness.  
- The evaluation metric (Euclidean distance to true ACE) is problematic because it conflates intervals with point estimates.  
- Some sections are difficult to follow due to complex parameter space and abrupt transitions between abstractions.  
- Key concepts like the meaning of relaxation parameters and their relation to witness identification need clearer explanation.  
- The algorithm description is detailed but would benefit from more intuitive explanation of the back-substitution process.  
- WPP is an original and meaningful extension of prior work on causal bounds and IV methods.  
- The focus on generating bounds under weak faithfulness violations distinguishes the paper from related work.  
- Current implementation may produce loose bounds, limiting immediate practical adoption.  
- Conceptual framework is valuable for incorporating uncertainty about causal assumptions into analyses.  
- Overall the paper is technically sound and conceptually interesting but experimental and interpretive aspects remain challenging for practitioners.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: wording replacements (e.g., ‘unstable’→‘unreliable’, ‘cartoon representation’→‘schematic representation’), and several phrasing clarifications.  
- Clarify assumptions and roles: causal sufficiency/graph structure assumptions and that witness W identifies admissible set Z rather than being used directly in ACE.  
- Improve transitions and linkages: smoother move to parameter constraints, link back to equation (1), and explain dependence of admissible set search on relaxation parameters.  
- Clarify whether “10 free parameters” refers to dimension of Z or number of variables.  
- Avoid overstating novelty (there may be related analytical bounds like Geiger et al., UAI 2014).  
- Practical concerns: 5000 data points may be unrealistic for many studies; Table 1 does not clearly show WPP’s practical benefit; claims of stability may be misleading given wide bounds.  
- Emphasize the important clarification about interpreting bounds, avoid emotive language, and fix minor typos (e.g., ‘inuenza’→‘influenza’).
