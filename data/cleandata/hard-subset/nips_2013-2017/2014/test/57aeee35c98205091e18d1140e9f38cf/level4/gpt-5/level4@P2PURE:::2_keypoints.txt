- Proposes a new method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using inverse application of Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints, solved via linear programming.  
- The computational scheme yields very tight and accurate bounds compared to state-of-the-art methods despite not being fully optimized.  
- Paper is interesting and successful at producing tighter ACE bounds by combining plausible principles.  
- The inverse use of Entner’s Rule 1 is not sufficiently justified; the authors should explicitly assume and discuss the structure shown in Fig. 2.  
- No general criteria are provided for choosing relaxation parameters, though these strongly affect the resulting bounds.  
- Theoretical exposition is largely comprehensive but misses the essential discussion on the inverse Entner’s Rule and parameter selection.  
- Experimental presentation and algorithm implementation lack sufficient detail; parameter definitions (e.g., in Table 1/5) are unclear.  
- Typos and inconsistencies in table references (e.g., mentions of Table 1 and Table 5 that are not present) reduce clarity.  
- Originality is limited since prior work addressed similar settings, but unifying the three principles has some novel aspects.  
- The validity of combining the three principles is not clearly discussed or substantiated.  
- Method is significant for deriving tighter ACE bounds, but empirical evaluation is limited and the generality and theoretical validity need stronger assessment.
