- Summary: Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) that inverts Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to generate IV-like constraints, relaxes faithfulness with bounded deviations, and produces ACE bounds via LP and Bayesian constrained-Dirichlet posteriors.
- Originality: Novel idea of inverting Rule 1 to obtain IV-style bounds and introducing tunable, local relaxations of faithfulness; symbolic bounds and back-substitution are methodological contributions.
- Quality: Formulation via polytopes and LP aligns with prior causal-bounds work; Bayesian constrained-Dirichlet approach appropriately captures sampling uncertainty without priors on latent structures.
- Significance: Bounding approach is useful when identification is fragile; yields tighter bounds than standard IVs in experiments and could aid practitioners.
- Clarity (high level): High-level motivation is clear and both numerical and analytical tools are provided.
- Key weakness — role of Rule 1: Unclear why satisfying Rule 1 matters once faithfulness is relaxed; need clarification on how Rule 1 influences which (W,Z) pairs are considered, which constraints are imposed, and how relaxation parameters are chosen.
- Key weakness — parameter selection: Performance depends on w, x, y, β, β̄; the paper lacks principled guidance, sensitivity analyses, and default choices tied to diagnostics.
- Key weakness — scope and scalability: Method is limited to binary variables and modest |Z|; missing discussion of runtime, scalability, and how back-substitution bounds compare to full LP as size grows.
- Key weakness — technical clarity: Notation is heavy and dependence on ζ, κ, η, ω is opaque; a diagram and a brief worked example would help.
- Technical soundness: Framework appears coherent and consistent with bounding literature; numerical procedures are standard, though full derivation verification is not shown.
- Exposition issues: Exposition is dense; main text and supplement need tighter alignment and some inconsistencies/typos should be fixed.
- Reiterated originality: The combined elements (inverted witness, bounded faithfulness violations, posterior over bounds, symbolic constraints) are novel.
- Reiterated significance: Likely useful for observational studies where IVs are weak and identification is uncertain.
- Pros for acceptance: Conceptually interesting compromise between faithfulness-based identification and IV bounds; methodologically solid with exact and approximate tools; demonstrated empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: Insufficient clarity on Entner conditions under relaxed faithfulness; limited guidance on relaxation parameter selection and sensitivity; exposition and notation need streamlining.
- Requested fix 1: Rephrase Section 3 to clarify that the paper allows bounded deviations from independence (not the usual notion of extra independencies as “faithfulness violations”).
- Requested experiment 1: Report performance of Entner et al.’s method under stringent thresholds (treating “don’t know” as uninformative) to isolate effects of the relaxations.
- Requested fix 2: Reconcile variable/sign definitions in the flu example so ACE sign interpretation is consistent and clear.
- Requested fix 3: Correct table numbering inconsistencies (e.g., Table 1 cited as Table 5).
- Requested addition 1: Provide practical guidance on computational cost, typical runtimes, and accuracy trade-offs between LP and back-substitution.
- Requested addition 2: Include sensitivity plots for the real-data example showing how bounds vary with w, x, y, β, β̄ to help calibration.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and potentially impactful contribution that would be much stronger with clearer articulation of Rule 1’s role and more guidance on choosing and presenting relaxation parameters.
