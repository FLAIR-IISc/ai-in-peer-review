This paper introduces a novel approach for bounding the average causal effect (ACE) from a variable X to a variable Y. The method leverages a reversed application of Entner's Rule 1, relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellation, and incorporates inequality constraints to define these bounds. A linear programming scheme is then formulated to compute these ACE bounds. While the authors acknowledge a lack of algorithmic optimization, the resulting bounds appear tighter and more accurate compared to existing state-of-the-art techniques.

**Quality**

The paper presents a potentially significant contribution by attempting to derive more accurate ACE bounds than currently available. The combination of Entner's Rule, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints is a promising approach, and the reported tightness of the bounds is encouraging. 

However, several aspects of the methodology require further clarification. The justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse is not adequately explained. This application implicitly assumes a specific structural relationship, as illustrated in Figure 2, and this assumption needs to be explicitly stated and thoroughly discussed. 

Furthermore, the paper lacks guidance on selecting appropriate parameter values for the relaxation of the faithfulness assumption. Given the substantial impact of these parameters on the resulting bounds, a more detailed investigation into their determination and a principled approach to their selection are crucial.

**Clarity**

The theoretical exposition is generally comprehensive, with the exception of the critical points regarding the reverse application of Entner’s Rule and parameter selection.

The presentation of experimental results, however, needs improvement. The implementation details of the algorithm are not fully described, and the parameters presented in Table 1 (and referenced as Table 5) lack clear definitions. 

Additionally, the manuscript contains minor errors. For example, the statement “The full algorithm is shown in Table 1” on page 6 is inaccurate, as no algorithm table appears there. Similarly, “Results are summarized in Table 5” on page 8 likely refers to Table 1.

**Originality**

The problem of bounding ACEs has been previously addressed in the literature, as the authors recognize. Consequently, the core problem setting itself does not represent a substantial departure from existing work. 

The originality lies in the unified combination of Entner's Rule, relaxed faithfulness, and inequality constraints. However, the validity and benefits of this combination, as previously noted, are not fully substantiated through rigorous discussion.

**Significance**

The method demonstrates potential significance in its ability to derive tighter ACE bounds. However, the evaluation is based on a limited set of experiments. A more comprehensive assessment of its generality, both theoretically and through broader experimentation, is needed. The paper tackles an important problem – quantifying the strength of causal relationships – but the validity of the proposed approach requires more robust justification in both theoretical terms and empirical demonstration.
