- Proposes a method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (allowing some path cancellation), inequality constraints, and linear programming.

- Algorithm is unoptimized but produces narrow, accurate bounds compared to state-of-the-art methods.

- The use of Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not convincingly justified; authors should explicitly assume and explain the Fig. 2 structure.

- No generic criteria are provided for selecting relaxation parameters, which crucially affect the bounds.

- Some defining criteria for the bounds and the validity of combining the proposed principles are not comprehensively explained.

- Experimental results and implementation details are poorly described; parameter definitions in the referenced tables are unclear.

- Typos and table misreferences (e.g., “full algorithm shown in Table 1” and “Results summarized in Table 5”) create confusion.

- Originality is limited because the problem setting is not new, though combining the three ideas has some novel elements.

- Significance: the method can yield tighter ACE bounds, but evaluation is limited and the generality is not thoroughly assessed.

- Overall, theoretical justification and experimental validation of the proposed assumptions need to be strengthened.
