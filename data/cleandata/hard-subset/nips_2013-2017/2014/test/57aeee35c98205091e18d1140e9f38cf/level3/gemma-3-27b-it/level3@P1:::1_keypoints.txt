- Introduces a method to bound the Average Causal Effect (ACE) under unmeasured confounding by relaxing faithfulness and using linear programming plus Bayesian inference.  
- Core idea: invert Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to use potential instrumental variables for bounding ACE rather than identifying back-door sets.  
- Novel combination of techniques (relaxed faithfulness, inverted Rule 1, LP, Bayesian) with practical focus on bounds instead of point estimates.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real-world (influenza vaccination) data supports applicability.  
- Addresses an important problem in observational causal inference and is clearly motivated.  
- Unclear justification for inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1 when faithfulness is not assumed; the causal interpretation of the rule’s conditions is not resolved.  
- Method’s reliance on Entner et al.’s conditions is questionable; authors must clarify how the method behaves without those conditions.  
- The phrase “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading; bounding dependency magnitude differs from unfaithfulness (extra independencies).  
- Lacks a direct comparison to Entner et al.’s original method (e.g., using their thresholds) as a baseline.  
- Technical soundness is uncertain due to the unclear role of Rule 1 and dependence on its conditions.  
- Presentation has minor clarity issues (e.g., ACE interpretation in the vaccination example, incorrect table citation).  
- Paper is generally well-written but technical details are hard to follow and need more intuitive explanations and examples.  
- Originality is notable but tempered by dependence on prior work and unclear core motivation.  
- Potentially significant if technical issues are resolved and robustness demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: reject now, encourage resubmission after major revisions addressing Rule 1’s role, comparisons, clarity, and robustness analysis.
