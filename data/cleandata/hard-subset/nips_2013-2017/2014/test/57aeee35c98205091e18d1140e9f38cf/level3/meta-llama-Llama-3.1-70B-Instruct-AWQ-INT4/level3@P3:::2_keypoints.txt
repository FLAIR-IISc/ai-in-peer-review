- Proposes a novel method to bound average causal effect (ACE) by combining Entner's Rule 1 (used inversely), relaxed faithfulness, inequality constraints, and linear programming.
- Method generates candidate instrumental variables by inverting Entner's Rule 1 to derive LP-based bounds on ACE.
- Provides comprehensive theoretical derivation of the linear programming formulation and relaxation parameters to model faithfulness violations.
- Introduces a Bayesian learning framework to estimate bounds while accounting for parameter uncertainty.
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show the method can yield tighter ACE bounds than existing approaches.
- Strength: offers a novel compromise between point estimators (faithfulness) and IV-style bounds, with clear exposition.
- Limitation: lacks clear justification for applying Entner's Rule 1 in the inverse direction and should explicitly assume/discuss the Fig. 2 causal structure.
- Limitation: no generic criteria or guidance for selecting relaxation parameters, which strongly influence resulting bounds.
- Limitation: potential computational expense and scalability concerns.
- Limitation: experimental implementation and algorithmic details are poorly described; referenced tables lack clear/missing parameter definitions.
- Presentation issues: contains typos and incorrect/missing table references that reduce clarity.
- Originality: builds on prior work; unified combination of ideas has some novelty but is not fully validated.
- Significance: promising due to tighter ACE bounds, but limited experiments and insufficient theoretical/empirical validation leave generality and robustness unassessed.
- Recommendations: justify inverse use of Entner's Rule 1 and Fig. 2 assumptions, provide criteria for relaxation parameters, fix implementation/table details and typos, and add more experiments/theoretical validation.
