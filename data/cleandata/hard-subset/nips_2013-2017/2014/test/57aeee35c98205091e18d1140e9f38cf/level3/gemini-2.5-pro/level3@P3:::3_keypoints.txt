- Summary: Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to compute ACE bounds under possible violations of faithfulness using linear programming and user-specified relaxation parameters, outputting a posterior over bounds.
- Strength: Tackles an important, timely problem of weak unfaithfulness in causal inference and moves beyond binary faithfulness assumptions.
- Strength: Uses a principled LP-based approach combining IV bounds and relaxed conditional independencies to derive valid ACE bounds.
- Strength: Original synthesis of Entner et al.’s covariate selection, IV bounding, and LP frameworks—novel and creative.
- Strength: Offers a valuable research direction encouraging more skeptical, robustness-focused observational causal inference.
- Weakness: Practical applicability limited because relaxation parameters (θ) are left to the user with no guidance, making the method hard to apply.
- Weakness: Paper is hard to follow; many similar parameters (η, δ, ζ, κ, ω) and unclear relationships hinder comprehension and reproducibility.
- Weakness: Connection between the (W, Z) search, bound computation, and dependence of admissible sets on θ is insufficiently explained.
- Weakness: Experimental metric (“bias”) is flawed—treats intervals and point estimates inconsistently and can reward trivially wide intervals.
- Weakness: Experiments do not clearly show when WPP improves over faithfulness-based point estimates; Table 1 summaries are uninformative.
- Weakness: Evaluation uses large sample size (N=5000); performance on smaller, typical datasets is unclear.
- Weakness: Resulting bounds can be very wide and potentially uninformative (example: [-0.10, 0.17] in influenza case).
- Weakness: Method gives no preferred point estimate, risking misuse (e.g., taking midpoints) and user confusion.
- Weakness: “Posterior distribution over bounds” is conceptually difficult and depends on unquantified likelihoods of unfaithful scenarios; not confidence intervals.
- Suggestion: Provide more intuition and practical guidance for choosing relaxation parameters and relating them to domain beliefs.
- Suggestion: Clarify the role of witness W, why multiple (W,Z) pairs yield multiple bounds, and how users should interpret/combine them.
- Suggestion: Add a detailed, step-by-step algorithmic description and clarify ambiguous phrases to improve reproducibility.
- Suggestion: Improve empirical evaluation—use appropriate metrics for intervals vs points, test smaller sample sizes, and demonstrate benefits of back-substitution.
- Suggestion: Position novel analytical bounds relative to closely related work (e.g., Geiger, Janzing, & Schölkopf 2014).
- Suggestion: Proofread to fix typos and grammatical errors.
