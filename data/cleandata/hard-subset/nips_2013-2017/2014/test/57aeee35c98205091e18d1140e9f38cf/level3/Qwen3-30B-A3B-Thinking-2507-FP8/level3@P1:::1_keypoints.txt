- Introduces WPP: a framework to bound the average causal effect (ACE) of binary X on Y when causal structure is partially unknown by relaxing faithfulness and using candidate witnesses as instrumental variables.  
- Builds on Entner et al.'s admissible-set rule and Ramsahai's linear-programming bounds, adding Bayesian inference over relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄).  
- Provides conservative bounds to address practitioners' lack of confidence in faithfulness-based point estimates, improving robustness in observational studies.  
- Uses constrained Dirichlet priors for Bayesian inference to avoid sensitivity issues in latent-variable models and cites Richardson et al. (2011).  
- Derives symbolic bounds and a back-substitution procedure that improves computational efficiency and scalability.  
- Demonstrates real-world applicability via a flu vaccination case study, claiming tighter bounds than standard IV by leveraging observed conditional independences.  
- Major flaw: fails to justify why Entner's rule conditions remain meaningful once faithfulness is relaxed, undermining the paper's core premise.  
- Critical point: Entner's rule relies on faithfulness to infer causal structure; dropping faithfulness makes the witness conditions insufficient for causal interpretation.  
- The connection between Entner's conditions and the relaxation parameters (how w, x, y are chosen or implied) is left unresolved.  
- Language issue: describing "bounded violations of local independence" is misleading since unfaithfulness implies extra independences, not violations.  
- Editorial error: Table 1 is misreferenced as "Table 5" in the text, indicating carelessness.  
- Interpretation error: the paper misstates the sign/interpretation of ACE in the flu vaccine example, confusing desirable directions.  
- Experimental gap: fails to report Entner et al.'s estimator performance under Entner's own thresholds (treating "don't know" as uninformative), weakening comparisons.  
- Originality/significance: novel framing of bounded causal inference as a faithfulness relaxation and novel integration of Bayesian inference with LP; potentially impactful for observational work.  
- Recommendation: promising but requires a clear justification of the witness conditions under relaxed faithfulness (otherwise only a weak accept); minor errors are fixable and Bayesian/symbolic contributions should be preserved.
