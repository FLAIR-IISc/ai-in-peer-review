- Introduces a novel method (Witness Protection Program, WPP) to bound average causal effect (ACE) under unobserved confounding by relaxing faithfulness and combining linear programming with Bayesian inference.
- Builds on Entner et al. (2013) and Ramsahai (2012), inverting Entner’s Rule 1 to suggest instruments and bound faithfulness violations.
- Situates work well in the causal inference literature and correctly identifies limitations of back-door adjustment and faithfulness.
- Novelty: WPP offers a fresh perspective by allowing bounded violations of faithfulness and producing posterior bounds on ACE.
- Practical relevance: Addresses a common real-world problem of estimating causal effects with incomplete causal graphs.
- Thoroughness: Provides algebraic bounds, computational details, and experimental evaluation.
- Clear motivation: Authors clearly explain why existing methods are insufficient and why relaxing faithfulness is useful.
- Lack of clarity: The link between Entner et al.’s Rule 1 conditions and the method without faithfulness is unresolved and hard to interpret.
- Dependence on Entner’s Rule: Unclear whether the method crucially relies on Rule 1 or merely uses it to guide relaxation parameter search.
- Conceptual wording issues: Describing unfaithfulness as “bounded violations of local independence” is awkward and potentially misleading.
- Incomplete empirical comparison: Needs direct comparison to Entner et al.’s original method using stringent thresholds and “don’t know” handling.
- Minor errors and ambiguities: Textual inconsistencies (table reference) and unclear interpretation of negative ACE in the vaccine example.
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles an important problem, proposes a potentially impactful method, and supplies initial empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: Core methodological connection is unclear, comparison to prior work is insufficient, and presentation needs improvement.
- Overall recommendation: Interesting paper with potential; recommend acceptance with major revisions to clarify connections, expand comparisons, fix errors, and better explain faithfulness relaxation.
