- Proposes the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP): use Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to find a witness W and conditioning set Z, treat W as an instrument with relaxed IV assumptions, and derive ACE bounds via linear programming.  
- Translates user-defined relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) into linear inequality constraints to allow small violations of ideal IV structure.  
- Builds on Dawid & Ramsahai for LP bounding, and adds a Bayesian inference framing plus a faster approximate "back-substitution" algorithm.  
- Strength: tackles an important, hard problem of causal inference under approximate faithfulness and uncertain graphs.  
- Strength: formulating relaxed conditional independencies as linear constraints is technically interesting.  
- Major conceptual flaw: the paper never justifies the crucial graphical assumption that a witness from Rule 1 can legitimately serve as (approximate) instrument—Rule 1 does not rule out confounding between W and Y.  
- Major practical weakness: bounds are highly sensitive to user-specified relaxation parameters, with no principled guidance for choosing them.  
- Experimental validation is limited in scope, making it hard to assess robustness and general applicability.  
- Clarity issues: paper is poorly written, dense, has typos and missing/misreferenced tables, and provides insufficient detail to reproduce Bayesian learning or the back-substitution algorithm.  
- Originality: individual components are known (conditional independence discovery, Entner's witnesses, LP bounds), but their synthesis is novel—though novelty is weakened by the core unjustified leap.  
- Significance: solving this problem would be impactful; preliminary results show promise if technical and practical issues are addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, presents a novel synthesis of ideas, and shows preliminary empirical gains over naive and standard IV methods.  
- Arguments against acceptance: critical conceptual omission about witness→IV validity, no guidance on relaxation parameter selection, poor clarity/reproducibility, and insufficient experimental support.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; requires substantial revision to justify core assumptions, provide parameter-selection guidance, improve clarity, and expand validation.
