- Presents a novel method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) for binary variables by combining Entner et al.'s witness-based search with Ramsahai-style linear-program bounds.  
- Uses witnesses not for point identification under faithfulness but to inform relaxed constraints that allow violations of faithfulness.  
- Main strength: original and practically significant idea that softens faithfulness to obtain tighter, more robust causal bounds between point estimates and wide IV bounds.  
- Major weakness: methodological clarity — it's unclear what causal information a witness/admissible set provides once faithfulness is dropped.  
- Critical gap: the paper does not justify why approximately satisfying Entner et al.’s rules warrants the proposed bounding procedure, leaving the choice of relaxation parameters unexplained.  
- Minor point 1: wording is imprecise—unfaithfulness implies extra independencies, whereas the method models near-independencies by bounding dependency magnitude.  
- Minor point 2: experiments should include a direct comparison to Entner et al.’s original method, treating its "don't know" output as [-1, 1] bounds.  
- Minor point 3: clarify interpretation that negative ACE implies vaccine desirability given Y=1 denotes hospitalization.  
- Minor point 4: typo on p.8 referring to "Table 1" as "Table 5".  
- Overall summary: promising contribution but needs a much clearer connection between the witness search and the bounding machinery to be a complete paper.
