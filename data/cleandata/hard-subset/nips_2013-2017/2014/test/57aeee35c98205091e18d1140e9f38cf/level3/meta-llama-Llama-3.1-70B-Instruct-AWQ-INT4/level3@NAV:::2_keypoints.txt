- Proposes a novel method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using observational conditional independencies to infer "weak" paths in an unknown causal graph.
- Introduces an ACE bounding method based on inverse Entner's Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, inequality constraints, and a linear programming formulation.
- Parameterizes the joint distribution of {W, X, Y, U} to obtain LP-based ACE bounds.
- Strength: Offers a middle ground between point estimators from faithfulness and wide IV-style bounds, producing tighter bounds.
- Strength: Handles violations of faithfulness by allowing limited "path cancellations," yielding more robust ACE estimates.
- Strength: Includes a thorough experimental evaluation showing the method outperforms existing approaches in several scenarios.
- Weakness: The inverse application of Entner's Rule 1 is not clearly justified; Fig.2 structure and related assumptions need explicit discussion.
- Weakness: No general guidance is provided for choosing relaxation parameters, which strongly influence bounds.
- Weakness: Theoretical exposition omits essential discussion on inverse-rule validity and parameter selection.
- Weakness: Experimental implementation and algorithmic details are poorly described; parameter definitions in tables are unclear or missing.
- Weakness: Typos and incorrect/missing table references reduce clarity.
- Originality: Combines existing ideas in a unified way with some novelty, but its contribution is limited by prior work and insufficient validation.
- Significance: Promising because it yields tighter ACE bounds, but limited experiments and validation leave generality and robustness uncertain.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions addressing inverse-rule justification, parameter selection criteria, expanded discussion, clearer algorithm/experimental details, and correction of typos/references.
