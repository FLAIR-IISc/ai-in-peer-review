- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE of binary X→Y using covariates W by combining causal discovery and bounding methods.
- Reinterprets Entner et al.'s witness/admissible set as a noisy-instrument setup and embeds relaxations into a linear program to derive ACE bounds.
- Puts the bounding procedure in a Bayesian framework to obtain posterior distributions over upper and lower ACE bounds.
- Strength: sensible middle ground between fragile point estimates (faithfulness) and overly wide bounds, addressing a practical problem in observational studies.
- Strength: novel synthesis of causal discovery heuristics with formal bounding methods; symbolic bounds and efficient back-substitution algorithm improve practicality.
- Main weakness: unclear methodological justification for using Entner et al.’s Rule 1 once faithfulness is relaxed — the inference from the rule to the IV-like setup is not established.
- Related weakness: the paper does not justify why a (W,Z) pair found by Rule 1 is preferable to an arbitrary pair when faithfulness is not assumed.
- Key missing piece: no guidance on choosing the relaxation parameters {w, y, x, β, β̄}, weakening the procedure’s practical applicability.
- Suggested natural link unexplored: the strength of observed conditional (in)dependencies could inform relaxation parameters (e.g., use violation magnitude to set 'y').
- Terminology/clarity issue: confusing wording about “violations of faithfulness” vs. modeling near-unfaithfulness or weak dependencies needs clarification.
- Minor clarity issues: better placement of variable definitions in the influenza example and a citation/table labeling error on page 8.
- Originality/significance: highly original approach to formally relax faithfulness and produce bounds; potentially high impact if theoretical justification is strengthened.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a fundamental problem, offers a novel compromise between assumptions and informativeness, and provides a full Bayesian and scalable framework.
- Argument against acceptance: central motivation is unclear without faithfulness, lack of parameter-selection guidance, and unclear technical guarantees could mislead readers.
- Suggested improvements: (1) provide theoretical justification for using Rule 1 under relaxed faithfulness, (2) connect observed data weaknesses to choices of relaxation parameters (even heuristics/sensitivity), (3) evaluate Entner et al.’s method with their recommended thresholds and treat “I don’t know” as the uninformative [-1,1] interval.
