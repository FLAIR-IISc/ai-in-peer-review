- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP), using linear programming to bound the average causal effect (ACE) under relaxed faithfulness.  
- Introduces relaxation parameters to model weak interactions and uses Bayesian learning to obtain a posterior over ACE bounds.  
- Strength: principled attempt to quantify weak unfaithfulness using familiar mathematical tools.  
- Strength: original combination of linear programming and Bayesian methods, distinct from recent related work.  
- Strength: directionally significant—could inspire new methods for causal inference when faithfulness is violated.  
- Weakness: interpretability is weak because posterior over bounds depends on unquantified likelihoods of unfaithful instances.  
- Weakness: many relaxations yield very wide, potentially uninformative bounds.  
- Weakness: method does not provide a preferred point estimate, risking misuse of intervals as point estimates.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is poor, unbalanced, and undermines practical usefulness claims.  
- Weakness: the “bias” metric used for comparisons is meaningless because it measures different quantities across methods.  
- Weakness: influenza dataset results are uninformative and raise practical performance concerns.  
- Clarity: technical supplement is detailed but main text has unclear context, many parameters, and potentially unreproducible algorithmic description.  
- Suggestions: clarify the role of witness W, provide intuition for relaxation parameters, and explain how admissible sets Z depend on θ.  
- Suggestions: clarify the search step vs. bounds computation, fix ambiguous sentences/typos, and cite closely related analytical-bounds work.  
- Suggestions: empirically justify the back-substitution refinement, explain why multiple bounds arise, and analyze sample-size sensitivity.  
- Suggestions: improve reported summaries (e.g., Table 1) to allow fair comparisons to standard faithfulness-based point estimates and tone down dismissive language.  
- Conclusion: WPP is a novel and promising direction but needs clearer interpretation, stronger experiments, and methodological clarifications before being practically convincing.
