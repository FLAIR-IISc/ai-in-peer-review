- Proposes a novel method to estimate causal effects from observational data by exploiting conditional independencies to suggest "weak" paths in unknown causal graphs.  
- Relaxes the standard faithfulness assumption to allow bounded "path cancellations" and outputs a posterior over ACE bounds via linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Provides a sensible compromise between point estimators under faithfulness and wide bounds from instrumental-variable approaches.  
- Main idea is potentially useful, technically sound, and well-explained, with a clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Introduces a Bayesian learning framework that strengthens inference by providing posterior distributions over bounds.  
- High originality and significance: builds on Entner et al. and Ramsahai but offers a new interpretation and application.  
- Major concern: the role of Entner et al.'s rule conditions is unclear when faithfulness is dropped—these conditions do not obviously imply causal structure without faithfulness.  
- The paper fails to explore or justify how Entner et al.'s conditions inform the selection of relaxation parameters.  
- Minor wording issue: phrasing "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is odd and needs clarification (unfaithfulness implies extra independences).  
- Suggestion: report Entner et al.'s method performance using stringent thresholds (treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds) for direct comparison.  
- Clarify interpretation on page 8 where a negative ACE is said to indicate a "desirable vaccine."  
- Fix citation/table error: "Table 1" on page 8 is referred to as "Table 5" in the text.  
- Some parts remain hard to follow and require clearer exposition, despite overall clarity.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the Entner-rule clarification, linking it to relaxation parameter choice, and fixing the minor comments and errors.
