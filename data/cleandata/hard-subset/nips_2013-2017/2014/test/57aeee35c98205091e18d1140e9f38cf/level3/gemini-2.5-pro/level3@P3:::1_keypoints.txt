- Presents WPP: a novel method to bound ACE between binary variables from observational data when causal structure is unknown.  
- Combines Entner et al.'s witness heuristic with linear-programming bounds (Ramsahai/Dawid style) and Bayesian inference for sampling uncertainty.  
- Relaxes strict faithfulness by allowing bounded violations of conditional independencies, controlled by user-defined relaxation parameters.  
- Offers a complete framework including an efficient algebraic approximation ("back-substitution") for scalability.  
- Well-situated in and builds on relevant causal discovery and bounding literature.  
- Major concern: unclear justification for using Entner et al.'s Rule 1 when faithfulness is dropped—no theoretical link showing such (W,Z) pairs imply a good relaxed-IV approximation.  
- The bounding machinery appears generic, so the special role of Entner-selected (W,Z) pairs is not justified and feels ad-hoc.  
- The paper defers tying the degree to which Entner conditions hold to the choice of relaxation parameters, leaving a crucial methodological gap.  
- Terminology unclear: phrase "express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence" is misleading and needs clarification.  
- Experimental comparison is weak: should include Entner et al.'s original method (treating "don't know" as [-1,1]) as a direct benchmark.  
- Real-data clarity: explicitly state that a negative ACE means P(hospitalized | do(shot)) < P(hospitalized | do(no shot)).  
- Minor typo: text refers to "Table 5" but likely means Table 1.  
- Overall: interesting and promising approach, but needs stronger theoretical justification for the foundational use of Entner's rule and improved experiments/clarifications.
