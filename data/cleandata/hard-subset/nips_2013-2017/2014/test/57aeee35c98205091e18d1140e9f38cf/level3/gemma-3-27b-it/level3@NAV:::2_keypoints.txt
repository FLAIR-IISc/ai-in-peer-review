- Proposes a novel method to bound Average Causal Effects by combining inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (path-cancellation), and linear programming.
- Reinterprets Entner’s Rule to identify instruments and uses LP to compute ACE bounds allowing adjustable faithfulness relaxations.
- Incorporates a Bayesian component to capture uncertainty within the LP framework, extending beyond frequentist approaches.
- Builds on established causal inference literature (do-calculus, instrumental variables, prior bounding techniques) and extends Entner et al. (2013).
- Strength: unifies several concepts into a coherent framework with a largely comprehensive theoretical exposition.
- Strength: relaxation parameters offer flexibility to handle faithfulness violations and may yield tighter bounds without optimization.
- Weakness: insufficient justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in reverse and reliance on a specific assumed graph structure (Fig. 2).
- Major limitation: no clear theoretical or empirical guidance for selecting relaxation parameters, undermining practical applicability.
- Experimental description is poor: unclear or missing parameter definitions, typos, and incorrect table references hinder reproducibility.
- Empirical validation is limited and does not convincingly show superiority or robustness across diverse scenarios.
- For acceptance: method’s potential for tighter ACE bounds and novel integration of existing ideas could advance causal inference.
- Against acceptance: weak justification of assumptions, lack of parameter-selection guidance, poor experimental clarity, limited validation, and presentation errors.
- Overall assessment: interesting and promising idea but not ready for a top venue; recommend rejection with encouragement to address theoretical, parameter-selection, and reproducibility issues before resubmission.
