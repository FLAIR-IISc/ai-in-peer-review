- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to estimate bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) between two binary variables when covariate causal structure is incomplete.  
- Uses Entner et al. (2013) rule to identify witnesses and produce bounds rather than point estimates.  
- Allows "almost violations" of faithfulness by constraining dependence magnitude instead of only local independence bounds.  
- Employs linear programming and Bayesian inference to generate ACE bounds.  
- Presents an innovative compromise between point estimators and instrumental-variable-style bounds.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear background, methodology, and experiments.  
- Reviewer finds the role and necessity of Entner et al. conditions in the method unclear and requests clarification.  
- Requests a more detailed comparison with existing methods (e.g., Entner et al.'s point estimator) to clarify strengths and weaknesses.  
- Experiments indicate WPP is stable and yields robust bounds, but more datasets and scenarios are needed for stronger validation.  
- Some minor errors and inconsistencies were found that should be corrected.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Overall assessment: good contribution likely to be strong after revisions addressing clarification, comparisons, and minor issues.
