Paper introduces a novel method exploiting observational conditional independencies and relaxed faithfulness to produce posterior bounds on the ACE via linear programming and Bayesian inference.

Builds on Entner et al. and Ramsahai by reinterpreting Entner’s search to generate candidate instruments and adapting Dawid/Ramsahai’s LP for bounding ACE.

Presents the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm with a clear, detailed methodological description and technical supplement.

Method outputs a distribution over bounds rather than a point estimate, risking misinterpretation of intervals as point estimates.

Conceptual interpretability is weak because the posterior over bounds depends on unquantified likelihoods of unfaithful causal instances.

Many relaxation choices yield very wide, potentially uninformative bounds and the paper defers guidance on selecting relaxation parameters.

Experimental evaluation is poor and unbalanced; the “bias” metric compares different quantities across methods and is therefore meaningless.

Influenza dataset results are uninformative and raise concerns about practical performance and sample-size sensitivity (results reported for n=5000).

Algorithmic description contains many subtly different parameters and ambiguities, hindering reproducibility.

Some derivations and overall context remain unclear despite the supplement; the manuscript contains ambiguous sentences and typos that need fixing.

Authors should clarify that derived bounds are not confidence intervals and provide practical interpretation and guidance for users.

Require clearer explanation of the witness W’s role, intuition for relaxation parameters (weak interactions), and how admissible sets Z depend on relaxations θ.

Must cite closely related work (e.g., Geiger; Janzing & Schölkopf UAI2014) when claiming novelty of analytical bounds.

Effectiveness of the back-substitution refinement step should be questioned and supported with empirical evidence.

Paper’s significance lies in novel direction and combination of techniques, but it is not yet ready for immediate practical use.
