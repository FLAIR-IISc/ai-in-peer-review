- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to compute ACE bounds under potential faithfulness violations using modified Entner et al.'s Rule 1 and instrumental-variable style constraints.
- Uses linear programming with relaxation parameters to produce conservative bounds and quantify weak unfaithfulness.
- Original and technically sound combination of existing techniques, offering a novel conceptual framework for weak unfaithfulness.
- Mathematical derivations are detailed and appear correct, with supporting material in the supplement.
- Conceptual interpretability is weak: bounds depend on unquantified likelihoods of unfaithful instances and are not confidence intervals.
- Fails to provide clear practical guidance on the real-world meaning or use of the produced bounds.
- Experimental evaluation is poor and unbalanced; the "bias" metric is misleading because it measures different quantities across methods.
- Influenza dataset results are uninformative (e.g., bounds like -0.23 to 0.64), casting doubt on practical performance.
- Many relaxation-parameter settings yield very wide, uninformative bounds, and the method lacks a recommended point estimate.
- Algorithm description is hard to follow with many subtly different parameters and insufficient reproducibility details.
- Key roles—such as the witness W—and the relationship between relaxation parameters and weak interactions are unclear.
- Fails to adequately cite closely related work (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI 2014) when claiming novelty.
- Significance lies in conceptual direction rather than immediate practitioner readiness; current evaluation doesn't demonstrate practical utility.
- Sample-size sensitivity (e.g., reliance on 5000 points) is questionable for typical observational studies.
