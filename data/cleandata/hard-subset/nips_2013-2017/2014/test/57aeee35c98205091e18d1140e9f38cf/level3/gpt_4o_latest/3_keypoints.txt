- Addresses a timely, significant problem in causal inference relevant to the community.  
- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a novel algorithm combining linear programming and Bayesian inference to bound ACE under faithfulness violations.  
- Methodology is mathematically rigorous and provides a systematic way to handle weak unfaithful interactions often ignored in observational studies.  
- Symbolic bounds in Section 4 illuminate how relaxation parameters affect results.  
- Back-substitution algorithm improves computational efficiency.  
- Original approach that could stimulate further research by offering a middle ground between point estimates and worst-case bounds.  
- Produces a posterior distribution over bounds that is conceptually hard to interpret and may be mistaken for confidence intervals.  
- Relies on large datasets and gives limited or no useful insight for smaller datasets, raising practical applicability concerns.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: the bias metric is not meaningful and performance is poor on the influenza dataset.  
- Choice of critical relaxation parameters is deferred, leaving the method incomplete for practical use.  
- Paper clarity is uneven, with algorithmic steps and parameters insufficiently explained, hindering reader comprehension.  
- Suggests stronger, balanced experiments with SOTA comparisons and clearer metric justification.  
- Recommends a robust discussion on practical implications and interpretability of bounds and concrete guidance for selecting relaxation parameters.  
- Recommends acceptance only if interpretability, experimental validation, and parameter-selection guidance are addressed; otherwise suggests workshop revision.
