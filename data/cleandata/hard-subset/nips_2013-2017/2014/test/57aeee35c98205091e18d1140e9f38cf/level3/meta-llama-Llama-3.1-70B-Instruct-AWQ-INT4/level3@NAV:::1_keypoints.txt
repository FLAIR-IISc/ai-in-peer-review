- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the average causal effect (ACE) with unobserved confounders by combining instrumental variable and causal discovery ideas.
- Novel contribution: a new method that merges IV analysis and causal discovery.
- Relaxes the faithfulness assumption and uses linear programming to compute bounds on ACE.
- Produces bounds rather than point estimates, offering more robust causal inference.
- Paper is technically dense; several derivations and details are hard to follow.
- Role of Entner et al.'s rule for generating candidate instruments is not clearly explained.
- Choice and interpretation of relaxation parameters controlling faithfulness are unclear.
- Contains minor errors (e.g., citation and table mistakes).
- Addresses a significant problem with potential impact in economics, epidemiology, and social sciences.
- Clarity is mixed: main ideas are clear but technical sections lack accessibility.
- Overall quality is good but marred by technical opacity and small errors.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify technical details, Entner rule usage, and relaxation parameter choices.
