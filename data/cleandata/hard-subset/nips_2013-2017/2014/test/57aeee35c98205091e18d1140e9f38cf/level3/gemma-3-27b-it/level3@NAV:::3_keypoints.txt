- Tackles causal effect estimation in observational studies by relaxing the faithfulness assumption.  
- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm that uses relaxation parameters and linear programming to bound the Average Causal Effect (ACE).  
- Novel idea: invert Entner’s Rule 1 to use witnesses for bounding rather than identification, quantifying weak unfaithfulness.  
- Combines linear programming with Bayesian inference and exploration of relaxation parameters, distinct from exact-identification work.  
- Well-motivated problem: unmeasured confounding and violations of faithfulness are realistic concerns.  
- Technical development is thorough and detailed, with supplementary material demonstrating mathematical depth.  
- Major weakness: derived bounds have weak interpretability because they depend on unquantified likelihoods of unfaithful instances.  
- The paper defers guidance on choosing relaxation parameters, limiting practical applicability.  
- Many relaxation settings produce excessively wide, uninformative bounds.  
- No preferred point estimate is provided, increasing risk that bounds will be misinterpreted as confidence intervals.  
- Experimental evaluation is severely lacking and unconvincing.  
- Influenza dataset result (ACE in (-0.23, 0.64)) is uninformative and casts doubt on practical utility.  
- Comparison uses an ill-defined “bias” metric that measures different quantities across methods.  
- Writing has occasional ambiguity and minor typos that reduce clarity.  
- Related-work coverage is incomplete; should cite Geiger, Janzing, and Schölkopf (2014) when claiming novelty in analytical bounds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel combination of techniques, technical soundness, and principled attempt to address faithfulness violations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor interpretability/practicality, weak experimental validation, ill-defined evaluation metric, and risk of misinterpretation.  
- Overall recommendation: reject for now but encourage revision—ideas are significant though not yet practitioner-ready.
