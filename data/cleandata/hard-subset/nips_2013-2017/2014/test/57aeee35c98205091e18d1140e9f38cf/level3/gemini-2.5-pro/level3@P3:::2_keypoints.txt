- Proposes WPP: repurposes Entner et al.'s Rule 1 and relaxes IV/faithfulness assumptions to compute ACE bounds via LP and a Bayesian treatment of sampling uncertainty.
- Novel synthesis of three ideas: reinterpreting causal discovery for bounding, parameterizing faithfulness violations, and LP-based bounding.
- Addresses a practical need by providing a pragmatic sensitivity-analysis tool for near-conditional-independence situations.
- Promising empirical results on synthetic data: bounds are narrower than trivial ones and more robust than fragile point estimators.
- Core assumption is insufficiently justified: inverting Rule 1 to motivate a relaxed IV structure is circular without faithfulness and may be invalid under path cancellations.
- No clear guidance on selecting relaxation parameters ({w, y, x, β, β̄}), limiting usability and interpretability.
- Clarity and reproducibility issues: terse experimental description, back-substitution algorithm unclear, typos/incorrect references, and missing or unclear definitions/tables.
- Limited experimental validation: comparisons are narrow (two naive estimators and pure-faithfulness method) and the single real-data example is unconvincing due to unexplained parameter choices.
- Pros: tackles an important causal-inference problem with an original idea that can yield tighter, useful bounds.
- Cons: weak theoretical justification of the core assumption, lack of practical parameter guidance, unclear exposition, and insufficient empirical evaluation.
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful work but incomplete; needs stronger foundational arguments, clearer guidance, and more thorough, reproducible evaluation.
