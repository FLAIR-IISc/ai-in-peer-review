- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) algorithm that relaxes faithfulness and provides bounds on the ACE using linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Novel combination of familiar techniques: relaxation parameters to constrain unfaithful interactions and LP to compute bounds.  
- Paper is technically sound and generally well-written with a clear explanation of methodology and assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is poor and not thorough enough to support claims.  
- The relevance and proper interpretation of the output distribution over bounds remain unclear.  
- Comparing WPP to competitors using the 'bias' metric is meaningless.  
- Practical performance on the influenza dataset is weak and concerning.  
- The back-substitution process’s ability to meaningfully refine bounds is doubted.  
- Algorithmic steps and subsequent modifications are hard to follow due to missing detail.  
- Current implementation appears immature and unlikely to be ready for practitioner use despite authors’ claims.  
- Significance lies in the ideas and direction offered, but practical efficacy and evaluation need substantial improvement.  
- Paper should include more detailed explanations and a more thorough experimental evaluation before strong acceptance.
