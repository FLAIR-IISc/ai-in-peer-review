- Paper proposes WPP: bounds ACE for binary X,Y when covariate causal structure unknown by inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1, using LP bounds with relaxations for faithfulness violations and Bayesian posteriors over bounds.  
- Introduces algebraic back-substitution to speed computation and connects to Balke–Pearl, Dawid, and Ramsahai formulations.  
- High-level idea is sensible and potentially useful; LP/Bayesian approach aligns with prior constrained methods.  
- Major conceptual gap: unclear role and justification of Entner’s Rule 1 once faithfulness is relaxed—Rule 1 no longer guarantees the underlying structure.  
- The paper leaves unresolved how Rule 1 should inform the constraints (3)–(6), witness selection, or the magnitudes of relaxation parameters.  
- As written, the method appears to run with any candidate (W,Z); need clarification why Entner’s conditions matter for validity or efficiency.  
- Clarity issues: paper is hard to follow in places and defers explanations (e.g., how to choose relaxation parameters).  
- Terminology/confusing phrasing: “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading and should be rephrased (unfaithfulness produces extra independences; authors are bounding dependences).  
- Originality: novel synthesis of Rule-1-driven search, IV-style bounding, Bayesian posteriors over bounds, and algebraic back-substitution.  
- Significance: could be a practical tool for observational studies if clarified and strengthened.  
- Specific suggestion: evaluate Entner et al.’s method using their stringent thresholds and treat “don’t know” as an uninformative bound for fair comparison.  
- Specific suggestion: clarify the p.8 example where variable codings make negative ACE indicate a desirable vaccine—align definitions and text.  
- Specific suggestion: fix citation/table error (Table 1 referred to as Table 5 on p.8).  
- Specific suggestion: provide principled guidance for choosing (w, x, y, β, β̄), include sensitivity analyses, and report how often witness selection tightens bounds versus naive IV or no-witness baselines.  
- Pros: timely problem, principled partial-structure bounding, computational advances, and Bayesian uncertainty quantification over bounds.  
- Cons: insufficient justification for using Entner’s conditions without faithfulness, lack of parameter-choice guidance and adequate baselines, and presentation/notation hinder readability.  
- Overall: interesting and promising work but needs clearer methodological connections, justification, parameter guidance, and better presentation before acceptance.
