- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the ACE by relaxing the faithfulness assumption using witness variables and relaxation parameters within an LP/posterior framework.
- Re-purposes witness variables as approximate instrumental variables and quantifies near-faithfulness with parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄).
- Novel synthesis of witness-based search and IV bounding; addresses brittleness of faithfulness — significant and original contribution.
- Principled framework allowing graded violations of faithfulness, bridging point estimators and conservative IV bounds.
- Technical contribution: back-substitution algebraic approximation to speed up the computationally expensive LP.
- Major weakness: no practical guidance on choosing the relaxation parameters, limiting immediate applicability.
- Suggestion: provide intuition, heuristics, or sensitivity analyses showing how bounds change with parameter choices.
- Output interpretation issue: posterior over bounds is complex and not equivalent to CI/credible interval; practitioners lack guidance.
- Suggestion: add a dedicated section clarifying what a distribution over bounds means and correct interpretation.
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing: error metric (zero if true ACE in interval) favors overly wide intervals and is unfair.
- Suggestion: evaluate both coverage and interval width and compare WPP bound widths to faithfulness-based estimator errors.
- Empirical results (influenza dataset) show only modest improvement over standard IV bounds and remain too wide to be decisive.
- Clarity/exposition problems: confusing roles of witness W vs admissible set Z, high-level algorithm description, and ambiguous phrasing.
- Suggestion: clarify interplay of W and Z, give concrete algorithmic details for (W,Z) search under relaxations, and tighten language.
- Related-work gap: claims about novel analytical bounds should be better contextualized, e.g., relation to Geiger, Janzing, and Schölkopf (UAI 2014).
- Overall stance: promising, original idea but practical utility is questionable now due to parameter-selection, empirical, and clarity weaknesses; reviewer leans to reject but would reconsider if these are addressed.
