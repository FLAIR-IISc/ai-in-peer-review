- Introduces WPP: a method to bound ACE under relaxed faithfulness by inverting Entner et al.'s Rule 1, using witnesses and LP/Bayesian inference.  
- Technical foundation builds on Ramsahai [16] and Bayesian posterior sampling, but has critical theoretical gaps.  
- The inverse application of Rule 1 is assumed without proof or justification of the Fig. 2 structure’s necessity or generality.  
- Relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) strongly affect bound tightness, yet no general criteria or principled selection method are provided.  
- Experiments show narrower bounds than standard IV in synthetic cases, but the "hard case" is constructed to favor WPP and sensitivity to parameters is unexplored.  
- Influenza case study substitutes DM/AGE/SEX for the theoretically correct GRP without causal justification, undermining empirical credibility.  
- Key terms (e.g., "path cancellations") are undefined and core parameters/tables (Table 1) are missing or ambiguously described.  
- Algorithm 1’s search procedure and the back-substitution method lack specification, pseudocode, or implementation details.  
- Typos and incorrect table references reduce reproducibility and clarity.  
- The Bayesian model assumes modest Z-dimensionality and uses Dirichlet priors without justification despite sensitivity concerns.  
- The paper’s novelty is mainly a synthesis of known components (Entner et al., Ramsahai, Dawid), not clearly distinguished from prior LP-based methods.  
- Prior work (Geiger et al., Spirtes et al.) already addresses related inequality constraints and relaxed faithfulness, limiting perceived originality.  
- Potential significance: could produce tighter, more reliable ACE bounds than standard IV (demonstrated in one case).  
- Significance is limited by small experimental scope (100 synthetic runs, one real dataset), no scalability analysis, and untested parameter robustness.  
- WPP can be unstable (e.g., "no witness" scenario yields false solutions), indicating reliability issues.  
- Pros: principled handling of faithfulness violations, Bayesian posterior over bounds, promising empirical improvement in one case.  
- Cons: unjustified inverse Rule 1, no guidelines for relaxation parameters, superficial experimental validation, and missing critical methodological details.  
- Recommendation: reject pending major revisions—justify inverse Rule 1, provide criteria and sensitivity analysis for relaxation parameters, and expand/clarify experiments and implementation details.
