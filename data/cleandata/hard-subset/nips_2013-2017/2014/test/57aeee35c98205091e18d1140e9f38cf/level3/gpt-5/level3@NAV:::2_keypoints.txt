- Paper proposes bounding the ACE via inverse Entner’s Rule 1, bounded “path-cancellation” faithfulness, inequality constraints, and an LP with a Bayesian posterior; also gives symbolic inequalities and a fast back-substitution relaxation.
- Technical development is careful and empirical bounds are often narrower and more accurate than standard IV bounds, even with an unoptimized algorithm.
- Central methodological move—using Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction—is insufficiently justified and needs stronger defense.
- Method implicitly assumes a structural template (e.g., W has no direct effect on Y given Z with controlled violations); this assumption must be explicitly stated and defended.
- Relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄) critically determine bound width, but the paper provides no generic selection or diagnostic criteria.
- Sensitivity analysis for the relaxation parameters is limited and inadequate to assess robustness.
- Exposition of the LP and symbolic bounds is mostly clear, but experimental and algorithmic details are under-specified.
- Parameter definitions in tables are unclear or missing; there are typos and incorrect/missing table references that hurt reproducibility.
- The paper should fully specify constraints, priors, proposal/rejection thresholds, and back-substitution steps.
- Originality is modest: ingredients have appeared separately in prior work, and the unified combination is only modestly novel.
- Validity and novelty hinge on the inverse-rule assumption and principled parameter calibration, which are currently underdeveloped.
- Potential significance: if validated, the approach can give practically tighter ACE bounds than standard IV methods.
- Current limitations—limited experiments (synthetic + one real dataset) and lack of principled parameter selection—prevent assessing generality and robustness.
- Pros: promising tighter ACE bounds, useful symbolic approximations, fast solver, and a pragmatic unification of several strands (faithfulness, IV bounds, inequalities).
- Cons: missing justification for inverse Entner’s Rule 1, no principled relaxation-parameter procedure, incomplete experimental/algorithmic details, clarity issues, and incremental novelty.
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject; could become impactful with clearer assumptions, principled parameter-selection guidance, fixed presentation, and stronger empirical validation.
