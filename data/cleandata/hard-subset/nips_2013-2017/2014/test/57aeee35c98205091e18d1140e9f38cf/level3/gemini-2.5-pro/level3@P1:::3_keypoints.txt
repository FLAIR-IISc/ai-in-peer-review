- Proposes WPP: bounding ACE under unmeasured confounding by relaxing faithfulness using witness-based admissible sets and LP constraints.
- Wraps LP bounds in a Bayesian framework to produce a posterior over bounds and offers an algebraic approximation to speed computation.
- Technical approach is sound and builds on Entner et al., Dawid, and Ramsahai, with detailed derivations in supplementary material.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and uses a problematic "error" metric that mixes point-estimator error with interval coverage.
- Synthetic experiments are limited and the real-data influenza example yields wide, likely uninformative bounds (e.g., [-0.10, 0.17]).
- Claim that the back-substitution algorithm is efficient without widening bounds lacks empirical evidence in the main text.
- Paper is hard to follow; methodology details, result interpretation, and implementation are often obscure.
- The posterior over bounds is not well explained—practitioners may misinterpret it since it’s not a standard credible or confidence interval.
- Notation and many parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄, θ, η, δ, ζ, κ, ω) are confusing, impeding reproducibility.
- Key algorithmic steps—e.g., how admissible set search depends on relaxation parameters—are insufficiently specified.
- Several sentences are ambiguous and reduce clarity (example quoted).
- The combination of techniques is original and addresses near-unfaithfulness in a novel way, filling a gap between strict-faithfulness and agnostic methods.
- Should cite related relaxations for bounding causal effects (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI 2014).
- Main significance is directional: the approach is principled but not immediately ready for practice.
- Lack of guidance on choosing relaxation parameters critically limits practical applicability and risks producing useless or unjustifiably tight bounds.
- Producing intervals instead of point estimates is honest but may be impractical for decision-makers seeking single-number estimates.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, integrates relaxed CI constraints into bounding in a novel way, and could inspire further robust causal inference research.
- Arguments for rejection: wide real-data bounds and no parameter-setting guidance; weak, flawed experimental evaluation; significant clarity and reproducibility issues.
- Suggested improvements: clarify interpretation of posterior bounds and practitioner use.
- Suggested improvements: provide intuition or sensitivity analysis for relaxation parameters and guidance for setting them.
- Suggested improvements: redesign experiments to report WPP interval widths and compare to point-estimator bias; test smaller sample sizes.
- Suggested improvements: clarify role of witness W after Z is identified and how to consolidate multiple (W, Z) outputs beyond BDeu score selection.
- Suggested improvements: include a concrete step-by-step worked example and discuss closely related work on bounding confounding.
- Suggested improvements: adopt a more neutral tone regarding other methodological approaches.
