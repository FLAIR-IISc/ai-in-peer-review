- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the average causal effect (ACE) when the causal graph is partially unknown, allowing bounded violations of faithfulness.  
- Builds on Entner et al.'s Rule 1 for admissible sets and Ramsahai's linear programming approach for ACE bounds.  
- Reinterprets Entner's "witness" variables as potential instrumental variables and introduces relaxation parameters to model bounded faithfulness violations.  
- Converts Entner's rule from a point-identification procedure into a bounding procedure for more robust causal inference.  
- Develops a Bayesian framework to quantify uncertainty over the resulting ACE bounds.  
- Proposes a back-substitution algorithm to improve computational efficiency and scalability.  
- Addresses a practical problem in observational causal inference: robustness to unmeasured confounding and possible unfaithfulness.  
- Theoretical foundations appear plausible but the reviewer did not verify all technical details.  
- Major open issue: the connection between Entner's rule conditions and the relaxation parameters is unclear and acknowledged as unresolved.  
- Language and conceptual clarity problems, notably misleading phrasing about "violations of faithfulness" (unfaithfulness implies extra independences).  
- Presentation errors and confusing elements, e.g., incorrect table reference and unclear interpretation of negative ACE in the influenza example.  
- Originality: meaningful synthesis of prior methods; the WPP concept and bounded-violation approach are novel contributions.  
- Significance: potentially valuable for practice, but the paper does not convincingly show how much WPP outperforms existing methods.  
- Key strengths: practical relevance, novel reinterpretation of Entner's rule, Bayesian uncertainty quantification, and efficient algorithm.  
- Key weaknesses: unresolved theoretical gap regarding Entner's conditions, misleading terminology, insufficient justification for the method's dependence on those conditions, interpretation and technical errors, and limited empirical comparison.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, contingent on resolving the major theoretical concern and clarifying the role of Entner's conditions.
