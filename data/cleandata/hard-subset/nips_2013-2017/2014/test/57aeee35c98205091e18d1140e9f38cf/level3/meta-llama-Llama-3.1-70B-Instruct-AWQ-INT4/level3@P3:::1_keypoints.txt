- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP): uses Entner et al.'s ideas to generate candidate instruments and a linear program to bound the ACE.  
- WPP positioned as a compromise between faithfulness-based point estimates and instrumental-variable bounds.  
- Main idea is sensible and potentially useful, but reviewer did not verify all technical details.  
- Major concern: Entner et al.'s rule conditions may not imply causal structure once faithfulness is dropped.  
- Unclear how Entner et al.'s conditions should guide the choice of relaxation parameters; paper leaves this connection unresolved.  
- Reviewer doubts the proposed machinery critically depends on Entner et al.'s conditions and requests clarification why those conditions matter.  
- Writing and organization are good; paper is mostly clear.  
- Terminology critique: phrase “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is odd—unfaithfulness implies extra independences; framework really bounds dependency magnitude.  
- Suggests reporting Entner et al.'s method performance using stringent thresholds and treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds for comparison.  
- Requests clarification of interpretation on page 8 (why negative ACE with X=1, Y=1 implies desirable vaccine).  
- Notes a citation/table error: "Table 1" is referenced as "Table 5" in the text.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially significant contribution but needs methodological clarifications and refinement.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Acceptance pros: novel, well-motivated approach to a fundamental causal inference problem.  
- Acceptance cons: unresolved role of Entner et al.'s conditions, needs clearer methodological connections and some revisions.
