- Introduces WPP, a method that inverts Entner’s Rule 1 to bound ACE by treating witnesses instrumentally and relaxing faithfulness, formulated as a linear program.  
- Proposes analytical approximations and algorithmic relaxations to improve computational efficiency.  
- Situates the method within existing causal inference literature (Pearl, Entner, Dawid, Ramsahai) and connects to instrumental variable and faithfulness discussions.  
- Novel combination: inversion of Entner’s Rule 1 plus faithfulness relaxation with LP/Bayesian elements.  
- Empirical results indicate tighter ACE bounds than naive estimators and some existing techniques.  
- Strong theoretical grounding using causal graphs, do-calculus, and linear programming, with analytical bounds and scalability considerations.  
- Explicitly addresses and relaxes the faithfulness assumption to better handle real-world data.  
- Core inversion lacks sufficient justification and explicit statement of assumptions (Figure 2 assumption not fully discussed).  
- No practical guidance for choosing relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), limiting usability.  
- Experimental section omits key implementation details and has unclear/missing parameter definitions.  
- Presentation issues (typos, incorrect/missing table references) reduce clarity.  
- Empirical validation is limited: mainly synthetic tests and a single real-world example.  
- Contribution may be incremental since individual components have prior exploration; novelty is largely combinatorial.  
- For acceptance: method could substantially improve ACE bounding, rests on solid theoretical principles, and pragmatically relaxes faithfulness.  
- Against acceptance: weak justification for core assumption, lack of parameter-selection guidance, poor reproducibility details, and limited validation.  
- Overall: promising but currently flawed; recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing justification, parameter guidance, and reproducibility.
