- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to produce a posterior distribution over bounds on the ACE for binary variables using Entner et al.'s rule and a Ramsahai-style bounding method.
- WPP relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow bounded "path cancellations" and uses linear programming plus Bayesian inference to produce ACE bound posteriors.
- Core contribution claimed: a novel compromise between point estimators (faithfulness) and wide instrumental-variable-style bounds.
- Major concern: unclear role and necessity of Entner et al.'s rule when faithfulness is relaxed—conditions don’t obviously imply causal structure without faithfulness.
- Unclear how Entner et al.'s conditions inform the choice of relaxation parameters; the paper leaves this connection unresolved.
- Wording issue: describing violations of faithfulness as "bounded violations of local independence" is misleading because unfaithfulness implies extra independences, not violations.
- Suggestion: report Entner et al.'s method performance using stringent thresholds (treating "don't know" as uninformative bounds) for fair comparison.
- Result interpretation unclear: sign of ACE in the flu-shot example (X=1, Y=1) seems counterintuitive and needs clarification.
- Editing errors: inconsistent table/citation reference (Table 1 referred to as Table 5) that needs correction.
- Strengths: potentially useful framework for observational studies, novel methodological compromise, and R code is available for implementation.
- Weaknesses: methodology details are hard to follow and key connections need clearer exposition.
- Recommendation: paper is interesting and of good quality but requires substantial clarification and presentation improvements; with revisions it could make a valuable contribution.
