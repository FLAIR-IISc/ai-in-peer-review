- Tackles bounding the Average Cusal Effect (ACE) under unmeasured confounding, building on Entner et al. and Ramsahai’s LP approach while relaxing faithfulness.  
- Core idea: use Entner’s Rule 1 to identify candidate instruments for bounding ACE (not point estimation).  
- Novelty: combines existing techniques into a “Witness Protection Program” (WPP) framework to allow path cancellations.  
- Strength: addresses a realistic problem (relaxing faithfulness) and proposes a potentially useful practical method.  
- Strength: experiments (small-scale) show method stability and compare it to naive estimators.  
- Major concern: unclear role of Entner et al.’s rule without faithfulness—paper doesn’t explain why those conditions remain relevant.  
- Major concern: insufficient explanation of how Entner-derived conditions inform choice of relaxation parameters.  
- Major concern: unclear whether the method critically depends on Entner’s conditions or if they are merely suggestive.  
- Needed work: experiments or sensitivity analysis demonstrating dependence on Entner’s conditions and relaxation choices.  
- Minor issues: awkward phrasing about “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” (unfaithfulness implies extra independences).  
- Minor fixes: report Entner et al.’s method performance using their original thresholds; clarify interpretation in vaccine example (p.8); correct citation/table error (Table 1 referenced as Table 5).  
- Recommendation: interesting and promising but currently unclear and incomplete—recommend rejection now but encourage revision and clarification.
