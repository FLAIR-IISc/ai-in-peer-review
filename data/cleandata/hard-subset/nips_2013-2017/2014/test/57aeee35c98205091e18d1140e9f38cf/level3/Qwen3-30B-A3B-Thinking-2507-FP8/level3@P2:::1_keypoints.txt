- Paper introduces WPP to bound ACE of a binary treatment when causal structure is partially unknown, using Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to find witnesses/admissible sets and deriving bounds via LP with relaxed path-cancellation constraints.  
- Strength: Addresses practical problem of relying on unverifiable faithfulness and favors principled bounds over point estimates.  
- Strength: Bayesian implementation (Algorithm 1) provides uncertainty quantification and is well-motivated.  
- Strength: Real-world flu vaccination case study shows improved bounds versus standard IV; synthetic experiments show robustness where faithfulness-based methods fail.  
- Strength: Algebraic bounds in Section 4 speed computation relative to full LP and connections to prior work (Ramsahai 2012, Entner et al. 2013) are well-grounded.  
- Core theoretical flaw: Using Entner’s Rule 1 to relax faithfulness is unjustified because Rule 1’s conditional-independence tests require faithfulness to infer causal structure.  
- Methodological ambiguity: Paper contradicts itself by relying on conditional independences to justify edges while claiming to relax faithfulness; relationship between Rule 1 and the relaxation parameters is unexplained.  
- Minor issues: Mischaracterizes unfaithfulness as "violations" of local independence; confuses sign/interpretation of ACE in the flu example (clarify Y); table numbering error (Table 1 labeled as Table 5); Entner et al.’s method not compared using their original thresholds.  
- Overall: Strong motivation and reasonable experiments, but the unresolved logical gap about Rule 1 undermines the main contribution and novelty beyond existing IV bounds.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — fix theoretical justification for using Rule 1 without faithfulness or clarify how Rule 1 informs parameter choices to make this a strong contribution.  
- Pro/Con summary: Pro — practical bounding approach, Bayesian uncertainty, real-data case study, computational efficiency; Con — reliance on faithfulness despite claims otherwise, unexplained link between Rule 1 and relaxation parameters.
