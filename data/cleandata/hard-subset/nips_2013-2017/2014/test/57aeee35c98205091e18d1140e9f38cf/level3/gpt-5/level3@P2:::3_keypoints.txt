- Paper proposes WPP to bound ACE with binary X,Y under unmeasured confounding and relaxed faithfulness via “witness” W as an approximate instrument.  
- Builds on Entner et al.’s Rule 1, uses relaxation parameters (w,x,y,β,β̄) to allow near-unfaithfulness and LP-based polytopes to compute ACE bounds.  
- Adds algebraic/symbolic inequalities and a back-substitution refinement to speed inference and offers constrained-Dirichlet Bayesian propagation over bounds.  
- Strength: fills a methodological gap between brittle faithfulness point-identification and overly-wide IV bounds by formalizing weak violations.  
- Strength: technically careful mapping to known binary IV polytopes and plausible constrained-Bayesian approach to handle sampling uncertainty.  
- Strength: back-substitution/message-passing and symbolic bounds provide computational gains and intuition; code is open-source.  
- Weakness: no principled method or guidance provided for choosing relaxation parameters, limiting practical application.  
- Weakness: many relaxation settings yield very wide, weakly informative bounds; trade-off between relaxation strength and informativeness is not characterized.  
- Weakness: “posterior over bounds” is not a posterior over the ACE; exposition needs clearer explanation to avoid misinterpretation.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited/unbalanced and uses an inappropriate “bias” metric comparing point estimates to intervals.  
- Weakness: influenza dataset results are unconvincing—the interval is wide and not actionable—so practical utility is overstated.  
- Clarity: notation proliferation (ζ, κ, ω, η) and multiple parameterizations make the paper hard to follow; a definitions table is needed.  
- Clarity: role and handling of witness W (multiple (W,Z) pairs, combining intervals) must be clarified for users.  
- Clarity: relaxation parameters need intuitive mapping to measurable quantities with small-graph illustrations.  
- Clarity: link between the search/acceptance step (approximate Rule 1) and subsequent LP constraints should be made explicit.  
- Originality: novel combination of witness-based approximate IV bounds, parameterized faithfulness relaxation, and constrained-Bayesian propagation.  
- Related work: should better cite and position against prior symbolic/graphical constraints (e.g., Geiger et al., Richardson & Robins, Evans).  
- Significance: conceptually important middle ground for robust causal inference, but current empirical guidance is insufficient for practice.  
- Suggestions: provide principled parameter-selection strategies (data-driven calibration, recommended sensitivity grids, priors) and better evaluation metrics (coverage, width, decision regret).  
- Suggestions: add small-sample experiments, quantify back-substitution tightening and speedups, and give guidance for aggregating multiple (W,Z) bounds.  
- Suggestions: improve reproducibility with an algorithmic schematic, symbol-definition table, and default prior/hyperparameter settings.  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses an important gap, technically sound and novel, and can stimulate follow-on research.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient empirical validation, no guidance on relaxation parameter choice, and unclear exposition impede practical adoption.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger empirical validation, clearer parameter-selection guidance, and improved clarity/reproducibility.
