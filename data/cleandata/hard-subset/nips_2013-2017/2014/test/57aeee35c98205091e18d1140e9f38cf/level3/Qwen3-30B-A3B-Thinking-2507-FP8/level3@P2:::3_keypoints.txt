- Paper introduces WPP: a framework that formalizes faithfulness violations as bounded deviations and computes posterior distributions over ACE bounds via Bayesian inference.  
- Originality & technical soundness: principled relaxation of faithfulness, novel Bayesian uncertainty quantification, and algebraic bounds with an efficient back-substitution algorithm.  
- Conceptual contribution: offers a meaningful middle ground between fragile faithfulness-based point estimators and vacuous IV bounds; the "witness" idea clarifies how conditional independencies tighten bounds.  
- Clarity: core methodology and Algorithm 1 are well-structured and the supplement provides reproducible technical detail.  
- Invalid experimental metric: the "error" measure treats intervals as point estimates (counts any interval containing true ACE as zero error), which rewards trivially wide intervals.  
- Influenza case study is uninformative: presenting a narrower interval than the standard IV bound without justification or comparison to a true ACE gives no evidence of superiority.  
- Unrealistic sample sizes: experiments assume 5,000 samples with no analysis of performance at typical observational-study sizes.  
- Posterior interpretation unclear: the posterior over bounds is not a confidence interval and risks misinterpretation; the role of the witness W is ambiguous.  
- Arbitrary relaxation parameters: choices for w, y, x, β, β̄ are ad hoc in experiments with no guidance or sensitivity analysis for practitioners.  
- Falsification test is ad hoc (rejecting >95% of proposals) and the paper does not specify how to aggregate multiple bounds from different witnesses/Z for decisions.  
- Reproducibility issues: ambiguous phrasing and unexplained terms (e.g., "Naïve Estimator") hinder clarity and replication.  
- Undemonstrated claims about back-substitution: the claimed negligible increase in bound width from back-substitution is asserted but not empirically shown.  
- Overstated practical impact: the method’s real-world utility is undermined by reliance on witness selection and resulting wide bounds in practice.  
- Pro: the approach is original, technically rigorous, and handles uncertainty in a principled Bayesian way.  
- Con: experimental flaws, lack of practical guidance, risk of misinterpretation, and unjustified parameter choices argue against acceptance as-is.  
- Required fixes recommended by reviewer: replace the invalid error metric with a width-adjusted evaluation; provide sensitivity analyses for relaxation parameters; clarify interpretation and use of bounds; include experiments on smaller datasets (e.g., 500–1,000 samples).  
- Overall verdict: conceptually promising but experimentally and practically unproven — weak reject.
