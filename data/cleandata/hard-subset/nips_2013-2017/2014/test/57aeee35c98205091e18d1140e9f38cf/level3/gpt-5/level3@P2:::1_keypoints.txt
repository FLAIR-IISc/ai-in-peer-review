- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE of binary X→Y by treating Entner et al. Rule 1 “witness” as an approximate conditional instrument and relaxing faithfulness via bounded deviations.  
- Encodes relaxations as local inequality constraints (η, δ, β, β̄) and uses LPs (Balke–Pearl / Dawid–Ramsahai style) to produce ACE bounds.  
- Adds a constrained-Dirichlet Bayesian posterior-over-bounds, symbolic algebraic bounds, and a back-substitution algorithm for faster computation.  
- Positions method between point-identification under faithfulness and worst-case IV bounds, aiming for a tunable robustness trade-off.  
- Builds on Entner et al. (AISTATS’13), classical IV bounds literature, Manski-style partial identification, and polyhedral characterizations of binary IV models.  
- High-level idea is sensible: convert approximate CIs into bounded violations and propagate them to bounds via LPs.  
- Algebraic relaxation and back-substitution solver are practical and useful computational contributions.  
- Main conceptual gap: insufficient justification for using Rule 1 to select witnesses once faithfulness is relaxed.  
- Paper does not clearly explain which parts of Rule 1 are retained or abandoned under relaxation and why Rule 1-informed pairs are informative for bounding.  
- Mapping from observed near-independences to relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄) is unspecified and needs principled guidance or calibration schemes.  
- Bayesian treatment is reasonable but lacks detail on priors, constrained sampling efficiency, handling contradictory posteriors, and the ad-hoc falsification threshold.  
- Experiments lack a crucial baseline: Entner et al.’s original method with stringent thresholds (counting “don’t know” as trivial bounds).  
- Missing reports on frequency of model falsification and distribution of interval widths to contextualize empirical performance.  
- Presentation is dense; notation is heavy and would benefit from an early causal interpretation of parameters, a legend, and a guiding example.  
- Algorithm 1’s falsification and model-selection steps need clearer description (thresholds, priors, number of candidate pairs).  
- Influenza-vaccine example has possible coding/interpretation errors (confusion about Y=1 meaning hospitalized vs. not hospitalized) that must be fixed.  
- Readers need more intuition and worked examples for the algebraic bounds showing how relaxations affect tightness relative to IV.  
- Originality: novel combination of reinterpreting Rule 1, posterior-over-bounds with constrained Dirichlet priors, algebraic relaxations, and back-substitution solver.  
- Significance: addresses a practical gap for observational studies with weak CI evidence and no gold-standard IV, potentially broadly useful if CI-to-parameter links are clarified.  
- Pros: tackles an important problem, offers Bayesian partial-identification stance, and includes practical computational advances and illustrative experiments.  
- Cons: under-justified conceptual link to Rule 1, unspecified relaxation parameter selection, dense presentation, and missing baseline/sensitivity analyses.  
- Minor issues: confusing phrasing about faithfulness violations, citation/table mismatches, and requested inclusion of Entner et al.’s baseline and clearer influenza coding.  
- Recommendation: Borderline — weak accept if authors clarify Rule 1’s role and add requested baseline/sensitivity analyses; otherwise lean weak reject.
