- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the average causal effect (ACE) when causal structure is partially unknown, modifying Entner et al.’s rule and relaxing faithfulness to bounded local-independence violations.  
- Identifies “witness” variables as instrumental variables under relaxed assumptions.  
- Employs linear programming (with further relaxations) to compute ACE bounds and improve computational efficiency.  
- Builds on and extends Entner et al. (2013) and Ramsahai (2012) to handle partial causal-structure knowledge.  
- Experiments (synthetic and influenza vaccination data) show WPP gives meaningful bounds where point estimators can fail.  
- Main contribution is a sensible complementary framework for causal inference.  
- Critical issue: the role and justification of Entner et al.’s rule conditions without faithfulness is unclear; link to relaxation parameters is unresolved.  
- Paper fails to convincingly explain why Entner et al.’s specific conditions are necessary, undermining their fundamental importance.  
- Minor problems: conceptually inaccurate phrasing about faithfulness violations, a citation/table error (Table 1 cited as Table 5), and unclear interpretation of a negative ACE in the vaccine example.  
- Technical approach and computational claims appear sound, but the theoretical justification for key methodological choices needs strengthening.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and promising but recommend rejection pending substantial revision to clarify theoretical connections and methodological choices.
