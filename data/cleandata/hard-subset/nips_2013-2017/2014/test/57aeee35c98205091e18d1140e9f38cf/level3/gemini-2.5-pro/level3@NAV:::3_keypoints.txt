- Proposal: Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to compute ACE bounds by relaxing faithfulness, combining Entner et al.’s witness search with linear programming and relaxation parameters.
- Novelty: Novel combination of existing search and bounding techniques, pushing toward interval-based reasoning rather than brittle point estimates.
- Strength: Principled attempt to quantify near-violations of faithfulness using LP; technical formulation appears sound.
- Major weakness: Selection method for critical relaxation parameters is deferred to future work, undermining practical applicability and assessment.
- Major weakness: Conceptual interpretation of outputs (a distribution over bounds) is unclear and offers no guidance on using these nonstandard intervals.
- Major weakness: Experimental results often produce extremely wide, potentially uninformative bounds (e.g., influenza dataset ACE in [-0.23, 0.64]).
- Major weakness: The “bias” metric used for comparison is ill-defined for interval estimates, making comparisons to point estimators meaningless.
- Major weakness: Evaluation does not demonstrate clear advantage over standard faithfulness-based estimates and omits sensitivity analysis to sample size.
- Clarity issues: Algorithmic description is hard to follow; many parameters are introduced without sufficient intuition.
- Related work omission: Key analytical-bounds literature (e.g., Geiger et al., UAI 2014) is not cited.
- Overall: Interesting and original idea with potential, but requires substantial revision on parameter selection, interpretation, clarity, and experimental validation.
