- Proposal: Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to partially identify ACE of binary X→Y by inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to generate (W,Z) witness–admissible-set pairs and constructing IV-like bounds under relaxed faithfulness.
- Method: Encodes faithfulness violations as bounded deviations via parameters (w,x,y,β,β̄), adapts Dawid/Ramsahai/Balke-Pearl LP polytopes, and uses a constrained-Dirichlet Bayesian sampler to produce a posterior over bounds.
- Scalability: Derives algebraic inequalities and a back-substitution “message-passing” algorithm yielding conservative box constraints and fast approximate bounds.
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments compare WPP to naïve back-door and faithfulness-based estimators; a flu-vaccine case study shows narrower bounds than classical binary-IV bounds.
- Relation to prior work: Sits between faithfulness-based causal discovery and partial-identification/instrumental-variable literature, combining witness search with parameterized relaxations and Bayesian uncertainty over observables.
- Strength — conceptual: Elegant inversion of Rule 1 to propose candidate conditional-IV pairs when exact faithfulness is dubious.
- Strength — theoretical: LP/polytopal formulation and Bayesian treatment are appropriate and conservative for partial identification.
- Strength — practical: Algebraic bounds and back-substitution provide scalable, monotone approximations.
- Strength — empirical: Demonstrates cases with and without valid witnesses and a real-data example with tighter bounds than standard IV.
- Weakness — justification of witness use: Paper treats Rule-1 (W,Z) as a proposal mechanism but does not formally justify why these pairs yield smaller relaxation parameters or tighter/nonvacuous bounds under relaxed faithfulness.
- Weakness — parameter calibration: Informativeness depends on user-chosen (w,x,y,β,β̄); lacking principled mapping from observed near-independences to these parameters and guidance/defaults.
- Weakness — model falsification procedure: The 95% threshold for constrained-Dirichlet rejection is ad hoc; sensitivity, false acceptance/rejection rates, and fallback-frequency/impact are not reported.
- Clarity issue — wording: The phrase about “violations of faithfulness” is misleading; should say encoding approximate/near-independences by bounding pathway magnitudes.
- Clarity issue — mapping tests to parameters: No explicit connection from statistical tests (e.g., W ⟂ Y | Z ∪ {X}) to numeric parameter choices; an illustrative subsection is needed.
- Clarity issue — minor refs: Page/figure/table cross-references are inconsistent and need fixing.
- Originality query: Novel combination overall, but need to clarify how algebraic bounds extend Ramsahai’s results (especially beyond fixing w) and distinguish new vs. prior derivations.
- Significance conditional: WPP could be a valuable middle ground between point estimates and wide IV bounds if witness-selection justification and parameter calibration are strengthened.
- Empirical recommendations: Add comparison to Entner et al.’s method (treating “don’t know” as [−1,1]), ablations (random/heuristic (W,Z), different mappings from near-independence to parameters, LP vs. back-substitution runtimes), and report coverage and average width alongside existing metrics.
- Flu example issues: Inconsistent coding of Y and captioning; need to reconcile Y=1 definition, justify excluding GRP from admissible sets, and show sensitivity of bounds to GRP inclusion and parameter choices.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, bridges discovery and partial identification novelly, and provides exact and fast approximate algorithms with code.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient theoretical justification for relying on Entner’s conditions after relaxing faithfulness, lack of principled parameter calibration, and limited sensitivity/coverage reporting plus clarity issues.
- Overall evaluation: Interesting and potentially impactful, but needs clearer theoretical justification for witness selection under approximate faithfulness, principled parameter calibration, and more thorough empirical validation to be strengthened.
