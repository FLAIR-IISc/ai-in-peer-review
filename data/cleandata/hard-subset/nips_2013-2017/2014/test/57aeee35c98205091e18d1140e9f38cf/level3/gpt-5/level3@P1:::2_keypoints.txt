- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP): combines inverse use of Entner et al.’s Rule 1, bounded faithfulness relaxations, and LP to bound average causal effect (ACE).
- Generates candidate (W, Z) pairs by reversing Rule 1 and treats W as instrument-like conditional on Z.
- Introduces bounded violations of local independences (parameters w, x, y, β, β̄) to allow controlled path cancellations.
- Formulates sharp/approximate ACE bounds via linear programming derived from these relaxations.
- Produces a posterior over bounds by constrained-Dirichlet priors on P(Y,X,W|Z) with sampling under inequality constraints.
- Derives algebraic (symbolic) bounds and a fast back-substitution procedure that yields slightly looser but much faster bounds.
- Empirical results: synthetic and influenza vaccine data show WPP can produce narrower intervals than standard IV and be more robust than naive faithfulness point estimates.
- Situates work between faithfulness-based discovery (Spirtes, Pearl; Entner et al.) and partial-identification IV bounds (Balke–Pearl; Dawid; Ramsahai–Lauritzen).
- Technical soundness: LP and algebraic relaxations are plausible and consistent with IV literature.
- Major theoretical gap: inverse use of Entner’s Rule 1 lacks formal conditions guaranteeing validity of resulting bounds.
- Bayesian component coherent in principle, but the >95% rejection falsification rule is ad hoc and unstudied; sensitivity to priors/BDeu is underexplored.
- Empirical limitations: synthetic scenarios tuned to favor failure of naive back-door; limited range of SCMs, small Z, and missing stress-tests for near-unfaithfulness.
- Real-data evaluation is limited: single dataset, few baselines, and sparse robustness checks.
- Clarity issues: scattered/underspecified definitions (η, δ, ζ, κ, ω; relaxation params), typos, broken references, and ambiguous figures/tables.
- Algorithmic details lacking: search over Z, independence testing setup, constraint construction, sampling diagnostics, and complexity analysis need clearer pseudocode.
- Originality: novel synthesis of existing components and original algebraic bounds under bounded violations, but novelty depends on validity of inverse Rule 1.
- Significance: promising practical tool that interpolates between point identification and conservative IV bounds if validated and made usable.
- Pros for acceptance: conceptual bridge, formal LP + fast algebraic relaxations, Bayesian uncertainty over bounds, and promising empirical signs of tighter bounds.
- Cons for acceptance: no rigorous treatment of inverse Rule 1, no data-driven parameter selection, narrow experiments, and reproducibility/clarity shortcomings.
- Key suggestions: formalize conditions for inverse Rule 1, provide guidance/heuristics and sensitivity plots for relaxation params, expand experiments (diverse SCMs, baselines, ablations), and strengthen Bayesian diagnostics and algorithmic documentation.
