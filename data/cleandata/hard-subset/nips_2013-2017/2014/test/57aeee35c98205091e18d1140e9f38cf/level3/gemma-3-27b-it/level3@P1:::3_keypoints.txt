- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE under potential faithfulness violations by inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1 and adding relaxation parameters.
- Core idea: use conditional independencies with relaxation parameters to derive ACE bounds via linear programming and Bayesian inference.
- Builds on prior causal inference work (Pearl, Spirtes et al., Entner et al.) and connects to instrumental variable literature.
- Strength: technically rigorous mathematical formulation and sound linear programming framework.
- Strength: systematic relaxation of faithfulness using interpretable parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) and a Bayesian component to handle estimation uncertainty.
- Weakness: poor conceptual interpretability of the relaxation parameters and unclear links to real-world scenarios.
- Weakness: resulting ACE bounds can be very wide and thus uninformative in practice.
- Weakness: method provides no preferred point estimate, risking misinterpretation of bounds as uncertainty around a single value.
- Experimental issues: synthetic studies are unbalanced and use a problematic “bias” metric that measures different things across methods.
- Experimental issues: real-data application (influenza vaccination) yields an extremely wide interval (-0.23, 0.64), offering little practical insight.
- Major limitation: no guidance on how to choose relaxation parameters, hindering application and evaluation.
- Clarification needed: clearer explanation of the witness W’s role and why W isn’t directly used in ACE computation.
- Clarification needed: explain the relationship between the search for admissible sets Z, bounds computation, and dependence of Z on relaxation parameters θ.
- Presentation problems: ambiguous sentences, minor typos, and overall clarity issues throughout the text.
- Arguments for acceptance: originality in combining faithfulness relaxation, LP, and Bayesian inference; technical soundness; potential research significance.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical impact due to wide bounds and no parameter guidance; weak experimental validation; conceptual clarity problems.
- Overall assessment: reviewer recommends weak reject pending significant revisions (better experiments, clearer parameter interpretation, limitations discussion).
