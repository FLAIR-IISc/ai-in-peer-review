- Introduces WPP: a framework to bound ACE under weak faithfulness violations using LPs and relaxation parameters to generate posterior bounds.  
- Leverages Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to find witness-admissible set pairs (W,Z) and computes bounds via Bayesian inference.  
- Uses symbolic algebraic bounds and a back-substitution algorithm to improve computational efficiency.  
- Originality is a clear strength: novel combination of causal discovery, IV bounds, and Bayesian inference with principled relaxation parameters.  
- Analytical bounds as functions of relaxation parameters advance beyond prior numerical-only approaches.  
- Technical approach is sound: rigorous LP formulation and Bayesian posterior sampling.  
- Practical utility is limited: reported bounds are often too wide to be informative.  
- Interpretation is unclear: paper fails to state whether bounds are confidence intervals or conservative estimates.  
- Experimental evaluation is unbalanced: synthetic stability shown, but the "hard" case (no witness) is poorly addressed.  
- Influenza application does not convincingly show WPP outperforms standard IV bounds.  
- The "bias" metric is invalid: it conflates point-estimate distance with interval coverage, misrepresenting comparisons.  
- Clarity problems: the role of the witness W is ambiguous since W identifies Z but is reportedly not used in ACE computation.  
- Parameter selection is opaque (e.g., w=x=y=0.2) and deferred to future work, hindering practical application.  
- Algorithm description lacks details on how the (W,Z) search interacts with relaxation parameters; supplement insufficient for reproduction.  
- Significance is directionally important but currently limited by lack of practical guidance and demonstrated impact.  
- The empirical impact of the back-substitution refinement is unquantified, leaving its value unclear.  
- Pro-accept: addresses a timely problem, conceptually novel, and the Bayesian approach mitigates latent-model hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Con-accept: practical utility questionable due to wide bounds, weak experimental support, omitted implementation details, and misleading metrics.  
- Recommended fixes: clarify interpretation of bounds; show bounds tighten as relaxation parameters approach faithfulness; clarify witness role and parameter selection; compare to state-of-the-art using consistent error metrics.
