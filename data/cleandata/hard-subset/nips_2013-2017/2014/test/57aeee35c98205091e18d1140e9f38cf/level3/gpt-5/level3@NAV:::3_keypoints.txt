- Paper proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE under violations of faithfulness using quasi-instruments, relaxation parameters, LP, and a Bayesian posterior over bounds.  
- Originality/significance: timely problem, novel combination of witness search, relaxations, and LP with solid technical derivations.  
- Conceptual interpretability: posterior over bounds is not a posterior over the ACE and depends on unquantified likelihoods of unfaithful cases.  
- Many relaxation settings produce very wide, often uninformative bounds, limiting practical usefulness.  
- Method provides no preferred point estimate, risking misinterpretation of intervals as point estimates.  
- Experimental evaluation is unbalanced and undermines practical claims: inappropriate "bias" metric, lack of coverage/width/calibration/decision-risk analysis.  
- Experiments use large sample size (n=5000), so results may not generalize to typical smaller samples.  
- Real-data example (influenza) yields intervals too broad to guide practice, questioning practical impact.  
- Role of the witness W is unclear: used to find Z but not needed for ACE computation; interaction between search and bounds needs clarification.  
- How admissible sets depend on relaxation parameters θ and how to choose θ is underexplained; applied use is difficult without guidance.  
- Algorithmic description contains many subtly different parameters, harming clarity and reproducibility.  
- Claims of novelty for analytical bounds need careful comparison to prior work (e.g., Geiger et al., standard IV bounds).  
- Impact of the back-substitution refinement is asserted but not empirically substantiated; ablation studies requested.  
- Requested edits: clarify background usage, explain "max. 10 free parameters," fix typos, explain multiple bounds from different admissible sets, and explicitly state bounds ≠ confidence intervals.  
- Positive for acceptance: addresses important gap, original approach, solid technical core, promising direction for future work.  
- Negative for acceptance: weak/misleading empirical evaluation, ambiguity in interpretation and parameter selection, reproducibility concerns, and often uninformative bounds.  
- Overall recommendation: interesting and promising but falls short on clarity, evaluation, and practical guidance; reviewer leans toward rejection with invitation to resubmit after substantial revision.
