- Proposes WPP algorithm to compute ACE bounds by relaxing faithfulness with parameters and linear programming.
- Core idea of inverting Entner’s Rule 1 and using witnesses as instrumental variables with bounded path cancellations is interesting.
- Conceptual interpretability is weak because bound distributions depend on unquantified likelihoods of unfaithful instances.
- Many relaxation choices produce excessively wide, potentially uninformative bounds.
- No recommended point estimate is provided, risking misinterpretation of bounds as confidence intervals.
- Experimental evaluation is unbalanced and lacks strong evidence of practical benefit.
- The “bias” comparison metric is ill-defined across methods, making comparisons meaningless.
- Influenza dataset results (ACE in (-0.23, 0.64)) are particularly uninformative for real-world utility.
- Technical derivations appear sound and detailed in the supplement, but some derivations and overall context remain unclear.
- Deferring selection of relaxation parameters to future work severely limits practical application.
- Algorithm description is complex with many subtly different parameters, harming clarity and reproducibility.
- The paper fails to adequately cite closely related work (e.g., Geiger, Janzing, Schölkopf UAI2014) when claiming novelty.
- The back-substitution refinement step’s effectiveness is questionable and lacks empirical validation.
- Explanation for why multiple bounds arise (different admissible sets for different witnesses) is insufficient.
- Sensitivity to sample size is a critical concern; results from 5000 samples may not generalize to smaller datasets.
- Reported tables and summaries do not allow proper assessment of WPP’s benefit over standard faithfulness-based estimators.
- Paper’s tone sometimes veers into emotive or dismissive language about Bayesian/theory-driven approaches and should be neutralized.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel combination of techniques, principled attempt to handle faithfulness violations, promising direction for future work.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak interpretability, overly wide bounds, poor experimental validation, lack of guidance, reproducibility concerns, deferred parameter choices.
- Revision request: clarify the role of the witness W and acknowledge it may not be necessary for ACE computation.
- Revision request: provide intuition for relaxation parameters and their link to weak interactions.
- Revision request: describe the search step and how admissible sets Z depend on relaxation parameters θ.
- Revision request: fix ambiguous sentences and typos, and explicitly cite related literature.
- Revision request: empirically evaluate and justify back-substitution, explain multiple bounds, address sample-size sensitivity, provide meaningful comparisons, and give clearer guidance on interpreting the bounds (emphasize they are not confidence intervals).
