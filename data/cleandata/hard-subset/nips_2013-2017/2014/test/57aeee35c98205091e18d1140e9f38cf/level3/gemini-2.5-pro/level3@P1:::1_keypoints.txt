- Presents the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the ACE from observational data by using conditional independence tests to suggest a weak IV structure and relaxing faithfulness via bounded dependencies.  
- Novelty/significance: offers a principled middle ground between strong faithfulness-based point estimates and uninformative wide bounds, combining Entner et al.'s search with IV-style bounding.  
- Technical contribution: full pipeline from data to posterior over bounds, including Bayesian handling of sampling uncertainty, model falsification, symbolic algebraic bounds, and a scalable back-substitution algorithm.  
- Potential impact: could become a practical tool for causal inference if methodological justifications are strengthened.  
- Main concern — justification for Rule 1: unclear why (approximate) satisfaction of Entner et al.’s independence conditions implies a structure near a standard IV when faithfulness is relaxed.  
- Main concern — leap to structural constraints: paper jumps from observed statistical patterns to imposing equations (3)–(6) without clear theoretical grounding.  
- Choice of relaxation parameters: leaving w, y, x, β, β̄ to the user is weak; the data’s degree of conditional dependence should inform these settings and a more integrated method is needed.  
- Question of generality: unclear if machinery depends crucially on Rule 1 or could use any candidate W with data-driven relaxation parameter selection.  
- Minor — wording on “violations of faithfulness”: should clarify that the method addresses near-unfaithful distributions (small dependencies) rather than modeling arbitrary faithfulness violations.  
- Minor — experimental comparison: recommend reporting Entner et al.’s method under original stringent thresholds and treating “I don’t know” cases as uninformative intervals for fairer comparison.  
- Minor — influenza study wording: explicitly state that a negative ACE means the vaccine reduces hospitalization to improve clarity.  
- Minor — typographical error: reference to "Table 5" vs. the labeled table (mislabeled in text).
