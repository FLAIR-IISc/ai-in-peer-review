- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE by relaxing faithfulness, inverting Entner’s Rule 1, and using linear programming to account for unmeasured confounding.  
- Properly positions work in causal-inference literature (Pearl, Spirtes, Dawid, Ramsahai) and builds on Entner et al. and Ramsahai (2016).  
- Novel combination of existing ideas, but originality limited because LP ACE-bounding and Entner’s rule are pre-existing.  
- Primary weakness: insufficient justification for inverting Entner’s Rule 1; validity is not theoretically established.  
- Reliance on the specific graph structures shown in Figure 2 should be stated as an explicit assumption, not left implicit.  
- The assumed graph structure (Figure 2) appears ad-hoc and not foundationally motivated.  
- No principled guidance for choosing relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), despite their strong effect on bounds.  
- Parameter selection discussion is deferred to the supplement; the main text needs core guidance and methods (e.g., sensitivity or data-driven selection).  
- Theoretical exposition omits key justifications (inverse-rule validity and parameter choice implications).  
- Transition from conceptual framework to LP formulation is abrupt and needs clearer derivation showing how relaxations map to LP constraints.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: implementation details are vague and parameter definitions in tables are unclear or missing.  
- Synthetic experiments lack robustness checks and sensitivity analyses over parameter settings and graph structures.  
- Real-data example is interesting but provides limited validation due to questionable witness selection and post-hoc interpretation.  
- Writing issues (typos, inconsistent table references) reduce clarity and professionalism.  
- Potential upside: method can yield tighter ACE bounds than some existing methods.  
- Promising idea by combining Entner’s Rule, LP, and relaxed faithfulness to address confounding.  
- Significance uncertain because limited experiments and theoretical gaps leave generality and robustness unproven.  
- Recommendation: reject now; require stronger theoretical justification, principled parameter-selection, clear implementation details, and comprehensive empirical validation for reconsideration.
