- Introduces WPP to estimate ACE bounds under unmeasured confounding by leveraging observed conditional independencies and relaxing faithfulness.  
- Re-purposes Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to identify candidate "witness" variables as instrumental variables rather than admissible back-door sets.  
- Introduces relaxation parameters {w, y, x, β, β̄} to quantify violations of conditional independencies and encodes them as LP constraints.  
- Builds on Dawid (2003) and Ramsahai (2012) to produce a posterior distribution over ACE bounds via linear programming.  
- Experiments indicate WPP can yield narrower and potentially more accurate bounds than standard approaches.  
- Major concern: treating a Rule 1 witness as an IV lacks theoretical justification and required causal assumptions are unstated.  
- Method performance depends critically on user-specified relaxation parameters with no principled, data-driven selection procedure.  
- No sensitivity analysis or practical guidance for choosing relaxation parameters, harming interpretability and reproducibility.  
- Clarity problems: algorithmic details (e.g., back-substitution) and synthetic data generation are insufficiently described.  
- Manuscript contains carelessness (missing Table 1 and incorrect/missing references) that impedes understanding.  
- Originality: novel synthesis of existing ideas (variable selection, near-unfaithfulness, LP bounds) though components are not individually new.  
- Significance: high potential value if theoretical grounding and parameter-selection issues are resolved; currently limited by those weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, proposes a clever "soft" faithfulness approach, and shows promising preliminary results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical basis for the central premise, reliance on arbitrary parameters, clarity/errors, and limited empirical validation.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful idea but incomplete—requires stronger theoretical justification and practical guidance before it can be confidently evaluated.
