**Paper Review: Causal Inference through a Witness Protection Program**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a novel approach for estimating causal effects in the presence of unobserved confounders. The method, called the Witness Protection Program (WPP), combines ideas from instrumental variable analysis and causal discovery to provide bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) of a variable X on a variable Y. The authors relax the faithfulness assumption, which is commonly used in causal discovery, and instead use a linear programming approach to generate bounds on the ACE.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper proposes a new method for estimating causal effects in the presence of unobserved confounders, which is a significant contribution to the field of causal inference.
2. **Relaxing faithfulness assumption**: The authors relax the faithfulness assumption, which is a common assumption in causal discovery, and instead use a linear programming approach to generate bounds on the ACE.
3. **Providing bounds**: The method provides bounds on the ACE, which is a more robust estimate than a single point estimate.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Technical details**: The paper is technically dense, and some of the details are hard to follow.
2. **Unclear role of Entner et al.'s rule**: The authors use Entner et al.'s rule to generate candidate instrumental variables, but the role of this rule in the method is not entirely clear.
3. **Lack of clarity on relaxation parameters**: The authors introduce relaxation parameters to control the degree of faithfulness, but the choice of these parameters is not clearly explained.
4. **Minor errors**: There are some minor errors in the paper, such as a citation error and a table error.
