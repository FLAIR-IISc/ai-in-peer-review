- Proposal: Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE under bounded faithfulness violations via relaxation parameters and LP-based bounds.  
- Method: Inverts Entner–Hoyer–Spirtes Rule 1, treating a witness W as an instrument conditional on admissible Z and encoding relaxations as linear constraints defining a polytope.  
- Approximation: Derives algebraic/back-substitution bounds as a faster, looser alternative to full LP, but tightness is unproven and empirical benefits are underquantified.  
- Bayesian layer: Places Dirichlet priors on P(Y,X,W|Z) and P(Z) to return a posterior over ACE bounds, not point estimates.  
- Technical soundness: LP formulation and mapping from relaxations to linear constraints are sound and supported by supplement derivations.  
- Interpretability issue: Posterior over bounds depends heavily on selected independence tests, θ, and priors, making results hard to interpret without guidance on θ.  
- Risk of misinterpretation: Readers may mistake intervals for point estimates; the paper needs explicit warnings and decision-theoretic summaries.  
- Empirical weaknesses: Synthetic experiments use unusually large n (5000), limited baselines, and an inappropriate "bias" metric that conflates intervals and point estimates.  
- Evaluation metrics: Should use interval-specific criteria (coverage, width, decision loss) and test smaller realistic sample sizes (e.g., 300–1000).  
- Influenza example: WPP gives a narrower interval than IV under chosen θ but lacks justification that the relaxations are appropriate or robust.  
- Relation to prior work: Novel combination of faithfulness relaxations, LP bounds, and Bayesian posterior, but should cite and contrast Geiger–Janzing–Schölkopf (UAI’14) and other inequality-based methods.  
- Clarity issues: Descriptions of the search for (W,Z), role of W, dependence of admissible sets on θ, and aggregation across (W,Z) are insufficiently explained.  
- Notation and reproducibility: Algorithmic steps, interplay of parameters (ζ, κ, ω, η, δ), typos, broken URL, and missing implementation details hinder reproducibility.  
- Search dependence on θ: Unclear whether search for (W,Z) is re-run as θ changes and how falsification/thresholds are handled; report how often models are rejected.  
- Back-substitution needs quantification: Must report runtime and bound-width differences versus full LP and whether it affects accept/reject decisions.  
- Relaxation parameter guidance: Provide intuition, interpretable scales (risk differences/ORs), sensitivity plots, default priors/heuristics, or place priors on θ and marginalize.  
- Aggregation of multiple bounds: Explain why multiple bounds arise and recommend aggregation strategies (intersection, union, or posterior-weighted averaging).  
- Baselines missing: Compare to Entner et al.’s estimator, standard IV (with/without monotonicity), and methods that bound under partial confounding (e.g., Geiger et al.).  
- Role of W clarified: State explicitly that W is used to identify Z via Rule 1; once Z is fixed, ACE bounds depend on (X,Y,Z) and relaxations, not W.  
- Tone and presentation: Soften emotive claims about Bayesian/theory-driven approaches and fix ambiguous sentences.  
- Pros: Addresses robustness to weak unfaithfulness, sound LP formulation, and useful algebraic approximation; distinct from standard IV and faithfulness-based identification.  
- Cons: Posterior-over-bounds lacks interpretability without θ guidance, many relaxations yield wide intervals, experiments and metrics are unconvincing, and reproducibility/clarity need work.  
- Recommendation: Promising and technically solid direction but not practitioner-ready; revise with clearer exposition, stronger evaluation, θ guidance, and reproducible code.
