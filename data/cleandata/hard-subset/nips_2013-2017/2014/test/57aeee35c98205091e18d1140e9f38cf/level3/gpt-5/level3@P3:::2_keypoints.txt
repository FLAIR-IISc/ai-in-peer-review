1. Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE of binary X on Y using observational data by combining inverse use of Entner’s Rule 1, bounded faithfulness relaxations, and LP-based constraints.

2. Introduces relaxations: bounded path-cancellation (w,x,y) and bounded W–U dependence (β, β̄), plus algebraic bounds, back-substitution solver, and a Bayesian posterior over ACE bounds.

3. Positions WPP between faithfulness-based point identification and worst-case nonparametric bounds, building on Balke–Pearl/Dawid/Ramsahai LPs, Entner Rule 1, and faithfulness literature.

4. Strength: Clear and compelling motivation for a middle-ground approach when point estimates are fragile and IV bounds are wide.

5. Strength: Conceptual novelty in integrating inverse Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, and LP bounds into a unified framework.

6. Strength: Technical soundness of the LP derivations and algebraic inequalities, with appropriate connections to known IV polytopes.

7. Strength: Empirical promise—reported bounds are often substantially tighter than standard IV bounds even with a non-optimized implementation.

8. Strength: Back-substitution is a useful engineering contribution that reduces computational cost while largely preserving tightness.

9. Weakness: No theoretical justification for applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction; unclear when resulting constraints are conservative for ACE.

10. Weakness: No generic, data-driven criteria or principled guidance for selecting relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄), despite their strong influence on bounds.

11. Weakness: Experimental section is limited and under-described—missing algorithmic details, priors, Z-search, falsification rates, undefined table parameters, and sampler settings.

12. Weakness: Bayesian posterior over bounds lacks clarification on its inferential meaning and how to fairly summarize or compare interval estimates.

13. Clarity issues: Omits essential discussion of inverse-Rule validity and parameter selection; contains typos, incorrect/missing table references, and unclear parameter definitions.

14. Originality: Core components are known; novelty lies in their integration, which is meaningful but incremental without theoretical validation of inverse-Rule step.

15. Significance: Potentially significant as a practical default for ACE bounding, but generality and robustness are unassessed due to limited experiments and missing comparisons.

16. Suggestion: Explicitly state and justify the inverse-Rule assumption, provide structural conditions (e.g., ADMG/PAG), and prove constraint conservativeness or give counterexamples.

17. Suggestion: Provide principled guidance for choosing relaxation parameters—mapping empirical evidence to relaxation magnitudes, defaults, priors, sensitivity grids, and reporting bound variation.

18. Suggestion: Expand experiments with baselines (Balke–Pearl, Ramsahai, Entner Rule 1, back-door), ablations, stress tests, and computational cost/acceptance-rate reporting.

19. Suggestion: Clarify algorithmic details—Z search, model selection criteria for (approximate) independence, aggregation over (W,Z) candidates, and falsification procedures.

20. Suggestion: Fix typos, align notation, ensure correct table/figure references, and include full parameter definitions in tables and supplement.

21. Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important practical need, presents a unified technically coherent framework with promising tightness, and offers a scalable back-substitution tool.

22. Cons for acceptance: Lacks theoretical justification for inverse Rule 1, no principled parameter-selection methodology, and limited/under-documented experiments with clarity issues.

23. Overall evaluation: Promising and potentially impactful but requires revision—add theoretical justification for inverse Rule 1, principled parameter guidance and sensitivity analysis, and substantially improved experimental and algorithmic clarity.
