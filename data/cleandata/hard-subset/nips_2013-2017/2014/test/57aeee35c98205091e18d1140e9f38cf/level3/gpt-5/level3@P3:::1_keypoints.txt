- Paper proposes WPP: bounding ACE between binary X and Y by treating Entner et al.’s Rule 1 witness W as a conditional instrument and relaxing faithfulness via bounded “approximate independence” parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄).  
- Uses LP-based Ramsahai/Dawid-style bounds, Bayesian posterior over bounds, and algebraic back-substitution for faster approximate bounds.  
- Connects IV bounds, faithfulness-based covariate selection, partial identification, and sensitivity analysis literature.  
- Strength: tackles credible causal inference with incomplete causal structure and possible unfaithfulness.  
- Strength: sensible idea to treat discovered (approximate) independencies as soft constraints that shrink IV bounds instead of forcing point identification.  
- Strength: interpretable parametric relaxations, integration of LP and Bayesian layers, and scalable algebraic approximations.  
- Strength: empirical results show robustness in synthetic data and tighter intervals than standard IV bounds on a flu vaccination dataset.  
- Strength: code availability and practical guidance provided.  
- Major weakness: unclear role of Entner et al.’s Rule 1 once faithfulness is dropped—W ⊥ Y | X,Z and W ⊥̸ Y | Z do not guarantee IV-like structural separations without faithfulness.  
- Request: clarify what Rule 1 premises imply absent faithfulness—are there any nonparametric structural constraints or is Rule 1 just a heuristic for choosing (W,Z)?  
- Request: explain how relaxation parameters are “tethered” to observed (approximate) independencies (e.g., mapping from p-values or BDeu score gaps to parameter magnitudes).  
- Request: show whether WPP requires Entner’s conditions or if identical bounds result from searching arbitrary (W,Z) pairs (ablation suggested).  
- Concern: paper defers calibration of (w,x,y,β,β̄); needs concrete guidance, mappings from model selection evidence to default ranges, sensitivity analyses, and prior elicitation schemes.  
- Conceptual issue: phrasing “violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is misleading—unfaithfulness typically yields extra independencies, not “almost independence”; rephrase and clarify.  
- Experimental concern: quantify risk of spurious/approximate witnesses when no exact witness exists, and show impact on coverage; compare to Entner et al.’s original stringent thresholds with “don’t know” = [−1,1].  
- Clarity/reproducibility: provide a small worked example mapping observed tables to κ, ω, constraints, and final ACE bounds; summarize key back-substitution inequalities and quantify widening vs. exact LP.  
- Technical questions: state coverage guarantees—if DGP satisfies relaxation constraints, do LP bounds always contain true ACE, and how do sampling error and posterior rejection thresholds affect this?  
- Technical questions: sensitivity of posterior over bounds to constrained Dirichlet hyperparameters and whether rejection sampling pathologies occur beyond the 95% cutoff heuristic.  
- Technical questions: provide empirical runtime scaling in |Z| and sample size for both LP and back-substitution methods.  
- Minor comments: fix sign-convention inconsistency for X and Y coding in text; correct table reference (Table 5 → Table 1); revise wording on faithfulness violations.  
- Evaluation: idea is sound, technically substantial and original, but main weakness is unclear justification for Entner’s rules without faithfulness and need for stronger empirical validation.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially useful, but requires clarification of methodological connections (especially role of Entner et al.), parameter-selection guidance, and additional experiments to strengthen claims.
