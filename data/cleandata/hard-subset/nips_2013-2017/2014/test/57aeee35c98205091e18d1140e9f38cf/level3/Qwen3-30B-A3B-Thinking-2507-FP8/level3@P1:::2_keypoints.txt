- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to estimate average causal effects under possible unobserved confounding by relaxing faithfulness and allowing path cancellations.  
- Inverts Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to use "witnesses" as instrumental variables and formulates ACE bounds via linear programming.  
- Extends Dawid (1979) and Ramsahai (2011) LP frameworks and introduces constraints that encode relaxed faithfulness violations.  
- Produces posterior distributions over ACE bounds using a Bayesian treatment that avoids priors on unidentifiable parameters.  
- Introduces a back-substitution algorithm to improve computational efficiency.  
- Strength: clear motivation and theoretically grounded approach offering more honest (interval) inference than point estimates.  
- Strength: empirical results show tighter bounds than standard IV methods in synthetic and a real influenza vaccination study.  
- Strength: Bayesian uncertainty treatment is notable for stability compared to prior methods sensitive to priors.  
- Critical weakness: no clear justification or formal conditions for inverting Entner's Rule 1; required structural assumptions are not stated.  
- Critical weakness: no generic criteria or guidance for choosing relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄); defaults appear arbitrary.  
- Theoretical exposition omits essential discussion on inverse-rule validity and parameter selection.  
- Experimental implementation details are poorly described, with unclear parameter definitions, incorrect table references, typos, and an under-specified Algorithm 1 harming reproducibility.  
- Originality limited: method combines existing ideas (inverse Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness, LP) but does not sufficiently distinguish itself from Entner et al., Ramsahai, Geiger et al.  
- Significance promising but currently unproven due to limited experiments and lack of robustness/generalizability validation.  
- Recommendation: reject unless authors strengthen theoretical justification for inverse Rule 1, provide clear parameter-selection guidance, improve experimental details, and better differentiate from prior work.
