- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a linear-programming framework to bound Average Causal Effect (ACE) under bounded violations of faithfulness.  
- Proposes relaxing the faithfulness assumption via bounded local independence violations to handle unmeasured confounding.  
- Combines instrumental-variable ideas and causal discovery (conditional independencies) to generate admissible sets and witnesses.  
- Develops symbolic bounds and a Bayesian posterior inference framework for ACE estimation.  
- Presents a computationally efficient back-substitution algorithm as a faster alternative to full numerical solutions.  
- Provides R code, supporting transparency and reproducibility.  
- Strength: novel, theoretically grounded method that bridges point estimation and theoretical bounds.  
- Strength: practical relevance demonstrated by synthetic experiments and an influenza vaccination study.  
- Strength: clear theoretical connections to prior work and insightful derivation of symbolic bounds.  
- Weakness: dense exposition assumes strong familiarity with causal inference; needs clearer examples/visuals.  
- Weakness: limited empirical validation—more diverse real-world datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: choice and sensitivity of relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄) not fully explored.  
- Weakness: potential scalability issues due to exhaustive search for admissible sets in high dimensions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand validation, and give guidance on parameter selection.
