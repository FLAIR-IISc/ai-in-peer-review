- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP): relaxes faithfulness to produce bounds on ACE using conditional independencies, linear programming, and Bayesian inference.  
- Framework identifies "witness" variables and admissible sets to exploit conditional independencies for causal bounds.  
- Builds on Entner et al., providing a clear theoretical foundation and meaningful faithfulness relaxations.  
- Bayesian treatment of uncertainty over bounds is appealing and avoids pitfalls of point estimates.  
- Empirical demonstration on synthetic data and one real-world influenza vaccination study; claims WPP can yield tighter bounds than standard IV methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only one real dataset) and synthetic results do not convincingly show practical advantages.  
- Insufficient evidence that the produced bounds are actually tighter or more useful in practice despite being "more conservative."  
- Lacks clear, practical guidance for choosing relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), which is crucial for users.  
- Theoretical novelty is solid but the paper overstates practical significance and adoption claims without broader validation.  
- Back-substitution algorithm may improve computational efficiency but the paper does not adequately justify why it is preferable to standard LP methods.  
- Overall: technically sound with potential impact, but needs more comprehensive experiments, parameter-selection guidance, and stronger practical evidence.
