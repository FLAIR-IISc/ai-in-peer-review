- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the average causal effect (ACE) by relaxing faithfulness to allow path cancellations.
- Builds on instrumental variable bounding and Entner et al.’s approach, using a linear programming formulation plus Bayesian inference.
- Motivation is clear: observational studies struggle to ensure all confounders are accounted for.
- Strength: tackles unobserved confounding with a potentially practical methodology.
- Strength: novel combination of relaxing faithfulness and a linear programming framework.
- Demonstrates awareness of and connection to key causal inference literature (Pearl, Spirtes, Dawid, Entner et al.).
- Considers analytical approximations and Bayesian learning, suggesting attention to computational scalability.
- Includes synthetic experiments and a real-world influenza vaccination example to support the approach.
- Exposition is dense and difficult to follow, with many variables introduced without sufficient intuition.
- Linear programming formulation is mathematically sound but lacks intuitive explanation and discussion of practical limits.
- Bayesian component (constrained Dirichlet priors, rejection sampling) is under-explained and feels rushed.
- Important methodological details are deferred to supplemental material, reducing the paper’s self-containedness.
- Experimental section lacks thorough comparative analysis and clear performance metrics.
- Claims of WPP being “quite stable” are not backed by clear metrics or comparisons to other methods.
- Discussion of the influenza data is brief and does not convincingly show practical advantages over existing methods.
- Claim of “greater control over violations of faithfulness” needs more rigorous justification and clearer mechanism explanation.
- Unclear how WPP compares to methods designed for robustness to model misspecification.
- Scalability and computational complexity for large-scale problems remain insufficiently addressed.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical relevance, methodological novelty, literature grounding, and presence of experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense presentation, limited comparisons, reliance on supplemental material, weak justification for claims, and unclear scalability.
- Recommended revisions: clearer intuitive explanations and examples, more thorough benchmarking against established methods, move key details into main text, and provide rigorous justification for faithfulness-related claims.
- Overall assessment: promising idea but execution falls short of NIPS standards; significant revisions and a strong rebuttal are required.
