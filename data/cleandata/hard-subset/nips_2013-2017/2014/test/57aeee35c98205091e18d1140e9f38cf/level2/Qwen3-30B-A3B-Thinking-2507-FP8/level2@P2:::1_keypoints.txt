- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a method for ACE estimation that relaxes faithfulness to allow path cancellations.  
- Reinterprets Entner et al.'s Rule 1 by using witness variables as instrumental variables rather than for back-door adjustment.  
- Proposes a joint-distribution parameterization with bounded faithfulness violations (w, y, x, β, β̄) and derives ACE bounds via linear programming.  
- Develops a Bayesian framework to produce posterior distributions over ACE bounds and quantify uncertainty.  
- Presents a back-substitution algorithm as a computationally efficient alternative to full linear programming.  
- Synthetic and influenza-vaccination experiments show WPP can yield tighter, more informative bounds than standard IV methods when faithfulness is violated.  
- Strength: Theoretical contribution is sound and provides a principled middle ground between point estimates and worst-case IV bounds.  
- Weakness: No practical guidance on selecting relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), limiting applicability for practitioners.  
- Weakness: Computational complexity and scalability are not empirically evaluated or compared against alternatives.  
- Weakness: Real-world influenza example is underdeveloped and does not convincingly demonstrate practical advantage over standard IV bounds.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to recent related methods, weakening claims of originality.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to small-dimensional problems (|W| = 8), not demonstrating utility in complex real-world settings.  
- Quality: Theoretical derivations are clear and correct, but limited empirical validation reduces practical impact.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured and precise but assumes substantial prior causal-inference knowledge and lacks implementation examples.  
- Originality: Meaningful relaxation of faithfulness, but the approach overlaps with prior ideas and isn't sharply distinguished from recent work.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem, but lack of parameter-selection guidance and scalability tests prevent it from being transformative.  
- Recommendation: Strengthen by providing concrete parameter-selection heuristics, scalability/timing experiments, fuller real-world analysis, and thorough comparisons to recent alternatives.
