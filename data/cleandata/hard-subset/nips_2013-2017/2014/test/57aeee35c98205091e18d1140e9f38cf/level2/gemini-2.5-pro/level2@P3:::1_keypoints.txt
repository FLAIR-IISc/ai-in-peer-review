- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to derive bounds on the Average Causal Effect (ACE) by relaxing the faithfulness assumption.  
- Builds on Entner et al.’s Rule 1, repurposing witness-admissible set pairs by treating the witness as a candidate instrumental variable (IV).  
- Parameterizes and bounds potential IV-assumption violations with user-defined relaxation parameters to allow weak dependencies/path cancellations.  
- Uses linear programming frameworks (Dawid, Ramsahai) to derive ACE bounds that account for these bounded violations.  
- Introduces a Bayesian approach with a constrained Dirichlet prior over observables to obtain a posterior over ACE bounds.  
- Proposes algebraic bounds and an efficient back-substitution algorithm as computationally cheaper approximations to the full LP.  
- Methodology is technically sound and well-grounded in causal discovery, IVs, and bounding literature.  
- Motivation to relax faithfulness is compelling and the relaxation formulation via bounded parameters is rigorous.  
- Experimental evaluation is strong: synthetic and real influenza-vaccination data, comparisons to relevant baselines, and tests on hard/biasy cases.  
- Paper is well-organized and generally well-written but dense, assuming high familiarity with advanced causal inference.  
- Descriptions of the linear programming steps and back-substitution algorithm are condensed and need more detailed pseudo-code or worked examples.  
- Highly original: inverts a causal discovery rule to create a robust bounding procedure, bridging constraint-based discovery and effect bounding.  
- Potentially significant impact by providing a principled sensitivity analysis for faithfulness and encouraging more robust causal conclusions from observational data.  
- Main weaknesses: lacks concrete guidance or data-driven heuristics for choosing relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄).  
- Implementation clarity needs improvement: expand LP setup and back-substitution exposition, possibly moving some background to the supplement.
