- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to derive ACE bounds under unobserved confounding, bridging faithfulness-based identification and IV-based bounds.
- Builds on Entner et al.; uses a witness W as a candidate conditional IV and interprets observed conditional independencies as evidence of "weak" rather than absent confounding.
- Introduces relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) to quantify possible violations of IV assumptions.
- Adapts a linear programming (LP) bounds framework to incorporate these relaxation constraints and compute ACE upper/lower bounds.
- Embeds the procedure in a Bayesian framework to propagate sampling uncertainty into the bounds.
- Proposes a computationally efficient approximate back-substitution algorithm to improve scalability.
- Evaluated on synthetic experiments and a real-world influenza vaccination dataset.
- Strength: original and significant contribution that provides a principled middle ground relaxing the strong faithfulness assumption.
- Strength: technically sound—clever generalization of LP bounds, Bayesian treatment, and development of algebraic bounds.
- Strength: generally clear writing, good motivation, and a helpful real-data illustration.
- Weakness: lacks detailed, principled guidance on choosing the relaxation parameters, limiting practical applicability and risking subjectivity.
- Weakness: back-substitution algorithm is described too briefly—needs more detail or pseudocode for reproducibility.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation should more thoroughly probe robustness, including adversarial cases where conditional independencies fail.
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on stronger treatment of relaxation-parameter selection and expanded algorithmic/experimental details.
