- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a novel method for bounding average causal effect (ACE) in observational studies with unobserved confounding.  
- Relaxes the standard faithfulness assumption to allow limited "path cancellations" while still exploiting conditional independencies.  
- Framework uses conditional independencies to identify "weak" paths and generate candidate instrumental variables.  
- Produces posterior distributions over ACE bounds via a linear programming approach combined with Bayesian inference.  
- Builds on Entner et al. for candidate selection and modifies Dawid & Ramsahai’s linear programming for bound computation.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical development and theoretical bounds.  
- Introduces a computationally efficient back-substitution algorithm for practical use.  
- Experimental evaluation includes synthetic simulations and a real-world influenza vaccination application showing method effectiveness.  
- Bayesian treatment gives a more honest representation of uncertainty than point estimates under faithfulness.  
- Authors analyze how relaxation parameters affect bounds, offering useful practitioner insights.  
- Limitations: experimental section needs more analysis on conditions of success and failure.  
- Supplemental Material is referenced repeatedly but not sufficiently detailed within the paper.  
- Lacks clear practical guidance for selecting relaxation parameters.  
- Well-situated in causal inference literature, significant practical impact, and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
