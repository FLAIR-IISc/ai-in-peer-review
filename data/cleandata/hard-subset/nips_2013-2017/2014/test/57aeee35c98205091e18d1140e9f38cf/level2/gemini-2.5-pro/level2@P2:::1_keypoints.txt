- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a method to derive bounds on ACE from observational data with unmeasured confounding by re-purposing "witness" variables as candidate IVs and relaxing faithfulness.  
- Synthesizes causal discovery (faithfulness/witnesses) and bounds-based causal estimation (IV linear programming) into a unified framework.  
- Extends Ramsahai’s LP formulation to incorporate controlled relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) and outputs a posterior distribution over ACE bounds.  
- Uses a Bayesian approach with a constrained Dirichlet prior to handle sampling uncertainty.  
- Proposes a back-substitution algebraic approximation as a fast, scalable alternative to the full Bayesian LP.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides accessible background and effective experiments, including a real influenza vaccine dataset application.  
- Main weakness: insufficient guidance in the main text on choosing relaxation parameters, leaving practitioners with arbitrary choices.  
- Presentation of the back-substitution algorithm is dense; needs a clearer, high-level intuitive description of the iterative tightening.  
- Lacks discussion on how to interpret or combine multiple high-scoring (witness, admissible set) pairs that may give conflicting bounds.  
- The proposed falsification test (>95% rejection) feels ad-hoc and would benefit from commentary on its interpretation (e.g., whether it signals overly strict relaxations or model mismatch).  
- Overall assessment: a novel, significant, and technically sound contribution to causal inference; weaknesses are mostly clarity/practical-guidance issues and are addressable.
