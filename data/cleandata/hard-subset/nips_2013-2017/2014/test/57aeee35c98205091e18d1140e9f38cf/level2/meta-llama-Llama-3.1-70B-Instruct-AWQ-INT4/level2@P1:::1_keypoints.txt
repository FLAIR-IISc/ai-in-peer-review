- Paper proposes a novel "witness protection program" framework that relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow for path cancellations while still estimating causal effects.
- Main technical contribution is a linear programming method to compute bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) from observational conditional independencies.
- Authors also introduce a Bayesian learning approach to capture uncertainty in ACE estimation.
- Experiments show the method is robust and yields more accurate ACE estimates than naive and faithfulness-based approaches.
- Paper is well-written and provides a clear, concise explanation of methodology.
- Authors give a thorough discussion of limitations and potential applications.
- Strength: relaxes a common causal inference assumption (faithfulness) in an innovative way.
- Strength: combines LP-based bounding with Bayesian uncertainty quantification.
- Weakness: method complexity may hinder implementation and interpretation by non-experts.
- Weakness: further theoretical and empirical research is needed to fully understand properties and limits of the approach.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited, relying on a small number of experiments.
- NIPS review criteria: scores highly on quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
- Recommendation: accept for publication, with suggestion to provide additional evaluation and discussion of limitations.
