- Proposes a novel method for bounding the average causal effect (ACE) by relaxing faithfulness and treating violations as bounded deviations from conditional independence.  
- Combines linear programming and Bayesian inference (the "Witness Protection Program") to derive robust ACE bounds using instrumental/admissible set ideas.  
- Analytic approximation and a fast algorithm are provided to mitigate computational complexity.  
- Well-motivated idea that addresses a critical and fragile assumption (faithfulness) in causal inference.  
- Promising combination of LP and Bayesian methods that can yield more realistic, informative bounds than point estimates.  
- Experimental evaluation includes synthetic data and a real influenza vaccination example, offering preliminary empirical support.  
- Presentation is dense, uses heavy notation, and assumes significant background, reducing accessibility.  
- Jump from conceptual discussion to technical LP formulation is abrupt; parameterization section feels disconnected and hard to follow.  
- Sensitivity analysis of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) is limited and insufficiently explored.  
- Computational costs are acknowledged but lack detailed benchmarking and scalability analysis.  
- Comparison to existing methods is superficial and practical impact claims appear overstated.  
- Overall assessment: technically interesting and potentially valuable but current weaknesses (clarity, evaluation, scalability) warrant rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit.  
- Recommended revisions: improve clarity and notation, expand experimental validation, analyze computational scalability, and provide a more nuanced discussion of limitations and practical impact.
