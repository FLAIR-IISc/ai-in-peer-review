- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP): a linear-programming method to generate bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) using observational conditional independencies while relaxing faithfulness.  
- Allows "path cancellations" (conditional independencies that may coexist with confounding) and outputs a posterior distribution over ACE bounds via Bayesian inference.

- Novel contribution to causal-effect estimation in observational studies.  
- Relaxes the common faithfulness assumption to handle more realistic scenarios.  
- Uses Bayesian inference to produce posterior bounds on ACE, offering robustness.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on synthetic and real datasets.

- Can be computationally expensive, particularly for large datasets.  
- Lacks clear guidance for selecting relaxation parameters, which affect performance.  
- Designed for binary variables; generalizability to other variable types is unclear.

- Overall novel and potentially impactful approach with demonstrated experimental support.

- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and logically organized with adequate background.

- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, empirical evaluation, and practical potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational complexity, limited generalizability, and need for more evaluation.

- Recommended revisions: provide guidelines for choosing relaxation parameters; investigate extensions beyond binary variables; and conduct broader experimental evaluation.
