- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) that relaxes faithfulness to allow path cancellations and bounds the Average Causal Effect (ACE) in observational studies.  
- Inverts Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to identify instruments, uses linear programming for ACE bounds, and proposes relaxations for computational efficiency.  
- Novelty: pragmatic relaxation of faithfulness offers a potentially valuable contribution to causal inference.  
- Technical soundness: builds on established techniques (LP, IVs); mathematical formulations and bounding approach are sensible.  
- Strong positioning: clearly connects to relevant literature (Pearl, Spirtes, Entner, Dawid, Ramsahai).  
- Computational considerations: authors acknowledge costs and propose relaxations and a back-substitution algorithm.  
- Experiments: includes synthetic and real-world (influenza) studies and comparisons with naive and faithfulness-based estimators.  
- Clarity/readability: paper is dense, Section 3 is complex, notation heavy, and likely hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Parameter sensitivity: relies on tuning parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) with limited guidance and insufficient sensitivity analysis.  
- Computational complexity: scalability concerns remain, back-substitution scaling not demonstrated, and reliance on tools like Polymake/SCDD may hinder adoption.  
- Empirical evaluation limitations: synthetic data generation under-described and real-world comparisons lack depth against established methods.  
- Bayesian implementation: description is superficial; Dirichlet prior choices, rejection sampling, and ESS=10 rationale are inadequately justified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with practical awareness and promising empirical signs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, parameter sensitivity, computational limits, and limited empirical rigor.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and potentially significant but requires major revisions on clarity, sensitivity analysis, computational and Bayesian details.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance conditional on major revisions addressing readability, parameter choice/sensitivity, expanded experiments, and implementation details.
