- Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to estimate bounds on ACE by relaxing faithfulness and bridging faithfulness-based and IV methods.
- Repurposes Entner et al.'s witness/admissible set search to motivate a bounding procedure instead of point estimation.
- Parameterizes violations of conditional independence and incorporates them as constraints in a linear programming framework to derive ACE bounds.
- Extends to a Bayesian framework to handle sampling uncertainty.
- Proposes an efficient back-substitution algorithm to reduce computational cost of the bounding step.
- Strength: original and significant synthesis of causal discovery and bounding, offering robustness when faithfulness may be violated.
- Strength: technically sound LP formulation and appropriate Bayesian treatment; back-substitution is a major computational advantage.
- Strength: clear writing and motivation; experiments demonstrate stability relative to naive and faithfulness-based estimators.
- Weakness: limited guidance in the main text on selecting relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄); crucial discussion deferred to supplement.
- Weakness: scalability concerns because the initial search for a (witness, admissible set) pair is combinatorial and relies on brute-force for small covariate sets.
- Overall: high-quality, novel contribution likely to be influential; strong recommendation for acceptance.
