- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound the average causal effect by relaxing faithfulness, allowing path cancellations, and using linear programming plus Bayesian inference.  
- Builds on Entner et al. and Dawid & Ramsahai, and proposes a fast approximate algorithm plus analytical bounds for efficiency.  
- Well-motivated and addresses the core problem of confounding in observational causal inference.  
- Natural extension of prior work with a helpful “witness” concept for identifying potential instruments.  
- Incorporates Bayesian learning and algebraic bounds to balance statistical rigor and computational scalability.  
- Experiments are well-conducted and provide preliminary evidence of method utility, though limited in scope.  
- Presentation is dense and notation-heavy; needs more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples to aid understanding.  
- The “witness protection” metaphor and its connection to technical details are not made sufficiently clear.  
- Computational complexity is a major concern: LP and Bayesian sampling may not scale to large datasets, and runtime/cost analysis is insufficient.  
- The proposed back-substitution/approximate algorithm is promising but its performance relative to full LP needs quantitative evaluation.  
- Sensitivity analysis for relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) is inadequate; no clear guidance or justification for chosen values and their effect on bound width.  
- Evaluation is limited: synthetic experiments are useful but real-world validation is shallow and lacks comparisons with existing methods (e.g., propensity scores, IPW).  
- The falsification test appears ad hoc (95% threshold) and its impact on result reliability is not investigated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theoretical foundation, potential for more informative ACE bounds, and feasible preliminary experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: hard-to-follow presentation, scalability concerns, limited sensitivity exploration, insufficient evaluation/comparisons, and ad-hoc falsification.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution but recommend weak acceptance contingent on improving clarity, computational analysis, parameter guidance, and empirical comparisons.
