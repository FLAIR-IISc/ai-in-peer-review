## Review of "Causal Inference through a Witness Protection Program"

This paper introduces a novel methodology for bounding the Average Causal Effect (ACE) in observational studies, aiming to address the challenges of unmeasured confounding. The core idea is to relax the faithfulness assumption, allowing for “path cancellations” that suggest potential confounding paths while still leveraging conditional independence constraints. The authors propose a “Witness Protection Program” (WPP) that modifies existing linear programming approaches and introduces relaxations for computational efficiency, ultimately providing posterior distributions over bounds on the ACE.

The paper is well-motivated, tackling a fundamental problem in causal inference. The authors correctly identify the limitations of relying solely on faithfulness and the instability of point estimates from previous work (Entner et al., 2013). The proposed approach, building upon Dawid (1990) and Ramsahai (2004), is a logical extension, and the introduction of the WPP framework is a clever way to frame the relaxation of faithfulness. The inclusion of both analytical approximations and a fast algorithm demonstrates a practical consideration for scalability. The experiments, while limited, provide initial evidence of the method's robustness, particularly in scenarios where standard techniques fail.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of clarity in several areas. The notation is dense and assumes a high level of familiarity with the causal inference literature, potentially hindering accessibility. While the authors mention the computational expense, a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity and scalability limitations would be beneficial. The experimental section is relatively small, and the comparison to other methods is not exhaustive. The influenza data example, while interesting, lacks a thorough comparison to existing analyses. Furthermore, the justification for the choice of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) is somewhat lacking. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to bounding ACE under relaxed faithfulness assumptions.
*   Addresses a critical limitation of standard causal inference techniques.
*   Provides both theoretical and algorithmic contributions.
*   Demonstrates robustness in challenging scenarios.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Dense notation and high assumed background knowledge.
*   Limited experimental evaluation and comparison.
*   Lack of detailed discussion of computational complexity.
*   Insufficient justification for parameter choices.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a promising new approach to causal inference. While the clarity and experimental validation could be improved, the core idea is novel and potentially impactful. I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**, specifically requesting the authors to improve the clarity of the presentation, expand the experimental evaluation, and provide a more detailed discussion of the computational aspects and parameter selection. Addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its contribution to the field.
