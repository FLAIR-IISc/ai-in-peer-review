- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a framework that relaxes faithfulness to bound average causal effects by treating "witnesses" as instrumental variables instead of discarding them.

- Extends Entner et al.'s Rule 1 by allowing controlled violations of faithfulness via bounded path cancellations.

- Core technical contribution: linear programming formulation plus Bayesian inference to produce posterior distributions over ACE bounds, complementing point-estimation methods.

- Validated on synthetic data and a real influenza vaccination study, showing practical utility and tighter bounds than standard IV approaches.

- Rigorous formalization of faithfulness relaxation via constraints (3)-(6) and correct LP handling of bounded dependencies.

- Bayesian learning uses constrained Dirichlet priors to account for estimation uncertainty and avoids problems from priors on unidentifiable latent variables.

- Proposes a back-substitution algorithm as a computationally efficient alternative to full LP, with evidence of preserved bound quality.

- Experiments show WPP is robust where faithfulness-based methods fail and produces meaningful bounds in real-world data.

- Addresses a significant gap in observational causal inference by providing honest, interpretable posterior bounds under weaker assumptions.

- Integrates into standard observational study workflows without requiring full causal graph knowledge.

- Original conceptual advance: inverting Rule 1 to treat witnesses as IVs and systematically relaxing faithfulness via explicit parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄).

- Novel algebraic bounds (Table 2) and the back-substitution algorithm distinguish this work from previous IV methods.

- Clarity is generally good but notation (e.g., η?xw, ζyx.w) becomes dense and would benefit from an early notation table.

- The "Witness Protection" metaphor is evocative but potentially distracting; a more technical title is recommended.

- Algorithm 1's witness-search procedure is under-specified; it should reference Entner et al.'s method and clarify use of BDeu scores.

- Suggest quantifying the approximation error of the back-substitution method versus full LP in supplementary material.

- Overall: strong technical soundness, practical significance, and clear experiments; minor revisions are straightforward and would improve accessibility without changing core contributions.
