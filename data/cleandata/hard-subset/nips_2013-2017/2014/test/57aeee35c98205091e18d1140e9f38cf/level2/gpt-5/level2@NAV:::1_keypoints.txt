- Proposal: Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) that bounds ACE by inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to get IV-like constraints and allowing bounded faithfulness violations via parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄).  
- Method: Computes bounds with a linear program over latent-response parameters, uses constrained Dirichlet priors and rejection sampling for a Bayesian posterior over bounds.  
- Scalability/interpretability: Derives algebraic symbolic bounds and a back-substitution/message-passing procedure to speed up and simplify results.  
- Empirics: Synthetic and flu-vaccination data show WPP can yield tighter, more robust intervals than standard IV bounds and naive back-door when faithfulness is approximate.  
- Technical quality: Formulation is sound and well-connected to existing IV and partial-identification theory; relaxation parameters transparently interpolate between assumptions.  
- Missing details: Key proofs and constraint derivations are deferred to the supplement, reducing accessibility.  
- Testing/decision procedure concerns: The falsification/test procedure (95% threshold) and CI decision rule (BDeu) are somewhat ad hoc.  
- Computational concerns: Bayesian sampling under constraints may be computationally heavy; sensitivity and coverage properties are not fully characterized.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense and notation-heavy; many crucial details are in the supplement and guidance on choosing relaxation parameters is lacking.  
- Originality: High — inverting Rule 1 for IV-like bounds under controlled faithfulness violations and the algebraic/back-substitution bounds are novel.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful as a practical complement to IV and faithfulness-based methods, especially in near-unfaithful regimes, but empirical evaluation is limited.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide principled strategies for selecting relaxation parameters (elicitation, empirical Bayes, calibration).  
- Suggested improvements: Assess finite-sample properties (coverage, interval length) and sensitivity to CI testing errors.  
- Suggested improvements: Compare to other partial-identification/sensitivity methods (e.g., monotone IV/LATE, marginal sensitivity models).  
- Suggested improvements: Extend beyond binary variables, report scalability and ablation of LP vs. back-substitution, expand real-data evaluations and release reproducible code.  
- Decision rationale: Arguments for acceptance — novel compromise between faithfulness and IV, solid polyhedral grounding, practical Bayesian posterior; arguments against — tuning lacks principled guidance, heavy reliance on supplement, limited real-data validation, computational concerns; overall weak accept contingent on stronger clarity and evaluation.
