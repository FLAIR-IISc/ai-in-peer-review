- Proposes a novel method ("Witness Protection Program") that exploits observational conditional independencies by relaxing faithfulness to allow path cancellations.  
- Uses linear programming and Bayesian inference to obtain posterior bounds on the average causal effect.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a thorough introduction and clear statement of contributions.  
- Methodology section is detailed and clearly explains the LP approach and Bayesian estimation.  
- Authors provide a thorough discussion of assumptions and limitations.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data are comprehensive and show improved causal-effect estimates despite unmeasured confounding.  
- Strong connection to and extension of prior work; positions contribution clearly in the literature.  
- Strength: novel perspective on causal inference in observational studies.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Strength: careful discussion of assumptions and limitations.  
- Weakness: approach is computationally expensive and may not scale to large datasets.  
- Weakness: no clear guidance on choosing relaxation parameters; selection may require more research.
