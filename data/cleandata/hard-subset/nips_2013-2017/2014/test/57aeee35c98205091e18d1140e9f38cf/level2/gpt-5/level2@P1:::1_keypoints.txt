- Summary: Proposes bounds on average causal effect by treating Entner et al.’s witness W as an instrument under bounded violations of faithfulness, parameterized by (w,y,x,β,β̄), computed via LPs and symbolic inequalities.  
- Relation to prior work: Bridges causal discovery (faithfulness/PC) with partial identification and IV bounds, reducing to Balke–Pearl and extending Dawid/Ramsahai frameworks.  
- Strength: Introduces an interpretable relaxation framework that interpolates between strict faithfulness (point ID) and unconstrained IV (wide bounds).  
- Strength: Formal LP formulation with clear special cases and symbolic inequalities that provide intuition and computational speedups.  
- Strength: Bayesian approach that accounts for sampling uncertainty and avoids overconfident point estimates under near-unfaithfulness.  
- Strength: Careful experiments including scenarios with/without valid witnesses and a real-world flu vaccination application; provides code and a practical workflow.  
- Weakness: Lacks principled guidance for choosing relaxation parameters (w,y,x,β,β̄); needs calibration plots, elicitation templates, and data-driven checks.  
- Weakness: Bayesian posterior over bounds relies on rejection sampling with ad hoc falsification thresholds; suggest advanced MCMC in polytopes and reporting ESS/acceptance rates.  
- Weakness: Witness detection under relaxed tests proposes witnesses too often (>90%), risking anti-conservative narrowing; needs false-positive rates and sensitivity to model-selection priors.  
- Weakness: Evaluation missing key metrics — empirical coverage of true ACE, average interval widths, and runtime comparisons (full LP vs back-substitution).  
- Weakness: Method assumes binary variables; paper should discuss extensions to categorical/continuous settings and applicability of algebraic bounds.  
- Weakness: Theoretical claims (monotonicity/nesting, conservativeness relative to Entner et al.) need formal statements/proofs or references.  
- Quality: Generally sound; reductions to known results are correct and relaxations are reasonable though not tight; more formal analysis of coverage under mis-specified witnesses is needed.  
- Clarity: Dense but mostly clear; recommend a concise table for parameters, a numeric toy example, and pseudocode for back-substitution.  
- Originality: Novel inversion of Entner’s Rule 1 to generate instrument-like witnesses under bounded faithfulness violations, combined with posterior-over-bounds and symbolic relaxations.  
- Significance: Potentially useful for observational studies by providing honest, informative bounds that blend learned structure with controlled faithfulness deviations.  
- Questions for authors: Provide monotonicity/nesting guarantees, sensitivity to witness search prior and thresholds, empirical coverage of bounds, and whether back-substitution suffices in large problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Clear, original methodology, practical algorithms and code, correct special-case reductions, and likely impact on applied causal inference.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient guidance/validation for parameter choice, incomplete evaluation of coverage/width/runtime, ad hoc Bayesian thresholds, and binary-variable limitation.  
- Overall evaluation: Strong, thought-provoking contribution leaning toward acceptance if supplemented with parameter elicitation guidance, stronger Bayesian diagnostics, and expanded empirical evaluation.
