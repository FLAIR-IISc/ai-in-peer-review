- Overall: Novel, interesting method to bound causal effects under questionable faithfulness; strong idea but technically dense and needs clearer presentation and more experiments.  
- Summary: Proposes inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1 to bound ACE using witness–admissible covariate pairs, LP-based relaxations, and a fast approximate algorithm.  
- Related work: Builds appropriately on IV, causal discovery, and LP bounding literature (Entner et al., Dawid, Ramsahai, Pearl) and situates contributions clearly.  
- Strength — Novelty: The "Witness Protection Program" and inverting Rule 1 are conceptually elegant and original.  
- Strength — Robustness: Explicitly models faithfulness violations via controllable relaxation parameters, improving robustness over faithfulness-dependent methods.  
- Strength — Theory: LP foundation and derived algebraic bounds give solid theoretical grounding and analytical insight.  
- Strength — Computation: Authors propose relaxations and a fast message-passing-like algorithm to address LP cost.  
- Strength — Connections: Clear links drawn to back-door adjustment, IV methods, and causal discovery frameworks.  
- Weakness — Complexity: Paper is dense, heavily notated, and requires strong background; LP formulation needs clearer exposition.  
- Weakness — Experiments: Empirical evaluation is limited, with synthetic scenarios only and a single real example lacking thorough method comparisons.  
- Weakness — Parameter sensitivity: Little practical guidance on choosing relaxation parameters and insufficient analysis of their effect on bound width.  
- Weakness — Scalability: Questions remain about performance in high-dimensional settings and the Bayesian Dirichlet/rejection sampling step.  
- Weakness — Interpretation: Unclear how wide/non-informative bounds add value versus acknowledging uncertainty; interpretability needs discussion.  
- Detail Q — Bayesian learning: Rejection sampling may bottleneck; acceptance rates and alternatives (e.g., MCMC) should be discussed.  
- Detail Q — Algebraic bounds: Need more on back-substitution implementation, speed vs. full LP, and effect of relaxations on tightness.  
- Detail Q — Experiments: Recommend comparisons to standard IV methods, sensitivity analyses, and visualizations of posterior ACE bounds.  
- Detail Q — Synthetic data: Clarify range of true ACEs used and sensitivity to degrees of faithfulness violation (w, y, x).  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded, computationally aware, and potentially impactful contribution.  
- Against acceptance: Accessibility hindered by complexity, limited experiments, and insufficient parameter-selection guidance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand experiments, clarify parameter selection, and improve technical clarity.
