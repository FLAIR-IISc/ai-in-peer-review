- Introduces a method for bounding the Average Causal Effect (ACE) by reinterpreting admissible-set search as identifying instrumental variables and modeling bounded violations of faithfulness.  
- Uses linear programming to derive ACE bounds and proposes relaxations plus a fast back-substitution/message-passing-like algorithm for approximation.  
- Demonstrates the approach on synthetic data and an influenza vaccination observational study.  
- Situates work within causal inference literature, extending Entner et al. (2013) and addressing limits of strict faithfulness assumptions.  
- References key causal discovery and instrumental variable literature comprehensively.  
- Novel framework (“Witness Protection Program”) that explicitly models bounded deviations from faithfulness.  
- Strong theoretical grounding with algebraic bounds and linear-programming formulation.  
- Practical focus on scalability with proposed relaxations and a back-substitution algorithm.  
- Empirical validation includes both simulations and a real-world case study.  
- Clear motivation to develop methods robust to faithfulness violations in observational studies.  
- Methodological complexity may limit accessibility for practitioners without advanced causal inference and LP knowledge.  
- Many tuning parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) require careful justification and may confuse users.  
- Sensitivity of bounds to relaxation parameters is insufficiently explored in experiments.  
- Computational cost remains a concern for larger datasets despite algorithmic relaxations; runtime analysis is lacking.  
- Limited guidance on how to interpret and apply the resulting bounds in practical decision-making.  
- Bayesian implementation raises concerns (high sample rejection rate) without clear discussion of bias or efficiency implications.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, extends existing techniques, empirical promise, and clear acknowledgment of limitations.  
- Against acceptance: method complexity, parameter sensitivity, computational demands, and limited practical guidance warrant further work.  
- Overall assessment: strong, innovative contribution with solid theory but needs refinements; recommended accept with minor revisions.  
- Revision recommendations: expand sensitivity analysis, provide quantitative runtime evaluation, add guidance on bound interpretation, clarify Bayesian rejection rationale, and include a simpler illustrative case study.
