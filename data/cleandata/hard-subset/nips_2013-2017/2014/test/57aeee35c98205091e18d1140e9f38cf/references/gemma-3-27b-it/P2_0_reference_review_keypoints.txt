- Proposes the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) to bound the Average Causal Effect (ACE) for binary X→Y using observed covariates and reinterpretation of Entner et al.'s search for instruments.  
- Extends linear programming approaches (Dawid, Ramsahai) and introduces relaxations that yield symbolic bounds and a faster, message-passing-like algorithm.  
- Aims to improve robustness to faithfulness violations and unobserved confounding in observational causal inference.  

- Addresses a crucial problem in causal inference by providing tighter ACE bounds under weaker assumptions.  
- Offers a novel framework (WPP) for exploiting covariate information and identifying candidate instrumental variables.  
- Builds on strong theoretical grounding and links to existing literature (Pearl, Dawid, Ramsahai, Entner).  
- Proposes computationally-minded relaxations and an efficient algorithm to make the approach more practical.  
- Validates the method on synthetic and real data, showing empirical promise.  
- Paper is generally well-structured and mostly clear in presenting core ideas.  

- Paper is dense and requires substantial prior knowledge, with many new terms introduced without repeated clarification.  
- Method performance is sensitive to chosen relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), lacking systematic guidance for selection.  
- Scalability concerns remain for larger covariate sets despite relaxations; analytical approximations need more validation.  
- Real-world interpretation of bounds (e.g., policy significance of non-significant intervals) is underdeveloped.  
- Algebraic bounds are not tight and the paper does not fully exploit potential algebraic optimizations.  

- Quality: technically sound but dependent on multiple relaxations and parameter choices, raising robustness and computational concern.  
- Clarity: organized but dense; some sections need more intuitive explanations and examples.  
- Originality: meaningful combination of prior techniques with the new WPP perspective and robustness focus.  
- Significance: potentially high impact if computational challenges and parameter sensitivity are addressed.  

- For acceptance: tackles a fundamental problem, is theoretically grounded, shows promising empirical results, and offers robustness against faithfulness violations.  
- Against acceptance: high complexity and accessibility barriers, parameter sensitivity, scalability issues, and incomplete validation of analytical approximations.  

- Overall assessment: strong, above-average paper leaning toward acceptance, recommended accept with minor revisions.  

- Suggested revision: simplify and clarify presentation with more intuitive explanations and illustrative examples.  
- Suggested revision: provide a systematic parameter sensitivity analysis and practical guidance on parameter selection.  
- Suggested revision: expand discussion of computational limitations and strategies for scaling to larger datasets.  
- Suggested revision: elaborate on real-world interpretation of bounds and their policy implications.
