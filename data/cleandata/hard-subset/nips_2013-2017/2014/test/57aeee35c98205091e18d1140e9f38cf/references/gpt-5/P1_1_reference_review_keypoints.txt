- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP): bounds ACE of binary X→Y using W as an approximate instrument conditional on Z, inverting Entner–Hoyer–Spirtes Rule 1.  
- Introduces relaxation parameters (w,x,y,β,β̄) that control direct W→Y effects, deviations between latent and observed conditionals, and dependence between W and unobserved confounders.  
- Special cases recover standard binary IV (w=0, β=β̄=1) and chain W→X→Y (x=y=0), providing a continuous interpolation between extremes.  
- Forms a linear program (extending Dawid–Ramsahai) to obtain sharp or relaxed ACE bounds conditional on Z and aggregates over P(Z).  
- Develops algebraic symbolic bounds and a back-substitution tightening routine to speed computation and avoid repeated dual polytope solves.  
- Presents a Bayesian procedure using constrained Dirichlet priors and rejection sampling to produce posteriors over ACE bounds.  
- Provides synthetic experiments and a real influenza encouragement dataset; code is publicly available.  
- Connects faithfulness-based causal discovery with IV bounding and partial identification literature, offering principled interpolation between point-identification and wide IV bounds.  
- Algebraic inequalities extend understanding of the binary IV polytope and deliver computational speed-ups.  
- Technical construction (LP, relaxations, linkage to observed variables) is sound and consistent with partial identification methodology.  
- Bayesian treatment and model falsification via rejection rates are reasonable but somewhat ad hoc.  
- Algebraic bounds and back-substitution are useful conservative relaxations; paper acknowledges conservativeness.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks calibration analyses (frequentist coverage) and systematic sensitivity studies to relaxation parameters.  
- Runtime/complexity claims for back-substitution lack quantitative benchmarks.  
- Many technical proofs/details are deferred to the supplement; key tables are referenced but not shown.  
- Notation is heavy and some constraints (3)–(6) need clearer intuition and domain-level interpretation.  
- Algorithmic search procedure and the BDeu-based independence test are underspecified.  
- Novelty: inverting Rule 1 to generate conditional-IV bounds under controlled faithfulness violations is original and interesting.  
- Significance: practically relevant middle ground for observational studies with questionable faithfulness; potential to influence reporting under model uncertainty.  
- Pros: principled bridge between causal discovery and partial identification, transparent robustness knobs, numerical and algebraic solutions, Bayesian uncertainty quantification, real-data code.  
- Cons: little guidance on selecting relaxations (risk of subjective choices), incomplete evaluation (coverage, interval widths, baselines), limited to binary variables, potential selection/multiple-testing bias in witness selection, asymmetric interval error metric.  
- Suggested improvements: provide principled guidance for choosing relaxations and domain interpretations, run sensitivity and coverage studies, report runtimes and LP vs back-substitution tightness, compare to more baselines, include a worked example, discuss extensions beyond binary, and address multiplicity via sample splitting or cross-fitting.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept — strong methodological contribution but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer guidance on relaxation parameter choice.
