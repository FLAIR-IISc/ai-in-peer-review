- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE for binary X→Y by treating Entner et al.’s witness as a conditional instrument with bounded faithfulness violations.
- Introduces slack parameters (w,x,y,β,β̄) to control local deviations from conditional independences and hidden dependence.
- Derives ACE bounds via an LP extending Balke–Pearl and Dawid–Ramsahai frameworks.
- Provides conservative algebraic bounds and a back-substitution "message passing" algorithm to speed computation.
- Develops a Bayesian procedure with constrained Dirichlet priors to produce posterior distributions over ACE bounds accounting for sampling uncertainty.
- Empirical tests include synthetic stress tests and a real flu-vaccine dataset showing WPP can tighten bounds relative to standard IV.
- Builds on IV bounds, faithfulness-based covariate selection, and sensitivity/partial identification literature.
- Conceptually related to "plausibly exogenous" IV and Rosenbaum-style sensitivity analyses, extended to nonparametric binary models.
- Constrained-Dirichlet Bayesian treatment connects to inequality-constrained Bayesian modeling.
- Strength: principled compromise between point identification under faithfulness and worst-case IV bounds with interpretable control knobs.
- Strength: technical contributions include bounded-faithfulness LP formulation, symbolic algebraic bounds, and an efficient back-substitution algorithm.
- Strength: Bayesian treatment acknowledges finite-sample uncertainty and model falsification.
- Strength: Practicality via leveraging causal discovery steps and available code.
- Weakness: No principled guidance for selecting relaxation parameters; results may be sensitive to these choices.
- Weakness: Posterior over [L,U] intervals is conservative and awkward for decision-making; alternative summaries recommended.
- Weakness: Using BDeu for approximate independencies can propose spurious witnesses; model selection and bound computation should be more clearly separated.
- Weakness: Scalability concerns; need systematic runtime and tightness benchmarks comparing back-substitution to exact LP.
- Weakness: Evaluation metrics favor wide intervals; should report interval widths, empirical coverage, and combined performance scores; experiments use large n only.
- Weakness: Limited to binary variables; extensions to multinomial/continuous settings are not discussed.
- Weakness: Flu application needs comparisons to LATE/monotonicity IV, sensitivity plots for chosen slacks, and justification of variable exclusion/ordering.
- Clarity: Heavy notation and deferred supplement material; a running example and explicit LP would improve readability.
- Quality: Technically sound overall, but some heuristics (model falsification, parameter selection) need stronger theory/diagnostics.
- Originality: Novel inversion of Rule 1 to generate conditional-IV bounds under bounded-faithfulness and symbolic/back-substitution method.
- Significance: Fills gap between faithfulness-based point ID and conservative IV bounds; could be practical with better parameter guidance and diagnostics.
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled framework, nontrivial technical advances, practical relevance with real-data demonstration.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient guidance on relaxation parameters, heuristic model selection risks, limited evaluation and binary scope.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept; support conditional on clearer guidance/sensitivity analyses for θ, strengthened evaluation (width/coverage/runtime), and clarified theory/notation.
