- Novel contribution: proposes the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) framing to bound average causal effects by reinterpreting Entner et al.'s method and using linear programming.  
- Theoretical rigor: combines LP-based bounds with relaxations and introduces parameters to quantify deviations from faithfulness.  
- Addresses faithfulness issues: offers a more robust approach than methods relying strictly on faithfulness.  
- Strong connection to IV literature: builds on and relates to standard instrumental variable bounds.  
- Computational approach: proposes analytical approximations and a fast, message-passing-like algorithm to improve scalability.  
- Clarity problems: paper is dense, notation-heavy, and hard to follow; needs clearer exposition and motivation.  
- Section 3 rushed: methodology lacks sufficient step-by-step explanation and intuitive examples.  
- Limited experiments: synthetic results okay but real-world validation (influenza dataset) is insufficient and broader comparisons are needed.  
- Parameter selection unclear: provides little practical guidance on choosing relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄).  
- Computational complexity concern: full LP may not scale to high-dimensional data; lacks runtime/scaling analysis.  
- Identifiability not addressed: paper does not state when bounds are tight or what is required for tighter bounds.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on improving clarity, experiments, parameter guidance, complexity analysis, and identifiability discussion.  
- Specific fixes suggested: simplify notation, add a glossary, expand Sections 3–4 with examples and algorithm details, perform sensitivity analysis and broader experiments.  
- Related work: citations and contextualization (Pearl, Spirtes, Entner, Dawid, Ramsahai) are adequate and appropriate.
