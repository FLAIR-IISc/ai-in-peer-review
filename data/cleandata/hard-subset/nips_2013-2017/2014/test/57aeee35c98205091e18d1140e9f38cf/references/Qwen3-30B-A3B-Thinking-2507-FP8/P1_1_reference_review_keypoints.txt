- Summary: Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound average causal effects under bounded faithfulness violations using relaxed local independence constraints and linear programming.  
- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Formalizes relaxations of faithfulness and derives algebraic bounds that illuminate parameter effects on ACE.  
- Strength — Practical framework: Provides a principled middle ground between point estimates and conservative IV bounds for handling faithfulness violations.  
- Strength — Computational efficiency: Proposes a back-substitution algorithm claimed to be much faster than full LP while retaining reasonable accuracy.  
- Strength — Experiments: Synthetic and real-world influenza vaccination experiments show robustness where standard conditioning can be biased.  
- Weakness — Novelty claims: Core techniques (LP for causal bounds, Bayesian selection) are incremental and symbolic bounds are not convincingly differentiated from prior work (e.g., Ramsahai).  
- Weakness — Computational evidence: The “order of magnitude” efficiency claim lacks concrete runtime benchmarks in the main text.  
- Weakness — Parameter selection: Provides minimal guidance for choosing relaxation parameters and uses fixed values in experiments without justification.  
- Weakness — Bayesian approach: The Dirichlet prior/rejection sampling validation is somewhat ad hoc and not well justified versus alternatives.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with clear derivations and reasonable experiments, but novelty is incremental and some claims need stronger evidence.  
- Clarity assessment: Well-organized and readable with thorough math, though the Bayesian sections could be clearer.  
- Originality assessment: WPP’s systematic handling of faithfulness violations is novel conceptually, but many techniques build on existing work.  
- Significance assessment: Addresses an important causal inference problem and offers a useful, practical tool for applied researchers.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Better differentiate symbolic bounds from prior work, include concrete runtime comparisons for the back-substitution algorithm, and justify or provide guidance on relaxation-parameter selection.
