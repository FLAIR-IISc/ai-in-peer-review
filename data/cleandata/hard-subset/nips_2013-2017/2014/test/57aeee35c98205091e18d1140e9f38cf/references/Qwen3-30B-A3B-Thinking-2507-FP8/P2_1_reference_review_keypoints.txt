- Introduces the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) to bound the average causal effect (ACE) from observational data.  
- Builds on Entner et al. by treating "witness" variables as instruments and shifting from point estimates to bounds.  
- Introduces relaxation parameters to handle violations of the faithfulness assumption.  
- Provides linear programming formulation and a computationally efficient back-substitution algorithm to compute bounds.  
- Incorporates a Bayesian approach to handle uncertainty in the constraints.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid, grounded in Pearl's do-calculus and prior work.  
- Method is more conservative than point estimation but more robust to faithfulness violations.  
- Experiments include synthetic data and a real influenza vaccination dataset, but are limited to small-scale problems.  
- Paper lacks clear guidance on choosing relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), a major practical limitation.  
- Insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets.  
- Comparison with existing methods is limited, particularly for computational efficiency.  
- Generally well-written and organized, with clear definitions and precise algorithm descriptions.  
- Explanation of how relaxation parameters interact (and interpretation of algebraic bounds) needs clearer, concrete examples.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add parameter guidance, complexity comparison, and illustrative examples.
