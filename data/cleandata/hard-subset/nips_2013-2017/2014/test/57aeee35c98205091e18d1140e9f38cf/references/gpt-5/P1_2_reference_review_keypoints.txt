- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE of binary X→Y from observational data when back-door or IV assumptions fail.  
- Inverts Entner et al.’s Rule 1 by treating a witness W (conditional on Z) as a candidate instrument and deriving bounds under bounded violations.  
- Introduces interpretable tolerance parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) to relax faithfulness and IV assumptions.  
- Computes bounds via LPs adapted from Dawid/Ramsahai to the conditional-on-Z and relaxed-constraint setting.  
- Develops algebraic (symbolic) conservative bounds and a fast back-substitution procedure to avoid repeated dual polytope construction.  
- Proposes a Bayesian constrained-Dirichlet posterior and rejection sampling to account for sampling uncertainty and model falsification.  
- Experiments (synthetic and influenza encouragement) show WPP often gives informative intervals and can outperform naïve back-door or faithfulness-only estimates.  
- Sits at the intersection of causal discovery under faithfulness and partial identification/IV literature, bridging binary faithfulness decisions with inequality relaxations.  
- Technical soundness: constraint specification, LP mapping, algebraic relaxations, and Bayesian approach are well-motivated and correctly implemented.  
- Empirical support: comparisons use reasonable metrics and demonstrate practical value, though trade-offs between bias and interval width are acknowledged.  
- Caveat (i): Choice and calibration of relaxation parameters are crucial but mostly deferred and somewhat ad hoc.  
- Caveat (ii): The falsification rule (>95% infeasible proposals) is heuristic rather than formally justified.  
- Caveat (iii): Exhaustive search/BDeu selection of (W, Z) risks multiple-testing/post-selection bias; Bayesian mitigation is partial.  
- Caveat (iv): Runtime and scalability are discussed qualitatively but lack systematic complexity/timing results.  
- Clarity: paper is dense but organized; needs more pedagogical explanation of tolerance parameters and a consolidated notation table.  
- Minor presentation issues: essential figures/tables must be legible; fix typos and broken reference URLs.  
- Originality: inverting Entner’s Rule 1 and propagating bounded relaxations through LP/algebraic bounds is novel and nontrivial.  
- Novel Bayesian constrained-Dirichlet posterior over bounds adds useful finite-sample uncertainty quantification.  
- Significance: provides a practical continuum between fragile point identification and wide IV bounds, useful across applied domains.  
- Pros: principled bridge between causal discovery and partial ID, exact and fast approximations, Bayesian uncertainty treatment, demonstrated robustness, and available code.  
- Cons: limited guidance on parameter choice, heuristic falsification/post-selection concerns, conservative algebraic bounds may lose tightness, and scalability/search limits not quantified.  
- Key suggested improvements: provide calibration guidance for parameters, report interval widths and coverage, quantify algebraic vs LP tightness, address model-selection uncertainty (e.g., split-sample), and give runtime scaling with |W| and |Z|.  
- Specific requests: clarify which parameters map to exclusion/independence/weak faithfulness with toy examples, and show sensitivity analyses (e.g., influenza example GRP exclusion).  
- Overall: solid, novel contribution with practical potential; lean toward acceptance pending stronger parameter calibration, model-selection uncertainty handling, and scalability/tightness evaluations.
