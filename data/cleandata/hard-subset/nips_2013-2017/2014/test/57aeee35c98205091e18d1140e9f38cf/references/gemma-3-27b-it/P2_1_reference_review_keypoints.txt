- Summary: Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound Average Causal Effect from observational data using instrumental-variable style witnesses, LP-based bounds with relaxations, analytical approximations, and a fast algorithm.  
- Strength: Addresses a core problem in causal inference by targeting ACE estimation from observational data.  
- Strength: Novel WPP framework reconciling instrumental-variable search with robustness to faithfulness violations.  
- Strength: Technically sound integration of do-calculus, instrumental-variable ideas, and modified linear programming with symbolic/analytical bounds.  
- Strength: Practical awareness—introduces relaxations and a fast algorithm to improve computational tractability.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation includes both synthetic and a real-world experiment.  
- Weakness: Presentation is dense and assumes high familiarity with causal inference; jumps between theory and algorithms are abrupt.  
- Weakness: Method remains computationally demanding and experiments are limited to small-dimensional problems; scaling claims rest on approximations needing validation.  
- Weakness: Sensitive to choice of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) with insufficient guidance on selection and effect on bounds.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical scope—only one real dataset and possible post-hoc choice of witness/admissible set.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of connections to related methods for unobserved confounding, e.g., sensitivity analysis.  
- Assessment (Quality): Good—solid theoretical foundation but concerns about complexity and parameter sensitivity.  
- Assessment (Clarity): Fair—notation and explanations are dense and could be more intuitive.  
- Assessment (Originality): Good—WPP and LP relaxation combination is novel.  
- Assessment (Significance): Fair-to-good—potentially valuable if computational issues and scalability are addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles a fundamental problem with a novel, theoretically grounded approach and promising experiments.  
- Arguments for rejection: High computational cost, parameter sensitivity, limited empirical validation, and clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept conditional on major revisions.  
- Revision request: Improve clarity—simplify notation and provide more intuitive explanations.  
- Revision request: Address scalability—analyze computational complexity, optimize, and test on larger datasets.  
- Revision request: Provide systematic guidance for selecting relaxation parameters and explain their impact.  
- Revision request: Expand empirical evaluation with more diverse real-world datasets.  
- Revision request: Clarify connections to existing work on causal inference under unobserved confounding (e.g., sensitivity analysis).
