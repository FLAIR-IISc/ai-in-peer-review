- Overall: Novel, theoretically grounded approach to bounding causal effects; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Core contribution: "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) reinterprets Entner’s Rule 1 to identify instruments and bound ACE under bounded faithfulness violations.  
- Method: Models faithfulness violations as bounded deviations and frames bounding ACE as a linear program with relaxations and a fast algorithm.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Pearl’s causal calculus, PC algorithm, LP-based bounds (Dawid, Ramsahai), and Entner et al. (2013) conditional independences.  
- Novelty: Recasting conditional-independence rules for bounding rather than identifying causal effects is highly original.  
- Theoretical soundness: Methodology is well-motivated and grounded in causal inference theory.  
- Robustness: Explicitly accounts for faithfulness violations, promising more reliable bounds than strict-assumption methods.  
- Practicality: Authors introduce relaxations and a fast, message-passing-like algorithm to address computational cost.  
- Experiments: Synthetic and real-data experiments provide initial validation and comparisons to naive and prior methods.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense, heavy in notation, and difficult to follow; LP formulation needs clearer exposition and intuitive examples.  
- Complexity: WPP framework is complex and may hinder practitioner adoption despite proposed relaxations.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Performance depends on relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) with insufficient guidance on choosing them.  
- Scalability: Concerns remain about scaling to high-dimensional datasets due to posterior sampling and large polytope optimization.  
- Empirical limitations: Influenza data analysis is cursory; needs deeper sensitivity analyses and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, addresses key causal inference challenges, and shows promising results.  
- Against acceptance: Hard to understand, potentially impractical due to complexity and scalability, and empirical evaluation could be stronger.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on improving clarity, adding intuitive examples, parameter guidance, expanded empirical analysis, and handling no-witness cases.
