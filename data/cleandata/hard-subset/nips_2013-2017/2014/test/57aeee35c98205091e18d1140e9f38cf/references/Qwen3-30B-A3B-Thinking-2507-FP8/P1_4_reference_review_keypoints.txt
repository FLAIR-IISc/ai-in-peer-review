- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), which inverts Entner et al.'s Rule 1 to use "witness" variables as instruments for bounding ACE under weakened faithfulness assumptions.  
- Proposes relaxation parameters to quantify faithfulness violations and provide a practical way to trade assumptions for bound tightness.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical derivations and analytical bounds for ACE under WPP.  
- Develops an efficient back-substitution algorithm to compute bounds, addressing computational bottlenecks of LP approaches.  
- Validates the method with well-designed synthetic experiments and an application to an influenza vaccination dataset.  
- Connects WPP to existing causal inference and instrumental variable literature, extending Manski/Ramsahai-style approaches.  
- Paper is candid about limitations and includes thoughtful comparisons with standard methods.  
- Includes practical implementation details and algorithm descriptions to aid adoption (e.g., Polymake/SCDD use).  
- Major practical limitation: minimal guidance on selecting relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄).  
- Insufficient contrast with other approaches to handling faithfulness violations (e.g., Meek’s strong completeness).  
- The choice and interpretation of the specific relaxation parameters could be better motivated and linked to "degree of unfaithfulness."  
- Real-world validation is limited to a single influenza dataset, reducing demonstrated practical impact.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong technical contribution and practical relevance despite parameter-selection issues.  
- Suggests adding detailed guidance and examples for parameter selection and a sensitivity analysis for the influenza application.  
- Recommends an explicit comparison with Meek’s work and discussion of integrating WPP with causal discovery algorithms (e.g., PC).  
- Requests clarification of the computational complexity of the back-substitution algorithm versus the full LP approach.
