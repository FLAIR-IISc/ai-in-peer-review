- Introduces a novel method to bound the Average Causal Effect (ACE) by reinterpreting Entner’s Rule 1 to find instrumental variables.  
- Formulates ACE bounding under relaxed faithfulness as a linear program (LP).  
- Proposes practical relaxations and a fast, message-passing-like algorithm to approximate LP bounds.  
- Explicitly models and bounds the impact of faithfulness violations, improving robustness to real-world departures from strict faithfulness.  
- Bridges instrumental variable techniques and conditional-independence-based causal discovery, building on Entner et al., Dawid, and Ramsahai.  
- Theoretical foundation is rigorous through the LP framework.  
- Empirical evaluation includes both synthetic and real-data experiments, showing comparisons with naive estimators and existing methods.  
- Paper is generally well-written and provides sufficient methodological context.  
- Re-interpretation of Entner’s Rule is insightful and potentially powerful.  
- Computational complexity remains a concern despite relaxations; tuning parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) are required.  
- Bounds are sensitive to the choice of relaxation parameters; trade-offs between tightness and conservatism are underexplored.  
- Back-substitution algorithm is described briefly; more detail on performance and limitations is needed.  
- Real-data experiment (influenza vaccine) is unconvincing; WPP bounds mirror standard IV and covariate choices seem post-hoc.  
- Strong assumption that covariates are not effects of X or Y needs deeper discussion and exploration of violations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach addressing faithfulness violations with practical relaxations and comprehensive evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: remaining computational/tuning issues, unconvincing real-data validation, and limited discussion of key assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested improvements: provide guidance/sensitivity analysis for parameter selection, expand details on back-substitution, strengthen real-data analysis, and elaborate on assumption implications.
