- Proposes treating witnesses from Entner et al.’s Rule 1 as candidate conditional instruments and introduces bounded faithfulness violations via parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄).  
- Computes ACE bounds using an LP approach extending Dawid and Ramsahai’s polytopal methods, recovering IV and chain limits as special cases.  
- Derives algebraic (symbolic) inequality bounds and a faster back-substitution procedure that is looser but more scalable than the LP.  
- Provides a Bayesian implementation with constrained Dirichlet priors, rejection sampling, and a BDeu-scored search over (W, Z).  
- Empirical results on synthetic data and a flu vaccine study show intervals can be tighter than standard IV by leveraging approximate independencies.  
- The bounded-violation parameterization is coherent and interpolates between point identification and vacuous bounds.  
- LP construction and symbolic derivations are technically sound, with back-substitution preserving feasibility and convergence.  
- Claims of tighter-than-IV bounds and computational gains are supported, but main-text timing/accuracy comparisons are limited.  
- Synthetic evaluation emphasizes coverage (zero error if interval covers) but underreports interval width; width analyses are deferred to supplement.  
- Real-data analysis is sensible but selection of sensitivity parameters lacks strong justification; sensitivity plots are recommended.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: LP computational cost, user-chosen relaxation parameters, and looser algebraic bounds.  
- Paper is dense but clear for causality experts; heavy notation and reliance on the supplement reduce accessibility.  
- Suggests adding a toy example and parameter “map” to improve readability and a pseudocode for back-substitution.  
- Novelty lies in repurposing witnesses as conditional instruments, formalizing near-faithfulness with local bounds, and new algebraic bounds/back-substitution.  
- Bayesian constrained-Dirichlet approach to propagate sampling uncertainty is a thoughtful contribution.  
- Significant practical contribution offering a middle ground between ignorability and perfect IV assumptions; code availability enhances impact.  
- Main adoption barrier is choosing relaxation parameters; clearer guidance would broaden use.  
- Pros: conceptual bridge between faithfulness-based selection and IV bounding, solid theory, practical algorithms, Bayesian treatment, and useful empirical illustrations.  
- Cons: limited guidance on sensitivity parameters, evaluation lacks width and runtime metrics, baselines could be richer, rejection-sampling and 95% rule are ad hoc, and derivations are deferred to supplement.  
- Concrete suggestions: provide mappings/calibrations for sensitivity parameters, report coverage+mean width+runtime, add standard IV and other baselines, consider sample-splitting to avoid selection bias, and include a numerical example.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, original, and impactful; leans toward acceptance pending stronger guidance on sensitivity choices and fuller empirical characterization.
