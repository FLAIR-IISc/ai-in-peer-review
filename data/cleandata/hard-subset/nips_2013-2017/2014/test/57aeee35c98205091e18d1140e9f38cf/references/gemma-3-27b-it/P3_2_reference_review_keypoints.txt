- Overall: WPP is a valuable, technically sophisticated method for bounding ACE under relaxed faithfulness, and is a strong candidate for acceptance despite clarity issues.
- Builds on Entner et al. by reinterpreting admissible set search as identifying potential instruments.
- Uses linear programming (Dawid, Ramsahai) to derive ACE bounds and explicitly models sensitivity to faithfulness violations.
- Introduces relaxations and a back-substitution algorithm to reduce computational burden.
- Strong theoretical grounding connecting causal graphical models, algebraic bounds, and relaxation parameters.
- Novel approach that moves beyond point estimates and quantifies uncertainty from faithfulness violations.
- Computational advancements make the method more practical, though still costly.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real data shows WPP outperforms naive estimators; influenza example is illustrative.
- Sensitivity parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) are a significant strength for honest uncertainty quantification.
- Paper is dense with challenging notation; presentation and explanation need improvement for non-experts.
- Guidance on choosing/tuning relaxation parameters is limited and needs expansion.
- Computational cost and scalability to high-dimensional data remain concerns.
- Real-world validation is limited in scope and lacks thorough comparison to domain-specific methods.
- The Bayesian learning component is mentioned but underdeveloped and not well justified.
- Highly original and significant—combines prior ideas in a novel way to produce more robust observational causal inference.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a core problem, sound methodology, practical algorithms, empirical support, explicit handling of faithfulness.
- Cons for acceptance: dense presentation, parameter tuning and scalability issues, limited real-data scope, weak Bayesian treatment.
- Recommendation: accept (lean strongly); improve clarity, add parameter sensitivity and scalability analyses, expand real-world experiments, and flesh out the Bayesian section.
- Rating: 8/10.
