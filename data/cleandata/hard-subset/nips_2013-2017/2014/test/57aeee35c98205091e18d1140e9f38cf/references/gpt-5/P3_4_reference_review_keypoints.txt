- Paper proposes WPP: treat a discovered witness W (conditional on Z) as a “soft” instrument and compute ACE bounds under controlled relaxations of Entner-Hoyer-Spirtes Rule 1.  
- Relaxations are parameterized by w (exclusion violation), x and y (discrepancies between interventional and observational conditionals), and β, β̄ (dependence between W and latent U).  
- LP over response-function polytope yields conditional ACE bounds which are averaged over P(Z); connects to Dawid (2002) and Ramsahai (2012).  
- Bayesian implementation uses constrained Dirichlet posteriors with rejection sampling to propagate sampling uncertainty and includes a simple falsification check.  
- Authors provide symbolic (non-tight) inequalities and a back-substitution algorithm as a fast approximation to repeated LP solves.  
- Experiments: WPP yields stable bounds with lower error tails compared to naive estimators and Entner et al.’s point estimator, and performs well with near-independences.  
- Real-data case: influenza study where WPP produces narrower bounds than standard IV under moderate relaxations.  
- Strength: conceptually bridges faithfulness-based causal discovery and IV-style partial identification via interpretable sensitivity parameters.  
- Strength: methodological soundness—LP formulation follows established potential-outcome/response-function frameworks and implements clear relaxations.  
- Strength: practical contribution—symbolic bounds and back-substitution provide pragmatic, faster alternatives with good empirical approximation.  
- Strength: Bayesian treatment and falsification check address finite-sample issues inherent in constraint-based decisions.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation explores varied regimes and indicates robustness to approximate independences.  
- Weakness: choice and calibration of relaxation parameters is ad hoc with limited guidance; users could tune parameters to desired bounds.  
- Weakness: post-selection uncertainty from searching over (W,Z) candidates is not integrated; current Bayesian step conditions on chosen (W,Z) only and the falsification rule is heuristic.  
- Weakness: substantive interpretation of witnesses (e.g., DM) as quasi-instruments is questionable and needs guidance on plausibility assessment.  
- Weakness: dense notation and deferred proofs/inequalities to Supplement hinder clarity and reproducibility; starred/unstarred parameter distinctions are confusing.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—rejection sampler may be inefficient for tighter constraints or larger |Z|; runtime comparisons not quantified.  
- Weakness: no theoretical characterization of worst-case gap between back-substitution and LP bounds or conditions for tightness.  
- Suggestion: provide principled guidance/calibration for w, x, y, β, β̄ (e.g., map to log-odds, partial R2, or sensitivity metrics) and contour plots of bound widths.  
- Suggestion: report coverage properties in simulations and consider posterior intervals over bounds to reflect sampling uncertainty.  
- Suggestion: quantify computational performance (median runtimes, acceptance rates, mixing issues) for LP vs back-substitution and sampler efficiency.  
- Suggestion: address selection effects by averaging across top-k (W,Z) with model weights or developing post-selection corrections/falsification tests.  
- Suggestion: in influenza study, compare to standard IV with LATE/monotonicity and Rosenbaum-style sensitivity analyses, and report bounds under alternative witnesses/parameters.  
- Suggestion: improve exposition—unify notation, include a worked numerical example, and move key inequalities from Supplement to main text.  
- Evaluation: generally sound and original, with importance for bridging brittle point-identification and wide IV bounds; clarity and some theoretical aspects need strengthening.  
- Pros for acceptance: innovative synthesis, practical algorithms and code, and interpretable sensitivity parameters.  
- Cons for acceptance: parameter selection and post-selection inference not fully principled; limited guarantees for approximate algorithm; exposition needs improvement.  
- Overall assessment: strong, thought-provoking contribution recommended for acceptance after revisions addressing parameter calibration, selection effects, and clarity.
