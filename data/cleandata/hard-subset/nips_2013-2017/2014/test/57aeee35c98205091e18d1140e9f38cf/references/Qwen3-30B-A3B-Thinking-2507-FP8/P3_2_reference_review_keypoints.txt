- Proposal of Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE using covariates while relaxing faithfulness with tunable parameters.  
- Introduces relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) to allow bounded faithfulness violations and interpolate between point estimation and IV bounds.  
- Combines linear programming with a faster back-substitution algorithm for computing bounds and offers a Bayesian framework for selection and uncertainty quantification.  
- Validated on synthetic data and applied to a real influenza vaccination study.  

- Strength: Clearly identifies the practical problem of fragile faithfulness assumptions in causal inference.  
- Strength: The relaxation framework is well-motivated and connects appropriately to prior work (Dawid, Ramsahai).  
- Strength: Back-substitution algorithm is a useful computational contribution that improves efficiency over LP.  
- Strength: Experimental design includes controlled synthetic tests and a real-world case study.  

- Weakness: No principled method or guidance provided for choosing relaxation parameters, limiting practical use.  
- Weakness: Comparisons are limited (only against naive estimators and Entner); missing contrasts with other bounding methods (e.g., Manski, Geiger).  
- Weakness: Influenza example is underdeveloped and does not convincingly show practical advantage over standard IV bounds.  
- Weakness: Claims about conservativeness relative to back-door adjustment lack formal proof.  

- Suggestion: Provide principled parameter-selection methods or practical heuristics (sensitivity analysis, cross-validation).  
- Suggestion: Add broader comparisons (table) with other causal bounding approaches on assumptions, complexity, and tightness.  
- Suggestion: Expand influenza case with sensitivity analysis over parameter choices and discussion of practical impact.  
- Suggestion: Supply formal theoretical justification/proof for conservativeness claims.  

- Overall: Technically sound and novel, with a valuable computational algorithm, but limited by lack of parameter guidance and insufficient empirical/theoretical demonstration of advantages.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on major revisions addressing parameter selection, comparisons, real-world validation, and theoretical gaps.
