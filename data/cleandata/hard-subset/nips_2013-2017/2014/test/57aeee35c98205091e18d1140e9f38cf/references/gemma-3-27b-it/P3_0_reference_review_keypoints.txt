- Overall: Novel, technically sophisticated method for bounding ACE; leans toward acceptance but needs clarification and streamlining.  
- Core idea: Reinterpret Entner’s Rule 1 to find witness variables and formulate ACE bounds as a linear program with scalable relaxations.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Entner et al. and Dawid/Ramsahai, connecting LP IV bounds with practical relaxations for scalability.  
- Novelty strength: Original reinterpretation and memorable “Witness Protection Program” framing.  
- Technical strength: Rigorous LP formulation, clever relaxations, analytical approximations, and a back-substitution-like fast algorithm.  
- Practical relevance: Provides more robust bounds for causal inference in observational studies compared to strict faithfulness point estimates.  
- Awareness of limitations: Explicitly addresses possible faithfulness violations via bounded relaxations.  
- Experimental strength: Empirical results and a real-world example show improvements over naive estimators (though limited).  
- Clarity weakness: Paper is dense with heavy notation; key concepts and the “witness protection” intuition need clearer exposition.  
- Computational weakness: Overall complexity remains a concern; more detailed cost analysis as functions of covariates and data size is needed.  
- Parameter sensitivity weakness: Introduction of parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) requires sensitivity analysis and practical selection guidelines.  
- Experimental scope weakness: Experiments are limited to small datasets and lack comprehensive comparisons and scalability tests.  
- Prior choice weakness: Use of BDeu prior with effective sample size 10 is insufficiently justified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, rigorous, and practically minded approach that advances causal bounds methodology.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Readability, computational concerns, limited experiments, and unclear parameter/prior selection.  
- Specific suggestions: Rewrite intro/Section 3 for clarity, add examples/glossary, provide computational cost analysis, include sensitivity analysis, expand experiments, justify BDeu choice, and relate to IV/LATE literature.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Major Revisions.
