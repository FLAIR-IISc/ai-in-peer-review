- Introduces a method to bound the Average Causal Effect (ACE) using covariates assumed unaffected by the causal relation, via witness/admissible pairs and an LP formulation with relaxations and a faster algorithm; evaluated on synthetic and influenza data.  
- Builds on instrumental variable and Entner et al. work, extending LP-based bounds (Dawid & Ramsahai) and situating the approach within Pearl’s do-calculus and causal discovery literature.  
- Novel “Witness Protection Program” perspective that bridges admissible set identification and instrumental-variable-style bounding.  
- Explicitly models potential violations of faithfulness using parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), addressing a common limitation in causal discovery.  
- Proactively addresses computational concerns by proposing relaxations and a back-substitution/message-passing-like algorithm for faster bound computation.  
- Provides preliminary empirical validation on synthetic and a real-world influenza vaccination dataset, showing promise.  
- Clearly motivated and well-connected to existing literature, making the methodology accessible to causal inference researchers.  
- Introduces new parameters that require justification and careful tuning; experimental parameter choices are not fully justified and sensitivity is unclear.  
- Core LP formulation still appears computationally demanding for high-dimensional data; scalability limits and complexity analysis are not provided.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and lacks comparisons to mainstream causal methods (e.g., propensity score matching, inverse probability weighting).  
- Notation (especially in Section 3) is dense and sometimes inconsistent (ζ and η definitions), reducing readability.  
- Use of the BDeu score is insufficiently justified and appears arbitrary.  
- For acceptance: method is novel, technically sound, addresses key challenges, and shows promising preliminary results.  
- Against acceptance: practical applicability limited by computational cost and parameter tuning needs; empirical support and presentation clarity are insufficient.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revision: expand experiments, include comparisons to other causal methods, and perform parameter sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggested revision: provide a detailed complexity analysis of the LP formulation and proposed relaxations.  
- Suggested revision: improve notation and systematically explain variables and relationships.  
- Suggested revision: justify the BDeu score choice (and consider alternatives) and explicitly discuss limitations and failure scenarios.
