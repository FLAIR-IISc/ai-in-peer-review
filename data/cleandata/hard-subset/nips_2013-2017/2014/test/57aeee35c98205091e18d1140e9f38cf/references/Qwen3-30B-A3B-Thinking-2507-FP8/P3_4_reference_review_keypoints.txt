- Paper introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound average causal effect (ACE) by relaxing faithfulness using a "witness" and bounded local independence violations.  
- Novel contribution: addresses sensitivity to faithfulness and quantifies uncertainty from potential faithfulness violations.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous formulation of relaxation parameters and a back-substitution algorithm for efficient bounding.  
- Empirical validation: synthetic experiments show stability versus naive and faithfulness-based estimators; influenza study yields a different witness and tighter bounds in that case.  
- Honest trade-off discussion: acknowledges interval width vs. assumptions trade-off (cites Manski).  
- Limited comparisons: lacks sufficient benchmarking against existing causal-bounding methods (e.g., refs [7], [8], [16]).  
- Arbitrary relaxation parameters: no principled method for choosing (w, y, x, β, β̄), undermining practical applicability.  
- Weak real-world validation: influenza study does not convincingly show WPP outperforms standard IV methods or why the chosen witness is preferable.  
- Overstated Bayesian approach: ad hoc rejection-sampling model checking (95% threshold) lacks theoretical justification and interaction analysis with relaxation parameters.  
- Recommend expanding comparisons to established bounding methods to demonstrate relative advantages.  
- Recommend providing principled guidance or sensitivity analysis for selecting relaxation parameters.  
- Recommend strengthening real-world validation by explaining witness choice and comparing bounds to standard IV results.  
- Recommend clarifying and empirically supporting the claimed computational advantage of back-substitution.  
- Overall: technically solid and novel but diminished by limited comparisons, arbitrary parameter choices, and weak validation; reviewer recommends Weak Accept conditional on revisions.
