- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP): inverts Entner et al.'s witness idea to generate bounds on the average causal effect (ACE) using bounded violations of faithfulness, linear programming, algebraic symbolic bounds, and a Bayesian learning framework; evaluated on synthetic and influenza vaccine data.

- Technically strong and rigorous: clear theoretical derivations, connections to prior work, and acknowledgment of limitations; back-substitution offers a practical computational alternative to full LP.

- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: controlled synthetic tests demonstrate robustness to faithfulness violations and a real-world application shows practical utility.

- Generally clear and well-organized: background and core concepts are well-defined, though some technical sections (especially Bayesian learning) demand causal inference expertise.

- Highly original: novel inversion of the witness concept, relaxation of faithfulness via bounded local independence violations, and development of algebraic bounds and back-substitution algorithm.

- Significant contribution: addresses a practical problem of unstable faithfulness assumptions and offers a useful compromise between faithfulness-based point estimators and IV-based bounds.

- Strength: novel framework that provides robust causal bounds.

- Strength: well-motivated and theoretically grounded relaxation of faithfulness assumptions.

- Strength: computationally efficient algebraic bounds and back-substitution algorithm.

- Strength: comprehensive evaluation on both synthetic and real-world data.

- Strength: clear positioning relative to prior work and specific contributions articulated.

- Weakness: Bayesian learning section lacks implementation detail.

- Weakness: trade-offs between bound width and relaxation parameters (w, x, y) not fully explored.

- Weakness: no practical guidance on choosing relaxation parameters (left to future work).

- Weakness: synthetic experiments could analyze more precisely when WPP outperforms alternatives.

- Recommendation: accept — strong, novel, and significant contribution that meets NIPS criteria, with minor issues addressable in revision.
