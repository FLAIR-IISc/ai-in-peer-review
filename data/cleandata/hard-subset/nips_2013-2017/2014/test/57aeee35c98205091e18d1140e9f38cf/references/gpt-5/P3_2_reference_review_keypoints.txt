- Proposal: Witness Protection Program (WPP) bounds average causal effect (ACE) with binary X, Y using witness W as a conditional instrument and relaxation parameters to allow bounded violations of conditional independences.
- Method: Inverts Entner–Hoyer–Spirtes Rule 1, uses LP/polytope machinery (Dawid/Ramsahai-style extreme points) to derive bounds, interpolating between IV bounds and back-door point ID.
- Contributions: Introduces relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄) plus κ, ω to handle β-perturbations, and develops analytical inequalities and a back-substitution heuristic for fast conservative bounds.
- Bayesian component: Constrained Dirichlet priors over P(Y,X,W|Z=z) with posterior inference over bounds and a pragmatic falsification check.
- Empirics: Synthetic experiments show stability over naive/back-door methods; flu-vaccine case yields tighter bounds than unconditional IV.
- Positioning: Bridges constraint-based causal discovery, partial identification, and sensitivity/bounding literature with tunable faithfulness relaxations.
- Strength—conceptual: Novel inversion of Rule 1 to generate conditional IVs and a controllable spectrum between point ID and worst-case IV bounds.
- Strength—methodological: Rigorous extension of nonparametric binary IV LP machinery to incorporate explicit relaxations and selection bounds.
- Strength—computational: Algebraic inequalities and back-substitution provide practical, interpretable, and fast approximate bounds.
- Strength—uncertainty quantification: Posterior propagation of sampling uncertainty via constrained Dirichlet priors is more honest than plug-in methods.
- Strength—empirical robustness: Demonstrated stability when exact witnesses are absent and informative real-data bounds.
- Weakness—parameter elicitation: Limited guidance for choosing/tuning (w,x,y,β,β̄); risk of researcher degrees-of-freedom without principled calibration or interpretable scales.
- Weakness—model checking/falsification: The 95% rejection heuristic is ad hoc; lacks power/size analysis or formal posterior predictive checks.
- Weakness—approximation fidelity: Back-substitution can produce infeasible contingency tables; final reporting sometimes uses these approximations undermining correctness guarantees.
- Weakness—comparisons: Baselines are limited; needs comparisons to conditional Balke–Pearl IV bounds, other sensitivity/partial-ID methods, and reporting of interval widths and coverage.
- Weakness—theoretical guarantees: Claims about conservativeness relative to Entner’s back-door and convergence need formal proofs or sketch and conditions for consistency as relaxations shrink.
- Clarity—notation/exposition: Key symbols and tables are partially specified; recommend a self-contained definition block, schematic, and clearer constraint-to-violation mapping with a running example.
- Clarity—algorithmic details: Algorithm 1 omits concrete search/test pipeline (BDeu settings, multiple-testing control, near-independence treatment) and complexity analysis.
- Originality: Novel combination of Rule-1-based discovery with IV partial identification and bounded faithfulness; analytical inequalities/back-substitution are original.
- Significance: Potentially impactful if accompanied by practical θ selection guidance and stronger empirical validation; code availability is positive.
- Specific Q1: How should practitioners elicit and interpret (w,x,y,β,β̄) in domain terms (e.g., effect sizes or odds ratios)?
- Specific Q2: Can authors prove that as θ→(0,0,0,1,1) and sample size→∞, WPP recovers back-door ACE when Z is admissible and detects falsification otherwise?
- Specific Q3: How sensitive are results to BDeu hyperparameters and exhaustive Z search; can robust CI testing/multiple-testing control be integrated?
- Specific Q4: For flu data, show why GRP was rejected and DM accepted with independence scores, falsification diagnostics, and θ sensitivity analysis.
- Pros for acceptance: Novel principled bridge of discovery and IV bounds, sound LP extension, fast approximate algorithm, Bayesian UQ, code provided.
- Cons for acceptance: Insufficient θ selection guidance, limited baselines/metrics, possible infeasible approximations, notation/assumption clarity issues, and unjustified real-data θ choices.
- Overall: Promising, original, and technically solid approach that needs clearer exposition, principled parameter selection, stronger comparisons, and rigorous validation of approximations and falsification.
