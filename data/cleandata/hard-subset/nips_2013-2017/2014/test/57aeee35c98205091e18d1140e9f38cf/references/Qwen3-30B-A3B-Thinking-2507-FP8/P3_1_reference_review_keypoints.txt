- Introduces a framework to bound the average causal effect (ACE) using covariates unaffected by X or Y and reinterprets Entner et al.'s "witness" variables as instrumental variables.
- Core method: linear programming to generate ACE bounds allowing controlled faithfulness violations, with a Bayesian uncertainty component and an efficient back-substitution algorithm.
- Demonstrated on synthetic data and an influenza vaccination case study.

- Strength — Quality: technically sound, rigorous derivations, and principled Bayesian implementation with constrained Dirichlet priors.
- Strength — Originality: novel reinterpretation of witnesses as IVs and a systematic framework for faithfulness violations; useful back-substitution algorithm.
- Strength — Significance: addresses practical issues from faithfulness violations and yields more informative bounds in a real-world case.
- Strength — Clarity: well-written, organized, and reproducible methodology explanations.

- Weakness — Parameter selection: lacks clear, principled guidance for choosing relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄).
- Weakness — Efficiency claims: claims the back-substitution is much faster but provides no concrete benchmarks against full optimization.
- Weakness — Comparative context: insufficient distinction and comparison with existing bounds approaches (e.g., Manski 1990, Ramsahai 2016).
- Weakness — Application justification: insufficient causal justification for selecting DM with AGE and SEX as a valid witness in the influenza study.

- Recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance at NIPS.

- Minor suggestions: add a complexity/benchmark table for back-substitution vs full LP; provide guidance for selecting relaxation parameters; clarify distinctions from existing bounds methods; strengthen witness-selection justification in the case study.
