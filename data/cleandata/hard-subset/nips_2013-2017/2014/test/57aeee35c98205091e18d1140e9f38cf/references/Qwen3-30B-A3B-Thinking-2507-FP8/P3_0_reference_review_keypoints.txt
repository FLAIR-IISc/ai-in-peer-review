- Summary: Proposes using "witnesses" as instrumental variables with bounded faithfulness violations to bound the average causal effect (ACE) of binary X on Y, using relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) and symbolic bounds with a back-substitution algorithm.

- Novelty: Core insight of treating witnesses as IVs under bounded faithfulness violations is novel and addresses limitations of existing methods.

- Relaxation framework: Introduces principled relaxation parameters (Equations 3–6) to model bounded departures from faithfulness.

- Computational contribution: Back-substitution algorithm and symbolic algebraic bounds are much faster than standard linear programming while retaining reasonable tightness.

- Bayesian treatment: Uses a Bayesian approach with constrained Dirichlet priors and rejection sampling to handle uncertainty more principledly than plug-in estimators.

- Empirical validation: Synthetic experiments show robustness to faithfulness violations and a real-world influenza vaccination case study demonstrates practical applicability.

- Related work comparison: Insufficient distinction from existing bounded/robust causal inference methods (e.g., Manski, Geiger); paper should clarify improvements over prior work.

- Overstated claims and limited validation: Claim that method should become standard is premature given testing on only one real dataset and limited synthetic graph structures.

- Parameter selection: Lacks practical guidance on choosing relaxation parameters; default values used appear arbitrary and need justification.

- Theoretical gaps: Does not fully analyze when witness-based approach outperforms standard IV methods or provide tightness guarantees for bounds.

- Suggested revisions: Provide deeper comparisons to related work, justify/guide relaxation parameter selection, expand empirical validation across datasets, and clarify theoretical characterization of bound tightness.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — technically sound and valuable, with mostly presentation and validation improvements requested.
