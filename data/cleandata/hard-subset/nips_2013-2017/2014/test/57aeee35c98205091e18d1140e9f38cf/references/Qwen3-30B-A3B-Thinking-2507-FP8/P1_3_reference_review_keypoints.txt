1. Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a novel method that bounds average causal effects by reinterpreting witness variables as instrumental variables and relaxing faithfulness.

2. Derives bounds via linear programming and provides symbolic bounds to improve computational efficiency and interpretability.

3. Proposes a back-substitution algorithm to avoid expensive LPs and offers a Bayesian implementation for uncertainty quantification.

4. Demonstrates empirical robustness on synthetic data and a real influenza vaccination study, outperforming baselines when faithfulness is violated.

5. Provides clear theoretical foundations and explicitly states relaxed assumptions, with symbolic bounds showing how relaxation parameters affect results.

6. Clearly differentiates WPP from prior work (e.g., Dawid, Ramsahai, Entner) and positions it as a middle ground between point estimates and conservative bounds.

7. Lacks formal theoretical comparisons of bound tightness against other bound-based approaches (e.g., Geiger et al.).

8. Claims the back-substitution is "an order of magnitude more efficient" but omits concrete complexity analysis or runtime comparisons to LP methods.

9. Offers insufficient practical guidance for selecting relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄), relying on defaults without thorough sensitivity analysis.

10. Method is restricted to binary variables, limiting applicability to continuous or multi-category real-world problems.

11. Some key technical details and proofs are relegated to the supplementary material, making full assessment from the main paper difficult.

12. Overall recommendation: Accept — the paper is a meaningful, theoretically sound, and practically useful contribution to robust causal inference.
