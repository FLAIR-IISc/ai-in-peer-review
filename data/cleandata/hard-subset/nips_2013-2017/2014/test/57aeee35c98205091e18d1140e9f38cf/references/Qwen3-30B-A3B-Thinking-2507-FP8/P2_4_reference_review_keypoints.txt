- Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE using witnesses as instrumental variables by inverting Entner et al.'s Rule 1.
- Expresses faithfulness violations as bounded deviations in local independence and generates ACE bounds via constrained linear programming.
- Provides novel symbolic bounds and a fast back-substitution algorithm for computational efficiency.
- Presents a Bayesian framework to handle uncertainty and produce credible intervals rather than point estimates.
- Theoretical development is rigorous with clear ties to prior work (Pearl, Entner et al., Ramsahai).
- Experiments include synthetic tests with known ground truth and a real influenza vaccination case study.
- Empirical comparisons against NE1, NE2, and Entner's estimator show WPP produces more conservative but robust bounds.
- Work fills a gap between faithfulness-based point estimates and IV-based bounds through principled relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄).
- Code availability enhances reproducibility.
- Paper is well-organized and accessible to causal inference researchers; key concepts are well-defined and illustrated.
- Methodology section could use more intuitive explanations of constraints (3)–(6).
- Algebraic bounds (Section 4) are technically sound but may be difficult for some readers; a simple worked example is missing.
- Limited guidance on choosing relaxation parameters in practice.
- Limited discussion of scalability to high-dimensional settings and comparison to more recent unobserved-confounder methods.
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, novel contribution with strong theory and experiments despite minor accessibility and guidance issues.
