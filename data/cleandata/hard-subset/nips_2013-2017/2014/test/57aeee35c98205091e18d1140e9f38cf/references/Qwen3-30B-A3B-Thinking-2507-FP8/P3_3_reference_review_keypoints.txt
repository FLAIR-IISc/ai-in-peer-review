- Summary: Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE by relaxing faithfulness, using witnesses as IVs with relaxation parameters, linear programming, Bayesian model selection, and an efficient back-substitution algorithm.  
- Strength: Addresses fragility of faithfulness and offers a principled middle ground between point estimates and conservative IV bounds.  
- Strength: Introduces relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) to quantify faithfulness violations.  
- Strength: Uses BDeu-based Bayesian model selection well suited to the context.  
- Strength: Back-substitution algorithm yields substantial computational gains over full LP.  
- Strength: Influenza vaccination case study demonstrates practical utility and more informative bounds than standard IV in that example.  
- Weakness: No practical guidance on choosing relaxation parameters; method’s usefulness depends on them.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison with established robust/partial-identification methods (e.g., Manski, Geiger et al.).  
- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation—comparisons only to naive estimators and Entner, not to standard IV or other bounds.  
- Weakness: Algebraic/back-substitution bounds are conservative and may be too wide for practical use.  
- Weakness: Lacks sensitivity analysis on how bounds respond to relaxation-parameter choices.  
- Originality: Novel framing of faithfulness violations via systematic relaxation, but core idea of using witnesses as IVs is related to existing IV approaches.  
- Significance: Moderate—useful methodological refinement but not a major advance without stronger empirical and practical validation.  
- Recommendation: Technically sound but needs better parameter-selection guidance, comparisons, and empirical evaluation; overall recommendation — Weak Accept.
