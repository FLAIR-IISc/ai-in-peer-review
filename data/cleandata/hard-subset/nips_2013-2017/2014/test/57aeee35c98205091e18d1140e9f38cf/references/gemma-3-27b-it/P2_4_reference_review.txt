## Review of "Causal Inference through a Witness Protection Program"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel methodology for bounding the Average Causal Effect (ACE) utilizing a “Witness Protection Program” (WPP) framework. It builds upon the work of Entner et al. by reinterpreting their search procedure for instrumental variables and leveraging linear programming (LP) relaxations to generate bounds on the ACE, even in the presence of potential violations of the faithfulness assumption. The authors also develop efficient, scalable algorithms, including a back-substitution method, to tackle the computational complexity of the LP approach. The paper includes both synthetic experiments and an application to influenza vaccination data.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The "Witness Protection Program" framing is a clever and potentially impactful way to address the challenges of causal inference with observational data, particularly the sensitivity to faithfulness assumptions. The authors successfully combine ideas from instrumental variable analysis, causal discovery, and linear programming in a unique way.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid theoretical grounding for the proposed method, building upon existing work in causal inference and clearly outlining the assumptions and relaxations made. The development of algebraic bounds and the back-substitution algorithm are significant theoretical contributions.
* **Practicality:**  The authors recognize the computational challenges of their approach and propose practical relaxations and algorithms (back-substitution) to improve scalability. This focus on implementability is crucial.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments, while limited, demonstrate the potential of the method in both synthetic and real-world scenarios. The comparison to naive estimators and the application to the influenza data provide initial evidence of effectiveness.
* **Clear Writing (mostly):** While dense in places, the paper generally presents the ideas clearly, particularly with the helpful use of figures. The authors acknowledge the limitations and potential issues with the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The method is complex, requiring a strong background in causal inference, linear programming, and graphical models to fully understand. This complexity might limit its adoption by practitioners.
* **Computational Cost:** While the authors address scalability, the reliance on LP, even with relaxations, is still potentially computationally expensive for high-dimensional datasets.  The back-substitution algorithm is a good step, but its performance characteristics need further scrutiny.
* **Sensitivity to Parameters:** The performance of the method appears sensitive to the choice of relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄). The paper acknowledges this, but the guidance on how to select these parameters is limited.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments are somewhat limited in scope.  More extensive validation on a wider range of datasets, including those with varying degrees of confounding and faithfulness violations, is needed. The choice of evaluating performance by the width of the resulting interval (or the probability that it contains the true ACE) is not immediately obvious and could benefit from justification.
* **Clarity in Specific Sections:**  Section 4 describing the algebraic bounds and back-substitution algorithm feels rushed and could benefit from more detailed explanations and illustrative examples.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with a clear theoretical foundation and rigorous methodology. The claims are supported by experimental results, although further validation is needed. It's a complete piece of work within the scope it sets, but acknowledging the limitations is vital.
* **Clarity:** Generally good, but the complexity of the method makes it challenging to follow in some sections. More detailed explanations and examples, especially for Section 4, would improve clarity.
* **Originality:** The WPP framework and the combination of techniques are novel. The authors adequately reference related work and position their contribution within the existing literature.
* **Significance:** The potential impact of the method is significant. Providing robust bounds on causal effects, even in the presence of unmeasured confounding and violations of faithfulness, is a crucial problem in many fields. The development of efficient algorithms could make this approach practical for a wider range of applications.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem in causal inference.
* Potential to provide more robust and reliable estimates of causal effects than existing methods.
* Addresses a critical limitation of existing methods (sensitivity to faithfulness assumptions).
* Presents practical algorithms to improve scalability.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  Complexity of the method may limit its adoption.
*  Computational cost remains a concern, despite efforts to address it.
*  Limited empirical validation.
*  Parameter sensitivity requires further investigation.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of causal inference. While the method is complex and requires further refinement and validation, the novelty of the approach, the solid theoretical foundation, and the potential for significant impact outweigh the weaknesses. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept with Minor Revisions**

I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions. The authors should:

1.  Provide more detailed explanations and examples for Section 4, specifically clarifying the back-substitution algorithm.
2.  Expand the empirical evaluation to include a wider range of datasets and assess the sensitivity of the method to the choice of relaxation parameters.
3.  Discuss the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms in more detail.
4. Clarify the motivation behind using the interval width as a performance metric.
