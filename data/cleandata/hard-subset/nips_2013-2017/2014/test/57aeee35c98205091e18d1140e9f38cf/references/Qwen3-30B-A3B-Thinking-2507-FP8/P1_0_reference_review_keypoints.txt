- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), reinterpreting Entner et al.'s "witness" as a protected instrumental variable to bound the average causal effect from observational data.  
- Proposes a linear-programming framework with relaxed constraints and a computationally efficient symbolic back-substitution method to derive ACE bounds.  
- Provides detailed mathematical derivations and symbolic algebra revealing interactions of relaxation parameters.  
- Uses Bayesian learning to handle uncertainty and demonstrates utility on synthetic data and an influenza vaccination case study.  
- Novel contribution: systematically relaxing faithfulness assumptions to generate candidate IVs rather than discarding witnesses.  
- Significance: offers a practical middle ground between fragile faithfulness-based point estimates and wide IV bounds, with real-world applicability.  
- Clarity: well-structured, consistent notation, helpful figures, and useful supplemental material for reproducibility.  
- Computational efficiency concern: claim of major speedup for back-substitution lacks sufficient empirical runtime comparisons to baseline LP methods.  
- Parameter selection issue: reliance on arbitrary default relaxation parameters (e.g., 0.2) without systematic guidance limits practical use.  
- Theoretical bound tightness: bounds are acknowledged as conservative but not quantitatively compared to alternative methods.  
- Scalability concern: insufficient discussion of how WPP scales to high-dimensional settings with hundreds or thousands of covariates.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution, appropriate for NeurIPS, and recommended for acceptance despite areas needing strengthening.  
- Actionable suggestions for authors: provide empirical runtime comparisons, expand parameter-selection guidance and sensitivity analyses, address scalability, and compare bound widths to standard IV bounds.
