- Proposes a new ACE bounding method using inverse Entner’s Rule 1, relaxed faithfulness (path-cancellation), inequality constraints, and LP formulation.  
- Obtained bounds are narrow and appear more accurate than existing approaches, despite the algorithm not being optimized.  
- The validity of applying Entner’s Rule 1 in the inverse direction is not clearly justified; authors should explicitly assume and discuss the Fig.2 structure.  
- No generic criteria are given for selecting relaxation parameters, though these strongly affect the resulting bounds.  
- Theoretical exposition is mostly comprehensive but misses the essential discussion on the inverse-rule validity and parameter choices.  
- Experimental implementation and algorithm details are poorly described; parameter definitions in the referenced tables are unclear or missing.  
- Typos and incorrect/missing table references (e.g., “Table 1”/“Table 5”) reduce clarity.  
- Originality is limited by prior work on the problem, though the unified combination of three ideas has some novelty that is not fully validated.  
- Significance: promising tighter ACE bounds, but limited experiments and insufficient theoretical/empirical validation leave generality and robustness unassessed.
