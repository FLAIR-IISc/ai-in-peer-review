- Paper addresses bounding the average causal effect (ACE) for binary variables when covariate causal structure is not fully known, using Entner et al.'s rule and a Ramsahai (2012)-style method.
- Reviewer finds the main idea sensible and potentially useful but has not checked all technical details.
- Major concern: unclear role of Entner et al.'s rule conditions without the faithfulness assumption — the conditions do not obviously imply causal structure when faithfulness is dropped.
- Suspected that Entner et al. conditions might inform choice of relaxation parameters, but the paper explicitly leaves this connection unresolved.
- Doubt that the proposed machinery depends crucially on Entner et al.'s conditions; asks for clarification on why those conditions matter for the method and experiments.
- Minor comment 1: wording “express violations of faithfulness as bounded violations of local independence” is odd—unfaithfulness means extra independences; the framework seems to allow “almost violations” by bounding dependency magnitude.
- Minor comment 2: suggest reporting Entner et al.'s method performance using their stringent thresholds, treating “don’t know” as uninformative bounds, for comparison.
- Minor comment 3: clarification needed on interpretation in second paragraph p.8 — with X=1 (flu shot) and Y=1 (not hospitalized), why does a negative ACE indicate a desirable vaccine?
- Minor comment 4: citation/table error — “Table 1” on p.8 is referred to as “Table 5” in the text.
- Overall: interesting paper but details are hard to follow and key methodological connections need clarification.
