- Proposal: Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE under confounding and approximate faithfulness by treating Entner–Hoyer–Spirtes Rule 1 witnesses as “almost-instruments.”

- Key idea: Inverts Rule 1 to generate near-IV candidates and uses linear programming under explicit relaxations of faithfulness to derive ACE bounds.

- Relaxations: Models bounded violations of exclusion, independence, and stability via parameters w, x, y, β, β̄, extending Balke–Pearl and IV polytopes.

- Computational tools: Combines LP, analytic algebraic inequalities, and a back-substitution heuristic for faster feasibility checks and bound computation.

- Bayesian component: Uses constrained Dirichlet priors on P(Y,X,W|Z) and rejection sampling to produce posterior distributions over upper and lower ACE bounds.

- Empirics: Synthetic experiments and a flu-vaccine encouragement example show WPP can give substantially tighter and more robust intervals than standard IV and naive/faithfulness-based estimators.

- Novelty: The inversion of Rule 1 to produce tunable near-IVs and the specific algebraic bounds/back-substitution approach appear new.

- Conceptual strength: Provides a principled middle ground between faithfulness-driven point identification and IV-based partial identification with interpretable knobs.

- Methodological strength: Solid use of convex-analytic machinery and useful computational heuristics that extend existing IV theory to conditional settings.

- Practical strength: End-to-end algorithm (searching witnesses/admissible sets, feasibility testing, aggregating posterior bounds) with available code.

- Bayesian strength: Addresses sampling uncertainty over observables rather than fragile priors on latent causal parameters.

- Main weakness: No principled method provided for selecting relaxation parameters; guidance or data-driven calibration is needed.

- Sampling weakness: Rejection sampling from constrained Dirichlets may be inefficient; acceptance-rate diagnostics and scalable sampler comparisons are missing.

- Experimental weakness: Missing important baselines (conditional IV bounds, Manski bounds, Rosenbaum-style sensitivity), and lacking width vs. coverage reporting and falsification rates.

- Robustness concern: When Rule 1 fails, WPP can still propose witnesses under approximate independencies, risking anti-conservatism without additional diagnostics or multiplicity correction.

- Clarity issues: Exposition is dense; needs a compact glossary, clearer mapping between ζ/κ/η/ω, and a worked example for a single z-cell.

- Minor fixes: Typos and small manuscript glitches should be corrected.

- Originality assessment: Combination of faithfulness-derived near-IVs, algebraic inequalities, LP-based bounds, and Bayesian posterior-over-bounds is novel.

- Significance assessment: Addresses an important practical gap but currently limited to binary variables and modest Z; extensions would increase impact.

- Suggested improvements: Provide strategies to set/learn relaxations (priors, sensitivity plots), quantify computational complexity and sampler acceptance rates, and compare LP vs back-substitution.

- Suggested additions: Add missing baselines, report joint width/coverage metrics, and implement multiplicity corrections or Bayesian model averaging across (W,Z) pairs.

- Diagnostic recommendation: Flag posterior mass concentrating near constraint boundaries to detect model misspecification.

- Pros summary: Timely conceptual compromise, strong convex-analytic foundation, Bayesian uncertainty handling, and promising empirical behavior.

- Cons summary: User-dependent relaxation choices, limited experimental breadth and diagnostics, binary-only scope, and clarity problems.

- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and potentially influential but needs better guidance on relaxations and clearer exposition.
