- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE for binary X→Y by inverting Entner et al.’s Rule 1, treating W as a conditional IV and using LP under relaxations of faithfulness (parameters w,x,y,β,β̄).  
- Encodes bounded deviations from conditional independences to allow small direct effects/residual confounding while avoiding vacuous bounds.  
- Generalizes existing IV bounds (Balke–Pearl, Dawid, Ramsahai) and connects to do-calculus and causal discovery under (approximate) faithfulness.  
- Provides algebraic (symbolic) bounds, a back-substitution approximation with substantial speedups, and a Bayesian procedure giving a posterior over bounds via constrained Dirichlet priors and rejection sampling.  
- Technical soundness: careful mapping of relaxations to latent/observable constraints, LP construction follows prior work, algebraic relaxations are conservative, and Bayesian treatment acknowledges uncertainty/falsification.  
- Evidence: synthetic experiments stress-test collider bias and Rule 1 cases; influenza dataset shows WPP often yields tighter intervals than standard IV.  
- Main caveat: informativeness depends on user-chosen relaxation parameters; guidance deferred to supplement and risks post-hoc tuning.  
- Falsification procedure is ad hoc (reject if >95% posterior samples fail); more formal posterior predictive checks or Bayes factors preferred.  
- Computational concerns: exhaustive Z search and constrained rejection sampling may not scale; back-substitution helps but empirical scaling analysis is limited.  
- Clarity: generally well organized with strong background, but heavy notation and some one-off definitions; recommend a running symbol table and more detail on Z-search thresholds.  
- Originality: novel inversion of Rule 1, parameterized faithfulness relaxations interpolating between IV and back-door, and combining LP bounds with a posterior over bounds; algebraic/back-substitution approximations are new.  
- Significance: addresses partial identification under tunable relaxations, offering a principled alternative to naive adjustment and worst-case IV bounds; broader impact depends on parameter elicitation, scalability, and non-binary extensions.  
- Pros: elegant conceptual bridge between causal discovery and IV bounding; recovers known models; offers exact and fast approximations; quantifies uncertainty; empirically robust and informative.  
- Cons: relies on user-specified relaxations without principled elicitation, scalability and binary-only limitation, heuristic falsification, and incomplete comparisons to other sensitivity/partial ID methods.  
- Suggestions: provide practical elicitation/calibration of parameters and sensitivity curves; report scaling/runtime comparisons LP vs back-substitution; discuss non-binary extensions; replace ad-hoc falsification with formal checks; broaden baseline comparisons; justify witness choice and robustness in application.  
- Recommendation: technically sound, original, and significant; despite open issues on parameter selection, scalability, and comparisons, the reviewer leans toward acceptance.
