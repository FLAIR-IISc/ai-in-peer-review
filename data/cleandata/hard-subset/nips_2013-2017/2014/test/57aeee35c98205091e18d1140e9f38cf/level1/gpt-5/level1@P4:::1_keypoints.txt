- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE for binary X,Y by treating a discovered witness W as a conditional instrument and relaxing faithfulness via tunable parameters.
- Uses linear programming over polytopes mapping latent-response probabilities to observables to derive ACE bounds conditional on Z.
- Introduces relaxation parameters (w, x, y, β, β̄) that interpolate between IV, chain, and unconstrained confounding models by bounding conditional-independence violations and W–U dependence.
- Parameterizes latent-response η and treatment propensities δ, imposes inequality constraints, and maps to observable contingency tables to obtain bounds.
- Provides algebraic/symbolic inequalities as functions of relaxation parameters and a back-substitution “message-passing-like” algorithm to approximate LP bounds efficiently.
- Uses a Bayesian approach sampling constrained Dirichlet posteriors over P(Y,X,W|Z) to produce posterior distributions over ACE bounds and to perform model falsification via rejection rates.
- Evaluated on synthetic datasets designed to bias naive estimators and on an influenza encouragement design, yielding narrower bounds than standard IV for the chosen witness/admissible set.
- Strength: Sound construction of bounds via polytopes with interpretable relaxations, building on established IV bounding literature.
- Strength: Mapping between ζ, κ and ω under bounds on P(U|W) appears internally consistent.
- Strength: Algebraic inequalities and back-substitution provide useful, conservative approximations that reduce computational load.
- Strength: Bayesian treatment of observable uncertainty via constrained Dirichlet priors is principled and avoids plug-in overconfidence.
- Weakness: Restricted to binary variables and discretization without analysis of sensitivity to binning.
- Weakness: No principled guidance for choosing relaxation parameters, limiting practical applicability and interpretability.
- Weakness: Bayesian rejection-sampling may be inefficient in higher dimensions; the 95% rejection threshold is heuristic.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation limited—no benchmarking against alternative bounding methods (e.g., IV with monotonicity, potential-outcomes sensitivity analyses).
- Weakness: Coverage properties of the posterior over bounds are not reported (e.g., true ACE inclusion frequency).
- Weakness: Influenza example changes witness/admissible set from original IV, complicating comparison and requiring deeper sensitivity analysis.
- Clarity strength: Problem statement, positioning between discovery and IV bounds, and role of relaxation parameters are clearly presented.
- Clarity weakness: Dense notation and key derivations relegated to supplement reduce reproducibility; κ and ω substitutions need clearer exposition.
- Originality: Novel inversion of Rule 1 to produce conditional-IV-style bounds under bounded faithfulness violations and tunable interpolation between models; back-substitution approximation is a new practical addition.
- Significance: Addresses key challenge of bounding ACE under unmeasured confounding and, if adopted, could complement faithfulness-based discovery and IV analysis.
- Limitation to impact: Scalability concerns, binary discretization, and lack of guidance for parameter selection moderate immediate practical impact.
- Suggested improvements: Provide principled defaults or elicitation protocols for relaxation parameters and discretization guidance.
- Suggested improvements: Report computational complexity, acceptance rates, wall-clock times for LP and back-substitution across Z dimensions.
- Suggested improvements: Evaluate coverage and calibration of posterior bounds and compare against more baselines (IV w/monotonicity, sensitivity analyses).
- Suggested improvements: Expand main-text derivations for κ, ω constraints and clarify witness search and prior-setting guidance.
- Arguments for acceptance: Conceptually novel bridge between faithfulness-based discovery and IV bounds, solid technical basis, Bayesian uncertainty treatment, code and real-data demo.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited empirical validation and benchmarking, no coverage assessment, unclear parameter selection, scalability and presentation issues.
- Overall: Technically sound and novel framework with promising potential but needs stronger empirical validation, clearer parameter guidance, and improved presentation to maximize practical utility.
