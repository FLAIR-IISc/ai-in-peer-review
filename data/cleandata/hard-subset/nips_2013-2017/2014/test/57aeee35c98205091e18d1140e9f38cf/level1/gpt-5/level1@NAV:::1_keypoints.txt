- Proposes Witness Protection Program (WPP) to convert conditional independence evidence into partial identification of the average causal effect (ACE).
- Core idea: invert Entner–Hoyer–Spirtes “Rule 1” by treating witness W as a candidate instrument conditional on Z to derive ACE bounds.
- Introduces bounded relaxations of faithfulness via parameters controlling path cancellations and residual dependencies (w, x, y, β, β̄).
- Computes bounds using a linear programming formulation extending Dawid and Ramsahai’s IV work.
- Provides a Bayesian posterior over bounds using constrained Dirichlet priors and rejection sampling to capture sampling uncertainty.
- Presents algebraic (symbolic) bounds and a back-substitution procedure as a faster approximation to the LP solution.
- Experiments on synthetic data and a flu vaccination study show WPP yields informative intervals, improved stability, and can beat Balke–Pearl IV bounds when conditional independencies are informative.
- Strong and appropriate relation to prior work (Pearl, faithfulness/PC, Entner et al., Manski, Balke–Pearl, Dawid, Ramsahai).
- Methodology is technically sound and carefully positioned; LP and algebraic relaxations are plausible.
- Coverage guarantees for the posterior over bounds are not fully quantified; frequentist properties remain unclear.
- The falsification heuristic (95% rejection in constrained Dirichlet sampling) appears ad hoc and insufficiently justified.
- Guidance for selecting relaxation parameters is limited and deferred to the supplement, hindering practical reproducibility.
- Paper is dense but organized; notation could be introduced earlier, algorithmic complexity clarified, and pseudo-code for back-substitution added.
- Originality and significance are high; the approach meaningfully bridges faithfulness-based discovery with IV bounds and could influence practice.
- Pros: novel principled relaxation of faithfulness, solid integration of LP/Bayesian/symbolic methods, and supportive empirical evidence.
- Cons: limited parameter-selection guidance and sensitivity analysis, heuristic model falsification, unclear frequentist coverage, and need for expanded scalability/clarity discussion.
