- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a novel method to bound the average causal effect (ACE) by relaxing the faithfulness assumption to allow path cancellations.  
- Formulates ACE bounding as a linear program (LP) building on admissible sets and instrumental-variable ideas (Entner et al., Ramsahai).  
- Proposes further relaxations and a fast, message-passing-like algorithm to mitigate LP computational challenges.  
- Demonstrates the approach on synthetic data (with ground truth) and a real-world influenza vaccination dataset.  
- Well-grounded in causal graphical model literature and clearly positions itself relative to foundational and IV work.  
- Novelty: offers a nuanced alternative to strict faithfulness, bridging point estimation and bounding techniques.  
- Practical considerations: addresses computational issues and provides algorithmic speedups.  
- Clear writing and thorough exposition of core ideas and technical details.  
- Computational complexity remains a concern, especially as covariate dimensionality grows.  
- Method appears sensitive to relaxation parameter choices (w, y, x, β, β̄) and needs systematic sensitivity analysis.  
- Limited discussion on practical interpretation and use of wide vs. narrow bounds for decision-making.  
- Bayesian implementation lacks detail (Dirichlet priors, rejection sampling) and the effect of small effective sample size is unexplored.  
- Missing comparisons to other recent bounding methods, which could clarify trade-offs in assumptions and complexity.  
- Acceptance arguments: theoretically sound, novel, addresses unobserved confounding, and supported by experiments.  
- Rejection arguments: scalability and parameter-sensitivity concerns, and insufficient practical interpretation and comparative evaluation.  
- Overall: significant contribution worth publication with minor revisions to address scalability, sensitivity analysis, interpretation, and comparative baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
