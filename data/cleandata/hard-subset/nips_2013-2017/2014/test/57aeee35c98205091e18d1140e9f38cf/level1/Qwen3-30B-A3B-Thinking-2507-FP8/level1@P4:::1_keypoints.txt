- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) for estimating causal effects when confounders may be unobserved.  
- Tackles ACE estimation under possible unobserved confounding and violated faithfulness.  
- Builds on Entner et al. (2013) and Dawid & Ramsahai’s LP approaches for instrumental variable bounds.  
- Key innovation: relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow path cancellations that produce conditional independencies.  
- Produces posterior distributions over bounds on the ACE via a linear programming framework instead of point estimates.  
- Reinterprets Entner’s Rule 1 to generate candidate instrumental variables for a bounding approach.  
- Introduces an LP formulation with constraints that implement the faithfulness relaxation.  
- Develops a Bayesian inference framework using constrained Dirichlet priors for uncertainty quantification.  
- Provides a computationally efficient back-substitution algorithm to derive symbolic bounds.  
- Validated through synthetic experiments and a real-world influenza vaccination case study.  
- Theoretical derivations are rigorous and the technical approach is sound.  
- Experimental results support the paper’s claims; supplementary material adds technical detail.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, notably selecting relaxation parameters and the computational cost of full LP.  
- The Bayesian approach avoids sensitivity issues of priors on unidentifiable latent variables.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear background and mathematical notation.  
- Some implementation details (e.g., Bayesian specifics) are in the supplement; more guidance on choosing relaxation parameters would help.  
- Originality: meaningfully relaxes faithfulness, bridging point estimators and IV bounds.  
- Symbolic bounds are novel, giving algebraic relations between free parameters and bounds.  
- Related work is adequately cited and distinctions from prior methods are clear.  
- Significance: offers practical, more robust causal estimates when faithfulness is questionable.  
- Influenza study shows WPP yields more informative bounds than standard IV in practice.  
- Strengths: clear theory, novel relaxation, Bayesian uncertainty quantification, efficient algorithm, comprehensive validation.  
- Weaknesses: unclear parameter selection strategy, LP scalability concerns, limited real-world evaluation (one case study).  
- Conclusion: technically strong, novel, and impactful method; reviewer recommends acceptance.
