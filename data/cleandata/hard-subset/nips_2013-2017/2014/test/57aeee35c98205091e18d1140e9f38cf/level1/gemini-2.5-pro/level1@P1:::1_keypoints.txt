- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE from observational data with unmeasured confounding by combining faithfulness-based discovery and IV methods.  
- Re-purposes Entner et al.'s witness/admissible set search to propose W as a candidate IV conditional on Z, then performs relaxed bounding instead of taking point estimates.  
- Introduces explicit user-defined relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) that quantify allowable violations of faithfulness and direct effects.  
- Uses a linear programming framework (building on Dawid and Ramsahai) to derive ACE bounds consistent with observed data and the relaxation parameters.  
- Embeds the LP approach in a Bayesian constrained Dirichlet model and proposes a fast back-substitution algorithm for tractable posterior inference.  
- Technical quality is high: methodology is sound, well-motivated, and properly grounded in existing causal-bounds literature.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with effective figures, tables, and a helpful Algorithm 1.  
- The synthesis of faithfulness-based search and relaxed IV bounding is highly original and conceptually important.  
- The method has substantial potential significance for applied researchers, encouraging explicit quantification of assumptions.  
- Synthetic experiments demonstrate robustness where standard methods fail; the influenza example shows tighter, more credible bounds than standard IV.  
- Major weakness: insufficient guidance on how practitioners should choose the crucial relaxation parameters, reducing practical objectivity.  
- Real-world experiment needs more detail on why the standard instrument (GRP) failed validation and why the chosen alternative was selected.  
- The proposed falsification test (95% rejection heuristic) is pragmatic but lacks discussion of its properties and sensitivity to proposal count M.  
- Conclusion: excellent, novel contribution with mostly expositional/practical limitations; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
