- Summary: Introduces Witness Protection Program (WPP) to bound ACE by connecting constraint-based causal discovery and partial identification using Entner-style witnesses treated as approximately valid instruments.  
- Summary: Allows bounded violations of IV assumptions via user-specified relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) and derives ACE bounds using LP methods within a Bayesian framework.  
- Summary: Presents a fast back-substitution approximation to avoid solving full LPs in Monte Carlo, and demonstrates robustness on synthetic data and informativeness on an influenza vaccination dataset.

- Strength: Highly original synthesis linking causal discovery and partial identification, offering a principled continuum between trusting and rejecting adjustment sets.  
- Strength: Strong technical development—extension of LP bounding to relaxation parameters, Bayesian uncertainty propagation, and a practical computational speed-up.  
- Strength: Clear, well-written exposition with helpful intuition and logical progression from witnesses to relaxed IVs.

- Weakness: Provides little practical guidance for choosing relaxation parameters, limiting real-world applicability and interpretability.  
- Weakness: Two-stage procedure (select (W,Z) then relax) is potentially suboptimal; model selection could integrate bounded violations or combine multiple witnesses.  
- Weakness: The proposed falsification test (reject if >95% posterior samples rejected) is ad-hoc and lacks discussion of statistical properties or formal goodness-of-fit alternatives.  
- Weakness: Search for witness and admissible set is combinatorial and may not scale; the severity of this limitation for moderate covariate sets is not fully explored.

- Overall: Excellent, impactful paper with solid technical and conceptual contributions, though selection of relaxation parameters requires further work.  
- For acceptance: Original synthesis, addresses faithfulness violations, technically sound with computational innovation, high applied impact potential.  
- Against acceptance: Insufficient guidance on choosing relaxation parameters and somewhat ad-hoc search/falsification steps.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
