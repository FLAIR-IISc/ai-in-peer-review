- Introduces the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP): a relaxation of faithfulness to allow bounded local independence violations and bound the Average Causal Effect (ACE) using conditional independencies.
- Uses linear programming to compute ACE bounds and augments this with Bayesian inference to account for parameter uncertainty.
- Derives algebraic bounds and a back-substitution algorithm to improve computational efficiency and scalability.
- Novelty: offers a middle ground between faithfulness-based point estimators and instrumental-variable bounds, filling a gap in causal inference.
- Technical rigor: solid theoretical grounding and clear extensions of prior work (Entner et al., Dawid); thoughtful integration of LP and Bayesian methods.
- Practical relevance: applicable to real-world observational studies, demonstrated in an influenza vaccination case study.
- Transparency and reproducibility: authors provide R code and detailed supplementary material.
- Clarity concern: paper is dense and assumes strong familiarity with causal inference (do-operator, faithfulness), limiting accessibility.
- Parameter sensitivity: choices for relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) are not fully justified, impacting interpretability and adoption.
- Limited experimental scope: synthetic and real-world tests are informative but need expansion to diverse and high-dimensional datasets.
- Remaining reliance on faithfulness: despite relaxation, method still depends on conditional independencies, reducing applicability when faithfulness is strongly violated.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, well-supported contribution that balances theoretical rigor and practical utility; computational innovations enhance usability.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity/accessibility issues, parameter sensitivity, and limited generalizability from experiments.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on improving exposition and expanding experimental validation.
