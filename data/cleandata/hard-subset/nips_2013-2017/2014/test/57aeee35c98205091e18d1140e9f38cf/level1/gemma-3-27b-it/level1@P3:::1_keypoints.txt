- Proposes the Witness Protection Program (WPP) to relax faithfulness and bound the ACE using linear programming, building on Entner et al. and IV literature.  
- Well-motivated problem statement with appropriate connections to Pearl’s do-calculus, instrumental variables, and existing work.  
- Key contribution: introduction of relaxation parameters to model faithfulness violations and derive algebraic bounds.  
- Presents a back-substitution algorithm intended to improve computational efficiency.  
- Provides initial experimental evidence with synthetic data and an influenza vaccination case study.  
- Major clarity issues: dense notation and equations (Sections 3.1 and 4) make the paper hard to follow.  
- The link between theory and Algorithm 1 is unclear; selection of relaxation parameters and the “search procedure” are unspecified, hurting reproducibility.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: synthetic experiments lack variety (degrees of violation, network structures) and the real-data application is underdeveloped.  
- The rationale for excluding GRP as an admissible witness and the lack of direct comparison to using GRP as an IV are insufficiently explained.  
- Missing direct comparisons to alternative approaches for unobserved confounding (e.g., Rosenbaum sensitivity analysis, latent-variable methods).  
- Computational complexity and scalability are not analyzed in depth; the “order of magnitude” improvement claim lacks concrete benchmarks.  
- Minor referencing errors (missing publication years) detract from presentation.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, solid theoretical foundation, potential for more robust causal bounds, promising initial results.  
- Rejection arguments/overall assessment: low clarity, limited empirical validation, insufficient comparisons and complexity analysis — reviewer recommends weak reject pending major revisions.
