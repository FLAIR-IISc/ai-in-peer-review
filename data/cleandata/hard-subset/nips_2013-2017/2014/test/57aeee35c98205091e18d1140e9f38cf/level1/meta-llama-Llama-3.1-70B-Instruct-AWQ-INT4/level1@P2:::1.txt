The paper "Causal Inference through a Witness Protection Program" presents a novel approach to estimating causal effects in the presence of confounding variables. This approach is used to obtain a posterior distribution over bounds on the average causal effect via a linear programming approach and Bayesian inference.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed methodology. The authors also provide a thorough review of the relevant literature and a detailed description of the background and related work.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a compromise between point estimators given by the faithfulness assumptions and bounds based on instrumental variables. The authors also provide a clear explanation of the limitations of their approach and the potential for future work.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One potential weakness is the reliance on a linear programming approach, which may not be computationally efficient for large datasets.
