- Introduces a novel method that uses observational conditional independencies to suggest "weak" paths in unknown causal graphs.  
- Relaxes the standard faithfulness assumption to allow for graded "path cancellations" that produce conditional independencies without excluding confounding paths.  
- Produces a posterior distribution over bounds on the average causal effect using linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Paper is well-written and explains methodology and relation to prior work clearly.  
- The "witness protection program" idea for estimating causal effects is clever and original.  
- Experiments demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Offers a middle ground between point estimators under faithfulness and bounds from instrumental variable methods.  
- Can provide more accurate estimates than traditional methods when faithfulness is violated.  
- Provides a good discussion of the limitations of the faithfulness assumption and the need for more robust methods.  
- Explains trade-offs between estimation accuracy and computational efficiency.  
- Does not provide a clear analysis of computational complexity; linear programming may be computationally expensive.  
- Lacks a detailed sensitivity analysis regarding choice of method parameters.  
- Overall contribution is significant; technical quality, clarity, originality, and significance rated highly (9/10).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include computational complexity details, parameter sensitivity analysis, and more experimental setup/results detail.
