- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a framework that bounds average causal effects while relaxing the faithfulness assumption.  
- Builds on Entner et al. by using witnesses to allow conditional independencies from path cancellations without ruling out confounding paths.  
- Uses linear programming and a Bayesian inference layer to generate bounds on ACE instead of point estimates.  
- Connects to prior work: faithfulness (Spirtes et al.), Entner et al., and IV/bounding literature (Balke & Pearl, Manski).  
- Theoretical development is rigorous with a clear parameterization and both numerical and algebraic bounding approaches.  
- Bayesian treatment of parameter uncertainty is well-motivated and addresses limits of faithfulness-based point estimates.  
- Proposes a computationally efficient back-substitution algorithm as an alternative to full linear programming.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: synthetic tests use one graph with 8 covariates and only one real-world influenza case study.  
- Computational complexity analysis is insufficient, raising concerns for larger datasets and scalability.  
- Some technical details (e.g., dual polytope constraints in Section 3) lack intuitive explanation for non-expert readers.  
- Supplementary material contains important details not fully elaborated in the main text.  
- Core contribution is original: using witnesses to relax faithfulness and produce ACE bounds is novel.  
- Significance is high given the prevalence of observational causal inference and fragility of faithfulness-based estimates.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, rigorous theory, practical implementation, Bayesian uncertainty handling, and efficient algorithm.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, weak complexity analysis, unclear technical exposition, and underexplored relaxation-parameter justification.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen experiments, clarify technical points, and analyze computational costs.
