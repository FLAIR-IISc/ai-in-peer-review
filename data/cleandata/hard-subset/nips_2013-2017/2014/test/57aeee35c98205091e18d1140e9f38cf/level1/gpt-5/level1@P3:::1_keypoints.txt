- Summary: Proposes WPP, which bounds ACE by treating witnesses as conditional instruments and relaxing faithfulness via parameters (w,x,y,β,β̄), using LP-based IV bounds averaged over Z and Bayesian constrained Dirichlet posteriors.

- Relation to prior work: Sits between faithfulness-based point ID (PC, Entner et al.) and worst-case IV (Balke–Pearl), connecting to sensitivity analysis and inequality constraints in graphical models.

- Novelty: Inverts Entner–Hoyer–Spirtes Rule 1 to produce IV-type bounds under bounded faithfulness violations; introduces algebraic bounds and a back-substitution tightening procedure.

- Strength: Integrates constraint-based discovery with IV bounding to produce informative yet robust bounds instead of fragile point estimates.

- Strength: Clear, interpretable relaxation parameterization that interpolates between chain assumptions and standard IV, allowing user-controlled robustness.

- Strength: Sound LP formulation over latent-variable polytopes and appropriate Bayesian treatment for sampling uncertainty.

- Strength: Algebraic bounds and back-substitution significantly speed computation while preserving much tightness.

- Strength: Experiments show stability relative to naive back-door and faithfulness methods, and real-data example has tighter bounds than Balke–Pearl IV.

- Weakness: Key relaxation parameters (w,x,y,β,β̄) are under-motivated in the main text and risk subjective choices without principled elicitation.

- Weakness: Assumptions framed in latent-parameter space are hard for practitioners to interpret and anchor in domain knowledge.

- Weakness: Method limited to binary variables; no clear extension provided for multi-valued or continuous treatments/outcomes.

- Weakness: Witness search depends on BDeu model selection and a simple falsification test, with potential multiple-testing and finite-sample instability issues.

- Weakness: Evaluation metric treats any interval covering truth as zero error and relegates interval width to supplement, masking width–coverage trade-offs.

- Weakness: Influenza example’s narrowed bounds depend on chosen relaxation parameters and lacks substantive sensitivity analysis in the main text.

- Quality: Technically sound LP construction and coherent relaxation constraints; Bayesian uncertainty propagation appropriate though computationally heavy without algebraic speedups.

- Clarity: High-level idea is clear but notation for latent/observable parameters is dense and relies on supplemental derivations; a compact figure and toy example would help.

- Originality: Strong — inversion of Rule 1 and explicit parameterization of bounded faithfulness combined with IV LP machinery appears novel.

- Significance: Potentially high, offering a practical middle path between brittle point ID and conservative worst-case bounds if extended and better parameter-elicited.

- Arguments for acceptance: Conceptually elegant bridge between discovery and IV bounding; produces tighter, robust bounds; includes nontrivial computational contributions.

- Arguments against acceptance: Heavy reliance on user-chosen parameters without principled elicitation, binary-only scope, limited experiments, and clarity issues due to supplemental dependence.

- Suggestions: Provide main-text guidance for setting/ interpreting relaxation parameters (map to Rosenbaum’s Γ, partial R^2), include sensitivity plots, and add coverage–width analyses.

- Suggestions: Add a toy example showing parameter effects, discuss extensions to non-binary data, and address multiple-testing/independence decision robustness.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — novel and useful contribution with solid methodology, but needs improved parameter elicitation, interpretability, and sensitivity analysis to increase impact.
