- Proposes the "Witness Protection Program" (WPP) to bound ACE from observational data by treating discovered conditional independencies as evidence of "weak" causal pathways rather than definitive structure.

- Combines causal discovery (Entner et al.) and IV-bounding methods (Balke & Pearl, Dawid, Ramsahai) into a unified framework.

- Re-purposes a witness variable W from Entner’s Rule 1 as an imperfect instrumental variable and uses LP to derive ACE bounds.

- Introduces user-defined relaxation parameters (w, y, x, β, β̄) to quantify allowed violations of standard IV assumptions, enabling principled sensitivity analysis.

- Embeds the LP bounding in a Bayesian procedure to account for sampling uncertainty and produce a posterior over ACE bounds.

- Develops symbolic algebraic bounds and a fast back-substitution algorithm to make computations tractable.

- Experiments: synthetic tests show WPP is robust compared to naive and faithfulness-based estimators, especially in hard cases or when assumptions are violated.

- Real-data application (influenza vaccine) demonstrates tighter, more informative bounds than standard IV while remaining more conservative than point estimates.

- Technical quality is high; mathematical formulation for relaxation parameters and polytope constraints appears correct and thorough.

- Originality: novel synthesis reframing faithfulness outputs for robust sensitivity analysis and moving from point identification to bounds.

- Significance: provides a practical middle way between strong-assumption point estimates and weak-assumption wide bounds, with potential for wide impact.

- Major weakness: paper lacks clear guidance on choosing the crucial relaxation parameters, limiting practical applicability and objectivity.

- Additional weakness: the proposed model falsification test (reject if >95% of rejection samples discarded) feels ad-hoc; a more principled Bayesian model-check would be preferable.

- Presentation and clarity are strong; method is well-motivated and organized, with helpful high-level algorithmic overview and pointers to technical supplement.
