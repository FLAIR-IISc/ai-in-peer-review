- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a method to bound average causal effect (ACE) under possible confounding by relaxing faithfulness.
- Relaxes faithfulness to allow partial path cancellations that yield conditional independencies without excluding confounding paths.
- Provides posterior distributions over ACE bounds using linear programming combined with Bayesian inference instead of point estimates.
- Builds on Entner et al. and Dawid/Ramsahai, extending them with a flexible relaxation framework and symbolic algebraic bounds.
- Uses a clear parameterization of the joint distribution and derives bounds via linear programming with attention to assumption–bound trade-offs.
- Employs a Bayesian framework with constrained Dirichlet priors to handle uncertainty and avoid sensitivity from priors on unidentifiable latent variables.
- Experiments on synthetic and influenza vaccination data show robustness and more informative bounds than standard IV methods, despite conservatism.
- Authors acknowledge the conservative (wider) nature of bounds compared to point estimates but argue for greater reliability when faithfulness may fail.
- Supplementary material provides additional theoretical details and algorithmic implementations, enhancing reproducibility.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains core concepts (witness variables, admissible sets) clearly for the target audience.
- Mathematical notation and algorithm descriptions (e.g., Algorithm 1) are consistent, precise, and accessible to conference readers.
- Suggests a minor clarity improvement: add a brief glossary of causal inference terms for less familiar readers.
- Novel conceptual contribution: treating witness variables as protected against faithfulness violations rather than discarding them.
- Technical innovations include symbolic algebraic bounds and a back-substitution algorithm for computational efficiency building on Ramsahai.
- Significant practical impact: offers a middle ground between unreliable point estimates and overly conservative bounds, applicable to real-world data.
- Strengths: realistic faithfulness relaxation, theoretical rigor with computational improvements, Bayesian uncertainty handling, and empirical validation.
- Weaknesses: potential computational cost and scalability limits; choice of relaxation parameters is somewhat ad hoc and needs more guidance.
- Weaknesses (cont.): limited discussion on performance when multiple witness–admissible set pairs exist beyond selecting the top-scoring pair.
- Pro acceptance: solid theoretical and practical contribution with clear advantages when faithfulness may be violated and strong experimental support.
- Con acceptance: computational complexity and arbitrary-seeming relaxation parameter choices temper applicability, though they are acknowledged and left for future work.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, technically sound and practically significant — recommended for acceptance.
