- Presents a novel method ("Witness Protection Program") that relaxes faithfulness to allow path cancellations and estimates causal-effect bounds via LP and Bayesian inference.  
- Well-written with clear methodological explanation and thorough literature/background review.  
- Main contribution: a more robust estimator against faithfulness violations producing a posterior over ATE bounds.  
- Provides a compromise between faithfulness-based point estimates and instrumental-variable-style bounds.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness through experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Strength: clear exposition of limitations and directions for future work.  
- Weakness: reliance on linear programming may be computationally inefficient for large datasets.  
- Alternative algebraic-bounds approach is provided but may be less practical/effective.  
- Weakness: assumes modest dimensionality of the admissible set, with limited discussion of high-dimensional cases.

- Clarity score: 9/10 — very clear and well-presented.  
- Originality score: 9/10 — novel relaxation of faithfulness.  
- Significance score: 8.5/10 — meaningful contribution but not a paradigm shift.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, empirical validation, clarity of presentation.
