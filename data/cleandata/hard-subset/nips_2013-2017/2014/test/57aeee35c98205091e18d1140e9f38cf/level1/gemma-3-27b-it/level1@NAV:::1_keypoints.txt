- Proposes a novel method (Witness Protection Program) to bound average causal effects by relaxing faithfulness and identifying “witness” variables, using linear programming and Bayesian inference.  
- Builds on Pearl’s do-calculus, instrumental-variable theory, and causal discovery literature, addressing instability of point estimates under strict faithfulness.  
- Strength: tackles unmeasured confounding with a mathematically grounded, innovative relaxation of faithfulness.  
- Strength: combines linear programming, Bayesian methods, and algorithmic optimizations for practical implementation.  
- Strength: includes synthetic and real-world experiments (influenza vaccination dataset) and thorough literature referencing.  
- Weakness: presentation clarity suffers—dense notation and unclear connections between relaxations, back-substitution, and Bayesian steps.  
- Weakness: computational complexity and scalability remain a concern despite introduced relaxations.  
- Weakness: experiments lack sensitivity analysis of relaxation parameters and rigorous comparisons to alternative bounding methods.  
- Weakness: theoretical implications of the relaxed faithfulness assumption need deeper development.  
- For acceptance: technically novel, well-motivated, and addresses a significant practical challenge.  
- Against acceptance: complexity and limited empirical validation may limit immediate impact; presentation needs improvement.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on clarity, more detailed experimental analysis, and expanded theoretical discussion.
