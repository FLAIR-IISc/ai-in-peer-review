- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP) that relaxes the faithfulness assumption to allow for "path cancellations" while still estimating causal effects.
- Uses linear programming and Bayesian inference to produce a posterior distribution over bounds on the average causal effect (ACE).
- WPP is presented as a significant, more robust and flexible tool for causal inference under confounding.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains methodology and assumptions.
- Provides thorough discussion of related work and explicit acknowledgement of limitations.
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets are promising and demonstrate WPP’s effectiveness.
- Relaxing faithfulness allows WPP to capture more complex relationships and confounding in real-world applications.
- Bayesian component helps handle uncertainty and potential non-linear relationships.
- Major limitation: WPP is computationally intensive and may not scale to large datasets.
- Authors propose algebraic bounds for efficiency, but further work is needed to make WPP scalable.
- Another limitation: results are sensitive to the choice of relaxation parameters and require a more systematic selection method.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and original with high potential impact; scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, significant contribution, thorough discussion, and promising empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: computational complexity and sensitivity to relaxation-parameter choice.
