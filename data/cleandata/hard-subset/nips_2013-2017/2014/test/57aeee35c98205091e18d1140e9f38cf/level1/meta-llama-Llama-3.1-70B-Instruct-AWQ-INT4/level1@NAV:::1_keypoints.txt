- Introduces the Witness Protection Program (WPP), a novel method for estimating causal effects under confounding by using observational conditional independencies to suggest "weak" graph paths.

- Relaxes the classical faithfulness assumption to permit "path cancellations" that create conditional independencies without excluding confounding paths.

- Employs linear programming and Bayesian inference to derive bounds on the average causal effect.

- Presents a clear, step-by-step WPP algorithm and is overall well-written.

- Provides a thorough related-work discussion and argues advantages over existing methods.

- Includes empirical results that demonstrate the method's effectiveness.

- Method complexity may make it hard for non-experts to understand and implement.

- Relies on the assumption of modest dimensionality of covariates Z, which may not hold in many applications.

- Uses a uniform prior on the independence hypothesis, which may be suboptimal or uninformative in some settings.

- Lacks a clear analysis of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.

- Reviewer recommends acceptance, contingent on minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
