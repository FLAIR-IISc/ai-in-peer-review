- Proposes a random-forest method for zero-shot learning that incorporates attribute classifier uncertainty by using validation-measured performance (TPR/FPR) in information gain during tree learning.
- Empirical and quantitative results support the approach well.
- Lacks qualitative analysis showing which attributes and categories benefit from the method.
- Unclear how hyperparameters were chosen (e.g., rationale for the 80/20 train-validation split).
- Paper is well-written and easy to read.
- Novelty: explicitly models attribute reliability (as opposed to attribute strength), which is a less-explored angle despite related implicit approaches.
- Significance: idea of using validation-measured unreliability to improve zero-shot learning is interesting and relevant, though the random-forest component itself is not novel.
