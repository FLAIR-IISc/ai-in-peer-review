- Goal: Bridge theory and practice of attribute-based zero-shot learning by addressing difficulty of learning attribute detectors.
- Method: Train random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors.
- Reliability handling: Account for attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree during training.
- Extension: Method can be extended to few-shot learning by incorporating a few training examples of test categories.
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on three standard attribute datasets across zero- and few-shot settings.
- Evaluation: Strong experimental protocol with ablation studies, artificial noise tests, multiple settings, and literature comparisons.
- Quality: Model is elegant, theoretically sound, and well-executed.
- Clarity: Paper is well written overall, though Section 3.2.1 has a confusing explanation about threshold t that is clarified later.
- Originality: Nice and appropriate application of a random forest framework to the problem.
- Significance: Addresses a fundamental problem in zero-shot learning and is considered publication-worthy.
