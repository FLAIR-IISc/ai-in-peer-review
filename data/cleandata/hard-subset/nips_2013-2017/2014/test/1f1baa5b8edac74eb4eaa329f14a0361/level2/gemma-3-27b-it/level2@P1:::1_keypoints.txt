- Addresses zero-shot learning by explicitly modeling unreliable attribute predictions.
- Proposes a random forest method using ROC statistics to select discriminative and predictable splits.
- Extends the approach to few-shot learning and to handle uncertain class-attribute associations.
- Provides a clear, well-motivated, and novel contribution that challenges treating attribute predictions as ground truth.
- Related work section is comprehensive and correctly positions the gap in prior methods.
- Technical approach is sound; ROC-based splitting and signature-uncertainty extensions are logical.
- Empirical validation on AwA, aPY, and SUN shows consistent improvements over the DAP baseline.
- Ablation studies demonstrate the contributions of ROC propagation and signature uncertainty components.
- Paper is clearly written with consistent notation and informative figures/tables.
- Practical relevance: useful for scenarios with little or no labeled data for new categories.
- Computational complexity of the ROC-based criterion is not analyzed and may be high for large attribute vocabularies.
- Requires a separate attribute-labeled validation set to estimate ROC curves; impact of its size is not studied.
- Possible sensitivity to hyperparameters (tree depth, λ) with limited sensitivity analysis provided.
- Lacks thorough discussion of failure cases and attributes/classes where the method may perform poorly.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address complexity analysis, parameter sensitivity, and limitations/failure cases.
