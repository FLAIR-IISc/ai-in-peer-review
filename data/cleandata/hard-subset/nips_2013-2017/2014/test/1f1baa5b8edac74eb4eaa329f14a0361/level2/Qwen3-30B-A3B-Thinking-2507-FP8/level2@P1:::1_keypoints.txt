- Problem: Addresses attribute unreliability in zero-shot learning and its catastrophic effect on performance.  
- Method: Proposes a novel random forest that models attribute classifier unreliability during training using ROC statistics from validation data.  
- Technical novelties: Uses fractional propagation of signatures and models class-signature uncertainty; includes a natural few-shot extension.  
- Empirical strength: Thorough experiments on AwA, aPY, SUN with 20 trials and comparisons to strong baselines (DAP, SIGNATURE RF).  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art on AwA, generalizes to discovered attributes, and shows significant gains from modeling unreliability (e.g., +10.2% on “giant panda”).  
- Ablations: Clear ablation studies isolate the impact of ROC-based propagation versus other components.  
- Clarity: Well-written paper with clear formulation, good supplemental material, and a solid connection to fractional tuples.  
- Weakness: Depends on attribute validation data (20% of labeled images); unclear scalability when such data are scarce or unavailable.  
- Weakness: Tree depth choices set by cross-validation without sensitivity analysis or deeper justification.  
- Weakness: Few-shot evaluation does not examine extreme low-sample regimes (e.g., 1–5 images).  
- Significance: High originality and practical impact—retains human-describable attributes while making zero-shot more robust and broadly applicable.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid, well-validated contribution with reasonable limitations.
