- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning approach that explicitly models attribute prediction unreliability.  
- Introduces a random forest algorithm that uses per-attribute error statistics to pick discriminative and predictable decision nodes.  
- Evaluated on three large datasets and reports significant improvements over existing state-of-the-art methods.  
- Main claims: effectively models attribute unreliability and achieves state-of-the-art zero-shot object and scene recognition.  
- Experimental results strongly support the claims with extensive comparisons to prior work.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the method.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive and shows good literature understanding.  
- Strength: novel solution addressing a key limitation of current zero-shot methods.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating notable performance gains.  
- Strength: clear, easy-to-follow presentation.  
- Weakness: requires substantial attribute-labeled data, which may be unavailable in some settings.  
- Weakness: potential scalability concerns for very large datasets or complex attribute spaces.  
- Overall: significant contribution, recommended for acceptance to NIPS.
