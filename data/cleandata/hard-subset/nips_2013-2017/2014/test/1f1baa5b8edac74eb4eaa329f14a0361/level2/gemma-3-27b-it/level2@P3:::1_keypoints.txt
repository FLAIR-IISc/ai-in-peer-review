- Tackles zero-shot learning by modeling unreliable attribute predictions with a random forest that incorporates attribute error tendencies.  
- Uses ROC curves to account for attribute unreliability and extends the approach to few-shot learning and unreliable class-attribute associations.  
- Evaluated on three datasets, showing consistent improvements over baselines (e.g., DAP) and through ablation studies.  
- Well-motivated: addresses a practical limitation of zero-shot learning—unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Novel contribution: explicit modeling of attribute unreliability via ROC curves and signature uncertainty.  
- Thorough experimental setup with clear tables/figures and comparisons to established baselines.  
- Strengths: practical focus, convincing results, comprehensive related-work positioning, and clear presentation.  
- Lacks deeper analysis of why the method works (e.g., which attributes benefit most and how trees use them).  
- Limited justification/sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (tree depth, λ).  
- Limited discussion of computational cost and scalability, including overhead of computing/storing ROC curves.  
- Needs stronger connection to prior work on handling missing/uncertain data in decision trees to highlight unique contributions.  
- Table clarification required: the meaning of “OURS+TRUE ROC” is unclear and should be explained.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor weaknesses that can be addressed; merits acceptance.
