- Paper proposes a novel random-forest framework for zero-shot learning that models attribute prediction unreliability using attribute ROC curves and is extended to few-shot and unreliable attribute descriptions.  
- Novelty: intelligently selects discriminative and reliable decision nodes via attribute ROC, addressing a major ZSL limitation.  
- Experimental rigor: evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN with controlled noise experiments, showing generalizability and robustness.  
- Practical utility: applicable to real-world issues like long-tailed distributions and emerging categories, working in both zero-shot and few-shot settings.  
- Clarity: well-organized paper with detailed methodology, pseudocode, illustrations, and thorough ablation studies.  
- Significance: substantial improvements over DAP and other baselines, especially under unreliable attribute conditions.  
- Weakness—scalability: unclear how the method scales to large attribute vocabularies or hundreds of unseen classes.  
- Weakness—inter-attribute correlations: does not explicitly model attribute correlations, which could improve robustness and is left for future work.  
- Weakness—few-shot benchmarking: few-shot extension is promising but not thoroughly compared against other few-shot methods.  
- Weakness—dependence on validation data: requires attribute-labeled validation data to estimate ROC curves, which may not always be available.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, citing novel, well-executed contributions and strong results.  
- Pro/Con summary: pros include innovation, strong results, practical relevance, and clarity; cons include limited scalability discussion, lack of attribute-correlation modeling, and reliance on labeled validation data.
