## Review of "Zero-Shot Recognition with Unreliable Attributes"

This paper addresses the critical issue of attribute prediction unreliability in zero-shot learning (ZSL). The authors correctly identify that the standard ZSL paradigm falters due to imperfect attribute classifiers and propose a novel random forest approach that explicitly accounts for these errors. The core idea – leveraging ROC statistics of attribute classifiers during training to build more robust discriminative models – is well-motivated and potentially impactful. 

The paper is clearly written and logically organized. The introduction effectively frames the problem and motivates the proposed solution. The related work section is comprehensive, accurately positioning the contribution within the existing literature on ZSL and attribute-based learning. The proposed method is explained in detail, with a clear description of the modifications to the standard random forest algorithm. The extension to the few-shot setting is a valuable addition, demonstrating the versatility of the approach.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, utilizing three datasets (AwA, aPY, SUN) and comparing against several baselines, including the widely used DAP method. The controlled noise experiments effectively demonstrate the benefit of accounting for attribute unreliability. The results consistently show improvements over existing methods, particularly on datasets with less reliable attribute classifiers. The ablation studies help isolate the contributions of different components of the proposed method.

However, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the limitations. While the authors acknowledge the difficulty in modeling inter-attribute correlations, a more detailed explanation of why this remains a challenge would be helpful. Additionally, the choice of validation data size (20% of attribute-labeled images) feels somewhat arbitrary and could be justified with a sensitivity analysis. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant limitation of existing ZSL methods.
*   Novel and well-motivated approach leveraging ROC statistics.
*   Strong experimental results on multiple datasets.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of limitations and potential future work beyond inter-attribute correlations.
*   The choice of validation data size could be better justified.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of zero-shot learning. The proposed method is well-designed, thoroughly evaluated, and addresses a critical issue that has hindered the practical application of ZSL. Despite minor limitations, the potential impact of this work is significant. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
