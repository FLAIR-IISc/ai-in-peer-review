- Addresses unreliability of attribute classifiers in attribute-based zero-shot learning and its practical impact.  
- Proposes a novel random forest method that trains on class attribute signatures and explicitly models attribute classifier errors.  
- Uses fractional propagation of class signatures down trees based on attribute ROC statistics estimated on a validation set.  
- Learns splits that are discriminative for ideal signatures and robust to expected prediction errors at test time.  
- Extends the framework to handle uncertainty in class-attribute signatures and to few-shot learning.  
- Introduces a modified information gain (IGzero) and recursive propagation of validation data (DV(n)) for node-specific ROC estimation.  
- Demonstrates robustness to varying attribute noise and preference for reliable attributes in controlled experiments.  
- Evaluates on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets and shows substantial improvements over strong baselines including DAP.  
- Ablation study isolates contributions of model components and validates design choices.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on AwA with both named and discovered attributes.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear intuition, technical exposition, and supportive figures/tables.  
- Weakness: trains one-vs-all RFs per unseen class, raising potential scalability concerns for many unseen classes.  
- Weakness: relies on ROC statistics from a held-out validation set; sensitivity to validation size/composition is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: limited explanation in main text on how the method was generalized to continuous/discovered attribute strengths.  
- Overall conclusion: technically sound, practically significant, minor weaknesses, strong recommendation for acceptance.
