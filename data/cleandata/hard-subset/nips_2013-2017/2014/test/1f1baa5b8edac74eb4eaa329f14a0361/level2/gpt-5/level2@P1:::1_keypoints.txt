- Proposes a random-forest framework that models attribute unreliability during training of unseen-class classifiers via ROC-aware fractional propagation.  
- Represents unseen classes as attribute signatures and trains one-vs-all trees using signatures as exemplars.  
- Selects splits by propagating fractional counts based on attribute classifiers’ ROC statistics from held-out validation data.  
- Ensures trees prefer attributes and thresholds that remain discriminative given expected TP/FP rates at test time.  
- Introduces handling for uncertainty in class-attribute associations (signature uncertainty).  
- Adds a few-shot extension combining signature-based information gain with gains from a small set of class-labeled images.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline on AwA, aPY, and SUN.  
- Uses controlled-noise studies and ablations to clarify the advantages and failure modes.  
- Novelty: trains for test-time unreliability by integrating ROC behavior into tree building for zero-shot learning.  
- Connects to decision-tree methods for uncertain/missing features but adapts fractional propagation to signature-trained trees.  
- Strength: principled ROC-aware approach that moves beyond assuming perfect attribute predictions.  
- Strength: trees favor discriminative, reliable attributes and discard unlearnable splits (TPR≈FPR).  
- Strength: careful experimental design, practical implementation details, few extra hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: assumes ROC statistics transfer from seen to unseen classes—domain shift may break this.  
- Weakness: node data sparsity fallback (global DV) — sensitivity to the minimum-count threshold (e.g., 10) is unclear.  
- Weakness: univariate splits ignore attribute dependencies; correlated attributes can mislead local ROC estimates.  
- Weakness: training one forest per unseen class scales linearly with K, raising scalability concerns for large K.  
- Weakness: few-shot comparisons lack broader baselines that blend semantics and data (e.g., embedding or metric-learning methods).  
- Weakness: SUN unseen-class splits are random; fixed community splits would aid comparability.  
- Weakness: details and isolated ablation of signature-uncertainty mechanism are deferred to the supplement.  
- Quality: methodology is technically sound, information-gain derivations convincing, and experiments support claims.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, though main text could include more on signature uncertainty and complexity.  
- Significance: meaningful practical improvements for zero/few-shot learning and likely adoption by attribute-based researchers.  
- Suggestions: analyze DV size/composition sensitivity, evaluate calibration/covariate-shift corrections, report computational cost, study multivariate splits, and add more few-shot baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, timely contribution; recommend acceptance after minor clarifications and sensitivity analyses.
