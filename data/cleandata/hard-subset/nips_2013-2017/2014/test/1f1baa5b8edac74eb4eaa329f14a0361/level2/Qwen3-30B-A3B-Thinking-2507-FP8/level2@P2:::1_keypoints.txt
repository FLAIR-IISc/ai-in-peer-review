- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning method that explicitly models attribute prediction unreliability.  
- Introduces a random forest framework that uses attribute classifiers' ROC curves to select discriminative decision nodes.  
- Core innovation: fractional propagation of class signatures through trees according to attribute error tendencies.  
- Extends the approach to handle signature uncertainty and few-shot learning.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over strong baselines (DAP, SIGNATURE RF) on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets.  
- Controlled noise experiments show robustness to varying per-attribute noise levels.  
- Ablation studies isolate and validate the contributions of ROC-based propagation and signature uncertainty modeling.  
- Paper is well-written, technically clear, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Related work is properly contextualized and relevant prior papers are cited.  
- Minor concern: paper lacks analysis on why some attributes are inherently more reliable than others.  
- Minor concern: reliance on validation data for ROC estimation may be problematic when validation data is scarce.  
- Main weakness: does not fully explore how attribute reliability relates to class discriminability or whether gains come from filtering less discriminative attributes.  
- Significance: strong practical impact for zero-shot learning, generalizable beyond visual attributes, with state-of-the-art results on discovered attributes.  
- Recommendation: accept.
