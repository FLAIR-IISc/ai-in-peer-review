- Addresses unreliability of attribute classifiers in attribute-based zero-shot learning (ZSL).
- Proposes a random forest trained on class attribute signatures instead of images.
- Introduces fractional propagation using attribute classifier ROC stats to split signatures probabilistically.
- Trains trees to be robust to expected test-time attribute prediction errors.
- Extends the method to handle uncertainty in class-attribute signatures and a few-shot setting.
- Uses local ROC estimates by propagating validation data down the tree to capture attribute dependencies.
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets, showing significant gains over DAP.
- Controlled noise experiments validate the core hypothesis about modeling attribute-specific error rates.
- Ablation studies and an upper-bound experiment (OURS+TRUE ROC) isolate component contributions and indicate room for improvement.
- Technically sound formulation, clear motivation, and principled incorporation of classifier error statistics.
- Paper is very well-written and organized, with clear equations, figures, and implementation detail.
- Minor clarity fix: state the source/role of validation data (DV) earlier in the methodology.
- Original application of random forests and fractional propagation to ZSL; a creative adaptation of missing-data ideas.
- Significant impact: could revive attribute-based ZSL and influence methods that train for robustness to detector error profiles.
- Limitations: one-vs-all forest may not scale to many unseen classes.
- Limitation: method depends on quality and representativeness of validation data for ROC estimation.
- Limitation/future work: current single-attribute splits may not fully capture attribute correlations; joint tests could help.
