- Addresses the practical problem of attribute unreliability in zero-shot learning by explicitly modeling attribute classifier errors.  
- Proposes a random forest method that uses ROC curves to choose discriminative, predictable decision nodes.  
- Extends the approach to few-shot learning via a λ-weighted incorporation of limited labeled data.  
- Also handles unreliable class-attribute associations within the proposed framework.  
- Well-motivated: identifies attribute prediction as a key bottleneck for real-world ZSL.  
- Technical contributions are detailed, including specific modifications to random forest training to account for attribute uncertainty.  
- Experimental evaluation on AwA, aPY, and SUN shows consistent improvements over strong baselines like DAP and signature-based random forests.  
- Ablation studies isolate the benefits of signature uncertainty modeling and ROC propagation, clarifying component impacts.  
- Demonstrates robustness to noisy attribute predictions, with meaningful gains even under noise.  
- Limitations: limited treatment of inter-attribute correlations and their modeling.  
- Limitations: parameter sensitivity (tree depth, (m,t) tests) analysis is insufficiently thorough.  
- Limitations: experiments are confined to existing datasets and lack a demonstration on a truly dynamically defined category.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with technical novelty and solid results; accept with minor revisions to address noted limitations.
