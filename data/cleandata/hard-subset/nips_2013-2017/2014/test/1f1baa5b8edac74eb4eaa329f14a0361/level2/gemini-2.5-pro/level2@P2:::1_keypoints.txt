- Addresses unreliability of attribute classifiers in attribute-based zero-shot learning and the failure of the standard two-stage predict-then-classify pipeline.  
- Proposes training random forests directly on class attribute signatures rather than image data to handle unseen classes.  
- Key innovation: modify split selection by using expected TP/FP rates from attribute ROC curves and fractionally propagate signatures according to classifier error statistics.  
- Estimates error statistics on a held-out attribute-labeled validation set and recursively propagates validation data to capture attribute dependencies.  
- Extends the method to model uncertainty in class signatures and to the few-shot setting by combining signature-based and example-based information gain.  
- Novel and significant shift: makes the ZSL model robust to known attribute predictor errors rather than only improving attribute classifiers; builds on DAP but explicitly integrates classifier performance into training.  
- Strong technical quality: elegant fractional propagation, well-motivated ROC-based information gain, and natural few-shot extension.  
- Thorough experiments and ablations: controlled noise study, substantial gains over DAP across datasets, oracle upper-bound, and tests on named/discovered attributes.  
- Clear writing and logical presentation from baseline to full model.  
- Weakness—scalability: one-vs-all random forests per unseen class may not scale to many classes; multi-class adaptation suggested.  
- Weakness—validation dependence: performance depends on DV providing representative ROC estimates; sensitivity to DV size/composition should be analyzed.  
- Weakness—missing detail: important components (e.g., signature uncertainty model) are in the supplement despite providing non-trivial gains and merit a concise main-paper summary.
