- Paper proposes training random-forest zero-shot classifiers on class-level attribute signatures while explicitly modeling attribute unreliability via per-attribute ROC statistics estimated from held-out validation data.
- During tree construction, class signatures are fractionally propagated to children according to node-specific TPR/FPR at candidate thresholds, and information gain is computed with fractional counts.
- Method handles signature uncertainty and extends to few-shot learning by combining signature-based and example-based information gains with a trade-off parameter.
- Empirical results on AwA, aPY, and SUN show consistent improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline, with controlled-noise experiments and ablations.
- Strength: principled, intuitive formulation that directly addresses attribute unreliability with node-specific ROC modeling.
- Strength: simple, scalable implementation leveraging random forests and a sensible few-shot prior.
- Strength: thorough empirical validation including controlled-noise studies, ablations, and tests with discovered attributes.
- Strength: preserves semantic control over unseen classes while improving robustness to imperfect attributes.
- Weakness: unclear/possibly inconsistent data usage—paper states 20% reserved for ROC validation but also suggests pooling back to train attribute classifiers; needs exact counts and consistent baseline training sets.
- Weakness: unclear evaluation metric—must state whether reported accuracies are per-class or overall to enable fair comparison with prior work.
- Weakness: lacks detailed complexity/scalability analysis (time/memory, asymptotics in K, M, tree depth, number of trees) and discussion of practicality for large K.
- Weakness: key details on signature uncertainty are deferred to the supplement; include a concise main-text description (formula, intuition, hyperparameters).
- Optional improvement: analyze robustness to domain shift of ROC estimates and consider lightweight recalibration strategies.
- Optional improvement: consider limited oblique splits or pairwise tests to better capture correlated attributes.
- Optional improvement: add broader baselines (semantic-embedding methods) on aPY and SUN for fuller comparison.
- Optional improvement: provide interpretability examples (top-level splits and operating points) and sensitivity analyses (tree depth/number, candidate tests, node counts).
- Overall: sound, novel, and significant contribution addressing practical attribute unreliability with strong empirical support, contingent on clarifications about data usage, metrics, and scalability.
