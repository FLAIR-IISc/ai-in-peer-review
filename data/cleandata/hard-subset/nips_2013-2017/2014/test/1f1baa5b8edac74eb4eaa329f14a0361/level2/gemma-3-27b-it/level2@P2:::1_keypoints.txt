- Novelty: Introduces a novel random-forest approach that incorporates attribute classifier reliability into zero-shot learning, addressing unrealistic assumptions of existing methods.  
- Technical soundness: Method is well-defined with a reliability-aware information gain and sensible extensions for signature uncertainty and few-shot learning.  
- Empirical validation: Strong results on AwA, aPY, and SUN with controlled noise experiments and ablations showing consistent improvements over baselines like DAP.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains technical details and equations concisely.  
- Significance: Addresses an important limitation in zero-shot learning with practical extensions to few-shot scenarios, increasing real-world relevance.  

- Validation data dependency: Requires a separate validation set to estimate attribute ROC, which may be unavailable and whose impact is not fully studied.  
- Computational cost: Training the extended random forest is likely more expensive than simpler methods, but computational overhead is not quantified.  
- Limited exploration of signature uncertainty: Experiments on class-signature noise are limited; more study of noise models and modeling choices is needed.  
- Attribute discovery discussion brief: Results using discovered attributes are mentioned but lack detailed analysis of associated challenges and benefits.  

- For acceptance: Novel, technically sound approach with compelling empirical gains, clear writing, and practical significance.  
- Against acceptance: Reliance on validation data and unexplored computational overhead could limit applicability.  

- Overall: Strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to detail computational costs, analyze validation data effects, and expand discussion on discovered attributes.
