- Addresses zero-shot learning's reliance on perfect attribute predictions by explicitly modeling attribute classifier unreliability.
- Proposes a novel random forest that uses ROC statistics from validation data to model attribute errors during training.
- Propagates attribute signatures fractionally through trees according to estimated true/false positive rates to avoid uninformative splits.
- Extends the approach to handle signature uncertainty and few-shot learning via weighted information gain with labeled examples.
- Experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN show consistent improvements over DAP and signature-based random forests, with 5–10% gains on unreliable-attribute datasets.
- Few-shot extension yields clear benefits with very few labeled examples.
- Controlled noise experiments demonstrate robustness to attribute prediction errors.
- Method generalizes to both hand-designed and discovered attributes, matching state-of-the-art on AwA with discovered attributes.
- Strengths: well-motivated, sound ROC-based theoretical grounding, thorough experimental validation, and clear writing.
- Weakness: requires extra attribute-labeled validation data for ROC estimation, increasing data collection cost.
- Weakness: SUN results, while improved, remain lower than AwA and lack analysis of consistently lower attribute reliability on SUN.
- Weakness: "signature uncertainty" component is underdeveloped in the main text and pushed to supplementary material.
- Originality: novel adaptation of fractional propagation and node-specific ROC dependencies for zero-shot learning, extending prior work.
- Minor suggestions: discuss validation data size vs. performance, clarify handling of multi-modal class signatures, and compare to recent attribute-discovery work [30].
