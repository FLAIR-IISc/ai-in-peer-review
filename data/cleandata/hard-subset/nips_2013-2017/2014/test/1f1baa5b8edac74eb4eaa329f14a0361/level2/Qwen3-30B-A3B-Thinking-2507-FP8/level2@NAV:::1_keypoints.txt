- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning method that explicitly models unreliability of attribute predictions using attribute error statistics in a random-forest framework.  
- Extends the method to few-shot scenarios and to handle unreliable attribute descriptions.  
- Addresses a key limitation of prior work (e.g., DAP) that assumes perfect attribute predictions.  
- Uses ROC-based propagation of fractional training signatures through trees; approach is theoretically sound and well-motivated.  
- Empirical evaluation on AwA, aPY, and SUN shows significant gains over DAP and SIGNATURE-RF, with controlled-noise tests demonstrating robustness.  
- Experiments are rigorous (20 random trials), include ablation studies, are clearly described, and appear reproducible.  
- High practical significance for real-world zero-shot applications and rare/dynamically defined classes; few-shot extension increases applicability.  
- Minor limitations: requires attribute-labeled validation data for ROC estimation and needs more discussion of computational complexity.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with solid experiments and clear contribution; recommendation for acceptance.
