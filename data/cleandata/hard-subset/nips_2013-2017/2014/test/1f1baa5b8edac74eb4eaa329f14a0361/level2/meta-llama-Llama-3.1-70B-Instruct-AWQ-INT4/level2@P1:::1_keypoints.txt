- Proposes a novel random-forest method for zero-shot recognition that uses per-attribute error statistics to pick discriminative, predictable nodes.  
- Extends the approach to handle unreliable class-attribute associations and few-shot learning.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, outperforming state-of-the-art on three challenging datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with thorough explanation of methods and extensions.  
- Technically sound with a well-motivated approach and thorough analysis of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across scenarios and datasets.  
- Originality: tackles unreliable attribute predictions in zero-shot learning using a new algorithmic strategy.  
- Significance: method shows promise and could generalize to other domains using semantic mid-level concepts.  
- Limitation: approach depends on availability of attribute classifiers, which may not always exist.  
- Limitation: performance may suffer with highly correlated attributes or noisy attribute predictions.  
- Limitation: paper lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability.
