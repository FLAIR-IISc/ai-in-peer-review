- Tackles attribute prediction unreliability in zero-shot learning (ZSL), a key practical limitation.  
- Proposes a novel random forest approach that explicitly models attribute unreliability during training.  
- Leverages ROC curves of attribute classifiers to inform splitting criteria and prioritize reliable attributes.  
- Extends the method to handle unreliable class-attribute associations (signature uncertainty).  
- Further extends to a few-shot setting with an adjustable parameter λ.  
- Demonstrates performance improvements over DAP on three datasets (AwA, aPY, SUN).  
- Controlled noise experiments strongly support the benefit of modeling attribute unreliability.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive, citing ZSL, attribute discovery, and missing-data literature.  
- Strengths: technical soundness, novelty, clear empirical gains, and practical applicability.  
- Writing is clear, though the random forest technical details could use a concrete illustrative example.  
- Needs a more detailed ablation study to isolate contributions of signature uncertainty and ROC propagation.  
- Should investigate the impact of validation data size on performance.  
- Inter-attribute correlations are acknowledged but not addressed and represent future work.  
- Discussion of the few-shot parameter λ should be expanded.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, empirical improvements, well-motivated and well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ablation/analysis and limited investigation of validation size and attribute correlations.  
- Overall score/recommendation: 8/10 — strong paper, recommend acceptance.
