- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning approach that trains recognition models for new categories with minimal examples by correcting unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Leverages per-attribute error statistics to build more robust discriminative models for unseen classes.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise overview of related work and limitations of existing methods.  
- Methodology is clearly explained and well-motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, covering three large datasets and comparisons to multiple state-of-the-art methods.  
- Results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  
- Strength: Tackles the significant challenge of unreliable attribute predictions in zero-shot learning.  
- Strength: Novel and well-motivated approach with clear exposition.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental validation supporting claimed gains.  
- Weakness: Assumes the attribute vocabulary is sufficiently expressive to discriminate categories, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of potential applications and broader limitations.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, effective approach addressing a key problem with comprehensive experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: Reliance on expressive attributes, missing complexity analysis, and limited discussion of applications/limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong contribution to zero-shot learning and recommended for acceptance.
