- Problem: Attribute-based ZSL is brittle due to unreliable attribute classifiers used by methods like DAP.  
- Proposal: A random forest framework trained on clean class attribute signatures that is robust to attribute prediction errors.  
- Key technique: Use a validation set to estimate per-attribute ROC (TP/FP) and fractionally propagate class signatures down tree splits to model expected test-time noise.  
- Extensions: Framework naturally supports few-shot learning and modeling uncertainty in class signatures.  
- Empirical evidence: Strong gains over baselines on AwA, aPY, and SUN, plus controlled noise experiments that clarify why the method works.  
- Ablations: Studies isolate contributions of components, supporting technical soundness.  
- Writing: Manuscript is clear, well-motivated, and easy to follow.  
- Weakness — validation dependency: Performance depends on quality, size, and distribution of the validation set used to estimate ROC statistics.  
- Weakness — scalability: One-vs-all random forest training may not scale well to very large numbers of unseen classes.  
- Overall: High-quality, novel contribution that addresses a key ZSL bottleneck; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses.
