- Addresses attribute unreliability in zero-shot learning by modeling attribute prediction errors during random forest training to create more robust models for unseen classes.
- Method extends to few-shot learning and handles unreliable class-attribute associations.
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN, showing significant improvements over DAP and other baselines.
- Novel contribution: explicitly modeling attribute unreliability is an important step toward practical ZSL.
- Situates work relative to prior methods (which treat attribute predictions as ground truth) and contrasts with attribute-discovery approaches.
- Connects to decision-tree techniques for missing/uncertain data while highlighting ZSL-specific challenges.
- Technically sound: random forest framework, fractional propagation, ROC-based splitting, and use of validation data are well-motivated.
- Incorporation of validation data for error estimation is clever and effective.
- Empirical results are compelling, including controlled noise experiments and performance with discovered attributes.
- Paper is clear and well-written, with appropriate formalization via equations.
- Thorough experimental evaluation with ablation studies and fair baseline comparisons.
- Weakness: added computational overhead from ROC computations and validation-based estimation raises scalability concerns.
- Weakness: potential sensitivity to hyperparameters (tree depth, number of trees, λ) needs deeper analysis.
- Weakness: reliance on a separate validation dataset for estimating attribute error rates may limit applicability.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—unclear which specific attribute error types are best mitigated.
- For acceptance: addresses a critical limitation, is technically solid, empirically strong, clearly presented, and likely impactful.
- Against acceptance: concerns about computational complexity, parameter sensitivity, and dependence on validation data.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution; recommended for acceptance and likely to be highly cited.
- Suggested improvements: discuss computational complexity and scalability strategies.
- Suggested improvements: perform detailed parameter-sensitivity analysis, explore validation-free reliability estimates, add error analysis, and discuss limitations/future directions.
