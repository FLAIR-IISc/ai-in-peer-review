- Proposes a novel zero-shot recognition method that explicitly models unreliability of attribute predictions.  
- Develops a random forest algorithm that uses per-attribute error statistics to choose discriminative and predictable splits.  
- Generalizes the approach to handle unreliable class–attribute associations and extends it to few-shot learning.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and show effectiveness on three challenging datasets.  
- Method is more robust to attribute prediction errors than existing approaches.  
- Few-shot extension effectively leverages a small number of labeled images to improve performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation and helpful figures.  
- Technical treatment is strong and well-motivated, with convincing empirical results.  
- Approach is original and departs meaningfully from prior work on zero-shot learning.  
- Method has significant potential impact across object recognition, scene understanding, and image classification.  
- Limitation: assumes the attribute vocabulary is sufficiently expressive to discriminate categories.  
- Limitation: requires large amounts of attribute-labeled data and lacks deeper analysis of failure modes.
