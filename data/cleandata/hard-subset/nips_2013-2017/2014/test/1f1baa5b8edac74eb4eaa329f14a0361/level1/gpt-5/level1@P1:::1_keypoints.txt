- Addresses attribute unreliability in attribute-based ZSL by training random forests on class-attribute signatures with ROC-modeled attribute errors.  
- Trains trees by fractionally propagating class signatures according to node-specific TP/FP rates estimated from attribute-labeled validation data.  
- Incorporates signature uncertainty and extends to few-shot learning via a convex combination of signature-based and data-based information gain (λ prior).  
- Shows consistent improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline on AwA, aPY, and SUN, with controlled-noise experiments demonstrating robustness to heterogeneous attribute noise.  
- Novelty: integrates attribute error models into ZSL training objective and adapts decision-tree ideas to signature-vs-data ZSL setting.  
- Soundness: ROC-driven fractional propagation and node-specific ROC estimation are well-motivated and technically solid; few-shot extension is principled.  
- Empirical support: multi-dataset evaluation, >250 attributes, 20 trials, and ablation studies validate claims.  
- Limitation: depends on attribute-labeled validation data representative of unseen-class error modes; limited analysis of domain shift.  
- Limitation: ROC estimates can be data-sparse at deep nodes; current fallback to global DV is heuristic.  
- Limitation: scalability concerns due to one-vs-all forest training per unseen class; runtime and complexity not reported.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized, with intuitive notation and distinctions between training/test procedures.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: include full pseudocode, detail threshold sampling and (#m,#t) per node, and report computational complexity and runtimes.  
- Originality: non-trivial adaptation of error-aware splitting to ZSL and a novel combined signature/data IG for few-shot.  
- Significance: tackles a central practical ZSL problem and is applicable to discovered attributes and other semantic mid-levels.  
- Strengths: principled treatment of attribute unreliability, node-specific ROC estimation, solid empirical evaluation, and useful few-shot extension.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to other uncertainty-handling ZSL paradigms and implicit treatment of inter-attribute correlations.  
- Suggested improvements: use Bayesian smoothing/hierarchical shrinkage for ROC estimates, explore oblique/multivariate splits, expand comparisons to embedding-based ZSL, and analyze calibration/sensitivity.  
- Argument for acceptance: principled approach addressing a key ZSL limitation with consistent empirical gains and potential broader impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient comparisons to broader baselines, limited robustness analysis to domain shift, and missing scalability/runtime evaluation.  
- Recommendation: lean accept.
