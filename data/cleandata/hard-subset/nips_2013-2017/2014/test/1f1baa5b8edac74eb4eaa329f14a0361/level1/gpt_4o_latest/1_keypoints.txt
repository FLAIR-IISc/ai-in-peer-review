- Proposes a random forest-based zero-shot learning method that models attribute prediction unreliability using ROC statistics to choose robust splits.
- Leverages attribute classifier error tendencies to build discriminative models for unseen classes.
- Extends the approach to handle class signature uncertainty and few-shot learning scenarios.
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN, showing significant improvements over DAP and other baselines.
- Novel technical contribution: principled incorporation of ROC statistics into random forest training to address attribute unreliability.
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation, including controlled noise experiments, demonstrating robustness.
- Useful few-shot extension that bridges zero-shot and supervised learning.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with reproducible methodology and ablation studies.
- Significant practical impact by addressing a key limitation of ZSL and improving real-world applicability.
- Scalability concerns: unclear performance with larger attribute vocabularies or more complex datasets.
- Does not explicitly model inter-attribute correlations, which could limit performance on correlated attributes.
- Relies on validation data to estimate attribute error statistics, which may not always be available or representative.
- Limited few-shot analysis scope; more diverse scenarios (varying unseen classes/attributes) are needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical ZSL limitation, technically sound, and experimentally compelling.
- Arguments against acceptance: uncertain scalability, reliance on validation data, and missing inter-attribute modeling.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability and inter-attribute correlation concerns.
