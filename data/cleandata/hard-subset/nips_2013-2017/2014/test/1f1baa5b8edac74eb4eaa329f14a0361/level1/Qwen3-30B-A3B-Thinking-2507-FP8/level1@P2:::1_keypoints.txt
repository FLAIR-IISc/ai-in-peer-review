- Addresses the overlooked problem that attribute predictions are unreliable in zero-shot learning and proposes handling this explicitly.  
- Proposes a novel random forest method that uses per-attribute ROC statistics to account for attribute prediction error when selecting split nodes.  
- Method is well-motivated and tackles a fundamental practical limitation of prior zero-shot approaches.  
- Technically sound with detailed methodology and reproducible training details (random forest procedure, ROC usage, few-shot extension).  
- Extensive experiments with ablation studies show both modeled signature uncertainty and recursive propagation of validation data improve performance.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over state-of-the-art on AwA, aPY, and SUN, with up to 10.2% gains on particular classes.  
- Method is especially effective on datasets with less reliable attribute classifiers (aPY and SUN).  
- Experimental setup is thorough: standard datasets/baselines, results reported as mean and SE over 20 trials.  
- Novel contribution: explicitly incorporates attribute unreliability into training via ROC statistics, distinct from prior DAP-style work.  
- Extends naturally to few-shot learning, increasing practical utility.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on AwA when using discovered attributes, indicating strong empirical impact.  
- Strengths: identifies a critical gap, proposes a simple effective fix, shows multi-dataset gains, and provides solid ablation analysis.  
- Weaknesses: requires a separate validation set to estimate attribute error rates, which may be hard for truly novel classes.  
- Weaknesses: sensitivity to validation set size not thoroughly explored (they use 20% but optimality is unclear).  
- Overall recommendation: strong acceptance—technically sound, original, well-written, and significant.
