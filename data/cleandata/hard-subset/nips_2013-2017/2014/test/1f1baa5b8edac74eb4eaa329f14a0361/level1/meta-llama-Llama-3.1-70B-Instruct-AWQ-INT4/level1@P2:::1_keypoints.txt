- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning method using a random forest that accounts for attribute-prediction unreliability via ROC-aware node selection.  
- Extends the approach to handle unreliable class-attribute associations and few-shot learning scenarios.  
- Well-motivated problem: attribute unreliability is a real and important issue for zero-shot learning.  
- Clear and concise related work overview that highlights limitations of existing methods.  
- Methodology is well-explained with clear mathematical formulations.  
- Comprehensive experiments on three large datasets showing performance gains over state-of-the-art.  
- Controlled noise and real unreliable-attribute experiments demonstrate robustness and provide insights into method behavior.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and includes clear figures and tables.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach that directly addresses attribute unreliability.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation and promising results.  
- Weakness: assumes an expressive attribute vocabulary, which may not hold in all domains.  
- Weakness: relies on the quality of attribute classifiers; performance may degrade with poor classifiers.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale datasets.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution with potential real-world impact, recommendation to accept.
