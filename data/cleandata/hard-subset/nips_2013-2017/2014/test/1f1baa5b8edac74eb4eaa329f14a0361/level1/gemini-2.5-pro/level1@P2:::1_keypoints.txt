- Addresses unreliability of attribute classifiers in zero-shot learning and trains random forests on class attribute signatures rather than images.
- Key innovation: fractional propagation of signatures through tree splits using estimated attribute error rates (ROC) from a validation set to build robustness to attribute noise.
- Extends method to model uncertainty in class signatures and to few-shot learning by using attribute signatures as priors.
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, SUN and shows substantial, consistent improvements over the DAP baseline.
- High technical quality: method is sound, well-motivated, and supported by thorough experiments.
- Controlled noise experiment convincingly validates that gains increase as attribute predictions become less reliable.
- Ablation studies demonstrate the contributions of signature uncertainty and recursive ROC propagation.
- Honest evaluation practices, including an upper-bound experiment using test ROC, clarify dependence on validation quality.
- Exceptionally clear and well-written presentation with helpful examples and clear equations.
- Highly original application of fractional propagation and training on signatures using classifier error statistics.
- Extension to few-shot learning frames attribute signatures as useful descriptive priors.
- Significant contribution: addresses a core ZSL weakness, improves SOTA, and introduces a broadly applicable robustness principle.
- Main weakness: relies on validation-set ROC being representative of unseen-class errors, which may not hold across domain shifts.
- Practical limitation: one-vs-all training requires a separate forest per unseen class, raising scalability concerns.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor but meaningful limitations related to validation representativeness and scalability.
