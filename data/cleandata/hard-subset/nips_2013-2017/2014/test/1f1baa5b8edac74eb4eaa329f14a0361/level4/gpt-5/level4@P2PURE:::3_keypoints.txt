- Paper proposes an attribute-based zero-shot learning method using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and random forests on class–attribute signatures, with attribute reliability estimated from a validation set; also presents a few-shot extension.
- Writing and clarity are strong, with a solid related work summary.
- Novelty is moderate; claim of uniquely addressing unreliable attributes is overstated because prior work (e.g., DAP) also models attribute uncertainty.
- Proposed method differs mainly by using ROC-based calibration instead of Platt scaling and by implicitly modeling joint attribute error statistics via validation examples propagated down trees.
- Suggests reframing the contribution as improved modeling of attribute correlations rather than a fundamentally new treatment of attribute unreliability.
- Recommends citing related work such as Scheirer et al., “Multi-Attribute Spaces,” which may be relevant.
- Potential weakness: method may require a large validation set because recursive partitioning during tree growth reduces data for reliable probability estimates.
- Mitigation for limited validation data is heuristic and not thoroughly analyzed.
- Experiments are appropriate and clearly presented, but empirical improvements are modest and may not justify acceptance on quantitative gains alone.
- Few-shot extension is underdeveloped and less polished than the main method.
- Suggests an alternative few-shot approach: combine class–attribute priors with image-based attribute estimates rather than using a weighted combination of information gains (Eq. 6).
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well written, but central novelty and claims about handling unreliable attributes remain questionable.
