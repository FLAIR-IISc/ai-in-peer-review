- Proposes random-forest approach for attribute-based zero-shot learning with SVM-trained attribute classifiers and validation data to estimate attribute reliability per tree node.  
- Paper is clear, well organized, with thorough and readable related work coverage.  
- Novelty is moderate; claim of handling unreliable attributes as a new problem is overstated given prior methods like DAP.  
- Both this work and prior DAP-style methods use a two-step pattern: train attribute classifiers then learn a class-level estimator calibrated on validation data.  
- Main technical advances are a richer noise model: ROC-based calibration instead of sigmoidal Platt scaling and propagation of validation data to capture joint attribute-error structure.  
- Framing the contribution as improved modeling of attribute correlations is more accurate than claiming a fundamentally new paradigm.  
- Suggest adding citation to Scheirer et al., “Multi-Attribute Spaces: Calibration for Attribute Fusion and Similarity Search.”  
- Potential drawback: method may require a large validation set because it is partitioned down trees; sensitivity to validation/training size is not explored.  
- The fallback strategy for small validation sets (line 323) seems heuristic and is not analyzed in depth.  
- Experiments are sound and support claims but absolute gains are modest and may not alone justify acceptance.  
- Few-shot extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped and brief compared to the rest; alternative fusion mechanisms for combining priors with image-level estimates (instead of Eq. 6) might be more intuitive.
