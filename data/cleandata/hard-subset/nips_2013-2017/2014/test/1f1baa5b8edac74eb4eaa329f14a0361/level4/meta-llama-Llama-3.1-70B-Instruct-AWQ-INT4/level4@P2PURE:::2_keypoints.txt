- Proposes a random forest-based zero-shot learning method that models uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Measures attribute classifier uncertainty via validation-set metrics (e.g., TPR, FPR) and incorporates them into information gain during tree learning.  
- Allows category-attribute signatures to be pursued along multiple tree paths, accommodating noisy attribute predictions.  
- Empirically well-supported with quantitative results demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis on which attributes or categories benefit most from the approach.  
- Needs more justification and insight into parameter choices (e.g., the 80/20 train–validation split).  
- Clear and well-written presentation, making the work accessible.  
- Novel contribution in explicitly addressing "attribute reliability" rather than just "attribute strength," though the random forest method itself is not new.  
- The idea of modeling unreliability via validation performance is interesting and potentially impactful for attribute-based zero-shot learning.
