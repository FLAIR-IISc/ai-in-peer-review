- Proposes a novel random forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly models uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Measures attribute classifier performance on a held-out validation set and incorporates this uncertainty into information gain during tree learning.  
- Allows a category-attribute signature to be pursued along multiple tree paths, accommodating attribute prediction uncertainty.  
- Empirical and quantitative analyses strongly support the proposed ideas and demonstrate improved zero-shot performance.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., which attributes/categories benefit most) to deepen understanding of strengths and limitations.  
- Some hyperparameter choices (e.g., 80/20 training-validation split) are not well-justified or explored.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Novel focus on explicit modeling of attribute reliability, distinct from prior work on attribute strength.  
- Method is thought-provoking and has potential significance for the attributes and zero-shot learning community.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel uncertainty modeling, solid empirical support, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient qualitative analysis and unclear parameter selection.  
- Overall: valuable contribution with a strong case for acceptance.
