- Addresses gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning: attribute detectors are hard to learn in practice.  
- Proposes a random forest framework to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors.  
- Key innovation: training procedure that accounts for attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree during construction.  
- Extends the method to few-shot learning by incorporating a small number of labeled examples for test categories.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on several standard attribute datasets.  
- Quality: study is high-quality, with an elegant and theoretically sound model.  
- Experimental validation is thorough: extensive ablation studies, robustness tests with artificial noise, and direct literature comparisons.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow overall.  
- Minor clarity issue: ambiguity around the threshold t in Section 3.2.1 and how continuous attribute predictions are propagated down the tree; later clarified but should be introduced earlier.  
- Originality: novel application of random forests that incorporates attribute reliability into tree-building.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental, practical problem in zero-shot learning and makes a valuable contribution.  
- Recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance.
