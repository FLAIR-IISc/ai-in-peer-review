- Goal: Close the gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning, noting attribute classifiers can be as hard to learn as object classifiers.
- Method: Proposes a random-forest-based approach that predicts unseen classes from attribute vectors while explicitly modeling attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each tree.
- Few-shot extension: Shows how to incorporate a small number of labeled examples from test categories to extend the method to few-shot learning.
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on several standard attribute datasets (strong results on three datasets).
- Empirical rigor: Thorough experimental evaluation including ablations, artificial-noise tests, zero-shot and few-shot settings, and comprehensive comparisons to prior work.
- Quality: Model is elegant and theoretically well-grounded.
- Clarity issue: Section 3.2.1 is unclear about the threshold t and how novel test examples are propagated down the tree; later clarified but needs earlier explanation.
- Originality: Thoughtful and well-executed application of a random-forest framework to an important ZSL problem.
- Significance/recommendation: Tackles a fundamental problem, is technically sound and interesting, and deserves to be published.
