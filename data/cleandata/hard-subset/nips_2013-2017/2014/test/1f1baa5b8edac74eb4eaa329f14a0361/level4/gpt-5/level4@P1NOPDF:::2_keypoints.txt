- Proposal: Random-forest-based zero-shot learning method that explicitly models attribute classifier uncertainty using validation-measured performance (TPR/FPR).  
- Mechanism: Reliability estimates are integrated into the information gain computation so category–attribute signatures can propagate across multiple tree branches under uncertainty.  
- Goal: Designed to better handle noisy attribute predictions during learning.  
- Empirical results: Solid quantitative evaluation that supports the main claims and shows reported gains.  
- Missing qualitative analysis: Lacks examples showing which attribute types (global/local, visual/semantic) or categories benefit, and failure cases.  
- Hyperparameter concerns: Insufficient detail on parameter selection and sensitivity, including unjustified 80/20 train–validation split and lack of alternative-split experiments.  
- Ablation/sensitivity: No thorough ablation or sensitivity analysis to strengthen causal claims about design choices.  
- Clarity: Writing and motivation are clear; integration into forest training is coherently described.  
- Originality: Novel emphasis on explicit attribute reliability and its incorporation into split criteria, distinct from prior work on attribute strength.  
- Significance: Practical and intuitive idea likely to stimulate further work, though methodological novelty is incremental and would benefit from deeper analysis and limitation discussion.  
- Overall: Well-executed and clearly presented with compelling results; would be improved by qualitative examples and clearer parameter justification.
