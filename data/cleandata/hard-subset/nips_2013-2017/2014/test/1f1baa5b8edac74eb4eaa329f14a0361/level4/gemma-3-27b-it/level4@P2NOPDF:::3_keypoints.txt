- Introduces a random forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning, training attribute SVMs then a random forest on class-attribute signature vectors.  
- Estimates attribute reliability at each tree node using a validation set of images with attribute labels.  
- Presents an extension to the few-shot learning setting.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and provides a strong related-work summary.  
- Novelty is incremental rather than groundbreaking; central claims of fundamentally improving handling of unreliable attributes are overstated.  
- Prior methods like DAP already address attribute uncertainty; both approaches share the two-stage framework (attribute classifiers then class estimator).  
- Main differences are ROC-based reliability estimation vs. sigmoidal/probabilistic models, and implicit modeling of joint attribute-error statistics via validation-set propagation in trees.  
- Should acknowledge nuance and cite related calibration/multi-attribute work (e.g., Scheirer et al.).  
- Potential weakness: heavy reliance on a substantial validation set as trees subdivide data, which is under-addressed in the paper.  
- The proposed heuristic to mitigate small validation subsets (line 323) lacks sufficient justification.  
- Recommend experiments analyzing the impact of training/validation set size on performance.  
- Experiments are appropriate and generally support claims, but quantitative gains are modest and may not justify acceptance on empirical results alone.  
- Few-shot extension is underdeveloped and brief; the combined information-gain approach (Eq. 6) is less intuitive than directly integrating class-attribute priors with image-based attribute estimates.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-presented, but the contribution to handling unreliable attributes is more of a refined method than a fundamentally new approach.
