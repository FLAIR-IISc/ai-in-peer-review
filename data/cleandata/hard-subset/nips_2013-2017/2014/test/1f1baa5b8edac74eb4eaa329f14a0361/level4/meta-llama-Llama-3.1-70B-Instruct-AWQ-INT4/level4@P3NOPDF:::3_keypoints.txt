- Proposes attribute-based zero-shot learning using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and random forests on class-attribute signatures, with validation-set-based attribute reliability estimation; includes a few-shot extension.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and summarizes related work effectively.
- Originality is limited; the claim of uniquely handling unreliable attribute predictions is overstated.
- Direct Attribute Prediction (DAP) and prior work already address attribute uncertainty via a two-step process and validation-based reliability estimation.
- Main methodological novelty is using a random-forest model with ROC-based attribute noise modeling instead of Platt-scaling’s sigmoidal model.
- By propagating the validation set down trees, the approach implicitly models joint attribute error statistics and attribute correlations.
- Should cite related work on attribute fusion/calibration (e.g., Scheirer et al., "Multi-Attribute Spaces").
- Requires a large validation set because class-wise probability estimates are subdivided during tree propagation; this limitation is underemphasized and the proposed fix is heuristic.
- Experiments are appropriate and technically sound but show only modest quantitative gains that may not justify acceptance on empirical results alone.
- The few-shot learning extension (Section 3.3) is brief and less polished; a more intuitive probability-combination method might be preferable to the weighted information-gain approach.
- Overall conclusion: technically sensible and well-presented, but the central claim about novel handling of unreliable attributes is questionable.
