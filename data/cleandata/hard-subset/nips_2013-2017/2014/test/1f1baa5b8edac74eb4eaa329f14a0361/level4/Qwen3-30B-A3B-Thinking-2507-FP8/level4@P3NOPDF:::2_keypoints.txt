- Introduces a novel ZSL approach that integrates attribute classifier uncertainty into random forest training using validation-set performance metrics.  
- Core innovation: explicitly models attribute unreliability (TPR, FPR, etc.) during information gain computation to weight predictions by empirical reliability.  
- Well-motivated method with clear technical exposition and sufficient algorithmic detail.  
- Strong empirical results: consistent, statistically significant top-1/top-5 accuracy gains over baselines on standard ZSL benchmarks (AwA, CUB).  
- Effectively situates contribution in ZSL literature by distinguishing attribute reliability from prior focus on attribute strength.  
- Main weakness: lacks qualitative analysis identifying which specific attributes and categories benefit most from uncertainty modeling.  
- Secondary weakness: key hyperparameters (e.g., 80/20 train–validation split for uncertainty estimation) are unexplained and lack sensitivity analysis, raising reproducibility concerns.  
- Originality/significance: not transformative but a meaningful conceptual advance by formalizing attribute reliability as a first-class component of decision-making.  
- Practical and interpretable refinement to existing frameworks, particularly valuable where attribute classifiers have high variance on unseen classes.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add qualitative case studies, justify/tune hyperparameters, and clarify the distinction between attribute strength and reliability.
