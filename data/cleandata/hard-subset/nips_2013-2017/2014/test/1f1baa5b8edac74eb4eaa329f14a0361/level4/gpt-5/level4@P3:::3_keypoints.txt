- Core contribution: a random-forest zero-shot method that trains on class–attribute signatures while explicitly modeling attribute unreliability via node-specific ROC-based fractional propagation.  
- Training detail: per-node ROC (TPR/FPR) estimated from a validation set and used to probabilistically propagate class signatures down the tree so splits are robust to attribute predictor errors.  
- Extensions: mechanism to model uncertainty in class–attribute signatures and a few-shot extension that mixes signature-based and image-based information gain via a weighting parameter.  
- Experiments: evaluated on AwA, aPY, SUN with controlled noise studies, ablations (removing signature uncertainty and node-specific ROC), discovered-attribute tests, and few-shot experiments.  
- Results: consistently outperforms DAP and SIGNATURE-RF, with larger gains when attributes are less reliable and benefits in the few-shot regime.  
- Strength: clear problem motivation that attribute noise hampers zero-shot learning and a method directly addressing that issue.  
- Strength: methodologically sound and intuitive fractional propagation backed by per-node ROC estimates.  
- Strength: good experimental design including multiple datasets, controlled noise, ablations, and discovered-attribute evaluation.  
- Strength: few-shot extension is natural and shows expected empirical gains as labeled examples increase.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on a validation set — per-node ROC estimates use shrinking validation subsets and reversion to global stats is heuristic.  
- Weakness: few-shot mixing via information-gain interpolation lacks a clear probabilistic grounding or principled justification.  
- Weakness: limited baselines — missing comparisons to calibrated fusion methods (e.g., stacking/logistic fusion) that could isolate gains from tree structure vs. calibration.  
- Clarity issue: few-shot section is brief and needs more intuition for λ selection, computational details, and interaction of attribute/image evidence.  
- Clarity issue: missing complexity/runtime discussion for per-node ROC estimation, number of candidate splits, and training-time impact.  
- Originality: meaningful but incremental — extends prior attribute-based zero-shot work by integrating ROC operating points and node-conditioning into tree training.  
- Significance: practical, simple-to-implement improvement with consistent gains, especially when attribute reliability is low, but modest improvements in some settings.  
- Suggestion: temper claims that prior work ignores attribute unreliability and clarify the precise novelty of ROC-based, node-conditional estimation.  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to validation set size and the minimum-count threshold for using global ROC statistics.  
- Suggestion: provide runtime/complexity metrics and scalability discussion for M attributes and K unseen classes.  
- Suggestion: expand few-shot section with a probabilistic rationale for Eq. 6 or ablations comparing IG interpolation to probabilistic fusion baselines.  
- For acceptance: addresses a practical limitation, is technically sound, reproducible, and supported by solid empirical evidence and ablations.  
- Against acceptance: novelty is incremental, method depends on validation data with limited analysis of deep-node robustness, and few-shot extension is under-justified.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept — useful and well-executed contribution but would benefit from deeper analysis of validation dependence and stronger few-shot justification.
