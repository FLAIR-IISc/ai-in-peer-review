- Proposes a random forest-based zero-shot learning method that models uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Quantifies uncertainty by measuring attribute classifier performance on a held-out validation set using metrics like TPR and FPR.  
- Incorporates this performance information into information-gain calculations during random forest training to account for attribute prediction uncertainty along tree paths.  
- Empirical and quantitative results strongly support the proposed method.  
- Paper would benefit from additional qualitative analysis (which attributes/categories improve) and more justification of parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train–validation split).  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Novel contribution lies in explicitly addressing “attribute reliability” rather than the more-studied “attribute strength,” though related work may implicitly handle unreliability.  
- Modeling unreliability via validation performance is an interesting, useful idea for attribute-based zero-shot learning, even if the random forest approach itself isn’t highly novel.
