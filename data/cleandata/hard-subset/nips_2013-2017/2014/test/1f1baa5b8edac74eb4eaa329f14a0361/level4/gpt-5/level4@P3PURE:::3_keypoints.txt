- Paper proposes an attribute-based zero-shot method using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and random forests trained on class–attribute signatures, with a validation set to estimate attribute reliability; includes a few-shot extension.  
- Clarity: paper is very clear and well written with a good related-work summary.  
- Originality: novelty is limited; claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated.  
- Comparison to prior work: DAP and this method follow the same high-level recipe of calibrating attribute reliability with a validation set.  
- Distinct contributions: more flexible noise model via ROC-based estimates and modeling joint attribute-error statistics by propagating validation data down trees.  
- Suggestion: cite Scheirer et al., “Multi-Attribute Spaces,” as a potentially related reference.  
- Weakness: method may rely on a large validation set because validation data are split along tree paths, making deep-node reliability estimates data-hungry; the mitigation is heuristic.  
- Experimental gaps: need sensitivity analysis to training/validation set sizes to strengthen claims.  
- Quality: technical choices are sound and experiments are appropriate, but quantitative gains are modest.  
- Few-shot extension: presentation is brief and less polished; the weighted-sum information-gain mechanism is less intuitive than an explicit probabilistic integration of priors and image-based estimates.  
- Summary judgment: paper is solid but its main contribution is better framed as improved modeling of attribute correlations rather than a fundamentally new approach to attribute uncertainty.
