Paper proposes a random-forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning, training attribute SVMs and a forest on class-attribute signature vectors with node-wise attribute reliability estimated from a validation set.  
Also presents an extension to few-shot learning.

Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written with a strong related-work summary.  
Technical quality is good with sensible choices and clear descriptions.

Novelty is incremental; the claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated.  
Direct Attribute Prediction (DAP) and other existing methods already address attribute uncertainty similarly.

Main contribution is better framed as refined modeling of attribute correlations and reliability (ROC-based reliability vs. sigmoidal models) and implicitly modeling joint attribute-error statistics via validation-set propagation in trees.  
Related work such as Scheirer et al. (“Multi-Attribute Spaces…”) deserves more discussion.

A potential weakness is reliance on a large validation set for accurate probability estimates as data is split during tree propagation; this point is underexplored.  
The heuristic for addressing small validation sets (line 323) is insufficiently justified.

Experimental evaluation is appropriate and generally supports claims, but quantitative improvements are modest and may not justify acceptance on empirical gains alone.  
Further experiments varying training/validation set sizes would strengthen the paper.

Few-shot extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped and concise; the proposed weighted information-gain combination (Eq. 6) is less intuitive than combining class-attribute priors with image-derived estimates.  
Recommend deeper analysis of validation-size requirements and a clearer rationale or alternative for the few-shot method.
