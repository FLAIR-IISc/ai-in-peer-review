- Addresses attribute unreliability in zero-shot learning.  
- Proposes training random forests on unseen-class attribute signatures.  
- Explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability during tree training.  
- Uses attribute-labeled validation data to estimate error characteristics (e.g., ROC) for split selection.  
- Extends the framework to the few-shot learning setting.  
- Demonstrates significant gains and achieves state-of-the-art on three attribute-based datasets.  
- Method is elegant and theoretically well-motivated.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough across three standard benchmarks.  
- Validated with comprehensive ablation studies, controlled-noise experiments, and direct comparisons to leading methods.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with a minor initial confusion about threshold t that is later clarified.  
- Presents a novel adaptation of random forest training tailored to zero-shot learning.  
- Tackles a fundamental practical bottleneck, shows strong empirical results, and is likely to be influential.
