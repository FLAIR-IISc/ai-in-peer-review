- Proposal: A random-forest based zero-shot learning method that incorporates attribute classifier uncertainty by using attribute performance (TPR/FPR) on a held-out validation set in information gain computation.
- Quality: Empirically and quantitatively well-supported, but lacks qualitative analysis to show which attributes/categories benefit most.
- Quality (cont.): Parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train-validation split) are insufficiently justified and their impact is not explored.
- Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Originality: Novel in explicitly modeling attribute classifier unreliability, whereas prior work focused on attribute strength or handled unreliability implicitly.
- Significance: Interesting and valuable application to improve zero-shot learning, though it builds on existing random forest techniques and is not wholly new.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach to attribute uncertainty, strong empirical support, and a useful contribution to modeling unreliability.
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs additional qualitative analysis, better justification of parameter choices, and the core method extends rather than radically departs from existing techniques.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address qualitative analysis and parameter-justification concerns.
