- Proposes a random forest-based zero-shot learning method that explicitly incorporates attribute classifier uncertainty.  
- Quantifies uncertainty using validation-set performance metrics (TPR, FPR, etc.).  
- Integrates these performance metrics into the information-gain computation during tree learning to follow category-attribute signatures along multiple paths.  
- Empirical and quantitative analyses support the effectiveness of the approach.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis (no examples showing which attributes or categories are most impacted).  
- Key parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train–validation split) are not justified.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible.  
- Novelty lies in explicitly modeling attribute “reliability” via validation metrics, differing from prior work focused on attribute strength.  
- Significance: idea is interesting and useful for attribute-based zero-shot learning, though the random forest framework itself is not novel and methodological novelty is limited.
