- Paper proposes attribute-based zero-shot learning using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and a random-forest trained on class-attribute signature vectors, with validation-set-based attribute reliability estimates; also proposes a few-shot extension.  
- Clarity: very clear, well-written, and provides a good related-work summary.  
- Originality: novelty is limited; the claim of uniquely addressing unreliable attribute predictions is overstated.  
- Relation to prior work: method is similar to direct attribute prediction (DAP) and other two-stage approaches (train attributes, then train class estimator using validation-set reliability estimates).  
- Distinction: contribution is better characterized as a refined model of attribute noise—using ROC-based estimates and modeling joint attribute-error statistics via tree propagation—rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Missing citation: Scheirer et al., “Multi-Attribute Spaces…” may be a relevant related work to include.  
- Weakness: approach likely needs a large validation set for reliable probability estimates as data is split in trees; the paper glosses over this and relies on a heuristic fix.  
- Experiments: technical choices and experiments are sensible and generally supportive, but quantitative improvements are modest and may not justify acceptance on empirical gains alone.  
- Few-shot extension: underdeveloped and brief; alternative ways to combine class-attribute priors and image estimates might be more effective than the proposed weighted information-gain approach.  
- Overall: well-written with sound decisions, but the central novelty claim about handling unreliable attributes is questionable.
