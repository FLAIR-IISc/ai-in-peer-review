- Addresses gap between theoretical promise and practical difficulty of attribute-based zero-shot learning: training attribute detectors is often as hard as training object classifiers.  
- Proposes using random forests to predict unseen classes from their attribute vectors.  
- Incorporates a validation set into training by propagating it through each decision tree to handle varying attribute detector reliability.  
- Extends the approach to few-shot learning when few labeled examples from test categories are available.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance across multiple benchmark attribute datasets.  
- Presents an elegant, theory-grounded adaptation of random forests to zero-shot learning.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, including ablation studies, artificial noise experiments, zero- and few-shot analyses, and detailed comparisons.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear and well-written overall.  
- Minor clarity issue: role of threshold t in Section 3.2.1 is initially ambiguous but later clarified.  
- Original contribution: novel and effective application of random forests to a challenging problem.  
- Significance: robust, methodologically sound work that makes a valuable contribution and merits publication.
