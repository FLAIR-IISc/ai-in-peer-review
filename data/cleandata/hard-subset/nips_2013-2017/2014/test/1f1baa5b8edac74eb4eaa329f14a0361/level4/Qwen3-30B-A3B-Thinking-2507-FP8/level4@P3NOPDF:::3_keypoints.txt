- Clarity: exceptionally clear and well-written with precise technical descriptions.  
- Originality is modest; the primary novelty claim is overstated because prior DAP-style work already addresses attribute uncertainty.  
- Main technical contribution is a refined modeling of attribute noise/correlation (ROC-based reliability vs Platt scaling; joint error statistics via tree propagation vs independent treatment).  
- Missing citation/context: should reference Scheirer et al. "Multi-Attribute Spaces" for correlated attribute errors.  
- Technical execution is sound and experiments broadly support claims, but empirical gains are marginal.  
- Few-shot learning extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped, lacks justification, and Eq.6 appears ad hoc rather than theoretically grounded.  
- Major methodological gap: approach requires a large validation set because node-wise reliability estimates suffer as the validation set is partitioned.  
- No ablation or analysis of validation set size impact; practicality in label-scarce zero-shot settings is untested.  
- Heuristic fallback for small-node estimates (use root node) is not rigorously justified and may introduce bias.  
- Overall assessment: technically competent but overstated novelty, insufficient treatment of validation-size limitations, and marginal gains lead to a Weak Reject recommendation.
