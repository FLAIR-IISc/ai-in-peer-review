- Introduces random-forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning: train SVM attribute classifiers, then random forest on class-attribute signatures; estimate attribute reliability per tree node from a validation set; includes a few-shot extension.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear and well-written with a strong related-work summary.  
- Originality: novelty is modest but acceptable; claims of uniquely handling unreliable attributes are overstated.  
- Prior DAP method already handles attribute uncertainty in a similar high-level way.  
- Both methods train attribute classifiers on image features and use a validation set to estimate classifier reliability for a second-stage class probability estimator.  
- Key technical differences: this paper uses ROC-based reliability estimation instead of sigmoidal Platt scaling, and it models joint attribute error via tree propagation rather than assuming independence.  
- The contribution is better framed as improved modeling of attribute correlations (cite related work like Scheirer et al.’s "Multi-Attribute Spaces").  
- Method likely requires a large validation set because validation data is subdivided across tree nodes; this critical data-size dependence is glossed over.  
- The heuristic fix at line 323 lacks empirical justification; experiments on validation-set size and its effects are needed.  
- Experiments are technically sound and support claims, but quantitative gains are marginal.  
- Few-shot extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped and inconsistently brief; the weighted information-gain formulation (Eq.6) is less intuitive than a direct probability combination.  
- Overall: technically competent and well-executed, but the primary claim of a novel solution to unreliable attributes is questionable.
