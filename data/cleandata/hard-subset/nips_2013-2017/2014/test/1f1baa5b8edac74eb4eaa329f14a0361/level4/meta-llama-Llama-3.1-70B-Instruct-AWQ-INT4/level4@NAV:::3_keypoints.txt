- Introduces an attribute-based zero-shot learning method using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and a random forest on class-attribute signature vectors, with validation-set-based estimation of attribute reliability and a few-shot extension.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Novelty is limited; claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is exaggerated.  
- Method is similar in spirit to direct attribute prediction (DAP) and other prior work that also use a validation set to estimate attribute reliability.  
- Main technical contributions are ROC-based estimation of attribute noise and modeling joint attribute-error statistics by propagating validation samples down trees.  
- Improved modeling of attribute correlations is a more accurate characterization than a fundamentally new solution to attribute unreliability.  
- A significant weakness is dependence on a large validation set since validation samples are subdivided through the trees; the proposed remedy is heuristic and not fully justified.  
- Experiments are reasonable and support claims, but quantitative gains are modest and insufficient alone for strong acceptance.  
- The few-shot learning extension (Section 3.3) is less polished; combining class-attribute priors with image attribute estimates might be more intuitive than the current weighted information-gain approach.  
- Suggested to cite related work on attribute fusion and calibration (e.g., multi-attribute spaces) and to evaluate sensitivity to training/validation set sizes.
