- Goal: Narrow the gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning by addressing the difficulty of training reliable attribute classifiers.
- Method: Train random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors while accounting for attribute-detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each tree during training.
- Extension: Show how to extend the approach to the few-shot setting using a small number of training examples from test categories.
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple attribute datasets and strong results across three standard datasets.
- Experiments: Thorough experimental evaluation including ablations, injected artificial noise, zero-shot and few-shot settings, and comprehensive comparisons to prior work.
- Quality: Model is elegant, theoretically well-grounded, and the empirical results are convincing.
- Clarity: Paper is very well written, but Section 3.2.1 contains a confusing explanation of the threshold t and test-example propagation (later clarified).
- Originality & Significance: A well-executed application of random forests to an important, fundamental zero-shot learning problem that merits publication.
