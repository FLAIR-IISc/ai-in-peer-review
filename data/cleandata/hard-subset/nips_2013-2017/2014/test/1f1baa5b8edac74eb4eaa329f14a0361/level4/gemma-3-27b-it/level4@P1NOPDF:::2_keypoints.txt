- Proposal: Novel random-forest method for zero-shot learning that measures attribute classifier uncertainty on a validation set and incorporates it into information-gain during tree learning.

- Empirical support: Proposed ideas are well-supported by relevant empirical and quantitative analysis.

- Missing qualitative analysis: Paper would benefit from qualitative results showing which attributes and categories gain most from the method.

- Unclear parameters: Rationale for several parameter choices (notably the 80/20 train–validation split) is not explained.

- Clarity: Manuscript is well-written and easy to understand.

- Originality: Novel in explicitly modeling attribute reliability (distinct from prior focus on attribute strength), though many methods implicitly handle unreliability.

- Significance: Uses existing techniques but the idea of measuring classifier unreliability to improve zero-shot learning is interesting and potentially impactful.
