- Goal: Bridge theoretical and practical aspects of attribute-based zero-shot learning, noting that training attribute classifiers can be as hard as learning object classes.  
- Method: Use random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors and incorporate attribute detector reliability during training.  
- Reliability modeling: Propagate a validation set through each decision tree to account for attribute detector errors.  
- Extension: Method naturally extends to few-shot learning with a small number of examples from test categories.  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple attribute datasets.  
- Quality: Well-executed, elegant, and theoretically grounded approach.  
- Experimental rigor: Robust results supported by thorough ablation studies, controlled noise injection, and comprehensive zero- vs few-shot evaluations.  
- Comparisons: Rigorous and convincing comparisons with prior work.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written overall.  
- Minor clarity issue: Section 3.2.1 introduces a threshold t (0<t<1) that seems irrelevant given binary attribute signatures, later clarified in subsequent sections.  
- Significance & verdict: Addresses a foundational ZSL challenge with practical impact; strong paper that merits publication.
