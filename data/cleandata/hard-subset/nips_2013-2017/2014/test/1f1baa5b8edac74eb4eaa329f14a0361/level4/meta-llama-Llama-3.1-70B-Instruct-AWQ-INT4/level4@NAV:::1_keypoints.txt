- Paper addresses reconciling theoretical and practical aspects of attribute-based zero-shot learning using pre-trained attribute predictors.
- Method uses random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors and incorporates attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each tree during training.
- Method is extended to handle few-shot settings with limited examples of test categories.
- Approach achieves state-of-the-art results on multiple attribute datasets.
- Quality: provides an elegant, theoretically sound solution with impressive results and thorough ablation studies (including artificial noise) across zero-shot and few-shot settings.
- Clarity: well-written overall, with a minor confusion about the threshold t in Section 3.2.1 and the propagation of test examples (clarified later).
- Originality: notable application of a random forest framework to zero-shot learning.
- Significance: tackles a fundamental challenge in zero-shot learning with a strong, convincing contribution that merits publication.
