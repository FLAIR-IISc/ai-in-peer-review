The paper aims to bridge the gap between the theoretical foundations and practical applications of attribute-based zero-shot learning. The underlying theory posits that novel classes can be recognized automatically using pre-trained attribute predictors; however, in practice, learning these attribute classifiers can be as challenging or even more so than learning the object classes themselves.

The authors employ random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors and incorporate the reliability of the attribute detectors by propagating a validation set through each decision tree during training. They also demonstrate how the method can be extended to accommodate training with a few training examples of test categories. The proposed approach achieves state-of-the-art results on several attribute datasets.

QUALITY: The authors provide an elegant and theoretically sound solution to the fundamental problem of zero-shot learning using a random forest framework. The results on three standard datasets are strong, and the authors conduct thorough ablation studies, introduce artificial noise, evaluate several settings (zero-shot versus few-shot learning), and compare their method with existing literature.

CLARITY: The paper is well-written, with one minor point of confusion in Section 3.2.1. The introduction of the threshold t is unclear, as the attribute signatures remain binary, allowing t to take any value between 0 and 1 without affecting equation (2). Furthermore, it is not apparent how a novel test example can be propagated down the tree, as the definition of t seems ambiguous in lines 207-212. However, this confusion is later clarified in subsequent sections.

ORIGINALITY: This work presents a nice application of a random forest framework to an important problem.

SIGNIFICANCE: The paper addresses a fundamental issue in zero-shot learning, providing a strong, interesting, and sound contribution to the field. The results on three datasets across various settings are convincing, making the paper deserving of publication.
