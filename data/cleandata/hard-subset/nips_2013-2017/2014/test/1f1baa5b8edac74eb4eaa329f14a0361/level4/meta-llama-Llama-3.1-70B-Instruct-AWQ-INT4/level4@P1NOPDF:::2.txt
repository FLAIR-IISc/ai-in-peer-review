This paper proposes a novel random forest-based method for zero-shot learning, which effectively accounts for the uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions. The uncertainty is quantified by measuring the performance of attribute classifiers on a held-out validation set, using metrics such as true positive rate and false positive rate. The authors incorporate this performance information into the information gain computation during the learning of the random forest, allowing the method to accommodate the "uncertainty" of attribute predictions and pursue a given category-attribute signature along multiple paths of a decision tree.

Quality: The proposed ideas are well-supported by relevant empirical and quantitative analysis. However, the paper lacks qualitative analysis, which would have provided deeper insights into the method's performance. For instance, it would be beneficial to see the types of attributes and categories that are most affected or improved by this method. Additionally, it is unclear how many of the parameters involved were selected, such as the choice of 80%-20% split for training-validation, and the rationale behind these choices.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to read, making it accessible to a wide range of audiences.

Originality: While previous works have primarily addressed the problem of "attribute strength", this paper focuses on the lesser explored issue of "attribute reliability". Although many learning algorithms and techniques implicitly account for classifier unreliability, this work is novel in its explicit handling of unreliability.

Significance: Although the proposed random forest method itself is not new, the idea of modeling unreliability by measuring classifier performance on validation data to improve zero-shot learning performance is interesting and thought-provoking for researchers working on attributes. While the idea and method introduced in this paper may not be significantly novel, their application towards improving zero-shot learning is revealing and interesting.
