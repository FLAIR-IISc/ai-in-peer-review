- Paper proposes a random-forest framework that models attribute unreliability by fractionally propagating class attribute signatures using node-specific ROC (TPR/FPR) estimates from validation data.
- Method represents unseen classes by attribute signatures and uses validation-derived ROC stats to create splits robust to expected attribute prediction errors.
- Extends naturally to few-shot learning by combining signature-based information gain with standard data-driven gain via a trade-off parameter λ.
- Builds on two-stage attribute ZSL (e.g., DAP) but novelly integrates attribute error statistics into training objective and adapts uncertain-feature tree ideas to the zero-shot setup.
- Experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN show favorable performance, especially when attributes are noisy, with controlled-noise studies and useful ablations.
- Ablations include removing signature uncertainty and node-specific ROC propagation, which demonstrate the benefit of modeling unreliability.
- Technical formulation is well-motivated and careful; using node-specific validation subsets to capture attribute dependencies is a strong design choice.
- Few-shot extension is coherent, simple, and interpretable via λ.
- Missing analyses: hyperparameter selection and sensitivity (tree depth, candidate splits, λ) are underreported and need more detail.
- Missing analyses: fixed 80/20 split of attribute-labeled data into validation/training—performance vs. different splits and absolute validation size is not explored.
- Missing analyses: computational cost, runtime, and scalability (per-node ROC estimation, test-time complexity) are not reported.
- Missing analyses: limited qualitative analysis—no exemplar decision paths, attribute importance, or representative success/failure cases.
- Comparisons to alternatives that don’t require attributes (label/feature embeddings) are cited but not consistently included empirically in the main text.
- Clarity: manuscript is well-written with clear notation, but needs clarification on threshold selection for binary signatures and consolidation of key algorithmic details from the supplement.
- Originality: contribution is novel in injecting attribute error stats into ZSL training via ROC-driven fractional routing, and the few-shot prior is a neat, low-complexity addition.
- Significance: advances attribute-based zero-shot learning, particularly under noisy attribute predictors, and is of interest to ZSL/few-shot and robust tree researchers.
- Suggested improvements: report sensitivity to validation data amount/composition, provide qualitative insights (attribute importance, decision paths, confusion analysis).
- Suggested improvements: include runtime/complexity figures and discuss scalability to larger attribute vocabularies and more unseen classes.
- Suggested improvements: clarify signature uncertainty model in main text and compare empirically or discuss non-attribute embedding baselines.
- Suggested improvements: discuss calibration of forest outputs and whether class probabilities are well-calibrated.
- Pros: clear, well-motivated method addressing a key limitation; solid empirical gains; principled few-shot extension; easy to implement on standard random forests.
- Cons: limited hyperparameter and validation-split analysis; sparse qualitative interpretability; missing computational/scalability discussion; limited empirical baselines beyond DAP.
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely contribution with methodological clarity and empirical gains; recommend acceptance subject to suggested analyses and clarifications.
