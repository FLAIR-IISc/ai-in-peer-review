- Addresses difficulty of turning attribute-based zero-shot learning theory into practice, noting training effective attribute classifiers is often hard.
- Proposes using random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors.
- Incorporates attribute detector reliability into training by using a validation set during each tree’s construction.
- Extends method to few-shot learning scenarios and demonstrates adaptability with limited examples from test categories.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple benchmark attribute datasets.
- Evaluation is thorough: includes ablation studies, noise analysis, and comparisons across zero- and few-shot settings.
- Paper is very well written and clearly presented overall.
- Minor clarity issue: threshold t in Section 3.2.1 is initially ill-defined and appears to have no effect for binary signatures, though later clarified.
- Original contribution: valuable and practical application of random forest methods to zero-shot learning.
- Significance: tackles a fundamental zero-shot learning problem with strong, convincing empirical results and merits publication.
