- Proposes attribute-based zero-shot learning using random forests with SVM-trained attribute classifiers and a validation set to estimate attribute reliability; extends to few-shot learning.
- Paper is very clear, well-written, and summarizes related work coherently.
- Novelty is moderate; contribution is not fundamentally new but incremental.
- Claim that the approach uniquely handles unreliable attribute predictions is overstated/misleading.
- Baseline DAP and related work also address attribute uncertainty via training attribute classifiers and a second-stage class estimator using validation data.
- Key differences: uses ROC-based estimates instead of Platt scaling, and effectively models joint attribute-error statistics by propagating validation data down trees.
- Suggests rephrasing contribution as better modeling of attribute correlation and cites Scheirer et al. as a relevant related work.
- Potential weakness: requires large validation sets for reliable probability estimates as trees subdivide data; proposed remedy is heuristic and underexplored.
- Lacks experiments evaluating the effect of training and validation set size.
- Experiments are appropriate and support claims, but quantitative improvements are modest.
- Few-shot extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped and less polished than the rest of the paper.
- Suggests exploring probabilistic combinations of class-attribute priors and image attribute estimates rather than the proposed weighted information-gain fusion (Eq. 6).
- Overall: technically sound and well-presented, but main selling point about handling unreliable attributes is questionable.
