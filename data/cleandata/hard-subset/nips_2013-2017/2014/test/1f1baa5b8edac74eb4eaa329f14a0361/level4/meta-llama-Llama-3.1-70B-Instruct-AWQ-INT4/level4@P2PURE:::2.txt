This paper proposes a novel random forest-based approach to zero-shot learning that effectively addresses the uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions. The authors measure this uncertainty by evaluating the performance of attribute classifiers on a held-out validation set, using metrics such as true positive rate and false positive rate. They then incorporate this performance information into the information gain computation during random forest learning, allowing the method to accommodate the uncertainty of attribute predictions by pursuing a given category-attribute signature along multiple paths of a decision tree.

Quality: The paper is well-supported by relevant empirical and quantitative analysis, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, it would benefit from additional qualitative analysis, such as examining the types of attributes and categories that are most impacted or improved by this approach. Furthermore, the authors could provide more insight into the selection of parameters, including the choice of 80%-20% split for training-validation, to enhance the paper's overall rigor.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a broad audience.

Originality: While previous works have primarily focused on addressing the issue of "attribute strength," this paper makes a novel contribution by explicitly addressing the lesser-explored problem of "attribute reliability." Although many learning algorithms implicitly account for classifier unreliability, this work is distinct in its explicit handling of this issue.

Significance: Although the proposed random forest method is not new, the idea of modeling unreliability by measuring classifier performance on validation data to improve zero-shot learning performance is interesting and thought-provoking for researchers working on attributes. While the idea and method introduced in this paper may not be significantly novel in themselves, their application to improving zero-shot learning is revealing and noteworthy.
