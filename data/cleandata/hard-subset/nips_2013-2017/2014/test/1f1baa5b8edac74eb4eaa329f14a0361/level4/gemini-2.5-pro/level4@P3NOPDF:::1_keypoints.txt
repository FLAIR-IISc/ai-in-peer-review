- Addresses gap between ZSL theory and practice by predicting unseen classes directly from attribute vectors using random forests.  
- Core contribution: a training procedure that accounts for attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through trees to down-weight unreliable attributes.  
- Framework is extended to the few-shot learning setting when limited labeled examples are available.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on standard attribute datasets (AwA, CUB, aP&Y).  
- Strength: elegant, effective, and theoretically sound solution to noisy attribute predictions.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: ablation studies, artificial-noise robustness tests, zero-/few-shot evaluations, and broad comparisons to prior methods.  
- Novel and insightful application of random forests to ZSL; paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Weakness: minor confusion in Section 3.2.1 regarding the split threshold t for binary attribute signatures versus continuous probabilistic predictions.  
- Suggested clarification: explicitly state that t is learned with respect to continuous-valued attribute predictions from the validation set.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — significant contributions, sound method, and convincing experimental results.
