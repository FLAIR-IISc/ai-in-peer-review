- Proposes a novel random forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly models uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Quantifies attribute uncertainty using validation-set performance metrics (e.g., true positive and false positive rates).  
- Integrates attribute reliability information into the information gain calculation during random forest construction.  
- Empirical results strongly support the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis, e.g., which specific attributes or categories benefit most from the method.  
- Some parameter choices (notably the 80%/20% train-validation split) are not justified or explained.  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to understand.  
- Original contribution: focuses on “attribute reliability” rather than the more common “attribute strength.”  
- Explicitly modeling classifier unreliability is a notable and less-explored contribution, despite related implicit handling in prior work.  
- Significance: idea is potentially impactful for attribute-based learning, though the underlying random forest technique is well-established; overall interesting but not radically novel.
