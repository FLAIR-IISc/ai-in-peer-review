- Proposes attribute-based zero-shot learning using random forests: SVM-trained attribute classifiers, random forest on class-attribute signatures, and validation-set-based estimation of attribute reliability per tree node; includes a few-shot extension.

- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a good related-work summary and sufficient technical detail.

- Originality is modest; approach not a fundamentally new way to handle attribute uncertainty.

- Claim that prior work doesn't handle unreliable attribute predictions is overstated—baseline DAP and related methods also use validation-based calibration.

- Key differences from prior work: (1) uses ROC-based estimates instead of Platt scaling, and (2) effectively captures joint attribute-error statistics by propagating validation data down trees.

- More accurate framing would be that the method better models attribute correlations; recommend citing related work (e.g., Scheirer et al.).

- Potential weakness: method may require a large validation set because validation data is subdivided across tree nodes; proposed remedy is heuristic and under-explored.

- Experiments are appropriate and support claims, but quantitative improvements are modest and may not suffice alone for acceptance.

- Few-shot learning extension is brief and less polished; current weighted information-gain combination (Eq. 6) is less intuitive than alternatives (e.g., combining class-attribute priors with image-based estimates).

- Technical choices are sensible and the overall paper quality is good.
