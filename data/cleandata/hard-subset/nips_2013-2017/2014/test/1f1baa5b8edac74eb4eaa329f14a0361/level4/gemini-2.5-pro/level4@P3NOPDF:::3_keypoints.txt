- Introduces a two-stage attribute-based ZSL using SVM attribute classifiers and random forests trained on class-attribute signatures, with validation-set-based per-node reliability estimates.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and situates the work in the literature; technical descriptions are sound and justified.  
- Novelty is overstated: method shares the same two-stage paradigm as DAP and other prior work; main novelty is modeling joint attribute error statistics via random forests.  
- Recommendation to reframe contribution as improved modeling of attribute correlations/noise rather than a fundamentally new approach, and to cite related multi-attribute fusion work.  
- Major methodological concern: reliance on a sufficiently large validation set—validation samples get split down trees, producing unreliable estimates at deep nodes; proposed fix is heuristic.  
- Experimental validation is incomplete: lacks ablation/sensitivity studies on training/validation set sizes to quantify robustness to small sample counts.  
- Empirical gains over baselines are modest, so results alone do not strongly justify the approach’s superiority.  
- Few-shot extension is underdeveloped, briefly presented, and the weighted information-gain formulation lacks clear motivation; a more principled alternative is suggested.  
- Overall: technically solid and interesting idea, but contribution is incremental; addressing validation dependency and reframing novelty would strengthen the paper.
