- Goal: Bridge theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning by addressing difficulty of learning attribute predictors.
- Method: Use random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors and incorporate attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each tree during training.
- Extension: Method can be naturally extended to few-shot settings by incorporating a small number of training examples for novel categories.
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple standard attribute datasets.
- Quality: Presents an elegant, theoretically sound random-forest solution that is well-motivated.
- Experiments: Strong empirical evaluation with thorough ablation studies, artificial-noise tests, and comparisons across zero-shot and few-shot settings.
- Clarity: Generally well-written and easy to follow, but Section 3.2.1 has minor confusion about the threshold t and how test samples propagate down the tree (later clarified).
- Originality: A solid application of the random forest framework to an important zero-shot learning problem.
- Significance: Addresses a fundamental problem with substantial contributions and merits publication.
