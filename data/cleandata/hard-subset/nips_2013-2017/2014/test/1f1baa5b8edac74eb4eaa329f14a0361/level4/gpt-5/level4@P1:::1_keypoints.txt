- Addresses gap between ideal attribute-based zero-shot learning and practical unreliability of learned attribute classifiers.  
- Proposes a random forest framework trained on class attribute signatures that explicitly models attribute unreliability.  
- Models attribute error tendencies by propagating a validation set through trees and using ROC statistics during training.  
- Extends approach to few-shot learning by combining signature-driven splits with limited class-labeled images.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple standard attribute datasets.  
- Methodology is elegant, well-justified, and technically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: strong results, informative ablations, controlled noise experiments, and appropriate comparisons.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized overall.  
- A clarity issue: threshold t in Section 3.2.1 appears moot for binary signatures and makes test-time example propagation unclear until later clarification.  
- Original contribution: novel integration of ROC-based attribute uncertainty into random forest split selection.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental challenge in zero/few-shot recognition and offers a practical, principled solution with broad relevance.
