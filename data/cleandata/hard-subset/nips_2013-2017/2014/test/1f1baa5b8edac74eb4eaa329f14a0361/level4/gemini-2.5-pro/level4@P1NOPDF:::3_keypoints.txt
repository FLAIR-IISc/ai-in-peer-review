- Presents a random-forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning, training SVM attribute classifiers then a forest on class-attribute signatures with validation-based attribute reliability estimates.
- Also proposes a brief extension for few-shot learning.
- Paper is clear, well-written, and summarizes related work effectively.
- Novelty is limited; central claim of a novel way to handle unreliable attributes is overstated.
- DAP already handles attribute uncertainty; both methods use a two-stage pipeline and validation data.
- Main differences are ROC-based reliability estimation vs. Platt scaling, and implicit modeling of joint attribute-error statistics via validation propagation in trees.
- Contribution is better framed as an improved method for modeling attribute correlations/noise rather than a new paradigm.
- Method likely requires a large validation set because validation examples are repeatedly split in trees, risking unreliable estimates in deep nodes; proposed remedy appears heuristic.
- Experiments are appropriate and technically sound but show only modest quantitative improvements over baselines.
- Few-shot extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped, poorly motivated, and lacks detailed explanation.
- Suggested improvements: analyze sensitivity to training/validation set sizes, cite related attribute-fusion work, and consider more principled few-shot formulations.
