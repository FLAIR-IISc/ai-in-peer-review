- Addresses gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning: training attribute classifiers can be as hard as learning object classes.  
- Proposes a random forest framework to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors.  
- Training incorporates attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree.  
- Method can be adapted for few-shot settings with limited examples for test categories.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple attribute datasets.  
- Effectively tackles a core challenge in zero-shot learning with an elegant, theoretically robust model.  
- Strong empirical results on three standard datasets with convincing evaluations.  
- Provides thorough ablation studies, artificial-noise experiments, and comparisons to prior work.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear.  
- Ambiguity in Section 3.2.1 about the threshold t and how novel examples are propagated, though later sections clarify it.  
- Original and innovative application of random forests to zero-shot learning.  
- Significant contribution that is robust, well-founded, and merits publication.
