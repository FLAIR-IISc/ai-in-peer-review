- Problem: Reconciles theoretical attribute-based zero-shot learning with practical difficulties of learning attribute classifiers, which can be as hard as learning object classes.  
- Method: Uses random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors and incorporates attribute detector reliability by validating each decision tree during training.  
- Extension: Adapts the method to few-shot settings, enabling training with limited examples of test categories.  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on three standard attribute datasets.  
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation including ablation studies, artificial noise injection, multiple settings, and comparisons to prior work.  
- Quality: Presents an elegant, theoretically sound model that effectively addresses a fundamental zero-shot learning problem.  
- Clarity: Well-written overall, but Section 3.2.1 needs clarification about the unnecessary threshold t and the ambiguous description of test-example propagation (later resolved).  
- Originality: Notable contribution in applying a random forest framework to this problem.  
- Significance: Strong, convincing work whose results across datasets and settings support publication.
