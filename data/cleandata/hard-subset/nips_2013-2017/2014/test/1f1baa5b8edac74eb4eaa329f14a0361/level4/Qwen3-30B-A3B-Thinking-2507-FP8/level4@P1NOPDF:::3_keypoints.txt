- Proposes random-forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning: train attribute SVMs, build forests on class-attribute signatures, use validation set to estimate attribute reliability, and provide a few-shot extension.  
- Paper is clear and well-written with a strong literature review.  
- Novelty is modest; claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated because prior work (e.g., DAP) also trains attribute classifiers and uses validation-based reliability modeling.  
- Key methodological distinctions are ROC-based reliability estimation and explicit modeling of joint attribute error statistics via tree propagation, versus Platt-scaling and independence assumptions in prior work.  
- Suggest citing related work on modeling attribute correlations (e.g., Scheirer et al., "Multi-Attribute Spaces").  
- Major concern: method requires a large validation set—splitting that set down trees can make reliability estimates unreliable; the paper glosses over this and offers an unjustified heuristic.  
- Needs experiments analyzing impact of training/validation set sizes to validate robustness of reliability estimates.  
- Technical execution is sound and choices are well-motivated; experiments support claims but quantitative improvements are modest.  
- Few-shot extension is underdeveloped, brief, and lacks intuitive grounding; a posterior-style combination of class-attribute priors with image-based estimates would be more natural than the proposed weighted information-gain mix.  
- Overall: main contribution is improved modeling of attribute correlations, but overstated novelty, dependency on validation data, and limited empirical gains weaken the case for acceptance.
