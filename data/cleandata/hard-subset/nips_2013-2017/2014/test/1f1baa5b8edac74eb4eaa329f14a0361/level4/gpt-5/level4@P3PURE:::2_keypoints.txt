- Proposes a random-forest-based zero-shot method that explicitly models attribute classifier uncertainty.  
- Estimates uncertainty from attribute classifier performance (TPR, FPR) on a held-out validation set.  
- Incorporates this performance information into information gain during tree construction so category–attribute signatures can propagate along multiple paths.  
- Empirical and quantitative analyses support the ideas.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., which attributes or categories benefit most).  
- Parameter selection is unclear (e.g., why an 80/20 train–validation split).  
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Novelty lies in explicitly addressing attribute reliability, whereas many prior methods only implicitly handle unreliability.  
- Significance: the random-forest method itself isn’t new, but applying validation-based unreliability modeling to zero-shot learning is interesting and insightful.
