- Addresses limitation in zero-shot learning by modeling attribute classifier reliability (not just attribute strength).  
- Key idea: use ROC statistics from a held-out validation set to account for attribute prediction uncertainty during training.  
- Proposes a random forest-based zero-shot method that propagates class signatures through trees while modeling attribute classifier errors.  
- Extends the approach to handle signature uncertainty and few-shot learning scenarios.  
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets, consistently outperforming baselines (including DAP) by 4–8 percentage points.  
- Thorough empirical validation with controlled experimental conditions and multiple datasets.  
- Ablation studies isolate contributions: ROC-based propagation and signature uncertainty both provide clear gains.  
- Demonstrates superiority over SIGNATURE RF, highlighting the importance of modeling attribute unreliability.  
- Clarity is excellent: problem, method, and experiments are well-explained with helpful pseudocode and illustrations.  
- Originality: novel explicit incorporation of attribute classifier performance statistics into random forest training for zero-shot learning.  
- Significance: provides a principled, general solution to a core zero-shot limitation and improves practical performance, including on discovered attributes.  
- Limitation: performance depends on quality of attribute classifiers and validation data; ideal validation (unseen-class images) is not available.  
- Weaknesses: lacks qualitative analysis of which attributes/categories benefit and insufficient justification for the 80/20 training-validation split.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add qualitative analysis and justify the validation split.
