- Addresses gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning: attribute predictors are often as hard to learn as object classes.
- Proposes training random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors.
- Training accounts for attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree.
- Extends the method to few-shot learning when a small number of class examples are available.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on multiple standard attribute datasets.
- Model is elegant and theoretically sound.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: ablation studies, artificial-noise tests, zero-shot and few-shot analyses, and thorough literature comparisons.
- Paper is very well written overall.
- Minor clarity issue: threshold t in Section 3.2.1 appears ill-defined for binary attribute signatures, but this is clarified later.
- Contribution is novel, significant, and the paper merits publication.
