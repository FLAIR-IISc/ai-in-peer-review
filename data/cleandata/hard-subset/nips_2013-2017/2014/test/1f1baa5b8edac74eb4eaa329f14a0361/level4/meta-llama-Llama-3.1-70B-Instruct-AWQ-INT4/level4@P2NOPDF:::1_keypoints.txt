- Proposes a novel approach to bridge theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning.
- Notes that learning attribute classifiers can be as hard or harder than learning object classes.
- Uses random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors.
- Incorporates attribute-detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree during training.
- Extends the method to handle few-shot (limited training examples from test categories) scenarios.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on three standard attribute datasets.
- Paper is theoretically sound and methodologically elegant.
- Experiments are thorough: ablation studies, artificial noise, zero-shot and few-shot evaluations, and comparisons to prior work.
- Writing is clear overall, but Section 3.2.1’s introduction of threshold t and the explanation of test-example propagation (lines 207–212) need minor clarification.
- Contribution is original (novel use of random forests for zero-shot) and significant; results justify publication.
