- Proposes a random forest-based zero-shot learning method that models attribute classifier uncertainty using validation-set TPR/FPR and incorporates it into information gain to handle unreliable attribute predictions.
- Empirically well-supported with quantitative results showing clear improvement over existing methods, though lacking qualitative analysis of which attributes/categories benefit most.
- Parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 training/validation split) are not fully justified; more discussion of parameter selection is needed.
- Technical approach appears sound and results demonstrate improvement over strong baselines.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and logically organized.
- Novel contribution: explicitly models attribute reliability (vs. prior focus on attribute strength) within zero-shot learning.
- Although the underlying random forest is not new, the idea of using classifier performance estimates to model unreliability is interesting and potentially impactful.
- Results, especially improvements over DAP, suggest practical significance for attribute-based recognition tasks.
- Overall, a valuable and worthwhile contribution though not a fundamental algorithmic breakthrough.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important practical problem; novel ROC-based reliability modeling; significant performance gains on multiple datasets; well-motivated and thoroughly evaluated; extends to few-shot learning.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks qualitative analysis explaining why/how it works for specific attributes; parameter selection is insufficiently justified; requires a separate validation set, increasing data requirements.
