- Introduces a random-forest zero-shot learning method that explicitly models attribute classifier uncertainty using operating characteristics measured on a validation set.  
- Uses these reliability estimates in the information-gain criterion so category–attribute signatures can traverse multiple branches to reflect attribute ambiguity.  
- Empirical results and quantitative analyses are convincing and well-aligned with the paper’s goals.  
- Missing qualitative analysis—no breakdown of which attributes/categories benefit most or illustrative examples.  
- Needs clearer justification for design choices and hyperparameters (e.g., 80/20 train–validation split) and a sensitivity analysis to demonstrate robustness.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Novelty: explicit integration of measured attribute reliability into random-forest learning is a less-explored and seemingly novel contribution.  
- Significance: moderate methodological novelty; interesting and potentially impactful application to zero-shot recognition despite random forests themselves being familiar.
