- Problem: Attribute predictors are imperfect and their errors hurt attribute-based zero-shot class recognition.  
- Method: Proposes random forests trained on class-attribute signatures with fractional propagation using attribute-specific ROC statistics to model unreliability.  
- Technique details: During tree growth, class signatures are fractionally routed through splits based on ROC-estimated error rates from a held-out attribute-labeled validation set.  
- Extensions: Incorporates uncertainty in class signatures and a few-shot extension that blends signature-driven and supervised information gain via a weighting parameter.  
- Empirical results: Strong gains over DAP and signature-only random forest on AwA, aPY, and SUN, including ablations and controlled-noise studies; works with discovered attributes.  
- Relation to prior work: Differs from DAP and two-stage methods by not trusting attribute predictions and from embedding methods by retaining interpretable language-based specifications.  
- Novelty: Novel application of random forests to zero-shot learning via ROC-driven fractional propagation of signatures.  
- Strength: Clear, elegant formulation linking ROC curves to fractional propagation in trees.  
- Strength: Strong, consistent empirical improvements across multiple datasets and noisy-attribute scenarios.  
- Strength: Thorough evaluation with ablations, controlled-noise experiments, and sensible few-shot behavior.  
- Strength: Practical and interpretable approach compatible with standard attribute SVMs and discovered attributes.  
- Weakness: Clarify threshold t in Sec. 3.2.1—thresholds are ill-defined for binary signatures until unreliability is introduced; move preview earlier.  
- Weakness: Method depends on validation ROC statistics—needs sensitivity analysis to validation set size/domain match and reported runtime/overhead for node-specific ROC estimation.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—need discussion/measurements of training time and memory versus number of attributes, unseen classes, and tree depth.  
- Weakness: Attribute correlations—model tests one attribute at a time; discuss potential multi-attribute tests or decorrelation strategies beyond future work.  
- Weakness: Additional baselines—include recent embedding-based zero-shot methods on same splits or clarify comparability; consider calibrated-DAP baseline.  
- Quality: Technically sound and well-motivated with careful implementation, statistical reporting, and sensible hyperparameters.  
- Clarity: Well written overall; main confusion is role of t before unreliability is introduced—minor reorganization would fix this.  
- Significance: Addresses a key practical barrier in attribute-based zero-shot learning with consistent improvements and a useful few-shot extension.  
- Acceptance recommendation: Accept — well-executed, impactful, and convincingly evaluated despite some analysis and clarity gaps.
