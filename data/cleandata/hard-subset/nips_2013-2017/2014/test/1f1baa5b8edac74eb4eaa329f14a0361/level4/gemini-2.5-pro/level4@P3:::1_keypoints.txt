- Problem: Attribute-based zero-shot learning is hindered by unreliable/noisy attribute classifiers.
- Core idea: Train random forests on class attribute signatures while explicitly modeling attribute classifier error characteristics.
- Method detail: Augment tree building with node-specific ROC estimates from attribute-labeled validation images to guide fractional propagation of signatures.
- Extensions: Model uncertainty in class-attribute signatures and incorporate labeled examples for few-shot learning.
- Novelty: Innovative adaptation of random forests to train on abstract signatures and integrate classifier reliability into split decisions.
- Principle: Integrates attribute ROC statistics directly into information gain calculation for principled robustness to prediction errors.
- Experimental strength: Thorough evaluation on AwA, aPY, and SUN, showing significant improvements over strong baselines including DAP.
- Ablations and analyses: Controlled noise experiments, ablation studies, and an upper-bound ROC experiment convincingly support claims.
- Results: State-of-the-art performance on AwA with both named and discovered attributes; practical and versatile method.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized presentation with informative figures and tables.
- Minor clarity issue: Initial description of binary signatures and splitting threshold t is ambiguous until fractional propagation is introduced.
- Significance: Addresses a critical bottleneck in zero-shot learning and is likely to be influential and broadly applicable.
- Acceptance arguments: Strong novelty, convincing results, comprehensive evaluation, high practical significance, and clear writing.
- Rejection arguments: No substantive weaknesses; only a minor expositional ambiguity that is easily fixed.
