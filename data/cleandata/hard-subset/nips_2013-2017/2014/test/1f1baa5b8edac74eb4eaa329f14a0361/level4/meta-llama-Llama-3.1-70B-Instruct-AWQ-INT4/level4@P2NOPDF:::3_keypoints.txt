- Proposes attribute-based zero-shot learning using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and random forests on class-attribute signatures, with a validation set to estimate attribute reliability; includes a few-shot extension.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and summarizes related work effectively.  
- Originality is limited; the claim of uniquely addressing unreliable attribute predictions is overstated.  
- Direct attribute prediction (DAP) and prior work already handle attribute uncertainty via similar two-stage frameworks.  
- Main technical differences are using an ROC-based estimate instead of Platt scaling and implicitly modeling joint attribute error statistics via tree propagation.  
- More accurate framing: the method better models attribute correlations/noise rather than introducing a fundamentally new uncertainty-handling paradigm.  
- Related work to cite: Scheirer et al., "Multi-Attribute Spaces: Calibration for Attribute Fusion and Similarity Search."  
- Potential weakness: approach likely requires large validation sets because the set is subdivided down trees; the paper glosses over this and proposes only a heuristic fix.  
- Would benefit from experiments analyzing sensitivity to training and validation set sizes.  
- Experiments are sound and support claims, but quantitative gains are modest and may not justify acceptance alone.  
- Few-shot learning extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped and less polished than the rest of the paper.  
- Suggestion: combine class-attribute priors and image attribute estimates directly for p(a|...) rather than the proposed weighted information-gain combination.
