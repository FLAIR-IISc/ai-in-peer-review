- Proposal: Combine SVM-trained attribute classifiers with a random forest on class–attribute signatures, using an attribute-labeled validation set to estimate per-node attribute reliability; extend to few-shot by interpolating information gains.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized, but the few-shot section (Sec. 3.3) is too brief and needs more explanation.  
- Novelty claim: Claim of handling unreliable attributes is overstated since DAP-style methods already calibrate attributes; novelty lies elsewhere.  
- Real contribution: The value is in modeling correlated attribute noise via ROC-based calibration and propagating validation examples down trees (relaxing independence assumptions).  
- Framing suggestion: Reframe contribution as improved modeling of correlated attribute errors and cite related calibration/fusion work (e.g., Scheirer et al.).  
- Technical soundness: Method is sensible and flexible; random forests enable node-specific reliability and attribute interactions, but empirical gains are modest.  
- Data-efficiency concern: Estimating reliability per node partitions the validation set repeatedly, risking high variance and requiring large validation pools; the proposed sparsity heuristic is underexplained and unevaluated.  
- Few-shot critique: Few-shot extension is ad hoc (interpolated info gains); recommend probabilistic/Bayesian integration of p(a|x) and p(a|class) for a principled transition from prior- to data-driven.  
- Empirical gaps: Need analyses of sample efficiency, ablations (ROC vs Platt, joint vs independent), sensitivity to attribute quality/number and seen classes, calibration plots, and regularization/back-off strategies for sparse nodes.  
- Evaluation recommendations: Vary training/validation sizes, tree depth, min samples per node; test out-of-bag estimates and provide deeper few-shot experiments across label counts.  
- Minor issues: Clarify tree hyperparameter selection, report computational cost vs DAP, ensure calibration uses strictly held-out data, use statistical tests, and provide code/implementation details.  
- Overall assessment: Clear and well-executed with a useful focus on correlated attribute modeling and node-specific reliability, but novelty/impact are modest and the paper is borderline without stronger analysis and a more principled few-shot treatment.
