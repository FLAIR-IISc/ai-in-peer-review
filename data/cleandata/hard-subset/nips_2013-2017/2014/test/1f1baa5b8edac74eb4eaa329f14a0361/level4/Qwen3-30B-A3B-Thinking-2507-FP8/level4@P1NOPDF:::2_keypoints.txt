- Introduces a random forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly models uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Measures attribute uncertainty using validation-set performance metrics (e.g., TPR, FPR).  
- Integrates these performance metrics into information gain during tree training to account for attribute reliability across decision paths.  
- Empirically well-supported, showing improvements, but lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., which attributes/categories benefit most).  
- Key parameter choices (e.g., 80-20 train-validation split) are not well justified.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Novel focus on “attribute reliability” (trustworthiness of predictions) rather than just “attribute strength.”  
- Although unreliability is sometimes handled implicitly by other methods, explicitly using validation performance for uncertainty modeling is a distinctive contribution.  
- Methodological novelty is moderate (random forests not new), but the application offers practical, interpretable gains and meaningful insights for the zero-shot learning community.
