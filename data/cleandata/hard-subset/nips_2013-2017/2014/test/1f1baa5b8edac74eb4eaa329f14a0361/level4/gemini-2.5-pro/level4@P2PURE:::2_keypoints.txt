- Proposes a random-forest zero-shot method that quantifies attribute uncertainty via classifier performance on a held-out validation set.  
- Integrates attribute reliability into information gain during tree training, allowing categories' signatures to follow multiple paths when attributes are unreliable.  
- Empirical results quantitatively support the proposed idea.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., which attributes/categories benefit) and provides unclear justification for parameter choices like the 80/20 train/validation split.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to read.  
- Novel emphasis on explicitly modeling attribute reliability rather than attribute strength; many prior methods only handle unreliability implicitly.  
- Contribution is interesting and relevant to attribute-based and zero-shot learning, though the underlying model (random forests) is not new.
