- Highlights gap between ZSL theory and practice: learning attribute predictors can be as hard as learning object classes.  
- Proposes a random-forest method to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors.  
- Incorporates attribute-detector reliability by propagating a validation set through trees during training.  
- Provides an extension to few-shot learning when few examples from test categories are available.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on three standard attribute datasets.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: ablation studies, artificial-noise tests, zero- and few-shot analyses, and comparisons to prior work.  
- Method is elegant, theoretically sound, and empirically strong.  
- Paper is well written overall; minor confusion about the threshold t in Section 3.2.1 is later clarified.  
- Novel and significant contribution that merits publication.
