- Proposes a random-forest-based zero-shot method trained on class attribute signatures that uses a validation set to model attribute classifier unreliability via ROC statistics and fractionally propagate signatures down the tree; includes a few-shot extension.

- Clarity: paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-organized.

- Originality: contributes a novel way to incorporate attribute uncertainty, though it should be framed as a superior modeling approach rather than the first to handle uncertainty (DAP already models uncertainty).

- Key novel advantages: uses the full ROC curve to choose operating points and propagates validation data to implicitly model attribute error dependencies and joint statistics.

- Suggests adding related citation(s), e.g., Scheirer et al. "Multi-Attribute Spaces," for calibration/attribute fusion context.

- Quality: technically sound formulation and appropriate experiments showing consistent improvements over DAP on three standard datasets.

- Concern: ROC estimates at deeper tree nodes may be unreliable due to small validation subsets; the heuristic of reverting to a global set needs deeper analysis and sensitivity experiments.

- Concern: Few-shot extension is underdeveloped; the weighted average of two information gains (Eq.6) lacks strong justification and should be compared to alternative, more probabilistic formulations.

- Significance: addresses an important practical limitation of attribute-based systems and could increase real-world viability of zero-shot methods.

- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a known problem, introduces a clever ROC-guided fractional propagation idea, is clearly written, and shows consistent empirical gains.

- Arguments against acceptance: novelty framing vs prior work could be more precise, statistical validity of local ROC estimates is not fully explored, and few-shot treatment is brief/insufficiently justified.

- Overall: a solid contribution with strengths that outweigh weaknesses but could be improved by deeper analysis of validation-set effects and a stronger few-shot treatment.
