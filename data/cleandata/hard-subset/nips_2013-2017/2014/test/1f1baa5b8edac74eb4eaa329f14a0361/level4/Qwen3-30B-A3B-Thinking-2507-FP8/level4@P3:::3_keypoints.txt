- Proposes a random forest-based zero-shot learning method that explicitly models unreliable attribute predictions using ROC-based error statistics.  
- Uses attribute error tendencies to construct more robust discriminative models for unseen classes.  
- Extends the framework to handle few-shot scenarios and unreliable attribute descriptions.  
- Situates work well relative to prior literature, noting differences from DAP and methods that treat attributes as perfect.  
- Highlights that some prior methods use attribute confidence but do not model inter-attribute error dependencies.  
- Main technical contribution: integrating ROC-based reliability into tree training and propagating error stats to capture inter-attribute correlations.  
- Empirical results: outperforms DAP on AwA, aPY, and SUN, with strong gains for classes that differ from training data.  
- Ablation studies show importance of ROC propagation and signature uncertainty components.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with solid mathematical formulation and intuitive examples.  
- Few-shot extension is valuable but feels less developed and somewhat ad hoc compared to the zero-shot tree method.  
- Primary weakness: limited analysis of validation set size requirements and heuristic handling of small validation sets.  
- Overstates slightly that prior work ignores attribute unreliability entirely.  
- Limited exploration of model complexity vs. performance trade-offs; absolute improvements are meaningful but moderate (~5–10% on AwA).  
- Overall recommendation: strong, technically sound paper; accept with minor revisions to address validation set discussion and strengthen few-shot section.
