- Proposal: attribute-based zero-shot learning using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and random forests learned on class–attribute signatures, with a held-out attribute-labeled set to estimate node-wise attribute reliability; includes a few-shot extension.
- Clarity: manuscript is clear, well organized, and summarizes related work effectively.
- Originality: contribution is moderately novel; claim of uniquely addressing unreliable attributes is overstated.
- Relation to prior work: method closely parallels DAP’s two-stage setup (train attribute classifiers, then calibrate class estimator with validation data).
- Main technical difference: richer noise modeling via ROC-based reliability estimates and implicit modeling of joint attribute error statistics by the random forest.
- Recommended reframe: characterize contribution as improved modeling of attribute calibration and inter-attribute correlations rather than a fundamentally new solution.
- Missing citation: suggest citing Scheirer et al., “Multi-Attribute Spaces,” as relevant related work.
- Data-efficiency weakness: node-wise reliability estimation repeatedly subdivides the validation set, potentially requiring large validation pools; mitigation is heuristic and not thoroughly analyzed.
- Experiments/quality: technical choices and experimental design are sound, but quantitative gains are modest and not sufficient alone for acceptance.
- Few-shot extension: underdeveloped and brief; a probabilistic mechanism combining class–attribute priors with image evidence would be more natural than the proposed weighted information-gain approach.
- Recommendation: add analysis of validation/training size effects and strengthen the few-shot section to improve the paper.
