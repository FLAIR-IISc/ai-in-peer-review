- Introduces a random forest-based zero-shot learning method that explicitly incorporates attribute classifier uncertainty.  
- Leverages validation-set performance metrics (e.g., TPR, FPR) to inform information gain during tree construction.  
- Weights attribute contributions based on validated reliability and dynamically adjusts along decision paths.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and shows consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis identifying which specific attributes or categories benefit most.  
- Key hyperparameter choices (notably the 80-20 train-validation split) are unexplained and sensitivity is unclear.  
- Paper is very well written with clear technical explanations and accessible methodology.  
- Novel focus on modeling attribute reliability (measurable and context-dependent) rather than just attribute strength.  
- Significance is practical: bridges attribute-based zero-shot learning and real-world classifier uncertainty, though methodological novelty is incremental.  
- Overall recommendation: suitable for acceptance with minor revisions to add qualitative analysis and justify hyperparameters.
