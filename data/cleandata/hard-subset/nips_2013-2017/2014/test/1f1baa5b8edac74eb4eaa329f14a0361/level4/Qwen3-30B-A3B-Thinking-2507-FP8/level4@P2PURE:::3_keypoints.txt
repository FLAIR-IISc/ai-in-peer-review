Paper proposes random-forest-based attribute-centric zero-shot method with SVM-trained attribute classifiers and a validation-set-based node-wise attribute reliability estimate; includes a few-shot extension.  
Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written with a concise related-work summary.  
Novelty is modest; acceptance acceptable but the claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated.  
DAP and related prior work already address attribute uncertainty through the same two-stage paradigm (train attribute classifiers; second-stage class estimator with reliability from a validation set).  
Key technical distinctions are ROC-based reliability estimation here versus Platt scaling in prior work, and modeling joint attribute error statistics via tree propagation instead of assuming attribute independence.  
Method should be framed as improved modeling of attribute correlations (cite Scheirer et al. “Multi-Attribute Spaces”) rather than a fundamentally new solution to unreliable attributes.  
Validation-set propagation in trees likely requires large validation sets for reliable probability estimates; this limitation is under-emphasized.  
The heuristic fix (line 323) is insufficient; experiments exploring validation-set size effects are needed.  
Technically sound with well-justified choices and clear descriptions.  
Experimental gains are marginal quantitatively.  
Few-shot learning extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped, brief, and less clear than the main contribution.  
The proposed weighted information-gain combination (Eq. 6) is less intuitive than a direct probability-based integration.
