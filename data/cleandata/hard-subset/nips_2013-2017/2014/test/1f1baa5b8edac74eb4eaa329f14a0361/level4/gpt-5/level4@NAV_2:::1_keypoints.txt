- Addresses the gap between idealized attribute-based zero-shot learning and the practical unreliability of attribute predictors.  
- Proposes training random forests on attribute vectors while explicitly modeling attribute unreliability by propagating a validation set through the trees.  
- Presents an extension to incorporate a small number of labeled target-class examples (few-shot).  
- Reports state-of-the-art performance on multiple attribute datasets.  
- Method is technically sound and well-conceived.  
- Experiments are thorough: ablations, controlled noise studies, zero- and few-shot evaluations, and comprehensive comparisons.  
- Manuscript is clear and well organized, with a minor initial ambiguity about threshold routing in Sec. 3.2.1 that is resolved later.  
- Novel adaptation of random forests that integrates attribute error characteristics into tree construction.  
- Tackles a fundamental zero-shot challenge, shows convincing gains, and merits publication.
