- Proposes a random forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability.  
- Core idea: use attribute classifier performance metrics (TPR/FPR/ROC) from validation data during tree training.  
- Incorporates ROC-based reliability into information gain to let trees pursue multiple signature paths.  
- Extends approach to few-shot learning and accounts for uncertain class-attribute associations.  
- Well-motivated framing: fills gap where prior work models attribute strength but not prediction reliability.  
- Technically sound and clearly defined with logical presentation and helpful diagrams/pseudocode.  
- Evaluated on three datasets (AwA, aPY, SUN) with strong quantitative gains over state-of-the-art (notably DAP).  
- Ablation studies (removing signature uncertainty or ROC propagation) clarify component contributions.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis—visual examples or decision boundary plots would add insight.  
- No discussion of sensitivity to the chosen 80/20 train/validation split.  
- Missing analysis of computational complexity and runtime.  
- Novel contribution: using ROC propagation in tree-building to handle unreliable attributes.  
- Significant impact: meaningful improvement in zero-shot/few-shot scenarios and relevance to transfer learning.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add qualitative analysis, parameter-sensitivity discussion, and complexity details.
