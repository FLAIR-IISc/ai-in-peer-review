- Proposes attribute-based zero-shot learning using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and a random forest on class-attribute signature vectors, with attribute reliability estimated from a validation set.  
- Also presents an extension to few-shot learning.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and summarizes prior work strongly.  
- Novelty is moderate; claim of fundamentally addressing unreliable attributes is potentially overstated.  
- DAP and prior methods already address attribute uncertainty via similar two-stage structures and validation-based reliability estimation.  
- Main technical contribution is using ROC-based reliability estimates and implicitly modeling joint attribute error statistics by propagating the validation set through trees.  
- Reframe contribution as more effective modeling of attribute correlations rather than a wholly novel solution to unreliable attributes.  
- Missing relevant citation: Scheirer et al., "Multi-Attribute Spaces: Calibration for Attribute Fusion and Similarity Search."  
- Potential limitation: large validation set may be required for reliable probability estimates as trees subdivide data.  
- The paper glosses over this data-size issue; the heuristic at line 323 needs deeper investigation.  
- Experiments are appropriate and support claims, but quantitative improvements are modest and may not justify acceptance alone.  
- Few-shot extension (Section 3.3) is underdeveloped and lacks detail compared to the rest of the paper.  
- Suggests exploring an alternative that integrates class-attribute priors with image attribute estimates for p(a|...) rather than the current weighted information gain (Eq. 6).  
- Overall: technically sound and well-executed, but the central argument about a novel approach to unreliable attributes requires stronger substantiation.
