- Introduces a novel random forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly models attribute classifier uncertainty.  
- Quantifies uncertainty using validation-set metrics (TPR, FPR) and integrates them into information gain during tree training.  
- Empirically and quantitatively well-supported with relevant experiments.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis showing which attributes or categories benefit most from the method.  
- Key parameter choices are unexplained (e.g., why use an 80%–20% training/validation split).  
- Paper is clearly written and accessible.  
- Novel focus on "attribute reliability" rather than just "attribute strength," explicitly using validation performance to model unreliability.  
- Methodological novelty is moderate since random forests are standard, but the idea of using classifier validation performance is compelling and significant for attribute-based zero-shot learning.
