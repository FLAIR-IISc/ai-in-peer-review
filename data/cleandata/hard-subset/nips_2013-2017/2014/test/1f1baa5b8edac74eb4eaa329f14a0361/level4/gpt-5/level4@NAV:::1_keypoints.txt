- Aim: Narrow the gap between zero-shot learning theory and practice by addressing difficulty of training attribute classifiers.  
- Method: Train random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors, incorporating detector reliability during training.  
- Reliability handling: Pass a validation set through each tree during training to account for attribute detector accuracy.  
- Few-shot extension: Method can be extended to handle scenarios with a few labeled examples of test categories.  
- Performance: Achieves state-of-the-art results on several standard attribute datasets.  
- Experimental quality: Strong empirical evaluation with ablations, injected noise tests, zero- and few-shot settings, and thorough comparisons to prior work.  
- Model quality: Elegant, theoretically well-grounded application of random forests to the problem.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written overall, but Section 3.2.1 contains confusion about threshold t and how examples propagate down the tree (clarified later).  
- Originality: Thoughtful and novel application of a random forest framework to zero-shot learning.  
- Significance: Tackles a fundamental zero-shot learning problem convincingly and merits publication.
