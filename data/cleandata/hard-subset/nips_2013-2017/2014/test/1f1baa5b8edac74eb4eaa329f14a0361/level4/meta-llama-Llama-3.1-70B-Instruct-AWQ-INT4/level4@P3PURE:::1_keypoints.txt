- Goal: Bridge theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning by using pre-trained attribute predictors to recognize novel classes.
- Practical challenge: Learning reliable attribute classifiers can be as hard or harder than learning object classes, undermining zero-shot assumptions.
- Method: Use random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors, incorporating attribute detector reliability during training via validation-set propagation through each tree.
- Extension: Method can be naturally extended to few-shot settings to incorporate a small number of training examples for test categories.
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple standard attribute datasets.
- Quality: Presents an elegant, theoretically sound solution with strong empirical results and thorough ablation studies (including artificial noise and multiple settings).
- Clarity: Well written overall, but Section 3.2.1 has minor confusion—threshold t appears unnecessary/ambiguous and test-example propagation description is unclear (later clarified).
- Originality: A novel application of a random forest framework that offers a fresh perspective on attribute-based zero-shot learning.
- Significance: Addresses a fundamental problem in zero-shot learning with convincing results; paper is strong and deserving of publication.
