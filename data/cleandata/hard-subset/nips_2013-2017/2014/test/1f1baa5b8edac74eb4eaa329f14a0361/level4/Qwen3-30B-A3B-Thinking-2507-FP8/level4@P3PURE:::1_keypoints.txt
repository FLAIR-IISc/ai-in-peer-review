- Problem: Addresses gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning—difficulty of learning reliable attribute classifiers.  
- Contribution: Proposes a random forest framework that predicts unseen classes from attribute vectors while accounting for attribute detector reliability.  
- Method detail: Incorporates attribute detector reliability via validation-set propagation during tree construction.  
- Extension: Method naturally extends to few-shot learning with limited examples from test categories.  
- Empirical results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance across three standard attribute benchmark datasets.  
- Quality: Solution is elegant, theoretically grounded, and empirically strong with sound model design.  
- Experiments: Thorough ablation studies, controlled noise tests, and comprehensive comparisons to prior work.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written overall; minor confusion in Section 3.2.1 about threshold t and test-example propagation, but later clarified.  
- Originality: Novel application of random forests to zero-shot learning offering a fresh perspective.  
- Significance and verdict: Tackles a core, practically important challenge; strong contribution that deserves publication.
