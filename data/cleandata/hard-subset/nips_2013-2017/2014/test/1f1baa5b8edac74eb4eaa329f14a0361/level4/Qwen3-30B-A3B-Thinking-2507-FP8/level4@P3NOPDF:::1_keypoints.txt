- Proposes a novel random-forest approach that uses validation-set propagation to incorporate attribute detector reliability for attribute-based zero-shot learning (ZSL).  
- Extends the method to few-shot learning with minimal examples per test category.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on AWA, CUB, and SUN datasets with statistically significant gains.  
- Well-motivated and theoretically grounded; random-forest framework effectively models attribute uncertainty and avoids overfitting to unreliable attributes.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and reproducible: ablation studies, controlled noise injections, and comprehensive zero-/few-shot comparisons.  
- Minor clarity issue: ambiguity in Section 3.2.1 about the threshold parameter t (binary vs. continuous attribute treatment), which is resolved later but should be clarified earlier.  
- Highly original integration of validation-aware propagation with random forests, leveraging tree structure to model attribute dependencies and uncertainty.  
- Significant contribution: addresses the practical gap in ZSL due to unreliable attribute predictors and broadens applicability via few-shot extension; merits publication.
