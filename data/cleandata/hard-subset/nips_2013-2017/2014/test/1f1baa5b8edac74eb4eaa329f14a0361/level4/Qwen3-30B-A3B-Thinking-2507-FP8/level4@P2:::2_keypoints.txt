- Addresses unrealistic zero-shot assumption of perfect attribute predictions by modeling attribute prediction unreliability.  
- Proposes a novel random forest method that incorporates attribute classifier ROC (TPR/FPR) during tree training.  
- Models error tendencies of attribute classifiers to build trees that better discriminate unseen classes under attribute noise.  
- Positions work against DAP and prior zero-shot methods focused on attribute strength rather than reliability.  
- Technically sound with clear mathematical exposition and well-organized presentation.  
- Comprehensive experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN demonstrate consistent improvements over DAP and SIGNATURE RF.  
- Controlled noise experiments show strong robustness, especially when noise varies per attribute.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis illustrating which attributes/categories benefit or still fail.  
- The 80%-20% split for attribute classifier training vs. validation is not adequately justified.  
- Novelty lies in adapting random forests to incorporate attribute reliability, not in the forest model itself.  
- Significant practical contribution: improves zero-shot performance under realistic imperfect attribute predictions and includes a useful few-shot extension.  
- Strengths: practical problem focus, well-motivated method, solid empirical validation, simplicity and effectiveness.  
- Weaknesses: missing qualitative insights and deeper exploration of varying error profiles across categories; unclear validation-split rationale.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor improvements.
