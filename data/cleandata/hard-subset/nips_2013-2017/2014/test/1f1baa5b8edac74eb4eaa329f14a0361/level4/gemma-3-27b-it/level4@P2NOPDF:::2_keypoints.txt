- Introduces a random forest–based zero-shot learning method that explicitly models uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Shifts focus from attribute prediction strength to attribute reliability, measured via validation set TP and FP rates.  
- Integrates classifier reliability into information gain computation during decision tree construction.  
- Aims to improve zero-shot accuracy by pursuing category-attribute signatures with awareness of classifier uncertainty.  
- Empirical results strongly support the benefit of incorporating attribute reliability.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis showing which attribute types or categories gain the most from the method.  
- Key parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train/validation split) are not justified, harming reproducibility and interpretation.  
- Paper is exceptionally well written and clear.  
- Originality: novel explicit, systematic modeling of attribute reliability within zero-shot learning, distinct from prior work focusing on strength.  
- Significance: applies a well-established technique (random forests) in an insightful way to improve generalization via validation-based reliability estimates; not radical but valuable.
