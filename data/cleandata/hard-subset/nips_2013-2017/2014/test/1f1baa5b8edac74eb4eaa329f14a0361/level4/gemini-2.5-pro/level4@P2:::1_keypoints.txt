- Problem: Attribute-based ZSL is hindered by unreliable attribute classifiers, creating a gap between the ideal attribute-signature model and practice.  
- Main idea: Train random forests directly on class attribute signatures (not images) to perform ZSL.  
- Handling unreliability: Use a validation set with attribute-labeled images to estimate ROC stats (TPR/FPR) for each attribute.  
- Fractional propagation: During tree construction, propagate class signatures fractionally using the ROC estimates to encode expected attribute prediction errors.  
- Extensions: Model uncertainty in the class-attribute signatures themselves and provide a natural extension to few-shot learning.  
- Evaluation: Achieves state-of-the-art results, significantly outperforming strong baselines on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets.  
- Robustness evidence: Controlled noise experiment shows the method's advantage increases as attribute reliability decreases.  
- Ablations: Detailed ablation studies isolate the contributions of signature uncertainty and recursive ROC propagation.  
- Originality: Novel application of random forests trained on signatures combined with separate ROC-based modeling of test-time uncertainty.  
- Technical soundness: Elegant, well-grounded formulation that trains the model to anticipate attribute prediction errors.  
- Clarity suggestion: Section 3.2.1 should clarify the role of split threshold t (appears arbitrary for binary signatures) and foreshadow its ROC-based importance.  
- Recommendation: High-quality paper, strongly recommended for acceptance.
