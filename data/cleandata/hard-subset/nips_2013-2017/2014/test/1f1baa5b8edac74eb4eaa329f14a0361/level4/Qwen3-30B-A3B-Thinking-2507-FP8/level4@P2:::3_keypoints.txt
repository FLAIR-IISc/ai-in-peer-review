- Introduces a zero-shot method using random forests that explicitly models attribute prediction unreliability via validation-set-based ROC estimates at each tree node.  
- Trains attribute classifiers with SVMs and builds trees from class-attribute signature vectors; also proposes a few-shot extension combining signatures with limited labeled data.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and provides reproducible technical detail and thorough related work background.  
- Contribution is a meaningful refinement rather than a fundamental breakthrough: models ROC-based reliability and joint error statistics via recursive propagation.  
- Claim that prior work ignores attribute unreliability is overstated—DAP already models reliability (Platt scaling/probabilistic) though assumes independence.  
- Should cite Scheirer et al. "Multi-Attribute Spaces" which addresses attribute correlation to better position the contribution.  
- Technical execution and experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN are sound and show consistent, statistically significant improvements over DAP and SIGNATURE RF.  
- Absolute gains are modest (typically 2–5% over DAP; 5–10% on less reliable datasets like aPY and SUN), limiting the perceived impact.  
- Lacks analysis of how validation set size affects performance; handling of small validation sets appears heuristic and under-validated.  
- The few-shot extension is underdeveloped and uses an ad hoc information-gain weighting instead of a more principled probabilistic integration.  
- Strengths: clear exposition, thorough experiments, works with hand-designed and discovered attributes, and sound recursive modeling of attribute unreliability.  
- Weaknesses: novelty is somewhat overstated, few-shot section needs more justification, heuristic treatment of small validation sets, and modest empirical improvements.  
- Overall: incremental but valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with revisions to better position relative to DAP/Scheirer, justify few-shot design, and add validation set size experiments.
