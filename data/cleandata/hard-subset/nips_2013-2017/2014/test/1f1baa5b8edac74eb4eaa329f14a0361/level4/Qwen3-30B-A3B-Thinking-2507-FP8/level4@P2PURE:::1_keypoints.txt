- Tackles the gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning, noting attribute classifiers can be as hard or harder to learn than object classes.  
- Proposes training random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors while modeling attribute detector reliability.  
- Incorporates detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree during training.  
- Extends the method to few-shot learning with minimal examples of test categories.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results across multiple attribute datasets.  
- Experimental rigor: strong results on three standard datasets, with thorough ablation studies, artificial noise experiments, and comprehensive comparisons.  
- Quality: model is elegant, theoretically grounded, and well-executed.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, though Section 3.2.1 has a temporary ambiguity about threshold t with binary attribute signatures (resolved later).  
- Originality: novel application of random forests to a core zero-shot learning problem.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental challenge in zero-shot learning.  
- Recommendation: paper is strong and should be accepted.
