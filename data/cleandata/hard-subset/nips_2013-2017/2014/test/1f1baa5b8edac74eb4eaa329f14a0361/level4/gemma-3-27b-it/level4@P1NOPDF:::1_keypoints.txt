- Goal: Bridge theory and practice of attribute-based zero-shot learning, noting practical difficulty of learning attribute classifiers.  
- Method: Train random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors, incorporating attribute-detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each tree during training.  
- Extension: Method can handle few-shot learning by training with a few examples from test categories.  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple attribute datasets (three standard datasets).  
- Evaluation: Thorough experiments including ablation studies, artificial noise tests, zero-shot and few-shot settings, and comprehensive comparisons to prior work.  
- Quality: Strong, elegant, and theoretically sound solution to a fundamental ZSL problem.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written overall; minor confusion in Section 3.2.1 about the threshold t and test-example propagation (clarified later).  
- Originality: Novel and compelling application of a random forest framework to zero-shot learning.  
- Significance: Addresses a core challenge in ZSL with convincing results; recommended for publication.
