This paper aims to close the gap between the theory and practice of attribute-based zero-shot learning. In theory, novel classes can be recognized using pre-trained attribute predictors; in practice, however, learning accurate attribute classifiers can be as hard as (or harder than) learning object classifiers themselves. The authors propose a random-forest-based approach that predicts unseen classes from attribute vectors, explicitly accounting for attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree during training. They also show how to extend the method to the few-shot setting by incorporating a small number of labeled examples from test categories. The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance on several standard attribute datasets.

QUALITY: The paper provides a strong and principled treatment of a fundamental zero-shot learning problem using a random forest framework. The model is elegant and theoretically well-grounded. Results on three standard datasets are strong. The empirical study is thorough, including ablations, experiments with artificial noise, evaluations in both zero-shot and few-shot settings, and comprehensive comparisons to prior work.

CLARITY: The paper is very well written overall. One point of confusion arises in Section 3.2.1: when the threshold t is introduced, the attribute signatures are still binary, so any choice of t with 0 < t < 1 leaves Equation (2) unchanged. Consequently, in lines 207–212 it is unclear how a novel test example is propagated down the tree, since t appears ill-defined at that stage. This issue is clarified in later sections, but an earlier explanation would improve readability.

ORIGINALITY: This is a thoughtful and well-executed application of a random forest framework to an important zero-shot learning problem.

SIGNIFICANCE: The paper tackles a fundamental problem in zero-shot learning. It is strong, interesting, and technically sound, with convincing results on three datasets across multiple settings. The paper deserves to be published.
