- Paper proposes a novel random forest method for zero-shot recognition that explicitly models unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Method leverages per-attribute error statistics to build more robust discriminative models for unseen classes.  
- Approach is extended to handle few-shot learning and noisy/unreliable attribute descriptions.  
- Experiments are thorough: evaluations on three standard datasets with comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strong empirical results, with solid ablation studies and tests with artificial noise.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow; one minor confusion about threshold t in Sec. 3.2.1 that is clarified later.  
- Model is described as elegant and theoretically sound, effectively addressing a fundamental zero-shot learning problem.  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel elegant solution, convincing experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor confusion in Sec. 3.2.1 and could use more discussion of limitations and future work.
