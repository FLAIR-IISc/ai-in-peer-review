- Addresses the challenge that learning reliable attribute predictors can be as hard as learning object classes, limiting practical zero-shot learning.  
- Proposes a novel random forest framework that trains zero-shot models while explicitly accounting for attribute prediction unreliability.  
- Uses validation data to model attribute detector reliability and propagates this unreliability through each decision tree during training.  
- Extends the method to handle few-shot learning and noisy or unreliable class-attribute associations.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on multiple standard attribute datasets.  
- Method is technically sound, well-conceived, and elegantly integrates unreliability into random forests.  
- Evaluation is thorough, including ablation studies, artificial noise experiments, and comparisons across zero- and few-shot settings.  
- Comparisons with existing literature are comprehensive and convincing.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Minor clarity issue: the role of the threshold t in Section 3.2.1 is initially confusing but later clarified.  
- Original contribution: novel application of random forests to zero-shot learning and explicit modeling of attribute unreliability.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental limitation of zero-shot learning and has strong practical impact potential.  
- Recommendation: strong accept; no substantial arguments against acceptance were identified.
