- Proposes a random-forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and a validation set to estimate attribute reliability at each tree node; includes a few-shot extension.  
- Explicitly models attribute uncertainty during training to address unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written with logical organization and strong exposition.  
- Novelty is moderate—method is sensible but incremental, and the claim of addressing a previously unhandled limitation is overstated.  
- Main difference from prior work (e.g., DAP) is using ROC-based error estimates and implicit joint-statistics via validation-data propagation versus Platt-scaling/probabilistic modeling.  
- Method requires a substantial validation set for reliable ROC estimates; the proposed heuristic needs more rigorous evaluation.  
- Few-shot learning extension is underdeveloped and lacks sufficient explanation and justification.  
- Technical quality is high; methodological choices and descriptions are sound.  
- Experiments are well-designed (including controlled noise tests) and convincingly show benefits of modeling attribute uncertainty.  
- Results show consistent improvements on AwA, aPY, and SUN but the magnitude of improvement is modest.  
- Practical impact is uncertain due to limited gains despite statistical significance.  
- Work is significant for tackling attribute unreliability and is likely useful in real-world noisy-attribute settings.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should better situate novelty relative to prior work and analyze the effect of validation-set size.
