- Problem: Attribute-based zero-shot learning fails in practice because attribute classifiers are unreliable.  
- Core idea: Integrate attribute classifier error characteristics (ROC) into random forest training.  
- Mechanism: Fractionally propagate class attribute signatures down trees based on attribute error statistics.  
- Benefit: Encourages splits that are discriminative for ideal signatures and robust to noisy predictions.  
- Extension: Framework extended to few-shot learning.  
- Results: State-of-the-art performance on three standard attribute datasets.  
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation including controlled noise tests, ablation studies, and comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., DAP).  
- Originality: Novel application of random forests by embedding attribute reliability into tree construction.  
- Clarity issue: Section 3.2.1 could better foreshadow how split threshold t becomes meaningful once attribute unreliability is introduced.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—technically sound, significant, well-executed contribution.
