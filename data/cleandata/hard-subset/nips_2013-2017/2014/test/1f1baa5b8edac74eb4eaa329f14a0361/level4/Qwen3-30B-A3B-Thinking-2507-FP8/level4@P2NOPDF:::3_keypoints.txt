- Paper proposes a random-forest method for attribute-driven zero-shot learning, training attribute SVMs and estimating attribute reliability per tree node using a validation set; also extends to few-shot learning.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and clearly structured with precise technical exposition.  
- Originality: Claims of novelty in “handling unreliable attributes” are overstated—prior work also trains attribute classifiers and calibrates reliability using validation data.  
- Key technical distinction: replaces Platt-sigmoid calibration with ROC-based reliability estimates and models correlated attribute errors via tree-structured propagation rather than assuming independence.  
- Framing critique: The contribution is better characterized as improved modeling of attribute correlation, not a new paradigm for uncertainty handling.  
- Quality: Methodological choices are well-justified and experiments support claims, but quantitative gains are modest.  
- Few-shot extension: Underdeveloped—lacks explanation and intuition; Equation 6’s weighted information-gain feels arbitrary versus a probabilistic fusion approach.  
- Validation-set dependence: Reliance on large validation sets for node-level reliability is inadequately addressed; proposed heuristic glosses over overfitting risks in deep trees.  
- Missing experiments: Needs sensitivity analysis to training/validation set sizes to assess practicality and robustness.  
- Overall: Technically competent and plausible refinement, but novelty is incremental and empirical improvements insufficiently compelling to recommend acceptance without reframing and additional validation.
