- Paper introduces a random-forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning, using SVM-trained attribute classifiers and estimating attribute reliability per tree node via a labeled validation set; also extends to few-shot learning.

- Writing and clarity are excellent; related work is well summarized.

- Novelty is incremental: approach refines existing paradigms rather than presenting a fundamentally new solution to attribute unreliability.

- Claim of uniquely addressing unreliable attributes is overstated because prior methods (e.g., DAP) already handle attribute uncertainty via similar two-stage frameworks.

- Key technical differences are ROC-based calibration instead of Platt scaling and modeling joint attribute error statistics through propagation in trees.

- Characterizing the contribution as improved modeling of attribute correlation is more accurate than claiming unique handling of unreliable attributes.

- Related work such as Scheirer et al. ("Multi-Attribute Spaces...") should be cited.

- A significant weakness is dependence on a large validation set for robust probability estimates as data is partitioned in trees; this is not sufficiently analyzed.

- The proposed heuristic for handling small validation sets (line 323) lacks justification and deeper evaluation.

- Experiments are sensible and support claims but improvements are modest and may not justify acceptance on their own.

- The few-shot learning extension is underdeveloped compared to the rest of the paper.

- An alternative, more intuitive approach might be combining class-attribute priors with image-based attribute estimates for p(a|...) instead of the weighted information-gain combination in Eq. 6.
