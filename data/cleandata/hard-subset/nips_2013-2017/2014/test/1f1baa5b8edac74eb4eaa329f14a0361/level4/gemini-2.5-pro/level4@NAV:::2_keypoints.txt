- Proposes a novel random forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly accounts for uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Quantifies attribute uncertainty by measuring classifier performance (e.g., TPR/FPR) on a held-out validation set.  
- Integrates these performance measures into the information gain calculation during tree construction.  
- Allows a single category-attribute signature to follow multiple decision-tree paths, incorporating measured prediction uncertainty.  
- Quality: claims are well-supported with thorough quantitative and empirical results.  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., which attributes/categories benefit most) and justification for key parameter choices (notably the 80/20 train/validation split).  
- Clarity: manuscript is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Originality: novel explicit modeling of "attribute reliability" versus prior focus on "attribute strength."  
- Significance: idea is interesting and valuable for attribute-based zero-shot learning, despite building on established random forests.
