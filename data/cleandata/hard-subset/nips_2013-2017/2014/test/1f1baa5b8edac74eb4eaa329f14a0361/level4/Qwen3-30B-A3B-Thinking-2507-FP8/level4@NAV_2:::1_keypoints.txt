- Paper connects theoretical promise of attribute-based zero-shot learning with the practical challenge that learning attribute detectors is often as hard as learning object categories.
- Proposes a novel random forest approach that explicitly models attribute detector unreliability by propagating validation data through trees during training.
- Method leverages receiver operating characteristics to account for attribute prediction errors, distinguishing it from prior methods that assume perfect attributes.
- Extends naturally to few-shot learning by incorporating limited category-labeled examples.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on multiple standard attribute-based zero-shot learning benchmarks.
- Method is elegant, theoretically sound, and technically robust.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: ablation studies, controlled noise experiments, zero-shot and few-shot settings, and comprehensive comparisons to prior work.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized overall.
- Minor clarity issue: introduction of threshold t in Section 3.2.1 is initially confusing with binary attribute signatures but is clarified later for continuous predictions.
- Addresses a fundamental limitation hindering practical adoption of zero-shot learning and shows attribute-based approaches can be effective with imperfect attributes.
- Strong contribution with convincing results and potential for broader applications beyond visual recognition; recommendation for acceptance.
