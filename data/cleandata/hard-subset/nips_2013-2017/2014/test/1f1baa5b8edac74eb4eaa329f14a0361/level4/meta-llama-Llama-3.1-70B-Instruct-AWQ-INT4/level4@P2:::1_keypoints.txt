- Presents a novel random-forest-based approach for zero-shot recognition that models unreliable attribute predictions.
- Accounts for attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each decision tree during training.
- Method is extended to handle few-shot learning by incorporating a few examples of test categories.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a transparent method description.
- Provides clear explanation of the random forest components and how attribute unreliability is handled.
- Experiments are thorough and convincing, evaluated on several standard datasets.
- Strong ablation studies, including artificial noise experiments and multiple evaluation settings (zero- vs few-shot).
- Comprehensive comparisons to prior work showing state-of-the-art results on multiple attribute datasets.
- Minor confusion about the threshold t in Section 3.2.1, but this is clarified later in the paper.
- Addresses a fundamental problem in zero-shot learning (unreliable attribute predictions) with an elegant, theoretically sound solution.
- Quality, clarity, originality, and significance are all assessed positively.
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated theory, clear exposition, strong empirical results.
- No arguments against acceptance reported.
