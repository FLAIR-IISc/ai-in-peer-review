- Introduces a random forest–based zero-shot method that incorporates attribute classifier performance (TPR/FPR) from a validation set into information gain computation.  
- Explicitly models “attribute reliability” (classifier unreliability) rather than focusing on attribute strength.  
- Accounts for uncertainty by allowing multiple paths through trees for a given category-attribute signature.  
- Random forest backbone is standard, but the explicit incorporation of classifier unreliability is a novel and valuable contribution.  
- Shows consistent performance improvements over baselines (e.g., DAP) on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets.  
- Empirical results are strong and technically well-supported.  
- Lacks in-depth qualitative analysis—would benefit from visualizing decision boundaries and example cases of gains.  
- Needs clearer justification and sensitivity analysis for parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 training-validation split).  
- Dependency on a separate validation set to estimate attribute performance is a potential limitation.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with effective explanation of motivation and methods.  
- Original within the zero-shot context; reviewer found no similar approach in recent NIPS proceedings.  
- Addresses a significant limitation of zero-shot learning and has practical significance, including a few-shot extension.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel approach, consistent gains, clear presentation, few-shot extension.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited qualitative analysis, insufficient parameter discussion, extra validation-set requirement.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: valuable, novel, and effective work; recommend acceptance for NIPS 2014.
