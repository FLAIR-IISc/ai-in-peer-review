- Goal: close the gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning by addressing unreliable attribute detectors.  
- Method: train random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors and model attribute-detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each tree during training.  
- Extension: handle few-shot learning by incorporating a small number of labeled examples for target categories.  
- Results: achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple standard attribute datasets.  
- Quality: presents a strong, elegant, and theoretically grounded random-forest-based solution.  
- Experiments: thorough evaluation including ablation studies, robustness to synthetic noise, comparisons to prior work, and both zero-shot and few-shot assessments.  
- Clarity: overall clear, but Section 3.2.1 is confusing about the threshold t and how novel test examples are propagated (later clarified).  
- Originality: thoughtful and principled adaptation of random forests with careful treatment of attribute unreliability.  
- Significance: addresses a central challenge in zero-shot learning; strong, interesting, sound work that merits publication.
