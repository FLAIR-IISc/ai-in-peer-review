This paper presents a method for attribute-based zero-shot learning using random forests. The authors first train attribute classifiers using SVMs and then use a random forest to model the class-attribute signature vectors. A key contribution of the paper is the use of a validation set of images with attribute labels to estimate attribute reliability at each node of the tree, allowing the method to account for the unreliability of attribute predictions. The authors also propose an extension to few-shot learning.

The paper is well-written and clear, and the authors do a good job summarizing related work. However, the novelty of the paper is somewhat limited, as earlier work, such as Direct Attribute Prediction (DAP), also deals with attribute uncertainty. The proposed approach uses a more complex model for modeling attribute noise, including an ROC-based estimate and joint statistics in attribute errors. However, it is unclear whether this approach is fundamentally new or simply a better way to model attribute correlation.

The quality of the paper is good, and the technical choices and descriptions make sense. The experiments are appropriate and support the claims of the paper, although the level of quantitative improvement is not extremely high. One potential weakness of the proposed approach is the need for a large validation set to obtain valid probability estimates, which is glossed over in the paper. The extension to few-shot learning is also somewhat brief and could be improved with more explanation and evaluation.

Overall, the paper is well-written and technically sound, but the main selling point of the paper as a new way of handling unreliable attributes is somewhat questionable. The authors could strengthen the paper by providing more evidence for the novelty and effectiveness of their approach, as well as addressing potential weaknesses and limitations.

Strengths:
- Clear and well-written paper
- Good job summarizing related work
- Effective use of a validation set to estimate attribute reliability
- Extension to few-shot learning is a nice addition

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty, as earlier work also deals with attribute uncertainty
- Need for a large validation set may be a limitation
- Extension to few-shot learning could be improved with more explanation and evaluation
