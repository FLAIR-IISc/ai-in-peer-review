- Proposes a random-forest-based zero-shot learning method that explicitly accounts for attribute classifier uncertainty.  
- Quantifies uncertainty using validation-set performance metrics (TPR, FPR, etc.).  
- Integrates reliability into information-gain during tree training, allowing category–attribute signatures to traverse multiple paths.  
- Empirical/quantitative evaluations support the approach, but qualitative analysis is limited (e.g., which attributes/categories improve is missing).  
- Some parameter choices are unexplained (e.g., why an 80%–20% train–validation split).  
- Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Originality lies in explicitly modeling attribute unreliability, a less-explored angle despite prior implicit handling.  
- Significance: leveraging validation-set reliability for improved ZSL is interesting and engaging, though the use of random forests is not novel.
