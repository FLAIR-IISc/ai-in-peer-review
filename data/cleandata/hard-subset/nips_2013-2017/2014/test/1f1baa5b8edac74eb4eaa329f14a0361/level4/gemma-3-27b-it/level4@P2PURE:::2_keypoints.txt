- Introduces a random forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly handles uncertainty in attribute classifier predictions.  
- Quantifies uncertainty via attribute classifiers' true positive and false positive rates on a held-out validation set.  
- Integrates this performance information into information gain during random forest training to account for attribute reliability.  
- Enables decision trees to weigh the reliability of attribute predictions when using category-attribute signatures.  
- Empirical and quantitative evaluation is comprehensive and well-supported.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., which attributes or categories benefit most) that would strengthen insights.  
- Rationale for certain parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train-validation split) is unclear.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to understand.  
- Original in explicitly modeling attribute reliability rather than just attribute strength.  
- Significance: not groundbreaking methodologically but an interesting and potentially impactful application to zero-shot learning.
