- Proposes a random-forest-based zero-shot learning method that explicitly models attribute classifier reliability using validation-set TP/FP rates in information gain.
- Strong quantitative empirical results demonstrating effectiveness of the reliability-aware approach.
- Lacks qualitative analysis showing which attributes or categories benefit most and concrete examples of success due to handling unreliable attributes.
- Missing details and justification for parameter selection (e.g., the 80/20 train/validation split) and other hyperparameters; sensitivity analyses are needed.
- Exceptionally clear and well-written; motivation and method are explained logically and accessibly.
- Original contribution is the explicit modeling of attribute reliability—distinct from prior work that focuses on attribute strength—even though random forests themselves aren’t novel.
- Potentially significant and integrable with other zero-shot frameworks; highlights the importance of considering classifier confidence.
- Overall well-executed and insightful but incrementally novel; addressing qualitative results and parameter choices would strengthen the paper.
