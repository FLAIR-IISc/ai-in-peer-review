- Proposes attribute-based zero-shot learning: train SVM attribute classifiers, then train a random forest on class-attribute signatures using a validation set to estimate attribute reliability; includes a few-shot extension.

- Paper is very clear, well-written, and provides a good related-work summary.

- Novelty is limited; the claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated.

- Direct Attribute Prediction (DAP) and related prior work already handle attribute uncertainty in a comparable way.

- The proposed method is better characterized as a refined/random-forest-based model for attribute noise and for capturing joint attribute-error statistics rather than a fundamentally new approach.

- Technical contributions include using ROC-based estimates instead of Platt scaling and propagating validation data down trees to model attribute correlations.

- Potential weakness: requires a substantial validation set for reliable probability estimates as data gets subdivided in trees; the heuristic to address this needs stronger justification.

- Experiments are sensible and support claims but improvements are modest and may not justify acceptance on empirical gains alone.

- Few-shot extension is underdeveloped; combining class-attribute priors with image-attribute estimates might be more effective than the proposed weighted information-gain scheme.

- Suggest adding citation/discussion of related work such as Scheirer et al. on multi-attribute calibration.
