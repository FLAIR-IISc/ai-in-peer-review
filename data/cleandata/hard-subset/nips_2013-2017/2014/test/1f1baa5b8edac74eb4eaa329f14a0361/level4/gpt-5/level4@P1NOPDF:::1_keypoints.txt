- Goal: Bridge the gap between theory and practice in attribute-based zero-shot learning, noting attribute classifiers can be as hard to learn as object classes.  
- Method: Train random forests to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors while explicitly modeling attribute detector reliability by propagating a validation set through each tree during training.  
- Extension: Method can incorporate a few labeled examples of test categories (few-shot extension).  
- Results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on several attribute datasets and strong results on three standard benchmarks.  
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation including ablation studies, artificial noise injection, zero- vs few-shot comparisons, and comprehensive literature baselines.  
- Quality: Model is elegant and theoretically sound.  
- Clarity: Paper is very well written, though Section 3.2.1 contains a confusing point about threshold t (later clarified).  
- Originality: Solid, well-motivated application of a random forest framework to zero-shot learning.  
- Significance: Addresses a fundamental zero-shot learning problem and is convincing; recommended for publication.
