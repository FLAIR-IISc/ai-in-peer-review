- Proposes a random-forest method for attribute-based zero-shot learning that explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability using a validation set.  
- Core idea: train forests on class-attribute signatures and use validation-derived ROC stats at each node to prefer discriminative, error-robust splits.  
- Also presents an extension to few-shot learning via a linear combination of information gains (briefly described).  
- Paper is very clear, well-written, with good motivation and related-work coverage.  
- Claim of novelty is overstated: prior methods like DAP already account for attribute uncertainty in a similar two-stage framework.  
- Key technical advances are incremental but valuable: ROC-based noise estimates instead of sigmoid/Platt scaling, and modeling some joint attribute-error statistics by propagating validation samples down trees.  
- Should reframe contribution as improved modeling of attribute noise and correlations rather than a wholly new paradigm.  
- Suggested additional citation: work on attribute correlation/calibration (e.g., Scheirer et al.).  
- Potential weakness: reliance on the validation set — ROC estimates at deep nodes may be unreliable due to small sample sizes; the fallback heuristic is not fully justified.  
- Experiments show consistent but modest gains over DAP, so the contribution is stronger conceptually than empirically.  
- Few-shot extension is underdeveloped and Eq.6’s linear combination lacks deep motivation; a more principled integration of priors and image evidence is needed.  
- Overall: high technical quality and sound modeling, but novelty framing and sensitivity analyses (validation size) should be improved.
