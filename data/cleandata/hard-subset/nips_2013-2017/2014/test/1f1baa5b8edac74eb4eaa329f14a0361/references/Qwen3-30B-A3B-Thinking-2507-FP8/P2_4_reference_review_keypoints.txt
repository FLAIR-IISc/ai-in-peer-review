- Addresses ZSL's unrealistic assumption of perfect attribute predictions by modeling attribute classifier unreliability.  
- Proposes a novel random-forest method that uses ROC curves during tree construction to propagate training signatures according to expected FP/TP rates.  
- Extends the method to handle signature uncertainty and a practical few-shot learning setting.  
- Demonstrates significant improvement over DAP on AwA, aPY, and SUN, with larger gains when attribute classifiers are less reliable.  
- Method is theoretically grounded (modified information gain) and technically sound.  
- Experimental design is rigorous, reproducible, and statistically validated (20 random trials) with thorough ablation studies.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and provides sufficient implementation detail and pseudocode.  
- The ROC-based modeling of attribute unreliability is a novel and meaningful methodological contribution to ZSL.  
- Practical significance: requires no extra data, generalizes to semantic and discovered attributes, and improves robustness of ZSL.  
- Strengths include clear problem identification, novel theory, comprehensive experiments, and a practical few-shot extension.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on validation-data representativeness, scalability to many attributes/classes, and somewhat limited few-shot ablations.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; meets high standards of quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
