- Addresses a key gap in zero-shot learning by modeling unreliable attribute predictions rather than assuming perfect attributes.  
- Proposes a random forest-based method that explicitly incorporates attribute prediction uncertainty using ROC curves during tree construction.  
- Extends the approach to handle signature uncertainty and a few-shot learning setting using attribute signatures as priors.  
- Strong technical contribution: theoretically sound modeling of attribute unreliability and clear algorithmic formulation.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on AwA, aPY, and SUN with controlled noise experiments and ablation studies.  
- Ablations isolate the benefits of attribute unreliability modeling, signature uncertainty handling, and the few-shot extension.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized writing with reproducible technical details and helpful figures/tables.  
- Original insight: first to incorporate ROC characteristics of attribute classifiers into zero-shot training.  
- Novel few-shot extension that leverages attribute signatures as priors.  
- Significant practical impact: outperforms state-of-the-art DAP across datasets, especially where attributes are less reliable.  
- Demonstrates that attribute-based ZSL can be made robust to real-world attribute prediction errors.  
- Strengths: addresses an overlooked limitation, strong empirical gains, solid experiments, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: requires validation data to estimate ROC curves, potential unexamined computational cost, and incomplete handling of expected error rates for unseen attribute combinations.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, original, and significant paper — recommended for acceptance.
