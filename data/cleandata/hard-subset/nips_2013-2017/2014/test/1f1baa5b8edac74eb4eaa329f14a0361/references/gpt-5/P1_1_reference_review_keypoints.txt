- Proposes a random-forest ZSL method that models attribute classifier unreliability by fractionally propagating class attribute signatures using ROC-estimated TPR/FPR during tree construction.
- Uses soft information gain computed from fractional signature counts to prefer splits that are both discriminative and predictable given attribute error tendencies.
- Extends the method to handle uncertain class-attribute associations (signature noise) and to few-shot learning by mixing signature-based and data-based gains with a parameter λ.
- Experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN show consistent improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline, with ablations validating ROC-based propagation and signature uncertainty modeling.
- Controlled noise studies demonstrate robustness when attribute reliabilities vary; few-shot extension gives additional gains and sensible λ behavior.
- Novelty: fractional propagation driven by ROC estimates in RFs is an elegant, apparently new approach for ZSL.
- Technical contribution: node-conditional ROC estimation DV(n) accounts for dependencies introduced by upstream splits.
- Sound objective: soft information gain naturally down-weights unlearnable attributes (TPR≈FPR).
- Practical and extensible: straightforward to implement on standard RFs, handles multi-modal/continuous signatures, and supports discovered attributes.
- Empirical validation is thorough with ablations, controlled noise experiments, and comparisons to a strong baseline (DAP).
- Major concern: ROC generalization from seen to unseen classes is uncertain and may limit method reliability (sensitivity indicated by “OURS+TRUE ROC” upper bound).
- Limited baseline breadth: lacks comparisons to embedding-based ZSL methods (e.g., DeViSE, label embeddings), especially on AwA.
- Important methodological details (signature uncertainty model) are deferred to the supplement and should be in the main paper for reproducibility.
- Potential data leakage: unclear whether DV used to estimate ROCs is excluded from training attribute SVMs, risking optimistic ROC estimates.
- Sample scarcity at deep nodes: backing off to global DV when DV(n) has <10 samples may cause distribution mismatch; sensitivity analysis needed.
- Scalability concerns: number of candidate tests and recomputing node-conditional ROCs could increase cost; runtime/complexity analysis is missing.
- Clarity suggestions: include exact signature uncertainty formulation in main text, clarify DV treatment vis-à-vis SVM training, and provide pseudocode and runtime stats.
- Originality: novel application of ROC-driven fractional propagation distinct from prior missing/uncertain feature methods and offers a principled few-shot integration.
- Significance: addresses a practical ZSL bottleneck and is likely to influence attribute-based ZSL and robustness-aware concept pipelines.
- Questions for authors: clarify whether DV is excluded from SVM training when computing ROCs to avoid optimistic estimates.
- Questions for authors: report sensitivity to DV size/composition and the <10 sample threshold for backing off to global DV.
- Questions for authors: describe how thresholds t are sampled per node and per-attribute calibration choices.
- Questions for authors: provide runtime/training complexity comparisons versus DAP and the signature-only RF.
- Questions for authors: clarify SUN evaluation protocol—are results averaged over multiple unseen-class splits or just random seeds?
- Questions for authors: provide empirical analysis of correlated attributes and whether DV(n) captures such dependencies.
- Recommendation: lean accept — well-motivated, novel, and empirically supported method, but would be stronger with broader baselines, clarified methodological details, and scalability analysis.
