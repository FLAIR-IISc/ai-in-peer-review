- Proposes zero-shot learning via random forests trained on class-level attribute signatures while modeling attribute classifier unreliability using node-specific ROC (TPR/FPR) statistics for fractional signature propagation.
- Redefines information gain to operate on fractional counts, encouraging splits that are both discriminative and reliably predictable given attribute error tendencies.
- Incorporates signature uncertainty to model intra-class variation and extends to few-shot learning by combining signature-based and data-based gains with tradeoff parameter λ.
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN with multiple trials, ablations, controlled noise studies, and experiments using discovered (pseudo)attributes.
- Main strength: principled handling of attribute unreliability with node-specific ROC fractional propagation.
- Algorithmic strengths: careful design of fractional information gain and recursively filtered DV enabling conditional-error estimation at nodes.
- Generality: supports binary/continuous signatures, noisy class-attribute associations, and a natural few-shot extension.
- Empirical strengths: clear performance gains over DAP and signature-only RF, especially when attributes are unreliable, backed by ablations and noise studies.
- Concern: method assumes ROC estimated on validation seen classes transfers to unseen classes, and domain shift between DV and test can hurt performance.
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to DV composition and size, and consider importance-weighted ROC or conditioning DV on nearest seen signatures.
- Baselines are limited: comparisons beyond DAP and signature-RF (e.g., label/feature embedding methods like DeViSE) are missing and would strengthen claims.
- Estimation at deep nodes can be data-sparse; fallback to global DV when <10 samples may cause abrupt shifts—recommend smoothing (hierarchical shrinkage) or monotonic constraints on TPR/FPR.
- Attribute correlation remains an issue because splits are axis-aligned; discuss oblique/multivariate splits or feature grouping/whitening to mitigate redundancy/brittleness.
- Scalability concern: training one forest per class is costly for large K; report runtime, memory, and consider multi-class or shared-tree strategies.
- Clarify split threshold selection per attribute (uniform grid, quantiles, percentiles) and how many thresholds are evaluated.
- Few-shot λ selection is fragile with few labeled examples; suggest Bayesian/evidence-based schemes or heuristics tied to DT size and report λ sensitivity/variance plots.
- Key details deferred to supplement—signature uncertainty model and pseudocode should be included in main text for reproducibility, plus formulas for continuous/discovered attributes handling.
- Overall technically sound and novel: fractional propagation using node-specific ROC for zero-shot is original and distinct from DAP and trees for missing/probabilistic features.
- Significance: practical and conceptual impact for attribute-based zero/few-shot learning, with likely generalization to other mid-level semantic detectors.
- Requested diagnostics/questions: sensitivity to DV size/composition, candidate threshold selection, training/testing runtimes and memory, impact of ROC calibration bias, and per-class accuracy/confusion matrices.
- Minor reproducibility notes: specify random seeds/release splits for SUN and briefly describe how continuous signatures are used in split tests.
- Overall recommendation: lean accept; address clarity, broader baselines, runtime reporting, and domain-shift diagnostics to strengthen the paper.
