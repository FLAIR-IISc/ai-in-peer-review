- Paper addresses the unrealistic assumption of perfect attribute classifiers in zero-shot learning and proposes a random-forest method that models attribute unreliability via ROC-based fractional propagation.

- Novel technical contribution: fractional propagation of class signatures through trees using attribute ROC curves to account for prediction errors.

- Extends approach to few-shot learning and demonstrates improved performance on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets.

- Comprehensive experiments including controlled noise tests and comparisons to DAP and other baselines.

- Practical relevance: method works with real-world, imperfect attribute classifiers and uses only 80% of attribute-labeled data in experiments.

- Clear ablation studies showing the contributions of ROC propagation and signature uncertainty modeling.

- Paper is well-written with helpful figures and useful supplementary material for reproducibility.

- Limited theoretical analysis: lacks proofs or theoretical guarantees about the optimality of fractional propagation.

- Method depends on a separate validation set to estimate ROC curves, raising concerns about generalization to unseen classes.

- Insufficient comparison with recent deep learning / transformer-based ZSL methods, narrowing contextual relevance.

- No statistical significance testing reported for performance gains over baselines.

- Scalability concerns: unclear how approach scales to very large numbers of attributes or classes (e.g., beyond M=102).

- Quality assessment: technically sound experiments and strong empirical gains, but missing theory and significance testing weaken claims.

- Clarity assessment: logically presented method, but mathematical formulation could be simplified and main-text pseudocode expanded.

- Originality: novel application of ROC-based fractional propagation to ZSL, but could better distinguish from related attribute-learning work.

- Significance: addresses a real practical limitation in ZSL and shows modeling unreliability can outperform improving attribute classifiers.

- Suggestion: add statistical significance testing (p-values/confidence intervals) for reported results.

- Suggestion: analyze how validation data from seen classes generalizes to unseen classes; consider validation selection strategies.

- Suggestion: expand discussion and handling of correlated attributes and their impact on reliability estimates.

- Suggestion: compare more thoroughly with recent deep learning ZSL approaches to position contribution in broader literature.

- Suggestion: include more detailed pseudocode for the main algorithm in the main text to aid reproducibility.

- Final recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address statistical testing and validation-generalization concerns.

- Pro acceptance: addresses overlooked limitation, novel and effective method, thorough experiments, practical utility, few-shot extension.

- Con acceptance: limited theory, validation dependency, insufficient deep-learning comparisons, no significance testing, limited discussion of attribute correlation.
