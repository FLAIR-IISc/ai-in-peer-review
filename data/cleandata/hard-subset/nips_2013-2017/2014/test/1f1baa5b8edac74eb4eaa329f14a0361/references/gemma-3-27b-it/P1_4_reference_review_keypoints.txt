- Models zero-shot learning attribute unreliability via a random forest using attribute classifier ROC curves, and extends to few-shot learning.  
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets, showing significant improvements over baselines including DAP.  
- Builds on two-stage attribute-based ZSL but uniquely and explicitly models attribute prediction uncertainty.  
- Positions work relative to external-knowledge and mid-level feature approaches and links to handling missing/uncertain data in decision trees.  
- Novel contribution: explicitly modeling attribute unreliability is important and under-addressed.  
- Technical soundness: ROC-informed split selection in random forests is well-motivated and effective.  
- Strong empirical results and convincing controlled-noise experiments.  
- Clear writing, logical organization, and reasonably clear notation.  
- Thorough ablation studies and honest discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Potential computational complexity and scalability issues from ROC propagation and training cost.  
- Dependence on a validation set to estimate attribute ROCs may make performance sensitive to validation representativeness.  
- Limited analysis of failure cases; more diagnostics on remaining error types would help.  
- Unclear sensitivity to other hyperparameters (tree depth, number of trees) beyond the few-shot λ cross-validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, is novel, technically sound, effective, and well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, validation-data sensitivity, and insufficient failure-case analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to ZSL with recommendation to accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10; recommendation: Accept.
