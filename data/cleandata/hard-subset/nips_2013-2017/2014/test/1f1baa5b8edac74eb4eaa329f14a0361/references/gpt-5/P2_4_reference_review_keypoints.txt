- Proposes training a random forest on class-level attribute signatures while modeling attribute classifier unreliability via node-conditional ROC statistics estimated from validation images.  
- Uses fractional propagation of class signatures to children according to TPR/FPR at splits, so information gain reflects both discriminativeness and learnability.  
- Extends naturally to few-shot learning by convexly combining signature-based and example-based information gains via a mixing parameter.  
- Differs from prior attribute-based zero-shot work by incorporating attribute prediction reliability during training rather than assuming perfect attributes or only using confidences at test time.  
- Novelty: node-conditional ROC propagation in a signature-trained random forest for zero-shot is, to the reviewer’s knowledge, new and nontrivial.  
- Technical soundness: fractional propagation and recursive conditioning for node-specific TPR/FPR are well-motivated and convert signatures into expected distributions under the error model.  
- The information gain formulation with soft counts and the few-shot IG convex combination are reasonable and implementable.  
- Empirical support: experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN with 250+ attributes show consistent gains over DAP and a signature-only RF, plus ablations and controlled-noise studies.  
- Performance gains are largest when attributes are less reliable, matching the paper’s motivation; few-shot trends behave sensibly as λ varies.  
- Main caveat: approach depends on DV representing attribute error statistics for unseen classes, so domain shift in attribute errors is a concern.  
- Potential protocol ambiguity: unclear sequencing of ROC estimation and final SVM retraining may introduce optimistic bias and needs clarification and control.  
- Method largely treats attributes independently at split time; correlations between attributes remain unaddressed.  
- Clarity: generally clear with intuitive explanations, but key implementation details (pseudocode, signature-uncertainty) are deferred to the supplement, limiting reproducibility.  
- Would benefit from clearer sequencing of attribute SVM training/ROC estimation and a brief complexity/runtime analysis.  
- Strengths: principled ROC-aware RF approach, modular and easy to integrate, preserves semantic interpretability, strong empirical and diagnostic results, natural few-shot extension.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on representativeness of DV, ROC estimation protocol ambiguity, limited main-table comparisons to recent embedding-based baselines, and deferred methodological details.  
- Questions for authors: clarify SVM/ROC training sequence; robustness of node-conditional ROC estimates at deep nodes and smoothing/Bayesian fixes; runtimes/tree depth and overfitting behavior; comparisons to label-embedding methods under same features.  
- Reasons to accept: tackles an important practical issue with a principled, effective, and extensible method supported by solid experiments.  
- Reasons to reject: potential optimism from validation protocol, limited SOTA comparisons in main tables, and some reproducibility-hindering omissions to the supplement.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept.
