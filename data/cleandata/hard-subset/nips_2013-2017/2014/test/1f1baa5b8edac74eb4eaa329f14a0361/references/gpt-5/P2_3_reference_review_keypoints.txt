- Proposes training random forests on class attribute signatures with node-specific, ROC-based fractional propagation to account for attribute classifier unreliability.
- Injects attribute validation data to estimate per-attribute TP/FP rates and uses these to fractionally propagate signatures and modify information gain.
- Extends the method to handle uncertainty in class-attribute associations and to a few-shot setting by combining signature-based and data-based gains.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline on AWA, aPY, and SUN, including with discovered attributes and in few-shot regimes.
- Provides controlled noise experiments and ablations showing the contributions of ROC propagation and signature uncertainty.
- Formulation of soft counts via node-conditional ROC estimates and modified information gain is principled and internally consistent.
- Argues that with matched validation statistics, the learned forest approximates training on test-time attribute outputs.
- Validation data usage is unclear: potential leakage or mismatch if the same data is used to estimate ROCs and train attribute SVMs; requests strict hold-out clarification and results.
- Signature uncertainty model details are relegated to the supplement; more explanation and sensitivity analysis should be in the main text.
- Scalability concerns: one-vs-all forests per unseen class and per-node ROC computations may be costly for large numbers of classes/attributes.
- Clarity is generally good; intuition for fractional propagation is well explained but some implementation details (threshold sampling, small DV(n) handling) are in the supplement.
- Novelty lies in ROC-aware, node-conditional fractional propagation for zero-shot trees and combining signature priors with few-shot data.
- Significance: tackles a practical ZSL limitation, shows meaningful gains, and is adaptable beyond vision where semantic predictors exist.
- Pros: well-motivated problem, elegant solution, strong empirical gains, insightful diagnostics, ablative analysis, and applicability to discovered attributes.
- Cons/questions: possible DV leakage, limited empirical comparisons to embedding-based ZSL methods, runtime/complexity analysis missing, and single-signature-per-class assumption not evaluated for multi-modality.
- Suggestions: clarify DV splits and report strict hold-out results; add runtime/complexity discussion; compare to label-embedding baselines; move signature uncertainty details to main text and add sensitivity studies.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, impactful, and well-supported work that merits acceptance pending clarifications on validation protocol and additional comparisons.
