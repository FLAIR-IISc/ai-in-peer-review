- Core idea: Train one-vs-all random forests on class-level attribute signatures and inject attribute unreliability by fractionally propagating signatures using per-node ROC estimates from a held-out validation set.
- Split objective: Information gain favors splits that both discriminate classes and are predictable given expected attribute errors, with additional class-signature uncertainty modeling.
- Few-shot extension: Combine signature-based and image-based information gain with weight λ to incorporate labeled images.
- Experiments: Evaluated on AwA, aPY, SUN; shows consistent gains over DAP and signature-only RF with ablations and controlled-noise studies.
- Soundness: Fractional propagation with node-conditional ROC statistics is principled and consistent with RF training under uncertainty.
- Empirics quality: Strong baselines, ablations, and meaningful gains, especially with fewer positive attribute examples.
- Caveat — domain shift: Method depends on ROC estimates from seen-class DV; transfer of node-conditional ROC stats to unseen classes is uncertain and needs further analysis.
- Caveat — ROC estimation optimism: Unclear whether ROC curves are computed without using DV in training; using training predictions could bias ROC estimates.
- Caveat — attribute dependence: Node-wise marginal ROC approach may not fully capture correlations among attribute predictors even after recursive subsetting.
- Caveat — scalability: One-vs-all forest per unseen class may not scale to large K; no complexity/runtime reported.
- Caveat — threshold sampling: Details on (#m,#t) and threshold sampling strategy are missing and affect stability/speed.
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear but crucial details (pseudocode, signature-uncertainty model, ROC protocol) are deferred to the supplement.
- Originality: Novel use of fractional propagation driven by ROC statistics on class signatures in zero-shot learning; connection to probabilistic/missing features is apt.
- Significance: Addresses unreliable attribute predictors in ZSL with practical gains; likely to influence follow-up work and other mid-level cues.
- Strength: Conceptually clean incorporation of attribute unreliability via per-node ROCs and soft propagation.
- Strength: Strong empirical results and ablations; benefits increase when attributes are less reliable.
- Strength: Few-shot extension provides expected improvements and is straightforward.
- Strength: Applicable to discovered pseudo-attributes and continuous signatures.
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on DV composition/size for ROC estimation; needs quantification and possible domain-adaptation remedies (e.g., Bayesian TPR/FPR priors).
- Weakness: Potential optimistic ROC estimation if DV used in attribute training; recommend cross-fitting or separate calibration.
- Weakness: Limited handling of inter-attribute correlations; oblique or multi-attribute splits might help.
- Weakness: No runtime/memory/accounting for many unseen classes; single multi-class forest alternative not explored.
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to reliability-aware DAP variants, calibrated thresholds, and recent embedding baselines.
- Suggestions: Provide threshold-sampling details, (#m,#t), tree depth, and runtime/sensitivity plots for reproducibility.
- Suggestions: Move signature-uncertainty formulation to main text and analyze its impact more thoroughly.
- Suggestions: Add experiments varying DV size/composition and use cross-fitted ROC estimation to avoid optimism.
- Suggestions: Report per-class accuracies/confusions and explore a multi-class forest or runtime/memory tradeoffs.
- Acceptance argument: Principled, practical solution to a key ZSL limitation with solid empirical gains and simplicity.
- Rejection argument: Dependence on seen-class ROC estimates with limited analysis, missing implementation/scalability details, and incomplete baseline comparisons.
- Recommendation: Weak accept to accept; high confidence but needs tighter ROC analysis, clearer implementation, and scalability discussion.
