- Tackles unreliability of attribute predictions in attribute-based zero-shot learning by modeling attribute uncertainty during training with a novel random forest approach using ROC curves and fractional propagation.
- Extends method to handle unreliable class-attribute associations and generalizes to the few-shot setting via a weighted combination of signature-based and data-driven training.
- Builds on the two-stage attribute pipeline and adapts missing-data/random-tree ideas to train classifiers from signatures rather than labeled data, distinguishing from attribute-strength, hierarchy, and embedding methods.
- Novelty: incorporation of attribute ROC information into random forest training and fractional propagation of signatures to inject uncertainty.
- Strong empirical results: significant improvements over baselines (including DAP and signature-based RF) across three datasets, with controlled noise experiments showing robustness.
- Evaluation is comprehensive and the paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Weakness: additional computational overhead from ROC validation and recursive propagation; scalability to large attribute vocabularies/datasets not discussed.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (tree depth, (m,t) sampling) with limited analysis or practical guidelines provided.
- Weakness: reliance on representative attribute-labeled validation data to estimate ROCs; unclear performance under distribution shift and "OURS+TRUE ROC" is impractical.
- Weakness: limited explicit treatment of attribute correlations; method may implicitly handle some but lacks direct modeling.
- For acceptance: addresses an important overlooked problem, technically sound and novel, with compelling empirical evidence and a useful few-shot extension.
- Against acceptance: computational/hyperparameter concerns, dependence on representative validation data, and insufficient analysis of attribute correlations.
- Overall assessment: strong paper making a valuable contribution to zero-shot learning; merits acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
- Reviewer confidence 8/10; recommendation: Accept.
