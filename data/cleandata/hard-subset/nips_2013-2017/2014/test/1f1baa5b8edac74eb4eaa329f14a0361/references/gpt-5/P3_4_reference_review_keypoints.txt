- Problem: Attribute-based zero-shot learning suffers because attribute classifiers are imperfect and training ignores their errors.  
- Proposal: Random forest trained on class-level attribute signatures that explicitly models attribute unreliability via ROC statistics estimated on validation data.  
- Mechanism: During tree construction, signatures are fractionally propagated to children using node-specific TPR/FPR for candidate attributes and thresholds; splits maximize information gain on fractional counts.  
- Extensions: Accounts for signature uncertainty and extends to few-shot by mixing signature-based and labeled-image information gain with parameter λ.  
- Empirical results: Consistent gains over DAP and signature-only RF on AwA, aPY, SUN; ablations and controlled-noise experiments support robustness; works with discovered attributes.  
- Novelty: Unlike prior attribute-based methods, trains to respect attribute reliability (not just trust predictions at test time); fractional propagation is related to probabilistic-feature trees but differs in train/test setup.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical deployment issue—noisy, correlated, data-poor attribute predictors—directly and effectively.  
- Strength: Elegant integration of attribute ROC curves; node-specific ROC via recursively filtered validation sets captures context-dependent behavior.  
- Strength: Simple, effective few-shot λ-weighted joint information gain with sensible behavior as sample size changes.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation, informative ablations, and applicability to named and discovered attributes.  
- Weakness: ROC estimation/training pipeline ambiguous: reserving 20% DV for ROC then pooling to train final classifiers may induce mismatch—needs clarification or recomputation.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns: training one RF per unseen class scales linearly in K; unclear training/inference cost and need to retrain when adding classes.  
- Weakness: Node-wise ROC estimates can be noisy due to small DV(n); fallback to global DV is ad hoc—requires sensitivity analysis or shrinkage/Bayesian fixes.  
- Weakness: Limited modeling of inter-attribute dependence; per-attribute ROC assumption may fail when attributes are strongly correlated.  
- Weakness: Baseline coverage limited on aPY/SUN—missing comparisons to embedding/metric-learning methods (ALE/DeViSE/WSABIE/ConSE).  
- Weakness: Missing evaluation details: per-class vs per-image accuracy, hyperparameter tuning protocol, computational costs, and variance across splits.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical grounding—no formal analysis linking information gain criterion to expected test risk or consistency.  
- Suggestion: Clarify and, if needed, correct ROC estimation/training pipeline and quantify any mismatch impact.  
- Suggestion: Add comparisons to representative embedding-based zero-shot methods and report top-5 accuracy for large K.  
- Suggestion: Ablate amount of attribute-labeled validation data to show robustness to scarce DV.  
- Suggestion: Explore calibration of attribute scores (e.g., isotonic regression) and whether it benefits the method versus DAP.  
- Suggestion: Discuss approaches to incorporate new unseen classes without retraining all per-class forests (e.g., shared multi-class trees or incremental forests).  
- Quality/Clarity: Technically sound and generally well-written; include pseudocode and explicit metrics in main paper to aid reproducibility.  
- Originality/Significance: Novel and well-motivated node-specific ROC-driven fractional propagation in RFs; likely to impact follow-up work.  
- Recommendation: Accept (leaning accept), subject to clarifications on ROC estimation, scalability, and baseline breadth.
