- Problem: Zero-shot recognition with unreliable attribute classifiers; train random forests on class-level attribute signatures while accounting for attribute prediction errors.  
- Key idea: Use per-attribute ROC estimates from attribute-labeled validation images to model attribute error rates.  
- Training mechanism: Fractionally propagate each class signature down the tree using TP/FP rates at node-specific thresholds.  
- Objective: Information gain criterion prefers splits that are both discriminative and learnable under attribute noise.  
- Class-signature uncertainty: Method models uncertainty in class-attribute signatures.  
- Few-shot extension: Combine signature-based and data-based information gain via a mixing parameter λ.  
- Empirical results: Consistent gains over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline on AwA, aPY, and SUN.  
- Ablations: Studies (no signature uncertainty, global vs node-specific ROC) validate contributions.  
- Controlled noise experiments: Support robustness claims when attributes are less reliable.  
- Strength: Directly addresses unreliability of attribute predictors with a clean ROC-grounded mechanism.  
- Strength: Methodologically novel for zero-shot; fractional propagation and node-specific ROC are conceptually adapted to the setting.  
- Strength: Node-specific ROC using recursively filtered validation sets partially captures inter-attribute dependencies from upstream splits.  
- Weakness: Potential leakage/mismatch in ROC estimation—unclear whether ROC validation data (DV) is excluded from attribute classifier training.  
- Weakness: Scalability concern—training one-vs-all forest per unseen class scales poorly with many unseen classes.  
- Weakness: Limited baselines in main tables; missing comparisons to label/semantic embedding methods (e.g., DeViSE, ALE) on aPY/SUN.  
- Weakness: Models only per-attribute error rates; joint attribute correlations beyond split history are not fully modeled.  
- Weakness: Node-specific ROC estimates may be noisy for deep nodes; fallback to global DV helps but smoothing/Bayesian calibration could be needed.  
- Weakness: Calibration and metric reporting unclear—per-class vs overall accuracy and posterior calibration not discussed.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter selection protocol (cross-validation data) for unseen-class settings is not specified.  
- Quality: Technically sound and well-motivated; fractional-propagation is a coherent approximation of expected test-time discriminativeness.  
- Clarity: Generally clear, but important implementation details (signature uncertainty, continuous attributes, ROC usage) are in the supplement and need clarification.  
- Originality: Moderate-to-high—novel application of ROC-driven fractional propagation to zero-shot learning.  
- Significance: Practically significant; addresses a key blocker for zero-shot deployment and provides a pragmatic few-shot extension.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify attribute SVM/ROC training pipeline and whether DV is held out; provide computational cost as function of K,M,J,depth; report sensitivity to Platt scaling and threshold choices; state whether metrics are per-class or overall; explain how λ is chosen without unseen labels.  
- Recommendation: Lean toward acceptance pending ROC protocol clarification and ideally a strict hold-out ROC experiment to confirm reported gains.
