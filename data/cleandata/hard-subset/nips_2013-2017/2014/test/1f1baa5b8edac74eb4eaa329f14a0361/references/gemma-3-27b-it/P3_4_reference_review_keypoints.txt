- Overall assessment: Valuable, well-motivated contribution to zero-shot learning; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.  
- Problem addressed: Zero-shot learning with inherently unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Core idea: Incorporate attribute uncertainty into random forest training using attribute ROC curves to choose informative splits and propagate uncertainty; extended to few-shot.  
- Related work: Well-positioned against prior ZSL and attribute methods; distinguishes attribute reliability vs strength and cites probabilistic/missing-feature decision tree literature and cognitive science.  
- Strength — impact: Directly tackles a critical practical bottleneck in attribute-based ZSL.  
- Strength — novelty: ROC-informed splitting in decision trees to handle attribute unreliability is clever and original.  
- Strength — empirical: Strong performance over state-of-the-art on AwA, aPY, SUN with clear ablation studies.  
- Strength — presentation: Well-written, organized, and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Strength — evaluation: Thorough tests including controlled noise and real unreliability comparisons; includes few-shot extension.  
- Weakness — compute: Computational complexity and scalability of ROC-aware splitting and multiple forest training not discussed.  
- Weakness — validation dependence: Method reserves 20% attribute-labeled data for validation; sensitivity to this choice is unexplored.  
- Weakness — analysis: Limited qualitative/error-case analysis and lack of attribute-level failure inspection or decision-boundary visualization.  
- Weakness — model choice: No discussion of why random forests were chosen over other ensembles (e.g., gradient boosting).  
- Significance: Technically sound, original, and potentially impactful for practical ZSL systems.  
- For acceptance: Addresses major challenge, novel/effective method, strong multi-dataset results, clear writing, real-world relevance.  
- Against acceptance: Concerns about computational cost, validation-data dependency, and limited error analysis.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept with suggestions to address computational cost and validation sensitivity in the camera-ready version.
