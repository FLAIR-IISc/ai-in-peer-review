- Core idea: Train random forests on unseen-class attribute signatures and explicitly model attribute classifier error via node-specific ROC estimates from held-out validation data.  
- Mechanism: Fractionally propagate class signatures down tree splits using TP/FP/TN/FN rates to form an information gain (IGzero) that prefers splits robust to attribute noise.  
- Extensions: Incorporates signature-attribute association uncertainty and a few-shot variant that blends signature-based and image-based IGs with weight λ.  
- Empirical evaluation: Tests on AwA, aPY, and SUN show consistent improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline, with larger gains when attributes are noisier and in few-shot settings.  
- Ablations: Paper includes ablations removing signature uncertainty and node-specific ROC propagation, plus an “upper bound” using true ROC from unseen classes.  
- Technical soundness: Formulation is coherent; node-specific ROC propagation and IGzero are principled and well defined.  
- Important detail: Propagating validation data per-node captures conditional dependencies induced by earlier splits.  
- Main caveat: Method depends on validation ROCs estimated from seen classes, which may not match unseen-class statistics (domain shift).  
- Scalability concern: One-vs-all forests per unseen class may pose calibration and memory/computation issues as K grows; no complexity analysis provided.  
- Clarity: Overall clear and well organized, but key details (pseudocode, noise model, signature uncertainty) are relegated to the supplement.  
- Reproducibility gap: Need brief description of signature uncertainty, hyperparameter ranges (#m, #t, λ), and clearer explanation of the 20% validation/data usage to avoid confusion.  
- Originality: Novel application of ROC-aware, fractional-propagation random forests trained from signatures in the zero-shot context; few-shot blending is also novel.  
- Significance: Addresses an important bottleneck in attribute-based zero-shot learning and advances practical viability, including for discovered attributes.  
- Pros: Conceptually clean treatment of attribute unreliability, solid empirical gains, node-specific ROC handling, natural few-shot extension, works with discovered attributes.  
- Cons: Reliance on seen-class validation ROC, potential scalability/calibration issues for many classes, essential details in supplement, limited comparison to non-attribute embedding-based baselines.  
- Suggested improvements: Analyze sensitivity to validation set size/composition and domain shift; report training/inference time and memory; discuss calibration and multi-class tree variants; include signature uncertainty description and broader baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — principled, novel method with thorough experiments and meaningful gains; remaining concerns are incremental and addressable.
