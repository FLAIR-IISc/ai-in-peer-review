- Paper tackles attribute-based zero-shot learning by modeling unreliable attribute predictors using a random forest that incorporates attribute uncertainty via ROC-guided splits and fractional signature propagation.  
- Extends the method to a few-shot setting and shows improvements on three datasets.
- Strength: Directly addresses the practical limitation that attribute classifiers are inaccurate.
- Strength: Novel approach using ROC curves and fractional signatures to incorporate attribute uncertainty.
- Strength: Strong, statistically significant empirical gains over baselines (DAP, Signature RF) and controlled noise experiments support the claims.
- Strength: Clear writing, well-organized presentation, and sufficient methodological detail for reproducibility.
- Strength: Thorough evaluation including ablations, comparisons, and tests with both named and discovered attributes.
- Weakness: Likely increased computational cost from ROC analysis and fractional propagation; complexity/scalability not discussed.
- Weakness: Multiple hyperparameters introduced (λ, tree depth, sampling) with limited analysis of sensitivity and selection strategy.
- Weakness: Requires a separate validation set to estimate ROC curves; impact of validation size/representativeness not fully explored.
- Weakness: Limited error analysis on real datasets to characterize remaining failure modes.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with well-supported claims and significant technical contribution.
- Clarity assessment: Paper is clearly written and easy to follow.
- Originality assessment: Novel incorporation of attribute uncertainty into ZSL training via ROC and fractional signatures.
- Significance assessment: Results are important and improve the practicality of attribute-based ZSL.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses major ZSL limitation, proposes a novel approach, demonstrates strong results, is clearly written, and contributes significantly to the field.
- Arguments against acceptance: Potential computational cost, parameter sensitivity, and dependence on validation data.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with compelling contributions and minor issues that do not undermine its quality.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
