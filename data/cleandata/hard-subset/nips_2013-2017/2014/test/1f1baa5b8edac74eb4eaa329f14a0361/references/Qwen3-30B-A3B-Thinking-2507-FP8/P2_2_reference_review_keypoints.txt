- Tackles zero-shot learning's unrealistic assumption of perfect attribute prediction and models attribute unreliability explicitly.  
- Proposes a novel random-forest method that incorporates attribute classifier ROC statistics during training.  
- Trains trees on class attribute signatures and propagates signatures using expected false/true positive rates.  
- Extends the approach to handle signature uncertainty and a few-shot learning setting.  
- Empirically evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN with strong baselines and 20 random trials for statistical significance.  
- Ablation studies isolate the benefits of modeling attribute unreliability versus signature uncertainty.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over DAP, with gains up to ~10.2% on some categories.  
- Shows applicability to discovered (pseudo)attributes, indicating broader applicability.  
- Provides an "upper bound" analysis (OURS+TRUE ROC) to indicate potential if unseen-class error rates were known.  
- Requires attribute validation data to estimate ROC statistics, which may not reflect unseen-class error patterns.  
- Does not explore alternative ways to obtain better validation data (e.g., transfer or active learning) or robustness when validation mismatches occur.  
- Few-shot extension adds an extra tuning parameter (λ), increasing method complexity.  
- Well-written with clear figures, pseudocode, and supplementary implementation details, but lacks detailed computational complexity/time-memory analysis.  
- Novel application of ROC and random forests to zero-shot attribute unreliability; recommended for acceptance given strong technical and empirical contributions.
