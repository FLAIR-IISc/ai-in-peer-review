- Paper introduces a zero-shot random forest trained on class attribute signatures that integrates empirical ROC statistics of attribute classifiers to handle unreliable attribute predictions.
- At each tree node, class signatures are fractionally propagated to children using estimated TP/FP rates from held-out validation data, and splits are chosen by information gain over these fractional counts.
- Framework extends to model signature (class–attribute association) uncertainty and to few-shot learning by mixing signature-based and example-based information gain via a λ parameter.
- Experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN show consistent improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline, with larger gains when attributes are less reliable; ablations validate ROC-aware propagation and signature uncertainty.
- Situates work against attribute-based zero-shot methods (DAP, signature matching) and embedding/hierarchical transfer approaches (DeViSE, label embeddings).
- Novelty lies in explicitly incorporating attribute unreliability into RF training to address the training/testing mismatch between signatures and predicted attributes.
- Fractional propagation is related to trees for missing/uncertain inputs but adapted to signature→prediction mismatch at test time.
- Method is general and applicable to continuous/discovered attributes and few-shot scenarios, not tied to specific features or classifiers.
- Strength: simple, practical mechanism to inject attribute ROC into training objective addressing a key weakness of attribute-based zero-shot models.
- Strength: clear methodology with per-node ROC estimation to capture conditional dependencies induced by splits.
- Strength: solid empirical validation including controlled noise experiments and sensible few-shot behavior with λ.
- Weakness: ROC estimated on seen classes is assumed to transfer to unseen classes; sensitivity to this distribution shift is untested.
- Weakness: relies on Platt-calibrated SVM scores; miscalibration and inter-attribute correlations may undermine ROC transfer, with only partial mitigation via node-specific propagation.
- Weakness: evaluation omits comparisons to modern embedding-based zero-shot baselines (SJE/ALE/ESZSL) that could leverage attributes.
- Weakness: scalability concern—training one RF per unseen class (one-vs-all) may not scale to large K; complexity and alternatives are not discussed.
- Weakness: ambiguity about whether validation data used for ROC estimation is disjoint from attribute classifier training, risking optimistic ROC estimates.
- Requests for clarification: whether DV is disjoint from attribute training, sensitivity to miscalibration, runtime vs K and M and multi-class alternatives, details of signature uncertainty model, and adding a label-embedding baseline.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and novel, with practical impact and solid experiments; recommend accept pending clarifications on ROC estimation, broader baselines, and scalability.
