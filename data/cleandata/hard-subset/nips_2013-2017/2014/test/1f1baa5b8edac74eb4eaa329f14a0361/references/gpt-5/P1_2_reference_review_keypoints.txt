- Problem: Attribute predictors are imperfect and their errors strongly degrade attribute-based zero-shot learning (ZSL); paper proposes random-forest training on class attribute signatures while modeling attribute unreliability.
- Core method: Use validation data to estimate node-specific ROC (TPR/FPR) and fractionally propagate class signatures down both children to compute a modified information gain that favors discriminative and learnable splits.
- Extensions: Handles uncertainty in class-attribute associations (signature noise) and naturally extends to few-shot learning by mixing signature-based and data-based information gains.
- Experiments: Shows improvements over DAP and a signature-only RF baseline on AwA, aPY, SUN, plus controlled-noise studies, ablations, few-shot gains, and work with discovered pseudo-attributes.
- Relation to prior work: Differs from prior ZSL and embedding methods by explicitly modeling attribute unreliability; related to fractional/fuzzy trees but unique in using class signatures and ROC-driven fractional propagation.
- Soundness: Fractional propagation and IGzero are well-motivated; node-specific ROC estimates are a key, sensible detail; few-shot extension is straightforward and reasonable.
- Empirical support: Results, controlled experiments, and ablations collectively support claims; gains increase when attributes are less reliable.
- Limitations: No theoretical guarantees; several reproducibility-critical details are deferred to the supplement; runtime/scalability not reported; possible inconsistency about using 80% vs 100% of attribute training data.
- Clarity: Generally clear and well organized; suggestions include moving signature-uncertainty details to the main text, clarifying threshold sampling (#m, #t), tree depth and J, and the attribute training split.
- Originality: Novel contribution in training ZSL forests on signatures with node-specific ROC-driven fractional propagation and combining signature priors with few-shot data via dual IG.
- Significance: Practically important and general idea with substantial gains over DAP, though impact is moderated by reliance on attribute-labeled data and potential scalability issues for large K.
- Strengths: Simple, principled modification addressing a known ZSL failure mode; careful node-specific design and ablations; strong empirical results; works with named and discovered attributes.
- Weaknesses/concerns: Missing main-text implementation details; potential computational cost of OVA forests; limited baseline comparisons (missing embedding-based methods); risk of seen→unseen ROC shift; minor training-data inconsistency.
- Suggested improvements: Add embedding-based baselines (e.g., ALE/DeViSE), report time/memory and scalability, sensitivity analyses (validation size, λ, #t, #m, J), consider multi-class forest or justify OVA, explore mitigating ROC shift, and clarify attribute-training-split discrepancy.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important, under-explored ZSL failure mode with a principled, effective method and solid empirical evidence; idea likely to be adopted and extended.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited baselines and implementation/runtime analysis; dependence on attribute-labeled data and curated signatures may limit applicability.
- Overall recommendation: Lean accept.
