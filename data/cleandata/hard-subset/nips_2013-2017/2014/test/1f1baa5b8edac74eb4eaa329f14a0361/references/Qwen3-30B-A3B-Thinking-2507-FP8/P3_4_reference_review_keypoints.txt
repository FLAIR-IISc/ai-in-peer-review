- Identifies a critical overlooked limitation in ZSL: attribute predictions are often unreliable, yet prior methods assume they are perfect.  
- Proposes random forests that use attribute classifiers' ROC (TPR/FPR) to propagate class signatures through trees based on real prediction behavior.  
- Extends the method to handle class-signature uncertainty and provides a natural few-shot extension.  
- Differentiates clearly from approaches that abandon semantic attributes and from decision-tree methods for missing values; emphasizes unreliability vs. attribute strength.  
- Method is technically sound with clear formulation, detailed pseudocode, and thorough ablation studies.  
- Empirical results on AwA, aPY, and SUN show significant improvements over state-of-the-art, especially DAP.  
- Controlled noise experiments convincingly demonstrate robustness to attribute unreliability.  
- Theoretical justification for using ROC in this context is underdeveloped and would benefit from a more rigorous analysis.  
- Requires ~20% attribute-labeled validation data for ROC estimation; scalability and practical applicability need more discussion.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with effective figures/tables and reproducible supplementary material.  
- Lacks explicit discussion of limitations, notably robustness to distribution shift between validation and test and mismatches with unseen classes.  
- Original contribution: focusing on modeling attribute classifier unreliability is novel and impactful for ZSL.  
- Shows that modeling unreliability can outperform simply improving attribute classifiers (8-point gain over DAP with only 80% labeled data).  
- Significance: makes ZSL more practical and suggests broader implications for transfer learning and intermediate representation reliability.  
- Strengths: addresses a key limitation, simple effective solution, strong empirical gains, comprehensive ablations, useful few-shot extension.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical basis for ROC use, validation-data requirement, few-shot analysis shallow, scalability to larger attribute/class sets unclear.  
- Recommendation: accept for NeurIPS — meaningful, well-executed contribution despite noted limitations.
