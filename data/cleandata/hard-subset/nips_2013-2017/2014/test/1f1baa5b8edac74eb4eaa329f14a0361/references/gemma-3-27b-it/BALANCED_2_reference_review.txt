This paper addresses the crucial problem of unreliable attribute predictions in zero-shot learning. The core idea – to explicitly model and account for attribute prediction errors during training – is a significant and well-motivated contribution. The authors propose a random forest approach that leverages ROC curves to select discriminative and predictable decision nodes, and extend it to the few-shot setting. The experimental results on three datasets demonstrate clear advantages over existing methods, including the widely used DAP approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty and Significance:** The explicit modeling of attribute unreliability is a key insight.  Existing zero-shot methods largely treat attribute predictions as ground truth, which is unrealistic. Addressing this limitation has the potential to significantly improve the practicality of zero-shot learning.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed approach, based on random forests and ROC analysis, appears well-reasoned and technically sound. The extensions to handle signature uncertainty and few-shot learning are logical and well-integrated.  The mathematical formulation is clear and concise.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments are comprehensive, using three datasets and comparing against strong baselines. The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the contribution of each component of the proposed method. The inclusion of results with discovered attributes ([30]) strengthens the findings.
* **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clearly articulated, and the technical details are explained in a reasonably accessible manner. The connections to related work are well-established.
* **Thoroughness:** The authors address potential issues like varying attribute reliabilities and incorporate validation data to estimate ROC curves. The few-shot extension adds to the completeness of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** While not explicitly stated, the ROC estimation with validation data and the fractional propagation of signatures likely add significant computational overhead compared to standard random forest training. A discussion of the computational complexity and potential scalability issues would be beneficial.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance may be sensitive to parameters such as the tree depth, the number of trees, and the weighting parameter λ in the few-shot extension. A more detailed analysis of parameter sensitivity and guidance on parameter tuning would be helpful.
* **Validation Data Dependency:** The performance relies on the quality and representativeness of the validation data used for ROC estimation. The paper doesn't delve into how the choice of validation data affects the results, or potential strategies for mitigating the impact of a biased validation set.
* **Limited Error Analysis:** While the paper demonstrates improved accuracy, a more detailed error analysis would be valuable. Understanding *which* types of errors the method reduces (e.g., confusing specific classes) and *why* could provide further insights and guide future work.
* **ROC Estimation Practicality:**  In a real-world scenario, obtaining sufficient validation data for accurate ROC estimation for each attribute could be challenging, especially if the attribute vocabulary is large.  Addressing this practical concern is important.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant and practical problem in zero-shot learning.
* The proposed approach is novel, technically sound, and well-motivated.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods.
* The paper is well-written, organized, and provides a thorough evaluation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost and parameter sensitivity of the method are not fully addressed.
* The reliance on validation data for ROC estimation could be a limitation in certain scenarios.
* A more detailed error analysis would strengthen the conclusions.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of zero-shot learning. The proposed approach is innovative and addresses a critical limitation of existing methods. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not outweigh the significant strengths of the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding computational cost, parameter sensitivity, and the validation data dependency.  Specifically, including a discussion of computational complexity, providing guidance on parameter tuning, and briefly exploring the impact of different validation data strategies would significantly strengthen the paper.



**Specific Suggestions:**

* **Section 3.2.2:**  Elaborate on the computational cost of estimating ROC curves and propagating signatures.
* **Section 4.1:**  Include a discussion of parameter sensitivity and how the parameters were chosen.
* **Section 4.1:**  Investigate the impact of different validation data selection strategies (e.g., random sampling, stratified sampling).
* **Add an Error Analysis:** Provide a breakdown of the types of errors made by the proposed method and the baselines.
* **Discuss Scalability:**  Comment on the potential scalability issues of the approach, particularly with large attribute vocabularies and datasets.
