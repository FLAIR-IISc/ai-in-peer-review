- Paper proposes a random forest framework that explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability using ROC curves and signature uncertainty for zero-shot learning, extended to few-shot learning.  
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets, showing consistent improvements over DAP and a signature-based random forest baseline.  
- Builds on attribute-based ZSL (Lampert/Farhadi) but differs by explicitly modeling attribute prediction uncertainty; leverages soft node memberships/fractional tuples in trees.  
- Addresses a realistic limitation of ZSL by acknowledging and handling unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Novel use of ROC-guided split selection and propagation of validation data to incorporate attribute reliability into tree training.  
- Strong empirical results and controlled noise experiments that validate the approach.  
- Clear writing, well-justified design choices, and thorough connections to related work.  
- Includes useful ablation studies analyzing impacts of signature uncertainty and ROC propagation.  
- Extends framework to few-shot learning, increasing practical applicability.  
- Potential computational cost and scalability concerns due to ROC propagation and tree-building overhead.  
- Requires a separate validation dataset to estimate ROC curves, increasing data requirements.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (tree depth, tests/node, lambda) not fully explored; more analysis needed.  
- Limited exploration of alternative models for signature uncertainty beyond the simple approach used.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, offers a novel and effective solution, strong experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: concerns about computational cost, validation-data dependence, and insufficient parameter/uncertainty analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance pending minor revisions.  
- Reviewer score: 8.5/10; confidence: 9/10.
