- Tackles zero-shot learning by explicitly modeling unreliability of attribute predictions rather than treating them as ground truth.  
- Proposes a random forest method that incorporates attribute classifier ROC curves and propagates fractional class signatures during training.  
- Generalizes to handle uncertainty in class-attribute associations and extends to a few-shot learning setting.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements over baselines (including DAP) on AwA, aPY, and SUN, with convincing controlled noise experiments.  
- Positions work relative to prior ZSL and mid-level feature discovery methods, emphasizing novelty in handling attribute unreliability.  
- Connects to missing data/uncertainty handling in decision trees while clarifying the contribution specific to ZSL.  
- Novel idea: using ROC curves to inform decision tree splits and fractional signature propagation.  
- Provides clear mathematical formulation and modified information gain calculation.  
- Includes a useful few-shot extension showing method flexibility.  
- Paper is generally well-written, well-organized, and clearly notated.  
- Potential scalability/computational complexity concerns from using ROC data and fractional propagation, not fully discussed.  
- Method depends on representative validation data for accurate ROC estimation; sensitivity to validation set quality/size needs more study.  
- Ablation study is limited; more analysis of signature uncertainty vs. ROC propagation and alternative treatments would help.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., λ in few-shot, tree depth) is underexplored and needs analysis.  
- Lacks visualizations (e.g., examples of learned decision trees) that would aid interpretability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important overlooked problem, is novel, empirically strong, extends to few-shot, and is well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, validation-data dependence, and limited hyperparameter exploration.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor weaknesses that do not outweigh the merits.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address scalability, validation sensitivity, ablation, hyperparameter analysis, and add a visualization.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
