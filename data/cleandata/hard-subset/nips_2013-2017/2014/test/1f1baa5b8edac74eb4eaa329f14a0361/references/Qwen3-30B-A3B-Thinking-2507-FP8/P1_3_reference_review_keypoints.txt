- Addresses a critical gap in zero-shot learning: prior work assumes perfect attribute predictions, but attribute classifiers are unreliable in practice.  
- Proposes a novel random forest approach that uses attribute classifier ROC curves to model attribute unreliability during training.  
- Propagates class attribute signatures probabilistically through decision trees based on estimated false positive/true positive rates.  
- Extends the method to handle signature uncertainty and a natural few-shot learning variant using attribute priors.  
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN; shows significant, statistically validated improvements over DAP and other baselines.  
- Strong ablation studies and controlled noise experiments support the theoretical information-gain formulation.  
- Requires a separate validation set to estimate attribute ROCs, which may not always be available or sufficient.  
- Performance depends on the underlying attribute classifier quality and may fail when classifiers are near-random.  
- Scalability to large numbers of classes or attributes is not addressed; experiments use moderate K (10–12).  
- Assumes an expressive attribute vocabulary and does not explore cases where the vocabulary is insufficient for discrimination.  
- Few-shot extension is promising but lacks comprehensive analysis across varying labeled-data regimes.  
- Recommendation: strongly accept — technically sound, well-motivated, and practically impactful.  
- High reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
