- Proposes zero-shot random forests trained on class-level attribute signatures, not images, to handle unreliable attribute predictors.
- Trains trees with fractional propagation using node-specific ROC estimates from validation attribute data to model TP/FP rates at chosen thresholds.
- Models signature uncertainty and extends to few-shot by mixing signature-driven and supervised information gains with weight λ.
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN, showing strong gains over DAP and signature-only RF, plus controlled-noise studies and ablations.
- Also shows competitive performance using discovered (pseudo)attributes.
- Situates work relative to attribute-based zero-shot methods, embedding/transfer approaches, and discriminative mid-level discovery.
- Technically related to decision trees with missing/uncertain features, but novel in training on signatures and injecting calibrated attribute unreliability.
- Methodology is sound and well-motivated; fractional propagation based on ROC is principled and node-specific DV(n) is useful.
- Experimental support is strong: multiple datasets, >250 attributes, ablations, controlled-noise, and few-shot validation of λ.
- Main caveat: no formal guarantees and reliance on validation attribute data being representative of test-time errors for unseen classes.
- Some important implementation details (signature uncertainty modeling, threshold sampling) are in the supplement rather than main text.
- Suggest bringing signature uncertainty into the main paper, clarifying threshold sampling and sensitivity to (#m, #t), and adding complexity/scalability discussion.
- Novelty lies in node-specific ROC-driven fractional propagation during zero-shot training on signatures.
- Significant contribution: practical, interpretable fix for unreliable attributes with broad applicability and measurable SOTA improvements.
- Strengths: clear motivation, principled ROC use, strong empirical gains, informative ablations, and a useful few-shot prior.
- Weaknesses: dependence on representativeness of DV, potential scalability issues with K one-vs-all forests, and only indirect handling of attribute correlations.
- Recommend further comparisons to embedding baselines (ALE, DeViSE) and discussion of attribute predictor calibration and distribution shift remedies (e.g., importance weighting).
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to size/composition of DV, computational overhead of node-specific vs global ROC, feasibility of a single multi-class forest, and quantitative modeling of signature uncertainty.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a central problem, is novel and well-justified, and shows consistent empirical gains with clear ablations.
- Cons against acceptance: reliance on DV representativeness, some details relegated to supplement, and limited comparisons to non-attribute baselines.
- Overall recommendation: Accept; method is principled, effective, and of broad interest.
