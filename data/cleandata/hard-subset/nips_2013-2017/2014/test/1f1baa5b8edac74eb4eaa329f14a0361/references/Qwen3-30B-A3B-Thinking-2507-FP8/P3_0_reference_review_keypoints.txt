- Paper tackles unreliable attribute predictions in zero-shot learning and proposes a random-forest approach that uses attribute ROC curves for fractional signature propagation.
- Builds on the standard two-stage attribute-based zero-shot framework while retaining semantic attributes rather than abandoning them for discovered features.
- Conceptually distinct from prior uncertainty analyses by providing a practical method that integrates ROC-based unreliability into training.
- Novel and well-motivated idea: fractional propagation based on ROC is theoretically sensible and practically effective.
- Strong experimental validation across AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets with controlled-noise tests showing consistent improvements over baselines.
- Practical significance: outperforms DAP even when trained with only 80% of attribute-labeled data, suggesting modeling reliability matters more than more labels.
- Theoretically sound: information gain formulation and connection to ROC characteristics are well-explained.
- Natural and effective few-shot extension that leverages attribute signatures as priors and yields clear benefits with limited labeled data.
- Lacks theoretical justification for why ROC-based propagation is optimal versus other error metrics; needs formal argument or proofs.
- Method performance depends critically on validation data quality, with insufficient analysis of mismatched unseen-class attribute distributions.
- No thorough computational complexity or runtime analysis; recursive propagation may be costly with large attribute vocabularies.
- Limited treatment of attribute correlations despite acknowledging their importance; unclear how the method handles highly correlated attributes.
- Missing comparisons to some recent zero-shot methods (e.g., newer attribute-based work and GNN-based approaches), weakening empirical claims.
- Implementation details are insufficient for reproducibility, especially the few-shot combined information gain (Equation 6).
- Overall assessment: significant and convincing contribution that addresses a key challenge in zero-shot learning, with important but non-fatal limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Suggested improvements: provide theoretical analysis of ROC choice, study high attribute-correlation scenarios, include comparisons to recent methods, add runtime analysis, and supply detailed implementation info and discussion of validation-data limitations.
