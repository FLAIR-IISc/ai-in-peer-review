- Summary: Proposes a random forest method that models attribute unreliability using attribute classifier ROCs for zero-shot recognition, with extensions for signature uncertainty and few-shot learning.  
- Strength: Directly tackles the important practical problem of noisy attribute predictions in ZSL.  
- Strength: Novel integration of attribute classifier ROC information into random forest training.  
- Strength: Strong empirical gains over baselines (DAP, SIGNATURE-RF) on AwA, aPY, and SUN, especially when attributes are unreliable.  
- Strength: Provides theoretical rationale connecting to decision trees with missing/uncertain data (fractional tuples).  
- Strength: Logical and valuable extension to few-shot learning.  
- Strength: Well-written and organized presentation.  
- Weakness: Introduces extra methodological complexity relative to simpler ZSL methods; adoption may be hindered without computational cost analysis.  
- Weakness: Relies on a validation set to estimate attribute ROCs; performance may be sensitive to validation size and representativeness.  
- Weakness: Ablation studies are present but not deep enough (e.g., alternative ROC estimation methods, other ways to model uncertainty).  
- Weakness: Assumes a predefined attribute vocabulary; performance could suffer if the vocabulary is poor or incomplete.  
- Quality: Technically sound and complete, with experiments supporting claims.  
- Clarity: Generally clear but ROC integration details can be dense; more examples/diagrams could help.  
- Originality: Novel contribution by incorporating ROC information into random forest ZSL.  
- Significance: Addresses a key barrier to practical ZSL and shows impactful improvements.  
- Decision rationale: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational cost discussion, validation sensitivity analysis, and expanded ablation studies.
