- Tackles zero-shot recognition by explicitly modeling unreliable attribute predictions during training.
- Proposes a novel random forest approach that uses attribute ROC curves to probabilistically propagate class signatures.
- Extends the method to handle signature uncertainty and provides a few-shot learning variant.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains over baselines (DAP, SIGNATURE-RF) across three datasets, especially with noisy attributes.
- Controlled noise experiments and ablation studies convincingly isolate the impact of ROC propagation and signature uncertainty.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and grounded with a cognitive-science motivation.
- Method is relatively complex with multiple components and parameters, making implementation potentially challenging.
- Training and ROC computation may be computationally expensive and scalability is not thoroughly analyzed.
- Relies on a separate validation set to estimate ROC curves, creating sensitivity and potential bias from validation representativeness.
- Does not address the upstream problem of acquiring or selecting high-quality attribute vocabularies.
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution that makes ZSL more practical and robust.
- Arguments for acceptance: sound technical quality, clarity, originality, and demonstrated significance.
- Arguments against acceptance: complexity/compute barriers and dependence on validation data for ROC estimation.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
