- Proposes a novel random forest framework for zero-shot learning that models error tendencies of attribute classifiers.  
- Extends the method to few-shot learning by incorporating a small number of labeled images.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and a thorough related work review.  
- Technical details and mathematical notation are clear and concise.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: three standard attribute datasets, zero- and few-shot settings.  
- Includes ablation studies and artificial noise tests to assess robustness.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the evaluated datasets.  
- Model is high-quality and presents an elegant, well-suited random forest solution to unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Overall clarity is high, though the explanation of threshold t in Section 3.2.1 is initially confusing but later clarified.  
- Originality is high due to the novel application of random forests to this ZSL problem.  
- Significance is high, addressing a fundamental problem in zero-shot learning with practical impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel/effective method, clear technical presentation, comprehensive experiments, fundamental problem addressed.  
- Cons against acceptance: initial confusion over threshold t; could include more discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Overall recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance.
