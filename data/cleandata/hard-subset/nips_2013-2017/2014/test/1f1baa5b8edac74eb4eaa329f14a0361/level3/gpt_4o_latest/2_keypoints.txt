- Proposes a novel random forest-based ZSL method that explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability.  
- Integrates attribute uncertainty (true positive/false positive rates) into information gain during training.  
- Extends the approach to few-shot learning in a natural, integrated way.  
- Uses validation data to estimate attribute error tendencies and propagate uncertainty through the model.  
- Shows strong empirical results, outperforming DAP and signature-based random forests on AwA, aPY, and SUN.  
- Paper is well-written and includes ablation studies and controlled noise experiments.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis (examples of attributes or categories where the method excels).  
- Does not adequately justify parameter choices (e.g., 80/20 split, tree depths).  
- Limited discussion connecting to broader ZSL literature and other approaches to attribute uncertainty.  
- Scalability to large numbers of unseen classes or attributes is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, and empirically strong contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited interpretability, unclear parameter rationale, and scalability concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add qualitative insights and address parameter/scalability issues.
