- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning method that explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability.  
- Develops a random forest algorithm that incorporates validation-measured TPR/FPR into information gain during tree learning.  
- Approach is particularly relevant to real-world settings with noisy, unreliable attribute predictions.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and explains relation to prior zero-shot work.  
- Authors provide a thorough discussion of strengths and weaknesses and potential cross-domain applications.  
- Empirical results are strong: method outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on three challenging datasets.  
- Detailed analysis of method components and their impact is provided.  
- Novelty is a key strength: modeling attribute unreliability is a less-explored angle in zero-shot learning.  
- Potential for broad applicability across domains due to robustness to attribute noise.  
- Limitation: lacks qualitative analysis showing which attributes or categories benefit most.  
- Limitation: hyperparameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train-validation split) are not clearly justified.  
- Recommendation summary: technically sound and significant, but would benefit from deeper qualitative analysis and clearer hyperparameter rationale.
