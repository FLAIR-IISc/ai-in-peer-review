- Proposes a novel random-forest approach for zero-shot learning that explicitly models unreliability of attribute predictions.  
- Uses a validation set to estimate per-attribute classifier error tendencies and incorporates this into tree construction.  
- Results in more robust and discriminative decision boundaries for zero-shot recognition.  
- Method is extended to the few-shot setting (small number of labeled examples per class).  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on three standard attribute datasets in both zero- and few-shot settings.  
- Experimental protocol is strong: includes ablation studies, artificial noise tests, multiple settings, and comparisons to prior work.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations; model is elegant, theoretically sound, and well-executed.  
- Originality: novel application of a random-forest framework to address attribute detector reliability in zero-shot learning.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental problem with potential applicability across vision, NLP, and robotics.  
- Minor weakness: Section 3.2.1 contains a confusing explanation about threshold t (later clarified).  
- Additional weakness: could benefit from further experiments or analyses to more fully demonstrate robustness.
