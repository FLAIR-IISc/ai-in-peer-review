- Proposes zero-shot learning method that models attribute prediction unreliability by training attribute classifiers and using ROC-derived reliability in random forest construction.  
- Incorporates validation data to estimate attribute reliability at each tree node and propagate joint attribute error statistics.  
- Presents a few-shot extension that combines few labeled examples with attribute-signature-based learning.  
- Builds on established attribute-based ZSL paradigm and reframes attribute predictions as uncertain rather than ground truth.  
- Uses ROC curves from validation set to weight splits by attribute reliability during tree training.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, method description, and thorough related work.  
- Technical approach is sound; ROC-based modeling and validation-data propagation are sensible and novel in combination.  
- Experimental evaluation on AwA, aPY, and SUN is comprehensive and shows consistent improvements over strong baselines (including DAP).  
- Originality is somewhat overstated: addressing attribute uncertainty is not new, and prior methods (e.g., DAP) already handle uncertainty implicitly.  
- Should better situate contribution as improved modeling of attribute correlations/noise and cite broader literature on noise-robust learning.  
- Requires substantial validation data; recursive subdivision may produce unstable reliability estimates when validation is small.  
- The heuristic to handle small validation sets is under-justified and needs further exploration.  
- Few-shot extension is less polished; the weighted information gain formulation lacks strong motivation and alternative approaches may be preferable.  
- Quantitative gains are consistent but modest, not decisively large.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to acknowledge related work, address validation-data concerns, and strengthen few-shot justification.
