- Addresses unreliable attribute classifiers in attribute-based zero-shot learning by explicitly modeling detector unreliability during training.
- Core contribution: random forest trained on class attribute signatures with node-wise validation-set ROC estimates to fractionally propagate signatures, embedding expected test-time error.
- Extends framework to model uncertainty in class-attribute signatures and to few-shot learning by combining signature and example information gain.
- Significance: tackles a fundamental practical limitation of attribute-based ZSL, improving robustness and real-world applicability.
- Originality/technical quality: novel, elegant RF formulation that recursively accounts for attribute dependencies and detector error statistics.
- Experimental validation: strong and thorough—controlled noise experiments, SOTA gains on AwA/aPY/SUN, ablations, upper-bound ROC analysis, and few-shot results.
- Minor clarity issue: Section 3.2.1 should better explain the role of threshold t for binary signatures and when it becomes meaningful.
- Scalability concern: one-vs-all training (K forests for K classes) may be computationally prohibitive for very large numbers of unseen classes.
- Dependence on validation data: method relies on DV reflecting test-time attribute errors; sensitivity to DV size/composition is not fully analyzed.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical problem with a principled, novel solution and convincing experiments; well-written and impactful.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential scalability limitations and a small presentation clarity issue.
