- Proposes a random forest approach for zero-shot learning that explicitly models attribute prediction unreliability using validation-set ROC estimates and fractional signature propagation.  
- Method trains attribute classifiers, then builds trees on class-attribute signature vectors while estimating attribute reliability at each node from a validation set.  
- Introduces a few-shot extension that combines information gain from signatures and labeled training images via a weighting parameter (Equation 6).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with a comprehensive related-work overview.  
- Core idea builds on the two-stage attribute-prediction paradigm; novelty is handling attribute unreliability during random forest training via ROC-based propagation.  
- Experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN show consistent (often modest) improvements over baselines including DAP, and controlled noise tests show robustness to attribute errors.  
- Strengths: technically sound, clear exposition, strong empirical evaluation, and insightful analysis of attribute reliability’s impact.  
- Claim of substantial novelty is overstated—prior methods like DAP already address attribute uncertainty; the paper’s technical differences are incremental.  
- Needs clearer comparison and acknowledgement of related methods for uncertain data (e.g., Scheirer et al. 2011) and feature-noise handling techniques.  
- Reliance on a sizable validation dataset is a potential weakness; recursive validation splitting may require more labeled data than typical and the proposed heuristic is under-justified.  
- Few-shot extension is underdeveloped; the weighted information-gain formulation is unintuitive and lacks analysis of λ sensitivity and effect of sample size.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sound execution, clear presentation, consistent empirical gains, and valuable uncertainty-aware tree construction idea.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable novelty, validation-data dependency, underdeveloped few-shot analysis, and non-decisive quantitative gains.  
- Overall recommendation: accept as a solid, incremental contribution contingent on addressing novelty claims and validation-data concerns in the camera-ready.
