- Proposes a novel random-forest method for zero-shot learning that explicitly models attribute prediction unreliability by incorporating attribute ROC (TPR/FPR) into split selection.  
- Incorporates handling of unreliable class–attribute associations and extends the framework to few-shot learning.  
- Evaluated on AwA, aPY, and SUN datasets, showing consistent improvements over state-of-the-art methods, especially Direct Attribute Prediction (DAP).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and convincingly motivated to address attribute unreliability in ZSL.  
- Authors position the work well within existing literature and cite relevant prior work.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, including strong baselines and ablation studies.  
- Results are strong and demonstrate particular benefits in scenarios with noisy attribute predictions.  
- Novelty: explicitly modeling attribute reliability (not just strength/confidence) is a less-explored and sensible extension.  
- Significance: addressing attribute unreliability improves practical applicability of zero-shot learning; few-shot extension increases relevance.  
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis showing which attributes/categories benefit most and concrete examples of improved predictions.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter selection (e.g., 80/20 split, tree depth, number of trees) and sensitivity analysis for reproducibility.  
- Suggestion: briefly discuss connections to Bayesian or other uncertainty-modeling approaches to contextualize the ROC-based technique.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant problem addressed, novel and well-motivated method, strong experimental evidence, clear writing, and useful few-shot extension.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing qualitative insights, incomplete hyperparameter explanation, and limited discussion of broader uncertainty modeling literature.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add qualitative analysis and more hyperparameter details.
