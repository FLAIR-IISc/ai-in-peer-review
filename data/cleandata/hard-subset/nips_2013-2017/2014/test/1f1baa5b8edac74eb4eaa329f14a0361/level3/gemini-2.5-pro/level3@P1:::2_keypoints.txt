- Addresses unreliability of attribute classifiers in attribute-based zero-shot learning and brittleness of the standard two-stage ZSL pipeline.
- Core contribution: a random forest framework that models attribute classifier error (TPR/FPR) during training using ROC statistics from a validation set.
- Training modifies information gain by fractionally propagating class signatures down tree paths weighted by attribute classifier error rates.
- Encourages splits that are discriminative for class signatures and robust to expected test-time attribute prediction errors.
- Extensions presented for uncertainty in class signatures and for few-shot learning.
- Distinguishes from DAP and other prior work by explicitly modeling classifier error tendencies rather than only attribute presence or strength.
- Relates fractional propagation to missing-value handling in decision trees but argues novelty in applying it to signature-vs-classifier-output mismatch.
- Strength: well-motivated and novel idea to incorporate classifier error statistics directly into the learning objective.
- Strength: strong empirical validation on AwA, aPY, SUN with controlled noise experiments, outperforming DAP and supported by ablation studies.
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and articulates relation to related work.
- Weakness: lacks qualitative analysis (e.g., visualizing top splits or per-class error patterns) to explain why the method favors more learnable attributes.
- Weakness: insufficient justification and sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters, notably the 80/20 attribute train/validation split.
- Weakness: relies on ROC estimates from seen classes with implicit assumption they generalize to unseen classes; “OURS+TRUE ROC” gap highlights this limitation.
- Requests/questions: include visuals/tables of top attributes selected by proposed method vs SIGNATURE-RF to show prioritization of robust attributes.
- Requests/questions: justify choice of 80/20 split and report sensitivity (e.g., 90/10 or 70/30) to demonstrate robustness.
- Requests/questions: clarify how λ was cross-validated in few-shot experiments given very few examples per class (e.g., leave-one-out or other procedure).
