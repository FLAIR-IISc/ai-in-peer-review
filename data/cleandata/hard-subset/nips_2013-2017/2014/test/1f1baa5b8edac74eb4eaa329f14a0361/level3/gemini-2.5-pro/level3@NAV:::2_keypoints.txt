- Proposes a ZSL method that explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability by incorporating validation-measured TPR/FPR into random forest information gain.  
- Novel focus on attribute reliability distinguishes it from prior attribute-based ZSL work.  
- Clear motivation and well-written manuscript.  
- Strong technical quality and sound methodology.  
- Comprehensive experiments on AwA, aPY, and SUN showing consistent, significant improvements over DAP and other SOTA methods.  
- Ablation studies effectively demonstrate each component’s contribution.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis showing which attributes or categories benefit most from the reliability modeling.  
- Methodological choices (e.g., 80/20 train-validation split) are not well-justified, impacting completeness and reproducibility.  
- Overall, a valuable, pragmatic contribution likely to impact attribute-based ZSL despite minor analysis and explanation gaps.
