- Proposes a novel, statistically grounded approach to weighted expert voting, revisiting a classical decision-theoretic problem.  
- Analyzes consistency of the optimal Nitzan-Paroush weighted majority rule.  
- Provides sharp error estimates for the optimal rule when expert competence levels are known.  
- Addresses unknown competence levels with both frequentist and Bayesian analyses.  
- Models attribute unreliability explicitly, arguing this improves zero-shot learning performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Empirical analysis supports the proposed ideas but qualitative analysis is limited.  
- Lacks clarity and detailed explanation on parameter selection.  
- Thorough theoretical development with multiple theorems, lemmas, and related-work discussion.  
- Significant contribution: better handling of a difficult problem and raising challenging open problems (e.g., Bayesian empirical decision rule error computation).  
- Originality lies in combining familiar techniques under a statistical learning perspective for this problem.  
- Accessibility concern: some notation and terminology may be unfamiliar to readers without strong statistical learning/decision-theory background.
