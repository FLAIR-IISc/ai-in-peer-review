- Problem: addresses unreliability of attribute classifiers in attribute-based zero-shot learning (ZSL).  
- Method: trains a random forest on attribute signatures of unseen classes instead of images.  
- Key innovation: propagates an attribute-labeled validation set through trees to model detector fallibility.  
- Split selection: chooses splits based on both discriminative power and expected reliability using node-specific ROC statistics.  
- Contrast: avoids assuming perfect attribute predictions, unlike methods such as Direct Attribute Prediction (DAP).  
- Extension: naturally extends to the few-shot learning setting.  
- Strength — significance: tackles a major practical bottleneck, improving real-world ZSL robustness.  
- Strength — quality & originality: elegant, theoretically sound use of random forests and validation-based reliability; strong ablation studies and controlled-noise experiments.  
- Strength — empirical: achieves state-of-the-art results on three standard datasets with rigorous evaluation.  
- Strength — completeness: full problem formulation, method, and extensive evaluation provided.  
- Weakness — clarity: minor confusion about the role and selection of threshold t for binary signatures in Section 3.2.1.  
- Overall: high-quality, well-supported contribution that is highly suitable for publication.
