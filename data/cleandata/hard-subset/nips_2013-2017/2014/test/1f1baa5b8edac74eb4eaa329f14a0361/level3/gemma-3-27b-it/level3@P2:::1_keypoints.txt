- Addresses zero-shot learning's key problem: attribute prediction unreliability in real-world classifiers.  
- Proposes a novel random forest trained on class attribute signatures rather than image features.  
- Training incorporates attribute classifier error tendencies via a validation set that propagates uncertainty through trees.  
- Learns splits that are robust to noisy attributes and prioritizes more reliable attributes.  
- Extends naturally to few-shot learning by combining signature-based priors with limited labeled data.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements over baselines (including DAP) on three standard datasets.  
- Includes thorough experiments and ablations, including controlled noise tests and signature uncertainty analysis.  
- Positions work clearly within prior attribute-based zero-shot literature and related probabilistic/missing-feature methods.  
- Technically sound: random forests are a natural fit and the reliability modeling is well-motivated.  
- Paper is generally well-written with a logical flow and clear exposition.  
- Minor clarity issue: initial explanation of threshold 't' (Section 3.2.1) is confusing until later clarification.  
- Does not explicitly model inter-attribute correlations, which could limit performance.  
- Validation data propagation description could benefit from an intuitive example or diagram.  
- Reviewer judgment: novel, significant, and empirically validated—strong recommendation to accept.
