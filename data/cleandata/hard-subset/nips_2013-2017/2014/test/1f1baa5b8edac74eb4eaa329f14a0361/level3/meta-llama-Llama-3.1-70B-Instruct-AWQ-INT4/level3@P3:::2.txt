This paper proposes a novel approach to zero-shot learning that explicitly models the unreliability of attribute classifiers. The authors develop a random forest algorithm that incorporates attribute classifier uncertainty by using validation-measured performance (TPR/FPR) in information gain during tree learning. This approach is particularly relevant in real-world settings where attribute predictions are often noisy and unreliable.

The paper is well-written and easy to read, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its relation to previous work in zero-shot learning. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach, including its ability to handle attribute classifier unreliability and its potential applications to other domains.

The empirical results presented in the paper are impressive, with the proposed method outperforming several state-of-the-art baselines on three challenging datasets. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the results, including an examination of the impact of different components of their method.

One of the key strengths of this paper is its novelty. The authors propose a new approach to modeling attribute classifier uncertainty, which is a less-explored angle in zero-shot learning.
