- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning method that models attribute prediction error tendencies.  
- Develops a random forest algorithm that uses attribute classifier ROC curves to choose discriminative and predictable split nodes.  
- Extends the approach to few-shot learning by incorporating a small number of labeled examples.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a thorough introduction and background.  
- Provides a comprehensive related work review, highlighting limitations of prior methods.  
- High technical quality with a sound, well-described methodology and training procedure.  
- Thorough, well-designed experiments with clear evaluation protocol and state-of-the-art comparisons.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods on three challenging datasets.  
- Includes analysis of method components, such as signature uncertainty modeling and recursive validation propagation.  
- Clarity is generally good, but the explanation of threshold t in Section 3.2.1 is initially confusing (later clarified).  
- High originality: novel application of random forests to zero-shot learning and a unique solution for attribute unreliability.  
- High significance: addresses a fundamental problem in zero-shot/few-shot learning with broad application implications.  
- Main strengths: technical soundness, thorough experiments, and impactful results.  
- Main weaknesses: minor explanatory clarity issue for threshold t and lacks detailed computational complexity analysis.
