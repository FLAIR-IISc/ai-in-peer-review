- Novelty: Trains random forests on class-attribute signatures and explicitly models attribute detector unreliability via node-specific ROC-driven fractional propagation.
- Method: At each split, class signatures are fractionally sent to child nodes using node-specific TPR/FPR from a held-out attribute-labeled validation set; information gain is modified for fractional signatures.
- Extensions: Naturally handles uncertain class signatures and combines signature-based and image-based gains for few-shot learning via a mixing parameter.

- Strength: Clear, principled formulation—fractional propagation guided by node-specific ROC curves intuitively compensates for detector errors.
- Strength: Strong empirical evidence across AwA, aPY, SUN with thorough ablations, controlled noise experiments, and few-shot analysis.
- Strength: Practical relevance—improves both zero-shot and few-shot performance; signature priors help even with many labeled examples.
- Strength: Careful experimental protocol—20 trials, standard splits/features, validation for ROCs, sensitivity analyses.

- Weakness: Requires attribute-labeled validation data and assumes ROC transfer from seen to unseen classes; domain-shift effects need more analysis.
- Weakness: Node-level ROC estimates can be unstable when few validation samples reach deep nodes; fallback to global DV needs reporting (frequency/impact/sensitivity to 10-sample threshold).
- Weakness: Scalability concerns—one-vs-all forests per unseen class tested up to K=12; training/test time and complexity for large K (e.g., 100) unclear.
- Weakness: Clarity issues—role of threshold t in Sec.3.2.1 and deferred signature-uncertainty description reduce readability.
- Weakness: Comparisons limited beyond DAP; comparisons to label/feature embedding baselines would strengthen claims.

- Quality assessment: Technically sound, well-motivated modified information gain, and experiments convincingly support claims.
- Clarity assessment: Generally well written but minor clarity issues and some supplemental details should be moved into main text.
- Originality assessment: Novel adaptation of RFs to ZSL with node-wise ROC fractional propagation and few-shot combination.
- Significance assessment: Addresses a key practical problem in attribute-based ZSL with consistent gains, likely to influence future work.

- Question: How often does the DV(n)→DV fallback occur and how sensitive are results to the 10-sample threshold?
- Question: Report training/test time and memory as a function of M and K; any tricks for reducing per-class forests (shared/multi-label trees)?
- Question: Did you try weighting attributes by calibration quality (e.g., AUC) when sampling candidate splits?
- Question: How sensitive are results to the proportion of data reserved for DV (20%)?

- Pros for acceptance: Conceptually clean solution to attribute unreliability, strong consistent empirical gains, extends to few-shot, and is timely for transfer/recognition research.
- Cons for acceptance: Relies on representativeness of attribute-labeled validation data, unclear scalability to many classes/deep trees, and some details deferred to supplement.

- Overall recommendation: Solid, well-executed contribution; recommendation to accept.
