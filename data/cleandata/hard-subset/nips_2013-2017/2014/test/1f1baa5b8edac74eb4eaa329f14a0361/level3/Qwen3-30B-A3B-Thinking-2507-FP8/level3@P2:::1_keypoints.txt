- Problem: Addresses unreliability of attribute predictions in attribute-based zero-shot learning, which existing methods assume to be perfect.  
- Proposed method: Novel random forest approach that explicitly models attribute prediction unreliability using attribute error statistics during training.  
- Key technique: Propagates validation data through decision trees to simulate how attribute classifier errors affect final classification.  
- Extensions: Method handles signature uncertainty and includes a logical few-shot learning extension.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on DAP but fills the gap that DAP ignores attribute predictor unreliability; distinct from missing-feature methods since training uses only attribute signatures.  
- Technical quality: Formulation is rigorous and theoretically sound.  
- Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation across multiple datasets with 20 random trials and comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Controlled experiments: Controlled noise experiments effectively show robustness to varying attribute unreliability.  
- Ablation studies: Ablations demonstrate that both signature uncertainty modeling and node-specific ROC propagation are essential.  
- Few-shot validation: Few-shot extension is consistent and well-validated.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written and reproducible for experts, though Section 3.2.1’s threshold t explanation is confusing until clarified later.  
- Originality: Novel and creative application of random forests to zero-shot learning with principled handling of attribute errors.  
- Significance: Makes attribute-based zero-shot learning more practical and achieves state-of-the-art results on standard datasets, improving applicability to rare/emerging categories.  
- Strengths: Elegant, practical, theoretically grounded method with strong empirical support and thorough evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: Could better justify superiority over simply improving attribute classifiers and more strongly link design to specific attribute error characteristics.  
- Practical constraint: Requires a separate validation set for attribute classifiers, which may be viewed as an extra requirement.  
- Acceptance rationale: Addresses a fundamental overlooked limitation, offers a principled novel solution, comprehensive experiments, and extends to few-shot — reviewer recommends acceptance.
