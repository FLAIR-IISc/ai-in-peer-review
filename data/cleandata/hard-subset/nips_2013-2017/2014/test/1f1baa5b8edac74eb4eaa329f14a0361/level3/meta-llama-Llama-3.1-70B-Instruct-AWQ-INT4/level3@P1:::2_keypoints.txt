- Introduces Zero-Shot Random Forests (ZSRF) that incorporate attribute classifier uncertainty into a random forest framework.  
- Uses validation-measured TPR/FPR in information gain to account for attribute prediction unreliability.  
- Method is thoroughly explained, including attribute vocabulary, signatures, and learning procedure.  
- Empirical evaluation on three challenging datasets demonstrates effectiveness in zero-shot object and scene recognition.  
- Strength: Well-motivated approach addressing the unreliability of attribute predictions in zero-shot learning.  
- Strength: Comprehensive quantitative analysis across multiple datasets showing robustness.  
- Strength: Clear and concise exposition that makes the method easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Lacks qualitative analysis of which attributes and categories specifically benefit from the method.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train-validation split) are not clearly justified.  
- Weakness: Related work section could be more concise and better focused on the most relevant contributions.  
- Novelty: Explicit modeling of attribute reliability is a less-explored and valuable angle; random forest component itself is not novel.  
- Significance: Contribution improves zero-shot learning by incorporating attribute classifier uncertainty.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, well-motivated, empirically supported, and clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Missing qualitative insights, insufficient justification of hyperparameters, and a bloated related work section.  
- Overall: Valuable contribution appropriate for a top-tier venue, but would be strengthened by addressing the noted weaknesses.
