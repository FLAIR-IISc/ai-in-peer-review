- Presents a comprehensive analysis of consistency of weighted majority votes for attribute-based zero-shot learning.
- Proposes a random-forest-based method to predict unseen classes from attribute vectors accounting for attribute-detector reliability.
- Paper is well-written, elegant, and theoretically sound.
- Strong empirical results on three standard datasets with thorough ablation studies.
- Effectively addresses a fundamental problem in zero-shot learning.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on several attribute datasets and can extend to few-shot training of test categories.
- Provides a thorough analysis of the Nitzan-Paroush optimal decision rule with sharp error estimates.
- Offers two solutions for unknown expert competences: a frequentist approach and a Bayesian approach.
- Assumes expert competences are known or can be accurately estimated, which may not hold in practice.
- Does not provide a non-trivial estimate for the error probability induced by the Bayesian empirical decision rule.
- Lacks sufficient discussion of practical implications and potential applications of the method.
- Overall assessment: suitable for publication in a top-tier conference (NIPS).
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address practical implications and provide (if possible) a non-trivial Bayesian error estimate.
