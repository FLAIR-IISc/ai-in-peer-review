- Proposes a random-forest method for zero-shot learning that models attribute prediction unreliability using SVM attribute classifiers, class-attribute signatures, and validation-data-driven reliability estimates per tree node; includes a few-shot extension.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the approach, with a good related-work summary.  
- Core idea is technically sound; experiments (including controlled-noise tests) support robustness claims.  
- Uses ROC-based reliability estimates and propagates validation data down trees to capture joint attribute error statistics—clever technical contributions.  
- Few-shot extension broadens applicability but is less polished than the zero-shot part.  
- Novelty is modest; claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated because DAP already uses validation data to address attribute uncertainty.  
- Should reframe contribution as improved modeling of attribute correlations/noise rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Needs more explicit comparison/citation of prior work on noisy/uncertain features in decision trees (e.g., fractional tuples [21], probabilistic feature representations [28]).  
- Potential practical limitation: method may require a large validation set since validation data is subdivided down trees; proposed heuristic (line 323) is insufficiently justified.  
- Impact of limited validation data on performance is not thoroughly investigated and should be evaluated.  
- Quantitative gains over baselines, particularly DAP, are modest and not always decisive despite some statistical significance.  
- Few-shot weighted information-gain formulation (Eq. 6) is less intuitive; sensitivity of λ and cross-validation selection are underexplored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, addresses an important problem, provides an incremental novelty, and shows experimental robustness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited core novelty, validation-data requirement, modest quantitative improvements, and underdeveloped few-shot component.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting clearer contrast with DAP/noisy-feature work, justification and evaluation of the validation heuristic, analysis of limited validation data, and expanded few-shot discussion including λ analysis.
