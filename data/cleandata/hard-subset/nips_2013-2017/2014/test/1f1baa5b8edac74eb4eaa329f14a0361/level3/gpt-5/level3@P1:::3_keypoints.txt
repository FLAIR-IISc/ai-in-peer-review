- Paper proposes zero-shot recognition via class-level attribute-signature random forests that model attribute unreliability using node-specific ROC statistics.
- Pipeline: train attribute SVMs, grow class-specific trees on signatures, estimate per-node ROC from validation data, propagate signatures fractionally, and redefine information gain using soft counts.
- At test time, image attribute predictions route down trees to produce class posteriors; extensions include signature-uncertainty and a few-shot IG blend.
- Positioned within two-stage attribute-based zero-shot methods (e.g., DAP) but focuses on selecting splits robust to observed attribute errors rather than assuming independence.
- Connects to decision trees for uncertain/missing inputs but adapts fractional propagation to train on signatures and test on noisy attribute outputs.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with thorough related work and easy-to-follow experiments.
- Strength: principled ROC-based fractional propagation and information gain that reward learnable, reliable splits.
- Strength: propagating validation data captures node-specific attribute dependencies as a useful insight.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across three datasets, ablations, noise studies, and discovered-attribute results.
- Strength: simple, practical few-shot extension with intuitively behaving cross-validated λ.
- Weakness: node-specific ROC estimates can become sparse deep in trees; the fallback to global ROC (<10 samples) is heuristic and insufficiently justified.
- Weakness: signature-uncertainty model relegated to supplement; core equations and intuition should be in the main paper.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental — prior work models attribute uncertainty and uses ROC/soft trees; claim of unique handling of unreliability is overstated.
- Weakness: improvements are consistent but often modest; statistical significance tests and stronger baselines are needed.
- Experimental suggestions: analyze sensitivity to validation size, per-node minimum counts, tree depth, and report frequency/effect of global-ROC fallback.
- Experimental suggestions: compare to calibrated attribute-probability baselines (Platt/Isotonic) and expected IG under simple error models to isolate node-ROC value.
- Experimental suggestions: explore joint/confusion-matrix error models or low-rank shared error structures to mitigate sparsity.
- Few-shot suggestions: consider Bayesian pseudo-count priors from signatures, joint routing of signatures and images, or fine-tuned DAP baselines instead of the ad hoc λ mixture.
- Broader context: strengthen discussion linking to ROC operating-point selection and open-set/reliability-aware recognition (e.g., Scheirer et al. CVPR 2013).
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, practical improvement with insightful node-wise ROC use and consistent empirical gains.
- Arguments against acceptance: modest novelty, validation-data dependence and heuristic fallbacks, modest gains without significance tests, and a brief few-shot treatment.
- Overall recommendation: borderline — accept if claims are moderated and further analysis of validation dependence and few-shot baselines is added; otherwise borderline reject.
