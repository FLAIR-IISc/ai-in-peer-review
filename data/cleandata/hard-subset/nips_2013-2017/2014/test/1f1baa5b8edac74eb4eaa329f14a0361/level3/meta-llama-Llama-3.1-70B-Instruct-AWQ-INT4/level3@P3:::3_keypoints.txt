- Proposes a random-forest approach for zero-shot recognition that explicitly models unreliability of attribute predictions.  
- Method leverages ROC-based attribute reliability statistics and propagates validation data down trees to capture joint attribute error correlations.  
- Demonstrated significant empirical gains over DAP and SIGNATURE-RF on three large object/scene datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and summarizes related work clearly.  
- Key contribution: random-forest model that yields more robust discriminative models for unseen classes by accounting for attribute classifier unreliability.  
- Extension to few-shot learning is provided but is brief and less polished.  
- Novelty is modest; similar goals (handling attribute uncertainty) are addressed by prior methods like DAP.  
- Differences from prior work mainly: ROC-based reliability estimates vs. Platt-sigmoid and effective capture of joint errors via tree propagation.  
- Claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated and should be toned down.  
- Missing citation/context: should cite related work such as Scheirer et al. for completeness.  
- Requires a large validation set due to data subdivision in trees; proposed heuristic fix is insufficiently justified.  
- Few-shot weighted information-gain (Eq. 6) feels unintuitive; alternative formulations and more design/trade-off details are needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address novelty claims, validation-set concerns, few-shot design choices, and related-work citations.
