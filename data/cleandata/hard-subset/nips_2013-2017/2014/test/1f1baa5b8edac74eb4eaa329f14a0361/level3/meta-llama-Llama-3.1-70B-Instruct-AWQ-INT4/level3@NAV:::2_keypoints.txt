- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning method that models attribute unreliability using a random forest with validation-measured TPR/FPR in information gain.
- Method significantly improves over existing approaches that assume attribute predictions are reliable.
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and provides a thorough background on zero-shot learning and attribute-based recognition.
- Method description is detailed, including use of validation data to estimate attribute classifier error statistics.
- Empirical results are impressive across three challenging datasets, with thorough analysis, ablation studies, and comparisons to state of the art.
- Novelty: explicitly modeling attribute reliability is a less-explored and impactful angle in zero-shot learning.
- Limitation: lacks qualitative analysis showing which attributes and categories benefit from the method.
- Limitation: hyperparameter selection process is unclear, including no rationale for the 80/20 train-validation split.
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and demonstrates state-of-the-art improvements, making a valuable contribution.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify hyperparameter selection and add qualitative analysis.
- Rating: 8/10.
