- Problem: addresses unreliability of attribute detectors in attribute-based zero-shot learning.  
- Method: novel random forest trained on attribute signatures of unseen classes instead of image features.  
- Error modeling: incorporates attribute classifier error tendencies via ROC estimates from a validation set during training.  
- Robustness: ROC propagation lets trees learn decision boundaries less sensitive to noisy attribute predictions.  
- Few-shot extension: method naturally extends to incorporate a small number of labeled examples.  
- Experiments: comprehensive evaluation on AwA, aPY, and SUN, with comparisons to baselines including DAP.  
- Ablations: studies isolate contributions of signature uncertainty and ROC propagation.  
- Controlled-noise tests: controlled experiments convincingly show benefits of modeling attribute unreliability.  
- Results: consistently strong zero- and few-shot performance, competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Writing: generally clear and well-organized; initial explanation of threshold 't' (Sec 3.2.1) needs clarification.  
- Suggestion: add more detailed pseudocode or a visual illustration of the training process.  
- Originality: thoughtful adaptation of random forests with ROC-based propagation to address attribute uncertainty.  
- Significance: improves practicality of zero-shot learning with imperfect detectors and has potential broad impact.  
- Minor cons: could evaluate on more diverse datasets/attribute types; clarify threshold description.  
- Overall: high-quality, technically sound work; strong recommendation for acceptance.
