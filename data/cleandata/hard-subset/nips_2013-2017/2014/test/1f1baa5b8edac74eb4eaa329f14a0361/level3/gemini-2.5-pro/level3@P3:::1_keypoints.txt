- Addresses unreliability of attribute classifiers in attribute-based zero-shot learning (ZSL) and its impact on standard two-stage methods like DAP.  
- Core contribution: a random-forest framework that trains trees on class attribute signatures instead of images.  
- Key innovation: propagate a validation set of attribute-labeled images during tree-building to compute node-specific ROC stats and account for attribute unreliability.  
- Uses information gain that favors splits both discriminative for class signatures and reliable under expected test-time classifier behavior.  
- Extends to handle uncertainty in class signatures and naturally incorporates few-shot learning by combining signature priors with labeled examples.  
- Strong experimental protocol: controlled synthetic-noise experiments provide clear empirical validation of the method’s mechanism.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on AwA, aPY, and SUN, significantly outperforming DAP.  
- Thorough ablation studies that isolate contributions of signature uncertainty and recursive ROC propagation.  
- Few-shot extension is effective, outperforming training on few examples alone.  
- Originality: novel and appropriate application of random forests and local ROC-based reliability estimation to ZSL; adaptation of fractional-instance ideas is non-trivial and well-tailored.  
- Minor clarity issue: Section 3.2.1 could better explain threshold t’s role (irrelevant for binary signatures but critical for real-valued classifier scores) to avoid reader confusion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, practically significant, well-executed—strongly recommend acceptance.
