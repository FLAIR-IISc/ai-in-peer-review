- Proposes modeling attribute classifier uncertainty via validation-measured TPR/FPR in random forest information gain.
- Key insight: incorporate ROC/operating points into tree learning so the model prefers more reliable attributes/operating points.
- Addresses a gap: existing zero-shot methods assume reliable attribute predictions, but classifiers are often error-prone.
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements on three datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art methods like DAP.
- Extends method to few-shot learning in a well-motivated and effective way.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.
- Strong technical quality with well-supported experiments and a novel perspective distinct from attribute strength.
- High significance: shows modeling attribute unreliability can be more valuable than merely improving attribute classifiers and may generalize to other semantic learning scenarios.
- Lacks qualitative analysis showing which specific attributes and categories benefit most.
- Unclear rationale for the 80/20 train-validation split and no explanation of hyperparameter selection/validation.
- Random forest component itself is not novel, though its application to uncertainty-aware zero-shot learning is innovative.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter rationale and add qualitative analysis.
