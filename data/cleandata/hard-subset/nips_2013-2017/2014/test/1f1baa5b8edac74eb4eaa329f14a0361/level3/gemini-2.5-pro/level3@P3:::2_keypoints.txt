- Problem & core idea: Addresses zero-shot learning degraded by unreliable attribute classifiers by training random forests that account for attribute unreliability.  
- Novel technique: Modifies information gain to fractionally propagate class attribute signatures based on attribute classifier TPR/FPR.  
- Validation strategy: TPR/FPR are estimated on a held-out attribute-labeled validation set to guide tree building.  
- Extensions: Framework handles uncertainty in class signatures and adapts to few-shot learning.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on two-stage attribute-based ZSL (e.g., DAP) but uniquely models classifier reliability rather than just attribute strength.  
- Originality & significance: Novel, insightful idea with potential applicability to other pipelines of noisy classifiers.  
- Technical quality & experiments: Method is sound with extensive empirical gains on AwA, aPY, SUN, controlled noise experiments, and informative ablations.  
- Clarity: Paper is very well-written, well-organized, and clearly presented.  
- Weakness — qualitative analysis: Lacks inspection of which attributes the model trusts/avoids and per-category qualitative explanations for gains.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter justification: No rationale or sensitivity analysis for the 80/20 train/validation split used to estimate ROC statistics.  
- Overall summary: Strong, well-executed contribution to ZSL; strengths outweigh the listed weaknesses.
