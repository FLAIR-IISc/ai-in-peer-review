- Proposes a random-forest method for zero-shot recognition that models unreliable attribute predictions using ROC-based reliability estimates during tree construction.
- Trains SVM attribute classifiers and builds forests from class-attribute signature vectors, propagating validation data down nodes to estimate attribute error tendencies.
- Extends the method to few-shot learning with a weighted information gain combination (Eq. 6).
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear technical exposition and a solid related-work summary.
- Method is technically sound and experiments are properly designed across three benchmark datasets.
- Shows consistent improvements over baselines (e.g., DAP, SIGNATURE RF), especially when attribute classifiers are less reliable.
- Novelty is overstated: using validation data to handle attribute uncertainty has been explored before (e.g., DAP and related methods).
- The main technical contribution (ROC-based propagation vs. Platt-sigmoid) is meaningful but modest; should be framed as improved modeling of attribute noise/correlations rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Important prior work (e.g., Scheirer et al. 2013) addressing similar issues should be cited.
- Validation-data requirement is concerning: subdividing validation examples per tree node may demand a large validation set, and the proposed heuristic fix is insufficiently justified.
- Few-shot extension is weaker and less intuitive than the zero-shot approach; the weighted information gain feels ad hoc.
- Quantitative gains are modest (e.g., 8–10% on some categories) and the paper does not convincingly demonstrate transformative impact for NIPS-level novelty.
