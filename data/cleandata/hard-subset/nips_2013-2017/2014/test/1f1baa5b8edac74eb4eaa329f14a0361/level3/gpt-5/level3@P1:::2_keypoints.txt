- Tackles attribute-based ZSL limitation: test-time attribute predictions are error-prone.
- Proposes a random-forest trained on class signatures that models attribute unreliability using validation-measured TPR/FPR.
- Uses held-out attribute-labeled validation set to estimate per-attribute ROCs and candidate thresholds.
- Fractionally propagates class signatures through tree nodes using ROC estimates and redefines information gain to favor reliable discriminative splits.
- Adds modeling of signature uncertainty (class-attribute noise).
- Extends to few-shot learning by mixing signature-based and data-based information gain with parameter λ.
- Empirical results: consistent improvements over DAP and signature-only RF on AwA, aPY, and SUN.
- Controlled noise analyses and ablations demonstrate benefit of modeling attribute unreliability and node-specific ROC propagation.
- Works with both named and discovered (pseudo-)attributes.
- Few-shot extension shows sensible gains as labeled images increase and λ behaves intuitively under cross-validation.
- Novelty: embedding attribute unreliability into RF training with node-specific ROC-informed fractional propagation is new for attribute ZSL.
- Technical soundness: coherent formulations (fractional propagation, IG variants) and thoughtful use of DV(n) to capture dependencies.
- Weakness: limited qualitative analysis of which attributes/thresholds are selected and per-class effects.
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices (80/20 ROC split, tree depth, #m/#t) lack justification and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: need clarity on ROC estimation sequencing to avoid using DV for training attribute classifiers and on SVM probability calibration sensitivity.
- Weakness: computational/scalability concerns—node-specific ROC computations may be costly for large M; no complexity or runtime analysis.
- Weakness: limited explicit handling of correlated attributes; benefit of node-specific vs global ROCs needs more empirical backing in correlated settings.
- Weakness: domain shift between DV (seen classes) and unseen classes not fully addressed; “TRUE ROC” shows room for improvement.
- Clarity: paper is well-written but should clarify attribute training/validation schedule, hyperparameter selection, and threshold sampling; pseudocode/code release recommended.
- Suggestions: add qualitative analyses, sensitivity studies for DV size/tree depth/#m/#t/λ, runtime/complexity, and calibration metrics (e.g., ECE).
- Suggestions: explore simple strategies to mitigate DV–unseen domain shift (importance weighting or DV selection by signature similarity).
- Overall assessment: strong, practical contribution with convincing experiments; recommend acceptance despite missing analyses.
