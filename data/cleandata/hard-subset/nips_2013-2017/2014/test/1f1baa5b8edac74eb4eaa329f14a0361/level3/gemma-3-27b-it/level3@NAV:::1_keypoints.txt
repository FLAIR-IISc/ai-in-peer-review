- Problem: Addresses unreliability of attribute detectors in attribute-based zero-shot learning.
- Contribution: Proposes a novel random forest approach that explicitly models attribute unreliability during training.
- Mechanism: Propagates validation data through trees to capture attribute error tendencies.
- Extension: Method extends naturally to the few-shot setting.
- Novel perspective: Focuses on attribute prediction reliability rather than just prediction strength.
- Adaptation: Creative and appropriate use of random forests for zero-shot learning.
- Theoretical quality: Method is theoretically sound and elegantly presented.
- Experimental validation: Strong experiments (ablation, controlled noise) on AwA, aPY, SUN showing consistent improvements, especially with low-quality attribute classifiers.
- Clarification: Section 3.2.1’s explanation of threshold t is initially confusing but clarified later.
- Weakness: Relies on a validation set to estimate attribute reliability, adding data requirements.
- Weakness: Computational complexity of the approach is not fully analyzed.
- Overall verdict: High-quality work, significant contribution, reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
