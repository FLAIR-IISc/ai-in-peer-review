- Proposes a zero-shot recognition method that explicitly models unreliability of attribute predictions.
- Uses a random forest that leverages per-attribute error statistics to choose discriminative and predictable decision nodes.
- Pipeline: train SVM attribute classifiers, then train a random forest on class-attribute signature vectors.
- Key innovation: use a validation set of attribute-labeled images to estimate attribute reliability at tree nodes.
- Presents a few-shot extension that mixes signature-based priors with traditional training examples.
- Paper is well-written and clearly summarizes related work.
- Overall novelty is modest; the claim of uniquely handling unreliable attributes is overstated given prior methods like DAP.
- Technical distinctions include ROC-based reliability estimates (vs Platt-sigmoid) and propagation of validation data to capture joint attribute error statistics.
- Better characterized as improved modeling of attribute correlations and noise, not a fundamentally new unreliability framework.
- Related prior work (e.g., Scheirer et al.) on attribute correlations/noise should be more fully acknowledged and built upon.
- Major concern: method may need a large validation set because of data subdivision in trees; proposed heuristic fix is weakly justified.
- Experiments support claims but quantitative gains are modest and not decisive.
- Few-shot section is brief/unpolished, and the weighted information-gain formulation (Eq. 6) is less intuitive than simpler alternatives.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — address validation-set requirement, strengthen the few-shot section, and recharacterize the contribution.
