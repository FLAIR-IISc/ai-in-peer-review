- Proposes a novel zero-shot learning method that explicitly models attribute classifier unreliability.  
- Key insight: incorporate attribute ROC (TPR/FPR) into random forest tree construction instead of assuming perfect attribute predictions.  
- Shifts focus from attribute strength to attribute reliability, addressing a practical gap in prior work (e.g., DAP).  
- Propagates class signatures through trees according to estimated true/false positive rates, naturally handling signature uncertainty.  
- Method extends to few-shot learning, broadening applicability.  
- Strong empirical results on AwA, aPY, and SUN, significantly outperforming DAP and a baseline that assumes perfect attributes.  
- Improvements are largest on datasets with less reliable attributes (aPY, SUN), with gains up to 10.2% on some categories.  
- Controlled noise experiments validate robustness as attribute reliability decreases.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art on AwA using discovered attributes, showing generality beyond standard attribute sets.  
- Paper is very well written, clearly organized, and provides precise methodological description.  
- Thorough experimental setup including ablation studies that isolate component contributions.  
- Technical approach is sound and well-motivated; relates to fractional tuples for missing values but could contrast more explicitly.  
- Reproducibility concerns: insufficient justification for hyperparameter choices (e.g., 80/20 train/validation split) and handling when validation data from unseen classes is unavailable.  
- Lacks qualitative analysis identifying which attributes or categories benefit most, limiting deeper insight.  
- Overall significant and original contribution to zero-shot learning; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses, making it a strong candidate for acceptance.
