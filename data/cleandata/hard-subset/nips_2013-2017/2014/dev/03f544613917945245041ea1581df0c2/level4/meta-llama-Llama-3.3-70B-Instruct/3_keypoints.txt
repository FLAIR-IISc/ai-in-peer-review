- Builds on [11] and [12], extending their applicability to more general contexts.
- Proposed computational framework effective for (a) strongly convex, twice-differentiable loss functions and (b) decomposable regularization functions.
- Theoretical underpinnings are sound and numerical results are persuasive.
- Decomposable regularization naturally fits sparse learning; requiring strong convexity for the loss may be restrictive since many sparse problems are not strongly convex.
- Concern 1: The condition in Eq.(16) appears stringent; illustrative examples are requested to justify it.
- Concern 2: A detailed comparison with block coordinate descent methods (especially for Section 5.2) is needed.
- Rebuttal point: Statistical error often reduces the need for overly precise optimization solutions, possibly diminishing practical gains from local quadratic convergence.
- Recommended experiments: compare computational time vs classification error (e.g., in multi-task learning) to better evaluate trade-offs with first-order block coordinate descent.
- Overall view: Positive; paper would be a valuable addition to NIPS.
