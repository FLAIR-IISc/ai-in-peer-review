**Summary:** The authors propose a new Newton-like method for optimizing the sum of a smooth (convex) cost function and multiple differentiable criteria. Their contributions include: (1) an active subset selection process that allows for speeding up the solution of a quadratic approximation problem, (2) a proof that solving a quadratic approximation problem on a (changing) active subset still converges. The authors also provide numerical results showing that, for two important problems, their methods are 10 times faster than state-of-the-art methods, and in the appendix, numerical results are given that indicate which fraction of the speed increase is due to the quadratic approximation technique and which fraction of the speed increase is due to the active subset selection method.

**Quality:** The amount of important information in the appendix makes this paper more suitable for a journal than for a conference.

Although I have not checked all the evidence, the evidence I have checked is correct and well-written (except for a few minor typos).

**Clarity:** The paper is very well-written and organized. Some minor suggestions and questions are given below. References can be numbered in order. Including a curly bracket inside the $\min$ in equation (1) would make it clearer. In line 114, shouldn't the subscript of $\|x\|$ be $(1,\alpha)$, as written in line 116? In Introduction 1, line 236, it would be helpful to clarify that the orthogonal subset is a function of $\theta$. In line 259, it seems that the over-bar above $Q$ is a notation that was not introduced. In line 266, it would be helpful to clarify that $D$ does not need to be diagonal. Line 299 refers to equation 11, and Theorem 1, line 303, refers to equation 8. The appendix also refers to equation 8. This is misleading because the optimization in equation 11 is constrained, and it is not in equation 8. It would help to clarify that this is because the problem is quadratic, that optimization over the "free" subset is equivalent to optimization over the entire space. In line 308, "gives" should be "give". In line 354, it might be helpful to remind the reader again that $\Delta_D$ does not need to be diagonal. In Figure 1, the authors should explain what the percentage means and what the time means.

**Originality:** Although there are ideas in the literature related to this work (and these are clearly stated by the authors), their specific active selection method is new, and therefore their proof of convergence is also new.

**Importance:** The 10-fold speed is very encouraging, and I am sure that the algorithm in this paper will be important for many practitioners. A well-written paper with important theoretical and practical contributions. Unfortunately, the amount of important information in the appendix makes this paper more suitable for a journal than for a conference (so that reviewers can read all the evidence) rather than for a conference where there is an 8-page limit on the portion of the papers that reviewers have to review.
