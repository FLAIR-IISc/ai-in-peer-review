- Proposes a Newton-like optimizer for a smooth convex cost plus multiple decomposable norms with two main contributions: active subspace selection and a convergence proof for solving the quadratic approximation within the active subspace.  
- Empirical results report ~10× speedup over state-of-the-art on two problems; appendix quantifies how much speedup comes from the quadratic approximation vs. active subspace selection.  
- Appendix contains extensive critical material, making the work more appropriate for a journal than a conference with strict page limits.  
- Reviewed proofs appear correct and well-written, aside from minor typographical errors.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and well-structured.  
- Minor presentation/clarity issues to fix: reorder references numerically; add curly brace in Eq.(1); fix norm subscript at line 114; clarify orthogonal subspace dependence on θ in Proposition 1; define over-bar notation for Q (line 259); state D is not required to be diagonal (lines 266 and 354); resolve/confuse Eq.(8) vs Eq.(11) and explain equivalence of free-subspace optimization; correct "gives"→"give" at line 308; clarify percentages and time values in Figure 1.  
- Originality: active subspace selection method and convergence proof are novel contributions building on prior work.  
- Significance: the reported 10× speedup is promising and likely impactful for practitioners, but reliance on a large appendix raises concerns about conference suitability.
