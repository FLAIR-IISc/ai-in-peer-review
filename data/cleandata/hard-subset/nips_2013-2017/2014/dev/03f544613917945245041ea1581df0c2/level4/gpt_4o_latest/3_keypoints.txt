- Paper extends results of [11] and [12] to more general scenarios.  
- Framework applies to strongly convex, twice-differentiable losses.  
- Framework applies to decomposable regularizers (suited to sparse learning).  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and numerical results are convincing.  
- Strong convexity/twice-differentiability requirement (a) is restrictive for many sparse learning problems that are not strongly convex.  
- Main concern 1: Eq.(16) seems a strong assumption—examples or clarification requested.  
- Main concern 2: Block coordinate descent is a competitive alternative; a more detailed comparison is needed.  
- Suggested experiment: timing vs. classification error (e.g., in multi-task learning) to compare proposed method and block coordinate descent.  
- Reviewer hypothesis: local quadratic convergence may not translate to better statistical error, though very accurate solutions can be useful if resources permit.  
- Overall verdict: high quality paper; recommend acceptance for presentation at NIPS.
