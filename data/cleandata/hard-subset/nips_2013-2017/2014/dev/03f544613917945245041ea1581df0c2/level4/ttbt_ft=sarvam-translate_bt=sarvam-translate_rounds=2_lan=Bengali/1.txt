The authors propose a new method similar to Newton's method for optimizing the sum of a smooth (convex) cost function and multiple differentiable norms. Their contributions include: (1) an active subset selection method that allows for rapid solutions to quadratic approximation problems; (2) a proof that the solution to quadratic approximation problems in (varying) active subsets still yields convergence. The authors show that their method is 10 times faster than state-of-the-art methods for two important problems, and provide numerical results in the appendix that explain how much of the speed is due to the quadratic approximation technique and how much to the active subset selection method.

Quality: The amount of important information in the appendix makes this paper more suitable for a journal than a conference. Although I have not tested all the evidence, those I have tested are correct and well-written (except for a few small typos).

Clarity: The paper is very well-written and organized. A few small suggestions and questions are given below. References can be numbered in order. Adding a curly bracket inside the $\min$ of equation (1) would make it clearer. On line 114, the subscript of $\|x\|$ should be $(1,\alpha)$, which is written as (1,\alpha)$ on line 116? On line 236 of proposal 1, it needs to be clarified that the orthogonal subset is a function of $\theta$. On line 259, it seems that the over-bar on $Q$ is a symbol that was not given earlier. On line 266, it needs to be clarified that $D$ does not need to be diagonal. On line 299, equation 11 and on line 303 of proposal 1, equation 8 is mentioned. Equation 8 is also mentioned in the appendix. This is confusing because the optimization is limited to equation 11, not equation 8. It needs to be clarified that the problem is "unbounded" because it is quadratic, and optimizing over the "unbounded" subset across the entire space is equivalent to optimizing over the "bounded" subset. On line 308, "by" should be written instead of "given by". On line 354, the reader needs to be reminded again that $\Delta_D$ does not need to be diagonal. In figure 1, the authors' percentage meaning and duration meaning should be explained.

Originality: Although the concept related to this work exists in the literature (and the authors clearly mention it), their specific active selection method is new, and their proof of convergence is also new.

Significance: The 10-fold speed is very encouraging, and I am sure that the algorithm in this paper will be important for several practitioners. It is a well-written paper with important theoretical and practical contributions. Unfortunately, the amount of important information in the appendix makes this paper more suitable for a journal than a conference (so that reviewers can read all the evidence), whereas the paper section for reviewers' reviews has an 8-page limit.
