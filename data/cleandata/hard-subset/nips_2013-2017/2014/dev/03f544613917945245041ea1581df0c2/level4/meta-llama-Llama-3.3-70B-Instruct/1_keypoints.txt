- Introduces a Newton-like method for a smooth convex cost plus multiple decomposable norms, with an active subspace selection procedure and a convergence proof.  
- Reports numerical results showing roughly 10× speedup over state-of-the-art on two key problems; appendix attributes speedup to quadratic approximation and active subspace selection.  
- Novelty: the specific active selection strategy and the accompanying convergence analysis are original contributions despite related prior work.  
- Quality concern: substantial critical information and proofs are in the appendix, making the paper better suited to a journal than an 8-page conference format.  
- Clarity: overall well-organized and clear, but needs minor presentation fixes (e.g., sequentially number references; add curly brace in Eq. (1)).  
- Notational issues: fix subscript of ||x|| (line 114), clarify the over-bar on Q (line 259), and state that D need not be diagonal (line 266).  
- Conceptual clarification: explain how optimizing over the “free” subspace relates to optimizing over the whole space to resolve confusion between Eqs. (8) and (11).  
- Figure 1 should include explanations for the percentages and times shown.  
- Overall assessment: substantial theoretical and practical impact, well-written, but the appendix-heavy critical material reduces suitability for a conference-length paper.
