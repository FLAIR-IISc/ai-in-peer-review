- Proposes a novel Newton-like method for optimizing a smooth cost plus multiple decomposable norms.  
- Introduces an original active subspace selection procedure that extends active-subspace ideas to multiple regularizers.  
- Provides a convergence proof for the proposed algorithm.  
- Demonstrates empirical speedups (~10x) over state-of-the-art on latent variable graphical model selection and multi-task learning.  
- Makes significant theoretical and practical contributions with potential impact on practitioners.  
- Algorithm shows thorough analysis of convergence properties.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Much critical information is placed in the appendix, making the main paper lengthy and possibly unsuitable for a conference format.  
- Due to appendix content, conference attendees may have difficulty fully appreciating the contributions.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Suggests revising to move critical appendix material into the main body to better fit a conference presentation.
