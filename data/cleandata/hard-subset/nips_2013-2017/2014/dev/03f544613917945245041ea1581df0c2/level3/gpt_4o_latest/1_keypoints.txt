- Novel active subspace selection framework extends single-regularizer methods to multiple decomposable norms, a meaningful innovation for high-dimensional optimization.  
- Solid theoretical foundation addressing Hessian non-positive-definiteness and dynamic active subspace, with global and super-linear convergence proofs.  
- Empirical results show ~10x speed-up over state-of-the-art on latent variable GGM and multi-task learning; appendix provides breakdown of speed-up sources.  
- Practical relevance: efficiently handles multiple regularizers (e.g., sparse+low-rank, sparse+group-sparse) important for ML/statistics practitioners.  
- Clarity issues: some notations unexplained, typos, and inconsistent/missing citations reduce readability.  
- Over-reliance on appendix: critical implementation and speed-up details are relegated to the appendix, which may hinder accessibility.  
- Limited experiments: evaluation confined to two applications, weakening generalizability claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an impactful problem with theoretical and practical advances, significant runtime improvements, and a novel framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on appendix for key details and minor presentation issues reduce accessibility and polish.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address clarity and presentation.
