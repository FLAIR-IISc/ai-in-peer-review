- Novel approach: Proposes proximal Newton optimization for "dirty models" using quadratic approximations and active subspace selection.  
- Theoretical extension: Generalizes decomposable norms to a broader setting beyond prior uses like nuclear norm minimization.  
- Algorithmic innovation: Introduces active subspace selection to reduce search space and handle multiple regularizers.  
- Convergence guarantees: Provides theoretical analysis including super-linear convergence despite Hessian indefiniteness.  
- Empirical speedup: Demonstrates ~10x speedup over state-of-the-art first-order methods on tasks like latent variable graphical model selection and multi-task learning.  
- Practical significance unclear: Paper fails to convincingly argue real-world benefits of the speedup in terms of ease of optimization or parallelization.  
- Broad applicability: Framework supports various decomposable norms and hybrid regularizers, relevant to many high-dimensional problems.  
- Writing problems: Poor writing, language errors, and inconsistent grammar reduce readability and professionalism.  
- Formatting issues: Figures and formulas overflow margins; paper lacks a conclusion/discussion section and future directions.  
- Algorithmic clarity concerns: Errors in Algorithm 1 (notation and variable updates) raise reproducibility and correctness questions.  
- Incremental contribution: Builds on established proximal Newton and decomposable-norm ideas, so theoretical novelty is limited.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject with potential for reconsideration after major revisions addressing clarity, presentation, and practical impact.  
- Acceptance arguments: Strong theory, significant empirical speedups, and generalization of active subspace methods.  
- Rejection arguments: Poor presentation, incremental advance over prior work, and inadequate discussion of practical implications.
