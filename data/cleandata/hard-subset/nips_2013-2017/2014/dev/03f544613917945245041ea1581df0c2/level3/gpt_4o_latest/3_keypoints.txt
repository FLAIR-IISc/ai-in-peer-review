- Proposes a novel proximal Newton family for high-dimensional estimation with superposition-structured regularizers using active subspace selection for decomposable norms.  
- Extends prior work to handle strongly convex, twice-differentiable losses and hybrid regularization efficiently.  
- Reports over 10× speedups compared to state-of-the-art first-order methods on latent variable graphical model selection and multi-task learning.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees including global convergence and asymptotic quadratic convergence under relaxed Hessian positive-definiteness conditions.  
- Generalizes active subspace selection to decomposable norms, improving computational efficiency without losing accuracy.  
- Empirically validated on real datasets (e.g., gene expression, multi-task learning), showing scalability and practical utility.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical rigor, notably relaxing positive-definiteness assumptions on the Hessian.  
- Weakness: Reliance on strongly convex loss functions may limit applicability to many sparse-learning settings.  
- Weakness: Assumption (16) is strong and not sufficiently justified or illustrated.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to block coordinate descent methods, which are relevant competitors.  
- Weakness: Claim that statistical error reduces need for high accuracy needs more experiments on time vs. error trade-offs.  
- Suggestions: Provide examples/intuition for Assumption (16), add comparisons with block coordinate descent, and run experiments analyzing timing vs. statistical error.  
- Recommendation: Strong contribution; recommend acceptance at NeurIPS with minor revisions addressing the above concerns.
