- Proposes a proximal Newton framework to optimize "superposition-structured" (dirty) estimators in high-dimensional problems.  
- Framework combines a quadratic approximation of the loss with an active subspace selection technique to reduce problem size.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including convergence guarantees and asymptotic quadratic convergence rates.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness with extensive experiments on Gaussian Markov Random Fields with latent variables and multi-task learning.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated optimization approach tailored to superposition-structured regularizers.  
- Strength: strong theoretical results and convergence analysis.  
- Strength: impressive empirical performance on two real-world applications.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation that is easy to follow.  
- Weakness: relies on assumption of strongly convex loss functions, which may not always hold in practice.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: computational complexity analysis of the proposed framework could be more detailed.  
- Note: authors argue that accounting for statistical error may reduce need for highly accurate solutions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
