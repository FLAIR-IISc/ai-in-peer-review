- Paper proposes a novel proximal Newton family for "superposition-structured" (dirty) regularization with active subspace selection to reduce complexity.  
- Key contributions: quadratic loss approximation, active subspace selection for multiple regularizers, and theoretical convergence guarantees even with non-PD Hessians.  
- Empirical claim: substantial efficiency gains (10x+) over first-order methods on tasks like GMRF structure learning and multi-task learning.  
- Strength: addresses an important gap in optimizing dirty statistical models and extends active subspace selection to multiple regularizers.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical results including global convergence and asymptotic quadratic convergence under relaxed assumptions.  
- Strength: strong practical impact demonstrated by scalability and large speedups on large-scale datasets (e.g., RCV1).  
- Strength: clear application contexts (GMRF, multi-task learning) showing versatility.  
- Weakness: exposition can be dense; active subspace selection and quadratic approximation derivations may be hard for non-experts.  
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to two applications, possibly insufficient to demonstrate broad applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit discussion of limitations, e.g., Hessian overhead in very high dimensions and sensitivity to subspace selection tuning.  
- Suggestion: improve clarity of active subspace selection with intuitive explanations or visual aids.  
- Suggestion: broaden experiments to more datasets/domains (e.g., image or time-series) to show wider applicability.  
- Suggestion: add discussion on limitations and extensions, such as non-convex losses or approximate quadratic subproblem solvers.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity and experimental scope concerns.
