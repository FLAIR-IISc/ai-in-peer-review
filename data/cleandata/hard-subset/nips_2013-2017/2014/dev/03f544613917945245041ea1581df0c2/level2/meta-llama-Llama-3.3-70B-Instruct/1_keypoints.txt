- Proposes a proximal Newton framework for optimizing superposition-structured (“dirty”) estimators.  
- Develops a quadratic approximation approach with active subspace selection to improve efficiency.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including convergence guarantees and asymptotic quadratic convergence rates.  
- Claims QUIC & DIRTY is >10× faster than state-of-the-art first-order methods for latent-variable graphical model selection and multi-task learning.  
- Claims super-linear convergence when quadratic sub-problems are solved exactly.  
- Claims active subspace selection markedly speeds up the solver.  
- Supports claims with detailed proofs and empirical experiments on real-world tasks (e.g., Gaussian Markov Random Fields, multi-task learning).  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of methodology and results.  
- Active subspace selection identified as the key innovation enabling substantial speedups.  
- References are comprehensive and demonstrate solid knowledge of related work.  
- Novel contribution: combining proximal Newton framework with active subspace selection, improving over mainly first-order prior approaches.  
- Paper is largely complete with sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Limitation: method assumes quadratic sub-problems can be solved exactly, which may be impractical.  
- Limitation: lacks detailed comparisons with other second-order methods for additional context.  
- Could benefit from expanded discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contributions and potential for wide adoption.
