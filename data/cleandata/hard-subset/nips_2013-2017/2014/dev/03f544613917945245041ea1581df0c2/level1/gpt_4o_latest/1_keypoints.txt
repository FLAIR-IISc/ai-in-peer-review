- Proposes a novel family of second-order proximal Newton algorithms for superposition-structured regularization using active subspace selection.
- Leverages quadratic loss approximations and active subspace selection to reduce computational complexity.
- Provides convergence guarantees, including global and asymptotic quadratic rates, even with non–positive-definite Hessians and varying subspaces.
- Empirically >10× faster than state-of-the-art first-order methods on tasks like latent-variable Gaussian graphical model selection and multi-task learning.
- Novelty: extends proximal Newton methods to multiple regularizers and introduces an innovative active subspace framework.
- Theoretical rigor: offers strong, nontrivial convergence analysis.
- Empirical validation: convincing real-world experiments showing efficiency and scalability.
- Presentation: well-organized with helpful examples (e.g., decomposable norms).
- Broader impact: potential to advance optimization in ML, statistics, and computational biology.
- Notation is dense and may limit accessibility for nonexperts.
- Limited comparisons: lacks benchmarking against other second-order or hybrid methods.
- Scalability to extremely large datasets (e.g., millions of features) is not fully explored.
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, is theoretically sound, empirically effective, and novel.
- Against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to dense notation and incomplete second-order comparisons.
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to simplify exposition and broaden comparisons in future work.
