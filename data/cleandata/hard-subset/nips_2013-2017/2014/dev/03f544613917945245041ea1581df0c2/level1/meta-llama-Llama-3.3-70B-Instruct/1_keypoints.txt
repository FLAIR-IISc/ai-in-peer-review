- Proposes a novel proximal Newton framework for optimizing "superposition-structured" (dirty) estimators common in high-dimensional problems.
- Develops a quadratic approximation scheme with active subspace selection to reduce problem size and improve efficiency.
- Targets applications such as Gaussian graphical models with latent variables and multi-task learning, with a clear background exposition.
- Introduces the QUIC & DIRTY algorithm that uses block coordinate descent and active subspace selection to update parameter components.
- Provides convergence guarantees including global convergence and an asymptotic quadratic convergence rate.
- Empirical results on two real-world tasks show the method is more than 10× faster than state-of-the-art methods.
- Strength: fills a gap in optimization literature for superposition-structured estimators.
- Strength: active subspace selection enables efficient optimization.
- Strength: theoretical convergence guarantees strengthen the algorithm’s reliability.
- Strength: compelling empirical performance demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Weakness: assumes Hessian matrix is positive definite, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: requires the loss function to be strongly convex, which may be restrictive.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other optimization methods (e.g., SGD, quasi-Newton).
- Overall assessment: significant, well-written contribution with strong potential for practical use.
