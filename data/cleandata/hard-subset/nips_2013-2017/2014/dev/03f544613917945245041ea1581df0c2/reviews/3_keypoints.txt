- Paper extends results from [11],[12] to more general settings.  
- Proposed computational framework applies to (a) strongly convex, twice-differentiable loss functions and (b) decomposable regularizers.  
- Theoretical analysis is reasonable.  
- Numerical results are convincing.  
- Concern: assumption (a) strong convexity is restrictive for many sparse learning problems that are non-strongly convex.  
- Concern 1: Eq.(16) appears strong — reviewer requests concrete examples.  
- Concern 2: Need detailed comparison with block coordinate descent methods for the optimization in Section 5.2.  
- Rebuttal note: including statistical error often means extremely accurate solutions are unnecessary.  
- Suggestion: run more experiments (e.g., timing vs. classification error in multi-task learning) to compare practical performance.  
- Conjecture: local quadratic convergence may not translate into better statistical error vs first-order methods, though very accurate solutions are fine if resources permit.  
- Overall recommendation: positive — would like to see the paper accepted to NIPS.
