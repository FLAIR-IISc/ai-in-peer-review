- Proposes a novel discriminative clustering framework linking clustering and multi-class classification using KDE-derived similarity and generalization error bounds.  
- Reformulation clarifies the optimization objective and reduces parameter tuning complexity.  
- Theoretical contribution: derives generalization error bounds for plug-in and nearest neighbor classifiers, explaining kernel similarity in clustering.  
- Connects error bounds to weighted cluster-boundary volume and Low Density Separation, providing a principled justification.  
- Introduces the Plug-In Exemplar Clustering (PIEC) algorithm with improved discriminative capability over exemplar-based methods.  
- Empirical results show improvements on benchmark datasets but comparisons are limited mainly to exemplar-based baselines.  
- Paper devotes extensive space to KDE consistency proofs that could be shortened for more experiments.  
- Presentation suffers from excessive, dense notation and lacks a clear, consolidated algorithm description.  
- Practical significance, especially for high-dimensional or real-world datasets, is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Reviewer recommendation: weak reject pending stronger experimental evaluation and improved clarity.
