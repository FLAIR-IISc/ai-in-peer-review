- Proposes Junctor: a novel recursive characterization of clique trees with dynamic programming for learning chordal Markov networks.
- Claims Junctor outperforms existing methods (e.g., GOBNILP) in scalability and efficiency.
- Presentation is clear and well-structured with a thorough problem formulation and algorithm description.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the approach.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation shows Junctor solves moderate-size instances to optimality in reasonable time.
- Originality lies in combining recursive clique-tree characterization with dynamic programming for this problem.
- Significance: method could advance structure learning for Markov networks and apply to many domains.
- Lacks detailed comparison with other methods (e.g., MM clustering, information-theoretic approaches).
- Needs more discussion of limitations and directions for future research.
- Clarity could be improved by a concise algorithm summary; excessive notation may hinder non-experts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient approach with solid theory and experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons, limited discussion of limitations/future work, and heavy notation.
