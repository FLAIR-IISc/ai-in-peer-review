This paper presents a clustering framework using a pairwise similarity function model.

Various non-parametric classifiers are used to create different data partitions.

Optimal clustering is achieved by minimizing the learned classifier's generalization error associated with the data partition.

The authors consider two non-parametric classifiers in their framework: plug-in and nearest- প্রতিবেশী (nearest-neighbour) classifiers.

The authors also prove an interesting result, namely that the generalization error threshold for unsupervised plug-in clustering asymptotically equals the weighted volume of the cluster boundary for low density separation.

This paper combines two important machine learning topics: the multi-class classification problem and clustering. Furthermore, it has a strong theoretical background.

However, this paper is very poorly written.

The introduction is well-organized, although in the following sections, the authors delve into many technical details and do not summarize their results in a concise, understandable manner. The two technical theorems given in Section 2 further increase confusion. Thus, it is extremely difficult to measure the actual impact of this paper.

Section 2 contains a lot of technical details that are given before giving the reader any kind of intuition. In addition to the presented theorem statements, they are completely unclear. For example:

Theorem 1:
 - Is it possible to say how n{0}, \sigma{0} and K depend on the VC characteristics?
 - The statements do not appear to be grammatically correct; the authors should review them.

Theorem 2:
 - Again, the authors introduce many parameters that depend on each other; it is almost impossible to predict what it actually says from all the assumptions required by the theorem.

Theorem 3:
 - Similar to the problems mentioned above.

The theorem statements are illegible, and some of them are placed at the beginning of the paper itself. The authors should spend more time working on the presentation of their results. Perhaps some summary of the theoretical results obtained should be organized before giving precise statements. The authors should also provide some intuition about all the theorems presented in the main part of the paper.

The authors present how their techniques can be applied to example-based clustering, but a comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods is missing. The experimental section is very short and does not shed any light on other clustering algorithms developed by the authors. The difficulty of clustering depends very much on the setting considered: is the data actually high-dimensional, or perhaps located on a low-dimensional manifold, are the data points sparse or dense, are the groundtruth clusters convex or there is no notion about their shape, what objective function the authors aim to minimize or what groundtruth clustering they want to predict well. Without a detailed analysis of the algorithm's performance in the specific setting defined by these parameters, it is almost impossible to say that the presented method can be applied in practice and solves a non-trivial problem. What is the computational complexity of the algorithms using the presented technique? This issue is not addressed at all. Is it possible to extend the analysis for other classifiers (only nearest- প্রতিবেশী and plug-in classifiers were analyzed)? Is it possible to release some source code files (with the implementation of the presented method) that the authors used to test their approach? (Although the last comment is not important.) In summary, this paper focuses on a very important machine learning problem and is somehow innovative (presents new ideas that, according to the authors: "bridge the gap between clustering and multi-class classification"; to some extent I agree with the authors' statement). However, it seems to me that the authors do not spend much time preparing a readable version of the paper. The presentation is chaotic; it is very difficult to understand what the main theoretical results of the paper are. The experimental section is incomplete. No comparison has been made with the methods currently available. I would suggest that the authors spend more time completely reorganizing the paper according to the points mentioned above. In particular, Sections: 2 and 3 should be completely rewritten.
