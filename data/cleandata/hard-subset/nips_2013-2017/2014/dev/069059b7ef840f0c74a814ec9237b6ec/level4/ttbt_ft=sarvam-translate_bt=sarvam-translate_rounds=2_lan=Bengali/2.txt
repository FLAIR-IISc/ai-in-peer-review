This paper presents a clustering framework using a pair-based similarity function model.

Various nonparametric classifiers are created using different data partitions. The generalization of the learned classifiers associated with the data partitions results in the best clustering, reducing the generalization error.

The authors consider two nonparametric classifiers in their framework: plug-in and nearest neighbor. The authors also proved an interesting result, namely that the generalization error bound for unsupervised plug-in clustering is approximately equal to the weighted volume of the cluster boundary for low-density separation.

This paper combines two important machine learning topics: multi-class classification problems and clustering. Furthermore, it has a strong theoretical background.

However, the paper is very poorly written.

The introduction is well-organized, but in subsequent sections, the authors delve into several technical details and do not present their results in a concise and understandable manner. The two technical lemas provided in section 2 further increase confusion. Therefore, it is extremely difficult to measure the paper's actual impact.

Section 2 contains too many technical details that are provided before giving the reader any kind of idea. The lemma statements presented are also completely unclear. For example:

Lemma 1:
 - Is it possible to say how n{0}, \sigma{0} and K depend on the VC properties?
 - The statements do not seem grammatically correct; the authors should review them.

Lemma 2:
 - Again, the authors have presented many parameters that are interdependent. It is almost impossible to guess what it actually says from all the assumptions required by the lemma.

Lemma 3:
 - Similar to the problems mentioned above.

The lemma statements are not understandable, and some of them are given at the very beginning of the paper. The authors should have spent more time presenting their results. Perhaps a summary of the theoretical results obtained before giving the correct statement should have been provided. The authors should have given some idea about all the lemas presented in the main body.

The authors have presented how their techniques can be applied for example-based clustering, but there is a lack of comparison with existing state-of-the-art methods. The experimental section is very small and does not shed any light on the advantages of the techniques invented by the authors.

The difficulty of clustering depends greatly on the settings considered: whether the data is truly high-dimensional, or located in low-dimensional dimensionality, whether the data points are scattered or dense, whether the groundtruth clusters are convex or there is no assumption about their size, what purpose function the authors want to reduce, or whether they want to estimate groundtruth clustering well. Without detailed analysis of the performance of the algorithm in a specific setting defined by these parameters, it is almost impossible to say whether the presented method can be applied in practice and whether it solves a non-trivial problem.

What is the computational complexity of the algorithm using the presented technique? This problem is not solved at all. Is it possible to extend the analysis to other classifiers (only nearest neighbor and plug-in classifiers are analyzed)? Is it possible for the authors to publish the source code files they used to test their method (including the implementation of the presented method)? (The last comment is not very important).

In summary, this paper focuses on a very important machine learning problem and is somehow innovative (presenting new ideas that, according to the authors: "fill the gap between clustering and multi-label classification"; in some cases I agree with the authors' statements). However, I think the authors did not spend too much time preparing a readable version of the paper. The presentation is unstructured, and it is very difficult to understand what the paper's main theoretical results are. The experimental section is incomplete. No comparison has been made with the currently existing methods. I would advise the authors to spend more time completely restructuring the paper according to the above points. In particular, sections 2 and 3 should be completely rewritten.
