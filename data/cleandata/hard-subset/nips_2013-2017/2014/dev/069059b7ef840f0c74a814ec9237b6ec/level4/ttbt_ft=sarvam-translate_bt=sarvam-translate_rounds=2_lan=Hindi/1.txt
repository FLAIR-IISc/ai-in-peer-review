This paper proposes a new pairwise clustering framework that achieves non-parametric pairwise similarity using an unsupervised non-parametric classifier, minimizing the generalization error. The proposed framework bridges the gap between clustering and multi-class classification, and explains kernel similarity, which is widely used for clustering. The authors also prove that the generalization error bound for the unsupervised plug-in classifier is equal to the weighted volume of the cluster boundary for low-density separation. Based on the non-parametric pairwise similarity obtained using the plug-in classifier, the authors propose a new non-parametric example-based clustering method with better discriminatory power compared to existing example-based clustering methods.
