This paper proposes a new joint-based clustering framework where a nonparametric joint-based similarity is determined by an unsupervised nonparametric classifier to minimize generalization errors. The proposed framework bridges the gap between clustering and multi-class classification and explains the kernel similarity, which is widely used for clustering. The authors further prove that the generalization error bound of the unsupervised plug-in classifier is approximately equal to the weighted volume of the cluster boundary for low-density separation. Based on the nonparametric joint-based similarity obtained using the plug-in classifier, the authors propose a new nonparametric example-based clustering method with improved discriminative power compared to existing example-based clustering methods.
