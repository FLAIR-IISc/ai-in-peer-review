- Proposes a novel pairwise clustering framework that obtains nonparametric pairwise similarity by minimizing the generalization error of an unsupervised nonparametric classifier.  
- Effectively bridges clustering and multi-class classification, providing an explanation for commonly used kernel similarities in clustering.  
- Shows the generalization error bound of the unsupervised plug-in classifier is asymptotically equivalent to the weighted volume of the cluster boundary under low-density separation.  
- Derives a nonparametric pairwise similarity from the plug-in classifier and introduces a new exemplar-based clustering method with improved discriminative capability.  
- Issue: Equation (6) appears to be missing a π^{(i)} term in the numerator (inconsistent with line 123).  
- Issue: In Lemma 2 the kernel bandwidth h should be denoted h_n for consistency.  
- Minor edit: Remove the extra phrase "double the" in line 63.  
- Assessment: The theoretical contributions—especially the connection to low-density separation—are noteworthy, but the resulting exemplar-based algorithm seems somewhat complex to apply.  
- Overall: An interesting and valuable contribution offering a unique perspective on clustering and classification.
