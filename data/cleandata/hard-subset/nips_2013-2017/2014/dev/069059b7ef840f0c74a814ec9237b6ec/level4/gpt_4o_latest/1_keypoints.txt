- Proposes a novel pairwise clustering framework using nonparametric pairwise similarity derived by minimizing the generalization error of an unsupervised nonparametric classifier.  
- Bridges clustering and multi-class classification and provides a theoretical explanation for widely used kernel similarity in clustering.  
- Shows the generalization error bound for the unsupervised plug-in classifier asymptotically corresponds to the weighted volume of the cluster boundary under the low-density separation assumption.  
- Derives a nonparametric pairwise similarity from the plug-in classifier and proposes a new exemplar-based clustering method with improved discriminative power.  
- Comment: Equation (6) may be missing a π^{(i)} factor in the numerator (cf. line 123).  
- Comment: In Lemma 2 the kernel bandwidth h should be denoted h_n.  
- Comment: Typo on line 63 — remove the repeated word “the.”  
- Strength: Theoretical results are compelling, especially the link to the low-density separation principle.  
- Weakness: Applying the theory to develop the new exemplar-based algorithm appears somewhat complex.  
- Overall: An engaging and thought-provoking paper.
