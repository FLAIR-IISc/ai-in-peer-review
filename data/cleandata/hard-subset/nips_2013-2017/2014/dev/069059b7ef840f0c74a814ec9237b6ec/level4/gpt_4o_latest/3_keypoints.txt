- Paper proposes a novel discriminative clustering view: derive a classifier generalization-error bound and optimize it via belief-propagation clustering.  
- Key innovation: reformulate cost as a similarity measure using kernel density estimation to reduce extensive parameter search.  
- Not entirely parameter-free: still requires balance parameters and a kernel variance parameter (h).  
- Conceptually promising for reducing parameter space and clarifying the clustering objective.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: main comparison is only to an exemplar-based clustering method.  
- Reviewer suggests more relevant baselines (e.g., MM clustering or information-theoretic approaches) would be more informative.  
- Space spent proving KDE consistency is unnecessary since results largely follow established theory.  
- Presentation is hard to follow due to heavy notation; a consolidated "here is our algorithm" section is needed.  
- Tracing parameter roles (especially the variance h) is difficult in current exposition.  
- Overall: worthwhile idea but needs stronger experiments and clearer presentation.
