- Central idea: derive a generalization-error bound for a classifier that separates cluster classes and optimize that bound with a belief-propagation clustering algorithm.  
- Novelty: reformulate the cost as a new similarity measure using a kernel density estimate to avoid an extensive parameter search.  
- Remaining parameters: method still requires balance parameters and a kernel variance (h).  
- Contribution: reduces the number of parameters for discriminative clustering and clarifies the objective being optimized.  
- Experimental weakness: empirical evaluation is thin and primarily compares only to an exemplar-based clustering approach.  
- Missing baselines: should compare against MM clustering and information-theoretic methods to support the claimed advantages.  
- Theory vs. experiments: space spent proving KDE consistency is unnecessary (follows from known results) and could be better used for additional experiments.  
- Readability issues: excessive notation and lack of a concise “here is our algorithm” summary make the paper hard to follow and obscure parameter usage.  
- Overall judgment: reasonable and promising idea but requires a more thorough experimental evaluation.
