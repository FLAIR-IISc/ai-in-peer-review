- Proposes a pairwise clustering framework where nonparametric pairwise similarity is obtained by minimizing the generalization error of an unsupervised nonparametric classifier.  
- Framework bridges clustering and multi-class classification and provides an explanation for commonly used kernel similarity in clustering.  
- Proves that the generalization error bound for the unsupervised plug-in classifier is asymptotically equal to the weighted volume of the cluster boundary under low-density separation.  
- Derives a nonparametric exemplar-based clustering method based on the plug-in similarity, claiming enhanced discriminative capability over existing exemplar methods.

- Positive: The theoretical results are interesting, especially the connection to low-density separation.

- Overall: An interesting paper.
