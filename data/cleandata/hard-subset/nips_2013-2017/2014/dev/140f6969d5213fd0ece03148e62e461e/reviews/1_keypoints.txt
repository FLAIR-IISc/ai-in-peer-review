- Proposes a novel multi-view face representation using a neural-net-like model with deterministic and probabilistic units.  
- Network is trained to recover identity and view and can synthesize novel views.  
- Paper effectively disentangles factors contributing to method success and reports strong results.  
- Reviewer requests more justification for the model design choices and structure.  
- Questions necessity of stochastic/probabilistic units versus a fully deterministic feed-forward model.  
- Concern that stochastic approach forces variational/importance sampling inference; alternatives not explored.  
- Quality: generally a good, well-grounded paper evaluated on a competitive benchmark.  
- Clarity: paper is mostly well-written and easy to read.  
- Originality: approach is comparable to Hinton-style stacked RBMs with label-aware top layer.  
- Significance: likely interesting to face-recognition community and researchers of hybrid deterministic/generative methods.  
- Requests definition of q(h) (line 165) and explanation of Figure 2 design insights.  
- Asks whether MCEM is crucial and requests more analysis on choice of MCEM optimization.  
- Requests inference details: size S (line 235), computational cost, timings and O() complexity.  
- Asks whether LDA is necessary and requests results without LDA.  
- Advises against extensive claims about similarity to human brain processing.  
- Asks whether the method was evaluated on the Labeled Faces in the Wild (LFW) benchmark.
