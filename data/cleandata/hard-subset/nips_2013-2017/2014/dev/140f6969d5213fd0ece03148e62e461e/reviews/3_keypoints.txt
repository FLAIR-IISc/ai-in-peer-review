- Paper proposes a multi-layer multi-view perceptron (MVP) to disentangle identity and view-angle representations from a single 2D image.  
- MVP can synthesize novel face views by varying the view-angle representation sequentially.  
- Learning uses a Monte Carlo Expectation Maximization (MCEM) procedure with a conditional diagonal Gaussian assumption for output faces.  
- MVP is trained on 200 persons from MultiPIE; identity features are classified with LDA on the remaining 137 persons, achieving competitive performance versus recent CNN methods.  
- Technical approach is generally sound and performance on MultiPIE is competitive.  
- Method appears to require well-aligned, normalized 32×32 face images, raising concerns about robustness to real-world variability.  
- Scalability is unclear: unclear how performance would change with more training samples or on unconstrained face recognition tasks.  
- Important methodological questions remain: how critical is the diagonal Gaussian assumption, how many training pairs were used, and why synthesis must be sequential.  
- The claim that MVP mimics human brain encoding of view representations is weak and unsupported by evidence.  
- Overall assessment: decent and competitive work but with notable concerns about scalability and practical applicability.
