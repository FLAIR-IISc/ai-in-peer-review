- Proposes a novel model for brain states from LFP data combining an infinite HMM with state-dependent tensor factorization.  
- Model can infer the number of brain states and mixture components automatically.  
- Uses variational inference with split-merge moves to improve optimization and avoid local optima.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on three datasets (toy, sleep, novel environment).  
- Recovers ground truth in toy data and infers distinct states in real datasets.  
- Effectively captures temporal dependencies in brain activity.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and claims are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Lacks sufficient comparison to existing methods (e.g., DCM and prior tensor factorization work).  
- Missing details on hyperparameter settings and sensitivity to those choices.  
- May have limited appeal outside the neuroscience community.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
