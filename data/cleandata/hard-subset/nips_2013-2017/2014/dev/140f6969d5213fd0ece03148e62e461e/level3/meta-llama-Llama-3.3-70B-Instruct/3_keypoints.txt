- Introduces an iHMM combined with state-dependent tensor factorization to model time-varying brain states from multi-region LFP data.  
- Model jointly estimates the number of brain states and mixture components in a Gaussian-process mixture.  
- Uses variational inference with split-merge moves, improving efficiency and flexibility for complex state dynamics.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed explanations of model components.  
- Demonstrates recovery of ground truth on toy data and identifies distinct states in sleep and novel-environment LFP datasets.  
- Strength: novel and flexible modeling framework tailored to brain-state analysis.  
- Strength: methodological contribution of variational inference with split-merge moves.  
- Strength: real-world applications show potential utility for neuroscience research.  
- Limitation: assumes conditional diagonal Gaussian outputs, but the importance and validity of this assumption are not assessed.  
- Limitation: unclear how many training pairs were used and how sensitive results are to training data size.  
- Limitation: claim that the model simulates human view-encoding is weak and lacks convincing evidence or discussion.  
- Limitation/concern: scalability and applicability to larger or more complex datasets remain unclear.  
- Overall: significant contribution with promising results; revisions to address assumptions, evidence for biological claims, and scalability could make it a strong candidate for acceptance.
