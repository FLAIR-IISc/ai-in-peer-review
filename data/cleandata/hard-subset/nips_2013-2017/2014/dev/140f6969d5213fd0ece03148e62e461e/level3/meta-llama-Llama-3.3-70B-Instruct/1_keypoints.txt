- Proposes Multi-View Perceptron (MVP), a neural model with deterministic and probabilistic units to synthesize views and disentangle identity/view from a single 2D face.
- Inspiration drawn from primate face-processing networks with specialized neurons for view or identity.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explaining architecture and training.
- Uses variational inference and importance sampling as notable methodological contributions.
- Demonstrates strong experimental results in recognition, viewpoint estimation, and multi-view reconstruction.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on the MultiPIE dataset, outperforming other deep learning methods.
- Originality is a strength due to the novel combination of deterministic and probabilistic units.
- Lacks sufficient justification/evidence for key design choices (need for non-determinism and use of variational/importance sampling).
- Needs more analysis of optimization method, inference costs, and why LDA is necessary for best results.
- Raises questions about robustness to poor-quality images and occlusions.
- Questions whether MVP can handle other variations (illumination, expression) and how it scales computationally.
- Asks about potential extensions/applications (face verification, tracking) and comparative efficiency/scalability.
- Pro-accept arguments: novelty, strong empirical performance, clarity, and potential field impact.
- Con-accept arguments: insufficient analysis of optimization/inference, weak justification for design choices, and limited experiments on limitations and applications.
