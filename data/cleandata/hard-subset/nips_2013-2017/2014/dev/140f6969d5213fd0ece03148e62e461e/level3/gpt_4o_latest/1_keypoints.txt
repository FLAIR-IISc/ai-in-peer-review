- Introduces Multi-View Perceptron (MVP), a novel hybrid neural model combining deterministic and probabilistic units to disentangle identity and view in faces.  
- MVP can generate a full spectrum of multi-view images from a single 2D face and performs strongly on the MultiPIE benchmark.  
- Model is inspired by primate face-processing brain regions that separate view and identity representations.  
- Framework builds on prior generative models (e.g., RBMs) but uniquely mixes deterministic and stochastic units.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of architecture and experiments.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness in face recognition, view synthesis, and interpolation of unseen viewpoints.  
- Ability to reconstruct unobserved viewpoints is highlighted as a novel, human-like reasoning capability.  
- Design choices (e.g., inclusion of non-deterministic units) lack sufficient justification and comparisons to fully deterministic alternatives.  
- Role and necessity of MCEM optimization and the contribution of LDA to top performance are not adequately explained.  
- Computational complexity and scalability, especially regarding sampled space size (S), need more analysis and numerical evaluation.  
- Speculative comparisons to human brain functionality are unnecessary and detract from scientific rigor.  
- Evaluation is limited to MultiPIE; adding LFW would better assess generalizability.  
- Strengths: novel hybrid framework, strong MultiPIE results, interpolation of unseen views, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient design justification, limited scalability analysis, lack of broader evaluations, and speculative biological claims.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing design justification, scalability analysis, and reducing speculative biological claims.
