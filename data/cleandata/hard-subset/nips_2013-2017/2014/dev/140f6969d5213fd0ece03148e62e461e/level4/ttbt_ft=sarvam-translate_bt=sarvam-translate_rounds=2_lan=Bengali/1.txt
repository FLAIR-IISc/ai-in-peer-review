This paper presents a new approach for multi-view representation of faces using models like neural nets, featuring deterministic and probabilistic units. The network is trained to recover identity and perspective. It can also be used to synthesize a new perspective. Generally, this paper does a good job of distinguishing the reasons for the method's success, and the results seem quite good.

I would like the authors to present more evidence of how they designed their model. Firstly, it is not clear whether an un నిర్ణated approach is really needed in this case – could they have created a complete feed-forward model with deterministic neurons? This would certainly avoid the need for variational/importance sampling estimation.

Quality: Generally, a good paper with an interesting and well-established model, which is well-evaluated within a competitive benchmark.
Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to read in most parts.
Originality: In some cases, this approach is comparable to the work of Geoff Hinton and his colleagues, who use stacks of restricted Boltzmann machines, where the top RBM label can be a side.
Importance: This is probably of interest to the facial recognition community, but also to other researchers who work on problems where a hybrid deterministic and generative approach might be a good solution.

Comments and Questions:
- How did the authors create the structure in Figure 2? What are the insights behind the design choices?
- The authors should have defined q (h) at line 165.
- Is the MCEM method extremely important for the presented model? Further analysis is needed as to why this particular optimization method was used, as it is not a very commonly used method within the MCEM community.
- For the estimation: How large is S at line 235? How high is the estimation cost typically? Some numbers and O () notation would be better.
- Is LDA needed to get the best results? How do the results look without LDA?
- I don't think the authors should use tangents with how their brains work, very rarely. There is not much evidence that the presented method is very close to human facial recognition or view point interpolation.
- Did the authors test their method on labeled faces from the Wild Benchmark? If yes, do they have results to report?

An interesting new approach to solving face recognition problems using a model like a graphical neural net trained to recover identity and perspective of input faces. Competitive results on the MultiPIE benchmark and interesting hard tests.
