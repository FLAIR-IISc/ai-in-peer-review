This research paper studies multi-view face recognition in a constrained environment. It proposes a multi-layer multi-view perceptron (MVP) network to estimate the recognition and visual angle representation from a 2D image using deterministic and random neurons. The MVP is capable of synthesizing new visual representations of faces by sequencing visual representations from a 2D face. The paper achieves this by using Monte Carlo Expectation Maximization (MCEM) to learn the MVP from a pair of faces and visual labels, assuming that the output face follows a conditional diagonal Gaussian distribution. The MVP is learned from 200 individuals in a multi-PE dataset, and the recognition features are used to classify 137 individuals reset by the LDA, achieving performance similar to recent methods using convolutional neural networks.

This research paper proposes a new MVP network to separate face recognition and visual angle representation, and uses recognition features in the LDA for multi-view face recognition. Changing the visual angle feature in the MVP allows new visual representations of faces to be synthesized. Overall, the technical approach is sound with some limitations, and the performance on multi-PE is very competitive. Here are some technical concerns.

This approach relies on well-aligned generalized 32x32 face images. Experiments on face recognition among 137 individuals on multi-PE raise questions about the scalability of the proposed method. It is unclear whether the method could benefit from more training samples and how to extend the approach to unrestricted face recognition in real-world applications.

How important is it to assume a conditional diagonal Gaussian distribution for the output y? How many training pairs from 200 individuals are used to learn the MVP in multi-PE? Why should new visual representations of faces be synthesized "incrementally"?

The claim that this new MVP "simulates how the human brain encodes visual representations" is quite weak. The paper does not present any concrete evidence or discussion on how the human brain encodes visual representations and why the mechanism is similar to the MVP. Therefore, I will not list it as a major contribution of the research paper.

This is a good work on multi-view perceptron (MVP) learning for multi-view face recognition. Performance on multi-PE is competitive. However, I have concerns about the scalability of this method and how it should be applied to face recognition systems.
