This paper studies multi-view face recognition in a constrained environment. It proposes a multi-level multi-view perceptron (MVP) network to estimate identity and visual angle representation from a 2D image, using deterministic and random neurons. The MVP is capable of synthesizing new visual representations of faces by indexing visual representations from a 2D face. The paper achieves this by learning the MVP from a pair of faces and visual labels using Monte Carlo Expectation Maximization (MCEM), assuming that the output face follows a conditional diagonal Gaussian distribution. The MVP is learned from 200 individuals in a multi-PE dataset, and the identity features are used to classify 137 individuals reset by the LDA, achieving performance similar to recent methods using convolutional neural networks.

This paper proposes a new MVP network to separate face recognition and visual angle representation, and uses identity features in the LDA for multi-view face recognition. Changing the visual angle feature in the MVP allows for the synthesis of new visual representations of faces. Overall, the technical approach is solid with some limitations, and the performance on multi-PE is very competitive. Here are some technical concerns.

This approach relies on well-aligned generalized 32x32 face images. Experiments on face recognition among 137 individuals on multi-PE raise questions about the scalability of the proposed method. It is unclear whether the method could benefit from more training samples and how to extend the approach to unrestricted face recognition in real-world applications.

How important is assuming a conditional diagonal Gaussian distribution for the output y? How many training pairs from 200 individuals are used to learn the MVP in multi-PE? Why should new visual representations of faces be synthesized "incrementally"?

The claim that this new MVP "simulates how the human brain encodes visual representation" is quite weak. The paper does not present any concrete evidence or discussion about how the human brain encodes visual representation and why the mechanism is similar to the MVP. Therefore, I will not list it as a major contribution to the paper.

This is a good work on learning multi-view perceptrons (MVP) for multi-view face recognition. Performance on multi-PE is competitive. However, I have concerns about the scalability of this method and how it should be applied to face recognition systems.
