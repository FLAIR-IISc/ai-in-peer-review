This paper presents a new approach for multi-view representation of faces using models like neural nets, featuring deterministic and probabilistic units. The network is trained to recover identities and views. It can also be used to synthesize a new view. Generally, this paper does a good job of distinguishing the reasons for the method's success, and the results seem quite good.

I wish the authors had presented more evidence for how they designed their model. Firstly, it's not clear whether a non-deterministic approach is really needed in this case – could they have created a fully feed-forward model with deterministic neurons? This would certainly avoid the need for variational/importance sampling assumptions.

Quality: Generally, a good paper with an interesting and well-established model, which has been well-evaluated in a competitive benchmark.
Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to read in most parts.
Originality: In some cases, this approach is comparable to the work of Geoff Hinton and his colleagues, who use stacks of Restricted Boltzmann machines, where the top RBM label can be a side.
Importance: This is likely of interest to the facial recognition community, but also to other researchers who work on problems where a hybrid deterministic and generative approach might be a good solution.

Comments and Questions:
- How did the authors create the structure in Figure 2? What are the insights behind the design choices?
- The authors should have defined q(h) at line 165.
- Is the MCEM method extremely important for the presented model? Further analysis is needed as to why this particular optimization method was used, as MCEM is not a very commonly used method in the community.
- For the assumptions: How large is S at line 235? How high is the assumption cost typically? Some numbers and O() notation would be better.
- Is LDA necessary to get the best results? What do the results look like without LDA?
- I don't think the authors should use tangent with how their brain works, very rarely. There is not much evidence that the presented method is very close to human facial recognition or view point interpolation.
- Did the authors test their method on labeled faces in the Wild Benchmark? If yes, do they have results to report?

An interesting new approach to solving face recognition problems using a model like a graphical neural net trained to recover the identity of input faces and their views. Competitive results and interesting hard tests in the MultiPIE benchmark.
