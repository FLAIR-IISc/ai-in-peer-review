This paper discusses multi-view face recognition in a constrained environment. The paper proposes a multi-layer multi-view perceptron (MVP) network that can estimate identity and view angle representations from a single 2D image using deterministic and random neurons. The MVP is capable of generating new views of a face through a sequence of view representations from a single 2D face. The paper develops a Monte Carlo Expectation Maximization (MCEM) method for learning MVP from a pair of face and view labels, assuming that the output face follows a conditional diagonal Gaussian distribution. The MVP was learned from 200 individuals in a multi-PIE dataset, and identity features were used to classify 137 individuals reset by LDA, achieving performance similar to recent methods using convolutional neural networks.

The paper proposes a new MVP network for distinguishing face identity and view angle representations and uses identity features in LDA for multi-view face recognition. New views of a face can be generated by changing the view angle feature in the MVP. Overall, the technical approach is good with some limitations, and its performance in multi-PIE is very competitive. Some technical concerns are listed below:

This method seems to rely heavily on well-aligned normalized 32x32 face images. Experiments on face recognition in multi-PIE with 137 individuals raise questions about the scalability of the proposed method. It is unclear whether the method can benefit from more training samples and how the method can be extended for unrestricted face recognition in real-world applications.

How important is assuming a conditional diagonal Gaussian distribution for the output y? How many training pairs of 200 individuals are used to learn the MVP in multi-PIE? Why do new face views have to be synthesized "sequentially"?

The claim that this new MVP "simulates how the human brain encodes view representations" is quite weak. The paper does not present any convincing evidence or discussion about how the human brain encodes view representations and why this process is similar to the MVP. Therefore, I will not list it as a core contribution of the paper.

It is a good work on learning multi-view perceptron (MVP) for multi-view face recognition. Its performance in multi-PIE is competitive. Although I have concerns about the scalability of this method and how it can be applied to a face recognition system.
