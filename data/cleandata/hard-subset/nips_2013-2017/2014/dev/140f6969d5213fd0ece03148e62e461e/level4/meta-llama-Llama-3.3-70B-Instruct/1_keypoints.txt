- Introduces an innovative multi-view face representation combining deterministic and probabilistic neural components.  
- Network trained to reconstruct identity and viewpoint and capable of generating novel views.  
- Paper isolates key contributing factors and reports high-quality results.  
- Needs stronger justification for the non-deterministic design; unclear if a fully feed‑forward deterministic model would suffice.  
- Unclear whether variational/importance-sampling inference is necessary versus deterministic alternatives.  
- Quality: well-grounded model evaluated on a competitive benchmark with generally good results.  
- Clarity: manuscript is well-written, clear, and concise.  
- Originality: resembles Hinton-style RBM work but the hybrid deterministic/generative angle is a novel perspective.  
- Significance: relevant to face-recognition researchers and those exploring hybrid deterministic/generative methods.  
- Request to explain design choices behind the architecture shown in Figure 2.  
- Request to explicitly define q(h) referenced at line 165.  
- Ask whether MCEM is crucial to performance and rationale for choosing this relatively uncommon optimizer.  
- Request details on inference: size of S at line 235, computational cost, numerical results, and big-O analysis.  
- Ask whether LDA is essential and to report results without LDA.  
- Caution against over-claiming biological plausibility of the method’s relation to human face recognition/viewpoint interpolation.  
- Ask whether results were evaluated on Labeled Faces in the Wild (LFW) and, if so, to report them.  
- Overall: novel, interesting graphical neural-network-like approach with competitive MultiPIE results and solid experiments.
