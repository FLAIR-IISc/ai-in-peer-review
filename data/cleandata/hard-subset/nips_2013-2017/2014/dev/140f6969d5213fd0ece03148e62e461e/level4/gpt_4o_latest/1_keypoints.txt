- Introduces a novel multi-view face representation model combining deterministic and probabilistic units to recover identity and view and synthesize new views.
- Effectively disentangles factors contributing to performance and reports strong results on MultiPIE.
- Unclear justification for using non-deterministic units — could a fully feed-forward deterministic model suffice and avoid variational/importance-sampling inference?
- Paper quality: generally strong, interesting model, and effective evaluation on a competitive benchmark.
- Clarity: well-written and mostly easy to follow.
- Originality: bears resemblance to prior work (Hinton et al.) using stacked RBMs with top RBM labels.
- Significance: likely of interest to facial recognition researchers and those exploring hybrid deterministic/generative models.
- Request for motivation/justification of the specific architecture shown in Figure 2.
- Need explicit definition of q(h) referenced on line 165.
- Question whether MCEM is critical to the model and request for more analysis justifying its use given limited adoption in the community.
- For inference, ask for the size of S (line 235), overall computational cost, and numerical or O()-notation complexity.
- Ask whether LDA is essential for top results and for comparative results without LDA.
- Recommend minimizing speculative references to how the brain works unless strongly justified.
- Ask whether the method was evaluated on Labeled Faces in the Wild (LFW) and request comparative results if available.
