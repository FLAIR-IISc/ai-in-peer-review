- Proposes a multi-layer multi-view perceptron (MVP) that disentangles identity and view-angle representations using deterministic and random neurons.
- MVP can generate new face views from a single 2D image by sequencing view representations.
- Learning is performed via a Monte Carlo Expectation Maximization (MCEM) procedure with a conditional diagonal Gaussian output assumption.
- Trained on 200 individuals from MultiPIE; learned identity features used with LDA to classify the remaining 137 individuals.
- Achieves performance comparable to recent convolutional neural network–based methods on MultiPIE.
- Technical contribution: novel disentangling approach and viable generative mechanism for view synthesis.
- Major limitation: relies on well-aligned, normalized 32×32 face images, raising concerns about real-world applicability.
- Scalability concerns due to experiment scale (137-class recognition) and unclear generalization to larger, unconstrained datasets.
- Methodological concerns about the conditional diagonal Gaussian assumption and unspecified number of training pairs used.
- Questionable necessity and efficiency of sequential view synthesis as presented.
- Claim that MVP simulates human brain encoding of view representations is not convincingly supported and should not be a key contribution.
- Overall: competent and competitive study but with unresolved issues around scalability and practical deployment.
