This paper proposes using an autoencoder with both deterministic and stochastic units to learn to reconstruct the face of the same person from different three-dimensional perspectives.

It is well-written, and the experiments are quite robust.

The model in Figure 2 has some similarities to [25]'s model. The main difference is that v is added and it is predicted by both y and h3. This is useful for being able to use a feedforward prediction of v instead of using Bayes' rule. Perhaps it would be a good idea to discuss these differences in the text.

Small points:
Summary: The word 'instinct' could be replaced with 'intuition' or 'insight'.
Are there any test or training images in Figures 1 and 6?

To further strengthen the paper, the authors could try using [24]'s STOA Siamese CNN method and compare it with the proposed method. This paper proposes using an autoencoder for pose representation learning on facial data. The problems are interesting, and the experiments indicate the usefulness of the method.
