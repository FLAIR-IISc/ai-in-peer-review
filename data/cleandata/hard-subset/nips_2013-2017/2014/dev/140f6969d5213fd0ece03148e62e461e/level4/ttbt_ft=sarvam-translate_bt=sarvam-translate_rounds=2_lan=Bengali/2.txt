This paper proposes using an autoencoder with both deterministic and stochastic units to learn to reconstruct the face of the same person from three different perspectives.

It is well-written, and the experiments are quite robust.

Figure 2 shows some similarities between the model and [25]'s model. The main difference is that v is added and it is predicted by both y and h3. This is useful for being able to use a feedforward prediction of v without using Bayes' rule. Perhaps it would be a good idea to discuss these differences in the text.

Minor points:
Summary: The word 'instinct' could be replaced with 'intuition' or 'insight'.
Are there any test or training figures in figures 1 and 6?

To further strengthen the paper, the authors could compare the proposed method with [24]'s STOA Siamese CNN method. This paper proposes using an autoencoder for pose representation learning in facial data. The problems are interesting, and the experiments indicate the usefulness of the method.
