- Proposes a multi-layer MVP network that disentangles identity and view-angle representations and can synthesize novel face views from a single 2D image.  
- Introduces an MCEM training procedure assuming the output image follows a conditional diagonal Gaussian distribution.  
- Evaluated on MultiPIE: trained on 200 individuals, identity features used with LDA to classify the remaining 137, achieving performance comparable to recent CNN methods.  
- Method is technically sound and innovative in separating identity and view for recognition and synthesis.  
- Relies on well-aligned, normalized 32×32 images, raising scalability and resolution concerns.  
- Experiments are limited to 137 test individuals, leaving applicability to larger datasets uncertain.  
- Unclear whether and how performance improves with additional training samples or extends to unconstrained real-world recognition.  
- Key technical questions remain: how critical is the conditional diagonal Gaussian assumption, how many training pairs were used, and why must synthesis be “sequential.”  
- The claim that MVP mimics human brain encoding of views is unsubstantiated and should not be treated as a main contribution.  
- Overall: solid, competitive work on multi-view face recognition but with notable concerns about scalability and real-world applicability.
