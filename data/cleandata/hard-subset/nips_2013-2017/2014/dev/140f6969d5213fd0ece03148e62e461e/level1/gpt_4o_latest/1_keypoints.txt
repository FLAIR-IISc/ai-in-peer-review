- Introduces Multi-View Perceptron (MVP), a probabilistic DNN that disentangles identity (deterministic neurons) and view (random neurons) to reconstruct and interpolate multi-view faces from a single 2D image.
- Evaluated on MultiPIE, showing superior face recognition across views/illuminations and capability for viewpoint estimation and interpolation.

- Novel, biologically inspired architecture separating identity and view representations.
- Generative capability to reconstruct and interpolate unseen viewpoints.
- Strong empirical results on MultiPIE, achieving state-of-the-art performance under challenging conditions.
- Efficient training using a variational lower bound and MCEM with few samples needed for convergence.

- Dense mathematical derivations and limited intuitive explanations reduce accessibility.
- Comparisons are limited to a few baselines, especially in viewpoint estimation.
- Reconstructed images exhibit noise and blurring, limiting high-resolution applicability.
- Unclear scalability and generalization to larger, more diverse real-world datasets.

- Novel approach with strong empirical support merits acceptance.
- Generative/interpolation capabilities represent a significant contribution.
- Method is computationally efficient and robust under challenging conditions.

- Clarity should be improved for wider accessibility.
- Evaluation needs broader comparisons and larger-scale testing.
- Reconstruction quality may limit practical applications.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve readability and expand comparative evaluation.
