- Proposes unsupervised invariance learning by creating surrogate classes from transformed random image patches.  
- Uses 2D transformations including rotations, scaling, translations, and color shifts to form surrogate classes.  
- Formal analysis is compelling and offers useful theoretical insights into the method’s effectiveness.  
- Manuscript is well-written and experiments investigate effects of number of transformations and network size.  
- Limitation: two randomly selected patches with very similar content can be "forced apart" if assigned to different surrogate classes.  
- Mitigation: authors cap patches at 32,000, but this raises concerns about generalization to larger datasets and needs clarification.  
- Suggestion: evaluate transferability by using the same surrogate classes for unsupervised learning and then training dataset-specific classifiers (e.g., STL-10 vs CIFAR-10) instead of retraining per dataset.  
- Suggestion: include experiments varying patch sizes to further analyze the method’s behavior.
