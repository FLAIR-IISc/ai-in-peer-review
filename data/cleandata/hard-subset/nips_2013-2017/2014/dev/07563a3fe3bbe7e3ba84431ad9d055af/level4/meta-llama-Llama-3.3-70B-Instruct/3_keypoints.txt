- Presents a compelling method for acquiring invariances in unsupervised image classification.  
- Core idea: construct surrogate classes by applying transformation groups (rotations, scaling, translations, color shifts) to random patches.  
- Provides formal analysis that explains why the methodology could be effective.  
- Manuscript is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Experiments are thorough, exploring effects of number of transformations and network size.  
- Potential limitation: method may forcefully treat highly similar random patches as distinct surrogate classes.  
- Authors mitigate this by limiting surrogate patches to 32,000, but this restriction may harm generalization on larger datasets—authors should comment.  
- Suggested experiment: use a fixed set of surrogate classes for unsupervised learning and then train dataset-specific classifiers (e.g., transfer between STL-10 and CIFAR-10) instead of retraining surrogates per dataset.  
- Suggested analysis: study the impact of varying patch sizes on results.
