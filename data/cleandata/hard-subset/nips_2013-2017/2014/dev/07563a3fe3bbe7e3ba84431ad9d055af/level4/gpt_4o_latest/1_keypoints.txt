- Proposes a novel unsupervised CNN training method to learn invariance to common transformations.  
- Method is simple and elegant: train the CNN to distinguish image patches and their transformations from other patches and transformations.  
- Enables learning of transformation invariances but is vulnerable to "collisions" where distinct patches share identical content.  
- Innovative and clever approach, though not immediately intuitive; merits publication with minor revisions.  
- Theoretical analysis is unconvincing, essentially stating that a perfect invariant representation would be optimal (a tautology).  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, probing collision effects with increasing surrogate classes and measuring learned invariance.  
- Major concern: reported results for STL-10, CIFAR-10, and Caltech-101 omit comparisons to true state-of-the-art, especially for CIFAR-10.  
- Excluding standard discriminatively-trained CNN baselines is unjustified and creates a misleading impression about performance.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-supported paper with a simple, clever idea.
