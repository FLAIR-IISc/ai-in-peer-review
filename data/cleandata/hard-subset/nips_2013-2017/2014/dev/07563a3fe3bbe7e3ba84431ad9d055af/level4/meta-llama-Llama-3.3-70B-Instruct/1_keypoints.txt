- Proposes a simple, elegant unsupervised method training CNNs to learn invariance by distinguishing image patches from their transformed versions.  
- Method is straightforward but effective at encouraging transformation invariance.  
- Susceptible to "collisions" where distinct patches with identical content are incorrectly distinguished by the model.  
- Theoretical analysis is weak/trivial, essentially restating that perfect invariance yields the global optimum and may overlook subtler insights.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, including tests on vulnerability as the number of surrogate classes increases and direct measures of learned invariance.  
- Presentation of state-of-the-art results is misleading: comparisons omit true SOTA baselines, notably discriminatively-trained CNNs for CIFAR-10.  
- Authors’ justification for excluding standard discriminative baselines appears arbitrary and could mislead readers about performance.  
- Recommend adding true SOTA comparisons (with and without dataset transformations) to fairly contextualize results.  
- Overall: well-written, novel, and clever work that merits publication after minor revisions.
