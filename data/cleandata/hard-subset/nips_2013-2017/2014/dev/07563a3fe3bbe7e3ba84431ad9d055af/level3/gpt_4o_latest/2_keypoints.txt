- Proposes Exemplar-CNN: unsupervised feature learning using surrogate classes from seed patches and transformations to train a CNN.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on STL-10, CIFAR-10, and Caltech-101, especially in low-data regimes.
- Method is conceptually simple, combining discriminative supervised-style training with unsupervised data efficiency.
- Provides insightful analysis of transformation choices and invariance properties of the learned features.
- Paper is well-written with sufficient experimental details (architectures, training, hyperparameters) for reproducibility.
- Lacks clarity on some design choices, notably the rationale for 32×32 patch size and detailed patch extraction procedure.
- Would benefit from visual illustrations of the CNN during training and feature extraction to aid accessibility.
- Does not compare against pre-trained CNNs (e.g., ImageNet) to contextualize performance.
- Fails to thoroughly explore the relationship between surrogate task validation and final classification validation; tabular results would help.
- Contains discrepancies between supplementary material and main paper results that need clarification.
- Scalability concerns: surrogate classes may conflict and the surrogate task may be too simple for very large datasets.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution worthy of acceptance, but would be improved by addressing clarity, scalability, and comparative analysis issues.
