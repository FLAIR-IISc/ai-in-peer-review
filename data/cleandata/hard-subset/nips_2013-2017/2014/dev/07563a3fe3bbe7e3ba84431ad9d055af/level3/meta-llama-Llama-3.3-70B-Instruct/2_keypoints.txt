- Proposes a novel denoising method for highly noisy sensor networks using local best-response dynamics enabling fast learning from few label queries.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for denoising power, including a positive result for synchronous updates.  
- Proves a negative result showing failure for arbitrary-order asynchronous updates.  
- Experimental results demonstrate the approach’s practical effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with a clear motivation and concise contributions.  
- Related work section is thorough and situates the paper well.  
- Technical analysis is sound and detailed, with well-argued proofs and reasoning.  
- Choice of 32x32 patch size is not well-justified and needs further explanation or analysis.  
- Lacks visual aids (e.g., diagrams/pictures) to illustrate the convnet during surrogate training and feature extraction.  
- Needs more detailed comparison to baseline methods (e.g., pre-trained ImageNet convnet + linear classifier).  
- Not robust to adversarial update orders, which may limit applicability.  
- Raises interesting future-work questions, such as finding dynamics robust to adversarial orders and improving denoising near decision boundaries.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical analysis, empirical validation, and clear future directions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unexplained patch size choice, missing visuals, insufficient baseline comparisons, and adversarial vulnerability.
