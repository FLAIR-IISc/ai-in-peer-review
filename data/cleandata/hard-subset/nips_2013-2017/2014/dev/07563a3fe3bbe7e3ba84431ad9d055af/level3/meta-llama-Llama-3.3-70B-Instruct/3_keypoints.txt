- Proposes a novel game-theoretic, local-communication approach to denoising highly noisy sensor networks.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for the denoising power of the proposed dynamics.  
- Thorough analysis of dynamics: positive result for synchronous updates.  
- Negative result showing failure under arbitrary-order asynchronous updates.  
- Proposes an alternative dynamics robust to arbitrary update orders (in supplement) but more computationally expensive.  
- Experimental results show effective denoising and learning a target function from few label queries.  
- Empirically outperforms active and passive learning baselines without denoising.  
- Demonstrates handling of different noise types, including random noise and pockets of noise.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow with clear problem setup and presentation.  
- Weakness: not robust to adversarial/update-order attacks per Section 3.2.  
- Weakness: alternative robust dynamics may be impractical due to higher computational cost.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to synthetic data and may not generalize to real-world scenarios.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10; Confidence 0.8.
