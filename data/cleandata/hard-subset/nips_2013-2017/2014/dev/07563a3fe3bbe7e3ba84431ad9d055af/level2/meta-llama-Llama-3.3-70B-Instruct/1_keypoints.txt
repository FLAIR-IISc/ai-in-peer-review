- Novel unsupervised feature learning method: CNN trained discriminatively to distinguish surrogate classes formed by transformations of randomly sampled image patches.
- Key claim: achieves state-of-the-art performance on STL-10, CIFAR-10, and Caltech-101 without labeled data.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise explanation of the approach.
- Related work section is comprehensive and situates the contribution well.
- Experimental results are impressive and include detailed analysis across datasets.
- Strength: innovative methodology with clear exposition.
- Strength: strong empirical performance on multiple popular benchmarks.
- Strength: thorough literature review and overall paper quality.
- Weakness: scalability concerns — surrogate class count grows with dataset size and many classes may become similar.
- Weakness: surrogate task is relatively simple and may not encourage invariance to complex variations (e.g., 3D viewpoint changes, inter-instance variation).
- Overall recommendation: Accept — significant contribution to unsupervised feature learning.
