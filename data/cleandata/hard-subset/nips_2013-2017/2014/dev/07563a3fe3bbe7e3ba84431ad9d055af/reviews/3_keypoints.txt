- Proposes a novel unsupervised method to learn invariances by creating surrogate classes of transformed random patches (rotations, scaling, translations, color shifts).
- Formal analysis is interesting and illuminates why the approach could work.
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.
- Experiments cover several aspects, including number of transformations and network size.
- Drawback: very similar randomly selected patches may be forced apart because they become different surrogate classes.
- Authors partially sidestep this by limiting to 32,000 patches, but using few surrogate patches may impede generalization on larger datasets.
- Suggested experiment: reuse the same surrogate classes across datasets (e.g., train unsupervised once and apply to STL-10 and CIFAR-10) instead of retraining per dataset.
- Suggested experiment: evaluate effect of varying patch size.
