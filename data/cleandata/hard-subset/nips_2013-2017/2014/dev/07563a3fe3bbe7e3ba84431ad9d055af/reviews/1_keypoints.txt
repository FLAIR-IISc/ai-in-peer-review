- Proposes a novel, simple unsupervised method to train CNNs to be invariant to common transformations.  
- Method: train the CNN to distinguish image patches and their transformations from other patches and transformed patches.  
- Approach effectively encourages invariance but is vulnerable to "collisions" where distinct patches share the same content.  
- The idea is novel and clever; with minor revisions it merits publication.  
- Theoretical analysis is unconvincing — it mostly restates that a perfect invariant representation would optimize the objective.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and explores the collision vulnerability and invariance properties.  
- Results appear solid and mature on datasets like STL-10, CIFAR-10, and Caltech-101.  
- Major concern: authors omit true state-of-the-art comparisons (especially discriminatively-trained CNNs on CIFAR-10), creating a misleading impression of performance.  
- Request: include true SOTA comparisons for each dataset, with and without dataset transformations.
