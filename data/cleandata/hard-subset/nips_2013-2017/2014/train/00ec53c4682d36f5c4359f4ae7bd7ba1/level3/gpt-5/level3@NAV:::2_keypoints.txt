- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial stream on single RGB frames (ImageNet pre-trained) and temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow; streams fused at score level (averaging or linear SVM).
- Conducts thorough ablations on optical-flow inputs: stack length, trajectory vs. location stacking, mean subtraction, and bi-directional flow.
- Introduces multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited training data.
- Achieves substantial improvements over prior ConvNet-based methods and is competitive with state-of-the-art improved dense trajectory pipelines.
- Properly situates work relative to prior deep video models and the two-stream hypothesis; demonstrates that optical-flow inputs generalize classic motion descriptors within a learnable CNN.
- Methodology is technically sound with extensive, careful experimentation; benefits of optical-flow input and two-stream fusion are convincingly shown.
- Minor methodological gap: lacks analysis explaining why the spatial stream outperforms comparable single-frame CNN baselines.
- Paper is clearly written and organized; implementation details are sufficient for reproduction but frame-sampling policy and runtime/storage costs for precomputed flow need clarification.
- Originality: components are known, but their combination into a simple, effective two-stream framework and systematic exploration of flow strategies is novel and insightful.
- Significance: advances deep video recognition, narrows the gap to trajectory-based methods, and is likely to influence future video modeling and multimodal fusion.
- Suggestions: provide controlled comparison for spatial stream gains; evaluate different frame-sampling schemes (uniform, random, motion-guided); report compute/storage/throughput; discuss end-to-end learned motion.
- Pros: strong empirical gains, thorough ablations and clear insights, simple general approach with effective fusion, careful evaluation on two benchmarks with multi-task benefits.
- Cons: limited analysis of spatial stream superiority, dependence on precomputed optical flow (compute/storage overhead), and missing exploration of spatial frame-sampling strategies.
