- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture separating spatial (frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) processing.  
- Demonstrates that incorporating optical flow yields significant performance gains for temporal modeling.  
- Explores multi-task learning to further improve accuracy.  
- Extends image-based deep learning techniques to the temporal domain, addressing prior limited success of frame-only CNNs.  
- Methodology and implementation are described in detail, aiding reproducibility.  
- Achieves strong, competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, rivaling hand-crafted feature methods.  
- Novelty lies in decoupling spatial and temporal streams and the multi-task learning exploration.  
- Paper feels fragmented, presenting three distinct contributions without a strong unifying narrative.  
- Recommend tighter focus emphasizing the two-stream/optical-flow integration to increase impact.  
- Related work is missing important citations (CVPR 2011 Independent Subspace Analysis; BMVC 2010 evaluation of spatio-temporal features).  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2014) conditional on addressing focus and missing citations.
