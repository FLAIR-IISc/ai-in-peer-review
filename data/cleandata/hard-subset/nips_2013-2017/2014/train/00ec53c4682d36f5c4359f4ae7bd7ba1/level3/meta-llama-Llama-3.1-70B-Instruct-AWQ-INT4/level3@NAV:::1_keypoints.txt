- Proposes two-stream ConvNets (separate spatial and temporal networks) for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream is trained on multi-frame dense optical flow; spatial stream uses individual frames; predictions are averaged.  
- Achieves competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, comparable to state-of-the-art hand-crafted feature methods.  
- Strength: simple ConvNet application that yields strong performance.  
- Strength: novel demonstration that optical-flow inputs are effective for action recognition.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation, including different input configurations and multi-task learning effects.  
- Weakness: model only captures short fixed-length temporal windows (L=10), not whole-video structure.  
- Weakness: experiments lack error-mode analysis (no per-class failures or analysis of failure types).  
- Paper is clearly written, model well-explained, and related work is thoroughly discussed.  
- Originality: novel application of ConvNets to optical-flow features.  
- Significance: approach could substantially impact video/motion research and applications (HCI, surveillance, healthcare).  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address limitations and add discussion of future research directions.
