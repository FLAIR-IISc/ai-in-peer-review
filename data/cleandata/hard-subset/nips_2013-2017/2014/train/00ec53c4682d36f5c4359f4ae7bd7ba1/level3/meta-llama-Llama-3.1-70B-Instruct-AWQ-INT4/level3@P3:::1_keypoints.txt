- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture separating spatial (frames) and temporal (optical flow) recognition, fused late.  
- Achieves results comparable to state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Key innovation: decoupling spatial and temporal streams and using ConvNets for both.  
- Novel contribution: using optical flow as input to the temporal stream to explicitly represent motion.  
- Experiments are well-designed and clearly demonstrate the architecture’s effectiveness.  
- Temporal-stream input ablations (stacking multiple flows, trajectory stacking, mean displacement subtraction) were investigated.  
- Findings: stacking multiple flows and mean subtraction improve performance; differences between stacking methods are marginal.  
- Explores multi-task learning to increase effective training data and shows it improves performance on both datasets.  
- Paper is clear and well-written; model and results are easy to understand.  
- Major limitation: model only captures short fixed-length temporal windows and does not model whole-video sequence structure.  
- Significant weakness: lacks detailed error-mode analysis to characterize failure cases.  
- Originality is high due to the two-stream design and optical-flow temporal input.  
- Significance is high; architecture has potential to impact video and motion research.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address long-term dependency limitation and add detailed error analysis.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
