- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial net on RGB frames and temporal net on stacked dense optical flow, fused by averaging/SVM.  
- Temporal network uses L=10 flow frames with mean-flow subtraction; analyzes variants like trajectory stacking and bi-directional flow.  
- Employs multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate data scarcity.  
- Achieves competitive results with hand-crafted pipelines (e.g., improved dense trajectories + Fisher vectors).  
- Clearly outperforms prior deep models trained on stacked RGB frames and shallow HMAX-style systems.  
- Central contribution: empirically demonstrates that feeding optical flow to ConvNets is highly effective for action recognition.  
- Experimental design is careful with sensible ablations (flow length, stacking, mean subtraction, fusion, multi-task).  
- Results substantiate the claims and provide a practical recipe likely to influence future work.  
- Limitation: models only short, fixed temporal windows (L=10) and lacks sequence-level temporal modeling.  
- Limitation: little error-mode analysis (per-class confusions, typical failure modes, sensitivity to camera motion/scene).  
- Limitation: not fully end-to-end since optical flow is precomputed; runtime/storage tradeoffs and sensitivity to flow quality are not analyzed.  
- Clarity: paper is well written, organized, and detailed enough for reproduction; adding confusion matrices would help.  
- Originality: novel application of ConvNets to stacked optical flow and timely relative to prior deep video work.  
- Significance: simple, strong approach that narrows gap with hand-crafted SOTA and suggests useful hybrids.  
- Suggestions: add per-class/attribute error analysis; evaluate longer temporal contexts (temporal pyramids/sequence models); compare flow algorithms; report computational cost; explore end-to-end differentiable motion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear demonstration of optical-flow efficacy, solid ablations, likely high impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited temporal modeling, missing error-mode and computational analyses, reliance on precomputed flow and storage overhead.
