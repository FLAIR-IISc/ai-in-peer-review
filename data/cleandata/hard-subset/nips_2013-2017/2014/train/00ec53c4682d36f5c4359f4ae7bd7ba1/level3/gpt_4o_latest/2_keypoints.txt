- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet combining spatial (single-frame) and temporal (optical flow) streams for video action recognition, achieving SOTA on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Uses dense optical flow for the temporal stream, explicitly capturing motion and outperforming raw stacked frames.  
- Employs multi-task learning across datasets to boost performance on smaller datasets like HMDB-51.  
- Strength: Architecture effectively decouples spatial and temporal information and is biologically plausible.  
- Strength: Achieves competitive state-of-the-art results vs. deep and hand-crafted methods.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation, including optical flow configurations, multi-task learning, and fusion strategies.  
- Strength: Practical scalability considerations (pre-computed flow, multi-GPU training).  
- Weakness: Paper does not explain why the spatial stream outperforms similar prior architectures.  
- Weakness: Limited investigation of frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.  
- Weakness: Reliance on pre-computed optical flow restricts end-to-end learning and may hinder real-time use.  
- Weakness: Superficial treatment of the two-stream hypothesis; missed theoretical/biological insights.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, effective architecture with strong empirical results on standard benchmarks.  
- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient theoretical insight and limited explanations reduce scientific depth.  
- Argument against acceptance: Dependency on external optical-flow computation may limit practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve theoretical analysis and clarify design choices.
