- Proposes a novel two-stream convolutional network for action recognition that incorporates optical flow to capture motion information.  
- Clear, well-written description of methodology, implementation, and procedures that supports reproducibility.  
- Strong empirical results with impressive improvements over prior work and competitiveness with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Extensive evaluation on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 demonstrating the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Uses multi-task learning for the temporal ConvNet to increase effective training data and reduce overfitting.  
- Technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.  
- Excellent clarity and logical organization; figures and tables are well-placed and understandable.  
- Paper feels split across multiple contributions and would benefit from refocusing the abstract and introduction on core contributions.  
- Missing citations to relevant prior work (e.g., CVPR 2011 ISA paper and BMVC 2010 local spatio-temporal features evaluation).  
- Original and significant contributions—two-stream formulation and multi-task learning advance video classification.  
- High significance and potential for wide adoption; advances the state of the art in action recognition.  
- Recommendation: accept at NIPS 2014 conditional on addressing suggested improvements.
