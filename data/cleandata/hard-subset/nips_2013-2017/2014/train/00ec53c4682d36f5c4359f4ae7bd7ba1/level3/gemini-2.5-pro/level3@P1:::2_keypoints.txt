- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: a spatial stream (ImageNet-pretrained frames) and a temporal stream (ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow), fused for final action classification.
- Shows temporal ConvNet on optical flow greatly improves learning of motion compared to feeding raw frames.
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving performance competitive with or better than prior deep and hand-crafted methods.
- Technically sound with comprehensive experiments that strongly support the claims.
- Includes a detailed ablation study (stacked flows, trajectory stacking, mean subtraction, bi-directional flow) that clarifies effective design choices for the temporal stream.
- Uses multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited temporal-stream training data, a principled solution.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient implementation detail for reproducibility.
- Clearly motivates the two-stream idea (analogy to ventral/dorsal streams) and relates learned features to hand-crafted descriptors like HOF/MBH.
- Originality lies in applying ConvNets to dense optical flow and separating appearance/motion into dedicated deep streams, not in new layer types.
- Significant contribution demonstrating deep learning can match trajectory-based methods and that explicit motion input is crucial for video.
- Strength: excellent empirical performance advancing deep models for action recognition.
- Strength: strong empirical/ablation evaluation offering actionable insights to the community.
- Strength: elegant, effective two-stream idea and clear reproducibility.
- Weakness: spatial network's unusually strong ImageNet validation result needs more substantiation or direct comparison.
- Weakness: simple uniform frame sampling (25 frames) for the spatial stream may be suboptimal; smarter sampling (e.g., high-motion regions) could improve results.
- Overall: a strong, influential empirical paper with rigorous experiments; recommended for acceptance.
