- Proposes a two-stream CNN that decouples spatial appearance and temporal motion for action recognition.  
- Trains separate networks: one on individual frames and one on stacked dense optical flow, then averages predictions.  
- Conceptually simple yet effective, achieving competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Demonstrates that ConvNets trained on optical flow can reach state-of-the-art performance.  
- Builds on prior video-CNN attempts and effectively generalizes trajectory-based representations in a data-driven way.  
- Uses multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to boost performance.  
- Strength: straightforward architecture, strong empirical results, and clear, well-organized writing.  
- Weakness: limited temporal modeling due to short fixed-length window (L=10), missing long-range dependencies.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed error-mode analysis (e.g., per-class failures or types of challenging videos).  
- Experimental quality: well-designed experiments and clear results; missing error analysis is a minor shortcoming.  
- Originality: applying CNNs to optical flow is not entirely novel but is a significant and impactful contribution.  
- Significance: approach is promising for advancing video and motion research toward more robust action recognition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong performance, effective optical-flow CNN demonstration, clarity, and field impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: constrained temporal modeling and absent error analysis.  
- Overall: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommendation for acceptance.
