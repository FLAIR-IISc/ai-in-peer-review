- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a spatial stream (RGB frames) and a temporal stream (stacked dense optical flow) for video action recognition.  
- Claims three contributions: two-stream architecture, ConvNets trained on dense optical flow work well with limited data, and a multi-task learning framework to combine datasets.  
- Builds on the two-stream hypothesis from neuroscience but implements it with deep learning instead of hand-crafted features.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and technically sound, using UCF-101 and HMDB-51 datasets.  
- Temporal stream using optical flow outperforms previous deep learning approaches by ~20% absolute accuracy.  
- Two-stream fusion achieves competitive performance with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.  
- Multi-task learning effectively addresses small dataset issues, improving HMDB-51 from 42.1% to 45.3%.  
- Methodology is well-described and reproducible; optical flow stacking is a clever, effective design choice.  
- Paper is very clear, well-organized, with good figures and accessible writing.  
- Originality is significant: using optical flow as temporal input is novel relative to prior stacked-frame deep methods.  
- Demonstrates that learned filters generalize hand-crafted motion descriptors (HOF, MBH) with supportive visualizations.  
- Critique: the paper should emphasize optical flow as the core insight rather than one of several contributions.  
- Critique: abstract and introduction should be refocused to highlight the two-stream/optical-flow innovation.  
- Critique: missing important related-work citations (CVPR 2011 ISA paper and BMVC 2010 spatio-temporal features evaluation).  
- Critique: clarify differences from prior HMAX two-stream architecture [16] and better integrate the multi-task learning as a natural extension of the two-stream framework.  
- Overall judgment: significant, well-executed contribution with recommendation for acceptance pending minor revisions (refocusing, added citations, clearer differentiation).
