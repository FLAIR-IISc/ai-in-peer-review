- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a spatial network (single frames) and a temporal network (stacked dense optical-flow).  
- Spatial stream processes appearance from individual frames; temporal stream processes motion via stacked optical flow.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving results comparable to state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature methods.  
- Strength: simple, straightforward ConvNet application that yields strong performance.  
- Novelty: demonstrates that optical-flow inputs are effective for ConvNet-based action recognition.  
- Weakness: only models short fixed-length temporal windows (L=10) and does not capture whole-video sequence structure.  
- Suggested fix: incorporate RNNs or other sequence modeling techniques to capture long-range temporal structure.  
- Experiments are of good quality but lack an error-mode analysis to diagnose failure cases.  
- Additional evaluation on other action recognition datasets would strengthen the results.  
- Paper is clearly written with a thorough related-work review and sufficient implementation/experimental details.  
- Original contribution is the novel application of ConvNets to optical-flow features.  
- Significance is high, with potential impact on video/motion research and applications (HCI, surveillance, robotics).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong results, clear exposition, and simplicity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited temporal modeling, no error-mode analysis, and no comparison with RNN/LSTM-based deep approaches.  
- Overall recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and should be accepted.
