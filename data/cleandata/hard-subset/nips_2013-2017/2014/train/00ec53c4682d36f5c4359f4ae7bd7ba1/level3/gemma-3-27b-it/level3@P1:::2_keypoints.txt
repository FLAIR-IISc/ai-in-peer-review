- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture that decouples spatial (single frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) processing and fuses their outputs.  
- Aims to combine appearance and motion cues to generalize and supersede hand-crafted motion features.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep-net attempts and matching hand-crafted-feature methods.  
- Builds on prior work contrasting hand-crafted descriptors (HOG/HOF/trajectories) and earlier ConvNet attempts using stacked frames.  
- Explicitly models temporal information via optical flow rather than stacked RGB frames.  
- Notes biological inspiration from ventral/dorsal visual streams but does not investigate this connection.  
- Strength: strong empirical results showing significant gains over previous deep-learning video methods.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation and ablation studies on temporal-stream configurations and mean-flow subtraction.  
- Strength: novel successful application of ConvNets to dense optical flow, removing need for manual motion descriptors.  
- Strength: uses multi-task learning to mitigate limited video training data and improve generalization.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written, well-organized, with informative figures and tables.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why the spatial stream matches prior ConvNet performance beyond ImageNet pre-training.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of frame sampling schemes and temporal subsampling strategies during testing.  
- Weakness: brief and incomplete discussion of the biological ventral/dorsal inspiration.  
- For acceptance: technically sound, well-supported, impressive empirical advance, likely influential.  
- Against acceptance: improvements are largely empirical with limited theoretical justification, and biological motivation is underdeveloped.  
- Overall: strong empirical contribution to video action recognition; recommended for acceptance.
