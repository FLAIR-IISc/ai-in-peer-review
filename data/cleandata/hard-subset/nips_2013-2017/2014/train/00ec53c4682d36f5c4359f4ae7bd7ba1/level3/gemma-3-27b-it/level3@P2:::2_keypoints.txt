- Proposes a two-stream convolutional network that separately models spatial appearance and motion (dense optical flow) and fuses predictions for video action recognition.  
- Achieves significant improvements over prior deep-learning methods and is competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted feature approaches on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Leverages recent CNN advances and uses dense optical flow and multi-task learning to strengthen temporal modeling.  
- Motivated by biological two-pathway vision analogy (ventral/dorsal), though the paper does not deeply explore this connection.  
- Decoupling spatial and temporal streams allows effective use of ImageNet pretraining for the spatial network.  
- Presents strong empirical results that substantially outperform prior ConvNet-based video methods.  
- Conducts thorough evaluations of design choices, including optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking, and mean flow subtraction.  
- Novel combination of established techniques (ConvNets, dense flow, multi-task learning) applied in a principled architecture.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides detailed experimental and implementation descriptions.  
- Addresses an important and challenging problem in video action recognition.  
- Weakness: The superior performance of the spatial network over previous models is noted but not adequately explained.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of alternative frame sampling strategies (e.g., adaptive sampling based on motion).  
- Weakness: Computational cost of dense optical flow computation and training the two-stream model may hinder practical deployment; optimizations are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical validation, competitive state-of-the-art results, clear presentation, and a valuable novel combination of techniques.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear explanation for spatial network gains, limited sampling exploration, and potentially prohibitive computational cost.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions requesting more analysis of the spatial network’s performance and discussion of computational optimizations.
