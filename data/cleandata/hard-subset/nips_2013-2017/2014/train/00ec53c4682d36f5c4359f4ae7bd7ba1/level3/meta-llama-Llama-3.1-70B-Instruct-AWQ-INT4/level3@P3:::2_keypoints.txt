- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial network on single frames + temporal network on dense optical flow) for video action recognition.  
- Temporal network trained on short dense optical-flow sequences and fused with the spatial single-frame ConvNet.  
- Significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Architecture is motivated by the two-streams hypothesis in human vision, though the neurobiological link is not further investigated.  
- Novel contribution: applying standard image ConvNet pipelines to dense optical flow inputs.  
- Experiments are thorough and demonstrate clear benefits of optical-flow input for ConvNet training.  
- Shows multi-task learning increases effective training data and improves performance on both datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and organized for both experts and non-experts.  
- Technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results; reproducible details provided.  
- Originality: a novel combination of familiar techniques that generalizes hand-crafted features within a data-driven framework.  
- Significance: important results likely to be adopted or built upon by other researchers; advances the state of the art.  
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network outperforms models from [13] despite similar architectures—needs more discussion.  
- Suggested future work: evaluate different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream rather than fixed equal spacing.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS based on solid methodology, thorough evaluation, and impressive empirical results.
