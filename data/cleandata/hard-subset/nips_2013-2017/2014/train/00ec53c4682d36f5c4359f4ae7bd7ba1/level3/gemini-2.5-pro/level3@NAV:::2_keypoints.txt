- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture for video action recognition with separate spatial (single-frame) and temporal (dense optical flow) streams.  
- Temporal stream processes short sequences of dense optical flow to explicitly model motion rather than learning it from raw pixels.  
- Final prediction obtained by late fusion of the spatial and temporal stream outputs.  
- Builds on image ConvNet success while diverging from prior video work (e.g., Karpathy et al.) by explicitly encoding motion.  
- Strong empirical performance, significantly outperforming other ConvNet-based methods and matching state-of-the-art on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Clear, impactful contribution: applying standard image ConvNets to optical flow plus fusion yields impressive results.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show the benefits of optical-flow input and different training choices.  
- Minor weakness: unclear why the spatial network alone outperforms similar architectures in [13]; data augmentation claim needs more direct analysis.  
- Minor weakness: fixed temporal spacing for frames—evaluation could be improved by testing alternative frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-executed, influential contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance.
