- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet that separately processes spatial frames and dense optical flow for temporal information, then fuses outputs.  
- Achieves results competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted feature methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Novel application of ConvNets to optical flow, showing motion can be learned without complex hand-crafted features.  
- Architecture is motivated by the neuroscience two-stream hypothesis and complements prior shallow methods.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over previous deep-learning attempts that stack raw frames.  
- Uses multi-task learning to combine datasets, improving performance especially on HMDB-51 with limited data.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized with sufficient implementation and training details for reproducibility.  
- Experiments are well-designed with thorough comparisons to prior work.  
- Limited temporal modeling due to processing only short fixed-length windows (L=10).  
- Lacks detailed error analysis (per-class failures and problematic video types).  
- Does not address handling of variable-length videos or practical deployment concerns.  
- Overall technically sound and significant contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses and recommendation is acceptance.
