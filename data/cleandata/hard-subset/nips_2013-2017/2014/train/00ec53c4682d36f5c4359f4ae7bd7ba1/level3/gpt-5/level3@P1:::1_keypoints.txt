- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition: spatial net on RGB and temporal net on stacked dense optical flow, fused at score level.
- Temporal ConvNet explores optical-flow stacking variants (L-frame stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, mean-flow subtraction).
- Multi-task learning jointly trains on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited video data without manual class alignment.
- Temporal net on optical flow substantially outperforms RGB-only models; two-stream fusion is competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted pipelines.
- Architecture is clear, simple, and yields strong performance despite limited training data.
- Key empirical finding: temporally stacked optical flow is a more effective ConvNet input for motion than raw RGB frame stacks.
- Thorough ablation studies on temporal input design and practical training details (multi-GPU, data augmentation).
- Multi-task learning effectively leverages multiple datasets and improves performance on both benchmarks.
- Results convincingly improve over prior deep-learning baselines and rival hand-crafted methods like improved dense trajectories and MBH.
- Limited temporal modeling: short fixed windows (L=10) fail to capture long-range structure or full action phases.
- Heavy dependence on precomputed optical flow (Brox) creates a computational bottleneck and untested sensitivity to flow quality.
- Fusion is limited to late score-level combination; joint feature fusion attempts overfit and lack exploration of regularization or alternative architectures.
- Missing error-mode analyses: no per-class breakdowns, failure cases, or sensitivity to camera motion and background clutter.
- Generalization and scalability to larger/noisier datasets (e.g., Sports-1M) and the storage cost of precomputing flow are not demonstrated.
- Technical execution is sound with appropriate baselines, protocols, and justified multi-task formulation.
- Claims are well supported by experiments on standard splits; spatial and temporal complementarity is convincing.
- Clarity is high: writing, temporal input definitions, and implementation details enable reproducibility.
- Originality in applying ConvNets to dense optical-flow inputs and combining with two-stream decomposition; novel compared to prior shallow two-path work.
- Likely significant impact by establishing optical-flow ConvNets and two-stream fusion as strong baselines for video recognition.
- Suggested improvements: incorporate longer-range temporal modeling (pyramids, dilated convs, sequence models).
- Suggested improvements: explore stronger fusion strategies (shared layers, lateral connections, feature-level fusion with regularization).
- Suggested improvements: provide detailed error analysis (per-class confusion, categories helped by motion vs. appearance, failure cases).
- Suggested improvements: evaluate robustness to different optical flow methods and report computational/storage costs for scalability.
- Suggested improvements: consider camera motion compensation and trajectory-aligned pooling to better exploit motion boundaries.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, simple interpretable architecture, bridges hand-crafted features and learned models, reproducible and relevant.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited temporal scope, reliance on precomputed optical flow without robustness/efficiency analysis, lack of detailed error insights.
- Recommendation: Accept — two-stream ConvNets with stacked optical flow make a significant, practical contribution to action recognition.
