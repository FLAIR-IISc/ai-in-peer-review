- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet that separately models appearance (frames) and motion (optical flow) for video action recognition.  
- Demonstrates optical flow as effective input for temporal ConvNets, achieving competitive performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to leverage additional datasets and reduce overfitting on small video datasets.  
- Decoupling spatial and temporal streams allows specialization and complementary performance gains.  
- Experiments are extensive and well-designed on two standard benchmarks.  
- Multi-task learning is an innovative component that improves generalization.  
- Results are competitive with state-of-the-art and present a viable alternative to hand-crafted representations.  
- Limitation: relies on fixed-length adjacent frames, hindering modeling of long-term temporal dependencies.  
- Limitation: lacks error-mode analysis and insights into misclassified videos, reducing interpretability.  
- Limitation: high computational cost from pre-computing optical flow and dependence on pre-trained spatial ConvNets, impacting scalability and real-time use.  
- Paper is technically sound and reproducible, but misses discussion of failure cases.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, adding failure analysis and addressing long-term dependency and computational concerns.
