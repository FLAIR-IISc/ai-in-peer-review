- Paper proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial RGB stream + temporal optical-flow stream) with late fusion for video action recognition.
- Temporal stream uses stacked dense optical flow to learn motion-sensitive features, motivated by ventral/dorsal pathways.
- Temporal stream generalizes hand-crafted motion features and outperforms prior deep approaches that use raw frame stacks.
- Multi-task learning across datasets is introduced to mitigate overfitting in the temporal stream.
- Strength: clear architectural insight—separating spatial and temporal streams addresses prior ConvNet failures on motion.
- Strength: thorough empirical ablations (flow stacking length, trajectory vs location stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction, fusion).
- Strength: strong quantitative results, improving over deep baselines and competitive with hand-crafted pipelines.
- Strength: practical, reproducible training recipe (ImageNet pretraining for spatial stream; multi-task learning for temporal stream).
- Strength: solid engineering (multi-GPU training, data augmentation, sensible test-time protocol).
- Weakness: reliance on precomputed optical flow creates computational/storage bottlenecks and prevents end-to-end learning.
- Weakness: limited temporal modeling—short temporal receptive field (L ≤ 10) and simple score averaging for long-range aggregation.
- Weakness: minimal camera motion handling (only mean displacement subtraction) versus explicit compensation in trajectory methods.
- Weakness: spatial stream’s superior ImageNet pretraining results are not rigorously controlled/explained.
- Weakness: fusion is shallow (score-level) and deeper joint fusion attempts overfit without further regularization exploration.
- Quality: methodology and ablations are sound and evaluations are consistent on standard splits/metrics.
- Quality: stronger experiments needed on sensitivity to flow algorithms, multi-task loss weighting, and controlled spatial-stream comparisons.
- Clarity: paper is clearly written, well organized, and provides sufficient implementation/training details for reproduction.
- Originality: two-stream ConvNet with optical-flow input is a novel, clean synthesis bridging deep learning and trajectory-based insights.
- Significance: likely influential baseline demonstrating explicit motion input is critical and approaching hand-crafted state of the art.
- Question: how sensitive are results to choice and parameters of optical flow algorithms (e.g., Brox vs TV-L1) and is mean subtraction always beneficial?
- Question: what task-loss weighting was used in multi-task learning and was there negative transfer for overlapping classes?
- Question: were longer flow stacks (L > 10) or denser test-time temporal sampling tried, and where do diminishing returns occur?
- Suggestion: evaluate alternative spatial-frame sampling schemes (denser or motion-adaptive) and report their impact.
- Suggestion: provide controlled experiment isolating spatial stream augmentation vs architecture to explain ImageNet gap.
- Suggestion: explore stronger regularization (dropout, weight decay, batch norm, bottlenecks) to enable learned joint fusion.
- Suggestion: analyze long-range temporal aggregation alternatives (temporal convolutions or recurrent layers vs average pooling).
- Suggestion: report compute/storage costs for flow precomputation and runtime throughput; discuss trade-offs vs raw-frame models.
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical gains, clear insight, simple general architecture, thorough ablations, and reproducible details.
- Cons for acceptance: not end-to-end for motion, limited long-range temporal and camera-motion exploration, unresolved spatial-stream questions.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
