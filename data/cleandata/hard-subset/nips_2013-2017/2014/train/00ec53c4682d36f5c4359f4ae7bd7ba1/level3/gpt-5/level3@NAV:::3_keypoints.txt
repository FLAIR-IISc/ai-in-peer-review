- Proposes a two-stream convolutional architecture separating appearance (RGB spatial stream) and motion (stacked dense optical-flow temporal stream) with late score-level fusion.  
- Key novelty: explicitly presents multi-frame optical-flow volumes to the network rather than relying on multi-frame RGB or 3D convs.  
- Positioned against classical trajectory/HOF/MBH/Fisher pipelines and earlier shallow two-stream ideas.  
- Empirical results show large gains over prior deep baselines and competitive performance with hand-crafted methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Methodology is technically sound, carefully executed, and reproducible with clear implementation and training details.  
- Informative ablation studies on flow stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction, and fusion.  
- Limitation: relies on precomputed optical flow (not end-to-end), raising storage, latency, and scalability concerns that need more discussion.  
- Paper is very clear and well written but feels split across multiple contributions, diluting a single through-line.  
- Recommendation to refocus abstract/introduction to emphasize the two-stream/optical-flow core, framing multi-task learning as an enhancer.  
- Originality: two-stream deep formulation and stacked optical-flow inputs are novel and complementary to existing frameworks.  
- Reviewer requests adding missing citations (CVPR 2011 ISA work and BMVC 2010 evaluation of spatio-temporal features).  
- Significance: strong results suggest high impact and likely adoption.  
- Pros: well-motivated two-stream design with large optical-flow gains; thorough, reproducible experiments; multi-task learning is practical and effective.  
- Cons: narrative spread across multiple contributions; dependence on precomputed flow with limited runtime discussion; related work coverage needs broadening.  
- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2014) conditional on tightening the narrative around the two-stream/optical-flow contribution and adding the suggested citations.
