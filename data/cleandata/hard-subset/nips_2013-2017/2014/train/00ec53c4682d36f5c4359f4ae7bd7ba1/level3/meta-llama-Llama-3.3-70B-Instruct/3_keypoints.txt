- Proposes a novel framework for learning distributed attribute representations jointly with word embeddings.
- Introduces a third-order multiplicative model where word context and attribute vectors interact to predict the next word, enabling conditional word similarity.
- Paper is well-written with clearly described methodology and implementations.
- Experimental results show effectiveness across tasks: sentiment classification, cross-lingual document classification, and blog authorship attribution.
- Demonstrates significant improvements over baseline approaches.
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, thorough experimental evaluation, and practical applicability to many NLP tasks.
- Weakness: paper lacks a single coherent theme and appears split across multiple contributions.
- Weakness: abstract and introduction need to be more focused to clarify main contributions.
- Weakness: missing citations from deep learning and computer vision literature (e.g., independent subspace analysis, local spatio-temporal features).
- Overall recommendation: accept to NIPS 2014 with suggested improvements.
