- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet combining a spatial network on frames and a temporal network on stacked optical flow, achieving strong results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Thorough evaluation of several ways to train ConvNets on optical flow, showing clear benefits of flow input.  
- Investigates different input configurations (stacking flows, trajectory stacking, mean displacement subtraction) and reports their effects.  
- Demonstrates that spatial and temporal streams capture complementary information, improving action recognition performance.  
- Empirical results significantly outperform other ConvNet-based approaches and match state-of-the-art on standard datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of architecture and experiments.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review that situates the contribution within existing research.  
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network outperforms models from [13]; lacks detailed comparative analysis.  
- Evaluation limitation: frame-sampling for the spatial stream uses a single fixed scheme (25 equally spaced frames); other schemes are not explored.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper of interest to the video-classification community with a significant architectural contribution.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear presentation of methods and results, but could use deeper analysis of spatial-network differences.  
- Clarity: high — explanations and experimental setup are well-presented.  
- Originality: novel two-stream architecture and use of optical flow for the temporal network represent a meaningful departure from prior work.  
- Significance: important contribution because it effectively fuses spatial and temporal cues and achieves competitive dataset performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, strong empirical gains, clear writing and setup.  
- Arguments for rejection: lacks detailed analysis comparing the spatial network to similar prior models; limited exploration of frame-sampling strategies.
