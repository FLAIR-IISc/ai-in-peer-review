- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet combining a spatial stream (single frames) and a temporal stream (dense optical flow) to capture appearance and motion.  
- Argues optical flow input better captures motion than stacking consecutive frames.  
- Relates to prior work on shallow spatio-temporal encodings and deep methods, highlighting limitations of existing deep-frame-stacking approaches.  
- Provides clear architectural description and fusion strategies for combining the two streams.  
- Delivers a thorough experimental evaluation across architectures, input configurations, and training methods.  
- Evaluates on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 and achieves state-of-the-art or matching top performance.  
- Demonstrates clear benefits of using optical-flow inputs and explores training methods for flow-based ConvNets.  
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network outperforms similar models from [13].  
- Minor concern: limited evaluation of different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Paper is technically sound with well-supported claims and careful empirical validation.  
- Writing and presentation are clear, with sufficient details for reproduction by experts.  
- Originality is high due to the novel spatial–temporal fusion and comprehensive input/training analysis.  
- Significance is high given strong results and potential impact on video analysis systems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, comprehensive experiments, SOTA results, important community contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor issues regarding comparison with [13] and limited frame-sampling analysis.
