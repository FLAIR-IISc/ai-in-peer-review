- Proposes a Two-Stream ConvNet: separate spatial (RGB frames) and temporal (stacked dense optical flow) networks processed in parallel.  
- Temporal stream uses dense optical flow as explicit motion input to avoid learning motion from raw pixels.  
- Spatial and temporal outputs are combined via late fusion of softmax scores (averaging or SVM).  
- Outperforms prior ConvNet-based methods (Karpathy et al. 2014) and is competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted feature systems on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Replaces hand-crafted trajectory/MBH features with a learnable deep representation informed by optical flow.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation, including ablations on temporal inputs, flow configurations, frame counts, bi-directional flow, and training schemes.  
- Shows that fine-tuning from ImageNet and multi-task learning improve performance; evaluation is extensive and rigorous.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and connects neatly to prior deep and shallow video-recognition work.  
- Strength: intuitive and effective decoupling of appearance and motion learning.  
- Strength: significant empirical advance that helped establish ConvNets for video understanding.  
- Weakness: simple late fusion limits learning of joint spatio-temporal features; intermediate fusion strategies not explored.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why the spatial net outperforms similar ImageNet models (attributed to data augmentation without detailed evidence).  
- Weakness: uniform sampling of 25 frames may miss brief/critical action moments; alternative sampling schemes not evaluated.  
- Overall: technically sound, original in applying ConvNets to optical flow, highly significant, and strongly recommended for acceptance.
