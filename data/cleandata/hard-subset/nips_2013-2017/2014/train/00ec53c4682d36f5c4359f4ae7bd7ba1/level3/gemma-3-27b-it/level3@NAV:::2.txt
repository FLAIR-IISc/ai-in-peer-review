## Review of "Two-Stream Convolutional Networks for Action Recognition in Videos"

This paper presents a two-stream convolutional network architecture for action recognition, comprising a spatial stream operating on single frames and a temporal stream processing dense optical flow. The core idea of decoupling spatial and temporal information within separate convolutional networks, and then fusing their outputs, is well-motivated and builds upon the established “two-stream hypothesis” from visual neuroscience. The approach leverages the power of deep learning while explicitly addressing the need to model motion, a critical component for video understanding. The work is situated within the context of recent advances in both image and video classification, clearly outlining limitations of prior attempts to directly apply ConvNets to stacked video frames.

The strength of this paper lies in its strong empirical validation. The authors demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing ConvNet-based approaches on both UCF-101 and HMDB-51 datasets, achieving results competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted features.  The thorough experimentation, including ablation studies on optical flow configurations and a detailed investigation of multi-task learning, convincingly supports their claims.  The exploration of different training strategies for the temporal stream, revealing the benefits of optical flow as input, is particularly valuable. The use of pre-training on ImageNet further enhances the performance, showcasing effective transfer learning.

However, a minor concern remains regarding the superior performance of the spatial network compared to models reported in [13] despite similar architectures. While not a critical flaw, a more in-depth discussion of this observation would strengthen the paper. Additionally, while the authors mention the potential benefits of different frame-sampling schemes, evaluating these empirically within the spatial stream could offer further insights and improvements. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** Significantly outperforms existing deep learning approaches and matches state-of-the-art.
*   **Clear and Logical Approach:** Effectively decouples spatial and temporal information, leading to improved performance.
*   **Thorough Experimentation:** Comprehensive ablation studies and analysis of various configurations.
*   **Well-Written and Contextualized:** Clearly situates the work within the broader field of action recognition.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Unexplained superior performance of the spatial network compared to prior work ([13]).
*   Lack of evaluation of different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream. 

**Overall:** This is a strong empirical paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of video classification. The proposed two-stream architecture is well-motivated, effectively implemented, and thoroughly evaluated. It is of high interest to the video-classification community and I recommend acceptance.
