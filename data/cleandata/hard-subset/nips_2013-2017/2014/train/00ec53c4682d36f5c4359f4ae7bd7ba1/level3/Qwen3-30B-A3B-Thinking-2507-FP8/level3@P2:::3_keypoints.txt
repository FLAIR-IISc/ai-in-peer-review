- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture separating spatial (RGB frames) and temporal (optical flow) processing for video action recognition.  
- Main contributions: two-stream architecture, strong ConvNet performance on optical flow despite limited data, and a multi-task learning scheme to combine datasets.  
- Demonstrates strong results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, with the temporal (optical flow) stream substantially outperforming prior deep-learning methods.  
- Temporal and spatial streams provide complementary information, and fusion improves overall performance.  
- Multi-task learning effectively mitigates limited video training data by combining datasets.  
- Methodology is well-described and reproducible (optical flow computation, network architecture, training details).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including ablation studies on optical flow configurations, fusion methods, and multi-task setups.  
- Writing, organization, and figures are clear and accessible to readers familiar with deep learning for vision.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension applying deep ConvNets to both streams and introducing an optical flow–based temporal ConvNet.  
- Would benefit from stronger contextualization with prior optical-flow-based action recognition work and adding missing citations (e.g., CVPR 2011, BMVC 2010).  
- Presentation feels somewhat fragmented across multiple contributions; abstract/introduction should focus more tightly on the two-stream framework and optical flow significance.  
- Needs clearer differentiation from prior approaches that used stacked RGB frames and related methods.  
- Overall significance is high; paper advances video recognition and is recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions to focus narrative and add missing related-work citations.
