This paper proposes a two-stream convolutional architecture for video action recognition that explicitly decouples appearance (spatial stream on RGB frames) from motion (temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow), and further augments training via multi-task learning across datasets. Relative to prior deep video work (e.g., 3D ConvNets and multi-frame RGB stacks), the key novelty is to present motion explicitly to the network through multi-frame optical-flow volumes and to fuse the two streams by late score-level fusion. The approach is positioned against classical shallow pipelines based on trajectories, HOF/MBH, and Fisher vectors, and against earlier two-stream ideas realized with shallow HMAX. Empirically, the method shows large gains over previous deep baselines and is competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.

Quality: The methodology is technically sound and carefully executed. Architectural choices, training protocols, data augmentation, and implementation details (multi-GPU training, optical-flow preprocessing) are described clearly enough to reproduce. Ablations on flow stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction, and fusion are informative. A limitation is reliance on precomputed optical flow (non end-to-end), with storage/latency implications that merit more discussion.

Clarity: The paper is very clear and well written. However, it feels split across multiple contributions (two-stream design, optical-flow input variants, and multi-task learning), diluting a single through-line. I recommend refocusing the abstract and introduction to highlight the central significance of the two-stream formulation and the role of optical flow, with multi-task learning framed as a practical enhancer.

Originality: The two-stream deep formulation and the specific design of stacked optical-flow inputs are novel and complementary to existing deep frameworks. Please add missing citations to: CVPR 2011 “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis” and BMVC 2010 “Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition,” to better situate the motion-feature lineage.

Significance: Results are strong—substantial improvements over prior deep baselines and competitive with the state of the art—suggesting high impact and likely adoption.

Pros:
- Strong, well-motivated two-stream formulation; optical flow yields large gains.
- Thorough, reproducible experiments; competitive results on standard benchmarks.
- Multi-task learning is practical and effective.

Cons:
- Narrative spread across multiple contributions.
- Dependence on precomputed flow; limited discussion of runtime and scalability.
- Related work can be broadened as noted.

Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2014), conditional on tightening the narrative around the two-stream/optical-flow contribution and adding the suggested citations.
