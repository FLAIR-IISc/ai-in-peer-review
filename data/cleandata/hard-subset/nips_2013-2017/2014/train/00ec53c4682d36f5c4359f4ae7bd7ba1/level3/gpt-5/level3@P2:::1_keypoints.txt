- Paper proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition: a spatial ConvNet on RGB frames and a temporal ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow, fused at test time.  
- Introduces multi-task training across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited data.

- Key idea: feeding explicit optical flow to a temporal ConvNet bridges the gap between deep RGB nets and hand-crafted trajectory-based pipelines.  
- Explores motion input design choices: flow stacking vs. trajectory stacking, forward vs. bi-directional flow, and mean-flow subtraction.

- Strength: Clear architectural decomposition that enables effective ImageNet pretraining for appearance.  
- Strength: Temporal ConvNet on stacked flow outperforms RGB-only baselines and combined streams are competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.  
- Strength: Insightful ablation studies on motion input parameters providing practical guidance.  
- Strength: Practical, effective multi-task training across datasets.  
- Strength: Good engineering and reproducibility details (training schedules, multi-GPU, Caffe).

- Weakness: Temporal modeling limited to short fixed windows (L=10) with no long-range sequence modeling.  
- Weakness: Limited error analysis — lacks per-class breakdowns and discussion of failure modes like camera motion or small/fast actions.  
- Weakness: Reliance on precomputed optical flow creates a computational bottleneck and breaks end-to-end training; impact of different flow algorithms not studied.  
- Weakness: Fusion is shallow (score-level); feature-level joint fusion not explored due to overfitting concerns.  
- Weakness: More detailed head-to-head comparisons (including computational cost) to trajectory Fisher-vector systems would strengthen claims.

- Quality: Technically sound with thorough ablations and standard, well-justified training methodology; evaluation follows standard protocols and supports claims.  
- Clarity: Clearly written and organized with useful figures and sufficient implementation details for reproduction; minor suggestions for added diagnostics.  
- Originality: Novelty in combining ConvNets with explicit stacked optical flow as temporal stream and multi-task training, representing a meaningful advance over prior deep raw-RGB approaches.  
- Significance: Addresses a central challenge in video understanding and provides a viable deep alternative to hand-crafted pipelines with practical guidance likely to influence future work.

- Suggestion: Provide per-class accuracies and confusion matrices to reveal where motion vs. appearance helps or fails.  
- Suggestion: Analyze longer window lengths L or temporal pooling to approximate long-range reasoning.  
- Suggestion: Study different optical flow methods/parameters and discuss end-to-end alternatives (learning flow-like features).  
- Suggestion: Explore modest feature-level fusion or shared layers across streams to reduce overfitting.  
- Suggestion: Analyze robustness to camera motion and compare mean subtraction to explicit global motion compensation.  

- For acceptance: Well-motivated architecture with strong empirical results, careful ablations, practical training strategy, and reproducible details.  
- For acceptance: Demonstrates that optical-flow-as-input is key to strong deep performance on action recognition.

- Against acceptance: Limited long-range temporal modeling and potential performance plateau without sequence-level mechanisms.  
- Against acceptance: Dependence on precomputed optical flow (compute overhead, not end-to-end) with limited analysis of this dependency.  
- Against acceptance: Insufficient error-mode analysis and lack of qualitative diagnostics.  
- Against acceptance: Fusion remains at score level; feature-level integration not demonstrated.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed paper that meaningfully shows two-stream ConvNets with explicit optical flow substantially close the gap between deep and hand-crafted methods; recommend acceptance despite noted limitations.
