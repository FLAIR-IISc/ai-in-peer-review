- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that combines spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) information for video action recognition.
- Addresses the challenge of capturing complementary static and motion cues, improving over prior deep-video methods.
- Implements the two-stream idea with discriminatively trained deep ConvNets rather than hand-crafted features.
- Spatial stream uses an ImageNet-pretrained image-classification ConvNet on individual frames.
- Temporal stream processes dense optical flow and outperforms training on stacked RGB frames.
- Demonstrates that stacking multiple optical flow frames (L=10) and mean subtraction yields the most discriminative motion features.
- Evaluates multiple temporal input configs (optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction).
- Late fusion of spatial and temporal streams achieves state-of-the-art results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 improves performance on the smaller HMDB-51 dataset.
- Experiments are thorough with clear implementation details and appropriate baselines.
- Paper is generally well-written and figures effectively illustrate inputs and architecture.
- Needs clarification on why the spatial stream (ImageNet pretrained) outperforms the single-frame ConvNet in prior work—architecture, training, or dataset reasons.
- Weaknesses: limited ablation on the multi-task component, insufficient analysis linking learned filters to trajectory-based features, and incomplete assessment of computational costs.
- Overall original, significant, and well-executed work that advances deep learning for video recognition; recommendation to accept.
