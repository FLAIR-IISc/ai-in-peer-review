- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture: a spatial stream on RGB frames for appearance and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow for motion.
- Shows ImageNet pre-training of the spatial stream is highly effective for video action recognition.
- Demonstrates stacked optical flow input for the temporal stream greatly outperforms training on raw stacked frames.
- Combines stream outputs via late fusion (averaging or SVM) to improve final predictions.
- Introduces a multi-task learning framework to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, improving regularization and performance.
- Provides extensive experiments on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.
- Technically sound with thorough ablation studies (number of flow frames, mean subtraction, stacking strategies) and clear implementation/training details for reproducibility.
- Well-written and clearly motivated, with helpful diagrams and logically organized experiments.
- Contribution is original: first successful deep two-stream implementation separating appearance and motion using ConvNets on optical flow.
- Highly significant: closes the gap between deep learning and hand-crafted feature methods and influenced subsequent work in video understanding.
- Argument for acceptance: novel effective architecture, state-of-the-art results, strong empirical validation, and high potential impact.
- Argument against acceptance: narrative feels slightly unfocused by giving equal weight to multi-task learning and the two-stream idea.
- Suggestion: tighten the narrative to foreground the two-stream architecture and present multi-task learning as a supporting technique.
- Suggestion: expand related-work comparison to more directly contrast learned temporal features with prior unsupervised or hand-crafted features.
