- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture: spatial stream on frames and temporal stream on multi-frame dense optical flow.  
- Demonstrates strong, competitive performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 datasets.  
- Builds on prior image-classification networks, addressing previous methods’ inability to capture motion.  
- Core contribution: simple, effective ConvNet trained on multi-frame optical flow for video action recognition.  
- Uses multi-task learning to combine training data from different datasets.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and compares to several baselines and state-of-the-art methods.  
- Architecture is conceptually clear, modular, and easy to understand.  
- Separation of spatial and temporal streams provides a clean design.  
- Shows optical flow as effective ConvNet input, enabling data-driven motion cue learning.  
- Experimental results substantially improve over previous deep-learning video methods.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and accompanied by clear, informative figures.  
- Temporal modeling is limited to short fixed-length windows (L=10), preventing long-range dependency capture.  
- Lacks comprehensive error-mode analysis to identify which actions fail and why.  
- Fails to discuss computational complexity, especially the cost of optical flow computation.  
- Technical soundness is high and claims are supported by experiments; ablation studies are useful.  
- Originality: novel application of ConvNets to optical flow; two-stream idea adapted effectively to deep learning.  
- Significance: results are important and likely to influence future video action recognition research.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggestion to address temporal limitations, error analysis, and computational cost in future work.
