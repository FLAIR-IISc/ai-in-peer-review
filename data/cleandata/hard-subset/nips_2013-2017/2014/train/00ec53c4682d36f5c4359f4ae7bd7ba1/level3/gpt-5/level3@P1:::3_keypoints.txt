- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet separating appearance (RGB spatial stream) and motion (stacked dense optical flow temporal stream) for video action recognition.
- Explores flow input variants (flow vs. trajectory stacking, forward vs. bidirectional, mean subtraction) and finds stacking multiple flow fields with mean subtraction especially effective.
- Fuses spatial and temporal streams by late fusion (averaging or SVM on softmax scores).
- Introduces simple multi-task learning with dataset-specific softmax heads to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited labels.
- Provides extensive ablations and practical implementation details (data augmentation, training schedules, multi-GPU, flow precomputation/compression).
- Temporal stream alone outperforms prior deep models trained on raw frames; fused model is competitive with state-of-the-art trajectory-based shallow pipelines.
- Positions the work between hand-crafted pipelines (IDT/FV) and prior deep video models, explicitly injecting motion via optical flow.
- Argues and shows that explicit motion representation better captures temporal cues on small-to-medium datasets than implicit motion learning.
- Strength: clear conceptual contribution decoupling appearance and motion with effective late fusion.
- Strength: strong empirical evidence with careful ablations across many design choices.
- Strength: practical training strategy (ImageNet pretraining, multi-task learning) and reproducible details.
- Weakness: fragmented narrative—paper reads like multiple contributions rather than a single focused story.
- Weakness: reliance on precomputed optical flow is not end-to-end and is a computational/storage bottleneck.
- Weakness: limited handling of camera motion; mean subtraction is simplistic compared to explicit camera compensation.
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases and invariances (viewpoint, background bias, motion magnitude).
- Related-work scope needs tightening and explicit positioning to ISA (Le et al. 2011), Wang et al. BMVC 2010, 3D CNNs, and Taylor et al. 2010.
- Quality: technically sound with appropriate protocols and diagnostic experiments, but lacks large-scale evaluation (e.g., Sports-1M).
- Clarity: generally clear and reproducible, but the manuscript should be tightened to emphasize the core contribution.
- Originality: novel architectural decomposition and explicit optical-flow input for ConvNets; multi-task and fusion are known but effectively integrated.
- Significance: high—substantially improves deep baselines and offers a simple, general recipe likely to be influential.
- Pros for acceptance: strong performance, thorough ablations, practical impact, and reproducibility.
- Cons for acceptance: dependence on precomputed flow, fragmented narrative, limited large-scale validation and camera-motion handling.
- Actionable suggestions: refocus abstract/introduction on the two-stream optical-flow insight; add Le et al. 2011 and Wang et al. 2010 citations; report runtime/storage costs for flow; provide qualitative failure/class-wise analyses; discuss end-to-end/learned-flow directions.
- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2014), conditional on addressing presentation and related-work clarifications.
