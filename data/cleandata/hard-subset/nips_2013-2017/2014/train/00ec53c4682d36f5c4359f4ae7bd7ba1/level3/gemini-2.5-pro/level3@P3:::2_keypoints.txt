- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: a spatial stream on single frames and a temporal stream on stacked optical flow, with outputs fused for final prediction.  
- Uses dense optical flow as temporal input to explicitly provide motion information rather than learning spatio-temporal features from raw frames.  
- Spatial stream is a standard image ConvNet that can be pre-trained on ImageNet, improving appearance learning.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior ConvNet approaches and rivaling hand-crafted feature methods.  
- Novel contribution: combining image ConvNets with optical-flow-based ConvNets into a trainable, complementary two-stream architecture.  
- Systematically evaluates optical flow input configurations (stack length, trajectory stacking, mean subtraction) to justify design choices.  
- Ablation studies show benefits of ImageNet pretraining, temporal input design, stream complementarity, and multi-task learning to reduce overfitting.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and the architecture is simple and easy to understand.  
- Unclear why spatial stream outperforms similar prior work—needs deeper ablation to confirm the impact of the proposed data augmentation.  
- Spatial stream uses uniform sampling of 25 frames; alternative, content-aware sampling strategies are not explored.  
- Practical limitation: heavy computational and storage cost from precomputing and compressing dense optical flow.  
- Overall: a strong, influential empirical contribution with minor issues; recommended for acceptance.
