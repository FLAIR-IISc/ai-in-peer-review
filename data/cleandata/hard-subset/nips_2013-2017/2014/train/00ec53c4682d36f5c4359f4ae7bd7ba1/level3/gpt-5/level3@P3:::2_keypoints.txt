- Summary: Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition with a spatial RGB stream and a temporal optical-flow stream, fused at score level.  
- Relation to prior work: Explicitly feeds optical flow to learn motion, outperforming RGB-stack ConvNets and matching shallow trajectory-based methods.  
- Temporal-stream design: Explores various optical-flow inputs (stacking, trajectory-stacking, bi-directional, mean-flow subtraction) and benefits from longer motion contexts.  
- Spatial-stream design: Uses ImageNet pretraining and shows strong performance, but the source of its advantage over similar baselines is underexplained.  
- Fusion: Simple late fusion (averaging or linear SVM) effectively combines streams, demonstrating complementarity.  
- Multi-task learning: Joint training on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 improves temporal-stream generalization.  
- Implementation: Careful, reproducible details provided (training schedules, augmentation, pretraining, multi-GPU, flow precomputation).  
- Empirical results: Substantially better than previous ConvNet methods and competitive with state of the art on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Technical soundness: High; architecture and training are standard but well adapted and experiments are extensive.  
- Ablations: Strong ablation studies on temporal input configurations and comparisons to RGB-frame stacks support core claims.  
- Dataset limitations: Acknowledges small video datasets and lack of trajectory-aware pooling.  
- Weakness — spatial-net gap: Lacks controlled ablations to explain why the spatial net outperforms closely related image ConvNet baselines.  
- Weakness — flow dependence: Relies on a specific optical-flow algorithm (Brox et al.) without analyzing sensitivity to flow choice or failure modes.  
- Weakness — camera motion: Camera motion handling is limited to mean-flow subtraction; stronger compensation is not evaluated.  
- Weakness — fusion design space: Only late-score fusion is explored; intermediate/parameter-sharing fusion variants and lightweight learned calibrators are not tried.  
- Weakness — frame sampling: Spatial stream uses fixed uniform sampling at test time; alternative sampling schemes are not evaluated.  
- Clarity: Very clearly written and well-organized with sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Originality: Novel instantiation of the two-stream idea with optical-flow ConvNets; contribution is mainly in integration and strong empirical validation.  
- Significance: High — advances deep video recognition and provides a simple, scalable baseline likely to influence follow-up work.  
- Suggested improvements: Ablate spatial-net training choices, test different frame-sampling schemes, analyze flow algorithm sensitivity, explore lightweight fusion methods, and evaluate explicit camera-motion compensation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong empirical gains, thorough experiments, and a practical, influential contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited component novelty and some missing analyses (spatial-net rationale, flow sensitivity, camera motion).  
- Overall recommendation: Strong empirical paper with clear impact; recommend acceptance despite modest novelty and a few missing analyses.
