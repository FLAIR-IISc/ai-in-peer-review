## Review of “Two-Stream Convolutional Networks for Action Recognition in Videos”

This paper presents a two-stream convolutional network architecture for action recognition, comprising a spatial stream operating on single frames and a temporal stream processing dense optical flow. The core idea of decoupling spatial and temporal information and leveraging the strengths of ConvNets for both is a logical and well-executed approach. The work builds upon the recent resurgence of deep learning in computer vision, specifically extending successful image classification architectures to the video domain, addressing the limitations of previous attempts like [14] which struggled to capture motion effectively. 

The authors demonstrate significant performance gains over other ConvNet-based methods and achieve competitive results on the UCF-101 and HMDB-51 datasets. The thorough experimentation, including ablation studies on different optical flow input configurations and the exploration of multi-task learning, strengthens the validity of their findings. The clear benefits shown by using optical flow as input to a ConvNet are particularly noteworthy, effectively generalizing previous hand-crafted feature approaches within a learned framework. 

A minor point of concern is the authors’ observation that their spatial network outperforms models from [13] despite similar architectures. A deeper investigation into the specific differences in training procedures, data augmentation, or network initialization could provide valuable insight. Additionally, evaluating different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream, as suggested, could further optimize performance.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The proposed architecture achieves state-of-the-art or near state-of-the-art performance on standard benchmarks.
*   **Clear and Logical Approach:** Decoupling spatial and temporal streams is a well-motivated and effective strategy.
*   **Thorough Experimentation:** Extensive ablation studies validate the design choices and demonstrate the benefits of different components.
*   **Well-Written and Organized:** The paper is easy to follow and clearly presents the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Unexplained Performance Discrepancy:** The superior performance of the spatial network compared to [13] warrants further investigation.
*   **Limited Exploration of Spatial Stream:** The spatial stream could benefit from exploring different frame-sampling strategies.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant advancement in video action recognition.
*   The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   The work is well-written, clearly organized, and thoroughly evaluated.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The minor concern regarding the spatial network’s performance requires further clarification.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong empirical paper that makes a valuable contribution to the video classification community. The proposed two-stream ConvNet architecture is a promising approach, and the thorough experimentation provides strong evidence of its effectiveness. I recommend acceptance.
