This paper proposes a novel two-stream convolutional network architecture for action recognition in videos. The main idea is to capture both spatial and temporal information in videos by using two separate streams: one for appearance (spatial stream) and one for motion (temporal stream). The authors demonstrate that their architecture significantly outperforms previous deep learning approaches and is competitive with state-of-the-art shallow representations.

The paper is well-written and presents a clear case for the proposed methods. The authors provide a thorough description of their methodology, implementation, and procedures, making it easy to follow and understand their approach. The results are impressive, showing significant improvements over previous deep learning methods and competitive performance with state-of-the-art shallow representations.

One of the strengths of the paper is the novelty of the proposed two-stream framework. The incorporation of optical flow into the temporal stream yields significant improvements in performance, demonstrating the importance of motion information for action recognition. The authors also propose a multi-task learning framework, which allows for the combination of multiple datasets and improves the performance of the temporal stream.

However, the paper feels split across multiple contributions rather than having a single cohesive theme. While the two-stream framework is the main contribution, the paper also discusses the importance of pre-training, the effect of different input configurations, and the benefits of multi-task learning. While these contributions are valuable, they could be better integrated into the main narrative of the paper.

The authors provide a thorough review of related work, including both shallow and deep learning approaches to action recognition. However, there are a few missing citations, including "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis" (CVPR 2011) and "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition" (BMVC 2010).

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of action recognition in videos. The proposed two-stream framework is novel and effective, and the results demonstrate its potential for improving performance. With some minor revisions to address the suggested improvements, this paper is a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS 2014.
