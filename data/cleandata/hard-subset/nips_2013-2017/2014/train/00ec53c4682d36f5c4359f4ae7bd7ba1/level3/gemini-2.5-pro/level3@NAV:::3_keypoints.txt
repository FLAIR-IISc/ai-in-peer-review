- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet that decouples spatial (RGB frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) reasoning for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream operating on pre-computed dense optical flow is a key contribution that effectively learns motion patterns.  
- Multi-task learning framework introduced to leverage multiple datasets and reduce overfitting.  
- Significant empirical gains over prior deep-learning approaches and competitive performance with hand-crafted methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Technically sound work with extensive experiments supporting the claims.  
- Original architecture that combines image-pretrained ConvNets for appearance with a dedicated motion network.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and sufficient methodological detail for reproducibility.  
- Minor cohesion issue: paper feels split across multiple contributions rather than focused on the most impactful idea (two-stream + optical flow).  
- Missing relevant citations on spatio-temporal features (suggested: Le et al. CVPR 2011; Wang et al. BMVC 2010).  
- Recommendation: Accept (NIPS 2014), conditional on tightening focus and adding the suggested citations.
