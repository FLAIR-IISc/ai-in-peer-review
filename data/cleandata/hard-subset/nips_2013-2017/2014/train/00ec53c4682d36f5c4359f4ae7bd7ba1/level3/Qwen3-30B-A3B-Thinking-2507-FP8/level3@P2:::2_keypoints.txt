- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture to capture appearance (spatial) and motion (temporal) information for video action recognition.  
- Implements two separate ConvNets: one operating on individual frames for spatial cues and one on stacked dense optical flow for temporal cues.  
- Grounded in the two-stream (ventral/dorsal) hypothesis from neuroscience as motivation.  
- Contribution 1: introduces a decoupled spatial-temporal two-stream ConvNet architecture.  
- Contribution 2: shows ConvNets trained on dense optical flow perform strongly even with limited training data.  
- Contribution 3: develops a multi-task learning framework to combine UCF-101 and HMDB-51 training data for improved performance.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation including ablation studies on temporal input configurations (optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow) and fusion methods.  
- Demonstrates that optical flow inputs significantly outperform stacked raw frames and achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art shallow representations.  
- Implementation and evaluation are technically sound and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and supported by helpful figures.  
- Originality: first deep-learning implementation of the two-stream hypothesis, novel use of dense optical flow as ConvNet input, and useful multi-dataset training approach.  
- Significance: substantial advance for video action recognition with clear applications and directions for future work.  
- Minor weakness: unclear explanation for why the spatial stream (pretrained on ImageNet) outperforms similar prior architectures.  
- Minor weakness: limited exploration of different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, comprehensive experiments, clear explanation, competitive results, novel data-driven input representation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: only minor issues regarding spatial-stream explanation and frame-sampling exploration.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
