- Proposes a novel two-stream convolutional network separating spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) information for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream uses optical flow, which substantially improves performance, highlighting motion’s importance.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over prior deep-learning methods and competitive results with state-of-the-art shallow representations.  
- Well-written paper with clear methodology, implementation details, and thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Multi-task learning framework enables combining datasets and further boosts temporal-stream performance.  
- Strength lies in the novel two-stream framework and effective use of motion cues.  
- Paper feels fragmented across multiple contributions (two-stream, pre-training effects, input configs, multi-task learning) rather than a single cohesive narrative.  
- Missing a few relevant citations (e.g., CVPR 2011 ISA paper; BMVC 2010 evaluation of local spatio-temporal features).  
- Technical soundness and experimental support are strong; strengths and weaknesses are carefully evaluated.  
- Likely significant impact on action recognition and potential for wide adoption.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing minor revisions (integration of contributions and missing citations).
