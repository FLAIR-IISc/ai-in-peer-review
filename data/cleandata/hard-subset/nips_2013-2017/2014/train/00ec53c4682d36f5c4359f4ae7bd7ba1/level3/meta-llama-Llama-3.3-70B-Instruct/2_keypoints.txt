- Proposes a novel framework to learn distributed attribute representations jointly with word embeddings.  
- Introduces a third-order multiplicative model where word context and attribute vectors interact to predict the next word, enabling conditional word similarity.  
- Uses a tensor decomposition to represent word embeddings and attribute vectors, a novel and interesting approach.  
- Evaluated on sentiment classification, cross-lingual document classification, and blog authorship attribution, outperforming several baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanations of the model and experiments.  
- Strength: captures complex, context-dependent interactions between words and attributes for nuanced representations.  
- Provides thorough comparisons with existing approaches, highlighting advantages and limitations.  
- Weakness: performance could be improved on low-resource tasks (e.g., limited parallel data for cross-lingual classification).  
- Weakness: should explore different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Weakness: some experiments (notably conditional word similarity) need more thorough evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses and expand evaluations.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
