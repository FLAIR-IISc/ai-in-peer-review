- Proposes a discriminatively trained two-stream ConvNet separating spatial (RGB) and temporal (stacked optical flow) streams.
- Spatial stream uses ImageNet pre-training; temporal stream is trained from scratch on multi-frame optical flow volumes.
- Evaluates several temporal input variants: flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, and mean-flow subtraction.
- Introduces multi-task learning to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate data scarcity and improve generalization.
- Shows explicit optical flow input substantially outperforms raw-frame ConvNets and complements appearance via late fusion.
- Connects the architecture to neuroscience two-stream hypothesis and to classical hand-crafted features (HOG/HOF/MBH, dense trajectories).
- Temporal stream narrows the gap to trajectory-based state-of-the-art; fused model is competitive on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Strength: clear, well-executed idea decoupling appearance and motion with complementary ConvNets.
- Strength: strong empirical evidence with substantial gains from the temporal stream and complementary fusion.
- Strength: thoughtful ablations and detailed training/testing protocols enhance credibility and reproducibility.
- Strength: multi-task learning is a practical contribution that consistently improves performance, notably on HMDB-51.
- Strength: convincingly argues ConvNets can subsume classical motion descriptors like HOF/MBH.
- Weakness: paper feels split across multiple contributions without a single unifying narrative.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on precomputed optical flow raises compute, storage, and deployment concerns and prevents end-to-end training.
- Weakness: experiments limited to relatively small datasets (UCF-101, HMDB-51); Sports-1M left as future work.
- Weakness: fusion limited to late score fusion; joint fusion attempts overfit and lack deeper analysis.
- Weakness: camera-motion compensation reduced to mean-flow subtraction; more principled comparisons (homography/egomotion) are missing.
- Quality: technically sound, carefully evaluated, with thorough implementation details supporting reproducibility.
- Clarity: well written and organized; suggests refocusing abstract/intro to emphasize two-stream optical-flow core message.
- Originality: two-stream ConvNet with explicit optical flow is novel relative to prior RGB-stack deep models; multi-tasking application is useful though not novel.
- Significance: advances deep video recognition practice, likely to influence future architectures and is practically useful given dataset constraints.
- Suggestions: emphasize main take-away in abstract/intro and present multi-tasking as a practical enhancer.
- Suggestions: provide computational profiles (flow runtime, storage, training/inference throughput) and sensitivity analysis to flow quality.
- Suggestions: analyze failure cases, explore stronger camera-motion compensation, and investigate ways to prevent overfitting in joint fusion.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, well-motivated architecture with strong empirical gains and thorough experiments.
- Cons against acceptance: diluted narrative, dependence on precomputed flow, limited evaluation scale, and weaker camera-motion/deployment treatment.
- Recommendation: Accept (conditional) — clarify main narrative, strengthen related-work positioning, and add discussion of computational trade-offs and fusion overfitting.
