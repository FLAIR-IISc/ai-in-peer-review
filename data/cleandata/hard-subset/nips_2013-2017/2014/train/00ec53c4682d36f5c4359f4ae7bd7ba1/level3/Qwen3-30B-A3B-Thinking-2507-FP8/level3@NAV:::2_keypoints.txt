- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet combining a spatial stream (frames) and a temporal stream (short dense optical flow) for video action recognition.  
- Significantly outperforms prior deep-learning methods and matches state-of-the-art on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with a simpler architecture.  
- Contribution: effectively decouples spatial and temporal recognition streams.  
- Contribution: shows ConvNets trained on optical flow outperform those trained on raw RGB frames.  
- Contribution: introduces a multi-task learning framework to leverage multiple datasets during training.  
- Thorough evaluation of different optical flow input configurations, demonstrating clear benefits over stacked RGB inputs.  
- Comprehensive experiments and careful ablation studies showing the two streams are complementary.  
- High quality: claims are well-supported by extensive experimental results and clear architecture description tied to the two-stream hypothesis.  
- Clear exposition with sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Minor weakness: unclear why the spatial stream outperforms Karpathy et al.’s models despite similar architectures—needs clarification.  
- Suggested additional analysis: evaluate different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical contribution, technically sound, significant for video classification; recommend acceptance.
