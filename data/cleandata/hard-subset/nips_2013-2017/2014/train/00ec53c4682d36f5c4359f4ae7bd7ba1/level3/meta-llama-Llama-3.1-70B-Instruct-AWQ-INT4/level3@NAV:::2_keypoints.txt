- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial on single frames, temporal on short dense optical flow) for video action recognition.  
- Method significantly outperforms prior ConvNet-based approaches and matches state-of-the-art on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Thorough evaluation, including analysis of input configurations (stacking flows, trajectory stacking, mean subtraction).  
- Demonstrates the benefit of using optical flow as input to the temporal network.  
- Explores multi-task learning to boost temporal network performance.  
- Lacks clear explanation for why the spatial network outperforms similarly architected prior models.  
- Needs more detailed analysis of learned features and their relation to motion information.  
- Paper is well-written, experiments are thorough and well-designed, and results are impressive.  
- Strong empirical contribution likely to interest video classification researchers.  
- For acceptance: novel and effective approach, thorough evaluations, and clear benefit of optical flow.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient feature analysis and unclear reasons for spatial network gains.  
- Recommendation: provide a deeper analysis of learned features and their motion relevance.  
- Recommendation: explore different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Recommendation: add more context/background on the two-stream hypothesis and its relation to the approach.
