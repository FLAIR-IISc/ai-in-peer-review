- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet combining a spatial stream (single frames, ImageNet pretraining) and a temporal stream (dense optical flow) with late fusion.  
- Demonstrates that feeding optical flow into a ConvNet outperforms prior deep approaches using stacked RGB frames.  
- Shows the temporal ConvNet generalizes hand-crafted motion features (HOF, MBH) via a learnable convolutional representation.  
- Spatial stream leverages ImageNet pretraining; temporal stream processes dense optical flow inputs.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine UCF-101 and HMDB-51, improving performance on the smaller HMDB-51.  
- Architecture is simple, elegant, and empirically strong with clear experimental comparisons.  
- Includes thorough implementation details and ablation studies on temporal stream input configurations.  
- Establishes that ConvNets can effectively model optical flow for action recognition, with significant impact on the field.  
- Builds on prior two-stream/neuroscience ideas but is the first effective deep ConvNet implementation for this two-stream approach.  
- Major limitation: only processes short fixed-length temporal windows (L=10), hindering long-range temporal modeling.  
- Evaluation lacks error-mode and per-class analysis, limiting insight into failure modes.  
- Does not fully address camera motion beyond simple mean subtraction.  
- Overall technically sound, well-written, and recommended for acceptance despite temporal-modeling limitations.
