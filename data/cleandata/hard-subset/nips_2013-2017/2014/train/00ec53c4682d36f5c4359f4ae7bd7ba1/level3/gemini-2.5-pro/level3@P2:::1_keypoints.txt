- Proposes a two-stream deep architecture: a spatial ConvNet on video frames and a temporal ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow, with late fusion of predictions.  
- Spatial stream uses ImageNet pretraining for strong appearance features; temporal stream provides explicit motion information via optical flow stacks.  
- Temporal ConvNet significantly outperforms the spatial stream and prior raw-RGB deep models for motion modeling.  
- Fusion of the two streams (averaging or SVM on softmax scores) yields competitive state-of-the-art results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine datasets and reduce overfitting for the temporal network.  
- Differs from prior video ConvNet work by using optical flow rather than stacked RGB, acting as a learned generalization of hand-crafted motion features (HOF/MBH).  
- Strength — Novelty/effectiveness: ConvNet on dense optical flow is a simple, powerful solution to represent motion.  
- Strength — Simplicity: Decoupling appearance and motion is conceptually elegant and empirically effective, rivaling engineered pipelines.  
- Strength — Experimental validation: Comprehensive ablations (stack length, mean flow subtraction, multi-task learning) support design choices.  
- Weakness — Limited temporal modeling: only processes short fixed windows (L=10) and averages clip scores, so long-range/sequence structure is not modeled.  
- Weakness — Lacks deeper qualitative error analysis to pinpoint failure modes (e.g., subtle motions, camera movement).  
- Quality: Technically sound with well-supported claims and reproducible details.  
- Clarity: Clearly written and well-organized with effective figures and implementation details.  
- Originality: Highly original instantiation of a temporal ConvNet on optical flow within a two-stream framework.  
- Significance: High impact; advanced state-of-the-art and influenced subsequent video understanding research.  
- Acceptance arguments: Simple, novel, highly effective idea with strong empirical results and major field impact.  
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient long-range temporal reasoning and missing qualitative failure-case analysis.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses.
