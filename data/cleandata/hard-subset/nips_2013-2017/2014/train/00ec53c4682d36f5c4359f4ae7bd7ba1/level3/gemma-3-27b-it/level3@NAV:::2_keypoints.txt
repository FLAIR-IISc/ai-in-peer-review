- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture: a spatial stream on single frames and a temporal stream on dense optical flow, with fused outputs.  
- Builds on the two-stream hypothesis to explicitly decouple spatial and temporal information for action recognition.  
- Leverages deep learning and transfer learning (ImageNet pretraining) to improve video classification.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, matching state-of-the-art hand-crafted features.  
- Provides thorough experimentation, including ablations on optical flow configurations and multi-task learning.  
- Shows the temporal stream benefits particularly from optical flow inputs and careful training strategies.  
- Pre-training on ImageNet substantially enhances performance.  
- Minor concern: spatial network outperforms prior work [13] despite similar architecture and lacks deeper explanation.  
- Minor concern: did not empirically evaluate different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear/ logical approach, comprehensive experiments, and good writing/contextualization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unexplained superior spatial network performance and missing frame-sampling evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong empirical paper with significant contribution to video classification; recommend acceptance.
