- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture that decouples spatial and temporal processing for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream processes individual frames using ImageNet pre-training.  
- Temporal stream processes dense optical flow over a fixed-length window (L=10).  
- Achieves competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep learning methods and matching hand-crafted feature approaches.  
- Demonstrates novel and effective use of optical flow within a CNN without complex hand-crafted features.  
- Architecture and experiments are technically sound; temporal features complement spatial ones and multi-task learning helps with limited video data.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Main strengths: simplicity combined with strong empirical performance and convincing evidence of optical flow utility.  
- Key limitation: fixed-length temporal window (L=10) prevents modeling long-range temporal dependencies.  
- Evaluation lacks error-mode analysis and does not fully explore varying video durations or complex motion patterns.  
- Scores high on originality and significance; suitable for NIPS, though temporal limitation slightly limits broader impact.  
- Overall: high-quality contribution to video action recognition; future work should address longer-range temporal modeling.
