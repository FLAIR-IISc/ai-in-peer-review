- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial frames + temporal optical flow) for video action recognition that outperforms prior deep methods.  
- Shows strong empirical results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, competitive with state-of-the-art shallow representations.  
- Temporal stream with optical flow input is particularly effective.  
- Uses multi-task learning to better leverage small/limited video datasets.  
- Methodology and implementation (optical flow ConvNet inputs, multi-task framework) are well documented and clear.  
- Writing and organization are excellent, but the paper lacks cohesion across multiple contributions.  
- Fails to cite key related work (CVPR 2011 "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features..." and BMVC 2010 "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features...").  
- Strengths: technically sound architecture, comprehensive experiments, significant performance improvements.  
- Weaknesses: diffuse focus and missing critical citations that must be addressed.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally, pending narrative refocus and inclusion of the omitted citations.
