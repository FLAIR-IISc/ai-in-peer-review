- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture combining appearance (frames) and motion (optical flow) for video classification.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to increase training data and improve performance across tasks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness of ConvNets trained on multi-frame dense optical flow.  
- Achieves strong, competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 benchmarks.  
- Well-written, clear paper with thorough methodology and implementation details enabling reproducibility.  
- Provides thorough analysis showing the importance of motion and benefits of using optical flow as input.  
- Shows that a single convolutional layer can generalize hand-crafted features like HOG and HOF.  
- Technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments; careful evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Originality lies in combining familiar techniques into a novel two-stream and multi-task framework.  
- Significance: advances state of the art in video classification and is competitive with shallow representations.  
- Paper feels split across multiple contributions rather than having a single cohesive theme.  
- Missing citations to relevant prior work (e.g., CVPR 2011 ISA paper and BMVC 2010 evaluation of spatio-temporal features).  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing suggested improvements.  
- Suggested improvements: refocus abstract/introduction on two-stream/optical flow, add missing citations, and unify contributions into a cohesive theme.
