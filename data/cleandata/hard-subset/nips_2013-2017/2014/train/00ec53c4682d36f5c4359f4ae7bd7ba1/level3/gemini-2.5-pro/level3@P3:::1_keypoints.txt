- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition: a spatial stream for appearance and a temporal stream for motion (stacked dense optical flow).
- Spatial stream is pre-trained on ImageNet; temporal stream is trained from scratch on optical flow from target datasets.
- Final prediction uses late fusion of softmax scores by averaging or SVM on concatenated scores.
- Approach departs from end-to-end spatio-temporal learning by explicitly separating appearance and motion using pre-computed optical flow.
- Strength: elegant simplicity that matches or outperforms complex hand-engineered methods (e.g., iDT).
- Strength: key novelty is showing ConvNets can learn powerful motion features directly from dense optical flow.
- Weakness: limited temporal modeling — temporal stream uses a short fixed stack (L=10) and averages over snippets, losing long-range temporal structure and ordering.
- Weakness: lacks qualitative error-mode analysis to identify failure cases or challenging video types.
- Quality: technically sound with systematic ablations and experiments supporting claims.
- Clarity: paper is very well written and organized, with helpful figures explaining architecture and inputs.
- Originality: novelty lies in applying ConvNets to pre-computed optical flow rather than in the network architecture itself.
- Significance: high impact — established a strong baseline and influenced much subsequent video understanding research despite limitations.
