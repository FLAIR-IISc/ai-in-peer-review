- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that processes spatial and temporal information separately for video action recognition.
- Spatial stream ConvNet operates on single RGB frames to capture appearance cues.
- Temporal stream ConvNet is trained on stacks of dense optical flow to learn motion patterns.
- The two networks are trained independently and fused by averaging their softmax scores.
- This explicitly supplies motion information to the network, addressing prior failures to learn motion from raw pixels.
- Strength: novel and effective application of ConvNets to optical flow, yielding powerful motion representations.
- Strength: performance comparable to state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods (e.g., improved Dense Trajectories) on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Strength: conceptually simple architecture with clear, well-documented experiments enabling reproducibility.
- Weakness: limited temporal modeling—only short, fixed 10-frame windows, so long-range temporal structure is not captured.
- Weakness: would benefit from deeper error-mode analysis (per-class failures or video characteristics causing errors).
- Overall: high-quality, original work with significant impact; strongly recommended for acceptance.
