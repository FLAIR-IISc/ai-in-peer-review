- Proposes a two-stream CNN that combines a spatial network on frames and a temporal network on stacked dense optical flow, trained separately and fused for action recognition.  
- Addresses a gap between hand-crafted feature methods and deep learning for video by explicitly modeling motion with CNNs on optical flow.  
- Leverages multi-task learning and pre-trained spatial CNN to mitigate limited video dataset sizes.  
- Strong performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, comparable to state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature methods.  
- Demonstrates that CNNs trained on multi-frame dense optical flow effectively capture motion information.  
- Architecture is simple, easy to implement, and benefits from pre-training, aiding practical adoption.  
- Experiments are well-designed and include useful ablations (e.g., flow stacking, mean subtraction).  
- Writing, figures, and implementation details are clear and facilitate reproducibility.  
- Novel contribution is the effective application of CNNs to optical flow within a two-stream framework.  
- Significant potential impact on video/motion research by showing a promising deep-learning direction.  
- Main limitation: restricted temporal context due to fixed short windows (L=10), hindering long-range action modeling.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis (per-class failures, challenging scenarios), limiting understanding of failure modes.  
- Limited exploration of fusion strategies between streams; more sophisticated fusion (e.g., attention) might improve results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong results, novel and effective deep-learning approach for motion, clear presentation, and validation of explicit motion modeling.  
- Arguments against acceptance: short temporal modeling horizon and insufficient error analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant contribution; recommend acceptance despite noted limitations.
