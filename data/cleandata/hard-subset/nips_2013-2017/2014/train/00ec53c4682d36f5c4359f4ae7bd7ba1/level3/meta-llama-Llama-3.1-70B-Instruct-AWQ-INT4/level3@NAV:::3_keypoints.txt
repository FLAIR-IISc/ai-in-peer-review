- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture (spatial + temporal) with late fusion for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream recognizes actions from still frames; temporal stream uses dense optical flow to capture motion.  
- Method is well-described, reproducible, and the paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- The approach is novel, complements existing frameworks, and incorporating optical flow yields significant performance gains.  
- Experimental results are strong, outperform previous deep-net attempts and competitive with state-of-the-art shallow methods.  
- Thorough evaluation on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 demonstrates effectiveness across challenging datasets.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Main weakness: the paper feels split between two contributions (two-stream architecture vs. multi-task learning) rather than presenting a cohesive theme.  
- Suggest refocusing the abstract/introduction on the two-stream/optical-flow contribution as the core idea.  
- Missing citations: CVPR 2011 “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features…” and BMVC 2010 “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features…” should be added.  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS 2014) conditional on addressing the suggested improvements.
