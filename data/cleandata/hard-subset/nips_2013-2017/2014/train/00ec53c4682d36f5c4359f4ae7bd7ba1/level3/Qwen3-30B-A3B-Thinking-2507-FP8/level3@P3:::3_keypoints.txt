- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that processes spatial (RGB) and temporal (optical flow) information separately for action recognition.
- Trains a spatial stream on still frames and a temporal stream on optical flow, showing optical-flow input yields superior motion modeling.
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine UCF-101 and HMDB-51 training data, improving performance on both datasets.
- Positions work against prior trajectory-based hand-crafted features and stacked-frame deep approaches, showing clear empirical gains.
- Temporal stream substantially outperforms stacked RGB-frame ConvNets (e.g., 69.3% vs. 56.4% on UCF-101).
- Experiments are rigorous, methodology well-documented, and results convincingly compared to state-of-the-art.
- Notes limitation of requiring optical flow computation and absence of trajectory pooling component.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and reproducible, with clear figures and implementation details.
- Main clarity issue: paper feels split across multiple contributions instead of a single cohesive theme; abstract/introduction should be refocused.
- Novelty: introduces a significant departure from previous deep approaches via two-stream architecture and optical-flow input; multi-task dataset combination is valuable.
- Misses important citations (CVPR 2011 and BMVC 2010) that should be added to strengthen literature positioning.
- Significance: yields large performance gains, competes with shallow state-of-the-art, and provides a clear direction for future video understanding research.
- Strengths: clear two-stream presentation, strong empirical gains, reproducible methodology, effective multi-task learning, comprehensive comparisons.
- Weaknesses: unfocused contributions, missing prior-work citations, limited discussion on extending approach to other video tasks, insufficient deeper explanation for why optical flow outperforms stacked RGB.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to emphasize two-stream/optical-flow contribution in abstract/introduction and add the missing citations.
