- Proposes a novel framework that jointly learns distributed attribute representations and word embeddings using a third-order tensor whose slices correspond to attributes.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on sentiment classification, cross-lingual document classification, and blog authorship attribution.  
- Strength: simple and novel use of attribute vectors as a contribution to deep learning for NLP.  
- Strength: well-designed experiments showing the model outperforms several baselines.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written, well-explained, and well-organized.  
- Weakness: model only models fixed-length adjacent frames and cannot capture overall sequence structure.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of error modes and characterization of texts where the model fails.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of applicability to other NLP tasks like MT or QA.  
- Quality: technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments; authors are careful and honest.  
- Originality: novel third-order tensor approach for learning attribute representations building on prior embedding work.  
- Significance: potential to improve NLP model performance across tasks by learning attribute representations.  
- Recommendation: accept—significant contribution with promising results.
