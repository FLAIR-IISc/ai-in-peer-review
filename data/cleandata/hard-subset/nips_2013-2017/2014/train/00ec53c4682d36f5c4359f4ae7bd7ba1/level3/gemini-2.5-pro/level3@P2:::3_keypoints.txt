- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet architecture separating appearance (spatial) and motion (temporal) for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream operates on individual frames and leverages ImageNet pre-training.  
- Temporal stream is trained on stacked dense optical flow fields to capture motion dynamics.  
- Spatial and temporal outputs are combined via late fusion (averaging or SVM) for final classification.  
- Multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 is used to mitigate overfitting, especially for the temporal stream.  
- Methodology is described in extensive detail, enabling reproducibility.  
- Strong experimental evaluation on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with comprehensive ablation studies (flow stacking, mean subtraction, pre-training, multi-tasking).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized with clear motivation and illustrative figures.  
- Minor clarity critique: narrative could be tightened to emphasize the two-stream/optical-flow contribution, with multi-task learning framed as supportive.  
- Highly original: novel application of ConvNets trained directly on dense optical flow, integrating neuroscience-inspired separate pathways into deep learning.  
- Significant impact: substantially improves deep learning performance on action recognition and provides a simple, effective baseline.  
- Main drawback: requires pre-computed optical flow, so not fully end-to-end from raw pixels.  
- Minor drawback: paper covers multiple contributions, which slightly diffuses the main narrative.  
- Suggestion: more direct contrast between learned temporal features and hand-crafted/unsupervised features (MBH, HOF, ISA) to highlight advantages.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, novel, well-evaluated, and impactful.
