- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition separating spatial (single frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) processing.  
- Temporal stream uses stacked dense optical flow to explicitly capture motion, improving on prior ConvNet attempts on raw frames.  
- Achieves significant performance gains over previous ConvNet-based methods and is competitive with hand-crafted feature approaches on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Leverages multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to improve generalization given small datasets.  
- Thorough ablation studies investigate temporal input configurations (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction).  
- Clear, well-structured paper with sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Analysis links learned convolutional filters to hand-crafted features (e.g., HOF, MBH), showing the network generalizes prior features.  
- Builds on prior work and is motivated by complementary spatial/temporal information and the ventral/dorsal stream analogy.  
- Spatial network unexpectedly outperforms similar architectures from prior work without a clear explanation.  
- Frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream are not explored, leaving potential performance gains untested.  
- Paper does not discuss limitations or inaccuracies of the optical flow computation used, which may affect temporal stream performance.  
- Strong empirical contribution and methodological novelty argue for acceptance.  
- Multi-task learning and comprehensive experiments further support the paper’s value.  
- Minor weaknesses (unexplained spatial network advantage, unexplored sampling strategies) are not critical.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper with a well-motivated, effective two-stream architecture that merits acceptance.
