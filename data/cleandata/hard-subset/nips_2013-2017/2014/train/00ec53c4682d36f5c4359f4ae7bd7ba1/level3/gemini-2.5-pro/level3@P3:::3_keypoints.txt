- Proposes a novel two-stream deep architecture separating spatial (frame-based) and temporal (optical-flow-based) processing for action recognition.  
- Spatial stream: ConvNet trained on individual frames to capture appearance cues.  
- Temporal stream: ConvNet trained on stacks of dense optical flow to explicitly model motion, improving temporal representation.  
- Multi-task learning framework to combine datasets and reduce overfitting on limited video data.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: streams are complementary and fusion yields substantial accuracy gains, competitive with top hand-crafted methods.  
- Method outperforms prior deep-learning attempts on video classification and scales well despite small datasets.  
- Paper is very clear and reproducible, with detailed methodology, implementation, and training procedures provided.  
- Practical contribution: principled approach to leverage optical flow and multi-dataset training for temporal ConvNets.  
- Weakness: paper lacks a single cohesive narrative and should emphasize the optical-flow-driven two-stream finding as the central contribution.  
- Weakness: misses relevant prior work citations (Le et al. CVPR 2011; Wang et al. BMVC 2010) and should discuss them.  
- Suggestion: revise abstract/introduction to highlight the primary insight that explicit optical flow enables effective temporal ConvNet learning.  
- Note: the experimental finding that simple optical-flow stacking matches or outperforms trajectory stacking is interesting and should be discussed in the main text.  
- Qualitative assessment: technically sound, original, clearly presented, and highly significant for advancing deep learning in video analysis.  
- Recommendation: strongly accept, conditional on addressing minor issues (sharpen focus and add missing citations).
