- Proposes a two-stream deep architecture separating spatial (RGB ConvNet) and temporal (ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow) streams, fused by late fusion.
- Spatial stream: standard ConvNet pretrained on ImageNet and trained on individual RGB frames.
- Temporal stream: key contribution — ConvNet trained on stacked dense optical flow to explicitly provide motion information.
- Networks trained separately; class scores combined via averaging or SVM late fusion.
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 and achieves competitive results with hand-crafted methods.
- Method bridges prior deep models and engineered pipelines, treating temporal stream as a learned generalization of HOF/MBH and iDT features.
- Novelty/impact: the optical-flow-based temporal stream is a highly effective, influential idea in video understanding.
- Simplicity: the two-stream design is conceptually simple, intuitive, and allows specialization for appearance vs. motion.
- Empirical strength: strong performance on benchmarks with thorough ablation studies supporting design choices.
- Ablations justify benefits of multi-frame optical flow and multi-task learning.
- Weakness: limited temporal modeling — short fixed-length stacks (L=10) and score averaging ignore long-range temporal structure.
- Weakness: lacks qualitative error analysis (no confusion matrices or detailed failure-mode discussion).
- Quality: technically sound with complete methodology and reproducible implementation details.
- Clarity: clearly written, well-organized, and illustrated, facilitating understanding and reproduction.
- Originality: novel application of ConvNets to stacked optical flow fields, a fresh bridge between classic descriptors and deep learning.
- Significance: high — foundational contribution that influenced many subsequent video models.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel motion incorporation, simple yet powerful architecture, competitive benchmark results, and thorough experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate long-range temporal modeling and insufficient qualitative analysis of errors.
- Overall: strengths (originality, impact, performance) outweigh weaknesses; a foundational and influential contribution to video understanding.
