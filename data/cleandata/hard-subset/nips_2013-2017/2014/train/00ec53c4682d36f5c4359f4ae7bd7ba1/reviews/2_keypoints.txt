- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial network on single frames + temporal network on short dense optical flow sequences for action classification.
- Significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches and matches state-of-the-art on two standard video datasets.
- Essentially applies standard image ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow and fuses it with a single-frame ConvNet; experiments are thorough and results impressive.
- Evaluates several ways to train ConvNets on optical flow, showing clear benefits of optical-flow input.
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network beats models from [13] given similar architectures.
- Suggestion: evaluate different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.
- Overall assessment: a strong empirical paper of interest to the video-classification community.
