- Proposed model: two convolutional nets—one for a single appearance frame and one for a stack of dense optical-flow features; each trained separately and predictions averaged.  
- Evaluation: tested on two datasets and achieves results comparable to state-of-the-art methods using heavy hand-crafted features.  
- Strength: simple application of convolutional nets that obtains strong performance.  
- Strength: novel and important demonstration that optical-flow inputs are useful for action recognition.  
- Weakness: only models short fixed-length temporal windows (L=10) and does not capture whole-video sequence structure.  
- Quality: experiments are well-designed but lack error-mode analysis (e.g., per-class failures or types of videos causing errors).  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.  
- Originality: novel application of convolutional nets to optical flow features.  
- Significance: approach could significantly impact video/motion research and shows promising results.
