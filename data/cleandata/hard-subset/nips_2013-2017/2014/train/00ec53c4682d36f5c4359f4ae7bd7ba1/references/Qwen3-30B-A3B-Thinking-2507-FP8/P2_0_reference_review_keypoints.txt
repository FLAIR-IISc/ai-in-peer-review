- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture separating spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) processing, fused for action recognition in videos.  
- Spatial stream uses ConvNet pre-trained on ImageNet to process individual frames; temporal stream processes stacked optical flow between frames.  
- Demonstrates significant improvement over prior deep learning methods and competitive performance with state-of-the-art hand-crafted shallow representations on UCF101 and HMDB-51.  
- Builds on the two-streams hypothesis from human vision and uses multi-task learning to leverage limited video training data.  
- Thorough ablation studies validate architectural choices (flow stacking variants, bi-directional flow, multi-task learning) and show temporal stream provides the largest gains.  
- Shows that explicitly using optical flow as input yields substantially better motion representation than stacked RGB frames.  
- Technically sound, well-executed experiments with consistent evaluation across dataset splits.  
- Multi-task learning approach is well-motivated and effective for limited video data.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes clear figures and reproducible implementation details.  
- Carefully acknowledges limitations, including lagging behind some hand-crafted methods in aspects like local feature pooling over spatio-temporal tubes.  
- Lacks deeper analysis explaining why optical flow input outperforms alternatives—insufficient insight into underlying causes.  
- Computational efficiency concerns are under-addressed; pre-computing optical flow is storage- and computation-intensive (e.g., 27GB for UCF-101).  
- Explanation for why trajectory stacking underperforms optical flow stacking is insufficiently detailed.  
- Practical implications and scalability to longer videos or more complex actions are not well explored.  
- Novel implementation of two-stream idea using discriminatively trained ConvNets and optical flow input distinguishes it from prior deep approaches.  
- Does not sufficiently differentiate from earlier two-stream HMAX work or address integration with recent DeepFlow-like optical flow networks.  
- Advances the field by bringing deep learning performance closer to hand-crafted methods and offering a practical architecture for video analysis.  
- Does not fully close the gap with the best hand-crafted methods and lacks a clear roadmap for bridging that gap or extending to broader video understanding tasks.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, convincing experimental support, technical soundness—recommended for acceptance.
