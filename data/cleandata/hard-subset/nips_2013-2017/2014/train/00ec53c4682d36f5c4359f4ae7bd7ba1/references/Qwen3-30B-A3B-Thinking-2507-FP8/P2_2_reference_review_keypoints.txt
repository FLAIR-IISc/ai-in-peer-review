- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture decomposing video into spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) streams.
- Spatial stream uses a pre-trained ImageNet model on individual frames; temporal stream uses stacked dense optical flow to capture motion.
- Shows that spatial and temporal streams are complementary and fusing them outperforms prior deep-learning video methods.
- Introduces multi-task learning to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, addressing limited video training data.
- Grounds the approach in the two-stream hypothesis from human vision.
- Argues optical flow is a better input than stacked raw frames for learning motion representations.
- Technically sound architecture with clear rationale and reproducible implementation details.
- Thorough experimental evaluation with detailed ablation studies on temporal stream variations (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction).
- Multi-task learning is a significant practical contribution for dataset-limited video recognition.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest future directions (camera motion handling, trajectory-based pooling).
- Lacks a more detailed comparison with the most recent hand-crafted methods beyond the results in Table 4.
- Does not fully explore the implications of training on large datasets like Sports-1M.
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear figures, formulations, and implementation specifics.
- Could provide a deeper theoretical explanation of why optical flow outperforms stacked frames.
- Novel in applying the two-stream concept to deep ConvNets for video recognition; distinct from prior stacked-frame approaches.
- Two-stream hypothesis itself is not new, but its specific ConvNet application is original.
- Makes a significant impact: advances the state of the art, widely cited, and influential in subsequent research.
- Lacks full analysis of computational cost, which may limit real-time applicability.
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, novel, well-supported contribution with only minor weaknesses.
