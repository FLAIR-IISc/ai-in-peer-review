- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial stream on RGB and temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow (2L channels).
- Explores temporal-input variants: optical-flow stacking vs trajectory stacking, uni- vs bi-directional flow, and mean flow subtraction.
- Spatial stream is ImageNet-pretrained; temporal stream trained from scratch with multi-task heads to leverage UCF101 and HMDB51.
- Fusion of streams done by score averaging or linear SVM; extensive implementation and training details provided.
- Strong empirical results and ablations show temporal stream outperforms RGB-stacking baselines and two-stream fusion yields sizable accuracy gains.
- Operationalizes the two-stream (ventral/dorsal) hypothesis with discriminatively learned, deep ConvNets.
- Temporal stream generalizes classical motion descriptors by learning filters on raw displacement fields.
- Explicit optical-flow input outperforms 3D CNN/slow-fusion approaches on small datasets.
- Multi-task training is a pragmatic way to expand supervision and reduces overfitting, notably improving HMDB51.
- Practical contributions include multi-GPU training, reproducible protocols, and a scalable optical-flow precomputation pipeline.
- Major weakness: reliance on precomputed optical flow breaks end-to-end learning and ties performance/latency to the chosen flow algorithm.
- Camera-motion handling is limited to mean-flow subtraction; stronger compensation (e.g., homography) is not evaluated.
- Key ablations are mostly on a single UCF101 split, so generalization of design choices across splits/datasets is under-quantified.
- Trajectory stacking underperforms plain stacking but the paper lacks analysis explaining this discrepancy.
- Fusion is limited to late score combination; joint fusion over fully connected layers overfits and more regularized fusion strategies are unexplored.
- Paper omits end-to-end inference cost (including flow computation), hindering practical deployment assessment.
- Technically sound and coherent, but sensitivity analyses (flow quality, compression, splits) would strengthen claims.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with sufficient implementation detail for reproduction.
- Originality is moderate-to-high: novel combination of explicit optical-flow temporal ConvNet within two-stream architecture and multi-task training.
- Significance is high: provides a practical, competitive alternative to hand-crafted pipelines and likely to become a strong baseline.
- Suggested experiments: accuracy vs. L and temporal stride, end-to-end ms/frame including flow, impact of flow JPEG compression, analysis of trajectory stacking failure, regularized joint fusion, stronger camera compensation, and per-class/robustness breakdowns.
- Pros: simple effective architecture, thorough implementation details, useful ablations, competitive performance with hand-crafted methods.
- Cons: not end-to-end, heavy reliance on precomputed flow without sensitivity analysis, limited fusion and camera-motion exploration, some ablations limited to one split.
- Recommendation: Accept.
