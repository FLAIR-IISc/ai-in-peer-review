- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture that separates spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked optical flow) processing and fuses them for action recognition.
- Demonstrates significant improvement over prior deep learning methods (stacked-frame CNN) by explicitly modeling motion with optical flow.
- Achieves competitive results with, and sometimes exceeds, hand-crafted feature baselines on UCF101 and HMDB51.
- Thorough experimental evaluation exploring temporal stream configurations (stacking, mean subtraction, bi-directional flow) and fusion strategies.
- Well-written and organized paper with clear figures and notation.
- Novel contribution in decoupling spatial and temporal processing, inspired by the two-streams hypothesis.
- Uses multi-task learning to leverage multiple datasets and improve generalization.
- Effectively leverages ImageNet pre-training for the spatial stream.
- Relies on pre-computed optical flow, adding computational cost and dependence on flow quality; joint learning of flow is not explored.
- Fusion strategy exploration is limited; learned fusion within the network is not sufficiently investigated.
- Lacks systematic ablation on temporal stream network depth/width to assess complexity effects.
- Limited analysis of failure cases and scenarios where the model underperforms.
- Some references are dated; broader review of recent literature would strengthen the paper.
- Overall technically sound, claims supported by experiments, but could use additional ablations for robustness.
- Reviewer recommendation: strong accept — significant, well-presented contribution with minor limitations to address in future work.
