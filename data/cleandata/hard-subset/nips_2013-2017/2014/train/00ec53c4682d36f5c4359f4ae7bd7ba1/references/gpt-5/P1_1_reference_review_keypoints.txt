- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video action recognition that decouples spatial appearance and temporal motion (optical flow) with late score fusion.  
- Spatial stream: image ConvNet pretrained on ImageNet; temporal stream: ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow fields with several input variants.  
- Uses late fusion (averaging or linear SVM) and multi-task learning with two softmax heads to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Temporal stream on optical flow significantly outperforms frame-only ConvNet and multi-frame RGB “slow fusion” baselines.  
- Spatial and temporal streams are complementary, yielding strong gains when fused.  
- Multi-task learning improves generalization, especially on HMDB-51.  
- Achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art trajectory-based pipelines while using deep learning.  
- Situates work relative to hand-crafted trajectory methods and prior deep models; novelty lies in combining optical flow with deep ConvNets and systematic input design.  
- Technical choices are well-motivated with thorough ablation studies (flow stacking, trajectory vs flow stacking, bidirectional flow, mean subtraction, training regimes, fusion).  
- Empirical evaluation is solid and reproducible, following standard protocols with detailed implementation/training specifics.  
- Runtime/storage overhead of optical flow is acknowledged but not deeply quantified.  
- Paper is well written, organized, and unusually detailed on implementation for reproducibility.  
- Suggest clarifying SVM-fusion training protocol and reporting per-clip/per-video runtimes for flow computation and inference.  
- Not end-to-end trainable with respect to motion estimation; sensitivity to different flow algorithms is not explored.  
- Camera motion compensation is only approximated via mean flow subtraction; homography-based baseline is suggested.  
- Fusion exploration is limited to score-level; intermediate fusion overfitting and regularization strategies could be investigated.  
- Limited error analysis; class-wise performance or confusion patterns would clarify complementarity.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — impactful, well-validated, and practically useful contribution to video recognition.
