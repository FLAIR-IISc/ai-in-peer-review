- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet architecture for video action recognition: a spatial stream for frames and a temporal stream using dense optical flow.
- Key innovation: using dense optical flow as ConvNet input for the temporal stream rather than stacked RGB frames.
- Demonstrates significant improvement over single-stream ConvNets and competitive results with state-of-the-art shallow representations.
- Proposes a multi-task learning framework to combine datasets (UCF101 and HMDB-51) effectively.
- Builds on prior two-stream and stacked-frame work but extends to deep ConvNets for both streams.
- Provides thorough experimental evaluation on UCF101 and HMDB-51 with ablation studies on optical flow configurations.
- Strength: comprehensive ablation studies comparing flow lengths, stacking methods, mean subtraction, and bi-directional flow.
- Strength: multi-task learning is well-motivated and yields clear performance benefits over naive dataset merging.
- Strength: implementation details are thorough and reproducible, including GPU training and flow computation optimizations.
- Strength: results are statistically significant and show clear improvements over relevant baselines.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical explanation for why explicit optical-flow-based temporal stream outperforms implicit motion learning.
- Weakness: limited direct comparison to prior deep architecture [14]; no re-implementation to show exact performance gap.
- Weakness: missing error analysis and qualitative examples of successes and failures to illuminate model behavior.
- Clarity strength: paper is well-organized with clear figures illustrating architecture and input configurations.
- Clarity strength: optical flow input configurations and multi-task framework are clearly described.
- Clarity weakness: motivation for mean flow subtraction is under-explained and lacks quantitative evidence of its effect.
- Clarity weakness: relation to prior shallow two-stream work [16] could be more explicit about differences.
- Clarity weakness: some terms (e.g., "slow fusion") are introduced without sufficient explanation for unfamiliar readers.
- Originality strength: novel application of deep ConvNets to optical flow for temporal modeling in video recognition.
- Originality weakness: core idea of separating spatial and temporal streams is not entirely new and is somewhat incremental.
- Originality weakness: limited exploration of connections to other optical-flow-based approaches like "deep matching" [30].
- Significance strength: shows deep learning can match or beat state-of-the-art shallow methods for action recognition.
- Significance strength: demonstrates optical flow input and multi-task learning as practical improvements for small video datasets.
- Significance weakness: computational cost of optical flow computation is not fully addressed, especially at test time.
- Significance weakness: no experiments on larger datasets (e.g., Sports-1M) or analysis of scaling to longer/more complex videos.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, significant contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
- Specific comment 1: Add theoretical discussion on why explicit optical flow is better than implicit motion learning.
- Specific comment 2: Provide more context and quantitative impact for mean flow subtraction.
- Specific comment 3: Include qualitative examples of correct and incorrect predictions for error analysis.
- Specific comment 4: More explicitly contrast your approach with [16] to highlight novelty.
- Specific comment 5: Discuss computational cost of optical flow at test time and possible mitigations.
- Specific comment 6: Analyze why multi-task learning particularly benefits the temporal stream.
