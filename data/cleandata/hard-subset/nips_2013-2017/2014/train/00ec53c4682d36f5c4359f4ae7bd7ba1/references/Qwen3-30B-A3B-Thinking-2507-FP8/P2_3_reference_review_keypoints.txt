- Proposes a novel two-stream CNN that decouples spatial (single frames) and temporal (optical flow) processing, combined via late fusion.  
- Argues stacked RGB frames fail to capture motion effectively; optical flow as ConvNet input better models temporal information.  
- Technically sound with thorough experimentation and proper controls.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies on temporal inputs (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, mean subtraction, bidirectional flow).  
- Multi-task learning framework for combining UCF-101 and HMDB-51 is well-motivated and validated.  
- Experimental setup follows standard protocols and reports results with appropriate metrics.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, including missing elements of state-of-the-art shallow representations.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with helpful visual explanations and reproducible implementation details.  
- Neuroscience connection (two-stream hypothesis) is cited appropriately without overstating the analogy.  
- Could provide slightly more intuition about why optical flow outperforms stacked RGB frames (partially addressed in Section 3.2).  
- Two-stream architecture is a significant conceptual advance and a first application to deep ConvNets for action recognition.  
- Using optical flow as ConvNet input is a novel contribution distinguishing this work from prior deep-learning approaches.  
- Multi-task dataset combination is a valuable methodological contribution.  
- Demonstrates substantial empirical gains (~20% over previous best deep method) and achieves state-of-the-art results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Method is practical, adoptable by researchers, and likely to influence future video recognition research.  
- Strengths: novel architecture, comprehensive validation, multi-task learning, clear neuroscience link, and significant empirical improvements.  
- Weaknesses: needs more intuition on optical flow vs RGB, temporal stream is computationally expensive, and some shallow-method elements remain unaddressed.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong, novel, well-executed work that advances the state of the art.
