- Overall: Significant, well-executed contribution showing benefits of decoupling spatial and temporal processing; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.

- Proposal: Two-stream architecture combining a spatial ConvNet on RGB frames and a temporal ConvNet on dense optical flow, fused by averaging or linear SVM.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on ConvNet and two-stream hypotheses, improves over stacked-frame approaches by explicitly modeling motion and using ImageNet pre-training.

- Performance: Achieves state-of-the-art or competitive results on UCF101 and HMDB51, outperforming previous stacked-frame ConvNet methods.

- Decoupling benefit: Separating spatial and temporal processing enables specialized feature learning and effective use of ImageNet pre-training for the spatial stream.

- Ablations: Thorough experimental evaluation exploring temporal input configurations (stacking, trajectories, bidirectional flow, mean subtraction) and fusion methods.

- Clarity and presentation: Paper is clear, well-written, and supported by helpful diagrams.

- Implementation detail: Provides practical details on network architecture, training, optical flow computation, GPU use, and data compression to aid reproducibility.

- Multi-task learning: Introduces multi-task learning to leverage multiple datasets, addressing limited dataset sizes.

- Optical flow dependency (weakness): Reliance on pre-computed optical flow is a potential computational bottleneck despite compression efforts.

- Limited stream interaction (weakness): Fusion is primarily late-stage; earlier fusion strategies or attention mechanisms are not extensively explored.

- Theoretical depth (weakness): Lacks deeper theoretical justification for why decoupling spatial and temporal streams is representationally beneficial.

- Error analysis (weakness): Missing detailed analysis of failure cases and types of actions the model struggles with.

- For acceptance: Significant empirical improvement, novel effective architecture, extensive validation, clear writing, and addresses a challenging problem.

- Against acceptance: Dependence on optical flow, limited exploration of inter-stream interactions, and insufficient theoretical grounding.

- Recommendations for authors: Discuss potential for efficient or learned motion representations; report any early fusion or attention experiments; provide qualitative error analysis; and release code/models for reproducibility.
