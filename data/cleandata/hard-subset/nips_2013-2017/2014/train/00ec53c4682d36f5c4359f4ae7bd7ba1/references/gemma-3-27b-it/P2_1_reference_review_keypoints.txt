- Proposes a two-stream CNN separating spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked optical flow) streams, fused for action classification.  
- Demonstrates strong performance on UCF101 and HMDB51, outperforming prior deep-learning baselines and matching hand-crafted features.  
- Spatial stream benefits significantly from ImageNet pre-training; temporal stream improved via multi-task learning across datasets.  
- Decoupling spatial and temporal information allows specialized optimization and pre-training strategies.  
- Thorough ablation studies on temporal inputs (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bidirectional flow, mean subtraction) and fusion methods.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with helpful figures and detailed experimental setup.  
- Reliance on pre-computed optical flow adds computational cost and depends on flow quality.  
- Limited exploration of more sophisticated fusion strategies (e.g., attention mechanisms) beyond SVM/averaging.  
- Paper predates and does not compare to later advances (3D CNNs, Transformers), reducing its contemporary significance.  
- Biological ventral/dorsal stream analogy is mentioned but not deeply explored.  
- Technically sound but could better discuss limitations and field developments since publication.  
- Original and influential at the time, though some related ideas existed previously and later work diminished novelty.  
- Highly influential and foundational for many subsequent video-understanding methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, strong empirical results, effective pre-training and multi-tasking, clarity, historical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: diminished current relevance, dependency on optical flow, limited fusion exploration.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution best accepted with a minor revision to acknowledge and contextualize subsequent advances.  
- Recommendation/rating: Accept with Minor Revision.
