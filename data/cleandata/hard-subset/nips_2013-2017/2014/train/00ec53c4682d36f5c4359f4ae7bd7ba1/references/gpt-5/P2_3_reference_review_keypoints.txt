- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet that decouples spatial appearance (RGB, ImageNet pretraining) and temporal motion (stacked dense optical flow) for video action recognition.

- Temporal stream inputs include variants: trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, and mean-flow subtraction, with late score fusion (averaging or SVM) to combine streams.

- Introduces multi-task learning with dual softmax heads to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited video labels.

- Strong ablation studies show stacked optical flow (L=10), mean subtraction, and flow stacking outperform alternatives and that two-stream fusion substantially improves accuracy.

- Temporal stream alone outperforms prior deep baselines; fused two-stream results are competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted trajectory methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.

- Well situated relative to prior work: contrasts with stacked-RGB ConvNets, connects to HOF/MBH/kinematic features, and cites two-pathway biological analogies.

- Technical choices are sound and clearly implemented; empirical protocols follow community standards.

- Limitations include reliance on precomputed Brox optical flow (not end-to-end) and no comparison of different flow algorithms or compression effects.

- Fusion is limited to score-level due to overfitting in deeper fusion attempts; no detailed analysis of why SVM fusion outperforms averaging.

- Camera motion is crudely handled via mean subtraction; no exploration of more principled global-motion compensation.

- Clarity is high with precise definitions and reproducibility-minded details, but some exact parameters (flow settings, temporal stride) and runtime/memory budgets are missing.

- Originality lies in explicitly feeding optical flow to a temporal ConvNet and the pragmatic multi-task joint training; components are known but the combination is novel and impactful.

- Significance is strong: simple, practical design with substantial empirical gains likely to influence video recognition practice.

- Weaknesses/suggestions: ablate different flow methods and compression, report feature-fusion attempts and diagnostics, per-class analysis of stream contributions, sensitivity to sampling/stride, and test-time computational costs.

- Experiments are limited to UCF-101 and HMDB-51; lacking large-scale validation (e.g., Sports-1M) to confirm broader generalization.

- Recommendation: Accept — well-executed, insightful, and influential work with minor additional experiments recommended.
