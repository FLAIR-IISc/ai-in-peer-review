- Summary: Proposes a two-stream CNN that separates spatial (RGB frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) processing and fuses outputs for action recognition.  
- Key idea: Decouples spatial and temporal information to leverage ImageNet pre-training and capture motion via optical flow.  
- Motivation: Addresses limitations of 3D convolutions and naive frame-stacking for video recognition.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions well between hand-crafted feature methods and recent deep-learning attempts, inspired by the two-stream neuroscience hypothesis.  
- Novelty: Two-stream architecture is a well-motivated, effective design enabling specialized processing and pre-training.  
- Empirical results: Shows substantial improvements on UCF101 and HMDB51, competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted features.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, explains architecture, training, and experiments clearly with helpful diagrams.  
- Ablation studies: Thoroughly evaluates impacts of stacked flow frames, mean flow subtraction, bidirectional flow, etc.  
- Multi-task learning: Uses multi-task training to improve generalization given limited dataset sizes.  
- Reproducibility: Provides detailed implementation and training parameters, including optical flow computation.  
- Optical flow dependency: Reliance on dense optical flow incurs computational cost and potential errors, limiting real-time applicability.  
- Fusion exploration: Fusion methods are limited to averaging and SVMs; more advanced fusion strategies are not evaluated.  
- Dataset bias: Acknowledges dataset limitations but lacks analysis of potential dataset biases and their effects.  
- Neuroscience link: Reference to the two-stream hypothesis is superficial and not deeply integrated.  
- Minor issues: Small textual inconsistencies (e.g., figure referencing) need correction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective approach with strong results, clear presentation, and solid ablations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Optical flow dependency, limited fusion exploration, and superficial neuroscience justification.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant contributions; weaknesses are minor and addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to discuss optical flow cost, explore fusion briefly, address dataset bias, and fix textual inconsistencies.
