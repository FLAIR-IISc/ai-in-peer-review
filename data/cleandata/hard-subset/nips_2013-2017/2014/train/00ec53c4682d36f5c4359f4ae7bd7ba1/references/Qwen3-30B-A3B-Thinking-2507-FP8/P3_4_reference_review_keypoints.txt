- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture that decouples spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) processing for video action recognition.
- Spatial stream is ImageNet-pretrained; temporal stream processes dense optical flow (10-frame stacking).
- Key contribution: explicit optical flow input significantly outperforms end-to-end spatio-temporal feature learning.
- Key contribution: introduces multi-task learning to combine limited video datasets effectively.
- Key contribution: establishes a strong deep learning baseline for video action recognition.
- Major strength: decoupling spatial and temporal processing is more effective than learning both from raw frames.
- Ablation studies show the optical-flow temporal stream outperforms single-frame spatial stream by ~20% and prior deep methods by ~25%.
- Multi-task learning improves HMDB-51 performance by ~3.5% over single-dataset training.
- Experimental design is robust with thorough ablations (flow stacking, trajectory vs. flow, bi-directional flow) and clear comparisons to shallow and prior deep methods.
- Results: UCF-101 81.5% and HMDB-51 57.4%, competitive with top shallow methods.
- Paper provides insight into why prior deep video methods failed: end-to-end spatio-temporal learning is challenging.
- Major limitation: reliance on precomputed optical flow is computationally and storage intensive (e.g., 1.5TB before compression).
- Limitation: camera motion compensation is only handled via simple mean flow subtraction, not sophisticated global compensation.
- Limitation: lacks direct comparison to the very recent shallow method reporting 70.4% on UCF-101.
- Limitation: does not explore integration with 3D CNNs or more advanced fusion mechanisms for future work.
- Overall assessment: well-executed, meaningful contribution that advances deep learning baselines for video; recommended for acceptance.
