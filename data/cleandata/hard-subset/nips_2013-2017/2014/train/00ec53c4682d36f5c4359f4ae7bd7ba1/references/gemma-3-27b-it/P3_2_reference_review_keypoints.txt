- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed contribution; two-stream CNN is effective and paper is worthy of acceptance.  
- Core idea: Decouple spatial (RGB) and temporal (optical flow) processing into separate CNN streams and fuse predictions.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends image ConvNets to video, contrasts with single-stack-frame CNNs and trajectory-based hand-crafted methods.  
- Neuroscience link: Mentions ventral/dorsal two-stream hypothesis but does not explore it deeply.  
- Effective architecture: Two-stream design is simple, allows specialization, and captures complementary information.  
- Strong performance: Competitive or state-of-the-art results on UCF101 and HMDB51, improving over prior deep approaches.  
- Thorough experimentation: Extensive analysis of design choices, input representations, and fusion methods.  
- Multi-task learning: Uses multi-dataset training to improve generalization on limited video data.  
- Clear presentation: Well-written, organized, and supported by informative figures/tables.  
- Pre-training strategy: ImageNet pre-training for spatial stream is crucial and effectively used.  
- Optical flow dependency: Reliance on pre-computed flow is a bottleneck; integration into the network is suggested for future work.  
- Fusion limitations: Limited exploration of fusion strategies and weak rationale for chosen final fusion method.  
- Network depth ablation missing: Insufficient exploration of varying network depths for each stream.  
- Failure analysis lacking: Needs more detailed examination of action classes where the model fails.  
- Originality: Not entirely novel conceptually, but novel in applying deep ConvNets with optical flow and combined training techniques.  
- Significance: Provides a strong, practical baseline likely to influence future video action recognition work.  
- Pros: Strong results, clear writing, thorough analysis, practical solution, well-positioned in literature.  
- Cons: Dependence on pre-computed optical flow, limited fusion exploration, superficial neuroscience connection.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
