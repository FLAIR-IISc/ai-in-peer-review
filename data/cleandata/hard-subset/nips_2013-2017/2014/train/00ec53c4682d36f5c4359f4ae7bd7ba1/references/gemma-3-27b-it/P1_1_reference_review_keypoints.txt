- Overall: Two-stream CNN (spatial + temporal on optical flow) is well-executed, effective, and merits acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary & Related Work: Proposes decoupling spatial and temporal info with separate CNNs, fusing outputs; leverages ImageNet pretraining and situates work among hand-crafted and prior CNN approaches.  
- Related Work Coverage: Comprehensive and current, correctly identifies limits of stacked-frame CNNs and cites two-stream hypothesis inspiration.  
- Strength — Strong Empirical Results: Substantially improves over previous deep-learning methods on UCF101 and HMDB51 and competes with hand-crafted features.  
- Strength — Effective Architecture: Separating spatial and temporal streams with optical flow for motion is well-motivated and effective.  
- Strength — Pre-training: ImageNet pretraining for spatial stream is a sensible choice for small action datasets.  
- Strength — Multi-task Learning: Multi-task training for the temporal stream improves generalization by combining datasets.  
- Strength — Clear Writing: Paper is well-structured, clearly explained, and easy to follow.  
- Strength — Ablation Studies: Thorough ablations (flow stacking, mean subtraction, fusion) provide useful component insights.  
- Weakness — Optical Flow Reliance: Performance depends heavily on pre-computed optical flow; robustness to noisy flow is not thoroughly studied.  
- Weakness — Limited Fusion Exploration: Fusion methods beyond SVM or averaging are under-explored; attention or advanced fusion might help.  
- Weakness — Computational Cost: Computing and storing optical flow is expensive; discussion of scalability is limited.  
- Weakness — Limited Theoretical Analysis: Paper is primarily empirical with little theoretical justification for the two-stream design.  
- Weakness — Incremental Novelty: Combines established techniques; novelty is incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Qualitative Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.  
- For Acceptance: Strong benchmark performance, well-motivated architecture, clear presentation, thorough experiments, and useful multi-task approach.  
- Against Acceptance: Incremental novelty, reliance on accurate optical flow, and limited fusion/robustness analysis.  
- Recommendation — Optical Flow Robustness: Add discussion or experiments on sensitivity to corrupted/inaccurate flow.  
- Recommendation — Fusion Exploration: Explore or justify fusion choices and consider more sophisticated fusion methods.  
- Recommendation — Computational Cost: Provide detailed analysis of flow computation/storage costs and potential mitigation strategies.  
- Recommendation — Minor Clarification: Clarify the meaning of “generalised” in describing how the temporal ConvNet relates to hand-crafted features.
