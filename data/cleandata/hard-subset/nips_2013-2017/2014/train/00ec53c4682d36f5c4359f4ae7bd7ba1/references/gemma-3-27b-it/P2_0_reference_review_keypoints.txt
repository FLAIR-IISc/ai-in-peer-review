- Paper proposes a two-stream CNN architecture separating spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked optical flow) processing for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream is pre-trained on ImageNet and combined with a temporal stream to improve performance on UCF101 and HMDB51.  
- Significant empirical improvement over previous deep learning baselines and competitive results with hand-crafted feature methods.  
- Well-designed ablation studies (e.g., temporal input variants, mean subtraction) provide useful insights.  
- The decoupling of spatial and temporal streams is a novel and influential contribution linking deep learning to ventral/dorsal stream ideas.  
- The paper is clear, well-organized, and includes comprehensive related work.  
- Sufficient implementation and training details are provided to aid reproducibility, including multi-GPU discussion.  
- Analysis highlights the complementary nature of spatial and temporal streams and connects to human visual processing.  
- Major weakness: reliance on pre-computed optical flow, which adds computational cost and potential error sources.  
- Limited exploration of fusion strategies; learned fusion within the network is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Lack of ablation studies on network depth and alternative architectural choices.  
- Insufficient discussion of failure cases and types of actions the method struggles with.  
- Overall judged technically sound, original, and significant with lasting impact on video action recognition.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept — strengths outweigh minor limitations.
