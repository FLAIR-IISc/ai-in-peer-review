- Proposes a two-stream CNN separating spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked optical flow) processing, fused for action classification.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on UCF101 and HMDB51, improving over prior deep-learning approaches.
- Motivated by neuroscience two-stream hypothesis and generalizes prior hand-crafted feature pipelines (dense trajectories, optical flow).
- Uses pre-training on ImageNet to boost performance via transfer learning.
- Employs multi-task learning to mitigate limited video dataset sizes.
- Thorough ablation studies showing benefits of design choices (stacked flow, mean subtraction).
- Links learned CNN layers to traditional hand-crafted features, providing interpretability.
- Novel architecture that effectively leverages complementary appearance and motion information.
- Well-written, clear exposition and logically organized presentation.
- Strong empirical validation with complete experimental evaluation.
- Temporal stream heavily depends on optical flow quality and incurs computational cost.
- Paper lacks a deep analysis of sensitivity to optical flow errors.
- Limited discussion of overall computational complexity and runtime implications.
- Fusion approach (averaging or SVM) appears ad hoc; more advanced fusion methods not explored.
- Insufficient detailed analysis/visualization of what temporal convolutional filters learn.
- No comparison or discussion relating to more recent video models (3D CNNs, Transformers).
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution to video action recognition.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, optical flow limitations, and relation to recent advances.
