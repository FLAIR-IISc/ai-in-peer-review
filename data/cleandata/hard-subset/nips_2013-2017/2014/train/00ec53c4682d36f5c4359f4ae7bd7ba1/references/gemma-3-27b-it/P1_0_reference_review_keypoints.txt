- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture separating spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked optical flow) streams, fused for action classification.
- Demonstrates significant improvement over prior deep-learning methods and competitive results with hand-crafted features on UCF101 and HMDB51.
- Builds on prior work by addressing limitations of stacked-frame CNNs and aligning with the ventral/dorsal two-stream hypothesis.
- Uses deeper, discriminatively-trained CNNs and relates temporal-stream representations to trajectory-based features like HOF and MBH.
- Novel, simple, and effective architectural idea that explicitly models spatial and temporal information.
- Strong empirical results supported by benchmarks, showing clear advantage over previous approaches.
- Effective use of ImageNet pre-training for the spatial stream to mitigate limited video training data.
- Thorough ablation studies exploring input configurations, training techniques, and multi-task learning.
- Well-written, well-organized paper with clear motivation, architecture description, and helpful diagrams.
- Biologically plausible connection to neuroscience via ventral/dorsal stream analogy.
- Heavy reliance on optical flow quality; noisy or inaccurate flow could limit performance and robustness.
- Computational cost and dependence on optical flow computation are not thoroughly analyzed.
- Lacks visualizations and deeper analysis of learned features to substantiate claims of matching hand-crafted descriptors.
- Fusion approach (SVM-based) performs better but lacks explanation of why it outperforms alternatives.
- Future work suggestions are conservative and do not propose major departures from the current approach.
- Technically sound with well-supported claims; considered a significant and influential contribution to video understanding.
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial performance gains, strong evaluation, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on accurate optical flow and limited feature analysis.
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommended for acceptance.
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
