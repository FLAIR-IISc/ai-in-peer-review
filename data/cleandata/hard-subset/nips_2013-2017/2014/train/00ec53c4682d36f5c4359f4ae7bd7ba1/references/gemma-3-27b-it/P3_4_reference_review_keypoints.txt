- Overall: Two-stream ConvNet (spatial + temporal) is a significant, well-written contribution that provides a strong baseline for video action recognition; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Summary: Proposes separate spatial (frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) ConvNets, fused for classification, with ImageNet pre-training for the spatial stream.
- Related work: Thorough comparison to hand-crafted features and prior deep/video methods, correctly positioning improvements over stacked frames and shallow two-stream models.
- Strength — Effective architecture: Decoupling spatial and temporal information enables specialized learning and use of large image datasets.
- Strength — Strong performance: Outperforms stacked-frame methods and is competitive with hand-crafted features on UCF101 and HMDB51.
- Strength — Detailed experiments: Comprehensive ablation studies on temporal inputs, fusion strategies, and implementation choices.
- Strength — Clear presentation: Paper is well-written, well-organized, with informative figures and tables.
- Strength — Practical details: Addresses training procedures, GPU usage, and optical flow computation.
- Strength — Multi-task learning: Using multi-task learning helps mitigate overfitting on limited video data.
- Weakness — Optical flow dependence: Performance relies heavily on dense optical flow, with limited discussion of its failure modes.
- Weakness — Limited datasets: Evaluation on relatively small datasets (UCF101, HMDB51) leaves generalization to larger datasets uncertain.
- Weakness — Fusion strategy: Fusion is limited to averaging and SVM; more advanced learned or attention-based fusion not explored.
- Weakness — Lack of qualitative analysis: No visualizations of learned temporal filters or examples of misclassifications to interpret model behavior.
- Weakness — Missing 3D CNN comparison: No comparison or discussion of end-to-end 3D CNN approaches.
- Originality & significance: Novel combination of ConvNets and optical flow in a two-stream design yields a valuable, well-engineered baseline though components themselves are not individually novel.
- Pro acceptance: Strong results, clear writing, thorough ablations, practical solution, and use of multi-task learning.
- Con acceptance: Reliance on optical flow, small-scale evaluation, and relatively basic fusion methods.
- Suggestions/questions: Discuss optical flow limitations, scalability to large datasets (e.g., Sports-1M), explore advanced fusion strategies, add qualitative visualizations, and compare or discuss 3D CNNs.
