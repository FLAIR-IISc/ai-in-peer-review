- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition that decouples appearance (RGB) and motion (stacked dense optical flow).  
- Spatial stream: ImageNet-pretrained ConvNet fine-tuned on RGB frames; temporal stream: ConvNet on stacked optical flow fields.  
- Explores temporal input variants: forward stacking, trajectory-aligned stacking, bidirectional flow, and mean-flow subtraction for camera motion.  
- Introduces a simple multi-task learning setup with dataset-specific softmax heads to combine UCF-101 and HMDB-51 without manual label deduplication.  
- Fuses streams at score level via averaging or a linear SVM, with SVM fusion consistently outperforming averaging.  
- Empirical findings: temporal stream outperforms spatial when trained on same data; fusion yields substantial improvement.  
- Optical-flow-based temporal ConvNets outperform stacked-RGB ConvNets for motion modeling.  
- Multi-task training improves performance on small datasets (notably HMDB) and slightly on UCF.  
- Methodology is sound with thorough ablations validating design choices (stacking L=10, mean subtraction, slight gains from bidirectional flow).  
- Training/testing protocols, augmentations, schedules, and architectures are described in reproducible detail.  
- Comparison baselines include strong prior deep methods and standard shallow pipelines; results competitive with trajectory-based methods.  
- Limitation: not end-to-end—requires precomputed optical flow, posing computational and inference-time costs.  
- Runtime/compute analysis at inference is lacking despite practical implementation details provided (Brox flow, compression, multi-GPU).  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and defines temporal inputs precisely with helpful figures and equations.  
- Originality lies in the clean two-stream design, feeding stacked dense optical flow to a ConvNet, and the pragmatic multi-task formulation.  
- Mean-flow subtraction is a useful adaptation for camera-motion mitigation but camera-motion handling remains limited.  
- Significance: narrows gap between deep and trajectory-based shallow pipelines and provides actionable insight that optical flow is superior to stacked RGB for motion.  
- Strengths: clear architecture, thorough ablations, strong empirical gains, practical multi-tasking, reproducible engineering, and insightful analysis.  
- Weaknesses: computational bottleneck from precomputed flow, limited camera-motion compensation, lack of trajectory-aligned pooling integration, small-dataset evaluation, and missing precise hyperparameters/error bars.  
- Pros for acceptance: significant, reproducible empirical advance with simple, adoptable design and careful evaluation.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on precomputed flow (not end-to-end), incremental components whose novelty is combinatorial, and limited analysis of inference scalability.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (high) — technically solid, clearly presented, and likely influential despite pragmatic trade-offs.
