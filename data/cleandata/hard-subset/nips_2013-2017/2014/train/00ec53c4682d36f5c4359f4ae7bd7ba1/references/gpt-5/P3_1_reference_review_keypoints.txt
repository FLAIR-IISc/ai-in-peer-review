- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video action recognition: an ImageNet-pretrained spatial stream on RGB and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow.  
- Temporal stream variants include forward stacking, trajectory-based stacking, bi-directional flow, and mean flow subtraction to compensate global motion.  
- Streams are fused by late score fusion (averaging or linear SVM) and temporal net uses multi-task learning with separate softmax heads for UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Experiments show temporal ConvNets on stacked flow outperform spatial and RGB multi-frame ConvNets, and spatial + temporal streams are complementary.  
- Multi-task training improves generalization, notably on the smaller HMDB-51 dataset.  
- The best two-stream system is competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted pipelines while using modest training data.  
- Situates work relative to hand-crafted trajectory pipelines, deep video models, Sports-1M CNNs, and dorsal/ventral inspiration; main novelty is explicit optical-flow input to ConvNets.  
- Demonstrates that explicit optical flow is more effective than learning motion from stacked RGB frames (contra Karpathy et al.).  
- Methodology is sound with thoughtful ablations on stacking length, stacking method, mean flow subtraction, flow direction, and RGB baselines.  
- Implementation details and protocols are sufficient for reproduction and evaluations follow standard three-split protocols.  
- Limitations: only Brox flow is evaluated; no analysis of flow quality, parameters, or compression impact.  
- Architecture exploration is limited (temporal net is shallow CNN-M-like) with little analysis of capacity vs overfitting beyond dropout.  
- Trajectory-stacking underperforms but is underexplored (e.g., trajectory length, stride, normalization).  
- Lacks timing/throughput analysis and an explicit accuracy–compute trade-off evaluation beyond flow precomputation.  
- Paper is clearly written; however, clarifications needed on Brox parameters, shot-change handling, validation use for hyperparameters, and bi-directional stacking details.  
- The composition of known components into a two-stream, flow-based ConvNet with multi-task training is novel and influential.  
- Significance is high: closes gap between deep and hand-crafted methods and establishes a canonical baseline, but reliance on precomputed flow limits end-to-end learning and adds cost.  
- Strengths: simple reproducible architecture, strong ablations, effective ImageNet pretraining and multi-tasking, competitive results, and careful implementation.  
- Weaknesses/questions: dependence on precomputed flow, underexplored deeper architectures and fusion methods, lack of trajectory-aware pooling integration, no per-class or robustness analysis, and unclear impact of JPEG-compression on flow.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate alternative/faster flow methods and accuracy–compute trade-offs, explore deeper temporal nets and regularization, investigate mid-level/gated fusion and trajectory-aware pooling, analyze failure modes and class-wise performance, and consider end-to-end learned flow representations.  
- Recommendation: Accept (strong) — well-executed, novel, and likely to influence future work.
