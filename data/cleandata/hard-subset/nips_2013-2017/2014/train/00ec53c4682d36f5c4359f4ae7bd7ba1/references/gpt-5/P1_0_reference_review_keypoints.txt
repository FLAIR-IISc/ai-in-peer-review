- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial per-frame (ImageNet pretrained) and temporal on stacked dense optical flow, trained separately and fused late.  
- Temporal net trained from scratch with heavy dropout and multi-task learning across UCF101/HMDB-51.  
- Thoroughly studies temporal input designs: stacked L flows, trajectory-aligned stacking, bi-directional flow, and mean flow subtraction.  
- Best temporal input: stacked flow with mean subtraction (L=10); temporal > spatial; fusion yields substantial gains.  
- Key novelty: feeding explicit optical flow into a ConvNet as a first-class input, bridging hand-crafted motion features and deep models.  
- Outperforms prior deep RGB-based models and is competitive with improved dense trajectories (IDT).  
- Methodology is sound with clear architectures, training procedures, and ablations isolating important factors.  
- Multi-task learning with two softmax heads is a simple, effective way to leverage small video datasets.  
- Experimental protocol follows community standards and compares relevant baselines (single-frame, slow-fusion RGB, IDT).  
- Missing empirical analyses: computational cost/throughput of temporal stream and optical-flow precomputation, and sensitivity to flow algorithm/parameters and JPEG compression.  
- Paper is well organized, readable, and provides helpful implementation details aiding reproducibility.  
- Minor clarity gaps: exact optical flow parameter settings, how UCF/HMDB samples are batched and losses weighted, runtime/storage, and why the second LRN layer was removed in the temporal net.  
- Originality is moderate: two-stream concept known, but discriminative ConvNet on stacked flow and motion-design ablations are valuable contributions.  
- Significance: provides a strong, simple baseline for injecting motion into deep video models and yields practically useful ablation insights.  
- Strengths: clear two-stream design, careful temporal input ablations, practical regularization/data strategies, solid empirical gains, reproducibility via detailed procedures and Caffe.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on precomputed optical flow limits end-to-end learning and introduces computational/storage bottlenecks; limited analysis of speed and scalability.  
- Fusion is only at score level; learned joint fusion overfits and intermediate fusion approaches were not explored.  
- Does not integrate trajectory-centric pooling (important for IDT) and uses a relatively short temporal horizon (L=10) without long-range aggregation methods.  
- Statistical reporting could be stronger (confidence intervals, per-class breakdowns).  
- Suggestions: report runtime/storage, evaluate different flow methods and compression effects, try lightweight learned fusion with stronger regularization, add trajectory-aware pooling, investigate longer temporal aggregation, and clarify multi-task batching/loss weighting.  
- Acceptance arguments: strong, simple idea with clear empirical gains, thorough ablations, reproducible methodology, and practical impact.  
- Rejection arguments: not end-to-end, heavy reliance on precomputed flow, limited novelty in some components, and missing analyses on flow choice, robustness, and timing.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, impactful contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
