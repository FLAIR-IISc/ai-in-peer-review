- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommends accept with high confidence.  
- Proposal: two-stream convolutional network with spatial (frames) and temporal (stacked optical flow) streams.  
- Motivation: decouples “what” (appearance) from “how” (motion), inspired by biological two-stream hypothesis.  
- Relation to prior work: improves over prior frame-stacking deep models and shallow two-stream HMAX approaches by using deep CNNs.  
- Pre-training: effectively leverages ImageNet pre-training for the spatial stream.  
- Temporal representation: using optical flow for the temporal stream explicitly captures motion and is a smart design choice.  
- Performance: significantly outperforms single-stream baselines and is competitive with hand-crafted feature methods on UCF101 and HMDB51.  
- Evaluation: extensive experiments including input configurations (stacking, trajectories, bidirectional flow, mean subtraction) and fusion methods (averaging, SVM).  
- Multi-task learning: helps regularize training and improves performance.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and figures are helpful.  
- Originality: novel combination of deep CNNs with two-stream architecture and explicit optical flow use.  
- Significance: likely to be influential and provides a strong baseline for future research.  
- Computational cost: optical flow computation and storage remain a significant practical limitation.  
- Ablation studies: many presented, but justification for specific parameter choices (e.g., L=10) is limited.  
- Comparisons: lacks comparison to more recent state-of-the-art methods, making some comparisons dated.  
- Reproducibility: details of the optical flow implementation and parameter choices are insufficiently specified.  
- Acceptance arguments: strong empirical results, principled idea, thorough evaluation, and clear writing favor acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: concerns about optical flow cost, limited parameter justification, and missing newer baselines argue against acceptance.  
- Recommendation: accept and offer a presentation slot due to potential impact.
