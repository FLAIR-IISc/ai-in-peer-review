- (Summary) Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video action recognition: a spatial stream on RGB frames and a temporal stream on stacked optical flow, fused late.  
- (Summary) Shows competitive results with hand-crafted methods on UCF101 and HMDB-51 and introduces multi-task learning for limited video data.

- (Strength) Well-motivated technical contribution that effectively separates spatial and temporal processing.  
- (Strength) Comprehensive experimental evaluation including ablations on optical flow configurations and multi-task learning.  
- (Strength) Provides practical implementation details (architecture, training, flow computation) aiding reproducibility.  
- (Strength) Clearly demonstrates complementarity of spatial and temporal streams (notably ~6% gain).

- (Weakness) Lacks theoretical justification for why optical flow inputs outperform stacked RGB frames.  
- (Weakness) Limited analysis of failure modes and scenarios where motion is less discriminative or camera motion dominates.  
- (Weakness) Inadequate comparison with more recent related works and prior two-stream shallow models.  
- (Weakness) Overstates "state-of-the-art" competitiveness without precise comparisons to top hand-crafted methods.  
- (Weakness) Does not adequately address computational cost and practicality of optical flow pre-computation.

- (Technical) Unclear details in multi-task learning: how losses from two softmax layers are weighted or normalized.  
- (Technical) Claim that optical flow stacking outperforms trajectory stacking may be overstated given small margins in reported results.  
- (Technical) Does not discuss relation to recent deep learning alternatives (e.g., 3D ConvNets, spatio-temporal feature methods).  
- (Technical) No ablation study on network architecture choices (layer counts, filter sizes) to isolate architectural impact.

- (Recommendation) Add analysis/visualizations explaining why optical flow is superior to stacked RGB frames.  
- (Recommendation) Include failure-case analysis, especially for low-motion actions and high camera motion.  
- (Recommendation) Expand related work to include post-2014 deep video recognition methods and prior two-stream models.  
- (Recommendation) Provide precise performance comparisons to best hand-crafted methods with absolute metrics.  
- (Recommendation) Discuss computational cost, inference time, and memory requirements of optical flow and the whole pipeline.

- (Overall) Valuable and well-executed contribution showing deep learning can match hand-crafted video methods, but needs deeper analysis and broader comparisons.  
- (Decision) Recommend accept with minor revisions to address theoretical justification, comparisons, and failure analysis.

- (Pro) Novel two-stream architecture, thorough experiments, reproducible details, multi-task learning for data scarcity, and demonstrates deep models’ competitiveness.  
- (Con) Lacks deeper theory for design choices, insufficient comparisons to recent deep methods, limited failure analysis, and potentially overstated state-of-the-art claims.
