- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial frames + temporal optical flow) with late fusion and multi-task training on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Spatial stream uses pre-trained ImageNet ConvNet; temporal stream consumes dense optical flow rather than stacked RGB.  
- Thorough experiments and ablation studies (optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow) with reproducible implementation details.  
- Innovation: explicitly using optical flow as temporal input departs from prior stacked-frame deep approaches and generalizes hand-crafted motion features.  
- Paper is very well written with clear figures and logical presentation of methods and results.  
- Results significantly outperform previous deep-learning video methods and are competitive with state-of-the-art shallow methods.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to the very latest state-of-the-art works (e.g., Wang et al. 2014, Feichtenhofer 2015).  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of why optical flow outperforms stacked RGB frames.  
- Weakness: does not analyze failure cases to reveal current method limitations.  
- Weakness: acknowledges but does not explore practical/compute limitations of optical flow or robustness to lower-quality flow.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS; minor weaknesses are addressable in a camera-ready version.  
- Pros summary: clear, well-executed approach; comprehensive evaluation; reproducible details; strong, significant results.  
- Cons summary: limited recent comparisons; insufficient explanation of optical flow benefits; minimal failure-case analysis.
