- Overall: Proposes a two-stream CNN that decouples spatial and temporal information, achieves strong results, and is recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Summary & related work: Uses a spatial CNN on frames and a temporal CNN on dense optical flow, fuses outputs, and situates the work among hand-crafted and prior deep-learning approaches.  
- Strength — Effective architecture: Decoupling spatial and temporal streams enables dedicated optimization and captures motion cues well.  
- Strength — Strong performance: Outperforms prior deep-learning baselines and is competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted features on UCF101 and HMDB51.  
- Strength — Pre-training: ImageNet pre-training for the spatial stream is an effective strategy given limited video data.  
- Strength — Thorough evaluation: Comprehensive experiments on temporal input variants and fusion methods are provided.  
- Strength — Multi-task learning: Multi-task setup helps mitigate overfitting and improves generalization.  
- Strength — Presentation: Paper is well written, well organized, and figures clarify the approach.  
- Weakness — Optical flow dependency: Reliance on dense optical flow is computationally costly and performance hinges on flow quality.  
- Weakness — Limited fusion exploration: Only averaging and SVM fusion explored; more advanced learned fusion methods are not evaluated.  
- Weakness — Multi-task ablation lacking: No detailed ablation showing each dataset’s contribution in multi-task learning.  
- Weakness — Camera motion handling: Only mean flow subtraction used; more robust camera motion compensation could help.  
- Weakness — Computational cost omitted: Training and inference time details are not reported, limiting practical assessment.  
- Originality & significance: While components aren’t novel individually, their combination into the two-stream architecture is original, significant, and establishes a strong baseline.  
- Revision suggestions: Discuss optical-flow trade-offs and alternatives, mention advanced fusion avenues, provide multi-task ablation per dataset, report computational costs, and add qualitative error analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant empirical improvement, competitive results, solid architecture and evaluation, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Heavy reliance on expensive optical flow, limited fusion and ablation analyses.  
- Final recommendation: Strong accept with minor revisions to address the above weaknesses.
