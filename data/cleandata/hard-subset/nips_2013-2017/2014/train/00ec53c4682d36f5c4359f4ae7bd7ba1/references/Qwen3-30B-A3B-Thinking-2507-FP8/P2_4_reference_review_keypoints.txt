- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture separating spatial (RGB) and temporal (optical flow) information and fusing them for video action recognition.  
- Spatial stream uses an ImageNet-pretrained ConvNet; temporal stream processes dense optical flow to capture motion explicitly.  
- Technically sound with thorough ablation studies on optical flow configurations (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction).  
- Addresses limited video training data via multi-task learning to combine datasets.  
- Experimental protocol is rigorous, using standard datasets (UCF101, HMDB-51) with three standard splits and detailed comparisons to prior work.  
- Implementation details are thorough and reproducible, including training procedures, augmentations, and flow compression to reduce storage.  
- Writing and figures are clear and accessible, with precise mathematical descriptions of inputs and architecture.  
- Originality stems from applying the two-stream hypothesis to deep learning and using optical flow as explicit temporal input instead of stacked RGB frames.  
- Multi-task learning for dataset combination is a practical and clever solution to small-data problems.  
- Demonstrates that deep models can match or exceed hand-crafted methods and significantly outperform prior deep approaches.  
- Strong empirical performance on benchmarks and clear path for future work (e.g., larger datasets like Sports-1M).  
- Weaknesses: misses some elements of top shallow methods (trajectory-based spatial pooling), has computational cost from optical flow precomputation, and comparisons to other recent deep methods could be broader.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, significant, and well-executed paper; strong candidate for acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
