- Proposes a two-stream CNN for video action recognition: one spatial (RGB, ImageNet-pretrained) and one temporal (optical flow).  
- Demonstrates that explicitly representing motion via optical flow outperforms stacking RGB frames for learning temporal information.  
- Ablation studies show stacking multiple optical flow frames is crucial for temporal-stream performance.  
- Multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 effectively mitigates small-dataset issues for the temporal network.  
- Provides useful implementation details, including pre-computing optical flow and multi-GPU training efficiency.  
- Fails to address the scalability and computational cost of optical flow computation for large datasets like Sports-1M.  
- The neuroscience two-stream hypothesis is referenced but not deeply analyzed or connected to the learnable ConvNet findings.  
- Comparison to the state of the art is limited—omits direct comparison to Wang et al. (2013) and other recent trajectory-based methods.  
- Lacks deeper analysis explaining why optical flow succeeds and stacked RGB frames fail beyond stating implicit learning is difficult.  
- Suggests adding discussion on scaling optical flow costs, strengthening neuroscience implications, expanding state-of-the-art comparisons, and clarifying the reasons for optical flow superiority.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-evaluated, significant contribution to video understanding; recommendation: Accept.
