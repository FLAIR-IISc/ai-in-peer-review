- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial stream on RGB frames, temporal stream on optical flow) with late fusion of softmax scores.  
- Temporal stream novelty: use optical flow as explicit ConvNet input instead of learning motion from stacked RGB frames.  
- Multi-task learning framework to combine training data from UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Extensive experiments and ablation studies (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow) show consistent improvements.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance for deep methods and is competitive with hand-crafted shallow representations.  
- Well-written, clearly described architecture with sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations (no local feature pooling over spatio-temporal tubes; mean flow subtraction for camera motion).  
- Lack of theoretical justification for why optical flow input outperforms stacked RGB frames.  
- Limited comparison to more recent deep learning video recognition methods published after [14].  
- Does not discuss computational cost or practical impact of precomputing optical flow.  
- Limited exploration of fusion strategies (only averaging vs SVM; no learned fusion layers).  
- Little analysis of failure cases or where the model fails on benchmark data.  
- Recommendation: accept—significant, well-supported contribution to video action recognition.  
- Suggestions: acknowledge recent methods, discuss optical flow preprocessing cost and integration options, add failure-case examples, further explain temporal vs spatial stream performance, and analyze computational complexity.
