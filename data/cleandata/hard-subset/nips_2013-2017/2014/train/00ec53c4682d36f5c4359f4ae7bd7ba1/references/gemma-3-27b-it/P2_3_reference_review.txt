## Review of "Two-Stream Convolutional Networks for Action Recognition in Videos"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a two-stream convolutional network architecture for action recognition in videos. The core idea is to decouple spatial and temporal information processing into separate CNN streams – one operating on individual frames (spatial stream) and the other on dense optical flow (temporal stream).  The streams are then fused via averaging or a linear SVM.  The authors demonstrate that this approach significantly outperforms previous deep learning-based methods for video action recognition and achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art handcrafted features on standard benchmarks (UCF101 and HMDB51).  A key contribution is leveraging pre-training on ImageNet for the spatial stream.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  The two-stream architecture, while inspired by biological visual pathways, represents a practical and effective way to handle spatio-temporal information in video.  Decoupling the streams and leveraging optical flow as a direct input to a CNN is a clear contribution.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper provides compelling experimental evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  The improvements over previous deep learning methods ([14]) are substantial, and the competitive performance against handcrafted features is noteworthy.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the architecture and experimental setup.  The rationale behind design choices (e.g., optical flow stacking, mean subtraction) is well-articulated.
* **Multi-Task Learning:** The inclusion of a multi-task learning framework for combining data from multiple datasets is a clever and practical addition, demonstrating an understanding of the challenges associated with limited video data.
* **Thorough Evaluation:**  The authors evaluate a variety of configurations (different input representations for the temporal stream, fusion methods) and provide ablation studies to understand the contribution of each component.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Biological Inspiration:** While the paper briefly mentions the two-stream hypothesis, it doesn't delve deeper into the connection to neuroscience. This isn’t a critical flaw, but a slightly more detailed discussion could enhance the paper’s motivation.
* **Optical Flow Dependency:**  The strong performance relies heavily on accurate optical flow computation. The paper acknowledges this but doesn’t extensively discuss the potential impact of optical flow errors on the results.  Exploring robustness to imperfect flow could be valuable.
* **Complexity:** The two-stream architecture doubles the number of parameters compared to a single-stream network. While the authors demonstrate good performance, a discussion of the computational cost and potential for model compression would be beneficial.
* **Minor Clarity Issues:**  The transition between sections could be smoother in some places. For example, the motivation for different input configurations in Section 3.1 could be made more explicit.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound.  The experimental results are convincing and well-presented. The method appears complete and the authors are careful about discussing limitations (e.g., dependency on optical flow). The ablation studies demonstrate a rigorous evaluation.
* **Clarity:** The paper is mostly clear and well-organized. The explanations of the architecture and experiments are understandable. However, some minor improvements to flow and transitions could enhance readability.
* **Originality:** The two-stream architecture and the application of CNNs to optical flow stacks represent a novel contribution. While inspired by prior work on visual pathways, the specific implementation and results are new.  Related work is adequately cited.
* **Significance:**  The results are important and represent a significant advance in video action recognition. The approach is likely to be adopted by other researchers and practitioners.  It provides a strong baseline for future work in this area.

**Arguments for and against acceptance:**

**Pro Acceptance:**

* Significant performance improvements over existing deep learning methods.
* Novel and well-motivated architecture.
* Thorough experimental evaluation.
* Clear and generally well-written presentation.

**Con Acceptance:**

* Reliance on accurate optical flow computation.
* Increased model complexity.
* Limited discussion of biological inspiration.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a **strong paper** that makes a valuable contribution to the field of video action recognition. The two-stream convolutional network architecture is a clever and effective approach that achieves state-of-the-art results.  The weaknesses are relatively minor and do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.  

**Recommendation:** **Accept** with minor revisions (addressing the clarity issues and potentially expanding the discussion on robustness to optical flow errors and computational cost).
