- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture splitting spatial (RGB) and temporal (optical flow) processing for video action recognition.  
- Uses optical flow as explicit input to the temporal stream instead of learning motion implicitly from raw frames.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine training data from UCF-101 and HMDB-51 effectively.  
- Thorough ablation studies show stacking multiple flow fields (L=10) with mean subtraction is optimal for the temporal stream.  
- Fusion of spatial and temporal streams significantly outperforms each stream alone and prior deep learning approaches.  
- Achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Well-motivated by neuroscience two-stream hypothesis and practical considerations.  
- Implementation and training details (including flow computation and storage) are provided to aid reproducibility.  
- Pre-computes optical flow with a standard method rather than integrating flow estimation into end-to-end learning.  
- Limited analysis of higher-layer representations learned by the temporal stream beyond first-layer filters.  
- Comparisons focus on older hand-crafted baselines and lack benchmarks against recent deep-learning and 3D CNN approaches.  
- Paper omits discussion of the computational cost of optical flow pre-processing relative to training.  
- Brief mention of alternatives (e.g., 3D convolutions) but no empirical exploration of them.  
- Technically sound with well-designed experiments and strong ablation evidence for the proposed components.  
- Clearly written and organized, though multi-task learning details could be explained more visually.  
- Original in applying a two-stream deep CNN with optical flow input, influencing later work in video recognition.  
- Significant contribution that advances deep learning for video action recognition and establishes a lasting paradigm.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, worthy of acceptance to NIPS.  
- Suggested improvements: deeper analysis of temporal representations, comparisons to 3D CNNs, computation-cost discussion, action-level benefit analysis, and a diagram of the multi-task setup.
