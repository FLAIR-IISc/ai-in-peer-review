- Introduces a simple dual-path CNN: one pathway for RGB frames and one for stacks of dense optical flow, trained independently and fused for final video prediction; achieves (near) SOTA on two benchmarks.  
- Strength: simplicity and efficacy—combining pre-computed optical flow with a standard CNN yields competitive results without complex architectural or training changes.  
- Strength: fills a prior gap by explicitly leveraging motion; demonstrates optical flow is a highly useful complementary input modality to pixel data.  
- Weakness: uses a fixed 10-frame window, limiting the model’s ability to capture long-range temporal dynamics and multi-step or variable-duration actions.  
- Experimental quality: evaluations are sound and thorough with appropriate baselines, and results clearly validate the approach.  
- Suggested analysis: include deeper error-mode analysis to identify specific failing action classes and video conditions (e.g., low motion, occlusion, complex backgrounds).  
- Originality/significance: novel and impactful integration of optical flow into a deep learning framework, providing a strong, simple baseline for future video understanding work.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance—well-motivated, rigorous, and convincing results, with the fixed-window limitation noted as a minor issue.
