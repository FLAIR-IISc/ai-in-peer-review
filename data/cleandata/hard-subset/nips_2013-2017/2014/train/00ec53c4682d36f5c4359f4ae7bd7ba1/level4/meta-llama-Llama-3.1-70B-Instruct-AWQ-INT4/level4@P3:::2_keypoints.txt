- Introduces a novel two-stream ConvNet combining a spatial (single frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) network for action recognition.  
- Two-stream architecture significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Extends deep image ConvNets to video by adding a temporal stream and situates work within prior shallow/deep video recognition literature.  
- Main contribution: shows multi-frame dense optical flow ConvNet achieves strong performance even with limited training data.  
- Uses multitask learning across datasets to increase training data and boost performance.  
- Provides thorough evaluation of input configurations, training methods, and fusion techniques.  
- Demonstrates the spatial and temporal streams are complementary and that fusion substantially improves results.  
- Finds temporal ConvNets outperform spatial ConvNets, emphasizing the importance of motion cues.  
- Strengths: comprehensive evaluation, impressive results, clear presentation, and reproducible implementation details.  
- Weaknesses: lacks explanation for why their spatial network beats similar prior architectures.  
- Weaknesses: does not explore different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper of interest to the video classification community.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective architecture, thorough experiments, strong results, well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing analysis of spatial network superiority and limited sampling-scheme experiments.  
- Reviewer rating: 9/10; confidence: 8/10.
