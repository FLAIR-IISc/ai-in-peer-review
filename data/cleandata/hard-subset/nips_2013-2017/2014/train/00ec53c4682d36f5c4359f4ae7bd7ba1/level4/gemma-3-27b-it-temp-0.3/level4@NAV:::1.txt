This paper introduces a novel approach to discriminative tasks involving video data, leveraging a dual-stream convolutional network architecture. The model comprises two separate convolutional neural networks: one processing appearance frames and the other utilizing densely computed optical flow features. Each network is independently trained for classification, with the final video-level prediction derived from a weighted average of their individual outputs.

The proposed model’s performance is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating compelling results that achieve performance comparable to, and in some cases matching, state-of-the-art methods relying on extensively engineered hand-crafted features.

**Strengths:**

- The model presents a straightforward application of convolutional networks that yields strong performance.
- Unlike many recent deep learning approaches that prioritize learning features directly from pixel data, this work effectively incorporates optical flow (and by extension, other hand-crafted features) into a deep learning framework. This demonstrates the continued utility of optical flow as a valuable input representation, particularly for tasks like action recognition, and represents a significant contribution.

**Weaknesses:**

- The current model focuses on analyzing fixed-length segments of adjacent frames (L=10) and does not explicitly model the temporal relationships and overall sequence of actions across the entire video.

**Quality:**

The experimental setup is robust and well-executed. Enhancing the analysis with a discussion of error modes would provide further insight. Specifically, investigating whether the model exhibits particular difficulties with certain classes or types of videos could reveal valuable information about its limitations and potential areas for improvement.

**Clarity:**

The paper is well-written and the proposed model is clearly explained.

**Originality:**

The application of a convolutional neural network to optical flow features is a novel contribution.

**Significance:**

This work has the potential to significantly influence research concerning the use of video and motion information in computer vision. The model’s simplicity, coupled with its promising results, and the novel integration of optical flow features with convolutional networks, make it a noteworthy advancement in the field.
