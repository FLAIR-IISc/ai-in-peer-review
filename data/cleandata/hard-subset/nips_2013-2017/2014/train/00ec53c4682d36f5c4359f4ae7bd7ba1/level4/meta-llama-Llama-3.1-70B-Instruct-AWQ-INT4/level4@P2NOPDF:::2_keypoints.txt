- Proposes a novel two-stream architecture combining a spatial ConvNet on RGB frames and a temporal ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow, achieving state-of-the-art results on two standard video datasets.  
- Effectively adapts the standard ConvNet image-classification pipeline to dense optical flow inputs.  
- Thorough and comprehensive experiments exploring different ways to train a ConvNet on optical flow, demonstrating clear benefits of using optical flow.  
- Empirical results are impressive and significantly outperform other ConvNet-based action recognition methods.  
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network outperforms the similar-architecture model in [13]; deeper analysis is needed.  
- Suggestion: include comparisons of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream to evaluate behavior and robustness.  
- Overall assessment: a well-executed empirical paper of strong interest to video-classification researchers.
