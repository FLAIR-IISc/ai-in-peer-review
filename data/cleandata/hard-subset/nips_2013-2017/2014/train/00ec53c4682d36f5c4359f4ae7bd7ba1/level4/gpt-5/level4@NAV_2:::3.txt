The paper introduces new approaches to deep-learning-based video classification, specifically a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning setup. The methodology, implementation details, and procedures are described with sufficient clarity and specificity. The manuscript is clearly written and makes a persuasive case for the proposed methods through solid empirical results.

The contributions are novel. Although the work extends the framework of [13], the two-stream design is particularly compelling because it demonstrates that explicitly leveraging optical flow yields substantial gains—an outcome that will interest many researchers. While the exposition is clear, the narrative feels distributed across several contributions rather than centered on a single overarching theme. The results show substantial improvements over [13] and are competitive with the current state of the art.

Suggestions for improvement:
1. Because the main impact centers on the two-stream architecture and the integration of optical flow, the abstract and introduction could adopt a more focused narrative around this theme.
2. Missing citations in deep learning:
- Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
Missing citations in computer vision:
- Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC 2010.

Overall, the paper offers strong practical contributions and presents ideas that usefully complement core deep learning methods. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, conditional on addressing the above suggestions.
