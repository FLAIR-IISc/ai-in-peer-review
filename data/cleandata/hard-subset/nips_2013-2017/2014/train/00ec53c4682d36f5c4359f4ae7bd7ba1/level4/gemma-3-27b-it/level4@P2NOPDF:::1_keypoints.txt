- Two-stream model: separate CNNs for appearance (frames) and motion (dense optical flow), trained independently and fused by averaging (optionally with learned weights).
- Achieves competitive or matching state-of-the-art results on two datasets with a relatively simple architecture.
- Reinforces the usefulness of optical flow as an effective input representation for action recognition within a deep-learning framework.
- Simplicity of the approach makes it easy to understand and adapt to other video-based tasks.
- Novelty in applying convolutional networks directly to densely computed optical flow together with appearance cues.
- Major limitation: limited temporal scope—operates on fixed-length segments (L=10) and does not model long-range temporal dependencies.
- Experimental setup is well-defined and provides strong evidence for model effectiveness.
- Recommends adding a detailed failure-case analysis to identify classes or video characteristics (e.g., fast motion, clutter) where the model struggles.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with concise explanations of model and implementation.
- Potential significance: could influence future video understanding research by effectively combining deep learning and traditional motion cues.
