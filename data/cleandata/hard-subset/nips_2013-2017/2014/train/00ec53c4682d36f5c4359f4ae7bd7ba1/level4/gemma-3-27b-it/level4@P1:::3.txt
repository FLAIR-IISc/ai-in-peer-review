The paper proposes novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are described in detail. The paper is clearly written and presents a compelling case for the proposed methods, demonstrating strong results on standard benchmarks.

These methods represent a worthwhile contribution. While building upon the work of Karpathy et al. [14], the two-stream framework is particularly interesting, as it highlights the significant performance gains achieved by incorporating an optical flow component. This is a result likely to be of broad interest to the research community. The paper is well-presented, though it covers multiple contributions which slightly dilutes the central theme. Importantly, the results demonstrate substantial improvements over [14] and achieve performance competitive with state-of-the-art methods.

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest the following:

1.  The abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused theme, emphasizing the primary contribution—the two-stream network and the benefits of incorporating optical flow. Currently, the scope feels somewhat broad.
2.  The paper would benefit from acknowledging several relevant prior works. Specifically, Le et al. [18] ("Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis", CVPR 2011) is a relevant work in deep learning for video. Wang et al. [27] (“Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition”, BMVC, 2010) is a key paper on spatio-temporal feature evaluation.
3. The discussion of related work could be more thoroughly integrated into the introduction, clearly positioning the current work within the existing landscape and highlighting its specific novelty.

Arguments for acceptance:

*   **Strong performance:** The proposed two-stream network achieves competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, surpassing the performance of [14] and approaching state-of-the-art hand-crafted features.
*   **Novelty:** The two-stream architecture, leveraging both spatial and temporal streams, is a significant contribution. The effective use of optical flow as input to a ConvNet is also noteworthy.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and the methodology is clearly explained, facilitating understanding and potential reproduction of the results.
*   **Thorough evaluation:** The authors provide a comprehensive experimental evaluation, including ablation studies and comparisons to existing methods.

Arguments against acceptance:

*   **Multiple contributions:** The paper addresses several contributions (two-stream architecture, multi-task learning, input configurations), which slightly weakens the overall focus.
*   **Missing citations:** Some relevant prior work is not cited, potentially overlooking important context and related research.

Overall, this paper offers a significant practical contribution with several interesting ideas that complement current work in deep learning for video understanding. I recommend accepting the paper to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements regarding focus and citation of relevant work.
