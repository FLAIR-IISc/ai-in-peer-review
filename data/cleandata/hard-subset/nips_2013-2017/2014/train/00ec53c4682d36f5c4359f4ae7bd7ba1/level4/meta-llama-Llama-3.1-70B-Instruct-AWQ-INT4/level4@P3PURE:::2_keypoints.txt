- Proposes a novel neural architecture combining a single-frame (spatial) ConvNet with a short-sequence dense optical flow (temporal) ConvNet for action classification.  
- Achieves significant improvement over other ConvNet-based methods and attains state-of-the-art results on two standard video datasets.  
- Applies standard image ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow frames and integrates it with a spatial stream.  
- Experiments are thorough and results are empirically strong and convincing.  
- Evaluates several training methods for optical-flow ConvNets, demonstrating clear advantages of using optical flow input.  
- Lacks a clear explanation for why the spatial network substantially outperforms the similar-architecture models in [13].  
- Would benefit from comparisons of different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream to better understand performance factors.  
- Overall, a solid empirical contribution of interest to researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
