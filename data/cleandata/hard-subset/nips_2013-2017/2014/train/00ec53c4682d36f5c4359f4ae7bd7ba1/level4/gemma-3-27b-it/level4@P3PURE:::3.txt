The paper proposes novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly. The paper is clearly written and convincingly demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods through strong experimental results.

The presented work is novel. While building upon the framework of [13], the two-stream approach is particularly compelling, demonstrating a significant performance improvement through the incorporation of optical flow. This finding will be of broad interest to the research community. The paper is well-organized; however, it feels somewhat fragmented across multiple contributions rather than centered around a single, unifying theme. The results represent substantial improvements over [13] and are competitive with current state-of-the-art approaches.

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest the following:

1. Given that the primary significance appears to lie in the two-stream network and the integration of optical flow, the abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused narrative.
2. The following relevant citations are missing and should be included: “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis” (CVPR 2011) and “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition” (BMVC, 2010).

Overall, this paper offers significant practical contributions and introduces interesting ideas that complement existing work in deep learning. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
