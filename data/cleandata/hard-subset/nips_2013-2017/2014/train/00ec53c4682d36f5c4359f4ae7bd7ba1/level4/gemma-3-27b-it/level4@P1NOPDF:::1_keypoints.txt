- Proposes a two-stream model: one CNN on an appearance frame and another CNN on stacked dense optical flow; networks trained independently and fused by weighted averaging for video-level prediction.
- Evaluated on two datasets and achieves results matching or close to state-of-the-art methods using hand-crafted features.
- Strength: simple, straightforward CNN application yields strong performance.
- Strength: demonstrates the value of optical flow as an input representation for action recognition.
- Weakness: operates on fixed-length segments (L=10) and does not model long-range or whole-video temporal structure.
- Experiments are well-designed; lacking an error-mode analysis to identify class-specific or video-type failure cases.
- Paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow features.
- Significance: simple, effective approach with potential to impact video/motion-based computer vision research.
