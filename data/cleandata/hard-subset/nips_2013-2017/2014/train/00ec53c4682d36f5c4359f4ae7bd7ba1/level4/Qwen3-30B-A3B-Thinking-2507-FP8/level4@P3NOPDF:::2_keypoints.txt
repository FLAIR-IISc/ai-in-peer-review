- Introduces a two-stream CNN architecture combining a spatial (frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) network for action classification.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior ConvNet-based methods.  
- Effectively adapts standard CNN pipelines to optical flow, showing flow is a strong complementary modality to frames.  
- Thorough empirical validation and comparative analysis of multiple optical flow training strategies.  
- Methodologically straightforward (two distinct streams + late fusion) but robustly executed.  
- Uses late fusion to combine spatial and temporal streams, with clear justification for its effectiveness.  
- Comprehensive experimental design: evaluates flow preprocessing, computation methods, architectures, and training protocols.  
- Ablation studies isolate component contributions and highlight the temporal stream’s importance for motion dynamics.  
- Inclusion of multiple optical flow variants and augmentation strategies adds practical depth and insights.  
- Minor concern: spatial stream substantially outperforms closely related prior work ([13]) without clear explanation—needs discussion of training/data/hyperparameter differences.  
- Minor suggestion: compare frame sampling schemes (uniform, random, motion-aware) to validate spatial-stream robustness.  
- Well-written, technically sound, empirically rigorous, and appropriately modest in claims.  
- Code/data availability not specified; releasing them would improve reproducibility and impact.  
- Overall strong empirical contribution, simple effective baseline for future work; recommended for acceptance.
