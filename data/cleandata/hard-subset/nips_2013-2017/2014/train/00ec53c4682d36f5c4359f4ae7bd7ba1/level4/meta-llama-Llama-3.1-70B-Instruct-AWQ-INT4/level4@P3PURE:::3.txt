The paper proposes several new methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically the two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation, and procedures are described in good detail, making the paper clear and well-written. The authors present a compelling case for the proposed methods by demonstrating good results.

These methods are novel, and although the paper builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream framework is particularly interesting as it shows that the optical flow component adds significant improvements. This finding is likely to be of interest to many researchers. The paper is clearly presented, but it appears to be fragmented across multiple contributions rather than having a single, cohesive theme. Nevertheless, the results offer impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.

To improve the paper, I suggest the following:

1. Given that the significance of the paper is largely concentrated on the two-stream approach and the incorporation of optical flow, the abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused theme to better reflect the main contributions of the paper.

2. The paper lacks citations to relevant deep learning and computer vision works, specifically:
- Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis (CVPR 2011)
- Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition (BMVC 2010)

The paper offers significant practical contributions and presents some interesting ideas that complement the main concepts in deep learning. I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, conditional upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
