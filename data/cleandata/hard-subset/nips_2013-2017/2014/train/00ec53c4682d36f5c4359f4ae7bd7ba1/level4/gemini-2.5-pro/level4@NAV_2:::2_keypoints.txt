- Proposes a two-stream deep architecture: a spatial ConvNet on frames for appearance and a temporal ConvNet on stacked optical flow for motion, fused for final classification.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, substantially outperforming prior ConvNet methods and competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature approaches.  
- Major contribution: demonstrating that providing optical flow to ConvNets is much more effective than learning motion from raw stacked frames, closing the gap with traditional methods.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, with thorough ablation studies for the temporal stream (number of flows, trajectory stacking, mean subtraction) that justify design choices.  
- Spatial stream shows much better performance than Karpathy et al. despite similar architectures; attributed to data augmentation but lacking detailed analysis.  
- Uses fixed sampling of 25 equally spaced frames per video; paper should assess sensitivity to different temporal sampling strategies (e.g., random sampling).  
- Fusion is simple late fusion (averaging or SVM on softmax scores); exploring earlier or more sophisticated fusion could be valuable.  
- Concept is clear and original in combining standard ConvNet classifiers with optical-flow-based inputs and relating learned temporal features to HOF/MBH.  
- Overall assessment: a well-written, high-quality, and impactful paper that advances deep learning for video action recognition.
