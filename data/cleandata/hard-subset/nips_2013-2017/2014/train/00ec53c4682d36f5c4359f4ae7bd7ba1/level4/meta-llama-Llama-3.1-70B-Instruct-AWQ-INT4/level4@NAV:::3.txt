This paper introduces several innovative methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning approach. The authors provide a thorough description of their methodology, implementation, and procedures, making the paper clear, well-written, and effectively argued. The proposed methods demonstrate promising results, showcasing their potential.

The novelty of these methods is a significant strength, building upon and improving the framework established in [13]. The two-stream framework, in particular, yields notable improvements with the incorporation of optical flow, a finding that will likely garner considerable interest among researchers. While the paper is well-presented, it appears to span multiple contributions rather than focusing on a single, cohesive theme. Nevertheless, the results exhibit impressive gains over [13] and remain competitive with state-of-the-art approaches.

To further enhance the paper, the authors may consider the following suggestions:

1. Refine the abstract and introduction to concentrate on the core theme, as the significance of the paper appears to be primarily centered around the two-stream formulation and optical flow incorporation.

2. Include the following relevant citations:
- Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis (CVPR 2011)
- Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition (BMVC 2010)

The paper offers substantial practical contributions, complemented by intriguing ideas that expand upon existing deep learning concepts. I recommend accepting the paper for NIPS 2014, pending the authors' incorporation of the suggested improvements.
