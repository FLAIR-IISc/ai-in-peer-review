- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet that separates appearance (spatial) and motion (temporal) processing with late fusion.  
- Spatial stream: standard ConvNet on individual frames to capture static appearance cues.  
- Temporal stream (main contribution): ConvNet trained on stacked dense optical flow to explicitly learn motion features.  
- Shows strong results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep methods and matching hand-crafted-feature approaches.  
- Key insight: providing optical flow as an explicit intermediate representation enables effective motion learning that raw pixel stacks failed to achieve.  
- Learning from optical flow offers a deep, learnable generalization of hand-crafted motion descriptors (e.g., HOF, MBH).  
- Strength: simple, elegant, and highly effective architecture that significantly advanced video action recognition.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation and ablation studies that justify design choices (e.g., stacked frames, mean flow subtraction).  
- Strength: clear presentation and strong contextualization with prior work.  
- Originality: novel use of dense optical flow as a primary ConvNet input for video analysis.  
- Weakness: spatial stream’s unusually high performance merits deeper analysis to isolate causes (e.g., data augmentation).  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the test-time frame sampling strategy (number and selection of frames) is not analyzed.  
- Weakness: reliance on pre-computed optical flow decouples motion estimation from end-to-end learning; integrating flow estimation is left for future work.
