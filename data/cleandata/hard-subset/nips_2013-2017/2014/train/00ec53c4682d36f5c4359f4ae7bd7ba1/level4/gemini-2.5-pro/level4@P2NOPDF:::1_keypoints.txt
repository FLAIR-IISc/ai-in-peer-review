- Summary: Introduces a two-stream ConvNet (spatial stream on RGB frames + temporal stream on stacks of optical flow) whose outputs are fused for video action recognition.  
- Performance: Achieves competitive or superior results to state-of-the-art hand-engineered methods on standard action recognition benchmarks.  
- Strength: Elegant, effective architecture that is conceptually simple and empirically strong.  
- Strength: Demonstrates value of combining deep models with classical representations by training a ConvNet on pre-computed optical flow.  
- Weakness: Limited to short-term temporal reasoning—operates on fixed-length snippets and cannot model long-range temporal dependencies or sequence ordering.  
- Weakness: Final video prediction uses simple aggregation of snippet scores, neglecting higher-level temporal structure.  
- Quality: Experimental evaluation is thorough, uses standard datasets/protocols, and supports the paper’s claims.  
- Suggestion: Would benefit from qualitative error analysis to identify failure modes (e.g., subtle motion, camera movement, clutter).  
- Clarity: Well-written and detailed enough for understanding and reproducibility.  
- Originality: Novel contribution is the two-stream formulation and training a ConvNet on optical flow rather than novel ConvNet architectures.  
- Significance: Highly significant and influential work that established a new paradigm for disentangling appearance and motion in video understanding.
