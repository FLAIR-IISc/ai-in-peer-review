- Proposes a Two-Stream Convolutional Network that decouples spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) learning into two parallel ConvNets.
- Spatial stream operates on single RGB frames and benefits from ImageNet pre-training.
- Temporal stream inputs stacked dense optical flow fields to capture motion information.
- Final predictions are fused via late fusion (softmax averaging or SVM) showing complementary strengths of both streams.
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive, state-of-the-art results and closing the gap with hand-crafted-feature methods.
- Demonstrates effective use of multi-task learning across datasets to improve generalization.
- Effective and pragmatic design: using pre-computed optical flow yields strong motion representations when video data is limited.
- Thorough experimental evaluation and ablation studies validate each component and many design choices.
- Clear, simple, and reproducible architecture that is easy to understand and build upon.
- Limitation: short fixed-length temporal window (L=10) restricts modeling of long-range or multi-phase actions.
- Limitation: reliance on pre-computed optical flow creates a two-stage pipeline with significant storage and compute overhead.
- Limitation: simple late fusion prevents deeper spatio-temporal interaction; intermediate fusion could be more powerful.
- Suggestion: include failure-case analysis to identify classes or video types where the model underperforms.
- Original and significant contribution that introduced a widely adopted paradigm and had lasting impact on video understanding.
