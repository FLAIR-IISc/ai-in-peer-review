- Proposes a two-stream convolutional model: one CNN for appearance (frames) and one for dense optical flow, trained independently and fused by averaging (optionally weighted).
- Evaluated on two datasets, showing competitive results that match or approach state-of-the-art methods using hand-crafted features.
- Strength: achieves strong performance with a straightforward CNN application.
- Strength: demonstrates the value of using optical flow as input to CNNs for action recognition.
- Weakness: operates on fixed 10-frame segments and does not model long-range temporal relationships across full videos.
- Experimental setup is robust and well-executed; recommends more error-mode analysis (class- or video-specific failure cases).
- Paper is well-written and clear in describing architecture and methods.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks directly to optical flow features.
- Significance: simple, effective approach likely to influence future video and motion-based vision research.
