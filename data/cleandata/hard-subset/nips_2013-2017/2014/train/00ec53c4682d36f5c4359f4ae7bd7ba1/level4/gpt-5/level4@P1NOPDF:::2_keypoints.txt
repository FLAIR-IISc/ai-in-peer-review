- Proposes a two-stream neural architecture fusing a spatial (single-frame) ConvNet and a temporal (dense optical flow) ConvNet for action classification.
- Temporal stream uses short sequences of dense optical flow images; spatial stream uses individual video frames.
- Method significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based action-recognition methods and matches state of the art on two standard datasets.
- Approach mainly adapts standard image-classification ConvNet pipelines to optical flow and combines them, rather than introducing radically new architectures.
- Experiments are thorough and compelling, with strong empirical results.
- Systematic comparison of several training strategies for the optical-flow ConvNet clearly demonstrates the value of using optical flow as input.
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network outperforms models in [13] given similar architectures.
- Suggested additional experiment: compare different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream inputs.
