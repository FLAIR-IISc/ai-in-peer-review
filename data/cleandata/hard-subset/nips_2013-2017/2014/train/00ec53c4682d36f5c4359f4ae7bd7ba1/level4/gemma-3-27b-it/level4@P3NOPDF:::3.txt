## Review of NIPS 2014 Submission

This paper presents novel methods for video classification leveraging deep learning, specifically a two-stream network architecture and a multi-task learning approach. The methodology is described in considerable detail, and the paper is generally clear and well-written, making a compelling case for the proposed techniques through strong empirical results.

The contributions of this work are noteworthy. While building upon the framework introduced in [13], the introduction of the two-stream network – effectively integrating optical flow as a complementary input stream – is particularly impactful. The demonstrated improvement gained by incorporating optical flow is a significant finding that will likely be of broad interest to the research community. The paper effectively demonstrates the benefit of explicitly modeling motion information alongside appearance. 

However, the paper’s strength also reveals a potential weakness. The presentation feels somewhat fragmented, encompassing multiple contributions rather than centering on a single, unifying theme. While both the two-stream network and multi-task learning are valuable additions, their combined presentation dilutes the impact of each individual contribution. 

The results presented are impressive, showing substantial improvements over the baseline in [13] and achieving competitive performance with current state-of-the-art methods. This suggests significant practical value and potential for adoption within the field.

To strengthen the paper, I offer the following suggestions:

1. **Focus and Clarity:** The primary significance of the work appears to lie in the two-stream architecture and the effective utilization of optical flow. The abstract and introduction should be restructured to more clearly emphasize this core contribution. Highlighting the limitations of relying solely on RGB data and the benefits of explicitly modeling motion would strengthen the motivation and central argument.

2. **Related Work:** The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work. Specifically, the following papers should be cited:
    *  “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis.” CVPR 2011. This work is relevant to the hierarchical feature learning aspect.
    *  “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition.” BMVC, 2010.  This paper provides a relevant baseline and context for the feature representation choices. 

These omissions are not critical, but including these references would demonstrate a more thorough understanding of the existing literature.

Overall, this paper offers significant and practical contributions to the field of video classification. The proposed techniques, particularly the two-stream network, complement existing deep learning approaches and demonstrate promising results. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements regarding focus and related work. Specifically, a more tightly focused presentation and the inclusion of the suggested citations would substantially enhance the paper's impact and clarity.
