The manuscript introduces new deep learning approaches for video classification, namely a two-stream formulation and a multi-task learning setup. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly. The paper is clearly written and supports the proposed methods with strong empirical results.

The contributions are novel. Although the work extends and improves upon the framework of [13], the two-stream framework is particularly compelling, as it demonstrates that incorporating optical flow yields substantial gains—a finding many researchers will find noteworthy. While the presentation is clear, the narrative feels spread across multiple contributions rather than centered on a single unifying theme. The results show sizable improvements over [13] and are competitive with the state of the art.

Some suggestions to improve the paper:
1. Since the main impact centers on the two-stream approach and the incorporation of optical flow, consider sharpening the focus of the abstract and introduction accordingly.
2. Missing citations in deep learning:
   Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
   Missing citations in computer vision:
   Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC, 2010.

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions, with ideas that complement core directions in deep learning. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, conditional on addressing the suggested improvements.
