This paper proposes two novel deep learning methods for video classification: a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning approach. The methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are described in sufficient detail. The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and makes a fair case for the proposed methods by demonstrating strong results.

The proposed methods are novel. Although the work extends the framework of [13], the two-stream architecture is a particularly interesting contribution. The finding that the optical flow stream provides significant performance improvements is a key result that will be of considerable interest to the research community. While the paper is clearly presented, its contributions seem somewhat divided rather than unified under a single theme. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate impressive improvements upon [13] and are competitive with the state-of-the-art.

Suggestions for improvement:
1.  The paper's primary significance appears to be the two-stream architecture and the incorporation of optical flow. The abstract and introduction could be revised to better focus on this as the central theme.
2.  The following relevant citations appear to be missing:
    *   In deep learning: Le, Q. V., et al. "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis." CVPR 2011.
    *   In computer vision: Wang, H., et al. "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition." BMVC 2010.

In summary, this paper offers significant practical contributions, with compelling ideas that complement existing work in deep learning. I recommend that the paper be accepted to NIPS 2014, conditional upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
