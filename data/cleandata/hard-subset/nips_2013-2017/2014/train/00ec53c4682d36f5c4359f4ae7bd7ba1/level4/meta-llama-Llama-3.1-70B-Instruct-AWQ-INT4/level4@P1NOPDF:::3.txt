The paper proposes several new methods for video classification using deep learning, including a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementations, and procedures are described in good detail, making the paper clear and well-written. The authors present a compelling case for the proposed methods by demonstrating good results.

These methods are novel, and although the paper builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream framework is particularly interesting as it shows that the optical flow component significantly improves performance. This result is likely to be of interest to many researchers. The paper is well-presented, but its contributions seem somewhat fragmented, lacking a single unifying theme. Nevertheless, the results offer impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:

1. The abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused theme, as the majority of the significance is concentrated on the two-stream approach and the incorporation of optical flow.
2. The paper is missing citations to relevant deep learning work, including "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis" (CVPR 2011).
3. Additionally, the paper lacks citations to relevant computer vision work, such as "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition" (BMVC 2010).

The paper offers significant practical contributions, with some interesting ideas that complement the main ideas in deep learning. I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, conditional upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
