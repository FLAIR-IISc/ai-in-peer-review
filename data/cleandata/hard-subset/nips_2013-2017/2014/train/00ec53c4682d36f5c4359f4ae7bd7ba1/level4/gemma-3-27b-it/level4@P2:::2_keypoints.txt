- Introduces a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture that separately models spatial appearance (frames) and temporal motion (dense optical flow).  
- Leverages complementary information by training separate spatial and temporal ConvNets and fusing their predictions.  
- Demonstrates significant performance improvements and competitive state-of-the-art results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Builds on prior ConvNet and video work but distinguishes itself by decoupling spatial and temporal processing.  
- Uses multi-task learning to improve training data availability and generalization.  
- Strong empirical results with clear, well-written presentation.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation including informative ablation studies on temporal stream input configurations.  
- Consistent gains from using dense optical flow and the two-stream fusion validate the approach.  
- Spatial network outperforms prior models, but the paper lacks in-depth analysis of architectural/training differences causing this gap.  
- Does not address computational cost or runtime implications of computing dense optical flow.  
- Fusion methods are limited (averaging and SVM); exploring alternative fusion strategies could yield further gains.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — well-executed, impactful contribution to video action recognition.
