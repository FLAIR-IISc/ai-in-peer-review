- Proposes a two-stream CNN for video: one pathway for an appearance frame and one for a stack of dense optical flow, fused by a weighted average of predictions.  
- Evaluated on two datasets, achieving results that match or approach state-of-the-art methods relying on hand-crafted features.  
- Main strengths: model simplicity and effectiveness.  
- Demonstrates the utility of optical flow fields as an input representation for action recognition.  
- Novel contribution: applying convolutional nets to optical flow features.  
- Limitation: cannot model long-range or full-video action sequences, only fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames.  
- Experiments are well-designed and provide a solid evaluation framework.  
- Suggested improvement: include an error-mode analysis (e.g., classes or video types the model fails on).  
- Paper is clearly written and potentially impactful for video/motion research.
