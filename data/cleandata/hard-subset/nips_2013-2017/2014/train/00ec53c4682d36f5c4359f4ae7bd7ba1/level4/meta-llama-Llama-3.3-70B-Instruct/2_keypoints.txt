- Introduces a novel two-stream ConvNet framework combining a frame-based spatial network and an optical-flow-based temporal network for human action recognition.
- Integrates predictions from the two ConvNets to classify action videos.
- Achieves superior performance over other ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art on two standard video classification benchmarks.
- Largely adapts standard ConvNet image-classification pipelines to dense optical flow, but provides a comprehensive and convincing experimental evaluation.
- Valuable contribution: systematic comparison of methods for training ConvNets on optical flow and clear demonstration of the benefits of optical-flow input.
- Minor concern: needs clarification why the spatial network significantly outperforms models from [13] despite architectural similarities.
- Minor suggestion: include a comparison of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper likely to appeal to researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
