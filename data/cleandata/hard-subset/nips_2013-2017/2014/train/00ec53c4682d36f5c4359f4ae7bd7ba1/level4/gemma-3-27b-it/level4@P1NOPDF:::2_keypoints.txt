- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a single-frame (spatial) network and a short-sequence dense optical flow (temporal) network for video action classification.  
- Demonstrates substantial improvement over other ConvNet-based methods and achieves state-of-the-art results on two standard video datasets.  
- Applies a standard image-classification ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow frames and fuses this temporal stream with the spatial stream.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and results are compelling, supporting the proposed approach.  
- Provides a systematic comparison of ConvNet training strategies for optical flow, showing clear benefits of using optical flow as an input modality.  
- Minor critique: lacks explanation for why the spatial stream outperforms the similar-architecture models in [13].  
- Suggested addition: compare different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper of high interest to researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
