- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial for frames, temporal for multi-frame optical flow) that outperforms prior deep methods for video action recognition.
- Demonstrates that training on optical flow yields strong performance despite limited video data.
- Introduces a multi-task learning framework to combine datasets (UCF-101/HMDB-51) and improve accuracy.
- Technical approach is sound, thoroughly validated, and reproducible with documented implementation details (optical flow precomputation, multi-GPU training).
- Experiments on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 show clear improvements over prior deep methods, with ablations confirming the role of optical flow and multi-task learning.
- Authors address limitations (e.g., motion compensation via mean subtraction) and validate claims through controlled comparisons (trajectory vs. flow stacking).
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well organized, with effective figures illustrating optical flow inputs.
- Suggests improving focus in abstract/introduction to better unify the core insight: optical flow enables superior motion modeling.
- Two-stream architecture is an original and meaningful advance over hand-crafted two-stream models by using discriminatively trained ConvNets on flow.
- Temporal stream generalizes hand-crafted motion features (HOF, MBH) via convolutional layers, supported by supplementary visualizations.
- Results are significant: state-of-the-art performance with a data-driven approach that reduces reliance on hand-crafted features.
- Multi-task learning offers a practical, principled solution for data-scarce video recognition settings.
- Strengths: clear technical innovation, rigorous empirical validation, practical implementation details, and strong quantitative gains.
- Weaknesses: narrative could better unify two-stream and multi-task contributions and more explicitly contrast with prior deep learning works (e.g., [18], [27]).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to refine the introduction to emphasize the unifying theme of motion modeling via optical flow and explicitly contrast with prior deep methods.
