- Summary: Proposes a dual-pathway convolutional network—one pathway for appearance frames, one for dense optical flow—trained independently and combined via weighted average, achieving (near) state-of-the-art on two benchmarks.  
- Strength: Simple, effective convolutional architecture with minimal complexity that yields strong results.  
- Strength: Demonstrates that optical flow is a valuable input modality for deep learning–based action recognition.  
- Weakness: Processes only fixed-length 10-frame sequences, limiting modeling of long-range temporal dependencies and complex actions.  
- Quality: Experiments are well-designed and clearly presented but lack detailed error analysis (e.g., per-class errors or correlations with video characteristics).  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured and clearly explains the dual-pathway architecture and training procedure.  
- Originality: Novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow as a primary input within a deep learning pipeline.  
- Significance: Offers a lightweight, broadly applicable framework that highlights complementary roles of appearance and motion for motion-sensitive vision tasks.
