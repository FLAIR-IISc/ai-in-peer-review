- Proposes a two-stream convolutional architecture combining a spatial (RGB single-frame) ConvNet and a temporal ConvNet trained on short stacks of dense optical flow.
- Fusing spatial and temporal networks yields substantial gains over prior ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art on two standard video classification benchmarks.
- The method mainly adapts standard image ConvNet pipelines to optical flow and complements them with a single-frame ConvNet.
- Experimental evaluation is careful and convincing, with thorough exploration of multiple training strategies for the optical-flow network.
- Results clearly demonstrate the advantage of using motion (optical flow) as input for action recognition.
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial stream significantly outperforms a similar prior model—need clarification on differences in training data, initialization, optimization, regularization, or data augmentation.
- Suggested additional experiment: compare alternative frame-sampling strategies for the spatial stream (uniform vs. random temporal sampling, varied temporal spacing, multi-crop/multi-scale per frame) to quantify sampling contributions.
- Overall assessment: solid empirical contribution with impressive results likely to interest researchers in ConvNet-based video classification.
