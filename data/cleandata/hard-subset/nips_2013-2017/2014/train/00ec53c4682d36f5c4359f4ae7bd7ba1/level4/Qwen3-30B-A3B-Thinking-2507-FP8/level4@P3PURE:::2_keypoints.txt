- Introduces a novel architecture combining two ConvNets: one on single RGB frames (spatial) and one on short sequences of dense optical flow (temporal) for action classification.  
- Integrates predictions from both networks and significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based action recognition methods.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on two standard video classification benchmarks.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation with compelling results.  
- Valuable exploration of multiple training strategies for ConvNets on optical flow, demonstrating clear benefits.  
- Minor issue: the spatial network's large performance advantage over the models in [13] is not explained despite architectural similarities.  
- Minor suggestion: include a comparison of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: a strong empirical contribution of high interest to video classification researchers.
