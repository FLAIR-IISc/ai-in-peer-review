- Proposes a novel two‑stream ConvNet architecture that fuses a spatial (single-frame) and a temporal (optical flow) network for action recognition.  
- Spatial stream is pre-trained on ImageNet; temporal stream is trained on stacked optical flow images.  
- Leverages both spatial and temporal information to significantly outperform other ConvNet-based methods and reach competitive state‑of‑the‑art on UCF‑101 and HMDB‑51.  
- Comprehensive literature review and clear motivation tied to the two‑streams hypothesis from neuroscience.  
- Experiments explore input configurations (stacked flows, trajectory stacking, mean displacement subtraction).  
- Evaluates multi‑task learning effects on the temporal ConvNet.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with detailed comparisons and analysis.  
- Paper is well‑written and provides sufficient architectural and experimental details.  
- Strength: novel, effective architecture demonstrating strong empirical results.  
- Strength: extensive experimental analysis and investigation of input variants and multi‑task learning.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear explanation why the spatial network outperforms similar architectures from [13].  
- Weakness: does not study different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Suggestion: provide analysis explaining the spatial network’s superior performance relative to [13].  
- Suggestion: investigate and report the effect of alternative frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, strong empirical performance, thorough evaluation, and clear writing.  
- Rejection arguments: missing explanation for spatial network gains and absent frame sampling analysis.
