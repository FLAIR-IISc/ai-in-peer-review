

The paper introduces novel deep learning approaches for video classification, centered on a two-stream architecture and multi-task learning framework. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described with sufficient clarity and rigor. The writing is polished and the results convincingly support the proposed methods, demonstrating substantial improvements over the baseline [13] and competitive performance against state-of-the-art techniques. The two-stream formulation—particularly the significant contribution of optical flow features—represents a compelling and broadly relevant finding for the action recognition community.  

While the technical contributions are strong, the paper would benefit from greater thematic cohesion. The current structure presents multiple advances (two-stream refinement, multi-task learning) without a unifying narrative, making the core contribution somewhat diffuse. A more focused presentation—emphasizing the optical flow integration as the central innovation—would strengthen the argument. The abstract and introduction should reflect this refined emphasis to better orient readers.  

Additionally, the paper omits key citations that contextualize its technical contributions:  
- *Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis* (CVPR 2011) for deep learning-based feature extraction.  
- *Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition* (BMVC 2010) for foundational computer vision work on spatio-temporal features.  
Including these would better position the work within existing literature and clarify the novelty.  

The practical contributions are noteworthy, with the optical flow integration offering a valuable complement to current deep learning paradigms. The empirical results are robust and well-justified, addressing a meaningful gap in video classification.  

**Recommendation**: Accept pending minor revisions. Specifically:  
1. Revise the abstract and introduction to center on the two-stream framework’s optical flow innovation as the primary contribution, streamlining the narrative.  
2. Add the missing citations above to properly contextualize the methodological advances.  

The paper makes a solid contribution to video understanding, and these adjustments will significantly enhance its clarity and scholarly rigor. It is well-suited for publication at NIPS 2014.
