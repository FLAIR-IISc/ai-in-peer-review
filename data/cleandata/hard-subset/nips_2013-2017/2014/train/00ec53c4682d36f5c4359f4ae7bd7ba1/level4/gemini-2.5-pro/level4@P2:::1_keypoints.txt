- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video action recognition: a spatial stream on RGB frames (ImageNet pre-trained) and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow.
- Temporal and spatial streams are trained independently and combined via late fusion (averaging softmax or SVM on concatenated scores).
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive, state-of-the-art performance and outperforming prior deep-learning approaches.
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine datasets and reduce overfitting when training the temporal network on limited video data.
- Strength: simple, effective, and modular architecture that decouples appearance and motion learning and leverages image pre-training.
- Strength: novel and effective use of dense optical flow as input to a ConvNet, showing benefits of pre-computed motion representations.
- Strength: strong empirical results that set a new deep-learning baseline for action recognition.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation and ablation studies exploring input configurations and training strategies.
- Weakness: limited long-range temporal modeling—temporal stream uses short fixed windows (L=10) and global averaging discards ordering and long-range dependencies.
- Weakness: reliance on pre-computed optical flow prevents end-to-end learning and adds computational/storage overhead.
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous experiments; would benefit from more qualitative/error analysis to identify failure modes.
- Clarity: well-written, well-organized, and reproducible with clear figures and implementation details.
- Originality: highly original in applying a dedicated temporal ConvNet on optical flow, distinct from prior handcrafted or raw-pixel approaches.
- Significance: highly impactful work that provides a simple, reproducible baseline and has influenced subsequent video-understanding research.
