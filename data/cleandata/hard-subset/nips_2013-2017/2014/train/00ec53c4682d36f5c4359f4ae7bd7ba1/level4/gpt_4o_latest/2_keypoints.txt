- Introduces a novel architecture combining a single-frame (spatial) ConvNet and a short-sequence dense optical-flow (temporal) ConvNet for action classification.  
- Integrates predictions from both ConvNets, yielding substantial improvements over other ConvNet-based methods and matching state-of-the-art on two benchmark video datasets.  
- Primarily adapts the standard image-classification ConvNet framework to the dense optical-flow modality and fuses it with the spatial stream.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and results are compelling, clearly demonstrating the advantages of using optical flow as input.  
- Presents multiple training strategies for optical-flow ConvNets and shows their benefits.  
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network significantly outperforms models from [13] despite similar architectures.  
- Suggested addition: compare different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper of interest to researchers in video classification using ConvNets.
