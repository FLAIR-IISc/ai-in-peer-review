- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet splitting spatial (ImageNet pre-trained) and temporal (stacked dense optical flow) feature learning for video action recognition.  
- Temporal stream novelty: feeding pre-computed optical flow to ConvNet explicitly models motion and outperforms learning motion from raw RGB.  
- Spatial and temporal outputs are combined via late fusion (averaging or an SVM).  
- Introduces multi-task learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to increase effective training data and reduce overfitting, especially for the temporal stream.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation and ablation studies validate pretraining, temporal input configurations (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, frame count, mean subtraction), and multi-task learning.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art or near state-of-the-art performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, substantially improving deep learning results for this task.  
- High potential impact: likely to become a strong, influential baseline for future video analysis research.  
- Paper is well-written and provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility.  
- Suggests reframing to emphasize the two-stream architecture and temporal stream as the primary contribution, treating multi-task learning as a supporting training strategy.  
- Fusion approach is simple (late fusion); the paper should discuss limitations and future work on earlier or more integrated fusion strategies.  
- Practical limitation: precomputing and storing dense optical flow is computationally expensive and storage-intensive, warranting discussion of trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
