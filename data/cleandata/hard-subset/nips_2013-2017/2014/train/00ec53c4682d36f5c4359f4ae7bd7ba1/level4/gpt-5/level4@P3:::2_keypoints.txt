- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial stream on RGB frames and temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused by late fusion; uses multitask learning across UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Temporal ConvNet explores optical-flow stacking variants (fixed vs trajectory stacking), uni-/bi-directional flow, and mean-flow subtraction.
- Ablation studies clearly justify temporal input design choices and show optical flow input outperforms stacked RGB “slow fusion” baselines.
- Multitask learning yields tangible gains when labeled video data are limited, especially on HMDB-51.
- Empirically outperforms prior deep models and is competitive with state-of-the-art trajectory-based methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Paper is technically sound, carefully executed, and generally well written and organized.
- Needs clarification on why the spatial stream outperforms prior ConvNet baselines; controlled comparisons with matched training protocols are needed.
- Lacks sufficient analysis of computational trade-offs: cost/time/storage of precomputing optical flow and inference throughput for each stream and the fused model.
- Clarity improvements suggested: consolidate notation, map equations to visuals, report mean±std over splits, and list exact L values and flow algorithms in captions.
- Original contribution: explicit motion stream trained on stacked dense optical flow and systematic ablations; multitask training across datasets is a useful novelty.
- High significance: bridges hand-crafted motion features and deep learning, provides a practical recipe likely to influence the field.
- Strength: clear architectural decomposition, neuroscience grounding, and pragmatic training (ImageNet pre-training + multitask).
- Suggestion to ensure fair attribution: perform head-to-head comparisons with prior deep models under matched pre-training, augmentation, and fine-tuning.
- Suggest evaluating spatial-stream sampling policies (uniform vs random, more frames, multi-crop) to isolate gains cheaply.
- Recommend assessing sensitivity to different optical flow algorithms and L, and discussing accuracy vs speed/quality trade-offs.
- Ask to report whether flow quantization/rescaling/JPEG compression affects accuracy versus raw float flow.
- Reproducibility request: report training/inference time per video, GPU memory usage, and commit to releasing models/flow preprocessing.
- Suggest exploring earlier fusion alternatives (e.g., fc-level joint training) and calibration of softmax outputs before SVM.
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical results, informative ablations, practical multitask contribution, likely community impact.
- Cons for acceptance: limited computational/deployment analysis, ambiguity attributing spatial-stream gains, narrow evaluation scope (two datasets, no per-class/error analysis).
- Overall assessment: solid, timely, and impactful contribution recommended for acceptance after addressing the clarifications and additional analyses.
