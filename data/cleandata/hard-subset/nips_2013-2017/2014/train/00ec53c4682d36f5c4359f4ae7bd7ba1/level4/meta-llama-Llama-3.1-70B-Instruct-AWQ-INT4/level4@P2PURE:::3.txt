The paper proposes several novel methods for video classification using deep learning, including a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementations, and procedures are described in sufficient detail, making the paper clear and well-written. The authors present a compelling case for their proposed methods by demonstrating promising results.

The methods presented in the paper are innovative, building upon the framework of [13] and introducing a two-stream framework that showcases the significant improvements achieved by incorporating the optical flow component. This finding is likely to be of interest to many researchers. While the paper is well-presented, it appears to be fragmented across multiple contributions rather than focusing on a single theme. Nevertheless, the results demonstrate impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with the state-of-the-art.

To further enhance the paper, the following suggestions are offered:

1. The abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused theme, as the majority of the significance is concentrated on the two-stream approach and the incorporation of optical flow.

2. The authors may want to consider adding the following citations to provide a more comprehensive overview of the relevant literature:
- Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis (CVPR 2011) in the context of deep learning.
- Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition (BMVC 2010) in the context of computer vision.

The paper offers significant practical contributions and presents interesting ideas that complement the main concepts in deep learning. I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
