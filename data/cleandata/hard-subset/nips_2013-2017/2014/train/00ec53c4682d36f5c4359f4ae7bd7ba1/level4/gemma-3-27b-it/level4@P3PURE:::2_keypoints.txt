- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a spatial stream (single frames) and a temporal stream (short sequences of dense optical flow) for action classification in video.

- Leverages complementary strengths of spatial and temporal streams and significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods, achieving state-of-the-art on two standard datasets.

- Largely applies the established image-classification ConvNet pipeline to optical flow frames and integrates it with a single-frame ConvNet.

- Experiments are thorough and the results are compelling.

- Provides a systematic comparison of training strategies for ConvNets on optical flow, clearly demonstrating the benefits of using optical flow as input.

- Suggests further discussion on why the spatial stream outperforms the comparable model in [13] despite architectural similarities.

- Recommends evaluating different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream to assess their impact on performance.

- Overall assessment: a strong empirical paper of considerable interest to researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
