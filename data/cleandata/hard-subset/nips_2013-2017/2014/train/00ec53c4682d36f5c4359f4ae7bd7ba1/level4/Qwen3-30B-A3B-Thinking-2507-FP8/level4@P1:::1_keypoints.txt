- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet for video action recognition: a spatial stream (ImageNet-pretrained frames) and a temporal stream (multi-frame dense optical flow), trained separately and fused late.  
- Achieves competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, extending prior deep learning video approaches.  

Strengths:
- Effectively integrates optical flow into a deep learning framework, bridging hand-crafted and data-driven features.  
- Temporal ConvNet on optical flow outperforms previous deep-learning attempts on video.  
- Multi-task learning combining UCF-101 and HMDB-51 addresses limited training data.  
- Thorough ablation study of temporal input configurations (flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, etc.) offers design insights.  

Weaknesses:
- Temporal stream processes a fixed-length sequence (L=10), limiting modeling of long-range temporal dependencies.  
- No exploration of architectural fixes for long-range modeling (e.g., recurrent networks or temporal models beyond fixed-length).  
- Lacks detailed error analysis identifying classes or video types where the model fails.  

Quality:
- Experimental design is rigorous with appropriate baselines, ablations, and benchmark validation.  
- Would benefit from deeper error-pattern analysis linking failures to motion or visual ambiguities.  

Clarity:
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized with effective figures illustrating temporal inputs.  

Originality:
- Novel application of optical flow as ConvNet input for video, generalizing hand-crafted features within a data-driven approach.  

Significance:
- Demonstrates the value of integrating domain-specific features into deep learning, outperforms prior deep methods, and offers a practical multi-dataset training strategy.  

Overall:
- Strong, technically sound contribution that bridges traditional and deep video recognition approaches; recommend acceptance despite fixed-length temporal limitation.
