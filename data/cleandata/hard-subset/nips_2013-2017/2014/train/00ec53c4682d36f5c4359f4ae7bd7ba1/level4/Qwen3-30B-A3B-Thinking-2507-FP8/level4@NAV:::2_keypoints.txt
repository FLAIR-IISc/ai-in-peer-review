- Introduces a novel neural architecture combining two ConvNets: one for individual video frames (spatial) and one for short sequences of dense optical flow (temporal).  
- Integrates predictions from the spatial and temporal networks to perform human action classification in videos.  
- Achieves superior performance to other ConvNet-based action recognition methods and attains state-of-the-art results on two common video benchmarks.  
- Methodology builds on established ConvNet image-classification techniques applied to dense optical flow and combined with a single-frame network.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the reported results are compelling.  
- Inclusion and comparison of multiple training approaches for ConvNets on optical flow is particularly valuable and empirically justified.  
- Minor concern: clarify why the spatial network shows a substantial performance advantage over the models in [13] despite architectural similarities.  
- Minor suggestion: include a comparative analysis of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream to provide further insight.  
- Overall assessment: a well-executed empirical study of high interest to researchers in ConvNet-based video classification.
