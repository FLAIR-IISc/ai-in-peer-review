- Proposes novel extensions for video classification combining a two-stream architecture and multi-task learning.  
- Methodology is well-motivated with clear technical descriptions, implementation details, and procedures.  
- Strong empirical results showing significant absolute gains over baseline [13] and competitive performance with state-of-the-art.  
- Two-stream formulation (optical flow integration) is the paper’s most compelling and impactful contribution.  
- Paper lacks thematic cohesion: multiple contributions (two-stream, multi-task, refinements) are not unified in the abstract/introduction.  
- Experimental design is thorough with ablation studies isolating optical flow and multi-task contributions and appropriate statistical rigor.  
- Technical contributions are novel in implementation and integration, building on but not simply repeating [13].  
- Multi-task learning is less strongly justified but serves as a complementary enhancement rather than undermining the core idea.  
- Required revision 1: refocus abstract/introduction to emphasize the two-stream framework as the primary contribution.  
- Required revision 2: add missing citation coverage—specifically CVPR 2011 “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features…” and BMVC 2010 “Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features…”  
- Recommendation: accept pending the thematic refinement and citation updates.
