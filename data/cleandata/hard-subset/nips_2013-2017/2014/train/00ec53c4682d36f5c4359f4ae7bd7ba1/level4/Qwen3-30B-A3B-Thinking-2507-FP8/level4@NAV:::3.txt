

The paper introduces novel deep learning approaches for video classification, specifically a two-stream architecture and multi-task learning framework. The methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are thoroughly described with clarity. The writing is precise, and the proposed methods are convincingly validated through strong empirical results.  

While the work builds upon [13], the two-stream formulation demonstrates significant value, particularly in showing that optical flow integration substantially enhances performance—a finding of broad interest to the research community. The paper is well-structured and presents compelling results that achieve substantial gains over [13] and competitive performance against state-of-the-art methods. However, the narrative appears fragmented across multiple contributions rather than cohesively unified under a single theme.  

Recommended improvements:  
1. The abstract and introduction should refocus to emphasize the core contribution of optical flow integration within the two-stream framework, as this represents the most significant advancement.  
2. Critical missing citations in deep learning:  
   - *Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis*, CVPR 2011.  
   - *Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition*, BMVC 2010.  

The paper offers meaningful practical contributions with ideas that complement contemporary deep learning research. I recommend acceptance at NIPS 2014, contingent on addressing the suggested revisions.
