- Model uses two separate CNNs: one on an appearance frame and one on a stack of dense optical flow; final video prediction is a weighted average of both.
- Each network is trained independently on its input modality.
- Evaluated on two datasets with results comparable to or better than state-of-the-art methods using hand-crafted features.
- Strength: simple, straightforward CNN application that achieves strong performance without unnecessary complexity.
- Strength: demonstrates the effectiveness of using optical flow as an input representation for CNNs in action recognition.
- Weakness: does not model long-range temporal structure—only processes fixed-length adjacent-frame clips (L=10).
- Experiments are well-designed, but the paper lacks detailed error analysis (per-class errors or types of challenging videos).
- Paper is clearly written with a well-explained model description.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow features.
- Significance: simple, effective approach with potential to influence future video/motion research.
