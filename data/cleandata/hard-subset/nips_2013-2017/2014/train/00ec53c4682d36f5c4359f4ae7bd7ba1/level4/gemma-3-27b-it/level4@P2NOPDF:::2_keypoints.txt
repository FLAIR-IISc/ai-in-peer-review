- Introduces a two-stream neural architecture for action recognition: a spatial ConvNet on frames and a temporal ConvNet on dense optical flow, combined for final prediction.  
- Leverages complementary strengths of appearance and motion cues within a deep learning framework.  
- Achieves substantial gains over existing ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art on two standard video datasets.  
- Builds on image-classification ConvNets by extending them to dense optical flow and combining with single-frame ConvNet.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive and shows substantial performance improvements.  
- Thorough comparison of training strategies for the optical-flow ConvNet demonstrates clear benefits of using motion input.  
- Ablation studies on optical-flow inputs are well-executed and clarify the temporal stream’s contributions.  
- The spatial stream’s notable performance boost is unexplained given architectural similarities to prior work [13]; this requires further analysis.  
- Recommends future work: evaluate different frame-sampling strategies (densities/methods) for the spatial stream to optimize performance.  
- Overall: strong empirical contribution with clear presentation and compelling results for video classification research.
