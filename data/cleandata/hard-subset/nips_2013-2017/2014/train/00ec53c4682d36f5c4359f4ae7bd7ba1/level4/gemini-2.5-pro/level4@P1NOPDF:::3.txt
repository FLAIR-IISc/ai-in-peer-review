This paper proposes several new methods for video classification using deep learning, most notably a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning framework. The methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are described in sufficient detail. The paper is clear, well-written, and makes a compelling case for the proposed methods by demonstrating strong empirical results.

The proposed methods are novel. While the work builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream architecture is a particularly interesting contribution. The clear demonstration that the optical flow stream provides significant performance gains is a key finding that will be of broad interest to the community. Despite the clear presentation, the paper's focus feels somewhat divided across its multiple contributions rather than being unified by a single, central theme. Nevertheless, the results represent a substantial improvement over [13] and are competitive with the current state-of-the-art.

Suggestions for improvement:

1.  Given that the main contribution appears to be the two-stream architecture and the incorporation of optical flow, the abstract and introduction could be revised to present a more focused narrative around this theme.

2.  The authors should consider including the following relevant citations:
    *   In deep learning: Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
    *   In computer vision: Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC, 2010.

Overall, this paper offers significant practical contributions and interesting ideas that complement existing work in deep learning. I recommend that the paper be accepted to NIPS 2014, on the condition that the authors address the suggestions outlined above.
