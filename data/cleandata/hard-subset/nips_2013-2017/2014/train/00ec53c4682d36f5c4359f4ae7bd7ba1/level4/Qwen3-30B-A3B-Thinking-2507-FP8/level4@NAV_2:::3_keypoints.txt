- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial frames + temporal dense optical flow) with multi-task learning across datasets.  
- Methodology is technically sound and reproducible (optical flow computation, multi-GPU training).  
- Thorough ablation studies (flow stacking variants, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction) justify design choices.  
- Rigorous evaluation on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with clear experimental results.  
- Temporal stream alone outperforms Karpathy et al. by ~20% on UCF-101.  
- Fused two-stream model is competitive with state-of-the-art shallow representations despite limited training data.  
- Multi-task learning effectively boosts performance on small datasets (HMDB-51).  
- Two-stream decoupling is an original, distinct framework that generalizes hand-crafted motion features (HOF, MBH).  
- Visualized filters in supplementary material support the motion-feature interpretation.  
- Clarity issue: paper does not coherently unify two-stream architecture and multi-task learning as a single conceptual contribution.  
- Related-work omission: fails to cite key trajectory-based methods (Le et al., CVPR 2011; Wang et al., BMVC 2010) that predate optical flow stacking.  
- Overall recommendation: substantial contribution with minor revisions to clarify core innovation and update related work; recommend acceptance.
