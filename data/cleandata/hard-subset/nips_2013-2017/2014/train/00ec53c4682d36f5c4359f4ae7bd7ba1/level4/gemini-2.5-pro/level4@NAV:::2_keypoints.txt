- Proposes a novel two-stream neural network that fuses a spatial stream (individual frames) and a temporal stream (sequences of dense optical flow) for human action recognition in video.
- Temporal stream is trained on dense optical flow while the spatial stream processes RGB frames.
- Achieves superior performance and state-of-the-art results on two widely used video classification benchmarks.
- Core idea adapts a standard CNN image-classification framework to optical flow and combines it with a frame-based CNN.
- Empirical evaluation is rigorous and reports remarkable performance gains.
- Thorough comparison of multiple training methods for the optical flow network, validating optical flow as a useful input modality.
- Minor comment: clarify why the spatial stream significantly outperforms the architecturally similar model in [13].
- Minor suggestion: investigate the impact of different frame sampling strategies on the spatial network’s performance.
- Overall assessment: strong, empirically driven contribution highly relevant to video analysis with deep learning.
