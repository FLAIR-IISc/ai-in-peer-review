- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture: a spatial stream for RGB frames and a temporal stream for stacked dense optical flow, with late fusion of predictions.
- Temporal stream uses pre-computed optical flow as input, demonstrating that combining hand-crafted motion representations with ConvNets is highly effective.
- Each stream is trained independently and fused (averaging or SVM) for final action classification.
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving large improvements over prior deep-learning methods and competitive results with engineered-feature approaches.
- Multi-task learning across datasets is shown to improve performance by leveraging additional data.
- Elegant, intuitive design that effectively combines appearance and motion cues for action recognition.
- Key contribution: demonstrating ConvNets can learn from stacked optical flow, yielding state-of-the-art performance in this setting.
- Limitation: temporal reasoning is limited to a short fixed-length window (L=10), failing to capture long-range video structure.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with systematic ablation studies on pre-training, input configurations, and fusion methods.
- Claims are well-supported by experiments, but the paper lacks analysis of failure modes and class-specific errors.
- Paper is clearly written and detailed enough to enable reproduction.
- Novelty: applying ConvNets directly to stacked optical flow is a novel and effective approach; two-stream implementation as parallel deep ConvNets is a new architectural choice.
- Significance: establishes a strong baseline for deep-learning-based action recognition and highlights the value of combining traditional representations with deep models.
