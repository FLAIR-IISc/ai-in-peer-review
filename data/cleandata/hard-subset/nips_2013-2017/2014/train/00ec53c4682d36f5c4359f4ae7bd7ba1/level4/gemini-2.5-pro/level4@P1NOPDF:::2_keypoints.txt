- Introduces a two-stream neural architecture combining a spatial ConvNet (single frames) and a temporal ConvNet (stacks of dense optical flow).  
- Temporal stream is trained on dense optical flow stacks, and its fusion with the spatial stream improves action recognition.  
- The two-stream model significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches and achieves state-of-the-art results on two standard video datasets.  
- Core novelty is applying standard ConvNet pipelines to a new modality (dense optical flow) and fusing it with single-frame predictions.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with systematic comparisons of strategies for training ConvNets on optical flow.  
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial network substantially outperforms models from [13]; authors should discuss possible reasons.  
- Minor suggestion: strengthen analysis by comparing different frame-sampling strategies for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper of high interest to video classification researchers.
