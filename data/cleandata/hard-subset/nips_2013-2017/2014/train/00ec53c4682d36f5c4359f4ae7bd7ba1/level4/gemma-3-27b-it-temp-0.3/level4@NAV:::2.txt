This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture for human action classification in videos. The proposed model leverages the complementary strengths of two convolutional neural networks: one processing individual video frames and the other analyzing short sequences of dense optical flow images. The approach demonstrates substantial improvements over existing ConvNet-based methods for action recognition, achieving performance comparable to the current state-of-the-art on two commonly used video classification benchmarks. 

Although the work builds upon established ConvNet image classification techniques, applying them to dense optical flow data and integrating them with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the findings are compelling. The systematic comparison of various ConvNet training strategies for optical flow is particularly valuable, clearly illustrating the advantages of incorporating this input modality.

I have a few minor suggestions for consideration:
- The significantly superior performance of the spatial network compared to models reported in [13], despite architectural similarities, warrants further discussion.
- Exploring the impact of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream could provide additional insights. 

Overall, this is a strong empirical contribution that will be of significant interest to researchers in the field of video classification using convolutional neural networks.
