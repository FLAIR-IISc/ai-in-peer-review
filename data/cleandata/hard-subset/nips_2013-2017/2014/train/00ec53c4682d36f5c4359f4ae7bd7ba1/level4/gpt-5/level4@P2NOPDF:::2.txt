This paper introduces a two-stream convolutional architecture for human action recognition in video, combining a spatial network trained on individual RGB frames with a temporal network trained on short sequences of dense optical flow. Fusing their predictions yields substantial gains over prior ConvNet-based methods and matches state-of-the-art performance on two widely used video classification benchmarks.

Although the approach primarily adapts a standard image ConvNet pipeline to optical flow and complements it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental work is careful and convincing. The authors thoroughly explore multiple training strategies for the optical-flow network and clearly demonstrate the advantage of using motion (optical flow) as input.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why the spatial stream significantly outperforms the models in [13], given the apparent similarity in architectures. Please clarify any differences in training data, initialization, optimization, regularization, or data augmentation that might explain the gap.
- A comparison of alternative frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream would be valuable (e.g., uniform vs. random temporal sampling, varying temporal spacing, multi-crop or multi-scale sampling per frame). This could help quantify the contribution of sampling choices to overall performance.

Overall, this is a solid empirical contribution with impressive results, likely to interest researchers working on ConvNet-based video classification.
