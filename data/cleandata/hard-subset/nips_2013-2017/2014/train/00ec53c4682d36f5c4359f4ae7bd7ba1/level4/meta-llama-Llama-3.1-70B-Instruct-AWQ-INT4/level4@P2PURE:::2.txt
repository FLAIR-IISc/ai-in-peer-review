This paper proposes a novel neural network architecture for classifying videos of human actions, achieving state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets by outperforming other ConvNet-based approaches. The model combines predictions from two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames and the other trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images.

While the paper primarily applies the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to a new type of data (dense optical flow frames) and integrates it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experiments are comprehensive, and the results are impressive. It is notable that the paper explores various methods of training a ConvNet on optical flow and presents clear results demonstrating the benefits of using optical flow as input.

I have a few minor comments: 
- The significant performance improvement of the spatial network over the models from [13] is not entirely clear, given the similarities in their architectures.
- A comparison of different sampling schemes for frames fed into the spatial stream would be an interesting addition.
This is a solid empirical paper that will be of interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
