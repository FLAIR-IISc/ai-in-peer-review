- Proposes a dual-stream CNN for action recognition: one network for appearance (frames) and one for motion (stacked optical flow), combined by weighted averaging.
- Networks are trained independently and their predictions are fused to produce video-level classifications.
- Demonstrates strong, competitive performance on two challenging datasets, sometimes surpassing state-of-the-art handcrafted-feature methods.
- Strength is elegant simplicity and effective use of CNNs applied directly to optical flow, re-establishing the value of motion features alongside learned representations.
- Novelty lies in applying CNNs specifically to optical flow within a dual-stream architecture for action recognition.
- Key limitation: processes local, fixed-length temporal windows (L=10) independently, lacking explicit long-range temporal or sequential modeling.
- Temporal integration via weighted averaging is limited and may miss complex relationships across a full video.
- Experiments are well-designed with clear results and thorough comparisons to existing methods.
- Paper lacks deeper error analysis: which classes fail, and which video conditions (camera motion, occlusion, clutter) cause misclassifications.
- Recommends an ablation study on the weighting/fusion scheme to better understand contributions of each stream.
- Writing and figures are clear and accessible, and the approach has significant potential impact as a simple, hybrid strategy for video understanding.
