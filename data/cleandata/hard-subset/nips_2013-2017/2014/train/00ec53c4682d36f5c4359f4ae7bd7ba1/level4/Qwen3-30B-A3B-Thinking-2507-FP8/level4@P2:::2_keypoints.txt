- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: a spatial stream on individual frames plus a temporal stream on dense optical flow.  
- Shows substantial improvement over prior deep-learning video methods and competitive performance with hand-crafted features on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Builds on the two-stream hypothesis and adapts image ConvNet advances to the video domain.  
- Temporal stream explicitly models motion via optical flow, enabling superior performance compared to earlier video ConvNets.  
- Introduces a multi-task learning framework to leverage multiple datasets and address limited video training data.  
- Experimental design is thorough, including ablation studies on flow input configurations, fusion methods, and multi-task strategies.  
- Demonstrates that spatial and temporal streams provide complementary information and that the combined model outperforms single-stream models.  
- Provides detailed implementation specifics (optical flow computation, training protocols, network configs) for reproducibility.  
- Original contribution: data-driven learning of motion features analogous to hand-crafted descriptors (e.g., HOF, MBH).  
- Significance: establishes a new deep-learning benchmark for action recognition and a practical architecture for future work.  
- Strengths: comprehensive experiments, clear evidence of complementary streams, practical multi-tasking, reproducible details, strong empirical gains.  
- Weakness: unclear explanation for why the spatial stream outperforms prior work (≈15–20% gap) without implementation comparison.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of alternative motion representations beyond optical flow.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational efficiency, inference time, and memory constraints.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, well-written, and a substantial contribution to video action recognition.
