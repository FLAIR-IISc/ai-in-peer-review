- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture that fuses predictions from a frame-based network and an optical-flow-based network for video action classification.  
- Achieves significant improvements over prior ConvNet-based methods and state-of-the-art results on two standard video benchmarks.  
- Adapts the standard image ConvNet pipeline to operate on dense optical flow and combines it with an RGB-frame stream.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the results are convincing.  
- Provides a thorough exploration of multiple training strategies for the optical-flow ConvNet, empirically demonstrating optical flow’s superiority as an input modality.  
- Requests clarification: why the spatial (frame) stream outperforms models in [13] despite similar architectures.  
- Requests inclusion of a comparison of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream to assess robustness.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical contribution that advances video action recognition and will interest researchers.
