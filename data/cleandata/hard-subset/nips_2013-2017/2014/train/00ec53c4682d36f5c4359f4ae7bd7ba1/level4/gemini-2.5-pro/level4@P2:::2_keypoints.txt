- Proposal: two-stream ConvNet separating spatial (single-frame appearance) and temporal (stacked dense optical flow) streams, combined via late fusion; evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with strong results.  
- Strength: effective and elegant architecture—decoupling appearance and motion and using precomputed optical flow yields a powerful motion representation.  
- Strength: state-of-the-art performance competitive with best hand-crafted-feature methods (e.g., iDT).  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation with ablations on pretraining, temporal input configurations, multi-task learning, and fusion strategies.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation enabling understanding and likely reproducibility.  
- Weakness: not end-to-end—the system depends on precomputed optical flow, introducing preprocessing cost and preventing joint optimization.  
- Weakness: unclear why the spatial stream outperforms prior single-frame models—need controlled analysis isolating data augmentation effects.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of temporal sampling strategies (e.g., denser or random sampling) and sensitivity of spatial-stream performance to sampling.  
- Overall: strong, influential empirical contribution to video action recognition; recommended for acceptance despite the noted limitations.
