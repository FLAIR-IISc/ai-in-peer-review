- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video: one spatial net on single frames and one temporal net on stacked dense optical flow, fused by weighted averaging.  
- Each pathway is trained separately to classify its input.  
- Addresses complementary information between appearance (still frames) and motion (optical flow).  
- Novel contribution: applying convolutional nets to optical flow features.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 versus state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.  
- Shows temporal ConvNet (motion) outperforms spatial ConvNet and that motion is important for action recognition.  
- Strength: simplicity and clear, well-explained model.  
- Strength: effective application of ConvNets to video and motion features with potential broad impact.  
- Experiments are well-designed and provide a thorough evaluation of the approach.  
- Limitation: only models fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames, not entire action sequences.  
- Limitation: lacks analysis of error modes or classes where the model fails.  
- Limitation: may be less effective on videos with complex or variable-length actions.  
- Recommendation balance: novel and effective approach arguing for acceptance, but shortcomings in sequence modeling and error analysis argue against.
