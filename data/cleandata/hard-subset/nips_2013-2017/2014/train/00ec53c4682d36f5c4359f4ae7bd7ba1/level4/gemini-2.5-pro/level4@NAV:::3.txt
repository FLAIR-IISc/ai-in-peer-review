This paper introduces novel deep learning methods for video classification, specifically a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning framework. The methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are described with sufficient detail. The manuscript is clearly written and effectively justifies the proposed techniques by presenting strong empirical results.

The contributions are original. While the work builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream formulation is particularly compelling, as it demonstrates that the optical flow component provides a significant performance boost. This is a finding that will be of broad interest to the research community. Although the paper is presented with clarity, its narrative appears to be divided among several contributions rather than being unified by a single, central theme. Ultimately, the results show impressive gains over [13] and are on par with state-of-the-art approaches.

To further strengthen the manuscript, the following revisions are suggested:
1.  The primary significance of the work seems to lie in the two-stream architecture and the incorporation of optical flow. The abstract and introduction could be reframed to better reflect this as the core theme.
2.  The authors should include citations to relevant prior work, including:
    *   In deep learning: Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
    *   In computer vision: Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC, 2010.

The paper offers significant practical contributions and presents interesting concepts that complement existing ideas in deep learning. I recommend acceptance for NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors incorporating the suggested improvements.
