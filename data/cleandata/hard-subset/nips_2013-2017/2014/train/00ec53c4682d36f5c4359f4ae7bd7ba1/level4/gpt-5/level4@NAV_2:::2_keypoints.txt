- Introduces a dual-stream ConvNet architecture fusing a spatial network (individual frames) and a temporal network (stacks of dense optical flow).  
- Method substantially outperforms previous ConvNet-based approaches and reaches state-of-the-art performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Core idea: apply a standard image ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow and combine it with a single-frame ConvNet.  
- Experimental study is careful and the results are strong.  
- Systematically explores several ways to train a ConvNet on optical flow, with clear evidence that optical flow input is advantageous.  
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial stream outperforms the models in [13] given similar architectures.  
- Minor suggestion: a comparison of alternative frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream would be informative.  
- Overall: a solid empirical paper likely to interest researchers in ConvNet-based video classification.
