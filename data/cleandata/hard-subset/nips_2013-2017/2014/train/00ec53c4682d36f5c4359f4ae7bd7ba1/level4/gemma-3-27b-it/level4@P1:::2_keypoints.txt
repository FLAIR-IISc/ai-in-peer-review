- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture (spatial on frames + temporal on dense optical flow) whose combined predictions improve over prior ConvNet approaches and are competitive on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Extends image ConvNet paradigms to video by training a ConvNet directly on multi-frame dense optical flow, effective even with limited data.
- Builds conceptually on the two-stream hypothesis from neuroscience and implements it robustly.
- Thorough experiments comparing temporal-stream inputs (optical flow stacking vs. trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow) and fusion methods (averaging vs. SVM).
- Uses multi-task learning to effectively enlarge training data and strengthen results.
- Strongly situates the work within prior literature, relating to both hand-crafted feature methods and recent deep learning approaches.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis explaining why the spatial ConvNet outperforms prior similar architectures.
- Weakness: limited exploration of frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream (temporal densities, learned sampling).
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear motivation, comprehensive experiments, and well-connected related work.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited explanation of spatial-net gains and limited spatial frame-sampling evaluation.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, technically sound paper with clarity and thorough evaluation; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses, merits acceptance.
