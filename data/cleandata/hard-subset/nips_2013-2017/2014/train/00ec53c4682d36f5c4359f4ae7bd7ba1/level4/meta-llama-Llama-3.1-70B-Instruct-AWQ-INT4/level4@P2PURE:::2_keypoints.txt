- Proposes a novel two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a spatial network (single frames) and a temporal network (short sequences of dense optical flow) for action classification.  
- Treats dense optical flow as image-like input and applies the standard ConvNet image-classification pipeline to it.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets, outperforming other ConvNet-based approaches.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and convincingly show the benefits of using optical flow as input.  
- Explores several methods for training ConvNets on optical flow and presents clear comparative results.  
- Minor concern: the large improvement of the spatial network over model [13] is not fully explained given architectural similarities.  
- Suggested addition: compare different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: solid empirical paper of interest to video-classification researchers.
