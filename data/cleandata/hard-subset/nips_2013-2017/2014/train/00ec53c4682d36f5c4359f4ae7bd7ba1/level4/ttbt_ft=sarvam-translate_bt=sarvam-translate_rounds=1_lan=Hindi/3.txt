This paper proposes some new methods for video classification with deep learning, namely two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation, and processes are well-described. The paper is clear, well-written, and presents a compelling case for the proposed methods, demonstrating good results.

These methods are innovative. While the paper adds improvements to the framework of [13], the two-stream framework is very interesting, as it shows that the optical flow component adds significant improvements. This is a result that many researchers will be interested in. The paper is clearly presented, and it seems divided into several contributions rather than being a single topic. Ultimately, the results provide a significantly impactful improvement over [13] and are competitive with the state of the art.

Here are some suggestions to improve the paper:

1. It seems that most of the emphasis is focused on the inclusion of two-stream and optical flow; the abstract and introduction could be more focused on the subject.
2. Missing citations in deep learning:
Learning Hierarchical Spatio-Temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
Missing citations in computer vision:
Evaluating Local Spatio-Temporal Features for Action Recognition. BMVC, 2010.

This paper seems to make a significant practical contribution, with some interesting ideas that complement the main ideas in deep learning. I would recommend accepting the paper at the NIPS 2014, provided that the authors provide the suggested improvements.
