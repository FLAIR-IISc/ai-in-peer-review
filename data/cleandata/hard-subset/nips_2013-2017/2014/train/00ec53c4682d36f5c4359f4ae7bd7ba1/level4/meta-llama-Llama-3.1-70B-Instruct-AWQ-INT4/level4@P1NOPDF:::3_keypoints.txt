- Proposes several new deep-learning methods for video classification, including a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning.
- Methodology, implementation, and procedures are clearly described; paper is well-written and comprehensible.
- Demonstrates compelling empirical results with substantial improvements over [13] and competitive performance with state-of-the-art.
- Two-stream framework (appearance + optical flow) is particularly interesting; optical flow significantly improves performance.
- Methods are novel and practically significant, complementing existing deep-learning approaches.
- Contributions feel somewhat fragmented and lack a single unifying theme.
- Recommend sharpening the abstract and introduction to focus on the two-stream/optical-flow significance.
- Missing citations to relevant deep-learning work (e.g., CVPR 2011 "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition...").
- Missing citations to relevant computer-vision work (e.g., BMVC 2010 "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition").
- Recommendation: accept at NIPS 2014 conditional on addressing the suggested improvements.
