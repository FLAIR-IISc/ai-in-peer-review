The paper proposes a few novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementations, and procedures are described in good detail. The paper is clear, well-written, and presents a fair case for the proposed methods by showing good results.

These methods are novel and build upon the framework of [13]. The two-stream framework is particularly interesting, as it demonstrates that the optical flow component adds significant improvements. This is a result that many researchers will be interested in. The paper is clearly presented, although it seems to be split across multiple contributions rather than having a single theme. Nevertheless, the results offer impressive improvements upon [13] and are competitive with the state-of-the-art.

To further improve the paper, I suggest the following:

1. The abstract and introduction could have a more focused theme, as most of the significance is concentrated on the two-stream formulation and incorporation of optical flow.
2. The authors should include missing citations in deep learning, such as "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis" (CVPR 2011).
3. Additionally, the authors should include missing citations in computer vision, such as "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition" (BMVC, 2010).

The paper offers significant practical contributions, with some interesting ideas that complement the main ideas in deep learning. I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, conditional upon the authors providing the suggested improvements.

Strengths:

* Novel two-stream formulation for video classification
* Effective incorporation of optical flow into the deep learning framework
* Good results, with improvements upon [13] and competitive with the state-of-the-art
* Clear and well-written presentation

Weaknesses:

* Paper seems to be split across multiple contributions rather than having a single theme
* Missing citations in deep learning and computer vision

Arguments for acceptance:

* Novel and interesting methods for video classification
* Good results and competitive with the state-of-the-art
* Clear and well-written presentation

Arguments against acceptance:

* Paper may benefit from a more focused theme
* Missing citations need to be addressed

Overall, I believe that the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of video classification using deep learning. With some revisions to address the suggested improvements, I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014.
