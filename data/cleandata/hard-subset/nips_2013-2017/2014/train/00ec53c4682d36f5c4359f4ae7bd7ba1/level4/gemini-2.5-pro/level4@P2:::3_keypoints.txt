- Proposes a two-stream deep architecture for video action recognition: a spatial ConvNet on frames and a temporal ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow, plus multi-task training across UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to reduce overfitting.

- Central contribution is explicitly separating appearance and motion learning, motivated by the two-streams hypothesis.

- Providing optical flow as input enables the network to learn strong temporal features and yields substantial performance gains over prior end-to-end pixel-based models.

- Strong technical quality with detailed ablation studies (number of flow frames, trajectory stacking, mean flow subtraction) and evidence that multi-task learning regularizes training on limited video data.

- Implementation details are well-described and experiments appear reproducible.

- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and supported by informative figures and tables.

- Narrative focus should be sharpened to emphasize the two-stream architecture as the primary contribution; multi-task learning is more of a practical add-on.

- Related-work discussion could be expanded to better contextualize differences from earlier spatio-temporal feature learning (e.g., Wang et al. BMVC 2009; Le et al. CVPR 2011).

- Model currently lacks trajectory-based pooling and sophisticated camera-motion compensation, highlighting directions for future integration with hand-crafted pipeline strengths.

- Overall conclusion: significant, practical advance in action recognition, competitive with engineered shallow methods, likely high impact — recommend acceptance.
