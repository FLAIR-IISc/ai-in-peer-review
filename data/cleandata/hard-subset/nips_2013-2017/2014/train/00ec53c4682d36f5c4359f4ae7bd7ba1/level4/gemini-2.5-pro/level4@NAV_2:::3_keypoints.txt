- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet that processes spatial (static frames) and temporal (dense optical flow) information separately and fuses them late for video action recognition.
- Temporal ConvNet trained on pre-computed optical flow is a strong motion representation that generalizes hand-crafted features like HOF and MBH.
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to increase training data and reduce overfitting on small video datasets.
- Methodology is technically sound, described in sufficient detail for reproducibility (architecture, training, preprocessing).
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, with ablation studies on input configurations and training strategies supporting the claims.
- Paper is well-written and organized, but the presentation feels split across multiple contributions rather than focused on a single theme.
- The two-stream framework is a novel and meaningful contribution; demonstrating that optical-flow-trained ConvNets outperform raw-frame ConvNets is an original insight.
- Related work review is incomplete and should be expanded.
- Results are significant: competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted features and substantially better than prior deep-learning video models on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/effective architecture, strong motion descriptor finding, competitive empirical results, and thorough analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrative lacks focus on the primary contribution and the related work section is insufficient.
- Suggested improvements: revise abstract/introduction to emphasize the two-stream and optical-flow contribution, and add missing citations (e.g., Le et al. CVPR 2011; Wang et al. BMVC 2009).
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing focus and related-work citation improvements.
