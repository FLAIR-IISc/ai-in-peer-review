

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture for human action classification in videos, leveraging a fusion of predictions from two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames and another on short sequences of dense optical flow. The proposed method significantly outperforms previous ConvNet-based approaches to action recognition and achieves state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification benchmarks.

While the core approach adapts the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to optical flow data and combines it with a frame-based stream, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the results are compelling. The authors' thorough exploration of multiple training strategies for optical flow ConvNets is particularly valuable, with clear empirical evidence demonstrating the superiority of optical flow as an input modality.

The paper raises two minor points for clarification:
1. The spatial stream (frame-based network) achieves substantially better results than the models in [13], despite similar architectural designs. The authors should elaborate on why this performance gap exists.
2. It would be beneficial to include a comparison of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream, as this could provide further insight into the model's robustness.

Overall, this is a strong empirical contribution that advances the state of the art in video action recognition using ConvNets. The work will be of significant interest to researchers in the field.
