- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture: spatial stream on RGB frames and temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused for final prediction.
- Intuitive and well-motivated separation of appearance and motion, adapting proven image ConvNet practices to temporal inputs.
- Strong empirical performance: substantial gains over prior ConvNet baselines and state-of-the-art on two standard action recognition benchmarks.
- Thorough experimental evaluation, especially multiple constructions and training schemes for the temporal stream using dense optical flow.
- Practical and straightforward to implement and train; simple yet effective score-level fusion.
- Novelty is incremental—the main contribution is applying standard image ConvNet pipelines to stacked optical flow and fusing with a single-frame ConvNet.
- Need clarification why the spatial stream substantially outperforms model [13] despite similar architectures (pretraining, augmentation, training schedule, etc.).
- Compare spatial-frame sampling strategies (uniform vs. random vs. motion-aware; single vs. multiple frames) and quantify their impact on accuracy and robustness.
- Computational overhead from precomputing dense optical flow needs reporting (training/inference runtimes, flow computation cost, storage).
- Sensitivity to optical flow algorithm and parameters (window size, stack length/stride) should be ablated to assess robustness.
- Fusion method limited to score-level fusion—compare with feature-level or learned fusion weights to see if gains can increase.
- Suggestion: provide a detailed comparison and ablation versus [13] to pinpoint sources of improvement.
- Suggestion: add studies on spatial-stream sampling, temporal-stream ablations (stack length, stride, frame rate, forward vs. bidirectional flow).
- Suggestion: report wall-clock times, memory/storage requirements, and comment on generalization and known failure modes (e.g., camera motion, short actions).
- Overall: strong empirical paper with modest architectural novelty but thorough validation and practical importance; recommended for acceptance.
