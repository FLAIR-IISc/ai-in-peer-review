This paper introduces novel deep learning methods for video classification, specifically leveraging a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly and clearly. The writing is of high quality, and the results presented provide a compelling justification for the proposed approaches.

The presented work is innovative. While building upon the framework established in [13], the two-stream approach is particularly noteworthy, demonstrating a substantial performance gain through the inclusion of optical flow – a finding likely to be of broad interest to the research community. The paper is well-structured, though the contributions feel somewhat dispersed rather than unified around a central theme. The reported results demonstrate significant improvements over [13] and achieve competitive performance compared to current state-of-the-art methods.

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest the following:

1. Given the primary impact appears to stem from the two-stream architecture and the integration of optical flow, the abstract and introduction could benefit from a more focused narrative.
2. The authors should include citations to relevant prior work, specifically: “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis” (CVPR 2011) in the deep learning domain, and “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition” (BMVC, 2010) within computer vision. 

Overall, this paper offers valuable practical contributions and presents interesting ideas that complement existing deep learning techniques. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
