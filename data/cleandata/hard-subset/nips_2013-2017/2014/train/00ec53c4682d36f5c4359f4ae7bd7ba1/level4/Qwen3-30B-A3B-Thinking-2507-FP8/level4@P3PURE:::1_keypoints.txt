- Summary: Proposes a dual-branch CNN—one for appearance frames, one for stacked optical flow—trained independently and fused by weighted averaging, achieving competitive results on benchmarks.
- Strength: Demonstrates that a straightforward convolutional network yields strong video classification performance.
- Strength: Establishes optical flow fields as a valuable input modality for CNN-based action recognition.
- Weakness: Processes only fixed-length (L=10) frame sequences, limiting modeling of long-range temporal dynamics.
- Quality: Experiments are sound but lack error analysis identifying specific failure modes or underperforming classes/videos.
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written with the architecture and methodology well explained.
- Originality: Novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow features for video action recognition.
- Significance: Presents a simple, promising framework that can substantially influence video analysis and motion-based vision research by matching state-of-the-art results.
