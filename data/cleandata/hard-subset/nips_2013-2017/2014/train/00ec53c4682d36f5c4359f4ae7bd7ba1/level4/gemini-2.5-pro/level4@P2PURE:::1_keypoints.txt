- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for video: a spatial stream on single frames and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused for final prediction.  
- Each stream is trained independently and combined (e.g., weighted average) to classify videos.  
- Evaluated on two standard action recognition datasets, achieving results comparable to state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature methods.  
- Key strength: simple yet effective application of ConvNets to video, delivering excellent performance.  
- Key strength: demonstrates the value of using optical flow as an input representation for deep networks in action recognition.  
- Main weakness: cannot capture long-range temporal dynamics, operating only on short fixed-length snippets (L=10 frames).  
- Experiments are well-designed, but the paper lacks an analysis of model error modes and failure cases.  
- Paper is clearly written with a thorough explanation of the model and architecture.  
- Originality: novel use of ConvNets on dense optical flow as a separate temporal stream.  
- Significance: provides a strong, simple baseline for video-based deep learning and points to combining learned and engineered features as a promising direction.
