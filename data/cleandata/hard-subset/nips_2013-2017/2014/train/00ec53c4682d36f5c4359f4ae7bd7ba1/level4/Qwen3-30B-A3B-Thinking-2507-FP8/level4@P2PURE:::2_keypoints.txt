- Proposes a dual-stream CNN architecture combining a spatial network (RGB frames) and a temporal network (dense optical flow) for video action classification.  
- Significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based action recognition methods and achieves state-of-the-art on two standard datasets.  
- Adapts the standard image ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow frames and fuses it with a single-frame ConvNet.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the results are compelling.  
- Demonstrates multiple training strategies for optical-flow ConvNets with clear empirical evidence that optical flow improves performance.  
- Minor issue: the paper does not clearly explain why the spatial network outperforms the similar-architecture model in [13].  
- Minor suggestion: a comparison of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream would be valuable.  
- Overall assessment: a strong empirical contribution of significant interest to video classification researchers.
