- Proposes a two-stream CNN architecture combining a spatial stream (single frames) and a temporal stream (short sequences of dense optical flow).
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on standard video action benchmarks, significantly outperforming prior ConvNet-based methods.
- Main novelty is adapting existing CNN pipelines to dense optical flow and integrating it with frame-based processing rather than proposing a fundamentally new architecture.
- Provides a thorough empirical evaluation of multiple training strategies for optical-flow CNNs, showing optical flow is an effective complementary input for action recognition.
- Experimental rigor, clarity of results, and empirical exploration are strong and provide valuable insights for the community.
- Minor issue 1: The spatial stream achieves much higher accuracy than the similar-architecture models in [13]; the discrepancy should be explained.
- Minor issue 2: A comparative analysis of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream is needed given sensitivity to temporal resolution.
- Overall: A well-executed, clearly written empirical study that represents a solid advancement and useful reference for CNN-based video analysis.
