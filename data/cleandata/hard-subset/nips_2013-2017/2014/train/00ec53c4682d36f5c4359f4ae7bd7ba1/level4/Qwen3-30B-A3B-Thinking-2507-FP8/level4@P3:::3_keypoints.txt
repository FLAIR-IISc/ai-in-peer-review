- Proposes a two-stream convolutional architecture that decouples spatial (RGB) and temporal (optical flow) processing and fuses them via late fusion.  
- Key insight: explicitly feeding optical flow to a CNN captures motion better than expecting networks to learn motion from stacked RGB frames.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to improve training on limited video datasets by jointly optimizing multiple action recognition tasks.  
- Builds on the two-stream hypothesis and extends image CNN advances to the video domain.  
- Technically sound with clear architecture, precise writing, and reproducible experimental methodology.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies on optical flow configurations (L=5 vs L=10, trajectory vs flow stacking, bi-directional flow).  
- Strong empirical results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming prior deep-learning video approaches and rivaling shallow state-of-the-art.  
- Original contribution: two-stream design is a significant departure from prior deep video models; multi-task aspect is less novel but useful.  
- Acknowledged limitations: requires camera motion compensation and lacks spatial pooling over trajectories.  
- Misses citing some relevant prior works (BMVC 2010; CVPR 2011).  
- Presentation could better integrate the two-stream framework and multi-task learning into a single cohesive narrative.  
- Does not fully explain mechanistically why optical flow outperforms stacked RGB beyond empirical observation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, significant paper; recommend acceptance (NIPS 2014).
