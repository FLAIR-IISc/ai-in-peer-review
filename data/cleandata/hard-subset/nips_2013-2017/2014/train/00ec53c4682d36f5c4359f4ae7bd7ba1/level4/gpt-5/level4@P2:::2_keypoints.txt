- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture: a spatial stream on RGB frames for appearance and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow for motion.  
- Fuses the two streams at the score level to combine appearance and motion cues.  
- Introduces multi-task learning across datasets to alleviate limited video training data.  
- Achieves substantial improvements over prior ConvNet-based methods on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 and reaches performance comparable to hand-crafted feature state of the art.  
- Provides a careful experimental study comparing optical flow input constructions (stacking length, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction).  
- Shows clear gains from using optical flow inputs and demonstrates strong complementarity between spatial and temporal cues.  
- Demonstrates the value of late fusion for combining streams.  
- Overall, a solid empirical contribution likely to interest video classification researchers.  
- Minor critique: the spatial stream’s advantage over similar prior models is not fully analyzed—factors like data augmentation, input sampling, training schedules, and pretraining/fine-tuning should be examined.  
- Minor suggestion: compare different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream (uniform vs. random temporal sampling, number of frames, crop policies) to assess impact and reproducibility.
