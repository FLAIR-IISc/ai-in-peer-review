- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: a spatial net (single appearance frame) and a temporal net (stacked dense optical flow), trained separately and combined by weighted averaging.  
- Reviews related work thoroughly, emphasizing complementary appearance vs. motion cues and benefits of ImageNet pretraining and multi-task learning.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive state-of-the-art performance.  
- Explores input variations (stacking multiple flows, trajectory stacking, mean displacement subtraction) and shows these improve performance.  
- Novel contribution: applying convolutional nets directly to optical flow features.  
- Strength: simplicity and effectiveness, matching or approaching methods using heavy hand-crafted features.  
- Limitation: models only fixed-length adjacent frames and does not capture long-range or whole-video action sequences.  
- Experiments are solid but lack analysis of error modes, failure cases, or which classes/videos fail.  
- Paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.  
- Potential impact: promising direction for leveraging motion in video/vision tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective model, valuable use of optical flow, simplicity, thorough related work and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited sequence modeling, missing in-depth error analysis, and uncertain generalization to other datasets/tasks.
