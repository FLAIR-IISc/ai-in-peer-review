- Proposes a two-stream CNN: one stream on raw frames, one on stacked optical-flow fields, trained independently with video-level predictions obtained by averaging outputs.
- Evaluated on two datasets and achieves results that match or closely approach state-of-the-art methods using hand-engineered features.
- Strength: simple, effective application of CNNs to video that yields strong performance.
- Strength: demonstrates that optical flow is a valuable input representation for deep models in action recognition.
- Weakness: only captures short temporal windows (fixed length L=10) and does not model long-range temporal dependencies.
- Experiments are well designed but lack analysis of failure modes (e.g., class confusions, rapid camera motion, subtle actions, occlusions).
- Paper is clearly written and explains the model thoroughly.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks directly to optical-flow inputs.
- Significance: likely to influence video and motion research by showing a straightforward, effective use of optical flow with CNNs.
