- Introduces a novel neural architecture that fuses a frame-based ConvNet with a ConvNet trained on short stacks of dense optical flow for action classification.
- Uses optical-flow stacks as a new input modality and adapts standard image-classification ConvNet pipelines to this modality.
- Substantially outperforms other ConvNet-based action recognition methods and matches state-of-the-art on two widely used video datasets.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and results are compelling.
- Provides multiple strategies for training ConvNets on optical flow and clear evidence of the benefits of optical flow input.
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial (frame) network performs much better than the similar architectures in prior work [13].
- Minor suggestion: compare different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.
- Overall assessment: strong empirical paper of interest to video-classification researchers.
