This paper presents a new neural network architecture for human action recognition in videos. The model combines predictions from two convolutional neural networks: a spatial stream network trained on individual video frames and a temporal stream network trained on stacks of dense optical flow fields. The resulting two-stream model significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches to action recognition and achieves state-of-the-art performance on two standard video classification datasets.

While the paper's core contribution is applying the standard ConvNet pipeline to a new modality (dense optical flow) and fusing it with a single-frame network, the experimental evaluation is thorough and the results are impressive. A key strength of the work is the systematic comparison of different strategies for training a ConvNet on optical flow, which clearly demonstrates the benefits of this input representation.

I have a few minor comments:

- It is unclear why the spatial network substantially outperforms the models from [13], given that the architectures are described as being similar. A brief discussion of potential reasons for this performance gap would be beneficial.

- The analysis could be strengthened by a comparison of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.

This is a strong empirical paper that will be of great interest to researchers working on video classification with convolutional networks.
