- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a spatial network (single RGB frames) and a temporal network (short sequences of dense optical flow) for action recognition.
- Temporal stream uses stacked dense optical flow as input; spatial stream uses single video frames.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on two standard video classification datasets, significantly outperforming other ConvNet-based methods.
- Thorough experiments, including comparisons of different ways to train ConvNets on optical flow, showing clear benefits of optical-flow input.
- Main technical contribution is adapting the standard image ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow and fusing it with a spatial stream.
- Minor concern: the large performance gap between the spatial network and the similar-architecture model in [13] is not fully explained.
- Suggested addition: compare different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.
- Overall assessment: well-executed empirical paper of strong interest to researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
