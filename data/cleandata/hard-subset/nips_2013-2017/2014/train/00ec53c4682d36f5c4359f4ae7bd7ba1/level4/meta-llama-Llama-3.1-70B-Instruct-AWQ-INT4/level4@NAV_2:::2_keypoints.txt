- Contributions: proposes a two-stream ConvNet (spatial + temporal), shows ConvNet on multi-frame dense optical flow performs well with limited data, and uses multitask learning to augment training data and improve performance.  
- Evaluation: tested on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 and is competitive with the state of the art.  
- Motivation/related work: well-motivated, connects to prior video representation and action recognition literature, and highlights challenges of capturing appearance vs. motion.  
- Key technical idea: decouples spatial and temporal recognition—spatial stream can be ImageNet pre-trained; temporal stream trained on stacked dense optical flow.  
- Optical flow details: uses multi-frame dense optical flow computed via intensity/gradient constancy and smoothness of displacement.  
- Experimental thoroughness: compares architectures and input configurations (stacked flows, trajectory stacking, mean subtraction) across datasets.  
- Results: significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches and matches current state-of-the-art on the two benchmarks.  
- Strengths: thorough experiments, clear presentation, well-motivated architecture, and detailed related-work discussion.  
- Minor issues: unclear why spatial net outperforms similarly architected prior work [14]; no comparison of different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream.  
- Technical quality: sound methodology, well-supported claims, and explicit discussion of method limitations.  
- Clarity: well-written with helpful figures and tables that clarify the approach and results.  
- Originality: significant contribution by combining two ConvNets and demonstrating effective temporal modeling via optical flow.  
- Significance: has potential impact on video action recognition and to guide future system development.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel architecture, thorough evaluation on standard datasets, strong motivation, and comprehensive related work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: does not substantially improve SOTA, lacks analysis of computational complexity, and omits comparisons of spatial frame sampling schemes.
