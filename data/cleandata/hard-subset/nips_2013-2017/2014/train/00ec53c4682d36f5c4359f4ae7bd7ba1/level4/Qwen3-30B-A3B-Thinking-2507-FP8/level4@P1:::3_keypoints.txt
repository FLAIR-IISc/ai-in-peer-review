- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that decouples spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) processing with late fusion.  
- Temporal stream processes dense optical flow (instead of raw frame stacks), showing explicit motion representation outperforms implicit motion learning.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to combine UCF-101 and HMDB-51 training data to overcome limited video-data sizes.  
- Strong empirical results: fused model achieves 89.2% on UCF-101 and 70.4% on HMDB-51, >20% better than prior deep methods and comparable to hand-crafted features.  
- Technically sound and well-executed, with sufficient implementation detail for reproducibility (flow precomputation, multi-GPU training).  
- Architecture motivated by two-stream hypothesis and validated through thorough ablation studies.  
- Key experimental findings: multi-frame optical flow outperforms single-frame input; trajectory stacking gives marginal benefit over optical flow stacking; multi-task learning is effective.  
- Main strengths: clear technical contribution, strong empirical validation, practical relevance, and a principled solution to leveraging motion in deep learning.  
- Writing is generally clear and organized.  
- Abstract and introduction should more tightly unify the two-stream framework and multi-task learning as interdependent contributions.  
- Related-work omissions: should cite Le et al. (CVPR 2011) and Wang et al. (BMVC 2010) to better contextualize the use of optical flow.  
- Discussion of optical flow should better distinguish the ConvNet’s generalization from hand-crafted motion features (HOF, MBH) and earlier motion-based methods.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, impactful work that meets NIPS criteria; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to citations and thematic focus.
