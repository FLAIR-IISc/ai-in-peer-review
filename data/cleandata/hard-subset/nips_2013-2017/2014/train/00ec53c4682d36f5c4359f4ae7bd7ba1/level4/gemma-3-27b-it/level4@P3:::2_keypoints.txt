- Proposes a two-stream CNN that fuses spatial (RGB) and temporal (dense optical flow) streams, achieving strong performance on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, competitive with state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.

- Extends ConvNet success to video by decoupling spatial and temporal processing, inspired by the two-stream hypothesis, enabling ImageNet pretraining and training directly on dense optical flow; also explores multi-task learning.

- Strength: strong empirical results with significant improvement over prior deep-learning action recognition methods.

- Strength: well-motivated, logical architecture that leverages complementary spatial and temporal information.

- Strength: thorough ablation study examining temporal inputs (stacking flow, trajectories, bi-directional flow) and fusion strategies.

- Strength: effective use of ImageNet pretraining and multi-task learning to boost generalization.

- Strength: connection to biological vision (two-stream hypothesis) provides useful motivation.

- Weakness: limited novelty—core components (ConvNets, optical flow, late fusion) are established; main contribution is their combination.

- Weakness: insufficient explanation for why the spatial network outperforms similar prior ConvNet approaches despite similar architectures.

- Weakness: limited discussion/experimentation on frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.

- Weakness: computational cost of dense optical flow is high and trade-offs are not addressed.

- For acceptance: solid, well-executed empirical study, clear writing, reproducible results, and valuable ablation insights.

- Against acceptance: lack of novel core techniques, missing analysis of computational limits and spatial network performance.

- Overall: strong paper with valuable contributions through effective combination and thorough evaluation; recommendation: Accept.
