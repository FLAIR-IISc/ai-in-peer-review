- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that decouples spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) processing for video action recognition.  
- Trains the temporal stream on multi-frame dense optical flow to capture motion information effectively.  
- Uses multi-task learning across action classification datasets to improve generalization with limited data.  
- Evaluated on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, achieving competitive results with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Builds on deep ConvNet advances from image classification and addresses prior failures of frame-stacking approaches.  
- Inspiration drawn from the two-streams hypothesis in human visual cortex, separating spatial and temporal pathways.  
- Demonstrates that ConvNets trained on dense optical flow can bridge hand-crafted features and end-to-end learning.  
- Multi-task learning is shown to be a practical technique for performance gains when labeled data is scarce.  
- Experiments are well-designed with ablation studies (e.g., number of stacked flow frames) and fair comparisons.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides reproducible implementation details.  
- Focuses on short-term temporal relationships (fixed-length L=10), potentially missing long-range dependencies.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of failure cases and error modes to identify challenging actions or video characteristics.  
- Brief mention of the optical flow computation method without discussing its limitations or alternatives.  
- Temporal stream lacks investigation of potential pre-training on large optical flow datasets.  
- Contribution is original in integrating deep ConvNets with dense optical flow and multi-task learning for action recognition.  
- Overall assessment: significant and influential advance with strengths outweighing weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
