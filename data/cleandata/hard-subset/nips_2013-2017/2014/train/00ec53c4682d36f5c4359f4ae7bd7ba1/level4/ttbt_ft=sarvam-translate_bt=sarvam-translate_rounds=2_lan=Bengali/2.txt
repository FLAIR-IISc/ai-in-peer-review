This paper presents a new neural network architecture for classifying human activity video. The model combines predictions from two convolutional neural networks: one trained on a single video frame and the other trained on a small sequence of dense optical flow images. This model performs significantly better than other ConvNet-based methods for action recognition and matches current state-of-the-art methods on two standard video classification datasets.

Although this paper primarily applies a standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to a new type of data (dense optical flow frames) and combines it with a single frame ConvNet, the experiments are thorough and the results are impressive. It was interesting to see the various ways ConvNet can be trained on optical flow, compared to the results clearly showing the advantages of using optical flow as input.

I have a few minor comments:
- It is not clear why the architectures are so much better than models from spatial networks [13] despite being similar.
- It would be interesting to see a comparison of different sampling schemes for frames fed into the spatial flow. This would be a good empirical paper that would be of interest to anyone working on video classification with ConvNet.
