This paper proposes new methods for video classification with deep learning, namely a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation details, and procedures are presented in good detail. The paper is clear, well written, and makes a fair case for the proposed methods by demonstrating strong empirical results.

These methods are novel. Although the paper builds on the framework of [13], the two-stream architecture is particularly compelling because it shows that incorporating optical flow yields significant performance gains—a result that will interest many researchers. While the presentation is clear, the paper feels split across multiple contributions rather than tightly centered on a single theme. Nevertheless, the results show impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with the state of the art.

Suggestions for improvement:
1. Since most of the significance lies in the two-stream design and the incorporation of optical flow, the abstract and introduction should present a more focused theme around these contributions.
2. Add the following missing citations:
   - Deep learning: “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis,” CVPR 2011.
   - Computer vision: “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition,” BMVC 2010.

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions, with interesting ideas that complement core approaches in deep learning. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, conditional on addressing the suggested improvements.
