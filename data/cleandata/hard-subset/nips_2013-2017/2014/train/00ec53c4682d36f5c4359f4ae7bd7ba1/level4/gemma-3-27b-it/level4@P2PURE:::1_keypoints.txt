- Introduces a two-stream CNN model for video: one network on appearance frames and one on dense optical flow stacks, trained independently and fused by weighted averaging for video-level prediction.
- Achieves strong performance on two datasets, matching or approaching state-of-the-art methods that use hand-crafted features.
- Demonstrates that straightforward CNNs can be competitive for video tasks without elaborate engineering.
- Novel integration of optical flow as CNN input, showing the continued value of hand-crafted motion representations.
- Networks are trained independently per modality, then combined with learned fusion weights.
- Main limitation: processes only fixed-length segments (L=10) and lacks explicit modeling of temporal relationships across entire videos.
- Experiments are well-designed, but additional error-mode analysis (misclassified classes, challenging video types) would give further insight.
- Paper is clearly written and the model is well-explained.
- Significant potential impact on video and motion analysis research by providing a simple, effective CNN-based approach.
