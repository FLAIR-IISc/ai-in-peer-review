- Proposes a two-stream CNN for video classification: one RGB (appearance) stream and one stacked optical flow (motion) stream, fused by averaging.  
- Simple, effective architecture applying CNNs to both appearance and motion yields strong performance.  
- Novel input representation: precomputed optical flow stacks as a complementary CNN input.  
- Achieves competitive results on benchmarks, rivaling hand-crafted feature methods.  
- Main weakness: limited temporal modeling—operates on short fixed-length clips (L=10) and misses long-range dependencies.  
- Experimental design is solid with appropriate baselines and a well-motivated fusion strategy.  
- Lacks detailed error analysis, e.g., per-class accuracy breakdowns and confusion matrices.  
- Missing characterization of robustness to video attributes (camera motion, low light, motion blur, occlusion, rapid cuts).  
- Needs sensitivity analysis to clip length L and stride to understand temporal context vs. performance trade-offs.  
- Paper is clearly written and reproducible with sufficient architectural and training details.  
- Originality: explicitly incorporating optical flow stacks into CNNs for action recognition is a notable contribution.  
- Significance: bridges hand-crafted motion features and learned representations, likely to influence future video understanding work.  
- Suggest exploring long-term temporal modeling (temporal pooling, RNNs/attention, hierarchical aggregation).  
- Suggest adding detailed error/robustness analyses and qualitative visualizations of successes/failures for each stream.  
- Suggest investigating alternative fusion strategies and ablation studies (number of flow frames, flow algorithms, data augmentation).  
- Overall: a clear, well-executed, and impactful paper; addressing temporal modeling and error analysis would further strengthen it.
