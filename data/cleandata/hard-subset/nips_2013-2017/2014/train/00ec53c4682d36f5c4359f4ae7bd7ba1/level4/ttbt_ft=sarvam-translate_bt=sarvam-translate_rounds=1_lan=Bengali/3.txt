The paper proposes several new approaches for video classification using deep learning, namely the two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methods, implementations, and processes are described in detail. The paper is clear, well-written, and presents a fair argument for the proposed methods, showing good results.

These methods are new. Although the paper improves upon the structure of [13], the two-stream framework is very interesting because it shows that optical flow components add significant improvements. This is a result that many researchers will be interested in. The paper is clearly presented, but it seems divided across multiple contributions, rather than having a single theme. Ultimately, the results provide a rather impressive improvement over [13] and are competitive with state-of-the-art technology.

Some suggestions for improving the paper:
1. It seems that most of the emphasis is focused on two-stream and optical flow inclusion; the summary and introduction could have been more focused on the theme.
2. Citations are missing in deep learning:
Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
Citation missing in computer vision:
Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC, 2010.

The paper seems to make significant practical contributions, including some interesting concepts that complement the core ideas of deep learning. I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, provided that the authors provide the proposed improvements.
