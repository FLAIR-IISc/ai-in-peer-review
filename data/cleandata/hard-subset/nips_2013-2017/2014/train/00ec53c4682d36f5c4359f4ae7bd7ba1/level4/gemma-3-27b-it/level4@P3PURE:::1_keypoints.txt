- Proposes a two-stream CNN: one network for an appearance frame and one for a stack of dense optical flow, trained independently with final prediction as a weighted average.  
- Evaluated on two datasets, achieving impressive results that match or approach state-of-the-art methods using hand-crafted features.  
- Strength: Straightforward application of CNNs that attains strong performance.  
- Strength: Demonstrates that optical flow remains a valuable input for action recognition, an important contribution.  
- Weakness: Does not explicitly model long-range temporal relationships, only fixed-length segments (L=10).  
- Experiments are well-designed; the paper would benefit from an error-mode analysis to identify consistently misclassified classes or challenging video types.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.  
- Originality: Novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow features.  
- Significance: Simple, effective approach with potential impact on video and motion research; using optical flow as CNN input is a notable contribution.
