This paper presents a novel neural network architecture for classifying videos of human actions, achieving state-of-the-art results on two standard video classification datasets. The proposed model combines the predictions of two convolutional neural networks (ConvNets): one trained on individual video frames and the other on short sequences of dense optical flow images. This approach significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods for action recognition.

Although the paper essentially adapts the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to a new type of data (dense optical flow frames) and combines it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experiments are thorough, and the results are impressive. The authors' comprehensive evaluation of different ways to train a ConvNet on optical flow is particularly noteworthy, as the results clearly demonstrate the benefits of using optical flow as input.

I have a few minor comments to further improve the paper. Firstly, it is not entirely clear why the spatial network outperforms the models from [13] given the similarities in their architectures. A more in-depth analysis of this aspect would be beneficial. Secondly, it would be interesting to see a comparison of different frame sampling schemes for the spatial stream. This would provide additional insights into the model's behavior and robustness.

Overall, this is a well-executed empirical paper that will be of significant interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
