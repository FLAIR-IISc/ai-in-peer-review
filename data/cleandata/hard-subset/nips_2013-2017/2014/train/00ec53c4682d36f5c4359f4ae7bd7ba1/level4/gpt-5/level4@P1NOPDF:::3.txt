This paper proposes new deep-learning methods for video classification, notably a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning setup. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly. The writing is clear, and the empirical evaluation makes a convincing case for the proposed approaches by demonstrating strong performance.

The contributions appear novel. Although the work builds on the framework of [13], the two-stream design is particularly compelling: incorporating optical flow yields substantial gains. This is a result of broad interest to the community. While the presentation is clear, the paper feels somewhat split across multiple contributions rather than centered on a single unifying theme. Nevertheless, the reported results show impressive improvements over [13] and are competitive with the state of the art.

Suggestions for improvement:
1. Because most of the significance comes from the two-stream formulation and the use of optical flow, the abstract and introduction should be more focused around this central theme.
2. Add the following missing citations:
   - In deep learning:
     Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis. CVPR 2011.
   - In computer vision:
     Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition. BMVC 2010.

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions, with interesting ideas that complement core directions in deep learning. I recommend acceptance to NIPS 2014, conditional on addressing the above suggestions.
