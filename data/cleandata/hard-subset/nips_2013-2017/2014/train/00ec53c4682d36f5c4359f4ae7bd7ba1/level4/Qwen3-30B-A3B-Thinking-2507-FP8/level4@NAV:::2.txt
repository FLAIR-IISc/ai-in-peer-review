

This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture for human action classification in videos. The proposed model integrates predictions from two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames and another trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images. The approach achieves superior performance compared to other ConvNet-based action recognition methods and attains state-of-the-art results on two widely used video classification benchmarks.

While the methodology builds upon established ConvNet image classification techniques by applying them to dense optical flow data and combining it with a single-frame network, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the results are compelling. The inclusion of multiple training approaches for ConvNets on optical flow, with clear empirical evidence of its advantages, is particularly valuable.

The following minor points should be addressed:
- The substantial performance advantage of the spatial network over the models in [13] requires clarification, given the architectural similarities.
- A comparative analysis of diverse frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream would provide further insight.

This well-executed empirical study will be of significant interest to researchers focused on ConvNet-based video classification.
