This paper proposes a model for solving discriminative tasks with video inputs, consisting of two convolutional nets. The input to one net is an appearance frame, while the input to the second net is a stack of densely computed optical flow features. Each pathway is trained separately to classify its input, and the prediction for a video is obtained by taking a weighted average of the predictions made by each net.

The model is evaluated on two datasets, yielding impressive results that match or come close to state-of-the-art methods using intensively hand-crafted features.

Strengths:

- The model is a simple application of convolutional nets that achieves good results.
- Unlike previous deep learning models, this paper demonstrates the effectiveness of using optical flow (or other hand-crafted features) for vision tasks, rather than relying solely on learning features from pixels directly. This is an important contribution, particularly for action recognition, where optical flow fields are shown to be a useful input representation.

Weaknesses:

- The model only considers fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames and does not account for the overall sequence of actions throughout the entire video.

Quality:

The experiments are well-designed, but it would be more insightful if the authors included an analysis of the error modes. For instance, are there specific classes or groups of classes that the model struggles to classify accurately? Can the authors characterize the types of videos on which the model performs poorly?

Clarity:

The paper is clearly written, and the model is well-explained.

Originality:

The application of a convolutional net to optical flow features is a novel contribution.

Significance:

This approach has the potential to significantly impact research in using videos and motion for various vision problems. The model is a simple yet effective application of convolutional nets to video data, yielding promising results. The use of optical flow features as inputs to a convolutional net is a novel contribution that could inspire further research in this area.
