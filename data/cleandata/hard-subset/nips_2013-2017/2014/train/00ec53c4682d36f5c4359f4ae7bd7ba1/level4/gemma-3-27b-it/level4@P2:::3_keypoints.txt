- Proposes a two-stream convolutional network (spatial and temporal) to integrate appearance and motion for video action recognition.
- Trains the temporal stream on multi-frame dense optical flow and shows it works even with limited training data.
- Explores multi-task learning across different action datasets to boost performance.
- Novel idea: decoupling spatial and temporal processing, motivated by the two-stream hypothesis from neuroscience.
- Clear, well-written paper with thorough methodology and implementation details for reproducibility.
- Strong empirical gains: temporal stream improves performance, outperforming prior deep learning [14] and competing with hand-crafted features [20,26].
- Comprehensive evaluation on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 with analyses of different configurations.
- Paper feels fragmented, presenting several contributions without a single strong unifying narrative.
- Missing citations: should include Le et al. (2011) and Wang et al. (2010) in related work.
- Lacks sufficient discussion of limitations and directions for future research.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective approach with compelling results and thorough evaluation on an important problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak overarching theme and omission of key related work.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; strengths outweigh weaknesses but needs tightening and additional citations.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on refining abstract/introduction, adding missing citations, and expanding limitations/future work discussion.
