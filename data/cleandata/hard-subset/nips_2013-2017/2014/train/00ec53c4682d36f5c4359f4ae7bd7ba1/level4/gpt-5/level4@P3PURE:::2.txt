Summary:
This paper proposes a new neural network architecture for classifying human actions in videos. The model combines two convolutional neural networks: a spatial network trained on individual video frames and a temporal network trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images. The approach significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based methods for action recognition and matches the current state of the art on two standard video classification datasets.

Strengths:
- Although the method essentially applies the standard ConvNet image-classification pipeline to a new input modality (dense optical flow) and fuses it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental evaluation is thorough and the results are impressive.
- The paper systematically explores several ways to train a ConvNet on optical flow and clearly shows the benefits of using optical flow as input.

Minor comments:
- It is not clear why the spatial network performs substantially better than the models from [13], given that the architectures are similar.
- It would be interesting to see a comparison of different sampling schemes for selecting frames fed into the spatial stream.

Overall:
This is a solid empirical paper that will be of interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
