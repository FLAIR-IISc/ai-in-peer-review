- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition, fusing a spatial stream (single frames) and a temporal stream (stacked dense optical flow).
- Temporal stream trains a ConvNet on stacks of dense optical flow fields to capture motion information.
- Fusing the two streams yields significant improvements over prior ConvNet-based methods and achieves state-of-the-art results on two benchmarks.
- Paper distinguished by thorough experimental validation and impressive empirical results.
- Ablation study on the temporal stream compares training methods on optical flow and shows clear benefits of using optical flow as an input modality.
- Minor comment: clarify why the spatial network outperforms the model in [13] despite architectural similarities.
- Minor comment: strengthen analysis by comparing different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.
- Overall assessment: a strong empirical paper of high interest to researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
