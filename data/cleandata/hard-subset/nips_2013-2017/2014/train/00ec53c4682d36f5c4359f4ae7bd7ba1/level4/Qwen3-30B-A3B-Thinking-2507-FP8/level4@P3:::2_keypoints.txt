- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet that explicitly separates spatial (single-frame, ImageNet-pretrained) and temporal (multi-frame dense optical flow) processing for video action recognition.  
- Trains one stream on RGB frames and another on stacked optical flow, then fuses predictions to capture appearance and motion separately.  
- Achieves strong empirical gains: 81.5% on UCF-101 and 75.3% on HMDB-51 for the temporal stream, significantly outperforming prior deep methods and matching hand-crafted approaches.  
- Demonstrates that optical flow input plus multi-task learning overcomes video data scarcity that hindered prior video ConvNets.  
- Ablation studies rigorously show key design choices: L=10 flow stacking, mean flow subtraction, and multi-task learning are critical to performance.  
- Multi-task learning framework effectively leverages multiple datasets without manual class alignment, a novel and practically useful contribution.  
- The temporal stream generalizes hand-crafted motion descriptors (e.g., HOF, MBH), showing ConvNets can learn hierarchical motion features.  
- The approach avoids complex spatio-temporal filters by decoupling spatial and temporal processing, simplifying model design.  
- Experiments are validated across standard benchmarks with three train-test splits, making results statistically meaningful and reproducible.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear technical explanations, thorough implementation details, helpful figures, and effective tables isolating design choices.  
- Minor clarity issue: does not explicitly attribute spatial-stream gains to ImageNet pre-training versus differences from [14].  
- Methodological gap: lacks analysis explaining why trajectory stacking underperforms optical flow stacking.  
- Practical significance could be strengthened by reporting runtime/deployment speed comparisons versus hand-crafted pipelines.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, impactful contribution that meaningfully advances deep video recognition; minor issues are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
