- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial stream on frames and temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused at score level, achieving strong results on benchmarks.  
- Adapts standard image-classification ConvNet pipeline to optical flow inputs and combines it with a single-frame ConvNet.  
- Systematically evaluates optical-flow input constructions and empirically supports using stacked flow fields.  
- Shows explicit optical flow input outperforms relying on ConvNets to learn motion from raw RGB stacks.  

Strengths:
- Clear and effective spatial/temporal architectural decomposition.  
- Careful experimental study of optical-flow design choices (stack length, trajectory vs. location stacking, mean subtraction, directional flow).  
- Strong empirical performance: substantial improvement over prior ConvNet methods and competitive with state of the art.  
- Empirical focus and comparisons make findings practical and reproducible for practitioners.  

Weaknesses / suggestions:
- Unclear why the spatial network outperforms the similar model in [13]; discussion of training details or augmentation is missing.  
- Missing comparison of different frame-sampling schemes for the spatial stream to assess temporal sampling impact.  

Review-criteria summary:
- Quality: technically sound with comprehensive experiments and well-supported claims.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; experimental setup enables reproduction.  
- Originality: conceptually incremental but the two-stream formulation plus thorough empirical exploration is a useful contribution.  
- Significance: high; establishes a strong baseline and is likely influential for video ConvNet research.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Large empirical gains and competitive SOTA performance.  
- Thorough experiments that yield clear insights about motion representations.  
- Practical, impactful method for video classification community.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited conceptual novelty—mainly adapts existing ConvNet practices to optical flow and fuses two streams.  
- Some ablations (e.g., spatial frame sampling) are missing and the gap to [13] is unexplained.  

Overall:
- Strong empirical paper with clear takeaways and high relevance to researchers and practitioners in video classification.
