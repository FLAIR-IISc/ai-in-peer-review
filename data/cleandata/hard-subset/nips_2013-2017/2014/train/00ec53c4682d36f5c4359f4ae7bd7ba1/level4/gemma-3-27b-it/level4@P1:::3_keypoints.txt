- Proposes novel video classification methods: a two-stream formulation (spatial + temporal) and multi-task learning.
- Methodology, implementation, and experiments are described in detail and the paper is clearly written.
- Two-stream framework is particularly interesting; incorporating optical flow yields significant performance gains.
- Demonstrates strong results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, surpassing Karpathy et al. [14] and nearing state-of-the-art hand-crafted methods.
- Represents a worthwhile, broadly interesting contribution to video deep learning.
- Paper covers multiple contributions (two-stream, multi-task, input configurations), which slightly dilutes the central theme.
- Recommend tightening abstract/introduction to emphasize the primary contribution: the two-stream network and optical flow benefits.
- Missing relevant citations that should be acknowledged (e.g., Le et al. [18], Wang et al. [27]) and possibly others.
- Related work discussion should be better integrated into the introduction to clarify novelty and positioning.
- Strengths: strong empirical performance, architectural novelty (effective optical-flow ConvNet), clear presentation, thorough evaluation including ablations.
- Weaknesses: diluted focus due to multiple contributions and incomplete citation of prior work.
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS 2014), contingent on addressing focus and citation concerns.
