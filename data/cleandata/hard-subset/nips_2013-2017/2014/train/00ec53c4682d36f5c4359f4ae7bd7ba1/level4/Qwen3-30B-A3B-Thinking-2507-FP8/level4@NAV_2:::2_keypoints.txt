- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a spatial stream (single frames) and a temporal stream (dense optical flow) for video action recognition.  
- Shows significant improvement over prior ConvNet-based video methods and achieves competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Adapts standard image-classification ConvNet techniques to optical flow as a novel input modality.  
- Thorough experiments demonstrate clear benefits of explicitly using motion information via optical flow.  
- Comprehensively compares optical flow input configurations, finding that stacking multiple flow frames (L=10) and mean flow subtraction boost performance.  
- Uses multi-task learning to combine datasets, yielding notable gains on smaller datasets such as HMDB-51.  
- Experimental design and results are strong, supporting the complementary nature of spatial and temporal streams.  
- Minor suggestion 1: provide a more detailed explanation for why the ImageNet-trained spatial stream outperforms the similar architecture results reported in [14].  
- Minor suggestion 2: include an analysis of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream to offer additional insights.
