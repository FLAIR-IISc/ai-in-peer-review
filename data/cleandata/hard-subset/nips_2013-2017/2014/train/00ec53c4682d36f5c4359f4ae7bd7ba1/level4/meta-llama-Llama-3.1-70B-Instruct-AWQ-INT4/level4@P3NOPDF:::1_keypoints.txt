- Model: two independent CNN pathways—one on an appearance frame, one on stacked optical flow—whose predictions are combined by a weighted average.  
- Results: evaluated on two datasets, achieving impressive performance comparable to or better than state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature methods.  
- Strength: simple yet effective use of CNNs for video-based discriminative tasks.  
- Strength: novel and significant use of optical flow as input to CNNs, highlighting the value of domain knowledge.  
- Weakness: limited temporal context—uses a fixed short sequence (L=10) and cannot model long-range dependencies.  
- Experiments: well-designed and convincing, but would benefit from an error-mode analysis (per-class failures and types of videos the model struggles on).  
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and technical ideas are well explained.  
- Originality: novel application of CNNs to optical flow and emphasis on combining domain-specific features with deep learning.  
- Significance: approach may substantially influence video vision research and inspire further work integrating CNNs with domain knowledge.
