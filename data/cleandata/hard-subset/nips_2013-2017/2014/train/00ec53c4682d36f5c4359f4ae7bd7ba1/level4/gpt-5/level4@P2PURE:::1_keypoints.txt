- Proposes two-stream convolutional model: one net for a single appearance frame and one for a stack of dense optical flow; predictions fused by weighted average for video-level classification.
- Evaluated on two datasets and achieves results matching or close to state-of-the-art methods that use hand-crafted features.
- Strength: straightforward application of convnets that attains strong empirical performance.
- Strength: shows optical flow is a valuable input representation for action recognition, contrasting prior trends toward raw-pixel learning.
- Weakness: does not capture full video temporal dynamics, only models fixed-length (L=10) adjacent-frame segments.
- Experiments are well designed but lack error-mode analysis (e.g., consistently misclassified classes or poorly handled video types).
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow features.
- Significance: simple, promising approach likely to influence research on leveraging video and motion cues; optical flow as an impactful contribution.
