

The paper introduces novel approaches for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning framework. The methodology, implementation, and experimental procedures are described in sufficient detail, resulting in a well-written paper that clearly presents a compelling case for the proposed methods through strong empirical results.  

The work builds upon [13] but offers meaningful improvements, particularly in demonstrating that incorporating optical flow streams yields substantial gains—a finding that will be of significant interest to the computer vision community. While the results show impressive improvements over [13] and competitive performance with the state of the art, the paper feels fragmented across multiple contributions rather than cohesively unified around a single theme.  

To strengthen the submission, we recommend the following revisions:  
1. The abstract and introduction should more clearly articulate a unified central contribution, as the current focus on optical flow and two-stream enhancements currently dominates the narrative without a clear overarching theme.  
2. The authors should cite key foundational works in the field, including:  
   - *Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis*, CVPR 2011 (missing in deep learning context).  
   - *Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition*, BMVC 2010 (missing in computer vision context).  

The paper makes valuable practical contributions, with ideas that complement recent advances in deep learning for video analysis. I recommend acceptance at NIPS 2014 pending the authors’ incorporation of these minor revisions.
