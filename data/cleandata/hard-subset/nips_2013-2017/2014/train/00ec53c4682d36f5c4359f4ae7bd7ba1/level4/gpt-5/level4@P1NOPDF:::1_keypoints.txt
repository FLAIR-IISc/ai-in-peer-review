- Proposes two-stream convolutional model: one network on appearance frames, one on stacked dense optical flow, fused by weighted averaging.
- Evaluated on two datasets and achieves impressive results comparable to state-of-the-art methods using hand-crafted features.
- Strength: straightforward convnet application that yields strong empirical performance.
- Strength: demonstrates optical flow is a valuable input for action recognition, challenging the trend away from hand-crafted features.
- Weakness: models only fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames and does not capture long-range video sequence information.
- Quality: experiments are well designed but lack error-mode analysis (per-class failures or types of videos with poor performance).
- Clarity: paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional networks to optical flow features.
- Significance: simple, effective approach that could strongly influence work on using motion and videos for vision tasks.
