This paper proposes a two-stream convolutional network architecture for human action recognition in videos. The model fuses predictions from a spatial ConvNet trained on individual video frames and a temporal ConvNet trained on dense optical flow fields. The proposed method significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches and matches the state-of-the-art on two standard action recognition benchmarks.

While the core idea involves a straightforward application of the standard ConvNet pipeline to a new modality (dense optical flow) fused with a single-frame model, the paper is supported by thorough experiments and impressive results. The authors are commended for comparing several strategies for training on optical flow, with results that clearly demonstrate the benefits of this input representation.

I have a few minor comments:

- An explanation is needed for why the spatial network significantly outperforms the models from [13], given their architectural similarities.
- The analysis would be strengthened by a comparison of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.

This is a strong empirical paper that should be of interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
