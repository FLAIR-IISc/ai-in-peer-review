- Proposes a two-stream convolutional network: a spatial stream for RGB frames and a temporal stream for stacked optical flow, trained separately and fused for final prediction.
- Evaluated on two standard action recognition datasets, achieving performance comparable to or near state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature methods.
- Strength: simple, straightforward architecture that effectively applies convolutional networks to video analysis.
- Strength: demonstrates the value of incorporating engineered optical flow fields as a powerful input representation for learning motion within a deep learning framework.
- Limitation: does not model long-term temporal structure—operates on short, fixed-length clips (L=10 frames).
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed but lack analysis of failure cases or classes where the model underperforms.
- Paper is well-written and clear; model and experimental setup are effectively explained.
- Originality: main novelty is applying a ConvNet directly to pre-computed optical flow as a distinct motion input modality.
- Significance: likely to have strong impact on video understanding and motion analysis, opening new directions for applying deep learning to motion-based vision tasks.
