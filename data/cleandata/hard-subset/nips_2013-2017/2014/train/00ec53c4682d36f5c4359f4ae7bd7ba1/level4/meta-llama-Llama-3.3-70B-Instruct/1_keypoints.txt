- Proposes a dual CNN: one network on an appearance frame and one on a stack of densely computed optical flow features, fused by a weighted average of their predictions.
- Trains each network independently for classification before combining outputs.
- Uses optical flow fields as input to a convolutional net rather than learning features directly from pixels—a novel and significant contribution.
- Achieves impressive, competitive results on two datasets, rivaling state-of-the-art methods that use heavy hand-crafted features.
- Demonstrates a simple yet effective application of convolutional nets for video-based discriminative tasks.
- Major limitation: only models fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames, not the full temporal sequence of the video.
- Experimental design is robust but lacks detailed error analysis identifying struggling classes or challenging video types.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with concise explanation and strong potential impact on motion/video-based vision research.
