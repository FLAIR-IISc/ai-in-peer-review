- Dual-path convolutional model: one network for appearance frames and one for stacked dense optical flow, trained separately and fused by a weighted average; achieves strong results on two datasets, matching or approaching SOTA with hand-crafted features.  
- Simple CNN application yields high accuracy.  
- Demonstrates optical flow as a valuable input modality, challenging the trend of learning all features directly from pixels.  
- Limitation: processes only fixed-length sequences (L=10), ignoring longer-range temporal dynamics across full videos.  
- Experiments are well-executed but lack error analysis (e.g., per-class or video-type failure modes).  
- Paper is clearly written with a well-explained model.  
- Novelty in applying convolutional networks to optical flow features.  
- Significant: simple, effective approach with potential influence on future motion-driven video analysis.
