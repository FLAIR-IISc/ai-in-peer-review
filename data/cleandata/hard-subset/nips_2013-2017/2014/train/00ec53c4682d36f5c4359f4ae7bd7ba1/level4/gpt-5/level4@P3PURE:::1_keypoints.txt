- Proposes a two-stream convnet: one pathway on a single appearance frame and one on stacked dense optical flow; networks trained separately and combined by weighted averaging; evaluated on two datasets with near state-of-the-art results.
- Simple application of convolutional networks that yields strong empirical performance.
- Shows optical flow is a valuable input representation for action recognition, challenging pixel-only end-to-end learning trends.
- Does not capture long-range temporal structure—operates on fixed-length windows (L=10).
- Experiments are well designed but lack analysis of error modes (e.g., class-wise or video-type failures).
- Paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.
- Original in applying convolutional networks to optical flow features.
- Approach could significantly impact research using video and motion for vision problems.
