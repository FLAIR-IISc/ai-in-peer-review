The paper proposes a novel approach to video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The methodology, implementations, and procedures are described in good detail, making the paper clear and well-written. The authors present a strong case for their proposed methods, demonstrating good results that showcase the effectiveness of their approach.

The methods presented in the paper are novel and build upon the framework established in [13]. The two-stream framework, in particular, is noteworthy as it highlights the significant improvements gained by incorporating the optical flow component. This result is likely to be of interest to many researchers in the field. While the paper is well-presented, it appears to be split across multiple contributions rather than having a single, unified theme.

The results presented in the paper offer impressive improvements upon [13] and are competitive with the state-of-the-art. However, to further improve the paper, the authors could consider the following suggestions:

1. Refine the abstract and introduction to have a more focused theme, emphasizing the significance of the two-stream formulation and incorporation of optical flow.
2. Include missing citations in deep learning, such as "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis" (CVPR 2011).
3. Add missing citations in computer vision, including "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition" (BMVC, 2010).

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions and presents interesting ideas that complement the main ideas in deep learning. I would recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, conditional upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
