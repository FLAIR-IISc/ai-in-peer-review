The paper proposes a novel approach to video classification using deep learning, introducing a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The authors provide a detailed description of their methodology, implementations, and procedures, making the paper clear and well-written. The proposed methods are presented in a fair and convincing manner, with good results that demonstrate their effectiveness.

The novelty of the methods is a significant strength of the paper. Although the authors build upon the framework of [13], the two-stream framework is particularly interesting, as it highlights the importance of the optical flow component in achieving significant improvements. This result is likely to be of great interest to researchers in the field.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, but it appears to be split across multiple contributions rather than having a single, unified theme. Nevertheless, the results are impressive, offering substantial improvements over [13] and competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.

To further improve the paper, I suggest that the authors focus the abstract and introduction on the two-stream formulation and the incorporation of optical flow, as these are the most significant contributions. Additionally, the authors should include missing citations in deep learning, such as "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis" (CVPR 2011), and in computer vision, such as "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition" (BMVC 2010).

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions and interesting ideas that complement the main ideas in deep learning. I recommend accepting the paper at NIPS 2014, conditional upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to video classification using deep learning
* Detailed description of methodology, implementations, and procedures
* Good results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods
* Interesting and significant contributions to the field

Weaknesses:

* Paper appears to be split across multiple contributions rather than having a single theme
* Missing citations in deep learning and computer vision

Suggestions for improvement:

* Focus the abstract and introduction on the two-stream formulation and the incorporation of optical flow
* Include missing citations in deep learning and computer vision

* Conditional acceptance, pending the authors' address of the suggested improvements.
