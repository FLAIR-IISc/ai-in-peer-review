- Introduces novel video classification methods: a two-stream network plus a multi-task learning approach.  
- Methodology, implementation, and experiments are described thoroughly.  
- Paper is clearly written and demonstrates benefits of the proposed methods with strong experimental results.  
- Contributions are noteworthy, particularly the two-stream architecture integrating optical flow.  
- Two-stream/optical-flow integration yields significant performance gains and is of high interest to the community.  
- Work builds on framework of [13], but shows substantial improvements over it and is competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Presentation is clear but the paper feels dispersed across multiple contributions rather than centered on a single theme.  
- Suggested improvement: refocus abstract and introduction on the two-stream architecture and optical-flow significance.  
- Suggested improvement: add citations to relevant prior work (e.g., CVPR 2011 “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features...” and BMVC 2010 “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features...”).  
- Overall recommendation: accept (NIPS 2014) pending authors address the suggested revisions.
