- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: a spatial stream on RGB (ImageNet-pretrained) and a temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, fused by averaging or a linear SVM.  
- Explores motion encodings: optical flow stacking, trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, and mean-flow subtraction.  
- Uses multi-task learning to jointly train on UCF-101 and HMDB-51 to mitigate limited video data.  
- Achieves results competitive with hand-crafted pipelines (e.g., improved dense trajectories) and substantially improves over prior deep RGB-only models.  
- Positions work relative to dense trajectories/Fisher vectors and prior deep 3D/RGB models; explicitly inputs optical flow rather than learning motion implicitly.  
- Strength: clear architectural decomposition leveraging complementary appearance and motion cues.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance with thorough ablations on temporal input design (number of flow frames, stacking variants, mean subtraction).  
- Strength: demonstrates ConvNets on optical flow outperform RGB-stack ConvNets under limited data.  
- Strength: effective multi-task learning and practical reproducibility (Caffe, implementation details, multi-GPU, flow compression).  
- Weakness: temporal modeling is local (short windows, e.g., L=10), lacking long-range sequence modeling.  
- Weakness: depends on precomputed optical flow—computationally heavy and breaks end-to-end learning; camera motion handled only by mean subtraction.  
- Weakness: fusion performed at score level; deeper or joint fusion not explored due to overfitting concerns.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to trimmed datasets (UCF-101, HMDB-51); no robustness tests on untrimmed videos or different flow algorithms.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure modes and per-class performance.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear experiments and ablations; protocols and hyperparameters are well specified.  
- Recommended additional analyses: quantify training/inference cost (including flow), sensitivity to flow algorithm and compression, and detailed error/confusion analysis.  
- Clarity: well written and organized; could better explain loss weighting in multi-task learning and rationale for L=10.  
- Originality: novel and effective use of stacked optical flow as a temporal ConvNet stream and dataset-level multi-task training for action recognition.  
- Significance: high—provides a practical, influential baseline and guidance for injecting motion into ConvNets.  
- Suggestions: explore longer-term temporal modeling, end-to-end motion feature learning or robustness to flow algorithms, per-class confusion analysis, earlier/mid-level fusion with regularization, and report computational trade-offs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, clear motivations, thorough ablations, and a practical multi-task strategy.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited temporal context, reliance on precomputed flow with unquantified overhead, and evaluation restricted to trimmed datasets.  
- Overall: well-executed, influential paper that should be accepted.
