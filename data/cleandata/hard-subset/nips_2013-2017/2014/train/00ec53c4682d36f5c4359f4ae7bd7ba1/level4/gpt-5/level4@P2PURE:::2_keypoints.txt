- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet architecture that fuses a spatial (single-frame) network and a temporal (stacked dense optical flow) network for video action classification.
- Temporal stream processes short stacks of dense optical flow and is trained with several strategies; experiments show clear benefits of using optical flow as input.
- The fused model significantly outperforms previous ConvNet-based action recognition methods and matches state-of-the-art on two standard datasets.
- Method largely repurposes conventional image-classification ConvNet pipelines by applying them to optical flow and combining with single-frame ConvNets.
- Experimental study is thorough with convincing comparisons across training strategies and ablations.
- Minor concern: unclear why the spatial stream substantially outperforms the similar-architecture models in [13].
- Suggested additional experiment: compare different frame-sampling schemes for inputs to the spatial stream.
- Overall assessment: strong empirical contribution likely to interest researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
