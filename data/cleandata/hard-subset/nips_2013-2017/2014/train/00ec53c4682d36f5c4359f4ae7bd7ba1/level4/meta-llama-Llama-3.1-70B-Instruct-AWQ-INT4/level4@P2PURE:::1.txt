Summary:
This paper proposes a model for discriminative tasks involving video inputs, comprising two separate convolutional neural networks. One network takes an appearance frame as input, while the other network processes a stack of densely computed optical flow features. Each network is trained independently to classify its respective input, and the final prediction for a video is obtained by computing a weighted average of the predictions from both networks.

The model is evaluated on two datasets, yielding impressive results that are comparable to, or even surpass, those achieved by state-of-the-art methods relying heavily on hand-crafted features.

Strengths:
- The model represents a straightforward application of convolutional neural networks, achieving good results without unnecessary complexity.
- Unlike previous deep learning models, which often learn features directly from pixels, this paper demonstrates the effectiveness of using optical flow (or other hand-crafted features) for vision tasks, particularly action recognition. This contribution is significant, as it highlights the potential benefits of incorporating optical flow fields as input representations.

Weaknesses:
- A notable limitation of the model is its inability to account for the overall sequence of actions across the entire video, instead focusing solely on fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames.

Quality:
The experiments are well-designed, providing a solid foundation for evaluating the model's performance. However, it would be beneficial to include additional analysis of error modes, such as identifying specific classes or groups of classes that the model struggles to classify accurately. Furthermore, characterizing the types of videos that challenge the model could offer valuable insights.

Clarity:
The paper is clearly written, with the model explanation being particularly well-articulated.

Originality:
The application of a convolutional neural network to optical flow features is a novel contribution, setting the paper apart from existing work in the field.

Significance:
This approach has the potential to significantly impact research on using videos and motion for various vision problems. The model's simplicity, combined with its promising results, makes it an attractive solution for video data analysis. The use of optical flow features as inputs to a convolutional neural network is a notable innovation, offering a new direction for future research.
