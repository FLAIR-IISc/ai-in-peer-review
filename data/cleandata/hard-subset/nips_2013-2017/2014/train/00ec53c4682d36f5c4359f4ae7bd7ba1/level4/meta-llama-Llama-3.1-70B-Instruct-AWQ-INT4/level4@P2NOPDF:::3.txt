The paper presents several novel methods for video classification using deep learning, including a two-stream formulation and multi-task learning. The authors provide a thorough description of their methodology, implementations, and procedures, making the paper clear and well-written. The results demonstrate a strong case for the proposed methods, showcasing impressive performance.

The proposed methods are innovative, building upon the framework established in [13] while introducing significant improvements. The two-stream framework is particularly noteworthy, as it highlights the substantial contribution of the optical flow component. This finding is likely to generate interest among researchers in the field. Although the paper is well-presented, it appears to be divided across multiple contributions rather than focusing on a single theme. Nevertheless, the results exhibit considerable improvements over [13] and remain competitive with state-of-the-art performance.

To further enhance the paper, the authors may consider the following suggestions:

1. Refocusing the abstract and introduction to emphasize the primary theme, as the majority of the significance lies in the two-stream formulation and incorporation of optical flow.
2. Adding missing citations in the deep learning literature, including "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis" (CVPR 2011), and in the computer vision literature, including "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition" (BMVC 2010).

The paper offers substantial practical contributions and interesting ideas that complement the main concepts in deep learning. Based on the paper's merits, I recommend accepting it for NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
