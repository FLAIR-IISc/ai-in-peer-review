This paper introduces novel methods for video classification using deep learning, specifically a two-stream network and a multi-task learning approach. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described thoroughly. The paper is clearly written and effectively demonstrates the benefits of the proposed methods through strong experimental results. 

The contributions are noteworthy. While the work builds upon the framework of [13], the two-stream architecture is particularly compelling, as it highlights the significant performance gains achieved by incorporating optical flow. This finding will be of considerable interest to the research community. The presentation is clear, though the paper feels somewhat dispersed across multiple contributions rather than focusing on a single, unifying theme. Overall, the results demonstrate substantial improvements over [13] and are competitive with the current state-of-the-art.

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest the following:

1. Given that the primary significance appears to lie in the two-stream architecture and the integration of optical flow, the abstract and introduction would benefit from a more focused narrative.
2. The paper would benefit from including citations to relevant prior work, such as “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis” (CVPR 2011) and “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition” (BMVC, 2010).

Despite these minor points, this paper offers significant practical contributions and presents interesting ideas that complement existing deep learning approaches. I recommend accepting the paper to NIPS 2014, contingent upon the authors addressing the suggested improvements.
