This paper introduces a novel neural architecture for classifying human action videos. The approach blends the outputs of two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual frames and another trained on short stacks of dense optical flow images. The method substantially outperforms other ConvNet-based action recognition approaches and matches the current state of the art on two widely used video classification datasets.

While the work essentially adapts the standard ConvNet image-classification pipeline to a new input modality (dense optical-flow frames) and couples it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental evaluation is thorough and the results are compelling. It was valuable to see multiple strategies for training a ConvNet on optical flow, with clear evidence demonstrating the advantages of using optical flow as input.

I have only a few minor comments:
- It is unclear why the spatial network performs so much better than the models in [13], given the similarity in architectures.
- It would be informative to compare different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.

This is a strong empirical paper that will be of interest to researchers working on video classification with ConvNets.
