- Introduces a two-stream ConvNet architecture combining a spatial stream (single video frames) and a temporal stream (short sequences of dense optical flow) for action recognition.
- Leverages complementary strengths of spatial and temporal modalities to substantially outperform prior ConvNet-based methods.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on two benchmark video classification datasets.
- Extends the standard image ConvNet pipeline to dense optical flow inputs and shows this modality’s practical value.
- Provides a comprehensive experimental evaluation with systematic comparison of different ConvNet training strategies for optical flow.
- Notes that the spatial stream shows a significant performance advantage over the models in [13] despite architectural similarities, which needs further investigation.
- Suggests adding a comparative analysis of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.
- Overall, a strong empirical contribution of high interest to researchers in video classification and ConvNet applications.
