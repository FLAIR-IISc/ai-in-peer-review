- Proposes a novel deep learning framework for video classification combining a two-stream convolutional network and multi-task learning.  
- Two-stream architecture separates spatial (still frames) and temporal (optical flow) pathways, effectively capturing appearance and motion; optical flow stream yields significant performance gains.  
- Strong empirical results: substantial improvement over baseline [13] and competitive with or surpassing state-of-the-art on standard action-recognition benchmarks.  
- Paper is clearly written with sufficient methodological and implementation detail to aid reproducibility.  
- Weakness: manuscript feels split across multiple contributions and would benefit from a more focused, single-theme narrative.  
- Suggestion: revise abstract/introduction to center the paper on the two-stream architecture and explicit motion modeling.  
- Suggestion: strengthen related work by citing Le et al. (CVPR 2011, Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features with ISA) and Wang et al. (BMVC 2010, Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features).  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS 2014) conditional on addressing minor revisions—refine narrative and add suggested citations.
