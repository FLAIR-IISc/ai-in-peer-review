This paper introduces new methods for video classification with deep learning, notably a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning setup. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described in commendable detail. The manuscript is clear and well written, and the empirical results present a convincing case for the proposed approach.

The contributions appear novel. While the work builds on [13], the two-stream design is particularly compelling: incorporating optical flow yields substantial improvements over appearance-only models, a result that will interest many researchers. The presentation is clear overall, though the paper can feel somewhat fragmented across multiple contributions rather than tightly centered on a single theme. Despite this, the reported gains over [13] are impressive, and the performance is competitive with the state of the art.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Focus the abstract and introduction more tightly on the two-stream architecture and the role of optical flow, which seem to drive most of the performance gains.
- Add missing deep learning citation: “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis,” CVPR 2011.
- Add missing computer vision citation: “Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition,” BMVC 2010.

Overall, the paper offers significant practical contributions and presents ideas that complement mainstream deep learning approaches. I recommend acceptance at NIPS 2014, contingent on addressing the above suggestions.
