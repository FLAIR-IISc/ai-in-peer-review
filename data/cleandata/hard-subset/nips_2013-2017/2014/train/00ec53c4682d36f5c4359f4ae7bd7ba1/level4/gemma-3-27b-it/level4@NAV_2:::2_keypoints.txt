- Proposes a two-stream CNN (separate spatial and temporal networks) for video action recognition, with the temporal stream using multi-frame dense optical flow.  
- Achieves strong, competitive results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51, outperforming previous deep learning-based methods.  
- Main contributions: novel two-stream architecture, effective ConvNet training on optical flow with limited data, and multi-task learning to improve cross-dataset generalization.  
- Well-motivated work tackling an important computer vision problem.  
- The two-stream design is a logical and effective way to combine spatial (appearance) and temporal (motion) cues.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation including ablation studies on temporal input configurations (stacking, mean subtraction) and fusion techniques.  
- Results demonstrate the clear benefits of using optical flow and the complementary nature of the two streams.  
- Multi-task learning is a practical solution to limited video dataset sizes and improves generalization.  
- Minor suggestion: provide deeper analysis of why the spatial network performs well despite similarities to prior models [14], e.g., feature or training comparisons.  
- Minor suggestion: investigate different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream and sensitivity to number of sampled frames.  
- Minor suggestion: expand discussion of biological plausibility/two-streams hypothesis [9] for additional context.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong performance, novel architecture, thorough evaluation, clear writing, and significant contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: applying ConvNets to optical flow is not wholly novel, and the paper doesn't explore all alternative architectures or training techniques.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution; recommendation to accept.
