- Two-stream model: spatial ConvNet on frames and temporal ConvNet on stacked dense optical flow, trained independently and fused by score averaging.  
- Validated on two benchmarks, achieving performance on par with or close to state-of-the-art methods using engineered features.  
- Strength — simplicity: straightforward ConvNet architecture yields strong empirical results.  
- Strength — input representation: shows pre-computed optical flow is an effective modality for action recognition, unlike prior raw-pixel-only approaches.  
- Weakness — limited temporal modeling: cannot capture long-range dependencies, only short fixed-length snippets (e.g., L=10).  
- Quality: experiments are thorough and well-conducted but lack detailed failure-case analysis (confused classes, video properties causing poor performance).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and components are clearly explained.  
- Originality: main novelty is applying ConvNets directly to computed optical flow fields.  
- Significance: work likely to influence future motion/video analysis by demonstrating that simple ConvNet + optical flow yields strong results.
