This paper introduces a novel neural network architecture for human action classification in videos. The model leverages the complementary strengths of two convolutional neural networks: one trained on individual video frames (spatial stream) and the other trained on short sequences of dense optical flow images (temporal stream). The proposed approach substantially exceeds the performance of existing ConvNet-based methods for action recognition, achieving state-of-the-art results on two benchmark video classification datasets. 

Although the work largely extends the standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to dense optical flow data and combines it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive and the results are compelling. The systematic comparison of different ConvNet training strategies applied to optical flow is particularly valuable, clearly demonstrating the benefits of incorporating this input modality.

I have a few minor points for consideration:
- The significant performance advantage of the spatial stream over the models presented in [13], despite architectural similarities, warrants further investigation.
- A comparative analysis of different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream would be a worthwhile addition. 

Overall, this is a strong empirical contribution that will be of considerable interest to researchers in video classification and ConvNet applications.
