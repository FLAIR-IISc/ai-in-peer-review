- Proposes a two-stream model: a spatial CNN on appearance frames and a temporal CNN on stacks of pre-computed optical flow, fused for final prediction.
- Evaluated on two action-recognition datasets, achieving strong results comparable to state-of-the-art hand-crafted-feature methods.
- Shows pre-computed optical flow is a powerful input representation for deep models, challenging the need to learn all features end-to-end.
- Main limitation: cannot capture long-range temporal dependencies because it operates on short, fixed-length snippets (L=10).
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and well-designed but lacks analysis of error modes and per-class or per-video failure cases.
- Paper is well-written and clear, with components and methodology explained effectively.
- Original contribution is applying a convolutional network directly to computed optical flow as a dedicated motion-processing stream.
- Significant potential impact on video understanding and motion-based vision; simple architecture plus optical-flow input could inspire multi-modal and hybrid deep learning work.
