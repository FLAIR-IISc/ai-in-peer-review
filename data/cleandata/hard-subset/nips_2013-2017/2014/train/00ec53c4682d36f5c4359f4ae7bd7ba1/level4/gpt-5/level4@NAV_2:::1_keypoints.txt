Summary: Proposes a discriminative two-stream ConvNet (one for RGB frames, one for stacked dense optical flow) trained independently and fused for video-level action recognition, achieving strong results on two benchmarks.

Strength: A simple two-stream ConvNet design yields strong performance.

Strength: Demonstrates that stacked optical flow is a highly effective input for learning motion, outperforming attempts to learn motion directly from RGB stacks.

Weakness: Models only short fixed-length temporal windows (e.g., L=10) and does not capture long-range sequence dynamics across full clips.

Quality: Experimental setup and comparisons are sound; paper would benefit from deeper error analysis to identify failing classes and video/motion types.

Clarity: Presentation is clear, well-organized, and describes model and training procedures in sufficient detail.

Originality: Using a ConvNet trained directly on stacked optical flow fields for supervised action recognition is novel relative to prior RGB-focused deep models.

Significance: The approach could influence future video and motion understanding work; the choice of optical flow as ConvNet input is a broadly useful contribution.
