- Proposes a two-stream model: one convnet on an appearance frame and one on a stack of optical-flow features, combined by a weighted average of predictions.
- Evaluated on two datasets and achieves results matching or approaching state-of-the-art methods that use heavy hand-crafted features.
- Strength: simple application of convolutional nets that achieves strong performance.
- Strength: shows optical flow (hand-crafted motion features) is an effective input for convnets, especially for action recognition.
- Weakness: processes fixed-length (L=10) frame snippets and does not model long-range temporal structure across full videos.
- Experiments are well-designed but lack an error-mode analysis (e.g., per-class failures or video types where the model struggles).
- Paper is clearly written and the model is well explained.
- Originality: novel application of convolutional nets to optical-flow inputs.
- Significance: simple, effective approach with potential to impact research on using video and motion for vision tasks.
