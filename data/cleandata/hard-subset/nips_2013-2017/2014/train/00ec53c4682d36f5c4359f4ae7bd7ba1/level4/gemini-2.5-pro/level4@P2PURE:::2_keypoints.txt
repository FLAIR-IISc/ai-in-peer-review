- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: a spatial stream on individual frames and a temporal stream on dense optical flow, with fused predictions.  
- Temporal ConvNet trained on optical flow is a novel modality application and is shown to be effective.  
- Method significantly outperforms other ConvNet-based approaches and matches state-of-the-art on two standard action-recognition benchmarks.  
- Experiments are thorough and results are impressive, strengthening the paper despite the straightforward core idea.  
- Authors compare several training strategies for optical flow and convincingly demonstrate the benefits of this input representation.  
- Minor comment: need explanation for why the spatial network substantially outperforms the architecturally similar models from [13].  
- Minor comment: analysis would be strengthened by comparing different frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream.  
- Overall assessment: a strong empirical paper of interest to researchers in video classification with ConvNets.
