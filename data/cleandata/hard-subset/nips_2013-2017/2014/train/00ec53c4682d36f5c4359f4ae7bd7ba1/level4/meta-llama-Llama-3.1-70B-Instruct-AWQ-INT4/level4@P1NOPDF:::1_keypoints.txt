- Summary: Proposes a two-stream CNN (appearance frame + stacked optical flow) trained separately with predictions fused by a weighted average; evaluated on two datasets achieving near state-of-the-art results.
- Strength: Simple, effective CNN design that obtains strong results.
- Strength: Demonstrates the value of optical flow (hand-crafted motion features) as inputs to deep networks for action recognition.
- Weakness: Does not model long-range temporal structure, only fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames.
- Quality: Experiments are well-designed; lacks error-mode analysis (which classes or video types fail).
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written and well-structured.
- Originality: Novel application of convolutional neural networks to optical flow features.
- Significance: Simple, promising approach with potential impact on video/motion-based vision tasks.
