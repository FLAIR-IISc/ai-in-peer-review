- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet for action recognition: spatial stream on RGB, temporal stream on stacked optical flow, with softmax fusion.  
- Explicitly decouples appearance and motion, arguing optical flow input makes learning motion-sensitive features easier.  
- Temporal network explores flow variants: trajectory stacking, bi-directional flow, mean subtraction, and different stack lengths.  
- Spatial network is ImageNet-pretrained and fine-tuned for video classification.  
- Introduces multi-task learning to jointly train on HMDB-51 and UCF-101 with dataset-specific heads to mitigate limited video data.  
- Shows substantial improvement over prior deep models that use raw frame stacks and approaches state-of-the-art hand-crafted pipelines.  
- Argues temporal ConvNet can subsume classical motion features (HOF/MBH) by learning orientation- and gradient-sensitive filters.  
- Methodology is sound with careful ablations on temporal inputs, fusion, and the benefit of ImageNet pretraining.  
- Experiments use standard splits and strong baselines, supporting the paper’s claims.  
- Multi-task learning is pragmatic, effective, and yields consistent performance gains.  
- Provides useful implementation details (training schedule, augmentation, multi-GPU, flow precomputation/compression) aiding reproducibility.  
- Paper is clearly written and organized, with precise descriptions of temporal input configurations.  
- Narrative is sometimes diffuse across multiple contributions; abstract/introduction should better foreground the two-stream thesis.  
- Decoupling streams and flow-stacking with mean subtraction are original and effective contributions.  
- Approach is significant: simple, scalable, practical, and likely influential due to strong empirical gains.  
- Strengths: clear modular design, strong empirical improvements, thorough ablations, practical multi-task training, and implementation guidance.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on precomputed optical flow adds computational overhead and a non-learned component.  
- Lacks more direct quantitative comparisons to HOF/MBH within the same pipeline to strengthen the claim of subsumption.  
- Camera motion compensation discussion is limited (only mean flow subtraction); more analysis or stronger compensation comparisons are needed.  
- Suggestions: refocus abstract/introduction on two-stream design, expand discussion of computational cost and end-to-end motion learning, clarify differences from 3D ConvNets/unsupervised methods, and ensure broader citation of related work.  
- Pros: strong empirical results, well-motivated simple architecture, thorough ablations, and multi-task strategy addressing data limits.  
- Cons: engineering overhead for precomputed flow, somewhat diffuse narrative, and limited runtime/camera-motion analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to sharpen narrative and broaden discussion of related work and computational trade-offs.
