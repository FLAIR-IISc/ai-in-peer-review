This paper introduces a dual-stream convolutional architecture for human action recognition in videos. The approach fuses predictions from two ConvNets: a spatial network trained on individual frames and a temporal network trained on short stacks of dense optical flow. The method substantially outperforms previous ConvNet-based approaches and reaches performance comparable to the state of the art on the UCF-101 and HMDB-51 benchmarks.

While the core idea is to apply a standard image ConvNet pipeline to a different input modality (dense optical flow) and combine it with a single-frame ConvNet, the experimental study is careful and the results are strong. The paper systematically explores several ways of training a ConvNet on optical flow, with clear evidence that optical flow input is advantageous.

I have only a few minor comments:
- The reason the spatial stream outperforms the models in [13] is not clear, given the close similarity in architectures.
- A comparison of alternative frame sampling strategies for the spatial stream would be informative.

This is a solid empirical paper that will interest researchers working on ConvNet-based video classification.
