- Introduces novel video classification methods: a two-stream architecture and a multi-task learning framework.  
- Methodology, implementation, and experiments are described in sufficient detail; the paper is well-written with strong empirical results.  
- Builds upon [13] and offers meaningful improvements, notably demonstrating that incorporating optical flow streams yields substantial gains.  
- Results show impressive improvements over [13] and competitive performance with the state of the art.  
- Paper feels fragmented across multiple contributions and lacks a cohesive, unified central theme.  
- Revision 1: Abstract and introduction should more clearly articulate a unified central contribution rather than focusing mainly on optical flow and two-stream enhancements.  
- Revision 2: Authors should cite missing foundational works, including CVPR 2011 “Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition…” and BMVC 2010 “Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition.”  
- Overall recommendation: Valuable practical contributions complementary to recent deep-learning advances; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
