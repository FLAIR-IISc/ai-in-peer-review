- Proposes a two-stream ConvNet: spatial stream on RGB frames and temporal stream on stacked dense optical flow, with late fusion; strong results on UCF-101 and HMDB-51.  
- Clear, simple, and practical architecture that effectively leverages ConvNets for video.  
- Shows optical flow is a highly discriminative input for temporal modeling, outperforming approaches that learn motion from raw frames.  
- Empirical results are strong and demonstrate complementarity between spatial and temporal cues.  
- Temporal modeling is limited to short fixed-length clips (e.g., L=10), lacking mechanisms for long-range sequence dynamics.  
- Experimental design and training protocols are sound.  
- Lacks an error analysis identifying challenging classes or failure-mode correlates (camera motion, occlusion, subtle motions).  
- Paper is clearly written and reproducible with detailed model and input descriptions.  
- Novel application of ConvNets to stacked dense optical flow combined with a separate spatial ConvNet via late fusion.  
- Potentially influential for incorporating motion into deep video recognition, especially with limited training data.  
- Pros: strong benchmark performance, simple reproducible design, and clear evidence that optical flow benefits ConvNet-based action recognition.  
- Cons: restricted temporal receptive field and insufficient analysis of failure cases.
