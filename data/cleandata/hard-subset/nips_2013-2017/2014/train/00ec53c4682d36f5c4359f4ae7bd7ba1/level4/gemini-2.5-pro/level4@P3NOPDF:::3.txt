This paper proposes a novel deep learning framework for video classification, introducing two main contributions: a two-stream convolutional network architecture and the application of multi-task learning. The methodology, implementation details, and experimental procedures are described with sufficient clarity and detail. Overall, the paper is well-written and makes a convincing case for the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Strengths**

1.  **Novelty and Significance:** The proposed two-stream architecture is a particularly interesting and novel contribution. By designing separate pathways for processing spatial information from still frames and temporal information from optical flow, the model effectively captures both appearance and motion dynamics. The empirical finding that the optical flow stream provides a significant performance improvement is a key result that will be of great interest to researchers in action recognition and related fields.

2.  **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents impressive experimental results that show a substantial improvement over the baseline method presented in [13]. The performance is highly competitive with, and in some cases surpasses, the state-of-the-art on standard action recognition benchmarks.

3.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is clearly presented, making the core ideas and technical details easy to follow. The authors have done a good job of documenting their approach, which should aid in the reproducibility of their work.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

While the paper presents several strong ideas, it sometimes feels split across multiple contributions rather than being driven by a single, focused theme. The following suggestions are offered to further strengthen the manuscript:

1.  **Refine the Narrative Focus:** The most significant contribution appears to be the two-stream architecture and the explicit modeling of motion with optical flow. The abstract and introduction could be revised to better center the paper's narrative around this core concept. A more focused theme would enhance the paper's impact and cohesion.

2.  **Inclusion of Relevant Citations:** The related work section could be strengthened by acknowledging prior work in both deep learning and traditional computer vision for action recognition. I suggest including the following citations:
    *   On early work in learning spatio-temporal features: "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition with Independent Subspace Analysis." (Le et al., CVPR 2011).
    *   On the evaluation of classic spatio-temporal features, which provides important context for the problem: "Evaluation of local spatio-temporal features for action recognition." (Wang et al., BMVC 2010).

**Recommendation**

This paper offers significant practical contributions and introduces interesting ideas that advance the application of deep learning to video understanding. The two-stream framework, in particular, is a valuable and well-executed concept.

I recommend that this paper be **accepted** at NIPS 2014, conditional upon the authors addressing the minor suggestions for improvement outlined above. Refining the narrative and including the suggested citations would further polish an already strong submission.
