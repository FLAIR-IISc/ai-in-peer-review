- Introduces novel deep learning approaches for video classification using a two-stream architecture and multi-task learning.  
- Methodology, implementation details, and experiments are described with clarity and rigor.  
- Writing is polished and results show substantial improvement over baseline [13] and competitive performance with state-of-the-art.  
- Two-stream formulation—particularly the optical flow features—is a significant, broadly relevant contribution to action recognition.  
- Paper lacks thematic cohesion; multiple advances are presented without a unifying narrative, making the core contribution diffuse.  
- Recommend refocusing presentation to emphasize optical flow integration as the central innovation; update abstract and introduction accordingly.  
- Missing key citations that should be added: CVPR 2011 "Learning Hierarchical Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition..." and BMVC 2010 "Evaluation of Local Spatio-temporal Features for Action Recognition."  
- Practical contributions are noteworthy and empirical results are robust and well-justified.  
- Recommendation: Accept pending minor revisions (refine narrative focus and add the missing citations); suitable for NIPS 2014.
