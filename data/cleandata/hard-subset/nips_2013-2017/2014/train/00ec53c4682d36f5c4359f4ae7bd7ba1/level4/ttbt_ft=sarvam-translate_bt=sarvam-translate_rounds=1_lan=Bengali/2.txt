This paper presents a new neural network architecture for classifying human activity video. This model combines the predictions of two convolutional neural networks: one trained on a single video frame and the other trained on a short sequence of dense optical flow images. This model performs significantly better than other ConvNet-based methods for action recognition and matches current state-of-the-art methods on two standard video classification datasets.

Although this paper primarily applies a standard ConvNet image classification pipeline to a new type of data (dense optical flow frames) and combines it with a single frame ConvNet, the experiments are thorough and the results impressive. It was interesting to see different ways of training ConvNet on optical flow compared to the results clearly showing the advantages of using optical flow as input.

I have a few minor comments:
- It is not clear why spatial networks are so much better than models from [13] even though the architectures are similar.
- It would be interesting to see a comparison of different sampling schemes for frames fed into the spatial stream. This is a good empirical paper that would be of interest to anyone working on video classification with ConvNet.
