- Summary: Two-branch CNN—one for appearance frames, one for dense optical flow stacks—trained independently and combined by weighted average; evaluated on two benchmarks with near state-of-the-art results.
- Strength: Simple convolutional architecture that achieves strong empirical performance.
- Strength: Novel use of optical flow as an explicit input representation, showing motion features improve action recognition beyond pixel-only models.
- Weakness: Cannot model long-range sequence structure, only processes fixed-length (L=10) adjacent frames.
- Quality: Experimental setup is robust but lacks in-depth error analysis (per-class failure modes and types of challenging videos).
- Clarity: Paper is well written and explanations are clear.
- Originality: Applying CNNs to optical flow features is an innovative contribution.
- Significance: A simple, effective method with potential to influence video-based vision research by leveraging motion/optical flow features.
